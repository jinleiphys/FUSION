# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering, Direct Reaction, and Fusion Cross Sections for the 9Be + 208Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies

> Based on the extended optical model approach in which the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts, simultaneous $χ^{2}$ analyses are performed for elastic scattering, DR, and fusion cross section data for the $^{9}$Be+$^{208}$Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. Similar $χ^{2}$ analyses are also performed by only taking into account the elastic scattering and fusion data as was previously done by the present authors, and the results are compared with those of the full analysis including the DR cross section data as well. We find that the analyses using only elastic scattering and fusion data can produce very consistent and reliable predictions of cross sections particularly when the DR cross section data are not complete. Discussions are also given on the results obtained from similar analyses made earlier for the $^{9}$Be+$^{209}$Bi system.

# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering, Direct Reaction, and Fusion Cross Sections for the 9Be + 208Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that simultaneous chi-squared analyses using only elastic scattering and fusion data (EF approach) can produce very consistent and reliable predictions of cross sections for the 9Be+208Pb system, particularly when direct reaction (DR) cross section data are not complete. This validates the EF method previously used for the 9Be+209Bi system, showing that the semi-experimental DR cross sections extracted from elastic scattering fits can substitute for missing experimental DR data.

## Method

Simultaneous chi-squared analyses were performed within the extended optical model framework, where the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts. Two types of analyses were carried out: the EDF approach using experimental elastic scattering, DR, and fusion cross section data, and the EF approach using elastic scattering and fusion data together with semi-experimental DR cross sections extracted from optical model fits to elastic scattering data alone. The optical potential included a bare nuclear potential, a volume-type fusion potential, and a surface-derivative-type DR potential, with energy-dependent strength parameters constrained by dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energies extracted from linear systematics: E0,D^exp = 30.0 MeV (DR), E0,F^exp = 35.0 MeV (fusion)
- Semi-experimental DR threshold: E0,D^semi-exp = 32.5 MeV
- Fusion cross sections range from 10 mb at Elab=38 MeV to 580 mb at Elab=50 MeV
- Experimental DR cross sections range from 109 mb at Elab=38 MeV to 410 mb at Elab=50 MeV
- Semi-experimental DR cross sections are systematically larger than experimental DR values, ranging from 71 mb at Elab=38 MeV to 597 mb at Elab=50 MeV
- Theoretical suppression factor R^th = 0.82 (EF case) compared with experimental R^exp = 0.79
- Theoretical suppression factor R^th = 0.87 (EDF case) compared with experimental R^exp = 0.77
- Revised suppression factor for 9Be+209Bi system: R^th = 0.81, R^exp = 0.79

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous extended optical model analyses of the 9Be+209Bi system (So et al., Phys. Rev. C 69, 064606, 2004) to the similar 9Be+208Pb system, where complete experimental DR cross section data became available from Woolliscroft et al. The study addresses the long-standing puzzle of why threshold anomaly is not observed in polarization potentials for loosely bound projectiles like 9Be, by showing that the DR potential dominates the net polarization potential at the strong absorption radius (12.3 fm) and exhibits smooth energy dependence, while the fusion potential separately shows the characteristic threshold anomaly seen in tightly bound projectile systems. The results enable future validation of the EF method when comprehensive experimental DR data become available, including inelastic scattering and other missing reaction channels.
