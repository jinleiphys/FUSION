# Coulomb dissociation, a tool for nuclear astrophysics

> A short status report on Coulomb dissociation, an indirect method for nuclear astrophysics is given. An analytically solvable approach to study electromagnetic excitation in ^{11}Be, the archetype of a halo nucleus, is proposed.

## Key claim

Coulomb dissociation is presented as a viable indirect method for determining astrophysical S-factors, but the authors emphasize that theoretical interpretation of experimental results is always necessary to extract the astrophysical cross sections. The paper proposes an analytically solvable model for electromagnetic excitation in the halo nucleus 11Be that can serve as a benchmark for more sophisticated approaches like the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) model.

## Method

The authors develop a semiclassical model of Coulomb excitation in the sudden approximation, treating higher-order electromagnetic effects to all orders analytically. They model 11Be as a 10Be core with a single halo neutron, using pure single-particle wave functions for the 2s1/2 ground state and 1p1/2 bound excited state, with binding energies of 504 keV and 184 keV respectively. The excitation amplitude is expanded in terms of a dimensionless strength parameter y, and the lowest-order and next-to-leading-order contributions to the excitation probability and total cross section are calculated analytically.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies: E0 = 504 keV for the 2s1/2 state, E1 = 184 keV for the 1p1/2 state in 11Be
- B(E1) value calculated: 0.193 e2fm2, compared to experimental value of 0.105(12) e2fm2 from GANIL data analysis
- Core radius R = 2.78 fm used in the model
- Adiabatic cutoff energy omega = 320 keV for the 1/2- state in 11Be
- Scattering length for p1/2 continuum: a(l=1, j=1/2) = 456 fm3 found from analysis of 11Be Coulomb dissociation data

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytically solvable model for higher-order electromagnetic excitation of neutron halo nuclei (Typel and Baur 2001) to include a p-wave bound state in addition to the s-wave state, as is the case in 11Be. The paper contrasts its simple, transparent approach with the more sophisticated XCDCC calculations of Summers et al. (2007), proposing the analytical model as a benchmark for testing such involved reaction models. The effective-range approach to low-lying E1 strength developed here applies to deuteron photodisintegration and s1/2-neutron halo nuclei like 11Be, 15C, and 19C, and enables future studies of r-process nuclei and 2p-capture reactions via Coulomb dissociation at upcoming radioactive beam facilities.
