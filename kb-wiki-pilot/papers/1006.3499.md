# Transverse electron scattering response function of $^3$He with $Δ$-isobar degrees of freedom

> A calculation of the $^3$He transverse $(e,e')$ inclusive response function, $R_T$, which includes $Δ$ degrees of freedom is performed using the Lorentz integral transform method. The resulting coupled equations are treated in impulse approximation, where the $NNN$ and $NNΔ$ channels are solved separately. As NN and NNN potentials we use the Argonne V18 and UrbanaIX models respectively. Electromagnetic currents include the $Δ$-isobar currents, one-body N-currents with relativistic corrections and two-body currents consistent with the Argonne V18 potential. $R_T$ is calculated for the breakup threshold region at momentum transfers near 900 MeV/c. Our results are similar to those of Deltuva {\it et al.} in that large $Δ$-isobar current contributions are found. However we find that these are largely canceled by the relativistic contribution from the one-body N-currents. Finally a comparison is made between theoretical results and experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the transverse electron scattering response function \(R_T\) of \(^3\)He including \(\Delta\)-isobar degrees of freedom using the Lorentz integral transform method in impulse approximation, finding that large \(\Delta\)-isobar current contributions are largely canceled by relativistic corrections from the one-body nucleon currents, leading to good agreement with experimental data at very low energy transfer but underestimation at higher energies.

## Method

The coupled \(NNN\) and \(NN\Delta\) channels are treated separately in impulse approximation, first solving the \(NNN\) part with the Argonne V18 and UrbanaIX potentials, then using that solution to compute the \(NN\Delta\) part. Electromagnetic currents include \(\Delta\)-isobar currents, one-body nucleon currents with relativistic corrections to order \(M^{-3}\), and two-body meson-exchange currents consistent with the Argonne V18 potential. The Lorentz integral transform method is used to compute the inclusive response function, with the Lorentz states expanded on a correlated hyperspherical basis and the Lanczos method employed for inversion.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers: \(q \approx 862\) MeV/c and \(q \approx 927\) MeV/c, with energy transfer \(\omega\) up to 20 MeV above the \(^3\)He breakup threshold.
- \(\Delta\)-probability in the \(^3\)He ground state: 1.14% (compared to 1.44% from Deltuva et al. using CDBonn+\(\Delta\) coupled channel model).
- Lorentz integral transform computed with \(\sigma_I = 5\) MeV.
- Maximum total angular momentum \(\mathcal{J}\) restricted to 5/2 for multipole transitions.
- \(\Delta\)-isobar form factor parameters: \(\Lambda_{\Delta N1} = 840\) MeV, \(\Lambda_{\Delta N2} = 1200\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Lorentz integral transform calculations of three-body response functions (e.g., DEKLOTY08, LEOT10, ELOT10) by incorporating \(\Delta\)-isobar degrees of freedom for the first time in this method. It contrasts with the full coupled channel calculation of Deltuva et al. (which uses CDBonn+\(\Delta\) and neglects relativistic currents) by using impulse approximation and including relativistic corrections, finding that these corrections largely cancel the \(\Delta\)-isobar contributions. The results enable future studies of \(\Delta\) effects in inclusive electron scattering at higher energies and in heavier nuclei.
