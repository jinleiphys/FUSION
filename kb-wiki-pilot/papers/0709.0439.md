# Cluster Model for Near-barrier Fusion Induced by Weakly Bound and Halo Nuclei

> The influence on the fusion process of coupling transfer/breakup channels is investigated for the medium weight $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co systems in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier. Coupling effects are discussed within a comparison of predictions of the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels model. Applications to $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co induced by the borromean halo nucleus $^{6}$He are also proposed.

## Key claim
The Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) model can provide a consistent theoretical description of elastic scattering, total fusion, transfer, and breakup for weakly bound projectiles on medium-mass targets, but it does not separate complete fusion from incomplete fusion. The presence or absence of a "breakup threshold anomaly" for lithium projectiles on light- and medium-mass targets remains an open question due to insufficiently precise elastic scattering data at sub-barrier energies.

## Method
CDCC calculations were performed for the $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co systems using the FRESCO coupled-channels code, treating $^{6}$Li as an $\alpha$-$d$ two-body cluster and including couplings to resonant and non-resonant continuum states up to 8 MeV. For the $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co system, a dineutron cluster model was adopted treating $^{6}$He as a two-body $^{4}$He+$^{2}$n structure with couplings to resonant (2$^+$, E$_{ex}$ = 0.826 MeV) and non-resonant continuum states up to f-waves. Short-range imaginary fusion potentials were used for each fragment separately, equivalent to incoming wave boundary conditions.

## Key numbers
- Total calculated breakup cross sections for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co were between 3.7% and 9.7% of the total reaction cross sections, small compared to total fusion cross sections
- Sequential breakup of $^{6}$Li via the 2.18 MeV 3$^+$ state: CDCC cross section $\sigma_{seq}$ = 22.5 mb, compared to experimental value $\sigma_{exp}$ = 45 $\pm$ 10 mb at 41 MeV
- Total $^{6}$Li $\rightarrow$ $\alpha + d$ breakup cross section $\sigma_{tot}$ $\approx$ 80 mb
- CDCC calculations for $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co predicted much larger total fusion cross sections compared to $^{4}$He+$^{59}$Co and $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, with inclusion of breakup couplings notably increasing total fusion for all energies

## Context
This work extends earlier CDCC calculations by Diaz-Torres, Thompson, and Beck (2003) by including full continuum couplings to reproduce elastic scattering data, whereas previous calculations had neglected imaginary parts of off-diagonal couplings. The predictions for $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co somewhat overpredict data published for other medium-mass targets such as $^{64}$Zn (Di Pietro et al., 2004) and $^{63,65}$Cu (Navin et al., 2004). The results enable future application of four-body CDCC models (currently under development by Moro et al., 2007) for an accurate $\alpha$-n-n description of $^{6}$He, and highlight the need for high-intensity radioactive ion beams to permit measurements at deep sub-barrier energies.
