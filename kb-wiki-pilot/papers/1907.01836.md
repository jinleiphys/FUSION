# Introduction to Nuclear-Reaction Theory

> These notes summarise the lectures I gave during the summer school "International Scientific Meeting on Nuclear Physics" at La Rábida in Spain in June 2018. They offer an introduction to nuclear-reaction theory, starting with the basics in quantum scattering theory followed by the main models used to describe breakup reactions: the Continuum Discretised Coupled Channel method (CDCC),the Time-Dependent approach (TD) and the eikonal approximation. These models are illustrated on the study of the exotic structure of halo nuclei.

## Key claim

These lecture notes provide an introductory overview of nuclear-reaction theory, covering quantum scattering basics and three main models for breakup reactions (CDCC, Time-Dependent approach, and eikonal approximation), with the caveat that the selection of recent literature on halo-nuclei structure reflects the author's personal biases and own research activity rather than being exhaustive.

## Method

The notes begin with non-relativistic scattering theory for two colliding particles interacting through a potential, introducing the cross section and its calculation from the stationary Schrodinger equation via the partial-wave expansion of the wave function. The optical model is presented to account for other reaction channels beyond elastic scattering. The three main breakup models are then described: the Continuum Discretised Coupled Channel method (CDCC), the Time-Dependent approach (TD), and the eikonal approximation, with a comparison of their advantages, drawbacks, and ranges of validity.

## Key numbers

- 1 barn = 10^-24 cm^2 = 100 fm^2 (definition of the cross-section unit)
- The proton-neutron phase shift in the ^3S_1 partial wave varies by 180 degrees, while in H and I partial waves it varies by less than 2 degrees (illustrating decreasing partial-wave contribution with increasing L)
- A resonance in the p-^13C elastic scattering occurs at a proton energy of about 550 keV, causing a sharp increase in the ^13C(p,gamma)^14N radiative capture cross section

## Context

These notes extend the textbook treatment of quantum scattering theory found in Cohen-Tannoudji, Diu and Laloë (Chapter VIII) and the more detailed presentation by Taylor (1972). The work enables students and researchers to understand how breakup reactions are used to study the exotic structure of halo nuclei, with more advanced developments referenced in works by Nunes and Thompson (2009) and Bertulani and Danielewicz (2004). The analysis of nucleon-nucleon phase shifts from Navarro-Pérez, Amaro and Ruiz-Arriola (2013) is used to illustrate how experimental scattering data constrain phenomenological potentials like Argonne V18 and CD-Bonn, as well as chiral effective field theory interactions.
