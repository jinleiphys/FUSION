# Scale and Scheme Independence and Position-Momentum Equivalence of Nuclear Short-Range Correlations

> Ab-initio Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations of nuclei from deuterium to 40Ca, obtained using four different phenomenological and local chiral nuclear potentials, are analyzed using the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF). We extract spin- and isospin-dependent "nuclear contact terms" for each interaction in both coordinate and momentum space. The extracted contact terms, that count the number of short-range correlated (SRC) pairs with different quantum numbers, are dependent on the nuclear interaction model used in the QMC calculation. However, the ratios of contact terms for a nucleus A to deuterium (for spin-1 pn pairs) or to 4He (for all NN pairs) are independent of the nuclear interaction model and are the same for both short-distance and high-momentum pairs. This implies that the relative abundance of short-range pairs in the nucleus is a long-range (mean-field) quantity that is insensitive to the short-distance nature of the nuclear force. Measurements of exclusive (e,e'NN) pair breakup processes are instead more sensitive to short-range dynamics

## Key claim

The ratios of nuclear contact terms for a nucleus A to deuterium (for spin-1 pn pairs) or to 4He (for all NN pairs) are independent of the nuclear interaction model and are the same for both short-distance and high-momentum pairs, implying that the relative abundance of short-range pairs in the nucleus is a long-range (mean-field) quantity insensitive to the short-distance nature of the nuclear force, while exclusive (e,e'NN) measurements remain sensitive to short-range dynamics.

## Method

The authors used the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) to analyze ab-initio Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations of two-nucleon densities in both coordinate and momentum space for nuclei from deuterium to 40Ca. The QMC calculations employed four different NN+3N interactions: the phenomenological Argonne V18 (AV18)+Urbana X (UX), its tensor-less reduction AV4'+UIXc, and two local chiral effective field theory interactions at N2LO with coordinate-space cutoffs of 1.0 fm and 1.2 fm. Contact terms were extracted by fitting the factorized GCF expression to the QMC densities at short distances and high momenta for each spin-isospin channel separately.

## Key numbers

- The scaling onset for two-nucleon momentum distributions occurs at 3.5-4 fm^-1 for phenomenological potentials and at 2-2.5 fm^-1 for chiral interactions for pn pairs
- For pp pairs the scaling starts at slightly higher momenta than the equivalent pn scaling
- The universal function normalization was chosen such that its integral above 1.3 fm^-1 (approximately k_F ≈ 250 MeV/c) equals unity
- Contact ratios were extracted by fitting at approximately 1 fm in coordinate space and at 4.5 fm^-1 (phenomenological) or 3.5 fm^-1 (chiral) in momentum space
- The systematic uncertainty from VMC/DMC differences was conservatively fixed at 10% (1σ) for all extracted contacts

## Context

This work extends previous GCF studies that validated the factorization using only the AV18+UX interaction, and earlier evidence from Refs. Chen et al. (2016) and Lynn et al. (2019) for scale-and-scheme independence of ρ_A(r)/ρ_d(r) ratios without spin-isospin separation. The results are consistent with prior calculations by van Halst et al. (2012), Colle et al. (2013-2015), and Ryckebusch et al. (2015, 2018) that found the relative abundance of SRC pairs to be a mean-field property. The findings enable using soft interactions to compute contact ratios for heavy nuclei, then multiplying by hard-interaction contacts for light reference nuclei to obtain absolute contacts for any nucleus.
