# 7Be breakup on heavy and light targets

> In this paper we present all-order quantum mechanical calculations of 7Be breakup on heavy (208Pb) and light (12C) targets. We examine the issues concerning the extraction of the astrophysical S-factor from the breakup data. We discuss the interplay between Coulomb and nuclear breakup, and the importance of higher-order couplings on the cross section. We show that nuclear and Coulomb contributions are not separable using the standard angular selection criterion as nuclear breakup remains large for small scattering angles, even for the heavy target. However, by selecting an upper limit on the relative energy between the final fragments, the contribution from the nuclear breakup can be significantly reduced such that Coulomb breakup is the main reaction mechanism. We show that the extraction of the asymptotic normalization coefficient may require more careful consideration of the nuclear interior than previously used.

# 7Be breakup on heavy and light targets

## Key claim

The paper shows that for 7Be breakup on both heavy (208Pb) and light (12C) targets, nuclear and Coulomb contributions cannot be separated using the standard angular selection criterion, as nuclear breakup remains large even at small scattering angles; however, by imposing an upper limit on the relative energy between the final fragments (approximately 1 MeV), the nuclear contribution can be significantly reduced so that Coulomb breakup becomes the dominant reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors perform all-order quantum mechanical calculations using the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method implemented in the coupled-channels code FRESCO, treating 7Be breakup into alpha + 3He as a three-body problem. The alpha+3He potential is taken from Buck et al. with parity-dependent depths (V_odd = 83.77 MeV for p- and f-waves, V_even = 66.10 MeV for s- and d-waves) and a spin-orbit depth of 3.8 MeV. Cluster-target optical potentials are fixed by elastic scattering data at energies corresponding to the cluster energy within the projectile, with calculations performed for 7Be on 208Pb at 100 MeV/nucleon and on 12C at 25 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 7Be ground state (2p3/2): S_alpha = 1.587 MeV
- Binding energy of 7Be first excited state (2p1/2): S_alpha = 1.158 MeV
- Calculated B(E2:3/2- to 1/2-) = 19.0 e^2 fm^4
- Buck model rms charge radius for 7Be: 2.62 fm (experimental: 2.52 +/- 0.03 fm)
- Breakup cross section from impact parameters below sum of radii: 28% of total for 208Pb target, 16% for 12C target
- Impact parameter beyond which nuclear effects are negligible: approximately 16 fm for 208Pb (corresponding to theta_cm = 2.5 degrees), approximately 13 fm for 12C (corresponding to theta_cm = 1.4 degrees)
- Non-relativistic kinematics at 100 MeV/nucleon introduces 3% error in momentum
- Sensitivity of breakup cross section to different optical potentials: less than 2% at 0 degrees for both targets

## Context

This work addresses uncertainties in the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be capture rate (S34(0) = 0.53 +/- 0.05 keV b as recommended by Adelberger et al.), which is important for solar neutrino physics and big-bang nucleosynthesis. The study extends previous CDCC calculations on 8B breakup (Nunes et al., Thompson et al., Esbensen et al.) and earlier studies of 7Li and 6Li resonant breakup (Shyam et al., Shotter et al., Hirabayashi et al.) to the specific case of 7Be. The results enable evaluation of whether Coulomb dissociation or asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) methods can improve upon direct radiative capture measurements for S34(0), though the authors conclude it remains unclear whether these methods will improve on the direct measurements.
