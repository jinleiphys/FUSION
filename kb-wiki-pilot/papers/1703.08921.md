# Nuclear physics insights for new-physics searches using nuclei: Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay and dark matter direct detection

> Experiments using nuclei to probe new physics beyond the Standard Model, such as neutrinoless $ββ$ decay searches testing whether neutrinos are their own antiparticle, and direct detection experiments aiming to identify the nature of dark matter, require accurate nuclear physics input for optimizing their discovery potential and for a correct interpretation of their results. This demands a detailed knowledge of the nuclear structure relevant for these processes. For instance, neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements are very sensitive to the nuclear correlations in the initial and final nuclei, and the spin-dependent nuclear structure factors of dark matter scattering depend on the subtle distribution of the nuclear spin among all nucleons. In addition, nucleons are composite and strongly interacting, which implies that many-nucleon processes are necessary for a correct description of nuclei and their interactions. It is thus crucial that theoretical studies and experimental analyses consider $β$ decays and dark matter interactions with a coupling to two nucleons, called two-nucleon currents.

## Key claim

Neutrinoless ββ decay nuclear matrix elements and dark matter spin-dependent structure factors are highly sensitive to nuclear correlations and the distribution of nuclear spin among nucleons, and many-nucleon processes (two-nucleon currents) are necessary for a correct description of nuclei and their interactions in both processes.

## Method

The paper reviews nuclear structure calculations for neutrinoless ββ decay nuclear matrix elements using multiple many-body approaches (shell model, quasiparticle random phase approximation, energy density functional, interacting boson model) and compares their predictions. For dark matter direct detection, the author analyzes WIMP-nucleus scattering cross-sections using a nonrelativistic effective field theory framework and chiral effective field theory to include one- and two-nucleon operators, calculating nuclear structure factors for coherent and spin-dependent scattering.

## Key numbers

- Neutrinoless ββ decay experimental sensitivity reaches half-lives longer than T_{1/2}^{0νββ} = 10^{26} years
- Dark matter direct detection excludes scattering cross-sections smaller than σ_{χN} = 10^{-40} cm²
- Two-neutrino ββ decay half-lives are of order T_{1/2}^{2νββ} ∼ 10^{19}–10^{21} years
- Different nuclear structure approaches disagree in predicted neutrinoless ββ decay matrix elements by up to a factor of three
- Two-neutrino ββ decay involves momentum transfers q ∼ 1 MeV; neutrinoless ββ decay involves q ∼ 100 MeV
- Shell model calculation for ⁴⁸Ca extending configuration space from one to two major harmonic oscillator shells produced a 30% enhancement in the nuclear matrix element
- Two-body current parameter δ ∼ −0.2 encodes two-nucleon contributions to spin-dependent dark matter scattering structure factors
- Expected WIMP masses are M_χ ∼ 1–1000 GeV; lighter masses M_χ ∼ 1 MeV are probed via scattering off electrons

## Context

This work extends the understanding of nuclear structure effects in neutrinoless ββ decay and dark matter direct detection, building on prior calculations by the Jyvaskyla, Tubingen, and Chapel Hill QRPA groups, the Michigan and Strasbourg-Madrid shell model groups, and the interacting boson model calculations of Barea et al. The paper identifies that uncorrelated nuclear matrix element disagreements between shell model and energy density functional methods are limited to 30% or less, and that proton-neutron (isoscalar) pairing correlations are a key source of discrepancies. The findings enable more accurate interpretation of experimental results from KamLAND-Zen, LUX, and next-generation tonne-scale experiments, and motivate ab initio calculations in lighter systems to benchmark ββ decay matrix elements and understand the "g_A quenching" phenomenon.
