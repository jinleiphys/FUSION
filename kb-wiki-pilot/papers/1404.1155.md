# Eikonal reaction theory for two-neutron removal reactions

> The eikonal reaction theory (ERT) proposed lately is a method of calculating one-neutron removal reactions at intermediate incident energies in which Coulomb breakup is treated accurately with the continuum discretized coupled-channels method. ERT is extended to two-neutron removal reactions. ERT reproduces measured one- and two-neutron removal cross sections for 6He scattering on 12C and 208Pb targets at 240 MeV/nucleon and also on a 28Si target at 52 MeV/nucleon. For the heavier target in which Coulomb breakup is important, ERT yields much better agreement with the measured cross sections than the Glauber model.

## Key claim

The eikonal reaction theory (ERT), previously developed for one-neutron removal, is extended to two-neutron removal reactions and shown to reproduce measured cross sections for 6He scattering on light and heavy targets with no free parameters; for heavy targets where Coulomb breakup is important, ERT yields much better agreement with data than the Glauber model, which neglects Coulomb breakup and underestimates elastic breakup and two-neutron removal cross sections.

## Method

The authors formulate a four-body model (n + n + alpha + target) for 6He scattering and apply the eikonal approximation to the Schrodinger equation, treating the nuclear interactions of the two neutrons and the alpha particle with the target under the adiabatic approximation while treating the Coulomb interaction of the alpha particle exactly via the continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). The S-matrix is factorized into separate operators for each constituent, and the one- and two-neutron removal cross sections are decomposed into elastic breakup, one-neutron stripping, and two-neutron stripping contributions, all computed with four-body CDCC using the Melbourne nucleon-nucleon g-matrix interaction folded with target densities.

## Key numbers

- One- and two-neutron removal cross sections for 6He on 12C and 208Pb targets at 240 MeV/nucleon are reproduced by ERT with no free parameters
- For 6He on 28Si at 52 MeV/nucleon, ERT results are consistent with experimental data for both sigma_{-2n} and sigma_R
- For the 28Si target at 52 MeV/nucleon, the deviation of Glauber-model results from ERT results for sigma_{-2n} and sigma_R is about 10%, while the error of the adiabatic approximation itself is 3% for those quantities
- For the 208Pb target at 240 MeV/nucleon, the Glauber model underestimates ERT results for elastic breakup and two-neutron removal cross sections, while one- and two-neutron stripping cross sections from the Glauber model remain close to ERT results

## Context

This work extends the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) previously developed for one-neutron removal reactions (Yahiro et al., Prog. Theor. Phys. 126, 2011) to two-neutron removal, building on four-body CDCC methods developed for 6He scattering (Matsumoto et al., Phys. Rev. C 82, 2010). The approach solves the long-standing problem of Coulomb breakup in the Glauber model, which previously required ad-hoc corrections (Ibrahim and Suzuki, Capel et al.) and could only be applied to light targets. The method enables consistent analysis of two-neutron removal reactions on both light and heavy targets, allowing determination of spectroscopic factors and two-neutron correlations in halo nuclei.
