# Coulomb and nuclear effects in breakup and reaction cross sections

> We use a three-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) model to investigate Coulomb and nuclear effects in breakup and reaction cross sections. The breakup of the projectile is simulated by a finite number of square integrable wave functions. First we show that the scattering matrices can be split in a nuclear term, and in a Coulomb term. This decomposition is based on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and requires the scattering wave functions. We present two different methods to separate both effects. Then, we apply this separation to breakup and reaction cross sections of 7Li + 208Pb. For breakup, we investigate various aspects, such as the role of the alpha + t continuum, the angular-momentum distribution, and the balance between Coulomb and nuclear effects. We show that there is a large ambiguity in defining the 'Coulomb' and 'nuclear' breakup cross sections, since both techniques, although providing the same total breakup cross sections, strongly differ for the individual components. We suggest a third method which could be efficiently used to address convergence problems at large angular momentum. For reaction cross sections, interference effects are smaller, and the nuclear contribution is dominant above the Coulomb barrier. We also draw attention on different definitions of the reaction cross section which exist in the literature, and which may induce small, but significant, differences in the numerical values.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the total breakup cross section is uniquely defined, the separation into "Coulomb" and "nuclear" components is highly ambiguous: two different exact methods for decomposing the scattering matrix, although yielding identical total breakup cross sections, produce strongly different individual Coulomb and nuclear contributions.

## Method

The authors use a three-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) model for the 7Li + 208Pb system, where the 7Li projectile is described as an alpha + triton two-body cluster. The scattering matrices are decomposed into Coulomb and nuclear terms using two different exact methods based on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, both requiring the full scattering wave functions. The breakup and reaction cross sections are then computed from these decomposed scattering matrices for energies below and above the Coulomb barrier (27.7 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier height: 27.7 MeV; Coulomb barrier radius: 11.9 fm
- Grazing angular momentum L_g ≈ 26 at E = 42 MeV; L_g ≈ 17 at E = 34 MeV
- Maximum energy of pseudostates (E_max) = 15 MeV; maximum angular momentum in 7Li (l_max) = 3
- The 7/2- narrow resonance in 7Li is at 2.19 MeV experimentally, approximated at 2.1 MeV in the model
- At least E_max = 10 MeV is necessary for fair convergence of breakup cross sections
- The 7/2- resonance contribution starts dominating the breakup cross section around 31 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of Coulomb-nuclear interference in breakup reactions (Refs. DLV98, KB12, HLN06, CGD15, RLC16) by providing exact decomposition methods within the CDCC framework, rather than relying on approximate DWBA or weak-coupling approximations. The results enable more rigorous analysis of breakup experiments involving exotic nuclei, and the authors also highlight that different definitions of the reaction cross section in the literature (Refs. Th88, TN09 versus their Eq. eqr1) can produce small but significant numerical differences for projectiles with spin I_ω ≥ 1.
