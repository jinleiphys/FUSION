# Suppressed fusion cross section for neutron halo nuclei

> Fusion reactions of neutron-halo nuclei are investigated theoretically with a three-body model. The time-dependent wave-packet method is used to solve the three-body Schrodinger equation. The halo neutron behaves as a spectator during the Coulomb dissociation process of the projectile. The fusion cross sections of 11Be-209Bi and 6He-238U are calculated and are compared with measurements. Our calculation indicates that the fusion cross section is slightly hindered by the presence of weakly bound neutrons.

# Suppressed fusion cross section for neutron halo nuclei

## Key claim
The fusion cross section for neutron-halo nuclei is slightly suppressed (not enhanced) by the presence of weakly bound neutrons, contrary to earlier theoretical predictions of enhancement. The suppression originates from the core-target Coulomb field inducing dissociation of the halo neutron, which acts as a spectator carrying away a portion of the incident energy.

## Method
The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation using a time-dependent wave-packet method with a partial wave expansion in the body-fixed frame. The projectile is modeled as a core plus one or two halo neutrons (10Be+n for 11Be; alpha+2n for 6He), and fusion is defined by flux loss through an imaginary potential in the core-target interaction inside the Coulomb barrier. Calculations include total angular momenta from J=0 to J=30 with a maximum n-C relative angular momentum l_max >= 30 to achieve convergence.

## Key numbers
- 11Be ground state: 2s bound orbital at -0.6 MeV (close to experimental one-neutron separation energy)
- 6He ground state: bound at -0.97 MeV (close to experimental two-neutron separation energy)
- For 11Be-209Bi: V_nC parameters V0=-53 MeV, r0=1.3 fm, a=0.7 fm; real V_CT V0=-65 MeV, r0=1.16 fm, a=0.8 fm; imaginary V_CT V0=-60 MeV, r0=0.6 fm, a=0.4 fm; V_nT V0=-44.019 MeV, r0=1.27 fm, a=0.67 fm
- For 6He-238U: V_nC parameters V0=-69.305 MeV, r0=0.667 fm, a=0.65 fm; real V_CT V0=-95 MeV, r0=1.1101 fm, a=0.5834 fm; imaginary V_CT V0=-10 MeV, r0=0.6 fm, a=0.7 fm; V_nT V0=-93.4 MeV, r0=0.986 fm, a=1.184 fm
- Model space: 0<R<50 fm, 0<r<60 fm with steps (Delta R, Delta r) = (0.2, 1.0) fm for 11Be and (0.3, 0.6) fm for 6He
- l_max = 70 used for converged results; l_max >= 30 required for convergence with V_nT included
- J range: 0 to 30; Omega_max = 0 (no-Coriolis approximation) sufficient for cross section calculation

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous time-dependent wave-packet calculations (Yabana et al., 2003, 2004; Nakatsukasa et al., 2004) from head-on collisions (J=0) to full impact parameter range, presenting fusion cross sections for the first time with this method. It contradicts earlier coupled-channel calculations by Hagino et al. (2000) and Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002) that predicted substantial sub-barrier fusion enhancement; the authors show this discrepancy arises from insufficient l_max cutoff (l_max=4 in those works versus l_max>=30 needed for convergence). The results are consistent with experimental data from Raabe et al. (2004) for 6He-238U and Signorini et al. (2004) for 11Be-209Bi, which show no evidence for fusion enhancement. The work enables future high-statistics measurements to determine whether fusion is truly suppressed by halo neutrons.
