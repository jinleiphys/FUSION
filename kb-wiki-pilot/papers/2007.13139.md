# Asymptotic normalization coefficient method for two-proton radiative capture

> The method of asymptotic normalization coefficients is a standard approach for studies of two-body non-resonant radiative capture processes in nuclear astrophysics. This method suggests a fully analytical description of the radiative capture cross section in the low-energy region of the astrophysical interest. We demonstrate how this method can be generalized to the case of three-body $2p$ radiative captures. It was found that an essential feature of this process is the highly correlated nature of the capture. This reflects the complexity of three-body Coulomb continuum problem. Radiative capture $^{15}$O+$p$+$p \rightarrow ^{\,17}$Ne+$γ$ is considered as an illustration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method, standard for two-body radiative capture, can be generalized to three-body two-proton (2p) radiative captures, but only under the condition that the highly correlated nature of the three-body Coulomb continuum is properly accounted for, a feature the abstract mentions but whose complexity is fully revealed only in the detailed analysis.

## Method

The authors generalize the two-body ANC2 method to an ANC3 method for three-body radiative captures by solving the three-body Schrödinger equation in the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) approach and in a simplified Hamiltonian (SH) approximation. For the 2p capture case, they use the SH approach where the Hamiltonian is factorized into core-proton and residual interactions, allowing semi-analytical expressions for the E1 strength function via inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation solutions with outgoing wave boundary conditions. The method is illustrated for the radiative capture \(^{15}\text{O}+p+p \rightarrow ^{17}\text{Ne}+\gamma\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{17}\text{Ne}\), the ANC3 value is \(\mathcal{C}_3 = 13400\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\) with effective charge \(Z_{\text{hh}} = 27.5\) and \(\mathcal{C}_3 = 5958\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\) with fitted \(Z_{\text{hh}} = 26.14\).
- The effective charges for \(^{17}\text{Ne}\) are \(Z_{\text{hh}}^{(000)} = 27.50\) and \(Z_{\text{hh}}^{(101)} = 27.41\) for the \(K=0\) and \(K=1\) hyperspherical channels.
- In the SH approach for \(^{17}\text{Ne}\), the saddle point parameters are \(\varepsilon_0 = 0.48\) and \(Z_{\text{sh}} = 23.282\).
- The Coulomb correction energy for the \(Y\) coordinate motion is \(E_{\text{cy}} = 3.67\ \text{MeV}\).
- The ANC3 approximation of the overlap integral deviates by about \(7\%\) from direct calculation in a broad energy range (\(E_T \lesssim 1\ \text{MeV}\)) for the no-Coulomb case.
- For \(^{6}\text{He}\), the ANC3 value is \(\mathcal{C}_3 = 0.3\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the ANC method from two-body radiative captures (standard in nuclear astrophysics for reactions like those studied by Xu, Timofeyuk, Mukhamedzhanov, and others) to three-body 2p captures, building on prior numerical three-body calculations for \(^{4}\text{He}+n+n \rightarrow ^{6}\text{He}+\gamma\) and \(^{15}\text{O}+p+p \rightarrow ^{17}\text{Ne}+\gamma\) by Grigorenko, Parfenova, and collaborators. The paper resolves a discrepancy between HH and SH approaches by showing that the HH method with a single \(K_f=1\) channel fails to capture the correlated nature of two-proton emission, while the SH approach correctly reproduces the low-energy E1 strength function behavior. This enables fully analytical calculations for processes previously requiring bulky numerical three-body computations, with direct applicability to explosive hydrogen burning in novae and X-ray bursts.
