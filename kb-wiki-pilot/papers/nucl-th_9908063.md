# An Update on NA50 and LUCIFER

> Recent analysis of both new (1998) and old (1995,1996) data obtained by the NA50 Collaboration on J/Psi suppression in Pb+Pb are examined in light of our already existing calculations with the relativistic heavy ion simulation LUCIFER. In particular we comment on the unexplained change of transverse energy scale by NA50 and the apparent disappearance from the data of discontinuities in the suppression with transverse energy.

# An Update on NA50 and LUCIFER

## Key claim

The apparent disappearance of discontinuities in J/ψ suppression with transverse energy in the new NA50 data was anticipated by cascade-based simulations like LUCIFER, and the change in the transverse energy scale was also predicted by the authors, who had previously noted a scaling factor of ~1.25–1.30 between the LUCIFER and NA50 Et scales. The authors argue that present deviations between their cascade calculations and NA50 data do not justify claims for plasma-like medium-based effects.

## Method

The authors compare their existing relativistic heavy ion simulation LUCIFER, a cascade-based multi-scattering formalism, to new (1998) and old (1995, 1996) NA50 data on J/ψ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV. They examine the ratio of J/ψ to Drell-Yan production as a function of transverse energy, using both scaled and unscaled Et comparisons, and also compare LUCIFER's transverse energy distributions to NA49 inclusive meson and baryon spectra. Breakup cross-sections for J/ψ on baryons are determined from nucleon-nucleus production data, while breakup on mesons is taken as 2/3 of that on nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The scaling factor between LUCIFER and NA50 '96 Et scales was close to 1.30
- A scaling factor of ~1.25 was previously employed in Reference Kahana98
- The authors cite an alternative endpoint of 125 GeV for transverse energy, compared to the previously published value of 150–160 GeV
- The normalization error from taking a ratio to Drell-Yan is estimated at 7% or less
- A slightly revised value (by ~5%) for the Drell-Yan denominator is used in the new unscaled comparison
- Breakup cross-sections on mesons are taken as 2/3 of that on nucleons

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cascade calculations (Kahana98, Kahana99) and directly addresses the NA50 collaboration's presentations at QM'99 (Kluberg) and the Erice proceedings (Kluberg99a). The paper contrasts LUCIFER's cascade approach with "Glauber" calculations that yield neither inclusive meson spectra nor direct Et distributions, and which were used to justify the inability of standard theory to explain J/ψ suppression in Pb+Pb. The results enable future comparisons at RHIC, where the authors argue that demonstrating clear deviation from cascade background will be necessary for claims of plasma production.
