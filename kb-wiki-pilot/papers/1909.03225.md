# Yield ratio of neutrons to protons in $^{12}C(d,n)^{13}$N and $^{12}C(d,p)^{13}$C from 0.6 MeV to 3 MeV

> The neutron yield in $^{12}$C(d,n)$^{13}$N and the proton yield in $^{12}C(d,p)^{13}$C have been measured by deuteron beam from 0.6 MeV to 3 MeV which is delivered from a 4-MeV electro static accelerator bombarding on the thick carbon target. The neutrons are detected at $0\degree$, $24\degree$, $48\degree$ and the protons at $135\degree$ in the lab frame. The ratios of the neutron yield to the proton one have been calculated and can be used as an effective probe to pin down the resonances. The resonances are found at 1.4 MeV, 1.7 MeV, 2.5 MeV in $^{12}C(d,p)^{13}$C and at 1.6 MeV, 2.7 MeV in $^{12}$C(d,n)$^{13}$N. This method provides a way to reduce the systematic uncertainty and helps to confirm more resonances in compound nuclei.

## Key claim

The ratio of neutron yield in $^{12}C(d,n)^{13}N$ to proton yield in $^{12}C(d,p)^{13}C$, measured using a thick carbon target, can identify resonances in both reactions while eliminating systematic uncertainties from incident beam intensity fluctuations; however, the method becomes less sensitive at higher incident energies because the thick target integrates over a wider energy range, making only the strongest resonances detectable.

## Method

A deuteron beam from a 4-MeV electrostatic accelerator (energy range 0.6–3 MeV, voltage fluctuation below 2 keV) bombarded a thick natural graphite target (1 mm thickness, 98.93% $^{12}C$ abundance). Neutrons were detected at $0\degree$, $24\degree$, and $48\degree$ in the lab frame using three EJ-301 liquid scintillators (efficiency 0.4–0.5, calibrated with a Cf-252 time-of-flight system), while protons were detected at $135\degree$ using an Au-Si surface barrier detector (100% efficiency). The yield ratio $Y_n/Y_p$ was calculated from the corrected counts, and resonances were identified by rapid changes (maxima or minima) in the ratio as a function of incident deuteron energy.

## Key numbers

- Resonances found in $^{12}C(d,p)^{13}C$: 1.4 MeV, 1.7 MeV, and 2.5 MeV (incident deuteron energy)
- Resonances found in $^{12}C(d,n)^{13}N$: 1.6 MeV and 2.7 MeV (incident deuteron energy)
- Neutron detector solid angle: $1.2\times10^{-2}$ sr (each of three detectors, 112.9 cm from target)
- Proton detector solid angle: $2.42\times10^{-3}$ sr (135 mm from target)
- Beam energy fluctuation: lower than 2 keV
- Statistical errors on yields: below 4.2% (each measurement above 560 counts)
- Contribution from $^{13}C(d,n)^{14}N$ reaction: within 3.3% of total neutron yield (due to 1.109% abundance of $^{13}C$)

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of $^{12}C(d,n)^{13}N$ cross sections (Jaszczak et al., Phys. Rev. 181, 1428, 1969; Michelnn et al., Nucl. Instr. Meth. B 51, 1, 1990) and $^{12}C(d,p)^{13}C$ cross sections (Papillon and Walter, Nucl. Instr. Meth. B 132, 468, 1997), which showed discrepancies in resonance energies around 1.4 MeV. The ratio method reduces systematic uncertainties from beam intensity variations and target conductivity issues, enabling resonance identification without precise beam current measurement. The results enable more reliable cross section determinations for applications in the CNO cycle via the Trojan Horse Method, ion beam analysis of carbon in materials, and correction of d(d,n)$^{3}$He measurements in laser-induced plasma experiments.
