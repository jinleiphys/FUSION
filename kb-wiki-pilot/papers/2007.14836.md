# Applications of the chiral potential with the semi-local regularization in momentum space to the disintegration processes

> We apply the chiral potential with the momentum space semi-local regularization to the $^2$H and $^3$He photodisintegration processes and to the (anti)neutrino induced deuteron breakup reactions. Specifically, the differential cross section, the photon analyzing power and the final proton polarization have been calculated for the deuteron photodisintegration at the photon energies 30 MeV and 100 MeV. For the $^3$He photodisintegration predictions for the semi-inclusive and exclusive differential cross sections are presented for the photon energies up to 120 MeV. The total cross section is calculated for the (anti)neutrino disintegrations of the deuteron for the (anti)neutrino energies below 200 MeV. The predictions based on the Argonne V18 potential or on the older chiral force with regularization applied in coordinate space are used for comparison. Using the fifth order chiral nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented with dominant contributions from the sixth order allows us to obtain converged predictions for the regarded reactions and observables. Our results based on the newest semi-local chiral potentials show even smaller cutoff dependence for the considered electroweak observables than the previously reported ones with a coordinate-space regulator. However, some of the studied polarization observables in the deuteron photodisintegration process reveal more sensitivity to the regulator value than the unpolarized cross section. The chiral potential regularized semi-locally in momentum space yields also fast convergence of results with the chiral order. These features make the used potential a high quality tool to study electroweak processes.

## Key claim

The semi-local momentum-space (SMS) regularized chiral potential yields even smaller cutoff dependence for electroweak observables than the previously reported coordinate-space regularized potential, though some polarization observables in deuteron photodisintegration show more sensitivity to the regulator value than the unpolarized cross section.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger and Lippmann-Schwinger equations for two-nucleon (2N) reactions and the Faddeev formalism for three-nucleon (3N) reactions, using the SMS chiral potential up to N4LO+ order. For electromagnetic reactions, the single-nucleon current was supplemented implicitly by two-body contributions via the Siegert theorem; for weak reactions, only the single-nucleon weak current was used. Calculations were performed in momentum space with partial wave decomposition up to total angular momentum j=4 for 2N systems and J=15/2 for 3N systems.

## Key numbers

- For deuteron photodisintegration at E_gamma = 30 MeV, the difference between N2LO and N3LO differential cross sections is approximately 0.06% at the maximum
- At E_gamma = 100 MeV, the difference between N2LO and higher-order predictions remains below 3%
- The truncation error band at N4LO+ is around 0.1% of the cross section magnitude at E_gamma = 30 MeV and 2.5% at E_gamma = 100 MeV
- The difference between N4LO SMS and SCS cross sections reaches 1.07 microbarn/sr (approximately 3%) at theta_p = 79 degrees for E_gamma = 30 MeV, and 0.467 microbarn/sr (approximately 8%) at theta_p = 52 degrees for E_gamma = 100 MeV
- For the photon analyzing power A_X at E_gamma = 100 MeV, the dependence on the regulator value amounts up to 14%
- For the proton polarization P_y at E_gamma = 100 MeV, the relative difference between predictions with different regulator values is less than 5%
- For neutrino-induced deuteron breakup at E_nu = 100 MeV, the relative difference between N4LO and N4LO+ total cross sections is 0.093% for the neutral-current reaction and 0.029% for the charged-current reaction
- The relative difference for total cross sections obtained using different cutoff values at N4LO and E_nu = 100 MeV is 0.96% for the neutral-current reaction and 0.90% for the charged-current reaction

## Context

This work extends applications of the SMS chiral potential, previously used only for strong interactions (nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and nucleon-induced deuteron breakup), to electroweak reactions including deuteron and 3He photodisintegration and neutrino-induced deuteron breakup. It improves upon earlier studies using the semi-local coordinate-space (SCS) regularized potential (Epelbaum et al., 2014) and the Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa et al., 1995), which showed stronger cutoff dependence. The results enable future tests of consistent two-body electroweak currents once they are fully derived and regularized for the SMS potential.
