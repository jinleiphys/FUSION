# Concurrent application of ANC and THM to assess the $^{13}{\rm C}(α,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ absolute cross section at astrophysical energies and possible consequences for neutron production in low-mass AGB stars

> The $^{13}{\rm C}(α,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction is considered to be the main neutron source responsible for the production of heavy nuclides (from ${\rm Sr}$ to ${\rm Bi}$) through slow $n$-capture nucleosynthesis ($s$-process) at low temperatures during the asymptotic giant branch (AGB) phase of low mass stars ($\lesssim 3-4\;{\rm M}_{\odot}$, or LMSs). In recent years, several direct and indirect measurements have been carried out to determine the cross section at the energies of astrophysical interest (around $190\pm40\;{\rm keV}$). However, they yield inconsistent results causing a highly uncertain reaction rate and affecting the neutron release in LMSs. In this work we have combined two indirect approaches, the asymptotic normalization coefficient (or ANC) and the Trojan Horse Method (THM), to unambiguously determine the absolute value of the $^{13}{\rm C}(α,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ astrophysical factor. Therefore, we have determined a very accurate reaction rate to be introduced into astrophysical models of $s$-process nucleosynthesis in LMSs. Calculations using such recommended rate have shown limited variations in the production of those neutron-rich nuclei (with $86\leq A\leq 209$) receiving contribution only by slow neutron captures.

## Key claim

By combining the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) and Trojan Horse Method (THM) in a new normalization procedure, the authors resolve discrepancies between previous indirect measurements and identify which direct datasets are compatible with both the ANC of the threshold resonance and the THM data, yielding a consistent absolute cross section for the $^{13}{\rm C}(\alpha,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed THM data from La Cognata et al. (2013) using the updated resonance energy (4.7 keV above threshold) and width ($\Gamma_n = 136 \pm 5$ keV) from Faehlmann et al. (2015) for the $1/2^+$ state of $^{17}{\rm O}$, together with the ANC value of $3.6 \pm 0.7$ fm$^{-1}$ from Avila et al. (2015). Instead of the usual THM normalization to direct data at high energies, they reversed the procedure: they used the literature ANC to scale the THM data to the threshold resonance, then performed HOES (half-of-energy-shell) $R$-matrix fits to determine which direct measurement datasets best matched the THM data across the full energy range from $-0.3$ to $1.2$ MeV.

## Key numbers

* The $1/2^+$ state of $^{17}{\rm O}$ is now centered at $4.7 \pm 3$ keV above the $^{13}{\rm C}+\alpha$ threshold (from Faehlmann et al. 2015), no longer a sub-threshold resonance
* Neutron partial width of the $1/2^+$ state: $\Gamma_n = 136 \pm 5$ keV (from Faehlmann et al. 2015)
* Squared Coulomb-modified ANC: $(\tilde{C}_{\alpha^{13}{\rm C}}^{^{17}{\rm O}(1/2^+)})^2 = 3.6 \pm 0.7$ fm$^{-1}$ (from Avila et al. 2015)
* The combination of Bair & Haas (1973) data and Drottleff et al. (1993) data at low energies ($E_{c.m.} \lesssim 0.75$ MeV) gave the best fit to THM data with reduced $\tilde{\chi}^2 = 1.65$
* The ANC value extracted from THM data using the new resonance parameters would be $12.1^{+1.9}_{-1.6}$ fm$^{-1}$, significantly larger than literature values

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy between ANC measurements from transfer reactions (Pellegriti et al. 2008, Guo et al. 2012, Avila et al. 2015) converging around $4$ fm$^{-1}$ and THM measurements (La Cognata et al. 2012, 2013) that gave larger ANC values around $7-8$ fm$^{-1}$. The new normalization procedure demonstrates that the Bair & Haas (1973) and Drottleff et al. (1993) direct data are compatible with both the THM data and the precise ANC from transfer reactions, while other direct datasets (Davison et al. 1968, Heil et al. 2008, Harissopulos et al. 2005, Kellogg et al. 1989) are not. This enables a recommended reaction rate with reduced systematic errors for $s$-process nucleosynthesis models in low-mass AGB stars, superseding the NACRE II compilation (Xu et al. 2013) which had uncertainties up to $+36\%$ and $-28\%$ at $0.09$ GK.
