# Coupled-channels analyses for $^{9,11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb fusion reactions with multi-neutron transfer couplings

> We discuss the role of two-neutron transfer processes in the fusion reaction of the $^{9,11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb systems. We first analyze the $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction by taking into account the coupling to the $^{7}$Li + $^{210}$Pb channel. To this end, we assume that two neutrons are directly transferred to a single effective channel in $^{210}$Pb and solve the coupled-channels equations with the two channels. By adjusting the coupling strength and the effective $Q$-value, we successfully reproduce the experimental fusion cross sections for this system. We then analyze the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction in a similar manner, that is, by taking into account three effective channels with $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb, $^{9}$Li + $^{210}$Pb, and $^{7}$Li + $^{212}$Pb partitions. In order to take into account the halo structure of the $^{11}$Li nucleus, we construct the potential between $^{11}$Li and $^{208}$Pb with a double folding procedure, while we employ a Wood-Saxon type potential with the global Akyüz-Winther parameters for the other channels. Our calculation indicates that the multiple two-neutron transfer process plays a crucial role in the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb fusion reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The paper argues that multi-step two-neutron transfer couplings, rather than collective excitations or single-step transfer, are essential to reproduce experimental fusion cross sections for both the $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb and $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb systems simultaneously, but the parameters for the $^{11}$Li system are not uniquely determined because experimental data exist at only four energy points.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels equations including effective transfer channels: a two-channel model for $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb (coupling to $^{7}$Li + $^{210}$Pb) and a three-channel model for $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb (coupling to $^{9}$Li + $^{210}$Pb and $^{7}$Li + $^{212}$Pb). For the $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb channel, the nuclear potential was constructed using a double folding procedure with a dineutron cluster model for the halo density, while Akyuz-Winther global potentials were used for all other channels. The effective $Q$-values and coupling form factor parameters were adjusted to fit experimental fusion cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state-to-ground-state $Q$-value for $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb two-neutron transfer: $Q_{gg} = +3.0$ MeV
- Best-fit effective $Q$-value for $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb transfer coupling: $Q_{23} = -3.204$ MeV
- Best-fit effective $Q$-value for $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb transfer coupling (channel 1 to 2): $Q = +8.346^{+1.163}_{-0.897}$ MeV
- Ground-state-to-ground-state $Q$-value for $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb two-neutron transfer: $Q_{gg} = +8.852$ MeV
- Coulomb barrier height for $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb: $V_b = 29.1$ MeV at $R_b = 11.5$ fm with $\hbar\Omega = 4.07$ MeV
- Coulomb barrier height for $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb: $V_b = 27.3$ MeV at $R_b = 12.1$ fm with $\hbar\Omega = 3.04$ MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy for $^{11}$Li: $S_{2n} = 0.369$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channels studies of $^{9}$Li + $^{208}$Pb fusion (Vinodkumar et al., Phys. Rev. C 80, 054609, 2009) that found collective excitations alone could not reproduce experimental data. It also builds on the approach of Rowley (2001) for treating transfer to excited states via effective channels. The results enable future work that simultaneously treats transfer and breakup channels for weakly bound halo nuclei, and motivate additional experimental measurements of $^{11}$Li + $^{208}$Pb fusion at energies below the Coulomb barrier.
