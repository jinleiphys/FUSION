# Improved photomeson model for interactions of cosmic ray nuclei

> Photon-hadronic interactions are important for the sources and the transport of Ultra-High Energy Cosmic Rays (UHECRs). Current state-of-the-art cosmic ray transport simulations handle nuclear disintegration at energies of the Giant Dipole Resonance at a more sophisticated level, as well as the photohadronic interactions of nucleons in the high-energy regime above the pion production threshold. However, the interactions of nuclei above the pion production threshold are commonly modeled by treating the nucleus as a superposition of free nucleons -- ignoring the effect of the nuclear medium. We construct an improved, inclusive model for the photomeson regime for nuclei with $A \leq 56$ by employing more accurate, data-driven parametrizations of the interaction cross section, the fragmentation of the primary nucleus and the inclusive pion production cross section that directly affects the production of astrophysical neutrinos. We apply our results to two multi-messenger scenarios (Tidal Disruption Events and Gamma-Ray Bursts) in which photonuclear interactions in the photomeson regime are the dominant cooling process for the highest energy cosmic rays. While we find moderate changes to the mass composition of UHECRs, the astrophysical neutrino fluxes exhibit a significant (factor of a few) reduction compared to the naïve superposition of free nucleons for sources of UHECR nuclei with a populated cascade. The numerical code implementing the model has been made publicly available, which facilitates the integration of our results in similar frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an improved inclusive model for photomeson interactions of cosmic ray nuclei with mass number A ≤ 56, finding that while changes to the mass composition of ultra-high energy cosmic rays (UHECRs) are moderate, astrophysical neutrino fluxes exhibit a significant reduction (factor of a few) compared to the naive superposition of free nucleons for sources where UHECR nuclei have a populated cascade.

## Method

The authors replaced the standard "Single Particle Model" (SPM) treatment of nuclei as superpositions of free nucleons with data-driven parametrizations of three key ingredients: the total photonuclear absorption cross section (using a universal curve at low energies and an energy-dependent mass scaling exponent α(ε_r) at high energies), the inclusive pion production cross section (with a separate mass scaling exponent α_π(ε_r) derived from experimental data), and nuclear fragmentation (using an empirical data-driven parametrization). The model was implemented in a publicly available numerical code and applied to two astrophysical scenarios: Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs) and Gamma-Ray Bursts (GRBs).

## Key numbers

- The model applies to nuclei with mass number A ≤ 56
- At the Δ-resonance peak, the nuclear cross section per nucleon is 30% lower and 20% wider at half height compared to the free nucleon case
- For ε_r < 1 GeV, the total cross section scales with A (α = 1) using a universal curve
- For ε_r ≥ 1 GeV, the mass scaling exponent α(ε_r) transitions from 1 toward the theoretical limit α_lim = 2/3
- The inclusive pion production cross section scales as A^(2/3) near threshold (ε_r < 1 GeV), transitioning to A scaling for ε_r > 50 GeV
- Neutrino fluxes are reduced by "a factor of a few" compared to the SPM prediction for sources with populated nuclear cascades

## Context

This work extends prior photohadronic interaction models used in UHECR transport simulations, which handled nuclear disintegration at Giant Dipole Resonance energies and nucleon photohadronic interactions above pion production threshold with codes like Sophia, but treated nuclei above the pion production threshold as superpositions of free nucleons (the SPM approach used in references such as Anchordoqui:2007tn, Murase:2008mr, Batista_2016, boncioli2016nuclear, and Aloisio:2017iyh). The improved model enables more accurate predictions for multi-messenger signals from astrophysical sources where photomeson production is the dominant cooling process for the highest energy cosmic rays, particularly affecting neutrino flux predictions from TDEs and GRBs.
