# Microscopic effective reaction theory for deuteron-induced reactions

> The microscopic effective reaction theory is applied to deuteron-induced reactions. A reaction model-space characterized by a $p+n+{\rm A}$ three-body model is adopted, where A is the target nucleus, and the nucleon-target potential is described by a microscopic folding model based on an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in nuclear medium and a one-body nuclear density of A. The three-body scattering wave function in the model space is obtained with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT), an extension of CDCC, is applied to the calculation of neutron removal cross sections. Elastic scattering cross sections of deuteron on $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb target nuclei at several energies are compared with experimental data. The total reaction cross sections and the neutron removal cross sections at 56 MeV on 14 target nuclei are calculated and compared with experimental values.

# Microscopic effective reaction theory for deuteron-induced reactions

## Key claim
The microscopic effective reaction theory, combining continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with microscopic nucleon-target optical potentials derived from folding the Melbourne g-matrix interaction, can describe deuteron elastic scattering, total reaction cross sections, and neutron removal cross sections across a wide energy range without free parameters, but the microscopic optical potentials become invalid at low nucleon energies (around 10 MeV) because the folding model does not fully antisymmetrize the nucleon-nucleus wave function.

## Method
The authors treat deuteron-induced reactions as a p+n+A three-body system, solving the Schrödinger equation with CDCC. The nucleon-target potentials are obtained by folding the Melbourne g-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with target densities from Hartree-Fock calculations using the Gogny D1S force, including knockon-exchange nonlocality via the Brieva-Rook localization method. For inclusive neutron removal cross sections, the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) is applied, separating the scattering matrix into proton and neutron parts using the adiabatic approximation for the neutron-target potential.

## Key numbers
- Elastic breakup cross section on $^{58}$Ni at 21.6 MeV: 72.82 mb total, with p-wave contribution 19.78 mb and d-wave 17.78 mb
- Elastic breakup cross section on $^{58}$Ni at 56 MeV: 149.01 mb total, with p-wave 42.96 mb and d-wave 39.28 mb
- Elastic breakup cross section on $^{208}$Pb at 56 MeV: 287.50 mb total, with p-wave 139.75 mb and d-wave 55.38 mb
- Total reaction cross section on $^{58}$Ni at 56 MeV: 1692 mb (CDCC) vs 1662 mb (experimental)
- Total reaction cross section on $^{208}$Pb at 56 MeV: 2978 mb (CDCC) vs 3061 mb (experimental)
- Neutron removal cross section on $^{58}$Ni at 56 MeV: 524 mb (CDCC) vs 582 mb (experimental)
- Neutron removal cross section on $^{209}$Bi at 56 MeV: 810 mb (CDCC) vs 1082 mb (experimental)
- At 56 MeV on $^{208}$Pb, $\sigma_{\text{EB}}$ at $\ell_{\text{max}}=2$ differs from $\ell_{\text{max}}=6$ by about 8%; at 21.6 MeV on $^{58}$Ni, the difference is about 26%
- Adding $\ell=8$ changes $\sigma_{\text{EB}}$ by less than 2% typically, at most 4.5% for 56 MeV on $^{208}$Pb

## Context
This work extends previous CDCC calculations that used phenomenological nucleon-target optical potentials (e.g., Yahiro et al. 1986) by implementing fully microscopic optical potentials with no adjustable parameters, building on the microscopic reaction theory framework of Yahiro et al. (2012). The study improves upon earlier work by including odd-partial $\ell$ waves of p-n breakup states (previously ignored in Refs. Yahiro 1986, Piyadasa 1989, 1999) and clarifying the role of $\ell>2$ contributions not considered in studies by Chau Huu Tai et al. (2006). The success enables future application of microscopic CDCC to deuteron-induced reactions involving unstable nuclei, where phenomenological potentials are unavailable.
