# BeAGLE: Benchmark $e$A Generator for LEptoproduction in high energy lepton-nucleus collisions

> The upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will address several outstanding puzzles in modern nuclear physics. Topics such as the partonic structure of nucleons and nuclei, the origin of their mass and spin, among others, can be understood via the study of high energy electron-proton ($ep$) and electron-nucleus ($e$A) collisions. Achieving the scientific goals of the EIC will require a novel electron-hadron collider and detectors capable to perform high-precision measurements, but also dedicated tools to model and interpret the data. To aid in the latter, we present a general-purpose $e$A Monte Carlo (MC) generator - BeAGLE. In this paper, we provide a general description of the models integrated into BeAGLE, applications of BeAGLE in $e$A physics, implications for detector requirements at the EIC, and the tuning of the parameters in BeAGLE based on available experimental data. Specifically, we focus on a selection of model and data comparisons in particle production in both $ep$ and $e$A collisions, where baseline particle distributions provide essential information to characterize the event. In addition, we investigate the collision geometry determination in $e$A collisions, which could be used as an experimental tool for varying the nuclear density.

## Key claim

BeAGLE is a general-purpose Monte Carlo event generator for electron-nucleus collisions that integrates multiple physics models (PYTHIA-6, DPMJet, PyQM, FLUKA) to simulate lepton-nucleus scattering, but it currently does not include coherent diffractive processes, which are expected to be enhanced in nuclei due to gluon saturation effects and will need to be added in future development.

## Method

The generator models lepton-nucleus collisions by selecting a struck nucleon according to a Glauber-type model with EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions, simulating the partonic interaction and fragmentation via PYTHIA-6 with optional partonic energy loss from PyQM, then propagating hadrons through an intra-nuclear cascade using DPMJet, and finally treating the excited nuclear remnant breakup via FLUKA (including evaporation, fission, and Fermi breakup). The parameter tau0, which controls the formation time of produced particles in the intra-nuclear cascade, was tuned to tau0 = 10 fm using neutron multiplicity data from the E665 experiment on muon-lead collisions, assuming a 24% coherent diffractive fraction.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range for the EIC: 20 to 140 GeV
- EIC luminosity: 10^33-34 cm^-2 s^-1
- Default formation time parameter tau0 = 10 fm, determined from E665 muon-lead data where average neutron multiplicity was 4.7 +/- 0.5
- PYTHIA-6 tuned parameters: MSTP(94)=2, PARP(97)=6, PARJ(170)=0.32 for leading proton data; default fragmentation parameters PARJ(21)=PARP(91)=0.4
- E665 kinematic cuts for muon-xenon comparisons: theta > 3.5 mrad, Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, 8 < W < 30 GeV, 0.1 < nu/E_mu < 0.85
- ZEUS leading proton kinematic range: Q^2 > 3 GeV^2, 45 < W < 225 GeV, y > 0.03, p_T^2 < 0.5 GeV^2, x_L > 0.32

## Context

This work extends the BeAGLE generator from previous studies focused on exclusive observables in the far-forward region to inclusive particle production in both forward and central regions, comparing with fixed-target data from the E665 experiment at Fermilab and leading proton data from the ZEUS experiment at HERA. The generator has already been used for exploring diffractive and spectator-tagging physics at the EIC, including studies of gluon imaging in nuclei via incoherent vector meson production and extraction of free nucleon structure via spectator tagging in deuteron collisions. The tuning and validation presented here enable reliable simulations for EIC detector design, particularly for the far-forward region where detection of spectator neutrons and nuclear breakup products is critical.
