# Properties of nuclear and Coulomb breakup of 8B

> The dependence of breakup cross sections of 8B at 65 MeV/nucleon on the target mass number A_T is investigated by means of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with more reliable distorting potentials than those in the preceding study. The A_T^(1/3) scaling law of the nuclear breakup cross section is found to be satisfied only in the middle A_T region of 40 < A_T < 150. The interference between nuclear and Coulomb breakup amplitudes vanishes in very forward angle scattering, independently of the target nucleus. The truncation of the relative energy between the p and 7Be fragments slightly reduces the contribution of nuclear breakup at very forward angles, while the angular region in which the first-order perturbation theory works well does not change essentially.

## Key claim

The $A_{\rm T}^{1/3}$ scaling law of the nuclear breakup cross section for $^8$B at 65 MeV/nucleon holds only in the middle target mass region $40 \la A_{\rm T} \la 152$, not universally as previously assumed; the interference between nuclear and Coulomb breakup amplitudes vanishes at very forward angles ($\theta_8 \sim 0^\circ$) for all target nuclei, contrary to an earlier conclusion that this occurred only for $^{208}$Pb.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) based on a $^7$Be$+p+$A three-body model to calculate breakup cross sections of $^8$B at 65 MeV/nucleon on seven target nuclei ($^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{152}$Sm, and $^{208}$Pb). They employed more realistic $p$-target and $^7$Be-target optical potentials than in the preceding study: a Dirac-based global potential (EDAD1) for $p$-A and a modified $^7$Li global optical potential for $^7$Be-A. For partial waves with orbital angular momentum $L>1000$, the eikonal-CDCC method was used to obtain $S$-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The $A_{\rm T}^{1/3}$ scaling law for nuclear breakup cross section $\sigma_{\rm N}$ is satisfied only in the region $40 \la A_{\rm T} \la 152$
- The scaling formula $\bar{\sigma}_{\rm N}(A_{\rm T}^{1/3}) = 12.8 A_{\rm T}^{1/3} + 16.5$ (in mb) reproduces $\sigma_{\rm N}$ for $A_{\rm T}=40$, 58, 90, and 152
- The grazing angular momentum parameter $L_{\rm g} / K_{\rm A}$ scales as $1.4A_{\rm T}^{1/3} + 2.7$ fm across all targets
- The transmission coefficient $T_{L_{\rm g}}$ is approximately constant (0.28-0.31) for $40 \le A_{\rm T} \le 152$, but drops to 0.14 for $^{12}$C and 0.25 for $^{208}$Pb
- For $^{208}$Pb target, the first-order perturbation theory error is less than 10% for $\theta_8 \le 2.4^\circ$
- The critical angle $\theta_{\rm cr}$ where nuclear-Coulomb interference is less than 10% ranges from 0.30$^\circ$ ($^{12}$C) to 2.30$^\circ$ ($^{208}$Pb)
- The corresponding critical impact parameters $b_{\rm cr}$ range from 26.5 fm ($^{12}$C) to 47.6 fm ($^{40}$Ca)

## Context

This work extends the systematic CDCC analysis of Hussein et al. (Phys. Lett. B640, 2006) on breakup reactions of $^7$Be, $^8$B, and $^{11}$Be, using more reliable distorting potentials. It corrects the earlier conclusion that nuclear-Coulomb interference vanishes only for $^{208}$Pb, showing instead that it vanishes at $\theta_8 \sim 0^\circ$ for all target nuclei. The results enable extraction of B(E1) strength of $^8$B with approximately 10% error from $^{208}$Pb breakup data at forward angles using first-order perturbation theory, but emphasize that accurate optical potentials are necessary for systematic CDCC analysis of halo nuclei including $^6$He, $^{11}$Li, and $^{11}$Be.
