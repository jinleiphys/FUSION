# A self-consistent spectral framework for inclusive non-elastic breakup, with the Trojan Horse method as the sub-Coulomb resonant limit

> The Trojan Horse Method (THM) extracts low-energy charged-particle resonance strengths through a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) reduction of a three-body transfer matrix element. The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) inclusive non-elastic breakup framework has not been brought to the sub-Coulomb astrophysical regime where THM operates. I introduce a diagonal isolated-pole spectral ansatz for the absorptive participant-target optical potential with three explicit validity conditions, two closed by $R$-matrix tabulations and the third a model-dependent continuum-decoupling diagnostic. The IAV inclusive cross section reduces in the isolated-resonance limit to a sum of per-pole distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) pole cross sections weighted by channel branching ratios. The factorized PWIA-THM resonance-strength formula is then identified as a non-perturbative reduction of the per-pole DWBA cross section under four approximations (plane-wave substitution on the entrance and exit distorted waves, zero-range or surface-localized treatment of the spectator-participant interaction, on-shell evaluation of the binary subreaction vertex, and post-form remnant neglect), not a multiplicative correction factor. The per-pole DWBA pole cross section is the natural extraction quantity for sub-Coulomb resonance-strength analysis.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a diagonal isolated-pole spectral ansatz for the absorptive participant-target optical potential that connects the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) inclusive non-elastic breakup framework to the Trojan Horse Method (THM) at sub-Coulomb energies, identifying the per-pole distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) pole cross section, not a multiplicative correction factor on top of the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), as the natural extraction quantity for resonance-strength analysis, with the standard PWIA-THM formula emerging as a non-perturbative reduction under four explicit approximations.

## Method
The author formulates three explicit validity conditions for a diagonal isolated-pole spectral ansatz of the absorptive participant-target optical potential, where two conditions reduce to closed dimensionless bounds computable from R-matrix tabulations (off-diagonal coupling smallness and pole isolation) and the third is a model-dependent continuum-decoupling diagnostic. Within this ansatz, the IAV inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section is reduced in the isolated-resonance limit to a sum of per-pole DWBA pole cross sections weighted by Lorentzian profiles, with a three-layer Feshbach decomposition resolving the width dictionary ξ_n^IAV = Γ_n^non-el/2 ≃ Γ_n^tot/2. The factorized PWIA-THM resonance-strength formula is then identified as a non-perturbative reduction of the per-pole DWBA cross section under four approximations: plane-wave substitution on entrance and exit distorted waves, zero-range or surface-localized treatment of the spectator-participant interaction, on-shell evaluation of the binary subreaction vertex, and post-form remnant neglect.

## Key numbers
- The width dictionary gives ξ_n^IAV = Γ_n^non-el/2 ≃ Γ_n^tot/2, resolving half-width-versus-full-width ambiguities in the literature
- The off-diagonal coupling controlling ratio for condition (A1) satisfies |W_nm|/|E_n-E_m| ≤ √(ξ_n^IAV ξ_m^IAV)/|E_n-E_m|
- A recent THM measurement of the 11 keV resonance strength in ¹⁹F(p,αγ)¹⁶O disagrees by approximately a factor of six in the partial decay strength with the R-matrix evaluation using JUNA direct measurements
- The Lorentzian spectral weight ω_n(E_x) = (ξ_n^IAV/π)/[(E_x-E_n)²+(ξ_n^IAV)²] has full width at half maximum 2ξ_n^IAV

## Context
This work extends the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) inclusive non-elastic breakup framework, previously applied at intermediate and high energies, to the sub-Coulomb astrophysical regime where the Trojan Horse Method (THM) operates. It addresses the validity question of the PWIA reduction raised by Bertulani, Hussein, and Typel (BHT2018) and builds on the Breit-Wigner spectral decomposition of Lei and Moro (2018), while relating to the surface-integral formulation of Mukhamedzhanov, Pang, and Kadyrov (2019) and the IAV-revival implementations of Potel, Nunes, and Thompson (2015) and Carlson, Capote, and Sin (2016). The framework enables controlled sub-Coulomb resonance-strength extraction through the per-pole DWBA pole cross section rather than through multiplicative correction factors on the PWIA-THM expression, with the ¹⁹F(p,αγ)¹⁶O 11 keV resonance disagreement as a prominent quantitative target.
