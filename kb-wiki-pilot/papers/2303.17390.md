# Examination of cluster production in excited light systems at Fermi energies from new experimental data and comparison with transport model calculations

> Four different reactions, $^{32}$S+$^{12}$C and $^{20}$Ne+$^{12}$C at 25 and 50 MeV/nucleon, have been measured with the FAZIA detector capable of full isotopic identification of most forward emitted reaction products. Fragment multiplicities, angular distributions and energy spectra have been measured and compared with Monte Carlo simulations, i.e. the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and the heavy-ion phase space exploration (HIPSE) models. These models are combined with two different afterburner codes (HF$l$ and SIMON) to describe the decay of the excited primary fragments. In the case of AMD, the effect of including the clustering and inter-clustering processes to form bound particles and fragments is discussed. A clear confirmation of the role of cluster aggregation in the reaction dynamics and particle production for these light systems, for which the importance of the clustering process increases with bombarding energy, is obtained.

## Key claim
The paper confirms that cluster aggregation plays a significant role in reaction dynamics and particle production for light systems (total mass A_tot < 50) at Fermi energies, with the importance of clustering increasing with bombarding energy; however, this confirmation is qualified by the observation that the AMD model with clustering (AMD-CC) reproduces charge distributions better than HIPSE, while HIPSE better reproduces the velocity distributions near the center-of-mass velocity, indicating that no single model fully captures all observables.

## Method
Four reactions (32S+12C and 20Ne+12C at 25 and 50 MeV/nucleon) were measured using the FAZIA detector array, which provides full isotopic identification of forward-emitted reaction products via DeltaE-E and pulse shape analysis techniques. Experimental fragment multiplicities, angular distributions, and energy spectra were compared with two Monte Carlo models: the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model (with and without cluster correlations) and the heavy-ion phase space exploration (HIPSE) model, each combined with afterburner codes (HFl and SIMON, respectively) to describe the decay of excited primary fragments. The AMD model used a Skyrme SLy4 effective force with asy-soft parametrization, while HIPSE employed a coalescence algorithm for cluster formation.

## Key numbers
- Four reactions studied: 32S+12C and 20Ne+12C at 25 and 50 MeV/nucleon
- FAZIA detector covered polar angles from about 2 degrees to 8 degrees, located 80 cm from the target
- Target thickness: 240 micrograms/cm2 of enriched 12C
- Beam currents: about 10^9 particles per second
- Total statistics: around 5x10^7 events for each system
- AMD simulations: about 60,000 primary events per system, with impact parameter from 0 to 9 fm
- HIPSE simulations: 15 million secondary events per system
- For the 25 MeV/nucleon reactions, fusion-like event fractions of approximately 13% (S+C) and 4% (Ne+C) are expected from systematics in a 4pi configuration
- The AMD model used Skyrme SLy4 force with K_sat = 230 MeV, S_0 = 32 MeV, and L_sym = 46 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of cluster production in light systems, which were mainly performed on C+C systems (Tian et al., PhysRevC.95.044613; Tian2, PhysRevC.97.034610; Han, PhysRevC.102.064617), to heavier systems (S+C and Ne+C) at Fermi energies. The paper provides new experimental data for light N=Z systems in the Fermi energy region, where data are sparse compared to the Coulomb barrier energy region (Freer 1997, 2007; Baiocco 054614; Morelli, PhysRevC.99; Camaiani, PhysRevC.97.044607). The results enable further testing of transport models (AMD and HIPSE) for hadrontherapy applications, where accurate reproduction of charged fragment differential yields is fundamental for treatment planning (Dudouet1, Dudouet2, DeNapoli 2012).
