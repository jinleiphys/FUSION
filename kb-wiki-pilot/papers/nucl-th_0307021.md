# How does breakup influence the total fusion of $^{6,7}$Li at the Coulomb barrier?

> Total (complete + incomplete) fusion excitation functions of $^{6,7}$Li on $^{59}$Co and $^{209}$Bi targets around the Coulomb barrier are obtained using a new continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method of calculating fusion. The relative importance of breakup and bound-state structure effects on total fusion is particularly investigated. The effect of breakup on fusion can be observed in the total fusion excitation function. The breakup enhances the total fusion at energies just around the barrier, whereas it hardly affects the total fusion at energies well above the barrier. The difference between the experimental total fusion cross sections for $^{6,7}$Li on $^{59}$Co is notably caused by breakup, but this is not the case for the $^{209}$Bi target.

## Key claim

Breakup enhances total fusion of $^{6,7}$Li at energies just around the Coulomb barrier, but has very little effect (enhancement by approximately 2-3.5%) at energies well above the barrier; the difference between experimental total fusion cross sections for $^{6,7}$Li on $^{59}$Co is notably caused by breakup, but this is not the case for the $^{209}$Bi target.

## Method

Total fusion cross sections were calculated using a three-body continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method with short-range imaginary fusion potentials for each projectile fragment separately (alpha+deuteron for $^{6}$Li, alpha+triton for $^{7}$Li), rather than a single imaginary potential in the center-of-mass coordinate. Full coupled channels calculations were performed with the code FRESCO, including bound-continuum and continuum-continuum couplings, reorientation couplings, and continuum partial waves up to $l=2$ for $^{6}$Li and $l=3$ for $^{7}$Li, with the target treated as a spherical spin-zero nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co: $V_B = 11.74$ MeV; for $^{7}$Li+$^{59}$Co: $V_B = 11.68$ MeV
- Coulomb barrier for $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi: $V_B = 29.71$ MeV; for $^{7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi: $V_B = 29.57$ MeV
- Breakup enhances total fusion by approximately 2% for $^{59}$Co target and approximately 3.5% for $^{209}$Bi target at energies well above the barrier
- Off-diagonal imaginary couplings reduce total fusion cross sections by approximately 13% around the barrier and approximately 6% well above the barrier for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co
- Target excitations increase total fusion by approximately 5% for $^{59}$Co and approximately 3% for $^{209}$Bi around the barrier
- $^{6}$Li ground state binding energy: -1.47 MeV; $^{7}$Li ground state binding energy: -2.47 MeV; $^{7}$Li bound excited state binding energy: -1.99 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CDCC fusion method previously applied to $^{11}$Be+$^{208}$Pb (Diaz-Torres and Thompson, Phys. Rev. C 65, 024606, 2002) by using separate short-range fusion potentials for each fragment, necessary for two-cluster projectiles with charged fragments of similar mass. The results contradict the barrier penetration model (BPM) approach used by Keeley et al. (Phys. Rev. C 65, 014601, 2002; Phys. Rev. C 67, 041604R, 2003), which underestimates total fusion by approximately 40% around the barrier and incorrectly predicts suppression rather than enhancement. The calculations reproduce experimental data for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co (Beck et al., Phys. Rev. C 67, 054602, 2003) and $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi (Dasgupta et al., Phys. Rev. C 66, R041602, 2002), but underestimate $^{7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi data around the barrier, suggesting missing transfer channel couplings.
