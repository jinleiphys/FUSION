# Fusion of light proton-rich exotic nuclei at near-barrier energies

> We study theoretically fusion of the light proton-rich exotic nuclei $^{17}$F and $^8$B at near-barrier energies in order to investigate the possible role of breakup processes on their fusion cross sections. To this end, coupled channel calculations are performed considering the couplings to the breakup channels of these projectiles. In case of $^{17}$F, the coupling arising out of the inelastic excitation from the ground state to the bound excited state and its couplings to the continuum have also been taken into consideration. It is found that the inelastic excitation/breakup of $^{17}$F affect the fusion cross sections very nominally even for a heavy target like Pb. On the other hand, calculations for fusion of the one-proton halo nucleus $^8$B on a Pb target show a significant suppression of the complete fusion cross section above the Coulomb barrier. This is due to the larger breakup probability of $^8$B as compared to that of $^{17}$F. However, even for $^8$B, there is little change in the complete fusion cross sections as compared to the no-coupling case at sub-barrier energies.

## Key claim

Coupled-channel calculations show that breakup of the one-proton halo nucleus $^8$B significantly suppresses complete fusion cross sections above the Coulomb barrier on a Pb target, but the effect is minimal for $^{17}$F; however, even for $^8$B there is little change in complete fusion at sub-barrier energies compared to the no-coupling case.

## Method

Coupled-channel calculations were performed using the incoming wave boundary condition (IWBC) method, with the projectile treated as a core plus single valence proton. The continuum was discretized into 10 bins of 200 keV width up to 2 MeV, including $s$-, $p$-, $d$-, and $f$-waves, with multipolarities 1 and 2 for transitions into the continuum. For $^{17}$F, couplings from the ground state (0$d_{5/2}$) to the bound excited state (1$s_{1/2}$) and from that state to the continuum were included; for $^8$B, transitions into the continuum with $J$ = 1, 2, and 3 final channels were considered, along with the 1$^+$ and 3$^+$ resonances at 0.637 MeV and 2.183 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For $^8$B + Pb at 60 MeV centre-of-mass energy, the complete fusion cross section is approximately 69% of the total fusion cross section.
- The rms proton radius of $^{17}$F is 3.7 fm; the rms proton radius of $^8$B is 4.24 fm.
- The one-proton separation energy in $^{17}$F is 0.6 MeV, approximately 4.5 times that in $^8$B (0.137 MeV).
- The semiclassical electric dipole breakup cross section of $^8$B on Pb at 60 MeV laboratory energy is 5.1 b, close to 4.6 b for $^{11}$Be on the same target at the same energy.
- For $^{17}$F on Pb at near-barrier energy, the electric dipole dissociation cross section is only 46.46 mb.
- At sub-barrier energies, there is approximately 20% increase of the total (complete + incomplete) fusion cross sections for $^8$B + Pb compared to the zero-coupling case.
- Including only dipole transitions for $^8$B + Pb increases the complete fusion cross section by about 4% at 60 MeV compared to the full (dipole + quadrupole) calculation.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Hagino et al. (Phys. Rev. C61, 037602, 2000) for neutron-halo nuclei ($^{11}$Be, $^6$He) to proton-rich systems, and compares with the first fusion measurement for $^{17}$F by Rehm et al. (Phys. Rev. Lett. 81, 3341, 1998). The calculations show that $^8$B behaves similarly to the one-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Be in suppressing complete fusion above the barrier, but with smaller sub-barrier enhancement, while $^{17}$F behaves like an ordinary nucleus. The results suggest that measurement of the different components of fusion cross sections for $^8$B on a heavy target would be valuable.
