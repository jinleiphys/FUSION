# Projectile breakup dynamics for $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co: kinematical analysis of $α$-$d$ coincidences

> A study of the kinematics of the $α$-$d$ coincidences in the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system at a bombarding energy of $E_{lab} = 29.6$ MeV is presented. With exclusive measurements performed over different angular intervals it is possible to identify the respective contributions of the sequential projectile breakup and direct projectile breakup components. A careful analysis using a semiclassical approach of these processes provides information on both their lifetime and their distance of occurrence with respect to the target. Breakup to the low-lying (near-threshold) continuum is delayed, and happens at large internuclear distances. This suggests that the influence of the projectile breakup on the complete fusion process can be related essentially to direct breakup to the $^6$Li high-lying continuum spectrum. %

## Key claim

The influence of projectile breakup on complete fusion in the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system is essentially due to direct breakup to the $^{6}$Li high-lying continuum spectrum, because breakup to the low-lying (near-threshold) continuum is delayed and occurs at large internuclear distances, whereas the shorter lifetimes of high-lying continuum states cause breakup closer to the target.

## Method

Exclusive $\alpha$-$d$ coincidence measurements were performed for the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system at $E_{lab} = 29.6$ MeV using 11 triple telescopes covering angular intervals from $-45^\circ$ to $-15^\circ$ and $15^\circ$ to $75^\circ$. A 3-body kinematics analysis distinguished sequential projectile breakup (SBU) from direct projectile breakup (DBU) and incomplete fusion/transfer (ICF/TR) contributions. A semiclassical approach using Coulomb trajectories was applied to extract the distance of closest approach and lifetime for each breakup component, with barrier tunnelling calculations for DBU lifetimes based on the WKB approximation.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: $E_{lab} = 29.6$ MeV ($E_{cm} = 26.9$ MeV), approximately twice the Coulomb barrier energy ($E_{cm} \approx 12$ MeV)
- Experimental SBU cross section for the first resonant state (2.186 MeV, $3^+$): $20.6 \pm 4.0$ mb (CDCC prediction: 22.1 mb)
- Experimental DBU cross sections: $3.04 \pm 0.41$ mb ($E^* = 1.66$ to 2.10 MeV), $6.8 \pm 4.2$ mb ($E^* = 2.20$ to 2.40 MeV), $4.45 \pm 0.94$ mb ($E^* = 3.10$ to 3.25 MeV)
- SBU lifetime: $\tau_{SBU} = 2.7 \times 10^{-20}$ s, with distance of occurrence $r = 831.0 \pm 1.3$ fm
- DBU lifetimes and distances: $4.9 \times 10^{-21}$ s at $r \approx 143$-$147$ fm ($E^* = 1.7$ MeV); $6.3 \times 10^{-22}$ s at $r = 19.8 \pm 0.8$ fm ($E^* = 2.3$ MeV); $4.4 \times 10^{-22}$ s at $r = 14.3 \pm 1.8$ fm ($E^* = 3.2$ MeV)
- Most probable distances of closest approach $R_{min}^{MP}$ for all processes: $8.1$ to $9.2$ fm

## Context

This work extends previous inclusive measurements for the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system (Souza et al., Nucl. Phys. A 821, 2009) by providing exclusive $\alpha$-$d$ coincidence data that separate SBU and DBU contributions. The results are consistent with CDCC calculations (Beck et al., Phys. Rev. C 75, 2007) and complement studies of breakup dynamics for other systems such as $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi (Santra et al., Phys. Lett. B 677, 2009) and $^{6}$Li + $^{198}$Pt (Shrivastava et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 2009). The finding that DBU to high-lying continuum states is the primary mechanism diverting flux from complete fusion to incomplete fusion supports the classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup (Diaz-Torres et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 98, 2007) and enables further work on ICF processes within that framework.
