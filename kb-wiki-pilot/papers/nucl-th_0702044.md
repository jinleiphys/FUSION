# Neutrino Breakup of A=3 Nuclei in Supernovae

> We extend the virial equation of state to include 3H and 3He nuclei, and predict significant mass-three fractions near the neutrinosphere in supernovae. While alpha particles are often more abundant, we demonstrate that energy transfer cross-sections for muon and tau neutrinos at low densities are dominated by breakup of the loosely-bound 3H and 3He nuclei. The virial coefficients involving A=3 nuclei are calculated directly from the corresponding nucleon-3H and nucleon-3He scattering phase shifts. For the neutral-current inelastic cross-sections and the energy transfer cross sections, we perform ab-initio calculations based on microscopic two- and three-nucleon interactions and meson-exchange currents.

# Neutrino Breakup of A=3 Nuclei in Supernovae

## Key claim

The virial equation of state extended to include 3H and 3He nuclei predicts that mass-three fractions can be significant (up to 10%) near the neutrinosphere, and that for matter temperatures T greater than about 4 MeV, the neutral-current inelastic energy transfer cross-sections for muon and tau neutrinos are dominated by breakup of the loosely-bound 3H and 3He nuclei rather than by excitations of the more abundant alpha particles.

## Method

The authors extended the virial equation of state to include 3H and 3He nuclei, calculating the second virial coefficients involving A=3 nuclei directly from nucleon-3H and nucleon-3He scattering phase shifts. For the neutral-current inclusive inelastic cross-sections and energy transfer cross-sections, they performed ab-initio calculations using the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions with meson-exchange currents, including full final-state interactions via the Lorentz integral transform method solved with the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics approach.

## Key numbers

- Total mass-three fraction (x_3H + x_3He) reaches approximately 0.1 in symmetric matter at density 10^12 g/cm^3
- For temperatures T greater than about 5 MeV, the 3H mass fraction exceeds the alpha particle fraction, especially at low proton fractions
- Neutral-current inclusive inelastic cross-sections on mass-three nuclei are approximately 20 times larger than on 4He at neutrino temperature 4 MeV, and 10 times larger at 6 MeV
- Energy transfer cross-sections on mass-three nuclei are 8 times larger than on 4He at 4 MeV and 2 times larger at 6 MeV
- Meson-exchange current contributions to cross-sections are about 16% at neutrino temperature 1 MeV, decreasing to less than 2% for temperatures above 4 MeV
- Cutoff dependence of the calculated observables is less than 2% at 1 MeV and less than 1% at higher temperatures
- Averaged neutrino-3H cross-section per nucleon at T_nu = 4 MeV: 2.68 x 10^-42 cm^2; energy transfer cross-section: 3.37 x 10^-42 MeV cm^2
- Averaged neutrino-3He cross-section per nucleon at T_nu = 4 MeV: 3.14 x 10^-42 cm^2; energy transfer cross-section: 3.77 x 10^-42 MeV cm^2
- Binding energy of 4He: 28.3 MeV; binding energy of A=3 nuclei: approximately 8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the virial equation of state previously developed for matter composed of neutrons, protons, and alpha particles (Horowitz and Schwenk, Nucl. Phys. A776, 2006) to include 3H and 3He nuclei, providing model-independent predictions for composition near the neutrinosphere. It builds on prior studies of neutrino energy transfer via inelastic excitations of 4He and heavier nuclei (Haxton, 1988; Gazit and Barnea, 2004) and the investigation of neutrino interactions with two interacting nucleons (Hannestad and Raffelt, 1998). The results enable inclusion of A=3 nuclei in supernova simulations to improve the theoretical nuclear microphysics, particularly for energy transfer from muon and tau neutrinos, and suggest that electron capture rates at low temperatures may be dominated by capture on 3H rather than on 4He or free protons.
