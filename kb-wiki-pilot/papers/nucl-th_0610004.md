# Near-barrier Fusion and Breakup/Transfer induced by Weakly Bound and Exotic Halo Nuclei

> The influence on the fusion process of coupling to collective degrees of freedom has been explored. The significant enhancement of the fusion cross section at sub-barrier energies was compared to predictions of one-dimensional barrier penetration models. This was understood in terms of the dynamical processes arising from strong couplings to collective inelastic excitations of the target and projectile. However, in the case of reactions where at least one of the colliding nuclei has a sufficiently low binding energy, for breakup to become an important process, conflicting model predictions and experimental results have been reported in the literature. Excitation functions for sub- and near-barrier total (complete + incomplete) fusion cross sections have been measured for the $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co reactions. Elastic scattering as well as breakup/transfer yields have also been measured at several incident energies. Results of Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel ({\sc Cdcc}) calculations describe reasonably well the experimental data for both reactions at and above the barrier. A systematic study of $^{4,6}$He induced fusion reactions with a three-body {\sc Cdcc} method is presented. The relative importance of breakup and bound-state structure effects on total fusion (excitation functions) is particularly investigated. The four-body {\sc Cdcc} model is being currently developed.

## Key claim

Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) calculations predict a considerable enhancement of sub-barrier total fusion cross sections for the halo nucleus $^{6}$He on a medium-mass $^{59}$Co target compared to both $^{6}$Li and $^{4}$He induced fusion, but this prediction is based on a di-neutron model approximation whose dipole coupling strength may need reduction for heavy targets and whose results have not yet been tested against experimental data for this specific system.

## Method

Excitation functions for total (complete + incomplete) fusion, elastic scattering angular distributions, and breakup/transfer yields were measured for the $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co reactions at several incident energies near and below the Coulomb barrier. Three-body CDCC calculations, treating $^{6}$Li and $^{7}$Li as two-body clusters ($\alpha$+$d$ and $\alpha$+$t$ respectively), were performed using the FRESCO code to simultaneously describe elastic scattering, total fusion, and breakup. For $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co, a three-body CDCC calculation using a di-neutron ($^{4}$He+$^{2}$n) cluster model was applied, including couplings to resonant (2$^{+}$, E$_{ex}$ = 0.826 MeV) and non-resonant continuum states up to f-waves with a maximum continuum energy cutoff of 8 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Total calculated breakup cross sections for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, obtained by integrating continuum contributions up to 8 MeV, are small (10-50 mb) compared to total fusion cross sections.
* The ground state binding potential of the $\alpha$ particle and di-neutron in the $^{6}$He model provides a 2s bound state at about -0.975 MeV.
* The 2$^{+}$ resonant state of $^{6}$He is at E$_{ex}$ = 0.826 MeV with a width of 0.075 MeV.
* The Broglia-Winther Woods-Saxon potential parameters used for fragment-target interactions are: V$_{o}$ = -16.89 MeV, r$_{o}$ = 1.09 fm, a = 0.63 fm.
* The extracted total reaction cross sections confirm a small enhancement (approximately a factor of 2) of total fusion cross section for the more weakly bound $^{6}$Li nucleus at sub-barrier energies compared to $^{7}$Li.

## Context

This work extends previous CDCC studies of weakly bound and halo nuclei, addressing conflicting results in the literature where some calculations (e.g., Rusek et al. for $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi) failed to reproduce experimental $\alpha$-particle yields, while other experiments (Raabe et al., Kolata et al., Di Pietro et al., Navin et al.) indicated that the $^{6}$He halo does not enhance fusion probability, suggesting instead the importance of one- and two-neutron transfer processes. The paper enables future comparisons with upcoming experimental data from SPIRAL beams at GANIL on medium-mass targets, and motivates the development of four-body CDCC models (Moro et al., Matsumoto et al.) needed for proper treatment of three-body projectiles like $^{6}$He ($\alpha$+n+n) and for distinguishing complete fusion from incomplete fusion.
