# Solar neutrino physics

> As a free, intensive, weakly interacting, and well directional messenger, solar neutrinos have been driving both solar physics and neutrino physics developments for more than half a century. Since more extensive and advanced neutrino experiments are under construction, being planned or proposed, we are striving toward an era of precise and comprehensive measurement of solar neutrinos in the next decades. In this article, we review recent theoretical and experimental progress achieved in solar neutrino physics. We present not only an introduction to neutrinos from the standard solar model and the standard flavor evolution, but also a compilation of a variety of new physics that could affect and hence be probed by solar neutrinos. After reviewing the latest techniques and issues involved in the measurement of solar neutrino spectra and background reduction, we provide our anticipation on the physics gains from the new generation of neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current state of solar neutrino physics, arguing that while the standard MSW-LMA solution and standard solar models provide a coherent framework, the "solar metallicity problem" remains unresolved, with competing high-Z and low-Z models predicting significantly different neutrino fluxes for certain components, and precision measurements of solar neutrino fluxes will be crucial for resolving this tension.

## Method

The authors compile and compare theoretical predictions from two standard solar model calculations (Bahcall-Serenelli-Basu 2005 and Barcelona 2016) using two different solar chemical composition datasets (GS98 with high metallicity and AGSS09 with low metallicity), alongside experimental measurements from Borexino, SNO, and Super-Kamiokande. They review the MSW-LMA solution for neutrino flavor evolution through matter, including the adiabatic approximation and the resulting survival probability formula, and discuss the complementarity between solar neutrino observations and reactor neutrino experiments (KamLAND, Daya-Bay, RENO, Double-Chooz) for determining oscillation parameters.

## Key numbers

- pp chain powers about 99% of total solar energy; CNO cycle accounts for remaining ~1%
- hep neutrinos have the highest energy endpoint at 18.77 MeV; 8B neutrinos have endpoint at 15.04 MeV (with a forbidden decay extending to 16.95 MeV)
- Solar atmospheric neutrino flux at 300 GeV: dΦ/dEν ~ 3 × 10^-13 cm^-2 s^-1 GeV^-1, following power law dΦ/dEν ∝ Eν^-3 within 10 < Eν/GeV < 10^5
- Electron capture neutrino fluxes suppressed by ~10^-4 relative to beta+ decay fluxes: Φ_e13N = 7.9×10^-4 Φ_13N, Φ_e15O = 3.9×10^-4 Φ_15O, Φ_e17F = 5.8×10^-4 Φ_17F
- Latest NuFit 5.2 (November 2022) oscillation parameters: sin^2θ12 = 0.303 ± 0.012, Δm^2_21 = 7.41 (+0.21/-0.20) × 10^-5 eV^2, sin^2θ13 = 2.225 (+0.056/-0.059) × 10^-2
- pp neutrino flux: Φ_pp = 5.98 × 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-GS98) to 6.03 × 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-AGSS09)
- 8B neutrino flux: Φ_8B = 5.46 × 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-GS98) versus 4.50 × 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-AGSS09), a ~21% difference
- 13N neutrino flux: Φ_13N = 2.78 × 10^8 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-GS98) versus 2.04 × 10^8 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-AGSS09)
- 15O neutrino flux: Φ_15O = 2.05 × 10^8 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-GS98) versus 1.44 × 10^8 cm^-2 s^-1 (B16-AGSS09)
- Survival probability Pee transitions from ~0.55 (low-energy vacuum limit) to ~0.3 (high-energy matter-dominated limit), with significant energy dependence in the range 2 MeV ≲ Eν ≲ 6 MeV (the "up-turn")
- A ~2σ tension between KamLAND and Super-Kamiokande measurements of Δm^2_21 (7.53 vs 4.8 × 10^-5 eV^2 in 2014) has reduced to ~1σ with the 2022 Super-Kamiokande update (best-fit ~6 × 10^-5 eV^2)

## Context

This review extends the long line of solar neutrino physics dating to the original SSM work of Bahcall (1989) and the MSW effect, and updates the status following the B16 solar model calculations by Vinyoles et al. (2016). It addresses the ongoing "solar metallicity problem" where high-Z models (GS98, Grevesse and Sauval 1998) favored by helioseismology conflict with low-Z models (AGS05/AGSS09, Asplund et al. 2004/2009) that incorporate improved 3D simulations but disagree with helioseismic constraints. The paper enables future work by identifying that upcoming experiments (JUNO, next-generation solar neutrino detectors) can resolve the metallicity problem through precision measurements of CNO and 8B neutrino fluxes, and can probe new physics through precise measurement of the up-turn region of the survival probability.
