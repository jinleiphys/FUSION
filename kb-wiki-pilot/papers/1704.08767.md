# Inclusive Breakup Theory of Three-Body Halos

> We present a recently developed theory for the inclusive breakup of three-fragment projectiles within a four-body spectator model \cite{CarPLB2017}, for the treatment of the elastic and inclusive non-elastic break up reactions involving weakly bound three-cluster nuclei in $A\,(a,b)\,X$ / $a = x_1 + x_2 + b$ collisions. The four-body theory is an extension of the three-body approaches developed in the 80's by Ichimura, Autern and Vincent (IAV) \cite{IAV1985}, Udagawa and Tamura (UT) \cite{UT1981} and Hussein and McVoy (HM) \cite{HM1985}. We expect that experimentalists shall be encouraged to search for more information about the $x_{1} + x_{2}$ system in the elastic breakup cross section and that also further developments and extensions of the surrogate method will be pursued, based on the inclusive non-elastic breakup part of the $b$ spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper presents a four-body extension of the three-body inclusive breakup theories (IAV, UT, HM) developed in the 1980s, specifically for reactions where the projectile breaks into three fragments ($a = x_1 + x_2 + b$). The authors expect that experimentalists will be encouraged to search for more information about the $x_1 + x_2$ system in the elastic breakup cross section, and that further developments of the surrogate method will be pursued based on the inclusive non-elastic breakup part of the $b$ spectrum.

## Method

The authors derive a four-body spectator model for inclusive breakup of three-fragment projectiles in $A(a,b)X$ collisions, starting from the many-body Hamiltonian for the $b + x_1 + x_2 + A$ system. The inclusive breakup cross section is separated into an elastic breakup term and an inclusive non-elastic breakup term, where the latter contains contributions from single-fragment absorption ($W_{x_1}$, $W_{x_2}$) and a three-body absorption term ($W_{3B}$) arising from a three-body optical potential. The source function is expressed in terms of the four-body scattering wave function, and the formalism is connected to the Hussein-McVoy (HM), Udagawa-Tamura (UT), and Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) approaches through the CFH generalization.

## Key numbers

- The density of $b$ continuum states is given by $\rho_b(E_b) = \mu_b k_b / [(2\pi)^3 \hbar^3]$, where $\mu_b$ is the reduced mass of the $b + A$ system
- The inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section is expressed as a sum of three terms: $\frac{d^2\sigma^{INEB}_b}{dE_b d\Omega_b} = \rho_b(E_b) \frac{k_a}{E_a} \left[ \frac{E_{x_1}}{k_{x_1}} \sigma_R^{x_1} + \frac{E_{x_2}}{k_{x_2}} \sigma_R^{x_2} + \frac{E_{CM}(x_1,x_2)}{(k_{x_1}+k_{x_2})} \sigma_R^{3B} \right]$
- The single fragment inclusive cross sections are $\sigma_R^{x_i} = \frac{k_{x_i}}{E_{x_i}} \langle \hat{\rho}_{x_1,x_2} | W_{x_i} | \hat{\rho}_{x_1,x_2} \rangle$ for $i=1,2$
- The double fragment inclusive cross section is $\sigma_R^{3B} = \frac{(k_{x_1}+k_{x_2})}{E_{CM}(x_1,x_2)} \langle \hat{\rho}_{x_1,x_2} | W_{3B} | \hat{\rho}_{x_1,x_2} \rangle$

## Context

This work extends the three-body inclusive breakup theories developed in the 1980s by Ichimura, Autern and Vincent (IAV), Udagawa and Tamura (UT), and Hussein and McVoy (HM) to four-body systems with three-fragment projectiles. The theory is applied to Borromean nuclei, two-nucleon halo nuclei, and unstable three-fragment halo nuclei, where fingerprints of Efimov physics and universality could be revealed through long-range correlations between fragments. The formalism enables extensions of the surrogate method (used for (d,p) and (d,n) reactions) to reactions like (t,d) and ($^3$He,d), and provides a framework for the Trojan Horse Method with three-fragment projectiles.
