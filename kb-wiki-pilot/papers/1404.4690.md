# Breakup and finite-range effects on the 8B(d,n)9C reaction

> The astrophysical factor of $^8$B($p$,$γ$)$^9$C at zero energy, $S_{18}(0)$, is determined by a three-body coupled-channels analysis of the transfer reaction $^{8}$B($d$,$n$)$^{9}$C at 14.4 MeV/nucleon. Effects of the breakup channels of $d$ and $^9$C are investigated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method. It is found that, in the initial and final channels, respectively, the transfer process through the breakup states of $d$ and $^9$C, its interference with that through their ground states in particular, gives a large increase in the transfer cross section. The finite-range effects with respect to the proton-neutron relative coordinate are found to be about 20%. As a result of the present analysis, $S_{18}(0)=22 \pm 6~{\rm eV~b}$ is obtained, which is smaller than the result of the previous distorted-wave Born approximation analysis by about 51%.

## Key claim

The astrophysical factor \(S_{18}(0)\) for \(^8\)B(\(p\),\(\gamma\))\(^9\)C is determined to be \(22 \pm 6\) eV b from a three-body coupled-channels analysis of the transfer reaction \(^8\)B(\(d\),\(n\))\(^9\)C at 14.4 MeV/nucleon, which is about 51% smaller than the previous distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) result, but this value depends on including breakup channels of both the deuteron and \(^9\)C and their interference with the elastic transfer channel.

## Method

The transfer reaction \(^8\)B(\(d\),\(n\))\(^9\)C at 14.4 MeV/nucleon was analyzed using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) within a three-body (\(p+n+^8\)B) model. The transition matrix in post form was calculated with the coupled-channels Born approximation (CCBA), including breakup channels of both the projectile deuteron and the residual nucleus \(^9\)C, and a finite-range correction to the zero-range approximation was applied. The cross section was normalized to experimental data from Beaumel et al. to extract the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) and then the astrophysical factor \(S_{18}(0)\).

## Key numbers

- \(S_{18}(0) = 22 \pm 6\) eV b for the \(^8\)B(\(p\),\(\gamma\))\(^9\)C reaction at zero energy
- \((C_{p {}^8\text{B}}^{{}^9\text{C}})^2 = 0.59 \pm 0.02\) (theor.) \(\pm 0.13\) (exp.) fm\(^{-1}\) for the ANC of the \(p\)-\(^8\)B(g.s.) overlap in \(^9\)C
- Inclusion of breakup channels of both \(d\) and \(^9\)C gives a large increase of about 58% in the transfer cross section at \(0^\circ\)
- Finite-range effects with respect to the proton-neutron relative coordinate are about 20%
- The result is about 51% smaller than the previous DWBA analysis value of \(45 \pm 13\) eV b from Beaumel et al.

## Context

This work extends the three-body coupled-channels analysis previously applied to \(^9\)C breakup reactions (Ref. breakupS18, which gave \(S_{18}(0)=66 \pm 10\) eV b) to the transfer reaction \(^8\)B(\(d\),\(n\))\(^9\)C, resolving a discrepancy with the earlier DWBA analysis by Beaumel et al. The study follows the approach of Ref. transferS17 for the \(^7\)Be(\(d\),\(n\))\(^8\)B reaction, where deuteron breakup effects were found essential, but here both projectile and residual nucleus breakup channels are included. The results enable a more reliable determination of \(S_{18}(0)\) for the hot \(pp\) chain in low-metallicity supermassive stars.
