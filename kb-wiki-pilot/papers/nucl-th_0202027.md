# Fusion of light exotic nuclei at near-barrier energies : effect of inelastic excitation

> The effect of inelastic excitation of exotic light projectiles (proton- as well as neutron-rich) $^{17}$F and $^{11}$Be on fusion with heavy target has been studied at near-barrier energies. The calculations have been performed in the coupled channels approach where, in addition to the normal coupling of the ground state of the projectile to the continuum, inelastic excitation of the projectile to the bound excited state and its coupling to the continuum have also been taken into consideration. The inclusion of these additional couplings has been found to have significant effect on the fusion excitation function of neutron-rich $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb whereas the effect has been observed to be nominal for the case of proton-rich $^{17}$F on the same target. The pronounced effect of the channel coupling on the fusion process in case of $^{11}$Be is attributed to its well-developed halo structure.

## Key claim

Including the bound first excited state of the projectile and its coupling to the continuum in coupled-channel calculations has a significant effect on the fusion excitation function of the neutron-rich halo nucleus \(^{11}\)Be on \(^{208}\)Pb, but only a nominal effect for the proton-rich \(^{17}\)F on the same target; the pronounced effect for \(^{11}\)Be is attributed to its well-developed halo structure, while \(^{17}\)F is not a one-proton halo nucleus despite being proton-rich.

## Method

Coupled-channel calculations were performed for \(^{17}\)F + Pb and \(^{11}\)Be + Pb at near-barrier energies, including couplings from the projectile ground state to the continuum and, in a second set, also including the bound first excited state and its coupling to the continuum. The continuum was discretized into 10 bins of 200 keV (up to 2 MeV for \(^{17}\)F, up to 8 MeV for \(^{11}\)Be), with multipole transitions of order 1 and 2 for \(^{17}\)F and dipole transitions only for \(^{11}\)Be, including \(s\)-, \(p\)-, and \(d\)-waves in the continuum. The target was assumed inert, and both Coulomb and nuclear couplings were included; continuum-continuum couplings were also considered.

## Key numbers

*   \(^{11}\)Be rms matter radius: 2.91 fm, about 20% larger than the systematic value of 2.45 fm from \(r_0 A^{1/3}\) with \(r_0 = 1.1\) fm.
*   Coulomb barrier lowering for \(^{11}\)Be + Pb due to the large rms radius: about 1.6 MeV.
*   \(^{17}\)F rms radius: 3.04 fm (normal size).
*   One-nucleon separation energies: 0.504 MeV for \(^{11}\)Be, 0.6 MeV for \(^{17}\)F.
*   First excited state energy: 495 keV above ground state for \(^{17}\)F; 320 keV above ground state for \(^{11}\)Be.
*   \(B(E1)\) value for the ground-state to first-excited-state transition in \(^{11}\)Be: 0.36 \(\pm\) 0.03 W.u.
*   Inclusion of the first excited state of \(^{11}\)Be reduces the complete fusion cross section at above-barrier energies by about 21% compared to the case with only ground-state-to-continuum couplings.
*   Sub-barrier complete and total fusion cross sections for \(^{11}\)Be become larger by almost a factor of two at the lowest energies when the first excited state is included.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel formalism of Hagino et al. (Phys. Rev. C61, 037602, 2000) for neutron-halo nuclei by including continuum-continuum couplings, a realistic rms radius for \(^{11}\)Be, and the bound first excited state. It contrasts the behavior of the neutron-halo \(^{11}\)Be with the proton-rich \(^{17}\)F, for which no fusion enhancement was observed in measurements by Rehm et al. (Phys. Rev. Lett. 81, 3341, 1998). The results enable future calculations for more exotic proton-rich candidates such as \(^{8}\)B, which has a one-proton separation energy of only 0.137 MeV and may exhibit halo-like effects.
