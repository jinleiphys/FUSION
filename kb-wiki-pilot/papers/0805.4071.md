# Nuclear enhancement and suppression of diffractive structure functions at high energies

> We compute diffractive structure functions for both protons and nuclei in the framework of Color Glass Condensate models with impact parameter dependence. These models have previously been shown to provide good agreement with inclusive F_2 measurements and exclusive vector meson measurements at HERA. For nuclei, they provide good (parameter free) agreement with the inclusive F_2 data. We demonstrate good agreement of our computations with HERA measurements on inclusive diffraction. We extend our analysis to nuclei and predict the pattern of enhancement and suppression of the diffractive structures functions that can be measured at an Electron Ion Collider. We discuss how the impact parameter dependence crucially affects our analysis, in particular for large invariant masses at fixed Q^2.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that impact-parameter-dependent Color Glass Condensate (CGC) models, specifically the IPsat and bCGC models, can describe HERA diffractive structure function data with good agreement, and then predict that nuclear diffractive structure functions will show a pattern of enhancement at small invariant masses and suppression at large invariant masses relative to the proton scaled by atomic number, with the impact parameter dependence being crucial for this behavior, particularly at large invariant masses and fixed Q².

## Method

The authors compute diffractive structure functions for protons and nuclei using the dipole model framework, where the virtual photon fluctuates into q\bar{q} and q\bar{q}g Fock states that interact elastically with the target. For the proton, they use two CGC-based dipole cross-section parametrizations (IPsat and bCGC) with explicit impact parameter dependence, fitting parameters to HERA inclusive F₂ data. For nuclei, they generalize the dipole cross section using a Glauber-like averaging over nucleon positions distributed according to the Woods-Saxon distribution, with no additional free parameters. The diffractive structure functions are computed from the q\bar{q} component (dominant at large β) and the q\bar{q}g component (dominant at small β), using an interpolation formula between the large-Q² and β→0 limits.

## Key numbers

- χ² = 1.3 for the IPsat model fit to combined HERA diffractive data (343 data points) with α_s = 0.14
- χ² = 1.3 for the bCGC model fit to combined HERA diffractive data with α_s = 0.22
- For the IPsat model fit to only LPS data: χ² = 0.5 with α_s = 0.21
- For the bCGC model fit to only FPS data: χ² = 0.8
- The saturation scale Q_s is defined as the solution of dσ(r² = 1/Q_s², x, b) = 2(1 - e^{-1/4})
- The bCGC model uses parameters γ_s = 0.628 and κ = 9.9 from leading logarithmic BFKL dynamics
- The proton impact parameter profile in IPsat uses T_p(b) = (1/2πB_G)exp(-b²/2B_G) normalized to unity

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of nuclear inclusive structure functions and elastic q\bar{q} scattering (Kowalski et al., 2007) by including the q\bar{q}g Fock state contribution to diffraction, which was incomplete in that earlier study. It builds on the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff dipole model and its DGLAP-improved version (IPsat), as well as the bCGC model incorporating small-x evolution. The results enable predictions for diffractive structure functions at a future Electron Ion Collider, where the predicted pattern of nuclear enhancement and suppression can be tested. The authors compare their approach with earlier work on nuclear diffraction by Nikolaev, Frankfurt, Gotsman, Levin, Kopeliovich, and others, noting that their impact-parameter-dependent treatment differs from factorized b-dependence assumptions used in some previous calculations.
