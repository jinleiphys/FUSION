# Transfer to the continuum and Breakup reactions

> Reaction theory is an essential ingredient when performing studies of nuclei far from stability. One approach for the calculation of breakup reactions of exotic nuclei into two fragments is to consider inelastic excitations into the single particle continuum of the projectile. Alternatively one can also consider the transfer to the continuum of a system composed of the light fragment and the target. In this work we make a comparative study of the two approaches, underline the different inputs, and identify the advantages and disadvantages of each approach. Our test cases consist of the breakup of $^{11}$Be on a proton target at intermediate energies, and the breakup of $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni at energies around the Coulomb barrier. We find that, in practice the results obtained in both schemes are in semiquantitative agreement. We suggest a simple condition that can select between the two approaches.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that breakup reactions of exotic nuclei can be described either as inelastic excitations into the projectile continuum (CDCC) or as transfer to the continuum of the light fragment and target, and finds that these two approaches produce results in semiquantitative agreement, but only when the same physical ingredients are used and within the limitations of practical calculations.

## Method

The authors performed a comparative study using the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method, which treats breakup as projectile excitation to the continuum, and a prior-form transfer-to-continuum (TR*) approach, which expands the final three-body wavefunction in terms of fragment-target continuum states. Both methods were applied to the same model Hamiltonian for two test cases: the breakup of 11Be on a proton target at 38.5 MeV/u, and the breakup of 8B on 58Ni at 25.6 MeV. The calculations used the same fragment-target interactions and binding potentials where possible, and were performed with the computer code FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be + p at 38.5 MeV/u: total integrated cross section for BU is 125 mb and for TR* is 140 mb
- For 11Be + p: most BU strength is below excitation energy ε_x(11Be) ≈ 5 MeV, while TR* strength is concentrated around ε_x(d) ≈ 20 MeV
- For 11Be + p: both angular momentum distributions peak around J = 5, corresponding to impact parameter b ≈ 4 fm
- For 11Be + p: the 10Be energy distribution peaks around 3 MeV in both methods, ranging from zero to about 6 MeV
- For 8B + 58Ni at 25.6 MeV: BU calculation required partial waves up to l_max = 4 and energies up to ε_max = 8 MeV for the p-7Be system
- For 8B + 58Ni: TR* calculation required partial waves up to l_max = 17 and energies up to ε_max = 10 MeV for the p+58Ni system

## Context

This work extends the comparison of reaction models for breakup of exotic nuclei, following earlier studies comparing eikonal, adiabatic, and local momentum approximations (Bon01, Shy01, Zad04). The CDCC method (Yah86, Aus87) and the semiclassical transfer-to-continuum approach (Bon88, Bon91a, Bon91b, Bon92, Bon01, Gar05) are two widely used but previously uncompared frameworks. The results enable a better understanding of when each approach is appropriate and suggest a simple condition for selecting between them, which is important for extracting nuclear structure information from radioactive beam experiments.
