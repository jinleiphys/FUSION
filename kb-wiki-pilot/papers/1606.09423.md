# Probing transfer to unbound states of the ejectile with weakly bound 7Li on 93Nb

> The two-step process of transfer followed by breakup is explored by measuring a rather complete set of exclusive data for reaction channels populating states in the ejectile continua of the $^7$Li+$^{93}$Nb system at energies close to the Coulomb barrier. The cross sections for $α+α$ events from one proton pickup were found to be smaller than those for $α+d$ events from one neutron stripping and $α+t$ events from direct breakup of $^7$Li. Coupled channels Born approximation and continuum discretized coupled channels calculations describe the data well and support the conclusion that the $α+d$ and $α+α$ events are produced by direct transfer to unbound states of the ejectile.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for the 7Li+93Nb system at near-barrier energies, the alpha+d and alpha+alpha coincidence events are produced by direct one-neutron stripping to the unbound 6Li(3_1+) state and direct one-proton pickup to the unbound 8Be(0_1+) state, respectively, rather than by sequential processes of transfer followed by excitation of the ejectile.

## Method

Exclusive measurements of alpha+d, alpha+alpha, and alpha+t coincidence events were performed using segmented large-area Si-telescope detectors at beam energies of 24, 28, and 30 MeV for the 7Li+93Nb system. The experimental data were analyzed using Monte Carlo simulations to determine detection efficiencies, and theoretical calculations were performed using the coupled channels Born approximation (CCBA) for transfer-breakup channels and continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) for direct breakup of 7Li.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for 1n-stripping to 6Li(3_1+) at 28 MeV: 5.8 +/- 0.4 mb (experimental), 6.2 mb (CCBA calculation)
- Cross section for 1p-pickup to 8Be(g.s.) at 28 MeV (E*(92Zr) <= 3 MeV): 0.7 +/- 0.1 mb (experimental), 0.56 mb (CCBA calculation)
- Cross section for 7Li*(7/2-) -> alpha+t at 28 MeV: 3.3 +/- 0.6 mb (experimental), 2.9 mb (CDCC calculation)
- Inclusive alpha production cross section at 28 MeV: 321 +/- 48 mb
- Total reaction cross section at 28 MeV: 1310 mb (calculated)
- Relative energy peaks in ejectile breakup: 92 keV for 8Be(g.s.), 710 keV for 6Li(3_1+), 2.16 MeV for 7Li(7/2-)
- 1n-stripping accounts for approximately 2% and 1p-pickup approximately 0.8% of the inclusive alpha yield at 28 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transfer-breakup mechanisms for weakly bound nuclei, particularly the 7Li+65Cu system studied by Shrivastava et al. (2006), and addresses the importance of 1p-pickup over direct breakup highlighted by Luong et al. (2011) and Simpson et al. (2016) in explaining fusion suppression. The comprehensive data set provides a benchmark for understanding reaction mechanisms relevant to radioactive ion beam facilities, as the combined CCBA and CDCC calculations confirm that only about 8% of the inclusive alpha yield comes from these measured breakup processes, with the remainder attributed to fusion-evaporation and t-stripping/capture.
