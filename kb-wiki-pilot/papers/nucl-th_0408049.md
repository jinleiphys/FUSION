# Interference effects in the Coulomb dissociation of 15,17,19C

> In this work the semiclassical model of pure Coulomb excitation was applied to the breakup of 15,17,19C. The ground state wave functions were calculated in the particle-rotor model including core excitation. The importance of interference terms in the dipole strength arising after including core degrees of freedom is analyzed for each isotope. It is shown that Coulomb interference effects are important for the case of 17C.

# Interference effects in the Coulomb dissociation of 15,17,19C

## Key claim

Coulomb interference effects arising from core excitation are important for the dipole strength in 17C, where they contribute destructively and account for about 30% of the total squared terms, but are practically insignificant for 15C and 19C, validating the simple cluster picture for those isotopes.

## Method

The authors applied a semiclassical model of pure Coulomb excitation using virtual photon numbers formalism to calculate breakup cross sections for 15,17,19C on a 208Pb target. Ground state wave functions were obtained from a particle-rotor model including core excitation, with continuum states treated as plane waves, and the coupled channel equations were solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh.

## Key numbers

- 19C ground state composition: 71.5% s1/2 ⊗ 0+, 25.3% d5/2 ⊗ 2+, 3.2% d3/2 ⊗ 2+ (with Sn = 0.65 MeV, ε2+ = 1.2 MeV)
- 17C ground state composition: 14.5% d3/2 ⊗ 0+, 76.5% s1/2 ⊗ 2+, 9.0% d3/2 ⊗ 2+ (with Sn = 0.73 MeV, ε2+ = 1.776 MeV)
- 15C ground state composition: 87.2% s1/2 ⊗ 0+, 10.9% d5/2 ⊗ 2+, 1.9% d3/2 ⊗ 2+ (with Sn = 1.22 MeV, ε2+ = 7.1 MeV)
- For 19C, the six non-dominant contributions to the 1/2+ → 3/2- transition add up to only 6% of the total
- For 17C, the total interference contribution amounts to about 30% of the total squared terms, acting destructively
- 19C one-neutron separation energy: adopted value 0.58 MeV (from Audi et al. 2003); weighted average from time-of-flight experiments 242 ± 95 keV; Nakamura analysis gives 0.53 ± 0.13 MeV; Maddalena yields 0.65 ± 0.15 MeV and 0.8 ± 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the particle-rotor model calculations of Ridikas et al. (Nucl. Phys. A628, 363, 1998) for carbon isotopes, providing detailed analysis of interference terms in the dipole strength function. It confirms the earlier finding of Shyam and Danielewicz (Phys. Rev. C 63, 054608, 2001) that transitions to core excited states are weak in 19C, while demonstrating that for 17C the core-excited configuration dominates and interference effects are crucial. The results enable improved interpretation of Coulomb breakup data from Nakamura et al. (1999) and Datta Pramanik et al. (Phys. Lett. B 551, 63, 2003), and suggest that a two-cluster model may better describe the higher-energy structure seen in 19C data.
