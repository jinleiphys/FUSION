# Fusion of light nuclei in a multicluster realization of the three-body problem

> This work describes a few-body dynamics method based on the Faddeev integral equations in momentum space for determining the total cross sections of fusion and breakup reactions with two- and three-body final channels in the continuum, employing a cluster representation of the colliding nuclei. Total cross sections were obtained for the reactions $^3\text{He}(T,D)^4\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(T,np)^4\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(T,nD)^3\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(^3\text{He},2p)^4\text{He}$, $^3\text{He}(^3\text{He},pD)^3\text{He}$, $^7\text{Li}(^3\text{He},\phantom{0}^4\text{He})^6\text{Li}$, $^7\text{Li}(^3\text{He},D^4\text{He})^4\text{He}$, and $^7\text{Li}(^3\text{He},T^3\text{He})^4\text{He}$, in which both the projectile and the target nucleus were treated in a cluster representation. The work also implements a two-potential method to determine the Coulomb $t-$matrix and to account for Coulomb effects in short-range dynamics in momentum space. Calculations of the initial-state Coulomb interaction were performed; furthermore, an estimate was obtained for the magnitude of the off-shell effect of the Coulomb $t-$matrix, as well as the magnitude of the atomic electron anti-screening effect on the Coulomb interaction of the colliding nuclei. The calculated contribution of the cluster mechanism to the total cross section of the considered fusion reactions in the kinetic energy range $T\in[1~\text{keV},20~\text{MeV}]$ is in good agreement with known experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a multicluster realization of the Faddeev integral equations in momentum space can provide a unified description of elastic scattering, rearrangement, and three-body breakup for light-nuclei fusion reactions, with the calculated total cross sections showing good agreement with known experimental data over the kinetic energy range 1 keV to 20 MeV, but only when the cluster representation is applied to both projectile and target nuclei and when Coulomb effects are treated via a two-potential method.

## Method

The author solved a system of coupled Faddeev integral equations in three-dimensional vector form for three-body scattering, using cluster representations where the triton is treated as a bound nD system and the 3He nucleus as a bound pD system. Two-body scattering t-matrices were constructed from local potentials for fusion and elastic channels and from separable potentials fitted to known partial-wave phase shifts for n-3He, n-4He, T-4He, and 4He-4He interactions; the elastic nD scattering amplitudes were precomputed using the Bonn NN potential and stored as numerical tables. The Coulomb interaction was incorporated via a two-potential method to determine the Coulomb t-matrix in momentum space, and the system of equations was solved iteratively up to second order to obtain breakup and rearrangement amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections were calculated for eight reactions: 3He(T,D)4He, 3He(T,np)4He, 3He(T,nD)3He, 3He(3He,2p)4He, 3He(3He,pD)3He, 7Li(3He,4He)6Li, 7Li(3He,D4He)4He, and 7Li(3He,T3He)4He
- The kinetic energy range studied was T in [1 keV, 20 MeV]
- The three-body scattering energy E is related to the laboratory kinetic energy T by E = T * M_t/(M_t + m_1) - |E_b|, where E_b is the binding energy of the initial subsystem
- The invariant mass of the 2'3' subsystem varies in the range from m_2' + m_3' + Q to m_1 + M_t + T * M_t/(M_t + m_1) - m_1'
- A [2/2] Pade approximant was sufficient to describe elastic nD scattering
- The direct solution of the iterative recurrence relations was performed up to n = 2 inclusive

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigations of cluster structure in light nuclei using Faddeev integral equations for neutron- and deuteron-induced reactions on lithium nuclei, 12C, and 16O, and continues the approach from EgorovFBSY. It addresses the long-standing challenge of treating Coulomb repulsion in few-body dynamics at energies below 1 MeV, where the CDCC method faces difficulties with boundary conditions for rearrangement and breakup into three or more fragments, and where DWBA fails to capture specific features of reaction dynamics. The method enables a unified description of elastic scattering, rearrangement, and three-body breakup channels without the need for selecting asymptotic boundary conditions in coordinate space, and it allows for the inclusion of atomic electron anti-screening effects on the Coulomb interaction.
