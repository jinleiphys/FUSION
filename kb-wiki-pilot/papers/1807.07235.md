# Radiative pion capture in 2H, 3He and 3H

> The $π^- + {^2{\rm H}} \rightarrow γ+ n + n$, $π^- + {^3{\rm He}} \rightarrow γ+ {^3{\rm H}}$, $π^- + {^3{\rm He}} \rightarrow γ+ n + d$, $π^- + {^3{\rm He}} \rightarrow γ+ n + n + p$ and $π^- + {^3{\rm H}} \rightarrow γ+ n + n + n$ capture reactions are studied with the AV18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana~IX three-nucleon potential. We provide for the first time realistic predictions for the differential and total capture rates for all these processes, treating consistently the initial and final nuclear states. Our results are based on the single nucleon Kroll-Ruderman-type transition operator and concentrate on the full treatment of the nuclear final state interactions. They are compared with older theoretical predictions and experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper provides the first realistic predictions for differential and total capture rates of radiative pion capture on 2H, 3He, and 3H using consistent treatment of initial and final nuclear states from realistic two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, but only with a single-nucleon Kroll-Ruderman-type transition operator, leaving two-nucleon contributions and chiral corrections for future work.

## Method
The authors solved the nonrelativistic nuclear dynamics using the AV18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential in momentum space with partial wave decomposition, including all partial wave states with total angular momentum j ≤ 4. They computed nuclear matrix elements of the transverse components of the single-nucleon axial current operator, treating final state interactions fully for two-nucleon and three-nucleon scattering states. The capture rates were obtained by integrating over phase space with 60 photon energy points and 50 angular points for the deuteron case, using analogous formulas from their previous muon capture work with the pion mass replacing the muon mass.

## Key numbers
- For π⁻ + ²H → γ + n + n: total capture rate Γ_nn = 0.318 × 10¹⁵ 1/s (plane wave) and Γ_nn = 0.328 × 10¹⁵ 1/s (full dynamics with final state interactions)
- Maximum photon energy for the deuteron reaction: 131.459 MeV (relativistic) and 131.454 MeV (nonrelativistic)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn values used: -18.8 fm (original AV18 without electromagnetic contributions), -21.8 fm (strengthened by 1% in ¹S₀), -16.5 fm (weakened by 1% in ¹S₀)
- The nn FSI peak appears around neutron energy E_n ≈ 2 MeV, the quasi-free peak around E_n ≈ 9 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous theoretical framework for muon capture on A ≤ 3 nuclei to radiative pion capture, using the same momentum-space techniques and realistic nuclear forces. It contrasts with older calculations by Phillips and Roig that used simple separable s-wave potentials and analytical 3N bound states, and with the chiral effective field theory calculations by Gårdestig and Phillips that included two-body currents. The results enable future extraction of the neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn with modern nuclear forces and provide a baseline for including two-nucleon transition operators and chiral corrections in few-nucleon systems.
