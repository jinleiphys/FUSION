# Effect of deuteron breakup on the deuteron-$Ξ$ correlation function

> The hadron-deuteron correlation function has attracted many interests as a potential method to access the three-hadron interactions. However, the weakly-bound nature of deuteron has not been considered in the preceding studies. In this study, the breakup effect of deuteron on the deuteron-$Ξ^-$ ($d$-$Ξ^-$) correlation function $C_{dΞ^-}$ is investigated. The $d$-$Ξ^-$ scattering is described by a nucleon-nucleon-$Ξ$ three-body reaction model. The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method, which is a fully quantum-mechanical and non-perturbative reaction model, is adopted. $C_{dΞ^-}$ turns out to be sensitive to the strong interaction and enhanced by the deuteron breakup effect by 6--8 % for the $d$-$Ξ^-$ relative momentum below about 70 MeV/$c$. Low-lying neutron-neutron continuum states are responsible for this enhancement. Within the adopted model, the deuteron breakup effect on $C_{dΞ^-}$ is found to be appreciable but not very significant. Except for the enhancement by several percent, studies on $C_{dΞ^-}$ without the deuteron breakup effect can be justified.

## Key claim

The deuteron breakup effect enhances the deuteron-Ξ⁻ correlation function by 6–8% for relative momenta below about 70 MeV/c, but within the adopted three-body model this effect is "appreciable but not very significant," meaning that studies neglecting deuteron breakup can be justified except for this several-percent enhancement.

## Method

The authors describe d-Ξ⁻ scattering using a nucleon-nucleon-Ξ three-body reaction model and adopt the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), a fully quantum-mechanical and non-perturbative approach. The Argonne V4' NN interaction and an N-Ξ interaction from lattice QCD are employed, with both s-wave NN channels (T,S)=(0,1) and (1,0) included. The correlation function is computed from the three-body scattering wave function under an incoming boundary condition, assuming a Gaussian source function for the d-Ξ⁻ pair with source size b=1.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement of C_dΞ⁻ by deuteron breakup: 6–8% for d-Ξ⁻ relative momentum q between 30 and 70 MeV/c
- The enhancement decreases monotonically and becomes less than 1% for q > 100 MeV/c
- NN continuum states are truncated at k_max = 2.0 fm⁻¹ (approximately 400 MeV/c)
- Bin size Δ_c = 0.2 fm⁻¹ (approximately 40 MeV/c) for the ¹³S₁ (pn) channel and Δ_c = 0.005 fm⁻¹ (approximately 1 MeV/c) for the ³¹S₀ (nn) channel
- Source size b = 1.2 fm (with additional results shown for b = 1.6 fm and 3.0 fm)
- Integration over R carried out up to R_max = 10 fm for b = 1.2 fm and 1.6 fm, and 15 fm for b = 3.0 fm

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies of hadron-hadron correlation functions, previously applied to pairs such as pΛ, ΛΛ, pΞ⁻, and pΩ, to the hadron-deuteron case, which offers sensitivity to three-hadron interactions. Prior exploratory studies of pd, K⁻d, Λd, and Ω⁻d correlation functions (e.g., by Morita and Sako, Haidenbauer et al., and Etminan et al.) ignored deuteron breakup effects; the present work quantifies this effect for the first time using CDCC. The results enable more precise extraction of N-Ξ and NNΞ interactions from future femtoscopic data, and the authors note that a combined treatment of pre-formed deuteron and three-body source functions remains a theoretical challenge for future work.
