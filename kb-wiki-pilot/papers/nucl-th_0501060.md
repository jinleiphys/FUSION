# A Study of Final State Effects in the electrodisintegration of a polarized Helium-3 target

> An approach for the description of the final state interaction in the evaluation of inclusive electromagnetic responses of a polarized Helium-3 target, is briefly illustrated. Preliminary results of calculations, where the final state interaction is fully taken into account for the two-body break-up channel, are compared with experimental data, showing a very encouraging improvement with respect to the plane wave impulse approximation results.

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary calculations of inclusive electromagnetic responses for a polarized Helium-3 target that include final state interaction (FSI) fully in the two-body break-up channel, showing encouraging improvement over plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) results, but only for the two-body break-up channel while the three-body break-up channel still uses PWIA.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive electromagnetic response functions for polarized Helium-3 by evaluating matrix elements of the electromagnetic current using three-nucleon scattering states obtained from a variational approach with the Kohn variational principle. The final state interaction is included by decomposing the three-nucleon scattering wave function into asymptotic and core components, with the asymptotic part describing nucleon-deuteron scattering and the core part expanded in Hyperspherical Harmonics Polynomials. The AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction is used without Coulomb effects or three-body forces, and FSI is included for total angular momentum up to j' ≤ 5/2 for center-of-mass energies up to 10 MeV, up to j' ≤ 7/2 for 10-30 MeV, and up to j' ≤ 11/2 for 30-100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)², PWIA calculations used up to j' ≤ 17/2; for Q² = 0.2 (GeV/c)², up to j' ≤ 19/2
- FSI included for two-body break-up channel with center-of-mass energy E_fin_CM ≤ 10 MeV (j' ≤ 5/2), 10-30 MeV (j' ≤ 7/2), and 30-100 MeV (j' ≤ 11/2)
- Unpolarized response functions R_L and R_T calculated at |q| ~ 175 MeV
- Asymmetry A calculated at Q² = 0.1 and 0.2 (GeV/c)², compared to experimental data from W. Xu et al. and F. Xiong et al.

## Context

This work extends previous PWIA calculations by the same group (Kievsky, Pace, Salme, Viviani, 1997) that used realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and relativistic electron-nucleon cross sections but disregarded FSI between the knocked-out nucleon and the spectator pair. It also extends non-relativistic FSI calculations by the Bochum group (Ishikawa, Golak, Witala, Kamada, Glockle, 1998; Golak et al., 2001) by including relativistic effects. The approach enables future inclusion of FSI in the three-body break-up channel, Coulomb effects, three-body forces, and extension to pion electroproduction from polarized Helium-3 targets.
