# Electromagnetic Radiation from Broken Symmetries in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> A new channel of direct photon production from a quark gluon plasma (QGP) is explored in the framework of high-temperature QCD. This process appears at next-to-leading order, in the presence of a charge asymmetry in the excited matter. The photon production rate from this new mechanism is suppressed compared to the QCD annihilation and Compton scattering at low baryon density but assumes importance in baryon-rich matter.

## Key claim

A new channel of direct photon production from a quark gluon plasma (QGP) via a gluon-gluon-photon vertex appears at next-to-leading order when the medium has a finite baryon chemical potential, but the photon production rate from this mechanism is suppressed compared to the standard QCD annihilation and Compton scattering at low baryon density and only assumes importance in baryon-rich matter.

## Method

The authors computed the hard photon production rate from a QGP with finite charge density using the Hard-Thermal-Loops (HTL) effective field theory of QCD at high temperature, assuming two massless quark flavors with equal chemical potentials and a fixed strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.4. They evaluated the photon self-energy from the effective two-gluon-photon vertex, summing over Matsubara frequencies and extracting the discontinuity across the real energy axis to obtain the thermal emission rate. The calculation focused on hard photons with momenta p ~ T, requiring at least one of the gluons in the self-energy diagram to be hard.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 200 MeV used for the comparison plot of photon emission rates
- Strong coupling constant fixed at alpha_s = 0.4
- Chemical potential set as mu_u = mu_d = mu = mu_B/3 for two massless quark flavors
- The new channel contribution is much smaller than QCD annihilation and Compton processes at low chemical potential, but increases more swiftly with increasing chemical potential at fixed temperature

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of photon production rates at finite chemical potential by Kapusta, Lichard, and Seibert (1991), Dumitru et al. (1993), and Vija and Thoma (1994), which focused on processes non-vanishing at zero baryon chemical potential. The new channel arises solely at finite chemical potential due to the breaking of charge conjugation invariance by the medium, as first pointed out by Majumder and Gale (2001) and Majumder, Bourque, and Gale (2004). The results enable future exploration of photon production from non-equilibrium scenarios such as the early stages of relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions where gluon populations exceed quark populations, and from jet-plasma interactions as studied by Fries, Muller, and Srivastava (2003).
