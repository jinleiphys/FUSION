# Coulomb and nuclear breakup effects in the single neutron removal reaction 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X

> We analyze the recently obtained new data on the partial cross sections and parallel momentum distributions for transitions to ground as well as excited states of the 16C core, in the one-neutron removal reaction 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X at the beam energy of 61 MeV/nucleon. The Coulomb and nuclear breakup components of the one-neutron removal cross sections have been calculated within a finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory and an eikonal model, respectively. The nuclear contributions dominate the partial cross sections for the core excited states. By adding the nuclear and Coulomb cross sections together, a reasonable agreement is obtained with the data for these states. The shapes of the experimental parallel momentum distributions of the core states are described well by the theory.

# Coulomb and nuclear breakup effects in the single neutron removal reaction 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X

## Key claim

The sum of Coulomb and nuclear breakup cross sections, calculated separately within a finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory and an eikonal model respectively, provides a reasonable description of the partial cross sections for transitions to excited states of 16C, but fails by more than an order of magnitude for the ground state transition. This discrepancy cannot be resolved by a different spin-parity assignment for the 17C ground state, and the authors suggest mechanisms beyond simple direct one-neutron removal may be required.

## Method

The authors analyzed partial cross sections and parallel momentum distributions for the one-neutron removal reaction 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X at 61 MeV/nucleon, measuring gamma decays from 16C residues. Coulomb breakup cross sections were calculated using a post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory, while nuclear breakup cross sections (including both diffraction dissociation and stripping mechanisms) were calculated within an eikonal model using the black disc approximation and the optical limit of Glauber theory. The projectile ground state was described with configurations coupling a valence neutron with specific single-particle quantum numbers and spectroscopic factors to the core states.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 61 MeV/nucleon
- 17C ground state spin-parity: 3/2+, with one-neutron separation energy of 0.729 MeV
- Partial cross section for 16C ground state (0+): experimental 113 +/- 26 mb; theoretical (sum) 261 mb with spectroscopic factor 0.03 giving 8 mb weighted
- Partial cross section for 16C 2+ state at 1.77 MeV: experimental 162 +/- 56 mb; theoretical (sum) 379 mb with weighted sum 197 mb
- Partial cross section for 16C states near 4.1 MeV (2,3(+),4+): experimental 75 +/- 25 mb; theoretical (sum) 197 mb with weighted sum 78 mb
- Pure Coulomb partial cross section for ground state: 148 mb; nuclear partial cross section: 113 mb (38 mb diffraction dissociation + 75 mb stripping)
- For the 2+ state at 1.77 MeV with l=0 configuration: Coulomb 110 mb, nuclear 163 mb; with l=2: Coulomb 32 mb, nuclear 74 mb

## Context

This work extends the (a,b gamma) type of breakup reaction studies previously performed on light 9Be targets for projectiles including 11Be, 12Be, 14B, and 16,17,19C, where nuclear breakup dominated. The results contrast with measurements on the same heavy target using the one-neutron halo nucleus 14B, where no transitions to excited core states were observed. The analysis supports the conclusion from earlier work on a 9Be target that 17C is not a halo nucleus despite its small separation energy, and underlines the need for a proper quantum mechanical calculation including Coulomb-nuclear interference terms.
