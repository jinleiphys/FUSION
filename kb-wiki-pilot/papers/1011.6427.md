# Breakup reaction models for two- and three-cluster projectiles

> Breakup reactions are one of the main tools for the study of exotic nuclei, and in particular of their continuum. In order to get valuable information from measurements, a precise reaction model coupled to a fair description of the projectile is needed. We assume that the projectile initially possesses a cluster structure, which is revealed by the dissociation process. This structure is described by a few-body Hamiltonian involving effective forces between the clusters. Within this assumption, we review various reaction models. In semiclassical models, the projectile-target relative motion is described by a classical trajectory and the reaction properties are deduced by solving a time-dependent Schroedinger equation. We then describe the principle and variants of the eikonal approximation: the dynamical eikonal approximation, the standard eikonal approximation, and a corrected version avoiding Coulomb divergence. Finally, we present the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (CDCC), in which the Schroedinger equation is solved with the projectile continuum approximated by square-integrable states. These models are first illustrated by applications to two-cluster projectiles for studies of nuclei far from stability and of reactions useful in astrophysics. Recent extensions to three-cluster projectiles, like two-neutron halo nuclei, are then presented and discussed. We end this review with some views of the future in breakup-reaction theory.

## Key claim

The paper reviews breakup reaction models under the explicit assumption that the projectile possesses a cluster structure described by a few-body Hamiltonian with effective forces between clusters, and that only elastic breakup (leaving the target unchanged in its ground state) is considered, with other channels simulated through optical potentials.

## Method

The authors review three main classes of reaction models for breakup of two- and three-cluster projectiles: semiclassical models (where projectile-target relative motion follows a classical trajectory and a time-dependent Schroedinger equation is solved), eikonal approximations (including the dynamical eikonal approximation, standard eikonal approximation, and a Coulomb-corrected version), and the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (CDCC, where the projectile continuum is approximated by square-integrable states). The projectile internal structure is described by eigenstates of a few-body Hamiltonian, and the projectile-target interaction is the sum of optical potentials between each cluster and the target.

## Key numbers

- Breakup cross section of 11Be on Pb at 68 MeV/nucleon is shown as a function of relative energy E between the 10Be core and neutron, with experimental data from Nak94 scaled by a factor of 0.85 as suggested in FNA04
- Impact parameter cutoff at b_min = 13 fm is used to simulate nuclear interaction in purely Coulomb calculations
- Impact parameter cutoff at b_min = 30 fm is used to simulate a forward-angle cut
- The asymptotic behaviour of the dipole breakup probability dP_bu^E1/dE for b goes to infinity is proportional to exp(-2*omega*b/v), where omega = (E - E0)/hbar

## Context

This review extends earlier work on breakup reactions for two-cluster projectiles (such as deuteron and halo nuclei studies) to three-cluster projectiles like two-neutron halo nuclei, building on developments in Refs. BCD09, EMO09, and RAG09. The paper contrasts the semiclassical approximation of Alder and Winther (AW75) with the eikonal approximation of Glauber and the CDCC method of Kam86 and TNT01, noting that recent progress in Faddeev-type approaches (DFS05c, DFS05l) has been limited to light targets. The models enable extraction of nuclear structure information from breakup measurements of exotic nuclei and indirect determination of astrophysical reaction cross sections.
