# Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels calculations with core excitation

> The effect of core excitation in the elastic scattering and breakup of a two-body halo nucleus on a stable target nucleus is studied. The structure of the weakly-bound projectile is described in the weak-coupling limit, assuming a particle-rotor model. The eigenfunctions and the associated eigenvalues are obtained by diagonalizing this Hamiltonian in a square-integrable basis (pseudo-states). For the radial coordinate between the particle and the core, a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis is used. For the reaction dynamics, an extension of the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method, which takes into account dynamic core excitation and deexcitation due to the presence of non-central parts in the core-target interaction, is adapted to be used along with a pseudo-states (PS) basis.

## Key claim
Core excitation effects in elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei are shown to be significant when treated with a full coupled-channels formalism (XCDCC) using a pseudo-state basis, contrary to earlier XCDCC calculations that found very small effects due to programming errors and equation bugs in Ref. [Summers06].

## Method
The projectile (e.g., 11Be) is described in a particle-rotor model with a deformed core, and its bound and continuum states are obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, yielding square-integrable pseudo-states that discretize the continuum. The three-body scattering problem is solved by expanding the total wave function in these pseudo-states and solving coupled-channel equations that include dynamic core excitation and deexcitation via non-central parts of the core-target interaction. The coupling potentials are evaluated using multipole expansions of both Coulomb and nuclear core-target interactions, with the nuclear part derived from a deformed optical potential.

## Key numbers
- For 11Be structure: quadrupole deformation parameter β2 = 0.67 (deformation length δ2 = 1.664 fm); Woods-Saxon central potential with radius R = 2.483 fm, diffuseness a = 0.65 fm, parity-dependent strengths Vc = -54.24 MeV (positive parity) and Vc = -49.67 MeV (negative parity); spin-orbit strength Vso = 8.5 MeV
- THO basis parameters: m = 4, b = 1.6 fm; number of oscillator functions N and γ parameter determined per reaction
- 11Be first excited core state: 2+ at Ex = 3.368 MeV
- 11Be+p breakup data at 63.7 MeV/nucleon: angular distributions for relative energy intervals Erel = 0–2.5 MeV and Erel = 2.5–5.0 MeV; the first interval contains a narrow 5/2+ resonance at Erel = 1.28 MeV

## Context
This work extends the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method by incorporating core excitation, revisiting the XCDCC formalism of Summers et al. (2006) which contained errors that led to underestimation of core excitation effects. It improves upon the no-recoil Distorted Wave Born Approximation (XDWBA) approach of Crespo et al. (2011) and Moro et al. (2012) by including higher-order effects such as continuum-continuum couplings, and overcomes the energy limitations of the adiabatic model of Horoi et al. (2010). The method enables systematic study of core excitation in elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei on light, medium, and heavy targets (p, 64Zn, 208Pb).
