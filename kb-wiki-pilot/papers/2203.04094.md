# Status of deep subbarrier $\mathbf{{}^{12}{\rm C}+{}^{12}{\rm C}}$ fusion and advancing the Trojan horse method

> In this paper, I will update the current status of the carbon-carbon fusion research taking into account that after the latest analysis [Beck {\it et al.} Eur. Phys. J. A {\bf 56}, 97 (2020), Letter to the Editor] new important experimental and theoretical results had been published and will discuss how to advance new THM measurements to extract the low-energy astrophysical $S$-factors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the sharp rise of the astrophysical S-factor for deep subbarrier 12C+12C fusion reported in earlier Trojan horse method (THM) measurements was an artifact of neglecting Coulomb interactions in the initial, intermediate, and final states; including Coulomb-nuclear distorted waves reduces the extracted low-energy astrophysical factor by up to three orders of magnitude.

## Method

The author reviews the current status of 12C+12C fusion research, comparing direct measurements (STELLA and Notre Dame-Mexico collaborations, reaching E ~ 2.2 MeV) with indirect THM results from the 14N+13C reaction. The paper derives the full formalism for THM analysis including distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), R-matrix multi-level two-channel treatment, and a Coulomb factor for narrow resonances, then applies this to renormalize the earlier THM data. Two THM reactions are proposed for future measurements: 12C(14N,d)24Mg* and 12C(13C,n)24Mg*.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier for head-on 12C+12C collision: ≈ 6.6 MeV (calculated with carbon radius 1.4 × 12^(1/3) fm)
- Astrophysically relevant Gamow window for (8-10) solar mass stars: E = 1.2-1.8 MeV (temperature 0.35-0.64 T9)
- Gamow window for ~25 solar mass stars: E = 1.8-2.6 MeV (temperature 0.64-1.15 T9)
- Lowest measured resonance energy from direct measurements: E ≈ 2.14 MeV (Spillane et al.)
- Lowest energy reached by THM: E = 0.8 MeV (Tumino et al.)
- Coulomb parameter in the entry channel of the THM reaction: 4.4
- AMD calculations show resonances at 0.94 MeV, 1.5 MeV, and 2.2 MeV
- Including Coulomb-nuclear distortions causes drop of low-energy astrophysical factor by factor of about 10^3

## Context

This work updates the status of carbon-carbon fusion following the analysis by Beck et al. (Eur. Phys. J. A 56, 97, 2020) and incorporates new experimental results from the STELLA collaboration and Notre Dame-Mexico group (both 2020), as well as the first fully microscopic AMD calculations by Taniguchi and Kimura (2021). The paper extends the critique of the plane-wave approximation used in the original THM analysis (Tumino et al., Nature) and provides the theoretical framework for advancing new THM measurements that can properly account for Coulomb-nuclear distortions, potentially resolving the long-standing debate about low-energy resonance structure in 12C+12C fusion.
