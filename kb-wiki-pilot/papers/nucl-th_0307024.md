# Determination of $S_{17}$ from systematic analyses on $^8$B Coulomb breakup with the Eikonal-CDCC method

> A new version of the method of Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels method (CDCC) is proposed, that is, the Eikonal-CDCC method (E-CDCC). The E-CDCC equation, for Coulomb dissociation in particular, can easily and safely be solved, since it is a first-order differential equation and contains no huge angular momentum in contrast to the CDCC one. The scattering amplitude calculated by E-CDCC has a similar form to that by CDCC. Then one can construct hybrid amplitude in an intuitive way, i.e., CDCC amplitude is adopted for a smaller angular momentum $L$ and E-CDCC one for a larger $L$ related to an impact parameter $b$. The hybrid calculation is found to perfectly reproduce the quantum mechanical result for $^{58}$Ni($^8$B,$^7$Be$+p$)$^{58}$Ni at 240 MeV, which shows its applicability to systematic analysis of $^8$B dissociation to extract the astrophysical factor $S_{17}$ with high accuracy.

# Determination of $S_{17}$ from systematic analyses on $^8$B Coulomb breakup with the Eikonal-CDCC method

## Key claim

The hybrid calculation combining standard CDCC for small angular momenta and the new Eikonal-CDCC (E-CDCC) for large angular momenta reproduces the fully quantum-mechanical result for $^{58}$Ni($^8$B,$^7$Be$+p$)$^{58}$Ni at 240 MeV with less than 1% error, enabling systematic analysis of $^8$B dissociation to extract $S_{17}$ with the required 5% accuracy; however, the full-eikonal calculation without the hybrid combination shows "fairy large difference" from the quantum-mechanical result, indicating that the eikonal approximation alone is insufficient.

## Method

The authors propose the Eikonal-CDCC method (E-CDCC), which describes the center-of-mass motion between projectile and target using a straight-line eikonal approximation while treating projectile excitation non-adiabatically and non-perturbatively through discretized continuum states as in standard CDCC. The E-CDCC equation is a first-order differential equation containing no huge angular momentum, making it easier to solve than standard CDCC. A hybrid scattering amplitude is constructed by adopting standard CDCC amplitudes for smaller angular momentum $L$ and E-CDCC amplitudes for larger $L$ (larger impact parameter $b$), with the connection point $L_{\rm C}$ chosen so that the two amplitudes coincide for $L > L_{\rm C}$.

## Key numbers

- The hybrid calculation reproduces the quantum-mechanical result for $^{58}$Ni($^8$B,$^7$Be$+p$)$^{58}$Ni at 240 MeV with error less than 1% for both elastic and total breakup cross sections.
- The required number of partial waves for standard CDCC analysis of MSU data is approximately 15,000; for GSI data at 250 MeV/nucleon, more than 100,000 partial waves would be required.
- The modelspace used 16, 8, and 8 bin-states for s-, p-, and d-states of $^8$B respectively, with maximum excitation energy 10 MeV, $r_{\rm max}=100$ fm, $R_{\rm max}=500$ fm, and $L_{\rm max}=1000$.
- The extracted $S_{17}(0)$ from a previous CDCC+ANC analysis of Notre Dame data at 25.8 MeV was 22.83 $\pm$ 0.51 (theo) $\pm$ 2.28 (expt) eVb.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method developed by the Kyushu group to handle the extremely large partial waves required for Coulomb breakup at intermediate energies, where standard CDCC becomes impractical. The method is designed for systematic analysis of $^8$B Coulomb dissociation data from RIKEN, GSI, and MSU experiments, which aim to extract the astrophysical factor $S_{17}$ for the $^7$Be($p,\gamma$)$^8$B reaction. The hybrid approach is intended to be combined with the ANC method previously proposed by the same group (Yamashita, 2003) to determine $S_{17}(0)$ with less than 5% error, addressing the contradiction between MSU and RIKEN data regarding E2 component contributions and the unresolved role of nuclear-Coulomb interference.
