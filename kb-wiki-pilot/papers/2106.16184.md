# Investigation of neutron transfer in $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction

> The relative importance of neutron transfer and breakup process in reaction around Coulomb barrier energies have been studied for the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system. Coupled channel calculations have been performed to understand the one neutron stripping and pickup cross sections along with the breakup in the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system. The systematics of one and two neutron striping and pickup cross sections with $^7$Li projectile on several targets show an approximate universal behaviour that have been explained by a simple model. Complete reaction mechanism have been studied by comparing the reaction cross sections with cumulative cross sections of total fusion and one neutron transfer.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that one-neutron stripping and pickup cross sections in the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system, along with systematics across multiple targets, show an approximate universal behaviour that can be described by a modified Wong formula incorporating transfer probability, but the calculations for pickup significantly underpredict the measured data due to incomplete spectroscopic information for the residual nucleus $^{123}$Sn.

## Method
Coupled Reaction Channel (CRC) calculations were performed using both phenomenological global optical model potentials (CRC1) and normalized microscopic Sao Paulo potentials (CRC2) for the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system. Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) and combined CDCC+CRC calculations were carried out using the FRESCO code to simultaneously account for breakup and neutron transfer channels, with the $^{7}$Li nucleus treated as an $\alpha$+t two-body cluster structure and continuum states discretized into bins of constant momentum width $k = 0.20$ fm$^{-1}$ up to $k_{max} = 0.8$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers
- One-neutron stripping cross sections for $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn were measured by offline $\gamma$ counting in Ref. [VVP18] and compared with calculations at multiple energies around the Coulomb barrier
- Spectroscopic factors used for $^{7}$Li $\rightarrow$ $^{6}$Li+$n$ transfer: C$^2$S = 0.43 (1p$_{3/2}$) and 0.29 (1p$_{1/2}$); for $^{7}$Li+$n$ $\rightarrow$ $^{8}$Li transfer: C$^2$S = 0.98 (1p$_{3/2}$) and 0.056 (1p$_{1/2}$), taken from Cohen and Kurath
- Fitting parameters for universal behaviour of transfer cross sections: for 1n stripping, $a = -3.58$ MeV and $c = 0.44$ MeV$^{-1}$; for 2n stripping, $a = -2.86$ MeV and $c = 0.37$ MeV$^{-1}$; for 1n pickup, $a = -1.86$ MeV and $c = 2.16$ MeV$^{-1}$
- Non-capture breakup (NCBU) calculated from CDCC contributes higher than 1n transfer at below barrier energies

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of neutron transfer with weakly bound projectiles, complementing earlier work on $^{9}$Be projectiles by Fang et al. which showed similar universal behaviour in neutron stripping cross sections. The study builds on previous measurements of elastic scattering, fusion, and transfer for $^{7}$Li on various targets including $^{65}$Cu (Shrivastava et al. 2006), $^{93}$Nb (Pandit et al. 2017), $^{124}$Sn (Parkar et al. 2018), $^{197}$Au (Palshetkar et al. 2014), $^{198}$Pt (Shrivastava et al. 2013), and $^{209}$Bi (Dasgupta et al. 2004). The results enable a more complete understanding of the reaction mechanism by comparing reaction cross sections with cumulative cross sections of total fusion and one-neutron transfer, while identifying that other channels such as proton transfer, non-capture breakup, and target inelastic states may be necessary for a full description.
