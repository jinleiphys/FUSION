# Alpha particle production by molecular single-particle effect in reactions of $^{9}$Be just above the Coulomb barrier

> The $α$-particle production in the dissociation of $^{9}$Be on $^{209}$Bi and $^{64}$Zn at energies just above the Coulomb barrier is studied within the two-center shell model approach. The dissociation of $^{9}$Be on $^{209}$Bi is caused by a molecular single-particle effect (Landau-Zener mechanism) before the nuclei reach the Coulomb barrier. Molecular single-particle effects do not occur at that stage of the collision for $^{9}$Be+$^{64}$Zn, and this explains the absence of fusion suppression observed for this system. The polarisation of the energy level of the last neutron of $^{9}$Be and, therefore the existence of avoided crossings with that level, depends on the structure of the target.

## Key claim

The dissociation of ⁹Be on ²⁰⁹Bi at energies just above the Coulomb barrier is caused by a molecular single-particle effect (Landau-Zener mechanism) at two very close avoided crossings before the nuclei reach the barrier, whereas for ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn such molecular effects do not occur at that stage of the collision, explaining the absence of fusion suppression observed for that system. The polarisation of the energy level of the last neutron of ⁹Be, and therefore the existence of avoided crossings with that level, depends on the structure of the target.

## Method

The authors used the adiabatic two-center shell model (TCSM) to calculate single-particle neutron levels for the systems ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi and ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn as functions of the internuclear separation. The dissociation probability of ⁹Be at avoided crossings was computed using the Landau-Zener formula, with radial velocities obtained from classical equations of motion for the nucleus-nucleus potential derived from the TCSM and Strutinsky method. Cross sections for alpha-particle production were calculated by summing over partial waves, distinguishing between complete fusion (0 ≤ l ≤ l_cr) and scattering (l_cr ≤ l ≤ l_max) trajectories.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi at E_cm = 44.1 MeV: l_cr = 8, l_max = 14
- For ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi at E_cm = 57.5 MeV: l_cr = 16, l_max = 26
- Coupling matrix elements at avoided crossings 1 and 2: |H'_12| = 0.1073 MeV and 0.1250 MeV, respectively
- Energy differences at avoided crossings 1 and 2: d/dr(ε₁-ε₂) = 0.0184 MeV fm⁻¹ and 0.0352 MeV fm⁻¹, respectively
- For ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn: Coulomb barrier V_B ≈ 15.5 MeV at r_B ≈ 9.43 fm (calculated); experimental values V_B_exp = 16.2 MeV, r_B_exp = 10 fm
- For ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn: touching configuration distance r_t ≈ 7.45 fm; neck formation begins at r ≈ 9.21 fm
- Calculated cross sections for alpha-particle production in ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi underestimate experimental data by a factor 3.7-4.7 in the low range of incident energies studied

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TCSM study of ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi (Diaz-Torres, Thompson, and Scheid, Nucl. Phys. A, in press) to compare with ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn, motivated by experimental observations that ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi shows fusion suppression (Signorini, Nucl. Phys. A 693, 2001) while ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn does not (Moraes et al., Phys. Rev. C 61, 2000). The calculated alpha-production cross sections for ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi agree with preliminary data for the similar system ⁹Be+²⁰⁸Pb (Fulton, private communication) but underestimate data for ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi (Signorini et al., BO2000 proceedings), suggesting new experiments are needed. The work enables future studies using a more general TCSM for deformed nuclei and quantum mechanical descriptions of relative motion for sub-barrier energies.
