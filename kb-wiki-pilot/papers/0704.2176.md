# Theoretical treatments of fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We review the theoretical methods to evaluate fusion cross sections in collisions of weakly bound nuclei. We piont out that in such collisions the coupling to the breakup channel leads to the appearance of different fusion processes. The extentsion of the coupled-channel method to coupling with the continuum is the most successful treatment for these collisions. However, evaluating separate cross section for each fusion process remains a very hard task.

# Theoretical treatments of fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

## Key claim

The Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method is the most successful treatment for fusion of weakly bound nuclei, but evaluating separate cross sections for complete fusion (CF) and incomplete fusion (ICF) remains a very hard task, and the available CDCC calculations do not provide separate descriptions of these processes.

## Method

The authors review theoretical approaches to fusion of weakly bound nuclei, focusing on the CDCC method which replaces the continuous breakup spectrum with a finite number of discrete states (bins) built from wave packets in momentum space. They also discuss early schematic models using polarization potentials, a CDCC-Dynamic Polarization Potential (DPP) approach that extracts polarization potentials from elastic scattering calculations, classical trajectory methods, and time-dependent wave packet descriptions of three-body systems.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb collision without continuum-continuum couplings, the CF cross section is strongly enhanced at low energies and slightly hindered at high energies, with a transition energy $E_{tr} > V_B$
- When continuum-continuum couplings are included for $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb, both CF and TF cross sections are much smaller, with $E_{tr}$ falling below the Coulomb barrier
- For $^{6,7}$Li projectiles on $^{209}$Bi and $^{59}$Co targets, the breakup channel enhances total fusion around the Coulomb barrier with little effect at higher energies; the effect is more marked for $^{209}$Bi than for $^{59}$Co
- For $^{6}$He + $^{208}$Pb, when breakup couplings are included, the static halo effect is cancelled and the fusion cross section becomes similar to that for $^{6}$Li + $^{208}$Pb, consistent with data for $^{6}$He + $^{209}$Bi

## Context

This review extends earlier work on coupled-channel theory for stable nuclei (Dasso, Landowne and Winther, 1983) to the case of weakly bound projectiles where coupling to the continuum becomes important. It contrasts early conflicting results from schematic models: Hussein et al. (1992) found CF suppression around the barrier using an imaginary polarization potential, while Dasso and Vitturi (1994) found strongly enhanced CF using a bound effective channel to represent the continuum. The review enables future development of classical and semiclassical models that can separate complete from incomplete fusion, a task the current CDCC calculations cannot perform.
