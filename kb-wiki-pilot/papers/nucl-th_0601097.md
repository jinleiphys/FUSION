# Coulomb breakup in a Transformed Harmonic Oscillator Basis

> The problem of Coulomb breakup in the scattering of a two-body loosely bound projectile by a heavy target is addressed. A basis of transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) wave functions is used to discretize the projectile continuum and to diagonalize the Hamiltonian of the two-body system. Results for the reaction $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni at subcoulomb energies are presented. Comparison of different observables with those obtained with the standard Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method shows good agreement between both approaches.

## Key claim

The Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis method, previously restricted to s-wave breakup and analytical ground states, is extended to arbitrary partial waves and numerically-obtained ground states; it reproduces standard Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) results for the Coulomb-dominated reaction ⁸B+⁵⁸Ni at subcoulomb energies, provided the basis is large enough to describe higher excitation energies.

## Method

The ground-state wave function of the two-body projectile (⁸B modeled as p+⁷Be) is used to define a local scale transformation (LST) that maps it to a harmonic oscillator ground state. This same LST is then applied to higher harmonic oscillator states to generate a discrete, orthogonal THO basis for the projectile continuum; the internal Hamiltonian is diagonalized in this basis to obtain pseudostates for ℓ=0 and ℓ=1 partial waves. These pseudostates replace the continuum bins in a standard CDCC coupled-channels calculation, with coupling potentials computed from the proton-⁵⁸Ni and ⁷Be-⁵⁸Ni interactions, and the continuous breakup S-matrix is reconstructed via overlap integrals with true scattering wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Reaction: ⁸B+⁵⁸Ni at 25.8 MeV (subcoulomb energy)
- Maximum excitation energy for retained pseudostates: 10 MeV
- Number of bins in standard CDCC: 16 for ℓ=1, 32 for ℓ=0
- Bin wave functions calculated up to 100 fm; coupled equations integrated to 500 fm
- Maximum projectile-target orbital angular momentum: L_max = 500
- THO basis sizes tested: N=10, 20, 40, and 60 states for s and p waves
- For N=60, number of continuum states actually included in coupled equations after energy cuts: N_coup = 27 for both s and p waves
- A deeply bound ℓ=0 state appears at ≈ -15 MeV (Pauli-forbidden, removed)
- Breakup S-matrix shown for total angular momentum J=150 (corresponding to ≈10° scattering angle)

## Context

This work extends the THO method, previously applied to deuteron s-wave breakup (Mor02, Per01b), to arbitrary partial waves and numerically-obtained ground states, enabling its use for realistic loosely-bound nuclei like ⁸B. It directly compares with the standard CDCC method (Rawitscher, Yahiro, Kamimura, Austern) and with CDCC using a Gaussian basis (Ega04), showing good agreement for elastic and breakup angular distributions and energy-differential cross sections. The method enables future application to three-body continuum problems (manoli05) and collisions of three-body loosely-bound projectiles.
