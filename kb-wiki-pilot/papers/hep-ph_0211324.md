# Proton breakup in high-energy pA collisions from perturbative QCD

> We argue that the distribution of hadrons near the longitudinal light-cone in central high-energy pA collisions is computable in weak coupling QCD. This is because the density of gluons per unit transverse area in the dense target at saturation provides an intrinsic semi-hard momentum scale, Qs. We predict that the longitudinal distribution of (anti-)baryons and mesons steepens with increasing energy and atomic number of the target, and that the transverse momentum distribution broadens. We show that the evolution of high moments of the longitudinal net baryon distribution with Qs is determined by the anomalous dimension gamma_qq.

## Key claim

The distribution of hadrons near the longitudinal light-cone in central high-energy pA collisions is computable in weak coupling QCD, but only when the target approaches the "black body" limit where the saturation momentum Qs provides an intrinsic semi-hard scale; the authors argue that in this regime the projectile coherence is completely destroyed and the proton decays predominantly into leading mesons rather than baryons.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive hadron production in the forward region of central pA collisions by convoluting the quark distribution function of the proton (CTEQ5L) with fragmentation functions (KKP parameterizations) and the quark-nucleus cross section derived from the Color Glass Condensate model. The transverse momentum distribution of quarks is given by the correlation function of two Wilson lines evaluated in the background field of the dense target, with the saturation momentum Qs providing the factorization scale. The inelastic qA cross section is obtained from a Gaussian average over stochastic classical non-abelian Yang-Mills fields describing the small-x gluon distribution of the target.

## Key numbers

- Qs^2 ~ 10 GeV^2 at x ~ 10^{-4} for BNL-RHIC energy (for A ~ 200 central nuclear targets)
- Qs^2 ~ 50 GeV^2 at x ~ 10^{-6} for CERN-LHC energy (for A ~ 200 central nuclear targets)
- For LHC energies, head-on central pp collisions may exhibit gluon densities as high as those for A ~ 200 nuclear targets at RHIC energy
- At asymptotically large q_t >> Qs, the correlator C(q_t) matches leading-twist perturbation theory: C(q_t) -> Qs^2/q_t^4
- The evolution of high moments of the longitudinal net baryon distribution with Qs^2 is given by d/d(log Qs^2) <z^n>_{B-Bbar} = (alpha_s(Qs)/pi) * gamma^{(0)}_{qq}(n+1) * <z^n>_{B-Bbar}, applicable for n >= 5

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach to leading hadron production, contrasting with nonperturbative models based on "gluon junction" and "diquark breaking" mechanisms (Kopeliovich and Zakharov, Kharzeev, Capella and Kopeliovich, Vance et al.) that predict baryons and anti-baryons produced mainly at small transverse momentum. The paper builds on earlier work by Berera et al. (1997) and Dumitru and Jalilian-Marian (2002) on forward quark jets from protons shattering the colored glass, and enables predictions for forward hadron measurements at RHIC (STAR, BRAHMS) and LHC that can distinguish between the saturation scenario and soft-process models.
