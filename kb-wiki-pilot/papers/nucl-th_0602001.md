# The Trojan Horse Method: an Indirect Technique in Nuclear Astrophysics

> The Trojan Horse (TH) method is a powerful indirect technique that provides information to determine astrophysical factors for rearrangement processes at astrophysically relevant energies. A short coming for understanding the reliability of the technique has been determining the importance of nuclear and Coulomb effects on the energy dependence of the yield. Using a simple model, we demonstrate that off-energy-shell and Coulomb effects in the entry channel and the final state nuclear interactions do not change the energy dependence of the astrophysical factor extracted from the TH reaction. Some examples are presented.

## Key claim

Using a simple model, the authors demonstrate that off-energy-shell effects, Coulomb effects in the entry channel, and final-state nuclear interactions do not change the energy dependence of the astrophysical factor extracted from the Trojan Horse reaction, though the absolute value of the astrophysical factor must still be found by normalization to direct measurements at higher energies.

## Method

The authors constructed a simple theoretical model comparing half-off-energy-shell (HOF) reaction amplitudes for the binary subprocess with on-shell (ON) amplitudes, using the reactions 6Li(d,α)4He and 7Li(p,α)4He as test cases. They calculated the energy dependence of the astrophysical factor S(E) for both direct and resonant processes under quasifree kinematics, varying the off-shell parameter σx and including or excluding Coulomb interactions in the entrance channel and final-state interactions described by the Ali-Bodmer potential.

## Key numbers

- For the 7Li(p,α)4He reaction (a=d, x=p, y=n), the half-off-shell and on-shell astrophysical factors were compared and found to have practically identical energy dependence at low energies, with normalization performed at ExA = 1 keV
- For the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction (a=6Li, x=d, y=α), the half-off-shell astrophysical factor was calculated for σd values of 0 (on-shell), εdα6Li (QF kinematics), 1.5 εdα6Li, and 2.5 εdα6Li, showing that energy dependence remains unchanged while the magnitude changes
- The TH reaction 7Li(d,nα)4He at Ed7Li = 4.20 MeV covers the energy region 0 < Ep7Li ≤ 800 keV, with σp varying by 7% around the QF value σp = 2.224 MeV
- The TH reaction 6Li(6Li,αα)4He was calculated at E6Li6Li = 3.14 MeV, and the TH reaction 7Li(d,nα)4He at Ed7Li = 4.03 MeV

## Context

This work addresses reservations about the Trojan Horse method first suggested by Baur (1986) regarding the reliability of neglecting off-shell effects and initial/final state interactions in the 2→3 reaction amplitude. The results justify the standard analysis procedures used in prior TH experiments (Spitaleri et al. 2001, Lattuada et al. 2001, Tumino et al. 2003) and enable future applications of the method to determine astrophysical factors for rearrangement processes down to zero relative kinetic energy without distortion from electron screening, allowing comparison with direct measurements to determine the screening potential.
