# Calculation of the 12C+12C sub-barrier fusion cross section in an imaginary time-dependent mean field theory

> The 12C+12C sub-barrier fusion cross section is calculated within the framework of a Time Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) based classical model using the Feynman Path Integral Method. The modified astrophysical S*-factor is compared to direct and indirect experimental results. A good agreement with the direct data is found. In the lower energy region, where recent analyses of experimental data obtained with the Trojan Horse Method (THM) lead to contrasting results, the model predicts an S* factor half way between those results. Low energy resonances revealed in the THM data are added to the calculation and the relative reaction rate in the Gamow region is calculated. The role of different resonances is discussed in detail and their influence on the reaction rate at temperatures relevant to stellar evolution is investigated.

## Key claim
The model predicts an S*-factor at sub-barrier energies that lies halfway between the two contrasting sets of results obtained from recent Trojan Horse Method (THM) analyses, rather than fully agreeing with either.

## Method
The authors compute the 12C+12C sub-barrier fusion cross section using a Time Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) based classical model, combined with the Feynman Path Integral Method to treat tunneling. The resulting modified astrophysical S*-factor is compared to both direct experimental data and indirect THM results. Low-energy resonances observed in the THM data are then added to the calculation to evaluate their impact on the reaction rate in the Gamow region.

## Key numbers
- The model’s S*-factor at the lowest energies falls midway between the two contrasting THM experimental results.
- The reaction rate in the Gamow region is calculated with and without the inclusion of low-energy resonances from THM data.
- The influence of specific resonances on the reaction rate is quantified at temperatures relevant to stellar evolution.

## Context
This work extends previous TDHF-based approaches to sub-barrier fusion by incorporating path-integral tunneling, and directly addresses the discrepancy between recent THM analyses of 12C+12C fusion. The results enable a more constrained prediction of the stellar reaction rate for carbon burning, particularly at temperatures where the Gamow peak lies below the Coulomb barrier, and provide a theoretical benchmark for future experimental efforts to resolve the THM controversy.
