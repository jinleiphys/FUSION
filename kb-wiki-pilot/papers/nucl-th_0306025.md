# Kinetic description of charmonium production in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We study the evolution of charmonia as they collide with the constituents of the fireball produced in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The latter evolves in a manner controlled by the equation of state as given by lattice QCD, and is constructed in such a way that the observed hadronic spectra are correctly reproduced. A kinetic description of charmonium interactions with both quark-gluon and hadronic degrees of freedom allows to study in detail the evolution in different regimes, controlled by collision energy, kinematics and geometry. The data collected at the CERN-SPS accelerator are well described and new estimates for J/psi production at BNL-RHIC are presented.

## Key claim

The authors argue that charmonium suppression in high-energy nuclear collisions can be described by a kinetic approach that treats the fireball evolution as independently constrained by hadronic observables, rather than fitting the medium properties to J/psi data; they hedge that this framework is "consistent with" SPS data and provides "new estimates" for RHIC, not definitive predictions.

## Method

The authors factorize charmonium production into an initial Glauber-model stage (with nuclear absorption cross sections extrapolated from pA data) and a subsequent kinetic evolution in the fireball. The fireball is modeled as a thermalized system using a quasi-particle equation of state fitted to lattice QCD, with its time evolution (temperature, volume, density) fixed by reproducing observed hadronic spectra at freeze-out. A transport equation for J/psi is solved including dissociation via the Bhanot-Peskin cross section in the quark-gluon plasma and regeneration via detailed balance, with the medium's temperature and volume evolution as input.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_c = 170 MeV (from lattice QCD with 2+1 flavors)
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.5 GeV; factorization scale: mu_c = 1.4 GeV; K-factor = 2 for open charm production
- Nuclear absorption cross section for J/psi: sigma_NUC = 4.5 mb at SPS energy sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV, with energy scaling exponent lambda = 0.12
- Initial temperature: T_0 = 320 MeV at SPS (Pb+Pb, sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV), T_0 = 390 MeV at RHIC (Au+Au, sqrt(s) = 200 GeV)
- Thermalization time: tau_0 = 1.0 fm/c at SPS, tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c at RHIC
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 100 MeV at SPS, T_f = 130 MeV at RHIC
- Freeze-out proper time: tau_f = 18 fm/c at SPS, tau_f = 17 fm/c at RHIC
- Number of c-cbar pairs in Au+Au grows by nearly two orders of magnitude from SPS to RHIC energy

## Context

This work extends the kinetic description of charmonium interactions in heavy-ion collisions by using a fireball evolution independently constrained by hadronic observables (following the authors' earlier work on dilepton yields, RSW02), rather than fitting the medium to J/psi data alone. It contrasts with models that attribute anomalous suppression solely to QGP formation (e.g., Debye screening approaches by DPS01, W01) and with models incorporating nuclear effects like initial-state gluon radiation (HK98) or color excitation (HKP01). The approach enables predictions for J/psi production at RHIC energies where regeneration from coalescence of c-cbar pairs may become significant, as suggested by BMS00, TSR01, GR01, and GOR01.
