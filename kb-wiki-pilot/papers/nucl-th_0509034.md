# Momentum-space description of three-nucleon breakup reactions including the Coulomb interaction

> The Coulomb interaction between the two protons is included in the calculation of proton-deuteron breakup and of three-body electromagnetic disintegration of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$. The hadron dynamics is based on the purely nucleonic charge-dependent (CD) Bonn potential and its realistic extension CD Bonn + $Δ$ to a coupled-channel two-baryon potential, allowing for single virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation. Calculations are done using integral equations in momentum space. The screening and renormalization approach is employed for including the Coulomb interaction. Convergence of the procedure is found at moderate screening radii. The reliability of the method is demonstrated. The Coulomb effect on breakup observables is seen at all energies in particular kinematic regimes.

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction between the two protons can be included in calculations of proton-deuteron breakup and three-body electromagnetic disintegration of ³He using a screening and renormalization approach in momentum space, with convergence achieved at moderate screening radii for most kinematic regimes, though the method requires larger screening radii near the pp final-state interaction regime where the renormalization factor becomes ill-defined at zero relative momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations in momentum space using the screening and renormalization approach, where the Coulomb potential is screened with a smooth cutoff function (exponent n=4) and the resulting scattering amplitudes are corrected by renormalization factors to recover the unscreened Coulomb limit. The hadronic dynamics uses the CD Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + Δ, which allows for single virtual Δ-isobar excitation. For breakup, the full transition matrix is decomposed into a pure Coulomb breakup part and a Coulomb-modified short-range part, with the screened Coulomb interaction treated in two-baryon partial waves up to orbital angular momentum L ≤ 15.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius R = 20 fm is sufficient for convergence of spin-averaged differential cross section and spin observables in most kinematic situations
- Screening radius up to R = 60 fm required for convergence near pp final-state interaction (pp-FSI) regime at low relative pp energy
- Maximal three-baryon total angular momentum considered: J = 61/2
- Two-baryon partial waves with orbital angular momentum L ≤ 6 sufficient for convergence in electromagnetic disintegration of ³He
- Calculations performed at proton lab energies of 10.5, 13, 19, and 65 MeV for pd breakup
- Photon lab energies of 15 and 55 MeV for ³He photodisintegration

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous treatment of Coulomb in pd elastic scattering and electromagnetic reactions (Deltuva et al., 2005) to pd breakup and three-body electromagnetic disintegration of ³He, following the renormalization technique of Taylor (1974) and Alt et al. (1978). It improves upon earlier attempts by Alt et al. (1994), Kievsky et al. (1997), and Suslov et al. (2004) by using realistic hadronic potentials without separable expansion and a complete treatment of the Coulomb interaction. The method enables benchmark comparisons with experimental pd breakup data and future comparisons with configuration-space calculations.
