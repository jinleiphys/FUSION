# Diffractive structure functions in nuclei

> We calculate proton and nuclear diffractive structure functions in the IPsat (Kowalski-Teaney) dipole model. This parametrization has previously been shown to provide good agreement with inclusive F_2 measurements and exclusive vector meson measurements at HERA. We discuss how the impact parameter dependence crucially affects our analysis, in particular for small beta.

# Diffractive structure functions in nuclei

## Key claim
The IPsat (Kowalski-Teaney) dipole model, which includes a realistic impact parameter dependence rather than a factorizable Gaussian profile, can describe proton diffractive structure function data from HERA and be extended to predict nuclear diffractive structure functions. The impact parameter dependence crucially affects the analysis, particularly at small β, because the qq̄g component is sensitive to larger impact parameters and vanishes in the black disk limit, requiring a smaller αs value than in earlier factorized calculations.

## Method
The authors decompose the diffractive structure function into transverse qq̄, longitudinal qq̄, and qq̄g components, using the IPsat dipole cross section parametrization that eikonalizes a DGLAP-evolved gluon distribution with an impact parameter profile. For the proton, the impact parameter profile is taken as a Gaussian T(bT) ∼ exp(-b²/2BG); for nuclei, the profile is constructed by summing over nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution. The qq̄g component is evaluated by interpolating between the small-β large-Nc formula and the finite-β large-Q² form, and the free parameter αs in the qq̄g term is fitted to HERA diffractive data.

## Key numbers
- χ²/Ndof = 1.3 for the combined HERA dataset (ZEUS FPC, H1 LRG, ZEUS LPS, H1 FPS) with αs = 0.14 for the qq̄g term
- χ² = 0.5 for ZEUS LPS data alone and χ² = 0.8 for H1 FPS data alone
- For LPS data alone, αs = 0.21 still gives χ² < 1
- For gold nuclei at Q² = 5 GeV² and xℙ = 10⁻³, the diffractive structure function in the "non breakup" (coherent) case is about 15% lower than in the "breakup" (incoherent) case
- Expected ratio σD/σtot in eA collisions: increase from ∼15% (ep) to 20%–25%, combining nuclear shadowing of inclusive structure function by factor ∼0.8 and nuclear enhancement of diffraction by factor ∼1.2 at moderate β ≳ 0.2

## Context
This work extends the IPsat dipole model, previously shown to agree with inclusive F₂ measurements and exclusive vector meson measurements at HERA, to nuclear diffractive structure functions. It improves upon earlier calculations such as those by Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff (1999) and Marquet (2007) that used a factorizable impact parameter dependence, which forces the qq̄g component to have the same b-dependence as the qq̄ component and does not approach the correct unitarity limit for b ≠ 0. The results enable predictions for future electron-ion colliders (EIC, LHeC) and show that measuring intact recoil nuclei at small t is experimentally challenging but would provide tests of high-energy QCD.
