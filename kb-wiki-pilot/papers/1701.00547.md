# Comprehensive analysis of large $α$ yields observed in $^{6}$Li induced reactions

> Background: Large $α$ yields have been reported over the years in reactions with $^{6}$Li and $^{7}$Li projectiles. Previous theoretical analyses have shown that the elastic breakup (EBU) mechanism (i.e., projectile breakup leaving the target in its ground state) is able to account only for a small fraction of the total $α$ inclusive breakup cross sections, pointing toward the dominance of non-elastic breakup (NEB) mechanisms. Purpose: We aim to provide a systematic study of the $α$ inclusive cross sections observed in nuclear reactions induced by $^{6}$Li projectiles. In addition to estimating the total $α$ singles cross sections, it is our goal to evaluate angular and energy distributions of these $α$ particles and compare with experimental data, when available. Method: We compute separately the EBU and NEB components of the inclusive breakup cross sections. For the former, we use the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, which treats this mechanism to all orders. For the NEB part, we employ the the model proposed in the eighties by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 432 (1982)], within the DWBA approximation. Results: Overall, the sum of the computed EBU and NEB cross sections is found to reproduce very well the measured singles cross sections. In all cases analyzed, we find that the inclusive breakup cross section is largely dominated by the NEB component. Conclusions: The presented method provides a global and systematic description of inclusive breakup reactions induced by $^{6}$Li projectiles. It provides also a natural explanation of the previously observed underestimation of the measured $α$ yields by CDCC calculations. The method used here can be extended to other weakly-bound projectiles, including halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The sum of elastic breakup (EBU) and non-elastic breakup (NEB) cross sections, computed separately using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method and the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model within the DWBA approximation respectively, reproduces measured alpha singles cross sections very well; the previously observed underestimation of alpha yields by CDCC calculations alone is naturally explained because the inclusive breakup cross section is largely dominated by the NEB component in all cases analyzed.

## Method

The authors computed the elastic breakup (EBU) component using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, which treats breakup as an inelastic excitation to continuum states of the projectile to all orders. For the non-elastic breakup (NEB) component, they employed the model of Ichimura, Austern and Vincent (IAV) within the DWBA approximation, using a two-cluster (alpha+d) model of the 6Li projectile with alpha as the observed spectator and deuteron as the participant. The EBU and NEB contributions were summed to obtain the total inclusive breakup cross section and compared with experimental angular distributions of alpha particles for reactions on 208Pb, 159Tb, and 118Sn targets at various incident energies.

## Key numbers

- 6Li separation energy into alpha+d is 1.474 MeV (compared to single nucleon separation energy of 5.39 MeV)
- For 208Pb(6Li,alpha X) reaction: data compared at incident energies between 29 and 39 MeV; nominal Coulomb barrier for this system is around 29.5 MeV
- For 159Tb(6Li,alpha X) reaction: data compared at incident energies between 23 and 35 MeV
- For 118Sn(6Li,alpha X) reaction: data compared at incident energies between 18 and 24 MeV
- 7Li breakup threshold (for process involving neutron pickup) is 2.468 MeV

## Context

This work extends the IAV model, originally proposed in the 1980s by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 432 (1982)] and recently revisited by several groups (Jin15, Jin15b, Jin16, Car15, Pot15), to provide a systematic study of alpha inclusive cross sections from 6Li-induced reactions. It directly addresses the long-standing puzzle that CDCC calculations, which successfully reproduce coincidence alpha+d measurements (Signorini03), largely underestimate inclusive alpha cross sections. The method enables a unified description of inclusive breakup reactions for weakly-bound projectiles and can be extended to other systems including halo nuclei, and provides a framework that subsumes the separate processes (non-capture breakup, deuteron incomplete fusion, single-nucleon stripping, and neutron pickup) previously treated individually by Pradhan et al. (Pra13).
