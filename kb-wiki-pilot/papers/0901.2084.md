# $J/ψ$ suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> At high temperatures, strongly interacting matter becomes a plasma of deconfined quarks and gluons. In statistical QCD, deconfinement and the properties of the resulting quark-gluon plasma can be investigated by studying the in-medium behaviour of heavy quark bound states. In high energy nuclear interactions, quarkonia probe different aspects of the medium formed in the collision. So, we first reviewed the fate of quarkonia in the different stages of the (dynamical) system produced at the collision. We have then presented our present work on the dissociation of the heavy quarkonium states in a hot QCD medium by investigating the medium modifications to heavy quark potential. In contrast to the usual screening picture, interestingly our theory gives rise the screening of the charge, not the range of the potential.

## Key claim

The authors propose that in-medium modifications to the heavy quark potential lead to screening of the color charge (effective charge) rather than screening of the range of the potential (Debye screening), which is the usual picture; this result depends on retaining the full Cornell potential (including the string term) above the critical temperature and using a dielectric function approach.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium heavy quark potential by taking the Fourier transform of the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear string term) and dividing by a dielectric permittivity that depends on the Debye mass, then performing the inverse Fourier transform. They solve the Schrodinger equation using the resulting long-range Coulombic potential (with effective charge \(2\sigma/m_D^2\)) to obtain binding energies for charmonium and bottomonium states, and determine dissociation temperatures by requiring the binding energy to equal the mean thermal energy \(3T\). Three forms of the Debye mass are employed: leading-order perturbative (\(m_D^{\rm LO}\)), non-perturbative (\(m_D^{\rm NP}\)), and a lattice-parametrized form (\(m_D^L\)).

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of \(J/\psi\) at zero temperature: approximately 640 MeV
* String tension used: \(\sigma = 0.184\ {\rm GeV}^2\)
* Dissociation temperatures for \(J/\psi\) with leading-order Debye mass: \(1.1\,T_c\) (pure QCD), \(1.3\,T_c\) (2-flavor), \(1.2\,T_c\) (3-flavor)
* Dissociation temperatures for \(J/\psi\) with lattice-parametrized Debye mass: \(0.8\,T_c\) (pure QCD), \(0.9\,T_c\) (2-flavor), \(0.9\,T_c\) (3-flavor)
* Dissociation temperatures for \(\Upsilon\) with leading-order Debye mass: \(1.4\,T_c\) (pure QCD), \(1.7\,T_c\) (2-flavor), \(1.6\,T_c\) (3-flavor)
* Dissociation temperatures for \(\Upsilon\) with lattice-parametrized Debye mass: \(1.0\,T_c\) (pure QCD), \(1.2\,T_c\) (2-flavor), \(1.2\,T_c\) (3-flavor)
* Critical temperatures used: 270 MeV (pure gluonic), 203 MeV (2-flavor), 197 MeV (3-flavor)
* Coefficients for non-perturbative Debye mass in SU(3): \(c_N = 2.46 \pm 0.15\), \(b_N = -0.49 \pm 0.15\)

## Context

This work extends the sequential suppression model of Matsui and Satz (1986) and the potential-model approach to quarkonium dissociation, but differs from standard treatments by retaining the full Cornell potential above \(T_c\) rather than only the Coulomb part. The results are compared with lattice QCD studies of spectral functions by Mocsy and Petreczky and with other potential-model calculations by Satz and by Wong. The authors note that their dissociation temperatures are reasonably close to the findings of Mocsy and Petreczky but significantly smaller than predictions by Satz and by Alberico et al. The work enables further study of flavor dependence (pure gluonic, 2-flavor, 3-flavor) in dissociation patterns, a perspective not yet achieved by lattice spectral function studies.
