# An overview of $XYZ$ new particles

> In the past decade, more and more charmoniumlike and bottomoniumlike states have been reported in experiments, which have led us to extensive discussions on the underlying structure of these states. In this review paper, we briefly summarize the experimental and theoretical status of these observed states.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the experimental and theoretical status of 23 observed charmoniumlike and bottomoniumlike $XYZ$ states, but emphasizes that many of these states lack consistent confirmation across experiments and that their underlying structures remain ambiguous, with multiple competing explanations (molecular states, tetraquarks, hybrids, conventional charmonia) that often contradict each other or fail to match all observed decay patterns.

## Method

The paper categorizes the $XYZ$ states into five groups based on their production mechanisms: $B$ meson decay, $e^+e^-$ annihilation, double charm production, $\gamma\gamma$ fusion, and hidden-charm/bottom dipion and open-charm decays of higher charmonia/bottomonia. For each category, the author reviews the experimental observations (masses, widths, decay channels) and the corresponding theoretical interpretations proposed in the literature, including molecular state, tetraquark, hybrid charmonium, and conventional charmonium assignments, along with dynamical calculations using one-pion-exchange and one-boson-exchange potentials.

## Key numbers

- Number of observed $XYZ$ states: 23
- $X(3872)$ mass: average value near the $D^0D^{*0}$ threshold (3871.69 MeV); mass difference between $\chi_{c1}^\prime$ and $X(3872)$ is $50-200$ MeV
- $X(3872)$: $BR[X(3872)\to \gamma J/\psi] / BR[X(3872)\to J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-] = 0.14\pm0.05$ (Belle) and $\approx 0.25$ (BaBar); molecular picture prediction was $7\times 10^{-3}$
- $X(3872)$: $BR[X(3872)\to D^0\bar{D}^0\pi^0] / BR[X(3872)\to \pi^+\pi^- J/\psi] = 9.4^{+3.6}_{-4.3}$; molecular prediction was $0.054$
- $X(3872)$: $BR[B^0\to X(3872)K^0] / BR[B^+\to X(3872)K^+] \approx 1.62$; molecule prediction was much smaller
- $X(3872)$: $BR(X(3872)\to \psi^\prime\gamma) / BR(X(3872)\to J/\psi\gamma) = 3.4\pm 1.4$ (BaBar)
- $X(3872)$: lattice $DD^*$ scattering length $a_0 = -1.7 \pm 0.4$ fm, effective range $r_0 = 0.5 \pm 0.1$ fm
- $Y(4140)$: mass $4143.0\pm2.9(\mathrm{stat})\pm1.2(\mathrm{syst})$ MeV, width $11.7^{+8.3}_{-5.0}(\mathrm{stat})\pm 3.7(\mathrm{syst})$ MeV
- $Y(3940)$: mass $3943\pm11(\mathrm{stat})\pm13(\mathrm{syst})$ MeV, width $87\pm 22(\mathrm{stat})\pm 26(\mathrm{syst})$ MeV
- $Z^+(4430)$: mass $4433\pm4\pm2$ MeV, width $45^{+18+30}_{-13-13}$ MeV
- $Z^+(4051)$: mass $4051\pm14^{+20}_{-41}$ MeV, width $82^{+21+47}_{-17-22}$ MeV
- $Z^+(4248)$: mass $4248^{+44+180}_{-29-35}$ MeV, width $177^{+54+316}_{-39-61}$ MeV
- $X(3940)$: mass $3942^{+7}_{-6}\pm6$ MeV, width $37^{+26}_{-15}\pm8$ MeV
- $X(4160)$: mass $4156^{+25}_{-20}\pm15$ MeV, width $139^{+111}_{-61}\pm21$ MeV
- $Y(4260)$: mass values from different experiments range from $4245\pm5\pm4$ MeV (BaBar) to $4295\pm10^{+10}_{-3}$ MeV (Belle); widths range from $73^{+39}_{-25}\pm5$ MeV (CLEO) to $134.1\pm16.4\pm5.5$ MeV (Belle)
- $Y(4630)$ and $Y(4660)$: possible same state with mass $4660.7\pm 8.7$ MeV and width $61\pm23$ MeV (Cotugno et al. reanalysis)
- CMS reported additional enhancement at mass $4313.8\pm5.3\pm7.3$ MeV, width $38^{+30}_{-15}\pm16$ MeV; D$\varnothing$ reported enhancement at $4328.5\pm12.0$ MeV

## Context

This review extends the ongoing discussion of exotic hadron candidates that began with the discovery of $X(3872)$ by Belle in 2003, and covers the subsequent observations by BaBar, CDF, D$\varnothing$, LHCb, CMS, BESIII, and CLEO. It summarizes the theoretical landscape including molecular state models (Swanson, Wong, Tornqvist, Liu, Thomas and Close, Li and Zhu), tetraquark interpretations (Maiani et al.), hybrid charmonium proposals (Zhu, Close, Kou), and conventional charmonium assignments (Li and Chao with screened potential, Kalashnikova with coupled-channel model). The paper enables future experimental confirmation of disputed states (e.g., $Z^+(4430)$, $Z^+(4051)$, $Z^+(4248)$ not confirmed by BaBar; $Y(4140)$ initially not seen by LHCb but later confirmed by D$\varnothing$ and CMS) and further theoretical work to distinguish between molecular, tetraquark, hybrid, and conventional charmonium explanations.
