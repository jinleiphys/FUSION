# Core excitation in Coulomb breakup reactions

> Within the pure Coulomb breakup mechanism, we investigate the one-neutron removal reaction of the type A(a,b$γ$)X with $^{11}$Be and $^{19}$C projectiles on a heavy target nucleus $^{208}$Pb at the beam energy of 60 MeV/nucleon. Our intention is to examine the prospective of using these reactions to study the structure of neutron rich nuclei. Integrated partial cross sections and momentum distributions for the ground as well as excited bound states of core nuclei are calculated within the finite range distorted wave Born approximation as well as within the adiabatic model of the Coulomb breakup. Our results are compared with those obtained in the studies of the reactions on a light target where the breakup proceeds via the pure nuclear mechanism. We find that the transitions to excited states of the core are quite weak in the Coulomb dominated process as compared to the pure nuclear breakup.

## Key claim
The paper investigates whether Coulomb breakup reactions on heavy targets can be used to study core-excited states of neutron-rich nuclei, finding that transitions to excited core states are "quite weak" in the pure Coulomb-dominated process compared to pure nuclear breakup, a result the abstract states without quantifying that the excited-state cross sections are less than 1-2% of the ground-state cross section.

## Method
The authors calculate integrated partial cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions for one-neutron removal from ¹¹Be and ¹⁹C projectiles on a ²⁰⁸Pb target at 60 MeV/nucleon, using both the finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) and the adiabatic model of Coulomb breakup. The projectile ground state is described as a valence neutron coupled to a specific core state with spectroscopic factors taken from the literature, and the neutron single-particle wave function is calculated in a Woods-Saxon well whose depth is adjusted to reproduce the one-neutron separation energy for each core state.

## Key numbers
- For ¹¹Be → ¹⁰Be: ground state (0⁺) partial cross section = 1137.00 mb (C²S = 0.74); sum of excited states (2⁺, 1⁻, 2⁻) = 3.31 mb, which is less than 1% of the ground-state cross section
- For ¹⁹C → ¹⁸C: ground state (0⁺) partial cross section = 576.1 mb (C²S = 0.58); sum of excited states = 11.37 mb, about 2% of the ground-state cross section
- Full width at half maximum of the longitudinal momentum distribution for the ¹⁰Be ground state = 44 MeV/c, consistent with the experimental value of 47.5 ± 6 MeV/c from measurements on a ⁹Be target
- For the ¹⁴B → ¹³B(g.s.) reaction on ¹⁹⁷Au at 60 MeV/nucleon, the calculated pure Coulomb cross section is 401 mb, compared to the experimental value of 638 ± 45 mb, suggesting nuclear and interference contributions of 40-50%

## Context
This work extends the study of (a,bγ) reactions, previously performed on light ⁹Be targets where nuclear breakup dominates, to heavy ²⁰⁸Pb targets where pure Coulomb breakup can be isolated. The results contrast with measurements on light targets (e.g., Navin et al. 1998, Aumann et al. 2000) where excited core states represent about 22% of the total cross section for ¹¹Be. The calculations use the same spectroscopic factors from Warburton and Brown (1992) as those earlier nuclear-breakup studies, and the finding that Coulomb breakup strongly suppresses excited-state transitions supports the experimental observation by Guillemaud-Mueller et al. (2000) that no core-excited transitions were seen in ¹⁴B breakup on ¹⁹⁷Au. The work enables future development of a full theory combining Coulomb and nuclear breakup contributions on an equal footing.
