# Reaction dynamics for fusion of weakly-bound nuclei

> We discuss several open problems of fusion reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei. For this purpose, we solve a one dimensional three-body Hamiltonian with the coupled-channels formalism. We show that the continuum-continuum couplings substantially reduce the total fusion probability at energies above the barrier compared with the no-breakup case, although the fusion probability remains enhanced at subbarrier energies. We then discuss a role of transfer process in fusion of weakly bound nuclei, and point out that removing spurious Pauli forbidden transfer components from the calculation may be crucial at energies below the barrier. Calculations based on the three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method are also presented in order to discuss how to model complete fusion process.

## Key claim

The paper argues that continuum-continuum couplings substantially reduce total fusion probability at energies above the barrier compared with the no-breakup case, although fusion probability remains enhanced at subbarrier energies; however, the authors qualify that their main conclusions for complete fusion remain valid only at least in a qualitative way when more refined calculations are performed.

## Method

The authors solve a one-dimensional three-body Hamiltonian using the coupled-channels formalism, discretizing the continuum states of the projectile nucleus on an orthogonal polynomial basis. They also employ a three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method to model complete fusion processes, solving two-dimensional classical Newtonian equations for the time evolution of projectile fragments. The coupled-channels calculations include up to seven discretized continuum states together with the ground state, and the modified log-derivative method is used to obtain numerically stable solutions at low energies where some continuum channels are closed.

## Key numbers

- Energy eigenvalues for the first five states of the projectile Hamiltonian: epsilon_0 = -26.09 MeV, epsilon_1 = -11.42 MeV, epsilon_2 = -0.515 MeV, epsilon_3 = 0.135 MeV, epsilon_4 = 0.193 MeV
- Five bound states in the target nucleus at -40.5, -31.9, -20.6, -8.85 and -0.357 MeV
- Absorption radius for projectile fragments: r_abs = 1.1 x (A_T^{1/3} + A_{P_i}^{1/3})
- Polynomial basis used up to n=28 for diagonalization of the projectile Hamiltonian
- Angular momentum up to l=70 required in time-dependent wave packet approach for convergence

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channels calculations by Hagino, Vitturi, Dasso, and Lenzi (Phys. Rev. C 61, 037602, 2000) that did not include couplings among continuum channels, and addresses the contradictory predictions between the coupled-channels approach and the time-dependent wave packet approach of Yabana and Suzuki (Nucl. Phys. A588, 99c, 1995). The paper also builds on the work of Diaz-Torres and Thompson (Phys. Rev. C 65, 024606, 2002) regarding continuum-continuum couplings, and enables future studies that combine breakup and transfer couplings in a consistent quantum mechanical framework while properly excluding Pauli forbidden channels.
