# Examination of the adiabatic approximation for $(d,p)$ reactions

> Background: Deuteron-induced one-neutron transfer reactions have been used to extract single-particle properties of nuclei, and the adiabatic (AD) approximation is often used to simply treat the deuteron breakup states. Purpose: The primary goal is to examine the validity of the AD approximation for the ($d,p$) reaction systematically. We clarify also the role of the closed-channels often ignored in the description of breakup reactions. Methods: We calculate the ($d,p$) cross sections with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for 128 reactions systems and compare the results with those obtained by the CDCC calculation with the AD approximation. Effect of the closed channels are investigated by ignoring the closed channels in CDCC. Results: The AD approximation affects in general the ($d,p$) cross section by less than 20 %, but some exceptional nonadiabatic cases for which the AD approximation breaks down are found. The closed channels turn out to give significant effects on the cross section at deuteron energies less than about 10 MeV. Conclusions: The use of the AD approximation in the description of the ($d,p$) reaction can be justified in many cases, with the uncertainty of less than about 20 %. The existence of some nonadiabatic cases nevertheless should be realized. The neglect of the closed channels without confirming the convergence of the CDCC result is not recommended.

## Key claim

The adiabatic (AD) approximation affects the (d,p) cross section by less than 20% in general, but some exceptional nonadiabatic cases exist where the approximation breaks down; the uncertainty of the AD approximation is about 20% or less for many systems, but the existence of nonadiabatic cases should be recognized.

## Method

The authors calculated (d,p) cross sections using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for 128 reaction systems, varying four target nuclei (20Ne, 40Ca, 100Zr, 200Hg), four deuteron incident energies (5, 10, 20, 40 MeV), four transferred angular momenta (ℓ_f = 0, 1, 2, 3), and two neutron separation energies (S_n = 0.1 MeV and 8.0 MeV). They compared CDCC results with CDCC calculations using the adiabatic approximation (CDCC-AD), where all p-n continuum states were made degenerate with the deuteron ground state energy. The effect of closed channels was investigated by performing CDCC calculations with a reduced maximum p-n linear momentum determined by the incident energy.

## Key numbers

- The AD approximation affects the (d,p) cross section by less than 20% in general, and by about 35% at most
- For S_n = 8 MeV and E_d ≥ 20 MeV, the AD approximation typically works with about 10% error (e.g., S_AD = 0.87 for 40Ca(d,p)41Ca at 40 MeV)
- For S_n = 0.1 MeV and E_d = 5 MeV with ℓ_f = 0, S_AD values ranged from 0.71 (20Ne) to 1.08 (40Ca)
- The deuteron elastic breakup cross section with CDCC-AD was 107 mb versus 73 mb with CDCC for 40Ca(d,p)41Ca at 40 MeV with S_n = 0.1 MeV
- Closed channels give significant effects on the cross section at deuteron energies less than about 10 MeV
- For E_d ≥ 20 MeV, closed channels have no significant effect (S_OP ∼ 1), but in some cases affect results by more than 10%

## Context

This work systematically extends previous numerical tests of the adiabatic distorted-wave approximation (ADWA) that were done for only a few reaction systems (e.g., Refs. [ND11, Upa12, PM14]). The study uses CDCC as a three-body reaction model, which has theoretical foundation in the distorted-wave Faddeev formalism [Aus89, Aus96] and is a good approximation to the Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (FAGS) theory [Fad60, Alt67]. The results enable practitioners to understand when the AD approximation can be safely used for (d,p) reactions and when full CDCC calculations are necessary, particularly at low incident energies and for systems with small neutron separation energy.
