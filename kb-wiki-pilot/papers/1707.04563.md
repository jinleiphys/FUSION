# Assessing the foundation of the Trojan Horse Method

> We discuss the foundation of the Trojan Horse Method (THM) within the Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) theory. We demonstrate that the direct part of the INEB cross section, which is of two-step character, becomes, in the DWBA limit of the three-body theory with appropriate approximations and redefinitions, similar in structure to the one-step THM cross section. We also discuss the connection of the THM to the Surrogate Method (SM), which is a genuine two-step process.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Trojan Horse Method (THM) cross section, which is typically treated as a one-step direct process, can be rigorously derived from the two-step Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) theory in the DWBA limit, but only after several approximations that must be assessed in future work. The authors show that the direct part of the INEB cross section "collapses" from a two-step into a one-step process when using the Hussein-McVoy (HM) source function rather than the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formulation.

## Method

The authors analyze the THM within the framework of the Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) theory developed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV) and by Hussein and McVoy (HM). They decompose the imaginary part of the optical potential for the interacting fragment x into direct (D) and compound nucleus (CN) components, then extract the exclusive cross section for the process a + A → b + y + B by isolating the direct part. The derivation uses the post-form DWBA approximation of the three-body scattering wave function and compares the IAV and HM formulations to show their equivalence for the direct process.

## Key numbers

- The INEB cross section is given by d²σᵢᴺᴱᴮ_b/(dE_b dΩ_b) = σ̂_Rˣ × ρ_b(E_b), where σ̂_Rˣ is the total reaction cross section of fragment x and ρ_b(E_b) = μ_b k_b/[(2π)³ℏ³] is the density of states of spectator b
- The THM cross section factorizes as σ_THM(a+A → b+y+B) = K_THM × |φ(k_b)|² × σ(x+A → y+B), where K_THM is a kinematic factor and φ(k_b) is the momentum-space internal wave function of the Trojan horse projectile a
- The direct part of the IAV cross section equals the HM direct part: σ_(IAV, D, 2-step) = σ_(HM, D, 1-step)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation of the Trojan Horse Method by connecting it to the established Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup theory of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV, 1985) and Hussein and McVoy (HM, 1985). The paper also discusses the relationship between THM and the Surrogate Method (SM), which is a genuine two-step compound nucleus process used for neutron-induced reactions on actinides like ²³⁸U and ²³²Th (Escher et al., 2006, 2012, 2016). The analysis enables potential improvements to the THM by accounting for the principal part of the Green's function that is neglected in the standard one-step approximation, and provides a framework for assessing the validity of the approximations required to reduce the two-step INEB process to the one-step THM cross section.
