# Systematics of capture and fusion dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> We perform a systematic study of capture excitation functions by using an empirical coupled-channel model. In this model, a barrier distribution is used to take effectively into account the effects of couplings between the relative motion and intrinsic degrees of freedom. The shape of the barrier distribution is of an asymmetric Gaussian form. The effect of neutron transfer channels is also included in the barrier distribution. Based on the interaction potential between the projectile and the target, empirical formulas are proposed to determine the parameters of the barrier distribution. Theoretical estimates for barrier distributions and calculated capture cross sections together with experimental cross sections of 220 reaction systems with $182 \leqslant Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \leqslant 1640$ are tabulated. The results show that our empirical formulas work quite well in the energy region around the Coulomb barrier. This model can provide prediction of capture cross sections for the synthesis of superheavy nuclei as well as valuable information on capture and fusion dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors propose an empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model that uses an asymmetric Gaussian barrier distribution to effectively account for couplings between relative motion and intrinsic degrees of freedom, including neutron transfer channels, and demonstrate that this model can systematically describe capture excitation functions for 220 reaction systems with product charges \(182 \leqslant Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \leqslant 1640\) in the energy region around the Coulomb barrier, though they note there is no proof or mathematical derivation of the barrier distribution approach from the coupled Schrodinger equations.

## Method

The capture cross section is calculated as a sum over partial waves using the Hill-Wheeler formula for penetration probability, with a barrier distribution \(f(B)\) of asymmetric Gaussian form (left width \(\varDelta_1\), right width \(\varDelta_2\), central value \(B_{\rm m}\)) integrated over barrier heights. The interaction potential includes a deformed Woods-Saxon nuclear potential and Coulomb potential with quadrupole deformations, and the barrier distribution parameters are determined empirically from the static and dynamical deformations of the colliding nuclei, with the effect of positive \(Q\)-value neutron transfer channels included by broadening the widths. For 89 systems with positive \(Q(2n)\), the widths are increased by \(g Q(2n)\) with \(g = 0.32\), and the parameters \(a, b, c\) in Eqs. (disp) and (disp_d) are set separately for spherical and deformed systems depending on whether \(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} < 1150\) or \(\ge 1150\).

## Key numbers

- 220 reaction systems with \(182 \leqslant Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \leqslant 1640\) are studied
- 89 of these systems have positive \(Q\) value for one neutron pair transfer channel
- For spherical systems: \(a = 0.26\) (\(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} < 1150\)) or \(a = 0.5\) (\(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \ge 1150\)), \(b = 0.32\), \(c = 0.93\)
- For deformed systems: \(a = 0.23\) (\(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} < 1150\)) or \(a = 0.37\) (\(Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \ge 1150\)), \(b = 0.12\), \(c = 1.12\)
- For the reaction \(^{48}{\rm Ca}+{}^{208}{\rm Pb}\): \(V_{\rm B}^{\rm Sp} = 180.2\) MeV, \(V_{\rm B}^{\rm S} = 163.6\) MeV, difference 16.6 MeV, deformation energy at saddle point 12.44 MeV
- Nuclear potential parameters: \(V_0 = 80\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.16\) fm, diffuseness \(a\) given by Eq. (pot_para)
- Fitting uses a \(\chi^2\) merit function with assumed 3% experimental error

## Context

This work extends empirical coupled-channel approaches that use barrier distributions to model fusion enhancement at sub-barrier energies, following earlier models by Zagrebaev (2001, 2004) who assumed an asymmetric Gaussian shape, and Liu (2006) who used a superposition of two Gaussians. The systematic study of 220 systems provides empirical formulas for barrier distribution parameters based on static and dynamical deformations, enabling predictions of capture cross sections for superheavy nucleus synthesis where full coupled-channel calculations become intractable due to the large number of channels required.
