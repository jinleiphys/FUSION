# Probing nucleon-nucleon interactions in breakup of a one-neutron halo $^{11}$Be on a proton target

> A comparison between full few-body Faddeev/Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (Faddeev/AGS) and continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations is made for the resonant and nonresonant breakup of $^{11}$Be on proton target at 63.7 MeV/u incident energy. A simplified two-body model is used for $^{11}$Be which involves an inert $^{10}$Be(0$^+$) core and a valence neutron. The sensitivity of the calculated observables to the nucleon-nucleon potential dynamical input is analysed. We show that with the present NN and N-core dynamics the results remain a puzzle for the few-body problem of scattering from light exotic halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that breakup observables for p+$^{11}$Be at 63.7 MeV/u are very sensitive to the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential input, particularly the P-wave components, but even with realistic NN potentials the Faddeev/AGS calculations still underestimate the measured breakup angular distribution around the resonance by about 40%, leaving the results "a puzzle for the few-body problem of scattering from light exotic halo nuclei."

## Method

Full few-body Faddeev/Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (Faddeev/AGS) calculations were solved exactly in momentum space after partial wave decomposition, including nuclear interactions between all three pairs and Coulomb interaction between the proton and $^{10}$Be. Continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations were performed using the binning method to discretize the n-$^{10}$Be continuum up to 40 MeV excitation energy. Both frameworks used a simplified two-body model of $^{11}$Be as an inert $^{10}$Be(0$^+$) core plus a valence neutron, and compared results using realistic NN potentials (CD Bonn, AV18) against simple L-independent Gaussian potentials with parameters fitted to the deuteron binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 63.7 MeV/u for $^{11}$Be on proton target
- Resonance energy: $E_{\rm r} = 1.275$ MeV for the n-$^{10}$Be relative energy
- Energy integration ranges: $E_{\rm rel} = 0-2.5$ MeV (around resonance) and $E_{\rm rel} = 2.5-5.0$ MeV (higher relative energies)
- Gaussian potential parameters: $v_0=72.15$ MeV, $r_0=1.484$ fm, fitted to deuteron binding energy and low-energy $^3S_1$ phase-shifts
- Faddeev/AGS calculations included n-p partial waves with $L \leq 6$, p-$^{10}$Be with $L \leq 21$, n-$^{10}$Be with $L \leq 6$, and three-body total angular momentum up to 40
- CDCC calculations used n-$^{10}$Be partial waves $L \leq 4$, maximum excitation energy 40 MeV, total angular momentum up to 60, matching radius 60 fm
- Faddeev/AGS with CD Bonn underestimates data at $\theta_{\rm c.m.}=20^\circ$ by about 40%
- Using a proton-core optical potential that fits elastic data increases the calculated observable at $\theta_{\rm c.m.}=20^\circ$ by less than 10%

## Context

This work extends previous benchmark calculations between Faddeev/AGS and CDCC frameworks (Deltuva et al., Phys. Rev. C 76, 064602, 2007) and earlier comparisons of Faddeev/AGS with experimental data from MSU (Shrivastava et al., Phys. Lett. B 596, 54, 2004; Cravo et al., Phys. Rev. C 79, 064610, 2009). The present study achieves better agreement between Faddeev/AGS and CDCC than the earlier benchmark, attributed to studying regions dominated by small n-core relative energies and angular momenta where CDCC converges faster. The results enable future work to understand the 40% discrepancy with data and motivate extensions of CDCC codes to incorporate realistic NN potentials for extracting physically meaningful information from nucleon-nucleus scattering data.
