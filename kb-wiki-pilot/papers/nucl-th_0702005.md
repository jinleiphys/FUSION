# First Order Relativistic Three-Body Scattering

> Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies are formulated in momentum space and in first order in the two-body transition-operator directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance is incorporated within the framework of Poincare invariant quantum mechanics, and presented in some detail. Based on a Malfliet-Tjon type interaction, observables for elastic and break-up scattering are calculated up to projectile energies of 1 GeV. The influence of kinematic and dynamic relativistic effects on those observables is systematically studied. Approximations to the two-body interaction embedded in the three-particle space are compared to the exact treatment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic three-body scattering calculations can be performed at projectile energies up to 1 GeV laboratory kinetic energy by using direct integration methods that avoid partial wave decomposition, but the results are limited to first order in the two-body transition operator and use spin-independent (three-boson) interactions as a simplification.

## Method

The authors formulate relativistic Faddeev equations in momentum space within the framework of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics, solving them directly in terms of momentum vectors without partial wave decomposition. They employ a Malfliet-Tjon type Yukawa interaction and use the Kamada-Glöckle approach, which applies a unitary rescaling of momentum variables to make the nonrelativistic and relativistic two-body interactions phase-shift equivalent. The calculations are performed for the first order term of the Faddeev multiple scattering series, comparing different approximations to the two-body interaction embedded in the three-particle space against the exact treatment.

## Key numbers

- Projectile energies up to 1 GeV laboratory kinetic energy are considered
- Below about 200 MeV projectile energy, non-relativistic momentum-space Faddeev equations can be solved with high accuracy for realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions
- Relativistic effects are quantitatively small for three-nucleon scattering below 200 MeV, with the exception of some breakup cross sections in certain phase space regions
- Calculations are performed for elastic and breakup processes in the intermediate energy regime from 0.2 to 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous momentum-space Faddeev calculations that were limited to low energies (below 200 MeV) and relied on partial wave decompositions, which become computationally prohibitive at higher energies. It builds on the first application of vector-variable Faddeev calculations for bound states (Ref. bound3d) and scattering at intermediate energies (Ref. scatter3d). The paper enables systematic study of relativistic effects in three-body scattering at energies where special relativity becomes relevant, and provides a framework for testing the limitations of nucleon-only models of nuclei as energy scales increase toward the pion production threshold.
