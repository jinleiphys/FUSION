# Eikonal Reaction Theory for Neutron-Removal Reaction

> We present an accurate method of treating the one-neutron removal reaction at intermediate incident energies induced by both nuclear and Coulomb interactions. In the method, the nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes are consistently treated by the method of continuum discretized coupled channels without making the adiabatic approximation to the Coulomb interaction, so that the removal cross section calculated never diverges. This method is applied to recently measured one-neutron removal cross section for $^{31}$Ne+$^{12}$C scattering at 230 MeV/nucleon and $^{31}$Ne+$^{208}$Pb scattering at 234 MeV/nucleon. The spectroscopic factor and the asymptotic normalization coefficient of the last neutron in $^{31}$Ne are evaluated.

# Eikonal Reaction Theory for Neutron-Removal Reaction

## Key claim

The authors present the Eikonal Reaction Theory (ERT) as an accurate method for treating one-neutron removal reactions at intermediate incident energies (around 200 MeV/nucleon) that consistently handles both nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes without the adiabatic approximation for the Coulomb interaction, thereby avoiding the divergence that plagues the standard Glauber model when Coulomb is included. The method is applied to deduce that the last neutron in $^{31}$Ne is predominantly in the $1p3/2$ orbital, but the spectroscopic factor and asymptotic normalization coefficient depend sensitively on the poorly-known one-neutron separation energy $B_n = 0.29 \pm 1.64$ MeV.

## Method

The three-body (core+neutron+target) Schrödinger equation is solved using the eikonal approximation, but the adiabatic approximation is applied only to the short-range nuclear interactions (neutron-target and core-target nuclear potentials) while the long-range Coulomb interaction is treated exactly via continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) without the adiabatic approximation. The S-matrix factorizes into a neutron part $S_n$ (calculated from the nuclear potential in the adiabatic approximation) and a core part $S_c$ (calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation for the core-target system using eikonal-CDCC). The one-neutron removal cross section $\sigma_{-n}$ is obtained as the sum of the elastic breakup cross section $\sigma_{bu}$ and the neutron-stripping cross section $\sigma_{str}$, computed from the $S$-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For $^{31}$Ne($1p3/2$) + $^{12}$C at 230 MeV/nucleon: $\sigma_{-n}^{th} = 114$ mb; spectroscopic factor $\mathcal{S} = 0.693$ (experimental $\sigma_{-n}^{exp} = 79$ mb)
- For $^{31}$Ne($1p3/2$) + $^{208}$Pb at 234 MeV/nucleon: $\sigma_{-n}^{th} = 1044$ mb; $\mathcal{S} = 0.682$ (experimental $\sigma_{-n}^{exp} = 712$ mb)
- For $^{31}$Ne($0f7/2$) + $^{12}$C: $\mathcal{S} = 2.47$; for $^{208}$Pb: $\mathcal{S} = 5.65$ (inconsistent with a physical spectroscopic factor less than 1)
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient for $1p3/2$ at $B_n = 0.33$ MeV: $C_{ANC} = 0.320 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.028$ fm$^{-1/2}$ ($^{12}$C target) and $0.318 \pm 0.008 \pm 0.029$ fm$^{-1/2}$ ($^{208}$Pb target)
- Error from the adiabatic approximation for nuclear potentials: 0.2% for $\sigma_R$, 1.9% for $\sigma_{bu}$, 4.1% for $\sigma_{str}$, and 3.3% for $\sigma_{-n}$

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model (Glauber, 1959) and the Coulomb-corrected eikonal model (CCE) of Capel, Baye, and Suzuki (2008) by providing a fully consistent treatment of Coulomb breakup without the adiabatic approximation that causes divergence in the removal cross section. It also extends CDCC (Kamimura et al., 1986; Austern et al., 1987) to inclusive reactions by combining it with the eikonal approximation. The method is applied to the $^{31}$Ne halo nucleus recently measured by Nakamura et al. (2009), and the results are compared with the Glauber model calculations of Horiuchi et al. (2010). The finding that $C_{ANC}$ has smaller theoretical uncertainty than $\mathcal{S}$ suggests a future strategy for spectroscopy of unstable nuclei using peripheral reactions.
