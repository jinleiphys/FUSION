# Coulomb breakup of $^6\rm{Li}$ into $α\,+\,\rm{d}$ in the field of ion $^{208}\rm{Pb}$

> The triple differential cross section of $^{208}\rm{Pb}(^6\rm{Li};α,\rm{d})^{208}\rm{Pb}$ elastic Coulomb breakup is calculated using the semiclassical method. We fit the parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential using the experimental $α-\rm{d}$ phase shifts for different states to describe the relative motion of $α$-particle and deuteron. In order to check the validity of the two particle approach for $α-d$ system we apply a potential model to describe the $^2\rm{H}(α,γ)^6\rm{Li}$ radiative capture. Our results for the Coulomb breakup of $^6\rm{Li}$ show large value of the forward-backward asymmetry of the $α$-particle and deuteron emission around zero energy in the $^6\rm{Li}$ center-of-mass (c.m.) system. Comparison of the results of our calculation with experimental data gives evidence for the dominance of the Coulomb dissociation mechanism at the $α-d$ relative energy larger than $E_{α\,d}>300\,\rm{keV}$ and sufficiently big contribution of nuclear distortion at $E_{α\,d}$ near zero energy, but essentially smaller than the value reported in Ref.[F. Hammache et al. Phys.Rev. C82, 065802 (2010)].

# Coulomb breakup of $^6\rm{Li}$ into $\alpha + d$ in the field of ion $^{208}\rm{Pb}$

## Key claim

The Coulomb dissociation mechanism dominates the $^{208}\rm{Pb}(^6\rm{Li},\alpha d)^{208}\rm{Pb}$ breakup at $\alpha-d$ relative energies above 300 keV, while nuclear distortion contributes significantly near zero relative energy but is substantially smaller than the contribution reported by Hammache et al. (Phys. Rev. C 82, 065802, 2010). The forward-backward asymmetry of $\alpha$-particle and deuteron emission around zero energy in the $^6\rm{Li}$ center-of-mass system is large.

## Method

The triple differential cross section for elastic Coulomb breakup of 156 MeV $^6\rm{Li}$ on $^{208}\rm{Pb}$ at laboratory scattering angles $2^\circ$-$6^\circ$ was calculated using semiclassical time-dependent perturbation theory for the Coulomb contribution and diffraction theory for the nuclear breakup contribution. Woods-Saxon potential parameters were fitted to experimental $\alpha-d$ phase shifts for $S$, $P$, and $D$ states, and the bound state wave function was adjusted via a phase-equivalent potential to yield an asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of 2.3 fm$^{-1/2}$. The $^2\rm{H}(\alpha,\gamma)^6\rm{Li}$ radiative capture was calculated using the same potential model to validate the two-particle approach.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: 156 MeV for the $^6\rm{Li}$ beam
- Scattering angle range: $2^\circ$ to $6^\circ$ in the laboratory frame
- Woods-Saxon potential depths: $V_0 = 60.73$ MeV for $^3S_1$ state; $V_0 = 57.0$ MeV, $V_{sl} = 4.0$ MeV for $P$ states; $V_0 = 55.9$ MeV, $V_{sl} = 4.0$ MeV for $^1D_1$ and $^2D_1$ states; $V_0 = 55.9$ MeV, $V_{sl} = 5.06$ MeV for $^3D_1$ state
- ANC of the $^6\rm{Li}$ ground state: 2.3 fm$^{-1/2}$ (used for breakup and capture calculations)
- Astrophysical $S$ factor at zero energy: $S(0) = 1.22$ MeV nb, with $S_{E1}(0) = 1.01$ MeV nb and $S_{E2}(0) = 0.21$ MeV nb
- Black disk radii $R_{bl}$ from $\chi^2$ fitting: 6.30 fm ($2^\circ$), 6.94 fm ($3^\circ$), 6.34 fm ($4^\circ$), 6.25 fm ($6^\circ$)
- At $E_{\alpha d} = 100$ keV, the $E1$ Coulomb breakup cross section is smaller than the $E2$ by a factor of 70; at $E_{\alpha d} = 10$ keV, the ratio is 1/24

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kiener et al. (Phys. Rev. C 44, 2195, 1991) by calculating triple differential cross sections at all four measured scattering angles ($2^\circ$, $3^\circ$, $4^\circ$, $6^\circ$) rather than only $3^\circ$, and by including both Coulomb and nuclear breakup contributions. It contradicts the claim of Hammache et al. (Phys. Rev. C 82, 065803, 2010) of a very large nuclear contribution, finding instead that nuclear breakup increases the total cross section by at most a factor of 2 above $E_{\alpha d} > 0.8$ MeV. The results enable improved extraction of the astrophysical $S$ factor for the $d(\alpha,\gamma)^6\rm{Li}$ reaction from Coulomb breakup data, though significant discrepancies remain near zero relative energy that may require inclusion of higher-order effects such as three-body Coulomb effects (Alt et al., 2003, 2005).
