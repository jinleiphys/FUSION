# Nuclear breakup of $^8$B in a direct fragmentation model

> We calculate the cross sections of the elastic and inelastic breakup modes for the inclusive breakup reaction $^{28}$Si($^8$B,$^7$Be) at beam energies between 10 - 40 MeV/nucleon within a direct fragmentation model formulated in the framework of the post form distorted-wave Born-approximation. In contrast to the case of the stable isotopes, the inelastic breakup mode is found to contribute only upto 30% to the total breakup cross section, which is in agreement with the recently measured experimental data. However, the high energy tail of the energy spectra of $^7$Be fragment is dominated by the inelastic breakup mode. The breakup amplitude is found to be dominated by the contributions from distrances well beyond the nuclear surface.

# Nuclear breakup of $^8$B in a direct fragmentation model

## Key claim

The inelastic breakup mode contributes only up to 30% of the total breakup cross section for $^{28}$Si($^8$B,$^7$Be) at 10-40 MeV/nucleon, in contrast to stable isotopes where it makes up 75-80%, but this result depends on the use of a constant range approximation within the post-form distorted-wave Born-approximation and on the specific optical potential parameters chosen for the $^8$B,$^7$Be + $^{28}$Si system.

## Method

The authors calculate elastic and inelastic breakup cross sections for the inclusive reaction $^{28}$Si($^8$B,$^7$Be) using a direct fragmentation model formulated in the post-form distorted-wave Born-approximation (PFDWBA). To handle the p-wave relative motion between $^7$Be and the proton in the $^8$B ground state, they introduce a constant range approximation (CRA) that reduces the six-dimensional transition amplitude to three dimensions while allowing non-zero orbital angular momentum values, unlike the zero-range approximation which would exclude the relevant p-state. The inelastic breakup amplitude is obtained using the surface approximation, where the form factor is expressed in terms of the elastic scattering wave function and Coulomb functions, with summation over all non-elastic channels performed via unitarity of the S-matrix.

## Key numbers

* Inelastic breakup contributes only up to 30% of the total breakup cross section for $^{28}$Si($^8$B,$^7$Be), compared to 75-80% for stable isotopes
* Breakup probability peaks at an impact parameter of about 8 fm, well beyond the sum of radii ($R_s \simeq$ 6 fm) of $^8$B and $^{28}$Si
* The $^8$B ground state wave function uses a $0p_{3/2}$ proton single-particle state with separation energy 0.137 MeV, giving $D_a$ = -39.0 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.8 fm
* Alternative wave function parameters from Barker ($r_0$ = 1.25 fm, $a_0$ = 0.65 fm) give $D_a$ = -40.4 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.8 fm; from Esbensen and Bertsch ($r_0$ = 1.25 fm, $a_0$ = 0.52 fm) give $D_a$ = -41.7 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.7 fm; from RPA models give $D_a$ = -38.0 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.8 fm
* Optical potential parameters used: $V_R$ = 123.0 MeV, $r_V$ = 0.75 fm, $a_V$ = 0.80 fm, $W$ = 65.0 MeV, $r_W$ = 0.78 fm, $a_W$ = 0.80 fm (Woods-Saxon volume terms)

## Context

This work extends the direct fragmentation model previously applied to neutron halo nuclei like $^{11}$Li and $^{11}$Be (Shyam et al., Nucl. Phys. A 540, 1992) to the proton-rich nucleus $^8$B, which is a candidate for having a proton halo structure due to its last proton binding energy of only 137 keV. The calculations improve upon semi-classical approaches such as the Serber-type model (Negoita et al., Phys. Rev. C 54, 1996) and the diffraction dissociation picture (Hencken et al., Phys. Rev. C 54, 1996) by providing a fully quantum mechanical treatment. The results are relevant for interpreting breakup data to probe the proton halo question, where conflicting evidence exists: Minamisono et al. found a large quadrupole moment suggesting extended proton distribution, while Nakada and Otsuka reproduced the quadrupole moment with shell model calculations without invoking a halo, and Kelley et al. argued that reaction mechanism rather than extended spatial distribution explains narrow momentum distributions. The work also has implications for extracting the astrophysical S-factor for $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B from Coulomb dissociation experiments.
