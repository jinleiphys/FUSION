# 6Li direct breakup lifetimes

> alpha-d coincidence data were studied for the 6Li + 59Co reaction at E(lab) = 29.6 MeV. By using a kinematic analysis, it was possible to identify which process, leading to the same final state, has the major contribution for each of the selected angular regions. Contributions of the 6Li sequential and direct breakup to the incomplete fusion/transfer process were discussed by considering the lifetimes obtained by using a semiclassical approach, for both breakup components.

# 6Li direct breakup lifetimes

## Key claim
The direct breakup (DBU) of 6Li into the continuum has lifetimes at least one order of magnitude shorter than the sequential breakup (SBU) lifetime of the 2.186 MeV 3+ resonant state, implying that DBU occurs much closer to the target nucleus and is therefore the dominant source of flux diverted from complete fusion to incomplete fusion/transfer processes.

## Method
Alpha-deuteron coincidence data were measured for the 6Li + 59Co reaction at Elab = 29.6 MeV using eleven triple telescopes covering angular intervals from -45° to -15° and 15° to 75°. A three-body kinematic analysis was applied to identify contributions from sequential breakup, direct breakup, and incomplete fusion/transfer processes for different angular regions. Lifetimes for the direct breakup continuum states were estimated using a semiclassical approach with barrier tunneling, adopting the model of Tokimoto et al., while the sequential breakup lifetime was taken from the known width of the 3+ resonant state.

## Key numbers
- Sequential breakup lifetime for the 6Li 3+ state at E* = 2.186 MeV: tau_SBU = 2.73 × 10^(-20) s, corresponding to Gamma_SBU = (0.024 ± 0.002) MeV
- Direct breakup lifetimes for continuum states are at least one order of magnitude shorter than the sequential breakup lifetime
- Beam energy: Elab = 29.6 MeV for 6Li on a 2.2 mg/cm^2 59Co target
- Intermediate nucleus excitation energies: 24.6 MeV for 61Ni (d-ICF/TR) and 22.5 MeV for 63Cu (alpha-ICF/TR)
- Sommerfeld parameter: eta ~ 6
- Direct breakup angular distributions summed over 6Li continuum excitation energies from E* = 1.66 MeV to 2.10 MeV, with additional ranges E* = 2.20 to 2.40 MeV and E* = 2.41 to 3.98 MeV also analyzed

## Context
This work extends previous singles-mode measurements for the 6Li + 59Co benchmark system (Beck et al., Souza et al. 2009) by using alpha-d coincidence data to disentangle sequential and direct breakup contributions from incomplete fusion/transfer processes. The semiclassical lifetime analysis follows the approach of Kanungo et al. (1996) and Tokimoto et al. (2001). The results indicate that direct breakup, rather than sequential breakup, is the primary mechanism diverting flux from complete fusion to incomplete fusion/transfer in reactions with weakly bound projectiles, consistent with findings by Santra et al. (2009) and providing guidance for future continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations.
