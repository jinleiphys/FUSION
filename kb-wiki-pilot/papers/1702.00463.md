# Subthreshold resonances and resonances in the $R$-matrix method for binary reactions and in the Trojan Horse method

> In this paper we discuss the $R$-matrix approach to treat the subthreshold resonances for the single-level and one channel, and for the single-level and two channel cases. In particular, the expression relating the ANC with the observable reduced width, when the subthreshold bound state is the only channel or coupled with an open channel, which is a resonance, is formulated. Since the ANC plays a very important role in nuclear astrophysics, these relations significantly enhance the power of the derived equations. We present the relationship between the resonance width and the ANC for the general case and consider two limiting cases: wide and narrow resonances. Different equations for the astrophysical S-factors in the $R$-matrix approach are presented. After that we discuss the Trojan Horse Method (THM) formalism. The developed equations are obtained using the surface-integral formalism and the generalized $R$-matrix approach for the three-body resonant reactions. It is shown how the Trojan Horse (TH) double differential cross section can be expressed in terms of the on-the-energy-shell astrophysical S-factor for the binary sub-reaction. Finally, we demonstrate how the THM can be used to calculate the astrophysical S-factor for the neutron generator $^{13}{\rm C}(α,\,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ in low-mass AGB stars. At astrophysically relevant energies this astrophysical S-factor is controlled by the threshold level $1/2^{+}, E_{x}= 6356$ keV. Here, we reanalyzed recent TH data taking into account more accurately the three-body effects and using both assumptions that the threshold level is a subthreshold bound state or it is a resonance state.

## Key claim

The paper derives new R-matrix equations connecting asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) with observable reduced widths for subthreshold resonances, both when the subthreshold bound state is the only channel and when it is coupled with an open resonance channel; these relations are then applied to reanalyze Trojan Horse Method data for the 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction, considering whether the threshold level at 6356 keV excitation energy is treated as a subthreshold bound state or as a resonance state.

## Method

The authors use the surface-integral formalism and generalized R-matrix approach to derive expressions for reaction amplitudes and astrophysical S-factors for binary reactions involving subthreshold resonances, considering single-channel/single-level and two-channel/single-level cases. They then extend this formalism to the Trojan Horse Method (THM) for three-body resonant reactions, expressing the TH double differential cross section in terms of the on-the-energy-shell astrophysical S-factor for the binary sub-reaction. The derived equations are applied to reanalyze recent TH data for the 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction, accounting for three-body effects more accurately than in previous analyses.

## Key numbers

- The threshold level in 17O controlling the astrophysical S-factor for 13C(alpha,n)16O is at excitation energy Ex = 6356 keV with spin-parity 1/2+
- The subthreshold bound state in 17O = (alpha 13C) lies at -3 keV relative to the alpha-13C threshold
- The observable partial resonance width of a subthreshold resonance is given by: Gamma_i(s)(E_i) = 2 P_i (gamma_i(s))^2, where P_i is the penetrability factor and gamma_i(s) is the observable reduced width
- The ANC for a subthreshold state is related to the observable reduced width by: [C_i(s)]^2 = (2 mu_i R_i (gamma_i(s))^2) / (W^2(-eta_i(s), l_i+1/2)(2 kappa_i(s) R_i)), where W is the Whittaker function
- For narrow resonances, the ANC is approximately: C_f^2 ≈ (mu_f Gamma_f(E_f(R))) / (k_f(R)) * exp(i[2 delta_lf(k_f(R)) - i pi l_f])
- The astrophysical S-factor formula includes the factor nu_N^2 = (0.2118 fm)^2 and E_N = 931.5 MeV as conversion constants

## Context

This work extends the classical R-matrix formalism of Lane and Thomas (1958) to specifically treat subthreshold resonances coupled with open channels, building on earlier work by Mukhamedzhanov et al. (1999) that established the relationship between ANCs and reduced widths for bound states. The paper also generalizes the ANC definition to real resonances following previous work (Izv. AN, ANC references). The derived THM equations provide a theoretical foundation for the Trojan Horse Method as reviewed in the review paper, enabling experimentalists to extract astrophysical S-factors for reactions like 13C(alpha,n)16O that are critical for understanding s-process nucleosynthesis in low-mass AGB stars. The formalism enables future analyses of subthreshold resonances in nuclear astrophysics where direct measurements are impossible due to Coulomb barrier suppression.
