# Two-photon final states in peripheral heavy ion collisions

> We discuss processes leading to two photon final states in peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC. Due to the large photon luminosity we show that the continuum subprocess $γγ\to γγ$ can be observed with a large number of events. We study this reaction when it is intermediated by a resonance made of quarks or gluons and discuss its interplay with the continuum process, verifying that in several cases the resonant process ovewhelms the continuum one. It is also investigated the possibility of observing a scalar resonance (the $σ$ meson) in this process. Assuming for the $σ$ the mass and total decay width values recently reported by the E791 Collaboration we show that RHIC may detect this particle in its two photon decay mode if its partial photonic decay width is of the order of the ones discussed in the literature.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the continuum process γγ → γγ can be observed with a large number of events in peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC due to the large photon luminosity, and that resonant processes (including a possible σ meson) can overwhelm the continuum in several cases. However, the observation of the σ meson in its two-photon decay mode is conditional on its partial photonic decay width being of the order of values discussed in the literature (3.8–10 keV), and the interference between the resonant and continuum processes is destructive for the scalar case.

## Method
The authors compute the cross section for γγ → γγ via the fermion box diagram (including leptons e, μ and quarks u, d, s) using the helicity amplitudes of Karplus and Neuman and De Tollis, folded with the two-photon luminosity from the Weizsäcker-Williams equivalent-photon approximation in impact parameter space using the Cahn-Jackson fit. Resonant production γγ → R → γγ is treated with a Breit-Wigner profile, and for the σ meson the full helicity amplitudes from an effective scalar Lagrangian are computed to study interference with the continuum. A cut |cos θ| < 0.5 is imposed on the scattering angle in the two-photon center-of-mass system.

## Key numbers
- For Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV/nucleon with luminosity 2.0 × 10²⁶ cm⁻²s⁻¹, choosing a 200 MeV bin centered at 700 MeV (free of strong resonances), the continuum process yields 1532 events/year assuming 100% efficiency.
- Continuum cross section is strongly peaked at |cos θ| ≈ 1 and relatively flat out to |cos θ| ≈ 0.4.
- The electron loop dominates the continuum; muon contribution is at least one order of magnitude smaller; d and s quark contributions are insignificant.
- Event rates above background: η (m=547.3 MeV, Γ_total=1.18 keV, Γ_γγ=0.46 keV) gives 7.44 × 10⁵ events/year; η′ (m=957.78 MeV, Γ_total=0.203 MeV, Γ_γγ=4.06 keV) gives 2.67 × 10⁴ events/year; f₀(1710) (m=1715 MeV, Γ_total=125 MeV, Γ_γγ=21.25 keV) gives 42 events/year.
- For the σ meson, using E791 values (m_σ=478 MeV, Γ_total=324 MeV) with Γ_γγ = 3.8, 4.7, or 10 keV, the resonant peak is comparable to or above the continuum, but destructive interference reduces the total cross section.

## Context
This work extends earlier proposals by Baur, Bertulani, and Natale for two-photon physics in peripheral heavy ion collisions, and specifically addresses the possibility of observing glueball candidates (η(1440), f₀(1710)) and the σ meson via their two-photon decays. The continuum γγ → γγ calculation follows Karplus and Neuman and De Tollis; the photon distribution uses the Cahn-Jackson fit. The σ meson parameters are taken from the E791 Collaboration (2001). The paper enables future experimental searches at RHIC for scalar and pseudoscalar resonances in the two-photon channel, complementing hadronic decay studies and potentially clarifying the quark/gluon content of light mesons.
