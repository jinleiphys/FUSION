# Time-dependent wave-packet approach for fusion reactions of halo nuclei

> The fusion reaction of a halo nucleus 11Be on 208Pb is described by a three-body direct reaction model. A time-dependent wave packet approach is applied to a three-body reaction problem. The wave packet approach enables us to obtain scattering solutions without considering the three-body scattering boundary conditions. The time evolution of the wave packet also helps us to obtain intuitive understanding of the reaction dynamics. The calculations indicate a decrease of the fusion probability by the presence of the halo neutron.

## Key claim

The fusion probability of a halo nucleus projectile is suppressed by the presence of the halo neutron, contrary to many other theoretical works that claimed an increase; this suppression is caused principally by the core-target Coulomb interaction, with the n-T nuclear potential playing only a minor role.

## Method

The authors applied a time-dependent wave-packet approach to a three-body direct reaction model (n + core + target) for the system 11Be on 208Pb, solving the time-dependent Schrodinger equation in the Jacobi coordinates of the incident channel. They performed a partial wave expansion up to l_max = 70 for zero total angular momentum (head-on collision), discretized the radial coordinates on a grid (0 < R < 50 fm, 0 < r < 60 fm), and used a complex C-T potential to absorb flux representing fusion. The fusion probability for a fixed incident energy was extracted by an energy projection procedure comparing the initial and final wave-packet energy distributions.

## Key numbers

*   The n-C potential strength was set to give the 2s orbital energy at -0.6 MeV, close to the neutron separation energy of 11Be.
*   The initial wave packet was placed at R_0 = 25 fm with a Gaussian width parameter gamma = 0.05 fm^-2.
*   The radial grid used steps of Delta R = 0.5 fm and Delta r = 1 fm, with l_max = 70, resulting in approximately 420,000 grid points for the wave function.
*   The average total energy E_0 of the wave packet shown in the dynamics was about 38 MeV, close to the barrier top energy.
*   Three-body calculations were performed with different n-T potentials: V_nT = 0, V_nT = -48 MeV, and V_nT = -42 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of fusion reactions of halo nuclei, which were restricted to head-on collisions on a medium-mass target (40Ca) and found fusion hindrance. It directly contradicts the conclusions of Hagino et al. (2000) and Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002), who reported strong enhancement of the fusion probability for the same 11Be + 208Pb system. The authors attribute this discrepancy to the insufficient partial wave truncation (low l-values) used in those prior studies, and show that calculations with l_max = 2 produce an artificial enhancement that disappears when high partial waves (l_max = 70) are included.
