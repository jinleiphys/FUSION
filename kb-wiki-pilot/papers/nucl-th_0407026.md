# Momentum distributions in stripping reactions of radioactive projectiles at intermediate energies

> The theory of one-nucleon removal in the stripping reaction (inelastic breakup) on a light target is extended to cover two-dimensional momentum distributions of the reaction residues with the use of realistic profile functions for the core-target and nucleon-target interactions. Examples of the calculated projected parallel- and transverse momentum distributions are given. The transverse momentum distributions, projections on a Cartesian axis perpendicular to the beam direction, show an interesting intermingling of the stripping reaction with elastic scattering of the reaction residue on the target. We also obtain doubly-differential distributions of the cross section on the parallel- and transverse-momentum variables. The distributions depend strongly on the value of the magnetic quantum number $m$. They will be of importance for evaluating acceptance corrections in experiments, and they lead to alignment with the possibility of anisotropic emission of subsequent gamma rays, an interesting spectroscopic tool. Experimental data for proton stripping of $^{8}$B agree with our calculations.

## Key claim

The paper extends the theory of one-nucleon removal in stripping reactions to cover two-dimensional momentum distributions of the reaction residues, using realistic profile functions for the core-target and nucleon-target interactions, and finds that the transverse momentum distributions show an intermingling of the stripping reaction with elastic scattering of the reaction residue on the target, while the longitudinal momentum distributions remain close to those obtained in the simpler black-disk approximation.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential stripping cross section using eikonal S-matrices for the core-target and nucleon-target interactions, obtained from optical potentials and the Glauber theory in the optical limit. The single-particle bound state wave functions for the core-nucleon subsystem are specified by radial wave functions and spherical harmonics, and the integrals in the expressions for longitudinal and transverse momentum distributions are evaluated numerically using Gaussian expansions of the S-matrices. The calculations are performed for several cases including halo states (11Be, 8B) and deeply bound states (34Ar) at intermediate energies.

## Key numbers

- For the 9Be(11Be,10Begs)X reaction at 60 MeV/nucleon, the transverse momentum distribution is lower in intensity than the longitudinal one by about 10%
- The transparent-core limit overestimates the stripping cross section by a factor of 2 for a pronounced halo system such as 11Be, and by up to a factor of 25 for deeply bound nucleons
- For the 12C(8B,7Begs)X reaction at 76 MeV/nucleon, the proton binding energy for this proton halo state is 0.137 MeV
- For the 9Be(34Ar,33Ar(3/2+)) reaction at 68.2 MeV/nucleon, the neutron separation energy is 18.42 MeV for the l=2 case
- For the l=0 neutron knockout from 34Ar, the neutron separation energy is 17.06 MeV
- For the 9Be(15C,14Cgs) reaction at 103 MeV/nucleon, the halo neutron is bound by 1.218 MeV
- The theoretical uncertainty on the integral single-particle cross section is of the order of 5% for a halo state and 15% for a very deeply bound l=2 state (Sn=22 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the theory of knockout reactions from the earlier black-disk approximation used by Hansen, Bertsch, and Esbensen (1995, 1996) to include realistic S-matrices from Tostevin's calculations (1998, 1999, 2001), enabling the calculation of full three-dimensional momentum distributions rather than just longitudinal projections. The results enable evaluation of acceptance corrections in experiments and provide a spectroscopic tool through alignment leading to anisotropic emission of subsequent gamma rays, while also confirming that the simpler black-disk approximation remains valid for longitudinal momentum distributions.
