# High-energy direct reactions with exotic nuclei and low-energy nuclear astrophysics

> Indirect methods in nuclear astrophysics are discussed. Recent work on Coulomb dissociation and an effective-range theory of low-lying electromagnetic strength of halo nuclei is presented. Coulomb dissociation of a halo nucleus bound by a zero-range potential is proposed as a homework problem (for further references see G. Baur and S. Typel, nucl-th/0504068). It is pointed out that the Trojan-Horse method (G. Baur, F. Rösel, D. Trautmann and R. Shyam, Phys. Rep. 111 (1984) 333) is a suitable tool to investigate subthreshold resonances.

## Key claim

Indirect methods such as Coulomb dissociation and the Trojan-Horse method can extract low-energy nuclear astrophysics cross sections from high-energy direct reactions with exotic beams, but the Trojan-Horse method relies on more phenomenological inputs (optical model parameters, effective nuclear interactions) than Coulomb dissociation, which depends primarily on QED.

## Method

The authors review two indirect methods: (1) Coulomb dissociation, where a projectile breaks up in the Coulomb field of a heavy target, and the breakup cross section is related to the radiative capture cross section of astrophysical interest via detailed balance and equivalent-photon methods; (2) the Trojan-Horse method, where a three-body reaction A + a → C + c + b (with a = x + b) is measured under quasi-free kinematics, and the two-body subprocess A + x → C + c is extracted using the surface approximation in DWBA. An effective-range theory for electromagnetic strength in one-neutron halo nuclei is also developed, using the square-well potential model and the effective-range expansion to describe the continuum.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be: inverse bound-state decay length q = 0.1486 fm^-1, cutoff radius R = 2.78 fm, asymptotic normalization coefficient C_0 = 0.724(8) fm^-1/2, spectroscopic factor C^2S = 0.704(15)
- Halo expansion parameter for 11Be: γ = qR = 0.4132
- For the p1/2 channel in 11Be: binding energy 184 keV, q = 0.094 fm, γ^2 = 0.068, scattering length a_1 = 210 fm^3, c_1 = 2.14
- For 11Be continuum: c^{3/2}_1 = -0.41(86,-20) and c^{1/2}_1 = 2.77(13,-14)
- 8B Coulomb dissociation at GSI used beam energy 254 A MeV
- 6Li Coulomb dissociation at GSI used beam energy 150 A MeV, reaching α-d relative energies down to 50 keV
- Trojan-Horse reaction 2H(6Li,α 3He)n measured at E_6Li = 25 MeV, extracting cross sections from E_cm = 2.4 MeV down to astrophysical energies

## Context

This work extends the effective-range approach previously applied to the deuteron (the prime halo nucleus example) to arbitrary nucleon+core systems, and generalizes the well-known relation a_0 = 1/q for s-waves to higher partial waves. The Coulomb dissociation method builds on earlier work by Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel (1986) and the review by Baur, Hencken, and Trautmann (2003). The Trojan-Horse method, introduced by Baur et al. (1984, 1976), is shown to be applicable to subthreshold resonances and to the study of single-particle states in exotic nuclei near the particle threshold. The approach enables extraction of bare-nucleus astrophysical S factors free from electron screening effects, which is relevant for interpreting direct measurements such as those from the LUNA collaboration.
