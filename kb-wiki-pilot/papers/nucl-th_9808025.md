# J/Psi Suppression in Heavy Ion Collisions at the CERN SPS

> We reexamine the production of J/Psi and other charmonium states for a variety of target-projectile choices at the SPS. For this study we use a newly constructed cascade code LUCIFER II, which yields acceptable descriptions of both hard and soft processes, specifically Drell-Yan and hidden charm production, and soft energy loss and meson production, at the SPS. Glauber calculations of other authors are redone, and compared directly to the cascade results. The modeling of the charmonium states differs from that of earlier workers in its unified treatment of the hidden charm meson spectrum, which is introduced from the outset as a set of coupled states. The result is a description of the NA38 and NA50 data in terms of a conventional hadronic picture. The apparently anomalous suppression found in the most massive Pb+Pb system arises from three sources: destruction in the initial nucleon-nucleon cascade, use of coupled channels to exploit the larger breakup in the less bound Chi and Psi' states, and comover interaction in the final low energy phase.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the "anomalous" J/ψ suppression observed in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS can be explained within a purely hadronic framework, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation, through three combined effects: destruction in the initial nucleon-nucleon cascade, coupled-channel exploitation of larger breakup in less bound χ and ψ' states, and comover interaction in the final low-energy phase.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-phase cascade code called LUCIFER II, which separates hard and soft processes by time scale. The first phase records high-energy nucleon-nucleon collisions and produces charmonium states (ψ, χ^i, ψ') as a set of coupled channels, while the second phase performs a low-energy cascade among secondary mesons and baryons after a formation time. The code uses a reaction matrix for charmonium states that permits transitions between states and includes breakup on both baryons and comoving mesons, with parameters constrained by pp, p+A, and Drell-Yan data.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ absorption cross-section on baryons: σ_abs ∼ 7.0 mb (hard sphere nuclear density) from p+A data at 800 GeV/c
- χ to J/ψ production ratio in pp: χ/ψ ∼ 4.5 (from ISR data)
- ψ' to J/ψ production ratio in pp: taken near 0.33, yielding final ratio ∼ 0.15 after ψ' decay
- ψ' branching ratio into J/ψ: 0.57 (standard value)
- χ branching ratio into J/ψ: 0.18 (weighted average over electromagnetic and hadronic components)
- χ absorption cross-section: σ_abs(χ) = 3 × σ_abs(J/ψ)
- J/ψ direct breakup cross-section in coupled-channel model: 5-6 mb
- Meson formation time parameter: τ_f ∼ 0.5-1 fm/c
- Generic meson masses: M_q\bar q ∼ 0.3-1.0 GeV, centered near 700 MeV
- Generic baryon masses: M_qqq ∼ 0.94-2.0 GeV
- Decay time for generic excitations: τ_d ∼ 1/125 MeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-based calculations of Kharzeev and Satz, who argued that hadronic models cannot account for the suppression seen in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS, and the comover annihilation suggestion of Gavin. The authors directly compare their cascade results to Glauber calculations and show that the first high-energy phase produces dynamics very similar to Glauber, but the inclusion of coupled channels for χ and ψ' states plus comover interactions in the second phase allows a purely hadronic description of the NA38 (S+U at 200 GeV/c) and NA50 (Pb+Pb at 158 GeV/c) data. The approach enables future extension to RHIC and LHC energies, where partonic degrees of freedom may become more important.
