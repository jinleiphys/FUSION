# Faddeev-type calculation of nonelastic breakup in deuteron-nucleus scattering

> The nonelastic breakup (NEB), one of channels in $(d,p)$ inclusive reactions, is studied using the Faddeev-type scattering theory. The NEB differential cross section is obtained in terms of the imaginary part of the neutron-nucleus optical potential sandwiched between the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas three-body transition operators. The momentum-space calculations including the Coulomb force are extended to higher charge numbers. Well converged numerical results are obtained for the energy distribution of the NEB cross section, being roughly consistent with previous works. The spin-dependent interaction terms do not play a significant role. The optical potential nonlocality effect shows up at higher proton energies, but is comparable to local potential uncertainties.

# Faddeev-type calculation of nonelastic breakup in deuteron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross section can be calculated using rigorous Faddeev-type three-body scattering theory via the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) transition operators in momentum space, yielding results that are roughly consistent with previous works but 10 to 35% higher at the peak, with the optical potential nonlocality effect being comparable to uncertainties among different local potential parametrizations.

## Method

The NEB differential cross section is derived from the imaginary part of the neutron-nucleus optical potential sandwiched between AGS three-body transition operators, following the spectator assumption for the detected proton and closure over final states of the undetected neutron-nucleus subsystem. The AGS equations are solved numerically in momentum-space partial-wave representation with orbital angular momenta up to 3, 12, and 22 for the neutron-proton, neutron-nucleus, and proton-nucleus pairs respectively, with total angular momentum up to 35, and the Coulomb force is included via the screening and renormalization method extended to higher charge numbers up to Z=40.

## Key numbers

- NEB cross section for 80 MeV deuteron scattering on ⁵⁸Ni, ⁷⁴Ge, ⁸⁴Kr, and ⁹⁰Zr shows increase with Z, not strictly linear, possibly indicating dependence on neutron-proton asymmetry (A-2Z)/A
- For ⁵⁸Ni at 80 MeV, Faddeev-type approach predicts NEB cross section at peak 10% higher than Ref. [PhysRevC.92.044616] and 35% higher than Ref. [Torabi_2023]
- Coulomb screening radius convergence: R=6 fm sufficient for ¹²C (Z=6) at 50 MeV; R around 15 fm at least needed for ⁹⁰Zr (Z=40) at 80 MeV
- Spin-dependent interaction terms produce almost negligible effect on NEB cross section

## Context

This work extends Faddeev-type three-body calculations, previously limited to ⁵⁸Ni (Z=28) for elastic, inelastic, transfer, and breakup reactions, to NEB for nuclei up to Z=40, addressing the challenge noted by Moro and Lei [PhysRevC.92.044616] that Faddeev equation solutions were considered too complicated for NEB implementation. The approach enables evaluation of optical potential nonlocality effects that coordinate-space NEB calculations have not yet incorporated, and provides a rigorous alternative to prior NEB methods based on DWBA, Glauber model, or continuum-discretized coupled-channel approaches.
