# Quark-Meson-Coupling (QMC) model for finite nuclei, nuclear matter and beyond

> The Quark-Meson-Coupling model, which self-consistently relates the dynamics of the internal quark structure of a hadron to the relativistic mean fields arising in nuclear matter, provides a natural explanation to many open questions in low energy nuclear physics, including the origin of many-body nuclear forces and their saturation, the spin-orbit interaction and properties of hadronic matter at a wide range of densities up to those occurring in the cores of neutron stars. Here we focus on four aspects of the model (i) a full comprehensive survey of the theory, including the latest developments, (ii) extensive application of the model to ground state properties of finite nuclei and hypernuclei, with a discussion of similarities and differences between the QMC and Skyrme energy density functionals, (iii) equilibrium conditions and composition of hadronic matter in cold and warm neutron stars and their comparison with the outcome of relativistic mean-field theories and, (iv) tests of the fundamental idea that hadron structure changes in-medium.

## Key claim

The Quark-Meson-Coupling model self-consistently relates the internal quark structure of hadrons to relativistic mean fields in nuclear matter, with the key mechanism being that the effective scalar coupling constant for the sigma meson to the composite nucleon decreases with density due to a "scalar polarizability" effect, which naturally leads to saturation of nuclear forces without requiring the very large scalar fields needed in Quantum Hadrodynamics.

## Method

The authors use the MIT bag model to describe quark confinement in hadrons, coupling the quarks to classical sigma, omega, rho, and pion meson fields. For finite nuclei, they employ a Born-Oppenheimer approximation where the bag moves in slowly varying meson fields, deriving the spin-orbit force from both the isoscalar magnetic moment interaction and Thomas precession. The model parameters (bag constant B, zero-point energy Z0, quark masses, color coupling constant alpha_c, and strange quark correction factor F_s) are fixed by fitting to free nucleon and Delta masses, with the bag radius set to 1.0 fm by matching lattice QCD calculations of the isovector charge radius at large pion masses.

## Key numbers

- Scalar polarizability d = 0.1797 for the nucleon (with bag radius R_N^free = 1.0 fm)
- Nucleon effective mass relation: M_N^* = M_N - g_sigma*sigma + (d/2)*(g_sigma*sigma)^2
- Free nucleon isoscalar magnetic moment mu_IS(0) = 0.88 mu_N; isovector magnetic moment mu_IV(0) = 4.7 mu_N
- Bag model parameters for R_N^free = 1.0 fm: B = 0.284 fm^{-4}, Z0 = 1.771, alpha_c = 0.560, m_s = 326 MeV (with F_s = 1) or m_s = 289 MeV (with F_s = 0.758)
- Hyperon masses from fit with F_s = 1: M_Lambda = 1132 MeV, M_Sigma = 1180 MeV, M_Xi = 1353 MeV (experimental: 1116, 1195, 1315 MeV)
- Weights for sigma coupling: w_N = 1, w_Lambda = 0.7114, w_Sigma = 0.5847, w_Xi = 0.3400; tilde-w_N = 1, tilde-w_Lambda = 0.68956, tilde-w_Sigma = 0.6629, tilde-w_Xi = 0.3688
- Confinement radius increase in medium is only 1-2% at nuclear matter density

## Context

This review extends the original QMC model proposed by Guichon in 1988, which provided an alternative to traditional approaches based on phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials (one-boson exchange, realistic forces, chiral effective field theory) that typically require 20-30 parameters for the NN force plus additional parameters for three-body forces. The QMC model contrasts with Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD), where saturation requires very large scalar fields (of order half the nucleon mass) to reduce the scalar density, while QMC achieves saturation through the scalar polarizability mechanism with smaller mean fields. The model enables calculations of finite nuclei properties comparable to Skyrme energy density functionals, predictions for hypernuclei with no new parameters, and equations of state for neutron stars that naturally produce maximum masses around two solar masses when hyperons are included.
