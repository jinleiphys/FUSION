# Models for nuclear reactions with weakly bound systems

> In this contribution, I present a short overview of the theory of direct nuclear reactions, with special emphasis on the case of reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei. After introducing some general results of quantum scattering theory, I present specific applications to elastic, inelastic, transfer and breakup reactions. For each of them, I first introduce the most standard framework, followed by some alternative models or extensions suitable for the case of weakly bound nuclei. A short discussion on semiclassical theory of Coulomb excitation and its application to breakup of halo nuclei is also provided.

## Key claim

This paper presents an overview of direct nuclear reaction theory for weakly-bound systems, noting that standard formalisms designed for tightly-bound nuclei are not well adapted to describe halo and Borromean nuclei, and that reaction theories must incorporate the effect of strong coupling to breakup channels.

## Method

The paper reviews quantum scattering theory for direct reactions, introducing the model Hamiltonian and scattering wave function formalism, then derives the T-matrix expression and the Gell-Mann-Goldberger two-potential formula. It presents the Feshbach projection method to reduce the full scattering space to a tractable modelspace, and applies these frameworks specifically to elastic, inelastic, transfer and breakup reactions, with extensions for weakly-bound nuclei including a semiclassical theory of Coulomb excitation for halo nuclei breakup.

## Key numbers

- Interaction cross sections at 800 MeV/nucleon for light exotic isotopes (6He, 11Li, 14Be) were found to be much higher than for neighboring isotopes, indicating abnormally large radii
- Direct reaction channels have characteristic collision times of about 10^-21 seconds
- Compound nucleus reactions take place over longer time scales of about 10^-18 to 10^-16 seconds
- Momentum distributions of 9Li following 11Li+12C -> 9Li+X fragmentation were found to be abnormally narrow, suggesting a long tail in the density distribution of 11Li

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework originally developed for reactions with stable, tightly-bound nuclei to the case of weakly-bound systems near the driplines, following the pioneering experiments by Tanihata and collaborators (1985) that revealed the unusual properties of halo nuclei. The paper builds on the Feshbach formalism (1958, 1962) and the Gell-Mann-Goldberger transformation, and discusses specific signatures such as the disappearance of the Fresnel peak at near-barrier energies in reactions induced by halo nuclei on heavy targets (San08, Pie12, Cub12, Pes17). This enables quantitative understanding of phenomena including enhanced near-threshold breakup in Coulomb dissociation experiments and abnormal elastic scattering cross sections.
