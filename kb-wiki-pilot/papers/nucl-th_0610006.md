> The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. For identical bosons this results in a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables, magnitudes of relative momenta and angles. # Three-Body Elastic and Inelastic Scattering at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim
The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering can be solved directly in momentum space using vector variables without partial wave decomposition, enabling calculations of elastic and breakup cross sections up to about 1 GeV projectile energy. However, for exclusive breakup processes at 1 GeV, the full Faddeev solution is still required to obtain converged results, as the multiple scattering series converges very slowly or oscillates for certain kinematic configurations.

## Method
The Faddeev equation for three identical bosons was formulated in momentum space and solved as a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables (magnitudes of relative momenta and angles), avoiding partial wave decomposition. A Malfliet-Tjon type potential (short-range repulsive and intermediate-range attractive Yukawa forces) was employed, with parameters adjusted to support a bound state at Ed = -2.23 MeV. The scattering amplitude was then used to calculate elastic differential cross sections and both semi-exclusive and exclusive breakup cross sections, with convergence of the multiple scattering series investigated by comparing successive orders against the full Faddeev solution.

## Key numbers
- Elastic differential cross sections calculated at Elab = 0.2, 0.5, 0.8, and 1.0 GeV projectile energy as function of center-of-mass scattering angle
- Semi-exclusive cross section d(N,N') at 1.0 GeV laboratory incident energy for emission angles 15 degrees and 33 degrees
- For exclusive breakup at Elab = 1.0 GeV, the energy of the outgoing particle Eq = (2/3)Elab + Ed ≈ 664 MeV
- Quasi-free scattering (QFS) peak in exclusive breakup at approximately 168 MeV (one quarter of total energy)
- Star configuration particle energy approximately (2/3 Elab + Ed)/2 = 332 MeV at 1.0 GeV
- Semi-exclusive cross section at Elab = 495 MeV and θlab = 18 degrees for first order multiple scattering, comparing non-relativistic, relativistic kinematics, and full relativistic calculations

## Context
This work extends the traditional partial-wave-based Faddeev approach (which becomes computationally prohibitive at intermediate energies due to the proliferation of partial waves) by solving directly in vector variables, as previously demonstrated in Refs. [scatter3d, liuthesis]. The method enables calculations at energies up to 1 GeV where partial wave convergence becomes problematic. The results show that for exclusive breakup at 1 GeV, low-order reaction mechanisms are insufficient for star configurations and certain QFS conditions, indicating that previous theoretical analyses of p(d,ppn) reactions relying on low order approximations need reexamination. The authors also present first steps toward incorporating relativistic effects within Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics, following the scheme of Ref. [witala], with relativistic kinematics already shifting the QFS peak position to match experimental data from Chen et al. (1993).
