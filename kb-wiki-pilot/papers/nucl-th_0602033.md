# Core Transitions in the Breakup of Exotic Nuclei

> An interesting physical process has been unveiled: dynamical core excitation during a breakup reaction of loosely bound $core+N$ systems. These reactions are typically used to extract spectroscopic information and/or astrophysical information. A new method, the eXtended Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (XCDCC) method, was developed to incorporate, in a consistent way and to all orders, core excitation in the bound and scattering states of the projectile, as well as dynamical excitation of the core as it interacts with the target. The model predicts cross sections to specific states of the core. It is applied to the breakup of $^{11}$Be on $^9$Be at 60 MeV/u, and the calculated cross sections are in improved agreement with the data. The distribution of the cross section amongst the various core states is shown to depend on the reaction model used, and not simply on the ground state spectroscopic factors.

## Key claim

Dynamical core excitation during breakup reactions of loosely bound core+N systems is a real physical process that must be treated consistently to all orders, not added as an incoherent correction. The distribution of breakup cross section among core states depends on the reaction model used and not simply on the ground state spectroscopic factors.

## Method

The authors developed the eXtended Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (XCDCC) method, which incorporates core excitation in both bound and scattering states of the projectile and allows dynamical excitation/de-excitation of the core through its interaction with the target. The projectile is described as a multi-component system based on several core states, with continuum states discretized into coupled-channel bins. The method was applied to the breakup of 11Be on 9Be at 60 MeV/u, using a coupled-channel model for 11Be that includes the 0+ ground state and 2+ first excited state of the 10Be core, with the valence neutron in s-wave and d-wave components.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of 11Be contains 88.3% s-wave neutron coupled to 10Be(0+) and 11.7% d-wave neutron coupled to 10Be(2+)
- XCDCC predicts breakup cross section to 10Be(0+): 109 mb; to 10Be(2+): 8 mb (total 117 mb)
- Single particle incoherent sum (SPIS) predicts breakup cross section to 10Be(0+): 109 mb; to 10Be(2+): 1 mb (total 110 mb)
- Eikonal model predicts breakup cross section to 10Be(0+): 105 mb; to 10Be(2+): 3.4 mb (total 108 mb)
- Combined XCDCC breakup plus eikonal stripping gives total cross sections: 200 mb for 0+ (experiment 203 +/- 31 mb) and 14 mb for 2+ (experiment 16 +/- 4 mb)
- 11Be has a positive parity bound state at E+ = -0.5 MeV and a negative parity level at E- = -0.18 MeV

## Context

This work extends the spectator core model of Tostevin (1999) and the preliminary core-excitation study of Batham et al. (2005) by providing a fully coupled treatment of core excitation in breakup reactions. It addresses lingering disagreements between single-particle breakup models and data for 11Be on 9Be at 60 MeV/u from Aumann et al. (2000), where eikonal predictions were too low for both the ground state (165 mb theory vs 203 mb experiment) and 2+ excited state (9 mb theory vs 16 mb experiment). The XCDCC method enables consistent treatment of reactions where core degrees of freedom are important, including 8Li, 8B, 11Be, 17C, 17F, and 19C, and suggests that previous spectroscopic analyses and astrophysical S-factor extractions need to be revisited.
