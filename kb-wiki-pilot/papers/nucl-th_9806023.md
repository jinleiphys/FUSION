# Charmonium Production in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> This is a review of theoretical attempts to describe production of heavy quark bound states in nucleus-nucleus collisions, in particular, the relative suppression of $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ production observed in these reactions. The review begins with a survey of experimental data for proton-induced reactions and their theoretical interpretation. The evidence for additional suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions is discussed and various theoretical models of charmonium absorption by comoving matter are presented and analyzed. The review concludes with suggestions for future research that would help clarify the implications of $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb + Pb collisions observed by the NA50 experiment.

## Key claim

This review argues that while all charmonium suppression data from proton-nucleus and light-ion (O, S) collisions up to 1994 can be explained by final-state interactions with nucleons (absorption cross section ~7.3 mb), the "anomalous" additional suppression of J/ψ observed in Pb+Pb collisions by NA50 may indicate quark-gluon plasma formation, but only if all other conceivable mechanisms, particularly absorption by comoving hadrons, can be ruled out on quantitative grounds.

## Method

The review surveys experimental data from Fermilab E772 (p+A) and CERN experiments NA38 (O+U, S+U) and NA50 (Pb+Pb), analyzing the A-dependence and transverse-energy dependence of J/ψ and ψ' production cross sections within the Glauber model. It then evaluates three categories of theoretical models for charmonium absorption by comoving matter: deconfinement in a quark-gluon plasma (screening of the color force), thermal dissociation in a hadronic gas, and pre-thermal dissociation mechanisms (parton plasma or color flux tubes), comparing predictions from the Bhanot-Peskin perturbative approach, the constituent quark model, and effective hadronic Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

- Absorption cross section for (c̄c) pairs on nucleons: σ_{c̄c N}^{(abs)} = (7.3 ± 0.6) mb, extracted from Glauber model analysis of all p+A data
- Nuclear A-dependence exponent for J/ψ and ψ' in p+A: α ≈ 0.92 (same for both states)
- Critical dissociation temperature for J/ψ: T_d^{(J/ψ)} ≈ 1.2 T_c, where T_c is the QCD phase transition temperature
- Critical dissociation temperature for Υ: T_d^{(Υ)} ≈ 2 T_c
- Critical dissociation temperature for ψ' and χ_c: T_d ≈ T_c
- Color octet-to-singlet neutralization time: τ_{8→1} ≈ 0.3 n fm/c (where n is the principal quantum number)
- Peak cross section for J/ψ dissociation by a gluon in dipole approximation: about 3 mb at gluon momentum around 1 GeV/c
- Pion momentum must reach 5 GeV/c to attain J/ψ absorption cross section above 1 mb in the Bhanot-Peskin formalism
- Thermally averaged J/ψ absorption cross section on pions: less than 0.1 mb for any realistic temperature range
- Constituent quark model calculation (Martins, Blaschke, Quack): π + J/ψ → D* + D̄ cross section peaks at 15 mb around 1 GeV kinetic energy (c.m.)

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Matsui and Satz (1986) on J/ψ suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signature, and the systematic framework of Karsch and Satz (1991) for heavy quarkonium spectroscopy in superdense matter. It addresses the puzzle that Capella et al. (1988) and Gerschel and Hüfner (1992) showed all pre-1994 data follow Glauber model predictions, while the NA50 Pb+Pb results (1996) show enhanced suppression. The color octet model, motivated by CDF Tevatron data (Braaten et al.), provides a natural explanation for the unexpectedly large absorption cross section. The review enables future quantitative tests to distinguish between comover absorption models and the deconfinement scenario, and calls for lattice simulations of color field fluctuation spectra and coupled-channel potential model calculations of the octet-to-singlet transition time.
