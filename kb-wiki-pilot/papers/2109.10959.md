# Application of the Lagrange mesh method in continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations

> We apply the Lagrange-mesh method to discretize continuum states of weakly bound nuclei for continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations of three-body breakup reactions. This discretization method is compared with the bin method, which is regarded as the standard continuum discretization method, for the $d$ and $^6$Li induced reactions. An improved Numerov algorithm is used to solve the coupled channels equations, which permits a fast integration of equations and a convenient treatment of the closed channels. A new CDCC model code named \emph{CDCC-R} is developed. In all cases, the combination of the Lagrange-mesh method and the Numerov algorithm shows high efficiency and accuracy for the CDCC calculations of the elastic scattering and breakup reactions. Especially, various numerical and physical aspects are discussed for $^6$Li induced reactions. The coupling effects of the continuum states with high orbital angular momentum and closed channels are discussed. Moderate effects are found in the calculations for elastic scattering and breakup reaction when $F$- and $G$-wave continuum states are included in CDCC model space for $^6$Li induced reactions at the incident energies well above the Coulomb barrier. The closed channel effect is found to be indispensable for $^6$Li breakup reaction calculation when the incident energy is around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The Lagrange-mesh method, combined with an improved Numerov algorithm, provides a highly efficient and accurate alternative to the standard bin method for discretizing continuum states in continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations, but the closed channel effect is indispensable for \(^6\)Li breakup reaction calculations when the incident energy is around the Coulomb barrier, and moderate effects are found when \(F\)- and \(G\)-wave continuum states are included at energies well above the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors applied the regularized Lagrange-Laguerre mesh method (RLLM) to discretize the continuum states of weakly bound projectiles (deuteron and \(^6\)Li) for CDCC calculations of three-body breakup reactions. An improved Numerov algorithm was used to solve the coupled-channel equations, and a new code named CDCC-R was developed. The RLLM results were benchmarked against the standard bin method for \(d+^{58}\)Ni at 80.0 MeV and for \(^6\)Li+\(^{12}\)C and \(^6\)Li+\(^{59}\)Co reactions at various energies.

## Key numbers

- For \(d+^{58}\)Ni at EL=80.0 MeV: CDCC-RLLM used \(N\)=30 and \(h\)=0.4 fm for elastic scattering, with \(N_{0,0,0}\)=15, \(N_{2,2,2}\)=13, \(N_{4,4,4}\)=12 discretized states, while CDCC-Bin used \(\Delta k\)=0.04 fm\(^{-1}\) and \(r_{bin}\)=40 fm with \(N_{0,0,0}\)=31, \(N_{2,2,2}\)=30, \(N_{4,4,4}\)=30 states.
- For \(d+^{58}\)Ni breakup: CDCC-RLLM used \(\{N=70, h=0.4 \text{ fm}\}\) with 165 discretized states; CDCC-Bin used \(\Delta k\)=0.03 fm\(^{-1}\) and \(r_{bin}\)=100 fm with 201 states.
- For \(^6\)Li: the \(\alpha-d\) interaction generated a ground state binding energy of -1.47 MeV; calculated resonance energies were 0.710 MeV (3\(^+\)), 3.00 MeV (2\(^+\)), and 4.24 MeV (1\(^+\)), compared with experimental values of 0.716, 2.84, and 4.18 MeV respectively.
- For \(^6\)Li+\(^{12}\)C at EL=168.6 MeV: convergence of elastic \(S\)-matrix was achieved with \(k_{\max}=2.2\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(N=20\), \(R_m=18\) fm, and \(l_{\max}=4\); the \(S\)-matrix for \(J^\pi=27^-\) was 626.2+254.1\(i\) (multiplied by 1000) under these conditions.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Lagrange-mesh method to CDCC calculations, previously introduced by T. Druet et al. (2010) for \(d+^{58}\)Ni and by T. Druet and P. Descouvemont (2012) for \(^{11}\)Be+\(^{64}\)Zn, but those studies used the \(R\)-matrix approach which is time-consuming for large channel numbers. The present work replaces the \(R\)-matrix approach with an improved Numerov algorithm (Yang, 1980) for faster integration and convenient treatment of closed channels. The new CDCC-R code enables efficient calculations for heavy weakly bound nuclei where the number of channels typically exceeds 100, and provides a platform for studying coupling effects of high orbital angular momentum continuum states and closed channels in \(^6\)Li induced reactions.
