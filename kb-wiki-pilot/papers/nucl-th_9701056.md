# Polarization observables in dp backward elastic scattering at high and intermediate energies

> The tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ and the polarization transfer coefficients $κ_0 (= 3/2 K_y^y)$ and $K_{xz}^y$ are investigated for dp backward elastic scattering by the invariant-amplitude method. Discrepancies between the conventional calculations and the experimental data on $T_{20}$ and $κ_0$ at high and intermediate energies are mostly dissolved by including imaginary parts in the amplitudes. The quantity $K_{xz}^y$ is shown to be useful in criticizing nuclear force assumptions.

## Key claim

By including imaginary parts in the invariant amplitudes (parameterized as free phases $\Theta$ and $\Theta^\prime$), the discrepancies between conventional one-nucleon-exchange model calculations and experimental data for $T_{20}$ and $\kappa_0$ in dp backward elastic scattering at high and intermediate energies are "mostly dissolved," though the authors note that assuming $\Theta$ and $\Theta^\prime$ independent of momentum transfer $k$ is a convenience with no justification, and more quantitative reproduction would require varying these parameters with $k$.

## Method

The authors apply the invariant-amplitude method in a non-relativistic framework assuming the one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) mechanism for dp backward elastic scattering at $\theta = \pi$. They express the tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ and polarization transfer coefficients $\kappa_0$ and $K_{xz}^y$ in terms of one scalar amplitude $U$ and two tensor amplitudes $T$ and $T^\prime$, introducing relative magnitudes $R$, $R^\prime$ and relative phases $\Theta$, $\Theta^\prime$ as free parameters. The magnitudes $R$ and $R^\prime$ are calculated in the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) using Fourier transforms of deuteron S- and D-state wave functions from several nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris, RSC, Nijmegen, Bonn B), while $\Theta$ and $\Theta^\prime$ are varied freely to explore the effects of imaginary parts.

## Key numbers

- The zero point of the deuteron S-state Fourier transform $u(k)$ occurs at $k_0 = 0.39$ GeV/c for the RSC potential and $k_0 = 0.45$ GeV/c for the Bonn B potential.
- In the PWIA limit, $T_{20} = -1/\sqrt{2}$ and $\kappa_0 = -1$ at the point Y in the $\kappa_0$-$T_{20}$ plane.
- At $r = 0$ (where $r = w(k)/u(k)$), $T_{20} = 0$ and $\kappa_0 = 1$, defining point X.
- Experimental data for small $k$ are mostly located between trajectories for $\Theta = 120^\circ$ and $135^\circ$ with $\Theta^\prime = 0^\circ$.
- For large $k$, most data lie between trajectories calculated with $\Theta^\prime = 105^\circ$ and $120^\circ$ (with $\Theta = 180^\circ$).
- A local maximum in $T_{20}$ is observed experimentally in the range $k = 0.25-0.45$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier model-independent formulae for $T_{20}$ and $\kappa_0$ in (d,p) reactions (Tanifuji, 1995) that were applicable only at low energies due to additional approximations. It addresses the "puzzle" of serious discrepancies between PWIA/ONE model predictions (Vasan, 1973; Kobushkin et al., 1994) and experimental data (Punjabi et al., 1995), where measured quantities deviate from the predicted circle in the $\kappa_0$-$T_{20}$ plane along a spiral-like curve. The results enable criticism of nuclear force assumptions through the observable $K_{xz}^y$, which vanishes at $k = k_0$ (a force-dependent quantity) and is sensitive to the signs of the phase parameters.
