# Particle removal reactions with deformed projectiles

> Single-particle removal reactions are becoming an important tool for studying radioactive nuclei. The nuclei far from stability may reveal new regions of large deformation. We discuss the influence of the projectile deformation on the stripping cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions of the core. The Glauber formalism for describing such reactions is developed and compared with the simpler geometric approach and with existing experimental data. The most significant effects are expected for nuclei with valence spherical orbitals strongly mixed by deformation. The shape of the momentum distribution, in turn, can be used for determining the degree of deformation of the projectile.

## Key claim

The influence of projectile deformation on stripping cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions is most significant for nuclei with valence spherical orbitals strongly mixed by deformation; the shape of the momentum distribution can be used for determining the degree of deformation of the projectile, but the most significant effects are expected only under this specific condition.

## Method

The authors developed a Glauber formalism for stripping reactions that incorporates projectile deformation by fixing the orientation of the core symmetry axis during the collision (adiabatic approximation) and using Nilsson model wave functions for the deformed single-particle orbitals. They also employed a simpler geometric "black disc" approach that treats the target as an absorbing cylinder and calculates the Fourier transform of the valence nucleon wave function restricted to the overlap region. Both methods were applied to calculate longitudinal momentum distributions and total stripping cross sections for reactions such as $^{9}$Be($^{25}$Al,$^{24}$Mg)X.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{9}$Be($^{25}$Al,$^{24}$Mg)X, the valence proton in $^{25}$Al occupies the Nilsson orbital $[202\,\,5/2]$, which is generated by the spherical $j=5/2$, $m_j=5/2$ basis function and is not mixed by the core deformed mean field with other spherical orbitals from the $sd$-shell
- The deformation effect on total (angle-integrated) reaction cross sections is less than 1%, consistent with the conclusion of Christley and Tostevin
- The geometric approach uses $b_{min}=R_c+R_T$ as the lowest possible impact parameter, with $R_c=r_oA_c^{1/3}$ as the effective core radius
- The harmonic oscillator wave functions used have incorrect asymptotic behavior, decaying too fast at large distances, which is not very serious for tightly bound projectiles with binding energies $\geq$5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Glauber formalism for stripping reactions, previously applied to spherical nuclei, to include deformed projectiles, building on the adiabatic approximation of Drozdov and Inopin from the 1950s and the combined coupled-channel Glauber approach of Abgrall et al. (1979) and Fäldt and Glauber (1990). The formalism is motivated by recent experiments on neutron-rich nuclei in the "island of inversion" region ($N\approx 30$), such as $^{26,28}$Ne and $^{30,32,34}$Mg isotopes, where large deformation is expected from theoretical calculations (Cauvin et al. 1998, Warburton et al. 1990) and Coulomb excitation measurements (Pritychenko et al. 1999). The approach enables extraction of shape parameters from stripping reactions on deformed radioactive nuclei, which is needed because existing techniques were developed for spherical or weakly deformed nuclei.
