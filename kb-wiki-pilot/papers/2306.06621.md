# Continuum-discretized coupled-channel calculations for $^{6}$Li fusion reactions with closed channels

> Fusion reactions induced by the weakly bound nucleus $^{6}$Li with targets $^{28}$Si, $^{64}$Ni, $^{144}$Sm and $^{209}$Bi at energies around the Coulomb barrier are investigated within a three-body model where $^{6}$Li is described with an $α+ d$ cluster model. The total fusion (TF) cross sections are calculated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method and the complete fusion (CF) cross sections are extracted through the sum-rule model. The calculations demonstrate that (i) for the TF cross section calculations, the continuum states up to 40 MeV are found to be necessary, which corresponds to the inclusion of closed channels for light and medium mass targets, such as $^{28}$Si, $^{59}$Co and $^{144}$Sm, (ii) the converged CDCC results for TF cross section at energies above the Coulomb barrier are almost the same as single channel results in which the continuum coupling effect is neglected, and (iii) the continuum coupling strongly influences partial wave fusion cross sections and the closed channels play a significant role in the improvement of the description of the CF cross sections at energies below the Coulomb barrier for the $^6$Li+$^{28}$Si, $^{59}$Co and $^{144}$Sm systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for total fusion (TF) cross sections of $^{6}$Li with light and medium mass targets ($^{28}$Si, $^{59}$Co, $^{144}$Sm), continuum states up to 40 MeV must be included in CDCC calculations to achieve convergence, which corresponds to the inclusion of closed channels; however, for the heavy target $^{209}$Bi, the coupling effect of closed channels is weak, with only a 0.3% difference in TF cross section when using $\varepsilon_{max}=\varepsilon_{th}$ versus $\varepsilon_{max}=40.0$ MeV.

## Method

The authors describe $^{6}$Li as an $\alpha+d$ cluster model and calculate total fusion (TF) cross sections using the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method with pseudo-states generated by the regularized Lagrange-Laguerre mesh method. Complete fusion (CF) cross sections are extracted through the sum-rule model, which separates CF and incomplete fusion (ICF) by a cut-off angular momentum $J_c$. The short-range imaginary parts of the $\alpha$-target and $d$-target optical potentials are parameterized in Woods-Saxon form with radius factor $r_W=0.8$ fm and diffuseness $a_W=0.1$ fm, while the real parts use the S$\tilde{a}$o Paulo potential version 2 (SPP2).

## Key numbers

- $^{6}$Li has a separation energy of 1.47 MeV for breakup into $\alpha$ and $d$ fragments
- For $^{6}$Li+$^{28}$Si, $V_B=6.87$ MeV; for $^{6}$Li+$^{64}$Ni, $V_B=12.41$ MeV; for $^{6}$Li+$^{144}$Sm, $V_B=25.15$ MeV; for $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi, $V_B=30.40$ MeV
- For $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi at $E_{cm}=V_B$, the TF cross section with $\varepsilon_{max}=\varepsilon_{th}$ differs from that with $\varepsilon_{max}=40.0$ MeV by only 0.3% when $W_0=50$ MeV
- With $\varepsilon_{max}=40$ MeV, changes in TF cross sections are less than 2% when the imaginary potential depth changes from 20 to 80 MeV for all four reaction systems
- The $3^+$ resonance state of $^{6}$Li has calculated energy 0.710 MeV and width 0.084 MeV (experimental: 0.716 MeV and 0.024 MeV)
- The $2^+$ resonance state has calculated energy 3.00 MeV and width 1.12 MeV (experimental: 2.84 MeV and 1.30 MeV)
- The $1^+$ resonance state has calculated energy 4.24 MeV and width 2.93 MeV (experimental: 4.18 MeV and 1.50 MeV)
- The calculated root-mean-square matter radius and charge radius of $^{6}$Li are both 2.54 fm, agreeing with the experimental value 2.54$\pm$0.03 fm

## Context

This work extends previous CDCC studies of $^{6}$Li fusion reactions by Diaz-Torres et al. (2003) and Lubian et al. (2022), who set the maximum continuum energy $\varepsilon_{max}$ to 6.0-8.0 MeV, far below the threshold energy $\varepsilon_{th}=E_{cm}-\varepsilon_b$. The authors follow their own previous CDCC analysis for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co (Chen et al., 2022) which found that $\varepsilon_{max}$ should be at least 50.0 MeV for converged elastic breakup cross sections. The sum-rule model of Wilczynski et al. (1973, 1980, 1982) is adopted to distinguish CF and ICF processes. This work enables future studies of continuum coupling effects on $^{6}$Li complete fusion with light and medium mass targets, where closed channels play a significant role at energies below the Coulomb barrier.
