# Interplay of projectile breakup and target excitation in reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei

> In this work, we reexamine the extension of the CDCC formalism to include target excitation and apply it to a variety of reactions to study the effect of breakup on inelastic cross sections. We use a transformed oscillator basis to discretize the continuum of the projectiles in the different reactions and use the extended CDCC method developed in this work to solve the resulting coupled differential equations. A new code has been developed to perform the calculations. Reactions 58Ni(d, d) 58Ni*, 24Mg(d, d) 24Mg* , 144Sm( 6Li, 6Li) 144Sm* and 9Be( 6Li, 6Li) 9Be* are studied. Satisfactory agreement is found between experimental data and extended CDCC calculations. The studied CDCC method is proved to be an accurate tool to describe target excitation in reactions with weakly-bound nuclei. Moderate effects of breakup on inelastic observables are found for the reactions studied. Cross section magnitudes are not modified much, but angular distributions present smoothing when opposed to calculations without breakup.

## Key claim

The extended Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) formalism, which treats projectile breakup and target excitation on equal footing, produces satisfactory agreement with experimental data for several reactions; however, the effect of breakup on inelastic observables is only moderate, smoothing angular distributions without substantially modifying cross section magnitudes.

## Method

The projectile continuum was discretized using a transformed harmonic oscillator (pseudo-state) basis, and the resulting coupled differential equations were solved with a newly developed code implementing the extended CDCC method. The projectile was modeled as a two-body system (core plus valence particle), and target collective excitations were described using the particle-rotor model with a deformed optical potential. The coupling potentials were derived in a j-j coupling scheme, and calculations were performed for deuteron and 6Li projectiles on various targets.

## Key numbers

- For 58Ni(d,d)58Ni* at Ed = 80 MeV: deformation parameter β = 0.179 for 58Ni; continuum states up to 40 MeV with l = 0-2 included; l = 1 and l = 4 contributions found negligible
- For 24Mg(d,d)24Mg* at Ed = 70 MeV: deformation parameter β = 0.5 for 24Mg; inclusion of 4+ state (β4 = -0.017) produced small reduction at inelastic peak
- For 144Sm(6Li,6Li)144Sm* at Elab = 23, 28, 30, 35 MeV: 6Li ground state at E = -1.47 MeV; d-wave resonance at E = 0.7 MeV; 144Sm 2+ state at 1.66 MeV and 3- state at 1.81 MeV; deformation parameters β2 = 0.087 and β3 = 0.130

## Context

This work extends the CDCC formalism originally developed by the Kyushu-Pittsburg group (Yahiro et al., 1986) for deuteron scattering, applying it to heavier projectiles like 6Li and using a j-j coupling scheme instead of the earlier L-S coupling. It enables consistent treatment of both projectile breakup and target excitation in reactions where weakly-bound nuclei collide with collective targets, complementing Faddeev/AGS approaches (Deltuva, 2013) that also allow target excitation but are more computationally demanding. The method is applied to reactions previously studied with decoupled treatments (Woodard et al., 2012) and compared with Faddeev calculations (Deltuva, 2016).
