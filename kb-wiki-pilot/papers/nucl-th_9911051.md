# Photo- and Electro-Disintegration of 3He at Threshold and pd Radiative Capture

> The present work reports results for: pd radiative capture observables measured at center-of-mass (c.m.) energies in the range 0--100 keV and at 2 MeV by the TUNL and Wisconsin groups, respectively; contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) integral in 3He from the two- up to the three-body breakup thresholds, compared to experimental determinations by the TUNL group in this threshold region; longitudinal, transverse, and interference response functions measured in inclusive polarized electron scattering off polarized 3He at excitation energies below the threshold for breakup into ppn, compared to unpolarized longitudinal and transverse data from the Saskatoon group. The calculations are based on a realistic Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon interactions and a realistic current operator, including one- and two-body components. The theoretical predictions obtained by including only one-body currents are in violent disagreement with data. These differences between theory and experiment are, to a large extent, removed when two-body currents are taken into account, although some rather large discrepancies remain in the c.m. energy range 0--100 keV, particularly for the pd differential cross section and tensor analyzing power at small angles, and contributions to the GDH integral. A rather detailed analysis indicates that these discrepancies have, in large part, a common origin, and can be traced back to an excess strength obtained in the theoretical calculation of the E1 reduced matrix element associated with the pd channel having L,S,J=1,1/2,3/2. It is suggested that this lack of E1 strength observed experimentally might have implications for the nuclear interaction at very low energies. Finally, the validity of the long-wavelength approximation for electric dipole transitions is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that while including two-body currents largely resolves the violent disagreement between theory and experiment for pd radiative capture, photo-disintegration, and electro-disintegration observables, significant discrepancies remain in the 0–100 keV center-of-mass energy range, particularly for the pd differential cross section and tensor analyzing power at small angles, and contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral. These residual discrepancies are traced to an excess strength in the theoretical calculation of the E1 reduced matrix element for the pd channel with L,S,J = 1,1/2,3/2, suggesting possible implications for the nuclear interaction at very low energies.

## Method

The authors used pair-correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (PHH) wave functions for both the 3He bound state and pd scattering states, obtained from a realistic Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. The electromagnetic current operator included one- and two-body components, with two-body currents associated with Delta-isobar degrees of freedom treated non-perturbatively via the transition-correlation-operator method. The calculations were compared to experimental data for pd radiative capture at 0–100 keV (TUNL) and 2 MeV (Wisconsin), contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral in 3He (TUNL), and response functions from inclusive polarized electron scattering off polarized 3He (Saskatoon).

## Key numbers

- S-factor at zero energy in S-wave capture: 0.110 eV-b (theory), compared to experimental value 0.109 ± 0.010 eV-b
- S-factor at zero energy in P-wave capture: 0.109 eV-b (theory), compared to experimental value 0.074 ± 0.01 eV-b
- At Ep = 40 keV, two-body currents increase the doublet M1 reduced matrix element by 87% and reduce the quartet M1 by 10%
- At Ep = 40 keV, two-body currents increase E1(1,1/2,1/2) and E1(1,1/2,3/2) reduced matrix elements by 7% and 10% respectively
- AV18/UIX PHH wave functions predict B(3H) = 8.48 MeV, B(3He) = 7.73 MeV, with matter radii 1.725 fm and 1.928 fm respectively
- Momentum transfers for electrodisintegration: q = 0.88, 1.64, and 2.74 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pd radiative capture at 0–100 keV and nd radiative capture at thermal neutron energies (Viviani et al., 1996) by including improved PHH treatment of the pd continuum, calculations at 2 MeV, contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral, and response functions from polarized electron scattering. The results contradict the impulse approximation (one-body currents only), which is in violent disagreement with data, and demonstrate that two-body currents are essential for describing these observables. The residual discrepancies suggest that the nuclear interaction at very low energies may need refinement, and the work enables future studies of three-body currents and relativistic effects in few-nucleon systems.
