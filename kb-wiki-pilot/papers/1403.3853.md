# Elastic scattering of 17F, 17O and 19F on a heavy target in microscopic continuum discretized coupled-channels method

> Background: Microscopic description of the projectile, based on an effective NN interaction, in a microscopic CDCC (MCDCC) model [PRL 111, 082701 (2013)] has been successfully applied to the 7Li+208Pb scattering. Purpose: The MCDCC method is applied to the low energy elastic scattering of 17F, 17O and 19F on 58Ni and 208Pb targets. The goal of the calculations is twofold - to test the adequacy and the accuracy of the MCDCC model for the heavier projectiles, and to study the contribution of various channels to the elastic scattering cross sections. Methods: The elastic scattering cross sections are calculated using the MCDCC method. The nucleon-target optical potential is folded with the projectile densities resulting from an effective NN interaction, which includes central nuclear, spin-orbit and Coulomb terms. Discretization of the continuum is achieved via the pseudo state method. Coupled equations are solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. Results: For the test case of 17F at 10 MeV/nucleon, the cross sections are weakly sensitive to the choice of the effective NN interaction, three different energy dependent optical nucleon-target potentials provide a similar reasonable agreement with data. Just below the Coulomb barrier, the MCDCC significantly underestimates the cross sections at larger angles. The coupling to continuum is not significant in most of the assessed cases. Conclusions: The MCDCC is very satisfactory in the sense, that it includes the microscopic properties of the projectile in a reaction model. Well above the Coulomb barrier, the cross sections are in a good agreement with data. The reasons for the discrepancy between the data and the calculated cross sections at the lower energies, which is also observed in a traditional CDCC, are unclear.

## Key claim

The microscopic continuum discretized coupled-channels (MCDCC) method, which describes the projectile structure via an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, provides satisfactory elastic scattering cross sections for 17F, 17O, and 19F on heavy targets well above the Coulomb barrier, but significantly underestimates the cross sections at larger angles just below the Coulomb barrier, a discrepancy whose origin remains unclear and is also observed in traditional CDCC.

## Method

The elastic scattering cross sections were calculated using the MCDCC method, where the projectile is described microscopically as a two-cluster system (16O + nucleon for A=17 nuclei, 15N + alpha for 19F) using an effective NN interaction with central nuclear, spin-orbit, and Coulomb terms. The nucleon-target optical potential was folded with projectile transition densities obtained from the microscopic structure calculation, and the continuum was discretized via the pseudostate method. Coupled-channel equations were solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, with the Koning-Delaroche nucleon-target optical potential employed for the final results.

## Key numbers

- 17F valence proton bound by 0.6 MeV; 17O neutron separation energy Sn = 4.1 MeV; 19F alpha separation energy S_alpha = 4.014 MeV and proton separation energy Sp = 7.994 MeV
- Coulomb barrier for 17F+208Pb: V_B,lab = 91.7 MeV; for 17F+58Ni: V_B,lab approximately 46 MeV; for 17O+208Pb: V_B,lab approximately 83 MeV; for 17O+58Ni: V_B,lab approximately 44 MeV; for 19F+208Pb: V_B,lab approximately 94 MeV
- For 17F+208Pb at 170 MeV (10 MeV/nucleon), the real part of the Koning-Delaroche potential needed renormalization by a factor of 0.65 to better reproduce data; for 17F+58Ni at 170 MeV, a renormalization factor of 0.8 was used
- Experimental B(E2, 5/2+ -> 1/2+) for 17F is 64.9 e^2 fm^4; GCM calculation gives 50.1 e^2 fm^4
- Minnesota interaction parameters: for 17F, u = 0.917 and S0 = 33.8 MeV fm^5; for 17O, u = 0.9245 and S0 = 34.3 MeV fm^5; for 19F, u = 0.8185 and S0 = 35.0 MeV fm^5
- Pseudostate cutoff energies: 20 MeV for A=17 projectiles, 10 MeV for 19F; maximum angular momentum j_max = 5/2 for 17F and 19F, 7/2 for 17O

## Context

This work extends the MCDCC method, previously applied to 7Li+208Pb scattering [Descouvemont and Hussein, Phys. Rev. Lett. 111, 082701 (2013)], to heavier projectiles (17F, 17O, 19F) on 58Ni and 208Pb targets. The results are compared with traditional CDCC calculations [Kubo et al., Moro et al.] and experimental quasielastic scattering data from Refs. [Li09, Ro04, Ma10, Si13, Li87, Li01]. The study enables future improvements to the MCDCC model, such as including target excitations and additional core configurations, and highlights the need to understand the systematic discrepancy between theory and experiment at near-Coulomb barrier energies.
