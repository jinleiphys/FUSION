# Complete fusion of $^6$Li with $^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni nuclei in the framework of the continuum discretized coupled channel method

> The complete fusion of $^6$Li with $^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni nuclei, ranging from subbarrier energies up to values well above the Coulomb barrier, is studied using the continuum discretized coupled channels method. We investigate the sensitivity of the results to the largest energy used in the discretization of the continuum, including closed channels. Our results reveal that, as long as the states in the continuum are not limited to too small energies, the specific upper bound adopted is not relevant as the complete fusion cross-section is fairly insensitive to it above a threshold. Convergence with respect to this parameter is rapidly reached, so that closed channels play a role only at low collision energies. A good agreement with the available experimental data is obtained.

## Key claim

The complete fusion cross-section for $^6$Li + $^{28}$Si and $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni is fairly insensitive to the upper energy bound used in continuum discretization above a threshold of approximately 10 MeV, with deviations of about 5% between $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=10$ and $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=20$ MeV, contrary to earlier suggestions that closed channels might require much higher energy cutoffs.

## Method

The authors solved the continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) equations using an R-matrix formalism with a Lagrange-Laguerre basis to generate pseudo-states for the $^6$Li projectile, which breaks up into a deuteron and an alpha particle. The complete fusion cross-section was obtained through the CF-ICF model, where the direct complete fusion (DCF) probability and inclusive fragment fusion probabilities are computed from radial integrals of imaginary potentials, and the sequential complete fusion (SCF) probability is taken as the product of the two inclusive fragment fusion probabilities. The Akyuz-Winther potential was used for the real part of the fragment-target interactions, and calculations were performed for center-of-mass energies ranging from subbarrier to well above the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers

- For $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni: the barrier of the folded potential $V_{00}^B$ is 11.6 MeV, compared to the single-channel potential barrier $V_{PT}^B$ of 12.4 MeV (reduction of 0.8 MeV)
- For $^6$Li + $^{28}$Si: $V_{00}^B$ is 6.4 MeV, compared to $V_{PT}^B$ of 7.0 MeV (reduction of 0.6 MeV)
- Convergence in $\varepsilon_{\rm max}$ is reached between 10 and 15 MeV, with deviations of approximately 5% between $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=10$ and $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=20$ MeV
- At $V_{00}^B - 2$ MeV for $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni, removing all closed channels (about 24% of total channels) changes the CF cross-section by about 20%
- Suppression factor at the highest energies: 0.77 for $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni, 0.84 for $^6$Li + $^{28}$Si
- Varying $l_{\rm max}$ from 3 to 4 changes the CF cross-section by approximately 10% at subbarrier energies, with differences becoming negligible a few MeV above the barrier

## Context

This work extends the CF-ICF model previously applied to weakly bound projectiles on heavy targets (Rangel et al. 2020, Canto et al. 2020, Ferreira et al. 2022, 2023) to lighter targets ($^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni). The results contradict the conclusions of Colucci et al. (2023) regarding the importance of very high-energy continuum states, finding instead that low-lying continuum states (below 5 MeV) are the most relevant for fusion. The good agreement with experimental data from Ref. MRR14 for $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni and Refs. SiL17, SMB10 for $^6$Li + $^{28}$Si validates the approach for lighter systems and enables future applications to other weakly bound projectiles on medium-mass targets.
