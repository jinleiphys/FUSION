# First Experimental Test of the Ratio Method

> The ratio is a new reaction observable suggested to extract accurately structure information on halo nuclei. It corresponds to the ratio of differential cross sections for scattering and breakup, which is predicted to remove the uncertainty related to the reaction dynamics. We present here the first experimental test of the method for the 11Be + 12C collision at ELab = 20A MeV performed at Texas A&M University. Differential cross sections for scattering and inclusive one-neutron breakup have been measured with the new detector array BlueSTEAl. The ratio of cross sections is very smooth and independent of the projectile-target interaction, which demonstrates the validity of the ratio method. We extend our analysis to existing 11Be + 208Pb data, confirming that the method works well on any target.

## Key claim

The ratio of differential cross sections for elastic scattering and inclusive one-neutron breakup of halo nuclei is experimentally shown to be smooth and independent of the projectile-target interaction, validating the ratio method as a tool to extract structure information on halo nuclei without the uncertainty from reaction dynamics.

## Method

A 22.8A MeV 11Be beam produced at the Texas A&M University K500 cyclotron was directed onto a natC target, and differential cross sections for elastic scattering and inclusive one-neutron breakup were measured using the BlueSTEAl detector array. The ratio of these cross sections was computed and compared to dynamical reaction calculations using two different choices of optical potentials for the 10Be-C interaction. The analysis was extended to existing 11Be + 208Pb data at 19.1A MeV from Duan et al.

## Key numbers

- 11Be beam energy: 22.8A MeV (produced from 30A MeV 13C primary beam on 9Be target)
- 11Be beam purity: about 60%
- Individual differential cross sections for scattering and breakup vary by about three orders of magnitude over the measured angular range
- Reanalysis of 11Be + 208Pb data at 19.1A MeV from Duan et al. (2022) confirms the method works on any target

## Context

This work provides the first experimental validation of the ratio method, which was previously studied theoretically by Capel et al. (2011, 2013) using accurate reaction calculations compared to the Recoil Excitation and Breakup (REB) model predictions. The method builds on the observation by Crespo et al. (2010) that elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei exhibit similar diffraction patterns. This experimental confirmation enables future studies of heavier halo nuclei such as 19C using the high beam intensity at FRIB.
