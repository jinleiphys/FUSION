# Electron and neutrino scattering off the deuteron in a relativistic framework

> We build a relativistic model to perform calculations of exclusive, semi-exclusive and inclusive unpolarized cross sections and various polarization observables in electron and neutrino scattering experiments with deuteron targets. The strong interaction dynamics is defined by an explicit dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group, where representations of space translations and rotations in the interacting and non-interacting representations are the same. The Argonne V18 potential is used to construct a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction reproducing the experimental deuteron binding energy and nucleon-nucleon scattering observables. Our formalism does not include the pion production channel and neglects two-body contributions in the electromagnetic as well as in the weak nuclear current operator. We show that it is applicable to processes at kinematics, where the internal two-nucleon energy remains below the pion production threshold but the magnitude of the three-momentum transfer extends at least to several GeV.

## Key claim
The authors construct a relativistic model for electron and neutrino scattering off deuterons that is applicable only when the internal two-nucleon energy remains below the pion production threshold, despite allowing three-momentum transfers up to several GeV. The model explicitly neglects two-body contributions in both electromagnetic and weak nuclear current operators and does not include the pion production channel.

## Method
The strong interaction dynamics is defined by a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction, where the Argonne V18 potential is used to construct a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The dynamical mass operator is built from the non-relativistic Hamiltonian such that wave functions and phase shifts are identical to those from the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation. Current matrix elements are computed using the one-boson exchange approximation, with the electromagnetic and weak currents treated in impulse approximation, and covariance is enforced via the Wigner-Eckart theorem for the Poincaré group.

## Key numbers
- The Argonne V18 potential is used to construct the relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, reproducing the experimental deuteron binding energy and nucleon-nucleon scattering observables.
- The fine structure constant α ≈ 1/137 is used for electromagnetic coupling.
- The model is applicable for three-momentum transfers extending at least to several GeV, provided the internal two-nucleon energy remains below the pion production threshold.
- The deuteron wave function includes s-wave (l=0) and d-wave (l=2) components.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic treatment of deuteron electroweak scattering using the Bakamjian-Thomas approach, building on prior work by Kamada et al. (2007) for constructing relativistic interactions from the Argonne V18 potential. The formalism enables consistent calculations of spin-dependent observables in electron and neutrino scattering, which can be compared to experiments to constrain model interactions. The authors note that the model can be extended to include pion production channels and two-body currents in future investigations, and that ignoring two-body currents can help determine which reactions are sensitive to dynamical parts of the current.
