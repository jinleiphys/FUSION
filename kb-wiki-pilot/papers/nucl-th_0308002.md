# Determination of S17 from Systematic Analysis of 8B Coulomb Dissociation

> Systematic analysis of 8B Coulomb dissociation with the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) method is proposed to determine the astrophysical factor S17 accurately. An important advantage of the analysis is that uncertainties of the extracted S17 coming from the use of the ANC method can quantitatively be evaluated, in contrast to previous analyses using the Virtual Photon Theory (VPT). Calculation of measured spectra in dissociation experiments is done by means of the method of Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC). From the analysis of 58Ni(8B,7Be+p)58Ni at 25.8 MeV, S17=22.83 +/- 0.51(theo) +/- 2.28(expt) (eVb) is obtained; the ANC method turned out to work in this case within 1% of error. Preceding systematic analysis of experimental data at intermediate energies, we propose hybrid (HY) Coupled-Channels (CC) calculation of 8B Coulomb dissociation, which makes numerical calculation much simple, retaining its accuracy. The validity of the HY calculation is tested for 58Ni(8B,7Be+p)58Ni at 240 MeV. The ANC method combined with the HY CC calculation is shown to be a powerful technique to obtain a reliable S17.

## Key claim

The authors propose that systematic analysis of 8B Coulomb dissociation using the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) method, rather than the Virtual Photon Theory (VPT), allows quantitative evaluation of uncertainties in the extracted astrophysical factor S17, with the ANC method shown to work within 1% error for the specific case analyzed, though the experimental uncertainty remains large.

## Method

The authors applied the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method to calculate the angular distribution of 7Be fragments from 8B Coulomb dissociation on a 58Ni target at 25.8 MeV, then extracted S17 using the ANC method by fitting the spectroscopic factor and computing the ANC from the product of the square root of the spectroscopic factor and the single-particle ANC. For intermediate energies, they developed a hybrid (HY) Coupled-Channels calculation combining standard CDCC for low partial waves with Eikonal-CDCC (E-CDCC) for high partial waves, testing it on 58Ni(8B,7Be+p)58Ni at 240 MeV.

## Key numbers

- S17(0) = 22.83 +/- 0.51(theo) +/- 2.28(expt) eVb from analysis of 58Ni(8B,7Be+p)58Ni at 25.8 MeV
- ANC value C = 0.59 +/- 0.004 fm^{-1/2}, extracted from the same analysis, showing the ANC method works within 1% error
- Spectroscopic factor S_exp = 0.96 for the Kim 8B wave function and 1.20 for the Esbensen-Bertsch (EB) 8B wave function
- For the HY calculation at 240 MeV, the appropriate connecting point L_C was found to be 400 for total breakup cross section and 200 for elastic cross section, with error less than 1% compared to full quantum-mechanical CDCC
- Maximum number of partial waves required for CDCC at intermediate energies is approximately 15,000 for MSU data at 44 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the ANC method, previously applied to p-transfer reactions (Azhari et al., 1999) and one-nucleon breakup reactions (Trache et al., 2001), to 8B Coulomb dissociation, replacing the Virtual Photon Theory (VPT) used in earlier analyses (RIKEN, GSI, MSU, Notre Dame experiments) which could not quantitatively evaluate uncertainties from nuclear interaction effects and E2 contributions. The CDCC method, developed by the Kyushu group (Kamimura et al., 1986), provides accurate treatment of breakup processes beyond the extended Glauber model used by Trache et al. The proposed HY calculation enables systematic analysis of experimental data across wide energy ranges (from Notre Dame at 3.2 MeV/nucleon to GSI at 250 MeV/nucleon) to determine S17 with reliable uncertainty estimates, which is essential for interpreting 8B solar neutrino flux measurements.
