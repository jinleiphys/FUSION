# Trojan horse method as surrogate indirect approach to study resonant reactions in nuclear astrophysics

> We review the Trojan horse method and its applications to resonant reactions in nuclear astrophysics.

## Key claim

The Trojan horse method (THM) provides an indirect technique to determine astrophysical factors and reaction rates for resonant rearrangement and radiative-capture reactions at very low energies where direct measurements are impossible, but the method relies on approximations because an exact theory of reactions with three charged particles is not available; the paper presents a new few-body formalism that explicitly accounts for off-shell effects and rescattering, addressing the limitations of the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) which fails when the subreaction energy is significantly smaller than the binding energy of the Trojan horse particle.

## Method

The authors develop a theory of the THM based on a surface-integral formalism and the R-matrix approach, treating the reaction as a two-step process: first, a transfer reaction populates a resonance state in the x+A system via the Trojan horse particle a=(sx), and second, the resonance decays into b+B. The prior-form distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) amplitude is split into internal (r_xA ≤ R_ch) and external (r_xA > R_ch) parts, with the internal part transformed into a surface integral at the channel radius R_ch, expressed in terms of observable reduced width amplitudes, boundary conditions, and channel radii from the conventional R-matrix method.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy ε_sx for the virtual decay a → s+x determines the bound-state wave number κ_sx = √(2μ_sx ε_sx), which sets the momentum scale p_sx ≲ κ_sx for quasi-free kinematics.
- The Coulomb parameter of the bound state (i,j) is η_ij = Z_i Z_j e^2 μ_ij / κ_ij, which modifies the singularity of the form factor from a pole to a branching point when both s and x are charged.
- In quasi-free kinematics (p_sx = 0), the x-A relative kinetic energy is E_xA = p_xA(0)^2/(2μ_xA) - ε_sx, and the condition E_xA >> ε_sx is required for the PWIA to be valid.
- The external matrix element in the prior form is expressed in terms of the resonance width Γ_(xA) and the outgoing Coulomb scattering wave O(k_0(xA), r_xA), with k_0(xA) the real part of the x-A resonance momentum.

## Context

This review extends the original THM proposed by Baur (1986) and developed under Spitaleri, which previously relied on the PWIA that neglects Coulomb-nuclear interactions and fails at astrophysically relevant energies E_xA << ε_sx. The new surface-integral approach provides a consistent framework for analyzing transfer reactions populating resonance states, addressing the divergence problem in the post-form DWBA and enabling treatment of subthreshold resonances and resonant radiative-capture reactions such as 12C(α,γ)16O via the indirect reaction 12C(6Li,dγ)16O. The theory enables determination of astrophysical factors for key reactions like 13C(α,n)16O (neutron generator in AGB stars) and carbon-carbon fusion, where direct measurements are hindered by the Coulomb barrier.
