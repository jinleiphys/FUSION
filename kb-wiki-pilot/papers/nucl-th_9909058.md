# Inclusive Scattering of Polarized Electrons on Polarized 3He Effects of Final State Interaction and the Magnetic Form Factor of the Neutron

> Effects of final state interaction on asymmetries in inclusive scattering of polarized electrons on polarized 3He are investigated using consistent 3He bound state wave function and 3N continuum scattering states. Significant effects are found, which influence the extraction of the magnetic neutron form factor from A_T'. The enhancement found experimentally for A_TL' near the 3N breakup threshold, which could not be explained in calculations carried through in plane wave impulse approximation up to now, occurs now also in theory if the full final state interaction is included.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including full final-state interactions (FSI) among the three nucleons in the continuum, treated consistently with the \(^3\)He bound state via exact solutions of three-body Faddeev equations, produces significant effects on the spin-dependent asymmetries \(A_{T'}\) and \(A_{TL'}\). In particular, the experimentally observed enhancement of \(A_{TL'}\) near the three-nucleon breakup threshold, which could not be reproduced by plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations, now appears in theory when FSI is included.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equations using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces (the Bonn B potential) to generate both the \(^3\)He ground-state wave function and the 3N continuum scattering states. They computed the inclusive cross section for polarized electron scattering on polarized \(^3\)He, including the spin-dependent structure functions \(R_{T'}\) and \(R_{TL'}\) via a generalization of the Lorentz integral transform method. The calculations were performed in a nonrelativistic framework with a single-nucleon current operator, and the results were compared to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The principal S-state component of the \(^3\)He wave function accounts for about 92% of the norm (using the Bonn B potential).
- The convection current contribution to \(R_T\) is typically of order \(10^{-3}\) relative to the dominant magnetic term at the momentum transfers considered.
- The transverse asymmetry \(A_{T'}\) (for \(\theta^* = 0^\circ\)) is proportional to \((G_M^{(n)})^2\) in the PWIA principal S-state limit.
- The transverse-longitudinal asymmetry \(A_{TL'}\) (for \(\theta^* = 90^\circ\)) is proportional to \(F_1^{(n)} G_M^{(n)}\) in the PWIA principal S-state limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elastic and inelastic electron scattering on \(^3\)He (references [12–16] by the same group) from unpolarized to polarized experiments, using the same dynamical framework of exact Faddeev solutions with realistic NN forces. It directly addresses the failure of earlier PWIA calculations (references [10,11]) to reproduce the experimental \(A_{TL'}\) enhancement near breakup threshold. The results enable more reliable extraction of the neutron magnetic form factor \(G_M^{(n)}\) from polarized \(^3\)He experiments, which had previously been attempted using the deuteron (reference [2]) or simplified \(^3\)He treatments (references [3,4]).
