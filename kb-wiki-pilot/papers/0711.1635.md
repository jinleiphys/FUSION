# Three-body scattering in Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics

> The relativistic three-nucleon problem is formulated by constructing a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space. Two-body interactions are included that preserve the Poincaré symmetry, lead to the same invariant two-body S-matrix as the corresponding non-relativistic problem, and result in a three-body S-matrix satisfying cluster properties. The resulting Faddeev equations are solved by direct integration, without partial waves for both elastic and breakup reactions at laboratory energies up to 2 Gev.

# Three-body scattering in Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Poincaré-invariant Faddeev equations for three-nucleon scattering can be solved by direct integration (without partial waves) at laboratory energies up to 2 GeV, using a Malfliet-Tjon type two-body interaction that preserves the same invariant two-body S-matrix as the corresponding non-relativistic problem. The authors note that while the model interaction lacks the spin complexities of a realistic interaction, the success suggests the method is suitable for modeling reactions in the few-GeV energy range.

## Method

The authors construct a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space by adding interactions to the mass Casimir operator of the non-interacting irreducible representation. Two-body interactions are chosen so that the relativistic mass square operator \(M^2 = 4(k^2 + m^2) + 4mv_{NN}\) yields the same invariant two-body S-matrix as the non-relativistic Hamiltonian \(H = P^2/4M + k^2/m + v_{NN}\), with the relative momentum \(k\) identified under inverse Lorentz boost. The resulting Faddeev equations for the mass operator are solved by direct integration rather than partial-wave expansion, using the first resolvent equation to obtain off-shell transition matrix elements from half-shell input.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory energies up to **2 GeV** for which converged calculations are demonstrated
- For elastic scattering, the largest difference between relativistic and non-relativistic calculations occurs at angles above **60 degrees** at **1 GeV** beam energy
- The first-order term in the multiple scattering series is not adequate even at **2 GeV** for large angles in elastic scattering

## Context

This work extends the program of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics initiated by Wigner (1939) to three-body scattering at GeV energies, where sub-nuclear degrees of freedom (baryon resonances, meson production channels) become relevant. It builds on the off-shell T-matrix formalism of Keister and Polyzou (2006) and the direct integration methods developed by Liu, Elster, and Glöckle (2005) and Lin et al. (2007). The approach contrasts with partial-wave methods used for realistic interactions at lower energies (Witała et al., 2005), and enables future calculations with realistic spin-dependent interactions at few-GeV energies.
