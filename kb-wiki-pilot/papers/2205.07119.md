# Uncertainty Quantification in Breakup Reactions

> Breakup reactions are one of the favored probes to study loosely bound nuclei, particularly those in the limit of stability forming a halo. In order to interpret such breakup experiments, the continuum discretized coupled channel method is typically used. In this study, the first Bayesian analysis of a breakup reaction model is performed. We use a combination of statistical methods together with a three-body reaction model (the continuum discretized coupled channel method) to quantify the uncertainties on the breakup observables due to the parameters in the effective potential describing the loosely bound projectile of interest. The combination of tools we develop opens the path for a Bayesian analysis of not only breakup processes, but also a wide array of complex processes that require computationally intensive reaction models.

## Key claim

This study performs the first Bayesian analysis of a breakup reaction model, using Gaussian process emulators to circumvent the computational cost of direct Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling with the continuum discretized coupled channel method (CDCC). The analysis is limited to uncertainty from the core-valence effective interaction parameters only, with all other model inputs (optical potentials) fixed to values from prior work.

## Method

The authors generate CDCC calculations using the code FRESCO for the breakup reaction 8B + 208Pb → 7Be + p + 208Pb at 80 MeV/A, varying four parameters of the Woods-Saxon core-valence interaction (Coulomb radius, Woods-Saxon radius, diffuseness, and spin-orbit depth). They train Gaussian process emulators on 393–500 Latin hypercube samples of these parameters, using principal component analysis to reduce the high-dimensional angular and energy distribution outputs, then perform Bayesian calibration via parallel-tempering ensemble Markov chain Monte Carlo to obtain posterior distributions for the interaction parameters and propagate uncertainties to breakup observables.

## Key numbers

- CDCC calculation time: approximately 25 hours per run on a single core
- Training set size: 500 samples for angular distribution emulator; 393 samples after filtering for energy distribution emulator
- Angular distribution output dimension reduced from 401 to 39 principal components (99% variance captured); energy distribution from 20 to 19 principal components
- Combined emulator uses 45-dimensional observable (25 angles within 3 degrees, 20 energy bins) reduced to 32 principal components
- Test r² value for combined emulator: 0.99
- Posterior credible interval for breakup cross sections: roughly 20% uncertainty (within two standard deviations of predicted mean)
- Extracted 8B ANC squared: C² = 0.49 ± 0.01 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends Bayesian uncertainty quantification methods previously applied to elastic scattering within the optical model (Lovell et al. 2017, 2018; King et al. 2019; Catacora et al. 2019, 2021; Whitehead et al. 2021) to the more computationally demanding three-body CDCC framework for breakup reactions. The approach enables rigorous uncertainty propagation from the core-valence interaction parameters to breakup observables, and the methodology is designed to be applicable to other computationally intensive reaction models beyond breakup processes.
