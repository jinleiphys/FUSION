# Investigation of subthreshold resonances with the Trojan horse method

> references added, typo corrected;6 pages, 1 figure, to be published in the AIP Proceedings of Fusion06, International Conference on Reaction Mechanisms and Nuclear Structure at the Coulomb Barrier, March 19-23, 2006, San Servolo, Italy

## Key claim

The Trojan Horse method can be extended to study subthreshold resonances in nuclear systems, with the cross section showing a continuous transition from positive to negative relative energies; the method works because quantities like the modified shape function can be analytically continued below threshold, and the S-matrix element for a closed channel is replaced by a normalization constant from the exponentially decaying wave function.

## Method

The authors extend the inclusive breakup theory of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV) to negative relative energies, and also develop a two-channel model with a surface delta coupling potential where channel 1 (A+x) can be closed or open while channel 2 (C+c) remains open. The logarithmic matching conditions at the surface radius R determine the S-matrix elements, and the model shows how the S-matrix element S_12 for the transition from channel 1 to channel 2 can be extended to closed channels. The standard Breit-Wigner resonance form is recovered with effective resonance parameters E_R and partial widths Gamma_1 and Gamma_2.

## Key numbers

- The product |S_lc * J_l^+|^2 scales as (kR)^3 for neutral particles near threshold, leading to a cross section that tends to a finite constant independent of k
- For neutral particles, the imaginary part of the logarithmic derivative q_l^+ behaves as Im q_l^+ ~ (kappa_1)^(2l+1)/((2l-1)!!)^2 near threshold, where kappa_1 = k_1 R
- The real part of q_l^+ for neutral particles near threshold is Re q_l^+ = -l + O(k^2)
- The example reaction discussed is d + ^6Li -> alpha + ^3He + n, applied to the ^6Li(p,alpha)^3He two-body reaction

## Context

This work extends the Trojan Horse method, originally introduced by Baur et al. (Phys. Rep. 111, 1984) for extracting low-energy astrophysical cross sections, to the study of single-particle states in exotic nuclei around the particle threshold. It builds on the theoretical formulation of Typel and Baur (2002) and the IAV inclusive breakup theory, and relates to the approach of Mukhamedzhanov et al. The method enables experimental investigation of subthreshold resonances via three-body reactions like A + (b+x) -> C + c + b, which is demonstrated with the recent experimental data from Auricchio et al. (2003) on the d + ^6Li reaction.
