# Three-body calculation of deuteron-nucleus scattering using microscopic global optical potential

> We test microscopic global optical potential in three-body calculations of deuteron-nucleus scattering. We solve Faddeev-type equations for three-body transition operators. We calculate differential cross section and analyzing power for the deuteron elastic scattering and breakup in collisions with ${}^{12}$C, ${}^{16}$O and ${}^{24}$Mg nuclei, and find a reasonable agreement with available experimental data. Comparison with respective predictions using phenomenological optical potentials reveals systematic deviations in particular kinematic regimes.

## Key claim

The microscopic global optical potential (MGOP) provides a reasonable description of deuteron elastic scattering and breakup data for 12C, 16O, and 24Mg nuclei in the 100-200 MeV beam energy range, but no single potential (MGOP or phenomenological) accounts for the data perfectly across the entire energy and angle regime; systematic deviations between MGOP and phenomenological predictions appear under particular kinematic conditions, most notably at forward angles.

## Method

The authors solved Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) three-body Faddeev-type equations for transition operators in momentum space, using the MGOP potentials (MGOP1 from relativistic-mean-field and MGOP2 from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS models) as well as the phenomenological Koning-Delaroche (KD) and Weppner et al. optical potentials for the nucleon-nucleus interactions. The neutron-proton interaction was described by the high-precision CD Bonn potential. The MGOP potentials, originally given in coordinate space as sums of 12 Gaussians, were transformed numerically to momentum space, and the proton-nucleus Coulomb force was included via screening and renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam energies studied: 110, 140, 170, and 200 MeV for d + 12C elastic scattering
- Deuteron beam energy 171 MeV for d + 16O elastic scattering
- Deuteron beam energy 170 MeV for d + 24Mg elastic scattering
- Deuteron beam energy 140 MeV for semi-inclusive deuteron breakup on 12C
- MGOP valid for nucleon beam energies 50-400 MeV and nuclei with mass numbers 10-276
- MGOP1 and MGOP2 predictions are "almost indistinguishable" for d + 12C elastic scattering cross sections
- At forward angles below 10 degrees for d + 12C, MGOP predictions are "slightly lower" than Weppner and KD potentials

## Context

This work extends the testing of the microscopic global optical potential developed by Furumoto et al. (2019) from nucleon-nucleus scattering to the more complicated few-body case of deuteron-nucleus reactions. It provides a comparison with the phenomenological optical potentials of Koning and Delaroche (2003) and Weppner et al. (2009), and uses rigorous Faddeev-type AGS equations rather than the more approximate continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method benchmarked by Deltuva et al. (2007). The results support the applicability of MGOP in few-body reaction calculations and identify kinematic regimes where systematic deviations from phenomenological potentials occur, enabling further refinement of microscopic optical models.
