# Nuclear energy release from fragmentation

> Nuclear energy released by splitting Uranium and Thorium isotopes into two, three, four, up to eight fragments with nearly equal size are studied. We found that the energy released come from equally splitting the $^{235,238}$U and $^{230,232}$Th nuclei into to three fragments is largest. The statistical multifragmentation model is employed to calculate the probability of different breakup channels for the excited nuclei. Weighing the the probability distributions of fragments multiplicity at different excitation energies for the $^{238}$U nucleus, we found that an excitation energy between 1.2 and 2 MeV/u is optimal for the $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, $^{230}$Th and $^{232}$Th nuclei to release nuclear energy of about 0.7-0.75 MeV/u.

## Key claim

The paper claims that splitting heavy nuclei (Uranium and Thorium isotopes) into three nearly equal fragments releases the most nuclear energy per nucleon compared to splitting into two, four, five, six, seven, or eight fragments, but this is conditional on the excitation energy being between 1.2 and 2 MeV/u for the process to be practically realizable given the low probability of ternary fission at low excitation energies.

## Method

The authors used the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to calculate the probability of different breakup channels for excited nuclei of $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, $^{230}$Th, and $^{232}$Th. They computed the nuclear energy release from the binding energy difference between initial and final fragments using the AME2012 mass table, and weighted the energy release by the SMM probability distributions for fragment multiplicities at excitation energies from 1 to 4 MeV/u.

## Key numbers

- Splitting $^{238}$U into three equal fragments releases 0.95 MeV/u, compared to 0.7-0.8 MeV/u for two equal fragments
- Splitting $^{238}$U into four equal fragments releases 0.80 MeV/u
- For two, four, and five fragments with nearly equal size, the energy release is about 0.8 MeV/u
- At excitation energy $E^*=1$ MeV/u, the probability is approximately 0.6 for binary events and 0.4 for ternary events for $^{238}$U
- The optimal excitation energy range for releasing nuclear energy of about 0.7-0.75 MeV/u is between 1.2 and 2 MeV/u for $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, $^{230}$Th, and $^{232}$Th

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear energy release beyond the traditional binary fission process, which typically releases about 0.85 MeV/u for n+$^{235}$U reactions. The authors note that ternary fission occurs with a probability of only about 1/300 per binary fission at low excitation energies, and quaternary fission at 1/3000 per binary fission, citing Qian et al. The study uses the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), previously applied to intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions and spallation reactions, to determine the optimal excitation energies where multifragmentation channels become accessible. This enables future investigation of whether controlled multifragmentation could release more nuclear energy than conventional fission.
