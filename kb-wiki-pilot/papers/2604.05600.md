# Channel couplings redirect absorbed flux from peripheral loss to fusion in weakly bound nuclear reactions

> In reactions of weakly bound nuclei, the absorption cross section mixes two physically distinct contributions: inner capture associated with compound-nucleus formation, and peripheral losses from breakup, transfer, and other direct reactions. Within a framework that combines an ingoing-wave boundary condition (IWBC) at an inner radius with a complex potential in the external region, we derive the exact flux identity $σ_{\rm abs}=σ_{\rm fusion}+σ_W$ from the radial continuity equation. The resulting partition is exact within the adopted CC/CDCC model space and provides a practical diagnostic of where absorbed flux is removed. Applied to $^6$Li+$^{209}$Bi, the analysis reveals that channel couplings qualitatively reorganize the absorbed flux: the dominant absorption mechanism shifts from peripheral loss at sub-barrier energies to inner capture above the barrier, whereas the single-channel baseline remains peripheral-loss dominated throughout. The resulting IWBC-defined inner-capture cross section tracks the measured complete-fusion excitation function with only a modest dependence on the chosen boundary radius. Together with the exact identity $σ_{\rm abs}=σ_{\rm fusion}+σ_W$, this agreement supports interpreting the peripheral term $σ_W$ as a major spatial contributor to the well-known CF suppression in weakly bound systems.

# Channel couplings redirect absorbed flux from peripheral loss to fusion in weakly bound nuclear reactions

## Key claim

Channel couplings qualitatively reorganize the absorbed flux in reactions of weakly bound nuclei, shifting the dominant absorption mechanism from peripheral loss at sub-barrier energies to inner capture above the barrier, whereas the single-channel baseline remains peripheral-loss dominated throughout. The exact flux identity σ_abs = σ_fusion + σ_W holds within the adopted CC/CDCC model space and supports interpreting the peripheral term σ_W as a major spatial contributor to the well-known complete-fusion suppression in weakly bound systems.

## Method

The authors derive an exact flux decomposition from the radial continuity equation for a coupled-channel system divided at an inner radius r_a, combining an ingoing-wave boundary condition (IWBC) at r_a with a complex potential in the external region. They apply this formalism to the benchmark weakly bound system ^6Li+^209Bi, performing both single-channel optical-model calculations and multichannel continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations in which ^6Li is described as an α+d cluster with the breakup continuum discretized into bins up to 12 MeV relative kinetic energy and relative orbital angular momentum ℓ ≤ 2.

## Key numbers

- For the single-channel calculation at 52 MeV: σ_abs = 1888.8 mb, σ_fusion = 715.5 mb, σ_W = 1182.0 mb (fusion fraction f_fusion = 0.38)
- For the multichannel calculation at 52 MeV: σ_abs = 1770.8 mb, σ_fusion = 1193.7 mb, σ_W = 577.1 mb (fusion fraction f_fusion = 0.67)
- For the multichannel calculation at 26 MeV: σ_abs = 39.4 mb, σ_fusion = 1.7 mb, σ_W = 37.6 mb (fusion fraction f_fusion = 0.04)
- The two fractions cross near E_lab ≈ 36 MeV in the coupled-channel case
- The IWBC radius is set to r_a = 10 fm, corresponding to the nuclear touching configuration R_P+R_T ≈ 1.3(A_P^{1/3}+A_T^{1/3}) fm, with sensitivity quantified by varying r_a over 9.5–10.5 fm
- Closure error ε/σ_abs remains below 5×10^{-3} for single-channel and |ε|/σ_abs < 10^{-4} for multichannel calculations

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Satchler (relating imaginary potentials to total reaction cross section via the optical theorem), Udagawa et al. (separating direct-reaction and compound-nucleus contributions within a local-potential model), and the generalized optical theorem applied in CDCC to extract elastic-breakup components from total absorption. The derived exact spatial partition provides a diagnostic tool for coupled-channel reaction dynamics that was previously absent, enabling a sharper basis for discussing fusion suppression and enhancement in weakly bound systems. The framework can be extended to other weakly bound projectiles such as ^7Li, ^9Be, and ^11Be to test whether the crossover between peripheral loss and inner capture is a generic feature.
