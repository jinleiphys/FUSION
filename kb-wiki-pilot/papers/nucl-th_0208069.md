# Theory of the Trojan-Horse Method

> The Trojan-Horse method is an indirect approach to determine the energy dependence of S-factors of astrophysically relevant two-body reactions. This is accomplished by studying closely related three-body reactions under quasi-free scattering conditions. The basic theory of the Trojan-Horse method is developed starting from a post-form distorted wave Born approximation of the T-matrix element. In the surface approximation the cross section of the three-body reaction can be related to the S-matrix elements of the two-body reaction. The essential feature of the Trojan-Horse method is the effective suppression of the Coulomb barrier at low energies for the astrophysical reaction leading to finite cross sections at the threshold of the two-body reaction. In a modified plane wave approximation the relation between the two-body and three-body cross sections becomes very transparent. The appearing Trojan-Horse integrals are studied in detail.

## Key claim

The Trojan-Horse method (THM) can extract the energy dependence of astrophysical S-factors for two-body reactions by studying three-body reactions under quasi-free scattering conditions, with the essential feature being the effective suppression of the Coulomb barrier at low energies leading to finite cross sections at the threshold of the two-body reaction. The authors develop the theory starting from a post-form distorted wave Born approximation of the T-matrix element, but the actual relation between three-body and two-body cross sections relies on the surface approximation and plane wave approximations that may affect the absolute cross section while preserving the correct energy dependence.

## Method

The T-matrix element for the three-body reaction A + a → C + c + b is derived in a post-form distorted wave Born approximation, where the Trojan Horse a is assumed to be composed of clusters x and b. The surface approximation replaces the full scattering wave function of the C+c system by its asymptotic form for radii larger than a cutoff radius R, and the potential V_{xb} describing the interaction between x and b in the Trojan Horse is related to the ground state momentum wave function of a. In a modified plane wave approximation for both the initial A-a and final B-b distorted waves, the T-matrix element factorizes into a momentum amplitude W and a matrix element containing the Trojan-Horse integrals J_l^{(\pm)}, which involve Coulomb wave functions and spherical Bessel functions integrated from the cutoff radius R to infinity.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff radius R is typically in the range of the sum of the radii of the two colliding nuclei
- The surface approximation was checked for the (d,p) reaction at E_d = 26 MeV and found to be quite good
- The binding energy ε_a = (m_x + m_b - m_a)c^2 = Q_2 - Q_3 > 0 appears in the relation between the momentum amplitude W and the ground state momentum wave function Φ_a
- The Trojan-Horse integrals J_l^{(H)}(R,η,k,Q) = k ∫_R^∞ dr H_l(η;kr) z_l(Qr) are regularized by transforming to a form with a converging integral I_l^{(H)} and surface terms, with the condition k < Q from kinematical considerations

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical considerations of the THM developed in Typ00 and provides a more rigorous foundation than the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) used in previous quasi-free scattering studies (Jai70). The THM is complementary to the Coulomb dissociation method (Bau94) and the ANC method (Azh01, Xu94), which are limited to radiative capture reactions, whereas the THM can be applied to reactions where no photon is involved. The theory enables extraction of two-body S-matrix elements from three-body cross section measurements, with applications to reactions such as those studied in Mus01, Spi01, Che96, Lat01, Ali00, Spi99, Cal97, Spi00, and Pel00.
