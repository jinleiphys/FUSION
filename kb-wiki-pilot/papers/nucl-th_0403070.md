# A Semiclassical Approach to Fusion Reactions

> The semiclassical method of Alder and Winther is generalized to study fusion reactions. As an illustration, we evaluate the fusion cross section in a schematic two-channel calculation. The results are shown to be in good agreement with those obtained with a quantal Coupled-Channels calculation. We suggest that in the case of coupling to continuum states this approach may provide a simpler alternative to the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels method.

## Key claim

The semiclassical Alder-Winther method can be generalized to evaluate complete fusion cross sections in collisions of weakly bound projectiles, and in a schematic two-channel calculation the results agree well with quantal Coupled-Channels calculations. However, this good agreement does not hold at sub-barrier energies because the classical trajectory does not reach the barrier radius and therefore the effective barrier lowering that enhances the cross section is not accounted for.

## Method

The authors generalized the Alder-Winther semiclassical method by using classical mechanics for the projectile-target separation vector with a trajectory determined by the ground-state expectation value of the interaction potential, while treating the intrinsic degrees of freedom quantum mechanically via time-dependent Schrodinger equation. The fusion probability for each partial wave was approximated as the product of the tunneling probability and the probability that the projectile is in a given intrinsic state at the time of closest approach. A schematic two-channel calculation was performed for the scattering of 6He projectiles on a 238U target, representing the breakup channel by a single effective channel with an electric dipole coupling form factor.

## Key numbers

- Breakup threshold energy for 6He dissociation into 4He and two neutrons: B = 0.975 MeV
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters used: V0 = -50 MeV, r0r = 1.25 fm, ar = 0.65 fm, W0 = -50 MeV, r0i = 1.0 fm, ai = 0.65 fm

## Context

This work extends the Alder-Winther semiclassical method, originally developed for Coulomb excitation, to fusion reactions involving weakly bound projectiles where coupling to the breakup channel is important. It provides a simpler alternative to the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method used by Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002) and Hagino et al. (2000), and can be considered an improved semi-quantal version of the classical three-body model of Hinde et al. (2002). The approach enables future calculations of both complete and incomplete fusion cross sections for reactions with weakly bound projectiles, including coupling to continuum states.
