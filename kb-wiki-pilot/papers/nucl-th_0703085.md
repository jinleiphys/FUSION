# Coupled-channels effects in elastic scattering and near-barrier fusion induced by weakly bound nuclei and exotic halo nuclei

> The influence on fusion of coupling to the breakup process is investigated for reactions where at least one of the colliding nuclei has a sufficiently low binding energy for breakup to become an important process. Elastic scattering, excitation functions for sub-and near-barrier fusion cross sections, and breakup yields are analyzed for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co. Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations describe well the data at and above the barrier. Elastic scattering with $^{6}$Li (as compared to $^{7}$Li) indicates the significant role of breakup for weakly bound projectiles. A study of $^{4,6}$He induced fusion reactions with a three-body CDCC method for the $^6$He halo nucleus is presented. The relative importance of breakup and bound-state structure effects on total fusion is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether breakup couplings enhance or suppress fusion for weakly bound and halo nuclei, finding that Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations describe elastic scattering and fusion data well at and above the barrier, but the effect of breakup on total fusion is small for stable weakly bound projectiles like $^{6,7}$Li, while for the halo nucleus $^{6}$He the CDCC calculations overpredict measured total fusion cross sections by 20-30%, suggesting that other couplings (such as single neutron stripping) may reduce the fusion cross section.

## Method

Standard three-body CDCC calculations were performed assuming $\alpha+d$ and $\alpha+t$ cluster structures for $^{6}$Li and $^{7}$Li respectively, with the continuum discretized into bins in momentum space up to $k_{\mathrm{max}} = 1.0$ fm$^{-1}$ and couplings up to multipolarity $\lambda = 3$ included. For $^{6}$He, a simplified two-body di-neutron model ($^{4}$He + $^{2}$n) was used with the CDCC fusion model, including couplings to the $2^{+}$ resonant state and non-resonant continuum states up to f-waves and a maximum continuum energy of 8 MeV. All calculations were carried out using the code FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- $^{6}$Li breakup threshold: $S_\alpha = 1.47$ MeV; $^{7}$Li breakup threshold: $S_\alpha = 2.47$ MeV
- For $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, calculated breakup cross sections are 3.7-9.7% of total reaction cross sections (e.g., 61.4 mb out of 1581 mb at 30 MeV, 7.46 mb out of 116.0 mb at 12 MeV)
- For $^{7}$Li+$^{59}$Co, calculated breakup cross sections are 0.6-1.0% of total reaction cross sections (e.g., 16.6 mb out of 1610 mb at 30 MeV, 0.31 mb out of 83.5 mb at 12 MeV)
- Measured total fusion cross sections for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co: 988 $\pm$ 199 mb at 26 MeV, 467 $\pm$ 94 mb at 18 MeV, 57 $\pm$ 12 mb at 12 MeV
- Measured total fusion cross sections for $^{7}$Li+$^{59}$Co: 1014 $\pm$ 204 mb at 26 MeV, 547 $\pm$ 110 mb at 18 MeV, 38 $\pm$ 8 mb at 12 MeV
- For $^{6}$He+$^{65}$Cu at 30 MeV: CDCC with continuum couplings gives 1846 mb, measured total residue cross section is 1334 mb; at 19.5 MeV: CDCC gives 1606 mb, measured is 1292 mb
- For $^{6}$He+$^{63}$Cu at 30 MeV: CDCC with continuum couplings gives 1830 mb, measured total residue cross section is 1400 mb
- $^{6}$He $\alpha$-$^{2}$n ground state binding energy: -0.975 MeV; $2^{+}$ resonant state energy: 0.826 MeV with width 0.075 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous CDCC studies of weakly bound nuclei (e.g., $^{6,7}$Li scattering by Sakuragi et al., and $^{11}$Be fusion by Diaz-Torres and Thompson) to the $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co systems and to $^{6}$He-induced fusion on medium-mass targets. The results contradict earlier predictions of enhanced sub-barrier fusion for halo nuclei (Hagino et al.) and instead support findings from experiments with $^{6}$He beams (Raabe et al., Navin et al., Di Pietro et al.) that the halo does not enhance fusion probability, while highlighting the need for future exclusive measurements to disentangle breakup, transfer, and fusion components.
