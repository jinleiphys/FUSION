# Inclusive breakup reactions with non-spectator fragments: Generalization of the IAV sum rules

> The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) sum rule formalism for inclusive breakup reactions $a + A \to b + \mathrm{anything}$ treats the detected fragment $b$ as a spectator by replacing its interaction with the target by an optical potential. This assumption becomes questionable when $b$ is a loosely bound composite particle such as a deuteron. I derive a generalization that removes the spectator approximation and retains $b$'s internal degrees of freedom, providing state-resolved inclusive cross sections. Within the DWBA, all non-spectator effects enter through the source function via the operator $V_{bA} - U_{bA}$. The exact sum rule involves the full $x + A$ resolvent $(E_{x,0}^+ - H_{xA})^{-1}$, while a single-channel IAV-like expression is recovered only when the explicit target dependence of $V_{bA}$ is neglected; post-prior equivalence is preserved in both cases. A key conceptual finding is that the standard IAV result for structureless $b$ corresponds, under closure, to the \emph{total} inclusive cross section summed over all of $b$'s internal states, rather than the cross section for $b$ in a specific state. An operator-level estimate for $b = d$ on ${}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ shows that the non-spectator correction is not a small perturbation at the nuclear surface. The present work is purely formal: it establishes the theoretical framework and identifies the relevant operators, while quantitative assessment of the cross-section impact awaits a full numerical evaluation of the source integrals.

## Key claim

The standard IAV sum rule for inclusive breakup reactions, which treats the detected fragment \(b\) as a spectator by replacing its interaction with the target by an optical potential, actually corresponds under closure to the *total* inclusive cross section summed over all of \(b\)'s internal states rather than the cross section for \(b\) in a specific state; the generalization derived here removes the spectator approximation and provides state-resolved inclusive cross sections, with all non-spectator effects entering through the operator \(V_{bA} - U_{bA}\) within the DWBA.

## Method

The author derives a generalization of the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) sum rule formalism by retaining the full internal Hamiltonian \(h_b(\zeta)\) of the detected fragment \(b\) and the full interaction \(V_{bA}\) between \(b\) and the target \(A\), rather than replacing \(V_{bA}\) with an optical potential \(U_{bA}\). The post-form DWBA transition amplitude is constructed using the exit-channel Hamiltonian \(H_{\mathrm{exit}} = h_b + K_b + U_{bB} + H_{xA}\), and the source function \(|\rho_0\rangle\) is defined by projecting \(V_{\mathrm{post}}|\chi_a^{(+)}\varphi_a\Phi_A\rangle\) onto \(b\)'s ground state \(\phi_0\) and the distorted wave \(\chi_b^{(-)}\), integrating over \(b\)'s spatial and internal coordinates. The exact sum rule involves the full \(x + A\) resolvent \((E_{x,0}^+ - H_{xA})^{-1}\), while a single-channel IAV-like expression is recovered only when the explicit target dependence of \(V_{bA}\) is neglected; post-prior equivalence is preserved in both cases.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is 2.224 MeV, and its root-mean-square proton-neutron separation is approximately 3.9 fm, making it highly susceptible to polarization, excitation, and breakup in nuclear and Coulomb fields.
- An operator-level estimate for \(b = d\) on \({}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) shows that the non-spectator correction is not a small perturbation at the nuclear surface.
- The projectile binding energy \(\epsilon_a > 0\) appears in the bound-state equation for \(\varphi_a(\mathbf{r}_{bx}, \zeta)\).
- The energy available to the \(x + A\) system when \(b\) is in its ground state is \(E_{x,0} = E - E_b - \varepsilon_0\), where \(E_b = \hbar^2 k_b^2/(2\mu_b)\) and \(\mu_b\) is the \(b\)-\((x+A)\) reduced mass.

## Context

This work extends the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formalism developed by Udagawa, Tamura, and collaborators (the prior-form or UT approach) and independently by Austern, Vincent, and Ichimura (the post-form or AV approach), which treats the detected fragment \(b\) as a spectator. The generalization addresses a limitation noted by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent themselves in Sec. V of their 1985 paper, where they remarked that "the importance of excitation during breakup should be no surprise" and that "a reduced theory of a many-body system, in terms of the coordinates of only a few 'active' particles, requires the careful introduction of effective interactions." The formalism enables future quantitative assessment of non-spectator effects in reactions where \(b\) is a loosely bound composite particle such as a deuteron (e.g., in \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}\)-, \({}^{7}\mathrm{Li}\)-, or triton-induced reactions) or an unbound fragment like \({}^{8}\mathrm{Be}\) in \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be}\)-induced reactions, and provides the theoretical framework for extending existing IAV codes by replacing the spectator source \(\tilde\rho_0\) with the total source \(\tilde\rho_0^{\mathrm{tot}}\).
