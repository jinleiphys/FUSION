# The continuum discretized coupled-channels method and its applications

> This is a review on recent developments of the continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) and its applications to nuclear physics, cosmology and astrophysics, and nuclear engineering. The theoretical foundation of CDCC is shown, and a microscopic reaction theory for nucleus-nucleus scattering is constructed as an underlying theory of CDCC. CDCC is then extended to treat Coulomb breakup and four-body breakup. We also propose a new theory that makes CDCC applicable to inclusive reactions

## Key claim

This review argues that the continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) is an accurate and theoretically well-founded approach for treating projectile breakup in nuclear reactions, and that its recent extensions (eikonal-CDCC, four-body CDCC, eikonal reaction theory) make it applicable to Coulomb breakup, four-body breakup, and inclusive reactions, provided the model-space parameters (angular momentum cutoff, momentum cutoff, and bin width) are chosen to yield converged S-matrix elements.

## Method

The authors recapitulate three-body CDCC, in which the total wave function is expanded in eigenfunctions of the projectile's internal Hamiltonian (including bound and discretized continuum states), with truncation in orbital angular momentum and linear momentum followed by discretization of the continuum via average, midpoint, or pseudo-state methods. They then construct a microscopic reaction theory for nucleus-nucleus scattering based on the multiple scattering theory with an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (Brueckner g-matrix or t-matrix), which reduces to the double-folding model when breakup is weak. Extensions presented include eikonal-CDCC for Coulomb-dominated breakup, four-body CDCC for three-body projectiles, and the eikonal reaction theory for inclusive breakup reactions.

## Key numbers

- The eikonal approximation for nucleon-nucleon scattering is tested at 150 MeV laboratory energy using the JLM g-matrix, showing good agreement between exact and eikonal calculations
- The Brieva-Rook localization of nonlocal microscopic optical potentials is validated for proton elastic scattering from 90Zr at incident energies from 65 to 800 MeV, working well for momentum transfers q less than about 2 fm^-1 at 65 MeV and q less than about 4 fm^-1 at 800 MeV
- The double-folding model with the Melbourne g-matrix reproduces 12C+12C elastic scattering data at 135 MeV/nucleon and reaction cross sections for 12C scattering from 12C, 20Ne, 23Na, and 27Al
- Reaction cross sections were measured by Takechi et al. for 28-32Ne isotopes near or in the island of inversion
- A halo structure of 31Ne was reported by Nakamura et al. from one-neutron removal cross section experiments

## Context

This review extends the earlier CDCC reviews by Kamimura (1986) and Austern (1987), which treated nuclear breakup but not Coulomb breakup and relied mainly on phenomenological optical potentials. The present work constructs a microscopic foundation for CDCC via the multiple scattering theory, generalizing the Kerman-McManus-Thaler formalism from nucleon-nucleus to nucleus-nucleus scattering. The developments enable CDCC to be applied to unstable nuclei (including halo nuclei like 31Ne), reactions relevant to cosmology and astrophysics (nucleosynthesis in the early universe, stellar evolution, supernova explosions), and nuclear engineering (the Li(d,n) reaction for the international fusion material irradiation facility).
