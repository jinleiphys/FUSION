# Anisotropic escape mechanism and elliptic flow of bottomonia

> We study the role of anisotropic escape in generating the elliptic flow of bottomonia produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We implement temperature-dependent decay widths for the various bottomonium states, to calculate their survival probability when traversing through the anisotropic hot medium formed in non-central collisions. We employ the recently developed 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamic simulation to model the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. We provide a quantitative prediction for transverse momentum dependence of bottomonium elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor for Pb$\,+\,$Pb collisions in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

The anisotropic escape mechanism generates a few-percent-level elliptic flow ($v_2$) for bottomonia in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV, but this prediction is conditional on the assumption that recombination effects are negligible for bottomonia and that the observed $v_2$ arises purely from path-length-dependent survival in the anisotropic medium.

## Method

The authors used the 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamic (QaHydro) framework to simulate the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, with initial conditions from an optical Glauber model and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s = 0.2$. They implemented temperature-dependent complex in-medium decay widths for five bottomonium states ($\Upsilon(1S)$, $\Upsilon(2S)$, $\Upsilon(3S)$, $\chi_b(1P)$, $\chi_b(2P)$) obtained from solving the 3D Schrodinger equation with a complex heavy-quark potential, then calculated survival probabilities by integrating these decay widths along straight-line trajectories through the evolving medium. Feed-down contributions from excited states to the $\Upsilon(1S)$ yield were included using fixed fractions from $p+p$ data.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature: $T_0 = 600$ MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: $\eta/s = 0.2$
- Hydrodynamic evolution start time: $\tau = 0.2$ fm/$c$
- Freeze-out temperature: $T_f = 130$ MeV
- Critical temperature: $T_c = 160$ MeV
- Intrinsic formation times for bottomonium states: $\tau^0_{\rm form} = 0.2$ fm/$c$ ($\Upsilon(1S)$), 0.4 fm/$c$ ($\Upsilon(2S)$, $\chi_b(1P)$), 0.6 fm/$c$ ($\Upsilon(3S)$, $\chi_b(2P)$)
- Feed-down fractions to $\Upsilon(1S)$: $f_{2S\rightarrow 1S}=8.6\%$, $f_{3S\rightarrow 1S}=1\%$, $f_{1P\rightarrow 1S}=17\%$, $f_{2P \rightarrow 1S}=5.1\%$, $f_{3P \rightarrow 1S}=1.5\%$
- Centrality class for main results: $40-50\%$ with average impact parameter $10.41$ fm
- Maximum $v_2$ for $\Upsilon(1S)$ inclusive yield is obtained for $50-60\%$ centrality
- $\Upsilon(1S)$ melting temperature: approximately 900 MeV

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic escape mechanism previously applied to $J/\psi$ by Wang (2002) and to bottomonia in qualitative discussions by Das et al. (2018), providing the first quantitative prediction for bottomonium elliptic flow using realistic 3+1d hydrodynamics. It builds on prior calculations of temperature-dependent bottomonium decay widths by Strickland and collaborators (2011-2017) and uses the QaHydro framework previously validated against ALICE identified-particle spectra at 2.76 TeV. The results are consistent with the transport model findings of Du et al. (2017) and enable future comparisons with the first ALICE measurement of $\Upsilon(1S)$ $v_2$ at 5.02 TeV (Acharya et al., 2019), as well as motivate inclusion of medium-induced bound-state transitions predicted by open quantum system approaches.
