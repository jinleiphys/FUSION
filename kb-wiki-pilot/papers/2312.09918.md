# Improved Wong and classical approximations, and reduction of fusion data

> We present an improved version of the Wong formula for heavy-ion fusion, where the parameters of the parabolic approximation of the Coulomb barrier are replaced by parameters of the l-dependent potential at an effective partial-wave. A pocket formula for this l-dependence is given. This version reproduces the fusion cross sections of quantum mechanical calculations very well, even when the original Wong formula is not valid. The same procedure is used to derive an improved expression for the classical fusion cross section, which is very accurate at above-barrier energies. Based on this classical expression, we propose a new method to reduce fusion data in this energy range. This method is used to perform a comparative study of complete fusion suppression in collisions of weakly bound projectiles. This study indicates that the suppression of complete fusion is essentially due to the action of nuclear breakup couplings.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved Wong formula for heavy-ion fusion cross sections that replaces the s-wave barrier parameters with effective parameters obtained from an angular-momentum-dependent potential at a grazing partial wave, and this improved version reproduces quantum mechanical calculations even where the original Wong formula fails. The authors also propose a new data reduction method based on an improved classical expression, and their comparative study indicates that complete fusion suppression in weakly bound projectile collisions is essentially due to nuclear breakup couplings rather than static effects.

## Method

The authors first benchmarked the barrier penetration model (BPM) against full quantum mechanical one-channel calculations with the Sao Paulo potential and short-range absorption, establishing BPM as the reference cross section. They then derived improved versions of the Wong formula and classical fusion cross section by replacing the s-wave barrier radius \(R_B\) and curvature \(\hbar\omega\) with effective parameters obtained from an angular-momentum-dependent potential at the grazing angular momentum \(\lambda_g\), using a quadratic expansion of the barrier radius in \(\lambda\). The improved classical expression was used to develop a fusion data reduction method, which was applied to compare complete fusion suppression for \(^6\)Li, \(^7\)Li, \(^9\)Be, and \(^6\)He projectiles on various targets.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^7\)Li + \(^{27}\)Al system at 30 MeV above the Coulomb barrier, the original Wong formula overestimates the BPM cross section by approximately 45%.
- For the \(^7\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi system at 30 MeV above the Coulomb barrier, the original Wong formula overestimates BPM by 10%.
- For the \(^{24}\)Mg + \(^{138}\)Ba system at 30 MeV above the Coulomb barrier, the original Wong formula overestimates BPM by approximately 5%.
- The ratio \(R_g^2 / R_B^2\) at \(E = V_B + 30\) MeV is 0.53 for \(^7\)Li + \(^{27}\)Al, 0.83 for \(^7\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi, and 0.91 for \(^{24}\)Mg + \(^{138}\)Ba.
- The Coulomb barrier parameters for \(^7\)Li + \(^{27}\)Al: \(R_B = 8.5\) fm, \(V_B = 6.1\) MeV, \(\hbar\omega = 2.4\) MeV.
- The Coulomb barrier parameters for \(^7\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi: \(R_B = 11.4\) fm, \(V_B = 29.4\) MeV, \(\hbar\omega = 4.1\) MeV.
- The Coulomb barrier parameters for \(^{24}\)Mg + \(^{138}\)Ba: \(R_B = 11.1\) fm, \(V_B = 81.6\) MeV, \(\hbar\omega = 3.9\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Wong formula (Wong, 1973) and the classical fusion cross section by addressing their known limitations for light systems and at energies far from the barrier, as previously discussed by Canto et al. (2009a, 2009b, 2009c). The improved classical expression enables a new reduction method that extends the fusion function (FF) reduction approach, which was based on the original Wong formula and reviewed by Canto et al. (2015). The method is used to study complete fusion suppression in weakly bound projectiles, building on prior work by Rowley and Hagino (2015) who proposed replacing \(R_B\) with \(R_g\) in the Wong formula, and enables comparative studies of fusion suppression across different weakly bound systems.
