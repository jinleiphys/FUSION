# Exploring the breakup and transfer coupling effects in 9Be elastic scattering

> Two cluster structures of 9Be namely, 5He+4He and 8Be+n have been considered for its breakup to describe the available data on elastic scattering for 9Be projectile with 28Si, 64Zn and 144Sm targets. The results of these calculations suggest that the breakup coupling effects are significant for 5He+4He cluster model at above the barrier energies, while they are dominant at relatively lower energies for 8Be+n model. The addition of one neutron stripping channel in the 8Be+n model gives an overall good description of the elastic data for all the systems considered. The couplings generated by breakup in 8Be+n model have different behaviour than the coupling effects obtained within the 5He+4He model, the former being more prominent at lower energies and for the heavier target systems. The behaviour of extracted dynamic polarisation potentials (DPP) generated due to breakup and one neutron transfer couplings have been investigated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the breakup coupling effects in 9Be elastic scattering are significant for the 5He+4He cluster model at above-barrier energies, while they are dominant at relatively lower energies for the 8Be+n model, with the addition of one neutron stripping channel in the 8Be+n model giving an overall good description of the elastic data for all systems considered.

## Method

Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations were performed for 9Be elastic scattering on 28Si, 64Zn, and 144Sm targets, treating 9Be as either a 5He+4He or 8Be+n two-body cluster. Coupled Reaction Channel (CRC) calculations were additionally performed for the one neutron stripping channel in the 8Be+n model, using the code FRESCO. Dynamic Polarisation Potentials (DPP) were extracted from the coupled channels calculations to investigate the nature of the coupling effects.

## Key numbers

- Breakup thresholds: 2.46 MeV for 5He+4He cluster, 1.67 MeV for 8Be+n cluster
- Q-values for one neutron stripping: 6.81 MeV (28Si target), 6.31 MeV (64Zn target), 5.09 MeV (144Sm target)
- Spectroscopic amplitudes for 9Be ground state in 5He+4He model: 0.81 (L=0 component) and 0.5358 (L=2 component)
- Strong absorption radii used for DPP extraction: 11.3 fm (144Sm target), 9.4 fm (64Zn target), 8.2 fm (28Si target)
- Incident energies studied: 12-40 MeV range for 28Si, 26-40 MeV for 64Zn, 37-50 MeV for 144Sm

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Pandit et al. (2011) on 9Be+208Pb at sub-Coulomb energies and Keeley et al. (2001) on 9Be+208Pb at around-barrier energies, validating both cluster models for lighter targets. The results enable systematic understanding of how breakup and transfer coupling effects evolve with target mass and incident energy, and suggest the need for full four-body CDCC calculations treating 9Be as an alpha+alpha+n three-body system.
