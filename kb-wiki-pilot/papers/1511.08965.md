# Theoretical study of fusion reactions $^{32}$S + $^{94,96}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca + $^{94,96}$Zr and quadrupole deformation of $^{94}$Zr

> The dynamic coupling effects on fusion cross sections for reactions $^{32}$S + $^{94,96}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca + $^{94,96}$Zr are studied with the universal fusion function formalism and an empirical coupled channel (ECC) model. An examination of the reduced fusion functions shows that the total effect of couplings to inelastic excitations and neutron transfer channels on fusion in $^{32}$S + $^{94}$Zr ($^{40}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr) is almost the same as that in $^{32}$S + $^{96}$Zr ($^{40}$Ca + $^{96}$Zr). The enhancements of the fusion cross section at sub-barrier energies due to inelastic channel coupling and neutron transfer channel coupling are evaluated separately by using the ECC model. The results show that effect of couplings to inelastic excitations channels in the reactions with $^{94}$Zr as target should be similar as that in the reactions with $^{96}$Zr as target. This implies that the quadrupole deformation parameters $β_2$ of $^{94}$Zr and $^{96}$Zr should be similar to each other. However, $β_2$'s predicted from the finite-range droplet model, which are used in the ECC model, are quite different. Experiments on $^{48}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr or $^{36}$S + $^{94}$Zr are suggested to solve the puzzling issue concerning $β_2$ for $^{94}$Zr.

## Key claim
The total dynamic coupling effect (inelastic excitations plus neutron transfer) on sub-barrier fusion is nearly identical for reactions with $^{94}$Zr and $^{96}$Zr targets, which implies that the quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta_2$ of these two isotopes should be similar, contradicting the quite different values ($0.062$ vs $0.217$) predicted by the finite-range droplet model used in the empirical coupled channel calculations.

## Method
The authors applied the universal fusion function (UFF) formalism to eliminate geometrical and static potential effects, then used an empirical coupled channel (ECC) model to separately evaluate enhancements from inelastic excitations and from positive $Q$-value neutron transfer (PQNT) channels. The ECC model introduces an asymmetric Gaussian barrier distribution whose parameters depend on quadrupole deformation from the finite-range droplet model, with neutron transfer effects simulated by broadening the distribution according to the $Q(2n)$ value.

## Key numbers
- $Q(2n)$ values for one neutron pair transfer: $^{32}$S + $^{94}$Zr = 5.10 MeV, $^{32}$S + $^{96}$Zr = 5.74 MeV, $^{40}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr = 4.89 MeV, $^{40}$Ca + $^{96}$Zr = 5.53 MeV
- FRDM $\beta_2$ values: $^{94}$Zr = 0.062, $^{96}$Zr = 0.217
- Experimental $\beta_2$ from $B(E2)$: $^{94}$Zr = 0.09, $^{96}$Zr = 0.08
- RMF (NL3) $\beta_2$: $^{94}$Zr = 0.169, $^{96}$Zr = 0.243
- RMF (NL3*) $\beta_2$: $^{94}$Zr = 0.002, $^{96}$Zr = 0.233
- Barrier parameters for $^{32}$S + $^{94}$Zr: $V_B$ = 80.64 MeV, $\hbar\omega$ = 3.95 MeV, $R_B$ = 10.64 fm
- Barrier parameters for $^{40}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr: $V_B$ = 99.07 MeV, $\hbar\omega$ = 3.97 MeV, $R_B$ = 10.84 fm

## Context
This work extends previous ECC model studies of 217 reaction systems (Wang et al., arXiv:1504.00756) where calculations underestimated sub-barrier cross sections for $^{32}$S + $^{94}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr. It builds on quantum coupled channel calculations by Jia et al. (2014, PRC89-064605) that suggested stronger neutron transfer effects in $^{94}$Zr reactions. The results enable future experiments on $^{48}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr or $^{36}$S + $^{94}$Zr (where PQNT channels are closed) to resolve the puzzling discrepancy in $\beta_2$ for $^{94}$Zr.
