# Reaction Theory and Advanced CDCC

> The Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) has been successfully employed to describe elastic and breakup of nuclear reactions induced by weakly bound projectiles. In this contribution, we review some other, less widespread applications of the CDCC wavefunction, some of them in combination with other reaction formalisms, which are being currently employed in the analysis of reactions involving three or more fragments in the initial or final state.

# Reaction Theory and Advanced CDCC

## Key claim

The Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method, originally developed for two-body projectiles and elastic scattering, can be extended and combined with other reaction formalisms to describe reactions involving three or more fragments in the initial or final state, including transfer, knockout, and non-elastic breakup processes, though the accuracy of these combined approaches depends on the specific reaction and energy regime.

## Method

The CDCC method expands the three-body scattering wavefunction in terms of eigenstates of the projectile internal Hamiltonian, including both bound and discretized continuum states (via pseudo-state or binning methods), and solves coupled-channel equations for the projectile-target relative motion. Extended versions incorporate target excitations via deformed fragment-target potentials (XCDCC) and projectile fragment excitations using particle-rotor models. The CDCC wavefunction is then used within transition amplitudes for transfer reactions (post-form for (d,p) and prior-form for (p,pN) and transfer to unbound states) and within the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formalism for non-elastic breakup calculations.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{10}\text{Be}(d,p)^{11}\text{Be}$ reaction at two deuteron incident energies, both deuteron breakup and target excitation contributions gain importance at the higher energy, with interference effects appearing from the coherent sum of elastic transfer, inelastic transfer, elastic breakup transfer, and inelastic breakup transfer paths.
- For the $^{18}\text{C}(p, pn)^{17}\text{C}^*$ reaction below 100 MeV/u, the $(p,d)$ transfer contribution is significant for populating the $E_x = 0.33$ MeV state, affecting extracted spectroscopic factors.
- For the $^{9}\text{Li}(d,p)^{10}\text{Li}$ reaction at 2.4 MeV/u and 11.1 MeV/u, the $s$-wave virtual state contribution is very small at the higher incident energy and not apparent in the data, attributed to different angular ranges in the two experiments.
- For the $^{209}\text{Bi}(^{6}\text{Li},\alpha)X$ reaction at 36 MeV, most of the measured inclusive cross section is due to non-elastic breakup (NEB) processes, with elastic breakup (EBU) dominant only at small angles; the full EBU+NEB calculation reproduces the data shape and magnitude with some overestimation at the maximum.

## Context

This work reviews extensions of the CDCC method beyond its original formulation by Rawitscher and the Pittsburgh-Kyushu collaboration for deuteron elastic scattering. It extends prior work by Yahiro et al. on target excitation in deuteron breakup and by Sumi et al. and Diaz-Torres et al. on XCDCC for projectile fragment excitations. The combined CDCC-transfer formalism for $^{10}\text{Be}(d,p)^{11}\text{Be}$ builds on earlier adiabatic approximations by Johnson and Tandy. The IAV-CDCC implementation for non-elastic breakup, recently achieved by Jin et al., enables calculations that were previously hampered by numerical complexity, allowing comparison with inclusive breakup data such as those from Santra et al. for $^{6}\text{Li}+^{209}\text{Bi}$.
