# Two-body Photodisintegration of $^{4}$He with Full Final State Interaction

> The cross sections of the processes $^4$He($γ,p$)$^3$H and $^4$He($γ,n$)$^3$He are calculated taking into account the full final state interaction via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method. This is the first consistent microscopic calculation beyond the three--body breakup threshold. The results are obtained with a semirealistic central NN potential including also the Coulomb force. The cross sections show a pronounced dipole peak at 27 MeV which lies within the rather broad experimental band. At higher energies, where experimental uncertainties are considerably smaller, one finds a good agreement between theory and experiment. The calculated sum of three-- and four--body photodisintegration cross sections is also listed and is in fair agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The cross sections for the two-body photodisintegration of 4He into p+3H and n+3He show a pronounced dipole peak at 27 MeV, favoring the strongly peaked experimental data over the flatter measurements, but this result is obtained with a semirealistic central NN potential (MTI-III) and does not include a three-nucleon force, so the size of the giant dipole peak remains to be confirmed with more realistic potentials.

## Method

The authors calculated the exclusive cross sections for 4He(γ,p)3H and 4He(γ,n)3He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method with a correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) expansion, employing the semirealistic MTI-III central NN potential including the Coulomb force. The LIT method avoids explicit calculation of continuum wave functions by solving localized equations with a Lorentzian kernel, and the transition matrix elements were evaluated using the Lanczos technique with a maximal grand-angular quantum number Kmax=29. The calculation includes full final state interaction (FSI) beyond the three-body and four-body breakup thresholds for the first time in a microscopic calculation.

## Key numbers

- Dipole peak at 27 MeV for both the (γ,p) and (γ,n) channels
- Cross sections calculated up to 120 MeV (pion threshold)
- Kmax=29 used for the hyperspherical harmonics expansion
- Lorentz transform calculated with σI=20 MeV at En,3He=27.9 MeV
- Coulomb interaction effect: suppression of the (γ,p) cross section near threshold, compensated by an increase at higher energies

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the total 4He photoabsorption cross section (ELO97, BELO01) by separately computing the two-body breakup channels, and contradicts the earlier calculation by Ellerkmann et al. (1996) which found a suppressed giant dipole peak using the same MTI-III potential but only up to the three-body breakup threshold. The results enable future calculations with modern realistic NN potentials and three-nucleon forces to confirm the size of the giant dipole peak, and provide an indirect determination of the three- and four-body breakup cross section by subtraction from the total photoabsorption.
