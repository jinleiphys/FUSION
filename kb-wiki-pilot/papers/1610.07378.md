# The dynamic polarization potential and dynamical non-locality in nuclear potentials: Deuteron-nucleus potential

> The consequences for direct reactions of the dynamical non-locality generated by the excitation of the target and projectile are much less studied than the effects of non-locality arising from exchange processes. Here we are concerned with the dynamical non-locality due to projectile excitation in deuteron induced reactions. The consequences of this non-locality can be studied by the comparison of deuteron induced direct reactions calculated with alternative representations of the elastic channel wave functions: (i) the elastic channel wave functions from coupled channel (CC) calculations involving specific reaction processes, and, (ii) elastic channel wave functions calculated from local potentials that exactly reproduce the elastic scattering $S$-matrix from the same CC calculations. In this work we produce the local equivalent deuteron potentials required for the study of direct reactions involving deuterons. These will enable the study of the effects of dynamical non-locality following a method previously employed in an investigation of the effects of non-locality due to target excitation. In this work we consider only excitations due to deuteron breakup, and some new properties of the breakup dynamical polarization potential (DPP) emerge that reveal dynamical non-locality directly. In addition, we evaluate the TELP inversion method and find that it fails to reproduce some features of the DPP due to breakup.

# The dynamic polarization potential and dynamical non-locality in nuclear potentials: Deuteron-nucleus potential

## Key claim

The dynamical non-locality generated by deuteron breakup coupling produces local equivalent potentials that exhibit non-additivity, providing direct evidence for dynamical non-locality in deuteron-nucleus scattering; however, the trivially equivalent local potential (TELP) inversion method fails to reproduce some features of the breakup dynamical polarization potential (DPP), particularly the undulatory structure and correct volume integrals.

## Method

Coupled discretized continuum channel (CDCC) calculations were performed for 30 MeV deuterons incident on 16O, with the n+p continuum divided into momentum bins up to kmax = 0.7 fm−1 and including various combinations of relative angular momenta L = 0, 2, and 4. The elastic channel S-matrix from these CDCC calculations was then inverted using the iterative-perturbative (IP) algorithm to obtain local equivalent potentials that exactly reproduce the elastic scattering observables, and the DPP was extracted by subtracting the bare potential from the inverted potential.

## Key numbers

- For 30 MeV deuterons on 16O with L02 coupling: ΔJR = −9.04 MeV fm3, ΔJI = 35.42 MeV fm3, ΔCS = 69.60 mb, breakup cross section = 83.32 mb
- For 30 MeV deuterons on 16O with L024 coupling: ΔJR = −11.55 MeV fm3, ΔJI = 38.11 MeV fm3, ΔCS = 55.60 mb, breakup cross section = 93.04 mb
- For L0 coupling alone: ΔJR = +15.61 MeV fm3 (attractive), ΔJI = 26.14 MeV fm3
- For L2 coupling alone: ΔJR = −24.89 MeV fm3 (repulsive), ΔJI = 35.25 MeV fm3
- The sum of L0 and L2 ΔJR values (−9.28 MeV fm3) differs from the L0andL2 case (−14.31 MeV fm3), demonstrating non-additivity
- For the L024 case at 30 MeV on 16O, the TELP inversion gives ρI = 0.553 compared to the exact value of 1.459
- For 56 MeV deuterons on 58Ni with L02 coupling: ΔJR = −1.55 MeV fm3, ΔJI = 18.29 MeV fm3, ΔCS = 86.7 mb

## Context

This work extends the method introduced in Ref. [KM14a] for studying dynamical non-locality due to target excitation to the case of projectile excitation in deuteron-induced reactions. It builds on earlier studies of the generalized Perey factor (GPF) in Refs. [mic88] and [cm90], which found that non-locality from inelastic processes produces effects very different from the standard Perey effect, including anti-Perey effects where the GPF exceeds unity. The local equivalent potentials produced here will enable the study of dynamical non-locality effects in (d,p), (d,t), and (d,6Li) reactions in a subsequent paper [paper2], and the results demonstrate that the TELP inversion method is inadequate for determining DPPs, consistent with earlier findings in Ref. [pang1].
