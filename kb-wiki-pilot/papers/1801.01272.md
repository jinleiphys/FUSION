# Three-body radiative capture reactions

> Radiative capture reaction rates for $^6$He, $^9$Be and $^{17}$Ne formation at astrophysical conditions are studied within a three-body model using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator method to calculate their states. An alternative procedure to estimate these rates from experimental data on low-energy breakup is also discussed

## Key claim
Three-body radiative capture reaction rates for Borromean nuclei (6He, 9Be, 17Ne) can be calculated using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) method, but the computed rates show significant differences from previous sequential estimations, particularly at low temperatures where direct three-body capture dominates.

## Method
The authors applied the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) method to generate three-body states of the compound nuclei and computed photodissociation cross sections expanded in electromagnetic multipoles (E1 and M1 transitions). Reaction rates were obtained from the inverse photodissociation cross section using the energy-averaged rate formula, which includes both sequential and direct contributions. An alternative procedure was also proposed to estimate these rates from inclusive low-energy breakup data on the compound nucleus, valid only at small scattering angles where the process is Coulomb dominated.

## Key numbers
- For 6He formation via α(2n,γ)6He: reaction rate governed by E1 transitions between 0+ ground state and 1- continuum states; the THO result differs significantly from the sequential estimation by Bartlett et al. (2006) assuming dineutron formation
- For 17Ne formation via 15O(2p,γ)17Ne: computed reaction rate is several orders of magnitude larger than previous calculations by Grigorenko et al. (2006)
- For 9Be formation via α(αn,γ)9Be: THO calculations agree with sequential estimations at high temperature but show enhancement at low temperature from three-body direct E1 capture to low-energy 1/2+ states
- For the alternative method using 6He + 208Pb breakup: reaction rates can be obtained in the temperature region between 1-3 GK from dedicated low-energy, small-angle experiments

## Context
This work extends previous three-body radiative capture studies by applying the THO method to Borromean nuclei, building on the framework developed by RdDiego10 and Garrido11 that showed direct three-body capture plays an important role at low temperatures. The results contradict sequential reaction descriptions by Angulo99, Sumiyoshi02, and Arnold12 for 9Be formation, and by Bartlett06 for 6He formation. The proposed alternative method using inclusive breakup data, previously applied to 11Li in JCasal16R, enables experimental determination of reaction rates without relying on theoretical structure models.
