# Complete and incomplete fusion of $^{7}$Li projectiles on heavy targets

> We present a detailed discussion of a recently proposed method to evaluate complete and incomplete fusion cross sections for weakly bound systems. The method is applied to collisions of $^{7}$Li projectiles on different heavy targets, and the results are compared with the available data. The overall agreement between experiment and theory is fairly good.

## Key claim

The proposed CDCC-based method for separating complete fusion (CF) and incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections, which assumes that matrix elements of imaginary potentials connecting bound channels to continuum bins are negligible, yields "fairly good" overall agreement with experimental data for \(^{7}\)Li projectiles on heavy targets, but the agreement is significantly worse at energies well below the Coulomb barrier (approximately 6 MeV below) where cross sections drop to \(\sim 10^{-4}\) mb.

## Method

The authors performed continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations treating \(^{7}\)Li as a \(^{3}\)H + \(^{4}\)He two-cluster system with separation energy 2.45 MeV. They split the total fusion cross section into contributions from bound channels (direct complete fusion, DCF) and continuum-discretized bins (incomplete fusion ICF and sequential complete fusion SCF), using short-range Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials (depth 50 MeV, radius 1.0 fm, diffusivity 0.2 fm) for fragment-target interactions. The continuum was discretized with bins up to \(\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = 8\) MeV and \(l_{\text{max}} = 3\hbar\), and a spectroscopic amplitude \(\mathcal{S} = 0.8\) was used to scale bound-continuum matrix elements.

## Key numbers

*   \(^{7}\)Li cluster separation energy: \(B = 2.45\) MeV.
*   Imaginary potential parameters: \(W_0 = 50\) MeV, \(R_{\text{w}} = 1.0\,(A_i^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3})\) fm, \(a_{\text{w}} = 0.2\) fm.
*   Continuum discretization cut-off: \(\varepsilon_{\text{max}} = 8\) MeV, \(l_{\text{max}} = 3\hbar\).
*   Spectroscopic amplitude used for best fit: \(\mathcal{S} = 0.8\).
*   For the \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{198}\)Pt system, the theoretical CF cross section overestimates data at energies \(\sim 6\) MeV below the Coulomb barrier, where experimental cross sections are \(\sim 10^{-4}\) mb.
*   For the \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi system, the theoretical CF cross section changes by up to \(\sim 20\%\) when \(\mathcal{S}\) varies between 0.7 and 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the method introduced in a recent letter (RCL20) for the \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi system to additional heavy targets (\(^{197}\)Au, \(^{124}\)Sn, \(^{198}\)Pt). It contrasts with prior classical (e.g., Diaz-Torres et al.) and semiclassical approaches that do not account properly for quantum mechanical effects, and with quantum mechanical models that either only give total fusion (e.g., CDCC works by KKR01, DTB03) or require one fragment to be much heavier than the other (HVD00, DiT02). The method enables calculation of individual CF and ICF cross sections for any projectile breaking into two fragments, using CDCC wave functions, and can be applied to test fusion models at energies far below the barrier.
