# Practical method for decomposing discretized breakup cross sections into components of each channel

> Background: In the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method, a breakup cross section (BUX) is obtained as an admixture of several components of different channels in multi-channel scattering. Purpose: Our goal is to propose an approximate way of decomposing the discretized BUX into components of each channel. This approximation is referred to as the "probability separation (P-separation)". Method: As an example, we consider $^{11}$Be scattering by using the three-body model with core excitation ($^{10}\mathrm{Be}+n+\mathrm{T}$, where T is a target). The structural part is constructed by the particle-rotor model and the reaction part is described by the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA). Results: The validity of the P-separation is tested by comparing with the exact calculation. The approximate way reproduces the exact BUXs well regardless of the configurations and/or the resonance positions of $^{11}$Be. Conclusion: The method proposed here can be an alternative approach for decomposing discretized BUXs into components in four- or five-body scattering where the strict decomposition is hard to perform.

## Key claim

The authors propose an approximate method called "probability separation (P-separation)" to decompose discretized breakup cross sections (BUX) into components of each channel in multi-channel scattering, which reproduces exact BUXs well regardless of the configurations and/or resonance positions of $^{11}$Be, provided that a threshold condition is imposed to prevent unphysical core-excited BUX below the breakup threshold energy.

## Method

The authors test the P-separation on $^{11}$Be scattering using a three-body model with core excitation ($^{10}\mathrm{Be}+n+T$, where T is a target), constructing the structural part with the particle-rotor model and the reaction part with the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA). The P-separation decomposes discretized BUX by weighting each pseudostate's BUX with its probability of having a given core state configuration, computed from the overlap between the pseudostate wave function and the channel wave functions. A threshold condition is imposed such that below the $^{10}\mathrm{Be}(2_1^+)+n$ breakup threshold energy (3.368 MeV), the core-excited probability is set to zero and the core-ground probability to one.

## Key numbers

- For $^{11}\mathrm{Be}+p$ scattering at 63.7 MeV/nucleon: exact total BUX = 54.8 mb, exact core-ground BUX = 47.8 mb, exact core-excited BUX = 7.0 mb; approximate core-ground BUX = 44.3 mb, approximate core-excited BUX = 10.5 mb
- For $^6\mathrm{Li}+{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ scattering at 39 MeV: predicted $d\alpha$-BUX = 45.3 mb, predicted $np\alpha$-BUX = 23.4 mb
- For $^6\mathrm{Li}+{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ scattering at 210 MeV: predicted $d\alpha$-BUX = 89.9 mb, predicted $np\alpha$-BUX = 47.1 mb
- Seven parameter sets tested (sets 1-7) with varying separation energies (0.1-5.0 MeV), potential depths, and core excitation energies (0.5-3.368 MeV), all showing reasonable agreement between exact and approximate BUX

## Context

This work extends the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (CDCC) framework, specifically addressing the limitation of the pseudostate method in four-body CDCC where breakup cross sections from mixed channels (e.g., three-body and four-body channels in $^6$Li scattering) cannot be disentangled. The P-separation was previously applied in Ref. [Wat15] to separate channel-coupling effects on elastic cross sections, but here it is applied after reaction calculations to decompose BUX. The method provides an alternative to the numerically demanding complex-scaled Lippmann-Schwinger equation (CSLS) approach of Kikuchi et al. [Kik11], which has been limited to $^6$He scattering. The results contradict the earlier finding in Ref. [Wat15] that four-body-channel coupling is negligible in $^6$Li elastic scattering, since the predicted $np\alpha$-BUX is almost comparable to the $d\alpha$-BUX.
