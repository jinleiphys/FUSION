# Ballistic protons in incoherent exclusive vector meson production as a measure of rare parton fluctuations at an Electron-Ion Collider

> We argue that the proton multiplicities measured in Roman pot detectors at an electron ion collider can be used to determine centrality classes in incoherent diffractive scattering. Incoherent diffraction probes the fluctuations in the interaction strengths of multi-parton Fock states in the nuclear wavefunctions. In particular, the saturation scale that characterizes this multi-parton dynamics is significantly larger in central events relative to minimum bias events. As an application, we study the centrality dependence of incoherent diffractive vector meson production. We identify an observable which is simultaneously very sensitive to centrality triggered parton fluctuations and insensitive to details of the model.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the multiplicity of ballistic protons measured in Roman pot detectors at an Electron-Ion Collider can serve as a centrality classifier for incoherent diffractive events, enabling the selection of rare parton configurations with enhanced saturation scale. The hedge is that this centrality selection works only for the subset of incoherent events that produce ballistic nucleons, and the absolute cross section for such events is not calculated.

## Method

The authors compute coherent and incoherent diffractive vector meson production cross sections using the dipole model framework, where the dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude is built from the IPsat dipole-proton amplitude via an independent scattering approximation with nucleon positions sampled from a Woods-Saxon distribution. Central events are approximated by fixing the impact parameter to b=0, while minimum bias events are obtained by integrating over all impact parameters. The double ratio of cross sections for different vector meson species (J/Psi, rho, phi) between central and minimum bias events is then evaluated as a function of Q^2 at fixed xpom = 0.005.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed for an EIC with 15 GeV electron beams scattering off nuclear beams with 100 GeV/nucleon
- The kinematic region studied requires xpom less than 0.01 and Q^2 less than 10 GeV^2 for saturation effects to be relevant
- Ballistic nucleons are identified by laboratory transverse momentum in the 400 MeV to 1 GeV range
- The double ratio for J/Psi/rho and J/Psi/phi shows significant enhancement at low Q^2, reducing to unity above Q^2 ~ 10 GeV^2
- The phi/rho double ratio remains near unity for the entire Q^2 range studied
- The calculation is performed at xpom = 0.005, which is within EIC kinematic reach

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event multi-parton spatial fluctuations in protons and nuclei, previously investigated in the context of high-multiplicity p+p and p/d+A collisions at LHC and RHIC. It builds on the dipole model framework of Lappi and Mantysaari (2010) and the IPsat model of Kowalski and Teaney (2003), which successfully describe HERA diffractive vector meson data and ALICE ultraperipheral heavy ion collision results. The approach complements recent work by Zheng et al. (2014) on centrality selection using evaporation neutrons in inclusive DIS, but focuses specifically on diffractive events where final state interactions do not complicate the extraction of spatial parton structure.
