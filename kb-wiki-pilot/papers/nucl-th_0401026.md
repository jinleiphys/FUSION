# Fusion and breakup in the reactions of 6,7Li and 9Be

> We develop a three body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method to dicsuss the effect of the breakup process on heavy-ion fusion reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei. This method follows the classical trajectories of breakup fragments after the breakup takes place, and thus provides an unambiguous separation between complete and incomplete fusion cross sections. Applying this method to the fusion reaction $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi, we find that there is a significant contribution to the total complete fusion cross sections from the process where all the breakup fragments are captured by the target nucleus (i.e., the breakup followed by complete fusion).

## Key claim

The three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method shows that breakup followed by complete fusion (where all breakup fragments are captured by the target) makes a significant contribution to total complete fusion cross sections for the $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi reaction at energies above the Coulomb barrier, a process not accounted for in standard coupled-channels calculations.

## Method

A three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method was developed, solving Newtonian equations in two dimensions for the projectile fragments ($P_1$, $P_2$) and the target nucleus. Initial conditions were set with the target at rest, the projectile center of mass moving toward the target at a given impact parameter, and the relative motion of the fragments confined inside the binding potential at the breakup Q-value with random orientation. Trajectories were sampled by Monte Carlo over random values of the initial fragment separation and orientation angle, with absorption defined when a fragment-target distance fell below $1.1 (A_i^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3})$ fm and breakup defined when the fragment-fragment separation exceeded the barrier radius of their mutual potential.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi reaction at $E_{cm}$ = 40 MeV, sample trajectories were shown for impact parameters $b$ = 2.5 fm (capture before breakup), 4.65 fm (breakup followed by complete fusion), and 5.5 fm (incomplete fusion with only deuteron capture)
- The initial projectile-target separation was set to $R_{\rm max}$ = 200 fm
- The relative momentum between fragments was given by $p_{\rm rel} = \sqrt{2\mu[Q_{\rm bu} - V_{12}(r_{12}(0))]}$ where $\mu = m_1 m_2 / (m_1 + m_2)$

## Context

This work extends classical trajectory methods from atomic and molecular physics (the CTMC approach of Abrines and Percival, Cohen, and Fiol et al.) to nuclear fusion of weakly bound projectiles. It addresses a limitation of continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations (Hagino et al., Diaz-Torres and Thompson), which cannot easily compute cross sections for breakup followed by complete fusion. The results enable a consistent definition of complete fusion for comparison between experimental data and theoretical calculations, particularly for reactions with $^{6,7}$Li and $^{9}$Be beams.
