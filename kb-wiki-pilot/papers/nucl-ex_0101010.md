# S17(0) Determined from the Coulomb Breakup of 83 MeV/nucleon 8B

> A kinematically complete measurement was made of the Coulomb dissociation of 8B nuclei on a Pb target at 83 MeV/nucleon. The cross section was measured at low relative energies in order to infer the astrophysical S factor for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction. A first-order perturbation theory analysis of the reaction dynamics including E1, E2, and M1 transitions was employed to extract the E1 strength relevant to neutrino-producing reactions in the solar interior. By fitting the measured cross section from Erel = 130 keV to 400 keV, we find S17(0) = 17.8 (+1.4, -1.2) eV b.

## Key claim
The astrophysical S-factor for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction at zero energy is S17(0) = 17.8 (+1.4, -1.2) eV b, extracted from Coulomb breakup data between 130 keV and 400 keV relative energy, but only after accounting for E2 transitions that had been neglected in prior Coulomb breakup analyses and after imposing a strict scattering angle cut of 1.8 degrees to minimize nuclear contributions.

## Method
A kinematically complete measurement of the Coulomb dissociation of 83 MeV/nucleon 8B on a Pb target was performed using the A1200 fragment separator at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. A 1.5 T dipole magnet separated the 7Be and proton breakup fragments, with four multiwire drift chambers measuring positions and angles and an array of plastic scintillators providing particle identification and time-of-flight measurements. First-order perturbation theory including E1, E2, and M1 transitions was employed to extract the E1 strength, with the E2 matrix elements scaled by a factor of 0.49 relative to the model of Esbensen and Bertsch based on prior inclusive measurements.

## Key numbers
- S17(0) = 17.8 (+1.4, -1.2) eV b from fitting data between 130 keV and 400 keV relative energy
- S17(0) = 19.1 (+1.5, -1.8) eV b from fitting data between 130 keV and 2 MeV relative energy
- Best-fit normalization factor for the 130-400 keV range: 0.93 (+0.05, -0.04)
- Best-fit normalization factor for the 130 keV to 2 MeV range: 1.00 (+0.02, -0.06)
- Total systematic uncertainty: 7.5 percent, including beam intensity (1 percent), target thickness (2.6 percent), momentum calibration (4.2 percent), and theoretical uncertainty (5.6 percent)
- 1-sigma relative energy resolution: 100 keV at Erel = 300 keV, 250 keV at Erel = 1.5 MeV
- 1-sigma proton momentum resolution: 4 MeV/c
- 1-sigma angular resolution in reconstructed 8B scattering angle: 4.5 mrad
- Ratio of E2 to E1 S factors at Erel = 0.6 MeV: 4.7 (+2.0, -1.3) x 10^{-4}
- Impact parameter cut corresponding to 1.8 degree scattering angle: 30 fm

## Context
This work resolves long-standing discrepancies among direct measurements of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B S-factor (Hammache et al., Filippone et al., Vaughn et al.) and prior Coulomb breakup experiments at RIKEN (Kikuchi et al., S17(0) = 18.9 +/- 1.8 eV b) and GSI (Iwasa et al., S17(0) = 20.6 +/- 1.2 +/- 1.0 eV b) by properly accounting for E2 transitions that had been neglected in those analyses. The result agrees with asymptotic normalization coefficient determinations (Azhari et al., 17.3 +/- 1.8 eV b) and demonstrates consistency between direct radiative capture, Coulomb breakup, and ANC methods for the first time. This enables more precise predictions of high-energy solar neutrino fluxes from 8B decay for experiments like SuperKamiokande and SNO.
