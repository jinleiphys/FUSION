# High-Dimensional Bayesian Calibration of Expensive Nuclear Models with Differentiable Emulation

> Full Bayesian calibration of expensive nuclear models has been blocked not by the cost of any single solve, but by the absence of exact likelihood gradients in legacy parameter-dependent operators, which forces gradient-free samplers to spend $\mathcal{O}(10^5)$ evaluations exploring high-dimensional correlated posteriors. I introduce DREAM, a differentiable calibration strategy in which the parameter-dependent operator is sampled offline by any legacy code, compressed by singular value decomposition, and reconstructed online in a differentiable framework so that automatic differentiation delivers exact likelihood gradients through the full forward solve at the cost of one additional evaluation per Hamiltonian Monte Carlo step. The construction is operator-level and depends only on smooth, compressible parameter dependence; the underlying physics solver is treated as a black box. As a representative demonstration, DREAM is applied to a continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) analysis of $d$+$^{58}$Ni elastic scattering at $20$~MeV with eighteen optical-potential parameters, for which No-U-Turn Sampling converges on a single GPU in under ten minutes from a cold start with zero divergent transitions, yielding a full Bayesian posterior for a breakup reaction. The mean emulator error is more than an order of magnitude below the inferred model discrepancy, so the posterior is set by the reaction model rather than the surrogate. Treating the Koning-Delaroche systematics as an informative prior, the data update the well-determined parameter combinations, raising the mean deuteron surface absorption about $36\%$ above the Koning-Delaroche value, while the under-determined directions remain at the prior; this is a representative payoff that the multi-energy datasets DREAM is designed to accommodate can sharpen into a full physics interpretation.

# High-Dimensional Bayesian Calibration of Expensive Nuclear Models with Differentiable Emulation

## Key claim
The paper claims that full Bayesian calibration of expensive nuclear models has been blocked not by the cost of any single solve, but by the absence of exact likelihood gradients in legacy parameter-dependent operators, which forces gradient-free samplers to spend O(10^5) evaluations exploring high-dimensional correlated posteriors. The hedge is that the construction depends only on smooth, compressible parameter dependence and treats the underlying physics solver as a black box, meaning the method is not guaranteed universal but rather demonstrated as proof of concept for models whose parameter-dependent operator varies smoothly enough to admit low-rank SVD compression.

## Method
The DREAM (Differentiable Reduced-basis Emulator with Automatic gradients for Markov-chain inference) framework operates in two stages. Offline, the parameter-dependent operator is sampled on a dense grid by any legacy code, compressed by singular value decomposition (SVD) into proper orthogonal decomposition (POD) modes, and stored as a database. Online, the compressed operator is reconstructed by bilinear interpolation of the scalar expansion coefficients, the reduced Galerkin system is solved by direct linear algebra, and reverse-mode automatic differentiation (including implicit differentiation through the linear solve) returns the exact gradient of the likelihood with respect to all calibrated parameters at the cost of one additional forward evaluation per Hamiltonian Monte Carlo step. The method is applied to continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) analysis of d+58Ni elastic scattering at 20 MeV with eighteen optical-potential parameters, using No-U-Turn Sampling (NUTS) on a single GPU.

## Key numbers
- NUTS converges from a cold start in approximately 9 minutes on a single GPU with zero divergent transitions across 1.6 x 10^4 post-warmup samples and split-hat-R <= 1.001 across all nineteen parameters (eighteen optical parameters plus model-discrepancy hyperparameter beta)
- The online emulator forward pass takes 0.34 ms per call on a single NVIDIA H100 GPU; the full autodiff gradient takes 0.99 ms
- The mean emulator error is more than an order of magnitude below the inferred model discrepancy: the POD-I emulator reproduces the Fortran reference S-matrix to a mean relative error of 1.1 x 10^-3 and the elastic ratio sigma(theta)/sigma_Ruth to 3.4 x 10^-3
- The mean deuteron surface absorption is raised to bar-W_d = 11.6 +/- 0.7 MeV, about 36% above the Koning-Delaroche value of 8.5 MeV
- The model-discrepancy hyperparameter converges to beta = 0.071 +/- 0.027, a systematic theory error of about 7%
- The effective dimensionality of the Fisher information matrix is D_eff = 1.3 out of 18, with condition number kappa of order 10^14
- Only K_f = 6-11 modes per form factor are needed to achieve reconstruction errors below 10^-4
- The offline construction cost for the six-form-factor database is approximately 1.5 hours using 7 parallel Fortran processes

## Context
This work extends the reduced-basis emulation literature for nuclear physics, specifically the non-differentiable CDCC emulator of Lei (2025) which lowered per-call cost to seconds but could not supply likelihood gradients. It builds on eigenvector continuation (Frame 2018), the empirical interpolation method (Barrault 2004), and the broader reduced-basis literature (Quarteroni 2016), but here the compressed object is the legacy operator itself rather than a surrogate observable. Differentiable emulators have been deployed in cosmology (CosmoPowerJAX 2023) and for non-affine optical models by reduced-basis (Odell 2024) and coupled-channels reduced-basis emulation (Catacor-Rios 2025), but the latter speeds up evaluation without exposing likelihood gradients for sampling. The work enables full Bayesian posterior inference for breakup reactions at a dimensionality previously inaccessible to gradient-based inference, and is designed to accommodate multi-energy and polarization datasets that could break the single-energy optical-model ambiguity.
