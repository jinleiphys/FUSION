# Non-relativistic bound states in a moving thermal bath

> We study the propagation of non-relativistic bound states moving at constant velocity across a homogeneous thermal bath and we develop the effective field theory which is relevant in various dynamical regimes. We consider values of the velocity of the bound state ranging from moderate to highly relativistic and temperatures at all relevant scales smaller than the mass of the particles that form the bound state. In particular, we consider two distinct temperature regimes, corresponding to temperatures smaller or higher than the typical momentum transfer in the bound state. For temperatures smaller or of the order of the typical momentum transfer, we restrict our analysis to the simplest system, a hydrogen-like atom. We build the effective theory for this system first considering moderate values of the velocity and then the relativistic case. For large values of the velocity of the bound state, the separation of scales is such that the corresponding effective theory resembles the soft collinear effective theory (SCET). For temperatures larger than the typical momentum transfer we also consider muonic hydrogen propagating in a plasma which contains photons and massless electrons and positrons, so that the system resembles very much heavy quarkonium in a thermal medium of deconfined quarks and gluons. We study the behavior of the real and imaginary part of the static two-body potential, for various velocities of the bound state, in the hard thermal loop approximation. We find that Landau damping ceases to be the relevant mechanism for dissociation from a certain "critical" velocity on in favor of screening. Our results are relevant for understanding how the properties of heavy quarkonia states produced in the initial fusion of partons in the relativistic collision of heavy ions are affected by the presence of an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper develops effective field theories for non-relativistic bound states moving at constant velocity through a homogeneous thermal bath, and finds that for temperatures larger than the typical momentum transfer, Landau damping ceases to be the relevant mechanism for dissociation above a certain "critical" velocity, with screening taking over instead.

## Method

The authors employ non-relativistic effective field theories (NRQED and pNRQED) in the rest frame of the bound state, where the thermal bath moves with velocity **v**. They consider two distinct temperature regimes: temperatures smaller or of the order of the typical momentum transfer (studied for hydrogen-like atoms), and temperatures larger than the typical momentum transfer (studied for muonic hydrogen in a photon/electron/positron plasma). For the high-temperature regime, the static two-body potential is analyzed using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, with boosted Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac distribution functions accounting for the moving bath.

## Key numbers

- The effective temperature in the moving frame is given by \( T_{\rm eff}(\theta,v) = T\sqrt{1-v^2}/(1-v\cos\theta) \), leading to two distinct scales \( T_+ = T\sqrt{(1+v)/(1-v)} \) and \( T_- = T\sqrt{(1-v)/(1+v)} \)
- For the hydrogen atom at moderate velocities (\( v \lesssim 0.5 \)) and \( T \ll 1/r \), the decay width for S-wave states is \( \delta\Gamma_n = (2Z^2\alpha^3 T\sqrt{1-v^2})/(3n^2 v) \log((1+v)/(1-v)) \)
- For the hydrogen atom at moderate velocities and \( T \ll E \), the thermal corrections are of order \( {\cal O}(\alpha^3 T^4/E^3) \), independent of velocity
- The tensor \( T_{ij} \) in the \( T \sim 1/r \) regime is given by \( T_{ij} = \frac{1}{6\pi^2}[\log(\mu/T) - \log 2 - \log(2\pi) + \gamma + \frac{3}{8v}(1+1/(3v^2))\log((1+v)/(1-v)) - 1/(4v^2)]\delta_{ij} + \rho(v)/(8\pi^2) v_i v_j/v^2 \)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonia in static thermal media (Matsui and Satz 1986; Chu and Matsui 1988) to the case of moving bound states, relevant for heavy-ion collisions where heavy quarkonia produced in initial parton fusion are not comoving with the quark-gluon plasma. It builds on earlier effective field theory treatments of heavy quarkonia in thermal baths (Escobedo, Mannarelli, Soto 2008; Brambilla, Escobedo, Soto, Vairo 2008, 2010) and on the soft collinear effective theory (SCET) framework of Bauer, Fleming, Pirjol, Rothstein, Stewart (2000). The results enable quantitative understanding of how the properties of heavy quarkonia are modified by an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly addressing the observed suppression patterns at different rapidities.
