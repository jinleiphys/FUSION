# Fusion reaction of halo nuclei: A real-time wave-packet method for three-body tunneling dynamics

> We investigate fusion cross section of a nucleus with a valence neutron, using the time-dependent wave-packet method. For a stable projectile, in which the valence neutron is tightly bound (e_n < -3 MeV), the neutron could enhance the fusion probability when the matching condition of orbital energies are satisfied. In contrast, for a halo nucleus, in which the binding energy of the neutron is very small (e_n>-1 MeV), the fusion probability is hindered by the presence of the weakly bound neutron.

## Key claim

For a stable projectile with a tightly bound valence neutron (binding energy less than -3 MeV), the neutron can enhance fusion probability when orbital energies in projectile and target are matched; however, for a halo nucleus with a very weakly bound neutron (binding energy greater than -1 MeV), the fusion probability is hindered by the presence of that neutron, contrary to some earlier predictions of strong enhancement.

## Method

The authors use a time-dependent wave-packet (TDWP) method to solve the three-body Schrodinger equation for a projectile modeled as a core plus a valence neutron in an s-wave orbital colliding with a target. The core-target potential includes an imaginary part inside the Coulomb barrier to absorb flux representing fusion, and the fusion cross section is defined by the loss of flux. The wave function is expressed in a body-fixed frame with radial coordinates discretized on a mesh of 0.2 to 1 fm, and time evolution is performed using fourth-order Taylor expansion.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy threshold for stable projectile: epsilon_n < -3 MeV (tightly bound)
* Binding energy threshold for halo projectile: epsilon_n > -1 MeV (very weakly bound)
* For the stable projectile case, the valence neutron binding energy used in calculations: -3.5 MeV (2s orbital)
* For the halo projectile case, the neutron binding energy used: -0.6 MeV (representing halo 11Be)
* Maximum partial waves included in the calculation: up to ell = 70 hbar (compared to CDCC calculations which include at most ell = 4 hbar)
* The halo neutron carries about 1/11 of the original projectile energy when it escapes

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on fusion of halo 11Be on 208Pb, where they found suppression of fusion probability, contradicting coupled discretized continuum channels (CDCC) calculations by Hagino et al. (2000) and Diaz-Torres et al. (2002) that predicted strong enhancement. The authors identify that the discrepancy arises from different truncation of relative angular momentum between the neutron and core; when they truncate at ell <= 4 hbar like CDCC, they also see enhancement, but it disappears with higher partial waves. The results are consistent with recent measurements showing no enhancement for halo nuclei 11Be on 209Bi (Signorini et al., 2004) and 6He on 238U (Raabe et al., 2004).
