# Continuum Response and Reaction in Neutron-Rich Be Nuclei

> We study E1 resonances, breakup and fusion reactions for weakly bound Be nuclei. The absorbing-boundary condition (ABC) is used to describe both the outgoing and incoming boundary conditions. The neutron continuum plays important roles in response and reaction of neutron drip-line nuclei.

## Key claim

The absorbing-boundary condition (ABC) method, which replaces the infinitesimal imaginary part in the Green's function with a finite, coordinate-dependent function that is zero in the interacting region and positive outside, provides a practical tool for treating continuum wave functions in both time-dependent and time-independent approaches for neutron-rich Be nuclei, though the wave functions obtained are only meaningful in the interacting region.

## Method

The authors applied the ABC method in three distinct calculations: (1) time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a three-dimensional coordinate grid with Skyrme energy functional (SGII parameter set) to compute E1 response in 8Be and 14Be; (2) a three-body quantum-mechanical breakup reaction calculation for 11Be on 12C solving an inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation; (3) a time-dependent wave-packet calculation for fusion of 11Be on 208Pb near the Coulomb barrier energy, where the ABC simulates the incoming boundary condition inside the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers

- Model space for TDHF calculations: sphere of radius 22 fm, with iϵ(r)=0 for r<10 fm and non-zero for r>10 fm
- Time evolution carried out up to T=30 ħ/MeV with smoothing parameter Γ=0.5 MeV
- For breakup reaction: radial region up to 30 fm for R and 50 fm for r, with iϵ non-zero in 20 fm<R<30 fm and 25 fm<r<50 fm
- n-10Be relative angular momenta included up to l=3
- For fusion calculation: n-C partial waves included up to l=2 (showing strong enhancement) and up to l≤70 (showing suppression)
- Deformation splitting of GDR peak in Be isotopes: approximately 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ABC method, previously applied to electronic continuum in molecules and clusters, to nuclear physics problems including giant dipole resonances in deformed nuclei and reactions of halo nuclei. The breakup calculation is compared with the continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method and eikonal approximation, showing that the eikonal approximation fails below 50 MeV/A incident energy. The fusion calculation extends prior work on 11Be+40Ca (Yabana, 1997) to the 208Pb target, demonstrating that high partial waves (l≤70) for neutron-core relative motion are necessary to correctly describe breakup and transfer dynamics under strong Coulomb fields.
