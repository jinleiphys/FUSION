# From response functions to cross sections in neutrino scattering off the deuteron and trinucleons

> Response functions, differential cross sections and total cross sections for several (anti)neutrino induced reactions on 2H, 3He and 3H are calculated in momentum space for (anti)neutrino energies up to 160 MeV, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and a single-nucleon weak current operator. This work is a continuation of our investigations presented in J. Golak et al. [Phys. Rev. C 98, 015501 (2018)].

## Key claim

The authors compute response functions, differential cross sections, and total cross sections for (anti)neutrino induced reactions on deuteron, trinucleons (³He and ³H) using only a single-nucleon weak current operator with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential, finding that two-nucleon current contributions would lead to effects smaller than 2-4% for energies up to 160 MeV, based on comparison with prior work by Shen et al. [Phys. Rev. C 86, 035503 (2012)].

## Method

The authors solve the nuclear many-body problem in momentum space using Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon systems and direct integration for the two-nucleon system, employing the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and a single-nucleon weak current operator with relativistic corrections. They compute nuclear response functions on a dense two-dimensional grid defined by the internal nuclear energy (E₂N or E₃N) and the magnitude of the three-momentum transfer Q, then use two-dimensional interpolation to obtain differential and total cross sections for (anti)neutrino energies up to 160 MeV. For the three-nucleon systems, they use a closure-based Faddeev scheme to compute response functions more economically, while for the deuteron they perform direct phase space integration.

## Key numbers

- (Anti)neutrino energies considered: up to 160 MeV
- Fermi constant used: G_F = 1.1803 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Cabibbo angle cosine: cosθ_C = 0.97425
- Maximal relative difference between relativistic and nonrelativistic formulas for maximal lepton energy: less than 1% for E ≤ 160 MeV over the whole allowed range of scattering angles
- Expected two-nucleon current effects: smaller than 2-4% based on comparison with Shen et al. [Phys. Rev. C 86, 035503 (2012)]
- Grid sizes for deuteron response functions: 7200 to 17200 points
- Interpolation quality criterion: results from three different interpolation methods must not deviate from the average by more than 1%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous momentum-space calculations from J. Golak et al. [Phys. Rev. C 98, 015501 (2018)], which were limited to total cross sections for deuteron reactions and only response function examples for trinucleons. The present paper provides full differential and total cross sections for trinucleon systems, complementing coordinate-space calculations by Shen et al. [Phys. Rev. C 86, 035503 (2012)] and Baroni and Schiavilla [Phys. Rev. C 96, 014001 (2017)] who used chiral effective field theory input. The results enable comparison with experimental data from detectors like the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory and provide a benchmark for future calculations including two-nucleon current contributions.
