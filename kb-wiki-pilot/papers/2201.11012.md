# Breakup effects in the $^{16}{\rm C}+p$ and $^{16}{\rm C}+d$ reactions

> We analyze the $^{16}{\rm C}+p$ and $^{16}{\rm C}+d$ reactions within the four- and five-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method. The $^{16}$C nucleus is described by a $^{14}{\rm C}+n+n$ configuration in hyperspherical coordinates. This description reproduces fairly well several $^{16}$C low-lying states. First we analyze the $2^+\rightarrow 0^+$ $E2$ transition amplitude, which confirms that an effective charge must be introduced to reproduce the experimental value. Then, proton and deuteron elastic and inelastic scattering are investigated by including $^{16}$C pseudostates, which simulate the $^{14}{\rm C}+n+n$ continuum. In $^{16}{\rm C}+d$, the deuteron breakup is taken into account with $p+n$ two-body pseudostates. A fair agreement with experiment is obtained without any fitting parameter. Breakup effects are in general small, but improve the agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a four- and five-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, with no adjustable parameters, can achieve fair agreement with experimental elastic and inelastic scattering data for the \(^{16}{\rm C}+p\) and \(^{16}{\rm C}+d\) systems, but only when an effective charge \(\delta e/e \approx 0.3\pm 0.1\) is introduced to reproduce the \(B(E2,2^+\rightarrow 0^+)\) transition probability; the abstract's claim of "fair agreement without any fitting parameter" glosses over this necessary effective charge for the E2 transition.

## Method

The authors describe \(^{16}{\rm C}\) as a three-body \(^{14}{\rm C}+n+n\) system using hyperspherical coordinates and solve the three-body Schrödinger equation with the Lagrange-mesh method, using the Minnesota potential for \(n+n\) and a potential from Horiuchi and Suzuki (set B) for \(^{14}{\rm C}+n\). They then apply the CDCC method to treat \(^{16}{\rm C}+p\) as a 3+1 body system and \(^{16}{\rm C}+d\) as a 3+2 body system, including pseudostates to simulate the continuum of \(^{16}{\rm C}\) and, for the deuteron target, also including \(p+n\) two-body pseudostates to account for deuteron breakup; the coupled-channel equations are solved using an \(R\)-matrix method with Lagrange-mesh basis functions.

## Key numbers

- \(B(E2,2^+\rightarrow 0^+)\) transition probability in \(^{16}{\rm C}\): experimental value \(4.34^{+2.27}_{-1.85}\) \(e^2.{\rm fm}^4\) from Ref. [JLY20]; the three-body model without effective charge gives a value close to zero, requiring \(\delta e/e \approx 0.3\pm 0.1\) to match experiment.
- Proton and neutron matrix elements from the three-body model: \(M_p = 0.173\) fm\(^2\) and \(M_n = 14.93\) fm\(^2\).
- Beam energy: 24 MeV/nucleon for \(^{16}{\rm C}\), corresponding to center-of-mass energies \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 22.59\) MeV for \(^{16}{\rm C}+p\) and \(E_{\rm c.m.} = 42.67\) MeV for \(^{16}{\rm C}+d\).
- For \(^{16}{\rm C}+p\) elastic scattering, convergence is achieved with pseudostates up to \(E_{\rm max}=10\) MeV, \(K_{\rm max}=16\), and \(J_{\rm max}=25\); breakup effects are insignificant for \(\theta < 25^\circ\) but important for \(\theta > 40^\circ\).
- For \(^{16}{\rm C}+d\) elastic scattering, the full five-body calculation involves 504 channels; calculations with only the \(0_1^+\) and \(2_1^+\) states of \(^{16}{\rm C}\) are not very different from full calculations in the angular range of available data.
- Inelastic scattering to the \(2_1^+\) state of \(^{16}{\rm C}\) at 33 MeV/nucleon on a proton target: full CDCC calculations are nearly double those reported in Ref. [KO19] up to about \(2^\circ\) in the lab frame.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method to three-body projectiles (following earlier work by Matsumoto et al. [MHO04]) and to systems with a two-body projectile and a two-body target (following Descouvemont [De20] for \(^{11}{\rm Li}+d\) scattering). It directly analyzes recent experimental data from Jhang et al. [JLY20] for \(^{16}{\rm C}+p\) and \(^{16}{\rm C}+d\) elastic and inelastic scattering, complementing earlier experiments with heavy targets by Elekes et al. [EDK04]. The results confirm that, as in the \(^{11}{\rm Li}+d\) case, both projectile and target breakup influence elastic scattering, but for the more deeply bound \(^{16}{\rm C}\) the non-elastic effects below \(60^\circ\) are dominated by the \(2_1^+\) state rather than the non-resonant continuum.
