# Calculation of Exclusive Cross Sections with the Lorentz Integral Transform Method

> The longitudinal structure function of the d(e,e'p) exclusive cross section is calculated with the Lorentz integral transform method. In this approach final state interaction is fully taken into account, but without using a final state wave function. Cross sections are obtained via the inversion of the transform. It is shown that the inversion results are very stable. The comparison to a conventional calculation with an explicit np final state wave function shows that the obtained results are also very precise. Thus the method opens up the possibility to obtain exclusive cross sections for reactions with more than two particles, where it is generally very difficult to calculate the exact final state wave function.

# Calculation of Exclusive Cross Sections with the Lorentz Integral Transform Method

## Key claim

The Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method can be applied to exclusive reactions (not just inclusive ones) and yields results for the d(e,e'p) longitudinal structure function that agree with conventional calculations to better than 1% for most transitions, provided the inversion of the transform is performed with sufficient basis functions and an appropriately small Lorentz width parameter σ_I.

## Method

The authors calculate the longitudinal structure function f_L for the exclusive deuteron breakup reaction d(e,e'p)n using the LIT method, which avoids explicit construction of the final state wave function. They solve inhomogeneous differential equations with bound-state-like boundary conditions to obtain the Lorentz integral transform of the transition matrix elements, then invert the transform using an expansion in basis functions χ_n(E,β) = E^{l+1/2} exp(-βE/n) with a regularization parameter N. The FSI contribution to the T-matrix is obtained from the inverted function R_lj(E) via a principal value integral, and the results are compared to a conventional calculation using an explicit np final state wave function with the semi-realistic TN potential.

## Key numbers

- For kinematics (i) (E_np = 120 MeV, q_cm^2 = 5 fm^{-2}): relative FSI effects compared to Born term are approximately -40% (l=0) and -30% (l=2) for real parts; LIT results agree with conventional calculation to less than 1% relative difference for all multipoles
- For kinematics (ii) (photon line, E_γ = 70 MeV): relative FSI effects are -100% (l=0), -25% (l=2), and -1.5% (l=4); real parts agree to less than 1% with conventional calculation
- For kinematics (iii) (near threshold, E_np = 1 MeV, q_cm^2 = 2 fm^{-2}): relative FSI effects are -230% (l=0) and +2.5% (l=2); LIT results with σ_I = 5 MeV show deviations of about 1% from conventional calculation
- The elastic monopole contribution R_00(E_0) is approximately 1.1 fm^{3/2} for kinematics (i), 14 fm^{3/2} for kinematics (ii), and 15 fm^{3/2} for kinematics (iii)
- For the l=4 transition in kinematics (i), the FSI matrix element is only -2.5% of the corresponding Born term, giving relative differences of order 10^{-4} for the total matrix element

## Context

This work extends the LIT method, previously applied successfully to inclusive breakup cross sections in few-body physics (Efros, Leidemann, Orlandini 1994, 1999), to exclusive reactions for the first time. It follows a theoretical proposal by Efros (1985) for applying integral transforms to exclusive reactions using the Stieltjes transform. The paper demonstrates that the LIT avoids the inversion difficulties that plagued the Stieltjes transform approach (Efros, Leidemann, Orlandini 1992) and opens the possibility to calculate exclusive cross sections for reactions with more than two particles in the final state, where exact final state wave functions are generally impossible to compute.
