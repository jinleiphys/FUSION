# Consistent account of deuteron-induced reactions on Cr up to 60 MeV

> Purpose: Accurate new measurements of low-energy deuteron-induced reaction cross sections for natural Cr target can enhance the related database and the opportunity for an unitary and consistent account of the involved reaction mechanisms. Methods: The activation cross sections of $^{51,52,54}$Mn, $^{51}$Cr, and $^{48}$V nuclei for deuterons incident on natural Cr at energies up to 20 MeV, were measured by the stacked-foil technique and high resolution gamma spectrometry using U-120M cyclotron of the Center of Accelerators and Nuclear Analytical Methods (CANAM) of the Nuclear Physics Institute of the Czech Academy of Sciences (NPI CAS). They as well as formerly available data for deuteron interactions with Cr isotopes up to 60 MeV are the object of an extended analysis of all processes from elastic scattering until the evaporation from fully equilibrated compound system, but with a particular attention given to the BU and DR mechanisms. Results: The new measured activation excitation functions proved essential for the enrichment of the deuteron database, while the theoretical analysis of all available data strengthens for the first time their consistent account provided that (i) a suitable BU and DR assessment is completed by (ii) the assumption of PE and CN contributions corrected for decrease of the total-reaction cross section due to the leakage of the initial deuteron flux towards BU and DR processes. Conclusions: The suitable description of nuclear mechanisms involved within deuteron-induced reactions on chromium, taking into account especially the BU and DR direct processes, is validated by an overall agreement of the calculated and measured cross sections including particularly the new experimental data at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent account of deuteron-induced reactions on chromium up to 60 MeV is achieved only when (i) suitable breakup (BU) and direct reaction (DR) assessments are completed by (ii) pre-equilibrium (PE) and compound-nucleus (CN) contributions corrected for the decrease of the total-reaction cross section due to leakage of the initial deuteron flux towards BU and DR processes.

## Method

Activation cross sections for production of $^{51,52,54}$Mn, $^{51}$Cr, and $^{48}$V from deuterons on natural Cr were measured up to 20 MeV using the stacked-foil technique and high-resolution gamma spectrometry at the U-120M cyclotron. These new data, together with previously available data up to 60 MeV, were analyzed using an extended theoretical framework including elastic scattering, breakup (BU), direct reactions (DR) via DWBA with FRESCO, and pre-equilibrium plus compound-nucleus (PE+CN) processes via TALYS-1.9, with the deuteron optical potential of Daehnick et al. used throughout.

## Key numbers

- New measured cross sections for $^{nat}$Cr$(d,xn)^{54}$Mn, $^{52}$Mn$^g$, $^{52}$Mn$^m$, $^{51}$Mn, $^{nat}$Cr$(d,x)^{51}$Cr, and $^{nat}$Cr$(d,x)^{48}$V at 14 energies from 3.91 to 19.45 MeV, with uncertainties including statistical errors, charge measurement uncertainty (approximately 5 percent), foil thickness uncertainty (2 percent), and HPGe detector efficiency uncertainty (2 percent)
- The $^{nat}$Cr$(d,xn)^{54}$Mn cross section at 19.45 MeV: 17.59 mb (111); at 4.30 MeV: 33.22 mb (193)
- The $^{nat}$Cr$(d,xn)^{52}$Mn$^g$ cross section at 19.45 MeV: 143.17 mb (831); at 8.29 MeV: 2.95 mb (17)
- The $^{nat}$Cr$(d,xn)^{51}$Mn cross section at 8.29 MeV: 5.50 mb (114); at 4.30 MeV: 5.99 mb (83)
- The $^{nat}$Cr$(d,x)^{51}$Cr cross section at 19.45 MeV: 41.93 mb (459); at 3.91 MeV: 20.92 mb (218)
- The $^{nat}$Cr$(d,x)^{48}$V cross section at 19.45 MeV: 0.661 mb (40); at 3.91 MeV: 0.445 mb (26)
- Normalization factor of 0.75 applied to the spin cut-off parameter for the residual nucleus $^{52}$Mn to describe the measured isomeric cross section ratio

## Context

This work extends a series of recent analyses (Avrigeanu et al. on Al, Cu, Nb, Fe, Ni) seeking consistent inclusion of deuteron breakup contributions within activation cross-section calculations, and addresses the long-standing problem that reaction cross sections recommended for high-priority elements (e.g., via Pade approximations in Hermsdorf 2018) lack predictive power and lag behind nuclear modeling advances. The new low-energy data and the validated theoretical framework enable improved deuteron-induced reaction databases for fusion and accelerator applications (ITER, IFMIF, NFS) and provide a benchmark for future microscopic CDCC calculations.
