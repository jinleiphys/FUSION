# Four-body continuum effects in 11Be+d scattering

> We present a new reaction model, which permits the description of reactions where both colliding nuclei present a low threshold to breakup. The method corresponds to a four-body extension of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) model. We first discuss the theoretical formalism, and then apply the method to 11Be+d scattering at Ecm = 45.5 MeV. The 11Be nucleus and the deuteron are described by 10Be+n and p + n structures, respectively. The model involves very large bases, but we show that an accurate description of elastic-scattering data may be achieved only when continuum states of 11Be and of the deuteron are introduced simultaneously. We also discuss breakup calculations, and show that the cross section is larger for 11Be than for the deuteron. The present theory provides reliable wave functions that may be used in the analysis of (d,p) or (d,n) experiments involving radioactive beams.

## Key claim

The paper presents a four-body extension of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method that includes breakup of both colliding nuclei simultaneously, and shows that for 11Be+d scattering at Ecm = 45.5 MeV, accurate description of elastic-scattering data is achieved only when continuum states of both 11Be and the deuteron are introduced together, not when either is treated alone.

## Method

The method extends the standard CDCC approach to a four-body system where both colliding nuclei (11Be described as 10Be+n, and the deuteron as p+n) are treated with explicit continuum discretization via pseudostates. The total wave function is expanded over internal states of both nuclei, and the coupled-channel equations are solved using the R-matrix method with a Lagrange basis. The model uses optical potentials from the Koning-Delaroche global potential for the n+10Be and p+10Be interactions, and the Minnesota potential for the n+n and n+p interactions.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: Ecm = 45.5 MeV (corresponding to Elab(11Be) = 26.9A MeV)
- 11Be neutron separation energy: 0.50 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2 MeV
- Maximum energy for 10Be+n continuum: Emax = 10 MeV with angular momenta I1 = 1/2±, 3/2±, 5/2+
- Maximum energy for p+n continuum: Emax = 15 MeV with I2 = 0+, 2+
- Maximum angular momentum for 11Be+d relative motion: Jmax = 71/2
- Channel radius: a = 25 fm with 50 Gauss-Legendre basis functions
- Number of pseudostates per two-body system: 25 Gauss-Laguerre functions with scaling parameter h = 0.4 fm
- Elastic cross section without breakup overestimated by up to a factor of two near the minimum around 22 degrees
- At large angles (above approximately 35 degrees), full calculation is about 15% lower than experimental data

## Context

This work extends the standard CDCC method, previously limited to three-body systems where one colliding nucleus is treated as structureless, to a four-body framework needed when both nuclei have low breakup thresholds. It directly addresses the 11Be+d system studied experimentally by Chen et al. (2016, Phys. Rev. C 94, 064620), who performed three-body CDCC calculations treating the deuteron as point-like and using effective optical potentials. The present model uses only well-known nucleon-nucleus optical potentials and provides reliable wave functions for future analysis of (d,p) or (d,n) experiments with radioactive beams, as well as potential applications to systems like 8Li+d, 7Li+7Be, or 13C+13C.
