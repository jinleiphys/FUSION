# Bottomonium suppression at RHIC and LHC

> The strong suppression of heavy quarkonia is a good indicator that one has generated a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision. Recent advancements in first principles calculations of the heavy quark potential provide additional insight into this suppression and can be used to further pin down the in-medium properties of the QGP. Realistic 3+1d dissipative hydrodynamical models can be used to simulate the QGP and, with bottomonium as a probe, one can make inferences for the initial temperature of the QGP and other phenomenological parameters such as the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. However, progressing to LHC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions, one expects regeneration to have an increasingly important impact on the suppression observables. In this proceedings, we present a brief overview to set up the model and then provide model results for bottomonium suppression and regeneration, for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim
The paper presents a comprehensive model for bottomonium suppression and regeneration at both RHIC and LHC energies, but the authors note that the pNRQCD-based Strickland-Bazow potential underestimates suppression at RHIC 200 GeV/nucleon collisions while the lattice-vetted Rothkopf potential slightly overestimates suppression for LHC Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV/nucleon.

## Method
The authors used a 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) code to simulate the quark-gluon plasma, combined with two different heavy quark potentials (Strickland-Bazow and Rothkopf) to compute binding energies for six bottomonium states as functions of energy density and momentum-space anisotropy. A first-order rate equation incorporating temperature-dependent breakup rates and bottom quark fugacity was used to model regeneration, with input from experimental bottomonium state cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperatures for RHIC 200 GeV/nucleon collisions: T0 = {0.442, 0.440, 0.439} GeV for 4π η/s = {1, 2, 3}, respectively
- Initial temperatures for LHC 5.02 TeV collisions: T0 = {0.641, 0.632, 0.629} GeV for 4π η/s = {1, 2, 3}, respectively
- Suggested QGP initial temperatures at LHC: T0 = 600-700 MeV at τ0 = 0.25 fm/c for √sNN = 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio range: 4π η/s ~ 1-3
- Bottomonium vacuum binding energies: less than 1 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonium suppression using anisotropic hydrodynamics and first-principles calculations of the heavy quark potential, building on prior work by Strickland and Bazow, and lattice QCD studies by Rothkopf. The model enables more precise inferences about QGP initial temperature and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at both RHIC and LHC energies, while the authors note that future continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD potential extractions may resolve remaining tensions with experimental data.
