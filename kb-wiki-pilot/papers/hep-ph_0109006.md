# From SPS to RHIC: Breaking the Barrier to the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> After 15 years of heavy-ion collision experiments at the AGS and SPS, the recent turn-on of RHIC has initiated a new stage of quark-gluon plasma studies. I review the evidence for deconfined quark-gluon matter at SPS energies and the recent confirmation of some of the key ideas by the new RHIC data. Measurements of the elliptic flow at RHIC provide strong evidence for efficient thermalization during the very early partonic collision stage, resulting in a well-developed quark-gluon plasma with almost ideal fluid-dynamical collective behaviour and a lifetime of several fm/c.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the combined evidence from SPS and early RHIC data strongly supports the formation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions, but with the hedge that at SPS energies only the region at and slightly above the critical temperature can be probed, while RHIC provides access to deeper penetration into the QGP phase. The claim that the prehadronic state at RHIC is indeed a QGP is presented as difficult to avoid given the agreement between elliptic flow data and ideal hydrodynamic calculations, but the paper acknowledges that at SPS the search for direct electromagnetic radiation from the QGP did not provide convincing answers.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental evidence from SPS (Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV) and RHIC (Au+Au at sqrt(s)=130 and 200 A GeV) by analyzing hadron spectra and yields, dilepton spectra, J/psi suppression patterns, and elliptic flow measurements. The author compares these data to predictions from lattice QCD, hydrodynamic models, and hadronic rescattering simulations to assess whether the observed collective behavior, chemical equilibration, and suppression patterns are consistent with QGP formation.

## Key numbers
- Critical energy density for deconfinement: approximately 1 GeV/fm^3
- Critical temperature Tc: approximately 170 MeV ± 10%
- Initial energy density at SPS (Pb+Pb, central): 3.2 ± 0.3 GeV/fm^3 at tau_0 = 1 fm/c
- Initial energy density at RHIC (Au+Au, sqrt(s)=130 A GeV): 60% larger than SPS value
- Initial temperature at SPS: approximately 210-220 MeV
- Initial temperature at RHIC: possibly as high as 350 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature at SPS: approximately 100 MeV with mean transverse flow velocity 0.55 c
- Freeze-out temperature at RHIC: approximately 125 MeV with 20% larger radial flow than SPS
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at SPS: 168 MeV with mu_B = 266 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC: approximately 175 MeV with mu_B = 50 MeV
- Strangeness enhancement: ratio of strange to non-strange quarks in final hadrons is about a factor 2 higher than in pp collisions
- Omega baryon enhancement relative to pBe collisions: factor 17
- Thermalization time scale at RHIC: approximately 0.5-0.6 fm/c

## Context
This review extends the line of work from the AGS and SPS heavy-ion programs (spanning 15 years) into the new RHIC era, confirming key predictions for QGP formation such as strangeness enhancement (predicted by Rafelski), J/psi suppression (predicted by Matsui and Satz), and elliptic flow as an early signature (developed by Ollitrault, Sorge, and others). The paper enables future studies of QGP properties at RHIC and LHC, particularly through electromagnetic radiation and jet quenching, and notes that the hadronic chemical equilibrium temperature coinciding with the lattice QCD critical temperature supports the interpretation that hadrons are born into chemical equilibrium at hadronization.
