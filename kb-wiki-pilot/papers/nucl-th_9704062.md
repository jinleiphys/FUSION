# Breakup probabilities and the optical potential in elastic deuteron scattering

> We investigate the effect of the deuteron breakup on the optical potential for the elastic scattering of 56 MeV deuterons from $^{51}$V. The breakup probabilities calculated within the post-form distorted wave Born-approximation theory of breakup reactions are fitted to derive the contribution to the optical potential from the breakup channels. The breakup potentials are found to have large real as well as imaginary parts. Thus the dynamical polarization potential due to the breakup process is expected to modify strongly the real part of the optical potential calculated by the double folding model.

## Key claim

The breakup process for 56 MeV deuterons on 51V produces a dynamical polarization potential (DPP) with both large real and imaginary parts, which strongly modifies the real part of the optical potential calculated by the double folding model; however, the authors note that there are ambiguities in the extracted breakup potentials, as reducing the number of Fourier-Bessel terms from 14 to 4 still yields a reasonable fit to the breakup probabilities.

## Method

The authors calculated deuteron breakup probabilities for the (d,p) and (d,n) channels using the post-form distorted wave Born approximation (PFDWBA) theory, including both elastic and inelastic breakup modes. They then fitted these PFDWBA breakup probabilities by varying a Fourier-Bessel expansion of the breakup potential (both real and imaginary parts) in an optical model framework, where the full optical potential was taken from the global parameter set of Daehnick, Childs and Vrcelj, and the neutron and proton optical potentials from Becchetti and Greenlees. The fitting procedure minimized chi-squared by simultaneously varying 14 Fourier-Bessel coefficients for each potential component.

## Key numbers

- Total breakup cross section for 56 MeV deuterons on 51V: 627 mb (inelastic (d,p): 290 mb, inelastic (d,n): 214 mb, elastic (d,pn): 122 mb)
- Total (d,p) breakup cross section: 412 mb, compared to the measured value of 481 mb reported by Matsuoka et al.
- Reaction cross section for the full potential: 1571 mb
- Reaction cross section with the real dissociation potential subtracted: 1405 mb (difference of 166 mb, larger than the 122 mb elastic breakup cross section)
- Reaction cross section with the complex dissociation potential subtracted: 2173 mb (increase rather than decrease)

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied by Ingemarsson to alpha-particle breakup on 62Ni at 172.5 MeV, where only the (alpha,3He) channel was considered. The deuteron case is simpler (only two breakup channels) and allows testing the method in detail before tackling the more ambitious task of calculating all alpha breakup channels. The results contradict the findings of Yabana et al. for 80 MeV deuterons on 58Ni, who obtained weaker real and imaginary DPPs but neglected Coulomb breakup; the present work includes Coulomb and nuclear breakup on equal footing and reproduces experimental breakup cross sections. The study enables future calculations of breakup potentials for all alpha breakup channels and suggests that all light projectiles require dynamical polarization potentials in folding model calculations at higher energies.
