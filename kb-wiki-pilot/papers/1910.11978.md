# Theoretical analysis of $^8$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction and the critical angular momentum for complete fusion

> In a theoretical approach, the complete and incomplete fusions are investigated by considering the $^8$Li+$^{208}$Pb reaction. By decreasing the projectile ground-state binding energy $\varepsilon_b$ from its known experimental value, the complete fusion is shown to have insignificant dependence on such variations, whereas the incomplete fusion strongly depends on that. The complete and incomplete fusion cross sections are calculated by using a combination of both continuum-discretized coupled-channel and sum-rule models. To this end, an incident-energy dependent cut-off angular momentum $L_c$ is first obtained by using the available complete fusion experimental data, within an approach which is extended to model results obtained for other incident-energies. An approximated fitted expression linking $L_c$ to the well-known critical value $L_{\rm crit}$ derived by Wilczyński [Nucl. Phys. A 216 (1973) 386] suggests a generalization of the corresponding sum-rule model to energies around and below the Coulomb barrier.

# Theoretical analysis of $^8$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction and the critical angular momentum for complete fusion

## Key claim

The complete fusion cross section for $^8$Li + $^{208}$Pb shows insignificant dependence on variations of the projectile ground-state binding energy below its experimental value (2.033 MeV), whereas the incomplete fusion cross section strongly depends on such variations. This supports the conclusion that weak binding energy alone is not a sufficient condition to explain the suppression of complete fusion, consistent with recent experimental findings in $^{7,8}$Li + $^{209}$Bi reactions.

## Method

The authors combine the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) method with the sum-rule model of Wilczyński to calculate total, complete, and incomplete fusion cross sections. The total fusion cross section is first obtained from CDCC calculations, then the complete fusion cross section is extracted by summing partial total fusion cross sections from $L=0$ up to an energy-dependent cutoff angular momentum $L_c$, which is determined by fitting to available experimental complete fusion data. The incomplete fusion is obtained as the difference between total and complete fusion. The projectile ground-state binding energy is artificially varied from the experimental value of 2.033 MeV down to 0.5 MeV by adjusting the depth of the Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ground-state binding energy of $^8$Li: $\varepsilon_b = 2.033$ MeV; three additional values considered: 1.5, 1.0, and 0.5 MeV
- Cutoff angular momenta $L_c$ (in units of $\hbar$) for incident energies with experimental data: $L_c = 9$ at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 32.10$ MeV, 11 at 33.00 MeV, 12 at 33.90 MeV, 13 at 34.80 MeV, 14 at 35.70 MeV, 15 at 36.60 MeV, and 16 at 37.50 MeV
- For higher incident energies (45–75 MeV), $L_c$ ranges from approximately 24–25 at 45 MeV to 38–39 at 75 MeV
- Critical angular momentum from the sum-rule model: $L_{\text{crit}} \approx 41$ for the $^8$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction
- Coulomb barrier $V_B = 28.65$ MeV (from the São Paulo Potential)
- Complete fusion suppression of about 35% for $\varepsilon_b = 2.03$ MeV at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 37.5$ MeV, increasing to about 75% suppression for $\varepsilon_b = 0.50$ MeV
- Fitted expression: $L_c \simeq \left\{1-\exp\left[-1.7\left(E_{\text{c.m.}}/V_B - 1\right)\right]\right\} L_{\text{crit}}$

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule model originally developed by Wilczyński [Nucl. Phys. A 216 (1973) 386] for energies well above the Coulomb barrier to energies around and below the barrier, by introducing an energy-dependent cutoff angular momentum $L_c$. It provides theoretical support to recent experimental results by Cook et al. (2020, 2021) on $^{7,8}$Li + $^{209}$Bi reactions, which suggested that charge clustering rather than weak projectile binding is the crucial factor in complete fusion suppression. The methodology follows the approach of Lei et al. for $^{6,7}$Li projectiles, and the results enable future extension of the combined CDCC and sum-rule model to incident energies below the Coulomb barrier.
