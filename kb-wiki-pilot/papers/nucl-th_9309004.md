# Relativistic description of electron scattering on the deuteron

> Within a quasipotential framework a relativistic analysis is presented of the deuteron current. Assuming that the singularities from the nucleon propagators are important, a so-called equal time approximation of the current is constructed. This is applied to both elastic and inelastic electron scattering. As dynamical model the relativistic one boson exchange model is used. Reasonable agreement is found with a previous relativistic calculation of the elastic electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron. For the unpolarized inelastic electron scattering effects of final state interactions and relativistic corrections to the structure functions are considered in the impulse approximation. Two specific kinematic situations are studied as examples.

## Key claim

Within a quasipotential framework, the authors construct an equal-time (ET) approximation to the deuteron electromagnetic current by assuming that the singularities from the nucleon propagators dominate and that the relative-energy dependence in the vertex functions can be neglected. This approximation yields reasonable agreement with a previous relativistic calculation of elastic form factors, but the authors note that the dip position in the magnetic form factor is at too low momentum transfer compared to data, and that the results are sensitive to the inclusion of negative-energy states.

## Method

The analysis uses the relativistic one-boson-exchange (OBE) model as the dynamical nucleon-nucleon interaction, with exchange of π, ρ, ω, η, ε (σ), and δ mesons regularized by monopole form factors. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is reduced using the Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze (BSLT) quasipotential approximation, and the electromagnetic current is treated in an equal-time (ET) approximation where the relative-energy dependence is integrated out of the nucleon propagators only. For inelastic scattering, the impulse approximation is used, including final-state interactions via the half-off-shell NN t-matrix, and the structure functions R_L, R_T, R_TT, and R_TL are computed for exclusive and inclusive reactions.

## Key numbers

- The meson-nucleon coupling constants g_ε and g_δ were adapted from the original fit (set A) to include all spinor states (set B), yielding phase shifts up to J=2 that reproduce experimental data from Arndt et al.
- For elastic scattering, the charge form factor F_C and quadrupole form factor F_Q show that negative-energy components contribute noticeably only at high momentum transfer; the dip in F_C is sensitive to the model used.
- The magnetic form factor F_M shows large contributions from negative-energy states in the dip region, shifting the dip position to higher momentum transfer when these states are included.
- The electric form factor A and magnetic form factor B in the ET approximation yield essentially the same results as the BSLT approximation, except for small differences at high momentum transfer for A and in the dip region for B.
- The tensor polarization t_20 agrees with experimental data up to q > 4 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations of elastic deuteron electromagnetic form factors by Hummel and Tjon (using the BSLT quasipotential approximation) to include inelastic electron scattering, specifically the breakup reaction d(e,e'p)n and inclusive (e,e') processes. The approach is based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation framework with the OBE model of Fleischer and Tjon, and it contrasts with nonrelativistic models that use different forms of electromagnetic operators (e.g., F_1 vs. G_E). The authors find that relativistic effects are particularly large in the interference structure function R_TL, showing the failure of the nonrelativistic models discussed, and that the next-leading-order correction to these operators reasonably represents the relativistic predictions in the kinematics studied. The paper sets the stage for a subsequent detailed treatment of meson-exchange current contributions needed to ensure gauge invariance.
