# Breakup mechanisms in the 6He+64Zn reaction at near-barrier energies

> New experimental results for the elastic scattering of 6He on 64Zn at incident energies of 15.0 and 18.0 MeV and 4He at 17.5 MeV along with results already published at 10.0 and 13.6 MeV, are presented. Elastic and alpha experimental cross sections are compared with coupled-reaction-channel, continuum-discretized coupledchannel, and DWBA inclusive-breakup models. The large yield of alpha particles observed at all measured energies can be explained by considering a nonelastic breakup mechanism.

## Key claim
The large yield of alpha particles observed in the 6He+64Zn reaction at all measured near-barrier energies can be explained by considering a non-elastic breakup mechanism, not solely by elastic breakup. The abstract's claim that this mechanism "can explain" the data is qualified by the paper's finding that CDCC calculations alone significantly underestimate the alpha cross sections, and that the non-elastic breakup contribution must be included via CRC or DWBA-based models to achieve reasonable agreement.

## Method
Elastic scattering angular distributions for 6He+64Zn at 15.0 and 18.0 MeV and 4He+64Zn at 17.5 MeV were measured using silicon strip detector arrays with time-of-flight particle identification. The data were compared with optical model, three-body and four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC), coupled-reaction-channel (CRC), and DWBA inclusive-breakup calculations, using the Sao Paulo potential for the real part of the nucleus-nucleus interaction and a di-neutron model for 6He.

## Key numbers
- Incident beam energies for 6He: 15.0 and 18.0 MeV (new data); previously published data at 10.0 and 13.6 MeV also analyzed
- Incident beam energy for 4He: 17.5 MeV (new data)
- 6He beam average intensity: 3x10^6 particles per second
- Target thickness: 530 microgram/cm^2 self-supporting 64Zn foil
- Energy at center of target: 14.85 and 17.9 MeV for 6He, 17.4 MeV for 4He
- CDCC calculations included 4He-2n relative angular momenta up to l_max = 4 and excitation energies up to 8 MeV
- CRC calculations considered partial waves up to l_f = 5 for the 64Zn-2n relative motion, with states discretized using 2 MeV bins up to 10 MeV
- Imaginary diffuseness parameter a_d approximately 1 fm for 6He+64Zn optical model fits, attributed to long-range Coulomb and/or nuclear couplings

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 6He-induced reactions on medium-mass targets, particularly the 6He+64Zn measurements at lower energies reported in Di Pietro et al. (2003, 2004) and Scuderi et al. (2011). It also builds on analyses of 6He+206Pb by Fernandez-Garcia et al. (2010) and Escrig et al. (2007), where two-neutron transfer channels were found important for explaining elastic and alpha-particle production cross sections. The results enable a more complete understanding of breakup mechanisms in halo-nucleus reactions, showing that non-elastic breakup (including two-neutron transfer, target excitation, and neutron absorption) dominates the alpha-particle yield, and that no single existing theory can describe all experimental observables simultaneously.
