# Lectures on Diffraction and Saturation of Nuclear Partons in DIS off Heavy Nuclei

> The Lorentz contraction of ultrarelativistic nuclei entails a spatial overlap and fusion (recombination, saturation) of partons belonging to different nucleons at the same impact parameter. In these lectures we present a consistent description of the fusion of partons in terms of nuclear attenuation of color dipole states of the photon and collective Weizsäcker-Williams (WW) gluon structure function of a nucleus. The point that all observables for DIS off nuclei are uniquely calculable in terms of the nuclear WW glue amounts to a new form of factorization in the saturation regime. We start with the theory of multichannel propagation of color dipoles in a nuclear medium including the color-singlet to color-octet to color-octet transitions. We show how the Glauber-Gribov formulas are recovered from the multichannel formalism. Then we derive the two-plateau momentum distribution of final state (FS) quarks produced in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei in the saturation regime. Then we comment on the signatures of saturation in exclusive diffractive DIS. A large body of these lectures is on the recent theory of jet-jet inclusive cross sections. We show that for hard dijets the decorrelation momentum is of the order of the nuclear saturation momentum $Q_A$. For minijets with the transverse momentum below the saturation scale we predict a complete disappearance of the azimuthal jet-jet correlation. We conclude with comment on a possible relevance of the decorrelation of jets to the experimental data from the STAR-RHIC Collaboration.

## Key claim

All observables for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei are uniquely calculable in terms of the nuclear Weizsacker-Williams (WW) gluon structure function, which amounts to a new form of factorization in the saturation regime; however, this claim holds only for the regime where the Lorentz contraction of ultrarelativistic nuclei entails spatial overlap and fusion of partons from different nucleons at the same impact parameter, specifically at momentum fraction \(x \lesssim x_A = 1/(R_A m_N)\).

## Method

The authors develop a multichannel propagation formalism for color dipoles in a nuclear medium, including color-singlet to color-octet to color-octet transitions, and show how the Glauber-Gribov formulas are recovered from this multichannel approach. They then derive the two-plateau momentum distribution of final-state quarks produced in DIS off nuclei in the saturation regime, and extend the analysis to jet-jet inclusive cross sections using the \(k_{\perp}\)-factorization framework with the unintegrated gluon structure function of the target.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale for heavy nuclei is denoted \(Q_A\), and for hard dijets the decorrelation momentum is of order \(Q_A\).
- For minijets with transverse momentum below the saturation scale \(Q_A\), a complete disappearance of the azimuthal jet-jet correlation is predicted.
- For coherent diffractive DIS off heavy nuclei, the decorrelation momentum \(|\mathbf{\Delta}| = |\mathbf{p}_+ + \mathbf{p}_-| \lesssim 1/R_A \sim m_{\pi}/A^{1/3}\).
- The fraction of diffractive DIS off free nucleons is \(\eta_D \lesssim 6-10\%\), leaving little room for genuine saturation effects even at HERA.
- For heavy nuclei at small \(x\), diffractive DIS makes precisely 50% of the total DIS events.

## Context

This work extends the early 1975 treatment of nuclear opacity in terms of parton fusion and saturation by Nikolaev and Zakharov, and the pQCD link between nuclear opacity and saturation considered in 1991 by Nikolaev and Zakharov and by Mueller, as well as the work of McLerran et al. on fusion of nuclear gluons. The lectures provide a consistent multiple-scattering theory of jet-jet decorrelation in DIS off nuclei, and the authors speculate on the relevance of their predictions for the decorrelation of jets to experimental data from the STAR-RHIC Collaboration on the dissolution of away jets in central nuclear collisions.
