# Disentangling the transfer and breakup contributions from the inclusive 8Li+ 208Pb reaction

> An analysis of the elastic and one-neutron removal channel for the reaction 8Li+208Pb reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier is presented. For the elastic scattering, an optical model analysis of the experimental data is performed. The observed 7Li is interpreted as the superposition of the one-neutron transfer reaction, 208Pb(8Li,7Li)209Pb, and the breakup reaction. The separate contribution of each one of these processes has been calculated within the DWBA formalism. The sum of both contributions explains adequately the experimental angular distribution of 7Li.

## Key claim

The observed \(^{7}\)Li yield in the \(^{8}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier is a superposition of one-neutron transfer and breakup contributions; the DWBA calculations show that transfer accounts for about 80% of the yield, but breakup becomes the dominant mechanism at scattering angles above 120 degrees.

## Method

The elastic scattering data were analyzed with an optical model, using potentials from \(^{7}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb as starting parameters and fitting the imaginary depth and diffuseness to reproduce the \(^{8}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb data. The one-neutron transfer reaction \(^{208}\)Pb(\(^{8}\)Li,\(^{7}\)Li)\(^{209}\)Pb and the breakup reaction (transfer of the valence neutron to the target continuum) were calculated separately within the post-form Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA). For the breakup, the \(n\)-\(^{208}\)Pb continuum was discretized into energy bins up to 6 MeV relative energy, retaining partial waves \(0 \leq \ell \leq 6\).

## Key numbers

- The valence neutron binding energy in \(^{8}\)Li is \(\epsilon_0 = 2.03\) MeV.
- The \(^{8}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb reaction was measured at \(E_{lab} = 34.4\) MeV; the \(^{7}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb comparison data were at \(E_{lab} = 33\) MeV.
- The optical potential "Pot 1" for \(^{8}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb has \(V_0 = 37.1\) MeV, \(W = 154\) MeV, \(a_w = 0.57\) fm, giving \(\sigma_R = 1231\) mb and \(\chi^2 = 1.11\).
- The optical potential "Pot 2" has \(V_0 = 15.4\) MeV, \(W = 58.4\) MeV, \(a_w = 0.70\) fm, giving \(\sigma_R = 1275\) mb and \(\chi^2 = 1.51\).
- The transfer channel accounts for about 80% of the observed \(^{7}\)Li yield.
- The \(^{8}\)Li \(1^{+}\) excited state lies at \(\epsilon_x = 0.98\) MeV; the \(^{7}\)Li \(1/2^{-}\) excited state lies at \(\epsilon_x = 0.48\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of reactions with weakly bound radioactive beams at low energies, following the observation of a strong \(^{4}\)He group in the \(^{6}\)He+\(^{209}\)Bi reaction (Aguilera et al., 2000, 2001). The \(^{8}\)Li projectile is simpler than \(^{6}\)He because a two-cluster model (\(^{7}\)Li core plus valence neutron) is adequate, whereas \(^{6}\)He would require a three-cluster model. The results enable a quantitative disentangling of transfer and breakup mechanisms in one-neutron removal channels, and the optical model analysis shows a more absorptive nature for \(^{8}\)Li compared to \(^{7}\)Li, in apparent contradiction with earlier work by Smith et al. (1991) and Becchetti et al. (1993).
