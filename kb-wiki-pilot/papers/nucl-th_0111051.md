# Effect of continuum couplings in fusion of halo $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb around the Coulomb barrier

> The effect of continuum couplings in the fusion of the halo nucleus $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb around the Coulomb barrier is studied using a three-body model within a coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism. We investigate in particular the role of continuum-continuum couplings. These are found to hinder total, complete and incomplete fusion processes. Couplings to the projectile $1p_{1/2}$ bound excited state redistribute the complete and incomplete fusion cross sections, but the total fusion cross section remains nearly constant. Results show that continuum-continuum couplings enhance the irreversibility of breakup and reduce the flux that penetrates the Coulomb barrier. Converged total fusion cross sections agree with the experimental ones for energies around the Coulomb barrier, but underestimate those for energies well above the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim
Continuum-continuum couplings in the breakup of the halo nucleus \(^{11}\)Be on \(^{208}\)Pb hinder total, complete, and incomplete fusion processes, contrary to earlier suggestions that breakup might enhance fusion; the converged total fusion cross sections agree with experimental data for energies around the Coulomb barrier but underestimate them by about 41% for energies well above the barrier.

## Method
A three-body model (core + valence neutron + target) was used within the coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism, implemented with the code FRESCO. The projectile \(^{11}\)Be was described by bound states (\(2s_{1/2}\) ground state and \(1p_{1/2}\) excited state) and continuum states discretised into bins for partial waves up to \(g\)-waves, with couplings between all excited states (bound-continuum and continuum-continuum) included. Fusion cross sections were defined via absorption by a short-ranged imaginary potential placed well inside the Coulomb barrier, distinguishing complete fusion (absorption from projectile bound channels) from incomplete fusion (absorption from breakup channels).

## Key numbers
- The Coulomb barrier for the elastic channel is approximately \(V_B \approx 36\) MeV in the center-of-mass system.
- Converged total fusion cross sections for \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb underestimate experimental data for \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{209}\)Bi by about 41% for energies well above the Coulomb barrier.
- A maximum continuum energy beyond 9 MeV is needed for convergence; fusion cross sections change by about 10% around the barrier when increasing from 9 MeV to 10 MeV.
- A continuum discretisation density greater than 1.67 bins/MeV is needed; fusion cross sections change by about 6.5% around the barrier when increasing from 1.67 to 2 bins/MeV.
- Continuum partial waves beyond \(f\)-waves and potential multipoles beyond \(K=3\) are needed; including up to \(g\)-waves and \(K \leq 4\) changes total fusion by about 8%, complete fusion by about 100%, and incomplete fusion by about 3% around the barrier.
- The contribution to complete fusion from the elastic channel is 1.7–8 times smaller than from the bound excited state \(1p_{1/2}\) for energies above the Coulomb barrier when continuum-continuum couplings are included.

## Context
This work extends earlier coupled-channels calculations by Hagino et al. (Phys. Rev. C 61, 037602, 2000) for the same system, which did not include continuum-continuum couplings, the projectile's halo structure in the monopole potential, or partial waves beyond \(p_{3/2}\) in the continuum. The results contradict predictions of fusion enhancement from breakup by Dasso and Vitturi (Phys. Rev. C 50, R12, 1994), instead showing suppression due to continuum-continuum couplings. The study enables future experiments to clarify the \(^{11}\)Be fusion mechanism, as the existing data for \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{209}\)Bi (Signorini et al., Eur. Phys. J. A 2, 227, 1998) may have overestimated fission cross sections and missed the 3n channel.
