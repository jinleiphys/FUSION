# Towards a microscopic description of 12C+12C fusion at stellar energies

> I present a fully microscopic description of the 12C+12C fusion reaction at stellar energies. Utilizing the multichannel Resonating Group Method (RGM), my model explicitly includes 12C+12C and alpha+20Ne reaction channels (with excited states). This approach provides a consistent, simultaneous, description of fusion, elastic scattering, and 24Mg spectroscopy. Results for 12C+12C elastic scattering show excellent agreement with experimental data, significantly improving the single-channel approximations. Spectroscopic analysis reveals that 24Mg states and resonances are highly mixed configurations, contradicting the concept of pure "molecular states." The calculated fusion S-factor is consistent with available experimental data and predicts both narrow and broad resonances near the Coulomb barrier. Main resonance widths originate primarily from the alpha+20Ne exit channels. The S-factor exhibits a decrease at low energies, providing a microscopic support for the hypothesis of fusion hindrance. This work is a first step towards a reliable theoretical extrapolation of the 12C+12C reaction to deep stellar burning temperatures. Future works should include the neutron and proton channels to provide a complete description of the 12C+12C fusion.

## Key claim
The paper presents a fully microscopic, multichannel Resonating Group Method (RGM) calculation of the 12C+12C fusion reaction that explicitly includes the alpha+20Ne exit channels, thereby eliminating the need for a phenomenological imaginary potential. The calculated fusion S-factor is consistent with available experimental data and exhibits a decrease at low energies, providing microscopic support for the hypothesis of fusion hindrance, but this conclusion is explicitly limited to the alpha channel and could be affected by the omitted proton and neutron channels.

## Method
The author uses the multichannel Resonating Group Method (RGM), equivalent to the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM), to describe the 24-nucleon system. The model explicitly includes 12C+12C and alpha+20Ne reaction channels, with the 12C and 20Ne clusters described by all p-shell and sd-shell shell-model configurations, respectively. The scattering matrices are obtained via the microscopic R-matrix method, and the alpha component of the fusion cross section is computed from the transfer matrix elements to the alpha+20Ne channels, with no phenomenological imaginary potential.

## Key numbers
- The 12C+12C ground-state binding energy is calculated to be 11.9 MeV, slightly lower than the experimental value of 13.9 MeV.
- The 12C+12C channel represents at most 10% of the wave function for the lowest 0+, 2+, and 4+ states of 24Mg.
- Resonance widths are dominated by the alpha+20Ne exit channels; for example, a 0+ resonance at E_cm = 1.14 MeV has a 12C+12C partial width of 1.2e-21 MeV and an alpha+20Ne(2+) partial width of 0.25 MeV.
- The S-factor is calculated for the alpha channel only, and the model predicts narrow 0+ and 4+ resonances at low energies, including a 4+ resonance at E_cm = 0.30 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous single-channel microscopic calculations of the 12C+12C system (e.g., Baye and Descouvemont 1984) by including multiple 12C states and the dominant alpha+20Ne reaction channels. It contradicts the concept of pure "molecular states" found in those earlier single-channel calculations, showing that 24Mg states are highly mixed. This calculation is a first step towards a reliable theoretical extrapolation of the 12C+12C reaction to stellar energies, and future work should include the p+23Na and n+23Mg channels to provide a complete description.
