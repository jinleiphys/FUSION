# In search of beyond mean-field signatures in heavy-ion fusion reactions

> Examination of high-resolution, experimental fusion excitation functions for $^{16,17,18}$O + $^{12}$C reveals a remarkable irregular behavior that is rooted in the structure of both the colliding nuclei and the quasi-molecular composite system. The impact of the $\ell$-dependent fusion barriers is assessed using a time-dependent Hartree-Fock model. Barrier penetrabilities, taken directly from a density-constrained calculation, provide a significantly improved description of the experimental data as compared to the standard Hill-Wheeler approach. The remaining deviations between the parameter-free theoretical mean-field predictions and experimental fusion cross sections are exposed and discussed.

## Key claim

High-resolution fusion excitation functions for $^{16,17,18}$O + $^{12}$C reveal irregular oscillatory behavior rooted in nuclear structure, but the parameter-free TDHF$^*$ model (a hybrid density-constrained TDHF/TDHF approach) systematically overestimates the measured cross section for $^{17}$O at center-of-mass energies between 14 and 21 MeV, while providing satisfactory agreement for $^{16}$O and $^{18}$O.

## Method

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations were performed on a 3D Cartesian grid using the UNEDF1 energy density functional for collisions of $^{16,17,18}$O with $^{12}$C across a range of impact parameters and energies above the barrier, with approximately 3000 individual trajectories simulated. Barrier penetrabilities for each $\ell$-wave were extracted directly from density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) potentials rather than using the standard Hill-Wheeler approximation, and this hybrid approach (designated TDHF$^*$) was compared to raw TDHF results and experimental data from multiple combined datasets.

## Key numbers

- Fusion excitation functions measured for $^{16,17,18}$O + $^{12}$C from approximately 8 to 22 MeV center-of-mass energy
- Prominent peaks observed in $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C at $E_{\rm c.m.} \approx 11$ MeV, 14 MeV, and 16.5 MeV
- TDHF calculations performed for angular momenta $8 < \ell \leq 20\hbar$
- Collision energy scanned in steps of 0.25 MeV across the reported range
- Approximately 3000 individual trajectories simulated for the entire study
- For $^{17}$O + $^{12}$C, 200 random initial orientations of the deformed projectile were considered

## Context

This work extends prior studies of oscillatory structure in light heavy-ion fusion excitation functions, including $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C (Sperr et al., 1976), $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C (Sperr et al., 1976a; Kovar et al., 1979), and $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O (Fernandez et al., 1978; Kolata et al., 1979), by providing a parameter-free microscopic confrontation with TDHF calculations. The remaining discrepancies, particularly the suppression of the $^{17}$O fusion cross section, point to the need for beyond-mean-field effects such as breakup and transfer channels, specialization energy hindrance, or transient cluster configurations, motivating future high-resolution exclusive measurements and continued theoretical development.
