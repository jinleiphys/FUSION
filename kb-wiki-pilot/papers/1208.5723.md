# Solar Neutrinos: Status and Prospects

> We describe the current status of solar neutrino measurements and of the theory -- both neutrino physics and solar astrophysics -- employed in interpreting measurements. Important recent developments include Super-Kamiokande's determination of the neutrino-electron elastic scattering rate for 8B neutrinos to 3%; the latest SNO global analysis in which the inclusion of low-energy data from SNO I and II significantly narrowed the range of allowed values for the neutrino mixing angle theta12; Borexino results for both the 7Be and pep neutrino fluxes, the first direct measurements constraining the rate of ppI and ppII burning in the Sun; global reanalyses of solar neutrino data that take into account new reactor results on theta13; a new decadal evaluation of the nuclear physics of the pp chain and CNO cycle defining best values and uncertainties in the nuclear microphysics input to solar models; recognition of an emerging discrepancy between two tests of solar metallicity, helioseismological mappings of the sound speed in the solar interior, and analyses of the metal photoabsorption lines based on our best current description of the Sun's photosphere; a new round of standard solar model calculations optimized to agree either with helioseismology or with the new photospheric analysis; and, motivated by the solar abundance problem, the development of nonstandard, accreting solar models, in order to investigate possible consequences of the metal segregation that occurred in the proto-solar disk. We review this progress and describe how new experiments such as SNO+ could help us further exploit neutrinos as a unique probe of stellar interiors.

## Key claim

Solar neutrino measurements have now reached sufficient precision that, with neutrino oscillation parameters largely determined, the remaining discrepancies between standard solar model predictions and helioseismological data, particularly the "solar abundance problem" where photospheric metal abundances from 3D models are ~30% below 1D values, can be investigated using neutrinos as a probe of stellar interiors, though the interpretation still depends on which metallicity scale (GS98 or AGSS09) is adopted.

## Method

The authors review and synthesize results from multiple experiments: Super-Kamiokande's measurement of neutrino-electron elastic scattering for 8B neutrinos; SNO's global analysis combining data from phases I, II, and III including low-energy data; Borexino's measurements of 7Be and pep neutrino fluxes; and global reanalyses incorporating reactor measurements of θ13 from Daya Bay, Reno, and Double Chooz. They also evaluate the Solar Fusion II nuclear physics compilation for pp-chain and CNO-cycle reaction rates, compare standard solar models using GS98 (1D photospheric) and AGSS09 (3D photospheric) metallicity abundances against helioseismological sound-speed profiles, and examine nonstandard accreting solar models motivated by the solar abundance problem.

## Key numbers

* Super-Kamiokande determined the ν-e elastic scattering rate for 8B neutrinos to 3% precision
* SNO's inclusion of low-energy data from SNO I and II significantly narrowed the allowed range for the neutrino mixing angle θ12
* The photospheric metal abundance from 3D models (AGSS09) is ~30% below 1D values (GS98)
* The GS98-SFII SSM predicts 8B neutrino flux of 5.58(1 ± 0.14) × 10^6/cm^2/s; AGSS09-SFII predicts 4.59(1 ± 0.14) × 10^6/cm^2/s; the solar value from luminosity-constrained analysis is 5.00(1 ± 0.03) × 10^6/cm^2/s
* The 7Be neutrino flux: GS98-SFII predicts 5.00(1 ± 0.07) × 10^9/cm^2/s; AGSS09-SFII predicts 4.56(1 ± 0.07) × 10^9/cm^2/s; solar value is 4.82(1+0.05-0.04) × 10^9/cm^2/s
* The pp neutrino flux: GS98-SFII predicts 5.98(1 ± 0.006) × 10^10/cm^2/s; AGSS09-SFII predicts 6.03(1 ± 0.006) × 10^10/cm^2/s; solar value is 6.05(1+0.003-0.011) × 10^10/cm^2/s
* The pep neutrino flux: GS98-SFII predicts 1.44(1 ± 0.012) × 10^8/cm^2/s; AGSS09-SFII predicts 1.47(1 ± 0.012) × 10^8/cm^2/s; solar value is 1.46(1+0.010-0.014) × 10^8/cm^2/s
* The chlorine experiment final result: 2.56 ± 0.16 ± 0.16 SNU, about a factor of three below the GS98-SFII SSM prediction of 8.00 ± 0.97 SNU
* The S-factor for 7Be(p,γ)8B: S17(0) = 20.8 ± 0.7 (expt) ± 1.4 (theor) from Solar Fusion II
* Helioseismology extracts sound speed throughout most of the Sun's interior to accuracy ~0.1%
* The convective zone radius from GS98-SFII: 0.7124 R⊙; from AGSS09-SFII: 0.7231 R⊙; helioseismic value: 0.713 ± 0.001 R⊙

## Context

This review extends the line of work summarized in earlier Annual Reviews articles (Haxton 1995) and builds on the discoveries of neutrino oscillations by Super-Kamiokande (1998 atmospheric neutrino results) and SNO's measurements of both electron and heavy-flavor components of the 8B solar neutrino flux. It incorporates the Solar Fusion II nuclear physics evaluation (SFII), new photospheric abundance analyses from AGSS09 using 3D atmospheric models, and reactor measurements of θ13 from Daya Bay, Reno, and Double Chooz. The work enables future experiments such as SNO+ to further exploit neutrinos as a unique probe of stellar interiors, particularly to resolve the solar abundance problem by comparing SSM predictions against precise neutrino flux measurements.
