# Possibility of bottom-catalyzed matter genesis near to primordial QGP hadronization

> We study bottom flavor abundance in the early Universe near to a temperature $T_\mathrm{H}\simeq150\,\mathrm{MeV}$, the condition for hadronization of deconfined quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We show bottom flavor abundance nonequilibrium lasting microseconds. In our study we use that in both QGP, and the hadronic gas phase (HG) $b$ and $\bar b$ quarks near $T_\mathrm{H}$ are bound in B-mesons and antimesons subject to $CP$ violating weak decays. A coincident non-equilibrium abundance of bottom flavor can lead to matter genesis at required strength: a) The specific thermal yield per entropy is $n_b^{th}/σ=10^{-10}\sim10^{-13}$. b) Considering time scales, millions of cycles of B-meson decays, and $b\bar b$-pair recreation processes occur.

## Key claim

Bottom flavor abundance in the early Universe can achieve a chemical non-equilibrium lasting microseconds near the QGP hadronization temperature, and the resulting millions of cycles of B-meson decays and reformation can produce the observed matter-antimatter asymmetry if a small CP-violating asymmetry of order epsilon ~ 10^-5 per cycle is present, contingent on a yet-unknown baryon number violation mechanism.

## Method

The authors compute the thermal equilibrium abundance of bottom quarks near T_H ~ 150 MeV using Boltzmann statistics and compare it to the entropy density. They then solve a master equation for the fugacity of B_c mesons, balancing the gluon-fusion production rate of b-bbar pairs against the weak-interaction decay rate of B_c mesons (lifespan ~ 10^-12 s), and compare these rates to the Hubble expansion rate to determine the freeze-out condition. The number of formation-decay cycles during the ~10 microsecond QGP-to-hadron-gas phase transition is calculated, and the required per-cycle asymmetry epsilon is derived from the observed baryon-to-entropy ratio.

## Key numbers

- QGP hadronization temperature: T_H ~ 150-160 MeV
- Bottom quark thermal yield per entropy at T_H: n_b^th / sigma = 10^-10 to 10^-13
- B_c meson lifespan: tau_Bc = 0.51 x 10^-12 s; B_u, B_d, B_s lifespans: 1.51-1.64 x 10^-12 s
- Duration of QGP-to-HG phase transition: tau_H ~ 10 microseconds
- Number of bottom formation-decay cycles: Cycle = tau_H / tau_Bc ~ 2 x 10^7
- Required per-cycle matter-antimatter asymmetry: epsilon ~ 2.75 x 10^-5
- Present-day baryon-to-entropy ratio: B/S ~ 7.69 x 10^-11
- Present-day baryon-to-photon ratio: eta = 5.8 x 10^-10 to 6.5 x 10^-10
- Relative yield of Upsilon(b-bbar) to open bottom: Upsilon/b ~ 10^-4 (from Yao and Muller)
- Charm-to-light-quark abundance ratio at T_H: c/(u+d+s) ~ 0.0015

## Context

This work extends the search for matter genesis mechanisms beyond the conventional GUT-scale (T_G ~ 10^16 GeV) and electroweak (T_W ~ 130 GeV) baryogenesis scenarios, proposing that the QGP hadronization era near 150 MeV can satisfy the Sakharov conditions. It builds on prior studies of strangeness and charm flavor in the early Universe (Fromerth, Rafelski) and on heavy quarkonium physics in QGP (Karsch, Brambilla, Aarts, Bazavov). The mechanism is distinct from the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev) and relies on CP violation in B-meson decays as established by LHCb (Aaij). The results motivate experimental study of bottom flavor in relativistic heavy ion collisions and searches for baryon number violation involving bottomonium mesons.
