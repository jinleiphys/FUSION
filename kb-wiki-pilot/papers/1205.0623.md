# Effects of four-body breakup on 6Li elastic scattering near the Coulomb barrier

> We investigate projectile breakup effects on 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering near the Coulomb barrier with the four-body version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (four-body CDCC). This is the first application of four-body CDCC to 6Li elastic scattering. The elastic scattering is well described by the p+n+4He+209Bi four-body model. We propose a reasonable three-body model for describing the four-body scattering, clarifying four-body dynamics of the elastic scattering.

# Effects of four-body breakup on 6Li elastic scattering near the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim

The four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (four-body CDCC) reproduces measured elastic cross sections for 6Li+209Bi scattering at 29.9 and 32.8 MeV, whereas previous three-body CDCC calculations could not, but only if the single-folding potential UdSF (which excludes deuteron breakup) is used as the deuteron-target interaction in the three-body model.

## Method

The authors applied four-body CDCC to 6Li+209Bi scattering, modeling the system as a p+n+4He+209Bi four-body system. The internal Hamiltonian of 6Li was described by the p+n+4He orthogonality condition model using the Bonn-A interaction for the p-n subsystem and the KKNN interaction for the n-alpha and p-alpha subsystems. Continuum states of 6Li were discretized using the pseudostate method with Gaussian basis functions, diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian for Ipi = 1+, 2+, and 3+ states with excitation energies up to 20 MeV. Coupled-channel equations were solved for the elastic and breakup S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering was analyzed at 29.9 MeV and 32.8 MeV near the Coulomb barrier
- The four-body CDCC calculation included 65, 57, and 63 discrete states for 1+, 2+, and 3+ states respectively (including the ground state)
- Continuum states were truncated at 20 MeV excitation energy of 6Li
- Gaussian basis functions used range parameters from 0.1 fm to 12 fm in geometric series
- The neutron optical potential UnOP was fitted to n+209Bi elastic scattering data at 5 MeV, with parameters matching Koning and Delaroche global potential except aV = 0.55 fm, WV = 0 MeV, and WD = 4.0 MeV
- The d-alpha potential reproduced the 6Li ground state energy (-1.47 MeV) and the 3+ resonance state energy (0.71 MeV)

## Context

This work extends four-body CDCC, previously applied only to 6He scattering, to 6Li scattering for the first time. It addresses the failure of earlier three-body CDCC calculations by Keeley et al. (2003) which could not reproduce 6Li+209Bi elastic scattering data without normalization factors for the projectile-target potentials. The paper clarifies that deuteron breakup is suppressed in 6Li scattering near the Coulomb barrier because the alpha particle screens the deuteron from the target, enabling a simplified three-body model with a single-folding deuteron potential. This enables future application of four-body CDCC to n+6Li scattering, relevant for nuclear engineering neutron spectra calculations.
