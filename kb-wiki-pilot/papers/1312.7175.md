# Extracting electric dipole breakup cross section of one-neutron halo nuclei from breakup observables

> How to extract an electric dipole (E1) breakup cross section σ(E1) from one- neutron removal cross sections measured by using 12C and 208Pb targets, σ_(-1n)^C and σ_(-1n)^Pb, respectively, is discussed. It is shown that within about 5% error, σ(E1) can be obtained by subtracting Γσ_(-1n)^C from σ_(- 1n)^Pb, as assumed in preceding studies. However, for the reaction of weakly-bound projectiles, the scaling factor Γis found to be two times as large as that usually adopted. As a result, we obtain 13-20% smaller σ(E1) of 31Ne at 250 MeV/nucleon than extracted in a previous analysis of experimental data. By compiling the values of Γobtained for several projectiles, Γ=(2.30 +/- 0.41)\exp(- S_n)+(2.43 +/- 0.21) is obtained, where S_n is the neutron separation energy. The target mass number dependence of the nuclear parts of the one-neutron removal cross section and the elastic breakup cross section is also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the standard formula for extracting the electric dipole (E1) breakup cross section from inclusive one-neutron removal measurements holds within about 5% error for one-neutron halo nuclei at 250 MeV/nucleon, but the scaling factor Gamma used in previous analyses is about two times too small for weakly-bound projectiles, leading to 13-20% smaller sigma(E1) for 31Ne than previously reported.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with eikonal approximation (E-CDCC) to describe elastic breakup and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) to describe neutron stripping in a c+n+A three-body system. They calculated one-neutron removal cross sections for 10 projectile nuclei (including 11Be, 15C, 19C, 31Ne, and several Mg and Si isotopes) on 6 target nuclei (12C, 16O, 48Ca, 58Ni, 90Zr, and 208Pb) at 250 MeV/nucleon, using microscopic folding models with the Melbourne nucleon-nucleon g matrix and Hartree-Fock wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Total error of the E1 extraction formula for one-neutron halo nuclei: 1.9% (31Ne) to 6.7% (19C), with all errors below 8%
- Scaling factor Gamma for 31Ne: 3.86, approximately two times larger than the range of 1.7-2.6 assumed in the previous analysis by Nakamura et al.
- Resulting sigma(E1) for 31Ne: 13-20% smaller than extracted in the previous analysis
- Fitted functional form for Gamma: Gamma = (2.30 +/- 0.41)exp(-S_n) + (2.43 +/- 0.21), where S_n is the neutron separation energy in MeV
- For 11Be: Gamma = 4.12; for 15C: Gamma = 3.26; for 19C: Gamma = 4.26
- For 8B (proton halo case): E2 contribution is about 20% of E1, and nuclear-Coulomb interference is about 12%, invalidating the formula
- Nuclear elastic breakup cross section on 12C target: 12.67 mb (11Be), 4.59 mb (15C), 8.77 mb (19C), 5.58 mb (31Ne)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Nakamura et al. (2009) who first used the subtraction formula sigma(E1) = sigma_{-1n}^{Pb} - Gamma * sigma_{-1n}^{C} to extract E1 breakup cross sections for 19C and 31Ne, but without quantitative justification. The present study validates the formula for one-neutron halo nuclei at 250 MeV/nucleon while correcting the scaling factor Gamma, which was previously assumed to lie between R_Pb/(R_C) and (R_Pb+R_P)/(R_C+R_P). The results enable more accurate extraction of E1 breakup cross sections for future experiments on neutron halo nuclei, and the compiled Gamma formula provides a practical tool for analyzing data across different projectiles.
