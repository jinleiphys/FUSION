# Probing the weakly-bound neutron orbit of $^{31}$Ne with total reaction and one-neutron removal cross sections

> A candidate of a neutron-halo nucleus, $^{31}$Ne, contains a single neutron in the $pf$ shell. Within the Glauber and eikonal models, we analyze reactions used to study $^{31}$Ne. We show in a $^{30}$Ne+n model that the magnitudes of the total reaction and above all of the one-neutron removal cross sections of $^{31}$Ne on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb targets strongly depend on the orbital angular momentum of the neutron, thereby providing us with efficient ways to determine both the spin-parity and structure of the ground state of $^{31}$Ne. Besides these inclusive observables, we also calculate energy and parallel-momentum distributions for the breakup of $^{31}$Ne, and show their strong dependence upon the orbital of the valence neutron in the bound state of $^{31}$Ne.

## Key claim

The total reaction and one-neutron removal cross sections of $^{31}$Ne on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb targets strongly depend on whether the valence neutron occupies the $1p_{3/2}$ or $0f_{7/2}$ orbital, providing a way to determine the spin-parity and structure of the ground state, but only if the neutron separation energy ($S_{\rm n}=0.33$ MeV with large uncertainty) and the diffuseness parameter of the core-neutron potential are sufficiently well constrained.

## Method

The authors model $^{31}$Ne as a $^{30}$Ne core plus a weakly-bound valence neutron, and compute total reaction cross sections within the Glauber formalism using the symmetrized phase-shift function, and one-neutron removal cross sections as the difference between $^{31}$Ne and $^{30}$Ne reaction cross sections. They also perform Coulomb-corrected eikonal model calculations to obtain energy and parallel-momentum distributions for breakup, using Woods-Saxon potentials adjusted to reproduce the experimental $S_{\rm n}=0.33$ MeV for either the $1p_{3/2}$ or $0f_{7/2}$ orbital.

## Key numbers

* Neutron separation energy $S_{\rm n}$ of $^{31}$Ne: 0.33 MeV (with large uncertainty)
* Total reaction cross section $\sigma_{\rm R}(^{31}{\rm Ne})$ on $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon: 1.45 b for $1p_{3/2}$ orbit, 1.37 b for $0f_{7/2}$ orbit (difference of 87 mb)
* Increase in $\sigma_{\rm R}$ from $^{30}$Ne to $^{31}$Ne on $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon: 96 mb for $1p_{3/2}$, 26 mb for $0f_{7/2}$
* Increase in $\sigma_{\rm R}$ from $^{30}$Ne to $^{31}$Ne on $^{208}$Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon: from 4.36 b to 4.69 b for $1p_{3/2}$, from 4.33 b to 4.37 b for $0f_{7/2}$
* RMS radius of valence neutron orbit $r_{\rm n}$: about 7 fm for $1p_{3/2}$, about 4 fm for $0f_{7/2}$
* Difference in matter radius $\Delta r = r^{\rm m}_P - r^{\rm m}_c$: 0.19-0.20 fm for $1p_{3/2}$, 0.04 fm for $0f_{7/2}$
* $\sigma_{\rm R}(^{30}{\rm Ne})$ on $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon: 1.34-1.36 b depending on potential parameters

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of halo nuclei and shell inversion near the neutron dripline, particularly for Ne isotopes with $N\approx 20$ where the vanishing of the $N=20$ shell gap causes mixing of normal and intruder configurations (Refs. PR94, CNP98, UOM99, KH01, KH04, Dom06, Doo09). The paper directly responds to recent measurements of total reaction cross sections on $^{12}$C (Oht09pc) and one-neutron removal cross sections on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb (Nak09l) at RIKEN at around 230 MeV/nucleon. The calculations enable determination of whether the $^{31}$Ne ground state has spin-parity $3/2^-$ ($1p_{3/2}$) or $7/2^-$ ($0f_{7/2}$), a question previously addressed by shell model (PR94) and microscopic cluster model (Des99) calculations that predict shell inversion.
