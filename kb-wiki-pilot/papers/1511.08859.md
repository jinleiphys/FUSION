# Indirect methods in nuclear astrophysics

> We discuss recent developments in indirect methods used in nuclear astrophysics to determine the capture cross sections and subsequent rates of various stellar burning processes, when it is difficult to perform the corresponding direct measurements. We discuss in brief, the basic concepts of Asymptotic Normalization Coefficients, the Trojan Horse Method, the Coulomb Dissociation Method, (d,p), and charge-exchange reactions.

# Indirect methods in nuclear astrophysics

## Key claim
Indirect methods (ANC, Trojan Horse, Coulomb dissociation, (d,p), and charge-exchange reactions) can determine capture cross sections and reaction rates for stellar burning processes when direct measurements are impractical due to extremely small cross sections (nanobarns to picobarns) at stellar energies or short lifetimes of unstable nuclei; however, each method carries specific conditions and approximations that must be satisfied for reliable extraction of astrophysical quantities.

## Method
The Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) method extracts the amplitude of the tail of the nuclear overlap function from peripheral transfer reactions or one-nucleon breakup reactions, then uses this ANC to calculate direct capture cross sections via the relation sigma = (C^2) * w(E). The Trojan Horse Method (THM) uses a two-body-to-three-body reaction (A + x -> c + B + y) under quasi-free kinematics to extract the cross section for the binary sub-process (A + a -> c + B) at ultra-low relative energies, free from Coulomb barrier and electron screening effects. The Coulomb Dissociation (CD) method measures the breakup of a projectile in the electromagnetic field of a heavy target, relates this to photodisintegration cross sections via virtual photon numbers, and then uses detailed balance to obtain the radiative capture cross section. For (d,p) and charge-exchange reactions, the AGS formalism (for three-body scattering) or simplified distorted-wave approaches are used to extract neutron-induced reaction cross sections and Gamow-Teller transition strengths, respectively.

## Key numbers
- Stellar reaction energies are typically of order tens or hundreds of keV/u
- Cross sections for charged-particle reactions at stellar energies are in the nanobarn to picobarn range
- For the THM, the relative energy E_Aa = (m_a/(m_a+m_A))*E_A - epsilon_ay, where epsilon_ay is the binding energy of the a-y cluster in nucleus x
- For charge-exchange reactions at around 100 MeV/nucleon, the cross section at zero momentum transfer is given by dsigma/dq(q=0) = KF * N_D * |J_sigmatau|^2 * B(alpha)

## Context
This review extends the comprehensive treatment of indirect techniques in nuclear astrophysics given by Tribble et al. (2014, Rep. Prog. Phys. 77 106901) and Bertulani and Gade (2010, Physics Reports 485 195). The ANC method builds on the formalism of Xu et al. (1994, Phys. Rev. Lett. 73 2027) for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction, while the THM follows the approach of Baur (1986, Phys. Lett. B 178 135) and Spitaleri et al. (2000, Eur. Phys. J. A 7 181). The CD method is based on the work of Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel (1986, Nucl. Phys. A 458 188) and has been applied to neutron capture reactions on 8Li, 14C, and 15N. The treatment of (d,p) reactions via the AGS formalism follows Deltuva and Fonseca (2009, Phys. Rev. C 79 014606), while the simplified approach for charge-exchange reactions (Taddeucci et al., 1987, Nucl. Phys. A 469 125) is noted to be under current theoretical scrutiny. These methods enable determination of astrophysical S-factors and reaction rates for processes in the pp-chain, CNO-cycle, r-process, and s-process where direct measurements are infeasible.
