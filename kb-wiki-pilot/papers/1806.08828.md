# Astrophysical factors of ${}^{12}{\rm C}+{}^{12}{\rm C}$ fusion from Trojan horse method

> Carbon-carbon burning plays an important role in many stellar environments. Recently, using the indirect Trojan horse method A. Tumino {\em et al.} reported [Nature {\bf 557} 687 (2018)] a strong rise of the astrophysical factor for the carbon-carbon fusion at low resonance energies. In this paper, we demonstrate that this rise is the artifact of using an invalid plane-wave approximation. It is shown that the calculated renormalization factor decreases the astrophysical factor from [A. Tumino {\em et al.}, Nature {\bf 557} 687 (2018)] at the resonance energies of $E=0.8-0.9$ MeV by as much as $\approx 10^{3}$ times.

## Key claim

The strong rise of the astrophysical factor for carbon-carbon fusion at low resonance energies reported by Tumino et al. (Nature 557, 687, 2018) is an artifact of using an invalid plane-wave approximation (PWA) to analyze Trojan horse method (THM) data; when Coulomb distorted waves are properly included, the renormalization factor decreases the astrophysical factor at resonance energies of E = 0.8–0.9 MeV by as much as ≈10³ times.

## Method

The authors demonstrate that the PWA used by Tumino et al. is invalid for the THM reaction ¹²C(¹⁴N, d b)B proceeding through resonant states of ²⁴Mg*, because the outgoing deuterons are well below the Coulomb barrier in the d-²⁴Mg system (≈3 MeV). They calculate the differential cross sections (DCS) for the ¹⁴N + ¹²C → d + ²⁴Mg* transfer reaction using the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) with both pure Coulomb and Coulomb-plus-nuclear distorted waves, and compare these to the PWA DCS. Using the FRESCO code, they compute a renormalization factor R(E) defined as the ratio of the DWBA zero-range DCS at the normalization energy (2.664 MeV) to that at lower energies, which multiplies the astrophysical S*-factors from Tumino et al.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier in the d-²⁴Mg system is about 3 MeV; at the normalization interval E = 2.5–2.63 MeV, the d-²⁴Mg relative kinetic energy is 0.9–1.06 MeV, well below this barrier.
- At resonance energy E = 0.805 MeV in the ¹²C-¹²C channel, the d-²⁴Mg relative kinetic energy is 2.75 MeV, still below the Coulomb barrier.
- The renormalization factor R(E) decreases the THM astrophysical factors from Tumino et al. at resonance energies E = 0.8–0.9 MeV by as much as ≈10³ times.
- The reaction rate calculated in Tumino et al. at temperature T ∼ 2 × 10⁸ K exceeds the adopted value (Fowler, Iliadis) by a factor of 500.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the findings of Tumino et al. (Nature 557, 687, 2018), who reported a strong rise in the astrophysical S*-factor for ¹²C+¹²C fusion at low energies using the THM with a plane-wave approximation. The authors extend the generalized R-matrix approach developed by Mukhamedzhanov (2011) and show that proper inclusion of Coulomb distorted waves eliminates the anomalous rise. This correction has profound implications for stellar carbon burning rates, as the renormalized astrophysical factors are orders of magnitude lower than those claimed by Tumino et al., bringing them more in line with extrapolations from direct data (Fowler, Iliadis).
