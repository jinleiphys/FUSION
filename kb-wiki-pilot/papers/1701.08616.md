# Extracting three-body breakup observables from CDCC calculations with core excitations

> Core-excitation effects in the scattering of two-body halo nuclei have been investigated in previous works. In particular, these effects have been found to affect in a significant way the breakup cross sections of neutron-halo nuclei with a deformed core. To account for these effects, appropriate extensions of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method have been recently proposed. We aim to extend these studies to the case of breakup reactions measured under complete kinematics or semi-inclusive reactions in which only the angular or energy distribution of one of the outgoing fragments is measured. We use the standard CDCC method as well as its extended version with core excitations (XCDCC), assuming a pseudo-state basis for describing the projectile states. Two- and three-body observables are computed by projecting the discrete two-body breakup amplitudes, obtained within these reaction frameworks, onto two-body scattering states with definite relative momentum of the outgoing fragments and a definite state of the core nucleus. The presented method provides a tool to compute double and triple differential cross sections for outgoing fragments following the breakup of a two-body projectile, and might be useful to analyze breakup reactions with other deformed weakly-bound nuclei, for which core excitations are expected to play a role. We have found that, while dynamical core excitations are important for the proton target at intermediate energies, they are very small for the Zn target at energies around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim
Core excitation effects in breakup reactions of two-body halo nuclei can be computed for fully exclusive or semi-inclusive observables using an extended CDCC formalism (XCDCC), but the importance of these dynamical core excitations depends strongly on the target and energy regime: they are significant for proton targets at intermediate energies but very small for heavy targets like Zn at energies around the Coulomb barrier.

## Method
The authors use the standard continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method and its extended version with core excitations (XCDCC), employing a pseudo-state basis (Transformed Harmonic Oscillator, THO) to describe the projectile states. Two- and three-body breakup observables are computed by projecting the discrete two-body breakup amplitudes obtained from these reaction frameworks onto two-body scattering states with definite relative momentum of the outgoing fragments and a definite state of the core nucleus. The method is applied to the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be, studying breakup reactions on proton and 64Zn targets.

## Key numbers
- 11Be breakup on proton target at 63.7 MeV/nucleon and on 64Zn target at 28.7 MeV
- 11Be structure model uses Woods-Saxon central potential with radius R=2.483 fm, diffuseness a=0.65 fm, parity-dependent strength (Vc=-54.24 MeV for positive parity, Vc=-49.67 MeV for negative parity), spin-orbit strength Vso=8.5 MeV
- 10Be core assumed with permanent quadrupole deformation beta2=0.67 (delta2=1.664 fm)
- Only core ground state (0+) and first excited state (2+, Ex=3.368 MeV) included in model space
- THO basis with N=12 radial functions and valence-core orbital angular momenta l <= 5
- XCDCC coupled equations integrated up to 100 fm for total angular momenta JT <= 65
- JLM interaction range t=1.2 fm, imaginary part renormalized by factor Ni=0.8

## Context
This work extends previous studies of core-excitation effects in scattering of two-body halo nuclei (particularly 11Be) that used DWBA-based formalisms (Crespo et al. 2011, Moro et al. 2012) or the XCDCC method (de Diego et al. 2014). It builds on the fivefold fully exclusive cross sections derived for CDCC by Tostevin et al. (2001) and extends them to the XCDCC framework. The method enables calculation of double and triple differential cross sections for outgoing fragments, which is useful for analyzing breakup reactions with other deformed weakly-bound nuclei and for comparing with experimental data from exclusive breakup measurements.
