# Search of systematic behavior of breakup probability in reactions with weakly bound projectiles at energies around Coulomb barrier

> Comparing the capture cross sections calculated without the breakup effect and experimental complete fusion cross sections, the breakup was analyzed in reactions with weakly bound projectiles $^{6,7,9}$Li, $^{9,11}$Be, and $^{6,8}$He. A trend of a systematic behavior for the complete fusion suppression as a function of the target charge and bombarding energy is not achieved. The quasielastic backscattering is suggested to be an useful tool to study the behavior of the breakup probability in reactions with weakly bound projectiles.

# Search of systematic behavior of breakup probability in reactions with weakly bound projectiles at energies around Coulomb barrier

## Key claim

No systematic trend of complete fusion suppression as a function of target charge or bombarding energy could be established across the reactions studied; the authors instead propose that quasielastic backscattering measurements can serve as an indirect tool to extract breakup probabilities, circumventing the need for direct breakup measurements.

## Method

The authors used the quantum diffusion approach to calculate capture cross sections without breakup effects for reactions induced by weakly bound projectiles (6,7,9Li, 9,11Be, 6,8He) on various targets. They compared these calculated capture cross sections with experimental complete fusion cross sections to extract breakup probabilities via P_BU = 1 - sigma_fus_exp / sigma_c_th, taking into account neutron transfer and nuclear deformation effects. The relationship between quasielastic backscattering probability and breakup probability was derived from flux conservation to propose an experimental method for extracting breakup probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Complete fusion suppression factors (average normalization factors between calculated capture and experimental fusion): 0.7 (9Be+209Bi), 0.75 (9Be+208Pb), 0.9 (9Be+144Sm), 0.64 (9Be+124Sn), 0.7 (9Be+89Y), 1 (9Be+64Zn), 0.9 (9Be+27Al); 0.52 (6Li+209Bi), 0.5 (6Li+208Pb), 0.5 (6Li+198Pt), 0.42 (6Li+144Sm), 0.65 (6Li+64Zn); 0.6 (7Li+209Bi), 0.7 (7Li+197Au), 0.65 (7Li+165Ho), 0.6 (7Li+159Tb), 0.65 (7Li+64Zn), 0.75 (7Li+27Al); 0.6 (9Li+208Pb); 0.68 (6He+209Bi), 0.4 (6He+64Zn), 0.8 (6He+197Au), 0.7 (8He+197Au); 0.82 (11B+209Bi), 0.95 (11B+159Tb)

- Breakup probability P_BU values vary from 0% to 84% at energies above the Coulomb barrier across all reactions studied

- In 9Be+208Pb reaction, the fraction of fusion following breakup (sigma_fus_BU) in the experimental fusion cross section does not exceed a few percent at E_c.m. - V_b < 4 MeV, and reaches about 12-20% at E_c.m. - V_b approximately 10 MeV

- For 9Be+144Sm,208Pb,209Bi reactions, P_BU increases with target charge at E_c.m. - V_b > 3 MeV; for 6Li+144Sm,198Pt,209Bi reactions, P_BU decreases with increasing target charge at E_c.m. - V_b > 3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of complete fusion suppression in reactions with weakly bound projectiles previously reported by Canto, Gomes, Lubian, Chamon, and Crema (2009) and Gomes, Lubian, and Canto (2009), who found approximately 30% suppression for 6,7Li+208Pb,209Bi and 9Be+89Y,124Sn,208Pb,209Bi but noted the 9Be+144Sm system showed only about 15% suppression. The present study confirms the lack of systematic behavior and attributes it to possible problems with experimental data analysis, as earlier suggested by Gomes et al. (2011) from a universal fusion function perspective. The proposed quasielastic backscattering method enables future experimental studies of breakup probability without direct breakup measurements, using reaction pairs such as 6He+68Zn versus 4He+70Zn, 6He+122Sn versus 4He+124Sn, and 9Be+208Pb versus 10Be+207Pb leading to the same compound nuclei.
