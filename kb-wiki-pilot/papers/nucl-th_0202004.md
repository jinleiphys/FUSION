# Absorbing Boundary Condition Approach for Breakup Reactions of Halo Nuclei

> Application of the absorbing boundary condition is discussed to analyse breakup reactions of weakly bound nuclei. The key ingredient is an introduction of the absorbing potential outside the physical area which simulates the outgoing boundary condition approximately. The scattering problem is then recasted into the Schroedinger like equation with a source term in the interaction region and with the vanishing boundary condition at the boundary. We demonstrate usefulness of the method taking a few examples. Deuteron breakup reactions are examined comparing present results with those by the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method. We next discuss the breakup reactions of single-neutron halo nucleus, 11Be.

## Key claim

The absorbing boundary condition (ABC) approach provides a convenient and flexible description of breakup reactions for weakly bound nuclei by recasting the scattering problem into a linear inhomogeneous equation with vanishing boundary conditions, avoiding the need to construct artificial discretized continuum states as required by the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method. The trade-off for this conceptual simplicity is a heavy computational cost to calculate the wave function in real-space, requiring iterative solvers for large sparse matrices.

## Method

The authors replace the infinitesimal positive number epsilon in the standard Green's function formulation of scattering with a finite, space-dependent absorbing potential placed outside the physical interaction region, which simulates the outgoing boundary condition approximately. After partial wave expansion and radial discretization on a grid, the scattering problem becomes a linear algebraic equation with a sparse, complex symmetric coefficient matrix, solved using the Bi-CG (Conjugate Gradient) iterative method with diagonal preconditioning. The method is applied first to deuteron breakup on a Ni-58 target at 80 MeV for validation against CDCC results, then to single-neutron halo nucleus Be-11 on a C-12 target.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron on Ni-58 at E_d = 80 MeV: radial grid spacing 0.2 fm for R (projectile-target) and 0.5 fm for r (neutron-core), radial region up to 50 fm, absorbing potentials placed beyond 25 fm with strengths W_R = 50 MeV and W_r = 20 MeV, matrix dimension N_dim = 100,000 (N_R=250, N_r=100, N_J=4)
- Be-11 on C-12: Be-10-n binding energy 0.504 MeV (2s orbital), radial grid 0.2 fm for R and 0.5 fm for r, radial region up to 30 fm for R and 50 fm for r, absorbing potential placed 20 fm < R < 30 fm and 25 fm < r < 50 fm, neutron-C-12 relative angular momenta up to l=3 included, matrix size about 240,000
- For potential scattering demonstration: Be-10-C-12 at E_lab = 300 MeV, Woods-Saxon potential parameters V=123 MeV, r_V=0.75 fm, a_V=0.80 fm, W=65 MeV, r_W=0.78 fm, a_W=0.80 fm, absorber outside R=20 fm with Delta R=10 fm, grid spacing Delta r = 0.2 fm
- Absorbing potential strength condition: 20 * E^(1/2) / (Delta R * sqrt(8m)) < W_abs < (1/10) * Delta R * sqrt(8m) * E^(3/2)

## Context

This work extends the absorbing boundary condition approach originally developed for chemical reactions (Seideman and Miller, J. Chem. Phys. 1992) to nuclear breakup reactions of halo nuclei. It provides an alternative to the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method (Kamimura et al., Prog. Theor. Phys. Suppl. 1986), which requires construction of artificial discretized continuum states and delicate convergence examination. The ABC approach enables direct calculation of real-space scattering wave functions without asymptotic forms, and the authors demonstrate that the eikonal approximation becomes inaccurate for Be-11 breakup on C-12 below incident energies of 50 MeV per nucleon, substantially underestimating the breakup cross section.
