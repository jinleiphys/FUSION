# Lorentz Integral Transform for Inclusive and Exclusive Cross Sections with the Lanczos Method

> The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method is reformulated via the Lanczos algorithm both for inclusive and exclusive reactions. The new technique is tested for the total photoabsorption cross section of 3H and 4He. Due to the rapid convergence of the algorithm one has a decrease in cpu time by two orders of magnitude, but at the same time an excellent agreement with the results of a conventional LIT calculation. The present work opens up the possibility of ab initio calculations for inclusive and exclusive processes for A greater equal 6 with inclusion of complete final state interactions.

# Lorentz Integral Transform for Inclusive and Exclusive Cross Sections with the Lanczos Method

## Key claim

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method can be reformulated using the Lanczos algorithm to avoid explicit inversion of the Hamiltonian matrix, reducing CPU time by about two orders of magnitude while maintaining agreement with conventional LIT results to better than 0.1% relative difference; this opens the possibility of ab initio calculations for inclusive and exclusive processes for A ≥ 6 with complete final state interactions, provided the Lanczos vectors retain sufficient orthogonality (overlap of order 10⁻⁴ after 300 steps).

## Method

The authors replace the direct inversion of the Hamiltonian matrix required to solve for the Lorentz states (Eq. 1) with a Lanczos tridiagonalization procedure. For inclusive reactions, the LIT is expressed as a continued fraction of the Lanczos coefficients a_i and b_i obtained from the starting vector |φ₀⟩ = Ô|ψ₀⟩/√⟨ψ₀|Ô†Ô|ψ₀⟩. For exclusive reactions, the LIT is computed from sums over Lanczos basis states of matrix elements x_{0i} that are recursively calculated from the continued fraction. The method is tested on total photoabsorption cross sections of ³H and ⁴He using the dipole approximation, the semirealistic MTI-III NN potential, and an expansion on the hyperspherical harmonics basis via the EIHH approach.

## Key numbers

- CPU time reduction: two orders of magnitude compared to conventional LIT calculation
- Relative difference between Lanczos and conventional LIT results: far below 0.1% for ³H (T=1/2 channel)
- Lanczos steps required for convergence: 200–300 coefficients sufficient for the ⁴He test case with 1372 basis states
- Relative error with 150 Lanczos coefficients: smaller than 0.5%
- Orthogonality measure: overlap ⟨φ₀|φ₃₀₀⟩ ≈ 1.1 × 10⁻⁴ after 300 Lanczos steps
- Lorentz width parameter used in tests: σ_I = 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the LIT method originally developed by Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (Phys. Lett. B 338, 1994) for inclusive reactions and later applied to exclusive processes (La Piana and Leidemann, Nucl. Phys. A677, 2000). Previous LIT calculations for A ≥ 3 used hyperspherical harmonics expansions requiring direct matrix inversion, which limited the number of basis states. The Lanczos adaptation is inspired by condensed matter applications (Dagotto, Rev. Mod. Phys. 66, 1994; Hallberg, Phys. Rev. B 52, 1995). The new method enabled the first microscopic calculation of the total photoabsorption cross section of A=6 nuclei with complete final state interactions (Bacca et al., nucl-th/0112067).
