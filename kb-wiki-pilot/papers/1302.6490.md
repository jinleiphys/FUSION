# Breakup of a nucleus with two weakly bound neutrons on a proton target: Single-scattering approximation

> Three- and four-cluster breakup reactions in the ${}^{16}$C-proton scattering are studied using three-body core-neutron-neutron model for ${}^{16}$C. Single-scattering approximation (SSA) of four-particle equations for transition operators is used to calculate three- and four-cluster breakup amplitudes at 200 and 300 MeV/nucleon energy near proton-neutron ($pn$) quasi free scattering (QFS) conditions. The differential cross section is sharply peaked at $pn$ QFS point and decreases rapidly whenever kinematical conditions deviate from $pn$ QFS. The accuracy of the SSA for the three-cluster breakup is estimated from a three-body model and is found to be as good as 6\% at higher reaction energies in suitable angular regimes. Furthermore, under an additional approximation the three-cluster breakup amplitude factorizes into the $pn$ transition operator and the overlap integral of two- and three-particle bound states. That approximation usually reduces the cross section, in some cases even up to 10\%.

## Key claim

The single-scattering approximation (SSA) for four-particle breakup amplitudes is shown to be accurate to about 6% for three-cluster breakup of \(^{16}\)C on a proton target at 300 MeV/nucleon, but only in suitable angular regimes where the relative energies between the final-state clusters are sufficiently large; the approximation fails when either the \(pB\) or \(nB\) relative energy is not large enough, with \(E_{pB}\) being more decisive, possibly due to Coulomb effects.

## Method

The four-particle scattering problem is formulated using Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for transition operators, and the single-scattering approximation (SSA) is obtained by keeping only first-order terms in two-particle transition operators. The three-cluster breakup amplitude is calculated for \(p+^{16}\text{C} \to p+n+^{15}\text{C}\) using a core-neutron-neutron model for \(^{16}\text{C}\), with CD Bonn potentials for \(pn\) and \(nn\) interactions, Woods-Saxon \(nA\) potentials, and a three-body force adjusted to reproduce the \(^{16}\text{C}\) ground state binding energy. The accuracy of the SSA is estimated by comparing with full three-body Faddeev-type calculations in an effective three-body model where the composite cluster \(^{15}\text{C}\) is treated as a single inert particle.

## Key numbers

- SSA accuracy for three-cluster breakup: as good as 6% at 300 MeV/nucleon in suitable angular regimes (e.g., \(\Theta_p = 45^\circ\) and \(60^\circ\))
- SSA accuracy degrades to about 15% at 200 MeV/nucleon at the QFS peak
- Overlap integral approximation (OIA) underestimates SSA-4b results by up to 9% at 300 MeV/nucleon and up to 11% at 200 MeV/nucleon
- The \(^{16}\text{C}\) ground state binding energy used: \(\epsilon_\rho = -5.468\) MeV
- The \(^{15}\text{C}\) neutron separation energy: \(\epsilon_B = -1.218\) MeV
- The \(^{14}\text{C}\) first excited state lies at 6.093 MeV above ground state
- The \(pn\) relative energy at QFS peaks is around 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the single-scattering approximation approach previously applied to three-body \(p+(An)\) breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei (e.g., \(^{11}\text{Be}\) at 200 MeV/nucleon by Crespo et al., 2008) to the four-particle system of a two-neutron halo nucleus (\(^{16}\text{C}\)) on a proton target. It provides a computationally tractable alternative to full four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations and four-body CDCC methods, which face technical difficulties near \(pn\) quasi-free scattering kinematics. The results enable calculation of semi-inclusive observables for four-cluster breakup reactions at energies relevant for present and future experiments, though no experimental data currently exist for comparison.
