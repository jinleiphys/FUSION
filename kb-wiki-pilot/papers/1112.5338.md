# Testing the continuum discretized coupled channel method for deuteron induced reactions

> The Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method is a well established theory for direct nuclear reactions which includes breakup to all orders. Alternatively, the 3-body problem can be solved exactly within the Faddeev formalism which explicitly includes breakup and transfer channels to all orders. With the aim to understand how CDCC compares with the exact 3-body Faddeev formulation, we study deuteron induced reactions on: i) $^{10}$Be at $E_{\rm d}= 21.4, 40.9 \; {\rm and} \; 71$ MeV; ii) $^{12}$C at $E_{\rm d} = 12 \; {\rm and} \; 56$ MeV; and iii) $^{48}$Ca at $E_{\rm d} = 56$ MeV. We calculate elastic, transfer and breakup cross sections. Overall, the discrepancies found for elastic scattering are small with the exception of very backward angles. For transfer cross sections at low energy $\sim$10 MeV/u, CDCC is in good agreement with the Faddeev-type results and the discrepancy increases with beam energy. On the contrary, breakup observables obtained with CDCC are in good agreement with Faddeev-type results for all but the lower energies considered here.

## Key claim

The Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method provides a good approximation to the exact three-body Faddeev-AGS solution for deuteron-induced elastic, transfer, and breakup reactions, but its accuracy depends strongly on beam energy and observable: transfer cross sections agree well at low energies (around 10 MeV/u) but discrepancies increase with beam energy, while breakup observables agree well at higher energies but CDCC breaks down at low energies.

## Method

The authors compared CDCC calculations with exact Faddeev-AGS (FAGS) solutions for deuteron-induced reactions on 10Be, 12C, and 48Ca targets at various beam energies, using the same three-body Hamiltonian with a Gaussian n-p interaction and CH89 nucleon-nucleus optical potentials. Elastic, transfer (d,p), and breakup (d,pn) cross sections were computed, with spins neglected and Coulomb interactions omitted for breakup comparisons due to convergence difficulties in the Faddeev-AGS method.

## Key numbers

- 10Be(d,p)11Be at Ed = 21.4 MeV: CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections agree within 3 ± 1% at the peak
- 10Be(d,p)11Be at Ed = 71 MeV: CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections disagree by -53 ± 47% at the peak
- 12C(d,p)13C at Ed = 12 MeV: CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections agree within 6 ± 3% at the peak
- 12C(d,p)13C at Ed = 56 MeV: CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections disagree by -21 ± 1% at the peak
- 48Ca(d,p)49Ca at Ed = 56 MeV: CDCC and FAGS1 transfer cross sections disagree by 39 ± 14% at the peak
- Breakup calculations for 10Be at Ed = 21.4 MeV and 12C at Ed = 12 MeV show large discrepancies between CDCC and FAGS, while agreement improves at higher energies (40.9, 71, and 56 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons between CDCC and Faddeev methods (Ref. nunes2) that found good agreement for elastic and breakup on 12C and 58Ni but disagreement for 11Be on protons, and complements studies of the finite-range adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) against Faddeev-AGS (Refs. nunes11a, nunes11b). The results establish the range of validity for CDCC in deuteron-induced reactions and show that ADWA performs comparably to or better than CDCC for low-energy l=0 transfer, while CDCC breakup calculations require careful convergence testing at low beam energies.
