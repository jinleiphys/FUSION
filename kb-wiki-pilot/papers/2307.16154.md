# Testing the validity of the surface approximation for reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei with a fully quantum-mechanical model

> We examine the validity of surface approximation for breakup reactions using a fully quantum-mechanical model proposed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV). Analogous to the semi-classical picture, we introduce radial cut-offs to scattering waves in the IAV framework, which we refer to as IAV-cut. Systematic calculations are conducted for nonelastic breakup reactions induced by $^6$Li and deuterons at various incident energies. A comparison between the results obtained from IAV and IAV-cut is performed. The excellent agreement observed between IAV and IAV-cut in $^{6}$Li induced reactions, regardless of incident energy and target nuclei, signifies their insensitivity to the inner part of the scattering wave function, thus providing validation for the semi-classical picture. However, for deuteron induced breakup reactions, the IAV-cut results exhibit a suppression in the cross section, suggesting a strong dependence on the interior wave functions. This suppression is further enhanced as the incident energy increases.

## Key claim

The surface approximation (relying only on the asymptotic part of scattering wave functions) is valid for nonelastic breakup reactions induced by $^6$Li across various incident energies and target nuclei, but fails for deuteron-induced breakup reactions, where the cross section is suppressed when radial cut-offs are applied, indicating a strong dependence on interior wave functions.

## Method

The authors introduced radial cut-offs to the scattering wave functions in both the entrance and exit channels within the fully quantum-mechanical Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) framework, calling this approach IAV-cut. They performed systematic calculations of nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross sections for reactions induced by $^6$Li (treated as $\alpha$+$d$ cluster) and deuterons at various incident energies, comparing the IAV and IAV-cut results by varying the cut-off radius.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb($^6$Li, $\alpha X$) at $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=100$ MeV, cut-off radii of 4 fm, 6 fm, and 10 fm were tested; even with a 10 fm cut-off, the difference from the full IAV calculation was smaller than typical experimental uncertainty
- The nominal Coulomb barrier for the $^6$Li+$^{208}$Pb system is approximately 30.1 MeV
- For $^{208}$Pb($^6$Li, $\alpha X$) at 100 MeV, the model space required partial waves $l\le90$ for $^6$Li+$^{208}$Pb and $\alpha$+$^{210}$Bi$^*$ relative motion, and $l\le40$ for the $d$+$^{208}$Pb channel
- For $^{208}$Pb($d,p X$) at $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=70$ MeV, the model space required partial waves $l\le38$ for $d$+$^{208}$Pb and $p$+$^{209}$Pb$^*$ relative motion, and $l\le18$ for the $n$+$^{208}$Pb channel
- The radius parameter of the imaginary part of the optical potential between $^6$Li and $^{208}$Pb is $R_0=9.08$ fm

## Context

This work tests the surface approximation that underlies semi-classical models such as the Hussein-McVoy (HM) model and the transfer to continuum (TC) model, which rely on asymptotic properties of scattering wave functions. The fully quantum-mechanical IAV model, which makes no trajectory assumptions, provides a rigorous benchmark. The results validate the surface approximation for heavy-ion induced knockout reactions with $^6$Li but reveal its breakdown for deuteron-induced reactions, motivating further study of the interior sensitivity in light-projectile breakup.
