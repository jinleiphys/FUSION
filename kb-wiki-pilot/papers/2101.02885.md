# A unified description of the structure and electromagnetic breakup of $^{\bf 11}$Be

> We study both the static properties of $^{11}$Be and its reaction dynamics during electromagnetic breakup under a unified framework. A many-body approach - the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) is used to describe the structure of the neutron-halo nucleus, $^{11}$Be. The same AMD wave function is then adapted as an input to the fully quantum theory of Coulomb breakup under the aegis of the finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory. The calculated observables are also compared with those obtained with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential model wave function. The experimental core-valence neutron relative energy spectrum and dipole response along with other observables are well described by our calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified description of both the static properties and the electromagnetic breakup reaction dynamics of the neutron-halo nucleus 11Be can be achieved by combining antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) structure calculations with the finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) reaction theory, but only after the AMD wave function is supplemented with an AMD plus resonating group method (AMD+RGM) treatment and a tail correction to properly describe the halo asymptotics and reproduce the experimental one-neutron separation energy.

## Method

The structure of 11Be was calculated using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, including parity projection before variation and generator coordinate method (GCM) superposition over quadrupole deformation. To overcome the insufficient asymptotic description of the halo neutron wave function inherent to AMD, the authors introduced an AMD plus resonating group method (AMD+RGM) approach that superposes 338 basis wave functions of the 10Be+n system placed on a grid, and applied a tail correction to match the experimental one-neutron separation energy. The resulting valence neutron overlap amplitude was then used as input to the fully quantum mechanical finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory to calculate Coulomb breakup observables of 11Be on a 208Pb target, and the results were compared with those obtained using a phenomenological Woods-Saxon (WS) potential wave function.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy of 11Be: AMD gives -0.22 MeV (unbound), AMD+RGM gives 0.48 MeV, experimental value is 0.50 MeV.
* Root mean square matter radius of 11Be: AMD gives 2.59 fm, AMD+RGM gives 2.71 fm, experimental value is 2.73 ± 0.05 fm.
* Root mean square charge radius of 11Be: AMD gives 2.54 fm, AMD+RGM gives 2.55 fm, experimental value is 2.463 ± 0.015 fm.
* Spectroscopic factor for the 10Be(0+1) ⊗ s1/2 channel: AMD gives 0.46, AMD+RGM gives 0.82, experimental values range from 0.72(4) to 1.0(2).
* Spectroscopic factor for the 10Be(2+1) ⊗ d3/2 channel: AMD gives 0.38, AMD+RGM gives 0.18, experimental value is 0.18.
* Root mean square matter radius of 11Be from cluster model using WS wave function: 2.79 fm; using AMD wave function: 2.78 fm; experimental: 2.73 ± 0.05 fm.
* Root mean square charge radius of 11Be from cluster model using WS wave function: 2.35 fm; using AMD wave function: 2.35 fm; experimental: 2.463 ± 0.015 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of halo nuclei by providing a unified microscopic description of both structure and reaction dynamics for 11Be, combining AMD structure calculations with FRDWBA reaction theory. It addresses the known deficiency of AMD in describing the asymptotic behavior of halo wave functions by introducing the AMD+RGM method, which has previously been used for 31Ne. The results are compared with phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential model calculations and with experimental data from Nakamura et al. on the dipole response and relative energy spectrum of 11Be, and the calculated spectroscopic factors are consistent with values from Aumann, Zwieglinski, and Fukuda. This unified framework enables consistent calculations of both static properties (radii, separation energy) and reaction observables (triple differential cross sections, neutron energy distributions, parallel momentum distributions, relative energy spectra, and dipole response) within a single theoretical approach.
