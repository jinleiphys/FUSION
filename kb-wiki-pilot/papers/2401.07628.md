# Fusion of $^{7}$Li with $^{205}$Tl at near barrier energies

> The complete and incomplete fusion cross sections for the $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl reaction were measured at near barrier energies by online characteristic $γ$ ray detection technique. The complete fusion (CF) cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier were found to be suppressed by $\sim$ 26 \% compared to the coupled channel calculations. Reduced fusion cross sections for the present system at energies normalised to the Coulomb barrier were also found to be systematically lower than those with strongly bound projectiles forming a similar compound nucleus. The suppression observed in CF cross sections is found to be commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections. In the ICF cross sections, t capture is found to be dominant than $α$ capture at all the measured energies. The systematic study of available CF, ICF and total fusion (TF) data with $^7$Li projectile is performed.

## Key claim
Complete fusion (CF) cross sections for the $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl system at energies above the Coulomb barrier are suppressed by approximately 26% compared to coupled channel calculations, and this suppression is commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections, confirming that the suppression is target-independent for $^{7}$Li projectiles.

## Method
Excitation functions for complete and incomplete fusion were measured using the online characteristic gamma-ray detection technique with nine Compton-suppressed HPGe Clover detectors from the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) at the BARC-TIFR Pelletron LINAC Facility. Beam energies from 25 to 40 MeV were used on a $^{205}$Tl target of 1 mg/cm$^2$ thickness with carbon backing, and cross sections for evaporation residues following CF ($^{208,209}$Po) and ICF ($^{207,208}$Bi from alpha-capture, $^{206,207}$Pb from triton-capture) were extracted from gamma-ray yields normalized to Rutherford scattering in monitor detectors. Coupled channel calculations using CCFULL and statistical model calculations using PACE were performed for comparison.

## Key numbers
- CF cross sections suppressed by 26 $\pm$ 4% compared to coupled channel calculations at above-barrier energies
- Measured CF cross sections range from 0.50 $\pm$ 0.18 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 24.0 MeV to 729 $\pm$ 67 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 38.6 MeV
- Coulomb barrier height V$_b$ = 29.4 MeV, barrier radius R$_b$ = 11.2 fm, curvature $\hbar\omega$ = 4.46 MeV for $^{7}$Li+$^{205}$Tl system
- Universal fusion function (UFF) analysis shows CF functions are approximately 30% less than UFF, t-capture data fit UFF multiplied by 0.26, alpha-capture data fit UFF multiplied by 0.08, and total ICF fits UFF multiplied by 0.34
- Total t-capture cross sections are significantly larger than alpha-capture cross sections at all measured energies

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of fusion suppression with weakly bound $^{7}$Li projectiles on medium and heavy mass targets, previously established for systems such as $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn (Parkar et al.), $^{7}$Li+$^{159}$Tb (Brodsky et al.), $^{7}$Li+$^{197}$Au (Palshetkar et al.), $^{7}$Li+$^{198}$Pt (Shrivastava et al.), and $^{7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi (Dasgupta et al.). The present measurement for $^{205}$Tl fills a gap in simultaneous CF and ICF data and confirms the target-independent nature of CF suppression. The comparison with strongly bound projectiles $^{12}$C+$^{198}$Pt and $^{18}$O+$^{192}$Os forming similar compound nuclei demonstrates systematically lower CF cross sections for the weakly bound projectile. The comprehensive systematic of CF, ICF, and TF data with $^{7}$Li projectile presented here enables further understanding of the role of cluster transfer and breakup in fusion suppression.
