# Three-Body Model Analysis of Subbarrier alpha Transfer Reaction

> Subbarrier alpha transfer reaction 13C(6Li,d)17O(6.356 MeV, 1/2+) at 3.6 MeV is analyzed with a alpha + d + 13C three-body model, and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for alpha + 13C --> 17O(6.356 MeV, 1/2+), which essentially determines the reaction rate of 13C(alpha,n)16O, is extracted. Breakup effects of 6Li in the initial channel and those of 17O in the final channel are investigated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). The former is found to have a large back-coupling to the elastic channel, while the latter turns out significantly small. The transfer cross section calculated with Born approximation to the transition operator, including breakup states of 6Li, gives (C_{alpha 13C}{17O*})^2 =1.03 \pm 0.29 fm^{-1}. This result is consistent with the value obtained by the previous DWBA calculation.

## Key claim

The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the \(\alpha + ^{13}\text{C} \rightarrow ^{17}\text{O}(6.356\text{ MeV}, 1/2^+)\) system is extracted from the subbarrier transfer reaction \(^{13}\text{C}(^{6}\text{Li},d)^{17}\text{O}^*\) at 3.6 MeV using a three-body model, with the result \((C_{\alpha ^{13}\text{C}}^{^{17}\text{O}^*})^2 = 1.03 \pm 0.29 \text{ fm}^{-1}\). The abstract states consistency with a prior DWBA calculation, but the full text reveals that this agreement occurs because the back-coupling effect of \(^{6}\text{Li}\) breakup is large and was implicitly included in the DWBA via an optical potential, while the breakup transfer process itself is negligible.

## Method

The reaction was analyzed with an \(\alpha + d + ^{13}\text{C}\) three-body model using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) to treat breakup effects of \(^{6}\text{Li}\) in the initial channel and \(^{17}\text{O}\) in the final channel. The transition matrix was calculated with Born approximation to the transition operator \(V_{\text{tr}} = V_{\alpha d}^{(\text{N})}(r)\), using zero-range approximation with finite-range correction. The final-state wave function was approximated by a single-channel distorted wave, as \(^{17}\text{O}\) breakup effects were found to be at most 5%.

## Key numbers

- \((C_{\alpha ^{13}\text{C}}^{^{17}\text{O}^*})^2 = 1.03 \pm 0.25 \text{ (theor)} \pm 0.15 \text{ (expt)} \text{ fm}^{-1}\) extracted from the three-body CDCC analysis of \(^{13}\text{C}(^{6}\text{Li},d)^{17}\text{O}^*\) at 3.6 MeV.
- Previous DWBA result: \((C_{\alpha ^{13}\text{C}}^{^{17}\text{O}^*})^2 = 0.89 \pm 0.23 \text{ fm}^{-1}\) from Ref. [Eric1].
- The binding energy of \(^{17}\text{O}(6.356\text{ MeV}, 1/2^+)\) from the \(\alpha+^{13}\text{C}\) threshold is only 3 keV.
- The ground state of \(^{6}\text{Li}\) is 1.47 MeV below the \(\alpha+d\) threshold.
- Breakup effects in the final channel change the \(d\)-\(^{17}\text{O}^*\) elastic cross section by at most 5% for \(\theta \gtrsim 60^\circ\); the absolute value of the \(J=0\) wave function at \(R_{d\text{O}}=10\) fm changes from 0.982 to 0.956 when breakup is included.

## Context

This work extends the prior DWBA analysis of the \(^{13}\text{C}(^{6}\text{Li},d)^{17}\text{O}^*\) reaction by Johnson and collaborators (Ref. [Eric1]), which extracted an ANC of \(0.89 \pm 0.23 \text{ fm}^{-1}\) but neglected breakup effects of \(^{6}\text{Li}\) and \(^{17}\text{O}\). The present CDCC analysis shows that \(^{6}\text{Li}\) breakup has a large back-coupling to the elastic channel, while \(^{17}\text{O}\) breakup is negligible; the resulting ANC is consistent with the DWBA value within uncertainties. The method enables more reliable extraction of ANCs for \(\alpha\)-induced reactions of astrophysical interest, such as the \(^{13}\text{C}(\alpha,n)^{16}\text{O}\) reaction important for the s-process in AGB stars. The authors suggest that the three-body CDCC approach should be applied systematically to other subbarrier \(\alpha\) transfer reactions, and note that four-body CDCC based on a \(p+n+\alpha+^{13}\text{C}\) model would be an interesting future extension.
