# Physics case for quarkonium studies at the Electron Ion Collider

> The physics case for quarkonium-production studies accessible at the US Electron Ion Collider is described.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Electron Ion Collider (EIC) will provide unique opportunities for quarkonium studies across multiple production regimes (photoproduction, leptoproduction, and fully integrated Q² measurements), but the precision of these studies depends critically on detector configuration choices, particularly the inclusion of a muon detector, and on proper treatment of QED radiative corrections, which fundamentally limit the accuracy of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) measurements in certain kinematic regimes.

## Method

The paper reviews the planned EIC accelerator and detector systems, including the baseline ePIC detector design, and evaluates their capabilities for reconstructing vector quarkonia (J/ψ, Υ(nS)) via di-electron and di-muon decay channels. It presents a hybrid QCD+QED factorisation formalism using lepton distribution functions (LDFs) and lepton fragmentation functions (LFFs) to resum logarithmically-enhanced QED radiative corrections in SIDIS, and discusses intrinsic charm models (Brodsky et al., meson-cloud, sea-like) as probes of nonperturbative charm content via associated J/psi + charm production in photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- Centre-of-mass energy range: 28–141 GeV for electron-proton collisions, up to 89 GeV/nucleon for electron-ion collisions
- Expected instantaneous luminosity: 10³³–10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹ for electron-proton, ~10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹ for electron-ion
- Integrated luminosity: 10–100 fb⁻¹ per year
- Beam polarisation: ~70% for electron, proton, and ³He beams
- Pseudorapidity coverage: -4 < η < 4
- Magnetic field: 1.7 T (ePIC baseline)
- Particle identification momentum range: up to 50 GeV
- Intrinsic charm probability extracted from earlier analyses: 0.1%–1% (P⁰_ic5)
- LHCb result: intrinsic charm component consistent with up to 1% at large Z rapidity

## Context

This work extends the physics case for quarkonium studies from the EIC White Paper (Accardi et al., 2012), the NAS report (NAP25171), and the EIC Yellow Report (AbdulKhalek et al., 2021) by providing detailed detector requirements and systematic considerations. It builds on prior QED correction studies (Liu et al., 2021) showing that radiative effects limit SIDIS accuracy, particularly for TMD factorisation, and on intrinsic charm formulations from Brodsky et al. (1980, 1981) and lattice calculations (Sufian et al., 2020). The paper enables future experimental designs for a second EIC detector with muon identification and provides a framework for precision quarkonium measurements that can test nonperturbative QCD, TMD factorisation, and intrinsic heavy-quark content.
