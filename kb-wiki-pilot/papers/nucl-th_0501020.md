# Solar Hydrogen Burning and Neutrinos

> We summarize the current status of laboratory measurements of nuclear cross sections of the pp chain and CN cycle. We discuss the connections between such measurements, predictions of solar neutrino fluxes, and the conclusion that solar neutrinos oscillate before reaching earth.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the current status of laboratory measurements of nuclear cross sections for the pp chain and CN cycle, emphasizing that while solar neutrino oscillations are now established, the reliability of this conclusion depends critically on the quality of our understanding of the nuclear reaction rates governing these cycles, with the \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction remaining the most important nuclear uncertainty in the solar neutrino problem.

## Method

The authors review laboratory measurements of nuclear cross sections for each reaction in the proton-proton chain and CN cycle, including direct capture experiments, residual activity measurements, and underground accelerator studies (LUNA). They describe the standard reaction rate formalism using the astrophysical S-factor, which removes the steep energy dependence of the Coulomb barrier, and discuss how laboratory data taken at higher energies must be extrapolated to the solar Gamow peak energies (typically 10–30 keV) where cross sections are too small to measure directly for most reactions. The paper also addresses electron screening corrections needed to relate laboratory measurements to bare-nucleus cross sections relevant for the solar plasma.

## Key numbers

- \(S_{pp}(0) = (3.92 \pm 0.08) \times 10^{-25}\) MeV·b for the pp reaction, determined from effective field theory and tritium beta decay constraints
- \(S(0) = 0.216 \pm 0.006\) eV·b for the d(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{3}\)He reaction, measured down to \(E_{cm} = 2.5\) keV by LUNA
- \(S(0) = 5.32 \pm 0.08\) MeV·b for the \(^{3}\text{He}(^{3}\text{He},2p)^{4}\text{He}\) reaction, directly measured across the solar Gamow peak (21 ± 5 keV) by LUNA, with cross section \(\sigma = 20\) fb at 16 keV
- \(S_{34}(0) = 0.53 \pm 0.05\) keV·b for the \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction, with a systematic discrepancy between direct-capture measurements (0.507 ± 0.016 keV·b) and residual activity measurements (0.572 ± 0.026 keV·b)
- \(S(0) = 0.53 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.01\) keV·b from a recent series of \(^{7}\text{Be}\)-activity measurements
- Cross sections at the solar Gamow peak: \(^{3}\text{He}(^{3}\text{He},2p)^{4}\text{He}\) ~ 1.5 pb; \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) ~ 3 × 10\(^{-5}\) pb; \(^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}\) ~ 1.5 × 10\(^{-3}\) pb; \(^{14}\text{N}(p,\gamma)^{15}\text{O}\) ~ 2.2 × 10\(^{-7}\) pb
- Solar core temperature \(T_c \sim 1.5 \times 10^7\) K, central density \(\rho_c \sim 153\) g/cm\(^3\)
- The pp chain produces over 98% of solar energy, CN cycle contributes the remaining 2%
- The \(^{8}\text{B}\) neutrino flux has a doubling period of about 0.9 billion years in the standard solar model

## Context

This review extends the work of the 1997 INT working group (Ref. [29]) and earlier compilations by Fowler et al. (Ref. [13]) and Angulo et al. (Ref. [14]), providing an updated status of laboratory measurements for solar hydrogen burning reactions. The paper connects these nuclear physics measurements to solar neutrino flux predictions and the discovery of neutrino oscillations, which required mixing of massive neutrinos beyond the standard electroweak model. The results enable improved standard solar model calculations and help resolve the remaining nuclear uncertainties, particularly the \(^{3}\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}\text{Be}\) reaction rate, which is the subject of at least four new experimental studies including direct-capture, activity, and Coulomb-dissociation approaches.
