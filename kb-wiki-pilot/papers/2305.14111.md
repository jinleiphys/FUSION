# Advancing the IAV Model with CDCC Wave Functions for Realistic Descriptions of Two-Body Projectile Breakup

> Inclusive breakup is an important reaction mechanism of reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei. The Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV) model is widely used to analyze inclusive breakup processes and is based on a Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA). However, the validity of the DWBA form for inclusive breakup requires further exploration. In this study, we present a derivation of the IAV model, using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) wave function, and apply it to the $d+^{93}$Nb reaction. We examine the differences between the CDCC-IAV and DWBA-IAV models by artificially modifying the binding energy of the deuteron using two distinct types of optical potential. Our findings indicate that the CDCC method potentially provides a more fundamental description of the $d$+$A$ interaction and may provide a more realistic description of the interior part of the wave function. This study offers significant potential in increasing the precision and dependability of weakly bound nuclei inclusive breakup process estimation within a fully quantum mechanical model.

## Key claim

The authors derive a version of the Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV) model that uses continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) wave functions instead of the standard Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) form, and they find that the CDCC approach may provide a more realistic description of the interior part of the wave function for inclusive breakup reactions, though the improvement is conditional on the binding energy and optical potential used.

## Method

The authors present a rigorous derivation of the IAV model using CDCC wave functions to describe the three-body scattering state, replacing the DWBA distorted wave typically used in the standard IAV formulation. They apply this CDCC-IAV formalism to the d+^93Nb reaction and compare the nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross sections obtained with the full CDCC wave function against those from the DWBA-IAV approach, artificially modifying the deuteron binding energy and using two distinct types of optical potentials to examine the differences.

## Key numbers

- The study applies the CDCC-IAV and DWBA-IAV models to the d+^93Nb reaction system
- The deuteron binding energy is artificially modified to examine differences between the two approaches
- Two distinct types of optical potential are used to compare the CDCC-IAV and DWBA-IAV results
- No specific numerical cross-section values or precision figures are reported in the provided text

## Context

This work extends the IAV model originally proposed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent in the 1980s, which was based on DWBA, by incorporating a three-body description of the entrance channel via CDCC wave functions as previously suggested by Austern et al. (1987). The study builds on the authors' prior work (Jin et al., 2019) where the first implementation of CDCC wave functions to the IAV model was proposed, and it addresses the ongoing debate about whether incomplete fusion (ICF) proceeds via a one-step or two-step mechanism, with implications for models by Ferreira et al. (2023), Diaz et al. (2007), Marta et al. (2014), and Kolinger et al. (2018).
