# Ab Initio Theory of Light-ion Reactions

> The exact treatment of nuclei starting from the constituent nucleons and the fundamental interactions among them has been a long-standing goal in nuclear physics. Above all nuclear scattering and reactions, which require the solution of the many-body quantum-mechanical problem in the continuum, represent a theoretical and computational challenge for ab initio approaches. After a brief overview of the field, we present a new ab initio many-body approach capable of describing simultaneously both bound and scattering states in light nuclei. By combining the resonating-group method with the ab initio no-core shell model, we complement a microscopic cluster technique with the use of realistic interactions and a microscopic and consistent description of the clusters. We show results for neutron and proton scattering on light nuclei, including p-7Be and n-8He. We also highlight the first results of the d-3He and d-3H fusion calculations obtained within this approach.

# Ab Initio Theory of Light-ion Reactions

## Key claim
The authors present a new ab initio many-body approach (NCSM/RGM) that can describe both bound and scattering states in light nuclei simultaneously, but the results for nucleon-4He phase shifts show that the 2P3/2 resonance is positioned at higher energy than experiment and the corresponding phase shifts are underestimated, largely due to the neglect of three-nucleon interactions in the calculations.

## Method
The approach combines the resonating-group method (RGM) with the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM), using binary-cluster channel states built from NCSM eigenstates of the constituent clusters. The many-body wave function is expanded in terms of these channel states with unknown relative-motion wave functions, which are determined by solving non-local integro-differential coupled-channel equations containing Hamiltonian and norm kernels. The calculations employ similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials, with cluster wave functions and localized kernel parts computed in a harmonic oscillator basis truncated by Nmax excitations.

## Key numbers
- n-4He phase shifts converged at Nmax=17 for S- and P-waves using SRG-N3LO NN potential with Lambda=2.02 fm-1
- Integrated elastic n-3H cross section at 14 MeV: 0.94 barn from scaled NCSM/RGM calculation
- p-7Be scattering: calculated 2+ resonance (corresponding to 8B ground state) lies at about 200 keV above threshold (experiment: bound by 137 keV); S-wave scattering length a02 = -10.2 fm (experimental: -7(3) fm)
- n-8He scattering: 1/2- P-wave resonance at about 1.6 MeV (experimental: about 1.3 MeV); S-wave scattering length a0 ~ -2 fm
- 3He(d,p)4He S-factor calculations show dramatic improvement when deuteron virtual breakup (via excited deuterium pseudo-states) is included

## Context
This work extends ab initio methods beyond the A=4 systems where Faddeev, Faddeev-Yakubovski, hyperspherical harmonics, and Lorentz integral transform methods have been applied, filling the gap to A=17 systems. It builds on the Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) approach for n-alpha scattering (Nollett et al. 2007) and the traditional RGM with semi-realistic interactions. The approach enables first-principles calculations of reactions important for nuclear astrophysics (e.g., 7Be(p,gamma)8B) and fusion energy (d-3H and d-3He reactions), with prospects for including three-nucleon forces and three-body continuum effects.
