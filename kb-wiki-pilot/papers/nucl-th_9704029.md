# The Delta(1232)-nucleon interaction in the 2H(p,n) charge exchange reaction

> The 2H(p,n) charge exchange reaction at T_p=790 MeV is used to study the Delta(1232)-nucleon interaction in the Delta resonance excitation energy region. For the Delta-N potential, a meson exchange model is adopted where pi, rho, omega, and sigma meson exchanges are taken into account. The deuteron disintegration below and above pion threshold is calculated using a coupled channel approach. Various observables, such as the inclusive cross section, the quasifree Delta decay, the coherent pion production, and the two-nucleon breakup are considered. It is shown that these observables are influenced by the dynamical treatment of the Delta degrees of freedom. Of special interest is the coherent pion decay of the Delta resonance which is studied by means of the exclusive reaction 2H(p,n pi+)2H. Both the peak energy and the magnitude of the coherent pion production cross section depend very sensitively on the strength of the Delta-N potential. The coherent pions have a peak energy of 300 MeV and a strongly forward peaked angular distribution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the coherent pion production cross section in the exclusive reaction 2H(p,nπ+)2H depends "quite sensitively" on the strength of the Delta-N potential, but this sensitivity is demonstrated only within a specific meson-exchange model where the Delta is treated as a quasi-particle with an intrinsic width, and where the Delta-N channel is effectively decoupled from the NN channel.

## Method

The authors used a coupled channel approach in configuration space to describe the Delta-N system, solving the equations with the Lanczos method. The Delta-N potential was constructed from a meson exchange model including π, ρ, ω, and σ meson exchanges, with coupling constants determined from the Chew-Low relation and the static quark model. The inclusive cross section was decomposed into partial cross sections for two-nucleon breakup, coherent pion production, and quasifree Delta decay, with the deuteron wave function treated nonrelativistically in the Breit frame and distortion effects calculated within the eikonal approximation.

## Key numbers

- Projectile kinetic energy: T_p = 790 MeV
- Coherent pion peak energy: ω = 300 MeV
- Delta resonance mass: M_Δ = 1232 MeV
- Downward energy shift of Delta resonance peak in nuclear targets (A ≥ 10): ≈ 70 MeV
- Landau-Migdal parameter for V_{NΔ}: g_{NΔ} = 1/3
- Ratio of spin-longitudinal to spin-transverse excitation: LO/TR = 1/2
- Coupling constant relation for π/ρ NΔ: f_{π/ρ NΔ} = 2 f_{π/ρ NN}
- Coupling constant relation for π/ρ ΔΔ: f_{π/ρ ΔΔ} = (1/5) f_{π/ρ NN}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Delta dynamics in nuclei using inclusive and exclusive (p,n) and (3He,t) charge exchange reactions, particularly the Delta-hole model calculations by Oset, Udagawa, and others that attributed the downward energy shift of the Delta resonance to attractive Delta-N interaction in the spin-longitudinal channel. The deuteron target offers advantages over heavier nuclei because its wave function is well known and Fermi motion can be treated properly, allowing more direct study of the Delta-N interaction than in previous work with heavier targets. The results enable comparison with experimental data from Prout et al. and provide a framework for understanding how the coherent pion spectrum reveals the attractive Delta-N potential in the spin-longitudinal channel.
