# New Observables in Inclusive Production of Quarkonia

> After an introduction motivating the study of quarkonium production, we review the recent developments in the phenomenology of quarkonium production in inclusive scatterings of hadrons and leptons. We naturally address data and predictions relevant for the LHC, the Tevatron, RHIC, HERA, LEP, B factories and EIC. An up-to-date discussion of the contributions from feed downs within the charmonium and bottomonium families as well as from b hadrons to charmonia is also provided. This contextualises an exhaustive overview of new observables such as the associated production along with a Standard Model boson (photon, W and Z), with another quarkonium, with another heavy quark as well as with light hadrons or jets. We address the relevance of these reactions in order to improve our understanding of the mechanisms underlying quarkonium production as well as the physics of multi-parton interactions, in particular the double parton scatterings. An outlook towards future studies and facilities concludes this review.

# New Observables in Inclusive Production of Quarkonia

## Key claim

The Color Evaporation Model (CEM) predictions for $P_T$-differential cross sections of $J/\psi$ production at NLO accuracy still overshoot the data at high $P_T$, challenging the universality of the non-perturbative parameter $\P^{\rm prompt}_{J/\psi}$ across different kinematic regimes, though the NLO computation reduces theoretical uncertainties compared to LO.

## Method

The authors performed the first NLO fit of $\P^{\rm prompt}_{J/\psi}$ using $P_T$-differential ATLAS single-$J/\psi$ data at $\sqrt{s}=8$ TeV with 11.4 fb$^{-1}$, employing MadGraph5_aMC@NLO for the CEM calculations of $J/\psi$ plus a recoiling parton with finite $P_T$. The NLO NNPDF 2.3 PDF set with $\alpha_s(M_Z)=0.118$ was used, with the charm quark mass set to $m_c=1.27$ GeV and scale variations within $\frac{1}{2}\mu_0 \le \mu_R,\mu_F \le 2\mu_0$ where $\mu_0$ is the transverse mass of the $J/\psi$ in $J/\psi$ + parton.

## Key numbers

- $\P^{\rm LO, prompt}_{J/\psi} = 0.014 \pm 0.001$ from fitting ATLAS data at $\sqrt{s}=8$ TeV with $m_c=1.27$ GeV
- $\P^{\rm NLO, prompt}_{J/\psi} = 0.009 \pm 0.0004$ from the same fit
- The $K$ factor affecting $d\sigma/dP_T$ is close to 1.6
- The $\P^{\rm (N)LO, prompt}_{J/\psi}$ values from $P_T$-differential yields are about a factor of 2-3 smaller than those obtained from $P_T$-integrated yields
- For photoproduction, $\P_{J/\psi} = 0.048 \div 0.056$ with $m_c \simeq 1.45$ GeV describes total $J/\psi$ photoproduction data at NLO
- For $e^+e^-$ annihilation at B factories, $\sigma(J/\psi+X_{\text{non } c \bar c}) = 0.095$ pb with $\P_{J/\psi}=0.025$ and $m_c=1.4$ GeV, about 4.5 times below the latest Belle measurement
- The NRQCD-CEM total cross section overshoots data by a factor as large as 100 at both low and high energies

## Context

This work extends the long-standing CEM phenomenology for quarkonium production, which has been compared to data from the Tevatron, RHIC, and LHC since the late 1990s. The NLO computation addresses the known issue that CEM predictions tend to overshoot data at high $P_T$, previously identified in LO studies by Bedjidian et al. (2004) and Brambilla et al. (2004). The results challenge the universality of the CEM parameter $\P^{\rm prompt}_{J/\psi}$, similar to how NRQCD color-octet LDME fit values differ between $P_T$-differential and $P_T$-integrated yields. The improved CEM (ICEM) proposed by Ma and Vogt (2016) addresses some kinematic issues but does not solve the high-$P_T$ excess. This work enables future NLO studies of other quarkonia in different kinematic regions, as recently done for $\Upsilon$ production.
