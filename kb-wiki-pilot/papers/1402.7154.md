# Understanding fusion and its suppression for the $^{9}$Be projectile with different targets

> The role of the breakup process and one neutron stripping on the near barrier fusion are investigated for the weakly bound projectile $^{9}$Be on $^{28}$Si, $^{89}$Y, $^{124}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb targets. Continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations for the breakup with a $^{8}$Be + n model of the $^{9}$Be nucleus and coupled reactions channels (CRC) calculations for the one neutron stripping to several single particle states in the target are performed for these systems. A good description of the experimental fusion cross sections above the Coulomb barrier is obtained from the CDCC-CRC calculations for all the systems. The calculated incomplete fusion probabilities for different target systems are found to be consistent with the systematic behaviour of the complete fusion suppression factors as a function of target atomic mass, obtained from the experimental data.

# Understanding fusion and its suppression for the $^{9}$Be projectile with different targets

## Key claim

The complete fusion suppression for $^{9}$Be projectiles is nearly independent of target atomic mass across a wide range of targets from light ($^{28}$Si) to heavy ($^{208}$Pb), contrary to earlier empirical predictions that the incomplete fusion probability should decrease with target charge. This conclusion depends on a new method for calculating incomplete fusion cross sections from absorption cross sections obtained in CDCC calculations with three different choices of short-range imaginary potentials.

## Method

Continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations were performed using a $^{8}$Be + n two-body cluster model of $^{9}$Be, with breakup couplings to continuum states up to 7 MeV above threshold and relative angular momenta up to $l=4$. Coupled reaction channels (CRC) calculations for one-neutron stripping to several single-particle states in each target were combined with the CDCC calculations in a CDCC-CRC approach using the code FRESCO. Incomplete fusion cross sections were estimated from absorption cross sections obtained with three choices of short-range imaginary potentials: applied to both projectile fragments, to the $^{8}$Be-target part only, or to the neutron-target part only, allowing separation of neutron and alpha partial fusion contributions.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron stripping Q-values: $^{28}$Si (6.81 MeV), $^{89}$Y (5.19 MeV), $^{124}$Sn (4.07 MeV), $^{144}$Sm (5.09 MeV), $^{208}$Pb (2.27 MeV)
- Short-range imaginary potential parameters: W$_0$ = 50 MeV, r$_0$ = 0.9 fm, a = 0.25 fm
- Continuum discretization up to E$_{max}$ $\approx$ 7 MeV above the $^{8}$Be + n breakup threshold
- Relative angular momentum cutoff: $l=4$ for neutron-core relative motion (higher $l$ values change calculated fusion by less than $\approx$2 percent)
- ICF probabilities evaluated at lab energy 1.3 $V_b$ for each system

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fusion suppression for weakly bound projectiles, particularly the systematic study by Gomes et al. that predicted incomplete fusion probability should decrease with target charge $Z_T$, and the work of Rafiei et al. suggesting CF suppression is nearly independent of $Z_T$. The present calculations resolve a discrepancy for the $^{9}$Be + $^{89}$Y and $^{9}$Be + $^{124}$Sn systems measured at BARC-TIFR, showing they are consistent with the nearly constant suppression behavior. The results support the view that complete fusion suppression being almost independent of target mass may be a universal feature for all weakly bound nuclei, consistent with systematics observed for $^{6}$Li and $^{10}$B projectiles.
