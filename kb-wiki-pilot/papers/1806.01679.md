# Semiclassical calculations of complete and incomplete fusion in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We use an improved version of the semiclassical method described in Refs. [1,2,3] to evaluate fusion cross sections in collisions of weakly bound nuclei. This version takes into account the static effects of the low breakup threshold, uses better bin states in the discretization of the continuum and avoids the excitation of closed channels. The population of these channels is a consequence of the violation of energy conservation, which is inherent in the semiclassical method. The method is employed to evaluate complete fusion and total fusion cross section in collisions of the weakly bound 6,7Li projectiles with 159Tb and 197Au targets, for which data is available. The overall agreement between theory and experiment is fairly good.

## Key claim

The improved semiclassical method achieves "fairly good" overall agreement with experimental data for complete fusion and total fusion cross sections in collisions of weakly bound \(^{6,7}\)Li projectiles with \(^{159}\)Tb and \(^{197}\)Au targets, but the agreement is qualified by the inherent violation of energy conservation in the semiclassical treatment, which the authors address by avoiding excitation of closed channels.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical approach where the projectile-target relative motion is treated by classical mechanics while the breakup dynamics of the weakly bound projectile is handled by time-dependent quantum mechanics. The continuum of unbound states is discretized using energy bins (wave packets) constructed from the scattering wave functions of the two-cluster projectile, and the optical potential is taken as the expectation value of the total fragment-target interaction in the projectile ground state. The time-dependent coupled-channel equations are solved for each impact parameter to obtain the occupation probabilities of bound and bin states, from which complete fusion and total fusion cross sections are extracted.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach of Marta, Canto and Donangelo (Refs. [MCD02, MCD08, MCD14]) by improving the treatment of static effects from the low breakup threshold, using better bin states for continuum discretization, and avoiding excitation of closed channels that violate energy conservation. The method enables evaluation of individual complete fusion and incomplete fusion cross sections for projectiles that break into fragments of comparable masses, which is not possible with the full quantum CDCC approach of Hagino et al. [HVD00] and Diaz-Torres and Thompson [DiT02] that is limited to projectiles breaking into one heavy and one light fragment.
