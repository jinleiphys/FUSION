# Review of quasi-elastic charge-exchange data in the nucleon-deuteron breakup reaction

> The available data on the forward charge exchange of nucleons on the deuteron up to 2GeV per nucleon are reviewed. The value of the inclusive nd->pnn/np->pn cross section ratio is sensitive to the fraction of spin-independent neutron-proton backward scattering. The measurements of the polarisation transfer in d(\vec{n},\vec{p})nn or the deuteron analysing power in p(\vec{d},pp)n in high resolution experiments, where the final nn or pp pair emerge at low excitation energy, depend upon the longitudinal and transverse spin-spin np amplitudes. The relation between these types of experiments is discussed and the results compared with predictions of the impulse approximation model in order to see what new constraints they can bring to the neutron-proton database.

## Key claim

The forward charge-exchange cross section ratio $R_{np}(0)$ in the inclusive $nd\to pnn/np\to pn$ reaction is sensitive to the fraction of spin-independent neutron-proton backward scattering, but the Dean sum rule interpretation provides only a qualitative description at lower energies because the longitudinal momentum transfer must be negligible and terms other than the $np\to pn$ impulse approximation should not contribute significantly.

## Method

The authors review all available inclusive and exclusive measurements of forward charge exchange of nucleons on the deuteron up to 2 GeV per nucleon, comparing the data with predictions from the impulse approximation model using the energy-dependent SAID SP07 phase shift analysis. They relate the forward $d(n,p)n/p(n,p)n$ cross section ratio $R_{np}(0)$, the polarisation transfer in $d(\vec{n},\vec{p})nn$, and the deuteron tensor analysing power in $p(\vec{d},\{pp\})n$ to the longitudinal and transverse spin-spin neutron-proton amplitudes evaluated in the backward direction.

## Key numbers

- $R_{np}(0)$ values from $d(n,p)nn$ experiments range from $0.19$ at 13.9 MeV (Moscow, 1965) to $0.56\pm0.05$ at 2000 MeV (JINR VBLHE, 2009)
- The PSI Freiburg group measured $R_{np}(0)$ at 14 energies from 300 to 560 MeV, with an average value of $0.62\pm0.01$, close to the upper limit of $2/3$
- The Dubna bubble chamber measurement at 3.35 GeV/$c$ deuteron momentum gave $d\sigma/dt(dp\to\{pp\}n)|_{t=0} = 30\pm4$ mb/(GeV/$c$)$^2$
- The SAID SP07 prediction for $R_{np}(0)$ at 100 MeV is 0.600, at 500 MeV is 0.621, and at 1.0 GeV is 0.609
- The deuteron analysing power $A_{NN}(0)$ predicted by SP07 ranges from $-0.027$ at 10 MeV to $-0.305$ at 1.0 GeV
- The longitudinal polarisation transfer $K_{LL}(0)$ predicted by SP07 ranges from $-0.370$ at 10 MeV to $-0.740$ at 1.0 GeV

## Context

This review extends the earlier phenomenology of $d(n,p)$ and $d(p,n)$ charge exchange by Lehar and Wilkin (2008) and compares inclusive sum-rule measurements with high-resolution exclusive experiments. The work builds on the Dean closure sum rule (1972) which showed that integrating over all proton energies removes dependence on the $nn$ wave function, and on the realisation by Bugg and Wilkin (1987) that deuteron tensor analysing powers in $p(\vec{d},\{pp\})n$ are sensitive to differences between neutron-proton spin-flip amplitudes. The consistency between information from $d(\vec{n},\vec{p})nn$ and $p(\vec{d},\{pp\})n$ reactions in the forward direction contributes positively to the neutron-proton charge exchange phenomenology and constrains the neutron-proton database, particularly above 1 GeV where the JINR VBLHE Nuclotron measurements provide new data up to 2 GeV.
