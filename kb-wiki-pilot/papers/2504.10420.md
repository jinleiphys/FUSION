# Role of Coulomb-nuclear breakup of 6,7Li projectiles with heavy deformed 232Th target

> The significance of both Coulomb and nuclear couplings and their interference effects in the breakup processes of 6,7Li with a non-spherical nucleus 232Th has been evaluated. The continuum discretized coupled channel(CDCC) calculations are carried out in a nonstandard way, using short-range imaginary potentials for the fragment-target interaction at energies close to the Coulomb barrier. The present calculations employing short-range imaginary potentials exhibit better agreement with the experimental elastic scattering angular distributions than those using standard systematic value (0.78xWSPP ) used to describe elastic scattering. Including the excitation of the 232Th inelastic shows significant coupling effects on the elastic scattering below the barrier energies compared to higher incident energies. Subsequently, the CDCC framework was used to analyze the nuclear, Coulomb, and total breakup predictions separately. The breakup cross sections for the 6Li+232Th system are greater than those for the 7Li+232Th system across various energies. The present study predicts destructive Coulomb-nuclear interference in the breakup processes involving both 6Li and 7Li projectile nuclei with the deformed 232Th target. Additionally, the breakup reaction cross-sections are compared with experimentally measured fusion cross-sections near the barrier energies for both 6,7Li+232Th systems.

# Role of Coulomb-nuclear breakup of 6,7Li projectiles with heavy deformed 232Th target

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that destructive Coulomb-nuclear interference occurs in the breakup processes of both 6Li and 7Li projectiles with the deformed 232Th target, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of a specific decomposition method where the interference term is obtained by subtracting separately calculated Coulomb and nuclear breakup cross-sections from the total breakup cross-section, which the authors acknowledge is an approximation.

## Method

Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations were performed using the FRESCO code, treating 6Li and 7Li as two-cluster systems (alpha core with deuteron or triton fragments). Two approaches were compared: a standard method using the double-folding Sao Paulo Potential (SPP) with a 0.78 scaling factor for the imaginary part, and a nonstandard approach using short-range imaginary potentials (depth 50 MeV, radius 1.06 fm, diffuseness 0.2 fm) for fragment-target interactions. The continuum was discretized in energy bins up to 10 MeV for 6Li and 11 MeV for 7Li, with multipole expansion up to lambda=4 and relative angular momentum up to J=1000 hbar. Target inelastic excitations of 232Th (2+, 4+, 6+ states) were included via coupled channel calculations using a quadrupole deformation parameter beta2=0.261.

- 6Li breakup threshold: 1.47 MeV; 7Li breakup threshold: 2.45 MeV
- 232Th quadrupole deformation parameter: beta2 = 0.261
- Short-range imaginary potential parameters: Wi = 50 MeV, ri = 1.06 fm, ai = 0.2 fm
- For 6Li+232Th at 26 MeV: sigma_CBU = 10.40 mb, sigma_NBU = 0.64 mb, sigma_TBU = 6.88 mb, interference ratio = 0.60

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Coulomb-nuclear interference in breakup reactions from spherical targets (such as 208Pb and 59Co studied by Otomar et al., 2009 and 2012) to the deformed actinide target 232Th. The findings confirm that destructive Coulomb-nuclear interference, previously reported for spherical targets, also occurs with deformed targets. The study enables future investigations into whether the cancellation between repulsive breakup polarization potentials and attractive collective state couplings at energies above the Coulomb barrier can be systematically exploited, and highlights the need for experimental data to constrain fragment-target optical potentials for CDCC calculations.
