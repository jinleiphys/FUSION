# Uncorrelated scattering approximation for the scattering and break-up of weakly bound nuclei on heavy targets

> The scattering of a weakly bound (halo) projectile nucleus by a heavy target nucleus is investigated. A new approach, called the Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation, is proposed. The main approximation involved is to neglect the correlation between the fragments of the projectile in the region where the interaction with the target is important. The formalism makes use of hyper-spherical harmonics, Raynal-Revay coefficients and momentum-localized wave functions to expand projectile channel wave functions in terms of products of the channel wave function of the individual fragments. Within this approach, the kinetic energy and angular momentum of each fragment is conserved during the scattering process. The elastic, inelastic and break-up S-matrices are obtained as an analytic combination involving the bound wave function of the projectile and the product of the S-matrices of the fragments. The approach is applied to describe the scattering of deuteron on $^{58}$Ni at several energies. The results are compared with experimental data and continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations.

## Key claim

The Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation (USA) neglects the correlation between projectile fragments in the region where the interaction with the target is strong, which is justified for weakly bound systems when the tidal forces overcome the binding force below a critical distance \(R_m\) corresponding to a maximum hyper-angular momentum \(K_m\). The approach is applied to deuteron scattering on \(^{58}\)Ni as a test case, with results compared to experimental data and continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations.

## Method

The formalism expands projectile channel wave functions using hyper-spherical harmonics, Raynal-Revai coefficients, and momentum-localized wave functions to express the three-body S-matrix as an analytic combination of the bound wave function of the projectile and the product of the S-matrices of the individual fragments. For components with hyper-angular momentum \(K \le K_m\), the Hamiltonian is approximated by the sum of two independent fragment-target Hamiltonians, while for \(K > K_m\) the folding potential approximation is used. The S-matrix is then given by the sum of the folding model S-matrix (diagonal in elastic channel) plus a correction term \(\Delta S = P(\bar S - S_F)P\) that accounts for excitation and break-up effects.

## Key numbers

- The critical angular momentum \(L_m\) is defined by the turning point condition: \({L_m (L_m+1) \over 2 M {R_m}^2} + v_f(R_m) = E - \epsilon_0\)
- The hyper-angular momentum \(K\) provides an upper bound for \(L\) and \(J\): \(J \le L+I \le K\)
- The number of momentum localized states is given by \(n_\ell = [(K_m - L_A - L_B)/2] + 1\)
- The system studied is deuteron on \(^{58}\)Ni at several energies (specific energies not given in truncated text)

## Context

This work extends the sudden approximation approach used by the Aarhus group for halo nuclei fragmentation reactions, which treated the participant-target interaction through phenomenological optical potentials and neglected processes where multiple halo particles interact simultaneously with the target. The USA also relates to the adiabatic approach of the Surrey group for Coulomb break-up reactions, which considered only core-target interactions. The new formalism enables analytic calculation of elastic, inelastic, and break-up S-matrices for weakly bound projectiles on heavy targets, providing an alternative to continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations.
