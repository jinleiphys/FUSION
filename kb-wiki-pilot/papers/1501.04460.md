# Nuclear Matter Incompressibility Effect on the Cross Section of Fusion Reactions with a weakly bound projectile

> Fusion reactions with a weakly bound projectile are studied using the double-folding model along with a repulsive interaction modifying term. Using this modified potential, including nuclear matter incompressibility effects, the fusion reaction cross sections and suppression parameters are calculated for 9Be +209Bi,208Pb,29Si and 27Al reactions. The results show that applying these effects at energies near the Coulomb barrier improves the agreement between the calculated and experimental cross sections, and modifies the mean values of the suppression parameter.

## Key claim

Applying a repulsive core term to the double-folding potential, which simulates the effect of nuclear matter incompressibility and the Pauli exclusion principle at high density overlap, improves the agreement between calculated and experimental fusion cross sections for reactions with the weakly bound projectile 9Be, and modifies the mean values of the suppression parameter.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear interaction potential using the double-folding model with the M3Y-Paris effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. They then added a repulsive core term to the potential, with its strength and diffuseness adjusted so that the repulsive potential at zero separation equals the energy increase from compressing nuclear matter to twice saturation density, using the nuclear matter incompressibility K. Fusion cross sections were computed using the coupled-channels code CCFULL for the systems 9Be + 209Bi, 208Pb, 29Si, and 27Al, and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 9Be + 209Bi: suppression parameter SP = 18.27% with M3Y potential, reduced to 6.14% with the modified (MIP) potential including incompressibility effects.
- For 9Be + 208Pb: SP = 46.00% (M3Y), reduced to 44.98% (MIP).
- For 9Be + 29Si: SP = 22.46% (M3Y), reduced to 20.20% (MIP).
- For 9Be + 27Al: SP = 14.31% (M3Y), reduced to 11.28% (MIP).
- The difference in SP(M3Y) between the 9Be+209Bi and 9Be+208Pb systems is approximately 28 percent.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ghodsi, Seyyedi, Mohammadi, 2012) on complete fusion suppression in reactions with light and heavy targets. It addresses the known discrepancy that theoretical complete fusion cross sections for weakly bound projectiles are about 25-30 percent larger than experimental values when breakup is not included (Gomes et al., 2011). The modified potential approach, based on the equation of state of cold nuclear matter and the Pauli repulsion treatment of Misicu and Esbensen (2007), enables more accurate predictions of fusion cross sections and suppression parameters for reactions used in super-heavy element formation.
