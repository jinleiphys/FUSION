# Breakup dynamics of a neutron-halo projectile on heavy target at deep sub-barrier energies

> By studying the total fusion and breakup cross-sections in the interaction of the neutron-halo $^{11}{\rm Be}$ projectile on the lead target $^{208}$Pb, it is shown that, even for the neutron-halo projectile, the breakup channel remains the most dominant reaction channel at sub-barrier energies, following a characteristic behavior that was also previously verified for the case of the proton-halo projectile $^8{\rm B}$. This feature is found to emanate from the enhancement of the breakup cross-section, due to the continuum-continuum couplings coming exclusively from its Coulomb component. We further speculate that the enhancement of the Coulomb breakup cross-section at sub-barrier incident energies by the continuum-continuum couplings could be associated with the projectile breaking up on the outgoing trajectory, provided these couplings can be proven to delay the breakup process.

# Breakup dynamics of a neutron-halo projectile on heavy target at deep sub-barrier energies

## Key claim

Even for a neutron-halo projectile (11Be on 208Pb), the breakup channel remains the dominant reaction channel at sub-barrier energies, following the same characteristic behavior previously verified for the proton-halo projectile 8B. The authors speculate that the enhancement of the Coulomb breakup cross-section at sub-barrier energies by continuum-continuum couplings could be associated with the projectile breaking up on the outgoing trajectory, provided these couplings can be proven to delay the breakup process.

## Method

The authors performed continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations for the 11Be + 208Pb reaction system, modeling 11Be as a 10Be core with a weakly-bound valence neutron in an s-wave configuration (binding energy -0.504 MeV). The projectile-target coupling matrix elements were constructed using Woods-Saxon optical potentials, with both long-range and short-range imaginary potentials tested, and the coupled differential equations were solved using the FRESCO computer code with convergence achieved at maximum core-neutron excitation energy of 8 MeV and maximum orbital angular momentum of 6 hbar.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy of 11Be: -0.504 MeV (s-wave, 2s1/2+ configuration)
- First excited bound-state energy: -0.183 MeV (p1/2- state)
- Narrow resonance energy: 1.274 MeV (d5/2+ continuum state)
- Coulomb barrier height VB = 37.90 MeV (from Sao Paulo potential)
- Energy range studied: Ecm/VB from 0.5 to 1.3
- Core-target optical potential parameters: V0 = 70 MeV, R0 = 7.43 fm, a0 = 1.04 fm, W0 = 58.9 MeV, Rw = 7.19 fm, aw = 1.0 fm
- Short-range imaginary potential parameters used for comparison: W0 = 50 MeV, rw = 1.0 fm, aw = 0.1 fm
- Convergence parameters: lmax = 6 hbar, rmax = 100 fm, emax = 8 MeV, Lmax = 1000 hbar, Rmax = 500 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the proton-halo projectile 8B (Pakou et al. 2020, Mukeru et al. 2021, Yang et al. 2022) to a neutron-halo projectile to test whether the dominance of breakup over fusion at sub-barrier energies is a universal feature of weakly-bound systems. The fundamental difference between proton-halo and neutron-halo projectiles is the absence of a Coulomb barrier in the core-neutron system, allowing the authors to test whether the phenomenon arises from dynamical effects (projectile-target interaction) or static effects (projectile ground-state wave function). The results confirm that the breakup channel dominance extends to neutron-halo projectiles, with continuum-continuum couplings identified as the key dynamical mechanism, and the enhancement coming exclusively from the Coulomb component of the breakup.
