# Mechanisms for Direct Breakup Reactions

> We review some simple mechanisms of breakup in nuclear reactions. We mention the spectator breakup, which is described in the post-form DWBA. The relation to other formulations is also indicated. An especially important mechanism is Coulomb dissociation. It is a distinct advantage that the perturbation due to the electric field of the nucleus is exactly known. Therefore firm conclusions can be drawn from such measurements. Some new applications of Coulomb dissociation for nuclear astrophysics are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews simple mechanisms for nuclear breakup reactions, particularly the spectator breakup described in post-form DWBA and Coulomb dissociation, but notes that while the perturbation due to the electric field of the nucleus is exactly known (allowing firm conclusions), the choice of Hamiltonian decomposition for the final state remains an unresolved issue that requires further clarification, especially regarding post-acceleration effects.

## Method

The authors examine two primary breakup mechanisms: spectator breakup, where one constituent interacts strongly with the target while the other moves undisturbed, treated using post-form DWBA with a three-body Hamiltonian; and Coulomb dissociation, treated in a semiclassical framework using the equivalent photon approximation. They compare these approaches and discuss their relation through post-prior identities, also considering higher order electromagnetic effects using coupled channels approaches and the sudden approximation for small adiabaticity parameter values.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of ${}^{19}$C determined to be $530\pm130$ keV from Coulomb dissociation experiments
- For grazing collisions in RIKEN experiments with ${}^{11}$Be and ${}^{19}$C, the adiabaticity parameter $\xi$ is of order 0.1
- Higher order effects decrease strongly with impact parameter, scaling as $1/b^4$
- The ${}^{8}$B Coulomb dissociation cross section calculated for projectile energies of 46.5 $A\cdot$MeV and 250 $A\cdot$MeV on ${}^{208}$Pb at a ${}^{7}$Be-p relative energy of 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Oppenheimer and Phillips (1935) on deuteron breakup and the comprehensive review by Baur et al. (1984, Phys. Rep. 111). It relates post-form DWBA to the adiabatic approach of Tostevin, Rugmai and Johnson (1998) and discusses the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formalism for inclusive breakup. The paper enables applications of Coulomb dissociation to nuclear astrophysics, particularly for radiative capture reactions relevant to the solar neutrino problem (${}^{7}$Be(p,$\gamma$)${}^{8}$B) and for neutron capture cross sections needed for r-process nucleosynthesis.
