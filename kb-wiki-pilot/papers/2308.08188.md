# Elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections of $^{6}$Li studied with a microscopic continuum discretized coupled channels model

> We present a systematic study of $^{6}$Li elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections at incident energies around the Coulomb barrier within the continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework, where $^{6}$Li is treated in an $α$+$d$ two-body model. Collisions with $^{27}$Al, $^{64}$Zn, $^{138}$Ba and $^{208}$Pa are analyzed. The microscopic optical potentials (MOP) based on Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interaction for $α$ and $d$ are adopted in CDCC calculations and satisfactory agreement with the experimental data is obtained without any adjustment on MOPs. For comparison, the $α$ and $d$ global phenomenological optical potentials (GOP) are also used in CDCC analysis and a reduction no less than 50$\%$ on the surface imaginary part of deuteron GOP is required for describing the data. In all cases, the $^6$Li breakup effect is significant and provides repulsive correction to the folding model potential. The reduction on the surface imaginary part of GOP of deuteron reveals a strong suppression of the reaction probability of deuteron as a component of $^{6}$Li as compared with that of a free deuteron. A further investigation is made by taking the $d$ breakup process into account equivalently within the dynamic polarization potential approach and it shows that $d$ behaves like a tightly bound nucleus in $^{6}$Li induced reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that microscopic optical potentials (MOPs) based on Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interactions can reproduce experimental elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections for \(^{6}\)Li on multiple targets without any parameter adjustment, provided the continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework properly accounts for breakup effects. However, when using global phenomenological optical potentials (GOPs), a reduction of at least 50% on the surface imaginary part of the deuteron GOP is required to fit the data, revealing that the deuteron cluster inside \(^{6}\)Li behaves like a tightly bound nucleus with suppressed breakup probability compared to a free deuteron.

## Method

The authors performed CDCC calculations treating \(^{6}\)Li as an \(\alpha+d\) two-body system, using the Lagrange-mesh method to discretize continuum states up to \(\varepsilon_{\max}=30\) MeV and \(l_{\max}=2\). Microscopic optical potentials for \(\alpha\) and \(d\) were constructed via folding with Skyrme SkMP nucleon-nucleon interactions and nucleon density distributions from Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations, while for comparison global phenomenological optical potentials for \(\alpha\) and \(d\) were also employed with a reduction factor \(\lambda_d\) applied to the surface imaginary part of the deuteron GOP.

## Key numbers

- Breakup threshold energy of \(^{6}\)Li: 1.47 MeV
- Coulomb barriers in laboratory system: 8.0 MeV (\(^{27}\)Al), 14.5 MeV (\(^{64}\)Zn), 22.0 MeV (\(^{138}\)Ba), 30.6 MeV (\(^{208}\)Pb)
- Optimized reduction factors \(\lambda_d\) for deuteron GOP surface imaginary part: 0 (\(^{27}\)Al), 0.45 (\(^{64}\)Zn), 0.50 (\(^{138}\)Ba), 0.35 (\(^{208}\)Pb), all no more than 0.5
- Convergence criteria: \(\varepsilon_{\max}=30\) MeV and \(l_{\max}=2\) give total reaction cross sections within 2% of converged values
- For \(d\) breakup analysis in \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{208}\)Pb at \(E_{\mathrm{lab}}=33.0\) MeV, the equivalent deuteron energy is \(E_d=11.0\) MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier CDCC studies by Sakuragi et al. at energies well above the Coulomb barrier to energies around the barrier, and builds on the authors' previous development of microscopic optical potentials for light ions including \(^{2,3}\)H, \(^{3,4,6}\)He and \(^{7}\)Li. The systematic comparison between MOP and GOP approaches across four target masses (\(^{27}\)Al, \(^{64}\)Zn, \(^{138}\)Ba, \(^{208}\)Pb) enables a consistent determination of the deuteron GOP reduction factor, resolving ambiguities in earlier works where the adjustment varied between targets. The finding that the deuteron behaves as a tightly bound nucleus within \(^{6}\)Li, supported by dynamic polarization potential analysis of the \(d\) breakup process, provides a foundation for applying the same microscopic CDCC approach to heavier weakly bound projectiles such as \(^{11}\)Li and \(^{11}\)Be.
