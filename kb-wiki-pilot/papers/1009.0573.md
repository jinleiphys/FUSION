# Enhancement of sub-barrier fusion of two-neutron halo nuclei

> The tunneling of composite systems, where breakup may occur during the barrier penetration process is considered in connection with the fusion of halo-like radioactive, neutron- and proton-rich nuclei on heavy targets. The large amount of recent and new data clearly indicates that breakup hinders the fusion at near and below the Coulomb barrier energies. However, clear evidence for the halo enhancements, seems to over ride the breakup hindrance at lower energies, owing, to a large extent, to the extended matter density distribution. In particular we report here that at sub-barrier energies the fusion cross section of the Borromean two-neutron halo nucleus $^{6}$He with the actinide nucleus $^{238}$U is significantly enhanced compared to the fusion of a no-halo $^{6}$He. This conclusion differs from that of the original work, where it was claimed that no such enhancement ensues. This sub-barrier fusion enhancement was also observed in the $^{6}$He + $^{209}$% Bi system. The role of the corresponding easily excitable low lying dipole pygmy resonance in these systems is therefore significant. The consequence of this overall enhanced fusion of halo nuclei at sub-barrier energies, on stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis is evident.

## Key claim

The fusion cross section of the Borromean two-neutron halo nucleus 6He with the actinide nucleus 238U is significantly enhanced at sub-barrier energies compared to the fusion of a non-halo 6He, contrary to the conclusion of the original work by Raabe et al. (2004) which claimed no such enhancement. This enhancement arises from the static effect of the extended matter density distribution of the halo, which lowers the Coulomb barrier and increases penetrability, and this effect overrides the breakup hindrance at lower energies.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing fusion data for 6He + 238U and 6He + 209Bi using the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) method, which transforms energy and cross section into dimensionless quantities to isolate channel coupling effects. They performed coupled channels calculations using the double-folding Sao Paulo potential (SPP), comparing results obtained with the realistic halo density of 6He against results using a scaled non-halo density (treating 6He as an alpha particle with six nucleons). The dynamic polarization potential (DPP) formalism was used to separate breakup coupling effects from static halo effects.

- For 6He + 209Bi, the same qualitative behavior is observed: the static halo effect enhances fusion at sub-barrier energies compared to the non-halo calculation
- The Coulomb barrier for 6He + 238U is lower when calculated with the realistic halo density compared to the non-halo density (Fig. 3)
- For tightly bound systems (16O + 144,154Sm, 4He + 209Bi), the renormalized experimental fusion function closely follows the Universal Fusion Function F0(x)
- For weakly bound systems, complete fusion for 9Be + 208Pb and 6,7Li + 209Bi is suppressed by about 30% at energies above the barrier

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fusion for weakly bound and halo nuclei, directly contradicting the conclusion of Raebe et al. (Nature, 2004) that no sub-barrier fusion enhancement occurs for 6He + 238U. The paper builds on the Universal Fusion Function method developed by Canto et al. (2009) and the Sao Paulo potential of Chamon et al. (1997, 2002), and it reconciles conflicting interpretations of data from Kolata et al. (1998) for 6He + 209Bi. The results have implications for stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis, particularly the r-process, as the enhanced fusion of halo nuclei at sub-barrier energies combined with threshold dipole strength (pygmy resonances) could alter nucleosynthesis scenarios as predicted by Goriely (1998).
