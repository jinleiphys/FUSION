# Three-body electrodisintegration of the three-nucleon bound state with $Δ$-isobar excitation: Processes below pion-production threshold

> Electron scattering from the three-nucleon bound state with two- and three-body disintegration is described. The description uses the purely nucleonic charge-dependent CD-Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD-Bonn + $Δ$. Exact solutions of three-particle equations are employed for the initial and final states of the reactions. The current has one-baryon and two-baryon contributions and couples nucleonic with $Δ$-isobar channels. $Δ$-isobar effects on the observables are isolated. The $Δ$-isobar excitation yields an effective three-nucleon force and effective two- and three-nucleon currents beside other $Δ$-isobar effects; they are mutually consistent.

## Key claim

The paper describes electron scattering from the three-nucleon bound state with two- and three-body disintegration, using the purely nucleonic CD-Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD-Bonn + Δ, but only for processes below pion-production threshold where the Δ-isobar excitation remains virtual.

## Method

Exact solutions of three-particle equations were employed for the initial and final states of the reactions, using Chebyshev expansion of the two-baryon transition matrix as interpolation technique. The electromagnetic current has one-baryon and two-baryon contributions and couples nucleonic with Δ-isobar channels, augmented with electromagnetic form factors. The baryonic potential was taken into account in purely nucleonic and in nucleon-Δ partial waves up to total two-baryon angular momentum I=3, while the electromagnetic current was allowed to act between partial waves up to I=6.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of ³He: -7.941 MeV for CD Bonn and -8.225 MeV for CD Bonn + Δ (purely hadronic values without Coulomb)
- With Coulomb interaction included, binding energies shift to -7.261 MeV and -7.544 MeV respectively, compared to experimental value -7.718 MeV
- Magnetic moments: ³He = -2.073 μN (CD Bonn), -2.139 μN (CD Bonn + Δ), experimental -2.127 μN; ³H = 2.906 μN (CD Bonn), 2.970 μN (CD Bonn + Δ), experimental 2.979 μN
- Nucleon-Δ transition form factor coupling strength g_ΔN^{M1}(0) = 4.59 μN, corresponding to transition magnetic moment G_M*(0) = 3.06 μN
- Δ-isobar rest mass m_Δ c² = 1232 MeV
- Maximum total three-baryon angular momentum considered: J_max = 25/2

## Context

This work updates previous calculations by Yuan et al. (2002) of three-nucleon electron scattering, extending the description to higher energies and including three-nucleon breakup, though still below pion-production threshold. The underlying purely nucleonic reference potential is CD Bonn (Machleidt, 2001), and its coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + Δ was fitted to experimental two-nucleon data up to 350 MeV nucleon lab energy. The paper enables comparison with alternative descriptions by Golak et al. (1995, 2002) that employ different two-nucleon potentials and electromagnetic currents, though theoretical predictions turn out qualitatively similar where comparable.
