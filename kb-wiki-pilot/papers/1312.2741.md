# Importance of $1n$-stripping process in the $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb reaction

> The inclusive cross sections of the $α$-particles produced in the reaction $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb have been measured at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The measured cross sections are found to be orders of magnitude larger than the calculated cross sections of $^{6}$Li breaking into $α$ and $d$ fragments, thus indicating contributions from other processes. The experimental cross sections of $1n$-stripping and $1n$-pickup processes have been determined from an entirely different measurement, reported earlier. Apart from incomplete fusion and/ $d$-transfer processes, the $1n$-stripping process is found to be a significant contributor to the inclusive $α$-particle cross sections in this reaction.

## Key claim

The inclusive alpha-particle cross sections measured for the reaction $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb at energies around the Coulomb barrier are orders of magnitude larger than the calculated no-capture breakup (NCBU) cross sections from CDCC calculations, and the $1n$-stripping process is found to be a significant contributor to these cross sections, in contradiction to earlier findings for the $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi reaction but in agreement with observations for $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb.

## Method

Inclusive alpha-particle cross sections were measured using four $\Delta$E-E telescopes of Si-surface barrier detectors at laboratory angles from 30$^{\circ}$ to 165$^{\circ}$ for $^{6}$Li beam energies of 23, 25, 27, 30, and 35 MeV incident on a self-supporting $^{159}$Tb target of thickness approximately 450 $\mu$g/cm$^{2}$. The NCBU cross sections were calculated using the continuum-discretized-coupled channels (CDCC) method with the code FRESCO, assuming $^{6}$Li has an $\alpha$+$d$ cluster structure. Contributions from $d$-capture incomplete fusion (ICF), $1n$-stripping, and $1n$-pickup processes were extracted from previously reported $\gamma$-ray measurements of the same reaction system.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive alpha-particle cross sections were measured at five beam energies: $E_{lab}$ = 23, 25, 27, 30, and 35 MeV
- The $1n$-stripping cross sections (populating excited states of $^{160}$Tb) were found to be much larger than the calculated NCBU cross sections at all measured energies
- The sum of $d$-ICF cross sections (including $d$-transfer and $1p$-stripping) and $1n$-stripping cross sections (excluding ground state transfer) lies very close to the measured total inclusive alpha-particle cross sections
- DWBA calculations for the $1n$-stripping process to the 63.68 keV (1$^{-}$) state of $^{160}$Tb required a spectroscopic factor of 0.25 to fit the measured cross sections at higher energies, while a spectroscopic factor of 1.0 over-predicted the data

## Context

This work extends systematic measurements of complete and incomplete fusion excitation functions for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{159}$Tb and $^{10,11}$B+$^{159}$Tb reactions previously reported by the same group (Mukherjee et al. 2006, 2010; Pradhan et al. 2011). The finding that $1n$-stripping dominates over direct breakup contradicts the results reported for $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi by Santra et al. (2012) but agrees with observations by Luong et al. (2011, 2013) for $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb and $^{6,7}$Li reactions with $^{207,208}$Pb and $^{209}$Bi targets. The work enables future exclusive measurements and theoretical descriptions of transfer-breakup processes in reactions with weakly bound projectiles, particularly for lighter systems and systems involving halo and skin nuclei.
