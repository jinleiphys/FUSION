# Production of Heavy Clusters with an Expanded Coalescence Model in CEM

> The production of heavy clusters in nuclear reactions is important in a wide variety of applications: radiation shielding, space engineering and design, medical physics, accelerator design, and more. According to the Cascade Exciton Model (CEM), there are three ways high-energy heavy clusters can be produced. The first way is via coalescence of nucleons produced in the IntraNuclear Cascade (INC). The second way is via the preequilibrium model. The last way is via Fermi breakup. Previous work in CEM examines the impact of expansions of the preequilibrium model and Fermi breakup model on heavy cluster production. The present work studies the impact of expanding the coalescence model on heavy cluster spectra. CEM03.03, the default event generator in the Monte Carlo N-Particle transport code version 6 (MCNP6) for intermediate-energy nuclear reactions, is capable of producing light fragments up to He4 in its coalescence model. In the present study, we have expanded the coalescence model to be able to produce up to Be7. Preliminary results are promising.

# Production of Heavy Clusters with an Expanded Coalescence Model in CEM

## Key claim

Expanding the coalescence model in the Cascade Exciton Model (CEM) to produce clusters up to Be7 (from the previous limit of He4) yields increased production of heavy clusters in nuclear spallation reactions and improved agreement with experimental data, but the results are described as "preliminary" and the paper presents only a limited set of comparisons for specific reactions and angles.

## Method

The authors extended the existing coalescence model in CEM03.03, which previously formed only d, t, He3, and He4 from cascade nucleons, to additionally form He6, Li6, Li7, and Be7 by allowing coalescence among pre-formed 2-nucleon pairs, 3-nucleon groups (tritium or He3), and He4 clusters. The coalescence condition checks that the momentum difference between each nucleon in a group and the average momentum of all nucleons in that group is less than a coalescence radius parameter p_c, with new p_c values introduced for light fragments (p_c(LF) = 175 MeV/c for incident energies below 300 MeV or above 1000 MeV, and 250 MeV/c for 300-1000 MeV), while also increasing the p_c for He4 from 115 to 130 MeV/c (low/high energy) and from 175 to 205 MeV/c (intermediate energy) to compensate for alpha particles lost to heavy cluster formation.

## Key numbers

- CEM03.03 previously produced light fragments only up to He4 in its coalescence model; the expansion extends production up to Be7
- Coalescence radii p_c for light fragments (LF): 175 MeV/c for incident energies T < 300 MeV or T > 1000 MeV; 250 MeV/c for 300 MeV < T < 1000 MeV
- p_c(He4) was increased from 115 to 130 MeV/c (low/high energy) and from 175 to 205 MeV/c (intermediate energy) to compensate for alpha particles lost to heavy cluster formation
- The expansion requires an insignificant amount (2-3%) of increased computation time
- Results shown for reactions: 1200 MeV p + Au197 producing He6 and Li6 at 20 degrees; 480 MeV p + natAg producing Li7 and Be7 at 40 degrees; 480 MeV p + natAg producing Li6 at 60 degrees

## Context

This work extends previous expansions of the preequilibrium model and Fermi breakup model in CEM (references 3-7) by now expanding the coalescence model, which is the third mechanism for heavy cluster production in the Cascade Exciton Model. The coalescence approach is similar to that used in the Liege intranuclear cascade (INCL) model (references 11-14), though CEM coalesces cascade nucleons outside the nucleus while INCL coalesces on the nuclear border. The upgraded code (CEM03.03F) with the coalescence expansion is part of a larger project aimed at producing high-energy light fragments in spallation reactions, and the authors plan to implement these upgrades into MCNP6.
