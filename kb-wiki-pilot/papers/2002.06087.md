# Three-nucleon force effects in inclusive spectra of the neutron-deuteron breakup reaction

> We investigate the sensitivity of the non-exclusive nucleon induced deuteron breakup reaction to the three-nucleon interaction and distributions of three-nucleon force effects in inclusive spectra. To this end we solve the three-nucleon Faddeev equation at a number of incoming nucleon laboratory energies using the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction alone or combined with the 2π-exchange Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force. Based on these solutions energy spectra of an outgoing nucleon, at a specified detection angle as well as spectra integrated over that angle, are calculated. By integrating the spectra at a given angle over the energy of the outgoing nucleon the angular distributions of three-nucleon force effects in the breakup process are additionally obtained. Contrary to elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering, where at higher energies significant three-nucleon force effects were encountered for scattering angles around the minimum of the cross section, for the breakup process only moderate effects are found and they are restricted to forward angles. Results of the present investigation show that the large three-nucleon force effects found for some specific complete breakup configurations are reduced substantially in the incomplete spectra when averaging over contributing complete geometries is performed.

## Key claim
Contrary to elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering where large three-nucleon force (3NF) effects appear at higher energies around the cross-section minimum, only moderate 3NF effects are found in inclusive breakup spectra, restricted to forward angles. The large 3NF effects previously found for specific complete breakup configurations are substantially reduced when averaging over contributing complete geometries in incomplete spectra.

## Method
The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation in a partial-wave momentum-space basis at laboratory energies of 14, 70, 100, 135, 200, 250, and 294 MeV, using the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential alone or combined with the 2π-exchange Tucson-Melbourne (TM99) three-nucleon force. They calculated threefold differential cross sections d³σ/dΩ₁dE₁ for outgoing nucleons at specific laboratory angles, angle-integrated energy spectra dσ/dE₁, and angular distributions obtained by integrating spectra over outgoing nucleon energy. All 3N partial wave states with total two-nucleon angular momentum j ≤ 5 and total 3N angular momentum J ≤ 25/2 were included.

## Key numbers
- 3NF effects in energy spectra at a specific angle (10°) reach up to ≈10% at 70 MeV and strengthen to ≈20% at 200 MeV
- In angle-integrated energy spectra, 3NF effects are ≈2-3% at 70 MeV and ≈4-6% at 200 MeV
- In angular distributions of breakup cross section: ≈2-3% up to θ₁^lab ≈ 100° at 70 MeV; ≈3-4% up to θ₁^lab ≈ 120° at 135 MeV; ≈4-5% up to θ₁^lab ≈ 110° at 200 MeV
- For comparison, 3NF effects in elastic nd scattering reach ≈50% at 200 MeV in the region of the cross-section minimum
- At 200 MeV, the TM99 3NF explains ≈60% of the difference between experimental total nd cross section and CD Bonn prediction; at 250 MeV ≈45%; at 294 MeV only ≈30%
- 3NF effects in total nd breakup cross section are ≈2-3%; in total elastic scattering cross section ≈6-10%

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 3NF effects in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering (Witała et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 81, 1183 (1998); Phys. Rev. C 63, 024007 (2001)) and in specific kinematically complete breakup configurations (Kuroś-Żołnierczuk et al., Phys. Rev. C 66, 024003 (2002)), where 3NF effects up to ≈90% were found at 200 MeV. The results show that the long-range 2π-exchange TM99 3NF is insufficient to explain total nd cross section data above ≈135 MeV, indicating the need for short-range 3NF components such as those from π-ρ or ρ-ρ exchanges (Coon and Peña, Phys. Rev. C 48, 2559 (1993)) or from chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum et al., Phys. Rev. C 66, 064001 (2002); Bernard et al., Phys. Rev. C 77, 064004 (2008)).
