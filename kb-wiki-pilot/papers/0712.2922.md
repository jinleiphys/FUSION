# Absorption-Fluctuation Theorem for Nuclear Reactions: Brink-Axel, Incomplete Fusion and All That

> We discuss the connection between absorption, averages and fluctuations in nuclear reactions. The fluctuations in the entrance channel result in the compound nucleus, Hauser-Feshbach, cross section, the fluctuations in the intermediate channels, result in modifications of multistep reaction cross sections, while the fluctuations in the final channel result in hybrid cross sections that can be used to describe incomplete fusion reactions. We discuss the latter in details and comment on the validity of the assumptions used in the develpoment of the Surrogate method. We also discuss the theory of multistep reactions with regards to intermediate state fluctuations and the energy dependence and non-locality of the intermediate channels optical potentials.

# Absorption-Fluctuation Theorem for Nuclear Reactions: Brink-Axel, Incomplete Fusion and All That

## Key claim

The absorption-fluctuation theorem connects fluctuations at different stages of a nuclear reaction to specific cross section components: entrance-channel fluctuations yield the compound nucleus Hauser-Feshbach cross section, intermediate-channel fluctuations modify multistep reaction cross sections, and final-channel fluctuations produce hybrid cross sections that describe incomplete fusion reactions. The paper argues that the Surrogate Method's success relies on the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation and on-shell descriptions, but raises theoretical issues about off-shell effects and multistep formation of the compound nucleus that may limit its validity.

## Method

The author derives the incomplete fusion cross section from a decomposition of the final-channel wave function into optical and fluctuation pieces, showing that the inelastic breakup cross section separates into a direct term and an incomplete fusion term related to the fusion imaginary potential W_xA^(F). For intermediate-state fluctuations, the author decomposes the intermediate-channel Green's function into an average part containing the damping width of the one-phonon collective state and a fluctuation part that gives rise to the Brink-Axel mechanism, then derives the n-step cross section including fluctuation contributions. The paper also examines the Feshbach optical potential formalism to analyze the energy dependence, non-locality, and complexity of optical potentials, and critiques the FKK statistical multistep direct reaction theory's treatment of dual scattering states.

## Key numbers

- The fluctuation contribution to the two-step cross section for excitation of the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) in 208Pb + 208Pb at 640 MeV per nucleon bombarding energy contributes 30% to the cross section
- At the lower energy of 100 MeV per nucleon for the same system, the fluctuation contribution is 100%
- The general n-step cross section including Brink-Axel fluctuations is given by: sigma-bar^(n) = [1 + sum_{k=1}^n ((n-k)!/n!)*((n-k)/(n+k))*C(n,k)*(Gamma_d1_down* tau_c(E)/hbar)^k] * sigma_MS^(n)

## Context

This work extends the statistical multistep direct reaction theory of Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin (FKK80) by incorporating intermediate-state fluctuations through the Brink-Axel mechanism, building on prior work by Carlson et al. (Car99, CH99, Can99) and Gu and Weidenmuller (GW01) on multiphonon excitations. The incomplete fusion formalism connects to the Surrogate Method (Ple05, ED06) and the Trojan-Horse method (Typel and Baur, TB03), and the paper raises theoretical questions about why these methods work despite using the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation and ignoring off-shell effects. The analysis of dual scattering states for energy-dependent optical potentials addresses issues raised by Koning and Akkermans (KA91) and Arbanas et al. (Ar95) regarding the proper formulation of multistep direct reaction cross sections.
