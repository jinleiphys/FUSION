# A Smoothing Method of Discrete Breakup S-matrix Elements in the Theory of Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channels

> We present a practical way of smoothing discrete breakup S-matrix elements calculated by the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method (CDCC). This method makes the smoothing procedure much easier. The reliability of the smoothing method is confirmed for the three-body breakup reactions, 58Ni(d,pn) at 80 MeV and 12C(6He,4He2n) at 229.8 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors present a practical smoothing formula for discrete breakup S-matrix elements obtained from continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations, using the complex scaling method (CSM) to compute the smoothing factors. The method is claimed to be accurate and practical for both three-body and four-body breakup reactions, though the test cases shown are limited to three-body systems.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the internal Hamiltonian of the projectile using L2-type Gaussian basis functions to obtain pseudostates, then solve the CDCC equations to get discrete S-matrix elements. They compute smoothing factors using the complex scaling method, where the full propagator is approximated by a spectral representation using eigenstates of the complex-scaled Hamiltonian obtained with real-range Gaussian basis functions. The smoothed S-matrix elements are obtained by summing the discrete CDCC S-matrix elements weighted by these smoothing factors.

## Key numbers

- For the 58Ni(d,pn) reaction at 80 MeV, the grazing total angular momentum is J=17; the S-matrix elements converge to the exact solution as the scaling angle theta increases from 5 degrees to 15 degrees
- For the 12C(6He,4He2n) reaction at 229.8 MeV, the grazing total angular momentum is J=25; the S-matrix elements show the 2+ resonance of 6He and converge to exact results with increasing theta
- The continuum states are truncated at k=1.5 fm-1 for both reactions
- The complex-range Gaussian basis uses 2N=40 functions with a1=1.0 fm and aN=30.0 fm; the real-range Gaussian basis uses N=30 functions with the same range parameters

## Context

This work extends the pseudostate discretization method for CDCC previously developed by Matsumoto et al. (2003) and Egami et al. (2004), which was limited in its ability to smooth discrete S-matrix elements for four-body breakup reactions. The new method using CSM is simpler than the earlier smoothing approach proposed by Egami (2008) for four-body reactions. This enables future CDCC calculations for four-body breakup processes of three-body projectiles such as 6He, and the authors plan to apply it to experimental data from Aumann, Wang-MSU, and Chulkov.
