# Systematic analysis of $t$ and $^3$He breakup reactions

> Systematic measurement of $t$ and $^3$He knockout processes is planned. The weakly-bound nature of these nuclei may affect the interpretation of forthcoming knockout reaction data. Purpose: We aim at clarifying breakup properties of $t$ and $^3$He by investigating their elastic and breakup cross sections. We employ the four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with the eikonal approximation to describe the $t$ and $^3$He reactions. The breakup cross section of $t$ is found to be almost the same as that of $^3$He and is about one-third of that of $d$. Coulomb breakup plays negligible role in the breakup of $t$ and $^3$He, in contrast to in the deuteron breakup reaction. It is found that $t$ and $^3$He tend to breakup into three nucleons rather than $d$ and a nucleon. It is shown that the breakup cross sections of $t$ and $^3$He are not as large as those of d but non-negligible. Because about 80% of them corresponds to the three-nucleon breakup process, a four-body breakup reaction model is necessary to quantitatively describe the breakup of $t$ and $^3$He.

## Key claim

The breakup cross sections of triton ($t$) and helium-3 ($^3$He) are nearly identical and about one-third that of deuteron ($d$), with Coulomb breakup playing a negligible role; however, about 80% of the breakup corresponds to three-nucleon breakup rather than two-body breakup into a deuteron and a nucleon, meaning a four-body reaction model is necessary for quantitative description.

## Method

The authors employed the four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with the eikonal approximation (E-CDCC) to describe $t$ and $^3$He reactions, treating each projectile as a three-nucleon system interacting with a target nucleus. The Gaussian expansion method (GEM) was used to obtain ground and discretized-continuum states of $t$ and $^3$He, with the nucleon-nucleon Minnesota interaction and a phenomenological three-body interaction to reproduce binding energies. The P-separation method was applied to decompose breakup channels into $d+p$ and $p+p+n$ contributions.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $t$: $-8.45$ MeV (calculated) vs. $-8.48$ MeV (experimental); root-mean-square radius: 1.68 fm (calculated) vs. 1.84 fm (experimental)
- Ground-state energy of $^3$He: $-7.77$ MeV (calculated) vs. $-7.71$ MeV (experimental); root-mean-square radius: 1.70 fm (calculated) vs. 1.99 fm (experimental)
- Total breakup cross section of $^3$He with $^{40}$Ca target: 62 mb at 40 MeV/nucleon, 48 mb at 70 MeV/nucleon, 20 mb at 150 MeV/nucleon
- Total breakup cross section of $d$ with $^{40}$Ca target: 148 mb at 40 MeV/nucleon, 114 mb at 70 MeV/nucleon, 47 mb at 150 MeV/nucleon
- $^3$He breakup cross section is about one-third of $d$ breakup cross section for all target systems studied
- Probability of deuteron existence in $^3$He ground state: approximately 0.9 (90%)
- $p+p+n$ three-body breakup contribution is about five times larger than $d+p$ two-body breakup contribution
- Four-body E-CDCC total breakup cross section is twice as large as three-body E-CDCC calculation

## Context

This work extends the four-body CDCC methodology previously developed by Matsumoto et al. (2004, 2006) and the eikonal CDCC approach by Ogata et al. (2003, 2009, 2010) to systematically analyze $t$ and $^3$He breakup reactions for the first time. It contrasts with the earlier three-body treatment by Iseri et al. (1986), which described $^3$He as a $d+p$ two-body system, and demonstrates that such a simplified model underestimates breakup cross sections by a factor of two. The results enable proper interpretation of forthcoming systematic $t$ and $^3$He knockout reaction measurements, which are motivated by predictions of cluster states in medium-heavy nuclei by Typel (2010) and experimental confirmation by Tanaka et al. (2021).
