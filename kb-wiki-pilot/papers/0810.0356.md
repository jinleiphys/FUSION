# Dissipative quantum dynamics in low-energy collisions of complex nuclei

> Model calculations that include the effects of irreversible, environmental couplings on top of a coupled-channels dynamical description of the collision of two complex nuclei are presented. The Liouville-von Neumann equation for the time-evolution of the density matrix of a dissipative system is solved numerically providing a consistent transition from coherent to decoherent (and dissipative) dynamics during the collision. Quantum decoherence and dissipation are clearly manifested in the model calculations. Energy dissipation, due to the irreversible decay of giant-dipole vibrational states of the colliding nuclei, is shown to result in a hindrance of quantum tunneling and fusion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that environment-induced decoherence and energy dissipation, specifically from the irreversible decay of giant-dipole vibrational states of the colliding nuclei, can result in a hindrance of quantum tunneling and fusion at sub-barrier energies, going beyond standard coherent coupled-channels descriptions. The authors show that this effect is spatially distinct from the short-range absorption inside the fusion barrier and can suppress tunneling even when the nuclei are still approaching the barrier.

## Method

The authors solve the Liouville-von Neumann equation for the time-evolution of the reduced density matrix of a dissipative system, using the Lindblad axiomatic approach for open quantum systems. The model couples two intrinsic channels (ground state and giant-dipole resonance excitation) with two auxiliary environmental states representing capture inside the fusion pocket and irreversible decay of the GDR into surrounding complex states. The coupled equations are solved numerically using a Faber polynomial expansion of the time-evolution superoperator and the Fourier method for the kinetic energy commutator, applied to the \(^{16}\)O + \(^{144}\)Sm reaction with zero relative orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The nominal fusion barrier for \(^{16}\)O + \(^{144}\)Sm is \(V_B = 61.1\) MeV
- The GDR excitation energy is \(E_{1^{-}} = 15\) MeV with a spreading width of 6 MeV
- The GDR coupling deformation parameter is \(\beta_1 = 0.2\)
- At \(E_0 = 45\) MeV, including only the capture state \(|X\rangle\) gives purity Tr\([\hat{\rho}^2] = 1.0000\) and energy loss \(\Delta E = 0.0004\) MeV; including both \(|X\rangle\) and \(|Y\rangle\) gives purity 0.9196 and \(\Delta E = 1.8718\) MeV
- At \(E_0 = 55\) MeV, including only \(|X\rangle\) gives purity 0.9996 and \(\Delta E = 0.0109\) MeV; including both \(|X\rangle\) and \(|Y\rangle\) gives purity 0.8759 and \(\Delta E = 3.6100\) MeV
- At \(E_0 = 60\) MeV, including only \(|X\rangle\) gives purity 0.6067 and \(\Delta E = 14.862\) MeV; including both \(|X\rangle\) and \(|Y\rangle\) gives purity 0.5127 and \(\Delta E = 18.908\) MeV
- The absorption rate to state \(|X\rangle\) uses a Fermi function with depth 10 MeV and diffuseness 0.1 fm, located at the pocket radius of approximately 7 fm
- The initial wave-packet has width \(\sigma_0 = 20\) fm, centered at \(r = 150\) fm, with FWHM energy spread of approximately 3%

## Context

This work extends the standard coupled-channels approach (e.g., the code ccfull by Hagino et al.) by incorporating environment-induced irreversibility through the Lindblad formalism, which has previously been applied to nuclear problems such as charge equilibration in deep-inelastic collisions (Sandulescu, Scutaru, Scheid), fission (Stefanescu et al.), and giant resonance decay (Stefanescu, Liotta, Sandulescu). The model addresses the long-standing inability of coherent coupled-channels calculations to simultaneously describe elastic scattering and fusion measurements (Newton, Angeli) and the more recent failure to consistently describe below-barrier tunneling and above-barrier fusion yields (Dasgupta et al.). The results suggest that deep-inelastic processes occurring at sub-barrier energies (Wolfs) can compete with and inhibit quantum tunneling, providing a mechanism for the fusion hindrance phenomenon.
