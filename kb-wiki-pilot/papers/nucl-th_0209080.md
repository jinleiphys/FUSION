# Strange Particles from Dense Hadronic Matter

> After a brief survey of the remarkable accomplishments of the current heavy ion collision experiments up to 200A GeV, we address in depth the role of strange particle production in the search for new phases of matter in these collisions. In particular, we show that the observed enhancement pattern of otherwise rarely produced multistrange antibaryons can be consistently explained assuming color deconfinement in a localized, rapidly disintegrating hadronic source. We develop the theoretical description of this source, and in particular study QCD based processes of strangeness production in the deconfined, thermal quark-gluon plasma phase, allowing for approach to chemical equilibrium and dynamical evolution. We also address thermal charm production. Using a rapid hadronization model we obtain final state particle yields, providing detailed theoretical predictions about strange particle spectra and yields as function of heavy ion energy. Our presentation is comprehensive and self-contained: we introduce in considerable detail the procedures used in data interpretation, discuss the particular importance of selected experimental results and show how they impact the theoretical developments.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement pattern of multistrange antibaryons in 200A GeV heavy ion collisions can be consistently explained by assuming color deconfinement in a localized, rapidly disintegrating hadronic source (a quark-gluon plasma fireball), but this interpretation requires that the transition from the deconfined state to the confined hadronic gas phase occurs sufficiently rapidly to preserve the memory of the high strangeness density from the early phase.

## Method

The authors develop a thermal fireball model for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase, using perturbative QCD expressions for strangeness production via thermal gluon fusion processes, with the running coupling constant determined by renormalization group methods. They apply conservation of energy and baryon number to determine fireball properties at different evolution stages, then use a rapid hadronization model to obtain final state particle yields for all systems studied at CERN SPS and BNL AGS facilities. The theoretical predictions are compared with experimental data from the WA85, WA80, NA35, and NA34/3 collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Inverse slope temperature of strange particle spectra in S–Au collisions at 200A GeV: T = 232 MeV (from WA85 and WA80 data)
- Inverse slope temperature for S–S collisions: T = 210 MeV (based on WA94 results)
- Central rapidity for 200A GeV S–Au/W/Pb interactions: y_CM = 2.6 ± 0.1
- Temperature scale for QGP perturbative calculations: T ≃ 250 MeV, with α_s/π ≤ 0.3
- Proper decay length of Λ hyperon: cτ = 7.9 cm
- Mass of Λ(uds): 1.116 GeV
- Invariant mass window for dilepton signal: M ≃ 2 ± 0.6 GeV (between φ and J/ψ masses)
- Expected enhancement factor for direct photon signal in Pb–Pb compared to S–Pb: factor 4

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier predictions from Raf82 that enhanced production of multistrange antibaryons is specifically related to color deconfinement, and builds on their recent advances in analyze and dynamic studies of strange particle production. The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical framework that contrasts with the hadronic gas (HG) model of Hagedorn and addresses the experimental program at AGS (BNL) and SPS (CERN), with forward-looking predictions for RHIC and LHC energies. The results enable quantitative comparison between QGP-based strangeness production and the observed enhancement pattern seen by NA35 and WA85 collaborations, while showing that 15A GeV BNL data cannot be interpreted in terms of a suddenly disintegrating QGP fireball.
