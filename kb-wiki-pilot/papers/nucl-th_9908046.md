# Imaginary deuteron optical potential due to elastic and inelastic breakup

> The contributions to the reaction cross section from the elastic and inelastic breakup processes, calculated within the post-form distorted wave Born-approximation theory, are used as constraints to determine the contributions to the imaginary part of the deuteron optical potentials (IPDOP) due to the breakup channels. The Coulomb part of this potential due to the elastic breakup process is seen to account for the long range absorption in the optical potential. The nuclear parts of the IPDOP due to the elastic and inelastic breakup modes peak in different regions of the nuclear surface, with the latter being almost an order of magnitude larger than the former. This makes the IPDOP due to the breakup channels determined by us stronger than those calculated earlier ignoring the inelastic breakup mode.

## Key claim

The imaginary part of the deuteron optical potential due to breakup channels is determined by using elastic and inelastic breakup cross sections calculated within post-form distorted wave Born-approximation as constraints, and the resulting potential is stronger than those calculated earlier because the inelastic breakup mode, which dominates the total breakup cross section, is included.

## Method

The authors decompose the total phenomenological deuteron optical potential into a bare potential and a breakup potential, then use unitarity of the S-matrix to split the transmission coefficient into contributions from breakup channels and from all other reaction channels. Breakup cross sections for elastic and inelastic modes are calculated separately within post-form DWBA theory, and the imaginary potentials due to each mode are determined by fitting the calculated partial-wave breakup probabilities using a sum-of-Gaussians parameterization, with the constraint that the breakup potential does not exceed the total imaginary potential at any radius.

## Key numbers

- For 56 MeV deuterons on ⁵¹V: elastic breakup cross section = 122 mb, inelastic (d,p) = 290 mb, inelastic (d,n) = 284 mb, total deuteron breakup = 698 mb
- Total (d,p) breakup cross section (elastic + inelastic) = 412 mb, compared to measured value of 481 mb
- Nuclear dissociation potential peaks around 8.0 fm; inelastic breakup potential peaks around 6.5 fm; both have width approximately 2 fm (FWHM)
- Inelastic breakup potential is approximately an order of magnitude larger than the nuclear dissociation potential
- Coulomb dissociation potential has radial dependence approximately e^{-0.3r}, while Woods-Saxon potentials behave as e^{-2.0r} at large distances
- Adding the Coulomb dissociation potential changes the total reaction cross section by less than 1%

## Context

This work extends the method introduced by Baur, Shyam, Rosel and Trautmann (1980) for using unitarity to separate breakup contributions to the reaction cross section, and applies it to determine the imaginary part of the deuteron optical potential. It contrasts with coupled-channels calculations by Sakuragi, Yahiro and Kamimura (1986) and Yabana, Ogawa and Suzuki (1992), which could not include the inelastic breakup mode and therefore produced weaker imaginary potentials due to breakup channels. The method can be applied to other projectiles with strong breakup channels, such as halo nuclei ¹¹Li and ¹¹Be.
