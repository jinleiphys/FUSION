# $N\bar D $ system: A challenge for $\bar {\rm P}$ANDA

> We study the $N \bar D$ system by means of a chiral constituent quark model. This model, tuned in the description of the baryon and meson spectra as well as the $NN$ interaction, provides parameter-free predictions for the charm -1 two-hadron systems. The presence of a heavy antiquark makes the interaction rather simple. We have found sharp quark-Pauli effects in some particular channels, generating repulsion, due to lacking degrees of freedom to accommodate the light quarks. Our results point to the existence of an attractive state, the $Δ\bar D^*$ with $(T,S)=(1,5/2)$, presenting a resonance close to threshold. It would show up in the scattering of $\bar D$ mesons on nucleons as a D wave state with quantum numbers $(T)J^P=(1)5/2^-$. This resonance resembles our findings in the $ΔΔ$ system, that offered a plausible explanation to the cross section of double-pionic fusion reactions through the so-called {\it ABC} effect. The study of the interaction of $D$ mesons with nucleons is a goal of the $\bar {\rm P}$ANDA Collaboration at the European facility FAIR and, thus, the present theoretical study is a challenge to be tested at the future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of a bound state in the $\Delta \bar D^*$ channel with $(T,S)=(1,5/2)$ and a binding energy of 3.87 MeV, which would appear as a D wave resonance with quantum numbers $(T)J^P=(1)5/2^-$ in the scattering of $\bar D$ mesons on nucleons. This prediction is conditional on the chiral constituent quark model's parameter-free extension to the charm -1 sector, and the authors note that the bound state exists for any reasonable choice of the charm quark wave function parameter.

## Method

The authors employed a chiral constituent quark model previously tuned to describe baryon and meson spectra and the nucleon-nucleon interaction, using a Born-Oppenheimer approximation to derive local baryon-meson potentials from the basic quark-quark interaction. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for negative energies using the Fredholm determinant method, considering coupled channels in the isospin-spin $(T,J)$ basis with S and D wave couplings, and analyzed the effects of quark antisymmetrization through the norm kernel and exchange diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 3.87 MeV for the $\Delta \bar D^*$ state with $(T,S)=(1,5/2)$
- The $\Delta \bar D^*$ channel with $(T,S)=(2,5/2)$ shows Pauli blocking ($C(S,T)=1$), generating strong repulsion
- The $N \bar D^*$ channel with $(T,J)=(0,1/2)$ shows Pauli suppression ($C(S,T)=2/3$), giving strong repulsion at short range
- Predicted $N \bar D$ cross sections are of order 20 mb, roughly a factor two larger than for the $NK$ system
- Quark model parameters: light quark mass $m_{u,d}=313$ MeV, charm quark mass $m_c=1752$ MeV, light quark oscillator parameter $b=0.518$ fm, charm quark oscillator parameter $b_c=0.6$ fm

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model, previously applied to the $NN$ interaction and $\Delta\Delta$ system, to the charm -1 sector of $N\bar D$ and $\Delta\bar D^*$ systems. The predicted $\Delta \bar D^*$ resonance resembles the authors' earlier findings in the $\Delta\Delta$ system (Ref. Val01), which offered a plausible explanation for the cross section of double-pionic fusion reactions through the ABC effect. The study contradicts hadronic model predictions by Yas09 and Yam11, who found the $(T)J^P=(0)1/2^-$ channel to be the most attractive with a bound state of 1.4 MeV; the present work shows that quark-exchange effects make this channel repulsive. The results are intended to be tested by the $\bar{\rm P}$ANDA Collaboration at the FAIR facility.
