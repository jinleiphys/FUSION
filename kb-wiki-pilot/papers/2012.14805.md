# Inclusive Breakup Cross Sections in Reactions Induced by the Nuclides $^6$He and $^{6,7}$Li in the Two-Body Cluster Model

> Calculations of inclusive $α$ particle production are performed to interpret reaction experimental data induced by the two-neutron halo $^6$He and the isotopes $^{6,7}$Li on different target nuclei. We have implemented the zero-range post-form DWBA to compute the elastic and nonelastic breakup cross sections. Integrated cross sections, angular distributions and spectra are presented. Projectiles are approximated as two-body clusters, namely $^6$He as $α$+dineutron and $^{6}$Li ($^{7}$Li) interpreted as $α$+deuteron(triton). The São Paulo optical potential is employed in the calculation of the distorted wave functions for the incident channels, while standard phenomenological interactions are used for the fragment-target interactions. Calculations for $^6$Li and $^7$Li furnish a good description of the data, while the reaction involving $^6$He is found to be a complicated case, where data interpretation is still considered a major obstacle. The present analysis identifies an overall large contribution from inclusive breakup emissions for all the cases studied.

## Key claim

The post-form zero-range DWBA inclusive breakup formalism, combined with the São Paulo optical potential and a two-body cluster model for the projectile, provides a good description of α-particle production data for reactions induced by ⁶Li and ⁷Li, but the description for ⁶He-induced reactions remains incomplete, with data interpretation still considered a major obstacle.

## Method

The authors implemented the zero-range post-form distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV) to compute elastic and nonelastic breakup cross sections for inclusive α-particle production. Projectiles were approximated as two-body clusters: ⁶He as α+dineutron, ⁶Li as α+deuteron, and ⁷Li as α+triton. The São Paulo optical potential (SPP) was used for the incident channel distorted waves, with a reduced diffuseness parameter of 0.65 fm, while standard phenomenological optical potentials (Koning-Delaroche for nucleons, Han-Shi-Shen for deuterons, Becchetti-Greenlees for tritons, Avrigeanu et al. for α particles) were employed for fragment-target interactions in the exit channels.

## Key numbers

- Contact strength D₀ = 70 MeV·fm³/² for ⁶He (α+dineutron model)
- Contact strength D₀ = 45 MeV·fm³/² for ⁶Li (α+deuteron model)
- Contact strength D₀ = 42 MeV·fm³/² for ⁷Li (α+triton model)
- Modified binding energy ε_b = -1.6 MeV used for the ⁶He α+dineutron two-body model (compared to experimental two-neutron separation energy S₂n = 0.975 MeV)
- ⁶Li α+deuteron separation energy ε_b = -1.474 MeV
- ⁷Li α+triton separation energy ε_b = -2.468 MeV
- SPP diffuseness parameter set to a₀ = 0.65 fm for all entrance channels, with imaginary depth W₀ = 0.78 V₀
- Coulomb barrier for ⁶He+¹²⁰Sn approximately V_B ≈ 13.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the post-form DWBA inclusive breakup formalism, previously applied successfully to deuteron breakup by Carlson et al. (2016, 2017), Hussein et al. (2017), and Potel et al. (2017), to reactions induced by weakly bound light nuclei ⁶He, ⁶Li, and ⁷Li. It contrasts with continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) methods, which describe elastic breakup well but do not capture the dominant nonelastic (incomplete fusion) component. The dineutron approximation for ⁶He follows the pioneering idea of Migdal (1972) and recent experimental evidence for dineutron correlations in ¹⁶Be (Spyrou et al., 2012) and ¹¹Li (Kubota et al., 2020; Sun et al., 2021). The results enable future application of the four-body DWBA formalism discussed by Carlson et al. (2017) and extension to more complex projectiles.
