# Breakup of 8B and the S17 astrophysical factor revisited

> Existing experimental data for the breakup of 8B at energies from 30 to 1000 MeV/nucleon on light through heavy targets are analyzed in detail in terms of an extended Glauber model. The predictions of the model are in excellent agreement with independent reaction data (reaction cross sections and parallel momentum distributions for core like fragments). Final state interactions have been included in the Coulomb dissociation component. We extract asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) from which the strophysical factor S17(0) for the key reaction for solar neutrino production, 7Be(p,gamma)8B, can be evaluated. Glauber model calculations using different effective interactions give consistent, though slightly different results. The differences give a measure of the precision one can expect from the method. The unweighted average of all ANCs extracted leads to S17(0)=18.7+/-1.9 eVb. The results of this new analysis are compared with the earlier one. They are consistent with the values from most direct measurements and other indirect methods.

## Key claim

The unweighted average of all asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) extracted from breakup data using three different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions yields S17(0) = 18.7 ± 1.9 eV·b for the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction, but the individual interaction-dependent averages differ by up to 13%, indicating that the precision of this indirect method is limited not only by experimental uncertainties but also by the accuracy of the reaction model calculations.

## Method

Existing experimental data for the breakup of 8B at energies from 30 to 1000 MeV/nucleon on targets ranging from carbon to lead were analyzed using an extended Glauber model that includes final state interactions in the Coulomb dissociation component. The one-proton removal cross sections were calculated as an incoherent superposition of single-particle contributions from the 1p3/2 and 1p1/2 orbitals, and the asymptotic normalization coefficients were extracted by comparing experimental cross sections (corrected for core excitation contributions) with calculations using three different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions: the Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) interaction, a "standard" finite-range interaction, and the Ray interaction.

## Key numbers

- S17(0) = 18.7 ± 1.9 eV·b (unweighted average of all 31 determinations using three different NN interactions)
- C2tot(JLM) = 0.454 ± 0.048 fm-1, leading to S17(0) = 17.5 ± 1.8 eV·b (JLM interaction, data up to 285 MeV/nucleon)
- C2tot("standard") = 0.503 ± 0.032 fm-1 (standard finite-range interaction, 50-1000 MeV/nucleon)
- C2tot("Ray") = 0.517 ± 0.041 fm-1 (Ray interaction, 50-1000 MeV/nucleon)
- Ratio of 1p1/2 to 1p3/2 contributions: A2p1/2/A2p3/2 = C2p1/2/C2p3/2 = 0.13(2) from mirror nucleus 8Li study
- Core excitation contribution ratio: C2e/C2tot = 0.16(4) from 936 MeV/nucleon data (σ(exc.) = 12(3) mb, σ(tot) = 94(9) mb)
- Proton separation energy of 8B: Sp = 0.137 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Trache et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 87, 271102, 2001) by including newer breakup data from Enders et al. (2003) and Warner et al. (2002), incorporating final state interactions in the Coulomb dissociation component, and testing three different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions to assess the systematic uncertainty of the method. The results are consistent with values from most direct measurements and other indirect methods, including transfer reaction ANC determinations (Azhari et al., 1999) and the mirror neutron transfer study (Trache et al., Phys. Rev. C 67, 062801(R), 2003), but disagree with the larger value claimed by Junghans et al. (Phys. Rev. Lett. 88, 041101, 2002). The analysis enables the use of breakup reactions with rare isotope beams produced by fragmentation as a complementary technique to direct measurements for determining astrophysical S-factors.
