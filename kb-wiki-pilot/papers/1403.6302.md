# Microscopic description of $^7$Li in the $^{7}\text{Li}+^{12}\text{C}$ and $^7\text{Li}+^{28}\text{Si}$ elastic scattering at high energies

> We employ a microscopic continuum-discretized coupled-channels reaction framework (MCDCC) to study the elastic angular distribution of the $^7$Li$=α+t$ nucleus colliding with $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si targets at $E_{\text{Lab}}$=350 MeV. In this framework, the $^7$Li projectile is described in a microscopic cluster model and impinges on non-composite targets. The diagonal and coupling potentials are constructed from nucleon-target interactions and $^7$Li microscopic wave functions. We obtain a fair description of the experimental data, in the whole angular range studied, when continuum channels are included. The inelastic and breakup angular distributions on the lightest target are also investigated. In addition, we compute $^{7}$Li$+^{12}$C MCDCC elastic cross sections at energies much higher than the Coulomb barrier and we use them as reference calculations to test the validity of multichannel eikonal cross sections.

## Key claim

The microscopic continuum-discretized coupled-channels (MCDCC) framework provides a fair description of the experimental elastic scattering data for \(^{7}\text{Li}+^{12}\text{C}\) and \(^{7}\text{Li}+^{28}\text{Si}\) at \(E_{\text{Lab}}=350\) MeV only when continuum channels are included, but the predictions overestimate the data by about a factor of 2 at larger angles (\(\theta > 15^\circ\) for \(^{12}\text{C}\) and \(\theta > 10^\circ\) for \(^{28}\text{Si}\)).

## Method

The \(^{7}\text{Li}\) projectile is described microscopically as an \(\alpha + t\) cluster using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction, producing bound states and pseudostates at positive energies. The diagonal and coupling potentials are constructed by folding nucleon-target optical potentials (from Koning and Delaroche for \(^{28}\text{Si}\) and Weppner et al. for \(^{12}\text{C}\)) with the microscopic transition densities of the projectile. The coupled-channel equations are solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange-Legendre mesh, with partial waves summed up to \(J_{\text{max}}=200\) and a channel radius \(a=18\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Incident laboratory energy: \(E_{\text{Lab}}=350\) MeV for both \(^{7}\text{Li}+^{12}\text{C}\) and \(^{7}\text{Li}+^{28}\text{Si}\) systems.
- Convergence of the CDCC calculations is reached at a maximum projectile angular momentum \(J_{\text{Pmax}}=7/2\) and a cutoff excitation energy \(E_{\text{max}}=15\) MeV (measured from the \(\alpha+t\) threshold).
- The improvement from including breakup channels relative to single-channel calculations is about a factor of 2 for \(^{12}\text{C}\) and a factor of 4 for \(^{28}\text{Si}\) at large angles.
- The sensitivity of the \(^{7}\text{Li}+^{28}\text{Si}\) elastic cross section to the choice of nucleon-target potential is about 20% at \(\theta > 7^\circ\).
- For the eikonal-CDCC test, agreement between CDCC and eikonal-CDCC cross sections improves to within less than 0.15 (in units of \(\sigma/\sigma_R\)) at \(E_{\text{Lab}}=1050\) MeV, but the influence of breakup channels becomes very small at that energy.

## Context

This work extends the MCDCC method previously applied to \(^{7}\text{Li}+^{208}\text{Pb}\) at near-barrier energies (Ref. [DH13]) to lighter targets (\(^{12}\text{C}\) and \(^{28}\text{Si}\)) and higher incident energy (350 MeV). It provides a test of the multichannel eikonal method (Ref. [OY03]) by comparing eikonal-CDCC and exact CDCC calculations at 350, 700, and 1050 MeV, showing that the eikonal approximation requires incident energies of at least \(\sim 1000\) MeV to be valid, at which point breakup effects are negligible. The framework enables future studies of reactions involving exotic nuclei such as Borromean nuclei.
