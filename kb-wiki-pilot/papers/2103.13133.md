# Updated systematics of intermediate-energy single-nucleon removal cross sections

> The body of experimental measurements of intermediate-energy reactions that remove a single nucleon from a secondary beam of neutron- or proton-rich nuclei continues to grow. These data have been analysed consistently using an approximate, eikonal-model treatment of the reaction dynamics combined with appropriate shell-model descriptions of the projectile initial state, the bound final states spectrum of the reaction residue and single-particle removal strengths computed from their wave-function overlaps. The systematics of the ratio $R_s$ of the measured inclusive cross-section to all bound final states and the calculated cross-section to bound shell-model states -- in different regions of the nuclear chart and involving both very weakly-bound and strongly-bound valence nucleons -- is important in relating the empirically deduced orbital occupancies to those from the best available shell-model predictions. Importantly, several new higher-energy measurements, for which the sudden-approximation aspect of the dynamical description is placed on an even stronger footing, now supplement the previously-analysed measurements. These additional data sets are discussed. Their $R_s$ values are shown to conform to and reinforce the earlier-observed systematics, with no indication that the approximately linear reduction in $R_s$ with increasing nucleon separation energy is a consequence of a breakdown of the sudden approximation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the ratio \(R_s\) of measured to calculated inclusive single-nucleon removal cross sections, when plotted against the neutron-proton separation energy asymmetry \(\Delta S\), follows the same approximately linear trend for new higher-energy data (up to 1.6 GeV/nucleon) as for earlier lower-energy measurements, with no evidence that the previously observed reduction in \(R_s\) with increasing \(\Delta S\) arises from a breakdown of the sudden approximation at the lower beam energies.

## Method

The authors reanalyze a body of single-nucleon removal reaction data from secondary beams of neutron- or proton-rich nuclei using an eikonal-model treatment of the reaction dynamics combined with shell-model descriptions of the projectile initial state and the bound final states of the residue. The theoretical inclusive cross section \(\sigma_\text{th}\) is computed as the sum of partial cross sections to all bound shell-model states, using single-particle cross sections from elastic and inelastic breakup mechanisms, and the ratio \(R_s = \sigma_\text{exp}/\sigma_\text{th}\) is evaluated as a function of the asymmetry parameter \(\Delta S\). New data sets at 220-240 MeV/nucleon and 1.6 GeV/nucleon are added to the existing compilation and analyzed with the same methodology.

## Key numbers

* The trend line for the entire data collection is \(R_s = 0.61 - 0.016\,\Delta S\), with a scatter of about 0.1 in \(R_s\) independent of \(\Delta S\).
* For the \(^{25}\text{F}(-p)\) reaction at 218 MeV/nucleon on a carbon target, \(R_s = 0.48(5)\) with \(\Delta S = 10.17\) MeV.
* For the \(^{16}\text{C}(-p)\) reaction, \(R_s = 0.34(4)\) with \(\Delta S = 18.1\) MeV.
* The collision time at 100 MeV/nucleon is of order \(8 \times d \times 10^{-24}\) s (with \(d\) in fm), and at 1.6 GeV/nucleon (\(v/c \approx 0.92\)) it is reduced by more than a factor of 2 relative to 100 MeV/nucleon.
* The fraction of the removal cross section due to elastic breakup ranges from 40% to 17% in cases where the relative yields have been distinguished by measurements.

## Context

This work extends the systematics first compiled in Ref. [Gad08] and subsequently enhanced in Ref. [TG], which documented the approximately linear reduction of \(R_s\) with increasing \(\Delta S\) across the nuclear chart. The new higher-energy data points, including measurements at RIKEN (RIBF), the Institute of Modern Physics in Lanzhou, and GSI Darmstadt, reinforce these earlier trends and rule out a breakdown of the sudden approximation as the cause. The results enable future comparisons with alternative reaction probes such as single-nucleon transfer reactions, \((p,2p)\) and \((p,pn)\) knockout, and intra-nuclear-cascade model calculations, and motivate further investigation into the role of short-range correlations and shell-model truncation effects on the absolute cross sections.
