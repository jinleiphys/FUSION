# Three-body description of direct nuclear reactions: Comparison with the continuum discretized coupled channels method

> The continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method is compared to the exact solution of the three-body Faddeev equations in momentum space. We present results for: i) elastic and breakup observables of d-12C at E_d=56 MeV, ii) elastic scattering of d-58Ni at E_d=80 MeV, and iii) elastic, breakup and transfer observables for 11Be+p at E_{11Be}/A=38.4 MeV. Our comparative studies show that, in the first two cases, the CDCC method is a good approximation to the full three-body Faddeev solution, but for the 11Be exotic nucleus, depending on the observable or the kinematic regime, it may miss out some of the dynamic three-body effects that appear through the explicit coupling to the transfer channel.

## Key claim

The continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method is a good approximation to the exact three-body Faddeev solution for deuteron-induced reactions on stable targets (d-12C and d-58Ni), but for reactions involving the exotic halo nucleus 11Be, CDCC may miss some dynamic three-body effects that appear through explicit coupling to the transfer channel, depending on the observable or kinematic regime.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas (AGS) form of the three-body Faddeev equations in momentum space with non-separable potentials, using a screening and renormalization technique to handle the Coulomb interaction between charged particles. They compared these exact three-body results to CDCC calculations performed with two different Jacobi coordinate choices: the standard breakup (CDCC-BU) approach using projectile continuum states, and the transfer-to-continuum (CDCC-TR*) approach using the continuum of the final composite system. The three test cases were treated as effective three-body problems with inert cores: p+n+12C, p+n+58Ni, and 10Be+n+p.

## Key numbers

- d-12C elastic scattering at E_d = 56 MeV: CDCC reproduces exact three-body results up to very large scattering angles; agreement with data at forward angles (theta_CM < 60 degrees) but deteriorates at backward angles
- d-58Ni elastic scattering at E_d = 80 MeV: CDCC is a good approximation to the full three-body Faddeev solution
- 11Be+p at E_{11Be}/A = 38.4 MeV: CDCC may miss dynamic three-body effects depending on observable and kinematic regime
- For d-12C breakup: CDCC calculations required np partial waves l <= 8, bins up to E_max = 46 MeV, and total angular momentum J <= 60
- For d-58Ni: CDCC included np partial waves l <= 2, continuum truncated at E_max = 30-50 MeV, J <= 100
- For 11Be+p: CDCC-BU required n-10Be partial waves up to l_max = 8, Q_max = 8, bins up to E_max = 34 MeV; CDCC-TR* used np partial waves l <= 8, bins up to E_max = 35 MeV

## Context

This work provides the first direct numerical comparison between the CDCC method and exact solutions of the three-body Faddeev/AGS equations for nuclear reactions, building on recent developments in treating Coulomb interactions within the AGS framework by Deltuva et al. (2005-2006). The study extends earlier formal discussions by Austern et al. (1989, 1996) that argued CDCC should approach the exact solution as model space increases, but lacked numerical verification. The results enable benchmarking of CDCC calculations used extensively for reactions with radioactive nuclear beams (e.g., Tostevin 2001, Moro 2003, Shrivastava 2004), and address concerns raised by Moro et al. (2006) about discrepancies between CDCC-BU and CDCC-TR* approaches for the 11Be+p system.
