# Measurement of transparency ratios for protons from short-range correlated pairs

> Nuclear transparency, Tp(A), is a measure of the average probability for a struck proton to escape the nucleus without significant re-interaction. Previously, nuclear transparencies were extructed for quasi-elastic A(e,e'p) knockout of protons with momentum below the Fermi momentum, where the spectral functions are well known. In this paper we extract a novel observable, the transparency ratio, Tp(A)/T_p(12C), for knockout of high-missing-momentum protons from the breakup of short range correlated pairs (2N-SRC) in Al, Fe and Pb nuclei relative to C. The ratios were measured at momentum transfer Q^2 > 1.5 (GeV/c)^2 and x_B > 1.2 where the reaction is expected to be dominated by electron scattering from 2N-SRC. The transparency ratios of the knocked-out protons coming from 2N-SRC breakup are 20 - 30% lower than those of previous results for low missing momentum. They agree with Glauber calculations and agree with renormalization of the previously published transparencies as proposed by recent theoretical investigations. The new transparencies scale as A^-1/3, which is consistent with dominance of scattering from nucleons at the nuclear surface.

## Key claim

The transparency ratios for protons knocked out from short-range correlated pairs (2N-SRC) in Al, Fe, and Pb relative to C are 20-30% lower than previously published transparency ratios for mean-field protons, supporting the claim that earlier mean-field transparencies were overestimated due to incorrect correction factors for 2N-SRC strength.

## Method

The experiment used a 5.014 GeV electron beam at Jefferson Lab Hall B with the CLAS detector to measure semi-exclusive A(e,e'p) events from solid targets of C, Al, Fe, and Pb. Events were selected in kinematics dominated by scattering from 2N-SRC pairs: momentum transfer Q^2 >= 1.5 (GeV/c)^2, Bjorken scaling variable x_B >= 1.2, and missing momentum between 300 and 600 MeV/c. The transparency ratio T_p(A)/T_p(12C) was extracted from the ratio of measured cross sections per nucleon, scaled by the relative number of 2N-SRC pairs per nucleon in each nucleus (a_2 factors taken from world data on inclusive (e,e') cross section ratios).

## Key numbers

- Average transparency ratios: T_p(Al/C) = 0.776 +/- 0.019 (stat) +/- 0.043 (sys); T_p(Fe/C) = 0.579 +/- 0.010 (stat) +/- 0.036 (sys); T_p(Pb/C) = 0.385 +/- 0.010 (stat) +/- 0.034 (sys)
- The a_2 scaling factors used: a_2(Al/C) = a_2(Fe/C) = 1.100 +/- 0.055; a_2(Pb/C) = 1.080 +/- 0.054
- A-dependence exponent: alpha = -0.34 +/- 0.02, consistent with A^{-1/3} scaling and with the Glauber result of alpha = -0.322 +/- 0.007
- The mean-field transparency ratio exponent was alpha = -0.22 +/- 0.01 (from Garrow02)
- Radiative correction to transparency ratios was approximately 7% for all ratios

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of nuclear transparency for quasi-elastic A(e,e'p) knockout of mean-field protons (with momentum below Fermi momentum) from experiments by O'Neill et al. (1995), Garino et al. (1992), Abbott et al. (1998), and Garrow et al. (2002). The new results directly address the discrepancy between those published transparencies and Glauber calculations by Frankfurt et al. (2000) and Lava et al. (2004), who argued that the earlier PWIA calculations overcorrected for 2N-SRC effects. The measured A^{-1/3} scaling is consistent with dominance of scattering from nucleons at the nuclear surface, as expected from the Lava et al. (2004) attenuation model. This enables improved extraction of nuclear transparencies and better understanding of final-state interactions in nuclei.
