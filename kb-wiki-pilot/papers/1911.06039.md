# Fusion of Borromean nucleus $^{9}$Be with $^{197}$Au target at near barrier energies

> To probe the role of the intrinsic structure of the projectile on sub-barrier fusion, measurement of fusion cross sections has been carried out in $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system in the energy range E$_{c.m.}$/V$_B$ $\approx$ 0.82 to 1.16 using off-beam gamma counting method. Measured fusion excitation function has been analyzed in the framework of the coupled-channel approach using CCFULL code. It is observed that the coupled-channel calculations, including couplings to the inelastic state of the target and the first two states of the rotational band built on the ground state of the projectile, provide a very good description of the sub-barrier fusion data. At above barrier energies, the fusion cross section is found to be suppressed by $\approx$ 39(2)\% as compared to the coupled-channel prediction. A comparison of reduced excitation function of $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au with other $x$ + $^{197}$Au shows a larger enhancement for $^9$Be in the sub-barrier region amongst Z=2-5 weakly and tightly bound projectiles, which indicates the prominent role of the projectile deformation in addition to the weak binding.

# Fusion of Borromean nucleus \(^{9}\)Be with \(^{197}\)Au target at near barrier energies

## Key claim

The coupled-channel calculations including couplings to the inelastic state of the target and the first two states of the rotational band built on the ground state of the projectile provide a very good description of the sub-barrier fusion data, but at above-barrier energies the fusion cross section is suppressed by approximately 39(2)% compared to the coupled-channel prediction. Among Z=2-5 weakly and tightly bound projectiles on \(^{197}\)Au, \(^{9}\)Be shows the largest sub-barrier fusion enhancement, which the authors attribute to the prominent role of projectile deformation in addition to weak binding.

## Method

Fusion cross sections were measured for the \(^{9}\)Be + \(^{197}\)Au system in the energy range E\(_{c.m.}\)/V\(_B\) ≈ 0.82 to 1.16 using the off-beam gamma counting method with HPGe detectors at the Pelletron Linac facility at TIFR, Mumbai. Evaporation residues \(^{203}\)Bi (3n) and \(^{202}\)Bi (4n) were identified by their characteristic gamma rays and half-lives, and the measured cross sections were corrected for missing channels (2n, 5n, fission) using the statistical model code PACE2 with Ignatyuk level density prescription. The fusion excitation function was analyzed using the coupled-channel code CCFULL, including couplings to the first two excited states of the ground-state rotational band of \(^{9}\)Be (deformation parameter \(\beta_2 = 1.3\)) and the first excited state of \(^{197}\)Au (\(\beta = 0.1\)).

## Key numbers

- Energy range: E\(_{c.m.}\) = 31.6 to 44.7 MeV, corresponding to E\(_{c.m.}\)/V\(_B\) ≈ 0.82 to 1.16, with V\(_B\) = 38.4 MeV
- Complete fusion cross sections measured from 0.021 ± 0.004 mb at 31.6 MeV to 367 ± 7 mb at 44.7 MeV
- Suppression factor S = 0.61(2), corresponding to 39 ± 2% suppression of complete fusion at above-barrier energies relative to coupled-channel predictions
- Maximum correction for missing channels (2n, 5n, fission) was approximately 4% at the extreme energy points
- For \(^{9}\)Be, the \(\alpha + \alpha + n\) break-up threshold energy is 1.575 MeV

## Context

This work extends systematic fusion studies of weakly bound projectiles on \(^{197}\)Au targets, previously reported for \(^{6}\)He (Penionzhkevich et al., 2007), \(^{8}\)He (Lemasson et al., 2009), \(^{6,7}\)Li (Palshetkar et al., 2014), and \(^{11}\)B (Shrivastava et al., 2017). The observed 39% suppression is consistent with the 25-40% range reported for \(^{9}\)Be on other heavy targets (Dasgupta et al., 2004; Dasgupta et al., 2010; Fang et al., 2015). The finding that \(^{9}\)Be shows the largest sub-barrier enhancement among Z=2-5 projectiles, even exceeding that of halo nuclei \(^{6,8}\)He, enables further investigation of the interplay between deformation and weak binding in sub-barrier fusion dynamics.
