# Reduced basis emulator for elastic scattering in continuum-discretized coupled-channel calculations

> I develop a reduced basis emulator for continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations that achieves speedups of $\sim 10^2$ while maintaining sub-percent accuracy. The emulator is constructed using the proper orthogonal decomposition (POD) method applied to snapshots of CDCC solutions computed at sampled points in the optical potential parameter space. The prediction is performed via Galerkin projection onto the reduced basis. I demonstrate the method using deuteron scattering on $^{58}$Ni at 21.6 MeV as a test case, emulating 18 optical potential parameters simultaneously. The emulator reproduces elastic scattering cross sections with errors below 0.1\% across a wide parameter range. This development enables efficient uncertainty quantification and Bayesian parameter estimation for nuclear reaction calculations that were previously computationally prohibitive.

## Key claim

The reduced basis emulator for continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) calculations achieves speedups of approximately 200 times while maintaining sub-percent accuracy, but this performance is demonstrated only for deuteron scattering on $^{58}$Ni at 21.6 MeV with 18 optical potential parameters, and the emulator accuracy depends on the training set size and the specific parameter range covered.

## Method

The emulator is constructed using proper orthogonal decomposition (POD) applied to snapshots of CDCC solution vectors computed at Latin hypercube sampled points in the 18-dimensional optical potential parameter space. The reduced basis is extracted via singular value decomposition of the snapshot matrix, retaining basis vectors that capture more than $1 - \epsilon_{\text{tol}}$ of the variance. Predictions for new parameter values are performed via Galerkin projection of the full linear system onto the reduced basis, exploiting the decomposition of the system matrix into parameter-independent and parameter-dependent components to enable rapid online evaluation.

## Key numbers

- Speedup of approximately 200 times for online evaluations compared to full CDCC calculations
- Emulator reproduces elastic scattering cross sections with errors below 0.1% across a wide parameter range
- Relative errors in $|S_{11}^J|$ remain below 1% for all partial waves and test cases, with most partial waves showing errors in the range $10^{-4}$ to $10^{-2}$%
- Training uses either $N_s = 200$ or $N_s = 400$ Latin hypercube samples in the 18-dimensional parameter space
- System dimension per partial wave is approximately $N_{\text{tot}} = 6660$ (37 channels times 180 radial mesh points)
- Reduced basis dimension $n_b$ is typically 5 to 50 basis vectors
- Calculation uses $J_{\max} = 30$ partial waves and $R_{\max} = 100$ fm with $N = 180$ Lagrange mesh points
- Tested on deuteron scattering on $^{58}$Ni at $E_{\text{lab}} = 21.6$ MeV

## Context

This work extends reduced basis emulation techniques, previously applied to bound-state calculations via eigenvector continuation (Frame et al., 2018; Konig et al., 2020) and to scattering problems via the Kohn variational principle (Furnstahl et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2022), R-matrix theory (Bai et al., 2021, 2022), and complex scaling (Liu et al., 2024), to the more challenging CDCC framework with tens of coupled channels. The emulator enables efficient uncertainty quantification and Bayesian parameter estimation for nuclear reaction calculations that were previously computationally prohibitive, as Bayesian inference typically requires $10^4$ to $10^6$ forward model evaluations.
