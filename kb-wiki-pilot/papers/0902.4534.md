# Nuclear Physics with Electroweak Probes

> In recent years, the italian theoretical Nuclear Physics community has played a leading role in the development of a unified approach, allowing for a consistent and fully quantitative description of the nuclear response to electromagnetic and weak probes. In this paper I review the main achievements in both fields, point out some of the open problems, and outline the most promising prospects.

# Nuclear Physics with Electroweak Probes

## Key claim

The Italian theoretical nuclear physics community has developed a unified approach for a consistent and fully quantitative description of the nuclear response to both electromagnetic and weak probes, but the paper acknowledges that a quantitative understanding of final state interactions is required to unambiguously identify correlation effects in the region of low energy loss, and that the assumption of cancellation of FSI effects in cross section ratios remains controversial.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to inclusive electron-nucleus scattering using the Impulse Approximation with spectral functions, incorporating short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations and final state interactions through a convolution formalism. For neutrino-nucleus scattering, the author compares the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model with spectral function-based approaches and superscaling analyses, validating nuclear models by comparing theoretical results to electron scattering data from SLAC, JLab, and Frascati.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum of nucleons in nuclei: approximately 250 MeV
- Fermi energy: approximately 30 MeV
- Observed attenuation of outgoing proton flux in (e,e'p) measurements: 20-40% in Carbon, 50-70% in Gold
- Energy shift from FSI: approximately 10 MeV, barely visible on energy loss scale at momentum transfers above 1 GeV
- Nuclear effect on total quasi-elastic neutrino cross section: approximately 20% suppression when using realistic spectral function compared to RFGM
- Neutrino energy range for long baseline experiments: several hundred MeV to approximately 1.5 GeV
- Beam energies for electron scattering comparisons: 3.6 GeV (SLAC iron data), 5.8 GeV (JLab carbon data), 1.2 GeV and 880 MeV (Frascati oxygen data)
- Momentum transfer range for y-scaling analysis: approximately 1 GeV to approximately 3.5 GeV
- Cross section ratios R_A for A=4, 12, and 56 measured in range 1 < x < 2.8

## Context

This review extends the long-standing work of the Italian theoretical community on electron-nucleus scattering, generalizing approaches to neutrino scattering for applications in neutrino oscillation experiments (NUINT workshops, Refs. NUINT07, K2K, BOONE). The paper builds on prior work by Benhar (benhar89, benhar91, benhar94, benhar05, benhar06, benhar07, benhar08), Sick (sick80) on y-scaling, Donnelly (donnelly99) on superscaling, and the Pavia Group (cm1, cm2) on orthogonalization procedures. The results enable more accurate predictions of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for long baseline oscillation experiments and supernova simulations, while also informing the ongoing debate about extracting correlation information from cross section ratios proposed by Egiyan (egiyan03, egiyan06).
