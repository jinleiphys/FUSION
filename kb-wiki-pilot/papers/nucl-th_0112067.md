# Total Photoabsorption Cross Sections of A=6 Nuclei with Complete Final State Interaction

> The total photoabsorption cross sections of 6He and 6Li are calculated microscopically with full inclusion of the six-nucleon final state interaction using semirealistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method and the effective interaction approach for the hyperspherical formalism are employed. While 6Li has a single broad giant resonance peak, there are two well separated peaks for 6He corresponding to the breakup of the neutron halo and the alpha core, respectively. The comparison with the few available experimental data is discussed.

## Key claim

The first microscopic calculation of a six-body inclusive reaction (total photoabsorption on 6He and 6Li) with complete final state interaction, but using only semirealistic central S-wave nucleon-nucleon potentials (MTI-III and MN) rather than fully realistic forces including P-wave interactions, which likely explains the missing strength at very low energies compared to experiment.

## Method

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method was applied to avoid explicit calculation of the six-nucleon final state wave function, solving instead a bound-state-like Schrodinger equation. The ground state and Lorentz states were expanded in hyperspherical harmonics (HH) using the effective interaction (EIHH) approach, with the LIT evaluated via the Lanczos algorithm as a continued fraction. The dipole operator in the Siegert theorem was used for the photoabsorption transition, and calculations were performed for both 6He and 6Li with two semirealistic potentials (MTI-III with Yukawa-type potentials and strong short-range repulsion; MN with Gauss-type potentials and a softer core).

## Key numbers

- 6He low-energy peak (halo breakup) at ω = 7.5 MeV (MN potential) and 9.5 MeV (MTI-III potential)
- 6He high-energy peak (alpha core breakup) at approximately ω = 35 MeV, with about 40% of strength from the T=2 channel
- 6Li single broad giant resonance peak with no substructure
- Enhancements κ_TRK of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn value (59.74 NZ/A MeVmb): 0.42 (6Li, MN), 0.47 (6Li, MTI-III), 0.45 (6He, MN), 0.50 (6He, MTI-III)
- Ground state binding energies: 6He MN -30.48(10) MeV, 6He MTI-III -31.87(10) MeV; 6Li MN -34.90(10) MeV, 6Li MTI-III -35.88(20) MeV
- Ground state matter radii: 6He MN 2.37(4) fm, 6He MTI-III 2.23(4) fm; 6Li MN 2.18(2) fm, 6Li MTI-III 2.13(2) fm
- Convergence errors estimated at less than 10%, affecting mostly peak heights rather than general shape

## Context

This work extends the LIT method, previously applied to few-body nuclei up to A=4 (see Refs. ELO97a, ELO97b, Bar01, ELOT00), to the first microscopic calculation of an inelastic six-body reaction with complete final state interaction. It contrasts with cluster model descriptions of 6He using an inert alpha core with P-wave potentials (Cobis, Fedorov, and Jensen 1997; Danilin et al. 1998) which achieve better agreement near threshold. The results enable comparison with experimental data from 6He Coulomb excitation (Aumann et al. 1998) and 6Li(7Li,7Be)6He experiments (Nakayama et al. 2000), and motivate further theoretical work incorporating P-wave interactions and additional experimental measurements.
