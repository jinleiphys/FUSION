# Status on $\mathbf{{}^{12}{\rm C}+{}^{12}{\rm C}}$ fusion at deep subbarrier energies: impact of resonances on astrophysical $S^{*}$ factors

> Since the discovery of molecular resonances in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C in the early sixties a great deal of research work has been undertaken to study $α$-clustering and resonant effects of the fusion process at sub-Coulomb barrier energies. The modified astrophysical $S^{*}$ factors of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion have been extracted from direct fusion measurements at deep sub-barrier energies near the Gamow window. They were also obtained by the indirect Trojan horse method (THM). A comparison of direct measurements and the THM, which elucidates problems in the analysis of the THM, is discussed in this Letter to the Editor.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Trojan Horse Method (THM) data for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion $S^{*}$ factor, as presented in Tumino et al. (Nature, 2018), disagree with direct measurements and model calculations, and that the steep rise of the THM $S^{*}$ factor at low energies is an artifact of using a plane-wave approximation (PWA) which neglects Coulomb and nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors compare direct fusion measurements of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C from Spillane et al. (2007) and Jiang et al. (2018) with indirect THM data from Tumino et al. (2018). They perform a $\chi^{2}$ test on the $S^{*}$ factors in the energy interval 2.16–2.7 MeV, and critically review the THM analysis, specifically its use of the plane-wave approximation (PWA) instead of the distorted-wave-Born approximation (DWBA). They also calculate off-shell factors for the THM reaction ${}^{12}{\rm C}({}^{14}{\rm N},d){}^{24}{\rm Mg}$ to demonstrate the kinematical enhancement of high-spin resonances.

## Key numbers

- The reduced $\chi^{2}$ test comparing THM data (Tumino et al.) with direct data (Spillane and Jiang) in the energy interval 2.16–2.7 MeV yields $\chi^{2}=4.76$; for $\chi^{2}>2.226$, the probability of coincidence is only 0.1%.
- The THM $S^{*}$ factors are about 30% higher than the total $S^{*}$ obtained by direct measurement due to a normalization mistake (normalizing to the $\alpha_1$ channel instead of the total).
- Normalizing the THM result by a factor of 0.4 reduces the reduced $\chi^{2}$ to 0.72 in the 2.16–2.7 MeV interval, but increases the discrepancy in the normalization interval 2.50–2.63 MeV ($\chi^{2}=1.67$).
- The THM data show a strong resonance at 0.88 MeV and another at 1.5 MeV, which are below the lowest directly measured energy of about 2.1 MeV.
- The deuteron energy in the THM reaction is 1.47 MeV for a $^{24}$Mg resonance at $E=2.1$ MeV and 0.97 MeV for $E=2.6$ MeV, both well below the Coulomb barrier of 3 MeV in the $d+{}^{24}{\rm Mg}$ system.
- The DWBA cross section for the transfer reaction drops by two orders of magnitude over the 2.1–2.64 MeV interval.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate on the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion cross section at deep subbarrier energies, which is critical for stellar burning scenarios including massive stars, type Ia supernovae, and superbursts. It directly contradicts the THM results of Tumino et al. (Nature, 2018), which reported a sharp rise in the $S^{*}$ factor at low energies, and instead supports the upper limit based on CC-M3Y+Rep calculations (Esbensen et al., 2011) and the trend from direct measurements (Spillane et al., 2007; Jiang et al., 2018). The paper also notes that new direct data from Notre Dame University (Wiescher, 2019, private communication) show even stronger disagreement with the THM results. The discovery of resonances at 0.88 and 1.5 MeV in the THM data is acknowledged as an important achievement, but the authors call for new indirect measurements (e.g., at Texas A&M University) and improved theoretical models to resolve the discrepancies.
