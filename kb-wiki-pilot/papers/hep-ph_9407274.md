## Key claim

The paper argues that color coherent phenomena in QCD, including color transparency, color opacity, and color fluctuations, can be understood through analogies with QED coherence effects, but that the non-Abelian nature of QCD and the interplay between perturbative and non-perturbative regimes introduce important differences that must be accounted for when interpreting experimental data at currently accessible energies.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review that develops the theoretical framework of color coherence using the scattering eigenstates formalism, where hadrons are described as superpositions of configurations with different spatial sizes and interaction cross sections. They apply this formalism to analyze deep inelastic scattering at small x, hard diffractive processes (including vector meson electroproduction and pion diffraction into two jets), soft diffractive physics, nuclear quasielastic reactions, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, comparing QCD predictions with available experimental data from BNL, FNAL, CERN, and HERA.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length in deep inelastic scattering: \( l_c = 1/(2 M_N x) \approx (0.1/x) \) fm, where \( M_N \) is the nucleon mass
- Probability for a nucleon to be in a configuration with cross section \( \sigma \le 5 \) mb: approximately 2% for the pion
- Ratio of single diffraction to elastic cross section at t=0 for pp scattering: \( d\sigma^{single\ diff}/dt \div d\sigma^{el}/dt = 0.2-0.3 \)
- Expansion time estimate for a point-like configuration: \( \tau \le (E/1\ \text{GeV}) \) fm, giving about 5 fm for E ≈ 5 GeV
- Minimum momentum transfer for producing small configurations via non-perturbative effects: approximately 1-2 (GeV/c)^2
- Fraction of deep inelastic ep events at HERA with large rapidity gap: at least 5% for Q^2 ≥ 10 GeV^2

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on color transparency by Mueller (1982) and Brodsky (1982), and builds upon the two-gluon exchange model of Low (1975) and Gunion and Soper (1977). It synthesizes developments in the Gribov Reggeon calculus, the scattering eigenstates formalism of Feinberg and Pomeranchuk (1956) and Good and Walker (1960), and recent QCD calculations of hard diffractive processes. The paper enables future experimental searches for color transparency at CEBAF, BNL, FNAL, and HERA, and provides a framework for interpreting heavy ion collision data at RHIC and LHC energies.
