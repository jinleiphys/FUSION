# Overview of recent UPC measurements

> This contribution presents an overview of recent measurements of photon-induced processes in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) performed with the ALICE experiment at the LHC. Results from Run~2 include detailed studies of exclusive vector meson photoproduction in Pb-Pb collisions. Measurements of incoherent J/$ψ$ photoproduction as a function of energy and the Mandelstam-$|t|$ variable, combined with electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) classification, show a suppression of the energy evolution at large $|t|$, favouring saturation-based descriptions of the gluon structure. Complementary measurements of proton emission in Pb-Pb UPCs constrain nuclear breakup mechanisms and provide a handle on collision geometry through EMD tagging. Coherent $ρ^{0}$ photoproduction exhibits impact-parameter-dependent azimuthal anisotropy consistent with quantum interference effects, while the first polarisation measurement of coherently photoproduced J/$ψ$ confirms $s$-channel helicity conservation. Additional measurements of exclusive multi-hadron photoproduction, including four-pion and charged kaon pair final states, probe resonance contributions and light vector meson couplings to photons and nuclear targets. Run~3 data enable the study of inclusive photonuclear interactions. Measurements of inclusive open charm photoproduction constrain the gluon content of nuclei in the perturbative QCD regime. Studies of identified hadron production and baryon-to-meson ratios in photonuclear collisions provide new information on particle production mechanisms and possible collective effects in photon-induced systems. Photon-photon interactions are also studied through performance and sensitivity analyses for the measurement of the anomalous magnetic moment of the tau lepton, together with projections for future measurements with ALICE~3.

# Overview of recent UPC measurements

## Key claim

Measurements of incoherent J/ψ photoproduction as a function of energy and Mandelstam-|t|, combined with electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) classification, show a suppression of the energy evolution at large |t|, favouring saturation-based descriptions of the gluon structure over descriptions based solely on nuclear shadowing.

## Method

ALICE measured exclusive vector meson photoproduction in Pb-Pb ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC, separating coherent and incoherent contributions based on transverse momentum distributions. Electromagnetic dissociation classes, determined by forward neutron and proton detection in Zero Degree Calorimeters, provided experimental access to the impact parameter of the collision for geometry-dependent studies. Inclusive photonuclear interactions were studied using single-rapidity-gap topologies enabled by the upgraded forward detector systems in Run 3.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for production of gold nuclei via emission of three protons from 208Pb: σ_3p = 6.8 ± 2.2 b, comparable to the total inelastic Pb-Pb hadronic cross section at the LHC
- Bjorken-x values probed by J/ψ photoproduction: down to approximately 10^-5
- Expected reconstructed events in Run 3 Pb-Pb for the e+μ/π topology in γγ → τ+τ−: approximately 3.6 × 10^4 events with integrated luminosity of 2.3 nb^-1
- Coherent ρ0 photoproduction exhibits a cos(2φ) modulation that increases by nearly an order of magnitude from large to small impact parameters

## Context

This work extends the ALICE UPC physics program from Run 2 exclusive measurements into Run 3 inclusive photonuclear studies, building on prior measurements of coherent and incoherent J/ψ photoproduction at midrapidity and forward rapidity. The results provide constraints on nuclear parton distribution functions and gluon saturation models such as the Color Glass Condensate, while the observation of strangeness enhancement and baryon-to-meson modifications in photonuclear collisions challenges the traditional view of UPCs as purely dilute systems and motivates further investigation of collective phenomena in small photon-induced systems.
