# Open issues in extracting nuclear structure information from the breakup of exotic nuclei

> The open issues in the development of models for the breakup of exotic nuclei and the link with the extraction of structure information from experimental data are reviewed. The question of the improvement of the description of exotic nuclei within reaction models is approached in the perspective of previous analyses of the sensitivity of these models to that description. Future developments of reaction models are suggested, such as the inclusion of various channels within one model. The search for new reaction observables that can emphasise more details of exotic nuclear structure is also proposed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that extracting reliable nuclear structure information from breakup reactions of exotic nuclei requires addressing several open issues in reaction modelling, but notes that the necessity of fully microscopic projectile descriptions is uncertain because breakup is a peripheral reaction that primarily probes the asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) rather than the interior of the wave function.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical few-body framework for describing breakup reactions, comparing the continuum discretised coupled channel (CDCC) model, the time-dependent (TD) semiclassical model, and eikonal-based models including the dynamical eikonal approximation (DEA) and the Coulomb-corrected eikonal model (CCE). The paper examines how these models handle projectile description, core excitation, four-body breakup, coupling between reaction channels, and the search for new observables insensitive to reaction mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic corrections to E-CDCC affect Coulomb breakup cross sections at forward angles by approximately 10% at 100A MeV and 15% at 250A MeV
- The ratio method for one-neutron halo nuclei shows nearly identical results for light (C) and heavy (Pb) targets, confirming independence from the reaction mechanism
- CDCC calculations of elastic scattering of 6He on Bi at 22.5 MeV demonstrate that both three-body projectile description and breakup channel coupling are necessary to reproduce experimental data
- DWBA calculations including core excitation for 11Be breakup on C at 67A MeV show that single-particle models cannot reproduce the experimental diffractive pattern

## Context

This review extends previous work on breakup reaction modelling for exotic nuclei, particularly halo nuclei first discovered in the mid-1980s (Tanihata et al., 1985). It addresses open issues identified in comparisons between CDCC, TD, and DEA models (Capel et al., 2012) and builds upon the ratio method developed by Capel, Johnson, and Nunes (2011). The paper suggests future developments including inclusion of relativistic effects, extension of eikonal models to lower energies via Coulomb deflection corrections, and development of four-body breakup models within E-CDCC or DEA frameworks to enable more detailed confrontation between three-body structure models and experimental data.
