# New description of four-body breakup reaction

> We present a novel method of smoothing discrete breakup cross sections calculated by the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels. The method based on the complex scaling method is tested with success for $^{58}$Ni($d$, $pn$) reaction at 80 MeV as an example of a three-body breakup reaction, and applied to $^{12}$C($^6$He, $nn^4$He) reaction at 229.8 MeV as a typical example of a four-body breakup reaction. The new method does not need to derive continuum states of the projectile in order to evaluate the breakup cross section as a smooth factor of the excitation energy of the projectile. Fast convergence of the breakup cross section with respect to extending the modelspace is confirmed. For the $^6$He breakup cross section, the resonant component is separated from the non-resonant one.

# New description of four-body breakup reaction

## Key claim

The authors present a method for smoothing discrete breakup cross sections from continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations that does not require evaluating the continuum wave functions of the projectile, using the complex scaling method (CSM) to obtain the differential breakup cross section as a continuous function of excitation energy; the method is tested for a three-body breakup reaction and applied to four-body breakup reactions, with the caveat that the scaling operator acts only on spatially damping functions, making the calculation feasible and convergent.

## Method

The method combines CDCC with the complex scaling method (CSM) to compute the generalized response function for the breakup cross section without explicitly evaluating the continuum wave functions of the projectile. The internal Hamiltonian of the projectile and its scaled version are diagonalized using L²-type basis functions via the Gaussian expansion method (GEM), and the differential breakup cross section d²σ/dεdΩ_P is obtained from the imaginary part of a sum over scaled eigenstates. The method is tested for the ⁵⁸Ni(d, pn) reaction at 80 MeV and applied to ¹²C(⁶He, nn⁴He) at 229.8 MeV and 240 MeV/A, as well as ²⁰⁸Pb(⁶He, nn⁴He) at 240 MeV/A.

## Key numbers

- ⁵⁸Ni(d, pn) reaction at 80 MeV: new method reproduces the smoothing factor method result (validation test)
- ¹²C(⁶He, nn⁴He) reaction at 229.8 MeV: convergence achieved with basis set II (j_max=15, y_max=20.0 fm, i_max=15, r_max=20.0 fm)
- Scaling angle convergence at θ = -14° for the 2⁺ resonance peak region
- Coulomb breakup enhances the 1⁻ continuum cross section by a factor of 2 for ¹²C target at 229.8 MeV
- ⁶He + ¹²C at 240 MeV/A: theoretical result consistent with experimental data except for overestimation of the 2⁺ resonance peak around ε = 1 MeV
- ⁶He + ²⁰⁸Pb at 240 MeV/A: calculation underestimates experimental data at ε ≳ 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method for four-body breakup reactions beyond previous approaches that required direct evaluation of continuum wave functions or used the average (Av) method limited to three-body systems. The method improves upon earlier smoothing techniques using direct numerical integration or CSM that required complicated calculations and showed insufficient convergence. The results are compared with four-body distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) and eikonal approximation analyses of the same data from Aumann et al. (1999), and the formalism is in principle applicable to many-body breakup reactions if diagonalization of the projectile Hamiltonian is feasible.
