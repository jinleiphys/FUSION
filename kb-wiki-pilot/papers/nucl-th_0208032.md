# Radiation correction to astrophysical fusion reactions and the electron screening problem

> We discuss the effect of electromagnetic environment on laboratory measurements of the nuclear fusion reactions of astrophysical interest. The radiation field is eliminated using the path integral formalism in order to obtain the influence functional, which we evaluate in the semi-classical approximation. We show that enhancement of the tunneling probability due to the radiation correction is extremely small and does not resolve the longstanding problem that the observed electron screening effect is significantly larger than theoretical predictions.

# Radiation correction to astrophysical fusion reactions and the electron screening problem

## Key claim
The enhancement of tunneling probability due to coupling between the tunneling motion and the electromagnetic field (virtual and real photon emission during tunneling) is extremely small, at most on the order of 10⁻⁵ %, and therefore cannot resolve the longstanding discrepancy between observed electron screening energies and theoretical predictions. This conclusion holds under the semi-classical and dipole approximations, but otherwise the path-integral treatment is exact.

## Method
The authors employ the path-integral formalism for multi-dimensional tunneling, eliminating the radiation field to obtain an influence functional which is evaluated in the semi-classical approximation along a classical tunneling path in imaginary time. The regularized two-time influence functional is computed for a pure Coulomb potential from the outer to the inner classical turning point, separating virtual and real photon contributions after mass renormalization. Numerical estimates are performed for several fusion reactions of astrophysical interest, using the lowest experimental energies and inner turning points from prior work.

## Key numbers
- For the ³He(d,p)⁴He reaction at E_min = 5.88 keV with R₀ = 4.3 fm: enhancement factor f − 1 = 2.744 × 10⁻⁷
- For D(³He,p)⁴He at E_min = 5.38 keV with R₀ = 4.3 fm: f − 1 = 2.731 × 10⁻⁷
- For ⁶Li(p,α)³He at E_min = 10.74 keV with R₀ = 3.0 fm: f − 1 = 3.758 × 10⁻⁶
- For ⁷Li(p,α)⁴He at E_min = 12.70 keV with R₀ = 4.3 fm: f − 1 = 3.598 × 10⁻⁶
- For H(⁶Li,α)³He at E_min = 10.94 keV with R₀ = 3.0 fm: f − 1 = 3.761 × 10⁻⁶
- For H(⁷Li,α)⁴He at E_min = 12.97 keV with R₀ = 4.3 fm: f − 1 = 3.602 × 10⁻⁶
- For ¹⁰B(p,α)⁷Be at E_min = 18.70 keV with R₀ = 3.3 fm: f − 1 = 6.095 × 10⁻⁶
- For ¹¹B(p,α)⁸Be at E_min = 16.70 keV with R₀ = 2.0 fm: f − 1 = 1.169 × 10⁻⁵
- The enhancement factor never exceeds 10⁻³ % for any reaction considered; the effect is even smaller than the vacuum polarization effect studied in Balantekin et al. (Nucl. Phys. A627, 324, 1997).

## Context
This work extends the study of effects beyond standard electron screening on astrophysical fusion reactions, following Balantekin et al. (1997) which examined vacuum polarization, relativity, bremsstrahlung, and atomic polarization, all found too small to explain the screening discrepancy. It directly re-examines the proposal by Flambaum and Zelevinsky (Phys. Rev. Lett. 83, 3108, 1999) that virtual photon emission during tunneling could increase penetrability, but uses a more rigorous path-integral approach without the closure approximation for the relative motion. The result shows the radiation correction is negligible, leaving the electron screening problem, where experimental data systematically require larger energy shifts than the adiabatic upper limit predicts, still unsolved.
