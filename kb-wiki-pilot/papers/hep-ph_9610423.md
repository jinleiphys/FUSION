# Nuclear beams in HERA

> A study has been made of the physics interest and feasibility of experiments with nuclear beams in HERA. It is shown that such experiments widen considerably the horizon for probing QCD compared to that from free nucleon targets. In addition there is some sensitivity to physics beyond the standard model. Hence the option to include circulating nuclear beams in HERA allows a wide range of physics processes to be studied and understood.

## Key claim

The paper argues that using nuclear beams at HERA would allow an effective reduction of about two orders of magnitude in the Bjorken-x variable compared to free nucleon targets, making it feasible to explore nonlinear QCD effects at the energies available at HERA, whereas such effects are relatively small in standard ep scattering within the HERA kinematic domain.

## Method

The authors performed a feasibility study for storing nuclear beams (D, He, C, S, and potentially Sn and Pb) in the HERA collider, using the existing H1 and ZEUS detectors with luminosities of 1-10 pb^-1 per nucleon. They calculated expected statistical precisions for measurements of nuclear structure function ratios F2^A/F2^D, gluon density ratios via jet rates, scaling violations, and inelastic J/psi production, as well as diffractive structure functions, assuming the nucleon energy per nucleon would be 410 GeV (half the proton energy of 820 GeV) with electron energy of 27.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon energy per nucleon in nuclear beams: 410 GeV (half the HERA proton energy of 820 GeV)
- Electron energy assumed: 27.6 GeV (standard HERA value)
- Required luminosities: 1-10 pb^-1 per nucleon for most measurements
- Statistical errors on F2^A/F2^D ratios: less than 1% in many bins (smaller than the size of data points)
- Sensitivity to differences in gluon densities between bound and free nucleons: approximately 5% of the total gluon density at x near 10^-3
- Expected statistical precision on diffractive structure function ratios: approximately 5% with luminosities of 10/A pb^-1 per nucleus
- Energy loss of quarks and gluons traversing nuclear medium: estimated below 350 MeV/fm

## Context

This work extends the physics program of HERA beyond the existing ep scattering studies, which have confirmed rapid growth of parton densities at small x but cannot distinguish between DGLAP evolution and BFKL-type dynamics due to limited x range. The paper builds on fixed target lepton-nucleus scattering data from NMC, E665, and earlier EMC experiments, which only touch the surface of nonlinear QCD effects due to limited Q^2 range at small x. The proposed measurements would enable studies of gluon shadowing necessary for interpreting high-pT jet rates at the LHC and jet quenching phenomena in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, connecting to the theoretical work of Gribov, Bjorken, and Baier et al. on color transparency, perturbative color opacity, and the Landau-Migdal-Pomeranchuk effect in QCD.
