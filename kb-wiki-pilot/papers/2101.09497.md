# The Hussein-McVoy formula for inclusive breakup revisited. A Tribute to Mahir Hussein

> In 1985, Hussein and McVoy [Nuc. Phys. A445 (1985) 124] elucidated a formula for the evaluation of the nonelastic breakup ("stripping") contribution in inclusive breakup reactions. The formula, based on the spectator core model, acquires a particularly simple and appealing form in the eikonal limit, to the extent that it has become the standard procedure to analyze single-nucleon knockout reactions at intermediate energies. In this contribution, a critical assessment of this formula is presented and its connection with other, noneikonal expressions discussed. Some calculations comparing the different formulae are also presented for the one-nucleon removal of $^{14}$O+$^{9}$Be reaction at several incident energies.

## Key claim
The original Hussein-McVoy (HM) formula for nonelastic breakup, when taken in its noneikonal form, is an incomplete theory because it neglects the Udagawa-Tamura (UT) term, which can be very large or even dominant. However, the eikonal (Glauber) version of the HM formula incorporates three-body effects that go beyond its noneikonal form and is expected to be more accurate at high energies, representing the Glauber limit of the full three-body formula by Austern et al.

## Method
The authors critically assess the Hussein-McVoy formula for inclusive breakup by reviewing its derivation, its eikonal (Glauber) limit, and its connection with other noneikonal expressions (the three-body model of Austern et al., the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) formula, and the Udagawa-Tamura (UT) formula). They derive the relation between the post-form and prior-form IAV expressions, showing that the HM formula is recovered by neglecting the UT term, and demonstrate that for the HM choice of the distorting potential (U_a = U_bA + U_xA), the prior-form source term vanishes, recovering Austern's three-body formula. Numerical calculations comparing the HM, eikonal HM (EHM), and IAV formulae are performed for one-nucleon removal from the 14O+9Be reaction at 53 MeV/u and 80 MeV/u.

## Key numbers
- Incident energies considered: 53 MeV/u and 80 MeV/u for the 14O+9Be reaction
- The eikonal HM formula is expected to be accurate above approximately 100 MeV per nucleon (stated as the regime where the Glauber approximation is justified)
- The ratio R_s = sigma_exp/sigma_theo from knockout studies is typically less than 1, with a systematic dependence on the separation energy of the removed nucleon (R_s becomes smaller as separation energy increases)

## Context
This work revisits the seminal 1985 Hussein-McVoy formula (Nuc. Phys. A445, 124) for inclusive breakup, which has become the standard procedure for analyzing single-nucleon knockout reactions at intermediate energies. The paper extends and clarifies the connection between the HM formula and other approaches, particularly the IAV model (Ichimura, Austern, Vincent, 1985) and the three-body model of Austern et al. (1987). The results are relevant to ongoing debates about the interpretation of spectroscopic factors from knockout reactions, where the systematic reduction of R_s with increasing separation energy has been questioned by studies using transfer reactions (Fla13) and (p,pN) reactions (Ata18, Kaw18, Gom18). The work enables more rigorous testing of whether the observed trends in spectroscopic factors are genuine nuclear structure effects or artifacts of the reaction model.
