# Continuum-discretized coupled-channels method for four-body nuclear breakup in $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering

> We propose a fully quantum-mechanical method of treating four-body nuclear breakup processes in scattering of a projectile consisting of three constituents, by extending the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method. The three-body continuum states of the projectile are discretized by diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian of the projectile with the Gaussian basis functions. For $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV, the validity of the method is tested by convergence of the elastic and breakup cross sections with respect to increasing the number of the basis functions. Effects of the four-body breakup and the Borromean structure of $^6$He on the elastic and total reaction cross sections are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors propose a fully quantum-mechanical method (four-body CDCC) for treating four-body nuclear breakup in scattering of a three-body projectile, but the method is tested only for nuclear breakup processes (Coulomb breakup is neglected) and the imaginary part of the coupling potential is adjusted empirically with a free parameter N_I to fit experimental elastic data.

## Method

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) is extended to treat four-body systems by expanding the total scattering wave function in terms of bound and discretized-continuum states of the three-body projectile (^6He). The three-body continuum states of ^6He are discretized using the pseudo-state method, where the internal Hamiltonian of the projectile is diagonalized with Gaussian basis functions in the Gaussian expansion method (GEM). The coupled-channels equations are solved for ^6He+^12C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV, using double-folding model coupling potentials with the DDM3Y nucleon-nucleon effective interaction multiplied by a complex factor N_R+iN_I.

## Key numbers

- For ^6He+^12C at 18 MeV, the optimum value of N_I is 0.5, the same as for ^6Li scattering at various incident energies
- For ^6He+^12C at 229.8 MeV, the optimum value of N_I is 0.3
- High-lying states of ^6He with epsilon_nI > 12 MeV (for 18 MeV scattering) and epsilon_nI > 25 MeV (for 229.8 MeV scattering) give no effect on elastic and breakup S-matrix elements
- The real part of the dynamical polarization potential from four-body breakup is repulsive and about 30% of the real part of the double-folded potential at 18 MeV
- The imaginary part of the dynamical polarization potential from four-body breakup is about 20% of the imaginary part of the double-folded potential at 18 MeV
- A 10% enhancement of the total reaction cross section for ^6He+^12C at 18 MeV relative to ^6Li+^12C at similar energy is found, with half due to the Borromean structure of ^6He

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method, previously applied to three-body breakup in scattering of two-body projectiles, to treat four-body breakup in scattering of three-body projectiles like ^6He. It improves upon earlier semi-classical eikonal and adiabatic calculations that work well at higher energies but are not applicable at low energies, and upon the di-neutron model for ^6He which treated the two-neutron pair as a single particle. The method enables fully quantum-mechanical analysis of four-body breakup and allows quantification of Borromean structure effects on reaction cross sections, with further work needed to include Coulomb breakup using complex-range Gaussian basis functions as suggested by Egami et al.
