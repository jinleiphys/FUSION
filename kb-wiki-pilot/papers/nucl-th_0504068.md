# Direct Reactions with Exotic Nuclei

> We discuss recent work on Coulomb dissociation and an effective-range theory of low-lying electromagnetic strength of halo nuclei. We propose to study Coulomb dissociation of a halo nucleus bound by a zero-range potential as a homework problem. We study the transition from stripping to bound and unbound states and point out in this context that the Trojan-Horse method is a suitable tool to investigate subthreshold resonances.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that direct reaction theories for exotic nuclei, particularly Coulomb dissociation and transfer reactions, can be described using effective-range expansions and simple analytical models that capture universal features of halo nuclei, but the authors hedge that these approaches are limited to low energies and that the transition from bound to unbound states in stripping reactions is smooth only when proper treatment of the continuum and subthreshold resonances is included.

## Method

The authors develop an effective-range theory for electromagnetic strength distributions in one-neutron halo nuclei, applying it to the specific case of 11Be using experimental Coulomb dissociation data from GSI. They also formulate a three-body scattering problem with a zero-range neutron-core interaction to study Coulomb dissociation analytically in various limits (plane-wave, Coulomb-wave Born approximation, adiabatic approximation). For transfer reactions, they extend the inclusive breakup formalism of Ichimura, Austern, Vincent, and Kasano to negative energies, using a two-channel square-well model with delta-function coupling to demonstrate the smooth transition from stripping to bound and unbound states.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be: cutoff radius R = 2.78 fm, inverse bound-state decay length q = 0.1486 fm^-1, extracted asymptotic normalization coefficient C_0 = 0.724(8) fm^-1/2, spectroscopic factor C^2 S = 0.704(15)
- Halo expansion parameter gamma = qR = 0.4132 (less than 1)
- Scattering length parameters for 11Be: c^{3/2}_1 = -0.41(86,-20) and c^{1/2}_1 = 2.77(13,-14)
- Electric dipole polarizability of the deuteron measured as alpha_pol = (0.70 +/- 0.05) fm^3 from elastic scattering on 208Pb at 3.0 to 7.0 MeV
- For l=0 stripping to bound states: asymptotic normalization constant B ~ q^{3/2} (tends to zero as q goes to zero)
- For l>0 stripping to bound states: B ~ q^{l+1} R^{l-1/2} (stays finite as q goes to zero)

## Context

This work extends the effective-range approach previously applied to the deuteron (the prime example of a halo nucleus) to arbitrary nucleon+core systems, building on the authors' earlier work (Typel and Baur, Phys. Rev. Lett. 93, 142502, 2004) and related studies by Kalassa and Baur, Bertulani and Sustich, and Bertulani. The effective-range theory is closely related to effective field theories used for the nucleon-nucleon system and halo nuclei (Bertulani, Hammer, and van Kolck, Nucl. Phys. A 721, 37, 2002). The Trojan-Horse method, introduced by Baur et al. in 1976 and 1984, is presented as a tool to investigate subthreshold resonances in exotic nuclei, which is expected to become increasingly important as the dripline is approached and binding energies become small. The work enables systematic studies of how final-state interactions affect astrophysical S factors and radiative capture cross sections at low energies.
