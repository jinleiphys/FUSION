# Four-body dynamics in 6Li elastic scattering

> We analyze 6Li elastic scattering in a wide range of incident energies (Ein), assuming the n + p + alpha + target four-body model and solving the dynamics with the four-body version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). Four-body CDCC well reproduces the experimental data with no adjustable parameter for 6Li + 209Bi scattering at Ein = 24-50 MeV and 6Li + 208Pb scattering at Ein = 29-210 MeV. In the wide Ein range, 6Li breakup is significant and provides repulsive corrections to the folding potential. As an interesting property, d breakup is strongly suppressed in 6Li-breakup processes independently of Ein. We investigate what causes the d-breakup suppression.

## Key claim

Four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) calculations reproduce experimental elastic scattering data for 6Li on 209Bi and 208Pb targets with no adjustable parameters across a wide incident energy range, but the abstract's claim of "no adjustable parameter" glosses over that fine tuning of the Koning-Delaroche neutron-target potential parameters was performed for the 209Bi case (a_V=0.55 fm, W_V=0 MeV, W_D=4.0 MeV) to improve agreement with n+209Bi scattering data.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Schrodinger equation for the n+p+alpha+target system using the four-body version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). The internal structure of 6Li was constructed with the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) using three sets of Jacobi coordinates, the KKNN interaction for n-alpha and p-alpha potentials, and the Bonn-A interaction for the n-p potential, with a three-body force adjusted to reproduce the experimental binding energy and root-mean-square radius of 6Li. The model space included ground and discretized continuum states up to 10 MeV excitation energy for spins 1+, 2+, and 3+, and the coupled-channel equations were solved with coupling potentials obtained by folding n-target, p-target, and alpha-target optical potentials over the 6Li internal states.

## Key numbers

- 6Li ground state binding energy: -3.69 MeV (calculated) vs -3.6989 MeV (experimental)
- 6Li ground state matter root-mean-square radius: 2.43 fm (calculated) vs 2.44 +/- 0.07 fm (experimental)
- Incident energy range for 6Li+209Bi: 24-50 MeV
- Incident energy range for 6Li+208Pb: 29-210 MeV
- Coulomb barrier energy for 6Li+209Bi: approximately 30 MeV
- d-alpha probability in 6Li ground state: 0.70 (spectroscopic overlap 0.83), consistent with experimental estimate 0.85 +/- 0.04
- Number of np-alpha-dominant breakup states in model space: 140
- Number of d-alpha-dominant breakup states in model space: 15
- d-alpha threshold energy: -2.2 MeV
- n+p+alpha three-body threshold: 0 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous three-body CDCC analyses of 6Li scattering based on the d+alpha+target model, which required a normalization factor of 0.8 to d-target and alpha-target optical potentials to reproduce data near the Coulomb barrier. The four-body CDCC approach solves the problem identified by Satchler and Love that double-folding models failed for weakly-bound projectiles like 6Li. The finding that d-breakup suppression occurs independently of incident energy explains why three-body CDCC with phenomenological d-target potentials (which include d-breakup effects implicitly) fails, while using a single-folding potential for d-target (which excludes d-breakup) succeeds. This enables future effective descriptions of 6Li scattering using the d+alpha+target three-body model with appropriate folding potentials.
