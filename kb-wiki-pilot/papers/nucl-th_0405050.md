# Gaussian expansion approach to Coulomb breakup

> An accurate treatment of Coulomb breakup reactions is presented by using both the Gaussian expansion method and the method of continuum discretized coupled channels. As $L^2$-type basis functions for describing bound- and continuum-states of a projectile, we take complex-range Gaussian functions, which form in good approximation a complete set in a large configuration space being important for Coulomb-breakup processes. Accuracy of the method is tested quantitatively for $^{8}{\rm B}+^{58}$Ni scattering at 25.8 MeV.

## Key claim

The pseudo-state continuum discretized coupled channels (PS-CDCC) method, using complex-range Gaussian basis functions, can accurately describe Coulomb breakup processes despite the very large configuration space required (up to 100 fm), matching the "exact" average-method CDCC results for $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni scattering at 25.8 MeV; this is a necessary step before the method can be applied to four-body CDCC analysis of $^{8}$B breakup needed to determine $S_{17}$ within 5% errors.

## Method

The authors performed three-body CDCC calculations for $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni scattering at 25.8 MeV, comparing the pseudo-state method (PS-CDCC) using complex-range Gaussian basis functions with the standard average method (Av-CDCC). The internal Hamiltonian of the projectile was diagonalized using 60 complex-range Gaussian basis functions with geometric progression parameters $a_1=1.0$ fm, $a_n=35.0$ fm, and $b=\pi/2$, generating pseudo-states for s- and p-waves up to $k_{\rm max}=0.66$ fm$^{-1}$. The CDCC equations were solved with $J_{\rm max}=1000$, $R_{\rm max}=500$ fm, and $r_{\rm max}=130$ fm for the PS method, using the predictor-corrector Numerov method with stabilization.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 25.8 MeV for $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni scattering
- Cutoff momentum: $k_{\rm max}=0.66$ fm$^{-1}$ for both s- and p-states
- Number of basis functions: $2n=60$ complex-range Gaussian functions with $a_1=1.0$ fm and $a_n=35.0$ fm
- Maximum internal coordinate: $r_{\rm max}=130$ fm for PS method, $r_{\rm max}=100$ fm for Av method
- Maximum total angular momentum: $J_{\rm max}=1000$
- Proton separation energy in $^{8}$B: 137 keV
- Number of channels: 18 for both s- and p-states
- Bin widths for Av method: $\Delta_{i\ell}=0.66/16$ fm$^{-1}$ for $\ell=1$ and $0.66/32$ fm$^{-1}$ for $\ell=0$

## Context

This work extends the PS-CDCC method previously applied to nuclear breakup processes (Matsumoto et al., Phys. Rev. C 68, 064607 (2003)) to the more challenging case of Coulomb breakup, where the required configuration space is much larger (up to 100 fm for $^{8}$B Coulomb breakup versus about 20 fm for nuclear breakup of $d$ or $^{6}$Li). The method enables future four-body CDCC analysis of $^{8}$B dissociation (treating $^{8}$B as $p$+$^{3}$He+$^{4}$He) needed to determine the astrophysical $S_{17}$ factor with high accuracy, addressing the limitation of previous three-body CDCC analyses (Yamashita, OgataND) that could only provide an upper limit due to the $1/2^-$ $^{7}$Be excited state component.
