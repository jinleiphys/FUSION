# Relativistic Description of Exclusive Deuteron Breakup Reactions

> The exclusive deuteron break-up reaction is analyzed within a covariant approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation with realistic meson-exchange interaction. Relativistic effects in the cross section, tensor analyzing power and polarization transfer are investigated in explicit form. Results of numerical calculations are presented for kinematical conditions in forthcoming p + D reactions at COSY.

## Key claim
Relativistic corrections to the cross section, tensor analyzing power, and polarization transfer in exclusive deuteron breakup are negligible for the kinematical conditions of the planned COSY experiments (backward proton momentum up to 0.51 GeV/c), being much smaller than expected experimental errors; however, the authors note that their covariant Bethe-Salpeter treatment already subsumes some meson-exchange and pair-production effects that would appear as separate diagrams in non-relativistic calculations.

## Method
The authors compute the exclusive p + D → p₁(0°) + p₂(180°) + n₃(0°) reaction within the spectator mechanism using a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter formalism. They obtain numerical solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a realistic meson-exchange kernel (π, ω, ρ, σ, η, δ exchange) and transform the partial amplitudes to the ρ-spin basis, where only four amplitudes (³S₁⁺⁺, ³D₁⁺⁺, ¹P₁⁻⁺, ³P₁⁻⁺) contribute when one nucleon is on mass shell. The unpolarized cross section, tensor analyzing power T₂₀, and polarization transfer κ are expressed in terms of the corresponding wave functions U, W, V_{P₁}, V_{P₃}, with terms proportional to |p|/m identified as pure relativistic corrections from negative P-states.

## Key numbers
- The maximum backward proton momentum in the proposed COSY experiments at E_p ≈ 3 GeV is |p₂|_max ≈ 0.51 GeV/c
- Relativistic corrections become significant only at |p₂| > 0.6 GeV/c
- Within the spectator mechanism, the maximum value of |p₂| is restricted to about 0.8 GeV/c
- The P-wave vertices V_{P₁,₃} are a few orders of magnitude smaller than the S- and D-wave functions U and W
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used in the one-iteration approximation is g_π² ≈ 14.5

## Context
This work extends previous relativistic investigations of one-nucleon exchange diagrams for inclusive and elastic D+p scattering by Keister and Tjon (1982) and by Kaptari et al. (1995), which were based on Bethe-Salpeter solutions. It provides the first explicit covariant analysis of polarization observables in exclusive deuteron breakup, enabling planned experiments at COSY (proposal 20) using the ANKE spectrometer. The results show that at COSY kinematics, relativistic effects can be neglected, allowing future experiments to focus on non-nucleonic degrees of freedom such as meson-exchange currents and Δ isobar contributions, which are believed to cause the shoulder observed at |p₂| ≈ 0.3 GeV/c in inclusive measurements by Ableev et al. (1983) and others.
