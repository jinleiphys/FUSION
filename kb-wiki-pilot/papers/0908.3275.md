# Nuclear Reactions

> Nuclear reactions generate energy in nuclear reactors, in stars, and are responsible for the existence of all elements heavier than hydrogen in the universe. Nuclear reactions denote reactions between nuclei, and between nuclei and other fundamental particles, such as electrons and photons. A short description of the conservation laws and the definition of basic physical quantities is presented, followed by a more detailed account of specific cases: (a) formation and decay of compound nuclei; (b)direct reactions; (c) photon and electron scattering; (d) heavy ion collisions; (e) formation of a quark-gluon plasma; (f) thermonuclear reactions; (g) and reactions with radioactive beams. Whenever necessary, basic equations are introduced to help understand general properties of these reactions. Published in Wiley Encyclopedia of Physics, ISBN-13: 978-3-527-40691-3 - Wiley-VCH, Berlin, 2009.

## Key claim

Nuclear reactions can proceed through multiple distinct mechanisms, including direct reactions, compound nucleus formation, and high-energy fragmentation, with the compound nucleus mechanism being dominant at low energies where the projectile energy is distributed among all nucleons before decay.

## Method

The paper provides a comprehensive pedagogical review of nuclear reaction theory, covering conservation laws (baryonic number, charge, energy/momentum, angular momentum, parity, isospin), reaction kinematics with Q-values and threshold energies, cross section definitions and measurements, partial wave expansion for elastic and reaction cross sections, and detailed treatments of compound nucleus formation with statistical evaporation models and resonance phenomena. Specific reaction types examined include direct reactions, photon and electron scattering, heavy ion collisions, quark-gluon plasma formation, thermonuclear reactions, and reactions with radioactive beams.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for meson production in low-energy reactions is approximately 140 MeV
- Nuclear reaction timescales range from approximately 10^-22 to 10^-16 seconds
- 1 barn corresponds to an area of a circle with diameter approximately 8 fm (8 × 10^-15 m)
- The level density parameter a is approximately A/7.5 to A/8 MeV^-1 for nuclei far from magic regions
- For the reaction n+^235U, the cross section follows a 1/v law up to 0.3 eV, where resonances begin to appear
- The internal nuclear potential V_0 is approximately -35 MeV for the square-well model

## Context

This work extends the foundational experimental work of Rutherford (1910) and the compound nucleus hypothesis of Bohr (1936), and builds upon Ghoshal's experimental verification (1950) that compound nucleus decay is independent of formation mode. The paper enables further study of nuclear reaction mechanisms across energy scales, from low-energy neutron capture to high-energy quark-gluon plasma formation, and provides the theoretical framework for understanding stellar nucleosynthesis, nuclear reactor physics, and heavy-ion collision experiments.
