# Poincaré Invariant Three-Body Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> The relativistic Faddeev equation for three-nucleon scattering is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. The equation is solved through Padé summation, and the numerical feasibility and stability of the solution is demonstrated. Relativistic invariance is achieved by constructing a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space. Based on a Malfliet-Tjon type interaction, observables for elastic and break-up scattering are calculated for projectile energies in the intermediate energy range up to 2 GeV, and compared to their nonrelativistic counterparts. The convergence of the multiple scattering series is investigated as a function of the projectile energy in different scattering observables and configurations. Approximations to the two-body interaction embedded in the three-particle space are compared to the exact treatment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully Poincaré-invariant three-nucleon Faddeev equation can be solved numerically in momentum space without partial wave decomposition for projectile energies up to 2 GeV, but the authors caution that their model interaction (Malfliet-Tjon type) is too simple to be reliable above 1.5 GeV, and the optical theorem is satisfied to only about 3% at 2 GeV.

## Method

The relativistic Faddeev equation was formulated using Poincaré-Jacobi momenta and solved directly in terms of momentum vectors via Padé summation, avoiding partial wave decomposition. A dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group was constructed on the three-nucleon Hilbert space, and the two-body interaction was embedded in the three-body mass operator using the Coester-Pieper-Serduke scheme to ensure phase-shift equivalence with the nonrelativistic interaction. The numerical integration handled singularities in the three-nucleon propagator through subtraction and cubic Hermite splines, with the kernel applied successively and summed as Padé sums.

## Key numbers

- Projectile laboratory kinetic energy range: 0.1 to 2.0 GeV (but observables only shown up to 1.5 GeV)
- Optical theorem satisfied to about 1% or better up to 1 GeV, increasing to about 3% at 2 GeV
- At 1 GeV projectile energy, a q-grid of N_q = 50 points and an x_q-grid of at least 36 points were required for convergence
- At 200 MeV, the nonrelativistic 3N c.m. momentum q_0 is approximately 400 MeV/c; at 1 GeV, q_0 is approximately 900 MeV/c
- Estimated partial wave requirements for convergence: at 200 MeV, l_max = 5, lambda_max = 7, J_max = 12; at 1 GeV, l_max = 14, lambda_max = 15, J_max = 29
- Deuteron binding energy used: 2.23 MeV

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic three-dimensional Faddeev calculations without partial waves (previously developed by the same group for bound and scattering states) to the relativistic regime, building on the Poincaré-invariant formulation detailed in Lin et al. (2007). It contrasts with earlier relativistic three-nucleon calculations that employed partial wave decompositions and were limited to energies below the pion-production threshold (Witala et al.). The results enable future studies of relativistic effects in three-nucleon scattering at intermediate energies and provide a framework for incorporating realistic spin-dependent NN interactions in a fully relativistic three-body treatment.
