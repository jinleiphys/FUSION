# Flavor Production in Pb(160AGeV) on Pb Collisions: Effect of Color Ropes and Hadronic Rescattering

> Collective interactions in the preequilibrium quark matter and hadronic resonance gas stage of ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied in the framework of the the transport theoretical approach RQMD. The paper reviews string fusion into color ropes and hadronic rescattering which serve as models for these interactions. Hadron production in central Pb(160AGeV) on Pb collisions has been calculated. The changes of the final flavor composition are more pronounced than in previous RQMD studies of light ion induced reactions at 200AGeV. The ratio of created quark pairs $s\bar{s}$/($u\bar{u}$+$d\bar{d}$) is enhanced by a factor of 2.4 in comparison to $pp$ results. Color rope formation increases the initially produced antibaryons to 3 times the value in the `NN mode', but only one quarter of the produced antibaryons survives because of subsequent strong absorption. The differences in the final particle composition for Pb on Pb collisions compared to S induced reactions are attributed to the hadronic resonance gas stage which is baryon-richer and lasts longer.

# Flavor Production in Pb(160AGeV) on Pb Collisions: Effect of Color Ropes and Hadronic Rescattering

## Key claim

The enhancement of the strange quark pair ratio $s\bar{s}/(u\bar{u}+d\bar{d})$ by a factor of 2.4 compared to $pp$ results, and the tripling of initially produced antibaryons via color rope formation, are both substantially reduced by subsequent strong absorption in the hadronic resonance gas stage, with only one quarter of the produced antibaryons surviving; the final flavor composition changes are more pronounced than in previous RQMD studies of light ion induced reactions at 200AGeV, and the differences are attributed specifically to the baryon-richer and longer-lasting hadronic resonance gas stage in Pb+Pb compared to S-induced reactions.

## Method

The study uses the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) transport approach, a semi-classical microscopic model combining classical propagation with stochastic interactions, to simulate central Pb(160AGeV) on Pb collisions. String fusion into color ropes models collective interactions in the preequilibrium quark matter stage, where overlapping strings form chromoelectric flux-tubes with higher-dimensional color representations, while hadronic rescattering and resonance interactions model collectivity in the hadronic resonance gas stage, including meson-baryon annihilation into nonstrange baryon resonances and a string continuum.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of created quark pairs $s\bar{s}/(u\bar{u}+d\bar{d})$ enhanced by a factor of 2.4 compared to $pp$ results
- Color rope formation increases initially produced antibaryons to 3 times the value in the `NN mode'
- Only one quarter of the produced antibaryons survives due to subsequent strong absorption
- Rope tension scales with Casimir eigenvalue: $\kappa(p,q)=3/4 \cdot C(p,q) \cdot \kappa_{el}$, with elementary string tension $\kappa_{el}=0.9$ GeV/fm
- Transverse rope radius set to 0.8 fm, fixed by comparison to $e^+e^-$ hadroproduction data
- Minimum excited mass for string fragmentation set to $m+0.3$ GeV/$c^2$, with $m$ taken as 0.7 GeV for mesons without strange valence quarks and 0.85 GeV for mesons with strange valence quarks
- Cut-off value for nucleon string excitation set at 2 GeV/$c^2$

## Context

This work extends earlier RQMD studies of light ion induced reactions at 200AGeV (S on A collisions) to the heaviest projectile-target combination at CERN-SPS energies, and builds on the string fusion approach previously developed for QGSM, the Spanish version of the dual-parton model, and quark matter droplet formation in VENUS. The results enable quantitative comparison of collectivity effects between different nuclear systems and provide a baseline for interpreting experimental data from the CERN-SPS Pb beam program, with a follow-up paper planned to discuss the dynamical evolution of the created source in space-time until freeze-out.
