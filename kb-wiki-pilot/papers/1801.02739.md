# 9Be scattering with microscopic wave functions and the CDCC method

> We use microscopic 9Be wave functions defined in a alpha+alpha+n multicluster model to compute 9Be+target scattering cross sections. The parameter sets describing 9Be are generated in the spirit of the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM), and the optimal solution is obtained by superposing Slater determinants and by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian. The 9Be three-body continuum is approximated by square-integral wave functions. The 9Be microscopic wave functions are then used in a Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculation of 9Be+208Pb and of 9Be+27Al elastic scattering. Without any parameter fitting, we obtain a fair agreement with experiment. For a heavy target, the influence of 9Be breakup is important, while it is weaker for light targets. This result confirms previous non-microscopic CDCC calculations. One of the main advantages of the microscopic CDCC is that it is based on nucleon-target interactions only; there is no adjustable parameter. The present work represents a first step towards more ambitious calculations involving heavier Be isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that microscopic 9Be wave functions from an alpha+alpha+n multicluster model, when used in Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations without any adjustable parameters, achieve fair agreement with experimental elastic scattering data for both heavy (208Pb) and light (27Al) targets, confirming previous non-microscopic CDCC results that breakup effects are important for heavy targets but weaker for light targets.

## Method

The 9Be projectile is described using a microscopic alpha+alpha+n multicluster model with wave functions generated via the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM), where 175 Slater determinants with different Gaussian center parameters are superposed and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized. The three-body continuum of 9Be is approximated by square-integrable pseudostates with positive energies. These microscopic wave functions and their transition densities are then used in a CDCC calculation for 9Be+208Pb and 9Be+27Al elastic scattering, where the only input is the nucleon-target optical potential (Koning-Delaroche for neutrons, Coulomb-only for protons), with no parameter fitting to the scattering data.

## Key numbers

- 9Be ground state (3/2-) root mean square matter radius: 2.45 fm, in excellent agreement with experimental value 2.45 +/- 0.01 fm
- 9Be first excited state (1/2+) root mean square matter radius: 3.83 fm
- Quadrupole moment Q of ground 3/2- state: 5.82 e^2 fm^2 (experimental: 5.288 +/- 0.038 e^2 fm^2)
- B(E1) from ground 3/2- to first 1/2+ state: 0.0460 e^2 fm^2 (experimental range: 0.027-0.0685 e^2 fm^2)
- B(E2) from ground 3/2- to 5/2- state: 25.8 e^2 fm^4
- Minnesota potential exchange parameter: u=0.993 for positive parity, u=0.967 for negative parity
- Oscillator parameter b = 1.36 fm
- Number of Slater determinants superposed: N=175
- Truncation energy Emax = 15 MeV, maximum angular momentum jmax = 7/2
- Coulomb barrier: approximately 38.9 MeV for 9Be+208Pb, approximately 8.0 MeV for 9Be+27Al
- Energies studied: 9Be+208Pb at Elab = 38, 44, 50, and 75 MeV; 9Be+27Al at various energies around Coulomb barrier

## Context

This work extends the microscopic CDCC approach previously applied to 7Li (Descouvemont and Hilaire, 2013) and 6He (Descouvemont, 2016) to the three-cluster system 9Be, using the Stochastic Variational Method for basis generation. It confirms the findings of non-microscopic CDCC calculations for 9Be+target scattering by Descouvemont et al. (2015) and Casal et al. (2015), while eliminating the need for fragment-target optical potentials. The method enables future calculations for heavier Be isotopes (10Be, 11Be) where the number of degrees of freedom in the basis functions is larger.
