# LAQGSM03.03 Upgrade and its Validation

> This paper presents part of an internal LANL Progress Report on LAQGSM03.03, an upgrade of the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model event generator for MCNPX/6 and MARS15 transport codes and on its validation and testing against a large variety of recent measurements. We present here an analysis with LAQGSM03.03 of the recent PHENIX mid-rapidity spectra of pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, and p-bar produced in ultra-relativistic p + p interactions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV; GSI cross sections for the fragmentation of Pb208 at 1 GeV/nucleon on Be9; fragmentation cross sections of Si28 on H, C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb at energies from 290 to 1200 MeV/nucleon measred recently at HIMAC and BNL; recent HIMAC data on B, Be, Li, and He production cross sections from fragmentation of C12 on H, C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb at 290 and 400 MeV/nucleon; BNL data on fragmentation cross sections of Fe56 on H, C, Al, Cu, and Pb targets at 1.05 GeV/nucleon; recent pi+ and pi- spectra from 6.4, 12.3, and 17.5 GeV/c p + Be9 from the E910 BNL measurements; and fragmentation cross sections of Ca40, Ca48, Ni58, and Ni64 on Be9 and Ta181 at 140 MeV/nucleon, and of Kr86 at 64 MeV/nucleon on the same targets measured recently at NSCL-MSU and RARF-RIKEN, respectively.

# LAQGSM03.03 Upgrade and its Validation

## Key claim
LAQGSM03.03 describes nuclear reactions at energies from about 10 MeV/nucleon to about 1 TeV/nucleon with general agreement comparable to previous versions, but requires an energy-dependent transverse momentum parameter sigma_perp that increases from 0.51 GeV/c at projectile energies below 200 GeV to approximately 2.0 GeV/c at 21 TeV to properly describe ultra-relativistic p + p interactions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

## Method
The code models reactions as a three-stage process: Intra-Nuclear Cascade (INC) using an improved version of the Dubna Cascade Model, followed by preequilibrium emission via the Modified Exciton Model, then evaporation/fission using an extended version of the Generalized Evaporation Model GEM2 for compound nuclei with Z >= 65. Light excited nuclei with A < 13 are handled by a Fermi breakup model, and high-energy composite particles (d, t, He-3, He-4) are formed via a coalescence model using real information about emitted cascade nucleons rather than convolutions of nucleon spectra.

## Key numbers
- Transverse momentum parameter sigma_perp = 0.51 GeV/c at T_p <= 200 GeV, increasing to sigma_perp = 2.0 GeV/c at T_p = 21 TeV for ultra-relativistic p + p interactions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Coalescence momentum cutoffs: 90 MeV/c for deuterons, 108 MeV/c for tritons and He-3, 115 MeV/c for He-4
- LAQGSM03.02 produced 60 unstable light fragments (one H-5, twenty-three H-6, one Li-5, thirty Be-6, one Be-13, four B-6) from 10^7 simulated inelastic events of 1 GeV/nucleon Pb-208 + Be-9, representing less than 0.0006% of total yield; LAQGSM03.03 eliminates all such unstable products
- Fragmentation cross sections validated for Si-28 on H, C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb at energies from 290 to 1200 MeV/nucleon; C-12 on H, C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb at 290 and 400 MeV/nucleon; Fe-56 on H, C, Al, Cu, and Pb at 1.05 GeV/nucleon; Ca-40, Ca-48, Ni-58, and Ni-64 on Be-9 and Ta-181 at 140 MeV/nucleon; Kr-86 at 64 MeV/nucleon on same targets

## Context
This work extends the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM) for use in MCNPX/6 and MARS15 transport codes, improving upon earlier versions by fixing bugs, eliminating production of unstable light fragments, and incorporating energy-dependent transverse momentum for ultra-relativistic energies. The validation against recent PHENIX, GSI, HIMAC, BNL, NSCL-MSU, and RARF-RIKEN measurements shows that LAQGSM03.03 describes fragmentation data better than phenomenological systematics EPAX2, the NASA transport code NUCFRG2, the Japanese PHITS code, the Abrasion-Ablation model in LISE++, the HIPSE model, and the AMD model, as found by Mocko et al. in their analysis of calcium, nickel, and krypton fragmentation data.
