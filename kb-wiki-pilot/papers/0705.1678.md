# alpha-particle production in the scattering of 6He by 208Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier

> New experimental data from the scattering of 6He+208Pb at energies around and below the Coulomb barrier are presented. The yield of breakup products coming from projectile fragmentation is dominated by a strong group of $α$ particles. The energy and angular distributions of this group have been analyzed and compared with theoretical calculations. This analysis indicates that the $α$ particles emitted at backward angles in this reaction are mainly due to two-neutron transfer to weakly bound states of the final nucleus.

## Key claim

The alpha particles emitted at backward angles in the scattering of 6He on 208Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier are mainly due to two-neutron transfer to weakly bound states of the final nucleus (210Pb), rather than direct breakup of the projectile, based on the comparison of measured energy and angular distributions with theoretical calculations.

## Method

The experiment used a 6He beam produced via the 7Li(p,2p)6He reaction at the CYCLONE RNB facility, incident on 208Pb targets at laboratory energies of 14, 16, 18, and 22 MeV. Alpha particles were detected at backward angles (132-166 degrees) using the DINEX telescope array, which provided mass and charge separation via DeltaE-E identification. The double differential cross sections for alpha production were extracted by normalizing the ratio of breakup to elastic events to the known elastic scattering cross sections, and compared with transfer to the continuum (TC) calculations within the DWBA approximation, direct breakup calculations using continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC), and one-neutron transfer DWBA calculations.

## Key numbers

* At Elab = 22 MeV, the yield of alpha fragments exceeds the elastic yield by a factor of ten at backward angles.
* The breakup differential cross sections are highest around the Coulomb barrier (Elab = 18 MeV) and decrease at both lower and higher energies.
* At Elab = 14 MeV (well below the barrier), the breakup cross sections remain sizeable, indicating the mechanism is effective for projectile-target separations as large as 17.3 fm (distance of closest approach for a head-on collision at this energy).
* The 6He beam was produced at intensities of 4e6 ions per second for 14, 16, 18 MeV and 1.5e5 ions per second for 22 MeV.
* Target thicknesses were 0.950 mg/cm2 for 14, 16, 18 MeV measurements and 2.080 mg/cm2 for the 22 MeV measurement.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 6He-induced reactions on medium-heavy targets including 209Bi (Aguilera et al.), 64Zn (Di Pietro et al.), 65Cu (Navin et al.), and 238U (Raabe et al.), all of which observed large alpha particle production cross sections. The analysis supports the interpretation that two-neutron transfer to weakly bound states of the target, rather than direct breakup, is the dominant mechanism, consistent with conclusions from the 6He+209Bi reaction (DeYoung et al.) where more than half of alpha particles beyond the grazing angle arose from two-neutron transfer to unbound states. The elastic scattering data from the same reaction had previously revealed a long-range absorption effect, and this work identifies the two-neutron transfer mechanism as a source of that absorption.
