# Determination of S17 from 7Be(d,n)8B reaction: CDCC analyses based on three-body model

> The astrophysical factor $S_{17}$ for $^7$Be($p,γ$)$^8$B reaction is reliably extracted from the transfer reaction $^7$Be($d,n$)$^8$B at $E=7.5$ MeV with the asymptotic normalization coefficient method. The transfer reaction is accurately analyzed with CDCC based on the three-body model. This analysis is free from uncertainties of the optical potentials having been crucial in the previous DWBA analyses.

# Determination of S17 from 7Be(d,n)8B reaction: CDCC analyses based on three-body model

## Key claim
The astrophysical factor S17(0) for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction can be reliably extracted from the transfer reaction 7Be(d,n)8B at 7.5 MeV using the asymptotic normalization coefficient method with continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) analysis based on a three-body model, but the final value of 20.96 eV b carries a theoretical uncertainty of 6-7% and an experimental uncertainty of 13%, which together exceed the 5% precision required for neutrino oscillation parameter determination.

## Method
The authors analyzed the 7Be(d,n)8B transfer reaction at E=7.5 MeV using a three-body model (p+n+7Be) with 7Be treated as an inert core. The entrance channel dynamics were solved with CDCC, discretizing the deuteron breakup continuum into bins with kmax=1.7 fm^-1, ell=0,2, and Delta=1.7/40 fm^-1, while the exit channel was treated with the Johnson-Soper adiabatic approximation. The neutron-7Li optical potential was determined from elastic scattering data at 4.08 MeV and 4.26 MeV (using 7Li as the mirror nucleus of 7Be), then validated by comparing CDCC calculations for deuteron scattering on 7Li at 8.0 MeV against experimental data. The spectroscopic factor S_exp was extracted by fitting the calculated transfer cross section to experimental data for four different p+7Be single-particle potentials, and S17(0) was derived from the asymptotic normalization coefficient using the relation S17(0) = (S_exp * b^2)/0.026.

## Key numbers
- S17(0) = 20.96 (-0.3/+0.4) (ANC) +/- 0.63 (adiabatic approximation) +/- 0.42 (finite-range correction) +/- 2.7 (experimental) eV b, giving total S17(0) = 20.96 (-1.3/+1.4) (theoretical) +/- 2.7 (experimental) eV b
- S17(0) values from four different 8B single-particle models: 21.36 eV b (Kim et al.), 20.87 eV b (Tombrello), 20.93 eV b (Robertson), 20.67 eV b (Esbensen and Bertsch)
- Spectroscopic factor S_exp = 0.849 for the Kim et al. potential, with corresponding ANC parameter b = 0.809
- Deuteron breakup components in the incident channel contribute a 10% reduction in transfer cross section at forward angles
- Adiabatic approximation errors in the exit channel are less than 3% at forward angles for 8B(n,n)8B at E=4.18 MeV
- The recommended value from direct measurements is S17(0) = 19(+4/-2) eV b (Adelberger et al., 1998)
- A recent direct measurement gives S17(0) = 22.3 +/- 1.2 eV b (Junghans et al., 2002)

## Context
This work extends the asymptotic normalization coefficient method previously applied to 7Be(d,n)8B with distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) by Liu et al. (1996), which gave S17(0) = 27.4 +/- 4.4 eV b, inconsistent with the recommended value. The key advance is eliminating the large uncertainties (typically 30%) from ambiguous d+7Be and n+8B optical potentials that plagued earlier DWBA analyses by Gagliardi et al. (1998) and Fernandes et al. (1999). The CDCC approach, previously established by Kamimura et al. and Austern et al., explicitly treats deuteron breakup channels that are absent in standard DWBA. The result is consistent with both the recommended value from Adelberger et al. (1998) and the recent direct measurement by Junghans et al. (2002). The authors note that accurate measurements of 7Be(d,n)8B and 7Li(d,d)7Li at a few tens of MeV are needed to reduce the experimental error, and that full CDCC calculations for both entrance and exit channels could reduce theoretical uncertainty to approximately 2%.
