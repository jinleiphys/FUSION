# Saturation Physics in Ultra High Energy Cosmic Rays: Heavy Quark Production

> In this work we estimate the heavy quark production in the interaction of ultra high energy cosmic rays in the atmosphere, considering that the primary cosmic ray is a proton or a photon. At these energies the saturation momentum Q_{sat}^2 stays above the hard scale μ_c^2=4m_c^2, implying charm production probing the saturation regime. In particular, we show that the ep HERA data presents a scaling on τ_c = (Q^2+μ_c^2)/Q_{sat}^2. We derive our results considering the dipole picture and the Color Glass Condensate formalism, which one shows to be able to describe the heavy quark production in photon-proton and proton-proton collisions. Nuclear effects are considered in computation of cross sections for scattering on air nuclei. Implications on the flux of prompt leptons at the earth are analyzed and a large suppression is predicted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at ultra-high energies (above 10^18 eV), the saturation momentum Q_sat^2 exceeds the hard scale for charm production (mu_c^2 = 4m_c^2), meaning charm production in cosmic ray interactions probes the saturation regime for the first time; however, this claim is conditional on the specific energy threshold and on the validity of the Color Glass Condensate formalism used to model the dipole cross section.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark (charm and bottom) production cross sections using the color dipole picture and the Color Glass Condensate formalism, where the photon or gluon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark dipole that scatters off the target. For photon-hadron interactions, they use the transverse light-cone wavefunction of the photon convolved with the dipole-target cross section; for hadron-hadron interactions, they use a gluon-gluon fusion mechanism expressed in the dipole formalism with the projectile gluon density from the MRST2004 parameterization. Nuclear effects for scattering on air nuclei (average mass number A=14.5) are included via the Glauber-Gribov formalism, replacing the nucleon dipole cross section with a nuclear dipole cross section.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale exponent: lambda approximately 0.3, from the relation Q_sat^2 proportional to x^(-lambda)
- For charm production in gamma-air interactions at E_gamma >= 10^5 GeV, the cross section energy dependence is sigma proportional to E_gamma^0.12
- For charm production in proton-air interactions at E_p >= 10^7 GeV, the cross section energy dependence is sigma proportional to E_p^0.17
- Deviation between CGC and color transparency (leading twist) predictions for charm photoproduction: about one order of magnitude at E_gamma approximately 10^12 GeV, increasing with energy
- Deviation between CGC and leading twist for charm hadroproduction: factor of about six at E_p approximately 10^12 GeV, increasing with energy
- For bottom photoproduction, saturation effects contribute only approximately 10% at very high photon energies (E_gamma approximately 10^12 GeV)
- Charm production profile function peaks at dipole size r approximately 0.4 GeV^(-1); bottom production peaks at r approximately 0.1 GeV^(-1)

## Context

This work extends the application of saturation physics from HERA deep inelastic scattering data to ultra-high energy cosmic ray interactions, specifically heavy quark production. It builds on the geometric scaling property demonstrated for charm production at HERA (scaling variable tau_c = (Q^2 + mu_c^2)/Q_sat^2) and generalizes saturation models from Refs. MUNIER and ASW to charm production. The results contradict earlier prompt lepton flux calculations (leptonflux_tig, leptonflux_prs, leptonflux_ggv, leptonflux_mrs) that assumed collinear factorization and DGLAP evolution, predicting a large suppression of the prompt lepton flux due to saturation effects. The predictions are similar to the TIG (Thunman, Ingelman, Gondolo) results, which have been considered a lower bound for the lepton flux from pQCD calculations.
