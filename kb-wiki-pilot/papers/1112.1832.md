# Does deuteron-induced fission on actinide nuclei prevails over the breakup at low incident energies?

> An analysis of the $^{231}$Pa$(d,3n)$$^{230}$U reaction excitation function at energies around the Coulomb barrier has taken into account the pre-equilibrium and compound-nucleus cross sections corrected for the deuteron-breakup decrease of the total reaction cross section, as well as the inelastic breakup enhancement. The analysis reveals the dominance of the deuteron breakup mechanism unlike a former assessment in this respect of the deuteron-induced fission process.

# Does deuteron-induced fission on actinide nuclei prevails over the breakup at low incident energies?

## Key claim
Deuteron breakup, rather than deuteron-induced fission, is the dominant reaction mechanism for deuterons incident on actinide nuclei at energies around the Coulomb barrier, contrary to a prior assessment by Morgenstern et al. that had identified fission as the dominant decay channel. The authors qualify this claim by noting that the analysis relies on empirical parameterizations of breakup fractions and that additional measurements are needed to establish which description is better.

## Method
The authors analyzed the measured excitation function of the 231Pa(d,3n)230U reaction between 11.2 and 19.9 MeV using the TALYS-1.2 code with pre-equilibrium and compound-nucleus models, applying a reduction factor (1 − σBU/σR) to account for the deuteron breakup decrease of the total reaction cross section. They then added an inelastic breakup enhancement term calculated by convolving the measured 231Pa(p,2n)230U cross section ratio σ(p,2n)/σ(p,R) with a Gaussian distribution of breakup-proton energies from the Kalbach formula, using the deuteron optical potential of An and Cai and nucleon optical potentials for actinides from RIPL-3.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: Bd = 2.224 MeV
- Elastic breakup cross section for deuterons on 232Th at E = 15 MeV: σEB = 119 mb, corresponding to fEB = 0.164, used as a constant value for 231Pa across the incident energy range
- For deuteron incident energies above ~13 MeV, total nucleon-emission breakup cross sections from both the Avrigeanu and Kalbach parameterizations are approximately 50% of the deuteron total reaction cross section
- Measured fission probability for 231Pa(d,pf) at 15 MeV deuteron energy was found lower than 40% (Back et al., 1974)
- The (d,3n) reaction cross sections calculated with the BU reduction factor agreed with measured data just above the effective reaction threshold, but showed underestimation by a factor up to 3 at E ~ 20 MeV before including the inelastic breakup enhancement

## Context
This work extends the empirical parameterization of deuteron breakup fractions developed by Avrigeanu et al. (2009) and applies it to the specific case of 231Pa, contradicting the earlier conclusion of Morgenstern et al. (2009) that fission dominates. The analysis uses the simultaneous measurement of (d,3n) and (p,2n) excitation functions on the same target to isolate breakup effects, and the results are relevant to the surrogate nuclear reaction method where (d,xf) reactions are used as surrogates for (n,f) reactions, and to applications in fusion technology and nuclear medicine.
