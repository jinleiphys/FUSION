# Big-bang nucleosynthesis with a long-lived charged massive particle including $^4$He spallation processes

> We propose helium-4 spallation processes induced by long-lived stau in supersymmetric standard models, and investigate an impact of the processes on light elements abundances. We show that, as long as the phase space of helium-4 spallation processes is open, they are more important than stau-catalyzed fusion and hence constrain the stau property.

## Key claim

The paper claims that $^4$He spallation processes induced by long-lived staus in supersymmetric models are more important than stau-catalyzed fusion for constraining stau properties, but only "as long as the phase space of helium-4 spallation processes is open" (i.e., when the mass difference $\delta m$ between stau and neutralino exceeds approximately 0.026 GeV).

## Method

The authors analytically calculated the reaction rates for three $^4$He spallation channels from the stau-$^4$He bound state: producing triton+neutron, deuteron+two neutrons, or proton+three neutrons. They compared these rates with the stau-catalyzed fusion rate at cosmic temperature 30 keV, then numerically computed primordial light element abundances including all exotic reactions (spallation, catalyzed fusion, and internal conversion) to search for MSSM parameter regions consistent with observational constraints.

## Key numbers

- Spallation processes dominate over stau-catalyzed fusion when $\delta m \gtrsim 0.026$ GeV (the phase space opening threshold)
- For $\delta m \lesssim 0.026$ GeV, stau-catalyzed fusion dominates and the light gray region is forbidden due to $^6$Li overproduction
- At $\delta m \sim 0.1$ GeV, $^7$Li/H can be fitted to observational values without conflicting with other light element abundances
- The relic density is consistent with the allowed region at $Y_{\tilde{\tau}} = 2 \times 10^{-13}$ at 3 $\sigma$ for the high value of $^7$Li/H
- Cross sections of spallation processes are proportional to $\sin^2\theta_\tau$ and inversely proportional to $m_{\tilde{\tau}}$
- Reference values used: $m_{\tilde{\tau}} = 350$ GeV, $\sin\theta_\tau = 0.8$, $\gamma_\tau = 0$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of long-lived stau effects on BBN (Jittoh et al. 2007, 2008, 2010) by including $^4$He spallation processes that had been ignored due to the naive expectation that stau-catalyzed fusion (Pospelov 2006) would dominate. The spallation processes are only present in models with long-lived charged particles due to phase space suppression with weakly interacting daughter particles (not in gravitino LSP scenarios). The paper enables simultaneous resolution of the $^7$Li discrepancy and dark matter abundance constraints in specific MSSM parameter regions, complementing other proposed mechanisms (Jedamzik 2004, Bird 2007, Pospelov 2010, Kawasaki 2010, Erken 2011).
