# Generalized optical potential for weakly bound nuclei. I. Two-cluster projectiles

> A generalized optical potential for elastic scattering induced by light nuclei is calculated within the Feshbach projection operator method. The model explicitly takes into account the contribution of the projectile break-up continuum treated within a microscopic few-cluster model. In this work we formulate the model, deriving an explicit expression for the optical potential, and show ability of the model applying it to deuteron elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized optical potential for elastic scattering of weakly bound two-cluster projectiles, explicitly including the projectile break-up continuum via the Feshbach projection operator method, but the model neglects continuum-to-continuum coupling and approximates the total Green function by a separable form, which is justified when the collision time is shorter than the internal cluster-cluster motion time.

## Method

The Feshbach projection operator method is used to separate elastic and break-up channels, constructing the generalized optical potential as the sum of a cluster-folding term (local, from the projectile ground state folded with cluster-target interactions) and a non-local dynamical polarization potential (DPP) that couples elastic and break-up channels. The projectile internal structure is described within a microscopic two-cluster model, and the DPP is computed using a separable Green function approximated by the first term of a Born series, with the cluster-folding potential as the distorting potential. The resulting integro-differential Schrödinger equation for partial waves is solved by reducing it to a set of linear algebraic equations via finite difference and Simpson methods.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.22 MeV; r.m.s. radius: 1.97 fm; triplet scattering length: 5.46 fm (from the Gaussian p-n interaction used).
- For deuteron elastic scattering on heavy targets, calculations are performed at 80 MeV (⁵⁸Ni), 85 MeV (¹²⁰Sn), and 110 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb).
- The break-up transition matrix elements with even angular momentum transfer λ = 0, 2 dominate; the sum over λ is truncated at λ_max = 6, and integration over projectile excitation energy is performed up to 40 MeV.
- Non-diagonal terms in the polarization potential are about 10² times smaller than diagonal ones and are neglected.
- The cluster-folding potential alone significantly overestimates experimental cross sections at angles larger than the nuclear-rainbow angle; adding the DPP brings agreement with data.

## Context

This work extends earlier Feshbach-based generalized optical model studies of deuteron break-up (e.g., Testoni et al., Zagrebaev 1978) by removing simplifying assumptions: it includes particle spins, all partial waves of the continuum, Coulomb forces properly, and uses the total Green function instead of a free-particle Green function. It contrasts with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, which treats continuum-to-continuum coupling but becomes computationally difficult for few-body projectiles; the present approach is faster for such systems. The model is tested on deuteron elastic scattering and is intended for future application to reactions with weakly bound few-cluster nuclei such as ⁶He.
