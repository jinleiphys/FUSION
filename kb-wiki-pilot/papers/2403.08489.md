# Effect of the charge asymmetry and orbital angular momentum in the entrance channel on the hindrance to complete fusion

> The hindrance to complete fusion is studied as a function of the charge asymmetry of colliding nuclei and orbital angular momentum of the collision. The formation and evolution of a dinuclear system (DNS) in the heavy ion collisions at energies near the Coulomb barrier is calculated in the framework of the DNS model. The DNS evolution is considered as nucleon transfer between its fragments. The results prove that a hindrance at formation of a compound nucleus (CN) is related with the quasifission process which is breakup of the DNS into products instead to reach the equilibrated state of the CN. The role of the angular momentum in the charge (mass) distribution of the reaction products for the given mass asymmetry of the colliding nuclei has been demonstrated. The results of this work have been compared with the measured data for the quasifission yields in the $^{12}$C+$^{204}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca+$^{168}$Er reactions to show the role of the mass asymmetry of the entrance channel.

## Key claim

The hindrance to complete fusion is shown to depend on both the charge asymmetry of the colliding nuclei and the orbital angular momentum, with the condition that the quasifission process (breakup of the dinuclear system before reaching the compound nucleus) is the primary cause of this hindrance. The paper demonstrates that for the more charge-symmetric reaction (48Ca+168Er), the intrinsic fusion barrier is larger and the quasifission barrier is smaller compared to the more charge-asymmetric reaction (12C+204Pb), leading to greater fusion hindrance.

## Method

The evolution of the dinuclear system (DNS) formed after capture of the projectile by the target was calculated using the DNS model, treating the process as multinucleon transfer between the DNS fragments described by a transport master equation for the charge distribution. The potential energy surface (PES) was constructed from the energy balance Qgg and the nucleus-nucleus interaction potential (Coulomb, nuclear, and rotational parts), and the competition between complete fusion and quasifission was determined by comparing the heights of the intrinsic fusion barrier B*fus and the quasifission barrier Bqf. The yield of quasifission products was calculated as a function of charge number Z by summing over partial waves weighted by the capture probability and the decay width through the quasifission barrier.

## Key numbers

- For the 12C+204Pb reaction at ELab=73 MeV (CN excitation energy 40.4 MeV), the critical angular momentum Lcr was estimated as 31 hbar, and the reduced mass mu = 11.3 MeV.
- For the 48Ca+168Er reaction at ELab=153 MeV (CN excitation energy 39.6 MeV), the critical angular momentum Lcr was estimated as 54 hbar, and the reduced mass mu = 37.3 MeV.
- The asymmetric fission contribution in the 12C+204Pb reaction was only about 1.5%, while in the 48Ca+168Er reaction it was about 30%, as measured in Ref. [Chizhov2003].
- The quasifission barrier Bqf for the 12C+204Pb system (Z=6) was sufficiently larger than for the 48Ca+168Er system (Z=20), while the fusion barrier B*fus was smaller for the former reaction.

## Context

This work extends the DNS model approach previously applied to fusion reactions [Nasirov2005, Kayumov2022, Nasirov2024] by explicitly analyzing the role of both charge asymmetry and angular momentum in determining fusion hindrance. The results are compared with experimental data from the CORSET setup at JINR [Chizhov2003] for the 12C+204Pb and 48Ca+168Er reactions leading to the same compound nucleus 216Ra*. The findings enable better prediction of optimal projectile-target combinations for synthesizing superheavy elements, where the competition between quasifission and complete fusion is critical.
