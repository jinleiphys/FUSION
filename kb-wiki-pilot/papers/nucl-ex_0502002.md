# Effect of the break-up on the fusion and elastic scattering of weakly bound projectiles on $^{64}$Zn

> We study the behavior of the fusion, break-up, reaction and elastic scattering of different projectiles on $^{64}$Zn, at near and above barrier energies. We present fusion and elastic scattering data with the tightly bound $^{16}$O and the stable weakly bound $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li and $^{9}$Be projectiles. The data were analyzed by coupled channel calculations. The total fusion cross sections for these systems are not affected by the break-up process at energies above the barrier. The elastic (non-capture) break-up cross section is important at energies close and above the Coulomb barrier and increases the reaction cross sections. In addition we also show that the break-up process at near and sub-barrier energies is responsible for the vanishing of the usual threshold anomaly of the optical potential and give rise to a new type of anomaly.

## Key claim

The total fusion cross sections for the $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, and $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn systems are not affected by the break-up process at energies above the Coulomb barrier, but the elastic (non-capture) break-up cross section is significant at near-barrier and above-barrier energies and increases the total reaction cross section. The break-up process at near and sub-barrier energies causes the usual threshold anomaly of the optical potential to vanish and gives rise to a new type of anomaly, termed the Break-up Threshold Anomaly (BTA).

## Method

Total fusion cross sections were measured for $^{6}$Li + $^{64}$Zn at 16, 18, 20, 22, and 24 MeV and for $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn at 20, 22, and 24 MeV using the time-of-flight method at the TANDAR Laboratory. Elastic scattering angular distributions were measured for $^{16}$O, $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, and $^{9}$Be projectiles on $^{64}$Zn using surface barrier detectors at the Pelletron Laboratory of the Universidade de Sao Paulo. The data were analyzed by coupled channel calculations using the CCFULL code, and the elastic scattering was analyzed with the Sao Paulo Optical Potential (SPOP) to extract the energy dependence of the normalization factors for the real and imaginary potentials.

## Key numbers

- $^{6}$Li + $^{64}$Zn total fusion cross sections: 114 $\pm$ 13 mb at 16.0 MeV, 145 $\pm$ 14 mb at 18.0 MeV, 332 $\pm$ 25 mb at 20.0 MeV, 488 $\pm$ 41 mb at 22.0 MeV, 634 $\pm$ 48 mb at 24.0 MeV
- $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn total fusion cross sections: 140 $\pm$ 18 mb at 20.0 MeV, 472 $\pm$ 46 mb at 22.0 MeV, 747 $\pm$ 92 mb at 24.0 MeV
- $^{6}$Li + $^{64}$Zn reaction cross sections: 533 mb at 17.0 MeV, 878 mb at 20.0 MeV, 1094 mb at 22.0 MeV
- $^{7}$Li + $^{64}$Zn reaction cross sections: 854 mb at 20.0 MeV, 1100 mb at 22.0 MeV
- $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn reaction cross sections: 68 mb at 17.0 MeV, 199 mb at 19.0 MeV, 424 mb at 21.0 MeV, 590 mb at 23.0 MeV, 871 mb at 26.0 MeV, 1013 mb at 28.0 MeV
- For the $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn system, the incomplete fusion cross section (fusion of one alpha fragment) is less than 10% of the total fusion cross section
- The break-up threshold energies for the weakly bound projectiles are between 1.48 MeV and 2.45 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fusion and break-up processes for weakly bound projectiles on medium-mass targets, complementing earlier measurements for $^{6}$Li + $^{64}$Zn at higher energies (Padron et al., Physical Review C 66, 044608, 2002) and $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn using gamma-ray spectroscopy (Moraes et al., Physical Review C 61, 64608, 2000). The results contradict the expectation that break-up might suppress total fusion at above-barrier energies, and instead show that break-up primarily increases the reaction cross section through elastic break-up. The identification of the Break-up Threshold Anomaly (BTA) in the $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn system, where the imaginary potential increases rather than decreases as energy approaches the barrier, contrasts with the usual threshold anomaly observed for tightly bound systems like $^{16}$O + $^{64}$Zn (Tenreiro et al., Physical Review C 53, 2870, 1996) and provides a new framework for understanding the energy dependence of the optical potential for weakly bound projectiles.
