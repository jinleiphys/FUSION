# Big Bang Nucleosynthesis results refined via the Trojan Horse Method

> This work presents the Trojan Horse Method (THM) as a powerful technique for measuring nuclear reaction cross sections at astrophysical energies. We then explore the impact of THM-derived reaction rates on the predictions of Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (SBBN) using the PRIMAT code. Primordial abundances are shown for the single rate impact and, for the first time, also for all the THM rates together. The result shows significant differences with the use of THM rates, which in some cases goes in the direction of improving the agreement with the observations with respect to the use of only reaction rates from direct data, especially for the $^7$Li and deuterium abundances, which are still open issues for SBBN.

## Key claim

The combined use of six Trojan Horse Method (THM) reaction rates in the PRIMAT code produces significant differences in Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (SBBN) predictions compared to using only direct-data reaction rates, with improvements in the agreement with observed primordial abundances for deuterium and lithium-7, though the authors hedge that these results "in some cases goes in the direction of improving the agreement" rather than claiming definitive resolution of the cosmological lithium problem or deuterium tension.

## Method

The authors measured cross sections for six of the twelve primary reactions in the SBBN network using the Trojan Horse Method, which extracts bare-nucleus cross sections at astrophysical energies by studying three-body breakup reactions where the spectator particle remains unaffected. They implemented these THM-derived reaction rates individually and collectively into the PRIMAT code (version 0.2.2, 2023) to calculate primordial abundances, comparing results against both direct-data-only predictions and observational constraints for deuterium, helium-3, helium-4, lithium-7, and other light isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Combined THM rates yield Y_p = 0.24699^{+0.00005}_{-0.00006} for helium-4 mass fraction, compared to observed Y_p = 0.2453 ± 0.0034 (Aver et al.)
- Deuterium abundance with all THM rates: D/H × 10^5 = 2.61 ± 0.13, a 7.7% increase over the direct-data result
- Combined THM rates reduce the A=7 nuclei abundance (lithium-7 plus beryllium-7 decaying to lithium-7) by 13% relative to direct-data-only predictions
- All six THM rates together give (^7Li+^7Be)/H × 10^10 = 4.78, compared to observed ^7Li/H × 10^10 = 1.58^{+0.35}_{-0.28} (Sbordone et al.)
- Helium-3 plus tritium abundance with all THM rates: (^3He+T)/H × 10^5 = 1.06 ± 0.11
- ^6Li/H × 10^14 = 1.27373 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 1.18269; Coc2012 reference: 1.23)
- ^9Be/H × 10^19 = 11.92813 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 8.78250; Coc2012 reference: 9.60)
- ^11B/H × 10^16 = 2.56751 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 3.25468; Coc2012 reference: 3.05)
- CNO/H × 10^16 = 7.69346 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 7.82870; Coc2012 reference: 7.43)

## Context

This work extends previous THM applications to BBN reactions (Sparta et al. 2021, Pizzone et al. for ^3He(n,p)^3H, Hayakawa et al. 2021 for ^7Be(n,p)^7Li) by evaluating for the first time the combined impact of all six THM-measured reaction rates simultaneously using the updated PRIMAT code. The results address the cosmological lithium problem (CLiP), where SBBN predicts lithium-3 times higher than observed in stars (Sbordone et al. 2010), and the deuterium tension raised by Mossa et al. 2020 (D/H = 2.439 ± 0.037 × 10^5, a 1.8σ difference from CMB expectations). The findings suggest that precise THM measurements can reduce the discrepancy with observations and enable further investigations of other BBN reactions such as ^7Be(n,α)^4He and ^3H(d,n)^4He.
