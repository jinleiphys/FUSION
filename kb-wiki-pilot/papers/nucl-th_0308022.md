# Correlations and Fluctuations, A Summary of Quark Matter 2002

> Results for correlations and fluctuations presented at Quark Matter 2002 are summarized. These results include Hanbury-Brown Twiss interferometry of a wide variety of species, large scale fluctuations and correlations in $p_t$ and multiplicity, and charge fluctuations and charge balance functions.

## Key claim

The HBT puzzle persists: all hydrodynamic, Boltzmann, and hybrid models consistently overestimate both the longitudinal source size \(R_{\rm long}\) and the ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) at RHIC, despite fitting single-particle spectra. The extracted entropy per pion is significantly lower than expected for a thermal Bose gas, suggesting the initial state may not have been an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma but could have involved coherent fields or under-populated degrees of freedom.

## Method

The paper summarizes experimental results presented at Quark Matter 2002, covering Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) interferometry for pions, kaons, \(\Lambda\Lambda\), \(\Lambda p\), \(K_sK_s\), and two-photon correlations; fluctuations in transverse momentum and multiplicity; and charge fluctuations and balance functions. Source sizes were extracted from Gaussian fits to correlation functions, and phase-space densities were derived model-independently by combining HBT radii with single-particle spectra. Balance functions were constructed from the relative rapidity distribution of \(\pi^+\)-\(\pi^-\) pairs.

## Key numbers

- \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) at RHIC: \(\approx 0.85\) (PHENIX) to \(\approx 1.15\) (PHOBOS)
- Upper limit on emission duration: \(\Delta\tau < 10\) fm/c
- Mean emission time from \(R_{\rm long}\): \(\langle\tau\rangle \approx 10\) fm/c, rather than the expected 15-20 fm/c
- Blast wave outer radius: 13 fm, outer velocity \(\sim 0.7c\), breakup temperature \(\sim 110\) MeV
- Phase space density at low momentum: overpopulated by nearly a factor of two relative to a thermal Bose gas at the same temperature
- Entropy per pion: \(\sim 3\) units, significantly lower than expected for a Bose gas
- Direct photon fraction from WA98 two-photon HBT: 10% of photons are direct (not from \(\pi_0\) or \(\eta\) decays)
- \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) from CERES at SPS: near unity; from NA49: \(\sim 1.15\)

## Context

This summary extends the long-standing HBT puzzle from SPS to RHIC energies, where earlier work by Bertsch, Gong, and Tohyama (1988) and Gyulassy and Rischke (1996) had predicted a long-lived source from a first-order QGP phase transition. The results contradict hydrodynamic treatments by Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak; Huovinen; Soff, Bass, and Dumitru; and Kolb and Heinz, all of which overpredict source sizes. The charge balance functions from STAR, narrowing with centrality, support the delayed-hadronization picture proposed by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Bass, Danielewicz, and Pratt (2000). The two-photon HBT result from WA98 enables direct extraction of the direct photon spectrum, a new probe of the early stage.
