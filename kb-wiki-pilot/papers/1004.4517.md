# Charged-Particle Thermonuclear Reaction Rates: II. Tables and Graphs of Reaction Rates and Probability Density Functions

> Numerical values of charged-particle thermonuclear reaction rates for nuclei in the A=14 to 40 region are tabulated. The results are obtained using a method, based on Monte Carlo techniques, that has been described in the preceding paper of this series (Paper I). We present a low rate, median rate and high rate which correspond to the 0.16, 0.50 and 0.84 quantiles, respectively, of the cumulative reaction rate distribution. The meaning of these quantities is in general different from the commonly reported, but statistically meaningless expressions, "lower limit", "nominal value" and "upper limit" of the total reaction rate. In addition, we approximate the Monte Carlo probability density function of the total reaction rate by a lognormal distribution and tabulate the lognormal parameters μ and σ at each temperature. We also provide a quantitative measure (Anderson-Darling test statistic) for the reliability of the lognormal approximation. The user can implement the approximate lognormal reaction rate probability density functions directly in a stellar model code for studies of stellar energy generation and nucleosynthesis. For each reaction, the Monte Carlo reaction rate probability density functions, together with their lognormal approximations, are displayed graphically for selected temperatures in order to provide a visual impression. Our new reaction rates are appropriate for bare nuclei in the laboratory. The nuclear physics input used to derive our reaction rates is presented in the subsequent paper of this series (Paper III). In the fourth paper of this series (Paper IV) we compare our new reaction rates to previous results.

## Key claim

The paper provides tabulated charged-particle thermonuclear reaction rates for nuclei in the A=14 to 40 region using a Monte Carlo method, presenting low, median, and high rates corresponding to the 0.16, 0.50, and 0.84 quantiles of the cumulative distribution rather than the statistically meaningless "lower limit," "nominal value," and "upper limit" used in previous work. The rates are appropriate for bare nuclei in the laboratory and must be corrected for electron screening at elevated densities and thermal excitations of the target nucleus at elevated temperatures before use in stellar models.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo technique (described in Paper I) where each nuclear input quantity (resonance energies, strengths, S-factors, partial widths) is assigned a specific probability density function (Gaussian, lognormal, or Porter-Thomas), random values are drawn for each quantity, and the total reaction rate is computed from these sample values. The procedure is repeated at least 5,000 times per reaction to estimate the output reaction rate probability density function, with correlations between quantities carefully considered (e.g., the same random resonance energy value used in both Boltzmann factor and penetration factor). The total reaction rate probability density function is approximated by a lognormal distribution with parameters μ and σ tabulated at each temperature, and the Anderson-Darling test statistic quantifies the reliability of this approximation.

## Key numbers

- Temperature grid ranges from T=0.01 GK to 10 GK for all 62 reactions evaluated
- For 28Si(p,γ)29P at T=0.03 GK: median rate = 5.57×10⁻²⁵ cm³mol⁻¹s⁻¹, low rate = 3.79×10⁻²⁵ cm³mol⁻¹s⁻¹, high rate = 8.18×10⁻²⁵ cm³mol⁻¹s⁻¹; lognormal parameters μ=-55.85, σ=0.386; Anderson-Darling statistic = 0.134
- For 35Ar(p,γ)36K at T=0.03 GK: median rate = 7.65×10⁻³¹ cm³mol⁻¹s⁻¹, low rate = 5.16×10⁻³¹ cm³mol⁻¹s⁻¹, high rate = 1.17×10⁻³⁰ cm³mol⁻¹s⁻¹; lognormal parameters μ=-69.11, σ=1.436; Anderson-Darling statistic = 3377
- For 28Si(p,γ)29P at T=0.3 GK: lognormal σ=0.104, indicating a nearly Gaussian distribution; Anderson-Darling statistic = 0.532
- The 0.16, 0.50, and 0.84 quantiles correspond to a 68% confidence level (coverage probability)
- A value of Anderson-Darling test statistic less than approximately 1 indicates consistency with lognormal distribution; values in the range 1-30 indicate rejection but reasonable approximation; values exceeding approximately 30 indicate visual deviation from lognormality

## Context

This work (Paper II of a series) extends the Monte Carlo method for evaluating thermonuclear reaction rates presented in Paper I to nuclei in the A=14 to 40 mass range, providing statistically meaningful quantile-based rates that replace the classical "lower limit," "nominal value," and "upper limit" approach. The nuclear physics input used to derive these rates is presented in Paper III, and comparison to previous results is made in Paper IV. The rates are intended for use in stellar model calculations of energy generation and nucleosynthesis, with the lognormal approximation enabling direct implementation in reaction network codes, though the authors note that reactions on lighter targets (A<14) require different procedures such as the R-matrix approach used by Descouvemont et al. for Big Bang nucleosynthesis.
