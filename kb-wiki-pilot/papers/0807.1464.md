# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering and Fusion Cross Section Data for the 9Be+28Si, 144Sm, and 208Pb Systems at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies using Double Folding Potential

> Based on the extended optical model with the double folding potential, in which the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts, simultaneous $χ^{2}$ analyses are performed of elastic scattering and fusion cross section data for the $^{9}$Be+$^{28}$Si, $^{144}$Sm, and $^{208}$Pb systems at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. We find that the real part of the resultant DR part of the polarization potential is systematically repulsive for all the targets considered, which is consistent with the results deduced from the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations taking into account the polarization effects due to breakup. Further, it is found that both DR and fusion parts of the extracted polarization potentials satisfy the dispersion relation.

## Key claim
The real part of the direct reaction (DR) polarization potential is systematically repulsive for all three targets, consistent with CDCC calculations of breakup polarization effects, but this result is conditional on the simultaneous chi-squared analysis of both elastic scattering and fusion cross section data using the double folding potential.

## Method
Simultaneous chi-squared analyses of elastic scattering angular distributions and fusion excitation functions were performed for the 9Be+28Si, 144Sm, and 208Pb systems at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. The optical model potential was constructed from a double folding potential plus a polarization potential decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts. The DR and fusion parts were extracted by fitting both data sets simultaneously, and their real and imaginary components were checked against the dispersion relation.

## Key numbers
- For 9Be+28Si: elastic scattering data at energies from 12 MeV to 20 MeV; fusion cross sections measured from 10 MeV to 20 MeV.
- For 9Be+144Sm: elastic scattering data at energies from 40 MeV to 52 MeV; fusion cross sections measured from 36 MeV to 52 MeV.
- For 9Be+208Pb: elastic scattering data at energies from 38 MeV to 50 MeV; fusion cross sections measured from 34 MeV to 50 MeV.
- The real part of the DR polarization potential was found to be repulsive (positive) for all three targets, with magnitude varying with target mass and incident energy.

## Context
This work extends the extended optical model approach previously applied to stable projectiles, now applied to the weakly-bound 9Be projectile where breakup effects are significant. The results are consistent with CDCC calculations that account for breakup polarization effects, and the extracted potentials satisfy the dispersion relation linking their real and imaginary parts. This enables future systematic studies of breakup effects on fusion and elastic scattering for other weakly-bound nuclei.
