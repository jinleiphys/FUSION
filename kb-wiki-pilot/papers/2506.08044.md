# Estimation of Electron Screening Potential in the 6Li(d,α)4He Reaction Using Multi-Layer Perceptron Neural Network

> Reactions between light charged nuclei at sub-Coulomb energies are crucial in astrophysical environments, but accurate cross-section measurements are hindered by electron screening. Traditional methods, including polynomial extrapolation and the Trojan Horse Method, often yield screening potentials exceeding adiabatic predictions. Building on the success of an MLP-based Artificial Neural Network (ANN) for the 6Li(p, α)3He reaction [1], this work applies the same approach to the 6Li(d, α)4He reaction. Experimental astrophysical S-factor data from literature are reanalyzed using the ANN to model the energy-dependent S-factor. The bare S-factor is extracted from data above 70 keV, where screening effects are minimal, and the screening potential is obtained by comparing with the low-energy region. The resulting screening potential is 147.95 eV, demonstrating the robustness of ANN-based methods for evaluating electron screening in low-energy nuclear reactions involving light nuclei.

# Estimation of Electron Screening Potential in the 6Li(d,α)4He Reaction Using Multi-Layer Perceptron Neural Network

## Key claim

The electron screening potential for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction is determined to be 147.95 eV using a Multi-Layer Perceptron Artificial Neural Network, which agrees with the R-matrix value of 149.5 eV from Grineviciute et al. (2015) but is lower than the adiabatic prediction of 175 eV and substantially lower than earlier polynomial and Trojan Horse Method extractions (330-380 eV). The claim is conditional on the assumption that screening effects are negligible above 70 keV center-of-mass energy, where the bare S-factor is extracted.

## Method

Experimental astrophysical S-factor data for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction from the EXFOR database (Engstler et al. 1992, Spitaleri et al. 2001) were reanalyzed using a feedforward Multi-Layer Perceptron neural network with two hidden layers (32 and 16 neurons), tanh activation, LBFGS solver, and physics-informed polynomial feature expansion up to second degree. The ANN was trained on data above 70 keV to model the bare S-factor, then the screening potential was extracted by comparing the predicted bare S-factor with the total (screened) S-factor in the low-energy region using the relation S(E) = S_b(E) exp(U_e πη/E).

## Key numbers

- Electron screening potential U_e = 147.95 eV (no uncertainty quoted)
- Bare S-factor at zero energy S_b(0) = 20.86 MeV·barn
- Coefficient of determination R² = 0.982 (training), 0.971 (testing)
- Normalized Mean Squared Error = 4.3% (training), 5% (testing)
- Comparison values from literature: U_e = 380 ± 250 eV (Engstler atomic target, polynomial), 330 ± 120 eV (Engstler molecular target, polynomial), 340 ± 51 eV (Spitaleri, Trojan Horse Method), 248 eV (Barker, polynomial fit), 190 ± 50 eV (Ruprecht et al.), 149.5 eV (Grineviciute, R-matrix)
- Adiabatic theoretical limit: U_e = 175 eV

## Context

This work extends the same MLP-based ANN methodology previously applied to the 6Li(p,α)3He reaction (Chattopadhyay 2024) to the 6Li(d,α)4He system, which is a key destruction pathway for 6Li in astrophysical environments. The result (147.95 eV) agrees closely with the R-matrix analysis of Grineviciute et al. (2015) at 149.5 eV, while being significantly lower than values obtained from polynomial extrapolation (Engstler et al. 1992, Barker 2002) and the Trojan Horse Method (Spitaleri et al. 2001). The approach enables a data-driven alternative to traditional extrapolation methods for extracting screening potentials in light-ion reactions at sub-Coulomb energies.
