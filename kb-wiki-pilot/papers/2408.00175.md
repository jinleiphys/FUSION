# The art of modeling nuclear reactions with weakly bound nuclei: status and perspectives

> We give an overview of the theoretical description of nuclear reactions involving weakly-bound nuclei. Some of the more widespread reaction formalisms employed in the analysis of these reactions are briefly introduced, including various recent developments. We put special emphasis on the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method and its extensions to incorporate core and target excitations as well as its application to three-body projectiles. The role of the continuum for one-nucleon transfer reactions is also discussed. The problem of the evaluation of inclusive breakup cross sections is addressed within the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model. Other methods, such as those based on a semiclasical description of the scattering process, are also briefly introduced and some of their applications are discussed and a brief discussion on topics of current interest, such as nucleon-nucleon correlations, uncertainty evaluation and non-locality is presented.

# The art of modeling nuclear reactions with weakly bound nuclei: status and perspectives

## Key claim

The paper provides a comprehensive overview of theoretical frameworks for nuclear reactions involving weakly bound nuclei, with the central assertion that the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method and its extensions, incorporating core and target excitations and three-body projectiles, remain the most successful approach, but that no single formalism is sufficient; rather, the choice depends on the specific reaction mechanism and observable under study.

## Method

The authors review several reaction formalisms applied to weakly bound nuclei, with primary emphasis on the CDCC method, which expands the three-body wavefunction in eigenstates of the projectile Hamiltonian including both bound and unbound states, discretizing the continuum via pseudo-state or binning methods. They also discuss the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model for inclusive breakup cross sections, semiclassical descriptions of scattering, and the optical model with Feshbach polarization potentials. The review covers applications to deuteron scattering, halo nuclei such as \(^{6}\)He and \(^{11}\)Li, and Borromean systems like \(^{9}\)Be and \(^{6}\)He.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(E_b = 2.22\) MeV; proton-neutron separation: approximately 3.8 fm
- Deuteron dipole polarizability extracted from sub-Coulomb scattering on \(^{208}\)Pb: \(\alpha = 0.70 \pm 0.05\) fm\(^3\) (Rodning et al., 1982)
- Complete fusion suppression for weakly bound stable nuclei (\(^{6,7}\)Li, \(^{9}\)Be): approximately 20–30% compared to tightly bound nuclei
- Optical model parameters for \(^{4}\)He+\(^{208}\)Pb at 22 MeV: \(V_0 = 96.44\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.085\) fm, \(a_0 = 0.625\) fm, \(W_v = 32\) MeV, \(r_i = 0.958\) fm, \(a_i = 0.42\) fm
- Optical model parameters for \(^{6}\)He+\(^{208}\)Pb at 22 MeV: \(V_0 = 124.8\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.085\) fm, \(a_0 = 0.564\) fm, \(W_v = 6.8\) MeV, \(r_i = 0.958\) fm, \(a_i = 1.91\) fm (significantly larger imaginary diffuseness)

## Context

This review extends the long tradition of reaction theory for weakly bound systems, tracing back to low-energy deuteron scattering problems recognized decades earlier, and builds upon the foundational CDCC work by Rawitscher (1974) and the Pittsburgh-Kyushu collaboration (Yahiro, Austern, 1986–1987). It connects to experimental programs measuring interaction cross sections of halo nuclei (Tanihata et al., 1985), narrow momentum distributions (Kobayashi et al., 1988), and fusion suppression studies (Dasgupta, 1999–2004). The paper enables future work on nucleon-nucleon correlations, uncertainty quantification, and non-locality in reaction modeling.
