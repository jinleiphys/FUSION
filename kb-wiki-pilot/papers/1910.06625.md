# Unraveling the reaction mechanisms leading to partial fusion of weakly bound nuclei

> Collisions between complex nuclei may give rise to their total or partial fusion. The latter case is found experimentally to gain importance when one of the colliding nuclei is weakly bound. It has been commonly assumed that the partial fusion mechanism is a two-step process, whose first step is the dissociation of the weakly bound nucleus, followed by the capture of one of the fragments. To assess this interpretation, we present the first implementation of the three-body model of inclusive breakup proposed in the 1980s by Austern et al. [Phys. Rep. 154, 125 (1987)] that accounts for both the direct, one-step, partial fusion and the two-step mechanism proceeding via the projectile continuum states. Contrary to the widely assumed picture, we find that, at least for the investigated cases, the partial fusion is largely dominated by the direct capture from the projectile ground-state.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first implementation of the three-body IAV-CDCC model for inclusive breakup and finds that, contrary to the widely assumed breakup-fusion picture, partial fusion (incomplete fusion) is largely dominated by direct capture from the projectile ground state rather than a two-step mechanism proceeding through projectile dissociation, at least for the investigated cases and energy ranges.

## Method
The authors implemented the three-body version of the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model using continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) wavefunctions to describe the projectile-target scattering. They applied this IAV-CDCC model to the reactions $^{93}$Nb($d$,$pX$) at 25.5 MeV and $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi at several energies around the Coulomb barrier, comparing full three-body calculations with calculations retaining only the projectile ground-state component and with the DWBA approximation.

## Key numbers
- For $^{93}$Nb($d$,$pX$) at $E_d=25.5$ MeV, the IAV-DWBA, IAV-CDCC, and IAV-CDCC(gs) calculations give very similar results for the non-elastic breakup differential cross section at $E_p=14$ MeV
- For $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi at 40 MeV, omission of the two-step mechanism (IAV-CDCC(gs)) results in an 11% underestimation of the NEB cross section relative to full IAV-CDCC
- The IAV-DWBA differs from full IAV-CDCC by only 7% at $E=40$ MeV for $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi
- For the most weakly bound case tested ($S_{\alpha d}=1$ MeV), the IAV-CDCC(gs) underestimates the full IAV-CDCC result by approximately 11%
- The overall effect of the two-step mechanism is less than 12% for all explored incident and binding energies

## Context
This work extends the IAV model originally proposed by Austern et al. [Phys. Rep. 154, 125 (1987)] by implementing the full three-body CDCC wavefunction, going beyond the DWBA approximation used in previous applications by the same authors (Jin et al., 2015, 2017, 2019). The results contradict the commonly assumed breakup-fusion picture (Udagawa and Tamura, 1981; IAV, 1985) and support recent experimental suggestions (Cook et al., 2019) that incomplete fusion proceeds via a direct, one-step mechanism. This enables future studies to use the computationally simpler DWBA approximation with confidence for these systems.
