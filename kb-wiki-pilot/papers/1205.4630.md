# Coupled-Channel Effects in Collisions between Heavy Ions near the Coulomb Barrier

> With the recent availability of state-of-the-art heavy-ion stable and radioactive beams, there has been a renew interest in the investigation of nuclear reactions with heavy ions. I first present the role of inelastic and transfer channel couplings in fusion reactions induced by stable heavy ions. Analysis of experimental fusion cross sections by using standard coupled-channel calculations is discussed. The role of multi-neutron transfer is investigated in the fusion process below the Coulomb barrier by analyzing $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr as benchmark reactions. The enhancement of fusion cross sections for $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr is well reproduced at sub-barrier energies by NTFus code calculations including the coupling of the neutron-transfer channels following the Zagrebaev semi-classical model. Similar effects for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr fusion excitation functions are found. The breakup coupling in both the elastic scattering and in the fusion process induced by weakly bound stable projectiles is also shown to be crucial. In this lecture, full coupled-channel calculations of the fusion excitation functions are performed by using the breakup coupling for the more neutron-rich reaction and for the more weakly bound projectiles. I clearly demonstrate that Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel calculations are capable to reproduce the fusion enhancement from the breakup coupling in $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co.

# Coupled-Channel Effects in Collisions between Heavy Ions near the Coulomb Barrier

## Key claim
The enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections for neutron-rich systems such as $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr can be reproduced by coupled-channel calculations that include multi-neutron transfer couplings via the Zagrebaev semiclassical model, but only when the Q-values for neutron transfer are positive; for systems with negative Q-values (e.g., $^{32}$S+$^{90}$Zr), no such enhancement is observed and standard coupled-channel calculations without transfer couplings suffice.

## Method
The author analyzed experimental fusion excitation functions for $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr using the NTFus coupled-channel code, which incorporates neutron-transfer channel couplings via the Zagrebaev semiclassical model. For weakly bound projectiles ($^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co), Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) calculations were performed to study the role of breakup coupling on elastic scattering and fusion. The NTFus code calculates transmission coefficients using a modified Hill-Wheeler formula that includes neutron-transfer probabilities and Q-values, with the barrier distribution function extracted from experimental data via the three-point difference method.

## Key numbers
- $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr: sub-barrier fusion cross sections enhanced relative to $^{32}$S+$^{90}$Zr, reproduced by NTFus calculations including neutron-transfer couplings up to +4n channels (with similar agreement up to +5n and +6n)
- $^{96}$Zr: 2$^{+}$ state at 1.748 MeV and 3$^{-}$ state at 1.897 MeV [B(E3;3$^{-}$ $\rightarrow$ 0$^{+}$) = 51 W.u.]
- $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co: CDCC calculations with breakup coupling reproduce elastic scattering angular distributions at incident energies of 12, 18, 26, and 30 MeV
- $^{32}$S+$^{90}$Zr: no significant over-estimation at sub-barrier energies when neutron-transfer couplings are omitted
- $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr: similar neutron-transfer effects found in earlier work by Timmers et al. (1998)

## Context
This work extends previous coupled-channel analyses of sub-barrier fusion, particularly the studies of $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr by Timmers et al. (Nucl. Phys. A633, 421, 1998) and the Zagrebaev semiclassical model (Phys. Rev. C 67, 061601, 2003). It also builds on quasi-elastic scattering measurements by Yang et al. (Phys. Rev. C 77, 014601, 2008) for the same $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr systems. The CDCC analysis of $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co extends earlier work by Beck et al. (Phys. Rev. C 67, 054602, 2003) and provides a framework for understanding breakup effects in reactions induced by weakly bound and halo nuclei, enabling future studies with radioactive ion beams at facilities such as SPIRAL at GANIL.
