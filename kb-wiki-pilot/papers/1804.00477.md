# $^9$Be+$^{120}$Sn scattering at near-barrier energies within a four body model

> Cross sections for elastic and inelastic scattering of the weakly-bound $^9$Be nucleus on a $^{120}$Sn target have been measured at seven bombarding energies around and above the Coulomb barrier. The elastic angular distributions are analyzed with a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculation, which considers $^9$Be as a three-body projectile ($α$ + $α$ + n). An optical model analysis using the São Paulo potential is also shown for comparison. The CDCC analysis shows that the coupling to the continuum part of the spectrum is important for the agreement with experimental data even at energies around the Coulomb barrier, suggesting that breakup is an important process at low energies. At the highest incident energies, two inelastic peaks are observed at 1.19(5) and 2.41(5) MeV. Coupled-channels (CC) calculations using a rotational model confirm that the first inelastic peak corresponds to the excitation of the 2$_1^+$ state in $^{120}$Sn, while the second one likely corresponds to the excitation of the 3$_1^-$ state.

## Key claim

The four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) analysis shows that coupling to the continuum part of the spectrum is important for agreement with experimental data even at energies around the Coulomb barrier, suggesting that breakup is an important process at low energies; however, at 29.5 and 28 MeV the calculations underestimate the data in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region, an effect also seen for the $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb system.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions were measured for $^9$Be on $^{120}$Sn at seven bombarding energies (26, 27, 28, 29.5, 31, 42, and 50 MeV) using an array of eight surface-barrier detectors at the TANDAR laboratory. The elastic data were analyzed with four-body CDCC calculations treating $^9$Be as a three-body projectile ($\alpha + \alpha + n$) using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, and compared with optical model calculations using the São Paulo potential. Inelastic peaks were analyzed with coupled-channels (CC) calculations using a rotational model for $^{120}$Sn.

## Key numbers

- Seven bombarding energies measured: $E_{\rm lab}=26$, 27, 28, 29.5, 31, 42, and 50 MeV
- Two inelastic peaks observed at excitation energies of 1.19(5) MeV and 2.41(5) MeV
- Calculated ground-state energy of $^9$Be: $\varepsilon_B=-1.574$ MeV (experimental: $-1.5736$ MeV)
- Calculated rms matter radius of $^9$Be: $r_{\rm mat}=2.466$ fm (experimental: $2.53$ fm)
- Total reaction cross sections from CDCC calculations range from 191 mb (26 MeV) to 1832 mb (50 MeV)
- Total breakup cross sections from CDCC calculations range from 38.4 mb (26 MeV) to 140.4 mb (50 MeV)
- Coulomb barrier for the system: $V_{\rm C} \sim 28$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $^9$Be-induced reactions on $^{208}$Pb and $^{27}$Al (Refs. Wooll04, Gomes04, Yu10) and on $^{144}$Sm (Ref. gom09) to the intermediate-mass target $^{120}$Sn, which has been studied with other weakly-bound projectiles such as $^{6,7}$Li (Ref. sou10), $^6$He (Ref. net10), and $^8$Li (Ref. net09). The four-body CDCC approach follows the methodology of Refs. JCasal15 and Descouvemont15 for $^9$Be scattering, and the results enable future comparisons with breakup angular distribution measurements planned at the TANDAR Laboratory.
