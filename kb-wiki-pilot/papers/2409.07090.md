# Unraveling the Effects of Cluster Transfer-Induced Breakups on $^{12}$C Fragmentation in Hadron Therapy

> The capability of standard Geant4 PhysicsLists to address the fragmentation of $^{12}$C$-^{12}$C was assessed through a comparative analysis with experimental cross sections reported by Divay et al. and Dudouet et al. The standard PhysicsLists were found to be inadequate in explaining the fragmentation systematics. To address this limitation, the breakup component of fragmentation was systematically integrated into the standard PhysicsList, which successfully replicated the differential and double differential cross sections for $α$ production. This breakup component was modeled using fresco CDCC-CRC calculations. This novel physics process was then incorporated into the Geant4 framework, facilitating the calculation of dose distributions in water and tissue. The application of this method demonstrated a precise reproduction of the dose deposited at the Bragg peak region, corroborating the experimental data from Liedner et al., thereby enhancing the accurate visibility of dose tailing.

## Key claim
Standard Geant4 PhysicsLists (specifically QBBC_ABLA) fail to reproduce measured differential and double-differential cross sections for alpha particle production in $^{12}$C-$^{12}$C fragmentation, but this discrepancy can be resolved by coherently adding a direct-reaction breakup component modeled with the fresco CDCC-CRC approach, which includes both transfer-induced and inelastic breakup mechanisms.

## Method
The authors first benchmarked the QBBC_ABLA PhysicsList in Geant4 against experimental angular distributions and energy spectra for $^{12}$C($^{12}$C,xp) and $^{12}$C($^{12}$C,x$\alpha$) at 50 and 95 MeV/u. To address the observed discrepancies, they modeled the direct alpha production component using the fresco CDCC-CRC framework, considering two pathways: transfer-induced breakup (populating $^8$Be + $^{16}$O) and inelastic breakup (populating $^{12}$C states above the 3-alpha threshold). The calculated breakup cross sections were then incorporated as a new physics process into the Geant4 framework alongside the standard QBBC_ABLA model to simulate dose distributions in water and tissue.

## Key numbers
- At 50 MeV/u, the QBBC_ABLA model alone underpredicts alpha cross sections at forward angles below 10 degrees and overpredicts them above 10 degrees; the discrepancy is on the order of several magnitudes at mid-backward angles.
- At 95 MeV/u, the QBBC_ABLA model fails to reproduce the alpha particle angular distribution and energy spectra across all measured angles.
- The CDCC-CRC calculation for transfer-induced breakup included 21 excited states of $^{16}$O up to 13 MeV and 4 excited states of $^8$Be; the breakup continuum was discretized in wave number intervals of 0.1 fm$^{-1}$ up to 20 MeV.
- The excitation function for inclusive alpha production was calculated for energies up to 500 MeV/u and compared with experimental data at 50, 62, 95, and 400 MeV/u.
- Dose simulations for a 280 MeV/u $^{12}$C beam on water, incorporating the breakup component, reproduced the experimental Bragg peak dose profile from Liedner et al., including the dose tail region.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of $^{12}$C fragmentation beyond the statistical and phenomenological models (QMD, BIC, INCL++, Fermi breakups) previously assessed by Dudouet et al., which failed to reproduce alpha particle spectra. By integrating direct reaction mechanisms (transfer-induced and inelastic breakup) calculated with the CDCC-CRC method in fresco, the authors provide a physics-driven framework that successfully reproduces experimental data from Divay et al. and Dudouet et al. This enables more accurate dose calculations for hadron therapy treatment planning, particularly for the dose tail beyond the Bragg peak, and suggests that future Monte Carlo simulations should incorporate such quantum-mechanical breakup processes rather than relying solely on phenomenological models.
