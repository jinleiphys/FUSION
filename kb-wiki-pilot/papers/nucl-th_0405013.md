# New Forms of QCD Matter Discovered at RHIC

> We discuss two special limiting forms of QCD matter which may be produced at RHIC. We conclude from the available empirical evidence that an equilibrated, but strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma has been made in such collisions. We also discuss the growing body of evidence that its source is a Color Glass Condensate.

## Key claim

Based on the first three years of RHIC data, the authors conclude that an equilibrated but strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) has been produced in Au+Au collisions, and that there is growing evidence its source is a Color Glass Condensate (CGC); however, they note that the QGP hypothesis applies only to observables least influenced by poorly understood hadronic final-state dynamics, and that the CGC hypothesis is newer and "somewhat more tenuous" than the QGP hypothesis.

## Method

The authors review published experimental data from the four RHIC experiments (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR) on p+p, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies √s = 20–200 AGeV, comparing them to lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state, hydrodynamic models of collective flow, and perturbative QCD predictions for jet quenching and particle production. They analyze three convergent lines of empirical evidence: collective flow patterns (particularly elliptic flow v₂), suppression of high-pT hadrons (jet quenching), and the centrality and energy dependence of particle multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Energy density bounds from RHIC multiplicity data: 2–3 GeV/fm³ ≤ ε ≤ 20–30 GeV/fm³, compared to nuclear matter density ~0.15 GeV/fm³ and neutron star core density ~1 GeV/fm³
- Critical temperature for QGP transition: T_c ≈ 160 MeV, with energy density changing by about an order of magnitude over ΔT ~ 10–20 MeV
- Number of degrees of freedom: ~40–50 in QGP phase (1–3)T_c versus 3 in pion gas below T_c
- Saturation scale at RHIC: 1/Q_s ~ 0.2 fm/c (~5×10⁻²⁵ sec), comparable to the Lorentz-contracted nuclear crossing time
- RHIC energy range: √s = 20–200 AGeV, with fourth-year integrated luminosity ~1400/μb
- Sound velocity c_s² drops below 1/3 for T < 2T_c ≈ 300 MeV

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework developed over 30 years from the Bevalac, SIS, AGS, and SPS experiments, building on Bjorken's relativistic hydrodynamic theory (1982) and the Collins-Perry prediction of deconfined QCD matter (1974). It synthesizes the first three years of RHIC data to argue that the strongly coupled QGP (sQGP) regime, distinct from the weakly coupled plasma expected at asymptotically high temperatures, has been discovered, and that the CGC initial state, motivated by HERA data on gluon distribution functions, provides a rigorous QCD description of the initial conditions. The work enables future systematic studies of these matter forms at RHIC, the LHC, and proposed eRHIC facilities.
