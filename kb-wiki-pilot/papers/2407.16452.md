# The Hidden Variables: Harnessing Half-Shell Potentials for Enhanced Precision in Nuclear Reaction Calculations

> We explore the impact of half-shell components on nuclear reaction calculations, focusing on nonelastic breakup cross sections within the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model. By advocating for the use of a consistent Single Folding Model (SFM) for all optical potentials in IAV calculations, we aim to reduce the uncertainties associated with half-shell components and enhance agreement with experimental data. We present results from deuteron-induced reactions on $^{60}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb, which serve as surrogate targets for neutron-induced reactions on short-lived nuclei. The application of consistent optical potentials derived from the SFM shows improved alignment with experimental data compared to traditional global phenomenological potentials. Furthermore, we investigate the $^{59}$Co($^6$Li,$αX$) reaction, which reveals that the half-shell $T$-matrix plays a pivotal role in accurately modeling nuclear reactions. Our findings suggest that a unified approach to optical potentials, accounting for half-shell effects, is critical for a precise understanding of complex nuclear reactions. This work highlights the significance of the internal dynamics of the wave function, particularly in lighter targets, and underscores the importance of the half-shell $T$-matrix as a previously underappreciated variable in reaction calculations.

## Key claim

The half-shell T-matrix, which cannot be directly constrained by two-body elastic scattering data, acts as a "hidden variable" that significantly impacts nonelastic breakup cross sections within the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model; using a consistent Single Folding Model (SFM) for all optical potentials in IAV calculations reduces uncertainties from half-shell components and improves agreement with experimental data, particularly for lighter targets where internal wave function dynamics are more influential.

## Method

The authors employed the IAV model to compute nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross sections for deuteron-induced reactions on ⁶⁰Ni and ²⁰⁸Pb, and for the ⁵⁹Co(⁶Li,αX) reaction. They compared results using three different optical potential sets for the projectile-target interaction: the Single Folding Model (SFM) based on the KD02 nucleon-target interaction, and two global phenomenological potentials (Han-Shi-Shen and An-Cai). The half-shell T-matrices and radial scattering wave functions were computed for each potential to isolate the effects of half-shell components on the NEB cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the IAV model framework (Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent, 1985) by addressing systematic errors from inconsistent optical potentials in breakup reaction calculations. The half-shell T-matrix concept connects to earlier work by Polyzou and Glöckle (1990) showing that half-shell NN interactions can mimic three-nucleon force effects, and to the Phillips line in three-nucleon systems. The consistent SFM approach using KD02 nucleon-target interactions (Koning and Delaroche, 2003) provides a unified baseline that reduces discrepancies from different interior wave function behaviors. This enables more reliable surrogate reaction calculations for neutron-induced reactions on short-lived nuclei and improved modeling of knockout reactions.
