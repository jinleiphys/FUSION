# Inclusive breakup of three-body projectiles: A unified four-body framework for pair-detected and single-particle observables

> Inclusive breakup of three-body projectiles $a=i+j+k$ on a target $A$ admits two distinct inclusive observables: detection of a correlated pair $b=(ij)$ with $k+A$ unresolved, and detection of a single particle $i$ with $jk+A$ unresolved. A four-body DWBA sum-rule framework is derived for both channels from a common Hamiltonian. For the pair-detected channel, the unresolved propagator remains the two-body $k+A$ Green function and all three-body projectile effects enter through a pair-projected source built from $Φ_a$; a reference pair-target optical interaction splits this source into a target-elastic reference part and an explicit pair-target coupling part, yielding a state-resolved semi-inclusive coincidence observable and an amplitude-level diagnostic of the two-body cluster approximation. For the single-particle channel, the unresolved propagator is the three-body $jk+A$ resolvent, whose reference-channel Feshbach reduction reproduces the Carlson-Frederico-Hussein (CFH) absorptive kernel $W_j+W_k+W_{3B}$; the additional source $V_{iA}-U_{iA}$ drives target excitations, with its direct $g_Q$ component yielding target-excited CFH-like kernels under a diagonal-intermediate-states approximation. Prior forms are derived for both partitions, with reduced post-prior identities at the single-channel level (pair-detected) and at the CFH-optical level (single-particle). For $^{6}$Li$=α+n+p$, the explicit deuteron-target coupling has an E1/E2/monopole tidal structure evaluated on the full three-body wave function. The framework is validated by recovery of the two-body IAV, CFH, and detected-cluster limits, and separates exact DWBA identities from later optical and diagonal-target approximations.

## Key claim

A four-body DWBA sum-rule framework is derived that treats both pair-detected and single-particle inclusive breakup channels from a common Hamiltonian, with the pair-detected channel retaining the two-body $k+A$ Green's function and all three-body projectile effects entering through a pair-projected source, while the single-particle channel requires a three-body $jk+A$ resolvent whose Feshbach reduction reproduces the Carlson-Frederico-Hussein (CFH) absorptive kernel $W_j+W_k+W_{3B}$; the derivation separates exact DWBA identities from later optical and diagonal-target approximations.

## Method

The four-body Hamiltonian for a three-body projectile $a=i+j+k$ on a target $A$ is defined with Jacobi coordinates adapted to each exit-channel partition. For the pair-detected channel $a+A\to b+(k+A)^*$, the post-form DWBA transition amplitude is projected onto the pair internal state $\phi_\alpha$, and the sum over unobserved $k+A$ final states is performed analytically using the spectral representation of the full $k+A$ resolvent $G_{kA}^{\mathrm{full}}$, yielding a master sum rule in terms of the imaginary part of $G_{kA}^{\mathrm{full}}$ acting on a source function. For the single-particle channel $a+A\to i+(jk+A)^*$, the unresolved propagator is the three-body $jk+A$ resolvent, whose reference-channel Feshbach reduction reproduces the CFH absorptive kernel, with the additional source $V_{iA}-U_{iA}$ driving target excitations.

## Key numbers

* For ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}=\alpha+n+p$, the explicit deuteron-target coupling $V_{dA}-U_{dA}$ has an E1/E2/monopole tidal structure, with pair moments evaluated using the full three-body projectile wave function
* The Jacobi transformation between the two coordinate sets has unit determinant ($\det\mathcal J = 1$), preserving the internal volume element
* For ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}$ with $m_n\approx m_p$ and $m_i=m_n, m_j=m_p, m_k=m_\alpha$, the transformation gives $\boldsymbol\zeta' = -\tfrac{1}{2}\boldsymbol\zeta - \mathbf y$ and $\mathbf y' = \tfrac{M_a}{2(m_p+m_\alpha)}\boldsymbol\zeta - \tfrac{m_\alpha}{m_p+m_\alpha}\mathbf y$

## Context

This work extends the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) sum-rule formalism, previously confined to two-body projectiles, to three-body projectiles such as ${}^{6}\mathrm{He}=\alpha+n+n$, ${}^{11}\mathrm{Li}={}^{9}\mathrm{Li}+n+n$, and ${}^{9}\mathrm{Be}=\alpha+\alpha+n$. It provides a unified treatment of both pair-detected and single-particle inclusive observables, going beyond the CFH framework (Carlson, Frederico, and Hussein, 2017) which addressed only the single-particle channel, and beyond the two-body cluster approximations used in prior applications (Souza et al., 2021). The framework enables quantitative assessment of where the two-body cluster model departs from the full three-body description and provides a tractable complement to exact four-body Yakubovsky treatments.
