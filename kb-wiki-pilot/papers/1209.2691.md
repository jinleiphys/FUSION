# Status of reaction theory for studying rare isotopes

> Reactions are an important tool to study nuclear structure and for extracting reactions relevant for astrophysics. In this paper we focus on deuteron induced reactions which can provide information on neutron shell evolution as well as neutron capture cross sections. We review recent work on the systematic comparison of the continuum discretized coupled channel method, the adiabatic wave approximation and the Faddeev momentum-space approach. We also explore other aspects of the reaction mechanism and discuss in detail difficulties encountered in the calculations.

## Key claim
The paper argues that while the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method and its adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) are good approximations to the exact Faddeev momentum-space approach for (d,p) reactions around 10 MeV/u, their agreement deteriorates at higher beam energies; moreover, no benchmarks exist for heavier systems because Faddeev calculations cannot handle the Coulomb interaction for heavy nuclei.

## Method
The authors systematically compared three-body reaction models for deuteron-induced reactions: the exact Faddeev momentum-space integral formalism, the CDCC method (which includes breakup to all orders by discretizing the projectile continuum into bins), and the ADWA (which assumes all continuum components have radial behavior similar to the ground state). They performed calculations for (d,p) transfer and breakup reactions on light and medium-mass targets, using the same Hamiltonian across methods, and examined convergence properties, Coulomb effects, and optical potential dependence.

## Key numbers
- CDCC and ADWA are good approximations to Faddeev for reactions around 10 MeV/u, with agreement deteriorating at larger beam energies
- For $^{12}$C(d,p)$^{13}$C at $E_d$ = 12 MeV, the percentage difference at the peak between CH89 and KD optical potentials is approximately 10%
- For $^{48}$Ca(d,pn)$^{48}$Ca at $E_d$ = 56 MeV, using $l_{max}=6$ was sufficient to obtain convergence for breakup
- For $^{12}$C(d,pn)$^{12}$C at $E_d$ = 12 MeV, calculations with $l_{max}=8$ become numerically unfeasible with the numerical methods used in the code FRESCO
- The traditional DWBA should only be used when breakup has been proven insignificant; for $^{48}$Ca(d,p) at 56 MeV, breakup changes the shape of angular distributions completely

## Context
This work extends systematic comparisons of CDCC, ADWA, and Faddeev methods previously reported in Upadhyay et al. (2012) and Nunes and Deltuva (2011), which were performed within the TORUS collaboration. The paper identifies that no benchmarks exist for heavier systems due to the difficulty of handling Coulomb in momentum-space Faddeev calculations, motivating the development of a new formalism by Mukhamedzhanov et al. (2012) that can handle a wide range of beam energies (5-50 MeV/u) and both light and heavy systems.
