# Halo Excitation of $^6$He in Inelastic and Charge-Exchange Reactions

> Four-body distorted wave theory appropriate for nucleon-nucleus reactions leading to 3-body continuum excitations of two-neutron Borromean halo nuclei is developed. The peculiarities of the halo bound state and 3-body continuum are fully taken into account by using the method of hyperspherical harmonics. The procedure is applied for A=6 test-bench nuclei; thus we report detailed studies of inclusive cross sections for inelastic $^6$He(p,p')$^6$He$^*$ and charge-exchange $^6$Li(n,p)$^6$He$^*$ reactions at nucleon energy 50 MeV. The theoretical low-energy spectra exhibit two resonance-like structures. The first (narrow) is the excitation of the well-known $2^+$ three-body resonance. The second (broad) bump is a composition of overlapping soft modes of multipolarities $1^-, 2^+, 1^+, 0^+$ whose relative weights depend on transferred momentum and reaction type. Inelastic scattering is the most selective tool for studying the soft dipole excitation mode.

## Key claim

The paper develops a four-body distorted wave theory for nucleon-nucleus reactions that excite two-neutron Borromean halo nuclei into the three-body continuum, and applies it to $^6$He. The authors claim that the low-energy continuum of $^6$He contains not only the well-known $2^+$ three-body resonance but also a broad bump composed of overlapping soft modes of multipolarities $1^-$, $2^+$, $1^+$, and $0^+$, whose relative weights depend on transferred momentum and reaction type; however, the soft dipole mode is identified as a three-body virtual excitation rather than a true resonance, and its calculated position may be somewhat shifted to lower energy within the peak width due to convergence limitations in the hyperspherical harmonics expansion.

## Method

The authors used a four-body distorted wave (DW) framework with the method of hyperspherical harmonics (HH) to describe both the bound state and three-body continuum of $^6$He within an $\alpha+N+N$ three-body model. They calculated inclusive cross sections for the inelastic reaction $^6$He(p,p')$^6$He$^*$ and the charge-exchange reaction $^6$Li(n,p)$^6$He$^*$ at a nucleon energy of 50 MeV, using the free nucleon-nucleon $t$-matrix parametrization of Love and Franey as the effective interaction and phenomenological optical potentials for the distorted waves. The reaction amplitude was decomposed into partial waves, and the inclusive cross section was obtained by integrating over unobserved breakup particle coordinates, resulting in an incoherent sum over total transferred angular momentum $j$ and final state angular momentum $J_f$.

## Key numbers

- The $2^+_1$ resonance in $^6$He is calculated at 1.72 MeV excitation energy with a width of 0.04 MeV (HH method), compared to the experimental value of 1.8 MeV with width 0.113 MeV.
- A second $2^+_2$ resonance is predicted at 4.0 MeV with width 1.2 MeV (HH method).
- A $1^+$ resonance is predicted at 4.4 MeV with width 1.8 MeV (HH method).
- A $0^+_2$ resonance is predicted at 6.0 MeV with width 6.0 MeV (HH method).
- The $1^-$ soft dipole mode is not found as a resonance by any method (HH, Complex Scaling Method CS1, CS2); it is a three-body virtual excitation.
- The calculations were performed at a nucleon energy of 50 MeV for both reactions.
- The HH expansion included hypermoments $K \le 6$, corresponding to excitation energies up to about 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $^6$He structure using the HH method, which had successfully described weak and electromagnetic characteristics of $A=6$ systems and absolute differential cross sections for reactions to bound states. It builds on the authors' prior predictions of a rich continuum structure in $^6$He, including a second $2^+$ resonance, a $1^+$ resonance, and soft dipole and monopole modes. The paper enables experimental tests of these predictions using inclusive cross section measurements at facilities such as GANIL, Kurchatov Institute, NSCL (Michigan), RIKEN, JINR (Dubna), and GSI (Darmstadt), and contrasts with earlier heavy-ion charge-exchange experiments on $^6$Li($^7$Li,$^7$Be)$^6$He where a broad bump at $\sim$6 MeV was observed but with ambiguous multipolarity assignments.
