# Interplay of static and dynamic effects in 6He+ 238U Fusion

> We investigate the influence of the neutron halo and the breakup channel in 6He + 238U fusion at near-barrier energies. To include static effects of the 2n-halo in 6He nuclei, we use a single-folding potential obtained from an appropriate nucleon-238U interaction and a realistic 6He density. Dynamical effects arising from the breakup process are then included through coupled-channel calculations. These calculations suggest that static effects dominate the cross section at energies above the Coulomb barrier, while the sub-barrier fusion cross section appears to be determined by coupling to the breakup channel. This last conclusion is uncertain due to the procedure employed to measure the fusion cross-section.

## Key claim
The paper suggests that static effects from the neutron halo dominate the fusion cross section at energies above the Coulomb barrier, while coupling to the breakup channel determines the sub-barrier fusion cross section; however, this last conclusion is uncertain due to the procedure employed to measure the fusion cross section, and the authors later note that new data indicate the large sub-barrier enhancement may be due to transfer-fission rather than fusion-fission events.

## Method
The authors used a single-folding model with a realistic 6He density and a nucleon-238U interaction to construct the optical potential, accounting for static halo effects. They then performed schematic coupled-channel calculations where the breakup channel was represented by a single effective state at the breakup threshold energy of 0.975 MeV, including both Coulomb dipole coupling and nuclear dipole coupling. The nuclear form factor was treated with a free strength parameter, and the reduced transition probability B(E1,0→1) was taken from a cluster model as 1.37 e² fm².

## Key numbers
- Coulomb barrier height for 6He+238U: V_B ≈ 22.3 MeV
- Breakup threshold energy (effective channel energy): ε₁ = 0.975 MeV
- 6He r.m.s. radius: r_rms = 2.30 fm (from realistic parametrization)
- 4He r.m.s. radius: r_rms = 1.49 fm (used for scaling test)
- B(E1,0→1) value: 1.37 e² fm² (from cluster model)
- Nucleon-target potential parameters: V₀ = [50.378 - 27.073((N-Z)/A) - 0.354 E_Lab] MeV, with R_r = 1.264 A_T^(1/3) fm, a_r = 0.612 fm
- Imaginary potential: W₀ = 50 MeV, R_i = 1.0 (A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3)) fm, a_i = 0.10 fm

## Context
This work extends studies of fusion reactions with radioactive beams, particularly 6He, which exhibits a two-neutron halo structure. The authors compare their calculations to experimental data from Trotta et al. (2000) on 6He+238U fusion, which showed orders-of-magnitude enhancement over 4He+238U fusion. The paper contradicts earlier expectations that breakup coupling alone could explain the sub-barrier enhancement, and suggests that neutron-transfer channels (as noted by Switkowski et al., 1974) must be included for a full description. The work enables future studies incorporating transfer channels and was informed by new data from Trotta et al. (2003) indicating that the observed sub-barrier enhancement may arise from transfer-fission rather than fusion-fission.
