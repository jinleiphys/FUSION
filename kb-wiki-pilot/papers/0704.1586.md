# Core excitation in the elastic scattering and breakup of $^{11}$Be on protons

> The elastic scattering and breakup of $^{11}$Be from a proton target at intermediate energies is studied. We explore the role of core excitation in the reaction mechanism. Comparison with the data suggests that there is still missing physics in the description.

## Key claim

The inclusion of core excitation (the $2^+$ state of $^{10}$Be) in the reaction mechanism for $^{11}$Be scattering and breakup on protons does not resolve the persistent disagreement between theory and experiment, suggesting that "there is still missing physics in the description" beyond both continuum coupling and core excitation.

## Method

The authors performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) and eXtended Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (XCDCC) calculations for $^{11}$Be on a proton target at two intermediate energies (~40 and ~60 MeV/nucleon). The $^{10}$Be-proton optical potential was fitted to elastic data at each energy using a renormalized CH89 interaction, and for XCDCC calculations the $^{10}$Be $2^+$ excited state was included via a rotational model with deformation length fitted to the experimental $B$(E2) strength. The $^{11}$Be bound state was described using a $^{10}$Be+n two-body model, with continuum discretized up to 35 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering at ~40 MeV/nucleon: CDCC and XCDCC calculations still overestimate the $^{11}$Be elastic cross section at larger angles, with no improvement from core excitation
- Elastic scattering at ~60 MeV/nucleon: the same pattern of overpredicting the $^{11}$Be cross section persists
- Breakup at 63.7 MeV/nucleon, lower energy bin (0.5-3.0 MeV, spanning the 1.78 MeV resonance): converged CDCC calculations agree well with the data, contrary to earlier claims; XCDCC does not change results significantly
- Breakup at 63.7 MeV/nucleon, higher energy bin (3.0-5.5 MeV, spanning the 3.89 MeV resonance): CDCC does not reproduce the shape; XCDCC including core excitation lowers the cross section but does not change the shape; the breakup to $^{10}$Be($2^+$) alone reproduces the data shape when scaled by a factor of 10
- Optical potential parameters for $^{10}$Be+p at 40 MeV: V=60.84 MeV, R_V=1.000 fm, a_V=0.7 fm, W=23.16 MeV, R_W=0.600 fm, a_W=0.6 fm
- Optical potential parameters for $^{10}$Be+p at 60 MeV: V=31.64 MeV, R_V=1.145 fm, a_V=0.69 fm, W=8.78 MeV, R_W=1.134 fm, a_W=0.69 fm

## Context

This work extends the XCDCC method previously applied to breakup of $^{11}$Be on $^9$Be at ~60 MeV/nucleon (Summers, Nunes, and Thompson, 2006) to the proton target case, where recoil effects are important and the process is nuclear driven. It re-examines elastic scattering data from GANIL at 49.3 MeV/nucleon (Cortina-Gil et al., 1997) and NSCL at ~60 MeV/nucleon (Shrivastava et al., 2004), where large renormalizations of the optical potential were previously required. The work contradicts the suggestion in Shrivastava et al. that core excitation might resolve the disagreement in the higher-energy breakup bin, and shows instead that the structure model lacks the exotic resonances built on excited core components needed to describe that region. The results enable future work on including the deuteron transfer channel within CDCC and on Faddeev-based approaches to treat the three-body final state continuum more consistently.
