# Long Range Outlook for Short-Range Correlations

> Short range correlated (SRC) N N pairs are pairs of nucleons with high relative momentum (prel > kF where kF ~ 250 MeV/c is the Fermi momentum in medium to heavy nuclei) and lower center of mass momentum. The motivation for studying SRC pairs ranges from a desire to achieve a more comprehensive understanding of the many-body nuclear wave-function at high-resolution to searching for explicit QCD-dynamics effects within the nuclear medium, not to mention connections to many other open problems in nuclear physics. Exploring short-range correlations was one of the physics motivations for building CEBAF (now Jefferson Lab). Scientists used the high luminosity and high energy of this cutting-edge machine to find kinematics that cleanly showed the signals of short-range correlations. This paved the way in the last two decades for tremendous progress understanding these correlations. This paper reviews recent progress and highlights outstanding questions and areas that need further study.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while short-range correlations (SRCs) have been extensively studied and significant progress has been made, a complete understanding remains elusive; the authors aim to identify outstanding questions and propose a path forward, explicitly noting that no definitive community consensus or roadmap has yet been established despite dozens of topical workshops.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical advances in SRC research from the "modern" era, focusing on inclusive quasielastic electron scattering $(e,e')$, semi-inclusive proton knockout $(e,e'p)$, and two-nucleon knockout $(e,e'pp)$ and $(e,e'pn)$ reactions at high momentum transfers ($Q^2 \ge 1.5$ GeV$^2$) and large missing momenta ($p_{miss} > k_F \approx 250$ MeV/c). The authors analyze cross-section ratios of nuclei to deuterium, spectral function factorization, and comparisons with Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF), Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC), and other theoretical models to assess SRC pair abundances, isospin composition, and the transition from mean-field to SRC dominance.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum in medium to heavy nuclei: $k_F \approx 250$ MeV/c
- Minimum momentum for SRC dominance in inclusive scattering: $k_{min} \ge 300$ MeV/c (corresponding to $x_B \ge 1.5$ at $Q^2 \ge 1.5$ GeV$^2$)
- SRC dominance onset in missing momentum: $p_{miss} \ge 350$ MeV/c (scaling starts at $x_B = 0.7$ for $p_{miss}^{min}=350$ MeV/c)
- Ratio of $pp$ to $np$ SRC pairs: approximately 8-10% contribution from $pp$- and $nn$-SRC pairs relative to $np$ pairs
- Per-nucleon inclusive cross-section ratio $a_2$ for $^{12}$C/$d$: approximately 5; for $^3$He/$d$: approximately 2
- Measured per-nucleus cross-section ratio of $^{48}$Ca to $^{40}$Ca: $1.165 \pm 0.014$
- Measured $^3$H/$^3$He cross-section ratio in SRC-dominated region ($1.4 < x_B < 1.7$): $0.854 \pm 0.010$
- Threshold for 3N-SRC contribution: $\alpha_{3N} > 1.6$ (minimum), $\alpha_{3N} > 1.8$ (realistic for second scaling plateau)
- Predicted $a_3 \approx a_2^2$ for 3N-SRC scaling
- $Q^2$ values for inclusive 3N-SRC searches: up to 2.7 GeV$^2$ (Hall C), 2.3 and 3.2 GeV$^2$ (XEM2 experiment under analysis)

## Context

This review extends the line of SRC research dating back to the 1950s, building on foundational experimental work at SLAC (first evidence of NN SRCs via inclusive cross-section ratios at $1.5 \le Q^2 \le 1.9$ GeV$^2$ and $x_B > 1.5$) and Jefferson Lab (CEBAF) experiments such as E02-019, CLAS 2006, and Hall A measurements. It directly references prior results on $np$ dominance from two-body breakup measurements at Jefferson Lab ($A(e,e'pN)$) and Brookhaven Lab ($A(p,2pn)$), and the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) for SRC calculations. The paper enables future experimental searches for 3N-SRC plateaus, systematic studies of isospin dependence across nuclei with varying $N/Z$, and tests of probe independence through comparisons of electron, proton, and photonuclear reactions.
