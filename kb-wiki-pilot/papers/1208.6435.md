# Transfer/Breakup Channel Couplings in Sub-barrier Fusion Reactions

> With the recent availability of state-of-the-art radioactive ion beams, there has been a renew interest in the investigation of nuclear reactions with heavy ions near the Coulomb barrier. The role of inelastic and transfer channel couplings in fusion reactions induced by stable heavy ions can be revisited. Detailed Analysis of recent experimental fusion cross sections by using standard coupled-channel calculations is first discussed. Multi-neutron transfer effects are introduced in the fusion process below the Coulomb barrier by analyzing 32S+90,96Zr as benchmark reactions. The enhancement of fusion cross sections for 32S+96Zr is well reproduced at sub-barrier energies by NTFus code calculations including the coupling of the neutron-transfer channels following the Zagrebaev semi-classical model. Similar effects for 40Ca+90Zr and 40Ca+96Zr fusion excitation functions are found. The breakup coupling in both the elastic scattering and in the fusion process induced by weakly bound stable projectiles is also shown to be crucial. In the second part of this work, full coupled-channel calculations of the fusion excitation functions are performed by using the breakup coupling for the more neutron-rich reaction and for the more weakly bound projectiles. we clearly demonstrate that Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel calculations are capable to reproduce the fusion enhancement from the breakup coupling in 6Li+59Co.

## Key claim

The paper argues that multi-neutron transfer channels with positive Q-values produce a significant enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections for the neutron-rich system 32S+96Zr, but this enhancement is absent for 32S+90Zr where neutron transfer Q-values are negative; however, the agreement between calculations and the experimental fusion barrier distribution for 32S+96Zr shows a dip where the calculation predicts a strong low-energy peak, a disagreement that is not yet fully understood.

## Method

Standard coupled-channel (CC) calculations were performed using the NTFus code, which implements the Zagrebaev semi-classical model to couple multi-neutron transfer channels (up to +4n) together with low-lying quadrupole and octupole vibrational states. For weakly bound projectiles, Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) calculations were used to include breakup couplings to the continuum, with the alpha+d binning scheme adjusted to match measured continuum excitation energy ranges for the 6Li+59Co reaction.

## Key numbers

- Fusion cross sections for 32S+90Zr and 32S+96Zr were measured with small energy steps and good statistical accuracy at the HI-13 tandem accelerator at CIAE Beijing
- The 96Zr nucleus has a 2+ state at 1.748 MeV and a 3- state at 1.897 MeV with B(E3;3- → 0+) = 51 W.u.
- For 32S+96Zr, calculations including neutron transfer coupling up to +4n channels reproduced the sub-barrier fusion enhancement; agreement was also obtained up to +5n and +6n channels
- For 6Li+59Co, CDCC calculations were performed at incident 6Li energies of 12, 18, 26, and 30 MeV, and at Elab = 25.5 MeV and 29.6 MeV for breakup angular distributions
- For 7Be+58Ni, CDCC calculations were performed at incident 7Be energies of 15.09, 17.13, 18.53, 19.93, and 21.43 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of sub-barrier fusion enhancement due to neutron transfers, particularly the Zagrebaev model (Zagrebaev 2003) applied to 40Ca+90,96Zr reactions (Timmers 1998), and confirms recent fragment-gamma coincidence measurements for 40Ca+96Zr multi-neutron transfer channels (Corradi 2011). The CDCC analysis of 6Li+59Co builds on earlier elastic scattering measurements (Beck 2007b) and provides a framework for understanding breakup coupling effects that will be essential for interpreting future experiments with radioactive ion beams at SPIRAL2 and with active-target time-projection chambers at MSU/NSCL.
