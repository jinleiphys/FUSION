# Exciting Ions: a Systematic Treatment of Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions with Nuclear Breakup

> We present an updated theoretical treatment of ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) of heavy ions, within the SuperChic Monte Carlo generator. This in particular accounts for mutual ion excitation through additional photon exchanges between the colliding ions. This effect occurs frequently in UPCs, and indeed can be (and has been) measured in data through the use of zero degree calorimeter (ZDC) detectors installed in the far forward region. The theoretical approach presented here accounts for the non-trivial and non-negligible impact such ion dissociation has on the measured cross sections and distributions of the produced particles in the central detectors. This builds on previous work, whereby the survival factor probability of no additional inelastic ion-ion scattering due to the strong interaction, and its kinematic impact, are also accounted for within the same overall framework. We compare to data from ATLAS and CMS at the LHC, and STAR at RHIC, and find in general encouraging agreement for a range of observables and ZDC neutron tags, with some room for further improvement, suggesting the inclusion of higher order QED effects and/or tuning of the the $γA \to A^*$ cross section may be desirable. Overall, this gives confidence in the approach considered here and for applications to new phenomena within and beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper presents an updated theoretical treatment of ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions within the SuperChic Monte Carlo generator that accounts for mutual ion excitation through additional photon exchanges, finding generally encouraging agreement with data from ATLAS, CMS, and STAR but noting that inclusion of higher order QED effects and/or tuning of the gamma-A to A* cross section may be desirable for further improvement.

## Method

The author extends the SuperChic Monte Carlo generator to include mutual ion dissociation via additional photon exchanges between colliding ions, where the nuclear excitation is assumed to occur independently of the gamma-gamma to X production process. The breakup probability for each ion is computed from a convolution of the photon emission flux from the other ion and the measured gamma-A to A* cross section, using data from fixed target experiments for the giant dipole resonance region and higher energies. The survival factor probability of no additional inelastic ion-ion scattering due to the strong interaction is also accounted for within the same framework, with the full process and kinematic dependence preserved.

## Key numbers

- For Pb ions, the proton radius R_p = 6.680 fm and skin thickness d_p = 0.447 fm; neutron radius R_n = (6.67 +/- 0.03) fm and d_n = (0.55 +/- 0.01) fm
- The integrated single neutron emission cross section at the LHC is 260 mb at representative impact parameter b_perp = 3 R_A
- The high energy contribution (omega > 80 GeV) accounts for roughly 25% of the total contribution to the breakup probability at the LHC for b_perp = 3 R_A
- The photon energy cutoff for the high energy contribution is set at omega = 500 GeV, corresponding to |y_gamma_n| ~ 5 for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_nn) = 5.02 TeV
- The maximum photon energy at the LHC is of order 850 TeV, corresponding to omega_max ~ s/(m_A^2 R_A)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous treatment of the survival factor in ultraperipheral collisions implemented in SuperChic (Harland-Lang, 2018), and addresses incomplete modeling of ion dissociation effects identified in the Starlight Monte Carlo generator (Baltz et al., 2009) by CMS data on dimuon acoplanarity. The theoretical approach is consistent with calculations by Brandenburg et al. (2020) but provides a full Monte Carlo treatment, enabling more direct comparison with experimental measurements using zero degree calorimeter tags from ATLAS, CMS, and STAR, with applications to Standard Model precision studies and searches for new phenomena such as axion-like particles.
