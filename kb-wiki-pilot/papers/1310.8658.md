# Theoretical study of the ${}^{4}\rm{He}(γ,p)^3\rm{H}$ and ${}^{4}\rm{He}(γ,n)^3\rm{He}$ reactions

> {\it Ab initio} calculation of the total cross section for the reactions $^{4}\rm{He}(γ,p)^3\rm{H}$ and $^{4}\rm{He}(γ,n)^3\rm{He}$ is presented, using state-of-the-art nuclear forces. The Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method is applied, which allows exact treatment of the final state interaction (FSI). The dynamic equations are solved using the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method. In this calculation of the cross sections the three-nucleon force is fully taken into account, except in the source term of the LIT equation for the FSI transition matrix element.

## Key claim

This work presents the first *ab initio* calculation of the exclusive two-body photodisintegration cross sections for the reactions $^{4}\rm{He}(\gamma,p)^3\rm{H}$ and $^{4}\rm{He}(\gamma,n)^3\rm{He}$ using a state-of-the-art nuclear Hamiltonian, but the three-nucleon force (3NF) is fully included in the Hamiltonian and omitted from the source term of the exclusive Lorentz integral transform (LIT) equation for the final state interaction (FSI) transition matrix element.

## Method

The authors applied the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method to treat the final state interaction exactly, solving the dynamic equations using an extension of the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method that allows construction of asymptotic wave functions and isospin-symmetry breaking. The unretarded dipole approximation (UDA) was used, and the cross sections were calculated with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential supplemented with the UIX three-nucleon force (3NF), with the 3NF omitted from the source term of the LIT equation for the FSI.

## Key numbers

* The sum of the exclusive cross sections $\sigma_{n,^3\rm{He}}+\sigma_{p,^3\rm{H}}$ calculated with the KMTI-III NN potential (no 3NF) agrees excellently with the inclusive cross section up to approximately 34 MeV, with three- and four-body breakup effects becoming visible above that energy.
* For the AV18/UIX potential model, the sum of the exclusive cross sections $\sigma_{n,^3\rm{He}}+\sigma_{p,^3\rm{H}}$ does not reproduce the inclusive cross section, indicating a non-negligible role of the 3NF in the FSI despite its relative weakness and short range.
* The authors estimate an error of the order of 1% in the cross section from the convergence of the LIT and the variance in the inversion process.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of exclusive $^4\rm{He}$ photodisintegration cross sections that used semi-realistic potentials (Quaglioni et al., 2004) or ad-hoc 3NFs (Horiuchi et al., 2012), by employing a state-of-the-art nuclear Hamiltonian (AV18 NN potential plus UIX 3NF). The results enable future inclusion of the 3NF in the source term of the exclusive LIT equation to resolve the discrepancy between the sum of exclusive cross sections and the inclusive cross section, and the method can be applied to nucleon-nucleus scattering reactions.
