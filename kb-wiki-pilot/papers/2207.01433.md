# Theoretical study of the d(d,p)3H and d(d,n)3He processes at low energies

> We present a theoretical study of the processes d(d,p)3H and d(d,n)3He at energies of interest for energy production and for big-bang nucleosynthesis. We accurately solve the four body scattering problem using the ab-initio hyperspherical harmonic method, starting from nuclear Hamiltonians which include modern two- and three-nucleon interactions, derived in chiral effective field theory. We report results for the astrophysical factor, the quintet suppression factor, and various single and double polarized observables. An estimate of the "theoretical uncertainty" for all these quantities is provided by varying the cutoff parameter used to regularize the chiral interactions at high momentum.

## Key claim

The authors present ab-initio calculations of the d(d,p)3H and d(d,n)3He astrophysical S-factors and polarization observables using chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, with a preliminary theoretical uncertainty estimated from the spread between results obtained with cutoff values of 500 and 600 MeV, rather than a full Bayesian uncertainty quantification.

## Method

The four-body scattering problem is solved using the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) expansion method, including all partial waves up to L=4. The nuclear Hamiltonians incorporate chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at N3LO (with cutoffs 500 and 600 MeV) and a chiral three-nucleon interaction at N2LO, whose parameters c_D and c_E are fixed to reproduce A=3 binding energies and the tritium beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element. The T-matrix elements for transitions between d+d initial states and p+3H or n+3He final states are extracted from the asymptotic form of the scattering wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Astrophysical S-factor at zero energy for d(d,p)3H: S(0) = 50.8 ± 1.9 keV b
- Astrophysical S-factor at zero energy for d(d,n)3He: S(0) = 51.0 ± 1.4 keV b
- The astrophysical S-factor for d(d,n)3He is larger than that for d(d,p)3H for center-of-mass energies above 0.1 MeV
- Theoretical uncertainty for double-polarized observables A_{z,z} and A_{zz,zz} is estimated at about 5%
- The quintet suppression factor (QSF) is close to unity at low energies and slowly decreases with increasing energy, reaching a plateau around T_d = 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical calculations of d+d fusion using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations (Deltuva and Fonseca, 2010) and the Correlated Gaussian method (Aoyama et al., 2012), and provides the first HH-method treatment of these four-nucleon scattering processes. The results are relevant for reducing uncertainties in Big-Bang nucleosynthesis deuterium abundance predictions (Yeh et al., 2021; Pisanti et al., 2021) and for designing polarized fusion experiments such as PolFusion (Solovev et al., 2020) and the SORGENTINA-RF project for medical radioisotope production (Agostini et al., 2021). The calculations enable predictions for double-polarized observables that will be measured by the PolFusion experiment.
