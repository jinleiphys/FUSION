# Role of continuum in nuclear direct reactions with one-neutron halo nuclei: a one-dimensional model

> We study the evolution of a single-particle wave function during the collision of a one dimensional potential well by another well, which can be regarded as a simple model for the problem of the scattering of a one-neutron halo nucleus by another nucleus. This constitutes an effective three-body problem, whose solution in three dimensions can be extremely complicated, particularly when breakup and rearrangement channels are to be considered. Our one-dimensional model provides the essential three-body nature of this problem, and allows for a much simpler application and assessment of different methods of solution. To simplify further the problem, we assume that the potential well representing the projectile moves according to a predetermined classical trajectory, although the internal motion of the "valence" particle is treated fully quantum-mechanically. This corresponds to a semiclassical approach of the scattering problem. Different approaches are investigated to understand the dynamics involving one-body halo-like systems: the "exact" time-dependent solution of the Schrödinger equation is compared to a numerical continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CC) calculation presenting various model cases including different reaction channels. This framework allows us to discuss the reaction mechanism and the role of continuum, whose inclusion in the CC calculation results to be crucial to reproduce the "exact" solution, even when the initial and final states are well bound. We also link each dynamical situation with analogous problem solved in a three dimensional (3D) CC framework, discussing the main challenges experienced in the usual 3D models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including continuum states in coupled-channels (CC) calculations is crucial to reproduce the exact time-dependent solution of the Schrödinger equation for one-neutron halo nucleus collisions, even when the initial and final states are well bound and the breakup probability is negligible.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-dimensional semiclassical model where a projectile potential well moves along a predetermined classical trajectory while the valence neutron is treated fully quantum-mechanically. They solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation exactly using finite-difference methods and compared these results to approximate continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CC) calculations that included bound states and discretized continuum pseudostates for both target and projectile wells. The continuum was discretized using an infinite square well basis (BOX method) to produce square-integrable pseudostates.

## Key numbers

- Asymptotic projectile velocity of 0.1 × c, corresponding to asymptotic energy of 5.0 MeV (Case A)
- Reduced mass of 1.001 amu for the system
- Target ground state energy of -2.276 MeV for the weakly-bound case (Case B)
- Energy cutoff in the continuum of 4 MeV for the CC calculation with 5 continuum pseudostates per well in a [-20, 20] fm range
- For Case A: exact elastic probability 86.1%, inelastic 9.8%, transfer to projectile ground state 2.5%, transfer to first excited state 0.8%, breakup 0.7%
- For Case A: CC calculation with only target bound states gave elastic 89.8%, inelastic 10.1%
- For Case A: CC calculation with target and projectile bound states plus 5 continuum pseudostates each gave elastic 84%, inelastic 11.6%, transfer ground state 2.3%, transfer first excited 1.9%, breakup 0.13%

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical model of Esbensen (which included only bound states) to incorporate continuum effects essential for weakly-bound halo nuclei like 11Be. The one-dimensional framework allows direct comparison between exact time-dependent solutions and approximate CC methods, which is extremely difficult in full three-dimensional calculations. The results connect to three-dimensional CDCC calculations (e.g., YOMM12, MCNT02, LCAGQG09, KAKR09) and demonstrate that continuum-continuum couplings, known to influence fusion reactions for weakly-bound nuclei (HVDL00, DT02, CLGH09), must be included even when breakup channels appear negligible.
