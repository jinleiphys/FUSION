# Comparison of semiclassical transfer to continuum model with Ichimura-Austern-Vincent model in medium energy knockout reactions

> The full quantum mechanical (QM) model of inclusive breakup of Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) is implemented in this paper to calculate breakup from heavy radioactive nuclei on a $^9$Be target at intermediate energies. So far it had been implemented and applied only to low energy reactions with light projectiles. The IAV model is successful in predicting absolute cross sections among other observables. In order to get insight on the content of the model in the case of the complicated heavy-ion reactions, results are compared with those of the semiclassical transfer to the continuum (TC) model. Because the TC is based on analytical formulae the dynamics of the breakup as it is contained in the rather involved IAV formalism will become more transparent. Heavy-ion reactions at high energies ($>$50A.MeV) are demanding from the computational point of view because of the high number of partial waves involved, typically around 100. The TC constitutes a useful alternative to the full QM calculations whenever predictions and/or estimates are necessary. It allows also for a systematic, fast evaluation of breakup observables. In the applications of both methods we use state-of-the art optical potentials and structure information. Excellent agreement is found between the calculated results of both methods and with available experimental data which shows that the qualitative and quantitative understanding of most aspects of one nucleon breakup is well under control.

## Key claim
The semiclassical Transfer to the Continuum (TC) model produces results in excellent quantitative agreement with the full quantum mechanical Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model for one-nucleon knockout reactions from heavy radioactive nuclei on a $^9$Be target at intermediate energies (above 50A.MeV), validating the semiclassical approximation for the relative motion in this energy regime.

## Method
The IAV model was implemented for the first time at intermediate energies with heavy projectiles, using partial wave formalism with approximately 100 partial waves and Jacobi coordinate transformations between incoming and outgoing channels. The TC model treated the relative motion of reaction partners as a classical trajectory while retaining quantum mechanical treatment of the breakup process. Both methods used the same n-target energy-dependent AB optical potential and single folding method for core-target potentials, and calculations were compared for four reactions: proton and neutron knockout from $^{14}$O at 53A.MeV and $^{16}$C at 75A.MeV on a $^9$Be target.

## Key numbers
- Maximum number of partial waves required for IAV calculations at intermediate energies: approximately 100 (compared to at most 30 for low energy cases)
- Total single-particle cross sections for $^{14}$O(-p) at 53A.MeV: IAV = 33.91 mb, TC = 30.5 mb (experimental = 58 mb)
- Total single-particle cross sections for $^{16}$C(-n) from $2s_{1/2}$ state at 75A.MeV: IAV = 58.42 mb, TC = 47.7 mb (experimental = 36 mb)
- Total single-particle cross sections for $^{16}$C(-n) from $1d_{5/2}$ state at 75A.MeV: IAV = 36.29 mb, TC = 26.9 mb (experimental = 46 mb)
- Total single-particle cross sections for $^{14}$O(-n) at 53A.MeV: IAV = 13.72 mb, TC = 12.65 mb (experimental = 14 mb)
- Reduction factors R$_f$ = $\sigma_{exp}/(C^2S \sigma_{IAV})$ ranged from 0.3 to 1.4 across the four reactions
- Spectroscopic factor quenching of at maximum 35-40% relative to shell-model values obtained with standard single-particle potential parameters

## Context
This work extends the IAV model, previously applied only to low energy reactions with light projectiles such as deuteron and $^{6,7}$Li, to intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions. It provides a direct comparison between the fully quantum mechanical IAV approach and the semiclassical TC model, demonstrating that the TC constitutes a valid and computationally efficient alternative to full quantum mechanical calculations for medium energy knockout reactions. The results address ongoing debates about discrepancies between knockout reaction cross sections and those from transfer reactions (Lee11, Flavigny13, Flavigny18) and quasifree scattering with ($p$, $2p$) and ($e$, $e'p$) reactions, suggesting that the semiclassical approximation for relative motion is justified and could simplify future implementations of quantum mechanical methods.
