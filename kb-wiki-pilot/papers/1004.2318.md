# Solar fusion cross sections II: the pp chain and CNO cycles

> We summarize and critically evaluate the available data on nuclear fusion cross sections important to energy generation in the Sun and other hydrogen-burning stars and to solar neutrino production. Recommended values and uncertainties are provided for key cross sections, and a recommended spectrum is given for 8B solar neutrinos. We also discuss opportunities for further increasing the precision of key rates, including new facilities, new experimental techniques, and improvements in theory. This review, which summarizes the conclusions of a workshop held at the Institute for Nuclear Theory, Seattle, in January 2009, is intended as a 10-year update and supplement to Reviews of Modern Physics 70 (1998) 1265.

# Solar fusion cross sections II: the pp chain and CNO cycles

## Key claim
This review provides updated recommended values and uncertainties for key nuclear fusion cross sections in the pp chain and CNO cycles, intended as a 10-year update to the 1998 Solar Fusion I review. The authors note that while the solar neutrino problem has been resolved by neutrino oscillations, nuclear physics uncertainties remain a limiting factor in using neutrinos as probes of solar core temperature and metallicity, and in constraining the neutrino mixing angle θ₁₂.

## Method
The authors organized an international workshop at the Institute for Nuclear Theory in January 2009, modeled after the 1997 workshop, with working groups assigned to critically evaluate both new and older experimental data on low-energy S-factors for reactions in the pp chain and CNO bi-cycle. Working groups identified key papers for review, discussed the state of the data, and worked toward consensus on best-value cross sections and uncertainties throughout 2009, including treatment of discrepant data sets and model-dependent extrapolations to solar Gamow peak energies.

## Context
This work is a 10-year update and supplement to Adelberger et al., Reviews of Modern Physics 70 (1998) 1265 (Solar Fusion I), which had become one of the most heavily cited papers in nuclear astrophysics. The update was motivated by advances including the LUNA II program at Gran Sasso providing low-energy measurements of ³He(α,γ)⁷Be and ¹⁴N(p,γ)¹⁵O, high-precision measurements of ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B, new theoretical techniques for the p+p and hep neutrino reactions, and resolution of questions about screening corrections in plasmas. The improved cross sections enable more precise analyses of solar neutrino experiments (Borexino, Super-Kamiokande, SNO) for constraining neutrino oscillations and for using neutrinos as probes of solar core temperature and metallicity, particularly in light of the discrepancy between helioseismology and SSM predictions using new 3D photospheric abundance analyses by Asplund and collaborators.
