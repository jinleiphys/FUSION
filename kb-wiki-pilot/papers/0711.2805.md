# Exit Doorway Model for Nuclear Breakup of Weakly Bound Projectiles

> We derive closed expressions for the nuclear breakup cross sections in the adiabatic limit using the Austern-Blair theory. These expressions are appropriate for the breakup of weakly bound nuclei. The concept of an exit doorway that mediates the coupling between the entrance channel and the breakup continuum is used. We prove the validity of the scaling law that dictates that the nuclear breakup cross section scales linearly with the radius of the target. We also compare our results for the nuclear breakup cross section of $^{11}$Be, $^8$B on several targets with recent CDCC calculation.

## Key claim
The nuclear breakup cross section for weakly bound projectiles scales linearly with the target radius (and thus as A_T^{1/3}), not as the cubic root of the target mass number as some prior work claimed; this scaling law is derived analytically in the adiabatic limit using the Austern-Blair theory, but the model systematically underestimates full CDCC calculations for halo nuclei like 11Be, indicating that higher-order channel-coupling effects are important.

## Method
The authors treat nuclear breakup as an inelastic multipole excitation mediated by an "exit doorway" state that couples the entrance channel to the discretized breakup continuum. They apply the Austern-Blair adiabatic theory within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), assuming equal initial and final wave numbers and large orbital angular momenta, to derive closed-form expressions for the radial integrals and integrated cross section. Deformation lengths for dipole and quadrupole transitions are calculated using a cluster model of the projectile (core + valence particle), with the S-matrix approximated by a Fermi function to evaluate the sum over partial waves analytically.

## Key numbers
- For 11Be (10Be+n, Q=0.504 MeV): (δ1^(N))^2 = 0.84 ± 0.04 fm^2, (δ2^(N))^2 = 1.27 ± 0.25 fm^2, (ΔR/R)^2 = 0.0214, normalization factor c = 2.52
- For 8B (7Be+p, Q=0.137 MeV): (δ1^(N))^2 = 1.62 fm^2, (δ2^(N))^2 = 3.24 fm^2, (ΔR/R)^2 = 0.0179, normalization factor c = 0.307
- For 7Be (4He+3He, Q=1.587 MeV): (δ1^(N))^2 = 0.41 fm^2, (δ2^(N))^2 = 1.54 fm^2, (ΔR/R)^2 = 0.00576, normalization factor c = 1.37
- The integrated nuclear breakup cross section formula: σ = c [ (δ1^(N))^2 (3/2)^2 (ΔR_P/R_P)^2 + (δ2^(N))^2 ] (R/3a), where R = r0(A_p^{1/3}+A_t^{1/3}) and a = 0.65 fm is the diffuseness
- The exit-doorway spreading width Γ^↓ is typically about 10 MeV, while the breakup Q-value Ed is ≤ 1 MeV, giving the limit Γ^↓_d >> E_d

## Context
This work extends the exit-doorway concept previously applied to fusion and giant resonance excitation (Hussein and de Toledo Piza 1994, Canto et al. 1994) to the nuclear breakup of weakly bound projectiles. It provides an analytic alternative to full CDCC calculations (Hussein et al. 2006, Phys. Lett. B640) and clarifies the debate between the Serber-model A_T^{1/3} scaling and the linear A_T scaling claimed by Nagarajan et al. (2001, Phys. Lett. B503). The derived formula enables quick estimates of nuclear breakup contributions for halo and weakly bound nuclei, though the authors note that for 11Be the DWBA-based result underestimates CDCC calculations, suggesting that higher-order couplings not captured by the exit-doorway model are significant.
