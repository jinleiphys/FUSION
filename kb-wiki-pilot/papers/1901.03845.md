# Production Spectra of $^3$He($π$, $K$) Reactions with Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels

> We investigate theoretically $Λ$ production spectra of $^3$He($π$, $K$) reactions at $p_π=$ 1.05--1.20 GeV/$c$ in the distorted-wave impulse approximation, using the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method. The production cross section of a $^3_Λ$H(1/2$^+$) ground state is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates Lambda production spectra for $^3$He($\pi$, $K$) reactions using the continuum-discretized coupled-channel method, finding that recoil effects are very important for production on a light nuclear target like $^3$He, and that the integrated production cross section of $^3_\Lambda$H(1/2$^+$) is about 3 $\mu$b/sr at 1.05-1.20 GeV/$c$ and $\theta_{\rm lab}=3^\circ$, but only when recoil effects are included; without them the cross section is reduced by an order of magnitude or more.

## Method

The authors employ the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method to describe $NN$ continuum states above the $N+N+\Lambda$ breakup threshold. They calculate inclusive differential cross sections for $^3$He($\pi$, $K$) reactions at incident pion momenta $p_\pi=$ 1.05-1.20 GeV/$c$, using the Green's function method to sum over final states. The $\pi N\to\Lambda K$ amplitude in nuclear medium is obtained by the optimal Fermi-averaging method, and the $\Lambda N$ potential is a single Gaussian form fitted to the NSC97f scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

- $^3_\Lambda$H(1/2$^+$) binding energy: $B_\Lambda=$ 130$\pm$50 keV (experimental value)
- Integrated production cross section of $^3_\Lambda$H(1/2$^+$) in $^3$He($\pi^-$, $K^0$) at $p_\pi=$ 1.20 GeV/$c$, $\theta_{\rm lab}=3^\circ$: $d\sigma/d\Omega\,(^3_\Lambda{\rm H})=$ 3.13 $\mu$b/sr (full calculation with recoil)
- Integrated production cross section of $^3_\Lambda$H(1/2$^+$) at $p_\pi=$ 1.05 GeV/$c$, $\theta_{\rm lab}=3^\circ$: $d\sigma/d\Omega\,(^3_\Lambda{\rm H})=$ 3.07 $\mu$b/sr (full calculation with recoil)
- Momentum transfer $q_\Lambda$ at $p_\pi=$ 1.20 GeV/$c$, $\theta_{\rm lab}=3^\circ$: 326 MeV/$c$
- Effective momentum transfer $q_\Lambda^{\rm eff}$ at $p_\pi=$ 1.20 GeV/$c$, $\theta_{\rm lab}=3^\circ$: 217 MeV/$c$ (with recoil factor $M_C/M_A=2/3$)
- Elementary cross section $(d\sigma/d\Omega)^{\pi N\to\Lambda K}_{\rm elem.}$ (free) at $p_\pi=$ 1.20 GeV/$c$, $\theta_{\rm lab}=3^\circ$: 292 $\mu$b/sr
- Elementary cross section $(d\sigma/d\Omega)^{\pi N\to\Lambda K}_{\rm elem.}$ (in medium) at $p_\pi=$ 1.20 GeV/$c$, $\theta_{\rm lab}=3^\circ$: 225 $\mu$b/sr

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Harada and Hirabayashi (Nucl. Phys. A 934, 8, 2015) using the CDCC method to describe $NN$ continuum states in hypernuclear production. It addresses the hypertriton lifetime puzzle, where the world average lifetime $\tau^{\rm (av)}(^3_\Lambda{\rm H})=185^{+23}_{-28}$ ps is shorter than the free $\Lambda$ lifetime $\tau_\Lambda=263.2\pm2.0$ ps, and where the HypHI Collaboration found a bound $nn\Lambda$ system (Phys. Rev. C 88, 041001(R), 2013) despite theoretical predictions that no $^3_\Lambda n$ bound state exists (Gal and Garcilazo, Phys. Lett. B 736, 93, 2014). The results enable planning of $^3$He($K^-$, $\pi^0$) and $^3$He($\pi^-$, $K^0$) experiments at J-PARC to measure the hypertriton lifetime.
