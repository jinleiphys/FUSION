# Calculation of Coulomb breakup cross sections using a new Coulomb dynamical polarization potential

> A new method for calculating the Coulomb breakup of unstable neutron-rich isotopes at high energies is presented. The calculations employ the eikonal approximation and use a new Coulomb dynamical polarization potential (CDPP), calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation for the entire motion of the exotic projectile as a two-body cluster structure using the adiabatic approximation and incorporating excitations to the continuum. Calculations for some exotic isotopes are compared with Coulomb dissociation cross section data and found to be in good agreement.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for calculating Coulomb breakup cross sections of unstable neutron-rich isotopes at high energies, using a Coulomb dynamical polarization potential (CDPP) that includes both excited states of the core and excitations to the continuum. The method is claimed to produce results in good agreement with experimental data, but the agreement depends on the specific spectroscopic factors used and the choice of bound-state wave function (single-particle versus Whittaker/Yukawa form).

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation for the internal motion of a two-body (core + valence neutron) projectile using the adiabatic approximation, obtaining a continuum-based CDPP that depends on the relative excitation energy of the continuum. They then use the eikonal approximation to calculate the differential Coulomb dissociation cross section from the imaginary part of this CDPP via the continuity equation, integrating over continuum energies up to 10 MeV and summing over E1 transitions, core states, and angular momentum configurations. The calculations are performed for 11Be, 15C, and 17C projectiles incident on lead targets at energies of 496-605 MeV/nucleon, using both single-particle and Whittaker wave functions for the bound states.

## Key numbers

- 11Be on Pb at 520 MeV/nucleon: experimental cross section 605 +/- 30 mb; calculated cross sections 1563 mb (single-particle) and 1649 mb (Whittaker); cross section ratio R_sigma = 0.40 for both wave functions
- 15C on Pb at 605 MeV/nucleon: experimental cross section 324 +/- 15 mb for the |14C(0+) ⊗ nu 2s1/2> configuration; calculated cross sections 570 mb (single-particle) and 643 mb (Whittaker); R_sigma = 0.52 (single-particle) and 0.48 (Whittaker)
- 15C on Pb: experimental cross section 36 +/- 3 mb for core excited states; calculated contributions from 1-, 0-, and 2- core states total 12.1 mb (single-particle) and 25.4 mb (Whittaker) when multiplied by shell-model spectroscopic factors
- 17C on Pb at 496 MeV/nucleon: experimental cross section 62 +/- 7 mb for the |16C(2+) ⊗ nu 2s1/2> configuration; calculated cross sections 118.9 mb (single-particle) and 145.5 mb (Whittaker); R_sigma = 0.28 (single-particle) and 0.23 (Whittaker)
- 17C on Pb: experimental cross section 25 +/- 7 mb for the 4+ core state; calculated cross sections 15.5 mb (single-particle) and 30.1 mb (Whittaker)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDPP formalism (Maridi et al., Phys. Rev. C 104, 024614, 2021) by generalizing it to include both excited states of the core and excitations to the continuum. The method is applied to Coulomb dissociation data from GSI experiments (Pramanik et al., Phys. Lett. B 551, 63, 2003) for 11Be, 15C, and 17C, and the results are compared with spectroscopic factors from shell-model calculations (Sauvan et al., Phys. Lett. B 491, 1, 2000; Teranishi et al., 2004) and previous experimental extractions. The approach enables calculation of Coulomb breakup cross sections without the need for full continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations, and the authors note it can be generalized to compute neutron removal cross sections, gamma capture cross sections, and photo-absorption cross sections, as well as applied to low-energy scattering via the optical model or distorted wave Born approximation.
