# Reaction mechanisms for weakly-bound, stable nuclei and unstable, halo nuclei on medium-mass targets

> An experimental overview of reactions induced by the stable, but weakly-bound nuclei 6Li, 7Li and 9Be, and by the exotic, halo nuclei 6He, 8B, 11Be and 17F on medium-mass targets, such as 58Ni, 59Co or 64Zn, is presented. Existing data on elastic scattering, total reaction cross sections, fusion processes, breakup and transfer channels are discussed in the framework of a CDCC approach taking into account the breakup degree of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while weakly-bound and halo nuclei generally produce larger total reaction and fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies than predicted by one-dimensional barrier penetration models, the halo nature of projectiles like 6He does not enhance fusion probability as much as anticipated; instead, one- and two-neutron transfer channels play a prominent role, and for stable weakly-bound projectiles the effect of non-conventional transfer or stripping processes appears less significant.

## Method

The authors compiled and compared existing experimental data on elastic scattering, total reaction cross sections, fusion processes, breakup, and transfer channels for reactions induced by stable weakly-bound nuclei (6Li, 7Li, 9Be) and exotic halo nuclei (6He, 8B, 11Be, 17F) on medium-mass targets (58Ni, 59Co, 64Zn). They analyzed these data using Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) calculations that incorporate the breakup degree of freedom, and for the 6Li+59Co benchmark reaction they compared experimental fusion-evaporation residue yields with statistical-model predictions from CASCADE, PACE2, and EMPIRE-II codes.

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross sections for halo systems 6He+64Zn, 8B+58Ni, and 6He+209Bi lie well above the systematic behavior defined by tightly-bound systems like 16O+64Zn (blue curve in Fig. 1)
- 17F+58Ni total reaction cross sections follow the same trend as tightly-bound 16O+58Ni, not the halo curve
- Preliminary total reaction cross sections for 11Be+64Zn are at least twice those for 9Be+64Zn
- For 6Li+59Co at Elab = 29.6 MeV, the total experimental incomplete fusion (ICF) cross section is approximately 150 mb
- Statistical-model simulations with CASCADE for 6Li+59Co agree within 30% with both PACE2 and EMPIRE-II calculations
- No significant difference has been observed between 9,11Be+209Bi and 10Be+208Pb total reaction cross sections

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fusion dynamics for weakly-bound systems (Beck07a, Aguilera09, Chatterjee08, Pietro04) by systematically comparing stable weakly-bound projectiles with exotic halo projectiles on medium-mass targets. The paper contradicts earlier expectations that halo structure would strongly enhance fusion probability, instead demonstrating the importance of transfer channels for halo systems (Chatterjee08, Bychowski04, DeYoung04, Penion06, Escrig07) and the role of incomplete fusion for stable weakly-bound projectiles. The findings enable future experiments with high-intensity radioactive ion beams at facilities including SPIRAL/GANIL, Rex-Isolde, and MSU/NSCL, particularly using active-target time-projection chambers for reactions such as 8B+40Ar and 11Be+40Ar.
