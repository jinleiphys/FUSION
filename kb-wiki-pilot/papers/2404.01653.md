# Investigation of reaction and $α$ production cross sections with $^9$Be projectile

> In order to investigate the contribution of $α$ production in the reaction cross sections, measurements of elastic scattering and inclusive $α$ particle angular distributions have been carried out with the $^9$Be projectile on $^{89}$Y, $^{124}$Sn, $^{159}$Tb, $^{198}$Pt, and $^{209}$Bi targets over a wide angular range at energies near the Coulomb barrier. The measured elastic scattering angular distributions were fitted with optical model calculations, and reaction cross sections were extracted. The same data were also analysed using both global optical model potentials (Global OMP) and microscopic S$\tilde{a}$o Paulo potentials (SPP), to obtain the reaction cross sections. The data available in the literature for $^9$Be projectile includes the elastic scattering angular distributions, $α$ production cross sections, and complete fusion cross sections on these and other targets at several energies are also utilised for comparative studies. The reaction cross section extracted from the three potentials (Best Fit, Global OMP and SPP) are in reasonable agreement for all the targets except for the energies below the barrier where the results from SPP deviate by 30-50 \%. Inclusive $α$ particle production cross sections were also extracted by integrating the $α$ particle angular distributions. The present data and data available from literature of reaction and $α$-particle production cross sections were utilised to make systematic studies. Systematics of reaction and $α$-particle production cross sections revealed their universal behaviour.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for reactions induced by the weakly bound \(^9\)Be projectile on targets from \(^{89}\)Y to \(^{209}\)Bi, the sum of complete fusion (CF) and inclusive \(\alpha\) production cross sections nearly exhausts the total reaction cross section at all energies, with \(\alpha\) production dominating at sub-barrier energies and CF dominating above the barrier. However, this near-exhaustion holds only when using the reaction cross sections extracted from the best-fit optical model or global OMP; the microscopic Sao Paulo potential (SPP) deviates by 30-50% at sub-barrier energies.

## Method
Elastic scattering and inclusive \(\alpha\) particle angular distributions were measured for \(^9\)Be on \(^{89}\)Y, \(^{124}\)Sn, \(^{159}\)Tb, \(^{198}\)Pt, and \(^{209}\)Bi targets at energies near the Coulomb barrier using multiple silicon detector telescopes. Elastic scattering data were fitted with optical model calculations using three approaches: a best-fit Woods-Saxon potential, the global optical model potential (Global OMP) for \(^9\)Be, and the microscopic double-folding Sao Paulo potential (SPP). Inclusive \(\alpha\) production cross sections were obtained by integrating \(\alpha\) angular distributions after subtracting compound nuclear contributions estimated with the PACE2 statistical model code.

## Key numbers
- Reaction cross sections extracted from the three potentials (Best Fit, Global OMP, SPP) are in reasonable agreement for all targets except at sub-barrier energies, where SPP results deviate by 30-50%.
- For \(^{209}\)Bi at E\(_{lab}\) = 35.7 MeV (E\(_{c.m.}\)/V\(_B\) = 0.85): \(\sigma_\alpha\) = 203 \(\pm\) 47 mb, \(\sigma_{CF}\) = 0.62 \(\pm\) 0.12 mb, \(\sigma_R\) (Best Fit) = 55 \(\pm\) 18 mb.
- For \(^{159}\)Tb at E\(_{lab}\) = 35.3 MeV (E\(_{c.m.}\)/V\(_B\) = 1.02): \(\sigma_\alpha\) = 609 \(\pm\) 7.8 mb, \(\sigma_R\) (Best Fit) = 447 \(\pm\) 50 mb.
- For \(^{124}\)Sn at E\(_{lab}\) = 35.2 MeV (E\(_{c.m.}\)/V\(_B\) = 1.25): \(\sigma_\alpha\) = 663 \(\pm\) 16 mb, \(\sigma_{CF}\) = 517 \(\pm\) 11 mb, \(\sigma_R\) (Best Fit) = 1037 \(\pm\) 15 mb.
- Compound nuclear contributions to \(\alpha\) production were negligible (about 1% or less) for heavier targets (Z = 50, 65, 78, 83), but for \(^{89}\)Y at E\(_{c.m.}\) = 1.2 V\(_B\) it was around 7%.

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of \(\alpha\) production and reaction cross sections previously performed for \(^{6,7}\)Li projectiles (e.g., Santra et al., Pandit et al.) to the \(^9\)Be projectile, which has a Borromean cluster structure and lower neutron and \(\alpha\) separation energies. The results enable comparison of \(\alpha\) production mechanisms across weakly bound projectiles, showing a clear distinction that correlates with \(\alpha\) separation energies. The universal behavior of reduced reaction cross sections and the ratio (CF+\(\alpha\))/\(\sigma_R\) provides a framework for predicting reaction outcomes for other \(^9\)Be-induced reactions.
