# 2025 EIC-France Workshop: Physics Highlights and Perspectives

> This document presents a synthesis of the theory contributions and discussions from the 2nd EIC-France Workshop, held at IJCLab (Orsay) on 1-3 December 2025. The workshop brought together members of the French hadron-physics community to review recent theoretical developments relevant to the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and to coordinate national efforts in preparation for its early physics program. The report first summarizes the collider's initial running conditions and luminosity performance, as outlined in the EIC Early Science Matrix. It then provides concise overviews of the theoretical presentations on inclusive, semi-inclusive, exclusive, heavy-flavor, and small-x physics. Based on these discussions, two measurements emerged as especially well suited for early EIC operation and strongly aligned with areas of established French expertise: inclusive diffraction and inclusive quarkonium production. These channels offer clean signatures, robust theoretical interpretability, and direct sensitivity to fundamental QCD phenomena such as gluon saturation, heavy-quark dynamics, and the small-x structure of hadrons and nuclei. In addition, the workshop identified longer-term physics opportunities that will benefit from the full capabilities of the EIC after its ramp-up phase. These include accessing the three-dimensional structure of the pion through the Sullivan process and a broader program of exclusive three-body final states, both of which represent high-impact avenues for exploring hadronic structure and non-perturbative QCD. Together, the elements summarized in this report provide a coherent overview of the strategic priorities and scientific ambitions shaping the French community's contribution to the EIC physics program.

## Key claim

Based on the workshop discussions, inclusive diffraction and inclusive quarkonium production emerged as especially well suited for early EIC operation and strongly aligned with areas of established French expertise, though the authors note these represent a "realistic yet provisional roadmap" that will evolve as accelerator design is refined and physics priorities are reassessed.

## Key numbers

- Year 1: e+medium-mass nuclei (Ag, Ru, Cu) at 10 x 115 GeV with 0.9 fb^-1/year, no electron polarization (commissioning)
- Year 2: e+D at 10 x 130 GeV with 13.1 fb^-1/year, e+p at 10 x 130 GeV with 5.6-6.1 fb^-1/year, with longitudinal electron polarization
- Year 3: e+p at 10 x 130 GeV with 5.6-6.1 fb^-1/year, longitudinal electron polarization
- Year 4: e+Au at 10 x 100 GeV with 0.95 fb^-1/year, e+p at 10 x 250 GeV with 7.1-10.6 fb^-1/year
- Year 5: e+Au at 10 x 100 GeV with 0.95 fb^-1/year, e+^3He at 10 x 166 GeV with 9.8 fb^-1/year
- With 10 fb^-1 of data, the reach for inclusive prompt J/psi photoproduction will exceed 10 GeV, exceeding that of HERA
- For exclusive three-body final states, expected event rates from years 2 and 3 include ~10^4 events for gamma pi+, gamma rho0 and gamma rho+ production, and up to ~10^6 events for rho0 rho0, pi+ rho0 and pi- rho0 production

## Context

This workshop report synthesizes contributions from the 2nd EIC-France Workshop held at IJCLab (Orsay) on 1-3 December 2025, extending the work of the EIC Yellow Report (AbdulKhalek:2021gbh) and earlier EIC planning documents. The identified early priorities of inclusive diffraction and inclusive quarkonium production build on established theoretical frameworks including the Color Glass Condensate effective theory for saturation physics and non-linear QCD dynamics, while the longer-term opportunities for pion structure through the Sullivan process and exclusive three-body final states represent extensions of prior work on GPDs, TMDs, and factorization approaches. The projected yearly integrated luminosities already exceed, within a single year, the total integrated luminosity accumulated during the full operational lifetime of HERA.
