# Continuum effects in reactions involving weakly bound nuclei

> The relevance of the continuum effects in transfer reactions is discussed in conection with the determination of observables of astrophysical interest. In particular, we examine the validity of the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) as a tool to extract the astrophysical \emph{(S_{17}(0))} factor. For this purpose, we present calculations for the reaction (^{14})N((^{7} )Be,(^{8})B)(^{13})C comparing the DWBA method with the more sophysticated CDCC-Born approximation

## Key claim

The DWBA method is valid for extracting the astrophysical S17(0) factor from the transfer reaction 14N(7Be,8B)13C, despite the weak binding of 8B (epsilon0 = 0.137 MeV), because continuum effects are suppressed by the unfavourable Q value for transfer to continuum states, even though breakup couplings in the exit channel are strong.

## Method

The authors compared standard DWBA calculations with the CDCC-Born approximation (CDCC-BA) for the reaction 14N(7Be,8B)13C at 85 MeV bombarding energy. The CDCC-BA approach explicitly includes the 8B continuum by discretizing the 7Be-p relative motion into 10 bins up to 9 MeV excitation energy, including s, p, and d partial waves. The same core-core (7Be+13C) interaction was used in both methods, and the entrance channel was described with a renormalized double folding potential that reproduces 7Be+14N elastic data.

## Key numbers

- 8B binding energy: epsilon0 = 0.137 MeV
- Continuum discretization: N = 10 bins, uniformly spaced in energy up to epsilon_max = 9 MeV
- Spectroscopic factor for (8B|7Be) overlap: S(p3/2) = 0.733
- Spectroscopic factor for (14N|13C) overlap: 0.604 (p1/2 single-particle wavefunction)
- Bombarding energy for transfer reaction: 85 MeV (lab); CDCC elastic calculation for 8B+13C at Elab = 78 MeV
- Transfer reaction Q value: not explicitly given, but described as "unfavourable" for continuum transfer

## Context

This work extends the ANC method developed by Trache et al. (Phys. Rev. C61, 2000) and the DWBA analysis of Azhari et al. (Phys. Rev. C60, 1999) for the 14N(7Be,8B)13C reaction. It directly tests the validity of the DWBA extraction of the S17(0) factor by comparing with the more sophisticated CDCC-BA approach (Moro et al., Phys. Rev. C66, 2002). The results confirm that the DWBA analysis of Azhari et al. is reliable for this reaction, but the significant differences found between CDCC and folding potentials for 8B+13C elastic scattering at larger angles suggest caution when applying global parameterizations to weakly bound nuclei in other contexts.
