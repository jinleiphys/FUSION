# Uncorrelated scattering approximation revisited

> The formalism to describe the scattering of a weakly bound projectile nucleus by a heavy target is investigated, using the Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation. The main assumption involved is to neglect the correlation between the fragments of the projectile in the region where the interaction with the target is important. It is shown that the angular momentum of each fragment with respect to the target is conserved. Moreover, when suitable approximations are assumed, the kinetic energy of each fragment is also shown to be conserved. The S-matrix for the scattering of the composite system can be written as a combination of terms, each one being proportional to the product of the S-matrices of the fragments.

## Key claim

The Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation (USA) for weakly bound projectile scattering on a heavy target is valid when the collision time is much shorter than the correlation time (τ_C ≪ τ_R), where τ_R = ħ/Δ and Δ is the energy spread of the projectile ground state when the inter-fragment interaction is neglected; unlike the "frozen halo" (adiabatic) approximation, the USA does not depend on the strength of tidal forces.

## Method

The authors derive the USA by expanding the three-body T-matrix and neglecting the inter-fragment interaction v_AB in the propagator, replacing it with a renormalized Hamiltonian containing only kinetic energies of the two fragments. The S-matrix for the composite projectile-target system is then expressed as a weighted superposition of products of the on-shell two-body S-matrices for each fragment scattering independently from the target, with the weights determined by analytical coefficients and the projectile ground-state structure. The method is applied to deuteron scattering on ⁵⁸Ni at 80 MeV and compared to Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron scattering on ⁵⁸Ni at E_d = 80 MeV was used as the test case
- The p-n interaction was parametrized with v_0 = -72.15 MeV and a = 1.484 fm
- Maximum excitation energies for partial waves in CDCC: 40 MeV (s-waves), 45 MeV (d and f-waves), 50 MeV (g-waves), 60 MeV (h-waves)
- USA calculations with maximum hyperangular momentum K_m = 30 and K_m = 40 were compared; K_m = 40 gave convergence
- Both CDCC and USA predicted a maximum of the integrated breakup cross section at total angular momentum J = 17
- USA overestimated breakup cross sections compared to CDCC, consistent with the USA elastic angular distribution being somewhat underestimated at intermediate angles

## Context

This work revisits and extends the Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation previously introduced by the same authors (Mor01a) for describing reactions of weakly bound halo nuclei. It contrasts with the "frozen halo" (adiabatic) approximation (Glau59, Ron97, Ron97b), which assumes constant relative coordinates during scattering and depends on tidal force strength. The USA enables compact analytic expressions for elastic, inelastic, and breakup S-matrix elements in terms of two-body fragment-target S-matrices, and provides closed formulas for integrated breakup cross sections. The method is benchmarked against CDCC calculations (Aus87, Piy99, Ise86, Yah86) for deuteron scattering, showing good qualitative agreement but with some quantitative differences in breakup yields.
