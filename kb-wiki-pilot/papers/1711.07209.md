# Borromean Feshbach resonance in 11Li studied via 11Li(p,p')

> A dipole resonance of 11Li is newly found by a 9Li + n + n three-body model analysis with the complex-scaling method. The resonance can be interpreted as a bound state in the 10Li + n system, that is, a Feshbach resonance in the 9Li + n + n system. As a characteristic feature of the Feshbach resonance of 11Li, the 10Li + n threshold is open above the 9Li + n + n one, which reflects a distinctive property of the Borromean system. A microscopic four-body reaction calculation for the 11Li(p,p') reaction at 6 MeV/nucleon is performed by taking into account the resonance and nonresonant continuum states of the three-body system. The angular distribution of the elastic and inelastic scattering as well as the breakup energy spectrum recently observed are reproduced well.

## Key claim

A dipole resonance of 11Li is found at 0.42 MeV with a width of 0.28 MeV, interpreted as a "Borromean Feshbach resonance" where the 10Li + n threshold lies above the 9Li + n + n threshold, a distinctive property of Borromean systems. The claim is hedged by noting that the resonance is identified via complex-scaling method analysis of a three-body model, and its existence is validated by reproducing experimental data only when the three-body force strength is set to a specific value.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculation for the 11Li(p,p') reaction at 6 MeV/nucleon, using a complete set of 9Li + n + n three-body wave functions obtained by diagonalizing the three-body Hamiltonian with the Gaussian expansion method. The complex-scaling method (CSM) was employed to classify the three-body continuum states and identify resonances on the complex-energy plane, with the scaling angle set to 20 degrees. The Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was used for the projectile-target interaction, with a normalization factor N_I = 0.55 for the imaginary part determined by fitting elastic and breakup cross section data around 100 degrees.

## Key numbers

- Resonant energy of the dipole resonance in 11Li: ε_R = 0.42 MeV, with half-width Γ/2 = 0.14 MeV (total width Γ = 0.28 MeV)
- Ground state energy of 11Li: ε_0 = -0.369 MeV (used to determine three-body force strength V_3 = -11 MeV)
- Resonant energy (decay width) of 10Li in the 0p_{1/2} state: 0.46 MeV (0.36 MeV)
- Overlap probability (real part of α_ν^{θ_c}) for the 11Li resonance with 10Li + n structure: 0.92
- Model space included 93, 111, and 131 states for I^π = 0^+, 1^-, and 2^+ respectively, with ε < 5 MeV
- Breakup cross section integrated over ε from 0 to 1.13 MeV and over θ_c.m. from 115° to 124°

## Context

This work resolves a longstanding open question about the existence of an excited state in 11Li, the first discovered Borromean nucleus, which had contradictory results from hadronic scattering and Coulomb dissociation experiments (Nakamura et al. 2006, Korsheninnikov et al. 1997). The study extends previous three-body model analyses (Crespo et al. 2002, Ershov et al. 2004, Pinilla et al. 2012) and the macroscopic DWBA analysis of the recent 11Li(p,p') experiment (Tanaka et al. 2017) by providing a fully microscopic structure-reaction combined framework. The finding enables non-contradictory understanding of hadronic scattering and Coulomb dissociation data, and the Borromean Feshbach resonance concept may apply to other two-neutron halo nuclei such as 6He, 14Be, and 22C.
