# Coulomb-nuclear dynamics in the weakly-bound 8Li breakup

> A detailed study of total, Coulomb and nuclear breakup cross sections dependence on the projectile ground-state binding energy $\varepsilon_b$ is presented, by considering the $^8$Li+$^{12}$C and $^8$Li+$^{208}$Pb breakup reactions. To this end, apart from the experimental one-neutron separation energy of $^8$Li nucleus ($\varepsilon_b=2.03$~MeV), lower values of $\varepsilon_b$ down to $\varepsilon_b=0.01$~MeV, are also being considered. It is shown that all breakup processes become peripheral as $\varepsilon_b\to 0.01$ MeV, which is understood as due to the well-known large spacial extension of ground-state wave functions associated to weakly-bound projectiles. The Coulomb breakup cross section is found to be more strongly dependent on $\varepsilon_b$ than the nuclear breakup cross section, such that the Coulomb breakup becomes more significant as $\varepsilon_b$ decreases, even in a naturally nuclear-dominated reaction. This is mainly due to the long-range nature of the Coulomb forces, leading to a direct dependence of the Coulomb breakup on the electromagnetic transition matrix. It is also highlighted the fact that the nuclear absorption plays a minor role for small binding when the breakup becomes more peripheral.

# Coulomb-nuclear dynamics in the weakly-bound 8Li breakup

## Key claim
The authors show that as the projectile ground-state binding energy decreases toward 0.01 MeV, the Coulomb breakup becomes dominant over nuclear breakup even in a naturally nuclear-dominated reaction (8Li+12C), contradicting the expectation that target mass alone determines which breakup mechanism prevails. This effect is attributed to the long-range nature of Coulomb forces and the increasing peripherality of the reaction at very low binding energies.

## Method
The authors employ the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) formalism using the Fresco code, modeling 8Li as a 7Li core plus a valence neutron. They artificially vary the projectile ground-state binding energy from the experimental value (2.03 MeV) down to 0.01 MeV while keeping the projectile mass and charge fixed, and compute total, Coulomb, and nuclear breakup cross sections for 8Li+12C and 8Li+208Pb reactions at multiple incident energies. The separation of Coulomb and nuclear breakup components is achieved through a weak-coupling approximation where nuclear breakup includes only nuclear potentials plus the diagonal monopole Coulomb potential, while Coulomb breakup includes only the Coulomb component plus the monopole nuclear potential.

## Key numbers
- Experimental one-neutron separation energy of 8Li: ε_b = 2.03 MeV; artificially reduced values down to ε_b = 0.01 MeV are considered
- For 8Li+12C at E_lab = 14 MeV and 24 MeV: at ε_b = 2.03 MeV, dσ_nucl/dΩ ≈ dσ_tot/dΩ ≫ dσ_Coul/dΩ (nuclear-dominated); at ε_b = 0.01 MeV, dσ_nucl/dΩ ≪ dσ_Coul/dΩ ≈ dσ_tot/dΩ at forward angles (Coulomb-dominated)
- For 8Li+208Pb at E_lab = 36 MeV and 60 MeV: Coulomb breakup exceeds nuclear breakup by about three orders of magnitude as ε_b decreases
- Root-mean-square radius of 8Li ground state: √⟨r²⟩ = 2.39 fm at ε_b = 2.03 MeV, in agreement with reported values of 2.39±0.05 fm and 2.37±0.02 fm
- Nuclear absorption reduces breakup cross section by about one order of magnitude at ε_b = 2.03 MeV for 8Li+208Pb, but becomes negligible as ε_b → 0.01 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies on Coulomb-nuclear dynamics in weakly-bound projectile breakup (Refs. Thompson20, 1999Th, 2002Margueron, 2003Capel, Tarutina10, Hussein20, 2006Canto, 2009Lubian, 2009Canto) and recent investigations of binding energy dependence (Refs. Wang10, Rath100, 2016Rangel, Lei100, Mukeru15, 2020Mukeru). It specifically extends a previous analysis of the 8Li+208Pb reaction from Ref. 2020Mukeru. The results enable better understanding of how projectile binding energy affects the competition between Coulomb and nuclear breakup mechanisms, with implications for the Coulomb dissociation method (Refs. Bertulani10, Winther10, Baur10, Baur20) and for interpreting fusion suppression in weakly-bound systems (Ref. 2020Jha).
