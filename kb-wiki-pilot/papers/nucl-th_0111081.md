# A Possibility to Observe Short-Range NN Properties in the Deuteron Breakup $pd\to ppn$

> Quasi-binary reaction of the deuteron breakup $p+d\to (pp)+n$ with the final proton-proton pair $(pp)$ in the $^1S_0$ state is analyzed at initial energies $0.5 - 2$ GeV in the kinematics of backward elastic pd-scattering $pd\to dp$. On the basis of the main mechanisms of the $pd\to dp$ process, including initial and final state interaction, we show that unpolarized cross section and spin observables of this reaction exhibit important properties of the half-off-shell $pp(^1S_0)$-scattering amplitude, which are relevant to the nucleon-nucleon interaction at short distances.

## Key claim

The quasi-binary deuteron breakup reaction \(p+d\to (pp)+n\) with the final proton-proton pair in the \(^1S_0\) state, measured in the kinematics of backward elastic \(pd\to dp\) scattering, can reveal the node of the half-off-shell \(pp(^1S_0)\) scattering amplitude at off-shell momentum \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, provided the internal energy of the pp-pair is kept below 3 MeV to suppress contributions from higher orbital momenta.

## Method

The reaction amplitude was calculated using a coherent sum of one-nucleon exchange (ONE), single pN scattering (SS), and double pN scattering with \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation in the intermediate state, with initial and final state interactions treated in the eikonal distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) for the ONE mechanism. The half-off-shell \(pp(^1S_0)\) scattering amplitude was substituted into the upper vertex of the ONE diagram in place of the deuteron bound state, using the RSC potential for numerical calculations with the Paris potential giving similar results. The cross section and spin observables (tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) and spin-correlation parameter \(C_{yy}\)) were computed as functions of the proton beam kinetic energy \(T_p\) at neutron scattering angle \(\theta_{cm}=180^\circ\) and fixed pp-pair internal energy \(E_{pp}=3\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The node of the half-off-shell \(pp(^1S_0)\) amplitude occurs at off-shell momentum \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, corresponding to proton beam energy \(T_p\sim 0.7\) GeV at \(\theta_{cm}=180^\circ\)
- The deuteron S-wave function \(u(q)\) also has a node at \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, indirectly connected to the null of the deuteron charge form factor \(G_C(Q)\) at transferred momentum \(Q\sim 4.5\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- The \(\Delta\)-mechanism dominates in the node region \(T_p\sim 0.7\) GeV but becomes negligible at \(T_p>1\) GeV
- The ONE mechanism dominates at \(T_p<0.5\) GeV and above 1 GeV
- Maximal cross section is expected at pp-pair internal energy \(E_{pp}=0.3-0.7\) MeV; below 0.3 MeV the cross section decreases rapidly due to Coulomb repulsion
- The isospin factor of \(\frac{1}{3}\) suppresses the \(\Delta\)-isobar mechanism amplitude compared to elastic backward \(pd\to dp\) scattering
- The analysis covers initial energies \(0.5-2\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the ONE+SS+\(\Delta\) model previously applied to backward elastic \(pd\to dp\) scattering (Kondratyuk, Lev, Shevchenko 1981; Kolybasov, Smorodinskaya 1973; Uzikov 1998) to the breakup channel with a singlet NN-pair. It addresses the systematic deviation from impulse approximation observed in inclusive deuteron disintegration data (Perdrisat, Punjabi 1990) and the discrepancy between ed- and pd-interactions, which may involve exotic mechanisms like \(N^*\)-exchanges or three-baryon resonances. The proposed reaction enables direct observation of the node in the half-off-shell NN amplitude, which has not been seen in elastic \(pd\to dp\) due to D-state contributions, and is planned for measurement at COSY.
