# (^{8})B breakup in elastic and transfer reactions

> We have studied the transfer reaction $^{14}$N($^7$Be,$^8$B)$^{13}$C at (\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{lab}}=84) MeV, paying special attention to the effects of the coupling to the continuum in the exit channel. Using the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) formalism, we find that these effects are important for the description of the elastic scattering observables. However, for the transfer process, differences between the predictions of the differential cross section within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) and the CDCC-Born approximation (CDCC-BA) are found to be negligible. This result supports the use of the DWBA method as a reliable tool to extract the $S_{17}(0)$ factor in this case.

## Key claim

The coupling to the continuum of \({}^{8}\)B is important for describing elastic scattering observables in the exit channel, but for the transfer process the differences between the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) and the CDCC-Born approximation (CDCC-BA) predictions of the differential cross section are negligible, supporting the use of DWBA as a reliable tool to extract the \(S_{17}(0)\) factor in this specific reaction.

## Method

The authors applied the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) formalism to describe the \({}^{8}\)B + \({}^{13}\)C elastic scattering at 78.4 MeV, discretizing the \({}^{8}\)B continuum into 10 energy bins from 0 to 9 MeV with \(s\), \(p\), and \(d\) partial waves. For the transfer reaction \({}^{14}\)N(\({}^{7}\)Be,\({}^{8}\)B)\({}^{13}\)C at 84 MeV, they compared the DWBA approach using renormalized double folding (RDF) optical potentials with the CDCC-BA method, where the exit channel wavefunction includes continuum couplings but the transfer step is treated in Born approximation. All calculations were performed with the computer code FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- The \({}^{8}\)B proton separation energy is 0.137 MeV
- The transfer reaction was studied at \(\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{lab}} = 84\) MeV
- The CDCC calculation used a maximum total angular momentum of \(L_{\mathrm{max}} = 100\) and matching radius of 40 fm
- The DWBA calculation using the RDF potential with \(N_r = 0.366\) differed from the full CDCC-BA calculation by only 5% at the maximum of the angular distribution
- At 160 MeV, the CDCC-BA and DWBA transfer cross sections differ by less than 1% at angles up to 30 degrees; at 40 MeV, differences of around 8% were found at the maximum of the angular distribution
- The renormalization constants for the RDF potential that best reproduced the CDCC elastic scattering were \(N_r = 0.427\) and \(N_i = 0.883\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the \({}^{14}\)N(\({}^{7}\)Be,\({}^{8}\)B)\({}^{13}\)C reaction previously measured and analyzed within DWBA by Azhari et al. (1999) to extract the astrophysical \(S_{17}\) factor for \({}^{7}\)Be(\(p,\gamma\))\({}^{8}\)B. It addresses concerns raised by Nunes et al. (2001) about the importance of coupling to excited inelastic channels and extends the CDCC methodology to test whether continuum couplings of the weakly bound \({}^{8}\)B affect the transfer reaction. The results confirm the validity of the DWBA approach used in the ANC method for this specific reaction, enabling continued use of this indirect method for determining \(S_{17}(0)\) while suggesting that similar tests should be performed for other transfer reactions involving loosely bound nuclei.
