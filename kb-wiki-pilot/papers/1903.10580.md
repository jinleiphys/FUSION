# Elastic scattering, inelastic excitation, and 1n pick-up transfer cross sections for $^{10}$B+$^{120}$Sn at energies near the Coulomb barrier

> The $^{10}$B+$^{120}$Sn reaction has been systematically studied at laboratory energies around the Coulomb barrier: E$_{\rm LAB}=$31.5, 33.5, 35.0, and 37.5 MeV. Cross sections for the elastic scattering and some reaction processes have been measured: excitation to the $1^+$ state of $^{10}$B; excitation to the $2^+$ and $3^-$ states of $^{120}$Sn; and the one-neutron pick-up transfer $^{120}$Sn($^{10}$B,$^{11}$B)$^{119}$Sn. Coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations have been performed in the context of the double-folding São Paulo potential. The theoretical calculations result in a good overall description of the experimental angular distributions. The effect on the theoretical elastic-scattering angular distributions of couplings to the inelastic and transfer states (through the CRC calculations) and to the continuum states (through continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations) has been investigated.

# Elastic scattering, inelastic excitation, and 1n pick-up transfer cross sections for $^{10}$B+$^{120}$Sn at energies near the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim
The CRC calculations, using the same parameter set determined from the 37.5 MeV data, provide a good overall description of the complete data set for all four energies without adjustable parameters, except for the elastic scattering angular distributions in the Fresnel angular region at 33.5 and 37.5 MeV, where the theoretical calculations fail to reproduce the experimental data. The CDCC calculations show that couplings to the continuum (breakup into $^{4}$He + $^{6}$Li) have a very small effect on the elastic scattering and do not resolve this discrepancy.

## Method
Elastic scattering, inelastic excitation (to the 1$^+$ state of $^{10}$B and the 2$^+$ and 3$^-$ states of $^{120}$Sn), and one-neutron pick-up transfer ($^{120}$Sn($^{10}$B,$^{11}$B)$^{119}$Sn) cross sections were measured at four laboratory energies (31.5, 33.5, 35.0, and 37.5 MeV) using the SATURN silicon detector array at the LAFN facility. Coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations were performed using the double-folding Sao Paulo potential (SPP) with the FRESCO code, treating $^{120}$Sn excitations as collective vibrational modes and $^{10}$B as a rotor, with all parameters fixed from the 37.5 MeV analysis. Continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations were also performed to investigate the effect of $^{10}$B breakup into $^{4}$He + $^{6}$Li on the elastic scattering.

## Key numbers
- Laboratory energies: 31.5, 33.5, 35.0, and 37.5 MeV (around the Coulomb barrier)
- $^{10}$B first excited state: 1$^+$, E* = 0.718 MeV
- $^{120}$Sn 2$^+$ state: E* = 1.171 MeV
- $^{120}$Sn 3$^-$ state: E* = 2.400 MeV
- $^{10}$B breakup threshold ($^{10}$B $\rightarrow$ $^{6}$Li + $^{4}$He): Q = -4.461 MeV
- Normalization factor for imaginary part of SPP in CRC calculations: $N_I$ = 0.25 (significantly smaller than the $N_I$ = 0.78 value for tightly-bound nuclei)
- $Q$-value for 1n pick-up transfer ground state: $Q_{g.s.}$ = 2.350 MeV
- Two groups of $^{119}$Sn states resolved: first group includes g.s., 23.8 and 89.5 keV states; second group includes 787.0, 920.5, 921.4, 1089, and 1354 keV states

## Context
This work is part of the E-125 experimental campaign at LAFN studying light weakly-bound stable nuclei ($^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, $^{9}$Be, $^{10}$B, $^{11}$B) on the same $^{120}$Sn target, enabling systematic comparisons. It extends the previous analysis at 37.5 MeV (Gasques et al., Phys. Rev. C 97, 034629, 2018) to three lower energies, providing parameter-free predictions. The unexplained Fresnel region discrepancy mirrors a similar effect reported for $^{9}$Be + $^{120}$Sn (Arazi et al., Phys. Rev. C 97, 044609, 2018), where the Fresnel peak appears and disappears across energies in a way not predicted by CDCC calculations. The results enable future measurements at higher energies to investigate this systematic behavior.
