# Single Electron Transverse Momentum and Azimuthal Anisotropy Distributions: Charm Hadron Production at RHIC

> Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is a basic gauge field theory to describe strong interactions. Lattice QCD calculations predict a phase transition from hadronic matter to a deconfined, locally thermalized Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) state at high temperature and small baryon density. Plenty of exciting results from RHIC experiments in the first three years have demonstrated that a hot dense matter with strong collective motion which cannot be described with hadronic degrees of freedom was created at RHIC. Charm quarks are believed to be mostly created from initial gluon fusion in heavy ion collisions. Since they are massive, charm hadrons are proposed to be ideal probes to study the early stage dynamics in heavy ion collisions. We provide here an indirect measurement of charm semi-leptonic decay. Single electron transverse momentum ($p_T$) distributions from 200 GeV \dAu, \pp collisions and 62.4 GeV \AuAu collisions, and single electron azimuthal anisotropy ($v_2$) from 62.4 GeV \AuAu collisions are presented. (Abridged)

# Single Electron Transverse Momentum and Azimuthal Anisotropy Distributions: Charm Hadron Production at RHIC

## Key claim
Charm quarks, produced primarily through initial gluon fusion in heavy ion collisions, can serve as ideal probes of early-stage dynamics; however, the paper presents an *indirect* measurement via charm semi-leptonic decay electrons, and the claim that measuring non-photonic electron $v_2$ for $p_T$ above 2 GeV/c is a feasible way to measure charm quark $v_2$ is conditional on the electron carrying almost all the saturated $v_2$ of open charm hadrons in that momentum range.

## Method
Single electron transverse momentum ($p_T$) distributions were measured from 200 GeV $d$+Au and $p$+$p$ collisions and 62.4 GeV Au+Au collisions, and single electron azimuthal anisotropy ($v_2$) was measured from 62.4 GeV Au+Au collisions. Background electrons from $\pi^0$ Dalitz decays were subtracted statistically, while electrons from two-photon conversions were rejected using a topological method. The $v_2$ of electrons from charm decays was compared to simulations where $D$ meson $v_2$ was assumed either equal to light quark $v_2$ or zero, using about 50 million $D^0$s input into PYTHIA to obtain decayed electrons.

## Key numbers
- At $p_T$ above 2 GeV/c, electrons carry almost all the saturated $v_2$ of open charm hadrons.
- The ratio $\sigma_{J/\psi}/\sigma_{c\bar{c}}$ is on the order of $10^{-2}$ and shows a slight decrease from low energies up to RHIC energy.
- The measured $\sigma_{c\bar{c}}^{NN} = 1.4$ mb would imply at least a factor of 3 increase above binary scaling in $J/\psi$ yield in central Au+Au collisions if statistical coalescence models are used.
- The initial Bjorken energy density in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is $\sim 5$ GeV/fm$^3$ (with $\tau\approx 1$ fm/c), well above the critical energy density $\epsilon_c \sim 1$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- $73\pm6^{+12}_{-26}$ GeV of the initial 100 GeV per participant is deposited and available for excitations at RHIC.
- The power law fit to total charm cross section data gives $n \sim 1.9 \pm 0.2$.
- Open charm spectrum parameters from $d$+Au: $dN/dy = 0.0265 \pm 0.0035$, $\langle p_T \rangle = 1.32 \pm 0.08$, $n = 8.3 \pm 1.2$.

## Context
This work extends measurements of charm production at RHIC, following the first publication of charm production at RHIC energy by PHENIX at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV in Au+Au collisions. The paper addresses the discrepancy between NLO pQCD calculations (e.g., Vogt, Mangano) and measured charm cross sections at RHIC, noting that while $\pi^0$ spectra are well reproduced by pQCD down to $p_T \sim 1$ GeV/c, charm production is underpredicted. The results enable tests of the color octet model for $J/\psi$ production in $p$+$p$ collisions, and challenge statistical coalescence models for $J/\psi$ production in Au+Au collisions when using the measured $\sigma_{c\bar{c}} = 1.4$ mb. The measurement of charm quark $v_2$ via non-photonic electrons can provide constraints on whether light quarks ($u$, $d$, $s$) have reached thermalization in the QGP.
