# Multi-step effects in subcoulomb breakup

> Following earlier one-step calculations, we explore the contributions of multi-step effects for the breakup of low energy $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb within a coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism. The Coulomb multi-step differential cross section is significantly reduced for all angles, the largest effect being the destructive interference of nuclear couplings. The nuclear peak, at around $80^\circ$ in the one-step calculation for $^{58}$Ni, virtually disappears.

## Key claim

Multi-step effects in subcoulomb breakup of $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb produce significant reductions in the differential cross section compared to first-order DWBA calculations, with the nuclear peak at around $80^\circ$ for $^{58}$Ni virtually disappearing when full coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) calculations are performed, contrary to earlier one-step predictions.

## Method

The authors employed the coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism to solve the three-body scattering problem for $^8$B (treated as a proton + $^7$Be core) incident on $^{58}$Ni at 26 MeV and on $^{208}$Pb. Continuum states were discretised into energy bins for s, p, d, and f partial waves up to 3 MeV excitation energy, with monopole, dipole, and quadrupole contributions included for both nuclear and Coulomb mechanisms. The Born series for the S-matrix was resummed using Pad\'e approximants to handle the divergent multi-step DWBA expansion, with convergence verified against full CDCC calculations in a reduced subspace of 42 bins.

## Key numbers

- For $^{58}$Ni at 26 MeV: the Coulomb peak at $\theta \simeq 20^\circ$ is reduced by approximately 10% when multi-step effects are included
- For $^{58}$Ni: the nuclear peak shifts from $\theta \simeq 80^\circ$ (1-step) to $\theta \simeq 40^\circ$ (CDCC) and is reduced to 1/6 of its 1-step peak value
- Radial integration for bin wave functions used $R_{bin}=50$ fm
- Coupled equations used $R_{coup}=300$ fm and partial waves up to $L_{max}=600$ for convergence
- The Kim $^8$B structure model produces a 30% increase in the total differential cross section compared to the Esbensen model
- For $^{208}$Pb: nuclear contribution is zero up to $50^\circ$ and becomes important only at backward angles

## Context

This work extends earlier one-step calculations by Nunes and Thompson (Phys. Rev. C57, R2818, 1998) and Shyam and Thompson (Phys. Lett. B415, 315, 1997) for $^8$B breakup, and addresses the need for multi-step treatments identified in semiclassical approaches by Esbensen and Bertsch (Nucl. Phys. A600, 37, 1996) and CDCC studies of $^6$Li breakup by Hirabayashi and Sakuragi (Phys. Rev. Lett. 69, 1892, 1992). The results enable more reliable extraction of the astrophysical $S_{17}$ factor from Coulomb dissociation experiments, showing that prior-form DWBA overestimates nuclear breakup probabilities and that continuum-continuum couplings produce significant dynamical distortion and recombination effects during the reaction.
