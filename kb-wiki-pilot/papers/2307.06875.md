# Simultaneous calculation of elastic scattering, fusion, and direct cross sections for reactions of weakly-bound projectiles

> Simultaneous analyses are performed for cross section data of elastic scattering, fusion, Coulomb breakup, and other direct yields for the $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. The bare and dynamical polarization potentials are constructed microscopically from the structure of the colliding nuclei and they reproduce all the data well with only one adjustable parameter. This method of calculation can be successfully applied to the reactions of weakly-bound and exotic projectiles with heavy targets.

# Simultaneous calculation of elastic scattering, fusion, and direct cross sections for reactions of weakly-bound projectiles

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a generalized optical model potential, constructed microscopically from the structure of the colliding nuclei, can simultaneously reproduce elastic scattering, fusion, Coulomb breakup, and direct nuclear reaction cross sections for the $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi system with only two adjustable parameters (the strengths of the real and imaginary parts of the nuclear dynamical polarization potential), contrary to the abstract's claim of "one adjustable parameter" which is corrected in the full text to "two adjustable parameters."

## Method

The total optical potential combines a bare nuclear potential from the Sao Paulo potential (SPP), a Coulomb dynamical polarization potential (CDPP) derived by solving the Schrodinger equation for the internal motion of a two-body projectile under the adiabatic approximation, and a long-range nuclear dynamical polarization potential (NDPP) of derivative Woods-Saxon form. The CDPP is constructed from the projectile's structure parameters (separation energy, masses, charges) with no free parameters, while the NDPP geometry is fixed by semiclassical theory using the strong absorption radius and the decay length of the initial wave function. The fusion cross section is obtained from the volume imaginary part of the bare potential, and the direct reaction cross sections from the surface imaginary parts of the CDPP and NDPP, using the extended optical model formalism of Udagawa and collaborators.

## Key numbers

- For $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 14.3$ MeV: $\sigma_F = 0.4$ mb, $\sigma_{\mathrm{bu}}^C = 51$ mb, $\sigma_{\mathrm{DN}} = 162$ mb, $\sigma_{\mathrm{Reac}} = 214$ mb
- For $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 21.9$ MeV: $\sigma_F = 398$ mb, $\sigma_{\mathrm{bu}}^C = 227$ mb, $\sigma_{\mathrm{DN}} = 578$ mb, $\sigma_{\mathrm{Reac}} = 1202$ mb
- NDPP diffuseness for $^{6}$He: $a_L = 2.0$ fm using $\varepsilon_0 = 0.975$ MeV (actual $2n$ separation energy), or $a_L = 1.565$ fm using $\varepsilon_0 = 1.6$ MeV (effective separation energy)
- CDPP becomes purely real for large separation energies and has $|\delta W_C / \delta V_C| \rightarrow \sqrt{3}$ as $\varepsilon_0 \rightarrow 0$
- Dipole polarizability: $\alpha_0 = \frac{1}{16} \frac{\hbar^2 \mu_p Z_P^2 e^2}{m_c^2 \varepsilon_0^2}$

## Context

This work extends the extended optical model approach of Udagawa et al. (1984-1990) and the CDPP formalism developed by Maridi et al. (2021, 2022) to provide a unified description of all reaction channels for weakly-bound projectiles on heavy targets. It builds upon previous analyses of the $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi system by Kim et al. (2002), Garrido et al. (2007), and others, which required more adjustable parameters or treated channels separately. The method enables simultaneous prediction of elastic scattering angular distributions, fusion excitation functions, and direct reaction angular distributions for exotic nuclei, and can be extended to other weakly-bound projectiles such as $^{11}$Li and $^{11}$Be.
