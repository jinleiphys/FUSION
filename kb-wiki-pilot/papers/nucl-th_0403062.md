# Chaos driven fusion enhancement factor at astrophysical energies

> We perform molecular dynamics simulations to assess the screening effects by bound target electrons in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratories. Quantum effects corresponding to the Pauli and Heisenberg principle are enforced by constraints. We show that the enhancement of the average cross section and of its variance is due to the perturbations induced by the electrons.This gives a correlation between the maximum amplitudes of the inter-nuclear oscillational motion and the enhancement factor. It suggests that the chaotic behavior of the electronic motion affects the magnitude of the enhancement factor.

# Chaos driven fusion enhancement factor at astrophysical energies

## Key claim
The enhancement of the low-energy D+d fusion cross section due to bound target electrons is driven by chaotic behavior of the electronic motion, which couples to the inter-nuclear oscillational motion and determines the magnitude of the enhancement factor. The averaged enhancement factor does not exceed the adiabatic limit, but fluctuations from individual events can exceed it.

## Method
The authors performed constrained molecular dynamics (CoMD) simulations of the D+d system, enforcing the Heisenberg uncertainty principle as a constraint on the classical equations of motion. They treated the tunneling process by switching to imaginary time evolution when the collective momentum of the two nuclei becomes zero, computing the penetrability from the action integral. For each event at a given incident energy, they obtained an ensemble of enhancement factors by taking the ratio of the penetrability with electrons to the penetrability without electrons.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of deuterium atom reproduced by the simulation: -13.56 eV (experimental value: -13.59811 eV)
- Mean square radius of deuterium atom from simulation: 0.5327 Angstrom (Bohr radius: 0.529 Angstrom)
- Averaged screening potential from fit to simulation data: U_e = 15.9 +/- 2.0 eV
- Adiabatic limit screening potentials for D+d: U_e^(g) = 40.7 eV (gerade), U_e^(e) = 0.0 eV (ungerade)
- Enhancement factor at E_cm = 0.15 keV: values range from f_e = 6.5 (event B) to f_e = 170.8 (event A)
- Power law exponent for Sigma^2/f_e versus incident energy: -2.6

## Context
This work extends previous time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) studies of electron screening by Shoppa, Koonin, Langanke, and Seki (1993) and by Kimura, Takigawa, Abe, and Brink (2003), which suggested the screening potential does not exceed the adiabatic limit. The CoMD approach captures fluctuations and correlations beyond the mean-field TDHF scheme, which is cylindrically symmetric by construction. The results address the puzzle that experimentally extracted screening potentials from the Muenster group's data on 3He(d,p)4He and 2H(3He,p)4He reactions exceed the adiabatic limit. The authors suggest experiments with polarized targets at very low energies to control the enhancement factor by changing the polarization direction relative to the beam.
