# Coupled reaction channel study of the 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction, and the 8Be+8Be optical potential

> Given the established 2 alpha structure of 8Be, a realistic model of 4 interacting alpha clusters must be used to obtain a 8Be+8Be interaction potential. Such a four-body problem poses a challenge for the determination of the 8Be+8Be optical potential (OP) that is still unknown due to the lack of the elastic 8Be+8Be scattering data. The main goal of the present study is to probe the complex 8Be+8Be optical potential in the coupled reaction channel (CRC) study of the alpha transfer 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction measured at 65 MeV, and to obtain the spectroscopic information on the alpha+8Be cluster configuration of 12C. The 3- and 4-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) methods are used to calculate the elastic alpha+8Be and 8Be+8Be scattering at the energy around 16 MeV/nucleon, with the breakup effect taken into account explicitly. Using the CDCC-based OP and alpha spectroscopic factors given by the cluster model calculation, a good CRC description of the alpha transfer data without any adjustment of the (complex) potential strength.

# Coupled reaction channel study of the 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction, and the 8Be+8Be optical potential

## Key claim

The 8Be+8Be optical potential can be reliably determined from a 4-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculation using a shallow Ali-Bodmer alpha-alpha interaction, but only when the Pauli forbidden states are avoided; the deep Buck alpha-alpha potential fails to produce a smooth J-independent local equivalent potential due to spurious states, limiting the method to the shallow potential.

## Method

The authors performed 3-body and 4-body CDCC calculations for the alpha+8Be and 8Be+8Be systems, respectively, using the Ali-Bodmer alpha-alpha potential to generate pseudostates that simulate breakup effects. From the CDCC elastic scattering wave functions, they derived local equivalent optical potentials using two approaches: (i) a quantum-mechanical matrix inversion method with angular momentum averaging, and (ii) a standard Woods-Saxon parameter fit to the CDCC elastic cross sections. These potentials were then used as inputs in a coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculation of the alpha transfer reaction 12C(alpha,8Be) at 65 MeV, using the code FRESCO with spectroscopic factors from the complex scaling method of Kurokawa and Kato.

## Key numbers

- The 12C(alpha,8Be) reaction was measured at E_alpha = 65 MeV (from Wozniak et al.)
- CDCC calculations for 8Be+8Be were performed at E_c.m. = 41.3 MeV; for alpha+8Be at E_c.m. = 43.3 MeV (approximately 16 MeV/nucleon)
- The Ali-Bodmer alpha-alpha potential was used with maximum angular momentum lmax = 2 and maximum pseudostate energy Emax = 10 MeV
- The 8Be ground state is unbound by 92 keV; a quasi-bound approximation with 0.01 MeV binding energy was used for the transfer calculation
- Alpha spectroscopic factors from the complex scaling method: S_alpha(g.s.) = 0.36 for the 3S configuration, S_alpha(2+) = 0.38 for the 2D configuration
- Volume integrals of the 8Be+8Be optical potential: J_V = 95.88 MeV fm^3 (U_WSD) and 100.0 MeV fm^3 (U_LEP) for the real part; J_W = 46.87 MeV fm^3 (U_WSD) and 29.71 MeV fm^3 (U_LEP) for the imaginary part
- The interpolated WS potential from 7,8Be+9Be systems (U_Int WS) required a spectroscopic factor scaling factor of 25 to match the data, demonstrating its inadequacy

## Context

This work addresses the challenge of determining the 8Be+8Be optical potential, which cannot be obtained from standard elastic scattering measurements due to the short lifetime of 8Be. It extends the 4-body CDCC method previously applied to 11Be+d scattering by Descouvemont et al. (2018) to the 4-alpha system, and provides the first CDCC-based optical potentials for the 8Be+8Be and alpha+8Be systems. The CRC results validate the spectroscopic factors for alpha+8Be cluster configurations in 12C predicted by the complex scaling method of Kurokawa and Kato (2007), and demonstrate that phenomenological potentials interpolated from neighboring Be isotopes (as used by Romanyshyn et al., 2009) are unreliable for this system. The approach enables future studies of alpha transfer reactions involving 8Be in the exit channel, which probe the 4-alpha interaction and cluster structure of 12C.
