# Spin-Dependent Nucleon-Nucleus Interactions Constrained by Neutron Observables and Their Impact on Near-Barrier Proton Fusion

> We investigate the role of spin-dependent nucleon-nucleus interactions in nuclear reactions. To this end, we use neutron spin observables to constrain the dominant central spin-spin form factors and then apply the corresponding like-channel interactions to near-barrier fusion in the $p+{}^{93}$Nb system. The interactions are constructed within a folding framework based on a finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon force and organized in terms of radial form factors associated with their spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit components. Neutron spin observables in the $n+{}^{27}$Al, $n+{}^{59}$Co, and $n+{}^{93}$Nb target systems are analyzed within a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) framework to constrain the sign and normalization in the central spin-spin parts of the radial form factors and to examine the assembled operator conventions. The calculation reproduces the observed sign systematics of the neutron spin observables for the three targets, indicating that the essential spin-dependent structure is properly incorporated. The unlike-channel (neutron-proton) interaction constrained by neutron scattering is then reconstructed for the corresponding like-channel (proton-proton) interaction and applied to a coupled-channels description of near-barrier fusion for the $p+{}^{93}$Nb system. The resultant spin-dependent interactions lead only to a weak modification of the effective barrier and change the fusion cross section by about $0.01$-$0.03\%$ in the present calculation. These results show that the corresponding real spin-dependent correction in the like-channel is strongly suppressed in near-barrier fusion in $p+{}^{93}\mathrm{Nb}$. The present work thus connects the neutron-scattering constraints on the operator conventions with the fusion calculation in the proton channel, and quantifies the magnitude of the corresponding real spin-dependent correction in near-barrier fusion.

## Key claim

The real spin-dependent correction in the like-channel (proton-proton) interaction, when constrained by neutron spin observables and applied to near-barrier proton fusion in the p+93Nb system, is strongly suppressed and changes the fusion cross section by only about 0.01-0.03%, indicating that the dominant central spin-spin component has a negligible effect on near-barrier fusion despite being essential for reproducing neutron spin observables.

## Method

The authors constructed spin-dependent nucleon-nucleus interactions within a folding framework using the finite-range Tokyo-4-Yukawa (T4Y) effective nucleon-nucleon force, organized into radial form factors for central spin-spin (F10, F12, F32), tensor, and spin-orbit components. Neutron spin observables for n+27Al, n+59Co, and n+93Nb were analyzed within a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) framework to constrain the sign and normalization of the central spin-spin form factors and verify the operator conventions. The unlike-channel (neutron-proton) interaction constrained by neutron scattering was then reconstructed for the corresponding like-channel (proton-proton) interaction and applied to a coupled-channels description of near-barrier fusion for the p+93Nb system.

## Key numbers

* Fusion cross section change in p+93Nb: about 0.01-0.03% from the inclusion of spin-dependent interactions
* Valence-proton separation energies used: 8.271 MeV for 27Al, 7.364 MeV for 59Co, and 6.043 MeV for 93Nb
* Woods-Saxon potential parameters for bound-state wave functions: r0=1.20 fm, a0=0.65 fm
* Central depth parameters for valence-proton orbits: V0=-56.588 MeV (27Al), -56.657 MeV (59Co), -60.101 MeV (93Nb)
* Target polarization inputs: Pt=0.428064 (27Al), 0.500040 (59Co), 0.640000 (93Nb)
* Maximal change in neutron spin observable from including projectile spin-orbit term: about 2.4 mb in the low-energy peak region
* Ratio of form factors for 93Nb stretched g9/2 configuration: F32(ss)=(3/2)F12(ss)

## Context

This work extends the folding-model studies of polarized-neutron scattering by McAbee et al. (1990, 1986) and applies the same operator conventions to proton-induced fusion, connecting neutron-scattering constraints with fusion calculations in the proton channel for the first time. The study uses the T4Y effective interaction (Hosaka 1985) and the CH89 global optical model potential (Varner 1991) for distorted waves, and addresses the question of how large the neutron-constrained real spin-dependent correction remains when propagated into near-barrier proton fusion. The results enable future work to focus on absorption effects and reaction dynamics rather than the real spin-dependent correction as the source of remaining discrepancies in neutron scattering.
