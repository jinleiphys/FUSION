# Dynamical Origin of Spectroscopic Quenching in Knockout Reactions

> Nucleon-removal reactions are a primary tool for extracting single-particle structure of rare isotopes, yet the ratio $R_s=σ_{\exp}/σ_{\mathrm{th}}$ of measured to theoretical cross sections drops systematically below unity for deeply bound nucleons. I derive the exact effective three-body Hamiltonian for composite-projectile reactions using a sequential double Feshbach projection and show that the standard additive model misses two induced interactions: a non-additive term from virtual target excitations and a polarization potential from excluded projectile configurations. Their omission overestimates the stripping cross section, producing apparent quenching distinct from genuine nuclear-structure correlations. This mechanism offers a dynamical origin for the strong separation-energy dependence of the quenching ratio, a feature unique to knockout analyses. Existing four-body CDCC calculations for $^{6}$Li validate the framework: the proper Feshbach reference reproduces elastic scattering data, while a phenomenological optical potential double counts the breakup absorption and fails.

# Dynamical Origin of Spectroscopic Quenching in Knockout Reactions

## Key claim

The standard additive three-body Hamiltonian used in knockout reaction analyses misses two induced interactions, a non-additive term from virtual target excitations and a polarization potential from excluded projectile configurations, whose omission systematically overestimates the stripping cross section, producing apparent spectroscopic quenching (R_s < 1) that is a dynamical artifact rather than genuine nuclear-structure depletion. This mechanism offers a dynamical origin for the strong separation-energy dependence of the quenching ratio, a feature unique to knockout analyses that is absent in transfer and (e,e'p) reactions.

## Method

The author derives the exact effective three-body Hamiltonian for composite-projectile reactions using a sequential double Feshbach projection, first eliminating target excitations (QA-space) then projecting onto the projectile model space (Pbx-space). The resulting decomposition separates the effective Hamiltonian into the conventional additive three-body Hamiltonian H_3^(0) plus two induced terms: a non-additive interaction Unonadd from virtual target excitations coupling the projectile constituents, and a dynamical polarization potential Upol from excluded projectile configurations. The framework is validated against published four-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations for 6Li + 209Bi elastic scattering at 29.9 MeV, comparing three-body CDCC calculations using either a single-folding deuteron potential (U_d^SF, satisfying the Feshbach condition) or a phenomenological deuteron optical potential (U_d^OP, which violates it).

## Key numbers

- The quenching ratio R_s = sigma_exp / sigma_th drops systematically from near unity for loosely bound nucleons to approximately 0.25 for deeply bound ones, with a pronounced dependence on the separation-energy asymmetry Delta S
- Transfer reactions yield R_s approximately 0.5-0.7 with essentially no asymmetry dependence
- (e,e'p) data exhibit a uniform approximately 0.6-0.7 depletion
- 6Li + 209Bi elastic scattering at E_in = 29.9 MeV: four-body CDCC (benchmark) reproduces data; three-body CDCC with U_d^SF reproduces the four-body result with no adjustable parameters; three-body CDCC with U_d^OP strongly underestimates the elastic cross section and fails to reproduce the data
- The d+alpha spectroscopic factor S_dalpha is approximately 0.70 for 6Li, leaving approximately 30% of the wave function in non-d+alpha configurations
- The deuteron binding energy is epsilon_d = 2.22 MeV; the alpha particle binding energy is approximately 28 MeV

## Context

This work generalizes earlier formal results on effective three-body interactions for deuteron-induced reactions (Austern 1968, Kozack and Levin 1986, Polyzou 1979) to arbitrary composite projectiles and establishes the direct link between missing induced interactions and spectroscopic quenching observed in knockout reactions. It extends the formal framework of Timofeyuk (2013, 2013, 2014, 2020), Johnson (2014), and Bailey (2017) who quantified corrections for A(d,p) reactions but did not connect them to spectroscopic quenching. The paper resolves the long-standing puzzle that different probes applied to the same nuclei yield different spectroscopic factors, knockout shows strong Delta S dependence (Tostevin 2014, 2021) while transfer (Flavigny 2013) and (e,e'p) (Kramer 2001, Kay 2013) show uniform depletion, by identifying the dynamical origin as missing induced interactions specific to composite-projectile reactions. The framework enables distinguishing genuine structure depletion from dynamical artifacts in spectroscopic factor extractions at FRIB, RIBF, and GSI.
