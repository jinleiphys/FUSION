# Heavy flavors under extreme conditions in high energy nuclear collisions

> Heavy flavor hadrons have long been considered as a probe of the quark gluon plasma created in high energy nuclear collisions. In this paper we review the heavy flavor properties under extreme conditions and the realization in heavy ion experiments. After a short introduction on heavy flavor properties in vacuum, we emphasize the cold and hot nuclear matter effects on heavy flavors, including shadowing effect, Cronin effect and nuclear absorption for the former and Debye screening and regeneration for the latter. Then we discuss, in the frame of transport and coalescence models, these medium induced changes in open and closed heavy flavors in nuclear collisions and the comparison with nucleon-nucleon collisions. Considering the extremely strong electromagnetic and rotational fields generated in non-central nuclear collisions, which are widely studied in recent years, we finally investigate their effects on heavy flavor production and evolution in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

This review argues that heavy flavor hadrons serve as sensitive probes of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in high-energy nuclear collisions, but the actual utility depends on the factorization of cold and hot nuclear matter effects, the specific binding energies of different heavy flavor states, and the influence of strong electromagnetic and rotational fields generated in non-central collisions, conditions that are not all simultaneously present in simpler probes.

## Method

The authors review heavy flavor production and evolution using transport and coalescence models, where heavy quarks are treated as initially produced via hard QCD processes and then propagated through the QGP via Boltzmann-type transport equations with collisional and radiative energy loss terms. For closed heavy flavors (quarkonia), they employ non-relativistic and relativistic potential models based on the Schrödinger equation with Cornell potential and hyperspherical harmonic expansion to compute bound state properties, dissociation temperatures, and regeneration rates. For open heavy flavors, fragmentation functions (Peterson, PFF, BKK) and coalescence mechanisms are used to describe hadronization, with the Wigner function approximated as a Gaussian distribution.

## Key numbers

- QGP crossover temperature from lattice QCD: \(T_c = 155\) MeV at zero baryon density
- Heavy quark masses: \(m_Q \sim (1-5)\) GeV, much larger than QGP temperature \(T \sim (0.3-0.5)\) GeV at RHIC and LHC
- Heavy quark production time scales: \(\Delta \tau \sim 0.07\) fm for charm quarks, \(0.02\) fm for bottom quarks, much shorter than QGP formation time \(\tau_0 \sim 0.5\) fm/c
- Colliding energies: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV at RHIC, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV at LHC; future FCC at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 39\) TeV
- Model-calculated charmonium masses: \(J/\psi\) at 3.102 GeV (experiment 3.097 GeV), \(\psi(2S)\) at 3.720 GeV (experiment 3.686 GeV)
- Model-calculated bottomonium masses: \(\Upsilon(1S)\) at 9.459 GeV (experiment 9.460 GeV), \(\Upsilon(2S)\) at 9.977 GeV (experiment 10.023 GeV)
- Model-calculated heavy flavor baryon masses: \(\Omega_{ccc}\) at 4.797 GeV, \(\Omega_{bbb}\) at 14.363 GeV
- Average radii: \(J/\psi\) at 0.427 fm, \(\Upsilon(1S)\) at 0.214 fm, \(\Omega_{ccc}\) root-mean-squared radius 0.289 fm

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of using heavy flavors as QGP probes, building on the sequential dissociation scenario for charmonia (Satz 2005) and lattice QCD simulations of heavy quark potentials at finite temperature (Petreczky 2010). It contrasts with earlier work at SPS energies by incorporating regeneration of uncorrelated heavy quarks at RHIC and LHC energies, and it adds the novel consideration of electromagnetic and rotational field effects (chiral magnetic effect, chiral vortical effect) on heavy flavor production and evolution. The work enables future experimental comparisons at RHIC, LHC, and FCC, and provides a framework for studying heavy flavor baryons and exotic multi-charmed states.
