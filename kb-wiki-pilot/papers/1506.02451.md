# Decay analysis of compound nuclei with mass A$\sim 30-200$ formed in the reactions involving loosely bound projectiles

> The dynamics of the reactions forming compound nuclei using loosely bound projectiles is analysed within the framework of dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) of Gupta and Collaborators. We have analysed different reactions with $^{7}Li$, $^{9}Be$ and $^{7}Be$ as neutron rich and neutron deficient projectiles, respectively, on different targets at the three $E_{lab}$ values, forming compound nuclei within the mass region A$\sim 30-200$. The contributions of light particles LPs ($A\le4$) cross sections $σ_{LP}$, energetically favoured intermediate mass fragments IMFs ($5 \le A_2 \le 20$) cross sections $σ_{IMF}$ as well as fusion-fission $\it{ff}$ cross sections $σ_{ff}$ constitute the $σ_{fus}$ (=$σ_{LP}$+$σ_{IMF}$+$σ_{ff}$) for these reactions. The contribution of the emitted LPs, IMFs and ff fragments is added for all the angular momentum upto the $\ell_{max}$ value, for the resepctive reactions. Interestingly, we find that the $ΔR^{emp}$, the only parameter of model and uniquely fixed to address the $σ_{fus}$ for all other reactions having $\it same$ loosely bound projectile at the chosen incident energy. It may be noted that the dynamical collective mass motion of preformed LPs, IMFs and ff fragments or clusters through the modified interaction potential barrier are treated on parallel footing. We see that the values of modified interaction barrier heights $ΔV_{B}^{emp}$ for such reactions are almost of the same amount specifically at the respective $\ell_{max}$ values.

## Key claim

The dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) can reproduce measured fusion cross sections for reactions induced by the same loosely bound projectile at a given incident energy using a single, uniquely fixed value of the neck length parameter ΔR^emp, without needing to adjust this parameter for each target individually. The claim is conditional on the projectile and laboratory energy being the same across the set of reactions.

## Method

The authors applied the dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) of Gupta and collaborators to 18 reactions forming compound nuclei in the mass range A ~ 30–200, using loosely bound projectiles ⁷Li, ⁷Be, and ⁹Be on various targets at three fixed laboratory energies (10, 17, and 28 MeV). For each projectile-energy combination, they empirically fixed a single neck length parameter ΔR^emp and calculated the total fusion cross section σ_fus as the sum of light particle (A ≤ 4), intermediate mass fragment (5 ≤ A₂ ≤ 20), and fusion-fission contributions, integrated over all angular momenta up to ℓ_max. The preformation probability P₀ and WKB penetrability P were computed from the collective mass motion of preformed clusters through a modified interaction potential barrier.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷Be-induced reactions at E_lab ~ 17 MeV, ΔR^emp = 1.130 fm: σ_fus for ⁷Be+²⁷Al → ³⁴Cl* is 646.74 mb (experiment: 635 ± 76 mb); for ⁷Be+⁵⁸Ni → ⁶⁵Ge* is 62.84 mb (experiment: 61.1 ± 6.9 mb)
- For ⁷Li-induced reactions at E_lab ~ 10 MeV, ΔR^emp = 0.907 fm: σ_fus for ⁷Li+²⁷Al → ³⁴S* is 437.13 mb (experiment: 415 ± 67 mb); for ⁷Li+²⁸Si → ³⁵Cl* is 352.64 mb (experiment: 352.81 ± 8 mb); for ⁷Li+⁵⁹Co → ⁶⁶Zn* is 2.82 mb (experiment: 3.97 mb)
- For ⁹Be-induced reactions at E_lab ~ 28 MeV, ΔR^emp = 1.138 fm: σ_fus for ⁹Be+²⁷Al → ³⁶Cl* is 1130.823 mb (experiment: 1191 mb); for ⁹Be+²⁸Si → ³⁷Ar* is 892.69 mb (experiment: 945 ± 94 mb); for ⁹Be+¹²⁴Sn → ¹³³Xe* is 82 mb (experiment: 89.6 ± 3.0 mb); for ⁹Be+¹⁴⁴Sm → ¹⁵³Dy* is 0.77 mb (experiment: 1.72 ± 1.02 mb); for ⁹Be+¹⁶⁹Tm → ¹⁷⁸Ta* is 0.182 mb (experiment: 0.198 mb); for ⁹Be+¹⁸⁷Re → ¹⁹⁶Au* is 0.0263 mb (experiment: 0.030 mb)
- Barrier modification ΔV_B values for ⁷Be-induced reactions at ℓ_max are nearly constant, ranging from -2.634 MeV to -3.970 MeV across targets from ²⁷Al to ⁶⁵Cu

## Context

This work extends the dynamical cluster decay model (DCM) framework previously applied by Gupta and collaborators to reactions in light, medium, heavy, and superheavy mass regions, now specifically to reactions with loosely bound projectiles (⁷Li, ⁷Be, ⁹Be). It contrasts with statistical models (e.g., Moretto 1975, Gomez del Campo 1991, Matsuse 1997) that treat light particle emission, intermediate mass fragments, and fission on different footings depending on compound nucleus mass, whereas DCM treats all decay modes on the same footing through collective clusterization. The results enable prediction of fusion cross sections for reactions with the same projectile and energy on stable targets where experimental data are not yet available, and establish a systematic behavior of the neck length parameter and associated barrier lowering for loosely bound projectiles.
