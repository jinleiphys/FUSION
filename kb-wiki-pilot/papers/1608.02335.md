# Exploring contributions from incomplete fusion in $^{6,7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi and $^{6,7}$Li+$^{198}$Pt reactions

> We use the breakup absorption model to simultaneously describe the measured cross-sections of the Complete fusion (CF), Incomplete fusion (ICF), and Total fusion (TF) in nuclear reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei $^{6,7}$Li on $^{209}$Bi and $^{198}$Pt targets. The absorption cross-sections are calculated using the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method with different choices of short range imaginary potentials to get the ICF, CF and TF cross-sections. It is observed that the cross-sections for deuteron-ICF/deuteron-capture are of similar magnitude as the $α$-ICF/$α$-capture, in case of $^{6}$Li projectile, while the cross-sections for triton-ICF/triton-capture is more dominant than $α$-ICF/$α$-capture in case of $^{7}$Li projectile. Both these observations are also corroborated by the experimental data. The ratio of ICF to TF cross-sections, which defines the value of fusion suppression factor is found to be in agreement with the data available from the literature. The cross-section ratio of CF/TF and ICF/TF show opposite behavior, the former decreases while the latter increases as the energy is lowered, which shows the dominance of ICF at below barrier energies. We have also studied the correlation of the ICF cross-sections with the non-capture breakup (NCBU) cross-sections as a function of energy, which shows that the NCBU is more significant than ICF at below barrier energies.

# Exploring contributions from incomplete fusion in $^{6,7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi and $^{6,7}$Li+$^{198}$Pt reactions

## Key claim

Using a breakup-absorption model within the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) framework with short-range imaginary potentials applied selectively to projectile fragments, the authors simultaneously describe complete fusion (CF), incomplete fusion (ICF), and total fusion (TF) cross-sections for $^{6,7}$Li on $^{209}$Bi and $^{198}$Pt targets across a wide energy range, achieving this for the first time in a fully quantum mechanical calculation. The model's success is qualified by the observation that for the $^{6}$Li+$^{198}$Pt system, the calculated d-ICF cross-sections significantly underpredict the available data, which the authors attribute to possible contributions from direct d-transfer reactions not included in the model.

## Method

CDCC calculations were performed using the code FRESCO version 2.9 with cluster folding potentials for the real part and short-range imaginary volume potentials (W=25 MeV, r$_w$=0.6-0.69 fm, a$_w$=0.4 fm) applied to fragment-target interactions. Three independent calculations were performed with the short-range imaginary potential applied to: (i) both projectile fragments (Pot. A) to obtain TF, (ii) only the $\alpha$-target part (Pot. B) to obtain $\alpha$-ICF, and (iii) only the deuteron/triton-target part (Pot. C) to obtain d/t-ICF, with CF then extracted as TF minus total ICF. The couplings included resonances and $\alpha$-d or $\alpha$-t continuum states up to L=3 for $^{6}$Li and $^{7}$Li respectively.

## Key numbers

- The suppression factor (ratio of ICF to TF) at above-barrier energies is approximately 30%, in agreement with literature data for $^{6,7}$Li projectiles from various measurements.
- For $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi, the d-ICF and $\alpha$-ICF cross-sections are of similar magnitude.
- For $^{7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi, the t-ICF cross-section is much higher than the $\alpha$-ICF cross-section, consistent with experimental data.
- The $\sigma_{\textrm{ICF}}/\sigma_{\textrm{TF}}$ ratio increases at energies below the Coulomb barrier, while $\sigma_{\textrm{CF}}/\sigma_{\textrm{TF}}$ decreases, indicating dominance of ICF at sub-barrier energies.
- The $\sigma_{\textrm{ICF}}/\sigma_{\textrm{NCBU}}$ ratio decreases at below-barrier energies, showing non-capture breakup is more significant than ICF at those energies.
- For $^{6}$Li+$^{198}$Pt, the available d-ICF data at the last two measured energies exceeds the calculated total fusion cross-section, which the authors note is surprising.

## Context

This work extends the breakup-absorption model previously applied by the same group to $^{9}$Be-induced reactions (Jha et al., Phys. Rev. C 89, 034605, 2014) to the $^{6,7}$Li projectiles, which have well-defined cluster structures ($\alpha$+d and $\alpha$+t). It addresses the scarcity of fully quantum mechanical calculations for individual CF and ICF cross-sections, contrasting with earlier semiclassical approaches (Marta et al., Phys. Rev. C 89, 034625, 2014) that did not satisfactorily describe the data. The results enable systematic studies of the correlation between ICF and non-capture breakup as a function of energy, and provide a framework for understanding the universal fusion suppression observed in reactions with weakly bound projectiles.
