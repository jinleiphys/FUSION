# Fusion at near-barrier energies within quantum diffusion approach

> The nuclear deformation and neutron-transfer process have been identified as playing a major role in the magnitude of the sub-barrier fusion (capture) cross sections. There are a several experimental evidences which confirm the importance of nuclear deformation on the fusion. The influence of nuclear deformation is straightforward. If the target nucleus is prolate in the ground state, the Coulomb field on its tips is lower than on its sides, that then increases the capture or fusion probability at energies below the barrier corresponding to the spherical nuclei. The role of neutron transfer reactions is less clear. The importance of neutron transfer with positive Q-values on nuclear fusion (capture) originates from the fact that neutrons are insensitive to the Coulomb barrier and therefore they can start being transferred at larger separations before the projectile is captured by target-nucleus. Therefore, it is generally thought that the sub-barrier fusion cross section will increase because of the neutron transfer. The fusion (capture) dynamics induced by loosely bound radioactive ion beams is currently being extensively studied. However, the long-standing question whether fusion (capture) is enhanced or suppressed with these beams has not yet been answered unambiguously. The study of the fusion reactions involving nuclei at the drip-lines has led to contradictory results.

## Key claim

Within the quantum diffusion approach, the effect of neutron transfer on sub-barrier capture cross sections depends critically on whether the deformations of the interacting nuclei increase or decrease after the transfer, rather than simply on the sign of the Q-value; the transfer can enhance, suppress, or leave unchanged the capture probability depending on the resulting change in deformation parameters.

## Method

The authors model capture using a single collective variable (the relative distance between colliding nuclei) with fluctuation and dissipation effects included via a quantum diffusion formalism. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated with a double-folding Skyrme-type density-dependent interaction, and the capture probability is obtained by integrating a propagator for an inverted oscillator approximating the potential barrier. Neutron transfer is incorporated by assuming that one- or two-neutron transfer with positive Q-value occurs just before barrier crossing, changing the mass numbers, isotopic composition, and deformation parameters of the interacting nuclei, which in turn modifies the Coulomb barrier height and shape.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously developed by the same group (EPJSub, EPJSub1, EPJSub2, EPJSub3) to systematically study the role of neutron transfer in sub-barrier fusion across a wide range of systems. It contradicts the simple assumption that positive Q-value neutron transfer always enhances sub-barrier fusion, showing instead that the effect depends on the resulting deformation changes. The paper enables predictions for reactions with weakly bound nuclei near the drip lines, addressing the long-standing question of whether fusion is enhanced or suppressed with radioactive ion beams, and suggests new methods for studying breakup probability.
