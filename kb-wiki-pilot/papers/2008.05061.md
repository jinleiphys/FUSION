# A Guided Tour of Ab Initio Nuclear Many-Body Theory

> Over the last decade, new developments in Similarity Renormalization Group techniques and nuclear many-body methods have dramatically increased the capabilities of ab initio nuclear structure and reaction theory. Ground and excited-state properties can be computed up to the tin region, and from the proton to the presumptive neutron drip lines, providing unprecedented opportunities to confront two- plus three-nucleon interactions from chiral Effective Field Theory with experimental data. In this contribution, I will give a broad survey of the current status of nuclear many-body approaches, and I will use selected results to discuss both achievements and open issues that need to be addressed in the coming decade.

## Key claim

This paper provides a broad survey of the current status of ab initio nuclear many-body approaches, arguing that while ground and excited-state properties can now be computed up to the tin region and from the proton to the presumptive neutron drip lines, significant open issues remain that must be addressed in the coming decade, particularly regarding the shortcomings of chiral NN+3N interactions revealed by confronting them with experimental data.

## Method

The paper reviews the three essential elements of modern ab initio nuclear many-body calculations: chiral Effective Field Theory interactions organized by a power counting scheme in (Q/Lambda), Similarity Renormalization Group evolution that decouples low- and high-momentum physics via continuous unitary transformations, and a variety of many-body methods including Many-Body Perturbation Theory, In-Medium SRG, Coupled Cluster, Self-Consistent Green's Function methods, and no-core configuration space approaches. The author discusses how these methods solve the nuclear Schrodinger equation by expanding eigenstates on a basis of many-body states or by working directly with wave functions, with systematic convergence toward exact results achieved by adding particle-hole excitations or exhausting wave function parameters.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown scale Lambda_chi of chiral EFT is traditionally considered in the range 700-1000 MeV, though newer Bayesian analyses favor slightly lower values
- The SRG flow is re-parameterized by lambda = s^{-1/4}, which characterizes the band width in momentum space such that |k_i - k_f| is approximately less than lambda in a two-nucleon system
- The second-order MBPT ground-state energy calculation scales as O(N^4) in the single-particle basis size N, or more favorably as O(N_p^2 N_h^2) where N_h ~ A is much smaller than N_p
- The SRG evolution is exact for A <= 3 systems under the truncation of four- and higher-body induced terms

## Context

This work extends the rapid developments in ab initio nuclear theory over the past decade following the widespread adoption of Renormalization Group techniques and chiral EFT in the 2000s, building on foundational work by Bogner, Furnstahl, and Schwenk on SRG, and by Weinberg, Epelbaum, Machleidt, and others on chiral EFT. The paper enables future work by identifying specific open issues in interaction development and many-body method improvements that need to be addressed, particularly regarding regularization and renormalization of chiral interactions, and by documenting the progress from closed-shell nuclei through semi-magic isotopic chains to the current frontier of open-shell nuclei up to tin.
