# Long range effects on the optical model of 6He around the Coulomb barrier

> We present an optical model (OM) analysis of the elastic scattering data of the reactions 6He+27Al and 6He+208Pb at incident energies around the Coulomb barrier. The bare part of the optical potential is constructed microscopically by means of a double folding procedure, using the Sao Paulo prescription without any renormalization. This bare interaction is supplemented with a Coulomb dipole polarization (CDP) potential, which takes into account the effect of the dipole Coulomb interaction. For this CDP potential, we use an analytical formula derived from the semiclassical theory of Coulomb excitation. The rest of the optical potential is parametrized in terms of Woods-Saxon shapes. In the 6He+208Pb case, the analysis confirms the presence of long range components, in agreement with previous works. Four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels calculations have been performed in order to better understand the features of the optical potentials found in the OM analysis. This study searches to elucidate some aspects of the optical potential of weakly bound systems, such as the dispersion relation and the long range (attractive and absorptive) mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the long-range absorption observed in the elastic scattering of the weakly-bound halo nucleus 6He on a heavy target (208Pb) near the Coulomb barrier is partially, but not fully, accounted for by the Coulomb dipole polarization (CDP) potential; a remaining long-range component in both the real and imaginary parts of the optical potential is required, with the imaginary diffuseness parameter reduced from about 1.9 fm to about 1.1 fm when the CDP potential is included.

## Method

The authors performed an optical model (OM) analysis of elastic scattering data for 6He+27Al and 6He+208Pb at laboratory energies from 14 to 27 MeV. The bare nuclear interaction was constructed using a double-folding procedure with the Sao Paulo potential (SPP) without renormalization. This bare potential was supplemented with an analytical Coulomb dipole polarization (CDP) potential derived from semiclassical Coulomb excitation theory, which is fully determined by the B(E1) distribution of 6He. The remaining part of the interaction was parametrized with Woods-Saxon (WS) shapes, with parameters adjusted to fit the elastic data. Four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations were also performed to interpret the features of the extracted optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of 6He is S2n = 0.975 MeV
- For the 6He+208Pb system, the strong absorption radius is Rsa = 12.5 fm
- The imaginary diffuseness parameter required to fit the 6He+208Pb data without CDP potential is ai ≈ 1.9 fm (ranging from 1.6 to 2.6 fm across energies)
- When the CDP potential is included, the imaginary diffuseness is reduced to ai ≈ 1.1 fm
- The reduced radius for the imaginary WS potential was fixed at R0i = 7.856 fm
- For the 6He+27Al system, the SPP-based OM with renormalization factors NR=1.0 and NI=0.78 reproduces the data well at Elab = 9.5, 11, 12, and 13.4 MeV
- The CDP potential has negligible effect on the 6He+27Al elastic scattering angular distributions

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological OM analyses by Sanchez-Benitez et al. (2008) and Kakuee et al. (2006), who found that large imaginary diffuseness parameters (ai ≈ 2 fm) were needed to fit 6He+208Pb data, interpreted as evidence of long-range reaction mechanisms. The present study contrasts this with the 6Li+208Pb system, where such long-range behavior is not observed, and with the 6He+27Al system, which is well described by standard SPP potentials. The inclusion of the CDP potential partially accounts for the long-range absorption, but the remaining need for diffuse WS components suggests additional reaction mechanisms beyond dipole Coulomb breakup. The four-body CDCC calculations provide a microscopic foundation for the long-range attractive and absorptive tails found in the phenomenological potentials.
