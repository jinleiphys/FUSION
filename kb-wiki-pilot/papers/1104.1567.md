# Effective radii of deuteron induced reactions

> The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for exclusive reactions and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) as an extension of CDCC to inclusive reactions are applied to deuteron induced reactions. The CDCC result reproduces experimental data on the reaction cross section for $d+^{58}$Ni scattering at 200 MeV/nucleon and ERT does data on the neutron-stripping cross section for inclusive $^7$Li$(d,n)$ reaction at 40 MeV. For deuteron induced reactions at 200 MeV/nucleon, target-dependence of the reaction, elastic-breakup, nucleon-stripping, nucleon-removal, complete- and incomplete-fusion cross sections is clearly explained by simple formulae. Accuracy of the Glauber model is also investigated.

## Key claim

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) reproduces experimental data on the reaction cross section for \(d+^{58}\)Ni scattering at 200 MeV/nucleon, and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) reproduces data on the neutron-stripping cross section for the inclusive \(^7\)Li\((d,n)\) reaction at 40 MeV; however, the Glauber model shows large deviations from full-CDCC results for elastic scattering at angles \(\theta \ga 10^\circ\), indicating it does not work well in that regime.

## Method

The authors applied CDCC for exclusive reactions and ERT (an extension of CDCC to inclusive reactions) to deuteron induced reactions, using a \(p+n+\)T three-body model. The Koning-Delaroche global optical potential was used for the proton and neutron optical potentials, and the Ohmura potential was used for the deuteron intrinsic interaction. Integrated cross sections for reaction, elastic-breakup, nucleon-stripping, nucleon-removal, complete-fusion, and incomplete-fusion processes were calculated and analyzed for target-mass-number dependence.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross section \(\sigma_{\rm R}\) for \(d+^{58}\)Ni at 200 MeV/nucleon: CDCC gives 1056 mb, experimental value is 1083 mb
- Elastic-breakup cross section \(\sigma_{\rm EB}\) for \(d+^{58}\)Ni at 200 MeV/nucleon: 69 mb (CDCC with Coulomb breakup, without spin-orbit)
- Total-fusion cross section \(\sigma_{\rm TF}\) for \(d+^{58}\)Ni at 200 MeV/nucleon: 997 mb (CDCC with Coulomb breakup, without spin-orbit)
- Proton-stripping cross section \(\sigma_{p:{\rm STR}}\) for inclusive \(^7\)Li\((d,n)\) at 40 MeV: ERT gives 253 mb, Glauber model gives 214 mb, experimental extraction gives \(244 \pm 34({\rm theor.}) \pm 37({\rm exp.})\) mb
- Effective radius formula for elastic-breakup: \(R_{\rm EB} = 0.33 + 1.46 A^{1/3}\) (fm) for heavier targets
- Effective radius formula for total-fusion: \(R_{\rm TF} = 0.18 + 1.41 A^{1/3}\) (fm) for heavier targets
- Non-eikonal correction for \(d+^9\)Be at 200 MeV/nucleon: less than 1% for \(\sigma_{\rm R}\), \(\sigma_{\rm EB}\), \(\sigma_{n:{\rm STR}}\), and \(\sigma_{p:{\rm STR}}\)
- Non-eikonal correction for \(d+^{208}\)Pb at 200 MeV/nucleon: 1.5% for \(\sigma_{\rm R}\), \(\sigma_{\rm EB}\), and \(\sigma_{n:{\rm STR}}\); 16% for \(\sigma_{p:{\rm STR}}\)

## Context

This work extends the application of CDCC and ERT to deuteron induced reactions, building on prior developments of CDCC for exclusive reactions (reviewed in Refs. [CDCC-review1, CDCC-review2]) and the recent extension to inclusive reactions via ERT (Ref. [ERT]). The analysis provides simple formulae for the target-mass-number dependence of various cross sections at 200 MeV/nucleon, enabling accurate evaluation of fusion and stripping processes relevant to the international fusion materials irradiation facility (IFMIF) and to spectroscopy of unstable nuclei using nucleon removal reactions. The accuracy of the Glauber model (Ref. [Glauber]) is investigated and found to be insufficient for elastic scattering at larger angles, particularly due to the eikonal approximation.
