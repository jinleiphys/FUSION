# The p(d,p)d and p(d,p)pn reactions as a tool for the study of the short range internal structure of the deuteron

> In recent time the deuteron structure at short distances is often treated from the point of view nonnucleonic degrees of freedom. In this paper the measurements of T-odd polarization observables using tensor polarized deuteron beam and polarized proton target or proton polarimeter are proposed to search the quark configurations inside the deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measurements of T-odd polarization observables (specifically tensor-vector spin correlations and polarization transfer coefficients) in the p(d,p)d and p(d,p)pn reactions can identify exotic six-quark (6q) configurations inside the deuteron, but only if these observables show large values at internal momenta above 600 MeV/c, since mechanisms additional to one-nucleon exchange could also contribute at lower momenta.

## Method

The author calculates the matrix elements for deuteron breakup in collinear geometry (d+p → p(0°) + p(180°) + n) and deuteron-proton backward elastic scattering (d+p → p+d) using hybrid deuteron wave functions that include a complex 6q admixture added to the standard S and D components from Paris, Reid Soft Core (RSC), and Bonn C nucleon-nucleon potentials. The T-odd observables C_{0,SL,N,0} (polarization transfer from tensor polarized deuteron to proton) and C_{N,SL,0,0} (tensor-vector spin correlation) are derived from these matrix elements using the general spin observable formalism with Pauli matrices for protons and spin operators for the spin-1 deuteron.

## Key numbers

* Probability of 6q admixture: 3-4% from fits to experimental data on momentum density, T_{20}, and κ₀
* Relative phase χ between 6q and S components: ~40° for RSC, ~47° for Paris, ~55° for Bonn C DWFs
* Radius of 6q configuration: r ~ 0.6 fm for all used DWFs
* C_{0,SL,N,0} predicted to reach approximately -1 at internal momentum k ~ 600 MeV/c
* C_{N,SL,0,0} in dp backward elastic scattering predicted to reach minima of approximately -0.7 to -0.8 at k ~ 400 MeV/c
* For the s⁴p² configuration model, the D wave probability from 6q is about 0.5%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron structure at short distances where nonnucleonic degrees of freedom are invoked to explain experimental data that cannot be reproduced by conventional nucleon-nucleon potentials. The paper builds on earlier measurements of tensor analyzing power T₂₀ in inclusive deuteron breakup at Saclay and Dubna, and polarization transfer coefficient κ₀, which show strong deviations from impulse approximation predictions at k ≥ 0.2 GeV/c. The proposed T-odd observables are specifically designed to distinguish between the hybrid model of Kobushkin and Vizireva (1982) and the model of Lykasov and Efremov that includes NN*, NNπ, and higher Fock space components, enabling experimental tests of quark degrees of freedom in the deuteron at facilities such as COSY (ANKE) and JINR Dubna.
