# On the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron II

> The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron is applied to the calculation of the cross sections of the low-energy radiative neutron-proton capture n + p -> D + gamma and the low-energy two-proton fusion p + p -> D + e^+ + nu_e. For the low-energy radiative neutron-proton capture n + p -> D + gamma our result agrees well with both the experimental data and the potential model prediction. In the case of the two-proton fusion the cross section obtained is 2.9 times as much as that given by the potential approach. The obtained result is discussed in connection with the solar neutrino problem. ERRATUM: We correct the value of the cross section for pp-fusion p + p -> D + e^+ + nu_e. We find a contribution to the astrophysical factor δS_pp(0) = 2.01 x 10^{-25} (1 +/- 0.30) MeV b which is obtained only due to weak and strong low-energy interactions of the protons and the deuteron when neglecting the Coulomb repulsion between protons in the intermediate state. Minor misprints are corrected.

## Key claim

The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron, when applied to low-energy two-proton fusion (p + p → D + e⁺ + νₑ), yields a cross section 2.9 times larger than that predicted by the potential model approach, but this result is obtained only when neglecting Coulomb repulsion between protons in the intermediate state, as clarified in the erratum.

## Method

The authors revise their earlier relativistic field theory model of the deuteron, treating it as a bound state of proton and neutron via a σ-model approach with one-nucleon loop approximation and a cutoff Λ_D = 1/r_D = 45.688 MeV. They compute one-nucleon loop diagrams for the radiative neutron-proton capture n + p → D + γ and the weak two-proton fusion p + p → D + e⁺ + νₑ, fixing ambiguities from virtual momentum shifts by requiring electromagnetic gauge invariance for individual loops and gauge invariance under transformations of the deuteron field.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the deuteron: ε_D = 2.273 MeV (best fit using g_T/g_V = −√(3/8)), compared to experimental value 2.225 MeV (2% accuracy)
- Coupling constant g_V = 11.3, fixed from experimental electric quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.286 fm² and M_D ≈ 1876 MeV
- Cutoff scale: Λ_D = 45.688 MeV, derived from effective radius r_D = 4.319 fm
- Erratum: astrophysical factor contribution δS_pp(0) = 2.01 × 10⁻²⁵ (1 ± 0.30) MeV b for pp-fusion, obtained only when neglecting Coulomb repulsion between protons in the intermediate state

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (Ivanov et al., Ref. [1]) by revising the treatment of one-nucleon loop ambiguities and including anomalous magnetic moments of the proton and neutron as well as the Aronson interaction. The result for radiative neutron-proton capture agrees with both experimental data and potential model predictions, while the two-proton fusion cross section exceeds the potential approach result by a factor of 2.9, which is discussed in connection with the solar neutrino problem. The model is analogous to the Nambu–Jona–Lasinio approach but applied to the deuteron as a weakly bound, extended object rather than a strongly bound localized state.
