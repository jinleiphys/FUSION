# Numerical Assessment of Convergence in the Post Form Ichimura-Austern-Vincent model

> The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model provides a powerful theoretical framework for describing inclusive breakup reactions. However, its post-form representation presents significant numerical challenges due to the absence of a natural cutoff in the transition matrix integration. This work presents a systematic assessment of convergence methods for post-form IAV calculations, comparing the bin method and the Vincent-Fortune approach. We demonstrate that while the bin method offers implementation simplicity, it exhibits strong parameter dependence that compromises numerical stability. In contrast, the Vincent-Fortune method, which employs complex contour integration, achieves reliable convergence without arbitrary parameters. We further introduce a novel hybrid approach that integrates finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) with the Vincent-Fortune technique, combining the accuracy of finite-range treatment at short distances with the numerical stability of zero-range approximations in the asymptotic region. Numerical results for deuteron and $^6$Li-induced reactions confirm the efficacy of this hybrid method, showing consistent agreement with experimental data while eliminating the convergence issues that plague traditional approaches. This advancement enables more reliable calculations of nonelastic breakup cross sections and facilitates the extension of the IAV formalism beyond DWBA to incorporate continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) wave functions for a more comprehensive treatment of breakup processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the bin method for post-form Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) calculations offers implementation simplicity, it exhibits strong parameter dependence on bin size and matching radius that compromises numerical stability; in contrast, the Vincent-Fortune method using complex contour integration achieves reliable convergence without arbitrary parameters, though it is limited to zero-range approximations with finite-range corrections.

## Method

The authors systematically compared the bin method and the Vincent-Fortune approach for computing nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross sections within the post-form IAV model. They introduced a novel hybrid method that splits the computation of the relative wave function into two regions: an inner region (0 to R) using finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) source terms, and an outer region (R to infinity) using zero-range source terms with complex contour integration following the Vincent-Fortune technique. Numerical calculations were performed for deuteron-induced reactions on $^{62}$Ni at 25.5 MeV and $^6$Li-induced reactions, comparing results across different bin sizes and matching radii.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{62}$Ni($d$, $pX$) reaction at $E_d = 25.5$ MeV, bin sizes of $\Delta k = 0.08$, 0.06, 0.04, and 0.02 fm$^{-1}$ were tested, with matching radii set at nodes of the outgoing proton wave function (80, 105, 155, and 310 fm respectively)
- The bin method required $R_{max}$ values up to 300 fm even for the largest bin size ($\Delta k = 0.08$ fm$^{-1}$) to approach wave function vanishing
- Results for $\Delta k = 0.04$ fm$^{-1}$ and $\Delta k = 0.02$ fm$^{-1}$ showed negligible differences, suggesting convergence at these bin sizes
- The difference between finite-range and zero-range source terms decreased exponentially with increasing radial distance, supporting the hybrid method's validity

## Context

This work extends the IAV model originally developed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV85), which provides closed-form expressions for inclusive breakup reactions but suffers from numerical convergence issues in its post-form representation due to the absence of a natural cutoff in transition matrix integration. The paper directly compares the bin method proposed by Thompson (2011) and the Vincent-Fortune complex contour integration method (Vincent-Fortune), while introducing a novel hybrid approach that combines finite-range DWBA with the Vincent-Fortune technique. This advancement enables more reliable calculations of nonelastic breakup cross sections and facilitates extension of the IAV formalism beyond DWBA to incorporate continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) wave functions, building on prior work by Jin and Moro (Jin19, Jin23).
