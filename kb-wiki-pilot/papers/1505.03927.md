# Momentum-space calculation of ${}^4$He triatomic system with realistic potential

> The system of three ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms with realistic interactions is studied in the momentum-space framework. It is demonstrated that short and long-range difficulties encountered in the coordinate-space calculations can be reliably resolved in the momentum-space calculations. Well-converged and accurate results are obtained for the ground and excited trimer energies, atom-dimer scattering length, phase shifts, inelasticity parameters, and elastic and breakup cross sections. A significant correction to previously published results is found for the elastic cross section at very low energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that momentum-space integral equations for the three-${}^4$He system with a realistic potential (LM2M2) can reliably resolve both the short-range hard-core difficulties and the long-range convergence problems encountered in coordinate-space calculations, but the abstract's claim of "significant correction to previously published results" is hedged: it applies specifically to the elastic cross section at very low energy, where the unusual energy and angular momentum dependence of partial cross sections predicted by Suno and Esry (2008) is not confirmed, while the total breakup cross section results are consistent.

## Method

The author solves the symmetrized Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) version of the Faddeev equations in momentum-space partial-wave basis, using the LM2M2 realistic two-atom potential for ${}^4$He. The coordinate-space potential is transformed to momentum-space partial-wave matrix elements via a Bessel transform, with a modified potential that introduces cut-off parameters $r_{\rm min}$ (for the hard-core region) and $r_{\rm max}$ (for the long-range tail) to study convergence. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the two-particle transition matrix and the AGS equations for three-particle transition operators are solved using real-axis integration with subtraction, special weights, spline interpolation, and Pade summation, including partial waves up to $L \le 12$.

## Key numbers

- Dimer binding energy $e_d = 1.3035$ mK (converged value)
- Trimer ground state binding energy $E_t = 126.40$ mK
- Trimer excited state binding energy $E_{t*} = 2.271$ mK
- Atom-dimer scattering length $a_0 = 115.21$ $\AA$
- Atom-dimer phase shifts and inelasticity parameters for $l=0$ through $l=5$ at kinetic energies from 0.1 mK to 40 mK (see Table 2 in the paper); for example, at $E_k = 40$ mK: $\delta_0 = 24.93^\circ$, $\eta_0 = 0.8283$, $\delta_1 = -73.78^\circ$, $\eta_1 = 0.7676$
- Convergence achieved with $r_{\rm min} \le 1.7$ $\AA$ and $r_{\rm max} \ge 75$ $\AA$; coordinate-space calculations required integration up to well above 1000 $\AA$ for comparable precision
- Internal kinetic energies: $\langle K_t \rangle = 1660.5$ mK for ground trimer, $\langle K_{t*} \rangle = 122.13$ mK for excited trimer

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space techniques previously successful in three-nucleon scattering calculations (Glöckle et al., 1996; Deltuva et al., 2003) to the ${}^4$He triatomic system with realistic potentials, which had predominantly been studied using coordinate-space Faddeev equations (Kolganova et al., 2009; Roudnev, 2003; Lazauskas and Carbonell, 2006), adiabatic hyperspherical approach (Suno and Esry, 2008), or variational Gaussian expansion (Hiyama and Kamimura, 2012). The results provide high-precision benchmark data for trimer energies, atom-dimer scattering lengths, phase shifts, and cross sections, and enable future calculations of exclusive breakup observables that are difficult to obtain in coordinate-space frameworks, as well as comparisons with nucleon-deuteron breakup dynamics.
