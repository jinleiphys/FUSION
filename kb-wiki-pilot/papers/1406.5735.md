# Low-energy R-matrix fits for the 6Li(d,a)4He S factor

> Background: The information about the 6Li(d,a)4He reaction rates of the astrophysical interest can be obtained by extrapolating direct data to the lower energies, or by indirect methods. The indirect Trojan Horse method, as well as various R-matrix and polynomial fits to direct data, estimate the electron screening energies much larger than the adiabatic limit. Calculations that include the subthreshold resonance estimate smaller screening energies. Purpose: Obtain the 6Li(d,a)4He reaction R-matrix parameters and the astrophysical S factor for the energies relevant to the stellar plasmas by fitting the R-matrix formulas for the subthreshold resonances to the S factor data above 60 keV. Methods: The bare S factor is calculated using the single and the two-level R-matrix formulas for the closest to the threshold 0+ and 2+ subthreshold states at 22.2, 20.2 and 20.1 MeV. The electron screening potential Ue is then obtained by fitting it as a single parameter to the low energy data. Results: The low energy S factor is dominated by the 2+ subthreshold resonance at 22.2 MeV. The influence of the other two subthreshold states is small. R-matrix fits result in the electron screening that is smaller than the adiabatic value. Neglecting the electron screening above 60 keV reduces the electron screening potential significantly. Calculations show a large ambiguity associated with a choice of the initial channel radius. Conclusions: The R matrix fits do not show a significantly larger Ue than predicted by the atomic physics models. The R-matrix best fit produces Ue=149.5 eV and Sb(0)=21.7 MeV b.

# Low-energy R-matrix fits for the 6Li(d,α)4He S factor

## Key claim

The R-matrix fits to the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction do not show a significantly larger electron screening potential Ue than predicted by atomic physics models, contrary to earlier claims of enhanced screening. The best fit produces Ue = 149.5 eV, which is below the adiabatic limit of 186 eV, but this result depends strongly on whether the screening potential is fitted simultaneously with other parameters or separately after neglecting screening above 60 keV.

## Method

Single-level, two-level, and three-level R-matrix formulas were fitted to direct experimental S-factor data above 60 keV for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction, using the s-wave approximation for the incoming deuteron and considering the three subthreshold resonances closest to the 8Be threshold: the 2+ state at 22.2 MeV, the 0+ state at 20.2 MeV, and the 2+ state at 20.1 MeV. The bare astrophysical S factor was calculated using modified R-matrix formulas with observed parameters rather than formal parameters, and the electron screening potential Ue was then obtained by fitting it as a single parameter to low-energy data below 1 MeV, or by fitting all parameters simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit electron screening potential (simultaneous fit): Ue = 149.5 +19.3 -30.5 eV
- Best-fit bare S factor at zero energy (simultaneous fit): Sb(0) = 21.7 +0.5 -0.1 MeV b
- Electron screening potential from separate fit (neglecting screening above 60 keV): Ue = 71.4 eV
- Adiabatic limit for electron screening: 186 eV
- Single-level fit (unrestricted): Ue = 162.0 +7.6 -4.6 eV, Sb(0) = 21.61 +0.07 -0.06 MeV b
- Single-level fit (fixed subthreshold energy at -80 keV): Ue = 126.7 +43.2 -2.8 eV, Sb(0) = 22.16 +0.03 -0.53 MeV b
- Alpha partial width for dominant 2+ state at 22.2 MeV (three-level simultaneous fit): Γα = 0.775 +0.009 -0.369 MeV
- Alpha partial width for 0+ state at 20.2 MeV (three-level simultaneous fit): Γα = 0.016 +0.493 -0.000 MeV
- Alpha partial width for 2+ state at 20.1 MeV (three-level simultaneous fit): Γα = 0.095 +0.001 -0.094 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous R-matrix analyses by Barker (2002) and Ruprecht et al. (2004) for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction, which had reported electron screening potentials larger than the adiabatic limit (340 eV from Trojan Horse experiments, and values from polynomial fits by Engstler et al. 1992). The present study includes three subthreshold resonances rather than one, and finds that the screening potential is smaller than the adiabatic limit of 186 eV. The results contradict the assumption of Ruprecht et al. that electron screening is negligible above 50 keV, and show that the alpha partial width for the dominant 2+ state (0.77 MeV) is significantly larger than the 0.11 MeV obtained by Page (2005) from multichannel fits. The work enables improved reaction rate calculations for stellar plasmas and demonstrates the strong sensitivity of extracted parameters to the choice of channel radii and fitting procedure.
