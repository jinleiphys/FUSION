# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering and Fusion Cross Sections for 6Li + 208Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies by using Folding Potential

> Based on the extended optical model approach in which the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts, simultaneous $χ^{2}$ analyses are performed for elastic scattering and fusion cross section data for the $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. A folding potential is used as the bare potential. It is found that the real part of the resultant DR part of the polarization potential is repulsive, which is consistent with the results from the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations and the normalization factors needed for the folding potentials. Further, it is found that both DR and fusion parts of the polarization potential satisfy separately the dispersion relation.

## Key claim
Using an extended optical model with a double folding potential as the bare potential, the authors simultaneously describe elastic scattering and fusion cross section data for the 6Li+208Pb system without needing the arbitrary normalization factor (N=0.5-0.6) required in conventional one-channel optical model analyses, and they show that the threshold anomaly appears in the fusion part of the polarization potential rather than in the total potential.

## Method
Simultaneous chi-squared analyses were performed for elastic scattering angular distributions and fusion cross section data at five near-Coulomb-barrier energies (Elab=29-39 MeV). The optical potential was decomposed into a double folding bare potential plus separate complex polarization potentials for direct reaction (DR) and fusion channels, with the DR potential taken as surface-derivative Woods-Saxon form and the fusion potential as volume Woods-Saxon form. Semi-experimental DR cross sections were generated by subtracting measured fusion cross sections from optical-model-derived total reaction cross sections, and the energy-dependent strength parameters were determined in a two-step fitting procedure that enforced the dispersion relation.

## Key numbers
- Threshold energies: E0,D(semi-exp)=20.5 MeV (c.m.) for direct reactions, E0,F(exp)=26.0 MeV (c.m.) for fusion
- At the strong absorption radius (Rsa=12.27-12.75 fm), the ratio R=Vtot/V0 ranges from 0.19 to 0.37, compared to 0.51 from CDCC calculations and 0.59 from conventional folding model normalization
- The real part of the DR potential is repulsive at all energies, with values at Rsa ranging from +0.318 MeV to +0.553 MeV
- Fusion cross sections range from 22 mb at Elab=29 MeV to 507 mb at Elab=39 MeV
- The inner imaginary potential WI contributes 14-25% of the total theoretical fusion cross section

## Context
This work extends previous extended optical model analyses by So et al. (2004) and Kim et al. (2002) that used shallow real potentials, now employing a double folding potential as the bare potential for the 6Li+208Pb system. It resolves two long-standing problems identified by Satchler and Love (1979) and Keeley et al. (1994): the need for an arbitrary normalization factor N=0.5-0.6 in conventional optical model analyses, and the absence of threshold anomaly in the total optical potential for loosely bound projectiles. The repulsive DR potential agrees qualitatively with CDCC calculations by Keeley and Rusek (1998), and the approach enables future analyses of other systems such as 7Li+208Pb.
