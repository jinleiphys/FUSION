# Nucleus-nucleus potentials in the scattering of tightly and weakly bound systems

> \begin{description} \item[Background] Fusion reactions play an important role in nucleosynthesis and in applications to society. Yet they remain challenging to model. \item[Purpose] In this work, we investigate the features of the nucleus-nucleus potentials that describe fusion cross sections and compare with those needed for realistic calculations of elastic scattering and other direct-reaction cross sections. \item[Method] We perform coupled-channel calculations for studying elastic and fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier with a tightly bound projectile ($^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm). We also perform Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel calculations to study elastic ($^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni) and fusion ($^{6}$Li+$^{198}$Pt) of loosely bound projectiles in the same energy regime. \item[Results] We contrast the coupled-channel results with those obtained in a single-channel solution with different assumptions for polarization potentials to shed light on the relevant absorption terms required for the two different reaction channels. \item[Conclusions] Our results suggest that different approximations may be required for modeling direct processes and for modeling fusion reactions. \end{description}

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the same nucleus-nucleus potential approximations that successfully describe elastic scattering and direct reactions can also be used to calculate fusion cross sections, and finds that different approximations may be required for modeling direct processes versus modeling fusion reactions.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel (CC) calculations for the tightly bound system 16O+144Sm and Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations for the weakly bound systems 8B+58Ni (elastic) and 6Li+198Pt (fusion), all at energies around the Coulomb barrier. They contrasted these full coupled-channel results with single-channel solutions using different assumptions for polarization potentials, specifically the Thompson-Nagarajan-Lilley weighted-mean local polarization potential implemented in the FRESCO code, to examine the absorption terms needed for direct reactions versus fusion.

## Key numbers

- The short-range fusion absorption potential uses Woods-Saxon parameters: depth W0 = 50 MeV, reduced radius rw = 1.0 fm, and diffuseness aw = 0.2 fm
- The classical approximation for fusion cross section is valid for energies E ≳ VB + 2 MeV, where VB is the Coulomb barrier height
- The grazing angular momentum lg is defined as the l-value for which the barrier of the total real potential Vl(R) equals the collision energy

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy-ion collisions around the Coulomb barrier, including systematic studies of elastic scattering using the Sao Paulo potential (SPP) and the Akyuz-Winther potential, as well as prior CDCC calculations for weakly bound projectiles such as 6,7Li. The paper builds on the polarization potential formalism of Feshbach and the Thompson-Nagarajan-Lilley approach, and addresses the long-standing question of whether optical potentials fitted to elastic scattering data can reliably predict fusion cross sections, which has implications for nucleosynthesis modeling and applications.
