# Simultaneous analysis of elastic scattering and transfer/breakup channels for the 6He+208Pb reaction at energies near the Coulomb barrier

> The elastic and alpha-production channels for the 6He+208Pb reaction are investigated at energies around the Coulomb barrier (E_{lab}=14, 16, 18, 22, and 27 MeV). The effect of the two-neutron transfer channels on the elastic scattering has been studied within the Coupled-Reaction-Channels (CRC) method. We find that the explicit inclusion of these channels allows a simultaneous description of the elastic data and the inclusive alpha cross sections at backward angles. Three-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations are found to reproduce the elastic data, but not the transfer/breakup data. The trivially-equivalent local polarization potential (TELP) derived from the CRC and CDCC calculations are found to explain the features found in previous phenomenological optical model calculations for this system.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that explicit inclusion of two-neutron transfer channels within the Coupled-Reaction-Channels (CRC) method can simultaneously describe both the elastic scattering data and the inclusive alpha-particle cross sections for the 6He+208Pb reaction, whereas three-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations reproduce the elastic data but fail to describe the transfer/breakup data.

## Method
The authors performed Coupled-Reaction-Channels (CRC) calculations using a di-neutron model for 6He, treating the dissociation of the projectile as a two-neutron transfer mechanism populating bound and unbound states of 210Pb. They compared these with Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations based on a direct breakup picture, using the same bare Sao Paulo potential (SPP) as the starting interaction for both methods. The trivially-equivalent local polarization potential (TELP) was extracted from both CRC and CDCC calculations to quantify the net effect of couplings on the elastic channel.

## Key numbers
- Energies studied: Elab = 14, 16, 18, 22, and 27 MeV (near and above the Coulomb barrier)
- For the di-neutron model: Woods-Saxon potential with radius R=1.9 fm and diffuseness a0=0.39 fm; effective two-neutron separation energy S2n=1.6 MeV
- Maximum excitation energy for CDCC continuum ranged from 5 MeV (at Elab=14 MeV) to 8 MeV (at Elab=27 MeV)
- CRC calculations required lf=0-8 partial waves for the 2n-208Pb relative motion to achieve convergence
- CDCC calculations required only li=0,1,2 partial waves for 2n-4He relative motion for convergence of elastic scattering
- For the bare interaction: imaginary Woods-Saxon potential with W=50 MeV, R0=1 fm, a0=0.1 fm to simulate fusion absorption
- Previous phenomenological optical model analyses required imaginary diffuseness parameter ai approximately 2 fm, much larger than the matter distribution diffuseness (approximately 0.6 fm)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the 6He+208Pb reaction where phenomenological optical model calculations required unusually large imaginary diffuseness parameters (Kak03, Kak06, San08) to explain the "long range absorption" effect. It directly addresses the failure of CDCC calculations (Esc07) to reproduce alpha-particle distributions, which were instead well described by DWBA calculations assuming two-neutron transfer. The CRC approach goes beyond the Born approximation by including back couplings from transfer to elastic channels, enabling a unified description without phenomenological fitting of the elastic cross section. This enables future studies of other reactions induced by weakly bound projectiles where similar long-range absorption effects are observed.
