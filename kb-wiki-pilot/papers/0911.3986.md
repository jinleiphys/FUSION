# The Onset of Nuclear Structure Effects in Near-Barrier Elastic Scattering of Weakly-Bound Nuclei: $^6$He and $^6$Li Compared

> The elastic scattering of the halo nucleus $^6$He from heavy targets at incident energies near the Coulomb barrier displays a marked deviation from the standard Fresnel-type diffraction behavior. This deviation is due to the strong Coulomb dipole breakup coupling produced by the Coulomb field of the heavy target, a specific feature of the nuclear structure of $^6$He. We have performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels calculations for the elastic scattering of $^{6}$He and $^6$Li from $^{58}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm, $^{181}$Ta and $^{208}$Pb targets in order to determine the range of $Z_{\mathrm T}$ where this nuclear-structure specific coupling effect becomes manifest. We find that the strong Coulomb dipole breakup coupling effect is only clearly experimentally distinguishable for targets of $Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$.

# The Onset of Nuclear Structure Effects in Near-Barrier Elastic Scattering of Weakly-Bound Nuclei: $^6$He and $^6$Li Compared

## Key claim
The strong Coulomb dipole breakup coupling effect that causes $^6$He elastic scattering to deviate from standard Fresnel-type diffraction is only clearly experimentally distinguishable from $^6$Li elastic scattering for targets with $Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$, and only when the incident energy is a few MeV above the Coulomb barrier. For lower target charges, the difference between $^6$He and $^6$Li elastic scattering is either absent or too small to be measurable with achievable experimental precision.

## Method
Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations were performed for the elastic scattering of $^6$He and $^6$Li from five targets ($^{58}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm, $^{181}$Ta, $^{208}$Pb) at two incident energies corresponding to $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV and $5.534$ MeV. The $^6$He projectile was modeled using a modified two-body di-neutron ($\alpha + ^2n$) cluster structure with the binding energy adjusted to 1.6 MeV, and the continuum was discretized into bins up to a maximum excitation energy of 7.7 MeV including $L=0,1,2,3$ partial waves and the 1.8 MeV $2^+$ resonant state. Calculations were performed using the code Fresco with single-folding interaction potentials.

## Key numbers
- The critical target charge for clearly distinguishable structure effects is $Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$ (corresponding to $^{208}$Pb with $Z_{\mathrm T} = 82$)
- For $^{58}$Ni ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 28$): calculated $^6$He and $^6$Li angular distributions are "absolutely identical"
- For $^{120}$Sn ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 50$): difference is "too small to be measurable" at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV; identical at $5.534$ MeV
- For $^{144}$Sm ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 62$): complete lack of Coulomb rainbow at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV, but "any reasonable measurement would still be unable to detect any difference" from $^6$Li
- For $^{181}$Ta ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 73$): difference at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV would be "barely detectable" with $\pm 1\%$ precision for $^6$Li and $\pm 2\%$ for $^6$He
- For $^{208}$Pb ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 82$): "clearly measurable difference" at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV; at $5.534$ MeV difference would be "just detectable" with $\pm 1\%$ ($^6$Li) and $\pm 2\%$ ($^6$He) precision
- Incident energies for $^{208}$Pb target: 22.38 MeV ($^6$He) and 33.06 MeV ($^6$Li) at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier observations that $^6$He elastic scattering from heavy targets like $^{197}$Au and $^{208}$Pb shows a marked deviation from Fresnel diffraction due to strong Coulomb dipole (E1) breakup coupling (Rusek et al. 2003, Moro et al. 2007), while $^6$Li shows a much weaker effect from quadrupole (E2) breakup coupling (Keeley et al. 1994). The study systematically maps the target charge dependence to guide future radioactive beam experiments, concluding that only very heavy targets ($Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$) at energies just above the Coulomb barrier can reveal projectile structure-specific coupling effects. The authors note that $^{181}$Ta and other elements in the $Z=70$ region are impractical due to low-lying excited states that prevent clean elastic scattering measurements. The findings enable planning of experiments for other weakly-bound nuclei like $^{11}$Li and $^{11}$Be that share the property of low-lying continuum dipole strength.
