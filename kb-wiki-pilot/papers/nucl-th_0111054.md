# Breakup of $^{9}$Be on $^{209}$Bi above and near the Coulomb barrier as a molecular single-particle effect: its influence on complete fusion and scattering

> The breakup of the $^{9}$Be projectile on the $^{209}$Bi target at bombarding energies above and near the Coulomb barrier is studied in the adiabatic two-center shell model approach. The effect of $^{9}$Be$% \to $n$+2α$ breakup channel on complete fusion, elastic and inelastic cross sections is investigated. Results show that the breakup of the projectile $^{9}$Be could be due to a molecular single-particle effect shortly before the colliding nuclei reach the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The breakup of \(^{9}\text{Be}\) on \(^{209}\text{Bi}\) above and near the Coulomb barrier could be due to a molecular single-particle effect, specifically, the diabatic motion of the last neutron of \(^{9}\text{Be}\) into the continuum or its transfer to \(^{209}\text{Bi}\), shortly before the colliding nuclei reach the Coulomb barrier, rather than being a purely Coulomb or nuclear breakup process.

## Method

The authors used the adiabatic two-center shell model (TCSM) to calculate neutron single-particle level diagrams for the \(^{9}\text{Be}+^{209}\text{Bi}\) system as a function of the relative distance between the spherical nuclei. Classical trajectories including radial and tangential friction were computed, and the Landau-Zener mechanism was applied at pseudo-crossings of adiabatic levels to obtain breakup probabilities. Complete fusion cross sections were then calculated using a Glas-Mosel model modified by a hindrance factor \((1 - \overline{P_{bup}})\), and elastic/inelastic scattering cross sections were derived from classical scattering formulas with a similar reduction factor.

## Key numbers

- The neutron separation energy of \(^{9}\text{Be}\) is \(S_n = 1.67\) MeV.
- The \(Q\)-value for the breakup channel \(^{9}\text{Be} \rightarrow n + 2\alpha\) is \(Q = -1.57\) MeV.
- The Coulomb barrier height for the system is approximately \(26\) MeV, with frequency \(\hbar\omega \approx 2\) MeV and barrier position \(r_B = 11.4\) fm (spherical case) or \(r_B \approx 11.5\) fm (deformed pole-to-pole configuration).
- The touching configuration distance is \(r_t = 9.82\) fm; the critical distance for compound nucleus formation from Galin's formula is \(r_{cr} = 8.01\) fm.
- At pseudo-crossings 1 and 2, the coupling matrix elements are \(|H'_{12}| = 0.1073\) MeV and \(0.1250\) MeV, respectively, with energy differences \(\frac{d}{dr}(\epsilon_1 - \epsilon_2)\) of \(0.0184\) MeV/fm and \(0.0352\) MeV/fm.
- The radial friction coefficient used is \(c_r^0 = 4 \times 10^{-23}\) MeV\(^{-1}\)s; the tangential friction coefficient is \(c_\varphi^0 = 0.01 \times 10^{-23}\) MeV\(^{-1}\)s.
- For the deformed case, the static deformations are \(\beta_1 = 1.2\) for \(^{9}\text{Be}\) and \(\beta_2 = 1.05\) for \(^{209}\text{Bi}\), reducing the barrier by \(\sim 1.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of breakup effects on fusion for weakly bound projectiles, particularly \(^{9,10,11}\text{Be}\) on heavy targets (e.g., \(^{209}\text{Bi}\) and \(^{208}\text{Pb}\)), where experiments by Yoshida, Signorini, Dasgupta, and others observed suppression of complete fusion above the Coulomb barrier. The authors contrast their molecular single-particle explanation with prior theoretical approaches using time-independent and time-dependent semiclassical coupled channels (e.g., works by TH2, TH4, TH1, Yabana) and coupled channels calculations for \(^{11}\text{Be}+^{208}\text{Pb}\) by Hagino, which predicted both suppression and enhancement depending on energy. The TCSM-based Landau-Zener treatment enables a microscopic description of the breakup mechanism via neutron level crossings, and the results suggest that the presence or absence of fusion suppression in reactions with \(^{9}\text{Be}\) depends on the target structure and the evolution of the last neutron's molecular orbital.
