# A=3 (e,e') $x_B \geq 1$ cross-section ratios and the isospin structure of short-range correlations

> We study the relation between measured high-$x_B$, high-$Q^2$, Helium-3 to Tritium, $(e,e')$ inclusive-scattering cross-section ratios and the relative abundance of high-momentum neutron-proton ($np$) and proton-proton ($pp$) short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs in three-body ($A=3$) nuclei. Analysis of this data using a simple pair-counting cross-section model suggested a much smaller $np/pp$ ratio than previously measured in heavier nuclei, questioning our understanding of $A=3$ nuclei and, by extension, all other nuclei. Here we examine this finding using spectral-function-based cross-section calculations, with both an \textit{ab initio} $A=3$ spectral function and effective Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) spectral functions using different nucleon-nucleon interaction models. The \textit{ab initio} calculation agrees with the data, showing good understanding of the structure of $A=3$ nuclei. An 8\% uncertainty on the simple pair-counting model, as implied by the difference between it and the \textit{ab initio} calculation, gives a factor of 5 uncertainty in the extracted $np/pp$ ratio. Thus we see no evidence for the claimed ``unexpected structure in the high-momentum wavefunction for hydrogen-3 and helium-3''.

## Key claim

The measured Helium-3 to Tritium inclusive (e,e') cross-section ratios agree with a full *ab initio* spectral-function calculation using the AV18 interaction, contradicting the claim of "unexpected structure in the high-momentum wavefunction for hydrogen-3 and helium-3." An 8% uncertainty in the simple pair-counting model, as implied by the difference between it and the *ab initio* calculation, gives a factor of 5 uncertainty in the extracted np/pp SRC pair ratio, eliminating the previously reported discrepancy.

## Method

The authors performed factorized cross-section calculations using an exact *ab initio* A=3 spectral function (AV18 interaction) and effective Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) spectral functions with different nucleon-nucleon interaction models (AV18, AV4', Norfolk, N2LO chiral potentials). They compared these calculations to measured high-Q^2 (1.9 GeV^2), high-x_B (1.4 to 1.7) inclusive (e,e') cross-section ratios of Tritium to Helium-3, and examined the fractional contributions from different initial nucleon momentum and separation energy states to assess SRC-pair dominance.

## Key numbers

- The *ab initio* AV18 spectral-function calculation differs from the simple pair-counting model by 8% for the same np/pp SRC ratio of 11.5
- An 8% model uncertainty leads to a factor of 5 uncertainty in the extracted np/pp SRC ratio, spanning from 2.5 to 12
- The AV18-based GCF cross-section ratio calculation is about 15% larger than the exact AV18 spectral-function calculation
- Chiral EFT potential GCF cross-section ratios agree with AV18 to about 5%, while their corresponding np/pp ratios vary by a factor of 2.5
- The tensor-less AV4' potential gives a cross-section ratio only 10-15% smaller than AV18, but an np/pp ratio four times smaller
- At 1.4 ≤ x_B ≤ 1.7, there are still significant (≥20%) contributions to the cross-section from high-momentum low-energy nuclear states, with a ~10% contribution from two-body breakup at the highest x_B

## Context

This work extends and contradicts the analysis of Li et al. (2022), who used a simple pair-counting model to extract an np/pp SRC pair ratio of 4.34^{+0.49}_{-0.40} from the same A=3 inclusive (e,e') data and interpreted the discrepancy as evidence for unexpected nuclear structure. The present calculation shows that the data are consistent with standard nuclear theory using *ab initio* spectral functions (Ciofi degli Atti and Kaptari, 2005) and the AV18 interaction (Wiringa et al., 1995), and that non-SRC-pair contributions (one-body and three-body states) limit the applicability of SRC-based approximations for inclusive cross-section analysis. This enables future work using varied experimental measurements (e.g., exclusive knockout) and state-of-the-art calculations with quantified uncertainties to determine the SRC isospin structure in light nuclei.
