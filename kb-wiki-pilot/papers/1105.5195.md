# Systematic description of 6Li(n, n')6Li* $\to$ d + $α$ reactions with the microscopic coupled-channels method

> We investigate $^6$Li($n$, $n'$)$^6$Li$^*$ $\to$ $d$ + $α$ reactions by using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with the complex Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. In this study, the $^6$Li nucleus is described as a $d$ + $α$ cluster model. The calculated elastic cross sections for incident energies between 7.47 and 24.0 MeV are good agreement with experimental data. Furthermore, we show the neutron spectra to $^6$Li breakup states measured at selected angular points and incident energies can be also reproduced systematically.

# Systematic description of 6Li(n, n')6Li* → d + α reactions with the microscopic coupled-channels method

## Key claim
The continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method with the complex Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction can systematically reproduce elastic cross sections, inelastic cross sections to the 3+ resonance state, and neutron breakup spectra for 6Li(n, n') reactions at low incident energies (7.47–24.0 MeV) using a single normalization parameter λ_w = 0.1 for the imaginary part of the interaction. However, the method underestimates experimental data in the low neutron energy region of the breakup spectra, which the authors attribute to four-body breakup contributions (n + n + p + α) not included in the calculation.

## Method
The authors describe 6Li as a d + α cluster model and apply the CDCC method to the n + 6Li scattering system, treating it as a three-body n + d + α problem. The diagonal and coupling potentials are constructed using a single-folding model with the complex JLM effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, which has energy and density dependence. The d-α continuum states are discretized using the pseudostate method with complex-range Gaussian basis functions, including ℓ = 0 (S-wave) and ℓ = 2 (D-wave) relative angular momenta for the d-α system, and the coupled-channels equations are solved to obtain elastic and breakup S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of 6Li relative to d-α breakup threshold: 1.47 MeV
- Incident neutron energies studied: 7.47 to 24.0 MeV for elastic scattering
- Normalization factor for imaginary part of JLM interaction at low energies: λ_w = 0.1
- 3+ resonance state energy: 0.71 MeV above the d-α threshold
- Normalization factor required at 150 MeV: λ_w = 1.5 (from total cross section analysis)
- Maximum applicable energy of JLM interaction: 160 MeV
- Experimental total cross section data available up to: 560 MeV

## Context
This work extends the CDCC method previously applied by Sakuragi et al. to 6Li scattering on various targets, and addresses the need for reliable theoretical calculations of n + 6Li reactions for fusion reactor applications, particularly for the FENDL-3 nuclear data library. The authors note that experimental data for 6Li continuum breakup processes are extremely rare above 20 MeV (citing Chiba et al., Hogue et al., and Hansen et al.), and that the statistical model used for medium-to-heavy nuclei cannot be applied to 6Li. The results enable systematic data evaluation for n + 6Li reactions within a single theoretical framework, though the authors identify the need for four-body CDCC calculations (as developed for 6He breakup reactions by Matsumoto et al.) to handle the (n, 2n) channel at higher energies.
