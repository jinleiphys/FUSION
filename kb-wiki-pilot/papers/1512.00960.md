# Systematic study of complete fusion suppression in reactions involving weakly bound nuclei at energies above the Coulomb barrier

> Complete fusion excitation functions of reactions involving breakup are studied by using the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model with breakup effects considered. An exponential function with two parameters is adopted to describe the prompt-breakup probability in the ECC model. These two parameters are fixed by fitting the measured prompt-breakup probability or the complete fusion cross sections. The suppression of complete fusion at energies above the Coulomb barrier is studied by comparing the data with the predictions from the ECC model without the breakup channel considered. The results show that the suppression of complete fusion are roughly independent of the target for the reactions involving the same projectile.

## Key claim

The suppression of complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier is roughly independent of the target for reactions involving the same weakly bound projectile, based on calculations using the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model that incorporates a two-parameter exponential function for the prompt-breakup probability.

## Method

The authors used the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model, which accounts for couplings to inelastic excitations and neutron transfer channels via a barrier distribution, and extended it to include breakup effects (ECCBU model). A prompt-breakup probability was introduced as an exponential function of the distance of closest approach, with two parameters (ν and μ) determined by fitting measured prompt-breakup probabilities or complete fusion cross sections. Complete fusion cross sections were calculated by multiplying the penetration probability by the survival probability (1 − P_BU), and the results were compared to experimental data for reactions induced by ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, ¹⁰B, and ¹¹B on various targets.

## Key numbers

- For reactions induced by ⁹Be, the slope parameter μ = −0.884 fm⁻¹ and the suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.68, independent of the target.
- For reactions induced by ⁶Li, the mean slope parameter μ̄ = −0.798 fm⁻¹ and the suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.60, independent of the target.
- For reactions induced by ⁷Li, the mean slope parameter μ̄ = −0.964 fm⁻¹ and the suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.67, independent of the target.
- For reactions induced by ¹⁰B, the mean slope parameter μ̄ = −1.415 fm⁻¹ and the suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.80, independent of the target.
- For reactions induced by ¹¹B, the mean slope parameter μ̄ = −1.900 fm⁻¹ and the suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.91, independent of the target.

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of complete fusion suppression using the universal fusion function (UFF) prescription with the Sao Paulo potential (Wang et al., 2014) and a three-dimensional classical dynamical model (Rafiei et al., 2010), which found target-independent suppression for ⁹Be-induced reactions. The present ECCBU model reproduces these findings and generalizes them to ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ¹⁰B, and ¹¹B projectiles, showing that the suppression factor correlates with the lowest breakup threshold energy of the projectile. The results enable future CDCC calculations that treat prompt and delayed breakup separately to further clarify the mechanism of target-independent suppression.
