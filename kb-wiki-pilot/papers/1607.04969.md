# Applicability of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method to the deuteron breakup at low energies

> We re-examine the deuteron elastic breakup cross sections on 12C and 10Be at low incident energies, for which a serious discrepancy between the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) and the Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas theory (FAGS) was pointed out. We show the closed-channels neglected in the preceding study affect significantly the breakup cross section calculated with CDCC, resulting in good agreement with the result of FAGS.

## Key claim

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) is applicable to deuteron elastic breakup on 12C and 10Be at low incident energies (12 and 21 MeV, respectively), provided that closed channels are included in the model space; the previously reported factor-of-three discrepancy between CDCC and the exact Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (FAGS) theory was mainly due to the neglect of these closed channels.

## Method

The authors performed CDCC calculations for deuteron elastic breakup on 12C at Ed=12 MeV and 56 MeV, and on 10Be at Ed=21 MeV, using a p+n+A three-body model with the one-range Gaussian p-n interaction and the CH89 global nucleon-nucleus optical potential. They discretized the p-n continua using the momentum-bin average method with bin size Δk=0.05 fm^-1 and maximum p-n momentum kmax=0.9 fm^-1 (12C at 12 MeV) or 1.1 fm^-1 (other cases), and systematically compared results including both open and closed channels against results including only open channels, as well as against FAGS results from Ref. [Upa12].

## Key numbers

- For 12C(d,pn)12C at Ed=12 MeV: converged CDCC including closed channels agrees well with FAGS, resolving the previous factor-of-three overshoot from open-channel-only CDCC
- For 12C(d,pn)12C at Ed=56 MeV: closed channels are unimportant; the kmax giving convergence (0.9 fm^-1) is close to the open-channel threshold (1.05 fm^-1)
- For 10Be(d,pn)10Be at Ed=21 MeV: same qualitative behavior as the 12C case at 12 MeV, with closed channels essential for agreement with FAGS
- Convergence of CDCC results was confirmed to 98% accuracy by further increasing the model space
- Multipole expansion of the nucleon-nucleus optical potential was taken up to λ=16; multipoles for λ>8 had no effect on results

## Context

This work directly addresses the discrepancy reported by Upadhyay, Deltuva, and Nunes (2012, Phys. Rev. C 85, 054621) between CDCC and exact FAGS calculations for low-energy deuteron breakup. The authors show that the earlier CDCC calculations omitted closed channels, whose inclusion was previously shown to be important in one-dimensional scattering (Ahsan and Volya, 2010) and 11Be scattering (Druet and Descouvemont, 2012). The results validate CDCC as an effective three-body reaction model at low energies and enable future systematic studies of closed-channel effects in transfer reactions.
