# Sensitivity of one-neutron knockout to the nuclear structure of halo nuclei

> Background: Information about the structure of halo nuclei are often inferred from one-neutron knockout reactions. Typically the parallel-momentum distribution of the remaining core is measured after a high-energy collision of the exotic projectile with a light target. Purpose: We study how the structure of halo nuclei affects knockout observables considering an eikonal model of reaction. Method: To evaluate the sensitivity of both the diffractive and stripping parallel-momentum distributions to the structure of halo nuclei, we consider several descriptions of the projectile within a halo effective field theory. We consider the case of 11Be, the archetypical one-neutron halo nucleus, impinging on 12C at 68 MeV/nucleon, which are usual experimental conditions for such measurements. The low-energy constants of the description of 11Be are fitted to experimental data as well as to predictions of an ab initio nuclear-structure model. Results: One-neutron knockout reaction is confirmed to be purely peripheral, the parallel-momentum distribution of the remaining core is only sensitive to the asymptotics of the ground-state wavefunction and not to its norm. The presence of an excited state in the projectile spectrum reduces the amplitude of the breakup cross section; the corresponding probability flux is transferred to the inelastic-scattering channel. Although the presence of a resonance in the core-neutron continuum significantly affects the energy distribution, it has no impact on the parallel-momentum distribution. Conclusions: One-neutron knockout cross section can be used to infer information about the tail of the ground-state wavefunction, viz. its asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC). The independence of the parallel-momentum distribution on the continuum description makes the extraction of the ANC from this observable very reliable.

## Key claim

One-neutron knockout reactions are confirmed to be purely peripheral: the parallel-momentum distribution of the remaining core is sensitive only to the asymptotic tail of the ground-state wavefunction (its asymptotic normalization coefficient, or ANC) and not to its norm or short-range behavior, making ANC extraction from this observable very reliable.

## Method

The authors model the one-neutron halo nucleus ¹¹Be as a two-body system (¹⁰Be core plus neutron) using a halo effective field theory (Halo-EFT) potential truncated at next-to-leading order, with low-energy constants fitted to experimental binding energies and to ab initio predictions from Calci et al. They simulate the inclusive breakup (diffractive and stripping) of ¹¹Be on a ¹²C target at 68 MeV/nucleon within a Coulomb-corrected eikonal model, and systematically vary the projectile description to test sensitivity of the parallel-momentum distribution to the ground-state wavefunction, the presence of an excited bound state, and resonances in the continuum.

## Key numbers

- The ¹¹Be ground state (1/2⁺) has a binding energy of 0.504 MeV; the excited 1/2⁻ bound state has a binding energy of 0.184 MeV.
- The single-particle ANC for the 1s1/2 ground state is fitted to b₁ₛ₁/₂ = 0.786 fm⁻¹/² (from ab initio predictions); a variant with reduced spectroscopic factor (0.9) uses b₁ₛ₁/₂ = 0.829 fm⁻¹/².
- The presence of the 1/2⁻ excited state reduces the diffractive breakup parallel-momentum distribution peak amplitude by about 4.4%.
- The total diffractive breakup cross section with both bound states is 122.8 mb; without the p1/2 interaction (plane waves) it is 126.1 mb; the sum of breakup and inelastic cross sections is conserved at about 126.4 mb.
- A d5/2 resonance at 1.27 MeV with width 98 keV (matching the experimental 5/2⁺ state) changes the total breakup cross section by less than 1 mb relative to the plane-wave case (122.8 mb vs. 122.1–122.8 mb).

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Hansen (1996) and Bonaccorso & Hencken (2002) that stripping cross sections are proportional to the square of the single-particle ANC, confirming this result within a systematic Halo-EFT framework. It also builds on the analysis of Moro et al. (2019) showing flux conservation between breakup and inelastic channels in Coulomb breakup of ¹¹Be. The study enables reliable extraction of ANCs from inclusive knockout measurements, while cautioning that spectroscopic factors extracted from such data may be unreliable due to the insensitivity to the wavefunction norm.
