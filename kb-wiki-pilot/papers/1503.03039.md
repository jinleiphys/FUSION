# Tomography of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma by Charm Quarks

> We study charm production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions by using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The initial charm quarks are produced by the Pythia event generator tuned to fit the transverse momentum spectrum and rapidity distribution of charm quarks from Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) calculations. The produced charm quarks scatter in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with the off-shell partons whose masses and widths are given by the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) which reproduces the lattice QCD equation-of-state in thermal equilibrium. The relevant cross section are calculated in a consistent way by employing the effective propagators and couplings from the DQPM. Close to the critical energy density of the phase transition, the charm quarks are hadronized into $D$ mesons through coalescence and/or fragmentation depending on transverse momentum. The hadronized $D$ mesons then interact with the various hadrons in the hadronic phase with cross sections calculated in an effective lagrangian approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry. Finally, the nuclear modification factor $\rm R_{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ of $D^0$ mesons from PHSD are compared with the experimental data from the STAR Collaboration for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ =200 GeV. We find that in the PHSD the energy loss of $D$ mesons at high $p_T$ can be dominantly attributed to partonic scattering while the actual shape of $\rm R_{AA}$ versus $p_T$ reflects the heavy quark hadronization scenario, i.e. coalescence versus fragmentation. Also the hadronic rescattering is important for the $\rm R_{AA}$ at low $p_T$ and enhances the $D$-meson elliptic flow $v_2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy loss of D mesons at high transverse momentum (p_T) is dominantly attributed to partonic scattering in the quark-gluon plasma, while the shape of the nuclear modification factor R_AA versus p_T reflects the heavy-quark hadronization scenario (coalescence versus fragmentation), and hadronic rescattering is important for R_AA at low p_T and enhances the D-meson elliptic flow v_2. This conclusion is conditional on the specific implementation within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach using the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) for partonic interactions.

## Method

The authors used the PHSD transport approach, which incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom as strongly interacting quasiparticles with masses and widths from the DQPM that reproduces the lattice QCD equation-of-state. Initial charm quarks were produced using the Pythia event generator tuned to fit FONLL calculations, then scattered in the QGP with off-shell partons using cross sections calculated from effective DQPM propagators and couplings. Charm quarks were hadronized into D mesons through coalescence at low p_T and fragmentation at high p_T, with subsequent hadronic interactions described by an effective Lagrangian approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.5 GeV
- Critical temperature T_c = 0.158 GeV for the phase transition
- Critical energy density for phase transition: approximately 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Average number of elastic scatterings per charm or anticharm quark before hadronization: about 6 (from approximately 19 charm-anticharm pairs produced in 0-10% central collisions, with about 135 scatterings with light quarks/antiquarks and about 83 with gluons)
- Fragmentation parameter epsilon_Q = 0.01 for charm quark fragmentation
- Hadronization probabilities: D^0 (0.2), D^+ (0.174), D^{*0} (0.213), D^{*+} (0.224), D_s^+ (0.08), Lambda_c (0.094)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charm dynamics in heavy-ion collisions by using a consistent microscopic transport approach (PHSD) rather than simplified descriptions such as Fokker-Planck equations with QGP hydrodynamics or Boltzmann-type partonic cascades with massless light quarks. It differs from earlier approaches (including earlier HSD studies on charm dynamics) by using massive, strongly interacting quasiparticles for the QGP instead of massless pQCD partons, employing non-equilibrium off-shell microscopic transport instead of assuming local equilibration, and including D-meson rescattering in the hadronic phase via an up-to-date effective Lagrangian approach from Tolos et al. (2013). The results enable comparison with recent STAR Collaboration data on D^0 meson R_AA and v_2, and the spatial diffusion coefficient D_s shows agreement with lattice QCD calculations from the Bielefeld collaboration, with a smooth transition between hadronic and partonic phases near T_c.
