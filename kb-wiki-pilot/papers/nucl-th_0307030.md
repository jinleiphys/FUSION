# Role of virtual break-up of projectile in astrophysical fusion reactions

> We study the effect of virtual Coulomb break-up, commonly known as the dipole polarizability, of the deuteron projectile on the astrophysical fusion reaction 3He(d,p)4He. We use the adiabatic approximation to estimate the potential shift due to the E1 transition to the continuum states in the deuteron, and compute the barrier penetrability in the WKB approximation. We find that the enhancement of the penetrability due to the deuteron break-up is too small to resolve the longstanding puzzle observed in laboratory measurements that the electron screening effect is surprisingly larger than theoretical prediction based on an atomic physics model. The effect of the 3He break-up in the 3He(d,p)4He reaction, as well as the 7Li break-up in the 7Li(p,alpha)4He reaction is also discussed.

## Key claim

The enhancement of tunneling probability due to virtual Coulomb break-up (dipole polarizability) of the deuteron projectile in the 3He(d,p)4He reaction is too small (about 0.2% at 5.8 keV) to resolve the longstanding puzzle where laboratory measurements show electron screening effects surprisingly larger than theoretical predictions based on atomic physics models.

## Method

The authors used a three-body model with an adiabatic approximation to estimate the potential shift from E1 transitions to continuum states in the deuteron, diagonalizing a two-dimensional coupling matrix at each internuclear distance R. They computed the barrier penetrability using the WKB approximation for low energies, treating the E1 strength as exhausted by a single state at E_gamma = 8*epsilon/5 with the total strength given by the Bertulani-Baur-Hussein formula. For target break-up effects, they extended the calculation to a 5x5 coupling matrix including projectile break-up, target break-up, and mutual break-up channels.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor of penetrability for deuteron break-up in d+3He: 0.21% at E_c.m. = 5.8 keV
- Enhancement factor for 3He break-up in d+3He: much smaller than deuteron break-up effect
- Enhancement factor for 7Li break-up in p+7Li: 2.7 x 10^-3 %
- Total B(E1) upward strength for deuteron: 0.558 e^2 fm^2
- Deuteron break-up Q-value (binding energy): 2.22 MeV
- 3He binding energy from d+p threshold: 5.49 MeV
- Classical turning point for d+3He at E_c.m. = 10 keV: 288 fm
- Inner turning point R0 used: 4.3 fm

## Context

This work extends previous examinations of small corrections to astrophysical fusion reactions including vacuum polarization, relativity, bremsstrahlung, atomic polarization, radiation correction during tunneling, and zero-point fluctuations (Balantekin et al. 1997; Hagino and Balantekin 2002; Fiorentini et al. 2003). The virtual break-up effect had not been previously computed for these systems, though similar calculations using continuum-discretized-coupled-channels (CDCC) methods existed for transfer reactions at higher energies (Fernandes et al. 1999; Ogata et al. 2003). The results indicate that the break-up effect alone cannot resolve the electron screening puzzle, suggesting that the atomic physics based model needs re-examination with more careful treatment of few-body dynamics including electrons.
