# Recent development of CDCC

> This paper shows a brief review on CDCC and the microscopic reaction theory as a fundamental theory of CDCC. The Kerman-McManus-Thaler theory for nucleon-nucleus scattering is extended to nucleus-nucleus scattering. New development of four-body CDCC is presented. An accurate method of treating inclusive reactions is presented as an extension of CDCC and the Glauber model.

## Key claim

This review paper claims that the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method and its extensions provide an accurate theoretical framework for treating nuclear reactions involving unstable nuclei, but the accuracy is conditional on the projectile breakup being weak for the microscopic reaction theory and on the eikonal approximation being reliable for the eikonal reaction theory.

## Method

The authors extend the Kerman-McManus-Thaler multiple scattering theory from nucleon-nucleus scattering to nucleus-nucleus scattering, using the Brueckner g-matrix as the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction instead of the bare NN potential. For four-body CDCC, the three-body continuum states of the projectile are discretized by diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian with Gaussian basis functions and the complex-scaling method. The eikonal reaction theory combines CDCC with the Glauber model by separating the S-matrix into neutron and core parts, where the core part is solved with eikonal-CDCC and the neutron part is treated with the adiabatic approximation.

## Key numbers

- The deformation effect increases the reaction cross section for Ne isotopes by at most 5%
- The tail correction to the reaction cross section for 31Ne is 35 mb, corresponding to 2.5% of sigma_R
- The breakup correction for 31Ne is 10 mb, corresponding to 0.7% of sigma_R
- The dynamical deformation effect on the reaction cross section for 30Ne+12C at 240 MeV/nucleon is less than 0.1%
- The reorientation effect for 31Ne+12C at 240 MeV/nucleon is 0.2%
- For 31Ne scattering from 208Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon, the error from replacing the neutron potential operator is 0.2% for sigma_R, 1.9% for the breakup cross section, and 4.1% for the stripping cross section

## Context

This work extends the Kerman-McManus-Thaler formalism for nucleon-nucleus scattering to nucleus-nucleus scattering, providing a microscopic foundation for CDCC. The four-body CDCC with complex-scaling method improves upon previous four-body distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations for 6He scattering. The eikonal reaction theory bridges CDCC and the Glauber model, enabling accurate treatment of inclusive reactions including the stripping component that CDCC alone cannot evaluate. This enables analysis of halo nuclei such as 31Ne in the Island-of-inversion region, where experimental data from Takechi et al. and Nakamura et al. have suggested the heaviest halo nucleus currently known.
