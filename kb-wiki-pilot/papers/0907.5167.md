# Continuum-continuum coupling and polarization potentials for weakly bound system

> We investigate the influence of couplings among continuum states in collisions of weakly bound nuclei. For this purpose, we compare cross sections for complete fusion, breakup and elastic scattering evaluated by continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations, including and not including these couplings. In our study, we discuss this influence in terms of the polarization potentials that reproduce the elastic wave function of the coupled coupled channel method in single channel calculations. We find that the inclusion of couplings among the continuum states renders the real part of the polarization potential more repulsive, whereas it leads to weaker apsorption to the breakup channel. We show that the non-inclusion of continuum-continuum couplings in CDCC calculations may not lead to qualitative and quantitative wrong conclusions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of continuum-continuum couplings (CCC) in CDCC calculations renders the real part of the polarization potential repulsive and reduces its imaginary part, which suppresses complete fusion and non-capture breakup cross sections while enhancing elastic scattering at large angles; without CCC, the polarization potential is attractive with stronger absorption, leading to qualitatively and quantitatively wrong conclusions.

## Method

The authors performed continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations for weakly bound projectile systems, comparing results with and without continuum-continuum couplings. They extracted approximate angular-momentum-independent polarization potentials using the prescription of Thompson et al., averaging over radial wave functions and S-matrices from the CDCC calculations. Cross sections for complete fusion, non-capture breakup, and elastic scattering were evaluated for the 11Be + 208Pb and 8B + 58Ni systems at near-barrier energies.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be + 208Pb, inclusion of CCC leads to a reduction of the complete fusion cross section by about two orders of magnitude at near- and sub-barrier energies
- For 8B + 58Ni at E_c.m. = 20.8 MeV (E_lab = 23.77 MeV), CCC inclusion strongly enhances the elastic scattering cross section compared to calculations without CCC
- The imaginary part of the polarization potential is always negative (representing flux loss to inelastic and breakup channels), but becomes weaker when CCC is included
- Without CCC, the real part of the polarization potential is attractive (reducing the fusion barrier); with CCC, it becomes repulsive (increasing the fusion barrier)

## Context

This work extends the earlier CDCC calculations of Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002) for 11Be + 208Pb and the breakup studies of Nunes and Thompson (1999), confirming that CCC is essential for properly representing breakup channels. The results are in complete qualitative agreement with the dynamic polarization potential analysis of Sakuragi et al. (1983) for 6Li + 28Si and 6Li + 40Ca systems. The findings demonstrate that a discretized continuum can only faithfully represent a breakup channel if continuum-continuum couplings are fully accounted for, and they enable future quantum transport theory approaches to incorporate these doorway and irreversibility effects.
