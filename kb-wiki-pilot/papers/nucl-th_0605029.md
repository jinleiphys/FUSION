# Near-barrier Fusion Induced by Stable Weakly Bound and Exotic Halo Light Nuclei

> The effect of breakup is investigated for the medium weight $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co system in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier. The strong coupling of breakup/transfer channels to fusion is discussed within a comparison of predictions of the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels model which is also applied to $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co a reaction induced by the borromean halo nucleus $^{6}$He.

## Key claim

The Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) model can provide a consistent description of elastic scattering, total fusion, and breakup for reactions induced by stable weakly bound nuclei like $^{6}$Li on medium-mass targets, but its application to the halo nucleus $^{6}$He using a dineutron cluster model is preliminary and the role of neutron-transfer channels remains to be quantified.

## Method

CDCC calculations were performed for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co using a two-body cluster description ($^{6}$Li $\rightarrow$ $\alpha$+$d$, $^{7}$Li $\rightarrow$ $\alpha$+$t$) with short-range imaginary fusion potentials for each fragment and full continuum couplings up to 8 MeV. For $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co, a dineutron model ($^{6}$He = $^{4}$He+$^{2}$n) was adopted with couplings to resonant (2$^{+}$, E$_{ex}$ = 0.826 MeV) and non-resonant continuum states up to f-waves, using the general coupled channels code FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- Total calculated breakup cross sections for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co were obtained by integrating continuum contributions up to 8 MeV and found to be rather small compared to total fusion cross sections
- The $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co CDCC calculations (both with and without breakup couplings) give much larger total fusion cross sections compared to $^{4}$He+$^{59}$Co experimental data from McMahan and Alexander (1980)
- Inclusion of couplings to breakup channels notably increases the total fusion cross section for $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co for all energies
- A small enhancement of total fusion cross section was observed for the more weakly bound $^{6}$Li compared to $^{7}$Li at sub-barrier energies, with similar cross sections for both reactions at and above the Coulomb barrier

## Context

This work extends previous CDCC studies of $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co reactions (Beck et al. 2003, Diaz-Torres et al. 2003) and compares with optical-model analyses by Souza et al. (2004). The preliminary $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co results are consistent with data published for other medium-mass targets such as $^{64}$Zn (Di Pietro et al. 2004) and $^{63,65}$Cu (Navin et al. 2004). The paper highlights the need for four-body CDCC models (under development by Matsumoto et al. 2004 and Thompson et al. 2005) for an accurate $\alpha$-n-n description of $^{6}$He, and for high-intensity radioactive ion beams to measure deep sub-barrier energies and transfer channel yields.
