# Relativistic Effects in Exclusive pd Breakup Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> The relativistic Faddeev equation for three-nucleon scattering is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance is achieved by constructing a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space. The exclusive breakup reaction at 508 MeV is calculated based on a Malfliet-Tjon type of two-body interaction and the cross sections are compared to measured cross sections at this energy. We find that the magnitude of the relativistic effects can be quite large and depends on the configurations considered. In spite of the simple nature of the model interaction, the experimental cross sections are in surprisingly good agreement with the predictions of the relativistic calculations. We also find that although for specific configurations the multiple scattering series converges rapidly, this is in general not the case.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in exclusive pd breakup at 508 MeV can be quite large and depend strongly on the specific kinematic configuration, but despite using only a simple Malfliet-Tjon type two-body interaction (rather than high-precision NN forces), the relativistic calculations show surprisingly good agreement with experimental cross sections.

## Method

The relativistic Faddeev equation for three-nucleon scattering was formulated in momentum space and solved directly using vector variables without partial wave decomposition, with Poincaré invariance achieved by constructing a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space. The two-body interaction was a superposition of attractive and repulsive Yukawa potentials (Malfliet-Tjon type) made phase-shift equivalent between relativistic and nonrelativistic calculations by adding the interaction to the square of the mass operator. The five-fold laboratory differential cross section for the exclusive breakup reaction 2H(p,2p)n was computed and compared to data from the Saturne-2 experiment at 508 MeV projectile kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- Projectile kinetic energy: 508 MeV (laboratory frame)
- For symmetric angle pairs theta1,2 = 38.1 deg and 41.5 deg: relativistic cross sections are considerably larger than nonrelativistic ones
- For symmetric angle pairs around theta1,2 = 44 deg and larger: relativistic cross sections fall below nonrelativistic ones
- For the quasi-free scattering condition at 41.5 deg - 41.4 deg: first order in the multiple scattering series nearly coincides with the full calculation
- For the coplanar star configuration at x_q = 1: first order result is about two orders of magnitude higher than the fully converged result
- For the space star configuration (phi_pq = 90 deg): first order does not contribute, and convergence requires orders higher than four
- Calculations converge well up to 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic momentum-space Faddeev equation techniques developed by Elster, Thomas, Gloeckle and collaborators (1998-2005) into the relativistic domain, and builds upon the first-order relativistic treatment of Lin, Elster, Polyzou, and Gloeckle (2007). It contrasts with the partial-wave-based relativistic Faddeev solutions of Witala, Golak, and Skibinski (2005-2006) which used realistic spin-dependent interactions below pion-production threshold. The results enable future calculations with realistic NN interactions (AV18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen) in the intermediate energy regime, and demonstrate that the multiple scattering series does not converge rapidly in general at these energies, requiring the full solution of the Faddeev equation for reliable predictions.
