# Approximations in Fusion and Breakup reactions induced by Radioactive Beams

> Some commonly used approximations for complete fusion and breakup transmission coefficients in collisions of weakly bound projectiles at near barrier energies are assessed. We show that they strongly depend on the adopted classical trajectory and can be significantly improved with proper treatment of the incident and emergent currents in the WKB approximation.

## Key claim

The commonly used factorization of the complete fusion transmission coefficient into a tunneling factor times a survival probability is a reasonable approximation but becomes inaccurate near the Coulomb barrier; an improved WKB treatment of the incident and emergent currents yields accurate fusion cross sections across all energies, while the breakup transmission coefficient requires inclusion of both the entrance and exit branches of the classical trajectory.

## Method

The authors test approximations for the complete fusion and breakup transmission coefficients by comparing them to exact quantum mechanical calculations for the \({}^{11}\)Li + \({}^{12}\)C system at near-barrier energies. They adopt a standard strong-absorption optical potential and a schematic polarization potential with equal real and imaginary strengths of 2.0 MeV, consistent with theoretical predictions. The old approximations factorize the fusion coefficient as a WKB tunneling factor times a survival probability evaluated along Rutherford or nuclear-inclusive classical trajectories; the improved method computes the attenuation integral directly from the complex WKB wavenumber without separating classically allowed and forbidden regions.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier for \({}^{11}\)Li + \({}^{12}\)C: \(V_B = 2.67\) MeV
- Optical potential parameters: \(V_0^{opt} = -60\) MeV, \(W_0^{opt} = 60\) MeV, \(r_r^0 = 1.25\) fm, \(r_i^0 = 1.00\) fm, \(a_r^0 = a_i^0 = 0.60\) fm
- Polarization potential strength: \(W_0^{pol} = V_0^{pol} = 2.0\) MeV
- Breakup threshold energy for \({}^{11}\)Li: \(B_{bu} = 0.2\) MeV, giving diffuseness \(\alpha = 6.6\) fm
- Fusion cross section converges for partial waves up to \(l = 10\); breakup cross section requires partial waves up to \(l \approx 80\)

## Context

This work extends the polarization potential approach of Hussein et al. (1992, 1993) and Canto et al. (1995, 1998) for reactions with weakly bound projectiles, where breakup channels strongly influence fusion. The authors show that the earlier factorization of the fusion transmission coefficient (used in Refs. [Ca98, Hu92, Hu93, Ta93, Ca95]) fails near the Coulomb barrier, and they provide an improved WKB method that reproduces exact quantum results. The breakup cross section treatment corrects the omission of the exit branch noted by Takigawa et al. (1993). The results enable more reliable calculations of complete fusion and breakup for radioactive beam reactions relevant to astrophysical processes and superheavy element searches.
