# Systematic study of breakup effects on complete fusion at energies above the Coulomb barrier

> A large number of complete fusion excitation functions of reactions including the breakup channel were measured in recent decades, especially in the last few years. It allows us to investigate the systematic behavior of the breakup effects on the complete fusion cross sections. To this end, we perform a systematic study of the breakup effects on the complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier. The reduced fusion functions F(x) are compared with the universal fusion functions which are used as a uniform standard reference. The complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier are suppressed by the breakup of projectiles. This suppression effect for reactions induced by the same projectile is independent of the target and mainly determined by the lowest energy breakup channel of the projectile. There holds a good exponential relation between the suppression factor and the energy corresponding to the lowest breakup threshold.

## Key claim

The suppression of complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier, caused by projectile breakup, is independent of the target nucleus for reactions induced by the same projectile and is primarily determined by the lowest-energy breakup channel of that projectile; a good exponential relation holds between the suppression factor and the energy corresponding to that lowest breakup threshold.

## Method

The authors collected complete fusion excitation functions for reactions induced by \({}^{6,7}\)Li, \({}^{9}\)Be, \({}^{10,11}\)B, \({}^{12,13}\)C, and \({}^{16}\)O projectiles on various targets. They reduced the cross sections and energies to dimensionless fusion functions \(F(x)\) and variables \(x\) using barrier parameters (radius \(R_{\rm B}\), height \(V_{\rm B}\), curvature \(\hbar\omega\)) derived from the double-folding Sao Paulo potential (SPP). These reduced data were compared to the universal fusion function (UFF) \(F_0(x) = \ln[1+\exp(2\pi x)]\), which serves as a standard reference representing fusion without breakup coupling; the suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.}\) was extracted by fitting the ratio \(F(x)/F_0(x)\) for \(x>0\).

## Key numbers

*   \({}^{6}\)Li: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.60\) (lowest breakup threshold \(E_{\rm B.U.} = 1.474\) MeV)
*   \({}^{7}\)Li: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.67\) (\(E_{\rm B.U.} = 2.467\) MeV)
*   \({}^{9}\)Be: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.68\) (\(E_{\rm B.U.} = 1.573\) MeV)
*   \({}^{10}\)B: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.80\) (\(E_{\rm B.U.} = 4.461\) MeV)
*   \({}^{11}\)B: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.91\) (\(E_{\rm B.U.} = 8.665\) MeV)
*   \({}^{12}\)C: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.88\) (\(E_{\rm B.U.} = 7.367\) MeV)
*   \({}^{13}\)C: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.94\) (\(E_{\rm B.U.} = 10.648\) MeV)
*   \({}^{16}\)O: suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.87\) (\(E_{\rm B.U.} = 7.162\) MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Gasques et al. (2009) and Dasgupta et al. (2010) that suggested CF suppression for \({}^{6,7}\)Li and \({}^{10}\)B is target-independent and correlated with breakup threshold energy, and by Gomes et al. (2011) who found no clear systematic behavior for \({}^{9}\)Be using the UFF formalism. The present systematic analysis covers a wider range of projectiles, including tightly bound ones (\({}^{11}\)B, \({}^{12,13}\)C, \({}^{16}\)O) where incomplete fusion evidence had recently been found, and establishes a quantitative exponential relation between the suppression factor and the lowest breakup threshold energy. This enables predictions of CF suppression for other weakly bound projectiles based solely on their breakup threshold.
