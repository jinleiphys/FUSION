# Systematics of direct-$α$ production with weakly and strongly bound projectiles

> The production of $α$-particles in reactions using both the strongly and weakly bound projectiles at energies around the Coulomb barrier show several interesting features. To understand these, the role of various reaction mechanisms responsible for $α$-production, such as non-capture breakup, capture of only one of the fragments subsequent to projectile breakup and their contribution to reaction cross sections have been investigated. A systematic study of the $α$-particle production based on available data for various projectile target systems have been performed and a classification based on projectile type is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that non-compound (direct) alpha-particle production cross sections exhibit a universal systematic behavior when plotted against reduced energy for each projectile class (strongly bound, stable weakly bound, and radioactive ion beams), but the abstract does not specify that this universality holds only for medium-to-heavy target nuclei and that for light targets the complete fusion contribution is larger, leading to lower non-CF alpha production values.

## Method

The authors compiled inclusive alpha-particle production data from the literature for many projectile-target systems, subtracted the compound-fusion (evaporation) contribution estimated using the statistical model code PACE2, and plotted the resulting non-compound alpha cross sections as a function of reduced energy (E_red = E_cm / [(Z_P Z_T)/(A_P^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3})]). They compared these systematics with measured reaction cross section minus complete fusion cross section (sigma_Reac - sigma_CF) values and with CDCC-absorption model calculations for d-capture and t-capture cross sections for weakly bound projectiles.

## Key numbers

* For strongly bound projectiles (SBP), the non-CF alpha production cross section is much smaller than sigma_Reac - sigma_CF, indicating other processes (inelastic, transfer, non-alpha-emitting ICF) contribute significantly.
* For ^6Li induced reactions, the non-CF alpha production cross section matches sigma_Reac - sigma_CF for the ^6Li+^209Bi system, showing that non-CF alpha production is the only important process apart from CF.
* For ^7Li induced reactions, sigma_Reac - sigma_CF for ^7Li+^209Bi is much larger than the non-CF alpha production cross section, indicating inelastic and other transfer processes contribute significantly.
* The energy where non-CF alpha production cross sections saturate is approximately 2V_B for SBP, but only about 1.2V_B for stable WBP and RIB.
* Non-capture breakup (NCBU) contributes approximately 10% to the non-CF alpha production cross section for both ^6Li and ^7Li projectiles with medium and heavy mass targets.

## Context

This work extends earlier systematic studies of alpha-particle production with weakly bound projectiles such as ^6,7Li, ^9Be, and ^6He on heavy targets (^208Pb, ^209Bi) reported by Kolata et al. (2001). It provides a comprehensive classification across three projectile types (SBP, stable WBP, RIB) and shows that for ^6Li the reaction cross section is fully accounted for by CF plus non-CF alpha production, while for ^7Li and SBP additional channels are needed. The CDCC-absorption model calculations for d-capture and t-capture (Parkar et al., 2018) are shown to describe the data well, enabling future predictions for radioactive ion beam reactions.
