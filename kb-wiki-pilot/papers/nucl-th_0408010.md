# Upper bounds for fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We obtain upper limits for the contributions of the incomplete fusion and sequential complete fusion processes to the total fusion cross section. Through those upper bounds we find that these processes are negligible in reactions induced by projectiles such as $^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li, which break up into neutrons and one fragment containing the full projectile charge.

## Key claim

The authors derive upper bounds for incomplete fusion (IF) and sequential complete fusion (SCF) cross sections in collisions of weakly bound nuclei, showing that these processes are negligible for projectiles like \(^{6}\)He and \(^{11}\)Li that break up into neutrons and a single charged fragment, but the bounds are less restrictive for projectiles that break into two charged fragments.

## Method

The authors employ a semiclassical approximation based on the Alder-Winther (AW) method, where the projectile-target relative motion is treated classically while the intrinsic dynamics of the projectile is treated quantum mechanically. The fusion probability for each channel is factorized as the product of the channel population at the point of closest approach and the tunneling probability through the effective potential barrier. Upper bounds for IF and SCF are obtained by setting the channel population probability to unity and using the appropriate tunneling factor for each breakup fragment, with the Akyuz-Winther parametrization for nuclear potentials and the ingoing wave boundary condition.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi system, the upper bound for IF induced by the \(^{3}\)H fragment exceeds the single barrier penetration model (BPM) cross section at low energies above the Coulomb barrier, while the \(^{4}\)He contribution is negligible.
- For the \(^{6}\)He + \(^{238}\)U system, the upper bounds for both IF and SCF are much smaller than the BPM estimate for complete fusion.
- The \(^{6}\)He breakup threshold energy is \(B = 0.975\) MeV.
- The \(^{4}\)He fragment from \(^{6}\)He breakup carries approximately 2/3 of the incident energy, and the \(^{4}\)He-\(^{238}\)U potential barrier is slightly higher than the entrance channel barrier.
- For the \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi system, experimental IF cross section is about 30% of the total fusion cross section.
- The SCF cross section for \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi becomes appreciable at \(E_{c.m.}/V_B \approx 1.5\).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Alder-Winther method previously applied to breakup and fusion calculations (refs. AW1, Hu04) to derive upper bounds for IF and SCF. It addresses the experimental difficulty in distinguishing complete fusion from IF in reactions with weakly bound projectiles, particularly when uncharged fragments are produced (refs. Da02, Si99, Si02, Ko98, Tr00, Al02, Re98). The results enable direct comparison of theoretical complete fusion predictions with measured total fusion cross sections for projectiles like \(^{6}\)He and \(^{11}\)Li, where IF contributions are shown to be negligible. The method is limited to energies above the Coulomb barrier, with an extended version including classically forbidden regions and fragment relative motion under development (ref. Longpaper).
