# A schematic model for the direct cross-section in reactions induced by exotic and stable projectiles

> A geometric model for the direct contribution of the reaction cross section induced by light ions on different targets is presented. The model separates the total reaction cross section into two components, one for total fusion and another for direct reactions. We show that the direct part scales as $2 πRa$, where $R$ is related to the nuclear radius and $a$ is the width of a ring, which is related to the nuclear diffuseness. A simple expression is presented to calculate the radius $R$ and the width parameter $a$ in terms of the masses and charges of the system. The method is applied to experimental data of exotic, weakly bound, and strongly bound projectiles on several targets. Different diffuseness parameters were obtained for different types of projectiles: exotic n-rich, stable weakly bound, stable strongly bound and exotic p-rich exotic projectiles.

# A schematic model for the direct cross-section in reactions induced by exotic and stable projectiles

## Key claim

The direct part of the total reaction cross section for light ions scales as $2\pi Ra$, where $R$ is related to the nuclear radius and $a$ is a ring width related to nuclear diffuseness, but the diffuseness parameter $a_n$ must be adjusted separately for different projectile types (exotic neutron-rich, stable weakly bound, stable strongly bound, and exotic proton-rich) to collapse the data onto a universal curve, and this scaling works well only for energies well above the Coulomb barrier ($x \ge 1.8$).

## Method

The total reaction cross section is separated into a fusion component and a direct component, where the direct part is modeled geometrically as a ring of radius $R_x$ and width $a$ around the fusion disk. The parameters $R_x$ and $a$ are calculated from the masses and charges of the system using a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential model that relates the Coulomb radius $R_b$ to the nuclear radius $R$, with $a = a_n \ln(X)$ where $X$ depends on $A/Z$ and $a_n$ is the nuclear diffuseness. Experimental direct cross section data from 146 systems (28 exotic n-rich, 71 weakly bound, 35 strongly bound, and 12 exotic p-rich) are reduced by dividing by $2\pi R_x a$ and compared to the universal curve $\sigma_{red} = 1 - 1/x$ where $x = E/V_b$.

## Key numbers

- Exotic neutron-rich projectiles: $\hat{a}_n^{n-rich} = 1.32(06)$ fm
- Stable weakly bound projectiles: $\hat{a}_n^{WB} = 0.66^{+0.16}_{-0.12}$ fm
- Stable strongly bound projectiles: $\hat{a}_n^{SB} = 0.68^{+0.16}_{-0.04}$ fm
- Exotic proton-rich projectiles: $a_n^{p-rich} = 0.55$ fm (no error estimation possible, only two points above $x \ge 1.8$)
- The standard nuclear diffuseness for stable ions is $a_n = 0.65$ fm
- The reduced nuclear radius used is $r_0 = 1.3$ fm
- The bi-dimensional reduced variance between raw and reduced cross sections decreases by a factor of approximately 5 in the $x > 1.8$ range

## Context

This work extends previous geometric reduction methods for total reaction cross sections (Canto et al., Gomes et al., Pires et al.) by explicitly separating the direct component and providing a simple analytic expression for its scaling. The model builds on the Woods-Saxon potential systematics of Ref. woods and the geometric scaling approach of Ref. scaling, and is applied to data from systems including $^6$He, $^8$He, $^{11}$Li, $^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^9$Be, $^8$B, $^{17}$F, $^4$He, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O on various targets. The results enable systematic comparison of direct reaction cross sections across different projectile types and show that the enhancement observed for neutron-rich projectiles can be parameterized by a larger diffuseness parameter, while the behavior below the Coulomb barrier ($x < 1.8$) remains unexplained and may require coupled channels effects or consideration of fusion contamination.
