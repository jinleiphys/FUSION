# Nuclear Astrophysics in Rare Isotope Facilities

> I discuss a few of the recent developments in nuclear reactions at very low energies with emphasis on the role of radioactive beam facilities.

## Key claim

Radioactive beam facilities have opened a new way to study nuclear reactions at very low energies relevant to stellar evolution, but the electron screening problem remains unresolved: laboratory measurements of screening enhancement disagree systematically by a factor of two with theoretical predictions, and this discrepancy persists even after including dynamical effects, vacuum polarization, and nuclear polarizabilities.

## Method

The paper reviews indirect methods for obtaining low-energy nuclear reaction cross sections using radioactive beams, including the Trojan Horse method (where the Fermi momentum of a particle inside a projectile compensates for the projectile velocity to induce reactions at vanishing relative energy), asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) extracted from peripheral transfer reactions, charge-exchange reactions to obtain Gamow-Teller matrix elements, and Coulomb breakup where the cross section for \(a+A \rightarrow b+x+A\) is related to the photodisintegration cross section and, via time reversal, to the radiative capture cross section \(b+x \rightarrow a+\gamma\). The author also discusses the electron screening problem in both stellar plasma (using the Debye-Huckel/Salpeter approach) and laboratory experiments with bound atomic electrons.

## Key numbers

- The reaction \(^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}\) has an S-factor \(S_{17}(0)\) value of 19.0 eV·b obtained from Coulomb breakup experiments, compatible with the value used in solar model calculations
- The \(^{12}\text{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\text{O}\) reaction cross section should be known to better than 20% for good modeling of stars, but this goal has not been achieved
- The \(^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}\) reaction S-factor was previously uncertain by as much as 30% according to Barker (1980)
- Plasma screening enhances reactions like \(^{3}\text{He}(^{3}\text{He},2p)^{4}\text{He}\) and \(^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}\) by as much as 20%
- The stopping power for proton+hydrogen collisions was calculated to be proportional to \(v^{\alpha}\) with \(\alpha=1.35\), while Andersen-Ziegler tables predict a larger value of \(\alpha\)
- For protons on helium targets at 4 keV, Golser and Semrad found \(S \sim v_p^{3.34}\)
- The most neutron-rich isotopes along the r-process path have lifetimes of \(10^{-2}\) to \(10^{-1}\) seconds

## Context

This review extends the work on indirect methods for nuclear astrophysics reactions, building on the Trojan Horse method proposed by Baur (1986), the ANC method proposed by Mukhamedzhanov and Timofeyuk (1990), and the Coulomb breakup method proposed by Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel (1986). The paper highlights the unresolved electron screening discrepancy that affects both laboratory measurements and stellar model inputs, and notes that this problem parallels difficulties in understanding color screening in quark-gluon plasma. The work enables future applications of radioactive beam facilities to measure reactions important for solar neutrino production, supernova dynamics, and r-process nucleosynthesis.
