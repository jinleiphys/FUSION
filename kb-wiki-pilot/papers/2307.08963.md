# Proton 0.01 MeV resonance width and low-energy $S$-factor of p+10B fusion

> {\bf{Purpose}:} In this paper, the proton resonance width of the near-threshold resonance is calculated using two different approaches. The values of the proton width are used to calculate the low-energy $S$-factor. \\ {\bf{Method:}} First, the proton resonance width is estimated using the mirror symmetry of the resonance $ {}^{11}{\rm C}(E_{x}=8.70\, {\rm MeV};\,\frac{5}{2}^{+})$ and the mirror bound state ${}^{11}{\rm B}(E_{x}=9.272\, {\rm MeV};\,\frac{5}{2}^{+})$. In the second approach, this width is estimated using the $R$-matrix definition of the observable resonance width, which is expressed in terms of the $p-{}^{10}{\rm B}$ resonant wave function calculated in the potential approach and the spectroscopic factor. \\ {\bf{Results}:} Depending on the method chosen, the calculated proton resonance width varies from $\,1.03 \times 10^{-19}\,$MeV to $\,2.96 \times 10^{-19}\,$MeV. The role of the near-threshold resonance is determined using fitting of two low-energy $S$-factors from direct measurements [C. Angulo {\it et al.}, Z. Phys. A {\bf 345}, 333 (1993)] and from the indirect Trojan horse method (THM) [ A. Cvetinović {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 97}, 065801 (2018)]. Within the framework of the $R$-matrix method using the determined proton resonance widths and the modified THM parameters for six low-lying resonances, the low-energy $S$-factors were calculated and compared with the corresponding experimental $S$-factors. The closest agreement with the data is achieved with the proton resonance widths $1.0 \times 10^{-19}$ MeV when fitting the $S$-factor from the THM indirect measurements, and $\,1.68 \times 10^{-19}\,$ MeV and $\,2.5 \times 10^{-19}\,$ MeV when fitting the $S$-factor from [Angulo {\it et al}, Z. Phys. A {\bf 345}, 333 (1993)].\

## Key claim

The proton resonance width of the near-threshold resonance in \(^{11}\mathrm{C}(E_x=8.70\,\mathrm{MeV}; \frac{5}{2}^{+})\) is calculated to be on the order of \(10^{-19}\) MeV, which is about an order of magnitude larger than the phenomenological value of \(2.3 \times 10^{-20}\) MeV deduced from the \(R\)-matrix fit to higher-energy direct measurements by Van de Kolk et al. (2022), but still allows excellent fits to low-energy \(S\)-factor data from both direct and indirect measurements.

## Method

The proton resonance width was calculated using two independent theoretical approaches: (1) mirror symmetry between the resonance state \(^{11}\mathrm{C}(E_x=8.70\,\mathrm{MeV}; \frac{5}{2}^{+})\) and the mirror bound state \(^{11}\mathrm{B}(E_x=9.272\,\mathrm{MeV}; \frac{5}{2}^{+})\), using the ratio of Wronskians of the resonant and bound-state wave functions; and (2) the \(R\)-matrix definition of the observable resonance width expressed in terms of the \(p-^{10}\mathrm{B}\) resonant wave function calculated in a Woods-Saxon potential and the spectroscopic factor. The resulting widths were then used in \(R\)-matrix calculations of the low-energy \(S\)-factor for the \(^{10}\mathrm{B}(p,\alpha)^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) reaction, fitting to experimental data from direct measurements by Angulo et al. (1993) and from the Trojan horse method (THM) by Cvetinović et al. (2018).

## Key numbers

- Proton resonance width from mirror symmetry (Eq. 3, using shell-model ANC \(C_n = 1.114\) fm\(^{-1/2}\)): \(1.87 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV for channel radius \(R_{ch} = 4.0\) and \(5.0\) fm
- Proton resonance width from mirror symmetry (Eq. 2, approximate, using same ANC): \(2.96 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV at \(R_{ch} = 4.6\) fm
- Proton resonance width from mirror symmetry (Eq. 3, using stripping-reaction ANC \(C_n = 0.83\) fm\(^{-1/2}\)): \(1.03 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV for \(R_{ch} = 4.0\) and \(5.0\) fm
- Proton resonance width from \(R\)-matrix approach (using spectroscopic factor \(S = 0.31\)): \(1.68 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV at \(R_{ch} = 4.0\) fm, and \(1.86 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV at \(R_{ch} = 5.0\) fm
- Best-fit proton resonance widths for the low-energy \(S\)-factor: \(1.0 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV when fitting THM data; \(1.68 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV and \(2.5 \times 10^{-19}\) MeV when fitting direct data from Angulo et al. (1993)
- \(\alpha\)-particle resonance width of the near-threshold resonance: \(0.015\) keV (from compilation by Kelley)
- Phenomenological proton resonance width from Van de Kolk et al. (2022): \(2.3 \times 10^{-20}\) MeV

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing uncertainty in the proton resonance width of the \(E_R = 0.01\) MeV near-threshold resonance in \(^{11}\mathrm{C}\), which dominates the ultra-low-energy cross-section of the \(^{10}\mathrm{B}(p,\alpha)^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) reaction. The calculated widths are about an order of magnitude larger than the phenomenological value extracted from the \(R\)-matrix fit by Van de Kolk et al. (2022) to higher-energy data (\(0.73-1.82\) MeV), but are two orders of magnitude smaller than the value \(2.2 \times 10^{-17}\) MeV from the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) calculation by Okolowicz et al. The results enable improved \(S\)-factor evaluations for nuclear astrophysics (boron destruction in stars) and for aneutronic fusion applications involving the \(^{11}\mathrm{B}(p,2\alpha)^{4}\mathrm{He}\) reaction, where the collateral \(^{10}\mathrm{B}(p,\alpha)^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) reaction produces radioactive \(^{7}\mathrm{Be}\).
