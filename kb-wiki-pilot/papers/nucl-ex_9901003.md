# Fusion versus Breakup: Observation of Large Fusion Suppression for ^9Be + ^{208}Pb

> Complete fusion excitation functions for $^{9}$Be + $^{208}$Pb have been measured to high precision at near barrier energies. The experimental fusion barrier distribution extracted from these data allows reliable prediction of the expected complete fusion cross-sections. However, the measured cross-sections are only 68% of those predicted. The large cross-sections observed for incomplete fusion products support the interpretation that this suppression of fusion is caused by $^{9}$Be breaking up into charged fragments before reaching the fusion barrier. Implications for the fusion of radioactive nuclei are discussed.

# Fusion versus Breakup: Observation of Large Fusion Suppression for ^9Be + ^{208}Pb

## Key claim

Complete fusion cross-sections for ^9Be + ^{208}Pb are suppressed to 68% of the values predicted by coupled channels calculations that correctly reproduce the measured fusion barrier distribution, demonstrating that breakup of the weakly-bound ^9Be projectile into charged fragments before reaching the fusion barrier removes flux that would otherwise lead to complete fusion. The suppression factor of 0.68 carries an uncertainty of ±0.07 arising from the uncertainty in the mean barrier energy, and at above-barrier energies there is no evidence for an energy dependence of the suppression factor, though a weak dependence cannot be excluded.

## Method

Complete fusion excitation functions were measured with high precision using pulsed ^9Be beams from the 14UD tandem accelerator at the Australian National University over the energy range 35.0–51.0 MeV, with targets of ^208PbS evaporated onto carbon foils. Heavy reaction products were stopped in aluminium catcher foils and identified by their distinctive alpha-energies and half-lives (270 ns to 138 days), with short-lived activity measured in-situ between beam bursts and long-lived products measured after irradiation. The experimental fusion barrier distribution was extracted from the complete fusion cross-sections by taking the second derivative of Eσ_fus with respect to energy, and coupled channels calculations including couplings to rotational states in ^9Be and octupole-phonon states in ^208Pb were performed to predict the expected fusion cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Measured complete fusion cross-sections are 68% of those predicted by coupled channels calculations (suppression factor 0.68 ± 0.07)
- Average barrier position from experimental barrier distribution: 38.3 ± 0.6 MeV (center-of-mass)
- Beam energy range: 35.0 – 51.0 MeV
- Target thickness: 340 – 400 μg/cm^2 ^208PbS on 15 μg/cm^2 C foils
- Catcher foil thickness: 360 μg/cm^2 aluminium
- Mean range of fusion evaporation residues at maximum bombarding energy: 130 μg/cm^2
- Energy step for barrier distribution extraction: 1.92 MeV (center-of-mass)
- ^9Be breakup Q-values: n + 2α (Q = -1.57 MeV); α + ^5He (Q = -2.47 MeV)
- ^11Be breakup Q-values: α + ^6He + n (Q = -7.91 MeV); α + α + 3n (Q = -8.89 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the quantitative understanding of fusion suppression due to projectile breakup, previously studied for reactions with lighter targets such as ^9Be + ^208Pb (Eck et al., 1983; Takahashi et al., 1997), by providing the first precise barrier distribution measurement that allows reliable prediction of expected fusion cross-sections. The results contradict the expectation that neutron-rich unstable nuclei like ^11Be would show greater fusion suppression than stable ^9Be, since ^9Be has more favourable Q-values for breakup into charged fragments (α + ^5He, n + 2α) compared with ^11Be. This finding has direct implications for interpreting fusion measurements with radioactive beams such as ^10,11Be + ^209Bi (Yoshida et al., 1996) and ^9,11Be + ^238U (Fekou-Youimbi et al., 1995), where ^9Be cannot be treated as a stable standard against which to judge breakup effects of radioactive isotopes.
