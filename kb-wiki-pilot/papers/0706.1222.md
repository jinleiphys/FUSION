# All orders proton breakup from exotic nuclei

> We present a semiclassical method to treat the proton breakup from a weakly bound state in an exotic nucleus. The Coulomb interactions between the proton, core and target are treated to all orders and including the full multipole expansion of the Coulomb potential. The nuclear proton-target interaction is also treated to all orders. The core-target interaction is included as an absorption. The method is semi-analytical thus allowing for a detailed understanding of the short range and long range effects of the interactions in the reaction dynamics. It explains also the origin of possible asymmetries in the core parallel momentum distributions when the full multipole expansion of the Coulomb potential is used. Calculations are compared to results of other, fully numerical, methods and to experimental data in order to establish the accuracy and reliability of the method.

# All orders proton breakup from exotic nuclei

## Key claim

The paper presents a semiclassical, semi-analytical method for treating proton breakup from weakly bound exotic nuclei that includes Coulomb interactions to all orders and with the full multipole expansion, but the method is a high-energy approximation and may require corrections (such as barrier penetration factors) when applied at lower beam energies (e.g., 65 A.MeV for 17F), where discrepancies of about an order of magnitude can arise compared to fully numerical methods.

## Method

The authors extend a previous all-orders formalism for neutron breakup to the proton case by writing the Coulomb phase as a sum of two terms: one describing core recoil and one describing direct proton-target Coulomb interaction. The differential cross section is expressed as an integral over core impact parameters of the squared sum of recoil, direct Coulomb, and nuclear breakup amplitudes, with the core-target interaction treated as an absorption via an eikonal S-matrix. The nuclear proton-target interaction is included to all orders via an eikonal phase, and the stripping contribution to nuclear breakup is added incoherently.

## Key numbers

- For 8B breakup on Pb at 936 A.MeV: inclusive cross section 652.4 mb (experiment: 662 ± 60 mb); exclusive cross section for excited state 57.8 mb (experiment: 56 ± 5 mb)
- For 8B breakup on C at 936 A.MeV: inclusive cross section 92.6 mb (experiment: 94 ± 9 mb); exclusive cross section for excited state 9.1 mb (experiment: 12 ± 3 mb)
- Exclusive/inclusive ratio for Pb target: theoretical 0.089, experimental 0.085 ± 0.021; for C target: theoretical 0.098, experimental 0.13 ± 0.03
- Spectroscopic factors used: ground state S_gs = 0.841, excited state S_ex = 0.101
- Barrier penetration factors w for 8B at proton-core energy 1.5 MeV: w_1 = 0.81; for 17F at same energy: w_2 = 0.39
- Sommerfeld parameters at 1.5 MeV: n_1 = 0.51 (8B), n_2 = 1.03 (17F)

## Context

This work extends the all-orders semiclassical method for neutron breakup developed by García-Camacho, Bonaccorso, and Brink (Nucl. Phys. A776, 2006) to the proton case, completing the theoretical framework for treating both neutron and proton knockout from halo nuclei. The method provides analytical insight into the origin of asymmetries in core parallel momentum distributions, confirming earlier numerical findings by Esbensen and Bertsch (Nucl. Phys. A600, 1996) that such asymmetries arise from higher multipole terms in the Coulomb potential and disappear in the dipole approximation. The formalism enables comparison with experimental data for 8B and 17F, which are important for understanding solar neutrino flux and explosive nucleosynthesis in X-ray bursts and novae.
