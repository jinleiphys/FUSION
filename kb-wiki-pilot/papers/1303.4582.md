# Complete Fusion of Weakly Bound Cluster-Type Nuclei at Near Barrier Energies

> We consider the influence of breakup channels on the complete fusion of weakly bound cluster-type systems in terms of dynamic polarization potentials. It is argued that the enhancement of the cross section at sub-barrier energies may be consistent with recent experimental observations that nucleon transfer, often leading to breakup, is dominant compared to direct breakup. The main trends of the experimental complete fusion cross sections are analyzed in the framework of the Dynamic Polarization Potential approach. The qualitative conclusions are supported by CDCC calculations including a sequential breakup channel, the one neutron stripping of $^7$Li followed by the breakup of $^6$Li.

## Key claim

The enhancement of complete fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies for weakly bound cluster-type nuclei may be consistent with the dominance of nucleon transfer (often leading to breakup) over direct breakup, as supported by CDCC calculations including a sequential breakup channel following one-neutron stripping of 7Li.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental complete fusion cross sections for 6Li and 7Li projectiles on a 209Bi target using the Dynamic Polarization Potential (DPP) approach, comparing the renormalized fusion functions to the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) derived from the Wong formula. They performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations that included a sequential breakup channel: one-neutron stripping of 7Li followed by breakup of the resulting 6Li into alpha + deuteron. The real part of the DPP was calculated around the strong absorption radius to quantify the effect of this transfer-plus-breakup process on the fusion barrier.

## Key numbers

- Complete fusion cross sections for 6Li + 209Bi and 7Li + 209Bi are suppressed by about 30% at energies above the fusion barrier
- Complete fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies are enhanced by nearly one order of magnitude for 6Li + 209Bi
- The suppression and enhancement effects are more pronounced for 6Li than for 7Li
- DPP calculations were performed for 7Li + 144Sm at laboratory energies of 30 MeV (above barrier), 25 MeV (near barrier), and 20 MeV (below barrier)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fusion suppression and enhancement for weakly bound projectiles (6Li, 7Li, 9Be) previously studied by Canto et al. (2006), Liang and Signorini (2005), and Keeley et al. (2007). It introduces a new process (transfer followed by breakup) beyond the standard four processes (complete prompt fusion, complete sequential fusion, incomplete fusion, and non-capture breakup), as confirmed experimentally by the Canberra group (Luong et al. 2011). The findings enable a more complete understanding of how nucleon transfer channels affect fusion cross sections near the Coulomb barrier, particularly explaining the sub-barrier enhancement that previous models could not fully account for.
