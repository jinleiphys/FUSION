# Effects of unconventional breakup modes on incomplete fusion of weakly bound nuclei

> The incomplete fusion dynamics of $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi collisions at energies above the Coulomb barrier is investigated. The classical dynamical model implemented in the {\sc platypus} code is used to understand and quantify the impact of both $^6$Li resonance states and transfer-triggered breakup modes (involving short-lived projectile-like nuclei such as $^8$Be and $^5$Li) on the formation of incomplete fusion products. Model calculations explain the experimental incomplete-fusion excitation function fairly well, indicating that (i) delayed direct breakup of $^6$Li reduces the incomplete fusion cross-sections, and (ii) the neutron-stripping channel practically determines those cross-sections.

# Effects of unconventional breakup modes on incomplete fusion of weakly bound nuclei

## Key claim

The neutron-stripping channel (producing $^5$Li) dominates the incomplete fusion cross-section in $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi collisions above the Coulomb barrier, contrary to most quantum mechanical calculations which assume direct breakup of $^6$Li is the dominant incomplete fusion channel. The model calculations agree reasonably well with experimental data only when both delayed direct breakup and transfer-triggered breakup modes are included, though the classical model cannot treat quantum tunneling so its description near the Coulomb barrier is not reliable.

## Method

The classical dynamical model implemented in the PLATYPUS code was used to simulate $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi collisions at energies above the Coulomb barrier. The code uses classical trajectories with stochastic breakup determined from sub-barrier breakup measurements via Monte-Carlo sampling, incorporating a new branching-ratio variable to differentiate between delayed (resonant) and prompt (non-resonant) breakup, as well as lifetime effects for short-lived projectile-like nuclei ($^5$Li and $^8$Be) produced via neutron-stripping and deuteron-pickup transfer channels. The Woods-Saxon nuclear interaction parameters between projectile fragments and the target were determined by approximately matching the corresponding Sao-Paulo potential barriers.

## Key numbers

- $^6$Li resonant states: $1^+$ state at $(E_{res},\Gamma_{res}) = (4.18, 1.5)$ MeV, $2^+$ state at $(2.84, 1.7)$ MeV, $3^+$ state at $(0.716, 0.024)$ MeV
- Half-lives of $^6$Li resonant states: $4.2 \times 10^{-22}$ s ($1^+$) and $3.7 \times 10^{-22}$ s ($2^+$), compared to typical collision time of $\sim 10^{-21}$ s
- $^5$Li ground state is a resonant alpha-proton $p$-state with $(E_{res},\Gamma_{res}) = (1.96, 2.0)$ MeV, with half-life $\sim 3.3 \times 10^{-22}$ s
- $^8$Be first $2^+$ resonant state at $(E_{res},\Gamma_{res}) = (3.0, 1.5)$ MeV; ground state half-life is very long ($\sim 10^{-16}$ s) compared to collision time
- Transfer Q-values: $Q_{tr}^{gg} = -1.059$ MeV (n-stripping) and $13.338$ MeV (d-pickup)
- Coulomb barriers: alpha-$^{209}$Bi $\sim 21.25$ MeV, deuteron-$^{209}$Bi $\sim 10.1$ MeV
- Breakup function exponential parameters ($\alpha$, $\beta$): $(-0.353738, 1.26228)$ for $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi, $(-0.799239, 8.03581)$ for $^5$Li + $^{210}$Bi, $(-1.57787, 14.3403)$ for $^8$Be + $^{207}$Pb

## Context

This work extends the classical dynamical model implemented in the PLATYPUS code (Diaz-Torres, 2011) to include resonance states and transfer-triggered breakup channels, building on sub-barrier breakup measurements by Luong et al. (2013) and Kalkal et al. (2016). The results contradict the assumptions in Parkar, Jha and Kailas (2016) that all observed incomplete fusion products originate from $^6$Li direct breakup, and instead demonstrate that the neutron-stripping channel dominates. The findings enable improved interpretation of incomplete fusion data for weakly bound nuclei at above-barrier energies, complementing quantum mechanical approaches such as the time-dependent wave-packet method (Boselli and Diaz-Torres, 2015) and continuum discretized coupled-channels calculations.
