# Low-energy $^{6}$He scattering in a microscopic model

> A microscopic version of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method is used to investigate $^{6}$He scattering on $^{27}$Al, $^{58}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The $^{6}$He nucleus is described by an antisymmetric 6-nucleon wave function, defined in the Resonating Group Method. The $^{6}$He continuum is simulated by square-integrable positive-energy states. The model is based only on well known nucleon-target potentials, and is therefore does not depend on any adjustable parameter. I show that experimental elastic cross sections are fairly well reproduced. The calculation suggests that breakup effects increase for high target masses. For a light system such as $^{6}$He+$^{27}$Al, breakup effects are small, and a single-channel approximation provides fair results. This property is explained by a very simple model, based on the sharp-cut-off approximation for the scattering matrix. I also investigate the $^{6}$He-target optical potentials, which confirm that breakup channels are more and more important when the mass increases. At large distances, polarization effects increase the Coulomb barrier, and provide a long-tail absorption component in the imaginary part of the nucleus-nucleus interaction.

## Key claim

The microscopic CDCC (MCDCC) model, using only well-known nucleon-target optical potentials with no adjustable parameters, fairly reproduces experimental elastic cross sections for 6He scattering on targets from 27Al to 208Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier; however, the agreement is not perfect and the model slightly underestimates the 6He charge radius compared to high-precision laser spectroscopy measurements.

## Method

The 6He projectile is described by a fully antisymmetric six-nucleon wave function using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) with an alpha core plus two neutrons in a hyperspherical formalism, solved via the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM). The 6He continuum is simulated by square-integrable positive-energy pseudostates up to a maximum energy Emax and maximum angular momentum jmax. The coupling potentials between projectile states and the target are computed by folding nucleon-target optical potentials (taken from the Koning and Delaroche compilation) with the microscopic GCM densities of 6He, and the coupled-channel system is solved using the R-matrix technique with a Lagrange basis.

## Key numbers

- 6He two-neutron separation energy: S2n = -0.973 MeV
- 6He matter radius: 2.35 fm (computed), compared to experimental 2.33 ± 0.04 fm
- 6He charge radius: 1.80 fm (computed), compared to experimental 2.054 ± 0.014 fm
- 6He+208Pb Coulomb barrier: VB = 18.4 MeV (c.m.)
- 6He+27Al Coulomb barrier: VB ≈ 3.9 MeV (c.m.)
- 6He+27Al energies studied: Ecm = 7.8, 9.0, 9.8, 11.0 MeV
- 6He+208Pb energy studied: Elab = 22 MeV (Ecm = 21.4 MeV)
- R-matrix channel radius: a = 24 fm with N = 120 mesh points
- Maximum angular momentum in projectile-target motion: Jmax ~ 100-150
- Generator coordinate values: R = 1.5 fm to 12 fm in steps of 1.5 fm (N=8 values)
- Oscillator parameter: b = 1.36 fm
- Minnesota interaction parameters: u = 1.0045, S0 = 37 MeV·fm5

## Context

This work extends the microscopic CDCC (MCDCC) approach, previously applied to 7Li scattering, to the three-cluster projectile 6He, building on earlier non-microscopic CDCC calculations for 6He+209Bi by Matsumoto et al. (2004) and for 6He+208Pb by Rodriguez-Gallardo et al. (2008). The model enables a common study of four target systems (27Al, 58Ni, 120Sn, 208Pb) under identical conditions, and the derived polarization potentials confirm that breakup effects increase with target mass, with a long-tail absorption component in the imaginary part of the nucleus-nucleus interaction at large distances. The results enable future applications to more complex weakly-bound three-body systems such as 11Li or 14Be, which are more difficult to describe microscopically.
