# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for multinucleon transfer and quasifission processes in the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction

> Background: Multinucleon transfer (MNT) and quasifission (QF) processes are dominant processes in low-energy collisions of two heavy nuclei. They are expected to be useful to produce neutron-rich unstable nuclei. Nuclear dynamics leading to these processes depends sensitively on nuclear properties such as deformation and shell structure. Purpose: We elucidate reaction mechanisms of MNT and QF processes involving heavy deformed nuclei, making detailed comparisons between microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations and measurements for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction. Methods: Three-dimensional Skyrme-TDHF calculations are performed. Particle-number projection method is used to evaluate MNT cross sections from the TDHF wave function after collision. Results: Fragment masses, total kinetic energy (TKE), scattering angle, contact time, and MNT cross sections are investigated for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction. They show reasonable agreements with measurements. At small impact parameters, collision dynamics depends sensitively on the orientation of deformed $^{238}$U. In tip (side) collisions, we find a larger (smaller) TKE and a shorter (longer) contact time. In tip collisions, we find a strong influence of quantum shells around $^{208}$Pb. Conclusions: It is confirmed that the TDHF calculations reasonably describe both MNT and QF processes in the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction. Analyses of this system indicates the significance of the nuclear structure effects such as deformation and quantum shells in nuclear reaction dynamics at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations reasonably describe both multinucleon transfer (MNT) and quasifission (QF) processes in the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction, but this agreement is qualified by the omission of pairing correlations due to computational cost, and the results depend sensitively on the initial orientation of the deformed $^{238}$U target.

## Method

Three-dimensional Skyrme-TDHF calculations were performed using the SLy5 energy density functional on a uniform grid with 0.8 fm mesh spacing, with no symmetry restrictions and no pairing correlations included. The particle-number projection method was applied to the TDHF wave function after collision to evaluate MNT cross sections for each channel specified by neutron and proton numbers. Calculations were performed for three initial orientations of the prolate $^{238}$U nucleus (symmetry axis parallel to the collision axis, parallel to the impact parameter vector, or perpendicular to the reaction plane) over impact parameters from 0 to 12 fm at center-of-mass energies around 307.35 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Barrier heights: 242.93 MeV for the $x$-direction (tip) case and 263.97 MeV for the $y$-direction (side) case at $b=0$ fm
- Contact time in tip collisions ($x$-direction): approximately 4-5 zeptoseconds (zs) at small impact parameters ($b \lesssim 4$ fm)
- Contact time in side collisions ($y$- and $z$-direction): 10-11 zs in head-on collisions
- TKEL in tip collisions: approximately 50-60 MeV at small impact parameters
- TKEL in side collisions: approximately 70-80 MeV at small impact parameters
- In tip collisions, the heavier fragment shows $N_{\rm H} \approx 120-126$ and $Z_{\rm H} \approx 78-82$, indicating influence of $^{208}$Pb quantum shells
- In the $y$-direction case at $b=0$ to 0.25 fm, the average number of nucleons changes by as much as 25

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of MNT processes in lighter systems ($^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb) and QF processes in actinide collisions ($^{238}$U+$^{238}$U, $^{232}$Th+$^{250}$Cf) to the asymmetric $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U system. It directly compares with experimental measurements by Corradi et al. (MNT cross sections) and Kozulin et al. (fragment mass and TKE distributions). The results enable quantitative testing of microscopic reaction theory against data for heavy deformed systems and clarify the interplay between nuclear orientation and shell effects (particularly $^{208}$Pb and $Z=50$) in quasifission dynamics, as previously suggested in TDHF studies of $^{40}$Ca+$^{238}$U by Wakhle et al.
