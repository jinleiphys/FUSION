# Reaction models to probe the structure of light exotic nuclei

> We review here theoretical models for describing various types of reactions involving light nuclei on the driplines. Structure features to be extracted from the analysis of such reaction data, as well as those that need to be incorporated in the reaction models for an adequate description of the processes, are also under focus. The major unsolved theoretical issues are discussed, along with some suggestions for future directions of the field.

## Key claim

This review argues that while high-energy reaction models (eikonal, adiabatic, Glauber) have been adequate for extracting general structural features of light dripline nuclei, a new era requires low-energy reaction models that can probe detailed structure, even though these are harder to formulate because adiabatic and eikonal simplifications no longer hold and couplings are more important.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical approaches for modeling reactions of weakly-bound, few-body light exotic nuclei, including the coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) method, the adiabatic (sudden) approximation, few-body Glauber methods, the optical limit of the Glauber model, and time-dependent semiclassical methods. They describe how each method approximates the few-body Schrödinger equation, handles projectile breakup effects, and is applied to observables such as total reaction cross sections, elastic scattering angular distributions, breakup cross sections, and stripping cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of halo nuclei are typically of the order of 1 MeV or less, making breakup effects even more important than for deuteron or lithium projectiles
- The eikonal approximation requires \(K_0 R_0 \gg 1\) (short wavelength condition) and \(E \gg |V_0|\) (high energy condition), but works well even when \(E \approx |V_0|\) provided the first condition holds and forward angle scattering is considered
- Corrections to the straight-line eikonal assumption allow the few-body Glauber approach to be applied below 20 MeV/A
- The optical limit of the Glauber model always overestimates the total reaction cross section for strongly absorbed particles (Johnson and Goebel inequality), thus requiring a smaller halo size than a full few-body calculation for a given cross section

## Context

This review fills a gap between experimental reviews of light exotic nuclei (Tanihata, Hansen, Orr) and conference proceedings on reaction models, by systematically covering the theoretical progress in modeling reactions with light dripline nuclei. It extends earlier work on deuteron, \(^6\)Li, and \(^7\)Li scattering (where CDCC was originally developed) to halo nuclei such as \(^6\)He, \(^{11}\)Li, \(^{11}\)Be, \(^8\)B, \(^{17}\)F, and \(^{19}\)C. The paper enables future work by identifying open issues including the need for four-body CDCC for Borromean nuclei, better treatment of low-energy reactions, and theoretical considerations for reactions with electron beams at new-generation ISOL facilities (SPIRAL-GANIL, REX-ISOLDE, ISAC-TRIUMF, RIA-ANL, and others).
