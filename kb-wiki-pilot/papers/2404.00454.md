# Revisiting the 3α reaction rates in helium burning stars

> Helium burning is one of the most fundamental steps of stellar nucleosynthesis, as it describes the formation of life-determining element of carbon, while it plays a key role in the evolution of Red Giant, accreting White Dwarfs and Neutron Stars. In this work we develop a generalized statistical theory for the 3α reaction, which is based on the use of the Imaginary Time Method, along with the semi-classical Hybrid α-Clustering (HαC) and Neck Model (NM) frameworks. The results compared to the methodology and data of the NACRE collaboration, following in several orders of magnitude discrepancies, especially at low temperatures. This may be crucial for the early dynamics of helium burning stars.

## Key claim
The paper develops a generalized statistical theory for the 3α reaction that yields reaction rates differing from the NACRE compilation by several orders of magnitude, especially at low temperatures (T ∼ 10⁷ K), where the original NACRE prescription overestimates rates by 20-30 orders of magnitude. The key qualifier is that the NACRE methodology incorrectly integrates reaction rates from 0 MeV rather than from the ⁸Be threshold energy (92.08 keV) and uses an incorrect width ratio (Γγ,2/Γα,2 instead of Γγ,2/Γ2) in the cross section formula.

## Method
The authors calculate fusion cross sections using the Imaginary Time Method (ITM) coupled with two semi-classical models: the Hybrid α-Clustering (HαC) model, which treats α particles as fundamental degrees of freedom with Hamiltonian dynamics, and the Neck Model (NM), a macroscopic description based on Wigner transforms of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock densities. The α widths are derived from the penetrability via the action in imaginary time without free parameters, while the γ widths are calculated using a resonant matrix element proportional to the α width, rather than the constant matrix element approximation used in NACRE. The reaction rate per α-triplet is then computed by integrating from the ⁸Be threshold energy (92.08 keV) rather than from 0 MeV.

## Key numbers
- ⁸Be threshold energy: E_⁸Be = 92.08 keV (Q₁ = -92.08 keV for α+α → ⁸Be)
- Hoyle state resonance energy: E_H = 7.654 MeV
- Q-value for second step (⁸Be+α → ¹²C): Q₂ = 7.3667 MeV
- α width of ⁸Be ground state: Γα,Be⁽⁰⁾ = 5.6 eV
- α width of Hoyle state: Γα,H⁽⁰⁾ = 9.3 eV
- γ width of Hoyle state: Γγ,H⁽⁰⁾ = 5.1 meV
- Astrophysical upper limit at T ∼ 10⁷·⁸ K: reaction rate ≤ 10⁻²⁹ cm⁶ s⁻¹ mol⁻² (from Suda et al. 2011)
- Temperature dependence lower limit: ν ≳ 10 in the region T ∼ 10⁸ – 1.2×10⁸ K (from Suda et al. 2011)

## Context
This work extends the statistical theory of fusion-evaporation reactions to the 3α process, directly challenging the NACRE compilation (Angulo 1999, NACRE II 2013) which has been the standard reference for helium burning rates. The authors identify two critical errors in NACRE: integrating from 0 MeV instead of the ⁸Be threshold, and using Γγ,2/Γα,2 instead of Γγ,2/Γ2 in the cross section formula. The results are consistent with constraints from Suda et al. (2011) on AGB star evolution and helium flash timing. The paper enables future experimental investigations using the Trojan Horse Method (Tumino 2018) to bypass the short ⁸Be lifetime and Coulomb suppression issues.
