# Microscopic optical potentials including breakup effects for elastic scattering

> We construct a microscopic optical potential including breakup effects for elastic scattering of weakly-binding projectiles within the Glauber model, in which a nucleon-nucleus potential is derived by the $g$-matrix folding model. The derived microscopic optical potential is referred to as the eikonal potential. For $d$ scattering, the calculation with the eikonal potential reasonably reproduces the result with an exact calculation estimated by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method. As the properties of the eikonal potential, the inaccuracy of the eikonal approximation used in the Glauber model is partially excluded. We also analyse the $^6$He scattering from $^{12}$C with the eikonal potential and show its applicability to the scattering with many-body projectiles.

## Key claim

The eikonal potential, a microscopic optical potential derived from the Glauber model using the $g$-matrix folding model, can include breakup effects for elastic scattering of weakly-binding projectiles, and its inaccuracy from the eikonal approximation is partially excluded, though the adiabatic approximation remains a source of discrepancy at low incident energies.

## Method

The authors construct the eikonal potential by first calculating the eikonal $S$ matrix for the projectile-target system using the Glauber model with nucleon-nucleus potentials derived from the Melbourne $g$-matrix folding model, then inverting the Abel transform to obtain a local optical potential. For validation, they compare elastic scattering cross sections from the eikonal potential model (EP model) with those from continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations for $d$ scattering on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb at 20-200 MeV/nucleon, and for $^6$He scattering on $^{12}$C at the same energies.

## Key numbers

- For $d$ scattering, the EP model well reproduces CDCC results for $E_{\rm in}/A_{\rP} \geq 80$ MeV on both $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb targets
- For $E_{\rm in}/A_{\rP} \leq 40$ MeV, agreement between the EP model and CDCC is "reasonably well" for $d$ scattering, though with "rather different behavior for the oscillation"
- For $^6$He scattering from $^{12}$C, the EP model well reproduces CDCC results even at $E_{\rm in}/A_{\rm P} \leq 40$ MeV
- The grazing angular momentum for $^6$He + $^{12}$C at 40 MeV/nucleon is $L=25$

## Context

This work extends the double-single folding (DSF) model for $^{3,4}$He scattering (Toyokawa et al., 2015; Egashira et al., 2014) by incorporating breakup effects that the DSF model omits, which were previously only treatable by CDCC for two- and three-body projectiles. The eikonal potential approach enables analysis of scattering with many-body projectiles (such as $^8$He described by a five-body model) where CDCC becomes computationally prohibitive, and the authors plan to apply it to $^8$He scattering and include Coulomb breakup effects in future work.
