# Polarization Observables in $A(d,p)$ Breakup and Quark Degrees of Freedom in the Deuteron

> The differential cross section, the tensor analyzing power, $T_{20}$, and the polarization transfer, $κ_0$, in $^{12}C(d,p)$ breakup at relativistic energy are calculated within a model which incorporates multiple scattering and Pauli principle at quark level. It is shown that the rescattering and quark exchange affect drastically the polarization observables for kinematical region corresponding to high internal momentum in the deuteron.

## Key claim
The paper shows that multiple scattering (MS) and quark exchange (QE) effects drastically affect the differential cross section, tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\), and polarization transfer \(\kappa_0\) in \(^{12}C(d,p)\) breakup at relativistic energy, but only for the kinematical region corresponding to high internal momentum in the deuteron. The abstract glosses over the condition that the calculations ignore the \(NA \to NA\) spin-flip amplitude, which may be important for \(T_{20}\) in \(^1H(d,p)\) breakup.

## Method
The authors modify the Bertocchi–Treleani approach, based on Sitenko–Glauber multiple scattering theory, by incorporating a relativistic deuteron wave function in the infinite momentum frame (IMF) with Melosh spin rotation, and by including the Pauli principle at the constituent quark level via the Resonating Group Method (RGM). The model computes the invariant differential cross section, \(T_{20}\), and \(\kappa_0\) for \(0^\circ\) inclusive \(^{12}C(d,p)\) breakup at a deuteron momentum of 9.1 GeV/c, summing over the neutron and the lowest 10 nucleon resonance states generated by quark exchange.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron beam momentum: 9.1 GeV/c.
- Total cross section \(\sigma^T_{n^{12}C}\) taken as experimental value for \(\sigma^T_{p^{12}C} = 340\) mb.
- Differential cross sections for \(N^{12}C\) approximated by universal curve \(A_1 e^{-B_1 k^2_{\perp}} + A_2 e^{-B_2 k^2_{\perp}}\) with \(A_1 = 2611.3\) mb/(GeV/c)\(^2\), \(B_1 = 69.3\) (GeV/c)\(^{-2}\), \(A_2 = 119.3\) mb/(GeV/c)\(^2\), \(B_2 = 4.3\) (GeV/c)\(^{-2}\).
- For the average nucleon resonance \(N_R\), parameters chosen as \(\sigma^T_{N_R^{12}C} = 400\) mb, \(A_1^R = 2373.9\) mb/(GeV/c)\(^2\), \(B_1^R = 77.0\) (GeV/c)\(^{-2}\), with \(A_2^R = A_2\) and \(B_2^R = B_2\).

## Context
This work extends earlier nonrelativistic calculations of multiple scattering effects in \(A(d,p)\) breakup by Perdrisat and Punjabi (1990) and Dakhno and Nikonov (1989), and incorporates quark exchange effects previously studied by Kobushkin, Syamtomov, and Glozman (1996). It provides the first systematic relativistic study combining multiple scattering and quark exchange, enabling future investigation of the \(A\)-dependence of polarization observables and the role of spin-flip amplitudes in \(A(d,p)\) breakup.
