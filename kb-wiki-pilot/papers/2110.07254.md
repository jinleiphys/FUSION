# Neutron transfer in $^{9}$Be + $^{159}$Tb system

> One neutron stripping cross sections ($σ_{-1n}$) are measured in $^{9}$Be+$^{159}$Tb system in the energy range E$_{cm}$/V$_{B}$ $\sim$ 0.79 - 1.24 using offline gamma counting technique. The CRC model calculations including the ground state and the 2$^{+}$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be, carried out using the FRESCO code, give a reasonable description of the measured data. In addition, comparisons of reduced 1n-stripping cross sections- $σ_{red}$ with $^{9}$Be for different target nuclei (A $\sim$150-200), and $σ_{red}$ for $^{9}$Be, $^{6}$Li with $^{159}$Tb target are presented. While no strong target dependence is observed with $^{9}$Be projectile, $σ_{red}$($^{9}$Be) is significantly larger than that for $^{6}$Li, which is consistent with the Q-value for transfer reactions and breakup threshold energy of projectiles.

## Key claim

One-neutron stripping cross sections in the $^{9}$Be + $^{159}$Tb system are measured over a wide energy range (E$_{cm}$/V$_{B}$ $\sim$ 0.79 - 1.24) and are reasonably described by coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations, but only when the $2^{+}$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be is included alongside the ground state; the abstract's claim of "reasonable description" glosses over the fact that the best agreement required a specific selection of $^{160}$Tb excited states up to 1.198 MeV, while calculations with states only up to 0.572 MeV under-predicted the data and those up to 1.397 MeV over-predicted it.

## Method

One-neutron stripping cross sections ($\sigma_{-1n}$) were measured using the offline gamma counting technique with a $^{9}$Be beam (E = 30-47 MeV) incident on self-supporting $^{159}$Tb targets (1.3-1.7 mg/cm$^{2}$) backed by aluminum catcher foils, at the BARC-TIFR Pelletron Linac Facility. The yield of the 879 keV $\gamma$-ray from $^{160}$Tb (half-life 77$\pm$9 days) was used to extract cross sections after appropriate decay corrections. CRC calculations were performed using the FRESCO code with Woods-Saxon optical potentials, including the ground state and $2^{+}$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be, and excited states of $^{160}$Tb up to 1.198 MeV with spectroscopic factors of 1.0.

## Key numbers

- $\sigma_{-1n}$ at E$_{lab}$ = 27.1 MeV (E$_{cm}$ = 25.6 MeV): 13 $\pm$ 1 mb
- $\sigma_{-1n}$ at E$_{lab}$ = 30.1 MeV (E$_{cm}$ = 28.5 MeV): 30 $\pm$ 2 mb
- $\sigma_{-1n}$ at E$_{lab}$ = 33.1 MeV (E$_{cm}$ = 31.3 MeV): 83 $\pm$ 12 mb
- $\sigma_{-1n}$ at E$_{lab}$ = 37.0 MeV (E$_{cm}$ = 35.0 MeV): 138 $\pm$ 16 mb
- $\sigma_{-1n}$ at E$_{lab}$ = 42.6 MeV (E$_{cm}$ = 40.3 MeV): 175 $\pm$ 20 mb
- Coulomb barrier V$_{B}$ = 32.5 MeV for the $^{9}$Be + $^{159}$Tb system
- Ground state Q-value for 1n-stripping in $^{9}$Be + $^{159}$Tb: 4.71 MeV
- Measured half-life of 879 keV $\gamma$-ray: 77 $\pm$ 9 days (consistent with literature value of 72.3 days)

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of one-neutron stripping with $^{9}$Be projectiles on heavy targets (A $\sim$ 150-200), previously reported for $^{169}$Tm, $^{181}$Ta, $^{186}$W, $^{187}$Re, and $^{197}$Au (Fang et al., Kaushik et al.), and finds no strong target dependence in reduced cross sections. The comparison with $^{6}$Li + $^{159}$Tb data from Pradhan et al. shows that $\sigma_{red}$($^{9}$Be) is significantly larger than for $^{6}$Li, consistent with the higher Q-value (4.71 MeV vs. 0.71 MeV) and lower breakup threshold of $^{9}$Be. The CRC calculations for $^{6}$Li + $^{159}$Tb, which were not previously available, now reproduce the energy dependence of the data with a scale factor of 0.65, enabling better understanding of spectroscopic factors in transfer reactions.
