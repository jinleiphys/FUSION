# Nuclear matrix elements from lattice QCD for electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model processes

> Over the last decade, numerical solutions of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) using the technique of lattice QCD have developed to a point where they are beginning to connect fundamental aspects of nuclear physics to the underlying degrees of freedom of the Standard Model. In this review, the progress of lattice QCD studies of nuclear matrix elements of electroweak currents and beyond-Standard-Model operators is summarized, and connections with effective field theories and nuclear models are outlined. Lattice QCD calculations of nuclear matrix elements can provide guidance for low-energy nuclear reactions in astrophysics, dark matter direct detection experiments, and experimental searches for violations of the symmetries of the Standard Model, including searches for additional CP violation in the hadronic and leptonic sectors, baryon-number violation, and lepton-number or flavor violation. Similarly, important inputs to neutrino experiments seeking to determine the neutrino-mass hierarchy and oscillation parameters, as well as other electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model processes can be determined. The phenomenological implications of existing studies of electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model matrix elements in light nuclear systems are discussed, and future prospects for the field toward precision studies of these matrix elements are outlined.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of nuclear matrix elements for electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model processes are only just beginning to provide controlled first-principles results, with existing studies performed at larger-than-physical quark masses and with significant uncertainties, rather than at the physical point with full control of discretization and finite-volume effects.

## Method

The authors review the application of lattice QCD to compute nuclear matrix elements by discretizing the QCD action on a four-dimensional spacetime lattice, using Monte Carlo methods with importance sampling to evaluate the functional integrals that define physical observables. Quark propagators are computed by solving linear systems involving the Dirac operator on gauge-field configurations generated via hybrid Monte Carlo algorithms, and the resulting correlation functions are matched to low-energy effective field theories and nuclear models to enable extrapolation to physical quark masses and infinite volume.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass recovered with better than percent-level precision including both QCD and QED interactions
- Lattice volumes as large as (L/a)^3 x (T/a) >= 128^3 x 256 with lattice spacing a ~ 0.05 fm are targeted in state-of-the-art calculations
- Calculations of systems up to atomic number A=5 have been performed over the past decade with unphysically-large quark masses
- The isovector magnetic moments, magnetic polarizabilities, and Gamow-Teller contributions to triton beta decay have been computed for nuclei up to A=4 at larger-than-physical quark masses

## Context

This review extends and summarizes the progress of lattice QCD studies of nuclear matrix elements that began with early attempts over 25 years ago, including the first clear identification of QCD bound states in the H-dibaryon system. The work connects to effective field theory approaches such as chiral perturbation theory and nuclear many-body techniques, and enables future precision studies that will impact low-energy nuclear astrophysics reactions, dark matter direct detection experiments, neutrino oscillation parameter determinations, and searches for beyond-Standard-Model physics including neutrinoless double-beta decay.
