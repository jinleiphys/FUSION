# Complete fusion of $^9$Be with spherical targets

> The complete fusion of $^9$Be with $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb targets is calculated in the coupled-channels approach. The calculations include couplings between the 3/2$^-$, 5/2$^-$, and 7/2$^-$ states in the $K=3/2$ ground state rotational band of $^9$Be. It is shown that the $B(E2)$ values for the excitation of these states are accurately described in the rotor model. The interaction of the strongly deformed $^9$Be nucleus with a spherical target is calculated using the double-folding technique and the effective M3Y interaction, which is supplemented with a repulsive term that is adjusted to optimize the fit to the data for the $^{144}$Sm target. The complete fusion is described by in-going-wave boundary conditions. The decay of the unbound excited states in $^9$Be is considered explicitly in the calculations by using complex excitation energies. The model gives an excellent account of the complete fusion (CF) data for $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm, and the cross sections for the decay of the excited states are in surprisingly good agreement with the incomplete fusion (ICF) data. Similar calculations for $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb explain the total fusion data at high energies but fail to explain the CF data, which are suppressed by 20%, and the calculated cross section for the decay of excited states is a factor of three smaller than the ICF data at high energies. Possible reasons for these discrepancies are discussed.

# Complete fusion of $^9$Be with spherical targets

## Key claim

The coupled-channels model, which includes couplings between the 3/2$^-$, 5/2$^-$, and 7/2$^-$ states in the $K=3/2$ ground state rotational band of $^9$Be and treats the decay of unbound excited states via complex excitation energies, gives an excellent account of complete fusion data for $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm but fails to explain the complete fusion data for $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb, which are suppressed by 20% at high energies relative to the calculation.

## Method

The double-folding technique with the effective M3Y interaction, supplemented with a repulsive term adjusted to optimize the fit to the $^{144}$Sm target data, was used to calculate the Coulomb plus nuclear interaction between the deformed $^9$Be nucleus and spherical targets. Coupled-channels calculations were performed in the rotating frame approximation with ingoing-wave boundary conditions imposed at the minimum of the pocket in the entrance channel potential, and the decay of excited states was included explicitly by employing complex excitation energies $E_I - \frac{i}{2}\Gamma_I$.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic quadrupole moment of $^9$Be: $Q_0 = 26.45(20)$ fm$^2$, derived from the measured ground state quadrupole moment of 5.29(4) fm$^2$
- Best fit repulsive strength: $v_r \approx 410$ MeV fm$^3$, determined from $\chi^2$ optimization to $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm complete fusion data
- $B(E2)$ values for the $K=3/2$ ground state rotational band: 14.0(2) e$^2$fm$^4$ (3/2$^-$), 40.7(6) e$^2$fm$^4$ (5/2$^-$), 18.9(3) e$^2$fm$^4$ (7/2$^-$); rotor model predictions: 14, 36, 20 e$^2$fm$^4$ respectively
- Width of the 7/2$^-$ state: $\Gamma = 1210$ keV; width of the 5/2$^-$ state: $\Gamma = 0.78$ keV
- For $^9$Be+$^{208}$Pb: calculated complete fusion cross sections are 20% higher than data at high energies; calculated absorption cross section (decay of excited states) is a factor of three smaller than measured incomplete fusion data at high energies
- For $^9$Be+$^{144}$Sm: complete fusion is suppressed by about 10% compared to total fusion at high energies, both experimentally and in calculations

## Context

This work extends coupled-channels fusion calculations for weakly bound, strongly deformed nuclei beyond previous approaches that used deformed Woods-Saxon potentials without considering the decay of excited states or incomplete fusion effects. The study builds on experimental measurements of $^9$Be fusion with $^{208}$Pb (Refs. anu99cf, anu04pb) and $^{144}$Sm (Ref. anu06sm), and uses the rotor model to describe the $K=3/2$ ground state rotational band of $^9$Be. The double-folding technique follows the method of Misicu and Greiner (Ref. misi2) for fusion between spherical and deformed nuclei. The results enable future theoretical work on neutron transfer from $^9$Be to bound states in the target and to continuum states as additional sources of incomplete fusion, as suggested by recent experiments (Ref. rafiei) showing that $^9$Be breakup following neutron transfer dominates the total breakup yield.
