# The nuclear and Coulomb breakup of the weakly bound $^{6}$Li with targets in the range from A = 59 to 208

> We have performed CDCC calculations for the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co, $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb systems, to investigate the dependence of the relative importance of nuclear and Coulomb breakup on the target charge (mass) at near barrier energies. The calculations were in good agreement with the experimental elastic scattering angular distributions for these systems and then, their predictions to the nuclear, Coulomb and total breakup were investigated. Although the relative importance of the nuclear breakup is, as expected, larger for lighter targets, this effect is not very pronounced. We also investigate a scaling of the nuclear breakup with the target mass and we compare the predictions for the integrated total breakup cross sections with experimental fusion cross sections at similar energies.

# The nuclear and Coulomb breakup of the weakly bound $^{6}$Li with targets in the range from A = 59 to 208

## Key claim

Although the relative importance of nuclear breakup is larger for lighter targets as expected, this effect is not very pronounced; the nuclear breakup cross section is actually strongest for the intermediate-mass $^{144}$Sm target rather than the lightest target, and the Coulomb-nuclear interference is always destructive, making the pure Coulomb breakup cross section alone larger than the total breakup cross section in some cases.

## Method

Continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations were performed for $^{6}$Li projectiles on $^{59}$Co, $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb targets at near-barrier energies, using the cluster model where $^{6}$Li is described as a bound state of the $d+\alpha$ system. The real parts of the cluster-target interactions were given by the double-folding Sao Paulo potential, the imaginary parts were Woods-Saxon functions with depth $W_0 = -50$ MeV representing short-range fusion absorption, and the calculations were performed using the computer code FRESCO. Inelastic channels corresponding to collective excitations of the targets were also included.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier heights: $V_B(^{59}\text{Co}) = 12.8$ MeV, $V_B(^{144}\text{Sm}) = 23.8$ MeV, $V_B(^{208}\text{Pb}) = 29.4$ MeV
- $^{6}$Li breakup threshold: $S_\alpha = 1.47$ MeV
- For $^{6}$Li + $^{208}$Pb at 29 MeV lab energy: Coulomb breakup cross section (46.8 mb) is larger than the total breakup cross section (37.2 mb), demonstrating destructive Coulomb-nuclear interference
- For $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co at 13.0 MeV: nuclear breakup = 4.33 mb, Coulomb breakup = 5.31 mb, total breakup = 5.68 mb
- For $^{6}$Li + $^{144}$Sm at 25.0 MeV: nuclear breakup = 30.0 mb, Coulomb breakup = 41.6 mb, total breakup = 48.0 mb
- For $^{6}$Li + $^{208}$Pb at 33.0 MeV: nuclear breakup = 38.7 mb, Coulomb breakup = 66.8 mb, total breakup = 82.5 mb
- Scaling law parameters for nuclear breakup at $E_{c.m.}/V_B = 1.07$: $P_1 = -49.89$ mb, $P_2 = 15.41$ mb
- For the light $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co system, breakup cross section is nearly two orders of magnitude smaller than the fusion cross section

## Context

This work extends the CDCC methodology previously applied to $^{6}$Li + $^{144}$Sm (Otomar et al., 2010) to a systematic study across three target masses, testing the scaling law for nuclear breakup proposed by Hussein et al. (2006) at near-barrier energies rather than the high-energy regime (tens of MeV/nucleon) where it was originally established. The results enable estimation of nuclear and Coulomb breakup cross sections for other target nuclei at near-barrier energies using the derived linear scaling relations with $A_T^{1/3}$ and $Z_T$, respectively, and provide quantitative comparisons with fusion data from Beck et al. (2003), Rath et al. (2009), and Wu et al. (2003) to assess the relative importance of breakup versus fusion channels.
