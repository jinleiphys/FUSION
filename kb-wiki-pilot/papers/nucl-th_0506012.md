# Continuum-discretized coupled-channels method for four-body breakup reactions

> Development of the method of CDCC (Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels) from the level of three-body CDCC to that of four-body CDCC is reviewed. Introduction of the pseudo-state method based on the Gaussian expansion method for discretizing the continuum states of two-body and three-body projectiles plays an essential role in the development. Furthermore, introduction of the complex-range Gaussian basis functions is important to improve the CDCC for nuclear breakup so as to accomplish that for Coulomb and nuclear breakup. A successful application of the four-body CDCC to $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV is reported.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the extension of the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method from three-body to four-body breakup reactions, with the key enabling development being the pseudo-state method based on Gaussian expansion for discretizing continuum states of two-body and three-body projectiles. The authors report that the four-body CDCC successfully reproduces elastic scattering data for $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 18 and 229.8 MeV, but note that the optimum imaginary potential parameter $N_I$ differs between the two energies (0.5 at 18 MeV versus 0.3 at 229.8 MeV), with the origin of this difference remaining unclear.

## Method

The authors extended the CDCC framework by discretizing the continuum states of three-body projectiles (such as $^6$He modeled as $n+n+^4$He) using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM), where the internal wave functions are expanded in Gaussian basis functions with ranges in geometric progression across three Jacobian coordinate sets. For two-body projectiles, they introduced complex-range Gaussian basis functions that oscillate with radial distance, enabling accurate description of Coulomb breakup processes requiring internal wave functions up to approximately 100 fm. The coupled-channel equations were solved using double-folding potentials constructed from the DDM3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the imaginary part scaled by a single parameter $N_I$ fitted to elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- $^6$He ground state energy: $-0.98$ MeV for the $0^+$ state (using Bonn A potential with adjusted $n-\alpha$ potential depth)
- $^6$He $2^+$ resonance energy: $0.72$ MeV
- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 18 MeV: optimum imaginary potential parameter $N_I = 0.5$, pseudo-states with $\epsilon_{nI} < 12$ MeV sufficient for convergence
- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 229.8 MeV: optimum $N_I = 0.3$, pseudo-states with $\epsilon_{nI} < 25$ MeV required
- For $^8$B+$^{58}$Ni at 25.8 MeV: modelspace cutoff momentum $k_{\rm max} = 0.66$ fm$^{-1}$, maximum internal coordinate $r_{\rm max} = 100$ fm
- Bound and pseudo-states below 100 MeV satisfy 99.9% of the energy-weighted cluster sum-rule limit for monopole, dipole and quadrupole transitions
- Computation time for all wave functions of $^6$He states (60 $0^+$ and 85 $2^+$ states) is 10 minutes on FUJITSU VPP5000

## Context

This work extends the three-body CDCC method (developed for two-body projectiles like $^6$Li = $\alpha+d$) to four-body CDCC for three-body projectiles such as $^6$He and $^{11}$Li, building on the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) previously established for few-body bound states. The pseudo-state discretization approach replaces the momentum-bin method (which is impractical for three-body projectiles) and enables treatment of both nuclear and Coulomb breakup. The method was subsequently applied to $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering at 19.0 and 22.5 MeV including both Coulomb and nuclear breakup effects, and the authors suggest the framework is ready for extension to five-body CDCC for four-body projectiles.
