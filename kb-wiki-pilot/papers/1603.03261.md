# $^9$Be elastic scattering on $^{208}$Pb and $^{27}$Al within a four-body reaction framework

> We investigate the low-energy $^9$Be elastic scattering on two different targets (heavy, light) within a four-body framework using the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method. The $^9$Be projectile is described in a $α+ α+ n$ three-body model using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates. We show that continuum couplings are important to describe the elastic cross section, especially at low energies and on heavy targets. The dipolar contribution to the elastic cross section at energies around the Coulomb barrier is important but small compared to the case of halo nuclei. The effect of the projectile low-energy resonances is also relevant. The agreement with the available experimental data supports the reliability of the method to describe reactions induced by three-body projectiles including more than one charged particle.

## Key claim

The four-body CDCC framework using an analytical THO basis for the three-body projectile can reliably describe elastic scattering of \(^9\)Be on both heavy and light targets, but the agreement with experimental data for the \(^9\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb system at energies around the Coulomb barrier shows discrepancies of up to 6% in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region, which may originate from either experimental data analysis issues or theoretical model limitations.

## Method

The \(^9\)Be projectile was described in a three-body \(\alpha + \alpha + n\) model using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates, with the continuum discretized via the pseudo-state method. The four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) formalism was applied to compute elastic scattering cross sections, using the Koning and Delaroche global parametrization for the n-target potential and the Barnett parametrization for the \(\alpha\)-target interaction, with multipole couplings up to order \(Q=5\) and total angular momentum up to \(J=301/2\).

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of \(^9\)Be: 1.5736 MeV below the \(\alpha + \alpha + n\) threshold
- Ground state charge radius: \(r_{\text{ch}} = 2.50\) fm (experimental value: 2.519 \(\pm\) 0.012 fm)
- Ground state quadrupole moment: \(Q_2 = 4.82\) e fm\(^2\) (experimental value: 5.29 \(\pm\) 0.04 e fm\(^2\))
- Coulomb barrier for \(^9\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb: approximately 47 MeV
- Calculations performed at laboratory energies \(E_{\text{lab}} = 60\), 44, and 38 MeV for \(^9\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb
- Maximum hypermomentum \(K_{\text{max}} = 10\) used for converged reaction calculations
- THO basis parameters: \(b = 0.7\) fm, \(\gamma = 1.2\) fm\(^{1/2}\), \(\xi = 4\)
- Discrepancy between CDCC calculations and experimental data in the interference region: below 6%

## Context

This work extends the four-body CDCC formalism, previously applied to halo nuclei such as \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li, to the stable but weakly-bound \(^9\)Be nucleus described in a three-body \(\alpha+\alpha+n\) model. It builds upon earlier two-body CDCC calculations for \(^9\)Be by Pandit et al. and recent three-body model calculations by Descouvemont et al. using the Lagrange-mesh basis. The results enable systematic studies of reactions induced by three-body projectiles containing more than one charged particle, and show that the dipolar contribution to the elastic cross section, while important, is smaller than for halo nuclei due to the absence of a strong dipole moment in \(^9\)Be.
