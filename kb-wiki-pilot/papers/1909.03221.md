# Fusion reactions in the $^9$Be + $^{197}$Au system above the Coulomb barrier

> The cross sections of complete fusion and incomplete fusion for the $ ^{9} $Be + $ ^{197} $Au system, at energies not too much above the Coulomb barrier, were measured for the first time. The online activation followed by offline $γ$-ray spectroscopy method was used for the derivation of the cross sections. A slightly higher value of ICF/TF ratio has been observed, compared to other systems reported in the literature with $ ^{9} $Be beam. The experimental data were compared with coupled channel calculations without taking into account the coupling of the breakup channel, and experimental data of other reaction systems with weakly bound projectiles. A complete fusion suppression of about 40\% was found for the $ ^{9} $Be + $ ^{197} $Au system, at energies above the barrier, whereas the total fusion cross sections are in agreement with the calculations.

# Fusion reactions in the \(^9\)Be + \(^{197}\)Au system above the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim

The complete fusion (CF) cross section for the \(^9\)Be + \(^{197}\)Au system is suppressed by about 40% at energies above the Coulomb barrier when compared to coupled-channel calculations that do not include breakup and transfer channels, while the total fusion (TF) cross section agrees with those calculations. The incomplete fusion to total fusion ratio (ICF/TF) is slightly higher (around 0.37–0.42) than for other \(^9\)Be-induced systems reported in the literature.

## Method

Cross sections for complete fusion (via 3n, 4n, and 5n evaporation channels) and incomplete fusion (via α2n and α3n channels) were measured using the online stacked-foil activation technique followed by offline γ-ray spectroscopy with HPGe detectors. A 50.4 MeV \(^9\)Be beam from HIRFL irradiated five \(^{197}\)Au targets of approximately 1 mg/cm\(^2\) thickness; the experimental data were compared with coupled-channel calculations using the São Paulo potential and with the universal fusion function (UFF) methodology.

## Key numbers

- CF suppression of about 40% for the \(^9\)Be + \(^{197}\)Au system at above-barrier energies, compared to CC calculations without breakup coupling
- ICF/TF ratios: 0.37 at \(E_{\text{lab}}\) = 48.9 MeV, 0.37 at 47.9 MeV, 0.38 at 46.9 MeV, and 0.42 at 45.7 MeV
- Experimental CF cross sections (after correction): 441.63 ± 39.59 mb at 48.9 MeV, 421.95 ± 36.06 mb at 47.9 MeV, 377.01 ± 30.04 mb at 46.9 MeV, and 304.29 ± 32.21 mb at 45.7 MeV
- Barrier parameters from the São Paulo potential: \(R_B\) = 11.35 fm, \(V_B\) = 37.50 MeV, \(\hbar\omega\) = 4.44 MeV
- The \(^9\)Be + \(^{197}\)Au system shows a CF suppression of about 40%, which is higher than the 30–35% suppression reported for \(^9\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb, \(^{181}\)Ta, \(^{169}\)Tm, \(^{187}\)Re, and \(^{6,7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi systems

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of fusion suppression in weakly bound projectile systems, following the methodology of Canto et al. (UFF method, Refs. Canto_2008, CANTO200951) and prior measurements for \(^9\)Be + \(^{144}\)Sm (Gomes et al.), \(^{169}\)Tm (PhysRevC.91.014608), \(^{181}\)Ta (Zhang et al.), \(^{186}\)W (Fang et al.), \(^{187}\)Re (Zhang et al.), and \(^{208}\)Pb (Dasgupta et al.). The results enable further comparison of CF suppression across different target masses and support the conclusion that breakup channels cause hindrance of CF at above-barrier energies while TF remains unaffected, though the authors note that the mass dependence of the hindrance remains inconclusive and more data for medium-mass systems are needed.
