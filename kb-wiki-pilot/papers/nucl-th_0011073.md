# Calculations of three-body observables in ^8B breakup

> We discuss calculations of three-body observables for the breakup of ^8B on a ^{58}Ni target at low energy using the coupled discretised continuum channels approach. Calculations of both the angular distribution of the ^7Be fragments and their energy distributions are compared with those measured at several laboratory angles. In these observables there is interference between the breakup amplitudes from different spin-parity excitations of the projectile. The resulting angle and the energy distributions reveal the importance of the higher-order continuum state couplings for an understanding of the measurements.

# Calculations of three-body observables in ^8B breakup

## Key claim

The convergence of CDCC calculations for three-body observables (angular and energy distributions of ^7Be fragments) requires a significantly more extended space of ^8B^* excitation energies (up to at least 8-10 MeV) than was needed for the center-of-mass cross section calculations, and the higher-order continuum-state couplings suppress the E1/E2 interference asymmetry that dominates the first-order (DWBA) predictions.

## Method

The coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) method was used to solve the three-body ^7Be + p + ^58Ni system, constructing continuum bin states for ^8B^* excitations with relative orbital angular momenta ℓ ≤ 3 (up to f_{7/2}) and maximum relative energy ℰ_max = 10 MeV. The coupled channels code FRESCO generated two-body inelastic scattering amplitudes, which were then interpolated and combined with the c+v final-state wave functions to construct three-body breakup T-matrices, from which triple differential cross sections were computed and integrated over proton angles and detector solid angles to compare with inclusive ^7Be fragment measurements.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: E_lab = 25.8 MeV for ^8B on ^58Ni target
- Continuum bins extended to maximum relative energy ℰ_max = 10 MeV for each J_p' state
- 32 bins used for s_{1/2} continuum; 16 bins for each other J_p' state
- Partial waves up to L_max = 1000 and radii R up to R_coup = 500 fm for projectile-target relative motion
- Continuum bins calculated using radii r ≤ r_bin = 60 fm
- Potential multipoles up to q ≤ 3 required for convergence (q ≤ 4 and ℓ=4 contributions shown to be small)
- Becchetti-Greenlees proton-^58Ni potential: W = 12 MeV, r_W = 1.32 fm, a_W = 0.534 fm at 3 MeV
- VG (van Oers-Greenlees) proton potential: W = 3.5 MeV, r_W = 1.23 fm, a_W = 1.2 fm
- Proton separation energy from ^59Cu(g.s.): S_p = 3.42 MeV
- Calculated proton energy distributions peak at E_p ≈ 3.8 MeV with width Γ ≈ 4 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier CDCC calculations by Nunes et al. (1996) and Esbensen and Bertsch that had shown the importance of higher-order breakup couplings for the ^8B^* center-of-mass cross section angular distribution, but had not addressed the three-body observables. The calculations are compared with new experimental data from the University of Notre Dame facility (ND1, ND2) for ^7Be fragment angular and energy distributions at E_lab = 25.8 MeV. The results contradict the isotropic approximation used by Shyam et al. and demonstrate that full three-body kinematics with exact angular dependence is necessary. The work enables future coincidence experiments with radioactive beams to be interpreted using fully exclusive three-body calculations rather than approximate transformations to the center-of-mass frame.
