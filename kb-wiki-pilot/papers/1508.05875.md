# MCNP6 simulation of light and medium nuclei fragmentation at intermediate energies

> Fragmentation reactions induced on light and medium nuclei by protons and light nuclei of energies around 1 GeV/nucleon and below are studied with the Los Alamos transport code MCNP6 and with its CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators. CEM and LAQGSM assume that intermediate-energy fragmentation reactions on light nuclei occur generally in two stages. The first stage is the intranuclear cascade (INC), followed by the second, Fermi breakup disintegration of light excited residual nuclei produced after the INC. CEM and LAQGSM account also for coalescence of light fragments (complex particles) up to 4He from energetic nucleons emitted during INC. We investigate the validity and performance of MCNP6, CEM, and LAQGSM in simulating fragmentation reactions at intermediate energies and discuss possible ways of further improving these codes

## Key claim

MCNP6 using CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 simulates fragmentation reactions on light and medium-light nuclei at intermediate energies well, in satisfactory agreement with experimental data; however, MCNP6 is not yet ready to predict heavy fragments from reactions with heavier nuclei having mass numbers around 100.

## Method

The authors used the Los Alamos transport code MCNP6 with its CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators to simulate fragmentation reactions induced by protons and light nuclei on light and medium targets at energies around 1 GeV/nucleon and below. Both CEM and LAQGSM assume a two-stage process for light nuclei: an intranuclear cascade (INC) followed by Fermi breakup disintegration of light excited residual nuclei, with coalescence of light fragments up to 4He from emitted cascade nucleons. The newer "F" versions of the codes extend preequilibrium emission to light fragments heavier than 4He up to 28Mg and coalescence up to A=7 (CEM03.03F) or A=12 (LAQGSM03.03F).

## Key numbers

- Excitation function for 14C production from p + 16O calculated with CEM03.03 using standard Fermi breakup model (AFermi = 12) and with cut-off values for AFermi of 16 and 14, compared with experimental data from the T16 Lib compilation
- 6Li spectra at 20, 45, 60, 90, and 110 degrees from experiments by Machner et al. compared with CEM03.03 and CEM03.03F calculations
- Triton spectra at 45, 60, 90, and 130 degrees from 800 MeV/nucleon 20Ne + 20Ne compared with LAQGSM03.03 using standard coalescence model (p0 = 0.108 GeV/c for t and 3He) and modified p0 values labeled "coal1" and "coal2"
- 6Li production at 60 degrees from 480 MeV p + natAg compared with standard CEM03.03, CEM03.03F without coalescence expansion, and CEM03.03F with coalescence expansion
- 4He spectra at 35 degrees from 1.2, 1.9, and 2.5 GeV p + 12C measured by the PISA collaboration compared with MCNP6 using CEM03.03
- Cross sections for 48Ca fragmentation on 9Be at 140 MeV/nucleon compared with LAQGSM03.03 predictions

## Context

This work extends previous validation and verification of MCNP6 and its CEM and LAQGSM event generators against a large variety of nuclear reactions, specifically testing fragmentation of light nuclei at intermediate energies as reported in Ref. NIMA2014. The paper identifies a gap in current modeling: for nuclei with mass numbers around 100, the fragments are too light for conventional fission models, too heavy for spallation residues, and too heavy for standard evaporation, preequilibrium, or coalescence models. The authors propose two future improvements: merging CEM and LAQGSM with the GEMINI sequential-binary-decay code by Charity et al. (as done previously in "G" versions) or implementing the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) by Botvina et al. (as done in "S" versions), neither of which has yet been incorporated into MCNP6.
