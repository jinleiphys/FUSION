# Modelling incomplete fusion dynamics of weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies

> The classical dynamical model for reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies is developed further. It allows a quantitative study of the role and importance of incomplete fusion dynamics in asymptotic observables, such as the population of high-spin states in reaction products as well as the angular distribution of direct alpha-production. Model calculations indicate that incomplete fusion is an effective mechanism for populating high-spin states, and its contribution to the direct alpha production yield diminishes with decreasing energy towards the Coulomb barrier. It also becomes notably separated in angles from the contribution of no-capture breakup events. This should facilitate the experimental disentanglement of these competing reaction processes.

## Key claim

The classical dynamical model for reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies is extended to predict asymptotic observables such as angular distributions of direct alpha-production and spin distributions of incomplete fusion products, but the calculations are performed for a simplified test case (pseudo-8Be projectile) and do not aim to adjust any measurements.

## Method

The model uses classical equations of motion for a two-body projectile (treated as a weakly-bound cluster of two fragments) interacting with a target nucleus, with breakup probability encoded empirically as an exponential function of the distance of closest approach. Breakup events are Monte Carlo sampled for each projectile angular momentum, and the resulting fragments are propagated in time to determine whether they undergo no-capture breakup (NCBU), incomplete fusion (ICF), or complete fusion (CF). The new development adds time propagation of the surviving breakup fragment and the ICF product after ICF formation, enabling calculation of their asymptotic angular distributions and spin/excitation energy distributions.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier for the 8Be + 208Pb s-wave is 39.9 MeV
- Breakup function parameters: A = 5.98 x 10^3, alpha = 0.85 fm^-1
- At E0 = 45 MeV: ICF cross section = 84.7 mb, NCBU cross section = 238.8 mb, CF cross section = 149.2 mb (with only 1.5% of CF following 8Be breakup)
- At E0 = 65 MeV: ICF cross section = 273.6 mb, NCBU cross section = 259.3 mb, CF cross section = 981.5 mb (with 41% of CF following 8Be breakup)
- Woods-Saxon potential for alpha + 212Po: (V, r, a) = (33.98 MeV, 1.48 fm, 0.63 fm) from Broglia-Winther parametrization

## Context

This work extends the classical dynamical model of Diaz-Torres et al. (2007, Phys. Rev. Lett. 98, 152701) by adding time propagation of ICF products and surviving fragments, enabling calculation of asymptotic observables. It addresses limitations of quantum models like continuum-discretised coupled channels (CDCC), which cannot calculate integrated ICF and CF cross sections unambiguously. The model is motivated by experimental efforts to disentangle breakup and competing reaction mechanisms (Souza et al. 2009, Rafiei et al. 2010) and enables predictions for particle-gamma coincidence experiments and gamma-ray spectroscopy applications.
