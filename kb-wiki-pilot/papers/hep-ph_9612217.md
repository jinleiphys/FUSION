# A Quantitative Analysis of Charmonium Suppression in Nuclear Collisions

> Data from J/psi and psi' production in p-A collisions are used to determine the cross section for absorption of pre-resonance charmonium in nuclear matter. The J/psi suppression in O-Cu, O-U and S-U collisions is fully reproduced by the corresponding nuclear absorption, while Pb-Pb collisions show an additional suppression increasing with centrality. We study the onset of this change in terms of hadronic comover interactions and conclude that so far no conventional hadronic description can consistently account for all data. Deconfinement, starting at a critical point determined by central S-U collisions, is in accord with the observed suppression pattern.

## Key claim

The paper claims that J/ψ suppression in O-Cu, O-U, and S-U collisions is fully explained by nuclear absorption of a pre-resonance charmonium state with cross section σ_abs = 7.3 ± 0.6 mb, while Pb-Pb collisions show an additional "anomalous" suppression increasing with centrality that cannot be consistently described by any conventional hadronic comover interaction model.

## Method

The authors used Glauber theory to model pre-resonance charmonium absorption in p-A collisions, determining the absorption cross section σ_abs from experimental survival probabilities. They then applied this same nuclear absorption formalism to nucleus-nucleus collisions (O-Cu, O-U, S-U, Pb-Pb), establishing the relation between impact parameter b, transverse energy E_T, and number of wounded nucleons N_w. Finally, they tested whether hadronic comover interactions could explain the additional suppression observed in Pb-Pb and ψ' data, using the ψ' suppression in S-U collisions to empirically determine comover model parameters.

## Key numbers

- σ_abs = 7.3 ± 0.6 mb for pre-resonance charmonium absorption in nuclear matter (from p-A data with p-D reference)
- σ_abs = 7.0 ± 1.5 mb from nuclear target ratios (pA/pC) at 95% confidence level
- J/ψ survival probability in S-U collisions: S_exp = 0.46 ± 0.05 (integrated), S_Gl = 0.49 ± 0.03
- J/ψ survival probability in Pb-Pb collisions: S_exp = 0.27 ± 0.02 (integrated), S_Gl = 0.39 ± 0.03
- ψ'/(J/ψ) ratio in nucleon-nucleon interactions: (1.81 ± 0.38) × 10^{-2}, independent of c.m.s. energy from 20 to 2000 GeV
- α_ψ' - α_ψ = 0.00 ± 0.02 from p-A data, ruling out variations of more than 10% between p-p and p-U
- For S-U collisions at highest E_T (82 GeV): S_ψ'(exp) = 0.09 ± 0.02 versus S_Gl = 0.43 ± 0.03
- For Pb-Pb collisions at highest E_T (149 GeV): S_ψ(exp) = 0.20 ± 0.02 versus S_Gl = 0.35 ± 0.03

## Context

This work extends the charmonium suppression analysis of NA38 and NA50 collaborations, using the pre-resonance absorption framework developed by Kharzeev and Satz (KS6) and the wounded nucleon model of Bialas. It contradicts earlier attempts to explain all suppression via hadronic comover interactions (Blaizot, Cassing), showing that no conventional hadronic description can consistently account for both S-U and Pb-Pb data. The paper enables the interpretation that deconfinement, starting at a critical point just above central S-U collisions, is in accord with the observed suppression pattern, providing a quantitative baseline for future quark-gluon plasma searches at RHIC and LHC.
