# Exploring the properties of the phases of QCD matter - research opportunities and priorities for the next decade

> This document provides a summary of the discussions during the recent joint QCD Town Meeting at Temple University of the status of and future plans for the research program of the relativistic heavy-ion community. A list of compelling questions is formulated, and a number of recommendations outlining the greatest research opportunities and detailing the research priorities of the heavy-ion community, voted on and unanimously approved at the Town Meeting, are presented. They are supported by a broad discussion of the underlying physics and its relation to other subfields. Areas of overlapping interests with the "QCD and Hadron Structure" ("cold QCD") subcommunity, in particular the recommendation for the future construction of an Electron-Ion Collider, are emphasized. The agenda of activities of the "hot QCD" subcommunity at the Town Meeting is attached.

## Key claim

The U.S. QCD community unanimously approved a set of four recommendations for the next decade, with the highest priority being a program to complete the search for the QCD critical point and exploit jet-based multi-scale exploration of the QGP at RHIC and LHC, while the highest priority for future new construction after FRIB is a high-luminosity, high-energy polarized Electron Ion Collider (EIC).

## Method

The document summarizes discussions from the joint QCD Town Meeting at Temple University in September 2014, which brought together the "hot QCD" and "cold QCD" subcommunities (244 registrants total). The community formulated compelling physics questions, voted on and unanimously approved four recommendations, and supported them with a broad discussion of underlying physics and connections to other subfields, including the agenda of activities from the parallel and joint sessions.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s) of the QGP determined to within less than a factor of 2, with present uncertainty conservatively estimated as +/-50%
- Transverse momentum diffusion parameter (hat q/T^3) constrained to within a factor of 2-3, representing an order-of-magnitude improvement in precision
- Gluons carry approximately 20-30% of the proton's helicity, similar to the contribution from quarks and anti-quarks
- EIC design aims for variable center-of-mass energies from ~20 to ~100 GeV, upgradable to ~140 GeV
- EIC design aims for high collision luminosity of ~10^33 to 10^34 cm^-2 sec^-1
- EIC design aims for polarized (~70%) beams of electrons, protons and light nuclei
- Flow coefficients measured up to the 7th or 8th multipole in heavy-ion collisions
- 244 registrants at the Town Meeting (108 from hot QCD, 136 from cold QCD), about 10% stronger than the corresponding 2007 meeting

## Context

This document extends the 2007 Nuclear Physics Long Range Plan, which designated the EIC as embodying the vision for reaching the next QCD frontier, and responds to the 2013 NSAC Subcommittee report declaring an EIC "absolutely essential." It builds on discoveries from RHIC (which established the QGP as a strongly coupled, almost perfect liquid) and the LHC heavy-ion program, and incorporates results from the JET Collaboration and other Topical Collaborations in Nuclear Theory. The recommendations enable a targeted experimental program at RHIC (including sPHENIX and low-energy running) and continued U.S. participation in the LHC heavy-ion program, while the EIC would unite and extend the scientific programs at CEBAF, RHIC and the LHC.
