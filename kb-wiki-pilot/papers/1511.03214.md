# Numerical assessment of post-prior equivalence for inclusive breakup reactions

> We address the problem of the post-prior equivalence in inclusive breakup reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei. The problem is studied within the DWBA model of Ichimura, Austern, Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985)]. The post and prior formulas obtained in this model are briefly recalled, and applied to several breakup reactions induced by deuterons and $^6$Li projectiles, to test their actual numerical equivalence. The different contributions of the prior-form formula are also discussed. A critical comparison with the prior-form distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) model of Udagawa and Tamura [Phys. Rev. C24, 1348 (1981)] is also provided.

## Key claim

The post and prior forms of the inclusive breakup NEB cross section within the IAV model are numerically equivalent when the additional non-orthogonality and interference terms are included, contrary to earlier claims by Udagawa and Tamura that these terms are unphysical and that the IAV model overestimates experimental data.

## Method

The authors applied the post-form and prior-form DWBA formulas of the IAV model to inclusive breakup reactions induced by deuterons on ⁶²Ni at 25.5 MeV and by ⁶Li on ²⁰⁹Bi at 36 MeV. To overcome the marginal convergence of the post-form integrals, the distorted waves of the outgoing fragment were averaged over small momentum bins (down to Δk ≈ 0.04 fm⁻¹), and the resulting converged post-form results were compared with the prior-form calculation decomposed into the UT term, the non-orthogonality term, and the interference term.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶²Ni(d,pX) at E_d = 25.5 MeV, the post-form NEB calculation converged for bin widths Δk ≈ 0.04 fm⁻¹
- The post and prior IAV calculations for the angle-integrated NEB cross section showed very satisfactory agreement, with only small differences attributed to numerical inaccuracies
- The EBU + NEB(IAV) sum reproduced the experimental double differential cross section at θ_p = 20° reasonably well, in contrast to the EBU + NEB(UT) sum which largely underpredicted the data
- For ²⁰⁹Bi(⁶Li,αX) at E = 36 MeV, the post and prior IAV calculations were also compared (results shown in the paper's Figure 2)

## Context

This work extends the IAV model of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985)] and directly addresses the long-standing controversy with the prior-form DWBA model of Udagawa and Tamura [Phys. Rev. C24, 1348 (1981)], who argued that the additional non-orthogonality terms in the IAV prior form are unphysical and that the IAV model overestimates data. The numerical demonstration of post-prior equivalence validates the regularization procedure of Thompson et al. (2011) and supports recent implementations by Carlson et al. (2015), Potel et al. (2015), and Lei and Moro (2015), enabling reliable calculations of inclusive breakup reactions for applications such as surrogate reactions and radioisotope production.
