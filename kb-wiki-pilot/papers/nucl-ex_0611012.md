# Quark-Gluon Matter

> A concise review of the experimental and phenomenological progress in high-energy heavy-ion physics over the past few years is presented. Emphasis is put on measurements at BNL-RHIC and CERN-SPS which provide information on fundamental properties of QCD matter at extreme values of temperature, density and low-x. The new opportunities accessible at the LHC, which may help clarify some of the current open issues, are also outlined.

## Key claim

The paper presents a concise review of experimental and phenomenological progress in high-energy heavy-ion physics, emphasizing that measurements at BNL-RHIC and CERN-SPS provide information on fundamental properties of QCD matter at extreme temperature, density, and low-x, while the LHC may help clarify current open issues.

## Method

The review compares observables measured in heavy-ion (AA) collisions to those in proton-proton (pp, "QCD vacuum") and proton(deuteron)-nucleus (p(d)A, "cold QCD matter") collisions as functions of center-of-mass energy, transverse momentum, rapidity, reaction centrality, and particle type. The nuclear modification factor R_AA is defined as the ratio of AA yields to the binary-collision-scaled pp cross section, quantifying deviations from an incoherent superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions. Hard probes (high-pT hadrons, jets, direct photons, heavy quarks, quarkonia) are emphasized because they originate from partonic scatterings with large momentum transfer, have short production time scales, and can be predicted using perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density for deconfinement: ε_crit ≈ 1 GeV/fm³ from lattice QCD
- Bjorken energy density at RHIC: ε_Bj ≈ 5 GeV/fm³ (at τ₀ = 1 fm/c)
- Bjorken energy density at LHC: ε_Bj ≈ 10 GeV/fm³ (at τ₀ = 1 fm/c)
- Mid-rapidity charged particle multiplicity in central AuAu at √s_NN = 200 GeV: dN_ch/dη|_(η=0) ≈ 700
- Saturation momentum scaling: Q_s² ∝ A^(1/3) x^(-λ) with λ ≈ 0.2–0.3
- Nuclear modification factor for J/ψ and high-pT π⁰ in dAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: maximum deviation from R_dAu = 1 is ~20%
- Hard cross sections in pp at √s = 200 GeV cover 9 orders of magnitude (from 10 mb to 10 pb) and pT up to ~45 GeV/c

## Context

This review extends the extensive experimental program at BNL-RHIC (experiments PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS, BRAHMS) and CERN-SPS (experiment NA60) that began in the early 2000s, building on earlier fixed-target programs at the Bevalac, AGS, and SPS. The work synthesizes results from the comprehensive reviews of the four RHIC experiments and recent NA60 results, focusing on observables that provide direct information on partonic phases of the reaction. The paper enables future comparisons with LHC PbPb collisions and outlines how LHC measurements may clarify open issues such as the equation of state, transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, and the nature of the phase transition.
