# Deuteron Stripping on Nuclei at Intermediate Energies

> A general analytical expression for the double differential cross section of inclusive deuteron stripping reaction on nuclei at intermediate energies of incident particles was obtained in the diffraction approximation. Nucleon-nucleus phases were calculated in the framework of Glauber formalism and making use of the double-folding potential. The exact wave function of deuteron with correct asymptotics at short and long distances between nucleons was used. The calculated angular dependencies of cross sections are in good agreement with corresponding experimental data.

# Deuteron Stripping on Nuclei at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim

The paper derives a general analytical expression for the double differential cross section of inclusive deuteron stripping reactions at intermediate energies, using a Gaussian basis expansion that transforms the previously intractable fivefold integral into a closed-form sum. The calculated angular distributions agree well with experimental data for the ²H(d,n)³He reaction at 787, 858, and 1242 MeV, but only when the exact deuteron wave function with correct asymptotics at both short and long internucleon distances is used; a model Gaussian wave function fails to reproduce the data at larger emission angles.

## Method

The double differential cross section for inclusive deuteron stripping was derived in the diffraction approximation using Glauber formalism, with nucleon-nucleus phases calculated from a double-folding potential. The deuteron wave function was expanded in a basis of 10 Gaussians fitted to the triplet nucleon-nucleon potential, reproducing the experimental binding energy and root-mean-square radius. The nucleon-nucleus profile functions and target density distributions were also expanded in Gaussian basis functions, allowing all integrals in the general stripping formula to be evaluated analytically, yielding a closed-form expression for the cross section as a function of neutron emission angle and energy.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron wave function expanded in N=10 Gaussian basis functions, fitted to the triplet potential V(r)=3720.0 exp[-(r/0.488)²] - 528.59 exp[-(r/0.976)²] (energies in MeV, distances in fm)
- Target density expansion parameter: a_N² = r_0²/ln2 with r_0² = 0.65 fm²
- Normalization factor N_W for the imaginary part of double-folding potential: 0.68 at T=787 MeV, 0.49 at 858 MeV, and 0.15 at 1242 MeV
- Model Gaussian wave function parameter ξ = 0.049 fm⁻² (reproducing experimental deuteron rms radius)
- Target density expansion used K=10 Gaussian basis functions with a_Tj² = R_rms²/j

## Context

This work extends the original Serber model of deuteron stripping (Serber, Phys. Rev. 72, 1008, 1947) and the diffraction formalism of Akhiezer and Sitenko (Sov. Phys. JETP 32, 1040, 1957) by removing the need for simplifying assumptions such as opaque non-diffuse nuclei or deuteron radius much smaller than target radius. The Gaussian expansion technique, previously applied to bound states (Varga and Suzuki, Phys. Rev. C 52, 2885, 1995) and charge density parametrization (De Vries et al., At. Data Nucl. Data Tables 36, 495, 1987), enables analytical calculation of the cross section. The results enable future analysis of stripping, pickup, and breakup reactions for light and heavy ions, and can be generalized to include spin-orbit interactions for polarization studies.
