# Post-prior equivalence for transfer reactions with complex potentials

> In this paper, we address the problem of the post-prior equivalence in the calculation of inclusive breakup and transfer cross sections. For that, we employ the model proposed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C 32, 431 (1985)], conveniently generalized to include the part of the cross section corresponding the transfer to bound states. We pay particular attention to the case in which the unobserved particle is left in a bound state of the residual nucleus, in which case the theory prescribes the use of a complex potential, responsible for the spreading width of the populated single-particle states. We see that the introduction of this complex potential gives rise to an additional term in the prior cross section formula, not present in the usual case of real binding potentials. The equivalence is numerically tested for reaction induced by deuterons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the post-prior equivalence for transfer reactions holds even when complex potentials are used to describe bound states of the residual nucleus, but only if the IAV post-form formula is used; the UT prior-form formula requires two additional non-orthogonality and interference terms to match the post-form result, contrary to the case of real binding potentials where these terms vanish.

## Method

The authors generalize the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model to include transfer to bound states by extending the complex optical potential for the x+A system to negative energies, and derive the relation between the post-form and prior-form inclusive breakup cross sections. They then numerically test the post-prior equivalence for the reaction 58Ni(d,pX) at Ed=80 MeV, comparing the IAV post-form calculation with the UT prior-form calculation and the sum of the UT term plus the non-orthogonality (NO) and interference (IN) terms.

## Key numbers

- Reaction studied: 58Ni(d,pX) at Ed(lab)=80 MeV
- The neutron-58Ni potential was extrapolated to negative energies by fixing its real and imaginary parts to their values at En=1 MeV for En ≤ 1 MeV
- When the imaginary part of the neutron-58Ni potential was reduced by a factor of 10, the IAV and UT formulas showed almost perfect agreement for En<0, recovering the usual post-prior equivalence for transfer reactions
- For constant Wx, the energy distribution of the double differential cross section follows a Breit-Wigner (Lorentzian) shape with width Γn = ⟨φn|Wx|φn⟩ = Wx

## Context

This work extends the IAV model for inclusive breakup reactions, previously applied only to unbound x+A states (Ex>0) in Ref. [Jin15b], to bound states (Ex<0) where the process becomes a transfer reaction. It builds on the extension of complex potentials to negative energies first proposed by Udagawa, Lee and Tamura [Udagawa86] and recently reexamined by Potel et al. [Potel:2015eqa], and clarifies the relation between the post-form IAV model and prior-form UT model for transfer reactions with complex binding potentials, which had not been established previously. The results enable the use of dispersive optical potentials to describe bound states of the residual system in inclusive breakup calculations.
