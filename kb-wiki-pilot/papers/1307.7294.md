# Toward a microscopic description of reactions involving exotic nuclei

> We propose an extension of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method, where the projectile is described by a microscopic cluster model. This microscopic generalization (MCDCC) only relies on nucleon-target interactions, and therefore presents an important predictive power. Core excitations can be included without any further parameter. As an example we investigate the $\lipb$ elastic scattering at $E_{lab}=27$ and 35 MeV. The $^7$Li nucleus is known to present an $α+t$ cluster structure, and is well described by the Resonating Group Method. An excellent agreement is obtained for the $\lipb$ elastic cross sections, provided that breakup channels are properly included. We also present an application to inelastic scattering, and discuss future applications of the MCDCC.

## Key claim

The authors propose a microscopic generalization of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (MCDCC) method that replaces the usual phenomenological projectile-target potentials with nucleon-target interactions, achieving predictive power without adjustable parameters, but only for projectiles whose cluster structure is well described by the Resonating Group Method (RGM) or Generator Coordinate Method (GCM).

## Method

The projectile is described by a microscopic cluster model using the Resonating Group Method, where the wave function is an antisymmetric product of cluster wave functions (here, alpha + triton for 7Li). The projectile states (bound and continuum) are obtained from a nucleon-nucleon Hamiltonian, and the total projectile-target wave function is expanded over this microscopic basis. The coupled-channel equations are solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, with coupling potentials computed from one-body matrix elements between Slater determinants and nucleon-target optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- 7Li+208Pb elastic scattering at Elab = 27 MeV and 35 MeV, normalized to Rutherford cross section
- 7Li described by alpha + t cluster structure with oscillator parameter b = 1.45 fm
- 20 values of generator coordinate S from 0.8 fm to 16 fm in steps of 0.8 fm
- Minnesota force with admixture parameter u = 1.011 and spin-orbit amplitude S0 = 20.0 MeV.fm^5
- Partial waves up to jmax = 7/2 (both parities) included
- Continuum states up to 20 MeV included in the basis
- B(E1, 3/2- -> 1/2-) = 7.5 e^2.fm^4 (experiment: 8.3 +/- 0.5 e^2.fm^4)
- Ground-state quadrupole moment = -37.0 e.mb (experiment: -40.6 +/- 0.8 e.mb)
- 7/2- resonance at Ecm = 2.18 MeV and 5/2- resonance at Ecm = 4.13 MeV
- Channel radius of 30 fm and 120 basis functions used for numerical stability

## Context

This work extends the standard CDCC method (originally developed for deuteron-induced reactions by Rawitscher in 1974) by replacing the phenomenological two-body projectile description with a microscopic cluster model from the RGM/GCM framework. It addresses limitations of traditional CDCC where optical potentials for projectile constituents are often unknown and core excitations are difficult to include. The approach enables future applications to exotic nuclei such as 11Be, 17C, and Borromean nuclei (6He, 11Li), as well as to breakup and fusion reactions.
