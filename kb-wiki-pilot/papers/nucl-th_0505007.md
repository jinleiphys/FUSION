# Determination of S17 from 8B breakup by means of the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels

> The astrophysical factor for 7Be(p,γ)8B at zero energy, S17(0), is determined from an analysis of 208Pb(8B, p+7Be)208Pb at 52 MeV/nucleon by means of the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) taking account of all nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes. The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method is used to extract S17(0) from the calculated breakup-cross-section. The main result of the present paper is S17(0)=20.9 +2.0/-1.9 eV b. The error consists of 8.4% experimental systematic error and the error due to the ambiguity in the s-wave p-7Be scattering length. This value of S17(0) differs from the one extracted with the first-order perturbation theory including Coulomb breakup by dipole transitions: 18.9 +/- 1.8 eV b. It turns out that the difference is due to the inclusion of the nuclear and Coulomb-quadrupole transitions and multi-step processes of all-order in the present work. The p-7Be interaction potential used in the CDCC calculation is also used in the ANC analysis of 7Be(p,γ)8B. The value of S17(0)=21.7 +0.62/-0.55 eV b obtained is consistent with the previous one obtained from a precise measurement of the p-capture reaction cross section extrapolated to zero incident energy, S17(0)=22.1 +/- 0.6 (expt) +/- 0.6 (theo) eV b, where (theo) stands for the error in the extrapolation. Thus, the agreement between the values of S17(0) obtained from direct 7Be(p,γ)8B and indirect 8B-breakup measurements is significantly improved.

## Key claim

The astrophysical factor \(S_{17}(0)\) extracted from \(^{208}\text{Pb}(^8\text{B}, p+^7\text{Be})^{208}\text{Pb}\) at 52 MeV/nucleon using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, which includes all nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes and multi-step effects to all orders, is \(20.9^{+2.0}_{-1.9}\) eV b, differing from the value of \(18.9 \pm 1.8\) eV b obtained with first-order perturbation theory that considered only Coulomb dipole transitions.

## Method

The authors analyzed \(^8\text{B}\) breakup data from the RIKEN experiment at 52 MeV/nucleon on a \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) target using the CDCC method within a \(p+^7\text{Be}+A\) three-body model, including all nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes and the intrinsic spins of \(p\) and \(^7\text{Be}\). They employed the eikonal-CDCC method for large orbital angular momenta and constructed a hybrid scattering amplitude from eikonal and fully quantum-mechanical CDCC results. The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method was then used to extract \(S_{17}(0)\) from the calculated breakup cross section, assuming the reaction is peripheral and the cross section is proportional to the square of the ANC.

## Key numbers

- \(S_{17}(0) = 20.9^{+2.0}_{-1.9}\) eV b from the CDCC analysis of \(^{208}\text{Pb}(^8\text{B}, p+^7\text{Be})^{208}\text{Pb}\) at 52 MeV/nucleon; the error includes 8.4% experimental systematic error and uncertainty from the s-wave \(p\)-\(^7\text{Be}\) scattering length.
- \(S_{17}(0) = 18.9 \pm 1.8\) eV b from first-order perturbation theory including only Coulomb dipole transitions (previous RIKEN analysis).
- \(S_{17}(0) = 21.7^{+0.62}_{-0.55}\) eV b from the ANC analysis of direct \(^7\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^8\text{B}\) using the same \(p\)-\(^7\text{Be}\) potential as in the CDCC calculation.
- \(S_{17}(0) = 22.1 \pm 0.6\) (expt) \(\pm 0.6\) (theo) eV b from a precise direct measurement of the \(p\)-capture reaction cross section extrapolated to zero energy (Junghans et al.).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of \(^8\text{B}\) Coulomb dissociation experiments at RIKEN, MSU, and GSI that used virtual photon theory with only electric dipole transitions, yielding values around 18–19 eV b that disagreed with direct \(^7\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^8\text{B}\) measurements. By including nuclear breakup, Coulomb-quadrupole transitions, and higher-order multi-step processes via CDCC, the authors significantly improve the agreement between indirect breakup and direct capture results, reducing the previous discrepancy of about 15%. This enables more reliable extraction of \(S_{17}(0)\) for solar neutrino phenomenology, where the required accuracy is within 5%.
