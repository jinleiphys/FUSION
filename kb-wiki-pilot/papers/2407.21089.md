# Determination of electron screening potential of 6 Li(p,α)3 He reaction using MultiLayer Perceptron based neural network

> Background: Understanding the nuclear reactions between light charged nuclei at sub-coulomb energy region holds significant importance in several astrophysical processes. Determination of the precise reaction cross-section within the astrophysically important Gamow range is difficult because of electron screening. Various polynomial fits, R-Matrix and Indirect Trojan horse method estimate much higher electron screening energies as compared to the adiabatic limit. Purpose: Obtain the bare astrophysical S-factor of 6 Li(p,α)3 He using Multi-Layer Perceptron based Artificial Neural Network based analysis and extract the electron screening energies. Methods: Experimental S-factor of 6 Li(p,α)3 He, available in literature, are reanalyzed using the Multi-LayerPerceptron based Artificial Neural Network based algorithm to obtain the energy dependent astrophysical S-factor. Bare astrophysical S-factor is also calculated using the same Feed-forward Artificial Neural Network from the data range above 60 keV where the electron screening effect is expected to be negligible. Electron screening potential is then obtained by taking the ratio of total shielded S-factor with the bare S-factor. Results and Conclusions: The electron screening potential obtained from the Present work through the Artificial Neural network based algorithm is found to be 220 eV. The extracted electron screening potential through the present analysis indicates that the Artificial Neural Network might be an alternative tools for estimation the electron screening potential involving light nuclei.

# Determination of electron screening potential of 6Li(p,α)3He reaction using MultiLayer Perceptron based neural network

## Key claim
The electron screening potential for the 6Li(p,α)3He reaction extracted via Multi-Layer Perceptron based Artificial Neural Network analysis is 220 eV, which matches reasonably well with other established techniques like R-Matrix and Trojan Horse Method within quoted uncertainties. The author claims ANN might serve as an alternative tool for estimating electron screening potentials, but notes this is a first application and the method's broader validity requires further testing.

## Method
Experimental S-factor data for 6Li(p,α)3He from eight literature sources (Engstler et al., Gemeinhardt et al., Elwyn et al., Kwon et al., Shinozuka et al., Feidler et al., and Cruz et al.) were extracted from the EXFOR database and reanalyzed using a Multi-Layer Perceptron based Feed-forward Artificial Neural Network with four hidden layers (128, 64, 32, 16 neurons) and ReLU/tanh activation functions. The network was trained on 70% of the data and tested on 30%, using RandomizedSearchCV for hyperparameter optimization with Adam optimizer (learning rate 0.001, decay constants 0.9 and 0.999) over 5000 maximum iterations. Bare astrophysical S-factor was calculated from data above 60 keV where electron screening is negligible, and the electron screening potential was obtained from the ratio of total shielded S-factor to bare S-factor.

## Key numbers
- Electron screening potential Ue = 220 eV (no uncertainty quoted) for 6Li(p,α)3He
- Bare astrophysical S-factor at zero energy Sb(0) = 3.36 ± 0.12 MeV-barn from ANN
- Data range used: center-of-mass energies from 10.7 keV to 927.4 keV
- Training/test split: 70% training, 30% testing
- Adiabatic limit for comparison: Ue = 175 eV
- Previous results for comparison: Engstler et al. Ue = 470 ± 150 eV and 440 ± 150 eV; Cruz et al. Ue = 273 ± 111 eV; Lamia et al. (THM) Ue = 355 ± 100 eV; Spitaleri et al. (R-Matrix) Ue = 290 ± 75 eV

## Context
This work extends previous attempts to determine electron screening potentials for the 6Li(p,α)3He reaction, which have consistently yielded values higher than the adiabatic limit of 175 eV. Prior approaches include polynomial fits by Engstler et al. (1992) and Cruz et al. (2008), R-Matrix analysis by Angulo et al. (1998) and Spitaleri et al. (2023), and indirect Trojan Horse Method by Lamia et al. (2013) and Tumino et al. (2003). The ANN result of 220 eV is lower than most previous extractions and closer to the adiabatic limit, though the author notes the value matches other methods within uncertainties. This work enables future application of machine learning techniques to electron screening extraction for other light-ion reactions of astrophysical interest.
