# A microscopic approach to $^{3}$He scattering

> We propose a practical folding model to describe $^{3}$He elastic scattering. In the model, $^{3}$He optical potentials are constructed by making the folding procedure twice. First the nucleon-target potential is evaluated by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix with the target density and localizing the nonlocal folding potential with the Brieva--Rook method, and second the resulting local nucleon-target potential is folded with the $^{3}$He density. This double single-folding model well describes $^{3}$He elastic scattering from $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb targets in a wide incident-energy range from 30 MeV/nucleon to 150 MeV/nucleon with no adjustable parameter. Spin-orbit force effects on differential cross sections are found to be appreciable only at higher incident energies such as 150 MeV/nucleon. Three-nucleon breakup effects of $^{3}$He are investigated with the continuum discretized coupled-channels method and are found to be appreciable only at lower incident energies around 40 MeV/nucleon. Effects of knock-on exchange processes are also analyzed.

## Key claim

The double single-folding (DSF) model, which first constructs a localized nucleon-target potential by folding the Melbourne g-matrix with the target density using the Brieva-Rook method and then folds that potential with the 3He density, describes 3He elastic scattering from 58Ni and 208Pb targets across 30–150 MeV/nucleon with no adjustable parameter, though the model's treatment of knock-on exchange processes is only approximate because it does not account for the change of the projectile density due to exchange.

## Method

The authors constructed 3He optical potentials by a double folding procedure: first evaluating the nucleon-target potential by folding the Melbourne g-matrix with the target density and localizing the nonlocal folding potential using the Brieva-Rook method, then folding the resulting local nucleon-target potential with the 3He density. Three-nucleon breakup effects of 3He were investigated using the continuum discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with a model space including 0+ and 2+ breakup states up to 20 MeV. The target densities were calculated with the spherical Hartree-Fock method using the Gogny-D1S interaction.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range tested: 30–150 MeV/nucleon for 3He scattering from 58Ni and 208Pb targets
- For 3He+208Pb scattering at 72.3 MeV/nucleon, the difference between g-matrices constructed from Melbourne (Bonn-B) and CD-Bonn nuclear forces was only 5 mb in total reaction cross section
- Spin-orbit force effects on differential cross sections are appreciable only at higher incident energies such as 150 MeV/nucleon
- Three-nucleon breakup effects are appreciable only at lower incident energies around 40 MeV/nucleon
- For CDCC calculations, 35 breakup states below 20 MeV were taken for each of 0+ and 2+ states

## Context

This work extends the model Hamiltonian approach previously applied successfully to deuteron and 4He scattering, testing it for 3He scattering to understand nucleus-nucleus scattering systematically. The DSF model yields better agreement with experimental data than the double-folding model with frozen-density approximation (DF-FDA) for total reaction cross sections and differential cross sections, particularly at lower and higher incident energies. The approach enables parameter-free predictions for 3He scattering and can be extended to treat projectile breakup with CDCC, which is important for fragile projectiles like 3He at lower energies.
