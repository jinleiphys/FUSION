# Reaction dynamics of weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies: impact of incomplete fusion on the angular distribution of direct alpha-production

> The classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup for nuclear collision dynamics of weakly-bound nuclei is further developed. It allows a quantitative study of the importance of incomplete fusion dynamics in the angular distribution of direct alpha-production. Model calculations indicate that the incomplete fusion contribution diminishes with decreasing energy towards the Coulomb barrier, notably separating in angles from the contribution of no-capture breakup events. This should facilitate the experimental disentanglement of these competing reaction processes.

# Reaction dynamics of weakly-bound nuclei at near-barrier energies: impact of incomplete fusion on the angular distribution of direct alpha-production

## Key claim
The classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup predicts that the incomplete fusion (ICF) contribution to direct alpha-production diminishes with decreasing energy toward the Coulomb barrier, and its angular centroid separates substantially from that of no-capture breakup (NCBU) events, which should facilitate experimental disentanglement of these competing reaction processes. The model calculations are performed for the test reaction 8Be + 208Pb, but the results are expected to represent those of the 9Be + 208Pb reaction based on empirical adjustments of the breakup probability density.

## Method
The three-dimensional classical dynamical reaction model propagates the trajectories of projectile fragments after stochastic breakup, determined by Monte Carlo sampling of the projectile's internal energy, angular momentum, and fragment separation at the breakup point. The local breakup probability density is taken to have an exponential form, P^L_BU(R) proportional to exp(-alpha R), with parameters A = 5.98 x 10^3 and alpha = 0.85 fm^-1, deduced from sub-barrier breakup measurements of 9Be + 208Pb. For ICF events, the three-body propagation is switched to two-body propagation when the captured fragment reaches the target radius, allowing calculation of the asymptotic angular distribution of the surviving fragment and the ICF product.

## Key numbers
- s-wave Coulomb barrier for 8Be + 208Pb: 39.9 MeV
- Breakup probability density parameters: A = 5.98 x 10^3, alpha = 0.85 fm^-1
- Woods-Saxon potential for alpha + 212Po: (V, r, a) = (33.98 MeV, 1.48 fm, 0.63 fm)
- At E0 = 45 MeV: sigma_ICF = 84.7 mb, sigma_NCBU = 238.84 mb, sigma_CF = 149.2 mb
- At E0 = 55 MeV: sigma_ICF = 252.3 mb, sigma_NCBU = 255.1 mb, sigma_CF = 624.6 mb
- At E0 = 65 MeV: sigma_ICF = 273.6 mb, sigma_NCBU = 259.3 mb, sigma_CF = 981.5 mb

## Context
This work extends the classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup (Diaz-Torres et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 98, 152701, 2007) by incorporating time propagation of the surviving breakup fragment and the ICF product, enabling calculation of asymptotic angular distributions. It addresses limitations of quantum continuum-discretised coupled channels (CDCC) approaches, which cannot calculate integrated ICF and CF cross sections unambiguously. The results enable experimental disentanglement of competing reaction mechanisms from inclusive and exclusive measurements such as total alpha-production yields (Souza et al., Nucl. Phys. A 821, 36, 2009), and have applications to gamma-ray spectroscopy (Dracoulis et al., J. Phys. G 23, 1191, 1997; Gasques et al., Phys. Rev. C 74, 064615, 2006).
