# Establishing a theory for deuteron induced surrogate reactions

> The goal of this work is to establish on firm grounds a theory for deuteron induced neutron capture reactions. This includes formulating elastic and inelastic breakup in a consistent manner. We describe this process both in post and prior form distorted wave Born approximation, and we apply our method to the 53Nb(d,p)X at Ed =15 MeV and 25 MeV, obtaining a good description of the data. We look at the various partial wave contributions, as well as elastic versus inelastic contributions. We also connect our formulation with transfer to neutron bound states.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a consistent theoretical framework for deuteron-induced surrogate reactions by demonstrating that the post-form (Austern-Vincent) and prior-form (Udagawa-Tamura) distorted wave Born approximation formulations are equivalent when no further approximations are made, resolving a long-standing controversy through the proper inclusion of a non-orthogonality term that arises naturally in the prior formulation.

## Method

The authors derived inclusive breakup cross sections for the A(d,p)X reaction in both post and prior form DWBA, using a spectator approximation for the proton where the proton-target interaction is replaced by an optical potential. They applied the formalism to the 93Nb(d,p)X reaction at Ed=15 MeV and 25.5 MeV, decomposing the cross section into elastic breakup (EB) and non-elastic breakup (NEB) contributions, and connected the formalism to neutron transfer to bound states through a Green's function approach.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: Ed=15 MeV and Ed=25.5 MeV for 93Nb(d,p)X
- Deuteron ground state wavefunction generated with Woods-Saxon potential: radius Rd=0.4 fm, diffusivity ad=0.6 fm
- Zero-range constant D0 = -122.5 MeV·fm^(3/2)
- Maximum partial wave lp=15 for Ed=15 MeV and lp=20 for Ed=25.5 MeV
- Neutron final states with l≥8 contribute very little
- Real part of neutron optical potential set to V=50.3 MeV across the whole energy range
- Surface imaginary part WD of Koning-Delaroche potential not allowed below 4 MeV (matching experimental energy resolution)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundations laid by Kerman and McVoy (1979), Udagawa and Tamura (1980), and Austern and Vincent (1981), resolving the decade-long controversy between the post and prior formulations by showing the non-orthogonality term is significant and essential. The framework enables reliable calculations for surrogate neutron capture reactions, which are critical for astrophysical nucleosynthesis and stewardship science, and calls for elastic breakup measurements to further constrain the model.
