# Nuclear astrophysics from direct reactions

> Accurate nuclear reaction rates are needed for primordial nucleosynthesis and hydrostatic burning in stars. The relevant reactions are extremely difficult to measure directly in the laboratory at the small astrophysical energies. In recent years direct reactions have been developed and applied to extract low-energy astrophysical S-factors. These methods require a combination of new experimental techniques and theoretical efforts, which are the subject of this presentation.

## Key claim

Direct reactions (transfer, Coulomb dissociation, knockout, charge-exchange) can extract low-energy astrophysical S-factors without directly measuring the extremely small cross sections at stellar energies, but each method carries specific caveats: the Trojan Horse technique cancels the Coulomb barrier penetration effect only for the breakup channel, the ANC method works only for peripheral reactions, and the Coulomb dissociation method requires careful treatment of higher-order effects and nuclear breakup contributions.

## Method

The paper reviews several direct reaction techniques applied to nuclear astrophysics: (1) the Trojan Horse method uses breakup reactions A+a → b+c+B where the Fermi momentum of particle x inside a=(b+x) compensates the projectile velocity, inducing the low-energy reaction A+x=B+c at vanishing relative energy; (2) the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) technique extracts the asymptotic normalization of overlap integrals from peripheral transfer reactions, which directly determines radiative capture cross sections; (3) Coulomb dissociation at intermediate energies (50-100 MeV/nucleon) relates the breakup cross section to photodisintegration via first-order perturbation theory, with time reversal yielding the radiative capture cross section; (4) charge-exchange reactions (p,n), (³He,t), and heavy-ion reactions provide Gamow-Teller matrix elements needed for supernova collapse simulations.

## Key numbers

- The stopping power in proton+hydrogen collisions is proportional to v^α with α=1.35, while Andersen-Ziegler table extrapolations predict a smaller α value
- For the reaction ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B, the S-factor is given by S₁₇(E) = (22.109 eV·b) × (1+5.30E+1.65E²+0.857E³)/(1+E/0.1375), where E is the relative energy in MeV of p+⁷Be in their center-of-mass
- ⁸B has a small proton separation energy of approximately 140 keV
- In the r-process, the most neutron-rich isotopes have lifetimes of 10⁻² to 10⁻¹ seconds
- For the ³²Cl(p,γ)³³Ar reaction, 2 orders of magnitude improvement in level energy uncertainty resulted in 3 orders of magnitude improvement in the calculated rate uncertainty

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on direct reactions for nuclear astrophysics, building on the Coulomb dissociation method proposed by Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel (1986), the ANC technique by Mukhamedzhanov and Timofeyuk (1990), and the Trojan Horse method by Baur (1986). It addresses the long-standing laboratory electron screening discrepancy (factor of two disagreement with theory) and proposes stopping power mechanisms as a possible resolution. The work enables future radioactive beam facilities (R3B and ELISE at FAIR) to study nuclei far from stability, and demonstrates that No-Core-Shell-Model calculations combined with the Resonating Group Method can reconcile nuclear structure with reaction calculations for astrophysical S-factors.
