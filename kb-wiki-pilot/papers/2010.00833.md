# Semi-classical approaches to heavy-ion reactions: fusion, rainbow, and glory

> A semi-classical approximation has been a powerful tool in understanding the dynamics of low-energy heavy-ion reactions. Here we discuss two topics in this regard, for which Mahir Hussein was a world leading pioneer. The first topic is heavy-ion fusion reactions of neutron-rich nuclei, in which the breakup process of the projectile nucleus plays a crucial role. The second is rainbow and glory scattering, for which characteristic oscillatory patterns in differential cross sections can be well understood in terms of intereferences among several semi-classical trajectories.

## Key claim

The paper reviews semi-classical approaches to heavy-ion reactions, focusing on two topics where Mahir Hussein was a pioneer: fusion of neutron-rich nuclei (where breakup of the projectile plays a crucial role) and rainbow/glory scattering (where oscillatory patterns in differential cross sections arise from interference among semi-classical trajectories). The abstract's claim that semi-classical approximation is "a powerful tool" is hedged by the paper's detailed discussion of limitations, including that classical models cannot describe sub-barrier fusion and that early calculations relied on "very drastic approximations."

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to heavy-ion fusion reactions of weakly bound nuclei, including the semi-classical model of Hussein et al. that estimates fusion cross sections using transmission probabilities modified by coupling strength F and survival probabilities against prompt breakup. For elastic scattering, the paper analyzes deflection functions Θ(λ) derived from scattering phase shifts, identifying rainbow scattering (where dΘ/dλ=0) and glory scattering (where Θ=0) as caustics where many angular momenta contribute coherently. The review covers continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) methods, classical three-body models, and the sum-of-differences (SOD) method for extracting nuclear scattering amplitudes.

## Context

This review extends the pioneering work of Mahir Hussein on semi-classical methods in heavy-ion reactions, as summarized in the textbook by Canto and Hussein (CaH13). The fusion section builds on Hussein et al.'s (HPC92) first discussion of breakup effects in fusion of weakly bound nuclei, and contrasts early approximations with modern CDCC-based methods (SYK83, SYK86, AIK87) and classical three-body models (DHH02, HDH04). The scattering section extends the concepts of rainbow and glory scattering from atomic and molecular physics to nuclear reactions, introducing the novel concept of "glory in the shadow of rainbow" from Hussein's work (UPH98, UPH99), and connects to the sum-of-differences method for extracting fusion cross sections from quasi-elastic scattering (HaR15).
