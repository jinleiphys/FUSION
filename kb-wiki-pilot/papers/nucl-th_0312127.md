# Three-body model for the complete fusion of a two-cluster composite projectile with a heavy target

> The purpose of this work is to show that within a three-body description, the complete fusion process and the target excitation by the projectile can be taken into account by introducing a three-body optical potential in the formalism. We give a schematic description of such a potential and points to ways of testing the validity of the CDCC.

# Three-body model for the complete fusion of a two-cluster composite projectile with a heavy target

## Key claim
The authors argue that a consistent three-body description of complete fusion requires introducing an effective three-body optical potential, which accounts for processes missing when only two-body optical potentials are used. They present a schematic form of this potential but note that its actual calculation remains a challenge, and that such a description would allow assessment of the CDCC model, which consistently fails to account for incomplete fusion.

## Method
The authors work within a Feshbach projection operator framework, starting from a three-body model of a projectile composed of two clusters (b and x) interacting with a target A. They derive expressions for incomplete fusion cross sections using two-body optical potentials U_xA and U_bA, then construct a candidate three-body optical potential U_Axb that allows the spectator particle to interact with the intermediate composite system, thereby including complete fusion. The complete fusion cross section is expressed as proportional to the expectation value of the imaginary part of this three-body potential, W_3B, in the full three-body wave function.

## Key numbers
- Incomplete fusion cross section for participant x is given by σ_IF = ∫ dΩ_b d²σ/(dΩ_b dE_b), where the double differential cross section involves an integral over r_x of the three-body wave function weighted by W_xA
- In the Glauber limit, σ_IF^(x) = (π/k²) Σ_lx (2l_x+1) ⟨T_x(l_x)(1-T_b(l_b))⟩
- Complete fusion cross section is given by σ_CF ∝ ⟨Ψ^(+)|W_3B|Ψ^(+)⟩

## Context
This work extends earlier formalisms by Canto et al. (1998) that described incomplete fusion using two-body optical potentials, and by Hussein, Frederico and Mastroleo (1990) on three-body models. It addresses a known difficulty: calculations of incomplete fusion underestimate total fusion because complete fusion is not included. The authors note that Yabana et al. (2003) underestimate total fusion due to neglecting b-target interaction. The proposed three-body optical potential framework is intended to enable a consistent treatment that can test the validity of the CDCC (Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels) model, which the authors state consistently fails to account for incomplete fusion (citing Diaz-Torres and Thompson, 2002).
