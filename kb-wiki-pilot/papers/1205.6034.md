# Determination of 8B(p,gamma)9C reaction rate from 9C breakup

> The astrophysical factor of the 8B(p,gamma)9C at zero energy, S18(0), is determined from three-body model analysis of 9C breakup processes. The elastic breakup 208Pb(9C,p8B)208Pb at 65 MeV/nucleon and the one-proton removal reaction of 9C at 285 MeV/nucleon on C and Al targets are calculated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT), respectively. The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of 9C in the p-8B configuration extracted from the two reactions show good consistency, in contrast to in the previous studies. As a result of the present analysis, S18(0) = 66 \pm 10 eVb is obtained.

## Key claim

The astrophysical factor S18(0) for the 8B(p,gamma)9C reaction is determined to be 66 +/- 10 eVb by showing that the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) extracted from two different breakup reactions (elastic breakup and one-proton removal) are consistent with each other, resolving a previous discrepancy where values ranged from about 45 eVb to 80 eVb.

## Method

The elastic breakup 208Pb(9C,p8B)208Pb at 65 MeV/nucleon was analyzed using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), while the one-proton removal reaction of 9C at 285 MeV/nucleon on C and Al targets was analyzed using eikonal reaction theory (ERT). The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of 9C in the p-8B configuration was extracted from both reactions by fitting calculated cross sections to experimental data, and the astrophysical factor S18(0) was then derived from the ANC.

## Key numbers

* S18(0) = 66 +/- 10 eVb (principal result of the paper)
* (C_{p 8B}^{9C})^2 = 1.7 +/- 0.3 fm^{-1} (ANC squared from combined analysis)
* From elastic breakup: S18(0) = 67.3 eVb, (C_{p 8B}^{9C})^2 = 1.78 fm^{-1}
* From one-proton removal (average of C and Al targets): S18(0) = 63.7 eVb, (C_{p 8B}^{9C})^2 = 1.69 fm^{-1}
* Uncertainty of ANC from elastic breakup analysis regarding the p-8B interaction: 8%
* Total uncertainty of S18(0) from one-proton removal: 21% (including 5% from neglect of Coulomb breakup)
* One-proton removal cross sections: 44.9 mb (calc.) vs 48 +/- 8 mb (expt.) for 12C target; 53.9 mb (calc.) vs 55 +/- 11 mb (expt.) for 27Al target

## Context

This work resolves a significant discrepancy in previous determinations of S18(0) for the 8B(p,gamma)9C reaction, where Coulomb dissociation measurements (Motobayashi, 2003) gave about 77 +/- 15 eVb while ANC methods (Beaumel et al., 2001; Trache et al., 2002) gave about 45 +/- 13 eVb and 46 +/- 6 eVb, respectively. The present result of 66 +/- 10 eVb lies between these values, slightly favoring the higher cluster model calculations (Descouvemont, 1999: 72, 80 eVb). The consistency between the two independent breakup analyses demonstrates the reliability of the three-body coupled-channel framework (CDCC and ERT) for extracting astrophysical factors, and enables more confident predictions for the hot pp chain in low-metallicity supermassive stars.
