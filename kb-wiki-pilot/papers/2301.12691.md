# A new dynamical mechanism of incomplete fusion in heavy-ion collision

> The incomplete fusion has been proved as the formation and emission of the $α$ particle by the increase in the rotational energy of the very mass-asymmetric dinuclear system. The results of the dinuclear system model have confirmed that the incomplete fusion in heavy-ion collisions occurs at a large orbital angular momentum ($L > 30 \hbar$) due to the strong increase of the intrinsic fusion barrier.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that incomplete fusion in heavy-ion collisions is not caused by projectile breakup (as assumed in prior models), but rather by a very mass-asymmetric quasifission of the dinuclear system formed after full capture, occurring specifically at large orbital angular momenta (L > 30 hbar) where the rotational energy of the DNS increases strongly.

## Method

The authors model incomplete fusion using the dinuclear system (DNS) concept, calculating the potential energy surface, intrinsic fusion barrier B*_fus, quasifission barrier B_qf, and DNS excitation energy E*_Z as functions of orbital angular momentum L and charge asymmetry Z. They solve a transport master equation to obtain the time evolution of the charge distribution D_Z and the yield Y_Z of alpha particles (Z=2) as quasifission fragments, then compute partial cross sections sigma_ICF(E_cm.,L) and evaporation residue cross sections using the statistical model KEWPIE2 for the de-excitation cascade.

## Key numbers

- Incomplete fusion occurs at orbital angular momentum L > 30 hbar, with favorable ranges L = 35-60 hbar for the 16O+130Te reaction and L = 25-40 hbar for the 18O+93Nb reaction
- The calculated favorable L ranges agree with measured average values <L> = 40 hbar from the 14N+159Tb reaction (Inamura1977) and <L> = 30 hbar from the 16O+146Nd reaction (Barker)
- The DNS excitation energy E*_Z for the incomplete fusion channel lies in the range 10-50 MeV for angular momenta L = 35-50 hbar in the 18O+93Nb reaction
- The partial cross section sigma_ICF(E_lab,L) is calculated using sigma_cap(E_cm.,L) multiplied by the alpha-particle yield Y_Z(E_cm.,L)

## Context

This work contradicts the projectile breakup mechanism assumed in prior theoretical approaches including the breakup fusion model (Udagawa and Tamura, 1980), the sum-rule model (Wilczynski et al., 1980, 1982), and the classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup (Bosshe and Torres, 2019). The DNS-based mechanism reproduces measured evaporation residue cross sections for the 18O+93Nb and 16O+130Te reactions better than standard statistical models ALICE-91 and PACE4, and explains the plateau in excitation functions of 3n-4n channels at high energies as a consequence of reduced excitation energy in the conjugate fragment due to rotational energy conversion.
