# Reaction mechanism in odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections

> It was recently suggested that the odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections is an evidence of the pair- ing anti-halo effect on projectile radii. We define the dimensionless staggering parameters, Γrds and ΓR, for projectile radii and reaction cross sections, respectively, and analyze the relation between Γrds and ΓR for the scattering of 14,15,16C from a 12C target at 83 MeV/A by taking account of projectile-breakup and nuclear- medium effects newly with the microscopic version of the continuum discretized coupled-channels method. The value of ΓR is deviated from that of Γrds by the projectile-breakup effect, the nuclear-medium effect and an effect due to the fact that the scattering are not the black-sphere scattering (BSS) exactly. The projectile-breakup and nuclear medium effects are nearly canceled for ΓR. The remaining non-BSS effect becomes small as an incident energy decreases, indicating that nucleus-nucleus scattering at lower incident energies are a good probe of evaluating Γrds from measured reaction cross sections.

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections is not a direct measure of the pairing anti-halo effect on projectile radii; the staggering parameter for reaction cross sections, Γ_R, is deviated from the staggering parameter for projectile radii, Γ_rds, by the projectile-breakup effect, the nuclear-medium effect, and a non-black-sphere-scattering (non-BSS) effect, with the breakup and medium effects nearly canceling for Γ_R.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations for the scattering of 14,15,16C from a 12C target at 83 MeV/A, using a two-body (n + 14C) model for 15C and a three-body (n + n + 14C) model for 16C. The optical potentials were constructed by folding the Melbourne g-matrix nucleon-nucleon interaction with densities of the projectile constituents and the target, and three models were compared: Model I (no nuclear-medium or breakup effects), Model II (medium effect only), and Model III (both medium and breakup effects).

## Key numbers

*   Γ_rds (dimensionless staggering parameter for projectile radii) for 14,15,16C: 1.3
*   Γ_R (dimensionless staggering parameter for reaction cross sections) from Model III at 83 MeV/A: 0.77
*   Γ_R from Model II (medium effect only) at 83 MeV/A: 0.56
*   Γ_R from Model I (no medium or breakup effects) at 83 MeV/A: 0.83
*   γ_3 (staggering parameter in mb) for 14,15,16C: 163 ± 52 mb, about 10% of σ_R(15C) = 1319 ± 40 mb
*   Matter radii from calculation: 14C = 2.51 fm, 15C = 2.87 fm, 16C = 2.83 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hagino and Sagawa (2011, 2012), who suggested that odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections is evidence of the pairing anti-halo effect and analyzed 14,15,16C+12C scattering using the Glauber model. The present study uses the microscopic version of CDCC to include projectile-breakup and nuclear-medium effects that were not accounted for in the previous analysis, and finds that at lower incident energies (50-80 MeV/A) the non-BSS effect is small, making such scattering a good probe for evaluating Γ_rds from measured reaction cross sections.
