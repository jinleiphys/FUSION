# Current Topics in Heavy Quarkonium Physics

> I review some recent progress, open puzzles and future opportunities in heavy quarkonium physics in the framework of effective field theories

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress in heavy quarkonium physics using effective field theories (EFTs), but emphasizes that the attribution of specific quarkonium states (e.g., lowest charmonia and bottomonia) to either the perturbative or nonperturbative soft regime remains ambiguous due to the lack of direct information on quarkonium radii.

## Method

The author constructs a hierarchy of EFTs, NRQCD (Nonrelativistic QCD) and pNRQCD (potential Nonrelativistic QCD), by systematically integrating out high-energy scales (hard scale \(m\), soft scale \(mv\)) from QCD, with matching coefficients encoding high-energy contributions and low-energy degrees of freedom remaining dynamical. For weakly coupled systems (soft scale \(\gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\)), perturbative calculations of potentials and observables are performed, including resummation of large logarithms and renormalon subtractions; for strongly coupled systems (soft scale \(\sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\)), nonperturbative methods such as lattice calculations of Wilson-loop operators are used.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-quark velocities: \(v^2 \sim 0.1\) for \(b\bar{b}\), \(v^2 \sim 0.3\) for \(c\bar{c}\), \(v^2 \sim 0.01\) for \(t\bar{t}\)
- Determination of \(\alpha_s\) at the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) mass: \(\alpha_s(M_{\Upsilon(1S)}) = 0.184^{+0.015}_{-0.014}\), giving \(\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.119^{+0.006}_{-0.005}\)
- Static energy extraction: \(r_0 \Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 0.622^{+0.019}_{-0.015}\) for the zero-flavor case
- Prediction for \(B_c\) hyperfine separation: \(\Delta = 50 \pm 17\ ({\rm th})^{+15}_{-12}\ (\delta \alpha_s)\) MeV
- Prediction for \(\Upsilon(1S)\)–\(\eta_b\) hyperfine separation: \(41 \pm 11\ ({\rm th})^{+9}_{-8}\ (\delta \alpha_s)\) MeV
- Magnetic dipole transition widths: \(\Gamma(J/\psi \to \gamma \eta_c) = (1.5 \pm 1.0)\) keV; \(\Gamma(\Upsilon(1S) \to \gamma \eta_b) = (k_\gamma/71\ {\rm MeV})^3\,(15.1 \pm 1.5)\) eV

## Context

This work extends the EFT framework for quarkonium developed in prior reviews (Brambilla et al. 2004, 2010) and builds on specific calculations of the static potential at three loops (Smirnov 2008, Anzai 2009) and the static energy at NNNLL precision (Brambilla 2010). It enables precision tests of QCD (e.g., determination of \(\alpha_s\) and heavy quark masses), systematic treatment of quarkonium in hot media via thermal EFTs (pNRQCD\(_{\rm HTL}\)), and a framework for interpreting new exotic states near threshold (e.g., \(X(3872)\)) and data from LHC, BESIII, and future facilities like PANDA.
