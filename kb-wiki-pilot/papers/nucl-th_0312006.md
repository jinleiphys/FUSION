# Electron Scattering on 3He - a Playground to Test Nuclear Dynamics

> The big spectrum of electron induced processes on 3He is illustrated by several examples based on Faddeev calculations with modern nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces as well as exchange currents. The kinematical region is restricted to a mostly nonrelativistic one where the three-nucleon c.m. energy is below the pion production threshold and the three-momentum of the virtual photon is sufficiently below the nucleon mass. Comparisons with available data are shown and cases of agreement and disagreement are found. It is argued that new and precise data are needed to systematically check the present day dynamical ingredients.

## Key claim
The paper argues that electron scattering on 3He provides a sensitive test of nuclear dynamics, but that current theoretical descriptions show both agreement and disagreement with data, and that new precise data are needed to systematically check the present-day dynamical ingredients including nucleon-nucleon forces, three-nucleon forces, and exchange currents.

## Method
The authors performed Faddeev calculations for electron-induced processes on 3He using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential, the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, and pi- and rho-like meson exchange currents. They solved Faddeev-like integral equations for the nuclear matrix elements and response functions, restricting kinematics to the nonrelativistic regime where the three-nucleon center-of-mass energy is below pion production threshold and the virtual photon three-momentum is sufficiently below the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers
- The asymmetry A was calculated at q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 as a function of energy transfer omega
- Response functions R_L and R_T were computed at Q = 300, 400, 500, and 600 MeV/c
- The magnetic neutron form factor G_M^n was extracted at q^2 = 0.1 and 0.2 (GeV/c)^2 from 3He data
- Elastic charge and magnetic form factors were shown for 3He and 3H up to q values of about 3 fm^-1
- Numerical accuracy in the observables is of the order of one percent
- For pd breakup, calculations were shown at Q = 250 MeV/c and Q = 431 MeV/c
- For three-body breakup, calculations used E_e = 390 MeV, omega = 37 MeV and Q = 100 MeV/c, and also omega = 113 MeV and Q = 250 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous Faddeev calculations of three-nucleon scattering observables to electron-induced processes on 3He, building on earlier studies of nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and breakup reactions. The authors note that while pure three-nucleon scattering data are generally well described by current force models, three-nucleon force properties remain unsettled. The paper enables systematic tests of nuclear dynamics through comparison with new experimental data, and has already been used to extract the magnetic neutron form factor in agreement with deuteron-based measurements from Anklin et al. (1994, 1998) and Gao et al. (1994).
