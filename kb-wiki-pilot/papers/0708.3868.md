# Relativistic Three-Body Scattering in a First Order Faddeev Formulation

> Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies are solved in first order in the two-body transition operator in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance is incorporated within the framework of Poincar{é} invariant quantum mechanics. Based on a Malfliet-Tjon type interaction, observables for elastic and breakup scattering are calculated and compared to the nonrelativistic ones.

# Relativistic Three-Body Scattering in a First Order Faddeev Formulation

## Key claim

Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering can be solved in first order in the two-body transition operator using momentum vectors without partial wave decomposition, and the resulting relativistic effects are mostly contained in the two-body amplitude; when phase-shift-equivalent interactions are used, the relativistic and nonrelativistic three-body cross sections are very close at the first-order level, with additional effects expected from the full Faddeev solution.

## Method

The authors solve the leading-order term of the Faddeev multiple scattering series within Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics, using spin-independent Yukawa-type interactions (Malfliet-Tjon type with a short-range repulsive and intermediate-range attractive Yukawa force). Relativistic two-body t-matrices are constructed from nonrelativistic ones using the Coester-Piper-Serduke (CPS) method, which provides a scattering-equivalent relativistic t-matrix via an analytic connection formula. The two-body t-matrix is then embedded into the three-particle Hilbert space using a first resolvent method, and observables for elastic and breakup scattering are computed directly in momentum vector variables.

## Key numbers

- The Malfliet-Tjon type interaction supports a bound state at E_d = -2.23 MeV
- Total elastic cross sections are computed for projectile kinetic energies from 10 MeV up to 1 GeV
- Differential elastic cross sections are shown at 0.5 GeV laboratory projectile energy
- Inclusive breakup cross sections are computed at 0.495 GeV laboratory projectile kinetic energy for emission angles of 18 degrees and 24 degrees
- The quasi-free scattering (QFS) peak shifts towards smaller ejectile energies under relativistic kinematics; the peak height can either decrease (at 18 degrees) or increase (at 24 degrees)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Liu, Elster, and Glöckle (2005), who solved Faddeev equations directly as functions of vector variables for scattering at intermediate energies, by incorporating relativistic invariance within Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics. The relativistic interaction construction follows the method of Coester, Piper, and Serduke (CPS, 1975) to ensure phase-shift equivalence between relativistic and nonrelativistic interactions. The results enable future full solutions of the relativistic Faddeev equation, which are expected to show additional relativistic effects beyond the first-order calculation presented here.
