# Neutron Removal from the Deformed Halo 31Ne Nucleus

> Experimental data on Coulomb breakup and neutron removal indicate that 31Ne is one of the heaviest halo nuclei discovered so far. The possible ground state of 31Ne is either 3/2- coming from p-wave halo or 1/2+ from s-wave halo. In this work, we develop a treatable model to include deformed wave functions and a dynamical knockout formalism which includes the dependence on the nuclear orientation to study the neutron removal from 31Ne projectiles at energies around E=200 MeV/nucleon. A detailed account of the effects of deformation on cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions is made. Our numerical analysis indicates a preference for the 31Ne ground state with spin parity 3/2-.

## Key claim

The numerical analysis indicates a preference for the 31Ne ground state with spin parity 3/2- (p-wave halo) over the 1/2+ (s-wave halo) alternative, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific ranges of quadrupole deformation and binding energy that yield cross sections consistent with the measured value of 3.3(1.5) fm² for the ground state feeding cross section.

## Method

The authors developed a treatable model combining deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions with a dynamical knockout formalism based on the Glauber model, including orientation-dependent core-target S-matrices. They solved coupled differential equations for deformed states with quadrupole deformation, expanded the core density to linear orders of deformation, and calculated stripping and diffraction dissociation cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions for neutron removal from 31Ne projectiles on carbon targets at 230 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Measured cross section feeding the ground state of the residual core: σ_exp^gs = 3.3(1.5) fm²
- Measured inclusive cross section: σ_exp^inc = 9.0(0.7) fm²
- For the 3/2- state with β₂=0.4 and E=-0.15 MeV: wave function composition is 24% p₃/₂, 1% f₅/₂, and 75% f₇/₂
- For the 1/2+ state with β₂=0.6 and E=-0.6 MeV: wave function composition is 59% s₁/₂, 37% d₃/₂, and 4% d₅/₂
- Full width at half maximum of calculated momentum distributions for the 3/2- state: approximately 82-93 MeV/c; measured FWHM: 77(18) MeV/c
- Diffraction dissociation cross section amounts to 15-19% of the stripping cross section
- For the 3/2- state with 0.3 ≤ β₂ ≤ 0.5, agreement with data requires 0.3 MeV ≤ |E| ≤ 0.45 MeV; for β₂ ≈ 0.2, requires 0.15 MeV ≤ |E| ≤ 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-type knockout formalism of Bertsch et al. (used in the MOMDIS code) to include deformation effects, improving upon prior studies by Zelevinsky, Tostevin, and others that used Nilsson model wave functions or absorbing-disk approximations. The approach is superior to the Nilsson model because harmonic oscillator wave functions decay too fast at long distances, which is critical for halo nuclei like 31Ne. The results enable determination of the ground state spin-parity of 31Ne and constrain the quadrupole deformation and neutron binding energy, building on the work of Hamamoto who suggested possible spin-parity assignments based on Coulomb breakup data.
