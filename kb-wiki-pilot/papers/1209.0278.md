# Dynamics aspect of subbarrier fusion reaction in light heavy ion systems

> Subbarrier fusion of the 7Li + 12C reaction is studied using an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model (AMD) with an after burner, GEMINI. In AMD, 7Li shows an α+ t structure at its ground state and it is significantly deformed. Simulations are made near the Coulomb barrier energies, i.e., E_{cm} = 3 - 8 MeV. The total fusion cross section of the AMD + GEMINI calculations as a function of incident energy is compared to the experimental results and both are in good agreement at E_{cm} > 3 MeV. The cross section for the different residue channels of the AMD + GEMINI at E_{cm} = 5 MeV is also compared to the experimental results.

# Dynamics aspect of subbarrier fusion reaction in light heavy ion systems

## Key claim

The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model combined with the GEMINI statistical decay code reproduces the experimental total fusion cross section for the 7Li + 12C reaction at center-of-mass energies above 3 MeV, but underestimates it below that energy because the AMD formulation does not incorporate quantum tunneling through the Coulomb barrier. The cluster structure of 7Li (an alpha particle plus a triton) leads to both complete and incomplete fusion channels, with the latter involving capture of only one cluster.

## Method

The authors used the AMD model with the Gogny interaction to generate initial ground-state nuclei of 7Li (showing an alpha + triton cluster structure) and 12C (showing a 3-alpha cluster structure). They simulated 7Li + 12C collisions at center-of-mass energies from 3 to 8 MeV over impact parameters from 0 to 7 fm, running a few thousand to ten thousand events per energy. The primary AMD fragments were then cooled using the statistical decay code GEMINI (C++ version), and fusion events were defined by the emission of fragments with atomic number Z > 6.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 7Li from AMD: 40.00 MeV (experimental: 39.24 MeV); root-mean-square radius: 3.02 fm (experimental: 2.43 fm, about 20% larger)
- Binding energy of 12C from AMD: 92.24 MeV (experimental: 92.16 MeV); root-mean-square radius: 2.53 fm (experimental: 2.47 fm)
- Center-of-mass energy range studied: 3 to 8 MeV
- Impact parameter range: 0 to 7 fm (no fusion observed for b > 7 fm)
- Complete fusion occurs in about 80% of cases at lower incident energies, decreasing to about 70% at higher energies
- Additional 20-30% incomplete fusion contribution present in the primary fusion process

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of subbarrier fusion in light heavy-ion systems, particularly addressing how cluster structure affects fusion mechanisms. It builds on earlier AMD calculations by Kaneda-En'yo and collaborators that predicted cluster structures in light nuclei, and on coupled-channel calculations by Hagino et al. that predicted energy-dependent enhancement or suppression of fusion for halo nuclei. The experimental data used for comparison come from Mukherjee et al. (1996). The results enable further investigation of the suppression of the 15N + alpha channel observed experimentally, which the authors note is not yet fully understood.
