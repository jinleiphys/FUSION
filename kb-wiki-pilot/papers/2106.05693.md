# Elastic scattering measurements for the $^{10}$C + $^{208}$Pb system at E$_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV

> Background: The influence of halo structure of $^6$He, $^8$B, $^{11}$Be and $^{11}$Li nuclei in several mechanisms such as direct reactions and fusion is already established, although not completely understood. The influence of the $^{10}$C Brunnian structure is less known. Purpose: To investigate the influence of the cluster configuration of $^{10}$C on the elastic scattering at an energy close to the Coulomb barrier. Methods: We present experimental data for the elastic scattering of the $^{10}$C+$^{208}$Pb system at $E_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV. The data are compared to the three- and the four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations assuming $^9$B+$p$, $^6$Be+$α$ and $^8$Be+$p$+$p$ configurations. Results: The experimental angular distribution of the cross sections shows the suppression of the Fresnel peak that is reasonably well reproduced by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations. However, the calculations underestimate the cross sections at backward angles. Couplings to continuum states represent a small effect. Conclusions: The cluster configurations of $^{10}$C assumed in the present work are able to describe some of the features of the data. In order to explain the data at backward angles, experimental data for the breakup and an extension of theoretical formalism towards a four-body cluster seem to be in need to reproduce the measured angular distribution.

## Key claim

The cluster configurations of $^{10}$C (assuming $^9$B+$p$, $^6$Be+$\alpha$, and $^8$Be+$p$+$p$ structures) can describe some features of the elastic scattering data at $E_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV, but the calculations underestimate the cross sections at backward angles, indicating that experimental breakup data and an extension of theoretical formalism toward a four-body cluster are needed to fully reproduce the measured angular distribution.

## Method

Elastic scattering of a radioactive $^{10}$C beam on a $^{208}$Pb target was measured at $E_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV using a double-sided silicon strip detector (DSSSD) $\Delta$E-E telescope at forward angles and a single DSSSD at backward angles, with a $^{10}$C beam produced via the $p$($^{10}$B,$^{10}$C)$n$ reaction and selected by the MARS spectrometer. The experimental angular distribution was compared to three-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (3b-CDCC) calculations assuming $^9$B+$p$ and $^6$Be+$\alpha$ configurations, and to four-body CDCC (4b-CDCC) calculations assuming a $^8$Be+$p$+$p$ configuration, using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) method for the three-body projectile structure.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: $E_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV for $^{10}$C + $^{208}$Pb, corresponding to an energy close to the Coulomb barrier ($V_{\rm B}$ = 61.3 MeV in the laboratory framework)
- Coulomb barrier parameters for $^{10}$C + $^{208}$Pb: $R_{\rm B}$ = 11.3 fm, $V_{\rm B}$ = 58.8 MeV, $\hbar\omega$ = 5.2 MeV
- Reaction cross section from optical model fit: $\sigma_R$ = 753 mb
- Binding energies for $^{10}$C cluster decay channels: $^8$Be+$p$+$p$ at 3.821 MeV, $^9$B+$p$ at 4.006 MeV, $^6$Be+$\alpha$ at 5.101 MeV
- Energy resolution: about 2.9 MeV (FWHM)
- Average production rate of $^{10}$C beam: $5\times10^3$ pps for 300 nA incident $^{10}$B$^{+5}$ beam
- Beam purity: better than 95%

## Context

This work extends previous elastic scattering studies of exotic nuclei on heavy targets, particularly the $^8$B + $^{208}$Pb system at $E_{\rm lab}$ = 50 MeV (Mazzocco et al., 2019) which also showed strong Fresnel peak suppression, and the $^{10}$C + $^{58}$Ni system at $E_{\rm lab}$ = 35.3 MeV (Guimaraes et al., 2019) where couplings to the $2_1^+$ state were important. The reduced reaction cross section for $^{10}$C lies above the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) and close to those of weakly-bound projectiles like $^6$He and $^8$B, suggesting that breakup and cluster effects contribute significantly. The results motivate future experimental breakup measurements and theoretical development of full four-body CDCC calculations to describe the backward-angle data.
