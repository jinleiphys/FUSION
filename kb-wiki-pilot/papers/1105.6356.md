# Fusion11 Conference Summary

> A summary account of the conference "Fusion11", held in Saint Malo, France, May 2-6, 2011.

## Key claim

The paper provides a subjective, selective summary of the Fusion11 conference, highlighting that many open questions in fusion physics cannot be resolved by simple literature searches or standard models, and that the field relies heavily on phenomenological approaches and uncontrolled theoretical approximations.

## Method

The author reviewed 77 expert talks presented at the conference, organized by topic areas including new facilities, fusion cross section models (barrier penetration, optical potentials, coupled channels), barrier distributions, fusion hindrance, transfer channels, incomplete fusion and fission, rare isotope reactions, cluster physics, nuclear astrophysics, and superheavy elements. The summary focuses on selected presentations that illustrate current experimental challenges and theoretical controversies, often with critical commentary on the limitations of standard approaches.

## Key numbers

- SPIRAL 2 facility at GANIL will produce radioactive beams at 1-20 MeV/nucleon, with fusion-evaporation cross sections possibly measurable at the nb level
- ELI-Nuclear Physics facility will use 20 petawatt lasers, up to 5 orders of magnitude higher than current laser intensity
- ITER project estimated at 15 billion euros
- New S-factor for the ³He+⁴He reaction: S₃₄ = 0.567 ± 0.018 ± 0.004 keV b
- Reduction in neutrino flux uncertainty due to improved S₃₄: Φ₈B from 7.5% to 4.3%, Φ₇Be from 8% to 4.5%
- Fusion of ¹⁵C+²³²Th shows enhancement by a factor of 5-6 at E/V_B ~ 0.85 compared to ¹²,¹³C+²³²Th
- Traditional diffuseness parameter a = 0.65 fm is found to be larger when extracted from fusion data at extremely low energies

## Context

This conference summary covers the state of fusion reaction physics in 2011, extending discussions from previous Fusion conferences and the broader nuclear reaction community. It reviews work on the São Paulo potential, M3Y interactions, Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock calculations (Umar, Simenel), Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) methods (Canto, Hagino, Moro), and the Universal Fusion Function approach (Gomes). The paper enables future work by identifying unresolved issues such as fusion hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies, the role of neutron transfer in sub-barrier fusion enhancement, and the need for better theoretical understanding of continuum-continuum couplings and breakup effects.
