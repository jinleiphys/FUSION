# Spectroscopy of the unbound: One-neutron knockout reaction of $^{14}$Be

> Full Faddeev-type calculations are performed for one-neutron knockout reaction of $^{14}$Be on proton target at 69 MeV/u incident energy. Inclusive transverse momentum distributions for the outgoing ($^{12}$Be + n) system and semi-inclusive cross sections are presented. A significant proton-core single scattering contribution emerges where the valence neutron has non zero angular momentum relative to the core. This indicates that distorted-wave impulse approximation is inadequate and the complete multiple scattering series must be taken into account for the considered reaction. The magnitude of the semi-inclusive cross section at quasifree scattering conditions is a clear signature of the angular momentum of the valence nucleon.

## Key claim

Full Faddeev-type calculations for one-neutron knockout of $^{14}$Be on a proton target at 69 MeV/u show that the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) is inadequate for this reaction, because a significant proton-core single scattering contribution emerges when the valence neutron has nonzero angular momentum relative to the core, meaning the complete multiple scattering series must be used.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Faddeev/Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) scattering equations exactly in momentum space after partial wave decomposition, treating the $^{14}$Be as a $^{13}$Be core plus a valence neutron interacting with a proton target. They included nuclear interactions between all three pairs (p-n, p-$^{13}$Be, n-$^{13}$Be) and the Coulomb interaction between the proton and $^{13}$Be, using the CD Bonn potential for p-n, a Watson global optical potential for p-$^{13}$Be, and Woods-Saxon potentials for n-$^{13}$Be configured for S-wave, P-wave, and D-wave bound states corresponding to $^{13}$Be resonances of $1/2^+$, $1/2^-$, and $5/2^+$, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 69 MeV/u for $^{14}$Be on proton target
- $^{13}$Be resonances considered: $1/2^-$ (P-wave) with $E_{\rm rel}=0.45$ MeV, $1/2^+$ (S-wave) with $E_{\rm rel}=1.17$ MeV, $5/2^+$ (D-wave) with $E_{\rm rel}=2.34$ MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy of $^{14}$Be: $S_{2n}=1.26$ MeV
- Binding energies for n-$^{13}$Be potentials: 32.922 MeV (1p1/2), 61.667 MeV (2s1/2), 68.980 MeV (1d5/2)
- QFS condition for $^{13}$Be LAB momentum: $(E_C^{\rm LAB},\theta_C^{\rm LAB},\phi_C^{\rm LAB})^{\rm QFS} = (897~{\rm MeV},0,0)$
- The semi-inclusive cross section with an S-wave bound state is one order of magnitude larger than with P-wave, and two orders of magnitude larger than with D-wave

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exact Faddeev/AGS studies of p-$^{11}$Be elastic scattering and breakup (Crespo et al. 2008, 2007; Deltuva et al. 2007) to the $^{14}$Be system, and directly addresses the experimental data from Kondo et al. (2007) on $^{13}$Be resonances measured via invariant mass method at RIKEN. The results demonstrate that standard DWIA approaches (e.g., Chant 1977) are inadequate for knockout reactions involving nonzero orbital angular momentum valence nucleons, and that full multiple scattering calculations are necessary to correctly interpret the angular momentum of the valence nucleon from semi-inclusive cross section magnitudes.
