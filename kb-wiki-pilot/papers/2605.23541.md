# Coulomb bridge mechanism for peripheral polarization of weakly bound projectiles

> We identify the matrix elements that carry peripheral polarization of weakly bound projectiles through the Feshbach dynamical polarization potential (DPP) within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework. Splitting the two P-Q bridge couplings into nuclear and Coulomb parts, while keeping a single Q-space propagator common to every term, decomposes the DPP into a nuclear, a Coulomb, and an interference component, $ΔU_{\rm DPP}=ΔU_N+ΔU_C+ΔU_{NC}$. Applied to $d+{}^{58}$Ni, ${}^{6}$Li$+{}^{208}$Pb, ${}^{11}$Be$+{}^{64}$Zn, and ${}^{8}$B$+{}^{64}$Zn, the decomposition reveals a controlled hierarchy: a nuclear bridge in the light system, a mixed bridge with strong destructive interference in the heavy stable case, and a Coulomb-dominated bridge in both halo systems, with the proton halo showing constructive nuclear-Coulomb interference. For the halo reactions, peripheral partial waves ($L\gtrsim 35$) satisfy $σ_R^L\simeqσ_{\rm DPP}^L\simeqσ_{\rm BU}^L$, with the high-$L$ DPP tail dominated by $σ_C^L$. Two diagnostic calculations isolate the responsible matrix elements: removing the off-diagonal Coulomb propagation inside Q leaves the pattern essentially intact, whereas removing the Coulomb part of the P-Q bridge collapses both DPP-induced absorption and breakup. The peripheral polarization of halo reactions is therefore a Coulomb-bridge effect, and the high-$L$ elastic-breakup yield serves as its observable signature.

# Coulomb bridge mechanism for peripheral polarization of weakly bound projectiles

## Key claim

The peripheral polarization of halo projectiles in reactions near the Coulomb barrier is carried predominantly by the Coulomb part of the P↔Q bridge couplings (U^{(C)}_{0γ} and U^{(C)}_{γ'0}) rather than by Coulomb propagation inside the Q-space or by nuclear couplings, as demonstrated by a decomposition that keeps a single common Q-space propagator for all terms.

## Method

The authors split the P↔Q bridge couplings in the Feshbach dynamical polarization potential (DPP) into nuclear and Coulomb components within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework, yielding ΔU_DPP = ΔU_N + ΔU_C + ΔU_NC with all three components sharing the same Q-space Green's function. They apply this decomposition to four reactions: d+⁵⁸Ni, ⁶Li+²⁰⁸Pb, ¹¹Be+⁶⁴Zn, and ⁸B+⁶⁴Zn, and perform diagnostic calculations that separately remove either the off-diagonal Coulomb propagation inside Q or the Coulomb part of the P↔Q bridge couplings.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹Be+⁶⁴Zn at E_lab = 28.7 MeV: peripheral partial waves with L ≳ 35 satisfy σ_R^L ≃ σ_DPP^L ≃ σ_BU^L, with the high-L DPP tail dominated by σ_C^L
- Neutron separation energy of ¹¹Be: S_n = 504 keV
- Proton separation energy of ⁸B: S_p = 137 keV
- Dipole effective charges: ~0.36 e for ¹¹Be and ~0.38 e for ⁸B (opposite sign)
- For ⁸B+⁶⁴Zn at E_lab = 36.11 MeV: the Coulomb component σ_C^L closely follows the total σ_DPP^L over almost the whole range of partial waves, determining the main peak near L ≃ 25
- For d+⁵⁸Ni: σ_DPP ≈ σ_N to within a few percent across eight energies from 20 to 80 MeV
- For ⁶Li+²⁰⁸Pb: continuum states discretized up to α-d relative kinetic energy of 12 MeV with six bin states per partial wave and ℓ ≤ 2

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach DPP formalism within CDCC previously developed by the same authors (Liu et al., 2025-2026) by identifying which specific matrix elements carry peripheral polarization. It refines the qualitative picture from Di Pietro et al. (2010, 2012) for ¹¹Be+⁶⁴Zn and Spartà et al. (2021) for ⁸B+⁶⁴Zn, who used switch-off calculations showing Coulomb couplings matter but could not identify the responsible bridge matrix elements. The decomposition also relates to the S-matrix level splitting by Descouvemont et al. (2017) for ⁷Li+²⁰⁸Pb. The results enable experimental identification of the Coulomb-bridge mechanism through high-L elastic-breakup yields as observable signatures, and can be extended to refractive observables sensitive to Re ΔU_DPP and to reactions on other halo nuclei such as ⁶He.
