# Exact Treatment of Continuum Couplings in Nuclear Optical Potentials via Feshbach Theory

> We present the first numerically exact implementation of full-coupling Feshbach theory for deriving effective optical potentials in nuclear reactions, overcoming long-standing computational barriers that previously necessitated weak-coupling approximations. By integrating Feshbach projection operators with the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method, we rigorously incorporate all continuum-continuum interactions, previously considered intractable, to extract dynamic polarization potentials that capture virtual excitations and absorption in reactions with weakly bound nuclei. Application to deuteron-induced reactions on $^{58}$Ni demonstrates that our full-coupling effective potentials reproduce CDCC observables exactly, while weak-coupling and folding-model approaches show significant deviations. The method uniquely separates elastic breakup from breakup-fusion processes through the optical theorem, enabling precise determination of incomplete and complete fusion cross sections, critical for interpreting rare-isotope beam experiments at facilities like FRIB. The non-local polarization potentials provide insights into the energy-dependent interplay between reaction mechanisms, with our analysis suggesting an increasing relative importance of elastic breakup at higher energies while continuum absorption shows more complex behavior. This exact treatment eliminates systematic uncertainties from coupling approximations, providing predictive power for unstable nuclei far from stability where experimental data are scarce.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first numerically exact implementation of full-coupling Feshbach theory for deriving effective optical potentials, overcoming the long-standing computational barrier that previously forced all prior work to rely on weak-coupling approximations which neglected continuum-continuum interactions.

## Method

The authors integrated Feshbach projection operators with the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method, constructing a non-diagonal coupled-channel Green's function from regular and irregular solutions of the Q-space Hamiltonian to incorporate all continuum-continuum couplings. They applied this formalism to deuteron-induced reactions on $^{58}$Ni, computing elastic scattering cross sections and dynamic polarization potentials, then used the optical theorem to separate elastic breakup from breakup-fusion absorption contributions.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering cross sections for $d+^{58}$Ni were computed at 20, 30, 40, and 56 MeV incident energies
- The full-coupling effective potential reproduced CDCC elastic scattering observables exactly at all four energies, while weak-coupling and Watanabe (folding-model) potentials showed significant deviations
- Total angular momentum was truncated at $J_{\text{max}}=120$ for convergence
- The dynamic polarization potential spatial extent was confined within 10 fm for the $d+^{58}$Ni system at 56 MeV with $J=0$
- Elastic breakup cross section increased gradually with incident energy, while the absorption cross section from DPP displayed a bell-shaped behavior (increasing, plateauing, then slightly decreasing at higher energies)
- The ratio of elastic breakup to DPP absorption cross section increased with energy, indicating increasing relative importance of elastic breakup at higher energies

## Context

This work extends Feshbach's foundational theory of nuclear reactions (Feshbach 1958, 1962) by overcoming the weak-coupling approximations used in prior studies such as Sargsyan et al. (2024) and Rao (1973), which neglected continuum-continuum couplings as numerically intractable. The method enables precise determination of incomplete and complete fusion cross sections critical for interpreting rare-isotope beam experiments at facilities like FRIB, RAON, and HIAF, and provides predictive power for unstable nuclei far from stability where experimental data are scarce.
