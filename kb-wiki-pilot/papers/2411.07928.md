# Nonlocal interaction and collective excitation in deuteron breakup on ${}^{24}$Mg nucleus

> Deuteron breakup in collision with a ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ nucleus is studied using rigorous three-body scattering equations, extended to include also the excitation of the nucleus. Predictions based on local and nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potentials with rotational quadrupole deformation enabling the excitation of the ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}(2^+)$ state are compared. The nonlocality effect is less pronounced than in the deuteron inelastic scattering ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}(d,d')$ at the same energies, and manifests itself quite differently for semi-inclusive differential cross sections of elastic and inelastic breakup.

# Nonlocal interaction and collective excitation in deuteron breakup on ${}^{24}$Mg nucleus

## Key claim

The nonlocality effect in deuteron breakup on ${}^{24}$Mg is less pronounced than in the deuteron inelastic scattering ${}^{24}$Mg(d,d') at the same energies, and manifests itself quite differently for semi-inclusive differential cross sections of elastic and inelastic breakup, with the effect being small but systematic across kinematic regimes.

## Method

The authors solved rigorous Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) three-body scattering equations in momentum-space partial wave representation, extended to include collective excitation of the ${}^{24}$Mg nucleus via rotational quadrupole deformation. Local and nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potentials with a nonlocality range of $\rho = 1$ fm, taken from Ref. [deltuva:23b], were used as dynamic input; the nonlocal potentials had been fitted to experimental $p+{}^{24}$Mg elastic and inelastic ($2^+$) differential cross section data at proton beam energies 30.4, 34.9, 39.9, and 44.9 MeV. The long-range Coulomb force was included using the method of screening and renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Core excitation energy $\Delta m_A = 1.369$ MeV for the first $2^+$ excited state of ${}^{24}$Mg
- Deuteron beam energies studied: $E_d = 70$ and 90 MeV
- Proton-nucleus reaction cross section at 34.9 MeV: $\sigma_R = 635 \pm 20$ mb (typical $\pm 3\%$ spread across nonlocal parameter sets)
- Integrated cross sections at $E_d = 90$ MeV:
  - Elastic breakup: 118(5) mb (local), 132(8) mb (nonlocal)
  - Inelastic breakup: 5.4(3) mb (local), 5.2(2) mb (nonlocal)
  - $(d,d')$ inelastic scattering: 48.9(5) mb (local), 43.5(8) mb (nonlocal)
- Inelastic breakup cross section is lower by roughly a factor of 20 than elastic breakup and by a factor of 10 than inelastic deuteron scattering
- Nonlocality effect on integrated cross section is slightly above 10% for both deuteron inelastic scattering and elastic breakup

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev-type calculations for deuteron stripping and pickup reactions on ${}^{10}$Be and ${}^{11}$Be [deltuva:23a] and inelastic deuteron scattering on ${}^{24}$Mg [deltuva:23b] to the deuteron breakup channels, including for the first time the simultaneous collective excitation of the target nucleus in a Faddeev-type formalism. The study enables future modeling of reactions with other light nuclei and various interaction models, as well as assessment of other scattering observables, demonstrating the feasibility of such calculations for breakup with core excitation.
