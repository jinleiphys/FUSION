# Scaling laws and higher-order effects in Coulomb excitation of neutron halo nuclei

> Essential properties of halo nuclei can be described in terms of a few low-energy constants. For neutron halo nuclei, analytical results can be found for wave functions and electromagnetic transition matrix-elements in simple but well-adapted models. These wave functions can be used to study nuclear reactions; an especially simple and instructive example is Coulomb excitation. A systematic expansion in terms of small parameters can be given. We present scaling laws for excitation amplitudes and cross sections. The results can be used to analyze experiments like ${}^{11}$Be Coulomb excitation. They also serve as benchmark tests for more involved reaction theories.

## Key claim

The paper presents analytical scaling laws for Coulomb excitation amplitudes and cross sections in neutron halo nuclei, valid in the sudden approximation when the adiabaticity parameter is small. The authors show that higher-order electromagnetic effects reduce the first-order cross section by 6-11% for existing ${}^{11}$Be experiments, with the reduction factor depending only on binding energies and a core size parameter.

## Method

The authors use a single-particle model with analytical halo wave functions for $s$-wave ground states and $p$-wave excited states, employing the sudden approximation in the semiclassical straight-line trajectory approach. They expand the excitation amplitude in powers of a dimensionless strength parameter $z = q_{\rm Coul}/(q_0+q_1)$ and derive closed-form expressions for the first-order and next-to-leading-order contributions. The model is applied to ${}^{11}$Be Coulomb excitation on a ${}^{208}$Pb target, using neutron separation energies $E_0=504$ keV and $E_1=184$ keV with a core radius $R=2.78$ fm.

## Key numbers

- ${}^{11}$Be neutron separation energies: $E_0 = 504$ keV (ground state $1/2^+$), $E_1 = 184$ keV (excited state $1/2^-$), giving excitation energy $\hbar\omega = 320$ keV
- Dimensionless scaling parameters for ${}^{11}$Be: $\gamma_0 = 0.4116$, $\gamma_1 = 0.2487$ (using $R=2.78$ fm)
- Calculated $B(E1) = 0.193$ $e^2$fm$^2$ for the $1/2^+ \rightarrow 1/2^-$ transition (compared to experimental value $0.105(12)$ $e^2$fm$^2$ from GANIL data)
- Reduction factors $R(v)$ for cross sections: 0.8902 (GANIL at 38.6 MeV/nucleon), 0.9025 (GANIL at 43.0 MeV/nucleon), 0.9321 (MSU at 60.0 MeV/nucleon), 0.9366 (RIKEN at 64.0 MeV/nucleon)
- Scattering length in $l=1$, $j=1/2$ channel of ${}^{11}$Be: $457(67,-66)$ fm$^3$; effective-range parameter $r_1 = -0.547$ fm$^{-1}$
- Minimum impact parameter $b_{\rm min} = 9.78$ fm for ${}^{11}$Be on ${}^{208}$Pb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of Coulomb dissociation of halo nuclei (Typel and Baur, Phys. Rev. C 64, 0246101, 2001; Phys. Lett. B 356, 186, 1995) by treating the case of bound-bound transitions between $s$-wave and $p$-wave halo states, as realized in ${}^{11}$Be. The analytical results provide benchmark tests for more sophisticated numerical approaches such as XCDCC (used by Summers et al., Phys. Lett. B 650, 124, 2007) and time-dependent calculations (Goldstein, Capel and Baye, Phys. Rev. C 76, 024608, 2007). The scaling laws enable analysis of Coulomb excitation experiments at GANIL, RIKEN, and MSU, and can be applied to future measurements at FAIR, SPIRAL2, and RIBF for more neutron-rich nuclei with low-lying single-particle states.
