# The Little Bang: Searching for quark-gluon matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> I review the status of the search for quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The available data provide strong evidence for the "three pillars of the Little Bang model": strong radial expansion of the collision fireball with Hubble-like scaling, thermal hadron emission and primordial hadrosynthesis. It is argued that the initial state of the reaction zone exhibits features which cannot be understood with conventional hadronic dynamics, but are consistent with the formation of deconfined quark-gluon matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that data from CERN SPS heavy-ion collisions provide "compelling evidence" for a new state of matter consistent with deconfined quark-gluon matter, but explicitly distinguishes this from a "discovery of QGP", the evidence is circumstantial and based on consistency arguments rather than direct proof, with several crucial issues requiring higher energies at RHIC and LHC for resolution.

## Method

The author reviews the "three pillars of the Little Bang model" by analyzing hadronic transverse mass spectra, two-particle Bose-Einstein (HBT) correlations, and particle abundance ratios from 158 A GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS. The analysis combines spectral slope measurements with HBT radius parameters to separate thermal temperature from collective transverse flow velocity, and compares measured particle ratios to a chemical equilibrium fireball model at the critical temperature for color deconfinement from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density from Bjorken estimate: ε_Bj^(Pb+Pb)(1 fm/c) = 3.2 ± 0.3 GeV/fm^3 for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS
- Critical energy density for QCD phase transition: ε_c ≈ 1 GeV/fm^3
- Critical temperature from lattice QCD: T_c ≈ 170 MeV ± 15%
- Freeze-out temperature from combined spectra and HBT analysis: T_f ≈ 100 MeV
- Mean transverse flow velocity at freeze-out: ⟨v_⊥⟩ ≈ 0.55 c
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from particle ratio fits: T_chem = 168 MeV with μ_B = 266 MeV
- Thermal energy density at freeze-out: ε_f ≈ 0.05 GeV/fm^3
- Fireball volume expansion factor from τ_0 to τ_f: factor 40
- Transverse area growth from HBT: factor 4-5
- Initial temperature if thermalized at 1 fm/c: T_0 ≈ 210-220 MeV
- Energy density required for T = 220 MeV: ε ≈ 3.5 GeV/fm^3 (about 6 times critical)
- dE_T/dy(y=0) ≈ 400 GeV for central Pb+Pb collisions
- Overlap area of two Pb nuclei: πR_rms^2 = 63 fm^2

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of the "Little Bang" model developed by Heinz and collaborators (HJ00) and builds on the Bjorken energy density estimate (Bj) for relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It synthesizes results from multiple CERN SPS experiments (NA44, NA49, NA52, CERES/NA45, WA97) and compares with lattice QCD calculations by Karsch and collaborators. The work directly addresses the CERN announcement of February 10, 2000 regarding evidence for a new state of matter, and identifies remaining open questions that require the higher energies of RHIC and LHC to resolve, particularly the need to penetrate deeper into the QGP phase beyond the near-critical region accessible at SPS energies.
