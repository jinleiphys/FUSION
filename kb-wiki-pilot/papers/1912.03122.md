# Investigation of interaction of circularly and linearly polarized photon beams with polarized 3He target

> Photodisintegration of polarized 3He by linearly or circularily polarized photons offers a rich choice of observables which can be calculated with high precision using a rigorous scheme of three-nucleon Faddeev equations. Using the (semi)phenomenological AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force we investigate sensitivity of 3He photodisintegration observables to underlying currents taken in the form of a single-nucleon current supplemented by two-body contributions for $π$- and $ρ$-meson exchanges or incorporated by the Siegert theorem. Promising observables to be measured for two- and three-body fragmentation of 3He are identified. These observables form a challenging test ground for consistent forces and currents being under derivation within the framework of chiral perturbation theory. For thre-body 3He photodisintegration several kinematicaly complete configurations, including SST and FSI, are also discussed.

## Key claim

Using the (semi)phenomenological AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, the authors identify promising observables in two- and three-body photodisintegration of polarized 3He that are sensitive to the underlying electromagnetic current operator, but the unpolarized cross section and photon analyzing power A^gamma_x show only small sensitivity to the treatment of two-body current contributions.

## Method

The authors solved three-nucleon Faddeev equations for 3He photodisintegration at photon energies of 15, 20, and 30 MeV, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. The electromagnetic current operator was taken as a single-nucleon current supplemented by two-body contributions either from explicit pi- and rho-meson exchanges or incorporated via the Siegert theorem. All possible polarization observables for two- and three-body fragmentation were calculated and compared between the two current treatments.

## Key numbers

* Photon energies studied: E_gamma = 15, 20, and 30 MeV
* For two-body fragmentation at E_gamma = 30 MeV, the 3He analyzing power A^{3He}_y reaches approximately 0.2
* The unpolarized two-body cross section diminishes by a factor of approximately 3 when photon energy increases from 15 to 30 MeV
* For semi-inclusive three-body fragmentation, the cross section drops by a factor of approximately 4 when photon energy changes from 15 to 30 MeV
* For the symmetric space-star (SST) configuration, the cross section changes from approximately 2 mb/(sr^2 MeV) at E_gamma = 15 MeV to approximately 0.3 mb/(sr^2 MeV) at E_gamma = 30 MeV
* The FSI cross section for exclusive three-body photodisintegration diminishes by a factor of approximately 2 when photon energy increases from 15 to 30 MeV
* Corresponding center-of-mass kinetic energies of the ppn system: 7.2 MeV (E_gamma=15), 12.2 MeV (E_gamma=20), 22.1 MeV (E_gamma=30)

## Context

This work extends previous rigorous Faddeev calculations of 3He photodisintegration to include polarization observables for both linearly and circularly polarized photons interacting with polarized 3He targets. It builds on the availability of high intensity polarized photon beams (Kii 2005, Weller 2008) and polarized 3He targets (Kramer 2007, Seki 2019). The results are intended to guide future experiments and provide a testing ground for consistent chiral forces and currents being derived within chiral perturbation theory (Skoll 2009, Skoll 2011, Krebs 2017, Krebs 2019, Pastore 2008, Pastore 2009), particularly for unresolved problems such as the symmetric space-star (SST) discrepancy in nucleon-deuteron breakup (Din1).
