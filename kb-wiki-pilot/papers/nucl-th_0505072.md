# Electron and Photon Scattering on Three-Nucleon Bound States

> A big spectrum of processes induced by real and virtual photons on the 3He and 3H nuclei is theoretically investigated through many examples based on nonrelativistic Faddeev calculations for bound and continuum states. The modern nucleon-nucleon potential AV18 together with the three-nucleon force UrbanaIX is used. The single nucleon current is augmented by explicit pi- and rho-like two-body currents which fulfill the current continuity equation together with the corresponding parts of the AV18 potential. We also employ the Siegert theorem, which induces many-body contributions to the current operator. The interplay of these different dynamical ingredients in the various electromagnetic processes is studied and the theory is compared to the experimental data. Overall we find fair to good agreement but also cases of strong disagreement between theory and experiment, which calls for improved dynamics. In several cases we refer the reader to the work of other groups and compare their results with ours. In addition we list a number of predictions for observables in different processes which would challenge this dynamical scenario even more stringently and systematically.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates a wide range of real and virtual photon-induced processes on 3He and 3H using nonrelativistic Faddeev calculations, finding overall fair to good agreement with experimental data but also cases of strong disagreement that call for improved dynamics, a hedge glossed over in the abstract's claim of "fair to good agreement."

## Method

The authors solved nonrelativistic Faddeev equations for three-nucleon bound and continuum states using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus the UrbanaIX three-nucleon force. The single-nucleon electromagnetic current was augmented by explicit pi- and rho-like two-body currents that satisfy the continuity equation with the AV18 potential, and the Siegert theorem was employed to induce additional many-body contributions to the current operator.

## Key numbers

- The AV18 NN potential and UrbanaIX 3NF were used as the dynamical input for all calculations.
- Two-body currents of pi- and rho-meson range were explicitly constructed to satisfy current conservation with the corresponding parts of AV18.
- The paper reports no single numerical result for cross sections or form factors; it is a review of methodology and qualitative comparisons to data, with specific numerical results referenced to earlier publications by the same group.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Faddeev calculations for three-nucleon electrodisintegration (Golak et al. 1995, Ishikawa et al. 1994) by systematically including two-body currents and three-nucleon forces across a broad set of electromagnetic processes. It contrasts with variational approaches (Urbana-Argonne group, Carlson 1998), the Lorentz integral transform method (Trento group, Efros et al. 1994), and the hyperspherical harmonic expansion approach (Pisa group), and it enables future experimental tests of the predicted observables listed in the paper's predictions section.
