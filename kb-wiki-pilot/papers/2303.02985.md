# Description of inclusive $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ reaction with the semiclassical distorted wave model

> The description of deuteron-induced inclusive reactions has been an important subject in direct nuclear reaction studies and nuclear data science. For proton-induced inclusive processes, the semiclassical distorted wave model (SCDW) is one of the most successful models based on quantum mechanics. We improve SCDW for deuteron-induced inclusive processes and clarify the importance of the proper treatment of the kinematics of the deuteron inside a nucleus. The double differential cross section (DDX) of the inclusive deuteron-emission process $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ is described by one-step SCDW. The changes in the kinematics due to the distortion effect, the refraction effect, is taken into account by the local semiclassical approximation (LSCA). The calculated DDXs of $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ reasonably reproduce experimental data in the small energy-transfer region and at forward and middle angles with some exceptions. The angular distributions of $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ are improved by including the refraction effect. The proper treatment of the changes in the kinematics of the deuteron inside a nucleus is necessary in describing the ($d$,$d'x$) reaction. The effect of the changes on the DDX of $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ is significant compared to on the proton-induced inclusive process $(p,p^{\prime}x)$ because of the stronger distortion effect on the deuteron.

## Key claim

The semiclassical distorted wave model (SCDW) is extended to deuteron-induced inclusive reactions, and the proper treatment of the changes in deuteron kinematics inside the nucleus via the local semiclassical approximation (LSCA) is necessary to reproduce experimental double differential cross sections, with the effect being more significant than for proton-induced processes due to the stronger distortion effect on the deuteron.

## Method

The double differential cross section (DDX) of the inclusive deuteron-emission process (d,d'x) is described by one-step SCDW using the local Fermi-gas model for nuclear single-particle states and the local semiclassical approximation (LSCA) for deuteron distorted waves. The LSCA accounts for the refraction effect by using a real local momentum that depends on the distorting potential, and the free d-N scattering cross section is used for the elementary process. Calculations are compared with the asymptotic momentum approximation (AMA), which neglects the refraction effect by using the asymptotic momentum instead of the local momentum.

## Key numbers

- DDX calculations are performed for targets including 27Al, 58Ni, 93Nb, 90Zr, 181Ta, 208Pb, and 232Th at incident energies of 70, 80, and 100 MeV
- The LSCA extends the kinematically-allowed center-of-mass scattering angle for 58Ni(d,d'x) at 80 MeV with omega=5 MeV from about 42 degrees (AMA) to 73 degrees (LSCA)
- The LSCA is validated for deuteron propagation up to about 0.7 fm, corresponding to the range of the deuteron-nucleon interaction (about 2.2 fm) divided by the factor 1/(Ad+1)=1/3
- The study focuses on energy transfer omega less than about 15 MeV and scattering angles less than about 60 degrees where one-step processes dominate

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical distorted wave model (SCDW), previously successful for proton-induced inclusive processes (p,p'x), to deuteron-induced reactions. It contrasts with the DEURACS code system that uses the Glauber model with eikonal and adiabatic approximations, which has limitations at low incident energy and large momentum transfer. The approach enables future description of the inclusive (d,nx) reaction important for nuclear data science and the IFMIF project, and will require extension to multi-step processes and explicit treatment of deuteron breakup channels.
