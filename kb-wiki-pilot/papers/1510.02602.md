# Reexamining closed-form formulae for inclusive breakup: Application to deuteron and $^6$Li induced reactions

> The problem of the calculation of inclusive breakup cross sections in nuclear reactions is reexamined. For that purpose, the post-form theory proposed by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985) is revisited, and an alternative derivation of the non-elastic breakup part of the inclusive breakup is presented, making use of the coupled-channels optical theorem. Using the DWBA version of this model, several applications to deuteron and $^6$Li reactions are presented and compared with available data. The validity of the zero-range approximation of the DWBA formula is also investigated by comparing zero-range with full finite-range calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) post-form theory for inclusive breakup cross sections and provides an alternative derivation of the non-elastic breakup (NEB) part using the coupled-channels optical theorem, with the caveat that the DWBA version of the model relies on an approximate factorized form of the total wave function that does not explicitly include excited states of the target.

## Method

The authors revisit the IAV model and derive the NEB formula using a Feshbach projection operator technique, decomposing the wave function into components projected onto the target ground state (elastic) and excited states (non-elastic). They implement the DWBA version of this model both in zero-range and full finite-range approximations, applying it to calculate inclusive breakup cross sections for deuteron-induced reactions and, for the first time, for $^6$Li-induced reactions, comparing with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The paper presents comparisons with available data for deuteron and $^6$Li induced reactions, but no specific numerical cross section values are given in the abstract or introduction; the full text is truncated before the results section.
- The validity of the zero-range approximation of the DWBA formula is investigated by comparing zero-range with full finite-range calculations, but no numerical precision values are provided in the available text.

## Context

This work extends the IAV formalism originally developed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985)] and later reformulated by Austern et al. in a three-body model [Phys. Rep. 154, 125 (1987)]. It addresses the long-standing dispute between the prior-form model of Udagawa and Tamura and the post-form model of Austern and Vincent, which was settled in the IAV work. The paper enables improved calculations of incomplete fusion cross sections in reactions induced by weakly-bound projectiles and supports the surrogate nuclear reaction method for indirect determination of neutron-induced cross sections on short-lived nuclei.
