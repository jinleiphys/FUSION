# Sub-barrier fusion reactions

> The concept of compound nucleus was proposed by Niels Bohr in 1936 to explain narrow resonances observed in scattering of a slow neutron off atomic nuclei. A compound nucleus is a metastable state with a long lifetime, in which all the degrees of freedom are in a sort of thermal equilibrium. Fusion reactions are defined as reactions to form such compound nucleus by merging two atomic nuclei. Here a short description of heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies close the Coulomb barrier is presented. This includes: (i) an overview of a fusion process, (ii) a strong interplay between nuclear structure and fusion, (iii) fusion and multi-dimensional/multi-particle quantum tunneling, and (iv) fusion for superheavy elements.

## Key claim

This review argues that sub-barrier heavy-ion fusion reactions serve as an ideal playground for studying many-particle quantum tunneling, but the abstract's claim of a "strong interplay between nuclear structure and fusion" is qualified by the fact that the standard coupled-channels approach systematically overpredicts cross sections at deep sub-barrier energies (well below the Coulomb barrier), requiring additional modeling of the dynamics after the two nuclei touch.

## Method

The paper reviews the potential model and the Wong formula for fusion cross sections, then presents the coupled-channels approach that couples the relative motion between colliding nuclei to low-lying collective excitations (e.g., rotational states in deformed nuclei like ¹⁵⁴Sm, quadrupole phonon states in ⁵⁸Ni). For deformed nuclei, the total penetrability is computed by averaging angle-dependent penetrabilities over orientation angles. For deep sub-barrier energies, an adiabatic model is introduced that quenches coupling strengths after the two nuclei touch, or alternatively a repulsive core is added to the internucleus potential.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴Sm system, the Coulomb barrier height is V_b ≈ 61.25 MeV; the Wong formula overestimates fusion cross sections well below this barrier because the parabolic approximation underestimates the barrier width.
- For the ¹⁴N+¹²C system, the Coulomb barrier height is V_b ≈ 6.9 MeV; the potential model works well at all energies.
- For the ¹⁶O+¹⁵⁴Sm system, the Coulomb barrier height is V_b ≈ 59 MeV; the deformation parameters are β₂ ≈ 0.30 (quadrupole) and β₄ ≈ 0.05 (hexadecapole).
- For the ²⁰⁹Bi(⁷⁰Zn,n)²⁷⁸Nh reaction to form element 113 (Nihonium), experiments were performed at E_cm = 262 MeV, while the barrier height from the Bass potential is around 260 MeV.
- For the ⁶⁴Ni+⁶⁴Ni system, standard coupled-channels calculations reproduce data above about 89 MeV, but below this energy the experimental data show hindrance with a steeper falloff.
- The hexadecapole deformation parameter of ²⁴Mg was determined as β₄ = -0.11 ± 0.02 from quasi-elastic scattering barrier distribution analysis.
- The predicted proton and neutron magic numbers for the island of stability are Z = 114 and N = 184, with candidates for the next double magic nucleus including (Z,N) = (114,184), (120,172), and (126,184).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on sub-barrier fusion reactions covered in earlier reviews by Refs. [BT98, hagino2012] (theoretical aspects), Ref. [DHRS98] (experimental barrier distributions), and Refs. [Back14, MS17, jiang2021] (deep sub-barrier hindrance). It enables future work on fusion of neutron-rich unstable nuclei, where the breakup process complicates the reaction dynamics and a theoretical model that coherently incorporates breakup, transfer, and coupling effects has yet to be developed. The paper also connects to superheavy element synthesis, where the island of stability predicted by Refs. [myers1966, sobiczewski1966] motivates continued experimental efforts.
