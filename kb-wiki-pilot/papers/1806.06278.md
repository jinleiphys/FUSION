# Four-body eikonal approach to three-body halo nuclei scattering

> Halo nuclei projectiles can undergo into a fragmentation process when scattered by a nucleus target. The corresponding core from exotic nuclei is usually observed while the others fragments of the reaction are not. We use the recently proposed theory by [Phys. Lett. B767, 53 (2017)] with a four-body description of the inclusive breakup reaction where the projectile is 20C described like a two-neutron halo nucleus. The momentum of both neutrons are integrated out giving a generic description of the core angular distribution. In this preliminary study we perform an analysis of the inclusive inelastic breakup cross-section using the eikonal approximation for the distorted wave function of the projectile. A study of the inclusive inelastic cross-sections of 20C from the collision with different targets are presented.

## Key claim
The paper presents a preliminary four-body eikonal treatment of inclusive inelastic breakup reactions for three-body halo nuclei, specifically applying it to the two-neutron halo projectile 20C. The authors hedge that this is a preliminary study and that the full four-body scattering state should ideally be obtained by solving Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, but they instead use an eikonal approximation with the Hussein-McVoy model.

## Method
The authors use the four-body theory of Carlson et al. (Phys. Lett. B767, 53 (2017)) to compute the inclusive inelastic breakup cross-section for 20C projectiles scattering off 12C and 208Pb targets. The core-target interaction is described by the Sao Paulo optical potential, while neutron-target interactions are represented by Woods-Saxon potentials. The projectile internal wave function is obtained from a three-body renormalized model, and the four-body scattering state is approximated using the eikonal approximation with straight-line trajectories for the core distorted waves.

## Key numbers
- Incoming projectile energy: 50 MeV/nucleon for all calculations
- Outgoing core energy: E'_b = 0.8 E_b (primary case) and E'_b = 0.5 E_b (for inelastic effect comparison)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for n-12C interaction: W0_R = 49.9395 MeV, W0_I = 1.8256 MeV, a_R = a_I = 0.676 fm, R_I = R_R = 2.5798 fm
- Neutrons fixed at impact parameters b1 = b2 = 8 fm for the T-matrix analysis

## Context
This work extends the inclusive three-body breakup model developed by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent (IAV), Udagawa and Tamura (UT), and Hussein and McVoy (HM) for two-fragment projectiles to the four-body case with three-fragment projectiles. It applies the formalism of Carlson et al. (2017) to the specific case of 20C, a non-Borromean two-neutron halo nucleus. The next steps identified by the authors include adding the two-fragment distorted waves, incorporating Coulomb scattering, and computing the double fragment inclusive cross-section to provide the complete inelastic cross-section.
