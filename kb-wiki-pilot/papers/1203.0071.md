# Complete Fusion Enhancement and Suppression of Weakly Bound Nuclei at Near Barrier Energies

> We consider the influence of breakup channels on the complete fusion of weakly bound systems in terms of dynamic polarization potentials. It is argued that the enhancement of the cross section at sub-barrier energies may be consistent with recent experimental observations that nucleon transfer, often leading to breakup, is dominant compared to direct breakup. The main trends of the experimental complete fusion cross section for $^{6,7}$Li + $^{209}$Bi are analyzed in the framework of the DPP approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed enhancement of complete fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies for weakly bound nuclei can be consistent with the dominance of nucleon transfer (often leading to breakup) over direct breakup, and that the suppression above the barrier arises from the imaginary part of the dynamic polarization potential associated with prompt direct breakup.

## Method

The authors analyzed high-precision experimental complete fusion cross section data for the $^{6,7}$Li + $^{209}$Bi systems using the dynamic polarization potential (DPP) approach. They compared the renormalized experimental fusion functions to the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) benchmark, and also examined the ratio of cross sections between the two lithium isotopes as a function of energy. The DPP framework was used to separate the effects of couplings to bound or long-lived states (which produce attractive real potentials and enhance fusion) from couplings to short-lived unbound states (which produce repulsive real potentials and absorptive imaginary potentials that suppress fusion).

## Key numbers

- Complete fusion suppression above the Coulomb barrier: 34% for $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi and 26% for $^{7}$Li + $^{209}$Bi, when compared to calculations matching experimentally determined average barrier energies without breakup effects.
- Breakup threshold energies: 1.48 MeV for $^{6}$Li and 2.47 MeV for $^{7}$Li (with one bound excited state at 0.478 MeV).
- Complete fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies are enhanced by nearly one order of magnitude for $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi.
- The ratio of complete fusion cross sections $\sigma_{CF}(^{6}\mathrm{Li})/\sigma_{CF}(^{7}\mathrm{Li})$ is smaller than unity above the barrier, increases below the barrier, and is probably larger than unity at sub-barrier energies.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of complete and total fusion cross sections for weakly bound nuclei using the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) approach introduced by Canto et al. (J. Phys. G 36, 015109, 2009; Nucl. Phys. A 821, 51, 2009). It addresses the apparent contradiction between earlier Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations by Diaz-Torres and Thompson (Phys. Rev. C 65, 024606, 2002), which showed suppression both above and below the barrier, and the experimental systematics showing enhancement at sub-barrier energies. The interpretation relies on recent experimental observations by Luong et al. (Phys. Lett. B 695, 105, 2011) that breakup triggered by nucleon transfer dominates over direct breakup, which enables a consistent explanation of both suppression above the barrier and enhancement below it.
