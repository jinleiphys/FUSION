# Influence of target deformation and deuteron breakup in (d,p) transfer reactions

> The effect of core excitations in transfer reactions of the form A(d, p)B has been found to affect significantly the calculated cross sections and to depend strongly and non-linearly on the incident deuteron energy in Faddeev/AGS calculations. Our goal is to investigate these effects within a coupled-channels formulation of the scattering problem which, in addition of being computationally less demanding than the Faddeev counterpart, may help shed some light into the physical interpretation of the cited effects. We use an extended version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method with explicit inclusion of target excitations within a coupled-channels Born approximation (CDCC-BA) formulation of the transfer transition amplitude. We compare the calculated transfer cross sections with those obtained with an analogous calculation omitting the effect of target excitation. We consider also an adiabatic coupled channels (ACC) method. Our working example is the 10 Be(d,p) 11 Be reaction. We find that both formalisms are able to reproduce the effects of deformation found in Faddeev calculations, whose origin stems from the interference of the direct one-step transfer, and the two-step transfer following target excitation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the strong, energy-dependent suppression of transfer cross sections due to target deformation, previously found in Faddeev/AGS calculations, can be reproduced by coupled-channels methods (CDCC-BA and ACC) that are computationally less demanding; the effect originates from destructive interference between direct one-step transfer and two-step transfer following target excitation, not from modifications of the scattering wave function by deformation.

## Method

The authors use an extended continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method that includes both deuteron breakup and target excitations, combined with a coupled-channels Born approximation (CDCC-BA) for the transfer transition amplitude. The total wave function for the d+A system is expanded in a basis of deuteron ground and discretized continuum states coupled to the target ground state (0+) and first excited state (2+), and the transfer amplitude is computed from four coherent paths: elastic transfer, inelastic transfer, elastic breakup transfer, and inelastic breakup transfer. The formalism is applied to the 10Be(d,p)11Be reaction at several deuteron energies, and results are compared with Faddeev/AGS calculations from Ref. [Del16] and with an adiabatic coupled channels (ACC) method.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors for 11Be ground state: S = 0.846 (s1/2), 0.130 (d5/2), 0.023 (d3/2) from a particle-rotor model with deformation length delta2 = 1.664 fm for 10Be
- The ratio Rx (defined as peak cross section with deformation divided by peak cross section without deformation, normalized by the spectroscopic factor) is computed at deuteron laboratory energies from approximately 10 to 60 MeV
- At Ed = 20 MeV, the main contributor after elastic transfer is deuteron breakup (path III); at Ed = 60 MeV, the second main contributor is target excitation (path II)
- The calculation excluding transfer from states with excited 10Be (paths II and IV) gives Rx close to 1, especially at higher energies

## Context

This work extends recent Faddeev/AGS calculations by Delaunay et al. (Refs. [Del13, Del15, Del16]) that found strong and non-linear energy dependence of core excitation effects in (d,p) transfer reactions, by showing that these effects can be reproduced within the more widely used coupled-channels framework (CDCC-BA and ACC). The method enables future analysis of transfer reactions induced by weakly-bound projectiles including halo nuclei, where both projectile breakup and target excitation must be treated simultaneously, and provides a computationally efficient alternative to the full Faddeev formalism.
