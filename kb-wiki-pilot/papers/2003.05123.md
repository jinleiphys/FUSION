# Investigation of contributions of the $2_2^+$ resonance in $^6$He via analysis of $^6$He($p$, $p'$)

> We investigate the contribution of the $2^{+}_{2}$ resonance in $^6$He to observables via analysis of the $^6$He($p,p'$) reaction by using the continuum-discretized coupled channels method combined with the complex-scaling method. In this study, we obtain the $2^{+}_{2}$ state with the resonant energy 2.25 MeV and the decay width 3.75 MeV and analyse contributions of resonances and nonresonant continuum states to the cross section separately. It is found that the $2^{+}_{2}$ state plays an important role in the energy spectrum. Furthermore, contributions of nonresonant continuum states are also important to clarify the properties of the $2^{+}_{2}$ state.

## Key claim

The $2_2^+$ resonance in $^6$He contributes to the breakup cross section as a shoulder peak rather than a distinct peak structure, and its extracted resonant energy and decay width are significantly underestimated if nonresonant continuum contributions are neglected in the analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a four-body continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) analysis of the $^6$He($p,p'$) reaction, treating the system as $^4$He + $n$ + $n$ + $p$. They combined CDCC with the complex-scaling method (CSM) to separate resonant and nonresonant continuum contributions to the double differential cross section, using the Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with antisymmetrization treated via the Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT) theory.

## Key numbers

- $2_2^+$ state: resonant energy $E_r = 2.25$ MeV, decay width $\Gamma = 3.75$ MeV (from CSM calculation)
- $2_1^+$ state: $E_r = 0.848$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.136$ MeV (from CSM calculation)
- Ground state energy of $^6$He: $-0.972$ MeV
- Nonresonant continuum contributions account for about 30% of the total inelastic cross section integrated up to $\varepsilon = 1.5$ MeV at $E/A_{\rm P} = 41$ MeV
- When fitting the breakup cross section with Breit-Wigner functions neglecting nonresonant continuum, the $2_2^+$ parameters shift to $E_r = 1.71$ MeV, $\Gamma = 1.97$ MeV (fit up to 3 MeV) or $E_r = 1.92$ MeV, $\Gamma = 2.72$ MeV (fit up to 4 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the CDCC+CSM approach previously applied to $^{11}$Li($p,p'$) reactions by Matsumoto et al. (2019) to the case of $^6$He. The calculated $2_2^+$ resonance parameters are consistent with those from Myo et al. (2007) using CSM. The analysis demonstrates that the $2_2^+$ state properties extracted from $^8$He($p,t$)$^6$He reactions by Mougeot et al. (2012) may underestimate the true resonant energy and width because nonresonant continuum contributions were not properly accounted for. The work enables more accurate determination of broad resonances in neutron-rich nuclei by showing that both resonant and nonresonant continuum contributions must be treated simultaneously.
