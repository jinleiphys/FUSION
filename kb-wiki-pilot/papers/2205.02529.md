# Nucleon-antinucleon interaction

> A review is presented of the antinucleon-nucleon interaction, and some related issues such as fundamental symmetries, annihilation mechanisms, antinucleon-nucleus scattering, antiprotonic atoms and neutron-antineutron oscillations. The overall perspective is historical but the modern approaches are also presented.

## Key claim

This review presents the antinucleon-nucleon interaction as a long-range part due to meson exchanges supplemented by annihilation, noting that the main pattern is a very strong annihilation with a tail of Yukawa-type contribution, and that filters are required to see the latter on top of a very strong absorption.

## Method

The paper reviews the historical development and modern approaches to the antinucleon-nucleon interaction, covering the G-parity rule relating NN and antinucleon-nucleon amplitudes, optical model descriptions with empirical complex cores, chiral effective field theory approaches, and phenomenological analyses of annihilation mechanisms at both hadronic and quark levels. Experimental results from LEAR, Brookhaven, and other facilities are surveyed, including elastic scattering, spin observables, annihilation branching ratios, antiprotonic atoms, and antinucleon-nucleus scattering.

## Key numbers

- Charge to mass ratios |q|/m of antiproton and proton are identical to about 10^{-10}
- Magnetic moments |mu| are identical to about 10^{-9}
- Annihilation cross section sigma_ann is nearly twice larger than elastic sigma_el
- Strong absorption needed up to at least 0.8 fm in optical potentials
- Analyzing power of elastic antiproton-proton scattering measured at p_lab = 679 MeV/c shows moderate values
- Asymmetry in antiproton-proton to pi pi and KK reactions reaches |A_n| ~ 1 in some wide ranges of energy and angles
- For neutron-antineutron oscillations in deuteron: T_r ~ 2.5 x 10^{22} s^{-1} (Dover et al.) and T_r ~ 1.1 x 10^{22} s^{-1} (Oosterhof et al.), with Haidenbauer and Meissner finding almost perfect agreement with Dover et al.
- For Ar-40 relevant to DUNE: T_r ~ 5.6 x 10^{22} s^{-1}
- Free neutron-antineutron oscillation limit: tau_nnbar >~ 10^{-8} s

## Context

This review extends the long tradition of antinucleon-nucleon studies dating from the antiproton discovery in 1955 by Chamberlain and Segre, through the LEAR era at CERN, to modern ELENA and PANDA programs. It covers the G-parity rule of Fermi and Yang (1959), optical models following Ball and Chew, Bryan and Phillips, the Dover-Richard and Kohno-Weise potentials, and chiral effective theory approaches by Dai et al. The work enables continued searches for baryonium states, improved understanding of annihilation mechanisms, and applications to neutron-antineutron oscillation searches in experiments like DUNE and Super-Kamiokande.
