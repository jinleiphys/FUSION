# Inclusive deuteron-induced reactions and final neutron states

> We present in this paper a formalism for deuteron-induced inclusive reactions. We disentangle direct elastic breakup contributions from other processes (which we generically call non-elastic breakup) implying a capture of the neutron both above and below the neutron emission threshold. The reaction is described as a two step process, namely the breakup of the deuteron followed by the propagation of the neutron-target system driven by an optical potential. The final state interaction between the neutron and the target can eventually form an excited compound nucleus. Within this context, the direct neutron transfer to a sharp bound state is a limiting case of the present formalism.

# Inclusive deuteron-induced reactions and final neutron states

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for deuteron-induced inclusive reactions that treats both elastic breakup and non-elastic breakup (including neutron capture above and below the neutron emission threshold) within a unified two-step framework, where the direct neutron transfer to a sharp bound state emerges as a limiting case when the imaginary part of the neutron-target optical potential is small compared to the level spacing and the resonance width.

## Method

The reaction is described as a two-step process: breakup of the deuteron followed by propagation of the neutron-target system driven by an optical potential. The formalism uses a three-body Hamiltonian with a spectator approximation for the outgoing proton, and expresses the non-elastic breakup cross section in terms of a neutron Green's function and source term in the prior representation. The neutron wavefunction is obtained via partial wave expansion and numerical evaluation of two-dimensional integrals, with boundary conditions implemented separately for positive (scattering) and negative (bound) neutron energies.

## Context

This work revives and extends theoretical efforts from the late 1970s to early 1990s (Kerman and McVoy 1979, Udagawa and Tamura 1980, Austern and Vincent 1981, Ichimura et al. 1985) that addressed inclusive breakup reactions but left unresolved controversies. The formalism is directly applicable to the surrogate reaction method for studying neutron-induced reactions on radioactive isotopes, where an exotic beam impinges on a deuteron target to form an excited compound nucleus that decays via gamma emission or neutron emission. The approach is implemented in computer codes developed alongside related work by Potel et al. (2015), Lei and Moro (2015), and Carlson et al. (2015).
