# Core-excitation effects in three-body breakup reactions studied using the Faddeev formalism

> Previous studies of $(d,p)$ reactions in three-body (proton, neutron, nuclear core) systems revealed a nontrivial effect of the core excitation: the transfer cross section cannot be factorized into the spectroscopic factor and the single-particle cross section obtained neglecting the core excitation. This observable, up to a kinematic factor, is the angular distribution of the core nucleus in the $(p,d)$ reaction. The study of the core excitation effect for the most closely related observable in the $(p,pn)$ three-body breakup, i.e., the core angular distribution, is aimed in the present work. Breakup of the one-neutron halo nucleus in the collision with the proton is described using three-body Faddeev-type equations extended to include the excitation of the nuclear core. The integral equations for transition operators are solved in the momentum-space partial-wave representation. Breakup of 11Be nucleus as well as of model $A=11$ $p$-wave nuclei is studied at beam energies of 30, 60, and 200 MeV per nucleon. Angular and momentum distributions for the 10Be core in ground and excited states is calculated. In sharp contrast to $(p,d)$ reactions, the differential cross section in most cases factorizes quite well into the spectroscopic factor and the single-particle cross section. Due to different reaction mechanisms the core excitation effect in the breakup is very different from transfer reactions. A commonly accepted approach to evaluate the cross section, i.e., the rescaling of single-particle model results by the corresponding spectroscopic factor, appears to be reliable for breakup though it fails in general for transfer reactions.

## Key claim

In sharp contrast to (p,d) transfer reactions, the differential cross section for (p,pn) three-body breakup factorizes quite well into the spectroscopic factor and the single-particle cross section, meaning the commonly accepted approach of rescaling single-particle model results by the spectroscopic factor appears reliable for breakup though it fails in general for transfer reactions.

## Method

The three-body system of proton, neutron, and nuclear core was treated using Faddeev-type equations (Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas version) extended to include excitation of the nuclear core in an extended Hilbert space with ground and excited state sectors. The integral equations for transition operators were solved in the momentum-space partial-wave representation, with the breakup operator obtained as a quadrature involving elastic and transfer operators. Breakup of 11Be and model A=11 p-wave nuclei was studied at beam energies of 30, 60, and 200 MeV per nucleon, using the CD Bonn potential for n-p interactions and Koning-Delaroche or Watson optical potentials for nucleon-core interactions.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor SF(0+) = 0.854 for the 10Be(0+) component in the 11Be(1/2+) bound state
- Core excitation energy Δm_C = 3.368 MeV for 10Be (2+ state)
- Deformation parameter β2 = 0.67 and deformation length δ2 = 1.664 fm for rotational quadrupole deformation
- Neutron separation energy S_n = 0.504 MeV for 11Be(1/2+)
- For the 11Be(1/2+) breakup at 60 MeV: integrated cross section σ(0+) = 67.1 mb, σ(2+) = 14.9 mb, total σ = 82.0 mb (KD potential)
- For the 11Be(1/2+) breakup at 60 MeV with Watson potential: σ(0+) = 72.5 mb, σ(2+) = 20.8 mb, total σ = 93.3 mb
- The deviation D = (R-1)×100% for breakup stays well below 10% in magnitude at 60 MeV, while for transfer it reaches almost 50% at forward angles
- For p-wave model with S_n = 5.0 MeV at 60 MeV: σ(0+) = 14.8 mb, σ(2+) = 7.5 mb, total σ = 22.3 mb
- The CX effect for ground-state core integrated cross section (D̅) remains below 5% in all considered cases

## Context

This work extends previous studies of core excitation effects in (d,p) transfer reactions using the same Faddeev-type formalism (Deltuva 2013, 2015, 2016, 2017) to the closely related (p,pn) three-body breakup reaction. It contrasts with earlier DWIA studies (Moro 2012) and XCDCC calculations (Summers 2007, de Diego 2014, 2017) that found important CX effects for resonant breakup. The results enable reliable use of the simple spectroscopic factor rescaling approach for breakup reactions, while confirming its failure for transfer reactions due to different reaction mechanisms - breakup being dominated by neutron-proton quasi-free scattering while transfer involves high-order rescattering between all three particles.
