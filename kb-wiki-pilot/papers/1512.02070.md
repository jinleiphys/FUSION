# Core momentum distribution in two-neutron halo nuclei

> The core momentum distribution of a weakly-bound neutron-neutron-core exotic nucleus is computed within a renormalized zero-range three-body model, with interactions in the s-wave channel. The halo wave-function in momentum space is obtained by using as inputs the two-body scattering lengths and the two-neutron separation energy. The core momentum densities are computed for $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be $^{20}$C and $^{22}$C. The model describes the experimental data for $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be and to some extend $^{20}$C. The recoil momentum distribution of the $^{20}$C from the breakup of $^{22}$C nucleus is computed for different two-neutron separation energies, and from the comparison with recent experimental data the two-neutron separation energy is estimated in the range $100\lesssim S_{2n}\lesssim 400$ KeV. The recoil momentum distribution depends weakly on the neutron-$^{20}$C scattering length, while the matter radius is strongly sensitive to it. The expected universality of the momentum distribution width is verified by also considering excited states for the system.

## Key claim

The renormalized zero-range three-body model can describe the core recoil momentum distributions of two-neutron halo nuclei using only two-body scattering lengths and the two-neutron separation energy as inputs, but the model's applicability is limited to the narrow (halo) component of the distribution and requires adding a separate wide distribution to match experimental data for the inner part of the halo neutron orbits.

## Method

The authors solve coupled subtracted Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equations for the spectator functions χ_nn(q) and χ_nc(q) within a renormalized zero-range three-body model, using s-wave interactions only. The core momentum distribution n(q_c) is obtained from the three-body wave function in momentum space, which is constructed from the spectator functions and depends on the two-neutron separation energy S_2n, the neutron-neutron virtual-state energy (fixed at 143 keV), and the neutron-core subsystem energy. The calculated narrow distributions are folded with experimental resolution and added to a phenomenological wide distribution before comparison with breakup data.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹Li: computed narrow σ = 22 MeV/c compared to experimental σ = 21(3) MeV/c, using S_2n = 369 keV and ¹⁰Li s-wave virtual state energy E_nc = 50 keV
- For ¹⁴Be: experimental FWHM = 92.7 ± 2.7 MeV/c with S_2n = 1.337 MeV; model constrains ¹³Be s-wave virtual state energy to less than 1 MeV
- For ²²C: two-neutron separation energy estimated in the range 100 ≲ S_2n ≲ 400 keV from comparison with experimental core recoil momentum distribution data
- For ²²C: root-mean-square matter radius computed as 3.5-4.5 fm for E_nc between 0 and 1 MeV, compared to experimental value 5.4 ± 0.9 fm
- For ²⁰C: S_2n = 3.5 MeV and ¹⁹C one-neutron separation energy S_1n = 580 keV used as inputs

## Context

This work extends the renormalized zero-range three-body model previously applied to halo nuclei (reviewed in FrePPNP12) to compute core momentum distributions for ¹¹Li, ¹⁴Be, ²⁰C, and ²²C. The model builds on earlier studies of universal scaling functions for three-body systems with contact interactions (YamPRA13, YamNPA04) and addresses the unresolved issue of ²²C's binding energy, where mass measurements give S_2n = -0.14(46) MeV (GauPRL12) and indirect evidence suggests binding below 70 keV (MosNPA13). The results enable constraints on the two-neutron separation energy of ²²C through combined analysis of core momentum distributions (KobPRC12), matter radius measurements (TanPRL10), and the ²¹C virtual-state energy.
