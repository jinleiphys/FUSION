# Semiclassical treatment of fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We describe a semiclassical treatment of nuclear fusion reactions involving weakly bound nuclei. In this treatment, the complete fusion probabilities are approximated by products of two factors: a tunneling probability and the probability that the system is in its ground state at the strong absorption radius. We investigate the validity of the method in a schematic two-channel application, where the channels in the continuum are represented by a single resonant state. Comparisons with full coupled-channels calculations are performed. The agreement between semiclassical and quantal calculations isquite good, suggesting that the procedure may be extended to more sophisticated discretizations of the continuum.

## Key claim

The semiclassical Alder-Winther method, when extended via analytical continuation of the time variable into the classically forbidden region and combined with a finite-width treatment of the resonant continuum channel, yields complete fusion cross sections that agree well with full quantum coupled-channels calculations both above and below the Coulomb barrier, provided the effective channel representing the continuum has a nonzero width.

## Method

The authors model the collision of a weakly bound projectile (e.g., 6He) with a target using a two-channel semiclassical approach: the elastic channel and a single effective resonant state representing the breakup continuum. The classical trajectory of the relative motion is computed, and the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the intrinsic degrees of freedom is solved via the Alder-Winther equations. To handle sub-barrier energies, the trajectory is analytically continued into the complex time plane, allowing the system to tunnel through the barrier. The effective resonant channel is given a finite width (Gamma = 2 MeV) via a constant imaginary potential to simulate the irreversible nature of breakup. The complete fusion probability is then approximated as the product of the survival probability (the probability that the projectile remains in its ground state at the point of closest approach) and the tunneling probability through the fusion barrier.

## Key numbers

- Breakup threshold energy for 6He: B = 0.975 MeV
- Width of the effective resonant channel: Gamma = 2 MeV
- Coulomb barrier height: V_B approximately 20 MeV
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters used: V_0 = -50 MeV, r_0r = 1.25 fm, a_r = 0.65 fm, W_0 = -50 MeV, r_0i = 1.0 fm, a_i = 0.65 fm
- Cut-off radius for the imaginary potential: R_infinity approximately 20 fm (beyond which results become independent of the cut-off)

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Alder-Winther method, previously applied to bound-state coupled-channels problems (Hussein et al., 2004), to the case where one channel represents a resonance in the continuum, as relevant for weakly bound projectiles like 6He. It addresses the failure of the standard semiclassical approach at sub-barrier energies, where it severely underestimated the fusion cross section. The improved method, using complex-time trajectories and a finite-width resonant state, reproduces the barrier-lowering effect seen in full quantum Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations (Diaz-Torres and Thompson, 2002). This enables future extension to more realistic discretizations of the continuum, potentially allowing estimates of incomplete fusion and sequential fusion contributions that are not accessible in standard CDCC descriptions.
