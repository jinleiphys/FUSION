# Coulomb breakup effects on the elastic cross section of $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering near Coulomb barrier energies

> We accurately analyze the $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering at 19 and 22.5 MeV near the Coulomb barrier energy, using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) based on the $n$+$n$+$^4$He+$^{209}$Bi four-body model. The three-body breakup continuum of $^6$He is discretized by diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian of $^6$He in a space spanned by the Gaussian basis functions. The calculated elastic and total reaction cross sections are in good agreement with the experimental data, while the CDCC calculation based on the di-neutron model of $^6$He, i.e., the $^2n$+$^{4}$He+$^{209}$Bi three-body model, does not reproduce the data.

## Key claim

The four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) based on the $n$+$n$+$^4$He+$^{209}$Bi model reproduces the measured elastic and total reaction cross sections for $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering at 19 and 22.5 MeV with no free adjustable parameters, while the three-body CDCC based on the di-neutron ($^2n$+$^4$He) model overestimates the total reaction cross section by a factor of about three and underestimates the elastic cross section at middle angles.

## Method

The authors applied four-body CDCC to the $n$+$n$+$^4$He+$^{209}$Bi system, discretizing the three-body breakup continuum of $^6$He by diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian of $^6$He in a space spanned by Gaussian basis functions (the Gaussian expansion method, GEM). They included $^6$He states with $I^\pi = 0^+$, $1^-$, and $2^+$ up to an excitation energy of 7 MeV, and solved coupled differential equations for the relative motion between $^6$He and $^{209}$Bi. For comparison, they also performed three-body CDCC calculations treating $^6$He as a $^2n$+$^4$He two-body system.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies: 19 MeV and 22.5 MeV (laboratory frame) for $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering near the Coulomb barrier
- $B(E1)$ strength from the $n$+$n$+$^4$He model: 0.9 $e^2$fm$^2$; from the $^2n$+$^4$He model: 1.5 $e^2$fm$^2$ (the former agrees with the experimental value from Aumann et al.)
- Number of discretized continuum states used: 37 for $0^+$, 44 for $1^-$, and 53 for $2^+$ states in four-body CDCC; 9 states per $I^\pi$ in three-body CDCC
- Maximum Gaussian range parameters: 15 fm for the four-body calculation (larger than the 10 fm used in previous nuclear breakup studies)
- Maximum orbital angular momentum $L$: 200; asymptotic matching at $R=200$ fm

## Context

This work extends the four-body CDCC formalism previously applied to $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering (where only nuclear breakup was significant) to low-energy scattering where both nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes are important. It contradicts the earlier three-body CDCC analysis by Keeley et al. (2003) using the di-neutron model, which overestimated the total reaction cross section by a factor of three and did not reproduce elastic angular distributions. The results are consistent with the finding of Rusek et al. (2005) that the di-neutron model overestimates the $E1$ excitation strength, but here the full four-body treatment achieves agreement without artificially scaling coupling potentials. This work enables accurate analysis of $^6$He scattering on heavy targets near the Coulomb barrier, with implications for understanding halo nucleus reaction mechanisms.
