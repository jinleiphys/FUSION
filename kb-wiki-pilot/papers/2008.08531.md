# Neutron capture cross sections of radioactive nuclei

> Alternative methods to calculate neutron capture cross sections on radioactive nuclei are reported using the theory of Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) developed by Hussein and McVoy [1]. The statistical coupled-channels theory proposed in Ref. [2] is further extended in the realm of random matrices. The case of reactions with the projectile and the target being two-cluster nuclei is also analyzed and applications are made for scattering from a deuteron target [3]. An extension of the theory to a three-cluster projectile incident on a two-cluster target is also discussed. The theoretical developments described here should open new possibilities to obtain information on the neutron capture cross sections of radioactive nuclei using indirect methods.

## Key claim

The Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) theory can be used to extract neutron capture cross sections on radioactive nuclei from indirect measurements of breakup reactions, but the method requires that the neutron capture cross section be obtained by subtracting other direct reaction contributions (such as inelastic excitation) from the total reaction cross section extracted from the inclusive proton spectrum.

## Method

The authors apply the INEB theory developed by Hussein and McVoy to reactions such as projectile + d → p + (n + target), where the differential cross section for the emitted proton is proportional to the total reaction cross section of the n + target system including medium corrections. They extend the statistical coupled-channels theory proposed in Ref. [BDH14] using random matrix methods to treat the coupling to a large number of weakly-coupled channels, deriving approximate expressions for the S-matrix of strongly-coupled states and the inclusive cross section to weakly-coupled states. The formalism is also extended to cases involving three-cluster projectiles incident on two-cluster targets.

## Key numbers

- Thermal neutron capture cross section of $^{135}$Xe: $2.65 \times 10^6$ barns (largest known neutron-induced reaction cross section)
- Thermal neutron capture cross section of $^{157}$Gd: $[2.54 \pm 0.008] \times 10^5$ barns
- Thermal neutron capture cross section of $^{88}$Zr: $[8.61 \pm 0.69] \times 10^5$ barns
- Thermal neutron capture cross section of $^{113}$Cd: $[2.06 \pm 0.04] \times 10^4$ barns
- Thermal neutron capture cross section of $^{149}$Sm: $[4.014 \pm 0.06] \times 10^4$ barns
- Thermal neutron capture cross section of $^{10}$B: approximately 4000 barns (for epithermal neutrons: $3.80 \times 10^3$ barns)
- Calculated neutron absorption cross section for (d,p) reactions at 30 MeV/nucleon on $^{135}$Xe: $7.02 \times 10^2$ mb (s-state contribution) and $2.85 \times 10^2$ mb (d-state contribution)
- Calculated neutron absorption cross section for (d,p) reactions at 30 MeV/nucleon on $^{157}$Gd: $7.31 \times 10^1$ mb (s-state) and $17.3$ mb (d-state)
- Calculated neutron absorption cross section for (d,p) reactions at 30 MeV/nucleon on $^{88}$Zr: $1.54 \times 10^2$ mb (s-state) and $0.37 \times 10^2$ mb (d-state)
- Predicted inclusive proton spectrum features: low energy peak at 2.22 MeV and weaker peak at approximately 5.00 MeV

## Context

This work extends the INEB theory originally developed in Refs. [IAV1985, UT1981, Ichimura1982, HM1985] and popularized by Hussein and collaborators, applying it to neutron capture on radioactive nuclei including neutron poisons such as $^{135}$Xe. The statistical coupled-channels approach from Ref. [BDH14] is extended using random matrix methods to handle the large number of channels typical in reactions with weakly-bound nuclei, complementing continuum-discretized-coupled-channels (CDCC) methods reviewed in Ref. [Mas212]. The formalism enables indirect determination of neutron capture cross sections on radioactive nuclei relevant to astrophysical r- and s-processes and nuclear reactor physics, and can be benchmarked using stable targets such as $^{10}$B before application to radioactive species.
