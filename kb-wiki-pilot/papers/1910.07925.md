# Quantum theory of complete and incomplete fusion in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We propose a new quantum mechanical method to evaluate complete and incomplete fusion in collisions of weakly bound nuclei. The method is applied to the $^{7}{\rm Li}+^{209}{\rm Bi}$ system and the results are compared to experimental data. The overall agreement between theory and experiment is very good, above and below the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim
The authors propose a new quantum mechanical method to evaluate complete fusion (CF) and incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections in collisions of weakly bound nuclei, applicable to any projectile that breaks up into two fragments. The method is applied to the 7Li + 209Bi system and shows very good overall agreement with experimental data above and below the Coulomb barrier, though above 35 MeV center-of-mass energy the calculated CF cross section underestimates experiment by about 10% and the TF cross section exceeds experiment by up to 15%.

## Method
The method uses continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations with an imaginary potential that acts separately on each fragment (3H and 4He) rather than on the projectile center of mass, allowing off-diagonal matrix elements within the bound and continuum subspaces. The total fusion cross section is split into contributions from bound channels (assigned to direct complete fusion, DCF) and continuum channels, from which ICF and sequential complete fusion (SCF) probabilities are extracted using absorption probabilities for each fragment. The calculations were performed with a new CF-ICF computer code using intrinsic and radial wave functions from the CDCC version of the FRESCO code, with the Sao Paulo potential for real interactions and Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials.

## Key numbers
- Breakup threshold of 7Li: 2.47 MeV (into 3H and 4He)
- Coulomb barrier height for 7Li + 209Bi: V_B = 28.2 MeV (1.2 MeV lower than the Sao Paulo potential barrier when projectile structure is ignored)
- Continuum discretization used orbital angular momenta up to l_max = 4 hbar and energies up to epsilon_max = 8 MeV
- For l = 0, 1, 2, and 4 hbar: bins of constant width with density of 1 bin/MeV
- At l = 3 hbar: bin density up to 10 bins/MeV around resonances at 2.2 and 4.2 MeV
- Woods-Saxon imaginary potential parameters: W_0 = 50 MeV, R_w = 1.0*(A_i^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3)) fm, a_w = 0.2 fm
- Above 35 MeV: calculated CF cross section underestimates experiment by ~10%
- Above 35 MeV: calculated TF cross section exceeds experiment by up to ~15%

## Context
This work extends earlier quantum mechanical approaches by Hagino et al. (HVD00) and Diaz-Torres and Thompson (DiT02), which were limited to projectiles like 11Be that break into a heavy charged fragment and a light uncharged one, and cannot handle projectiles like 7Li with fragments of comparable mass. It improves upon the semiclassical models of MCD14 and KCD18 by including full quantum mechanical tunneling effects, and addresses limitations of the recent spectator-participant inclusive breakup model by Lei and Moro (LeM19), which could not evaluate ICF individually. The method enables future generalization to projectiles that break into three fragments, such as 9Be.
