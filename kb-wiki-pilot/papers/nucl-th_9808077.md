# Hadron Probing of the Deuteron Structure at Short Distances in Deuteron Breakup Reactions

> We discuss deuteron $A(d,p)X$ breakup and cumulative pion production $A(d,π)X$ in the framework of constituent quark model of the deuteron. We demonstrate that consideration of the Pauli principle at the quark level, as well as multiple scattering, affect drastically cross section and polarization observables of these reactions and provide good description of the experimental data.

# Hadron Probing of the Deuteron Structure at Short Distances in Deuteron Breakup Reactions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including the Pauli principle at the constituent quark level and multiple scattering effects in the deuteron wave function drastically changes cross sections and polarization observables in deuteron breakup reactions, providing good agreement with experimental data, but only when using the simplest one-channel Resonating Group Method approach with the Nijm-I potential, and with the caveat that the Delta-Delta component does not contribute to (d,p) breakup despite being the largest non-nucleonic component.

## Method

The deuteron wave function was constructed using the Resonating Group Method with quark antisymmetrization, decomposing the six-quark system into 3q × 3q clusters that include not only the standard pn component but also Delta-Delta, NN*, N*N, and N*N*' components corresponding to baryon resonance states. Multiple scattering effects were incorporated using the Bertocchi-Treleani model. For cumulative pion production, the model assumed the deuteron decays into a virtual Delta-Delta pair, one Delta is absorbed by the target, and the other yields the cumulative pion and a nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The Delta-Delta component has the largest probability among the non-nucleonic components of the deuteron, but does not contribute to (d,p) breakup
- Calculations were performed for 0-degree inclusive 12C(d,p) breakup and cumulative pion production A(d,pi)X
- Results were obtained with the Nijm-I potential
- The maximum number of excitation quanta in the N* resonance in the oscillator quark model is denoted as N, with comparisons shown between IA (impulse approximation) and N=0 to demonstrate the importance of multiple scattering
- The tensor analyzing power T20 and polarization transfer kappa0 were calculated as functions of the relativistic internal momentum k in the deuteron

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach to the deuteron developed by Glozman and Kuchina (GBKK, 1988) and the Resonating Group Method renormalization of Wildermuth and Tang. It directly addresses experimental data from the Ableev (1983-1990), Perdrisat (1987), Punjabi (1989), Nomofilov (1994), Aono (1995), Azhgirey (1996), and Kuehn (1994) collaborations on deuteron breakup, and Baldin (1982) and Afanasiev (1997) on cumulative pion production. The results enable further study of short-distance deuteron structure through the P-wave components generated by quark exchange, which any known quark approach to the deuteron wave function produces.
