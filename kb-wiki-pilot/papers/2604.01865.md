# Tetraquark-Jet Systems at the High-Luminosity LHC

> We investigate the high-energy production of tetraquark-jet systems at the LHC and its forthcoming High-Luminosity upgrade. In this review, we examine the leading-power fragmentation of fully heavy tetraquarks ($T_{4Q}$) in hadronic collisions, highlighting their relevance as novel probes of multiquark dynamics in QCD. Our analysis relies on the hadron-structure-oriented TQ4Q1.1 fragmentation functions, built within a nonrelativistic QCD framework that incorporates both gluon- and heavy-quark-initiated channels. Threshold-consistent DGLAP evolution is performed through the HF-NRevo scheme, enabling a unified treatment of mass thresholds and scale variations. We also provide a systematic discussion of uncertainties arising from color-composite long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) and from perturbative hard- and fragmentation-scale inputs (H- and F-MHOUs). Phenomenological predictions are obtained using the (sym)Jethad framework at NLL/NLO$^+$ accuracy for semi-inclusive tetraquark-jet production at the LHC and beyond. This review connects the emerging spectroscopy of fully heavy exotics with modern fragmentation-based approaches to hadron structure and high-energy QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the leading-power fragmentation of fully heavy tetraquarks ($T_{4Q}$) in hadronic collisions, analyzed through the TQ4Q1.1 fragmentation functions evolved with the HF-NRevo scheme, provides novel probes of multiquark dynamics in QCD, but the analysis is limited to semi-inclusive tetraquark-jet production at NLL/NLO$^+$ accuracy and depends on systematic uncertainties from color-composite long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) and perturbative hard- and fragmentation-scale inputs (H- and F-MHOUs).

## Method

The authors constructed the TQ4Q1.1 fragmentation functions within a nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) framework, incorporating both gluon- and heavy-quark-initiated channels for fully heavy tetraquarks ($T_{4Q}$) in scalar ($0^{++}$), tensor ($2^{++}$), and axial-vector ($1^{+-}$) spin-parity channels. They performed threshold-consistent DGLAP evolution using the HF-NRevo scheme, which enables a unified treatment of mass thresholds and scale variations, and propagated uncertainties from LDMEs and missing higher-order uncertainties (MHOUs) through a replica-based strategy. Phenomenological predictions for semi-inclusive tetraquark-jet production at the LHC and HL-LHC were obtained using the (sym)Jethad framework at NLL/NLO$^+$ accuracy within the hybrid collinear and high-energy factorization (HyF) scheme.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers fully charmed tetraquarks ($T_{4c}$) and fully bottomed tetraquarks ($T_{4b}$) across scalar ($0^{++}$), tensor ($2^{++}$), and axial-vector ($1^{+-}$) channels
- The TQ4Q1.1 fragmentation functions update the earlier TQ4Q1.0 set, which targeted scalar and tensor channels for fully charmed tetraquarks
- The CMS Collaboration reported three resonant structures in the $4m_c < M < 7$ GeV region for fully charmed tetraquarks, with an angular analysis favoring $J^{PC}=2^{++}$ assignment for the leading peak
- The LHCb discovery of $X(6900)$ in the double $J/\psi$ spectrum is interpreted as either scalar ($0^{++}$) or tensor ($2^{++}$) excitation of a fully charmed $T_{4c}$ tetraquark
- The ANDY observation at RHIC reported a resonance near 18.15 GeV in Cu-Au collisions, consistent with predictions for $T_{4b}$

## Context

This work extends the fragmentation-based approach to exotic hadrons initiated by the first-generation TQ4Q1.0 fragmentation functions for fully charmed tetraquarks and the TQHL1.0 framework for heavy-light tetraquarks, building on NRQCD-based inputs for gluon fragmentation from Feng et al. (2020) and heavy-quark fragmentation from Bai et al. (2024). The analysis connects the emerging spectroscopy of fully heavy exotics (following the LHCb discoveries of $X(3872)$, $T_{cc}^+$, and $X(6900)$, and CMS spin-parity determinations) with modern fragmentation-based approaches to hadron structure and high-energy QCD, enabling systematic comparisons with data from the HL-LHC, EIC, and FCC.
