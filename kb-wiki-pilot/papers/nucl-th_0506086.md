# Influence of Chaos on the fusion enhancement by electron screening

> We study the effect of screening by bound electrons in low energy nuclear reactions. We use molecular dynamics to simulate the reactions involving many electrons: D+$d$, D+D, $^3$He+$d$, $^3$He+D, $^6$Li+$d$, $^6$Li+D, $^7$Li+$p$, $^7$Li+H. Quantum effects corresponding to the Pauli and Heisenberg principles are enforced by constraints in terms of the phase space occupancy. In addition to the well known adiabatic and sudden limits, we propose a new "dissipative limit" which is expected to be important not only at high energies but in the extremely low energy region. The dissipative limit is associated with the chaotic behavior of the electronic motion. It affects also the magnitude of the enhancement factor. We discuss also numerical experiments using polarized targets. The derived enhancement factors in our simulation are in agreement with those extracted within the $R$-matrix approach.

# Influence of Chaos on the fusion enhancement by electron screening

## Key claim
The authors propose a new "dissipative limit" for the electron screening enhancement factor, associated with chaotic behavior of electronic motion, which is expected to be important not only at high energies but in the extremely low energy region; the enhancement factors from their constrained molecular dynamics simulations agree with those extracted within the R-matrix approach, but the screening potential values depend on whether the projectile carries bound electrons and on the polarization of the target.

## Method
The authors used constrained molecular dynamics (CoMD) with Lagrange multipliers to enforce the Pauli exclusion principle and Heisenberg uncertainty principle, simulating head-on collisions for eight reaction systems. They treated tunneling by switching to imaginary-time evolution when the collective momentum of the two nuclei becomes zero, computing the penetrability from the action integral, and obtained the enhancement factor as the ratio of penetrability with electrons to that without electrons for each event.

## Key numbers
- D+d reaction: screening potential U_e = 15.9 ± 2.0 eV (from fit of enhancement factor data)
- D+D reaction: screening potential U_e = 21.6 ± 0.3 eV
- ^3He+d reaction: screening potential U_e = 82.4 ± 1.9 eV
- ^3He+D reaction: screening potential U_e = 102.8 ± 3.0 eV
- ^6Li+d reaction: screening potential U_e = 152.0 ± 9.9 eV
- ^6Li+D reaction: screening potential U_e = 214.4 ± 18.5 eV
- Adiabatic limit screening potentials: 40.7 eV (gerade) and 0.0 eV (ungerade) for D+d; 51.7 eV (ground state) and 31.9 eV (first excited state) for D+D; 119 eV for ^3He+d; 110 eV for ^3He+D; approximately 174-186 eV for Li reactions depending on initial state assumptions
- For D+d at E_cm = 0.15 keV, individual event enhancement factors ranged from 6.5 (event B) to 170.8 (event A)

## Context
This work extends previous time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) studies of electron screening (Shoppa et al., Kimura et al.) which were limited to systems with few bound electrons and cylindrical symmetry, to systems with many electrons (D+D, ^3He+D, Li isotopes) using molecular dynamics that includes fluctuations and correlations beyond mean-field. The results address the long-standing puzzle that experimentally extracted screening potentials often exceed the adiabatic limit (Rolfs and Somorjai), and the authors demonstrate that target polarization perpendicular to the beam axis yields larger enhancement factors, suggesting a possible experimental control of the screening effect.
