# Description of Four-Body Breakup Reaction with the Method of Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels

> We present a method for smoothing discrete breakup $S$-matrix elements calculated by the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC). This smoothing method makes it possible to apply CDCC to four-body breakup reactions. The reliability of the smoothing method is confirmed for two cases, $^{58}$Ni($d$, $p n$) at 80 MeV and the $E1$ transition of $^6$He. We apply CDCC with the smoothing method to $^6$He breakup reaction at 22.5 MeV. Multi-step breakup processes are found to be important.

## Key claim

The paper presents a "model space smoothing method" that extracts continuous breakup S-matrix elements from the discrete pseudostate results of four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations, thereby extending CDCC from three-body to four-body breakup reactions. The method relies on the approximation that the exact three-body continuum wave function of the projectile can be replaced by its projection onto the model space spanned by the pseudostates, which is justified only when the model space is sufficiently large to describe the reaction.

## Method

The authors first diagonalize the internal Hamiltonian of the three-body projectile (e.g., \(^6\)He as \(n+n+^4\)He) using \(L^2\)-type Gaussian basis functions to obtain pseudostates that discretize the continuum. They then solve the four-body CDCC coupled-channels equations for the scattering wave function, obtaining discrete breakup S-matrix elements \(\hat{S}_i\). Finally, they compute continuous S-matrix elements \(S(\bm{k},\bm{K},\bm{P}) = \sum_i \mathcal{F}_i(\bm{k},\bm{K}) \hat{S}_i\) where the smoothing factor \(\mathcal{F}_i\) is the overlap between the pseudostate and the exact continuum state, obtained by solving a set of linear equations derived from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation within the same model space.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{58}\)Ni\((d, pn)\) test at 80 MeV: the maximum momentum \(k_{\max}=1.3\) fm\(^{-1}\) with 17 states for each of \(\ell=0\) and \(\ell=2\); the difference between the approximate and exact smoothing factors is below 1%.
- For the \(^6\)He E1 transition: three parameter sets for the \(1^-\) pseudostates with \(r_{\max}\) and \(y_{\max}\) ranging from 50 to 80 fm; the discrete E1 strength \(B(\text{E1}; n)\) is calculated from the \(0^+\) ground state to the \(n\)th \(1^-\) pseudostate.
- For the \(^6\)He breakup reaction on \(^{209}\)Bi: incident energy 22.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method, previously limited to three-body breakup reactions of two-body projectiles (e.g., deuteron breakup), to four-body breakup reactions of three-body projectiles such as \(^6\)He. It builds on prior pseudostate discretization methods using Gaussian basis functions (Matsumoto et al.) and transformed harmonic oscillator bases (Moro et al.), which had successfully described elastic scattering and virtual breakup effects but could not handle real breakup processes. The smoothing method enables calculation of quintuple differential cross sections for four-body final states and allows comparison with experimental data from radioactive beam facilities.
