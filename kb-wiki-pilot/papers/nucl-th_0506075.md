# Initiative in Nuclear Theory at the Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre

> We recall the path breaking contributions of the nuclear theory group of the Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre, Kolkata. From a beginning of just one person in 1970s, the group has steadily developed into a leading group in the country today, with seminal contributions to almost the entire range of nuclear physics, viz., low energy nuclear reactions, nuclear structure, deep inelastic collisions, fission, liquid to gas phase transitions, nuclear matter, equation of state, mass formulae, neutron stars, relativistic heavy ion collisions, medium modification of hadron properties, quark gluon plasma, and cosmology of early universe.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear theory group at the Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre made path-breaking contributions across nearly all areas of nuclear physics, from low-energy reactions to quark-gluon plasma and cosmology, but this is presented as a subjective retrospective narrative rather than a systematic review, and the group's early work was conducted under severe constraints of limited personnel and computing resources.

## Method

The group developed and applied theoretical frameworks including the folding model for optical model potentials with density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions, linear response theory for nuclear dissipation in deep inelastic collisions, distorted wave Born approximation for break-up of light ions, stochastic nucleon exchange models for heavy ion reactions, and Langevin/Fokker-Planck approaches for fission dynamics. These methods were implemented through analytical derivations and numerical calculations, often using computer codes developed or adapted by group members.

## Key numbers

- The equivalent sharp radius of the nuclear optical model potential for protons (R_V) and the nuclear density distribution (R_rho) were related as: R_V = R_rho + 0.55 (no uncertainty given)
- Stripping channels contributed about 30% of the empirically observed energy dependence of the real part of the d-40Ca optical model potential
- One-body friction was found to be much stronger than two-body friction in deep inelastic collisions
- Nucleon exchange accounted for about 1/2 to 1/3 of total one-body friction, with the rest due to inelastic excitations
- Nucleon transfer contributed about half of the phenomenological strengths of the absorptive nucleus-nucleus optical model potential
- Viscosity coefficients for pre-scission neutron multiplicity followed the relation: mu(E/A, A_CN) = a(E/A) + b A^3_CN, with a = 0.160 ± 0.023 and b = 0.357 × 10^{-6} ± 0.26 × 10^{-7}
- The CDC-3600 computer initially available had 32 K-words of memory and a speed roughly one million times less than a personal computer of 2005

## Context

This paper reviews the historical development of the nuclear theory group at VECC from its founding in 1970 by N. K. Ganguly through the mid-2000s, extending the folding model approach pioneered by Bikash Sinha at King's College London and building on the linear response theory framework for dissipation. The work enabled subsequent studies of radioactive nuclei scattering, astrophysical cross-section extraction from break-up reactions, and detailed dynamical models of incomplete fusion and fission that could be compared directly with experimental data from Indian accelerator facilities.
