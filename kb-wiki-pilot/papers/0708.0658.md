# Trojan Horse as an indirect technique in nuclear astrophysics. Resonance reactions

> The Trojan Horse method is a powerful indirect technique that provides information to determine astrophysical factors for binary rearrangement processes $x + A \to b + B$ at astrophysically relevant energies by measuring the cross section for the Trojan Horse reaction $a + A \to y+ b + B$ in quasi-free kinematics. We present the theory of the Trojan Horse method for resonant binary subreactions based on the half-off-energy-shell R matrix approach which takes into account the off-energy-shell effects and initial and final state interactions.

# Trojan Horse as an indirect technique in nuclear astrophysics. Resonance reactions

## Key claim

The Trojan Horse method can reliably extract the astrophysical S-factor for resonant binary reactions at sub-Coulomb energies, provided the triple differential cross section is normalized to the on-energy-shell S-factor at a resonance energy, because the off-energy-shell effects and the ratio of DWBA amplitudes remain practically constant over energy intervals of a few hundred keV.

## Method

The authors develop a half-off-energy-shell R-matrix formalism for the Trojan Horse reaction a + A → y + b + B, where the binary subreaction x + A → b + B proceeds through one or two isolated resonances in the compound system F = x + A = b + B. The resonant Trojan Horse amplitude is expressed as a product of the DWBA amplitude for the direct transfer reaction a + A → y + F_τ (populating the compound state F_τ) and the resonant form factor for the decay F_τ → b + B, with the level matrix D_ντ accounting for resonance mixing. For two interfering resonances, the triple differential cross section is given by a coherent sum of two Breit-Wigner-like terms, each weighted by the corresponding DWBA transfer amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁵N(p,α)¹²C reaction, two interfering 1⁻ resonances are analyzed at E_R1 = 312 keV and E_R2 = 962 keV.
- Best-fit resonance parameters for the first resonance: Γ_p(1) = 1.1 keV (proton partial width), Γ_α(1) = 93.4 keV (alpha partial width).
- Best-fit resonance parameters for the second resonance: Γ_p(2) = 95.31 keV, Γ_α(2) = 45 keV.
- The ratio of DWBA amplitudes M²¹_0 ≈ 1.13, while the ratio of reduced widths γ_(xA)21 = 1.1 ± 0.1, showing excellent agreement between the Trojan Horse extracted S-factor and direct measurements.

## Context

This work extends the Trojan Horse method, originally suggested by Baur (1986), from direct reactions to resonant binary subreactions by providing a rigorous theoretical foundation based on the half-off-energy-shell R-matrix approach. It addresses previous reservations about the method's reliability due to off-shell effects and initial/final state interactions, which were first seriously treated in the surface approximation of Typel and Baur (2003). The formalism justifies the phenomenological plane-wave approximation used successfully in prior Trojan Horse analyses (Spitaleri et al., 2004) and enables extraction of the astrophysical S-factor for resonant reactions down to zero relative energy without distortion from the Coulomb barrier or electron screening.
