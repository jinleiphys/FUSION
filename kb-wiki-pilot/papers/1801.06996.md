# Investigation of complete and incomplete fusion in $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction around Coulomb barrier energies

> The complete and incomplete fusion cross sections for $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction were measured using online and offline characteristic $γ$-ray detection techniques. The complete fusion (CF) cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier were found to be suppressed by $\sim$ 26 \% compared to the coupled channel calculations. This suppression observed in complete fusion cross sections is found to be commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections. There is a distinct feature observed in the ICF cross sections, i.e., $\textit{t}$-capture is found to be dominant than $α$-capture at all the measured energies. A simultaneous explanation of complete, incomplete and total fusion (TF) data was also obtained from the calculations based on Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel method with short range imaginary potentials. The cross section ratios of CF/TF and ICF/TF obtained from the data as well as the calculations showed the dominance of ICF at below barrier energies and CF at above barrier energies.

### Key claim
Complete fusion (CF) cross sections for the ⁷Li+¹²⁴Sn reaction are suppressed by 26 ± 4% compared to coupled-channel calculations at energies above the Coulomb barrier, and this suppression is commensurate with the measured total incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections. The abstract's "~26%" is refined to 26 ± 4% when including uncertainties from the barrier height and CF cross-section measurements.

### Method
Online and offline characteristic γ-ray detection techniques were used to measure evaporation residues from complete fusion (³n, ⁴n, ⁵n channels producing ¹²⁶–¹²⁸I) and incomplete fusion (α-capture producing ¹²⁶,¹²⁷Te; t-capture producing ¹²⁴–¹²⁶Sb; plus 1n transfer channels). Online measurements used two Compton-suppressed clover detectors at 125° and 90° with a monitor detector at 30° for Rutherford normalization; offline measurements used an HPGe detector on irradiated target-catcher assemblies at six beam energies. Coupled-channel calculations (CCFULL) including projectile ground-state spin and first excited state (0.478 MeV) and target 3⁻ vibrational state were performed, and CDCC calculations (FRESCO 2.9) with short-range imaginary potentials were used to simultaneously describe CF, ICF, and total fusion (TF).

### Key numbers
- CF suppression factor: 26 ± 4% relative to CCFULL coupled-channel predictions at above-barrier energies.
- Coulomb barrier parameters: V_B = 19.7 MeV, R_B = 10.3 fm, ħω = 4.13 MeV (from Akyuz-Winther potential).
- Measured CF cross sections range from 0.64 ± 0.22 mb at E_cm = 15.5 MeV to 1056 ± 14 mb at E_cm = 36.6 MeV.
- Total ICF cross sections (t-capture + α-capture) are dominated by t-capture at all measured energies; e.g., at E_lab = 35.6 MeV, t-capture residues (¹²⁴Sb, ¹²⁵Sb, ¹²⁶Sb) sum to ~383 mb while α-capture residues (¹²⁶Te, ¹²⁷Te) sum to ~55 mb.
- Ratio R (PACE) for missing ER contribution ranges from 0.64 at lowest energy to 0.99 at most energies above 23.5 MeV.

### Context
This work extends systematic studies of CF suppression for weakly bound projectiles (⁶,⁷Li, ⁹Be) on medium and heavy targets, supporting the observation that suppression is target-mass independent (e.g., Gasques et al. 2009, Wang et al. 2014, Kundu et al. 2016). It provides a complete dataset of CF, ICF, and TF for ⁷Li+¹²⁴Sn, enabling CDCC-based simultaneous description of all fusion components, similar to prior work on ⁶,⁷Li+²⁰⁹Bi and ⁶,⁷Li+¹⁹⁸Pt (Parkar et al. 2016). The dominance of t-capture over α-capture in ICF is a distinct feature, consistent with the lower Coulomb barrier for triton compared to α-particle.
