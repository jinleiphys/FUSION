# Multi-neutron transfer in $^{8}$He induced reactions near the Coulomb barrier

> The measured inclusive $^6$He and $^4$He production cross sections of G. Marqu{í}nez-Dur{á}n {\em et al.}, Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 98}, 034615 (2018) are reexamined and the conclusions concerning the relative importance of 1n and 2n transfer to the production of $^6$He arising from the interaction of a 22 MeV $^8$He beam with a $^{208}$Pb target revised. A consideration of the kinematics of the 2n-stripping reaction when compared with the measured $^6$He total energy versus angle spectrum places strict limits on the allowed excitation energy of the $^{210}$Pb residual, so constraining distorted wave Born approximation calculations that the contribution of the 2n stripping process to the inclusive $^6$He production can only be relatively small. It is therefore concluded that the dominant $^6$He production mechanism must be 1n stripping followed by decay of the $^7$He ejectile. Based on this result we present strong arguments in favor of direct, one step four-neutron (4n) stripping as the main mechanism for $^4$He production.

## Key claim

The paper revises the earlier conclusion that two-neutron (2n) stripping dominates $^6$He production in $^8$He + $^{208}$Pb reactions at 22 MeV, instead arguing that one-neutron (1n) stripping followed by $^7$He decay is the dominant mechanism, and that direct four-neutron (4n) stripping is the main source of $^4$He production. The key qualifier is that the 2n-stripping contribution is constrained to be small "on kinematical grounds alone" by comparing the measured $^6$He total energy versus angle spectrum with the kinematics of the 2n-stripping reaction.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the inclusive $^6$He and $^4$He production data from Marquínez-Durán et al. (Phys. Rev. C 98, 034615, 2018) for 22 MeV $^8$He incident on a $^{208}$Pb target, using the two-dimensional total energy versus scattering angle spectra to place kinematic limits on the excitation energy of the residual nuclei ($^{210}$Pb for 2n stripping, $^{212}$Pb for 4n stripping). Distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations were performed with the code FRESCO for 1n, 2n, and 4n transfer reactions, and the calculated angular distributions were compared to the measured inclusive angular distributions to determine the relative contributions of each mechanism.

## Key numbers

- The total measured inclusive $^6$He cross section at 22 MeV is $871 \pm 31$ mb
- The best fit to the $^6$He angular distribution gives approximately 67% from 1n stripping, 16% from 2n stripping, and 17% from background (including breakup)
- The upper limit on the 2n-stripping contribution to $^6$He production is about one third of the total; the lower limit is about 12%
- The total measured inclusive $^4$He cross section at 22 MeV is $393^{+10}_{-33}$ mb
- Direct 4n transfer accounts for 73% of the $^4$He yield (355 mb) in the best fit
- The kinematically allowed excitation energy range for $^{210}$Pb populated by 2n stripping is $7 \leq E_\mathrm{x} \leq 13$ MeV, with $E_\mathrm{x} \approx 10$ MeV most likely
- The kinematically allowed excitation energy range for $^{212}$Pb populated by 4n stripping is $14 \leq E_\mathrm{x} \leq 22$ MeV

## Context

This work revises the conclusions of Marquínez-Durán et al. (Phys. Rev. C 98, 034615, 2018) regarding the relative importance of 1n and 2n transfer in $^8$He-induced reactions on $^{208}$Pb. It extends earlier studies of multi-neutron transfer including the work of Tribble et al. on the $^{64}$Ni($^4$He,$^8$He)$^{60}$Ni reaction suggesting strong one-step 4n transfer, and the work of Lemasson et al. on $^8$He + $^{65}$Cu showing dominance of neutron-transfer reactions. The results are consistent with the direct 4n transfer channel suggested by Tribble et al. and with the five-body cluster orbital shell model approximation (COSMA) calculations of Zhukov et al. showing configurations with 4n clustering in the $^8$He ground state. The findings enable future studies of multi-neutron correlations in neutron-rich nuclei and suggest that different reaction mechanisms emphasize different aspects of nuclear structure.
