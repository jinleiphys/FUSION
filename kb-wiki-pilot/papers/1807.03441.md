# Theoretical study of the capture of stable $^{39}$K and neutron-rich radioactive $^{46}$K by $^{181}$Ta

> The empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model and the universal fusion function (UFF) prescription are used to analyse the data of capture cross sections for reactions ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta and ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta reported recently by A. Wakhle {\it et al.} [Phys. Rev. C 97, 021602(R) (2018)]. The results of the ECC model are in good agreement with the data of ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta while, for ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta, the predictions of the ECC model overestimate the above-barrier capture cross sections. Comparing the reduced data of these two reactions, it is found that the above-barrier cross sections of ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta are consistent with the UFF and are larger than those of ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta. This implies that the capture cross sections of ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta are suppressed at energies above the Coulomb barrier. Furthermore, at sub-barrier energies, the reduced calculated capture cross sections of ${}^{39}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta are a little larger than those of ${}^{46}$K$+{}^{181}$Ta, which is owing to the coupling to the positive $Q$-value two-neutron transfer channel.

## Key claim

The empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model reproduces the capture cross sections for the stable-beam reaction $^{39}$K+$^{181}$Ta well, but overestimates the above-barrier capture cross sections for the neutron-rich radioactive-beam reaction $^{46}$K+$^{181}$Ta, implying a suppression of capture for the latter system at energies above the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors applied the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model, which treats coupled-channel effects by introducing an asymmetric Gaussian barrier weight function whose parameters are determined from empirical formulas based on a two-dimensional potential energy surface. They also used the universal fusion function (UFF) reduction procedure, which eliminates geometrical factors and static potential effects by reducing cross sections and energies using barrier parameters from the double-folding Sao Paulo potential. The ECC model accounts for couplings to low-lying collective states and, for reactions with a positive Q-value for two-neutron transfer, broadens the barrier weight function to simulate the effect of that transfer channel.

## Key numbers

* For $^{39}$K+$^{181}$Ta, the ECC barrier weight function parameters are: left width $\Delta_1 = 3.56$ MeV, right width $\Delta_2 = 23.46$ MeV, and most probable barrier height $B_m = 146.78$ MeV.
* For $^{46}$K+$^{181}$Ta, the ECC barrier weight function parameters are: $\Delta_1 = 2.38$ MeV, $\Delta_2 = 22.20$ MeV, and $B_m = 143.25$ MeV.
* The Q-value for two-neutron transfer is $3.67$ MeV for $^{39}$K+$^{181}$Ta (positive) and negative for $^{46}$K+$^{181}$Ta.
* Barrier parameters from the Sao Paulo potential for $^{39}$K+$^{181}$Ta: $V_B = 155.651$ MeV, $\hbar\omega = 4.328$ MeV, $R_B = 12.030$ fm; for $^{46}$K+$^{181}$Ta: $V_B = 152.141$ MeV, $\hbar\omega = 4.029$ MeV, $R_B = 12.333$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed ECC model (Wang et al., Atomic Data and Nuclear Data Tables 114, 281, 2017) to analyze recent capture cross section data from Wakhle et al. (Phys. Rev. C 97, 021602(R), 2018) for stable and neutron-rich potassium isotopes on tantalum. The study contradicts the earlier conclusion from Wakhle et al. that reduced excitation functions of the two reactions show no significant difference, instead finding clear suppression for the radioactive beam reaction when using the UFF reduction procedure. This result is important for planning future synthesis of neutron-rich superheavy nuclei using radioactive beams, as it indicates that capture suppression must be considered.
