# Molecular 9Be+p Fusion Reaction

> We study (nuclear) fusion reactions in Beryllium. We argue that some are nuclear long-distance molecular processes of interest for low energy nuclear reactions. For these we develop a novel reaction model. We note a chain of reactions that can naturally arise in Beryllium target created by nonequilibrium proton reactions.

# Molecular 9Be+p Fusion Reaction

## Key claim

The authors argue that the 9Be(p,d)8Be fusion reaction (Q=0.5592 MeV) is not adequately described by the standard R-matrix nuclear reaction model because the weakly-bound valence neutron in 9Be (separation energy 1.6654 MeV) transfers to the incoming proton at relatively large internuclear distances, making the process more akin to a molecular reaction with a dynamically changing tunneling barrier; they propose that this "molecular fusion" mechanism may exhibit enhanced low-energy reactivity through instanton resonances, though the enhancement in the traditional S-factor is expected to be "less dramatic" when averaging over all collision geometries.

## Method

The authors develop a semiclassical Born-Oppenheimer molecular model treating the 9Be+p collision system as a nuclear quasi-molecule, where the neutron is asymptotically bound either to an 8Be=α+α core or to a proton. They construct approximate eigenstates using a linear combination of nuclear orbitals (LCNO) method with square-well potentials fitted to match the neutron binding energies and core charge radii, then compute the dynamic tunneling barriers U±(R) and solve the resulting Landau-Zener equations for the neutron transfer probability as a function of internuclear separation R.

## Context

This work extends the molecular reaction approach previously applied to electron transfer in atomic collisions to nuclear neutron transfer reactions, specifically challenging the applicability of the R-matrix method for weakly-bound neutron systems like 9Be. The authors note that the 9Be(p,d)8Be reaction has been "recognized as needing additional theoretical attention due to not fully understood low p-energy reactivity as characterized by the large value of the Coulomb suppression factor (S-factor)." The work builds on the cluster model of Rakityansky (2024) for 9Be as an ααn system and references the R-matrix analysis of Barker (2001) and the low-energy cross section measurements of Zahnow et al. (1997). The proposed reaction chain (9Be+p→8Be+d, then 9Be+d→10Be+p, then 10Be+p→8Be+t) is motivated by the possibility of aneutronic fusion cycles analogous to the solar pp-chain and CNO cycles, with potential applications in laser-driven non-equilibrium fusion environments.
