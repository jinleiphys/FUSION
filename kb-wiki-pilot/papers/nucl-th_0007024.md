# Inclusive quasielastic electron scattering on $^6$He: a probe of the halo structure

> We investigate inclusive electron scattering reactions on two-neutron halo nuclei in the quasielastic region. Expressions for the cross section and structure functions are given assuming that the halo nucleus can be described as a three-body system (${core}+n+n$). The method is applied to $^6$He. We compute cross sections and structure functions, and investigate the kinematic conditions for which the observables are determined either by $α$-knockout or by halo neutron-knockout. The optimal kinematical domain to disantangle the momentum distributions of the various components of the three--body system ($q \lesssim 200$ MeV/c and $ω< q^2/2M_N + 20$ MeV) are explored.

# Inclusive quasielastic electron scattering on $^6$He: a probe of the halo structure

## Key claim

Inclusive electron scattering on $^6$He in the quasielastic region can separately probe the momentum distributions of the $\alpha$-core and the halo neutrons, provided the kinematics are restricted to $q \lesssim 200$ MeV/c and $\omega < q^2/2M_N + 20$ MeV, where core breakup contributions are expected to be negligible. The longitudinal structure function $W_L$ is dominated by $\alpha$-knockout at forward angles, while the transverse structure function $W_T$ arises solely from halo neutron knockout and dominates at backward angles.

## Method

The inclusive cross section $d^3\sigma/dE'_0 d\Omega_{p'_0}$ is computed as the sum of three terms corresponding to virtual photon absorption by each constituent ($\alpha$ and two neutrons) in a three-body ($\alpha + n + n$) description of $^6$He, using the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) with final-state interactions between the two spectator particles included. The $^6$He ground state wave function is obtained by solving Faddeev equations in coordinate space, and the spectral functions $S(E'_x, p_\alpha)$ and $S(E'_x, p_n)$ are constructed from overlaps with continuum wave functions of the residual two-body systems ($nn$ and $n\alpha$), using $n$-$n$ and $n$-$\alpha$ potentials fitted to low-energy scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Optimal kinematical domain: $q \lesssim 200$ MeV/c and $\omega < q^2/2M_N + 20$ MeV (where $M_N$ is the nucleon mass and 20 MeV is the nucleon separation energy in the $\alpha$-particle)
- For $q$ = 90, 150, and 200 MeV/c, the $\alpha$-peak in $W_L$ is centered at $\omega \approx q^2/2m_\alpha$; the halo neutron peak in $W_T$ is centered at $\omega \approx q^2/2m_n$
- For $q > 250$ MeV/c and $\omega > 25$ MeV, core breakup contributions to both $W_L$ and $W_T$ become sizeable
- Maximum values of $W_L \sim 1.5$ fm and $W_T \sim 2$ fm for $^4$He($e,e'$) occur at $q \sim 360$--380 MeV/c and $\omega \sim 70$--80 MeV (from Dytman et al., 1988)
- For inclusive $^6$He($e,e'$) at $E_0 = 100$ MeV: forward angles ($\theta_e = 10^\circ$, $60^\circ$) show only the $\alpha$-peak; backward angles ($\theta_e = 120^\circ$, $170^\circ$) show the halo neutron peak dominating
- For backward scattering: $\theta_e \gtrsim 160^\circ$, $50 \lesssim E_0 \lesssim 100$ MeV, and $\omega < 40$ MeV define the region where the inclusive cross section is dominated by the halo neutron peak

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Garrido and Moya de Guerra (1999, Nucl. Phys. A 650, 387) for elastic and exclusive $(e,e'\alpha)$ and $(e,e'n)$ scattering on $^6$He to the inclusive $(e,e')$ case, providing the first theoretical exploration of the quasielastic region for two-neutron halo nuclei. It addresses the need for nonhadronic probes to complement information from nucleus-nucleus collision experiments (Tanihata et al., 1985, 1988; Anne et al., 1990; Orr et al., 1992, 1995; Zinser et al., 1995, 1997) by identifying kinematical conditions where electron scattering can cleanly separate core and halo contributions. The results enable planning of coincidence $(e,e'\alpha)$ and $(e,e'n)$ measurements at facilities such as RIKEN, GSI, and Mainz, and the authors state the procedure will be extended to other halo nuclei.
