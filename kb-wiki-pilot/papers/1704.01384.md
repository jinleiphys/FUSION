# Reactions induced by $^9$Be in a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels framework

> We investigate the elastic scattering of $^9$Be on $^{208}$Pb at beam energies above (50 MeV) and below (40 MeV) the Coulomb barrier. The reaction is described within a four-body framework using the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method. The $^9$Be projectile states are generated using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates. Our calculations confirm the importance of continuum effects at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that continuum (breakup) effects are important for elastic scattering of \({}^{9}\)Be on \({}^{208}\)Pb even at energies below the Coulomb barrier, not just above it as previously assumed. The claim is hedged by the fact that the calculations are performed within a specific four-body CDCC framework using the THO basis, and the agreement with experimental data supports but does not prove the method's general reliability.

## Method

The authors describe the elastic scattering of \({}^{9}\)Be on \({}^{208}\)Pb using a four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method, treating \({}^{9}\)Be as a three-body projectile (\(\alpha + \alpha + n\)) interacting with a structureless target. Projectile states were generated using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates, including \(j^\pi = 3/2^\pm, 1/2^\pm, 5/2^\pm\) states. The coupled equations were solved including projectile-target interaction multipole couplings to all orders, and the ground state and low-energy resonances of \({}^{9}\)Be were fixed to experimental values.

## Key numbers

- The \({}^{9}\)Be binding energy below the \(\alpha + \alpha + n\) threshold is 1.5736 MeV.
- Elastic scattering was calculated at beam energies of 50 MeV (above the Coulomb barrier) and 40 MeV (below the Coulomb barrier).
- Experimental data used for comparison are from Wolliscroft et al. (2004) (circles) and Yu et al. (2010) (squares).

## Context

This work extends previous two-body projectile calculations for \({}^{9}\)Be (Pandit et al., 2011) and three-body projectile calculations (Descouvemont et al., 2015) by using a four-body CDCC framework with a more sophisticated THO basis. The results confirm that breakup effects are important even at sub-barrier energies, supporting the need for full continuum coupling in reactions involving weakly-bound Borromean nuclei like \({}^{9}\)Be. The method enables future studies of reactions induced by other three-body projectiles with more than one charged particle.
