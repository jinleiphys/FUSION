# Elastic and inelastic breakup of deuterons with energy below 100 MeV

> We present calculations of deuteron elastic and inelastic breakup cross sections and angular distributions at deuteron energies below 100 MeV obtained using the post-form DWBA approximation. The elastic breakup cross section was extensively studied in the past. Very few calculations of inelastic breakup have been performed, however. We also analyze the angular momentum - energy distributions of the cross section for formation of the compound nucleus after inelastic breakup.

## Key claim

The authors present calculations of deuteron elastic and inelastic breakup cross sections and angular distributions below 100 MeV using the post-form DWBA approximation, but note that the inelastic breakup cross sections have been studied very little in the past and that the low-energy inelastic breakup compound nucleus formation cross sections have well-defined structures in energy and angular momentum that complicate their substitution by a smooth distribution from systematics.

## Method

The authors used the zero-range post-form DWBA approximation to calculate elastic breakup cross sections and extended this formalism to absorption channels to calculate inelastic breakup cross sections. They expanded wave functions and matrix elements in partial waves of orbital angular momentum alone, neglecting spin-orbit coupling effects, and used Koning-Delaroche global optical potentials for proton and neutron channels and the Han et al. potential for deuteron scattering. The numerical integration of conditionally convergent elastic breakup matrix elements was performed by extending the integrals to the complex plane following the Vincent-Fortune method.

## Key numbers

- The zero-range DWBA normalization constant D0 was taken as -125 MeV-fm^(3/2)
- For 30 MeV deuterons on 48Ti, the breakup radius Rbu was found to be 10.6 fm, compared to a strong interaction radius of 6.0 fm
- For 30 MeV deuterons on 181Ta, the breakup radius Rbu was found to be 14.6 fm, compared to a strong interaction radius of 8.6 fm
- Deuteron absorption at small impact parameters is reduced by about 25% due to breakup reactions
- Inelastic breakup neutron and proton absorption probabilities in the nuclear interior are about 10% and 20%, respectively
- For 56 MeV deuterons on 118Sn, the calculated inclusive double differential proton spectrum is underpredicted by about 20% at an angle of 9.5 degrees and by a few percent at 13 degrees

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for deuteron breakup developed by Baur and collaborators (Baur-76, Baur-84) and the inelastic breakup formalism of Kasano and Ichimura (Kasano-82) and Austern et al. (Austern-87). The calculations are relevant for medical radioisotope production and surrogate reaction studies (Escher-12), and the authors plan to implement the model in the EMPIRE-III nuclear reaction code (Herman-97). The results show that breakup occurs predominantly in the Coulomb field of the target and that the structured energy-angular momentum distributions of inelastic breakup compound nucleus formation must be treated with care when used as surrogates for neutron- or proton-induced reactions.
