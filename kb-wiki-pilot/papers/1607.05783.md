# Theory of inclusive breakup cross section for Borromean nuclei within a four-body spectator model

> We develop a model to treat the inclusive non-elastic break up reactions involving weakly bound three-cluster nuclei. Borromean, two-nucleon, halo nuclei are candidates of unstable three-fragments projectiles. The model is based on the theory of inclusive breakup reactions commonly employed in the treatment of incomplete fusion and surrogate method. The theory was developed in the 80's by Ichimura, Autern and Vincent (IAV) [Phys. Rev. C 32, 431 (1985)] \cite{IAV1985}, Udagawa and Tamura (UT)[Phys. Rev. C 24, 1348 (1981)], \cite{UT1981} and Hussein and McVoy (HM)[Nucl. Phys. A 445, 124 (1985)], \cite{HM1985}. We extend these three-body theories to derive an expression for the fragment yield in the reaction $A\,(a,b)\,X$, where the projectile is $a = x_1 + x_2 + b$. The inclusive breakup cross section is found to be the sum of a generalized four-body form of the elastic breakup cross section plus the inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section which involves the "reaction" cross section of the participant fragments, $x_1$ and $x_2$. The final result is similar to the three-body case reviewed in Austern, et al. (Phys. Rep. \textbf{154}, 125 (1987)), \cite{Austern1987}, but with important genuine four-body effects added, both in the elastic breakup cross section, which now contains the full correlations between the participant fragments, and in the inclusive non- elastic breakup. These developments should encourage experimentalists to seek more information about the $x_{1} + x_{2}$ system in the elastic breakup cross section, and to theorists to further develop and extend the surrogate method, based on the inclusive non-elastic breakup part of the $b$ spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper extends the three-body inclusive breakup theory (IAV, UT, HM) to derive a four-body formula for the inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section in reactions where the projectile is a three-cluster Borromean nucleus, with the caveat that the derivation relies on treating the spectator fragment as structureless and the target as adiabatic, and the final expression contains genuine four-body effects that go beyond a simple generalization of the three-body Austern formula.

## Method

The authors start from the full four-body Hamiltonian for the system of three projectile fragments (x1, x2, b) and a target A, then apply the spectator model approximation where the b fragment interacts with the target via a complex optical potential and the target is treated adiabatically. They derive an expression for the inclusive breakup cross section by averaging over target ground state degrees of freedom, using projection operator techniques to obtain a three-body optical Green's function for the x1+x2+A subsystem. The final cross section is separated into an elastic breakup term and an inclusive non-elastic breakup term (the CFH formula), with the latter expressed in terms of a source function and the imaginary parts of optical potentials for x1, x2, and a three-body term W3B.

## Context

This work extends the three-body inclusive breakup theories developed in the 1980s by Ichimura, Austern and Vincent (IAV), Udagawa and Tamura (UT), and Hussein and McVoy (HM) to the case of four-body reactions involving Borromean nuclei such as ⁶He, ¹¹Li, ¹⁴Be, and ²²C. The three-body theories had been applied to (d,p) reactions and reactions like ⁶Li+²⁰⁹Bi → α+X, but could not treat the incomplete fusion part of total fusion for three-fragment projectiles. The new four-body formula enables experimentalists to extract information about the x1+x2 system from elastic breakup measurements and provides a theoretical foundation for extending the surrogate method to reactions with Borromean projectiles.
