# Faddeev equation approach for three-cluster nuclear reactions

> In this lecture we aim to present a formalism based on Faddeev-like equations for describing nuclear three-cluster reactions that include elastic, transfer and breakup channels. Two different techniques based on momentum-space and configuration-space representations are explained in detail. An important new feature of these methods is the possibility to account for the repulsive Coulomb interaction between two of the three clusters in all channels. Comparison with previous calculations based on approximate methods used in nuclear reaction theory is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents two recently developed techniques (momentum-space screening/renormalization and configuration-space complex scaling) that enable rigorous solution of three-cluster nuclear reactions including the repulsive Coulomb interaction between two of the three clusters in all channels, overcoming limitations of earlier approximate methods that could not handle the long-range Coulomb force with the same numerical reliability as short-range interactions alone.

## Method

The momentum-space method uses the Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) form of Faddeev equations with a screened Coulomb potential \(w_R(r) = w_C(r) e^{-(r/R)^n}\) and a renormalization procedure that extracts the full Coulomb amplitude from the \(R \to \infty\) limit. The configuration-space method employs Faddeev-Merkuriev equations with a complex scaling transformation that avoids explicit treatment of complicated asymptotic boundary conditions, splitting the Coulomb interaction into short-range and long-range parts via a cut-off function \(\chi_\alpha(x_\alpha, y_\alpha)\).

## Key numbers

- Screening radius values used for convergence tests: \(R = 5\) fm, 10 fm, 15 fm for \(\alpha\)-deuteron elastic scattering at 4.81 MeV deuteron lab energy; \(R = 10\) fm, 15 fm, 20 fm for \(\alpha\)-deuteron breakup at 15 MeV \(\alpha\) lab energy
- Optimal screening function exponent: \(3 \le n \le 8\) (with \(n=4\) used in calculations)
- Euler number \(C \approx 0.5772156649\) appears in the renormalization phase formula
- Fine-structure constant \(\alpha_{e.m.} \approx 1/137\) used in Coulomb parameter definition
- Cut-off function parameters \(x_0, y_0\) set comparable to charge radii of respective binary systems; parameter \(\mu\) set to \(\mu \approx 2\)

## Context

This work extends the rigorous Faddeev/AGS formalism originally developed for three-nucleon systems with short-range interactions (Faddeev 1960; Alt, Grassberger, Sandhas 1967) to include the Coulomb interaction via two complementary methods. The momentum-space approach builds on the screening and renormalization procedure developed by Taylor (1974) and Semon (1975), previously applied to proton-deuteron scattering (Deltuva et al. 2005a, 2005d). The configuration-space approach extends the complex scaling method (Nuttall, CSM 1971) recently applied to scattering problems (CSM Curdy 2004, Elander CSM) and the Merkuriev formulation of Faddeev equations with Coulomb (Merkuriev 1980, 1981). The methods enable calculation of elastic, transfer, and breakup reactions for three-cluster systems with arbitrary masses, going beyond earlier approximate methods and previous configuration-space solutions that were limited to \(p\)-\(d\) scattering (Kievsky 1997).
