# Four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations

> The development of a continuum-bins scheme of discretization for three-body projectiles is necessary for studies of reactions of Borromean nuclei such as 6He within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels approach. Such a procedure, for constructing bin states on selected continuum energy intervals, is formulated and applied for the first time to reactions of a three-body projectile. The continuum representation uses the eigenchannel expansion of the three-body S-matrix. The method is applied to the challenging case of the 6He + 208Pb reaction at 22 MeV, where an accurate treatment of both the Coulomb and the nuclear interactions with the target is necessary.

## Key claim

The paper formulates and applies for the first time a continuum-bin discretization scheme for three-body projectiles within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) approach, using the eigenchannel expansion of the three-body S-matrix to establish a hierarchy of continuum states. The method is applied to the challenging case of the 6He + 208Pb reaction at 22 MeV, where an accurate treatment of both Coulomb and nuclear interactions with the target is necessary, and the authors note that the B(E1) distribution is not converged with the chosen hypermomentum truncation (Kmax=8) but the scattering calculations are converged when the three-body potential parameters are adjusted to reproduce the same binding energy and rms matter radius.

## Method

The authors construct bin states for three-body projectiles by superposing three-body scattering states within selected continuum energy intervals, using a hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansion basis and the eigenchannel expansion of the three-body S-matrix. For each total angular momentum j and continuum energy, the S-matrix in the channel basis is diagonalized to obtain eigenchannels (EC) ordered by the magnitude of their eigenphases, and bin wave functions are built from these EC using a weight function (unity for non-resonant continuum, sin(delta) for resonant continuum). The method is applied to four-body CDCC calculations for 6He + 208Pb at 22 MeV, including states with jpi=0+, 1-, and 2+ and multipole couplings up to order Q=2, with the coupled equations solved using the code FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- 6He ground state binding energy: 0.953 MeV (model value; experimental value 0.973 MeV)
- 6He ground state rms matter radius: 2.46 fm (assuming alpha particle rms radius of 1.47 fm)
- 6He low-lying continuum: narrow jpi=2+ resonance at 0.825 MeV above threshold
- Maximum hypermomentum in CDCC calculation: Kmax = 8
- Number of channels beta: 15 (0+), 26 (1-), and 46 (2+)
- Partial waves up to J=150 included; matching radius Rm=200 fm
- Maximum excitation energy epsilon_max = 8 MeV (set I-IV) or 9 MeV (set V)
- Number of eigenchannels: 4 (sets I-III, V) or 5 (set IV)
- Total number of states: 85 (set I), 121 (set II), 157 (set III), 142 (set IV), 133 (set V)
- Contribution of fifth EC changes total breakup cross section by less than 2%

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method, previously formulated for two-body projectiles (Yah86, Aus87) and successfully applied to weakly-bound two-body systems (Sak86, Nun99, Tos01), to three-body (Borromean) projectiles using a continuum-bin discretization scheme rather than the pseudostate (PS) basis approach used in prior three-body projectile calculations (Mat04, Mat06, Rod08, Ega09). The authors note that PS basis calculations showed convergence problems for reactions where Coulomb interaction plays an important role, and the present eigenchannel-based binning scheme addresses this limitation. The method enables practical four-body CDCC calculations for reactions of Borromean nuclei such as 6He, with the specific application to 6He + 208Pb at 22 MeV showing good agreement with experimental elastic scattering data from San07.
