# $^9$Be scattering within a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels framework

> The scattering of $^9$Be on $^{208}$Pb is addressed within a four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) framework, considering a three-body $α+ α+ n$ projectile plus a structureless target. The projectile states are generated using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates. Both the elastic and breakup channels are described on the same footing. We find a good agreement between our calculations and the experimental data at beam energies around and below the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The paper reports good agreement between four-body CDCC calculations and experimental data for $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb scattering at beam energies around and below the Coulomb barrier, but notes that calculations underestimate the experimental data in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region, especially at 44 MeV, which may arise from experimental or theoretical problems requiring more exhaustive analysis.

## Method

The authors performed four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations treating $^9$Be as a three-body $\alpha + \alpha + n$ projectile interacting with a structureless $^{208}$Pb target. Projectile states were generated using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates, including states with $j^\pi=3/2^\pm,5/2^\pm,1/2^\pm$ and solving coupled equations with multipole couplings to all orders.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering cross sections relative to Rutherford were calculated at 44 MeV and 38 MeV (around and below the Coulomb barrier, respectively)
- Integrated breakup cross section at 44 MeV: 112.0 mb (compared to experimental value of 92.1 mb)
- Integrated breakup cross section at 38 MeV: 80.5 mb (compared to experimental value of 84.1 mb)
- $^9$Be ground state lies at 1.5736 MeV below the $\alpha + \alpha + n$ threshold

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method to four-body scattering involving a three-body projectile with more than one charged particle, building on prior pseudo-state methods for two-body projectiles (Moro et al., 2009) and three-body projectiles (Rodriguez-Gallardo et al., 2008). The results confirm earlier findings by Descouvemont et al. (2015) that continuum coupling improves agreement with elastic scattering data, and the calculated breakup cross sections show fair agreement with experimental values from Woolliscroft et al. (2003). The approach enables future exclusive Coulomb dissociation experiments to assess the model's validity for describing elastic and breakup processes on the same footing.
