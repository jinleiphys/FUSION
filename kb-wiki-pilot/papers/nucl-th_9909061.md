# Electromagnetic response functions of few-nucleon systems

> Inclusive electromagnetic reactions in few-nucleon systems are studied basing on accurate three- and four-body calculations. The longitudinal 4He(e,e') response function obtained at q\le 600 MeV/c completely agrees with experiment. The exact 4He spectral function obtained in a semirealistic potential model is presented, and the accuracy of the quasielastic response calculated with its help is assessed, as well as the accuracy of some simpler approximations for the response. The photodisintegration cross section of 3He obtained with the realistic AV14 NN force plus UrbanaVIII NNN force agrees with experiment. It is shown that this cross section is very sensitive to underlying nuclear dynamics in the E_γ\simeq 70-100 MeV region. In particular, the NNN nuclear force clearly manifests itself in this region.

## Key claim

The longitudinal \(^4\)He\((e,e')\) response function obtained at \(q\le 600\) MeV/c completely agrees with experiment, but this agreement tests only the low-momentum content of the wave function (below about 0.7 fm\(^{-1}\)) because the kinematics of the right wing of the response probes only such low momenta. The photodisintegration cross section of \(^3\)He obtained with the realistic AV14 NN force plus UrbanaVIII NNN force agrees with experiment, and the cross section is very sensitive to underlying nuclear dynamics in the \(E_\gamma\simeq 70-100\) MeV region, where the NNN nuclear force clearly manifests itself.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive electromagnetic response functions for \(A=3\) and \(A=4\) systems using a non-relativistic Hamiltonian and a method that avoids explicit construction of the complete set of final continuum states, requiring only bound-state-type calculations (the Lorentz integral transform method of Refs. [ELO, ELO99]). For \(^4\)He\((e,e')\) they use a semirealistic central NN potential (Trento potential) reproducing \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) phase shifts, with the conventional single-particle transition operator including Sachs form factors and the usual relativistic correction. For the \(^3\)He photodisintegration they employ the realistic AV14 NN interaction and the UrbanaVIII NNN force, using a new spin-isospin dependent correlated basis to handle the strong core, and work in the unretarded E1 approximation.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal \(^4\)He\((e,e')\) response calculated for \(q = 300, 400, 500, 600\) MeV/c; complete agreement with Bates and Saclay data is shown.
- At \(q=600\) MeV/c, the quasielastic (QE) approximation overestimates the peak height by about 9\%.
- For \(^4\)He with the Trento potential, the integrated values of the spectral function components are \(n_{tp}=0.89\) and \(n_{t^*p}=0.11\).
- \(^3\)H binding energy with AV14 alone: 7.71 MeV; with AV14+UrbanaVIII: 8.52 MeV; rms radius: 1.66 fm; D-state probability: 8.85\%.
- Photodisintegration cross section of \(^3\)He calculated with AV14+UrbanaVIII agrees with experimental data of Fetisov et al. (1965) up to pion threshold.
- At \(E_\gamma=110\) MeV in the N+\(d\) breakup, the N-\(d\) relative momentum is about 1.9 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier accurate calculations of trinucleon photodisintegration with central potentials (TN and Malfliet-Tjon, Ref. [ELO97b]) to realistic NN interactions including a three-nucleon force for the first time. It also provides the first exact \(^4\)He spectral function from a semirealistic potential model, which is used to assess the accuracy of the quasielastic approximation and simpler spectral function approximations (e.g., that of Carlson and Schiavilla, Ref. [cas]). The results enable future experimental discrimination between NN interaction models and detection of NNN force contributions in the 70-100 MeV photon energy region, and suggest even greater sensitivity in exclusive experiments.
