# Suppression of the elastic scattering cross section for 17Ne + 208Pb system

> We investigated the elastic scattering, inelastic scattering, breakup reaction, and total fusion reactions of 17Ne + 208Pb system using the optical model (OM) and a coupled channel (CC) approaches. The aim of this study is to elucidate the suppress of the elastic cross-section that is invisible in proton-rich nuclei such as 8B and 17F projectiles but appears in neutron-rich nuclei such as 11Li and 11Be projectiles. The results revealed that this suppression was caused mainly by the nuclear interaction between the projectile and target nucleus rather than the strong Coulomb interaction observed in neutron-rich nuclei and the contributions of Coulomb excitation interaction due to two low-lying E2 resonance states are relatively small. From the simultaneous chi-square analysis of the 17Ne + 208Pb system, we can infer a strong suppression effect in the elastic scattering cross-section due to the nuclear interaction between the projectile and target nucleus, rather than the Coulomb interaction as observed in neutron-rich nuclei. Also, the contribution of the direct reaction, comprising the inelastic scattering and breakup reaction cross-sections, accounted for almost half of the total reaction. Finally, we perform the CC calculation using the parameters obtained from our OM calculation but our CC calculations could not explain the 15O production cross section.

## Key claim

The suppression of the elastic scattering cross section for the 17Ne + 208Pb system is caused primarily by the nuclear interaction between the projectile and target nucleus, rather than the strong Coulomb interaction that dominates in neutron-rich nuclei such as 11Li and 11Be; the contributions of Coulomb excitation due to two low-lying E2 resonance states are relatively small.

## Method

The authors used the optical model (OM) with a dynamic polarization potential (DPP) decomposed into inelastic scattering, breakup, and fusion components, and performed simultaneous chi-square analysis of elastic scattering and breakup cross-section data at Elab = 136 MeV. They also performed coupled channel (CC) calculations considering the 3/2- state at Ex = 1.288 MeV, the 5/2- state at Ex = 1.764 MeV, and the 3/2- state at Ex = 2.692 MeV, using B(E2) values from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The total reaction cross section from the OM calculation was 1859.9 mb (set A) and 1889.3 mb (set B)
- The breakup cross section was 602.7 mb (set A: 320.0 mb nuclear + 282.7 mb Coulomb) and 594.5 mb (set B: 316.4 mb nuclear + 278.1 mb Coulomb)
- The inelastic scattering cross section (set B) was 379.2 mb (250.9 mb nuclear + 128.3 mb Coulomb)
- The total fusion cross section was 1126.8 mb (set A) and 915.6 mb (set B)
- The direct reaction (inelastic scattering plus breakup) accounted for almost half of the total reaction cross section
- The chi-square values were 2.6 (set A) and 2.1 (set B)
- The bare potential parameters were V0 = 67.0 MeV, a0 = 0.692 fm, r0 = 1.196 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of elastic scattering suppression in weakly bound nuclei, which is well established for neutron-rich projectiles like 11Li and 11Be but had not been clearly observed in proton-rich nuclei such as 8B and 17F. The authors compare their results with the prior analysis by Ovejas et al. (2023), who used a different OM approach with single real-volume and paired imaginary components that could not reproduce the breakup cross section. The present work enables simultaneous description of elastic scattering and breakup data for 17Ne, though the CC calculations could not properly explain the 15O production cross section.
