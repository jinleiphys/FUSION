# Intermediate energy four-body breakup calculations for 22C

> The heaviest particle-bound carbon isotope, $^{22}$C, is thought to have a Borromean three-body structure. We discuss and compare four-body, i.e. three-body projectile plus target, reaction model calculations of reaction cross sections for such systems that use the fast adiabatic approximation. These methods are efficient and well-suited for quantitative analyses of reactions of neutron-rich nuclei with light target nuclei at secondary beam energies of $\approx$300 MeV/nucleon, as are now becoming available. We compare the predictions of the adiabatic model of the reaction both without and when including the additional eikonal approximation that has been used extensively. We show that the reaction cross section calculations have only limited sensitivity to the size and structure of $^{22}$C and that the differences arising from use of the eikonal approximation of the reaction mechanism are of a similar magnitude.

# Intermediate energy four-body breakup calculations for 22C

## Key claim

The reaction cross section calculations for 22C have only limited sensitivity to the size and structure of the projectile, and the differences arising from use of the eikonal approximation versus the full adiabatic treatment of the reaction mechanism are of a similar magnitude to the structural sensitivity, at approximately 2%.

## Method

The authors performed four-body (three-body projectile plus target) coupled-channels adiabatic model calculations of reaction cross sections for 22C on a carbon target at 300 MeV per nucleon, using a 20C+n+n three-body framework for the projectile ground state. They compared these converged adiabatic results with calculations using the additional eikonal (Glauber) approximation, employing the same three-body wave functions and optical model interactions for both approaches. The calculations systematically included s-wave, p-wave, and d-wave breakup channels, with g-wave effects found to be negligible.

## Key numbers

- 22C reaction cross sections on a carbon target at 300 MeV/nucleon range from 1328.0 mb (model k1, most bound) to 1391.3 mb (model k4, least bound) in the most complete adiabatic calculation
- The eikonal (Glauber) cross sections are consistently smaller than the adiabatic results by approximately 2%, ranging from 1299.1 mb to 1353.3 mb
- The 22C point-nucleon rms matter radii for the five model wave functions range from 3.505 fm (k1) to 3.719 fm (k4), a variation of approximately 6%
- The two-neutron separation energies used range from -0.046 MeV (k4) to -0.442 MeV (k1)
- The n+20C 2s1/2 virtual state scattering lengths range from -16.1 fm (k4) to -333.3 fm (k1 and k5)
- The dominant 1S(nn) configuration probability ranges from 0.698 (k1) to 0.767 (k4)
- The [2s1/2]2 configuration dominates all wave functions with probabilities from 0.919 (k4) to 0.939 (k5)

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic four-body approach of Christley et al. (previously applied to 11Li elastic scattering) to the Borromean system 22C, and quantifies the accuracy of the more approximate eikonal/Glauber few-body approach used previously by Jakubassa-Amundsen and Tostevin for reaction cross sections. The results are relevant to recent measurements at RIKEN of 22C reaction cross sections on proton targets (Tanaka et al.) and neutron removal reactions on carbon targets (Kobayashi et al.), and provide a benchmark for the eikonal method at intermediate energies around 300 MeV/nucleon. The calculations show that the range of theoretical rms radii from different three-body structures is far more restricted for 22C (approximately 6%) than was obtained for lighter halo nuclei such as 11Li (approximately 30%).
