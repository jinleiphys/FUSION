# pQCD-based Approach to Parton Production and Equilibration in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> A pQCD-based model for parton production and equilibration in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is reviewed. The model combines pQCD processes including initial and final state radiations together with string phenomenology for nonperturbative soft processes. Nuclear effects on the initial parton production, such as multiple parton scattering and nuclear shadowing of parton distribution functions are considered. Comparisons with existing data are made and further tests of the model to constrain model parameters are proposed. With the obtained space-time history of the parton production, evolution of the minijet gas toward a fully equilibrated parton plasma is studied. Direct probes of the early parton dynamics, such as pre-equilibrium photon and dilepton production, open charm production, $J/ψ$ suppressionand jet quenching are also reviewed.

# pQCD-based Approach to Parton Production and Equilibration in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that at collider energies above √s ≈ 100 AGeV, hard and semihard parton scatterings become dominant in heavy-ion collisions and can be calculated via perturbative QCD, but only when combined with a phenomenological string model for nonperturbative soft interactions and with proper treatment of interference effects such as the Glauber multiple scattering formula and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. The model's predictive power depends on the phenomenological parameter p₀ ≈ 2 GeV/c that separates hard from soft processes, and this parameter is bounded from below by experimental total cross section data at p₀ = 1.2 GeV.

## Method

The HIJING (Heavy Ion Jet INteraction Generator) Monte Carlo model combines perturbative QCD calculations of hard and semihard parton production (minijets with pT ≥ p₀ ≈ 2 GeV/c) with a string phenomenology for nonperturbative soft interactions, using an eikonal formalism where the total inclusive quark-quark cross section is decomposed into a soft component σsoft and an inclusive jet cross section σjet. Nuclear effects including multiple parton scattering and nuclear shadowing of parton distribution functions are incorporated, and the space-time evolution of the produced partonic system toward equilibration is studied using pQCD with the LPM effect on induced gluon radiation.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum cutoff separating hard and soft processes is p₀ ≈ 2 GeV/c, with a lower bound of p₀ = 1.2 GeV from total cross section data
- The soft cross section is taken as σsoft = 57 mb at high energies
- Minijets are estimated to produce up to 50% of the transverse energy per unit rapidity at RHIC (√s = 200 AGeV) and up to 80% at LHC (√s = 5.5 ATeV)
- The nucleon radius is taken as RN ≈ 0.85 fm
- The overlap function parameter is μ₀ = 3.9
- The K-factor for next-to-leading order pQCD corrections is K ≈ 2
- The condition for independent multiple hard scatterings in nuclear collisions requires σjet ≲ 2σmax/A^(2/3), which for A=197 gives approximately 180 mb

## Context

This work extends the pQCD-inspired approach previously applied to pp and p̅p collisions (PYTHIA, ISAJET) to heavy-ion collisions, building on the HIJING model developed by Wang and Gyulassy. It contrasts with purely hadronic models like RQMD and ARC (valid at AGS energies) and string models like FRITIOF, DPM, ATTILA, and VENUS (valid at SPS energies), by incorporating hard and semihard parton processes that become dominant at RHIC and LHC energies. The approach enables quantitative predictions for direct probes of early parton dynamics including pre-equilibrium photon and dilepton production, open charm production, J/ψ suppression, and jet quenching, which can be tested against future experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
