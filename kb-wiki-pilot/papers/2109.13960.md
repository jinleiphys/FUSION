# Nuclear fusion catalyzed by doubly charged scalars: Implications for energy production

> A number of popular extensions of the Standard Model of particle physics predict the existence of doubly charged scalar particles $X^{\pm\pm}$. Such particles may be long-lived or even stable. If exist, $X^{--}$ could form atomic bound states with light nuclei and catalyze their fusion by essentially eliminating the Coulomb barrier between them. Such an $X$-catalyzed fusion ($X$CF) process does not require high temperatures or pressure and may have important applications for energy production. A similar process of muon-catalyzed fusion ($μ$CF) has been shown not to be a viable source of energy because of the sticking of negative muons to helium nuclei produced in the fusion of hydrogen isotopes, which stops the catalytic process. We analyze $X$CF in deuterium environments and show that the $X$-particles can only stick to $^6$Li nuclei, which are produced in the third-stage reactions downstream in the catalytic cycle. The corresponding sticking probability is very low, and, before getting bound to $^6$Li, each $X$-particle can catalyze $\sim 3.5\cdot 10^{9}$ fusion cycles, producing $\sim 7\cdot 10^{4}$ TeV of energy. We also discuss the ways of reactivating the $X$-particles from the Coulomb-bound (${\rm ^6Li}X$) states, which would allow re-using them in $X$CF reactions.

## Key claim

Doubly charged scalar particles $X^{--}$ can catalyze nuclear fusion in deuterium environments with a catalytic poisoning probability so low that each particle may produce $\sim 7\times 10^{4}$ TeV of energy before sticking to a $^6$Li nucleus, but this viability depends critically on the $X$-particles being sufficiently long-lived (lifetime $\tau_X \gtrsim 10^2$ s) and on the ability to reactivate or extract them from bound states, as the production cross section at accelerators is too small to make the process energy-positive without recycling.

## Method

The author analyzes the kinetics of $X$-catalyzed fusion ($X$CF) by considering the full catalytic cycle in deuterium: formation of $(ddX)$ atoms, stage I fusion reactions producing $^3$He, $^3$H, or $^4$He, stage II reactions from $(^3{\rm He}dX)$ and $(^3{\rm H}dX)$ atoms producing $^4$He, and stage III reactions from $(^4{\rm He}dX)$ atoms producing $^6$Li. Branching ratios for each reaction channel are calculated, including those where the $X$-particle remains bound to the fusion product, and the cumulative sticking probability to $^6$Li is derived from the full reaction chain. The author also estimates formation times of $X$-atomic systems, charge exchange rates, and reactivation efficiencies for stripping $X$-particles from $(^6{\rm Li}X)$ ions.

## Key numbers

* Each $X$-particle can catalyze $\sim 3.5\times 10^{9}$ fusion cycles before sticking to $^6$Li
* Each $X$-particle produces $\sim 7\times 10^{4}$ TeV of energy before catalytic poisoning
* Fraction of $X$-particles that end up bound to $^6$Li is $\sim 2.8\times 10^{-10}$
* Net reaction: $4d \to {^6{\rm Li}} + p + n + 23.1$ MeV
* $X$-particle lifetime must exceed $\sim 10^2$ s to survive $3.5\times 10^9$ fusion cycles
* For reactivation without recycling, $X$-particles need $\tau_X \gtrsim 3\times 10^{4}$ yr
* $(^6{\rm Li}X)$ Coulomb binding energy is about 2 MeV; stripping requires beam energy $\sim 0.05$ GeV for $m_X \simeq 1$ TeV/$c^2$
* Cross section for stripping $(^6{\rm Li}X)$ ions on target nuclei with $A\simeq 40$ is $\gtrsim 0.1$ b
* Production cross section for $X$-particles with $m_X\simeq 1$ TeV/$c^2$ and $\beta=0.3$ is $\sim 1$ fb
* Moderation time of $X$-particles at liquid hydrogen density: $\sim 6\times 10^{-11}$ s; in deuterium gas at 1 bar: $\sim 4.8\times 10^{-8}$ s
* Charge exchange reaction $d+(pX)\to(dX)+p$ has rate $\sigma_c v \simeq 10^{-14}$ cm$^3$/s with $Q\simeq 90$ keV

## Context

This work extends the concept of muon-catalyzed fusion ($\mu$CF), which was shown not to be viable for energy production due to muon sticking to helium nuclei with probability $\omega_s\simeq 10^{-2}$ (producing only $\sim 1.7$ GeV per muon) and the muon's short lifetime. Previous analyses of catalysis by singly charged or fractionally charged heavy particles (Zeldovich, Ioffe, Hamaguchi) also found catalytic poisoning insurmountable. The key advance here is that doubly charged $X^{--}$ particles only stick to $^6$Li produced in third-stage reactions, with a probability $\sim 10^{-10}$ times lower than muon sticking, and the bound $(^6{\rm Li}X)$ ions can be reactivated by stripping with modest beam energies. The work also connects to searches for superheavy exotic isotopes in terrestrial materials (Cahn, DeRujula, Mueller, Smith, Verkerk) and to cosmological implications for primordial nucleosynthesis (Pospelov).
