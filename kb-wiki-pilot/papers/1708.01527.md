# The Electron-Ion Collider: Assessing the Energy Dependence of Key Measurements

> We provide an assessment of the energy dependence of key measurements within the scope of the machine parameters for a U.S. based Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) outlined in the EIC White Paper. We first examine the importance of the physics underlying these measurements in the context of the outstanding questions in nuclear science. We then demonstrate, through detailed simulations of the measurements, that the likelihood of transformational scientific insights is greatly enhanced by making the energy range and reach of the EIC as large as practically feasible.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates through detailed simulations that while the saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) in e+A collisions at an EIC with \(\sqrt{s}_{\rm max}=90\) GeV is only about 50% larger than at \(\sqrt{s}_{\rm max}=40\) GeV, this difference is sufficient to generate dramatic changes in DIS observables, making the likelihood of transformational scientific insights greatly enhanced by maximizing the energy range and reach of the EIC.

## Method

The authors performed detailed simulations of key measurements within the machine parameters outlined in the EIC White Paper, using the dipole approach to deeply inelastic scattering to compute observables in DIS events at high energy. They evaluated the kinematic reach in \(x\) and \(Q^2\) for multiple center-of-mass energies, and compared saturation model predictions for inclusive and diffractive cross-sections, charged current processes, and dihadron correlations across the proposed energy range.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) in e+A collisions at the EIC is approximately 50% larger for \(\sqrt{s}_{\rm max}=90\) GeV compared to \(\sqrt{s}_{\rm max}=40\) GeV
- At fixed \(x=10^{-3}\), the dipole scattering amplitude \(N\) varies by only approximately 20% within the \(Q^2\) reach at \(\sqrt{s}=40\) GeV, but reaches up to a factor of 6 within the \(Q^2\) reach at \(\sqrt{s}=90\) GeV
- For diffractive events, the squared dipole amplitude changes by a factor of 25 over the \(Q^2\) range at \(\sqrt{s}=90\) GeV, but only by a factor of 1.7 at the lower center-of-mass energy
- Approximately 15% of ep scattering events at HERA were diffractive
- For \(Q^2_{\rm min}=100\) GeV\(^2\), the charged current DIS cross-section shows a relatively modest drop with \(\sqrt{s}\), but at higher \(Q^2_{\rm min}\) the dip at lower \(\sqrt{s}\) values becomes dramatic

## Context

This work extends the physics case for the U.S. based Electron-Ion Collider outlined in the EIC White Paper (Accardi et al., 2012) and builds on lessons from DIS experiments at HERA and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. The study demonstrates that the higher center-of-mass energy provides access to a significantly wider range of values for the dipole amplitude at small \(x\), enabling robust tests of the gluon saturation picture that are not possible at lower energies, analogous to how the factor of 5 suppression in \(R_{\rm CP}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV at RHIC provided an unambiguous signature of jet quenching compared to the 20% suppression at 39 GeV.
