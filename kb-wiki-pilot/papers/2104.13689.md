# Investigating neutron transfer in the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system

> In this work $\textit{n}$-transfer and incomplete fusion cross sections for $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system are reported over a wide energy range, E$_{c.m.}$ $\approx$ 29-45 MeV. The experiment was carried out using activation technique and off-line gamma counting. The transfer process is found to be the dominant mode as compared to all other reaction channels. Detailed coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations have been performed for $\textit{n}$-transfer stripping and pickup cross sections. The measured 1$\textit{n}$-stripping cross sections are explained with CRC calculations by including the ground state and the 2$^{+}$ resonance state (E = 3.03 MeV) of $^{8}$Be. The calculations for 1$\textit{n}$-pickup, including only the ground state of $^{10}$Be agree reasonably well with the measured cross sections, while it overpredicts the data at subbarrier energies. For a better insight into the role of projectile structure in the transfer process, a comprehensive analysis of 1$\textit{n}$-stripping reaction has been carried out for various weakly bound projectiles on $^{197}$Au target nucleus. The transfer cross sections scaled with the square of total radius of interacting nuclei show the expected Q-value dependence of 1$\textit{n}$-stripping channel for weakly bound stable projectiles.

## Key claim

Neutron transfer is the dominant reaction channel compared to complete fusion and incomplete fusion at subbarrier energies for the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system, but the CRC calculations for 1n-pickup overpredict the measured cross sections at subbarrier energies, indicating a limitation of the model at those energies.

## Method

The experiment used the activation technique with off-line gamma counting to measure cross sections for neutron transfer and incomplete fusion products in the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system over a center-of-mass energy range of approximately 29-45 MeV. Coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations using the FRESCO code were performed for 1n-stripping and 1n-pickup channels, incorporating spectroscopic factors and coupling to excited states of the residual nuclei, including the ground state and 2$^{+}$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be for stripping and the ground state of $^{10}$Be for pickup.

## Key numbers

- 1n-stripping ($^{198}$Au) cross sections range from 11.2 $\pm$ 0.1 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 29.1 MeV to 136 $\pm$ 1 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV.
- 1n-pickup ($^{196}$Au) cross sections range from 0.150 $\pm$ 0.006 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 31.6 MeV to 9.1 $\pm$ 0.4 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV.
- ICF product $^{200}$Tl cross sections range from 0.150 $\pm$ 0.002 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 31.6 MeV to 119.1 $\pm$ 1.2 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV.
- ICF product $^{199}$Tl cross sections range from 0.5 $\pm$ 0.2 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 36.0 MeV to 75.4 $\pm$ 5.1 mb at E$_{c.m.}$ = 44.7 MeV.
- The Coulomb barrier for this system is V$_{B}$ = 38.4 MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for $^{9}$Be/$^{8}$Be in 1n-stripping is 0.42 for the ground state and 1.0 for the 2$^{+}$ resonance state of $^{8}$Be.
- The spectroscopic factor for $^{9}$Be/$^{10}$Be in 1n-pickup is 1.58.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of fusion suppression in the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system (Kaushik et al., Phys. Rev. C 101, 034611 (2020)), which found approximately 39% suppression relative to CCFULL calculations, by directly measuring the competing transfer and ICF channels. The systematic comparison with $^{6,7}$Li + $^{197}$Au systems (Palshetkar et al., Phys. Rev. C 89, 024607 (2014)) and $^{10}$B + $^{197}$Au (Aversa et al., Phys. Rev. C 101, 044601 (2020)) demonstrates that the scaled 1n-stripping cross sections follow the expected Q-value dependence, with $^{9}$Be showing the highest cross sections due to its large positive Q-value of 4.85 MeV. The results enable a more complete understanding of the interplay between transfer, breakup, and fusion in reactions with weakly bound projectiles.
