# CMS physics highlights in the LHC Run 1

> The main physics results obtained by the CMS experiment during the first three years of operation of the CERN Large Hadron Collider (2010--2013, aka. Run 1) are summarized. The advances in our understanding of the fundamental particles and their interactions are succinctly reviewed under the following physics topics: (i) Quantum Chromodynamics, (ii) Quark Gluon Plasma, (iii) Electroweak interaction, (iv) Top quark, (v) Higgs boson, (vi) Flavour, (vii) Supersymmetry, (viii) Dark Matter, and (ix) other searches of physics beyond the Standard Model.

# CMS physics highlights in the LHC Run 1

## Key claim
The CMS experiment's Run 1 data (2010-2013) confirmed the Standard Model Higgs boson with mass 125.09 GeV and found no statistically significant deviations from Standard Model predictions across all physics sectors, despite the fact that the fundamental problems motivating the LHC (mass generation, flavour, hierarchy, dark matter, colour confinement) remain unsolved.

## Method
The CMS Collaboration collected proton-proton collisions at center-of-mass energies of 7 and 8 TeV (integrated luminosity 25 fb^-1), proton-lead collisions at 5.02 TeV (34 nb^-1), and lead-lead collisions at 2.76 TeV (170 μb^-1). Measurements were performed across nine physics topics: Quantum Chromodynamics, Quark Gluon Plasma, electroweak interactions, top quark, Higgs boson, flavour physics, supersymmetry, dark matter, and other beyond-Standard-Model searches, using the full CMS detector system for particle identification, tracking, and energy measurement.

## Key numbers
- Higgs boson mass: m_H = 125.09 ± 0.21 ± 0.11 GeV (combined CMS+ATLAS, uncertainty below 0.2%)
- Higgs boson width: Γ_H < 22 MeV (95% CL), less than 5.4 times the Standard Model prediction
- Top quark mass: m_top = 172.38 ± 0.67 GeV (0.4% uncertainty from combined CMS measurements)
- Strong coupling α_s measured up to energy scales Q ≈ 2 TeV
- Jet cross sections described by NLO pQCD over 14 orders of magnitude
- B^0_s → μ^+μ^- decay observed with 6.2σ statistical significance, branching ratio compatible with Standard Model prediction (~10^-8.5)
- Gluino and squark masses excluded above ≈1 TeV; stop and sbottom masses above ≈0.5 TeV; electroweak gauginos and sleptons above ≈0.3 TeV
- Dark matter-nucleon cross section limits: σ(χ-N) ≲ 10^-40 cm^2 (spin-independent) and 10^-41 cm^2 (spin-dependent) at 90% CL
- Quark compositeness scale Λ ≳ 15 TeV; ADD graviton scale Λ ≳ 5-7 TeV; Z', W' bosons excluded below 1.5-3.5 TeV; RS extra-dimension scale Λ ≳ 2.5 TeV; leptoquarks and heavy quark partners excluded below ≈0.6 TeV
- Strongly-interacting particles suppressed by up to a factor of seven in central Pb-Pb collisions (R_AA ratio)
- Weakly-interacting probes (γ, W, Z) show R_AA ≈ 1 in Pb-Pb collisions, consistent with no medium effects

## Context
This paper summarizes the complete CMS Run 1 physics program, extending the precision measurements of the Tevatron experiments (CDF, D0) on top quark and electroweak physics while achieving the first observation of the Higgs boson (previously searched for at LEP and Tevatron). The results confirm the Standard Model with unprecedented precision across all sectors, but the null results in supersymmetry and dark matter searches push new physics scales increasingly away from the electroweak scale, making supersymmetry less "natural" as a solution to the hierarchy problem. The upcoming LHC Run 2 at 14 TeV with hundreds of fb^-1 will search for direct or indirect signs of new particles and symmetries at the TeV scale.
