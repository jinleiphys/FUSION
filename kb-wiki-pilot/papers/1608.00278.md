# Inclusive breakup of Borromean nuclei

> We derive the inclusive breakup cross section of a three-fragment projectile nuclei, $a = b +x_1 + x_2$, in the spectator model. The resulting four-body cross section for observing $b$, is composed of the elastic breakup cross section which contains information about the correlation between the two participant fragments, and the inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section. This latter cross section is found to be a non-trivial four-body generalization of the Austern formula \cite{Austern1987}, which is proportional to a matrix element of the form, $\langle\hatρ_{{x_1},{x_2}}\left|\left[W_{x_1} + W_{x_2} + W_{3B}\right]\right|\hatρ_{{x_1}, {x_2}}\rangle$. The new feature here is the three-body absorption, represented by the imaginary potential, $W_{3B}$. We analyze this type of absorption and supply ideas of how to calculate its contribution.

## Key claim

The paper derives a four-body generalization of the Austern formula for inclusive breakup cross sections of three-fragment (Borromean) projectiles, where the key new feature is a three-body absorptive potential \(W_{3B}\) representing simultaneous absorption of two interacting fragments by the target. The derivation is presented within the spectator model, but the authors note that a full DWBA analysis using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations is still in progress and will be reported in a future publication.

## Method

The authors extend the spectator model formalism from two-fragment projectiles (\(a = b + x\)) to three-fragment projectiles (\(a = b + x_1 + x_2\)), treating \(b\) as the detected spectator and \(x_1, x_2\) as the participant fragments. The inclusive breakup cross section is separated into an elastic breakup (EBU) component, which contains information about \(x_1\)-\(x_2\) correlations, and an inclusive non-elastic breakup (INEB) component, which generalizes the Austern formula to a four-body form proportional to \(\langle \hat{\rho}_{x_1,x_2} | [W_{x_1} + W_{x_2} + W_{3B}] | \hat{\rho}_{x_1,x_2} \rangle\). The derivation is presented in detail in a companion paper (Carlson, Frederico, and Hussein, arXiv:1607.05783).

## Key numbers

- The four-body elastic breakup cross section (Eq. 4BEB) contains a sum over final states with a delta function \(\delta(E - E_b - E_{\mathbf{k}_X})\) conserving energy between the detected fragment \(b\) and the \(x_1+x_2\) system.
- The inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section (Eq. CFH) is proportional to \(\rho_b(E_b)\), the density of states of the observed fragment, given by \(\rho_b(E_b) = \mu_b k_b / [(2\pi)^3 \hbar^2]\).
- The four-body reaction cross section is decomposed as \(\sigma_{R,4B} = \sigma_{R,x_1} + \sigma_{R,x_2} + \sigma_{R,3B}\), where \(\sigma_{R,3B} = (2/\hbar v_a) \langle \hat{\rho}_X | W_{3B} | \hat{\rho}_X \rangle\) is the new three-body absorption term.
- The paper references experimental data on \(^6\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi \(\rightarrow \alpha + X\) at \(E_{Lab} = 24\) MeV and 32 MeV (Moro1-2015) as an application of the two-fragment hybrid picture.

## Context

This work extends the theory of inclusive breakup reactions, originally developed in the 1980s for two-fragment projectiles by Austern et al. (1987), Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (1985), Udagawa-Tamura (1981), and Hussein-McVoy (1985), to the case of three-fragment Borromean projectiles such as \(^9\)Be, \(^6\)He, \(^{11}\)Li, \(^{14}\)Be, and \(^{22}\)C. The derivation addresses a limitation of the effective two-body "four-body" Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels model, which cannot calculate the incomplete fusion part of the total fusion cross section (Diaz-Torres and Thompson, 2002). The new formalism enables calculation of incomplete fusion for Borromean nuclei and reactions involving two-neutron transfer to populate Giant Pairing Vibration states (Cappuzzello et al., 2015).
