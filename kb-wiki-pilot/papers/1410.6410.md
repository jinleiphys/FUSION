# Four-body effects on 9Be + 208Pb scattering and fusion around the Coulomb barrier

> We investigate the 9Be + 208Pb elastic scattering and fusion at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The $^9$Be nucleus is described in a α+ α+ n three-body model, using the hyperspherical coordinate method. The scattering with $^{208}$Pb is then studied with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, where the α+ α+ n continuum is approximated by a discrete number of pseudostates. Optical potentials for the $α+^{208}$Pb and $n+^{208}$Pb systems are taken from the literature. We present elastic-scattering and fusion cross sections at different energies, and investigate the convergence with respect to the truncation of the α+ α+ n continuum. A good agreement with experiment is obtained, considering that there is no parameter fitting. We show that continuum effects increase at low energies.

# Four-body effects on 9Be + 208Pb scattering and fusion around the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim
The authors show that a full three-body (alpha+alpha+n) description of 9Be, combined with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, reproduces experimental elastic scattering and total fusion cross sections for 9Be + 208Pb without any parameter fitting, but only if continuum couplings are included; continuum effects are more important at low energies, and the agreement with fusion data degrades at the very lowest energies where the calculation overestimates the data.

## Method
The 9Be projectile was described in a three-body alpha+alpha+n model using the hyperspherical coordinate method, with real alpha+alpha and alpha+n interactions taken from the literature and a phenomenological three-body force adjusted to reproduce the experimental ground-state binding energy. The scattering with 208Pb was then treated with the CDCC method, where the three-body continuum of 9Be was discretized into pseudostates up to a cutoff energy of 10 MeV, and optical potentials for alpha+208Pb and n+208Pb were taken from the literature without renormalization. The coupled-channel equations were solved using R-matrix theory, and total fusion cross sections were computed from the imaginary parts of the coupling potentials.

## Key numbers
- 9Be ground-state binding energy: -1.57 MeV (experimental) versus -3.12 MeV with bare two-body potentials alone; a three-body force with V0 = 2.7 MeV and rho0 = 6 fm restores the experimental value.
- 9Be ground-state r.m.s. radius: 2.36 fm (calculated) versus 2.45 +/- 0.01 fm (experimental).
- 9Be ground-state quadrupole moment: 4.96 e.fm^2 (calculated) versus 5.29 +/- 0.04 e.fm^2 (experimental).
- 9Be ground-state magnetic moment: -1.33 mu_N (calculated) versus -1.18 mu_N (experimental).
- Continuum cutoff energy for pseudostates: 10 MeV; partial waves included: j^pi = 3/2^-, 5/2^-, 1/2^+.
- Elastic scattering data compared at laboratory energies of 38, 44, and higher energies (from Refs. Woolliscroft et al. 2004 and Yu et al. 2010).
- Total fusion cross section compared with data from Dasgupta et al. 1999; agreement is good except at the lowest energies where the calculation overestimates the data.

## Context
This work extends previous CDCC calculations for 9Be + 208Pb that used two-body cluster approximations (9Be treated as 8Be+n or 5He+alpha), which suffer from the unbound nature of 8Be and 5He and the lack of reliable optical potentials for those subsystems. The three-body approach treats the alpha+alpha+n continuum without assuming a specific cluster structure, making the 8Be+n and 5He+alpha channels strictly equivalent. The results confirm earlier findings by Druet and Descouvemont (2012) that continuum couplings are more important at low energies, and are consistent with the conclusions of Jha et al. (2014) regarding breakup enhancement of sub-barrier fusion. The method enables future application to other reactions involving Borromean nuclei such as 6He and 11Li.
