# Coherent Absorption Dynamics: The Dual Role of Off-Diagonal Couplings in Weakly Bound Nuclei

> Disentangling reaction mechanisms in weakly bound nuclei remains a long-standing challenge, often compounded by the treatment of absorption as an incoherent sum of channel contributions. Within the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) framework, we apply the generalized optical theorem [Nucl.\ Phys.\ A \textbf{842}, 48 (2010)] and show that the total absorption cross section,, $σ_{\mathrm A}\propto -\langleΨ|W|Ψ\rangle$, decomposes as $σ_{\mathrm A}=σ_{\mathrm D}+σ_{\mathrm B}+σ_{\mathrm{int}}$, where $σ_{\mathrm{int}}$ is a coherent interference term between channel components. For the systems and fragment-target optical potentials considered, $σ_{\mathrm{int}}$ is negative and comparable in magnitude to the direct absorption terms. The off-diagonal imaginary couplings play a dual role, redistributing flux among channels and generating $σ_{\mathrm{int}}$, which is required for flux-balance consistency. Calculations for $d+{}^{93}\mathrm{Nb}$ and ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}+{}^{59}\mathrm{Co}/{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ show that retaining the full non-diagonal coupling matrix substantially enhances breakup-channel absorption for heavy targets while reducing the total absorption through interference effects. Neglecting off-diagonal imaginary couplings therefore leads to a systematically biased physical picture, overestimating total absorption and severely underestimating breakup contributions, implying that experimental analyses based on incoherent-sum models inherit this bias. Full-coupling CDCC calculations are thus essential for consistent, mechanism-resolved extraction of absorption cross sections in weakly bound systems.

## Key claim

The total absorption cross section decomposes into direct, breakup, and a coherent interference term ($\sigma_{\mathrm{int}}$), where $\sigma_{\mathrm{int}}$ is negative and comparable in magnitude to the direct absorption terms for the systems studied; neglecting off-diagonal imaginary couplings $W_{ij}$ ($i\neq j$) is not merely an approximation but leads to a systematically biased physical picture that overestimates total absorption and severely underestimates breakup contributions.

## Method

Within the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) framework, the authors applied the generalized optical theorem to derive a decomposition of the total absorption cross section into three components: direct absorption from the elastic channel ($\sigma_{\mathrm{D}}$), breakup absorption from continuum states ($\sigma_{\mathrm{B}}$), and an interference term ($\sigma_{\mathrm{int}}$) arising from off-diagonal imaginary couplings between the ground state and continuum. They performed controlled numerical experiments comparing full-coupling CDCC calculations against calculations where the imaginary couplings between the ground state and continuum bins were set to zero ($W_{1j}=0$), using the $d+{}^{93}\mathrm{Nb}$ system at 25.5 MeV as a test case with a simplified model space.

## Key numbers

- For the $d+{}^{93}\mathrm{Nb}$ system at $E_{\mathrm{lab}} = 25.5$ MeV, the interference term $\sigma_{\mathrm{int}}$ was found to be $-146.7$ mb (negative and substantial)
- The elastic scattering angular distributions from full-coupling and $W_{1j}=0$ calculations showed significant deviations in the intermediate-angle region ($30^\circ - 120^\circ$), with deeper minima around $\theta \approx 60^\circ$ and $120^\circ$ in the full calculation
- Calculations were performed for $d+{}^{93}\mathrm{Nb}$ and ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}+{}^{59}\mathrm{Co}/{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ systems, with the continuum states extended to 12 MeV divided into 6 bin states for the deuteron test case

## Context

This work extends the generalized optical theorem formalism [Nucl. Phys. A **842**, 48 (2010)] within the CDCC framework to address the long-standing challenge of disentangling reaction mechanisms in weakly bound nuclei. It contradicts the common practice of treating absorption as an incoherent sum of channel contributions, as done in approaches that neglect off-diagonal imaginary couplings (e.g., Refs. Diaz-Ion02, Diaz-ion-beck03) or that neglect couplings between the elastic channel and the continuum (e.g., Refs. ICF18, li7Canto, RANGEL2020135337, li6fusion). The results enable mechanism-resolved extraction of absorption cross sections and advocate that experimentalists adopt full-coupling CDCC calculations as the standard for consistent interpretation of absorption data in weakly bound systems.
