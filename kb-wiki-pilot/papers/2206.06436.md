# Proton and neutron exchange as a prelude to fusion at near-barrier energies

> Systematic examination of fusion for $^{39,41,45,47}$K + $^{28}$Si and $^{36,44}$Ar + $^{28}$Si provides insight into the impact of neutron and proton exchange on fusion for nuclei at and near the N=20 and N=28 shells. Comparison of the reduced excitation functions reveals a marked difference between the behavior of open-shell and closed-shell systems. While coupled channels calculations provide a good description for the closed-shell nuclei they significantly under-predict the fusion cross-section for open-shell nuclei. The observed trends are examined in the context of a potential energy surface, including shell effects, and multi-nucleon exchange with consideration of Pauli-blocking.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at sub-barrier energies, open-shell nuclei near the N=20 and N=28 shells exhibit a significantly larger reduced fusion cross-section compared to closed-shell nuclei, and this enhancement cannot be explained by ground-state density distributions, coupled channels calculations including low-lying excitations, or simple Q-value considerations for neutron transfer alone.

## Method

Fusion excitation functions were measured for radioactive beams of 39,41,45,47K and 36,44Ar incident on a 28Si target using the ReA3 linac at NSCL-MSU, with evaporation residues detected in annular Si detectors and identified via the energy/time-of-flight technique. The experimental cross-sections were compared with predictions from the Sao Paulo potential using Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov ground-state densities and coupled channels calculations including the 2+ and 4+ states of 28Si. The observed trends were interpreted using potential energy surfaces calculated at the strong absorption radius, incorporating liquid drop energy with shell corrections and proximity interactions.

## Key numbers

- 28Si target thickness: 258 +/- 10 micrograms/cm^2 (with oxygen contamination of 98 +/- 4 micrograms/cm^2)
- Beam intensities ranged from 1.0 x 10^4/s (44Ar) to 4.5 x 10^4/s (39K)
- Detection efficiency for evaporation residues: approximately 78-84% over the entire energy range
- Q-values for two-neutron transfer: -6.068 MeV (39K), +1.187 MeV (41K), +2.899 MeV (45K), +3.843 MeV (47K)
- Q-values for one-neutron transfer: -4.604 MeV (39K), -1.621 MeV (41K), -0.432 MeV (45K), +0.105 MeV (47K)

## Context

This work extends prior above-barrier measurements by Singh et al. (2021) that showed open-shell nuclei near N=20 and N=28 have larger fusion cross-sections than closed-shell nuclei, now examining sub-barrier energies for the first time for these systems. The results contradict the expectation that simple Q-value considerations for neutron transfer can explain fusion enhancement, as seen in earlier work on 60Ni+58Ni (Stefanini et al., 1995) and 40Ca+96Zr (Timmers et al., 1997). The findings enable development of more complete theoretical descriptions that properly account for multi-nucleon transfer and Pauli-blocking in the initial stages of fusion, building on the stochastic nucleon exchange framework of Randrup (1979) and Schroder (1984).
