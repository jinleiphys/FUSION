# Effect of the repulsive core in the proton-neutron potential on deuteron elastic breakup cross sections

> The role of the short-range part (repulsive core) of the proton-neutron ($pn$) potential in deuteron elastic breakup processes is investigated. A simplified one-range Gaussian potential and the Argonne V4' (AV4') central potential are adopted in the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method. The deuteron breakup cross sections calculated with these two potentials are compared. The repulsive core is found not to affect the deuteron breakup cross sections at energies from 40 MeV to 1 GeV. To understand this result, an analysis of the peripherality of the elastic breakup processes concerning the $p$-$n$ relative coordinate is performed. It is found that for the breakup processes populating the $pn$ continua with orbital angular momentum $\ell$ different from 0, the reaction process is peripheral, whereas it is not for the breakup to the $\ell=0$ continua (the s-wave breakup). The result of the peripherality analysis indicates that the whole spatial region of deuteron contributes to the s-wave breakup.

## Key claim

The short-range repulsive core of the proton-neutron potential does not affect deuteron elastic breakup cross sections on a target nucleus at incident energies from 40 MeV to 1 GeV, despite modifying the inner part of the deuteron wave function; this insensitivity arises because the breakup process is peripheral with respect to the p-n relative distance for all partial waves except the s-wave, and for the s-wave the whole spatial region of the deuteron contributes, smearing out the effect of the repulsive core.

## Method

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method was used to calculate deuteron elastic breakup cross sections on a 58Ni target, treating the system as a p+n+A three-body model with the target assumed inert. Two proton-neutron interactions were compared: the Argonne V4' (AV4') central potential, which contains a repulsive core, and a one-range Gaussian potential (1G-av4) fitted to reproduce the same deuteron binding energy and root-mean-square radius as AV4'. Peripherality of the breakup process was analyzed by computing the ratio f = sigma_bu / b^2, where b is the asymptotic normalization constant of the deuteron wave function, for six one-range Gaussian potentials generating deuteron wave functions with rms radii from 1.7 fm to 2.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron breakup cross sections calculated with AV4' and 1G-av4 potentials differ by less than 2% at 80 MeV and 1 GeV for each partial-wave component (s-wave, d-wave, g-wave)
- At 80 MeV: total breakup cross section 106.7 mb (AV4') vs 105.3 mb (1G-av4); s-wave 27.3 vs 27.0 mb; d-wave 58.4 vs 57.5 mb; g-wave 21.0 vs 20.7 mb
- At 1 GeV: total breakup cross section 40.8 mb (AV4') vs 40.9 mb (1G-av4); s-wave 10.5 vs 10.6 mb; d-wave 27.9 vs 27.9 mb; g-wave 24.3 vs 24.3 mb
- The one-range Gaussian potential parameters: V0 = 52.10 MeV, a = 1.812 fm, producing deuteron binding energy 2.24 MeV and rms radius 2.01 fm
- CDCC model space included p-n continua with l = 0, 2, and 4; at 80 MeV: momentum bin size Delta k = 0.05 fm^-1 up to kmax = 1.5 fm^-1, Jmax = 80; at 1 GeV: Delta k = 0.25 fm^-1, kmax = 3 fm^-1, Jmax = 200

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Iseri and collaborators (1991) who compared CDCC results with one-range Gaussian and Reid soft core pn interactions for deuteron elastic scattering, finding differences in tensor analyzing powers but not in cross sections. The present study focuses specifically on the central part of the pn interaction using the AV4' parameterization, which effectively includes the tensor and spin-dependent terms of the Argonne V18 interaction. The results enable future investigation of whether a breakup process that selectively probes r larger than 1 fm could serve as a probe of the short-range repulsive core of the pn potential.
