# Numerical assessment of post-prior equivalence for inclusive breakup reactions

> We address the problem of the post-prior equivalence in inclusive breakup reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei. The problem is studied within the DWBA model of Ichimura, Austern, Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985)]. The post and prior formulas obtained in this model are briefly recalled, and applied to several breakup reactions induced by deuterons and $^6$Li projectiles, to test their actual numerical equivalence. The different contributions of the prior-form formula are also discussed. A critical comparison with the prior-form distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) model of Udagawa and Tamura [Phys. Rev. C24, 1348 (1981)] is also provided.

## Key claim

The post and prior forms of the inclusive breakup NEB cross section within the IAV model are numerically equivalent when the additional non-orthogonality and interference terms are included, contrary to earlier claims by Udagawa and Tamura that these terms are unphysical and that the IAV model overestimates experimental data.

## Method

The authors applied the post-form and prior-form DWBA formulas of the IAV model to several inclusive breakup reactions induced by deuterons and 6Li projectiles, using a binning regularization procedure to handle the marginal convergence of the post-form integrals. They compared the post-form IAV results with the prior-form IAV results (which includes UT, NO, and IN terms) and also with the UT model and experimental data for 62Ni(d,pX) at 25.5 MeV and 209Bi(6Li,alpha X) at 36 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For 62Ni(d,pX) at Ed=25.5 MeV, the post-form IAV calculation converged for bin widths Delta k approximately 0.04 fm^-1
- The post-form and prior-form IAV calculations showed very satisfactory agreement, with only small differences attributed to numerical inaccuracies
- For 62Ni(d,pX) at theta_p=20 degrees in the LAB frame, the sum EBU + NEB(IAV) reproduced the experimental data reasonably well, while EBU + NEB(UT) largely underpredicted the data
- The results contradicted earlier findings by Mastroleo, Udagawa and Tamura who reported that IAV calculations overestimated the data for this reaction

## Context

This work extends the IAV model of Ichimura, Austern and Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985)] and provides the first numerical test of the post-prior equivalence for NEB, which had been debated for over a decade. It contradicts the prior-form DWBA model of Udagawa and Tamura [Phys. Rev. C24, 1348 (1981)] and the calculations by Mastroleo, Udagawa and Tamura [Phys. Rev. C39, 1025 (1989)] who claimed the IAV model overestimates data. The work validates the regularization procedure of Thompson et al. [Phys. Rev. C84, 044613 (2011)] and enables reliable application of the IAV model to inclusive breakup reactions.
