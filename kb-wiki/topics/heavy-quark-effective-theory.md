## Landscape

Heavy quark effective theory (HQET) is a systematic framework for simplifying the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) description of hadrons containing one heavy quark, such as charm or bottom. By exploiting the limit where the heavy quark mass is taken to infinity, HQET reveals an approximate spin-flavor symmetry that reduces the complexity of non-perturbative dynamics. This matters because it provides a controlled expansion in inverse powers of the heavy quark mass, enabling precise predictions for decay rates, mass spectra, and transport properties that are otherwise intractable in full QCD.

The main approaches within HQET include the use of effective Lagrangians and matching procedures to connect the full theory to its low-energy limit. The paper "Effective Field Theory and Matching in Non-Relativistic Gauge Theories" derives the power counting rules for the 1/c expansion, emphasizing that a multipole expansion is essential for predictive matching. Several works apply HQET to specific observables: "A way to estimate the heavy quark thermalization rate from the lattice" uses a Kubo relation within HQET to define the momentum diffusion coefficient via a Euclidean correlator of color-electric fields. The study "Charm diffusion in a pion gas implementing unitarity, chiral and heavy quark symmetries" combines HQET with chiral perturbation theory to compute drag and diffusion coefficients. Other papers, such as "Understanding the D_sJ(2317) and D_sJ(2460) with Sum Rules in HQET," employ QCD sum rules in the HQET framework to identify excited heavy meson states.

Open questions and active directions visible in recent papers include the puzzle of the Lambda_c spectrum, where a predicted 3/2+ member of an L=2 multiplet is missing, as discussed in "Puzzle of the Lambda_c spectrum." The thermalization of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma remains an active area, with "Thermalization of Heavy Quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma" introducing resonant heavy-light interactions motivated by lattice indications of hadronic states in the plasma. Additionally, the paper "Transverse Momentum Spectra at Threshold for Groomed Heavy Quark Jets" develops an effective field theory for heavy hadron production in groomed jets, addressing non-global logarithms and pile-up effects, which points to ongoing refinements in applying HQET to jet substructure and precision collider phenomenology.
