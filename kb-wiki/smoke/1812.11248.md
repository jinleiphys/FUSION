# The puzzle of complete fusion suppression in weakly-bound nuclei: a Trojan Horse effect?

> Experimental studies of nuclear collisions involving light weakly-bound nuclei show a systematic suppression of the complete fusion cross section by $\sim$30\% with respect to the expectation for tightly bound nuclei, at energies above the Coulomb barrier. Although it is widely accepted that the phenomenon is related to the weak binding of these nuclei, the origin of this suppression is not fully understood. In here, we present a novel approach that provides the complete fusion for weakly bound nuclei and relates its suppression to the competition between the different mechanisms contributing to the reaction cross section. The method is applied to the $^{6,7}$Li+$^{209}$Bi reactions, where we find that the suppression of complete fusion is mostly caused by the flux associated with non-elastic breakup modes, such as the partial capture of the projectile (incomplete fusion), whereas the elastic breakup mode is found to play a minor role. Finally, we demonstrate that the large $α$ yields observed in these reactions can be naturally explained as a consequence of a {\it Trojan Horse} mechanism.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the observed ~30% suppression of complete fusion in weakly-bound nuclei is primarily caused by non-elastic breakup modes (such as incomplete fusion) rather than direct elastic breakup, and that the large alpha-particle yields can be explained by a Trojan Horse mechanism without requiring the conventional two-step breakup-fusion picture.

## Method

The authors decompose the reaction cross section into complete fusion, inelastic scattering, elastic breakup (EBU), and non-elastic breakup (NEB) contributions, then compute the complete fusion cross section by subtracting the other calculated components from the total reaction cross section. Elastic breakup is calculated using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method, while non-elastic breakup contributions are computed using the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) inclusive breakup model in its DWBA approximation, applied to the 6,7Li+209Bi reactions with the projectiles modeled as alpha+d and alpha+t two-body systems.

## Key numbers

- Complete fusion suppression of ~30% for 6Li+209Bi and ~25% for 7Li+209Bi relative to single-barrier penetration model predictions, at energies above the Coulomb barrier (Vb ~ 30 MeV)
- For 6Li+209Bi at 36 MeV (1.2 Vb) and 7Li+209Bi at 44 MeV (1.5 Vb), as the separation energy is increased artificially, the complete fusion cross section tends to the BPM values for separation energies of ~3-4 MeV
- The Coulomb barrier for the d+209Bi and t+209Bi systems is ~10-11 MeV
- The alpha NEB yield at the nominal Coulomb barrier energy is about 5 times larger than the EBU yield for 6Li, and about 4 times larger for 7Li

## Context

This work extends previous experimental observations by Dasgupta (2002, 2004) showing systematic complete fusion suppression in weakly-bound nuclei, and addresses the failure of classical dynamical models by Diaz-Torres (2007) and Cook (2016) to reproduce the full suppression magnitude. The approach enables a unified quantum-mechanical explanation of both the fusion suppression and the large alpha-particle yields observed in these reactions, and provides numerical support for the suggestion by Cook (2018) that clustering and weak-binding, rather than breakup in the usual sense, are responsible for complete fusion suppression.
