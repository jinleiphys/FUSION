# Nuclear Force from Lattice QCD

> Nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential is studied by lattice QCD simulations in the quenched approximation, using the plaquette gauge action and the Wilson quark action on a 32^4 (\simeq (4.4 fm)^4) lattice. A NN potential V_{NN}(r) is defined from the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with a local interpolating operator for the nucleon. By studying the NN interaction in the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 channels, we show that the central part of V_{NN}(r) has a strong repulsive core of a few hundred MeV at short distances (r \alt 0.5 fm) surrounded by an attractive well at medium and long distances. These features are consistent with the known phenomenological features of the nuclear force.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential derived from lattice QCD simulations in the quenched approximation shows a repulsive core of a few hundred MeV at short distances and an attractive well at medium and long distances, consistent with phenomenological NN potentials. However, this result is obtained at an unphysical quark mass corresponding to a pion mass of about 0.53 GeV, and the authors note that the NN potential defined from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude may depend on the choice of the nucleon interpolating operator at short distances.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 32^4 lattice with lattice spacing a = 0.137 fm, using the plaquette gauge action and Wilson quark action at gauge coupling beta = 5.7 and hopping parameter kappa = 0.1665. They defined the NN potential from the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of two local nucleon interpolating operators separated by distance r, solving an effective Schrodinger equation to extract the central potential V_C(r) in the ^1S_0 channel and the effective central potential V_C^eff(r) in the ^3S_1 channel. The wave function was obtained from four-point nucleon correlators with wall sources, and 500 gauge configurations were analyzed after thermalization.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 32^4, corresponding to (4.4 fm)^4 with lattice spacing a = 0.137 fm (a^{-1} = 1.44(2) GeV)
- Pion mass: m_pi = 0.53 GeV; rho meson mass: m_rho = 0.89 GeV; nucleon mass: m_N = 1.34 GeV
- Energy eigenvalues in finite box: E(^1S_0) = -0.49(15) MeV and E(^3S_1) = -0.67(18) MeV
- Scattering lengths from wave function zero: a_0(^1S_0) = 0.066(22) fm and a_0(^3S_1) = 0.089(27) fm
- Repulsive core height: a few hundred MeV at distances r < 0.5 fm
- Attractive well depth: about -(20-30) MeV at distances 0.5 fm < r < 1.0 fm
- Preliminary lighter quark mass (m_pi = 0.36 GeV) shows repulsive core height increases by 40% at the origin

## Context

This work extends the approach of using Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes on the lattice, previously employed by the CP-PACS collaboration for pi-pi scattering (Ishizuka, 2005), to the nucleon-nucleon system for the first time. The results are compared with phenomenological NN potentials such as CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 2001), Reid93 (Stoks, 1994), and AV18 (Wiringa, 1995), and the long-range behavior is checked against the one-pion exchange potential. This enables future studies of tensor and spin-orbit forces, hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon potentials relevant for hypernuclei and neutron star cores, and full QCD simulations at physical quark masses.
