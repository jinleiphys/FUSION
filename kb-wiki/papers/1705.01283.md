# Significance of distinct electron correlation effects in determining the P,T-odd electric dipole moment of $^{171}$Yb

> Parity and time-reversal violating electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{171}$Yb is calculated accounting for the electron correlation effects over the Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) method in the relativistic Rayleigh-Schrödinger many-body perturbation theory, with the second (MBPT(2) method) and third order (MBPT(3) method) approximations, and two variants of all-order relativistic many-body approaches, in the random phase approximation (RPA) and coupled-cluster (CC) method with singles and doubles (CCSD method) framework. We consider electron-nucleus tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) and nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interactions as the predominant sources that induce EDM in a diamagnetic atomic system. Our results from the CCSD method to EDM ($d_a$) of $^{171}$Yb due to the T-PT and NSM interactions are found to be $d_a = 4.85(6) \times 10^{-20} \langle σ\rangle C_T \ |e| \ cm$ and $d_a=2.89(4) \times 10^{-17} {S/(|e|\ fm^3)}$, respectively, where $C_T$ is the T-PT coupling constant and $S$ is the NSM. These values differ significantly from the earlier calculations. The reason for the same has been attributed to large correlation effects arising through non-RPA type of interactions among the electrons in this atom that are observed by analyzing the differences in the RPA and CCSD results. This has been further scrutinized from the MBPT(2) and MBPT(3) results and their roles have been demonstrated explicitly.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the P,T-odd electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{171}$Yb using the relativistic coupled-cluster method with singles and doubles (CCSD) and find results that differ significantly from earlier calculations, attributing this discrepancy to large electron correlation effects arising through non-RPA type of interactions among the electrons.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic atomic many-body calculations of the $^{171}$Yb EDM using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) method, second-order (MBPT(2)) and third-order (MBPT(3)) relativistic Rayleigh-Schrodinger many-body perturbation theory, the random phase approximation (RPA), and the relativistic coupled-cluster method with singles and doubles (CCSD). They considered electron-nucleus tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) and nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interactions as the sources inducing EDM in this diamagnetic atomic system, and evaluated the ground state wave functions and electric dipole polarizability to benchmark the accuracy of their methods.

## Key numbers

* CCSD result for EDM due to T-PT interaction: $d_a = 4.85(6) \times 10^{-20} \langle \sigma \rangle C_T \ |e| \ cm$, where $C_T$ is the T-PT coupling constant
* CCSD result for EDM due to NSM interaction: $d_a = 2.89(4) \times 10^{-17} \ S/(|e|\ fm^3) \ |e| \ cm$, where $S$ is the NSM
* DHF value of electric dipole polarizability: $\alpha_d = 124.51 \ e a_0^3$
* CCSD value of electric dipole polarizability: $\alpha_d = 135.50 \ e a_0^3$
* RPA value of electric dipole polarizability: $\alpha_d = 179.76 \ e a_0^3$
* MBPT(2) value of electric dipole polarizability: $\alpha_d = 141.25 \ e a_0^3$
* MBPT(3) value of electric dipole polarizability: $\alpha_d = 115.70 \ e a_0^3$

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of atomic EDMs in diamagnetic systems, particularly following the observation that in $^{199}$Hg and $^{225}$Ra atoms, large discrepancies existed between RCC results and earlier calculations due to non-RPA correlation effects (Sahoo and co-workers, 2015-2016). The $^{171}$Yb atom is of current experimental interest as a co-magnetometer for $^{225}$Ra EDM measurements (Liu and co-workers, proposal), and previous calculations using RPA (Dzuba and co-workers, 2002-2009), CI (Angom and co-workers), and MCDF (Radziute and co-workers) gave differing results. The present CCSD results enable more reliable extraction of fundamental P,T-odd coupling constants from future $^{171}$Yb EDM measurements.
