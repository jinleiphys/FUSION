# Effects of asymmetric dark matter on a magnetized neutron star: A two-fluid approach

> We study the interaction between dark matter (DM) and highly magnetized neutron stars (NSs), focusing on how DM particle mass, mass fraction, and magnetic field (MF) strength affect NS structure and stability. We consider self-interacting, nonannihilating, asymmetric fermionic DM that couples to NSs only through gravitational interaction. Using the Quantum Monte Carlo Relativistic Mean Field (QMC-RMF4) model with density-dependent magnetic fields, we investigate the magnetized equation of state and examine the accumulation of DM under various conditions. Our results show that as the DM fraction increases, the maximum gravitational mass of the NS decreases, especially for heavier DM particles, while lighter DM particles can induce a transition from a dark core to a halo structure, increasing the maximum mass. Strong MFs soften the equation of state and reduce the dark mass a NS core can retain before transitioning to a halo. By comparing our results with observations from Neutro Star Interior Composition Explorer and GW170817, we identify the possible range of DM parameters for these objects. We find that the magnetic field slightly changes these limits, mainly affecting the maximum NS mass and tidal deformability. These findings provide key insights into how DM and MF jointly shape the mass-radius relation and the stability of DM-admixed magnetized NSs.

# Effects of asymmetric dark matter on a magnetized neutron star: A two-fluid approach

## Key claim

The paper claims that asymmetric dark matter (DM) interacting only gravitationally with magnetized neutron stars (NSs) can either increase or decrease the maximum gravitational mass depending on DM particle mass, with lighter DM particles (around 100-200 MeV) enabling a transition from a dark core to a dark halo structure that increases maximum mass, while heavier DM particles (around 600-1000 MeV) decrease maximum mass; however, the magnetic field effects on these results are small (a few percent) compared to the DM parameter variations.

## Method

The authors employ the Quantum Monte Carlo Relativistic Mean Field (QMC-RMF4) model with density-dependent magnetic fields to compute the magnetized equation of state for hadronic matter, and combine it with a self-interacting fermionic asymmetric dark matter equation of state that couples only gravitationally. They solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric, non-rotating configurations, assuming a chaotic magnetic field framework to obtain an effective isotropic pressure. The DM self-interaction strength is constrained by fixing the cross-section-to-mass ratio to sigma_chi/m_chi = 1 cm^2/g, consistent with galaxy cluster observations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum NS mass without DM: 2.20 solar masses, with canonical radius R_1.4 approximately 12.24 km and tidal deformability Lambda_1.4 = 454
- Magnetic field strengths considered: B_c = 1, 2, 3 x 10^18 G at the core, with surface field B_surf = 10^15 G
- DM particle masses explored: 100, 200, 600, and 1000 MeV
- For m_chi = 100 MeV with 5% DM fraction, the maximum mass increases above the pure NS value; for m_chi = 1000 MeV with 15% DM fraction, the maximum mass decreases
- The transition from DM core to halo occurs at DM fraction f_chi approximately 15% for m_chi = 600 MeV, and at f_chi less than 1% for m_chi = 100 and 200 MeV
- For m_chi = 600 MeV with 5% DM fraction, the configuration with R_chi = R_N has mass approximately 1.73 solar masses and radius approximately 11.79 km

## Context

This work extends previous studies of asymmetric dark matter in neutron stars by Nelson (2019), Ivanytskyi (2020), and Liu (2023, 2024) to include the effects of strong magnetic fields up to 10^18 G. It builds on the two-fluid TOV framework developed for non-magnetized DM-admixed NSs and incorporates the density-dependent magnetic field parametrization from Debades (1997) and the chaotic field averaging approach from Mariani (2019). The results enable future comparisons with NICER and GW170817 observations to constrain DM parameters in magnetized neutron stars, though the magnetic field effects are found to be minor relative to DM parameter variations.
