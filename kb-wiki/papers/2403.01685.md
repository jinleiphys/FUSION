# Shell-cluster transition in $^{48}$Ti

> Background: Whether or not the $α$ ($^4$He nucleus) clustering exists in the medium-mass region of nuclear systems is a fundamental and intriguing question. However, the recent analysis of the $α$ knockout reaction on $^{48}$Ti [Phys. Rev. C 103, L031305 (2021)] poses a puzzle: The microscopic wave function gives an $α$ knockout cross section that is two orders of magnitude smaller than the experiment, while basic nuclear properties such as the charge radius and the electromagnetic transition probabilities are well explained. Purpose: The ground-state structure of $^{48}$Ti is investigated by using proton- and $α$-nucleus elastic scattering at a few to several hundred MeV, which offers different sensitivity to the region of the nuclear density profiles. Method: Four types of density distributions, the $jj$-coupling shell model and three cluster model configurations, are generated in a single scheme by the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM). The angular distribution of the proton- and $α$-$^{48}$Ti elastic scattering cross sections are obtained with a reliable high-energy reaction theory, the Glauber model. Results: The $jj$-coupling shell model configuration is found to best reproduce the proton-nucleus elastic scattering cross section. On the other hand, the trace of the $α$ cluster structure in the tail region of the wave function is embedded in the $α$-nucleus elastic scattering cross section. Conclusion: Our results suggest that the structure of the nucleus changes as a function of distance from the center, from the $jj$-coupling shell model structure in the surface region to the $α$+$^{44}$Ca cluster structure in the tail region. This picture is consistent with the finding of the $α$ knockout reaction on $^{48}$Ti.

# Shell-cluster transition in $^{48}$Ti

## Key claim

The ground state of $^{48}$Ti exhibits a structural transition as a function of distance from the nucleus center: the $jj$-coupling shell model configuration dominates in the surface region (around 3-4 fm), while an $\alpha$+$^{44}$Ca cluster structure appears in the tail region (beyond about 5 fm), consistent with the puzzling $\alpha$ knockout reaction data that cannot be explained by a purely shell-model wave function.

## Method

Four types of density distributions for $^{48}$Ti were generated using the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM): one $jj$-coupling shell model configuration (S-type) and three $\alpha$+$^{44}$Ca cluster model configurations (C-type-1, C-type-2, C-type-3) with varying $\alpha$-$^{44}$Ca distances and size parameters, all reproducing the experimental charge radius. The differential cross sections for proton-$^{48}$Ti elastic scattering at 1000 MeV and $\alpha$-$^{48}$Ti elastic scattering at 240 MeV were calculated using the Glauber model in the optical limit approximation, with no adjustable parameters after the density inputs were fixed.

## Key numbers

- The $jj$-coupling shell model configuration (S-type) has total harmonic oscillator quanta $\langle N \rangle = 84.0$ and spin-orbit expectation $\langle LS \rangle = 12.0\ \hbar^2$, matching the ideal $f_{7/2}$ occupancy for eight valence nucleons
- The three cluster configurations have $\alpha$-$^{44}$Ca distances $d = 0.1$ fm (C-type-1), $d = 2.379$ fm (C-type-2), and $d = 4.5$ fm (C-type-3), with corresponding matter diffuseness values $a_m = 0.586$ fm, $0.599$ fm, and $0.620$ fm respectively, compared to $a_m = 0.536$ fm for the S-type
- All four configurations reproduce the experimental point-proton rms radius of $^{48}$Ti, $r_p = 3.50$ fm (extracted from Angeli (2013))
- The S-type configuration best reproduces the experimental proton-$^{48}$Ti elastic scattering data at 1044 MeV (Alkhazov (1976)) at both the first and second diffraction peaks
- The C-type-3 configuration ($d = 4.5$ fm) best reproduces the $\alpha$-$^{48}$Ti elastic scattering data at 240 MeV (Tokimoto (2006)) at the first diffraction peak
- The absolute difference in the radial scattering amplitude between S-type and C-type-3 for $\alpha$ scattering peaks at impact parameter $b \approx 5$ fm, corresponding to the sum of the matter radii of $\alpha$ and $^{48}$Ti

## Context

This work addresses the puzzle raised by Taniguchi (2021), where the $\alpha$ knockout reaction on $^{48}$Ti gave a cross section two orders of magnitude larger than predicted by a microscopic AMD wave function, while the same wave function correctly reproduced the charge radius and electromagnetic transition probabilities. The authors extend their previous AQCM-Glauber approach used for $^{44}$Ti and $^{52}$Ti (Horiuchi et al.) and for light nuclei such as $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{20}$Ne. The results suggest that the structural transition from shell to cluster as a function of radius, previously discussed for $^{44}$Ti where the spin-orbit interaction breaks $\alpha$ clusters at small distances but allows clustering at larger distances, also applies to $^{48}$Ti. This mechanism could help explain why traditional shell and mean-field models underestimate $\alpha$ decay probabilities in heavy nuclei.
