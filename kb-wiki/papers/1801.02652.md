# Doubly charmed baryon production in heavy ion collisions

> We give an estimate of $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ production rate and transverse momentum spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We use Boltzmann transport equations to describe the dynamical evolution of charm quarks and diquarks inside quark-gluon plasma. In-medium formation and dissociation rates of charm diquarks are calculated from potential non-relativistic QCD for the diquark sector. We solve the transport equations by Monte Carlo simulations. For $2.76$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions with $0-10\%$ centrality, the number of $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ produced in the transverse momentum range $0-5$ GeV and rapidity from $-1$ to $1$ is roughly $0.02$ per collision. We repeat the calculation with a melting temperature $250$ MeV above which no diquarks can be formed. The number of $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ produced in the same kinematic region is about $0.0125$ per collision. We discuss how to study diquarks at finite temperature on a lattice and construct the anti-triplet free energy in a gauge invariant but path dependent way. We also comment on extensions of the calculation to other doubly heavy baryons and doubly heavy tetraquarks and the feasibility of experimental measurements.

# Doubly charmed baryon production in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors estimate that the doubly charmed baryon $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ can be produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions at a rate of roughly 0.02 per central Pb-Pb collision at 2.76 TeV, but this yield is conditional on the assumption that charm diquarks can form at any temperature; imposing a melting temperature of 250 MeV reduces the yield to about 0.0125 per collision.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Boltzmann transport equations for charm quarks and charm diquarks via Monte Carlo test-particle simulations, using in-medium formation and dissociation rates calculated from potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) for the diquark sector. The charm quarks are initialized from FONLL calculations with nuclear PDFs and the Trento binary collision model, while the QGP background is evolved with 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNew). Diquarks that survive to the hadronization hypersurface at T_c ≈ 154 MeV combine with a thermal up or down quark to form $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ baryons.

## Key numbers

- For 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions with 0-10% centrality, the number of $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ produced per collision in the kinematic range $p_T \in [0, 5]$ GeV and $y \in [-1, 1]$ is roughly 0.02.
- With a melting temperature of 250 MeV above which no diquarks can form, the yield in the same kinematic region is about 0.0125 per collision.
- The charm quark mass is taken as $M = 1.3$ GeV, the relative velocity of the $cc$ pair inside the diquark is $v \sim 0.4$, and the strong coupling is set to $\alpha_s = 0.4$.
- The diquark Bohr radius is $a_B = \alpha_s M / 3$, and the 1S binding energy is $E_{1S} = -\alpha_s^2 M / 9$.
- The hadronization temperature is $T_c \approx 154$ MeV, and the hydrodynamical simulation starts at co-moving time $\tau = 0.6$ fm/c with time steps $\Delta t = 0.04$ fm/c.

## Context

This work extends prior coalescence-based estimates of doubly charmed baryon production in heavy ion collisions (Becattini 2005, Zhao 2016) by incorporating a dynamical in-medium evolution of charm diquarks via transport equations, analogous to treatments of heavy quarkonia such as $J/\psi$ (Yao 2017). The calculation enables future comparisons with experimental measurements at LHC energies and with lattice QCD studies of the anti-triplet free energy and diquark melting temperature, following the approach used for quarkonia melting temperatures (Mocsy 2013). The authors also note that the measured ratio $\Lambda_c/D^0 \approx 1$ in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Zhou 2017) suggests that yields of doubly heavy baryons and tetraquarks should be approximately equal.
