# Systematic study of low-lying E1 strength using the time-dependent mean field theory

> We carry out systematic investigation of electric dipole (E1) mode from light to heavy nuclei, using a new time-dependent mean field theory: the Canonical-basis Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) theory. The Cb-TDHFB in the three-dimensional coordinate space representation can deal with pairing correlation and any kind of deformation in the time-dependent framework. We report the neutron-number dependence of the low-energy E1 mode for light (A < 40) and heavy isotopes (A > 100) around N = 82.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-lying electric dipole (E1) strength in neutron-rich nuclei shows a characteristic neutron-number dependence, with sudden jumps at specific neutron numbers (N=16 for light nuclei and N=84 for heavy nuclei), but the abstract does not mention that the heavy isotopes (Z=38-54) also show a constant, significant PDR ratio of about 1.5% even for stable nuclei like Xe isotopes with N=76-82.

## Method

The authors used the Canonical-basis Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) theory in three-dimensional coordinate space with the Skyrme SkM* functional for the particle-hole channel and a schematic pairing functional with a cutoff. They applied a weak, instantaneous external E1 field to induce linear response, equivalent to quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA), and computed the strength function via Fourier transformation of the expectation value of the E1 operator.

## Key numbers

- For light O, Ne, and Mg isotopes with N=8-14, the ratio of low-lying E1 strength (m1(Ec)/m1) is less than 1.0%, with a sudden jump at N=14 to 16.
- For heavy isotopes (Z=38-54, Sr to Xe) with N=76-90, a sudden jump in the ratio occurs at N=82 to 84.
- For stable Xe isotopes (130-136Xe) with N=76-82, the low-lying E1 strength ratio is 1.5%.
- The cut-off energy Ec used in the ratio calculation is 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) from light to heavy neutron-rich nuclei using a computationally feasible time-dependent mean-field approach, building on earlier TDHFB and HFB+QRPA methods (Ring and Schuck, 1980; Hashimoto and Nakatsukasa, 2007; Scamps and Lacroix, 2011). The Cb-TDHFB method was previously introduced by Ebata et al. (2010). The results enable systematic predictions of E1 strength for nuclei on the r-process path, particularly around N=82 and N=126, and suggest that the PDR mechanism differs between light and heavy nuclei.
