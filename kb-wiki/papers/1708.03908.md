# Strangeon Matter in a Liquid Drop Model

> The liquid drop model of 2-flavored ($u$ and $d$) nucleus is well known and successful, analogically, a similar drop model for 3-flavored ($u$, $d$ and $s$) nucleus is developed. A 3-flavored nucleus conjectured could be stable only if its baryon number is lager than a critical one, $A_{\rm c}$, in which strangeons are the constituent as an analogy of nucleons for nucleus. We try to model strangeon matter in a sense of phenomenological liquid drop, with two free parameters: the mass per bayron of a strangeon in vacuum, $M$, and potential deep between strangeons, $ε$. It is found that, for $M\sim$ GeV and $ε\sim 100$ MeV, strangeon matter could be stable and its critical number could be as low as $A_{\rm c}=300$.

# Strangeon Matter in a Liquid Drop Model

## Key claim

The authors propose that a 3-flavored (up, down, strange) nucleus composed of strangeons could be absolutely stable for baryon numbers above a critical value A_c, but only if the mass per baryon of a strangeon in vacuum (M) is on the order of GeV and the potential depth between strangeons (epsilon) is on the order of 100 MeV. The stability condition is conditional on these two free parameters falling within a large but bounded parameter space, and the model explicitly excludes Coulomb and asymmetry energy contributions.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological liquid drop model for strangeon matter, analogous to the standard liquid drop model for 2-flavored nuclei, with energy per baryon given by E/A = M + b_vol,s + b_surf,s A^{-1/3}. They use the law of corresponding states with a Lennard-Jones interaction potential between strangeons to derive the volume and surface energy parameters, assuming the interaction is similar to that between inert gas atoms. The critical baryon number A_c is determined by equating the energy per baryon of strangeon matter to that of normal nuclei (approximately 938 MeV for 56Fe), scanning over two free parameters: M (mass per baryon of a strangeon in vacuum) and epsilon (potential depth).

## Key numbers

- For M ~ GeV and epsilon ~ 100 MeV, the critical baryon number for stable strangeon matter is A_c = 300.
- The model is evaluated for two parametric sets: {N_q = 6, n = 1.5 n_0} and {N_q = 18, n = 2.5 n_0}, where N_q is the quark number per strangeon and n is the baryon number density in units of nuclear density n_0.
- The energy per baryon of normal 2-flavored nucleus (56Fe) is approximately 938 MeV, used as the threshold below which strangeon matter is considered more stable.
- For strangeon matter at temperatures significantly lower than ~0.1 epsilon ~ 10 MeV, the drop is predicted to solidify.

## Context

This work extends the liquid drop model, traditionally successful for 2-flavored nuclei (protons and neutrons), to 3-flavored strangeon matter, building on the generalized Witten conjecture (Witten, 1984) that strange matter in bulk could be absolutely stable. The strangeon concept, previously termed solid quark matter (Xu, 2003), is contrasted with quarkyonic matter where chiral symmetry is restored but quarks remain confined (McLerran and Pisarski, 2007). The model enables predictions for three possible astrophysical manifestations of strangeon matter: strangeon stars (pulsar-like compact objects), strangeon cosmic rays from binary mergers, and strangeon dark matter from the QCD phase transition epoch.
