# Baryon-number violation by two units and the deuteron lifetime

> We calculate the lifetime of the deuteron with dimension-nine quark operators that violate baryon number by two units. We construct an effective field theory for $|ΔB|=2$ interactions that give rise to neutron-antineutron ($n$-$\bar{n}$) oscillations and dinucleon decay within a consistent power counting. We calculate the ratio of the deuteron lifetime to the square of the $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation time up to next-to-leading order. Our result, which is analytical and has a quantified uncertainty, is smaller by a factor $\simeq 2.5$ than earlier estimates based on nuclear models, which impacts the indirect bound on he $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation time and future experiments. We discuss how combined measurements of $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillations and deuteron decay can help to identify the sources of baryon-number violation.

# Baryon-number violation by two units and the deuteron lifetime

## Key claim
The ratio of the deuteron lifetime to the square of the neutron-antineutron oscillation time is smaller by a factor of approximately 2.5 than earlier nuclear-model estimates, but this result depends on the assumption that the dominant baryon-number-violating operators are among the first three of the four gauge-invariant dimension-nine operators considered; the fourth operator would produce a different ratio.

## Method
The authors constructed a chiral effective field theory (χEFT) for baryon-number-violating interactions with |ΔB|=2, including nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-antinucleon interactions with a consistent power counting. They calculated the deuteron decay rate up to next-to-leading order (NLO) by evaluating the imaginary part of the deuteron propagator pole, using diagrams that involve neutron-antineutron transitions followed by annihilation, as well as direct two-nucleon annihilation, with low-energy constants determined from scattering data and chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The ratio R_d = Γ_d^{-1}/τ_{n\bar n}^2 is (1.1 ± 0.3) × 10^{22} s^{-1}, where Γ_d is the deuteron decay rate and τ_{n\bar n} is the free neutron-antineutron oscillation time.
- The NLO corrections from known low-energy constants affect the result by roughly 50%, with the effective-range correction contributing 0.40, the nucleon-antinucleon scattering length contributing 0.20, and explicit pion contributions amounting to 13%.
- From the SNO limit Γ_d^{-1} > 1.18 × 10^{31} yr (90% C.L.), the indirect bound on the neutron-antineutron oscillation time is τ_{n\bar n} > 5.1 yr = 1.6 × 10^8 s, about a factor of 2 stronger than the direct ILL limit of τ_{n\bar n} > 0.86 × 10^8 s (90% C.L.).
- The neutron-antineutron scattering length used is a_{\bar n p} = (0.44 − i 0.96) fm, obtained from a chiral potential.
- The deuteron binding momentum is κ = √(m_N B_d) ≈ 45 MeV, with B_d ≈ 2.225 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to baryon-number violation by two units, following the construction of chiral Lagrangians for n-{\bar n} oscillations by Bijnens (2017) and others. It improves upon earlier nuclear-model estimates by Dover (1982) and Sandars (1980), which gave a ratio approximately 2.5 times larger, and reconciles discrepancies with diagrammatic approaches by Arafune (1981) and Kondratyuk (1996). The result enables combined analysis of future neutron-antineutron oscillation experiments at the European Spallation Source and deuteron decay searches to discriminate among the four possible gauge-invariant dimension-nine operators that violate baryon number by two units.
