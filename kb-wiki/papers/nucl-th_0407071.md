# On the degeneracies of the mass-squared differences for three-neutrino oscillations

> Using an algebraic formulation, we explore two well-known degeneracies involving the mass-squared differences for three-neutrino oscillations assuming CP symmetry is conserved. For vacuum oscillation, we derive the expression for the mixing angles that permit invariance under the interchange of two mass-squared differences. This symmetry is most easily expressed in terms of an ascending mass order. This can be used to reduce the parameter space by one half in the absence of the MSW effect. For oscillations in matter, we derive within our formalism the known approximate degeneracy between the standard and inverted mass hierarchies in the limit of vanishing $θ_{13}$. This is done with a mass ordering that permits the map $Δ_{31} \mapsto -Δ_{31}$. Our techniques allow us to translate mixing angles in this mass order convention into their values for the ascending order convention. Using this dictionary, we demonstrate that the vacuum symmetry and the approximate symmetry invoked for oscillations in matter are distinctly different.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the exact vacuum degeneracy (which allows interchanging the two independent mass-squared differences via a specific transformation of mixing angles) and the approximate matter-induced degeneracy between standard and inverted mass hierarchies (valid for small θ₁₃ and Δ₂₁ ≪ |Δ₃₁|) are distinctly different symmetries, not perturbations of each other.

## Method
The authors use an algebraic formulation of neutrino oscillations based on projection operators and trace identities, assuming CP conservation (U = U*). For vacuum oscillations, they derive the transformation of mixing angles under a π/2 rotation about the second mass axis combined with time-reversal (M → -M) to interchange Δ₂₁ and Δ₃₂. For matter oscillations, they analyze the MSW Hamiltonian in the limit of vanishing θ₁₃, where the matter potential decouples from the m₃ eigenstate, allowing the map Δ₃₁ → -Δ₃₁ while preserving the νₑ-νₑ probability. They provide a dictionary to translate between the ascending-mass convention (m₁ < m₂ < m₃) and the conventional hierarchy notation (Δ₂₁ = Δ⊙, |Δ₃₁| = Δatm).

## Key numbers
- The vacuum symmetry reduces the parameter space by one half in the absence of the MSW effect.
- For the matter hierarchy symmetry, the map Δ'₃₁ = -(Δ₃₁ - Δ₂₁) preserves oscillation parameters to "several tenths of a percent" for a realistic case with θ₁₃ = 0.13.
- The approximate matter symmetry requires Δ₂₁ ≪ |Δ₃₁| and θ₁₃ near zero; the paper notes Δ⊙ ≪ Δatm from data (Refs. SS, list).
- For θ₁₃ = 0, the vacuum symmetry gives θ₁₃' = θ₁₂ - π/2, while the matter symmetry gives θ₁₃'' = θ₁₂, showing they are distinct.

## Context
This work extends the algebraic formalism developed by the authors in prior papers (Latimer and Ernst, Refs. kchdcl1, kchdcl2, angles) to study degeneracies in three-neutrino oscillations. The vacuum symmetry was previously observed by Ahluwalia (Ref. dharam1). The approximate matter hierarchy degeneracy is well-known from phenomenology (Refs. SS, list) and is shown here to be distinct from the exact vacuum symmetry. The results enable clearer interpretation of oscillation data by distinguishing which degeneracy applies in vacuum versus matter environments, and have implications for searches for CP or CPT violation and for physics in high-density systems such as supernovae.
