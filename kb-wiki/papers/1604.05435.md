# Determination of hadron-quark phase transition line from lattice QCD and two-solar-mass neutron star observations

> We aim at drawing the hadron-quark phase transition line in the QCD phase diagram by using the two phase model (TPM) in which the entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model with vector-type four-quark interaction is used for the quark phase and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model is for the hadron phase. Reasonable TPM is constructed by using lattice QCD data and neutron star observations as reliable constraints. For the EPNJL model, we determine the strength of vector-type four-quark interaction at zero quark chemical potential from lattice QCD data on quark number density normalized by its Stefan-Boltzmann limit. For the hadron phase, we consider three RMF models, NL3, TM1 and model proposed by Maruyama, Tatsumi, Endo and Chiba (MTEC). We find that MTEC is most consistent with the neutron star observations and TM1 is the second best. Assuming that the hadron-quark phase transition occurs in the core of neutron star, we explore the density-dependence of vector-type four-quark interaction. Particularly for the critical baryon chemical potential at zero temperature, we determine a range for the quark phase to occur in the core of neutron star.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark phase transition line in the QCD phase diagram is constrained by combining lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential with two-solar-mass neutron star observations, but the exact location depends on the density-dependence of the vector-type four-quark interaction strength, which is treated as an inverse problem rather than determined from first principles.

## Method

The authors construct a two phase model (TPM) combining the entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model for the quark phase with the relativistic mean field (RMF) model for the hadron phase. The vector-type four-quark interaction strength at zero chemical potential is fixed from lattice QCD data on the normalized quark number density, while three RMF parameter sets (NL3, TM1, and MTEC) are tested against neutron star mass-radius relations from observations. For models where the quark phase appears in the neutron star core, a Gaussian density-dependence of the vector coupling is introduced and its lower bound is determined from the condition that the phase transition occurs before the maximum neutron star mass.

## Key numbers

* The vector-type four-quark interaction strength at zero chemical potential is determined as \(G_{\rm v4}(0)=0.36G_{\rm s4}\) from lattice QCD data on \(n_{\rm q}/n_{\rm SB}\) at high temperature.
* The critical baryon chemical potential at zero temperature for the hadron-quark phase transition lies in the range \(1560\ \mathrm{MeV} \le \mu^{\rm c}_{\rm B}\le 1910\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The MTEC RMF model yields a maximum neutron star mass of \(M_{\rm max}=2.02 M_{\rm sun}\) with radius \(R_{\rm max}=10.8\ \mathrm{km}\); TM1 yields \(M_{\rm max}=2.18 M_{\rm sun}\) with \(R_{\rm max}=12.3\ \mathrm{km}\); NL3 yields \(M_{\rm max}=2.77 M_{\rm sun}\) with \(R_{\rm max}=13.2\ \mathrm{km}\).
* For TPMa1 (MTEC hadron phase, no vector interaction), the quark phase appears at \(M=1.97 M_{\rm sun}\) with \(\mu^{\rm c}_{\rm B}=1750\ \mathrm{MeV}\); for TPMb1 (TM1 hadron phase, no vector interaction), the quark phase appears at \(M=2.04 M_{\rm sun}\) with \(\mu^{\rm c}_{\rm B}=1560\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the two phase model approach previously used by Ishii et al. (2015) and the authors' own prior work (2015) by incorporating new lattice QCD data from the imaginary chemical potential method (2014) and recent two-solar-mass neutron star observations (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013). The study enables future determination of the density-dependence of the vector-type four-quark interaction from neutron star observations and provides a constrained band for the hadron-quark phase transition line that can be tested by future gravitational wave or neutron star radius measurements.
