# Single-pole nature of Lambda (1405) and structure of K-pp

> We have studied the structure of K- pp by solving this system in a variational treatment, starting from ansatz that Lambda(1405) is a K-p quasi-bound state, Lambda* with mass 1405 MeV/c2. The structure of K-pp reveals a molecular feature, namely, the K- in an "atomic center", Lambda*, plays a key role in producing strong covalent bonding with the other proton. Deeply bound Kbar nuclear systems are formed by this "super-strong" nuclear force due to migrating real bosons, Kbar, a la Heitler-London-Heisenberg, which overcompensates the stiff nuclear incompressibility. Theoretical background of the Lambda (1405) ansatz is discussed in connection with the double-pole picture of Lambda (1405) based on chiral SU(3) dynamics. Detailed analysis reveals single-pole nature of the observable Lambda (1405). There are two kinds of Sigma pi invariant masses experimentally observable, the usual T22 invariant mass and the conversion T21 invariant mass. It is of vital importance to determine whether the Lambda* mass is 1405 MeV or 1420 MeV. The T21 invariant mass from K- absorption at rest in deuteron can provide decisive information about this Lambda* mass problem.

## Key claim

The observable Lambda(1405) resonance is of single-pole nature, contrary to the double-pole picture from chiral SU(3) dynamics; the second pole is irrelevant to any experimental peak structure in the mass region between the Sigma+pi and Kbar+N thresholds.

## Method

The authors solved the K-pp three-body system variationally using the ATMS method with complex, energy-independent Kbar-N potentials fitted to low-energy Kbar-N data, treating the Lambda(1405) as a K-p quasi-bound state (Lambda*). They also analyzed the coupled Kbar-N / Sigma-pi system using both the Akaishi-Myint-Yamazaki (AMY) phenomenological model and the Hyodo-Weise (H-W) chiral SU(3) two-channel model, computing T22 (Sigma-pi to Sigma-pi) and T21 (Kbar-N to Sigma-pi) invariant-mass spectra to compare with experimental data from stopped K- on 4He and deuterium.

## Key numbers

- K-p (Lambda*) total energy: -27.8 - i 20.0 MeV (kinetic 115.3 MeV, potential -143.1 - i 20.0 MeV)
- K-pp total energy: -47.5 - i 30.6 MeV (kinetic 167.0 MeV, potential -214.5 - i 30.6 MeV)
- Exchange integral from I=0 Kbar-N attraction: -52.6 - i 7.3 MeV, the source of deeper binding in K-pp versus Lambda*+p threshold
- Best-fit Lambda* mass from chi2 analysis of K-4He bubble chamber data: M = 1405.5 (+1.4 / -1.0) MeV/c2, width Gamma = 25.6 (+4 / -3) MeV
- H-W chiral model poles: 1st pole at 1432 - i 17 MeV, 2nd pole at 1398 - i 73 MeV
- AMY model complex Kbar-N potentials: v(I=0) = (-595 - i 83) exp[-(r/0.66)^2] MeV, v(I=1) = (-175 - i 105) exp[-(r/0.66)^2] MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior predictions of deeply bound kaonic nuclear states (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002, Dote 2004) and the "super-strong nuclear force" concept (Yamazaki 2007b), and directly contradicts the double-pole picture of Lambda(1405) from chiral SU(3) dynamics (Jido 2003, Magas 2005, Hyodo 2008) which claims a shallow K-p bound state around 1420 MeV. The analysis of T21 invariant-mass spectra from few-body kaonic atoms provides a method to resolve the mass ambiguity, with stopped K- on deuterium proposed as a decisive experiment at J-PARC.
