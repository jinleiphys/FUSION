# Fate of $Σ_c$, $Ξ_c'$ and $Ω_c$ baryons at high temperature with chiral restoration

> Masses of the singly heavy baryons (SHBs), composed of a heavy quark and a light diquark, are studied from the viewpoints of heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and chiral-symmetry restoration at finite temperature. We consider the light diquarks with spin-parity $J^P=0^\pm$ and $1^\pm$. Medium corrections to the SHBs are provided through the diquarks whereas the heavy quark is simply regarded as a spectator. The chiral dynamics of the diquark are described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model having (pseudo)scalar-type and (axial)vector-type four-point interactions and the six-point ones responsible for the $U(1)_A$ axial anomaly. The divergences are handled by means of the three-dimensional proper-time regularization with both ultraviolet and infrared cutoffs included, in order to eliminate unphysical imaginary parts. As a result, the mass degeneracies between the parity partners of all the SHBs are predicted in accordance with the chiral restoration. In particular, the HQS-doublet SHBs exhibit clear mass degeneracies due to the absence of the direct anomaly effects. We also predict a mass degeneracy of $Σ_c$ and $Ω_c$ above the pseudocritical temperature $T_{\rm pc}$ of chiral restoration, which results in a peculiar mass hierarchy for positive-parity HQS-doublet SHBs where $Ξ_c'$ becomes heavier than $Ω_c$ Besides, it is found that the decay width of $Σ_c\toΛ_cπ$ vanishes above $T_{\rm pc}$ reflecting a closing of the threshold. The predicted modifications of masses and decay widths of the SHBs are expected to provide future heavy-ion collision experiments and lattice simulations with useful information on chiral dynamics of the diquarks.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that above the pseudocritical temperature of chiral restoration, the masses of parity partners of all singly heavy baryons become degenerate, and specifically that the positive-parity HQS-doublet baryons exhibit a peculiar mass hierarchy where the Ξ_c' becomes heavier than the Ω_c, while the decay width of Σ_c → Λ_c π vanishes due to threshold closing.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with (pseudo)scalar-type and (axial)vector-type four-point interactions plus six-point interactions for the U(1)_A axial anomaly to describe the chiral dynamics of light diquarks (spin-parity 0^± and 1^±) at finite temperature. The heavy quark in each singly heavy baryon is treated as a spectator, so medium corrections enter only through the diquark masses, which are obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation at the one-loop level with three-dimensional proper-time regularization including both ultraviolet and infrared cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- The pseudocritical temperature T_pc of chiral restoration is the temperature above which mass degeneracies between parity partners of all SHBs are predicted.
- The decay width of Σ_c → Λ_c π vanishes above T_pc, reflecting a closing of the threshold.
- The inverse mass hierarchy of 0^- diquarks (where the ud (0^-) diquark can be heavier than the su (0^-) and sd (0^-) diquarks) is driven by the U(1)_A anomaly and persists at any temperature.
- The ratio of the K term to the G_S term is estimated to be 28% for M_q and 14% for M_s at zero temperature.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Suenaga and Oka, 2023) which investigated spin-0 diquark mass modifications at finite temperature, by now including spin-1 diquarks and presenting comprehensive predictions for the mass corrections of HQS-singlet and HQS-doublet singly heavy baryons. The results contradict earlier predictions by Kim et al. (2021, 2022) that Σ_c becomes lighter than Λ_c under chiral restoration, which were based on simply reducing chiral condensates rather than using a full chiral model. The predictions are intended to guide future heavy-ion collision experiments and lattice QCD simulations in probing chiral dynamics of diquarks.
