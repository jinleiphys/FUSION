# Oxygen-16 Spectrum from Tetrahedral Vibrations and their Rotational Excitations

> A reinterpretation of the complete energy spectrum of the Oxygen-16 nucleus up to 20 MeV, and partly beyond, is proposed. The underlying intrinsic shape of the nucleus is tetrahedral, as in the naïve alpha-particle model and other cluster models, and A, E and F vibrational phonons are included. The A- and F-phonons are treated in the harmonic approximation, but the E-vibrations are extended into a two-dimensional E-manifold of D2-symmetric, four-alpha-particle configurations, following earlier work. This allows for the underlying tetrahedral configuration to deform through a square configuration into the dual tetrahedron, so there is tunnelling between the tetrahedron and its dual, with the associated breaking of parity doubling. However, E-manifold states can still be interpreted in terms of E-phonons. Rotational excitations of the vibrational states are analysed as in the classic work of Dennison, Robson and others, with centrifugal corrections to the rotational energy spectrum included. States with F-phonons require Coriolis corrections too. The first-excited $0^+$ state at 6.05 MeV is modelled as a state with two E-phonons; this allows a good fit of the lowest $2^+$ and $2^-$ states as excitations with one E-phonon. The Coriolis parameter $ζ$ is chosen positive to ensure the right splitting of the $3^+$ and $3^-$ states near 11 MeV. Altogether, about 80 states with isospin zero are predicted below 20 MeV, and these match rather well the more than 60 experimentally tabulated states. Several high-spin states are predicted, up to spin 9 and energy 30 MeV, and these match some of the high-spin, natural parity states in this energy range that have been observed. The model proposed here is mainly phenomenological but it receives some input from analysis of Skyrmions with baryon number 16.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the complete energy spectrum of Oxygen-16 up to 20 MeV can be reinterpreted using a tetrahedral intrinsic shape with vibrational A-, E-, and F-phonons, where the E-vibrations are treated anharmonically via a two-dimensional E-manifold that allows tunnelling between the tetrahedron and its dual through a square configuration. The model is mainly phenomenological but receives some input from Skyrmion analysis, and the authors acknowledge that the Coriolis parameter ζ is chosen positive (approximately 0.2) rather than the previously assumed value of -0.5 to fit the splitting of the 3+ and 3- states near 11 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a rovibrational model for Oxygen-16 based on a tetrahedral intrinsic structure of four alpha particles, treating A- and F-phonons in the harmonic approximation while extending E-vibrations into a two-dimensional E-manifold of D2-symmetric configurations that interpolate between tetrahedral, square, and bent rhomb shapes. They quantized the E-manifold with a Hamiltonian combining kinetic and potential terms, numerically computed wavefunctions and vibrational energies, and added rotational excitations with centrifugal corrections following the approach of Dennison and Robson. For F-phonon states, Coriolis corrections were included with a fitted parameter ζ, and the moment of inertia was adjusted downward for vibrationally excited states.

## Key numbers

- The first-excited 0+ state at 6.05 MeV is modelled as a state with two E-phonons
- The ground-state rotational band parameter B (equivalent to 1/2I) is set to 0.56 MeV, with centrifugal correction parameters C = 4.5 × 10^{-3} MeV and D = 2.8 × 10^{-5} MeV
- The 3- state at 6.13 MeV is fitted by setting B = 0.56 MeV
- The Coriolis parameter ζ is chosen close to 0.2 to fit the splitting between the 11.08 MeV 3+ state and the 11.60 MeV 3- state
- The lowest 1- state at 7.12 MeV is identified as a 1-phonon F-excitation, giving an F-mode frequency of approximately 6 MeV plus 1 MeV rotational energy
- The A-mode frequency is approximately 12 MeV, derived from comparison with the Carbon-12 breather state at 9.9 MeV using a spring model ratio of 2/√3 ≈ 1.15
- About 80 states with isospin zero are predicted below 20 MeV, matching more than 60 experimentally tabulated states
- High-spin states are predicted up to spin 9 and energy 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the long history of modelling Oxygen-16 as a tetrahedral alpha-particle cluster, following the foundational work of Wheeler (1937), Dennison (1954), and Robson (1979), who applied molecular rovibrational theory to nuclear spectra. It directly addresses the failure of Robson's model, which predicted a 3- state at 9.85 MeV that was not observed experimentally, by introducing an anharmonic E-manifold that allows tunnelling between the tetrahedron and its dual via a square configuration. The model also builds on the authors' previous work (Halcrow, King, Manton, 2017) on the E-manifold quantization and draws inspiration from Skyrmion solutions with baryon number 16, while contrasting with the approach of Bijker and Iachello (2014) who assumed equal vibrational frequencies of about 6 MeV. The results enable a systematic classification of essentially all known isospin-zero states in Oxygen-16 up to 20 MeV and predict several new states, including a 4- state below 15 MeV and high-spin states between 20 and 30 MeV.
