# The a1(1260) as a rho pi resonance in nuclear matter

> We present a theoretical study of the properties of the a1(1260) axial-vector resonance in a cold nuclear medium. In the vacuum, the a1(1260) resonance is generated dynamically from the interactions of a pseudoscalar and vector meson (rho pi and K barK*) in a coupled channel chiral unitary approach. Medium effects are implemented through the modification of the rho and pi propagators at finite nuclear density from well established microscopic many-body calculations. The in-medium pion spectral function accounts for the coupling to N-hole and Delta-hole excitations including short range correlations, whereas the in-medium rho incorporates modifications of its virtual pion cloud as well as direct resonance-hole excitations. The resulting in-medium a1(1260) exhibits significant broadening with increasing density as reflected in the rho pi scattering amplitude. The possible relation of our results with partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter is discussed in terms of in-medium Weinberg sum rules.

## Key claim

The a1(1260) resonance can be described as a dynamically generated state from rho-pion interactions in a coupled-channel chiral unitary approach, and its in-medium broadening at finite nuclear density provides an estimate of medium modifications without requiring explicit a1 couplings to baryonic resonances, though the authors note that vertex corrections (neglected here) would likely enhance the reported effects.

## Method

The authors unitarize the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for pseudoscalar-vector meson interactions using a Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels (rho-pi and K-bar-K*), generating the a1(1260) as a pole in the scattering amplitude. Medium effects are introduced by replacing the vacuum rho and pion propagators in the two-meson loop function with in-medium spectral functions from established many-body calculations: the pion self-energy includes N-hole and Delta-hole excitations with short-range correlations, while the rho self-energy incorporates modifications of its two-pion cloud and direct resonance-hole excitations (e.g., N*(1520)). The in-medium rho-pi scattering amplitude is then evaluated at finite nuclear density using a cut-off regularization scheme.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum a1(1260) pole position is found at sqrt(s_pole) = (1117 - i 177) MeV, corresponding to a width of approximately 350 MeV (compared to the PDG mass of 1230 +/- 40 MeV and width 250-600 MeV).
* The rho meson vacuum width is approximately 150 MeV in the center-of-mass system.
* The pion decay constant used is f = 93 MeV.
* The cut-off parameter for regularization is q_max ~ 1 GeV.
* Nuclear saturation density is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
* For the pion self-energy model B, the Landau-Migdal parameters are g'_11 = 0.6, g'_12 = g'_22 = 0.2, with a form-factor cut-off Lambda_pi = 0.3 GeV.
* The N*(1520) resonance is fitted with an in-medium broadening of 250 MeV at nuclear saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation of axialvector resonances from chiral unitary approaches (Roca et al., 2005) into the nuclear medium, combining it with hadronic many-body techniques for rho and pion self-energies (Rapp et al., 1997; Urban et al., 1998). It contrasts with earlier schematic "a1-sobar" models (Kim et al., 1999) and linear sigma model approaches at finite temperature (Urban et al., 2001; Harada et al., 2008) that treat the a1 as an explicit degree of freedom. The results enable future evaluation of in-medium Weinberg sum rules to study partial chiral symmetry restoration, and the authors note that vertex corrections (required by PCAC) should be addressed in future work on the full axialvector spectral function.
