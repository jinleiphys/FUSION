# Saturation and Scaling of Multiplicity, Mean p_T and p_T Distributions from 200 GeV < sqrt{s} < 7 TeV - Addendum

> In the previous paper we have argued that the LHC data on multiplicity, average transverse momentum, and charged particle transverse momentum distributions are well described with minimal modeling in terms of a saturation scale Q_sat(s). As a consequence, the p_T spectra should exhibit geometric scaling. In this short note we show that recently released CMS data at sqrt{s}=0.9, 2.36 and 7 TeV fall on a universal curve when plotted in terms of suitably defined scaling variable τ.

# Saturation and Scaling of Multiplicity, Mean p_T and p_T Distributions from 200 GeV < sqrt{s} < 7 TeV - Addendum

## Key claim
The authors show that the recently released CMS data at sqrt{s} = 0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV fall on a universal curve when plotted in terms of a suitably defined scaling variable tau, confirming that geometric scaling holds for the actual data rather than only for Tsallis parametrizations used in their prior work. The quality of scaling depends on the actual value of the parameter lambda and is best for the larger values shown (lambda = 0.27), though the data on multiplicity show a slight preference for smaller lambda values.

## Method
The authors plot the CMS charged particle transverse momentum distributions d^2N_ch/dy d^2p_T for full pseudorapidity range |eta| < 2.4 as a function of p_T^2 (corresponding to tau with lambda = 0) and then as a function of the scaling variable tau = (p_T^2 / Q_0^2) * (p_T / W)^lambda for three choices of lambda = 0.23, 0.25, and 0.27, where Q_0 ~ 1 GeV and W ~ sqrt(s). They also perform fits to central rapidity multiplicity and average transverse momentum at central rapidity for the same lambda values, and extend the analysis to UA1 p-bar p data.

## Key numbers
- CMS data at sqrt{s} = 0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV are analyzed for geometric scaling.
- Three values of the parameter lambda are examined: lambda = 0.23, 0.25, and 0.27.
- The effective power of multiplicity growth is lambda_eff = 2*lambda / (2 + lambda); for lambda = 0.27, lambda_eff = 0.238.
- The saturation scale is defined as Q_sat^2(s) ~ Q_0^2 (s_0 / s)^(lambda/2) with Q_0 ~ 1 GeV.
- The power lambda ~ 0.2 to 0.3 is related to the growth of the gluon distribution in proton with decreasing Bjorken x as measured at HERA.

## Context
This addendum extends the authors' prior work (McLerran and Praszalowicz, 2010) which argued that LHC data on multiplicity, average transverse momentum, and charged particle transverse momentum distributions are well described in terms of a saturation scale Q_sat(s). The present note confirms geometric scaling using actual CMS data rather than Tsallis parametrizations, and also shows similar scaling for UA1 data. The work is based on the theory of saturation and the Color Glass Condensate, and references the geometric scaling concept from Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kwiecinski (2000). Other authors have since undertaken more ambitious calculations of particle spectra from Color Glass Condensate models incorporating both HERA and RHIC data, and this work supports the conclusion that saturated gluonic matter dominates particle production in gamma-p collisions at small Bjorken x and in heavy ion and pp collisions at high energies.
