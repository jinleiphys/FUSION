# Thermodynamic geometry in hadron resonance gas model at real and imaginary baryon chemical potential and a simple sufficient condition for quark deconfinement

> The thermodynamic geometry of the hadron resonance gas model with (without) excluded volume effects (EVE) of baryons is investigated. The case with imaginary mu, where mu is the baryon chemical potential, is investigated as well as the one with real mu. We calculate the scalar curvature R and use the R=0 criterion to investigate the phase structure in the mu^2-T plane where T is the temperature. The curve on which R=0 continues analytically from the imaginary mu region, where the lattice QCD is feasible, to the real mu one. In the presence of EVE, there are rich phase structures in the large real mu region as well as the Roberge-Weiss like region where mu is imaginary and a singularity appears, while there is no phase structure in the large real $μ$ region in the absence of EVE. The limitation temperature of the baryon gas is also obtained by using the baryon number fluctuation. The LQCD predicted critical point locates almost on the curve of the limitation temperature we determined. A simple empiric sufficient condition, n_B>1/(2v_B)$, is obtained for the quark deconfinement in the large real mu region, where n_B and v_B are the net baryon number density and the volume of a baryon, respectively.

## Key claim

The scalar curvature R in the hadron resonance gas model with excluded volume effects (EVE) satisfies R=0 on a curve that continues analytically from the imaginary baryon chemical potential region (where lattice QCD is feasible) to the real baryon chemical potential region, and a simple sufficient condition for quark deconfinement in the large real mu region is n_B > 1/(2v_B), where n_B is the net baryon number density and v_B is the volume of a baryon.

## Method

The authors calculated the thermodynamic scalar curvature R from the metric tensor derived from the grand-canonical potential phi = P/T in the hadron resonance gas model, both with and without excluded volume effects (EVE) of baryons. They used the Boltzmann distribution approximation for baryons and an ideal Bose gas approximation for mesons, and investigated the phase structure in the mu^2-T plane using the R=0 criterion for both real and imaginary baryon chemical potential mu. The limiting temperature of the baryon gas was also determined using baryon number fluctuation.

## Key numbers

- The Roberge-Weiss-like temperature T_RWL = 0.2103 GeV for the HRG model with EVE using baryon radius r_B = 0.8 fm and updated hadron resonance data
- The RW temperature T_RW for 2+1 flavor QCD is estimated as about 200 MeV by lattice QCD simulations
- In the nucleon gas model instead of baryon resonance gas, T_RWL = 0.316 GeV, which is much higher than T_RW ~ 0.2 GeV
- The baryon volume v_B = (4pi/3)r_B^3 with r_B = 0.8 fm used in numerical calculations
- The condition B(T) = 1/v_B is satisfied when T = T_RWL = 0.2103 GeV

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic geometry approach of Castorina, Imbrosciano and Lanteri (2018) and Castorina et al. (2018), who used the R=0 criterion to determine pseudo-critical temperatures in the HRG model with and without EVE. The study also builds on the authors' previous work (Oshima et al., 2023) on the theta-dependence of baryon number density and pressure in the HRG model with EVE. The results enable connecting lattice QCD simulations at imaginary chemical potential to the phase structure at real chemical potential, and provide a simple condition for quark deconfinement that can be tested against lattice QCD predictions for the critical point location.
