# The production of doubly charmed exotic hadrons in heavy ion collisions

> Hadron spectroscopy provides direct physical measurements that shed light on the non-perturbative behavior of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In particular, various exotic hadrons such as the newly observed $T_{cc}^+$ by the LHCb collaboration, offer unique insights on the QCD dynamics in hadron structures. In this letter, we demonstrate how heavy ion collisions can serve as a powerful venue for hadron spectroscopy study of doubly charmed exotic hadrons by virtue of the extremely charm-rich environment created in such collisions. The yields of $T_{cc}^+$ as well as its potential isospin partners are computed within the molecular picture for Pb-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy $2.76~\mathrm{TeV}$. We find about three-order-of-magnitude enhancement in the production of $T_{cc}^+$ in Pb-Pb collisions as compared with the yield in proton-proton collisions, with a moderately smaller enhancement in the yields of the isospin partners $T_{cc}^0$ and $T_{cc}^{++}$. The $T_{cc}^+$ yield is comparable to that of the $X(3872)$ in the most central collisions while shows a considerably stronger decrease toward peripheral collisions, due to a "threshold" effect of the required double charm quarks for $T_{cc}^+$. Final results for their rapidity and transverse momentum $p_T$ dependence as well as the elliptic flow coefficient are reported and can be tested by future experimental measurements.

## Key claim

Heavy ion collisions can enhance the production of the doubly charmed tetraquark \(T_{cc}^+\) by about three orders of magnitude compared to proton-proton collisions, but this enhancement is strongly centrality-dependent and the yield drops much faster toward peripheral collisions than that of the \(X(3872)\) due to a "threshold" effect requiring two charm quark pairs.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT transport model to simulate one million minimum bias Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76~\mathrm{TeV}\), collecting charm mesons (\(D\) and \(D^*\)) after hadronization. They formed \(T_{cc}\) states via coalescence in the hadronic molecular picture, requiring a relative distance between 5 fm and 7 fm and an invariant mass between \(2M_D\) and \(2M_{D^*}\). The relative abundances of \(D^*\) versus \(D\) mesons were estimated using a thermal model with freeze-out temperature \(T_{\text{freezeout}}\simeq 160~\mathrm{MeV}\), and the \(X(3872)\) yield was computed in the same framework as a benchmark.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive yields in central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76~\mathrm{TeV}\): \(X(3872) \approx 49000\), \(T_{cc}^0 \approx 44000\), \(T_{cc}^{++} \approx 44000\), \(T_{cc}^+ \approx 50000\) (for \(\mathrm{R}(\frac{D}{D+D^*})=70\%\)).
- The \(T_{cc}^+\) yield in central Pb-Pb collisions is enhanced by about three orders of magnitude compared to the yield in proton-proton collisions.
- The ratio of \(T_{cc}^+\) to \(X(3872)\) yield in ultra-peripheral collisions is at least three orders of magnitude smaller than in central collisions, extrapolating to consistency with LHCb measurements in \(pp\) collisions.
- The iso-triplet states \(T_{cc}^0\) and \(T_{cc}^{++}\) have yields at least two orders of magnitude smaller than \(T_{cc}^+\) in the ultra-peripheral limit.
- The thermal model fractions used: \(29.3\%\) \(D^{*+}\) versus \(70.7\%\) \(D^+\), and \(29.2\%\) \(D^{*0}\) versus \(70.8\%\) \(D^0\), with uncertainties estimated by varying fractions in the range \([20\%,40\%]\) and \([80\%,60\%]\) for \(D^+\) and \(D^{*+}\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the framework developed by Zhang et al. (2020) for \(X(3872)\) production in heavy ion collisions to the newly observed \(T_{cc}^+\) state reported by LHCb (2021). It demonstrates that the charm-rich environment of heavy ion collisions is even more advantageous for doubly charmed exotics than for singly charmed ones like \(X(3872)\), and predicts centrality, rapidity, transverse momentum, and elliptic flow distributions that can be tested by future experimental measurements at LHC and other facilities.
