# Deuteron NN*(1440) components from a chiral quark model

> We present a nonrelativistic coupled-channel calculation of the deuteron structure including Delta Delta and NN^*(1440) channels, besides the standard NN S and D-wave components. All the necessary building blocks to perform the calculation have been obtained from the same underlying quark model. The calculated NN^*(1440) probabilities find support in the explanation given to different deuteron reactions.

# Deuteron NN*(1440) components from a chiral quark model

## Key claim

The NN*(1440) probabilities in the deuteron are smaller than the Delta-Delta probabilities and do not significantly influence static deuteron properties, but these small components are compatible with indirect experimental evidence from reactions such as subthreshold antiproton production and pd → dp processes. The calculation avoids the model dependence of previous meson-exchange approaches by deriving all baryon-baryon interactions from the same underlying quark model without independently fitted parameters for different channels.

## Method

A nonrelativistic coupled-channel calculation was performed using the resonating group method (RGM) formulated via Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space, generalized to include NN, Delta-Delta, and NN*(1440) channels. The baryon-baryon interactions were derived from a constituent quark model with pseudoscalar-boson exchange, scalar-boson exchange, and one-gluon-exchange forces, with the transition potentials calculated using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. The deuteron binding energy and wave function were obtained by solving the discretized momentum-space eigenvalue problem via the Fredholm determinant method.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: Ed = -2.2246 MeV (reproduced in all calculations)
- NN*(1440) probability: 0.0022% in the 3S1 partial wave and 0.0148% in the 3D1 partial wave
- Delta-Delta total probability: 0.2405% (sum of 0.1224% 3S1, 0.0036% 3D1, 0.1245% 7D1, 0.0063% 7G1)
- NN probability: 95.1885% 3S1 and 4.5377% 3D1 in the full calculation
- Deuteron root-mean-square matter radius: rm = 1.985 fm (full calculation)
- Asymptotic S-wave amplitude: AS = 0.8941 fm^{-1/2} (full calculation)
- Asymptotic D/S ratio: eta = 0.0250 (full calculation)
- Energy difference from NN threshold: 501.0 MeV for NN*(1440) channel, 586.0 MeV for Delta-Delta channel

## Context

This work extends previous quark-model studies of nonnucleonic components in the deuteron (Kunz (1990), Glozman (1998)) by explicitly including NN*(1440) channels in a coupled-channel calculation without the ad hoc modifications of the NN interaction required in meson-exchange approaches such as Haapakoski and Saarela (1979) or Rost (1988). The results are a factor ten smaller than the NN*(1440) probability of 0.17% reported by Rost (1988) using the Reid hard-core potential, but agree in order of magnitude with the upper limit of 0.1% from Glozman (1998). The calculation enables more reliable predictions for deuteron reactions sensitive to nonnucleonic components, such as antiproton-deuteron annihilation (Dalkarov (1990)), subthreshold antiproton production (Dolinsky (1989)), and pd → dp processes (Uzikov (1998)).
