# Hidden supersymmetry in quantum bosonic systems

> We show that some simple well studied quantum mechanical systems without fermion (spin) degrees of freedom display, surprisingly, a hidden supersymmetry. The list includes the bound state Aharonov-Bohm, the Dirac delta and the Poschl-Teller potential problems, in which the unbroken and broken N=2 supersymmetry of linear and nonlinear (polynomial) forms is revealed.

## Key claim

Some simple, well-studied quantum mechanical systems without fermion (spin) degrees of freedom display a true hidden N=2 supersymmetry, revealed through the identification of a nonlocal grading operator (a reflection or twisted reflection operator) and a nontrivial integral anticommuting with it.

## Method

The authors identify the reflection operator R (or a twisted variant) as a grading operator in three bosonic systems with local Hamiltonians: the bound state Aharonov-Bohm effect on a circle, the one-dimensional Dirac delta potential, and the Pöschl-Teller potential. They construct supercharge operators Q1 and Q2 from the momentum operator and the grading operator, and verify that these satisfy the N=2 superalgebra {Qa,Qb}=2δabH. For the Pöschl-Teller system with integer parameter λ=l, the supercharges generate a nonlinear (polynomial) superalgebra of order 2l+1.

## Key numbers

- Bound state Aharonov-Bohm system: for integer α=n, the ground state is a singlet with energy E=0; for half-integer α=n+1/2, all energy levels are doubly degenerate with minimum ground state energy E=1/4.
- Dirac delta potential (attractive, β>0): the unique bound state ψ0(x)=√β e^{-β|x|} has zero energy; scattering states with E>β² are doubly degenerate.
- Pöschl-Teller potential with λ=l (l=1,2,…): there are l bound states with energies E_{l,n}=(l²-(l-n)²)ω² for n=0,…,l-1; the continuous spectrum starts at E_l(0)=l²ω² and is doubly degenerate except for the first level.
- Pöschl-Teller transmission coefficient: t_l(k)=A⁺_l(k)/A⁻_l(k) where A^∓_l(k)=(ik±lω)(ik±(l-1)ω)…(ik±ω).

## Context

This work extends the observation of hidden supersymmetry in systems with nonlocal Hamiltonians depending on a reflection operator (SUSYbos, ParaP) and in a conformal mechanics model where it appeared as a fictitious symmetry due to boundary conditions (SUSYconf). The authors show that the bound state Aharonov-Bohm, Dirac delta, and Pöschl-Teller systems, all with local Hamiltonians, possess a true hidden N=2 supersymmetry. This enables investigation of double supersymmetry in ordinary supersymmetric extensions of these systems, and suggests searching for hidden SUSY in nonlinear integrable systems (Korteweg-de Vries equation, φ⁴ and sine-Gordon field theories) and in (2+1)D models with Chern-Simons term.
