# Effective Field Theory of Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering on Large Discrete Lattices

> Nuclear effective field theory is applied to the effective range expansion of S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering on a discrete lattice. Lattice regularization is demonstrated to yield the effective range expansion in the same way as in the usual continuous open space. The relation between the effective range parameters and the potential parameters is presented in the limit of a large lattice.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice regularization (finite lattice spacing) yields the effective range expansion for S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering in the same algebraic form as in continuous open space, provided one works in the limit of large lattice volume and uses the K (reaction) matrix instead of the T matrix. The key condition is that the lattice spacing must satisfy \( |a/r_0| \lesssim \pi \) for the effective field theory expansion to converge.

## Method

The authors apply nuclear effective field theory with contact interactions (delta-function and momentum-dependent terms) to two-nucleon scattering on a large cubic lattice. They use the K matrix formalism with standing-wave boundary conditions appropriate for a closed space, and derive diagrammatic expansions relating the effective range parameters (scattering length \(a_0\) and effective range \(r_0\)) to the lattice potential parameters \(c_0(a)\) and \(c_2(a)\). The lattice regularization is implemented via a simple cubic lattice with spacing \(a\), using the standard four-point difference formula for the kinetic energy operator, and the momentum integrals are restricted to the first Brillouin zone.

## Key numbers

- Watson's triple integral constant for the lattice: \(\theta_1 = 1.58796\cdots\) (from \(\theta_1 = \pi A\) with \(A = 0.505462\cdots\))
- Lattice \(R\) function at zero momentum: \(R(0) = 0.754330\cdots\) (from \(R(0) = \pi^3 B/2\) with \(B = 0.0486566\cdots\))
- Lattice constant \(\theta_3 = 2/\pi\) for the \(L_3\) integral
- For a numerical example with lattice spacing \(a = 2.0\) fm (\(\pi/a = 1.57\) fm\(^{-1}\)), the inclusion of the momentum-dependent term increases \(c_0(a)\) by about 80% relative to the leading-order value
- The ratio \(|c_2(a)/c_0(a)| p_{\text{max}}^2\) is about 0.06 for \(a = 2.0\) fm, indicating perturbative control
- The inequality for lattice spacing validity: \(|a/r_0| \lesssim \pi\) (about 8.5–5.5 fm for nuclear systems)

## Context

This work extends the nuclear effective field theory approach of van Kolck (1999) and the power divergence subtraction scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) to the case of lattice regularization. It provides the necessary relation between effective range parameters and lattice potential parameters for use in many-body Monte Carlo calculations of nuclear matter, such as those initiated by Müller, Koonin, Seki, and van Kolck (2000) and currently underway by Seki and van Kolck. The method differs from Lüscher's finite-volume approach (1986) by focusing on finite lattice spacing effects in the large-volume limit rather than finite volume effects in the continuum limit.
