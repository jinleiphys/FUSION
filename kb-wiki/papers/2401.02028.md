# Pseudospin Symmetry: Microscopic origin of the ground-state inversion in neutron-rich odd-A Cu isotopes

> The role of the pseudospin symmetry in the inversion of the ground-state spin in Cu isotopes is investigated within the covariant density functional theory (CDFT). The density functional PC-PK1 gives a good agreement with the observed ground-state properties of the odd-A $^{69-79}$Cu. In particular, the inversion of the ground-state spin at $A=75$ is reproduced nicely, which can be explained by the level crossing between the proton pseudospin partners $π2p_{3/2}$ and $π1f_{5/2}$. It is found that the pseudospin symmetry plays a dominant role in the evolution of the energy splittings between the pseudospin partners. By comparing with the results of the nonrelativistic DFTs, our studies suggest that the pseudospin symmetry is essential to the shell evolution around this region.

## Key claim
The ground-state spin inversion in neutron-rich odd-A Cu isotopes at A=75 can be reproduced by the covariant density functional PC-PK1 without explicitly including the tensor force, suggesting that pseudospin symmetry, rather than tensor-force effects alone, plays a dominant role in the level crossing between the proton pseudospin partners π2p₃/₂ and π1f₅/₂.

## Method
The authors employed the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) method within the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) using the point-coupling functional PC-PK1, solved in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with 30 major shells. A separable pairing force was used in the particle-particle channel, and blocking effects were considered for the unpaired proton in odd-A Cu isotopes. The contribution of the pseudospin-orbit potential to single-particle energies was extracted by taking its expectation value on the lower component of the Dirac spinor from the Schrödinger-like equation derived from the radial Dirac equation.

## Key numbers
- The ground-state spin inversion occurs at A=75, where the ground state changes from 3/2⁻ in ⁷³Cu to 5/2⁻ in ⁷⁵Cu.
- The root-mean-square deviation of binding energies from experiment for PC-PK1 is 0.52 MeV (compared to 2.43 MeV for DD-ME2 and 0.74 MeV for SEI).
- The energy splitting between the pseudospin partners π2p₃/₂ and π1f₅/₂ at ⁶⁹Cu is about 1.11 MeV, comparable to the experimental excited energy of 1.23 MeV for the 5/2⁻ state.
- From ⁶⁹Cu to ⁷⁹Cu, the pseudospin-orbit potential V_PSO reduces the energy splitting by 4.69 MeV, while the mean-field potential Σ₊ reduces it by 0.96 MeV.
- The total energy splitting between the pseudospin partners decreases continuously from about 1.11 MeV at ⁶⁹Cu to -1.44 MeV at ⁷⁹Cu.

## Context
This work extends studies of shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei around ⁷⁸Ni, where the doubly magic property was discovered (Xu 2014, Olivier 2017, Welker 2017, Sahin 2017, Taniuchi 2019). Previous shell-model calculations attributed the lowering of the 5/2⁻ state in Cu isotopes to the tensor force (Franchoo 1998, Otsuka 2005, Sieja 2010), while nonrelativistic DFT studies showed the level crossing could be reproduced without tensor force using a simple effective interaction (Routray 2021, Bano 2022). The present work demonstrates that covariant density functional theory naturally incorporates pseudospin symmetry and can explain the ground-state inversion without explicit tensor force, suggesting pseudospin symmetry is essential for shell evolution in this region. Future beyond-mean-field calculations and precise experiments on 7/2⁻ states are needed to distinguish between pseudospin and tensor-force mechanisms.
