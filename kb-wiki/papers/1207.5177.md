# Momentum imbalance of isolated photon-tagged jet production at RHIC and LHC

> In collisions of ultra-relativistic nuclei, photon-tagged jets provide a unique opportunity to compare jet production and modification due to parton shower formation and propagation in strongly-interacting matter at vastly different center-of-mass energies. We present first results for the cross sections of jets tagged by an isolated photon to ${\cal O}(α_{\rm em} α_s^2)$ in central Au+Au reactions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at RHIC and central Pb+Pb reactions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at LHC. We evaluate the increase in the transverse momentum imbalance of the observed $γ$+jet state, induced by the dissipation of the parton shower energy due to strong final-state interactions. Theoretical predictions to help interpret recent and upcoming experimental data are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents first theoretical predictions for the cross sections of jets tagged by an isolated photon at next-to-leading order in QCD, showing that the transverse momentum imbalance of the photon-jet pair increases due to dissipation of parton shower energy in the quark-gluon plasma, with the effect depending on the coupling strength between the jet and the medium.

## Method

The authors combine perturbative QCD cross sections at O(α_em α_s^2) from the JETPHOX Monte Carlo program with initial-state cold nuclear matter effects and final-state medium-induced parton splitting and collisional energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma that undergoes Bjorken expansion. The medium-modified cross section is computed as a probabilistic superposition of cross sections where the jet has higher initial transverse momentum, using a probability distribution for the fraction of parton energy converted to a medium-induced parton shower and a function f(R) describing the fraction of shower energy redistributed inside the jet cone. The momentum imbalance distribution is obtained by changing variables from (p_Tγ, p_Tjet) to z_Jγ = p_Tjet / p_Tγ and integrating over p_Tjet.

## Key numbers

- For p+p collisions at LHC (√s_NN = 2.76 TeV): ⟨z_Jγ⟩ = 0.94
- For p+p collisions at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV): ⟨z_Jγ⟩ = 0.90
- For central Pb+Pb at LHC with g_med = 2.0 (radiative + collisional energy loss): ⟨z_Jγ⟩ = 0.80
- For central Au+Au at RHIC with g_med = 2.0 (radiative + collisional energy loss): ⟨z_Jγ⟩ = 0.74
- For central Pb+Pb at LHC with g_med = 2.2: ⟨z_Jγ⟩ = 0.71
- For central Au+Au at RHIC with g_med = 2.2: ⟨z_Jγ⟩ = 0.70
- CMS measured ⟨z_Jγ⟩ = 0.73 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.04 (syst.) for central Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- Photon transverse momentum range for nuclear modification factor: 32.5 < p_Tγ < 37.5 GeV
- Jet reconstruction radius R = 0.3 used for the nuclear modification factor and asymmetry distributions

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Vitev (2008) and applied to inclusive jets (2010) to the more exclusive photon-tagged jet channel, enabling direct comparison between RHIC and LHC energies for the first time. The results are compared to CMS measurements of photon-jet asymmetry in central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (Chatrchyan 2012) and to D0 measurements of isolated photon+jet cross sections in p+pbar collisions at √s = 1.96 TeV (D0 2008). The same range of coupling strengths g_med = 1.8-2.2 was previously used to describe di-jet asymmetry and inclusive jet suppression at LHC (He 2011) and to predict Z0+jet asymmetry in heavy-ion collisions (Neufeld 2012).
