# An effective-field-theory analysis of Efimov physics in heteronuclear mixtures of ultracold atomic gases

> We use an effective-field-theory framework to analyze the Efimov effect in heteronuclear three-body systems consisting of two species of atoms with a large interspecies scattering length. In the leading-order description of this theory, various three-body observables in heteronuclear mixtures can be universally parameterized by one three-body parameter. We present the next-to-leading corrections, which include the effects of the finite interspecies effective range and the finite intraspecies scattering length, to various three-body observables. We show that only one additional three-body parameter is required to render the theory predictive at this order. By including the effective range and intraspecies scattering length corrections, we derive a set of universal relations that connect the different Efimov features near the interspecies Feshbach resonance. Furthermore, we show that these relations can be interpreted in terms of the running of the three-body counterterms that naturally emerge from proper renormalization. Finally, we make predictions for recombination observables of a number of atomic systems that are of experimental interest.

## Key claim

The paper shows that next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections to Efimov physics in heteronuclear three-body systems, arising from finite interspecies effective range and finite intraspecies scattering length, can be systematically incorporated using effective field theory with only one additional three-body parameter beyond the leading-order description, provided the scale hierarchy \(r_0, a_{22} \ll a_{12}\) holds.

## Method

The authors constructed a short-range effective field theory with atomic fields \(\psi_1, \psi_2\) and molecular fields \(d_{12}, d_{22}\), treating the interspecies interaction non-perturbatively and the intraspecies interaction perturbatively. They expanded the interspecies dimer propagator to include the effective range correction at NLO and derived the Skorniakov–Ter-Martirosian equation for the atom-molecule scattering amplitude at LO and NLO, including diagrams with NLO dimer propagators, bare \(d_{22}\) propagators, and NLO three-body forces. The NLO three-body counterterm was shown to require one additional three-body observable for renormalization, and universal relations connecting different Efimov features (recombination minima, atom-molecule resonances, three-atom resonances) were derived in terms of mass-ratio-dependent universal numbers.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^6\)Li-Cs-Cs system (\(\delta = 4.511\times10^{-2}\)), the LO discrete scaling factor is \(\lambda = 4.865\) and the universal parameters are \(\theta_0 = 0.6114\), \(\theta_\ast = 3.388\times10^{-2}\), \(\theta_- = -1.349\).
* For the \(^6\)Li-Cs-Cs system, the NLO universal numbers are \(\sigma = 0.693\), \(J_\ast - J_0 = 0.840\), \(\bar{\sigma} = 0.141\), and \(Y_\ast - Y_0 = 0.680\).
* For the \(^7\)Li-Cs-Cs system (\(\delta = 5.263\times10^{-2}\)), the LO scaling factor is \(\lambda = 5.465\) and the NLO universal numbers are \(\sigma = 0.743\), \(J_\ast - J_0 = 0.828\), \(\bar{\sigma} = 0.204\), and \(Y_\ast - Y_0 = 0.821\).
* Using experimental data for \(^6\)Li-Cs-Cs, the predicted Efimov features are \(a_0^{(2)} = 4838 a_B\), \(a_0^{(3)} = 22074 a_B\), \(a_\ast^{(4)} = 5567 a_B\), and \(a_\ast^{(5)} = 28114 a_B\), deviating from universal relations to \(a_-^{(3)}\) by 11%, 4.4%, 2.3%, and 1.4% respectively.
* The predicted values for the two shallowest three-atom resonances in \(^6\)Li-Cs-Cs are \(a_-^{(0)} = -267 a_B\) and \(a_-^{(1)} = -1667 a_B\), compared to experimental values of \(-350 a_B\) and \(-1777 a_B\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order effective-field-theory analysis of Efimov physics in heteronuclear mixtures by Helfrich et al. (2010) to include finite-range corrections, and parallels the NLO treatment for three identical bosons by Ji et al. (2011). The universal relations derived here enable predictions for recombination features in systems such as Li-Cs and Li-Rb mixtures, and the renormalization-group analysis shows that the NLO corrections can be absorbed into a running Efimov parameter, consistent with the approach of Kievsky et al. (2012) and Ji et al. (2015). The results can be tested against experimental data from the Florence group (Ulmanis et al., 2016) and potential-model calculations by Blume and Yan (2014).
