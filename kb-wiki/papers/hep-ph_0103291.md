# The Transverse Energy as a Barometer of a Saturated Plasma

> The evolution of the gluon plasma produced with saturation initial conditions is calculated via Boltzmann transport theory for nuclear collisions at high energy. The saturation scale increases with the nuclear size and the beam energy, and thus we find that the perturbative rescattering rate decreases relative to the initial longitudinal expansion rate of the plasma. The effective longitudinal pressure remains significantly below the lattice QCD pressure until the plasma cools to near the confinement scale. Therefore, the transverse energy per unit of rapidity and its dependence on beam energy provides a sensitive test of gluon saturation models: the fractional transverse energy loss due to final state interactions is smaller and exhibits a weaker energy dependence than if ideal (nondissipative) hydrodynamics applied throughout the evolution.

## Key claim
The transverse energy per unit rapidity and its beam-energy dependence can serve as a sensitive barometer of gluon saturation in the initial state, because the fractional transverse energy loss due to final-state interactions is smaller and exhibits a weaker energy dependence than would be expected from ideal (nondissipative) hydrodynamics, provided that the saturation scale increases with nuclear size and beam energy.

## Method
The evolution of a gluon plasma produced with saturation initial conditions was calculated via Boltzmann transport theory in the relaxation time approximation for nuclear collisions at high energy. The initial condition assumed a strong correlation between space-time rapidity and momentum-space rapidity, with zero longitudinal pressure at the initial time tau_0 = 1/p_sat. The evolution was followed until the system reached the p/epsilon ratio from lattice QCD, and the final transverse energy was obtained assuming free streaming at epsilon_f = 2 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers
- Saturation scale parametrization: p_sat ≈ 0.208 A^0.128 sqrt(s)^0.191 (p_sat and sqrt(s) in GeV), with C_1 ≡ dE_T/dy / (p_sat dN/dy) ≈ 1.34 A^(-0.007) sqrt(s)^0.021 (EKRT parametrization, beta=1)
- At RHIC (sqrt(s)=200A GeV), the effective pressure reaches the LQCD curve at tau_L ≈ 1-2 fm, when energy density has dropped to epsilon_L = 6.5-12 GeV/fm^3 (for K_in=1-2)
- At LHC (sqrt(s)=5400A GeV), tau_L = 3-7 fm, with epsilon_L = 9.5-21.5 GeV/fm^3 (for K_in=1-2)
- Logarithmic slope kappa = d log E_T^f / d log sqrt(s): kappa = 0.50 for K_in=1, kappa = 0.46 for K_in=2, compared to kappa = 0.40 for isentropic expansion and kappa = 0.59 for the initial EKRT saturated E_T^i
- Preliminary PHENIX result: dE_t/deta ≈ 570 GeV for the 2% most central Au+Au events at sqrt(s)=130A GeV

## Context
This work extends the EKRT saturation model (Kimmo, 1999) by calculating the dynamical evolution of the saturated gluon plasma beyond the initial conditions, using transport theory rather than assuming ideal hydrodynamics. It contrasts with the approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Mueller (2000) based on classical Yang-Mills equations, and with the entropy-conserving expansion assumed in ideal fluid dynamics (Adil and Miklos, 2000). The results show that asymptotic freedom causes the effective longitudinal pressure to remain significantly below the lattice QCD equation of state for an increasingly long time as beam energy increases, enabling the transverse energy to serve as a direct probe of the initial saturation scale rather than being erased by hydrodynamic work.
