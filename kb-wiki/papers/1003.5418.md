# Constraint on nuclear symmetry energy from nuclear charge dependent neutron skin thickness of nuclei

> An alternative way to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy with the slope of the neutron skin thickness of nuclei which shows a linear relation to both the isospin asymmetry and the nuclear charge with a form of Z^{2/3} is proposed. The linear dependence of the neutron skin thickness on the nuclear charge and isospin asymmetry is systematically studied with the data from antiprotonic atom measurement experimentally and the extended Thomas-Fermi approach incorporated the Skyrme energy density functional theoretically. An obviously linear relationship between the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy and the slope of the neutron skin thickness on the isospin asymmetry can be found by adopting 66 Skyrme interactions in the calculation. Combining the available experimental data, the constraint of 16<L<66 MeV on the slope parameter of the symmetry energy is obtained. The Skyrme interactions satisfying the constraint are selected.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy can be constrained to \(16 \lesssim L \lesssim 66\) MeV by using the slope of the neutron skin thickness with respect to isospin asymmetry, rather than by using absolute neutron skin thickness values, thereby reducing systematic uncertainties from the antiprotonic atom measurement method.

## Method

The authors used the semi-classical extended Thomas-Fermi approach up to second order (ETF2) with a Skyrme energy density functional and Coulomb energy density to calculate neutron and proton density distributions for even-even nuclei with charge numbers \(20 \le Z \le 92\). They fitted the calculated neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R_{np}\) for each of 66 Skyrme interactions to the empirical form \(\Delta R_{np} = C_Z Z^{2/3} + C_\delta \delta\), then extracted the linear correlation between the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy and the fitted coefficient \(C_\delta\). The experimental constraint on \(C_\delta\) from 26 antiprotonic atom data points was then used to derive the allowed range for \(L\).

## Key numbers

* The slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy is constrained to \(16 \lesssim L \lesssim 66\) MeV, obtained from the experimental range \(C_\delta = (1.05 \pm 0.08)\) fm.
* The empirical expression for neutron skin thickness from antiprotonic atom data is \(\Delta R_{np} = (-0.0041 \pm 0.0027) Z^{2/3} + (1.05 \pm 0.08) \delta\) (in fm).
* The uncertainty in \(C_\delta\) is reduced from \(\pm 0.15\) fm in the earlier \(\delta\)-only fit to \(\pm 0.08\) fm when the \(Z^{2/3}\) charge dependence is included.
* The slope of \(\Delta R_{np}\) versus \(\delta\) for individual elements (Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, Yb, Pb, U) is about 1.10 fm per unit \(\delta\) in the range \(0 \le \delta \le 0.24\).
* The symmetry energy coefficients at saturation density for the selected Skyrme interactions range from 28 MeV to 32 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Jastrzebski et al. (2004), who extracted a linear relation \(\Delta R_{np} = (-0.03 \pm 0.02) + (0.90 \pm 0.15) \delta\) from antiprotonic atom data, by adding a nuclear charge dependent term proportional to \(Z^{2/3}\). The constraint on \(L\) is consistent with that obtained from double neutron-proton ratios and isospin diffusion in heavy ion collisions reported by Tsang et al. (2009). The method enables more precise future constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy as more neutron skin thickness data from antiprotonic atom measurements become available.
