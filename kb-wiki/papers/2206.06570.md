# Relations for low-energy coupling constants in baryon chiral perturbation theory derived from the chiral quark model

> The quark model symmetry can be adopted to establish relations between the low-energy constants (LECs) in the baryon chiral perturbation theory ($χPT$) if one assumes that a baryon-baryon-meson coupling is described equivalently by a quark-quark-meson coupling at the quark level. Through the correspondence between the $SU(2)$ description and the $SU(3)$ description for the same coupling vertex at the quark level, we find some relations between the LECs in $SU(2)_{χPT}$ and $SU(3)_{χPT}$ up to the third chiral order. The $SU(3)_{χPT}$ LEC relations at the same order are also obtained. The numerical analysis roughly supports these relations. In the situation that the available experimental data are not enough, one may employ such constraints to reduce the number of LECs.

## Key claim

The authors derive relations between low-energy constants (LECs) in baryon chiral perturbation theory by assuming that a baryon-baryon-meson coupling can be equivalently described by a quark-quark-meson coupling at the quark level, using the chiral quark model as a bridge. The resulting relations are approximate and intended to reduce the number of unknown LECs when experimental data are insufficient, rather than providing exact predictions.

## Method

The authors collect the complete chiral-invariant Lagrangians for baryon chiral perturbation theory up to the third chiral order in both SU(2) and SU(3) flavor sectors, and construct corresponding Lagrangians at the quark level in the chiral quark model. They perform a nonrelativistic reduction of the covariant interaction terms, then match matrix elements calculated at the hadron level (using baryon wave functions) with those at the quark level (using quark wave functions and the spectator approximation), thereby obtaining relations between the LECs of the two descriptions.

## Key numbers

* Leading order: \(g_A = \frac{5}{3} g_A^q\) for the SU(2) case, where \(g_A\) is the SU(2) chiral perturbation theory coupling and \(g_A^q\) is the quark-level coupling.
* Leading order: \(D = g_A^q\) and \(F = \frac{2}{3} g_A^q\) for the SU(3) case, where \(D\) and \(F\) are the SU(3) chiral perturbation theory LECs.
* At second chiral order in SU(2), the relations include \(\alpha_6 = \beta_6\) and \(\alpha_7 = 3\beta_7\) when the traceless field \(\tilde{\chi}_+\) is adopted (with \(\alpha_i\) and \(\beta_i\) denoting SU(2) chiral perturbation theory and SU(2) chiral quark model LECs, respectively).
* At third chiral order in SU(2), 21 relations are obtained between the \(\alpha_i\) and \(\beta_i\) LECs across 16 groups of interaction structures.

## Context

This work extends the well-known leading-order relation \(D+F = g_A\) (which connects SU(2) and SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory) to higher chiral orders by incorporating the chiral quark model of Manohar (1983) and baryon wave functions. It provides a systematic method to reduce the number of unknown LECs in high-order baryon chiral perturbation theory, complementing other approaches such as resonance saturation (Ecker, 1988) and large \(N_c\) methods (t Hooft, 1973; Witten, 1979). The derived relations enable more constrained phenomenological analyses of hyperon-nucleon interactions and other processes where experimental data are limited.
