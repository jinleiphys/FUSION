# Coulomb breakup in a Transformed Harmonic Oscillator Basis

> The problem of Coulomb breakup in the scattering of a two-body loosely bound projectile by a heavy target is addressed. A basis of transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) wave functions is used to discretize the projectile continuum and to diagonalize the Hamiltonian of the two-body system. Results for the reaction $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni at subcoulomb energies are presented. Comparison of different observables with those obtained with the standard Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method shows good agreement between both approaches.

## Key claim

The Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis method, previously restricted to continuum states with the same angular momentum as the ground state, can be generalized to arbitrary partial waves and applied to Coulomb breakup reactions, producing results in good agreement with the standard Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method for the reaction $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni at subcoulomb energies.

## Method

The authors generate a discrete representation of the projectile continuum by applying a local scale transformation (LST) that converts the numerically-obtained ground state wave function of the $^{8}$B system (modelled as proton+$^{7}$Be) into a harmonic oscillator ground state. The same LST is then applied to higher harmonic oscillator states to produce the THO basis for arbitrary partial waves ($\ell=0$ and $\ell=1$), which is diagonalized to obtain pseudostates representing the continuum. These pseudostates are used in coupled-channels calculations (CDCC-THO) for the $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni reaction at 25.8 MeV, and the results are compared with standard CDCC calculations using energy bins.

## Key numbers

- Reaction energy: 25.8 MeV for $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni at subcoulomb energies.
- Maximum excitation energy for continuum states: $\epsilon_{\max}=10$ MeV, with states above this value removed from coupled equations.
- Maximum projectile-target orbital angular momentum: $L_{\max}=500$, with integration radius $R_{\max}=500$ fm.
- Standard CDCC calculation used $N=16$ bins for $\ell=1$ and $N=32$ bins for $\ell=0$, with bin wave functions calculated up to 100 fm.
- Convergence of THO method for breakup S-matrix at $J=150$ (corresponding to a scattering angle of $10^\circ$) achieved with $N_p=N_s=10$ states; converged calculation used $N_p=N_s=20$ states.
- For the $N=60$ basis, the number of continuum states actually included in coupled-channels calculations after energy cuts was $N_\mathrm{coup}=27$ for both $s$ and $p$ waves.

## Context

This work extends the THO method previously developed by the same group (Moro 2002, Perez-Bernal 2001) for pure $s$-wave deuteron breakup without Coulomb effects, to arbitrary partial waves and Coulomb-dominated reactions. The method is validated against the standard CDCC approach of Rawitscher (1972) and later developers, and against previous CDCC analyses of the same $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni reaction by Nunes (1999) and Tostevin (2001), as well as CDCC with a Gaussian basis by Egami (2004). The approach enables treatment of continuum effects in reactions with loosely bound projectiles without the binning arbitrariness of standard CDCC, and the authors note a recent extension to three-body continuum systems (Manoli 2005) is underway.
