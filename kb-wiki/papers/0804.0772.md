# On a redundancy in the parity-violating 2-nucleon contact Lagrangian

> It is shown that the number of independent operators in the nucleon-nucleon parity-violating contact Lagrangian at the leading order in the low-momentum expansion, can be reduced to five, by using Fierz rearrangements of the nucleon fields.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the number of independent operators in the leading-order parity-violating two-nucleon contact Lagrangian can be reduced from six to five by applying Fierz rearrangements of the nucleon fields, but notes that this reduction has no practical consequences at the present stage of phenomenological analyses since only five combinations of low-energy constants are relevant at low energies anyway.

## Method
The author starts from the relativistic parity-violating four-nucleon contact Lagrangian and uses Fierz identities to rearrange both the flavor (isospin) and spinor structures of the operators. By combining these rearrangements with the field equations of motion and then performing a non-relativistic reduction, the number of independent operators is reduced from the previously identified set of six down to five, yielding a minimal non-relativistic contact Lagrangian.

## Key numbers
- The number of independent operators in the parity-violating two-nucleon contact Lagrangian at leading order is reduced to **5** (from 6).
- The minimal Lagrangian contains **5** coupling constants: \(C_1\), \(\tilde C_1\), \(C_2\), \(\tilde C_5\), and \(C_6\).
- The Lagrangian is expressed with an overall factor of \(1/\Lambda_\chi^3\), where \(\Lambda_\chi\) is the chiral symmetry breaking scale.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory description of nuclear parity violation developed by Holstein (2005) and Musolf (2005), who had previously identified a set of 11 non-relativistic operators (reducible to 10 in the potential) based on 12 relativistic operators. The paper also builds on the DDH meson-exchange model of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980). The result enables a one-to-one correspondence between physical observables and low-energy constants in the chiral effective theory, which is important for interpreting experiments such as NPDGamma at LANSCE and neutron spin rotation on \(^4\)He at NIST.
