# Thermodynamic consistency for nuclear matter calculations

> We investigate the relation between the binding energy and the Fermi energy and between different expressions for the pressure in cold nuclear matter. For a self-consistent calculation based on a $Φ$ derivable $T-$matrix approximation with off-shell propagators the thermodynamic relations are well satisfied unlike for a $G-$matrix or a $T-$matrix approach using quasi-particle propagators in the ladder diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a self-consistent, \(\Phi\)-derivable \(T\)-matrix approximation using off-shell propagators satisfies the Hugenholz-Van Hove theorem (equality of Fermi energy and binding energy per particle at saturation) and thermodynamic relations for pressure, whereas the standard \(G\)-matrix approximation and a quasi-particle \(T\)-matrix approach violate these relations, with the quasi-particle \(T\)-matrix being oversensitive to pairing singularities.

## Method

The authors compare four approximations for cold nuclear matter using a separable rank-two Mongan interaction in the \(S\) wave: a Hartree-Fock approximation, a Brueckner \(G\)-matrix resummation of particle-particle ladders, a quasi-particle \(T\)-matrix including both particle-particle and hole-hole propagation with on-shell propagators, and a fully self-consistent \(T\)-matrix approximation using off-shell propagators and full spectral functions. For each approximation, they compute the binding energy per particle, Fermi energy, and pressure as functions of density, and test the thermodynamic relations \(P = \rho^2 \partial(E/N)/\partial \rho\) and \(P = \rho (E_F - E/N)\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for the self-consistent \(T\)-matrix: \(\rho_s / \rho_0 = 1.39\) (where \(\rho_0\) is the reference density).
- Fermi energy at saturation for the self-consistent \(T\)-matrix: \(E_F = -9.9\) MeV.
- Binding energy per particle at saturation for the self-consistent \(T\)-matrix: \(E/N = -9.9\) MeV.
- Compression modulus for the self-consistent \(T\)-matrix: \(\mathcal{K} = 103\) MeV.
- For the \(G\)-matrix approximation, the difference between \(E_F\) and \(E/N\) at saturation is \(10.7\) MeV, with \(E_F = -21.6\) MeV and \(E/N = -10.9\) MeV.
- For the quasi-particle \(T\)-matrix, saturation density \(\rho_s / \rho_0 = 1.08\), \(E_F = -18.9\) MeV, \(E/N = -7.0\) MeV.
- The jump in the fermion density at the Fermi momentum in the self-consistent \(T\)-matrix is approximately \(0.7\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of thermodynamic consistency in nuclear matter calculations, following the formal framework of Baym (1962) on \(\Phi\)-derivable and conserving approximations. It directly addresses the violation of the Hugenholz-Van Hove theorem in the \(G\)-matrix approach, previously noted by authors such as Grangé and collaborators (1980s) and Jong and collaborators (1990s), and shows that including hole-hole lines in a quasi-particle \(T\)-matrix reduces but does not eliminate the violation. The results enable the use of the self-consistent \(T\)-matrix as a thermodynamically consistent framework for calculating nuclear matter properties, particularly the equation of state and compression modulus, without the unphysical Fermi energies that arise in non-conserving approximations.
