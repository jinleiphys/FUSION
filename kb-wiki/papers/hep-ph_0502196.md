# Solar mass-varying neutrino oscillations

> We propose that the solar neutrino deficit may be due to oscillations of mass-varying neutrinos (MaVaNs). This scenario elucidates solar neutrino data beautifully while remaining comfortably compatible with atmospheric neutrino and K2K data and with reactor antineutrino data at short and long baselines (from CHOOZ and KamLAND). We find that the survival probability of solar MaVaNs is independent of how the suppression of neutrino mass caused by the acceleron-matter couplings varies with density. Measurements of MeV and lower energy solar neutrinos will provide a rigorous test of the idea.

## Key claim

The solar neutrino deficit may be explained by oscillations of mass-varying neutrinos (MaVaNs), where the survival probability is independent of how the suppression of neutrino mass caused by acceleron-matter couplings varies with density, as long as propagation is adiabatic.

## Method

The authors introduce a model where neutrino masses vary due to Yukawa couplings to a light scalar field (the acceleron) that also couples to electrons and nucleons. They parameterize the matter dependence of the neutrino mass shift as $M_i(r)=\mu_i (n_e(r)/n_e^0)^k$, where $k$ describes the dependence on electron density, and solve the two-neutrino evolution equations for solar neutrinos. They then compare the predicted survival probabilities to extracted solar neutrino data and check consistency with KamLAND, CHOOZ, K2K, and atmospheric neutrino data.

## Key numbers

- The background neutrino mass-squared difference is $\delta m^2_s = 8\times 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$ for solar oscillations and $|\delta m^2_a| = 0.0021$ eV$^2$ for atmospheric oscillations.
- The chosen MaVaN parameters are $\mu_2 = 0.0077$ eV, $\mu_3 = i\,0.0022$ eV, $m_2 = 0.0089$ eV, and mixing angle $\theta = 0.62$ radians.
- The electron number density at the solar core for $pp$ neutrinos is $n_e^0 \sim 60 N_A/$cm$^3 \sim 10^{11}$ eV$^3$.
- The matter term $A^0$ at neutrino production points is about $10^{-4}$ eV$^2$ for $^8$B neutrinos ($E_\nu \sim 7$ MeV), $10^{-5}$ eV$^2$ for $^7$Be neutrinos ($E_\nu \sim 0.9$ MeV), and $10^{-6}$ eV$^2$ for $pp$ neutrinos ($E_\nu \sim 0.3$ MeV).
- The difference between MaVaN and standard oscillation probabilities at KamLAND, $|dP(\bar{\nu}_e \to \bar{\nu}_e)|$, does not exceed 0.005 for $k > 1/2$.
- For atmospheric neutrinos passing through the earth's core, $|dP(\nu_\mu \to \nu_\mu)|$ is smaller than about 0.005 for intermediate energies.

## Context

This work extends the MaVaN framework proposed by earlier authors (Newman et al., 2004; Peccei, 2004) to solar neutrino oscillations, which had not been studied previously. The scenario improves agreement with solar neutrino data compared to the standard Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution, which is somewhat discrepant with solar data while favored by KamLAND. The results enable future tests using MeV and lower energy solar neutrinos, and the scheme can be embedded in comprehensive models addressing the LSND anomaly as discussed by Barger et al. (2004).
