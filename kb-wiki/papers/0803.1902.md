# Phase diagram in the imaginary chemical potential region and extended Z3 symmetry

> Phase transitions in the imaginary chemical potential region are studied by the Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model that possesses the extended Z3 symmetry. The extended Z3 invariant quantities such as the partition function, the chiral condensate and the modifed Polyakov loop have the Roberge-Weiss (RW) periodicity. There appear four types of phase transitions; deconfinement, chiral, Polykov-loop RW and chiral RW transitions. The orders of the chiral and deconfinement transitions depend on the presence or absence of current quark mass, but those of the Polykov-loop RW and chiral RW transitions do not. The scalar-type eightquark interaction newly added in the model makes the chiral transition line shift to the vicinity of the deconfiment transition line.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, which possesses both chiral symmetry and extended Z3 symmetry, can reproduce all lattice QCD results in the imaginary chemical potential region, including the Roberge-Weiss (RW) periodicity and the four types of phase transitions, but only after adding a scalar-type eight-quark interaction that shifts the chiral transition line to the vicinity of the deconfinement transition line.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor PNJL model under the mean field approximation, solving the stationary conditions for the chiral condensate, the Polyakov loop, and its complex conjugate to find the global minimum of the thermodynamic potential. They introduced an imaginary chemical potential μ = iθ/β and analyzed the thermodynamic potential as a function of θ and temperature T, examining the effects of a finite current quark mass and a newly added scalar-type eight-quark interaction. The extended Z3 symmetry was used to derive the RW periodicity and the symmetry properties of the modified Polyakov loop Ψ = Φ exp(iθ).

## Key numbers

- The Polyakov potential parameters used are a0 = 6.75, a1 = -1.95, a2 = 2.625, a3 = -7.44, b3 = 0.75, and b4 = 7.5, all dimensionless, fitted to lattice QCD data in the pure gauge theory.
- In the previous paper (Sakai 2008), T0 was taken as 270 MeV from pure gauge lattice QCD, but this value yields a transition temperature at zero density larger than that from full lattice QCD simulations.
- The realistic current quark mass is stated as 5 to 10 MeV, which is much lighter than the masses used in the lattice simulations referenced.
- The RW periodicity period is 2π/3 for the partition function, chiral condensate, and modified Polyakov loop, with the Polyakov-loop RW transition occurring at θ = (2k+1)π/3.

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Sakai (2008) in the chiral limit by adding finite current quark mass and scalar-type eight-quark interactions to the PNJL model. The PNJL model combines the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) for chiral symmetry with the Polyakov loop for confinement, and the authors compare their results with lattice QCD simulations by Forcrand and Philipsen (2002), Elia and Lombardo (2003), and others. The study enables reliable extrapolation of lattice results from the imaginary chemical potential region to the real chemical potential region using the PNJL model, and it tests the validity of the PNJL model against first-principle lattice calculations.
