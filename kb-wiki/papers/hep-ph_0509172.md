# Extensive study of phase diagram for charge neutral homogeneous quark matter affected by dynamical chiral condensation -- unified picture for thermal unpairing transitions from weak to strong coupling --

> We study the phase structures of charge neutral quark matter under the beta-equilibrium for a wide range of the quark-quark coupling strength within a four-Fermion model. A comprehensive and unified picture for the phase transitions from weak to strong coupling is presented. We first develop a technique to deal with the gap equation and neutrality constraints without recourse to numerical derivatives, and show that the off-diagonal color densities automatically vanish with the standard assumption for the diquark condensates. The following are shown by the numerical analyses: (i) The thermally-robustest pairing phase is the two-flavor pairing (2SC) in any coupling case, while the second one for relatively low density is the up-quark pairing (uSC) phase or the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase depending on the coupling strength and the value of strange quark mass. (ii) If the diquark coupling strength is large enough, the phase diagram is much simplified and is free from the instability problems associated with imaginary Meissner masses in the gapless phases. (iii) The interplay between the chiral and diquark dynamics may bring a non-trivial first order transition even in the pairing phases at high density. We confirm (i) also by using the Ginzburg-Landau analysis expanding the pair susceptibilities up to quartic order in the strange quark mass. We obtain the analytic expression for the doubly critical density where the two lines for the second order phase transitions merge, and below which the down-quark pairing (dSC) phase is taken over by the uSC phase. Also we study how the phase transitions from fully gapped states to partially ungapped states are smeared at finite temperature by introducing the order parameters for these transitions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a unified picture of thermal unpairing transitions in charge-neutral quark matter across a wide range of diquark coupling strengths, showing that the two-flavor pairing (2SC) phase is the thermally most robust phase in all coupling cases, while the identity of the second-most robust phase depends on coupling strength and strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors use a four-Fermion (NJL-type) model with scalar quark-antiquark and scalar diquark interactions, imposing beta-equilibrium and local electric and color charge neutrality conditions. They develop an analytical technique to express the gap equations and neutrality constraints in terms of quasi-quark wave functions (eigenvectors of the Nambu-Gor'kov Hamiltonian), avoiding numerical derivatives. The thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to diquark gap parameters, constituent quark masses, and charge chemical potentials for five values of the diquark coupling strength, parameterized by the zero-temperature CFL gap at \(\mu=500\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Key numbers

* The strange quark current mass is set to \(m_s = 80\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the cutoff to \(\Lambda = 800\ \mathrm{MeV}\); the scalar coupling \(G_S\) is chosen so that the dynamical quark mass at \(\mu=0\) is \(400\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Five diquark coupling strengths are studied, corresponding to \(\Delta_0 = 25,\ 80,\ 125,\ 160,\ 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (the zero-temperature CFL gap at \(\mu=500\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in the chiral SU(3) limit), with \(G_D/G_S\) ratios of 0.42, 0.63, 0.78, 0.88, and 1.00 respectively.
* The dimensionless parameters used are \(m_{u,d}/\Lambda = 0\), \(m_s/\Lambda = 0.1\), and \(G_S \Lambda^2 / (8 N_c) = 2.17\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color-superconducting quark matter under neutrality constraints (Iida 2003, Fukushima 2004, Ruster 2005, Blaschke 2005) by systematically varying the diquark coupling strength and including dynamical chiral condensation. It resolves apparent contradictions between earlier claims about the dSC phase and provides a unified framework connecting weak-coupling results (where gapless phases appear) to strong-coupling results (where the phase diagram simplifies and Meissner instabilities are avoided). The analysis enables future studies of the interplay between chiral and diquark dynamics and the nature of thermal transitions from fully gapped to partially ungapped states.
