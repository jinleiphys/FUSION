# Modified MIT bag Models -- part I: Thermodynamic consistency, stability windows and symmetry group

> In this work we study different variations of the MIT bag model. We start with the so called non-ideal bag model and discuss it in detail. Then we implement a vector interaction in the MIT bag model that simulates a meson exchange interaction and fix the quark-meson coupling constants via symmetry group theory. At the end we propose an original model, inspired by the Boguta-Bodmer models, which allows us to control the repulsion interaction at high densities. For each version of the model we obtain a stability window as predicted by the Bodmer-Witten conjecture and discuss its thermodynamic consistency.

# Modified MIT Bag Models – Part I: Thermodynamic Consistency, Stability Windows and Symmetry Group

## Key claim

The authors propose that introducing a vector interaction with coupling constants fixed by symmetry group theory, and optionally adding a quartic self-interaction term inspired by Boguta-Bodmer models, can produce thermodynamically consistent MIT bag models that satisfy the Bodmer-Witten conjecture while also yielding quark star masses above 2.4 solar masses, but only if the vector field mass term is included (which earlier works omitted).

## Method

The authors start from the original MIT bag model and the non-ideal bag model (with an adimensional parameter a4 = 1 - 2αs/π), then introduce a vector field Vμ analogous to the ω meson with a mass term, coupling quarks via constants gqqV. They fix the relative coupling strengths using symmetry group theory, obtaining gssV = (2/5) guuV = (2/5) gddV. For each model variant, they compute the equation of state for beta-stable strange quark matter with leptons, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations, and determine the stability window (range of bag constant B1/4 where two-flavor matter is unstable but three-flavor strange matter is stable).

## Key numbers

- For the original MIT bag model: stability window B1/4 from 148 MeV to 159 MeV (with mu = md = 4 MeV, ms = 95 MeV)
- For the non-ideal bag model with c = 0.3: stability window B1/4 from 135 MeV to 145 MeV; maximum strange star mass 2.21 M⊙ at minimum B1/4
- For the vector MIT bag model with GV = 0.3 fm2 and XV = 1.0: maximum strange star mass 2.61 M⊙ at minimum B1/4 (139 MeV)
- For the vector MIT bag model with GV = 0.3 fm2 and XV = 0.4: maximum strange star mass 2.41 M⊙ at minimum B1/4 (139 MeV)
- For the original MIT bag model: maximum strange star mass 1.85 M⊙ at minimum B1/4 (148 MeV)
- Canonical star (1.4 M⊙) radius for non-ideal bag model with c = 0.3: 11.89 km at minimum B1/4
- Strangeness fraction at center for vector MIT bag model with GV = 0.3 fm2 and XV = 0.4: 0.402 at minimum B1/4

## Context

This work extends the MIT bag model (MITL, MIT_original) by adding vector interactions previously explored by Ro1, Ro2, Ro3, Klan1, Klan2, and weiwei, but corrects those earlier studies by including the vector field mass term that was missing. The non-ideal bag model (MR1, MR2) is shown to require a renormalized Fermi-Dirac distribution for thermodynamic consistency. The self-interacting vector field approach is inspired by Boguta-Bodmer non-linear terms in quantum hadrodynamics (Boguta, Bodmer). The results enable comparison with the massive pulsar MSP J0740+6620 (Cromartie) at 2.14 M⊙ and the GW170817 constraint on canonical star radius below 11.9 km (Capano 2020).
