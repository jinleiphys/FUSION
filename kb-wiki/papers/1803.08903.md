# Centrality and transverse momentum dependence of dihadron correlations in a hydrodynamic model

> In this work, we study the centrality as well as transverse momentum dependence of the dihadron correlation for Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV. The numerical simulations are carried out by using a hydrodynamical code NeXSPheRIO, where the initial conditions are obtained from a Regge-Gribov based microscopic model, NeXuS. In our calculations, the centrality windows are evaluated regarding multiplicity. The final correlations are obtained by the background subtraction via ZYAM methods, where higher harmonics are also considered explicitly. The correlations are evaluated for the 0 - 20\%, 20\%-40\% and 60\%-92\% centrality windows. Also, the transverse momentum dependence of the dihadron correlations is investigated. The obtained results are compared with experimental data. It is observed that the centrality dependence of the "ridge" and "double shoulder" structures is in consistency with the data. Based on specific set of parameters employed in the present study, it is found that different ZYAM subtraction schemes might lead to different features in the resultant correlations.

## Key claim

The centrality and transverse momentum dependence of dihadron correlations in Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV can be reasonably described by a hydrodynamic framework without explicitly modeling jet-medium interactions, but the specific features of the resultant correlations depend on which ZYAM subtraction scheme (including higher harmonics or not) is employed.

## Method

The authors used the NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic code, where initial conditions are generated by the NeXuS event generator based on Regge-Gribov field theory. Dihadron azimuthal correlations were computed for three centrality windows (0-20%, 20-40%, 60-92%) defined by multiplicity, with trigger particles in the range 2 < pTtrig < 3 GeV/c and associated particles in three intervals (0.4-1, 1-2, 2-3 GeV/c). The flow background was subtracted using the ZYAM method, considering either only elliptic flow v2 or including triangular flow v3 and quartic flow v4.

## Key numbers

- Centrality windows studied: 0-20%, 20-40%, and 60-92% for Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV
- Trigger particle transverse momentum range: 2 < pTtrig < 3 GeV/c; associated particle ranges: 0.4 < pTass < 1, 1 < pTass < 2, and 2 < pTass < 3 GeV/c
- For the mid-central window (20-40%), the correlation for 2 < pTass < 3 GeV/c is approximately 9 times smaller than for 0.4 < pTass < 1 GeV/c
- Elliptic flow v2 values for the mid-central window: 0.0729 (0.4-1.0 GeV), 0.1543 (1.0-2.0 GeV), 0.2466 (2.0-3.0 GeV)
- Triangular flow v3 values for the mid-central window: 0.0212 (0.4-1.0 GeV), 0.0600 (1.0-2.0 GeV), 0.1156 (2.0-3.0 GeV)
- Average number of tubes in initial conditions decreases from 3.37 (0-10% centrality) to 1.20 (80-92% centrality)
- Average energy density of tubes decreases from 31.02 (0-10%) to 10.85 (80-92%) in arbitrary units

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies using NeXSPheRIO that attributed the double-peak structure in away-side correlations to local fluid deflection by hot-spots generating the third harmonic coefficient v3 (Castilho and Qian, 2017). The results are compared with experimental data from the PHENIX collaboration. The study enables further investigation of how different background subtraction methods (including the Reaction Plane Fit method proposed recently) affect the interpretation of dihadron correlations, particularly whether the double-peak structure is a genuine signal or an artifact of the subtraction procedure.
