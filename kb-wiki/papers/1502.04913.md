# Core excitation in three-body nuclear reactions: Improved nucleon-core potential

> Three-body nuclear reactions in two-nucleon plus core systems are described in the framework of exact scattering equations including the core excitation. A nucleon-core optical potential is constructed that can be easily adjusted to the reference potential and thereby to the experimental two-body data, if available. This constitutes an important improvement over the simple deformation of the potential used previously that violated the original fit to the data. Predictions for elastic, inelastic, and transfer reactions involving $^{10}{Be}$ and $^{24}{Mg}$ nuclear cores are obtained. The new optical potential leads to a moderate increase of cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for constructing a nucleon-core optical potential that includes core excitation and, when used in exact two-body coupled-channel equations, exactly reproduces the standard optical potential results for elastic scattering and the DWBA results for inelastic scattering, thereby avoiding the double counting and loss of fit to experimental data that occurred with the previously used simple deformation of the potential.

## Method

The author constructs a nucleon-core potential coupling the ground and excited states of the core by subtracting from the original single-channel potential the explicit core excitation contributions generated in the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation. This "subtraction method" ensures that at a chosen energy the elastic transition matrix matches that from a standard optical potential without core excitation, and the inelastic transition matrix matches the DWBA amplitude. The resulting potentials are then used as input to exact three-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) scattering equations solved in momentum-space partial-wave framework, including the proton-core Coulomb force via screening and renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Core excitation energies: 3.368 MeV for \(^{10}\mathrm{Be}\) and 1.369 MeV for \(^{24}\mathrm{Mg}\).
- Quadrupole deformation parameters: \(\beta_2 = 0.67\) and \(\delta_2 = 1.664\) fm for \(N\)-\(^{10}\mathrm{Be}\); \(\beta_2 = 0.5\) and \(\delta_2 = 1.69\) fm for \(N\)-\(^{24}\mathrm{Mg}\).
- For \(^{10}\mathrm{Be}(p,p')^{10}\mathrm{Be}^*\) at \(E_p = 39\) MeV, the difference between the improved potential (CX) and the simple deformation (CX no subtr) reaches nearly 40% at forward angles.
- Spectroscopic factors for \(^{10}\mathrm{Be}(d,p)^{11}\mathrm{Be}\) transfer: 0.855 for the \(^{11}\mathrm{Be}\) ground state and 0.786 for the excited state.
- Deuteron energies studied: 21.4 MeV and 40.3 MeV for \(^{10}\mathrm{Be}(d,p)^{11}\mathrm{Be}\); 70 MeV for \(d+^{24}\mathrm{Mg}\) scattering.
- Proton energies studied: 39 MeV for \(p+^{10}\mathrm{Be}\); 35.3 MeV for \(p+^{11}\mathrm{Be}\).

## Context

This work extends the exact three-body scattering framework including core excitation developed by Deltuva (2013), which used a simple deformation of the nucleon-core potential that violated the original fit to elastic and inelastic two-body data. The new subtraction method, analogous to a technique used by Hajduk (1983) for the two-nucleon potential with \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation, restores consistency with experimental data. The improved potentials enable more reliable evaluation of core excitation effects in reactions such as \((d,p)\), \((p,d)\), and \((d,d')\) on \(^{10}\mathrm{Be}\) and \(^{24}\mathrm{Mg}\) cores, and the author notes that the \(^{10}\mathrm{Be}(d,p)^{11}\mathrm{Be}^*\) reaction remains an unresolved problem requiring a more sophisticated model.
