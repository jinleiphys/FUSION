# Quantum Fluctuation Effects on Nuclear Fragment Formation

> Multifragmentation in Au+Au collisions is investigated at incident energies in the range 100-400 MeV per nucleon by means of a recently developed quantal Langevin model. The inclusion of quantum fluctuations enhances the average multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments, especially in central collisions. This is mainly because the excitation energies of fragments are reduced due to the quantal behavior of intrinsic specific heat.

## Key claim

Including quantum fluctuations via a quantal Langevin force in Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) enhances the average multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) in Au+Au collisions, primarily because the excitation energies of primary fragments are reduced by the quantal behavior of the intrinsic specific heat; however, the experimental data are not yet reproduced quantitatively, and the improvement is qualitative rather than exact.

## Method

The authors embedded a recently developed quantal Langevin model into the QMD framework, using Gogny interactions and a Pauli potential to ensure reasonable nuclear sizes and bindings. The quantal Langevin force introduces a stochastic term that accounts for the energy dispersion within each many-body wavepacket, and the drift coefficient is reduced by a factor α relative to the classical Einstein relation. The center-of-mass motion of clusters was removed from the energy fluctuation, and a thermal distortion (cooling) procedure was applied to the wavepackets. The resulting primary fragments were then subjected to a statistical decay model (SDM) using the evaporation model of Pühlhofer to obtain final bound fragment yields.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions were studied at incident energies of 100, 250, and 400 MeV per nucleon.
- The effective level spacing is defined as D = σ_E² / E*, where σ_E² is the energy variance and E* is the excitation energy relative to the ground state.
- The level density parameter is a ≈ A/(8 MeV), giving a typical quantal behavior E* = a T² at low temperature.
- The classical gas relation is E*/A = 3T/2, and the two curves intersect at T ≈ 12 MeV.
- The maximum specific heat is obtained for temperature T ∼ 4 MeV.
- The quantal Langevin model agrees well with grandcanonical calculations when T ≥ 4 MeV.
- The mass distribution changes appearance in the temperature region T = 4–6 MeV.
- The maximum impact parameter was assumed to be 10 fm for scaling experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the quantal Langevin model (Ohnishi and Randrup, 1995, 1996) from simple soluble examples to actual nuclear collisions, and addresses a consistent shortcoming of QMD-like models (QMD, AMD, FMD) where primary fragments are too excited to survive statistical decay. The approach is similar in spirit to the work of Ono and Horiuchi (AMD-V), who included additional fluctuations and constrained wave function distortion in Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics. The results enable future quantitative assessment of quantum effects in multifragmentation once ambiguities in the mobility tensor and residual two-body collisions are resolved.
