# The pion-nucleon scattering lengths from pionic deuterium

> We use the framework of effective field theories to discuss the determination of the S-wave πN scattering lengths from the recent high-precision measurements of pionic deuterium observables. The theoretical analysis proceeds in several steps. Initially, the precise value of the pion-deuteron scattering length a_{πd} is extracted from the data. Next, a_{πd} is related to the S-wave πN scattering lengths a_+ and a_-. We discuss the use of this information for constraining the values of these scattering lengths in the full analysis, which also includes the input from the pionic hydrogen energy shift and width measurements, and throughly investigate the accuracy limits for this procedure. In this paper, we also give a detailed comparison to other effective field theory approaches, as well as with the earlier work on the subject, carried out within the potential model and multiple scattering framework.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the pion-deuteron scattering length can be systematically related to the S-wave pion-nucleon scattering lengths using a tower of effective field theories, but the theoretical uncertainty in extracting the isoscalar scattering length \(a_+\) remains large because the isoscalar scattering length is much smaller than the isovector one, requiring very high precision.

## Method

The authors construct a heavy-pion effective field theory (HP EFT) in which pion creation and annihilation processes are excluded from explicit Feynman diagrams and absorbed into low-energy constants, enabling analytic calculation of the pion-deuteron scattering length in terms of pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon threshold parameters. They match this HP EFT to chiral perturbation theory in the Weinberg scheme to reduce the uncertainty from unknown low-energy constants, and they also re-derive the electromagnetic corrections to the pionic deuterium energy shift at next-to-leading order in isospin breaking to validate the extraction of the scattering length from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The pion-deuteron scattering length extracted from PSI measurements is \(a_{\pi d} = \bigl( -0.0261\,(\pm 0.0005) + i\, 0.0063\,(\pm 0.0007) \bigr) M_\pi^{-1}\).
- The deuteron binding energy used is \(\epsilon = 2.22457\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The deuteron binding momentum is \(\gamma = \sqrt{\epsilon m} \simeq 45\ \mathrm{MeV}\), giving an expansion parameter \(\gamma / M_\pi \simeq 1/3\).
- The Coulomb binding energy of the pionic deuterium 1s state is \(E_{1s} = \frac{1}{2} \alpha^2 \mu_d \simeq 3.5 \times 10^3\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- The electromagnetic corrections to the \(E_{3p} - E_{1s}\) transition energy are calculated: point nucleus (Klein-Gordon) contributes \(3074.69\ \mathrm{eV}\), nuclear and pion finite size contributes \(-0.52\ \mathrm{eV}\) (or \(-0.53\ \mathrm{eV}\) with updated charge radii), vacuum polarization contributes \(3.72\ \mathrm{eV}\), and relativistic recoil contributes \(-0.02\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- The Coulomb correction to the strong shift formula is of order \(2 \times 10^{-3}\) when using the measured scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of pion-deuteron scattering by resolving a discrepancy between the heavy-pion EFT approach of Beane and collaborators and the Weinberg-scheme chiral perturbation theory calculations of Bernard and collaborators. The authors show that the HP EFT provides a systematic expansion in \(\gamma / M_\pi\) and that matching to chiral perturbation theory can reduce the uncertainty from unknown low-energy constants. The results enable a more reliable extraction of the pion-nucleon scattering lengths from pionic deuterium data, complementing the analysis of pionic hydrogen measurements from the PSI collaboration.
