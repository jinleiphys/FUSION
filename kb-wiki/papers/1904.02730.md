# NJL-type models in the presence of intense magnetic fields: the role of the regularization prescription

> We study the regularization dependence of the Nambu-Jona--Lasinio model (NJL) predictions for some properties of magnetized quark matter at zero temperature (and baryonic density) in the mean field approximation. The model parameter dependence for each regularization procedure is also analyzed in detail. We calculate the average and difference of the quark condensates using different regularization methods and compare with recent lattice results. In this context, the reliability of the different regularization procedures is discussed.

## Key claim

The reliability of NJL model predictions for magnetized quark matter depends critically on whether the regularization scheme separates the finite magnetic-field contribution from the divergent vacuum term (MFIR procedure); when this separation is not performed (nMFIR), smooth form factors can still produce non-physical oscillations and poor agreement with lattice QCD results, especially for magnetic fields above \(eB \sim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The authors computed the average quark condensate \(\Delta\bar\Sigma\) and the condensate difference \(\Sigma^{-}\) for the SU(2) NJL model in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature and baryonic density, using both non-covariant (Lorentzian N=5, Woods-Saxon \(\alpha=0.1\), Gaussian, Fermi-Dirac \(\alpha=0.01\Lambda\), 3D sharp cutoff) and covariant (4D sharp cutoff, proper-time, Pauli-Villars) regularization procedures. For each scheme, they compared results obtained with the magnetic-field-independent regularization (MFIR) method, which isolates the finite magnetic term, against the non-MFIR (nMFIR) approach where magnetic and vacuum divergences are regularized together. Model parameters were fitted to reproduce \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and average quark condensates in the range \(220\ \text{MeV} < -\bar\Phi_0^{1/3} < 260\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* The average condensate \(\Delta\bar\Sigma\) from nMFIR with Lorentzian (N=5) and Woods-Saxon (\(\alpha=0.1\)) form factors agrees with lattice data only up to \(eB \sim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\); beyond this, deviations appear.
* For the 3D sharp cutoff within MFIR, the band for \(-\bar\Phi_0^{1/3}\) from 241 MeV to 260 MeV covers the lattice points for \(\Delta\bar\Sigma\) as a function of \(eB\).
* The 4D sharp cutoff within MFIR yields a \(\Sigma^{-}\) difference more compatible with lattice results than the 3D sharp cutoff, but the average condensate band for \(-\bar\Phi_0^{1/3}\) between 220 MeV and 260 MeV lies somewhat above lattice values.
* The proper-time regularization within MFIR shows agreement with lattice only for small \(eB\) values; the Gaussian form factor (GR) produces no oscillations but compares poorly with lattice data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of NJL model regularization in magnetic fields, particularly the MFIR method developed by Ebert (1999) and Klimenko, which had been applied to avoid oscillations in color-superconducting quark matter (Scoccola, 2016; 2017). The authors directly compare their NJL results with lattice QCD simulations from Bali (2012), which provided benchmark data for the condensate changes \(\bar\Sigma\) and \(\Sigma^{-}\). The findings clarify which regularization prescriptions are reliable for modeling magnetized quark matter and guide future applications of NJL-type models to heavy-ion collision phenomenology and the QCD phase diagram under strong magnetic fields.
