# Probing the sensitivity of the total nucleus-nucleus reaction cross section at intermediate energies to medium effects and isospin asymmetries

> This paper presents reaction cross section predictions. These predictions are the result of a continuous pipeline which originates from a microscopic nuclear interaction. Density parameters and effective nucleon-nucleon cross sections (both involved in the reaction calculations) are by-products of the same equation of state. First, we perform tests of sensitivity to medium effects using reactions involving 208-Pb, a stable but weakly isospin-asymmetric nucleus. We also show predictions for collisions of some neutron-rich isotopes of Calcium and Argon. We observe significant sensitivity of the reaction cross section to medium effects but very weak sensitivity to inclusion of isospin asymmetry in the effective nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key claim

The total nucleus-nucleus reaction cross section at intermediate energies shows significant sensitivity to medium effects on the nucleon-nucleon cross sections, but very weak sensitivity to the inclusion of isospin asymmetry in those effective cross sections, meaning that discriminating between neutron and proton contributions in the medium does not appreciably change the predicted cross section.

## Method

The authors calculate total reaction cross sections within the optical limit of the Glauber model, using a fully consistent pipeline from a single microscopic nuclear interaction (the Bonn B potential within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach). Nuclear densities are obtained from two-parameter Fermi functions whose parameters are determined by energy minimization of a mass formula using the same equation of state, and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections are derived from the same microscopic framework including Pauli blocking and mean-field effects. Sensitivity tests are performed for collisions of stable and neutron-rich nuclei by comparing predictions using free-space versus in-medium cross sections, and by comparing predictions that include or ignore isospin-dependent effects in the densities and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For $^{40}$Ca on $^{208}$Pb, differences between predictions using separate neutron/proton densities versus identical densities scaled by Z/A and N/A factors amount to about 2-3%.
- For $^{40}$Ca on $^{208}$Pb, differences between predictions using two different equations of state (DBHF-based versus UIX with Argonne V18) are of the order of 4-5%.
- The neutron skin predicted for $^{208}$Pb is 0.169 fm from the DBHF-based equation of state, compared to 0.226 fm from the UIX-based equation of state (a 33% increase).
- The largest medium effects on the reaction cross section for $^{40}$Ca on $^{208}$Pb amount to about 6% at an average density of 0.089 fm$^{-3}$ and approximately 8-9% at 0.148 fm$^{-3}$.
- For Calcium isotopes, the predicted neutron skin increases from -0.046 fm (for A=40) to 0.553 fm (for A=64) as neutron number increases.
- The average isospin asymmetry parameter for the Calcium isotopes studied ranges from 0 to 0.375.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic calculations of nuclear matter properties and neutron skins (Sammarruca and White 2010, Sammarruca and Liu 2009) into predictions of reaction cross sections, providing a consistent pipeline from a single nucleon-nucleon potential. The results contrast with phenomenological approaches such as the relativistic Hartree theory of Hirata (1991) and the stochastic mean-field studies of Rizzo (2011), and suggest that reaction cross section measurements with precision at the 4-5% level could help discriminate between different equations of state, particularly for nuclei with very large neutron skins or halos. The work enables future studies where reliable structural information from high-energy reactions can be used to isolate medium effects in lower-energy collisions.
