# Color Transparency Effects in Electron Deuteron Interactions at Intermediate Q^2

> High momentum transfer electrodisintegration of polarized and unpolarized deuterium targets, $d(e,e'p)n$ is studied. We show that the importance of final state interactions-FSI, occuring when a knocked out nucleon interacts with the other nucleon, depends strongly on the momentum of the spectator nucleon. In particular, these FSI occur when the essential contributions to the scattering amplitude arise from internucleon distances $\sim 1.5~fm$. But the absorption of the high momentum $γ^*$ may produce a point like configuration, which evolves with time. In this case, the final state interactions probe the point like configuration at the early stage of its evolution. The result is that significant color transparency effects, which can either enhance or suppress computed cross sections, are predicted to occur for $\sim 4 GeV^2 \ge Q^2\leq~10~(GeV/c)^2$.

## Key claim

Color transparency effects in deuteron electrodisintegration can either enhance or suppress computed cross sections depending on kinematics, and are predicted to occur for momentum transfers in the range of approximately 4 GeV² to 10 (GeV/c)², with the effects depending strongly on the momentum of the spectator nucleon and the internucleon distances (∼1.5 fm) that dominate the final state interactions.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for the d(e,e'p)n reaction using the Glauber approximation derived from Feynman diagrams, including a kinematical factor Δ⁰ that accounts for nucleon recoil usually neglected in conventional nonrelativistic Glauber treatments. They then incorporate color transparency effects using two models: the quantum diffusion model, which introduces a coordinate-dependent rescattering amplitude that accounts for the expansion of the point-like configuration, and the three-state model, which represents the point-like configuration as a superposition of three baryonic states (N, N*, N**) and is solved using both a Green function method and a modified Glauber approximation approach.

## Key numbers

- The essential contributions to the scattering amplitude arise from internucleon distances ∼1.5 fm
- Color transparency effects are predicted for Q² in the range ∼4 GeV² to ∼10 (GeV/c)²
- The spectator neutron momentum range considered is 200-400 MeV/c, where deuteron wave functions have been determined experimentally
- The momentum of the knocked-out proton is larger than 1 GeV/c
- The average transverse size squared ratio ⟨r_t(Q²)²⟩/⟨r_t²⟩ is approximated as ∼1 GeV²/Q² for Q² ≥ 1.5 GeV²
- The formation length parameter l_h = 2p_f/ΔM² with ΔM² = 0.7-1.1 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the strategy proposed in Frankfurt et al. (1994) for using light nuclei to investigate color transparency, moving from helium targets to the deuteron, which is the best understood nuclear system with a wave function determined experimentally in a wide momentum range. The paper addresses the discrepancy between the (p,pp) experiment of Carroll et al., which found indications of color transparency, and the NE18 (e,e'p) experiment, which found no such indications, by proposing new observables using deuteron targets where expansion effects of the point-like configuration are minimal. The calculations differ from those of Arenhovel et al. and Leidemann et al. in the predicted angular dependence at higher spectator momenta, and the authors suggest searching for color transparency by examining ratios of experimentally measured quantities rather than absolute cross sections.
