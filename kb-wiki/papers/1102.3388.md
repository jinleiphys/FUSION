# Produced charged hadrons in central Pb + Pb collisions at LHC energies in the RDM

> The energy dependence of charged-hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is investigated in a nonequilibrium-statistical relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources. Theoretical pseudorapidity distributions are compared with Au + Au data at RHIC energies of sqrt(s_NN) = 0.13 and 0.2 TeV, and computed for Pb + Pb central collisions at LHC energies of 2.76 and 5.52 TeV. The central, nearly equilibrated source arising from gluon-gluon collisions becomes the major origin of particle production at LHC energies. The midrapidity dip is determined by the interplay of the three sources.

## Key claim
The relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources predicts that at LHC energies the midrapidity source from gluon-gluon collisions becomes the dominant origin of charged-hadron production, accounting for about 93% of the midrapidity density, but this result depends on extrapolating transport parameters from RHIC energies using intermediate values between two analytical extrapolation functions.

## Method
The author solves a linear Fokker-Planck equation in rapidity space for three Gaussian sources: two fragmentation sources from valence quark-gluon interactions centered near beam rapidities, and one central source from gluon-gluon collisions at midrapidity. The rapidity distributions are converted to pseudorapidity distributions via a Jacobian that depends on the ratio of average mass to average transverse momentum, using values of 0.6 and 0.7 GeV for mean transverse momentum at LHC energies of 2.76 and 5.52 TeV respectively. The model parameters (interaction time over relaxation time, widths, and particle numbers) are extrapolated from fits to RHIC Au+Au data at 0.13 and 0.2 TeV using logarithmic scaling with center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC energy of 0.13 TeV in 0-6% central Au+Au collisions: midrapidity source generates about 13% of produced particles; experimental midrapidity density is 547 ± 55 (PHOBOS, |η| < 1)
- At RHIC energy of 0.2 TeV in 0-6% central Au+Au collisions: midrapidity source generates about 26% of produced particles; experimental midrapidity density is 645 ± 65 (PHOBOS, |η| < 1)
- At LHC energy of 2.76 TeV in 0-5% central Pb+Pb collisions: predicted midrapidity density is 1601 ± 60 (adjusted to ALICE data, |η| < 0.5); midrapidity source accounts for 93% of midrapidity density; fragmentation sources are centered at <η> ≈ ∓4.09
- At LHC energy of 5.52 TeV in 0-5% central Pb+Pb collisions: predicted midrapidity density is approximately 1860 (extrapolated); fragmentation sources are centered at <η> ≈ ∓4.49
- Time parameter τ_int/τ_y extrapolated to 0.67 at 2.76 TeV and 0.66 at 5.52 TeV
- Widths (FWHM) of fragmentation sources: 4.2 at 2.76 TeV and 4.6 at 5.52 TeV (extrapolated linearly with log(sqrt(s_NN)))
- Widths (FWHM) of midrapidity source: 6.8 at 2.76 TeV and 7.5 at 5.52 TeV (extrapolated linearly with log(sqrt(s_NN)))
- Particle numbers in fragmentation sources: 3660 at 2.76 TeV and 4120 at 5.52 TeV (extrapolated)
- Particle numbers in midrapidity source: 11075 at 2.76 TeV and 14210 at 5.52 TeV (extrapolated)

## Context
This work extends the three-source relativistic diffusion model previously applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies by Wolschin (1999, 2007) and to d+Au collisions by Bialas and Czyz (2005). It provides predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies of 2.76 and 5.52 TeV, adjusting earlier predictions from K. Wolschin (2007) that underestimated the midrapidity density by a factor of 2.7 compared to the ALICE data point at 2.76 TeV. The results enable comparison with upcoming pseudorapidity distributions from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE, and are qualitatively consistent with microscopic net-baryon calculations by Mehtar-Tani and Wolschin (2009, 2010) that find very small valence quark contributions at midrapidity at LHC energies.
