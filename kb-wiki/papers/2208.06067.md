# Dark matter effects on tidal deformabilities and moment of inertia in a hadronic model with short-range correlations

> In this work we study the outcomes related to dimensionless tidal deformability $(Λ)$ obtained through a relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic model including short-range correlations (SRC) and dark matter (DM) content [Phys. Rev. D 105, 023008 (2022)]. As a dark particle candidate, we use the lightest neutralino interacting with nucleons through the Higgs boson exchange. In particular, we test the model against the constraints regarding the observation of gravitational waves from the binary neutron star merger GW170817 event provided by LIGO and Virgo collaboration (LVC). We show that $Λ$ decreases as the dark particle Fermi momentum ($k_F^{DM}$) increases. This feature favors the RMF-SRC-DM model used here to satisfy the limits of $Λ_{1.4}=190^{+390}_{-120}$ ($Λ$ of a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star), and $\tildeΛ=300^{+420}_{-230}$ given by the LVC. We also show that as $k_F^{DM}$ increases, $Λ_1$ and $Λ_2$, namely, tidal deformabilities of the binary system, are also moved to the direction of the GW170817 observational data. Finally, we verify that the inclusion of DM in the system does not destroy the \mbox{$I$-Love} relation (correlation between $Λ$ and dimensionless moment of inertia, $\bar{I}$). The observation data for $\bar{I}_\star\equiv\bar{I}(M_\star)=11.10^{+3.68}_{-2.28}$, with $M_\star=1.338M_\odot$, is attained by the RMF-SRC-DM model.

# Dark matter effects on tidal deformabilities and moment of inertia in a hadronic model with short-range correlations

## Key claim
The RMF-SRC-DM model can satisfy the LVC constraints on tidal deformabilities from GW170817 only when dark matter is included, specifically for a dark particle Fermi momentum of 0.03 GeV, whereas the model without dark matter fails to meet these constraints. The I-Love universal relation between dimensionless tidal deformability and dimensionless moment of inertia is preserved even with dark matter inclusion.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic model with short-range correlations (SRC) and dark matter content, where the lightest neutralino (mass 200 GeV) interacts with nucleons through Higgs boson exchange with coupling strength ξ=0.01 and Higgs-nucleon coupling f=0.3. They solve the TOV equations coupled to the tidal deformability differential equation for neutron stars under β-equilibrium with protons, neutrons, electrons, muons, and dark matter, using the FSU2R parametrization with symmetry energy slope L0 varied in the range (106±37) MeV from PREX-2. The dark matter is implemented with constant Fermi momentum kF^DM values of 0, 0.02, and 0.03 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the RMF-SRC-DM model from Lourenço et al. (2022) that previously showed compatibility with NICER mass-radius data and the 2.14 M⊙ neutron star constraint, by now testing it against GW170817 tidal deformability constraints from LIGO/Virgo. The decreasing trend of Λ with increasing dark matter content agrees with other RMF-DM models without SRC (e.g., those cited as rmfdm3 and eftdm1), while contrasting with models that produce dark matter halos (Nelson et al., Ellis et al., Sagun et al.) where Λ can increase. The preservation of the I-Love relation extends the universality result of Yagi and Yunes (2013) and Landry and Kumar (2018) to systems with dark matter. The results enable future studies combining dark matter effects with neutron star observations from gravitational wave events and NICER.
