# Infrared behaviour of the ghost-gluon vertex in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory

> A semi-perturbative calculation of the ghost-gluon vertex in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory in four and three Euclidean space-time dimensions is presented. Non-perturbative gluon and ghost propagators are employed, which have previously been calculated from a truncated set of Dyson--Schwinger equations and which are in qualitative and quantitative agreement with corresponding lattice results. Our results for the ghost-gluon vertex show only relatively small deviations from the tree-level one in agreement with recent lattice data. In particular, we do not see any sign for a singular behaviour of the ghost-gluon vertex in the infrared.

## Key claim

The ghost-gluon vertex in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory shows only relatively small deviations from its tree-level form for all momenta, and there is no sign of singular infrared behavior, based on a semi-perturbative calculation that uses non-perturbative gluon and ghost propagators as input.

## Method

The authors performed a single iteration of the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the ghost-gluon vertex in four and three Euclidean dimensions, using previously calculated non-perturbative gluon and ghost propagators as input. Two types of input for the vertex in the loop diagrams were employed: the bare tree-level vertex and an ansatz derived from the Slavnov-Taylor identity. The four-point ghost-gluon scattering kernel was neglected in the truncation.

## Key numbers

- In four dimensions, the infrared exponent for the ghost dressing function is κ ≈ 0.595, and for three dimensions κ ≈ 0.398.
- The infrared fixed point of the running coupling is α(0) ≈ 8.92 / N_c.
- The transverse part of the ghost-gluon vertex, 1+A, deviates from tree-level by less than 20% for all momenta in D=4.
- The longitudinal part B is smaller than 0.2 for almost all momenta in D=4.
- The smallest momentum available in the lattice comparison is 366 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to the ghost-gluon vertex, which had previously been assumed bare in calculations of gluon and ghost propagators by vonSmekal (1997) and others. The results confirm the infrared ghost dominance scenario of Zwanziger (2003) and the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement picture, and are consistent with lattice data from Cucchieri (2004). The findings validate the truncation scheme used in earlier propagator studies and support the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion.
