# Neutrino Magnetic Moment Contribution to the Neutrino-Deuteron Reaction

> We study the effect of the neutrino magnetic moment on the neutrino-deuteron breakup reaction, using a method called the standard nuclear physics approach, which has already been well tested for several electroweak processes involving the deuteron.

# Neutrino Magnetic Moment Contribution to the Neutrino-Deuteron Reaction

## Key claim

The neutrino magnetic moment contribution to the neutrino-deuteron breakup cross section is extremely small relative to the standard neutral current contribution, with a ratio of approximately 5 × 10⁻⁶ at 10 MeV neutrino energy, meaning that even if the neutrino magnetic moment exists at current experimental upper limits, it does not affect the interpretation of existing SNO data.

## Method

The authors extended the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA), previously tested for electroweak processes involving the deuteron, to calculate the electromagnetic contribution σ_EM from the neutrino magnetic moment. They used the Argonne V18 potential to generate two-nucleon wave functions, included both impulse approximation and exchange-current terms, and computed the full multipole expansion of the nuclear matrix elements without relying on the effective-range approximation, impulse approximation, or equivalent photon approximation used in prior work.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R = σ_EM/σ_NC reaches approximately 5 × 10⁻⁶ at E_ν = 10 MeV for μ_ν = 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B.
- σ_EM(TNSKM) is about 4 times larger than σ_EM(AB) when comparing to the value read from Fig. 1 of Akhmedov and Berezin.
- The simplified E1 contribution from the present work is larger than the E1 part of Eq. (18) in Akhmedov and Berezin by a factor of 2.
- The flux-weighted cross section ratio <σ_EM(TNSKM)> is approximately equal to <σ_EM(GMM)> but smaller by about 10%.
- Using σ_EM(TNSKM) gives Φ_SNO = Φ_SSM (1 + 5.46 × 10¹⁴ μ̃²), compared to the GMM result Φ_SNO = Φ_SSM (1 + 6.06 × 10¹⁴ μ̃²).
- The current experimental upper limits cited: μ_ν̅_e < 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (MUNU), μ_ν̅_e < 1.3 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (TEXONO), μ_μ < 6.8 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (LSND), μ_τ < 3.9 × 10⁻⁷ μ_B (DONUT), and astrophysical constraints μ_ν_a < (0.3–1) × 10⁻¹¹ μ_B.

## Context

This work extends the SNPA calculations of Nakamura et al. (2001) and Kubodera et al. (1993) for standard model neutrino-deuteron cross sections to include the electromagnetic contribution from a neutrino magnetic moment. It resolves discrepancies between the earlier estimates of Akhmedov and Berezin (1993), who used effective-range and impulse approximations, and Grifols, Masso, and Mohanty (1996), who used the equivalent photon approximation. The results were used as input in a global analysis by Nakahata et al. (2004) that set an upper limit μ_eff < 1.1 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B (90% CL) from Super-Kamiokande, SNO, and KamLAND data.
