# Proton momentum distribution in nuclei beyond Helium-4

> Proton momentum distributions of the $^{12}C$, $^{16}O$, $^{40}Ca$, $^{56}Fe$ and $^{208}$Pb nuclei are calculated by a model using the natural orbital representation and the experimental data for the momentum distribution of the $^{4}He$ nucleus. The model allows realistic momentum distributions to be obtained using only hole-state natural orbitals (or mean-field single-particle wave functions as a good approximation to them). To demonstrate the model two different sets of wave functions were employed and the predictions were compared with the available empirical data and other theoretical results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the high-momentum tail of the proton momentum distribution for any nucleus with A ≥ 4 can be approximated by that of ⁴He, because the particle-state contributions to the momentum distribution are nearly equal for all such nuclei; the model uses this assumption to construct realistic momentum distributions for heavier nuclei using only hole-state natural orbitals (or mean-field single-particle wave functions as a good approximation).

## Method

The authors decompose the proton momentum distribution into hole-state and particle-state contributions using the natural orbital representation. They assume the particle-state contribution is the same for all nuclei with A ≥ 4 and take the experimental proton momentum distribution of ⁴He from y-scaling analysis (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1991) as the correlated part. For each target nucleus (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Fe, ²⁰⁸Pb), they combine this ⁴He correlated part with hole-state contributions calculated from either Hartree-Fock single-particle wave functions (Skyrme forces) or multiharmonic oscillator shell-model wave functions, setting all hole-state occupation numbers to unity.

## Key numbers

- The high-momentum components of n(k) for k > 2 fm⁻¹ are nearly the same for all nuclei with A ≥ 4, including ⁴He, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Fe, and nuclear matter.
- For ¹²C and ⁵⁶Fe, the model results are compared with empirical data from y-scaling analysis (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1991); the multiharmonic oscillator wave functions give better agreement for the central part (k ≤ 0.5 fm⁻¹) than Hartree-Fock wave functions.
- For ¹⁶O, results using multiharmonic oscillator wave functions agree with variational Monte Carlo calculations (Pieper et al., 1992) and local density approximation results (Benhar et al., 1989, 1991, 1994) for k > 0.8 fm⁻¹.
- For ⁴⁰Ca, results agree with local density approximation calculations (Benhar et al., 1991) across the momentum range shown.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, some differences remain between the model and local density approximation results (Benhar et al., 1994) in the middle part of the momentum distribution.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using natural orbitals and correlation effects from nuclear matter (Benhar et al., 1989, 1991, 1994) by instead extracting correlation effects from experimental ⁴He data, which is the lightest nucleus exhibiting the universal high-momentum tail. The method enables practical calculation of momentum distributions for medium and heavy nuclei without full correlation calculations, and the results can be used to compute cross sections for particle scattering on nuclei, similar to the approach in Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1994).
