# Enhanced $ψ^{\prime}$ yield and $ψ^{\prime}/(J/ψ)$ yield ratio as a possible signature of QGP formation in high multiplicity $p+p$ collisions

> Suppression in the yield of quarkonia (heavy quark-antiquark bound states) has been considered one of the important signatures of the formation of the thermalized deconfined partonic matter, also known as the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collision Experiments (RHICE). Traditionally, the in-medium dissociation of quarkonium states has been presented by implicitly assuming an adiabatic approximation, which considers that the heavy quark Hamiltonian changes slowly over time owing to change in the medium. However, in high multiplicity smaller systems, such as in $p+p$ collisions, the early development of transverse flow resulting from the finite transverse size of the locally thermalized medium may cause the quarkonium states to undergo a non-adiabatic evolution. It has been argued that in the presence of such a non-adiabatic evolution, the suppression of heavy quark-antiquark bound state yields may not reliably indicate QGP formation~\cite{Bagchi:2023vfv}. We propose that, rather than concentrating on the suppression of $J/ψ$ yields, the enhancement in the yield ratio of $ψ^{\prime}$ to $J/ψ$ (i.e., $ψ^{\prime} / (J/ψ)$), along with an increase in $ψ^{\prime}$ yield, should be considered as a probe of QGP formation for small systems. Our findings, based on realistic modeling of the time evolution of small systems, suggest that the yield ratio $ψ^{\prime} / (J/ψ)$ and the yield of $ψ^{\prime}$ increase as a function of hydrodynamization temperature incorporating the non-adiabatic transitions in high multiplicity $p+p$ collisions.

## Key claim

The authors propose that in high-multiplicity p+p collisions, where the small transverse size causes rapid temperature evolution and non-adiabatic quarkonium evolution, the yield ratio of ψ′ to J/ψ and the absolute yield of ψ′ both increase with hydrodynamization temperature, unlike in the adiabatic case where the ratio increases only because J/ψ survival drops faster. This simultaneous enhancement is presented as a more reliable signature of QGP formation in small systems than traditional J/ψ suppression.

## Method

The authors model the time evolution of charmonium states using time-dependent perturbation theory with a sudden approximation, where the Hamiltonian changes due to the time-dependent Debye screening mass in the Karsch-Mehr-Satz potential. The temperature evolution is modeled in two stages: a pre-equilibrium phase using a power-law rise of effective temperature from QCD kinetic theory, followed by a hydrodynamic phase using either the analytical (1+1)-dimensional Gubser flow solution or a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulation with the MUSIC code, assuming boost invariance and constant shear viscosity to entropy ratio of 1/4π. The normalized survival probabilities of J/ψ, ψ′, and χc are calculated including regeneration effects from non-adiabatic transitions between states, with initial populations taken as NJ/ψ : Nψ′ : Nχc = 100 : 10 : 30.

## Key numbers

- The difference between dissociation probabilities of J/ψ obtained using adiabatic and non-adiabatic methods can be as large as 36% (Boyd 2019).
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at τHydro = 0.3 fm with initial hydrodynamization temperatures ranging from 1.5 Tc to 4 Tc, where Tc = 155 MeV.
- The transverse size for Gubser flow is rT = 1.5 fm.
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio used is 4πη/s = 1.
- The parameter α = 2 is used to model the rise of effective temperature in the pre-equilibrium stage.
- The evolution stops when energy density drops below 0.26 GeV fm−3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Bagchi 2023) which argued that non-adiabatic evolution in small systems can suppress J/ψ dissociation even above the Debye screening temperature, questioning J/ψ suppression as a QGP signature. It contrasts with the adiabatic treatment used in Singh (2021) and Das (2022), where the yield ratio ψ′/(J/ψ) increases only because J/ψ survival decreases faster than ψ′ survival. The proposed simultaneous enhancement of both ψ′ yield and the ψ′/(J/ψ) ratio enables a new experimental observable for QGP formation in high-multiplicity p+p collisions at LHC energies (√s = 5, 7, and 13 TeV), where elliptic flow data from CMS (2014, 2016) already suggests QGP formation in small systems.
