# Next-to-leading order transverse momentum-weighted Sivers asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering: the role of the three-gluon correlator

> We study the Sivers asymmetry in semi-inclusive hadron production in deep inelastic scattering. We concentrate on the contribution from the photon-gluon fusion channel at ${\cal O}(α_{em}^2 α_s)$, where three-gluon correlation functions play a major role within the twist-3 collinear factorization formalism. We establish the correspondence between such a formalism with three gluon correlation functions and the usual transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization formalism at moderate hadron transverse momenta. We derive the coefficient functions used in the usual TMD evolution formalism related to the quark Sivers function expansion in terms of the three-gluon correlation functions. We further perform the next-to-leading order calculation for the transverse-momentum-weighted spin-dependent differential cross section, and identify the off-diagonal contribution from the three-gluon correlation functions to the QCD collinear evolution of the twist-3 Qiu-Sterman function.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the collinear twist-3 factorization formalism and the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization formalism are consistent for the three-gluon correlation functions at moderate hadron transverse momenta, specifically in the kinematic region where the hadron transverse momentum is much smaller than the hard scale Q but much larger than the QCD scale.

## Method

The authors calculate the contribution of three-gluon correlation functions to the Sivers asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) within the twist-3 collinear factorization formalism, focusing on the photon-gluon fusion channel at order O(alpha_em^2 alpha_s). They then match this result onto the TMD factorization formalism by expanding the quark Sivers function in Fourier b-space in terms of the three-gluon correlation functions, deriving the coefficient functions C_{q<-g}. Finally, they perform a next-to-leading order calculation for the transverse-momentum-weighted spin-dependent differential cross section using dimensional regularization to identify the off-diagonal contribution from three-gluon correlators to the QCD collinear evolution of the twist-3 Qiu-Sterman function.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at order O(alpha_em^2 alpha_s) for the photon-gluon fusion channel
- The matching is demonstrated in the kinematic region Lambda_QCD << p_h_perp << Q, where p_h_perp is the hadron transverse momentum
- The coefficient functions C_{q<-g,1}(hat x) and C_{q<-g,2}(hat x) are derived at order alpha_s/(4 pi), containing terms proportional to ln(c^2/(b^2 mu^2)) and polynomial terms in hat x
- The splitting kernel P_{q<-g}(hat x) = T_R [hat x^2 + (1-hat x)^2] with T_R = 1/2 appears in the evolution equation
- The evolution equation for the Qiu-Sterman function T_{q,F}(x_B, x_B, mu_f^2) involves the three-gluon correlation functions O(x,x), O(x,0), N(x,x), and N(x,0) with the splitting kernel P_{q<-g}

## Context

This work extends the established correspondence between collinear twist-3 and TMD factorization formalisms, previously demonstrated for the quark-gluon correlation function (Qiu-Sterman function) by Ji (2006), Bacchetta (2008), and others, to include three-gluon correlation functions for the first time. The three-gluon correlation functions were originally classified by Ji (1992) and studied in the context of open charm production by Kang (2008) and Beppu (2010). The results enable future phenomenological extractions of the Sivers function and its evolution at next-to-leading order, and provide the coefficient functions needed for TMD evolution formalisms developed by Kang (2011), Aybat (2011), and Echevarria (2012).
