# Isotensor Hadronic Parity Violation

> Weak interactions between quarks give rise to hadronic parity violation which can be observed in nuclear and few-nucleon systems. We study the QCD renormalization of the isotensor component of parity violation at next-to-leading order accuracy. The renormalization group is employed to evolve the interaction down to hadronic scales. As the results are renormalization scheme dependent, we compare various schemes, including 't Hooft-Veltman dimensional regularization, and several regularization independent-momentum subtraction schemes.

## Key claim

The QCD renormalization of the isotensor parity-violating four-quark operator at next-to-leading order produces scheme-dependent Wilson coefficients at hadronic scales; the next-to-leading order corrections are approximately 20% of the leading-order value in the 't Hooft-Veltman scheme at 1 GeV, but the spread across different renormalization schemes is about 10%, indicating the size of higher-order perturbative uncertainties.

## Method

The author computes the one-loop QCD matching of the isotensor parity-violating four-quark operator at the W-boson mass scale, separating contributions from W and Z exchange. The renormalization group equation is then solved at next-to-leading order to evolve the Wilson coefficient from the weak scale down to hadronic scales (below 1 GeV), using two-loop running of the strong coupling and accounting for heavy quark thresholds at the charm and bottom masses. Results are presented in seven different renormalization schemes: 't Hooft-Veltman dimensional regularization, naive dimensional regularization, RI/MOM, and four RI/SMOM variants.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level Wilson coefficient contributions: C_Z^(0) = 1/2 - sin^2 theta_W and C_W^(0) = -1/2 |V_ud|^2
- One-loop matching at mu = M_W: C_W(M_W) = C_W^(0) [1 - 3 alpha_s(M_W)/(4 pi)]; C_Z(M_W) = C_Z^(0) [1 + (4 log(cos theta_W) - 3) alpha_s(M_W)/(4 pi)]
- Leading-order anomalous dimension: gamma_0 = 4; next-to-leading order anomalous dimension: gamma_1 = 419/3 - (76/9) N_f
- Leading-order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.70
- Next-to-leading order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV in 't Hooft-Veltman scheme: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.58
- Next-to-leading order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV in naive dimensional regularization: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.74
- Next-to-leading order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV in RI/MOM scheme: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.77
- Next-to-leading order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV in RI/SMOM(gamma_mu, slash q) scheme: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.67
- Next-to-leading order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV in RI/SMOM(gamma_mu, gamma_mu) scheme: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.75
- Next-to-leading order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV in RI/SMOM(slash q, slash q) scheme: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.73
- Next-to-leading order Wilson coefficient at 1 GeV in RI/SMOM(slash q, gamma_mu) scheme: C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.81

## Context

This work extends the next-to-leading order QCD analysis of the isovector parity-violating channel by Tiburzi (2012) to the isotensor channel, and updates the leading-order result by Kaplan (1992) which gave C(1 GeV)/C^(0) = 0.79 using one-loop running and historical heavy quark masses. The results provide the perturbative Wilson coefficients needed for lattice QCD calculations of isotensor parity violation, which is expected to be statistically cleaner than other channels due to the absence of weak operator self-contractions. The comparison across multiple renormalization schemes (including RI/MOM and RI/SMOM variants) enables non-perturbative lattice calculations by providing matching coefficients between continuum schemes and lattice regularization.
