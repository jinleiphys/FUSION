# A Fourier-Cumulant Analysis for Multiharmonic Flow Fluctuation

> The Fourier analysis of the final particle distribution followed by cumulant study of the Fourier coefficient event-by-event fluctuation is one of the main approaches for testing the collective evolution in the heavy-ion collision. Using a multidimensional generating function, we propose a method to extract any possible cumulant of multiharmonic flow fluctuations and classify them in terms of the order of cumulants and harmonics involved in them. In particular, we show that there are 33 distinct cumulants with orders $2,3,4,5$ and harmonics $2,3,4,5$. We compute the normalized version of these cumulants from hydrodynamic simulation for Pb--Pb collisions based on {\tt T$_\text{R}$ENTo}+{\tt VISH2+1}+{\tt UrQMD}. We compare the simulation with those normalized cumulants that the LHC has measured and predict the unmeasured ones. Comparing the initial and final state fluctuation normalized cumulants, we compute the linear and nonlinear hydrodynamic response couplings. We finally introduce the genuine three-particle correlation function containing information of all third-order cumulants.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a systematic method to extract all possible cumulants of multiharmonic flow fluctuations using a multidimensional generating function, claiming there are 33 distinct cumulants with orders 2 through 5 and harmonics 2 through 5. The hedge is that this classification is limited to cumulants up to fifth order and harmonics up to 5, and the method is demonstrated specifically for Pb-Pb collisions using a particular hydrodynamic simulation chain (T$_\text{R}$ENTo+VISH2+1+UrQMD) with parameters calibrated by a Global Bayesian analysis.

## Method

The author constructs a multidimensional generating function for the probability density function of flow harmonics in Cartesian coordinates, then imposes rotational symmetry from random reaction plane angles to obtain a symmetric characteristic function. By expanding the logarithm of this generating function in a Taylor series in the Fourier coefficients and a Fourier series in the symmetry plane angle differences, all nonvanishing cumulants of the form $c_{n_1,\ldots,n_k}^{\{\alpha_1,\ldots\alpha_{k-1}\}}\{m_1,\ldots,m_k\}$ are extracted symbolically using a Mathematica package. The cumulants are then computed from a Monte Carlo hydrodynamic simulation of Pb-Pb collisions using the T$_\text{R}$ENTo initial condition model, VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner, with parameters calibrated by a Global Bayesian analysis.

## Key numbers

- 33 distinct cumulants exist with orders 2, 3, 4, 5 and harmonics 2, 3, 4, 5
- The cumulant $c_n\{2\}$ for a single harmonic is given by $\langle v_n^2 \rangle$ (Eq. 2 in the text)
- The cumulant $c_n\{4\}$ for a single harmonic is given by $\langle v_n^4 \rangle - 2\langle v_n^2 \rangle^2$ (Eq. 2 in the text)
- The symmetric cumulant SC(k,l) is defined as $c_{k,l}^{\{0\}}\{2,2\} = \langle v_k^2 v_l^2 \rangle - \langle v_k^2 \rangle \langle v_l^2 \rangle$
- The asymmetric cumulant ac$_n\{3\}$ is defined as $c_{n,2n}^{\{2n\}}\{2,1\} = \langle v_n^2 v_{2n} \cos 2n(\psi_n - \psi_{2n}) \rangle$
- The cumulant $c_{2,4}^{\{4\}}\{2,3\}$ is given by $\langle v_2^2 v_4^3 \cos(4(\psi_2 - \psi_4)) \rangle - 2\langle v_4^2 \rangle \langle v_2^2 v_4 \cos(4(\psi_2 - \psi_4)) \rangle$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of flow harmonic cumulants including single harmonic cumulants (Borghini, 2001), symmetric cumulants (Bilandzic, 2013), generalized symmetric cumulants (Mordasini, 2019), and asymmetric cumulants (Jia, 2017) by providing a unified generating function framework that extracts all possible multiharmonic cumulants systematically. The paper also introduces a genuine three-particle correlation function containing information of all third-order cumulants. The method enables future experimental measurements of previously unmeasured cumulants and allows comparison with hydrodynamic simulations to validate the parameters tuned by Global Bayesian analysis.
