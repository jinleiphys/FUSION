# Neutral Meson Photoproduction in $SU_f(3)$ $χ$PT

> We present the results for the electric dipole amplitude for $γN \to π^0 N$ at threshold at the $O(p^2)$ level in SU$_f$(3) chiral perturbation theory. We find that the SU$_f$(3) results differ only slightly from the SU$_f$(2) results. At the $O(p^3)$ level one encounters new, unknown counterterms to fix which one is likely to need the threshold photoproduction data themselves, thus losing predictive power. We suggest, instead, that the {\it difference} between the proton and neutron $π^0$ photoproduction amplitudes may provide a test of the convergence properties of the $χ$PT in the present context. We urge that the neutron's electric dipole amplitude be measured.

# Neutral Meson Photoproduction in SU_f(3) χPT (I): γN → π⁰ N

## Key claim

The SU_f(3) extension of chiral perturbation theory for threshold neutral pion photoproduction yields results that differ only slightly from the SU_f(2) results at O(p²), and contrary to expectations, kaon and pion loops do not cancel significantly. The authors propose that the difference between the proton and neutron electric dipole amplitudes, rather than the individual amplitudes, may provide a testable prediction for the convergence of the chiral expansion, provided that higher-order counterterms do not contribute substantially to this difference.

## Method

The authors computed the electric dipole amplitude E₀⁺ for γN → π⁰N at threshold in SU_f(3) chiral perturbation theory at O(p²), including both tree-level contributions and one-loop graphs. The tree-level contributions were obtained from the relativistic theory using the heavy-baryon expansion, with the anomalous magnetic moment couplings of the nucleon determined from the measured values κ_p = 1.79 and κ_n = -1.91. The one-loop O(p²) contributions were evaluated by summing six nonvanishing loop graphs pairwise, using dimensional regularization and picking up baryon poles, with kaon loops evaluated explicitly alongside the previously known pion loops.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level O(p²) result for the proton: E₀⁺(γp → π⁰p) = (-3.28 + 1.13) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ (from the O(p¹) term and the anomalous magnetic moment term respectively)
- The one-loop O(p²) pion loop contribution to the proton amplitude: +3.01 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- The one-loop O(p²) kaon loop contribution to the proton amplitude: +0.11 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- The total O(p²) result for the proton: E₀⁺(γp → π⁰p) = (-3.28 + 1.13 + 3.01 + 0.11) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹ = approximately +0.97 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹, compared to the experimental value of approximately -1.5 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- The tree-level O(p²) result for the neutron: E₀⁺(γn → π⁰n) = (0 + 0.45) × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- The one-loop O(p²) pion loop contribution to the neutron amplitude: +3.01 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- The one-loop O(p²) kaon loop contribution to the neutron amplitude: -0.07 × 10⁻³ m_π⁻¹
- The predicted difference between proton and neutron amplitudes at O(p²) in SU_f(3): approximately -2.5 × 10⁻³ m_π⁺⁻¹, with the O(p¹) contribution being -3.3 × 10⁻³ m_π⁺⁻¹ and the total O(p²) contribution being +0.8 × 10⁻³ m_π⁺⁻¹
- The coupling constants used: D+F = g_A = 1.26, F = 0.4, D = 0.86, κ_D ≈ 2.87, κ_F ≈ 0.84

## Context

This work extends the SU_f(2) chiral perturbation theory calculations of Bernard, Gasser, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991) for threshold neutral pion photoproduction to SU_f(3), motivated by the observation that in other electromagnetic processes such as magnetic moment calculations by Jenkins, Luke, Manohar, and Savage (1993), kaon and pion loops tend to cancel partially. The authors find that this cancellation does not occur for π⁰ photoproduction, and the SU_f(3) results make the disagreement with experimental data marginally worse than the SU_f(2) results. The paper suggests that measuring the neutron electric dipole amplitude at CEBAF would enable a test of the convergence properties of chiral perturbation theory through the predicted amplitude difference, which unlike the individual amplitudes has a well-behaved perturbative expansion at O(p²).
