# Neutral Meson Photoproduction in $SU_f(3)$ $П‡$PT

> We present the results for the electric dipole amplitude for $ОіN \to ПЂ^0 N$ at threshold at the $O(p^2)$ level in SU$_f$(3) chiral perturbation theory. We find that the SU$_f$(3) results differ only slightly from the SU$_f$(2) results. At the $O(p^3)$ level one encounters new, unknown counterterms to fix which one is likely to need the threshold photoproduction data themselves, thus losing predictive power. We suggest, instead, that the {\it difference} between the proton and neutron $ПЂ^0$ photoproduction amplitudes may provide a test of the convergence properties of the $П‡$PT in the present context. We urge that the neutron's electric dipole amplitude be measured.

## Key claim

The SU_f(3) extension of chiral perturbation theory for threshold neutral pion photoproduction yields results that differ only slightly from the SU_f(2) results at O(pВІ), and contrary to expectations, kaon and pion loops do not cancel significantly. The authors propose that the difference between the proton and neutron electric dipole amplitudes, rather than the individual amplitudes, may provide a testable prediction for the convergence of the chiral expansion, provided that higher-order counterterms do not contribute substantially to this difference.

## Context

This work extends the SU_f(2) chiral perturbation theory calculations of Bernard, Gasser, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991) for threshold neutral pion photoproduction to SU_f(3), motivated by the observation that in other electromagnetic processes such as magnetic moment calculations by Jenkins, Luke, Manohar, and Savage (1993), kaon and pion loops tend to cancel partially. The authors find that this cancellation does not occur for ПЂвЃ° photoproduction, and the SU_f(3) results make the disagreement with experimental data marginally worse than the SU_f(2) results. The paper suggests that measuring the neutron electric dipole amplitude at CEBAF would enable a test of the convergence properties of chiral perturbation theory through the predicted amplitude difference, which unlike the individual amplitudes has a well-behaved perturbative expansion at O(pВІ).
