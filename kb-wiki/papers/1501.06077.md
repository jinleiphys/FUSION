# A New Phase of Matter: Quark-Gluon Plasma Beyond the Hagedorn Critical Temperature

> I retrace the developments from Hagedorn's concept of a limiting temperature for hadronic matter to the discovery and characterization of the quark-gluon plasma as a new state of matter. My recollections begin with the transformation more than 30 years ago of Hagedorn's original concept into its modern interpretation as the "critical" temperature separating the hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma phases of strongly interacting matter. This was followed by the realization that the QCD phase transformation could be studied experimentally in high-energy nuclear collisions. I describe here my personal effort to help develop the strangeness experimental signatures of quark and gluon deconfinement and recall how the experimental program proceeded soon to investigate this idea, at first at the SPS, then at RHIC, and finally at LHC. As it is often the case, the experiment finds more than theory predicts, and I highlight the discovery of the "perfectly" liquid quark-gluon plasma at RHIC. I conclude with an outline of future opportunities, especially the search for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The Hagedorn temperature, originally a limiting temperature for hadronic matter, is reinterpreted as the quasi-critical temperature (approximately 155 MeV) where a smooth cross-over from a hadron resonance gas to a quark-gluon plasma occurs at zero or small net baryon density, rather than a true phase transition singularity.

## Method

The paper traces the historical and conceptual development from Hagedorn's statistical bootstrap model with an exponentially growing hadron mass spectrum to lattice QCD simulations that calculate the equation of state of strongly interacting matter ab initio. It then reviews experimental programs at the SPS, RHIC, and LHC that used strangeness signatures, collective flow analysis, and jet quenching to characterize the quark-gluon plasma, including the beam energy scan at RHIC to search for the QCD critical point.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn temperature empirical range: 150 MeV < T_H < 200 MeV
- The quasi-critical temperature from lattice QCD: T_c approximately 155 MeV, defined by the peak of the chiral susceptibility
- The lowest beam energy in the RHIC beam energy scan: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV
- The top SPS energy in the beam energy scan comparison: sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV
- The top RHIC energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The temperature threshold for the transition: above 155 MeV

## Context

This work extends the original Hagedorn statistical bootstrap model (Hagedorn 1965) and the interpretation by Cabibbo and Parisi (1975) that the Hagedorn singularity signals a phase change to a quark-gluon plasma. It builds on lattice QCD results (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2014) that established the smooth cross-over nature of the transition at low baryon density. The paper enables future searches for the QCD critical point in the phase diagram and studies of the internal structure of the strongly coupled liquid quark-gluon plasma using heavy quarks and jets.
