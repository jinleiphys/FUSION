# Proton charge radius from electron scattering

> The rms-radius $R$ of the proton charge distribution is a fundamental quantity needed for precision physics. This radius, traditionally determined from elastic electron-proton scattering via the slope of the Sachs form factor $G_e(q^2)$ extrapolated to momentum transfer $q^2$=0, shows a large scatter. We discuss the approaches used to analyze the e-p data, partly redo these analyses in order to identify the sources of the discrepancies, and explore alternative parameterizations. The problem lies in the model dependence of the parameterized $G(q)$ needed for the extrapolation. This shape of $G(q<q_{min})$ is closely related to the shape of the charge density $ρ(r)$ at large radii $r$, a quantity which is ignored in most analyses. When using our {\em physics} knowledge about this large-$r$ density together with the information contained in the high-$q$ data, the model dependence of the extrapolation is reduced and different parameterizations of the pre-2010 data yield a consistent value for $R = 0.887 \pm 0.012fm$. This value disagrees with the more precise value $0.8409 \pm 0.0004 fm$ determined from the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

The rms charge radius of the proton extracted from electron scattering data shows a large scatter (0.84 to 0.92 fm) due to model dependence in the parameterized form factor used for extrapolation to q²=0; by incorporating physics knowledge about the charge density at large radii (r > 2.7 fm) together with high-q data, different parameterizations of pre-2010 data yield a consistent value of R = 0.887 ± 0.012 fm, which disagrees with the muonic hydrogen value of 0.8409 ± 0.0004 fm.

## Method

The author reanalyzed existing electron-proton scattering data using parameterizations that respect the physical charge density at large radii, including Sum-Of-Gaussians (SOG) densities and semi-phenomenological Vector Dominance Model (VDM) based fits. The analysis employed the full set of pre-2010 world data (604 data points for q_max < 10 fm⁻¹) and included relativistic corrections via the Licht-Pagnamenta transformation, fitting both charge and magnetic form factors simultaneously to cross section and polarization transfer data.

## Key numbers

- R = 0.887 ± 0.012 fm from pre-2010 electron scattering data using physics-constrained parameterizations
- Muonic hydrogen Lamb shift value: R = 0.8409 ± 0.0004 fm (Pohl 2010, Pohl 2016, Peset 2015)
- To determine R to 1% requires knowledge of G_e(q) to ±0.17% at the sensitivity maximum near q ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹
- The contribution of R to G_e(q) at q ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹ amounts to q²R²/6 ≈ 0.08
- To make higher moment contributions (n ≥ 2) smaller than 1% in R requires restricting q_max ≤ 0.34 fm⁻¹ (0.004 GeV²/c²)
- At q ≈ 0.34 fm⁻¹, the term q²R²/6 ≈ 0.015, requiring G measurement to 0.03% for 1% R precision
- Two-photon exchange correction increases R by approximately 0.01 fm (Sick 1998, Rosenfelder 2000)
- To obtain 98% of R, integration must extend to r = 2.7 fm where density has dropped to ~10⁻⁴ of central value

## Context

This work addresses the "proton radius puzzle" by identifying the source of discrepancies in electron scattering determinations of the proton charge radius. The analysis extends prior work by Sick (2003, 2012, 2014) and Lee et al. (2015) showing that the model dependence arises from ignoring the charge density at large radii, and that fits including data up to the highest momentum transfers (q_max ~ 12 fm⁻¹) produce the most trustworthy R values. The result contradicts the more precise muonic hydrogen value from Pohl (2010) and enables improved understanding of systematic uncertainties in electron scattering analyses, while leaving open the question of whether new physics is needed to resolve the discrepancy.
