# Investigation of $^{31}$P levels near the proton threshold by Nuclear Resonance Fluorescence and the impact on the $^{30}$Si(p,$γ$)$^{31}$P thermonuclear rate

> We investigated the nuclear structure of $^{31}$P near the proton threshold using Nuclear Resonance Fluorescence (NRF) to refine the properties of key resonances in the $^{30}$Si(p,$γ$)$^{31}$P reaction, which is critical for nucleosynthesis in stellar environments. Excitation energies and spin-parities were determined for several states, including two unobserved resonances at $E_r$ $=$ $18.7$~keV and $E_r$ $=$ $50.5$~keV. The angular correlation analysis enabled the first unambiguous determination of the orbital angular momentum transfer for these states. These results provide a significant update to the $^{30}$Si(p,$γ$)$^{31}$P thermonuclear reaction rate, with direct implications for models of nucleosynthesis in globular clusters and other astrophysical sites. The revised rate is substantially lower than previous estimates at temperatures below $200$~MK, affecting predictions for silicon isotopic abundances in stellar environments. Our work demonstrates the power of NRF in constraining nuclear properties, and provides a framework for future studies of low-energy resonances relevant to astrophysical reaction rates.

## Key claim

The authors used Nuclear Resonance Fluorescence (NRF) to determine excitation energies and spin-parities of 31P levels near the proton threshold, including two previously unobserved resonances at center-of-mass energies of 18.7 keV and 50.5 keV, and found that the revised 30Si(p,γ)31P thermonuclear reaction rate is substantially lower than previous estimates at temperatures below 200 MK, but this conclusion depends on the unambiguous determination of orbital angular momentum transfer for these states.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the High-Intensity Gamma-ray Source (HIγS) at TUNL using nearly monoenergetic, linearly polarized photon beams at energies of 7.1, 7.4, 7.6, and 7.9 MeV incident on a black phosphorus powder sample. Scattered gamma rays were detected using six clover-type germanium detectors arranged at specific polar and azimuthal angles, and angular correlation analysis was performed to determine spin-parities by comparing measured analyzing powers with calculated values from the formalism of Iliadis (2021). Excitation energies were determined from summed spectra of vertical detectors using a Bayesian linear calibration based on eight precisely known gamma-ray energies from an enriched 11B sample.

## Key numbers

- Two unobserved resonances were identified at center-of-mass energies Er = 18.7 keV and Er = 50.5 keV in the 30Si(p,γ)31P reaction
- The recommended excitation energy for the 7313.9 keV level (corresponding to the 18.7 keV resonance) is Ex = 7313.88 ± 0.42 keV
- The recommended excitation energy for the 7346 keV level (corresponding to the 50.5 keV resonance) is Ex = 7345.67 ± 0.93 keV
- The revised thermonuclear rate is substantially lower than previous estimates at temperatures below 200 MK
- The incident gamma-ray beam energies were 7.1 MeV, 7.4 MeV, 7.6 MeV, and 7.9 MeV with an energy spread of about 300 keV FWHM
- The black phosphorus sample mass was approximately 4.0 g in a cylindrical container of 2.2 cm diameter and 2.0 cm length
- The boron calibration sample was approximately 1.5 g enriched to 99.81% in 11B
- Eight calibration gamma-ray energies from 11B were used, ranging from 2264.96 ± 0.52 keV to 8916.56 ± 0.11 keV
- The systematic uncertainty in excitation energies was adopted as 0.3 keV based on tests using different calibration methods

## Context

This work extends the direct reaction measurements of Dermigny et al. (2020), who measured 30Si(p,γ)31P resonances down to Er = 422 keV, and the proton transfer study of Harrouz et al. (2022), which provided spectroscopic factors but had limited sensitivity to transferred orbital angular momentum for weakly populated levels. The results address uncertainties in the 30Si(p,γ)31P reaction rate that affect models of globular cluster nucleosynthesis, particularly the unexplained negative Mg-K correlation observed in NGC 2419 by Mucciarelli and collaborators, which requires temperatures between approximately 90 MK and 200 MK according to the self-pollution models of Iliadis et al. and Dermigny and Iliadis. The NRF technique demonstrated here provides a framework for future studies of low-energy resonances relevant to astrophysical reaction rates.
