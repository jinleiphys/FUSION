# Improved limits on lepton-flavor-violating decays of light pseudoscalars via spin-dependent $μ\to e$ conversion in nuclei

> Lepton-flavor-violating decays of light pseudoscalars, $P=π^0,η,η'\toμe$, are stringently suppressed in the Standard Model up to tiny contributions from neutrino oscillations, so that their observation would be a clear indication for physics beyond the Standard Model. However, in effective field theory such decays proceed via axial-vector, pseudoscalar, or gluonic operators, which are, at the same time, probed in spin-dependent $μ\to e$ conversion in nuclei. We derive master formulae that connect both processes in a model-independent way in terms of Wilson coefficients, and study the implications of current $μ\to e$ limits in titanium for the $P\toμe$ decays. We find that these indirect limits surpass direct ones by many orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The authors derive model-independent master formulae connecting lepton-flavor-violating decays of light pseudoscalars ($\pi^0,\eta,\eta'\to\mu e$) to spin-dependent $\mu\to e$ conversion in nuclei, and find that indirect limits from current $\mu\to e$ conversion in titanium surpass direct experimental limits on these pseudoscalar decays by many orders of magnitude, though fine-tuning of Wilson coefficients can relax these limits to some extent, especially for $\eta$ and $\eta'$.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective field theory (EFT) Lagrangian up to dimension 7 containing axial-vector, pseudoscalar, tensor, and gluonic operators that contribute to both $P\to\mu e$ decays and spin-dependent $\mu\to e$ conversion. They derived master formulae expressing both processes in terms of the same Wilson coefficients, incorporating hadronic matrix elements from phenomenological extractions and lattice QCD, nuclear structure factors from shell-model calculations for titanium isotopes using the KB3G interaction, and nucleon matrix elements including two-body current corrections. The limits were obtained by scanning over Wilson coefficient parameter space while accounting for uncertainties in hadronic and nuclear matrix elements, and by considering renormalization group corrections that generate spin-independent contributions.

## Key numbers

- Current experimental limit: $\Br[\mu\to e, \text{Ti}] < 6.1\times 10^{-13}$ (90% confidence level) from SINDRUM-II (Wintz 1998)
- Indirect limit on $\Br[\pi^0\to \mu e]$ assuming single Wilson coefficient dominance: $\lesssim 4\times 10^{-17}$
- Indirect limit on $\Br[\eta\to \mu e]$ assuming single Wilson coefficient dominance: $\lesssim 4\times 10^{-12}$
- Indirect limit on $\Br[\eta'\to \mu e]$ assuming single Wilson coefficient dominance: $\lesssim 5\times 10^{-13}$
- Rigorous limit on $\Br[\pi^0\to \mu e]$ after accounting for possible cancellations: $< 1.2\times 10^{-13}$
- Indirect limits from renormalization group corrections for $\eta$ and $\eta'$: $\Br[\eta\to \mu e] < 3.8\times 10^{-18}$, $\Br[\eta'\to \mu e] < 9.1\times 10^{-20}$
- Current direct experimental limits: $\Br[\pi^0\to \mu^+ e^-] < 3.8\times 10^{-10}$ (Appel 2000), $\Br[\pi^0\to \mu^- e^+] < 3.2\times 10^{-10}$ (NA62 2021), $\Br[\eta\to \mu e] < 6\times 10^{-6}$ (White 1995), $\Br[\eta'\to \mu e] < 4.7\times 10^{-4}$ (CLEO 1999)

## Context

This work extends the effective-field-theory approach to lepton-flavor violation (Crivellin 2013, Cirigliano 2017, Davidson 2018) by providing the first systematic connection between pseudoscalar decays and spin-dependent $\mu\to e$ conversion, including all required hadronic and nuclear matrix elements. The results show that existing $\mu\to e$ conversion limits in titanium already surpass direct searches for $\pi^0,\eta,\eta'\to\mu e$ by many orders of magnitude, and future measurements at Mu2e and COMET using aluminum targets will further improve these indirect constraints while also helping to break degeneracies in the Wilson coefficient parameter space.
