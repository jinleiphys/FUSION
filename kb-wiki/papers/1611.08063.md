# Parity- and Time Reversal-Violating Pion Nucleon Couplings: Higher Order Chiral Matching Relations

> Parity- and time reversal-violating (PVTV) pion-nucleon couplings govern the magnitude of long-range contributions to nucleon and atomic electric dipole moments. When these couplings arise from chiral symmetry-breaking CP-violating operators, such as the QCD $θ$-term or quark chromoelectric dipole moments, one may relate hadronic matrix elements entering the PVTV couplings to nucleon and pion mass shifts by exploiting the corresponding chiral transformation properties at leading order (LO) in the chiral expansion. We compute the higher-order contributions to the lowest order relations arising from chiral loops and next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) operators. We find that for the QCD $θ$-term the higher order contributions are analytic in the quark masses, while for the quark chromoelectric dipole moments and chiral symmetry-breaking four-quark operators, the matching relations also receive non-analytic corrections. Numerical estimates suggest that for the isoscalar PVTV pion-nucleon coupling, the higher order corrections may be as large as $\sim 20\%$, while for the isovector coupling, more substantial corrections are possible.

# Parity- and Time Reversal-Violating Pion Nucleon Couplings: Higher Order Chiral Matching Relations

## Key claim

The tree-level matching relations between parity- and time reversal-violating (PVTV) pion-nucleon couplings and hadron mass shifts receive higher-order corrections from chiral loops and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) operators; for the QCD theta-term these corrections are analytic in the quark masses and preserve the tree-level form, while for quark chromoelectric dipole moments and left-right four-quark operators the matching relations also receive non-analytic corrections, with numerical estimates suggesting the isoscalar PVTV pion-nucleon coupling may receive corrections as large as approximately 20% and the isovector coupling may receive more substantial corrections.

## Method

The authors employ two-flavor Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) in the SU(2)L × SU(2)R representation, using the spurion method to classify how dimension-6 effective operators break chiral symmetry. They compute one-loop chiral corrections to both the PVTV pion-nucleon couplings and the hadron mass shifts (nucleon and pion), then determine whether the tree-level matching relations are preserved or modified by these corrections, including contributions from higher-order low-energy constants (LECs) and explicit Delta-baryon degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- For the QCD theta-term, the tree-level matching for the isoscalar coupling ḡπ^(0) is preserved under one-loop correction (δ_loop^(0) = 0), while the isovector matching arises only at NNLO
- For the chromo-MDM/EDM operators, the one-loop deviation parameter δ_loop^(0) = 0.021 and δ_loop^(1) = -3.1 (evaluated at renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV)
- For the left-right four-quark (LR4Q) operator, the one-loop deviation parameter δ_loop^(0) = -0.12 and δ_loop^(1) = -3.2 (evaluated at μ = 1 GeV)
- The impact of higher-order LECs on the ḡπ^(0)-matching can be as large as 10-20%, while their effect on the ḡπ^(1)-matching is usually not much larger than 1%
- The pion decay constant used is Fπ ≈ 186 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of deVries (2015) on higher-order effects to matching formulae induced by the QCD theta-term in three-flavor ChPT, now covering all effective operators up to dimension 6 that include only first-generation quarks based on two-flavor HBChPT. The matching relations were originally developed by deVries (2012), Mereghetti (2010), Bsaisou (2012, 2014), and deVries (2015) to relate PVTV pion-nucleon couplings to nucleon and pion mass shifts via chiral symmetry. The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to extract PVTV hadronic matrix elements from the study of parity- and time reversal-conserving hadronic matrix elements (mass shifts), with quantified theoretical uncertainties from higher-order chiral corrections.
