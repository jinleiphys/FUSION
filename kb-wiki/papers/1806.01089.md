# Entanglement entropy, entropy production and time evolution in high energy QCD

> Working in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate effective theory of high energy QCD, we revisit the momentum space entanglement entropy of the soft gluons produced in high energy dilute-dense collisions. We extend the work of~\cite{Kovner:2015hga} by considering entropy produced in a single event. This entropy arises due to decoherence of eigenstates with different energies during the time evolution after the collisions with the target. We define it rigorously as the entanglement entropy of the produced system with the experimental apparatus. We compute the time dependent single event entropy in the limit of weak projectile field. Further we compute the entropy for the ensemble of events defined by the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the projectile wave function. Interestingly the entropy of the ensemble has a much weaker time dependence than the entropy in any single event. We attribute this feature to the so called monogamy of entanglement.

## Key claim

The paper defines a rigorous entanglement entropy for soft gluons produced in a single dilute-dense collision event, arising from decoherence of energy eigenstates due to the finite time resolution of the experimental apparatus, rather than from ensemble averaging over valence color charges as in prior work.

## Method

The authors work within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for the projectile wave function. They extend the Hilbert space by introducing an auxiliary "calorimeter" degree of freedom with a Gaussian wave function of width T, which couples to the energy of the soft gluon system. The reduced density matrix of the soft gluons is obtained by tracing over this auxiliary degree of freedom, and the von Neumann entropy is computed using the replica trick in the limit of weak projectile fields (small Δb̃²).

## Key numbers

- The entropy for a single event scales as σ^E ∼ −Δb̃² ln[Δb̃²] to leading logarithmic order in the small parameter Δb̃² (the squared Weizsäcker-Williams field difference after scattering).
- The entropy of the ensemble of events (averaged over the MV model) has a "much weaker time dependence" than the entropy in any single event, attributed to monogamy of entanglement.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kovner (2015) on momentum-space entanglement entropy in the CGC framework, which previously considered only the ensemble-averaged entropy from valence-soft entanglement. The new contribution is the definition of entropy for a single event via decoherence from finite experimental time resolution, using an auxiliary "calorimeter" degree of freedom. This enables future studies of entropy production in high-energy QCD collisions on an event-by-event basis, connecting to the observed thermal-like spectra of soft particles at colliders.
