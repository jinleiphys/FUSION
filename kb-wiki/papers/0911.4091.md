# Proton charge and magnetic rms radii from the elastic $ep$ scattering data

> The elastic electron-proton scattering data are analysed in order to determine proton charge and magnetic rms radii, r_E and r_M. Along with the usual statistical error, we try to estimate a systematic error in the radii, caused by the inadequacy of particular form factor parameterization employed. The range of data to use in the analysis is chosen so as to minimize the total (statistical + systematic) error. We obtain r_E = 0.912 +- 0.009 (stat) +- 0.007 (syst) fm, and r_M = 0.876 +- 0.010 (stat) +- 0.016 (syst) fm. The cross-section data were corrected for two-photon exchange. We found that without such corrections obtained r_E and r_M are somewhat smaller while the quality of fit is worse.

# Proton charge and magnetic rms radii from the elastic $ep$ scattering data

## Key claim
The proton charge and magnetic rms radii are extracted from elastic electron-proton scattering data with a new error estimation that separates statistical error from a systematic error caused by the inadequacy of the chosen form factor parameterization, and the data range is selected to minimize the total (statistical plus systematic) error. The results depend on including two-photon exchange corrections, without which the extracted radii are somewhat smaller and the fit quality is worse.

## Method
The author fits a polynomial parameterization of the electric and magnetic form factors (with a dipole prefactor) directly to experimental cross-section data from multiple experiments, treating each experiment's normalization as a free fit parameter. The systematic error from the choice of parameterization is estimated by generating pseudo-data from several realistic form factor models, fitting them with the chosen parameterization, and taking the maximal deviation as the systematic uncertainty. The optimal upper bound on momentum transfer $Q^2_{\rm max}$ and polynomial degree $n$ are chosen separately for the electric and magnetic radii to minimize the total error.

## Key numbers
- Electric radius: $r_E = 0.912 \pm 0.009\ {\rm (stat)} \pm 0.007\ {\rm (syst)}\ {\rm fm}$ (from fit with $n=3$, $Q^2_{\rm max}=0.30\ {\rm GeV}^2$)
- Magnetic radius: $r_M = 0.876 \pm 0.010\ {\rm (stat)} \pm 0.016\ {\rm (syst)}\ {\rm fm}$ (from fit with $n=4$, $Q^2_{\rm max}=0.98\ {\rm GeV}^2$)
- Without two-photon exchange corrections, $r_E$ decreases by approximately $0.01\ {\rm fm}$ and $r_M$ by approximately $0.03\ {\rm fm}$ (e.g., $r_E = 0.904\ {\rm fm}$ for $n=3$, $Q^2_{\rm max}=0.30\ {\rm GeV}^2$)
- The optimal $Q^2_{\rm max}$ values range from $0.10$ to $0.98\ {\rm GeV}^2$ depending on polynomial degree $n$ and whether fitting $r_E$ or $r_M$
- The electric radius value yields a predicted Lamb shift of $1\,057\,850(2)(4)\ {\rm kHz}$, consistent with the measured value $1\,057\,857.6(2.1)\ {\rm kHz}$

## Context
This work extends the approach of Sick (2003), who used a continued-fraction parameterization and treated the scatter from different fit choices as part of the statistical error; Borisyuk instead separates this scatter as a systematic error and selects the fit type to minimize total error. The results are compared to dispersion analysis by Mergell et al. and Belushkin et al., as well as to the Coulomb-correction analysis by Rosenfelder. The paper enables improved extraction of proton radii from low-$Q^2$ elastic scattering data and demonstrates that two-photon exchange corrections increase the extracted radii, contrary to earlier expectations from the author's own prior work.
