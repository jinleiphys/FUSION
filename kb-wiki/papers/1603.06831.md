# New experiments demand for a more precise analysis of continuum spectrum in 6He: technical details and formalism

> A simple three-body model of 6He is extended to include sd-continuum states in the picture in addition to the already investigated p-states. The role of different continuum components in the weakly bound nucleus 6He is studied by coupling unbound spd-waves of 5He by using simple pairing contact-delta interaction. The main focus of this paper is to outline the procedure that allows the calculation of different configurations of 6He including continuum states and to set up basic ingredients for computations. The method and results discussed here will be used for the calculation of monopole, dipole, quadrupole and octupole response of 6He.

## Key claim

The paper extends a simple three-body model of 6He to include sd-continuum states in addition to previously investigated p-states, but the focus is on outlining the computational procedure and tabulating coefficients for matrix elements rather than presenting final physical results, which will be reported elsewhere.

## Method

The authors generate spd-continuum single-particle states of 5He using a Woods-Saxon potential plus spin-orbit potential for energies from 0.1 to 10.0 MeV on a radial grid from 0.1 to 100.0 fm. These continuum wavefunctions are discretized using the midpoint method with energy spacings of 2.0, 1.0, 0.5, 0.2, and 0.1 MeV, corresponding to basis dimensions of N=5, 10, 20, 50, and 100. Two-particle states of 6He are constructed by tensor coupling two continuum states of 5He, and matrix elements of a pairing contact-delta interaction are calculated, with the pairing strength adjusted to reproduce the bound ground state energy of 6He.

## Key numbers

- Continuum single-particle states of 5He are generated for energies from 0.1 to 10.0 MeV on a radial grid from 0.1 to 100.0 fm
- Energy spacings used for discretization: 2.0, 1.0, 0.5, 0.2, and 0.1 MeV, corresponding to basis dimensions N=5, 10, 20, 50, and 100
- For ground state J=0+ and J=1-, calculation involves 15 matrix blocks (approximately 9 Gb of data); for J=2+ state, 28 matrix blocks; for J=3-, 6 matrix blocks
- The output pairing matrix for the largest ground state case is approximately 21 Gb of data
- Each component of the spd-continuum wavefunctions amounts to 2.2 Mb of data

## Context

This work extends a previous model by Fortunato et al. (2015) that studied the weakly bound ground state and low-lying continuum states of 6He by coupling two unbound p-waves of 5He. The authors have recently expanded the model space to include sd-continuum waves, as reported in Singh et al. (2015a, 2015b). The method and tabulated coefficients will be used for calculating monopole, dipole, quadrupole, and octupole electromagnetic response of 6He, motivated by recent experimental measurements at GANIL and other laboratories.
