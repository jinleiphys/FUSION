# Quenching of the Deuteron in Flight

> We investigate the Lorentz contraction of a deuteron in flight. Our starting point is the Blankenbecler-Sugar projection of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to a 3-dimensional quasi potential equation, wqhich we apply for the deuteron bound in an harmonic oscillator potential (for an analytical result) and by the Bonn NN potential for a more realistic estimate. We find substantial quenching with increasing external momenta and a significant modification of the high momentum spectrum of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The Lorentz contraction of a deuteron in flight leads to a substantial quenching of its spatial extent along the direction of motion, which significantly enhances the high-momentum components of the deuteron wave function; this effect is demonstrated analytically using a harmonic oscillator potential and confirmed qualitatively with the Bonn NN potential, though the quantitative enhancement is less pronounced for the realistic potential.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron and apply the Blankenbecler-Sugar projection to reduce it to a three-dimensional quasi-potential equation. They solve this equation analytically for a harmonic oscillator kernel to obtain the momentum-space wave functions for both the s-state and d-state components, then extend the calculation by applying the same quenching prescription to a deuteron wave function from the Bonn NN potential.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(\varepsilon_d \cong -2.2\) MeV (used to justify neglecting binding energy relative to \(2M\))
- Oscillator parameter for the static deuteron: \(a = 0.7\) fm (used as a typical scale)
- At momenta around \(1\) GeV/\(c\) for a deuteron with longitudinal momentum \(1\) GeV/\(c\), the momentum spectrum in both s-wave and d-wave is enhanced by typically one order of magnitude
- The quenching factor for the z-component in coordinate space is given by \(\lambda_z = (M_d/E_d)^2 a^2\), where \(M_d/E_d = M_d/\sqrt{M_d^2 + P_z^2}\)

## Context

This work extends the standard description of the deuteron as a static object with s and d wave components by incorporating Lorentz contraction effects that become important at momentum transfers around or above 1 GeV/\(c\). The authors note that most calculations avoid this issue by using approximate descriptions such as the Breit frame for the deuteron form factor (Feynman), and they contrast their instant-form approach with kinematical boosts on the light cone (Brodsky). The results are relevant for interpreting experiments at COSY and other facilities studying deuteron beams at high momentum transfers, and the authors call for future systematic studies of pd and dp reactions at COSY with polarized deuteron beams.
