# Heavy Flavor Dynamics in QGP and Hadron Gas

> We study heavy flavor evolution in the quark-gluon plasma matter and the subsequent hadron gas created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The motion of heavy quarks inside the QGP is described using our modified Langevin framework that incorporates both collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms; and the scatterings between heavy mesons and the hadron gas are simulated with the UrQMD model. We find that the hadronic interaction further suppresses the $D$ meson $R_\mathrm{AA}$ at high $p_\mathrm{T}$ and enhances its $v_2$. And our calculations provide good descriptions of experimental data from both RHIC and LHC. In addition, we explore the heavy-flavor-tagged angular correlation functions and find them to be a potential candidate for distinguishing different energy loss mechanisms of heavy quarks inside the QGP.

## Key claim
The authors find that including both collisional and radiative energy loss for heavy quarks in the QGP, plus hadronic interactions in the hadron gas, provides good descriptions of D meson suppression and flow at both RHIC and LHC; however, they note that tuning the transport coefficient allows either collisional or radiative energy loss alone to also describe the single-particle data within current experimental uncertainties, motivating the study of angular correlation functions as a more discriminating observable.

## Method
The authors simulate heavy quark evolution in the QGP using a modified Langevin framework that includes both collisional drag and thermal forces (related by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem) and a recoil force from gluon radiation, with the gluon emission probability calculated from the Higher-Twist energy loss formalism. Heavy quarks hadronize via a hybrid model of fragmentation plus coalescence, and the subsequent scatterings of D mesons with the hadron gas are simulated using the UrQMD model with scattering cross sections for charm mesons with pions and rho mesons. The bulk QGP evolution is modeled with (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics, and heavy quark initial positions and momenta are set by a Monte-Carlo Glauber model and pQCD calculations with CTEQ parton distribution functions modified by EPS09 nuclear shadowing.

## Key numbers
- The gluon transport coefficient hat-q is extracted as around 2.6 GeV^2/fm from fitting D meson R_AA at LHC.
- Additional scatterings of D mesons in an anisotropic hadron gas enhance the v_2 value by over 30%.
- Two different initializations of hydrodynamical evolution cause another 30% difference in the D meson v_2.

## Context
This work extends the Langevin-based heavy flavor transport framework developed by Cao et al. (2013) by adding full hadronic interactions via the UrQMD model (Bass et al., 1998) and by exploring angular correlation functions as a diagnostic tool. It builds on prior theoretical approaches including the Boltzmann-based parton cascade model (Uphoff et al., 2012), the linearized Boltzmann transport (Gossiaux et al., 2010), and the Langevin approach (Cao et al., 2013). The finding that single-particle spectra alone cannot distinguish energy loss mechanisms motivates future experimental measurements of heavy-flavor-tagged angular correlations to discriminate between collisional and radiative energy loss.
