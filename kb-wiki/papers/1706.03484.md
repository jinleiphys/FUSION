# Comment on "Six-body bound system calculations in the case of effective $α-$core structure" [Eur. Phys. J. Plus (2016) 131: 240]

> The authors argue that the calculated $^6$He binding energies by the solution of the coupled Yakubovsky integral equation in a partial wave decomposition reported by E. Ahmadi Pouya and A. A. Rajabi [Eur. Phys. J. Plus (2016) 131: 240] are incorrect. The formalism of the paper has serious mistakes and the numerical results are not reproducible and cannot be validated.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the calculated $^6$He binding energies reported by Ahmadi Pouya and Rajabi (2016) from solving the coupled Yakubovsky integral equation are incorrect, and that the formalism contains serious mistakes making the numerical results not reproducible and unvalidatable.

## Method

The authors identified specific errors in the partial-wave decomposition of the six-body Yakubovsky equations published by Ahmadi Pouya and Rajabi (2016), including incorrect angle variable definitions, missing factors in integral kernels, and wrong permutation operator matrix elements. They then solved the corrected coupled Yakubovsky integral equations using a hyperbolic-linear mapping for Jacobi momentum vectors on the domain [0,5] ∪ [5,10] ∪ [10,50] fm$^{-1}$ with seven Lanczos iterations, testing convergence across different grid sizes for the Yamaguchi I separable potential.

## Key numbers

- With $N_{jac}=10$, $N_{sph}=6$, $N_{pol}=6$ grid points, the corrected calculation gives a six-body binding energy of -118.23550 MeV, not the reported -92.34 MeV.
- With $N_{jac}=10$, $N_{sph}=14$, $N_{pol}=14$ grid points, the corrected calculation gives -115.76635 MeV, while the original paper reported -92.34 MeV.
- With $N_{jac}=14$, $N_{sph}=14$, $N_{pol}=14$ grid points, the corrected calculation shows no convergence, whereas the original paper claimed convergence at -92.34 MeV.
- The largest positive eigenvalue $\eta$ for an input binding energy of -92.34 MeV varies from 0.67553 to 1.28076 depending on grid size, while the original paper reported values of 0.973 and 1.000.

## Context

This comment extends the authors' previous critique of Ahmadi Pouya and Rajabi's work on six-body Yakubovsky equations in a three-dimensional approach (Hadizadeh et al., 2017). The errors identified prevent the formalism from reproducing the established partial-wave representation of Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations given in prior work (references cited as pw-0 and pw-1 in the paper). The comment demonstrates that the published numerical results cannot be validated and that the formalism fails even when simplified to four-body or three-body bound state problems.
