# Relativistic Hydrodynamics: A Singulant Perspective

> There is growing evidence that the hydrodynamic gradient expansion is factorially divergent. We advocate for using Dingle's singulants as a way to gain analytic control over its large-order behaviour for nonlinear flows. Within our approach, singulants can be viewed as new emergent degrees of freedom which reorganise the large-order gradient expansion. We work out the physics of singulants for longitudinal flows, where they obey simple evolution equations which we compute in Müller-Israel-Stewart-like models, holography and kinetic theory. These equations determine the dynamics of the large-order behaviour of the hydrodynamic expansion, which we confirm with explicit numerical calculations. One of our key findings is a duality between singulant dynamics and a certain linear response theory problem. Finally, we discuss the role of singulants in optimal truncation of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion. A by-product of our analysis is a new Müller-Israel-Stewart-like model, where the qualitative behaviour of singulants shares more similarities with holography than models considered hitherto.

## Key claim

The paper advocates that Dingle's singulants, scalar fields that reorganise the factorial divergence of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion, provide analytic control over its large-order behaviour for nonlinear flows, with the singulant dynamics being dual to a specific linear response theory problem.

## Method

The authors study longitudinal flows (one spatial direction singled out, translational invariance in transverse directions) in conformal theories, computing the gradient expansion for the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor up to sufficiently high order to assess its asymptotic behaviour. They derive recursion relations for the expansion coefficients in Müller-Israel-Stewart-like models (BRSSS, HJSW, and a new model), holography (via the dual bulk metric), and kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, then extract singulants from the large-order factorial-over-power form of the coefficients using root-test plots and Borel-plane analysis.

## Key numbers

- In the BRSSS model, the singulant equation of motion is \( D\chi_q(t,x) = 1/\tau_\Pi(T(t,x)) \), where \(\tau_\Pi T = 1/4\) and \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) were used in the numerical example.
- The root-test relation \( |\Pi_\star^{(n)}|^{1/n} \sim n/(e|\chi_d|) \) as \( n\to\infty \) gives the dominant singulant magnitude from the slope of the root-test plot.
- In the HJSW model, the singulant equation is \( D\chi_q = 1/\tau_\Pi \) with \(\tau_\Pi T = 1/2\) and \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\).
- In the new MIS-like model, the singulant equation is \( (D\chi_q)^2 = 1/(\tau_\Pi^2) + c_s^2 (\partial_\perp\chi_q)^2 \) with \(c_s^2 = 1/(d-1)\) and \(\tau_\Pi T = 1/2\), \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\).
- For Bjorken flow in RTA kinetic theory, the dominant singulant is \(\chi = \tau/T\) (where \(\tau\) is proper time) and the gradient expansion coefficients follow \(\Pi_\star^{(n)} \sim A \Gamma(n+\alpha)/\chi^{n+\alpha}\).

## Context

This work extends the growing evidence (Heller 2013, Basar 2015, Aniceto 2015, Denicol 2016, Florkowski 2016, Heller 2016, Buchel 2016, and others) that the hydrodynamic gradient expansion is factorially divergent, by introducing singulants as a systematic tool to control its large-order behaviour for nonlinear flows. The singulant approach connects to and complements the existing paradigms of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion and linear response theory (quasinormal modes), and enables the study of optimal truncation of the gradient expansion. A by-product is a new Müller-Israel-Stewart-like model whose singulant behaviour shares more similarities with holography than previous models.
