# Mesonic cloud contribution to the nucleon and delta masses

> Pion-nucleon elastic scattering in the dominant $P_{33}$ channel is examined in the model in which the interaction is of the form $π+ N\leftrightarrow N, Δ(1232)$. New expressions are found for the elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitude which differ from existing formula both in the kinematics and in the treatment of the renormalization of the nucleon mass and coupling constant. Fitting the model to the phase shifts in the $P_{33}$ channel does not uniquely fix the parameters of the model. The cutoff for the pion-nucleon form factor is found to lie in the range $β= 750\pm350$ MeV/c. The masses of the nucleon and the $Δ$ which would arise if there were no coupling to mesons are found to be $m_{_N}^{(0)}= 1200\pm 200$ MeV and $m_Δ^{(0)} = 1500\pm 200$ MeV. The difference in these bare masses, a quantity which would be accounted for by a residual gluon interaction, is found to be $δm^{(0)}=350\pm 100$ MeV.

## Key claim

The model does not uniquely determine its parameters from fitting the $P_{33}$ pion-nucleon phase shifts; instead, a continuum of solutions exists, with the cutoff for the pion-nucleon form factor lying in the range $\beta = 750 \pm 350$ MeV/c, and the bare masses of the nucleon and $\Delta$ (in the absence of meson coupling) are found to be $m_N^{(0)} = 1200 \pm 200$ MeV and $m_\Delta^{(0)} = 1500 \pm 200$ MeV.

## Method

The author constructs a model of pion-nucleon elastic scattering in the $P_{33}$ channel using an interaction of the form $\pi + N \leftrightarrow N, \Delta(1232)$, with the driving terms being a crossed nucleon pole and a direct $\Delta$ pole. New expressions for the elastic scattering amplitude are derived using a Lippmann-Schwinger equation with invariant phase space and normalizations, incorporating renormalization of the nucleon mass and coupling constant, and including coupling to inelastic channels via an $N/D$ approach. The model is fit to the $P_{33}$ phase shifts from the SAID database using a Gaussian form factor $v(q) = e^{-q^2/\beta^2}$ assumed identical for nucleon and $\Delta$ couplings.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff for the pion-nucleon form factor is found to lie in the range $\beta = 750 \pm 350$ MeV/c.
- The bare nucleon mass (without meson coupling) is $m_N^{(0)} = 1200 \pm 200$ MeV.
- The bare $\Delta$ mass (without meson coupling) is $m_\Delta^{(0)} = 1500 \pm 200$ MeV.
- The difference in these bare masses, attributed to residual gluon interaction, is $\delta m^{(0)} = 350 \pm 100$ MeV.
- The Chew-Low model alone (without $\Delta$ coupling) fits the data with a cutoff $\beta = 2285$ MeV/c, but this is much higher than other data indicate.
- The Lee model alone (without nucleon coupling) fits with $\beta = 400$ MeV/c but cannot simultaneously fit data below and above the $\Delta$ resonance.
- The combined model yields good fits for $\beta$ values of 400, 500, 800, and 1100 MeV/c, with a firm lower limit of 400 MeV/c and an upper limit of 1100 MeV/c based on fits up to $q \approx 500$ MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Chew-Low model (Chew and Low, 1956) and the Lee model (Lee, 1954) by combining both $\pi+N\leftrightarrow N$ and $\pi+N\leftrightarrow \Delta$ couplings in a unified framework, using new expressions for the scattering amplitude that differ from earlier treatments by Cloudy Bag Model (Thomas, 1981) and the matrix $N/D$ approach of Pearce and Jennings (1984). The key advance is a more complete treatment of nucleon mass renormalization that includes the $\Delta$ contribution through the crossed nucleon pole, which was absent in prior models. The results enable future work to extract the valence quark structure of baryons by removing the mesonic cloud contribution, and provide bare masses that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations and constituent quark models such as those of Isgur (1978).
