# Mean Field Effect on J/ψ Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

While the momentum-integrated nuclear modification factor R_AA is not sensitive to the J/ψ mass shift in the quark-gluon plasma, the reduced threshold for J/ψ regeneration due to the mass shift leads to a significant enhancement of R_AA at low transverse momentum at both RHIC and LHC energies, with the mean field force from the inhomogeneous medium being a second-order effect.

## Method

The authors solved a transport equation for J/ψ, ψ', and χ_c distribution functions including both gluon dissociation (loss term) and regeneration (gain term via detailed balance), plus a mean field force term arising from the spatial gradient of the temperature-dependent J/ψ mass. The mass shift was taken from QCD second-order Stark effect calculations, and the QGP evolution was described by 2+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics. The transport equation was solved perturbatively by treating the mean field term as a small perturbation, yielding a rapidly converging series for the J/ψ distribution.

## Key numbers

- At temperature T/T_c = 1.1, the J/ψ mass shift δm_J/ψ can reach -(100-200) MeV.
- At p_t = 0 in central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the total R_AA increases from 0.38 to 0.48 when including the mean field effect, an enhancement of 26%.
- The deviation between first-order and second-order R_AA is less than 3% in all cases, confirming rapid convergence of the perturbative expansion.
- The shadowing effect at LHC energy √s_NN = 2.76 TeV results in a 25% reduction of the J/ψ regeneration.
- The charm quark production cross section used at LHC is σ_cĉ = 0.38 mb.
- The mass-shift-induced enhancement of the p_t-integrated R_AA is less than 10% even in most central collisions.
- The enhanced J/ψ yield at low p_t leads to less than 10% suppression of ⟨p_t²⟩ in most central collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous transport calculations of charmonium production (Yan et al., Liu et al., Zhou et al.) by including the mean field effect from the temperature-dependent J/ψ mass, which had been neglected in almost all prior model calculations. The mass shift input comes from QCD sum rules (Megias et al., Morita et al.) and QCD second-order Stark effect (Lee et al.). The results show that the mean field effect is most visible at low transverse momentum, where current experimental data from PHENIX, STAR, and ALICE still have large uncertainties; more precise data are needed to distinguish this effect.
