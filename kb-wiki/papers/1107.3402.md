# Dilepton production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at top SPS energy within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach

> Dilepton production in In+In collisions at 158 AGeV is studied within the microscopic parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach that incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom, dynamical hadronization as well as the more familiar hadronic dynamics in the final reaction stages. A comparison to the data of the NA60 Collaboration shows that the measured dilepton yield is well described by including the collisional broadening of vector mesons, while simultaneously accounting for the electromagnetic radiation of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) via off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation, quark annihilation with additional gluon Bremsstrahlung and the gluon-Compton scattering mechanisms. In particular, the spectra in the intermediate mass range (1 GeV < M < 2.5 GeV) are dominated by quark-antiquark annihilation in the nonperturbative QGP. Also, the observed softening of the transverse mass spectra at intermediate masses (1 GeV < M < 2.5 GeV) is approximately reproduced. Furthermore, for dileptons of low masses (M < 0.6 GeV), we find a sizable contribution from the quark annihilation with additional gluon bremsstrahlung, thus providing another possible window for probing the properties of the sQGP.

## Key claim

The measured dilepton yield in In+In collisions at 158 AGeV is well described by the PHSD transport approach when including both collisional broadening of vector mesons and electromagnetic radiation from the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), with the intermediate mass range (1 GeV < M < 2.5 GeV) dominated by quark-antiquark annihilation in the nonperturbative QGP rather than by hadronic sources.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom, dynamical hadronization, and hadronic dynamics, to simulate In+In collisions at 158 AGeV. Dilepton production from the partonic phase was calculated using off-shell cross sections for quark-antiquark annihilation, gluon Compton scattering, and quark annihilation with gluon Bremsstrahlung, while hadronic sources included vector meson decays (with in-medium spectral functions for collisional broadening and mass shifts) and multi-meson channels (π+ρ, π+ω, ρ+ρ, π+a1). The results were compared to NA60 Collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- The collisional broadening coefficient for the ρ meson was extracted as α_coll ≈ 150 MeV, and for the ω meson as α_coll ≈ 70 MeV, based on the vector-meson collision rate in In+In reactions at 158 AGeV.
- The in-medium vector meson pole mass scaling parameter α was taken as 0.16 for the ρ meson and 0.12 for the ω meson, using the parametrization M_0*(ρ_N) = M_0 / (1 + α ρ_N/ρ_0) with normal nuclear density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The intermediate mass range for dilepton dominance by quark-antiquark annihilation is specified as 1 GeV ≤ M ≤ 2.5 GeV.
- The low-mass region for sizable contribution from quark annihilation with gluon Bremsstrahlung is M < 0.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies within the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach (Bratkovskaya, 2008), which found a discrepancy between hadronic-only calculations and NA60 data for dilepton masses above 1 GeV, suggesting partonic contributions. The results support the interpretation of Dusling and Zahed as well as Renk and Ruppert that the excess is due to partonic channels, in contrast to the multi-meson (4π) interpretation by Rapp and collaborators. The PHSD approach enables a consistent microscopic treatment of both partonic and hadronic phases, allowing identification of phase-space regions where partonic channels dominate and providing a framework for probing sQGP properties through dilepton observables.
