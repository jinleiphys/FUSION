# Compositeness of near-threshold states in charged hadronic systems

> We quantify the internal structure of near-threshold bound, virtual, and resonance states in systems where Coulomb and short-range interactions coexist by evaluating the compositeness. Using the Coulomb-modified effective range expansion, we derive an expression for the compositeness in terms of the eigenenergy and Coulomb effective range in the weak-binding limit. We then apply the formulation to several near-threshold states in hadronic and nuclear systems, including $pp$, $αα$, $Ω^{-}Ω^{-}$, $Ω_{ccc}^{++}Ω_{ccc}^{++}$, $Ξ^{-}α$, and $Ω^{-}p$.

## Key claim

The paper derives a weak-binding relation for the compositeness (probability of molecular component) of near-threshold states in systems where both Coulomb and short-range interactions act, but the resulting compositeness can exceed unity or become complex, requiring an additional "interpretable compositeness" \(X_C\) defined to always lie between 0 and 1 for probabilistic interpretation.

## Method

The authors employ the Coulomb-modified effective range expansion (ERE) to determine the eigenmomentum \(k_h\) from the pole condition of the scattering amplitude, using the Coulomb scattering length \(a_s^C\), Coulomb effective range \(r_e^C\), and the Coulomb momentum scale \(k_C = \mu \alpha Z_1 Z_2\). They then derive an expression for the compositeness \(X\) in terms of \(r_e^C\) and \(k_h\) in the weak-binding limit, and introduce a modified quantity \(X_C\) for states where \(X\) is complex or exceeds unity, following the prescription of Matuschek (2020) originally developed for short-range interactions.

## Key numbers

* \(pp\) system: virtual state at eigenenergy \(E = -0.14 - 0.47i\) MeV, with interpretable compositeness \(X_C = 0.81\)
* \(\alpha\alpha\) system: resonance (\({}^{8}\)Be) at \(E = 0.083 - 8.3\times 10^{-7}i\) MeV, with \(X_C = 0.71\)
* \(\Omega^{-}\Omega^{-}\) system: bound state at \(E = -0.85\) MeV, with \(X_C = 0.81\)
* \(\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\) system: resonance at \(E = 1.3 - 0.17i\) MeV, with \(X_C = 0.79\)
* \(\Xi^{-}\alpha\) system: bound state (\({}^{5}_{\Xi}\)H) at \(E = -0.22\) MeV, with \(X_C = 1.0\)
* \(\Omega^{-}p\) system: bound state at \(E = -1.07\) MeV, with \(X_C = 0.87\)

## Context

This work extends the compositeness analysis of near-threshold states, previously developed for short-range interactions by Weinberg (1965) and Hyodo (2013, 2014), to systems where the Coulomb interaction is present. It builds on the Coulomb-modified effective range formalism of Higa (2008) and Hammer (2008), and applies the framework to specific systems studied in lattice QCD by Gongyo (2017) for \(\Omega^{-}\Omega^{-}\), Lyu (2021) for \(\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\), and HAL QCD (2018) for \(\Omega^{-}p\), as well as to the \(\alpha\alpha\) system and \(pp\) scattering. The results enable future quantitative characterization of internal structure for near-threshold states in charged hadronic and nuclear systems, including exotic hadrons and hypernuclei.
