# Possible Striking Signals for a Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC

> We believe that one can have serious reservations as to whether heavy ion collisions (e.g. 100 GeV/n Au + 100 GeV/n Au) can lead to Thermal and Chemical equilibrium over large regions (particularly if it is assumed this happens whenever QGP is produced at RHIC-that is if it is produced). It is at present not clear that the collision dynamics and times available will lead to this. An alternate scenario proposed by Van Hove where localized in rapidity bubbles of plasma may well be more probable, and may well occur at least some of the time, and some of the time mainly survive to the final state. If this occurs we have developed a series of event generators to extend and describe these phenomena. A Van Hove type[6,7] spherical bubble at eta=0 is embedded in a resonable event generator in qualitative agreement with Hijing etc[12]. The plasma bubble hadronized at a temperature of 170 Mev according to the model developed by Koch, Muller and Rafelski[21]. The amount of available bubble energy is selected by that in a small central circular cross-section of radius approx 1.3fm or 2.5fm in 100 Gev/n Au+AU, central events The results predict Possible Striking Signals for a QGP. We are also applying these techniques to investigating Kharzeev and Pisarski bubbles of metastable vacua with odd CP.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the standard assumption of large-scale thermal and chemical equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC may be invalid, and propose instead that localized "bubbles" of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in rapidity, as originally suggested by Van Hove, are a more probable scenario; these bubbles, even if they occur only some of the time and with only 4.5% of the available collision energy, could produce striking signals in single events that would be detectable and distinguishable from conventional hadronic backgrounds.

## Method

The authors developed a series of event generators (RHIC::EVENT, RHIC::PLASMA, RHIC::LANDAU, RHIC::SMOKE, RHIC::CHIRAL) to simulate 100 GeV/nucleon Au+Au central collisions at RHIC. RHIC::EVENT generates non-plasma background events using a Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry and ISAJET-based nucleon-nucleon interactions. RHIC::PLASMA embeds spherical Van Hove-type plasma bubbles at midrapidity by tagging particles within a circular cross-section of radius R_tag (1.3 fm or 2.5 fm), converting their energy into a plasma bubble that hadronizes via deflagration at 170 MeV according to the Koch-Muller-Rafelski model, producing particles with a Boltzmann distribution. The generators also explore alternative bubble shapes (Landau fireball, pancake, chiral condensate) and odd-CP metastable vacuum bubbles.

## Key numbers

*   Plasma bubble hadronization temperature: 170 MeV, used for the Boltzmann distribution of produced hadrons.
*   Tagging radius R_tag = 1.3 fm corresponds to approximately 4.5% of the available energy in a central collision being converted to QGP.
*   Tagging radius R_tag = 2.5 fm corresponds to approximately 15% of the available energy being converted to QGP.
*   For the 4.5% energy case, the rapidity bump amplitude over background would increase by about a factor of three if the energy fraction were increased to 15%.
*   For odd-CP bubbles, Kharzeev estimated an average impulse of 30 MeV/c for a quark crossing the bubble diameter.
*   The chiral bubble (RHIC::CHIRAL) hadronizes into a pion gas at 70 MeV temperature.
*   The width of rapidity bumps from spherical bubbles is about 2 units of rapidity; for the Landau-type expansion (RHIC::LANDAU), the width increases to about 4 units, with 50% of tagged energy going into particle production and 50% into longitudinal expansion.

## Context

This work extends the non-equilibrium scenario proposed by Van Hove (1980s) for localized QGP droplets in heavy-ion collisions, in contrast to the more common assumption of large-scale thermal equilibrium used in models like HIJING (Gyulassy, Wang). The authors developed detailed event generators to make testable predictions for the STAR detector at RHIC, enabling single-event studies that could distinguish QGP signals from conventional backgrounds. The techniques are also applied to investigate odd-CP metastable vacuum bubbles proposed by Kharzeev and Pisarski, and the authors note future plans to use the VNI event generator (Geiger, Muller) for more realistic bubble production and hadron transport.
