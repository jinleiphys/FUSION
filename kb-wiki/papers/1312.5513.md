# Robust upper limit on the neutron single-particle energy of the $i_{13/2}$ orbit

> The boundary of the neutron $i_{13/2}$ single-particle energy is investigated with exact shell-model calculations, where random two-body interactions are adopted to overcome the bias from effective interactions. Excitation energies of $3^-_1$ state in $^{134}$Te and $^{136}$Xe, as well as those of $13/2^+_1$ states in $^{135}$Te and $^{137}$Xe, are taken as touchstones of our samplings. A robust upper limit of $\varepsilon_{i13/2}<3.1$ MeV emerges in our calculations, given the mixing of $i_{13/2}$ single-neutron configuration and $f_{7/2}\otimes 3^-$ configuration in $13/2^+_1$ states of $N=83$ isotones.

# Robust upper limit on the neutron single-particle energy of the $i_{13/2}$ orbit

## Key claim
The neutron $i_{13/2}$ single-particle energy has a robust upper limit of 3 MeV, based on random-interaction shell-model calculations that avoid the bias of specific effective interactions. This limit emerges from the requirement that the $i_{13/2}$ configuration must be able to mix physically with the $f_{7/2}\otimes 3^-$ configuration in the $13/2^+_1$ states of $N=83$ isotones.

## Method
Exact shell-model calculations were performed using the random-quasiparticle ensemble with single-particle energies (RQE-SPE), where two-body matrix elements are Gaussian random numbers with widths matched to the realistic CD-Bonn interaction (best-match scale $G=0.67$ MeV). The authors generated approximately 10000 Hamiltonian samples for each of four constraint levels: basic (ground-state spin-parity and first excitation energy within 0.2 MeV), $E_{3-}$ constraint (reproducing $3^-_1$ energies of $^{134}$Te and $^{136}$Xe within 0.2 MeV), $E_{13/2+}$ constraint (reproducing $13/2^+_1$ energies of $^{135}$Te and $^{137}$Xe within 0.2 MeV), and all constraints combined. The ratio $P_3/P_2$ of the $\varepsilon_{i13/2}$ distributions under combined versus $E_{13/2+}$-only constraints was used to identify the onset of physical two-configuration mixing.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit on $\varepsilon_{i13/2}$: 3 MeV, identified as the onset point of $\Delta P = P_3/P_2$ increase in both $^{134,135}$Te and $^{136,137}$Xe calculations.
- Best-match RQE-SPE energy scale: $G = 0.67$ MeV, fitted to the $c_J-J$ relation of the CD-Bonn interaction.
- Lower limit from neutron separation energy of $^{133}$Sn: $S_n \sim 2.4$ MeV, constraining the physical $\varepsilon_{i13/2}$ window to 2.4–3 MeV.
- Experimental $13/2^+_1$ energies used as constraints: 2.109 MeV for $^{135}$Te and 1.725 MeV for $^{137}$Xe.
- Acceptable spectral deviation: 0.2 MeV, following the uncertainty estimate of Ref. (Jin and collaborators, 2013).

## Context
This work extends previous efforts to determine the $i_{13/2}$ single-particle energy in the $^{132}$Sn region, where the level is unobserved experimentally and lies above the neutron separation energy. Prior predictions from the Nilsson model and relativistic mean-field theory placed the $i_{13/2}$ level above 3.5 MeV, while an estimate from $^{134}$Sb and $^{210}$Bi spectra gave $\varepsilon_{i13/2}=2.694(200)$ MeV (Ref. (Jin and collaborators, 2013)). The random-interaction approach is a novel application of the RQE-SPE method, previously used for generic many-body properties such as spin-zero ground-state dominance and collective motion predominance, to a specific nuclear-structure problem. The result enables tighter constraints on shell-model interactions for neutron-rich nuclei beyond $^{132}$Sn and impacts calculations of $r$-process abundances near $A\sim 132$ through $\beta$-decay lifetimes and neutron emission probabilities.
