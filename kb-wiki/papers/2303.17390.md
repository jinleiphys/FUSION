# Examination of cluster production in excited light systems at Fermi energies from new experimental data and comparison with transport model calculations

> Four different reactions, $^{32}$S+$^{12}$C and $^{20}$Ne+$^{12}$C at 25 and 50 MeV/nucleon, have been measured with the FAZIA detector capable of full isotopic identification of most forward emitted reaction products. Fragment multiplicities, angular distributions and energy spectra have been measured and compared with Monte Carlo simulations, i.e. the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and the heavy-ion phase space exploration (HIPSE) models. These models are combined with two different afterburner codes (HF$l$ and SIMON) to describe the decay of the excited primary fragments. In the case of AMD, the effect of including the clustering and inter-clustering processes to form bound particles and fragments is discussed. A clear confirmation of the role of cluster aggregation in the reaction dynamics and particle production for these light systems, for which the importance of the clustering process increases with bombarding energy, is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that cluster aggregation processes play a significant role in reaction dynamics and particle production for light systems (total mass A<50) at Fermi energies, with the importance of clustering increasing with bombarding energy, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the comparison relies on two specific model implementations (AMD with and without clustering) and the agreement is not perfect for all observables.

## Method

Four reactions (32S+12C and 20Ne+12C at 25 and 50 MeV/nucleon) were measured using the FAZIA detector array, which provides full isotopic identification of forward-emitted products via DeltaE-E and pulse shape analysis techniques. Experimental fragment multiplicities, angular distributions, and energy spectra were compared with two Monte Carlo models: the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model, run both with and without cluster correlations, and the heavy-ion phase space exploration (HIPSE) model; both models were coupled with afterburner codes (HFℓ for AMD, SIMON for HIPSE) to simulate statistical de-excitation of primary fragments.

## Key numbers

* For the 32S+12C reaction at 25 MeV/nucleon, the experimental charge distribution shows a fraction of approximately 13% fusion-like events in 4π configuration, based on fusion systematics from Eudes (2014).
* The AMD model was run with the Skyrme SLy4 effective force with incompressibility modulus Ksat = 230 MeV, symmetry energy parameters S0 = 32 MeV and Lsym = 46 MeV (asy-soft parametrization).
* The AMD dynamical phase was stopped at 500 fm/c after the onset of the interaction, with approximately 60000 primary events simulated for each of the four systems.
* For the 32S+12C reaction at 25 MeV/nucleon, the experimental event fraction with heavy fragment multiplicity MHF=0 is 33%, with MHF=1 is 62%, and with MHF≥2 is 5%; AMD-CC predicts 31%, 63%, and 6% respectively.
* For the 20Ne+12C reaction at 25 MeV/nucleon, the experimental event fraction with MHF=0 is 58%, with MHF=1 is 42%, and with MHF≥2 is less than 1%; AMD-CC predicts 45%, 55%, and less than 1% respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cluster production in light systems at Fermi energies, which had been performed mainly on C+C systems by Tian (2017), Tian (2018), and Han (2020), to heavier systems (S+C and Ne+C). The new experimental data provide a benchmark for transport model calculations, particularly testing the AMD model's cluster correlation implementation developed by Ono (2019) and the HIPSE event generator of Lacroix (2004); the results enable further refinement of clustering mechanisms in microscopic reaction models and have implications for hadrontherapy treatment planning, where accurate prediction of charged fragment yields is needed as noted by Dudouet (2014).
