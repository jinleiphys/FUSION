# Hadronic Effects in the Pionium Ion

> The hadronic properties of the pionium ion (Coulomb bound system of three charged pions) are estimated using the results for the positronium ion $P{s}^{-}$. It turns out that the hadronic shift of the ground state energy and the lifetime of the pionium ion are approximately the same as for pionium.

# Hadronic Effects in the Pionium Ion

## Key claim

The hadronic shift of the ground state energy and the lifetime of the pionium ion (a Coulomb bound system of three charged pions) are approximately the same as for pionium (the two-pion atom), based on scaling arguments from the positronium ion. The authors note that this analogy fails for the vacuum polarisation shift, which cannot be derived from the positronium ion results because it depends on both the pion and electron masses.

## Method

The authors use dimensional arguments to scale known numerical variational results for the positronium ion (e⁻e⁺e⁻) to the pionium ion (π⁻π⁺π⁻), exploiting the fact that both systems involve only one mass and the same charges. The hadronic shift of the pionium ion is estimated from the probability density of a π⁻ at the position of π⁺, integrated over all positions, using the ratio of probability densities from positronium ion calculations. The lifetime is estimated by the same scaling procedure, using the known ratio of lifetimes of positronium and the positronium ion.

## Key numbers

- The π⁻-affinity of pionium (binding energy of the pionium ion against dissociation into pionium and a free π⁻) is 89.2 eV, derived from 0.3267 (m_c/m_e) eV where m_c/m_e is the charged pion to electron mass ratio.
- The total binding energy of the pionium ion with respect to three free charged pions is 1947.3 eV, composed of 1858.1 eV from the Coulomb binding plus 89.2 eV affinity.
- The hadronic shift of the ground state energy of the pionium ion is approximately 2.8 eV, the same as for pionium from Moor et al. (1995) and Gashi et al. (1997).
- The lifetime of the pionium ion is approximately 3.3 × 10⁻¹⁵ s, the same as for pionium from Moor et al. (1995) and Gashi et al. (1997).
- The vacuum polarisation shift of the ground state energy of the pionium ion is approximately 0.9 eV, the same as for pionium from Gashi et al. (1997).
- The ratio 2ρ(e⁻e⁺e⁻)/ρ(e⁺e⁻) is approximately 1.05, derived from the positronium singlet lifetime of 1.25 × 10⁻¹⁰ s and the positronium ion lifetime of 0.478 × 10⁻⁹ s from Ho (1983) and Mills (1983).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of pionium (the π⁺π⁻ atom) to the three-body pionium ion, which had not been previously investigated despite the experimental production of such systems in the CERN experiment by Adeva et al. (1994). The authors use the well-studied positronium ion as a starting point, drawing on variational calculations by Kolos et al. (1960), Hylleraas (1947), Schroder (1963), and Frolov et al. (1989). The results enable estimation of the hadronic properties of the pionium ion without full three-body hadronic calculations, though the authors note that a separate treatment of vacuum polarisation is needed. A second note on the formation probability of the pionium ion in the CERN experiment is promised.
