# Heavy Quark Radiative Energy Loss - Applications to RHIC

> Heavy quark energy loss in a hot QCD plasma is computed taking into account the competing effects due to suppression of zeroth order gluon radiation bellow the plasma frequency and the enhancement of gluon radiation due to transition energy loss and medium induced Bremsstrahlung. Heavy quark medium induced radiative energy loss is derived to all orders in opacity, $(L/λ_g)^n$. Numerical evaluation of the energy loss suggest small suppression of high $p_\perp$ charm quarks, and therefore provide a possible explanation for the null effects observed by PHENIX in the prompt electron spectrum in $Au+Au$ as $\sqrt{s}=130$ and 200 AGeV.

## Key claim

Heavy quark medium-induced radiative energy loss is derived to all orders in opacity, $(L/\lambda_g)^n$, and numerical evaluation suggests small suppression of high $p_\perp$ charm quarks, providing a possible explanation for the null effects observed by PHENIX in the prompt electron spectrum in $Au+Au$ at $\sqrt{s}=130$ and 200 AGeV. The actual hedge is that the predicted suppression is small (0.6-0.8) and consistent with PHENIX data within large experimental errors, but possibly inconsistent with STAR data.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark radiative energy loss in a hot QCD plasma by generalizing the GLV opacity series to include massive quark kinematic effects and the QCD Ter-Mikayelian plasmon effect. They derive the medium-induced radiative energy loss to all orders in opacity, with the general result obtained by shifting all frequencies in the GLV series by $(m_g^2+x^2 M^2)/(2 x E)$. Numerical evaluations are performed for charm and bottom quarks in a plasma characterized by $\alpha_s=0.3$, $\mu=0.5$ GeV, and $L=5\lambda=5$ fm, using a static medium approximation for transition radiation from Zakharov (1998).

## Key numbers

* For charm quarks in the energy range $E \sim 5-15$ GeV, the first-order induced radiative energy loss fraction is $\Delta E^{(1)}/E \approx 0.2$, while for bottom quarks it is about half that value.
* The transverse plasmon mass effect reduces the zeroth order energy loss by approximately $30\%$ relative to the vacuum case; transition radiation further lowers this to approximately $15\%$.
* For a 10 GeV charm quark, the additional energy loss in the medium is approximately $15\%$ of the initial quark energy.
* The predicted charm quark suppression factor is in the range $0.6-0.8$, which is much smaller than the observed pion suppression of $0.2$.
* Even in the absence of a medium ($L=0$), a charm quark with energy $\sim 10$ GeV suffers an average energy loss $\Delta E^{(0)}_{vac}/E \approx 1/3$ due to the sudden change of color current; the dielectric plasmon effect reduces this to $\Delta E^{(0)}_{med}/E \approx 1/4$.

## Context

This work extends the GLV opacity expansion (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev 2000) for massless partons to heavy quarks, incorporating the kinematic "dead cone" effect from Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) and the QCD Ter-Mikayelian plasmon effect. The results provide a theoretical framework for interpreting upcoming high-statistics $D$ meson data from RHIC, which could serve as a decisive test for quark-gluon plasma existence when compared with the already observed jet quenching and elliptic flow of light partons.
