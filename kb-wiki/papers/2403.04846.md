# Scaling of pre-equilibrium dilepton production in QCD Kinetic Theory

> We use QCD kinetic theory to compute dilepton production coming from the pre-equilibrium phase of the Quark-Gluon Plasma created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate that the dilepton spectrum exhibits a simple scaling in terms of the specific shear viscosity $η/s$ and entropy density $dS/dζ\sim {\scriptstyle \left(Tτ^{1/3}\right)_\infty^{3/2}}$, which can be derived from dimensional analysis in the presence of a pre-equilibrium attractor. Based on this scaling we perform event-by-event calculations of in-medium dilepton production and determine the invariant mass range where the pre-equilibrium yield is the leading contribution.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium dilepton spectrum exhibits a simple scaling in terms of the specific shear viscosity η/s and entropy density dS/dζ, which can be derived from dimensional analysis in the presence of a pre-equilibrium attractor; the scaling is verified for different coupling strengths and works best for later evolution times (w̃ ≳ 1), while at very early times the spectra remain sensitive to the initial conditions for the kinetic evolution.

## Method

The authors use QCD kinetic theory to compute the phase-space distributions of quarks and gluons during the pre-equilibrium evolution of the QGP, incorporating all leading order processes into the Boltzmann equation with infrared divergences regulated by isotropic screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effects treated via effective vertex resummation. Starting from a gluon-dominated initial state, they numerically solve the Boltzmann equation to determine the evolution of quark and anti-quark distributions, then compute the dilepton production rate from quark anti-quark annihilation at each time step. The scaling function is verified by comparing results for different coupling strengths λ = 5, 10, 20, and the phenomenological application uses the hybrid model Trajectum with Bayesian-extracted parameters for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For coupling strengths λ = 5, 10, 20, the corresponding specific shear viscosities are η/s = 2.716, 0.994, and 0.385 respectively.
- The asymptotic temperature constants (τ^{1/3}T)_∞/Q_s^{2/3} for λ = 5, 10, 20 are 0.799, 0.612, and 0.474 respectively.
- The strong coupling α_s values for λ = 5, 10, 20 are 0.133, 0.265, and 0.531 respectively.
- The pre-equilibrium dilepton yield scales as (4π η/s)^2 times a universal scaling function of the rescaled invariant mass √(4π η/s) M / (Tτ^{1/3})^{3/2}_∞.
- The invariant mass range where pre-equilibrium production dominates is identified as 3 GeV < M < 4 GeV.
- The hydrodynamic matching is performed at τ_hydro = 1.0 fm, with the thermal QGP contribution computed for temperatures above T_c = 154 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the scaling approach previously developed for pre-equilibrium photon production by Garcia-Montero et al. (2023) to dilepton production, and provides a first-principles microscopic calculation that goes beyond earlier phenomenological parametrizations using anisotropic equilibrium distribution functions of the Romatschke-Strickland form by Coquet et al. (2021, 2023). The results enable efficient event-by-event phenomenological calculations of pre-equilibrium dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions, and the scaling is expected to extend to other pre-equilibrium probes such as heavy flavor quarks (Du et al., 2023) and jet quenching (Andres et al., 2022).
