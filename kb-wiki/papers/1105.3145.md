# Non-linear doublon production in a Mott insulator --- Landau-Dykhne method applied to an integrable model

> Doublon-hole pair production which takes place during dielectric breakdown in a Mott insulator subject to a strong laser or a static electric field is studied in the one-dimensional Hubbard model. Two nonlinear effects cause the excitation, i.e., multi-photon absorption and quantum tunneling. Keldysh crossover between the two mechanisms occurs as the field strength and photon energy is changed. The calculation is done analytically by the Landau-Dykhne method in combination with the Bethe ansatz solution and the results are compared with those of the time dependent density matrix renormalization group. Using this method, we calculate distribution function of the generated doublon-hole pairs and show that it drastically changes as we cross the Keldysh crossover line. After calculating the tunneling threshold for several representative one-dimensional Mott insulators, possible experimental tests of the theory is proposed such as angle resolved photoemission spectroscopy of the upper Hubbard band in the quantum tunneling regime. We also discuss the relation of the present theory with a many-body extension of electron-positron pair production in nonlinear quantum electrodynamics known as the Schwinger mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonlinear doublon-hole pair production in a Mott insulator under strong electric fields follows the Keldysh paradigm, with a crossover between multi-photon absorption and quantum tunneling regimes, but the Landau-Dykhne method combined with Bethe ansatz provides only an approximate analytical result valid when excitations are rare events (tunneling probability much less than one) and when field strength is not too large compared with the Schwinger limit.

## Method

The author combines the Landau-Dykhne quantum tunneling theory with the Bethe ansatz solution of the one-dimensional half-filled fermionic Hubbard model to calculate the momentum-resolved tunneling probability of doublon-hole pairs under DC and AC electric fields. The method reduces the many-body problem to independent two-level systems by exploiting momentum conservation, then computes the imaginary part of the dynamical phase difference between ground and excited states along a complex-time integration path where level crossing occurs. The total production rate is obtained by integrating the momentum-resolved probability over the Brillouin zone and multiplying by the attempt frequency (F0/2π for DC fields, Ω/2π for AC fields).

## Key numbers

- Tunneling threshold (Schwinger limit) for DC fields: Fth ≈ ΔMott/(2ξ), where ΔMott is the Mott gap and ξ is the correlation length
- For small U limit, production rate: Γp = (F0/2π) exp(-π(ΔMott/2)²/(veffF0)) with veff = 2 + U/2π + ...
- For large U limit, production rate: Γp = (F0/2π)(gτ/U)^(πU/(2F0)) with g ≈ 4.377
- AC field multi-photon regime production rate: Γ ∝ (F0ξ/(2πΩ))^(2ΔMott/Ω)
- DC tunneling regime production rate: Γ ∝ exp(-πFth/F0)
- Correlation length ξ in small U limit: ξ = (2τ + U/2π + ...)/Δ(U,τ)
- Correlation length in large U limit: ξ⁻¹ = ln(U/gτ) with g ≈ 4.377

## Context

This work extends the Keldysh crossover concept, originally developed for atom ionization (Keldysh 1965) and electron-positron pair production in QED (Popov 1972, 1974; Brezin 1970), to nonlinear excitations in strongly correlated condensed matter systems. The method builds on earlier numerical studies of dielectric breakdown in Mott insulators using exact diagonalization (Oka 2003), time-dependent density matrix renormalization group (Oka 2005a, Heidrich-Meisner 2010), and nonequilibrium dynamical mean field theory (Eckstein 2011). The results enable quantitative predictions for photo-induced insulator-to-metal transitions, including angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy of the upper Hubbard band in the quantum tunneling regime, and establish a direct analogy between the Schwinger mechanism in QED and dielectric breakdown in Mott insulators.
