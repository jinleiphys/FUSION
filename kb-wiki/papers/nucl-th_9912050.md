# Solutions of the Wick-Cutkosky model in the Light-Front Dynamics

> We study relativistic effects in a system of two scalar particles interacting via a scalar exchange in the Light Front Dynamcis framework. The results are compared to those provided by Bethe-Salpeter and non relativistic equations. It is found in particular that for massive exchange, the relativistic description is of crucial importance even in the limit of zero binding energy. PACS: 11.10, 03.70, 03.65P Keywords: Light-Front Dynamics, Relativistic equations, Quantum Field Theory

# Solutions of the Wick-Cutkosky model in the Light-Front Dynamics

## Key claim

For systems interacting via a massive exchanged field, relativistic effects are of crucial importance even in the limit of zero binding energy, contrary to the massless exchange case where a nonrelativistic limit exists. The relativistic description dramatically departs from nonrelativistic predictions for massive exchange regardless of how small the binding energy becomes.

## Method

The authors solved the Light-Front Dynamics (LFD) equation for two identical scalar particles of mass m interacting via scalar exchange of mass μ in the ladder approximation, using a spline function expansion and Gauss quadrature to handle the two-dimensional integral equation for S-wave bound states. They compared results with Bethe-Salpeter equation solutions obtained via the method of Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1996) and with nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation solutions for Yukawa or Coulomb potentials.

## Key numbers

- For μ=0 and α=0.3, the LFD binding energy is B=0.0117 (in units where ℏ=c=m=1), while the nonrelativistic value is 100% larger; the relativistic parameter R=<q²>/m² is only 0.011, showing large relativistic effects despite small average momentum
- For μ=0.15 and α=0.4, the LFD binding energy is B=0.00190; for α=7.26, B reaches 2.00 (the maximum value of 2m)
- The relative difference in coupling constant between LFD and Bethe-Salpeter for fixed binding energy B=m is limited to 10% even for strong couplings α≥5
- For the scalar deuteron model with Malfliet-Tjon parameters (V_R=7.29, V_A=3.18, μ_A=0.314 GeV, μ_R=2μ_A), the LFD binding energy is 0.96 MeV compared to the nonrelativistic 2.23 MeV; recovering 2.23 MeV requires decreasing the repulsive coupling constant by 10% to V_R=6.60
- The three-body Fock component normalization correction for μ=0.15 saturates at approximately 25-30% in the ultrarelativistic region; for deuteron (R≈1%) the correction is estimated at 4%

## Context

This work extends the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick and Cutkosky, 1954) from the Bethe-Salpeter framework into Light-Front Dynamics, following the covariant LFD formulation of Karmanov (1976) and recent applications to high-momentum processes at TJNAF by Carbonell and Karmanov (1998, 1999). The results are compared with Bethe-Salpeter calculations by Tjon and collaborators, and with nonrelativistic calculations. The authors find that LFD and Bethe-Salpeter results are close despite different ladder kernels, but both differ dramatically from nonrelativistic predictions for massive exchange, even at zero binding energy. This enables relativistic modeling of the deuteron using a scalar version of the Malfliet-Tjon potential (Malfliet and Tjon, 1969), and establishes that nonrelativistic dynamics is inadequate for systems interacting via massive fields, which applies to all strong interaction physics not described via gluon exchange.
