# Nonlocal Kinetic Equation and Simulations of Heavy Ion Reactions

> A kinetic equation which combines the quasiparticle drift of Landau's equation with a dissipation governed by a nonlocal and noninstantaneous scattering integral in the spirit of Enskog corrections is discussed. Numerical values of the off-shell contribution to the Wigner distribution, of the collision duration and of the collision nonlocality are presented for different realistic potentials. On preliminary results we show that simulations of quantum molecular dynamics extended by the nonlocal treatment of collisions leads to a broader proton distribution bringing the theoretical spectra closer towards the experimental values than the local approach.

# Nonlocal Kinetic Equation and Simulations of Heavy Ion Reactions

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating nonlocal and noninstantaneous binary collisions into quantum molecular dynamics simulations of heavy ion reactions produces broader proton distributions that are closer to experimental values than the local Boltzmann approach, but the authors explicitly note these are preliminary results using a separable Paris potential that is "not fully reliable" as seen from comparison of collision delay and hard core displacement.

## Method

The authors derive a nonlocal kinetic equation from nonequilibrium Green's functions using the extended quasiparticle approximation, keeping all linear gradient terms to obtain a scattering integral with Enskog-type spatial shifts and a finite collision delay time. They evaluate the collision delay time and hard-sphere shift from the T-matrix for different realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Bonn A-C, Paris, separable Paris) and then implement the nonlocal scattering integral into a quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) code for central collisions of 129Xe on 119Sn at 50 MeV/A.

## Key numbers

- Collision delay time Δ_t ~ 1 fm/c for isolated nucleon-nucleon collisions, with a negative minimum at small energies for forward angles indicating attractive behavior
- Hard core shift |Δ_HS| ~ 1 fm for isolated nucleon-nucleon collisions, behaving regularly at low energies
- The off-shell contribution to the Wigner distribution is shown at temperature T = 10 MeV, where the difference between the Wigner and Fermi-Dirac distributions is not small, particularly at high momenta where power-law off-shell tails dominate over the exponentially falling quasiparticle distribution
- The exclusive proton spectra are shown for central collision of 129Xe → 119Sn at 50 MeV/A, where nonlocal corrections bring the theoretical spectrum closer to experimental values from INDRA experiments

## Context

This work extends the Enskog corrections for classical hard spheres, first discussed for nuclear matter by Malfliet (1984) and recently implemented by Kortemayer, Daffin and Bauer (1996), to a fully quantum nonlocal and noninstantaneous treatment. The finite collision duration and its thermodynamic consequences had been studied by Schmidt, Röpke and Schulz (1990), while its effect on pressure was discussed by Danielewicz and Pratt (1996). The paper enables implementation of nonlocal corrections evaluated from the T-matrix into existing Monte Carlo simulation codes, though the authors note the separable Paris potential used in preliminary results is not fully reliable.
