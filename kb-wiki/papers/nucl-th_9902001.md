# Recent progress on the chiral unitary approach to meson meson and meson baryon interactions

> We report on recent progress on the chiral unitary approach, analogous to the effective range expansion in Quantum Mechanics, which is shown to have a much larger convergence radius than ordinary chiral perturbation theory, allowing one to reproduce data for meson meson interaction up to 1.2 GeV. Applications to physical processes so far unsuited for a standard chiral perturbative approach are presented. Results for the extension of these ideas to the meson baryon sector are discussed, together with applications to kaons in a nuclear medium and $K^-$ atoms.

# Recent progress on the chiral unitary approach to meson meson and meson baryon interactions

## Key claim
The chiral unitary approach, which implements exact unitarity and expands Re T^{-1} in powers of external four-momentum rather than expanding the T matrix itself, extends the convergence radius of chiral perturbation theory to reproduce meson meson scattering data up to about 1.2 GeV, whereas ordinary chiral perturbation theory is limited to about sqrt(s) = 400 MeV. The method dynamically generates low-lying resonances without requiring them as explicit inputs.

## Method
The authors start from a K-matrix approach in coupled channels where unitarity is automatically satisfied, writing T^{-1} = K^{-1} - i sigma, with sigma the phase-space matrix. They then use standard chiral perturbation theory up to O(p^4) to obtain an expansion for T_2 Re T^{-1} T_2, leading to the coupled-channel generalization of the inverse amplitude method: T = T_2 [T_2 - T_4]^{-1} T_2. For the L=0 sector, a further simplification uses a Bethe-Salpeter equation with the lowest-order chiral amplitude T_2 as the potential and the loop function G regularized with a cutoff, giving T = [1 - T_2 G]^{-1} T_2.

## Key numbers
- The chiral unitary approach reproduces meson meson data up to 1.2 GeV, while ordinary chiral perturbation theory is limited to about sqrt(s) = 400 MeV.
- For phi -> pi+ pi- gamma, the predicted branching ratio is 2.6 x 10^{-4}, with the f0(980) peak contributing 0.76 x 10^{-4}.
- For phi -> pi0 pi0 gamma, the predicted branching ratio is 1.3 x 10^{-4}, compared to the experimental value (1.14 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.12) x 10^{-4}.
- The coupled-channel calculation for K- p -> gamma Sigma^0 increases the rate by about a factor of 16 relative to the Born approximation.
- The photon energy chosen for the gamma p -> K+ Lambda(1405) reaction is E_gamma = 1.7 GeV.
- The K- momentum range for the K- p -> Lambda(1405) gamma reaction is 300 to 500 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the standard chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1980s) by incorporating exact unitarity, analogous to the effective range expansion in quantum mechanics. The approach builds on the inverse amplitude method of Truong (1988) and its coupled-channel generalization. It enables calculations of processes previously inaccessible to chiral perturbation theory, including gamma gamma -> meson pairs up to sqrt(s) = 1.4 GeV, phi radiative decays, and gamma p -> K+ Lambda(1405) reactions planned at SPring8/RCNP and TJNAF. In the meson baryon sector, it provides a framework for studying K- in nuclear matter and K- atoms, with implications for kaon condensation in neutron stars.
