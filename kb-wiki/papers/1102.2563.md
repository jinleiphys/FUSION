# System size effects in the N/Z dependence of balance energy for isotopic series

> We study the system size effects in the N/Z dependence of balance energy for the isotopic series. We find drastic effect of symmetry energy on the N/Z dependence of E$_{bal}$ throughout the mass range. We also find that the N/Z dependence of E$_{bal}$ for isotopic series of lighter system is slightly more sensitive to symmetry energy as compared to that of heavier systems. We also study the mass dependence of E$_{bal}$ for the N/Z range from 1.0-2.0. We find that the mass dependence of E$_{bal}$ varies with the N/Z ratio.

## Key claim

The N/Z dependence of balance energy (E_bal) for isotopic series is drastically affected by symmetry energy throughout the mass range, but the N/Z dependence for lighter isotopic series is slightly more sensitive to symmetry energy than for heavier series.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state and standard isospin- and energy-dependent cross sections reduced by 20% (σ = 0.8 σ_nn^free). They simulated collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Zr+Zr, Sn+Sn, and Xe+Xe with N/Z ratios varying from 1.0 to 2.0 in steps of 0.2 at impact parameter b/b_max = 0.2–0.4. The balance energy was extracted from the directed transverse momentum ⟨p_x^dir⟩ as a function of incident energy.

## Key numbers

- For the N/Z dependence of E_bal, the linear slope |m| values are: 33 for Ca+Ca, 25 for Ni+Ni, 21 for Zr+Zr, 18 for Sn+Sn, and 15 for Xe+Xe series.
- The power law exponent τ for the system size dependence of E_bal varies with N/Z: τ = -0.30±0.03 for N/Z=1.0, -0.28±0.02 for N/Z=1.2, -0.27±0.01 for N/Z=1.4, -0.21±0.02 for N/Z=1.6, and -0.20±0.03 for N/Z=2.0.
- The percentage difference ΔE_bal(%) between calculations without and with symmetry energy increases with N/Z for both Ca and Xe series, with a sharper increase for Ca than for Xe.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Gautam et al. (2010) on E_bal as a function of combined mass and colliding geometry for isobaric pairs, and by Sood on E_bal for Ca isotopes with N/Z from 1.0 to 2.0. The present paper systematically examines isotopic series across five mass systems (Ca through Xe) to separate the role of symmetry energy from Coulomb effects. The results enable future experimental constraints on the density dependence of symmetry energy using lighter isotopic systems, where the sensitivity is slightly greater.
