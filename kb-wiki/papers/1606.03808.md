# Pairing Phase Transitions of Matter under Rotation

> The phases and properties of matter under global rotation have attracted much interest recently. In this paper we investigate the pairing phenomena in a system of fermions under the presence of rotation. We find that there is a generic suppression effect on pairing states with zero angular momentum. We demonstrate this effect with the chiral condensation and the color superconductivity in hot dense QCD matter as explicit examples. In the case of chiral condensation, a new phase diagram in the temperature-rotation parameter space is found, with a nontrivial critical point.

# Pairing Phase Transitions of Matter under Rotation

## Key claim
Rotation generically suppresses scalar pairing states (those with zero total angular momentum) in fermion systems, but this suppression is demonstrated only in the limit of slow rotation and within a mean-field treatment of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model; the effect is not proven for all interaction strengths or for rapid rotation, where higher-spin condensates may become favorable.

## Method
The authors model a rotating fermion system by transforming to a rotating reference frame, expanding the Dirac Lagrangian to first order in angular velocity ω, and obtaining a Hamiltonian with an effective term −ω·J. They then apply the NJL model with four-fermion contact interactions, introduce mean-field condensates for chiral condensation (quark-anti-quark pairing) and color superconductivity (diquark pairing), and solve the gap equations numerically using the eigenstates of the rotating-frame Hamiltonian expressed in cylindrical coordinates with Bessel functions.

## Key numbers
- For chiral condensation at zero density, the critical end point in the T–ω phase diagram is located at T_CEP = 0.020 GeV and ω_CEP = 0.644 GeV.
- The mean-field mass gap M (at radius r = 0.1 GeV⁻¹) decreases with increasing ω at all temperatures, confirming rotational suppression.
- For color superconductivity at fixed μ = 400 MeV, the diquark condensate Δ (at radius r = 0.1 GeV⁻¹) decreases toward zero with increasing ω, through a first-order transition at low T and a smooth crossover at higher T.
- The transition temperature T_c for chiral condensation becomes smaller at larger ω.

## Context
This work extends the study of rotation effects on QCD matter phase structure, motivated by the analogy between rotation and magnetic fields noted in earlier work on chiral vortical effect and chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Son 2009). It builds on lattice gauge theory simulations of rotating QCD matter (Yamamoto 2013) and on the NJL model treatments of chiral condensation and color superconductivity (Klevansky, Alford reviews). The results enable future ab initio lattice studies of the T–ω phase diagram and suggest measurable consequences for heavy ion collisions and neutron star properties, as well as applications to cold atomic gases and BCS-BEC crossover.
