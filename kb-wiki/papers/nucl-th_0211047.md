# Transverse Momentum Spectra of Pions in Particle and Nuclear Collisions and Some Ratio-Behaviours: Towards A Combinational Approach

> The nature of transverse momentum dependence of the inclusive cross-sections for secondary pions produced in high energy hadronic($PP$), hadronuclear($PA$) and nuclear($AA$) collisions has here been exhaustively investigated for a varied range of interactions in a unified way with the help of a master formula. This formula evolved from a new combination of the basic Hagedorn's model for particle(pion) production in PP scattering at ISR range of energies, a phenomenological approach proposed by Peitzmann for converting the results of $NN(PP)$ reactions to those for either $PA$ or $AA$ collisions, and a specific form of parametrization for mass number-dependence of the nuclear cross sections. This grand combination of models(GCM) is then applied to analyse the assorted extensive data on various high energy collisions. The nature of qualitative agreement between measurements and calculations on both the inclusive cross-sections for production of pions, and some ratios of them as well, is quite satisfactory. The modest successes that we achieve here in dealing with the massive data-sets are somewhat encouraging in view of the diversity of the reactions and the very wide range of interaction energies.

# Transverse Momentum Spectra of Pions in Particle and Nuclear Collisions and Some Ratio-Behaviours: Towards A Combinational Approach

## Key claim

The authors propose that the transverse momentum spectra of pions produced in nucleus-nucleus (AA) and nucleon-nucleus (PA) collisions can be described by a "grand combination of models" (GCM) that multiplies the Hagedorn power-law form for proton-proton (PP) collisions by a factor (A·B)^(α p_T − β p_T^2), where the quadratic exponent parametrization is approximately universal across collision systems. The claim is explicitly qualified: the approach is phenomenological, cannot be derived from first principles, and the agreement with data is described as "modest" and "moderately fair" rather than precise.

## Method

The authors first fit the Hagedorn power-law form E d³σ/dp³ = C₁ (1 + p_T/p₀)^(−n) to inclusive pion production data in PP and P(Pbar) collisions at various center-of-mass energies, extracting p₀ and n as functions of √s. They then apply the Peitzmann approach, which relates nuclear cross sections to PP cross sections via a factor (A·B)^g(p_T), where g(p_T) is parametrized as a quadratic function (ε + α p_T − β p_T^2). Because ε and the normalization constant C₂ cannot be simultaneously determined by fitting, the authors absorb them into a single constant C₃ = C₂ (A·B)^ε, effectively fitting only α and β for each nuclear collision system. Fits are performed in the p_T range 0.8–3.0 GeV/c for most systems, using p₀ and n values interpolated from the PP parameterizations at the corresponding √s_NN.

## Key numbers

- For PP collisions, the parameter p₀(√s) is given by p₀ = 1.5 + 79.4/(√s ln(√s)) (with p₀ in unspecified units, √s in GeV).
- For PP collisions, the exponent n(√s) is given by n = 6.5 + 127/ln²(√s).
- The ratio p₀/n characterizes the intercept of the p_T spectrum slope at p_T → 0 and has a value near 150 MeV for PP and some nuclear collisions, with a prescribed limit of 130–170 MeV.
- For nuclear collisions, the parameters α and β show a "steadily flat nature" at values around α = 0.18 ± 0.03 and β = 0.035 ± 0.009, independent of the product A·B.
- For Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 130 GeV (RHIC), the values of α and β do not show appreciable change between minimum bias, peripheral, and central event sets, and β remains the same within errors.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Peitzmann (2000) and the earlier work of the WA80 Collaboration (Albrecht et al., 1990s), who proposed a linear form for g(p_T) and treated p₀ and n separately for PP and AB reactions. The authors instead use a quadratic parametrization (De-Bhattacharyya parametrization, DBP) and extract p₀ and n directly from PP/P(Pbar) data via energy-dependent parameterizations. The GCM is compared to the Mixed Model (MM) of WA80, the Thermal Model (Aggarwal et al.), the Modified Hydrodynamic Model of Peitzmann (2002), and the HIJING Monte Carlo model (Wang et al.). The approach enables a unified description of pion production across PP, PA, and AA collisions with only three free parameters (C₃, α, β) per collision system, but the authors note it cannot be derived from first principles and is insensitive to space-time evolution of the collision.
