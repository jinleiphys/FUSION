# Trigonometrically extended Cornell potential and deconfinement

> Non-perturbative methods of effective field theory such like Lattice QCD have allowed to establish connection between the QCD Lagrangian and quark potential models, a prominent outcome being the Cornell (linear plus Coulomb) potential. In being quite successful in explaining properties of heavy flavor hadrons, be them quarkonia or baryons, this potential has been found less spectacular in the description of the non-strange baryons, the nucleon and the Delta. This behavior indicates that the one-gluon exchange and the flux-tube interaction do not fully account for the complexity of the dynamics of three light quarks. Very recently, the Cornell potential which is of infinte range has been upgraded by us toward an exactly solvable trigonometric potential of finite range that interpolates between the inverse-distance potential and the infinite wells while passing through a region of linear growth, a reason for which it contains the inverse distance and the linear potentials as first terms in its Taylor series decomposition. We here first make the point that the upgraded Cornell potential can be viewed as the exact counterpart on a curved space to a flat space 1/r potential, a circumstance that equips it by two simultaneous symmetries, SO(4), and SO(2,1). These allow to place the trigonometric confinement potential in the context of AdS_5/CFT correspondence and thus to establish the link of the algebraic aspects of the latter to QCD potentiology. Next we report that the above potential when employed in the quark-diquark system, provides a remarkably adequate description of both nucleon and Delta spectra and the proton mean square charge radius as well, and moreover implies an intriguing venue toward quark deconfinement as shut-down of the curvature considered as temperature dependent.

## Key claim

The trigonometrically extended Cornell (TEC) potential, which is an exactly solvable finite-range potential on a curved three-dimensional hypersphere, provides a remarkably adequate description of both nucleon and Delta spectra and the proton mean square charge radius when employed in a quark-diquark system, and implies a deconfinement mechanism as a temperature-dependent shut-down of the curvature.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for a trigonometric potential derived from the Laplace-Beltrami equation on a three-dimensional hypersphere of constant radius, which interpolates between a Coulomb-like potential and an infinite well while passing through a region of linear growth. They applied this potential to the quark-diquark system to model non-strange baryons, fitting the nucleon and Delta spectra and calculating the proton electric form factor. The curvature parameter was then considered as temperature-dependent to explore a deconfinement scenario.

## Key numbers

- Reduced mass parameter: μ = 1.06 fm⁻¹, coupling constant: G = 237.55 MeV·fm, rescaled hyper-radius: d = 2.31 fm, used to fit the nucleon spectrum.
- For the Delta spectrum, the rescaled hyper-radius was modified to d = 3 fm while keeping the other parameters nearly the same.
- The proton mean square charge radius was calculated and compared to the experimental value of √⟨r²⟩ = 0.87 fm.
- The model predicts a total of 33 unobserved resonances of dominant quark-diquark configurations in the N and Delta spectra with masses below approximately 2500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Cornell (linear plus Coulomb) potential, which has been successful for heavy flavor hadrons but less so for non-strange baryons, by upgrading it to a trigonometric potential of finite range. The authors previously introduced this potential in Quiry (2008) and here establish its connection to the algebraic aspects of the AdS₅/CFT correspondence, following the adaptation of that correspondence to hadron properties by Brodsky (2004). The deconfinement mechanism as curvature shut-down extends earlier work by Takagi (2004) on deconfinement as gradual flattening of space.
