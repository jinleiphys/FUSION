# $1^{++}$ Nonet Singlet-Octet Mixing Angle, Strange Quark Mass, and Strange Quark Condensate

## Key claim

The paper determines the $f_1(1420)$-$f_1(1285)$ mixing angle $\theta$ by combining two independent analyses, finding $\theta = (19.4^{+4.5}_{-4.6})^\circ$; this value is conditional on the $K_1(1270)$-$K_1(1400)$ mixing angle $\theta_{K_1}=(-34\pm 13)^\circ$ extracted from $B$ and $\tau$ decay data, and on a two-fold solution from radiative decay ratios that is resolved by the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula constraint.

## Method

The mixing angle $\theta$ was determined via two strategies: (i) the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula using the $K_1(1270)$-$K_1(1400)$ mixing angle $\theta_{K_1}=(-34\pm 13)^\circ$ extracted from $B\to K_1\gamma$ and $\tau\to K_1\nu_\tau$ branching fractions; (ii) the ratio of $f_1(1285)\to\phi\gamma$ and $f_1(1285)\to\rho^0\gamma$ branching fractions. The strange quark mass and strange quark condensate were then computed from the $f_1(1420)$-$f_1(1285)$ mass difference QCD sum rule, with the operator-product-expansion series up to dimension six and to ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^3, m_s^2\alpha_s^2)$ accuracy.

## Key numbers

- $f_1(1420)$-$f_1(1285)$ mixing angle $\theta = (19.4^{+4.5}_{-4.6})^\circ$ from combining two analyses; the alternative solution $(51.1^{+4.5}_{-4.6})^\circ$ was excluded by the Gell-Mann-Okubo constraint.
- $K_1(1270)$-$K_1(1400)$ mixing angle $\theta_{K_1}=(-34\pm 13)^\circ$ extracted from $B\to K_1\gamma$ and $\tau\to K_1\nu_\tau$ data.
- Strange quark mass from the mass difference sum rule: $m_s(1\,{\rm GeV}) = 106.3 \pm 35.1~{\rm MeV}$ (corresponding to $m_s(2\,{\rm GeV}) = 89.5 \pm 29.5~{\rm MeV}$).
- Strange quark condensate ratio $\langle \bar{s} s\rangle / \langle \bar{u} u\rangle = 0.41 \pm 0.09$, obtained using the average of recent lattice results $m_s(2~{\rm GeV})=93.6\pm 1.0$~MeV as input.
- $f_1(1285) \to \phi\gamma$ branching fraction $(7.4\pm2.6)\times 10^{-4}$ and $f_1(1285) \to \rho^0\gamma$ branching fraction $(5.5\pm1.3)\%$ used as inputs.

## Context

This work extends previous determinations of the $1^{++}$ nonet mixing angle, which had been studied by Suzuki (1993), Cheng (2003), and Goldman (2005), and provides a more precise value by combining mass formula and radiative decay constraints. The QCD sum rule analysis of the $f_1(1420)$-$f_1(1285)$ mass difference offers a new framework for extracting the strange quark mass, cross-checking earlier sum rule results by Dominguez (2007), Chetyrkin (2005), and Narison (2005). The resulting strange quark condensate ratio $\langle \bar{s} s\rangle / \langle \bar{u} u\rangle = 0.41 \pm 0.09$ is consistent with the QCD sum rule result of Dominguez (2007) (range 0.4 to 0.7) but contrasts with the Schwinger-Dyson equation approach of Williams (2007) which gave $(1.0\pm 0.2)^3$.
