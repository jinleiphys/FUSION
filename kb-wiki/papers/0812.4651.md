# Hydrodynamic analysis of heavy ion collisions at RHIC

> Current status of dynamical modeling of relativistic heavy ion collisions and hydrodynamic description of the quark gluon plasma is reported. We find the hadronic rescattering effect plays an important role in interpretation of mass splitting pattern in the differential elliptic flow data observed at RHIC. To demonstrate this, we predict the elliptic flow parameter for phi mesons to directly observe the flow just after hadronisation. We also discuss recent applications of outputs from hydrodynamic calculations to J/psi suppression, thermal photon radiation and heavy quark diffusion.

# Hydrodynamic analysis of heavy ion collisions at RHIC

## Key claim
The observed mass splitting pattern in differential elliptic flow at RHIC results from a subtle interplay between perfect fluid dynamics of the early QGP stage and dissipative dynamics of the late hadronic stage, with hadronic rescattering effects playing an essential role in interpretation. The paper predicts that phi mesons will violate the usual hydrodynamic mass ordering at low pT because their small hadronic cross sections leave their elliptic flow nearly unchanged after hadronisation.

## Method
A hybrid model was constructed combining ideal three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics for the QGP phase with a hadronic cascade model (JAM) for the post-hadronisation stage. The hydrodynamic evolution used a bag model equation of state matched to a chemically frozen hadron resonance gas at critical temperature, with initial conditions tuned to reproduce charged hadron multiplicity at midrapidity via a Glauber model parametrisation. The Cooper-Frye formula provided the transition from fluid to particles at the critical temperature, after which the hadronic cascade simulated the gradual freezeout and rescattering.

## Key numbers
- Decoupling temperature T_dec = 100 MeV used in ideal hydrodynamic calculations for comparison with hybrid model results
- Impact parameter b = 7.2 fm for semi-central (20-30% centrality) Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Initial eccentricity fluctuations could enhance v2 by ~90% in 0-5% centrality and ~20% in 50-60% centrality
- Best fit to J/psi suppression data achieved with melting temperatures T_J/psi = 2 T_c and T_chi = 1.34 T_c
- Maximum temperature in central collisions found to be at most ~400 MeV
- Thermal photons from QGP phase dominate around pT ~ 3 GeV/c
- Drag force parameter gamma ~ 1-3 gives reasonable description of nuclear modification factor for electrons from heavy quark semileptonic decays
- v2 for phi mesons predicted to be larger than for protons (v2^p < v2^phi) for 0 < pT < 1.2 GeV/c, violating mass ordering since m_phi > m_p

## Context
This work extends the hybrid hydrodynamic+cascade approach developed by Hirano (2005, 2007) and others (Bass 2000, Teaney 2000, Nonaka 2006) to systematically test against RHIC elliptic flow data from PHOBOS and STAR. The finding that hadronic rescattering creates the observed mass splitting pattern contradicts the simpler sudden freezeout picture and explains why ideal hydrodynamics overshoots v2 at forward/backward rapidities. The results enable quantitative extraction of QGP transport properties through applications to J/psi suppression (following Matsui and Satz), thermal photon radiation (following Shuryak), and heavy quark diffusion via Langevin dynamics on the hydrodynamic background.
