# Jost function based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov formalism

> We formulate the Jost function formalism based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory which has been used to represent the nature of the superfluidity of nucleus. The Jost function based on the HFB can give the analytic representation of the S-matrix for the nucleon elastic scattering targeting on the open-shell nucleus taking into account the pairing effect. By adopting the Woods-Saxon potential, we show the numerical results of S-matrix poles and their trajectories with varying the pairing strength in two cases of Fermi energies. The total cross sections in each cases for neutron elastic scattering are also analyzed, and we confirmed some staggering shapes or sharp resonances originated from the effect of pairing can be seen in the cross section.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a Jost function formalism based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory to provide an analytic representation of the S-matrix for nucleon elastic scattering on open-shell nuclei, explicitly including the pairing effect. The authors demonstrate that the pairing correlation can produce staggering shapes or sharp resonances in the total cross section, but the work is limited to a Woods-Saxon potential and two specific Fermi energy values.

## Method

The authors derive the Jost function from the HFB equation, which is a coupled-channel equation with upper and lower components of the quasiparticle wave function, using the Green's function method and the Robin boundary condition at the origin. They define the S-matrix for nucleon scattering as the ratio of Jost functions and show that its denominator and numerator represent bound states and resonances, respectively. Numerical calculations are performed by solving the integral equation for the irregular solutions of the HFB equation with a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential and a volume-type pairing field, using the Nelder-Mead method to locate S-matrix poles.

## Key numbers

- Fermi energies used: λ = -8.0 MeV and λ = -1.0 MeV.
- Average pairing gap ⟨Δ⟩ varied from 0.0 to 10.0 MeV.
- Mass number A = 24 used for the Woods-Saxon potential radius parameter R = r₀A^(1/3).
- For d₅/₂ with ⟨Δ⟩ = 3.0 MeV and λ = -8.0 MeV, S-matrix poles are shown on the complex energy and momentum planes.
- A resonance found at E = 10.60 ± 4.60 i MeV with Δ(r) = 0 (no pairing limit) moves on trajectories as pairing strength increases.
- The certain point avoided by poles on the 2nd Riemann sheet is around E = -3/4 λ = 6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Jost function formalism, previously applied to the Schrodinger equation for nucleon-nucleus scattering, to the HFB framework to incorporate pairing correlations that are important for open-shell nuclei. It builds on prior studies of S-matrix pole trajectories as functions of potential depth by Hussein and on the analysis of quasiparticle resonances in neutron drip-line nuclei within HFB by Hamamoto, Zhang, and others. The formalism enables future investigation of how pairing affects reaction mechanisms and channel coupling, particularly in two-neutron transfer channels, and may lead to a new phenomenological model connecting low-energy scattering to microscopic nuclear structure theory.
