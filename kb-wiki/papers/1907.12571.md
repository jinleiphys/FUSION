# LIght scalars with lepton number to solve the $(g-2)_e$ anomaly

> Scalars that carry lepton number can help mediate would-be lepton-number-violating processes, such as neutrinoless double $β$ decay or lepton-scattering-mediated nucleon-antinucleon conversion. Here we show that such new scalars can also solve the anomaly in precision determinations of the fine-structure constant $α$ from atom interferometry and from the electron's anomalous magnetic moment, $a_e \equiv (g-2)_e/2$, by reducing $|a_e|$. Study of the phenomenological constraints on these solutions favor a doubly-charged scalar with mass below the GeV scale. Significant constraints arise from the measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry in Møller scattering, and we consider the implications of the next-generation MOLLER experiment at Jefferson Laboratory and of an improved $a_e$ measurement.

## Key claim

The paper shows that introducing scalars carrying lepton number can solve the anomaly in the electron's anomalous magnetic moment by reducing \(|a_e|\), but only if the scalars couple exclusively to first-generation fermions and have masses below the GeV scale, with the doubly-charged scalar being favored.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order contributions to the electron anomalous magnetic moment from three possible scalar representations (weak-isospin singlets \(X_1\) and \(X_2\) and a weak-isospin triplet \(X_3\)) that carry lepton number L = -2, using two-loop Feynman diagrams where the photon attaches either to the internal charged fermion line or to the charged scalar line. They then impose constraints from parity-violating Møller scattering measurements (E158 experiment) and from Bhabha scattering at LEP, and project sensitivities for the future MOLLER experiment at Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key numbers

* The anomaly in the electron's anomalous magnetic moment is \(\Delta a_e \equiv a_e^{\rm EXP} - a_e^{\rm SM}[{\rm Cs}] = (-88 \pm 36) \times 10^{-14}\), a discrepancy of approximately 2.4\(\sigma\).
* For the doubly-charged scalar \(X_1\) in the \(M_{X_i} \gg m_e\) limit, the solution requires \(3.2 \times 10^{-6} \le (m_e/M_{X_1}) |g_1^{11}| \le 9.7 \times 10^{-6}\) at 95% CL.
* For the singly-charged scalar \(X_2\) in the same limit, the solution requires \(6.5 \times 10^{-6} \le (m_e/M_{X_2}) |g_2^{1j}| \le 2.0 \times 10^{-5}\) at 95% CL.
* For the doubly-charged scalar \(X_3\) in the same limit, the solution requires \(3.4 \times 10^{-6} \le (m_e/M_{X_3}) |g_3^{11}| \le 1.0 \times 10^{-5}\) at 95% CL.
* The E158 experiment constrains \(|g_i^{11}| \leq 8.58 \times 10^{-5}\) for scalars with mass much less than \(\sqrt{s'} \simeq 0.23\) GeV, while the future MOLLER experiment will constrain \(|g_i^{11}| \leq 1.9 \times 10^{-5}\) for scalars with mass much less than \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 0.11\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of minimal scalar models previously employed to explore baryon-number-violating and lepton-number-violating processes (Arnold 2012, Arnold 2013, Gardner 2018) by showing they can also address the \((g-2)_e\) anomaly. It contrasts with models that explain both the electron and muon anomalies simultaneously (Davoudiasl 2018, Crivellin 2018, Liu 2018) by focusing solely on the electron anomaly, and it complements the analysis of doubly-charged scalars above 100 GeV (Dev 2018) by considering sub-GeV masses. The results enable future experimental searches for light scalars carrying lepton number through improved measurements of parity-violating Møller scattering at the MOLLER experiment and through improved determinations of \(a_e\).
