# Relationships between two--particle overlap functions and the two--body density matrix for many-fermion systems

> Relationships are obtained connecting the two-nucleon overlap function of the eigenstates in the (A-2) particle system with the asymptotic behavior of the two-body density matrix for the ground state of the A-particle system.This makes it possible to calculate the two-body overlap functions, spectroscopic factors and separation energies on the basis of a realistic two-body density matrix. The procedure can be used in describing the (e,e'NN) and (γ, NN) reactions where the two--body overlap functions are a key ingredient in the analysis.

## Key claim

The paper derives a formal relationship connecting the two-nucleon overlap function for bound states of the (A-2) system to the asymptotic behavior of the two-body density matrix of the A-particle ground state, but the method is explicitly limited to cases where the two nucleons are like nucleons (neutrons or protons) unbound to the rest of the system and where a single term (the state with the smallest two-nucleon separation energy) dominates the asymptotic sum.

## Method

The authors start from the definition of the two-body density matrix and the two-nucleon overlap function for a many-fermion system. They use the known hyperspherical asymptotic form of the two-body overlap function for two like nucleons unbound to the core, which decays exponentially with a factor determined by the two-nucleon separation energy. By considering the diagonal partial-wave decomposition of the two-body density matrix in relative and center-of-mass coordinates, they show that at large distances a single term dominates, allowing the overlap function to be expressed as the ratio of the density matrix element to the known asymptotic form. The constant k in the exponential defines the two-nucleon separation energy, and the norm of the extracted overlap function gives the two-particle spectroscopic factor.

## Key numbers

- The two-body density matrix is trace-normalized to A(A-1)/2, where A is the number of fermions in the ground state.
- The natural geminal occupation numbers lambda_alpha^(2) obey the inequalities: 0 <= lambda_i^(2) <= (A-1)/2 for odd A, and 0 <= lambda_i^(2) <= A/2 for even A.
- The asymptotic form of the two-body overlap function for two like nucleons unbound to the core is proportional to exp{-sqrt[(4m|E|/hbar^2)(R^2 + r^2/4)]} times (R^2 + r^2/4)^(-5/2), where E is the two-nucleon separation energy, m is the nucleon mass, r is the relative coordinate, and R is the center-of-mass coordinate.
- The two-particle spectroscopic factor S_alpha^(2) is bounded by the maximal natural geminal occupation number: S_alpha^(2) <= lambda_max^(2).

## Context

This work extends the earlier method of Van Neck et al. (1993) and Stoitsov et al. (1996) that related one-body overlap functions to the one-body density matrix, now generalizing to the two-body sector. The procedure is intended for use in analyzing electromagnetically induced two-nucleon knockout reactions such as (e,e'NN) and (gamma,NN), where two-body overlap functions are a key ingredient. The authors note that future work will apply the method using Jastrow-type density matrices from Stoitsov et al. (1993, 1995) for actual calculations of two-particle overlap functions.
