# Factorized distorted wave calculations for electron, neutrino, and BSM processes

> We point out that, under certain conditions, the nuclear currents that couple to vector bosons can be written as the trace of the product of two matrices. One contains `nucleon dynamics', e.g. form factors, the other contains the overlaps of nuclear wavefunctions. This factorized form may always be obtained and viewed as a `local' approximation, in which particle four-momenta that enter in the transition operator are fixed to their asymptotic values. We write the overlap matrix in a general form in terms of Dirac matrices. The current with arbitrary couplings to the nucleon can then be evaluated using standard trace identities. We show that this encompasses non-relativistic models as well. We have tabulated overlaps obtained in the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation, which can be used to compute the single-nucleon knockout cross section. We give a self-contained overview of the formulae. We discuss some properties of the overlap matrices which may be derived from general principles, and highlight differences with the commonly used plane-wave impulse approximation. The factorized form is attractive for (neutrino) event generators: it abstracts away the nuclear model and allows to easily modify couplings to the nucleon. This allows to consistently treat electron, neutrino and beyond Standard Model (BSM) processes.

## Key claim

The authors show that, under the assumption that the bilinear operator coupling to the nucleon does not depend on position, the hadron current for single-nucleon knockout can be written as the trace of a product of two matrices, one containing nucleon form factors and the other containing nuclear wavefunction overlaps, thereby achieving a factorized form that abstracts away the nuclear model while retaining final-state interactions, unlike the plane-wave impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors derive a factorized form of the hadron current in the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) by writing the current as the trace of the product of a vertex matrix (containing nucleon form factors) and an overlap matrix constructed from bound-state and scattering-state Dirac spinors. They decompose the overlap matrix into scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor components using a Fierz-like expansion, allowing analytic evaluation of the current for arbitrary couplings via standard trace identities. They tabulate these overlap components for carbon and oxygen using the Energy-Dependent Relativistic Mean Field (EDRMF) potential, and provide a code to compute cross sections for electron and charged-current neutrino interactions.

## Key numbers

* The overlap matrices become negligible for missing momenta greater than approximately 600 MeV, providing a practical cutoff for tabulation.
* The missing energy for the example phase-space calculation is given as E_kappa = 16 MeV, used to illustrate kinematic boundaries.
* The approximate position of the quasielastic peak is given as Q^2_QE = 2 M_N omega, where M_N is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA approach previously applied to neutrino-induced single-nucleon knockout by Gonzalez-Jimenez (2013, 2021), Franco-Patino (2022), and Nikolakopoulos (2022, 2024), and recently adopted by the NEUT event generator (McKean, 2025). Unlike the PWIA (Boffi, 1993), which neglects final-state interactions, the factorized form presented here retains FSI while offering the same flexibility as the PWIA to modify nucleon form factors or include beyond-Standard-Model couplings. The formalism enables consistent treatment of electron, neutrino, and BSM processes in event generators, and the authors plan to extend it to include two-body currents and single-pion production.
