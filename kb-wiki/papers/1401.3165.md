# Pion-assisted Nucleon-Delta and Delta-Delta dibaryons

> N-Delta and Delta-Delta dibaryon candidates are discussed and related quark-based calculations are reviewed. New hadronic calculations of L=0 nonstrange dibaryon candidates are reported. For N-Delta, I(JP)=1(2+) and 2(1+) S-matrix poles slightly below threshold are found by solving pi-N-N Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics, and for Delta-Delta several S-matrix poles below threshold are found by solving pi-N-Delta Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics in which the N-Delta interaction is dominated by the 1(2+) and 2(1+) resonating channels. In particular, the I(JP)=0(3+) Delta-Delta dibaryon candidate D_{03}(2370) observed recently by the WASA@COSY Collaboration is naturally explained in terms of long-range physics dominated by pions, nucleons and Delta's. These calculations are so far the only ones to reproduce the relatively small width, approximately 70 MeV, of D_{03}(2370). Predictions are also made for the location and width of D_{30}, the I(JP)=3(0+) exotic partner of D_{03}(2370).

# Pion-assisted Nucleon-Delta and Delta-Delta dibaryons

## Key claim
The authors report that the I(JP)=0(3+) Delta-Delta dibaryon candidate D_{03}(2370) observed by the WASA@COSY Collaboration can be naturally explained by long-range hadronic physics involving pions, nucleons, and Delta's, without invoking quark-based short-range interactions. Their calculations are the only ones so far to reproduce the relatively small width of approximately 70 MeV for this state, though the results depend on specific choices of form factors and on a quantum-statistics suppression factor applied to the Delta decay width.

## Method
The authors solved Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics for the pi-N-N three-body system to find N-Delta S-matrix poles, and for the pi-N-Delta three-body system to find Delta-Delta S-matrix poles. For the N-Delta case, the pi-N subsystem was dominated by the P33 Delta(1232) resonance channel and the NN subsystem by the 3S1 and 1S0 channels. For the Delta-Delta case, a three-body pi-N-Delta' model was used where Delta' is a stable Delta(1232) and the N-Delta' interaction was dominated by the D_{12} dibaryon, fitted to the NN 1D2 T-matrix with a momentum-space soft cutoff Lambda less than or approximately 3 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers
- D_{12} (N-Delta) S-matrix pole: 2147 - i60 MeV (large form factor) and 2159 - i70 MeV (small form factor), compared to phenomenological values of 2148 - i63 MeV (Arndt 1987) and 2144 - i55 MeV (Hoshizaki 1992)
- D_{21} (N-Delta) S-matrix pole: 2165 - i64 MeV (large form factor) and 2169 - i69 MeV (small form factor)
- D_{03} (Delta-Delta) S-matrix pole: 2383 - i47 MeV (large form factor) and 2342 - i31 MeV (small form factor) using the full Delta width of 118 MeV; 2383 - i41 MeV and 2343 - i24 MeV with the 2/3 width-suppression factor
- D_{30} (Delta-Delta) S-matrix pole: 2412 - i49 MeV (large form factor) and 2370 - i30 MeV (small form factor) using the full Delta width; 2411 - i41 MeV and 2370 - i22 MeV with the 2/3 width-suppression factor
- The averaged D_{03} mass and width: 2363 - i39 MeV (full width) and 2363 - i33 MeV (suppressed width)
- The averaged D_{30} mass and width: 2391 - i39 MeV (full width) and 2390 - i32 MeV (suppressed width)
- The Delta(1232) S-matrix pole used as input: 1211 - i49.5 MeV
- The D_{30} is located about 30 MeV above D_{03}

## Context
This work extends the 1964 Dyson-Xuong SU(6)-based classification of nonstrange dibaryons, which predicted D_{12} and D_{03} masses of 2.16 GeV and 2.35 GeV respectively, using a hadronic model rather than quark-based approaches. It contrasts with quark-based calculations by Muldwijk (1980), Oka (1980), Maltman (1985), Goldman (1989), and Salam (2002) that gave a broad range of D_{03} masses from 2.20 to 2.46 GeV, none of which simultaneously reproduced both D_{12} and D_{03} correctly. The calculations enable predictions for the exotic I=3 D_{30} partner, recently highlighted by Bashkanov, Brodsky, and Clement (2013), and demonstrate that hidden-color 6-quark configurations, which those authors emphasized, play a marginal role (enhancing binding by only 15 plus or minus 5 MeV according to recent quark-based calculations).
