# Are hyperon resonances required in the elementary $K^+Λ$ photoproduction?

> We have investigated the role of hyperon resonances in the kaon photoproduction process, $γp\to K^+Λ$, by using a covariant isobar model. To this end, new experimental data are used in the fitting process, whereas the old SAPHIR 1998 data are also used for comparison. The result indicates that the $Λ(1600)P_{01}$ and $Λ(1810)P_{01}$ hyperon resonances can significantly reduce the $χ^2$ and, simultaneously, can increase the hadronic form factor cut-off in the background terms. This finding is different from the result of the previous studies, which showed that the $Λ(1800)S_{01}$ was important for this purpose, instead of the $Λ(1600)P_{01}$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the hyperon resonances $\Lambda(1600)P_{01}$ and $\Lambda(1810)P_{01}$ are required to simultaneously reduce the $\chi^2$ and keep the hadronic form factor cut-off reasonably hard, which is partly different from previous studies that identified the $\Lambda(1800)S_{01}$ as the important resonance instead of the $\Lambda(1600)P_{01}$.

## Method

The authors used a covariant isobar model at tree level to describe the $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ reaction, including Born terms, nucleon resonances up to spin 3/2, vector mesons $K^*(892)$ and $K_1(1270)$ in the $t$-channel, and hyperon resonances in the $u$-channel. They fitted the model to both the old SAPHIR 1998 data and new experimental data (over 3500 data points) using a dipole-type hadronic form factor, then systematically excluded individual hyperon resonances to evaluate their contribution to reducing $\chi^2$ and maintaining a hard form factor cut-off.

## Key numbers

- The standard configuration fitted to SAPHIR data (Model A) gave $\chi^2/N = 8.11$; adding $N(1895)D_{13}$ reduced this to 4.11, while adding $\Lambda(1800)S_{01}$ and $\Lambda(1810)P_{01}$ gave 4.77
- The standard configuration fitted to new data (Model B) gave $\chi^2/N = 14.05$; adding $N(1895)D_{13}$ reduced this to 6.12, while adding $\Lambda(1800)S_{01}$ and $\Lambda(1810)P_{01}$ gave 9.18
- The hadronic form factor cut-off obtained for Model A was $\Lambda_{\rm had}=1.90$ GeV and for Model B was $\Lambda_{\rm had}=1.60$ GeV
- Including $\Lambda(1600)P_{01}$ in addition to $\Lambda(1800)S_{01}$ and $\Lambda(1810)P_{01}$ reduced $\chi^2/N$ from 2.57 to 2.49, with background cut-off 1.055 GeV and resonance cut-off 1.385 GeV
- A model without hyperon resonances gave best $\chi^2/N = 3.34$ with hadronic cut-off 0.769 GeV

## Context

This work extends the isobar model studies of Mart and collaborators (2003) and Janssen and collaborators (2002) on $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction, specifically revisiting the claim that $\Lambda(1800)S_{01}$ and $\Lambda(1810)P_{01}$ hyperon resonances could suppress diverging Born terms while keeping form factors hard. The new finding that $\Lambda(1600)P_{01}$ is important instead of $\Lambda(1800)S_{01}$ arises from the use of much larger modern datasets from CLAS. The result enables more reliable extraction of hyperon resonance parameters from photoproduction data and provides constraints for coupled-channels analyses of kaon-hyperon final states.
