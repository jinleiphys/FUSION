# Phase-transition-induced collapse of proto-compact stars and its implication for supernova explosions

> A hadron-quark phase transition (PT) may trigger supernova explosions during stellar core collapse. However, both success and failure have occurred in previous attempts to explode dying stars via this mechanism. We systematically explore the outcomes of the PT-induced collapse of mock proto-compact stars (PCSs) with a constant entropy and lepton fraction, with spherically symmetric general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations and a controlled series of hybrid equations of state. Our results reveal the qualitative dependence of successful and failed explosions on the PT and quark matter characteristics. A small portion ($\sim\!0.04\%\!-\!1\%$) of the released binding energy $ΔE_B$ transforms into the diagnostic explosion energy $E_{\rm exp,diag}$, which saturates at $\sim\!6\times10^{51}$ erg near the black hole formation. Note that our $E_{\rm exp,diag}$ represents an upper limit of the final explosion energies in realistic supernova simulations. We draw the phase diagrams indicative of the possible fates of supernova explosions driven by hadron-quark PTs, where the control parameters are the onset density, energy gap of the PT, and the quark matter speed of sound. Our findings can guide further self-consistent investigations on PT-driven core-collapse supernovae and help identify hadron-quark PT-induced PCS collapse from future observations.

## Key claim

The paper systematically maps how the characteristics of a hadron-quark phase transition (PT) determine whether a supernova explosion succeeds or fails, finding that only a small portion (approximately 0.04% to 1%) of the released binding energy converts into diagnostic explosion energy, which saturates at about 6 x 10^51 erg near black hole formation. The authors emphasize that their diagnostic explosion energy represents an upper limit for realistic supernova simulations.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of mock proto-compact stars (PCSs) with constant entropy and lepton fraction, using the GR1D code. They constructed a controlled series of hybrid equations of state using the constant-sound-speed (CSS) parameterization for quark matter with Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark mixed phase, based on the SFHo relativistic mean field model at entropy s = 4 k_B per baryon and electron lepton fraction Y_L = 0.3.

## Key numbers

- Diagnostic explosion energy E_exp,diag saturates at approximately 6 x 10^51 erg near black hole formation
- Transformation efficiency from released binding energy to explosion energy ranges from approximately 0.05% to 0.9%
- The onset density for phase transition was varied from 0.8 rho_0 to 1.2 rho_0, where rho_0 = 2.6 x 10^14 g cm^-3
- For the representative model with rho_on = 1.2 rho_0, Delta_epsilon/epsilon_on = 0.6, and c^2_qm = 2/3 c^2, the diagnostic explosion energy was approximately 1.0 x 10^51 erg
- The maximum diagnostic explosion energy for entropy s = 3 k_B per baryon was approximately 5 x 10^51 erg
- Black hole formation occurs when central density exceeds approximately 15-20 rho_0 and central lapse function drops below a few 10^-2
- The critical onset density above which prompt black hole formation is inevitable is approximately 2.5 rho_0 (corresponding to M_on approximately 2.15 solar masses)

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to model PT-driven supernova explosions, which have shown both success (Sagert 2009, Fischer 2018) and failure (Jakobus 2022 found only 2 successful explosions out of 97 simulations). The study provides a systematic framework for understanding why previous simulations gave conflicting results, and enables future self-consistent investigations by providing phase diagrams that map explosion outcomes to PT parameters. The results are compared to detailed simulations by Kuroda (2024), finding compatibility after accounting for differences in equation of state treatment and progenitor envelope effects.
