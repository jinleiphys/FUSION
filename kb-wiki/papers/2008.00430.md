# Chiral three-nucleon force and continuum for dripline nuclei and beyond

> Three-nucleon force and continuum play important roles in reproducing the properties of atomic nuclei around driplines. Therefore it is valuable to build up a theoretical framework where both effects can be taken into account to solve the nuclear Schrödinger equation. To this end, in this letter, we have expressed the chiral three-nucleon force within the continuum Berggren representation, so that bound, resonant and continuum states can be treated on an equal footing in the complex-momentum space. To reduce the model dimension and computational cost, the three-nucleon force is truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level and limited in the $sd$-shell model space, with the residual three-body term being neglected. We choose neutron-rich oxygen isotopes as the test ground because they have been well studied experimentally, with the neutron dripline determined. The calculations have been carried out within the Gamow shell model. The quality of our results in reproducing the properties of oxygen isotopes around the neutron dripline shows the relevance of the interplay between three-nucleon force and the coupling to continuum states. We also analyze the role played by the chiral three-nucleon force, by dissecting the contributions of the $2π$ exchange, $1π$ exchange and contact terms.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including both a chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) and continuum coupling within the Gamow shell model (GSM) is necessary to reproduce the properties of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes around the neutron dripline, but the 3NF is truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level and limited to the sd-shell model space, with the residual three-body term neglected.

## Method

The authors expressed the chiral N2LO three-nucleon force in the complex-momentum Berggren representation, enabling bound, resonant, and continuum states to be treated on equal footing. They performed Gamow shell model calculations for neutron-rich oxygen isotopes using a N3LO two-nucleon force and N2LO three-nucleon force, with 16O as the inert core and a model space consisting of {0d5/2, 1s1/2, 0d3/2, d3/2-continuum}. The 3NF was reduced to a normal-ordered two-body form, and the effective Hamiltonian was derived using the Q-box folded-diagram method extended to the complex-momentum Berggren space.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon potential gives bound 0d5/2 and 1s1/2 orbits at energies -5.31 MeV and -3.22 MeV, respectively, and a resonant 0d3/2 orbit with energy 1.06 - 0.09i MeV.
- The calculated two-neutron separation energy for 24O is 6.924 MeV with NN+3N, compared to the experimental value of 6.92 MeV.
- The calculated two-neutron separation energy for 25O is 3.259 MeV with NN+3N, compared to the experimental value of 3.453 MeV.
- The calculated two-neutron separation energy for 26O is -0.648 MeV with NN+3N, compared to the experimental value of -0.018 MeV.
- The 26O ground state resonance width is calculated as 15 keV, and a 2+ state is predicted with a resonance width of 97 keV.
- A second 2+ state in 26O is predicted at approximately 4 MeV with a width of 33 keV.

## Context

This work extends the Gamow shell model by incorporating a chiral three-nucleon force, building on prior GSM calculations with realistic two-nucleon forces by Sun (2017) and Tsukiyama (2009). It contrasts with conventional shell-model calculations by Otsuka (2010) that included 3NF but omitted continuum effects, and with continuum coupled-cluster calculations by Hagen (2012) that used a density-dependent effective 3NF. The results enable future studies of weakly-bound and unbound nuclei beyond the oxygen dripline, and the framework can be applied to other mass regions where both 3NF and continuum coupling are expected to be important.
