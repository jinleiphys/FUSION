# Dipole response in 208Pb within a self-consistent multiphonon approach

> Background: The electric dipole strength detected around the particle threshold and commonly associated to the pygmy dipole resonance offers a unique information on neutron skin and symmetry energy, and is of astrophysical interest. The nature of such a resonance is still under debate. Purpose: We intend to describe the giant and pygmy resonances in 208 Pb by enhancing their fragmentation with respect to the random-phase approximation. Method: We adopt the equation of motion phonon method to perform a fully self-consistent calculation in a space spanned by one-phonon and two-phonon basis states using an optimized chiral two-body potential. A phenomenological density dependent term, derived from a contact three-body force, is added in order to get single-particle spectra more realistic than the ones obtained by using the chiral potential only. The calculation takes into full account the Pauli principle and is free of spurious center of mass admixtures. Results: We obtain a fair description of the giant resonance and obtain a dense low-lying spectrum in qualitative agreement with the experimental data. The transition densities as well as the phonon and particle-hole composi- tion of the most strongly excited states support the pygmy nature of the low-lying resonance. Finally, we obtain realistic values for the dipole polarizability and the neutron skin radius. Conclusions: The results emphasize the role of the two-phonon states in enhancing the fragmentation of the strength in the giant resonance region and at low energy, consistently with experiments. For a more detailed agreement with the data, the calculation suggests the inclusion of the three-phonon states as well as a fine tuning of the single-particle spectrum to be obtained by a refinement of the nuclear potential.

## Key claim

The self-consistent multiphonon calculation, using an optimized chiral two-body potential plus a phenomenological density-dependent term, reproduces the giant dipole resonance (GDR) and generates a dense low-lying spectrum qualitatively consistent with experimental data, but the authors state that for a more detailed agreement the inclusion of three-phonon states and a refinement of the single-particle spectrum are required.

## Method

The authors adopt the equation of motion phonon method (EMPM) to perform a fully self-consistent calculation in a space spanned by one-phonon and two-phonon basis states, using an optimized chiral two-body potential (NNLO_opt). A phenomenological density-dependent term derived from a contact three-body force is added to obtain more realistic single-particle spectra. The calculation fully accounts for the Pauli principle and is free of spurious center-of-mass admixtures, and the Hamiltonian matrix is diagonalized in the multiphonon basis.

## Key numbers

- The ground state energy is depressed by ΔE = 7.3 MeV relative to the unperturbed Hartree-Fock state due to two-phonon coupling.
- Two-phonon components account for approximately 24% of the ground state wavefunction.
- The energy-weighted sum rule m_1 overestimates the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule by a factor of approximately 1.7.
- The calculation yields about 20 1^- levels between approximately 4.5 MeV and approximately 7 MeV, compared to the experimentally detected levels.
- The coupling constant for the density-dependent potential is set to C_ρ ~ 2000 MeV fm^6, as suggested by the dipole response.
- The truncated two-phonon basis contains approximately 2,500 correlated orthonormal 0^+ states and approximately 6,000 correlated orthonormal 1^- states.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous EMPM calculations on 132Sn (Knapp 2014) and an earlier study on 208Pb (Bianco 2012) that used a modified oscillator basis and a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the CD-Bonn potential. The calculation is compared to experimental data from (p,p') measurements (Tamii 2011, Poltoratska 2014), (γ,γ') and (n,γ) experiments (Ryez 2002, Enders 2003, Shizuma 2008, Schwengner 2010), and photo-absorption experiments (Veyss 1970, Schel 1988). The results emphasize the role of two-phonon states in enhancing fragmentation of the dipole strength, and the approach enables calculation of the dipole polarizability and neutron skin radius, which are linked to the symmetry energy and have astrophysical relevance.
