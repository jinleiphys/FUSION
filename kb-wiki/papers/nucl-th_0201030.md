# Semimicroscopical description of the simplest photonuclear reactions accompanied by excitation of the giant dipole resonance in medium-heavy mass nuclei

> A semimicroscopical approach is applied to describe photoabsorption and partial photonucleon reactions accompanied by the excitation of the giant dipole resonance (GDR). The approach is based on the continuum-RPA (CRPA) with a phenomenological description for the spreading effect. The phenomenological isoscalar part of the nuclear mean field, momentum-independent Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction, and separable momentum-dependent forces are used as input quantities for the CRPA calculations. The experimental photoabsorption and partial $(n,γ)$-reaction cross sections in the vicinity of the GDR are satisfactorily described for $^{89}$Y, $^{140}$Ce and $^{208}$Pb target nuclei. The total direct-neutron-decay branching ratio for the GDR in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb is also evaluated.

# Semimicroscopical description of the simplest photonuclear reactions accompanied by excitation of the giant dipole resonance in medium-heavy mass nuclei

## Key claim

The semimicroscopical continuum-RPA approach, augmented with phenomenological spreading and separable momentum-dependent forces, can satisfactorily describe both photoabsorption and partial photonucleon reaction cross sections in the giant dipole resonance region for medium-heavy nuclei, but only after introducing two adjustable parameters (the isovector momentum-dependent force strength κ' and the spreading width parameter α) to reproduce the experimental GDR energy, total width, and integrated cross section.

## Method

The authors solve continuum-RPA equations using a phenomenological isoscalar mean field (Woods-Saxon plus spin-orbit), a momentum-independent Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction with F' = 300 MeV·fm³, and separable momentum-dependent forces parameterized by isoscalar (κ₀) and isovector (κ') strengths. Spreading effects are included by replacing the photon energy ω with ω + iI(ω)/2, where I(ω) is an energy-dependent smearing parameter of the form I(ω) = α(ω − Δ)²/[1 + (ω − Δ)²/B²] with Δ = 3 MeV and B = 7 MeV fixed, and α adjusted to reproduce the GDR total width. Partial (n,γ) reaction cross sections are calculated from the same reaction amplitudes via detailed balance, multiplied by experimental spectroscopic factors for the final single-particle states.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb: the total direct-neutron-decay branching ratio for the GDR is calculated as b = 14% (sum over partial branching ratios b_μ to single-hole states in ²⁰⁷Pb, using unit spectroscopic factors).
- For ⁴⁸Ca: the calculated total direct-neutron-decay branching ratio is b = 27%, compared with the experimental value b_exp = 39 ± 5% from (e,e'n) reaction data (Str00).
- The integrated photoabsorption cross section is related to the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule as σ_int = (1 − κ₀)(1 + κ') σ_int_TRK, where σ_int_TRK = 60·NZ/A MeV·mb.
- The nuclear mean field uses Woods-Saxon parameters r₀ = 1.24 fm, a = 0.63 fm, and spin-orbit strength U_so = 13.9(1 + 2(N−Z)/A) MeV·fm².
- The Landau-Migdal parameter is f' = 1.0, giving F' = 300 MeV·fm³.
- Two values of the effective mass are tested: m* = m and m* = 0.9 m.

## Context

This work extends the earlier semimicroscopical approach of Chek94 and Rod00 by introducing a simpler energy-dependent smearing parameter for the spreading effect, including the isoscalar part of momentum-dependent forces via an effective nucleon mass, and calculating photonucleon reaction cross sections for multiple nuclei (⁸⁹Y, ¹⁴⁰Ce, ²⁰⁸Pb). It contrasts with the phenomenological direct+semidirect capture (DSD) model of Brown and the Ljubljana group (Lik98), which uses a large imaginary part of the GDR form factor without clear physical origin. The results enable predictions for partial (γ,n) reaction cross sections at new facilities like SPring-8 and provide a framework for analyzing direct-nucleon-decay branching ratios from (e,e'n) experiments.
