# M1 dipole strength from projected generator coordinate method calculations in the sd-shell valence space

> The low-energy enhancement observed in the deexcitation $γ$-ray strength functions, attributed to magnetic dipole (M1) radiations, has spurred theoretical efforts to improve on its description. Among the most widely used approaches are the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and its extensions. However, these methods often struggle to reproduce the correct behavior of the M1 strength at the lowest $γ$ energies. An alternative framework, the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM), offers significant advantages over QRPA by restoring broken symmetries and incorporating both vibrational and rotational dynamics within a unified description. Due to these features, PGCM has been proposed as a promising tool to study the low-energy M1 strength function in atomic nuclei. However, comprehensive investigations employing this method are lacking. The PGCM is presently used within the frame of sd-shell valence space calculations based on the USDB shell-model interaction to benchmark its performance against the solutions obtained via exact diagonalization. The reliability of two different sets of generator coordinates in the PGCM calculations is gauged using ${}^{24}$Mg as a test case. The ability of the PGCM to reproduce results from exact diagonalization in the sd valence space is demonstrated for $1^{+}$ states and M1 transitions. Future work will need to assess whether the proposed method can be applied systematically and extended to large-scale calculations while maintaining a reasonable computational cost.

# M1 dipole strength from projected generator coordinate method calculations in the sd-shell valence space

## Key claim
The projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) can reproduce the exact shell-model M1 strength function and level density up to about 20 MeV excitation energy in sd-shell nuclei, but only when the set of generator coordinates is chosen appropriately; the method's success is demonstrated specifically within the restricted sd-shell valence space using the USDB interaction, and the authors caution that future work must assess whether the approach can be extended to large-scale calculations at reasonable computational cost.

## Method
The authors performed PGCM calculations in the sd-shell valence space (0d5/2, 1s1/2, 0d3/2 orbitals) using the USDB effective two-body interaction, with 16O as an inert core. Two distinct sets of generator coordinates were tested on 24Mg: Set A used the M10 and M11 components of the magnetic dipole operator plus the isovector Jx operator as constraints, with standard HFB minimization followed by projection after variation; Set B used isoscalar and isovector proton-neutron pairing amplitudes plus the isoscalar Jx operator, with variation after particle-number projection followed by projection after variation. The PGCM results were benchmarked against exact shell-model diagonalization using the j-coupled code NATHAN, comparing excitation energies, B(M1) transition strengths, magnetic dipole moments, and occupation numbers for 1+ states up to approximately 23 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers
- The total B(M1) strength carried by the first 100 1+ states in 24Mg from exact diagonalization is 1.38 μN^2, amounting to about 98% of the total strength (1.41 μN^2).
- PGCM total B(M1) strength is 1.43 μN^2 (Set A) and 1.39 μN^2 (Set B), compared to the exact value of 1.38 μN^2.
- The centroid of the B(M1) distribution is 12.02 MeV (Set A), 12.39 MeV (Set B), and 12.10 MeV (exact); the widths are 3.06 MeV, 3.26 MeV, and 3.08 MeV respectively.
- Root mean square deviation of B(M1) values between PGCM and exact diagonalization reaches as low as 0.011 μN^2 (Set A) and 0.019 μN^2 (Set B) when using the largest sets of constrained states.
- The PGCM ground-state energies are -86.88 MeV (Set A) and -87.08 MeV (Set B), compared to -87.10 MeV from exact diagonalization.
- The largest number of natural basis states usable before linear dependencies appear is 240 (out of 440) for Jπ=0+ and 800 (out of 1320) for Jπ=1+ with Set A; with Set B, 320 (out of 342) for Jπ=0+ and 890 (out of 1026) for Jπ=1+.

## Context
This work extends the benchmark study of Frosini (2023), which showed that finite-temperature QRPA fails to describe the low-energy enhancement of M1 strength compared to exact shell-model results. The PGCM is proposed as an alternative that overcomes QRPA limitations including the harmonic approximation, incomplete symmetry restoration, and the quasi-boson approximation. The method has been used previously for low-lying collective states and giant resonances (Kimura 2017, Porro 2024), but this is the first comprehensive investigation of M1 transitions with PGCM. The results enable future application of PGCM to systematic calculations of M1 strength functions in heavier nuclei, though the authors note that computational cost remains a challenge for large-scale implementations.
