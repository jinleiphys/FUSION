# Entrainment parameters in cold superfluid neutron star core

> Hydrodynamical simulations of neutron star cores, based on a two fluid description in terms of a neutron-proton superfluid mixture, require the knowledge of the Andreev-Bashkin entrainment matrix which relates the momentum of one constituent to the currents of both constituents. This matrix is derived for arbitrary nuclear asymmetry at zero temperature and in the limits of small relative currents in the framework of the energy density functional theory. The Skyrme energy density functional is considered as a particular case. General analytic formulae for the entrainment parameters and various corresponding effective masses are obtained. These formulae are applied to the liquid core of a neutron star, composed of an homogeneous plasma of nucleons, electrons and possibly muons in beta equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper derives general analytic formulae for the Andreev-Bashkin entrainment matrix in cold superfluid neutron star cores using energy density functional theory, valid for arbitrary nuclear asymmetry at zero temperature and in the limit of small relative currents. The authors show that the entrainment parameters are well-behaved functions of nucleon densities when stability constraints are imposed, contrary to earlier assumptions that these parameters could be treated as constants.

## Method

The authors employ the Landau Fermi liquid theory framework to relate the entrainment matrix to the ℓ=1 Landau parameters, which they then compute from the second functional derivatives of a Skyrme-type energy density functional. They derive explicit analytic expressions for the entrainment matrix elements ρ_{qq'} and three distinct types of effective masses (Landau effective masses m_q^⊕, "sharp" effective masses m_♯^q, and "star" effective masses m_⋆^q) in terms of the coefficients of the energy density functional. The formalism is applied to neutron star matter in beta equilibrium using the SLy4 Skyrme force, with the composition determined by charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium conditions including electrons and muons.

## Key numbers

- The bottom edge of the crust corresponds to baryon density n_edge ≃ 0.076 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear saturation density n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The density domain considered is n_edge < n_b < 3 n_s
- Muons appear when electron chemical potential exceeds m_μ c^2 ≃ 105 MeV, occurring at baryon density n_b ≃ 0.12 fm^{-3}
- For the 27 Skyrme forces surveyed, the coefficient α̃_np ranges from 0 (SkT4, SkT5 parametrisations) down to -10.4168 (in units m_p^{-1}·fm^{-3}) for the SV parametrisation
- The SLy4 Skyrme force yields α̃_np ∼ -1.5, except SLy230a with α̃_np = -0.007360

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Comer et al. (2003) who used a minimal relativistic σ-ω mean field model without nonlinear meson couplings, and generalizes the results of Blaizot and Haensel (1981) for asymmetric nuclear matter to any energy density functional of the Skyrme type. The paper provides the first application of non-relativistic mean field models to calculate the mass density matrix for neutron star cores, enabling more realistic hydrodynamical simulations of oscillation modes and glitch events in neutron stars. The authors explicitly show that the common practice of treating the entrainment parameter ε or the dimensionless parameters ε_q as constants (as done by Lindblom (2000), Lee (2003), and Yoshida (2003)) is not justified, since these parameters vary with density according to the derived analytic formulae.
