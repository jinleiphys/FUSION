# Cooling of Neutron Stars Revisited: Application of Low Energy Theorems

> Cooling of neutrons stars proceeds mainly via neutrino emission; As an example we study the modified neutrino bremsstrahlung process $nn \to nnν\barν.$ The radiated energy is small compared to other scales in the system. Hence one can use low-energy theorems to compute the neutrino emissivity in terms of the non-radiative process, ie the on-shell $T$ matrix. We find that the use of the $T-$matrix as compared to previous estimates based upon one pion exchange leads a substantial reduction of the predicted emissivity.

## Key claim

The authors use low-energy theorems to compute the neutrino emissivity from the modified bremsstrahlung process \(nn \to nn\nu\bar{\nu}\) in terms of the on-shell \(T\)-matrix, and find that using the full \(T\)-matrix instead of the one-pion-exchange (OPE) approximation reduces the predicted emissivity by a factor of 4–5 for neutron matter at normal density.

## Method

The authors apply the soft-photon theorem, generalized to the weak axial-vector current, to express the leading-order bremsstrahlung amplitude in terms of the commutator of the on-shell NN \(T\)-matrix with the spin operator. They compute the cross section for weak nn bremsstrahlung in free space as a function of relative nn momentum, decomposing the \(T\)-matrix into central, spin-spin, tensor, spin-orbit, and quadratic spin-orbit components. The emissivity in medium is then obtained by convoluting the free-space matrix elements with Fermi-Dirac distributions, taking nucleon momenta equal to the Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

- The OPE exchange overestimates the full \(T\)-matrix result by about a factor of 5 for relative momenta appropriate for neutron matter at normal density (\(p_F = 1.3\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)).
- The ratio of the emissivity calculated with the full \(T\)-matrix to that from OPE (Friman and Maxwell) is about a factor of 4–5 reduction.
- The neutrino energy scale is \(\omega \approx T = 1\ \text{MeV}\).
- The axial coupling constants used are \(g_A^p = -g_A^n = -1.25\).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculations of Friman and Maxwell (1979), who computed neutrino bremsstrahlung in the soft-neutrino limit using Born approximation with a Landau-type interaction plus one-pion exchange. The authors address the accuracy of the Born approximation, the contribution of other mesons (such as \(\rho\) and \(\sigma\)), and relativistic effects. They find that the tensor force from OPE is largely canceled by \(\rho\)-exchange, bringing the sum closer to the full \(T\)-matrix result, consistent with findings by Hanhart, Phillips, and Reddy (2001). The work enables more reliable predictions for neutron star cooling by replacing the OPE approximation with an empirical \(T\)-matrix determined by phase shifts, and suggests future improvements including medium effects such as Pauli blocking, renormalization of the axial coupling, and dressing of Green functions.
