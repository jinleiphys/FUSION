# Anomalous Viscosity of an Expanding Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We argue that an expanding quark-gluon plasma has an anomalous viscosity, which arises from interactions with dynamically generated color fields. We derive an expression for the anomalous viscosity in the turbulent plasma domain and apply it to the hydrodynamic expansion phase, when the quark-gluon plasma is near equilibrium. The anomalous viscosity dominates over the collisional viscosity for weak coupling and not too late times. This effect may provide an explanation for the apparent ``nearly perfect'' liquidity of the matter produced in nuclear collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider without the assumption that it is a strongly coupled state.

## Key claim

An expanding quark-gluon plasma acquires an anomalous viscosity from interactions with dynamically generated color fields, which can dominate over collisional viscosity for weak coupling and not too late times; this mechanism may explain the nearly perfect fluid behavior observed at RHIC without requiring the plasma to be a strongly coupled state.

## Method

The authors generalize Dupree's turbulent plasma transport theory to a nonabelian plasma, starting from the Vlasov-Boltzmann equation for color charges in a color-magnetic field. They average particle trajectories over an ensemble of color-magnetic fields, derive a Fokker-Planck-type diffusion equation for the ensemble-averaged phase-space distribution, and take moments of the drift and diffusion terms with \(p_z^2\) to obtain an expression for the anomalous shear viscosity \(\eta_{\rm A}\). The result is then combined with the collisional viscosity \(\eta_{\rm C}\) via \(\eta^{-1} = \eta_{\rm A}^{-1} + \eta_{\rm C}^{-1}\) and solved self-consistently under the assumption that the turbulent field intensity scales linearly with the momentum anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative collisional shear viscosity is \(\eta_{\rm C} = d_f T^3 / (g^4 \ln g^{-1})\) with \(d_f \sim O(100)\) (leading logarithmic approximation, from Arnold (2000)).
- The postulated lower bound for viscosity is \(\eta_{\rm min} = s/4\pi\) (Kovtun (2004)).
- The anomalous viscosity self-consistency solution gives \(\eta_{\rm A} \propto s / g^{3/2}\), with a parametric dependence much weaker than \(\eta_{\rm C}\).
- The cross-over time \(\tau_c\) between anomalous and collisional regimes is estimated as \(\tau_c T(\tau_c) = 85.1 b_0 / (g^5 \ln^2(1/g))\) for \(n_f=2\) quark flavors (not shown in full text but referenced as inserted numerical values).
- The anisotropy in the local momentum distribution due to longitudinal expansion is given by \(2T_{zz}/(T_{xx}+T_{yy}) - 1 = - (8/T\tau) (\eta/s)\).

## Context

This work extends the theory of anomalous transport from electromagnetic plasmas (Dupree (1966, 1968); Abe and Niu (1980)) to the quark-gluon plasma, where previous studies of plasma instabilities (Mrowczynski (1988, 1993); Romatschke and Strickland (2003)) focused on the early, far-from-equilibrium stage. The paper provides an alternative to the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) interpretation of RHIC data (Shuryak (2004); Koch (2005); Liao (2005)), and it enables future numerical evaluation of the color-magnetic field correlation function to determine the anomalous viscosity quantitatively. The approach can also be extended to compute other transport properties, such as the radiative energy loss coefficient \(\hat{q}\) (Baier (2000)).
