# Equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and the tidal deformability of neutron star

> Neutron star (NS) is a unique astronomical compact object where the four fundamental interactions have been revealed from the observation and studied in different ways. While the macroscopic properties of NS like mass and radius can be determined within the General Relativity using a realistic equation of state (EOS) of NS matter, such an EOS is usually generated by a nuclear structure model like, e.g., the nuclear mean-field approach to asymmetric nuclear matter. Given the radius of NS extended to above 10 km and its mass up to twice the solar mass, NS is expected to be tidally deformed when it is embedded in a strong tidal field. Such a tidal effect was confirmed unambiguously in the gravitation wave signals detected recently by the LIGO and Virgo laser interferometers from GW170817, the first ever direct observation of a binary NS merger. A nonrelativistic mean-field study is carried out in the present work within the Hartree-Fock formalism to construct the EOS of NS matter, which is then used to determine the tidal deformability, gravitational mass, and radius of NS. The mean-field results are compared with the constraints imposed for these quantities by the global analysis of the observed GW170817 data, and a strong impact by the incompressibility of nuclear matter on the hydrostatic configuration of NS is shown.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock mean-field study shows that the nuclear incompressibility \(K\) has a strong impact on the hydrostatic configuration of neutron stars, and that the tidal deformability parameter \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star given by all six versions of the density-dependent NN interaction falls within the empirical 90% confidence level range inferred from GW170817, provided the interaction reproduces the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors performed a nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock calculation of asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature using six versions of the density-dependent M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction (CDM3Y3, CDM3Y4, CDM3Y5, CDM3Y6, CDM3Y8, and BDM3Y1), each with a different nuclear incompressibility \(K\) ranging from 217 to 270 MeV. The resulting equation of state was combined with the compressible liquid drop model for the neutron star crust and a relativistic Fermi gas model for electrons and muons to generate the \(\beta\)-stable np\(e\mu\) matter of the uniform core. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the linearized Einstein equation were then solved to determine the gravitational mass, radius, Love number, and tidal deformability of neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear incompressibility \(K\) values for the six interactions: 217 MeV (CDM3Y3), 228 MeV (CDM3Y4), 241 MeV (CDM3Y5), 252 MeV (CDM3Y6), 257 MeV (CDM3Y8), and 270 MeV (BDM3Y1), all at saturation density \(n_0 \approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Saturation binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter: \(E_0 \approx -15.8\) MeV at \(n_0 \approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{\rm sym}(n_0) \approx 30\) MeV, with slope parameter \(L \approx 50\) MeV for all six interactions.
- Tidal deformability parameter for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star: \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 142\) (CDM3Y3), 212 (CDM3Y4), 280 (CDM3Y5), 327 (CDM3Y6), 348 (CDM3Y8), and 407 (BDM3Y1); all within the empirical range \(\Lambda_{1.4} \approx 190^{+390}_{-120}\) at 90% confidence from GW170817.
- Maximum gravitational mass of neutron star: \(M = 1.55 M_\odot\) (CDM3Y3), 1.69 \(M_\odot\) (CDM3Y4), 1.83 \(M_\odot\) (CDM3Y5), 1.94 \(M_\odot\) (CDM3Y6), 1.97 \(M_\odot\) (CDM3Y8), and 2.12 \(M_\odot\) (BDM3Y1).
- Radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star: \(R_{1.4} = 10.6\) km (CDM3Y3), 11.2 km (CDM3Y4), 11.5 km (CDM3Y5), 11.7 km (CDM3Y6), 11.8 km (CDM3Y8), and 12.0 km (BDM3Y1).
- Pressure at twice saturation density: \(P(2n_0) = 2.9 \times 10^{34}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) (CDM3Y3) to \(4.2 \times 10^{34}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) (BDM3Y1), compared to empirical \(P(2n_0) \approx 3.5^{+2.7}_{-1.7} \times 10^{34}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) at 90% confidence.
- Pressure at six times saturation density: \(P(6n_0) = 3.7 \times 10^{35}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) (CDM3Y3) to \(9.6 \times 10^{35}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) (BDM3Y1), compared to empirical \(P(6n_0) \approx 9.0^{+7.9}_{-2.6} \times 10^{35}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) at 90% confidence.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic mean-field approach of Khoa et al. (1997, 2007, 2014) and Tan et al. (2016, 2020) by applying six versions of the density-dependent M3Y interaction to compute the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and the resulting neutron star properties. The results are compared with the constraints from the GW170817 gravitational wave event analyzed by Abbott et al. (2018) and with ab-initio calculations by Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) and Gandolfi et al. (2012). The study shows that the nuclear incompressibility \(K\) strongly influences the maximum mass, radius, and tidal deformability, and that interactions with \(K \gtrsim 252\) MeV are needed to reproduce the observed maximum neutron star mass of about \(2.01 M_\odot\) from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013) and the empirical pressure from GW170817.
