# Entanglement entropy of a color flux tube in (1+1)D Yang-Mills theory

> In recent work arxiv:2410.00112 , we computed a novel flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE$^2$) of the color flux tube stretched between a heavy quark-antiquark pair on a Euclidean lattice in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory. Our numerical results suggested that FTE$^2$ can be partitioned into an internal color entanglement entropy and a vibrational entropy corresponding to the transverse excitations of a QCD string, with the latter described by a thin string model. Since the color flux tube does not have transverse excitations in (1+1)D, we analytically compute the contribution of the internal color degrees of freedom to FTE$^2$ in this simpler framework. For the multipartite partitioning of the color flux tube, we find the remarkable result that FTE$^2$ only depends on the number of times the flux tube crosses the border between two spatial regions, and the dimension of the representation of the color group, but not on the string length. The result holds independently of whether the branching points are placed on the vertices of the lattice or in the center of plaquettes.

## Key claim

The paper analytically computes the flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE²) in (1+1)D Yang-Mills theory, finding that it depends only on the number of times the flux tube crosses the boundary between spatial regions and the dimension of the color group representation, but not on the string length; this result holds for both "cusp-on-vertex" and "cusp-in-plaquette" lattice discretizations, provided the coupling on special plaquettes is chosen consistently with the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors compute FTE² on a (1+1)D Euclidean lattice using the replica method, where the reduced density matrix for a spatial region V is obtained by partially tracing over gluon fields in the complement region. They evaluate Polyakov-loop correlators in the presence of static quark-antiquark sources by fixing a gauge where spatial links are set to unity, expressing temporal links through plaquette variables, and integrating over group elements using Haar measure identities. The calculation is performed for two distinct lattice discretizations: one where boundaries between replicas and spatial regions pass through lattice sites ("cusp-on-vertex"), and another where boundaries pass through plaquette centers ("cusp-in-plaquette"), with the latter requiring a modified gauge coupling on special q-sheeted plaquettes.

## Key numbers

* FTE² for a flux tube crossing F=2 boundaries (both sources in complement region): $\tilde{S}^{(q)}_{|Q\bar{Q}} = \log N_c^2$, where $N_c$ is the number of colors
* FTE² for a flux tube crossing F=1 boundary (one source in region V): $\tilde{S}^{(q)}_{Q|\bar{Q}} = \log N_c$
* For general F boundaries: $\tilde{S}^{(q)}_{|Q\bar{Q}} = F \cdot \log(\dim R_{Q\bar{Q}})$, where $\dim R_{Q\bar{Q}}$ is the dimension of the color representation
* The result is identical for both Rényi and von Neumann entanglement entropies for any number of replicas q
* FTE² is zero for all Abelian gauge theories, which have only one-dimensional representations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous numerical study of FTE² in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory (Amorosso, Syritsyn, Venugopalan 2024), where they conjectured that FTE² partitions into internal color entanglement and vibrational entropy from transverse string excitations. The (1+1)D calculation isolates the internal color contribution since transverse excitations are absent, confirming the conjecture that this term equals $F \cdot \log N_c$. The result connects to prior work on vacuum entanglement entropy in (1+1)D Yang-Mills by Gromov (2014) and Donnelly (2014), who observed an extra term $\propto F\log\dim R_b$ in the non-Abelian case relative to the Abelian case, arising from superselection sectors (surface charges). The calculation demonstrates that these surface charges are measurable through FTE², addressing a question raised by Donnelly (2014), and has implications for the dual-superconductor picture of confinement proposed by Mandelstam (1974) and 't Hooft (1981).
