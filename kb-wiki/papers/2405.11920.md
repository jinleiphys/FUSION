# Analysis of $^{115}$In $β$ decay through the spectral moment method

> We analyze the $^{115}$In $β$-decay energy spectrum through the spectral moment method (SMM), previously introduced in the context of $^{113}$Cd $β$ decay. The spectral moments $μ_n$ are defined as averaged $n^{\rm th}$ powers of the $β$ particle energy, characterizing the spectrum normalization ($n=0$) and shape ($n\geq 1$) above a given threshold. For $^{115}$In, we consider three independent datasets characterized by different thresholds. We also consider three nuclear model calculations with two free parameters: the ratio of axial-vector to vector couplings, $r=g_{\rm A}/g_{\rm V}$, and the small vector-like relativistic nuclear matrix element (NME), $s=s$-NME. By using the most recent of the three datasets, we show that the first few spectral moments can determine $(r,\, s)$ values in good agreement with those obtained by full-fledged experimental fits. We then work out the SMM results for the other datasets. We find that, although $g_{\rm A}$ quenching is generally favored, the preferred quenching factors may differ considerably depending on the chosen experimental data and nuclear models. We discuss various issues affecting both the overall normalization and the low-energy behaviour of the measured and computed spectra, and their joint effects on the experimentally quoted half-life values. Further $^{115}$In $β$-decay data at the lowest possible energy threshold appear to be crucial to clarify these issues.

## Key claim

The spectral moment method (SMM) can determine the free parameters (ratio of axial-vector to vector couplings \(r = g_{\rm A}/g_{\rm V}\) and the small vector-like relativistic nuclear matrix element \(s\)) for \(^{115}\)In beta decay in good agreement with full experimental fits, but the preferred quenching factors for \(g_{\rm A}\) differ considerably depending on which of three independent datasets and which nuclear model is used, and the low-energy behavior of the spectrum remains uncertain.

## Method

The authors applied the spectral moment method (SMM) to three independent experimental datasets of the \(^{115}\)In beta-decay energy spectrum (AC24, LE22, and PF79), each characterized by different energy thresholds. They computed the zeroth moment \(\mu_0\) (spectrum normalization above threshold) and higher moments \(\mu_n\) (shape parameters) from each dataset, then equated these experimental moments to theoretical moments calculated within three nuclear models (IBFM-2, MQPM, and ISM) as functions of the two free parameters \(r = g_{\rm A}/g_{\rm V}\) and \(s\) (the \(s\)-NME). The intersections of the conic sections (ellipse for \(\mu_0\), hyperbolas for \(\mu_n\)) in the \((r, s)\) plane were used to determine the best-fit parameter values without requiring full spectral fits or low-energy extrapolations.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{115}\)In beta-decay endpoint is \(Q = 497.489\) keV (Blachot 2012).
- For the AC24 dataset (threshold 80 keV): \(\mu_0^e = 3.18 \times 10^{-23}\) s\(^{-1}\), \(\mu_1^e = 2.43 \times 10^2\) keV, with quoted half-life \(t_{1/2} = 5.26 \times 10^{14}\) y (Pagnanini 2024).
- For the LE22 dataset (threshold 160 keV): \(\mu_0^e = 2.54 \times 10^{-23}\) s\(^{-1}\), \(\mu_1^e = 2.79 \times 10^2\) keV, with quoted half-life \(t_{1/2} = 5.18 \times 10^{14}\) y (Leder 2022).
- For the PF79 dataset (threshold 0 keV): \(\mu_0^e = 4.98 \times 10^{-23}\) s\(^{-1}\), \(\mu_1^e = 2.22 \times 10^2\) keV, with quoted half-life \(t_{1/2} = 4.41 \times 10^{14}\) y (Pfeiffer 1979).
- The PF79 half-life is roughly 15% lower than those quoted by AC24 and LE22.

## Context

This work extends the spectral moment method previously introduced by Kostensalo (2023) for \(^{113}\)Cd beta decay to the case of \(^{115}\)In, comparing three independent datasets from Pagnanini (2024), Leder (2022), and Pfeiffer (1979). The SMM approach complements the full spectrum-shape method (SSM) used by Haaranen (2016, 2017) and the enhanced SSM by Kumar (2020, 2021) and Kostensalo (2020), which required detailed experimental fits. The results highlight that further \(^{115}\)In beta-decay data at the lowest possible energy threshold are crucial to resolve ambiguities in the low-energy spectral shape and to clarify the quenching of \(g_{\rm A}\), which affects calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay rates.
