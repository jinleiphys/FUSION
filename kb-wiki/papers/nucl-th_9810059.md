# J/Psi Absorption Scenarios in Nuclear Collisions

> We study the production of $c \bar{c}$ pairs and dimuons from hard collisions in nuclear reactions within the covariant transport approach HSD. Adopting 6 mb for the $c \bar{c}$-baryon cross section the data on $J/Ψ$ suppression in p + A reactions are reproduced in line with calculations based on the Glauber model. Furthermore, using $J/Ψ$ absorption cross sections with mesons above the $D\bar{D}$ threshold in the order of 1.5 - 3 mb we find that all data on $J/Ψ$ suppression from NA38/NA50 can be described without assuming the formation of a quark-gluon plasma. Alternatively, we also investigate an 'early'-comover absorption scenario where the $c \bar{c}$ pairs are dissociated in the color electric fields of neighboring strings. Again we find good agreement with the experimental data with an estimate for the string radius of $R_s \approx 0.2-0.25 fm$.

# J/Psi Absorption Scenarios in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
All experimental data on J/Psi suppression from NA38/NA50 can be described by conventional hadronic absorption mechanisms, either late-stage absorption by mesonic comovers with cross sections of 1.5–3 mb, or early-stage dissociation by color electric fields of strings with radius ~0.2–0.25 fm, without requiring the formation of a quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic transport calculations using the covariant HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics) approach, propagating c-cbar pairs through nuclear collisions on an event-by-event basis. They modeled two scenarios: (I) a long-lived (10 fm/c) color-octet c-cbar-g Fock state that can be dissociated by baryons (6 mb) and mesons (0–1.5 mb), and (II) a short-lived (0.3 fm/c) pre-resonance state with subsequent J/Psi and Psi-prime absorption on baryons (3 mb) and mesons (0–3 mb). An alternative "early-comover" scenario treated dissociation of c-cbar pairs by the color electric fields of strings formed in the FRITIOF model, with the string radius Rs as a free parameter. Drell-Yan events were generated with PYTHIA 5.7 using GRV LO or MRS mode 43 structure functions, with K-factors of 2.0 and 1.6 respectively.

## Key numbers
- c-cbar-baryon dissociation cross section: 6 mb (with uncertainty ±1 mb), fixed from p+A data
- J/Psi absorption cross section on mesons (above D-Dbar threshold): 1.5–3 mb, varied as free parameter
- String radius Rs for best fit to Pb+Pb data: 0.2–0.25 fm (lower bound; adding c-cbar radius gives ~0.4–0.5 fm)
- In central Pb+Pb collisions with Rs = 0.2 fm, 40% of absorbed J/Psi's are dissociated by strings
- For p+U with Rs = 0.4 fm, only 2% of J/Psi's are absorbed by strings
- String constant sigma ≈ 2 GeV/fm; color electric field energy density ≈ 4 GeV/fm^3 for Rs ≈ 0.3 fm
- c-cbar-g lifetime: 10 fm/c (model I) or 0.3 fm/c (model II, following Kharzeev (1996))
- Drell-Yan K-factor: 2.0 for GRV LO structure functions, 1.6 for MRS mode 43 (NLO)

## Context
This work extends earlier Glauber-model analyses by Gavin and Vogt (1996) and Capella et al. (1996) that introduced mesonic comover absorption, but incorporates full space-time dynamics missing from those approaches. It directly challenges the interpretation of NA50 data as evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation, as argued by Matsui and Satz (1986) and later by Blaizot and Wong (1996). The string absorption scenario is motivated by earlier work of Loh et al. on c-cbar dissociation in color electric fields. The transport calculations supersede the initial analysis of Cassing et al. (1997) by including explicit Drell-Yan production and multiple absorption models. The results enable future discrimination between hadronic and QGP scenarios at RHIC and LHC energies, where string densities will be higher.
