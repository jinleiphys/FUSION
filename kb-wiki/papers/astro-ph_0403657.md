# Minimal Cooling of Neutron Stars: A New Paradigm

> A new classification of neutron star cooling scenarios, involving either ``minimal'' cooling or ``enhanced'' cooling is proposed. The minimal cooling scenario replaces and extends the so-called standard cooling scenario to include neutrino emission from the Cooper pair breaking and formation process. This emission dominates that due to the modified Urca process for temperatures close to the critical temperature for superfluid pairing. Minimal cooling is distinguished from enhanced cooling by the absence of neutrino emission from any direct Urca process, due either to nucleons or to exotica. Within the minimal cooling scenario, theoretical cooling models can be considered to be a four parameter family involving the equation of state of dense matter, superfluid properties of dense matter, the composition of the neutron star envelope, and the mass of the neutron star. Consequences of minimal cooling are explored through extensive variations of these parameters. Results are compared with the inferred properties of thermally-emitting neutron stars in order to ascertain if enhanced cooling occurs in any of them. All stars for which thermal emissions have been clearly detected are at least marginally consistent with the lack of enhanced cooling. The two pulsars PSR 0833-45 (Vela) and PSR 1706-44 would require enhanced cooling in case their ages and/or temperatures are on the lower side of their estimated values whereas the four stars PSR 0656+14, PSR 1055-52, Geminga, and RX J0720.4-3125 may require some source of internal heating in case their age and/or luminosity are on the upper side of their estimated values. The new upper limits on the thermal luminosity of PSR J0205+6449 and RX J0007.0+7302 are indicative of the occurrence of some enhanced neutrino emission beyond the minimal scenario.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "minimal cooling" paradigm for neutron stars that replaces the older "standard cooling" scenario by including neutrino emission from Cooper pair breaking and formation, which dominates over the modified Urca process at temperatures near the superfluid critical temperature. The authors find that all neutron stars with clearly detected thermal emissions are at least marginally consistent with the lack of enhanced cooling, given the combined uncertainties in ages and temperatures, but the upper limits on thermal luminosity for PSR J0205+6449 and RX J0007.0+7302 are indicative of some enhanced neutrino emission beyond the minimal scenario.

## Method

The authors constructed theoretical cooling models as a four-parameter family involving the equation of state of dense matter, superfluid properties, envelope composition, and neutron star mass. They solved the general relativistic equations of hydrostatic equilibrium (TOV equations) and thermal evolution using a Henyey-type stellar evolution code, employing four nonrelativistic potential model equations of state (WFF3, APR, BPAL21, BPAL31) that include only nucleonic degrees of freedom and forbid the direct Urca process. The resulting cooling curves were compared with inferred temperatures and luminosities from observations of thermally-emitting neutron stars, using both hydrogen atmosphere fits and blackbody fits.

## Key numbers

- The direct Urca process becomes possible in the APR model at baryon density n_B > 0.78 fm^{-3}, corresponding to a neutron star mass of M_cr = 1.97 M_sun
- The symmetry energies S_2(n_0) at equilibrium density are 29.5 MeV for WFF3 and 32.6 MeV for APR
- The equilibrium densities for WFF3 and APR are n_0 = 0.163 fm^{-3} and 0.16 fm^{-3}, with compression modulii of 269 MeV and 274 MeV respectively
- The critical proton fraction for direct Urca is of order 11% in matter with electrons only and 14% in matter with both electrons and muons
- The Vela pulsar (PSR 0833-45) has a kinematic age of log_10(t_kin) = 4.26^{+0.17}_{-0.31} years and log_10(L_inf) = 32.41-32.70 erg/s with hydrogen atmosphere fits
- The upper limit on thermal luminosity for PSR J0205+6449 (in 3C58) is log_10(L_inf) < 33.0 erg/s at a kinematic age of log_10(t_kin) = 2.91 years
- The upper limit on thermal luminosity for RX J0007.0+7302 (in CTA 1) is log_10(L_inf) < 32.3 erg/s at a kinematic age of log_10(t_kin) = 4.0-4.2 years

## Context

This work extends the earlier "standard cooling" scenario (as reviewed by Pizzochero 2002 and Prakash 1998) by incorporating Cooper pair breaking and formation neutrino emission, which was previously identified by Flowers, Ruderman, and Sutherland (1976) and Voskresensky and Senatorov (1987). The minimal cooling paradigm restricts the equation of state through the symmetry energy, since the direct Urca process requires the proton fraction to exceed a critical value (as shown by Lattimer, Pethick, Prakash, and Haensel 1991). The paper enables future work to determine whether enhanced cooling mechanisms (direct Urca processes involving nucleons, hyperons, Bose condensates, or deconfined quarks) are necessary to explain observations, and a follow-up paper exploring enhanced cooling is anticipated.
