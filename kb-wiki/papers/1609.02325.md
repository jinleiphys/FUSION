# B(E2) anomaly in $6^+$ isomers of $^{134-138}$Sn isotopes and neutron single-particle energies beyond N=82

> Isomeric studies in neutron-rich nuclei present a powerful tool to explore the structure at the nuclear extremes. We recently used the shell model calculations with Renormalized Charge Depen- dent Bonn (RCDB) effective interaction to calculate the properties of the $6^+$ seniority isomers in $^{134-138}$Sn in an attempt to resolve the anomalous B(E2) behavior of the $6^+$ isomer in $^{136}$Sn [Phys. Rev. C 91, 024321 (2015)]. We further explore these isomers by using the generalized seniority scheme for multi-j orbitals recently presented by us [Phys. Lett. B 753, 122 (2016)]; the B(E2) values so calculated reproduce the experimental data quite well, including the anomaly at $^{136}$Sn confirming the generalized seniority nature of the $6^+$ isomers. We then use the generalized seniority guided Large Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations, along with the latest single particle energies from Jones et al : [Nature (London) 465, 454 (2010)] and Allmond et al : [Phys. Rev. Lett. 112, 172701 (2014)] to estimate more accurate location of i$_{13/2}$ neutron orbital in the extreme neutron rich N = 82-126 region. This entails a new sub-shell closure at N = 112 due to the higher location of i$_{13/2}$ neutron orbital, also consistent with the choice of orbitals in the generalized seniority scheme. However, a small reduction in the f$_{7/2}$ two-body matrix elements is still required in the LSSM calculations to reproduce the experimental level energies as well as the transition probabilities in $^{134-138}$Sn isotopes in a consistent way.

# B(E2) anomaly in 6⁺ isomers of ¹³⁴–¹³⁸Sn isotopes and neutron single-particle energies beyond N=82

## Key claim

The B(E2) anomaly of the 6⁺ isomer in ¹³⁶Sn can be explained naturally by generalized seniority for multi-j orbitals without invoking core excitations, but a small reduction of 25 keV in the f₇/₂ two-body matrix elements is still required in large-scale shell-model calculations to reproduce both level energies and transition probabilities consistently.

## Method

The authors applied the generalized seniority scheme for multi-j degenerate orbitals, using all valence orbitals in the N=82–126 space except i₁₃/₂, to calculate B(E2) values for 6⁺ isomers in ¹³⁴–¹³⁸Sn. They also performed large-scale shell-model (LSSM) calculations using the Nushell code with the RCDB effective interaction, testing four variants (RCDBN, RCDBN1, RCDBN2, RCDBN3) that systematically varied the i₁₃/₂ single-particle energy and the f₇/₂ two-body matrix elements. The calculations used single-particle energies from Jones (2010) and Allmond (2014) with an effective neutron charge of 0.65.

## Key numbers

- The i₁₃/₂ neutron orbital is placed at 1.2 MeV (shifted from the original value of 0.39 MeV) to achieve best agreement with data.
- The diagonal and non-diagonal f₇/₂ two-body matrix elements are reduced by 25 keV in the RCDBN3 interaction.
- The pair degeneracy Ω for the valence space (excluding i₁₃/₂) is calculated as 15.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter used is ħω = 45A⁻¹/³ – 25A⁻²/³.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model study (Maheshwari and Jain, Phys. Rev. C 91, 024321, 2015) that addressed the B(E2) anomaly in ¹³⁶Sn using the RCDB interaction with a 25 keV TBME reduction. It also builds on the generalized seniority formalism recently introduced by the same authors (Phys. Lett. B 753, 122, 2016). The study uses single-particle energies from Jones (2010) and Allmond (2014) and addresses the earlier anomaly reported by Simpson et al. The results suggest a new sub-shell closure at N=112 due to the higher location of the i₁₃/₂ orbital, and confirm that the N=82 shell closure remains robust in the neutron-rich region. The work enables predictions of B(E2) values for 6⁺ isomers up to A=148 and for 2⁺ states in ¹³⁶,¹³⁸Sn where no experimental data exist.
