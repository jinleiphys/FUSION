# Physical characteristics of glasma from the earliest stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We present analytic results that describe the gluon field, or glasma, at very early times after a collision of relativistic heavy ions at proper time $τ=0$. We use a Colour Glass Condensate approach, and perform an expansion in $τ$. The full details of our method are described in our previous paper [1]. In this paper we present an analysis of various physical quantities that can be obtained from the energy-momentum tensor. We show that the expansion to order $τ^6$ can be trusted to about $τ=0.05$ fm. For times small enough that the expansion converges, the transverse and longitudinal pressures move towards their equilibrium values of one third of the energy density. The Fourier coefficients of the azimuthal flow are larger than expected, which contradicts the usual assumption that anisotropy is mostly generated during the hydrodynamic evolution of the plasma. We find a significant correlation between the elliptic flow coefficient and the eccentricity, which indicates that the spatial asymmetry introduced by the initial geometry is effectively transmitted to the azimuthal distribution of the gluon momentum field, even at very early times. This result is interesting because correlations of this kind are characteristic of the onset of hydrodynamic behaviour. We show that the angular momentum of the glasma is orders of magnitude smaller than the angular momentum of the initial system of ions colliding with non-zero impact parameter. This indicates that most of the angular momentum carried by the valence quarks is not transmitted to the glasma, and contradicts the picture of a rapidly rotating initial glasma state that has been proposed by several authors, but agrees with the current lack of experimental evidence for a significant polarization effect of the hyperons and vector mesons produced in heavy ion collisions at the highest accessible energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the expansion of the glasma energy-momentum tensor to order τ⁶ can be trusted up to about τ = 0.05 fm, and that at these early times the Fourier coefficients of azimuthal flow are larger than expected, contradicting the usual assumption that anisotropy is mostly generated during hydrodynamic evolution. The authors hedge that the range of validity of the Glasma Graph approximation has not been carefully studied and remains an open issue.

## Method

The authors use a Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory with a proper-time expansion (near-field expansion) to compute the gluon field energy-momentum tensor up to sixth order in τ. They implement non-constant nuclear area density functions via a gradient expansion of a two-dimensional projection of a Woods-Saxon distribution, following the method of Chen (2015). The calculation uses the Glasma Graph approximation to evaluate correlators of pre-collision potentials, and the energy-momentum tensor is expressed in Milne coordinates with symmetries exploited to reduce the number of independent components.

## Key numbers

- The expansion to order τ⁶ can be trusted to about τ = 0.05 fm.
- The angular momentum of the glasma is many orders of magnitude smaller than the angular momentum of the initial system of colliding ions with non-zero impact parameter.
- The energy density at order τ⁰ contains terms proportional to (3 ĥμ₁ ĥμ₂ Q_s⁴)/(8 π² g²) (2L-1)² plus symmetric terms, where L ≡ ln(Q_s/m) and Q_s is the saturation scale.
- The infrared regulator m is chosen as m ∼ Λ_QCD, and the ultraviolet cutoff Λ is set equal to the saturation scale Q_s.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (Carrington, 2020) which focused on technical details for the simple case of infinite uniform nuclei, while here they consider more physically realistic collisions with non-constant nuclear area density functions. The results contradict the picture of a rapidly rotating initial glasma state proposed by several authors (e.g., Liang, 2019) but agree with the current lack of experimental evidence for significant polarization effects in hyperons and vector mesons at highest accessible energies (Adam, 2018; Acharya, 2019). The finding of significant correlation between elliptic flow coefficient and eccentricity at very early times suggests hydro-like behavior onset earlier than commonly assumed, and enables future work to compare these analytic results with numerical methods.
