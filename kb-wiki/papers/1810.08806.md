# $p$-shell hypernuclear structures using the Gogny-interaction shell model

> We have systematically investigated the excitation spectra of $p$-shell hypernuclei within the shell model based on the nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions. For the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, we adopt the Gogny force instead of the widely-used empirical $p$-shell Cohen-Kurath interaction, while the hyperon-nucleon interaction takes the $ΛN$ interaction including the $ΛN$-$ΣN$ coupling effect. We find that the shell model with the Gogny force can give reasonable descriptions of both spectra and binding energies of the $p$-shell nuclei. With this confidence, combined with the $ΛN$ interaction, we have performed shell-model calculations for the $p$-shell hypernuclei. We compare our results with $γ$-ray data as well as various theoretical calculations, and explain recent experimental hypernuclear excitation spectra observed at JLab.

# p-shell hypernuclear structures using the Gogny-interaction shell model

## Key claim
The Gogny force, when used as the nucleon-nucleon interaction in shell-model calculations of p-shell hypernuclei, can reproduce both excitation spectra and binding energies without parameter adjustment, but some calculated energy spacings for certain hypernuclei (e.g., ⁷_ΛLi, ¹¹_ΛB, ¹²_ΛC, ¹³_ΛC, ¹⁵_ΛN) deviate slightly from γ-ray data and could be improved by refining single-particle energies or increasing the model space.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations with a ⁴He frozen core, placing valence nucleons in the 0p shell and the Λ or Σ hyperon in the lowest 0s₁/₂ orbit. The nucleon-nucleon interaction used the finite-range Gogny force with D1S parameters, while the hyperon-nucleon interaction included the ΛN-ΣN coupling effect with radial integral parameters from Millener (2010, 2012). The density dependence of the Gogny force was treated self-consistently through numerical iteration during Hamiltonian diagonalization.

## Key numbers
- The ΛN-ΣN coupling mass difference (Σ-Λ excitation energy) was taken as 80 MeV.
- For ⁹_ΛLi, the third experimental peak was assigned as a mixture of 3/2⁺ and 1/2⁺ states with a doublet spacing less than 0.1 MeV, unresolved by the experiment's energy resolution of 730 keV.
- For ¹⁰_ΛBe, the high-resolution spectroscopy at JLab achieved a resolution of approximately 0.78 MeV, yielding four observed levels.
- The harmonic-oscillator basis length parameter ħω was determined by minimizing the energy of each nucleus without the Λ hyperon, close to the empirical formula ħω = 45 A⁻¹/³ − 25 A⁻²/³.

## Context
This work extends the Gogny-force shell-model approach, previously applied successfully to p-shell and sd-shell nuclei by Jiang (2018), to hypernuclei. It contrasts with the widely-used empirical Cohen-Kurath (CK) interaction (Cohen 1965), which has shown deviations for neutron-rich hypernuclei such as ⁹_ΛLi, ¹⁰_ΛLi, and ⁷_ΛHe. The calculations are compared with γ-ray data from Tamura (2013) and recent high-resolution JLab experiments on ⁷_ΛHe (Gogami 2016a), ⁹_ΛLi (Urciuoli 2015), ¹⁰_ΛBe (Gogami 2016), and ¹²_ΛB (Tang 2014), as well as with other theoretical approaches including the translational invariant shell model (Richter 1991), four-body cluster model (Hiyama 2015), and Millener's calculations (Millener 2012). The work enables systematic studies of neutron-rich p-shell hypernuclei without refitting interaction parameters.
