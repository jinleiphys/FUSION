# Fluctuation sound absorption in quark matter

> We investigate the sound absorption in quark matter due to the interaction of the sound wave with the precritical fluctuations of the diquark-pair field above $T_c$. The soft collective mode of the pair field is derived using the time dependent Ginzburg-Landau functional with random Langevin forces. The strong absorption near the phase transition line may be viewed as a manifestation of the Mandelshtam-Leontovich slow relaxation time theory.

## Key claim

Sound absorption in quark matter due to precritical fluctuations of the diquark-pair field above the critical temperature T_c is predicted to be strong, with a temperature dependence even more pronounced than that of the fluctuation electrical conductivity; the result is derived under the assumption that the acoustic wavelength is much larger than the correlation radius of fluctuations (Eq. 17), and the absorption coefficient scales as (T_c/(T-T_c))^{3/2}.

## Method

The authors used a time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) functional with random Langevin forces to derive the fluctuation propagator (FP) for the diquark-pair field, describing a slow diffusion mode near T_c. They then evaluated the Aslamazov-Larkin (AL) diagram for the polarization operator, which contains two fluctuation propagators and is the dominant contribution near T_c, to obtain the imaginary part of the polarization operator that determines the sound absorption coefficient.

## Key numbers

- The Ginzburg-Levanyuk number for quark matter is estimated as Gi ~ (T_c/mu)^4 ~ 10^{-4}, where T_c ~ 40 MeV is the critical temperature for the 2SC phase and mu ~ 400 MeV is the quark chemical potential.
- The ultraviolet cutoff omega_D ~ 800 MeV, and ln(omega_D/(2 pi T_c)) ~ 1.
- The diffusion coefficient D = (1/3) v_0^2 tau, with tau ~ 0.3 fm, T_c = 40 MeV, and v_0^2 = 1/3, leading to the condition k^2/MeV^2 << 10^6 epsilon for the acoustic wavelength to exceed the correlation radius.
- The final sound absorption coefficient Im Pi scales as (T_c/(T-T_c))^{3/2} (Eq. 21).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of fluctuation electrical conductivity in quark matter (Kerbikov and Lukashov, 2015) to sound absorption, showing an even stronger temperature dependence. The approach follows the Mandelshtam-Leontovich slow relaxation time theory and the Aslamazov-Larkin diagram technique originally developed for BCS superconductors (Aslamazov and Larkin, 1968). The results are relevant for understanding transport in quark matter at moderate density and temperature, as may exist in neutron stars or future experiments at FAIR and NICA, and complement lattice calculations of the speed of sound in quark-gluon matter.
