# Effect of the chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations on heavy quark propagation at the LHC energies

> We consider the effect of the chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations in addition to the collisional as well as the radiative energy loss suffered by heavy quarks while propagating through the hot and densed deconfined medium of quarks and gluons created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations play an important role as it leads to an energy gain of heavy quarks of all momentum, significantly effective at the lower momentum region. With this, we have computed, for the first time, the nuclear modification factor (R_{AA}) of heavy mesons, viz., D-mesons and B-mesons and compared with the those experimental measurements in Pb-Pb collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76 TeV and \sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02 TeV by the CMS and ALICE experiments at the LHC. Our results are found to be in very good agreement with those available data measured by CMS and ALICE experiments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including the energy gain from chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations, in addition to the standard collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms, allows their model to describe the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) of D-mesons and B-mesons in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies across the entire transverse momentum range, whereas the standard losses alone overestimate the suppression at low to moderate p_T.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark production at next-to-leading order with CT10 parton distribution functions and EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions, then fragment charm and bottom quarks into D- and B-mesons using the Peterson fragmentation function. They model the QGP medium with isentropic cylindrical expansion and a lattice-QCD-based equation of state, and calculate the total energy modification of heavy quarks by combining the collisional energy loss from Peigne and Peshier (PP), the radiative energy loss from Abir, Jamil, Mustafa and Srivastava (AJMS), and the energy gain from chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations following Chakraborty, Mustafa and Thoma (CMT). The nuclear modification factor R_AA is then obtained by averaging over the medium evolution and the geometric path length distribution in Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks, the authors use mass M_c = 1.25 GeV; for bottom quarks, M_b = 4.2 GeV; the strong coupling constant is set to α_s = 0.3 throughout.
- The average path length for central Pb-Pb collisions is ⟨L⟩ = 6.14 fm.
- For √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, (0-10)% centrality: initial temperature T_0 = 0.467 GeV, number of participants N_part = 356, dN/dη = 1449, path length L = 5.73 fm.
- For √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, (0-100)% centrality: T_0 = 0.436 GeV, N_part = 113, dN/dη = 363, L = 4.16 fm.
- For √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, (0-10)% centrality: T_0 = 0.508 GeV, N_part = 359, dN/dη = 1749, L = 5.74 fm.
- For √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, (0-100)% centrality: T_0 = 0.469 GeV, N_part = 114, dN/dη = 463, L = 4.18 fm.
- The initial time is τ_0 = 0.3 fm and freeze-out time τ_f = 6 fm.
- The energy gain from field fluctuations is significant in the momentum range 4-20 GeV for heavy quarks, with the fractional energy gain being relatively more important at lower momentum (4-40 MeV region) than at very high momentum (>40 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the heavy quark energy loss phenomenology by incorporating the effect of chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations, which were previously calculated by Chakraborty, Mustafa and Thoma (CMT) but had not been applied to hadron spectra in heavy ion collisions. The authors use the collisional energy loss formalism of Peigne and Peshier (PP) and the radiative energy loss formalism of Abir, Jamil, Mustafa and Srivastava (AJMS), and show that only by including the fluctuation-induced energy gain can the R_AA data from ALICE and CMS be described across the full p_T range. This enables a more complete treatment of heavy quark jet quenching at LHC energies and suggests that field fluctuations are an essential ingredient for future phenomenological studies of heavy flavor suppression.
