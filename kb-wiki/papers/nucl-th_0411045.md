# Near-threshold pion production with radioactive beams at the Rare Isotope Accelerator

> Using an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model we study near-threshold pion production in heavy-ion collisions induced by radioactive beams at the planned Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA). We revisit the question of probing the high density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ using the $π^-/π^+$ ratio. It is found that both the total and differential $π^-/π^+$ ratios remain sensitive to the $E_{sym}(ρ)$ when the momentum-dependence of both the isoscalar and isovector potentials are consistently taken into account. Moreover, the multiplicity and spectrum of $π^-$ mesons are found more sensitive to the $E_{sym}(ρ)$ than those of $π^+$ mesons. Finally, effects of the Coulomb potential on the pion spectra and $π^-/π^+$ ratio are also discussed.

## Key claim

The $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio remains sensitive to the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(\rho)$ even when the momentum-dependence of both the isoscalar and isovector potentials are consistently taken into account, contrary to concerns that such momentum-dependence might wash out the sensitivity.

## Method

The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model IBUU04, which incorporates a single nucleon potential with momentum-dependent isoscalar and isovector terms fitted to Gogny Hartree-Fock and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. They simulated the reaction $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter 1 fm, varying the parameter $x$ (from 1 to -2) to mimic different density dependences of the symmetry energy. Pion production was modeled through $\Delta(1232)$ resonance formation and decay, with all four charge states of the $\Delta$ and $N^*(1440)$ resonances tracked separately.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon density reached in the central reaction is about **2 times normal nuclear matter density** ($\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$).
- The high-density phase lasts for about **15 fm/c** (from 5 to 20 fm/c) for the $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The $\pi^-$ multiplicity increases by about **20%** when varying the $x$ parameter from -2 to 1, while the $\pi^+$ multiplicity remains about the same.
- The multiplicity of $\pi^-$ is about **2 to 3 times** that of $\pi^+$ for the reaction studied.
- The average $(N/Z)^2$ of the reaction system is about **2.4**.
- The Coulomb effect increases the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio at $p_t=0$ by about **30%** while suppressing it at higher transverse momentum.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Li (2003) and others who proposed the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy, but it addresses the previously neglected role of momentum-dependent symmetry potentials. The authors note that prior transport model studies of pion production (e.g., by Stock and others) did not include momentum-dependent symmetry potentials. The findings are intended to guide experimental planning for the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) and the GSI facility, particularly for designing a Time Projection Chamber (TPC) to study the equation of state of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. The work also connects to earlier studies of isospin fractionation and the use of neutron/proton ratios, isoscaling, and neutron-proton differential flow as probes of symmetry energy.
