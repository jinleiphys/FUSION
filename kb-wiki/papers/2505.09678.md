# Towards testing $(g-2)_蟿$ in $e^+e^-\to蟿^+蟿^-$: radiative corrections and projections for Belle II

> The arguably most promising avenue towards testing physics beyond the Standard Model in the anomalous magnetic moment of the $蟿$ proceeds via suitably constructed asymmetries in $e^+e^-\to蟿^+蟿^-$ in the presence of a polarized electron beam. Such a program, as could be realized at Belle II assuming a polarization upgrade of the SuperKEKB $e^+e^-$ collider, crucially relies on a careful consideration of radiative corrections. In this work, we present the complete one-loop result for the fully polarized $e^+e^-\to蟿^+蟿^-$ process and its implementation in the Monte-Carlo integrator McMule. As an application, we discuss projections relevant for measurements at Belle II, both with and without electron polarization, and outline the necessary steps for a generalization to next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The paper presents the complete one-loop QED radiative corrections for the fully polarized $e^+e^-\to\tau^+\tau^-$ process, showing that the impact of box diagrams remains small due to suppression by the electron mass, and that a precision of $10^{-5}$ on $a_\tau$ should be achievable at Belle II with a polarized electron beam, while reaching $10^{-6}$ would require further advances in statistics and energy calibration.

## Method

The authors calculated the fully polarized next-to-leading-order (NLO) QED amplitude for $e^+e^-\to\tau^+\tau^-$, retaining the full mass dependence of both electron and tau, and implemented the result in the Monte-Carlo integrator McMule (v0.6.0). They employed the four-dimensional formulation of the FDH scheme (FDF) for regularization in $d=4-2\epsilon$ dimensions, used on-shell renormalization up to NLO QED, and applied the FKS$^\ell$ scheme for infrared subtraction of soft singularities with next-to-soft stabilization for real-virtual matrix elements. The asymmetries $A_N^\pm$, $A_T^\pm$, and $A_L^\pm$ were constructed from the spin-dependent cross sections using semileptonic tau decays to project out tau spin components.

## Key numbers

* Center-of-mass energy set to $s = M_{\Upsilon(4S)}^2 = (10\,583.0052\ \text{MeV})^2$ for Belle II projections
* Electron mass $m_e = 0.510998950\ \text{MeV}$ and tau mass $m_\tau = 1\,776.86\ \text{MeV}$ used as input parameters
* Fine-structure constant $\alpha = 1/137.035999084$ used for numerical calculations
* Polarization analyzer for spin-0 hadrons: $\alpha_h = 1$; for spin-1 hadrons: $\alpha_h = (m_\tau^2 - 2m_h^2)/(m_\tau^2 + 2m_h^2)$
* For the $\rho^\pm$ meson, longitudinal polarization analyzer $\alpha_h^{\lambda=0} = 0.72$ and transverse $\alpha_h^{\lambda=-1} = -0.28$
* For the $a_1^\pm$ meson, longitudinal polarization analyzer $\alpha_h^{\lambda=0} = 0.51$ and transverse $\alpha_h^{\lambda=-1} = -0.49$

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Crivellin et al. (2021) for testing the tau anomalous magnetic moment via asymmetries in $e^+e^-\to\tau^+\tau^-$, which itself built on the original idea by Bernabeu et al. (2007) to use polarized electron beams at lower $\Upsilon$ resonances. The calculation addresses the need for radiative corrections including box diagrams emphasized in the Belle II projections by the USBelleIIGroup (2022) and Aihara et al. (2024). The implementation in McMule enables future generalization to next-to-next-to-leading order and provides a tool for experimental use at Belle II with a proposed polarization upgrade of SuperKEKB.
