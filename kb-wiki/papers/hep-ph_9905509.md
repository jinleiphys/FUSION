# Additional Nucleon Current Contributions to Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We have examined the importance of momentum dependent induced nucleon currents such as weak-magnetism and pseudoscalar couplings to the amplitude of neutrinoless double beta decay in the mechanisms of light and heavy Majorana neutrino as well as in that of Majoron emission. Such effects are expected to occur in all nuclear models in the direction of reducing the light neutrino matrix elements by about 30%. To test this we have performed a calculation of the nuclear matrix elements of the experimentally interesting nuclei A = 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130, 136 and 150 within the pn-RQRPA. We have found that indeed such corrections vary somewhat from nucleus to nucleus, but in all cases they are greater than 25 percent. In the case of heavy neutrino the effect is much larger (a factor of 3). Combining out results with the best presently available experimental limits on the half-life of the neutrinoless double beta decay we have extracted new limits on the effective neutrino mass (light and heavy) and the effective Majoron coupling constant.

## Key claim

The inclusion of momentum-dependent induced nucleon currents (weak-magnetism and pseudoscalar couplings) reduces the light neutrino nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay by about 30%, with the reduction varying somewhat from nucleus to nucleus but always exceeding 25%; for heavy neutrino exchange the suppression is much larger, approximately a factor of 3.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of the nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay for nine experimentally interesting nuclei (A = 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130, 136, 150) using the proton-neutron renormalized Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-RQRPA). They incorporated the weak-magnetism and induced pseudoscalar terms into the nucleon current, with the pseudoscalar coupling given by the partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC) hypothesis, and compared the resulting matrix elements to those obtained with only the standard vector and axial-vector currents.

## Key numbers

- For light neutrino exchange, the reduction of the nuclear matrix element due to induced currents is greater than 25% for all nuclei studied, with an average reduction of about 30%.
- For heavy neutrino exchange, the suppression factor is approximately 3 (a factor of 3) relative to calculations without these induced currents.
- The largest light neutrino nuclear matrix element values after including induced currents are: 3.33 for A=150, 3.21 for A=100, and 2.80 for A=76.
- The largest heavy neutrino nuclear matrix element values are: 35.6 for A=150, 32.6 for A=76, and 30.0 for A=82.
- The smallest nuclear matrix elements are for A=136: light neutrino value of 0.66 and heavy neutrino value of 14.1.
- The average momentum of the exchanged neutrino in the light neutrino mechanism is about 100 MeV, corresponding to a mean nucleon-nucleon separation of about 2 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements that had considered only the vector and axial-vector parts of the nucleon current, as in the reviews by Haxton and Stephenson (1984) and by Tomoda (1991). The induced pseudoscalar term had been ignored in all previous calculations of the light Majorana neutrino mass mechanism, while the weak-magnetism term had been considered only in the context of right-handed current mechanisms by Tomoda et al. (1985) and Pantis et al. (1996). The results enable more reliable extraction of limits on the effective neutrino mass and the effective Majoron coupling constant from experimental half-life limits, and they demonstrate that nuclear model calculations must include these induced currents to obtain quantitative answers for lepton number violating parameters.
