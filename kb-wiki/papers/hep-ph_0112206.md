# Mass-Induced Crystalline Color Superconductivity

> We demonstrate that crystalline color superconductivity may arise as a result of pairing between massless quarks and quarks with nonzero mass m_s. Previous analyses of this phase of cold dense quark matter have all utilized a chemical potential difference δμto favor crystalline color superconductivity over ordinary BCS pairing. In any context in which crystalline color superconductivity occurs in nature, however, it will be m_s-induced. The effect of m_s is qualitatively different from that of δμin one crucial respect: m_s depresses the value of the BCS gap Δ_0 whereas δμleaves Δ_0 unchanged. This effect in the BCS phase must be taken into account before m_s-induced and δμ-induced crystalline color superconductivity can sensibly be compared.

# Mass-Induced Crystalline Color Superconductivity

## Key claim

Crystalline color superconductivity may arise from pairing between massless quarks and quarks with nonzero strange quark mass $m_s$, not only from a chemical potential difference $\delta\mu$ as previously assumed. The effect of $m_s$ is qualitatively different because it depresses the BCS gap $\Delta_0$, whereas $\delta\mu$ leaves $\Delta_0$ unchanged, and this difference must be accounted for before the two mechanisms can be sensibly compared.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor toy model with a point-like four-fermion interaction to study pairing between massless up quarks and strange quarks with mass $m_s$, allowing also for a chemical potential difference $\delta\mu$ between the two species. They derive a gap equation from a one-loop Schwinger-Dyson equation in a modified Nambu-Gorkov formalism that accommodates Cooper pairs with nonzero total momentum $2\mathbf{q}$. They solve this gap equation to map the regions in the $(m_s, \delta\mu)$ plane where BCS pairing and crystalline (LOFF) pairing are favored, using the BCS gap $\Delta_0$ at $m_s=\delta\mu=0$ to parametrize the interaction strength.

## Key numbers

- The BCS state persists for $|\delta\mu| < \delta\mu_1 = \Delta_0/\sqrt{2}$ in the weak coupling limit, where $\Delta_0$ is the BCS gap at $m_s=0$.
- The upper boundary of the LOFF window at $m_s=0$ in the weak coupling limit is $\delta\mu_2 = 0.754\Delta_0$, giving a window width $[\delta\mu_2(0)-\delta\mu_1(0)]/\Delta_0(0) \rightarrow 0.047$.
- For a coupling yielding $\Delta_0(0)=100$ MeV, the LOFF window closes at $m_s=0$ but reopens at larger $m_s$, demonstrating that $m_s$-induced crystalline color superconductivity can occur where $\delta\mu$-induced does not.
- The BCS gap $\Delta_0(m_s)$ decreases linearly with $m_s^2$ for small $m_s^2$, as shown for $\mu=400$ MeV with $\Delta_0(0)=10, 40, 80$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of crystalline color superconductivity, previously studied only with chemical potential differences (BowersLOFF, 2000; Larkin and Ovchinnikov, 1964; Fulde and Ferrell, 1964), to the physically relevant case where the Fermi surface splitting arises from the strange quark mass. The authors note that in any astrophysical context where crystalline color superconductivity occurs, it will be $m_s$-induced rather than $\delta\mu$-induced. The results enable future work on three-flavor quark matter and suggest that the LOFF window may be wider for $m_s$-induced pairing than for $\delta\mu$-induced pairing, especially at moderate coupling strengths relevant to compact stars.
