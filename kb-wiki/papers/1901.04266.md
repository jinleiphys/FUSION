# The Bethe-Salpeter approach to bound states: from Euclidean to Minkowski space

> The challenge to obtain from the Euclidean Bethe--Salpeter amplitude the amplitude in Minkowski is solved by resorting to un-Wick rotating the Euclidean homogeneous integral equation. The results obtained with this new practical method for the amputated Bethe--Salpeter amplitude for a two-boson bound state reveals a rich analytic structure of this amplitude, which can be traced back to the Minkowski space Bethe--Salpeter equation using the Nakanishi integral representation. The method can be extended to small rotation angles bringing the Euclidean solution closer to the Minkowski one and could allow in principle the extraction of the longitudinal parton density functions and momentum distribution amplitude, for example.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to obtain the Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter amplitude from the Euclidean solution by performing an "un-Wick rotation" of the Euclidean homogeneous integral equation, rotating the integration contour into the complex plane by a small angle \(\theta\) rather than the full \(\pi/2\) of the standard Wick rotation. The method works for small rotation angles (as small as \(\theta = \pi/128 \approx 1.4^\circ\)) and reveals the analytic structure of the vertex function, but cannot solve the equation exactly at \(\theta = 0\) (pure Minkowski space).

## Method

The authors start from the Euclidean-space ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for a two-boson bound state in the rest frame, then perform a change of variables \(k_0 \to k_0 e^{i\delta}\) and \(k'_0 \to k'_0 e^{i\delta}\) with \(\delta = \theta - \pi/2\), rotating the integration contour away from the Euclidean axis toward the Minkowski axis. They solve the resulting integral equation numerically by iteration, starting from the Euclidean solution (\(\delta = 0\)) and increasing \(\delta\) in small steps, using the previous solution as the initial guess. As a consistency check, they compare these results with solutions obtained from the Nakanishi integral representation (NIR) of the Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter equation, finding good agreement at \(\theta = \pi/16 \approx 11^\circ\).

## Key numbers

- The method is demonstrated for rotation angles as small as \(\theta = \pi/128 \approx 1.4^\circ\).
- Results are shown for exchange boson masses \(\mu/m\) ranging from \(0.1\) to \(1.5\).
- Two bound state masses are studied: \(M/m = 1.0\) (moderate binding) and \(M/m = 1.8\) (weak binding).
- The branch-points in the vertex function are located at \(|k_0| = k_0^\pm \equiv \sqrt{(m+\mu)^2 + k_v^2} \pm M/2\), with the separation between the closest branch-points given by \(2\sqrt{(m+\mu)^2 + k_v^2} - M\).
- The NIR consistency check is performed at \(\theta = \pi/16 \approx 11^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation (NIR) (Kusaka and Williams, 1995; Kusaka et al., 1997; Frederico et al., 2012) or direct integration (Sauli, 2001; Pimentel et al., 2016), which are algebraically involved. It also builds on Euclidean-space calculations (Maris and Tandy, 1997; Maris and Roberts, 2003) that are straightforward but make it difficult to extract light-front observables such as parton distributions. The method enables future extraction of longitudinal parton density functions and momentum distribution amplitudes from Euclidean solutions, and can be applied to further explore the phenomenology of strongly relativistic bound state systems.
