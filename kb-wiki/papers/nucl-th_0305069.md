# Kaon Interferometry at RHIC from AMPT Model

> The two-kaon interferometry at RHIC is studied in a multi-phase transport model. Similar to the pion case, we find strong space-time correlation at freeze-out for the kaon emission source, which results in a large positive $R_{\rm out}-t$ term and tends to reduce the $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$ ratio. Unlike the pion case, the source radii for kaons determined from the emission function are close to the radius parameters extracted from a Gaussian fit to the correlation function.

# Kaon Interferometry at RHIC from AMPT Model

## Key claim

The kaon emission source in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC shows a strong positive space-time correlation in the out-direction at freeze-out, similar to pions, but unlike pions the kaon source radii extracted directly from the emission function are close (within 30%) to the radius parameters obtained from a Gaussian fit to the three-dimensional correlation function.

## Method

The two-kaon correlation function was evaluated using the AMPT (A MultiPhase Transport) model for central (b=0 fm) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV and 200A GeV. The emission function was obtained from kaon space-time coordinates and momenta at kinetic freeze-out, and the correlation function was computed using the program Correlation After Burner. Source radii were extracted both directly from the curvature of the emission function at Q=0 and from Gaussian fits to the three-dimensional correlation function, using the "out-side-long" coordinate system.

## Key numbers

- Kaon source radii from the emission function for K0_S with -1<y<1 and 200<pt<400 MeV/c range between 2 and 5 fm, much smaller than low-pt pion source radii (between 7 and 25 fm)
- For kaons with 200<pt<400 MeV/c and sigma_p=10 mb, the numerical decomposition of R_out^2 is: 3.4^2 fm^2 = 35 fm^2 - 2*22 fm^2 + 20 fm^2, showing the x_out-t correlation term D_{x_out,beta_perp t} is +22 fm^2
- For mid-rapidity pions with 125<pt<225 MeV/c, the corresponding decomposition is: 17^2 fm^2 = 185 fm^2 - 2*168 fm^2 + 431 fm^2
- Kaon correlation functions change only slightly from sqrt(s)=130A GeV to 200A GeV at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pion interferometry using the AMPT model (Lin 2002), which found that small pion R_out/R_side ratios could arise from large positive space-time correlations in the emission source. The results contradict hydrodynamical model predictions (Rischke 1996, Soff 2000) that expected large R_out/R_side ratios from long emission times due to quark-gluon plasma to hadronic matter phase transition. The study enables future comparisons with strange hadron elliptic flow data (Adler 2003, Sorensen 2003) to probe strange quark interactions in dense matter.
