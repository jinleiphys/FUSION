# Extended-soft-core Baryon-Baryon Model II. Hyperon-Nucleon Interaction

> The YN results are presented from the Extended-soft-core (ESC) interactions. They consist of local- and non-local-potentials due to (i) One-boson-exchange (OBE), with pseudoscalar-, vector-, scalar-, and axial-vector-nonets, (ii) Diffractive exchanges, (iii) Two-pseudoscalar exchange, and (iv) Meson-pair-exchange (MPE). This model, called ESC04, describes NN and YN in a unified way using broken flavor SU(3)-symmetry. Novel ingredients are the inclusion of (i) the axial-vector-mesons, (ii) a zero in the scalar- and axial-vector meson form factors. We describe simultaneous fits to the NN- and YN-data, using four options in the ESC-model. Very good fits were obtained. G-matrix calculations with these four options are also reported. The obtained well depths (U_Λ, U_Σ, U_Ξ) reveal distinct features of ESC04a-d. The ΛΛ-interactions are demonstrated to be consistent with the observed data of_{ΛΛ}^6He. The possible three-body effects are investigated by considering phenomenologically the changes of the vector-meson masses in a nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The ESC04 model achieves very good simultaneous fits to both NN and YN scattering data by incorporating axial-vector meson potentials and a zero in the scalar- and axial-vector meson form factors, which for the first time allows the model parameters to stay close to the predictions of the 3P0 quark-antiquark creation model. The four options (ESC04a-d) give distinct hyperon well depths in nuclear matter, and the Lambda-Lambda interactions are consistent with the observed data of 6LambdaLambdaHe.

## Method

The authors constructed the Extended-soft-core (ESC) baryon-baryon interaction model ESC04, which includes local and non-local potentials from one-boson-exchange (pseudoscalar, vector, scalar, and axial-vector nonets), diffractive exchanges, two-pseudoscalar exchange, and meson-pair-exchange, all regulated by gaussian form factors. They performed simultaneous fits to 4233 NN data points (0 to 350 MeV lab energy) and 35 YN data points plus 3 Sigma+p cross sections from KEK experiment E289, using broken flavor SU(3) symmetry to connect NN and YN channels with only a few free YN parameters. G-matrix calculations were then performed for Lambda N, Sigma N, and Xi N pairs in nuclear matter to extract hyperon well depths.

## Key numbers

- For the selected 4233 NN data with energies 0 to 350 MeV, the fit reached chi-squared per data point of 1.22.
- For the usual set of 35 YN data plus 3 Sigma+p cross sections from KEK E289, the fit achieved chi-squared per YN data point of approximately 0.63.
- The inelastic capture ratio at rest r_R = 0.468 plus or minus 0.010 was fitted rather well.
- The four model options (ESC04a-d) produce distinct well depths U_Lambda, U_Sigma, and U_Xi in nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core OBE model series (MRS89, RSY99) by introducing axial-vector meson potentials and form-factor zeros, enabling a unified description of NN and YN interactions under broken SU(3) symmetry. The ESC04 model improves upon the earlier ESC00 version by restricting coupling constants to follow quark-model predictions from the 3P0 pair creation model (Mic69). The results enable future studies of hypernuclear systems, few-body computations, and extensions to the S=-2 channels (Lambda Lambda, Xi N) where experiments may be realized.
