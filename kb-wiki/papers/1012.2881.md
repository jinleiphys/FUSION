# From string theory to large N QCD

> We propose the dual gravity of a non conformal gauge theory which has logarithmic running of couplings in the IR but becomes almost conformal in the far UV. The theory has matter in fundamental representation, non-zero temperature and under a cascade of Seiberg dualities, can be described in terms of gauge groups of lower and lower rank. We outline the procedure of holographic renormalization and propose a mechanism to UV complete the gauge theory by modifying the dual geometry at large radial distances. As an example, we construct the brane configuration and sources required to attach a Klebanov-Witten type geometry at large r to a Klebanov- Strassler type geometry at small r. Using the supergravity description for the dual geometry, we compute thermal mass of a fundamental 'quark' in our theory along with drag and diffusion coefficients of the gauge theory plasma. We compute the stress tensor of the gauge theory and formulate the wake a probe leaves behind as it traverses the medium. Transport coefficient shear viscosity η and its ratio to entropy η/s are calculated and finally we show how confinement of 'quarks' at large separation can occur at low temperatures. We classify the most general dual geometry that gives rise to linear confinement at low temperatures and show how quarkonium states can melt at high temperatures to liberate 'quarks'.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a dual gravity description for a non-conformal gauge theory that exhibits logarithmic running of couplings in the infrared (IR) and becomes nearly conformal in the far ultraviolet (UV), but this construction relies on a specific brane configuration that attaches a Klebanov-Witten type geometry at large radial distances to a Klebanov-Strassler type geometry at small r, and the UV completion requires modifying the dual geometry at large radial distances.

## Method

The authors construct a supergravity dual for a large N gauge theory with fundamental matter at nonzero temperature by introducing a black hole in the dual geometry and implementing a cascade of Seiberg dualities that reduces the rank of the gauge groups. They perform holographic renormalization to compute thermal properties, including the thermal mass of a fundamental quark, drag and diffusion coefficients of the gauge theory plasma, and the stress tensor. The shear viscosity eta and its ratio to entropy eta/s are calculated, and the conditions for linear confinement at low temperatures and quarkonium melting at high temperatures are classified from the dual geometry.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT correspondence beyond the conformal case of Maldacena (1997) to incorporate renormalization group flow, building on the Klebanov-Witten and Klebanov-Strassler geometries. It enables the study of strongly coupled QCD-like plasmas at nonzero temperature using weakly coupled classical gravity, providing a framework to compute transport coefficients and quarkonium properties that are inaccessible to perturbative methods. The results enable future calculations of jet quenching and heavy quark dynamics in strongly coupled plasmas relevant to heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC.
