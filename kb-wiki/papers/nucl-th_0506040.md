# Modified Fragmentation Function from Quark Recombination

> Within the framework of the constituent quark model, it is shown that the single hadron fragmentation function of a parton can be expressed as a convolution of shower diquark or triquark distribution function and quark recombination probability, if the interference between amplitudes of quark recombination with different momenta is neglected. The recombination probability is determined by the hadron's wavefunction in the constituent quark model. The shower diquark or triquark distribution functions of a fragmenting jet are defined in terms of overlapping matrices of constituent quarks and parton field operators. They are similar in form to dihadron or trihadron fragmentation functions in terms of parton operator and hadron states. Extending the formalism to the field theory at finite temperature, we automatically derive contributions to the effective single hadron fragmentation function from the recombination of shower and thermal constituent quarks. Such contributions involve single or diquark distribution functions which in turn can be related to diquark or triquark distribution functions via sum rules. We also derive QCD evolution equations for quark distribution functions that in turn determine the evolution of the effective jet fragmentation functions in a thermal medium.

## Key claim

Within the constituent quark model, the single hadron fragmentation function of a parton can be expressed as a convolution of shower diquark or triquark distribution functions and quark recombination probability, provided the interference between amplitudes of quark recombination with different momenta is neglected; extending this formalism to field theory at finite temperature automatically yields contributions to the effective fragmentation function from recombination of shower and thermal constituent quarks.

## Method

The authors reformulate the standard parton fragmentation function, defined via matrix elements of parton field operators and hadron states, by replacing the hadronic intermediate states with constituent quark states in the constituent quark model. They define double (diquark) and triple (triquark) constituent quark distribution functions in a fragmenting jet, analogous to dihadron fragmentation functions, and express the single hadron fragmentation function as a convolution of these distributions with recombination probabilities determined by the hadron's wavefunction. They derive DGLAP evolution equations for the diquark and triquark distribution functions and extend the formalism to finite-temperature field theory to obtain contributions from shower-thermal and thermal-thermal quark recombination.

## Key numbers

* The initial gluon density in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is extracted to be about 30 times higher than that in cold Au nuclei (from cited prior work, not original to this paper).
* At intermediate p_T = 2-4 GeV/c, the baryon-to-meson ratio in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC is about a factor of 5 increase over the value in p+p collisions (from cited experimental data).
* The scaling relation v_2^M(p_T/2)/2 = v_2^B(p_T/3)/3 is observed for the azimuthal anisotropy of meson and baryon spectra (from cited experimental observation).
* The hadronic wavefunction normalization is given by the integral over transverse momentum and momentum fraction of |phi_h|^2 = 1.
* The cutoff Lambda for intrinsic transverse momentum of constituent quarks inside a hadron serves as the scale for hadronization in the diquark distribution function definitions.

## Context

This work provides a field-theoretical derivation of the quark recombination model of jet fragmentation, following closely the phenomenological approach of Hwa and Yang (2002) but enabling QCD evolution of the shower quark distributions that the Hwa-Yang model could not calculate. The formalism extends earlier work on medium modification of fragmentation functions with detailed balance by Osborne and Wang (2002) and allows consistent treatment of parton energy loss, detailed balance, and recombination of shower quarks with thermal quarks in heavy-ion collisions. This enables future calculations of the effective jet fragmentation functions in a thermal medium with QCD evolution, connecting the recombination model to perturbative QCD and experimental measurements at RHIC.
