# Surveying the Nucleon-Nucleon Momentum Correlation Function in the Framework of Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model

> Momentum correlation functions of the nucleon-nucleon pairs are presented for reactions with C isotopes bombarding a $^{12} \rm C$ target within the framework of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. The binding-energy dependence of the momentum correlation functions is also explored, and other factors that have an influence on momentum correlation functions are investigated. These factors include momentum-dependent nuclear equation of state, in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, impact parameters, total pair momenta, and beam energy. In particular, the rise and the fall of the strength of momentum correlation functions at lower relative momentum are shown with an increase in beam energy.

## Key claim

The nucleon-nucleon momentum correlation function strength at low relative momentum depends approximately linearly on the mean binding energy of the projectile isotopes, but this relationship is steeper when using a stable initialization of the projectile and target phase space (sampled from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock density distributions) compared to a random initialization.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) transport model to simulate collisions of a chain of carbon isotopes (projectiles) with a 12C target at beam energies of 100 and 800 MeV/nucleon, primarily for head-on collisions (impact parameter b = 0 fm). The phase-space information at freeze-out (t = 200 fm/c) was fed into the CRAB (Correlation After Burner) code to construct two-nucleon correlation functions for neutron-neutron, proton-neutron, and proton-proton pairs, incorporating final-state interactions. The study systematically varied the nuclear equation of state (soft vs. stiff momentum-dependent), in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, impact parameters, total pair momenta, and beam energy to examine their effects on the correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The proton-neutron correlation function strength at 5 MeV/c relative momentum (C_p-n) shows an approximate linear relationship with the mean binding energy per nucleon of C-isotope projectiles at 800 MeV/nucleon and b = 0 fm.
- Nucleon emission begins around 20 fm/c and emission rates peak 15-20 fm/c later for the 18C + 12C system at 100 MeV/nucleon and b = 0 fm.
- The HBT strength for n-n and p-n pairs at 5 MeV/c and for p-p pairs at 20 MeV/c increases with increasing total pair momentum (P_tot) for 18C + 12C at 100 MeV/nucleon and b = 0 fm.
- Correlation function values with the stiff momentum-dependent EOS (incompressibility K = 380 MeV) are larger than those with the soft momentum-dependent EOS (K = 200 MeV) for 18C + 12C at 100 MeV/nucleon and b = 0 fm.
- HBT strength increases with the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section (relative to the free cross section) for 18C + 12C at 100 MeV/nucleon and b = 0 fm.
- Larger HBT values are predicted for central collisions (smaller impact parameter) compared to peripheral collisions for 18C + 12C at 100 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of two-proton correlation functions in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions by Lynch et al. and investigations of isospin dependence using the isospin-dependent BUU model by Chen et al. The paper systematically surveys how multiple physical ingredients (EOS stiffness, in-medium cross sections, impact parameter, total pair momentum, and beam energy) affect the full shape of nucleon-nucleon momentum correlation functions, not just the strength at a single relative momentum. The results enable more detailed comparisons between transport model calculations and experimental HBT data, particularly for light reaction systems at beam energies above 100 MeV/nucleon where slow statistical emission components are minimized.
