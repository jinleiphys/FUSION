# Dense and Hot QCD at Strong Coupling

> We present a novel framework for the equation of state of dense and hot Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), which focuses on the region of the phase diagram relevant for neutron star mergers and core-collapse supernovae. The model combines predictions from the gauge/gravity duality with input from lattice field theory, QCD perturbation theory, chiral effective theory and statistical modeling. It is therefore, by construction, in good agreement with theoretical constraints both at low and high densities and temperatures. The main ingredients of our setup are the non-perturbative V-QCD model based on the gauge/gravity duality, a van der Waals model for nucleon liquid, and the DD2 version of the Hempel-Schaffner-Bielich statistical model of nuclear matter. By consistently combining these models, we also obtain a description for the nuclear to quark matter phase transition and its critical endpoint. The parameter dependence of the model is represented by three (soft, intermediate and stiff) variants of the equation of state, all of which agree with observational constraints from neutron stars and their mergers. We discuss resulting constraints for the equation of state, predictions for neutron stars and the location of the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel framework for the equation of state of dense and hot QCD that combines gauge/gravity duality predictions with lattice QCD, perturbative QCD, chiral effective theory, and statistical modeling, but the temperature dependence in the confined nuclear matter phase is not derived from first principles within the holographic model and instead relies on a van der Waals extrapolation because the gauge/gravity duality yields temperature-independent thermodynamics in confined phases.

## Method

The authors construct a thermodynamically consistent equation of state by combining three main components: the holographic V-QCD model for dense nuclear and quark matter at intermediate and high densities, a van der Waals model with excluded volume corrections to provide temperature dependence in the nuclear matter phase, and the HS(DD2) statistical model for low-density nuclear matter including nuclear clusters. The cold hybrid EoS at zero temperature is built by matching V-QCD predictions for homogeneous nuclear matter above approximately 1.5 times nuclear saturation density with the APR model at low densities, using a smoothed matching with two transition densities at 1.4 n_s and 1.8 n_s to ensure continuous speed of sound. The van der Waals model is calibrated to reproduce the cold V-QCD hybrid EoS at zero temperature, and the charge fraction dependence is taken from the HS(DD2) model throughout the nuclear matter regime.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon of ground state nuclear matter: epsilon_B / n_b = -16 MeV
- Excluded volume parameter for nucleons: v_0 = 0.56 fm^3
- Transition densities for smoothed cold EoS matching: 1.4 n_s and 1.8 n_s
- Weight function transition density parameter: n_0 approximately 0.0694 n_s
- Three EoS variants (soft, intermediate, stiff) defined by V-QCD data fits 5b, 7a, and 8b from Jokela et al. (2018, 2020)

## Context

This work extends previous cold hybrid EoS constructions by Ecker et al. (2019) and Jokela et al. (2020) that combined V-QCD with nuclear theory models at zero temperature, and by Chesler et al. (2019) who combined HS(DD2) with V-QCD quark matter EoS with temperature and charge fraction dependence. The new framework adds a consistent description of the temperature dependence of nuclear matter from gauge/gravity duality via a van der Waals extrapolation, enabling unified EoSs applicable to neutron star merger and core-collapse supernova simulations, and provides predictions for the location of the QCD critical point.
