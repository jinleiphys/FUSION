# Effect of Delta potential on the pion-/pion+ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Based on the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck(IBUU) transport model, effects of $ќФ$ resonance potential on the free n/p and $ѕА^-/ѕА^+$ ratios in the central collision of $^{197}Au+^{197}Au$ at beam energies of 200 and 400 MeV/A are studied. It is found that the effect of $ќФ$ potential on the ratio of pre-equilibrium free $n/p$ is invisible. The effect of $ќФ$ isovector potential on the kinetic energy integrating ratio of $ѕА^-/ѕА^+$ may be observable only at lower incident beam energies and with stiffer symmetry energy. The strength of the $ќФ$ isoscalar potential affects the height of the $ѕА^-/ѕА^+$ ratio around the Coulomb peak but does not affect the kinetic energy integrating ratio of $ѕА^-/ѕА^+$. In heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, relating to the question of non-conservation of energy on $ќФ$ or $ѕА$ productions, one can replace the $ќФ$ potential by nucleon isoscalar potential especially a soft symmetry energy is employed.

## Context

This work extends studies of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities using the ѕАвБї/ѕАвБЇ ratio as an observable, following earlier transport-model calculations by Ko (2014), Di Toro (2005), and Cozma (2014) that argued for modified threshold conditions due to ќФвАУnucleon potential differences.
