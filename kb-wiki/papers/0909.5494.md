# Nuclear constraints on the inner edge of neutron star crusts

> We show that the widely used parabolic approximation to the Equation of State (EOS) of asymmetric nuclear matter leads systematically to significantly higher core-crust transition densities and pressures. Using an EOS for neutron-rich nuclear matter constrained by the isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions in the same sub-saturation density range as the neutron star crust, the density and pressure at the inner edge separating the liquid core from the solid crust of neutron stars are determined to be 0.040 fm$^{-3}$ $\leq ρ_{t}\leq 0.065$ fm$^{-3}$ and 0.01 MeV/fm$^{3}$ $\leq P_{t}\leq 0.26$ MeV/fm$^{3}$, respectively. Implications of these constraints on the Vela pulsar are discussed.

## Key claim
The widely used parabolic approximation (PA) to the Equation of State (EOS) of asymmetric nuclear matter systematically yields significantly higher core-crust transition densities and pressures than the full EOS; the authors therefore use an EOS constrained by isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions to determine the transition density and pressure at the inner edge of neutron star crusts.

## Method
The authors employed both thermodynamical and dynamical (long-wavelength limit) methods to compute the core-crust transition density and pressure using the MDI (Momentum-Dependent Interaction) EOS. They compared results from the full EOS with those from its parabolic approximation (PA), which truncates the EOS at the quadratic term in isospin asymmetry. The slope parameter of the symmetry energy, \(L = 3\rho_0 \frac{\partial E_{sym}(\rho)}{\partial \rho}|_{\rho=\rho_0}\), was constrained to \(86 \pm 25\) MeV from transport model analysis of isospin diffusion data, and this constraint was applied to the \(\rho_t\)-\(L\) correlation from the full EOS in the dynamical method.

## Key numbers
- Transition density \(\rho_t\) constrained to \(0.040\ \text{fm}^{-3} \leq \rho_t \leq 0.065\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Transition pressure \(P_t\) constrained to \(0.01\ \text{MeV/fm}^3 \leq P_t \leq 0.26\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\).
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy \(L = 86 \pm 25\) MeV from isospin diffusion data.
- The difference between dynamical and thermodynamical methods for \(\rho_t\) is less than \(0.01\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Using upper bounds (\(\rho_t = 0.065\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), \(P_t = 0.26\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\)), a minimum radius constraint for the Vela pulsar is \(R \geq 4.7 + 4.0 M/M_\odot\) km; for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) mass, the radius exceeds \(10.5\) km.
- Using lower bounds (\(\rho_t = 0.040\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), \(P_t = 0.01\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\)), the radius constraint is approximately \(R = 5.5 + 14.5 M/M_\odot\) km.
- A previous constraint by Link (1999) using \(\rho_t = 0.075\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and \(P_t = 0.65\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\) gave \(R \geq 3.6 + 3.9 M/M_\odot\) km.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the neutron star crust inner edge by Kubis (2007), who used the parabolic approximation of the MDI EOS in a thermodynamical approach, and by Link (1999), who derived constraints from Vela pulsar glitches. The authors show that the parabolic approximation introduces large errors, especially for stiffer symmetry energies, and that using a full EOS constrained by terrestrial heavy-ion isospin diffusion data (Tsang 2004, Chen 2005, Li 2005) yields tighter bounds on \(\rho_t\) and \(P_t\). These constraints enable more restrictive predictions for the mass-radius relation of the Vela pulsar and can be combined with the observed crustal fraction of the moment of inertia (\(\Delta I/I > 0.014\)) to further test neutron star models.
