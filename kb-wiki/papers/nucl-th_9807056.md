# Infrared Behaviour of Propagators and Vertices

> We elucidate constraints imposed by confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking on the infrared behaviour of the dressed-quark and -gluon propagators, and dressed-quark-gluon vertex. In covariant gauges the dressing of the gluon propagator is completely specified by P(k^2):= 1/[1+Pi(k^2)], where Pi(k^2) is the vacuum polarisation. In the absence of particle-like singularities in the dressed-quark-gluon vertex, extant proposals for the dressed-gluon propagator that manifest P(k^2=0)=0 and Max[P(k^2)]~10 neither confine quarks nor break chiral symmetry dynamically. This class includes all existing estimates of P(k^2) via numerical simulations.

## Key claim

If the dressed-quark-gluon vertex has no particle-like singularities, then any dressed-gluon propagator that satisfies P(k²=0)=0 and has a maximum value of order 10 or less can neither confine quarks nor break chiral symmetry dynamically, contrary to what some lattice-QCD and Dyson-Schwinger equation studies had suggested.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (the "gap equation") for the dressed-quark propagator using a gluon propagator fitted to lattice-QCD data from Marenzoni et al. (1995), combined with a one-loop perturbative running coupling in the ultraviolet. They tested three different Ansätze for the dressed-quark-gluon vertex that lack particle-like singularities: the bare vertex, the Ball-Chiu vertex (1980), and the Curtis-Pennington vertex (1990), solving for the quark propagator functions A(p²) and B(p²) in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- The lattice-fitted gluon propagator form P_L(k²) has parameters: M ≈ 0.16 GeV, Z ≈ 0.1, η ≈ 0.53, with inverse lattice spacing 1/a ≈ 2.0 GeV
- P_L(k²) reaches a maximum value of 13.6 at k² = 21 Λ_QCD² (with Λ_QCD ≈ 220 MeV for N_f=4)
- The matching procedure yields g_m = 0.65 and k_m² = 30 Λ_QCD² for the combined infrared-ultraviolet gluon model
- With all three vertex Ansätze, the chiral-limit order parameter X = B(p²=0) was found to be zero, indicating no dynamical chiral symmetry breaking
- The quark wave-function renormalization Z(p²=0) was nonzero for all vertex Ansätze, indicating no confinement
- Only when the gluon propagator peak was artificially enhanced to a maximum value of 210 at k² = 0.98 Λ_QCD² (requiring a strength parameter ς ≥ 300) did the system show signs of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Hawes et al. (1997) and Bender et al. (1996) that showed a simple k⁴/(k⁴+b⁴) gluon propagator fails to produce confinement or dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. It directly tests the lattice-QCD results of Marenzoni et al. (1995) and the Dyson-Schwinger equation study of Stingl (1996), showing that their proposed infrared-suppressed gluon propagators are incompatible with observed strong-interaction phenomena unless the quark-gluon vertex contains particle-like singularities. The paper establishes that future lattice simulations must determine whether the infrared mass scale M vanishes in the continuum limit, and that Dyson-Schwinger studies must examine the sensitivity of their results to truncations of the ghost sector.
