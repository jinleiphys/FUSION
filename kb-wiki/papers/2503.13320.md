# Radiative corrections on vortical spin polarization in hot QCD matter

> We investigate the radiative corrections on spin polarization of relativistic fermions induced by vortical fields in thermal-equilibrium QCD matter at weak coupling. Such corrections stem from the self-energy gradients in quantum kinetic theory, which are further obtained by a more systematic and general approach through the Keldysh equation. By applying the hard-thermal-loop approximation, we obtain new corrections upon the spin-polarization spectrum and also the axial-charge current in connection to the axial/chiral vortical effect for massive quarks up to the leading order of the QCD coupling. Further influence on spin alignment of vector mesons from similar effects is also analyzed.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to spin polarization induced by vorticity in thermal-equilibrium QCD matter arise from self-energy gradients in quantum kinetic theory, and these corrections exist even under equilibrium conditions at leading order in the QCD coupling, contrary to the naive expectation that interaction-dependent spin corrections only appear out of equilibrium.

## Method

The authors derived interaction-corrected Wigner functions for chiral fermions by solving the equations of motion under quasi-particle approximations up to first order in both the Planck constant and the self-energy. They then developed a parallel formalism based on the Keldysh equation in phase space, which allows systematic inclusion of interaction corrections for massive fermions, and applied the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation to compute the one-loop self-energies in a weakly coupled QCD plasma.

## Key numbers

- Fermionic thermal mass: \( m_{\mathrm{f}}^{2} = \frac{C_{\mathrm{F}} g^{2}}{8} \left( T^{2} + \frac{\mu^{2}}{\pi^{2}} \right) \), where \( C_{\mathrm{F}} \) is the color factor and \( g \) is the QCD coupling
- The vector-sector self-energy at HTL order: \( \mathrm{Im}\,\Sigma_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{V},\beta} = -m_{\mathrm{f}}^{2} \left[ \frac{u^{\beta}}{|\boldsymbol{q}|} Q_{0}\left(\frac{q_{0}}{|\boldsymbol{q}|}\right) + \frac{q_{\perp}^{\beta}}{|\boldsymbol{q}|^{2}} Q_{1}\left(\frac{q_{0}}{|\boldsymbol{q}|}\right) \right] \)
- The axial-vector self-energy at HTL order: \( \mathrm{Im}\,\Sigma_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{A},\beta} = -\frac{C_{\mathrm{F}}g^{2}T\mu}{16\pi^{2}m_{\rm f}^2} \hbar \epsilon^{\beta\nu\rho\sigma} \Omega_{\rho\sigma} \mathrm{Im}\Sigma_{\mathrm{f},\nu}^{\mathrm{V}} \), where \( \Omega_{\rho\sigma} \) is the thermal vorticity
- Collision contributions are at least of order \( \mathcal{O}(g^{4}) \), while the radiative corrections studied are at order \( \mathcal{O}(g^{2}) \)

## Context

This work extends the study of radiative corrections to the axial/chiral vortical effect (AVE/CVE) previously investigated for massless fermions by Golkar (2012) and Hou (2012) to massive quarks in QCD matter. It provides a systematic framework using the Keldysh equation that parallels the quantum kinetic theory approach of Yamamoto (2023) and Fang (2023), and enables future calculations of interaction corrections to local spin polarization of Lambda hyperons measured by the STAR collaboration (2019, 2023) and to vector-meson spin alignment.
