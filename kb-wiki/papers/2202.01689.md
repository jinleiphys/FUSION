# Benchmarking intra-nuclear cascade models for neutrino scattering with relativistic optical potentials

> The description of final-state interactions (FSI) in the large phase space probed in neutrino experiments poses a great challenge. In neutrino experiments, which operate under semi-inclusive conditions, cascade models are commonly used for this task, while under exclusive conditions FSI can be treated with relativistic optical potentials (ROP). We formulate conditions under which the ROP approach and cascade model can be directly compared. We feed the NEUT cascade with events from a relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation calculation that uses the real part of an optical potential. Cuts on the missing energy of the resulting events are applied to define a set of events that can be directly compared to RDWIA calculations with the full optical potential. The NEUT cascade and ROP agree for proton kinetic energies $T_p > 150$ MeV for carbon, oxygen and calcium nuclei when a realistic nuclear density is used to introduce events in the cascade. For $T_p < 100$ MeV the ROP and NEUT cross sections differ in shape and differences in magnitude are larger than 50 \%. Single transverse variables allow to distinguish different approaches to FSI, but due to a large non-QE contribution the comparison to T2K data does not give an unambiguous view of FSI. We discuss electron scattering and argue that with a cut in missing energy FSI can be studied with minimal confounding factors in e.g. $e4ν$. The agreement of the ROP and NEUT for T2K conditions lends confidence to these models as a tool in oscillation analyses for sufficiently large nucleon kinetic energies. These results urge for caution when a cascade model is applied for small nucleon energies. The assessment of model assumptions relevant to this region are strongly encouraged. This paper provides novel constraints on cascade models from proton-nucleus scattering that can be easily applied to other neutrino event generators.

# Benchmarking intra-nuclear cascade models for neutrino scattering with relativistic optical potentials

## Key claim
The NEUT intra-nuclear cascade model and relativistic optical potential (ROP) calculations agree for proton kinetic energies above 150 MeV across carbon, oxygen, and calcium nuclei, but diverge strongly below 100 MeV where differences exceed 50%; this agreement is conditional on using a realistic nuclear density to introduce events in the cascade and on applying missing-energy cuts to isolate elastic final-state interactions.

## Method
The authors generated charged-current neutrino events using the T2K near-detector muon neutrino flux and a relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculation that employs only the real part of an optical potential (rROP). These events were fed into the NEUT intra-nuclear cascade model, and cuts on missing energy were applied to select events that underwent only elastic final-state interactions, enabling direct comparison to RDWIA calculations using the full complex optical potential. The comparison was performed for proton kinetic energy distributions across carbon, oxygen, and calcium targets, and single transverse variables were examined against T2K data.

## Key numbers
- Agreement between NEUT cascade and ROP for proton kinetic energies \(T_p > 150\) MeV for carbon, oxygen, and calcium nuclei
- For \(T_p < 100\) MeV, differences in magnitude between ROP and NEUT cross sections are larger than 50%
- The optical potentials used are fitted to proton-nucleus scattering data with kinetic energies up to 1040 MeV and down to 29 MeV for carbon, 23 MeV for oxygen, and 21 MeV for calcium (Cooper 1993)
- A dipole axial form factor with cut-off mass \(M_A = 1.05\) GeV was used for the axial current
- The NEUT reaction cross section for p-12C is comparable to ROP and data for kinetic energies above approximately 100 MeV but does not reproduce the sharp increase at smaller energies

## Context
This work extends the comparison of cascade models and optical potential approaches beyond previous validation studies (ValidationmethodsINC 2021, Niewczas Transparency 2021) that showed convergence at high energies but divergence at low energies across different cascade models. The method provides novel constraints on cascade models from proton-nucleus scattering phenomenology that can be applied to other neutrino event generators such as GENIE and NuWro. The results enable more reliable use of cascade models in neutrino oscillation analyses for sufficiently large nucleon kinetic energies, while urging caution for low-energy applications and encouraging assessment of model assumptions in that regime. The approach also offers a path to study final-state interactions with minimal confounding factors in electron scattering experiments such as the e4ν analysis of CLAS data.
