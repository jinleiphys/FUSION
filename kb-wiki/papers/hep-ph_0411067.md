# Description of Heavy Quark Systems by means of Energy Dependent Potentials

> We apply, for the first time, an energy dependent Schrodinger equation to describe static properties of heavy quark systems, i.e. charmonium and bottonium. We show that a good description of the eigenstates and reasonable values for the widths can be obtained. Values of the radii and of the density at the origin are also given. We compare the results to those deduced with a Schrodinger equation implemented with potentials used so far. We note that the energy dependence of the confining potential provides a natural mechanism for the saturation of the spectra. Our results introduce a new class of potentials for the description of heavy quark systems.

# Description of Heavy Quark Systems by means of Energy Dependent Potentials

## Key claim
For the first time, an energy-dependent Schrödinger equation is applied to describe charmonium and bottonium, showing that a good description of eigenstates and reasonable widths can be obtained; however, the linear radial dependence gives much better results than the harmonic oscillator form, and the density at the origin for the bottomonium 1S state is sensibly lower than the Buchmüller-Tye value, which may point to the need for a more universal radial shape or a more pertinent energy dependence.

## Method
The authors solve an energy-dependent Schrödinger equation with a potential of the form V(r,E) = (1 + γE)f(r) + D₀ℓ² + V₀, using two radial dependences for f(r): a linear form (λmr) and a harmonic form (mω²r²/2). Parameters are determined by constraining energy differences between the ground state and the 1P, 2S, 2P, and 3S states to experimental energies for both charmonium and bottomonium, with the 1D state also considered for c̄c. Leptonic widths are computed using the van Royen-Weisskopf formula without radiative or relativistic corrections.

- For c̄c with linear potential: Γ₁S = 7.51 keV, Γ₁S/Γ₂S = 2.18 (experimental: 5.26±0.37 keV, ratio 2.4)

## Context
This work extends the tradition of potential models for heavy quark systems, which includes the Buchmüller-Tye (1981) and Cornell potentials, by introducing energy-dependent potentials as a new class. The energy dependence provides a natural mechanism for saturation of the spectra, where state energies approach a limit as n and ℓ increase, unlike usual confining potentials. The approach enables future studies of other radial shapes, combinations of usual and energy-dependent potentials, and applications to other systems, while the connection to more fundamental theories of elementary particles requires further investigation.
