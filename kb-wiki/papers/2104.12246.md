# Mapping Dirac--Hartree--Fock approach onto relativistic mean field model

> The exchange part of energy density of the linear Dirac--Hartree--Fock (DHF) model in symmetric nuclear matter is evaluated in a parameter--free closed form and expressed as density functional. After the rearranging terms the relativistic mean-field approach with density-dependent couplings may be recovered with density dependence coming from the Fock exchange. The formalism developed, is then extended to the nonlinear DHF approximation with field self-couplings allowed. The nonlinear self--interactions result in essential density dependence (medium modification) of effective couplings that are decoupled from the Fock ones.

## Key claim

The exchange (Fock) part of the Dirac–Hartree–Fock energy density in symmetric nuclear matter can be evaluated analytically and re-expressed as a density functional, thereby mapping the DHF model onto a relativistic mean-field model with density-dependent couplings, where the density dependence arises from the Fock exchange and, in the nonlinear case, from field self-couplings that are decoupled from the Fock contributions.

## Method

The authors analytically evaluated the exchange integrals for the σ, ω, and π meson contributions to the DHF energy density in symmetric nuclear matter, obtaining closed-form expressions involving exchange shape functions Xθ, Xϕ, and Xπ. They then regrouped the direct and exchange terms into a form identical to the RMF potential energy density, but with effective density-dependent couplings Γσ and Γω that incorporate the Fock contributions. The formalism was extended to include nonlinear σ and ω self-couplings by introducing effective meson masses and self-coupling shape functions G2 and G3, which were solved analytically for cubic and quartic self-interactions separately.

## Key numbers

* The exchange correlations account for approximately 30% of the total coupling strength in both scalar and vector channels, as shown by the ratios of Fock to Hartree parts.
* The ω-meson exchange contributes from about 25% of the total vector coupling strength squared at zero density up to more than 35% for the scalar channel, decreasing with increasing Fermi momentum.
* The σ-meson exchange contribution to the scalar channel starts at about −12% at zero density and slowly goes to zero at high density; in the vector sector it starts at about 18% at zero density and decreases with increasing density.
* The π-meson addition to the effective scalar coupling squared starts at about 6% at zero density, decreases rapidly to about −5% at saturation density, and then remains roughly constant.
* At saturation, the potential energies per particle from the DHF calculation of Bouyssy (1987) and the mapped RMF model agree closely: direct σ term −171 MeV vs. −176.5 MeV, direct ω term 145 MeV vs. 145.1 MeV, exchange σ term 34 MeV vs. 34.2 MeV, exchange ω term −24 MeV vs. −25.4 MeV, and exchange π term −6.7 MeV vs. −6.53 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to incorporate Fock exchange into relativistic nuclear matter descriptions, such as the analytical evaluation of the σ–ω model exchange energy density by Schmid et al. (1995) and the kinetic approach using Wigner transforms by Greco et al. (2001). It provides a parameter-free mapping of the DHF model onto an RMF form with density-dependent couplings, reducing the arbitrariness in choosing functional forms for the couplings at low and high densities, which is relevant for studies of exotic nuclei and neutron star matter. The approach can be generalized to asymmetric nuclear matter and a full set of exchanged mesons.
