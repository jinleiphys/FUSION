# Heavy flavor jet production and substructure in electron-nucleus collisions

> Deep inelastic scattering on nuclei at the Electron-Ion Collider will open new opportunities to investigate the structure of matter. Heavy flavor-tagged jets are complementary probes of the partonic composition and transport coefficients of large nuclei, but introduce a new mass scale that modifies the structure of parton showers and must be carefully accounted for in perturbative calculations. In the framework of soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon interactions, we present the first calculation of inclusive charm-jet and bottom-jet cross sections in electron-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading order and compare them to the reference electron-proton case. We also show predictions for the heavy flavor-tagged jet momentum sharing distributions to further clarify the correlated in-medium modification of jet substructure.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of inclusive charm-jet and bottom-jet cross sections in electron-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading order, but the nuclear modification ratio results depend on the choice of jet radius and are significantly affected by initial-state nuclear parton distribution function effects that must be removed via a double-ratio technique to isolate final-state in-medium shower modifications.

## Method

The authors use soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon interactions (SCET_M) to compute semi-inclusive heavy flavor jet cross sections in deep inelastic scattering, combining NLO parton-level cross sections with semi-inclusive jet functions that include both vacuum and medium-induced contributions. The medium-induced splitting kernels are included up to first order in opacity, and the jet functions are evolved using DGLAP-like renormalization group equations in Mellin moment space. The soft-dropped momentum sharing distribution \(z_g\) is calculated for heavy flavor jets using the full splitting kernels including mass effects.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification ratio \(R_{eA}\) for charm and bottom jets is presented for 10 GeV (e) × 100 GeV (A) and 18 GeV (e) × 275 GeV (A) collisions, with jet radii \(R = 0.3, 0.5, 0.8\) in rapidity bins \(0<\eta<2\) and \(2<\eta<4\).
- The double ratio \(R_{eA}(R)/R_{eA}(R=0.8)\) for \(R=0.3\) shows suppression that can be as large as a factor of two for small jet radii.
- The nominal transport coefficient of cold nuclear matter for quarks is set to \(\langle q_\perp^2 \rangle / \lambda_q = 0.05\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, with theoretical uncertainties evaluated by varying this parameter up and down by a factor of two.
- For the substructure analysis, the jet energy in the nuclear rest frame is fixed at 94 GeV with jet radius \(R=0.4\), and transverse momenta from 10 GeV to 80 GeV are considered (with the note that \(p_T > 30\) GeV cannot be reached at EIC kinematics).
- The modification of the \(z_g\) distribution in e+A relative to e+p is on the order of 10% or smaller for the EIC-accessible kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed for heavy flavor jets in heavy-ion collisions (Li 2017, Li 2018, Kang 2018) to the cold nuclear matter environment of electron-nucleus collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider. It builds on previous calculations of light jet rates and jet charge in DIS on nuclei (Li 2020) and semi-inclusive D-meson and B-meson production in DIS (Li 2020). The results enable experimental studies of heavy flavor jet quenching and substructure modification in cold nuclear matter at the EIC, complementing measurements at RHIC and the LHC, and provide a new probe of gluon nuclear parton distribution functions and transport coefficients.
