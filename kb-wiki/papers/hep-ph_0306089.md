# The $p_T$-Spectra of Some Non-pion Secondaries in High Energy NN to NA/AA Collisions and the Combinational Approach

> In continuation of our perusal of the studies on transverse momentum spectra for the main varieties of secondaries from a consistent and comprehensive phenomenological approach, we propose to take up here -- after a successful completion of reporting in detail the results (Ref.[4] in the text) on our analyses of the $p_T$-spectra of pions -- the studies specially on production of kaons, protons and antiprotons in several proton-induced and nucleus-involved collisions at high energies. The measured data on inclusive cross sections of kaons, protons and antiprotons from the various published sources have here been assorted first. Next, data on the $p_T$-spectra of the specific secondaries produced in PP and $P\bar{P}$ reactions have been scanned and analyzed with the help of Hagedorn's model(HM). Thereafter a connector, named here the combinational approach(CA), has been constructed and used to analyze the data on $p_T$-spectra of some major category of non-pion secondaries produced in nucleus-nucleus(AA/AB) collisions at high energies. And these exercises have, finally, led to the modestly successful interpretations of a wide band of data with the revelation of some insightful physical aspects of high energy interactions. The limitations of the approach have also been precisely pointed out in the end.

# The $p_T$-Spectra of Some Non-pion Secondaries in High Energy NN to NA/AA Collisions and the Combinational Approach

## Key claim

The authors propose that a "combinational approach" (CA), combining Hagedorn's model for proton-proton collisions, Peitzmann's scaling approach for nuclear collisions, and a new quadratic parametrization of the nuclear exponent (De-Bhattacharyya parametrization), can describe the transverse momentum spectra of kaons, protons, and antiprotons across a wide range of proton-induced and nucleus-nucleus collisions, though the fits are notably worse at low $p_T$ for nuclear collisions compared to proton-proton reactions, and the approach cannot accommodate simulation-based superposition effects present in experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors first fit Hagedorn's power-law formula $E d^3\sigma/dp^3 = C_1 (1 + p_T/p_0)^{-n}$ to inclusive cross-section data for $K^\pm$, protons, and antiprotons in $PP$ and $P\bar{P}$ collisions at various energies, extracting $p_0$ and $n$ values and their $\sqrt{s}$-dependences. They then construct a "combinational approach" where the nuclear cross-section for $AB \rightarrow Q X$ is given by $C_3 (AB)^{(\alpha p_T - \beta p_T^2)} (1 + p_T/p_0)^{-n}$, with $p_0$ and $n$ taken from the $PP$ fits at the corresponding $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$, and $\alpha$, $\beta$, $C_3$ obtained by fitting to nuclear collision data. The quadratic exponent $g(p_T) = \epsilon + \alpha p_T - \beta p_T^2$ is reduced to $\alpha p_T - \beta p_T^2$ by absorbing the constant term into the normalization.

## Key numbers

- For $PP$ collisions, the parameters $p_0$ and $n$ for kaons, protons, and antiprotons show "very slow fall-off with energy" in $\sqrt{s}$, with the ratio $p_0/n$ signaling a "march towards a steady state of hadronization" (no specific numerical values given for individual fits in the truncated text).
- The $\sqrt{s}$-dependences of $p_0$ and $n$ are parametrized as $p_0(\sqrt{s}) = a + b/(\sqrt{s} \ln\sqrt{s})$ and $n(\sqrt{s}) = \acute{a} + \acute{b}/\ln^2(\sqrt{s})$, with constants $a$, $b$, $\acute{a}$, $\acute{b}$ presented in Table VI (values not shown in truncated text).
- The values of $\alpha$ and $\beta$ across different nuclear collision systems are described as "too close" to each other, with $\alpha$ uniformly non-zero but limited in magnitude, and $\beta$ negative, indicating "very finite degree of the nuclear effects" (specific numerical values from Tables VII-IX not shown in truncated text).
- The parameter $\epsilon$ (value of $g(p_T)$ at $p_T=0$) is noted to be less than unity for $p_T < 1.5$ GeV/c, based on earlier experimental works (Albrecht et al., Antreasyan et al., Aggarwal et al.).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful analysis of pion $p_T$-spectra (De and Bhattacharyya, 2002) to non-pion secondaries (kaons, protons, antiprotons), following the same combinational approach methodology. The approach builds on Hagedorn's model (originally proposed only for pions), Peitzmann's scaling relationship between nucleon-nucleon and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and a quadratic parametrization inspired by earlier work on the EMC effect by one of the authors (Bhattacharyya). The study enables a unified phenomenological description of particle production across $PP$, $P\bar{P}$, $pA$, and $AA$ collisions, though the authors acknowledge major limitations: the approach cannot predict $\alpha$ and $\beta$ values quantitatively, depends entirely on prior knowledge of $PP$ production characteristics, fails to address actual particle production mechanisms, and is insensitive to the charge state of secondaries.
