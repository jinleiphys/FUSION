# Hyper-neutron stars from an ab initio calculation

> The equation of state (EoS) of neutron matter plays a decisive role to understand the neutron star properties and the gravitational waves from neutron star mergers. At sufficient densities, the appearance of hyperons generally softens the EoS, leading to a reduction in the maximum mass of neutron stars well below the observed values of about 2 solar masses. Even though repulsive three-body forces are known to solve this so-called ``hyperon puzzle'', so far performing \textit{ab initio} calculations with a substantial number of hyperons for neutron star properties has remained elusive. Starting from the newly developed auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo algorithm to simulate hyper-neutron matter (HNM) without any sign oscillations, we derive three distinct EoSs by employing the state-of-the-art Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory. We include $NΛ$, $ΛΛ$ two-body forces, $NNΛ$, and $NΛΛ$ three-body forces. Consequently, we determine essential astrophysical quantities such as the neutron star mass, radius, tidal deformability, and the universal $I$-Love-$Q$ relation. The maximum mass, radius and tidal deformability of a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star are predicted to be $2.17(1)(1)~M_\odot$, $R_{1.4M\odot}=13.10(1)(7)~$km, and $Λ_{1.4M_\odot}=597(5)(18)$, respectively, based on our most realistic EoS. These predictions are in good agreement with the latest astrophysical constraints derived from observations of massive neutron stars, gravitational waves, and joint mass-radius measurements. Also, for the first time in \textit{ab initio} calculations, we investigate both non-rotating and rotating neutron star configurations. The results indicate that the impact of rotational dynamics on the maximum mass is small, regardless of whether hyperons are present in the EoS or not.

## Key claim
Using a newly developed auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo algorithm that avoids sign oscillations, the authors perform the first *ab initio* calculations of hyper-neutron matter with an arbitrary number of hyperons, finding that the maximum neutron star mass remains above 2 solar masses even when hyperons are present, provided that three-body forces are included.

## Method
The authors employ Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) with pionless effective field theory at leading order, using a Hamiltonian that includes nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-hyperon, and hyperon-hyperon two-body forces, as well as three-body forces (NNN, NNΛ, NΛΛ). They simulate hyper-neutron matter using an auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo method that introduces a single auxiliary field for the combined nucleon-hyperon density, treating the residual hyperon-hyperon interaction perturbatively. The resulting equations of state are then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating neutron stars and the Einstein field equations for rotating configurations using the RNS code.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star mass: \(2.17(1)(1)\,M_\odot\) (based on the most realistic equation of state)
- Radius of a \(1.4\,M_\odot\) neutron star: \(R_{1.4M_\odot}=13.10(1)(7)\) km
- Tidal deformability of a \(1.4\,M_\odot\) neutron star: \(\Lambda_{1.4M_\odot}=597(5)(18)\)
- Nucleon mass used: \(m_N=938.92\) MeV; hyperon mass used: \(m_\Lambda=1115.68\) MeV
- Coupling constants for NN interaction: \(c_{{}^1{\rm S}_0}=-1.21\times10^{-7}\) MeV\(^{-2}\) and \(c_{{}^3{\rm S}_1}=-1.92\times10^{-7}\) MeV\(^{-2}\)

## Context
This work extends the NLEFT framework, previously applied to pure neutron matter and light hypernuclei (Frame 2020), to hyper-neutron matter with arbitrary hyperon fractions, directly addressing the "hyperon puzzle" identified by Schaffner-Bielich (2008) and others. The results agree with astrophysical constraints from massive neutron star observations and gravitational wave measurements (LIGO/Virgo 2017, 2018), and demonstrate for the first time in *ab initio* calculations that rotational dynamics have only a small effect on the maximum mass regardless of hyperon presence. This enables future studies of neutron star mergers and multi-messenger astrophysics with microscopic hyperonic equations of state.
