# Uncertainties in modeling low-energy neutrino induced reactions on iron group nuclei

> Charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for 54,56Fe and 58,60Ni are calculated and compared using frameworks based on relativistic and Skyrme energy density functionals, and the shell model. The current theoretical uncertainties in modeling neutrino-nucleus cross sections are assessed in relation to the predicted Gamow-Teller transition strength and available data, multipole decomposition of the cross sections, and cross sections averaged over the Michel flux and Fermi-Dirac distribution. Employing different microscopic approaches and models, the DAR neutrino-56Fe cross section and its theoretical uncertainty are estimated: <sigma>_th=(258+-57) 10^{-42} cm^2, in very good agreement with the experimental value: <sigma>_exp=(256+-108+-43) 10^{-42} cm^2.

## Key claim

The theoretical uncertainty in the DAR neutrino-⁵⁶Fe cross section, estimated from multiple models, is actually smaller than the experimental uncertainty, but this agreement depends on the use of a quenching factor for the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) that is constrained by Gamow-Teller transition data.

## Method

Charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Fe and ⁵⁸,⁶⁰Ni were calculated using three frameworks: the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model plus Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RHB+RQRPA) with the DD-ME2 interaction; the shell model with the GXPF1J effective interaction for 1⁺ transitions supplemented by Skyrme RPA (SGII) for higher multipoles; and Skyrme RPA (SGII, SLy5) for comparison. The Gamow-Teller transition strengths, multipole decomposition of cross sections, and flux-averaged cross sections over the Michel spectrum and Fermi-Dirac distribution were compared across models and against experimental data from the KARMEN collaboration.

## Key numbers

- DAR neutrino-⁵⁶Fe theoretical cross section: \(<\sigma>_{th} = (258 \pm 57) \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\)
- DAR neutrino-⁵⁶Fe experimental cross section: \(<\sigma>_{exp} = (256 \pm 108 \pm 43) \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\)
- Individual model results for DAR neutrino-⁵⁶Fe: RHB+RQRPA (DD-ME2) = \(263 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\); shell model (GXPF1J) + RPA (SGII) = \(259 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\); RPA with Landau-Migdal force = \(240 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\) (Kolbe 2001); QRPA(SIII) = \(352 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\) (Lazauskas 2007); QRPA based on G-matrix = \(173.5 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\) (Cheoun 2010)
- GT⁻ transition strength B(GT⁻) for ⁵⁶Fe: shell model (GXPF1J) = 9.5; RQRPA (DD-ME2) = 9.7; experimental = \(9.9 \pm 2.4\) (Rapaport 1983), all with quenching factor 0.74 in \(g_A\)
- GT⁻ transition strength B(GT⁻) for ⁶⁰Ni: shell model = 9.9; experimental = \(7.2 \pm 1.8\) (Rapaport 1983)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of low-energy neutrino-nucleus reactions on iron group nuclei, including the QRPA with Skyrme functionals by Lazauskas (2007) and the relativistic QRPA by Paar (2008), and provides a systematic comparison with the shell model calculations of Suzuki (2009). The results enable improved estimates of theoretical uncertainties for neutrino detector response and supernova neutrino physics, and show that further reduction of uncertainty requires constraining the quenching of \(g_A\) using Gamow-Teller data, particularly for nuclei like ⁶⁰Ni where the shell model overestimates the measured strength.
