# Giant dipole resonance with exact treatment of thermal fluctuations

> The shape fluctuations due to thermal effects in the giant dipole resonance (GDR) observables are calculated using the exact free energies evaluated at fixed spin and temperature. The results obtained are compared with Landau theory calculations done by parameterizing the free energy. The Landau theory is found to be insufficient when the shell effects are dominating.

# Giant dipole resonance with exact treatment of thermal fluctuations

## Key claim
The Landau theory parameterization of free energies is insufficient for calculating giant dipole resonance (GDR) observables when shell effects are dominating, particularly at low temperatures and high spins; however, at experimentally observed temperatures (above approximately 1.5 MeV) the Landau theory and exact calculations give equivalent results despite differences in the free energy surfaces at larger deformations.

## Method
The authors performed Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations to obtain exact free energies at fixed deformation, spin, and temperature, then numerically integrated over the two-dimensional deformation space (β and γ) using 32-point Gaussian quadrature to incorporate thermal shape fluctuations. They compared these exact calculations with the extended Landau theory approach, where the free energy is parameterized using temperature-dependent constants fitted to Nilsson-Strutinsky results at zero spin and then extended to higher spins via a rigid-rotor relation. GDR cross sections were constructed from Lorentzian components with an energy-dependent width following a power law Γ_i ≈ 0.026 E_i^1.9.

## Key numbers
- For ⁹⁰Zr and ⁹²Mo at experimentally observed temperatures, the most probable shapes were β = 0.0, 0.09, and 0.1 for spins 9ħ, 22ħ, and 33ħ respectively, with γ = −180° for all spins.
- The thermally averaged deformation β̄ was 0.29, 0.33, and 0.41 for spins 9ħ, 22ħ, and 33ħ respectively, with γ̄ ≈ 148°.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at I = 0ħ, the Landau theory and exact calculations gave the same GDR widths above T ∼ 1.5 MeV, but deviated at lower temperatures due to strong shell effects.
- The parameter η (characterizing the isovector component) was set to 2.6 for ⁹⁰Zr and ⁹²Mo, and 3.4 for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The sum-rule parameter α was fixed at 0.3 for all nuclei considered.

## Context
This work extends the thermal fluctuation formalism of Alhassid et al. (1990s) by replacing the Landau theory parameterization of free energies with exact Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations at each integration point, enabled by modern computing capabilities. The results show that while Landau theory is adequate at higher temperatures where shell effects are weak, it fails at low temperatures where shell effects dominate, particularly for spin-driven shell effects that cannot be captured by the Landau theory's extrapolation from zero spin via the moment of inertia. This enables more accurate calculations of GDR observables in hot rotating nuclei at low temperatures, relevant to recent experiments by Heckman et al. (2003), Camera et al. (2003), and Bracco et al. (2003).
