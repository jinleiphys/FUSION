# Quasibound states of an antiproton and a hydrogen atom

> Accurate three-body quantal calculations of the system composed of a proton, an antiproton, and an electron are performed in perimetric coordinates with the Lagrange-mesh method, an approximate variational calculation with the simplicity of a calculation on a grid. Quasibound states with respect to the $\bar{p}$ + H($n = 2$) threshold are obtained for $L = 60$ to 71 for various vibrational excitations. Their energies have accuracies up to about $10^{-14}$ atomic units while less precise energies are determined for $L = 56-59$ broader resonances. Their structure is analyzed with the help of mean distances between the particles. These mean distances indicate that the proton-electron subsystem is in excited states, mostly $n = 2$, as predicted by Sakimoto (Phys. Rev. A 98, 042503, 2018) with the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. A comparison performed with this approximation provides the accuracies of its energies and of its proton-antiproton mean distances.

## Key claim

Accurate three-body quantal calculations confirm the existence of quasibound states of the proton-antiproton-electron system for total orbital momenta L = 60 to 71, with the proton-electron subsystem predominantly in the n = 2 excited state as predicted by Sakimoto (2018) using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, though the present fully quantal method reveals that the Born-Oppenheimer energies differ from the three-body results by up to about 4 × 10⁻⁴ atomic units and that no quasibound state exists for L = 72 despite the Born-Oppenheimer prediction.

## Method

The three-body Schrödinger equation for the proton, antiproton, and electron interacting via Coulomb forces was solved in perimetric coordinates using the Lagrange-mesh method, an approximate variational calculation on a grid that avoids analytical integral evaluations. The wave function was expanded in parity-projected Wigner angular functions with total orbital momentum L and natural parity (−1)ˡ, truncated at Kmax = 3, and the resulting sparse matrix eigenvalue problem was solved for L = 56 to 72. Mean distances between particles and probabilities of K components were computed from the eigenvectors to identify physical quasibound states and distinguish them from square-integrable approximations of continuum states.

## Key numbers

- Energies for L = 60 to 71 have accuracies up to about 10⁻¹⁴ atomic units; for L = 56 to 59, fewer stable digits are obtained due to broader resonances
- Ground-state energies (ν = 0) range from −0.15155 a.u. at L = 56 to −0.12494845876 a.u. at L = 72
- For L = 70, the ν = 5 energy is −0.12500309 a.u., very close to the p̄ + H(n = 2) dissociation threshold at −1/8 a.u.
- Mean proton-antiproton distance ⟨r_{pp̄}⟩ for ν = 0 ranges from 3.96 a.u. at L = 56 to 50.412 a.u. at L = 72
- Mean proton-electron distance ⟨r_{pe}⟩ for ν = 0 decreases from 12.5 a.u. at L = 56 to 5.554296 a.u. at L = 72, approaching 6 a.u. (the mean distance for free hydrogen n = 2) near L = 69
- The K = 1 probability P_{L0}(K=1) for ν = 0 decreases from 0.082 at L = 56 to 0.000474112 at L = 72
- Born-Oppenheimer energies differ from three-body energies by less than 4 × 10⁻⁴ a.u. for L = 58 to 71
- Born-Oppenheimer mean proton-antiproton distances are between 1 and 2 percent larger than quantal values for L = 58 to 70

## Context

This work provides the first fully quantal three-body confirmation of quasibound states in the proton-antiproton-electron system, which were previously predicted by Sakimoto (2018) using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for total orbital momenta between 60 and 73. The system is analogous to antiprotonic helium, where similar quasistable structures have been studied experimentally and theoretically, but with a proton replacing the helium nucleus. The results enable future studies of radiative decay channels (photon emission from the proton-electron subsystem) and may guide experimental searches for these long-lived states using low-energy antiproton beams.
