# The CP-odd nucleon interaction and the value of T-violation in nuclei

> The relations between the value of T- and P-violating correlations in neutron scattering and different models of CP violation are discussed. It is shown that a specific structure of CP-odd interactions gives the possibility to obtain the essential information about CP-odd interaction at the quark-gluon level from nuclear experimental data. The up-to-date estimations for CP- violating nucleon coupling constants show that CP violation in neutron scattering is sensitive to many models of CP violation.

# The CP-odd nucleon interaction and the value of T-violation in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that, despite the complexity of nuclear compound states, the ratio of CP-odd to P-odd matrix elements in neutron scattering can be related to the ratio of CP-odd to P-odd nucleon coupling constants without large suppression factors, enabling extraction of quark-gluon level CP-violating information from nuclear data. This claim is hedged by the condition that the approach works only if one calculates ratios of CP-odd to P-odd parameters at each level of the theoretical hierarchy, thereby canceling many model-dependent uncertainties.

## Method

The author derives relations between T- and P-violating correlations in neutron scattering (the difference of total cross sections and the neutron spin rotation angle) and the underlying CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interactions. Using a single-particle model with one-body potentials for P-violating and CP-violating interactions, the ratio of compound nuclear matrix elements is expressed in terms of the ratio of nucleon coupling constants. The CP-odd nucleon coupling constants are then estimated for four classes of CP-violation models (Kobayashi-Maskawa, left-right symmetric, Weinberg Higgs, and QCD theta-term) by comparing effective low-energy Lagrangians and using factorization approximations for meson-nucleon couplings.

## Key numbers

- The P-odd parameter Δσ_P in the vicinity of p-wave resonances has a relative value up to 10^{-1} compared with the neutron total cross section.
- The CP-odd parameter <λ> can be measured at a level up to 10^{-4} in neutron scattering experiments.
- The parameter ξ, relating nuclear matrix elements, has numerical values in the range 1 to 7.
- For the Kobayashi-Maskawa model, λ_KM ≤ 10^{-10}.
- For the left-right symmetric model (class b), λ_LR^W ≤ 10^{-6} using K-meson decay parameter ε, or λ_LR^W ∼ 4 × 10^{-3} from 19Ne-decay restrictions, or λ_LR^W ∼ 10^{-7} from neutron electric dipole moment limits (|D_n| ≤ 1.1 × 10^{-25} e·cm).
- For the Weinberg Higgs model, charged Higgs exchange gives λ_H^ch ∼ 10^{-4} (for Higgs mass m_H ∼ 2 GeV), and neutral Higgs exchange gives λ_H^0 ∼ 10^{-1}.
- The P-odd π-meson coupling constant g_P ≃ 1.6 × 10^{-7}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of T-violation in neutron scattering from compound nuclear effects (Bunakov, Gudkov, Flambaum, and collaborators, 1980s-1990s) to a systematic connection with quark-gluon level CP-violation models. It addresses the problem that P-odd effects in nuclei are too complex to extract weak interaction parameters (as noted in the Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein (1980) and Adelberger, Haxton (1985) reviews), and proposes that the simpler structure of CP-odd nucleon interactions (dominated by π-meson exchange, as shown by Gudkov, He, and McKellar) allows model discrimination. The results enable future experiments (Bowman, Masuda, and others) to distinguish between CP-violation mechanisms by measuring the ratio <λ> at p-wave compound resonances.
