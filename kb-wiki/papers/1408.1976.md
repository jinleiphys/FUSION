# Chiral asymmetry in cold QED plasma in a strong magnetic field

> The interaction induced chiral asymmetry is calculated in cold QED plasma beyond the weak-field approximation. By making use of the recently developed Landau-level representation for the fermion self-energy, the chiral shift and the parity-even chiral chemical potential function are obtained with the help of numerical methods. The results are used to quantify the chiral asymmetry of the Fermi surface in dense QED matter. Because of the weakness of the QED interactions, the value of the asymmetry appears to be rather small even in the strongest magnetic fields and at the highest stellar densities. However, the analogous asymmetry can be substantial in the case of dense quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the chiral asymmetry in cold QED plasma in a strong magnetic field beyond the weak-field approximation, finding that the asymmetry is small even at the highest stellar densities due to the weakness of QED interactions, but notes that the analogous asymmetry could be substantial in dense quark matter.

## Method

The authors used the Landau-level representation for the fermion self-energy in cold QED plasma at nonzero density, assuming the chemical potential is much larger than the magnetic field scale and the fermion mass. They employed a Monte Carlo integration algorithm with importance sampling to numerically compute the chiral shift and parity-even chiral chemical potential functions for individual Landau levels, using the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation for the photon propagator to account for screening effects.

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless model parameters used were \(a = m/\mu \approx 1.22\times10^{-3}\) (with \(m/m_e\) and \(\mu = 420\) MeV) and \(b = |eB|/\mu^2 \approx 1/30\) (for \(B = 10^{18}\) G and \(\mu = 420\) MeV).
* The dimensionless Debye mass is \(d = m_D/\mu \equiv \sqrt{2\alpha/\pi} \approx 6.816\times10^{-2}\).
* The chiral shift at the Fermi surface was fitted as \(\Delta_n \simeq -\frac{\alpha |eB|}{\mu}\left(0.53+0.32 \frac{|eB|n}{\mu^2}\right)\), where \(\alpha = 1/137\) and \(|eB| = \mu^2/30\).
* The chiral chemical potential at the Fermi surface was fitted as \(\mu_{5,n} \simeq -0.225 \frac{\alpha |eB|}{\mu}\sqrt{1-\left(\frac{2n|eB|}{\mu^2}\right)^2}\), with the same parameters.

## Context

This work extends the weak-field calculations of chiral asymmetry in QED by Xia et al. (2014) (Ref. chiral-shift-QED in the paper), which found a parametric dependence \(\Delta_n \propto \alpha |eB|\mu/m^2\) that was an artifact of the weak-field expansion. The present study resolves the infrared logarithmic singularity found in the weak-field limit by using the Landau-level representation, and provides numerical results that are proportional to \(\alpha |eB|/\mu\) rather than the much larger weak-field result. The findings enable quantification of the chiral asymmetry of the Fermi surface in dense QED matter and suggest that similar effects could be substantial in dense quark matter, relevant for studies of compact stars and heavy-ion collisions.
