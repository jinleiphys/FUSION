# Fixed point behavior mapping of cumulants between the three-dimensional Ising model and QCD

> Fixed point behavior was found in the temperature dependence of normalized cumulants of order parameter at different external magnetic fields in the three-dimensional Ising model in my last work. In this paper, considering possible existing QCD critical point belonging to the three-dimensional Ising universality class and the non-universal mapping parameters between the Ising model and QCD, effects of the mapping parameters on the fixed point behavior in the net-baryon chemical potential dependence of normalized cumulants along different experimental freeze-out curves is studied in this paper. We found that when the directions of Ising variables, the reduced temperature and external magnetic field, are orthogonal or not far from orthogonal after mapping to the QCD temperature and net-baryon chemical potential plane, the fixed point behavior exists in the net-baryon chemical potential dependence of the normalized cumulants and is closer to the QCD critical point than the peak in the cumulants.

## Key claim

Fixed point behavior in the net-baryon chemical potential dependence of normalized cumulants exists when the Ising variables (reduced temperature and external magnetic field) are mapped to the QCD phase diagram with directions that are orthogonal or not far from orthogonal; the fixed point is then closer to the QCD critical point than the peak in the cumulants, but its exact position depends on the mapping angle \(\alpha_2\).

## Method

The author uses a parametric representation of the three-dimensional Ising model (with critical exponents \(\beta=1/3\), \(\delta=5\)) to compute second- to fourth-order cumulants of the magnetization, then normalizes each cumulant by its peak value at fixed negative magnetic field. A linear ansatz maps the Ising variables (reduced temperature \(t\) and magnetic field \(h\)) to the QCD temperature \(T\) and net-baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) via four parameters: angles \(\alpha_1\) (fixed at \(4.5^\circ\)) and \(\alpha_2\) (varied as \(72^\circ\), \(94.5^\circ\), \(117^\circ\)), and widths \(\Delta t\) (1, 2, 4) and \(\Delta h\) (0.1, 0.2, 0.4). Three empirical freeze-out curves (Cleymans et al., 2006) shifted by 0, 5, and 10 MeV in temperature are mapped to the Ising \(t\)-\(h\) plane, and the \(\mu_B\) dependence of the normalized cumulants is computed along each curve.

## Key numbers

* The critical exponents used for the three-dimensional Ising model are \(\beta = 0.3267(10)\) and \(\delta = 4.786(14)\); the parametric calculation uses approximate values \(\beta=1/3\) and \(\delta=5\).
* The QCD critical point is set at \(T_c = 0.165\) GeV and \(\mu_{Bc} = 0.25\) GeV, with \(\Delta \mu_B = 0.1\) GeV and \(\Delta T/T_c = 1/8\).
* The freeze-out curve is parametrized as \(T_f(\mu_B) = 0.166 - 0.139\,\mu_B^2 - 0.053\,\mu_B^4\) (all in GeV units); the two shifted curves are \(T_{f1} = T_f - 0.005\) GeV and \(T_{f2} = T_f - 0.010\) GeV.
* The mapping angle \(\alpha_1\) is fixed at \(4.5^\circ\); \(\alpha_2\) is varied among \(72^\circ\), \(94.5^\circ\), and \(117^\circ\).
* The critical-region widths are varied as \(\Delta t = 1, 2, 4\) and \(\Delta h = 0.1, 0.2, 0.4\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous finding of fixed point behavior in the temperature dependence of normalized cumulants in the three-dimensional Ising model (Pan, 2022) to the QCD phase diagram via a linear mapping. It addresses the non-universal mapping parameters that are unknown between the Ising model and QCD, following the linear ansatz used by Mukherjee et al. (2015) and Mroczek et al. (2021). The results suggest that fixed point behavior in the energy (or \(\mu_B\)) dependence of cumulants could serve as a robust signature to locate the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collision experiments, complementing the peak-structure approach of Stephanov (2011) and the finite-size scaling methods of Antoniou et al. (2018).
