# The two-fermion vector potential of constraint theory from Feynman diagrams

> The relativistic fermion-antifermion bound state vector potential of constraint theory is calculated, in perturbation theory, by means of the Lippmann-Schwinger type equation that relates it to the scattering amplitude. Leading contributions of n-photon exchange diagrams are calculated in an approximation scheme that adapts eikonal approximation to the bound state problem. They produce terms proportional, in three-dimensional x-space, to $(α/r)^n$. The series of leading contributions is summed.

# The two-fermion vector potential of constraint theory from Feynman diagrams

## Key claim

The leading contributions of multiphoton exchange diagrams to the relativistic fermion-antifermion vector potential in constraint theory sum to a closed-form local expression in three-dimensional x-space, proportional to a hyperbolic tangent function of the one-photon potential. This result holds under an eikonal-type approximation scheme adapted to the bound state problem, and the authors note that a rigorous verification for all n-photon orders is not possible due to diagram complexity.

## Method

The authors calculate the constraint theory vector potential by iterating a Lippmann-Schwinger type equation that relates the potential to the off-mass-shell scattering amplitude, with external momenta constrained by the condition C(p) = 0. They evaluate ladder and crossed ladder diagrams for n-photon exchange in QED, using an approximation scheme that neglects transverse momenta in fermion propagator numerators (but not denominators) and neglects longitudinal photon momentum squared in nonleading terms, adapting the eikonal approximation to the bound state problem. Constraint diagrams, which arise from replacing fermion propagator pairs with a constraint factor, cancel spurious infrared singularities from the ordinary Feynman diagrams.

## Key numbers

- One-photon exchange potential in Feynman gauge, three-dimensional x-space: $\widetilde V^{(1)} = \frac{\alpha}{2 P_L r} \gamma_1.\gamma_2$, where $\alpha = e^2/4\pi$ and $r = \sqrt{-x^{T2}}$.
- Two-photon exchange leading contribution (timelike component): $\widetilde V^{(2)} = -\frac{\alpha^2}{2 P_L^2 r^2} \gamma_{1L} \gamma_{2L}$.
- n-photon exchange leading contribution (timelike component): $\widetilde V^{(n)} = -\frac{(2n)!}{(n+1)! n!} \left(-\frac{\alpha}{2 P_L r}\right)^n \gamma_{1L} \gamma_{2L}$.
- Summed total timelike potential: $\widetilde V = -\gamma_{1L} \gamma_{2L} \left(\frac{1 - \sqrt{1+\frac{2\alpha}{P_L r}}}{1 + \sqrt{1+\frac{2\alpha}{P_L r}}}\right)$.
- Parametrized vector potential: $V_2 = \frac{1}{4} \ln\left(1+\frac{2\alpha}{P_L r}\right)$, with $\widetilde V = \tanh(\gamma_1.\gamma_2 V_2)$ in the Feynman gauge.

## Context

This work extends the manifestly covariant constraint theory formalism (Crater and Van Alstine, 1980s; Sazdjian, 1980s) to sum multiphoton exchange contributions in QED bound states, where previous perturbative calculations in covariant gauges (Leutwyler, 1970s) or with covariant propagators for scalar exchanges (Bohm, Ciulli, and Mitter, 1970s) produced spurious infrared singularities that required cancellation from higher-order diagrams. The result generalizes the electromagnetic potential proposed by Todorov (1970s) in the quasipotential approach for spin-0 particles to the fermion-antifermion case. The summed local potential enables simplified analysis of relativistic bound state wave equations while retaining multiphoton exchange effects, with potential applications to short-distance interquark potentials in QCD where the coupling constant is of order 0.5, and to strong coupling regimes in QED.
