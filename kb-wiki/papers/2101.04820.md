# Unexpectedly enhanced $α$-particle preformation in $^{48}$Ti probed by the $(p,pα)$ reaction

> The formation of $α$ particle on nuclear surface has been a fundamental problem since the early age of nuclear physics. It strongly affects the $α$ decay lifetime of heavy and superheavy elements, level scheme of light nuclei, and the synthesis of the elements in stars. However, the $α$-particle formation in medium-mass nuclei has been poorly known despite its importance. Here, based on the $^{48}{\rm Ti}(p,pα)^{44}{\rm Ca}$ reaction analysis, we report that the $α$-particle formation in a medium-mass nucleus $^{48}{\rm Ti}$ is much stronger than that expected from a mean-field approximation, and the estimated average distance between $α$ particle and the residue is as large as 4.5 fm. This new result poses a challenge of describing four nucleon correlations by microscopic nuclear models.

## Key claim

The alpha-particle preformation probability in the medium-mass nucleus 48Ti is much larger than predicted by a mean-field approximation, with the estimated average distance between the alpha particle and the 44Ca residue being as large as 4.5 fm; however, the authors conclude that the ground state is an admixture of the mean-field and alpha+44Ca configurations, as the alpha+44Ca wave function that reproduces the knockout cross section fails to explain other basic properties of 48Ti.

## Method

The authors analyzed the 48Ti(p,pα)44Ca reaction using the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with accurate optical potentials for p-48Ti, p-44Ca, and α-44Ca scattering. They calculated the reduced width amplitude (RWA) for alpha-particle preformation using wave functions from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), including both a mean-field solution and artificially generated alpha+44Ca cluster wave functions with constrained inter-nuclear distances d. The triple differential cross sections from DWIA calculations using these RWAs were compared to experimental data from Carey et al. (1984).

## Key numbers

- The mean-field solution underestimates the observed cross section by three orders of magnitude.
- The RWA obtained from the alpha+44Ca wave function with inter-nuclear distance d = 4.5 fm gives the most plausible description of the observed cross section.
- The cross section increases by approximately one order of magnitude for every 1 fm increase of the inter-nuclear distance.
- At d = 4.5 fm, the binding energy underestimates the experimental value by approximately 10 MeV and yields a positive Q-value for alpha decay.
- The incident proton energy was Ep = 101.5 MeV, with emitted angles of the proton at θp = -70.0° and the alpha particle at θα = 45.0°.

## Context

This work extends the DWIA analysis framework developed by Yoshida et al. (2019), which was previously applied to the alpha-clustered nucleus 20Ne, to the medium-mass nucleus 48Ti. It contradicts the general belief that alpha-particle preformation is hindered in medium-mass nuclei due to largely negative alpha-decay Q-values and the dominance of mean-field dynamics. The results pose a challenge for microscopic nuclear models to describe four-nucleon correlations in medium-mass nuclei and call for systematic analysis of (p,pα) reactions to reveal the universality of alpha-particle preformation.
