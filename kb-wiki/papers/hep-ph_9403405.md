# Isospin Symmetry Breaking in Hadrons and Nuclei

> A brief review of the isospin symmetry breaking in hadrons and nuclei is given with emphasis on the $u-d$ quark mass difference. The off-shell $ρ^0-ω$ mixing is studied as a typical example of the symmetry breaking, and its relevance to the nuclear force and nuclei is discussed.

# Isospin Symmetry Breaking in Hadrons and Nuclei

## Key claim

The off-shell $\rho^0-\omega$ mixing matrix $\Theta(q^2)$ exhibits a strong $q^2$ dependence that changes sign between the time-like on-shell point and the space-like region relevant to nuclear forces, invalidating the common assumption that $\Theta$ is constant; this implies that $\rho^0-\omega$ exchange alone cannot explain the observed charge-symmetry breaking in nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclei, contrary to previous claims.

## Method

The author uses an unsubtracted dispersion relation for the $\rho^0-\omega$ correlation function to prove that the mixing matrix $\Theta(q^2)$ must be $q^2$ dependent in a model-independent way, then evaluates the slope parameter $\lambda$ using QCD sum rules with inputs from chiral perturbation theory for the quark mass ratio $(m_d-m_u)/(m_d+m_u) = 0.28 \pm 0.03$ and the condensate ratio $\langle \bar{u}u \rangle_0 / \langle \bar{d}d \rangle_0$. The resulting $\Theta(q^2)$ is Fourier-transformed to obtain a static central CSB nucleon-nucleon potential, which is compared to the earlier constant-mixing potential of Coon and Barrett.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses at 1 GeV: $m_u \simeq 4$ MeV, $m_d \simeq 9$ MeV (from Gasser, Leutwyler, and collaborators).
- Quark mass ratio: $(m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) = 0.28 \pm 0.03$ (from chiral perturbation theory).
- Condensate ratio: $\langle \bar{u}u \rangle_0 / \langle \bar{d}d \rangle_0 = 1 - (2 \text{ to } 10) \times 10^{-3}$ (from QCD sum rules).
- Slope parameter from QCD sum rules: $\lambda = 1.43 \sim 1.85$.
- On-shell $\rho^0-\omega$ mixing matrix element (experimental): $-4520 \pm 600$ MeV$^2$; estimated $O(\alpha)$ contribution: $\sim 610$ MeV$^2$; estimated $O(m_d-m_u)$ contribution: $\sim -5130$ MeV$^2$.
- Pion mass difference: $m_{\pi^\pm} - m_{\pi^0} = 4.6$ MeV (experimental), with $O(\alpha)$ theory estimate $4.6 \pm 0.1$ MeV and $O(m_d-m_u)$ contribution zero.
- Proton-neutron mass difference: $m_p - m_n = -1.29$ MeV (experimental), with $O(\alpha)$ theory estimate $0.76 \pm 0.3$ MeV and $O(m_d-m_u)$ contribution $\sim -2.05$ MeV.
- EM-subtracted CSB in nn-pp scattering length: $\bar{a}_{nn} - \bar{a}_{pp} = -1.5 \pm 0.5$ fm.
- EM-subtracted binding energy difference in mirror nuclei: $\bar{B}(^3\text{H}) - \bar{B}(^3\text{He}) = 80 \pm 24$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas that the $\rho^0-\omega$ mixing matrix may be momentum-dependent, and provides a QCD-based calculation of that dependence using sum rules, in collaboration with Meissner, Henley, and Krein. The result contradicts the constant-mixing assumption used in the Coon and Barrett (1980s) potential, which had been widely applied to nuclear CSB phenomena including the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly. The paper enables a systematic approach to CSB in nuclear forces by identifying the need to treat all sources of CSB (meson mixings, external lines, and meson-nucleon vertices) consistently, and points toward lattice QCD calculations of N-N scattering with $m_d \neq m_u$ as a future direction.
