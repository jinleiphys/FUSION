# Thoughts about the utility of perturbative QCD in the cores of neutron stars

> In this contribution, I discuss the utility that perturbative QCD offers in studying the matter in the cores of neutron stars. I discuss the reasons why perturbative QCD can constrain the equation of state at densities far below the densities where we can perform controlled calculations. I discuss how perturbative QCD can inform nuclear modelling of neutron stars and how it influences equation-of-state inference. And finally, I discuss the implications to the QCD phase diagram and argue that interesting features in the equation of state revealed by the QCD input may be used to argue for the existence of quark-matter cores in most massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD can robustly constrain the equation of state at neutron-star core densities (around 3-8 times saturation density) even though controlled perturbative calculations only become quantitatively reliable at much higher densities around 40 times saturation density, because thermodynamic consistency conditions propagate the high-density information downward.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of robust equation-of-state constraints that require mechanical stability, causality (speed of sound less than 1), and thermodynamic consistency between the reduced equation of state and the underlying grand canonical potential. The work by Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021) generalized the earlier Rhoades-Ruffini construction to include the full thermodynamic potential, showing that the high-density perturbative QCD limit constrains the equation of state down to densities as low as 2.3 times nuclear saturation density. The author then describes how this robust constraint was implemented as a likelihood function in equation-of-state inference, allowing the QCD input to be turned on and off to isolate its effect.

## Key numbers

- Perturbative QCD becomes quantitatively reliable at densities around n ~ 40 n_s (40 times nuclear saturation density)
- Neutron-star cores reach densities n ~ 3-8 n_s
- The robust QCD constraints start to constrain the equation of state as low as 2.3 n_s
- At n = 5 n_s, the QCD input removes 75% of otherwise allowed energy-density-pressure values
- A softening feature appears in the equation of state around 750 MeV/fm^3
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the QCD cross-over at zero baryon density is around T_c ~ 150 MeV
- PSR J0952-0607 has mass M = 2.35 +/- 0.17 solar masses

## Context

This contribution extends the line of work on model-independent equation-of-state inference that began with Hebeler et al. (2013) and Kurkela et al. (2014), which used piecewise polytropic functions constrained by the observation of 2-solar-mass neutron stars. The key advance is the demonstration by Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021) that thermodynamic consistency conditions make the perturbative QCD constraints robust, not dependent on interpolation choices. This enables future work to use QCD input as a complementary constraint to astrophysical observations, potentially arguing for quark-matter cores in the most massive neutron stars, and opens the possibility of testing Standard Model physics through inconsistencies between different observational channels.
