# Photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ in peripheral Oxygen-Oxygen collisions

> The photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ in peripheral Oxygen - Oxygen ($OO$) collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) is investigated considering distinct assumptions for the modeling of the nuclear photon flux, overlap function and dipole - proton scattering amplitude. Predictions for the associated rapidity distributions and total cross - sections are presented. Our results indicate that the experimental study of the photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ in peripheral $OO$ collisions is, in principle, feasible. In addition, they point out that the combination of the results for this final state in $OO$ collisions with those obtained for $PbPb$ collisions will allow us to derive important constraints on the description of photon - induced process at peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The photoproduction of J/Ψ in peripheral Oxygen-Oxygen collisions at the LHC is, in principle, feasible for experimental study given the integrated luminosity of the recent OO run (approximately 6 nb⁻¹), but the predictions for centralities smaller than 50% should be considered upper bounds because the impact of quark-gluon plasma formation on the photoproduced vector mesons cannot be disregarded for more central collisions.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross-section for J/Ψ photoproduction in peripheral OO collisions using the equivalent photon approximation, where the photon flux from one oxygen nucleus interacts with the other nucleus via color-singlet exchange (Pomeron). They consider four distinct models for the effective nuclear photon flux in peripheral collisions (labeled N⁰ through N³), three models for the dipole-proton scattering amplitude (bCGC, IP-SAT, and IPnon-SAT), and two models for the overlap function (Boosted Gaussian and Gaus-LC), all within the color dipole formalism using the Glauber-Gribov approach for the nuclear scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* Total cross-section for J/Ψ photoproduction in peripheral OO collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV, centrality 50-70%, using N⁰ flux and bCGC dipole model with BG overlap: 0.400 μb (central rapidity, -2 ≤ yΨ ≤ 2)
* Total cross-section for J/Ψ photoproduction in peripheral OO collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV, centrality 70-90%, using N⁰ flux and bCGC dipole model with BG overlap: 0.340 μb (central rapidity, -2 ≤ yΨ ≤ 2)
* Total cross-section for J/Ψ photoproduction in peripheral OO collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV, centrality 50-70%, using N² flux and IP-SAT dipole model with BG overlap: 0.265 μb (central rapidity, -2 ≤ yΨ ≤ 2)
* Ratio σ(PbPb at 5.02 TeV) / σ(OO at 5.36 TeV) for J/Ψ photoproduction, centrality 50-70%, N⁰ flux, bCGC dipole model, BG overlap: 3348.93 (central rapidity)
* Ratio σ(PbPb at 5.02 TeV) / σ(OO at 5.36 TeV) for J/Ψ photoproduction, centrality 50-70%, N⁰ flux, bCGC dipole model, BG overlap: 1950.02 (forward rapidity, 2 ≤ yΨ ≤ 4.5)
* Integrated luminosity of the LHC OO run: approximately 6 nb⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the analysis of J/Ψ photoproduction in peripheral heavy-ion collisions from PbPb systems (previously studied by da Costa et al. 2025) to the recently available OO collisions at the LHC. The study complements results from Klusek-Gawenda (2015), Gay Ducati (2018), and da Costa (2025) by providing predictions for a lighter colliding system, which allows for a cross-system comparison. The authors propose that the ratio of cross-sections between PbPb and OO collisions can help discriminate between different models for the effective nuclear photon flux, dipole-proton scattering amplitude, and overlap functions, thereby constraining the description of photon-induced processes in peripheral collisions. The first OO collision data have already been released by CMS (2025, 2026), and further analyses are expected.
