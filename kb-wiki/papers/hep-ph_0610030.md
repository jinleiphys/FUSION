# Static properties and semileptonic decays of doubly heavy baryons in a nonrelativistic quark model

> We evaluate static properties and semileptonic decays for the ground state of doubly heavy $Ξ, Ξ', Ξ^*$ and $Ω, Ω', Ω^*$ baryons. Working in the framework of a nonrelativistic quark model, we solve the three--body problem by means of a variational ansazt made possible by heavy quark spin symmetry constraints. To check the dependence of our results on the inter-quark interaction we use five different quark-quark potentials that include a confining term plus Coulomb and hyperfine terms coming from one--gluon exchange. Our results for static properties (masses, charge and mass radii, magnetic moments...) are, with a few exceptions for the magnetic moments, in good agreement with a previous Faddeev calculation. Our much simpler wave functions are used to evaluate semileptonic decays of doubly heavy $Ξ,Ξ'(J=1/2)$ and $Ω, Ω'(J=1/2)$ baryons. Our results for the decay widths are in good agreement with calculations done within a relativistic quark model in the quark--diquark approximation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a simple variational ansatz, made possible by assuming heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) is a good approximation for actual heavy quark masses, can reproduce the static properties of doubly heavy baryons obtained from more complex Faddeev calculations and provide manageable wave functions for evaluating semileptonic decays. The authors hedge that the nonrelativistic approach has limitations for describing light quark physics, but argue these are less relevant for the heavy-to-heavy transitions studied, and that relativistic effects are partially absorbed in the fitted potential parameters.

## Method
The authors solved the three-body problem for the ground state of doubly heavy baryons using a variational approach with Jastrow-type wave functions, assuming the total spin of the heavy quark subsystem ($S_h$) is well defined. They used five different quark-quark potentials (BD, AL1, AL2, AP1, AP2) that include a confining term plus Coulomb and hyperfine terms from one-gluon exchange, with parameters fixed from meson spectra. The variational wave functions were then used to evaluate static properties (masses, charge radii, magnetic moments) and semileptonic decay form factors and widths for $J=1/2$ baryons.

## Key numbers
- Mass of $\Xi_{cc}$: $3612^{+17}$ MeV (AL1 potential), compared to the SELEX experimental value of $3519\pm 1$ MeV/c$^2$.
- Mass of $\Xi_{bb}$: $10197^{+10}_{-17}$ MeV (AL1 potential).
- Mass splitting $M_{\Xi^*_{cc}}-M_{\Xi_{cc}}$: $94^{+5}_{-11}$ MeV (AL1 potential).
- Mass splitting $M_{\Xi^*_{bb}}-M_{\Xi_{bb}}$: $39^{+1}_{-6}$ MeV (AL1 potential).
- Charge mean square radius of $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$: $0.298^{+0.034}_{-0.028}$ fm$^2$ (AL1 potential).
- Charge mean square radius of $\Xi_{bb}^0$: $0.221^{+0.033}_{-0.025}$ fm$^2$ (AL1 potential).

## Context
This work extends previous nonrelativistic quark model calculations of doubly heavy baryons, specifically the Faddeev calculation by Silvestre-Brac and Semay (1996), by providing simpler variational wave functions that reproduce those static properties. It enables the evaluation of semileptonic decay widths for these baryons, which had been previously calculated in relativistic quark models by Ebert et al. (2004) and Faessler et al. (2001), and in HQET by Sanchis-Lozano (1995). The results for decay widths are reported to be in good agreement with those relativistic quark-diquark model calculations.
