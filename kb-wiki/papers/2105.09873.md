# Impact of inclusive hadron production data on nuclear gluon PDFs

> A precise knowledge of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) is -- among other things -- important for the unambiguous interpretation of hard process data taken in pA and AA collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The available fixed target data for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) lepton pair production mainly constrain the light quark distributions. It is hence crucial to include more and more collider data in global analyses of nPDFs in order to better pin down the different parton flavors, in particular the gluon distribution at small x. To help constrain the nuclear gluon PDF, we extend the nCTEQ15 analysis by including single inclusive hadron (SIH) production data from RHIC (PHENIX and STAR) and LHC (ALICE). In addition to the DIS, DY and SIH data sets, we will also include LHC W/Z production data. As the SIH calculation is dependent on hadronic fragmentation functions (FFs), we use a variety of FFs available in the literature to properly estimate this source of uncertainty. We study the impact of these data on the PDFs, and compare with both the nCTEQ15 and nCTEQ15WZ sets. The calculations are performed using a new implementation of the nCTEQ code (nCTEQ++) including a modified version of INCNLO which allows faster calculations using pre-computed grids. The extension of the nCTEQ15 analysis to include the SIH data represents an important step toward the next generation of PDFs.

## Key claim
Including single inclusive hadron (SIH) production data from RHIC and LHC in the nCTEQ15 global analysis can help constrain the nuclear gluon PDF at small x, but the impact depends on properly accounting for fragmentation function uncertainties and applying a lower transverse momentum cut of pT > 3 GeV to ensure perturbative reliability.

## Method
The authors extended the nCTEQ15 global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions by adding single inclusive hadron production data for pions, kaons, and eta mesons from RHIC (PHENIX and STAR at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) and LHC (ALICE at 5.02 and 8.16 TeV), along with previously included DIS, DY, and W/Z production data. They performed NLO QCD calculations using a new nCTEQ++ framework with pre-computed grids from a modified INCNLO program, testing ten different fragmentation function sets and adding DSS fragmentation function uncertainties as systematic errors in quadrature with data uncertainties. The fits used 19 free parameters including three for the strange quark distribution, with data cut at pT > 3 GeV to ensure perturbative reliability.

## Key numbers
- 77 out of 174 ALICE data points and 32 out of 77 RHIC data points survived the pT > 3 GeV kinematic cut
- The initial scale for PDF parameterization was Q0 = 1.3 GeV, with the factorization scale frozen to this value when c*pT < Q0 (i.e., for pT <= 2.6 GeV)
- The analysis included data at center-of-mass energies per nucleon of 200 GeV (RHIC), 5020 GeV, and 8160 GeV (ALICE)
- The gluon contribution dominates the p+Pb -> pi0+X cross section in the low to mid pT region at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and is the dominant contribution even in the mid- to high-pT region at 5 TeV

## Context
This work extends the nCTEQ15 analysis (Kovarik, 2015) and the nCTEQ15WZ analysis (Kusina, 2020) by incorporating single inclusive hadron production data to better constrain the gluon PDF, which is poorly constrained by DIS and DY data alone. The approach contrasts with microscopic models of nuclear effects in the shadowing region (Armesto, 2006; Frankfurt, 2011), antishadowing region (Brodsky, 1989; Kulagin, 2004), and EMC effect (Geesaman, 1995; Hen, 2013), and enables improved predictions for hard processes at RHIC, LHC, and future experiments like the EIC.
