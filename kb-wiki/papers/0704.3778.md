# From Pauli Principle to Hypernucleus, Neutron Star, and Econophysics

> Proposed by Wolfgang Pauli more than 80 years ago, the exclusion principle has been proven to have a far-reaching consequence, from femtoscopic world to macroscopic, super-dense, and fully relativistic physics. Starting from this principle, we discuss two interesting research topics, which have currently drawn considerable attention in the nuclear- and astrophysics communities; the hypernuclear and neutron star physics. Special attention is given to the electromagnetic production of the hypertriton and the consequences of the neutrino electromagnetic properties in dense matter. We also touch on the new arena which could also be fascinating for physicists; the econophysics.

## Key claim

The Pauli exclusion principle, through the degeneracy force it creates, connects microscopic quantum behavior to macroscopic astrophysical phenomena such as white dwarfs and neutron stars, and the paper argues that this same principle underlies the stability and decay properties of hypernuclei. The abstract's claim of covering "econophysics" is not substantiated in the text, which only mentions the topic in passing without any analysis.

## Method

The paper derives the degeneracy pressure for a fermion gas from the antisymmetrization requirement of the wave function, then applies this to calculate the equilibrium radius of an electron-degenerate star (white dwarf) and a neutron star by balancing degeneracy pressure against gravitational pressure. For hypernuclear physics, the author models the elementary reaction γ + p → K⁺ + Λ using tree-level Feynman diagrams, fits the unknown coupling constants to SAPHIR and CLAS data, and then computes the hypertriton photoproduction cross section by integrating the elementary amplitude over nuclear wave functions with 6j and 9j coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Degeneracy pressure for two "chargeless" electrons separated by 1 Å: p_deg ≈ 10¹⁴ N/m², corresponding to a force of order 10⁻⁶ N on an atomic area of 10⁻²⁰ m²
- White dwarf radius for one solar mass: R_es ≈ 1.1 × 10⁴ km (assuming N ≈ 1.2 × 10⁵⁷ neutrons and N_e = N/2)
- Neutron star radius for one solar mass: R_ns ≈ 10 km (using the same mass, substituting electron mass with neutron mass)
- Free Λ hyperon lifetime: about 10⁻¹⁰ s, decaying to p + π⁻ (63.9% branching fraction) or n + π⁰ (35.8% branching fraction)
- Hypertriton photoproduction cross section at forward angles is smaller than elementary kaon production by two orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the textbook treatment of degeneracy pressure found in Gasiorowicz (1974) to both hypernuclear and neutron star contexts, and builds on the hypernuclear production reactions established by Danysz and Pniewski (1953) and later electromagnetic production experiments by Dohrmann et al. (2006). The paper enables future experimental programs at MAMI in Mainz to resolve discrepancies between SAPHIR and CLAS data on kaon photoproduction, and highlights the need for improved theoretical treatment of hypernuclear electroproduction beyond the simple factorization approximation.
