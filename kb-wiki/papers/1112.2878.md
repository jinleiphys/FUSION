# Masses of neutron stars and nuclei

> We calculate the maximum mass of neutron stars for three different equations of state (EOS) based on generalized Skyrme functionals that are simultaneously fitted to essentially all the 2003 nuclear mass data (the rms deviation is 0.58 MeV in all three cases) and to one or other of three different equations of state of pure neutron matter, each determined by a different many-body calculation using realistic two- and three-body interactions but leading to significantly different degrees of stiffness at the high densities prevailing in neutron-star interiors. The observation of a neutron star with mass 1.97 $\pm$ 0.04 $\mathcal{M}_{\odot}$ eliminates the softest of our models (BSk19), but does not discriminate between BSk20 and BSk21. However, nuclear-mass measurements that have been made since our models were constructed strongly favor BSk21, our stiffest functional.

## Key claim

The observation of a neutron star with mass 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses eliminates the softest model (BSk19), but does not discriminate between BSk20 and BSk21; however, nuclear-mass measurements made since the models were constructed strongly favor BSk21, the stiffest functional.

## Method

The authors constructed three generalized Skyrme functionals (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) by fitting to 2149 measured nuclear masses from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method, while simultaneously constraining each functional to a different zero-temperature equation of state of pure neutron matter from realistic many-body calculations (FP-WFF for soft, APR for intermediate, LS2 for stiff). They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for spherical non-rotating neutron stars using the LORENE library, and also considered rigid rotation for PSR J1614-2230, to obtain maximum gravitational masses, radii, and central densities for each functional.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for BSk19: 1.86 solar masses (corresponding realistic force WFF gives 1.84 solar masses)
- Maximum mass for BSk20: 2.15 solar masses (corresponding realistic force APR gives 2.20 solar masses)
- Maximum mass for BSk21: 2.28 solar masses (corresponding realistic force LS2 gives 2.3 solar masses)
- Maximum mass for SLy4: 2.05 solar masses
- Observed mass of pulsar PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses
- All three mass models (HFB-19, HFB-20, HFB-21) fit the 2149 measured masses from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation with an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV
- For the 154 new mass measurements in the 2011 Atomic Mass Evaluation, HFB-21 gives rms deviation 0.681 MeV, compared to 0.824 MeV for HFB-19 and 0.803 MeV for HFB-20
- For the 73 new neutron-rich nuclei in the 2011 Atomic Mass Evaluation, HFB-21 gives rms deviation 0.735 MeV, compared to 0.948 MeV for HFB-19 and 0.893 MeV for HFB-20
- For the 17 Matos (2007) neutron-rich nuclei, HFB-21 gives rms deviation 0.536 MeV, compared to 0.912 MeV for HFB-19 and 0.878 MeV for HFB-20
- Causality limit for BSk19: baryonic density 1.45 fm^-3; for BSk20: 0.98 fm^-3; for BSk21: 0.99 fm^-3
- Radius at maximum mass for BSk19: 9.13 km; for BSk20: 10.6 km; for BSk21: 11.0 km

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous construction of the BSk19, BSk20, and BSk21 functionals (Goriely et al., 2010) by calculating neutron-star properties and comparing with the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010). The results contradict the softest model BSk19, which was fitted to the FP-WFF equation of state (Friedman and Pandharipande, 1981; Wiringa et al., 1988), while supporting BSk20 and BSk21, fitted to APR (Akmal et al., 1998) and LS2 (Li and Schulze, 2008) respectively. The new nuclear mass data from the 2011 Atomic Mass Evaluation and Matos (2007) strongly favor BSk21, providing a unified functional that performs well across both nuclear masses and neutron-star masses, though the authors caution that a more elaborate Skyrme parametrization might allow equally good mass fits with different high-density extrapolations.
