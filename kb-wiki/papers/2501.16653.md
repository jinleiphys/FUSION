# Confined Monopoles in Chiral Bag

> The chiral bag model offers a dual description of hadron physics in terms of quarks and hadrons in the sense of Cheshire Cat principle. In this work, we find that, within the chiral bag, confinement is likely caused by monopole condensation. The chiral bag surface can be interpreted as an $η'$ domain wall, where a dynamical Chern-Simons theory emerges. Under level-rank duality, the Chern-Simons theory serves as the counterterm introduced to block the so-called color charge leakage. To ensure the correct net baryon number of the full chiral bag, an additional Chern-Simons theory involving the vector meson field arises on the bag surface and extends outside the bag. This leads to a Chern-Simons-Higgs theory localized on the $η'$ domain wall, as previously conjectured. We also propose that the skyrmion description of baryons could be understood as a system of monopoles enveloped by a meson cloud.

# Confined Monopoles in Chiral Bag

## Key claim

The paper proposes that color confinement inside the chiral bag is caused by monopole condensation, and that the bag surface acts as an eta-prime domain wall supporting two distinct Chern-Simons theories: one that blocks color charge leakage and another that ensures the correct net baryon number. This interpretation is conditional on the specific choice of eta-prime vacuum values inside versus outside the bag, with the difference eta-prime_Sigma minus eta-prime_in set to 2 pi for the simplest case.

## Method

The authors analyze the color anomaly in the chiral bag model when the pseudoscalar isosinglet eta-prime field is included at the bag boundary, deriving that color charge leaks when eta-prime is time-dependent. They model confinement inside the bag using a magnetic Abelian-Higgs model with a complex scalar field carrying N_c magnetic charges, representing condensed monopoles, and describe the topological properties via a Z_{N_c} gauge field. On the bag surface, they introduce a U(N_f)_{-N_c} Chern-Simons theory for a dynamical gauge field A to cancel the color anomaly, and a separate U(N_f)_{N_c} Chern-Simons theory for the vector meson field V to generate the correct baryon number, with the vector meson flux extending outside the bag.

## Key numbers

- The color charge leakage equation gives dQ_G^a / d eta-prime_Sigma = (g^2 / 8 pi^2) times the surface integral of the color magnetic field B_G^a dot n, as derived from the color anomaly.
- The counterterm action S_CT contains a factor g^2 N_f / (16 pi^2) times the surface integral of eta-prime_Sigma times the Chern-Simons current K_5^mu.
- The shift in eta-prime across the bag surface is set to eta-prime_Sigma minus eta-prime_in = 2 pi to produce an SU(N_c)_{N_f} Chern-Simons theory on the surface.
- For the one-flavor case with N_f = 1, eta-prime_in = 0, eta-prime_Sigma = 2 pi, and the flux (1/2 pi) times the surface integral of dA = N_c, the model represents a one-flavor baryon.
- The flux relation for the vector meson field gives (1/2 pi) times the surface integral of tr(dV) = (N_f / (2 pi N_c)) times the surface integral of dA.

## Context

This work extends the chiral bag model of Brown (1979), Vento (1980), and Rho (1983) by incorporating the eta-prime meson and its domain wall physics, building on recent developments in higher-form symmetries and anomaly matching by Gaiotto (2017) and Komargodski (2018). The paper connects to prior studies of baryons as quantum Hall droplets on eta-prime domain walls by Ma (2019) and Lin (2023), and to the Chern-Simons-Higgs theory conjectured on such domain walls. The results enable a unified topological description of baryons where skyrmions can be understood as monopoles enveloped by a meson cloud, and suggest a duality between vector meson and gluon descriptions on the bag surface.
