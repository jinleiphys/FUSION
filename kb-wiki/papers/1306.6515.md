# Parton momentum and helicity distributions in the nucleon

> We review the current status of spin-averaged and spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the nucleon. After presenting the formalism used to fit PDFs in modern global data analyses, we discuss constraints placed on the PDFs by specific data types. We give representative examples of unpolarized and polarized PDFs and their errors, and list open questions in global QCD fitting. Finally, we anticipate how future facilities, with fixed-target and collider experiments, may impact our knowledge of PDFs and reduce their uncertainties.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the current status of both spin-averaged and spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the nucleon, emphasizing that PDFs are determined by simultaneously fitting a wide variety of data for large momentum transfer processes, and that the error estimates on PDFs are discussed at some length because precise knowledge of PDFs is required for accurate calculations of backgrounds for searches for new particles.

## Method

The authors present the QCD formalism used to fit PDFs in modern global data analyses, including the running coupling, Q^2 evolution equations, and the treatment of hard scattering processes such as lepton-hadron deep-inelastic scattering and hadron-hadron scattering. They discuss constraints placed on PDFs by specific data types including deep inelastic scattering, lepton pair production, and the production of photons, vector bosons, or jets at large transverse momentum, and describe the treatment of heavy quarks in fixed flavor number schemes and variable flavor number schemes.

## Key numbers

* The QCD beta function coefficients for n_f flavors are given: b_0 = (33 - 2 n_f)/(12 pi), b_1 = (153 - 19 n_f)/(24 pi^2), b_2 = (77139 - 15099 n_f + 325 n_f^2)/(3456 pi^3)
* Splitting functions for Q^2 evolution have been computed up to 3 loops (m=2) for unpolarized PDFs, which is the necessary accuracy for a NNLO analysis
* Spin-dependent splitting functions have been computed to 2 loops, enabling analyses at NLO accuracy
* The VFNS predictions deviate from the FFNS typically by about 10%, depending on the particular process and energy scale

## Context

This review extends earlier treatises of PDFs by Buras (1979) and Owens (1992), as well as more recent reviews by Forte (2013), Blumlein (2012), and Accardi (2013) which focused on unpolarized PDFs and LHC phenomenology. The spin structure of the nucleon was also reviewed by Lampe (1998), de Florian (2011), and Aidala (2012). The paper enables future improvements in PDF knowledge through anticipated results from ongoing and future fixed-target and collider experiments, and identifies open questions in global QCD fitting such as the need for complete NNLO heavy quark coefficient functions.
