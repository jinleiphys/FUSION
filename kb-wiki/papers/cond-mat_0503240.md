# Finite-size scaling exponents in the interacting boson model

> We investigate the finite-size scaling exponents for the critical point at the shape phase transition from U(5) (spherical) to O(6) (deformed $γ$-unstable) dynamical symmetries of the Interacting Boson Model, making use of the Holstein-Primakoff boson expansion and the continuous unitary transformation technique. We compute exactly the leading order correction to the ground state energy, the gap, the expectation value of the $d$-boson number in the ground state and the $E2$ transition probability from the ground state to the first excited state, and determine the corresponding finite-size scaling exponents.

## Key claim

The paper computes finite-size scaling exponents for the U(5)-O(6) shape phase transition in the Interacting Boson Model, finding that the leading-order 1/N expansion becomes singular at the critical point x_c = 1/2, which yields nontrivial scaling exponents for observables such as ground state energy, gap, d-boson number, and E2 transition probability.

## Method

The authors used a Holstein-Primakoff boson expansion to perform a 1/N expansion of the IBM Hamiltonian in the spherical (symmetric) phase, then applied the continuous unitary transformation (CUT) technique to diagonalize the Hamiltonian to order (1/N)^1 beyond the Random Phase Approximation (RPA). The flow equations were solved exactly order by order in 1/N, yielding analytical expressions for the ground state energy, excitation gap, d-boson number expectation value, and B(E2) transition probability. These analytical results were verified by numerical diagonalization of the Hamiltonian for up to N=1000 bosons.

## Key numbers

- Critical point at x_c = 1/2 for the U(5)-O(6) transition in the N → ∞ limit
- Ground state energy scaling exponent: -1/3 (i.e., E_0 ~ N^{-1/3} at the critical point)
- Gap Δ scaling exponent: -1/3 (i.e., Δ ~ N^{-1/3} at the critical point)
- Expectation value of d-boson number ⟨n_d⟩ scaling exponent: 1/3 (i.e., ⟨n_d⟩ ~ N^{1/3} at the critical point)
- B(E2) transition probability scaling exponent: 4/3 (i.e., B(E2) ~ N^{4/3} at the critical point)
- Numerical diagonalization performed up to N=1000 bosons to verify the scaling exponents

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling analysis previously applied to similar models by Dusuel et al. (2004, 2005) to the IBM U(5)-O(6) transition, which is known to be a second-order quantum phase transition with an integrable Hamiltonian (Alhassid, 1983; Pan, 1998; Dukelsky, 2001). The scaling exponent for the ground state energy was recently obtained by Rowe et al. (2004) using a collective model mapping onto a quartic potential, but the CUT method allows computation of finite-N corrections and observables such as B(E2) that are not accessible via that approach. The results enable characterization of how finite boson number systems approach the critical region as N → ∞, with applications to nuclei, molecules, and other mesoscopic systems.
