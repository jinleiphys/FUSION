# Towards the determination of the 3-dimensional structure of the proton using lattice QCD simulations

> State-of-the-art lattice QCD simulations enable the evaluation of nucleon form factors and Mellin moments with controlled systematics, yielding results with unprecedented accuracy. At the same time, new theoretical approaches are allowing the direct computation of nucleon generalized parton distributions. We review recent lattice QCD results on these quantities that are paving the way for extracting a wealth of information on the 3-dimensional structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

State-of-the-art lattice QCD simulations can now evaluate nucleon form factors and Mellin moments with controlled systematics at physical pion masses, but the direct computation of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) via large momentum effective theory (LaMET) is still maturing and not yet as precise as the Mellin moment approach.

## Method

The authors review lattice QCD results from the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration (ETMC) using three gauge ensembles at different lattice spacings (0.07957 fm, 0.06821 fm, 0.05692 fm) with twisted mass clover-improved fermions and pion masses tuned close to the physical value (approximately 140 MeV). They compute nucleon axial form factors from matrix elements of the axial-vector current, extract second Mellin moments of the energy-momentum tensor to decompose nucleon spin and momentum, and apply LaMET to compute quasi-PDFs that are matched to light-cone parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Axial charge: \( g_A = 1.245(28)(14) \) in the continuum limit using three ETMC ensembles, where the first error is statistical and the second systematic due to excited states.
- Axial radius: \( \langle r^2_A \rangle = 0.339(67)(06)~\mathrm{fm}^2 \) from the slope of the axial form factor in the continuum limit.
- Induced pseudoscalar coupling at the muon capture point: \( g_P^* = 8.99(39)(49) \), compared to the chiral perturbation theory value of 8.44(16).
- Pion-nucleon coupling: \( g_{\pi NN} = 13.25(67)(69) \).
- Goldberger-Treiman deviation: \( \Delta_{GT} = -0.0213(38) \), corresponding to the low energy constant \( \bar{d}_{18} = -0.73(13)~\mathrm{GeV}^{-2} \).
- Momentum sum: \( \sum_{q=u,d,s,c}\langle x \rangle_R^{q^+} + \langle x\rangle_R^g = 1.045(118) \), confirming the expected momentum sum rule.
- Spin sum: \( J_P = 94.6(14.2)(2.8)\% \) of the proton spin, with the gluon contribution providing the missing piece.
- Sum of \( B_{20} \) generalized form factors: \( \sum_{q=u,d,s} B_{20,R}^{q^+}(0) + B_{20,R}^g(0) = -0.099(91)(28) \), compatible with zero as required by momentum and spin sum rules.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing lattice QCD effort to compute nucleon structure, building on the foundational work of the European Muon Collaboration (1987, 1989) that revealed the proton spin puzzle. The ETMC results on axial form factors are compared with recent computations by the CLS-Mainz group (2022) and with experimental data from MINERvA (2023) antineutrino-hydrogen measurements. The direct computation of PDFs via LaMET, pioneered by Ji (2013), is shown to improve phenomenological analyses such as those from the JAM collaboration when lattice results are combined with experimental data. The work enables future extraction of three-dimensional nucleon structure through GPDs and transverse-momentum-dependent distributions, with continuum limit extrapolations for the spin decomposition still ongoing.
