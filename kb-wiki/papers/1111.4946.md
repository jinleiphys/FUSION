# Nuclear Pairing from Chiral Pion-Nucleon Dynamics: Applications to Finite Nuclei

> The 1S0 pairing gap in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and finite nuclei is investigated using the chiral nucleon-nucleon potential at the N3LO order in the two-body sector, and the N2LO order in the three-body sector. To include realistic nuclear forces in RHB (Relativistic Hartree Bolgoliubov) calculations we rely on a separable form of the pairing interaction adjusted to the bare nuclear force. The separable pairing force is applied to the analysis of pairing properties for several isotopic and isotonic chains of spherical nuclei.

# Nuclear Pairing from Chiral Pion-Nucleon Dynamics: Applications to Finite Nuclei

## Key claim
The chiral nucleon-nucleon potential at N3LO (two-body) and N2LO (three-body) can be mapped to a separable pairing interaction for use in Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations of finite nuclei, but the inclusion of three-body forces induces a sizable reduction of calculated pairing gaps that suggests missing contributions such as particle-vibration coupling may play an important role.

## Method
The authors solved the BCS gap equation for symmetric nuclear matter using the chiral NN potential at N3LO in the two-body sector and an effective two-body density-dependent potential derived from the N2LO three-body sector. They then employed a separable ansatz for the pairing interaction in momentum space, with two parameters (strength G and range a) adjusted to reproduce the density dependence of the nuclear matter pairing gap, and applied this separable force within the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework using the FKVW functional in the particle-hole channel to compute ground-state properties of spherical nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For the N3LO potential alone: separable force parameters G = 892.0 MeV fm^3 and a = 0.74 fm
- For the complete potential including three-body forces: G = 1045.0 MeV fm^3 and a = 0.86 fm
- For comparison with the Gogny D1S force: G = 728 MeV fm^3 and a = 0.644 fm
- The three-body force reduces the strong S-wave attraction in the 1S0 channel of symmetric nuclear matter

## Context
This work extends the FKVW relativistic energy density functional approach (Finelli et al., 2003-2006) by replacing the empirical Gogny force (Berger et al., 1991) in the particle-particle channel with a pairing interaction derived from chiral dynamics, following the separable mapping method of Tian et al. (2009). The results confirm findings by Lesinski et al. (2011) on the importance of three-body forces for pairing gaps, and enable future studies of polarization effects and particle-vibration coupling in open-shell nuclei.
