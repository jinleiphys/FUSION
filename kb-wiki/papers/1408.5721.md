# Production of two hadrons in semi-inclusive Deep Inelatic Scattering

> We present the general expression, in terms of structure functions, of the cross section for the production of two hadrons in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering. We analyze this process including full transverse-momentum dependence up to subleading twist and check, where possible, the consistency with existing literature.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete expression of the two-hadron semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) cross section up to subleading twist, including full transverse-momentum dependence, but the authors note that the number of structure functions is in principle unlimited because the presence of two sources of angular momentum (total and relative angular momenta of the pair) allows for infinitely many combinations, with the only constraint being that the sum of the coefficients of the azimuthal angles is limited to at most 3.

## Method

The authors derived the general cross section for dihadron production in SIDIS in terms of structure functions, using a covariant definition of the azimuthal angle \(\phi_{R_\perp}\) of the relative momentum vector \(R_T\) measured in the plane perpendicular to the virtual photon and target momentum. They performed a partial-wave expansion in Legendre polynomials for the polar angle \(\vartheta\) and Fourier series for the azimuthal angles, and cross-checked their results against existing literature at leading twist, identifying typographical errors in earlier expressions from Bacchetta (2002).

## Key numbers

- The cross section contains \(21 (\ell_{\max}+1)^2\) structure functions; for dihadrons with invariant mass \(M_h \lesssim 1\) GeV, stopping the sum at \(\ell_{\max}=2\) gives 189 structure functions.
- The covariant definition of \(\bm{R}_{T \perp}\) in the Target Rest Frame coincides with the HERMES Collaboration definition (Airapetian 2008) up to corrections of order \(1/Q^3\).
- In the Infinite Momentum Frame, the same definition coincides with other non-covariant definitions up to corrections of order \(1/Q^2\).
- The integrated cross section (over pair transverse momentum) contains 18 structure functions, with three classes of structure functions vanishing upon integration over \(P_{h \perp}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier leading-twist analysis of Bianconi (1999) and the partial-wave analysis of Bacchetta (2002), and generalizes the subleading-twist analysis of Bacchetta (2003) which was integrated over transverse momentum. The paper provides a covariant framework for dihadron SIDIS that is consistent with the Trento conventions (Bacchetta 2004) and enables future experimental analyses by experiments such as HERMES (Airapetian 2008), COMPASS (Adolph 2012), and Belle (Vossen 2011), while also correcting typographical errors in earlier theoretical expressions.
