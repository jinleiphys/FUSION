# Impact of dineutrons on nuclear compositions of a core-collapse supernova

> We study the nuclear compositions in the central region of a core-collapse supernova, assuming the existence of dineutrons ($^2n$) and tetraneutrons ($^4n$). At 100~ms after core bounce, ${}^2n$ and ${}^4n$ are more abundant than deuterons within radii of approximately 100 and 50~km, respectively. Compared to the model ignoring the existence of ${}^2n$ and ${}^4n$, the mass fraction of neutrons up to a radius of 100~km reduces, while the mass fractions of protons, deuterons, and $\rm{{}^4He}$ increase. Due to the uncertainties in the properties of $^2n$ and $^4n$, we investigate the influence of their binding energies on the nuclear composition. We find the binding energy of $^2n$ has only a modest effect on the overall composition, except for its own mass fraction, while that of $^4n$ has a negligible impact.

# Impact of dineutrons on nuclear compositions of a core-collapse supernova

## Key claim
Assuming the existence of dineutrons (²n) and tetraneutrons (⁴n) in the central region of a core-collapse supernova at 100 ms after core bounce, these multineutron states are more abundant than deuterons within radii of approximately 100 km and 50 km respectively; however, the binding energy of ²n has only a modest effect on the overall composition except for its own mass fraction, while that of ⁴n has a negligible impact.

## Method
The authors used thermodynamic inputs (mass density, electron ratio, and temperature) from a two-dimensional supernova simulation with axial symmetry approximation at 100 ms after core bounce. They applied the nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) approximation assuming ideal Boltzmann gases for nucleons and nuclei, with an excluded volume correction for nuclei, and solved the baryon number conservation and charge conservation equations to determine nuclear compositions including ²n and ⁴n. The binding energies adopted were B(²n) = -0.066 MeV and B(⁴n) = 0.42 MeV, with additional calculations exploring variations in these values.

## Key numbers
- At 100 ms after core bounce, ²n and ⁴n are more abundant than deuterons within radii of approximately 100 km and 50 km, respectively
- The binding energy of ²n adopted in the baseline model is -0.066 MeV, following Panov (2019)
- The binding energy of ⁴n adopted in the baseline model is 0.42 MeV, following Faestermann (2022)
- The maximum mass fraction of ²n with B(²n) = 1.0 MeV is approximately double that of the model with B(²n) = -3.0 MeV
- The temperature in the region of interest is higher than 0.4 MeV, justifying the NSE assumption
- 3556 nuclei are considered in the calculation, with binding energies from experimental data on Earth

## Context
This work extends the study of multineutron states in astrophysical environments, following Panov and Yudin (2019) who investigated ²n and ⁴n in supernova matter using a spherically symmetric simulation. It builds on the finding by Furusawa (2022) that deuterons are abundant in the central region of core-collapse supernovae, and uses the two-dimensional simulation data from Nagakura (2019). The results enable future studies of neutrino reactions with nucleons and light nuclei in supernova dynamics, and motivate the development of an equation of state that includes multineutron states, which the authors state they are currently developing.
