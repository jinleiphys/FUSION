# Triangle Singularities and Charmonium-like $XYZ$ States

> The spectrum of hadrons is important for understanding the confinement of quantum chromodynamics. Many new puzzles arose since 2003 due to the abundance of experimental discoveries with the $XYZ$ structures in the heavy quarkonium mass region being the outstanding examples. Hadronic resonances correspond to poles of the $S$-matrix, which has other types of singularities such as the triangle singularity due to the simultaneous on-shellness of three intermediate particles. Here we briefly discuss a few possible manifestations of triangle singularities in the $XYZ$ physics, paying particular attention to the formalism that can be used to analyze the data for charged $Z_c$ structures in the $ψπ$ distributions of the reaction $e^+e^-\to ψπ^+π^-$.

## Key claim

Triangle singularities from three intermediate particles going on-shell simultaneously can produce peaking structures in invariant mass distributions that mimic resonances; therefore, any analysis of the charged \(Z_c\) structures in \(e^+e^-\to \psi\pi^+\pi^-\) must include these triangle diagrams before concluding whether a genuine \(Z_c\) resonance exists, and the partial-wave nature of the \(D_1D^*\pi\) coupling (both \(S\)-wave and \(D\)-wave) significantly affects the predicted line shapes.

## Method

The author constructs the amplitudes for \(THH\)-type triangle diagrams (where \(T\) denotes a \(j_\ell^P=\frac32^+\) charmed meson and \(H\) a \(j_\ell^P=\frac12^-\) charmed meson) contributing to \(e^+e^-\to \psi\pi^+\pi^-\), specifically the \(D_1\bar D D^*+c.c.\), \(D_1\bar D^* D^*+c.c.\), \(D_2\bar D^* D+c.c.\) and \(D_2\bar D^* D^*+c.c.\) loops. The \(D_1D^*\pi\) coupling is treated with both an \(S\)-wave term (proportional to the pion energy, coupling constant \(|h_S|=0.57\)) and a \(D\)-wave term (coupling constant \(|h_D|=1.17\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) fixed from the \(D_2(2460)\) width). The scalar triangle loop integral is evaluated in the appendix using Feynman parameters, and the full amplitude is expressed in terms of the loop integral, the polarization vectors of the initial \(Y\) and the final \(\psi\), and the rescattering \(T\)-matrix elements for \(D^{(*)}\bar D^{(*)}\to\psi\pi\).

## Key numbers

- The \(D_1(2420)\) width is \((31.7\pm2.5)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The \(D_2(2460)\) width is \((47.5\pm1.1)\ \mathrm{MeV}\); from this the \(D\)-wave coupling constant is extracted as \(|h_D|=1.17\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\).
- Using \(|h_D|=1.17\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), the \(D\)-wave contribution to the \(D_1(2420)\to D^*\pi\) width is \(15.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (about half the total width).
- The \(S\)-wave coupling constant is \(|h_S|=0.57\).
- The \(S\)-\(D\) mixing angle for the \(D_1\) mesons is \((-0.10\pm0.04)\ \mathrm{rad}\) from Belle.
- The \(S\)-\(D\) mixing angle for vector charmonia above 4 GeV could be as large as about \(34^\circ = 0.59\ \mathrm{rad}\).
- Dalitz plot projections are shown at four c.m. energies: \(\sqrt{s}=4.226\), \(4.258\), \(4.358\) and \(4.416\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of triangle singularities in the \(XYZ\) physics, following earlier proposals that the \(D_1\bar D D^*\) loop is important for the \(Z_c(3900)\) (Wang 2013, Albaladejo 2015, Gong 2016). It addresses the discrepancy between Albaladejo (2015), who used a \(D\)-wave \(D_1D^*\pi\) coupling and concluded a \(Z_c(3900)\) resonance exists, and Pilloni (2016), who used an \(S\)-wave coupling and found the data could be fitted without a \(Z_c\). The formalism presented here, including both partial waves and all \(THH\)-type diagrams, is intended for use in experimental analyses of BESIII data for \(e^+e^-\to \psi\pi^+\pi^-\) to reliably extract resonance parameters of the \(Z_c\) states.
