# Gluon Parton Distribution of the Pion from Lattice QCD

> We present the first determination of the $x$-dependent pion gluon distribution from lattice QCD using the pseudo-PDF approach. We use lattice ensembles with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ), generated by MILC Collaboration, at two lattice spacings $a\approx 0.12$ and 0.15~fm and three pion masses $M_π\approx 220$, 310 and 690 MeV. We use clover fermions for the valence action and momentum smearing to achieve pion boost momentum up to 2.29 GeV. We find that the dependence of the pion gluon parton distribution on lattice spacing and pion mass is mild. We compare our results from the lightest pion mass ensemble with the determination by JAM and xFitter global fits.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD determination of the full $x$-dependent pion gluon parton distribution function (PDF), using the pseudo-PDF approach; the authors find that the dependence on lattice spacing and pion mass is mild, and their result is consistent with the JAM global fit for $x>0.2$ and with the xFitter global fit for $x>0.5$, within one standard deviation.

## Method

The authors used clover valence fermions on three MILC ensembles with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) at two lattice spacings (approximately 0.12 and 0.15 fm) and three pion masses (approximately 220, 310, and 690 MeV). They calculated gluon Ioffe-time distributions (ITDs) from lattice matrix elements of a gluon operator with a Wilson line, formed reduced ITDs (RITDs) via a double ratio to remove ultraviolet divergences, applied one-loop evolution to obtain evolved ITDs (EITDs), and then extracted the gluon PDF by fitting a two-parameter functional form to the EITDs using the scheme-conversion matching kernel.

## Key numbers

* Pion boost momentum up to 2.29 GeV was achieved using momentum smearing.
* Lattice spacings used: $a\approx 0.1184(10)$ fm and $0.1207(11)$ fm (both approximately 0.12 fm) and $a\approx 0.1510(20)$ fm (approximately 0.15 fm).
* Pion masses used: $M_\pi^\text{val}\approx 226.6(3)$ MeV, $311.1(6)$ MeV, and $319.1(31)$ MeV (corresponding to nominal 220, 310, and 690 MeV ensembles).
* The fitted exponent $C$ in the large-$x$ behavior $g(x)\sim(1-x)^C$ was $3.6(1.5)$, $3.3(2.0)$, and $4.7(2.8)$ for $M_\pi\approx 690$, 310, and 220 MeV, respectively, at $a\approx 0.12$ fm; at $M_\pi\approx 310$ MeV, $C=2.2(1.5)$ for $a\approx 0.15$ fm and $C=3.3(2.0)$ for $a\approx 0.12$ fm.
* The gluon-in-quark contribution to the matching was estimated to cause 5 to 10% differences in the mean PDF value for $x<0.9$, smaller than current statistical errors.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of pion structure from the valence-quark PDF (e.g., Chen (2018), Joo (2019), Sufian (2020)) to the gluon PDF, which had previously only been studied via its first moment (Meyer (2007) found $\langle x \rangle_g=0.37(8)(12)$; Shanahan (2018) found $\langle x \rangle_g=0.61(9)$). The results are compared with the NLO global fits from JAM (Barry (2018), Cao (2021)) and xFitter (Novikov (2020)), and the authors conclude that their lattice calculation provides a complementary theoretical constraint on the pion gluon PDF, with similar error size to the global analyses.
