# Exact Canonically Conjugate Momenta to Quadrupole-Type Collective Coordinates and Derivation of Nuclear Quadrupole-Type Collective Hamiltonian

> Exact canonically conjugate momenta Pi_{2mu} in quadrupole nuclear collective motions are proposed. The basic idea lies in the introduction of a discrete integral equation for the strict definition of canonically conjugate momenta to collective variables phi_{2mu}. A part of our collective Hamiltonian, the Pi_{2mu}-dependence of the kinetic part of the Hamiltonian, is given exactly. Further, phi_{2mu}-dependence of the kinetic part of the Hamiltonian is also given.

# Exact Canonically Conjugate Momenta to Quadrupole-Type Collective Coordinates and Derivation of Nuclear Quadrupole-Type Collective Hamiltonian

## Key claim

The paper proposes exact canonically conjugate momenta $\Pi_{2\mu}$ for quadrupole nuclear collective motions, defined through a discrete integral equation, which overcome the difficulty that the Tomonaga-type collective momenta $\pi_{2\mu}$ are not exactly canonical conjugate to the collective coordinates $\phi_{2\mu}$. The exact canonical commutation relation $[\Pi_{2\mu}, \phi_{2\mu'}] = -i\hbar \delta_{\mu\mu'}$ holds only if the Hilbert space is restricted to a collective subspace satisfying a subsidiary condition that equates the total angular momentum with a collective angular momentum.

## Method

The authors introduce quadrupole collective variables $\phi_{2\mu}$ and associated dynamical variables $\eta_{2\mu}$ following Miyazima and Tamura, then define modified variables $\pi_{2\mu}$ to simplify commutation relations. Using the Sunakawa integral equation method, they define exact canonically conjugate momenta $\Pi_{2\mu}$ via a discrete integral equation that iteratively eliminates the non-canonical terms. The kinetic part of the Hamiltonian is expanded in powers of $\Pi_{2\mu}$, and the expansion coefficients are determined by computing successive commutators with $\phi_{2\mu}$ using the explicit forms of the operators.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole collective variables are defined as $\phi_{2\mu} \equiv \frac{4\pi}{3AR_0^2} \sum_{n=1}^A r_n^2 Y_{2\mu}(\theta_n, \varphi_n)$, where $R_0$ is the nuclear equilibrium radius and $A$ is the mass number.
- The kinetic energy coefficient $T_{00}^{(2)}(\phi; R^2) = \frac{4\pi}{3AR_0^4} \frac{1}{m} \sqrt{5} R^2$ gives the mass parameter for the five-dimensional harmonic oscillator kinetic term.
- The coefficient $T_{2\Lambda}^{(2)}(\phi; R^2) = -\frac{4\pi}{3AR_0^2} \frac{1}{m} \sqrt{\frac{35}{8\pi}} R^4 \phi_{2\Lambda}$ yields a deformation-dependent mass term.
- All expansion coefficients for $n \geq 3$ vanish, and $T_{L\Lambda}^{(2)} = 0$ for $L = 1, 3, 4$.

## Context

This work extends the Tomonaga collective motion theory (Tomonaga, 1955) and the Sunakawa integral equation method (Sunakawa, 1962) to nuclear quadrupole collective motions, addressing difficulties pointed out by Miyazima and Tamura (1956) and Villars (1957) regarding non-canonical collective momenta and non-commutativity. It provides an alternative to the Gulshani and Rowe (1976) exact canonically conjugate momenta approach. The resulting kinetic Hamiltonian reproduces the irrotational flow mass parameter of the Bohr-Mottelson model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1974) and yields a deformation-dependent mass term corresponding to the Gneuss and Greiner (1971) correction, without requiring mean-field approximations. This enables a microscopic foundation for the Bohr collective Hamiltonian with exact canonical variables.
