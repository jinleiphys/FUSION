# Hadronic freeze-out in an effective relativistic mean field model

> We investigate an effective relativistic equation of state at finite values of temperature and baryon chemical potential with the inclusion of the full octet of baryons, the Delta-isobars and the lightest pseudoscalar and vector meson degrees of freedom. These last particles have been introduced within a phenomenological approach by taking into account of an effective chemical potential and mass depending on the self-consistent interaction between baryons. In this framework, we study of the hadron yield ratios measured in central heavy ion collisions over a broad energy range and present the beam energy dependence of underlying dynamic quantities like the net baryon density and the energy density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an effective relativistic mean field (RMF) model, which includes the full baryon octet, Delta-isobars, and mesons with phenomenologically defined effective masses and chemical potentials, can reproduce hadron yield ratios measured in central heavy ion collisions across a broad energy range. The key hedge is that the meson effective masses are introduced via a phenomenological postulate relating them to differences in effective baryon masses, which has "a certain degree of arbitrariness" and does not introduce additional parameters or couplings.

## Method

The author constructs an effective RMF equation of state at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, including the full baryon octet, Delta-isobars, and the lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons (pi, K, eta, eta', rho, omega, K*, phi). Meson degrees of freedom are treated as a quasi-particle Bose gas with effective chemical potentials derived from differences between effective baryon chemical potentials, and effective masses postulated from differences between effective baryon masses respecting strong interaction processes (e.g., Delta to pi N for pions, hyperon production channels for kaons). The model imposes conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and zero net strangeness, and uses two parameter sets (TM1 and GM3) with different Delta-isobar coupling scenarios (metastable and non-metastable Delta matter) to compute particle yields and compare with experimental data from AGS to RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7 to 200 GeV (from AGS to RHIC)
- Two parameter sets used: TM1 (compression modulus K = 281 MeV, effective nucleon mass M_N*/M_N = 0.63) and GM3 (K = 240 MeV, M_N*/M_N = 0.78)
- Delta scalar coupling ratio fixed at x_sigmaDelta = 1.25 (TM1) and 1.40 (GM3) for non-metastable scenarios; maximum values of 1.33 (TM1) and 1.50 (GM3) for metastable Delta matter scenarios
- Hyperon potential depths in saturated nuclear matter: U_Lambda_N = -28 MeV, U_Sigma_N = +30 MeV, U_Xi_N = -18 MeV
- Vacuum masses used: M_N = 939 MeV, M_Delta = 1232 MeV, M_pi = 140 MeV, M_K = 494 MeV, M_rho = 771 MeV, M_omega = 782 MeV, M_phi = 1020 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous RMF study (Lavagno, 2010) by including phenomenological in-medium meson masses, building on earlier statistical thermal model analyses of freeze-out parameters by Braun-Munzinger (2001), Andronic (2006, 2009, 2011), Becattini (2006), and Cleymans (2006, 2010, 2012). It contrasts with non-interacting gas models and excluded-volume approaches (Rischke, Yen, Mishustin), and with chiral SU(3) models (Zschiesche, 2002) that include meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions. The approach enables future study of the beam energy dependence of net baryon density and energy density at freeze-out, and may yield information about possible phase transition phenomena.
