# Nucleon Polarisabilities at and Beyond Physical Pion Masses

> We examine the results of Chiral Effective Field Theory ($Ď‡$EFT) for the scalar- and spin-dipole polarisabilities of the proton and neutron, both for the physical pion mass and as a function of $m_Ď€$. This provides chiral extrapolations for lattice-QCD polarisability computations. We include both the leading and sub-leading effects of the nucleon's pion cloud, as well as the leading ones of the $Î”(1232)$ resonance and its pion cloud. The analytic results are complete at N$^2$LO in the $Î´$-counting for pion masses close to the physical value, and at leading order for pion masses similar to the Delta-nucleon mass splitting. In order to quantify the truncation error of our predictions and fits as $68$\% degree-of-belief intervals, we use a Bayesian procedure recently adapted to EFT expansions. At the physical point, our predictions for the spin polarisabilities are, within respective errors, in good agreement with alternative extractions using experiments and dispersion-relation theory. At larger pion masses we find that the chiral expansion of all polarisabilities becomes intrinsically unreliable as $m_Ď€$ approaches about $300\;$MeV---as has already been seen in other observables. $Ď‡$EFT also predicts a substantial isospin splitting above the physical point for both the electric and magnetic scalar polarisabilities; and we speculate on the impact this has on the stability of nucleons. Our results agree very well with emerging lattice computations in the realm where $Ď‡$EFT converges. Curiously, for the central values of some of our predictions, this agreement persists to much higher pion masses. We speculate on whether this might be more than a fortuitous coincidence.

## Key claim

Chiral Effective Field Theory (chiEFT) predictions for nucleon polarisabilities become intrinsically unreliable when the pion mass approaches about 300 MeV, but the central values of some predictions agree with lattice computations at much higher pion masses, possibly beyond fortuitous coincidence.

## Method

The authors computed scalar and spin-dipole polarisabilities of the proton and neutron using chiEFT with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, including leading and subleading pion-cloud effects and leading Delta-resonance contributions. They employed delta-counting for pion masses near the physical value (regime i) and a single expansion parameter for pion masses comparable to the Delta-nucleon mass splitting (regime ii), quantifying truncation errors via a Bayesian procedure adapted to EFT expansions.

## Key numbers

- The Delta-nucleon mass splitting is approximately 300 MeV, which sets the scale for regime (ii) where the expansion parameter epsilon is about 0.4.
- The chiral expansion parameter P(m_pi) = m_pi / Lambda_chi uses Lambda_chi approximately 650 MeV, consistent with omega and rho meson masses.
- At the physical point, the leading-order pion-nucleon loop contributions to scalar polarisabilities scale as 1/m_pi (order e^2 delta^{-2}), and spin polarisabilities scale as 1/m_pi^2 (order e^2 delta^{-4}).
- The Delta contributions to scalar polarisabilities are order e^2 delta^{-1}, and to spin polarisabilities start at order e^2 delta^{-2}.
- The pi^0-pole contribution to spin polarisabilities has a numerical value of 11.5 for the proton at the physical pion mass (using g_piNN^2/(4pi) = 13.64).

## Context

This work extends the chiEFT framework for nucleon polarisabilities previously applied to Compton scattering analyses by Griesshammer, McGovern, and collaborators (2012, 2014), and provides chiral extrapolations needed to connect lattice-QCD computations (e.g., Chang 2015, Lujan 2014, Detmold 2010) at unphysical pion masses to the physical point. The results enable quantitative comparison between lattice data and chiEFT predictions, and inform the interpretation of the proton-neutron electromagnetic mass difference via the Cottingham sum rule as studied by Walker-Loud (2012) and Gasser (2015).
