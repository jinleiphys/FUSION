# Anisotropic linear waves and breakdown of the momentum expansion in spin magnetohydrodynamics

> We formulate spin magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) by including the magnetic-flux and total angular momentum conservation laws in the hydrodynamic framework. To specify the local angular momentum conservation, we choose the totally antisymmetric spin current. The entropy-current analysis allows for ten dissipative first-order transport coefficients including anisotropic spin relaxation rates and the conversion rate between a vorticity (shear) to a symmetric stress (antisymmetric torque), as well as anisotropic viscosities and resistivities. By employing the linear-mode analysis, we solve the first-order spin MHD equations to determine the dispersion relations with the complete information of anisotropy retained. Our analytic solutions indicate that the small-momentum expansion is spoiled by blow up of the higher-order terms when the angle between the momentum and the magnetic field approaches the right angle. This also reveals the existence of another expansion parameter, and, in light of it, we provide solutions in an alternative series expression beyond the critical angle. We confirm that these two series expansions work well in the appropriate angle ranges as compared with numerical results. Building on our findings regarding the breakdown of the small-momentum expansion in first-order theory, we proceed to discussing how these first-order solutions are modified when we include the relaxation dynamics for dissipative modes with the Israel-Stewart framework. We find that, due to the presence of the critical behavior in the first-order solutions, there remains a diffusive window even after the relaxation dynamics is introduced.

## Key claim

The small-momentum expansion in first-order spin magnetohydrodynamics breaks down when the angle between the wave vector and the magnetic field approaches a right angle, because higher-order terms diverge with powers of \(1/\cos\theta\); the authors provide an alternative series expansion that remains valid beyond this critical angle.

## Method

The authors formulate spin magnetohydrodynamics by including magnetic-flux conservation and total angular momentum conservation with a totally antisymmetric spin current, then perform an entropy-current analysis to identify ten dissipative first-order transport coefficients. They linearize the resulting hydrodynamic equations around an equilibrium state with a uniform magnetic field and zero spin density, solve the system for plane-wave perturbations at arbitrary angles between the wave vector and magnetic field, and compare analytic series expansions to numerical solutions. They also extend the analysis to include Israel-Stewart relaxation dynamics for the dissipative modes.

## Key numbers

* Ten dissipative first-order transport coefficients are identified, including anisotropic spin relaxation rates (\(\gamma_\perp\), \(\gamma_\parallel\)), the conversion rate between vorticity/shear and symmetric stress/antisymmetric torque (\(\xi_\parallel\)), anisotropic viscosities (\(\zeta_\perp\), \(\zeta_\parallel\), \(\zeta_\times\), \(\eta_\perp\), \(\eta_\parallel\)), and anisotropic resistivities (\(\rho_\perp\), \(\rho_\parallel\)).
* The small-momentum expansion fails when the angle \(\theta\) between momentum and magnetic field approaches \(\pi/2\), because higher-order terms acquire diverging powers of \(1/\cos\theta\).
* Semi-positivity of the entropy current requires \(\zeta_\perp \ge 0\), \(\zeta_\parallel \ge 0\), \(\zeta_\parallel \zeta_\perp \ge \zeta_\times^2\), \(\eta_\parallel \ge 0\), \(\eta_\perp \ge 0\), \(\gamma_\parallel \ge 0\), \(\gamma_\perp \ge 0\), \(\gamma_\parallel \eta_\parallel \ge \xi_\parallel^2\), \(\rho_\perp \ge 0\), and \(\rho_\parallel \ge 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous first-order MHD analysis (Fang 2024) by incorporating spin dynamics, building on formulations of spin hydrodynamics (Hattori 2019, Hongo 2021, Hu 2022) and relativistic MHD with anisotropic viscosities and resistivities (Grozdanov 2016, Hattori 2017, Denicol 2018). The finding that a diffusive window persists even after introducing Israel-Stewart relaxation dynamics, due to critical behavior in the first-order solutions, has implications for extending hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions to include spin degrees of freedom in the presence of strong magnetic fields.
