# Differential two-particle number and momentum correlations with the AMPT, UrQMD, and EPOS models in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{\textit{s}_{_{\rm NN}}}$= 2.76 TeV

> We report studies of charge-independent (CI) and charge-dependent (CD) two-particle differential number correlation functions, $R_{2} \left( Δη, Δφ\right)$, and transverse momentum correlation functions, $P_2 \left( Δη, Δφ\right)$ of charged particles produced in \PbPb\ collisions at the LHC centre-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 2.76 TeV with the UrQMD, AMPT and EPOS models. Model predictions for $R_2$ and $P_2$ correlation functions are presented for inclusive charged hadrons ($h^\pm$) in selected transverse momentum ranges and with full azimuthal coverage in the pseudorapidity range $|η|< 1.0$. We compare these predictions for the strength, shape, and particularly the width of the correlation functions with recent measurements of these observables by the ALICE collaboration. Our analysis indicate that comparative studies of $R_2$ and $P_2$ correlation functions provide valuable insight towards the understanding of particle production in Pb--Pb collisions. We find, in particular, that these models have quantitatively different predictions for these three observables but none reproduce the measured correlation functions reported by the ALICE collaboration. Accounting for quantum number conservation in models, particularly charge conservation, is mandatory to reproduce the detailed measurements of number and transverse momentum correlation functions.

## Key claim

None of the three models (AMPT, UrQMD, EPOS) reproduce the measured charge-independent and charge-dependent two-particle number and transverse momentum correlation functions reported by the ALICE collaboration for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and accounting for quantum number conservation, particularly charge conservation, is mandatory to match the detailed measurements.

## Method

The authors generated minimum-bias Pb-Pb collision events at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using the UrQMD hybrid model (version 3.4, ~340K events), AMPT with rescattering-on and string-melting-on (version ampt-v1.26t7-v2.26t7, ~200K events), and EPOS models. They computed charge-independent and charge-dependent differential two-particle number correlation functions R_2(Delta-eta, Delta-phi) and transverse momentum correlation functions P_2(Delta-eta, Delta-phi) for inclusive charged hadrons with 0.2 < p_T <= 2.0 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta| < 1.0, then compared the strength, shape, and width of these correlation functions to ALICE measurements.

## Key numbers

* UrQMD: ~340K minimum bias events generated with hybrid configuration, version 3.4, compiled with LHC option, using a cell size of 0.1 fm in the fluid description and transition time to hydrodynamics at 0.5 fm
* AMPT: ~200K RON/SON minimum bias events generated with version ampt-v1.26t7-v2.26t7
* Charged particles analyzed in transverse momentum range 0.2 < p_T <= 2.0 GeV/c
* Pseudorapidity acceptance range |eta| < 1.0 for all model calculations
* Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

## Context

This work extends prior studies of R_2 and P_2 correlation functions that were previously computed with PYTHIA and HERWIG models for smaller collision systems (PhysRevC.100.024909, 2019), and compares model predictions to ALICE measurements (Adam 2017, PhysRevC.100.044903) that showed the near-side peak of P_2 is significantly narrower than that of R_2 in both longitudinal and azimuthal directions. The results demonstrate that these three heavy-ion models, despite successfully reproducing single-particle spectra and flow coefficients, fail to capture the detailed correlation structures, motivating further development of models that properly implement charge conservation and other quantum number conservation laws.
