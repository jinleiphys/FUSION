# Electromagnetic radiation and collectivity in small quark-gluon droplets

> We study the multiplicity and rapidity dependence of thermal and prompt photon production in p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, using a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic framework. Direct photon anisotropic flow coefficients $v^γ_{2,3}$ and nuclear modification factor $R^γ_\mathrm{pPb}(p_T)$ are presented in both the p-going (backward) and the Pb-going (forward) directions. The interplay between initial state cold nuclear effect and final state thermal enhancement at different rapidity regions is discussed. The proposed rapidity dependent thermal photon enhancement and direct photon anisotropic flow observables can elucidate non-trivial longitudinal dynamics of hot quark-gluon plasma droplets created in small collision systems.

## Key claim
Thermal photons from the quark-gluon plasma can produce a measurable signal in direct photon observables in central p+Pb collisions, but only in the transverse momentum range 1–3 GeV and preferentially in the forward (Pb-going) rapidity region where the fireball is hotter and longer-lived; the effect is diluted by prompt photons at lower multiplicities and at other rapidities.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic simulations of p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV using the MUSIC code with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, a shear viscosity η/s = 0.1, and a switching temperature of 155 MeV for hadronization via UrQMD. Prompt photon production was computed at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD with nCTEQ15 nuclear parton distribution functions, while thermal photon emission was folded over the evolving hydrodynamic medium using thermal rates with out-of-equilibrium δf corrections.

## Key numbers
- Thermal photon yield scales as Nπ^1.69 in p+Pb collisions, much faster than the prompt photon yield which scales as Nπ^0.5.
- The δf corrections from shear viscosity suppress the direct photon anisotropic flow coefficients vγ2,3 by about 10–20%.
- The direct photon nuclear modification factor RγpPb reaches a maximum of approximately 2.5 in the rapidity window 1 < y < 2 for central 0–1% p+Pb collisions.
- Direct photon vγ2,3 values become comparable to charged hadron vchn in central p+Pb collisions when thermal radiation dominates over prompt contributions.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic modeling of small collision systems previously applied to hadronic flow observables by Shen et al. (2016) to include electromagnetic probes, complementing earlier studies of direct photons in heavy-ion collisions by Shen et al. (2015, 2016). The results enable future measurements of rapidity-dependent direct photon observables at the LHC to discriminate between initial-state cold nuclear effects and final-state thermal enhancement, thereby probing the longitudinal dynamics of quark-gluon plasma droplets created in p+Pb collisions.
