# Nuclear and neutron matter $G$-matrix calculations with Ch-EFT potential including effects of three-nucleon interaction

> Energies of symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter are evaluated in the lowest order Bruekner theory using the Ch-EFT potential including effects of the three-nucleon force (3NF). The 3NF is first reduced to density-dependent nucleon-nucleon (NN) force by folding single-nucleon degrees of freedom in infinite matter. Adding the reduced NN force to the initial NN force and applying a partial-wave expansion, we perform $G$-matrix calculations in pure neutron matter as well as in symmetric nuclear. We obtain the saturation curve which is close to the empirical one. It is explicitly shown that the cutoff-energy dependence of the calculated energies is substantially reduced by including the 3NF. Characters of the 3NF contributions in separate spin and isospin channels are discussed. Calculated energies of the neutron matter are very similar to those used in the literature for considering neutron star properties.

## Key claim

The lowest-order Brueckner theory (LOBT) calculation using the chiral effective field theory (Ch-EFT) N$^3$LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential combined with its N$^2$LO three-nucleon force (3NF), reduced to a density-dependent NN force, yields a nuclear matter saturation curve close to the empirical one, but the energy at the saturation point remains shallow by a few MeV, which is within the uncertainty of the lowest-order calculation and the uncertainties of low-energy constants and cutoff parameters.

## Method

The 3NF was reduced to a density-dependent NN force by folding single-nucleon degrees of freedom in infinite matter, summing over the third nucleon in the Fermi sea. This reduced NN force was added to the initial Ch-EFT NN interaction, and standard G-matrix calculations were performed in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using a partial-wave expansion up to total angular momentum J=7 and orbital angular momentum ℓ=7, with an angle-average approximation for the Pauli exclusion operator.

## Key numbers

- Saturation curve in symmetric nuclear matter: with 3NF included, the energy per nucleon at saturation is close to the empirical curve but shallow by a few MeV (exact numerical value not given in text).
- Scheerbaum factor B_S(T=1) in nuclear matter at k_F=1.35 fm$^{-1}$: 84.6 MeV·fm$^5$ without 3NF, 120.2 MeV·fm$^5$ with 3NF.
- Scheerbaum factor B_S(T=1) in neutron matter at k_F=1.35 fm$^{-1}$: 84.7 MeV·fm$^5$ without 3NF, 93.3 MeV·fm$^5$ with 3NF.
- Scheerbaum factor B_S(T=1) in nuclear matter at k_F=1.07 fm$^{-1}$: 86.5 MeV·fm$^5$ without 3NF, 109.8 MeV·fm$^5$ with 3NF.
- Scheerbaum factor B_S(T=1) in neutron matter at k_F=1.07 fm$^{-1}$: 87.0 MeV·fm$^5$ without 3NF, 92.3 MeV·fm$^5$ with 3NF.
- The difference between U_G(k) and U_E(k) single-particle potentials is on the order of 5 MeV for |k| ≤ 2 fm$^{-1}$; the total energy per nucleon is lowered by about 0.5 MeV by the additional term.
- Low-energy constants used: c_1=-0.81 GeV$^{-1}$, c_3=-3.4 GeV$^{-1}$, c_4=3.4 GeV$^{-1}$, c_D=-4.381, c_E=-1.126.

## Context

This work extends the lowest-order Brueckner theory calculations of nuclear matter using the Ch-EFT interaction, following a brief report by the same author (Kohno 2012) that focused on the effective spin-orbit strength, and similar LOBT calculations by Soma et al. (2012). The approach of reducing the 3NF to a density-dependent NN force follows methods used by Kato et al. (1974), Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), and Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2010). The results for neutron matter are close to the variational calculations of the Illinois group (Friedman and Pandharipande 1981; Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall 1998), which have been used as the canonical equation of state for neutron star properties. The work demonstrates that the 3NF constructed consistently with the NN part in chiral effective field theory can reproduce basic nuclear properties without phenomenological adjustments, and the calculated asymmetry dependence of the spin-orbit strength (approximately 1-0.22α) is consistent with recent fitting by Reinhard et al.
