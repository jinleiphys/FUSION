# Finite Temperature Meson Correlation Functions in HTL Approximation

> We calculate temporal correlators and their spectral functions with meson quantum numbers in the deconfined phase of QCD using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for the quark propagator. Although this approach does not result in a complete next-to-leading order perturbative calculation it takes into account important medium effects such as thermal quark masses and Landau damping in the quark-gluon plasma. We show that both effects lead to competing modifications of the free mesonic correlation functions. We find that correlators in scalar channels are only moderately influenced by the HTL medium effects, while the HTL-vertex corrections lead to divergent vector correlators.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, the competing effects of thermal quark masses (which steepen the correlator) and Landau damping (which flattens it) largely compensate each other in scalar meson channels, making the HTL-resummed correlators quite similar to free correlators; however, the vector channel suffers from an infrared divergence in the spectral function due to HTL-vertex corrections, making the vector correlator ill-defined without subtraction.

## Method

The authors computed temporal meson correlation functions and their spectral functions at zero external momentum in the deconfined phase of QCD using the HTL-resummed quark propagator (and HTL-resummed quark-meson vertex for the vector channel). They evaluated the pole-pole, pole-cut, and cut-cut contributions to the spectral functions separately, then inserted these spectral functions into the integral representation for the Euclidean-time correlator. For the vector channel, they defined a subtracted correlator to remove the infrared divergence.

## Key numbers

- Thermal quark mass: \( m_T = g(T) T / \sqrt{6} \)
- The plasmino branch of the quark dispersion relation exhibits a minimum at \(\omega = 0.47 m_T\) and \(1.856 m_T\), where van Hove singularities appear in the spectral function
- For \(\tilde{m}_T = 1\) (extrapolating to \(g = \sqrt{6}\)), the difference between the free and HTL-resummed pseudo-scalar correlator is largest at \(\tau T \simeq 1/2\)
- The ratio \(G_{ps}^{\rm HTL}(\tau) / G_{ps}^{\rm free}(\tau)\) is shown for thermal quark masses \(\tilde{m}_T = 0.5\), \(1.0\), and \(2.0\)
- The vector spectral function diverges linearly at low frequencies due to the cut-cut contribution, rendering the temporal correlator infrared divergent

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of meson spectral functions beyond the free-quark approximation by incorporating medium effects via the HTL resummation scheme of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and Klimov (1982). It provides a baseline for interpreting lattice QCD results for temporal correlation functions, such as those from Hasenfratz et al. (1993), Boyd et al. (1994, 1995), and de Forcrand et al. (1999), which show significant deviations from free correlators near \(T_c\). The paper concludes that HTL-resummed perturbation theory, while successful for bulk thermodynamics above \(2T_c\) (as shown by Blaizot et al. (1999) and Andersen et al. (1999)), is insufficient to explain lattice results for pseudo-scalar correlators, suggesting additional non-perturbative effects related to chiral symmetry restoration are needed.
