# Real-time dynamics of Chern-Simons fluctuations near a critical point

> The real-time topological susceptibility is studied in $(1+1)$-dimensional massive Schwinger model with a $θ$-term. We evaluate the real-time correlation function of electric field that represents the topological Chern-Pontryagin number density in $(1+1)$ dimensions. Near the parity-breaking critical point located at $θ=π$ and fermion mass $m$ to coupling $g$ ratio of $m/g \approx 0.33$, we observe a sharp maximum in the topological susceptibility. We interpret this maximum in terms of the growth of critical fluctuations near the critical point, and draw analogies between the massive Schwinger model, QCD near the critical point, and ferroelectrics near the Curie point.

## Key claim
Near the parity-breaking critical point at θ=π and m/g ≈ 0.33, the real-time topological susceptibility exhibits a sharp maximum, interpreted as critical fluctuations of the Chern-Simons number density; this behavior is analogous to electric susceptibility near the Curie point in ferroelectrics and may have implications for the QCD critical point search.

## Method
The authors placed the massive Schwinger model with a θ-term on a spatial lattice using staggered fermions and solved the Gauss law constraint to eliminate electric field operators. They computed the real-time topological susceptibility as the zero-frequency, zero-wavelength limit of the two-point correlation function of the electric field (which is proportional to the Chern-Pontryagin number density in 1+1 dimensions), using exact diagonalization via the QuSpin package after converting the lattice Hamiltonian to a spin Hamiltonian via the Jordan-Wigner transformation.

## Key numbers
- The critical point is located at m* ≈ 0.33 g (fermion mass to coupling ratio), in accord with previous studies by Schiller (1983), Hamer (1982), and Byrnes (2002).
- The dimensionless real-time topological susceptibility χ_CS/g² shows a sharp peak as a function of m/g, with the peak position at m*/g ≈ -0.33 (negative mass corresponding to θ=π).
- Finite-size scaling analysis uses the rescaled susceptibility N^{-7/4} χ_CS/g² plotted against N(m-m*)/g, with the exponent 7/4 corresponding to the universality class of the 2D classical Ising model.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of real-time vector current dynamics in the Schwinger model (Kharzeev, 2020) and builds on Coleman's (1976) demonstration of the quantum phase transition at θ=π. The phase diagram in the (m/g, θ) plane is reminiscent of the QCD phase diagram in the (T, μ) plane discussed by Rajagopal (2001) and Stephanov (2004). The critical behavior belongs to the universality class of model A in the Hohenberg-Halperin classification, and the findings enable future digital quantum simulations of real-time topological fluctuations near critical points, with potential applications to detecting topological charge fluctuations at RHIC through charged hadron asymmetries.
