# Shell-model study of octupole collectivity near 208Pb

> We show that the collectivity of the particle-hole wave function of low-lying octupole 3^- states in doubly magic nuclei is mainly due to the neutron-proton interaction. Both the enhanced reduced transition probability to the ground state, B(E3;3-_1 -> 0+_1), and the coupling of the octupole excitation to a nucleon result from the coherent action of all the components of the collective state. The results obtained with a realistic shell-model interaction both for 208Pb and 209Pb agree with the geometric collective model of Bohr and Mottelson, where octupole excitations are associated with phonons corresponding to collective shape oscillations of the surface of the nucleus.

## Key claim
The collectivity of the low-lying octupole 3⁻ state in doubly magic nuclei arises predominantly from the neutron-proton interaction, not from the like-nucleon interactions alone. The authors show that when the neutron-proton interaction is artificially set to zero, octupole collectivity is "greatly diminished," whereas removing the neutron-neutron and proton-proton interactions causes a less important loss of collectivity.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for ²⁰⁸Pb and ²⁰⁹Pb using a realistic Hamiltonian in a model space spanning two major shells for neutrons (N=82 to 184) and protons (Z=50 to 126), with effective single-nucleon energies from Warburton and Brown (1991) and two-body matrix elements from Wrzesinski (2001). For ²⁰⁸Pb, they diagonalized the Hamiltonian in a 1p1h basis to obtain the wave function of the collective 3⁻ state; for ²⁰⁹Pb, they used a 1p0h+2p1h basis and compared the resulting eigenstates to those from a two-state octupole model coupling single-particle orbitals to the 3⁻ phonon. They also employed a schematic surface delta interaction (SDI) model to derive analytic expressions for the wave function coefficients and transition probabilities.

## Key numbers
- The first-excited 3⁻ level in ²⁰⁸Pb is calculated at E_x = 2460 keV, compared to the experimental value of 2615 keV.
- The calculated B(E3; 3⁻_c → 0⁺_1) = 36 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 34.0(5) W.u. from Spear (1983).
- The overlap between the realistic and schematic (SDI) wave functions for the 3⁻_c state in ²⁰⁸Pb is approximately 0.87.
- For the ²⁰⁹Pb octupole-particle states, the squared overlap of the upper-energy eigenstate with the full-space wave function is 0.97 for J₂^π = 9/2₂⁺ and 0.91 for 15/2₂⁻.
- The J^π = 21/2⁺₁ state in ²⁰⁹Pb has a squared overlap of 0.996 with the |3⁻_c × j₁₅/₂; J⟩ configuration.
- The J^π = 13/2⁺₁ state in ²⁰⁹Pb has a squared overlap of 0.74 with the |3⁻_c × j₁₅/₂; J⟩ configuration.
- The SDI strength parameters are estimated as (25/A) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the geometric collective model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975), where octupole excitations are treated as surface vibrations, by providing a microscopic shell-model foundation for the phononic behavior of octupole states near ²⁰⁸Pb. The authors show that the neutron-proton interaction is the key driver of octupole collectivity, contradicting earlier implicit assumptions that like-nucleon interactions dominate. The results enable future shell-model studies of octupole correlations in heavy nuclei and provide a microscopic derivation of the particle-vibration coupling form factor, connecting the shell-model approach to the collective model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975).
