# The phi to pi+ pi- decay within a chiral unitary approach

> Starting from the Chiral Perturbation Theory Lagrangian, but keeping different masses for the charged and neutral mesons, and using a previously developed non-perturbative unitary scheme that generates the lightest meson-meson resonances, we construct K Kbar to K Kbar and K Kbar to pi+ pi- in the vector channel. This allows us to obtain the kaon-loop contribution to the phi-rho mixing and study the phi to pi+pi- decay. The dominant contribution to this decay comes from the phi to gamma to pi+pi- process. However, there can be large interferences with the subdominant contributions coming from phi-rho and phi-omega mixing, or of these two contributions among themselves. As a consequence, a reliable measurement of phi to pi+pi- decay could be used to differentiate between some phi-omega mixing scenarios proposed in the literature.

## Key claim

The dominant contribution to the $\phi \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ decay comes from the electromagnetic process $\phi \rightarrow \gamma \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$, but large interferences with subdominant contributions from $\phi-\rho$ and $\phi-\omega$ mixing mean that a reliable measurement could differentiate between different $\phi-\omega$ mixing scenarios proposed in the literature.

## Method

The authors start from the Chiral Perturbation Theory Lagrangian with different masses for charged and neutral mesons ($m_u \neq m_d$), and use a previously developed non-perturbative unitary scheme (the inverse amplitude method in coupled channels) that generates the lightest meson-meson resonances. They construct $K \bar{K} \rightarrow K \bar{K}$ and $K \bar{K} \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ amplitudes in the vector channel, extract the kaon-loop contribution to $\phi-\rho$ mixing from the residues of the $\Omega_8$ resonance pole, and combine this with tree-level electromagnetic and $\phi-\omega$ mixing contributions to obtain the total $\phi \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ decay width.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level electromagnetic contribution alone yields $BR(\phi \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-) = 1.7 \times 10^{-4}$, using $F_V = 154$ MeV from $\rho \rightarrow e^+ e^-$ decay.
- The $\rho-\omega$ mixing from quark mass differences is $-5221.6$ MeV$^2$, from electromagnetic contribution is $725.1$ MeV$^2$, and from kaon loops is $-130$ MeV$^2$ (evaluated at $s = M_\rho^2$).
- The $\Omega_8$ resonance (the octet component related to the $\phi$) appears with a mass around 910 MeV, close to the 930 MeV predicted by SU(3) mass formulae.
- The $\phi-\omega$ mixing parameter $|\delta_V| \simeq 0.05$, with two scenarios considered: "weak mixing" (Re $\tilde{\Theta}_{\phi\omega} = 0$, $g_{\phi\rho\pi} = 0.78$ GeV$^{-1}$) and "strong mixing" (Re $\tilde{\Theta}_{\phi\omega} = 20000$ to $29000$ MeV$^2$, $g_{\phi\rho\pi} = 0$).
- The kaon loop contribution to $\tilde{\Theta}_{\rho\omega}$ is of order $200$ MeV$^2$, numerically irrelevant compared to the quark mass difference contribution.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1998) to include isospin violation in the vector channel, building on earlier studies of $\phi-\rho$ mixing by Bramon (1972), Achasov (1979), and Genz (1980). The method allows calculation of the kaon-loop contribution to $\phi \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ decay, which is OZI violating and subleading in the large $N_c$ expansion, and addresses the conflicting experimental results from CMD-2 ($BR = (2.20 \pm 0.25 \pm 0.20) \times 10^{-4}$) and SND ($BR = (0.71 \pm 0.11 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-4}$) at VEPP-2M. The results enable using precise measurements of this decay to discriminate between different $\phi-\omega$ mixing scenarios, and demonstrate that the chiral unitary approach can handle OZI-violating processes through proper renormalization of loop contributions.
