# Form factors in lattice QCD

> Lattice simulations of QCD have produced precise estimates for the masses of the lowest-lying hadrons which show excellent agreement with experiment. By contrast, lattice results for the vector and axial vector form factors of the nucleon show significant deviations from their experimental determination. We present results from our ongoing project to compute a variety of form factors with control over all systematic uncertainties. In the case of the pion electromagnetic form factor we employ partially twisted boundary conditions to extract the pion charge radius directly from the linear slope of the form factor near vanishing momentum transfer. In the nucleon sector we focus specifically on the possible contamination from contributions of higher excited states. We argue that summed correlation functions offer the possibility of eliminating this source of systematic error. As an illustration of the method we discuss our results for the axial charge, gA, of the nucleon.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of nucleon vector and axial vector form factors show significant deviations from experimental determinations, but the authors argue that these discrepancies may be resolved by controlling systematic uncertainties, particularly contamination from excited states, rather than indicating a failure of QCD.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with Nf=2 flavors of O(a) improved Wilson fermions on ensembles at three lattice spacings (a≈0.08, 0.07, and 0.05 fm) and pion masses from approximately 250 MeV to 700 MeV. For the pion electromagnetic form factor, they employ partially twisted boundary conditions to access arbitrarily small momentum transfers and extract the charge radius from the linear slope near q²=0. For nucleon form factors, they argue that summed correlation functions can eliminate contamination from higher excited states, and illustrate the method with results for the axial charge gA.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings used: a≈0.08, 0.07, and 0.05 fm
- Pion mass range: mπ≈250 MeV to 700 MeV
- Lattice volumes satisfy mπL > 4 on all ensembles
- For the pion charge radius extraction, the linear slope was determined over the interval -0.15 ≤ (qr₀)² ≤ 0, corresponding to |q| ≲ 150 MeV (using r₀=0.5 fm)
- Lattice calculations of the nucleon axial charge gA typically lie 10-15% below the experimental value

## Context

This work extends the long effort by multiple collaborations (CP-PACS, MILC, UKQCD, BGR, PACS-CS, BMW, ETM, LHPC, QCDSF, RBC/UKQCD) to compute hadron properties from lattice QCD. While lattice QCD has successfully reproduced the hadron mass spectrum at the few percent level (as reviewed by the FLAG Working Group, Colangelo et al. 2010), nucleon form factors remain problematic. The authors' use of partially twisted boundary conditions for the pion form factor follows the technique developed by Bedaque (2004) and de Divitiis et al. (2004), and their summed correlation function method for nucleons aims to address the excited-state contamination that has plagued prior calculations by LHPC, QCDSF, RBC/UKQCD, and ETM collaborations. This work enables future benchmark calculations of nucleon structure with controlled systematics, including continuum extrapolation and chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass.
