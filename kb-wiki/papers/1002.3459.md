# The energy dependence of the pp->K+ n Sigma+ reaction close to threshold

> The production of the Sigma+ hyperon through the pp->K+nSigma+ reaction has been investigated at four energies close to threshold, 1.826, 1.920, 1.958, and 2.020 GeV. At low energies, correlated K+pi+ pairs can only originate from Sigma+ production so that their measurement allows the total cross section for the reaction to be determined. The results obtained are completely consistent with the values extracted from the study of the K+-proton correlation spectra obtained in the same experiment. These spectra, as well as the inclusive K+ momentum distributions, also provide conservative upper limits on the Sigma+ production rates. The measurements show a Sigma+ production cross section that varies roughly like phase space and, in particular, none of the three experimental approaches used supports the anomalously high near-threshold pp->K+ nSigma+ total cross section previously reported [T. Rozek et al., Phys. Lett. B 643, 251 (2006)].

## Key claim

The total cross section for the $pp\to K^+ n \Sigma^+$ reaction near threshold varies roughly like phase space, and none of the three experimental approaches used supports the anomalously high near-threshold cross section previously reported by Rozek et al. (2006).

## Method

The experiment was carried out at the COSY-ANKE facility using an unpolarized proton beam at five beam energies (1.775, 1.826, 1.920, 1.958, and 2.020 GeV), with the 1.775 GeV point below the Sigma threshold serving as a background reference. Three independent methods were used to extract the Sigma+ production cross section: inclusive K+ momentum distributions compared above and below threshold, K+proton missing-mass spectra, and K+pi+ coincidence measurements where the pi+ originates from the Sigma+ decay (branching ratio 48.3%). The luminosity was determined from pp elastic scattering using the SAID analysis.

## Key numbers

* Upper limit on the total cross section for Sigma+ production at 1.826 GeV (excess energy 13 MeV) is below 45 nb at the 98% confidence level, derived from inclusive K+ counting rate ratios.
* The ratio of combined Sigma production to Lambda production at 1.826 GeV is (4 +/- 2) x 10^-3, based on inclusive K+ data.
* The cross section ratio R(Sigma+/Sigma0) = sigma(pp -> K+ n Sigma+)/sigma(pp -> K+ p Sigma0) was reported by Rozek et al. (2006) as 230 +/- 70 at excess energy 13 MeV and 90 +/- 40 at 60 MeV, values that are excluded by the present measurements.
* Bubble chamber data at excess energy 350 MeV give R(Sigma+/Sigma0) = 2.3 +/- 0.9 (Lou 1961).
* The triangle inequality at excess energy 126 MeV constrains R(Sigma+/Sigma0) to lie between 1/6 and 6 (Abd 2004).

## Context

This work extends the earlier ANKE measurement at 2.16 GeV (Valdau 2007) to the near-threshold region and directly contradicts the anomalously high cross sections reported by Rozek et al. (2006) from COSY-11 neutron detection measurements. The results are consistent with the isospin constraints from the triangle inequality relating Sigma+ and Sigma0 production amplitudes, and with the COSY-TOF measurement at 126 MeV excess energy (Abd 2004). The measurements enable reliable determination of the Sigma+ production cross section near threshold and clarify the energy dependence of associated strangeness production in pp collisions.
