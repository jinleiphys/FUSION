# Impact of $(α,n)$ reactions on weak r-process in neutrino-driven winds

> After a successful core-collapse supernova, a neutrino-driven wind develops where it is possible to synthesize lighter heavy elements ($30<Z<45$). In the early galaxy, the origin of these elements is associated to the r-process and to an additional process. Here we assume the additional process corresponds to the weak r-process or alpha-process taking place in neutrino-driven winds. Based on a trajectory obtained from hydrodynamical simulations we study the astrophysics and nuclear physics uncertainties with our main focus on the $(α,n)$ reactions. These are critical to redistribute the matter and allow to further move it from light to heavy elements after nuclear statistical equilibrium freezes out. In this first sensitivity study, we vary all $(α,n)$ reactions by given constant factors which are justified based on the uncertainties of the statistical model and its nuclear physics input, mainly alpha optical potentials under the conditions found in the wind. Our results show that $(α,n)$ reactions are indeed very important to predict abundances. Therefore, further studies will follow to identify individual critical reactions. Since the nucleosynthesis path is close to stability, these reactions can be measured in the near future. This will uniquely allow to reduce the nuclear physics uncertainties and thus to use observations to constrain and understand the astrophysical conditions in the wind.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that uncertainties in (α,n) reaction rates, varied by constant factors justified by theoretical model spread, have a significant impact on the predicted elemental abundances of lighter heavy elements (Z=30–45) produced in the weak r-process in neutrino-driven winds, but only for electron fractions Ye ≤ 0.47; for Ye = 0.49 the impact is negligible.

## Method

The authors used the WINNET reaction network with 4412 nuclei from H to Ir, starting from nuclear statistical equilibrium at T ≈ 10 GK and using a single hydrodynamic trajectory ejected 9 s after bounce (entropy S ≈ 86 kB/nuc, expansion timescale τ = 11 ms). They calculated all (α,n) rates for isotopes with 26 < Z < 45 using the Hauser-Feshbach code TALYS 1.6, then multiplied and divided these rates by constant factors of 5, 10, and 50 to simulate the theoretical uncertainty arising mainly from different alpha optical potential models. The sensitivity study was performed for three initial electron fractions (Ye = 0.45, 0.47, 0.49) to explore different nucleosynthesis paths.

## Key numbers

- The trajectory used has entropy S ≈ 86 kB/nuc and expansion timescale τ = 11 ms, ejected 9 s after bounce.
- The weak r-process operates with a neutron-to-seed ratio Yn/Yseed ≲ 10⁻², compared to Yn/Yseed > 100 for the classical r-process.
- Theoretical (α,n) rates calculated with different alpha optical potentials disagree by more than a factor of 10 at temperatures T ≈ 2 GK.
- For Ye = 0.47, multiplying all (α,n) rates by a factor of 50 produces relative abundance changes up to ~100 for elements with Z > 38.
- For Ye = 0.45, large variations appear in the Zr/Sr, Nb/Sr, and Ag/Zr abundance ratios when (α,n) rates are varied by factors of 10 and 0.1.
- For Ye = 0.49, there is almost no effect on abundances when changing the (α,n) reactions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the weak r-process (also called alpha-process) in neutrino-driven winds by Arcones and Montes (2011) and Arcones and Bliss (2014), and builds on the theoretical uncertainty analysis of (α,n) reactions by Pereira and Montes (2016) and Mohr (2016). The paper shows that (α,n) reaction uncertainties are comparable in importance to astrophysical uncertainties (variation of electron fraction) for predicting the abundances of Sr through Ag, and that reducing these nuclear uncertainties through future measurements on unstable nuclei near stability will allow observations of metal-poor stars to constrain the astrophysical conditions in the wind.
