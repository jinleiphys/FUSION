# Induced theta-vacuum states in heavy ion collisions: a possible signature

> It has been suggested recently that an arbitrary induced theta-vacuum state could be created in heavy ion collisions. If such a state can be created, it would decay by various mechanisms to the fundamental theta=0 state which is the true ground state of our world. In the following we will discuss the possibility of studying this unusual state through the emission of pions, eta-mesons, and eta'-mesons. We will also present the spectrum of the produced particles in this non-zero theta background. We use the instantaneous perturbation theory for our estimates.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a macroscopically large domain with a nonzero induced theta-vacuum state (θ^{ind}) could be created locally and temporarily in heavy ion collisions after the QCD phase transition, and that this state would decay by emitting low-momentum neutral Goldstone bosons (π⁰, η, η') with a specific spectrum. The claim is hedged by the use of an instantaneous perturbation approximation for the decay, which the authors acknowledge is not fully justified since the shell lifetime τ_{shell} is expected to be comparable to (Λ_{QCD})⁻¹.

## Method

The authors model the formation of an induced θ-vacuum state using an effective chiral Lagrangian for three flavors of massive quarks, with fields φᵢ (i = u, d, s) representing the phases of the chiral condensate. They numerically solve the coupled equations of motion on a cubic lattice with spacing 1 fm and volume (8 fm)³, assigning random initial values to the phases and including a damping term γ. At a chosen time τ_{shell} when the separating shell is assumed to disappear instantaneously, the field configuration is embedded into a larger grid with zero field values (the θ=0 world), and the Fourier transform of the field configuration is used to compute the momentum spectrum of the produced π⁰, η, and η' mesons, with the overall normalization fixed by the available vacuum energy difference ΔE·V.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum energy density difference between the θ-state and the θ=0 state is ΔE = 2m|⟨q̄q⟩|(1 - |cos(θ/2)|), yielding approximately 20 GeV of available energy for a θ-state volume of (10 fm)³.
- For a θ-state volume of (8 fm)³, the available energy is ΔE·V = 10 GeV.
- The total collision center-of-mass energy is √s = 40 TeV, so only a fraction ρ ∼ 10⁻³ (V/(10 fm)³) of the total energy is released through θ-state decay.
- For τ_{shell} = t₁ (earlier time): N_{π⁰} = 0.1, N_{η} = 2.8, N_{η'} = 19.3 per collision.
- For τ_{shell} = t₂ (later time): N_{π⁰} = 0.5, N_{η} = 4.3, N_{η'} = 18.4 per collision.
- The momentum distribution of produced particles is primarily below k < 25 MeV for the chosen volume of (8 fm)³.
- In the θ-background, the decay widths are Γ_{θ≠0}(η' → ππ) ∼ 2 MeV (compared to Γ_{θ=0}^{total}(η') ∼ 0.2 MeV experimentally) and Γ_{θ≠0}(η → ππ) ∼ 0.2 MeV (compared to Γ_{θ=0}^{total}(η) ∼ 118 keV experimentally).
- The numerical simulation used 6000 time steps of size 10⁻²⁷ s on a cubic lattice with spacing a = 0.005 MeV⁻¹ = 1 fm.
- Quark masses used: m_u = 4 MeV, m_d = 8 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Buckley, Fugleberg, and Zhitnitsky (1999) that induced θ-vacuum states could be created in heavy ion collisions, and is closely analogous to the disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) scenario studied by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and others. The paper provides numerical estimates for the spectrum of Goldstone bosons produced when such a state decays, which could serve as an experimental signature at RHIC. The authors suggest this mechanism might also explain the unexplained large number of low momentum dileptons seen at CERN, as proposed in earlier work by Zhitnitsky (1999), but note that detailed calculations are needed to support this conjecture.
