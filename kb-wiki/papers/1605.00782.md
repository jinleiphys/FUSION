# Algebraic benchmark for prolate-oblate coexistence in nuclei

> We present a symmetry-based approach for prolate-oblate and spherical-prolate-oblate shape coexistence, in the framework of the interacting boson model of nuclei. The proposed Hamiltonian conserves the SU(3) and $\overline{\rm SU(3)}$ symmetry for the prolate and oblate ground bands and the U(5) symmetry for selected spherical states. Analytic expressions for quadrupole moments and $E2$ rates involving these states are derived and isomeric states are identified by means of selection rules.

# Algebraic benchmark for prolate-oblate coexistence in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper presents a symmetry-based Hamiltonian within the interacting boson model that can simultaneously accommodate prolate and oblate ground bands with pure SU(3) and $\overline{\rm SU(3)}$ symmetry, respectively, and selected spherical states with U(5) symmetry, but only at the critical point where the multiple minima in the energy surface are near-degenerate and the coexisting bands remain unmixed.

## Method

The authors construct a single number-conserving Hamiltonian using an intrinsic-collective resolution that annihilates the intrinsic states of both the prolate $(2N,0)$ SU(3) irrep and the oblate $(0,2N)$ $\overline{\rm SU(3)}$ irrep, as well as the spherical U(5) state when a parameter is set to zero. The Hamiltonian contains three-body boson interactions and a small $s$-parity breaking term to generate nonzero quadrupole moments, with the energy surface derived analytically to show degenerate minima at $(\beta=\sqrt{2},\gamma=0)$ and $(\beta=\sqrt{2},\gamma=\pi/3)$.

## Key numbers

- The degenerate global minima occur at $\beta=\sqrt{2}$ for both prolate ($\gamma=0$) and oblate ($\gamma=\pi/3$) shapes, with $\tilde{E}=0$ at these minima.
- For prolate-oblate coexistence, the $\beta$ and $\gamma$ vibration frequencies about each deformed minimum are $\epsilon_{\beta 1}=\epsilon_{\beta 2} = \frac{8}{3}(h_0+ 2h_2)N^2$ and $\epsilon_{\gamma 1}=\epsilon_{\gamma 2} = 4\eta_3N^2$.
- For triple spherical-prolate-oblate coexistence ($h_0=0$), the quadrupole vibration frequency about the spherical minimum is $\epsilon = 4h_2N^2$.
- The quadrupole moments of the prolate $g_1$ and oblate $g_2$ bands have equal magnitudes and opposite signs, given by $Q_L = \mp e_B\sqrt{16\pi/40}\frac{L}{2L+3}\frac{4(2N-L)(2N+L+1)}{3(2N-1)}$.
- The $B(E2)$ values for intra-band transitions in both ground bands are identical: $B(E2; g_i, L+2\to g_i, L) = e_B^2\frac{3(L+1)(L+2)}{2(2L+3)(2L+5)}\frac{(4N-1)^2(2N-L)(2N+L+3)}{18(2N-1)^2}$.
- For triple coexistence, the $B(E2)$ value for the spherical transition $(n_d=1,L=2\to n_d=0,L=0)$ is $e_B^2 N$.
- The $n_d=2$ component in the $L=0,2,4$ states of the deformed ground bands is of order $N^3 3^{-N}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of prolate-oblate phase transitions in the IBM (Jolie 2001, Jolie 2003) and asymmetric prolate-oblate coexistence using higher-order SU(3)-invariant interactions (Draayer 2012). It differs from configuration-mixing approaches (Foisson 2003, Frank 2004, Morales 2008) by using a single number-conserving Hamiltonian. The framework provides an algebraic benchmark for interpreting shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Kr (Clement 2007), Se (Ljungvall 2008), Hg (Bree 2014), and neutron-rich Sr (Clement 2016) isotopes, and can serve as a test-ground for more detailed microscopic treatments.
