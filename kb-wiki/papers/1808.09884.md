# Theoretical analysis of double parton scatterings in quarkonium production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> The production process of quarkonia in proton-proton (pp) collision is a very good probe of the parton structure of the proton. Recent experimental data of the production of J/psi+vector boson or quarkonium pairs at the LHC and Tevatron suggest the relevance of double parton scatterings (DPS). We discuss here the single parton scattering (SPS) contribution to the J/psi +Z, J/psi +W, and J/psi +J/psi productions in hadron collisions. By revisiting the computations of the SPS contributions to the J/psi+Z and J/psi +W productions, we demonstrate that the ATLAS data in fact show evidence for DPS.

## Key claim

The ATLAS data for \(J/\psi+Z\) and \(J/\psi+W\) production, which appear to exceed single-parton-scattering (SPS) predictions combined with double-parton-scattering (DPS) estimates using \(\sigma_{\rm eff}=15\) mb, actually show evidence for DPS when the SPS contribution is evaluated conservatively using the color evaporation model (CEM) and the DPS component is fitted to the data.

## Method

The authors computed the SPS contributions to \(J/\psi+Z\) and \(J/\psi+W\) production at next-to-leading order (NLO) in \(\alpha_s\) using the CEM, which provides an upper limit on the SPS yield because it overshoots single-quarkonium data at high transverse momentum. They then subtracted these CEM-based SPS predictions from the ATLAS inclusive cross-section measurements and fitted the remaining yield to the DPS pocket formula to extract \(\sigma_{\rm eff}\). For \(J/\psi+J/\psi\) production, they performed a LO CEM calculation to estimate the SPS contribution and compared it with existing NRQCD-based results.

## Key numbers

- \(\sigma_{J/\psi+Z} = 0.19^{+0.05}_{-0.04}\) pb from the NLO CEM calculation.
- \(\sigma_{J/\psi+W} = 0.28 \pm 0.07\) pb from the NLO CEM calculation.
- Fitted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (4.7 ^{+2.4}_{-1.5})\) mb for \(J/\psi+Z\) production from ATLAS data.
- Fitted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (6.1 ^{+3.3}_{-1.9})\) mb for \(J/\psi+W\) production from ATLAS data.
- ATLAS measured \(\sigma(J/\psi+Z) = 1.6 \pm 0.4\) pb and \(\sigma(J/\psi+W) = 4.5 ^{+1.9}_{-1.5}\) pb.
- DPS estimate using \(\sigma_{\rm eff}=15\) mb gives 0.46 pb for \(J/\psi+Z\) and 1.7 pb for \(J/\psi+W\).
- SPS predictions from color singlet model (CSM) give \(0.025-0.125\) pb for \(J/\psi+Z\) and \((0.11 \pm 0.04)\) pb for \(J/\psi+W\).
- SPS predictions from color-octet mechanism (COM) give \(<0.1\) pb for \(J/\psi+Z\) and \(0.16-0.22\) pb for \(J/\psi+W\).
- D0 extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (4.8 \pm 0.5_{\rm stat} \pm 2.5_{\rm sys})\) mb from \(J/\psi+J/\psi\) data.
- ATLAS extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (6.3 \pm 1.6_{\rm stat} \pm 1.0_{\rm sys})\) mb from \(J/\psi+J/\psi\) data.
- From CMS \(J/\psi+J/\psi\) data, Ref. Lansberg (2014) extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (8.2 \pm 2.0_{\rm stat} \pm 2.9_{\rm sys})\) mb.

## Context

This work extends previous NLO NRQCD calculations for \(J/\psi+W\) and \(J/\psi+Z\) by Li (2010), Mao (2011), Gong (2012), and Lansberg (2013), and addresses the discrepancy between those SPS predictions and ATLAS measurements (Aad 2014). The authors use the CEM to set an upper bound on SPS, following earlier work on single-quarkonium production by Lansberg (2006) and Andronic (2015). The extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff}\) values for quarkonium-associated production are smaller than those typically obtained from jet-based measurements, suggesting a possible flavor dependence of DPS. The results enable refined DPS modeling in multi-particle final states at the LHC and motivate further theoretical study of the parton structure of the proton.
