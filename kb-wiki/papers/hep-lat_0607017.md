# The chiral critical line of N_f=2+1 QCD at zero and non-zero baryon density

> We present numerical results for the location of the chiral critical line at finite temperature and zero and non-zero baryon density for QCD with N_f=2+1 flavours of staggered fermions on lattices with temporal extent N_t=4. For degenerate quark masses, we compare our results obtained with the exact RHMC algorithm with earlier, inexact R-algorithm results and find a reduction of 25% in the critical quark mass, for which the first order phase transition changes to a smooth crossover. Extending our analysis to non-degenerate quark masses, we map out the chiral critical line up to the neighbourhood of the physical point, which we confirm to be in the crossover region. Our data are consistent with a tricritical point at a strange quark mass of ~500 MeV. Finally, we investigate the shift of the critical line with finite baryon density, by simulating with an imaginary chemical potential for which there is no sign problem. We observe this shift to be very small or, conversely, the critical endpoint μ^c(m_{u,d},m_s) to be extremely quark mass sensitive. Moreover, the sign of this shift is opposite to standard expectations. If confirmed on a finer lattice, it implies the absence of a critical endpoint or phase transition for chemical potentials μ_B < 500 MeV. We thus argue that finer lattices are required to settle even the qualitative features of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim
The chiral critical line in the (m_u,d, m_s) plane at zero and non-zero baryon density is mapped using exact RHMC simulations on N_t=4 lattices, finding that the critical quark mass for N_f=3 is reduced by 25% compared to previous R-algorithm results, and that the curvature of the critical surface at μ_B=0 is negative, opposite to standard expectations, implying that if confirmed on finer lattices, there is no critical endpoint for physical QCD at chemical potentials μ_B < 500 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2+1 flavours of staggered fermions on N_t=4 lattices using the exact RHMC algorithm (free of step-size errors). They determined the chiral critical line by scanning in the light quark mass at fixed strange quark mass, using the Binder cumulant of the chiral condensate as the observable to locate second-order Z(2) critical points. For finite baryon density, they simulated at imaginary chemical potential (where the sign problem is absent) and analytically continued the results to real μ_B via truncated Taylor series.

## Key numbers
- The N_f=3 critical bare quark mass at μ=0 is am_0^c = 0.0260(5) with RHMC, a 25% reduction from the R-algorithm value am_0^c ≈ 0.033.
- The ratio m_π/T_0 at the N_f=3 critical point changes from 1.853(1) (R-algorithm) to 1.680(3) (RHMC), a 10% reduction in pion mass.
- Finite-size scaling for N_f=3 on L=8,12,16 lattices gives am_0^c = 0.0263(3) and ν = 0.67(13), consistent with 3d Ising universality (ν=0.63).
- The pseudo-critical temperature for N_f=3 varies as T_0(m,μ)/T_0(m_0^c,0) = 1 + 2.111(17) ((m-m_0^c)/(π T_0)) - 0.667(6) (μ/(π T_0))^2 + 0.23(9) (μ/(π T_0))^4.
- The N_f=3 critical mass shift with chemical potential is m^c(μ)/m^c(0) = 1 - 0.7(4) (μ/(π T))^2, indicating a negative curvature.
- The tricritical point (m_u,d=0, m_s) is consistent with m_s ~ 500 MeV from O(4) scaling extrapolation, giving m_s^{tric}/T ~ 2.8.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice determinations of the chiral critical point for N_f=3 by Karsch et al. (2001) and de Forcrand & Philipsen (2002), correcting significant step-size errors from the R-algorithm used in those studies. The finding of negative curvature for the critical surface contradicts the standard expectation (positive curvature) and the results of Fodor & Katz (2004) obtained at the same lattice spacing with the R-algorithm. The results imply that finer lattices (N_t > 4) are required to settle the qualitative features of the QCD phase diagram, and that a critical endpoint for physical QCD may be absent for μ_B < 500 MeV if the negative curvature is confirmed.
