# Effects of symmetry energy and momentum dependent interaction on low-energy reaction mechanisms

> We study the dipole response associated with the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) and the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance (IVGDR), in connection with specific properties of the nuclear effective interaction (symmetry energy and momentum dependence), in the neutron-rich systems $^{68}$Ni, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb. We perform our investigation within a microscopic transport model based on the Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation. We observe that the peak energies of PDR and IVGDR are shifted to higher values when employing momentum dependent interactions, with respect to the results obtained neglecting momentum dependence. The calculated energies are close to the experimental values and similar to the results obtained in Hartree-Fock (HF) with Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations.

## Key claim

The peak energies of both the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) and the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance (IVGDR) are shifted to higher values when momentum-dependent interactions are included in the transport model, bringing them closer to experimental values; however, the PDR peak position does not depend on the symmetry energy parametrization, while the IVGDR peak energy shows a clear dependence on the symmetry energy below saturation density only when momentum dependence is neglected.

## Method

The authors solved the two coupled Landau-Vlasov kinetic equations for neutrons and protons in the semi-classical limit of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock, using a test-particle method with 1500 test particles per nucleon. They employed three Skyrme effective interactions without momentum dependence (asysoft, asystiff, asysuperstiff) and three with momentum dependence (SAMi27, SAMi31, SAMi35), perturbing the ground state by boosting neutrons against protons and following the time evolution of the dipole moment up to 1800 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- For $^{132}$Sn, the IVGDR peak energies shift to higher values when momentum-dependent terms are included, becoming close to experimental values and similar to HF-RPA calculations.
- For $^{208}$Pb, the PDR peak position is around 7.35 MeV (cited from prior experimental work).
- For Sn isotopes, the PDR peak ranges from 8-10 MeV (cited from prior experimental work).
- For $^{68}$Ni, the PDR peak ranges from 9.55-11 MeV (cited from prior experimental work).
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is almost the same for the three EoS without momentum dependence, while for the SAMi interactions the symmetry energy is normalized around 0.65$\rho_0$ (where $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$).
- Saturation properties used: $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, $E/A = -16$ MeV, compressibility modulus $K \approx 200-240$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Coll2 (2013) and Coll21 (2014) that used the same Landau-Vlasov transport model but neglected momentum-dependent effects, finding that calculated peak energies were below experimental values, especially for the IVGDR. The present results are similar to those obtained in Hartree-Fock with Random Phase Approximation calculations by Coll4 (2014). The findings enable systematic studies of dipole response across a wider mass and isospin domain, and the sensitivity of PDR strength to symmetry energy connects to neutron skin thickness, as previously noted by Coll2 (2013).
