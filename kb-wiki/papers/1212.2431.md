# Hadron Formation in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions and the QCD Phase Diagram

> We analyze hadrochemical freeze-out in central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS and LHC energies. We determine the effects of baryon and antibaryon annihilation and/or regeneration occurring during the final cascade expansion stage of the collisions by deriving "survival factors" for each hadronic species and all energies considered by employing the UrQMD hybrid model. These survival factors are shown to resemble the pattern of data deviation from the statistical equilibrium calculations with the statistical model. We apply them in the SM data analysis, obtaining new points in the $T-μ_B$ plane which follow closely the parton-hadron phase boundary predicted by lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard chemical freeze-out curve derived from statistical model fits to hadron yields does not represent the point of latest hadrochemical equilibrium; after correcting for baryon-antibaryon annihilation and regeneration during the hadronic cascade stage using UrQMD survival factors, the reconstructed equilibrium points coincide with the lattice QCD parton-hadron phase boundary, resolving a long-standing discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD hybrid model, which couples a 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic expansion with a Cooper-Frye hadronization at a critical energy density of 0.8 GeV/fm^3, followed by a hadronic cascade afterburner. They computed modification factors (survival factors) as the ratio of final hadron multiplicities after the afterburner to those at hadronization for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6, 8.7, 17.3 GeV) and LHC energy (2.76 TeV). These factors were then applied as corrections in statistical hadronization model (SHM) fits to NA49 and ALICE data to reconstruct the latest hadrochemical equilibrium (LHCE) points in the (T, mu_B) plane.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for 20% central Pb+Pb: standard SHM fit gives T = 156 +/- 5 MeV, mu_B = 1 +/- 12 MeV, chi^2/NDF = 26.5/9; modified fit with survival factors gives T = 166 +/- 3 MeV, mu_B = 2 +/- 6 MeV, chi^2/NDF = 11.5/9.
- At SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV for 5% central Pb+Pb: standard fit gives T = 151 +/- 4 MeV, mu_B = 266 +/- 9 MeV; modified fit gives T = 163 +/- 4 MeV, mu_B = 250 +/- 9 MeV.
- At SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 8.7 GeV: standard fit gives T = 148 +/- 4 MeV, mu_B = 385 +/- 11 MeV; modified fit gives T = 161 +/- 6 MeV, mu_B = 376 +/- 15 MeV.
- At SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6 GeV: standard fit gives T = 140 +/- 1 MeV, mu_B = 437 +/- 5 MeV; modified fit gives T = 156 +/- 5 MeV, mu_B = 426 +/- 4 MeV.
- The lattice QCD phase boundary at mu_B = 0 MeV has T = 165 +/- 8 MeV.
- Antibaryon suppression factors from UrQMD at SPS energies range from 50% to 25% for antiprotons, anti-Lambdas, and anti-Xis.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Becattini 2012) showing that baryon-antibaryon annihilation distorts the initial equilibrium yields, and contradicts the standard assumption that chemical freeze-out coincides with the latest chemical equilibrium point (Cleymans 2005, Andronic 2008). It resolves a discrepancy where the conventional freeze-out curve fell below the lattice QCD phase boundary at higher baryochemical potential (McLerran 2008). The results provide the first confirmation of lattice QCD predictions at finite baryochemical potential (Endrodi 2011, Kaczmarek 2011) and enable future extension to higher mu_B using data from the RHIC beam energy scan program (Mohanty 2011).
