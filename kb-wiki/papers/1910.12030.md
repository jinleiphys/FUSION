# Calculation of asymptotic normalization coefficients in the complex-ranged Gaussian basis

> A new technique towards finding asymptotic normalization coefficients in the complex-ranged Gaussian basis is presented. It is shown that a diagonalisation procedure for the total Hamiltonian matrix in the given basis results in approximation for a radial part of the bound state wave function from the origin up to the far asymptotic distances, which allows to extract ANCs rather accurately. The method is illustrated by calculations of single-particle ANCs for nuclei bound states in cases of non-local nucleon-nucleus interactions, in particular, phenomenological global potentials with the Perey-Buck's non-locality.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix in a complex-ranged Gaussian basis (CRGB) yields accurate asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for bound nuclear states, including cases with non-local nucleon-nucleus interactions, provided the basis dimension is sufficiently large (e.g., K=30). The abstract glosses over the condition that the method's accuracy depends on choosing an adequate basis size and appropriate grid parameters.

## Method
The authors expand bound-state radial wave functions in a basis of real-valued functions formed from complex-scale Gaussians with oscillating factors, using a fixed parameter b=pi/4 and a Tchebyshev grid for the scale parameters alpha_n. They solve the generalized eigenvalue problem for the total Hamiltonian matrix in this basis to obtain bound-state energies and wave functions, then extract ANCs by matching the asymptotic wave function to the Whittaker function at large distances. For non-local interactions, they employ the Perey-Buck type non-locality with a Gaussian form factor and compute the required matrix elements numerically.

## Key numbers
- For the p+12C system (1p1/2 ground state), the ratio of the wave function to the Whittaker function approaches a constant value at r_m > 5 fm with basis dimension K=30 being sufficient for accurate results.
- For the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction: local ANC = 0.70 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 0.15 MeV); nonlocal ANC = 0.77 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 0.14 MeV) using non-locality parameter beta=0.85 fm.
- For the 7Li(n,gamma)8Li reaction: local ANC = 0.76 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 2.03 MeV); nonlocal ANC = 0.82 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 2.03 MeV).
- For the 12C(p,gamma)13N reaction: local ANC = 2.05 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 1.94 MeV); nonlocal ANC = 2.28 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 1.94 MeV).
- For the 12C(n,gamma)13C reaction: local ANC = 1.85 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 4.95 MeV); nonlocal ANC = 2.08 fm^{-1/2} (binding energy 4.95 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the complex-ranged Gaussian basis method, previously applied to bound-state calculations by Hiyama (2012) and scattering calculations by Rubtsova et al. (2018), to the extraction of asymptotic normalization coefficients. The results for local potentials agree with earlier calculations by Bertulani (cited in the paper), while the nonlocal results confirm the Perey effect (Austern, cited) and are consistent with calculations by Tian et al. (2018). The method enables future calculations of ANCs for few-body systems using a few-body complex-ranged Gaussian basis, which is relevant for nuclear astrophysics reactions such as radiative capture.
