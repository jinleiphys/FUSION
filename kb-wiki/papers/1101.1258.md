# Extraction of neutron structure from tagged structure functions

> We present work in a model used to describe semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off the deuteron. The model uses the virtual nucleon approximation to describe the interaction of the photon with the bound neutron and the generalized eikonal approximation is applied to calculate the final-state interaction diagram. Comparison with data taken at Jefferson Lab shows good agreement in the covered range of kinematics and points at a largely suppressed off-shell rescattering amplitude. The $W$ and $Q^2$ dependences of the total cross section and slope factor of the interaction of DIS products, $X$, off the spectator nucleon are extracted. Starting from the JLab data and our model calculations, we outline and apply an extrapolation method to obtain the neutron structure function $F_{2N}$ at high Bjorken $x$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off the deuteron that, when compared to Jefferson Lab data, points to a largely suppressed off-shell rescattering amplitude; the authors outline an extrapolation method to obtain the neutron structure function \(F_{2N}\) at high Bjorken \(x\) but note that with the current data set the method does not provide a robust prediction, as trajectories with different spectator angles yield a range of values.

## Method

The model uses the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) to describe the photon interaction with the bound neutron, taking the spectator proton on-shell while the interacting nucleon is off-shell. The generalized eikonal approximation is applied to calculate the final-state interaction (FSI) diagram, assuming diffractive rescattering over small angles with an amplitude parametrized by an effective total cross section \(\sigma\), a real part \(\epsilon\), and a slope factor \(B\). The authors fit these parameters to data from the JLab Deeps experiment for each \(Q^2\) and \(W\) bin, and then apply an extrapolation procedure analogous to the Chew-Low method, using a quadratic fit in \(t' = m_n^2 - p_i^2\) to extract \(F_{2N}\) at high \(x\).

## Key numbers

- The spectator proton momentum is limited to \(p_s \leq 700\) MeV/c to satisfy the VNA assumptions (i) and (ii).
- The factorization approximation used in deriving the cross section is valid up to spectator momenta around 400 MeV/c.
- The ratio of longitudinal to transverse cross section for scattering off the nucleon is fixed at \(R = \sigma_L / \sigma_T \approx 0.18\).
- The fitted total cross section \(\sigma\) drops to around 25 mb after an initial peak at \(W = 1.2\) GeV (corresponding to \(\Delta\) creation) and then rises with increasing \(W\).
- The extrapolation example targets \(F_{2N}\) at Bjorken \(x = 0.7\) using a quadratic fit to data points along a trajectory in \(t'\) and \(\hat{x}\).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical approaches to the \(d(e,e'p_s)X\) reaction, including those by Simula (1996), Melnitchouk (1996), Sargsian (2005), and Ciofi degli Atti (1999, 2002, 2003), by applying a model based on general properties of soft rescattering as described by Cosyn (2010). The method enables extraction of the neutron structure function at high Bjorken \(x\) from low-\(x\) kinematics, analogous to the Chew-Low procedure used for \(\pi\pi\) and \(NN\) cross sections (Chew, 1958). The results suggest a possible onset of color transparency at higher \(Q^2\), but more data points at other \(Q^2\) and \(W\) values are needed to make substantial claims.
