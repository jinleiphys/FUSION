# Line shape of ψ(3770) in e^+e^-\to D\bar D

> Interference phenomena observed in the $ψ(3770)$ resonance region in the reactions $e^+e^-$ $\to$ $ D\bar D$ are analyzed. To avoid ambiguities in the determination of the $ψ(3770)$ resonance parameters, when analyzing data between the $D\bar D$ and $D\bar Dπ$ thresholds, the amplitudes satisfying the elastic unitarity requirement should be used. In the lack of information on the $P$ wave of $D\bar D$ elastic scattering, the $ψ(3770)$ parameters, determined by fitting the $e^+e^-$ $\to $ $D\bar D$ data, can essentially depend on the model used for the total contribution of the resonance and background. The selection of the models can be toughened by comparing their predictions with the relevant data on the shape of the $ψ(3770)$ peak in the non-$D\bar D$ channels $e^+e^-$ $\to $ $γχ_{c0}$, $J/ψη$, $ϕη$, etc.

# Line shape of ψ(3770) in e⁺e⁻ → D D̅

## Key claim

The ψ(3770) resonance parameters extracted from e⁺e⁻ → D D̅ data depend strongly on the model used for the sum of resonance and background contributions because information on the P-wave D D̅ elastic scattering amplitude is unavailable; models that satisfy elastic unitarity can describe the data but yield very different resonance parameters, and the selection between models can be tightened by comparing predictions for non-D D̅ channels such as e⁺e⁻ → γχc0, J/ψη, and φη.

## Method

The authors constructed models for the isoscalar part of the D meson electromagnetic form factor F⁰D that satisfy elastic unitarity in the region between the D D̅ and D D̅π thresholds. They fitted these models to the combined e⁺e⁻ → D D̅ cross section data from BES, CLEO, BABAR, and Belle (87 data points between 3.72 and 3.9 GeV), using a resonance-plus-background model and a model including both ψ(3770) and ψ(2S) resonances with their mutual mixing through D D̅ intermediate states. The resulting form factors were then used to predict cross sections for e⁺e⁻ → γχc0, J/ψη, and φη at a single energy point for comparison with CLEO measurements.

## Key numbers

- In the resonance-plus-background model fit: χ²/n.d.o.f. ≈ 123/82 ≈ 1.5; the visible peak maximum is at √s ≈ 3.773 GeV with σmax ≈ 9.13 nb, full width at half maximum Γhmax ≈ 29.7 MeV, and effective electron width Γeffe⁺e⁻ ≈ 0.263 keV.
- In the resonance-plus-background model: the fitted bare ψ(3770) mass mψ″ = 3.799 GeV; the renormalized mass Mψ″ = 3.784 GeV, renormalized D D̅ width Γrenψ″D D̅ = 37.61 MeV, and renormalized electron width Γrenψ″e⁺e⁻ = 0.05181 keV with renormalization factor Zψ″ = 1.748.
- In the model with ψ(3770) and ψ(2S): χ²/n.d.o.f. ≈ 125/81 ≈ 1.54; the fitted bare mass mψ″ = 3.784 GeV; the renormalized mass Mψ″ = 3.789 GeV, renormalized D D̅ width Γrenψ″D D̅ = 58.03 MeV, and renormalized electron width Γrenψ″e⁺e⁻ = 0.2973 keV with Zψ″ = 0.6905.
- In the ψ(2S)+ψ″ model: the form factor zero occurs at √s ≈ 3.816 GeV; the extracted coupling g²ψ(2S)D D̅/(4π) ≈ 13.
- CLEO measurements at √s = 3773 MeV: σ(e⁺e⁻ → γχc0) = 72 ± 9 pb, σ(e⁺e⁻ → J/ψη) = 8.6 ± 3.9 pb, σ(e⁺e⁻ → φη) = 3.1 ± 0.8 pb.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by the KEDR Collaboration (2011-2012) who found two very different solutions for interfering resonance and background amplitudes that are indistinguishable by χ², and by BES, CLEO, BABAR, and Belle who observed an unusual ψ(3770) peak shape with a deep dip near 3.81 GeV. The authors build on the coupled-channel model of Eichten, Gottfried, Kinoshita, Lane, and Yan (1978) which predicted a zero in the form factor. The analysis enables future studies using high-statistics data from CLEO-c (818 pb⁻¹) and BES III (2.9 fb⁻¹) to discriminate between models, and suggests that measurements of ψ(3770) in non-D D̅ channels and in weak B decays or photoproduction can further constrain the resonance parameters.
