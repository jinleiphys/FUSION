# Confining Effective Theories Based on Instantons and Merons

> An effective theory based on ensembles of either regular gauge instantons or merons is shown to produce confinement in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory. When the scale is set by the string tension, the action density, topological susceptibility and low-lying glueball spectrum are similar to those arising in lattice QCD. The physical mechanism producing confinement is explained, and a number of analytical insights into the effective theory are presented.

# Confining Effective Theories Based on Instantons and Merons

## Key claim

An effective theory based on ensembles of regular gauge instantons or merons, with fields falling off as 1/x rather than 1/x^3, produces confinement in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory when the collective coordinates are dynamically correlated via Metropolis sampling, in contrast to earlier dilute instanton gas models that failed to confine. The physical mechanism is that color correlations required to superpose the long-range pseudoparticle fields into low-action configurations also produce the correlations necessary for an area law in large Wilson loops.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective theory by writing the partition function as a path integral over the positions and color orientations of ensembles of regular gauge instantons or merons (collectively called pseudoparticles) in a 4-volume V=16. They generated field configurations by superposing pseudoparticle gauge fields and sampled the collective coordinates using the Metropolis algorithm, with the standard Euclidean action as the weight. The gauge fields for each pseudoparticle in Lorentz gauge are given by a_mu(x) = xi * (eta_{a mu nu} x_nu)/(x^2 + rho^2) * sigma^a/2, with xi=1 for merons and xi=2 for instantons, and the standard choice for meron size and coupling was rho=0.16 and g^2=32.

## Key numbers

- Action density of the stochastic meron ensemble at the center of the cube: g^2 <s(x=0)> = 2.6 x 10^5, correctly predicted by the analytical expression g^2 <s(x=0)> = 4.5 N_M + 1.03 N_M(N_M-1) for N_M=500 merons.
- Dynamical correlations decreased the action density at the center from 261,000 to 1,700, a reduction of two orders of magnitude.
- For the stochastic meron ensemble, the Wilson loop fit parameters were omega = -2.1, tau = 2.3, sigma = 22.9; for the ensemble with dynamical color orientations, omega = -0.7, tau = 1.1, sigma = 11.8.
- The background contribution to the action density was 55-65% for dynamically correlated instanton ensembles and 65-75% for dynamically correlated meron ensembles.

## Context

This work extends the long history of seeking to understand nonperturbative QCD analytically by expanding around classical solutions, beginning with instantons (Belavin 1975) and merons (deAlfaro 1976). Earlier dilute instanton gas calculations (Callan 1978) showed that singular gauge instantons do not produce confinement, and meron ensembles had remained analytically intractable due to their 1/x behavior. The present work builds on previous instanton liquid models (Shuryak 1981, Schafer 1996, Diakonov 1983) that successfully described chiral symmetry breaking but did not include confinement, and on the authors' prior meron ensemble study (Lenz, Negele, Thies 2004). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations of the action density, topological susceptibility, and glueball spectrum, and are related to a finite-temperature pseudoparticle effective theory (Wagner 2006) that observed a crossover in the Polyakov loop.
