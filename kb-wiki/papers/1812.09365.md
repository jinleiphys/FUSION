# Baryonic susceptibilities, quark-diquark models, and quark-hadron duality at finite temperature

> Fluctuations of conserved charges such as baryon number, electric charge and strangeness may provide a test for completeness of states in lattice QCD for three light flavors. We elaborate on the idea that the corresponding susceptibilities can be saturated with excited baryonic states with an underlying quark-diquark structure with a linearly confining interaction. Using Polyakov-loop correlators we show that in the static limit, the quark-diquark potential coincides with the quark-antiquark potential in marked agreement with recent lattice studies. We thus study in a quark-diquark model the baryonic fluctuations of electric charge, baryon number and strangeness: $χ_{BQ}$, $χ_{BB}$ and $χ_{BS}$; by considering a realization of the hadron resonance gas model in the light flavor sector of QCD. These results have been obtained by using the baryon spectrum computed within a relativistic quark-diquark model, leading to an overall good agreement with the spectrum obtained with other quark models and with lattice data for the fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryonic susceptibilities at finite temperature can be saturated by excited baryonic states described by a quark-diquark structure with a linearly confining interaction, and that the quark-diquark potential coincides with the quark-antiquark potential in the static limit, but this agreement is conditional on the diquark being in the color-antitriplet representation and on the neglect of the sextet channel.

## Method

The authors compute baryonic susceptibilities (χ_BB, χ_BQ, χ_BS) within a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model using the baryon spectrum obtained from a relativistic quark-diquark model with a linearly confining potential. They derive the quark-diquark potential analytically from Polyakov-loop correlators in the static limit, showing it matches the quark-antiquark potential via the Clebsch-Gordan decomposition of 3⊗3 = 3̄⊕6. The spectrum is then computed by diagonalization of the two-body relativistic Hamiltonian, and the resulting susceptibilities are compared to lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2011) and Bazavov (2012).

## Key numbers

- The proton mass M_p = 938 MeV sets the low-temperature asymptotic behavior for χ_BB and χ_BQ, while the Λ^0 mass sets the asymptotic for χ_BS.
- The diquark mass difference between scalar and axial-vector diquarks is about 200 MeV, with scalar diquark mass approximately 600 MeV and axial-vector diquark mass approximately 800 MeV.
- The cumulative number of baryonic states scales as M^6 to M^8, significantly lower than the expected M^12 from a three-body system, suggesting effective two-body behavior.
- The high-temperature limits of susceptibilities are χ_BB → 1/3, χ_BQ → 0, and χ_BS → -1/3 for N_c=3 and flavors u,d,s.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic approach to QCD completeness pioneered by Hagedorn (1965, 1984), where the hadron resonance gas model represents the vacuum as non-interacting hadrons. It addresses the missing resonance problem highlighted by Capstick (1992) and the discrepancy between PDG states and quark model predictions for baryonic susceptibilities observed by Ruiz Arriola (2016). The quark-diquark model builds on relativistic analyses by Ferretti (2011) and Gutierrez (2014), and the Polyakov-loop derivation of the potential connects to lattice studies by Koma (2017) showing identical string tensions for quark-diquark and quark-antiquark systems. The results enable a global test of quark-hadron duality at finite temperature, complementing the deep inelastic scattering analysis by Masjuan (2017) and Ruiz Arriola (2017).
