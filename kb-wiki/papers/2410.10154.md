# Pentaquarks $P_c$ in a dynamical coupled-channel approach of $γp \to J/ψp$ reaction

> We construct a dynamical coupled-channel approach to photon-induced $J/ψ$ production to search for the trace of the pentaquarks $P_c$. In this approach, the considered open-charm intermediate states are $Λ_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $Σ_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$. Scattering amplitude involving $Λ_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$, $Σ_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $J/ψp$ interactions is calculated from solving a set of coupled-channel integral equations. The coupled-channel interaction potentials are generated from one-boson-exchange. The pentaquarks $P_c$ are dynamically generated from the coupled-channel interactions. Moreover, the background contributions to the $P_c$ signals are assumed to be the Pomeron exchange and $σ$ exchange. The predicted total and differential cross sections of $J/ψ$ photoproduction are within the range of the data from the GlueX and $J/ψ$-$007$ experiments. It is found that the $σ$ exchange plays a crucial role for determining the very near threshold cross section. The total cross section gets enhanced due to the open-charm state rescattering in the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ production regions, and is of order 1 nb.

## Key claim

The pentaquarks $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ are dynamically generated from coupled-channel interactions of $\Lambda_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$ states via one-boson-exchange potentials, and their contributions to $J/\psi$ photoproduction are calculated; the predicted cross sections are within the range of GlueX and $J/\psi$-$007$ data, but the $\sigma$ exchange plays a crucial role for determining the very near threshold cross section, and the total cross section enhancement from open-charm rescattering is of order 1 nb.

## Method

A dynamical coupled-channel approach was constructed for the $\gamma p \to J/\psi p$ reaction, solving the Blankenbecler-Sugar (BS) integral equation for scattering amplitudes involving $\Lambda_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$, $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $J/\psi p$ channels. The coupled-channel interaction potentials were generated from one-boson-exchange (including $\pi$, $\eta$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, $\omega$ exchanges for open-charm channels and $D$, $D^*$ exchanges for transitions to $J/\psi p$), while background contributions were modeled as Pomeron exchange and $\sigma$ exchange. Only $S$-wave coupled-channel interactions were considered, and the static approximation was used to handle singularities in the pion-exchange potentials.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section from open-charm rescattering in the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ production regions is of order 1 nb.
- Pomeron parameters: $\mu_0 = 1.1$ GeV$^2$, $\beta_c = 0.32$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\alpha'_P = 0.25$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\alpha_0 = 1.25$, determined by fitting $\gamma p \to J/\psi p$ total cross section data from threshold up to 300 GeV.
- $\sigma$ meson coupling: $g_{J/\psi \sigma \gamma} = 5.5 \times 10^{-3}$, derived from ${\rm Br}(J/\psi \to \sigma \gamma) = 1.14 \times 10^{-3}$ with $m_\sigma = 0.6$ GeV; $g_{NN\sigma} = 13.85$.
- $J/\psi$ decay constant: $f_{J/\psi} = 11.2$, determined from $\Gamma(J/\psi \to e^+ e^-)$.
- Coupling constants for $D^{(*)}N\Lambda_c$: $g_{DN\Lambda_c} = -13.24$, $g_{D^*N\Lambda_c} = -4.26$; for $D^{(*)}N\Sigma_c$: $g_{DN\Sigma_c} = 3.58$, $g_{D^*N\Sigma_c} = -2.46$, $\kappa_{D^*N\Sigma_c} = -0.47$, $\kappa_{D^*N\Lambda_c} = 2.66$.
- Photon-$D D^*$ couplings: $g_{D^{*\pm} D^{\pm} \gamma} = 0.141$ GeV$^{-1}$, $g_{D^{*0} D^{0} \gamma} = -0.558$ GeV$^{-1}$, from $\Gamma_{D^{*\pm}\to D^{\pm}\gamma}=1.33$ keV and $\Gamma_{D^{*0}\to D^{0}\gamma}=21.24$ keV.

## Context

This work extends dynamical coupled-channel approaches previously applied to open-charm systems (Du 2019, Du 2021) by including photon-induced $J/\psi$ production and comparing with GlueX (2019, 2023) and $J/\psi$-$007$ (Duran 2022) experimental data. It builds on the interpretation of $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ as $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^{(*)}$ molecular states (Liu 2019, Fernandez-Ramirez 2019, Du 2019) and on earlier Pomeron-exchange models for $J/\psi$ photoproduction (Donnachie 1984, Laget 1994, Oh 2000). The results enable future searches for pentaquark signatures in photoproduction experiments and provide a framework for extracting the $\sigma$ exchange contribution near threshold.
