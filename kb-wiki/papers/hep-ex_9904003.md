# Search for Supernarrow Dibaryons in pd Interactions

> The reaction pd-->pX at 305 MeV is studied with the aim to search for supernarrow dibaryons, the decay of which into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle. The experiment was carried out at the Moscow Meson Factory using the spectrometer TAMS, which detected the scattered proton in coincidence with another charged particle (either p or d) from the decay of particle X. The detection of the second charged particle at angles close to the emission angle of particle X with mass M allows to suppress essentially the contribution of background processes and to increase the relative contribution of a possible production of the dibaryons under consideration. Narrow peaks in missing mass spectra have been observed at 1905 and 1924 MeV with the statistical significance of ~4.8 SD. The widths of both observed peaks correspond to the experimental resolution (3 MeV). Comparison of the obtained data with theoretical predictions leads to the conclusion that the peak found at 1905 MeV most likely corresponds to a supernarrow dibaryon with the isotopic spin equal to 1. The possible origin of the peak at 1924 MeV is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports the observation of two narrow peaks in missing mass spectra at 1905 and 1924 MeV in the reaction pd → pX at 305 MeV, but the authors hedge that only the 1905 MeV peak is interpreted as a supernarrow dibaryon with isospin 1, while the 1924 MeV peak is discussed only as a possible candidate and was observed in only one kinematic configuration near the upper limit of the measurement range.

## Method

The experiment used the TAMS spectrometer at the Moscow Meson Factory to detect scattered protons from pd interactions at 305 MeV in coincidence with a second charged particle (proton or deuteron) from the decay of particle X. The left arm detected the scattered proton at fixed angles of 70° or 72.5°, while the right arm consisted of three telescopes at angles between 31° and 39° to detect decay products. Missing mass spectra were obtained by subtracting carbon target contributions from CD₂ target data, and the resulting spectra were fitted with second-order polynomials for background plus Gaussians for the observed peaks.

## Key numbers

- Narrow peaks observed at 1905 ± 2 MeV and 1924 ± 2 MeV in missing mass spectra
- Statistical significance of the 1905 MeV peak: 4.8 standard deviations (65 events above background out of 185 total events in the peak region)
- Statistical significance of the 1924 MeV peak: 4.9 standard deviations using the same method
- Widths of both observed peaks correspond to the experimental resolution of 3 MeV
- Differential cross section for the isovector dibaryon production at 1905 MeV: 8 ± 4 μb/sr
- For the D(1,1⁺) interpretation: η g₁²/4π = (0.44 ± 0.22) × 10⁻²
- For the D(1,1⁻) interpretation: η g₂²/4π = (0.2 ± 0.1) × 10⁻²
- Proton beam energy: 305 MeV with average effective intensity ~0.1 nA
- CD₂ target thickness: 0.14 g/cm²; ¹²C target thickness: 0.18 g/cm²
- Total exposition time with CD₂ target: 100 hours (two runs of 55 and 45 hours)

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by the same group (Konobeevski et al., 1996) that observed a narrow peak at 1905 MeV with 7 MeV resolution, now improved to 3 MeV resolution with better statistics and angular correlation measurements. The search for supernarrow dibaryons was motivated by theoretical predictions from the MIT bag model (Mulders et al., 1980) and the stretched rotating bag model (Kondratyuk et al., 1987), as well as earlier experimental claims of a dibaryon at ~1920 MeV from photon measurements (Khrykin, 1997) that had been disputed by upper limits from bremsstrahlung data (Calen et al., 1998). The results enable future searches for these states in charged pion photoproduction from the deuteron using polarized photons to determine the spin-parity quantum numbers of the observed state.
