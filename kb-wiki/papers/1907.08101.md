# What attracts to attractors?

> Whether, how, and to what extent solutions of Bjorken-expanding systems become insensitive to aspects of their initial conditions is of importance for heavy-ion collisions. Here we study 1+1D and phenomenologically relevant boost-invariant 3+1D systems in which initial conditions approach a universal attractor solution. In Israel-Stewart theory (IS) and kinetic theory where the universal attractor extends to arbitrarily early times, we show that all initial conditions approach the attractor at early times by a power-law while their approach is exponential at late times. In these theories, the physical mechanisms of hydrodynamization operational at late times do not drive the approach to the attractor at early times, and the early-time attractor is reached prior to hydrodynamization. In marked contrast, the attractor in strongly coupled systems is realized concurrent with hydrodynamization. This qualitative difference may offer a basis for discriminating weakly and strongly coupled scenarios of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

In Israel-Stewart theory and kinetic theory, the universal attractor extends to arbitrarily early times and initial conditions approach it via a power-law decay driven by the expansion rate, not by the interaction rate; this early-time attractor is reached prior to hydrodynamization. In marked contrast, the attractor in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super Yang-Mills theory is realized only concurrent with hydrodynamization, with information about initial conditions lost only at times \(t \sim 1\).

## Method

The authors study Bjorken-expanding systems in 1+1D and boost-invariant 3+1D using three frameworks: Israel-Stewart (IS) theory with a conformal equation of state and constant specific viscosity; massless kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation (RTA); and strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super Yang-Mills theory via holographic solutions of 5-dimensional Einstein equations. For each framework, they evolve multiple initial conditions from different initialization times \(t_0\) and analyze the transient dynamics of the ratio \(x = p_L/\varepsilon\) as it approaches the attractor solution, distinguishing between early-time power-law and late-time exponential approaches.

## Key numbers

- In IS theory with \(\eta = 0\) (ideal IS), small deviations from the attractor decay exponentially as \(\delta \sim e^{-3 t /2}\) at late times.
- In IS theory with finite \(a\), the early-time power-law approach to the attractor scales as \(\delta \sim t^{-\frac{8 \sqrt{5a }}{\sqrt{5a}+10}}\).
- In kinetic theory (RTA), the early-time power-law decay of \(\delta = x - x_A\) scales as \(\delta \sim t^{-8/3}\) for generic initial conditions, and higher moments approach their attractor as \(p_l(t)/\varepsilon(t) \propto x(t)^l\) with \(y \sim t^{-16/3}\).
- In \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM, different initial conditions do not reach a unique curve on time scale \(t_0\); convergence to a common attractor occurs only at times \(t \sim 1\).
- For 3+1D kinetic theory, the opacity parameter \(\hat\gamma = \gamma R^{3/4} \left( \varepsilon_0\, \tau_0 \right)^{1/4}\) characterizes the system, with estimated ranges: \(\hat\gamma \lesssim 2\) for proton-nucleus collisions, \(2 \lesssim \hat\gamma \lesssim 4\) for semi-peripheral PbPb collisions, and higher values for central PbPb collisions.

## Context

This work extends studies of attractor solutions in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior analyses of fluid-dynamic behavior onset by Janik (2006), Heller (2011, 2013, 2015), and Romatschke (2017), as well as kinetic theory attractor studies by Kurkela (2015) and Strickland (2019). It contrasts the early-time attractor behavior in Israel-Stewart theory and kinetic theory with the late-time-only attractor in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM, as previously studied by Romatschke (2017) and Spalinski (2018). The qualitative difference in early-time dynamics may enable discrimination between weakly and strongly coupled scenarios of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.
