# Cumulants of net-strangeness multiplicity distributions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> The higher-order cumulants of net-proton number, net-charge, and net-strangeness multiplicity distributions are widely studied to search for the quantum-chromodynamics critical point and extract the chemical freeze-out parameters in heavy-ion collisions. In this context, the event-by-event fluctuations of the net-strangeness multiplicity distributions play important roles in extracting the chemical freeze-out parameter in the strangeness sector. Due to having difficulties in detecting all strange hadrons event by event, the kaon ($K$) and lambda ($Λ$) particles serve as a proxy for the strangeness-related observables in heavy-ion collisions. We have studied the net-$K$, net-$Λ$, and net-($K$ + $Λ$) multiplicity distributions and calculated their different order of cumulants using the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics model and hadron resonance gas calculation. To adequately account for the net-strangeness cumulants, it has been found that the inclusion of resonance decay contributions in $K$ and $Λ$ is necessary.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the combined net-(K+Lambda) cumulant ratios C1/C2 and C3/C2 become negative at lower collision energies (below 39 GeV) in the UrQMD model, and that this behavior can only be reproduced in hadron resonance gas calculations when resonance decay contributions to kaon and Lambda yields are included.

## Method

The authors simulated Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 to 200 GeV using the UrQMD transport model, analyzing net-kaon, net-Lambda, and net-(K+Lambda) multiplicity distributions within transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 1.6 GeV/c and rapidity |y| < 0.5. They compared these results with hadron resonance gas calculations using chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from fits to STAR particle yields, both with and without including decay feed-down from higher mass resonances.

## Key numbers

- C1/C2 of net-(K+Lambda) is negative at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV, becoming positive only at 200 GeV in UrQMD
- C4/C2 of net-(K+Lambda) remains near unity across all energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV in both UrQMD and HRG
- The ratio of net-(K+Lambda) mean to total (K+Lambda) yield increases with decreasing beam energy when decay contributions are excluded, but agrees with STAR data only when decays are included
- HRG calculations use freeze-out parameters mu_B/T, mu_S/T, and mu_Q/T extracted from fits to STAR yields of pi, K, p, Lambda, Xi, and Omega

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of net-kaon cumulants (STAR 2017) and net-Lambda cumulants (STAR 2020) by proposing the combined net-(K+Lambda) observable as a more complete proxy for net-strangeness. The study provides a baseline for ongoing STAR Beam Energy Scan measurements and demonstrates that proper treatment of resonance decay feed-down from higher mass states (such as Sigma(1385), Lambda(1405), Lambda(1520), K*(892), and phi(1020)) is necessary to describe the energy dependence of net-strangeness cumulants, particularly at lower collision energies where hyperon production dominates.
