# Weinberg and few-nucleon forces

> Weinberg's contributions to the power counting and derivation of few-nucleon forces in Chiral EFT are briefly recalled. Subsequent improvements are reviewed, concluding with the recent suggestion of a combinatorial enhancement.

# Weinberg and few-nucleon forces

## Key claim
Weinberg's power counting for few-nucleon forces in Chiral EFT may require modification for systems with many nucleons (A > 4), where combinatorial enhancement from the larger number of possible few-body interactions could make three- and four-body forces comparable to two-body forces at leading order, despite being subleading for light nuclei.

## Method
The paper reviews the historical development of Weinberg's power counting for few-nucleon forces in Chiral EFT, including the original derivation of the potential as a sum of irreducible subdiagrams, the cancellation of pion-in-the-air diagrams that pushed three-body forces to higher order, and subsequent refinements accounting for the Delta isobar, proper loop factors (Friar's counting), and renormalization group consistency. It then presents a recent proposal by Yang et al. (2021) that the ordering of few-nucleon forces depends on the number of nucleons through combinatorial factors.

## Key numbers
- The nonperturbative QCD scale is M_QCD ~ 1 GeV, with nucleon mass m_N ~ 940 MeV
- Pion mass m_pi ~ 140 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi ~ 92 MeV
- Delta-nucleon mass splitting Delta ~ 300 MeV
- In Weinberg's original counting, the first three-nucleon force appears at relative O(Q^3/M_QCD^3), with four-body forces at O(Q^4/M_QCD^4)
- With an explicit Delta field, the leading three-nucleon force comes at relative O(Q^2/M_QCD^2)
- With Friar's counting and explicit Delta, the three-body force first appears at NLO, relative O(Q/M_QCD)
- Nuclear matter saturation density rho_0 ~ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- With Friar's counting and Q ~ 3f_pi, three-nucleon forces become comparable to two-nucleon forces for A ~ 20, four-body forces at A ~ 25
- Binding energy per nucleon B_A/A ~ 10 MeV is naturally generated at LO

## Context
This review extends Weinberg's foundational work (Weinberg 1990, 1991) on Chiral EFT for nuclear forces, addressing consistency issues that have emerged over three decades. It contrasts Weinberg's original power counting with subsequent improvements by Friar (1996) on loop factors, by van Kolck (1994) on the Delta isobar, and by Kaplan, Nogga, and Pavon Valderrama on renormalization group consistency. The paper enables future work on whether combinatorial enhancement of few-body forces resolves the instability of nuclei beyond A=4 at leading order, as observed by Yang (2020), and whether an "improved action" approach similar to lattice QCD can justify resumming three-body forces into LO for larger nuclei.
