# Baryon-Number Nonconservation and the Stability of Strange Matter

> If baryon-number nonconservation exists in the form of an effective six-quark operator which also changes strangeness by four units, it could have a tremendous impact on the absolute stability of strange quark matter. We show that such an operator is negligible in the supersymmetric standard model with $λ_{ijk} u_i^c d_j^c d_k^c$ terms in the superpotential, but may be of importance in models with exotic particle content. From the experimental lower limit of $10^{25}$ years on the stability of nuclei, we find a model-independent lower limit of the order $10^5$ years on the stability of strange matter against such decays.

# Baryon-Number Nonconservation and the Stability of Strange Matter

## Key claim

If baryon-number nonconservation exists via an effective six-quark operator with selection rule ΔB = 2 and ΔN_s = 4 (changing strangeness by four units), strange quark matter could decay, but the lifetime is constrained to be longer than 10^5 years by the experimental lower limit of 1.6 × 10^25 years on nuclear stability. This operator is negligible in the supersymmetric standard model with R-parity violating λ_{ijk} u_i^c d_j^c d_k^c terms, but may be significant in models with exotic particle content such as E_6-inspired extensions.

## Method

The authors consider an effective (uss)^2 operator with ΔB = 2 and ΔN_s = 4, which allows strangelets (stable or metastable quark configurations with large strangeness content) to decay into smaller strangelets plus kaons and pions. They evaluate contributions from the supersymmetric standard model with R-parity violating terms via box diagrams involving s s → \tilde b \tilde b exchange, and from extended models including an E_6-inspired model with exotic h and h^c fields and a model with a color-sextet scalar and color-octet fermion. They derive a phenomenological lower limit on strange matter lifetime by comparing the phase space for A → (A-2) + K K K π decays (which involve the (uss)^2 operator supplemented by weak transitions) against the known nuclear stability bound.

## Key numbers

- Experimental lower limit on nuclear stability (mode-independent): 1.6 × 10^25 years
- Model-independent lower limit on strange matter lifetime against ΔB = 2, ΔN_s = 4 decays: > 10^5 years
- In the supersymmetric standard model with λ_{usb} < 1 and \tilde m > 200 GeV, estimated strangelet lifetime: > 10^22 years
- In the E_6-inspired model with M = 1 TeV, estimated strangelet lifetime: ~ 2 × 10^7 years
- Suppression factor ξ for A → (A-2) + K K K π relative to A → (A-2) + K K: ~ 10^{-20}, using f_K = 160 MeV, T_{Kπ} = 0.07 MeV^2, and \tilde Λ = 0.3 GeV
- For the (uds)^2 operator (ΔB = 2, ΔN_s = 2), the lower bound on the effective mass scale M from neutron-antineutron oscillation: > 2.4 × 10^5 GeV
- Ground-state mass difference m_0(A) - m_0(A-2) for strangelets: approximately (1704 + 111 A^{-1/3} + 161 A^{-2/3}) MeV, assuming m_s = 100 MeV and bag factor B^{1/4} = 145 MeV

## Context

This work extends the discussion of baryon-number nonconservation in strange matter beyond the usual ΔB = 1 proton decay and ΔB = 2 neutron-antineutron oscillation (which were constrained by experiments such as those of the IMB and Super-Kamiokande collaborations). The authors build on earlier proposals by Witten (1984) and Farhi and Jaffe (1984) regarding absolutely stable strange quark matter, and on calculations by Madsen (1994) of strangelet mass formulas. The paper shows that while the supersymmetric standard model with R-parity violation cannot produce a significant (uss)^2 operator, models with exotic particles at the TeV scale could, and that the resulting strange matter lifetime bound of 10^5 years is relevant for searches for stable strangelets in cosmic rays and for understanding whether bulk strange matter could survive from the early Universe. The work also suggests that strange matter could be a long-lived emitter of kaons and pions, and that future colliders such as the LHC might test the existence of the mediating particles.
