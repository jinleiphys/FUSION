# Fluctuations and Entropy Indices of QCD Parton Showers

> The branching processes in parton showers are studied in perturbative QCD for both quark and gluon jets. The emphasis is on the nature of fluctuations of both the parton multiplicities and the spatial patterns of the final states. Effective measures of such fluctuations are calculated from the data obtained by Monte Carlo simulations. The entropy indices are used to characterize chaoticity. Both running and fixed couplings are considered. The fixed coupling case is used to study the onset of chaos. Implications of the results are discussed.

# Fluctuations and Entropy Indices of QCD Parton Showers

## Key claim

The authors find that QCD parton showers exhibit chaotic behavior as measured by entropy indices and multiplicity fluctuations, but contrary to expectations, decreasing the coupling strength (alpha_s) does not suppress chaos; instead, parton showers become more chaotic at very low alpha_s because the nonlinearity cannot be turned off without eliminating the branching mechanism itself.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of perturbative QCD parton showers using Odorico's algorithm, including both gluon and quark branching processes with splitting functions P_{G->GG}, P_{G->q qbar}, and P_{q->qG}. For the running coupling case, they used alpha_s(q^2) with N_f=3, N_c=3, and Lambda=250 MeV; for the fixed coupling case, they varied alpha_s from 0.05 to 0.3 at fixed Q/Q_0=10^3. They computed normalized multiplicity variances V_i as a function of average multiplicity <n_i> to extract the exponent kappa, and calculated factorial moments C_{p,q}(M) in cumulative X-space to extract entropy indices mu_q.

## Key numbers

- For Q jet at Q/Q_0=10^3: kappa = 0.62 for 10 < <n_i> < 50
- For G jet at Q/Q_0=10^3: kappa = 0.29 for 20 < <n_i> < 70
- QCD scale Lambda = 250 MeV
- Branching cutoff Q_0^2 = 1 GeV^2
- Fixed coupling range studied: alpha_s from 0.05 to 0.3
- Peak generation numbers at Q/Q_0=10^2: i_max = 6 for Q jet, 11 for G jet
- Peak generation numbers at Q/Q_0=10^3: i_max = 11 for Q jet, 22 for G jet
- For epsilon=0.3, the ratio R (probability of G->GG vs G->q qbar) is approximately 0.8

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Cao and Hwa (1995) and Hwa and Nazirov (1995) that introduced entropy indices and the exponent kappa as measures of chaoticity in branching processes, which previously considered only pure gauge theory (gluons only). The authors compare their results to the Abelian chi model from those earlier works, finding that non-Abelian QCD dynamics differs from Abelian dynamics in having kappa>0 and larger mu_q values. The inclusion of quarks reveals that quark jets exhibit larger spatial fluctuations than gluon jets, with entropy indices mu_q significantly larger for Q jets than G jets, a result not inferable from single-particle distributions. The fixed-coupling analysis shows that alpha_s cannot serve as a control parameter for tuning chaos, unlike in classical nonlinear dynamics.
