# Towards a holographic realization of the quarkyonic phase

> Large-N_c QCD matter at intermediate baryon density and low temperatures has been conjectured to be in the so-called quarkyonic phase, i.e., to have a quark Fermi surface and on top of it a confined spectrum of excitations. It has been suggested that the presence of the quark Fermi surface leads to a homogeneous phase with restored chiral symmetry, which is unstable towards creating condensates breaking both the chiral and translational symmetry. Motivated by these exotic features, we investigate properties of cold baryonic matter in the single flavor Sakai-Sugimoto model searching for a holographic realization of the quarkyonic phase. We use a simplified mean-field description and focus on the regime of parametrically large baryon densities, of the order of the square of the 't Hooft coupling, as they turn out to lead to new physical effects similar to the ones occurring in the quarkyonic phase. One effect, the appearance of a particular marginally stable mode breaking translational invariance and linked with the presence of the Chern-Simons term in the flavor brane Lagrangian, is known to occur in the deconfined phase of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, but turns out to be absent here. The other, completely new phenomenon that we, preliminarily, study using strong simplifying assumptions are density-enhanced interactions of the flavor brane gauge field with holographically represented baryons. These seem to significantly affect the spectrum of vector and axial mesons and might lead to approximate chiral symmetry restoration in the lowest part of the spectrum, where the mesons start to qualitatively behave like collective excitations of the dense baryonic medium. We discuss the relevance of these effects for holographic searches of the quarkyonic phase and conclude with a discussion of various subtleties involved in constructing a mean-field holographic description of a dense baryonic medium.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model can realize the conjectured quarkyonic phase of QCD, but only at parametrically large baryon densities of order the square of the 't Hooft coupling, where new physical effects appear; they find that a known instability toward translational symmetry breaking is absent in this confining regime, while density-enhanced meson-baryon interactions may lead to approximate chiral symmetry restoration in the lowest part of the meson spectrum.

## Method

The authors use the single-flavor Sakai-Sugimoto model at zero temperature, introducing a nonzero baryon density via an antisymmetric electric field on the U-shaped D8-brane, with baryons represented as D4-branes wrapping the S⁴. They adopt a mean-field description treating the D4-branes as a charged dust coupled to the D8-brane Dirac-Born-Infeld action, focusing on densities of order λ² where the D4-branes fill both the field theory and holographic directions. They then linearize the equations of motion for gauge field perturbations to search for marginally stable modes carrying nonzero momentum (indicating striped instabilities) and to compute the spectrum of vector and axial mesons in the dense medium.

## Key numbers

* The baryon densities studied are of order λ², where λ is the 't Hooft coupling, as only at this scale do new physical effects appear.
* The relative mass difference between the lowest axial and vector mesons in vacuum is approximately 40% and does not depend strongly on the asymptotic separation L between the D8- and anti-D8-branes.
* The Kaluza-Klein mass M_KK is given by M_KK = (3/2) u_KK^(1/2) / R^(3/2), setting the scale for meson masses via m = (2/3) M_KK ω̃.
* The Chern-Simons coefficient α in the five-dimensional action is 1/8.
* The overall factor c in the bulk action is c = (1/(768 π⁵ R³ M_KK³)) N_c λ³.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of dense baryonic matter in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, following the mean-field approach of Rozali (2007) for antipodal embeddings and the analysis of Chern-Simons-induced instabilities in the deconfined phase by Ooguri (2010). It contrasts with the quarkyonic phase conjectured by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) and the inhomogeneous phases studied by Kojo (2009, 2010), finding that the Chern-Simons instability present in the deconfined phase is absent in the confining regime studied here. The results enable future investigations of whether holographic models can reproduce the triple point in the QCD phase diagram and whether the density-enhanced meson-baryon interactions can lead to full chiral symmetry restoration.
