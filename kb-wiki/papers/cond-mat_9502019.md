# Universal Parametric Correlations of Eigenfunctions in Chaotic and Disordered Systems

> This paper establishes the universality of parametric correlations of eigenfunctions in chaotic and weakly disordered systems. We demonstrate this universality in the framework of the gaussian random matrix process and obtain predictions for a number of parametric correlators, one of them analytically. We present numerical evidence from different models that verifies our predictions.

# Universal Parametric Correlations of Eigenfunctions in Chaotic and Disordered Systems

## Key claim

The paper establishes that parametric correlations of eigenfunctions in chaotic and weakly disordered systems are universal when the parameter is scaled by the root-mean-square level velocity divided by the mean spacing, provided the process correlation function has a non-vanishing second derivative (i.e., the expansion f(x) ≈ 1 − κx² holds, excluding cases like Dyson's Brownian motion where the first derivative is non-zero).

## Method

The authors employ a Gaussian random matrix process (Gaussian orthogonal process, GOP) to model parametric dependence of Hamiltonians H(x) and derive universal correlators from the joint two-matrix distribution. They compute the averaged parametric overlap o(x−x′) = |⟨ψ_i(x)|ψ_i(x′)⟩|² and the projection correlator p(x−x′) = ⟨φ|ψ_i(x)⟩⟨φ|ψ_i(x′)⟩ numerically from GOP simulations with N=150, then verify universality by comparing against numerical simulations of the interacting boson model (IBM) for nuclear collective states and the Anderson model on a 27×27 lattice. A third correlator, the energy-resolved parametric overlap õ(ω, x−x′), is derived analytically using Efetov's supersymmetry method and evaluated via a 16-dimensional graded matrix integral reduced to a three-parameter integral.

## Key numbers

- The scaling transformation is x → x̄ = [ (∂ε_i/∂x)² ]^{1/2} x, where ε_i are unfolded energies in units of the mean spacing Δ.
- For the GOP generated by H(x) = H₁ cos x + H₂ sin x, the scaling is x → x̄ = (√(2N)/π) x.
- GOP simulations used N=150 matrices, taking the middle third of the spectrum.
- IBM calculations used 25 bosons with total spin J=2 (80 states out of 117) and J=6 (130 states out of 184) in the chaotic regime η=0, −0.8 < χ < −0.5.
- Anderson model simulations used a 27×27 lattice with cylindrical geometry at disorder strength W=4 and toroidal geometry at W=2, including the middle 200 states out of 729.
- The analytical result for õ(ω, x−x′) uses a Lorentzian regularization width δ=0.05 in units of the mean spacing.

## Context

This work extends the universal parametric correlations previously established for spectra by Simons and Altshuler (1993) and Szafer and Altshuler (1993) to eigenfunction correlators, using the Gaussian process framework of Alhassid, Kusnezov and Mitchell (1994) rather than the supersymmetry method or Dyson's Brownian motion model. The results enable predictions for wavefunction decorrelation in chaotic quantum dots, disordered electron systems, and atomic nuclei, and provide an analytical expression for the energy-resolved parametric overlap that can be tested in experiments on mesoscopic systems.
