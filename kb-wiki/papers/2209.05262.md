# Squeezed spectra and elliptic flow of bosons and anti-bosons with in-medium mass splitting

> We study the impact of the in-medium mass splitting between bosons and anti-bosons on their spectra and elliptic flow. The in-medium mass splitting may cause a separation in the transverse momentum spectra, as well as a division in the elliptic flow between bosons and anti-bosons. The magnitude of this effect becomes greater as the in-medium mass splitting increases. With the increasing rapidity, the splitting effect of the spectra increases and the splitting effect of the elliptic flow decreases. These phenomena may provide a way to differentiate whether the influences on boson and anti-boson in the medium are consistent.

# Squeezed spectra and elliptic flow of bosons and anti-bosons with in-medium mass splitting

## Key claim
In-medium mass splitting between bosons and anti-bosons can cause a separation in their transverse momentum spectra and a division in their elliptic flow, with the magnitude of both effects increasing as the mass splitting increases; however, the splitting effect on spectra increases with rapidity while the splitting effect on elliptic flow decreases with rapidity, providing a potential experimental signature to test whether medium effects on bosons and anti-bosons are identical.

## Method
The authors model the particle-emitting source using ideal relativistic hydrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions with Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using the s95p-PCE equation of state. They apply a Bogoliubov transformation to relate vacuum and in-medium creation/annihilation operators for bosons and anti-bosons, parameterizing the in-medium mass modification with a momentum-dependent common shift Δm and a momentum-independent energy splitting δ. The single-particle spectral functions and elliptic flow v₂ are computed for D mesons and anti-D mesons from the hydrodynamic freeze-out surface at T = 0.15 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The in-medium mass-shift parameter Δm₀ is taken as -5 MeV for D mesons at zero momentum.
- The freeze-out temperature for D mesons is 0.15 GeV.
- The initial hydrodynamic time is τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c.
- For elliptic flow calculations, the initial source radii are R_x = 4 fm and R_y = 5 fm; for spectra calculations, R_x = R_y = 5 fm.
- The energy splitting parameter δ is studied at values of 20 MeV and 40 MeV.
- The momentum-dependence parameter Λ_s is varied among 0, 500, 1000, and 1500 MeV.
- The ratio N_a/N_b of D meson spectra declines as δ increases from 20 MeV to 40 MeV for fixed Λ_s.
- The ratio v_{2a}/v_{2b} of elliptic flow decreases with increasing δ and with increasing Λ_s.
- For Λ_s = 500 MeV, the ratio N_a/N_b does not depend on rapidity within p_T range 1.1–1.2 GeV/c, while for Λ_s = 1000 and 1500 MeV it decreases with rapidity in that same p_T range.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of in-medium mass modification effects on boson spectra (Zhang 2021) and squeezed back-to-back correlations (Padula 2006, Zhang 2015a) by incorporating a more realistic hydrodynamic source model. It builds on the formalism of in-medium mass splitting between particles and antiparticles introduced by Xu, Peng, and Zhang (2019). The results suggest that measurements of D meson and anti-D meson spectra and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC energies could provide a new experimental probe for distinguishing whether medium effects on bosons and anti-bosons are identical, particularly in systems with wide particle emission time distributions where squeezed correlations may be suppressed.
