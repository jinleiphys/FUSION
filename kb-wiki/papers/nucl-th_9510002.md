# Universal Predictions for Statistical Nuclear Correlations

> We explore the behavior of collective nuclear excitations under a multi-parameter deformation of the Hamiltonian. The Hamiltonian matrix elements have the form $P(|H_{ij}|)\propto 1/\sqrt{|H_{ij}|}\exp(-|H_{ij}|/V)$, with a parametric correlation of the type $\log \langle H(x)H(y)\rangle\propto -|x-y|$. The studies are done in both the regular and chaotic regimes of the Hamiltonian. Model independent predictions for a wide variety of correlation functions and distributions which depend on wavefunctions and energies are found from parametric random matrix theory and are compared to the nuclear excitations. We find that our universal predictions are observed in the nuclear states. Being a multi-parameter theory, we consider general paths in parameter space and find that universality can be effected by the topology of the parameter space. Specifically, Berry's phase can modify short distance correlations, breaking certain universal predictions.

# Universal Predictions for Statistical Nuclear Correlations

## Key claim

The authors show that parametric random matrix theory can produce model-independent (universal) predictions for correlation functions and distributions of wavefunctions and energies in collective nuclear excitations, but only when the parameter path lies entirely within the chaotic regime of the Hamiltonian; Berry's phase can break these universal predictions by modifying short-distance correlations, and the topology of the parameter space affects universality.

## Method

The authors model collective nuclear excitations using the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) in its consistent-Q form, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian for boson number Nb=25 across multiple angular momentum channels (Jπ=0+,2+,4+,10+). They compare the IBM results to two parametric random matrix constructions: a cosine combination of two uncorrelated GOE matrices and a stochastic integral over Gaussian white noise, both with α=2 short-distance behavior. Universal predictions are obtained by scaling the parameter z by the diffusion constant Dα derived from the variance of energy level shifts, removing model and dimension dependence.

## Key numbers

- The distribution of IBM Hamiltonian matrix elements follows P(|Hij|) ∝ 1/√|Hij| exp(-|Hij|/V), with V of order unity in chaotic regimes and much smaller in regular regions
- The autocorrelation function Fibm(z) behaves as e^{-γ|z|} for short distances in both regular and chaotic regions
- The Brody parameter ω for level spacing distributions reaches ω=1 (GOE) in chaotic regions, while regular paths show average Brody parameters of 0.25 and 0.23
- The diffusion constant C(0)=D2 scales linearly with matrix dimension N, tested for Nb=10,15,20,25 corresponding to J=10+ state dimensions of N=16,56,121,211
- The curvature distribution P(k) follows the predicted form cν/(1+k²)^(ν/2+1) for ν=1 (GOE) in chaotic IBM regions
- The adiabatic survival probability Pn(|Unn(z)|²) follows the universal Lorentzian form 1/(1+c|z̃-ỹ|²) for ν=1
- The correlation length zc in the IBM for Jπ=0+,2+,4+,10+ is zc=0.16,0.14,0.11,0.05 respectively, following approximately zc ~ 1 - γJ

## Context

This work extends parametric random matrix theory approaches developed by Wilkinson (1990) and the scaling method of Szafer and Altshuler (1993) to collective nuclear excitations modeled by the Interacting Boson Model. The authors build on earlier studies of chaos in nuclei using random matrix theory by Brody (1981) and the classical chaotic behavior of the IBM mapped by Niall (1994). The results enable model-independent predictions for correlations between nuclear properties at different deformations, allowing comparison of shape transitions (rotational to vibrational, vibrational to γ-soft) within a universal framework, though the authors note that Berry's phase can break universality for certain parameter paths.
