# Learning the structure of giant resonances from their $γ$-decay

> The direct $γ$-decays of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) and the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) of $^{208}$Pb to low-lying states are investigated by means of a microscopic self-consistent model. The model considers effects beyond the linear response approximation. The strong sensitivity of $γ$-decay to the isospin of the involved states is proven. By comparing their decay widths, a much larger weight of the $3_{1}^{-}$ component in the GQR wave function of $^{208}$Pb is deduced, with respect to the weight of the $2_{1}^{+}$ component in the GDR wave function. Thus, we have shown that $γ$-decay is a unique probe of the resonance wave functions, and a testground for nuclear structure models.

## Key claim

The direct γ-decay of giant resonances to low-lying states is a sensitive probe of the microscopic wave function of the resonances; specifically, the proton-neutron averaged transition probability B(E1) for the GQR → 3₁⁻ decay is more than twice that for the GDR → 2₁⁺ decay, revealing a much larger component of 1p-1h coupled with the final phonon in the GQR wave function than in the GDR wave function.

## Method

The authors employ a fully self-consistent random phase approximation plus particle-vibration coupling (RPA+PVC) model, using the Skyrme functional LNS, to calculate the 12 lowest-order nuclear field theory (NFT) diagrams for the γ-decay of the GDR and GQR in ²⁰⁸Pb to the low-lying 2₁⁺ and 3₁⁻ states. The calculation is performed in a configuration space with a single-particle cutoff energy of 150 MeV and a box size of 20 fm, with energy-weighted sum rules satisfied at more than 99.50%. The isospin dependence is further tested by performing calculations for the N=Z nucleus ⁵⁶Ni with the Coulomb interaction turned off.

## Key numbers

- The calculated γ-decay width Γ_γ for GDR → 2₁⁺ in ²⁰⁸Pb is 285.74 eV, more than twice that for GQR → 3₁⁻, which is 123.54 eV (using the LNS interaction).
- The average transition energy for both decays is 9.12 MeV.
- For the GDR → 2₁⁺ decay, the proton contribution Γ_γ^π is 145.11 eV and the neutron contribution Γ_γ^ν is 49.15 eV; for the GQR → 3₁⁻ decay, these are 495.63 eV and 202.42 eV, respectively.
- In the isospin-symmetric limit (⁵⁶Ni without Coulomb), the GDR → 2₁⁺ width is 3877.72 eV and the GQR → 3₁⁻ width is exactly 0.00 eV.
- The experimental γ-decay width for GQR → 3₁⁻ in ²⁰⁸Pb is 5 ± 5 eV (from Beene (1989) and Veyssiere (1970)).
- The experimental B(E1↑) for the GDR is 62.5 ± 5.0 e²fm², and the experimental B(E2↑) for the GQR is 5.80 ± 1.60 × 10³ e²fm⁴.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RPA+PVC approach previously applied to single-particle levels and Gamow-Teller response (Colo (2010), Niu (2014), Niu (2015)) to the γ-decay of giant resonances, reviving a line of investigation last pursued with phenomenological models such as the surface coupling model (Bortignon (1984)) and the quasiparticle-phonon model (Ponomarev (1992)). The results confirm and clarify earlier wavelet analysis of fine structure (Shevchenko (2004), Poltoratska (2014)) by providing a direct microscopic interpretation of the wave function components. The work enables future experiments at facilities like ELI-NP and SLEGS to use γ-decay as a quantitative test of nuclear structure models, and can be extended to study pygmy resonances and their isospin properties.
