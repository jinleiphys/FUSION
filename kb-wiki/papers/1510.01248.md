# Skyrme RPA for spherical and axially symmetric nuclei

> Random Phase Approximation (RPA) is the basic method for calculation of excited states of nuclei over the Hartree-Fock ground state, suitable also for energy density functionals (EDF or DFT). We developed a convenient formalism for expressing densities and currents in a form of reduced matrix elements, which allows fast calculation of spectra for spherical nuclei. All terms of Skyrme functional were taken into account, so it is possible to calculate electric, magnetic and vortical/toroidal/compression transitions and strength functions of any multipolarity. Time-odd (spin) terms in Skyrme functional become important for magnetic M1 and isovector toroidal E1 transitions. It was also found that transition currents in pygmy region (low-lying part of E1 resonance) exhibit isoscalar toroidal flow, so the previously assumed picture of neutron-skin vibration is not the only mechanism present in pygmy transitions. RPA calculations with heavy axially-symmetric nuclei now become feasible on ordinary PC. Detailed formulae for axial Skyrme RPA are given. Some numerical results are shown in comparison with the approximate approach of separable RPA, previously developed in our group for fast calculation of strength functions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a full Skyrme RPA formalism for both spherical and axially symmetric nuclei that includes all time-even and time-odd terms of the Skyrme functional, enabling calculation of electric, magnetic, vortical, toroidal, and compression transitions; the authors find that transition currents in the pygmy region exhibit isoscalar toroidal flow, meaning the previously assumed picture of neutron-skin vibration is not the only mechanism present in pygmy transitions.

## Method

The authors developed a formalism expressing densities and currents in terms of reduced matrix elements for spherical nuclei, and provided detailed expressions for matrix elements in axial symmetry using cylindrical coordinates. The RPA equations are solved by matrix diagonalization for spherical nuclei and by constructing phonon operators from two-quasiparticle states for axially deformed nuclei, with the residual interaction derived from the second functional derivative of the Skyrme density functional including Coulomb and pairing interactions. Transition strengths and currents are computed for electric, magnetic, vortical, toroidal, and compression operators.

## Key numbers

- Skyrme functional parameters: \(t_0, t_1, t_2, t_3, t_4, x_0, x_1, x_2, x_3, \alpha\) define the interaction, with coefficients \(b_0, b_0', \tilde{b}_0, \tilde{b}_0'\) etc. given by explicit formulas (e.g., \(b_0 = t_0(2+x_0)/2\), \(b_0' = t_0(1+2x_0)/2\))
- Spin g-factors are reduced by a quenching factor \(\varsigma = 0.7\) in the magnetic transition operator
- The direct Coulomb interaction contributes a double integral with factor \(\frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{4\pi}{2\lambda+1}\) and radial dependence \(r^\lambda / r'^{\lambda+1}\) for \(r<r'\) or \(r'^\lambda / r^{\lambda+1}\) for \(r \geq r'\)
- The exchange Coulomb interaction is treated by Slater approximation: \(\mathcal{H}_\mathrm{xc} = -\frac{3}{4}\left(\frac{3}{\pi}\right)^{1/3} \frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \int d^3 r \, \rho_p^{4/3}(\vec{r})\)

## Context

This work extends the separable RPA (SRPA) approach previously developed by the same group (Nesterenko 2002, Nesterenko 2006) for fast calculation of strength functions, providing a full RPA formalism that handles both spherical and axially deformed nuclei. It enables systematic calculations of electric and magnetic transitions of any multipolarity in heavy axially-symmetric nuclei on ordinary PCs, and allows detailed analysis of transition currents to distinguish between toroidal, compression, and neutron-skin vibration mechanisms in the pygmy resonance region.
