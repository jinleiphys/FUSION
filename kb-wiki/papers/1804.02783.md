# A critical examination of constraints on the equation of state of dense matter obtained from GW170817

> The correlation of the tidal polarizabilities $Λ_1$-$Λ_2$ for GW170817 is predicted by combining dense-matter equations of state (EOSs) that satisfy nuclear physics constraints with the chirp mass and mass asymmetry for this event. Our models are constrained by calculations of the neutron-matter EOS using chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians with reliable error estimates up to once or twice the nuclear saturation density. In the latter case, we find that GW170817 does not improve our understanding of the EOS. We contrast two distinct extrapolations to higher density: a minimal model (MM) which assumes that the EOS is a smooth function of density described by a Taylor expansion and a more general model parametrized by the speed of sound that admits phase transitions. This allows us to identify regions in the $Λ_1$-$Λ_2$ plots that could favor the existence of new phases of matter in neutron stars. We predict the combined tidal polarizability of the two neutron stars in GW170817 to be $80\le \tildeΛ\le 580$ ($280\le \tildeΛ\le 480$ for the MM), which is smaller than the range suggested by the LIGO-Virgo data analysis. Our analysis also shows that GW170817 requires a NS with $M=1.4M_\odot$ to have a radius $9.0<R_{1.4}<13.6$~km ($ 11.3 <R_{1.4}< 13.6$~km for the MM).

## Key claim

GW170817 does not improve our understanding of the equation of state when neutron-matter calculations from chiral effective field theory are assumed valid up to twice nuclear saturation density; the predicted tidal polarizabilities are already more tightly constrained than the LIGO-Virgo analysis suggests.

## Method

The authors combine dense-matter equations of state (EOSs) constrained by chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter up to either one or two times nuclear saturation density with the chirp mass and mass asymmetry distributions for GW170817. They contrast two high-density extrapolations: a minimal model (MM) using a Taylor expansion about saturation density, and a speed-of-sound model (CSM) that allows phase transitions. Both models are required to be stable, causal, and support a maximum neutron-star mass of at least 1.9 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- Combined tidal polarizability for GW170817: 80 ≤ Λ̃ ≤ 580 for the CSM; 280 ≤ Λ̃ ≤ 480 for the MM (with n_tr = 2 n_sat)
- Radius of a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star: 9.0 < R₁.₄ < 13.6 km (CSM) and 11.3 < R₁.₄ < 13.6 km (MM) when using n_tr = n_sat and enforcing the LIGO-Virgo constraint
- Radius of a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star: 9.2 < R₁.₄ < 12.5 km (CSM) and 11.3 < R₁.₄ < 12.1 km (MM) when using n_tr = 2 n_sat
- Chirp mass of GW170817: M_chirp = 1.186 solar masses with σ_M = 10⁻³ solar masses
- Maximum neutron-star mass constraint: M_max ≥ 1.9 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the analysis of GW170817 by Abbott (2017, 2018) using nuclear-physics-informed EOS models rather than the agnostic approach of the LIGO-Virgo collaboration. It builds on chiral effective field theory calculations of neutron matter by Tews (2018) and the meta-model ELFc introduced by Margueron (2017). The results contradict the lower limit Λ̃ > 400 suggested by Radice (2017) based on merger simulations, showing that EOSs with Λ̃ = 300 can still support a stable long-lived merger remnant. The analysis enables future gravitational-wave detections to probe phase transitions at supra-nuclear densities if observational precision reaches ΔΛ̃ < 200.
