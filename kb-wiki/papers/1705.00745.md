# Multiparticle collectivity from initial state correlations in high energy proton-nucleus collisions

> Qualitative features of multiparticle correlations in light-heavy ion ($p+A$) collisions at RHIC and LHC are reproduced in a simple initial state model of partons in the projectile coherently scattering off localized domains of color charge in the heavy nuclear target. These include i) the ordering of the magnitudes of the azimuthal angle $n$th Fourier harmonics of two-particle correlations $v_n\{2\}$, ii) the energy and transverse momentum dependence of the four-particle Fourier harmonic $v_2\{4\}$, and iii) the energy dependence of four-particle symmetric cumulants measuring correlations between different Fourier harmonics. Similar patterns are seen in an Abelian version of the model, where we observe $v_2\{2\} > v_2\{4\}\approx v_2\{6\}\approx v_2\{8\}$ of two, four, six, and eight particle correlations. While such patterns are often interpreted as signatures of collectivity arising from hydrodynamic flow, our results provide an alternative description of the multiparticle correlations seen in $p+A$ collisions.

## Key claim
Qualitative features of multiparticle correlations in light-heavy ion collisions, including the ordering of two-particle Fourier harmonics, the energy and transverse momentum dependence of four-particle harmonics, and four-particle symmetric cumulants, can be reproduced by an initial-state model of partons scattering off color domains, without invoking hydrodynamic flow. The authors explicitly state that their results "do not necessarily mean that an initial state interpretation of the data is favored" and that "the v_n{m} measurements alone are insufficient to unambiguously distinguish between initial and final state approaches."

## Method
The authors model proton-nucleus collisions as eikonal scattering of nearly collinear quarks from the projectile off color domains of size 1/Q_{s,T} in the nuclear target, using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with a Gaussian distribution of color charges. They compute two- and four-particle cumulants by evaluating the expectation value of products of light-like dipole Wilson-line correlators, requiring a 24×24 matrix exponentiation for the four-dipole case. For higher-order cumulants (six- and eight-particle), they use an Abelian version of the model where the computation is tractable.

## Key numbers
- B_p = 4 GeV^{-2}, a scale controlling the quark transverse momentum and spatial resolution, fixed using dipole model fits to HERA data.
- The infrared cutoff Λ = 0.241 GeV is used to regulate the two-dimensional gluon exchange propagator; results are insensitive to variations in this scale.
- For p_⟂^{max} = 3 GeV, the two-particle Fourier harmonic results become independent of the upper integration limit.
- For fixed Q_{s,T}^2 = 2 GeV^2, the differential v_2{m}(p_⟂) results show qualitative agreement with data from light-heavy ion experiments at RHIC and LHC.
- The symmetric cumulant SC(2,3) becomes negative by Q_{s,T} = 1 GeV, while SC(2,4) is positive for all Q_{s,T} values shown.

## Context
This work extends prior initial-state correlation studies using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, specifically the Glasma graph scenario for two-particle correlations (Dumitru 2010, Dusling 2012-2015) and the MV model computations of v_n{2} (Lappi 2015). It provides the first systematic computation of v_2{m} for m ≥ 4 in an initial-state framework, including four-particle symmetric cumulants that had previously only been measured in heavy-ion collisions and interpreted via hydrodynamic models (Bozek 2012, Kozlov 2014, Bzdak 2013, Giacalone 2017). The results enable the conclusion that multiparticle correlation measurements alone cannot distinguish between initial-state and hydrodynamic (final-state) interpretations of collectivity in small systems.
