# Hydrodynamical evolution of dissipative QGP fluid

> In the framework of the Mueller-Israel-Stewart theory of dissipative fluid dynamics, we have studied the space-time evolution of a QGP fluid. For simplicity, we have considered shear viscosity only and neglected bulk viscosity and heat conductivity. Shear viscosity opposes the expansion and cooling of the fluid. As a result, the lifetime of the fluid is extended. We also find that the parton $p_T$ distribution is considerably flattened.

## Key claim

Shear viscosity in a QGP fluid, modeled using second-order (causal) dissipative hydrodynamics, opposes expansion and cooling, thereby extending the fluid's lifetime and flattening the parton \(p_T\) distribution; however, the authors note that the initial viscous pressure already flattens the initial \(p_T\) spectra, so retuning of initial conditions is required to match measured final spectra.

## Method

The authors solved the energy-momentum conservation equations and the Israel-Stewart relaxation equations for the shear viscous pressure tensor simultaneously, assuming longitudinal boost-invariance and cylindrical symmetry in the transverse plane. They used a simple equation of state \(p = \varepsilon/3\) with \(\varepsilon = a T^4\), treated the shear viscosity coefficient \(\eta\) and relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) as phenomenological parameters guided by perturbative QCD and AdS/CFT estimates, and employed the SHASTA-FCT algorithm for numerical integration. Initial conditions were a Woods-Saxon energy density profile with zero radial velocity, and the viscous pressure components were initially set to the Bjorken scaling expansion values.

## Key numbers

- For initial conditions \(T_i = 0.3\) GeV at \(\tau_i = 0.5\) fm/\(c\), the fluid lifetime at \(r=0\) is extended by 30% for \(\eta/s = 0.08\) and by 55% for \(\eta/s = 0.135\).
- For initial conditions \(T_i = 0.5\) GeV at \(\tau_i = 0.1\) fm/\(c\), the fluid lifetime at the fireball center is enhanced by more than 120% for \(\eta/s = 0.08\) and by more than 170% for \(\eta/s = 0.135\).
- Increasing the initial viscous pressure from zero to \(4\eta/(3\tau_i)\) extends the lifetime of the dissipative QGP by 20% (with \(\eta/s = 0.135\)).
- Increasing the relaxation time by a factor of 4 (from \(0.5 \tau_{\rm kin}\) to \(2 \tau_{\rm kin}\), where \(\tau_{\rm kin} = 3\eta/(2p)\)) decreases the freeze-out time at the fireball center by 25% (with \(\eta/s = 0.135\)).
- Viscous flow enhances high \(p_T\) parton production by a factor of 5–10, depending on viscosity, at freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 160\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dissipation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, which mostly used one-dimensional systems and first-order theories (e.g., Ka84, Da85, Ch00, Mu03, Te03, Te04, Mu04), by implementing a causal second-order (Israel-Stewart) theory numerically in a two-dimensional (radial and longitudinal) geometry. The results enable future studies that include a hadronization phase transition and retuning of initial conditions to match measured hadron \(p_T\) spectra, and they highlight the sensitivity of the freeze-out surface to the shear viscosity coefficient and relaxation time.
