# Isomeric 0- halo-states in 12Be and 11Li

> We predict the existence of an isomeric 0$^-$-state in $^{12}$Be at an excitation energy of about 2.5 MeV, and a 0$^-$-resonance in $^{11}$Li with both energy and width of about 1 MeV corresponding to two-neutron emission. The structure of these halo-like states are like the 1$^-$-states which means essentially a core surrounded by two neutrons in single-particle $s$ and p-states. The life-time of the $^{12}$Be state is determined by $M1$ or $M2$-emission, $τ(M1) \approx 10^{-11}$ s or $τ(M2) \approx 10^{-8}$ s estimated for photon energies of 0.1 MeV and 0.6 MeV, respectively.

# Isomeric 0⁻ halo-states in ¹²Be and ¹¹Li

## Key claim
The authors predict a particle-bound isomeric 0⁻ state in ¹²Be at an excitation energy of about 2.5 MeV, and a 0⁻ resonance in ¹¹Li with both energy and width of about 1 MeV corresponding to two-neutron emission. These predictions are based on a three-body model that reproduces the known 1⁻ states, but the exact binding of the 0⁻ state in ¹²Be depends on the choice of three-body force and could be unbound if the model uncertainties are larger than estimated.

## Method
The authors solve Faddeev equations with the hyperspheric adiabatic expansion method for a three-body system consisting of a ¹⁰Be core (for ¹²Be) or a ⁹Li core (for ¹¹Li) surrounded by two neutrons. Unbound resonant states are computed using the complex scaling method. The neutron-core interactions are constructed from ℓ-dependent gaussian potentials fitted to reproduce known single-particle states in ¹¹Be and ¹⁰Li, and the neutron-neutron interaction is taken from Garrido (1999).

## Key numbers
- ¹²Be 0⁻ state: two-neutron separation energy of −0.96 MeV (bound), corresponding to an excitation energy roughly 10 keV higher than the 1⁻ state at −0.97 MeV
- ¹¹Li 0⁻ resonance: energy and width (E_R, Γ_R) = (0.92, 0.82) MeV, roughly 300 keV higher than the 1⁻ resonance at (0.64, 0.32) MeV
- ¹²Be 0⁻ lifetime estimates: τ(M1) ≈ 1.06 × 10⁻¹¹ s for photon energy 0.1 MeV; τ(M2) ≈ 1.12 × 10⁻⁸ s for photon energy 0.6 MeV
- Root-mean-square distances for ¹²Be 0⁻ state: ⟨r_nn²⟩^{1/2} = 9.2 fm (neutron-neutron), ⟨r_nc²⟩^{1/2} = 5.9 fm (neutron-core)
- Root-mean-square distances for ¹¹Li 0⁻ state: ⟨r_nn²⟩^{1/2} = 8.7 fm, ⟨r_nc²⟩^{1/2} = 5.1 fm
- Hyperradius criterion ⟨ρ²⟩/ρ₀² = 2.4 for ¹²Be and 2.5 for ¹¹Li, both above the threshold of 2 indicating halo configuration
- Computed magnetic transition strengths for ¹²Be: ℬ(M1, 0⁻→1⁻) = 0.059 e²fm² and ℬ(M2, 0⁻→2⁺) = 0.936 e²fm⁴ (using g_ℓ⁽ⁿ⁾ = 0.28, g_s⁽ⁿ⁾ = −3.82)

## Context
This work extends the known 1⁻ excited states in ¹²Be (experimentally bound at 2.68 MeV, Iwasaki 2000) and ¹¹Li (unbound at about 1 MeV, Korsheninnikov 1997) to predict their 0⁻ partners, analogous to the 0⁻/1⁻ doublets observed in ¹⁴C (6.09 and 6.90 MeV, Ajzenberg-Selove 1991) and ¹⁶O (9.59 and 10.97 MeV, Ajzenberg-Selove 1986). A linear mass extrapolation from ¹⁶O over ¹⁴C to ¹²Be and ¹¹Li gives predictions of (2.8, 2.6) MeV and (0.80, 0.84) MeV for the 0⁻ and 1⁻ states respectively. The only prior prediction of a 0⁻ state in ¹²Be (Kanungo 2003) systematically overestimated energies, making the 1⁻ state unbound contrary to experiment. The predicted 0⁻ state in ¹²Be could be populated in reactions with a cross section about a factor of three smaller than the known 1⁻ state, and its lifetime qualifies it as an isomeric state comparable to the recently discovered 0⁺ isomer in ¹²Be (Shimoura 2003).
