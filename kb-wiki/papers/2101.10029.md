# Application of the Uniformized Mittag-Leffler Expansion to $Λ(1405)$

> We study the pole properties of $Λ(1405)$ in a model-independent manner by applying the Uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion proposed in our previous paper. The resonant energy, width and residues are determined by expanding the observable as a sum of resonant-pole pairs under an appropriate parameterization which expresses the observable to be single-valued, and fitting it to experimental data of the invariant-mass distribution of $π^+Σ^-$, $π^-Σ^+$, $π^0Σ^0$ final states in the reaction, $γp \rightarrow K^+ πΣ$, and the elastic and inelastic cross section, $K^-p\to K^-p$, $\bar{K}^0n$, $π^+Σ^-$, $π^-Σ^+$. As we gradually increase the number of pairs from one to three, the first pair converges while the second and third pairs emerge further and further away from the first pair, implying that the Uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion with three pairs is almost convergent in the vicinity of the $Λ(1405)$. The broad peak structure between the $πΣ$ and $\bar{K} N$ thresholds regarded to be $Λ(1405)$ is explained by a single pair with a resonant energy of 1420 $\pm$ 1 MeV, and a half width of 48 $\pm$ 2 MeV, which is consistent with the single-pole picture of $Λ(1405)$. We conclude that the Uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion turns out to be a very powerful method to obtain resonance energy, width and residues from the near-threshold spectrum.

## Key claim

The broad peak structure between the πΣ and K̅N thresholds, traditionally identified as Λ(1405), is explained by a single resonant-pole pair with a resonant energy of 1420 ± 1 MeV and a half width of 48 ± 2 MeV, consistent with a single-pole picture of Λ(1405), contrary to double-pole predictions from chiral-unitary models.

## Method

The authors applied the Uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion, which expresses observables as a sum of resonant-pole pairs in a uniformized complex plane where the S-matrix becomes meromorphic, to fit experimental data of the invariant-mass distributions of π⁺Σ⁻, π⁻Σ⁺, π⁰Σ⁰ final states from the reaction γp → K⁺πΣ measured by CLAS at Jefferson Lab, together with elastic and inelastic cross sections K⁻p → K⁻p, K̅⁰n, π⁺Σ⁻, π⁻Σ⁺. They gradually increased the number of resonant-pole pairs from one to three, fitting all datasets with common pole positions but different residues, and assessed convergence of the expansion.

## Key numbers

- Resonant energy of the first pole (Pole 1): 1420.3 ± 1.1 MeV (complex energy 1.4203 − 0.0475i GeV)
- Half width of the first pole: 48 ± 2 MeV (from imaginary part 47.5 ± 1.5 MeV)
- Reduced chi-squared values: 5.74 (m=1), 2.65 (m=2), 1.18 (m=3)
- Pole 2 complex energy: 1.4283 − 0.074i GeV (on sheet (− −))
- Pole 3 complex energy: 1.5138 − 0.0068i GeV (on sheet (− −))
- Pole 1 position on z-plane: 0.5243 + 0.3159i ± 0.0062 ± 0.0058i

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal of the Uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion (Yamada and Morimatsu, 2020) from a double-channel model calculation to actual experimental data. It addresses the long-standing debate between single-pole descriptions from phenomenological local potential models (e.g., Akaishi 2010, Revai 2017) and double-pole predictions from chiral-unitary models (e.g., Jido 2003, Oller 2000, Hyodo 2011, Ikeda 2011, 2012). The method enables model-independent extraction of resonance parameters from near-threshold spectra, and can be applied to other hadron resonances near thresholds.
