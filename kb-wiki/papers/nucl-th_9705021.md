# Remarks on the Delta(+) mass

> We question the reliability of current estimates for the Delta(+) mass. We explain why the standard value, determined from pion photoproduction, should not be used in conjunction with present estimates for the Delta(++) and Delta(0) masses, determined from pion-nucleon scattering. The resultant mass splittings are internally inconsistent. We also briefly comment on discrepancies found when `theoretical' and `experimental' values for the Delta masses are compared.

# Remarks on the Delta(+) mass

## Key claim

The standard Delta(+) mass of 1234.9 +/- 1.4 MeV, determined from pion photoproduction using outdated phase information, is internally inconsistent with the more recent Delta(++) and Delta(0) masses from pion-nucleon scattering; the resulting mass splittings do not fit the pattern exhibited by other baryon octet and decuplet members, and the Delta(+) mass should instead lie between the Delta(0) and Delta(++) masses.

## Method

The author compares the Delta(+) mass extracted from a multipole analysis of pion photoproduction (Miroshnichenko 1979) with Delta(++) and Delta(0) masses from pion-nucleon scattering analyses, tracing the inconsistency to the use of CERN phases from the 1960s in the photoproduction analysis via Watson's theorem. The paper examines alternative determinations that relax the Watson's theorem constraint (Berends and Donnachie 1975) and compares resonance masses with pole masses, as well as testing mass formulae relating Delta splittings to the neutron-proton mass difference and other baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- Delta(++) mass: 1230.9 +/- 0.3 MeV from pion-nucleon scattering (Koch and Pietarinen 1980, Particle Data Group 1996)
- Delta(0) mass: 1233.6 +/- 0.5 MeV from pion-nucleon scattering (Koch and Pietarinen 1980, Particle Data Group 1996)
- Delta(+) mass: 1234.9 +/- 1.4 MeV from pion photoproduction (Miroshnichenko 1979)
- Delta(0) - Delta(++) mass difference: 2.25 +/- 0.68 MeV from a fit to Pedroni total cross sections (Bernicha 1996), consistent with the original Pedroni determination of 2.7 +/- 0.3 MeV (Pedroni 1978)
- Delta(+) mass from analysis relaxing Watson's theorem constraint: 1231.8 MeV (Berends and Donnachie 1975)
- Delta(0) - Delta(++) splitting from Cutkosky (1993) fit to octet and decuplet masses: 1.53 MeV, dropping to 0.81 MeV when experimental Delta masses were excluded
- Delta(0) - Delta(++) splitting in pole masses: 0.40 +/- 0.57 MeV (Bernicha 1996)
- Delta(0) - Delta(++) splitting from Virginia Tech analysis: 0.8 MeV (Arndt, private communication)
- Predicted splitting from mass relation Delta(0) - Delta(++) = 2(n-p): approximately 2.6 MeV
- Predicted splitting from more formally correct mass relation including Sigma terms: 0.9 MeV using experimental values (Particle Data Group 1996)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of Delta mass splittings from Cutkosky (1993) and Luty (1996), who derived mass relations for the baryon decuplet, and directly contradicts the use of the Miroshnichenko (1979) Delta(+) mass in conjunction with modern pion-nucleon scattering results. The paper enables future photoproduction analyses to either drop the Watson's theorem constraint or allow small phase shifts, as attempted by Berends and Donnachie (1975), and highlights that accurate determination of the Delta(+) - Delta(0) splitting will be difficult given the small splittings found in pole positions compared to resonance masses.
