# Chiral multiplets of excited mesons

> It is shown that experimental meson states with spins J=0,1,2,3 in the energy range 1.9 - 2.4 GeV obtained in recent partial wave analysis of proton-antiproton annihilation at LEAR remarkably confirm all predictions of chiral symmetry restoration. Classification of excited $\bar q q$ mesons according to the representations of chiral $U(2)_L \times U(2)_R$ group is performed. There are two important predictions of chiral symmetry restoration in highly excited mesons: (i) physical states must fill out approximately degenerate parity-chiral multiplets; (ii) some of the physical states with the given $I,J^{PC}$ are members of one parity-chiral multiplet, while the other states with the same $I,J^{PC}$ are members of the other parity-chiral multiplet. For example, while some of the excited $ρ(1,1^{--})$ states are systematically degenerate with $a_1(1,1^{++})$ states forming (0,1)+(1,0) chiral multiplets, the other excited $ρ(1,1^{--})$ states are degenerate with $h_1(0,1^{+-})$ states ((1/2,1/2) chiral multiplets). Hence, one of the predictions of chiral symmetry restoration is that the combined amount of $a_1(1,1^{++})$ and $h_1(0,1^{+-})$ states must coincide with the amount of $ρ(1,1^{--})$ states in the chirally restored regime. It is shown that the same rule applies (and experimentally confirmed) to many other meson states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that experimental meson states with spins J=0,1,2,3 in the energy range 1.9-2.4 GeV, obtained from partial wave analysis of proton-antiproton annihilation at LEAR, remarkably confirm all predictions of chiral symmetry restoration in highly excited mesons, with the caveat that the restoration is approximate and quantified by a chiral asymmetry parameter typically within 0.01 at these masses.

## Method

The author classified excited $\bar q q$ mesons (with u,d valence quark content) according to irreducible representations of the chiral $U(2)_L \times U(2)_R$ group, specifically the parity-chiral group representations (0,0), (1/2,1/2), and (0,1)+(1,0). Experimental meson masses from recent partial wave analyses of $\bar p p$ annihilation at LEAR (Anisovich et al., 2000-2002) were then assigned to these chiral multiplets, and the mass degeneracies within each multiplet were examined to test the predictions of chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key numbers

- Chiral asymmetry $\chi = |M_1 - M_2|/(M_1+M_2)$ for low-lying states is typically 0.3-0.6 (e.g., comparing $\rho(770)$ and $a_1(1260)$ or $\rho(770)$ and $h_1(1170)$)
- Chiral asymmetry at meson masses about 2 GeV is typically within 0.01
- For $\pi$ and $f_0$ states: $\chi(\sqrt s \sim 1.3 \text{ GeV}) \sim 0.03 \div 0.1$, $\chi(\sqrt s \sim 1.8 \text{ GeV}) \sim 0.008$, $\chi(\sqrt s \sim 2.3 \text{ GeV}) \sim 0.005$
- J=2 (0,0) multiplet: $\omega_2(0,2^{--})$ at $1975 \pm 20$ MeV paired with $f_2(0,2^{++})$ at $1934 \pm 20$ MeV; second pair at $2195 \pm 30$ MeV and $2240 \pm 15$ MeV
- J=2 (1/2,1/2) multiplet: $\pi_2(1,2^{-+})$ at $2005 \pm 15$ MeV paired with $f_2(0,2^{++})$ at $2001 \pm 10$ MeV; second pair at $2245 \pm 60$ MeV and $2293 \pm 13$ MeV
- J=2 (0,1)+(1,0) multiplet: $a_2(1,2^{++})$ at $1950^{+30}_{-70}$ MeV paired with $\rho_2(1,2^{--})$ at $1940 \pm 40$ MeV; second pair at $2175 \pm 40$ MeV and $2225 \pm 35$ MeV
- J=1 (0,1)+(1,0) multiplet: $a_1(1,1^{++})$ at $1930^{+30}_{-70}$ MeV paired with $\rho(1,1^{--})$ at 1900 MeV; second pair at $2270^{+55}_{-40}$ MeV and $2265 \pm 40$ MeV
- J=3 (0,0) multiplet: $\omega_3(0,3^{--})$ at $2285 \pm 60$ MeV paired with $f_3(0,3^{++})$ at $2303 \pm 15$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Glozman, 2000) that spontaneously broken chiral symmetry of QCD must be restored in the upper part of hadron spectra, evidenced by parity doubling of N and Delta states at M >= 1.7 GeV. The idea was substantiated using operator product expansion and dispersion relations (Cohen and Glozman, 2002). The paper builds on a previous classification of J=0 mesons (Glozman, 2002) and uses experimental data from partial wave analyses of $\bar p p$ annihilation at LEAR (Anisovich et al., 2000-2002). The results enable future systematic study of higher spin states (J >= 4) and provide a framework for understanding the string picture with quarks of definite chirality at the endpoints, as well as motivating microscopic theories that simultaneously incorporate chiral symmetry breaking and confinement.
