# Parity-transfer $({}^{16}{\rm O},{}^{16}{\rm F}(0^-,{\rm g.s.}))$ reaction as a selective probe of isovector $0^-$ states in nuclei

> We demonstrate that the parity-transfer $({}^{16}{\rm O},{}^{16}{\rm F}(0^-,{\rm g.s.}))$ reaction provides a selective probe of isovector $0^-$ excitations in nuclei. This reaction selectively populates unnatural-parity states through a $0^+ \to 0^-$ transition in the projectile. The excitation-energy spectrum of $^{12}\mathrm{B}$ was reconstructed via the ${}^{12}{\rm C}({}^{16}{\rm O},{}^{16}{\rm F}(0^-,{\rm g.s.}))$ reaction at 247~MeV/u from the coincident detection of the ${}^{15}\mathrm{O}+p$ decay products of ${}^{16}\mathrm{F}$. The known $0^{-}$ state at $E_x = 9.3~{\rm MeV}$ was clearly observed with a strongly enhanced forward cross section, establishing the selectivity of the reaction. Structures observed at $E_x = 6.6 \pm 0.4$ and $14.8 \pm 0.3~{\rm MeV}$ exhibit forward-peaked angular distributions and are suggested to contain significant $0^-$ strength. These results demonstrate that the parity-transfer reaction provides a powerful probe of $0^-$ excitations and highlight its potential for systematic studies of spin-isospin modes, including pion-related dynamics, in nuclei.

## Key claim

The parity-transfer (¹⁶O, ¹⁶F(0⁻,g.s.)) reaction selectively populates unnatural-parity states in nuclei through a 0⁺ → 0⁻ transition in the projectile, and the authors demonstrate this selectivity specifically for isovector 0⁻ excitations using the ¹²C target as a benchmark, with the caveat that the quantitative understanding of normalization factors for the 0⁻ component remains incomplete and requires future study.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory using a ¹⁶O beam at 247 MeV/u incident on a 1 mm-thick active ¹²C plastic scintillation target (103 mg/cm² equivalent thickness). The outgoing proton-unstable ¹⁶F was detected via its ¹⁵O + p decay products using the SHARAQ spectrometer operated in "separated flow" mode, with the spectrometer fixed at 0° and reaction angles up to θ_lab ≃ 1°. The excitation-energy spectrum of ¹²B was reconstructed from the coincident detection of the decay products, and the ¹⁶F(0⁻,g.s.) channel was isolated by fitting the relative energy E_rel distributions of the ¹⁵O + p system in each excitation-energy bin.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 247 MeV/u for the ¹⁶O projectile
- Target thickness: 103 mg/cm² (equivalent ¹²C thickness from 1 mm plastic scintillator)
- Energy resolution: 80 keV FWHM for the relative energy E_rel of the ¹⁵O + p system
- Excitation-energy resolution: 2.6 MeV FWHM for the ¹²B spectrum
- Known 0⁻ state observed at E_x = 9.3 MeV in ¹²B
- Structures observed at E_x = 6.6 ± 0.4 MeV and 14.8 ± 0.3 MeV in ¹²B
- Detection efficiency: 19% for the ¹⁵O + p coincidence events
- Beam intensity: 8 × 10⁶ particles per second (maximum allowed by radiation-safety regulations)
- Angular resolution: 3 mrad FWHM
- Normalization factors from DWBA+SM comparison: 1.1 for 1⁺, 1.0 for 2⁻, and 3.8 for 0⁻ components
- Cross-section ratio of the 0⁻ state at 9.3 MeV to the 1⁺ ground state reaches about 0.6 in the present reaction, compared to much smaller than 0.1 in the (d,²He) reaction

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to identify 0⁻ states using polarization observables in (p,n) reactions (Dozono 2008, Wakasa 2012) and (d,²He) reactions (Okamura 1995, Okamura 2002, De Huu 2007), which remained experimentally challenging despite their sensitivity to spin-parity. The parity-transfer reaction provides a more direct probe that selectively enhances the 0⁻ signal at forward angles, enabling systematic studies of spin-isospin modes including pion-related dynamics in nuclei. The results also address the long-standing controversy regarding the spin-parity of the 7.5 MeV structure in ¹²B observed in (n,p) reactions (Olsson 1993, Yang 1993) and (d,²He) reactions (Okamura 1995, Okamura 2002, De Huu 2007), suggesting it is mainly 1⁻ with a small 0⁻ admixture rather than the previously debated 2⁻ assignment.
