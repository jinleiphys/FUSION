# Neutron-quark stars: Discerning viable alternatives for the higher-density part of the equation of state of compact stars

> We investigate binary neutron star (BNS) mergers using general-relativistic numerical simulations with hadronic and hybrid equations of state (EOSs), incorporating the latest observations and theoretical constraints. We address two viable scenarios for the transition to quark matter: a quark-hadron crossover (QHC) or a strong first-order phase transition (1PT). To distinguish between different models, we define neutron-quark stars (NQS) as configurations where quark effects emerge at masses below the lowest observed neutron-star mass. While traditional "hybrid stars" may be distinguished by purely hadronic configurations through mass-radius measurements, the mass-radius relations of NQSs resemble those of purely hadronic models, with no sharp boundary between hadrons and quarks. The name NQS effectively captures the absence of a phase boundary between hadrons and quarks in QHC scenarios. Our results indicate that QHC models can be distinguished from hadronic ones if both the inspiral and postmerger gravitational waves (GWs) are observed. In particular, the dominant postmerger frequency ($f_2$) tends to be lower than in hadronic models with the same tidal deformability ($Λ$). We also present the first general-relativistic simulations of BNS mergers where the stars already contain quark matter before merging. These involve a strong first-order phase transition (1PT) at 1.8 times nuclear saturation density, followed by a stiff quark EOS. Finally, we identify a robust linear correlation between the total GW energy emitted after the merger and the $f_2$ frequency. Remarkably, this relation holds regardless of the quark presence.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that quark-hadron crossover (QHC) models can be distinguished from purely hadronic equations of state through combined inspiral and post-merger gravitational wave observations, specifically because the dominant post-merger frequency ($f_2$) tends to be lower than in hadronic models with the same tidal deformability ($\Lambda$). This claim is hedged by the condition that both inspiral and post-merger gravitational waves must be observed, and that the numerical uncertainty in $f_2$ is estimated at most 50 Hz.

## Method
The authors performed general-relativistic numerical simulations of binary neutron star mergers using hadronic equations of state (Togashi, ChEFTex, SLy, APR4, LS220) and hybrid equations of state incorporating either a quark-hadron crossover (QHC21 models with different baseline EOSs) or a strong first-order phase transition (1PT-NQS model starting at 1.8 times nuclear saturation density). Equal-mass binary systems were simulated at three total mass ranges (low: 2.5 $M_\odot$, intermediate: 2.75 $M_\odot$, high: 2.86-2.96 $M_\odot$), with thermal effects approximated using an ideal-gas treatment with fixed thermal index $\Gamma=2$. The post-merger gravitational wave spectra were analyzed using Markov Chain Monte Carlo fitting to extract the dominant $f_2$ frequency and its mean value $f_{2,\text{mean}}$.

## Key numbers
- The 1PT-NQS model has a phase transition starting at approximately 1.8 times nuclear saturation density ($1.8 n_0$) and ending at approximately 2.75 $n_0$, followed by a stiff quark EOS with $c_s^2 = 2/3 c^2$
- The numerical uncertainty in $f_{2,\text{mean}}$ due to finite resolution is at most 50 Hz (observed for QHC21Ch-soft-M1.375 model)
- QHC models with strongest stiffening lie more than 200 Hz below the best-fit curve for hadronic models in the $f_{2,\text{mean}}-\Lambda$ relation
- The 1PT-NQS models show $f_{2,\text{mean}}$ values about 300 Hz higher than their hadronic baseline
- The total GW energy emitted in the $f_2$-mode is approximately 0.02-0.08 $M_\odot c^2$
- The best-fit linear relation between post-merger GW energy and instantaneous frequency is: $E_{\text{GW,post}} = 4.308 \times 10^{-5} (f_{\text{GW,post}} - 1687)$ (in units of $M_\odot c^2$)
- The NICER radius estimate for PSR J0437-4715 is $11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63}$ km and for PSR J0030+0451 is $11.71^{+0.88}_{-0.83}$ km (ST+PDT model)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of binary neutron star mergers (Bauswein 2019, Most 2018, Huang 2022) by incorporating the latest multi-messenger constraints from NICER and GW170817, and by presenting the first general-relativistic simulations of mergers where stars already contain quark matter before merging. The paper addresses the "masquerade effect" identified by Alford (2004), where hybrid stars with quark cores can appear indistinguishable from conventional neutron stars in mass-radius measurements. The results enable future discrimination between hadronic and hybrid equations of state using third-generation gravitational wave detectors such as Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer, and NEMO.
