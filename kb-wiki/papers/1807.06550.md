# Jet modification with medium recoil in quark-gluon plasma

> Jet energy transported to quark-gluon plasma during jet-medium interaction excites the QGP medium and creates energetic thermal partons -- recoil particles or recoils. Modification of the jet structure in heavy ion collisions is studied using \textsc{martini}, in which recoil simulation is enabled. In large systems such as central Pb-Pb collisions, the recoil effect is expected to be critical due to strong jet-medium interaction. We show the results of the jet mass function and jet shape function are improved when the recoil particles are included in the reconstructed jets. We conclude that the energy carried by the recoil particles are regarded as a part of reconstructed jets and are necessary in studying jet modification in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including recoil particles (energetic thermal partons created when jet energy excites the quark-gluon plasma) as part of reconstructed jets is necessary to correctly describe jet modification in heavy ion collisions, but this claim is conditional on the recoil momentum cutoff being set to approximately 4 times the medium temperature.

## Method

The authors used the MARTINI Monte Carlo event generator, which implements in-medium parton showers using the AMY formalism for radiative energy loss combined with collisional processes, with the thermal medium provided by 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamics from MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions. Recoil partons were generated by adding momentum transfer and the momentum of a thermal parton sampled from the medium, with only those exceeding a kinematic cutoff of approximately 4T being promoted to recoil partons that can participate in further jet-medium interactions. The initial vacuum shower and hadronization were handled by PYTHIA 8.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadrons in 0-5% Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV is consistent with CMS data, especially at high p_T
- The recoil momentum cutoff is set to p_cut approximately 4T, where T is the temperature of the thermal medium
- Jet mass distributions are shown for 3 different jet p_T windows in central (0-10%) Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV
- The ratio of jet shape functions between 0-10% PbPb and pp collisions at 2.76 TeV shows an increasing trend up to Delta r = 1 when recoils are included

## Context

This work extends the MARTINI framework (Schenke, 2009) by implementing recoil simulation, building on the AMY formalism for radiative energy loss (Arnold, 2002) combined with collisional processes (Schenke, 2009). The results are compared to ALICE measurements of jet mass (Acharya, 2017) and CMS measurements of jet shape functions (Chatrchyan, 2013; Khachatryan, 2016). The study enables more complete descriptions of full jet reconstruction in heavy ion collisions by demonstrating that energy transferred to the thermal medium must be counted as part of the jet substructure.
