# Recent developments in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory: Selected topics

> I review recent results in baryon chiral perturbation theory, in particular related to pion-nucleon scattering and first systematic attempts to go beyond next-to-leading order in the case of three flavors. New insight into the chiral expansion of the baryon masses and magnetic moments is presented.

# Recent developments in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory: Selected topics

## Key claim
Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) can be systematically extended to next-to-leading order in the three-flavor sector, but the chiral expansion parameter for SU(3) is significantly larger (M_K/(4πF_K) ≈ 0.4) than for SU(2) (M_π/(4πF_π) ≈ 0.1), meaning calculations in SU(3) cannot be expected to be as precise as their SU(2) counterparts at the same order.

## Method
The author reviews the determination of dimension-two low-energy constants (LECs) from pion-nucleon scattering observables at one-loop order p^3, and presents the first systematic calculation of octet baryon masses and magnetic moments at order p^4 in three-flavor HBCHPT. For the baryon masses, ten symmetry-breaking LECs were estimated using resonance exchange extended to include loop diagrams with intermediate decuplet and Roper octet states, with three renormalization constants determined from baryon masses and the pion-nucleon sigma term. For magnetic moments, seven LECs related to symmetry breaking were left as free parameters and fitted to the octet magnetic moments, while three LECs related to scattering processes were estimated from resonance exchange.

## Key numbers
- Chiral expansion parameters: SU(2) M_π/(4πF_π) ≈ 0.1; SU(3) M_K/(4πF_K) ≈ 0.4
- Dimension-two LECs (c_i' = 2mc_i): c_1' = -1.7 ± 0.2, c_2' = 6.3 ± 0.4, c_3' = -9.9 ± 0.5, c_4' = 6.8, c_5' = -0.17, c_6 = 5.8, c_7 = -3.0 (all in appropriate units from Table 1)
- Octet baryon mass in chiral limit: m_chiral = 770 ± 110 MeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_πN(0) = 45 MeV, with chiral expansion 58.3 × (1 - 0.56 + 0.33) MeV
- Strangeness fraction: y = 0.21 ± 0.20; sigma-hat = 36 ± 7 MeV
- Proton magnetic moment chiral expansion: μ_p = 4.69 × (1 - 0.57 + 0.16) = 2.79 nuclear magnetons
- Neutron magnetic moment: μ_n = -2.85 × (1 - 0.36 + 0.03) = -1.91 nuclear magnetons
- Lambda-Sigma0 transition moment prediction: μ_{ΛΣ^0} = (1.51 ± 0.01) μ_N
- Threshold electric dipole amplitude for K^0 Σ^+ photoproduction: E_{0+}^{thr} = 1.07 × 10^{-3}/M_π
- Kaon loop contribution to neutral pion photoproduction threshold: E_{0+, thr}^K = 0.14 × 10^{-3}/M_{π+}
- N-Delta mass splitting: Δ = 293 MeV ≈ 3F_π

## Context
This review extends the two-flavor HBCHPT work of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) to the three-flavor sector, addressing the long-standing question of whether SU(3) chiral perturbation theory breaks down due to large kaon loop corrections. The baryon mass calculation at order p^4 contradicts the breakdown claim of Gasser (1981) and does not support the conjecture of Jenkins (1992) that only leading non-analytic corrections are large. The magnetic moment calculation at order p^4 resolves the apparent worsening of Coleman-Glashow relations seen in the p^3 calculation of Caldi and Pagels (1974), and the kaon photoproduction results provide a framework for analyzing upcoming ELSA data. The work enables systematic higher-order calculations in three-flavor baryon CHPT and establishes methods for estimating symmetry-breaking LECs via resonance exchange with loop corrections.
