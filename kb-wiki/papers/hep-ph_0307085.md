# Random phase approximation and its extension for the quantum O(2) anharmonic oscillator

> We apply the random phase approximation (RPA) and its extension called renormalized RPA to the quantum anharmonic oscillator with an O(2) symmetry. We first obtain the equation for the RPA frequencies in the standard and in the renormalized RPA approximations using the equation of motion method. In the case where the ground state has a broken symmetry, we check the existence of a zero frequency in the standard and in the renormalized RPA approximations. Then we use a time-dependent approach where the standard RPA frequencies are obtained as small oscillations around the static solution in the time-dependent Hartree-Bogoliubov equation. We draw a parallel between the two approaches.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both standard RPA and renormalized RPA preserve a zero-frequency Goldstone mode in the broken-symmetry phase of the quantum O(2) anharmonic oscillator, but the renormalized RPA achieves this with self-consistent occupation numbers rather than the HFB ground-state expectation values used in standard RPA.

## Method

The authors derive the RPA and renormalized RPA equations for the O(2) anharmonic oscillator using the equation-of-motion method of Rowe, where excitation operators are constructed from boson creation and annihilation operators. They also employ a time-dependent Hartree-Bogoliubov approach to obtain standard RPA frequencies as small oscillations around the static solution. The renormalized RPA differs from standard RPA by evaluating two-body expectation values in the true RPA vacuum rather than the HFB mean-field vacuum, requiring self-consistent determination of occupation numbers tau_1 and tau_2.

## Key numbers

- For mu=1 and g=1 in the symmetric phase, mean-field approximation gives ground state energy E_0=1.74015, first excited state E_1=3.90645, and second excited state E_2=6.07275, compared to exact numerical values E_0=1.7242, E_1=3.8304, and E_2=6.214
- The gap equation for the symmetric solution is omega^3 - mu*omega - 8g = 0
- In the broken-symmetry phase, the standard RPA frequency equation reduces to a 4x4 system with the determinant condition (omega^2 - Omega_nu^2)((omega+Omega) - Omega_nu^2 + 8g_{12}(omega+Omega)) - 16h_{12}^2*omega*(omega+Omega) = 0
- The renormalized RPA frequency equation has the same form as standard RPA but with Sigma replaced by Sigma_r, which contains the densities tau_1 and tau_2

## Context

This work extends the application of renormalized RPA, originally formulated by Rowe, from nuclear many-body theory to quantum field theoretic models. It builds on prior applications of RPA to the linear sigma model by Aouissat et al. (2000) and Aouissat et al. (2001), where standard RPA correctly gave a massless pion but produced an artifact first-order phase transition. The paper also references Stevenson (1984) for the mean-field equations and a scalar lambda-phi^4 field theory application by Aouissat et al. (2002). The quantum mechanical O(2) anharmonic oscillator serves as a testbed without divergences, enabling future comparison with exact numerical results and eventual application to the linear sigma model.
