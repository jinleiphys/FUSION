# Bottomonia in the Quark-Gluon Plasma and their Production at RHIC and LHC

> We study the production of bottomonium states in heavy-ion reactions at collider energies available at RHIC and LHC. We employ an earlier constructed rate equation approach which accounts for both suppression and regeneration mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronization phases of the evolving thermal medium. Our previous predictions utilizing two limiting cases of strong and weak bottomonium binding in the QGP are updated by (i) checking the compatibility of the pertinent spectral functions with lattice-QCD results for euclidean correlators, (ii) adapting the initial conditions of the rate equation by updating bottom-related input cross sections and the charged-particle multiplicity of the fireball, and (iii) converting our calculations into observables as recently measured by the STAR and CMS experiments. Our main findings are a preference for strong $Υ$ binding as well as a significant regeneration component at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper finds a preference for the "Strong Binding Scenario" (SBS) for bottomonium in the quark-gluon plasma, where bound states retain vacuum binding energies throughout the QGP, and identifies a significant regeneration component at LHC energies, contrary to the common assumption that bottomonium regeneration is negligible.

## Method

The authors employ a rate-equation approach that accounts for both suppression and regeneration of bottomonium states in an expanding thermal fireball, using two limiting scenarios for in-medium binding energies: the Weak Binding Scenario (WBS) with quasifree dissociation and the Strong Binding Scenario (SBS) with gluo-dissociation. They check the compatibility of the resulting spectral functions with lattice-QCD results for euclidean correlators, update initial conditions using recent bottom-related cross sections and charged-particle multiplicity measurements, and convert calculations into observables comparable to STAR and CMS data.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=0.2$ TeV), the initial QGP temperature is $T_0=330$ MeV with formation time $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/$c$ for central Au-Au collisions
- At LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV), the initial QGP temperature is $T_0=610$ MeV with formation time $\tau_0=0.2$ fm/$c$ for central Pb-Pb collisions
- The total entropy is $S=10000$ for $N_{\rm part}=375$ at RHIC and $S=22000$ for $N_{\rm part}=375$ at LHC, yielding $dN_{\rm ch}/dy=800$ and $1750$ respectively
- The $\Upsilon$(1S) lifetime in the SBS at $T_c$ is almost two orders of magnitude larger than its WBS counterpart
- The inclusive $\Upsilon$ production cross section at LHC is $\sigma_{pp\to \Upsilon}^{\rm tot}(\sqrt{s}=2.76\,{\rm TeV},|y|<0.9)\simeq(75\pm8)$ nb
- The total $b\bar b$ cross section at LHC is $\sigma_{pp\to b\bar b}(\sqrt{s}=2.76\,{\rm TeV},|y|<0.9)\simeq(43\pm8)$ $\mu$b
- The $b$-quark kinetic relaxation time is taken as $\tau_{\rm eq}\simeq10$ fm/$c$ around $T\simeq2\,T_c$

## Context

This work extends the rate-equation approach of Grandchamp (2005) for bottomonium production at RHIC and LHC by incorporating constraints from lattice-QCD euclidean correlator calculations by Jakovac (2006) and Aarts (2010, 2011), which favor the Strong Binding Scenario over the Weak Binding Scenario previously considered more realistic. The updated calculations enable direct comparison with recent STAR and CMS measurements, and the finding of significant regeneration at LHC challenges the conventional view that bottomonium serves as a pristine probe of quarkonium dissolution without regeneration complications.
