# Measurements of ${}^{nat}Pb(p,xn){}^{201-207}Bi$, ${}^{204}Pb(p,1-4n){}^{201-204}Bi$ and ${}^{206}Pb(p,3n){}^{204}Bi$ cross-sections at astrophysical energies ($E_p \leq 30 \, MeV$)

> Cross-sections for ${}^{nat}Pb(p,xn){}^{201-207}Bi$, ${}^{204}Pb(p,1-4n){}^{201-204}Bi$ and ${}^{206}Pb(p,3n){}^{204}Bi$ reactions have been determined in the astrophysical energy range $20-30 \, MeV$. The analysis were performed by $γ$-spectroscopy associated with half-lives measurements. The results were compared with previously experimental data, when available, and with theoretical calculations performed using TALYS code. We report a possible new $γ$ transition from ${}^{204m}{Pb}$ and other theoretical discrepancies, probably due overestimation of the Coulomb barrier and neutron binding energy. Also reported are new determinations of the energies and intensities of the $γ$'s emitted in the decays of ${}^{205,206}Bi$. We discuss possible implications for the r/rp-processes in neutrino-driven wind in supernovae and neutron stars mergers.

## Key claim

The paper reports new cross-section measurements for proton-induced reactions on lead isotopes in the 20-30 MeV energy range, claiming improved accuracy (uncertainties of 1-5%) compared to previous works (which had uncertainties of approximately 10%), but notes that theoretical calculations using the TALYS code systematically overestimate the cross-sections and require an energy shift of about 4 MeV to match experimental data, likely due to incorrect estimation of the Coulomb barrier and neutron binding energies for deformed lead nuclei.

## Method

Natural lead foils (99.9% purity, less than 0.5 mm thickness) were irradiated with protons from 18 to 30 MeV at the IPEN/CNEN-SP cyclotron facility. Gamma spectroscopy with an HPGe detector (resolution 2 keV at 1332 keV) was performed continuously after irradiation, with spectra saved every 30 minutes to follow half-lives from 57.5 minutes to 31.55 years. Cross-sections were extracted from the gamma spectra using peak fitting with Gaussian shapes and tail corrections, with half-life analysis used to separate overlapping gamma lines from different bismuth isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Cross-section for natPb(p,xn)207Bi at 25.0 MeV: 859 (14) mb (first measurement below 30 MeV for this reaction)
- Cross-section for natPb(p,xn)204Bi at 30.0 MeV: 152 (7) mb
- Cross-section for natPb(p,xn)206Bi at 29.0 MeV: 755 (11) mb
- Cross-section for 204Pb(p,4n)201Bi at 30.0 MeV: 24.929 (18) mb (first measurement at threshold energy)
- Cross-section for 206Pb(p,3n)204Bi at 29.0 MeV: 1215 (42) mb
- Cross-section for 204Pb(p,n)204Bi at 20.0 MeV: 200.71 (15) mb
- Cross-section for natPb(p,xn)203Bi at 25.0 MeV: 14.1 (5) mb
- Neutron separation energy of 205Bi: 8.490 MeV
- Detector resolution at 1332 keV: 2 keV
- Uncertainty contributions: detector efficiency and counting statistics less than 10% combined; proton flux, sample mass, energy resolution, half-life and beta decay branching contribute less than 5%

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by Ditroi (2014), Kuhnhenn (2001), Lagunas-Solar (1987), and Bell (1956), which had discrepancies of about a factor of 2 among themselves, by providing cross-sections with uncertainties of 1-5% (a factor greater than 5 improvement in accuracy). The results enable testing of TALYS 1.9 code simulations and TENDL-19 libraries for (p,xn) reactions on lead, and are relevant for nucleosynthesis models of the rp-process and r2p-process in neutrino-driven winds from supernovae and neutron star mergers, as proposed by Woosley (1992) and Guillaumon (2020). The new gamma intensity measurements for 205,206Bi are compared with Manthuruthil (1972) and Hamilton (1972), and the possible new gamma transition from 204mPb has implications for nuclear structure data and astrophysical models.
