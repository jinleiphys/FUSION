# Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the fermion--gauge-boson vertex

> We present a workable model for the fermion-photon vertex, which is expressed solely in terms of functions that appear in the fermion propagator and independent of the angle between the relative momenta, and does not explicitly depend on the covariant-gauge parameter. It nevertheless produces a critical coupling for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking that is practically independent of the covariant-gauge parameter and an anomalous magnetic moment distribution for the dressed fermion that agrees in important respects with realistic numerical solutions of the inhomogeneous vector Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key claim

The authors present a model for the fermion-photon vertex that is expressed solely in terms of functions appearing in the fermion propagator and independent of the angle between relative momenta, yet it produces a critical coupling for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking that is practically independent of the covariant-gauge parameter. The hedge is that this gauge-independence is achieved only when all amplitudes are retained in the vertex, and the model's anomalous magnetic moment distribution agrees "in important respects" (not exactly) with numerical solutions of the inhomogeneous vector Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Method

The authors construct an eight-parameter Ansatz for the transverse part of the fermion-photon vertex, expressed in the Ball-Chiu tensor basis, with coefficient functions built from the fermion propagator's scalar functions A(p^2) and B(p^2). They impose constraints from one-loop perturbation theory, multiplicative renormalisability, and the one-loop anomalous magnetic moment, then solve the linearised gap equation near the bifurcation point for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in quenched QED. The parameters are tuned to minimise the gauge-parameter dependence of the critical coupling alpha_c over the range xi in [0,10], using the Ball-Chiu vertex result at Landau gauge as the reference value.

## Key numbers

* Critical coupling for DCSB in Landau gauge: alpha_c(0) = 0.934
* Mass anomalous dimension in Landau gauge: gamma_m = 1.058
* Anomalous magnetic moment at the Euclidean constituent fermion mass: kappa = 0.45-0.55
* Optimised vertex parameters: a_1 = 0, a_2 = 3.4, a_4 = 6, a_6 = -1/2, a_5 = -4/3, a_3 = 1, a_7 = -1/3, a_8 = -3.7

## Context

This work extends the line of vertex Ansatz construction following Ball and Chiu (1980) and Curtis and Pennington (1990), which previously overlooked the role of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in building the fermion-gauge-boson vertex. The authors note that the Curtis-Pennington vertex is unable to explain the rho-a_1 meson mass splitting, and that inclusion of a dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment is required, as shown by Chang (2010, 2011). The new Ansatz enables more reliable computation of hadron electromagnetic form factors and can be used in nonperturbative studies of truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations, though the authors caution that no Ansatz can fully capture spectral strength from the two-pion continuum and rho-meson pole region.
