# How far is normal nuclear matter from the chiral symmetry restoration?

> Properties of cold nuclear matter are studied within a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model formulated on the level of constituent nucleons. The model parameters are chosen to reproduce simultaneously the observed nucleon and pion masses in vacuum as well as saturation properties of nuclear matter. The strongest constraints on these parameters are given by the empirical values of the nucleon effective mass and compression modulus at nuclear saturation density. A preferable value of the cut-off momentum, determining density of active quasinucleon states in the Dirac sea, is estimated to about 400 MeV/c. With the most reasonable choice of model parameters we have found a first order phase transition of the liquid-gas type at subsaturation densities and the gradual restoration of chiral symmetry at about 3 times the saturation density. Fluctuations of the scalar condensate around its mean-field value are estimated and shown to be large in the vicinity of chiral transition.

## Key claim

Cold nuclear matter can be described within a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model formulated at the level of constituent nucleons, but only if the cutoff momentum is chosen around 0.4 GeV/c, which is significantly lower than the values typically used in quark-based NJL models. The chiral symmetry restoration is gradual rather than sharp, occurring at about three times saturation density, and the authors note that fluctuations of the scalar condensate become large near the chiral transition, indicating the mean-field approximation breaks down there.

## Method

The authors constructed a generalized NJL model with nucleonic degrees of freedom, including scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, and scalar-vector (8-fermion) interaction terms, plus an explicit bare nucleon mass. They solved the model in the mean-field (Hartree) approximation at zero temperature, regularizing divergent Dirac-sea integrals with a three-dimensional momentum cutoff Lambda. Model parameters (cutoff Lambda, bare mass m0, scalar coupling GS, vector coupling GV, and scalar-vector coupling G_{SV}) were fixed by simultaneously reproducing the vacuum nucleon and pion masses, the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation, and the nuclear saturation point (binding energy 16 MeV per nucleon at density 0.17 fm^{-3}).

## Key numbers

* Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}, with binding energy per nucleon of 16 MeV at that density.
* Preferred cutoff momentum: Lambda = 0.4 GeV/c, giving nucleon effective mass m_N^*(rho_0)/m_N = 0.683 and compression modulus K(rho_0) = 285 MeV.
* Critical density for chiral symmetry restoration: rho_c / rho_0 = 2.66 for Lambda = 0.4 GeV, with the scaling relation rho_c approx 0.06 Lambda^3.
* Pion-nucleon sigma term at zero density: Sigma_{pi N} = 42.1 MeV for Lambda = 0.4 GeV.
* Bare nucleon mass for Lambda = 0.4 GeV: m_0 = 41.3 MeV.
* Vacuum scalar condensate for Lambda = 0.4 GeV: |rho_S^{vac}| = 0.54 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to apply the NJL model to nuclear matter, particularly addressing the failure of standard NJL models to reproduce saturation properties found by Koch et al. (1987) and Buballa (1996). The authors show that by using a lower cutoff (Lambda approx 0.4 GeV instead of the typical 0.6 GeV) and including scalar-vector interactions, they can match the saturation point while also predicting chiral restoration. The results are compared with the Walecka-type TM1 model (Sugahara and Toki, 1994), the Chiral Hadron Model (Papazoglou et al., 1999), and the Variational Chain Model (Akmal et al., 1998), showing similar behavior up to about twice saturation density. The work enables future studies of chiral fluctuations and their role near the restoration density, and highlights the need to include quark degrees of freedom explicitly at momenta above the cutoff.
