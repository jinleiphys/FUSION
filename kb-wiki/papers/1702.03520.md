# The $1/N_c$ expansion in hadron effective field theory

> We study the $N_c$ scalings of pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon scatterings and find that a consistent large $N_c$ counting can be established if we assume Witten's counting rules are applied to matrix elements or scattering amplitudes which use the relativistic normalization for the nucleons. With the pionless effective field theory, we also find that the $S$-wave nucleon-nucleon interaction is so strong that it should be treated nonperturbatively at the leading order in the $1/N_c$ expansion. By summing all the leading order diagrams, we find that the deuteron binding energy is of order $1/N_c$. In contrast, meson-meson interaction is so weak that loosely-bound meson-meson molecular states may not exist in the large $N_c$ limit.

## Key claim
A consistent large \(N_c\) counting in hadron effective field theory can be established if Witten's counting rules are applied to matrix elements or scattering amplitudes that use the relativistic normalization for nucleons, rather than the nonrelativistic normalization; this resolves the large \(N_c\) nuclear potential puzzle and yields a deuteron binding energy of order \(1/N_c\).

## Method
The author assumes Witten's large \(N_c\) counting rules (meson-baryon coupling \(\sim \sqrt{N_c}\), meson-baryon scattering \(\sim N_c^0\), baryon-baryon scattering \(\sim N_c\)) apply to amplitudes with relativistic normalization, where each external nucleon line carries a factor \(\sqrt{2m_N}\) (with \(m_N \sim N_c\)). This leads to \(g_A \sim N_c^0\) and a nucleon-nucleon potential of order \(N_c^{-1}\). The analysis is carried out using chiral Lagrangians for pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions, and a pionless effective field theory for \(S\)-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering, summing all leading-order diagrams nonperturbatively.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron binding energy \(B\) is of order \(1/N_c\); in the pionless effective field theory, \(B = 16\pi^2 / (C_0^2 m_N^3)\), where \(C_0 \sim N_c^{-1}\) and \(m_N \sim N_c\).
- The nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\) is of order \(N_c^0\) (i.e., \(\mathcal{O}(1)\)) under the relativistic normalization assumption.
- The one-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude is proportional to \((m_N g_A / f_\pi)^2\) and is of order \(N_c\) (with \(f_\pi \sim \sqrt{N_c}\)).
- The two-pion exchange crossed-box amplitude is proportional to \(m_N^2 (g_A / f_\pi)^4\) and is of order \(N_c^0\), suppressed by \(1/N_c\) relative to the leading amplitude.
- The binding energy of an \(S\)-wave meson-meson molecular state scales as \(N_c^2\) (if it exists), but the meson-meson interaction is weak and only the tree-level diagram contributes at leading order.

## Context
This work extends the large \(N_c\) analysis of QCD initiated by 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979) to hadron effective field theory, addressing the "large \(N_c\) nuclear potential puzzle" identified by Belitsky (2002). It proposes a resolution corresponding to the second possibility in Belitsky (2002): that the assumption of an \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\) nucleon-nucleon potential may be invalid. The result that the deuteron binding energy is \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c)\) contrasts with the earlier analysis of Beane (2002), which found \(B \sim N_c^{-5}\) by treating \(g_A \sim N_c\) and using a different scaling for nucleon momenta. The work enables a consistent framework for applying the \(1/N_c\) expansion to low-energy nuclear phenomena and suggests that loosely-bound meson-meson molecular states do not exist in the large \(N_c\) limit.
