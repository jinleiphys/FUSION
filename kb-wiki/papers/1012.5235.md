# Nucleon polarizabilities and Delta-resonance magnetic moment in chiral EFT

> Recent chiral EFT calculations of nucleon polarizabilities reveal a problem in the current empirical determination of proton's electromagnetic polarizabilities. We also report on the progress in the empirical determination of the $Δ$(1232)-resonance magnetic moment in the process of $γp \to p π^0 γ'$ measured at MAMI.

## Key claim

Chiral EFT calculations of nucleon polarizabilities disagree with the Particle Data Group (PDG) values, but this discrepancy arises not from differences in theoretical approaches but because the experimental extraction from the TAPS data appears to have an unreliable error estimate; the cross-section data do not actually prefer the PDG values over the chiral EFT values.

## Method

The author compares next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) calculations of proton Compton scattering cross sections and polarizabilities with dispersion-relation (DR) calculations and with experimental data from the A2/TAPS Collaboration at MAMI. For the Delta-resonance magnetic dipole moment, the author analyzes radiative pion photoproduction (gamma p to p pi0 gamma') data from the Crystal Ball at MAMI experiment using a next-to-leading order chiral EFT calculation in the delta-counting scheme, and proposes a new backward-forward asymmetry observable that depends linearly on the Delta magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- PDG values for proton polarizabilities: alpha = (12.0 +/- 0.6) x 10^-4 fm^3, beta = (1.9 +/- 0.5) x 10^-4 fm^3
- NNLO BChPT calculation yields: alpha = (10.8 +/- 0.7) x 10^-4 fm^3, beta = (4.0 +/- 0.7) x 10^-4 fm^3
- The uncertainty of +/- 0.7 x 10^-4 fm^3 in both alpha and beta corresponds to the cyan band around the BChPT cross-section curves
- The chiral EFT calculation for pion photoproduction works in a region of 50 MeV around the Delta resonance

## Context

This work extends chiral EFT calculations of nucleon polarizabilities by Lensky (2008, 2009) and Delta magnetic moment studies by Pascalutsa and Vanderhaeghen (2004, 2007). It reveals a problem with the empirical extraction of proton polarizabilities from the TAPS experiment at MAMI (2001), which used the dispersion-relation model of L'vov (1980). The results enable future polarized Compton scattering experiments at the HIgammaS facility (Ahmed, 2008) to independently determine alpha and beta, and motivate improved measurements of the Delta magnetic moment through the proposed backward-forward asymmetry observable.
