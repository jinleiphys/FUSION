# Hydrodynamic event-plane correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 A TeV

## Key claim

The measured correlations between flow angles of different harmonic orders in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC are shown to be of hydrodynamic origin, but the paper explicitly states that neither the MC-Glauber nor the MC-KLN initial condition models provide a quantitatively satisfactory description of the experimental data, and that the correlation strength is sensitive to both the initial conditions and the shear viscosity of the expanding fireball medium.

## Method

The authors used the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 to evolve 11,000 fluctuating initial energy density profiles per centrality class from both MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 A TeV. They computed final-state event-plane angles Psi_n from charged hadron distributions using the same pseudorapidity range (0.5 < |eta| < 2.5) and lower pT cutoff (pT > 0.5 GeV) as the ATLAS experiment, and compared these to initial-state participant-plane angles Phi_n calculated from the initial energy density profiles. The specific shear viscosity was set to eta/s=0.08 for MC-Glauber and eta/s=0.2 for MC-KLN initial conditions, tuned to reproduce pT spectra and elliptic flows.

## Key numbers

- 11,000 events per centrality class were evolved for each of the two initial condition models (MC-Glauber and MC-KLN)
- Specific shear viscosity values used: eta/s = 0.08 for MC-Glauber initial conditions; eta/s = 0.2 for MC-KLN initial conditions
- Lower pT cutoff for charged hadron analysis: pT > 0.5 GeV
- Pseudorapidity range for event-plane reconstruction: 0.5 < |eta| < 2.5
- Hadron resonance decays included for masses up to 2.25 GeV
- The specific shear viscosity of the QGP is reported as eta/s = O(1/4pi) from prior work cited in the paper

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic analysis of anisotropic flow coefficients v_n (studied by Song (2010), Adare (2011), Schenke (2011), and Qiu (2011)) to correlations between event-plane angles of different harmonic orders, which were recently measured with good precision by the ATLAS Collaboration (Jia, 2012). The paper demonstrates that these correlations provide an independent constraint on the specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, complementing constraints from v_n magnitudes alone. The results suggest that a combined analysis of both flow coefficients and event-plane correlations can yield powerful constraints on initial state models, and that nonlinear mode-coupling during hydrodynamic evolution (first identified by Qiu (2011)) is essential for reproducing the qualitative features of the data.
