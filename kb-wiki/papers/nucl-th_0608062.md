# Nucleon sigma term and quark condensate in nuclear matter

> We study the bound nucleon sigma term and the quark condensate in nuclear matter. In the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model the nuclear correction to the sigma term is small and negative, i.e., it decelerates the decrease of the quark condensate in nuclear matter. However, the quark condensate in nuclear matter is controlled primarily by the scalar-isoscalar $σ$ field. Compared to the leading term, it moderates the decrease more than that of the nuclear sigma term alone at densities around and larger than the normal nuclear matter density.

## Key claim

The nuclear correction to the bound nucleon sigma term is small and negative, which decelerates the decrease of the quark condensate in nuclear matter, but the quark condensate is controlled primarily by the scalar-isoscalar sigma field, which moderates the decrease more than the nuclear sigma term alone at densities around and larger than normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model in two versions (QMC-I and QMC-II with parameter set B) to calculate the density dependence of the nuclear correction to the nucleon sigma term, defined via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem applied to the binding energy per nucleon. They then computed the nuclear matter to vacuum quark condensate ratio using the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GOR) relation, incorporating both the bound nucleon sigma term and the full expression involving the scalar-isoscalar sigma mean field. The calculations used standard parameters: current quark mass m_q = 5 MeV and free nucleon bag radius R_N = 0.8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon sigma term: sigma_N_free = 45 MeV (empirical value from Gasser)
- Saturation binding energy per nucleon: epsilon_B = -15.7 MeV at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- The nuclear correction to the sigma term (delta_sigma_N^*) is negative and small in magnitude for both QMC-I and QMC-II at densities up to and beyond rho_0

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium nucleon properties using the QMC model (QMC-I from 1995, QMC-II from 1998) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Bentz). The negative nuclear correction to the sigma term agrees with a result by Gammal based on the Skyrme model with two-body N-N interaction. The study contrasts with earlier qualitative estimates by Li and Brockmann of the m_q dependence of meson masses in N-N interactions. The results enable more precise calculations of how the quark condensate behaves in nuclear matter, with implications for understanding chiral symmetry restoration at finite density.
