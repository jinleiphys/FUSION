# Proton rms-radii from low-q power expansions?

> Several recent publications claim that the proton charge {\em rms}-radius resulting from the analysis of electron scattering data restricted to {\em low} momentum transfer agrees with the radius determined from muonic hydrogen, in contrast to the radius resulting from analyses of the full (e,e) data set which is $0.04fm$ larger. Here we show why these publications erroneously arrive at the low radii.

# Proton rms-radii from low-q power expansions?

## Key claim

The authors argue that recent publications claiming the proton charge rms-radius from low-momentum-transfer electron scattering agrees with the muonic hydrogen value (0.8409 fm) are erroneous; when the higher moments of the charge density (which are non-zero and known to be large) are properly accounted for, the low-q fits actually yield radii in the 0.88 fm region, consistent with standard analyses of the full data set.

## Method

The authors analyze the world electron-proton scattering data (603 cross section and polarization transfer points up to 10 fm^-1, corrected for two-photon exchange effects) using a Fourier transform of Laguerre functions of order 11 for both G_e and G_m, which provides controlled behavior at large radii and moments insensitive to the cutoff in the number of terms. They then repeat the low-q analyses of Higinbotham et al. (2016), Griffioen et al. (2016), and Horbatsch and Hessels (2016), but incorporate the higher moments (particularly ⟨r^4⟩) determined from the full high-q data set, showing that the originally reported low radii result from neglecting or poorly constraining these moments.

## Key numbers

- The proton charge rms-radius from standard analyses of the full (e,e) data set is 0.879 ± 0.009 fm (Arrington, 2015).
- The muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement gives 0.8409 ± 0.0004 fm (Pohl et al., 2010), representing a ≈ 5σ discrepancy.
- Higinbotham et al. (2016) reported R = 0.844 ± 0.014 fm from a linear q^2 fit with q_max = 0.9 fm^-1; repeating the analysis with added q^4 and q^6 contributions from high-q data yields R = 0.899 fm.
- Griffioen et al. (2016) reported R = 0.850 ± 0.019 fm from data with q < 0.72 fm^-1; using the better-determined a_4 from high-q data yields R = 0.877 ± 0.008 fm.
- The value of ⟨r^4⟩ from the Laguerre-function fit to world data is 2.01 ± 0.05 fm^4 (consistent with SOG giving 2.03 fm^4 and VDM giving 2.01 fm^4).
- For q_max = 0.9 fm^-1, the q^4-term contributes 10-15% of the finite size effect at the upper limit of the q-range.
- To neglect the q^4-term and maintain 1% accuracy in R would require q_max < 0.35 fm^-1, where the finite size effect is < 0.015 and experimental error bars yield huge uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Kraus et al. (2014) that low-order power-series fits to pseudo-data with known R produce systematically low values, and builds on Sick (2003) which identified problems with power-series expansions of proton form factors due to the rapid growth of higher moments ⟨r^(2n)⟩. The paper directly critiques the low-q analyses of Higinbotham et al. (2016), Griffioen et al. (2016), and Horbatsch and Hessels (2016), showing that their claimed agreement with the muonic hydrogen radius is an artifact of truncating the power series. The result enables a resolution of the proton radius puzzle by demonstrating that electron scattering analyses, when properly accounting for higher moments, consistently yield radii near 0.88 fm, maintaining the discrepancy with muonic hydrogen.
