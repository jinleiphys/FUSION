# The structure of the exact effective action and the quark confinement in MSSM QCD

> An expression for the exact (nonperturbative) effective action of $N$=1 supersymmetric gauge theories is proposed, supposing, that all particles except for the gauge bosons are massive. Analysis of its form shows, that instanton effects in the supersymmetric theories can lead to the quark confinement. The typical scale of confinement in MSSM QCD, calculated from the first principles, is in agreement with the experimental data. The proposed explanation is quite different from the dual Higgs mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hypothetical expression for the exact nonperturbative effective action of N=1 supersymmetric gauge theories (assuming all particles except gauge bosons are massive) and argues that instanton effects in such theories can lead to quark confinement, with the confinement scale in MSSM QCD calculated from first principles agreeing with experimental data. The mechanism is explicitly stated to be quite different from the dual Higgs mechanism and relies on the existence of the auxiliary field D in supersymmetric theories.

## Method

The author constructs the low-energy effective action for N=1 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with matter by summing instanton corrections, using the requirement that the holomorphic part of the effective action must satisfy renorminvariance and positivity of the effective charge. The parameter z in the effective action is derived from the one-loop beta-function and instanton measure, incorporating contributions from massive particles (gluinos, quarks, squarks) and massless gauge fields through the chiral superfield B = (D^a)^2 - (1/2)(F^a_mu nu)^2 - (i/2) F^a_mu nu tilde F^a_mu nu. The confinement mechanism is then analyzed by examining the bosonic part of the effective action as a function of the auxiliary field D and the electric field strength.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop beta-function coefficient for the theory is beta(e) = - (e^3)/(16 pi^2) (3 N_c - N_f), with contributions c_gauge = (11/3) N_c, c_lambda = -(2/3) N_c, c_q = -(2/3) N_f, c_sq = -(1/3) N_f.
- The extremum condition for the effective action occurs at a(b_0) = z^{-1/4}(b_0) = 4/pi.
- For MSSM QCD (N_c=3, N_f=6) with the coupling constant e^2 approx 1/2 at the Grand Unification scale M = 2 x 10^16 GeV, and setting all superpartner masses to 10-100 GeV, the confinement scale is estimated as Lambda_c approx 0.09 - 0.46 GeV.
- The confinement scale expression is Lambda_c = M e^{-16 pi^2 / 11} (m_q^4 m_lambda^2 m_sq^2 / M^8)^{1/11}.

## Context

This work extends the Seiberg-Witten (1994) exact solution for N=2 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory to the N=1 supersymmetric case relevant to the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model, proposing a confinement mechanism that differs fundamentally from the dual Higgs mechanism of Mandelstam (1976). The approach contrasts with earlier nonperturbative effective actions proposed by Affleck, Seiberg (1994), and others that did not lead to confinement. The paper enables first-principles calculation of the confinement scale in MSSM QCD, though the author notes the proposed effective action remains a hypothesis requiring confirmation through explicit instanton calculations similar to Yung (1998).
