# Residual interaction in Second RPA with density-dependent forces. Rearrangement terms

> We derive the expression for the residual interaction to be used in the framework of the Second RPA with density-dependent forces. The adopted procedure is based on a variational approach. It is found that the residual interaction to be used in RPA and beyond RPA matrix elements is not the same as far as the rearrangement terms are concerned. A detailed derivation of the matrix elements coupling 1 particle-1 hole with 2 particle-2 hole and 2 particle-2 hole among themselves has been done and the corresponding rearrangement terms are shown here. This formal result indicates that both the currently used prescriptions, namely (i) using the same type of rearrangement terms in RPA and beyond RPA matrix elements or (ii) neglecting the rearrangement terms in beyond RPA matrix elements, are not correct.

## Key claim

The residual interaction used in Second RPA matrix elements with density-dependent forces must differ from the standard RPA residual interaction regarding rearrangement terms; both common prescriptions, using the same rearrangement terms in RPA and beyond-RPA matrix elements, or neglecting rearrangement terms entirely in beyond-RPA matrix elements, are incorrect.

## Method

The authors derive the Second RPA equations variationally, following the approach of Providencia (1965), by expressing the ground state as an exponential of a linear superposition of 1p-1h and 2p-2h configurations built on the Hartree-Fock state. They expand the density-dependent interaction to second order in the density around the HF density and evaluate the second derivatives of the Hamiltonian expectation value with respect to the configuration coefficients to obtain the A and B matrices for SRPA, isolating the rearrangement contributions.

## Key numbers

* The single-particle energies in the RPA A11 matrix contain a rearrangement contribution: ε_ab = λ_ab(ρ^(0)) + (1/2) Σ_{kk'} [δV̂_{kk'kk'}/δρ_ba]_{ρ=ρ^(0)} ρ_ba (Eq. 19).
* The residual interaction in the RPA A11 matrix includes four terms: the bare interaction V̂_{mkip}(ρ^(0)) plus three rearrangement terms involving first and second derivatives of the interaction with respect to density (Eq. 20).
* The A22 matrix contains rearrangement terms only from the first term of the Hamiltonian expectation value, given by Eq. (21), which involve Kronecker deltas and first derivatives of the interaction.
* The A12 matrix has a single rearrangement term: A_{mi,pqkl}^(rearr) = [δV̂_{klpq}/δρ_{im}]_{ρ=ρ^(0)} ρ_{im} (Eq. 22).
* The B12 matrix has a non-zero rearrangement term: B_{mi,pqkl}^(rearr) = [δV̂_{klpq}/δρ_{mi}]_{ρ=ρ^(0)} ρ_{mi} (Eq. 23), unlike the two-body non-density-dependent case where B12 is zero.
* The B22 matrix remains zero even with density-dependent forces.

## Context

This work extends the variational derivation of Second RPA by Providencia (1965) to the case of density-dependent forces, addressing a gap in the literature. It corrects the prescription introduced by Adachi and Yoshida (1978) for rearrangement terms in beyond-RPA matrix elements, which did not explicitly include 2p-2h degrees of freedom and only considered the coupling between 1p-1h and 2p-2h configurations. The formal results enable future quantitative studies of the rearrangement terms in Eqs. (22) and (23) for realistic calculations with Skyrme or Gogny forces, and clarify the correct treatment of residual interactions for applications to giant resonances, low-lying states in magic nuclei, and double phonon excitations.
