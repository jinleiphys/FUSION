# Simulating lattice fermion doubling with a Floquet drive

> We consider a recently discovered mathematical correspondence between the spectra of a naively discretized lattice fermion and that of a periodically driven (i.e., Floquet) quantum system and enhance it into an infrared equivalence between the two systems. The equivalence can be framed as a duality relation, allowing us to simulate a two-flavor discrete-time fermion theory on the lattice side, where the two flavors arise from time discretization, using a single-flavor fermion theory on the Floquet side. Our demonstration establishes an equivalence between (i) the fermion content, (ii) the correlation functions, and consequently (iii) observables of the two theories in the infrared, going substantially beyond the previously discovered spectral equivalence. We also show how interactions may be incorporated into this enhanced infrared equivalence.

## Key claim
The paper establishes an infrared (IR) equivalence, not merely a spectral correspondence, between a two-flavor discrete-time lattice fermion theory (where the two flavors arise from time discretization) and a single-flavor Floquet-driven fermion system, provided one considers the doubly driven Floquet model and restricts to the long-wavelength, low-energy limit.

## Method
The authors start from a singly driven Floquet model (a periodically driven SSH-type Hamiltonian on a 1D spatial lattice) whose quasienergy spectrum was previously shown to match the discrete-time spectrum of a naively discretized Dirac fermion. They then construct a doubly driven Floquet model by repeating the drive cycle twice, which folds the π-paired quasienergy bands into degeneracy, producing an effective two-flavor fermion theory. By explicit change-of-basis transformations and by computing the Feynman propagators in the long-wavelength limit (|k| ≪ 1 and t ≫ T^(2)), they show that the correlation functions of the two theories coincide. They further demonstrate that tree-level two-to-two scattering amplitudes can be matched by identifying the coupling constants, with commutator contributions vanishing at low momenta.

## Key numbers
- The drive periods satisfy T^(2) = 2 T^(1), with T^(1) = t₀ + t₁ and t₀ = π/4 chosen to enforce π-pairing in the singly driven spectrum.
- The quasienergy eigenvalues of the singly driven system are given by ε₊^(1)(k) T^(1) = i log(λ₊(k)), where λ₊(k) = Q(k) − i√(1 − Q²(k)) and Q(k) = cos(2t₀)cos(2t₁) − cos(2k) sin(2t₀) sin(2t₁).
- For the doubly driven system, ε₊^(2)(k) T^(2) = 2 ε₊^(1) T^(1) mod 2π, and in the gapless limit (η = 0, m = 0) the dispersion becomes T^(2) ε^(2)(k)|_{η=0} = 4|k| for −π/4 ≤ k < π/4 and 4|k − π/2| for π/4 ≤ k < 3π/4.
- The tree-level scattering amplitude on the lattice side scales as λ (2/T^(2) sin⁻¹(m T^(2)/2))², while the Floquet-side amplitude from S_int scales as g (δT/˜T) (2/˜T sin⁻¹(m T^(2)/2))², leading to the identification g δT/˜T (T^(2)/˜T)² ∼ g δT/T^(2) with λ.

## Context
This work extends the spectral equivalence previously discovered by Iadecola et al. (2023) between a Floquet quasienergy spectrum and a discrete-time Dirac spectrum, elevating it to a full IR duality that includes particle content, correlation functions, and observables. The result enables the simulation of a two-flavor lattice fermion theory (with fermion doubling from time discretization) using a single-flavor Floquet system, which may be more amenable to experimental realization or quantum simulation. The authors note that while they restrict to 1+1D, generalization to 2+1D may be possible following the approach of Iadecola et al. (2024).
