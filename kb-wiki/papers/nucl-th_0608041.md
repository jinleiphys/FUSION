# Theoretical Analysis of Dilepton Spectra in Heavy Ion Collisions at GSI-FAIR Energies

> This paper addresses the theoretical analysis of dilepton spectra in C+C collisions at GSI-SIS energies. Theoretical predictions for the recent data of the HADES collaboration at SIS-energies are made with the help of a hadron-string transport model, the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. A mass shift of the $ρ$ meson due to kinematical effects is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the mass spectrum of the $\rho$ meson in C+C collisions at 2 AGeV is modified by kinematic effects from resonance decay chains, producing a double-peak structure even without explicit in-medium spectral functions; this kinematic mass shift can be tested by the HADES experiment.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) hadron-string transport model to simulate minimum-bias C+C collisions at 2 AGeV beam energy. Dilepton yields were computed from Dalitz decays of $\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\omega$, and $\eta'$ mesons, from direct decays of $\rho^0$, $\omega$, and $\phi$ mesons, and from $\Delta$ resonance Dalitz decays, using a time-integration method that accounts for collisional broadening. The calculated dilepton momentum vectors were filtered through the HADES acceptance matrix (release 1.0) to enable comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Mean pion multiplicity from UrQMD: $\langle N_{\pi^{0}} \rangle = 1.223$ per event for C+C at 2 AGeV.
- For masses below 110 MeV, the $\pi^0$-Dalitz decay contribution is up to two orders of magnitude higher than other contributions.
- The $\rho$ meson spectrum shows a double-peak structure: one peak around 500-600 MeV from $N^*(1520) \rightarrow N + \rho$ decays, and one at the vacuum mass of 770 MeV from $\pi\pi$ annihilation and higher-mass baryon resonance decays.
- Direct $\rho$ production from $\pi\pi$ scattering yields only up to 20% contribution at the $\rho$-peak mass.
- The $N^*(1520)$ decay channel contributes 75% of $\rho$ mesons with masses below 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends transport-model calculations of dilepton production at SIS energies, following the approach of Bass (1998) and Bleicher (1999) for the UrQMD model. The predictions are directly comparable to upcoming data from the HADES collaboration at GSI-SIS. The paper shows that kinematic effects from resonance decays can mimic in-medium modifications of the $\rho$ spectral function previously studied by Cozma (2006), and suggests that the HADES experiment can distinguish these kinematic shifts by measuring dilepton spectra at target/projectile rapidities.
