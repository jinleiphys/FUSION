# Some challenges for Nuclear Density Functional Theory

> 12 pages, 3 figures, invited talk at the 3rd ANL/MSU/INT/JINA RIA Theory workshop, April 4th - 7th, 2006, Argonne National Laboratory, USA. Proceeding to be published in World Scientific

## Key claim

Nuclear Density Functional Theory (DFT) faces significant challenges in developing a truly universal energy density functional applicable over the entire nuclear chart; the authors argue that current phenomenological functionals are too schematic and limited, and that fitting only global equations of state does not guarantee correct spin-isospin properties, as demonstrated by the failure of Skyrme functionals to reproduce ab-initio predictions for the (S,T) channel decomposition of the energy.

## Method

The authors discuss formal and practical challenges for nuclear DFT, including the need for a systematic long-term strategy, improved phenomenology for single-particle energies and pairing, and a connection to underlying methods such as Effective Field Theory. They then present a case study in which they construct three Skyrme parameterizations (f-, f0, f+) using the same fitting protocol as SLy5 but with different values of the isovector effective mass splitting, and compare their predictions for infinite nuclear matter against ab-initio Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and variational chain summation (VCS) calculations, including a decomposition of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state into spin-isospin (S,T) channels.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar effective mass m*_s is approximately 0.8, well determined by the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR); the isovector effective mass m*_v is constrained to approximately 0.7-0.9 via the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) but not decisively.
- Recent BHF calculations predict a neutron-proton effective mass splitting Δm*_{n-p} ≈ 0.22 for pure neutron matter (I=1); Dirac BHF gives Δm*_{n-p} ≈ 0.13.
- The SLy5' functional gives Δm*_{n-p} = -0.182, while the three constructed parameterizations give Δm*_{n-p} = -0.284 (f-), +0.001 (f0), and +0.170 (f+); all have m*_s = 0.70.
- The coefficient C_1^{Δρ} (scalar-isovector gradient term) takes values -5.4 (f-), -16.7 (SLy5'), -21.4 (f0), -29.4 (f+), and -35.0 (SkP); empirically, C_1^{Δρ} must be greater than approximately -30 MeV to avoid finite-size isospin instabilities.
- The saturation density ρ_sat for all four forces is 0.161-0.162 fm^{-3}, with binding energy E/A_sat between -15.987 and -16.036 MeV, incompressibility K_∞ = 230 MeV, and symmetry energy a_I = 32 MeV.

## Context

This paper extends the ongoing effort to improve nuclear energy density functionals by incorporating constraints from ab-initio calculations, following the strategy advocated by Perdew (2005) for electronic DFT. It builds on the SLyx family of Skyrme functionals developed by Chabanat et al. and compares with BHF calculations by Zuo et al. (2001) and VCS results by Akmal et al. (1998). The work identifies that fitting the global equation of state of pure neutron matter, as done for SLy5, leads to the wrong sign of the effective mass splitting and generates finite-size instabilities when one attempts to correct it. The authors conclude that the standard Skyrme functional is overconstrained when one tries to simultaneously reproduce the (S,T) channel decomposition, and that a density-dependent or higher-order derivative term in the odd-L channel is needed. This enables future work to refine the functional form and fitting strategy, potentially using data from radioactive beam facilities like RIA to discriminate between pairing models in neutron-rich systems such as 146/150Sn or 81Ni with an accuracy of δE = 50 keV.
