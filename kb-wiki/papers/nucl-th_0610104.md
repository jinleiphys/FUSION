# Saturation properties of nuclear matter in a relativistic mean field model constrained by quark dynamics

> We have built an effective Walecka-type hadronic Lagrangian in which the hadron masses and the density dependence of the coupling constants are deduced from the quark dynamics using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The parameters of this Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model have been determined using the meson properties in the vacuum but also in the medium through the omega meson mass in nuclei measured by the TAPS collaboration. Realistic properties of nuclear matter have been obtained.

# Saturation properties of nuclear matter in a relativistic mean field model constrained by quark dynamics

## Key claim

The authors constructed a Walecka-type hadronic Lagrangian where the density dependence of masses and couplings is derived from a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) quark model, rather than from the phenomenological Brown-Rho scaling used in prior work. Realistic nuclear matter saturation properties are obtained only when the NJL model is constrained by the TAPS collaboration measurement of the in-medium omega meson mass, and only for a narrow range of the constituent quark mass around 465-470 MeV.

## Method

The authors built an effective hadronic Lagrangian of Walecka type with density-dependent nucleon and meson masses (M*_N, m*_sigma, m*_omega) and coupling constants (g*_sigmaNN, g*_omegaNN), treated at the mean-field level for infinite symmetric nuclear matter. The density dependence of these quantities was determined from a two-flavor NJL quark model including four-quark and eight-quark interaction terms, by solving Bethe-Salpeter equations for meson masses and quark-meson couplings in medium. The nucleon effective mass was related to the in-medium quark condensate using the same relation as in finite-density QCD sum-rule calculations, and the nucleon-meson couplings were assumed proportional to the quark-meson couplings.

## Key numbers

- The omega meson mass in nuclei measured by TAPS collaboration: m*_omega(rho_B = 0.6 rho_0) = 722 (+4/-4 stat, +35/-5 syst) MeV, used to constrain the eight-quark term parameter g_3.
- Vacuum values used to fix NJL parameters: pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, omega meson mass m_omega = 782 MeV.
- Free nucleon mass: M_N = 939 MeV.
- The constituent quark mass range yielding realistic saturation properties: m ≈ 465-470 MeV, corresponding to cutoff Lambda = 572 ± 1 MeV.
- Empirical targets for saturation properties: effective nucleon mass m*_N/M_N ≈ 0.58-0.6, incompressibility K ≈ 250 ± 50 MeV, slope of nucleon-nucleus optical potential U_0/M_N ≈ 0.25-0.40.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Song, Min, and Rho (1998), who used Brown-Rho scaling with a 20% decrease of vector meson mass at saturation, by instead deriving the density dependence directly from an NJL quark model. The model is constrained by the TAPS collaboration measurement of the in-medium omega meson mass, which suggests a smaller decrease of approximately 10-15% at saturation density, in contrast to the earlier assumption. The result enables a bridge between quark-level dynamics and nuclear matter saturation properties with few free parameters, though the authors note the relation between M*_N and the quark condensate is likely valid only at low densities, and the equation of state becomes too hard at higher densities.
