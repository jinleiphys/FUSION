# Effects of beta-gamma coupling in transitional nuclei and the validity of the approximate separation of variables

> Exact numerical diagonalization is carried out for the Bohr Hamiltonian with a beta-soft, axially stabilized potential. Wave function and observable properties are found to be dominated by strong beta-gamma coupling effects. The validity of the approximate separation of variables introduced with the X(5) model, extensively applied in recent analyses of axially stabilized transitional nuclei, is examined, and the reasons for its breakdown are analyzed.

## Key claim

Exact numerical diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian with a beta-soft, axially stabilized potential reveals that wave function and observable properties are dominated by strong beta-gamma coupling effects, contrary to the approximate separation of variables assumed in the X(5) model. The approximate separation breaks down because the beta-gamma coupling inherent in the kinetic energy operator induces a centrifugal-like effect that strongly couples the beta and gamma degrees of freedom, even for large gamma stiffness.

## Method

Exact numerical diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian was carried out using a product basis constructed from optimized radial basis functions (Bessel functions vanishing at beta=1) and five-dimensional spherical harmonics, following the method of Rowe et al. (2004). The potential was taken as a square well in beta (zero for beta <= beta_w, infinite otherwise) plus a harmonic oscillator confining potential in gamma, V_gamma(gamma) = A*gamma^2 on the interval 0 <= gamma <= pi/3, with the gamma stiffness parameter a = 2AB*beta_w^2/hbar^2. Calculations were performed for gamma stiffness values ranging from a=0 to a=1000, with the most realistic range for transitional rare earth nuclei being a ≈ 200 to 300.

## Key numbers

- For a=200, mean beta values are ⟨beta⟩ = 0.64 for the ground state (0+_1), 0.54 for the beta band head (0+_beta), and 0.70 for the gamma band head (2+_gamma)
- For a=1000, mean beta values are ⟨beta⟩ = 0.71 for the ground state, 0.60 for the beta band head, and 0.76 for the gamma band head
- The energy ratio R_4/2 ≡ E(4+_1)/E(2+_1) ranges from 2.20 in the E(5) limit (a=0) to greater than 3.2 for large a
- For a ≈ 200, the ratio ⟨beta^{-2}⟩_{0+_beta}/⟨beta^{-2}⟩_{0+_1} has a maximum value of approximately 2.1, and the ratio of spacing scales [E(2+_beta)-E(0+_beta)]/E(2+_1) has a maximum value of approximately 2.3
- For a=1000, the gamma band head has ⟨gamma⟩ ≈ 16 degrees and the ground state has ⟨gamma⟩ ≈ 11 degrees
- An avoided crossing of the 2+_beta and 2+_gamma states occurs for a ≈ 450

## Context

This work extends the exact numerical diagonalization methods developed by Rowe et al. (2004) for the Bohr Hamiltonian to the X(5) model of Iachello (2001), which had previously relied on an approximate separation of variables. The results show that the approximate separation used in X(5) and its extensions by Bonatsos et al. (2004), Caprio (2004), Fortunato (2004), and Pietralla (2004) breaks down due to strong beta-gamma coupling in the kinetic energy operator. The findings enable future exact numerical studies of more realistic Hamiltonians, including those with softer beta potentials, realistic moments of inertia, and beta-gamma coupling terms in the potential such as those arising from the classical limit of the interacting boson model.
