# Nucleon-pair approximation with matrix representation

> In this paper, we propose an approach of the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA), in which the collective nucleon pairs are represented in terms of antisymmetric matrices, and commutations between nucleon pairs are given by using matrix multiplication that avoids angular-momentum couplings and recouplings. Therefore the present approach significantly simplifies the NPA computation. Furthermore, it is formulated on the same footing with and without isospin.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a reformulation of the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) in which collective nucleon pairs are represented by antisymmetric matrices, and all commutators between pairs and operators are evaluated through matrix multiplication, thereby completely avoiding angular-momentum couplings and recouplings throughout the entire computation.

## Method

The authors define collective nucleon-pair creation and annihilation operators in terms of antisymmetric structural matrices \(p(\alpha\beta)\), and one-body operators in terms of matrices \(q(\alpha\beta)\). They derive commutators between nucleon-pair operators and one-body operators, between multiple nucleon-pair annihilation operators and a pair creation operator, and between multiple pairs and two-body interaction operators, all expressed purely through matrix products and traces. Using these commutators, they recursively reduce overlaps of NPA basis states and matrix elements of the shell-model Hamiltonian for both even-even and odd-mass nuclei to sums of simpler overlaps, with the key computation being the overlap for systems without unpaired nucleons.

## Key numbers

* The computational bottleneck in previous NPA approaches occurs when "the valence proton number and/or valence neutron pair number is larger than eight" (no explicit number of operations given, but this threshold is stated as the point where calculations become heavy).
* The overlap for a single pair is given by \(\langle 0 | \hat{P} \hat{\mathbb{P}}^\dagger | 0 \rangle = -\frac{1}{2} \mathrm{tr}(\mathbb{p} p)\), where \(p\) and \(\mathbb{p}\) are the structural matrices of the pair operators.
* The commutator between a pair annihilation operator \(\hat{P}_3\) and a one-body operator \(\hat{Q}\) yields another pair annihilation operator with structural matrix \(p_4 = p_3 q + q^\top p_3\).
* The commutator between a single-particle annihilation operator \(\hat{a}\) and a pair creation operator \(\hat{P}^\dagger\) yields a single-particle creation operator with structural vector \(\vec{d} = -2p \vec{a}\).

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) originally developed by Chen (1990s) and refined by Zhao and collaborators, which previously required angular-momentum couplings and recouplings that became prohibitively heavy for more than eight valence pairs. It builds on matrix representation techniques previously used by Silvestre-Brac and Piepenbring for multi-phonon states, by Ginocchio and Johnson via generating functions, and by Mizusaki and Otsuka for shell-model foundations of the interacting boson model. The new formulation enables numerical calculations of rotational states for heavy nuclei that were previously intractable, and unifies the treatment of systems with and without isospin symmetry.
