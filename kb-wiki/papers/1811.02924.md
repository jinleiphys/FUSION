# Phase transitions and Bose-Einstein condensation in alpha-nucleon matter

> The equation of state and phase diagram of isospin-symmetric chemically equilibrated mixture of alpha particles and nucleons are studied in the mean-field approximation. The model takes into account the effects of Fermi and Bose statistics for nucleons and alphas, respectively. We use Skyrme-like parametrization of the mean-field potentials as functions of partial densities, which contain both attractive and repulsive terms. Parameters of these potentials are chosen by fitting known properties of pure nucleon- and pure alpha matter at zero temperature. The sensitivity of results to the choice of the alpha-nucleon attraction strength is investigated. The phase diagram of the alpha-nucleon mixture is studied with a special attention paid to the liquid-gas phase transitions and the Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha particles. We have found two first-order phase transitions, stable and metastable, which differ significantly by the fractions of alpha particles. It is shown that states with alpha condensate are metastable.

## Key claim
The paper finds two first-order phase transitions in alpha-nucleon matter, one stable and one metastable, which differ significantly by the fraction of alpha particles; states with Bose-Einstein condensate of alpha particles are shown to be metastable, not stable.

## Method
The authors model isospin-symmetric chemically equilibrated mixtures of alpha particles and nucleons using a mean-field approximation with Skyrme-like parametrizations of the mean-field potentials as functions of partial densities. They include both attractive and repulsive terms, fitting parameters to known properties of pure nucleon matter and pure alpha matter at zero temperature. The equation of state and phase diagram are computed by solving chemical equilibrium conditions and stability criteria, with the sensitivity to the alpha-nucleon attraction strength investigated by varying the cross-term coefficient a_{Nalpha}.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of pure nucleon matter: n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} with binding energy per baryon -15.9 MeV at T=0
- Saturation density of pure alpha matter: n_{0alpha} = 0.036 fm^{-3} with binding energy per baryon W_alpha = 12 MeV at T=0
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition in pure nucleon matter: T_c = 15.3 MeV (gamma=1/6) or 21.3 MeV (gamma=1)
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition in pure alpha matter: T_c = 10.2 MeV (gamma=1/6) or 13.7 MeV (gamma=1)
- Triple point temperature in pure alpha matter: T_tp = 3.56 MeV (gamma=1/6) or 3.65 MeV (gamma=1)
- Threshold cross-term coefficient a_* = 2.12 GeV fm^3 (gamma=1/6) separating regimes where alpha particles appear in the ground state
- Binding energy per baryon of alpha particle: B_alpha = 7.1 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of pure alpha matter (Satarov 2017) by including nucleons and chemical equilibrium via the reaction alpha <-> 4N. It contrasts with the virial approach of Horvath (2006) and the van der Waals model of Vovchenko (2017a), which did not include Bose-Einstein condensation phenomena. The results enable future studies of clusterized nuclear matter in astrophysical contexts such as neutron star crusts and supernova ejecta, where alpha particles and nucleons coexist at subsaturation densities.
