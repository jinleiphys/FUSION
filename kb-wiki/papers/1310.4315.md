# Pion production in neutrino-nucleus collisions

> We compare our pion production results with recent MiniBooNE data measured in mineral oil. Our total cross sections lie below experimental data for neutrino energies above 1 GeV. Differential cross sections show our model produces too few high energy pions in the forward direction as compared to data. The agreement with experiment improves by artificially removing pion final state interaction.

## Key claim

The authors' model for pion production in neutrino-nucleus collisions systematically underestimates MiniBooNE data for neutrino energies above 0.9 GeV, and the agreement with experiment improves significantly only when pion final state interaction (FSI) is artificially removed from the calculation.

## Method

The authors extended a previous nucleon-level pion production model (Hernandez 2007, Hernandez 2010) from the Delta(1232) resonance region up to 2 GeV neutrino energies by including the D13(1520) resonance. In-medium corrections incorporated Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, modified Delta resonance properties, and a new contribution (C_Q) from changes in the Delta width in the nuclear environment, while pion final state interaction was treated using a cascade program following Salcedo (1987). Coherent pion production was evaluated using the model of Amaro (2008) and Hernandez (2010).

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for charged current 1pi+ production lie below MiniBooNE data for neutrino energies above 0.9 GeV
- The D13(1520) resonance contributes about 8% of the total cross section at the highest neutrino energy (near 2 GeV)
- The C_Q term contributes around 8% of the total cross section for all energies
- Differential cross section dsigma/dT_pi for CC 1pi+ production disagrees with data for pion kinetic energy T_pi above 0.15 GeV
- Neglecting the C_Q contribution decreases the differential cross section by about 10% around the peak at T_pi = 0.08 GeV
- For CC 1pi0 production, the model agrees with data for pion momentum below 0.2 GeV/c but produces too few pions in the momentum region from 0.22 to 0.55 GeV/c
- For neutral current 1pi0 production, results show a depletion in the 0.25 to 0.5 GeV/c momentum region
- The neutrino flux used peaks at around 600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pion production model of Hernandez (2007, 2010) from the Delta(1232) region up to 2 GeV by including the D13(1520) resonance, following the observation by Leitner (2008) that this resonance gives the most important contribution in that extended energy region. The results are qualitatively similar to those obtained by Lalakulich (2012) and Sobczyk (2012). The systematic underprediction of forward-going high-energy pions and the improvement when FSI is removed suggest that the cascade model for pion final state interactions may need revision, and that a larger value of the axial nucleon-to-Delta form factor C^A_5(0) than the central value of 1.0 may be preferable in the high energy region.
