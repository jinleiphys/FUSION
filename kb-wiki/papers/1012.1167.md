# Novel Extrapolation Method in the Monte Carlo Shell Model

> We propose an extrapolation method utilizing energy variance in the Monte Carlo shell model in order to estimate the energy eigenvalue and observables accurately. We derive a formula for the energy variance with deformed Slater determinants, which enables us to calculate the energy variance efficiently. The feasibility of the method is demonstrated for the full $pf$-shell calculation of $^{56}$Ni, and the applicability of the method to a system beyond current limit of exact diagonalization is shown for the $pf$+$g_{9/2}$-shell calculation of $^{64}$Ge.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extrapolation method using energy variance within the Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) to estimate energy eigenvalues and observables accurately, claiming that the extrapolated energy for ⁵⁶Ni agrees with the exact diagonalization result within 1 keV, though this precision depends on excluding the first nine MCSM points from the quadratic fit because those points show comparably large fluctuation due to the stochastic procedure.

## Method

The authors derive a new formula for the expectation value of H² with deformed Slater determinants, factorizing the eightfold loop summation into a sixfold loop to reduce computation time drastically. They apply the MCSM to generate successive approximate wave functions, compute the energy variance for each, and fit the energy versus variance points by a second-order polynomial, extrapolating to zero variance. For observables other than energy, a first-order polynomial is used for the extrapolation.

## Key numbers

- The m-scheme dimension of ⁵⁶Ni in the pf-shell reaches 1.0 × 10⁹.
- The MCSM energy for the ground state of ⁵⁶Ni with N=150 is −203.161 MeV, while the exact energy from diagonalization is −203.198 MeV, giving a 37 keV error.
- The extrapolated energy for the ⁵⁶Ni ground state is −203.198 MeV, agreeing with the exact value within 1 keV.
- The extrapolated energy using particle-hole truncation (PHT) for ⁵⁶Ni is −203.217 MeV, with a discrepancy of 19 keV from the exact energy.
- The MCSM result with N=50 for ⁵⁶Ni is −203.115 MeV, and its extrapolated energy is −203.202 MeV.
- The m-scheme dimension for ⁶⁴Ge in the pf+g₉/₂ model space is 1.7 × 10¹⁴, roughly 10³ times larger than the current limitation of conventional diagonalization (∼10¹¹).
- The excitation energy of the 2⁺ state in ⁶⁴Ge from the MCSM-extrapolation is 0.95 MeV, close to the experimental value of 0.90 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the energy-variance extrapolation method developed by Imada and Kashima (2000) and applied to shell model calculations by Mizusaki and Imada (2002, 2003), overcoming previous limitations in computer resources by deriving a new formula for H² expectation values. The method addresses the long-standing problem of slow convergence in the MCSM, which was previously handled by empirical exponential extrapolation as attempted by Horoi, Volya, and Zelevinsky (1999). The approach enables large-scale shell model calculations beyond the reach of conventional diagonalization methods, such as the ⁶⁴Ge calculation with a model space roughly 10⁵ times larger than the current limit of exact solvers.
