# Microscopic study of higher-order deformation effects on the ground states of superheavy nuclei around $^{270}$Hs

> We study the effects of higher-order deformations $β_λ$ ($λ=4,6,8,$ and $10$) on the ground state properties of superheavy nuclei (SHN) near the doubly magic deformed nucleus $^{270}$Hs by using the multidimensionally-constrained (MDC) relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with five effective interactions PC-PK1, PK1, NL3$^{*}$, DD-ME2, and PKDD. The doubly magic properties of $^{270}$Hs are featured by the large energy gaps at $N=162$ and $Z=108$ in the single-particle spectra. By investigating the binding energies and single-particle levels of $^{270}$Hs in multidimensional deformation space, we find that the deformation $β_6$ has the greatest impact on the binding energy among these higher-order deformations and influences the shell gaps considerably. Similar conclusions hold for other SHN near $^{270}$Hs. Our calculations demonstrate that the deformation $β_6$ must be considered when studying SHN by using MDC-RMF.

## Key claim

The deformation parameter β₆ has the greatest impact on the binding energy among the higher-order deformations (β₄, β₆, β₈, and β₁₀) for superheavy nuclei around ²⁷⁰Hs, and must be considered when using multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field models; however, β₄ has a larger effect than β₆ on the shell gaps at Z=108 and N=162.

## Method

The authors used the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic mean-field (MDC-RMF) model with five effective interactions (PC-PK1, PK1, NL3*, DD-ME2, and PKDD) to calculate ground-state properties of ²⁷⁰Hs, even-even Hs isotopes from ²⁶⁴Hs to ²⁷⁶Hs, and N=162 isotones from ²⁶⁶Rf to ²⁷²Ds. Constraint calculations on mass multipole moments were performed in deformation spaces {βλ; λ=0,2,…,λmax} with λmax ranging from 0 to 10, and the axially symmetric deformations βλ with even λ were considered. A separable pairing force with strength G=1.1 G₀ (G₀=728 MeV fm³) and range a=0.644 fm was adopted in the particle-particle channel.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁷⁰Hs with PC-PK1, the binding energy changes by about 8.43 MeV when β₂ is included, 0.68 MeV from β₄, and about 1.87 MeV from β₆; including β₈ and β₁₀ has negligible effect.
- The binding energy of ²⁷⁰Hs with PC-PK1 is 1967.41 MeV; with PK1 it is 1973.77 MeV; the empirical value from AME2020 is 1969.65 MeV.
- After including rotational energy correction with PC-PK1, the binding energy of ²⁷⁰Hs changes from 1967.45 MeV to 1969.76 MeV.
- In the ground state (λmax=10) with PC-PK1, the shell gaps at Z=108 and N=162 are about 1.34 MeV and 1.85 MeV, respectively.
- Including β₂ creates shell gaps of 0.66 MeV (Z=108) and 1.17 MeV (N=162); adding β₄ increases these to about 1.28 MeV and 1.56 MeV; adding β₆ changes the proton gap by only 0.02 MeV and increases the neutron gap by about 0.26 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic studies of Patyk and Sobiczewski (1991), who found β₆ important for binding energies and deformed shells in heaviest nuclei, to a fully microscopic covariant density functional theory framework. It builds on previous MDC-CDFT calculations for ²⁷⁰Hs by Meng et al. (2020) and demonstrates that β₆ must be included for accurate ground-state descriptions of superheavy nuclei around ²⁷⁰Hs. The results enable systematic studies of higher-order deformation effects on moments of inertia, energy spectra, and nuclear mass tables using density functional theories, with the deformation relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (DRHBc) theory being a natural next step.
