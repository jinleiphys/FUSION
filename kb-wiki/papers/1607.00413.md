# Electromagnetic effects on meson production: a new tool for studying the space-time evolution of heavy ion collisions

> We review our studies of spectator-induced electromagnetic (EM) effects on the emission of charged mesons in the final state of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We argue that these effects offer sensitivity to the distance $d_E$ between the charged meson formation zone at freeze-out and the spectator system. As such, they can serve as an independent, new tool to probe the space-time and longitudinal evolution of the system created in the collision. As a phenomenological application for this tool in the context of resonance production and decay, we obtain a first estimate of the time of pion emission from EM effects. This we compare to existing HBT data.

## Key claim

Spectator-induced electromagnetic effects on charged meson emission are sensitive to the distance \(d_E\) between the meson formation zone at freeze-out and the spectator system, and can serve as an independent tool to probe the space-time and longitudinal evolution of the system created in heavy ion collisions. The authors obtain a first estimate of the time of pion emission from these EM effects, which they compare to existing HBT data, but note that the present analysis carries uncertainties from incomplete knowledge of resonance production.

## Method

The authors analyzed invariant double differential spectra of \(\pi^+\) and \(\pi^-\) mesons from the NA49 experiment for peripheral and intermediate centrality Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV, examining the distortion of spectra and \(\pi^+/\pi^-\) ratios as a function of transverse momentum and Feynman-\(x\). They also compared their results to charge splitting of directed flow seen by the STAR Collaboration for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV and to \(\pi^+\) directed flow from the WA98 experiment at top SPS energy. A Monte Carlo simulation of the relativistic motion of charged particles in the EM field was used to extract the distance \(d_E\), and a separate Monte Carlo model based on baryonic (\(\Delta\)) and mesonic (\(\rho\)) resonance production and decay was used to interpret the space-time evolution.

## Key numbers

- For fast pions with rapidity \(y \approx y_{beam}\) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV, the average longitudinal position of the formation zone is about \(d_E \approx 0.75\) fm behind the spectator system.
- For pions produced at low c.m.s. rapidities in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV, the charge splitting of directed flow corresponds to \(d_E \approx 3\) fm.
- For pions in target vicinity in top SPS energy Pb+Pb collisions, the very large \(\pi^+\) directed flow suggests \(d_E \approx 0.5\) fm.
- At midrapidity, the preliminary estimate for the time of pion emission is \(5.3 \pm 2.2\) fm/c, derived from \(d_E(y/y_\mathrm{beam}=0)/\beta\) where \(\beta\) is the spectator velocity.
- For comparison, the ALICE Collaboration HBT analysis shows pion decoupling times of approximately \(\tau_f \approx 6\) fm/c for the same range of collision energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of spectator-induced EM effects on charged meson spectra and directed flow (Rybicki et al., 2014; Rybicki et al., 2015) and provides a new method to probe the space-time evolution of the system created in heavy ion collisions. The results are compared to existing HBT data from the ALICE Collaboration (ALICE, 2015) and to directed flow measurements from the STAR Collaboration (STAR, 2014) and WA98 Collaboration (WA98). The approach offers sensitivity over a broad range of collision centralities and rapidities, and future measurements at SPS energies are expected to reinforce the present limited database and deepen understanding of the longitudinal expansion of hot and dense matter.
