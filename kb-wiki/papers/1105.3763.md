# Zero-energy neutron-triton and proton-Helium-3 scattering with \eftnopi

> Model-independent constraints for the neutron-triton and proton-Helium-3 scattering lengths are calculated with a leading-order interaction derived from an effective field theory without explicit pions. Using the singlet neutron-proton scattering length, the deuteron, and the triton binding energy as input, the predictions $\ants=9.2\pm2.6 $fm, $\antt=7.6\pm1.6 $fm, $\aphes=3.6\pm0.32 $fm, and $\aphet=3.1\pm 0.23 $fm are obtained. The calculations employ the resonating group method and include the Coulomb interaction when appropriate. The theoretical uncertainty is assessed via a variation of the regulator parameter of the short-distance interaction from $400 $MeV to $1.6 $GeV. The phase-shift and scattering-length results for the proton-Helium-3 system are consistent with a recent phase shift analysis and with model calculations. For neutron-triton, the results for the scattering lengths in both singlet and triplet channels are significantly smaller than suggested by R-matrix and partial-wave-analysis extractions from data. For a better understanding of this discrepancy, the sensitivity of the low-energy four-body scattering system to variations in the neutron-neutron and proton-proton two-nucleon scattering lengths is calculated. Induced by strong charge-symmetry-breaking contact interactions, this dependence is found insignificant. In contrast, a strong correlation between the neutron-triton scattering length and the triton binding energy analogous to the Phillips line is found.

# Zero-energy neutron-triton and proton-Helium-3 scattering with EFT(π̸)

## Key claim

Model-independent constraints for the neutron-triton and proton-Helium-3 scattering lengths are calculated at leading order in an effective field theory without explicit pions, but the predictions for neutron-triton scattering lengths are significantly smaller than those suggested by R-matrix and partial-wave-analysis extractions from data, while the proton-Helium-3 results are consistent with a recent phase shift analysis.

## Method

The resonating group method (RGM) was used to solve the four-body scattering problem numerically with a leading-order interaction derived from an effective field theory without explicit pions (EFT(π̸)). The low-energy constants were fitted to the singlet neutron-proton scattering length, the deuteron binding energy, and the triton binding energy, with the Coulomb interaction included when appropriate. Theoretical uncertainty was assessed by varying the regulator parameter of the short-distance interaction from 400 MeV to 1.6 GeV, and by varying the two-body input data including the neutron-neutron and proton-proton scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- Singlet neutron-triton scattering length: a_s(t-n) = 9.2 ± 2.6 fm
- Triplet neutron-triton scattering length: a_t(t-n) = 7.6 ± 1.6 fm
- Singlet proton-Helium-3 scattering length (Coulomb-corrected): a^C_s(³He-p) = 3.6 ± 0.32 fm
- Triplet proton-Helium-3 scattering length (Coulomb-corrected): a^C_t(³He-p) = 3.1 ± 0.23 fm
- Regulator parameter variation range: 400 MeV to 1.6 GeV
- Numerical RGM error contribution: 0.05 fm
- Triton binding energy fixed at: B(t) = 8.48 MeV
- The predicted value for a_t(t-n) changed by less than 1% when the neutron-neutron scattering length was varied between the two proposed experimental values of -18.7 ± 0.7 fm and -16.1 ± 0.4 fm, as long as the three-nucleon interaction fixed B(t) = 8.48 MeV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to four-nucleon scattering systems, establishing for the first time an analogous correlation to the Phillips line between the neutron-triton scattering length and the triton binding energy. Previous EFT applications had focused on bound four-nucleon systems such as Helium-4 (Platter, 2004; Bauchpinsel, 2005), while this analysis addresses scattering observables. The results are compared to model calculations using the v_18+UIX, v_14, and MT-I/III interactions (Lazauskas, 2005; Kievsky, 2005), and to phase-shift analysis extractions (Fisher, 2006). The correlation found between the singlet neutron-triton scattering length and the triton binding energy is analogous to the Tjon line for binding energies and supports the conjecture that quantum chromodynamics parameters place the system close to a critical renormalization-group trajectory (Braaten, 2004).
