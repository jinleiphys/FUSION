# Relevance of the coupled channels in the $ŽĢ$p and $Žü^0$p Correlation Functions

> The vector meson-baryon interaction in a coupled channel scheme is revisited within the correlation function framework. As illustrative cases to reveal the important role played by the coupled channels, we focus on the $ŽĢ$p and $Žü^0$p systems given their complex dynamics and the presence of quasi-bound states or resonances in the vicinity of their thresholds. We show that the $ŽĢ$p femtoscopic data provide novel information about a $N^*$ state present in the experimental region and anticipate the relevance of a future $Žü^0$p correlation function measurement in order to pin down the $S=0, Q=+1$ vector meson-baryon interaction as well as to disclose the characterizing features of the $N^*(1700)$ state.

## Key claim
The paper argues that coupled-channel effects are essential for correctly interpreting the $\phi$p and $\rho^0$p correlation functions, contrary to the earlier assumption by ALICE (2021) that the $\phi$p interaction was dominated by pure elastic scattering. The authors show that the elastic transition alone cannot reproduce the experimental data, and that inelastic transitions from channels such as $K^{*+}\Lambda$ and $K^{*}\Sigma$ contribute significantly to the observed correlation functions.

## Method
The authors employ a coupled-channel unitary scheme based on the hidden gauge formalism, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $S=0, Q=+1$ sector including seven channels: $\rho^0$p, $\rho^+$n, $\omega$p, $\phi$p, $K^{*+}\Lambda$, $K^{*0}\Sigma^+$, and $K^{*+}\Sigma^0$. The scattering amplitudes are constrained by fitting the $\phi$p femtoscopic data from ALICE (2021) using a bootstrap technique, with five subtraction constants and a normalization factor as free parameters. The $\rho^0$p correlation function is then predicted using the same constrained amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- $\phi$p scattering length from the Bootstrap model: $a^{\phi p}_{0} = (-0.034 \pm 0.035) + i\, (0.57 \pm 0.09)$ fm
- $\phi$p effective range from the Bootstrap model: $r^{\phi p}_{eff} = (-8.06 \pm 2.57) + i\, (0.05 \pm 0.53)$ fm
- $\rho^0$p scattering length from the Bootstrap model: $a^{\rho^0 p}_{0} = (0.09 \pm 0.03) + i\, (0.56 \pm 0.05)$ fm
- $\rho^0$p effective range from the Bootstrap model: $r^{\rho^0 p}_{eff} = (-3.05 \pm 0.28) + i\, (98.40 \pm 0.12)$ fm
- Pole mass of the $N^*$ state in the Bootstrap model: $M = 1959$ MeV with half-width $\Gamma/2 = 23$ MeV
- Source size used in the correlation function calculation: $R = 1.08$ fm, taken from ALICE (2021)
- Cutoff for the momentum integration: $q_{\rm cut} = 850$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel unitary approach of Oset (2010) and applies it to femtoscopic correlation functions, following the methodology of Sarti (2023) who used $K^-\Lambda$ data to constrain chiral Lagrangians. The results contradict the single-channel interpretation of the $\phi$p interaction by ALICE (2021), which extracted $a^{\phi p}_{0} = (0.85 \pm 0.48) + i\, (0.16 \pm 0.19)$ fm assuming elastic dominance. The paper provides predictions for a future $\rho^0$p correlation function measurement, which would help determine the properties of the $N^*(1700)$ state and the $a_{\rho N}$ subtraction constant that cannot be constrained from $\phi$p data alone.
