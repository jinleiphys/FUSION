# Neutrinoless double beta decay in seesaw models

> We study the general phenomenology of neutrinoless double beta decay in seesaw models. In particular, we focus on the dependence of the neutrinoless double beta decay rate on the mass of the extra states introduced to account for the Majorana masses of light neutrinos. For this purpose, we compute the nuclear matrix elements as functions of the mass of the mediating fermions and estimate the associated uncertainties. We then discuss what can be inferred on the seesaw model parameters in the different mass regimes and clarify how the contribution of the light neutrinos should always be taken into account when deriving bounds on the extra parameters. Conversely, the extra states can also have a significant impact, cancelling the Standard Model neutrino contribution for masses lighter than the nuclear scale and leading to vanishing neutrinoless double beta decay amplitudes even if neutrinos are Majorana particles. We also discuss how seesaw models could reconcile large rates of neutrinoless double beta decay with more stringent cosmological bounds on neutrino masses.

# Neutrinoless double beta decay in seesaw models

## Key claim
The neutrinoless double beta decay rate depends on the mass of the extra sterile neutrino states introduced in seesaw models, and the contribution from light Standard Model neutrinos must always be included when deriving bounds on the extra parameters; conversely, extra states with masses below the nuclear scale can cancel the Standard Model neutrino contribution, leading to unobservable decay amplitudes even if neutrinos are Majorana particles.

## Method
The authors computed the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decay as functions of the mass of the mediating fermion, using the interacting shell model (ISM) with the NATHAN code for six candidate nuclei: ⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹³⁰Te, and ¹³⁶Xe. They employed the closure approximation, included higher-order components of the nuclear current (magnetic, pseudoscalar, and tensor terms), and applied unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) transformations for short-range correlations, with the axial coupling unquenched at g_A(0)=1.25.

## Key numbers
- The typical virtual neutrino momentum in the decay is approximately 100 MeV, selected by the nuclear wavefunctions and the form factor cutoffs at Λ_V=0.85 GeV and Λ_A=1.09 GeV.
- For extra sterile neutrino masses below 1 MeV, the neutrinoless double beta decay rate is reduced by at least six orders of magnitude in the absence of extra contributions.
- The Heidelberg-Moscow claim requires a tuning of only about 50% amongst the extra contributions to be compatible with present cosmological bounds on neutrino masses.
- The overall uncertainty in the final NME is estimated at +25%/-35%, combining contributions from valence space size (15-20% increase), nuclear interaction dependence (5-10%), short-range correlations (5%), closure approximation (5-10%), missing operator terms (less than 5%), and axial quenching (+5%/-30%).

## Context
This work extends previous studies that treated light and heavy neutrino contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay independently (Benes 2005, Atre 2009, Belanger 1995, Simkovic 1999, del Aguila 2008), showing that the interplay between both contributions can produce cancellations in certain mass regimes. The computed NMEs as functions of neutrino mass enable future analyses to properly combine constraints from neutrinoless double beta decay experiments (such as GERDA, EXO, SNO+, and CUORE) with cosmological bounds on neutrino masses (Hannestad 2010, Komatsu 2010), and to explore whether seesaw models can reconcile a large decay signal with stringent mass limits.
