# Low density Neutron Matter at Finite and Zero Temperatures

> This report concerns the energy of neutron-matter for densities below $0.15 fm^{-3}$ and temperatures at and below $10 MeV$. Separable NN-interactions are obtained by inverse scattering from the experimental phase-shifts with specified momentum cut-offs $Λ$. Results of Brueckner-Bloch-DeDomicis as well as finite temperature Green's function calculations show independence of cut-off for $Λ\geq 3$. Agreement with the low-density virial expansion is found. Results of Hartree-Fock as well as second order calculations show considerable $Λ$-dependence and the agreement with the virial expansion is lost. The "best" first-order choice of $Λ$ is found to be $\sim 2.5 fm^{-1}$, which agrees with $V_{low k}$ studies. Reasonable agreement between the second order and the Brueckner results is also found for this value of $Λ$ except at low density. Only 2-body forces are used in this study.

# Low density Neutron Matter at Finite and Zero Temperatures

## Key claim
The energy of low-density neutron matter (below 0.15 fm⁻³) computed using Brueckner-Bloch-DeDomicis and finite-temperature Green's function methods shows independence of the momentum cut-off Λ only for Λ ≥ 3 fm⁻¹, while first-order and second-order calculations remain cut-off dependent; the "best" first-order choice of Λ ≈ 2.5 fm⁻¹ agrees with V_low k studies, but only the full ladder summation reproduces the low-density virial expansion.

## Method
Separable nucleon-nucleon interactions were constructed by inverse scattering from experimental phase shifts (Arndt phases, supplemented at low energy by scattering length a_nn = -18.5 fm and effective range r_c = 2.68 fm) with specified momentum cut-offs Λ between 2 and 8 fm⁻¹. The energy per particle of neutron matter was computed using Brueckner-Bloch-DeDomicis theory (ladder summation to all orders, neglecting dispersion corrections) and finite-temperature Green's function methods (self-consistent in-medium T-matrix with spectral functions), for densities up to 0.15 fm⁻³ and temperatures from 0 to 10 MeV, including partial waves up to L ≤ 7 in some calculations.

## Key numbers
- Density range studied: below 0.15 fm⁻³; temperatures at and below 10 MeV.
- Momentum cut-off independence observed for Λ ≥ 3 fm⁻¹ in full Brueckner calculations.
- "Best" first-order choice of Λ found to be ∼ 2.5 fm⁻¹, agreeing with V_low k studies.
- Scattering length a_nn = -18.5 fm and effective range r_c = 2.68 fm used for low-energy phase shifts.
- Phase shifts become repulsive above k_c ∼ 1.6 fm⁻¹ for the ¹S₀ state.
- BCS critical temperature estimated at T_c ∼ 3 MeV at density n ∼ 0.1 fm⁻³.
- At zero temperature, the ¹S₀ state is sufficient for densities ρ ≤ 0.03 fm⁻³.
- The phase-shift approximation requires k_f ≪ 0.05 fm⁻¹ for validity (based on k_f · a_s with a_s ∼ 20 fm).
- Virial coefficient b₂ increases by about 20% at T = 10 MeV when higher partial waves are included (Horowitz and Schwenk, 2006).

## Context
This work extends earlier zero-temperature Brueckner calculations by Sood and Moszkowski (1960) and finite-temperature Bloch-DeDomicis studies by Margueron et al (2003) and Baldo et al (1998). It compares with Kohn-Luttinger-Ward results by Tolos et al (2007) and virial expansion results by Horowitz and Schwenk (2006). The study demonstrates that separable interactions from inverse scattering are a useful tool for investigating momentum cut-off dependence, and that the full Brueckner ladder summation is necessary to reproduce the virial limit at finite temperatures. The results enable further exploration of neutron matter equation of state for supernova physics, though three-body forces are not included and would be needed for precision calculations.
