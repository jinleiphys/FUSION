# On the solar neutrino problem in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

> The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron suggested by us previously is applied to the calculation of the reaction rate of the low-energy two-proton fusion p + p -> D + e+ + nu. The theoretical prediction of the reaction rate obtained is 2.9 times larger than given by the potential approach. This leads to a strong suppression of the high energy solar neutrino fluxes.

# On the solar neutrino problem in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

## Key claim

The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron, using one-nucleon loop diagrams, predicts the reaction rate of low-energy two-proton fusion p + p -> D + e+ + nu_e to be 2.9 times larger than the rate calculated by non-relativistic potential approaches, but this enhancement carries a theoretical uncertainty of about 30% and depends on fixing the ambiguity of the AAV-anomaly by requiring gauge invariance under gauge transformations of the deuteron field.

## Method

The authors applied their previously developed relativistic field theory model of the deuteron, where strong interactions are described by one-nucleon loop diagrams, to compute the cross section and reaction rate of the two-proton fusion process p + p -> D + e+ + nu_e. The calculation included the one-pion-exchange contribution for low-energy pp-scattering, the AAV one-nucleon loop anomaly for the p + p -> D + W transition, and unitarization by summing an infinite series of proton-proton bubble diagrams. The ambiguity from the shift of virtual nucleon momentum in the loop diagrams was fixed by requiring gauge invariance under gauge transformations of the deuteron field.

## Key numbers

- The reaction rate of two-proton fusion at solar core temperature T_c = 15.5 x 10^6 K is <v sigma> = 3.44 x 10^(-43) cm^3 s^(-1), which is a factor of 2.90 +/- 0.87 larger than the potential approach result.
- The deuteron binding energy is epsilon_D = 2.225 MeV, fitted using a three-dimensional cutoff Lambda_D = 64.843 MeV, giving an effective deuteron radius r_D = 3.043 fm.
- The two-nucleon loop correction to the binding energy is (delta epsilon_D)_two-loop = 0.36 MeV, about 16% of the one-nucleon loop binding energy, leading to an estimated theoretical uncertainty of +/- 30% for cross sections.
- The cross section for the two-proton fusion is sigma = (1/(1 + 52743 v^2)) x (C^2(v)/v) x 1.34 x 10^(-48) cm^2.
- The predicted solar core temperature required to reconcile with solar luminosity is T_c = 13.8^(+0.5)_(-0.4) x 10^6 K.
- The predicted flux for ^8B neutrinos (KAMIOKANDE threshold above 7 MeV) is Phi_B = 0.37^(+0.61)_(-0.19) x 10^6 cm^(-2) s^(-1).
- The predicted signals are S_Ga = 91.9^(+6.2)_(-2.9) SNU (experiment: 77 +/- 8 SNU) and S_Cl = 1.10^(+0.88)_(-0.33) SNU (experiment: 2.55 +/- 0.25 SNU).

## Context

This work extends the authors' own relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (Ivanov et al., 1995, 1996) and directly contradicts the non-relativistic potential approach calculations of Bethe and Critchfield (1939) and the Standard Solar Model predictions of Bahcall and Pinsonneault (1995), which agree with each other to within 3%. The enhanced reaction rate implies a lower solar core temperature, following the analysis of Castellani et al. (1997), and provides a possible explanation for the solar neutrino problem by strongly suppressing high-energy neutrino fluxes, though the model underproduces the Chlorine and KAMIOKANDE signals compared to experimental data.
