# The collective excitation of nuclear matter in a bosonized Landau Fermi liquid model

> The collective excitation of nuclear matter is analyzed in a bosonized Landau Fermi liquid model. When the nonlinear self-interacting terms of scalar mesons are included in Walecka model, the collective excitation energy of nuclear matter can be obtained self-consistently, and the calculation results are consistent with the corresponding experimental data of the nucleus ${}^{208}Pb$ when the quantum number of the orientation of the total spin $m$ is zero. The cases with the nonzero $m$ values are also studied, and it is found that the collective excitation energy of nuclear matter decreases with the absolute value of $m$ increasing when the total spin is conserved. Moreover, four kinds of collective excitation modes of nuclear matter are discussed when the isospin and spin of nucleons are taken into account. The direct interaction between two nucleons near Fermi surface only changes the effective nucleon mass and Fermi velocity, while the exchange interaction plays an critical role in the collective excitation of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that when nonlinear self-interacting terms of scalar mesons are included in the Walecka model, the collective excitation energy of nuclear matter can be obtained self-consistently, and the calculation results are consistent with experimental data for the nucleus \({}^{208}\)Pb, but only when the quantum number of the orientation of the total spin \(m\) is zero; for nonzero \(m\) values, the collective excitation energy decreases as the absolute value of \(m\) increases.

## Method

The authors extend a bosonized Landau Fermi liquid model to nuclear matter by deriving the equation of motion from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, integrating over quasi-nucleon momentum to obtain a reduced equation for the quasi-nucleon density. The Fermi liquid function, representing the interaction between quasi-nucleons near the Fermi surface, is evaluated from the Walecka model Lagrangian including nonlinear scalar meson self-interactions, using the NL3 parameter set in the relativistic mean-field approximation. The quasi-nucleon density is expanded in spherical harmonics, and the resulting Hamiltonian is diagonalized to obtain collective excitation energies for different angular momentum quantum numbers \(l\) and \(m\).

## Key numbers

* Collective excitation energy of saturation nuclear matter for \(l=0\), \(m=0\): \(E_0 = 15.20\) MeV.
* Collective excitation energy of saturation nuclear matter for \(l=1\), \(m=0\): \(E_1 = 13.88\) MeV.
* Collective excitation energy of saturation nuclear matter for \(l=2\), \(m=0\): \(E_2 = 10.58\) MeV.
* Experimental isoscalar giant monopole resonance (l=0) central energy for \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(E_S = 14.17 \pm 0.28\) MeV.
* Experimental isovector giant dipole resonance (l=1) central energy for \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(E_V = 13.5 \pm 0.2\) MeV.
* Experimental isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (l=2) central energy for \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(E_S = 10.9 \pm 0.1\) MeV.
* Fermi momentum for saturation nuclear matter: \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* For \(l=1\), collective excitation energy with \(m=0\) is 13.88 MeV, while with \(m=\pm 1\) it is 10.12 MeV.
* For \(l=2\), collective excitation energy with \(m=0\) is 10.58 MeV, while with \(m=\pm 2\) it is 10.09 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the bosonized Landau Fermi liquid model previously developed by Sun (2010, 2012) by including nonlinear self-interacting terms of scalar mesons in the Walecka model, which allows for a self-consistent calculation of the effective nucleon mass rather than treating it as a parameter. The approach is related to the work of Friman (2019) who expanded the nucleon-nucleon interaction in Legendre polynomials, and to earlier Landau Fermi liquid studies of nuclear collective excitations by Blaizot (1978) and Holzwarth (1981). The results enable future comparison with experimental giant resonance data for finite nuclei and provide a framework for analyzing four distinct collective excitation modes (isoscalar/isovector and spin scalar/vector) in nuclear matter, with predictions that the isovector and spin vector mode energy may be about four times that of the isoscalar and spin scalar mode.
