# Global $Λ$ Polarization in high energy collisions

> With a Yang-Mills flux-tube initial state and a high resolution (3+1)D Particle-in-Cell Relativistic (PICR) hydrodynamics simulation, we calculate the $Λ$ polarization for different energies. The origination of polarization in high energy collisions is discussed, and we find linear impact parameter dependence of the global $Λ$ polarization. Furthermore, the global $Λ$ polarization in our model decreases very fast in the low energy domain, and the decline curve fits well the recent results of Beam Energy Scan (BES) program launched by the STAR collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The time evolution of polarization is also discussed.

## Key claim

The global Λ polarization in high-energy heavy-ion collisions originates from initial orbital angular momentum and shows a linear dependence on impact parameter, not the quadratic dependence of the angular momentum itself, because polarization is an intensive quantity while angular momentum is extensive. The model's polarization decline with decreasing collision energy fits the STAR Beam Energy Scan results, but the authors note that at 62.4 GeV the polarization signal vanishes at central collisions and the hydrodynamic model loses validity at late expansion stages.

## Method

The authors used a Yang-Mills flux-tube initial state with a Bag Model equation of state, then evolved the system with a high-resolution (3+1)D Particle-in-Cell Relativistic (PICR) hydrodynamics simulation. They computed the complete Λ polarization 3-vector including both the classical vorticity term and the previously neglected expansion term, then averaged the y-component over momentum space to obtain the global polarization. The calculation was performed for Au+Au collisions at multiple beam energies from 11.5 GeV to 200 GeV and for various impact parameters.

## Key numbers

* Global Λ polarization shows linear increase with impact parameter ratio b0 = b/2R at 11.5 GeV, 27.0 GeV, and 62.4 GeV, with vanishing signal at 62.4 GeV for central collisions below 20% centrality.
* The polarization drops very fast from 11.5 GeV to 62.4 GeV and tends to saturate above 62.4 GeV, fitting STAR BES data at energies 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27.0, 39.0, 62.4, and 200 GeV.
* The angular momentum per baryon from vortical flow is approximately 10^4 MeV·fm/c, which is 3-4 orders of magnitude larger than the spin angular momentum of 98.5 MeV·fm/c carried by one polarized baryon.
* At 62.4 GeV, negative polarization values appear after 10 fm/c, indicating loss of hydrodynamic model validity at late stages.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic polarization formalism of Becattini et al. (2013) and the earlier PICR hydrodynamics studies by the same group (My1, My2), by including the previously neglected second term of the polarization formula that accounts for system expansion and relativistic modifications. The results directly address the energy dependence of Λ polarization observed in the STAR Beam Energy Scan program (Lisa, 2016), which had not been seen in earlier experiments at Fermilab and higher RHIC energies. The findings enable future tests with the upgraded Event Plane Detector at RHIC's BES II program to precisely determine centrality dependence and verify the predicted momentum-space polarization patterns.
