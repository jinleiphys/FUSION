# Transverse spin polarization as a novel probe of medium-induced transverse-momentum-broadening effect

> The transverse polarization of $Λ$ hyperons within unpolarized jets originates from the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) fragmentation function $D_{1T}^\perp (z, p_T, μ^2)$. In the vacuum environment, the QCD evolution of this TMD fragmentation function is governed by the Collins-Soper equation. However, in the presence of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium, the jet-medium interaction induces a transverse-momentum-broadening effect that modifies the QCD evolution. As a result, the transverse spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions differs from that in $pp$ collisions. We demonstrate that this difference serves as a sensitive probe for studying jet-medium interaction, offering a novel perspective through the spin degree of freedom.

## Key claim

The transverse spin polarization of Λ hyperons within unpolarized jets in relativistic heavy-ion collisions differs from that in pp collisions because the jet-medium interaction in quark-gluon plasma induces a transverse-momentum-broadening effect that modifies the QCD Collins-Soper evolution of the TMD fragmentation function D₁T⊥; this difference is proposed as a sensitive probe of jet-medium interactions, though the authors adopt a naive model focusing on a u quark jet and neglect flavor-dependent effects to demonstrate the qualitative behavior.

## Method

The authors split the QCD evolution of the TMD fragmentation function D₁T⊥ in heavy-ion collisions into two parts: in-medium jet evolution encoding energy loss and transverse momentum broadening effects, and TMD hadronization in vacuum governed by the Collins-Soper equation. They model the medium-induced transverse-momentum-broadening effect using a Gaussian ansatz exp[-(1/4)⟨q̂L⟩ bT²] and also a non-Gaussian broadening function with a power-law tail, then compute the medium-modified TMD fragmentation functions via Fourier transforms from impact parameter space. The energy loss effect is incorporated through a cascade spectrum function Cji(ξ, τmax) obtained by solving the Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff/Zakharov evolution equation, with the whole process factorized into in-medium parton branching followed by vacuum hadronization.

## Key numbers

- Gaussian width for unpolarized TMD fragmentation function: ⟨p⊥²⟩ = 0.19 GeV²
- Gaussian width for polarized TMD fragmentation function: ⟨MD²⟩ = 0.118 GeV²
- Infrared cutoff bmax ≈ 1 GeV⁻¹ used in the b* prescription
- Non-Gaussian broadening function parameter: Λ = 0.2 GeV
- Color factor for quark jets: CF = 4/3; for gluon jets: CA = 3
- Number of active quark flavors: nf = 4
- Average path length assumed for energy loss calculation: ⟨L⟩ = 4 fm
- Strong coupling parameter for energy loss: ᾱs = 0.2
- The nuclear modification to transverse polarization becomes barely visible at p⊥ ∼ 4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of TMD fragmentation functions and their QCD evolution, previously established for the Sivers function by Aybat (2011) and Echevarria (2014), to the medium-modified environment of heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the framework for medium-modified unpolarized fragmentation functions developed by Chen (2016) and the transverse momentum broadening formalism from Mueller (2016) and Iancu (2014). The paper enables future experimental measurements at RHIC and the LHC to use transverse spin polarization as a novel probe of jet-medium interactions, complementing traditional jet quenching observables, and highlights the need for more data from pp and ep collisions to enable flavor-separated global analyses of the polarized fragmentation function.
