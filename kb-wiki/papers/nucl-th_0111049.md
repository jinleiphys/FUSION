# GRS computation of deep inelastic electron scattering on 4He

> We compute cross sections for inclusive scattering of high energy electrons on 4He, based on the two lowest orders of the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) series. The required one- and two-particle density matrices are obtained from non-relativistic 4He wave functions using realistic models for the nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interaction. Predictions for E=3.6 GeV agree well with the NE3 SLAC-Virginia data.

## Key claim

The GRS expansion truncated to the two lowest orders (0th and 1st) yields cross sections for inclusive electron scattering on 4He at E=3.6 GeV that agree well with the NE3 SLAC-Virginia data, but only for scattering angles where Q² exceeds approximately 2.0-2.5 GeV²; for smaller Q² the theory overpredicts the data around the quasi-elastic peak.

## Method

The authors computed the reduced response function φ(q,y) using the 0th and 1st order terms of the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) series expansion in inverse powers of the 3-momentum transfer q. The required one- and two-body non-diagonal density matrices were obtained from non-relativistic 4He ground state wave functions calculated with the Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (CHH) technique using the AV18, AV18+Urbana IX, and AV14+Urbana VIII nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions. The spectral function was taken from Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996), and the nuclear structure functions were constructed via a convolution of the point-nucleon structure function with averaged free nucleon structure functions including mixing coefficient C₂₂.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy E = 3.595 GeV used for comparison with NE3 SLAC-Virginia data at scattering angles θ = 16°, 20°, 25°, 30°
- Critical Q² threshold for validity of the convolution formula: Q²_c ≈ 2.0-2.5 GeV²
- For 4He, the minimal separation energy Δ₀ ≈ 20.2 MeV; average separation energy ⟨Δ⟩ ≈ 50 MeV
- The point-nucleon structure function f^{PN,A}(x=1,Q²) for 4He is approximately 2.2 times larger than for nuclei with A ≥ 12
- For the deuteron, f^{PN,A}(x=1,Q²) is approximately 5.5 times larger than for nuclei with A ≥ 12
- The AV18 model underbinds 4He by about 4 MeV relative to experiment
- Up to N_tot ≈ 200 basis functions were used in the CHH expansion for convergence

## Context

This work extends the GRS approach, previously applied mainly to medium and heavy nuclei (A ≥ 12), to the light nucleus 4He by exploiting accurate non-relativistic wave functions from the CHH method. It builds on the demonstration by Rinat and collaborators (2000) and by Jin and collaborators (1995) that the GRS and Impulse Series (IS) approaches are equivalent when expanded to the same order. The calculations enable comparison with existing NE3 data and provide predictions for future JLab experiments at 6 GeV with scattering angles 15°, 23°, 30°, 45°, and 60° covering 0.2 < x < 1.0 and 1 < Q² < 8.0 GeV². The method contrasts with the approach of Efros and co-workers (1990s), which is not feasible to extend to high energies.
