# Power Counting to Saturation

> We present a description of saturation in small $x$ deep inelastic scattering from power counting in a top-down effective theory derived from QCD. A factorization formula isolates the universal physics of the nucleus at leading power in $x$. The onset of saturation is then understood as a breakdown in the expansion in an emergent power counting parameter, which is defined by the matrix element of a gauge invariant operator. We identify a new radiation mode, which enables us to extend previous literature by distinguishing the appearance of the saturation scale from the transition to non-linear evolution.

# Power Counting to Saturation

## Key claim
Saturation in small-x deep inelastic scattering can be understood as a breakdown in the expansion of an emergent power counting parameter defined by the matrix element of a gauge invariant operator, rather than as a direct consequence of non-linear evolution. The authors distinguish the appearance of the saturation scale from the transition to non-linear evolution by identifying a new collinear-soft radiation mode that becomes relevant when x << 1/N << 1 for a nucleus with N nucleons.

## Method
The authors construct a top-down effective field theory from QCD using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) with a power counting parameter θ = x. They derive a factorization formula at leading power in x that separates the universal medium physics (encoded in a medium structure function B_n) from the probe dynamics (encoded in a soft function S_m). The expansion in Glauber interactions (the opacity expansion) is organized by the parameter λ2 = L_D/λ_mfp, where L_D is the correlation length of color sources and λ_mfp is the mean free path of the dipole probe. The authors resum the series of multiple independent Glauber exchanges to all orders, obtaining a path-ordered exponential that defines the mean free path as an operator matrix element.

## Key numbers
- The power counting parameter is x = Q^2/(2P·q), with x << 1 defining the small-x regime
- The Glauber exchange coupling constant is C_G(μ) = 8πα_s(μ)
- The mean free path scales as λ_mfp ~ xQ/(α_s(Q)^2 Γ), where Γ ~ Λ_QCD^2 is the nucleon density per unit area
- The parameter λ2 = L_D/λ_mfp ~ Γ/Q^2 << 1 ensures independent scattering centers
- The saturation scale Q_s(y) is defined by ∫_0^{L_N^-} dy^-/λ_mfp(|b|=1/Q_s, Q_s, y) = 1
- For a nucleus modeled as non-interacting nucleons, λ1 = Γ(y_⊥) (α_s π^2/N_C) b_⊥^2 xG_N(x, 1/b_⊥)
- The resummed saturation scale has power law scaling Q_s(x) = Q_s0 x^{-2.44 α_s N_C/π^2}
- The collinear-soft mode scales as p_cs ~ (H/Q)(N x^2, 1/N, x), where N is the number of nucleons seen by the probe

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism (McLerran, 1994; Iancu, 2003; Gelis, 2010) by providing a top-down effective field theory derivation from QCD, in contrast to the bottom-up approach of CGC. The factorization framework builds on the SCET approach of Bauer (2002) and Rothstein (2016), and the Glauber mode treatment follows Neill (2023) for small-x DIS factorization. The opacity expansion method is related to the work of Gyulassy (2000). The medium structure function B_n obeys the BFKL equation (Kuraev, 1977; Balitsky, 1978) at one loop, and the transition to non-linear evolution is expected to reproduce the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation (Balitsky, 1995; Kovchegov, 1999) and JIMWLK equation (Jalilian-Marian, 1997; Weigert, 2000; Iancu, 2000) when the collinear-soft mode becomes relevant. This work enables a systematic power counting framework for distinguishing saturation from non-linear evolution in small-x physics, with implications for the Electron Ion Collider program.
