# Axial vector diquark correlations in the nucleon: Structure functions and static properties

> In order to extract information on the strength of quark-quark correlations in the axial vector (a.v.) diquark channel ($J^P=1^+, T=1$), we analyze the quark light cone momentum distributions in the nucleon, in particular their flavor dependencies, and the static properties of the nucleon. To construct the nucleon as a relativistic 3-quark bound state, we use a simple 'static' approximation to the full Faddeev equation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, including correlations in the scalar ($J^P=0^+, T=0$) and a.v. diquark channels. It is shown that the a.v. diquark correlations should be rather weak compared to the scalar ones. From our analysis we extract information on the strength of the correlations as well as on the probability of the a.v. diquark channel.

## Key claim

The axial vector diquark correlations in the nucleon should be rather weak compared to the scalar diquark correlations, with an upper limit on the probability of the axial vector diquark channel of approximately 10% or less, based on the flavor dependence of valence quark distributions and static properties.

## Method

The authors construct the nucleon as a relativistic 3-quark bound state using a static approximation to the full Faddeev equation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, including correlations in both the scalar (J^P=0^+, T=0) and axial vector (J^P=1^+, T=1) diquark channels. They calculate quark light cone momentum distributions relevant for unpolarized structure functions, including the flavor dependence of valence quark distributions, and evaluate static properties such as magnetic moments, axial vector coupling constants, and the pion-nucleon coupling constant. The coupling constants G_s and G_a for the scalar and axial vector diquark channels are treated as parameters relative to the pion channel coupling G_pi, with ratios r_s = G_s/G_pi and r_a = G_a/G_pi.

## Key numbers

- The probability of the axial vector diquark channel is approximately 10% or less (stated as "less than about 10%" in the abstract and "less than or similar to 10%" in the introduction)
- For the 'color current' type interaction Lagrangian, the ratios are r_s = 0.5 and r_a = 0.25
- The normalization condition for the nucleon vertex function gives W_s + W_a = 1, where W_s and W_a are the contributions of scalar and axial vector diquark channels to the total baryon number
- The number sum rules give ∫_0^1 dx F_{Q/P}(x) = 1 and ∫_0^1 dx F_{Q(D)/P}(x) = 2

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Mineo et al. (2001) that considered only the scalar diquark channel in the static approximation to the Faddeev equation. It addresses the flavor asymmetry of valence quark distributions in the proton, where empirical data show d_v(x) is softer than u_v(x), and investigates how axial vector diquark correlations influence this asymmetry. The results enable further refinement of quark-diquark models of the nucleon and provide constraints on the strength of quark-quark interactions in the axial vector channel for future calculations of nucleon structure functions and electromagnetic form factors.
