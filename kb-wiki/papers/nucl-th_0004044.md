# Baryonic contributions to the dilepton spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the baryonic contributions to the dilepton yield in high energy heavy ion collisions within the context of a transport model. The relative contribution of the baryonic and mesonic sources are examined. It is observed that most dominant among the baryonic channels is the decay of N*(1520) and mostly confined in the region below the rho peak. In a transport theory implementation we find the baryonic contribution to the lepton pair yield to be small.

# Baryonic contributions to the dilepton spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The baryonic contribution to the dilepton yield in high energy heavy ion collisions is small when evaluated within a transport model, despite the fact that thermal rate calculations show baryons dominate the low invariant mass region below the rho peak. The dominant baryonic channel is the decay of the N*(1520) resonance, but its actual contribution is severely reduced by experimental acceptance cuts and by the low baryon content of the central rapidity region at CERN SPS energies.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate nuclear collisions and compute dilepton production from direct vector meson decays (rho, omega) and Dalitz decays of mesons (omega, a1) and baryons (N*(1520)). The differential partial widths for baryon resonance decays into nucleon plus lepton pair were calculated using both relativistic and nonrelativistic interaction Lagrangians with Vector Meson Dominance, and the transport calculation tracked each hadron's four-momentum and lifetime to accumulate the dilepton invariant mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1520) radiative decay width into N gamma is 0.78 MeV using the relativistic Lagrangian parameter set (experimental value 0.55 +/- 0.1 MeV); the N*(1520) decay width into N rho is 22.35 MeV (experimental value 24.0 +/- 6.0 MeV)
- Using the nonrelativistic Lagrangian with VMD, the coupling constant f_rho = 5.5 yields Gamma(N* -> N gamma) = 0.88 MeV and Gamma(N* -> N rho) = 30 MeV
- At an invariant mass of 400 MeV, the 160 AGeV calculation is 20% higher than the 40 AGeV one
- The chemical freeze-out temperature for SPS collisions at 160 GeV per nucleon is about T = 170 MeV with baryon density about rho = rho_0
- The mass resolution assumed for predictions is Delta M / M = 1%
- The baryonic contribution from N*(1520) Dalitz decay is most important in the invariant mass region 0.35 < M < 0.75 GeV/c^2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of dilepton excess observed by the HELIOS/3 and CERES collaborations at CERN SPS, which had been interpreted either as a dropping vector meson mass (Brown-Rho scaling) or as a broadened rho spectral function from coupling to baryonic resonances (Rapp, Urban, Buballa, Wambach; Friman and Pirner; Lenske). The authors explicitly test the baryonic contribution using a transport model rather than thermal rate calculations alone, finding that the baryonic contribution is much smaller than suggested by rate calculations from Rapp et al. (1996) and Steele, Yamagishi, and Zahed. The results are consistent with earlier estimates by Song and Koch (1996) and with hydrodynamic model results by Prakash et al. (1999). The predictions for low-energy CERN runs (40 AGeV) are intended to test whether in-medium modifications of vector mesons can be distinguished from vacuum spectral functions.
