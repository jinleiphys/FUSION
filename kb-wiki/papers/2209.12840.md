# The Radius of PSR J0740+6620 from NICER with NICER Background Estimates

> We report a revised analysis for the radius, mass, and hot surface regions of the massive millisecond pulsar PSR J0740+6620, studied previously with joint fits to NICER and XMM-Newton data by Riley et al. (2021) and Miller et al. (2021). We perform a similar Bayesian estimation for the pulse-profile model parameters, except that instead of fitting simultaneously the XMM-Newton data, we use the best available NICER background estimates to constrain the number of photons detected from the source. This approach eliminates any potential issues in the cross-calibration between these two instruments, providing thus an independent check of the robustness of the analysis. The obtained neutron star parameter constraints are compatible with the already published results, with a slight dependence on how conservative the imposed background limits are. A tighter lower limit causes the inferred radius to increase, and a tighter upper limit causes it to decrease. We also extend the study of the inferred emission geometry to examine the degree of deviation from antipodality of the hot regions. We show that there is a significant offset to an antipodal spot configuration, mainly due to the non-half-cycle azimuthal separation of the two emitting spots. The offset angle from the antipode is inferred to be above 25 degrees with 84% probability. This seems to exclude a centered-dipolar magnetic field in PSR J0740+6620.

## Key claim

The radius of PSR J0740+6620 inferred using only NICER data with the new 3C50 background estimates is consistent with previously published joint NICER+XMM-Newton results, but the inferred radius depends on how conservative the imposed background limits are: a tighter lower limit causes the inferred radius to increase, and a tighter upper limit causes it to decrease.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian pulse-profile modeling of NICER data from PSR J0740+6620 using the 3C50 background model, which uses three non-source count rates to select and rescale background components from libraries derived from blank-sky observations. Instead of fitting simultaneously with XMM-Newton data as in prior analyses, they used NICER background estimates to constrain the number of source photons, eliminating potential cross-calibration issues. They also extended the analysis to examine the degree of deviation from antipodality of the hot emitting regions.

## Key numbers

- Net count rate for PSR J0740+6620: 0.028 counts per second at 0.3-2.0 keV, with no detection at 2-4 or 4-12 keV
- Total exposure time for the 3C50 data set: 1.55569 Ms (from an initial 2.79 Ms)
- Adopted 3C50 background uncertainty (1 sigma) estimates: 0.018 counts per second (0.3-2.0 keV), 0.007 counts per second (2-4 keV), and 0.010 counts per second (4-12 keV)
- Equatorial circumferential radius from NICER-only 3C50+bkg analysis using Illinois-Maryland pipeline: median 13.959 km with 68% credible interval 12.097-16.747 km
- Equatorial circumferential radius from NICER+XMM 3C50+bkg analysis using Illinois-Maryland pipeline: median 13.362 km with 68% credible interval 11.980-16.177 km
- Equatorial circumferential radius from NICER-only 3C50 analysis using X-PSI: 12.50 km with 68% credible interval -1.58/+1.69 km
- Offset angle from antipodal spot configuration inferred to be above 25 degrees with 84% probability

## Context

This work extends the analyses of Riley et al. (2021) and Miller et al. (2021), who performed joint fits to NICER and XMM-Newton data for PSR J0740+6620, by using only NICER data with improved background estimates to provide an independent check of robustness. The results confirm that the radius inferences are consistent across methods, and the finding of significant non-antipodality of hot regions appears to exclude a centered-dipolar magnetic field in this pulsar. This enables future studies to use NICER-only analyses for neutron star radius measurements, reducing reliance on cross-calibration with other X-ray observatories.
