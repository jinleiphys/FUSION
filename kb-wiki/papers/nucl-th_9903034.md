# Particle Mixing and Charge Asymmetric $ΛN$ Forces

> We calculate the contributions of a particular set of charge asymmetric $ΛN$ interactions to the difference of the separation energies of $^4_Λ$He and $^4_Λ$H. We use perturbation theory with four-body variational Monte Carlo wave functions calculated from a Hamiltonian with two- and three-hadron forces. We compare with the data and with an earlier calculation made by one of us which employed a two-body wave function of the $Λ$-nucleus type.

# Particle Mixing and Charge Asymmetric ΛN Forces

## Key claim

The set of charge asymmetric ΛN interactions based on particle mixing (Σ⁰-Λ mixing, ρ⁰-ω-φ mixing, and π⁰-η-η' mixing) cannot explain the full charge asymmetry observed in the Λ-separation energy difference between ⁴ΛHe and ⁴ΛH, and the spin dependence of these potentials appears inconsistent with experiment. The authors note that the calculation omits possible D-state components of the hypernuclear wave function that could couple through tensor operators, and that an update of mixing and coupling constants may be needed.

## Method

The authors performed first-order perturbation theory using four-body variational Monte Carlo wave functions for ⁴ΛHe and ⁴ΛH, generated from a Hamiltonian containing charge-symmetric two- and three-hadron forces (Reid v₈ NN potential, Urbana-type ΛN and ΛNN potentials, and Urbana IX NNN potential). They evaluated the contributions of five charge-asymmetric potentials (π-exchange from Σ⁰-Λ mixing, ρ-exchange from Σ⁰-Λ mixing, π⁰-η mixing, ρ⁰-ω mixing, and ρ⁰-φ mixing) to the Λ-separation energy difference ΔBΛ, and compared the results with an earlier two-body Λ-plus-core-nucleus model calculation by Coon and collaborators (1979).

## Key numbers

- Experimental ground-state Λ-separation energy difference: ΔBΛ^exp = BΛ(⁴ΛHe) – BΛ(⁴ΛH) = +350 ± 50 keV, from ground-state separation energies of 2.39 ± 0.03 MeV (⁴ΛHe) and 2.04 ± 0.04 MeV (⁴ΛH)
- Experimental excited-state Λ-separation energy difference: ΔBΛ*^exp ≈ +230 keV
- Coulomb correction to ground-state ΔBΛ: ΔB^C_Λ = -42 keV (from variational Monte Carlo calculation)
- Coulomb correction to excited-state ΔBΛ: -28 keV
- Total ground-state ΔBΛ from particle mixing potentials (4-body calculation): +91 keV (compared to +174 keV from the 2-body model)
- Total excited-state ΔBΛ from particle mixing potentials (4-body calculation): -33 keV (compared to -36 keV from the 2-body model)
- Individual contributions to ground-state ΔBΛ (4-body): π-exchange Σ⁰-Λ = +45 keV, ρ-exchange Σ⁰-Λ = +75 keV, π⁰-η = -7 keV, ρ⁰-ω = -14 keV, ρ⁰-φ = -8 keV
- Calculated ³H energy: -8.27 MeV (experimental -8.48 MeV); ³He energy: -7.63 MeV (experimental -7.72 MeV); ⁴He energy: -28.39 MeV (experimental -28.30 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the earlier perturbative estimates of Dalitz and Von Hippel (1964) and Coon and collaborators (1979), which used a two-body Λ-plus-core-nucleus wave function, by employing a full four-body variational Monte Carlo approach developed by Carlson, Pandharipande, and Wiringa (1983) for light nuclei. The results show that the two-body model overestimates short-range contributions, particularly from ρ-exchange, and that the particle-mixing mechanism, which works well for NN charge asymmetry, appears inadequate to explain the full charge asymmetry in hypernuclei. The authors identify the need for future calculations with a more general charge-symmetric ΛN interaction that includes tensor components, and an update of the Coleman-Glashow mixing parameters and coupling constants.
