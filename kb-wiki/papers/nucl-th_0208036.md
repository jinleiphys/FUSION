# Regularization of Legendre Function Series for Charged Particles Improved Nearside-Farside Subamplitudes

> A simple regularization procedure is proposed for the Legendre function series of improved nearside-farside subamplitudes for charged particles elastic scattering. The procedure is the extension of the usual one which defines the partial wave series for the scattering amplitude in the presence of a long range Coulomb term in the potential, and it provides the same convergence rate.

# Regularization of Legendre Function Series for Charged Particles Improved Nearside-Farside Subamplitudes

## Key claim

The proposed regularization procedure makes the Legendre function series for improved nearside-farside subamplitudes as rapidly convergent as those of more conventional approaches, but only when a long-range Coulomb term is present in the potential; for short-range potentials, the extended Yennie-Ravenall-Wilson resummation already provides equivalent convergence with no practical advantage.

## Method

The author extends the standard regularization procedure for partial wave series in the presence of a Coulomb term, which adds the explicit Rutherford amplitude and subtracts its formal series, to resummed forms of the full amplitude and nearside-farside subamplitudes. For improved NF subamplitudes of order r, the method subtracts the pure Coulomb resummed coefficients from the full resummed coefficients in the Legendre function series, then adds the exact pure Coulomb improved NF subamplitudes expressed in terms of the usual Fuller-Rutherford subamplitudes plus simple functions of the resummation parameters. The resulting series for the difference term converges as rapidly as the standard r=0 regularized series.

## Key numbers

- Sommerfeld parameter η = 10 used for pure Coulomb test calculations, typical for heavy-ion scattering.
- For r=1 improved resummation with η=10: β₁ = 0.9802 + 0.1980 i.
- For r=2 improved resummation with η=10: β₁ = 1.0072 + 0.1166 i and β₂ = 0.7804 + 0.6052 i.
- For the ¹⁶O + ¹⁶O case at E_lab = 145 MeV: β₁ = -0.9997 - 0.0798 i.
- With 150 partial waves and the regularization procedure, reliable results are obtained; with extended YRW resummation of order 4, 150 partial waves are insufficient for θ ≲ 5°.
- For pure Coulomb scattering with extended YRW resummation of order 4, even 1000 partial waves do not give numerically satisfactory results for θ ≲ 5°.

## Context

This work extends the improved nearside-farside method proposed by Anni (2001, 2002) and the original Fuller (1975) NF decomposition, which separates elastic scattering amplitudes into nearside and farside contributions. The regularization procedure solves convergence problems at forward angles that arise when applying the extended YRW resummation technique of Anni (1981) to asymptotically Coulombic partial wave series. The method enables practical calculations of improved NF subamplitudes for α-particle and heavy-ion scattering without requiring excessive numbers of partial waves, though the NF decomposition remains unreliable near θ ≈ 180° due to a mathematical continuation artifact in the Legendre function splitting.
