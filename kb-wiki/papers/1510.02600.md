# Elastic photonuclear cross sections for bremsstrahlung from relativistic ions

> In this paper, we provide a procedure to calculate the bremsstrahlung spectrum for virtually any relativistic bare ion with charge 6$e$ or beyond, $Z\ge 6$, in ultraperipheral collisions with target nuclei. We apply the Weizsäcker-Williams method of virtual quanta to model the effect of the distribution of nuclear constituents on the interaction of the ion with the radiation target. This leads to a bremsstrahlung spectrum peaking at $2γ$ times the energy of the giant dipole resonance ($γ$ is the projectile energy in units of its rest energy). A central ingredient in the calculation is the cross section for elastic scattering of photons on the ion. This is only available in the literature for a few selected nuclei and, usually, only in a rather restricted parameter range. Hence we develop a procedure applicable for all $Z\geq6$ to estimate the elastic scattering. The elastic cross section is obtained at low to moderate photon energies, somewhat beyond the giant dipole resonance, by means of the optical theorem, a dispersion relation, and data on the total absorption cross section. The cross section is continued at higher energies by invoking depletion due to loss of coherence in the scattering. Our procedure is intended for any ion where absorption data is available and for moderate to high energies, $γ\gtrsim 10$.

## Key claim
The paper provides a procedure to calculate the bremsstrahlung spectrum for any relativistic bare ion with charge Z >= 6 in ultraperipheral collisions, but the procedure is only intended for moderate to high projectile energies (gamma >= 10) and requires that photon absorption data for the ion is available.

## Method
The authors apply the Weizsäcker-Williams method of virtual quanta to model the interaction of relativistic ions with target nuclei. They develop a general procedure to estimate the elastic photon scattering cross section for any ion with Z >= 6 by using the optical theorem, a dispersion relation, and total absorption cross section data at low to moderate photon energies, then extending to higher energies by introducing a depletion factor based on loss of coherence in scattering. The depletion factor includes an exponential term dependent on photon energy and scattering angle, plus a small incoherent contribution for lighter nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The bremsstrahlung spectrum peaks at 2γ times the energy of the giant dipole resonance (γ is the projectile energy in units of its rest energy).
- For lead (Z=82), the dividing energy between the two theoretical constructs is set at ℏω̃₂ = 22.0 MeV.
- The universal dividing energy formula is ℏω̃₂ [MeV] = 17 A^(-1/3) + 25 A^(-1/6) + 8.9, where A is the nucleon number.
- The damping parameter ε = 0.086 × (Z/82) and δ = 0.013 (dimensionless).
- For carbon (Z=6), the dividing energy is ℏω̃₂ = 32.8 MeV.
- The k-values extracted for different materials are: C = 1.99, O = 1.82, Ar = 2.15, Pb = 2.27.
- For lead at γ = 170, the bremsstrahlung spectrum shows a peak near 4 GeV.
- The procedure should not be used beyond the pion-production threshold of 140 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculation for lead ions (Sørensen 2010) to a general procedure applicable to any ion with Z >= 6. Prior approaches by Ziegler for lead and by Hayward (1983, 1992) for carbon and oxygen introduced angular dependence into the dispersion relation for forward scattering, which the authors note is internally inconsistent. The new procedure enables calculation of bremsstrahlung spectra for ions such as argon, which will be available for fixed-target experiments at the Super Proton Synchrotron at CERN starting in 2015. The method may also yield information about nuclear shapes and giant dipole resonances for nuclei with lifetimes down to the picosecond regime.
