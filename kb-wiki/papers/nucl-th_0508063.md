# Spectral properties of nuclear matter

> We review self-consistent spectral methods for nuclear matter calculations. The in-medium T-matrix approach is conserving and thermodynamically consistent. It gives both the global and the single-particle properties the system. The T-matrix approximation allows to address the pairing phenomenon in cold nuclear matter. A generalization of nuclear matter calculations to the superfluid phase is discussed and numerical results are presented for this case. The linear response of a correlated system going beyond the Hartree-Fock+Random-Phase-Approximation scheme is studied. The polarization is obtained by solving a consistent Bethe-Salpeter equation for the coupling of dressed nucleons to an external field. We find that multipair contributions are important for the spin(isospin) response when the interaction is spin(isospin) dependent.

# Spectral properties of nuclear matter

## Key claim

Self-consistent spectral methods for nuclear matter, particularly the in-medium T-matrix approach, can describe both global thermodynamic properties and single-particle properties, but the linear response calculations show that multipair contributions are important for the spin(isospin) response only when the interaction is spin(isospin) dependent; for scalar interactions, such multipair effects are small except for the damping of collective modes.

## Method

The paper reviews the self-consistent T-matrix approximation for nuclear matter, where nucleon propagators are dressed by a self-energy obtained from the sum of ladder diagrams, requiring iterative solution of the Dyson equation, the T-matrix equation, and the self-energy equation. For the superfluid phase, the formalism is generalized to include off-diagonal (anomalous) self-energies and a gap equation with dressed propagators. The linear response is calculated by solving a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed vertex, with the kernel derived consistently from the same generating functional as the self-energy, using a Gogny mean-field interaction plus a residual interaction.

## Key numbers

- The superfluid gap in correlated nuclear matter is strongly reduced compared to mean-field gap equation results, with the reduction explained by an effective modification of the density of states at the Fermi energy.
- For the isospin-dependent residual interaction, calculations are shown at momentum q=210 MeV and temperature T=15 MeV.
- The ω-sum rule for scalar residual interaction takes the form -∫(ω dω/2π) Im Π^r_{(ST)}(q,ω) = ρ q²/(2m) in all spin-isospin channels.
- The collective mode in the density response acquires a large width from coupling to multipair excitations, even at zero temperature (the small RPA width shown is due to finite temperature T=15 MeV).

## Context

This review extends the self-consistent T-matrix approach developed by Bozek (1998), Dickhoff (1999), and Dewulf (2000) for nuclear matter, and generalizes it to the superfluid phase following the work of Haussmann. The linear response method goes beyond the Hartree-Fock+RPA scheme by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with dressed vertices, addressing limitations of Fermi liquid theory (Pines) which does not allow for multipair excitations. The results enable improved calculations of neutrino rates in neutron stars and particle emission in hot nuclear matter, and the methods are applicable to other fermionic systems such as high-Tc superconductors and electron gases.
