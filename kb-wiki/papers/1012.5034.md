# Strong D* -> D+pi and B* -> B+pi couplings

> We compute g_{D* D pi} and g_{B* B pi} using a framework in which all elements are constrained by Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of QCD, and therefore incorporates a consistent, direct and simultaneous description of light- and heavy-quarks and the states they may constitute. We link these couplings with the heavy-light-meson leptonic decay constants, and thereby obtain g_{D* D pi}=15.9+2.1/-1.0 and g_{B* B pi}=30.0+3.2/-1.4. From the latter we infer \hat-g_B=0.37+0.04/-0.02. A comparison between g_{D* D pi} and g_{B* B pi} indicates that when the c-quark is a system's heaviest constituent, Lambda_{QCD}/m_c-corrections are not under good control.

## Key claim

The authors compute the strong couplings \(g_{D^* D\pi}\) and \(g_{B^* B\pi}\) using a Dyson-Schwinger equation framework that simultaneously describes light and heavy quarks, but they find that \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/m_c\) corrections are not under good control when the charm quark is the heaviest constituent, meaning the heavy-quark limit is unreliable for charm systems.

## Method

The calculation uses a symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) truncation scheme, where dressed-quark propagators are parametrized to incorporate dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) for light quarks, while heavy quarks (c, b) are treated with constituent-quark-like propagators. The meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for heavy-light mesons are modeled as Gaussian functions, with width parameters \(\omega_H\) and \(\omega_{H^*}\) fitted separately to reproduce the leptonic decay constants \(f_D\), \(f_{D^*}\), \(f_B\), and \(f_{B^*}\) from experiment and lattice QCD. The coupling \(g_{H^* H\pi}\) is then obtained from a triangle-diagram impulse approximation evaluated at the on-shell point for \(D^*\to D\pi\) and at \(q^2\to 0\) for the kinematically forbidden \(B^*\to B\pi\).

## Key numbers

- \(g_{D^* D\pi} = 15.9^{+2.1}_{-1.0}\) (dimensionless coupling for \(D^{*+}\to D^0\pi^+\) decay)
- \(g_{B^* B\pi} = 30.0^{+3.2}_{-1.4}\) (dimensionless coupling for the kinematically forbidden \(B^*\to B\pi\) process)
- \(\hat g_B = 0.37^{+0.04}_{-0.02}\) (inferred universal strong coupling from \(g_{B^* B\pi}\) via \(\hat g_H = g_{H^* H\pi} f_\pi / (2\sqrt{m_H m_{H^*}})\))
- \(\hat g_D = 0.53^{+0.07}_{-0.03}\) (corresponding value for charm systems)
- Width parameters from fit to leptonic decay constants: \(\omega_D = 1.26 \pm 0.10\) GeV, \(\omega_{D^*} = 1.21 \pm 0.14\) GeV, \(\omega_B = 2.26^{+0.76}_{-0.68}\) GeV, \(\omega_{B^*} = 1.58^{+0.52}_{-0.27}\) GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier DSE-based studies by Ivanov (1998) and Ivanov (2007) by allowing different sizes for pseudoscalar and vector heavy-light mesons and by letting the light quark carry a fraction of the meson's momentum, which significantly improves agreement with the CLEO measurement of \(g_{D^* D\pi}\) (Anastassov, 2001). The results contradict some QCD sum-rule estimates (Colangelo, 2000; Navarra, 2001; Duraes, 2004) and lattice-QCD simulations (de Divitiis, 1998; Abada, 2002; Ohki, 2008; Becirevic, 2009) that show larger values for \(\hat g_B\). The framework enables direct access to the chiral limit and a consistent treatment of light- and heavy-quark dynamics, providing a natural connection between leptonic decay constants and radiative-pion couplings.
