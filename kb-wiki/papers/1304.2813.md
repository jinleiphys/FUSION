# The EMC Effect and High Momentum Nucleons in Nuclei

> Recent developments in understanding the influence of the nucleus on deep-inelastic structure functions, the EMC effect, are reviewed. A new data base which expresses ratios of structure functions in terms of the Bjorken variable $x_A=AQ^2/(2M_A q_0)$ is presented. Information about two-nucleon short-range correlations from experiments is also discussed and the remarkable linear relation between short-range correlations and teh EMC effect is reviewed. A convolution model that relates the underlying source of the EMC effect to modification of either the mean-field nucleons or the short-range correlated nucleons is presented. It is shown that both approaches are equally successful in describing the current EMC data.

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence that the EMC effect (the reduction of nuclear structure functions relative to deuterium) is linearly correlated with the number of two-nucleon short-range correlated (SRC) pairs in a nucleus, but both a model where nucleon modification occurs in SRC pairs and a model where modification occurs in the mean field are equally successful in describing current EMC data.

## Method

The authors compile a new database of EMC ratios expressed in terms of the Bjorken variable \(x_A = AQ^2/(2M_A q_0)\), which corrects for the difference in the definition of the scaling variable between nuclei and deuterium. They review inclusive quasi-elastic \((e,e')\) cross section ratios measured at SLAC and Jefferson Lab for \(1.5 \le x_p \le 2\) and \(x_p \ge 2.25\) to extract scaling factors \(a_2\) and \(a_3\) that quantify the relative abundance of two-nucleon and three-nucleon SRC configurations. They also discuss exclusive \((e,e'pN)\) and \((p,2pn)\) measurements that confirm back-to-back correlations of high-momentum nucleons as a signature of SRC pairs. A convolution model is presented that attributes the EMC effect either to modification of mean-field nucleons or to modification of nucleons within SRC pairs.

## Key numbers

- The EMC effect reduces the per-nucleon DIS cross section ratio from about 1 at \(x_p \approx 0.3\) to as little as 0.8 at \(x_p \approx 0.7\) for iron relative to deuterium.
- The EMC effect is independent of \(Q^2\) for \(2 \le Q^2 \le 40\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The \(x_A\) correction reduces the slope of the EMC data for \(^{12}\)C by about 20% compared to the uncorrected \(x_p\) data.
- For inclusive QE scattering, cross section ratios for nuclei relative to \(^3\)He show a scaling plateau for \(1.5 \le x_p \le 2\) (two-nucleon SRC region) and for \(x_p \ge 2.25\) (three-nucleon SRC region).
- The minimum momentum component required to scatter at \(x_p \ge 1.4-1.5\) exceeds the Fermi momentum \(k_F\) of the nucleus.

## Context

This review extends the work of Frankfurt and Strikman (2012) on using the \(x_A\) scaling variable and the measurements of Seely (2009) that showed the EMC effect correlates with local rather than average nuclear density, breaking the simple density scaling observed by Gomez (1993). The paper enables future experiments to test whether nucleon modification occurs primarily in SRC pairs or in the mean field, and highlights that a modern model incorporating nucleon-nucleon correlations consistently with both nuclear DIS and Drell-Yan data does not yet exist.
