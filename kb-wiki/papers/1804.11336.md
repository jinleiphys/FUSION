# General structure of gauge boson propagator and its spectra in a hot magnetized medium

> Based on transversality condition of gauge boson self-energy we have systematically constructed the general structure of the gauge boson two-point functions using four linearly independent basis tensors in presence of a nontrivial background, i.e., hot magnetized material medium. The hard thermal loop approximation has been used for the heat bath to compute various form factors associated with the gauge boson's two point functions both in strong and weak field approximation. We have also analyzed the dispersion of a gauge boson (e.g., gluon) using the effective propagator both in strong and weak magnetic field approximation. The formalism is also applicable to QED. The presence of only thermal background leads to a longitudinal (plasmon) mode and a two-fold degenerate transverse mode. In presence of a hot magnetized background medium the degeneracy of the two transverse modes is lifted and one gets three quasiparticle modes. In weak field approximation one gets two transverse modes and one plasmon mode. On the other hand, in strong field approximation also one gets the three modes in Lowest Landau Level. The general structure of two-point function may be useful for computing the thermo-magnetic correction of various quantities associated with a gauge boson.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the general covariant structure of the gauge boson self-energy and propagator in a hot magnetized medium using four linearly independent basis tensors, based on the transversality condition; the formalism applies to both QCD and QED, but the explicit dispersion analysis is performed using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, which limits the results to the regime where the external momentum is soft compared to the temperature.

## Method

The authors first review the finite-temperature (zero magnetic field) case, where two projection tensors (longitudinal and transverse) suffice. They then introduce a constant magnetic field along the \(z\)-direction, which breaks rotational symmetry, and construct four mutually orthogonal projection tensors (\(B^{\mu\nu}\), \(R^{\mu\nu}\), \(Q^{\mu\nu}\), \(N^{\mu\nu}\)) from the four-vectors \(P^\mu\), \(u^\mu\) (heat bath), and \(n^\mu\) (magnetic field direction). Using these tensors, they write the general form of the gauge boson self-energy and invert the Dyson-Schwinger equation to obtain the effective propagator. The form factors are computed in the HTL approximation for two limiting cases: strong field (Lowest Landau Level, LLL) and weak field, using the one-loop gluon self-energy with quark loops.

## Key numbers

*   Strong field approximation scale hierarchy: \(m_f < T < \sqrt{|eB|}\), with \(m_f = 5\) MeV for two light quark flavors.
*   Weak field approximation scale hierarchy: \(\sqrt{|eB|} < m_f < T\).
*   The magnetic field in non-central heavy-ion collisions is estimated up to \(|eB| \sim 15 m_\pi^2\) at LHC (Skokov 2009).
*   The Yang-Mills contribution to the form factor \(c\) is given by \(c = \frac{C_A g^2 T^2}{3} \frac{1}{2} \left[ \frac{p_0^2}{p^2} - \frac{P^2}{p^2} \mathcal{T}_P(p_0,p) \right]\), with the strong-field quark contribution \(c_s = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the general structure of fermionic two- and three-point functions in a hot magnetized medium (Das 2017, Haque 2017) to gauge bosons. It provides the necessary two-point function for computing the quark-gluon free energy in a hot magnetized deconfined QCD system (Bandyopadhyay 2017). The formalism enables the calculation of thermo-magnetic corrections to various gauge boson observables, such as dispersion relations and screening masses, and is applicable to both QCD and QED plasmas.
