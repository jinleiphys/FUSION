# The QCD vacuum and its hadronic excitations

> A survey is given on selected topics of physics in the non-perturbative, hadronic sector of the QCD phase diagramme. Following a brief QCD primer, some highlights of Lattice QCD are summarised. Chiral effective field theory as the low-energy realisation of QCD is reviewed, with applications in pion-pion scattering, the scalar form factor of the nucleon and the interpolation of nucleon properties between lattice QCD results and actual observables. Chiral SU(3) dynamics, incorporating the strange quark, is developed with examples relating to low-energy kaon-nucleon interactions. The quest for quasiparticles in QCD and their correlations, beyond the simple partonic pictures of hadron structure, is discussed and aspects of QCD thermodynamics are explored in this context.

# The QCD vacuum and its hadronic excitations

## Key claim

The QCD vacuum undergoes spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, producing a non-perturbative ground state characterized by strong quark-antiquark condensates and a mass gap of approximately 1 GeV, with the pseudoscalar meson octet (pions, kaons, eta) emerging as Goldstone bosons; however, this picture relies on the approximation that light quark masses are small compared to the dynamical scale of 4πfπ ~ 1 GeV, and the precise nature of the transition from confinement to deconfinement depends on the number of quark flavors and the values of the physical quark masses.

## Method

The paper develops chiral effective field theory (ChPT) as the low-energy realization of QCD, constructing an effective Lagrangian in powers of derivatives and quark masses that respects the chiral SU(2)R × SU(2)L (or SU(3)R × SU(3)L) symmetry of massless QCD and its spontaneous breaking to SU(2)V (or SU(3)V). Lattice QCD simulations on discretized Euclidean space-time volumes are used to compute the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate and the static quark-antiquark potential, while QCD sum rules and finite-energy sum rules connect hadron masses to the chiral condensate and the pion decay constant.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: Tc = (173 ± 8) MeV for Nf = 2 flavors and Tc = (154 ± 8) MeV for Nf = 3 flavors (Karsch et al. 2001), from lattice QCD extrapolated to zero quark masses
- Pion decay constant: fπ = (92.4 ± 0.3) MeV, determined from the decay π+ → μ+ νμ + μ+ νμ γ (Particle Data Group 2004)
- Chiral condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ −(0.23 ± 0.03 GeV)³ ≈ −1.6 fm⁻³, derived from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation using mu + md ≈ 13 MeV (Pich and Prades 2000) at renormalization scale ~1 GeV
- Light quark masses: mu,d < 10 MeV and ms ≈ (100–150) MeV at renormalization scale μ ≈ 1 GeV
- Charm quark mass: mc ≈ (1.1–1.4) GeV; bottom quark mass: mb ≈ (4.1–4.4) GeV; top quark mass: mt = (174 ± 5) GeV
- QCD scale parameter: Λ ≈ 0.2 GeV for Nf = 4
- Running coupling at Z-boson mass: αs(MZ) ≈ 0.12 (Particle Data Group 2004)
- String tension from lattice QCD: σ ≈ 1 GeV/fm, from the static quark-antiquark potential V(r) = −4αs/(3r) + σr with αs ≈ 0.3 (Bali 2001)
- ρ meson mass: mρ ≈ 0.77 GeV; a1 meson mass: ma1 ≈ 1.23 GeV
- Leading-order relation from sum rules: ma1 = √2 mρ = 4πfπ

## Context

This survey extends the lecture series of Weise (2003) and reviews the framework of chiral effective field theory developed by Weinberg (1979), Gasser and Leutwyler (1984), and Leutwyler (1994), which provides a systematic low-energy expansion of QCD in powers of Q/(4πfπ). The lattice QCD results for the static quark-antiquark potential (Bali 2001) and the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate (Boyd et al. 1995, Karsch et al. 2001) demonstrate the non-perturbative structure of the QCD vacuum. The paper enables further applications of chiral SU(3) dynamics to low-energy kaon-nucleon interactions and provides a foundation for exploring modifications of hadronic spectral functions in dense and hot nuclear matter, as noted by Metag (2004).
