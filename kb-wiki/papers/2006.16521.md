# The outer crust of a cold, non-accreting neutron star within the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model

> The outer crust properties of cold non-accreting neutron stars are studied within the framework of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which includes the effects of modifications of the quark structure inside individual nucleons when they are within a high-density nuclear medium. With a unique set of five well-constrained adjustable parameters, which have a clear physical basis, the QMC model gives predictions for the ground state observables of even-even nuclei which agree with experiment as well as traditional models. Furthermore, it gives improved theoretical values for nuclei thought to play a role in the outer crusts of neutron stars but for which experimental data is not available. Using the latest experimental data tables wherever possible but otherwise the predictions from the QMC model, we construct an equation of state for the outer crust which is then used within stellar model calculations to obtain an equilibrium sequence of crustal layers, each characterized by a particular neutron rich nuclei. Various properties of the layers are calculated for a range of neutron-star masses, and comparisons are made with alternative equations of state from the literature. This leads to the conclusion that the QMC model successfully predicts the outer crust properties and is fully comparable with the more traditional mass models, which all depend on a larger number of parameters.

## Key claim

The Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model, using only five adjustable parameters, predicts the sequence of nuclei and equation of state for the outer crust of cold non-accreting neutron stars with accuracy comparable to traditional mass models that depend on a larger number of parameters, though the exact composition of the deepest crustal layers and the position of the neutron drip line remain model-dependent.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state for the outer crust by minimizing the Gibbs free energy per nucleon at each pressure, using experimental nuclear masses from the 2016 Atomic Mass Evaluation where available and theoretical masses from the QMCπ-III model for neutron-rich nuclei beyond experimental reach. The QMCπ-III energy density functional, which incorporates modifications to nucleon internal quark structure in dense matter, was implemented in the SKYAX code to calculate ground-state properties of even-even axially symmetric nuclei in the region approximately defined by 20 < Z < 50 and 50 < N < 90. The resulting outer crust EOS was then used within stellar model calculations by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium, using a composite EOS that included a QMCπ-III based core, the BPS EOS for the inner crust, and the QMCπ-III EOS for the outer crust.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, energy per particle E₀ = -15.7 MeV, asymmetry coefficient a_sym = 29.42 MeV, incompressibility K = 233 MeV, and slope L = 43 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter in the QMCπ-III parameterization
- Maximum baryon density at the outer-inner crust boundary: n_B^max = 2.61 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³
- Maximum neutron star mass from the composite EOS: 1.97 M_⊙ with radius 12.28 km
- For a 1.44 M_⊙ neutron star model: radius 13.00 km, compatible with NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (1.44⁺⁰·¹⁵₋₀·₁₄ M_⊙, radius 13.02⁺¹·²⁴₋₁·⁰⁶ km at 68% confidence)
- QMCπ-III model parameters: G_σ = 9.619 fm², G_ω = 5.213 fm², G_ρ = 4.712 fm², σ self-interaction λ₃ = 0.048 fm², σ meson mass m_σ = 506 MeV
- At the neutron star surface, ⁵⁶Fe nuclei are energetically favored up to energy density 4.568 × 10⁻⁶ MeV fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the application of the Quark-Meson Coupling model, previously used successfully for neutron star cores, to the outer crust for the first time. The results are compared with predictions from the finite range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller (2016), the non-relativistic energy density functional HFB-24 model of Goriely (2013) and Chamel (2016), and the relativistic mean-field model with NL3 interaction of Lalazissis (1999). The study builds on earlier outer crust calculations by Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971), Haensel, Zdunik, and Dobaczewski (1989), Rüster et al. (2006), and Pearson et al. (2011, 2018), and enables future work on the QMC model for the inner crust and pasta region, as well as providing a benchmark for comparison with next-generation experimental facilities measuring masses near the neutron drip line.
