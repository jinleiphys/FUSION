# Quark number susceptibilities, strangeness and dynamical confinement

> We report first results on the strange quark number susceptibility, chi_s, over a large range of temperatures, mainly in the plasma phase of QCD. Chi_s jumps across the phase transition temperature, T_c, and grows rapidly with temperature above but close to T_c. For all quark masses and susceptibilities in the entire temperature range studied, we found significant departures from ideal-gas values. We also observed a strong correlation between these quantities and the susceptibility in the scalar/pseudo-scalar channel, supporting ideas of ``dynamical confinement'' in the high temperature phase of the QCD plasma.

# Quark number susceptibilities, strangeness and dynamical confinement

## Key claim
The authors report the first lattice determination of the strange quark number susceptibility $\chi_s$ over a wide temperature range in the quenched approximation, finding significant departures from ideal-gas values for all quark masses and susceptibilities studied. The observed strong correlation between quark number susceptibilities and the scalar/pseudo-scalar susceptibility supports the idea of "dynamical confinement" in the high-temperature phase of QCD, but this correlation weakens above approximately $2T_c$.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD computations on $4\times12^3$ lattices (with additional volume studies on $4\times8^3$ and $4\times16^3$ lattices) at temperatures $T=0.75T_c$, $1.1T_c$, $1.25T_c$, $1.5T_c$, $2T_c$, and $3T_c$. They used staggered fermions with quark masses $m/T_c = 3$, 1.5, 1, 0.75, 0.30, and 0.03, evaluating the fermion traces stochastically with $N=80$ random vectors per configuration to achieve $\chi_0 = \chi_3$ within statistical errors.

## Key numbers
- At $T=1.5T_c$, the volume dependence of susceptibilities was found to be smaller than statistical errors on a $4\times8^3$ lattice.
- For $T < T_c$, all susceptibilities are consistent with zero.
- At temperatures as high as $3T_c$, results lie significantly below the free field theory (FFT) expectation.
- The scaling between $\chi_\pi$ and $\chi_3$ holds for $T \le 2T_c$ and $m/T_c \ge 1.5$, with the scaling function going to 1 as $T \to T_c$.
- The strange quark mass lies between $0.3T_c$ and $0.5T_c$ based on estimates of $T_c = 275$â€“$290$ MeV for the quenched theory.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of flavour $SU(2)$ singlet ($\chi_0$) and triplet ($\chi_3$) susceptibilities in 2-flavour dynamical QCD by Gottlieb et al. (1997) and the MILC collaboration (1999), which showed 20% departures from free field theory at $m/T=0.1$ and 0.15. The authors provide the first determination of $\chi_s$ and cover a larger temperature range (up to $3T_c$) with systematic studies of volume effects, quark mass dependence, and stochastic estimator quality. The observed scaling between $\chi_\pi$ and $\chi_3$ supports the dynamical confinement hypothesis of DeTar (1995) and suggests that for $T \ge 2T_c$, dimensional reduction becomes quantitatively correct, enabling tests of various ideas about the QCD equation of state.
