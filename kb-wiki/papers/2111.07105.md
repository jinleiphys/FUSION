# Isoscalar giant monopole resonance in $^{24}$Mg and $^{28}$Si: Effect of coupling between the isoscalar monopole and quadrupole strength

> Background: In highly deformed nuclei, there is a noticeable coupling of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) and the $K = 0$ component of the Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance (ISGQR), which results in a double peak structure of the isoscalar monopole (IS0) strength (a narrow low-energy deformation-induced peak and a main broad ISGMR part). The energy of the narrow low-lying IS0 peak is sensitive to both the incompressibility modulus $K_\infty$ and the coupling between IS0 and isoscalar quadrupole (IS2) strength. Objective: This study aims to investigate the two-peaked structure of the ISGMR in the prolate $^{24}$Mg and oblate $^{28}$Si nuclei and identify among a variety of energy density functionals based on Skyrme parameterisations the one which best describes the experimental data. This will allow for conclusions regarding the nuclear incompressibility. Because of the strong IS0/IS2 coupling, the deformation splitting of the ISGQR will also be analysed. Methods: The ISGMR was excited in $^{24}$Mg and $^{28}$Si using $α$-particle inelastic scattering measurements acquired with an $E_α= 196$ MeV beam at scattering angles $θ_{\text{Lab}} = 0^\circ$ and $4^\circ$. The K$600$ magnetic spectrometer at iThemba LABS was used to detect and momentum analyse the inelastically scattered $α$ particles. An experimental energy resolution of $\approx 70$ keV (FWHM) was attained, revealing fine structure in the excitation-energy region of the ISGMR. The IS0 strength distributions in the nuclei studied were obtained with the Difference-of-Spectrum (DoS) technique. The theoretical comparison is based on the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with a representative set of Skyrme forces.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the two-peaked structure of the isoscalar monopole (IS0) strength in deformed nuclei arises from coupling between the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) and the K=0 component of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), and that the narrow low-energy deformation-induced peak can serve as an additional sensitive measure of nuclear incompressibility, provided the IS0 and IS2 strengths are described simultaneously.

## Method

The ISGMR was excited in prolate 24Mg and oblate 28Si using alpha-particle inelastic scattering with a 196 MeV beam at scattering angles of 0 and 4 degrees, detected by the K600 magnetic spectrometer at iThemba LABS with an energy resolution of approximately 70 keV. IS0 strength distributions were extracted using the Difference-of-Spectrum (DoS) technique, and the experimental data were compared with quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using a representative set of Skyrme forces (SV-bas, SkM*, SkPdelta, SkT6, and SV-mas10).

## Key numbers

- Experimental energy resolution of approximately 70 keV (FWHM) was attained, revealing fine structure in the ISGMR excitation-energy region.
- In 24Mg, a narrow IS0 peak was observed at 13.87(2) MeV exhausting 2.8(3)% of the EWSR, with an IS0 strength of 37.7(38) fm4.
- In 24Mg, the IS0 strength is separated into a narrow structure at about 13-14 MeV and a broad structure at 19-26 MeV.
- In 28Si, the narrow IS0 structure appears at 17-19 MeV with a broad structure at 19-26 MeV.
- The Skyrme force SkPdelta, with an associated nuclear incompressibility K_infinity = 202 MeV, provided the best description of the IS0 data for both nuclei.
- The incompressibility values for the Skyrme forces used were: SV-bas (234 MeV), SkM* (217 MeV), SkPdelta (202 MeV), SkT6 (236 MeV), and SV-mas10 (234 MeV).
- The isoscalar effective mass m*0/m values were: SV-bas (0.9), SkM* (0.79), SkPdelta (1), SkT6 (1), and SV-mas10 (1).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the ISGMR in deformed nuclei performed at RCNP (Kawabata 2013, Gupta 2015, 2016) and TAMU (Youngblood 2007, 2009), which observed the two-peaked structure but did not fully explain the origin of the narrow low-energy peaks. The high energy resolution of the present experiment resolves discrepancies between earlier datasets, particularly confirming the strong narrow IS0 peak at 13.8 MeV in 24Mg that was absent in some RCNP data. The results enable future studies using the deformation-induced narrow IS0 peaks as an additional constraint on nuclear incompressibility, and demonstrate that in strongly deformed nuclei, only a simultaneous description of IS0 and IS2 strengths can be considered consistent.
