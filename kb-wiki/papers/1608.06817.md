# Effects of $ρ$-meson width on pion distributions in heavy-ion collisions

> The influence of the finite width of $ρ$ meson on the pion momentum distribution is studied quantitatively in the framework of the S-matrix approach combined with a blast-wave model to describe particle emissions from an expanding fireball. We find that the proper treatment of resonances which accounts for their production dynamics encoded in data for partial wave scattering amplitudes can substantially modify spectra of daughter particles originating in their two body decays. In particular, it results in an enhancement of the low-$p_T$ pions from the decays of $ρ$ mesons which improves the quantitative description of the pion spectra in heavy ion collisions obtained by the ALICE collaboration at the LHC energy.

## Key claim

The finite width of the ρ meson, treated via the S-matrix approach rather than the conventional zero-width or Breit-Wigner approximations, enhances the low-transverse-momentum pion yield from ρ decays by up to a factor of two at pT ≈ 0, and this improvement is sufficient to bring blast-wave model calculations into agreement with ALICE data for 0-10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, but only at the level of a few percent change in the total pion yield.

## Method

The authors combine the S-matrix formulation of Dashen, Ma, and Bernstein with a blast-wave model to describe particle emission from an expanding fireball. For the ρ meson channel (I=1, J=1 ππ scattering), they use an effective spectral weight B(M) = 2 dδ/dM derived from empirical phase shifts parameterized by a one-loop-inspired formula, with parameters constrained to reproduce the known P-wave scattering length a¹₁ = 0.038 mπ⁻³. They then evaluate the ρ momentum distribution via the Cooper-Frye formalism and apply conventional two-body decay kinematics to obtain the daughter pion spectra, comparing results to the zero-width and Breit-Wigner treatments.

## Key numbers

- At pT ≈ 0, the S-matrix treatment yields a factor of two increase in the differential pion yield from ρ decays compared to the zero-width approximation.
- At T = 155 MeV, the relative abundance of π⁺ from ρ decay is 25.1% of the total pion yield; thermal pions constitute 49.4%, three-body decays of η and ω give 12.2%, and other two-body channels (ππ S-wave, πK, Δ(1232)) sum to 13.3%.
- The S-matrix treatment increases the yield of pions from ρ decays by approximately 15% relative to the zero-width treatment.
- In the blast-wave fit to ALICE data for 0-10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, the S-matrix treatment produces up to a 7% increase in low-pT pions (pT ≲ 200 MeV), sufficient to match the measured spectra.
- The blast-wave parameters used are: freeze-out temperature T = 155 MeV, proper time τ = 13.7 fm, radius R = 10 fm, maximum radial flow velocity vmax = 0.8, and fireball volume V ≈ 4300 fm³.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix approach previously applied to ππ, πN, and πK interactions for thermodynamics (Venugopalan 1992, Broniowski 2015, Friman 2015) to differential pT spectra in heavy-ion collisions. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between fluid-dynamical calculations and ALICE data showing an excess of low-pT pions (Abelev 2013). The results demonstrate that the conventional hadron resonance gas model with zero-width resonances is insufficient for precision differential observables, and the S-matrix treatment of resonance widths provides a path toward improved modeling. Future work should incorporate medium-modified phase shifts from many-body Green's function approaches (Rapp 1995, Rapp 1997, Klingl 1997) and extend the treatment to three-body decays and full hydrodynamical simulations.
