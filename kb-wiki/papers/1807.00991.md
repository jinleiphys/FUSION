# Dirac Phenomenological Analyses of 1.047 GeV Proton Inelastic Scatterings from $^{62}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni

> Unpolarized 1.047 GeV proton inelastic scatterings from Ni isotopes, $^{62}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni are analyzed phenomenologically employing an optical potential model and the first order collective model in the relativistic Dirac coupled channel formalism. The Dirac equations are reduced to the Schrödinger-like second-order differential equations and the effective central and spin-orbit optical potentials are analyzed by considering mass number dependence. The multistep excitation via $2^+$ state is found to be important for the $4^+$ state excitation in the ground state rotational band at the proton inelastic scatterings from the Ni isotopes. The calculated deformation parameters for the 2$^+$, the 4$^+$ states of the ground state rotational band and the first 3$^-$ state are found to agree pretty well with those obtained in the nonrelativistic calculations.

# Dirac Phenomenological Analyses of 1.047 GeV Proton Inelastic Scatterings from $^{62}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni

## Key claim
The Dirac relativistic coupled-channel formalism reproduces experimental data for 1.047 GeV proton inelastic scatterings from $^{62}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni slightly better than nonrelativistic calculations, but the agreement is qualified: the deformation parameters for the $2^+$, $4^+$, and $3^-$ states agree "pretty well" with nonrelativistic results, and the multistep excitation via the $2^+$ state is found to be important for the $4^+$ state excitation in the ground state rotational band.

## Method
The authors performed a relativistic Dirac coupled-channel analysis using an optical potential model and a first-order collective model, solving the Dirac equations numerically with the ECIS code via the Numerov method. The Dirac equations were reduced to Schrodinger-like second-order differential equations to extract effective central and spin-orbit optical potentials. Optical potential parameters (scalar and vector, real and imaginary, in Woods-Saxon shapes) and deformation parameters ($\beta_S$ and $\beta_V$) were fitted to experimental differential cross-section data using a minimum $\chi^2$ method, with coupling schemes including only elastic, ground-plus-$2^+$, ground-plus-$4^+$, and all three states ($0^+$, $2^+$, $4^+$) coupled.

## Key numbers
- Incident proton energy: 1.047 GeV for all scatterings from $^{62}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni.
- For the $4^+$ state in $^{62}$Ni, $\chi^2/n$ reduced from 10.02 to 3.12 when coupling with the $2^+$ state was added.
- For the $4^+$ state in $^{64}$Ni, $\chi^2/n$ reduced from 10.03 to 3.09 when coupling with the $2^+$ state was added.
- Deformation parameters for $^{62}$Ni $2^+$ state: $\beta_S = 0.209$, $\beta_V = 0.233$; for $^{64}$Ni $2^+$ state: $\beta_S = 0.188$, $\beta_V = 0.199$.
- Deformation parameters for $^{62}$Ni $4^+$ state: $\beta_S = 0.037$, $\beta_V = 0.054$; for $^{64}$Ni $4^+$ state: $\beta_S = 0.046$, $\beta_V = 0.051$.
- Deformation parameters for $^{62}$Ni $3^-$ state: $\beta_S = 0.206$, $\beta_V = 0.194$; for $^{64}$Ni $3^-$ state: $\beta_S = 0.180$, $\beta_V = 0.191$.
- Excitation energies: $^{62}$Ni $2^+$ at 1.17 MeV, $^{64}$Ni $2^+$ at 1.35 MeV; $^{62}$Ni $4^+$ at 2.34 MeV, $^{64}$Ni $4^+$ at 2.61 MeV; $^{62}$Ni $3^-$ at 3.76 MeV, $^{64}$Ni $3^-$ at 3.55 MeV.
- Scalar real potential strength for elastic $^{62}$Ni: -356.5 MeV; for elastic $^{64}$Ni: -38.56 MeV (radius 7.204 fm, diffuseness 0.8492 fm).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Dirac phenomenological analyses of 1.047 GeV proton inelastic scatterings from $^{58}$Ni and $^{60}$Ni (Shim, 2018) to the heavier isotopes $^{62}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni, which have a closed proton shell (Z=28) with a few off-shell neutrons. The results confirm that multistep excitation via the $2^+$ state is important for $4^+$ excitation, as previously found for $^{58}$Ni and $^{60}$Ni. The calculated deformation parameters are compared with nonrelativistic results from references cited as Ref. 12, 17, 18, 19, 20, and 21 in the paper. The surface-peaked phenomena observed in effective central potentials for $^{62}$Ni resemble those seen in scatterings from $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg (Shim, earlier work). This work enables systematic Dirac analyses across the Ni isotopic chain and supports replacing nonrelativistic Schrodinger approaches with relativistic Dirac approaches for intermediate-energy proton-nucleus scattering.
