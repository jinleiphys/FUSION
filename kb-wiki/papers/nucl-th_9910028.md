# Studying the $ωN$ elastic and inelastic cross section with nucleons

> We explore the possibility to measure the elastic and inelastic $ωN$ cross section in $p{+}d{\to}d{+}ω{+}p_{sp}$ and $p{+}A$ reactions. Our studies indicate that the elastic scattering cross sections can be determined for $ω$ momenta above 1 GeV/c in $p{+}d$ reactions by gating on high proton spectator momenta whereas the $ωN$ absorption cross section down to low relative $ω$ momenta is most effectively studied in $p{+}A$ reactions at beam energies 2.0--2.7 GeV

## Key claim

The elastic ωN cross section can be determined for ω momenta above 1 GeV/c in p+d reactions by gating on high proton spectator momenta, while the ωN absorption cross section down to low relative ω momenta is most effectively studied in p+A reactions at beam energies 2.0–2.7 GeV, but the actual momentum dependence of the inelastic cross section cannot be extracted easily due to strong effects from elastic ωN scattering.

## Method

The authors used a Multiple Scattering Monte Carlo (MSMC) approach to simulate the reaction p+d→d+ω+p_sp, employing the eikonal approximation for ωN rescattering and the deuteron wave function from Lacombe (1981) with S- and D-waves. For p+A reactions, they employed a coupled-channel transport model (Ehehalt, 1996) with elementary production cross sections, using parameterized ωN elastic and inelastic cross sections from Lykasov (unpublished) to compute ω-meson momentum distributions and A-dependence of production cross sections at beam energies 2.0, 2.4, and 2.7 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The ωN elastic cross section assumed in p+d simulations was σ_el = 40 mb for all three angular distribution models (isotropic, b=6 GeV⁻², b=12 GeV⁻²).
- The ωN elastic cross section parameterization used in p+A transport calculations: σ_el = 5.4 + 10·exp(-0.6 p_ω) mb, where p_ω is the ωN relative laboratory momentum in GeV/c.
- The ωN inelastic cross section parameterization used in p+A transport calculations: σ_inel = 20 + 4.0/p_ω mb, with p_ω in GeV/c.
- For p+¹²C collisions at 2.4 GeV, the ratio κ of the ω spectrum without FSI to the spectrum including FSI shows substantial deviation from Glauber model predictions at low p_∥ (below ~0.5 GeV/c) and at p_∥ > 500 MeV/c.
- The A-dependence power-law parameter α from transport calculations without FSI at T_p=2.0, 2.4, and 2.7 GeV is higher than the Glauber model value α≈0.75; with FSI included, α approaches 2/3 above ≈2.4 GeV bombarding energy.
- The Glauber model with constant σ_ωN=35 mb gives α≈0.55 for the A-dependence with FSI.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson production in hadron-nucleus collisions by Golubeva (1992, 1993, 1996, 1997) and Sibirtsev (1998), and addresses the need for experimental determination of ωN cross sections to construct the ω spectral function at low baryon density. The theoretical predictions for ωN cross sections from Weise (1993), Ko (1994), Friman (1995), and Lykasov (unpublished) differ widely, motivating the proposed measurements. The results enable future experiments at COSY-Jülich to extract ωN elastic cross sections from p+d reactions and ωN absorption cross sections from p+A reactions, though the momentum dependence of the inelastic cross section remains difficult to isolate.
