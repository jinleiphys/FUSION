# Cascade hypernuclear production spectra at J-PARC

> We predict cascade hypernuclear production spectra expected in the forthcoming J-PARC experiment. In the Green's function method of the distorted wave impulse wave approximation with the local optimal Fermi averaging t-matrix, we can describe the Xi production spectra in the continuum and bound state region reasonably well. Predictions to the high resonlution spectra at J-PARC suggest hat we should observe Xi bound state peak structure in (K-,K+) spectra in light nuclear targets such as 12C and 27Al.

## Key claim

The authors predict that bound state peak structures of $\Xi^-$ hypernuclei should be observable in $(K^-,K^+)$ spectra at J-PARC on light nuclear targets such as $^{12}$C and $^{27}$Al, but only if the imaginary part of the $\Xi^-$-nucleus optical potential is not large ($|W_\Xi| \leq 3\ \mathrm{MeV}$) and the experimental resolution is good enough ($\Delta E \leq 2\ \mathrm{MeV}$).

## Method

The authors employ the Green's function method within the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with a local optimal Fermi averaging $t$-matrix (LOFAt) to describe $\Xi^-$ production spectra in both the continuum and bound state regions on the same footing. The $\Xi^-$-nucleus optical potential is taken as a Woods-Saxon form with a depth of 14 MeV and an imaginary part of -1 MeV, and the elementary $t$-matrix is re-parameterized to fit cross section data for $P_{K^-}\lesssim$ 3 GeV/c. An adjustable multiplicative factor is introduced to fit the quasi-free spectrum on light targets, compensating for center-of-mass effects and other ambiguities in the absolute yield.

## Key numbers

- The $\Xi^-$-nucleus potential depth preferred by the analysis is around 14 MeV, consistent with the shallow potential suggested from the twin hypernuclear event and previous DWIA analyses.
- The imaginary part of the $\Xi^-$-nucleus optical potential is assumed to be $W_0^\Xi = -1\ \mathrm{MeV}$, with results also shown for $W_0^\Xi = -3\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- The experimental resolution required to observe bound state peaks is $\Delta E = 2\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (FWHM), as expected at J-PARC.
- The isospin averaged cross sections used for kaon distortion are $\bar{\sigma}_{NK^-}= 28.90$ mb and $\bar{\sigma}_{NK^+}= 19.35$ mb at $P_{K^-}=1.65$ GeV/c.
- The multiplicative factors to fit the quasi-free spectrum on $^{12}$C are 1.7, 1.99, and 2.35 for potential depths of $V_0^{\Xi} = -24, -14, -4\ \mathrm{MeV}$, respectively.
- The center-of-mass correction factor $[F^{1/2}]^2$ amounts to 1.86 for $^{12}$C and 1.43 for $^{27}$Al at $q=500\ \mathrm{MeV/c}$.
- The width of $\Xi^-$ hypernuclear states on $^{12}$C, evaluated by Ikeda et al. (Nijmegen model D), is 1.2 MeV for $[\pi 0p_{3/2}^{-1}\otimes \Xi s_{1/2}]^{J=1}$ and 0.5 MeV for $[\pi 0p_{3/2}^{-1}\otimes \Xi p]^{J=0,2}$.

## Context

This work extends previous DWIA analyses of $\Xi^-$ production spectra by Fukuda et al. and Khaustov et al., which suggested a shallow $\Xi^-$-nucleus potential around 14-15 MeV, and addresses the need to verify consistency with production spectra in the quasi-free region measured by Iijima et al. The Green's function method with LOFAt allows simultaneous description of continuum and bound state spectra, and the predictions are intended to guide the J-PARC day-one experiment in searching for $\Xi^-$ nuclear bound states. The analysis also builds on earlier work by Harada on optimal Fermi averaging for $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ production, and by Kohno on semi-classical distorted wave calculations with local Fermi averaging.
