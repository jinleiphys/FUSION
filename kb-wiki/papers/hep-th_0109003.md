# Path Integral Treatment of Singular Problems and Bound States: Quantum Mechanics

> A path-integral approach for the computation of quantum-mechanical propagators and energy Green's functions is presented. Its effectiveness is demonstrated through its application to singular interactions, with particular emphasis on the inverse square potential--possibly combined with a delta-function interaction. The emergence of these singular potentials as low-energy nonrelativistic limits of quantum field theory is highlighted. Not surprisingly, the analogue of ultraviolet regularization is required for the interpretation of these singular problems.

## Key claim

The paper presents a path-integral framework for computing quantum-mechanical propagators and energy Green's functions for singular potentials, claiming that the inverse square potential (possibly combined with a delta-function interaction) can be treated via an analogue of ultraviolet regularization borrowed from quantum field theory, but only when the perturbative series is summed to all orders rather than truncated.

## Method

The authors develop a time-sliced path-integral formulation in Cartesian coordinates, then transform to hyperspherical coordinates before taking the continuum limit to avoid extra quantum-mechanical terms from nonlinear coordinate changes. They introduce a perturbation expansion of the potential term, followed by exact summation to all orders using the free-particle radial propagator as the unperturbed kernel, expressed in terms of modified Bessel functions. The radial path integral is evaluated using a Besselian pseudomeasure that incorporates the angular momentum quantum number \(l\) and dimensionality parameter \(\nu = D/2 - 1\) through the combination \(l+\nu\).

## Key numbers

* The free-particle radial propagator is given analytically as \(K^{(0)}_{l+\nu}(r'',r';T) = \frac{M}{i\hbar T} \sqrt{r' r''} \exp\left[\frac{iM}{2\hbar T}(r'^2 + r''^2)\right] I_{l+\nu}\left(\frac{Mr'r''}{i\hbar T}\right)\), where \(M\) is the particle mass and \(T\) the time interval.
* The free-particle radial energy Green's function is \(G^{(0)}_{l+\nu}(r'',r';E) = -\frac{2M}{\hbar^2} \sqrt{r'r''} I_{l+\nu}(\kappa r_{<}) K_{l+\nu}(\kappa r_{>})\), with \(\kappa = \sqrt{-2ME}/\hbar\) and \(r_{<}\) (\(r_{>}\)) the smaller (larger) of \(r'\) and \(r''\).
* The radial propagator measure factor per time slice is \(\mathcal{C}(M,\epsilon,\hbar,D) = (M/(2\pi i \epsilon \hbar))^{D/2}\), where \(\epsilon\) is the time-slice width.

## Context

This work extends the path-integral treatment of singular potentials beyond the Coulomb problem, building on prior Schrödinger-equation analyses of the inverse square potential by Gupta (1993) and Camblong and Ordonez (2000, 2001) that used regularization and renormalization concepts from quantum field theory. The paper also generalizes the perturbation-summation technique pioneered by Bhaumik and collaborators (1988, 1989) to arbitrary dimensions and to both propagators and Green's functions. The framework is intended to enable future treatment of bound states in quantum field theory, particularly within the chiral Lagrangian approach to nuclear physics.
