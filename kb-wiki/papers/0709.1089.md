# Universality of the diffusion wake in the gauge-string duality

> As a particle moves through a fluid, it may generate a laminar wake behind it. In the gauge-string duality, we show that such a diffusion wake is created by a heavy quark moving through a thermal plasma and that it has a universal strength when compared to the total drag force exerted on the quark by the plasma. The universality extends over all asymptotically anti-de Sitter supergravity constructions with arbitrary scalar matter. We discuss how these results relate to the linearized hydrodynamic approximation and how they bear on our understanding of di-hadron correlators in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The diffusion wake created by a heavy quark moving through a thermal plasma has a universal strength relative to the total drag force on the quark, specifically P^d : P^t = -1 : v^2, and this universality holds for all asymptotically anti-de Sitter supergravity constructions with arbitrary scalar matter, provided the quark is infinitely massive and represented as a trailing string.

## Method

The authors consider a five-dimensional holographic dual described by Einstein gravity coupled to scalars with no more than two derivatives in the action, with a trailing string representing an infinitely massive quark moving at constant velocity v through the plasma. They solve the linearized Einstein equations in axial gauge for metric perturbations sourced by the string, working in the small momentum (large distance) limit to extract the vector modes that control the diffusion wake. The Poynting vector is decomposed into longitudinal and transverse parts, with the transverse component S_T describing the diffusion wake, and its contribution to energy flow is integrated over a carefully chosen surface to isolate the diffusion wake power.

## Key numbers

- P^d : P^t = -1 : v^2, where P^d is the power delivered to the diffusion wake and P^t is the total power lost by the quark (the relative minus sign indicates the diffusion wake supplies energy to the quark)
- P^s : P^d : P^t = 1+v^2 : -1 : v^2, where P^s is the power delivered to sound waves
- The result holds for all quark velocities v between 0 and 1, with the most interesting case being v greater than the speed of sound c_s (limited perhaps by c_s ≤ 1/√3)
- The drag force F_1 = dp_1/dt = -π_ξ, and the total energy loss rate P^t = vπ_ξ

## Context

This work extends the drag force calculations of Herzog (2006) and Gubser (2006) for N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory to arbitrary holographic backgrounds with scalar matter, and generalizes the diffusion wake analysis of Gubser (2007) to show universality across all asymptotically AdS supergravity constructions. The result enables comparison with di-hadron correlator data from heavy ion collisions, though the authors note that the strong diffusion wake they find poses a challenge to current experimental understanding. The work is closely related to the linearized hydrodynamic analysis of Casalderrey-Solana (2004) and builds on prior numerical black hole constructions by Gubser (2000), Friess (2005), and others.
