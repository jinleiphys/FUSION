# Radiative Muon Capture by He-3

> The rate of the nuclear reaction $^3He+μ^- \to ^3H+γ+ν_μ$ has been calculated using both the elementary particle model (EPM) approach and the impulse approximation (IA) approach. Using the EPM approach, the exclusive statistical radiative muon capture (RMC) rate for photon energy greater than 57 MeV is found to be $0.245 s^{-1}$ and the ordinary muon capture (OMC) rate to be $1503 s^{-1}$. The IA calculation exhibits a slight dependence on the type of trinucleon wave functions used. The difference between the IA and EPM calculation is larger for RMC than for OMC. To resolve the difference between the two approaches a more detailed investigation including meson exchange corrections will be required.

## Key claim

The exclusive statistical radiative muon capture (RMC) rate for photon energy greater than 57 MeV is calculated to be 0.245 s⁻¹ using the elementary particle model (EPM) approach, but the impulse approximation (IA) calculation yields lower rates that depend on the choice of trinucleon wave function, and resolving the discrepancy between the two approaches requires future inclusion of meson exchange corrections.

## Method

The rate of the reaction ³He + μ⁻ → ³H + γ + ν_μ was calculated using two approaches: the elementary particle model (EPM), which treats both ³He and ³H as elementary spin-1/2 particles with nuclear form factors taken from experiment, and the impulse approximation (IA), which treats the capture as occurring on constituent nucleons using realistic 22-channel Faddeev wave functions derived from several model potentials (Bonn-A, Bonn-B, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Paris, AV14). The transition amplitude was constructed using the Adler and Dothan procedure to ensure gauge invariance and consistency with CVC and PCAC, including terms up to O(k⁰) and O(Q⁰), with the IA amplitude expanded non-relativistically in powers of the struck nucleon momentum.

## Key numbers

- EPM ordinary muon capture (OMC) rate: 1503 s⁻¹
- EPM exclusive statistical RMC rate for photon energy > 57 MeV: 0.245 s⁻¹
- EPM exclusive statistical RMC rate for photon energy > 5 MeV: 0.8263 s⁻¹
- IA OMC rates (full O(q²/m²) calculation) range from 1260.0 s⁻¹ (AV14 potential) to 1357.9 s⁻¹ (Bonn-A potential)
- IA RMC rates for photon energy > 57 MeV range from 0.1601 s⁻¹ (AV14 potential) to 0.1691 s⁻¹ (Bonn-A potential)
- IA RMC rates for photon energy > 5 MeV range from 0.5929 s⁻¹ (AV14 potential) to 0.6255 s⁻¹ (Bonn-A potential)
- Nuclear form factor parameters: F_V(0)=1, R_V=1.94 fm; F_M(0)=κ_He3 - κ_H3 = -8.369 n.m - 7.913 n.m, R_M=1.72 fm; F_A(0)=1.212±0.004, R_A=1.703 fm
- Nucleon form factor parameters: g_V(0)=1, r_V=0.7589 fm; g_M(0)=κ_p - κ_n, r_M=0.8781 fm; g_A(0)=-1.267±0.0035, r_A=0.6580 fm

## Context

This work updates the only similar calculation by Klieb and Rood (1981) for RMC on ³He, improving on their approximations by using modern realistic trinucleon wave functions, handling nucleon momentum terms more accurately, and including the full Adler and Dothan amplitude. The OMC results are consistent with those of Congleton (1993, 1996) and Govaerts (2000), where the difference between IA and EPM is understood to arise from meson exchange corrections that are implicitly included in the EPM but must be added by hand in the IA. The calculation enables interpretation of an ongoing TRIUMF experiment (E592) designed to measure the RMC rate and extract the nucleon pseudoscalar form factor g_P, which is theoretically predicted by PCAC.
