# Description of the recently observed hypernucleus $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C within a quark-meson coupling model

> We investigate within a quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the structure of the bound $Ξ^-$ hypernucleus $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C ($^{14}$N + $Ξ^-$), which has been observed in a recent analysis of the KEK-E373 experiment. In the QMC model, light quarks in nonoverlapping nucleon and $Ξ^-$ bags interact self-consistently with isoscalar-scalar ($σ$), isoscalar-vector ($ω$), and isovector-vector ($ρ$) mesons in the mean field approximation. The parameters of the model (quark-meson coupling constants and masses) are mostly fixed from the nuclear matter saturation properties. The QMC model closely reproduces the separation energies of the two $Ξ^-$ hyperon states in $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C reported in the KEK-E373 analysis, and identifies their quantum numbers. We also make predictions for the cross sections for the production of the $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C hypernuclear specta in the ($K^-, K^+$) reaction on a $^{15}$O target within a covariant effective Lagrangian model using the $Ξ^-$ bound state spinors obtained within the same QMC model.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, with no parameters fitted to the hypernucleus under study, reproduces the measured separation energies of the two bound $\Xi^-$ states in $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C within about 1 MeV, and identifies the 4.4 MeV state as $1s_{1/2}$ and the 1.1 MeV state as $1p_{3/2}$.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Dirac equations for nucleons and the $\Xi^-$ hyperon, where the effective masses of the baryons depend self-consistently on the scalar $\sigma$ field via the internal quark structure described by the MIT bag model. The scalar and vector mean fields are generated by couplings of $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons to the light quarks, with all coupling constants fixed from nuclear matter saturation properties. Using the resulting bound-state spinors, they also calculate $0^\circ$ differential cross sections for the $^{15}$O$(K^-,K^+)^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C reaction within a covariant effective Lagrangian model that includes six intermediate $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ resonant states.

## Key numbers

- $\Xi^-$ single-particle energy for $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C$(1s_{1/2})$: -5.366 MeV (compared to experimental binding energy 4.38 $\pm$ 0.25 MeV)
- $\Xi^-$ single-particle energy for $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C$(1p_{3/2})$: -0.779 MeV (compared to experimental binding energy 1.11 $\pm$ 0.25 MeV)
- $\Xi^-$ single-particle energy for $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C$(1p_{1/2})$: -0.099 MeV (nearly unbound)
- Non-relativistic equivalent potential depth V$_{NR}$ at $r=0$: about -11 MeV for the $s$-state and -14 MeV for the $p$-state
- Predicted $1s_{1/2}$ single-particle energy for $^{12}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$Be: -3.04 MeV
- Peak of $0^\circ$ differential cross section for populating the $1^-$ state occurs at $p_{K^-} \approx 0.78$ GeV/c
- Peak of $0^\circ$ differential cross section for populating the $2^+$ state occurs at $p_{K^-} \approx 0.84$ GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to $\Lambda$ hypernuclei (Tsushima 1998) and to $^{12}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$Be (Shyam 2012), to the newly observed $^{15}{\!\!\!_{Ξ^-}}$C hypernucleus from the KEK-E373 experiment (Nakazawa 2015). It contrasts with the analysis of Sun (2016), who fitted effective interactions in relativistic mean field and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models to the Kiso event binding energies, whereas the QMC model uses parameters fixed solely from nuclear matter saturation. The predicted differences in beam-momentum distributions for the $1s_{1/2}$ and $1p_{3/2}$ states could guide future experiments at JPARC to distinguish the two configurations.
