# Analysis of Meson Exchange and Isobar Currents in (e,e'p) Reactions from O-16

> An analysis of the effects of meson exchange and isobar currents in exclusive (e,e'p) processes from O-16 under quasi-free kinematics is presented. A model that has probed its feasibility for inclusive quasi-elastic (e,e') processes is considered. Sensitivity to final state interactions between the outgoing proton and the residual nucleus is discussed by comparing the results obtained with phenomenological optical potentials and a continuum nuclear shell-model calculation. The contribution of the meson-exchange and isobar currents to the response functions is evaluated and compared to previous calculations, which differ notably from our results. These two-body contributions cannot solve the puzzle of the simultaneous description of the different responses experimentally separated. Copyright 1999 by The American Physical Society

## Key claim

The authors find that meson-exchange and isobar currents cannot resolve the long-standing puzzle of simultaneously describing the experimentally separated longitudinal-transverse response functions for proton knockout from O-16, contrary to previous claims of large effects by Van der Sluys et al. (1994).

## Method

The authors calculate the five nuclear response functions for exclusive (e,e'p) reactions on O-16 under quasifree kinematics at momentum transfer 460 MeV/c and excitation energy 100 MeV, using a non-relativistic model that includes one-body and two-body (meson-exchange and isobar) currents. Final state interactions are treated by comparing plane waves, a continuum shell-model with a real Woods-Saxon potential, and two different phenomenological complex optical potentials (Schwandt et al. 1982 and Comfort and Karp 1980). All two-body matrix elements are evaluated exactly without the approximate reduction to an effective one-body current used by Boffi and Radici (1991).

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer: 460 MeV/c; excitation energy: 100 MeV
- For the 1p_{1/2} orbit, the total MEC+IC effect on the TL response at the peak is +32.2% with the Comfort-Karp optical potential (MEC: +29.1%, IC: +2.9%)
- For the 1p_{3/2} orbit, the total MEC+IC effect on the TL response at the peak is +8.4% with the Comfort-Karp optical potential (MEC: +9.2%, IC: -0.8%)
- For the T response, the total MEC+IC effect is at most +1.3% (1p_{1/2}) and -0.1% (1p_{3/2}) with the Comfort-Karp potential
- Scaling factors of 0.8 (1p_{1/2}) and 0.7 (1p_{3/2}) were needed to match the calculated T response to experimental data from Spaltro et al. (1993)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful model for inclusive (e,e') reactions (Amaro et al. 1992) to exclusive (e,e'p) processes, directly addressing discrepancies between two prior calculations: Boffi and Radici (1991), who used an approximate effective one-body current and found small MEC effects, and Van der Sluys et al. (1994), who used a continuum RPA approach and predicted large MEC and IC effects with strong orbital dependence. The results disagree notably with both prior works, showing that two-body currents cannot simultaneously describe the measured T and TL responses, and the authors suggest extension to other nuclei and kinematics as the next step.
