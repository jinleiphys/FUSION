# High density QCD and nucleus-nucleus scattering deeply in the saturation region

> In this paper we solve the equations that describe nucleus nucleus scattering, in high density QCD,in the framework of the BFKL Pomeron calculus. We found that (i) the contribution of short distances to the opacity for nucleus-nucleus scattering dies at high energies, (ii) the opacity tends to unity at high energy, and (iii) the main contribution that survives comes from soft (long distance) processes for large values of the impact parameter. The corrections to the opacity $Ω\Lb Y,b\Rb = 1$ were calculated and it turns out that they have a completely different form, namely($1 - Ω\to \exp\Lb - Const\,\sqrt{Y}\Rb$) than the opacity that stems from the Balisky-Kovchegov equation, which is($1 - Ω\to \exp\Lb - Const\,Y^2\Rb$). We reproduce the formula for the nucleus-nucleus cross section that is commonly used in the description of nucleus-nucleus scattering, and there is no reason why it should be correct in the Glauber-Gribov approach

## Key claim

The authors solve the BFKL Pomeron Calculus equations for nucleus-nucleus scattering and find that the opacity approaches unity at high energy, with corrections scaling as exp(-Const*sqrt(Y)), which differs from the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation prediction of exp(-Const*Y^2); the short-distance contribution to the opacity dies at high energies, leaving only soft (long-distance) processes at large impact parameters.

## Method

The authors formulate nucleus-nucleus scattering using the functional integral representation of the BFKL Pomeron Calculus, including the triple Pomeron vertex and Pomeron-nucleon interactions. They solve the resulting equations in the kinematic region where the product of the Pomeron-nucleon coupling, the nuclear thickness function, and the triple Pomeron vertex times exp(omega(0) Y) is of order unity, while the squared triple Pomeron vertex times exp(omega(0) Y) remains much smaller than one. The solution is obtained by summing the class of net diagrams that dominate in this regime, building on earlier work in zero transverse dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The opacity correction behaves as (1 - Omega) proportional to exp(-Const*sqrt(Y)), in contrast to the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation result (1 - Omega) proportional to exp(-Const*Y^2).
- The kinematic condition for the validity of the solution is g * S_A(b) * G_{3Pom} * exp(omega(0) Y) approximately 1, where g is the Pomeron-nucleon vertex, S_A(b) is the nucleon number density at impact parameter b, and G_{3Pom} is the triple Pomeron vertex.
- The BFKL eigenvalue is omega(nu) = 2 * bar{alpha}_S * (psi(1) - Re[psi(1/2 + i nu)]) = bar{alpha}_S * chi(gamma), with bar{alpha}_S = alpha_S * N_c / pi.

## Context

This work extends the BFKL Pomeron Calculus approach to dense-dense parton systems, building on the functional integral formulation of Braun (2000, 2004, 2006) and the zero-dimensional solutions of Gotsman, Kormilitzin, Levin, and Maor (2010). It contradicts the expectation from the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation (Balitsky 1999, Kovchegov 1999) that the opacity approaches unity with a Y^2 dependence, instead finding a sqrt(Y) dependence. The results enable a systematic treatment of nucleus-nucleus scattering in high-density QCD and reproduce the commonly used nucleus-nucleus cross section formula, though the authors note there is no justification for it within the Glauber-Gribov approach.
