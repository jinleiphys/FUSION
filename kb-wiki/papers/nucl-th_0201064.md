# Few-Nucleon Systems with Two-Nucleon Forces from Chiral Effective Field Theory

> Nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces from chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) are applied to systems with two, three and four nucleons. At NNLO, we consider two versions of the chiral potential which differ in the strength of the two-pion-exchange (TPE) but describe two nucleon observables equally well. The NNLO potential leads to unphysical deeply bound states in the low partial waves and effects of the 3N forces, which appear first at this order, are expected to be large. We provide arguments for a reduction of the TPE potential and introduce the NNLO* version of the NN forces. We calculate nd scattering observables as well as various properties of 3H and 4He with the NNLO* potential and find good agreement with the data and with predictions based upon the standard high-precision potentials. We find an improved description of the 3H and 4He binding energies.

## Key claim
The NNLO chiral potential with the large low-energy constants (LECs) \(c_1, c_3, c_4\) taken from \(\pi\)N scattering produces unphysical deeply bound states in low partial waves and strong cut-off dependence in D-waves; the authors therefore introduce a modified NNLO* potential by removing the \(\Delta\)-isobar content from \(c_3\) and \(c_4\) and refitting the contact interactions, which yields improved agreement with three- and four-nucleon data even without including three-nucleon forces.

## Method
The authors derived nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials from chiral effective field theory at NLO (\(\nu=2\)) and NNLO (\(\nu=3\)) using the method of unitary transformation to obtain energy-independent, Hermitian forces. They regulated the potentials with an exponential cut-off function \(\exp(-p^4/\Lambda^4)\) and solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-, three-, and four-nucleon systems. For the NNLO* variant, they subtracted the \(\Delta\)-resonance contributions from the LECs \(c_3\) and \(c_4\) and refitted the contact terms to NN data, then applied this potential to calculate \(nd\) scattering observables and binding energies of \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He.

## Key numbers
- The LECs from a \(Q^3\) analysis of \(\pi\)N scattering are: \(c_1 = -0.81\) to \(-1.53\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -4.70\) to \(-6.19\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 3.25\) to \(4.12\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The central TPE matrix element at NNLO at \(q=0\) is \(-10.3\) GeV\(^{-2}\), compared to the NLO value of approximately \(-3.4\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (for the isovector channel).
- The cut-off used for the NNLO* potential is \(\Lambda = 1000\) MeV with the exponential regulator.
- The NNLO potential with large \(c_i\) values produces unphysical deeply bound states in D-waves and lower partial waves; the Born approximation for the \(^1D_2\) phase shift deviates strongly from data already at \(E_{\rm lab} \sim 100\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces from the two-nucleon system (previously studied at NLO by the same authors in Epelbaum et al. (2000)) to three- and four-nucleon systems at NNLO. It contrasts with conventional high-precision potentials like CD-Bonn, which do not produce unphysical bound states, and addresses the problem that the large LECs from \(\pi\)N scattering (as determined in analyses by BKM (1995, 1997) and others) cause strong cut-off dependence in D-waves. The NNLO* potential enables improved predictions for \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He binding energies and \(nd\) scattering, and sets the stage for future inclusion of the three-nucleon forces that appear at NNLO.
