# From weak to strong: constrained extrapolation of perturbation series with applications to dilute Fermi systems

> We develop a method that uses truncation-order-dependent re-expansions constrained by generic strong-coupling information to extrapolate perturbation series to the nonperturbative regime. The method is first benchmarked against a zero-dimensional model field theory and then applied to the dilute Fermi gas in one and three dimensions. Overall, our method significantly outperforms Padé and Borel extrapolations in these examples. The results for the ground-state energy of the three-dimensional Fermi gas are robust with respect to changes of the form of the re-expansion and compare well with quantum Monte Carlo simulations throughout the BCS regime and beyond.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method called order-dependent-mapping extrapolation (ODME) that uses truncation-order-dependent re-expansions constrained by generic strong-coupling information (specifically the leading strong-coupling coefficients d1 and d2) to extrapolate perturbation series to the nonperturbative regime, and claims it significantly outperforms Pade and Borel extrapolations in the examples studied, though the abstract's claim of outperformance is qualified by the specific systems and coupling ranges tested.

## Method

The method constructs approximants FN(x) = [1-w(x)] sum_{k=0}^N h_k [w(x)]^k, where w(x) is a mapping function satisfying w(0)=0 and w(-infinity)=1, with the prefactor [1-w(x)] enforcing the correct strong-coupling limit xi. The mapping parameter alpha(N) is fixed by requiring that the strong-coupling expansion of FN(x) has a first-order coefficient equal to d1, with the choice among multiple solutions selected by minimizing the difference between d2 and the corresponding coefficient in FN(x). The method was benchmarked against a zero-dimensional model field theory and then applied to the dilute Fermi gas in one and three dimensions.

## Key numbers

* For the 3D Fermi gas at x = -2, ODME gives F_N(-2) approximately (0.644, 0.660, 0.663, 0.665) for N = (1,2,3,4), compared to the QMC value F_QMC(-2) = 0.676(12)
* The Bertsch parameter xi for the 3D Fermi gas is 0.376(4) from experiment and 0.372(5) from QMC
* For the 3D Fermi gas, the weak-coupling PT coefficients are c_k = (10/(9pi), (44-8ln2)/(21pi^2), 0.0303088(0), -0.0708(1), ...)
* For the 1D Fermi gas, the first few PT coefficients are c_k = (-6/pi^2, -1/pi^2, -12 zeta(3)/pi^6, -18 zeta(3)/pi^8, -36 zeta(3)/pi^10, ...)
* For the 1D Fermi gas, the strong-coupling limit is xi = 4, with SCE coefficients d1 = 16 ln 2, d2 = 48 (ln 2)^2, and d3 = 128 (ln 2)^3 - 32 zeta(3) pi^2 / 5
* For the 3D Fermi gas, the SCE coefficients are d1 approximately -0.9 and d2 approximately -0.8 from QMC
* The ODME prediction for the 3D Fermi gas at x = -2, extrapolated via Shanks transformation, is F_ODME(-2) = 0.664(7)

## Context

This work extends the order-dependent mapping (ODM) approach introduced by Seznec and Zinn-Justin (1979) by incorporating strong-coupling information to fix the mapping parameter, rather than using heuristic criteria like fastest apparent convergence. The method is applied to the dilute Fermi gas, which serves as an important benchmark for neutron matter and neutron stars, and the results are compared with quantum Monte Carlo simulations from Gandolfi (2015) and experimental measurements of the Bertsch parameter from Ku (2012). The ODME enables constrained extrapolations for systems where only limited perturbative and strong-coupling data are available, with potential applications to the unitary Fermi gas at finite temperature, hot and dense QCD matter, and the nuclear equation of state for astrophysical simulations.
