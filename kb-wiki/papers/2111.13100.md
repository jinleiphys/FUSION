# Lattice QCD with an inhomogeneous magnetic field background

> The magnetic fields generated in non-central heavy-ion collisions are among the strongest fields produced in the Universe, reaching magnitudes comparable to the scale of the strong interactions. Backed by model simulations, the resulting field is expected to be spatially modulated, deviating significantly from the commonly considered uniform profile. To improve our understanding of the physics of quarks and gluons under such extreme conditions, we use lattice QCD simulations with $2+1$ staggered fermion flavors with physical quark masses and an inhomogeneous magnetic background for a range of temperatures covering the QCD phase transition. We assume a $1/\cosh^2$ function to model the field profile and vary its strength to analyze the impact on the computed observables and on the transition. We calculate local chiral condensates, local Polyakov loops and estimate the size of lattice artifacts. We find that both observables show non-trivial spatial features due to the interplay between the sea and the valence effects.

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD simulations with a spatially inhomogeneous magnetic field modeled by a \(1/\cosh^2\) profile, the local chiral condensate and Polyakov loop exhibit non-trivial spatial features due to the interplay between valence and sea effects, but the results are limited to a single lattice volume (\(16^3\times6\)) and a fixed profile width (\(\epsilon/a=2\)), so the quantitative dependence on lattice spacing and volume remains to be checked.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with \(2+1\) flavors of staggered fermions at physical quark masses on a \(16^3\times6\) lattice, implementing an inhomogeneous magnetic field \(B(x) = B/\cosh^2((x-L_x/2)/\epsilon)\) via Abelian U(1) links. They computed the local chiral condensate and local Polyakov loop for several magnetic quantum numbers \(N_b = 0,2,4,6,8,10,16\) at four temperatures (113, 142, 155, 176 MeV) covering the QCD crossover. To separate valence and sea contributions, they also measured the \(B\neq0\) operator on \(B=0\) configurations and vice versa.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field strengths correspond to \(N_b = 0,2,4,6,8,10,16\) on a \(16^3\times6\) lattice with profile width \(\epsilon/a=2\), with flux quantization \(qB = \pi N_b / [L_y \epsilon \tanh(L_x/2\epsilon)]\).
- Temperatures studied: 113 MeV (\(\beta=3.450\)), 142 MeV (\(\beta=3.525\)), 155 MeV (\(\beta=3.555\)), and 176 MeV (\(\beta=3.600\)), where \(T_c \sim 155\) MeV is the crossover temperature.
- Lattice spacings: \(a = 0.290\) fm at 113 MeV, 0.232 fm at 142 MeV, 0.213 fm at 155 MeV, and 0.187 fm at 176 MeV.
- Light quark masses in lattice units: \(a\cdot m_{ud} = 0.0057\) (113 MeV), 0.0040 (142 MeV), 0.0035 (155 MeV), 0.0029 (176 MeV); strange quark masses: \(a\cdot m_s = 0.163, 0.112, 0.098, 0.082\) respectively.
- 80 random vectors per configuration were used for noisy estimators of the chiral condensate.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of magnetic fields beyond the uniform-field approximation used in earlier simulations (Bali et al., 2012; D'Elia et al., 2013) and analytical model studies (Andersen et al., 2016). The inhomogeneous \(1/\cosh^2\) profile is motivated by event-by-event simulations of heavy-ion collisions (Voronyuk et al., 2011; Deng et al., 2012). The results enable more realistic modeling of the QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collision scenarios, where the magnetic field is spatially modulated and time-dependent, and provide a first step toward incorporating such complexity into lattice simulations.
