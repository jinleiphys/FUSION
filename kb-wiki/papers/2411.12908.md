# Untangling the interplay of the Equation-of-State and the Collision Term towards the generation of Directed and Elliptic Flow at intermediate energies

> The mechanism for generating directed and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions is investigated and quantified for the SIS18 and SIS100 energy regimes. The observed negative elliptic flow $v_2$, at midrapidity has been explained either via (in-plane) shadowing or via (out-of-plane) squeeze-out. To settle this question, we employ the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD) to calculate Au+Au collisions at E$_\mathrm{lab}=0.6A$ GeV, E$_\mathrm{lab}=1.23A$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3.0$ GeV using a hard Skyrme type Equation-of-State to calculate the time evolution and generation of directed flow and elliptic flow. We quantitatively distinguish the impact of collisions and of the potential on $v_1$ and $v_2$ during the evolution of the system. These calculations reveal that in this energy regime the generation of $v_1$ and $v_2$ follows from a highly intricate interplay of different processes and is created late, after the system has reached its highest density and has created a matter bridge between projectile and target remnant, which later breaks. Initially, we find a strong out-of-plane pressure. Then follows a strong stopping and the built up of an in-plane pressure. The $v_2$, created by both processes, compensate to a large extend. The finally observed $v_2$ is caused by the potential, reflects the freeze-out geometry and can neither be associated to squeeze-out nor to shadowing. The results are highly relevant for experiments at GSI, RHIC-FXT and the upcoming FAIR facility, but also for experiments at FRIB, and strengthens understanding on the Equation-of-State at large baryon densities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the negative elliptic flow \(v_2\) observed at midrapidity in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions (SIS18 and SIS100 regimes) is caused neither by the squeeze-out mechanism nor by the shadowing mechanism alone, but instead results from a late-stage interplay of potential interactions and collision processes that largely compensate each other; the final observed \(v_2\) reflects the freeze-out geometry and is driven by the potential.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model with a hard Skyrme-type equation-of-state to simulate semi-peripheral (20-30% centrality, impact parameter b=7 fm) Au+Au collisions at three beam energies: \(E_\mathrm{lab}=0.6A\) GeV, \(E_\mathrm{lab}=1.23A\) GeV, and \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3.0\) GeV. They analyzed the time evolution of directed flow \(v_1\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) in both the x-z and x-y planes, and separately quantified the contributions from the collision term (cascade) and the potential term to the flow coefficients at each time step.

## Key numbers

*   Au+Au collisions at \(E_\mathrm{lab}=0.6A\) GeV, \(E_\mathrm{lab}=1.23A\) GeV, and \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3.0\) GeV were simulated at impact parameter b=7 fm (20-30% centrality).
*   The geometric overlap time \(t_\mathrm{overlap}\) is 10.8 fm/c at \(E_\mathrm{lab}=0.6A\) GeV, 8.7 fm/c at \(E_\mathrm{lab}=1.23A\) GeV, and 5.6 fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3.0\) GeV.
*   At \(t=1.5 t_\mathrm{overlap}\), the central density reaches 2-4 times saturation density \(\rho_0\).
*   The time of maximal compression is approximately \(t_\mathrm{overlap} \approx 10-12\) fm/c for the \(E_\mathrm{lab}=1.23A\) GeV case.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on flow generation at intermediate energies, particularly addressing the debate between the squeeze-out mechanism proposed by LeFevre (2016) and the shadowing mechanism proposed by Reichert (2023). The authors note a similar recent study by Wang (2024) focusing on lower energies. The results are relevant for experiments at GSI, RHIC-FXT, the upcoming FAIR facility, and FRIB, and strengthen understanding of the nuclear Equation-of-State at large baryon densities.
