# Chiral superfluidity of the quark-gluon plasma

> In this paper we argue that the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma can be considered as a chiral superfluid. The "normal" component of the fluid is the thermalized matter in common sense, while the "superfluid" part consists of long wavelength (chiral) fermionic states moving independently. We use several nonperturbative techniques to demonstrate that. First, we analyze the fermionic spectrum in the deconfinement phase (Tc < T < 2 Tc) using lattice (overlap) fermions and observe a gap between near-zero modes and the bulk of the spectrum. Second, we use the bosonization procedure with a finite cut-off and obtain a dynamical axion-like field out of the chiral fermionic modes. Third, we use relativistic hydrodynamics for macroscopic description of the effective theory obtained after the bosonization. Finally, solving the hydrodynamic equations in gradient expansion, we find that in the presence of external electromagnetic fields the motion of the "superfluid" component gives rise to the chiral magnetic, chiral electric and dipole wave effects. Latter two effects are specific for a two-component fluid, which provides us with crucial experimental tests of the model.

## Key claim
The strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) in the temperature range \(T_c < T \lesssim 2\,T_c\) can be modeled as a chiral superfluid, where the "superfluid" component consists of long-wavelength chiral fermionic states separated from the bulk spectrum by a gap, and the "normal" component is the thermalized matter; the model does not assume any spontaneously broken symmetry but uses the term "superfluidity" in the Landau sense of two independent motions separated by an energy gap.

## Method
The author analyzed the fermionic Dirac spectrum in the deconfinement phase using lattice (overlap) fermions in the quenched approximation, observing a gap between near-zero modes and the bulk. He then performed a bosonization procedure with a finite UV cutoff \(\Lambda\) on the fermionic spectrum, deriving an effective Lagrangian that includes a dynamical axion-like field \(\theta(x)\) from the chiral fermionic modes. Finally, he used relativistic hydrodynamics for a macroscopic description of the effective theory and solved the hydrodynamic equations in gradient expansion to predict transport effects in external electromagnetic fields.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range of the model: \(T_c < T \lesssim 2\,T_c\), where \(T_c\) is the deconfinement transition temperature (estimated typical for sQGP at RHIC).
- The cutoff \(\Lambda\) extracted from lattice data with dynamical fermions (\(N_f=2, N_c=3\)): \(\Lambda \simeq 3\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- For free quarks at high temperature, \(\Lambda = \pi\sqrt{\frac{2}{3}}\sqrt{T^2 + \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2}}\); in the limit \(T\gg\mu\) and \(T\gg\mu_5\), \(\rho_5 \propto T^2 \mu_5\).
- For free quarks in a strong external magnetic field, \(\Lambda = 2\sqrt{eB}\).
- The axion-like field decay constant: \(f = \frac{2\Lambda}{\pi}\sqrt{N_c}\).
- Lattice simulation parameters: \(\beta=8.45\), \(a = 0.095\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(V = 16^3\times 6\), \(N_f=1\), \(T_c \sim 260\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the two-component fluid model for sQGP proposed by Zakharov (2010) and applies it to chiral properties, building on lattice results for the Dirac spectrum by Edwards (2000) and others. It also generalizes the Chiral Magnetic Wave formalism of Kharzeev and Warringa (2010) to a full hydrodynamic two-fluid description. The model enables experimental tests via the predicted chiral magnetic, chiral electric, and dipole wave effects, which are specific to a two-component fluid and could be observed in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
