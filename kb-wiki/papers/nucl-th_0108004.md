# Is early thermalization achieved only near midrapidity at RHIC ?

> The pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at 130 $A$ GeV is studied within a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic model in the light-cone coordinate. First, we prepare two initial conditions in the hydrodynamic model for analyzing elliptic flow. Both initial conditions lead to reasonable agreement with single particle spectra in central and semi-central collisions. Next, by using these hydrodynamic simulations, we compare elliptic flow as a function of pseudorapidity with experimental data recently measured by the PHOBOS Collaboration. Our results are in agreement with experimental data only near midrapidity. This suggests that thermalization in the early stage of collisions is not achieved in forward and backward rapidity regions.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that early thermalization in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is fully achieved only near midrapidity, and only partially achieved in forward and backward rapidity regions, based on the agreement of a three-dimensional hydrodynamic model with PHOBOS elliptic flow data only near midrapidity.

## Method

The authors performed full three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations in the light-cone (Bjorken) coordinate, using two different initial conditions (IC A and IC B) for the energy density profile. Both initial conditions were tuned to reproduce single-particle pseudorapidity and transverse momentum spectra from PHOBOS and PHENIX for central and semi-central collisions. The simulations then computed the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow v2(eta) for charged particles in minimum bias events, which was compared to PHOBOS experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time tau0 = 0.6 fm for hydrodynamic evolution
- Maximum energy density Emax = 40 GeV/fm^3 for IC A and 43 GeV/fm^3 for IC B
- Freeze-out energy density Ef = 120 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to mean freeze-out temperature <Tf> ~ 137 MeV at midrapidity
- Mean radial flow at midrapidity: <vr> = 0.49 (b=2.4 fm) and 0.41 (b=8.9 fm) for IC A; 0.50 (b=2.4 fm) and 0.42 (b=8.9 fm) for IC B
- Impact parameters used: 2.4 fm for central collisions (342 participants) and 8.9 fm for semi-central collisions (94 participants)
- Flat region length Delta_eta_flat = 6.0 (IC A) and 2.8 (IC B); Gaussian width Delta_eta_Gauss = 0.4 (IC A) and 1.6 (IC B)

## Context

This work extends earlier (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic models using Bjorken's scaling solution (Kolb et al., 2001) which could only describe midrapidity physics, by implementing a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic model in light-cone coordinates. The results contradict the assumption that thermalization is achieved across all rapidity at RHIC, and instead support the idea that the Bjorken scaling picture is valid only near midrapidity. The paper enables future studies of the centrality dependence of the thermalized region and calls for experimental data on v2(eta) per centrality and transverse momentum distributions in forward rapidity regions to further constrain the dynamics.
