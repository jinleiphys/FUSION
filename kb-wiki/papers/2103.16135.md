# KIDS density functional for deformed nuclei: Examples of the even-even Nd isotopes

> Background: A global description of the ground-state properties of nuclei in a wide mass range in a unified manner is desirable not only for understanding exotic nuclei but for providing nuclear data for applications. Purpose: We demonstrate the KIDS functional describes the ground states appropriately with respect to the existing data and predictions for a possible application of the functional to all the nuclei by taking Nd isotopes as examples. Method: The Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov equation is solved for the Nd isotopes with the neutron numbers ranging from 60 to 160 by employing the KIDS functionals constructed to satisfy both neutron-matter equation of state or neutron star observation and selected nuclear data. Results: Considering the nuclear deformation improves the description of the binding energies and radii. We find that the discrepancy from the experimental data is more significant for neutron-rich/deficient isotopes and this can be made isotope independent by changing the slope parameter of the symmetry energy. Conclusions: The KIDS functional is applied to the mid-shell nuclei for the first time. The onset and evolution of deformation are nicely described for the Nd isotopes. The KIDS functional is competent to a global fitting for a better description of nuclear properties in the nuclear chart.

# KIDS density functional for deformed nuclei: Examples of the even-even Nd isotopes

## Key claim

The KIDS (Korea: IBS-Daegu-Sungkyunkwan) density functional, when applied to mid-shell deformed nuclei for the first time, describes the onset and evolution of deformation in Nd isotopes, but the discrepancy from experimental binding energies is more significant for neutron-rich and neutron-deficient isotopes and can be made isotope-independent by adjusting the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov equation was solved for even-even Nd isotopes with neutron numbers from 60 to 160 using the HFBTHO code, employing five KIDS functionals (KIDS0, KIDS-A, B, C, D) constructed hierarchically: first from nuclear matter properties, then fitted to binding energies and charge radii of doubly-closed shell nuclei (40Ca, 48Ca, 208Pb), and finally with pairing parameters fitted to the odd-even staggering data of 156Dy. Calculations were performed in Nmax=16 full spherical oscillator shells with deformation parameters varied from -0.3 to 0.3 in the initial configuration to find the global minimum.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon: discrepancy between experiment and calculation is of the order of per mille (less than 1% even in the worst case, where total binding energy exceeds 1000 MeV)
- For Group 1 models (KIDS0, SLy4): neutron drip line located at N=128; for Group 2 models (KIDS-A, B, C, D): neutron drip line extends to N=148
- KIDS-B' model (with L changed from 58 MeV to 49 MeV) predicts neutron drip line at N=134
- At N=120, the binding energy per nucleon differs by 0.2 MeV between Group 1 and Group 2, corresponding to a 36 MeV difference in total energy
- Charge radii: difference between spherical and deformed calculations amounts to 0.2 fm around N=100; agreement with experiment at the level of 0.5% or less
- KIDS0 model parameters: saturation density rho0=0.16 fm^-3, energy per nucleon EB=16 MeV, incompressibility K0=240 MeV, symmetry energy J=32.8 MeV, slope parameter L=49.1 MeV, K_tau=-375.1 MeV
- KIDS-A parameters: K0=230 MeV, J=33 MeV, L=66 MeV, K_tau=-420 MeV
- KIDS-B parameters: K0=240 MeV, J=32 MeV, L=58 MeV, K_tau=-420 MeV
- KIDS-C parameters: K0=250 MeV, J=31 MeV, L=58 MeV, K_tau=-360 MeV
- KIDS-D parameters: K0=260 MeV, J=30 MeV, L=47 MeV, K_tau=-360 MeV
- SLy4 parameters: K0=229.9 MeV, J=32.0 MeV, L=45.9 MeV, K_tau=-322.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the KIDS formalism, previously applied to homogeneous nuclear matter (Papakonstantinou 2018), closed-shell nuclei (Gil 2019a, 2019b), and spherical open-shell nuclei (Gil 2020b), to deformed mid-shell nuclei for the first time. The results are compared with the relativistic DRHBc model of Zhang (2020) and the Skyrme SLy4 model of Chabanat (1998). The study demonstrates that the KIDS functional is competent for a global fitting to produce a mass table from light to superheavy elements, similar in quality to the UNEDF model of Kortelainen (2010), and enables future systematic calculations of beta- and gamma-strength functions.
