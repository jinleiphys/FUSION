# Interaction and Identification of the Doubly Heavy Di-Hadronic Molecules

> We study the interesting problem of interaction and identification of the hadronic molecules which seem to be deuteron-like structure. In particular, we propose a binding mechanism in which One Boson Exchange Potential plus Yukawa screen-like potential is applied in their relative s-wave state. We propose the dipole-like interaction between two color neutral states to form a hadronic molecule. For the identification of the hadronic molecules, the Weinberg's compositeness theorem is used to distinguish the molecule from confined (elementary) state. The present formalism predict some di-hadronic molecular states, involving quarks (s, c, b or $\overline{s}$, $\overline{c}$, $\overline{b}$) as a constituents, namely, $pn$, $K\overline{K}$, $ρ\overlineρ$, $K^{*}\overline{K^{*}}$, $D\overline{D^{*}}$($\overline{D}D^{*}$), $D^{*}\overline{D^{*}}$, $B\overline{B^{*}}$, $B^{*}\overline{B^{*}}$, $D^{*\pm}\overline{D_{1}^{0}}$, $ D^{0}\overline{K^{\pm}}$, $D^{*0}\overline{K^{\pm}}$, with their possible quantum numbers.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a combination of One Boson Exchange Potential (OBE) plus a Yukawa screen-like potential can bind two color-neutral hadrons into molecular states, and that Weinberg's compositeness theorem can distinguish these molecules from elementary (confined) states. The key hedge is that the s-wave OBE potential alone cannot provide sufficient attractive strength to form bound states, even in attractive spin-isospin channels, so the additional screen Yukawa-like potential is necessary.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective inter-hadronic potential by summing s-wave OBE contributions from pseudoscalar (π, η), scalar (σ, δ), and vector (ω, ρ) mesons, then adding a phenomenological Yukawa screen-like potential with a single free screening parameter c. They solved the Schrödinger equation variationally using a hydrogenic trial wave function, fitting c to reproduce the deuteron binding energy (2.224 MeV) and then applying the same fixed potential to other di-mesonic systems. Weinberg's compositeness theorem was applied by computing scattering length a_s and effective range r_e for different values of the renormalization constant Z to classify states as composite (Z=0) or elementary (Z=1).

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: calculated 2.221 MeV, experimental 2.224 MeV, with rms radius 3.13 fm
- X(3872) as D D* molecule: calculated binding energy 2.395 MeV, mass 3869.4 MeV, rms radius 3.04 fm
- f0(980) as K Kbar molecule (I=0): calculated binding energy 6.154 MeV, mass 989.07 MeV, rms radius 2.11 fm
- a0(980) as K Kbar molecule (I=1): calculated binding energy 8.656 MeV, mass 986.57 MeV, rms radius 2.01 fm
- Z(10610) as B B* molecule: calculated binding energy 2.295 MeV, mass 10602.7 MeV, rms radius 2.99 fm
- Z(10650) as B* B* molecule: calculated binding energy 2.288 MeV, mass 10648.1 MeV, rms radius 2.99 fm
- Ds0(2317) as D0 K± molecule: calculated binding energy 15.63 MeV, mass 2342.9 MeV, rms radius 1.75 fm
- Screening parameter c = 0.0686 GeV (fitted to deuteron)
- For deuteron at Z=0: a_s = 5.78 fm, r_e = 1.46 fm (experimental: a_s = +5.41 fm, r_e = +1.75 fm)

## Context
This work extends the hadronic molecular model approach previously applied to deuteron-like systems by Tornqvist (1991) and the OBE potential framework developed by Machleidt (1987, 2001) for nuclear forces. It addresses the open problem noted by Esposito (2017) that the OPE potential alone cannot provide sufficient binding, and applies Weinberg's compositeness theorem (1965) to distinguish molecular from elementary states, following the approach of Baru (2004) for a0(980) and f0(980) and Kang and Oller (2017) for X(3872). The results enable future identification of which observed exotic states are genuine hadronic molecules versus compact tetraquarks or other configurations.
