# Properties of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}(3875)^-$ (and their heavy-quark spin partners) in nuclear matter

> We discuss the modification of the properties of the tetraquark-like $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}(3875)^-$ states in dense nuclear matter. We consider the $T_{cc}^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}^-$ in vacuum as purely isoscalar $D^{\ast} D$ and $\overline{D}{}^{\ast} \overline{D}$ $S$-wave bound states, respectively, dynamically generated from a heavy-quark effective interaction between the charmed mesons. We compute the $D$, $\overline{D}$, $D^*$, and $\overline{D}{}^{*}$ spectral functions embedded in a nuclear medium and use them to determine the corresponding $T_{cc}^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}^-$ self energies and spectral functions. We find important modifications of the $D^{\ast} D$ and $\overline{D}{}^{\ast} \overline{D}$ scattering amplitudes and of the pole position of these exotic states already for $ρ_0/2$, with $ρ_0$ the normal nuclear density. We also discuss the dependence of these results on the $D^{\ast} D$ ($\overline{D}{}^{\ast} \overline{D}$) molecular component in the $T_{cc}^+$ ($T_{\bar c\bar c}^-$ ) wave-function. Owing to the different nature of the $D^{(*)}N$ and $\overline{D}{}^{(*)}N$ interactions, we find characteristic changes of the in-medium properties of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}(3875)^-$, which become increasingly visible as the density increases. The experimental confirmation of the found distinctive density-pattern will give support to the molecular picture of these tetraquark-like states, since in the case they were colourless compact quark structures the density behaviour of their respective nuclear medium spectral functions would likely be similar. Finally, we perform similar analyses for the isoscalar $J^P=1^+$ heavy-quark spin symmetry partners of the $T_{cc}^+$ ($T_{cc}^{*+}$) and the $T_{\bar c\bar c}^-$ ($T_{\bar c\bar c}^{*-}$) by considering the $D^{*0}D^{*+}$ and $\overline{D}{}^{*0} D^{*-}$ scattering $T-$matrices.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the in-medium properties of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}(3875)^-$ states will exhibit a distinctive asymmetric density pattern because the $D^{(*)}N$ and $\overline{D}{}^{(*)}N$ interactions differ significantly, and this pattern could serve as a diagnostic to distinguish a molecular structure from a compact tetraquark structure, since the latter would likely produce a different density behavior.

## Method

The authors treat the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and $T_{\bar c\bar c}(3875)^-$ in vacuum as isoscalar $D^*D$ and $\overline{D}{}^*\overline{D}$ $S$-wave bound states dynamically generated from a heavy-quark effective interaction. They compute the $D$, $\overline{D}$, $D^*$, and $\overline{D}{}^*$ spectral functions in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter using a unitarized self-consistent coupled-channels approach, then use these to determine the in-medium $D^*D$ and $\overline{D}{}^*\overline{D}$ loop functions and scattering amplitudes via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The analysis is performed for two families of energy-dependent interaction kernels (type $A$ and type $B$) that allow varying the molecular probability content $P_0$ of the bound states.

## Key numbers

- The $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ binding energy is taken as $B = 0.8$ MeV with respect to the $D^*D$ threshold in the isospin limit.
- The normal nuclear density is $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Significant modifications of the scattering amplitudes and pole positions are found already at $\rho_0/2$.
- A sharp cutoff $\Lambda = 0.7$ GeV is used to regularize the two-meson loop function.
- The $D$ meson quasiparticle peak moves to lower energies with increasing density, while the $D^*$ meson quasiparticle peak moves to higher energies with density.
- The $\overline{D}$ quasiparticle peak is located below the $\overline{D}$ mass and below the $\Theta_c(2805) N^{-1}$ state.
- The $\overline{D}{}^*$ quasiparticle peak is located slightly above 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the $X(3872)$ in dense nuclear matter (Albaladejo 2021) to the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and its antiparticle, and is motivated by the possibility of studying these states in heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as CBM at FAIR. The analysis builds on prior calculations of $D^{(*)}N$ and $\overline{D}{}^{(*)}N$ interactions by Tolos (2009), Garcia-Recio (2010, 2011), and Gamermann (2010), and on the molecular interpretation of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ developed by Albaladejo (2021). The novel aspect is the prediction that the nuclear medium breaks particle-antiparticle symmetry for these states, providing a potential experimental test of their molecular versus compact tetraquark nature.
