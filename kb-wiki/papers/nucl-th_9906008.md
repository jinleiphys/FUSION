# On the origin of the short range NN repulsion

> We calculate S-wave singlet and triplet NN phase shifts stemming from the short-range flavor-spin hyperfine interaction between constituent quarks using the resonating group method approach. A strong short-range repulsion is found in both waves. A fair comparison is performed between the traditional picture, relying on the colour-magnetic interaction, and the present one, relying on the Goldstone boson exchange dynamics. It is shown that the latter one induces essentially stronger repulsion, which is a very welcome feature. We also study a sensitivity of phase shifts and wave function to extention from the one-channel to three-channel resonating group method approximation.

# On the origin of the short range NN repulsion

## Key claim

The short-range flavor-spin hyperfine interaction between constituent quarks, based on Goldstone boson exchange dynamics, produces a strong short-range repulsion in both S-wave singlet and triplet NN phase shifts that is essentially stronger than the repulsion supplied by the traditional colour-magnetic (one-gluon exchange) interaction, but this result is obtained within a toy model that includes only the short-range part of the pion exchange and neglects long-range Yukawa tails.

## Method

The authors calculate S-wave NN phase shifts using the resonating group method (RGM) with a six-quark wave function built from constituent quarks. They compare two hyperfine interaction models: a flavor-spin interaction parametrized as a short-range pseudoscalar exchange (Goldstone boson exchange, GBE) and a colour-magnetic interaction from one-gluon exchange (OGE), with both interactions fitted to reproduce the Delta-N mass splitting. The calculations are performed using both a one-channel ansatz (NN only) and a three-channel ansatz that includes NN, Delta-Delta, and hidden-colour CC channels.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: 313 MeV, harmonic oscillator parameter b = 0.5 fm, cutoff Lambda = 1.0 GeV for both models
- GBE model coupling strength a_chi = 0.068, OGE model coupling strength a_cm = 0.051
- GBE model confinement strength a_c = 47.7 MeV/fm^2, OGE model confinement strength a_c = 93.7 MeV/fm^2
- The flavour-spin interaction produces essentially stronger repulsion than the colour-magnetic interaction in both ^3S_1 and ^1S_0 channels, with the difference visible across the calculated momentum range
- Traditional OGE-based models fail to describe phase shifts above lab energy of about 300 MeV due to insufficient short-range repulsion

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model, previously successful in baryon spectroscopy (Glozman and Riska, 1996; Glozman, Plessas, Varga, and Wagenbrunn, 1998), to the NN interaction problem. It contradicts the traditional picture where short-range NN repulsion is attributed to the colour-magnetic part of one-gluon exchange (Oka and Yazaki, 1984; Shimizu, 1989) and where hybrid models (Kusainov, Neudatchin, and Obukhovsky, 1991; Fernandez, Valcarce, Straub, and Faessler, 1993) found pion exchange produced only insignificant repulsion. The present result enables a path toward a realistic NN description by adding long-range Yukawa tails from one-pion, two-pion (sigma and rho), and possibly omega exchanges.
