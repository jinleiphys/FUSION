# Analytic Transport from Weak to Strong Coupling in the O(N) model

> In this work, a second-order transport coefficient (the curvature-matter coupling $κ$) is calculated exactly for the 3+1d O(N) model at large N for any coupling value. Since the theory is `trivial' in the sense of possessing a Landau pole, the result for $κ$ only is free from cut-off artifacts much below the Landau pole in the effective field theory sense. Nevertheless, this leaves a large range of coupling values where this transport coefficient can be determined non-perturbatively and analytically with little ambiguity. Along with thermodyamic results also calculated in this work, I expect exact large N results to provide good quantitative predictions for N=1 scalar field theory with $ϕ^4$ interaction.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the second-order transport coefficient κ (curvature-matter coupling) exactly for the O(N) model at large N for any coupling value, but the result is only free from cutoff artifacts well below the Landau pole in an effective field theory sense, leaving a large range of coupling values where the coefficient can be determined non-perturbatively and analytically.

## Method

The author computes the Euclidean two-point correlation function of the energy-momentum tensor component T^{xy} for the massless O(N) model in 3+1 dimensions at large N, using the auxiliary field method to obtain the exact scalar propagator from the non-perturbative gap equation. The transport coefficient κ is extracted from the p^2 coefficient of the retarded two-point function via a Kubo formula that relates it to Euclidean correlators, with the calculation performed using dimensional regularization and the MSbar renormalization scheme.

## Key numbers

- κ = -13 N T^2 m_B^2 / (2520 π^2), where m_B is the solution of the gap equation (Eq. 13 in the text)
- In the free theory limit λ_R → 0, m_B → 0, giving κ → 0
- Cutoff scale sensitivity is smaller than 10 percent for λ_R ≲ 2.45
- The entropy density s = (N T^3 m_B^3)/(2π^2) Σ_{n=1}^∞ K_3(n m_B)/n
- The trace anomaly ε - 3P = -N T^4 m_B^4/(32 π^2)
- The speed of sound squared c_s^2 is very close to and above the conformal result of 1/3
- The ratio s/s_free approaches approximately 85 percent for λ_R → ∞

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of κ that were only available for free field theory (Romatschke 2009, Moore 2012), infinitely strongly coupled gauge theories (Bhattacharyya 2008, Baier 2007, Finazzo 2014, Grozdanov 2016), and SU(3) gauge theory from lattice simulations (Philipsen 2013). The result provides a non-perturbative analytic benchmark for testing approximation techniques at intermediate coupling and may be useful for early-time cosmology where curvature-matter couplings play a role in dynamics. The author conjectures that the large N results constitute quantitatively good approximations for finite N, including N=1 scalar φ^4 theory.
