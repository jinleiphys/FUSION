# Analytic expressions for the single particle energies with a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and the relation to Elliott's SU(3) model

> We present an analytical proof and a generalization of the Fayache-Sharon-Zamick relation between single particle energy splittings and the SU(3) limit in Elliott's model.

# Analytic expressions for the single particle energies with a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and the relation to Elliott's SU(3) model

## Key claim

The paper provides an analytical proof that the Fayache-Sharon-Zamick relation, where one third of the single-particle energy splitting in a major shell comes from the valence-core interaction and two thirds from the diagonal Q·Q term, is a general property of the Hamiltonian with a coordinate-space quadrupole-quadrupole interaction that allows ΔN=2 couplings, not merely a numerical coincidence for the s-d and f-p shells. The result holds for any major shell N, provided the valence particle is in a shell adjacent to the closed core.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian consisting of a spherical harmonic oscillator plus an isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in coordinate space only (omitting the momentum-space term). They derive closed-form analytic expressions for the single-particle energies E_{Nℓ} by separating the contribution into a diagonal (one-body) term E^{Q1} from the Q·Q interaction acting on the valence particle itself, and an exchange term E^{Q2} from the interaction of the valence particle with the closed core. Using harmonic oscillator wave functions and angular momentum algebra, they evaluate these contributions explicitly for a valence nucleon in orbital (Nℓ) outside a closed core, and compute the energy splitting Δ_{N(ℓ,ℓ')} between orbital partners within the same major shell.

## Key numbers

- For the N=2 (1s-0d) shell: Δ_{2(2,0)}^{Q1} = 12 χ̅, Δ_{2(2,0)}^{Q2} = 6 χ̅, total Δ_{2(2,0)} = 18 χ̅
- For the N=3 (1p-0f) shell: Δ_{3(3,1)}^{Q1} = 20 χ̅, Δ_{3(3,1)}^{Q2} = 10 χ̅, total Δ_{3(3,1)} = 30 χ̅
- General formula for splitting between ℓ and ℓ−2 partners: Δ_{N(ℓ,ℓ−2)} = 6 χ̅ (2ℓ−1)
- General decomposition: Δ^{Q1} = (2/3)Δ, Δ^{Q2} = (1/3)Δ for any ℓ,ℓ' within the same major shell
- With Elliott's full quadrupole operator Q^E (including momentum terms), the same splitting Δ = 3 χ̅ [ℓ(ℓ+1)−ℓ'(ℓ'+1)] is obtained, shared as: position term 1/6, momentum term 1/6, crossed term 2/3

## Context

This work extends and generalizes the numerical observation of Fayache, Sharon, and Zamick (1997) for the s-d and f-p shells, providing an analytical proof that the one-third/two-thirds division of the single-particle energy splitting is a general property of the coordinate-space Q·Q interaction with ΔN=2 couplings. The result is surprising because Elliott's SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) traditionally restricted the Q·Q interaction to a single major shell by eliminating ΔN=2 matrix elements; here the same SU(3) energy splittings are recovered despite including those inter-shell couplings. The paper also shows that when spin-orbit coupling is added, the SU(3) limit is generally destroyed, though proportionality to (2ℓ−1) is maintained in some cases. This enables further applications of the model Hamiltonian for deriving Q·Q interactions from realistic interactions without needing to justify momentum-dependent terms.
