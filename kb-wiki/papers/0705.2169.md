# Collective states of the odd-mass nuclei within the framework of the Interacting Vector Boson Model

> A supersymmetric extension of the dynamical symmetry group $Sp^{B}(12,R)$ of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), to the orthosymplectic group $OSp(2Ω/12,R)$ is developed in order to incorporate fermion degrees of freedom into the nuclear dynamics and to encompass the treatment of odd mass nuclei. The bosonic sector of the supergroup is used to describe the complex collective spectra of the neighboring even-even nuclei and is considered as a core structure of the odd nucleus. The fermionic sector is represented by the fermion spin group $SO^{F}(2Ω)\supset SU^{F}(2)$. The so obtained, new exactly solvable limiting case is applied for the description of the nuclear collective spectra of odd mass nuclei. The theoretical predictions for different collective bands in three odd mass nuclei, namely $^{157}Gd$, $^{173}Yb$ and $^{163}Dy$ from rare earth region are compared with the experiment. The $B(E2)$ transition probabilities for the $^{157}Gd$ and $^{163}Dy$ between the states of the ground band are also studied. The important role of the symplectic structure of the model for the proper reproduction of the $B(E2)$ behavior is revealed. The obtained results reveal the applicability of the models extension.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a supersymmetric extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) to the orthosymplectic group OSp(2Ω/12,R) can describe collective spectra of odd-mass nuclei, but this claim is qualified by the fact that only a simplified version is applied, where a single particle (or quasiparticle) with a fixed j-value is coupled to an even-even core, and the results agree "rather well" rather than exactly with experimental data.

## Method

The authors extend the dynamical symmetry group Sp^B(12,R) of the IVBM to the orthosymplectic group OSp(2Ω/12,R) by introducing fermion creation and annihilation operators that generate the SO^F(2Ω) algebra, with the fermion spin group SU^F(2) embedded within it. The bosonic sector describes the even-even core nuclei (^156Gd, ^172Yb, ^162Dy) using the Sp(12,R) ⊃ U(6) ⊃ U(3) ⊗ U(2) chain, and the odd-mass nuclei (^157Gd, ^173Yb, ^163Dy) are treated by coupling a single fermion with fixed spin j to the core, with the Hamiltonian expressed as a linear combination of Casimir operators of the subgroups in the chain OSp(2Ω/12,R) ⊃ SO^F(2Ω) ⊗ Sp^B(12,R). The model parameters are determined by fitting the ground and excited bands of the even-even cores first, then only two additional parameters (γ and ζ) are fitted to the ground state band of each odd-mass nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the earlier symplectic extension of the IVBM developed by Ganev, Georgieva, and collaborators (2003-2004) for even-even nuclei to odd-mass nuclei, providing an alternative to the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) of Iachello and Scholten (1979). Unlike the IBFM, where the even-even core is treated as inert, the orthosymplectic structure allows the core to change its number of phonon excitations N, corresponding to a strong coupling limit rather than the weak coupling limit of IBFM. The approach enables exact analytic solutions for odd-mass nuclei with only two additional parameters beyond those used for the even-even core, and it reproduces the correct ordering of band heads where IBFM fails for ^157Gd.
