# Feed-down effect on $Λ$ spin polarization

> We develop a theoretical framework to study the feed-down effect of higher-lying strange baryons on the spin polarization of the $Λ$ hyperon. In this framework, we consider two-body decays through strong, electromagnetic, and weak processes and derive general formulas for the angular distribution and spin polarization of the daughter particle by adopting the helicity formalism. Using the realistic experimental data as input, we explore the feed-down contribution to the global and the local $Λ$ polarizations and find that such a contribution suppresses the primordial $Λ$ polarization, which is not strong enough to resolve the discrepancy between the current theoretical and the experimental results on the azimuthal-angle dependence of $Λ$ polarization. Our paper may also be useful for the measurement of spin polarization of baryons heavier than $Λ$ (e.g., $Ξ^-$) in future experiments.

## Key claim

The feed-down contribution from decays of higher-lying strange baryons suppresses the primordial Λ polarization, but this suppression is not strong enough to resolve the sign discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements of the azimuthal-angle dependence of Λ polarization.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical framework using the helicity formalism to derive general formulas for the angular distribution and spin polarization of daughter particles produced in two-body decays via strong, electromagnetic, and weak processes. They then performed Monte Carlo simulations for noncentral Au + Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV in 10–60% centrality, using primordial particle yields from a statistical thermodynamic model (THERMUS package) and momentum distributions from a blast-wave model fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Only about 21% of final Λ hyperons are primordial; approximately 15% come from primordial Σ⁰ decay, 30% from primordial Σ*⁰, Σ*⁺, and Σ*⁻, 14% from primordial Ξ⁰ and Ξ⁻, 10% from primordial Ξ*⁰ and Ξ*⁻, and 10% from other higher-lying resonance states.
- The polarization transfer factor C = ⟨P_D⟩/P_P is −1/3 for strong decay 1/2⁺ → 1/2⁺ 0⁻, 1 for strong decay 1/2⁻ → 1/2⁺ 0⁻, 1 for strong decay 3/2⁺ → 1/2⁺ 0⁻, −3/5 for strong decay 3/2⁻ → 1/2⁺ 0⁻, (2γ+1)/3 for weak decay 1/2 → 1/2 0, and −1/3 for EM decay 1/2⁺ → 1/2⁺ 1⁻.
- The blast-wave parameters used in the simulation are Tkin = 140 MeV, ⟨β⟩ = 0.445, and l = 1.21, determined by fitting experimental pT spectra of Λ, Λ̄, Ξ⁻, and Ξ̄⁺ in 10–60% central Au + Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of feed-down effects on global Λ polarization by Becattini (2016), which found a linear relation ⟨P_D⟩ = C P_P, to the case of local Λ polarization where the angular dependence matters. The paper addresses the discrepancy between experimental measurements by the STAR Collaboration (2017, 2018, 2019) and theoretical calculations based on thermal vorticity, where the predicted azimuthal-angle dependence of longitudinal and transverse spin polarizations has the opposite sign compared to data. The framework developed here can also be applied to future measurements of spin polarization of heavier baryons such as Ξ⁻.
