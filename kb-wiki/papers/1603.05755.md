# Strange quark matter and quark stars with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model

> We calculate the equation of state of strange quark matter and the interior structure of strange quark stars in a Dyson-Schwinger quark model within rainbow or Ball-Chiu vertex approximation. We emphasize constraints on the parameter space of the model due to stability conditions of ordinary nuclear matter. Respecting these constraints, we find that the maximum mass of strange quark stars is about 1.9 solar masses, and typical radii are 9 --11 km. We obtain an energy release as large as $3.6 \times 10^{53}\,\text{erg}$ from conversion of neutron stars into strange quark stars.

## Key claim

The paper finds that strange quark matter (SQM) can exist only for a fairly narrow range of the Dyson-Schwinger model parameters, specifically requiring a sufficiently strong damping of the in-medium effective interaction (parameter α > 3.6 for the rainbow vertex and α > 2.1 for the Ball-Chiu vertex) and a low bag constant (50 < B_DS < 81 MeV fm⁻³ for rainbow, 53 < B_DS < 81 MeV fm⁻³ for Ball-Chiu); the abstract's claim of a maximum mass of about 1.9 solar masses is conditional on these constraints and is achieved only in the MIT bag-model limit (α⁻¹ = 0) with the smallest allowed bag constant.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator at finite chemical potential using either the rainbow (bare vertex) or Ball-Chiu vertex approximation, with a Gaussian effective interaction that is damped by a parameter α at finite chemical potential. They compute the equation of state of charge-neutral, beta-stable strange quark matter from the quark propagator, introducing a phenomenological bag constant B_DS to set the pressure zero-point. They then impose stability conditions that ordinary nuclear matter must be stable against two-flavor quark matter and that strange quark matter must be stable against ordinary nuclear matter, which constrains the allowed (α, B_DS) parameter space. Finally, they solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain the mass-radius relation and maximum mass of strange quark stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of strange quark stars: about 1.9 solar masses, found in the MIT limit (α⁻¹ = 0) with the smallest allowed bag constant B_min = 59 MeV fm⁻³.
- Typical radii of strange quark stars heavier than one solar mass: 10–11 km.
- Energy release from conversion of a neutron star with M = 2.1 solar masses into a strange quark star: up to 3.6 × 10⁵³ erg, for the MIT-B59 case.
- For a typical neutron star with M = 1.4 solar masses, the energy release ranges from 0.8 × 10⁵³ erg to 2.5 × 10⁵³ erg.
- Allowed parameter range for SQM with rainbow vertex: α > 3.6, 50 < B_DS < 81 MeV fm⁻³.
- Allowed parameter range for SQM with Ball-Chiu vertex: α > 2.1, 53 < B_DS < 81 MeV fm⁻³.
- In the MIT limit (α⁻¹ = 0), the allowed bag constant range is 59 < B_DS < 81 MeV fm⁻³.
- Surface densities of strange quark stars at zero pressure: about 0.25–0.4 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger model studies of hybrid stars (Chen 2011, Chen 2012, Chen 2015a, Chen 2015b) to the case of absolutely stable strange quark matter and strange quark stars. The results contrast with MIT bag-model calculations (Farhi 1984) and perturbative QCD approaches (Fraga 2001, Kurkela 2010) that can produce strongly bound SQM, but are consistent with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Buballa 1996, Klahn 2015) which also does not allow strange quark stars. The maximum mass of about 1.9 solar masses falls below the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (1.93 ± 0.02 solar masses) and PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses), suggesting that if these are strange quark stars, the model requires the MIT limit with minimal bag constant. The energy release results (up to 3.6 × 10⁵³ erg) are quantitatively similar to those of Bombaci (2000), who found a range of (1–4) × 10⁵³ erg.
