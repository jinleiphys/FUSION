# Amplitude reconstruction from complete experiments and truncated partial-wave expansions

> We compare the methods of amplitude reconstruction, for a complete experiment and a truncated partial wave analysis, applied to the photoproduction of pseudo-scalar mesons. The approach is pedagogical, showing in detail how the amplitude reconstruction (observables measured at a single energy and angle) is related to a truncated partial-wave analysis (observables measured at a single energy and a number of angles).

## Key claim

The paper pedagogically demonstrates that for pseudo-scalar meson photoproduction, a complete experiment analysis (CEA) and a truncated partial-wave analysis (TPWA) are mathematically equivalent when measurements are taken at a single energy and angle, but the TPWA can reduce the number of required observables by exploiting angular distributions, subject to the condition that the partial-wave expansion is truncated at a finite upper limit L.

## Method

The authors compare CEA and TPWA methods by working through explicit algebraic inversions for the J=1/2 case (E0+ and M1- multipoles), then generalize to higher truncation orders using the Omelaenko decomposition of transversity amplitudes into polynomial root structures. They derive closed-form solutions for the J=1/2 case showing how multipole moduli and relative phases are extracted from angular coefficients, and extend the counting to sets with up to 8 S,P,D wave multipoles using Table III.

## Key numbers

- For J=1/2 (E0+ and M1- multipoles): CEA requires 4 measurements at a single angle; TPWA requires 4 measurements at 1 angle or 3 observables (I, P, Cx) measured at 2 angles
- For 3 S,P wave multipoles (E0+, M1-, E1+): CEA requires 6 measurements at 1 angle; TPWA requires 6 measurements at 1 angle or 3 observables (I, Sigma, T) measured at 3 angles
- For full set of 4 S,P wave multipoles (E0+, M1-, E1+, M1+): CEA at 1 angle is not sufficient; TPWA requires 8 measurements at 2 angles with 5 observables or 8 measurements at 3 angles with 4 observables
- For 8 S,P,D wave multipoles: TPWA requires 16 measurements at 3 angles with 6 observables, or 16 measurements at 5 angles with 4 observables
- In the J=1/2 case, the moduli are given by |E0+| = sqrt((sigma0 + Cx0)/2) and |M1-| = sqrt((sigma0 - Cx0)/2), with the relative phase extracted from e^{i phi} = (-sigma1 + i P0)/(sqrt(sigma0+Cx0)*sqrt(sigma0-Cx0))

## Context

This work extends the foundational studies of Barker, Donnachie and Storrow (1975) on complete experiments and the truncated partial-wave analysis formalism of Omelaenko (1981) and Grushin (1986). It clarifies the relationship between the CEA approach of Chiang and Tabakin (1997), who showed that 8 observables suffice for a complete experiment, and the TPWA approach requiring angular distributions. The paper enables practical guidance for designing measurement campaigns at facilities like Jefferson Lab and Mainz, showing that the minimal number of observables and angles depends on the truncation order and that some sets (e.g., I, Sigma, F, H for 4 S,P wave multipoles) can work without recoil measurements.
