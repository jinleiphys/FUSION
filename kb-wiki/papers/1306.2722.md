# $ρ- ω$ Mixing in $J/ψ\to VP$ Decays

> The study on $ρ-ω$ mixing is mainly focused on vector mesons decay with isospin I=1, namely $ρ(ω)\toπ^+π^-$ process. In this paper, we present the study of $ρ-ω$ mixing in $ρ(ω)\toπ^+π^-π^0$ (I=0) using a flavor parameterization model for $J/ψ\to VP$ process. By fitting theoretical frame to PDG data, we obtain the SU(3)-breaking effect parameters $s_V=0.03\pm 0.12,\ s_P=0.17\pm 0.17$ and the $ρ-ω$ mixing polarization operator $Π_{ρω}=0.006\pm 0.011\text{\text{GeV}}^2$. The branching ratios are also renewed when mixing effect is incorporated: $Br(J/ψ\to ωπ^0) = (3.64 \pm 0.37)\times 10^{-4}$, $Br(J/ψ\to ωη) = (1.48 \pm 0.17)\times 10^{-3}$, $Br(J/ψ\to ωη^{\prime}) = (1.55\pm 0.56)\times 10^{-4}$; they are different from the corresponding PDG2012 values by 19%, 15% and 15%, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper studies rho-omega mixing in the isospin I=0 channel (rho(omega) to 3pi) using J/psi to VP decays, finding that the mixing polarization operator Pi_rhoomega = 0.006 +/- 0.011 GeV^2 has a significance of only 0.36, meaning it has a large probability to be zero.

## Method

The authors applied a flavor parameterization model to J/psi to vector-pseudoscalar (VP) decays, fitting the theoretical framework to 11 branching ratios from PDG2012 data. They incorporated rho-omega mixing effects into the amplitudes for J/psi to omega pi^0, omega eta, and omega eta' decays, using a chi-squared minimization over 12 parameters including SU(3)-breaking parameters s_V and s_P, mixing angles, and the polarization operator Pi_rhoomega.

## Key numbers

- Pi_rhoomega = 0.006 +/- 0.011 GeV^2 (rho-omega mixing polarization operator)
- SU(3)-breaking parameters: s_V = 0.03 +/- 0.12, s_P = 0.17 +/- 0.17
- Br(J/psi to omega pi^0) = (3.64 +/- 0.37) x 10^{-4} (19% difference from PDG2012)
- Br(J/psi to omega eta) = (1.48 +/- 0.17) x 10^{-3} (15% difference from PDG2012)
- Br(J/psi to omega eta') = (1.55 +/- 0.56) x 10^{-4} (15% difference from PDG2012)
- The rho-omega mixing effect on the 3pi invariant mass spectrum reaches about +/- 20% variance at the largest above or below omega nominal mass

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rho-omega mixing that focused on isospin I=1 decays (rho(omega) to pi+ pi-), such as those by Bramon (1986) and O'Connell (1997), to the isospin I=0 channel using J/psi decays. The flavor parameterization method follows the approach of Abraham (1988), Escribano (2010), and Thomas (2007) for J/psi to VP processes. The results enable future experimental checks of the mixing effect in e+ e- to J/psi to 4pi processes, with the SND group's framework for e+ e- to 3pi (Achasov 2003) providing a comparable calculation method.
