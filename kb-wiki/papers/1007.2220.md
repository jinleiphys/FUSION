# J/Psi mass shift in nuclear matter

> The $J/Ψ$ mass shift in cold nuclear matter is computed using an effective Lagrangian approach. The mass shift is computed by evaluating $D$ and $D^*$ meson loop contributions to the $J/Ψ$ self-energy employing medium-modified meson masses. The modification of the $D$ and $D^*$ masses in nuclear matter is obtained using the quark-meson coupling model. The loop integrals are regularized with dipole form factors and the sensitivity of the results to the values of form-factor cutoff masses is investigated. The $J/Ψ$ mass shift arising from the modification of the $D$ and $D^*$ loops at normal nuclear matter density is found to range from -16 MeV to -24 MeV under a wide variation of values of the cutoff masses. Experimental perspectives for the formation of a bound state of $J/Ψ$ to a nucleus are investigated.

## Key claim

The J/Psi mass shift in cold nuclear matter, arising from D and D* meson loop contributions with medium-modified masses, is predicted to be between -16 MeV and -24 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, but this range depends on the choice of form-factor cutoff masses and on whether a gauged or non-gauged Lagrangian is used.

## Method

The J/Psi self-energy was computed by evaluating D Dbar, D Dbar*, D* Dbar, and D* Dbar* meson loop diagrams using effective Lagrangians with coupling constants fixed by vector-meson dominance. The in-medium masses of the D and D* mesons were calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, where scalar and vector mean fields couple only to the light quarks in the charmed mesons. The loop integrals were regularized with dipole form factors, and the cutoff masses were varied between 1000 MeV and 3000 MeV to assess sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The J/Psi mass shift at normal nuclear matter density ranges from -16 MeV to -24 MeV for cutoff masses between 1000 MeV and 3000 MeV, using the non-gauged Lagrangian (xi = 0).
- Using the gauged Lagrangian (xi = 1), the J/Psi mass shift ranges from -13 MeV to -18.5 MeV over the same cutoff range.
- The QMC model predicts a 62 MeV mass drop for both the D and D* mesons at normal nuclear matter density.
- The D*D* loop gives the largest contribution to the mass shift, ranging from -11 MeV to -13 MeV depending on the cutoff, and is relatively insensitive to the cutoff value.
- For a medium-size nucleus with radius 5 fm, a potential depth greater than 1 MeV is sufficient to bind a J/Psi, based on the nonrelativistic s-wave bound-state condition.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of charmonium mass shifts in nuclear matter, particularly the study by Ko et al. (2000) which found a positive J/Psi mass shift using a gauged Lagrangian and a linear density-dependent D meson mass decrease of 50 MeV. The present calculation resolves that puzzling positive shift by using a non-gauged Lagrangian and more realistic medium-modified masses from the QMC model. The results enable future calculations of finite-nucleus J/Psi potentials and momentum-dependent effects, and motivate experimental searches for J/Psi-nucleus bound states at facilities like the upgraded CEBAF at Jefferson Lab and FAIR.
