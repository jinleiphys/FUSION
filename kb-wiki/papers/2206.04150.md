# Theoretical Uncertainty Quantification for Heavy-ion Fusion

> Despite recent advances and focus on rigorous uncertainty quantification for microscopic models of quantum many-body systems, the uncertainty on the dynamics of those systems has been under-explored. To address this, we have used time-dependent Hartree-Fock to examine the model uncertainty for a collection of low-energy, heavy-ion fusion reactions. Fusion reactions at near-barrier energies represent a rich test-bed for the dynamics of quantum many-body systems owing to the complex interplay of collective excitation, transfer, and static effects that determine the fusion probability of a given system. While the model uncertainty is sizable for many of the systems studied, the primary contribution comes from ill-constrained static properties, such as the neutron radius of neutron-rich nuclei. These large uncertainties motivate the use of information from reactions to better constrain existing models and to infer static properties from reaction data.

# Theoretical Uncertainty Quantification for Heavy-ion Fusion

## Key claim
The uncertainty in TDHF-predicted fusion cross-sections originates primarily from ill-constrained static properties of the energy density functional (EDF), particularly the neutron radius of neutron-rich nuclei, rather than from dynamical uncertainties in the reaction modeling itself.

## Method
The authors used the density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method to compute fusion potentials and cross-sections for four heavy-ion systems: $^{40,48}$Ca$+^{40,48}$Ca and $^{16}$O$+^{208}$Pb. They repeated the calculations for an ensemble of 100 EDFs sampled from Bayesian posterior distributions for the UNEDF1 parametrization, then used the resulting potential barriers to compute fusion cross-sections via incoming wave boundary conditions.

## Key numbers
- Barrier heights for $^{48}$Ca$+^{48}$Ca range from $V_B \simeq 47.7$ to 52.1 MeV, an $\sim 8\%$ fluctuation
- Barrier radii for $^{48}$Ca$+^{48}$Ca vary between $R_B \simeq 10.5$ and 11.2 fm
- $^{48}$Ca neutron skin thickness ranges from 0.13 to 0.22 fm across the EDF ensemble
- $^{208}$Pb neutron skin thickness fluctuates between 0.14 and 0.19 fm
- Sub-barrier fusion cross-sections for $^{48}$Ca$+^{48}$Ca vary by up to $\sim 3$ orders of magnitude across the ensemble
- TDHF calculations for calcium systems used a $44 \times 28 \times 28$ fm$^3$ box; oxygen-lead simulations used $42 \times 24 \times 24$ fm$^3$, both with 1.0 fm mesh spacing

## Context
This work extends Bayesian uncertainty quantification for EDF parameters, previously applied to static nuclear properties by McDonnell (2015), into the domain of nuclear reaction dynamics. It builds on the DC-TDHF method developed by Umar (2006) and subsequent applications to fusion by Simenel (2012, 2018) and others. The results demonstrate that reaction data, particularly fusion cross-sections for neutron-rich systems, could be used to constrain EDF parameters and infer static properties such as neutron skin thickness, motivating future inclusion of reaction observables in EDF fitting protocols.
