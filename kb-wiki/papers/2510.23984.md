# Symmetry Energy of 2+1-flavor dense quark matter from perturbative QCD

> The symmetry energy expansion was developed to connect isospin symmetric matter probed in nuclear experiments to asymmetric matter found in neutron stars. Using the isospin asymmetry derived from the Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula, we derive the symmetry energy expansion for quark matter that has unique properties compared to hadronic matter. To test our methods, we use perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (pQCD) calculations at next-to-leading-order, where realistic quark masses can be included. We find that pQCD at electroweak equilibrium is not isospin symmetric but rather obtains a small skewness term in the symmetry energy expansion. We predict that if equations of state for nuclear matter must match pQCD results, then a non-monotonic dip in the symmetry energy would appear.

## Key claim
The authors derive a symmetry energy expansion for 2+1-flavor quark matter using perturbative QCD at next-to-leading order, and find that under electroweak equilibrium (with finite strange quark mass) the expansion acquires a non-zero skewness term, unlike the isospin-symmetric case. They predict that if nuclear matter equations of state must match pQCD results at high densities, a non-monotonic dip in the symmetry energy would appear, though this is a conditional statement dependent on the matching requirement.

## Method
The authors use the NLO pQCD thermodynamic potential from Graf et al. (2015) for 2+1 flavors at zero temperature, including a finite strange quark mass via a running coupling and running mass prescription. They compute the energy per baryon as a function of isospin asymmetry δ_I (derived from the Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula) for two regimes: isospin-symmetric matter (μ_S = -μ_Q/2) and strange electroweak equilibrium (μ_S = 0), then extract the quadratic and cubic symmetry energy coefficients by fitting the expansion E/n_B = E_0 + E_sym,2 δ_I^2 + E_sym,3 δ_I^3.

## Key numbers
- The quadratic symmetry energy coefficient E_sym,2 ranges from approximately 50 to 120 MeV for pQCD at high baryon densities (up to about 12 fm^{-3} or 75 n_sat).
- For comparison, the hadronic CMF++ model gives E_sym,2 ~ 50-200 MeV over a lower density range.
- The skewness coefficient E_sym,3 in pQCD is approximately 6 times smaller than the corresponding term in the hadronic CMF++ model from Yang (2025).
- At δ_I = 0 and high n_B, the strangeness fraction Y_S approaches -1 from above, while the charge fraction Y_Q approaches 0 from above.
- The maximum isospin asymmetry δ_I^{max} = 3 occurs when only down quarks are present; the minimum δ_I = -3 occurs for only up quarks.

## Context
This work extends the symmetry energy expansion formalism developed by Bombaci (1991) and recently generalized to include strangeness by Yang (2025) for hadronic matter, now applied to quark matter using pQCD. It contrasts with prior quark matter symmetry energy studies (e.g., Chu 2012) that used an incomplete definition of isospin asymmetry and did not explore the skewness term. The results enable future work connecting hadronic equations of state to pQCD at high densities, and suggest that a non-monotonic dip in the symmetry energy would signal the hadron-to-quark transition.
