# Nuclear symmetry energy in a modified quark meson coupling model

> We study nuclear symmetry energy and the thermodynamic instabilities of asymmetric nuclear matter in a self-consistent manner by using a modified quark-meson coupling model where the confining interaction for quarks inside a nucleon is represented by a phenomenologically averaged potential in an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form. The nucleon-nucleon interaction in nuclear matter is then realized by introducing additional quark couplings to $σ$, $ω$, and $ρ$ mesons through mean-field approximations. We find an analytic expression for the symmetry energy ${\cal E}_{sym}$ as a function of its slope $L$. Our result establishes a linear correlation between $L$ and ${\cal E}_{sym}$. We also analyze the constraint on neutron star radii in $(pn)$ matter with $β$ equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytic expression for the nuclear symmetry energy \( \mathcal{E}_{sym} \) as a function of its slope \( L \), establishing a linear correlation between these two quantities within the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model. This linearity is a consequence of having only a single isovector parameter \( g_\rho^2 \) in the model, and the authors note that a nonlinear behavior would be expected for models with more than one isovector parameter.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier MQMC model for symmetric nuclear matter to asymmetric nuclear matter by including the isovector vector meson \( \rho \) in addition to the isoscalar scalar \( \sigma \) and vector \( \omega \) mesons. Nucleons are treated as composites of constituent quarks confined by a phenomenologically averaged potential in an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form, with quark couplings to the meson fields treated in a mean-field approximation. The nucleon mass in the medium is obtained by including center-of-mass, pionic, and gluonic corrections, and the model parameters are fit to reproduce the saturation properties of nuclear matter (binding energy, density, and symmetry energy) for three different quark masses (5, 40, and 300 MeV).

## Key numbers

* Saturation density \( \rho_0 = 0.15 \) fm\(^{-3}\) and binding energy per nucleon \( B_0 = -15.7 \) MeV are used to fit the model parameters for all three quark masses.
* Symmetry energy at saturation \( J = 32.0 \) MeV is fixed for all three quark masses.
* Compressibility \( K_0 \) values are 159, 208, and 349 MeV for quark masses 5, 40, and 300 MeV, respectively.
* Slope of symmetry energy \( L^0 \) values are 84.7, 84.9, and 89.1 MeV for quark masses 5, 40, and 300 MeV, respectively.
* The asymmetry incompressibility \( K_{asy} \) values are -535.9, -537.6, and -549.0 MeV for quark masses 5, 40, and 300 MeV, respectively, which are consistent with the experimental GMR value of \( K_{asy} = -550 \pm 100 \) MeV.
* Effective nucleon mass ratio \( M^*/M \) at saturation is 0.93, 0.91, and 0.76 for quark masses 5, 40, and 300 MeV, respectively; the 300 MeV value (0.76) compares well with the empirical value of 0.74.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier MQMC model for symmetric nuclear matter (Mishra et al., 2015) to asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star matter, providing an alternative to the MIT-bag-based quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) and subsequent works. The analytic correlation between \( \mathcal{E}_{sym} \) and \( L \) builds on the numerical study of Ducoin et al. (2011) and the analytic approach of Santos et al. (2014), but applies it for the first time in a QMC-based framework. The results enable further constraints on neutron star radii in beta-equilibrium matter and provide a test of the model against giant monopole resonance (GMR) measurements in neutron-rich tin isotopes.
