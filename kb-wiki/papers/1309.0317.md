# Properties of N=90 Isotones within the Mean Field Perspective

> In recent years, the N=90 isotones have been investigated to a large extent in relation to studies of quantum phase transitions. In this paper, we use the mean field approach with pairing-deformation self consistent Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations to study the N=90 isotones and neigh- bouring nuclei. The important probes, such as moments of inertia, quadrupole moments, the energy ratio of E(4+1)/E(2+1), octupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom are considered and the cal- culated results are compared with the available experimental data. From a microscopic point of view, the N=90 isotones characterize the onset of the deformed region and are very well described by mean field calculations. The results are compared with those from other studies in beyond mean-field approximations. Shape coexistence phenomena in the region of interest are discussed.

## Key claim

The N=90 isotones are well described by a static deformed mean field approach, but the authors argue that the oblate minima seen in axially symmetric calculations are spurious saddle points, and that there is no evidence for prolate-oblate shape coexistence in this mass region. The paper hedges that while the mean field works well from N=90 onward, the presence of pairing correlations and fluctuations makes it difficult to use the critical point symmetry value of 2.91 as a definitive characterization of a phase transition in real nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed pairing-deformation self-consistent Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations based on a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with the Strutinsky shell correction approach. Rotational states were generated via the cranking approximation, pairing correlations were included through a seniority pairing force and double-stretched quadrupole pairing interaction, and approximate particle number projection was employed via the Lipkin-Nogami method. The total energy was minimized with respect to shape parameters including triaxial, octupole, and hexadecapole deformations for even-even Nd, Sm, Gd, and Dy isotopes from N=82 to N=98.

## Key numbers

- The N=90 isotones from Nd to Dy all have well developed minima at β₂ ≈ 0.23.
- For N=98, the equilibrium shape has β₂ ≈ 0.28.
- The calculated E(4⁺₁)/E(2⁺₁) ratios for N=90 isotones: ²⁵⁰Nd = 2.90 (experimental 2.927), ²⁵²Sm = 3.04 (experimental 3.009), ²⁵⁴Gd = 3.12 (experimental 3.015), ²⁵⁶Dy = 3.07 (experimental 2.934).
- A value of 2.9 appears to be a lower limit for the R₄/₂ ratio obtainable in cranking calculations; the SU(3) symmetry limit of an ideal rigid rotor gives 3.3.
- The change in quadrupole moment with neutron number, dq/dN, is largest between N=88 and N=90 for the chains from Nd to Dy.
- Octupole softness is largest at N=86-88 (~0.8-0.9) for Nd and Sm isotopes, and at N=84 (~0.5) for Gd and Dy isotopes.

## Context

This work extends mean field studies of quantum phase transitions in N=90 isotones, comparing with Skyrme Hartree-Fock BCS (HFBCS) calculations and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny interaction by Robledo and collaborators. The authors find that their results are consistent with those of Tomas and Li in showing that oblate minima are saddle points, contradicting earlier suggestions of shape coexistence from axially symmetric calculations by Bonatsos, Rodrigues, and Robledo. The study enables future work to examine whether the mean field description of N=90 isotones relates to critical point symmetry, while cautioning that pairing correlations and fluctuations may smear out any sharp phase transition.
