# Ab Initio Calculations of Medium-Mass Nuclei with Explicit Chiral 3N Interactions

> We present the first ab initio coupled-cluster calculations of medium-mass nuclei with explicit chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions. Using a spherical formulation of coupled cluster with singles and doubles excitations including explicit 3N contributions, we study ground states of 16,24-O, 40,48-Ca and 56-Ni. We employ chiral NN plus 3N interactions softened through a similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation at the three-body level. We investigate the impact of all truncations and quantify the resulting uncertainties---this includes the contributions from triples excitations, the truncation of the set of three-body matrix elements, and the omission of SRG-induced four-body interactions. Furthermore, we assess the quality of a normal-ordering approximation of the 3N interaction beyond light nuclei. Our study points towards the predictive power of chiral Hamiltonians in the medium-mass regime.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first ab initio coupled-cluster calculations for medium-mass nuclei that include explicit chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions, rather than relying on approximate reductions to effective two-body forces. The authors demonstrate that, within quantified uncertainties of a few percent, the chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians predict ground-state energies in agreement with experiment, even though no information beyond the few-body domain (A less than or equal to 4) was used to fix the interaction parameters.

## Method

The authors performed spherical coupled-cluster calculations with singles and doubles excitations including explicit three-body contributions (CCSD3B) for the ground states of oxygen-16, oxygen-24, calcium-40, calcium-48, and nickel-56. They employed chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and chiral 3N interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), softened through a similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation at the three-body level. The calculations used a harmonic-oscillator single-particle basis truncated at principal quantum number e_max = 12, with three-body matrix elements limited to e1+e2+e3 less than or equal to E3max = 14, and the authors systematically investigated the impact of truncations including triples excitations via Lambda-CCSD(T), the truncation of three-body matrix elements, and the omission of SRG-induced four-body interactions.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of oxygen-16 with the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian at alpha = 0.08 fm^4: -128.2 MeV (CCSD3B, E3max=12, e_max=12, hbar-Omega=20 MeV).
* Ground-state energy of calcium-40 with the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian at alpha = 0.08 fm^4: -369.0 MeV (CCSD3B, E3max=12, e_max=12, hbar-Omega=24 MeV).
* Ground-state energy of nickel-56 with the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian at alpha = 0.08 fm^4: -517.0 MeV (CCSD3B, E3max=12, e_max=12, hbar-Omega=28 MeV).
* For the softest interactions (alpha = 0.08 fm^4), the inclusion of triples excitations via Lambda-CCSD(T) lowers ground-state energies by 1.5 to 2 percent for all nuclei and both Hamiltonians.
* The NO2B approximation deviates from full CCSD3B by at most 0.9 MeV (0.8 percent) for oxygen-16 and 4 MeV (0.8 percent) for nickel-56 across all Hamiltonians considered.
* For nickel-56 at alpha = 0.08 fm^4, a calculation with only SRG-evolved NN interactions yields an unphysical ground-state energy of about -950 MeV.

## Context

This work extends ab initio coupled-cluster calculations, previously limited to two-body Hamiltonians or approximate treatments of three-body forces, to include explicit chiral 3N interactions in medium-mass nuclei. It builds on the normal-ordering approximation (NO2B) introduced by Roth and Binder (2012) and on SRG-evolved chiral Hamiltonians developed by Roth et al. (2011). The results demonstrate that the NO2B approximation works extremely well for nuclei up to nickel-56, enabling future studies to use this computationally cheaper approach, while also quantifying the remaining uncertainties from triples excitations, three-body matrix element truncation, and SRG-induced many-body forces that must be addressed for heavier systems.
