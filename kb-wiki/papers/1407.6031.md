# Sub-barrier enhancement of fusion as compared to a microscopic method in 18O+12C

> Measurement of the energy dependence of the fusion cross-sec on at sub-barrier energies provides an important test for theoretical models of fusion. To extend the measurement of fusion cross-sections in the sub-barrier domain for the 18O+12C system. Use the new experimental data to confront microscopic calculations of fusion. Evaporation residues produced in fusion of 18O ions with 12C target nuclei were detected with good geometric efficiency and identified by measuring their energy and time-of-flight. Theoretical calculations with a density constrained time dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) theory include for the first time the effect of pairing on the fusion cross-section. Comparison of the measured fusion excitation function with the predictions of the DC-TDHF calculations reveal that the experimental data exhibits a smaller decrease in cross-section with decreasing energy than is theoretically predicted. The larger cross-sections observed at the lowest energies measured indicate a larger tunneling probability for the fusion process. This larger probability can be associated with a smaller, narrower fusion barrier than presently included in the theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The experimental fusion cross-sections for 18O+12C decrease more slowly with decreasing energy than predicted by density constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) calculations that include pairing for the first time, indicating a larger tunneling probability associated with a smaller, narrower fusion barrier than the theory presently includes.

## Method

Evaporation residues from fusion of 18O ions with a 12C target were directly detected using a segmented annular silicon detector measuring energy and time-of-flight, with beam pulsing and microchannel plate detectors providing timing signals. Theoretical calculations employed the density constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method, which for the first time incorporated pairing effects via a BCS pairing calculation for the static solution of 18O, resulting in a spherical nucleus with frozen occupations used in the time evolution.

## Key numbers

- The experiment measured fusion cross-sections down to 2.8 mb, approximately one order of magnitude lower than previous measurements which only reached the 25 mb level.
- At the highest energies, pairing reduces the DC-TDHF cross-section to approximately 80% of the value calculated without pairing; at the lowest energies, pairing reduces the cross-section to approximately 36% of the value without pairing.
- The ratio of experimental to DC-TDHF (with pairing) cross-sections reaches a minimum of 0.54 at E_c.m. = 7 MeV and increases to 1.32 at the lowest measured energy, E_c.m. = 6 MeV.
- The enhancement of experimental fusion cross-sections relative to DC-TDHF predictions is a factor of approximately 2.4 as incident energy decreases from E_c.m. = 7 MeV to E_c.m. = 6 MeV.
- The best fit to experimental data using an inverted parabolic barrier model yields R_c = 7.24 ± 0.16 fm, V = 7.62 ± 0.14 MeV, and ℏω = 2.78 ± 0.29 MeV; the DC-TDHF calculated barrier height is 7.60 MeV.
- The bombarding energy dependent geometric efficiency of the experimental setup lies between 50% and 59%.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of fusion for the 18O+12C system by Eyal (1976), Kovar (1979), and Heusch (1982), which only reached cross-sections down to approximately 25 mb, whereas the present data reaches 2.8 mb. The DC-TDHF calculations build on prior work by Umar (2012) and deSouza (2013) for O+O and O+C systems relevant to neutron star crust reactions, but now include pairing effects for the first time. The observed sub-barrier enhancement of fusion cross-sections relative to microscopic theory opens the question of whether relaxing the frozen-pairing approximation in the dynamics would yield larger theoretical cross-sections, and demonstrates the importance of measuring sub-barrier fusion for neutron-rich light nuclei to gain new insight into fusion dynamics.
