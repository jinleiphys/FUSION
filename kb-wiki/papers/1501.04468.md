# A study of vorticity formation in high energy nuclear collisions

> We present a quantitative study of vorticity formation in peripheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at sqrt(s)NN = 200 GeV by using the ECHO-QGP numerical code, implementing relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics in the causal Israel-Stewart framework in 3+1 dimensions with an initial Bjorken flow profile. We consider and discuss different definitions of vorticity which are relevant in relativistic hydrodynamics. After demonstrating the excellent capabilities of our code, which proves to be able to reproduce Gubser flow up to 8 fm/c, we show that, with the initial conditions needed to reproduce the measured directed flow in peripheral collisions corresponding to an average impact parameter b=11.6 fm and with the Bjorken flow profile for a viscous Quark Gluon Plasma with η/s=0.1 fixed, a vorticity of the order of some 10^{-2} c/fm can develop at freezeout. The ensuing polarization of Lambda baryons does not exceed 1.4% at midrapidity. We show that the amount of developed directed flow is sensitive to both the initial angular momentum of the plasma and its viscosity.

# A study of vorticity formation in high energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

Using the ECHO-QGP code with Bjorken initial conditions and a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of η/s=0.1, vorticity of order 10^{-2} c/fm can develop at freezeout in peripheral Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, but the resulting Lambda baryon polarization does not exceed 1.4% at midrapidity; the calculation does not include viscous corrections to particle distribution at freezeout or initial state fluctuations, as it aims for a general assessment rather than a precision fit.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic simulations using the ECHO-QGP code in the causal Israel-Stewart framework, with initial Bjorken flow profile (u^x=u^y=u^eta=0) and a modified energy density longitudinal profile that breaks eta→-eta symmetry to impart angular momentum to the plasma. They calculated several definitions of vorticity (kinematical, T-vorticity, and thermal vorticity) at the isothermal freezeout hypersurface at T_fo=130 MeV, using initial conditions tuned to reproduce the measured directed flow in peripheral Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with average impact parameter b=11.57 fm.

## Key numbers

- Vorticity magnitude at freezeout: of order 10^{-2} c/fm
- Lambda baryon polarization at midrapidity: does not exceed 1.4%
- Impact parameter: b=11.57 fm (corresponding to mean value of 40-80% centrality class, range 9.49 < b < 13.42 fm)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s=0.1 (fixed)
- Initial proper energy density: ε_0=30 GeV/fm^3
- Initial proper time: τ_0=0.6 fm/c
- Freezeout temperature: T_fo=130 MeV
- Beam rapidity at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: y_beam≈5.36
- Numerical viscosity upper bound from T-vorticity extrapolation: |η/s| ≲ 0.002
- Gubser flow test reproduced up to 8 fm/c with grid spacing 0.025 fm and time step 0.001 fm/c, η/s=0.2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vorticity in peripheral heavy-ion collisions by Csernai et al. (2013) and others, which used ideal fluid approximations and isochronous freezeout with Cartesian initial conditions, by incorporating dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics in 3+1D with Bjorken coordinates. The initial conditions follow the parametrization of Bozek (2010) that reproduces directed flow in 3+1D ideal hydrodynamics. The results enable quantitative predictions for Lambda hyperon polarization as a probe of vorticity in the quark-gluon plasma, and demonstrate that the ECHO-QGP code achieves numerical viscosity below η/s≈0.002, comparable to the performance reported by Karpenko et al. (2014).
