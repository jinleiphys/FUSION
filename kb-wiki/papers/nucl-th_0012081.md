# Structure functions for the three nucleon system

> The spectral functions and light-cone momentum distributions of protons and neutrons in 3He and 3H are given in terms of the three-nucleon wave function for realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. To reduce computational complexity, separable expansions are employed for the nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results for the light-cone momentum distributions suggest that they are not very sensitive to the details of the two-body interaction, as long as it has reasonable short-range repulsion. The unpolarised and polarised structure functions are examined for both 3He and 3H in order to test the usefulness of 3He as a neutron target. It is found that the measurement of the spin structure function of polarised 3H would provide a very clear test of the predicted change in the polarised parton distributions of a bound proton.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the light-cone momentum distributions for protons and neutrons in ³He and ³H are not very sensitive to the details of the two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction, provided it has reasonable short-range repulsion, and that measuring the spin structure function of polarised ³H would provide a very clear test of the predicted change in the polarised parton distributions of a bound proton.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon bound state using separable expansions of realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, specifically the PEST (separable Paris) potential, the unitary pole approximation (UPA) to the Reid Soft Core potential, and Yamaguchi-type potentials. They computed the spectral functions and light-cone momentum distributions for protons and neutrons in ³He and ³H from the resulting three-nucleon wave functions, then used the convolution formalism to evaluate unpolarised and polarised structure functions.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the three-nucleon system for the PEST 18-channel potential (including all nucleon-nucleon channels with J ≤ 2) underbinds the experimental value, while Yamaguchi potentials overbind; the UPA and PEST five-channel results are similar to each other.
- The authors adjusted the strength of the ¹S₀ interaction to reproduce the experimental binding energy of both ³He and ³H, allowing estimation of the error from neglecting the Coulomb energy for ³He.
- The normalisation of the total wave function is given by ⟨Ψ|Ψ⟩ = 3⟨φ_α|φ_α⟩ + 6⟨φ_α|φ_β⟩, and the truncated sum over partial waves must agree with this result.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering approach of Ciofi degli Atti and Liuti (1990), who used variational wave functions rather than Faddeev solutions, and who had to explicitly correct the proton momentum distribution for the tri-nucleon system. The present calculation uses a different technique (exact Faddeev equations with separable expansions) and studies the dependence on the assumed two-nucleon force explicitly. The results enable more reliable extraction of the neutron spin structure function from polarised ³He data and suggest that polarised ³H measurements could test bound-proton parton distribution modifications.
