# Relation between the density-matrix theory and the pairing theory

> The time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) gives a correlated ground state as a stationary solution of the time-dependent equations for one-body and two-body density matrices. The small amplitude limit of TDDM (STDDM) is a version of extended RPA theories which include the effects of ground state correlations. It is shown that the solutions of the Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov theory and the quasi-particle RPA satisfy the TDDM and STDDM equations, respectively, when only pairing-type correlations are taken into account in TDDM and STDDM.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory and the quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) are special cases of the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) and its small-amplitude limit (STDDM), but only when the two-body correlation matrix in TDDM is restricted to a separable product of pairing tensors and only pairing-type correlations are retained.

## Method

The authors analytically compared the equations of motion of TDDM for the one-body density matrix and the correlated part of the two-body density matrix with the corresponding equations of TDHFB and HFB. They substituted the approximation that the two-body correlation matrix \(C_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'}\) equals the product \(\kappa^*_{\alpha'\beta'}\kappa_{\alpha\beta}\) of pairing tensors into the TDDM equations and verified that the TDHFB and HFB equations are recovered. Similarly, they showed that the QRPA equations for the one-body and two-body transition amplitudes satisfy the STDDM equations when the two-body transition amplitude is approximated as \(X_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'} \approx \kappa^0_{\alpha\beta}\phi_{\beta'\alpha'} + \kappa^{0*}_{\alpha'\beta'}\psi_{\alpha\beta}\).

## Key numbers

- The stationary TDDM ground-state equations (Eqs. Agrc1 and Agrc2) reduce to the HFB equations (Eqs. hfb1 and hfb2) when \(C^0_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'} \approx \kappa^{0*}_{\alpha'\beta'}\kappa^0_{\alpha\beta}\) and only pairing-type correlations are kept.
- The STDDM eigenvalue equation (Eq. stddm0) with eigenvalue \(\omega_\mu\) reduces to the QRPA matrix equation (Eq. qrpam) under the same pairing-type approximation for the two-body transition amplitude.
- The TDHFB equations are expressed in matrix form as \(i\hbar\dot{\cal R}=[{\cal H},{\cal R}]\) (Eq. TDHFB), and the HFB stationary condition is \([{\cal H}^0,{\cal R}^0]=0\).

## Context

This work clarifies the formal relationship between the TDDM+STDDM scheme, previously applied to nuclear structure problems by Tohyama (2002) and Tohyama, Takahara, and Schuck (2001), and the standard HFB+QRPA framework used extensively in nuclear structure studies (Ring and Schuck, 1980; Matsuo, 2001; Khan et al., 2002; Yamagami, 2002). The result shows that HFB+QRPA provides a subset of TDDM+STDDM solutions where the two-body correlations are of separable form, enabling future work to assess which physical effects are captured only when the full non-separable two-body correlations of TDDM are included.
