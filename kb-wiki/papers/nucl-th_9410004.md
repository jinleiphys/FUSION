# Fine Structure Discussion of Parity-Nonconserving Neutron Scattering at Epithermal Energies

> The large magnitude and the sign correlation effect in the parity non-conserving resonant scattering of epithermal neutrons from $^{232}$Th is discussed in terms of a non-collective $2p-1h$ local doorway model. General conclusions are drawn as to the probability of finding large parity violation effects in other regions of the periodic table.

## Key claim

The large parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry and sign correlation observed in epithermal neutron scattering from \(^{232}\)Th can be explained by a single, non-collective, statistical \(2p-1h\) local doorway state in the compound nucleus \(^{233}\)Th, rather than by a collective \(O^{-}\) doorway or an unrealistically large parity-violating matrix element. However, the model predicts that such a phenomenon is a statistical accident, with a probability of only about \(1/(1+f^2)\) (where \(f \approx 4\) for Th), and is therefore not a global feature expected across the periodic table.

## Method

The authors model the parity-nonconserving (PNC) neutron scattering as proceeding through a single, dominant local \(p\)-wave \(2p-1h\) doorway state in the compound nucleus, which couples weakly to a nearby \(s\)-wave doorway via a parity-violating matrix element. They use perturbation theory to derive the average longitudinal asymmetry \(\langle P \rangle\) in terms of the ratio of reduced widths \(\gamma^{(0)}_{Ds}/\gamma^{(0)}_{Dp}\), the weak matrix element \(\bar M^{W}\), the energy spacing \(\Delta E\) between doorways, and the factor \(kR\). The fluctuation (variance) of \(P\) is then analyzed by including contributions from a second, less dominant nearby doorway \(D'\).

## Key numbers

*   The average longitudinal asymmetry for \(^{232}\)Th is \(\langle P \rangle_{q} = 0.08 \pm 0.06\).
*   The density of \(2p-1h\) doorway states in \(^{233}\)Th at excitation energy \(E^{*}=6\) MeV and spin \(J=1/2\) is \(\rho_{2p-1h} \simeq 34\) MeV\(^{-1}\), giving an average spacing \(D_{2p-1h} \simeq 30\) keV.
*   The characteristic energy distance between the \(p\)-wave and \(s\)-wave doorways is estimated as \(\Delta E \sim 50\) keV.
*   To reproduce the observed \(\langle P \rangle = 0.08\) with \(\bar M^{W} \sim 1.0\) eV and \(kR \sim 10^{-3}\) (for \(E_n \sim 1\) eV), the model requires the ratio of reduced widths \(\left| \gamma^{(0)}_{Ds} / \gamma^{(0)}_{Dp} \right| \simeq 4\).
*   The variance \(v\) of the asymmetry \(P\) is found from the TRIPLE data to be about unity.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of the TRIPLE collaboration data (Frankle et al., 1991; Bowman et al., 1993) on parity violation in \(^{232}\)Th, which showed a sign correlation in the longitudinal asymmetry that contradicts the predictions of the statistical theory of compound-nucleus reactions. It contrasts with the collective \(O^{-}\) doorway model proposed by Auerbach (1992) and Auerbach and Bowman (1992), instead proposing a statistical, non-collective \(2p-1h\) doorway. The analysis enables a probabilistic prediction for the occurrence of large PNC effects with sign correlation in other nuclei, suggesting that the phenomenon is a statistical fluctuation rather than a systematic feature, and points to the mass region around \(A \sim 170\) as a potentially more favorable but experimentally difficult case.
