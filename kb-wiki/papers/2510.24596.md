# Low-lying baryon resonances from lattice QCD

> Calculating the properties of baryon resonances from quantum chromodynamics requires evaluating the temporal correlations between hadronic operators using integrations over field configurations weighted by a phase associated with the action. By formulating quantum chromodynamics on a space-time lattice in imaginary time, such integrations can be carried out non-perturbatively using a Markov-chain Monte Carlo method with importance sampling. The energies of stationary states in the finite volume of the lattice can be extracted from the temporal correlations. A quantization condition involving the scattering $K$-matrix and a complicated ``box matrix'' also yields a finite-volume energy spectrum. By appropriately parametrizing the scattering $K$-matrix, the best-fit values of the $K$-matrix parameters are those that produce a finite-volume spectrum which most closely matches that obtained from the Monte Carlo computations. Results for the $Δ$ resonance are presented, and a study of scattering for energies near the $Λ(1405)$ resonance is outlined, showing a two pole structure. The prospects for applying this methodology to the Roper resonance are discussed.

# Low-lying baryon resonances from lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can now determine the masses and decay widths of unstable hadronic resonances such as the Δ and Λ(1405) by combining Markov-chain Monte Carlo evaluations of finite-volume energy levels with parametrized models of scattering amplitudes inserted into a quantization condition, though the results depend on the specific parametrization chosen (e.g., effective range expansion) and are subject to systematic errors from discretization, finite volume, and unphysically large quark masses.

## Method

The authors evaluate an N×N Hermitian matrix of temporal correlation functions C_{ij}(t) using Laplacian Heaviside (LapH) quark-field smearing and stout link smearing on a space-time lattice, with quark propagators contracted from carefully designed single-hadron and multi-hadron operators that include covariantly displaced smeared quark fields. Stationary-state energies are extracted from the correlation matrix using a variational method that involves diagonalizing C(τ₀)^{-1/2}C(t)C(τ₀)^{-1/2} with eigenvector pinning, then two-exponential fits to the diagonal rotated correlators. The finite-volume energies are related to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes through a quantization condition involving the real, symmetric K-matrix and a box matrix B^{(P)}; the K-matrix is parametrized using the effective range expansion (ERE), and best-fit parameters are determined by matching the resulting finite-volume spectrum to the lattice QCD energies.

## Key numbers

- For the Δ resonance on the CLS D200 ensemble (m_π ~ 200 MeV, a ~ 0.065 fm, 64³×128 lattice): m_Δ/m_π = 6.290(18), g_{Δ,BW} = 14.7(7), m_π a₀^{3/2} = -0.2735(81), m_π a₀^{1/2} = 0.142(22)
- For the Δ resonance at the physical point (a = 0.08 fm): M_R = 1269(39)_{Stat.}(45)_{Total} MeV, Γ_R = 144(169)_{Stat.}(181)_{Total} MeV
- The D200 ensemble uses m_π ~ 200 MeV and m_K ~ 480 MeV with lattice spacing a ~ 0.065 fm

## Context

This work extends the Luscher quantization condition framework (Luscher 1990, 1991) and its multi-channel generalizations (Briceno 2014) to baryon resonances, building on earlier lattice QCD methodology improvements for multi-hadron operators. The Δ resonance results on the D200 ensemble disagree with leading-order chiral perturbation theory (LO χPT) in both the magnitude ordering of scattering lengths and the absolute values. The Λ(1405) study represents the first lattice QCD coupled-channel analysis of this system including both single-hadron and all needed two-hadron operators, revealing a two-pole structure in the Σπ → Σπ transition amplitude. The methodology enables future study of the Roper resonance, the lightest excitation of the proton (Roper 1964).
