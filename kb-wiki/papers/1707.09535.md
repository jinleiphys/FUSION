# Revisiting the Equation of State of Hybrid Stars in the Dyson-Schwinger Equation Approach to QCD

> We investigate the equation of state(EoS) and the effect of the hadron-quark phase transition of strong interaction matter in compact stars. The hadron matter is described with the relativistic mean field theory,and the quark matter is described with the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of QCD. The complete EoS of the hybrid star matter is constructed with not only the Gibbs construction but also the 3-window interpolation. The mass-radius relation of hybrid stars is also investigated. We find that, although the EoSs of both the hadron matter with hyperon and $Δ$-baryon and the quark matter are generally softer than that of the nucleon matter, the 3-window interpolation construction may provide an EoS stiff enough for a hybrid star with mass exceeding 2$M_{\odot}^{}$ and, in turn, solve the so called "hyperon puzzle".

## Key claim
The 3-window interpolation construction for the hadron-quark phase transition can produce an equation of state stiff enough to support a hybrid star with mass exceeding 2 solar masses, even though both the hadron matter (including hyperons and Delta-baryons) and the quark matter individually yield softer equations of state than pure nucleon matter. This provides a potential resolution to the "hyperon puzzle" without requiring modifications to the hadronic interactions.

## Method
The hadron matter was described using the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the TW-99 parameterization, including the baryon octet (p, n, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and Delta-resonance states, with leptons (electron and muon) treated as free fermions. The quark matter was described using the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to QCD with a Gaussian-type effective interaction and rainbow approximation for the quark-gluon vertex, incorporating a chemical-potential-dependent interaction strength controlled by a parameter alpha. The complete equation of state for hybrid star matter was constructed using both the Gibbs construction (assuming a mixed phase with coexisting hadron and quark matter) and the 3-window interpolation (which assumes a smooth crossover transition region between hadron and quark phases).

## Key numbers
- The saturation nuclear matter density is 0.153 fm^-3.
- The TW-99 model parameters include meson masses: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 763 MeV; and coupling constants at saturation: g_sigmaN = 10.72854, g_omegaN = 13.29015, g_rhoN = 7.32196.
- The DSE model parameters: omega = 0.5 GeV, D = 1.0 GeV^2; the bag constant B_DS = 90 MeV fm^-3.
- The strange quark current mass is taken as 115 MeV (fitted to kaon mass in vacuum).
- The hyperon coupling ratios relative to nucleons: chi_sigma = 0.7, chi_omega = chi_rho = 0.783; Delta-baryon couplings are set to 1.0.
- The 3-window interpolation can yield hybrid stars with mass exceeding 2 solar masses (2 M_sun).

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Chen et al. (2011, 2012, 2015, 2016) that used only the Gibbs construction for hybrid star matter with the DSE approach, where the hadron-quark phase transition could only occur for pure nucleon matter (not including hyperons). The paper addresses the "hyperon puzzle" identified by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), where observations of ~2 solar mass neutron stars contradict the softening of the equation of state caused by hyperon appearance. The 3-window interpolation method, proposed by Masuda et al. (2013, 2016) and Kojo et al. (2015, 2016), is applied here as an alternative to the Gibbs construction to enable a stiff enough equation of state even with hyperons and Delta-baryons present. This enables future calculations of hybrid star properties that can be compared with astronomical observations of massive compact stars.
