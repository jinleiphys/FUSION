# Evolution of two-neutrons configuration from 11Li to 13Li

> In this work we investigate the two-neutron decay of 13Li and of the excited states of 11Li populated via one-proton removal from 14Be and 12Be, respectively. A phenomenological model is used to describe the decay of 11Li and 13Li. While the first one displays important sequential components, the second one appears dominated by the direct two-neutron decay. A microscopic three-body model is used to extract information on the spatial configuration of the emitted neutrons before the decay and shows that the average distance between the neutrons increases going from 11Li to 13Li.

## Key claim

The two-neutron decay of 13Li is dominated by direct (simultaneous) emission, whereas the decay of excited states of 11Li proceeds primarily through sequential decay via intermediate states in 10Li; the average distance between the two emitted neutrons increases from 11Li to 13Li, indicating a more dilute spatial configuration in the heavier isotope.

## Method

The experiment used one-proton knockout reactions from 14Be and 12Be beams at 265 and 340 MeV/nucleon, respectively, on a liquid hydrogen target at the RIKEN RIBF facility, to populate 13Li and excited states of 11Li. Decay products (Li fragments and neutrons) were detected and tracked using the SAMURAI spectrometer and the NEBULA neutron detector array, with cross-talk rejection applied to identify genuine two-neutron events. Three-body and two-body relative energy spectra were constructed from the measured momenta and interpreted using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz phenomenological model for final-state interactions, and a microscopic three-body model based on hyperspherical coordinates was used to extract the spatial configuration of the emitted neutrons.

## Key numbers

- 13Li ground-state resonance energy: 0.16(1) MeV (from three-body relative energy spectrum fit)
- 13Li additional resonant structures at 0.45(6) MeV, 1.47 MeV (fixed from Aksyutina 2008), and 2.8(2) MeV
- 12Li resonances observed in sequential decay of 13Li at 0.15(+0.05/-0.01) MeV and 0.50(+0.1/-0.01) MeV
- 11Li resonant structures at 0.08(2) MeV, 0.39(6) MeV, and 2.1(3) MeV
- 10Li intermediate states in 11Li decay: virtual state with scattering length a_s = -30 fm, and resonances at 0.30(6) MeV, 0.62(10) MeV, and 1.1(1) MeV
- RMS neutron-neutron distance from three-body model: 6.5 fm for 11Li ground state, 8.1 fm for 13Li ground state
- Total observed events after cross-talk rejection: 8275 13Li decays and 7700 10Li decays
- Two-neutron detection efficiency reaches maximum of nearly 7% at around 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dineutron correlations in 11Li using transfer reactions (Tanihata 2008), knockout reactions (Simon 2007), Coulomb breakup (Nakamura 2006, Marques 2000), and quasi-free scattering (Kubota 2020), and addresses the discrepancy between earlier measurements of 13Li spectroscopy by Aksyutina (2008) and Kohley (2013). The results enable improved theoretical descriptions of three-body decay dynamics and encourage further investigations of the partial-wave composition of the relative energy spectrum and the dependence of observed properties on the reaction mechanism.
