# Semi-microscopic description of the double backbending in some deformed even-even rare earth nuclei

> A semi-microscopic model to study the neutron and proton induced backbending phenomena in some deformed even-even nuclei from the rare earth region, is proposed. The space of particle-core states is defined by the angular momentum projection of a quadrupole deformed product state. The backbending phenomena are described by mixing four rotational bands, defined by a set of angular momentum projected states, and a model Hamiltonian describing a set of paired particles moving in a deformed mean field and interacting with a phenomenological deformed core. The ground band corresponds to the configuration where all particles are paired while the other rotational bands are built on one neutron or/and one proton broken pair. Four rare earth even-even nuclei which present the second anomaly in the observed moments of inertia are successfully treated within the proposed model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a semi-microscopic model that successfully describes the double backbending phenomenon in four rare earth even-even nuclei by mixing four rotational bands, but the model restricts the single-particle space to only the neutron $6i_{13/2}$ and proton $5h_{11/2}$ intruder orbitals and uses a linear deformation dependence for single-particle energies, which the authors acknowledge is an approximation.

## Method

The authors construct a particle-core model where four rotational bands (ground band, one-neutron broken pair band, one-proton broken pair band, and a band with both a neutron and a proton broken pair) are defined by angular momentum projection from quadrupole deformed product states. The model Hamiltonian includes a deformed core described by a quadrupole coherent state, single-particle terms for neutrons and protons in intruder orbitals with pairing interactions, and particle-core couplings via quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin interactions. The four bands are mixed by first orthogonalizing the projected basis and then diagonalizing the Hamiltonian, with the lowest eigenvalues defining the yrast band.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semi-microscopic model (Budaca and Raduta, 2012) which described only the first backbending using two-band mixing, to now include the second backbending by mixing four rotational bands. The model treats the first backbending as caused by a neutron broken pair from the $6i_{13/2}$ orbital and the second backbending as caused by a proton broken pair from the $5h_{11/2}$ orbital, consistent with the interpretation of Faessler (1974) and the CHFB calculations of Bengtsson (1980). The approach enables a fully quantal description of double backbending phenomena in $^{156}$Er, $^{158}$Er, $^{160}$Yb, and $^{162}$Hf, and can be extended to analyze $E2$ transition probabilities along the yrast band.
