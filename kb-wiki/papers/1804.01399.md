# Model independent radiative corrections to elastic deuteron-electron scattering

> The differential cross section for elastic scattering of deuterons on electrons at rest is calculated taking into account the QED radiative corrections to the leptonic part of interaction. These model-independent radiative corrections arise due to emission of the virtual and real soft and hard photons as well as to vacuum polarization. We consider an experimental setup where both final particles are recorded in coincidence and their energies are determined within some uncertainties. The kinematics, the cross section, and the radiative corrections are calculated and numerical results are presented.

## Key claim

The paper calculates model-independent QED radiative corrections to elastic deuteron-electron scattering in inverse kinematics, but the results are conditional on an experimental setup where both final particles are detected in coincidence and their energies are measured within specified uncertainties; the model-dependent corrections from photon emission by the deuteron vertex are explicitly excluded and left for future work.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for elastic deuteron-electron scattering at rest in the one-photon-exchange (Born) approximation, then add QED radiative corrections of order α from virtual photon emission, vacuum polarization, and real soft and hard photon emission from the electron vertex only. The hard photon contribution is integrated over the photon phase space subject to experimental energy resolution cuts (ΔE₂ and Δε₂), with the soft/hard separation scale ω̄ set by the detector uncertainties; the infrared-divergent parts are combined analytically and the regular parts integrated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The maximum four-momentum transfer squared for deuteron scattering on electrons at rest is given by (Q²)ₘₐₓ = 4m²|p|²/(M² + 2mE + m²), where m is the electron mass and M the deuteron mass.
- The maximum recoil electron energy is ε₂ₘₐₓ = m[2E(E+m) + m² - M²]/(M² + 2mE + m²), which is proportional to the electron mass.
- The maximum scattering angle for the deuteron is determined by sin θₕ,ₘₐₓ = m/Mₕ, where Mₕ is the hadron mass.
- The deuteron form factor normalizations are: G_C(0) = 1, G_M(0) = (M/m_N)μ_d with μ_d = 0.857, and G_Q(0) = M²Q_d with Q_d = 0.2857 fm².
- For numerical calculations, the experimental energy uncertainty is set to ΔE = 0.02(E - ε₂).
- The vacuum polarization correction for Q² ≪ m² is δ^(vac) = (2α/15π)(Q²/m²), and for Q² ≫ m² is δ^(vac) = (2α/3π)[-5/3 + ln(Q²/m²)].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of model-independent radiative corrections for elastic proton-electron scattering (Gakh et al., 2017) to the deuteron case, motivated by the proton radius puzzle from muonic hydrogen experiments (Antognini et al., 2013) and the recent muonic deuterium result from the CREMA collaboration (Pohl et al., 2016) giving r_d(μd) = 2.1256(8) fm, which is more than 7σ smaller than the CODATA-2010 value. The inverse kinematics approach allows access to very small four-momentum transfer where the deuteron can be treated as structureless, enabling a precise determination of the deuteron charge radius; the model-dependent corrections from deuteron vertex photon emission remain to be calculated.
