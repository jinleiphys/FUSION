# Nuclear coherent population transfer to the $^{229m}$Th isomer using x-ray pulses

> Population of the 8 eV $^{229m}$Th isomer via the second nuclear excited state at 29.19 keV by means of coherent x-ray pulses is investigated theoretically. We focus on two nuclear coherent population transfer schemes using partially overlapping x-ray pulses known from quantum optics: stimulated Raman adiabatic passage (STIRAP), and successive $π$ pulses. Numerical results are presented for three possible experimental setups. Our results identify the Gamma Factory as the most promising scenario, where two ultraviolet pulses combined with relativistically accelerated ions deliver the required intensities for efficient isomer population. Our simulations require knowledge of the in-band and cross-band nuclear transition probabilities. We give theoretically predicted values for the latter and discuss them in the context of recent experiments.

# Nuclear coherent population transfer to the $^{229m}$Th isomer using x-ray pulses

## Key claim

Coherent population transfer from the ground state to the 8 eV isomeric state of $^{229}$Th via the 29.19 keV second excited state is theoretically feasible using stimulated Raman adiabatic passage (STIRAP) or successive pi pulses, but only if sufficient x-ray intensities can be achieved; the Gamma Factory scenario with relativistically accelerated ions and ultraviolet pulses is identified as the most promising, while the required nuclear transition probabilities for the cross-band and in-band transitions are predicted theoretically and show only partial consistency with available experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the three-level Lambda system (ground state, isomeric state at 8 eV, and second excited state at 29.19 keV) using a density matrix approach and solve the master equation to compute population transfer. They consider two coherent transfer schemes: STIRAP (Stokes pulse preceding pump pulse) and successive pi pulses (pump pulse first), both adapted from quantum optics to nuclear x-ray transitions. Three experimental scenarios are evaluated: (1) relativistic $^{229}$Th ions in the Gamma Factory with UV laser fields boosted in the ion rest frame, (2) x-ray free electron laser (XFEL) pulses with moderately relativistic ions, and (3) a solid-state target with cavity-based x-ray sources such as an XFEL oscillator. The nuclear reduced transition probabilities $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ for both transitions are calculated using a collective quadrupole-octupole model coupled to a deformed shell model with Coriolis mixing and pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- Isomeric state energy: $E_{\text{iso}} = 8.28 \pm 0.17$ eV (Seiferle 2019), $8.30 \pm 0.92$ eV (Yamaguchi 2019), or $8.10 \pm 0.17$ eV (Sikorsky 2020)
- Second excited state energy: 29.19 keV
- Predicted $B(M1)$ for cross-band transition $|3\rangle \rightarrow |1\rangle$: ranges from 0.0012 W.u. to 0.0050 W.u. depending on quenching factors
- Predicted $B(M1)$ for in-band transition $|3\rangle \rightarrow |2\rangle$: ranges from 0.0332 W.u. to 0.0648 W.u. depending on quenching factors
- Predicted $B(E2)$ for cross-band transition $|3\rangle \rightarrow |1\rangle$: 27.11 W.u. (one parameter set) to 39.49 W.u. (another set)
- Predicted $B(E2)$ for in-band transition $|3\rangle \rightarrow |2\rangle$: 234.86 W.u. to 239.18 W.u.
- Internal conversion coefficients used: $\alpha(M1) = 151.07$ and $\alpha(E2) = 4401.61$ for both transitions
- Isomer population probability from alpha decay of $^{233}$U: approximately 2 percent

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of nuclear STIRAP for high-energy gamma-ray transitions (Liao 2011, Liao 2013) to the unique low-energy $^{229}$Th system, which is the only known nuclear isomer accessible by vacuum ultraviolet lasers and a candidate for a nuclear clock (Peik 2003, Campbell 2012). The nuclear structure calculations build on the authors' previously developed collective quadrupole-octupole model with Coriolis mixing (Minkov and Palffy 2017, 2019, 2021) that successfully described the isomer decay properties. The results enable experimental planning for coherent isomer population at the proposed Gamma Factory facility at CERN (Krasny 2015, Budker 2021) and provide theoretically predicted transition probabilities that can be tested against ongoing measurements (Masuda, Sikorsky 2020).
