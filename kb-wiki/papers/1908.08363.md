# Renormalization group improved pressure for cold and dense QCD

> We apply the renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT)to evaluate the QCD (matter) pressure at the two-loop level considering three flavors of massless quarks in a dense and cold medium. Already at leading order ($α_s^0$), which builds on the simple one loop (RG resummed) term, our technique provides a non-trivial non-perturbative approximation which is completely renormalization group invariant. At the next-to-leading order the comparison between the RGOPT and the pQCD predictions shows that the former method provides results which are in better agreement with the state-of-the-art $higher \, order$ perturbative results, which include a contribution of order $α_s^3 \ln^2 α_s$. At the same time one also observes that the RGOPT predictions are less sensitive to variations of the arbitrary $\bar{\rm MS}$ renormalization scale than those obtained with pQCD. These results indicate that the RGOPT provides an efficient resummation scheme which may be considered as an alternative to lattice simulations at high baryonic densities.

## Key claim

The renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) provides a non-perturbative approximation for the QCD matter pressure at cold and dense conditions that is completely renormalization group invariant already at leading order, and at next-to-leading order yields results in better agreement with higher-order perturbative calculations (up to order α_s^3 ln^2 α_s) while showing significantly less sensitivity to variations of the MSbar renormalization scale than standard perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors applied the RGOPT to evaluate the QCD pressure at two-loop level for three flavors of massless quarks at zero temperature and finite chemical potential. The method deforms the QCD Lagrangian by introducing a Gaussian interpolating mass term and rescaling the coupling, with the interpolation exponent fixed by requiring the reduced renormalization group equation to hold. The resulting pressure expressions were then optimized using the principle of minimal sensitivity to determine a non-perturbative dressed mass, and the results were compared with standard perturbative QCD predictions including the recently computed N3LO leading-logarithmic contribution.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop RGOPT vacuum mass at μ=0 is given by m̄(μ=0) = Λ_MSbar √e, where Λ_MSbar = M e^{-1/(2g b_0)} is the one-loop QCD scale
- For N_f=3, the s_1 subtraction coefficient modifies the optimization equation such that the term -1/2 in the c-function is replaced by -1/2 - 8π² s_1 = 11/84
- The QCD beta function coefficients used are b_0 = (1/(4π)²)(11 - 2N_f/3) and b_1 = (1/(4π)^4)(102 - 38N_f/3)
- The quark mass anomalous dimension coefficients are γ_0 = 1/(2π²) and γ_1^MSbar = (1/(8(2π)^4))(202/3 - 20N_f/9)

## Context

This work extends the RGOPT method, previously applied to the Gross-Neveu model, scalar φ⁴ theory, and QCD at zero temperature and density (Kneur 2018, Kneur 2019), to cold and dense QCD with finite chemical potential. It directly compares with the N3LO perturbative QCD results of Gorda (2018) which determined the α_s³ ln² α_s coefficient, and addresses the large renormalization scale dependence observed in standard pQCD calculations (Kurkela 2010) and HTLpt resummations (Haque 2014) at finite densities. The results suggest RGOPT could serve as an alternative to lattice simulations at high baryonic densities where the sign problem prevents reliable lattice calculations.
