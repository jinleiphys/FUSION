# Two-Pion Exchange Nucleon-Nucleon Potential: Model Independent Features

> A chiral pion-nucleon amplitude supplemented by the HJS subthreshold coefficients is used to calculate the the long range part of the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential. In our expressions the HJS coefficients factor out, allowing a clear identification of the origin of the various contributions. A discussion of the configuration space behaviour of the loop integrals that determine the potential is presented, with emphasis on cancellations associated with chiral symmetry. The profile function for the scalar-isoscalar component of the potential is produced and shown to disagree with those of several semi-phenomenological potentials.

# Two-Pion Exchange Nucleon-Nucleon Potential: Model Independent Features

## Key claim

The long-range part of the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential can be determined in a model-independent way using chiral pion-nucleon amplitudes supplemented by HJS subthreshold coefficients, but only for distances beyond approximately 2.5 fm where the integrals with m=n=0 dominate; the resulting scalar-isoscalar profile function disagrees with those of several semi-phenomenological potentials.

## Method

The authors evaluated the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential by computing on-shell NN scattering amplitudes from two-pion exchange diagrams, subtracting the iterated one-pion exchange potential, and expressing the result in configuration space via Fourier transforms. The intermediate pion-nucleon amplitude was decomposed into chiral contributions from the pure pion-nucleon sector plus a remainder expanded in powers of the variables ν/μ and θ using the HJS subthreshold coefficients, with the HJS coefficients factored out to allow clear identification of each contribution's origin. Loop integrals (bubble and triangle diagrams) were evaluated using dimensional regularization, and the potential was separated into central, spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor components.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless HJS coefficient α⁺₀₀ = 3.676 ± 0.138, which dominates the leading scalar-isoscalar central potential at large distances
- The ratio (g²μ/m)/α⁺₀₀ ≈ 8, indicating the dominance of diagrams mixing nucleon and HJS amplitudes over pure HJS contributions
- The asymptotic form of the leading bubble integral S_B(0,0) is given by (1/(4π)²) × 2√π × e^(-2x)/x^(5/2) × (1 + 3/(16x) - 15/(512x²) + ...), accurate to 1% up to 1.2 fm
- The leading triangle integral S_T(0,0) equals -S_B(0,0) in the limit (μ/m) → 0
- In the pure πN sector, as (μ/m) → 0, the box, crossed, triangle, and bubble contributions satisfy: □ = 0.5((!)) , ⋈ = 0.5((!)) , and △ = -((!))

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Cottingham and Vinh Mau (1960s) that led to the Paris potential (Cottingham 1973, Lacombe 1980), where the intermediate-range force is obtained from empirical πN information via dispersion relations. It also builds on the chiral symmetry approach of Brown and Durso (1971) and more recent chiral TPEP calculations by Ordóñez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994, 1996), who included delta degrees of freedom but did not closely reproduce experimental πN subthreshold behavior. The results enable model-independent predictions for NN interactions at large distances (j ≥ 2) that can be tested against phase shifts, and demonstrate that the scalar-isoscalar component of the TPEP differs from those in semi-phenomenological potentials such as the Paris potential.
