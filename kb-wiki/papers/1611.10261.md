# Novel Role of Superfluidity in Low-Energy Nuclear Reactions

> We demonstrate, within symmetry unrestricted time-dependent density functional theory, the existence of new effects in low-energy nuclear reactions which originate from superfluidity. The dynamics of the pairing field induces solitonic excitations in the colliding nuclear systems, leading to qualitative changes in the reaction dynamics. The solitonic excitation prevents collective energy dissipation and effectively suppresses fusion cross section. We demonstrate how the variations of the total kinetic energy of the fragments can be traced back to the energy stored in the superfluid junction of colliding nuclei. Both contact time and scattering angle in non-central collisions are significantly affected. The modification of the fusion cross section and possibilities for its experimental detection are discussed.

## Key claim
The dynamics of the pairing field in colliding superfluid nuclei creates solitonic excitations that suppress energy dissipation and fusion, with the effect depending on the relative phase difference between the pairing fields of the two nuclei; the abstract does not specify that the effect is strongest near Δφ = π and that the spin-orbit term was omitted in the calculations.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) simulations, which are equivalent to time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory, on a 3D spatial lattice with periodic boundary conditions and no symmetry restrictions. They used the FaNDF⁰ energy density functional without the spin-orbit term, with a lattice spacing of 1.25 fm, and simulated head-on and non-central collisions of ²⁴⁰Pu+²⁴⁰Pu and ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr systems at energies around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁴⁰Pu+²⁴⁰Pu head-on collisions, the contact time is about 550–600 fm/c depending on the phase difference.
- The energy stored in the superfluid junction can vary by several tens of MeV depending on the phase difference, as estimated from the Ginzburg-Landau approach.
- For ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr head-on collisions, the fusion threshold energy B varies with phase difference, with the barrier height V_Bass ≈ 192 MeV; the phase difference prevents fusion for energies up to 15% above the barrier.
- The predicted extra energy for fusion due to pairing effects is about E_extra ≈ 10 MeV, averaged over phase differences.
- The amount of nucleon transfer (Josephson current) does not exceed 1.5 neutrons and 0.5 protons during the collision.
- The ratio of the pairing field magnitude to the Fermi energy does not exceed 5%.

## Context
This work extends the study of pairing field dynamics in nuclear reactions beyond static treatments, building on prior work by Bertsch (1980), Barranco (1990), and Bertsch (1994) on the role of superfluidity in fission, and on recent TDSLDA simulations by the same group (PRL 2016) showing that dynamic pairing excitations affect fission timescales. The results contradict the common assumption that pairing field dynamics produce only small corrections, and they suggest that the fusion hindrance observed in experiments on Zr+Sn systems by Liang (2012) may be explained by pairing effects rather than the extra-push model of Swiatecki (1982). The work enables future experimental searches for pairing-induced fusion hindrance in symmetric collisions of Zr isotopes.
