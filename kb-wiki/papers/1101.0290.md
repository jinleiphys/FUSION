# A study of the correlations between jet quenching observables at RHIC

> Focusing on four types of correlation plots, $R_{\rm AA}$ vs. $v_2$, $R_{\rm AA}$ vs. $I_{\rm AA}$, $I_{\rm AA}$ vs. $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$ and $v_2$ vs.\ $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$, we demonstrate how the centrality dependence of \emph{correlations} between multiple jet quenching observables provide valuable insight into the energy loss mechanism in a quark-gluon plasma. In particular we find that a qualitative energy loss model gives a good description of $R_{\rm AA}$ vs.\ $v_2$ only when we take $ΔE\sim l^3$ and a medium geometry generated by a model of the Color Glass Condensate. This same $ΔE\sim l^3$ model also qualitatively describes the trigger $p_T$ dependence of $R_{\rm AA}$ vs.\ $I_{\rm AA}$ data and makes novel predictions for the centrality dependence for this $R_{\rm AA}$ vs.\ $I_{\rm AA}$ correlation. Current data suggests, albeit with extremely large uncertainty, that $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}\gg v_2$, a correlation that is difficult to reproduce in current energy loss models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that studying the centrality dependence of correlations between multiple jet quenching observables (rather than each observable individually) provides novel insight into the energy loss mechanism, but the conclusions are hedged by the use of a simplified "jet absorption model" and the statement that current data for the $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$ vs. $I_{\rm AA}$ correlation "suggests, albeit with extremely large uncertainty, that $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}\gg v_2$, a correlation that is difficult to reproduce in current energy loss models."

## Method

The authors used a phenomenological jet absorption model where the fractional energy loss $\epsilon$ of a high-$p_T$ parton is proportional to a line integral $I$ through the medium, $\epsilon = \tilde{\kappa} I$, with two path-length dependencies: $\Delta E \sim l^2$ (pQCD-like) and $\Delta E \sim l^3$ (AdS/CFT-like). The medium density was modeled using either a Glauber participant density profile or a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) gluon density profile, with event-by-event fluctuations included via a medium rotation procedure. The single free parameter $\kappa$ was tuned to reproduce $R_{\rm AA}\sim0.18$ for 0-5% most central $\pi^0$ at RHIC, and then the model predicted the centrality dependence of $R_{\rm AA}$, $v_2$, $I_{\rm AA}$, and $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$ for four combinations of path-length dependence and geometry.

## Key numbers

*   $R_{\rm AA}$ shows an almost $p_T$-independent factor of 5 suppression in central collisions for $p_T>4$ GeV/$c$.
*   The $\kappa$ values for the four model cases (tuned to $R_{\rm AA}\sim0.18$ for 0-5% central $\pi^0$) are: $l^2$ Glauber $\kappa=0.147$, $l^2$ CGC $\kappa=0.076$, $l^3$ Glauber $\kappa=0.082$, $l^3$ CGC $\kappa=0.046$.
*   The average matter density $\langle\rho_{\rm medium}\rangle$ in the 0-5% centrality bin is 2.96 for Glauber geometry and 6.40 for CGC geometry.
*   For the away-side spectrum, increasing the trigger momentum from 4-5 GeV/$c$ to 9-12 GeV/$c$ reduces the power-law index $n_a$ from 5.1 to 3.6, leading to an effective $\kappa_{\rm away}$ that is a factor of 2 to 4 smaller than $\kappa$.
*   The $l^3$ model with CGC medium describes the $R_{\rm AA}$ vs. $v_2$ data well, but the $l^3$ models systematically under-predict the $I_{\rm AA}$ data by about 2-3 standard deviations.
*   The $l^3$ model under-predicts the PHENIX data for $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$ by about one standard deviation, where the uncertainty is currently very large.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Horowitz (2005) which examined the centrality dependence of $R_{\rm AA}$ vs. $v_2$, by proposing three additional correlation plots ($R_{\rm AA}$ vs. $I_{\rm AA}$, $I_{\rm AA}$ vs. $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$, and $v_2$ vs. $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$) to constrain energy loss models. It contradicts the ability of pQCD-based models (e.g., the ACHNS model of Armesto (2009) and the ZOWW model of Zhang (2009)) to simultaneously describe $R_{\rm AA}$ and $I_{\rm AA}$ data, and it highlights the challenge of reproducing the large $v_2^{I_{\rm AA}}$ value seen in PHENIX data. The work enables future experimental studies at RHIC and the LHC to use these correlation plots to disentangle the path-length dependence of energy loss from collision geometry and parton spectra.
