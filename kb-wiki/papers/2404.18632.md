# Mass Spectra of $Ξ$ and $Ω$ Baryons using hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> Hadron spectroscopy is an important tool towards the study of internal quark dynamics in a composite system. The present article focuses on the study of resonance spectra of strange baryons with S=-2, -3. The non-relativistic approach utilizes screened potential as hypercentral one to obtain masses. The spin-dependent part for all possible hyperfine states has been incorporated.

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) with a screened potential, using a fixed screening parameter of 0.3, reproduces the known low-lying mass spectra of Ξ and Ω baryons within about 50 MeV of experimental values, but the screening effect becomes more significant for higher excited states and reduces hyperfine splitting compared to earlier linear potential models.

## Method

The authors solve the hyper-radial Schrödinger equation for a three-quark system using a non-relativistic hCQM with a screened confining potential of the form V⁰(x) = a(1 − e^(−μx))/μ, where the screening parameter μ is set to 0.3. Spin-dependent interactions (spin-orbit, spin-spin, and tensor terms) are incorporated separately to account for hyperfine splitting, and the resulting masses are computed for all possible hyperfine states of Ξ (uss/dss) and Ω (sss) baryons.

## Key numbers

- Ξ(1S) ground state: 1321 MeV (Jᴾ = 1/2⁺), matching the PDG value of 1321 MeV
- Ξ(1S) 3/2⁺ state: 1531 MeV, matching the PDG value of 1532 MeV
- Ξ(2S) 3/2⁺ state: 1971 MeV, differing by 21 MeV from the PDG value of 1950 MeV
- Ξ(1P) 3/2⁻ states: 1869 and 1873 MeV, within about 50 MeV of the PDG value of 1823 MeV
- Ξ(1D) 5/2⁺ state: 2246 MeV, within 4 MeV of the PDG value of 2250 MeV
- Ω(1S) ground state: 1672 MeV (Jᴾ = 3/2⁺), matching the PDG value of 1672 MeV
- Ω(1P) 1/2⁻ state: 1998 MeV, within 14 MeV of the PDG value of 2012 MeV
- Ω(2P) 1/2⁻ state: 2352 MeV, within 28 MeV of the PDG value of 2380 MeV
- Ω(1D) 3/2⁺ state: 2289 MeV, within 39 MeV of the PDG value of 2250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hCQM approach previously applied to heavy quark systems (Zalak 2018, Zalak 2016, Keval 2018, Keval 2020) into the light strange baryon sector, where experimental data remain scarce. The screened potential results are compared to the authors' earlier linear potential studies (CPC 2013, CPC 2015, IJMPA 2015, Akram 2015) and to algebraic models such as the U(7) model (Amiri 2023) and the string-like model of Bijker (2020). The computed spectra provide predictions for unobserved resonances that can guide searches at BESIII and PANDA, and the Regge trajectories offer a tool for assigning spin-parity to future experimental states.
