# Gluon mass scale through the Schwinger mechanism

> It has long been argued that the action of the Schwinger mechanism in the gauge sector of Quantum Chromodynamics leads to the generation of a gluon mass scale. Within this scenario, the analytic structure of the fundamental vertices is modified by the creation of scalar colored excitations with vanishing mass. In the limit of zero momentum transfer, these terms act as massless poles, providing the required conditions for the infrared stabilization of the gluon propagator, and producing a characteristic displacement to the associated Ward identities. In this article we offer an extensive overview of the salient notions and techniques underlying this dynamical picture. We place particular emphasis on recent developments related to the exact renormalization of the mass, the nonlinear nature of the pole equation, and the key role played by the Fredholm alternative theorem.

## Key claim

The Schwinger mechanism in QCD generates a gluon mass scale through the creation of scalar colored excitations with vanishing mass that modify the analytic structure of fundamental vertices, but this mass scale is a gauge- and renormalization-point-dependent reflection of a physical gluon mass gap at the level of Green functions, rather than the physical gluon mass itself.

## Method

The authors review the implementation of the Schwinger mechanism in QCD using the Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDEs) and the PT-BFM (pinch technique–background field method) framework, which provides Abelian (ghost-free) Slavnov-Taylor identities that make the analysis of the gluon propagator more transparent. They derive the gluon mass scale from the residue functions of massless poles in the three-gluon and ghost-gluon vertices, set up a Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for the formation of Schwinger poles, and apply the Fredholm alternative theorem to achieve exact multiplicative renormalization of the mass equation.

## Key numbers

* The gluon mass scale is defined as \(m^2 = \Delta^{-1}(0)\), where \(\Delta(q^2)\) is the scalar form factor of the gluon propagator that saturates at a finite nonvanishing value in the deep infrared.
* The gluon mass scale is expressed as \(m^2 = g^2 I^2\), with \(I := I(0)\) being the form factor of the gluon-scalar transition amplitude at zero momentum.
* SU(3) lattice data for the gluon propagator are renormalized at \(\mu = 4.3\) GeV, while SU(2) data are renormalized at \(\mu = 2.2\) GeV.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing argument by Schwinger (1962) that gauge bosons may acquire mass if their vacuum polarization develops a pole at zero momentum transfer, and builds on the work of Cornwall (1981) on dynamical gluon mass generation in QCD. It synthesizes recent developments by Aguilar, Binosi, Papavassiliou, and collaborators (2006–2024) on the exact renormalization of the mass, the nonlinear nature of the pole equation, and the role of the Fredholm alternative theorem, enabling a self-consistent continuum description of the gluon mass scale that can be compared with lattice QCD simulations.
