# Thermal hadron production in high energy collisions

> It is shown that hadron abundances in high energy e+e-, pp and p{\bar p} collisions, calculated by assuming that particles originate in hadron gas fireballs at thermal and partial chemical equilibrium, are in very good agreement with the data. The freeze-out temperature of the hadron gas fireballs turns out to be nearly constant over a large center of mass energy range and not dependent on the initial colliding system. The only deviation from chemical equilibrium resides in the incomplete strangeness phase space saturation. Preliminary results of an analysis of hadron abundances in S+S and S+Ag heavy ion collisions are presented.

## Key claim

Hadron abundances in high-energy e+e-, pp, and p-pbar collisions can be described by assuming particles originate from hadron gas fireballs in thermal and partial chemical equilibrium, with the freeze-out temperature being nearly constant across a large center-of-mass energy range and independent of the colliding system. The only deviation from full chemical equilibrium is incomplete strangeness phase space saturation, quantified by a parameter gamma_s that is always less than 1.

## Method

The model assumes formation of an arbitrary number of hadron gas fireballs, each at thermal and chemical equilibrium with temperature T, volume V, and conserved quantum numbers (electric charge, baryon number, strangeness, charm, beauty). The partition function is calculated in the canonical formalism of statistical mechanics, summing only over multi-hadronic states with the same quantum numbers as the fireball, with a parameter gamma_s introduced to account for incomplete strangeness chemical equilibrium. The three free parameters (T, V, gamma_s) are determined by fitting calculated multiplicities (including contributions from decays of heavier hadrons) to measured hadron abundances at each center-of-mass energy using chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature in e+e- collisions: 160.3 +/- 1.7 (3.3) MeV at sqrt(s) = 91 GeV; ranges from 163.6 +/- 3.6 MeV at 29 GeV to 169.6 +/- 9.5 MeV at 44 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature in pp collisions: 169.0 +/- 2.1 (3.4) MeV at sqrt(s) = 27.4 GeV; ranges from 159.0 +/- 9.5 MeV at 26.0 GeV to 194.4 +/- 17.3 MeV at 23.6 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature in p-pbar collisions: 175.0 +/- 14.8 MeV at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV; 181.7 +/- 17.7 MeV at 546 GeV; 170.2 +/- 11.8 MeV at 900 GeV
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_s in e+e-: 0.673 +/- 0.020 (0.028) at 91 GeV; ranges from 0.724 +/- 0.045 at 29 GeV to 0.788 +/- 0.045 at 35 GeV
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_s in pp: 0.510 +/- 0.011 (0.025) at 27.4 GeV; ranges from 0.460 +/- 0.067 at 23.6 GeV to 0.570 +/- 0.030 at 26.0 GeV
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_s in p-pbar: 0.537 +/- 0.066 at 200 GeV; 0.557 +/- 0.051 at 546 GeV; 0.578 +/- 0.063 at 900 GeV
- In S+S heavy ion collisions: T = 182.1 +/- 9.0 MeV, gamma_s = 0.732 +/- 0.037, mu_b/T = 1.243 +/- 0.071
- In S+Ag heavy ion collisions: T = 180.0 +/- 3.2 MeV, gamma_s = 0.830 +/- 0.061, mu_b/T = 1.323 +/- 0.069

## Context

This work extends the statistical-thermodynamical approach to hadron production previously developed by Becattini and collaborators (1994-1996) for e+e- collisions to include pp and p-pbar collisions, and presents preliminary results for heavy ion collisions (S+S and S+Ag). The canonical formalism used here differs from the grand-canonical approach employed by other authors (Sollfrank, Munzinger, Satz, Spieles) for heavy ion analyses, though the results become equivalent in the large-volume limit. The finding of a nearly constant freeze-out temperature around 170 MeV across all collision systems supports the idea of a statistical hadronization transition from quark-gluon matter at a critical temperature, and enables predictions of heavy-flavored hadron abundances (charmed and bottomed hadrons) that can be tested against LEP measurements.
