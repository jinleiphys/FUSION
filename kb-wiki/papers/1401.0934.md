# Novel Phenomenology of Parton Distributions from the Drell-Yan Process

> The Drell-Yan massive lepton-pair production in hadronic collisions provides a unique tool complementary to the Deep-Inelastic Scattering for probing the partonic substructures in hadrons. We review key concepts, approximations, and progress for QCD factorization of the Drell-Yan process in terms of collinear or transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions. We present experimental results from recent fixed-target Drell-Yan as well as $W$ and $Z$ boson production at colliders, focussing on the topics of flavor structure of the nucleon sea as well as the extraction of novel Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions via single transverse spin asymmetries and azimuthal lepton angular distribution of the Drell-Yan process. Prospects for future Drell-Yan experiments are also presented.

## Key claim

The Drell-Yan massive lepton-pair production in hadronic collisions provides a unique and complementary tool to Deep-Inelastic Scattering for probing partonic substructures, with the key condition that QCD factorization is rigorously proven only for the leading power contribution in a 1/Q expansion, while factorization breaks down for power-suppressed contributions beyond the first subleading corrections.

## Method

The authors review QCD factorization theorems for the Drell-Yan process in both the collinear and transverse momentum dependent (TMD) frameworks, examining how the cross section for massive lepton-pair production can be systematically organized as a power expansion in 1/Q. They analyze experimental data from fixed-target Drell-Yan experiments at Fermilab (E866/E906) and from W/Z boson production at colliders, focusing on the flavor structure of the nucleon sea and the extraction of Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions through single transverse spin asymmetries and azimuthal lepton angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- Gottfried integral measured by NMC: 0.235 ± 0.026, significantly below the flavor-symmetric prediction of 1/3, indicating that the up and down sea quark distributions in the proton are not equal
- Drell-Yan cross section ratios σ_DY(p+d)/σ_DY(p+p) measured at FNAL E866 as a function of x_2 (momentum fraction of target partons), with data compared to predictions from multiple PDF sets including MRS, GRV98, MRST, CTEQ4M, and CTEQ5M
- The K_factor (σ_Exp/σ_Thy) for the original Drell-Yan formalism was approximately 2, indicating the normalization of the predicted cross section was off by roughly a factor of 2 before QCD corrections were included
- The Drell-Yan process at BNL measured muon pair masses Q = 1-6.7 GeV in p+U collisions, with a shoulder-like structure near Q ~ 3 GeV later explained by J/ψ production

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on Drell-Yan physics from the original proposal by Drell and Yan (1970) and the QCD factorization proofs by Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1989). It builds upon earlier comprehensive reviews by Kenyon (1982), Schochet (1986), Freudenreich (1990), Stirling (1993), Pat (1999), Reimer (2007), and Dutta (2013). The paper enables future Drell-Yan experiments at Fermilab (E906), RHIC, COMPASS at CERN, and the LHC to test the predicted sign change of Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions when measured in Drell-Yan versus semi-inclusive DIS, and to further probe the flavor structure of the nucleon sea and the EMC effect in nuclei.
