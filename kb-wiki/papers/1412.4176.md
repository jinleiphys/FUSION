# Decay and structure of the Hoyle state

> The first $0^+$ resonant state of the $^{12}$C nucleus ${}^{12}$C$(0_2^+)$, so called the Hoyle state, is investigated in a three-$α$-particle (3-$α$) model. A wave function for the photodisintegration reaction of a $^{12}$C bound state to 3-$α$ final states is defined and calculated by the Faddeev three-body formalism, in which three-body bound- and continuum states are treated consistently. From the wave function at the Hoyle state energy, I calculated distributions of outgoing $α$-particles and density distributions at interior region of the Hoyle state. Results show that a process through a two-$α$ resonant state is dominant in the decay and contributions of the rest process are very small, less than 1 \%. There appear some peaks in the interior density distribution corresponding to configurations of an equilateral- and an isosceles triangles. It turns out that these results are obtained independently of the choice of $α$-particle interaction models, when they are made to reproduce the Hoyle state energy.

## Key claim

The Hoyle state decays almost entirely (over 99%) through the sequential decay process via the ${}^8$Be$(0_1^+)$ resonance, with direct decay modes contributing less than 1% of the total events; this result holds independently of the choice of alpha-particle interaction models, provided they reproduce the Hoyle state energy.

## Method

The author calculated the wave function for the E2 photodisintegration reaction of the ${}^{12}$C$(2_1^+)$ state into three alpha particles using the Faddeev three-body formalism in coordinate space, treating three-body bound and continuum states consistently. From this wave function at the Hoyle state energy, the outgoing flux distribution was computed to analyze decay modes, and the interior density distribution was examined to reveal geometric configurations of the three alpha particles.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state energy is calculated as $E[{}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_2^+)] = 0.379177$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.3794 MeV.
- The calculated 3-alpha decay width is $\Gamma_{3\alpha} = 5.8$ eV, compared to the experimental value of 8.3(1.0) eV.
- The sequential decay (SD) contribution accounts for about 99.9% of the total decay events.
- The direct decay with linear chain like configuration (DDL) contributes 0.03% of the total events.
- The direct decay with three alpha particles of equal energy (DDE) contributes 0.005% of the total events.
- The interior density distribution has three peaks: peak A at $(x, y) \sim (2.5\ \mathrm{fm}, 2.2\ \mathrm{fm})$, peak B at $(3.3\ \mathrm{fm}, 4.2\ \mathrm{fm})$, and peak C at $(5.3\ \mathrm{fm}, 1.9\ \mathrm{fm})$.
- The equilateral triangle configuration (peak A) has a probability of about 10%, and the bent-arm configuration (peaks B and C combined) has about 30%.
- The asymptotic root mean square radius of the Hoyle state is $R_\mathrm{rms} = 3.43$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation of the 3-alpha reaction rate via the inverse photodisintegration reaction (Ishikawa 2013) by now analyzing the decay modes and interior structure of the Hoyle state. The results confirm the dominance of sequential decay through ${}^8$Be$(0_1^+)$ as seen in experimental works by Freer (1994), Maki (2012), Kirsebom (2012), Rana (2013), and Itoh (2014), while providing quantitative limits on direct decay contributions. The interior density peaks corresponding to equilateral and isosceles triangle configurations are consistent with calculations by Nguyen (2013) and Vasilevsky (2012), and the large radius of 3.43 fm agrees with calculations by Chernykh (2007) and Kamimura (2007).
