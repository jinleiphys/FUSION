# Calculations of $^{8}$He+p Elastic Cross Sections Using Microscopic Optical Potential

> An approach to calculate microscopic optical potential (OP) with the real part obtained by a folding procedure and with the imaginary part inherent in the high-energy approximation (HEA) is applied to study the $^8$He+p elastic scattering data at energies of tens of MeV/nucleon (MeV/N). The neutron and proton density distributions obtained in different models for $^{8}$He are utilized in the calculations of the differential cross sections. The role of the spin-orbit potential is studied. Comparison of the calculations with the available experimental data on the elastic scattering differential cross sections at beam energies of 15.7, 26.25, 32, 66 and 73 MeV/N is performed. The problem of the ambiguities of the depths of each component of the optical potential is considered by means of the imposed physical criterion related to the known behavior of the volume integrals as functions of the incident energy. It is shown also that the role of the surface absorption is rather important, in particular for the lowest incident energies (e.g., 15.7 and 26.25 MeV/nucleon).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a microscopic optical potential combining a folding-model real part with a high-energy approximation (HEA) imaginary part can describe $^{8}$He+p elastic scattering data at energies from 15.7 to 73 MeV/nucleon, but only when the depths of the potential components are renormalized by fitted parameters and, at the lowest energies, a surface absorption term is added.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was obtained by folding the CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with point-nucleon densities of $^{8}$He from three models (Tanihata, Large-Scale Shell Model, and Jastrow correlation method). The imaginary part was derived from the high-energy approximation formalism using parametrized nucleon-nucleon total cross sections and forward scattering amplitudes. The spin-orbit potential was constructed from Woods-Saxon fits to the microscopic potentials. Differential cross sections were calculated by solving the Schrodinger equation with the DWUCK4 code, and the renormalization parameters $N_R$, $N_I$, $N_R^{SO}$, and $N_I^{SO}$ were fitted to experimental data at five beam energies.

## Key numbers

* Beam energies studied: 15.7, 26.25, 32, 66, and 73 MeV/nucleon
* For the LSSM density at 15.7 MeV/N with surface term: $N_R$=1.078, $N_I$=0.036, $N_S$=0.207, $a$=0.686 fm, yielding total reaction cross section $\sigma_R$=791.1 mb
* Volume integrals $J_V$ decrease from 411.1 MeV.fm$^3$ at 15.7 MeV/N to 330.0 MeV.fm$^3$ at 73 MeV/N (LSSM density, selected fit)
* Volume integrals $J_W$ range from 58.6 MeV.fm$^3$ at 15.7 MeV/N to 20.25 MeV.fm$^3$ at 73 MeV/N (LSSM density, selected fit)
* Point-proton rms radius of $^{8}$He: 1.76 fm; point-neutron rms radius: 2.69 fm (Tanihata model)
* For the CDM3Y6 density dependence: $C$=0.2658, $\alpha$=3.8033, $\beta$=1.4099 fm$^3$, $\gamma$=4.0 fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach for $^{6}$He+p scattering (Lukyanov 2007) to the neutron-rich isotope $^{8}$He, using the same combination of folding real potential and HEA imaginary potential. It contrasts with purely phenomenological optical potentials that require many free parameters, such as those used by Avrigeanu (2002) with M3Y-Paris interactions. The study enables systematic testing of nuclear density models (Tanihata 1992, LSSM from Kara 2000 and Amos 2000, JCM from Massen 1999 and Moustakidis 2000) against elastic scattering data, and establishes that the HEA imaginary potential requires significant renormalization at energies below about 30 MeV/N, where surface absorption becomes important.
