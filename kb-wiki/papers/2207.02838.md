# Halo EFT for $^{31}$Ne in a spherical formalism

> We calculate the electromagnetic properties of the deformed one-neutron halo candidate $^{31}$Ne using Halo Effective Field Theory (Halo EFT). In this framework, $^{31}$Ne is bound via a resonant $P$-wave interaction between the $^{30}$Ne core and the valence neutron. We set up a spherical formalism for $^{31}$Ne in order to calculate the electromagnetic form factors and the E1-breakup strength distribution into the $^{30}$Ne-neutron continuum at leading order in Halo EFT. The associated uncertainties are estimated according to our power counting. In particular, we assume that the deformation of the $^{30}$Ne core enters at next-to-leading order. It can be accounted for by including the $J^P=2^+$ excited state of $^{30}$Ne as an explicit field in the effective Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper calculates electromagnetic properties of the deformed one-neutron halo candidate \(^{31}\)Ne using Halo EFT at leading order, assuming that the deformation of the \(^{30}\)Ne core enters only at next-to-leading order and can be accounted for by including the \(J^P=2^+\) excited state of \(^{30}\)Ne as an explicit field.

## Method

The authors describe \(^{31}\)Ne as a shallow \(P\)-wave bound state of a \(^{30}\)Ne core (treated as a \(J^P=0^+\) bosonic field) and a valence neutron (a \(J^P=1/2^+\) spinor field), using a dimer field with \(J^P=3/2^-\) to capture the bound state and continuum. They set up a spherical formalism for the effective Lagrangian, derive the full dimer propagator via Dyson summation, match the scattering amplitude to effective range parameters, and compute the scalar and vector electromagnetic currents at leading order to obtain form factors, multipole moments, and radii. The \(P\)-wave effective momentum \(r_1\) is estimated in an interval around the breakdown scale, and the neutron separation energy fixes the scattering volume \(a_1\).

## Key numbers

* Neutron separation energy of \(^{31}\)Ne: \(B_1 = 150\) keV, corresponding to a binding momentum \(\gamma_1 = 16.5\) MeV.
* Excitation energy of the \(^{30}\)Ne core: \(E_{\text{ex}} = 792\) keV, giving a high-momentum scale \(M_{\text{hi}} \approx 40\) MeV.
* Estimated range for the \(P\)-wave effective momentum: \(r_1 \in [-150, -50]\) MeV.
* Quadrupole moment: \(\mu_Q = -\frac{y^{2} q_c e}{3 \gamma_1 r_1}\), where \(y = m_R / m_c\).
* Magnetic octupole moment: \(\mu_O = -\frac{\sqrt{6} (1-y)^{2} \kappa_n \mu_N}{15 \gamma_1 r_1}\).
* Electric monopole radius squared: \(\langle r_{\text{E}0}^{2} \rangle = -\frac{5 y^{2}}{2 \gamma_1 r_1}\).
* Electric quadrupole radius squared: \(\langle r_{\text{E}2}^{2} \rangle = \frac{3 y^{2}}{5 \gamma_1^{2}}\).

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT framework for \(^{31}\)Ne previously developed in a Cartesian formulation (FBS60.16) by introducing a spherical basis that uses the correct number of field components for a given partial wave, yielding more compact expressions and enabling calculation of magnetic observables. It builds on experimental evidence from Nakamura (2014) that \(^{31}\)Ne has a low one-neutron separation energy and is a deformed \(P\)-wave halo, and on particle-rotor model calculations by Urata (2011) that reproduced Coulomb dissociation data with a quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2 \approx 0.2\)–\(0.3\). The results enable predictions of universal correlations between electromagnetic moments and radii, and provide a systematic framework for including core deformation effects at next-to-leading order.
