# Different Coalescence Sources of Light Nuclei Production in Au-Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV

> We study the production of light nuclei in the coalescence mechanism in Au-Au collisions at midrapidity at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV. We derive analytic formulas of momentum distributions of two bodies, three bodies and four nucleons coalescing into light nuclei, respectively. We naturally explain the transverse momentum spectra of the deuteron ($d$), triton ($t$), helium-3 ($^3$He) and helium-4 ($^4$He). We reproduce the data of yield rapidity densities and averaged transverse momenta of $d$, $t$, $^3$He and $^4$He. We give proportions of contributions from different coalescence sources for $t$, $^3$He and $^4$He in their productions. We find that besides nucleon coalescence, nucleon$+$nucleus coalescence and nucleus$+$nucleus coalescence may play requisite roles in light nuclei production in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3\) GeV, the production of light nuclei (triton, helium-3, and helium-4) cannot be fully explained by nucleon coalescence alone; instead, coalescence processes involving pre-formed light nuclei (nucleon+deuteron, nucleon+triton, nucleon+helium-3, and deuteron+deuteron) contribute significantly in central and mid-central collisions, though the paper hedges that these additional sources appear to disappear in peripheral collisions (40-80% centrality).

## Method

The authors extended a coalescence model, previously applied at higher RHIC and LHC energies, to include not only nucleon-nucleon coalescence but also nucleon+nucleus and nucleus+nucleus coalescence channels. They derived analytic formulas for the Lorentz-invariant momentum distributions of two-body, three-body, and four-body coalescence processes, using a spherical harmonic oscillator wave function and a factorized coordinate-momentum distribution for the hadronic system. The model was applied to the hadronic system at midrapidity in Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3\) GeV, using primordial nucleon momentum distributions as input, to compute the transverse momentum spectra, yield rapidity densities, and average transverse momenta of deuteron, triton, helium-3, and helium-4.

## Key numbers

*   The root-mean-square radius of helium-4 (\(^4\)He) used in the model is \(R_{^4\text{He}} = 1.6755\) fm (from Angeli (2013)).
*   The width parameter \(C_w\) for the coordinate distribution is set to 2, the same as in prior work by Mrowczynski (2016) and Wang (2020).
*   The spin degeneracy factor for four-nucleon coalescence into \(^4\)He is \(g_{^4\text{He}} = 1/16\).
*   The paper reports that in 0-10%, 10-20%, and 20-40% centralities, nucleon+deuteron coalescence plays an important role in triton and helium-3 production, and that nucleon+deuteron (triton, helium-3) coalescence as well as deuteron+deuteron coalescence occupy significant proportions in helium-4 production.
*   In the peripheral 40-80% centrality, nucleon coalescence plays a dominant role, and nucleon+nucleus or nucleus+nucleus coalescence seems to disappear.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model of Wang (2020) and Zhao (2022), which successfully described light nuclei production at higher RHIC and LHC energies via nucleon coalescence, to the lower collision energy of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3\) GeV. It directly addresses recent STAR data (STAR (2021)) from the beam energy scan program, which show abundant light nuclei production and strong final-state interactions (e.g., proton-deuteron and deuteron-deuteron correlations) at this energy. The study enables a more complete understanding of light nuclei formation mechanisms in the low-energy regime, suggesting that multi-step coalescence processes are necessary to reproduce the data, and it provides a framework for future analyses of composite particle production in heavy-ion collisions.
