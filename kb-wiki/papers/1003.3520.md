# Effects of the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy on properties of superheavy nuclei

> Effects of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy on ground-state properties of superheavy nuclei are studied in the relativistic mean-field theory. It is found that the softening of the symmetry energy plays an important role in the empirical shift [Phys. Rev. C 67, 024309 (2003)] of spherical orbitals in superheavy nuclei. The calculation based on the relativistic mean-field models NL3 and FSUGold supports the double shell closure in $^{292}120$ with the softening of the symmetry energy. In addition, the significant effect of the density dependence of the symmetry energy on the neutron skin thickness in superheavy nuclei are investigated.

# Effects of the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy on properties of superheavy nuclei

## Key claim
The softening of the nuclear symmetry energy (achieved by including an isoscalar-isovector coupling in relativistic mean-field models) plays an important role in reproducing the empirical shift of spherical proton orbitals in superheavy nuclei, and supports the double shell closure in $^{292}120$, but only when the symmetry energy is softened; the effect on neutron level shifts is less satisfactory than for protons.

## Method
The author performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for spherical superheavy nuclei using the NL3 and FSUGold parameter sets, with an isoscalar-isovector coupling term $\Lambda_{\rm v}$ introduced to modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy. For each value of $\Lambda_{\rm v}$, the $\rho NN$ coupling constant $g_\rho$ was readjusted to keep the symmetry energy unchanged at $k_F=1.15$ fm$^{-1}$, and the $\sigma$ meson mass $m_\sigma$ was slightly refitted to the binding energy of $^{208}$Pb. The coupled Dirac and meson equations were solved iteratively for spherical nuclei, with BCS pairing included using constant pairing gaps from the Möller-Nix prescription.

## Key numbers
- The neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb decreases from 0.281 fm (NL3, $\Lambda_{\rm v}=0.000$) to 0.132 fm (NL3w50, $\Lambda_{\rm v}=0.050$), and from 0.207 fm (FSUGold, $\Lambda_{\rm v}=0.030$) to 0.158 fm (FSUGw45, $\Lambda_{\rm v}=0.045$).
- The two-proton gap $\Delta_{2p}$ in $^{292}120$ is 3.5 MeV with NL3, reducing to 2.7 MeV in $^{304}120$ and disappearing in $^{318}120$.
- The two-proton gap in $^{292}120$ with FSUGold is 2.7 MeV for N=172 and 2.3 MeV for N=184.
- The binding energy per nucleon of $^{208}$Pb is 7.889 MeV (NL3) and 7.883 MeV (FSUGold), with proton radii of 5.459 fm and 5.473 fm respectively.
- The incompressibility $\kappa$ is 271.78 MeV for NL3 and 230.00 MeV for FSUGold.

## Context
This work extends studies of shell closures in superheavy nuclei that have produced divergent predictions: the microscopic-macroscopic model predicts double shell closure at (Z=114,N=184) (Möller 1994); nonrelativistic Skyrme models predict various closures at (114,184), (120,172), or (126,184) (Bender 1999, Rutz 1997, Cwiok 1996); while most RMF models favor (120,172) (Bender 1999, Rutz 1997, Zhang 2005). The author builds on the introduction of isoscalar-isovector coupling in RMF models by Horowitz (2001) and the FSUGold parameter set (Todd-Rutel 2005), and connects to the empirical shift of spherical orbitals extracted from deformed nuclei in the A~250 region (Afanasjev 2003, 2006). The results enable future constraints on superheavy nuclei properties through precision measurements of the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb via parity-violating electron scattering at Jefferson Laboratory.
