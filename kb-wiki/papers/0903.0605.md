# Electron Scattering off 4He with Three-Nucleon Forces

> An ab-initio calculation of the 4He(e,e') inelastic longitudinal response function R_L is presented. Realistic two- and three-body forces are used. The four-body continuum dynamics is treated rigorously with the help of the Lorentz integral transform. The three-nucleon force reduces the quasi-elastic peak height by about 10% for momentum transfers q between 300 and 500 MeV/c. Experimental data are well described, but not sufficiently precise to resolve this effect. The reduction due to the three-nucleon force increases significantly at lower q reaching up to about 40% at q=100 MeV/c. However, at such q values data are missing.

## Key claim

The three-nucleon force reduces the quasi-elastic peak height of the 4He longitudinal response by about 10% for momentum transfers between 300 and 500 MeV/c, but existing experimental data are not precise enough to confirm this effect; at lower momentum transfers (100 MeV/c) the reduction reaches about 40%, but no data exist at those kinematics.

## Method

The authors performed an ab-initio calculation of the 4He(e,e') inelastic longitudinal response function R_L using the AV18 two-nucleon potential and the UIX three-nucleon force. The four-body continuum dynamics was treated rigorously via the Lorentz integral transform method, with the ground-state wave function and transform calculated using expansions in hyperspherical harmonics with the EIHH and Lanczos techniques.

## Key numbers

- Three-nucleon force reduces the quasi-elastic peak height by about 10% for momentum transfers q between 300 and 500 MeV/c
- Three-nucleon force reduction reaches up to about 40% at q = 100 MeV/c
- 4He binding energies: 24.3 MeV (AV18), 28.4 MeV (AV18+UIX), and 30.6 MeV (MT potential)
- Only one data set exists at lower q, at 200 MeV/c (Buki), which is not sufficiently precise for conclusions

## Context

This work extends ab-initio calculations of electron scattering on 4He by including three-nucleon forces (UIX) alongside the realistic two-nucleon AV18 potential, using the Lorentz integral transform method developed by Efros (1994). The results show that three-nucleon forces produce a significant reduction in the longitudinal response that cannot be attributed to simple binding effects, as demonstrated by comparison with the semirealistic MT potential. The calculations enable future experimental tests at lower momentum transfers, where the three-nucleon force effect is largest but data are currently missing.
