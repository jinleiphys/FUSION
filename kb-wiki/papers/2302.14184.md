# Bayesian calibration of viscous anisotropic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions

> Due to large pressure gradients at early times, standard hydrodynamic model simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions do not become reliable until $O(1)$\,fm/$c$ after the collision. To address this one often introduces a pre-hydrodynamic stage that models the early evolution microscopically, typically as a conformal, weakly interacting gas. In such an approach the transition from the pre-hydrodynamic to the hydrodynamic stage is discontinuous, introducing considerable theoretical model ambiguity. Alternatively, fluids with large anisotropic pressure gradients can be handled macroscopically using the recently developed Viscous Anisotropic Hydrodynamics (VAH). In high-energy heavy-ion collisions VAH is applicable already at very early times, and at later times transitions smoothly into conventional second-order viscous hydrodynamics (VH). We present a Bayesian calibration of the VAH model with experimental data for Pb--Pb collisions at the LHC at $\sqrt{s_\textrm{NN}}=2.76$\,TeV. We find that the VAH model has the unique capability of constraining the specific viscosities of the QGP at higher temperatures than other previously used models.

## Key claim

The Viscous Anisotropic Hydrodynamics (VAH) model can constrain the specific shear and bulk viscosities of the Quark-Gluon Plasma at higher temperatures than previously used models, but this capability depends on starting the hydrodynamic evolution at a very early time (τ₀ = 0.05 fm/c) and eliminating the pre-hydrodynamic free-streaming stage entirely, which the authors argue becomes a good approximation when using VAH.

## Method

The authors replaced the standard free-streaming pre-equilibrium module and second-order viscous hydrodynamic (VH) stage in the JETSCAPE simulation framework with a Viscous Anisotropic Hydrodynamics (VAH) module that evolves longitudinal and transverse pressures separately. Initial conditions were generated using the TRENTO model for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, with the VAH evolution starting at τ₀ = 0.05 fm/c. The fluid was particlized at a switching temperature T_sw using the CORNELIUS code and the PTMA prescription, then fed into the SMASH hadronic afterburner, and the resulting observables were compared to ALICE experimental data using Bayesian calibration with Gaussian process emulators and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at τ₀ = 0.05 fm/c, much earlier than the typical O(1) fm/c where standard viscous hydrodynamics becomes reliable
- 98 experimental observables for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV were used in the calibration
- The initial pressure ratio R = (P_L/P_⊥)₀ is restricted to the range R ∈ (0.3, 1) due to technical convergence constraints
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb are R = 6.62 fm and α = 0.546 fm
- Each hydrodynamic event was oversampled until a total of 10⁵ hadrons was generated, but not more than 1000 times per event

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian calibration approach previously applied to standard viscous hydrodynamic models by the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020) and others (Bernhard 2019, Nijs 2020), but replaces the free-streaming pre-hydrodynamic stage that introduces theoretical ambiguity from matching a conformal gas to a non-conformal QGP equation of state. The VAH approach builds on the anisotropic hydrodynamic framework developed by Martinez and Florkowski (2010), Alqahtani (2017), and McNelis (2021), and enables future studies to constrain QGP transport properties at higher temperatures without the model uncertainty from discontinuous pre-equilibrium matching.
