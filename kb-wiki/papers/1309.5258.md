# Full result for the QCD equation of state with 2+1 flavors

> We present a full result for the 2+1 flavor QCD equation of state. All the systematics are controlled, the quark masses are set to their physical values, and the continuum extrapolation is carried out. This extends our previous studies [JHEP 0601:089 (2006); 1011:077 (2010)] to even finer lattices and now includes ensembles with Nt = 6,8,10,12 up to Nt = 16. We use a Symanzik improved gauge and a stout-link improved staggered fermion action. Our findings confirm our earlier results. In order to facilitate the direct use of our equation of state we make our tabulated results available for download.

## Key claim
The paper presents a "full result" for the 2+1 flavor QCD equation of state, meaning results obtained using physical quark masses combined with a controlled continuum limit extrapolation from ensembles with at least three lattice spacings in the scaling regime. The authors confirm their earlier findings about the height of the trace anomaly peak, but note that a resolution of the discrepancy with the hotQCD collaboration remains a task for the future.

## Method
The authors used a tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action with 2-step stout-link improved staggered fermions on lattices with temporal extents Nt = 6, 8, 10, 12, and 16. They computed the trace anomaly (I = epsilon - 3p) from lattice simulations and obtained the pressure via integration, using a new normalization procedure with Nt = 16 data to achieve agreement with the hadron resonance gas model at low temperatures. Systematic uncertainties were estimated using a histogram method that considered 896 different analysis choices (various continuum extrapolation methods, subtraction terms, scale settings, and spline functions).

## Key numbers
- The peak height of the trace anomaly I/T^4 is slightly above 4, confirming the Wuppertal-Budapest group's 2005 value and their 2010 continuum limit result
- The continuum extrapolated pressure at T = 214 MeV was used as a starting point for integration; this temperature was selected because it lies in the peak region (200-230 MeV) where the discrepancy with hotQCD is strongest
- The maximal difference between the old parametrization (Borsanyi 2010) and the new one is only 2.8% of the Stefan-Boltzmann value for the energy density
- The transition temperature (remnant of the chiral transition) is about Tc = 150 MeV
- The Stefan-Boltzmann value for pressure is 5.209 (used as reference for comparison)
- The hadron resonance gas model predicts p/T^4 = 0.27 at low temperatures; the previous work obtained p/T^4 = 0.16, but the new normalization achieves complete agreement with HRG

## Context
This work extends the Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration's previous equation of state studies (Borsanyi 2010) to finer lattices including Nt = 16, and provides full continuum extrapolation over the entire temperature range rather than only at three characteristic temperatures. The results confirm the earlier finding that the trace anomaly peak is slightly above 4, which is lower than the range of 5-8 reported by the hotQCD Collaboration using p4, asqtad, and HISQ actions (Petreczky 2012). The paper enables direct use of the equation of state in heavy ion collision phenomenology and provides the missing mu=0 piece needed for the nonzero baryonic chemical potential equation of state (Borsanyi 2012).
