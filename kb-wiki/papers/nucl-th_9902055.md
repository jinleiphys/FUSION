# Analysis of reaction dynamics at RHIC in a combined parton/hadron transport approach

> We introduce a transport approach which combines partonic and hadronic degrees of freedom on an equal footing and discuss the resulting reaction dynamics. The initial parton dynamics is modeled in the framework of the parton cascade model, hadronization is performed via a cluster hadronization model and configuration space coalescence, and the hadronic phase is described by a microscopic hadronic transport approach. The resulting reaction dynamics indicates a strong influence of hadronic rescattering on the space-time pattern of hadronic freeze-out and on the shape of transverse mass spectra. Freeze-out times and transverse radii increase by factors of 2 - 3 depending on the hadron species.

# Analysis of reaction dynamics at RHIC in a combined parton/hadron transport approach

## Key claim

The combined parton/hadron transport approach shows that hadronic rescattering strongly modifies the space-time pattern of freeze-out and transverse mass spectra at RHIC, but the authors note that approximately 15% of protons and pions still freeze out directly after hadronization without any hadronic reinteraction, and the system does not fully thermalize during the reaction.

## Method

The authors combined the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) for initial partonic evolution, a cluster hadronization model with configuration space coalescence for hadronization, and the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model for hadronic transport. They simulated central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy of sqrt(s)=200 GeV per nucleon-pair with impact parameter b <= 1 fm, analyzing the time evolution of parton and hadron multiplicities, collision rates, freeze-out distributions, and transverse mass spectra.

## Key numbers

- Central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV per nucleon-pair with impact parameter b <= 1 fm
- Partonic and hadronic degrees of freedom overlap from t_cm approx 1 fm/c up to t_cm approx 4 fm/c for the midrapidity region
- Hadronic resonances like Delta(1232) and rho(770) remain populated up to t_cm approx 15-20 fm/c
- Hadron yields saturate at t_cm approx 25 fm/c (upper estimate for chemical freeze-out)
- Collision rates cease at t_cm approx 30-40 fm/c (kinetic freeze-out)
- Average proton freeze-out time increases from 3.2 fm/c without hadronic rescattering to 27.4 fm/c with hadronic rescattering
- Average proton transverse freeze-out radius increases from 3.6 fm to 13 fm
- Average pion freeze-out time increases from 6.3 fm/c to 18.6 fm/c
- Average pion transverse freeze-out radius increases from 5.6 fm to 9.6 fm
- Approximately 15% of protons and pions freeze out directly after hadronization without any hadronic reinteraction
- On average protons rescatter 14 times and hyperons 15 times
- Baryonic rescattering is dominated (to 99%) by meson-baryon interactions, with roughly 1/3 elastic, 1/3 exciting discrete baryon resonances below 2.2 GeV, and 1/3 exciting high-mass resonance continuum above 2.2 GeV
- The m_t slope for nucleons decreases by roughly 25% from T=350 MeV for non-rescattered nucleons to T=270 MeV for nucleons rescattered at least 4 times

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model of Geiger (1995) and the UrQMD model of the authors' group, which had been tested at SIS, AGS, and SPS energies. It contrasts with earlier hybrid approaches by Bertsch (1988) that did not implement the partonic phase fully microscopically, and with Geiger (1998) and Nara (1998) that focused on inclusive hadronic spectra rather than reaction dynamics. The results enable experimental tests of hadronic reinteraction effects at RHIC through radial flow analysis and HBT correlation measurements, with a detailed analysis of these phenomena reported as in preparation.
