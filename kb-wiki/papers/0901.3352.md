# Spatial correlation properties of the anomalous density matrix in a slab of nuclear matter with realistic NN-forces

> Spatial correlation characteristics of the anomalous density matrix $κ$ in a slab of nuclear matter with the Paris and Argonne v18 forces are calculated. A detailed comparison with predictions of the effective Gogny force is made. It is found that the two realistic forces lead to very close results which are qualitatively similar to those for the Gogny force. At the same time, the magnitude of $κ$ for realistic forces is essentially smaller than the one for the Gogny force. The correlation characteristics are practically independent of the magnitude of $κ$ and turn out to be quite close for the three kinds of the force. In particular, all of them predict a small value of the local correlation length at the surface of the slab and a big one, inside. These results are in agreement with those obtained recently by Pillet at al. for finite nuclei with the Gogny force.

## Key claim

The spatial correlation properties of the anomalous density matrix in a slab of nuclear matter are qualitatively similar for the realistic Paris and Argonne v18 forces and the effective Gogny D1S force, but the magnitude of the anomalous density for the realistic forces is essentially smaller than for the Gogny force; the correlation characteristics are practically independent of this magnitude and turn out to be quite close for all three forces.

## Method

The authors solved the gap equation and Bogolyubov equations for a nuclear slab embedded in a Saxon-Woods potential (depth -50 MeV, diffuseness 0.65 fm, half-width L=6 fm) with chemical potential -8 MeV, using a separable representation for the Paris potential and a general method for the Argonne v18 force. They calculated the anomalous density matrix in mixed coordinate-momentum representation, then computed the local correlation length ξ(X) and its components ξ_x(X) and ξ_s(X), the pairing tensor distributions ϰ²(X,x) and ϰ²(X,r), and the locally normalized pairing tensor W(X,r). The same calculations were repeated for the Gogny D1S force for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The ratio Δ_Fs(X≃L)/Δ_Fs(X=0) ≃ 2 for realistic forces and ≃ 1.5 for the Gogny force.
- The gap Δ_Fs for the Gogny force exceeds that from realistic forces by approximately a factor 1.5 at the surface and 2 inside the slab.
- The Fermi averaged effective pairing interaction for the Gogny force has inner value ≃ -160 MeV·fm³ and outer value ≃ -950 MeV·fm³; for the Argonne force the inner value is ≃ -95 MeV·fm³ and outer value ≃ -1100 MeV·fm³.
- The ratio V_eff^out/V_eff^in ≃ 12 for the Argonne force and ≃ 6 for the Gogny force.
- The minimum correlation length in the x-direction at the surface is ξ_x^min ≃ 1.5 fm for all three forces.
- The correlation length ξ_x for the Gogny force deviates from the realistic forces by no more than 15%.
- The difference between ξ^Arg(X) and ξ^Par(X) is about 5%.
- In infinite matter, the approximate formula ξ = v_F/(√8 Δ_F) works within 15% even at the gap maximum.

## Context

This work extends the study of spatial correlation properties of pairing from finite nuclei (Pillet et al., using the Gogny D1S interaction for Sn, Ni, and Ca isotopes) to a nuclear slab geometry with realistic NN forces. It confirms qualitatively the finding of Pillet et al. that the local correlation length is small at the surface (about 2 fm in nuclei, 1.5 fm in the slab) and large inside, but shows that the surface enhancement of the total Cooper pair probability distribution found in finite nuclei does not occur in the slab due to different geometry. The results enable comparison between effective Gogny force predictions and ab initio realistic force calculations for pairing in nuclear matter and neutron star crust ("lasagna" phase).
