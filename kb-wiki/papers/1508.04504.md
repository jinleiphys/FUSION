# Impact of pion dynamics on nuclear shell structure

> Spin-isospin response in exotic nuclear systems is investigated. It is found that in some nuclei excitations with pionic quantum numbers (0-, 1+, 2-, ...) appear at very low energies with large transition probabilities, which is an indication of the vicinity of the onset of pion condensation. As an example, 2- components of the spin-dipole resonance in 78-Ni and 132-Sn are considered. The existence of such modes points out to the necessity of taking into account their coupling to other elementary modes of excitation, e.g. single-quasiparticle ones. This coupling is introduced in the theory for the first time. Thereby, pion-exchange contribution to the nucleon-nucleon interaction is included in the relativistic framework beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation. Namely, classes of Feynman diagrams are selected according to their significance for nuclear spectroscopic characteristics, such as single-particle energies and strength functions, and included into the nucleonic self-energy in all orders of meson-exchange. As an illustration, the impact of these new contributions on the single-particle energies of 100-Sn is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in exotic neutron-rich nuclei, spin-isospin excitations with pionic quantum numbers (such as 2⁻ states) appear at very low energies with large transition probabilities, indicating a proximity to the onset of pion condensation; however, the author notes that the prevailing view from earlier calculations is that densities higher than those typically reached in nuclei are needed for pion condensation and that it most probably does not occur in nuclei.

## Method

The author employs a relativistic framework based on covariant density functional theory, using the proton-neutron relativistic random phase approximation (pn-RRPA) and the proton-neutron relativistic time blocking approximation (pn-RTBA) to calculate spin-isospin response in neutron-rich nuclei. For the first time, the coupling of low-lying spin-isospin vibrational modes (isovector phonons) to single-quasiparticle motion is introduced into the nucleonic self-energy via selected classes of Feynman diagrams, including pion-exchange contributions beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation. The Dyson equation for the single-particle propagator is solved numerically with this self-energy for the case of ¹⁰⁰Sn.

## Key numbers

- In ¹³²Sn and ⁷⁸Ni, the spin-dipole resonance (SDR) is localized mainly between 15-35 MeV (¹³²Sn) and 20-40 MeV (⁷⁸Ni), but strong 2⁻ states appear at very low energies with intensities comparable to those of the giant resonance.
- In ⁷⁸Ni, the lowest pn-RRPA state is 4⁻, with 5⁻ and 6⁻ states at very close energies.
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn, shifts of the dominant single-particle states due to coupling to isovector phonons amount from a few hundred keV to about 1 MeV.
- Isospin-flip vibrational states with spins and parities Jπ = 2±, 3±, 4±, 5±, 6± and frequencies below 20 MeV were included into the self-energy for ¹⁰⁰Sn.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin-isospin response by the "Moscow school" (Migdal 1974) and "Stony Brook school" (Brown and Bolsterli 1973, Brown and Weise 1976), which found softening of pion modes in medium-mass and heavy nuclei, and by self-consistent pn-RQRPA calculations (Paar et al. 2004, Marketin et al. 2005) that described giant Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole resonances. The inclusion of particle-vibration coupling effects in the response function (Litvinova et al. 2012, Litvinova et al. 2014) is extended here to incorporate isovector phonons, establishing a connection between soft pionic modes and single-particle degrees of freedom. This enables future studies of how pion dynamics affects nuclear shell structure, particularly in exotic nuclei near the drip lines.
