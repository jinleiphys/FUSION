# Structure of $^{43}$P and $^{42}$Si in a two-level shape-coexistence model

> Exclusive cross sections for the $^{43}$P$(-1p)^{42}$Si reaction to the lowest $0^+$ and $2^+$ states, measured at NSCL with GRETINA and the S800, are interpreted in terms of a two-level mixing (collective) model of oblate and prolate co-existing shapes. Using the formalism developed for deformed nuclei we calculate the spectroscopic amplitudes and exclusive cross-sections in the strong coupling limit, where for $^{43}$P the schematic wavefunction includes the coupling of the Nilsson [211]$\frac{1}{2}$ proton orbit. Good agreement with the experimental data is obtained when the amplitude of the oblate configuration is $\gtrsim$ 80\%, suggesting that both nuclei are predominantly oblate, in line with theoretical expectations.

## Key claim

The exclusive cross sections for the one-proton knockout reaction from $^{43}$P to $^{42}$Si are well reproduced by a two-level shape-coexistence model when the oblate configuration amplitude is $\gtrsim$ 80%, but the four available data points cannot uniquely determine whether both nuclei are predominantly oblate or whether one is highly mixed while the other is essentially completely prolate; the authors favor the oblate interpretation based on shell-model predictions.

## Method

The authors applied the strong-coupling limit of the Particle Rotor Model (PRM) to calculate spectroscopic amplitudes and exclusive cross sections for the $^{43}$P$(-1p)^{42}$Si reaction, using a two-level mixing model where the ground state of $^{42}$Si is a superposition of oblate and prolate deformed components. For $^{43}$P, the wavefunction includes the coupling of the Nilsson [211]$\frac{1}{2}$ proton orbit, with Nilsson coefficients calculated as functions of the deformation parameter $\epsilon_2$ using $\mu=0$ and $\kappa=0.105$. The model was fit to four experimental cross-section data points (to the $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$, tentative $0^+_2$, and tentative $2^+_2$ states) with four parameters: the mixing amplitudes $\A$ and $\alpha$, the quenching factor $R$, and the deformation $|\epsilon_2|$.

## Key numbers

- The $2^+_1$ state in $^{42}$Si lies at 737(8) keV; the tentative $0^+_2$ state lies at 2150(13) keV.
- The fitted deformation parameter is $|\epsilon_2| = 0.26(4)$.
- The fitted quenching factor is $R = 0.33(7)$.
- The deepest $\chi^2$ minimum corresponds to mixing parameters $(\A,\alpha) = (0.90(3), 1^{+0}_{-0.001})$, indicating the oblate amplitude is $\gtrsim$ 80%.
- Two other solutions exist: $(\A, \alpha) = (0^{+0.04}_{-0}, 0.45(7))$ and $(0.45(7), 0^{+0.04}_{-0})$.
- The quenching factor $R$ agrees with the established trend value $R_s = 0.36$ from Tos14/Tos21 and with the value 0.33(2) reported in Gad20.
- Shell-model calculations with the SDPF-U interaction predict for $^{42}$Si: $\epsilon_2 = 0.32(7)$ and $\gamma = 47(13)^\circ$ for the lower band, and $\epsilon_2 = 0.34(6)$ and $\gamma = 15(15)^\circ$ for the higher band.

## Context

This work extends the two-level shape-coexistence model previously applied to two-proton knockout reactions $^{44}$S(-2p)$^{42}$Si and $^{42}$Si(-2p)$^{40}$Mg by Crawford et al. (2014), which suggested a shape change from prolate in $^{44}$S to oblate in $^{42}$Si and back to prolate in $^{40}$Mg. The results are compared with large-scale shell-model calculations using the SDPF-U (Nowacki et al., 2009) and SDPF-MU (Utsuno et al., 2012) effective interactions, which both predict an oblate ground state band for $^{42}$Si but differ in the location of the coexisting prolate band. The findings enable further refinement using a full PRM calculation and support the interpretation of shape evolution along the $N=28$ isotones driven by the tensor-force-induced Jahn-Teller effect proposed by Utsuno et al. (2012).
