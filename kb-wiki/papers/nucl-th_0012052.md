# Photoproduction of quasi-bound omega mesons in nuclei

> We propose the (gamma,p) reaction as a means of producing possible quasi-bound states of omega mesons in nuclei. We use an effective Lagrangian, based on chiral SU(3) symmetry and vector meson dominance, in order to construct the omega-nuclear potential. The contribution of bound omega states is observable in the missing energy spectra of protons emitted in forward direction for several nuclei.

## Key claim

The (gamma,p) reaction on nuclei can produce quasi-bound omega meson states observable in missing energy spectra of forward-emitted protons, provided the omega-nucleon potential derived from chiral SU(3) symmetry and vector meson dominance is used; the signal sits on a background approximately five times larger, primarily from rho meson and continuum pi pi production.

## Method

The authors construct an energy-dependent complex omega-nucleus potential using an effective Lagrangian based on chiral SU(3) symmetry and vector meson dominance, with the omega self-energy evaluated in the local density approximation. They solve the Klein-Gordon equation self-consistently to obtain complex quasi-bound state energies for omega mesons in various nuclei. The (gamma,p) reaction cross section is calculated using the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with eikonal distortion for the outgoing proton, for photon energies around 1.5 and 2.75 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density, the omega mass is reduced by approximately 15% and its width is increased up to around 40 MeV
- For omega bound to 6He: 1s state at (epsilon = -49 MeV, Gamma = 36 MeV)
- For omega bound to 11B: 1s state at (epsilon = -66 MeV, Gamma = 41 MeV); 2s state at (epsilon = -14 MeV, Gamma = 34 MeV); 1p state at (epsilon = -40 MeV, Gamma = 39 MeV)
- For omega bound to 39K: 1s state at (epsilon = -88 MeV, Gamma = 44 MeV); 2s state at (epsilon = -54 MeV, Gamma = 45 MeV); 3s state at (epsilon = -16 MeV, Gamma = 41 MeV)
- Free gamma + p -> p + omega cross section taken as 0.3 microbarn/sr from the SAPHIR experiment
- Proton-nucleon cross section used for distortion: 40 mb
- Optimal photon energy for producing omega at rest: 2.75 GeV
- At E_gamma = 1.5 GeV, a free omega would have momentum around 130 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vector mesons in nuclear matter, particularly the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1990s) suggesting a 15% drop in vector meson masses, and dynamical models by Klingl, Kaiser, and Weise (late 1990s) predicting a moderate omega width increase. It contrasts with the (d,3He) reaction proposed by Klingl, Hayano, and Saito for producing eta and omega bound states, noting that the (gamma,p) reaction has about 10 times less absorption because only the outgoing proton is distorted. The results enable experimental searches at facilities such as ELSA in Bonn and Spring8 in Japan, though the authors note that detecting a characteristic omega decay mode alongside the forward proton would help reduce the expected background.
