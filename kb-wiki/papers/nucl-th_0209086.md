# Final State Interaction Effects in Incoherent Pion Photoproduction on the Deuteron

> Incoherent photoproduction of pions on the deuteron in the Delta(1232) resonance region is studied, where besides the impulse approximation complete two-body rescattering in the nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon final state subsystems is included.

# Final State Interaction Effects in Incoherent Pion Photoproduction on the Deuteron

## Key claim

Final state interactions (FSI) in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) and pion-nucleon (πN) subsystems significantly modify the total and differential cross sections for incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the Delta(1232) resonance region, with the largest effects seen for π⁰ production. The abstract's claim of "satisfactory agreement with experimental data" is qualified by the fact that the large FSI effect for π⁰ production arises partly because the impulse approximation (IA) already includes a fraction of coherent production due to non-orthogonality of the final plane wave to the bound state.

## Method

The authors computed the d(γ,π)NN reaction using a model that includes the impulse approximation plus complete two-body rescattering in both the NN and πN final-state subsystems. The elementary γN → πN amplitude, including Born and Δ(1232) resonance contributions, was taken from Schmidt et al. (1996), while the NN and πN t-matrices were obtained from separable interactions of Haidenbauer et al. (1984) and Nozawa et al. (1990), respectively.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for d(γ,π)NN: IA alone (dashed curve) and IA+complete rescattering (solid curve) are compared to experimental data from Asai et al. (1990), Benz et al. (1973), and Chiefari et al. (1975) across the Delta resonance region.
- GDH integral up to 550 MeV from d(γ,π)NN: IA gives I_d(γ,π)NN^(IA) = 187 μb, reduced to I_d(γ,π)NN^(IA+NN+πN) = 87 μb by final state rescattering.
- Differential cross sections for d(γ,π⁰)pn are shown for IA and IA+complete rescattering, compared to experimental data from Krusche et al. (1999).
- Spin asymmetry of d(γ,π⁰)pn is presented for IA, IA+NN rescattering, and IA+NN+πN rescattering, with comparison to the average of proton and neutron asymmetries.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion photoproduction on the deuteron by including complete two-body rescattering in both NN and πN subsystems, going beyond earlier impulse approximation calculations. The separable NN interaction of Haidenbauer et al. (1984) and πN interaction of Nozawa et al. (1990) are used as input. The results enable more reliable extraction of neutron information from deuteron data and improve the evaluation of the GDH sum rule on the deuteron, showing that FSI cannot be neglected, especially for the spin asymmetry where charged pion production shows much larger FSI effects than in the total cross section.
