# Study of exotic hadrons in s-wave chiral dynamics

> We study the exotic hadrons in s-wave scattering of the Nambu-Goldstone boson with a target hadron based on chiral dynamics. Utilizing the low energy theorem of chiral symmetry, we show that the s-wave interaction is not strong enough to generate bound states in exotic channels in flavor SU(3) symmetric limit, although the interaction is responsible for generating some nonexotic hadron resonances dynamically. We discuss the renormalization condition adopted in this analysis.

## Key claim

In the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit, the s-wave Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction between a Nambu-Goldstone boson and a target hadron is not strong enough to generate bound states in exotic channels, even though the same interaction produces nonexotic resonances; the maximum attractive coupling strength in exotic channels is always smaller than the critical value required for binding.

## Method

The authors derived the general expression for the Weinberg-Tomozawa coupling strength \(C_{\alpha,T}\) for arbitrary SU(3) representations of the target hadron, using the quadratic Casimir operator. They introduced an "exoticness" quantum number to identify exotic channels and computed the possible attractive coupling strength for those channels. They then constructed the unitarized scattering amplitude using the N/D method with a once-subtracted loop function, imposing a renormalization condition that matches the unitarized amplitude to the tree-level amplitude at a scale \(\mu = M_T\) to respect the low-energy theorem of chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction strength for exotic channels is given by \(C_{\text{exotic}} = 1\) (dimensionless), for target representations \(T = [p,0]\) with \(p \geq 3B\).
- The critical coupling strength required to generate a bound state is \(C_{\text{crit}} = \frac{2f^2}{m[-G(M_T + m)]}\), where \(f\) is the decay constant, \(m\) the NG boson mass, and \(M_T\) the target mass; the attraction in exotic channels (\(C_{\text{exotic}} = 1\)) is always smaller than this critical value for all physically allowed parameters.
- The renormalization condition requires the matching scale \(\mu_m\) to lie in the region \(M_T - m \leq \mu_m \leq M_T + m\) to keep the subtraction constant real.

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach that successfully described nonexotic hadron resonances as dynamically generated states in s-wave scattering of Nambu-Goldstone bosons with hadrons (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1998, Oller 2000, Lutz 2001). It directly addresses the puzzle of the near-absence of flavor-exotic hadrons, using the same framework that produced bound states in nonexotic channels in the SU(3) limit (Jido 2003). The result provides a model-independent chiral-symmetry explanation for why exotic hadrons are not observed as hadron-NG boson bound states, while leaving open the possibility of exotic states from other mechanisms such as quark dynamics or chiral soliton rotations.
