# One-loop analysis of the four-Fermi contribution to the atomic EDM within RPVMSSM

> The contribution in the R-parity violating (RPV) Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM) to the electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{199}$Hg at the one-loop level is evaluated. At the one-loop level, the $^{199}$Hg EDM receives RPV contribution whose couplings are of a different type from the tree level analysis. This contribution is shown to be constrained by using the limit to the CP-odd electron-nucleon (e-N) interaction given by the recent result of $^{199}$Hg EDM experiment.

## Key claim

The one-loop RPV contribution to the $^{199}$Hg EDM constrains combinations of RPV couplings (involving $W$ boson exchange and CKM flavor change) that are different from those constrained by the tree-level sneutrino exchange analysis of Herczeg, and these one-loop limits are tighter by one to two orders of magnitude than existing phenomenological bounds from other experiments.

## Method

The authors computed one-loop box diagrams within the R-parity violating Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (RPVMSSM) that contribute to P- and CP-odd electron-nucleon (e-N) interactions, focusing on the $C_N^{SP}$ coefficient which is most severely constrained by the $^{199}$Hg EDM data. They evaluated the loop integrals involving selectron, $W$ boson, and up-type quark propagators, and used nucleon matrix elements $\langle p \vert \bar u u \vert p \rangle = 3.5$ and $\langle p \vert \bar d d \vert p \rangle = 2.8$ to convert quark-level amplitudes into nucleon-level coefficients. The resulting constraints on the imaginary parts of specific RPV coupling products were then compared with the experimental upper limit on the $^{199}$Hg EDM.

## Key numbers

- $^{199}$Hg EDM experimental result: $d_{\rm Hg} = (0.49 \pm 1.29 \pm 0.76) \times 10^{-29} e\,{\rm cm}$ (Griffith et al.)
- Bound on scalar-pseudoscalar e-N interaction: $|0.40 C_p^{SP} + 0.60 C_n^{SP}| < 5.2 \times 10^{-8}$
- Bound on pseudoscalar-scalar e-N interaction: $|0.24 C_p^{PS} + 0.76 C_n^{PS}| < 5.1 \times 10^{-7}$
- Bound on tensor e-N interaction: $|0.24 C_p^T + 0.76 C_n^T| < 1.5 \times 10^{-9}$
- For sparticle masses set to 100 GeV: $|{\rm Im}(\lambda^*_{121} \lambda'_{221})| < 7.3 \times 10^{-6}$ (compared to previous limit $4.8 \times 10^{-4}$)
- For sparticle masses set to 100 GeV: $|{\rm Im}(\lambda^*_{131} \lambda'_{321})| < 7.3 \times 10^{-6}$ (compared to previous limit $6.0 \times 10^{-4}$)
- For sparticle masses set to 100 GeV: $|{\rm Im}(\lambda^*_{121} \lambda'_{231})| < 6.0 \times 10^{-4}$ (compared to previous limit $8.8 \times 10^{-3}$)
- For sparticle masses set to 100 GeV: $|{\rm Im}(\lambda^*_{131} \lambda'_{331})| < 6.0 \times 10^{-4}$ (compared to previous limit $6.0 \times 10^{-3}$)

## Context

This work extends the tree-level analysis of Herczeg (1994), who evaluated sneutrino exchange contributions to P- and CP-odd e-N interactions and constrained the combination $\sum_{k,j} {\rm Im}(\lambda^*_{1j1} \lambda'_{jkk})$. The present one-loop analysis constrains different combinations of RPV couplings involving $W$ boson exchange and CKM flavor mixing, using the improved $^{199}$Hg EDM limit from Griffith et al. (2009). The results enable tighter constraints on CP-violating RPV couplings by one to two orders of magnitude compared to previous phenomenological bounds from Chemtob (2005), and provide new input for understanding the baryon asymmetry of the Universe.
