# Vector boson-tagged jet production in heavy ion collisions at the LHC

> Vector boson-tagged jet production in collisions of heavy nuclei opens new opportunities to study parton shower formation and propagation in strongly interacting matter. It has been argued to provide a golden channel that can constrain the energy loss of jets in the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion reactions. We present theoretical results for isolated photon-tagged and $Z^0$ boson-tagged jet production in Pb+Pb collisions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV at the LHC. Specifically, we evaluate the transverse momentum imbalance ${\rm x_{JV}}$ distribution and nuclear modification factor ${\rm I_{AA}}$ of tagged jets and compare our theoretical calculations to recent experimental measurements by ATLAS and CMS collaborations. Our analysis, which includes both collisional and radiative energy losses, sheds light on their relative importance versus the strength of jet-medium interactions and helps quantify the amount of out-of-cone radiation of predominantly prompt quark-initiated jets.

# Vector boson-tagged jet production in heavy ion collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The transverse momentum imbalance and nuclear modification factor of vector boson-tagged jets in Pb+Pb collisions can be described by including both collisional and radiative energy losses, with the data favoring jet-medium coupling strengths in the range g = 2.0 to g = 2.2 (corresponding to alpha_s = 0.32 to alpha_s = 0.39 at tree level), though the asymmetry distributions prefer larger coupling while the I_AA distributions prefer smaller coupling.

## Method
The authors used Pythia 8 with CTEQ6L1 parton distribution functions to simulate the p+p baseline for isolated photon-tagged and Z^0 boson-tagged jet production, then applied medium modifications using a first-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss formalism with Poisson-distributed multiple gluon emissions. Collisional energy loss was included by assuming all energy deposited by collisional processes is transported out of the jet cone, and the out-of-cone energy fraction was calculated from the angular distribution of medium-induced gluon radiation for jet radius parameters R = 0.3 and R = 0.4.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC
- Jet radius parameters: R = 0.3 (CMS) and R = 0.4 (ATLAS)
- Transverse momentum cuts: p_T^Z > 60 GeV, p_T^gamma > 60 GeV, p_T^J > 30 GeV
- Delta <x_JZ> for Z^0+jet with p_T^Z = 60-80 GeV: 0.075 (g = 2.0) and 0.093 (g = 2.2), compared to CMS measurement 0.124 +/- 0.052
- Delta <x_Jgamma> for gamma+jet with p_T^gamma = 60-80 GeV: 0.065 (g = 2.0) and 0.085 (g = 2.2), compared to CMS preliminary 0.081 +/- 0.059
- Coupling strengths: g = 2.0 and g = 2.2, corresponding to alpha_s = 0.32 and alpha_s = 0.39 at tree level
- Medium formation time: tau_0 = 0.3 fm
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: sigma_in = 70 mb
- Jet-medium coupling values g = 2.0 and g = 2.2 have worked well in describing single inclusive hadron, heavy flavor mesons, and jet suppression data at the LHC

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of vector boson-tagged jet production in heavy ion collisions based on perturbative QCD frameworks (Dai 2012, Neufeld 2012), Boltzmann transport models (Wang 2013), the JEWEL event generator (Kunnawalkam Elayavalli 2016), and hybrid strong/weak coupling models (Casalderrey-Solana 2015). The analysis is motivated by recent measurements from the ATLAS and CMS collaborations at the LHC (Sirunyan 2017, CMS 2016, ATLAS 2016), and enables quantitative extraction of jet-medium coupling strength and the relative importance of radiative versus collisional energy loss for quark-initiated jets. The authors find that radiative energy losses dominate but collisional energy loss can be as large as 40% correction to the radiative energy loss for 100 GeV jets.
