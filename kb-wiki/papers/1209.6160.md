# Existence of hyperons in the pulsar PSRJ1614-2230

> The possibility of existence of hyperons in the recently measured $2M_\odot$ pulsar PSRJ1614-2230 is explored using a diverse set of nuclear equations of state calculated within the relativistic mean-field models. Our results indicate that the nuclear equations of state compatible with heavy-ion data allow the hyperons to exist in the PSRJ1614-2230 only for significantly larger values for the meson-hyperon coupling strengths. The maximum mass configurations for these cases contain sizable hyperon fractions ($\sim 60%$) and yet masquared their counterpart composed of only nucleonic matter.

## Key claim

Nuclear equations of state (EOSs) that are compatible with heavy-ion collision data can allow hyperons to exist in the pulsar PSRJ1614-2230, but only if the meson-hyperon coupling strengths for the ω and φ vector mesons are significantly augmented (by a factor of ~1.5) relative to their standard SU(6) quark model values.

## Method

The authors used nine different parameter sets (NL3, GM1, TM1, G2, BSR2, BSR6, BSR9, BSR13, BSP) of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models to compute EOSs for β-equilibrated nucleonic and hyperonic matter. They varied the meson-hyperon coupling strengths XωH and XφH over a wide range, with two representative choices: XSU6 (standard SU(6) values) and X180 (augmented values). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relationships for static compact stars, and the code by Stergioulas was used for rotating configurations.

## Key numbers

- The pulsar PSRJ1614-2230 has mass M = 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ (lower bound on maximum mass).
- For the X180 case, XωH = 1 and XφH = 0.8, which are larger by a factor of ~1.5 relative to SU(6) values (XωH = 2/3, XφH = √2/3).
- For the NL3 (BSP) parameterization, threshold densities for Λ hyperons are 0.28 (0.38) fm⁻³ for XSU6 and 0.32 (0.42) fm⁻³ for X180.
- The minimum mass at which hyperons begin to appear ranges from 1.2 to 1.5 M⊙ across the RMF models considered.
- For EOSs compatible with heavy-ion data (BSR9, BSR13, BSP, G2, TM1), maximum masses with hyperons using standard couplings are in the range 1.7 – 1.9 M⊙.
- With augmented couplings (X180), the TM1 and BSP parameter sets yield maximum masses consistent with the PSRJ1614-2230 mass.
- For the BSP case, the radius R1.4 for a 1.4 M⊙ star is 12.7 km, consistent with the observational range of 10.4 – 12.9 km from Steiner (2012).
- The maximum mass configurations with augmented couplings contain ~60% hyperonic matter fraction.
- For XφH = 1, the maximum mass increases by 0.1 M⊙ when XωH increases from 0.5 to 1.
- For rotating stars at ν = 317 Hz (the spin frequency of PSRJ1614-2230), XωH = 1 with XφH = 0.65 is sufficient to reach the mass lower bound.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Weissenborn et al. (2011) and Bednarek et al. (2011) that showed hyperonic stars could reach 2 M⊙ using stiff EOSs or SU(6) symmetry breaking, but it specifically tests whether EOSs compatible with heavy-ion collision data (Danielewicz, 2002) can also accommodate hyperons without invoking weakly interacting light bosons (WILBs) as in Sulaksono et al. (2011). The results enable future work to constrain meson-hyperon coupling strengths using combined heavy-ion data and pulsar mass measurements, and show that hyperonic stars can masquerade as purely nucleonic stars in their mass-radius relations.
