# pQCD vs. AdS/CFT Tested by Heavy Quark Energy Loss

> We predict the charm and bottom quark nuclear modification factors using weakly coupled pQCD and strongly coupled AdS/CFT drag methods. The log(pT/M_Q)/pT dependence of pQCD loss and the momentum independence of drag loss lead to different momentum dependencies for the R_{AA} predictions. This difference is enhanced by examining a new experimental observable, the double ratio of charm to bottom nuclear modification factors, R^{cb}=R^c_{AA}/R^b_{AA}. At LHC the weakly coupled theory predicts R^{cb} goes to 1; whereas the strongly coupled theory predicts R^{cb} .2 independent of pT. At RHIC the differences are less dramatic, as the production spectra are harder, but the drag formula is applicable to higher momenta, due to the lower temperature.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the double ratio of charm to bottom nuclear modification factors, \(R^{cb}=R_{AA}^c/R_{AA}^b\), will be radically different for weakly coupled pQCD versus strongly coupled AdS/CFT drag at LHC energies, with pQCD predicting \(R^{cb}\rightarrow 1\) and AdS/CFT predicting \(R^{cb}\sim 0.2\) independent of transverse momentum; however, the AdS/CFT drag formula may only be applicable up to a momentum-dependent "speed limit" given by \(\gamma_c \approx 4M^2/(\lambda (T^{SYM})^2)\), and at RHIC the differences are less dramatic because the production spectra are harder.

## Method

The author computed charm and bottom quark nuclear modification factors \(R_{AA}^Q(p_T)\) using two approaches: weakly coupled pQCD with the WHDG model of convolved elastic and radiative energy loss, and strongly coupled AdS/CFT using the drag coefficient \(\mu_Q = \pi\sqrt{\lambda}(T^{SYM})^2/(2M_Q)\) from the supergravity dual. The energy loss was implemented numerically with Bjorken expansion, a Woods-Saxon nuclear profile, and FONLL production spectra, with the pQCD coupling fixed at \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) and the AdS/CFT coupling mapped to QCD via an "obvious" prescription (\(\alpha_{SYM} = \alpha_s\), \(T^{SYM} = T^{QCD}\)) and an "alternate" prescription equating energy densities.

## Key numbers

- At LHC, the strongly coupled AdS/CFT drag predicts \(R^{cb} \sim 0.2\) independent of \(p_T\), while weakly coupled pQCD predicts \(R^{cb} \rightarrow 1\) as \(p_T\) increases.
- The asymptotic pQCD fractional energy loss scales as \(\bar{\epsilon}_{pQCD} = \kappa \alpha_s \hat{q} L^2 \log(p_T/M_Q)/p_T\), while the AdS/CFT drag gives \(\bar{\epsilon}_{AdS} = 1 - \exp(-\mu_Q L)\) independent of \(p_T\).
- The AdS/CFT drag coefficient is \(\mu_Q = \pi\sqrt{\lambda}(T^{SYM})^2/(2M_Q)\), with the "obvious" prescription using \(\alpha_{SYM} = 0.3\) giving a diffusion coefficient \(D \simeq 1.2/2\pi T\), while a value \(D \simeq 3/2\pi T\) would require \(\alpha_{SYM} \simeq 0.05\).
- The AdS/CFT "speed limit" is \(\gamma_c = (1 + 2M/(\sqrt{\lambda}T^{SYM}))^2 \approx 4M^2/(\lambda (T^{SYM})^2)\), above which significant corrections to the drag formula are expected.
- For the AdS/CFT drag, the asymptotic double ratio estimate gives \(R^{cb}_{AdS} \approx M_c/M_b \approx 0.27\), independent of momentum.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of pQCD and AdS/CFT approaches to heavy quark energy loss, motivated by the failure of pQCD to simultaneously explain the nearly ideal hydrodynamic flow (entropy to viscosity ratio \(\eta/s \sim 0.1\)), the highly suppressed nonphotonic electrons, and the nonperturbatively large azimuthal anisotropy at RHIC (Teaney 2003). The paper proposes the double ratio \(R^{cb}\) as a new observable to distinguish between the two coupling limits, building on prior drag calculations by Liu (2006) and the WHDG pQCD model by El (2007). The results enable experimental falsification of either approach at LHC, provided a \(p+Pb\) control run is performed to account for initial state effects.
