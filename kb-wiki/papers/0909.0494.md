# The Plane Wave Expansion, Infinite Integrals and Identities involving Spherical Bessel Functions

> This paper shows that the plane wave expansion can be a useful tool in obtaining analytical solutions to infinite integrals over spherical Bessel functions and the derivation of identites for these functions. The integrals are often used in nuclear scattering calculations, where an analytical result can provide an insight into the reaction mechanism. A technique is developed whereby an integral over several special functions which cannot be found in any standard integral table can be broken down into integrals that have existing analytical solutions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the plane wave expansion (Rayleigh equation) can be systematically used to derive analytical solutions for infinite integrals over spherical Bessel functions and identities involving them, though the author notes the technique itself is not new and was previously used in Mehrem, Londergan and Macfarlane (1991).

## Method

The author starts from the plane wave expansion of \(e^{i\vec k \cdot \vec r}\) in terms of spherical harmonics and spherical Bessel functions, then integrates over all space or over specific variables to extract integral representations. By applying orthogonality relations of spherical harmonics and Legendre polynomials, and using delta function identities, the method reduces integrals over one, two, and three spherical Bessel functions to closed-form expressions. For integrals involving four or more special functions, the closure relation for spherical Bessel functions is used to decompose them into products of integrals over fewer functions.

## Key numbers

- \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty j_L(kr)\,dr = \frac{(-i)^L}{k} \pi P_L(0)\) (equation for one spherical Bessel function)
- \(\int_0^\infty r^2 j_L(k_1 r) j_L(k_2 r)\,dr = \frac{\pi}{2 k_1^2} \delta(k_1 - k_2)\) (closure relation for spherical Bessel functions)
- \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty j_L(x) j_{L'}(x)\,dx = \frac{\pi}{2L+1} \delta_{L,L'}\) (orthogonality relation)
- \(\int_0^\infty j_L(k_1 r) j_L(k_2 r)\,dr = \frac{\pi}{2(2L+1)} \frac{(k_<)^L}{(k_>)^{L+1}}\) (Watson 1944, equation 1, page 405)
- \(\int_0^\infty r^2 j_1(kr) j_1(kr) j_2(kr) j_2(kr)\,dr = \frac{23\pi}{420 k^3}\) (example integral over four spherical Bessel functions)

## Context

This work extends the analytical integration techniques for spherical Bessel functions used in nuclear scattering calculations, building on prior results from Watson (1944), Sawaguri and Tobocman (1967), Jackson and Maximon (1972), and Mehrem, Londergan and Macfarlane (1991). The method enables analytical evaluation of integrals that cannot be found in standard tables such as Gradshteyn and Rhyzik (1965), and provides insight into reaction mechanisms by avoiding purely numerical complex-plane methods used by Davies, Strayer and White (1988) and Chen and Su (2004).
