# Shell Evolution in Neutron-rich Ge, Se, Kr and Sr Nuclei within RHB Approach

> The exotic even-even isotopic chains from Z=32 to Z=38 are investigated by means of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach with the explicit Density Dependent Meson-Exchange (DD-ME2) and Density Dependent Point-Coupling (DD-PC1) models. The classic magic number N=50 is reproduced and the new number N=70 is predicted to be a robust shell closure by analysing several calculated quantities such as: two-neutron separation energies, two-neutron shell gap, neutron pairing energy, potential energy surface and neutron single particle energies with and without the tensor force. The obtained results are corroborated by shell model calculations and compared with the predictions of finite range droplet model (FRDM) and with the available experimental data. A reasonable and satisfactory agreement between the theoretical models and experiment is established.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that N=70 is a robust new magic number in neutron-rich Ge, Se, Kr, and Sr isotopes, but only for these four specific isotopic chains; the shell gap at N=70 weakens and quenches for nuclei above Sr (Z>38) and is inaccessible for nuclei below Ge (Z<32) due to the position of the neutron drip line.

## Method
The authors employed the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach with two covariant density functionals: the finite-range Density Dependent Meson-Exchange (DD-ME2) model and the zero-range Density Dependent Point-Coupling (DD-PC1) model. Calculations were performed using the DIRHB computer code in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with 10 fermion shells and 20 boson shells, using a separable pairing interaction in coordinate space with parameters G=728 MeV.fm^3 and a=0.6442 fm. They analyzed Fermi levels, two-neutron separation energies, two-neutron shell gaps, neutron pairing energies, potential energy surfaces, and neutron single-particle energies for even-even isotopes with neutron numbers from 42 to 76.

## Key numbers
- Two-neutron separation energies show a "remarkable drop" at N=50 for all four isotopic chains (Ge, Se, Kr, Sr) and a similar drop at N=70 in DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 calculations, while FRDM predictions show no such drop at N=70.
- The two-neutron shell gap δ₂ₙ exhibits a "sharp peak" at N=50 and at N=70 for all four nuclei with DD-ME2 and DD-PC1; the peak at N=70 is "more significant" with DD-ME2 than with DD-PC1, especially for Kr and Sr.
- Neutron pairing energy vanishes at N=50 for all nuclei and at N=70 for all nuclei; for Se, Kr, and Sr with DD-PC1 the minimum "moves slightly away from zero" with decreasing proton number.
- Potential energy surfaces for N=70 isotones show a "sharp single minimum" at β₂=0 (spherical ground state) for all four nuclei with both functionals; for N=68 isotones, both models predict oblate ground states for Se, Kr, and Sr.
- The single-particle energy gap between the 3s₁/₂ and 1h₁₁/₂ states (which defines the N=70 shell gap) is "significantly large" for both functionals and is "slightly greater" with DD-ME2 than with DD-PC1; the gap is reduced when proton number decreases and becomes weaker by Z=38.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei, including the prediction of N=70 as a magic number in Ni (Z=28) by Bhattacharya using relativistic mean-field theory, and the confirmation of N=32 and N=40 as new magic numbers in Ca and Ar by El Adri and Oulne (2020) using the same RHB approach. The results contradict the predictions of the finite range droplet model (FRDM) by Moller, which shows no shell closure at N=70 in this region. The study enables future experimental searches for N=70 shell effects in neutron-rich Ge, Se, Kr, and Sr isotopes at radioactive beam facilities, and suggests that the neutron drip line for these nuclei lies above N=70.
