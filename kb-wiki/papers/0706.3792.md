# Renormalized Fermi hypernetted chain approach in medium-heavy nuclei

> The application of the Correlated basis function theory and of the Fermi hypernetted chain technique, to the description of the ground state of medium-heavy nuclei is reviewed. We discuss how the formalism, originally developed for symmetric nuclear matter, should be changed in order to describe finite nuclear systems, with different number of protons and neutrons. This approach allows us to describe doubly closed shell nuclei by using microscopic nucleon-nucleon interactions. We presents results of numerical calculations done with two-nucleon interactions of Argonne type,implemented with three-body forces of Urbana type. Our results regard ground-state energies, matter, charge and momentum distributions, natural orbits, occupation numbers, quasi-hole wave functions and spectroscopic factors of 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory and Fermi HyperNetted Chain (FHNC) technique, originally developed for symmetric nuclear matter, can be adapted to describe finite, doubly closed shell nuclei with unequal proton and neutron numbers, using microscopic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions supplemented by three-body forces.

## Method

The authors extend the FHNC formalism from infinite nuclear matter to finite systems by introducing state-dependent correlation functions and a single-particle basis in a jj-coupling scheme. They compute ground-state properties using the Renormalized Fermi HyperNetted Chain (RFHNC) approach with Argonne-type two-nucleon potentials (AV8', AV18) and Urbana-type three-nucleon interactions (UIX, UVII). The calculations are performed for several doubly closed shell nuclei, evaluating energies, densities, momentum distributions, and spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies are calculated for 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb using Argonne and Urbana interactions (specific numerical values are not provided in the truncated text).
- The formalism is applied to nuclei with mass numbers ranging from A=12 (12C) to A=208 (208Pb).
- Results include matter, charge, and momentum distributions, natural orbits, occupation numbers, quasi-hole wave functions, and spectroscopic factors for the five target nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the CBF/FHNC approach previously applied to nuclear matter by Fantoni and Pandharipande (1984, 1987) and to light nuclei by Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988) and Co' et al. (1992, 1994). It enables systematic microscopic calculations of ground-state properties for medium-heavy nuclei using realistic NN interactions, complementing Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods (Pieper and Wiringa, 2001) which are limited to lighter systems. The results provide benchmarks for nuclear structure models and input for reactions involving nucleon knockout.
