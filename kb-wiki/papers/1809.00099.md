# Bubble Structure in Magic Nuclei

> The existence of bubble nuclei identified by the central depletion in nucleonic density is studied for the conventional magic N (Z) $=$ 8, 20, 28, 40, 50, 82, 126 isotones (isotopes) and recently speculated magic N $=$ 164, 184, 228 superheavy isotones. Many new bubble nuclei are predicted in all regions. Study of density profiles, form factor, single particle levels and depletion fraction (DF) across the periodic chart reveals that the central depletion is correlated to shell structure and occurs due to unoccupancy in s-orbit (2s, 3s, 4s) and inversion of (2s, 1d) and (3s, 1h) states in nuclei upto Z $\le$ 82. Bubble effect in superheavy region is a signature of the interplay between the Coulomb and nn-interaction and depletion fraction (DF) is found to increase with Z (Coulomb repulsion) and decrease with isospin. Our results are consistent with the available data. The occupancy in s-state in $^{34}$Si increases with temperature which appears to quench the bubble effect.

# Bubble Structure in Magic Nuclei

## Key claim
The paper systematically predicts many new bubble nuclei across all mass regions from light to superheavy, but the central depletion effect is explicitly conditional on unoccupancy of s-orbitals near the Fermi level and, for nuclei up to Z ≤ 82, on shell structure; for superheavy nuclei the bubble effect is driven by the interplay between Coulomb repulsion and nucleon-nucleon interaction, with depletion fraction increasing with Z and decreasing with isospin.

## Method
The authors employ a Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) plus state-dependent Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approach using the TMA parameter set to calculate charge and neutron density profiles, single-particle spectra, charge form factors, and depletion fractions for isotonic and isotopic chains of magic nuclei with N(Z) = 8, 20, 28, 40, 50, 82, N = 126, and superheavy N = 164, 184, 228 isotones. For superheavy nuclei, deformation is included using axially deformed shapes. Temperature effects on the bubble nucleus ³⁴Si are studied using the Statistical Theory of hot nuclei.

## Key numbers
- ³⁴Si shows a depletion fraction (DF) of 25.23% with the TMA parameter, with an energy gap of 7 MeV between the 1d₅/₂ and 2s₁/₂ states at Z = 14 in N = 20 isotones.
- ⁴⁶Ar shows a DF of 42.00% (TMA) in N = 28 isotones, with inversion of 2s₁/₂ and 1d₃/₂ states separated by more than 3 MeV.
- ²⁵¹Fr (Z = 87, N = 164) shows the highest DF of 36.45% (TMA) among all heavy systems studied, making it the strongest bubble in heavy systems.
- ²²Si (N = 8 isotone) shows DF = 14.38% (TMA) for the proton bubble; its mirror ²²O shows neutron DF = 31.37% (TMA).
- ³⁴Ca (mirror of ³⁴Si) shows neutron DF = 40.61% (TMA).
- ²⁰⁶Hg (N = 126 isotone) shows inversion of 3s₁/₂ and 1h₁₁/₂ states leading to unoccupied 3s₁/₂ in all Z = 48–78 isotones.
- The bubble effect in ³⁴Si completely vanishes at a critical temperature T = 4 MeV, with occupancy in the 2s₁/₂ state increasing from nearly 0 at T = 0 to much higher values as T increases from 0.5 to 3.0 MeV.
- For N = 20 isotones, the energy gap between 2s₁/₂ and 1d₃/₂ decreases to approximately 0.2 MeV at Z = 14, leading to semi-occupancy and semi-bubble nuclei ³⁶S and ³⁸Ar.

## Context
This work extends the study of bubble nuclei from experimentally known cases such as ³⁴Si (reported by Wilson et al., Nature 2017) and ²⁰⁶Hg to a systematic survey across all magic numbers, including superheavy magic numbers N = 164, 184, 228 previously identified as magic by Saxena et al. and other groups. The paper builds on prior RMF calculations by Khan et al., Grasso et al., and Wang et al., and addresses the quenching effects of pairing correlations (Grasso et al., Yao et al.) and temperature (Tan et al.) on bubble structure. The results enable future experimental searches using facilities such as SCRIT and RIBF, with specific predictions for experimentally accessible candidates like ⁹²Zr, ³⁴Si, and ⁴⁶Ar.
