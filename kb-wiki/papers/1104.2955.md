# Chiral three-nucleon forces and pairing in nuclei

> We present the first study of pairing in nuclei including three-nucleon forces. We perform systematic calculations of the odd-even mass staggering generated using a microscopic pairing interaction at first order in chiral low-momentum interactions. Significant repulsive contributions from the leading chiral three-nucleon forces are found. Two- and three-nucleon interactions combined account for approximately 70% of the experimental pairing gaps, which leaves room for self-energy and induced interaction effects that are expected to be overall attractive in nuclei.

## Key claim

The first-order contribution from chiral three-nucleon (3N) forces to the pairing interaction in nuclei is repulsive and reduces theoretical pairing gaps by about 30%, so that two- and three-nucleon interactions combined account for approximately 70% of experimental pairing gaps, leaving room for higher-order attractive contributions from self-energy and induced interaction effects.

## Method

The authors performed systematic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations of odd-even mass staggering (three-point mass differences) for semi-magic spherical nuclei, using a first-order pairing kernel constructed from low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions. The NN interaction was the N³LO potential evolved via renormalization group to low-momentum interactions \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) with cutoffs \(\Lambda = 1.8-2.8\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), while the leading N²LO chiral 3N forces were included by normal-ordering them into a density-dependent two-body interaction \(\overline{V}_{\text{3N}}\) summed over occupied states in the Fermi sea. The normal self-energy part of the nuclear energy density functional was taken from a semi-empirical Skyrme parameterization with effective masses constrained by microscopic Hartree-Fock results from low-momentum NN and 3N interactions.

## Key numbers

- Two- and three-nucleon interactions combined account for approximately 70% of the experimental pairing gaps.
- The addition of first-order 3N contributions lowers pairing gaps systematically by about 30%.
- For lead isotopes with \(N>140\), the 3N reduction of gaps is \(-30\%\) average relative shift; for \(N<140\), it is \(-35\%\).
- For \(N=126\) isotones, the 3N reduction is \(-43\%\) for \(Z<70\) and \(-35\%\) for \(Z>70\).
- At the NN-only level, pairing gaps vary by approximately 100–200 keV over the NN cutoff range \(\Lambda = 1.8-2.8\,\text{fm}^{-1}\); including 3N forces, theoretical uncertainties are approximately 100–250 keV.
- The density-dependent two-body interaction \(\overline{V}_{\text{3N}}\) was fitted for Fermi momenta \(k_F^\tau\) from \(0.6\) to \(1.6\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), describing matrix elements to better than \(0.01\,\text{fm}\).
- Finite nuclei calculations used a spherical box of 24 fm radius with mesh size 0.3 fm and momentum cutoff \(k_{\text{cut}} = 4.0\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), ensuring convergence of pairing gaps to a fraction of a keV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Duguet et al. (2008, 2009) and Lesinski et al. (2009) that explored pairing contributions from Coulomb and NN interactions only, by including for the first time chiral three-nucleon forces for pairing in nuclei. The results show that first-order low-momentum NN+3N interactions leave about 30% room for higher-order contributions from induced interactions (e.g., coupling to density, spin, and isospin fluctuations), consistent with earlier work by Terasaki et al. (2002), Barranco et al. (2004), Gori et al. (2005), and Pastore et al. (2008) finding induced interactions to be overall attractive. The developed non-empirical pairing energy functional can next be applied to deformed nuclei, where phenomenological studies by Bertsch et al. (2008) and Yamagami et al. (2008) indicate different agreement between theory and experiment compared to spherical nuclei.
