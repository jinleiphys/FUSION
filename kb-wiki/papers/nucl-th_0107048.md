# Single-Particle Transfer and Nuclear Supersymmetry. Pick-Up and Stripping with SUSY

> Transfer reactions constitute a stringent test for nuclear supersymmetry, a theory that simultaneously describes neighboring nuclei with bosonic and fermionic character. We construct and analytically evaluate one-nucleon transfer matrix elements between supersymmetric partners with the U(6/4) case as an example, and stress the need for a careful treatment of bosonic and fermionic operators in the construction of mixed tensor operators.

# Single-Particle Transfer and Nuclear Supersymmetry. Pick-Up and Stripping with SUSY

## Key claim

One-nucleon transfer matrix elements between supersymmetric partners can be constructed and analytically evaluated using the U(6/4) dynamical supersymmetry scheme, but the theoretical analysis requires careful treatment of phase ambiguities that arise because the standard realization of the Spin(6) algebra combines a symmetric boson quadrupole operator with an antisymmetric fermion quadrupole operator.

## Method

The authors constructed one-nucleon transfer operators as tensor operators under the Spin(6) ⊃ Spin(5) ⊃ Spin(3) subgroup chain of the U(6/4) supersymmetry, using the example of proton transfer between the even-even nucleus 194Pt and the odd-even nucleus 195Au. They derived the explicit form of the transfer operators for both the antisymmetric and symmetric realizations of the SO(6) algebra, applying a canonical transformation to relate the two realizations and obtain the correct tensorial classification of boson creation and annihilation operators.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of 195Au is excited strongly with C²S = 0.175 in proton stripping reactions 194Pt(α,t)195Au and 194Pt(³He,d)195Au
- The first excited J=3/2 state of 195Au is excited weakly with C²S = 0.019 in the same reactions
- The transfer operator coefficients for the symmetric realization include factors of √(1/6) and √(5/6) for the (1/2,1/2,-1/2) representation, and √(5/6) and √(1/6) for the (3/2,1/2,1/2) representation

## Context

This work extends the supersymmetric classification of nuclear levels in the Pt and Au isotopes, which was re-examined following high-resolution transfer experiments at TU/LMU Munchen (Metz, 1999) and in-beam gamma ray spectroscopy at PSI and Bonn (Au196, 2000). The paper builds on the original supersymmetry proposals by Iachello (1980) and the extension to quartets of nuclei by Van Isacker (1981), specifically addressing the quartet 194Pt, 195Au, 195Pt, and 196Au. The results enable quantitative predictions for spectroscopic factors in one-nucleon transfer reactions between supersymmetric partners, which the authors identify as the most stringent remaining test of nuclear supersymmetry, and they call for new experiments exploiting improved experimental capabilities.
