# Particle correlations at RHIC from parton coalescence dynamics -- first results

> A new dynamical approach that combines covariant parton transport theory with hadronization channels via parton coalescence and fragmentation is applied to Au+Au at RHIC. Basic consequences of the simple coalescence formulas, such as elliptic flow scaling and enhanced proton/pion ratio, turn out to be rather sensitive to the spacetime aspects of coalescence dynamics.

# Particle correlations at RHIC from parton coalescence dynamics -- first results

## Key claim

The simple coalescence formulas that successfully reproduced RHIC data in earlier studies are highly sensitive to spacetime aspects of coalescence dynamics; when a dynamical approach with realistic freezeout is used, the baryon enhancement over mesons disappears and elliptic flow scaling is significantly reduced, contrary to expectations from static coalescence models.

## Method

The author implemented the Gyulassy-Frankel-Remler (GFR) formalism for coalescence into the Molnar Parton Cascade (MPC) 1.6.7, a covariant parton transport model, applied to Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. Parton evolution with 2-to-2 Debye-screened cross sections (sigma_gg = 3 and 10 mb) was run until freezeout, then GFR coalescence was applied using box Wigner functions with x_m = 1 fm; partons not coalescing were fragmented via JETSET 7.4.10, and unstable hadrons were decayed by JETSET.

## Key numbers

- Parton initial conditions used total parton dN(b=0)/dy = 2000 at midrapidity, motivated by observed dN_ch/dy ~ 600
- Two parton cross sections were studied: sigma_gg = 3 mb and sigma_gg = 10 mb
- For sigma_gg = 10 mb, pion elliptic flow v2 from coalescence was reduced by 20% relative to simple flow scaling expectations
- For sigma_gg = 10 mb, proton elliptic flow v2 from coalescence was reduced by 40% relative to simple flow scaling expectations
- The coalescence enhancement for both pions and protons was largest in the intermediate p_perp window 2 < p_perp < 4-5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the parton coalescence studies of Hwa and Yang (2003), Voloshin (2003), and the Duke group (2003) by replacing their static hadronization hypersurface assumptions with a dynamical transport approach. It addresses the unitarity violation in simple coalescence formulas noted by previous authors and the need for self-consistent freezeout distributions. The results call into question whether the quark number scaling of elliptic flow observed at RHIC can be preserved when realistic coalescence dynamics are included, and motivate further studies to determine what conditions are needed to maintain the basic features of the simple coalescence picture.
