# Direct photons from Au+Au collisions at RHIC: QGP vs. hot hadronic gas

> We have analysed the preliminary PHENIX data on the transverse momentum distribution of direct photons in 0-20% centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV. In ideal hydrodynamics, data are explained if Au+Au collision produces Quark-Gluon-Plasma at the temperature $T_i$=400 MeV, at an initial time $τ_i$=0.6 fm. PHENIX data are not explained in the alternate scenario when Au+Au collisions produces hot hadronic gas with initial temperature within physically acceptable limit.

# Direct photons from Au+Au collisions at RHIC: QGP vs. hot hadronic gas

## Key claim
The preliminary PHENIX direct photon data in 0-20% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV are explained by ideal hydrodynamics only if the collision produces a Quark-Gluon Plasma with initial temperature Ti=400 MeV at initial time τi=0.6 fm; the data are not explained if the collision produces a hot hadronic gas with initial temperature ≤220 MeV and initial time ≤5 fm, which the author argues is the physically acceptable upper limit for a hadronic state.

## Method
The author solved ideal hydrodynamic equations (∂μTμν=0) for a baryon-free, boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric fluid, using a bag model equation of state for the QGP phase (with bag constant B determined by Gibbs condition at Tc=180 MeV) and a hadronic equation of state including all mesonic resonances below 2 GeV. Photon emission rates from QGP and from hot hadronic gas (including processes such as ππ→ργ, πρ→πγ, ω→πγ, ρ→ππγ, and πρ→A1→πγ) were integrated over the space-time evolution, and the sum of thermal and pQCD hard photons was compared to the PHENIX preliminary data for three initial temperatures (350, 400, 450 MeV) in the QGP scenario and for initial temperature 220 MeV at three thermalisation times (3, 4, 5 fm) in the hadronic gas scenario.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature Ti=400 MeV with initial time τi=0.6 fm reproduces the PHENIX preliminary direct photon data in 0-20% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV.
- Initial temperature Ti=350 MeV underpredicts the data; Ti=450 MeV overpredicts the data.
- For the hot hadronic gas scenario at Ti=220 MeV with τi=5 fm, the data remain underpredicted by a factor of two or more.
- The critical temperature for confinement-deconfinement is taken as Tc=180 MeV; freeze-out temperature is TF=100 MeV.
- The average number of binary collisions in 0-20% centrality is ⟨Nbinary⟩≈779, corresponding to an effective nucleus Aeff≈143 with radius Reff=5.85 fm.
- Three central energy densities were used: ε(r=0)=27.8, 47.1, and 75.1 GeV/fm³, corresponding to Ti=0.35, 0.40, and 0.45 GeV respectively.
- The hadronic gas at Ti=220 MeV has density ρhadron≈1.7 fm⁻³, below the limit ρhadron<2 fm⁻³ for hadrons to retain their identity.

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic analyses of RHIC data (Kolb 2003, Kolb 2001) that had previously described hadron spectra and elliptic flow, to the new PHENIX preliminary direct photon measurements (Buesching 2005) which showed an excess over pQCD predictions (Jager 2002). The analysis directly compares two scenarios: QGP formation versus hot hadronic gas, motivated by earlier SPS results (Santo 1994, Aggarwal 2000) where direct photon data were later explained without QGP (Chaudhuri 1995, Chaudhuri 2000). The conclusion that only the QGP scenario fits the data enables using direct photons as a probe of the initial temperature of the matter produced at RHIC, and challenges the alternative hadronic interpretation.
