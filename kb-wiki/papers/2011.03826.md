# Influence of modified light-flavor hadron spectra on particle yields in the statistical hadronization model

> Hadron production in relativistic nuclear collisions is well described in the framework of the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM). We investigate the influence on SHM predictions of hadron mass spectra for light-flavor baryons and mesons modified by the addition of about 500 new states as predicted by lattice QCD and a relativistic quark model. The deterioration of the resulting thermodynamic fit quality obtained for PbPb collision data at sqrt(s_nn) = 2.76 TeV suggests that the additional states are not suited to be naively used since also interactions among the states as well as non-resonant contributions need to be considered in the SHM approach. Incorporating these effects via the pion nucleon interaction determined from measured phase shifts leads again to excellent reproduction of the experimental data. This is a strong indication that at least the additional nucleon excited states cannot be understood and used as independent resonances.

## Key claim

Adding about 500 new light-flavor hadron states predicted by lattice QCD and a relativistic quark model to the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) particle list significantly worsens the fit to Pb–Pb collision data at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, but after applying an S-matrix correction for the pion–nucleon interaction the fit quality is fully restored, indicating that the additional nucleon excited states cannot be treated as independent resonances.

## Method

The authors performed thermal fits using the SHM with three progressively extended hadron mass spectra: first adding non-strange baryon states (N* and Δ*) from lattice QCD (Edwards 2011), then adding strange baryons from lattice QCD (Edwards 2012), and finally adding mesons from a relativistic quark model (Ebert 2009). They compared the fit quality (χ²/N_dof) and extracted chemical freeze-out temperature T_CF for each spectrum against the standard PDG-based spectrum, using ALICE data for 22 particle species from central Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV. They then applied an S-matrix correction based on measured πN phase shifts to account for non-resonant and repulsive interactions.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_CF = 156.5 MeV for the original PDG spectrum, dropping by about 4 MeV (to ~152.5 MeV) for the spectra with additional states.
- Reduced χ² increases from 1.6 (original PDG) to about 8 for the spectrum with added non-strange baryons ('udB'), then drops slightly to just above 7 for the spectra with added strange baryons and mesons ('udsB' and 'udsBM').
- After S-matrix correction, the reduced χ² for the original PDG spectrum drops from 29/19 ≈ 1.5 to 16.9/19 ≈ 0.9; for the 'udB' spectrum the fit quality becomes identical to the PDG case.
- The proton yield increases by 50–60% due to additional states (driven by low-mass N* resonances below the Roper resonance N*(1440)), while the pion density changes by less than 10% and kaons by even less.
- Removing three particularly low-lying N* states raises T_CF by 2 MeV and reduces χ² from 7.15 to 4.3.

## Context

This work extends previous SHM studies (Andronic 2017) that used only experimentally established hadrons from the PDG compilation, and earlier investigations (Andronic 2008) that estimated the effect of missing states assuming Hagedorn-type growth. It directly tests the suggestion from lattice QCD (Bazavov 2014) to supplement experimental hadron spectra with theory input. The S-matrix correction applied here follows the approach of Lo (2017) and Andronic (2018), which resolved the proton yield discrepancy in the standard SHM. The results show that the extraction of chemical freeze-out parameters from mean hadron yields is robust against adding many states, but raise questions about whether this holds for higher moments of multiplicity distributions.
