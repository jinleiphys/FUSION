# `$f_0 (600)$' and chiral dynamics

> The role of light scalar meson `$f_0 (600)$' is investigated in nuclear matter in an Effective chiral model in the mean-field approach. For the purpose, we scan the properties of the matter at various saturation densities imposing constraint such as the vacuum value of the pion decay constant $f_π \approx 131 ~MeV$. With a simple approach, the bound on the mass of the scalar meson is calculated and found in the range $m_σ = 546 \pm 10 MeV$. Further, the present analysis show that nuclear matter favor high nucleon effective mass and dominant repulsive forces at high density, the insight and the implications of which are discussed.

# `$f_0 (600)$' and chiral dynamics

## Key claim
The mass of the scalar meson `$f_0(600)$` (also called the $\sigma$-meson) is constrained to $m_\sigma = 546 \pm 10$ MeV by requiring that the model simultaneously satisfy the vacuum pion decay constant $f_\pi \approx 131$ MeV and nuclear matter saturation properties, but this result holds only within a narrow range of saturation densities $\rho_0 = 0.155 \pm 0.005$ fm$^{-3}$ and depends on including nonlinear scalar field interactions.

## Method
The author uses an effective chiral Lagrangian with dynamically generated vector meson mass, including nonlinear terms up to quartic order in the scalar field, solved in the relativistic mean-field approximation. Model parameters (scalar, vector, and isovector coupling constants plus higher-order scalar field coefficients) are determined by imposing nuclear matter saturation at densities $\rho_0 = 0.12$–$0.18$ fm$^{-3}$ with binding energy per nucleon $\approx -16$ MeV, the vacuum pion decay constant $f_\pi = 131$ MeV, and the symmetry energy $J = 32$ MeV. The scalar meson mass is then extracted from the relation $m_\sigma = m \sqrt{C_\omega / C_\sigma}$ using the fitted couplings.

## Key numbers
- Scalar meson mass range: $m_\sigma = 546 \pm 10$ MeV, obtained for saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.155 \pm 0.005$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Pion decay constant constraint: $f_\pi \approx 131$ MeV, imposed as the vacuum expectation value of the scalar field.
- Nucleon effective mass ratio: $m^\star/m = 0.79$–$0.87$ for the nonlinear model across $\rho_0 = 0.12$–$0.18$ fm$^{-3}$; at $\rho_0 = 0.155$ fm$^{-3}$, $m^\star/m \approx 0.84$, corresponding to an $\approx 18\%$ drop from the free nucleon mass.
- Nuclear incompressibility: $K = 354$–$398$ MeV for $\rho_0 = 0.155 \pm 0.005$ fm$^{-3}$ in the nonlinear model; the linear model gives $K \approx 665$ MeV at $\rho_0 = 0.155$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Saturation density range explored: $\rho_0 = 0.12$–$0.18$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Binding energy per nucleon: $-16.3$ MeV for all nonlinear parameter sets.
- Scalar and vector potentials at $\rho_0 = 0.155$ fm$^{-3}$: $U_s \approx -160$ MeV, $U_v \approx 99$ MeV (nonlinear); for the linear model at the same density: $U_s \approx -207$ MeV, $U_v \approx 147$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral sigma-model approach of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960) and Lee and Wick (1974) by incorporating higher-order scalar field interactions and dynamically generated vector meson mass, following earlier work by Boguta (1983) and Glendenning. The analysis is compared with the relativistic mean-field NL3 parameterization (which gives $m^\star/m = 0.60$ at $\rho_0 = 0.148$ fm$^{-3}$), and with experimental constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data (incompressibility $K = 167$–$380$ MeV) and the BES II measurement of the $\sigma$ meson mass ($m_\sigma = 541 \pm 39$ MeV). The results enable improved modeling of neutron star matter at densities $(3$–$10)\rho_0$, where the predicted high nucleon effective mass and dominant repulsive forces may have astrophysical implications.
