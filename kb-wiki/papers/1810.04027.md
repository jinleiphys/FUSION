# Low-Energy Lepton-Proton Bremsstrahlung via Effective Field Theory

> We present a systematic calculation of the cross section for the lepton-proton bremsstrahlung process l + p --> l' + p + gamma in chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. This process corresponds to an undetected background signal for the proposed MUSE experiment at PSI. MUSE is designed to measure elastic scattering of low-energy electrons and muons off a proton target in order to extract a precise value of the proton's r.m.s. radius. We show that the commonly used peaking approximation, which is used to evaluate the radiative tail for the elastic cross section, is not applicable for muon-proton scattering at the low-energy MUSE kinematics. Furthermore, we point out a certain pathology with the standard chiral power counting scheme associated with electron scattering, whereby the next-to-next-to-leading order contribution from the pion loop diagrams is kinematically enhanced and numerically of the same magnitude as the next-to-leading order corrections. We correct a misprint in a commonly cited review article.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the commonly used peaking approximation for evaluating the radiative tail of elastic lepton-proton scattering is not applicable for muon-proton scattering at the low energies of the proposed MUSE experiment, and identifies a pathology in standard chiral power counting where NNLO pion loop contributions are kinematically enhanced for electron scattering to the same magnitude as NLO corrections.

## Method

The authors perform a systematic calculation of the lepton-proton bremsstrahlung cross section using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (χPT) up to next-to-leading order, including both kinematic and dynamical proton recoil corrections. They evaluate the LO amplitude from the leading-order χPT Lagrangian and the NLO contributions from the NLO Lagrangian containing the proton's anomalous magnetic moments, using the Coulomb gauge and a reference frame where the momentum transfer vector defines the z-axis to allow analytical integration over the photon azimuthal angle.

## Key numbers

- The MUSE experiment aims for a proton r.m.s. radius uncertainty of about 0.01 fm
- The chiral scale Λχ is approximately 1 GeV, where fπ = 93 MeV is the pion decay constant
- The nucleon axial vector coupling constant gA = 1.26
- The nucleon iso-vector anomalous magnetic moment κv = 3.71
- The nucleon iso-scalar anomalous magnetic moment κs = -0.12
- The iso-scalar radius r1s = 0.782 fm and iso-vector radius (r1v)2 = 0.585 fm2 are used to fix low-energy constants

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of low-energy lepton-proton bremsstrahlung beyond the peaking approximation developed by Maximon (1969) and Tsai (1961, 1969), which is valid for highly relativistic electrons but questionable for muon scattering at MUSE kinematics. The results corroborate the analysis by Ent (2001) showing that the peaking approximation leads to significant errors at lower energies. The calculation enables accurate background subtraction for the MUSE experiment at PSI, which aims to resolve the proton radius puzzle arising from discrepancies between muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Pohl 2010) and electron-proton scattering results (Bernauer 2010).
