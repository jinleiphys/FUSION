# Field theory of nucleon to higher-spin baryon transitions

> We discuss the nucleon to higher-spin $N$- and $Δ$-resonance transitions by pions and photons. The higher-spin baryons are described by Rarita-Schwinger fields and, as we argue, this imposes a stringent consistency requirement on the form of the couplings. Popular $πNΔ$ and $γNΔ$ couplings are inconsistent from this point of view. We construct examples of consistent interactions with the same nonrelativistic limit as the conventional ones.

## Key claim

The conventional $\pi N\Delta$ and $\gamma N\Delta$ couplings widely used in the literature are inconsistent because they violate the gauge symmetries of the free Rarita-Schwinger theory, activating unphysical lower-spin degrees of freedom; the authors construct alternative gauge-invariant interactions that become equivalent to the conventional ones at the $\Delta$-pole and in the nonrelativistic limit, thereby providing a consistent field-theoretic description.

## Method

The authors analyze the free Rarita-Schwinger (RS) formalism for higher-spin baryons, using a theorem that links local symmetries to the number of first-class constraints to count degrees of freedom. They then construct explicit gauge-invariant (GI) interactions for $\pi N\Delta$ and $\gamma N\Delta$ transitions using the RS field tensor $G^{\mu\nu}=\partial^\mu\psi^\nu-\partial^\nu\psi^\mu$ and its dual, imposing pole equivalence to relate the new coupling constants to the conventional ones. The consistency is demonstrated by showing that the $\Delta$-exchange amplitude becomes proportional to the spin-3/2 projection operator, decoupling the unphysical spin-1/2 sector.

## Key numbers

- The free massless RS field of spin $s=j+1/2$ has $N_{\rm comp} = 6j(j+1)+4$ independent components; for spin-3/2 ($j=1$), this gives $N_{\rm comp}=16$ components.
- For the massless spin-3/2 RS theory, the number of first-class constraints is $N_I=8$ and second-class constraints $N_{I\!I}=12$, satisfying the physical DOF count $2N_{\rm comp} - 2N_I - N_{I\!I} = 4$ (two helicity states for particle and antiparticle).
- The gauge-invariant $\pi N\Delta$ coupling constant is identified as $f = f_{\pi N\Delta}/(m_\pi M)$, where $f_{\pi N\Delta}$ is the conventional coupling and $m_\pi$, $M$ are the pion and $\Delta$ masses.
- For the $\gamma N\Delta$ couplings, the pole equivalence gives $g_3 = G_1/(2mM)$ relating the new $g_3$ coupling to the conventional $G_1$ coupling, with $m$ the nucleon mass.
- The magnetic-dipole and electric-quadrupole transition form factors at the $\Delta$-pole are given by $3G_M = 2m(M+m)g_1 - m(M-m)g_2 + m(3M+m)(g_3+g_4)$ and $3G_E = m(M-m)(g_3+g_4-g_2)$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of inconsistencies in higher-spin field theories previously discussed by Nath et al. (1971), Hagen (1971), Singh (1973), Benmerrouche et al. (1989), and Pascalutsa (1998), who identified pathologies in the conventional $\pi N\Delta$ and $\gamma N\Delta$ couplings. The authors provide a systematic remedy by constructing gauge-invariant interactions that preserve the local symmetries of the free massless RS theory, following the general possibility pointed out by Weinberg and Witten (1980). This enables consistent theoretical studies of meson-, photo-, and electroproduction of $N^*$-resonances, with recent calculations by Lahiff and Afnan (1999) and Pascalutsa and Tjon (1999) showing large qualitative differences from the conventional approach in $\pi N$ scattering.
