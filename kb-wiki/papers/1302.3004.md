# Coulomb breakup reactions of $^{11}$Li in the coupled-channel $^9$Li~+~$n$~+~$n$ three-body model

> We investigate the three-body Coulomb breakup of a two-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Li. We use the coupled-channel $^9$Li + $n$ + $n$ three-body model, which includes the coupling between last neutron states and the various $2p$-$2h$ configurations in $^9$Li due to the tensor and pairing correlations. The three-body scattering states of $^{11}$Li are described by using the combined methods of the complex scaling and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The calculated breakup cross section successfully reproduces the experiments. The large mixing of the s-state in the halo ground state of $^{11}$Li is shown to play an important role in explanation of shape and strength of the breakup cross section. In addition, we predict the invariant mass spectra for binary subsystems of $^{11}$Li. It is found that the two kinds of virtual s-states of $^9$Li-$n$ and $n$-$n$ systems in the final three-body states of $^{11}$Li largely contribute to make low-lying peaks in the invariant mass spectra. On the other hand, in the present analysis, it is suggested that the contributions of the p-wave resonances of $^{10}$Li is hardly confirmed in the spectra.

## Key claim

The coupled-channel $^9$Li + $n$ + $n$ three-body model, which includes tensor and pairing correlations in the $^9$Li core via $2p$-$2h$ configurations, successfully reproduces the observed Coulomb breakup cross section of $^{11}$Li, resolving a previous discrepancy where simpler three-body models could not simultaneously explain the charge radius and dipole strength.

## Method

The authors describe the three-body scattering states of $^{11}$Li using the combined methods of complex scaling and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (CSLS). The $^9$Li core is treated with the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) including $2p$-$2h$ configurations from tensor and pairing correlations, and the last two neutrons are described using a hybrid-VT variational approach with Gaussian basis functions. The Coulomb breakup cross section is calculated from the $E1$ transition strength using the equivalent photon method with a Pb target at 70 MeV/nucleon incident energy.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$ of $^{11}$Li: 377 keV (theoretical), compared to experimental 378 $\pm$ 5 keV
- Matter radius $R_m$ of $^{11}$Li: 3.39 fm (theoretical), compared to experimental values 3.12 $\pm$ 0.16 fm, 3.53 $\pm$ 0.06 fm, and 3.71 $\pm$ 0.20 fm
- Charge radius $R_{ch}$ of $^{11}$Li: 2.43 fm (theoretical), compared to experimental 2.467 $\pm$ 0.037 fm and 2.423 $\pm$ 0.034 fm
- $(s_{1/2})^2$ probability in $^{11}$Li ground state: 44.0% (theoretical), compared to experimental 45 $\pm$ 10%
- $(p_{1/2})^2$ probability in $^{11}$Li ground state: 46.9%
- $p_{1/2}$ resonance energies in $^{10}$Li: 275 keV ($1^+$) and 506 keV ($2^+$) with decay widths 150 keV and 388 keV, respectively
- Scattering length $a_s$ for $^{10}$Li($2^-$): -45.0 fm, compared to experimental $-30^{+12}_{-31}$ fm
- Low-lying enhancement in Coulomb breakup cross section peaks at approximately 0.25 MeV above the $^9$Li + $n$ + $n$ threshold

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel $^9$Li + $n$ + $n$ model developed by Myo et al. (2007, 2008) to the Coulomb breakup reaction, building on previous CSLS applications to $^6$He breakup by Kikuchi et al. (2009, 2010). The study resolves a problem identified by Esbensen et al. (2007), who noted that simpler three-body models could not simultaneously explain the charge radius and dipole strength of $^{11}$Li. The results enable detailed analysis of binary subsystem correlations ($^9$Li-$n$ and $n$-$n$) in the final three-body states and predict invariant mass spectra for experimental verification.
