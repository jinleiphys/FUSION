# Two-Fermion Bound States within the Bethe-Salpeter Approach

> To solve the spinor-spinor Bethe-Salpeter equation in Euclidean space we propose a novel method related to the use of hyperspherical harmonics. We suggest an appropriate extension to form a new basis of spin-angular harmonics that is suitable for a representation of the vertex functions. We present a numerical algorithm to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation and investigate in detail the properties of the solution for the scalar, pseudoscalar and vector meson exchange kernels including the stability of bound states. We also compare our results to the non relativistic ones and to the results given by light front dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel method for solving the spinor-spinor Bethe-Salpeter equation in Euclidean space using hyperspherical harmonics, claiming that this approach reduces the four-dimensional integral equation to a system of one-dimensional integral equations that converges rapidly (typically three to four terms suffice for high accuracy), though the authors note that more terms are needed for low binding energy and very light exchanged particles approaching zero mass.

## Method

The authors decompose the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function and interaction kernel into hyperspherical harmonics, which include a hyperangle variable in addition to the standard spherical angles, allowing analytical integration over three of the four momentum-space variables. For the spinor-spinor case, they construct a new basis of spin-angular harmonics that avoids mixing of angular and hyperangular dependence, enabling the same hyperspherical decomposition technique. The resulting system of one-dimensional integral equations for the expansion coefficients is solved numerically for scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector meson exchange kernels in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels.

## Key numbers

* The hyperspherical expansion requires only the first three to four terms in the decomposition to assure sufficiently high accuracy of the solution, except for low binding energy and very light exchanged particles where more terms are needed.
* In the \(^1S_0\) channel, the vertex function decomposition requires four basis matrices (\(n_\mathrm{max}=4\)); in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel, eight basis matrices are needed (\(n_\mathrm{max}=8\)).
* The interaction kernel expansion uses the coefficient \(V_n(\tilde p,\tilde k) = 4/(\Lambda_+ + \Lambda_-)^2 \cdot ((\Lambda_+ - \Lambda_-)/(\Lambda_+ + \Lambda_-))^n\), where \(\Lambda_\pm = \sqrt{(\tilde p \pm \tilde k)^2 + \mu^2}\).
* The propagator scalar part is \(S(k_4,|\bk|) = 1/[(k^2 + m^2 - M^2/4)^2 + M^2 k_4^2]\).

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical harmonics approach previously applied to scalar Bethe-Salpeter equations (Dorkin et al., 2005; 2006) to the spinor-spinor case, building on the foundational Bethe-Salpeter equation studies by Tjon (1975) and the two-dimensional Gaussian mesh methods of Rupp and Tjon (1979) and Umnikov (1994). The method is partially stimulated by results reported in Karmanov (1996) and enables more efficient numerical solutions for deuteron and meson bound states, with potential applications to electromagnetic form factors and scattering reactions that challenge existing nonrelativistic approaches.
