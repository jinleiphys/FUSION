# Elliptic Flow in Central Collisions of Deformed Nuclei

## Key claim
Non-trivial geometrical effects in relativistic central collisions of deformed nuclei are studied using a simple version of optical Glauber model. For very small impact parameters large centrality and eccentricity fluctuations are observed, and in very high multiplicity collisions of oblate nuclei (e.g. Au and Cu) a significant fraction of events with elliptic flow strength v_2 dependent on oblateness beta_2 is predicted, though the author notes that for unpolarized beams and targets the relative azimuthal orientation of nuclear spins is nearly random, so the measured v_2 contains an averaged contribution from different orientations.

## Method
The author performed an optical Glauber model (OGM) calculation for deformed nuclei, using deformed Woods-Saxon densities with deformation parameters beta_2 and beta_4 taken from MollerB2 for various nuclei (Au, Cu, Ho, Pb, Si, Ca). Transversal projections of nucleon density were generated at fixed polar and azimuthal angles, then shifted for selected impact parameter values to evaluate the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions and participant eccentricity. The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section was fixed at 42 mb, and binning of the density histograms was 0.1 fm with longitudinal integration steps of 0.05 fm.

## Key numbers
- For transversally polarized Ho-165 (prolate, beta_2 approx 0.3) colliding with spherical Pb-207 at b=0 fm, the participant eccentricity epsilon_p is non-zero and roughly corresponds to non-central Pb+Pb collisions at b = 4 fm.
- For increasing impact parameter in the direction orthogonal to the Ho-165 spin (b_x,0), eccentricity further increases; for increasing (0,b_y), eccentricity first decreases to zero at b_y approx 4 fm and then increases.
- For Si-29+Si-29 collisions with beta_2 = -0.3 and beta_4 = 0.13, the maximal number of binary collisions N_coll for longitudinally+transversally polarized collisions is approx 80 (at b=0 fm) with eccentricity tilde_epsilon_NN approx 0.15.
- The probability of a collision with deformed nuclei axes oriented at relative azimuthal angle 0 < Delta_phi < pi/2 is constant: P(Delta_phi) = const.
- The probability of a collision with polar angles theta_1 and theta_2 is P(theta_1, theta_2) = sin(theta_1) sin(theta_2) / 4.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear deformation effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which had previously been considered primarily in fusion reactions at the Coulomb barrier (CF) and in the context of highest energy densities from longitudinally oriented prolate nuclei (Shuryak). The author points out that elliptic flow measurements at RHIC (RHICv2) have interpreted results assuming spherical nuclei, and that the simple geometric relation between centrality and impact parameter from Bron and Flor is not valid for deformed nuclei. The results enable future studies comparing Au+Au collisions with spherical nuclei such as Pb-207 at the same energy, and suggest that elliptic flow in very high multiplicity central collisions of oblate nuclei could reveal changes in the equation of state of QCD matter, including possible kink-like signatures from phase transitions (Sorge, myKink).
