# A Generalisation For The Infinite Integral Over Three Spherical Bessel Functions

> A new formula is derived that generalises an earlier result for the infinite integral over three spherical Bessel functions. The analytical result involves a finite sum over associated Legendre functions, $P_l^m(x)$, of degree $l$ and order $m$. The sum allows for values of $|m|$ that are greater than $l$. A generalisation for the associated Legendre functions to allow for any rational $m$ for a specific $l$ is also shown

## Key claim

The paper derives a new analytical formula for the infinite integral over three spherical Bessel functions that generalizes an earlier result by Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane (1991). The new formula works only when the wavenumbers \(k_1\), \(k_2\), and \(k_3\) satisfy the triangular condition, unlike the earlier result where the triangular condition forced the wavenumbers to form the sides of a triangle.

## Method

The authors start from the known result for the integral over three spherical Bessel functions with \(\lambda = 0\) (Mehrem et al., 1991), multiply both sides by \(k_3^{\lambda_3+2}\), and integrate over \(k_3\) from 0 to \(K\). Using a standard integral from Gradshteyn and Rhyzik (1994) and a change of variables, the right-hand side becomes an integral over the Legendre polynomial \(P_l(\Delta)\), which is expressed in terms of the associated Legendre function \(P_l^{-1}(\Delta^\prime)\). Repeated integration then yields the general result for arbitrary integer \(\lambda\), expressed as a finite sum over associated Legendre functions \(P_l^{-\lambda}(\Delta)\) combined with 3j and 6j symbols.

## Key numbers

- The integral \(I(\lambda; \lambda_1, \lambda_2, \lambda_3; k_1, k_2, k_3)\) is given by equation (TWOPTEN), which involves a factor \((1-\Delta^2)^{\lambda/2}\) and a sum over associated Legendre functions \(P_l^{-\lambda}(\Delta)\).
- For the special case \(\lambda_3 = 0\) and \(\lambda_1 = \lambda_2 = \lambda^\prime\), the result reduces to \(I(\lambda; \lambda^\prime, \lambda^\prime, 0; k_1, k_2, k_3) = \frac{\pi \beta(\Delta)}{4 k_1 k_2 k_3} \left( \frac{k_1 k_2}{k_3} \right)^\lambda (1-\Delta^2)^{\lambda/2} P_{\lambda^\prime}^{-\lambda}(\Delta)\).
- For \(\lambda = 0\), \(\lambda_1 = 0\), \(\lambda_2 = 0\), \(\lambda_3 = 0\): \(I(0;0,0,0;k_1 k_2 k_3) = \frac{1}{4} \frac{\pi \beta(\Delta)}{k_1 k_2 k_3}\).
- For \(\lambda = 1\), \(\lambda_1 = 0\), \(\lambda_2 = 0\), \(\lambda_3 = 0\): \(I(1;0,0,0;k_1 k_2 k_3) = -\frac{1}{16} \frac{\pi \beta(\Delta)}{k_1 k_2 k_3^2} (k_3^2 - 2k_3 c_1 - 2k_3 c_2 + 2k_3 c_3 - k_1^2 - 2k_1 k_2 - k_2^2 + c_1^2 + c_2^2 + c_3^2)\), where \(c_1 = -k_1 + k_2 + k_3\), \(c_2 = k_1 - k_2 + k_3\), \(c_3 = k_1 + k_2 - k_3\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical result for the infinite integral over three spherical Bessel functions derived by Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane (1991), which was limited to \(\lambda = 0\). The new formula generalizes to arbitrary integer \(\lambda\) and involves associated Legendre functions rather than Legendre polynomials. The integral has applications in nuclear scattering theory (White, Davies, and Siemens, 1988; Davies, Strayer, and White, 1988; Davies, 1988; Mehrem, Londergan, and Walker, 1993) and in the angular integration of the homogeneous and velocity isotropic Boltzmann equation (Hohenegger, 2009). The result enables the evaluation of integrals over four spherical Bessel functions that arise in these contexts, reducing highly oscillatory numerical integrals to rapidly converging forms.
