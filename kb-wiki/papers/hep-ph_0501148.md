# Neutrino Electromagnetic Form Factor and Oscillation Effects on Neutrino Interaction With Dense Matter

> The mean free path of neutrino - free electron gas interaction has been calculated by taking into account the neutrino electromagnetic form factors and the possibility of neutrino oscillation. It is shown that the form factor effect becomes significant for a neutrino magnetic moment μ_ν> 10^{-10} mu_B and for a neutrino radius R > 10^{-6} MeV^{-1}. The mean free path is found to be sensitive to the nu_e-nu_mu and nu_e-nu_e^R transition probabilities.

## Key claim

The neutrino mean free path in a free electron gas becomes sensitive to the neutrino electromagnetic form factor only when the neutrino magnetic moment exceeds approximately \(10^{-10} \mu_B\) or the neutrino charge radius exceeds approximately \(10^{-6}\) MeV\(^{-1}\), values which the authors note are larger than the largest experimental upper bounds for these quantities.

## Method

The authors computed the differential cross section for neutrino-electron scattering in a free electron gas at zero temperature, including contributions from weak interactions, electromagnetic interactions (parameterized by Dirac, anapole, magnetic, and electric form factors), and their interference. They then integrated the cross section to obtain the mean free path for a neutrino energy of 5 MeV, and studied how the mean free path changes when allowing for \(\nu_e\)-\(\nu_\mu\) flavor oscillations or \(\nu_e\)-\(\nu_e^R\) helicity-flip transitions, using the survival probabilities \(P_{ee}\) and \(P_{LL}\) as parameters.

## Key numbers

- The form factor effect becomes significant for a neutrino magnetic moment \(\mu_{\nu} \geq 10^{-10} \mu_B\) (Bohr magneton).
- The form factor effect becomes significant for a neutrino radius \(R \geq 10^{-6}\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- The upper bound on the neutrino magnetic moment from Super-Kamiokande solar data is \((1.1-1.5) \times 10^{-10} \mu_B\).
- Experimental limits give \(\mu_{\nu} < 1.0 \times 10^{-10} \mu_B\), while Supernova 1987A requires \(\mu_{\nu} \le 1.0 \times 10^{-12} \mu_B\).
- The neutrino average squared radius from muon neutrino and antineutrino-electron scattering data is \(R^2 \sim 25 \times 10^{-12}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).
- The calculation uses a neutrino energy of 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the neutrino mean free path in dense matter by Horowitz and Wehrberger (1990s) and others (Niembro, Mornas, Leinson, Fabbri) that were based on the standard model, by adding neutrino electromagnetic form factors and oscillation effects. The results indicate that for realistic values of the magnetic moment and charge radius (below current upper bounds), the electromagnetic form factor does not significantly alter the mean free path, but the sensitivity to \(\nu_e\)-\(\nu_e^R\) helicity-flip transitions can be strong depending on the survival probability. The authors suggest that future realistic mean free path calculations for supernova simulations should incorporate appropriate transition probabilities and should also consider neutrino-nucleon scattering contributions.
