# Medium modifications of $1P$-wave charmonia $χ_{cJ}(1P)$ in cold nuclear matter

> In this work, we employ the quark-meson coupling model to investigate the mass shifts of $1P$-wave charmonia $χ_{cJ}(1P)$ ($J=0,1,2$) in cold symmetric nuclear matter by incorporating in-medium loop contributions to the $χ_{cJ}(1P)$ self-energy within the unquenched picture. At normal nuclear matter density, we obtain significant mass reductions of about 60 MeV for the $χ_{cJ}(1P)$ states, with the $χ_{c2}(1P)$ mass shift primarily arising from the vector-vector loop. Our results also indicate the absence of level crossing between the in-medium $χ_{cJ}(1P)$ mass and the $D\bar{D}$ mass threshold up to $ρ_B < 3ρ_0$-a feature that could be probed in the Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at FAIR and the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC.

## Key claim
The authors claim that at normal nuclear matter density, the mass shifts of all three $\chi_{cJ}(1P)$ states are approximately -60 MeV, but this result depends on the inclusion of vector-vector ($D^{*}\bar{D}^{*}$) loop contributions which had been omitted in prior QMC model studies; the mass shift for $\chi_{c2}(1P)$ is dominated by the $D^{*}\bar{D}^{*}$ loop, while for $\chi_{c0}(1P)$ the $D\bar{D}$ loop dominates.

## Method
The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to compute in-medium masses of $D^{(*)}$ mesons in cold symmetric nuclear matter, then incorporated these modified masses into one-loop self-energy calculations for $\chi_{cJ}(1P)$ states using an effective Lagrangian approach with dipole form factors. The in-medium $\chi_{cJ}(1P)$ masses were determined self-consistently from the density-dependent self-energies, with bare masses fixed by reproducing the free-space physical masses. The cutoff parameter $\alpha$ in the form factor $\Lambda = m_E + \alpha \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$ was varied from 2 to 4 to examine sensitivity.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}$, the $D$ meson mass shift is approximately $-62.4\ \text{MeV}$ and the $D^{*}$ meson mass shift is approximately $-62.1\ \text{MeV}$.
- For $\chi_{c0}(1P)$ at $\rho_0$, the total mass shift ranges from $-33.6\ \text{MeV}$ to $-58.8\ \text{MeV}$ depending on cutoff parameter $\alpha$; the $D\bar{D}$ loop alone contributes $-30.2\ \text{MeV}$ to $-50.2\ \text{MeV}$, while the $D^{*}\bar{D}^{*}$ loop alone contributes $-5.6\ \text{MeV}$ to $-18.2\ \text{MeV}$.
- For $\chi_{c1}(1P)$ at $\rho_0$, the total mass shift from both $D\bar{D}^{*}$ and $D^{*}\bar{D}$ loops ranges from $-44.9\ \text{MeV}$ to $-68.6\ \text{MeV}$; either loop alone contributes $-28.3\ \text{MeV}$ to $-48.3\ \text{MeV}$.
- The free-space masses used as input are $3414.71\ \text{MeV}$ ($\chi_{c0}$), $3510.67\ \text{MeV}$ ($\chi_{c1}$), and $3556.17\ \text{MeV}$ ($\chi_{c2}$).
- No level crossing between in-medium $\chi_{cJ}(1P)$ masses and the $D\bar{D}$ threshold occurs up to $\rho_B < 3\rho_0$.

## Context
This work extends prior QMC model studies of charmonia in nuclear matter by Krein (2010) and Zeminiani (2020), which omitted vector-vector loop contributions for $J/\psi$ and $\Upsilon$ due to their unexpectedly large effects. It also complements QCD sum rule calculations by Lee (2003) and Morita (2007) that predicted nearly degenerate mass shifts of about -60 MeV for all $\chi_{cJ}$ states at $\rho_0$, but without detailed channel-by-channel analysis. The results provide input for modeling cold nuclear matter effects in the Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at FAIR and the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, and suggest that stepwise $J/\psi$ suppression from sequential $\chi_{cJ}$ dissociation may not occur in cold nuclear matter.
