# Observable Properties of Quark-Hadron Phase Transition at the Large Hadron Collider

> Quark-hadron phase transition is simulated by an event generator that incorporates the dynamical properties of contraction due to QCD confinement forces and randomization due to the thermal behavior of a large quark system on the edge of hadronization. Fluctuations of emitted pions in the $(η,ϕ)$ space are analyzed using normalized factorial moments in a wide range of bin sizes. The scaling index $ν$ is found to be very close to the predicted value in the Ginzburg-Landau formalism. The erraticity indices $μ_q$ are determined in a number of ways that lead to the same consistent values. They are compared to the values from the Ising model, showing significant difference in a transparent plot. Experimental determination of $ν$ and $μ_q$ at the LHC are now needed to check the reality of the theoretical study and to provide guidance for improving the model description of quark-hadron phase transition.

## Key claim

The scaling index ν = 1.30 from the SCR event generator is very close to the Ginzburg-Landau predicted value of 1.304, but this agreement is conditional: the GL value is an average of Ising model values ν(T) ranging from 1.04 at T = Tc = 2.315 to 1.56 at T = 2.2, and the SCR result cannot be assigned a specific temperature dependence.

## Method

The authors constructed an event generator called SCR (Successive Contraction and Randomization) that simulates quark-hadron phase transition on a 2D surface in (η, φ) space. The algorithm seeds a unit square with 1000 qq̅ pairs in clusters, then iteratively applies three processes: pionization (recombining nearby qq̅ pairs into pions), contraction (redistributing quarks in dense bins toward cluster centers with Gaussian width σD = 0.05 ND^(1/2)), and randomization (redistributing quarks in dilute bins randomly throughout the square). This cycle repeats until 95% depletion, then new qq̅ pairs are added at decreasing temperatures (Ti = Ti-1 - 0.02 GeV) for 10 time steps. Normalized factorial moments Fq and erraticity moments Cp,q(M) were computed from the resulting pion configurations in (η, φ) space.

## Key numbers

- Scaling index ν = 1.30 from the lower scaling region (M < 500) of the SCR simulation, compared to the Ginzburg-Landau value ν = 1.304
- Upper scaling region index ν' = 1.75 from the SCR simulation (M > 4×10^3)
- Ising model critical temperature Tc = 2.315 in Ising units (J/kB), slightly above the analytical value of 2.27 for infinite lattice
- Erraticity index μ5 = 2.1 for q = 5 from the SCR simulation
- Derived erraticity indices: μ2 = 0.0128, μ3 = 0.309, μ4 = 1.265
- Power-law fit parameters for μq = A(q-1)^B: A = 0.013, B = 4.01
- Ising model erraticity parameters: A = 1.2×10^(-3), B = 2.42
- Initial temperature T = 0.4 GeV for the first time step, decreasing by 0.02 GeV per step
- Total of 3000 qq̅ pairs introduced over 10 time steps
- Final pT distribution inverse slope approximately 0.285 GeV/c for pT < 1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of critical phenomena in heavy-ion collisions using the Ginzburg-Landau formalism (Hwa and Nazirov, 1992) and the Ising model (Cao, Hwa, and Yang, 1999) by introducing a dynamical event generator that incorporates QCD confinement forces. The SCR model produces both the lower scaling region (matching Ising-type fluctuations with ν ≈ 1.3) and an upper scaling region with sharp spikes that generate erraticity indices μq significantly different from those of the Ising model. The results provide specific predictions for ν and μq that can be tested experimentally at the LHC, where higher multiplicities allow narrow pT cuts and wide bin-size ranges needed to reveal scaling properties that were inaccessible at lower-energy experiments at CERN SPS, Brookhaven, and JINR.
