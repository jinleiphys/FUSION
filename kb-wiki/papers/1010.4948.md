# Three-Fermion Bound States on the Light Front

> We investigate the stability of the relativistic three-fermion system with a zero-range force in the light front form. In particular, introducing an invariant cut-off, we study the dependence of the bound state on the coupling strength also for cases where the two-fermion system is unbound. The relativistic Thomas collapse is discussed by solving the fully coupled integral equation system. Furthermore, we explicitly investigate the ground state mass of the three-fermion system and compare to previous simplified calculations.

## Key claim

The relativistic three-fermion system with a zero-range force on the light front exhibits a Thomas collapse at a critical coupling, but the full coupled integral equation system yields a ground state whose critical two-fermion mass differs from that obtained by solving only the first Faddeev equation, as was done in prior work by Karmanov and Carbonell (2003).

## Method

The authors solved the fully coupled system of two Faddeev integral equations for three fermions on the light front, using a zero-range interaction kernel corresponding to a \(^1S_0\) channel. An invariant cut-off \(\Lambda\) (Lepage-Brodsky regularization) was introduced to regularize ultraviolet divergences, allowing the study of the three-body bound state even when no two-fermion bound state exists. The coupled equations were discretized on a momentum-space grid and solved as a matrix eigenvalue problem to obtain the three-fermion ground state mass as a function of the coupling strength.

## Key numbers

* The critical effective coupling for the three-fermion ground state Thomas collapse is \(\lambda^{\rm c}_{\rm eff} \approx 6.972\), corresponding to a critical two-fermion mass \(M^{\rm c}_{2B} \approx 1.42m\) (where \(m\) is the elementary fermion mass).
* For comparison, the critical two-fermion mass calculated by Karmanov and Carbonell (2003) using only the first Faddeev equation is \(M^{\rm c}_{2B} \approx 1.35m\).
* The critical two-fermion mass for the bosonic system is \(M^{\rm c}_{2B} \approx 1.45m\).
* The first excited state of the three-fermion system undergoes Thomas collapse at \(\lambda_{\rm eff} \approx 6.98\), corresponding to \(M^{\rm c}_{2B} \approx 1.35m\), matching the Karmanov and Carbonell (2003) result.

## Context

This work extends the three-boson light-front studies with zero-range forces by Frederico (1992) and Beyer (2003) to the three-fermion case, and revisits the three-fermion calculation of Karmanov and Carbonell (2003) by using the Lepage-Brodsky invariant cut-off regularization instead of the \(M_{2B}\)-regularization. The authors show that solving only the first Faddeev equation, as done by Karmanov and Carbonell (2003), yields the first excited state rather than the ground state, and that the full coupled system gives a different critical coupling for the Thomas collapse. The results enable future investigations of the Efimov effect in this relativistic framework and provide a foundation for more realistic nucleon models including spin degrees of freedom.
