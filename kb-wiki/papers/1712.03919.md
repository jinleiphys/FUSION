# Baryon spectroscopy in the unquenched quark model

> We discuss some applications of the unquenched quark model which is an extension of the CQM that includes the effects of sea quarks via a 3P0 quark-antiquark pair-creation mechanism. Particular attention is paid to the electromagnetic couplings and beta decays of baryons. It is shown that the observed discrepancies between the experimental data and the predictions of the CQM can be accounted for in large part by the effects of sea quarks in the unquenched quark model.

# Baryon spectroscopy in the unquenched quark model

## Key claim

The observed discrepancies between constituent quark model (CQM) predictions and experimental data for electromagnetic and weak couplings of baryons can be accounted for in large part, though not completely, by including sea quark effects via a 3P0 pair-creation mechanism, with the pion cloud providing the dominant contribution.

## Method

The unquenched quark model (UQM) extends the CQM by adding a 3P0 quark-antiquark pair-creation operator to generate higher-Fock components (qqq-qbar) in the baryon wave function. Matrix elements of observables are computed as sums over valence and continuum contributions, summing over intermediate octet and decuplet baryons combined with pseudoscalar mesons (pions, kaons, eta, eta-prime), while excluding radially excited baryons and mesons.

## Key numbers

- Radiative decay width of Delta resonance: CQM gives 399 keV, UQM (with pi, K, eta, eta-prime) gives 608 keV, experiment is 703 ± 61 keV
- Neutron axial coupling g_A(n → p): CQM gives 1.67, UQM (pion-only) gives 1.32, UQM (pi, K, eta, eta-prime) gives 1.34, experiment is 1.2701 ± 0.0025
- Sigma-minus axial coupling g_A(Sigma- → n): CQM gives -0.33, UQM (pi, K, eta, eta-prime) gives -0.31, experiment is -0.340 ± 0.017
- Cabibbo effective values: F = 0.465, D = 0.805, compared to CQM values F = 2/3 and D = 1

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model (CQM) by incorporating sea quark effects that address known failures of the CQM, such as the flavor asymmetry of the proton and the proton spin crisis. The UQM approach builds on earlier pair-creation models by Tornqvist (1980s) and Zenczykowski (1990s), and the results for axial couplings parallel conclusions from the meson-cloud model of Speth (1990s). The findings enable future calculations of baryon electromagnetic form factors and weak decay observables without requiring effective parameters like the Cabibbo F and D values.
