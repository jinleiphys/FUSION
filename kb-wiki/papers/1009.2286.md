# Conformal Holography of Bulk Elliptic Flow and Heavy Quark Quenching in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We show that the ``perfect fluid'' elliptic flow of the bulk hadrons and the unexpectedly strong quenching of heavy quark jet fragments in Au+Au reactions at 200 AGeV can be simultaneously accounted for within leading order AdS/CFT holography with a common large t'Hooft coupling $λ=g^2_{YM} N_c \sim 30$. In contrast, weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma models have so far failed to describe the observed correlation between these soft and hard observables even for couplings extrapolated to $α_s\sim 0.5$. We show that phenomenological applications of the classical trailing string AdS/CFT holographic solution are furthermore remarkably robust to higher order curvature corrections $\mathcal{O}(1/λ^{3/2})$ in type IIB supergravity theories, as well as to worldsheet fluctuation corrections $\mathcal{O}(1/λ^{1/2})$ that were not considered previously. We emphasize the importance of future measurements at RHIC and LHC of the correlation between {\em identified} charm and beauty quark hard ($p_T > 10-30$ GeV) jet quenching observables and low transverse momenta ($p_T\le 1$ GeV) bulk elliptic flow observables to further tests the limits of applicability of conformal holography to strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a single value of the t'Hooft coupling, λ ∼ 30, within leading-order AdS/CFT holography can simultaneously account for both the observed "perfect fluid" elliptic flow of bulk hadrons and the strong quenching of heavy quark jets in Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV, but only when using Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial geometry rather than classical Glauber initial conditions.

## Method

The authors compute the correlation between soft elliptic flow v₂ and the nuclear modification factor R_AA^e for non-photonic electrons from heavy quark jets using conformal holography of N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory. They employ viscous hydrodynamic simulations to relate v₂ to η/s, use the trailing string AdS/CFT solution to compute heavy quark energy loss including finite t'Hooft coupling corrections from quartic curvature corrections (O(1/λ^{3/2})) and worldsheet fluctuation corrections (O(1/λ^{1/2})), and perform path-length averaging over Glauber and CGC initial geometries with a longitudinally expanding plasma.

## Key numbers

- The t'Hooft coupling λ ∼ 30 provides simultaneous agreement with both soft and hard observables at RHIC.
- The viscosity to entropy ratio is η/s = (1/4π)(1 + c₃/λ^{3/2}) with c₃ = 15ζ(3) ≈ 18.
- The heavy quark energy loss in the ultrarelativistic limit is d ln E/dx̂ = -(√λ/2)(T/M_Q)[1 + (15/16)ζ(3)/λ^{3/2}].
- The worldsheet fluctuation coefficient κ₁ ∼ -1.33 is taken from the heavy quark potential in vacuum (Chu 2009).
- The fit to elliptic flow gives a₁ ≈ 2.5 and a(p_T=1)ε₂(20-60%) ≈ 0.14 for CGC initial conditions and 0.098 for Glauber initial conditions.
- The freeze-out temperature is T_f = 140 MeV and the equilibration time is τ₀ = 1 fm/c.
- The Gauss-Bonnet parameter is constrained to -7/36 < λ_GB < 9/100 by causality and positive energy flow.

## Context

This work extends the application of AdS/CFT holography to simultaneously describe soft and hard observables in heavy ion collisions, building on prior studies of the trailing string drag model (drag, drag1) and viscous hydrodynamic simulations (Luzum 2008). It contrasts with perturbative QCD-based transport models (coesterpaper, Djordjevic 2005) which fail to describe the observed correlation between elliptic flow and heavy quark quenching even with α_s ∼ 0.5. The results enable future tests at RHIC and LHC using identified charm and beauty jet quenching measurements to further probe the limits of conformal holography for strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma.
