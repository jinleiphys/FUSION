# Phase Unwrapping and One-Dimensional Sign Problems

> Sign problems in path integrals arise when different field configurations contribute with different signs or phases. Phase unwrapping describes a family of signal processing techniques in which phase differences between elements of a time series are integrated to construct non-compact unwrapped phase differences. By combining phase unwrapping with a cumulant expansion, path integrals with sign problems arising from phase fluctuations can be systematically approximated as linear combinations of path integrals without sign problems. This work explores phase unwrapping in zero-plus-one-dimensional complex scalar field theory. Results with improved signal-to-noise ratios for the spectrum of scalar field theory can be obtained from unwrapped phases, but the size of cumulant expansion truncation errors is found to be undesirably sensitive to the parameters of the phase unwrapping algorithm employed. It is argued that this numerical sensitivity arises from discretization artifacts that become large when phases fluctuate close to singularities of a complex logarithm in the definition of the unwrapped phase.

## Key claim

Phase unwrapping combined with a cumulant expansion can systematically approximate path integrals with sign problems as linear combinations of sign-problem-free path integrals, but the truncation errors are undesirably sensitive to the parameters of the phase unwrapping algorithm, and this sensitivity arises from discretization artifacts when phases fluctuate close to singularities of the complex logarithm.

## Method

The authors study zero-plus-one-dimensional complex scalar field theory on a uniform lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using a magnitude-phase decomposition of the field. They apply one-dimensional phase unwrapping algorithms to numerically integrate phase differences over spacetime separations, producing noncompact unwrapped phases, and then use a cumulant expansion to relate moments of these unwrapped phases to correlation functions with nonzero U(1) charge. The method is compared to standard Monte Carlo sampling and to an analytic dual-variable approach where phase fluctuations are integrated out exactly using modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The signal-to-noise ratio for charged correlation functions scales as $\sqrt{2N} e^{-Q E t}$ in standard Monte Carlo, where $Q$ is the U(1) charge and $E = 2\ \text{arcsinh}(M/2)$ is the scalar field energy.
- The scalar field propagator pole energy is $E = 2\ \text{arcsinh}(M/2)$ and the residue is $Z_{1;0,1} = 1/(M\sqrt{4+M^2})$ for the free theory.
- The variance of the dual-variable correlation function scales as $e^{-2|Q|Et}$, avoiding the exponential signal-to-noise degradation of standard Monte Carlo.
- Monte Carlo ensembles used 5000 field configurations with $M^2 = 0.00625$ and $L=512$ lattice sites.

## Context

This work extends the phase unwrapping idea briefly mentioned in Wagman (2016) for lattice QCD baryon correlation functions, applying it to a toy model of the baryon correlation function signal-to-noise problem. The analytic dual-variable approach using modified Bessel functions generalizes the dual lattice variable method of Endres (2006). The results show that one-dimensional phase unwrapping suffers from numerical instabilities that prevent robust application to lattice QCD, but suggest that multidimensional phase unwrapping algorithms might avoid these issues in future work.
