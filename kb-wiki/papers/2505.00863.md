# Holographic Heavy Quark Energy Loss in the Hybrid Model

> To date, holographic calculations in strongly coupled plasma have provided separate descriptions for the rates of energy loss either for ultrarelativistic massless quarks and gluons or for infinitely massive quarks, with the latter calculation valid for $\sqrtγ < M/(\sqrtλT)$, where $γ$ is the Lorentz boost factor for a heavy quark with velocity $v$ and mass $M$ moving through plasma with 't Hooft coupling $λ$ and temperature $T$. These two calculations should apply sequentially in the description of the energy loss of a heavy quark that starts out ultrarelativistic, loses energy, slows down, becomes non-relativistic at later times, and ultimately comes to rest and diffuses in the strongly coupled plasma. We provide an ansatz for uniquely incorporating both regimes to give an approximate but unified description of how a heavy quark that is initially ultrarelativistic loses energy all the way until it comes to rest. We implement this ansatz in the Hybrid Strong/Weak Coupling Model. With this new, consistent, treatment of heavy quark energy loss at strong coupling, we confront our predictions for the suppression and azimuthal anisotropies of D- and B-mesons, as well as B-tagged jets, with available experimental data.

# Holographic Heavy Quark Energy Loss in the Hybrid Model

## Key claim
The authors provide an approximate but unified description of heavy quark energy loss from ultrarelativistic to non-relativistic velocities by stitching together two separate holographic calculations (massless and infinitely massive limits) using a minimum-energy-loss ansatz, implemented within the Hybrid Strong/Weak Coupling Model. The composite description is valid only where the two regimes can be joined at a single crossing point, and the resulting energy-loss function has a discontinuous second derivative at that switch point.

## Method
The authors combine two existing AdS/CFT energy-loss formulas: the massless (light quark) result dE/dx = -4 x^2 E_0 / (π x_t^2 sqrt(x_t^2 - x^2)) from Chesler (2014, 2015) and the infinite-mass (heavy quark) drag result dE/dx = -η_D sqrt(E^2 - M^2) from Herzog (2006) and Gubser (2006). They construct a composite energy-loss rate by taking the minimum of these two formulas at each point, which yields a continuous and once-differentiable energy function E(x). This composite description is then implemented in the Hybrid Strong/Weak Coupling Model to compute observables for D- and B-mesons and B-tagged jets in heavy ion collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- The heavy quark drag coefficient is η_D = κ_HQ T^2 / M, where κ_HQ is a free parameter governing coupling strength between heavy quarks and strongly coupled plasma.
- The thermal stopping length for a light quark is x_t = (1/(2κ_sc)) (E_0 / T^4)^(1/3), where κ_sc is a free parameter.
- The composite model results for B-meson and D-meson R_AA and v_2 are shown for κ_HQ = 2.2, chosen for reasonable consistency with both R_AA and v_2 in central collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
- The b-jet R_AA results are completely insensitive to κ_HQ variation over the range 1.7 to 4.4.
- D-meson R_AA and v_2 agree well with data down to p_T approximately 10 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the Hybrid Strong/Weak Coupling Model of Casalderrey-Solana (2014, 2016, 2018, 2019) by incorporating heavy quark energy loss for the first time. It bridges two separate holographic calculations: the massless quark energy loss of Chesler (2014, 2015) and the infinite-mass heavy quark drag of Herzog (2006) and Gubser (2006). The composite description enables predictions for D- and B-meson suppression and azimuthal anisotropies that can be compared to ALICE and CMS data, though the authors note that recombination/coalescence effects (hadronization with light quarks from the QGP) must be included in future work to improve low-p_T agreement, particularly for D-mesons.
