# Correlations between the nuclear matter symmetry energy, its slope, and curvature from a nonrelativistic solvable approach and beyond

> By using point-coupling versions of finite range nuclear relativistic mean field models containing cubic and quartic self interactions in the scalar field $σ$, a nonrelativistic limit is achieved. This approach allows an analytical expression for the symmetry energy ($J$) as a function of its slope ($L$) in a unified form, namely, $\,L\,=\,3J\,+f(m^{*},ρ_{o},B_{o},K_{o})$, where the quantities $m^{*}$, $ρ_{o}$, $B_{o}$ and $K_{o}$ are bulk parameters at the nuclear matter saturation density $ρ_{o}$. This result establishes a linear correlation between $L$ and $J$ which is reinforced by exact relativistic calculations. An analogous analytical correlation is also found for $J$, $L$ and the symmetry energy curvature ($K_{sym}$). Based on these results, we propose graphic constraints in $L\times J$ and $K_{sym}\times L$ planes which finite range models must satisfy.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical linear correlation between the nuclear symmetry energy \(J\) and its slope \(L\) of the form \(L = 3J + f(m^{*},\rho_{o},B_{o},K_{o})\), where the function \(f\) depends on the effective mass \(m^{*}\), saturation density \(\rho_{o}\), binding energy \(B_{o}\), and incompressibility \(K_{o}\). This correlation is a consequence of the limitation to a single isovector parameter in the nonrelativistic limit of the point-coupling models studied, and the authors caution that models with more isovector parameters may exhibit different, not necessarily linear, behavior.

## Method

The authors start from finite-range relativistic mean-field (FR-RMF) models of the Boguta-Bodmer type, which contain cubic and quartic self-interactions in the scalar field \(\sigma\). They construct point-coupling versions of these models and then perform a nonrelativistic (NR) limit using normalized spinor wave functions after small component reduction, following Reinhard (1989). In this NR framework, the coupling constants are eliminated in favor of the bulk parameters \(m^{*}\), \(\rho_{o}\), \(B_{o}\), and \(K_{o}\), yielding analytical expressions for the symmetry energy \(J\), its slope \(L\), and its curvature \(K_{\text{sym}}\). These analytical results are then compared with exact relativistic mean-field calculations for a set of FR-RMF parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The linear correlation \(L = 3J + f(m^{*},\rho_{o},B_{o},K_{o})\) is derived analytically; for fixed \(\rho_{o}=0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(B_{o}=16\) MeV, the function \(g(\rho_{o})\) in \(f\) is \(24.5\) MeV.
- For the range \(250 \leq K_{o} \leq 315\) MeV, the variation in \(f\) due to \(K_{o}\) is \(|\Delta f|_{K_{o}} = 0.32\) MeV.
- For the range \(0.50 \leq m^{*} \leq 0.80\), the variation in \(f\) due to \(m^{*}\) is \(|\Delta f|_{m^{*}} = 18\) MeV.
- For a set of FR-RMF models with \(m^{*}=0.60\), the best-fit line for \(L\) vs. \(J\) has an angular coefficient of \(2.96\), close to the predicted value of \(3\).
- For the symmetry energy curvature, \(K_{\text{sym}} = 96.4\) MeV for models with \(\rho_{o}=0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(B_{o}=16\) MeV, \(K_{o}=270\) MeV, \(m^{*}=0.60\), and \(J\) in the range \(25 \leq J \leq 35\) MeV.
- The variation in \(K_{\text{sym}}\) due to the FRS constraint range \(0.58 \leq m^{*} \leq 0.64\) is \(|\Delta K_{\text{sym}}| = 20\) MeV, and due to the range \(250 \leq K_{o} \leq 315\) MeV is \(|\Delta K_{\text{sym}}| = 5.9\) MeV.
- The function \(p(\rho_{o})\) in the relation \(K_{\text{sym}} = [L-3J] p(\rho_{o}) + q(\rho_{o},B_{o},K_{o})\) is \(5.13\) at \(\rho_{o}=0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\); for FR-RMF models with \(J=32.5\) MeV and \(K_{o}=230\) MeV, the angular coefficient of \(K_{\text{sym}}\) vs. \(L\) is \(7.17\).

## Context

This work extends previous numerical studies of the correlation between \(L\) and \(J\) by Ducoin et al. (2011) by providing an analytical derivation within a specific class of models. It also builds on the Furnstahl-Rusnak-Serot (FRS) constraint (1998) linking the effective mass \(m^{*}\) to finite-nuclei spin-orbit splittings. The derived correlations enable the construction of graphic constraints in the \(L \times J\) and \(K_{\text{sym}} \times L\) planes that finite-range relativistic mean-field models must satisfy to be consistent with the FRS constraint and typical ranges of \(K_{o}\) and \(J\). The authors note that their results apply specifically to Boguta-Bodmer-type models with a single isovector parameter, and that density-dependent RMF models may not follow the same features.
