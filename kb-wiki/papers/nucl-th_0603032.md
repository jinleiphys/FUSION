# Spurious Shell Closures in the Relativistic Mean Field Model

> Following a systematic theoretical study of the ground-state properties of over 7000 nuclei from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line in the relativistic mean field model [Prog. Theor. Phys. 113 (2005) 785], which is in fair agreement with existing experimental data, we observe a few spurious shell closures, i.e. proton shell closures at Z=58 and Z=92. These spurious shell closures are found to persist in all the effective forces of the relativistic mean field model, e.g. TMA, NL3, PKDD and DD-ME2.

# Spurious Shell Closures in the Relativistic Mean Field Model

## Key claim
The relativistic mean field (RMF) model systematically produces spurious proton shell closures at Z=58 and Z=92, which persist across all four representative effective forces examined (TMA, NL3, PKDD, and DD-ME2), despite the model's overall fair agreement with experimental ground-state data for over 7000 nuclei. These spurious closures are identified as the cause of overbinding in nuclei such as 140Ce and 218U, with discrepancies of at least 8 MeV compared to experimental binding energies.

## Method
The authors performed spherical RMF+BCS calculations without pairing correlations for four nuclei (132Sn, 208Pb, 140Ce, and 218U) using four effective forces: TMA, NL3, PKDD, and DD-ME2. They computed binding energies and proton shell gaps (energy differences between occupied and unoccupied orbitals) at Z=50, 58, 82, and 92, comparing them to experimental data for 132Sn and 208Pb. Neutron shell closures at N=82 and N=126 were also evaluated for the same set of nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of 218U: TMA gives 1674.0 MeV, NL3 gives 1674.7 MeV, PKDD gives 1677.7 MeV, DD-ME2 gives 1673.5 MeV; experimental value is 1665.6 MeV (all forces overestimate by at least 8 MeV)
- Binding energy of 140Ce: TMA gives 1179.4 MeV, NL3 gives 1176.3 MeV, PKDD gives 1179.0 MeV, DD-ME2 gives 1175.9 MeV; experimental value is 1172.7 MeV
- Proton shell gap at Z=58 in 140Ce: TMA gives 4.12 MeV, NL3 gives 3.50 MeV, PKDD gives 3.63 MeV, DD-ME2 gives 3.08 MeV; experimental value for Z=58 is 0.96 MeV (from 132Sn reference)
- Proton shell gap at Z=92 in 218U: TMA gives 3.93 MeV, NL3 gives 3.54 MeV, PKDD gives 3.63 MeV, DD-ME2 gives 3.28 MeV; experimental value for Z=92 is 0.90 MeV (from 208Pb reference)
- Neutron shell gap at N=82 in 132Sn: TMA gives 6.78 MeV, NL3 gives 6.17 MeV, PKDD gives 6.50 MeV, DD-ME2 gives 6.17 MeV; experimental value is 4.84 MeV
- Neutron shell gap at N=126 in 208Pb: TMA gives 3.58 MeV, NL3 gives 4.60 MeV, PKDD gives 4.78 MeV, DD-ME2 gives 5.25 MeV; experimental value is 3.43 MeV

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of Geng et al. (2005) which first identified spurious shell closures at Z=58 and Z=92 in the TMA force, by demonstrating that the same problem appears in NL3 (Lalazissis et al., 1997), PKDD (Long et al., 2004), and DD-ME2 (Lalazissis et al., 2005). The authors note that no such common feature has been found in nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock models. The identification of these "critical nuclei" (following Bertsch et al., 2005) points to regions where the RMF model requires improvement, and the authors suggest that a more balanced fitting strategy or making magic nuclei slightly less bound could help remove these spurious closures for future applications in astrophysical studies and exotic nuclei research.
