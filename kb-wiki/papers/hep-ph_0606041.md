# The Scalar Mesons, Symmetry Breaking, Three Colors and Confinement

> The same, well known, det(Sigma)+ h.c. term in effective theories, which 't Hooft showed is generated by instantons in QCD and which resolves the UA(1) problem giving mass, in particular to the $η'$ is for three light flavors shown to give three classical minima along the UA(1) circle. The three minima are related to the Z(3) center of SU(3). The term also contributes, in a similar way as the diquark model of Jaffe, to an inverted scalar mass spectrum for the light scalars. The three vacua suggests a connection to the strong CP problem and confinement.

## Key claim

The well-known 't Hooft determinant term in effective QCD theories, which resolves the UA(1) problem by giving mass to the η', is shown to produce three classical minima along the UA(1) circle for three light flavors, rather than a single minimum; these three minima are related to the Z(3) center of SU(3) and suggest a connection to the strong CP problem and confinement.

## Method

The author analyzes the SU(3)L × SU(3)R linear sigma model with the addition of the 't Hooft determinant term det(Σ) + h.c., which explicitly breaks UA(1) symmetry. By substituting a pure singlet field Φ = φ·1/√3 into the potential and including the UA(1) phase angle θ, the potential is shown to have a cosine factor cos[3θ + 3 arg(φ)] that produces three distinct minima rather than the usual Mexican hat. The connection between the determinant term and Jaffe's diquark model is examined through the flavor-color antisymmetry required by Fermi-Dirac statistics.

## Key numbers

- The three minima are located at v₁ = v e^{-iθ}, v₂ = v e^{-iθ + i2π/3}, v₃ = v e^{-iθ + i4π/3}, where v = |φ_min|/√3 ≈ 130 MeV (corresponding to an average f_π and f_K decay constant of 130√(2/3) MeV ≈ 106 MeV)
- The singlet pseudoscalar (η') mass is given by m²_{p₀} = -6β|v| = 12(λ+3λ')|v|², yielding 958 MeV for the η'
- The singlet scalar mass is m²_{s₀} = 4(λ+3λ')v² = m²_{p₀}/3, or 553 MeV for a 958 MeV p₀
- The scalar octet mass is m²_{s₁..₈} = 16(λ + 3/2 λ')v² = 4/3 m²_{p₀} - 8λ'v², in the region of 1 GeV
- The determinant term contributes to an inverted scalar mass spectrum where m²_κ - m²_{a₀} = -(m²_K - m²_π) = 2β(v_{s̄s} - v_{d̄d})

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft instanton analysis (1976) and the UA(1) resolution of Weinberg (1975), showing that the determinant term produces three vacua rather than one. The inverted scalar mass spectrum predicted by the model parallels Jaffe's diquark model (1977) for the light scalar nonet, where the σ(600) is lightest, followed by κ near 800 MeV and a₀(980), f₀(980). The three-vacua structure suggests connections to the strong CP problem of Peccei and Quinn and to confinement mechanisms discussed by Huang, potentially enabling a topological understanding of baryon number and quark confinement through winding numbers.
