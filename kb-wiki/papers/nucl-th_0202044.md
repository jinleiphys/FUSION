# On the difference between proton and neutron spin-orbit splittings in nuclei

> The latest experimental data on nuclei at $^{132}$Sn permit us for the first time to determine the spin-orbit splittings of neutrons and protons in identical orbits in this neutron-rich doubly-magic region and compare the case to that of $^{208}$Pb. Using the new results, which are now consistent for the two neutron-rich doubly magic regions, a theoretical analysis defines the isotopic dependence of the mean field spin-orbit potential and leads to a simple explicit expression for the difference between the spin-orbit splittings of neutrons and protons. The isotopic dependence is explained in the framework of different theoretical approaches.

# On the difference between proton and neutron spin-orbit splittings in nuclei

## Key claim
The neutron spin-orbit splitting in neutron-rich doubly-magic nuclei is systematically larger than the proton splitting by about 10%, contrary to earlier suggestions that the opposite might hold for 208Pb. This difference is captured by a simple expression ε = (2/3)(N-Z)/A, derived from the properties of the two-body spin-orbit interaction, with the caveat that tensor forces also contribute but do not change the sign or general magnitude of the effect.

## Method
The authors compiled experimental single-particle energies from binding-energy differences and excitation energies for nuclei near the doubly-closed-shell regions 16O, 40Ca, 100Sn, 132Sn, and 208Pb, accounting for fragmentation of states where spectroscopic data were available. They then compared the spin-orbit splittings of neutrons and protons in identical orbits (e.g., 2d in 132Sn, 2f and 3p in 208Pb) and performed theoretical analyses using three approaches: the Walecka model in the Hartree approximation, a Woods-Saxon potential with parameter optimization, and Hartree-Fock calculations with the SIII Skyrme interaction.

## Key numbers
- In 132Sn, the 2d3/2-2d5/2 splitting is 1.48 MeV for protons and 1.65 MeV for neutrons (neutron larger by more than 11%).
- In 208Pb, after correcting for fragmentation, the 2f5/2-2f7/2 splitting is 1.93 MeV for protons and 2.13 MeV for neutrons (neutron larger by about 10%).
- In 208Pb, the 3p1/2-3p3/2 splitting is 0.85 MeV for protons and 0.90 MeV for neutrons (neutron larger by about 6%).
- In N=Z nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 100Sn), proton and neutron splittings are equal within 2.6%: for example, in 16O the 1p1/2-1p3/2 splitting is 6.32 MeV (protons) and 6.17 MeV (neutrons); in 40Ca the 1f5/2-1f7/2 splitting is 4.95 MeV (protons) and 4.88 MeV (neutrons).
- The derived parameter βℓs describing the isospin dependence of the mean spin-orbit field is approximately -2/3 from the simple two-body argument, with empirical effective values of -0.55 (2d in 132Sn), -0.60 (2f in 208Pb), and -0.27 (3p in 208Pb).

## Context
This work extends the qualitative suggestion by Bohr and Mottelson that neutron spin-orbit splitting should be larger in heavier nuclei due to a higher number of like particles, providing the first quantitative experimental confirmation using data from the 132Sn region (Blomqvist 1981) and corrected data from 208Pb. The results contradict the earlier conclusion by the same group (Isakov 2000) that 208Pb might show the opposite trend, and they disagree with Noble (1979) who proposed cancellation of the isotopic dependence through exchange terms. The derived expression enables predictions for nuclei with extreme neutron excess, where the difference could approach 40% and affect level ordering, and provides a benchmark for future self-consistent calculations in neutron-rich systems.
