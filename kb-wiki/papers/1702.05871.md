# Equation of state for hybrid stars with strangeness

> Considering the mass constraint from the resent pulsar observations, we study the properties of neutron stars including hyperons and quarks explicitly. Using the chiral quark-meson coupling model with relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation, the equation of state (EoS) for hadronic matter is calculated by taking into account the strange ($σ^{\ast}$ and $ϕ$) mesons as well as the light non-strange ($σ$, $ω$, $\vecρ$, and $\vecπ$) mesons in SU(3) flavor symmetry. On the other hand, the EoS for quark matter is constructed with the simple MIT bag or the flavor-SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and we investigate the effect of the hadron-quark coexistence on the neutron-star properties, imposing smooth crossover or Gibbs criterion for chemical equilibrium. The mass-radius relation of a neutron star, as well as physical quantities such as EoSs, particle fractions, and the speed of sound in matter are presented. We find that, in order to prevent the quark appearance at very low densities, the stiff hadronic EoS should be required under both of the hadron-quark crossover and the first-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a stiff hadronic equation of state (EoS) is required to prevent quark matter from appearing at very low densities, regardless of whether the hadron-quark transition is treated as a smooth crossover or a first-order phase transition, in order to satisfy the observed 2 solar mass neutron star constraint.

## Method

The authors calculate the hadronic EoS using the chiral quark-meson coupling (CQMC) model with relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation, including both light non-strange mesons (σ, ω, ρ, π) and strange mesons (σ*, φ) in SU(3) flavor symmetry. For quark matter, they use either the MIT bag model with a density-dependent bag constant or the flavor-SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a vector-type interaction. The hadron-quark coexistence is modeled using either the Gibbs criterion for a first-order phase transition or smooth crossover with three different interpolation schemes (ε-ρ, P-ρ, and P-ε).

## Key numbers

- The normal nuclear density is taken as ρ₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³, with binding energy per nucleon of -16.1 MeV and symmetry energy of 32.5 MeV at saturation
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter at ρ₀ with RHF approximation is K₀ = 269 MeV, compared to K₀ = 309 MeV with RH approximation
- For the MIT bag model with first-order phase transition, the parameter β in the density-dependent bag constant must satisfy β ≤ 0.025 to meet the 2M⊙ constraint
- Quarks appear at 0.6 fm⁻³ in the first-order phase transition case with the MIT bag model
- The NJL model uses ρ₀ᴺᴶᴸ = 0.17 fm⁻³ for the crossover interpolation parameters
- The speed of sound satisfies 0 < dP/dε < 1 in the P-ε interpolation with gV = 1.5GS for all crossover windows

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star EoS with hyperons by Miyatsu et al. (2013, 2014, 2015) and Kambe et al. (2016), addressing the challenge posed by the discovery of 2M⊙ pulsars by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013). The paper shows that while the CQMC model with SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry cannot support 2M⊙ neutron stars, the inclusion of φ meson repulsion in SU(3) flavor symmetry enables reaching this mass constraint. The work enables future studies of hybrid star properties by demonstrating that only the P-ε interpolation scheme simultaneously satisfies both the 2M⊙ mass constraint and causality conditions for the speed of sound.
