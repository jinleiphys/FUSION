# Analysis of ordinary and radiative muon capture in liquid hydrogen

> A simultaneous analysis is made of the measured rates of ordinary muon capture (OMC) and radiative muon capture (RMC) in liquid hydrogen, using theoretical estimates for the relevant atomic capture rates that have been obtained in chiral perturbation theory with the use of the most recent values of the coupling constants. We reexamine the basic formulas for relating the atomic OMC and RMC rates to the liquid-hydrogen OMC and RMC rates, respectively. Although the analysis is significantly influenced by ambiguity in the molecular state population, we can demonstrate that, while the OMC data can be reproduced, the RMC data can be explained only with unrealistic values of the coupling constants; the degree of difficulty becomes even more severe when we try to explain the OMC and RMC data simultaneously.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, while the measured ordinary muon capture (OMC) rate in liquid hydrogen can be reproduced using standard theoretical inputs from chiral perturbation theory, the radiative muon capture (RMC) data can only be explained with unrealistic values of the coupling constants; the difficulty becomes even more severe when trying to explain both OMC and RMC data simultaneously, and the analysis is significantly influenced by ambiguity in the molecular state population.

## Method

The authors reexamine the atom-liquid (A-L) formulas that relate the theoretically calculated atomic muon capture rates (for hyperfine singlet and triplet states of the muon-proton atom) to the measured OMC and RMC rates in liquid hydrogen, incorporating the actual experimental timing conditions (beam burst duration, data collection start times). They use atomic capture rates calculated in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with the most recent coupling constants, and investigate the sensitivity of the results to the ortho-para molecular transition rate λ_op and to variations in the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P and a molecular mixing parameter ξ.

## Key numbers

- Experimental OMC rate in liquid hydrogen: Λ_liq^exp = 460 ± 20 s⁻¹ (Bardin et al.)
- Experimental RMC partial branching ratio (E_γ ≥ 60 MeV): R_γ^exp = (2.10 ± 0.22) × 10⁻⁸ (Jonkmans et al.)
- Atomic singlet OMC rate (HBχPT): Λ_s^OMC = 695 s⁻¹
- Atomic triplet OMC rate (HBχPT): Λ_t^OMC = 11.9 s⁻¹
- Atomic singlet RMC rate (HBχPT): Λ_s^RMC = 0.891 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹
- Atomic triplet RMC rate (HBχPT): Λ_t^RMC = 20.1 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹
- Ortho-para transition rate (experimental): λ_op^exp = (4.1 ± 1.4) × 10⁴ s⁻¹
- Ortho-para transition rate (theoretical): λ_op^th = (7.1 ± 1.2) × 10⁴ s⁻¹
- Using λ_op^exp with the improved A-L formula gives Λ_liq = 460 s⁻¹, matching experiment; using λ_op^th gives Λ_liq ≈ 421 s⁻¹
- The calculated R_γ is smaller than the experimental value by a factor of approximately 1.5
- Reproducing R_γ^exp in a phenomenological model requires either g_P = 1.4 g_P^PCAC or ξ = 0.80, while the OMC data requires g_P ≤ 1.2 g_P^PCAC or ξ ≥ 0.95

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Ando, Myhrer, Kubodera, 2000) by incorporating the actual experimental timing conditions of the OMC measurement by Bardin et al. and the RMC measurement by Jonkmans et al., correcting the earlier use of the Bakalov et al. atom-liquid formula which assumed t_i=0. The paper reconfirms the longstanding discrepancy between the value of the induced pseudoscalar coupling g_P deduced from RMC (about 1.5 times the PCAC prediction) versus OMC (consistent with PCAC), and shows that even allowing variations in the molecular mixing parameter ξ cannot simultaneously explain both datasets. The authors note that a planned precise measurement of the OMC rate in hydrogen gas at PSI would eliminate the molecular transition rate ambiguity and directly test the HBχPT predictions.
