# Unpolarized gluon distribution in the nucleon from lattice quantum chromodynamics

> In this study, we present a determination of the unpolarized gluon Ioffe-time distribution in the nucleon from a first principles lattice quantum chromodynamics calculation. We carry out the lattice calculation on a $32^3\times 64$ ensemble with a pion mass of $358$ MeV and lattice spacing of $0.094$ fm. We construct the nucleon interpolating fields using the distillation technique, flow the gauge fields using the gradient flow, and solve the summed generalized eigenvalue problem to determine the glounic matrix elements. Combining these techniques allows us to provide a statistically well-controlled Ioffe-time distribution and unpolarized gluon PDF. We obtain the flow time independent reduced Ioffe-time pseudo-distribution, and calculate the light-cone Ioffe-time distribution and unpolarized gluon distribution function in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at $μ= 2$ GeV, neglecting the mixing of the gluon operator with the quark singlet sector. Finally, we compare our results to phenomenological determinations.

## Key claim

The authors present a first-principles lattice QCD determination of the unpolarized gluon Ioffe-time distribution in the nucleon, but they explicitly neglect the mixing of the gluon operator with the quark singlet sector, which introduces a systematic uncertainty in the matching to the light-cone PDF.

## Method

The calculation uses a $32^3 \times 64$ lattice ensemble with pion mass 358 MeV and lattice spacing 0.094 fm. Nucleon interpolating fields are constructed using the distillation technique, gauge fields are flowed using gradient flow, and a summed generalized eigenvalue problem is solved to determine gluonic matrix elements. The reduced Ioffe-time pseudo-distribution is obtained by taking ratios that cancel ultraviolet divergences, and the light-cone gluon PDF is extracted via short-distance factorization with one-loop matching in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at $\mu = 2$ GeV, parameterizing the reduced pseudo-ITD using Jacobi polynomials.

## Key numbers

- Lattice ensemble: $32^3 \times 64$ spatial volume, pion mass $M_\pi = 358(3)$ MeV, lattice spacing $a = 0.094(1)$ fm
- Number of gauge configurations: 349, with 64 temporal sources per configuration
- Distillation space dimension: $N_D = 64$ eigenvectors
- Gradient flow times used: from $\tau = a^2$ to $\tau = 3.8 a^2$
- Matching scale: $\mu = 2$ GeV in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme
- Spatial momentum discretization: $p = 2\pi l / (a L)$ with $L = 32$, boosts up to $l = 6$ using momentum smearing with $\vec{\zeta} = 2 \cdot (2\pi/L) \hat{z}$

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-PDF approach of Radyushkin (2017) to the gluon sector, building on prior lattice calculations of gluon momentum fraction by Alexandrou (2020) and Yang (2018), and on nucleon valence quark distributions using pseudo-PDFs by Karpie (2021). It enables first-principles comparisons with phenomenological gluon PDF determinations from global analyses such as those by Hou (2019) and Ball (2017), and provides a pathway toward future calculations that include the neglected quark-gluon mixing contributions.
