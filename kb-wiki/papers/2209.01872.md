# Mass splitting and spin alignment for $ϕ$ mesons in a magnetic field in NJL model

> Based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, we develop a framework for calculating the spin alignment of vector mesons and applied it to study $ϕ$ mesons in a magnetic field. We calculate mass spectra for $ϕ$ mesons and observe mass splitting between the longitudinally polarized state and transversely polarized states. The $ϕ$ meson in a thermal equilibrium system is preferred to occupy the state with spin $λ=0$ than those with spin $λ=\pm1$, because the former state has a smaller energy. As a consequence, we conclude that the spin alignment will be larger than 1/3 if one measures along the direction of the magnetic field, which is qualitatively consistent with the recent STAR data. Around the critical temperature $T_{C}=150$ MeV, the positive deviation from 1/3 is proportional to the square of the magnetic field strength, which agrees with the result from the non-relativistic coalescence model. Including the anomalous magnetic moments for quarks will modify the dynamical masses of quarks and thus affect the mass spectra and spin alignment of $ϕ$ mesons. The discussion of spin alignment in the NJL model may help us better understand the formation of hadron's spin structure during the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

The spin alignment of $\phi$ mesons measured along the magnetic field direction will be larger than 1/3, with the positive deviation proportional to the square of the magnetic field strength near the critical temperature T_C = 150 MeV, but this result applies specifically to a constant magnetic field in the NJL model framework and is qualitatively consistent with STAR data only for the direction of the deviation.

## Method

The authors use the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar and vector four-fermion interactions and a 't Hooft term, treating mesons as quantum fluctuations via random phase approximation resummation of quark bubbles. They calculate the quark propagator in a constant magnetic field using the Ritus method with Landau level quantization, then compute the vector meson self-energy at one-quark-loop level for the $\phi$ meson (strange quark-antiquark pair) to obtain spectral functions and mass spectra for different spin states. The spin alignment is derived from the spectral representation of the meson propagator in thermal equilibrium using Bose-Einstein statistics.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength up to $eB = 10\,m_{\pi}^2$ (approximately $10^{18}$ Gauss) is considered, comparable to fields in heavy-ion collisions
- The critical temperature for chiral phase transition is $T_C \approx 150$ MeV
- Vacuum dynamical masses: $M_{u,d} = 325$ MeV, $M_s = 554$ MeV (with current masses $m_{u,d}=10.3$ MeV, $m_s=236.9$ MeV)
- The $\phi$ meson vacuum mass is $M_\phi = 1.02$ GeV, used to fix the vector coupling $G_V\Lambda^2 = -4.67$
- Bound state dissociation temperatures: 215 MeV for $\lambda=\pm1$ states and 270 MeV for $\lambda=0$ states at $eB=5\,m_{\pi}^2$
- At $T=150$ MeV and $eB=5\,m_{\pi}^2$, the spin alignment for bound states agrees well with the non-relativistic coalescence model result $\rho_{00} = 1/3 + (4/9T^2)\mu_s^2 B^2$

## Context

This work extends the study of vector meson properties in magnetic fields within the NJL model, which previously focused on light-flavor mesons ($\sigma$, $\pi$, $\omega$, $\rho$) by authors such as Liu (2014), Avancini (2015-2016), and Mao (2018), but few works addressed the $\phi$ meson. The spin alignment calculation is motivated by recent STAR collaboration data (2022) showing positive deviation from 1/3 for $\phi$ mesons, and the NJL model approach complements the non-relativistic coalescence model of Yang (2017) and the strong force field fluctuation model of Sheng (2019-2022). The framework enables future study of momentum-dependent spin alignments along any measuring direction and can be extended to space-time dependent magnetic fields.
