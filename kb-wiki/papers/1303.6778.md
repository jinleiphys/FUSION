# A Constrained-Path Quantum Monte-Carlo Approach for the Nuclear Shell Model

> A new Quantum Monte-Carlo (QMC) approach is proposed to investigate low-lying states of nuclei within the shell model. The formalism relies on a variational symmetry-restored wave-function to guide the underlying Brownian motion. Sign/phase problems that usually plague QMC fermionic simulations are controlled by constraining stochastic paths through a fixed-node like approximation. Exploratory results in the sd and pf valence spaces with realistic effective interactions are presented. They prove the ability of the scheme to yield nearly exact yrast spectroscopies for both even- and odd-mass nuclei.

# A Constrained-Path Quantum Monte-Carlo Approach for the Nuclear Shell Model

## Key claim

The proposed phaseless Quantum Monte-Carlo (QMC) scheme, guided by a symmetry-restored mean-field trial wavefunction, can yield yrast energies for both even- and odd-mass nuclei that agree with exact diagonalization to within 0.3% relative error, but only when the fixed-node-like constraint on the stochastic paths is applied to control the sign/phase problem that otherwise plagues fermionic QMC simulations with realistic interactions.

## Method

The method decomposes the two-body Hamiltonian into a quadratic form of one-body operators via a Pandya transformation, then represents the imaginary-time propagator stochastically using auxiliary fields. A trial wavefunction with restored angular-momentum symmetry (obtained by projecting a Slater determinant onto good J, M via rotation operators) is used to guide the Brownian motion of walkers through an importance-sampling term in the drift equation, and to constrain the random walk via a phaseless approximation that discards walkers whose overlap phase changes by more than π/2 in a time step. The approximate yrast state for each spin J is reconstructed from the weighted average of walker contributions.

## Key numbers

- Yrast energies from the QMC method differ from exact values by no more than **0.3%** relative error, with statistical error bars of about **40-50 keV**.
- The symmetry-entangled mean-field (SEMF) trial energies differ from exact values by less than about **1 MeV** in all considered cases (sd-shell nuclei ²⁶Al, ²⁷Na, ²⁸Mg with the USD interaction; pf-shell nucleus ⁵⁶Ni with the GXPF1A interaction).
- For the odd-mass nucleus ²⁷Na, the QMC method successfully controls the phase problem arising from time-reversal symmetry breaking, achieving the same 0.3% accuracy as for even-even and odd-odd systems.

## Context

This work extends the phaseless QMC approach of Zhang and Krakauer (1997) from quantum chemistry to the nuclear shell model, where it addresses the sign/phase problem that limits the standard Shell Model Monte-Carlo (SMMC) method of Koonin et al. (1997) when realistic effective interactions are used. The symmetry-restored trial wavefunction is obtained via a variational procedure similar to the VAMPIR approach of Schmid (2001) and the symmetry-entangled mean-field method of Juillet (2007). The results enable future calculations in the full pf-0g9/2 shell for medium-mass neutron-rich and proton-rich nuclei that are inaccessible to conventional diagonalization without severe truncations, and open the possibility of computing excited states for complete spectroscopy.
