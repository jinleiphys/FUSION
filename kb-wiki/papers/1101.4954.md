# Normal ground state of dense relativistic matter in a magnetic field

> The properties of the ground state of relativistic matter in a magnetic field are examined within the framework of a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The main emphasis of this study is the normal ground state, which is realized at sufficiently high temperatures and/or sufficiently large chemical potentials. In contrast to the vacuum state, which is characterized by the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking, the normal state is accompanied by the dynamical generation of the chiral shift parameter $Δ$. In the chiral limit, the value of $Δ$ determines a relative shift of the longitudinal momenta (along the direction of the magnetic field) in the dispersion relations of opposite chirality fermions. We argue that the chirality remains a good approximate quantum number even for massive fermions in the vicinity of the Fermi surface and, therefore, the chiral shift is expected to play an important role in many types of cold dense relativistic matter, relevant for applications in compact stars. The qualitative implications of the revealed structure of the normal ground state on the physics of protoneutron stars are discussed. A noticeable feature of the $Δ$ parameter is that it is insensitive to temperature when $T \ll μ_0$, where $μ_0$ is the chemical potential, and {\it increases} with temperature for $T > μ_0$. The latter implies that the chiral shift parameter is also generated in the regime relevant for heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The normal ground state of dense relativistic matter in a magnetic field, realized at sufficiently high temperatures and/or large chemical potentials, necessarily develops a nonzero chiral shift parameter Δ even in perturbative dynamics, because the vanishing of Δ is not protected by any symmetry. The chiral shift parameter is insensitive to temperature when T ≪ μ₀ but increases with temperature for T > μ₀.

## Method

The authors analyze the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with one fermion flavor in a constant external magnetic field, using the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation in the mean-field (Hartree-Fock) approximation. They solve the gap equations for the dynamical mass m, effective chemical potential μ, and chiral shift parameter Δ at zero and nonzero temperature, employing both a gauge noninvariant momentum cutoff regularization and a gauge invariant proper-time regularization. The analytical and numerical solutions are compared to determine the ground state structure.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier finding (Gorbar 2009) that the normal ground state in a magnetic field is characterized by a chiral shift parameter Δ, which was shown to be linear in the coupling constant to leading order. The study builds on the topological axial current result of Metlitski (2005) and the magnetic catalysis phenomenon (MC1, MC2) where chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken in the vacuum. The results enable future investigations of transport and emission properties of dense matter in compact stars and heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that the chiral shift parameter is also generated in the regime relevant for heavy ion collisions where T > μ₀.
