# Phi^{zero}-N Bound State

> We show that the QCD van der Waals attractive potential is strong enough to bind a $ϕ^{0}$ meson onto a nucleon inside a nucleus to form a bound state. The direct experimental signature for such an exotic state is proposed in the case of subthreshold $ϕ^{0}$ meson photoproduction from nuclear targets. The production rate is estimated and such an experiment is found to be feasible at the Jefferson Laboratory.

# Phi-N Bound State

## Key claim

The QCD van der Waals attractive potential is strong enough to bind a phi meson onto a nucleon inside a nucleus, forming a bound state with a binding energy of 1.8 MeV, provided the interaction parameters are taken as alpha = 1.25 and mu = 0.6 GeV, which the authors argue are conservative estimates for the phi-N system.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic variational method with a Yukawa-type attractive potential V = -alpha * exp(-mu r)/r to model the QCD van der Waals interaction between the phi meson and a nucleon. They calculated the photoproduction cross section for forming a phi-N bound state from a carbon-12 target in the impulse approximation, using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions for the nuclear structure and a s-wave approximation for the intermediate phi-N state. The total cross section for gamma + C-12 -> d + A_f was evaluated as a function of photon energy, with the bound state formation amplitude determined from the variational wavefunction and the van der Waals potential.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the phi-N bound state: 1.8 MeV, obtained with alpha = 1.25 and mu = 0.6 GeV
- Coupling constant alpha = 1.25 for the phi-N interaction, compared to alpha = 0.6 used by Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond (1990) for the c-cbar quarkonium
- Range parameter mu = 0.6 GeV for the phi-N interaction
- Radius of the phi meson: 0.4 fm, twice the radius of the J/Psi meson
- Total cross section for phi-N bound state production from C-12 at photon energy E_gamma = 1450 MeV: sigma_tot = 1.4 nb for cutoff parameter Lambda = 3 Fermi
- Total cross section drops to 0.008 nb at E_gamma = 1800 MeV
- Peak of the cross section occurs around photon energy 1450 MeV, below the free production threshold of 1570 MeV
- Estimated triple coincidence event rate (proton, K+, K-) at Jefferson Laboratory: 180/hour at E_gamma = 1450 MeV with luminosity 10^35/cm^2/sec
- Oscillator wavefunction parameter for C-12: b = 1.64 Fermi

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction of nuclear-bound quarkonium by Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond (1990), who used a similar Yukawa potential to predict bound states of eta_c with He-3 and heavier nuclei, a result later confirmed by Wasson (1991) with a more realistic potential. The authors apply the same framework to the phi meson, which is almost a pure s-sbar state, and find that the interaction is enhanced relative to the c-cbar case by the factor (m_c/m_s)^3 due to the larger q-qbar separation. The experimental search for the phi-N bound state via subthreshold photoproduction is proposed as feasible at Jefferson Laboratory, and the authors note that such a measurement could help probe the strangeness content of the nucleon, as the simple gluonic van der Waals picture may be complicated by possible strange quark exchange contributions.
