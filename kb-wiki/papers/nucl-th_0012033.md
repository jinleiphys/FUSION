# Point Form Electrodynamics and the Construction of Conserved Current Operators

> A general procedure for constructing conserved electromagnetic current operators, for both finite and infinite degree of freedom systems, is given. A four-momentum operator consisting of matter, photon, and electromagnetic interactions is assumed to be polynomial in photon creation and annihilation operators. Commutators of the four-momentum operator with the four-vector potential operator at the space-time point zero give the electromagnetic field tensor and current operator at the space-time point zero. In this construction there are no equations of motion to be satisfied and field operators at an arbitrary space-time point-defined as the four-momentum translates of the corresponding operators at the space-time point zero-are not local operators. Several examples are given to show how the construction is carried out.

# Point Form Electrodynamics and the Construction of Conserved Current Operators

## Key claim

The paper presents a general procedure for constructing conserved electromagnetic current operators in point form relativistic quantum mechanics, but the construction requires that the total current operator be the sum of free, dynamically determined, and model dependent many-body operators, with the dynamically determined current carrying charge and the model dependent current carrying zero charge. The key hedge is that while the free current operator is conserved in the absence of hadronic interactions, it must be modified by adding many-body terms when hadronic interactions are present, and the model dependent part is not uniquely fixed by current conservation alone.

## Method

The author assumes a four-momentum operator in point form relativistic quantum mechanics that is the sum of hadronic, photon, and electromagnetic four-momentum operators, polynomial in photon creation and annihilation operators. The electromagnetic field tensor at the space-time point zero is defined as the commutator of the four-vector potential operator with the four-momentum operator, and the current operator at the space-time point zero is defined as the commutator of this field tensor with the four-momentum operator. The total current operator is then decomposed into a free one-body current operator, a dynamically determined many-body current operator (constructed to restore current conservation in the presence of hadronic interactions), and a model dependent many-body current operator (constructed as the commutator of an antisymmetric operator with the hadronic four-momentum operator).

## Key numbers

- The dynamically determined current matrix element is given by `<p'|J_mu^{DD}(0)|p> = -(Q_mu + Q_mu^{perp})/Q^2 <p'|[P^nu(I), J_nu^1(0)]|p>`, where Q is the four-momentum transfer and Q^{perp} is chosen to avoid a pole at Q^2=0.
- For a bound state of mass m made from n spinless constituents of mass m_c, the Jacobian converting to internal variables is J = M_0 / (product 2 omega_i), where M_0 = sum omega_i and omega_i = sqrt(m_c^2 + k_i^2).
- In the generalized Breit frame with initial and final masses m and m', the momentum transfer squared is Q^2(st) = (m'-m)^2 - 4 m' m sh^2(alpha), and is zero when sh(alpha) = (m'-m)/(2 sqrt(m' m)).
- The factor F needed for the charge operator in the bound state model is F = omega_1 / (M_0 (omega_1 - k_{1z})) when dynamically determined currents are included, compared to F = 1/M_0 without them.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous construction of one-body current operators for arbitrary spin particles in point form relativistic quantum mechanics (Klink, second paper in the series) and addresses the same problem of current conservation that arises in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics, where the continuity equation only fixes the parallel component of the current density operator, leaving the perpendicular component as model dependent. The motivation comes from calculations of elastic deuteron form factors (Klink, earlier work) that show the A structure function falling below data at momentum transfers of 2 GeV^2, and nucleon form factor calculations (Klink, earlier work) that give good agreement except for some static properties, suggesting that many-body currents constructed here are needed to improve the deuteron results without altering the nucleon results. The construction enables actual calculations of dynamically determined and model dependent currents for nucleon and deuteron form factors to be carried out in future work.
