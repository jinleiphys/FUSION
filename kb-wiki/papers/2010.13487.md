# $β$-decay rates of $^{(115,117)}$Rh into $^{(115,117)}$Pd isotopes in the microscopic IBFM-2

> The structure of odd-$A$ $^{(115,117)}$Rh and $^{(115,117)}$Pd isotopes is studied by means of the neutron-proton Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM-2). $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$ quantum number assignment for the $^{(115,117)}$Pd ground-states is critically discussed and the predicted energy levels are compared to the existing experimental data. The resulting nuclear wave functions are used to compute the $β$-decay $ft$ values of the transitions from $^{(115,117)}$Rh to $^{(115,117)}$Pd in the microscopic IBFM-2 and the results compared with the data.

# β-decay rates of ¹¹⁵,¹¹⁷Rh into ¹¹⁵,¹¹⁷Pd isotopes in the microscopic IBFM-2

## Key claim

The authors claim that their microscopic IBFM-2 calculations support the Jᴾ = ½⁺ assignment for the ground states of ¹¹⁵Pd and ¹¹⁷Pd, as reported in the most recent experimental studies (Kurpeta 2010, Kurpeta 2018), but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the experimental data for M1 transitions in these Pd isotopes are lacking, forcing the authors to use data from the isotone ¹¹⁷Cd as a reference instead.

## Method

The authors use the neutron-proton Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM-2) to describe odd-A ¹¹⁵,¹¹⁷Rh and ¹¹⁵,¹¹⁷Pd isotopes. First, the even-even core nuclei ¹¹⁶Pd and ¹¹⁸Pd are described with the IBM-2 using parameters from Kim (1996). Then, neutron single-particle energies for the Pd isotopes are extrapolated from Yoshida (2002), while proton single-particle energies for the Rh isotopes are obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential calculation. The IBFM-2 Hamiltonian is diagonalized after coupling the odd fermion to the core, and the resulting wave functions are used to compute β-decay ft values via one-nucleon transfer operators derived using the OAI mapping method with the Surface Delta Interaction.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹⁷Pd, the calculated B(M1; 3/2⁺₁ → 1/2⁺₁) = 0.009 W.u., compared to the experimental lower limit for ¹¹⁷Cd of B(M1; 3/2⁺₁ → 1/2⁺₁) > 0.0074 W.u.
- For ¹¹⁵Pd, the calculated B(M1; 3/2⁺₁ → 1/2⁺₁) = 0.016 W.u.
- For ¹¹⁷Pd, the calculated ratio B(M1; 3/2⁺₂ → 3/2⁺₁) / B(M1; 3/2⁺₂ → 1/2⁺₁) = 0.17; the experimental relative intensities from Kurpeta (2018) for the corresponding lines are 100(1) and 34.9(1.6), respectively.
- For ¹¹⁵Pd, the calculated ratio B(M1; 3/2⁺₂ → 3/2⁺₁) / B(M1; 3/2⁺₂ → 1/2⁺₁) = 0.55.
- For the ¹¹⁷Rh → ¹¹⁷Pd β-decay, the calculated log₁₀ft for the (7/2)₁ → (5/2)₃ transition is 5.33, compared to the experimental value of 5.1 from Kurpeta (2018).
- For the ¹¹⁷Rh → ¹¹⁷Pd (7/2)₁ → (5/2)₁ transition, the calculated log₁₀ft = 6.78, compared to the experimental value of 5.7.
- For the ¹¹⁷Rh → ¹¹⁷Pd (7/2)₁ → (7/2)₁ transition, the calculated log₁₀ft = 6.68, compared to the experimental value of 5.8.

## Context

This work extends the application of the microscopic IBFM-2 to neutron-rich A ≈ 115-117 nuclei in the region of the prolate-to-oblate shape transition, previously studied experimentally by Kurpeta (2010, 2018) and theoretically by Kim (1996) for the even-even Pd cores. The calculations support the ½⁺ ground-state assignment for ¹¹⁵,¹¹⁷Pd, resolving conflicting assignments from earlier works (Penttila 1991 suggesting 5/2⁺ for ¹¹⁷Pd; Urban 2004 suggesting 3/2⁺ for both isotopes; Fong 2005 suggesting 1/2⁺ for ¹¹⁵Pd and 3/2⁺ for ¹¹⁷Pd). The results enable future studies of β-decay systematics in this mass region and provide a test of the IBFM-2 framework for calculating weak interaction rates relevant to astrophysical nucleosynthesis.
