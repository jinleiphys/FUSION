# Transport properties of the QCD medium

> I present an overview of recent developments in the microscopic description of the quark-gluon plasma. I will concentrate on medium-induced emission and transverse momentum broadening. These are two key ingredients of the theory of jet modifications in the QCD medium and of the kinetic theory used for transport and thermalisation. The main focus is on progress towards a better understanding of theory and of its uncertainties.

## Key claim

This overview argues that recent progress in the microscopic description of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly in medium-induced emission and transverse momentum broadening, is enabling better quantification of theory uncertainties and narrowing the gap between the QCD Lagrangian and phenomenology, though significant challenges remain for anisotropic systems and intermediate coupling.

## Method

The paper reviews developments in the theoretical treatment of jet-medium interactions, focusing on the improved opacity expansion for medium-induced emission (which treats non-harmonic parts of the scattering kernel as perturbations) and on non-perturbative determinations of the transverse momentum broadening kernel using dimensionally-reduced lattice theory merged with perturbative QCD at short distances. It also discusses radiative corrections to transverse momentum broadening and their resummation, as well as next-to-leading order calculations of transport coefficients and thermalisation times in kinetic theory.

## Key numbers

- The improved opacity expansion agrees with full numerical solutions of the medium-induced emission propagator at the 10% or better level over its validity range (Figure 1, comparing results from Andres (2020) and Barata (2021))
- The non-perturbative transverse momentum broadening kernel shows an O(1) difference in medium-induced emission rates compared to NLO perturbative kernels, a much larger effect than the improved opacity expansion uncertainty (Moore (2021), Schlichting (2021))
- Next-to-leading order corrections reduce the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio by a factor of a few in the phenomenological T ~ few Tc range (Ghiglieri (2018))
- For isotropic systems at intermediate coupling with g^2 N_c = 10, the NLO correction to thermalisation time is approximately 40%, with the spread between different NLO collision operator constructions smaller than the LO-to-NLO shift (Fu (2021), Figure 3)

## Context

This review extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier (1996), Zakharov (1996)) for medium-induced radiation and the leading-order effective kinetic theory of QCD (Arnold (2002)), incorporating recent non-perturbative lattice results for the broadening kernel (Panero (2013), Moore (2019)) and radiative corrections to transverse momentum broadening (Liou (2013), Blaizot (2013), Iancu (2014)). The developments enable improved phenomenological comparisons to jet quenching data and better-controlled extrapolations of kinetic theory to the coupling strengths relevant for heavy-ion collisions, though open questions remain regarding anisotropic systems and plasma instabilities (Mrowczynski (1993), Romatschke (2003), Arnold (2003)).
