# Stellar properties and nuclear matter constraints

> We have analyzed stellar properties of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) parametrizations shown to be consistent with the recently studied constraints related to nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, symmetry energy and its derivatives [Dutra et al., Phys. Rev. C 90, 055203 (2014)]. Our results show that only two RMF parametrizations do not allow the emergence of the direct Urca process, important aspect regarding the evolution of a neutron star. Moreover, among all approved RMF models, fourteen of them produce neutron stars with maximum masses inside the range $1.93\leqslant M/M_\odot\leqslant 2.05$, with $M_\odot$ being the solar mass. Only three models yield maximum masses above this range and a discussion on the inclusion of hyperons is presented. Finally, we have verified that the models satisfying the neutron star maximum mass constraint do not observe the squared sound velocity bound, namely, $v_s^2 < 1/3$, corroborating recent findings. However, the recently proposed $σ$-cut scheme can make the RMF models consistent with both constraints depending on the isoscalar-vector interaction of each parametrization.

## Key claim

Only two relativistic mean-field (RMF) parametrizations (TW99 and DDHδ) produce neutron stars with maximum masses above the observational range of 1.93–2.05 solar masses, correcting an earlier claim that three models did so; the previously reported high masses for DDHδ and DD-MEδ were due to a technical mistake in the calculation.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using equations of state from 35 RMF parametrizations previously shown to satisfy nuclear matter constraints (Dutra et al., 2014). They computed maximum stellar masses, radii, direct Urca process thresholds, and squared sound velocities for matter in beta equilibrium, and applied the σ-cut scheme to modify models with maximum masses below 1.93 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- DDHδ corrected maximum mass: 2.142 solar masses, radius 11.138 km
- DD-MEδ corrected maximum mass: 1.958 solar masses, radius 10.241 km
- TW99 maximum mass: 2.080 solar masses, radius 10.613 km
- 13 parametrizations produce neutron stars with maximum masses in the range 1.93–2.05 solar masses (corrected from 12)
- Only 2 parametrizations (TW99 and DDHδ) yield maximum masses above 2.05 solar masses
- DDHδ with hyperons (SET1): maximum mass 1.859 solar masses; with SET2: 1.689 solar masses
- TW99 with hyperons (SET1): maximum mass 1.895 solar masses; with SET2: 1.700 solar masses
- All models satisfying the maximum mass constraint exceed the squared sound velocity bound of 1/3

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matter constraint analysis of Dutra et al. (2014) by testing the same 35 approved RMF parametrizations against astrophysical observations of massive pulsars (PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432). The results confirm the finding of Bedaque and Steiner (2015) that models producing maximum masses near 2 solar masses generally violate the sound velocity bound v_s^2 < 1/3, but show that the σ-cut scheme proposed by Voskrebenzev et al. (2015) can reconcile both constraints for models with appropriate isoscalar-vector interactions. The hyperon-puzzle remains an open problem, as including hyperons reduces maximum masses below the observational range for all tested parametrizations.
