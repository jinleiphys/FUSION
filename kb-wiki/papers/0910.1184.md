# Compton scattering from the proton: An analysis using the delta expansion up to N3LO

> We report on a chiral effective field theory calculation of Compton scattering from the proton. Our calculation includes pions, nucleons, and the Delta(1232) as explicit degrees of freedom. It uses the "delta expansion", and so implements the hierarchy of scales m_pi < M_Delta-M_N < Lambda_chi. In this expansion the power counting in the vicinity of the Delta peak changes, and resummation of the loop graphs associated with the Delta width is indicated. We have computed the nucleon Compton amplitude in the delta expansion up to N3LO for photon energies of the order of m_pi. This is the first order at which the proton Compton scattering amplitudes receive contributions from contact operators which encode contributions to the spin-independent polarisabilities from states with energies of the order of Lambda_chi. We fit the coefficients of these two operators to the experimental proton Compton data that has been taken in the relevant photon-energy domain, and are in a position to extract new results for the proton polarisabilities alpha and beta.

## Key claim
The delta-expansion chiral effective field theory calculation of proton Compton scattering up to N3LO (order e²δ⁴) can describe experimental data up to at least 200 MeV lab energy, but the fits are preliminary and the authors do not quote final results for the proton polarisabilities α_p and β_p due to tensions in the fit and the need to promote an additional counterterm from higher order.

## Method
The calculation uses chiral effective field theory with pions, nucleons, and the Delta(1232) as explicit degrees of freedom, employing the "delta expansion" which implements the scale hierarchy m_π < Δ ≡ M_Δ − M_N < Λ_χ through the counting m_π/Δ ∼ Δ/Λ_χ ∼ δ. The Compton amplitude was computed up to N3LO (order e²δ⁴) for photon energies ω ∼ m_π, including πN loops, πΔ loops, and contact operators that encode contributions to spin-independent polarisabilities from high-energy states. The coefficients of two contact operators were fit to experimental proton Compton data, with the γNΔ coupling constant also treated as a fit parameter and an additional counterterm promoted from higher order to control the spin polarisability γ_{M1}.

## Key numbers
- The lowest-order heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory predictions for proton polarisabilities are α = 12.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- A previous Delta-less fourth-order fit gave α_p = (12.1 ± 1.1 (stat.))_{-0.5}^{+0.5} (theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_p = (3.4 ± 1.1 (stat.))_{-0.1}^{+0.1} (theory) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- A previous Delta-full small-scale expansion fit gave α_p = 11.5 ± 1.4_stat × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_p = 3.4 ± 1.6_stat × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The calculation aims to describe data up to 240 MeV lab energy, with good description demonstrated up to at least 200 MeV.
- A random systematic error of 5% was added in quadrature to the Mainz TAPS data statistical errors.

## Context
This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of proton Compton scattering that either omitted the Delta resonance (McGovern et al., 2001; Beane et al., 2003) or included it only at lowest non-trivial order in the small-scale expansion (Hildebrandt et al., 2004). The delta-expansion approach was proposed by Pascalutsa and Phillips to treat the scale Δ = M_Δ − M_N more consistently than the small-scale expansion. The results enable production of reliable single-nucleon amplitudes for incorporation into calculations of Compton scattering from the deuteron (Griesshammer, 2009), which will allow analysis of data currently being taken at MAX-lab (Feldman, 2008). The authors note that reducing error bars on α_p and β_p requires more high-quality lower-energy data below pion production threshold.
