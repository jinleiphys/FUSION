# Nucleon-Nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering using quantum field theory: a comparative study

> In this paper, we use Yukawa theory to calculate differential and total cross-sections for elastic and inelastic scattering in nucleon-nucleon interactions. We start from the fundamental Lagrangian and derive the $T$-matrix and hence the invariant scattering matrix leading towards the differential and total cross section. We perform calculations utilizing two types of Yukawa interaction terms: 1) scalar current, and 2) pseudoscalar current. We also carry out calculations in a laboratory frame. Calculated results using scalar current exhibits the exact features of elastic and inelastic scattering. The results agree very well with the differential cross-sections for experimental data. The pseudoscalar current calculations do not offer reasonable results and features of the NN interactions. The simplicity of the theory makes it all the more impressive, thus it can be used in place of more complicated field theories to describe NN interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple Yukawa one-pion-exchange theory using a scalar current interaction term can reproduce experimental nucleon-nucleon differential cross-sections very well, while the pseudoscalar current version of the same theory does not yield reasonable results. The authors hedge that this approach works for elastic scattering at laboratory energies below the threshold for pion production (below about 270 MeV).

## Method

The authors start from the Yukawa Lagrangian density for a Dirac nucleon field interacting with a scalar Klein-Gordon pion field, derive the T-matrix using Wick's theorem and time-ordered products, and compute the spin-averaged squared invariant matrix element using Casimir's trick and trace techniques. They perform calculations for both scalar current and pseudoscalar current interaction terms, expressing the differential cross-section in terms of Mandelstam variables and center-of-mass energy and angle. The coupling constant is treated as a floating parameter fitted to experimental data, and calculations are carried out in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass M is taken as approximately 939 MeV
- Pion mass m_pi is given as 134.977 MeV
- The threshold for elastic scattering is set by requiring the laboratory energy E_lab to be below 2 * m_pi * c^2 (approximately 270 MeV)
- The Mandelstam variables are expressed as: s = 4E^2, t = -4(E^2 - M^2) sin^2(theta/2), u = -4(E^2 - M^2) cos^2(theta/2), where E is the single nucleon energy in the center-of-mass frame
- The spin-averaged squared amplitude for scalar current is given by equation (eq12) and in Mandelstam form as lambda^4 * [(s+u)^2/(t-m^2)^2 - (s^2 + s(t+u))/((t-m^2)(u-m^2)) + (s+t)^2/(u-m^2)^2]

## Context

This work extends the Yukawa one-pion-exchange approach by deriving the scattering amplitude directly from the Lagrangian density using quantum field theory, rather than using the one-pion-exchange-potential (OPEP) method or pre-assumed Mandelstam variable terms as in Norbury (2010) and Yao (2006). The authors avoid Feynman calculus and instead use Wick's theorem with time-ordering operators, treating the coupling constant as a floating parameter fitted to data. This approach enables accurate description of hadron resonance production in half-odd spin particles and can serve as a simpler alternative to more complicated field theories for describing NN interactions.
