# Two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations in Glauber-like models

> We investigate the role of the central two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations on typical quantities observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Basic correlation measures, such as the fluctuations of the participant eccentricity, initial size fluctuations, or the fluctuations of the number of sources producing particles, are sensitive at the level 10-20% to the inclusion of the two-body correlations. However, the realistic correlation function gives virtually indistinguishable results from the {\em hard-core repulsion} with the expulsion distance set to $\sim 0.9$ fm. In the second part of the talk we compare the spherical and Gaussian wounding profiles and find that the latter, which is more realistic, leads to reduced eccentricity and fluctuations. This has significance for precision studies of the elliptic flow.

## Key claim

Two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations affect basic correlation measures in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the 10-20% level, but the realistic Gaussian correlation function yields results virtually indistinguishable from a simple hard-core repulsion with an expulsion distance of about 0.9 fm.

## Method

The authors performed Glauber Monte Carlo simulations using the GLISSANDO code for 208Pb-208Pb collisions at the highest SPS energy, with the mixed wounded-nucleon model (alpha = 0.12). They compared three nucleon distributions: uncorrelated, correlated using the distributions of Alvioli et al. (2009), and hard-core repulsion with expulsion radius 0.9 fm. In the second part, they compared spherical (step-function) and Gaussian wounding profiles for the nucleon-nucleon collision probability.

## Key numbers

- Basic correlation measures (fluctuations of participant eccentricity, initial size fluctuations, fluctuations of number of sources) are sensitive at the 10-20% level to inclusion of two-body correlations.
- The realistic correlation function gives results indistinguishable from hard-core repulsion with expulsion distance set to about 0.9 fm.
- For the scaled standard deviation of participant eccentricity, the uncorrelated case shows up to 10% larger fluctuations at intermediate centralities compared to the correlated cases.
- The Gaussian wounding profile produces a 10-15% quenching of the average participant eccentricity in peripheral collisions compared to the spherical profile.
- The mixed model uses alpha = 0.12 for the fraction of binary collisions.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-model investigations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions by incorporating the central Gaussian two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations from Alvioli et al. (2009), which had not been used in previous Glauber Monte Carlo codes such as those of Wang (1991), Werner (1993), Broniowski et al. (2009), Alver et al. (2008), and Miller et al. (2007). The results confirm the earlier finding of Tavares et al. (2010) regarding eccentricity, and show that the simpler hard-core repulsion is sufficient for practical calculations. The use of Gaussian wounding profiles, motivated by Bialas et al. (1976), reduces eccentricity and fluctuations, which has significance for precision studies of elliptic flow in hydrodynamic models.
