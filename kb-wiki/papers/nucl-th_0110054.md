# Spectrum of 10Be in the basis of the leading representation of the SU(3) group

> A realization of the approximation of the SU(3) leading representation with the microscopic Hamiltonian and a nucleon-nucleon interaction is presented in detail. An effective Hamiltonian reproducing results of calculations with some known potentials is constructed. It is shown that its structure is quite similar to that of the triaxial rotator, and the wave functions in the Elliott's scheme are linear combinations of Wigner's D-functions although they should be properly normalized.

## Key claim

The SU(3) leading representation approximation, when implemented with a microscopic Hamiltonian and a nucleon-nucleon interaction, produces an effective Hamiltonian whose structure is similar to that of the triaxial rotator, but the wave functions in Elliott's scheme are linear combinations of Wigner's D-functions that require proper normalization because the density matrix corresponds to a mixed state rather than a pure state.

## Method

The authors constructed generating Slater determinants for ¹⁰Be using proton and neutron orbitals defined with unit vectors u and w, then calculated overlap integrals of these determinants with a microscopic Hamiltonian containing a central exchange potential of Gaussian form (Volkov and Minnesota potentials). The overlap integrals were expanded over Wigner's D-functions to project onto states of definite angular momentum, and the resulting energy spectra were compared with experimental data. An effective Hamiltonian was then constructed as a linear combination of the SU(3) group generators L², Ω (LQL), and Ω₁ (LQQL) to reproduce the microscopic results.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square radius of ¹⁰Be calculated with the Volkov potential (m=0.6) and optimized oscillator length r₀=1.64 fm is 2.29 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.3±0.2 fm
- For the Minnesota potential with u=0.98, the optimized oscillator length is r₀=1.43 fm
- The splitting between the two 2⁺ states in ¹⁰Be calculated with the Minnesota potential (r₀=1.64 fm) is 1.29 MeV, which is twice smaller than the observed value of 2.59 MeV
- The quantity ½(E₂²+E₂¹)-E₀ equals 1.125 MeV for the Minnesota potential, four times less than the experimental value and almost twice less than the Volkov potential value of 2.07 MeV
- The ground state energy E₀=5A+¼B, where A and B are coefficients expressed through amplitudes of the even components V₃₁ and V₁₃ of the nucleon-nucleon potential

## Context

This work extends Elliott's SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) by implementing it with microscopic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Volkov, 1965; Minnesota, 1977) rather than phenomenological Hamiltonians, and establishes a formal connection between the SU(3) leading representation and the triaxial rotator model (Davydov and Filippov, 1958). The approach enables future calculations for neutron-rich nuclei such as ⁹,¹¹Li and ¹⁰,¹¹Be, and provides a framework for constructing effective Hamiltonians that can be applied across a range of light and medium nuclei with different SU(3) symmetry indices.
