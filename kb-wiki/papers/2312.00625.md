# Partial-wave projection of the one-particle exchange in three-body scattering amplitudes

> As the study of three-hadron physics from lattice QCD matures, it is necessary to develop proper analysis tools in order to reliably study a variety of phenomena, including resonance spectroscopy and nuclear structure. Reconstructing the three-particle scattering amplitude requires solving integral equations, which can be written in terms of data-constrained dynamical functions and physical on-shell quantities. The driving term in these equations is the so-called one-particle exchange, which leads to a kinematic divergence for particles on-mass-shell. A vital component in defining three-particle amplitudes with definite parity and total angular momentum, which are used in spectroscopic studies, is to project the one-particle exchange into definite partial waves. We present a general procedure to construct exact analytic partial wave projections of the one-particle exchange contribution for any system composed of three spinless hadrons. Our result allows one full control over the analytic structure of the projection, which we explore for some low-lying partial waves with applications to three pions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general procedure to construct exact analytic partial-wave projections of the one-particle exchange (OPE) contribution for any system of three spinless hadrons, with the result expressed in terms of known functions including Legendre functions of the second kind, thereby giving full control over the analytic structure of the projection.

## Method

The authors first define the three-to-three scattering amplitude in the helicity basis, coupling the three-particle system to definite total angular momentum \(J\) and parity \(P\). They then project the one-particle exchange propagator, which contains a kinematic divergence from on-shell particle exchange, into definite partial waves by expressing it as a sum of a polynomial part and a term proportional to the zero-degree Legendre function of the second kind \(Q_0(\zeta_{pk})\). The projection uses Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and the spin-helicity matrix \(\mathcal{H}\) to couple the system to the desired \(J^P\) quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

* The one-particle exchange propagator projected to definite spin-parity \(J^P\) takes the form \(\mathcal{G}^{J^P} = \mathcal{K}_{\mathcal{G}}^{J^P} + \mathcal{T}^{J^P} Q_0(\zeta_{pk})\), where \(Q_0\) is the zero-degree Legendre function of the 2nd kind.
* The pair invariant mass-squared is defined as \(\sigma_k = (P-k)^2\) for the initial state and \(\sigma_p = (P-p)^2\) for the final state, with physical thresholds \(\sigma_k^{(\mathrm{thr.})} = (m_a + m_{a'})^2\) and \(\sigma_p^{(\mathrm{thr.})} = (m_b + m_{b'})^2\).
* The magnitude of the initial pair momentum in its rest frame is \(a_k^{\star} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\sigma_k}} \lambda^{1/2}(\sigma_k, m_a^2, m_{a'}^2)\), where \(\lambda\) is the Källén triangle function.
* The spectator momenta in the total CM frame are given by \(k = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{s}} \lambda^{1/2}(s, \sigma_k, m_k^2)\) and \(p = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{s}} \lambda^{1/2}(s, \sigma_p, m_p^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the three-body scattering formalism developed for lattice QCD applications by Hansen and Sharpe (2014-2016) and Briceño, Hansen, and Jackura (2017-2022), which previously focused primarily on \(S\)-wave projections for identical spinless bosons. The procedure enables the study of three-hadron systems with definite spin-parity \(J^P\) beyond \(S\)-wave, which is essential for resonance spectroscopy of states such as the \(a_1\) meson, and provides the analytic control needed for continuation of three-body amplitudes into the complex energy plane as done by Dawid (2023).
