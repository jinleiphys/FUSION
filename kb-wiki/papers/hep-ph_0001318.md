# The Nonperturbative Color Meissner Effect in a Two-Flavor Color Superconductor

> Color superconductivity in QCD breaks the SU(3) color gauge group down to SU(2), inducing masses in five of the eight gluons. This is a dynamical Higgs effect, in which the diquark condensate acts as the vacuum expectation value of a composite scalar field. In order to analyze this effect at low quark density, when gaps are large and generated nonperturbatively, we use instanton-induced quark interactions augmented with gauge-invariant interactions between quarks and perturbative gluons. The five gluon masses are found from the static limit of the relevant polarization operators, in which transversality is maintained via the Nambu-Goldstone modes of broken color symmetry. Working in the microscopic theory we calculate these masses to one-loop order and estimate their density dependence. Finally, we speculate that the Meissner effect may postpone the onset of color superconductivity to higher matter density than estimated previously.

## Key claim

The five gluon masses in a two-flavor color superconductor are found from the static limit of the polarization operator, and the Meissner effect may postpone the onset of color superconductivity to higher matter density than previously estimated, though the authors only speculate on this point and avoid exact calculations at nonzero chemical potential.

## Method

The authors use instanton-induced quark interactions (the 't Hooft interaction) as the nonperturbative source of diquark condensation, augmented with gauge-invariant interactions between quarks and perturbative gluons via path-ordered exponentials. They compute the gluon polarization operator to one-loop order in the static limit (\(q^2 \rightarrow 0\)) by summing quark loop diagrams, including contributions from Nambu-Goldstone modes of broken color symmetry, and extract the gluon masses from the transverse part of the polarization operator. The gap equation for the diquark condensate is solved self-consistently with the instanton density, and the density dependence of the masses is estimated by assuming they follow the behavior of \(g\Delta(\mu)\).

## Key numbers

* The gap in vacuum is \(\Delta_0 \simeq 400\) MeV, dropping to \(\Delta_0 \simeq 200\) MeV at the phase transition from chiral broken to color superconducting matter.
* At the critical chemical potential \(\mu \approx 300\) MeV, where the color superconductor becomes the stable phase, the gluon masses are about 120 MeV.
* The average instanton size is \(\rho \simeq 1/3\) fm, and the ratio of average instanton size to average inter-instanton spacing is \(\rho/R \simeq 1/3\).
* The wave function renormalization factor in vacuum is \(Z_\phi^{-1} = 8.07 \times 10^{-3}\).
* The fifth gluon (color index 8) has mass \(\sqrt{4/3}\) times that of the four degenerate gluons (color indices 4–7), a relation fixed by symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based approach to color superconductivity developed by Carter and Diakonov (1999) and others, where the diquark condensate is generated nonperturbatively via instanton-induced interactions. The calculation of gluon masses via the polarization operator parallels the Higgs mechanism in the electroweak theory, with the diquark field acting as a composite scalar. The results contrast with the perturbative regime at asymptotically high density, where Meissner masses become proportional to \(g\mu\) as shown by Son (1999) and others, and the paper highlights that the matching between low-density and high-density mechanisms remains unclear. The work enables future studies of the density dependence of the Meissner effect and the phase diagram of dense QCD.
