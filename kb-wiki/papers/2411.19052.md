# Phase diagrams of neutron-proton superfluid in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The finite-temperature phase structures for neutron-proton superfluidity in asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated, with a particular focus on the angular dependence of the pairing gap induced by the $^3SD_1$ $NN$ interaction. This angular dependence of the pairing gap results in the Cooper pair momentum in the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state exhibiting exactly two distinct stable orientations: one orthogonal (FFLO-ADG-O) and the other parallel (FFLO-ADG-P) to the symmetry axis of the pairing gap. The FFLO-ADG-O state dominates at low asymmetries, while the FFLO-ADG-P state prevails at high asymmetries. Additionally, these analysis of normal-superfluid phase separation reveals that the angular dependence of the pairing gap eliminates phase separation in the low-asymmetry regime, whereas the Cooper pair momentum effectively suppresses phase separation at high asymmetries. These two mechanisms act in concert to significantly prevent the occurrence of normal-superfluid phase separation across the entire phase diagram, ensuring the stability of the homogeneous superfluid state over a broad range of asymmetries. These results provide new insights into the interplay between the angular dependence of the pairing gap and the stability of superfluidity in asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the angular dependence of the pairing gap in the \(^3SD_1\) channel eliminates normal-superfluid phase separation in the low-asymmetry regime, while the Cooper pair momentum in the FFLO state suppresses phase separation at high asymmetries, together ensuring the stability of the homogeneous superfluid state over a broad range of asymmetries. This conclusion is conditional on using the bare Argonne \(v18\) potential and a free single-particle spectrum, with the authors noting that screening effects and self-energy corrections may affect the magnitude of the pairing gap but not the overall phase diagram structure.

## Method

The authors solve the Gorkov equations for neutron-proton pairing in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, using the coupled \(^3SD_1\) channel of the Argonne \(v18\) potential with an angle-dependent gap (ADG) that mixes \(S\)-wave and \(D\)-wave components. They compute the free energy, superfluid density tensor (transverse and longitudinal components), and the eigenvalues of the stability matrix \(\mathcal{M}\) to determine the boundaries of the homogeneous superfluid, FFLO, and phase-separated regions in the temperature-asymmetry plane at fixed nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- The net density is fixed at the empirical saturation density of nuclear matter, \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The temperature is normalized by the Fermi temperature \(T_F = k_F^2 / (2m)\) with average Fermi momentum \(k_F = (3\pi^2 \rho / 2)^{1/3}\).
- For the AAG state, a peak of the \(\rho_s = 0\) line appears near the temperature range \(0.109 < T/T_F < 0.114\), indicating emergence and subsequent disappearance of Cooper pair momentum.
- At zero temperature, the entire asymmetry range falls within the normal-superfluid phase separation region for the AAG state.
- For the ADG state, the transverse superfluid density \(\rho_T\) reaches a negative value first with increasing asymmetry, signaling the initial emergence of the FFLO-ADG-Orthogonal state.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of asymmetric two-component superfluid systems, including ultra-cold atomic Fermi gases (Zwierlein 2006, Partridge 2006, Shin 2006) and neutral dense quark matter, where mismatched Fermi surfaces lead to exotic phenomena such as the FFLO state (Fulde 1964, Larkin 1964) and normal-superfluid phase separation (Bedaque 2003, Sheehy 2006). It builds on the authors' prior work (Shang 2013, Shang 2015, Shang 2022) showing that the angle-dependent gap from non-\(S\)-wave pairing reduces suppression from mismatched Fermi surfaces, and that the orientation of Cooper pair momentum in the \(^3SD_1\) channel differs from both \(S\)-wave (He 2006, Sedrakian 2001) and \(D\)-wave pairing (Shang 2022, Zhang 2019). The results enable future studies of neutron-proton pairing in neutron star cooling and rotation dynamics, and may inform models of pion or kaon condensation in nucleon star models (Brown 1994).
