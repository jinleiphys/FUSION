# Reduction of the proton radius discrepancy by 3 sigma

> We show that in previous analyses of electron-proton scattering, the uncertainties in the statistical procedure to extract the proton charge radius are underestimated. Using a fit function based on a conformal mapping, we can describe the scattering data with high precision and extract a radius value in agreement with the one obtained from muonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

Using a fit function based on a conformal mapping that obeys the analytic structure of the form factors, the authors show that the proton charge radius extracted from electron-proton scattering data can be consistent with the muonic hydrogen value, reducing the previously claimed discrepancy. The authors emphasize the illustrative purpose of their work and treat their fits on the same footing as polynomial or spline fits, noting that unconstrained parameters generate an unphysical spectral function.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the elastic electron-proton scattering data from the A1 collaboration at Mainz using a fit function based on a conformal mapping variable z that maps the cut in the t-plane onto the unit circle, with the lowest singularity at t_cut = 4M_pi^2. The Sachs form factors G_E and G_M were expanded in powers of z up to k_max = 10, and the fits were performed using the same 1422 data points with fixed normalization parameters as in the original analysis. The number of terms was increased until the chi-squared reached a plateau, matching the chi-squared value of 1563 found in the original polynomial fit.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius r_E^p = 0.84 fm from the conformal mapping fit with k_max = 10, consistent with the muonic hydrogen value
- Proton magnetic radius r_M^p = 0.85 +/- 0.04 fm from the same fit
- Absolute chi-squared value of 1563 for k_max = 9, matching the best polynomial fit from Bernauer (2010)
- The fit used 1422 data points from the A1 collaboration

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation analysis of Lorenz (2012) and directly compares to the polynomial and spline fits of Bernauer (2010) that had yielded a proton charge radius differing by approximately 7 sigma from the muonic hydrogen measurement of Pohl (2010). The authors note that a previous analysis using conformal mapping by Hill and Paz (2010) used older form factor data extracted via the Rosenbluth method and showed strong ambiguity due to fit range. This work enables a later publication incorporating physics constraints from realistic spectral functions and asymptotic behavior to unambiguously distinguish between purely statistical and theoretically motivated data analyses.
