# Reflection-asymmetric nuclear deformations within the Density Functional Theory

> Within the nuclear density functional theory (DFT) we study the effect of reflection-asymmetric shapes on ground-state binding energies and binding energy differences. To this end, we developed the new DFT solver AxialHFB that uses an approximate second-order gradient to solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations of superconducting DFT with the quasi-local Skyrme energy density functionals. Illustrative calculations are carried out for even-even isotopes of radium and thorium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including reflection-asymmetric (octupole) deformations in density functional theory calculations improves the agreement with experimental data for the double-difference binding energy indicator δVpn in even-even radium and thorium isotopes, while two-nucleon separation energies S2n and S2p are not significantly affected. The hedge is that the improvement is demonstrated only for these two isotopic chains as illustrative calculations, and the spike around N=130 in the δVpn results is an artifact of mean-field theory that would be washed out by beyond-mean-field effects.

## Method

The authors developed a new DFT solver called AxialHFB that solves the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations using an approximate second-order gradient method, in contrast to the direct diagonalization technique used in the parity-conserving code HFBTHO. They computed 2D potential energy surfaces for even-even isotopes of radium and thorium by constraining both the axial quadrupole moment Q20 (characterizing elongation) and the octupole moment Q30 (characterizing reflection asymmetry), using the Skyrme energy density functional SLy4 with mixed pairing. For each isotope, 525 constrained calculations were performed over a grid of Q20 and Q30 values, followed by unconstrained calculations starting from the grid minimum to obtain precise ground-state binding energies.

## Key numbers

- 525 constrained calculations were performed for each isotope, with Q30 values ranging from 0 to 6000 fm³ in steps of 250 fm³
- The oscillator basis used 21 oscillator shells, corresponding to 2024 basis states
- For ²²⁰Ra, the calculation yielded a well-developed minimum with β₂ = 0.20 and β₃ = 0.198
- For ²¹⁶Ra, the ground state was spherical (β₂ = 0.00, β₃ = 0.00)
- For ²⁴⁰Ra, the ground state had β₂ = 0.24 and β₃ = 0.0 (reflection-symmetric)
- The octupole deformation β₃ maximizes around neutron number N=138 and gradually decreases with increasing neutron number

## Context

This work extends the nuclear DFT framework to include reflection-asymmetric shapes, building on prior studies of octupole correlations in nuclei such as Nazarewicz (1992, 1994) and Robledo (1987). The new AxialHFB solver enables systematic calculations of octupole-deformed ground states across the nuclear chart, which was previously difficult with parity-conserving codes like HFBTHO (Stoitsov, 2005). The results enable future large-scale mass table calculations including reflection-asymmetric shapes on leadership-class supercomputers, and the authors plan to extend the method to odd-A nuclei and test different energy density functionals.
