# Beta-Delayed Proton Emission Branches in 43Cr

> The beta+ decay of very neutron deficient 43Cr has been studied by means of an imaging time projection chamber which allowed recording tracks of charged particles. Events of beta-delayed emission of one-, two-, and three protons were clearly identified. The absolute branching ratios for these channels were determined to be 81(4)%, 7.1(4)%, and 0.08(3)%, respectively. The 43Cr is thus established as the second case in which the beta-3p decay occurs. Although the feeding to the proton-bound states in 43V is expected to be negligible, the large branching ratio of 12(4)% for decays without proton emission is found.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of beta-delayed three-proton emission in 43Cr, making it the second nucleus (after 45Fe) known to exhibit this decay mode, but the branching ratio for this channel is very small at 0.08(3)%, and the surprisingly large 12(4)% branching ratio for decays without proton emission contradicts expectations from isospin symmetry arguments.

## Method

The experiment used an Optical Time Projection Chamber (OTPC) at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory to record tracks of charged particles from the beta+ decay of 43Cr ions produced by fragmentation of a 161 MeV/nucleon 58Ni beam on a natural nickel target. Ions were selected using the A1900 fragment separator and identified by time-of-flight and energy-loss measurements, then stopped in the OTPC active volume. The OTPC recorded CCD images and photomultiplier tube time profiles of decay events, allowing identification of one-, two-, and three-proton emission channels, with absolute branching ratios determined from analysis of events collected in asynchronous mode.

## Key numbers

* Absolute branching ratio for beta-delayed one-proton emission: 81(4)%
* Absolute branching ratio for beta-delayed two-proton emission: 7.1(4)%
* Absolute branching ratio for beta-delayed three-proton emission: 0.08(3)%
* Absolute branching ratio for decays without proton emission: 12(4)%
* Half-life of 43Cr: 20.6 +/- 0.9 ms (consistent with the literature value of 21.1 +/- 0.4 ms from Dossat et al.)
* Total number of 43Cr events selected for analysis: about 40000, with 11502 single-proton, 1010 two-proton, and 12 three-proton events observed in synchronous mode

## Context

This work extends the study of beta-delayed multiparticle emission beyond the first observed case of beta-3p decay in 45Fe reported by Miernik et al. (2006), and provides the first direct absolute branching ratios for all proton-emission channels in 43Cr, improving upon the earlier work by Dossat et al. (2005) which could only determine a total proton-emission probability of 92.5(28)% without separating individual channels. The observation of a large branching ratio for decays without proton emission challenges the expected isospin symmetry between the mirror decays of 43Cr and 43K, and suggests the need for further studies combining high-resolution gamma and proton spectroscopy to understand the competition between gamma emission and proton emission from unbound states.
