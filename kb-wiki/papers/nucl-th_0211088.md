# Covariant kaon dynamics and kaon flow in heavy ion collisions

> The influence of the chiral mean field on the $K^+$ transverse flow in heavy ion collisions at SIS energy is investigated within covariant kaon dynamics. For the kaon mesons inside the nuclear medium a quasi-particle picture including scalar and vector fields is adopted and compared to the standard treatment with a static potential. It is confirmed that a Lorentz force from spatial component of the vector field provides an important contribution to the in-medium kaon dynamics and strongly counterbalances the influence of the vector potential on the $K^+$ in-plane flow. The FOPI data can be reasonably described using in-medium kaon potentials based on effective chiral models. The information on the in-medium $K^+$ potential extracted from kaon flow is consistent with the knowledge from other sources.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that a Lorentz force arising from the spatial component of the vector field provides an important contribution to in-medium kaon dynamics and strongly counterbalances the influence of the vector potential on the K+ in-plane flow, making it possible to describe the FOPI data with a relatively strong repulsive K+ potential; however, due to the smallness of the flow signal and present theoretical and experimental error bars, definite quantitative statements on the strength of the kaon potential require further investigation.

## Method

The authors performed transport calculations using Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) for the baryon dynamics, treating kaons as quasi-particles with effective masses and momenta derived from a chiral Lagrangian. The covariant equations of motion for kaons, which include a Lorentz-force-like term from the spatial components of the vector field, were solved and compared to the standard non-covariant treatment using a static potential. Two parameterizations of the in-medium kaon potential were applied: the Brown and Rho parameterization (BRP) with a Sigma_KN of 450 MeV and a reduced in-medium pion decay constant, and the Ko and Li parameterization (KLP) with a Sigma_KN of 350 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The repulsive K+ potential at saturation density for the Brown and Rho parameterization is approximately 30 MeV (U_K(rho_0) approximately 30 MeV), which is consistent with the impulse approximation.
- The Ko and Li parameterization gives a much weaker potential of approximately 5 MeV at saturation density (U_K(rho_0) approximately 5 MeV).
- The analysis used 1.93 AGeV 58Ni + 58Ni collisions at impact parameter b less than or equal to 4 fm, corresponding to the FOPI centrality cut.
- For the K+ production threshold, the Sigma_KN value used in the BRP is 450 MeV, and the in-medium pion decay constant squared is taken as 0.6 times the free pion decay constant squared for the vector field.
- The asymmetry parameter a=1.2 was used to reproduce the angular dependence of elementary BB to BYK+ cross sections.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon flow by Ko and Li (1995) and others that used non-covariant dynamics with a static potential, and directly addresses the earlier finding by Fuchs et al. (1998) that the Lorentz-force contribution was missing in those treatments. The results are compared to the new FOPI data from 1999 with improved precision, and the extracted in-medium K+ potential is found to be consistent with information from p+A reactions at COSY-ANKE and with the impulse approximation. The study enables a more reliable extraction of kaon in-medium properties from heavy ion collisions and shows that the kaon flow signal is not sensitive to the nuclear equation of state.
