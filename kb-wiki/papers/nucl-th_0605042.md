# From multifragmentation to supernovae and neutron stars

> The thermodynamics properties of globally neutral dense stellar matter are analyzed both in terms of mean field instabilities and structures beyond the mean field. The mean field response to finite wavelenght fluctuations is calculated with the realistic Sly230a effective interaction. A Monte Carlo simulation of a schematic lattice Hamiltonian shows the importance of calculations beyond the mean field to calculate the phase diagram of stellar matter. The analogies and differences respect to the thermodynamics of nuclear matter and finite nuclei are stressed.

# From multifragmentation to supernovae and neutron stars

## Key claim

The first-order liquid-gas phase transition of neutral nuclear matter is suppressed in globally neutral stellar matter due to high electron incompressibility, but a second-order transition between single-phase and coexistence configurations is still expected; however, the coexistence region is expanded relative to mean-field predictions, and the critical point is replaced by a first-order endpoint, meaning critical opalescence and divergent correlation lengths do not occur.

## Method

The authors compute the mean-field curvature matrix for dense stellar matter using the Sly230a Skyrme effective interaction, including finite-wavelength density fluctuations with gradient and Coulomb terms. They then perform Monte Carlo simulations of a schematic three-dimensional lattice Ising model with short-range nuclear-like interactions and long-range Coulomb-like interactions, using a multi-canonical ensemble to treat the two energy components independently. The model enforces global charge neutrality via a uniform electron background and uses periodic replicas to accelerate thermodynamic convergence.

## Key numbers

- At temperature T = 10 MeV, the lowest eigenvalue of the curvature matrix for homogeneous stellar matter is always positive, independent of temperature and proton fraction, indicating suppression of the spinodal zone.
- The instability envelope for finite-wavelength fluctuations exists even at high temperatures (T = 10 MeV shown) and is almost independent of the effective interaction employed.
- The lattice model uses N = L³ sites with L unspecified; the finite lattice is repeated a large number N_R of times in all three directions.
- For the uncharged Ising model, the critical point shows diverging Coulomb energy with increasing lattice size; for the charged system at the end point of the coexistence zone, the fluctuation does not increase with lattice size.

## Context

This work extends the study of dense stellar matter thermodynamics from mean-field treatments (Pethick, Ravenhall) to include fluctuations beyond mean field, using the Sly230a interaction previously applied to neutron star crusts by Haensel. It draws analogies to multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions modeled by the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (Raduta). The results contradict mean-field expectations that Coulomb energy reduces the instability region, and they support the possibility of pasta phases (Ravenhall, Watanabe, Horowitz) surviving at high temperatures. The suppression of critical opalescence has implications for neutrino scattering opacity and proto-neutron star cooling dynamics (Margueron).
