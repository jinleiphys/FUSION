# Symmetry-Based Approach to Shape Coexistence in Nuclei

> A symmetry-based approach for describing shape-coexistence, is presented in the framework of the interacting boson model of nuclei. It involves a construction of a number-conserving Hamiltonian which preserves the dynamical symmetry of selected bands associated with each shape, while breaking the symmetries in other states. The resulting structure embodies multiple partial dynamical symmetries. The procedure is applied to prolate-oblate and spherical-prolate-oblate coexistence, at and slightly away from the critical points.

## Key claim

The paper presents a symmetry-based approach to shape coexistence in the interacting boson model (IBM) that constructs a single number-conserving Hamiltonian preserving dynamical symmetry in selected bands associated with each shape while breaking symmetries in other states, embodying multiple partial dynamical symmetries (PDS). The approach is applied specifically at and slightly away from critical points where multiple minima in the energy surface are near-degenerate.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian that simultaneously annihilates basis states from incompatible dynamical symmetry chains (e.g., SU(3) for prolate, \(\overline{\text{SU(3)}}\) for oblate, U(5) for spherical shapes), ensuring these selected bands remain solvable eigenstates with good quantum numbers. The intrinsic part of the Hamiltonian is built from higher-order boson operators (\(P_0^\dagger\), \(G_3^\dagger\)) that enforce the vanishing conditions, while a collective part containing the SO(3) Casimir operator and a small \(s\)-parity breaking term (\(\alpha\hat{\theta}_2\)) lifts degeneracies and generates rotational spectra. The procedure is demonstrated for prolate-oblate coexistence and spherical-prolate-oblate triple coexistence.

## Key numbers

- For prolate-oblate coexistence, the normal mode frequencies about the deformed minima are \(\epsilon_{\beta 1} = \epsilon_{\beta 2} = \frac{8}{3}(h_0 + 2h_2)N^2\) and \(\epsilon_{\gamma 1} = \epsilon_{\gamma 2} = 4\eta_3 N^2\) for large \(N\).
- For spherical-prolate-oblate coexistence with \(h_0=0\), the quadrupole vibration frequency about the spherical minimum is \(\epsilon = 4h_2 N^2\).
- The oblate \(g_2\)-band experiences a shift of order \(\frac{32}{9}\alpha N^2\) relative to the prolate \(g_1\)-band when the \(s\)-parity breaking term is introduced.
- The analytic quadrupole moment for the prolate \(g_1\)-band (minus sign) and oblate \(g_2\)-band (plus sign) is \(Q_L = \mp e_B \sqrt{\frac{16\pi}{40}} \frac{L}{2L+3} \frac{4(2N-L)(2N+L+1)}{3(2N-1)}\).
- The \(B(E2)\) value for intraband transitions (\(g_i, L+2 \to g_i, L\)) is \(e_B^2 \frac{3(L+1)(L+2)}{2(2L+3)(2L+5)} \frac{(4N-1)^2(2N-L)(2N+L+3)}{18(2N-1)^2}\).
- For triple coexistence, the \(B(E2)\) value for the spherical transition \(n_d=1, L=2 \to n_d=0, L=0\) is \(e_B^2 N\).
- The \(n_d=2\) component in the \(L=0,2,4\) states of the deformed ground bands is of order \(N^3 3^{-N}\).

## Context

This work extends the partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) concept, previously applied to single-shape nuclei (Leviatan 1996, Leviatan and Sinai 1999, Casten 2014), to describe multiple coexisting shapes with incompatible symmetries. It provides an algebraic alternative to the IBM with configuration mixing approach (Duval and Barrett 1981, Foisson 2003, Frank 2004, Morales 2008), which uses separate Hamiltonians for normal and intruder configurations with a number-non-conserving mixing term. The results enable analytic, parameter-free predictions for \(E2\) observables and selection rules that can be tested against experimental data in nuclei exhibiting shape coexistence, such as Kr isotopes (Clement 2007), Se isotopes (Ljungvall 2008), Hg isotopes (Bree 2014), and \(^{186}\)Pb (Andreyev 2000).
