# Is the LHCb $P_c(4312)^+$ plausible in the GlueX $γp\to J/ψp$ total cross sections ?

> New high-statistics total cross section data for $γp\to J/ψp$ from the GLUonic EXcitation (GlueX) experiment are fitted in a search for the exotic $P_c(4312)^+$ state observed by the Large Hadron Collider beauty (LHCb) collaboration. The integrated luminosity of this GlueX experiment was about $320~\mathrm{pb^{-1}}$. The fits show that destructive interference involving an $S$-wave resonance and associated non-resonance background produces a sharp dip structure about $75~\mathrm{MeV}$ below the LHCb mass, in the same location as a similar structure is seen in the data. Limitations of the employed model and the need for improved statistics are discussed.

## Key claim

The GlueX total cross section data for γp → J/ψp can be fitted with a model where destructive interference between an S-wave resonance and a non-resonant background produces a sharp dip about 75 MeV below the LHCb Pc(4312)+ mass, but the statistics are insufficient to draw a definite conclusion about the existence of the pentaquark in photoproduction.

## Method

The authors fit new high-statistics GlueX total cross section data (integrated luminosity ~320 pb−1) for γp → J/ψp using a partial-wave amplitude that separates a non-resonant background (parameterized as a polynomial in the J/ψ center-of-mass momentum q) and a resonance term (relativistic Breit-Wigner) with a free relative phase shift α to allow either constructive or destructive interference. The fit uses six free parameters: resonance mass M, total width Γ, partial-width factor X, phase α, and two background coefficients A and B.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit resonance mass is M = 4235 ± 8 MeV, which is about 77 MeV below the LHCb Pc(4312)+ mass of 4311.9 ± 0.7 MeV.
- The best-fit total width is Γ = 35.4 ± 8.2 MeV, compared to the LHCb value of 9.8 ± 2.7 MeV for Pc(4312)+.
- The best-fit relative phase is α = 40.8 ± 5.7 degrees, indicating destructive interference.
- The χ²/ndf for the resonance-plus-background fit is 11.99/12 = 1.00, while the non-resonant-only fit gives 19.74/16 = 1.23.
- The dip structure in the GlueX data near W = 4.3 GeV has a local significance of 2.6σ, which reduces to 1.4σ when accounting for the full energy range (W = 4.05–4.71 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier GlueX analysis (Ali et al., 2019) that found no evidence for LHCb pentaquark candidates, using a new dataset with roughly five times higher statistics (2270 ± 58 events versus 469 ± 22). The authors relax the assumption of constructive interference used in prior interpretations and consider destructive interference between a resonance and background, following the approach of Strakovsky et al. (1984) and Levi-Setti (1973). The results motivate new high-statistics experiments to disentangle the resonance signal from open-charm cusp effects (Du et al., 2020) and gluon-exchange contributions.
