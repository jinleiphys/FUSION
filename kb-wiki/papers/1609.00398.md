# Net Baryon Fluctuations from a Crossover Equation of State

> We have constructed an equation of state which smoothly interpolates between an excluded volume hadron resonance gas at low energy density to a plasma of quarks and gluons at high energy density. This crossover equation of state agrees very well with lattice calculations at both zero and nonzero baryon chemical potential. We use it to compute the variance, skewness, and kurtosis of fluctuations of baryon number, and compare to measurements of proton number fluctuations in central Au-Au collisions as measured by the STAR collaboration in a beam energy scan at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. The crossover equation of state can reproduce the data if the fluctuations are frozen out at temperatures well below than the average chemical freeze-out.

## Key claim

The crossover equation of state can reproduce STAR measurements of net-proton fluctuations in central Au-Au collisions, but only if the fluctuations are assumed to freeze out at temperatures well below (about 26 MeV lower than) the average chemical freeze-out temperature.

## Method

The authors constructed a pressure function P(T,μ) that smoothly interpolates between an excluded-volume hadron resonance gas at low energy density and a perturbative QCD plasma at high energy density using a switching function S(T,μ). They computed the variance, skewness, and kurtosis of baryon number fluctuations from this equation of state and compared them to STAR collaboration measurements of net-proton multiplicity distributions in the 5% most central Au-Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The excluded volume model exI best fit gave n=5, T0=177.1 MeV, and ε0^(1/4)=306.5 MeV with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.34
- The excluded volume model exII best fit gave n=5, T0=177.6 MeV, and ε0^(1/4)=279.7 MeV with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.34
- The point particle model best fit gave n=4 and T0=145.3 MeV with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.56
- The hard core radius of the proton and neutron from exI is 0.580 fm and from exII is 0.655 fm
- The temperature parameter a in the freeze-out curve was reduced from 166 MeV to 140 MeV (a 26 MeV decrease) to match the STAR fluctuation data
- The ratio κ/S^2 shows excellent agreement with STAR data at the four lowest beam energies: 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, and 27 GeV

## Context

This work extends the crossover equation of state previously constructed by the same authors (Albright 2014, Albright 2015) and compares it to STAR beam energy scan data. Fukushima made a similar comparison using a point particle hadron resonance gas and a relativistic mean field model. The authors find that baryon fluctuations must freeze out at temperatures consistent with kinetic freeze-out rather than chemical freeze-out, and they note that their study does not suggest evidence for a critical point in the energy range 7.7 to 27 GeV under their assumptions. The work enables future studies incorporating a critical point via modified switching functions and higher statistics measurements from NICA.
