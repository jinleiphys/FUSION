# New method of analytic continuation of elastic-scattering data to the negative-energy region and asymptotic normalization coefficients for $^{17}$O and $^{13}$C

> A new method is proposed for extrapolation of elastic-scattering data to the negative-energy region for a short-range interaction. The method is based on the analytic approximation of the modulus-squared of the partial-wave scattering amplitude. It is shown that the proposed method has an advantage over the traditional one based on continuation of the effective-range function. The new method has been applied to determine the asymptotic normalization coefficients for the $^{17}$O and $^{13}$C nuclei in the $n+^{16}$O and $n+^{12}$C channels, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method for extrapolating neutron elastic-scattering data to negative energies that is based on analytic approximation of the modulus-squared of the partial-wave scattering amplitude, which the authors claim has faster convergence and higher accuracy than the traditional effective-range function method, but the extracted asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from experimental data show some variation depending on which version of the method is used.

## Method

The method approximates the modulus-squared of the partial-wave scattering amplitude, M_l(E) = |f_l(E)|^2, by a polynomial or rational function of energy E for positive energies, then analytically continues this function to the bound-state pole at negative energy to extract the ANC from the residue. Two versions are tested: one using the function F_l(E) derived from N_l(E) = K_l^2(E) + k^(4l+2), and another using G_l(E) from the relation M_l(E) = sin^2(delta_l)/k^2. The method is applied to s-wave neutron scattering on 16O and 12C, using both theoretical phase shifts from a square-well potential and experimental phase shifts from the literature, with polynomial fits of varying degree N and chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers

- For the n+16O system (J^pi = 1/2+ state, bound state at epsilon_1 = 3.27227 MeV experimental binding energy), the exact ANC from the square-well potential is C_0 = 2.83896 fm^{-1/2}; the best G_0(E) fit (N=9) reproduces this to six significant digits (2.83896 fm^{-1/2}).
- For n+16O experimental phase shifts, the mean ANC from all three continuation methods is C_0 = 2.23 +/- 0.30 fm^{-1/2}.
- For the n+12C system (J^pi = 1/2+ state, bound state at epsilon_2 = 1.856557 MeV), the exact ANC from the square-well potential is C_0 = 1.60 fm^{-1/2}; the best G_0(E) fit (N=7) gives C_0 = 1.60000 fm^{-1/2}.
- For n+12C experimental phase shifts, the mean ANC from all three continuation methods is C_0 = 2.07 +/- 0.13 fm^{-1/2}.
- For n+16O theoretical phase shifts, the ERF method best fit (N=9) gives C_0 = 2.84815 fm^{-1/2} (0.3% deviation from exact), while the G_0(E) method at N=9 gives the exact value to six digits.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on analytic continuation of scattering data for charged particles (Blokhintsev et al., 2015, 2017, 2018) to the case of neutron scattering with only short-range interactions. The new method avoids the differentiation step required in the traditional effective-range function approach used by earlier works (e.g., Sparenberg et al., 2000; Irgaziev et al., 2011), which impairs accuracy. The extracted ANCs for 17O and 13C can be used to evaluate cross sections of peripheral astrophysical nuclear reactions, and using mirror symmetry the neutron ANCs can be converted to proton ANCs for mirror nuclei such as 17F.
