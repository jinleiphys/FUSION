# Multi-winding flux tubes in CFL quark matter

> Color-flavor locked quark matter can be described as a three-component superconductor and thus shows unconventional behavior in the transition regime from type-I to type-II superconductivity. We discuss this behavior by studying magnetic line defects in a Ginzburg-Landau approach, taking into account all possible values of the three winding numbers. After a brief discussion of the defects that include baryon circulation we focus on pure magnetic flux tubes. We show that at strong coupling, relevant for neutron stars, type-II behavior is conceivable and the most preferred configuration has minimal total winding. Only at weak coupling we find a regime where multi-winding flux tubes are preferred, although this regime most likely requires an unrealistically large superconducting gap.

# Multi-winding flux tubes in CFL quark matter

## Key claim

Color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter, described as a three-component superconductor, can exhibit type-II behavior at strong coupling relevant for neutron stars, where the most preferred magnetic flux tube configuration has minimal total winding; multi-winding flux tubes are preferred only at weak coupling, but this regime likely requires an unrealistically large superconducting gap.

## Method

The authors study magnetic line defects in CFL quark matter using a Ginzburg-Landau approach with three complex scalar fields (diagonal order parameter) coupled to two gauge fields (one gluon field and one mixed gluon-photon field). They solve the coupled equations of motion numerically via successive over-relaxation for all possible winding number combinations with zero baryon circulation, computing free energies per unit length and critical magnetic fields H_c1 for each configuration.

## Key numbers

- The weak-coupling value of the coupling ratio is η = h/λ = -1/2, derived from perturbative QCD parameters.
- The critical strong coupling constant for the transition between CFL and normal phase versus CFL and 2SC phase is g_c ≈ 0.16 (using η = -1/2).
- For g = 0.1 and T_c/μ_q ≈ 0.009, the configuration (1,1,-2) is preferred over (1,0,-1) in a certain regime of T_c/μ_q.
- For g = 3.5 and T_c/μ_q ≈ 0.086, the configuration (1,0,-1) is the preferred configuration in the entire type-II regime.
- For g = 0.1 and T_c/μ_q = 0.004, the multi-winding flux tube (3,3,-6) becomes the preferred configuration.
- The Ginzburg-Landau parameters for the two gauge fields are κ_3 = √(2λ/g²) and κ̃_8 = √(2λ/(g² + (4/3)e²)).
- The eigenvalues of the scalar mass matrix are ν_1 = 2(1-2η) and ν_2 = 2(1+η); at weak coupling ν_1 = 4 and ν_2 = 1.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of CFL flux tubes by Iida (2002, 2004) and the authors' own earlier work (Haber 2017), generalizing the asymptotic analysis to three potentially different components with both baryon circulation and magnetic flux. It also connects to studies of unconventional superconductivity in two-band systems (Carlstrom 2010) and neutron star matter (Buckley 2004, Alford 2007, Haber 2016, 2017). The results enable future investigations of CFL behavior under combined external magnetic field and rotation, relevant for neutron star "color-magnetic mountains" that could produce detectable gravitational waves (Glampedakis 2012).
