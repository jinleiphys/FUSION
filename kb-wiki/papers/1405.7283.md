# Study of Rotating High Energy Systems with the Differential HBT Method

> Peripheral heavy ion reactions at ultra relativistic energies have large angular momentum that can be studied via two particle correlations using the Differential Hanbury Brown and Twiss method. In the present work we analyze the possibilities and sensitivity of the method in rotating, few source systems. Analytic results provide insight in the advantages of this method.

## Key claim

The Differential HBT method can detect rotation in peripheral heavy-ion collisions through two-particle correlations, but the direction of rotation is not observable in idealized symmetric few-source models; sensitivity to the rotation direction only appears when asymmetric emission probabilities (due to source self-shadowing and detector orientation) are included.

## Method

The authors construct analytic few-source models (one, two, and four Gaussian fluid cells) with Jüttner (relativistic Boltzmann) phase-space distributions and compute the two-pion correlation function \(C(k,q)\) using the standard HBT formalism with the emission function \(S(x,k)\). They introduce asymmetric emission weights \(w_c\) and \(w_s\) for sources closer to and farther from the detector, breaking the symmetry between source pairs, and evaluate the correlation function for different orientations of the average momentum \(\vec{k}\) and relative momentum \(\vec{q}\).

## Key numbers

- Vorticity in fluid dynamical model calculations at LHC Pb+Pb energies exceeds \(3\) c/fm, with circulation after \(6\) fm/c of flow development around \(4\text{--}5\) fm\(\cdot\)c (from prior work cited in the paper).
- In the four-source model with circulation \(\Gamma = 5\) fm\(\cdot\)c, correlation functions are shown for source velocities \(v=0.5c\) with displacement \(d_x = d_z = 1.6\) fm, and for \(v=0.8c\) with displacement \(d_x = d_z = 1.0\) fm, at temperature \(T_s = 0.20\) GeV.
- The correlation function \(C(k_x,q_x)\) for four sources is plotted for \(k_x\) values ranging from \(0.5\) to \(6.0\) fm\(^{-1}\) (blue lines) and \(0.1\) to \(1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) (green lines).
- The fluid cell size in high-resolution 3+1D fluid dynamical calculations is \((0.3\text{ fm})^3\).

## Context

This work extends the Differential HBT method introduced in Csernai, Velle, and Wang (2014) for measuring rotation in heavy-ion collisions, using simple analytic few-source models to isolate the effects of rotation on correlation functions. It builds on prior fluid dynamical estimates of rotation and vorticity from Csernai et al. (2012) and the Kelvin-Helmholtz instability studies of Csernai et al. (2012), and it addresses the need for alternative observables beyond the weak \(v_1\) flow signal at high energies. The results enable future experimental searches for rotation signatures in peripheral collisions at RHIC and LHC, provided that event-by-event reaction plane and center-of-mass identification (e.g., using zero-degree calorimeters) are performed.
