# Benchmarking Nuclear Fission Theory

> We suggest a small set of fission observables to be used as test cases for validation of theoretical calculations. The purpose is to provide common data to facilitate the comparison of different fission theories and models. The proposed observables are chosen from fission barriers, spontaneous fission lifetimes, fission yield characteristics, and fission isomer excitation energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a small, curated set of well-established fission observables to serve as common benchmarks for validating and comparing different theoretical fission models, but it explicitly notes that the chosen observables (fission barriers, mass distributions, total kinetic energies, spontaneous fission lifetimes, and fission isomer excitation energies) leave out many complex phenomena for which theory is less developed.

## Method

The authors selected benchmark cases from evaluated empirical data for fission barrier heights (15 even-even actinides from Th to Cm), spontaneous fission half-lives (6 nuclei from Pu to Rf), fission isomer excitation energies (9 even-even nuclei from U to Cm), mass distribution moments (for thermal neutron-induced fission of 235U, 239Pu, 255Fm and spontaneous fission of 252Cf), and average pre-neutron total kinetic energies (for 233U, 235U, 239Pu thermal neutron fission and 252Cf spontaneous fission). They recommend reporting the mean and variance of the logarithm of the ratio of theory to experiment for quantities spanning many orders of magnitude, and suggest that results should be presented without adjusting theory parameters to the benchmark data.

## Key numbers

- Fission barrier heights for 15 even-even actinides: inner barrier EA ranges from 4.8 MeV (234U) to 7.15 MeV (241Cm); outer barrier EB ranges from 4.8 MeV (248Cm) to 6.8 MeV (230Th); uncertainties range from 0.3 MeV to 1 MeV
- Spontaneous fission half-lives: 240Pu at 1.14 +/- 0.010 x 10^11 years; 252Cf at 86 +/- 1 year; 254Fm at 228 +/- 1 day; 258Fm at 0.37 +/- 0.02 ms; 256Rf at 6.2 +/- 0.2 ms; 260Rf at 20 +/- 1 ms
- Fission isomer excitation energies: 236U at 2750 keV; 238U at 2557.9 keV; 240Pu at approximately 2800 keV; 242Pu at approximately 2000 keV; 244Cm at approximately 2200 keV
- Mass distribution moments for higher-mass fragments: S_> values of 22.0 (236U*), 19.9 (240Pu*), 18.0 (252Cf), 12.3 (256Fm*); sigma_> values of 5.1, 5.7, 6.4, and 6.9 respectively
- Average pre-neutron total kinetic energies: 170.1 +/- 0.5 MeV (233U nth,f); 170.5 +/- 0.5 MeV (235U nth,f); 177.9 +/- 0.5 MeV (239Pu nth,f); 184.1 +/- 1.3 MeV (252Cf sf)

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of fission data evaluation by providing a minimal, focused benchmark set for theorists, drawing on the RIPL-3 compilation (Capote 2009) for fission barriers and the compilation by Holden (2000) for spontaneous fission half-lives. The paper arose from the INT Program 13-3 on quantitative large-amplitude shape dynamics, and it enables direct comparison between different theoretical approaches (e.g., macroscopic-microscopic models, self-consistent mean-field theories) by providing common reference data and recommended performance measures (mean and variance of log ratios). The authors intend this as a living document to be updated as fission theory improves.
