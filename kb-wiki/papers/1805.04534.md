# S- and p-wave structure of $S=-1$ meson-baryon scattering in the resonance region

> We perform a simultaneous analysis of s- and p-waves of the $S=-1$ meson-baryon scattering amplitude using all low-energy experimental data. For the first time, differential cross section data are included for chiral unitary coupled-channel models. From this model s- and p-wave amplitudes are extracted and we observe both well-known $I(J^P)=0(1/2^-)$ s-wave states as well as a new $I(J^P)=1(1/2^+)$ state absent in quark models and lattice QCD results. Multiple statistical and phenomenological tests suggest that, while the data clearly require an $I=1$ p-wave resonance, the new state just accounts for the absence of the decuplet $Σ(1385)3/2^+$ in the model.

## Key claim

The data clearly require an I=1 p-wave resonance, but the new I(J^P)=1(1/2^+) state found in the model just accounts for the absence of the decuplet Σ(1385)3/2^+ which is not included in the model.

## Method

The authors perform a simultaneous analysis of s- and p-waves of S=-1 meson-baryon scattering using a chiral unitary coupled-channel model based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The interaction kernel is taken from chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order, with low-energy constants and regularization parameters fitted to all available low-energy experimental data including total cross sections, differential cross sections (for the first time in such models), and threshold decay ratios. The scattering amplitude is analytically continued to the second Riemann sheet to extract resonance pole positions and couplings.

## Key numbers

- χ²/dof = 1.10 for the best fit, with contributions from data sets (a) total cross sections (62%), (b) differential cross sections (30%), and (c) threshold data (8%)
- Pole positions: I(J^P)=0(1/2^-) at 1430-15i MeV (narrow Λ(1405) pole) and 1360-43i MeV (broad Λ(1405) pole); I(J^P)=1(1/2^+) at 1360-11i MeV (new Σ(1380)1/2^+ state)
- Generalized variance |Σ| = 0.106 GeV^{-14} for the full fit, increasing to 9.74 GeV^{-14} when differential cross section data are omitted
- Likelihood ratio of fit at λ_crit (where p-wave resonance is forced to disappear) to best fit is 4.3 × 10^{-28}
- When Σ(1385)3/2^+ is explicitly included, its pole position is 1.366-0.009i GeV, with predicted decay width ratio Γ(Σπ)/Γ(Λπ)=0.183 compared to the PDG value of 0.135

## Context

This work extends chiral unitary coupled-channel models of S=-1 meson-baryon scattering (Mai 2014, Ikeda 2011, Guo 2012) by including differential cross section data for the first time and simultaneously describing s- and p-waves without introducing explicit states. The two Λ(1405) poles agree well with previous analyses, but the new Σ(1380)1/2^+ state is absent in quark models (Xie 2014, Helminen 2000) and lattice QCD results (Edwards 2012, Engel 2013). The finding that this state accounts for the missing Σ(1385)3/2^+ enables future work to include the decuplet baryon explicitly in the model, which would require incorporating spin-3/2 fields in both s- and u-channels.
