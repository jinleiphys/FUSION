# Hydrodynamical Evolution of a Quark-Gluon Plasma Drop With Boundary

> We consider the evolution of a spherically symmetric Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) drop with limited size in the framework of relativistic hydrodynamics. In the presence of the boundary, the expanding and cooling of QGP drop may appear as oscillating processes, which realize via multiple traveling of rarefaction and compression waves through the plasma and lead to increasing of plasma life-time. Within this picture, deflagration is the main possible way of hadronization. The slow cooling mechanism also leads to a significant enhancement of photon yield in comparison with Bjorken scenario.

# Hydrodynamical Evolution of a Quark-Gluon Plasma Drop With Boundary

## Key claim

The expanding and cooling of a spherically symmetric Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) drop with a boundary may appear as oscillating processes, realized via multiple traveling of rarefaction and compression waves through the plasma, which leads to an increased plasma lifetime; deflagration is the main possible way of hadronization because supersonic phase transitions are impossible, as the pressure can never fall below a minimum value of approximately -B/2.

## Method

The authors performed a direct numerical analysis of relativistic hydrodynamical equations (momentum and energy conservation) for a spherically symmetric, baryonless QGP drop, using an MIT-motivated equation of state with bag constant B. They employed a combined Eulerian-Lagrangian grid scheme based on the Flux-Corrected Transport (FCT) method, with the cell adjacent to the moving phase boundary treated as Lagrangian while internal cells remained Eulerian. The deflagration was simulated as a process starting at the outer surface and spreading toward the center with a constant velocity of 0.1 c, and the total yield of thermal photons was calculated by integrating the hydrodynamical evolution and using analytical expressions for photon emission from gluon Compton scattering and quark annihilation.

## Key numbers

- Initial drop radius: r0 = 3 fm, with initial pressure p0 = 6.5 GeV/fm³ and bag constant B = 0.2 GeV/fm³ (for the zero initial velocity case).
- The minimum pressure achievable during overcooling is p_min ≈ -B/2, which for B = 0.2 GeV/fm³ gives p_min ≈ -0.1 GeV/fm³.
- The threshold pressure for deflagration is p_th = 30 MeV/fm³.
- The deflagration velocity was taken as v_def = 0.1 c.
- Photon yield calculations were performed for initial temperatures T0 = 258 MeV and T0 = 319 MeV.
- The parameter α in the equation of state is α ≈ 23π²/45, corresponding to N_f = 2.5 (u-, d-, s-quarks).

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1983) by relaxing the assumption of space-time scaling and explicitly including a boundary between the QGP drop and vacuum, which leads to oscillatory expansion and cooling via propagating waves. The approach is complementary to Pratt (1993), who studied a spherically symmetric QGP drop focusing on pion-pion Bose-Einstein correlations, whereas this work revises the emission of thermal photons and includes both equilibrium and nonequilibrium hadronization scenarios. The results enable improved predictions for thermal photon and lepton pair signals from heavy ion collisions, and the finding that supersonic phase transitions are impossible restricts the possible proportions between different hadrons produced during hadronization.
