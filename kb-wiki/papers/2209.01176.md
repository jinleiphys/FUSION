# Statistical analysis of initial state and final state response in heavy-ion collisions

> We develop a general decomposition of an ensemble of initial density profiles in terms of an average state and a basis of modes that represent the event-by-event fluctuations of the initial state. The basis is determined such that the probability distributions of the amplitudes of different modes are uncorrelated. Based on this decomposition, we quantify the different types and probabilities of event-by-event fluctuations in Glauber and Saturation models and investigate how the various modes affect different characteristics of the initial state. We perform simulations of the dynamical evolution with KoMPoST and MUSIC to investigate the impact of the modes on final-state observables and their correlations.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general decomposition of initial-state energy density profiles into an average state and a basis of uncorrelated fluctuation modes, but the method is demonstrated only for fixed impact parameters (b = 0 and b = 9 fm) and only for energy density in the transverse plane, not yet extended to three spatial dimensions or full energy-momentum tensor fields.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the covariance matrix (density matrix) of event-by-event energy density fluctuations from an ensemble of 2^21 Monte Carlo events, obtaining orthogonal modes whose expansion coefficients are uncorrelated and have unit variance by construction. They apply this decomposition to two initial-state models for Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: a Glauber model with participant and binary collision scaling and a saturation model based on the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff (GBW) parametrization of unintegrated gluon distributions. The dynamical evolution of the modes is then simulated using KoMPoST for pre-equilibrium evolution and MUSIC for relativistic viscous hydrodynamics to study linear and quadratic response of final-state observables.

## Key numbers

- Number of events used for mode decomposition: N_ev = 2^21 (approximately 2 million) per model and impact parameter
- Spatial grid: 128 × 128 sites with spacing 0.19 fm (Glauber) and 0.21 fm (Saturation)
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: σ_inel = 67.6 mb
- Woods–Saxon parameters: half-density radius R = 6.62 fm, diffusivity a = 0.546 fm
- Glauber model: binary collision fraction α = 0.2, Gaussian smearing width σ = 0.4 fm, overall normalization 1246 GeV/fm²
- Saturation model: proton saturation scale parameters Q²_s,0 = 0.63 GeV², λ = 0.36, δ = 1, nucleon size B_G = 4 GeV⁻²
- Relative weight of average event: w̄ = 13% (Glauber), w̄ = 8% (Saturation) at both b = 0 and b = 9 fm
- First fluctuation modes at b = 0 have relative weights below about 1%; at b = 9 fm they range up to 2%–3%

## Context

This work extends the mode-by-mode approach to initial-state fluctuations pioneered by Floerchinger et al. (2013–2020), who used Bessel–Fourier decompositions and functional probability distributions, by constructing an optimal basis that diagonalizes the covariance matrix of fluctuations. The method enables systematic comparison of different initial-state models (Glauber and saturation/CGC-based) and allows calculation of linear and quadratic response of final-state observables without full event-by-event simulations. The framework can be applied to centrality-selected events and extended to three dimensions and full energy-momentum tensor fields in future work.
