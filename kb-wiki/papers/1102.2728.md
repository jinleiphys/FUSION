# Neutron-proton elliptic flow difference as a probe for the high density dependence of the symmetry energy

> We employ an isospin dependent version of the QMD transport model to study the influence of the isospin dependent part of the nuclear matter equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections on the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. We find that the extraction of useful information on the isospin-dependent part of the equation of state of nuclear matter from proton or neutron elliptic flows is obstructed by their sensitivity to model parameters and in-medium values of nucleon-nucleon cross-sections. Opposite to that, neutron-proton elliptic flow difference shows little dependence on those variables while its dependence on the isospin asymmetric EoS is enhanced. Comparison with experimental neutron-hydrogen FOPI-LAND data excludes the extreme super-soft scenarios for the high density dependence of asy-EoS. Existing neutron-proton elliptic flow difference experimental data are poised with uncertainties and cannot constrain the asy-EoS any further.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton elliptic flow difference (npEFD) is a more suitable observable than individual proton or neutron elliptic flows for constraining the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy (asy-EoS) in a model-independent way, because npEFD shows little dependence on model parameters and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections while its dependence on the asy-EoS is enhanced. However, existing experimental data for npEFD are too uncertain to provide a meaningful constraint, and a successful constraining will require experimental accuracy of 1% or better.

## Method

The study employed an isospin-dependent version of the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at intermediate energies. The model incorporated explicit density-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections using the Li-Machleidt parametrization and an isospin-dependent equation of state parametrized by the variable x (ranging from -2 for super-stiff to +2 for super-soft asy-EoS), with the compressibility modulus K set to 210 MeV and the nucleon wave-packet width 2L² set to 8 fm² unless otherwise stated. The elliptic flow parameter v₂ was computed for protons and neutrons, and the neutron-proton elliptic flow difference was analyzed as a function of impact parameter, incident energy, and model parameters.

## Key numbers

- The splitting between extreme asy-EoS cases (x=-2 and x=2) for npEFD is constant as a function of impact parameter and represents about 30-40% of the magnitude of either neutron or proton elliptic flow at the same energy and impact parameter.
- The sensitivity of npEFD to in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections is almost an order of magnitude smaller than the sensitivity of individual proton or neutron elliptic flows.
- The most favorable energy domain for constraining asy-EoS via npEFD stretches from 250 AMeV to almost 600 AMeV for Au+Au collisions.
- For incident energies larger than 600 AMeV, only mid-central collisions show important sensitivity of npEFD to the asy-EoS, with decreasing magnitude as energy increases.
- Successful constraining of the asy-EoS via npEFD requires experimental data with an accuracy of 1% or better.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of elliptic flow as a probe of the symmetry energy by Li (1996, 2000, 2001) and of neutron-proton elliptic flow difference by Greco (2003), Toro (2007), Russotto (2011), and Trautmann (2009). The study addresses contradictory results from earlier work on the π⁻/π⁺ multiplicity ratio, where Xia (2009) pointed towards a soft asy-EoS while Feng (2010) favored a stiff scenario. The comparison with FOPI-LAND experimental data for neutron-hydrogen elliptic flow difference sets an upper limit on the softness of the asy-EoS, and the work identifies the need for higher-accuracy measurements, which the ASY-EOS collaboration is planning to provide.
