# Femtoscopic study of the $ Ωα$ interaction in heavy-ion collisions

> The two-particle momentum correlation between the Omega-baryon ($Ω$) and the $^{4}He (α) $ in high-energy heavy ion collisions is explored. Such correlations as an alternative source of information can help us further understand the interaction between $ Ω$ and nucleons (N). $ Ωα$ potentials in the single-folding potential approach are constructed by employing two different available $ΩN $ interactions in $^{5}S_{2}$ channel, i.e, one is based on the (2 + 1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations near the physical point by the HAL QCD collaboration, and another is based on the meson exchanges with effective Lagrangian, where in the latter case coupled channels effect is considered. It is found that the correlation functions at small size source depends on the used potential model. This implicitly means that at high density nuclear medium, $ Ωα$ momentum correlation could drive the feature of $ ΩN $ interactions. Moreover, by extracting the scattering length and the effective range from obtained $ Ωα$ potentials, the correlation functions are calculated within the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) formalism. It is shown that since the $Ωα$ has large interaction range, the LL formula leads to different results at small source size.

# Femtoscopic study of the Ωα interaction in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The Ωα momentum correlation function at small source sizes (R ≈ 1-3 fm) is sensitive to the choice of ΩN potential model, implying that measurements of Ωα correlations in high-density nuclear matter could serve as an alternative probe of the ΩN interaction. However, the study is exploratory and uses simple techniques, with the caveat that for large source sizes (R ≳ 5 fm) the correlation functions from different potential models become nearly indistinguishable when Coulomb interaction is included.

## Method
The author constructed Ωα potentials using a single-folding potential approach, folding the nucleon density of the α-particle with two different ΩN interactions in the ⁵S₂ channel: one from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations by the HAL QCD Collaboration and another from a meson-exchange model with coupled-channel effects. The resulting potentials were fitted to Woods-Saxon forms, and the Schrödinger equation was solved to extract binding energies, scattering lengths, and effective ranges. Momentum correlation functions were then calculated using both the Koonin-Pratt formula (via the CATS code) and the Lednicky-Lyuboshits formalism for three source sizes (R = 1, 3, and 5 fm).

## Key numbers
- HAL QCD Ωα potential: binding energy B_Ωα = 22.9 MeV (without Coulomb), 24.2 MeV (with Coulomb); scattering length a₀ = 0.79 fm; effective range r₀ = 2.81 fm
- Meson-exchange Ωα potential: binding energy B_Ωα = 6.4 MeV (without Coulomb), 7.5 MeV (with Coulomb); scattering length a₀ = 2.65 fm; effective range r₀ = 1.30 fm
- Both Ωα potentials have interaction ranges of about 3 fm
- The ΩN scattering length from the HAL QCD potential is a₀^ΩN = 5.30 fm, with effective range r₀^ΩN = 1.26 fm and binding energy B^ΩN = 1.54 MeV
- The ΩN meson-exchange model reproduces the ΩN scattering length of 7.4 ± 1.6 fm from lattice simulations
- The α-particle rms radius used is 1.47 fm; the charge radius of the α-particle is about 1.68 fm
- For comparison, neutron-neutron scattering parameters are (a₀, r₀) = (-18.5, 2.80) fm

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic studies of hyperon-nucleus systems, specifically following recent investigations of Λα correlations (Jinno, 2024) and Ξα correlations (Kamiya, 2024). It builds on the HAL QCD Collaboration's lattice QCD results for ΩN interactions (Iritani, 2019) and the meson-exchange model of Sekihara, Kamiya, and Hyodo (2018). The study is motivated by STAR and ALICE measurements of proton-Ω correlations, where the HAL QCD potential was found most consistent with ALICE data. The results enable future experimental searches at FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC HI facilities, though the author notes that the Koonin-Pratt formula's validity for composite particles like α requires further investigation as a five-body problem.
