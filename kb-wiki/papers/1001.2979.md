# Pair-vibrational states in the presence of neutron-proton pairing

> Pair vibrations are studied for a Hamiltonian with neutron-neutron, proton-proton and neutron-proton pairing. The spectrum is found to be rich in strongly correlated, low-lying excited states. Changing theratio of diagonal to off-diagonal pairing matrix elements is found to have a large impact on the excited-state spectrum. The variational configuration interaction (VCI) method, used to calculate the excitation spectrum, is found to be in very good agreement with exact solutions for systems with large degeneracies having equal T=0 and T=1 pairing strengths.

## Key claim

Pair-vibrational states exist in a Hamiltonian with both isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) neutron-proton pairing, producing at least five low-lying excited states below the two-nucleon excitation energy, in contrast to the two such states found in systems with only like-particle pairing. The variational configuration interaction (VCI) method reproduces exact solutions to within a few keV for the lowest eigenstates and 10–50 keV for excited states when T=0 and T=1 pairing strengths are equal.

## Method

The authors solve a many-body pairing Hamiltonian for 16 neutrons and 16 protons distributed over 16 single-particle levels (eight at 0 MeV and eight at 1 MeV, each with J=1/2) using the variational configuration interaction (VCI) method. The Hamiltonian includes neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton pairing terms with both T=0 and T=1 components, and the VCI wave function is constructed from projected product states containing up to seven types of pair creation operators. The method is benchmarked against exact solutions from the two-level SO(8) model for the case of equal T=0 and T=1 strengths, then applied to cases with unequal diagonal/off-diagonal matrix elements and with T=0 strength reduced to 90% of T=1 strength.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies for case 1 (equal T=0 and T=1 strengths, equal diagonal/off-diagonal matrix elements) range from −3.0146 MeV at G=0.05 MeV to −38.780 MeV at G=0.30 MeV.
- The four lowest excited states in cases 1 and 2 are degenerate with energies: at G=0.05 MeV, E = −1.5206 MeV; at G=0.30 MeV, E = −36.902 MeV.
- The fifth excited state (T-even) at G=0.30 MeV in case 1 has energy −34.189 MeV.
- The moment of inertia, determined by the energy difference of the first 2+ state and the 0+ ground state, changes by 30–40% between case 1 and case 2 (which differ only in the ratio of diagonal to off-diagonal matrix elements).
- Ground-state off-diagonal correlation energies at G=0.1 MeV: case 1 gives 1.21 MeV for each pair type; case 2 gives 0.92 MeV; case 3 (10% reduction in T=0 strength) gives 1.07 MeV for T=1 types and 0.61–0.62 MeV for T=0 types.
- Each fully projected VCI basis state consists of 1.108×10^12 Slater determinants.

## Context

This work extends the classic treatment of pair vibrations by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and Hogaasen (1961), which considered only like-particle pairing, to include both isoscalar and isovector neutron-proton pairing. The study builds on the SO(8) algebraic framework of Flowers and Szpikowski (1964) and the exact solutions of Pang (1969) and Dussel (1986), and uses the VCI method previously developed by Chasman (2005). The results enable future studies of collective pair-vibrational states in N=Z nuclei, where the sensitivity of the moment of inertia to the ratio of T=0 to T=1 pairing strengths and to diagonal versus off-diagonal matrix elements must be disentangled.
