# Heavy-Flavor Theory at "Hard and Electromagnetic Probes 2018"

> An overview is given of the theoretical developments on heavy quarks and quarkonia in heavy-ion collisions as reported at the "Hard and EM Probes 2018" conference. Specifically, we address progress in the understanding of heavy-flavor diffusion and its hadronization, quarkonium transport and the extraction of quarkonium melting temperatures, energy loss of heavy quarks at high momentum, and their rescattering in small colliding systems.

## Key claim

Heavy-flavor observables in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions can be used to extract fundamental transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma, but the current extraction of the spatial diffusion coefficient \(2\pi T \mathcal{D}_s\) remains in the range 2-4, with significant uncertainties from the space-time evolution model and hadronization treatment.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical developments presented at the "Hard and Electromagnetic Probes 2018" conference, covering heavy-quark diffusion modeled via Fokker-Planck or Boltzmann transport equations, quarkonium transport described by rate equations with in-medium binding energies and dissociation rates, and high-\(p_T\) energy loss calculations comparing collisional and radiative regimes. The review synthesizes results from multiple groups using different approaches: perturbative QCD with running coupling, AdS/CFT correspondence, thermodynamic T-matrix calculations, and lattice-QCD extractions.

## Key numbers

- Spatial diffusion coefficient for charm quarks in QGP: \(2\pi T \mathcal{D}_s = 1.2-1.7\) from AdS/CFT-based calculations (Hambrock 2018), somewhat smaller than most other calculations
- Extracted heavy-quark scattering rate \(\Gamma_Q\) for charm quarks: 0.5 GeV or more, about an order of magnitude larger than the thermalization rate
- Initial temperatures estimated from hydrodynamic simulations: \(T_0^{\text{SPS}} \simeq 240\) MeV, \(T_0^{\text{RHIC}} \simeq 350\) MeV, \(T_0^{\text{LHC}} \simeq 550\) MeV
- \(\Lambda_c/D^0\) ratio in Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (STAR): close to or above one at \(p_T \simeq 3\) GeV
- \(\Lambda_c/D^0\) ratio in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV (ALICE): near one at average \(p_T \simeq 7\) GeV
- \(D\)-meson \(v_2\) in high-multiplicity \(p\)Pb collisions: up to 10% around \(p_T \simeq 3.5\) GeV (CMS)
- Extracted spatial diffusion coefficient range: \(\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T) = 2-4\)
- EPOS-3 framework favors \(\sim 30\%\) increase in \(\mathcal{D}_s\) values relative to EPOS-2 in the Nantes energy-loss model

## Context

This conference summary extends the ongoing effort to extract heavy-flavor transport coefficients from heavy-ion data, building on prior reviews by Andronic (2014), Rapp (2017), and Ferreiro (2018). The work connects heavy-quark diffusion to quarkonium transport through the in-medium QCD force, and identifies open questions including the unexpectedly large \(\Lambda_c/D^0\) ratio that challenges equilibrium hadronization models (He 2011), and the puzzle of large \(D\)-meson \(v_2\) without significant suppression in small collision systems. The results enable tighter constraints on the temperature dependence of the spatial diffusion coefficient and the in-medium quarkonium binding energies.
