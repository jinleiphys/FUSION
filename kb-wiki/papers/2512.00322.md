# Gravitational form factors of the baryon octet in holographic QCD

> The gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the baryon octet, including hyperons, are investigated in a bottom-up holographic QCD model that explicitly incorporates the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking through the strange quark mass. We fit the model parameters to reproduce the empirical masses of the baryon octet and examine the dependence of GFFs on the probe momentum. Our numerical results show distinct differences in the GFFs across the baryon octet. The computed GFFs are found to be in reasonable agreement with available lattice QCD results for the non-strange/nucleon sector. We also calculate the gravitational radii of the baryon octet and find that they decrease with increasing strangeness, indicating that heavier hyperons are more compact.

## Key claim

The gravitational form factor \(A(Q^2)\) and the corresponding gravitational radii for the entire baryon octet are predicted in a bottom-up holographic QCD model that explicitly incorporates SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking via non-zero strange quark mass and condensate, with all model parameters fixed solely by fitting the empirical octet masses, yielding the result that gravitational radii decrease with increasing strangeness content.

## Method

The authors construct a hard-wall bottom-up holographic QCD model with two five-dimensional Dirac spinor fields dual to left- and right-handed baryon operators, coupled to a bifundamental scalar field whose vacuum expectation value encodes flavor-dependent current quark masses and chiral condensates. The seven model parameters \(\{c_1, c_2, \sigma_q, \sigma_s, m_q, m_s, z_0\}\) are determined by a global fit to the ground-state masses of the baryon octet from the Particle Data Group, solving the coupled five-dimensional Dirac equations with UV/IR boundary conditions. The gravitational form factor \(A(Q^2)\) is then computed from the overlap integral of the bulk baryon wave functions with the bulk-to-boundary propagator of the transverse-traceless graviton fluctuation, and the gravitational radius is extracted from the slope at \(Q^2 = 0\).

## Key numbers

- Best-fit light quark mass: \(m_q = 14.8 \pm 0.5~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Best-fit strange quark mass: \(m_s = 152.7 \pm 0.9~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Best-fit light chiral condensate: \(\sigma_q = (301.86 \pm 32~\mathrm{MeV})^3\)
- Best-fit strange chiral condensate: \(\sigma_s = (318.7 \pm 0.5~\mathrm{MeV})^3\)
- Best-fit IR cutoff: \(z_0 = 3.424 \pm 0.005~\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\)
- Gravitational RMS radius for proton: \(\sqrt{\langle r_G^2 \rangle} = 0.488~\mathrm{fm}\)
- Gravitational RMS radius for \(\Lambda\): \(\sqrt{\langle r_G^2 \rangle} = 0.470~\mathrm{fm}\)
- Gravitational RMS radius for \(\Sigma\): \(\sqrt{\langle r_G^2 \rangle} = 0.461~\mathrm{fm}\)
- Gravitational RMS radius for \(\Xi\): \(\sqrt{\langle r_G^2 \rangle} = 0.446~\mathrm{fm}\)

## Context

This work extends previous holographic QCD studies of gravitational form factors, which were limited to the nucleon sector in the chiral limit (Abidin 2008), by incorporating explicit SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking through non-zero strange quark mass and condensate, thereby providing predictions for the entire baryon octet. The computed \(A(Q^2)\) for the proton shows improved agreement with lattice QCD results (Hackett 2023) compared to the chiral-limit holographic calculation, and the predicted ordering of gravitational radii (proton largest, \(\Xi\) smallest) provides testable predictions for future lattice QCD studies of hyperon structure and for understanding the role of strangeness in the equation of state of dense matter in neutron stars.
