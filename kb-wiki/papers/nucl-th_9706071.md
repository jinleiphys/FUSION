# ^1S_0 pairing correlations in relativistic nuclear matter and the two-nucleon virtual state

> We use the Gorkov formulation of the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation to nuclear pairing to study the ^1S_0 nucleon-nucleon correlations in nuclear matter. We find the short-range correlations of the ^1S_0 pairing fields to be almost identical to those of the two-nucleon virtual state. We obtain mutually consistent results for the pairing fields, using several different sets of effective interaction parameters, when we demand that each of these sets places the virtual-state pole at its physical location.

# ^1S_0 pairing correlations in relativistic nuclear matter and the two-nucleon virtual state

## Key claim

The short-range correlations of the ^1S_0 pairing fields in relativistic nuclear matter are almost identical to those of the two-nucleon virtual state, and mutually consistent results for the pairing fields can be obtained across different effective interaction parameter sets only when each set places the virtual-state pole at its physical location.

## Method

The authors used the Gorkov formulation of the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (DHFB) approximation to study ^1S_0 nucleon-nucleon pairing correlations in symmetric nuclear matter. They solved self-consistency equations for the pairing field components (scalar, time-like vector, and tensor) and the chemical potential, discarding negative-energy state contributions from the HFB propagator to avoid renormalization complications, and introduced a momentum cutoff Lambda as a crude approximation to nucleon-meson vertex form factors.

## Key numbers

- The two-nucleon virtual state in the ^1S_0 channel has momentum k_v approximately -0.05 i fm^{-1} and energy epsilon_v = k_v^2/M approximately 140 keV, corresponding to a singlet scattering length a_0 = 1/|k_v| approximately 23 fm.
- Non-trivial HFB solutions (pairing fields) exist only at baryon densities below about two thirds of saturation density, rho_B less than or equal to 2 rho_{B0}/3.
- The pairing fields reach a maximum at about 1/4 of the saturation density and fall back to zero before saturation density is reached.
- The coherence length of the physical bound pair never becomes smaller than about 4 fm, and near the physical position of the virtual state lies between about 4 to 10 fm.
- The gap function and pairing fields were evaluated at k_F = 0.5 fm^{-1} (about 1/8 of saturation density) for correlation studies.
- The tensor component Delta_T is three orders of magnitude smaller than the scalar Delta_S and time-like vector Delta_0 components.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach developed by Walecka and collaborators to include residual pairing correlations, building on earlier relativistic HFB formulations by Bailin, Love, and Vautherin (no calculations performed), and by Kring and collaborators (zero-range model and non-relativistic reduction). The authors specifically investigate the association between short-range correlations of the ^1S_0 virtual state and those of the pairing fields, previously studied in non-relativistic models by Khodel and collaborators. The results enable a consistent description of pairing across different effective relativistic interactions and reconcile relativistic calculations with non-relativistic ones, provided the interactions reproduce the two-nucleon singlet scattering length.
