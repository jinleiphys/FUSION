# Bayesian inference of nuclear incompressibility from collective flow in mid-central Au+Au collisions at 400--1500 MeV/nucleon

> The incompressibility $K$ of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) is determined through a Bayesian analysis of collective flow data from Au + Au collisions at beam energies $E = 400 -1500$ MeV/nucleon. This analysis utilizes a Gaussian process (GP) emulator applied to the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model for heavy-ion collisions, both with and without incorporating the momentum dependence of the single-nucleon potentials. Specifically, the inferred incompressibility values are $K=188.9^{+2.9}_{-4.5}$ MeV and $256.1^{+8.2}_{-8.7}$ MeV at $E = 400$ MeV/nucleon, respectively, at the 68\% confidence level using rapidity and transverse velocity dependence of proton elliptic flow data, with and without consideration of the momentum dependence. When the transverse momentum dependence of proton-like directed flow data is included, the inferred incompressibility values become $K=222.3^{+9.0}_{-9.9}$ MeV and $K=285.5^{+6.7}_{-7.3}$ MeV, respectively. Furthermore, we found that the value of $K$ derived from observables of proton elliptic flow increases with beam energy. This indicates that the equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter hardens at higher densities and temperatures in reactions with higher beam energies.

## Key claim

The incompressibility \(K\) of symmetric nuclear matter is inferred from collective flow data via Bayesian analysis, but the extracted value depends strongly on whether momentum-dependent single-nucleon potentials are included in the transport model and on which specific flow observables are used, with results ranging from about 189 MeV to 286 MeV at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using a Gaussian process emulator trained on 220 simulations from the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model for mid-central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 400 to 1500 MeV/nucleon. They compared cases with and without momentum-dependent interactions (MDI) in the single-nucleon potentials, and used Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to obtain posterior probability distributions for \(K\) from experimental data on proton elliptic flow (\(-v_2\)) and proton-like directed flow (\(v_1\)) measured by the FOPI collaboration.

## Key numbers

- At \(E = 400\) MeV/nucleon, using proton elliptic flow data only with MDI included: \(K = 188.9^{+2.9}_{-4.5}\) MeV at 68% confidence level.
- At \(E = 400\) MeV/nucleon, using proton elliptic flow data only without MDI: \(K = 256.1^{+8.2}_{-8.7}\) MeV at 68% confidence level.
- At \(E = 400\) MeV/nucleon, adding proton-like directed flow data with MDI included: \(K = 222.3^{+9.0}_{-9.9}\) MeV at 68% confidence level.
- At \(E = 400\) MeV/nucleon, adding proton-like directed flow data without MDI: \(K = 285.5^{+6.7}_{-7.3}\) MeV at 68% confidence level.
- At \(E = 600\) MeV/nucleon, using proton elliptic flow with MDI: \(K = 208.8^{+6.7}_{-7.3}\) MeV.
- At \(E = 1500\) MeV/nucleon, using proton elliptic flow with MDI: \(K = 314.3^{+6.3}_{-6.5}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier forward-modeling analyses of collective flow from the FOPI collaboration, particularly the study by Le Fevre et al. (2016) which extracted \(K = 190^{+30}_{-30}\) MeV from elliptic flow using IQMD with MDI, and the kaon ratio analysis by Fuchs (2006) favoring a soft EoS near \(K = 200\) MeV. The Bayesian approach provides quantified uncertainties narrower than the approximately 40 MeV error band from giant monopole resonance studies (Blaizot, 1980; Garg and Colo, 2018). The finding that extracted \(K\) increases with beam energy indicates that the nuclear equation of state hardens at higher densities and temperatures, enabling future multi-observable Bayesian analyses to map the density dependence of the EoS across a wider range.
