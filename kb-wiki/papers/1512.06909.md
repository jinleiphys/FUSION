# The quark susceptibility in a generalized dynamical quasiparticle model

> The quark susceptibility $χ_q$ at zero and finite quark chemical potential provides a critical benchmark to determine the quark-gluon-plasma (QGP) degrees of freedom in relation to the results from lattice QCD (lQCD) in addition to the equation of state and transport coefficients. Here we extend the familiar dynamical-quasiparticle model (DQPM) to partonic propagators that explicitly depend on the three-momentum with respect to the partonic medium at rest in order to match perturbative QCD (pQCD) at high momenta. Within the extended dynamical-quasi-particle model (DQPM$^*$) we reproduce simultaneously the lQCD results for the quark number density and susceptibility and the QGP pressure at zero and finite (but small) chemical potential $μ_q$. The shear viscosity $η$ and the electric conductivity $σ_e$ from the extended quasiparticle model (DQPM$^*$) also turn out in close agreement with lattice results for $μ_q$ =0. The DQPM$^*$, furthermore, allows to evaluate the momentum $p$, temperature $T$ and chemical potential $μ_q$ dependencies of the partonic degrees of freedom also for larger $μ_q$ which are mandatory for transport studies of heavy-ion collisions in the regime 5 GeV $< \sqrt{s_{NN}} <$ 10 GeV.

## Key claim

The extended dynamical-quasiparticle model (DQPM*) simultaneously reproduces lattice QCD results for the quark number density, quark susceptibility, and QGP pressure at zero and finite (but small) quark chemical potential, which the conventional DQPM with momentum-independent masses could not achieve.

## Method

The authors extended the dynamical-quasiparticle model (DQPM) by introducing partonic propagators that explicitly depend on three-momentum with respect to the partonic medium at rest, in addition to the existing temperature and chemical potential dependence. The quasiparticle masses and widths were parametrized following hard thermal loop (HTL) functional dependencies with multiplicative momentum-dependent factors and additive chiral mass terms, and the effective running coupling was determined by fitting the DQPM* entropy density to lattice QCD results. Thermodynamic quantities were computed using the entropy density formula from Baym, and the quark number density and susceptibility were derived from the same potential to maintain thermodynamic consistency.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at zero chemical potential is fixed at T_c(mu_q=0) approximately 0.158 GeV from lattice QCD.
- The coefficient kappa in the expansion T_c(mu_B) = T_c(0)(1 - kappa (mu_B/T_c(0))^2) is kappa_DQPM approximately 0.0122, compared to the lattice QCD result kappa = 0.013(2) from Bonati (2014).
- The gluon condensate mass m_chi_g is approximately 0.5 GeV, and the light quark chiral masses are m_chi_q = 0.003 GeV for u,d quarks and 0.06 GeV for s quarks.
- The momentum-dependent parameters are Lambda_g = 5 (T_c(mu_q)/T*)^2 GeV^{-2} and Lambda_q = 12 (T_c(mu_q)/T*)^2 GeV^{-2}.
- The DQPM* reproduces lattice QCD results for the baryon number density and susceptibility chi_2 for N_f=3 at mu_q=0, while the conventional DQPM with momentum-independent masses substantially underestimates the lattice data.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical-quasiparticle model (DQPM) developed by Cassing (2008) and Bratkovskaya (2011) to include momentum-dependent partonic propagators, addressing the long-standing challenge that standard quasiparticle models (QPM) could not simultaneously describe both the lattice QCD equation of state and quark susceptibilities, as pointed out by Bluhm (2004) and Plumari (2011). The DQPM* enables future transport studies of heavy-ion collisions in the center-of-mass energy range 5 GeV < sqrt(s_NN) < 10 GeV, where larger quark chemical potentials become relevant.
