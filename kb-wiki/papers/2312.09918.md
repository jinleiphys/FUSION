# Improved Wong and classical approximations, and reduction of fusion data

> We present an improved version of the Wong formula for heavy-ion fusion, where the parameters of the parabolic approximation of the Coulomb barrier are replaced by parameters of the l-dependent potential at an effective partial-wave. A pocket formula for this l-dependence is given. This version reproduces the fusion cross sections of quantum mechanical calculations very well, even when the original Wong formula is not valid. The same procedure is used to derive an improved expression for the classical fusion cross section, which is very accurate at above-barrier energies. Based on this classical expression, we propose a new method to reduce fusion data in this energy range. This method is used to perform a comparative study of complete fusion suppression in collisions of weakly bound projectiles. This study indicates that the suppression of complete fusion is essentially due to the action of nuclear breakup couplings.

# Improved Wong and classical approximations, and reduction of fusion data

## Key claim

The authors show that the standard Wong formula for heavy-ion fusion cross sections can be made accurate for light systems and at energies well above the barrier by replacing the s-wave barrier parameters with effective parameters obtained from an angular-momentum-weighted average, rather than using the grazing angular momentum parameters as proposed by Rowley and Hagino (2015). The improved classical expression derived from this procedure enables a new data reduction method that indicates complete fusion suppression in weakly bound projectile collisions is essentially due to nuclear breakup couplings rather than static effects.

## Method

The authors compared barrier penetration model (BPM) cross sections to one-channel quantum mechanical calculations using the Sao Paulo potential (SPP) with short-range Woods-Saxon imaginary absorption for three systems: 7Li+27Al, 7Li+209Bi, and 24Mg+138Ba. They derived an improved Wong formula by replacing the s-wave barrier radius with an angular-momentum-weighted average over partial waves up to the grazing angular momentum, assuming a quadratic dependence of barrier radius on angular momentum. The same averaging procedure was applied to derive an improved classical fusion cross section, which was then used to construct a new reduction method for above-barrier fusion data.

## Key numbers

- For 7Li+27Al at 30 MeV above the Coulomb barrier, the original Wong formula overestimates the BPM cross section by approximately 45%; the ratio Rg^2/RB^2 is 0.53, indicating the cross section would be reduced by a factor of about 2 using the grazing-radius correction
- For 7Li+209Bi at 30 MeV above the barrier, the Wong formula overestimates by 10%; Rg^2/RB^2 = 0.83
- For 24Mg+138Ba at 30 MeV above the barrier, the Wong formula overestimates by approximately 5%; Rg^2/RB^2 = 0.91
- The s-wave barrier parameters for 7Li+27Al: RB = 8.5 fm, VB = 6.1 MeV, hbar-omega = 2.4 MeV
- The s-wave barrier parameters for 7Li+209Bi: RB = 11.4 fm, VB = 29.4 MeV, hbar-omega = 4.1 MeV
- The s-wave barrier parameters for 24Mg+138Ba: RB = 11.1 fm, VB = 81.6 MeV, hbar-omega = 3.9 MeV
- The universal fusion function F0(x) can be safely approximated by its classical limit 2*pi*x for x greater than approximately 0.5, corresponding to about 1.5 MeV above VB for typical hbar-omega values of 2-4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Wong formula limitations discussed by Canto et al. (2009a, 2009b, 2009c) and proposes an alternative to the grazing-angular-momentum correction suggested by Rowley and Hagino (2015). The new reduction method based on the improved classical cross section enables comparative studies of complete fusion suppression in weakly bound projectiles such as 6Li, 7Li, 9Be, and 6He on various targets, addressing the long-standing problem of disentangling static barrier reduction from dynamic breakup coupling effects in fusion reactions.
