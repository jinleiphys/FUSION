# Dynamics and Equation of State Dependencies of Relevance for Nucleosynthesis in Supernovae and Neutron Star Mergers

> Neutron stars (NSs) and black holes (BHs) are born when the final collapse of the stellar core terminates the lives of stars more massive than about 9 Msun. This can trigger the powerful ejection of a large fraction of the star's material in a core-collapse supernova (CCSN), whose extreme luminosity is energized by the decay of radioactive isotopes such as 56Ni and 56Co. When evolving in close binary systems, the compact relics of such infernal catastrophes spiral towards each other on orbits gradually decaying by gravitational-wave emission. Ultimately, the violent collision of the two components forms a more massive, rapidly spinning remnant, again accompanied by the ejection of considerable amounts of matter. These merger events can be observed by high-energy bursts of gamma rays with afterglows and electromagnetic transients called kilonovae, which radiate the energy released in radioactive decays of freshly assembled rapid neutron-capture elements. By means of their mass ejection and the nuclear and neutrino reactions taking place in the ejecta, both CCSNe and compact object mergers (COMs) are prominent sites of heavy-element nucleosynthesis and play a central role in the cosmic cycle of matter and the chemical enrichment history of galaxies. The nuclear equation of state (EoS) of NS matter, from neutron-rich to proton-dominated conditions and with temperatures ranging from about zero to ~100 MeV, is a crucial ingredient in these astrophysical phenomena. It determines their dynamical processes, their remnant properties even at the level of deciding between NS or BH, and the properties of the associated emission of neutrinos, whose interactions govern the thermodynamic conditions and the neutron-to-proton ratio for nucleosynthesis reactions in the innermost ejecta. This chapter discusses corresponding EoS dependent effects of relevance in CCSNe as well as COMs. (slightly abridged)

## Key claim

The nuclear equation of state (EoS) of neutron star matter, from neutron-rich to proton-dominated conditions and with temperatures from about zero to roughly 100 MeV, determines the dynamical processes, remnant properties (including whether a neutron star or black hole forms), and neutrino emission in both core-collapse supernovae and compact object mergers, which in turn govern the thermodynamic conditions and neutron-to-proton ratio for nucleosynthesis in the innermost ejecta.

## Method

The authors review the dynamical evolution of core-collapse supernovae (CCSNe) and compact object mergers (COMs), focusing on how the high-density EoS of hot neutron star matter influences these processes. They discuss the neutrino-driven explosion mechanism, magnetorotational explosions, and merger dynamics, linking each to EoS-dependent effects on nucleosynthesis yields, neutrino emission, and gravitational wave signals. The chapter synthesizes results from numerical simulations and observational constraints from pulsar masses, NICER X-ray data, and the GW170817 event.

## Key numbers

- Densities up to more than \(2 \times 10^{15}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) (nearly 8 times nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 \approx 2.66 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)) are reached in core collapse and mergers
- Temperatures up to roughly 100 MeV (about \(10^{12}\) K) inside proto-neutron stars and during neutron star mergers; ejected matter starts expansion with temperatures exceeding \(\sim\)1 MeV (about \(10^{10}\) K)
- Maximum neutron star mass lower bounds: \(2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_\odot\) for PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie+2020), \(2.35 \pm 0.17 M_\odot\) for PSR J0952-0607 (Romani+2022); a minimum of 2.09 \(M_\odot\) at 3\(\sigma\) confidence (Romani+2022)
- GW170817 binary total mass \(2.74^{+0.04}_{-0.01} M_\odot\), individual masses (1.36–1.60) \(M_\odot\) and (1.17–1.36) \(M_\odot\) (90% credible intervals); neutron star radii from about \(10.8^{+2.0}_{-1.7}\) km to \(11.9^{+1.4}_{-1.4}\) km (90% credible level)
- NICER radius for PSR J0030+0451: \(12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19}\) km at mass \(1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} M_\odot\) (Riley+2019); for PSR J0740+6620: equatorial radius \(12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98}\) km at mass \(2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M_\odot\) (Riley+2021)
- Combined NICER and GW170817 constraints: 95% credible radius ranges of \(12.33^{+0.76}_{-0.81}\) km or \(12.18^{+0.56}_{-0.79}\) km for a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star (Raaijmakers+2021)
- Kilonova AT2017gfo ejecta mass approximately 0.05 \(M_\odot\); dynamical ejecta from collision phase up to roughly 0.02 \(M_\odot\) (Bauswein+2013, Hotokezaka2013)
- Lower limits on cold 1.6 \(M_\odot\) and maximum-mass neutron star radii: \(10.68^{+0.15}_{-0.04}\) km and \(9.60^{+0.14}_{-0.03}\) km, respectively (Bauswein+2017)
- Upper mass limits for non-rotating neutron stars: \(\sim\)2.17 \(M_\odot\) at 90% confidence (Margalit+2017), 2.15–2.25 \(M_\odot\) (Shibata+2017), between \(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\) and \(2.16^{+0.17}_{-0.15} M_\odot\) (Rezzolla+2018), 2.16–2.28 \(M_\odot\) (Ruiz+2018)

## Context

This chapter reviews the connections between nucleosynthesis in CCSNe and COMs and the high-density EoS of neutron star matter, extending prior work on neutron star mass-radius constraints from pulsar timing (Antoniadis+2013, Cromartie+2020), NICER observations (Riley+2019, Riley+2021, Miller+2019), and gravitational wave measurements from GW170817 (Abbott+2017). It enables future constraints on the hot EoS from gravitational wave ring-down signals, neutrino signals from galactic supernovae, and nucleosynthetic yields from kilonovae and supernova ejecta.
