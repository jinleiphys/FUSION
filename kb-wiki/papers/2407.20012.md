# Investigation of kaonic atom optical potential by the high precision data of kaonic $^3$He and $^4$He atoms

> We theoretically investigate kaonic atom states based on the latest high precision data of the $2p$ states of the kaonic atoms in $^3$He and $^4$He (J-PARC E62). We consider the phenomenological form for the optical potential proportional to the nuclear density distributions and determine the potential parameters by the latest data. We find two sets of potential parameters for each of $K^--^3$He and $K^--^4$He that reproduce the data. We then extract the potential parameters consistent with the data of $^3$He and $^4$He atoms simultaneously. Using the potential, we investigate the structure of the kaonic atoms and kaonic nuclei for various nuclei up to Sn. We also report the optical potential parameters suited for the global description of the atomic data, the possible strong isospin dependence of the potential, and the different strength of the data of $K^--^3$He and $K^--^4$He in the restrictions on the potential parameters. In addition, we find that the study of the $1s$ states of the kaonic helium atom is very interesting as a next step, and the determination of the orbital angular momenta of the kaonic nuclear states is helpful for the unified understandings of the atomic and nuclear bound states of kaon because of the mutual influence between the atomic and the nuclear states of kaon with the same orbital angular momentum.

# Investigation of kaonic atom optical potential by the high precision data of kaonic $^3$He and $^4$He atoms

## Key claim

The high-precision J-PARC E62 data for the $2p$ states of kaonic $^3$He and $^4$He allow extraction of two distinct families of optical potential parameters for each nucleus (a shallow-real/strong-absorption set and a deep-real/weak-absorption set), but the data for $^4$He provide stronger constraints than those for $^3$He, and the combined analysis suggests possible strong isospin dependence of the kaon-nucleus optical potential, though the accuracy is not yet sufficient to determine it definitively.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Klein-Gordon equation with a phenomenological optical potential proportional to the nuclear density distribution, $U(r) = (V_0 + iW_0) \rho(r)/\rho_0$, using realistic nuclear density distributions from precise few-body calculations for $^3$He and $^4$He. They determine the potential parameters $V_0$ and $W_0$ by fitting the experimental $3d \to 2p$ transition energy shifts and $2p$ level widths from the J-PARC E62 data, then extract parameter sets consistent with both nuclei simultaneously. They also investigate the $1s$ atomic states and estimate effects of possible $\rho^2$ terms in the potential using perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- For $K^--^3$He: two parameter sets found, HE3-A: $(V_0, W_0) = (-178, -274)$ MeV; HE3-B: $(V_0, W_0) = (-300, -95)$ MeV (potential strengths at normal nuclear density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$)
- For $K^--^4$He: two parameter sets found, HE4-A: $(V_0, W_0) = (-58, -56)$ MeV; HE4-B: $(V_0, W_0) = (-287, -43)$ MeV
- Combined isoscalar (IS) potentials: IS-A: $(V_0, W_0) = (-90, -120)$ MeV; IS-B: $(V_0, W_0) = (-280, -70)$ MeV
- Central nuclear densities: $\rho(0) \approx 1.32 \rho_0$ for $^3$He and $\rho(0) \approx 2.02 \rho_0$ for $^4$He
- Experimental $3d \to 2p$ shift for $K^--^3$He: $-0.2 \pm 0.5$ eV (combined error); width: $2.5 \pm 1.1$ eV
- Experimental $3d \to 2p$ shift for $K^--^4$He: $0.2 \pm 0.4$ eV (combined error); width: $1.0 \pm 0.7$ eV
- Predicted $1s$ state binding energy for $^3$He: $-38.1513$ keV (HE3-A) and $-38.6419$ keV (HE3-B); widths: $5.6302$ keV and $11.5738$ keV respectively
- Predicted $1s$ state binding energy for $^4$He: $-40.1397$ keV (HE4-A) and $-37.0823$ keV (HE4-B); widths: $5.2594$ keV and $4.6759$ keV respectively
- Overlap integral $I$ for $\rho^2$ term in $1s$ states: $3.0 \times 10^{-5}$ (HE3-A), $2.2 \times 10^{-4}$ (HE3-B), $3.5 \times 10^{-4}$ (HE4-A), $4.8 \times 10^{-4}$ (HE4-B); for $2p$ states these are 3-4 orders of magnitude smaller

## Context

This work extends the global analyses of kaonic atom spectra by Batty (1997), Friedman (2007), and the chiral unitary approach of Ramos (1999) and Oset (1997), using the new high-precision J-PARC E62 data that improved accuracy by nearly two orders of magnitude over previous measurements. The results contradict the large shifts and widths suggested theoretically by Akaishi (2005) in connection with kaonic nuclear states. The extracted potentials can be applied to heavier kaonic atoms up to Sn, and the predictions for $1s$ states in kaonic helium provide a clear next experimental target for X-ray spectroscopy or $(K^-, p)$ reactions to distinguish between the two families of potential parameters.
