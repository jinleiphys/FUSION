# Flavor and Charge Symmetry in the Parton Distributions of the Nucleon

> Recent calculations of charge symmetry violation(CSV) in the valence quark distributions of the nucleon have revealed that the dominant symmetry breaking contribution comes from the mass associated with the spectator quark system.Assuming that the change in the spectator mass can be treated perturbatively, we derive a model independent expression for the shift in the parton distributions of the nucleon. This result is used to derive a relation between the charge and flavor asymmetric contributions to the valence quark distributions in the proton, and to calculate CSV contributions to the nucleon sea. The CSV contribution to the Gottfried sum rule is also estimated, and found to be small.

# Flavor and Charge Symmetry in the Parton Distributions of the Nucleon

## Key claim
Charge symmetry violation (CSV) in valence quark distributions is dominated by the mass shift of the spectator diquark system rather than by changes in the struck quark wavefunction, and this effect can be expressed in a model-independent way using measured parton distributions. The CSV contribution to the Gottfried sum rule is estimated to be far too small (approximately 0.001 or less) to explain the observed deviation from the naive sum rule value of 1/3.

## Method
The authors use the Adelaide method to relate quark model wavefunctions to parton distributions, assuming the spectator diquark can be treated as an on-shell system with a definite mass. They expand the quark distribution expression to first order in the diquark mass shift, obtaining an analytic formula for the change in the distribution that depends only on the symmetric distribution and the mass shift. For valence quarks, they combine this with the color hyperfine interaction (with strength v = 75 MeV from the N-Delta splitting) and with quark mass and electromagnetic effects; for sea quarks, they model the nucleon as a valence state coupled to states with an additional quark-antiquark pair and estimate mass shifts from quark mass differences and electromagnetic energies.

## Key numbers
- The color hyperfine interaction strength is v = 75 MeV, normalized by the N-Delta splitting.
- The isovector diquark mass splitting from the bag model is -4 MeV, yielding delta_CSV M_d / delta_hf M_d ≈ 0.08.
- The proton-neutron mass difference used is delta M = -1.3 MeV.
- The constituent quark mass in the symmetric limit is m_q = 360 MeV.
- The CSV contribution to the Gottfried sum rule from charge-dependent quark-gluon coupling is estimated as 6.3 × 10^(-4).
- The CSV contribution to the Gottfried sum rule from combining flavor asymmetry with mass/electromagnetic effects is estimated as approximately -0.0012.
- The tetra-quark mass used for the light quark sea is 1440 MeV (roughly four times the constituent quark mass); for strange quark systems, a constituent quark mass of 500 MeV is assumed.
- The NMC experimental value for the Gottfried sum rule is S_G = 0.235 ± 0.026, implying a flavor asymmetry P_dbar/p - P_ubar/p = 0.147 ± 0.039 if charge symmetry is assumed.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Close and Thomas (1990) on spin-flavor correlations and the color hyperfine interaction, and the calculations of CSV by several authors (Rodionov et al., 1994; Sather, 1992; Henley et al., 1995) using quark models. It provides a model-independent relation connecting CSV valence distributions to the measured difference between u and d valence distributions, and rules out CSV as a significant contributor to the Gottfried sum rule violation observed by the NMC collaboration (Ama91). The results enable future experimental tests of CSV (Londergan et al., 1997) to be interpreted in terms of the underlying physics of QCD-inspired nucleon models.
