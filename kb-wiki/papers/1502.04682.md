# Accurate nuclear radii and binding energies from a chiral interaction

> With the goal of developing predictive ab-initio capability for light and medium-mass nuclei, two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory are optimized simultaneously to low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data, as well as binding energies and radii of few-nucleon systems and selected isotopes of carbon and oxygen. Coupled-cluster calculations based on this interaction, named NNLO$_{\rm sat}$, yield accurate binding energies and radii of nuclei up to $^{40}$Ca, and are consistent with the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. In addition, the low-lying collective $J^π=3^-$ states in $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are described accurately, while spectra for selected $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei are in reasonable agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The authors claim that simultaneously optimizing two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory to low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data plus binding energies and radii of few-nucleon systems and selected carbon and oxygen isotopes yields an interaction (NNLO$_{\rm sat}$) that accurately reproduces binding energies and charge radii of nuclei up to $^{40}$Ca, but this accuracy is conditional on including heavier nuclei in the fit and using a low-energy cutoff (35 MeV) for scattering data, rather than the traditional approach of fitting only to $A=2,3,4$ systems.

## Method

The authors simultaneously optimized 16 low-energy constants (LECs) of the chiral NNLO two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces using the POUNDerS algorithm, fitting to proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data up to 35 MeV laboratory energy, deuteron properties, and binding energies and charge radii of $^{3}$H, $^{3,4}$He, $^{14}$C, $^{16}$O, and binding energies of $^{22,24,25}$O. Few-body nuclei ($A\le 4$) were computed with the no-core shell model (NCSM) with infrared extrapolations, while heavier nuclei were computed with coupled-cluster methods (CCSD and $\Lambda$-CCSD(T)) in model spaces of 15 oscillator shells with $\hbar\Omega=22$ MeV, with three-nucleon forces truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level and residual contributions treated perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $^{40}$Ca: 326 MeV (experimental: 342 MeV)
- Charge radius of $^{40}$Ca: 3.48 fm (experimental: 3.4776(19) fm)
- Excitation energy of $3^-_1$ state in $^{16}$O: 6.34 MeV (experimental: 6.13 MeV)
- Excitation energy of $3^-_1$ state in $^{40}$Ca: 3.81 MeV (experimental: 3.736 MeV)
- Nuclear matter incompressibility $K$: 253 MeV (empirical range: 250-315 MeV)
- Binding energy of $^8$He: 30.9 MeV (experimental: 31.5 MeV)
- Charge radius of $^8$He: 1.91 fm (experimental: 1.959(16) fm)
- Binding energy of $^9$Li: 43.9 MeV (experimental: 45.34 MeV)
- Charge radius of $^9$Li: 2.22 fm (experimental: 2.217(35) fm)
- Proton separation energy $S_p$ for $^{16}$O: 10.69 MeV (experimental: 12.12 MeV)
- Neutron separation energy $S_n$ for $^{17}$O: 4.0 MeV (experimental: 4.14 MeV)
- Proton separation energy $S_p$ for $^{17}$F: 0.5 MeV (experimental: 0.6 MeV)
- Reduced matrix element for $^{3}$H $\beta$-decay: 0.6343 (experimental: 0.6848$\pm$0.0011)
- $\chi^2$/datum for NN scattering up to 35 MeV: approximately 4.3
- Regulator cutoff $\Lambda$: 450 MeV
- Spectral function regularization cutoff $\Lambda_{\rm SFR}$: 700 MeV

## Context

This work extends chiral effective field theory interactions beyond the traditional approach of fitting only to $A=2,3,4$ systems (as done by Epelbaum (2009), Machleidt (2011), and others), which had produced interactions that overbind medium-mass nuclei by about 1 MeV per nucleon and underestimate charge radii (Binder 2013). The new NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ interaction addresses the long-standing challenge of simultaneously reproducing nuclear binding energies, charge radii, and nuclear matter saturation properties, which previous chiral interactions (e.g., those from Hebeler (2011), Carbone (2013)) could not achieve. This enables reliable ab-initio predictions for medium-mass nuclei up to $^{40}$Ca and provides a foundation for extending such calculations to heavier systems and for studying nuclear structure with microscopically-founded forces.
