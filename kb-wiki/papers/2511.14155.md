# Cluster phenomena using few-body and Lattice QCD theories

> With the advancement of first-principles calculations for baryon-baryon interactions, it becomes possible to obtain reliable hyperon-nucleon potentials by lattice QCD simulations with the HAL QCD method. High-precision few-body methods, such as the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), are applicable to solve quantum few-body systems up to four- and five-body systems. By combining the HAL QCD potentials with the GEM, one can predict the level structure of novel hypernuclei prior to experimental observation. In this review, we utilize the lattice QCD $NΞ$ potential obtained by the HAL QCD method to investigate the few-body systems $NNΞ$ and $NNNΞ$. Our analysis indicates that the lightest bound $Ξ$ hypernucleus is the $NNNΞ$ system. To extract detailed information on the isospin and spin components of the $NΞ$ interaction, we perform a four-body calculation for the $ααNΞ$ system with the total isospin $T = 0$ and $T = 1$. We demonstrate that the level structure of this system is sensitive to the isospin and spin dependencies of the $NΞ$ interaction. Furthermore, we propose experimental investigations to produce the $NNNΞ$ and $ααNΞ$ systems via the $(K^-, K^+)$ and $(K^-, K^0)$ reactions on $^4$He and $^{10}$B targets, respectively.

## Key claim

By combining the HAL QCD lattice QCD potential for the NXi interaction with the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) for few-body calculations, the authors predict that the lightest bound Xi hypernucleus is the NNNXi system, and that the level structure of the alpha-alpha-N-Xi system is sensitive to the isospin and spin dependencies of the NXi interaction, though these predictions rely on the specific lattice QCD potentials obtained at nearly physical quark masses with associated statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors employed the time-dependent HAL QCD method with (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations on a (8.1 fm)^4 volume at nearly physical quark masses (m_pi=146 MeV, m_K=525 MeV) to determine NXi interactions in the S-wave channels (11S0, 31S0, 13S1, 33S1), including a coupled-channel analysis for the 11S0 channel involving Lambda-Lambda and NXi. They then used the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), which expands wavefunctions in Gaussian basis functions with geometric progression ranges on multiple Jacobi coordinate sets, to solve the few-body Schrodinger equation for the NNXi, NNNXi, and alpha-alpha-N-Xi systems using the fitted lattice QCD potentials.

## Key numbers

* The lattice QCD simulation used a spacetime volume of (8.1 fm)^4 with (L/a)^4 = 96^4 and a lattice spacing a = 0.0846 fm, at nearly physical quark masses (m_pi, m_K) = (146, 525) MeV.
* The pion and kaon masses used for fitting the lattice data were (m_pi, m_K) = (146, 525) MeV, while for calculating the Xi-nucleus systems the authors used (m_pi, m_K) = (138, 496) MeV.
* The effective single-channel XiN potential in the 11S0 channel was parametrized as U2 * exp(-(r/gamma)^2) with gamma = 1.0 fm.
* The experimental Xi-separation energy from the 12C(K-,K+)12_Be reaction at J-PARC was reported as B_Xi = 8.9 +/- 1.4 (stat.) +3.8/-3.1 (syst.) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the strategy of using first-principles lattice QCD interactions (HAL QCD Collaboration, 2019) combined with high-precision few-body methods to predict hypernuclear structure, moving beyond the previous iterative approach that relied on meson-exchange models (Nijmegen models, e.g., Nagels 1975, Maessen 1989) and experimental spectroscopy. The HAL QCD NXi interaction was previously validated by the ALICE Collaboration (2020) through p-Xi- momentum correlation measurements in pp collisions at the LHC. This enables predictions for novel Xi hypernuclei that can be tested via (K-, K+) and (K-, K0) reactions at J-PARC, and provides a framework for extracting isospin and spin components of the NXi interaction from future experiments.
