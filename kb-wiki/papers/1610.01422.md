# Pion-nucleon scattering in the Roper channel from lattice QCD

> We present a lattice QCD study of $Nπ$ scattering in the positive-parity nucleon channel, where the puzzling Roper resonance $N^*(1440)$ resides in experiment. The study is based on the PACS-CS ensemble of gauge configurations with $N_f=2+1$ Wilson-clover dynamical fermions, $m_π\simeq 156~$MeV and $L\simeq 2.9~$fm. In addition to a number of $qqq$ interpolating fields, we implement operators for $Nπ$ in $p$-wave and $Nσ$ in $s$-wave. In the center-of-momentum frame we find three eigenstates below 1.65 GeV. They are dominated by $N(0)$, $N(0)π(0)π(0)$ (mixed with $N(0)σ(0)$) and $N(p)π(-p)$ with $p\simeq 2π/L$, where momenta are given in parentheses. This is the first simulation where the expected multi-hadron states are found in this channel. The experimental $Nπ$ phase-shift would -- in the approximation of purely elastic $Nπ$ scattering -- imply an additional eigenstate near the Roper mass $m_R\simeq 1.43~$GeV for our lattice size. We do not observe any such additional eigenstate, which indicates that $Nπ$ elastic scattering alone does not render a low-lying Roper. Coupling with other channels, most notably with $Nππ$, seems to be important for generating the Roper resonance, reinforcing the notion that this state could be a dynamically generated resonance. Our results are in line with most of previous lattice studies based just on $qqq$ interpolators, that did not find a Roper eigenstate below $1.65~$GeV. The study of the coupled-channel scattering including a three-particle decay $Nππ$ remains a challenge.

## Key claim

This lattice QCD study finds three eigenstates below 1.65 GeV in the positive-parity nucleon channel, but no additional eigenstate near the Roper mass of approximately 1.43 GeV that would be expected from purely elastic Nπ scattering; this indicates that elastic Nπ scattering alone does not produce a low-lying Roper resonance, and coupling with other channels, most notably Nππ, appears essential for generating the Roper resonance.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical lattice QCD calculation on 197 PACS-CS gauge configurations with Nf=2+1 Wilson-clover fermions, mπ≈156 MeV, and L≈2.9 fm. They constructed a 5×5 correlation matrix using interpolating fields including three-quark (qqq) operators with two different smearing widths, Nπ operators in p-wave, and Nσ operators in s-wave, then extracted eigenenergies via the generalized eigenvalue method (GEVP) with t0=2.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: mπ = 153.9 ± 4.1 MeV (full set of 197 configurations) or 164.4 ± 2.1 MeV (reduced set "all-4" with 193 configurations)
- Nucleon mass: mN = 951 ± 19 MeV (full set) or 969 ± 12 MeV (reduced set "all-4")
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0907(13) fm, corresponding to 1/a ≈ 2.17 GeV
- Lattice volume: L ≈ 2.9 fm (32³ × 64 lattice)
- Four eigenenergies found below 1.65 GeV: E₁ = 0.4427 ± 0.0055 (in lattice units, ≈ 0.96 GeV), E₂ = 0.6196 ± 0.0266 (≈ 1.34 GeV), E₃ = 0.6873 ± 0.0195 (≈ 1.49 GeV), E₄ = 0.9527 ± 0.0338 (≈ 2.07 GeV)
- Non-interacting N(1)π(-1) energy: approximately 1.5 GeV (√(mπ²+(2π/L)²) + √(mN²+(2π/L)²))
- Experimental Roper mass: mR ≈ 1.43 GeV (range 1.41-1.45 GeV from PDG)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the Roper channel that used only three-quark interpolators (Alexandrou 2013, Edwards 2011, Mahbub 2013, Roberts 2013), which did not find a Roper eigenstate below 1.65 GeV. It is the first simulation to establish the expected multi-hadron states (Nπ and Nππ) in this channel. The results contradict the scenario where elastic Nπ scattering alone generates the Roper resonance and support the notion from phenomenological studies (Krehl 1999, Schutz 1998, Liu 2016) that the Roper could be a dynamically generated resonance from coupled channels including Nππ. The study of coupled-channel scattering including the three-particle decay Nππ remains a challenge for future work.
