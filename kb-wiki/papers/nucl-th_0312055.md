# First order shape transition and critical point nuclei in Sm isotopes from relativistic mean field approach

> The critical point nuclei in Sm isotopes, which marks the first order phase transition between spherical U(5) and axially deformed shapes SU(3), have been investigated in the microscopic quadrupole constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) model plus BCS method with all the most used interactions, i.e., NL1, NL3, NLSH and TM1. The calculated potential energy surfaces show a clear shape transition for the even-even Sm isotopes with $N = 82\sim 96$ and the critical point nuclei are found to be $^{148}$Sm, $^{150}$Sm and $^{152}$Sm. Similar conclusions can also be drawn from the microscopic neutron and proton single particle spectra.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the critical point nuclei marking the first-order shape phase transition between spherical U(5) and axially deformed SU(3) in the Sm isotopic chain are found to be three nuclei (148Sm, 150Sm, and 152Sm) rather than a single critical point, based on the flatness of their potential energy surfaces and the evolution of single-particle spectra across the chain.

## Method

The authors performed quadrupole-constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations with BCS pairing for even-even 144-158Sm using four parameter sets (NL1, NL3, NLSH, TM1). They computed potential energy surfaces as a function of quadrupole deformation by constraining the quadrupole moment and solving the RMF equations self-consistently in a deformed axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with N=20 shells. The binding energy difference between the spherical and ground states was extracted to quantify the softness against deformation.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for 144-158Sm are reproduced within 0.5% across all four parameter sets; for NL3, agreement is within 1 MeV for 146-158Sm
- For 144Sm, the spherical ground state has a barrier against deformation of about 12 MeV; for 146Sm the barrier is about 8 MeV
- The energy difference between spherical and ground states jumps from 0.02-0.33 MeV for 146Sm to 1.98-2.72 MeV for 148Sm, and again to 4.37-12.33 MeV for 154Sm, depending on the parameter set
- Experimental quadrupole deformation beta2 for 148Sm is 0.16, for 150Sm is 0.21, and for 152Sm is 0.24; RMF calculations give values ranging from 0.13-0.15, 0.16-0.25, and 0.19-0.34 respectively across parameter sets

## Context

This work provides the first microscopic investigation of the critical point concept in nuclear shape phase transitions, which had previously been studied only in algebraic models such as the Interacting Boson Model (Iachello, 1987) and through analytic solutions like X(5) (Iachello, 2001). The results confirm that 152Sm, previously identified as an empirical X(5) candidate (Casten, 2001), along with 148Sm and 150Sm, exhibit the flat potential energy surfaces characteristic of critical point nuclei. This enables future microscopic studies of other isotopic chains and isotones where critical point behavior has been suggested, such as the N=90 isotones (Caprio, 2002).
