# Searching for the QCD critical point with net-proton number fluctuations

> Net-proton number fluctuations can be measured experimentally and hence provide a source of important information about the matter created during relativistic heavy ion collisions. Particularly, they may give us clues about the conjectured QCD critical point. In this work the beam-energy dependence of ratios of the first four cumulants of the net-proton number is discussed. These quantities are calculated using a phenomenologically motivated model in which critical mode fluctuations couple to protons and anti-protons. Our model qualitatively captures both the monotonic behavior of the lowest-order ratio as well as the non-monotonic behavior of higher-order ratios, as seen in the experimental data from the STAR Collaboration. We also discuss the dependence of our results on the coupling strength and the location of the critical point.

## Key claim

The refined model, which modifies the coupling between critical mode fluctuations and net-proton number cumulants to account for the correct scaling of baryon number susceptibility, qualitatively captures the monotonic beam-energy dependence of the $C_2/C_1$ ratio and the non-monotonic behavior of higher-order ratios seen in STAR data, but the $C_3/C_2$ ratio from the model does not follow the experimental systematics, suggesting the QCD critical point is unlikely to be located close to the freeze-out curve.

## Method

The authors use a hadron resonance gas (HRG) baseline model and add critical fluctuations by coupling the critical mode (order parameter) to proton and anti-proton masses, with a coupling strength $g$. The critical mode cumulants are modeled using universality class arguments mapping QCD to the three-dimensional Ising model, with a linear mapping between the QCD phase diagram and the Ising model variables. The model is refined from Bluhm (2016) by replacing the proton mass in the second cumulant with the order parameter $\sigma$ to reproduce the correct scaling of the baryon number susceptibility, and net-proton number cumulant ratios are calculated along the chemical freeze-out curve from Andronic (2017).

## Key numbers

* Three critical point locations are considered: CP1 at ($\mu_{cp}=390$ MeV, $T_{cp}=149$ MeV), CP2 at ($\mu_{cp}=420$ MeV, $T_{cp}=141$ MeV), and CP3 at ($\mu_{cp}=450$ MeV, $T_{cp}=134$ MeV).
* For CP1 with coupling $g=3$ and $g=5$, the refined model shows substantial reduction of criticality in the $C_2/C_1$ ratio compared to the original model from Bluhm (2016), bringing it into agreement with STAR data.
* For CP3 with $g=5$, the $C_4/C_2$ ratio shows a strong increase at low beam energies ($\sqrt{s}<20$ GeV), while the $C_3/C_2$ ratio overshoots the HRG baseline, contrary to STAR data which stay below the baseline.
* In the refined model, the n-th cumulant scales as $g^{2n}$ according to the model equations.

## Context

This work extends the model of Bluhm (2016), which included resonance decays but produced a strong non-monotonic $C_2/C_1$ ratio not seen experimentally, by incorporating scaling properties of baryon number and chiral susceptibilities from Sasaki (2006, 2007). The refined model enables a more realistic comparison with preliminary STAR data from Thader (2016) and suggests that the QCD critical point is unlikely to be close to the freeze-out curve, though this conclusion requires further theoretical and experimental justification due to uncertainties in model parameters and data.
