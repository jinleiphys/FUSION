# Universality Constraints on Three-Body Recombination for Cold Atoms: from 4He to 133Cs

> For atoms with large scattering length, the dependence of the 3-body recombination rate on the collision energy is determined by the scattering length and the Efimov 3-body parameters and can be expressed in terms of universal functions of a single scaling variable. We use published results on the 3-body recombination rate for 4He atoms to constrain the universal functions. We then use those universal functions to calculate the 3-body recombination rate for other atoms with large scattering length at nonzero temperature. The constraints from the 4He results are strong if the scattering length is near the minimum of the 3-body recombination rate at threshold. We apply our results to 133Cs atoms with a large positive scattering length, and compare them with experimental results from the Innsbruck group.

# Universality Constraints on Three-Body Recombination for Cold Atoms: from 4He to 133Cs

## Key claim

The universal functions that determine the energy dependence of the three-body recombination rate for atoms with large scattering length can be constrained using published results for 4He atoms, but the constraints are strong only when the scattering length is near the minimum of the three-body recombination rate at threshold; for other values, the predictions for other atoms such as 133Cs have significant uncertainty.

## Method

The authors used published numerical results for the three-body recombination rate of 4He atoms interacting through the HFD-B3-FCI1 potential, calculated as a function of collision energy from threshold to 10 mK and separated into partial wave contributions J=0, 1, 2, and 3. They fit these results to determine the universal functions f_J(x) for J=1, 2, and 3, and used the J=0 data to constrain a linear combination of the unknown universal functions h_1(x) and h_2(x) that appear in Efimov's radial law. For systems with deep dimers, they applied the substitution ln a_{*0} -> ln a_{*0} - i eta_*/s_0 to obtain predictions including the additional parameter eta_*.

## Key numbers

- For 4He atoms with the HFD-B3-FCI1 potential: scattering length a = 172 a_0 from the potential, but using the dimer binding energy E_D = 1.600 mK gives a_He = 164.5 a_0
- The Efimov parameter for 4He determined from the threshold recombination rate K_3(0) = 7.10 x 10^{-28} cm^6/s is a_{*0}^{He} = 143.1 a_0
- The maximum value of the threshold recombination rate coefficient is C_max = 402.7, giving C_max hbar a_He^4/m = 3.67 x 10^{-26} cm^6/s
- The J=0 contribution for 4He has a deep local minimum at collision energy E_min = 2.1 mK with rate K^{(0)}(E_min) = 2.3 x 10^{-29} cm^6/s
- The universal functions near threshold: f_1(x) approx 3.43 x^6, f_2(x) approx 261 x^4, f_3(x) approx 90.3 x^6
- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is e^{pi/s_0} approx 22.7, with s_0 approx 1.00624
- For 133Cs, the Innsbruck group observed a resonant enhancement at a approx -850 a_0 and a local minimum near a approx 210 a_0, with width parameter eta_* = 0.06(1) from fitting zero-temperature universal formula

## Context

This work extends the universal theory of Efimov physics developed by Braaten and Hammer (2002) and the analytic expression for the threshold three-body recombination rate derived by Petrov (2004) and Macek et al. (2005). It uses the numerical calculations of Suno, Esry, Greene, and Burke (2002) for 4He atoms to constrain universal functions, enabling predictions for other systems. Previous efforts to calculate nonzero-temperature recombination rates by D'Incao, Suno, and Esry (2004), Jonsell (2006), and Yamashita, Frederico, and Tomio (2006) provided qualitative descriptions but not definitive universal predictions. The results enable calculation of the three-body recombination event rate constant alpha(T) as a function of scattering length and temperature for atoms with large positive scattering length, with application to the 133Cs experiments from the Innsbruck group.
