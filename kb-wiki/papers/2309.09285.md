# Persistent vibrational structure in $^{110-116}$Cd

> The empirical spectra and $E2$ decay rates in $^{110,112,114,116}$Cd are shown to be consistent with a vibrational interpretation for low-lying normal states, coexisting with a single deformed $γ$-soft band of intruder states. The observed deviations from this paradigm show up in particular non-yrast states, which are properly described by a Hamiltonian with U(5) partial dynamical symmetry. The latter is characterized by a good (broken) symmetry in most (in selected) normal states, weakly coupled to intruder states.

## Key claim

The empirical spectra and E2 decay rates in 110,112,114,116Cd are consistent with a vibrational interpretation for low-lying normal states coexisting with a single deformed γ-soft band of intruder states, but only because the observed deviations from this paradigm are confined to selected non-yrast states that are properly described by a Hamiltonian with U(5) partial dynamical symmetry, where the symmetry is good in most normal states but broken in a subset.

## Method

The authors employed the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), using a Hamiltonian that combines a U(5) partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) term for the normal configuration (N bosons) with a deformed γ-soft term for the intruder configuration (N+2 bosons), plus a mixing term. The parameters of the Hamiltonian and E2 operator were determined by a combined fit to the spectra and E2 transitions for normal states (2+1, 4+1, 2+2, 6+1), the anomalous states (0+α, 2+α), and the lowest intruder states in each isotope. The U(5)-PDS Hamiltonian breaks the U(5) symmetry but maintains a subset of U(5)-DS basis states (class-A states) as solvable eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) values: 27.0(8) W.u. for 110Cd, 30.31(19) W.u. for 112Cd, 31.1(19) W.u. for 114Cd, 33.5(12) W.u. for 116Cd
- B(E2; 0+α → 2+1) values: <7.9 W.u. for 110Cd, 0.0121(17) W.u. for 112Cd, 0.0026(4) W.u. for 114Cd, 0.79(22) W.u. for 116Cd
- B(E2; 0+α → 2+2) values: <1680 W.u. for 110Cd, 99(16) W.u. for 112Cd, 127(16) W.u. for 114Cd
- Quadrupole moments Q(2+1): -0.40(3) eb for 110Cd, -0.38(3) eb for 112Cd, -0.35(5) eb for 114Cd, -0.42(4) eb for 116Cd
- Normal component probability a² for class-A states: 0+1 ≥ 97.94%, 2+1 ≥ 95.10%, 4+1 ≥ 83.05%, 2+2 ≥ 78.77%
- Normal component probability a² for anomalous states: 0+α ~70-74%, 2+α ~41-68%
- Dominant n_d component in Ψ_n for 0+α states: n_d=3 with probability ≥ 69.54%
- Dominant n_d component in Ψ_n for 2+α states: n_d=4 with probability ≥ 40.13%

## Context

This work extends the U(5)-PDS approach previously applied to 110Cd by Leviatan (2018) to a series of cadmium isotopes (110-116Cd), demonstrating for the first time the relevance of partial dynamical symmetry to multiple isotopes. It contradicts the beyond-mean-field (BMF) calculation by Garrett (2019, 2020) that advocates multiple shape coexistence in 110,112Cd with states arranged in deformed rotational bands, rather than the vibrational interpretation with a single deformed intruder band proposed here. The results set the path for implementing a similar PDS-based approach to other regions of the nuclear chart where a prescribed collective structure paradigm holds for a segment of the spectrum.
