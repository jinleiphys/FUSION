# $π$- and $K$-Mesons Properties for Large $N_f$

> The restoration of dynamical chiral symmetry for a higher number of light-quark flavors $N_f$ implies suppression of the dynamically generated quark mass. The study of various larger values of $N_f$ may have a greater impact on the internal structure of light hadrons. In this work, we study the properties of the $π$- (pion) and $K$-meson (kaon), such as the mass, condensate, and leptonic decay constant, for various $N_f$. We use the symmetry-preserving vector-vector flavor-dependent contact interaction model of quark. The dynamical quark masses are calculated using the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE). The masses of pion ($m_π$) and kaon ($m_{K}$) for different values of $N_f$ and are determined using the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. For fixed $N_c=3$ and $N_f$ is increased, the dynamically generated quark mass $M_{u/d}$ ( mass of up and down quarks), strange quark mass ($M_s$), meson in-condensate $κ^{1/3}_{(π,K)}$, and decay constant $f_{(π, K)}$. monotonically decrease as a function of $N_f$, except for the pion and kaon mass $m_{(π, K)}$, which increase above a critical value of $N_f$ around $8$. This is the region where chiral symmetry is restored and the pion and kaon behave as free particles, similar to thier behavior in the the presence of a heat bath. The results obtained for fixed $N_f=2$ and $N_c=3$ are fairly in decent agreement with experimentally calculated statistics and previous model calculations based on the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE).

## Key claim

For a fixed number of colors \(N_c=3\) and increasing number of light-quark flavors \(N_f\), the dynamically generated quark masses, in-meson condensates, and decay constants of the pion and kaon monotonically decrease, while the meson masses increase above a critical flavor number around \(N_f \approx 8\), the region where chiral symmetry is restored and the mesons behave as free particles, analogous to their behavior at the Mott temperature in a heat bath.

## Method

The author employed a symmetry-preserving vector-vector flavor-dependent contact interaction (FCI) model as input to the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) to calculate the dressed quark masses for up/down and strange quarks. The masses of the pion and kaon were then determined using the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) within the rainbow-ladder truncation, using an optimal Schwinger proper-time regularization scheme. The flavor dependence was introduced by modifying the effective coupling \(\alpha_{\rm eff}(N_f)\) with a square-root dependence on \(N_f\) to reproduce the critical flavor number for chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key numbers

- Critical number of flavors for chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement in the chiral limit: \(N_f^{c} \approx 8\).
- Critical dissociation number of flavors for pion and kaon bound states with bare quark masses: \(N_f^{d} = N_{(\pi,K)f}^{d} \approx 8.2\).
- Bare quark masses used: up/down \(m_{u,d} = 0.007\) GeV, strange \(m_s = 0.17\) GeV.
- Gluon mass scale: \(m_g = 0.8\) GeV; infrared-enhanced interaction strength: \(\alpha_{\rm ir} = 0.93\pi\).
- Upper limit for asymptotic freedom in SU(3): \(N_f^{AF,c} = 16.5\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement at large \(N_f\) using Schwinger-Dyson equations and effective models (Bashir et al., 2013; Ahmad, 2020, 2022) and lattice QCD simulations (LSD Collaboration, 2014; Hasenfratz, 2016). The results for fixed \(N_f=2\) and \(N_c=3\) agree with experimental data and earlier SDE-BSE model calculations. The study introduces the concept of a bound-state dissociation number of flavors \(N_f^{d}\) analogous to the Mott temperature in finite-temperature QCD, enabling future investigations of hadron structure at the electron-ion collider (EIC).
