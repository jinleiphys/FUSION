# Dissipative hydrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions

> In a first order theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, we have simulated hydrodynamic evolution of QGP fluid with dissipation due to shear viscosity only. Simulation confirms that compared to an ideal fluid, energy density or temperature of a viscous fluid evolve slowly. Transverse expansion is also more in viscous fluid. We also study the effect of viscosity on particle production. Particle production is enhanced, more at large $p_T$. The elliptic flow on the otherhand decreases and shows a tendency to saturate at large $p_T$.

## Key claim

In a first-order (acausal) theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, shear viscosity slows the cooling of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and enhances transverse expansion, but the abstract's claim of "more" particle production and reduced elliptic flow is conditional on using either a perturbative estimate (η/s ≈ 0.135) or an ADS/CFT-motivated value (η/s ≈ 0.08), and the results are obtained under the assumption of boost invariance and zero net baryon density.

## Method

The author solved the 2+1 dimensional energy-momentum conservation equations for a QGP fluid with shear viscosity as the only dissipative flux, using a first-order (Eckart-type) theory implemented in the numerical code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA. The equation of state was a two-phase model (EOS-Q) with a hadronic resonance gas and a non-interacting quark-gluon phase matched by Maxwell construction at Tc = 164 MeV. Initial conditions were taken from an earlier ideal hydrodynamic study (QGP3) with central entropy density s_ini = 110 fm⁻³ at τ_i = 0.6 fm, zero initial fluid velocities, and dissipative fluxes set to their longitudinal boost-invariant values. Particle spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription with a non-equilibrium distribution function correction linear in the shear stress tensor.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity over entropy ratio from perturbative QCD: (η/s)_pert ≈ 0.135, estimated using α_s ≈ 0.5.
- Shear viscosity over entropy ratio from ADS/CFT correspondence: (η/s)_ADS/CFT = 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08.
- Critical temperature of the two-phase equation of state: T_c = 164 MeV.
- Initial proper time: τ_i = 0.6 fm.
- Initial central entropy density: s_ini = 110 fm⁻³.
- Freeze-out temperature for particle spectra: T_F = 158 MeV.
- Independent shear stress components at initial time: π^{xx} = 2η/τ_i, π^{yy} = 2η/τ_i, π^{ττ} = 0.

## Context

This work extends the ideal hydrodynamic simulations of QGP3 (2003) by incorporating shear viscosity, addressing the observed deviations from ideal behavior in peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC. It builds on recent numerical implementations of dissipative hydrodynamics by Teaney (2004), Muronga (2001), and Heinz (2005), and uses the first-order theory of Eckart (1940) and Israel-Stewart (1979). The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental elliptic flow saturation at high p_T and motivate extension to second-order (causal) dissipative hydrodynamics, which the author notes is under development.
