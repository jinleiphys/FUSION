# Glueball-Like Screening Masses in Pure SU(3) at Finite Temperatures

> We investigate the finite-temperature excitation spectrum in the gluon sector of $SU(3)$ pure gauge theory through measurements of screening masses in correlations of loop operators. We develop the classification of such operators under the symmetry group of the `$z$-slice'. In the confined phase of the theory, we find that the spectrum dynamically realises the zero temperature symmetries. We observe a large thermal shift of the $0^{++}$ glueball mass. In the deconfined phase, the spectrum distinguishes between operators coupling to electrically and magnetically polarised gluon fields. The former yields a screening mass equal to the Wilson-line screening mass; the latter, a method for the measurement of the magnetic mass in the high-temperature limit.

## Key claim
The paper claims that in the deconfined phase of pure SU(3) gauge theory, screening masses from operators coupling to electrically and magnetically polarised gluon fields yield distinct values: the electric screening mass equals the Wilson-line screening mass, while the magnetic screening mass provides a non-perturbative measurement of the magnetic mass in the high-temperature limit, though the authors note they have not been able to analyse the scaling behaviour of the magnetic mass.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations of pure SU(3) gauge theory on lattices of sizes 4×16³, 6×16³, and 8×16³ at β=5.93 and β=6.101, corresponding to temperatures of approximately 1.5Tc and 0.75Tc. They constructed screening correlation functions of loop operators (4-link, 6-link, and 8-link operators) classified under the symmetry group D₄×{1,σ_z} of the z-slice, and extracted screening masses using a variational procedure with fuzzed links for signal enhancement.

## Key numbers
- Confined phase (8×16³ lattice, β=5.93, T≈0.75Tc): A₁⁺⁺ screening mass (local A₁⁺⁺ operator) = 0.46(8) in lattice units at z=5
- Deconfined phase (4×16³ lattice, β=5.93, T≈1.5Tc): A₁⁺⁺ screening mass (operators with time-like links) = 0.73(5) in lattice units at z=3
- Deconfined phase (4×16³ lattice, β=5.93): A₁⁺⁺ screening mass (operators without time-like links) = 1.3(1) in lattice units at z=3
- Deconfined phase (6×16³ lattice, β=6.101): A₁⁺⁺ screening mass (operators with time-like links) = 0.43(11) in lattice units at z=5
- Deconfined phase (6×16³ lattice, β=6.101): A₁⁺⁺ screening mass (operators without time-like links) = 0.97(7) in lattice units at z=3

- Deconfined phase (4×16³ lattice, β=5.93): B₁⁺⁺ screening mass = 2.0(2) in lattice units at z=2

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of screening masses in pure gauge theories, particularly measurements of the Debye screening mass from Polyakov loop correlations by the APE collaboration (1990) and earlier attempts to measure the magnetic mass by other groups. The paper provides a systematic classification of loop operators under the finite-temperature symmetry group, enabling future non-perturbative studies of the magnetic mass in the high-temperature limit and connecting to perturbative calculations of the electric gluon mass by Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981) and Nadkarni (1986).
