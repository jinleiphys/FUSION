# Low-Energy Inelastic Neutrino Reactions on 4He

> The inelastic scattering of neutrino off 4He is calculated microscopically at energies typical for core collapse supernova environment. The calculation is carried out with the Argonne V18 nucleon--nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three nucleon force. Full final state interaction is included via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method. The contribution of axial meson exchange currents to the cross sections is taken into account from effective field theory of nucleons and pions to order O(Q^3).

## Key claim

The paper presents the first full *ab-initio* microscopic calculation of inelastic neutrino-⁴He reactions using a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian (Argonne V18 plus Urbana IX) and including meson exchange currents (MEC) via effective field theory, but the MEC contribution is found to be small (on the percentage level) for the low-energy supernova environment considered.

## Method

The calculation solves the four-body nuclear problem using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method to include final state interactions, combined with the effective interaction in hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) approach for the ground state wave function and LIT equations. The nuclear Hamiltonian uses the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, and axial meson exchange currents are included from effective field theory of nucleons and pions to order O(Q³) with a hybrid approach (MEEFT). Temperature-averaged cross sections are computed for neutral current reactions (ν_x, ν'_x) and charged current reactions (ν_e, e⁻) and (ν̄_e, e⁺) assuming Fermi-Dirac neutrino spectra.

## Key numbers

- Temperature-averaged neutral current inclusive inelastic cross section per nucleon for AV18+UIX+MEC: 1.66 × 10⁻⁴² cm² at T = 4 MeV; 1.92 × 10⁻⁴² cm² at T = 8 MeV; 3.93 × 10⁻⁴² cm² at T = 14 MeV (Table 1)
- Temperature-averaged neutral current cross section per nucleon for (ν_x, ν'_x) at T = 10 MeV: 7.09 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (Table 2)
- Temperature-averaged charged current cross section per nucleon for (ν_e, e⁻) at T = 10 MeV: 3.36 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (Table 2)
- Temperature-averaged energy transfer cross section per nucleon for (ν_x, ν'_x) at T = 10 MeV: 21.7 × 10⁻⁴² MeV cm² (Table 3)
- Effect of three-nucleon force on cross section: about 30% at low energies
- Overall accuracy of calculation: about 5%; numerical accuracy: about 1%; cutoff dependence of MEC: about 1%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino-⁴He reactions by Haxton (1988) and Woosley et al. (1990) by providing the first full microscopic calculation with a realistic Hamiltonian, including three-nucleon forces and meson exchange currents. The results are of the same order of magnitude as Woosley et al. (1990) but show a stronger temperature dependence with substantial increment at high temperatures, indicating a different resonance structure. The calculation enables more robust modeling of neutrino heating in the pre-shock region of core-collapse supernovae, where ⁴He plays a decisive role, and can help constrain the neutrino oscillation parameter θ₁₃ through charged current reaction rates in the helium layer.
