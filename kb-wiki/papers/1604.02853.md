# Flavour dependence of the pion and kaon form factors and parton distribution functions

> The separate quark flavour contributions to the pion and kaon valence quark distribution functions are studied, along with the corresponding electromagnetic form factors in the space-like region. The calculations are made using the solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the model of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio with proper-time regularization. Both the pion and kaon form factors and the valence quark distribution functions reproduce many of the features of the available empirical data. The larger mass if the strange quark naturally explains the empirical fact that the ratio $u_{K^+}(x)/u_{π^+}(x)$ drops below unity at large $x$, with a value of approximately $M^2_u/M_s^2$ as $x \to 1$. With regard to the elastic form factors we report a large flavour dependence, with the $u$-quark contribution to the kaon form factor being an order of magnitude smaller than that of the $s$-quark at large $Q^2$, which may be a sensitive measure of confinement effects in QCD. Surprisingly though, the total $K^+$ and $π^+$ form factors differ by only 10\%.

## Key claim
The NJL model with proper-time regularization reproduces many features of empirical data for pion and kaon form factors and valence quark distributions, but the calculations are limited to a model that simulates confinement via an infrared cutoff and does not include QCD's running coupling, which causes the DSE result for \(Q^2 F_\pi(Q^2)\) to decrease at high \(Q^2\) while the NJL result plateaus.

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with proper-time regularization, using an infrared cutoff \(\Lambda_{\rm IR}=240\) MeV to simulate quark confinement. They compute the electromagnetic form factors of the pion and kaon in the space-like region, both with a bare quark-photon vertex and with a dressed vertex obtained from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. The valence quark distribution functions are calculated from the same model using the operator product expansion in the infinite-momentum frame.

## Key numbers
- Pion form factor: \(Q^2 F_\pi(Q^2) \simeq 0.49\) for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 6\) GeV\(^2\) (plateau value)
- Pion charge radius: \(r_{\pi^+} = 0.629\) fm (model), compared to experimental \(0.672 \pm 0.008\) fm
- Kaon charge radius: \(r_{K^+} = 0.586\) fm (model), compared to experimental \(0.560 \pm 0.031\) fm
- Ratio of quark-sector radii: \(r^s_{K^+} / r^d_{\pi^+} = 0.70\)
- Ratio of \(u\)-quark radii: \(r^u_{K^+} / r^u_{\pi^+} = 1.027\)
- Flavour breaking measure: \([r^u_{K^+} + r^s_{K^+}]/[r^u_{K^+} - r^s_{K^+}] = 0.19\)
- The \(s\)-quark contribution dominates the \(K^+\) form factor for \(Q^2 \geq 1.6\) GeV\(^2\)
- The total \(K^+\) and \(\pi^+\) form factors differ by only 10%

## Context
This work extends earlier NJL model studies of hadron structure (e.g., Bentz (1999), Cloet (2005)) and provides a direct comparison with Dyson-Schwinger equation results from Chang (2013). The calculations show that the larger strange quark mass naturally explains the empirical ratio \(u_{K^+}(x)/u_{\pi^+}(x)\) dropping below unity at large \(x\), with a value approximately \(M_u^2/M_s^2\) as \(x \to 1\). The large flavour dependence of the kaon form factor, with the \(u\)-quark contribution an order of magnitude smaller than the \(s\)-quark contribution at high \(Q^2\), may serve as a sensitive probe of confinement effects in QCD and could be tested by future parity-violating measurements.
