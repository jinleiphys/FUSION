# Spontaneous magnetization under a pseudovector interaction between quarks in high density quark matter

> Spontaneous magnetization and magnetic susceptibility originated from the pseudovector-type four-point interaction between quarks are calculated in quark matter with zero temperature and finite quark chemical potential by using the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. It is shown that both the chiral condensate and spin polarized condensate coexist in a narrow region of the quark chemical potential. And then, it is also shown that, in this narrow region, the spontaneous magnetization appears. Also, the magnetic susceptibility due to quarks with the positive energy is evaluated in the spin polarized phase.

## Key claim

Spontaneous magnetization arises from the pseudovector-type four-point interaction between quarks, but only in a very narrow window of quark chemical potential where both the chiral condensate and spin-polarized condensate coexist; outside this narrow region, no spontaneous magnetization occurs.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a pseudovector-type four-point interaction between quarks at zero temperature and finite quark chemical potential. They applied the mean field approximation, introduced a weak external magnetic field, expanded the thermodynamic potential to order B and B², and then took the limit B→0 to extract the spontaneous magnetization and magnetic susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical quark mass M = 0.322 GeV in vacuum (μ=0) with parameters Λ=0.631 GeV and G_s=5.5 GeV⁻²
- Constituent quark mass M_q = 0.335 GeV when current quark mass m₀=0.005 GeV is included
- Spontaneous magnetization per unit volume ranges from 1.15849×10¹⁸ C/ms at μ=0.3224 GeV to 0.321587×10¹⁸ C/ms at μ=0.3246 GeV
- The spin-polarized phase exists in the chemical potential window from μ_cr,1 ≈ 0.3224 GeV to μ_cr,2 ≈ 0.3247 GeV
- Critical baryon density from the spin-polarized phase to chiral symmetric phase corresponds to 1.78 ρ₀ where ρ₀=0.17 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear density
- Magnetic susceptibility change Δχ ranges from -5.80492×10⁻⁶ at μ=0.3224 GeV to -0.60179×10⁻⁶ at μ=0.3246 GeV, with a large value of -336.097×10⁻⁶ at μ=0.3248 GeV where M=U=0

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by the same authors on spin-polarized phases in high-density quark matter using the NJL model with tensor interactions (Morimoto et al., 2014, 2015, 2016), where tensor interactions did not produce spontaneous magnetization. The pseudovector interaction studied here follows the approach of N. Maruyama et al. (2011) and T. Tatsumi (2000) on quark ferromagnetic phases. The results suggest that spontaneous magnetization of order 10¹³–10¹⁴ Gauss could appear in compact stars such as magnetars, and the narrow chemical potential window corresponds to densities inside the metastable region before the spinodal onset studied by Costa et al. (2010).
