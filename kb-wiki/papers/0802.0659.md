# Description of nuclear octupole and quadrupole deformation close to the axial symmetry: Critical-point behavior of 224Ra and 224Th

> The model, introduced in a previous paper, for the description of the octupole and quadrupole degrees of freedom in conditions close to the axial symmetry, is applied to situations of shape phase transitions where the quadrupole amplitude can reach zero. The transitional nuclei 224,226Ra and 224Th are discussed in the frame of this model. Their level schemes can be reasonably accounted for assuming a square-well potential in two dimensions. Electromagnetic transition amplitudes are also evaluated and compared with existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the level schemes of the transitional nuclei 224Ra, 226Ra, and 224Th can be reasonably accounted for by assuming a square-well potential in two dimensions (β₂ and β₃) that describes the critical-point behavior of both quadrupole and octupole deformation, with the caveat that the calculated 1⁻ level is always somewhat lower than the experimental one due to the inclusion of a region where β₃ remains large while β₂ tends to zero.

## Method

The authors extend a previously introduced model (Bizzeti and Bizzeti-Sona, 2004) for octupole and quadrupole degrees of freedom near axial symmetry to cases where the quadrupole amplitude can reach zero, applying it to shape phase transitions. They assume a square-well potential in the β₂-β₃ plane with a rectangular shape parameterized by the ratio b = β₃ʷ/β₂ʷ, solve the Schrödinger equation numerically using the finite difference method with the Implicitly Restarted Arnoldi-Lanczos method (ARPACK package), and compute electromagnetic transition amplitudes (E2 and E1) for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 224Ra, the best-fit parameter b = 0.81; for 226Ra, b = 0.68; for 224Th, b = 0.85.
- The energy ratio E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) = 2.91 is cited as the signature of the critical point for quadrupole deformation (X(5) symmetry), approximately corresponding to 224Ra and 224Th.
- Experimental E1 transition ratio (1₁⁻ → 2₁⁺)/(1₁⁻ → 0₁⁺) for 224Ra is 1.52 ± 0.14, compared to the model value of 1.50; for 226Ra it is 1.36 ± 0.12 (model 1.47); for 224Th it is 1.49 ± 0.26 (model 1.50).
- For 226Ra, the experimental E1 ratio (3₁⁻ → 4₁⁺)/(3₁⁻ → 2₁⁺) is 1.11 ± 0.18, compared to the model value of 1.2.

## Context

This work extends the X(5) model of Iachello (2001) for quadrupole critical-point behavior by incorporating octupole degrees of freedom, building on the authors' previous model (Bizzeti and Bizzeti-Sona, 2004) that described octupole phase transitions in Thorium isotopes with stable quadrupole deformation. It also relates to the Analytic Quadrupole-Octupole axially symmetric model (AQOA) proposed by Bonatsos et al. (2005) and the work of Lenis and Bonatsos (2006), as well as potential-energy surface calculations by Nazarewicz et al. (1985). The results enable future experimental investigations of electromagnetic transition probabilities in these transitional nuclei, particularly for E2 transitions where experimental data are scarce.
