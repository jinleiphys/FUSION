# Critical temperature for quenching of pair correlations

> The level density at low spin in the 161,162-Dy and 171,172-Yb nuclei has been extracted from primary gamma rays. The nuclear heat capacity is deduced within the framework of the canonical ensemble. The heat capacity exhibits an S-formed shape as a function of temperature, which is interpreted as a fingerprint of the phase transition from a strongly correlated to an uncorrelated phase. The critical temperature for the quenching of pair correlations is found at Tc=0.50(4) MeV.

## Key claim

The canonical heat capacity extracted from experimental level densities in four rare-earth nuclei exhibits an S-shaped curve as a function of temperature, which is interpreted as a fingerprint of the phase transition from strongly paired to unpaired states; the critical temperature for the quenching of pair correlations is found at Tc = 0.50(4) MeV, but this value depends on the assumption that the lower-energy part of the level density can be approximated by a constant-temperature form and on the specific extrapolation to higher energies using a shifted Fermi gas model.

## Method

Level densities at low spin (2-8 hbar) in 161,162Dy and 171,172Yb were extracted from primary gamma-ray spectra measured with the CACTUS multidetector array using the (3He,alpha-gamma) reaction at 45 MeV beam energy. The nuclear heat capacity was then calculated within the canonical ensemble from the experimental level densities, which were extrapolated to higher excitation energies using a shifted Fermi gas model normalized to known neutron resonance spacings. The critical temperature Tc was extracted by fitting a simple analytical expression derived from a composite level density model (constant-temperature part joined to a Fermi gas part) to the low-temperature portion of the heat capacity curve.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for quenching of pair correlations: Tc = 0.52 MeV for 161Dy, 0.49 MeV for 162Dy, 0.50 MeV for 171Yb, and 0.49 MeV for 172Yb; the overall value is reported as Tc = 0.50(4) MeV
- The extracted Tc values are delayed compared to a degenerated BCS model with Tc = 0.5*Delta, which yields Tc = 0.48, 0.46, 0.41, and 0.38 MeV for 161Dy, 162Dy, 171Yb, and 172Yb respectively
- The gamma strength function follows a power law C*E_gamma^n with n = 3.9 for the Dy isotopes and n = 4.5 for the Yb isotopes
- The level density extrapolation uses normalization factors f of 1.2, 0.94, 0.4, and 0.6 for 161Dy, 162Dy, 171Yb, and 172Yb respectively
- The maximum error on Tc is estimated as Delta Tc < 0.04 MeV (about 8%)

## Context

This work extends earlier reports of structures in level densities attributed to pair breaking (Melby et al., 1999) and provides the first experimental extraction of the critical temperature for pairing quenching in rare-earth nuclei, which had previously been studied theoretically using finite-temperature BCS and RPA models (Sano and Yamasaki, 1963; Goodman, 1981) and shell model Monte Carlo calculations (Lang et al., 1993; Koonin et al., 1997). The results enable direct comparison with SMMC calculations performed for a narrow spin window and test predictions that the decrease of pair correlations takes place over several MeV of excitation energy (Dossing et al., 1995).
