# Mixing of scalar glueballs and flavour-singlet scalar mesons

> We discuss in detail the extraction of hadronic mixing strengths from lattice studies. We apply this to the mixing of a scalar glueball and a scalar meson in the quenched approximation. We also measure correlations appropriate for flavour-singlet scalar mesons using dynamical quark configurations from UKQCD. This enables us to compare the results from the quenched study of the mixing with the direct determination of the mixed spectrum. Improved methods of evaluating the disconnected quark diagrams are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hadronic mixing strengths (specifically between scalar glueballs and flavour-singlet scalar mesons) can be extracted from lattice QCD using both quenched and dynamical quark configurations, but the extraction is only unambiguous when the unmixed states are nearly degenerate (on-shell); otherwise, excited-state contamination makes the determination of the mixing parameter \(x_{00}\) impossible in principle from cross-correlations alone, though combining cross and disconnected correlators can overconstrain the system.

## Method

The authors use the variational method on lattice correlators to extract masses and couplings of scalar glueballs and scalar mesons, then model the mixing via a two-state system with off-diagonal mixing strength \(x_{00}\). They evaluate connected and disconnected fermionic correlators, as well as glueball–meson cross-correlations, on quenched lattices at \(\beta=5.7\) and on dynamical \(N_f=2\) configurations from UKQCD at \(\beta=5.2\). Improved stochastic methods for disconnected quark diagrams are employed, and the mixing parameter is extracted from both the cross-correlation \(H(t)\) and the disconnected correlator \(D(t)\) using lattice-specific formulas that account for discrete time sums.

## Key numbers

* Quenched scalar \(q\bar{q}\) mass: \(1.39(5)\) in lattice units at \(\kappa=0.14077\) (approximately strange quark mass) and \(1.36(2)\) at \(\kappa=0.13843\) (approximately twice strange), from fits to local and smeared operators over \(t=2\) to \(8\).
* Glueball mass in quenched approximation: \(0.95(2)\) in lattice units, from higher-statistics studies at the same \(\beta\).
* Mixing strength for strange quarks (quenched, one flavour): \(x_{00}a = 0.26(4)\), corresponding to \(x_{00} r_0 = 0.76(12)\), using \(r_0/a = 2.94\) and \(r_0 \approx 0.5\) fm.
* Mixing strength for heavier quarks (approximately twice strange): \(x_{00}a = 0.32(4)\).
* For the dynamical \(N_f=2\) case, applying the quenched mixing strength \(x_{00}a = 0.3\) per flavour with \(\sqrt{2}\) scaling gives a mass mixing matrix with diagonal elements \(0.95\) and \(1.39\) and off-diagonal \(0.42\), yielding mixed eigenstates at \(ma = 0.69\) and \(1.65\) (a downward shift of the glueball mass by about 25%).
* Lattice spacing from \(r_0\): \(a^{-1} \approx 1.1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice studies of scalar glueball–meson mixing by Lee and Weingarten (1999), who used Wilson fermions and only the cross-correlation \(H\) to determine \(x_{00}\), finding \(x_{00}a = 0.211(16)\) at \(\beta=5.7\) for strange quarks. The present study uses SW-clover fermions (which reduce order-\(a\) lattice artefacts) and additionally analyses the disconnected correlator \(D\), providing a cross-check that was missing in prior work. The results suggest substantial mixing at coarse lattice spacings, in contrast to the conclusion of Lee and Weingarten that the continuum mixing is very small. The dynamical \(N_f=2\) measurements provide a direct determination of the mixed spectrum, enabling comparison with the quenched mixing model and paving the way for future studies with larger statistics (approximately 10,000 configurations) to control excited-state contamination and determine the continuum mixing strength.
