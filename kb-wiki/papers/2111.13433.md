# Multi-Source Thermal Model Describing Transverse Momentum Spectra of Final-State Particles in High Energy Collisions

> In this mini review article, the transverse momentum spectra of final-state particles produced in high energy hadron-hadron, hadron-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions described by the multi-source thermal model at the quark or parton level is summarized. In the model, the participant or contributor quarks or partons are considered to contribute together to the transverse momentum distribution of final-state particles with different modes of contributions. The concrete mode of contribution is generally determined by the difference of azimuthal angles of contributor partons in their emissions.

## Key claim

The multi-source thermal model, when applied at the parton level with a revised Tsallis-like function, can uniformly describe transverse momentum spectra of final-state particles across hadron-hadron, hadron-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, but the concrete mode of parton contribution is determined by the difference of azimuthal angles of contributor partons in their emissions, and the convolution method for the parallel case (azimuthal difference of 0 or pi) is found to be more convenient for fitting wide pT spectra than the general Monte Carlo approach.

## Method

The model treats final-state particles as produced from the interactions of two or three contributor partons (quarks or gluons), where each parton's transverse momentum follows a revised Tsallis-like distribution. The total transverse momentum of a given particle is obtained by vector summation of the parton transverse momenta, with the azimuthal angles of the partons either treated as independent random variables in a Monte Carlo approach (general case), or fixed to specific differences (0 or pi for parallel case, pi/2 for perpendicular case) allowing analytical convolution or integration methods. The model also incorporates a Lorentz-like transformation to extract the kinetic freeze-out temperature and average transverse flow velocity at the parton level.

## Key numbers

- The revised Tsallis-like function for parton transverse momentum is given by \( f_i(p_{ti}) = C_i p_{ti}^{a_0} \left[ 1-\frac{1-q}{T} \left( m_{ti}-m_{0i}\right) \right]^{q/(1-q)} \), where \(T\) is the effective temperature, \(q\) is the entropy index, and \(a_0\) is the revised index.
- The entropy index \(q\) generally satisfies \(1 < q < 1.25\) for approximate equilibrium, with \(q=1\) corresponding to equilibrium.
- The model was applied to p+p collisions at center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV, fitting the invariant cross-sections of various hadrons measured by the PHENIX Collaboration.
- The average transverse flow velocity \(\langle\beta_t\rangle\) is introduced as a constant value for a given spectrum, independent of \(p_T\) but dependent on rapidity \(y\).

## Context

This mini-review summarizes and extends the authors' multi-source thermal model, which was originally proposed based on single-, two-, and three-fireball models (Liu et al., earlier works) and later evolved from using Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics to Tsallis statistics at the parton level. The work enables extraction of the kinetic freeze-out temperature and average transverse flow velocity at the parton level from experimental transverse momentum spectra, and the sudden changes of these quantities with collision energy or system size are expected to relate to the formation of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC. The model differs from the blast-wave model (Schnedermann et al., 1993; others) in its treatment of collective flow via a Lorentz-like transformation rather than a blast-wave parametrization.
