# Model tests of cluster separability in relativistic quantum mechanics

> A relativistically invariant quantum theory first advanced by Bakamjian and Thomas has proven very useful in modeling few-body systems. For three particles or more, this approach is known formally to fail the constraint of cluster separability, whereby symmetries and conservation laws that hold for a system of particles also hold for isolated subsystems. Cluster separability can be restored by means of a recursive construction using unitary transformations, but implementation is difficult in practice, and the quantitative extent to which the Bakamjian-Thomas approach violates cluster separability has never been tested. This paper provides such a test by means of a model of a scalar probe in a three-particle system for which (1) it is simple enough that there is a straightforward solution that satisfies Poincaré invariance and cluster separability, and (2) one can also apply the Bakamjian-Thomas approach. The difference between these calculations provides a measure of the size of the corrections from the Sokolov construction that are needed to restore cluster properties. Our estimates suggest that, in models based on nucleon degrees of freedom, the corrections that restore cluster properties are too small to effect calculations of observables.

## Key claim

The Bakamjian-Thomas construction for three or more particles formally violates cluster separability, but model calculations using a scalar probe in a three-particle system suggest that the corrections needed to restore cluster properties are too small to affect observables in models based on nucleon degrees of freedom, though they may be more important for models based on sub-nucleon degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors constructed a model of a scalar probe interacting with a three-particle system consisting of a bound pair (12) and a non-interacting struck particle (3), where only particle 3 interacts with the probe. They computed matrix elements of the scalar density operator using both a tensor-product (TP) representation that manifestly satisfies cluster separability and Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) representations in instant-form, front-form, and point-form dynamics, then compared the results to quantify the violation of cluster properties.

## Key numbers

- The tensor-product (TP) result for the scalar density matrix element is \( \mathcal{F}_{TP} = m G(q^2) \), where \( m \) is the mass of particle 3 and \( G(q^2) \) is its invariant form factor, with no dependence on the spectator bound-state momentum \( \mathbf{p}_{12} \).
- In the nonrelativistic limit where \( \mathbf{k} \), \( \mathbf{q} \), and \( \mathbf{p}_{12} \) are all small with respect to the relevant masses, the BT result becomes identical to the TP result, as the Jacobians become approximately unity.
- The BT results for instant-form, front-form, and point-form dynamics each contain an integral over the bound-state wave function \( \phi_\lambda(\mathbf{k}) \) and Jacobians that depend on the spectator momentum \( \mathbf{p}_{12} \), in contrast to the \( \mathbf{p}_{12} \)-independent TP result.

## Context

This work extends the formal analysis of cluster separability violations in the Bakamjian-Thomas construction (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) by providing the first quantitative test of the effect's magnitude. The Sokolov construction (Sokolov, 1977; Coester, 1982) provides a method to restore cluster properties via unitary transformations, but its complexity has prevented practical implementation. The results suggest that for nuclear physics applications involving nucleon degrees of freedom, the corrections from the Sokolov construction can likely be ignored, enabling continued use of the Bakamjian-Thomas approach for four-body calculations such as relativistic Faddeev calculations (Lin, 2007; Witala, 2004, 2005, 2008, 2011) and electron scattering from three-nucleon systems.
