# Vector-current correlation and charge separation via chiral-magnetic effect

> We investigate the vector-current correlation Pi_{mu nu} (VCC) in the presence of a strong external magnetic field (B_0 in the z direction) at low temperature (T<T^chi_c) with C- and CP-violations, indicated by the nonzero chiral-chemical potential (mu_chi>0), i.e. the chiral-magnetic effect (CME). For this purpose, we employ the instanton-vacuum configuration at finite T with nonzero topological charge (Q_t>0). From the numerical calculations, it turns out that the longitudinal component of the connected VCC is liner in B_0 and shows a bump, representing a corresponding vector meson at |Q|=(300~400) MeV for T=0. The bump becomes enhanced as T increases and the bump position shifts to a lager |Q| value. In the limit of |Q|->0, the transverse component of the connected VCC disappears, whereas the longitudinal one remains finite and gets insensitive to B_0 with respect to T, due to diluting instanton contributions. Considering a simple collision geometry of HIC and some assumptions on the induced magnetic field and screening effect, we can estimate the charge separation (ChS) as a function of centrality using the present results for VCC. The numerical results show a qualitative agreement with experiments for the Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions. These results are almost independent on the source of CME, instanton or sphaleron, as long as the CME current is linear in B_0.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the longitudinal component of the connected vector-current correlation (VCC) in the presence of a strong external magnetic field is linear in the magnetic field strength \(B_0\) and exhibits a bump corresponding to a vector meson at momentum transfer \(|Q| = (300 \sim 400)\) MeV at zero temperature, with the bump becoming enhanced and shifting to larger \(|Q|\) as temperature increases; these results are stated to be almost independent of whether the chiral-magnetic effect (CME) source is instanton or sphaleron, as long as the CME current is linear in \(B_0\).

## Method

The author employs the instanton-vacuum configuration model at finite temperature (\(T \lesssim T^{\chi}_c\)) with nonzero topological charge (\(Q_t > 0\)), using the Harrington-Shepard caloron to incorporate temperature modifications on the average instanton size \(\bar{\rho}\) and inter-instanton distance \(\bar{R}\). The vector-current correlation (VCC) is computed from the effective action via functional derivatives with respect to an external vector source, expanding the gauged quark propagator to linear order in the magnetic field \(B_0\) and the CP-violating parameter \(\delta\) (proportional to the instanton-number fluctuation \(\Delta\)). The connected VCC components (transverse, longitudinal, temporal) are evaluated numerically using the fermionic Matsubara formalism, and the charge separation (ChS) is estimated from these results using a simple collision geometry for heavy-ion collisions (HIC) with assumptions on the induced magnetic field and screening effects.

## Key numbers

- The average inter-instanton distance in vacuum: \(\bar{R} = 1\) fm.
- The average instanton size in vacuum: \(\bar{\rho} = 1/3\) fm.
- The constituent quark mass in vacuum: \(M_0 = 325\) MeV.
- The current quark mass: \(m = 5\) MeV.
- The bump in the longitudinal connected VCC appears at momentum transfer \(|Q| = (300 \sim 400)\) MeV for \(T = 0\).
- The instanton-number density scale: \((N/V)^{1/4} = 197\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on the chiral-magnetic effect using the instanton-vacuum model (Nam 2009) by computing the connected vector-current correlation (VCC) in addition to the previously computed vacuum expectation value of the induced current. The results are compared qualitatively with experimental charge separation measurements from the STAR collaboration for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions, and the approach is contrasted with lattice QCD simulations (Buividovich 2009, 2010), effective QCD-like models such as the Polyakov-loop-augmented Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model (Fukushima 2010, Fu 2010), and gauge-gravity duality models (Yee 2009, Gorsky 2010, Rebhan 2009). The work enables further theoretical estimation of charge separation in heavy-ion collisions as a function of centrality, with the finding that the strength of charge separation for Cu+Cu collisions is generically larger than for Au+Au collisions at a given centrality.
