# Influence of final-state radiation on heavy-flavour observables in pp collisions

> The influence of final-state radiation (FSR) of heavy quarks on observables in high-energy proton-proton collisions is studied. The transverse momentum correlation of D and Dbar mesons, which have been emitted with an azimuthal difference angle close to 180 degrees, is identified as an observable which is sensitive to the FSR process. We demonstrate this by performing calculations with the EPOS3+HQ model and with the event generator Pythia 6. The initial symmetric pT = pT' correlation for back-to-back pairs does not completely vanish in EPOS3+HQ, neither for the final DDbar pairs nor for the ccbar pairs before hadronisation. Also a significant difference in the shape of the correlation distribution for EPOS3+HQ and Pythia 6 is observed. Therefore, particle correlations in pp data offer the possibility to study several aspects of energy loss in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum imbalance of back-to-back D and Dbar meson pairs is sensitive to final-state radiation (FSR) of heavy quarks, and this correlation does not completely vanish in the EPOS3+HQ model even after FSR, whereas Pythia 6 shows a different shape; the observable can therefore discriminate between different implementations of the parton shower.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV using two models: EPOS3+HQ (which couples heavy-quark transport to a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic medium) and Pythia 6 (with both LO and LO+NLO charm production settings). They selected charm-anticharm pairs and D-Dbar meson pairs with an azimuthal difference larger than 3pi/4 (back-to-back), constructed a 2-dimensional correlation function C_R(p_T, p_T') from the transverse momenta of the pair, and projected it onto the diagonal Delta p_T = p_T - p_T' axis for a fixed sum p_T + p_T' interval of 6 to 10 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

* For c-cbar pairs in EPOS3+HQ without in-medium interactions, the correlation function at small Delta p_T reaches C_R approximately 2.3, meaning such pairs are observed 3.3 times more often than if momenta were uncorrelated.
* For LO Pythia 6 c-cbar pairs, the correlation function starts at C_R = 0.9 at Delta p_T = 0 and decreases almost linearly, showing no enhancement at zero imbalance.
* The single charm quark spectra from EPOS3+HQ agree well with FONLL predictions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, while LO+NLO Pythia 6 overpredicts the FONLL total yield.
* For back-to-back charm quarks with p_T > 6 GeV/c, EPOS3+HQ and LO Pythia 6 show similar spectral shapes and absolute normalisation.
* In LO+NLO Pythia 6, about 87% of back-to-back c-cbar pairs come from NLO events (flavour excitation and gluon splitting).

## Context

This work extends studies of heavy-flavour energy loss in heavy-ion collisions, where traditional observables like R_AA and v_2 (Adam 2015, Sirunyan 2017, Abelev 2013) have not uniquely determined the interaction mechanisms. The transverse momentum imbalance was originally proposed to differentiate collisional from radiative energy loss in heavy-ion collisions (Song 2016, Nahrgang 2013). The authors show that in pp collisions, where quark-gluon plasma formation is debated (ALICE 2017, Khachatryan 2016), the observable is primarily sensitive to FSR and can constrain parton-shower implementations. The method can later be applied to pA and AA data to study cold and hot nuclear matter effects on heavy-quark energy loss.
