# New hadron configuration: The double-gluon hybrid state

> This is the first study on the double-gluon hybrid, which consists of one valence quark and one valence antiquark together with two valence gluons. We concentrate on the one with the exotic quantum number $J^{PC} = 2^{+-}$ that conventional $\bar q q$ mesons can not reach. We apply QCD sum rule method to evaluate its mass to be $2.26^{+0.20}_{-0.25}$ GeV, and study its possible decay patterns. Especially, its three-meson decay patterns are generally not suppressed severely compared to two-meson decay patterns, so the $S$-wave three-meson decay channels $f_1ωπ/f_1ρπ$ can be useful in identifying its nature, which is of particular importance to the direct test of QCD in the low energy sector.

# New hadron configuration: The double-gluon hybrid state

## Key claim

This is the first study of the double-gluon hybrid state, a hadron containing one valence quark, one valence antiquark, and two valence gluons. The authors claim that the state with exotic quantum numbers J^PC = 2^{+-} has a mass of 2.26^{+0.20}_{-0.25} GeV based on QCD sum rule calculations, but this result depends on the specific choice of current operator and only one of twelve constructed currents yields a mass below 3.0 GeV.

## Method

The authors constructed twelve double-gluon hybrid currents by combining a color-octet quark-antiquark field (with S-wave spin-parity J^P = 0^-) with relativistic color-octet double-gluon fields using both symmetric (d^{npq}) and antisymmetric (f^{npq}) SU(3) structure constants. They applied the QCD sum rule method, calculating the two-point correlation function at both hadron and quark-gluon levels, performing the operator product expansion up to dimension-eight condensates, and extracting the mass through Borel transformation with a one-pole-dominance assumption. The spectral density was computed including Feynman diagrams proportional to alpha_s^2 times g_s^0, g_s^1, and selected g_s^2 contributions.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the double-gluon hybrid state with J^PC = 2^{+-}: 2.26^{+0.20}_{-0.25} GeV, obtained from QCD sum rule analysis of the current J^{alpha_1 beta_1, alpha_2 beta_2}_{2^{+-}}
- Borel window: 1.61 GeV^2 <= M_B^2 <= 1.78 GeV^2, determined at threshold value s_0 = 7.0 GeV^2
- Minimum threshold value for non-vanishing Borel window: s_0^{min} = 6.3 GeV^2
- Working region for threshold value: 5.0 GeV^2 <= s_0 <= 9.0 GeV^2
- Quark condensate: <bar q q> = -(0.240 +/- 0.010)^3 GeV^3
- Gluon condensate: <alpha_s GG> = (6.35 +/- 0.35) x 10^{-2} GeV^4
- Three-gluon condensate: <g_s^3 G^3> = (8.2 +/- 1.0) x <alpha_s GG> GeV^2
- All other eleven double-gluon hybrid currents yielded masses larger than 3.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of hybrid hadrons beyond the single-gluon hybrid, which has three experimental candidates (pi_1(1400), pi_1(1600), pi_1(2015)) with exotic quantum numbers I^G J^PC = 1^- 1^{-+} observed by experiments such as E852 and COMPASS. Previous theoretical studies of single-gluon hybrids include flux tube model calculations by Isgur (1984) and Page (1998), constituent gluon model work by Iddir (2007), and quenched lattice QCD simulations by McNeile (1998), Dudek (2009), and Briceno (2017) giving masses from 1.7 to 2.0 GeV. The double-gluon hybrid state predicted here at 2.26 GeV is accessible to BESIII, GlueX, LHC, and PANDA experiments, and its three-meson decay channels (f_1 omega pi / f_1 rho pi) are proposed as a way to distinguish it from tetraquark interpretations, which have been suggested for the pi_1 candidates by Chung (2002) and Chen (2008).
