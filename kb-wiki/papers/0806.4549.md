# Light hadron spectroscopy using domain wall valence quarks on an Asqtad sea

> We calculate the light hadron spectrum in full QCD using two plus one flavor Asqtad sea quarks and domain wall valence quarks. Meson and baryon masses are calculated on a lattice of spatial size $L \approx 2.5$\texttt{fm}, and a lattice spacing of $a \approx 0.124$\texttt{fm}, for pion masses as light as $m_π\approx 300$\texttt{MeV}, and compared with the results by the MILC collaboration with Asqtad valence quarks at the same lattice spacing. Two- and three-flavor chiral extrapolations of the baryon masses are performed using both continuum and mixed-action heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. Both the three-flavor and two-flavor functional forms describe our lattice results, although the low-energy constants from the next-to-leading order SU(3) fits are inconsistent with their phenomenological values. Next-to-next-to-leading order SU(2) continuum formulae provide a good fit to the data and yield and extrapolated nucleon mass consistent with experiment, but the convergence pattern indicates that even our lightest pion mass may be at the upper end of the chiral regime. Surprisingly, our nucleon masses are essentially lineaer in $m_π$ over our full range of pion masses, and we show this feature is common to all recent dynamical calculations of the nucleon mass. The origin of this linearity is not presently understood, and lighter pion masses and increased control of systematic errors will be needed to resolve this puzzling behavior.

## Key claim
The nucleon mass extrapolated using next-to-next-to-leading order SU(2) continuum chiral perturbation theory agrees with experiment, but the convergence pattern indicates that even the lightest pion mass studied (approximately 300 MeV) may be at the upper end of the chiral regime, and the nucleon masses are essentially linear in the pion mass over the full range studied, a feature common to all recent dynamical calculations whose origin is not understood.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using 2+1 flavor Asqtad sea quarks (MILC configurations) with domain wall valence quarks on a 20^3 x 64 lattice with spatial size L approximately 2.5 fm and lattice spacing a approximately 0.124 fm. Hadron masses were extracted from correlation functions using single-exponential fits and fits including oscillating terms from the domain wall fermion transfer matrix, with statistical errors determined by jackknife and bootstrap procedures. Chiral extrapolations of baryon masses were performed using both continuum and mixed-action heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for two and three flavors.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.12406 fm (2% uncertainty)
- Spatial volume: V = (2.5 fm)^3
- Lightest pion mass: m_pi approximately 290 MeV (from the m007 ensemble)
- Pion mass on m007 ensemble: 0.1842(7) in lattice units (approximately 290 MeV)
- Valence strange quark mass: m_s^DWF = 0.081 (tuned on the N_f=3 ensemble)
- Domain wall height: M_5 = 1.7, fifth dimension length: L_s = 16
- Number of configurations per ensemble: 3085 (m007), 3729 (m010), 3389 (m020), 2255 (m030), 324 (m040), 425 (m050)
- Nucleon mass extrapolated using NNLO SU(2) continuum formulae consistent with experiment

## Context
This work extends the MILC collaboration's spectrum calculations (Bernard 2001, Aubin 2004) by using domain wall valence quarks instead of Asqtad valence quarks, providing a test of mixed-action systematics and enabling more straightforward baryon interpolating operators. The results enable future mixed-action effective field theory analyses (Bar 2002, Bar 2003, Tiburzi 2005, Golterman 2005) to extract low-energy constants for hadronic processes, and the unexplained linearity of nucleon mass in the pion mass motivates the need for lighter pion masses and better control of systematic errors to resolve this puzzling behavior.
