# Magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson effects in an interacting hadron gas in the presence of an external magnetic field

> The universality of electric charge as a quantum number allows thermoelectric properties to manifest across diverse systems, starting from a hot quantum chromodynamic matter in heavy-ion collisions at a high energy scale to semiconductors in condensed matter systems at a low energy scale. In this work, we explore the emergence of magneto-transport phenomena, specifically the magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson effects, in a hot and dense hadronic medium produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and Large Hadron Collider energies. These phenomena arise due to the combined influence of temperature gradients and non-zero baryon chemical potential, particularly in the presence of an external magnetic field. Using the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation time approximation, we analyze the behavior of the hadronic medium considering different frameworks of hadron resonance gas models. The presence of external magnetic fields breaks the isotropy of the thermoelectric transport coefficient matrix, giving rise to new components of the Thomson coefficient, namely, magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson coefficients. For the first time, we estimate the magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson coefficients, which originate from the temperature dependence of the magneto-Seebeck coefficient and Nernst coefficient, respectively, in hadron gas under the influence of a static and time-varying magnetic field. Our findings provide a novel perspective on the higher-order thermoelectric properties of the hot and dense hadronic medium in the context of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for the first time, the magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson coefficients can be estimated for a hot and dense hadronic medium produced in heavy-ion collisions, but this result depends on the assumption that the hadronic scattering cross-sections remain field-independent within the hard-sphere approximation, and the authors explicitly note that a detailed quantum-field-theoretic treatment of magnetic field effects on hadron-hadron interactions lies beyond the scope of their kinetic-theory framework.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation time approximation to derive the magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson coefficients for a hadron gas under static and time-varying external magnetic fields. They employed four different hadron resonance gas frameworks: the ideal HRG model, the excluded volume HRG model, the repulsive mean-field HRG model, and the van der Waals HRG model, considering all hadrons and resonances up to a mass cut-off of 2.6 GeV. The magnetic field was taken along the z-direction, and the temperature dependence of the magneto-Seebeck coefficient and Nernst coefficient was used to compute the higher-order Thomson coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For the static magnetic field picture, two representative field strengths were used: \(eB = 0.1\) and \(1.0~m_\pi^2\).
- For the dynamical magnetic field picture, a magnetic decay parameter \(\tau_B = 6\) fm was adopted.
- The magnetic field strength in peripheral heavy-ion collisions can peak around \(m_\pi^2 \sim 10^{18}\) G for Au-Au collisions at RHIC and reach up to \(15~m_\pi^2\) in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.
- The ideal HRG model successfully describes lattice QCD thermodynamic quantities up to temperatures of approximately \(T \approx 150\) MeV.
- The repulsive mean-field parameter for baryons was taken as \(K_B = 0.450\) GeV fm\(^3\) and for mesons as \(K_M = 0.050\) GeV fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Singh 2025) on the Thomson coefficient of interacting hot hadron gas in the absence of a magnetic field, now incorporating magnetic field effects that introduce anisotropy and give rise to the magneto-Thomson and transverse Thomson coefficients. The study builds upon prior calculations of leading-order thermoelectric coefficients in hadron gas using HRG models (Bhatt 2018, Das 2020, Das 2021) and magneto-thermoelectric effects in QCD matter (Singh 2023, Singh 2024), and it enables future investigations of higher-order thermoelectric transport in heavy-ion collisions and comparisons with lattice QCD predictions at finite baryon chemical potential.
