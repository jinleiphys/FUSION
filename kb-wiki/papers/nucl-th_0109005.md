# Neutron structure function $F_2^n(x)$ from deep inelastic electron scattering off few-nucleon systems

> The possibility of a reliable extraction of the neutron deep inelastic structure function, $F_2^n(x)$, for $ x < 0.85$ from joint measurements of deep inelastic structure functions of deuteron, $^{3}He$ and $^{3}H$ is investigated. The model dependence in this extraction, linked to the possible different interactions between nucleons in nuclei, is shown to be weak, if the nuclear structure effects are properly taken into account. A combined analysis of the deep inelastic structure functions of these nuclei is proposed to study effects beyond the impulse approximation.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a reliable extraction of the neutron deep inelastic structure function \(F_2^n(x)\) for \(x < 0.85\) can be achieved from joint measurements of the deuteron, \(^3He\) and \(^3H\) structure functions, with the model dependence linked to different nucleon-nucleon interactions being weak only if nuclear structure effects (including the Coulomb interaction in \(^3He\)) are properly taken into account via spectral functions rather than simpler momentum distributions.

## Method

The authors use the impulse approximation (IA) to express the deep inelastic structure functions of deuteron, \(^3He\) and \(^3H\) as convolutions of free nucleon structure functions with nucleon spectral functions (or momentum distributions) derived from realistic nuclear wave functions. They construct recurrence relations for the ratio \(r(x) = F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)\) from the experimental ratios \(E^{HeT}(x) = F_2^{He}(x)/F_2^T(x)\), \(E^{Dp}(x) = F_2^D(x)/F_2^p(x)\), and \(E^{HeD}(x) = F_2^{He}(x)/F_2^D(x)\), using spectral functions obtained from the correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) approach with interactions including RSC, AV14, AV14+Brazil TBF, AV18, AV18+UIX TBF, and AV18+TM' TBF.

## Key numbers

* The recurrence relation for extracting \(r(x)\) from \(E^{HeT}(x)\) converges to better than 1% accuracy after only ten iterations, up to \(x = 0.85\).
* When interactions that reproduce the experimental binding energy of \(^3H\) are used (AV14+Brazil, AV18+TM', AV18+UIX), the model dependence in the extracted \(r(x)\) is at most 1% in the range \(0 \le x \le 0.85\).
* If the Coulomb interaction is neglected in the \(^3He\) spectral functions, the extracted \(r(x)\) differs from the input by about 4% at \(x = 0.85\).
* Using momentum distributions instead of spectral functions to evaluate \(R^{HeT}(x)\) leads to extracted \(r(x)\) values that differ from the correct one by more than 13% for \(x \ge 0.8\).
* For the deuteron-proton case (\(E^{Dp}\)), using different two-body interactions yields differences in the extracted \(r(x)\) of less than 4% up to \(x = 0.80\) and 8% at \(x = 0.85\).
* For the \(^3He\)-deuteron case (\(E^{HeD}\)), using different interactions yields differences as high as 12% at \(x = 0.8\) and 25% at \(x = 0.85\), but restricting to interactions that reproduce the \(^3H\) binding energy reduces this to at most 1% up to \(x = 0.85\).

## Context

This work extends the recurrence procedure of Pace, Salme, Scopetta (2000) for extracting \(F_2^n(x)\) from the \(^3He/^3H\) ratio, and addresses concerns raised by Thomas (1990) about medium effects beyond the impulse approximation in deuteron analyses. The authors propose a joint analysis of deuteron, \(^3He\) and \(^3H\) structure functions as a consistency check to discriminate between different theoretical models for nuclear deep inelastic structure functions, including those of Frankfurt and Kaptari (various years), and to test effects beyond the impulse approximation. The approach is designed for the kinematic range accessible at TJLAB (Jefferson Lab).
