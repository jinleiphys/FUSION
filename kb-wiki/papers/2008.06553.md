# P-Wave Two-Particle Bound and Scattering States in a Finite Volume including QED

> The mass shifts for two-fermion bound and scattering P-wave states subject to the long-range interactions due to QED in the non-relativistic regime are derived. Introducing a short range force coupling the spinless fermions to one unit of angular momentum in the framework of pionless EFT, we first calculate both perturbatively and non-perturbatively the Coulomb corrections to fermion-fermion scattering in the continuum and infinite volume context. Motivated by the research on particle-antiparticle bound states, we extend the results to fermions of identical mass and opposite charge. Second, we transpose the system onto a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions and we calculate the finite volume corrections to the energy of the lowest bound and unbound $T_1^{\pm}$ eigenstates. In particular, power law corrections proportional to the fine structure constant and resembling the recent results for S-wave states are found. Higher order contributions in $α$ are neglected, since the gapped nature of the momentum operator in the lattice environnement allows for a perturbative treatment of the QED interactions.

## Key claim

The paper derives finite-volume energy shifts for P-wave two-fermion bound and scattering states in a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions, showing that the leading-order QED corrections are power-law (rather than exponential) in the inverse lattice size and proportional to the fine-structure constant, with the same sign as the corresponding shifts without electromagnetic interactions.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT) with a P-wave contact interaction between spinless fermions, adding Coulomb interactions via non-relativistic QED (NRQED) retaining only the electrostatic potential. They first compute the T-matrix for fermion-fermion scattering in infinite volume both perturbatively and non-perturbatively in the fine-structure constant, then transpose the system onto a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions and derive the finite-volume corrections to the lowest bound and unbound \(T_1^{\pm}\) eigenstates using L\"uscher-type quantization conditions adapted for long-range QED interactions.

## Key numbers

* The P-wave scattering length is given by \(a = \frac{M}{4\pi}\frac{D(E^*)}{3}\), where \(D(E^*)\) is the energy-dependent coupling constant and \(M\) is the fermion mass.
* The renormalized coupling constant in the PDS scheme is \(D(E^*,\mu) = 3a\left(\frac{4\pi}{M}+\mu\frac{\mathbf{p}^2}{2}\right)\), where \(\mu\) is the renormalization scale and \(\mathbf{p}\) is the center-of-mass momentum.
* The finite-volume energy shift for the lowest bound P-wave state is negative and proportional to the fine-structure constant, with the same magnitude as the S-wave shift when order \(1/L^3\) terms are neglected.
* The energy of the lowest unbound P-wave state in finite volume contains a factor \(\xi/M \equiv 4\pi^2/M L^2\) relative to the S-wave case, reflecting the non-zero energy of the \(T_1\) irrep state.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kong and Ravndal (2000) on S-wave proton-proton scattering with Coulomb interactions to the P-wave case, and generalizes the finite-volume results of Beane and Savage (2014) for S-wave states to P-wave states. The formalism is motivated by studies of proton-antiproton bound states (protonium) and provides the necessary framework for including QED corrections in lattice QCD calculations of two-body systems with non-zero angular momentum, such as \(\pi^{\pm}\pi^{\pm}\) scattering.
