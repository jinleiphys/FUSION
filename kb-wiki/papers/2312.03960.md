# Global density-dependent $α$-nucleon interaction for $α$-nucleus elastic scattering

> We provide a global density-dependent $^4$He-nucleon (DD-$αN$) interaction to construct the $α$-nucleus optical model potential (OMP) in a wide range of incident energies. The global parametrization for the DD-$αN$ interaction is obtained based on the proton-$^4$He OMP which reproduces the elastic scattering cross-section data very well in the incident energies of 12.04--500 MeV per nucleon. We derive the $α$-nucleus potential by a folding procedure with the point-nucleon density obtained by a microscopic mean-field model using the present DD-$αN$ interaction. The density dependence of the DD-$αN$ interaction is fixed phenomenologically to reproduce the $α$-nucleus elastic scattering cross-section data by the $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb targets at $E/A =$ 10--342.5 MeV. We also show the total reaction cross sections, which are helpful in fixing one free parameter, the renormalization factor for the imaginary part of the $α$-nucleus potential. Lastly, we show some examples, which clearly demonstrate the validity and power of the present DD-$αN$ approach.

## Key claim

The paper provides a global density-dependent alpha-nucleon (DD-alphaN) interaction that constructs the alpha-nucleus optical model potential (OMP) across a wide incident energy range, but the density dependence parameters (beta_0, beta_1, beta_W) are fixed phenomenologically to reproduce elastic scattering data for only five specific target nuclei (O-16, Ca-40, Ni-58, Zr-90, Pb-208) and one free parameter (N_I) for the imaginary part remains target-dependent.

## Method

The authors first constructed a phenomenological proton-He-4 OMP using a double-Woods-Saxon real potential and a single-Woods-Saxon imaginary potential, fitting parameters to reproduce p+He-4 elastic scattering data at 12.04-500 MeV per nucleon. They then introduced density-dependent functions (exponential for the first real term and imaginary term, linear for the second real term) into this alpha-nucleon interaction, and fixed the density-dependence parameters beta_0, beta_1, and beta_W by folding the interaction with point-nucleon densities from Skyrme-HF+BCS calculations (SkM* parameter set) to reproduce alpha-nucleus elastic scattering data for O-16, Ca-40, Ni-58, Zr-90, and Pb-208 targets at E/A = 10-342.5 MeV. A renormalization factor N_I was applied to the imaginary part of the folded potential.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing anomaly in alpha-nucleus folding model potentials, where renormalization factors for the real part (N_R) show a different trend for alpha-nucleus systems compared to heavy-ion systems when using standard effective nucleon-nucleon interactions like CDM3Y6 (Khoa 1997) and JLM (Jeukenne 1977). The authors treat the alpha-nucleon system as an elementary process rather than using nucleon-nucleon interactions designed for infinite nuclear matter, which is motivated by the fact that the alpha particle's inner density is substantially larger than saturation density. This approach enables a global alpha-nucleus potential with only one free parameter (N_I) that can be fixed using total reaction cross section data, and it can be applied to predict alpha-nucleus elastic scattering for a wide range of target masses and incident energies.
