# Characterization of the initial state and QGP medium from a combined Bayesian analysis of LHC data at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV

> We perform a global Bayesian analysis of a modern event-by-event heavy-ion collision model and LHC data at $\sqrt s$ = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. After calibration, the model simultaneously describes multiplicity, transverse momentum, and flow data at both beam energies. We report new constraints on the scaling of initial-state entropy deposition and QGP transport coefficients, including a quantitative estimate of the temperature-dependent shear viscosity $(η/s)(T)$.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the initial-state entropy deposition parameter \(p\) is strongly peaked near zero, implying entropy scales approximately as the geometric mean of nuclear thickness functions, and that the specific shear viscosity at the transition temperature \(T_c = 0.154\) GeV has a narrow posterior peak at \((\eta/s)_\text{min} = 0.06\), below the KSS bound of \(1/4\pi \approx 0.08\) but consistent within 90% credible interval; zero \(\eta/s\) is excluded at the 90% level, and a temperature-dependent (non-constant) \(\eta/s\) is confirmed at the 90% level.

## Method

The authors performed a global Bayesian analysis of a multi-stage heavy-ion collision model (Trento initial conditions, free-streaming, VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner) calibrated to ALICE data from Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt s = 2.76\) and 5.02 TeV. They evaluated the model at 500 parameter points via Latin-hypercube sampling, each with \(\mathcal O(10^4)\) minimum-bias events, then built Gaussian process emulators to interpolate the model output and used Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling to construct the posterior distribution for model parameters.

## Key numbers

- Entropy deposition parameter \(p\): posterior median and 90% credible interval strongly peaked near zero, corroborating saturation-based models such as IP-Glasma and EKRT.
- Specific shear viscosity at transition temperature: \((\eta/s)_\text{min} = 0.06\) (posterior median), with zero \(\eta/s\) excluded at the 90% level; the KSS bound is \(1/4\pi \approx 0.08\).
- Transition temperature \(T_c = 0.154\) GeV used in the shear viscosity parametrization.
- Bulk viscosity parameters: \((\zeta/s)_\text{max} \gtrsim 0.02\) or \((\zeta/s)_\text{width} \gtrsim 0.01\) GeV, but not both simultaneously; result is sensitive to implementation of bulk viscous corrections at particlization.
- Model simultaneously describes multiplicity, transverse momentum, and flow data at both beam energies within approximately 10%.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses by Bernhard et al. (2015, 2016) that placed preliminary constraints on QGP properties, now incorporating data at both 2.76 and 5.02 TeV with improved statistics and a more complete model. The result that entropy deposition scales as the geometric mean of nuclear thickness functions supports saturation-based initial-state models such as IP-Glasma (Schenke et al., 2012) and EKRT (Niemi et al., 2015). The temperature-dependent shear viscosity estimate enables future combined analyses including RHIC beam energies to further reduce uncertainty on \((\eta/s)(T)\) and potentially constrain the curvature parameter.
