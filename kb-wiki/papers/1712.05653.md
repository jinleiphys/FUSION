# $Z_c(3900)/Z_c(3885)$ as a virtual state from $πJ/ψ-\bar{D}^*D$ interaction

> In this work, we study the $J/ψπ$ and $\bar{D}^*D$ invariant mass spectra of the $Y(4260)$ decay to find out the origin of the $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(3885)$ structures. The $J/ψπ-\bar{D}^{*}D$ interaction is studied in a coupled-channel quasipotential Bethe-Saltpeter equation approach, and embedded to the $Y(4260)$ decay process to reproduce both $J/ψπ^-$ and $D^{*-}D^0$ invariant mass spectra observed at BESIII simultaneously. It is found out that a virtual state at energy about 3870 MeV is produced from the interaction when both invariant mass spectra are comparable with the experiment. The results support that both $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(3885)$ have the same origin, that is, a virtual state from $J/ψπ-\bar{D}^*{D}$ interaction, in which the $\bar{D}^*{D}$ interaction is more important and the coupling between $\bar{D}^*{D}$ and $J/ψπ$ channels plays a minor role.

## Key claim
The paper claims that both the \(Z_c(3900)\) and \(Z_c(3885)\) structures observed in \(Y(4260)\) decay originate from the same virtual state at about 3870 MeV produced by the \(\pi J/\psi-\bar{D}^*D\) interaction, with the \(\bar{D}^*D\) interaction being dominant and the coupling to \(\pi J/\psi\) playing a minor role. The claim is hedged by the condition that this interpretation holds only when both invariant mass spectra are simultaneously reproduced with a cutoff \(\Lambda=0.185\) GeV.

## Method
The authors studied the \(J/\psi\pi\) and \(\bar{D}^*D\) invariant mass spectra of \(Y(4260)\) decay using a coupled-channel quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach for the \(\pi J/\psi-\bar{D}^*D\) interaction. The interaction potential was constructed from effective Lagrangians based on heavy-quark effective theory, including one-boson-exchange contributions from \(\pi\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\sigma\), and \(J/\psi\) mesons. The rescattering amplitude was embedded into the three-body decay of \(Y(4260)\) to reproduce both the \(\pi^-J/\psi\) and \(D^{*-}D^0\) invariant mass spectra observed at BESIII simultaneously, with a parameterized background contribution.

## Key numbers
- The virtual state is found at an energy of about 3870 MeV from the \(\pi J/\psi-\bar{D}^*D\) interaction.
- The cutoff parameter used to reproduce both invariant mass spectra is \(\Lambda=0.185\) GeV.
- The background parameters for the \(\pi^-J/\psi\) invariant mass spectrum are \((a,b,c)=(0.5,1.2,3.6)\); for the \(D^{*-}D^0\) spectrum they are \((1.0,0.05,8.5)\).
- In the full model, a pole below the \(\bar{D}^*D\) threshold appears for cutoff values larger than about 2.1 GeV, with a pole energy of 3875 MeV at \(\Lambda=2.1\) GeV and 3873+1i MeV at \(\Lambda=2.3\) GeV.
- Without the \(\pi^-J/\psi\) channel, a bound state pole at 3875 MeV appears at \(\Lambda=2.2\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the \(Z_c(3900)/Z_c(3885)\) as hadronic molecular states, particularly following the one-boson-exchange model combined with quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach by He (2015). It contradicts the lattice work by Ikeda (2016) which suggested the off-diagonal \(\pi J/\psi-\bar{D}D^*\) coupling is more important than the \(\bar{D}D^*\) interaction. The analysis supports the suggestion by Albaladejo (2015) that the \(Z_c(3885)/Z_c(3900)\) may originate from a virtual state, but with the interaction couplings determined from heavy-quark symmetry rather than treated as free parameters. This enables future studies to distinguish between virtual state, bound state, and cusp interpretations for near-threshold exotic structures.
