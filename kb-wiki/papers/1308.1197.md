# Simulation of energy scan of pion interferometry in central Au + Au collisions at relativistic energies

> We present a systematic analysis of two-pion interferometry for the central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}$ = 3, 5, 7, 11, 17, 27, 39, 62, 130 and 200 GeV/c with the help of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. Emission source-size radius parameters $R_{long}$, $R_{out}$, $R_{side}$ and the chaotic parameter $λ$ are extracted and compared with the experimental data. Transverse momentum and azimuthal angle dependencies of the HBT radii are also discussed for central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV/c. The results show that the HBT radii in central collisions do not change much above 7 GeV/c. For central collisions at 200GeV/c, the radii decrease with the increasing of transverse momentum $p_{T}$ but not sensitive to the azimuthal angle. These results provide a theoretical reference for the energy scan program of the RHIC-STAR experiment.

# Simulation of energy scan of pion interferometry in central Au + Au collisions at relativistic energies

## Key claim
The AMPT model with string melting reproduces the qualitative trends of HBT radii across a wide range of beam energies, but the extracted radii are systematically larger than experimental results, while the chaotic parameter λ and the ratio Rₒ/Rₛ are consistent with data.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model with string melting to simulate central Au+Au collisions at ten beam energies from √sNN = 3 to 200 GeV. Two-pion (π⁺+π⁺) correlation functions were computed from the freeze-out phase-space distributions using the CRAB (CoRrelation After Burner) code, and the three-dimensional HBT radii (Rₒ, Rₛ, Rₗ) and chaotic parameter λ were extracted by fitting the correlation functions to a Gaussian form.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies simulated: √sNN = 3, 5, 7, 11, 17, 27, 39, 62, 130, and 200 GeV
- Centrality selection: 0–10% for all energies; pseudorapidity range |η| < 1
- For central collisions at 200 GeV, the ratio Rₒ/Rₛ from AMPT is approximately 1.0–1.2
- HBT radii show little dependence on beam energy above 7 GeV
- At 200 GeV, HBT radii decrease with increasing transverse momentum pT and show no strong dependence on azimuthal angle φ for 0–10% centrality

## Context
This work extends prior HBT measurements by the RHIC-STAR collaboration at √sNN = 130 GeV (STAR, 2001) and 200 GeV (STAR, 2003; PHENIX, 2003), as well as the STAR energy and system size dependence study (STAR, 2009). The AMPT model results are compared with experimental data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC experiments including NA44, NA49, PHOBOS, CERES, and STAR. The study provides a theoretical reference for the RHIC-STAR Beam Energy Scan program aimed at exploring the QCD phase diagram and the location of the critical point. The results also address the "HBT puzzle" previously discussed in hydrodynamic models (Hydrodynamic model, 2001; 3D hydrodynamic model, 2006) and the UrQMD model (UrQMD, 2009).
