# Nucleus-nucleus potential, energy dissipation and mass dispersion in fusion and transfer reactions

> The nucleus-nucleus potential and energy dissipation in fusion reactions are obtained from microscopic mean-field dynamics. The deduced potentials nicely reproduce the one extracted from experimental data. Energy dissipation shows a universal behaviour between different reactions. Also, the dispersion of mass distribution in transfer reaction is investigated in a stochastic mean-field dynamics. By including initial fluctuations in collective space, the description of the dispersion is much improved compared to that of mean field only. The result is consistent with the macroscopic phenomenological analysis of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The nucleus-nucleus potential extracted from TDHF dynamics shows a dynamical reduction of the barrier height from high-energy to low-energy trajectories, which brings the calculated barrier into agreement with experimental data; the energy dissipation extracted from the same dynamics exhibits a universal behavior nearly independent of system size and asymmetry.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation for head-on collisions using the SLy4d Skyrme effective force in a three-dimensional coordinate space with mesh sizes of 0.8 fm and 0.45 fm/c. From the TDHF density evolution, they extract the relative distance, momentum, and reduced mass, then invert a classical equation of motion with a linear friction term to deduce the nucleus-nucleus potential V(R) and friction coefficient γ(R). For fluctuations, they introduce a stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach where the initial state is represented by an ensemble of single-particle density matrices with random Gaussian fluctuations, each evolved independently by its own mean-field Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Mesh sizes used in TDHF calculations: 0.8 fm in space and 0.45 fm/c in time.
- For the head-on ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca reaction at E_cm = 100 MeV, the diffusion coefficient D_PP is shown as a function of relative distance.
- For the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca reaction, asymptotic σ²_AA values from standard TDHF are 0.004, 0.008, and 0.008 from low to high energies; the number of exchanged nucleons are 0.432, 1.441, and 3.634.
- For the same reaction, asymptotic σ²_AA values from the SMF approach are 0.730, 1.718, and 3.790.

## Context

This work extends the TDHF-based extraction of nucleus-nucleus potentials and friction coefficients from Washiyama (2008, 2009) and compares the friction coefficient to the microscopic model of Adamian (1997). The stochastic mean-field approach addresses a long-standing failure of TDHF to describe mass dispersion in deep inelastic collisions, noted by Davies (1978) and Dasso (1979), and provides a practical alternative to the more difficult Balian-Veneroni variational method (1984, 1985). The SMF results are consistent with the phenomenological nucleon exchange model of Randrup (1982) and Feldmeier (1987), enabling future microscopic calculations of fluctuations in fusion and transfer reactions.
