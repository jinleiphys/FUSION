# The phases of strongly interacting matter in heavy ion collisions

> In ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions the produced high temperature, high energy density state will cross different phases of the strongly interacting matter. The original idea of quark-gluon plasma formation has been evolved and the weakly interacting gaseous state of massless partons has been replaced by the picture of strongly interacting massive constituent quarks. Experimental results at SPS and RHIC supports this idea. We discuss the phases at LHC energies.

# The phases of strongly interacting matter in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is not a weakly interacting gas of massless partons, but rather a strongly interacting Quark-Antiquark Plasma (QAP) of massive constituent quarks and suppressed gluons, with a smooth crossover transition between QAP and QGP expected around T = 400–450 MeV, rather than a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The author analyzes SU(3) lattice QCD results on the equation of state within a thermodynamically consistent quasi-particle picture, where quarks, antiquarks, and gluons carry effective thermal masses and widths generated by many-body interactions. The resulting quasi-particle properties (masses, coupling, densities, speed of sound) are then used to interpret experimental data from CERN SPS and BNL RHIC, including quark coalescence models for soft particle production and high-pT data. The discussion is extended to predict the phase structure expected at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Effective thermal gluon mass at critical temperature: M_g,∞(T_c) ≈ 500 MeV (for N_f = 2)
- Effective thermal quark mass at critical temperature: M_q,∞(T_c) ≈ 330 MeV (for N_f = 2)
- Gluon-to-(quark+antiquark) number ratio around T_c: N_g/(N_q+N_antiq) ≈ 1/3 (for N_f = 2)
- Quark density reduction factor compared to massless case: factor 2.5 (for N_f = 2)
- Gluon density reduction factor compared to massless case: factor 5 (for N_f = 2)
- Speed of sound squared at critical temperature: c_s²(T_c) ≈ 0.15
- Temperature range for smooth QAP-to-QGP transition: T = 400–450 MeV
- Temperature where effective coupling reaches perturbative QCD value: T ∼ 3 T_c

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle description of deconfined matter developed by Levai and Heinz (1998) and Gorenstein and Yang (1995), replacing the earlier expectation of a first-order phase transition with large latent heat. The picture is supported by lattice-QCD results at finite baryon densities from Fodor and Katz (2002). The paper enables future investigation of the high-gluon-density regime at LHC energies, where a classical gluon field and Yang-Mills black body radiation may appear, and suggests that molecular dynamics simulations of heating and viscosity (Hartmann, 2005) can test these properties microscopically.
