# $C$ and $CP$ violation in effective field theories

> The quest for new sources of the simultaneous violation of $C$ and $CP$ symmetry was popular in the 1960s and has since been mostly neglected for more than half a century. In this work we revisit fundamental quark-level operators that break $C$ and $CP$ up to and including mass dimension 8 for flavor-conserving transitions, relying on the complete operator sets of the so-called Standard Model effective field theory and the low-energy effective field theory. With the formalism of chiral perturbation theory, we match these quark operators to light-meson physics, derive $C$- and $CP$-odd Lagrangians for several processes in the $η$, $η'$, and pion sectors, and furthermore, as a proof of principle, give estimates for the respective observables in explicit dependence of the underlying high-energy scale for new physics.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first complete set of quark-level operators that simultaneously violate C and CP symmetry up to mass dimension 8 in the low-energy effective field theory (LEFT), but the authors note that these operators are necessarily of higher dimension than the lowest-dimensional T-odd and P-odd operators that generate electric dipole moments, suggesting the former are further suppressed and more difficult to detect.

## Method

The authors start from the complete operator bases of SMEFT and LEFT up to dimension 8, restrict to flavor-conserving C- and CP-odd operators, and match these quark-level operators to light-meson physics using chiral perturbation theory (chiPT). They derive C- and CP-odd Lagrangians for processes in the eta, eta-prime, and pion sectors, and provide estimates for observables in explicit dependence on the high-energy scale for new physics.

## Key numbers

- The SMEFT expansion parameter is 1/Lambda, with operators of dimension D > 4 suppressed by powers of 1/Lambda^(D-4)
- The hadronic scale Lambda_chi is approximately 1.2 GeV
- Dimension-7 LEFT operators are suppressed by v/Lambda^4 relative to the leading terms
- Dimension-8 LEFT operators are suppressed by 1/Lambda^4 relative to the leading terms
- The LEFT basis up to dimension 6 contains 5963 hermitian operators, dimension 7 contains 5218 hermitian operators, and dimension 8 contains 35058 hermitian operators

## Context

This work revisits C- and CP-violating operators first discussed in the 1990s by Khriplovich (1990), Conti (1992), Engel (1995), Ramsey-Musolf (1999), and Kurylov (2000), who claimed the first such operators appear at dimension 7. The authors correct these earlier analyses by noting that the historic operators are chirality-violating and require an additional Higgs field, making them dimension 8 in SMEFT and dimension 7 in LEFT. The work extends prior EFT approaches used in nucleon EDM analyses by Maekawa (2011), de Vries (2012), and Dekens (2013), and provides the theoretical framework needed to interpret new experimental searches for rare eta decays by the REDTOP and JEF collaborations.
