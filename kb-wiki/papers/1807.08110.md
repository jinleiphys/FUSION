# Extrapolation for meson screening masses from imaginary to real chemical potential

> We first extend our formulation for the calculation of $π$- and $σ$-meson screening masses to the case of finite chemical potential $μ$. We then consider the imaginary-$μ$ approach, which is an extrapolation method from imaginary chemical potential ($μ=i μ_{\rm I}$) to real one ($μ=μ_{\rm R}$). The feasibility of the method is discussed based on the entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model in 2-flavor system. As an example, we investigate how reliable the imaginary-$μ$ approach is for $π$- and $σ$-meson screening masses, comparing "screening masses at $μ_{\rm R}$ in the method" with "those calculated directly at $μ_{\rm R}$". We finally propose the new extrapolation method and confirm its efficiency.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the imaginary-chemical-potential approach (analytic continuation from imaginary to real chemical potential) works reliably for pion and sigma meson screening masses only when the system is not too close to the chiral phase transition line; the method's accuracy degrades for sigma mesons at temperatures where the system crosses the chiral transition boundary as a function of imaginary chemical potential.

## Method

The authors extend their previous formulation for calculating meson screening masses to finite real and imaginary chemical potential using the entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model in a 2-flavor system. They compute screening masses for pion and sigma mesons at imaginary chemical potential, fit the results with a polynomial in theta (mu_I/T), then analytically continue to real chemical potential by replacing theta with -i mu_R/T, and compare these extrapolated results with direct calculations at real chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at vacuum: M_pi = 138 MeV; pion decay constant at vacuum: f_pi = 93.3 MeV (used for parameter fixing)
- Model parameters: m_0 = 6.3 MeV, Lambda = 768 MeV, G_S(0)*Lambda^2 = 2.95, alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.2, T_0 = 200 MeV
- Pseudocritical temperature for chiral transition at zero chemical potential: T_c^chi = 173 +/- 8 MeV (from lattice QCD, used for T_0 determination)
- Deconfinement transition temperature at theta=0: T_c^d(0) = 174 MeV; chiral transition temperature at theta=0: T_c^chi(0) = 177 MeV
- At (theta, T) = (pi/3 +/- 0.084, 187 MeV), the deconfinement transition changes from first order to second order
- The extrapolation method works reliably for mu_R/T less than approximately 0.4 for both pion and sigma screening masses

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for calculating meson screening masses at zero chemical potential (Ishii 2013, Ishii 2015, Ishii 2016) to finite chemical potential, using the EPNJL model which was shown by Sakai (2011) to quantitatively reproduce 2-flavor lattice QCD data. The imaginary-chemical-potential approach was previously applied to lattice QCD by Forcrand and Philipsen, D'Elia and Lombardo, and Nagata and Nakamura. The paper proposes a new "phase-transition-line extrapolation" method that varies temperature along with imaginary chemical potential to follow the chiral transition line, which may improve the accuracy of analytic continuation for sigma meson screening masses near the phase boundary.
