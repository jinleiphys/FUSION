# New low-$Q^2$ measurements of the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ Coulomb quadrupole form factor, pion cloud parametrizations and Siegert's theorem

> The novel measurements of the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ Coulomb quadrupole form factor in the range $Q^2=0.04$--0.13 GeV$^2$ changed the trend of the previous data. With the new data the electric and Coulomb form factors are both in remarkable agreement with estimates of the pion cloud contributions to the quadrupole form factors at low $Q^2$. The pion cloud contributions to the electric and Coulomb form factors can be parametrized by the relations $G_E \propto G_{En}/ \left(1 + \frac{Q^2}{2M_Δ(M_Δ-M)}\right)$ and $G_C \propto G_{En}$, where $G_{En}$ is the neutron electric form factor, and $M$, $M_Δ$ are the nucleon and $Δ$ masses, respectively. Those parametrizations are in full agreement with Siegert's theorem, which states that $G_E= \frac{M_Δ-M}{2M_Δ} G_C$ at the pseudothreshold, when $Q^2=-(M_Δ-M)^2$, and improve previous parametrizations. Also a small valence quark component estimated by a covariant quark model contributes to this agreement. The combination of the new data with the new parametrization for $G_E$ concludes an intense period of studying the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ quadrupole form factors at low $Q^2$, with the agreement between theory and data.

## Key claim

The new low-$Q^2$ measurements of the Coulomb quadrupole form factor $G_C$ in the range $Q^2=0.04$--0.13 GeV$^2$ changed the trend of previous data, and when combined with a new parametrization for the electric form factor $G_E$ that exactly satisfies Siegert's theorem, produce remarkable agreement with both the electric and Coulomb form factor data when small valence quark contributions from a covariant quark model are included.

## Method

The author parametrizes the pion cloud contributions to the $\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)$ quadrupole form factors using relations proportional to the neutron electric form factor $G_{En}$, with $G_E^\pi \propto G_{En}/(1 + Q^2/[2M_\Delta(M_\Delta-M)])$ and $G_C^\pi \propto G_{En}$, derived from large $N_c$ and $SU(6)$ symmetry breaking with two-body exchange currents. These pion cloud parametrizations are combined with valence quark contributions estimated by the covariant spectator quark model, extrapolated from lattice QCD simulations with large pion masses to the physical point. The total form factors are compared with experimental data from Jefferson Lab/Hall A, Mainz, MIT-Bates, and the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- New data for $G_C$ measured at $Q^2 = 0.04$, $0.06$, and $0.13$ GeV$^2$ from Jefferson Lab/Hall A (Blomberg 2016)
- New data for $G_E$ measured at $Q^2 = 0.06$ and $0.13$ GeV$^2$ from Jefferson Lab/Hall A (Blomberg 2016)
- The pseudothreshold occurs at $Q^2_{pt} = -(M_\Delta - M)^2 \simeq -0.1$ GeV$^2$
- Siegert's theorem gives $G_E = \kappa G_C$ at the pseudothreshold, where $\kappa = (M_\Delta - M)/(2M_\Delta)$
- Valence quark contributions are approximately 10% near $Q^2=0$
- The valence quark model uses a 0.72% mixture for both $D$-states in the $\Delta(1232)$ wave function
- The difference $R_{SM} - R_{EM} \simeq 0.05\%$ at $Q^2=0$
- The Galster parametrization for $G_{En}$ uses parameters $a=0.9$ and $d=2.8$
- The MAID2007 parametrization for $G_M$ uses $a_1 = 0.01$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $a_4 = 0.23$ GeV$^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the large $N_c$ and $SU(6)$ quark model approach of Pascalutsa (2007) and Buchmann (2009) by introducing a modified parametrization for $G_E^\pi$ that exactly satisfies Siegert's theorem, improving on the previous parametrization from Siegert-ND which satisfied the theorem only to order $1/N_c^4$. The new Jefferson Lab/Hall A data from Blomberg (2016) resolve a long-standing discrepancy between previous parametrizations and low-$Q^2$ data for $G_C$, and the combined pion cloud plus valence quark description concludes an intense period of studying the $\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)$ quadrupole form factors at low $Q^2$. The results enable future tests via lattice QCD simulations with pion masses near the physical point and chiral effective field theory calculations.
