# Charged kaon condensation in high density quark matter

> We show that at asymptotically high densities the ``color-flavor-locked + neutral kaon condensate'' phase of QCD develops a {\it charged} kaon condensate through the Coleman-Weinberg mechanism. At densities achievable in neutron stars a charged kaon condensate forms only for some (natural) values of the low energy constants describing the low-lying excitations of the ground state.

## Key claim

At asymptotically high densities, the color-flavor-locked + neutral kaon condensate phase of QCD develops a charged kaon condensate through the Coleman-Weinberg mechanism for most natural values of the low energy constants, but at densities achievable in neutron stars a charged kaon condensate forms only for some (natural) values of those constants.

## Method

The author constructed an effective chiral Lagrangian for the low-lying excitations of the CFL phase, including electromagnetic interactions, and computed the one-loop effective potential from photon loops in the Landau gauge. The competition between isospin-breaking quark mass terms, electromagnetic mass terms, and the Coleman-Weinberg radiative corrections was analyzed by minimizing the effective potential as a function of the ratio of charged to neutral kaon condensate amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The range of the gap for K0 condensation is (2 MeV)^2 < Delta^2 < (120 MeV)^2, using perturbative QCD values with m_u = 4 MeV and m_s = 150 MeV.
- The perturbative value of the decay constant is f = (21 - 8 ln 2) / (36 pi^2) and the velocity is v = 1/sqrt(3).
- The perturbative value of the coefficient a is a = 12/pi.
- The dielectric constant is epsilon = 1 + (8/(9 pi)) (tilde(alpha) mu^2 / Delta^2).
- The condition for the quark mass term to be negligible compared to electromagnetic terms is satisfied for mu > 3 GeV.
- At mu = 500 MeV with Delta = 50 MeV and f = 3 f_pert, changing bar(c) from 2.5 to 1.5 transforms the global minimum into a local minimum.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of meson condensation in the CFL phase initiated by Bedaque and Schafer (2001), who demonstrated that neutral kaon condensation occurs in response to quark mass asymmetries. It also builds on the study of the CFL+K0 phase by Shovkovy and others, who found that this phase has the symmetry breaking pattern SU_I(2) x U_Y(1) -> U_Q(1) with two Goldstone bosons. The paper addresses whether charged kaon condensation can occur, which would change quark matter from a perfect insulator to an electrical superconductor, and identifies the need for QCD model calculations to determine the low energy constants at densities relevant for neutron stars.
