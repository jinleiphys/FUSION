# Heavy-Ion Collisions and Black Holes in Anti-de-Sitter Space

> Recent developments linking non-perturbative quantum gauge theories in Minkowski space to classical gravity theories in anti-de-Sitter space are reviewed at a simple level. It is suggested how these spectacular advances may be extended to discuss the quark-gluon phase transition in terms of black holes in anti-de-Sitter space, with possible relevance to heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the quark-gluon phase transition and confinement in QCD may be described by an analogy with black holes in anti-de-Sitter (AdS) space, where the temperature-dependent phases of AdS black holes parallel the confined and deconfined phases of QCD, but the author notes that the AdS supergravity limit does not correspond to the physical QCD limit and that these are early days.

## Method

The author reviews the Maldacena conjecture that a d-dimensional conformal supersymmetric gauge theory may be equivalent to a conformal supergravity or string theory in (d+1)-dimensional AdS space in the large-N_c limit, then discusses Witten's extension to finite temperature. The paper further develops a non-conformal approach using Liouville field theory and world-sheet defects, showing that condensation of world-sheet vortices generates a string tension corresponding to confinement, and that stable AdS black holes correspond to condensation of world-sheet spikes at high temperature. The author then applies a van der Waals equation of state to model the behavior of a non-ideal gas of AdS black holes near the QCD transition temperature.

## Key numbers

- Glueball mass ratio: m_{0-+}/m_{0++} = 1.20, compared with a lattice computation value of 1.36 ± 0.32
- Gluon condensate ratio to lattice: ⟨0|GG|0⟩ / ⟨0|GG|0⟩|_{lattice} ≃ 0.9
- Topological susceptibility ratio to lattice: χ_t / χ_t|_{lattice} ≃ 1.7
- String tension ratio to lattice: μ / μ|_{lattice} ≃ 0.4
- Characteristic parton separation for hadronization probability peak: L ∼ 0.6 to 0.8 fm
- Top-down string calculation of gauge unification scale: m_GUT = few × 10^17 GeV
- Bottom-up estimate of gauge unification scale: m_GUT ∼ 2 × 10^16 GeV

## Context

This review extends the Maldacena conjecture (1997) and Witten's finite-temperature extension (1998) to non-conformal gauge theories relevant to QCD, building on the author's prior work with Geiger on parton-shower Monte Carlo hadronization and with Ghosh and Mavromatos on a van der Waals description of AdS black holes. The paper enables future calculations of non-perturbative QCD quantities such as glueball masses, vacuum properties, and heavy-quark potentials using classical gravity techniques in AdS space, with potential applications to heavy-ion collision phenomenology at RHIC.
