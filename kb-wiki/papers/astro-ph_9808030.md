# Bounds on the existence of neutron rich nuclei in neutron star interiors

> We address the question concerning the maximum density, at which nuclei (and more generally -- nuclear structures) can exist in neutron star interiors. An absolute upper bound to such a density is obtained using the bulk approximation, in which surface and Coulomb effects are neglected. A very good approximation to the limiting density is given by the threshold for the instability of a uniform n-p-e plasma with respect to density modulations; this threshold is calculated using the Extended Thomas-Fermi approximation for the Skyrme energy functionals. For recent SLy Skyrme forces, which are particularly suitable for the description of very neutron rich nucleon systems, the maximum density at which nuclear structures can exist is 0.08 nucleons/fm^3 (i.e., half of the normal nuclear density); at this density protons constitute only 4\% of nucleons.

# Bounds on the existence of neutron rich nuclei in neutron star interiors

## Key claim

The maximum density at which nuclei or nuclear structures can exist in neutron star interiors is about 0.08 nucleons/fm³ (half of normal nuclear density) for the SLy Skyrme forces, with protons constituting only 4% of nucleons at that density. This result is a lower bound derived from the instability threshold of uniform n-p-e plasma, while the bulk approximation provides an absolute upper bound of about 0.09 fm⁻³ for the same forces.

## Method

The authors calculated the maximum density for nuclear structures using two complementary approaches: the bulk approximation (neglecting surface and Coulomb effects) which gives an upper bound, and the instability threshold of homogeneous n-p-e matter with respect to density modulations using the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation for Skyrme energy functionals. The stability analysis involved computing the determinant of the matrix F_ij(q) for small periodic density perturbations, with the critical density ρ(Q) determined where this determinant first becomes non-positive as density decreases. Calculations were performed for four Skyrme forces (SLy4, SLy7, SkM*, and Sk1') with various approximations including Thomas-Fermi, ETF without spin-orbit, and ETF including spin-orbit terms.

## Key numbers

- For SLy4 force: ρ(Q) = 0.0794 fm⁻³ (ETF including spin-orbit), with critical wavenumber Q = 0.281 fm⁻¹ and proton fraction x(Q) = 3.57%
- For SLy7 force: ρ(Q) = 0.0786 fm⁻³ (ETF including spin-orbit), with Q = 0.292 fm⁻¹ and x(Q) = 3.55%
- For SkM* force: ρ(Q) = 0.0754 fm⁻³ (ETF including spin-orbit), with Q = 0.299 fm⁻¹ and x(Q) = 2.79%
- For Sk1' force: ρ(Q) = 0.1005 fm⁻³ (ETF including spin-orbit), with Q = 0.367 fm⁻¹ and x(Q) = 3.52%
- Bulk approximation upper bound ρ_{2↔1} for SLy4: 0.0905 fm⁻³ with proton fraction 3.84%
- Bulk approximation upper bound ρ_{2↔1} for SLy7: 0.0889 fm⁻³ with proton fraction 3.80%
- Characteristic wavelength of critical perturbations: 2π/Q ∼ 17-22 fm, about four times the inter-proton distance
- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star with SLy7 force: crust mass is 1.3% of stellar mass, crust contributes 2.8% to total moment of inertia

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star crust composition using Skyrme forces (Lorenz et al. 1993, Oyamatsu 1993, Pethick and Ravenhall 1995, Sumiyoshi et al. 1995, Cheng et al. 1997) by applying the newly developed SLy forces (Chabanat et al. 1997, 1998) which are specifically calibrated for neutron-rich matter. The results provide a unified description of the entire neutron star, from crust to liquid interior, and enable calculation of crust properties such as mass and moment of inertia. The finding that the maximum density for nuclear structures is about half of normal nuclear density has implications for neutron star cooling, pulsar glitches, and the possibility of continuous gravitational radiation from crustal deformations.
