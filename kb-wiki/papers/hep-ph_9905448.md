# On finite-density QCD at large Nc

> Deryagin, Grigoriev, and Rubakov (DGR) have shown that in finite-density QCD at infinite Nc the Fermi surface is unstable with respect to the formation of chiral waves with wavenumber twice the Fermi momentum, while the BCS instability is suppressed. We show here that at large, but finite Nc, the DGR instability only occurs in a finite window of chemical potentials from above Lambda_QCD to mu_critical = exp(gamma ln^2 Nc + O(ln Nc ln ln Nc))Lambda_QCD, where gamma = 0.02173. Our analysis shows that, at least in the perturbative regime, the instability occurs only at extremely large Nc, Nc > 1000 Nf, where Nf is the number of flavors. We conclude that the DGR instability is not likely to occur in QCD with three colors, where the ground state is expected to be a color superconductor. We speculate on possible structure of the ground state of finite-density QCD with very large Nc.

## Key claim

At large but finite Nc, the Deryagin-Grigoriev-Rubakov (DGR) instability of the Fermi surface toward chiral wave formation occurs only in a finite window of chemical potentials from above Lambda_QCD up to mu_critical approximately equal to exp(gamma ln^2 Nc + O(ln Nc ln ln Nc)) Lambda_QCD, where gamma = 0.02173; the instability requires Nc greater than approximately 1000 Nf, making it unlikely to occur in QCD with three colors.

## Method

The authors developed a renormalization group (RG) approach that reduces the 3+1 dimensional problem to an effective 1+1 dimensional non-Abelian Thirring model, resumming double logarithms from collinear divergences and near-mass-shell fermion propagators. They then extended this RG treatment to finite Nc by incorporating Thomas-Fermi screening and Landau damping of the gluon propagator, which modifies the effective coupling evolution and introduces a screening mass m proportional to g mu. The condition for the DGR instability to survive screening was derived by requiring that the Landau pole scale exceed the infrared cutoff set by the screening mass.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential for the DGR instability is mu_critical ~ exp(gamma ln^2 Nc + O(ln Nc ln ln Nc)) Lambda_QCD, with gamma = 0.02173.
- The DGR instability requires Nc greater than approximately 1000 Nf, where Nf is the number of flavors.
- The Thomas-Fermi screening mass is m = sqrt(Nf/(2 pi^2)) g mu.
- The binding energy of the particle-hole pair in the infinite Nc limit is E_bind ~ mu exp(-pi/h), where h^2 = g^2 Nc/(4 pi^2).
- The perpendicular momentum scale of the pair wave function is Delta_perp ~ mu exp(-pi/(2h)).
- The energy gain from a multi-patch chiral wave state is mu^4 exp(-2pi/h).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Deryagin, Grigoriev, and Rubakov (1992), who first showed the DGR instability at infinite Nc, by computing the finite Nc corrections due to screening. The paper contradicts the naive expectation that the large Nc limit might be relevant for Nc=3 QCD at finite density, finding instead that the DGR instability requires Nc of order thousands. The results enable future investigation of the true ground state of finite-density QCD at very large Nc, which the authors speculate may resemble a tomographic Luttinger liquid as proposed by Anderson for high-Tc cuprates.
