# Quark Mass Dependence of Heavy Quark Diffusion Coefficient from Lattice QCD

> We present the first study of the quark mass dependence of the heavy quark momentum and spatial diffusion coefficients using lattice QCD with light dynamical quarks corresponding to a pion mass of 320 MeV. We find that, for the temperature range 195 MeV $<T<$ 293 MeV, the spatial diffusion coefficients of the charm and bottom quarks are smaller than those obtained in phenomenological models that describe the $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow of open heavy flavor hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the quark mass dependence of the heavy quark spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) for charm and bottom quarks, finding that these coefficients are smaller than those from phenomenological models used to describe open heavy flavor hadron data. The quark mass dependence of \(D_s\) turns out to be quite small over the temperature range studied, contrary to expectations that finite-mass effects might explain the discrepancy between infinite-mass lattice results and phenomenological estimates.

## Method

The authors compute the chromo-magnetic field correlator \(G_B(\tau,T)\) using 2+1 flavor lattice QCD with Highly Improved Staggered Quarks at a pion mass of 320 MeV, on ensembles at five temperatures (195–352 MeV) with multiple lattice spacings for continuum extrapolation. Gradient flow is used to improve signal-to-noise and provide a nonperturbative renormalization scheme, with one-loop perturbative matching to obtain the renormalization-group invariant physical correlator. The magnetic diffusion coefficient \(\kappa_B\) is extracted by fitting the correlator to a spectral function model combining an infrared form \(\rho_B^{\mathrm{ir}}(\omega) = \omega \kappa_B/(2T)\) with leading-order or NLO perturbative QCD ultraviolet parts, using three different interpolation models and varying renormalization scales to estimate systematic uncertainties. The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) is then obtained from \(\kappa = \kappa_E + (2/3)\langle v^2\rangle \kappa_B\) using the previously computed \(\kappa_E\) and a quasi-particle model for the mean-squared thermal velocity \(\langle v^2\rangle\).

## Key numbers

- \(\kappa_B(T=195\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 10.42^{+2.66}_{-3.61}\ T^3\), \(\kappa_B(T=220\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 8.78^{+2.20}_{-3.21}\ T^3\), \(\kappa_B(T=251\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 7.18^{+1.94}_{-2.90}\ T^3\), \(\kappa_B(T=293\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 5.02^{+1.92}_{-2.24}\ T^3\), \(\kappa_B(T=352\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 5.66^{+1.50}_{-2.41}\ T^3\) (all in units of \(T^3\))
- Temperature range studied: 195 MeV \(< T <\) 352 MeV
- Lattice QCD uses 2+1 flavors with pion mass 320 MeV
- The spatial diffusion coefficients \(D_s\) for charm and bottom quarks are smaller than those from the quasi-particle model (QPM) of Sambataro (2023) and the T-matrix approach of Liu (2016) and Tang (2023)
- The quark mass dependence of \(D_s\) is found to be quite small across the entire temperature range

## Context

This work extends the previous lattice QCD calculation of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient for infinitely heavy quarks by Altenkort (2023) to finite-mass charm and bottom quarks, using the same gauge ensembles. The results contradict the expectation that the discrepancy between infinite-mass lattice results and phenomenological estimates from Rapp (2018), Dong (2019), and He (2022) could be resolved by the quark mass dependence of \(D_s\). The calculated \(D_s\) values are smaller than those from the quasi-particle model (QPM) and T-matrix approaches, and also smaller than phenomenological estimates from the ALICE collaboration (2021) that do not include mass dependence. These non-perturbative QCD results provide benchmarks for dynamical models of heavy quark thermalization in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC.
