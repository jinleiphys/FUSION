# Cumulants of conserved charges in hydrodynamic simulations

> We introduce a fast and simple method of computing cumulants of net-proton or net-charge fluctuations in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions. One evaluates the mean numbers of particles in every hydrodynamic event. Cumulants are then expressed as a function of these mean numbers. We implement the corrections due to global conservation laws. The method is tested using ideal hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ AGeV with the NeXSPheRIO code. Results are in good agreement with experimental data on net-proton and net-charge fluctuations by the STAR collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a fast method for computing cumulants of conserved charges in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations, but the method relies on the assumption that fluctuations originate mostly from the initial state and neglects hydrodynamic fluctuations; the agreement with experimental data is achieved only after rescaling the calculated mean particle numbers to match measured values.

## Method

The authors compute cumulants of net-proton and net-charge fluctuations by first evaluating the mean numbers of particles in each hydrodynamic event from ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the NeXSPheRIO code. They then express cumulants as functions of these mean numbers, implementing corrections due to global conservation laws by assuming that particle numbers follow a binomial distribution rather than a Poisson distribution. The method involves a two-step averaging process: first over freeze-out configurations for fixed initial conditions, then over initial conditions themselves.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 AGeV simulated with 10^4 events in the range 67 ≤ N_part ≤ 394 (50% most central collisions)
- Freeze-out temperature ranges from 128 MeV to 142 MeV depending on centrality
- Acceptance fraction for protons: α_+ = 0.042; for antiprotons: α_- = 0.056 (rapidity window |y| < 0.5, transverse momentum 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c)
- Acceptance fraction for net charge: α ≈ 0.09, varying from 0.0975 to 0.0858 across centrality windows (pseudorapidity |η| < 0.5, p_T 0.2-2.0 GeV/c)
- Global conservation law correction reduces cumulants: ~5% for C_2, up to 30% for C_4
- Monte Carlo sampling repeated 2500 times per hydrodynamic event to achieve desired accuracy on mean particle numbers

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to model fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions using hydrodynamics, building on the NeXSPheRIO code which was instrumental in describing event-by-event flow fluctuations at RHIC energies (Andrade 2006, Takahashi 2009, Gardim 2012). The method enables computation of cumulants with orders of magnitude fewer events than actual experiments or transport calculations (Hammelmann 2022, Xu 2016), and can be generalized to other quantities such as net-kaon number, off-diagonal cumulants (STAR 2019), and higher-order cumulants (STAR 2021). The authors note that future work should include viscous hydrodynamics and an equation of state with an adjustable critical point position (Parotto 2018, Karthein 2021).
