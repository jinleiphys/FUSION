# Fission life-time calculation using a complex absorbing potential

> A comparison between the semi-classical approximation and the full quantum calculation with a complex absorbing potential is made with a model of the fission of 258Fm. The potential barrier is obtained with the constrained Skyrme HF+BCS theory. The life-time obtained by the two calculations agree with each other the difference being only by 25%.

## Key claim

The semi-classical approximation for fission lifetime agrees with a full quantum calculation using a complex absorbing potential to within 25% for a model of 258Fm, but the comparison relies on a schematic constant mass tuned to reproduce the experimental half-life.

## Method

The authors first computed a one-dimensional potential energy surface for symmetric fission of 258Fm using constrained Skyrme HF+BCS theory with the Skm* interaction and volume pairing, using the EV8 code. They then solved the time-dependent Schrodinger equation with a complex absorbing potential by expanding the Hamiltonian on a bi-orthogonal basis, extracting the resonance width from the complex eigenvalue of the state with maximum overlap with the initial bound state. The collective mass was treated as a constant, adjusted to reproduce the experimental spontaneous fission half-life of 258Fm (0.37 ms) within the semi-classical WKB approximation.

## Key numbers

- Experimental spontaneous fission half-life of 258Fm: 0.37 ms (from Holden 2000)
- Adjusted constant collective mass: 480 MeV (10^-22 s)^2 fm^-2
- Full quantum calculation with complex absorbing potential yields half-life: 0.290 ms
- Difference between semi-classical (0.37 ms) and full quantum (0.290 ms) results: 25%
- Imaginary potential parameters: W0 = -0.1 MeV fm^-2, onset at Ra = 10.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the complex absorbing potential method for fission lifetime calculations (Scamps 2015), which had only been tested on a schematic barrier, to a more realistic potential from self-consistent mean-field theory. The result validates the semi-classical WKB approximation (used in prior work by Brack 1972, Sadhukhan 2013, and others) against a full quantum treatment for this system, while noting that the constant-mass assumption and harmonic approximation for the confining potential may contribute to the 25% discrepancy. The method enables future applications using microscopically calculated collective masses instead of the schematic constant mass employed here.
