# Correlations between light and heavy flavors near the chiral crossover

> Thermal fluctuations and correlations between the light and heavy-light mesons are explored within a chiral effective theory implementing heavy quark symmetry. We show, that various heavy-light flavor correlations indicate a remnant of the chiral criticality in a narrow range of temperature where the chiral susceptibility exhibits a peak structure. The onset of the chiral crossover, in the heavy-light flavor correlations, is therefore independent from the light flavors. This indicates that the fluctuations carried by strange charmed mesons can also be used to identify the chiral crossover, which is dominated by the non-strange light quark dynamics.

## Key claim

Various heavy-light flavor correlations (including those involving strange charmed mesons) exhibit a remnant of chiral criticality in a narrow temperature range near the chiral crossover, and this onset is independent of the light flavors, meaning that fluctuations carried by strange charmed mesons can also identify the chiral crossover which is dominated by non-strange light quark dynamics.

## Method

The authors formulate a chiral effective theory that implements both chiral symmetry and heavy quark symmetry, using a linear sigma model with three light flavors coupled to heavy-light meson fields (the H and G spin multiplets). They employ a mean-field approximation at finite temperature and vanishing chemical potential, with temperature-dependent couplings in the heavy-light sector extracted from lattice QCD chiral condensates to maintain thermodynamic consistency. The complete thermodynamic potential includes contributions from light mesons, heavy-light mesons, and quarks, and the authors compute the full 4x4 susceptibility matrix in the basis of the four mean fields (sigma_q, sigma_s, D_q, D_s).

## Key numbers

* The chiral crossover temperature is set to T_pc = 154 MeV, matching lattice QCD results.
* The sigma meson mass is fixed to m_sigma = 400 MeV to achieve this crossover temperature.
* The strange condensate sigma_s remains substantially larger than sigma_q at T_pc and melts gradually toward higher temperature, yielding a broad bump in chi_ss around T ~ 1.35 T_pc.
* The chi_sigma_s D_q correlation changes sign at T/T_pc ~ 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the effective theory formulated by Sasaki (2014) that implements chiral and heavy quark symmetry at finite temperature, and builds on lattice QCD results showing that charmed mesons deconfine together with light-flavor mesons near the chiral crossover (Lattice Charm Collaboration, 2014). The results contrast with pure light-flavored systems where observables with strangeness (like kaon effective masses) are insensitive to the chiral crossover onset. The paper enables future exploration of flavor fluctuations at finite density and quantification of chiral modifications of heavy-light hadrons in nuclear matter, building on prior QCD sum rule studies (e.g., Hilger, 2009; Buchheim, 2011) and effective theory approaches (e.g., Suhonen, 2012; Nagoya, 2013).
