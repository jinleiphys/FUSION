# Renormalization Group Approach towards the QCD Phase Diagram

> The idea of the functional renormalization group and one-loop improved renormalization group flows are reviewed. The associated flow equations and nonperturbative approximations schemes for its solutions are discussed. These techniques are then applied to the strong interaction in the framework of an effective quark meson model which is introduced in great detail. The renormalization group analysis of the two flavor quark meson model is extended to finite temperature and quark chemical potential which allows for an analysis of the chiral phase diagram beyond the mean field approximation.

## Key claim

The functional renormalization group (FRG) approach, applied to a two-flavor quark-meson model, predicts the chiral phase diagram of QCD beyond mean-field approximation, but the precise location of the tricritical point and the critical temperature depend on model parameters and are non-universal quantities.

## Method

The authors derive and solve proper-time renormalization group (PTRG) flow equations for the scale-dependent grand canonical potential of a two-flavor quark-meson model at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. The flow equations are obtained by inserting a half-integer regulator function (n=3/2) into the standard PTRG flow, which allows analytic evaluation of Matsubara sums. The effective potential is discretized on a regular grid in the chiral field variable φ² and integrated numerically from the compositeness scale k_φ down to the infrared, using a symmetric potential as initial condition at the chiral symmetry breaking scale k_χ.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale k_χ is typically around 500 MeV, below the compositeness scale k_φ which is below 1 GeV.
- The confinement scale Λ_QCD is approximately 200 MeV.
- The constituent quark mass M_q in the vacuum is of the order of 300 MeV.
- For two massless quark flavors at zero chemical potential, a second-order phase transition belonging to the O(4) universality class is found; the critical temperature is a non-universal quantity depending on model parameters.
- For finite chemical potential, the O(4)-universal second-order transition persists up to a tricritical point, which belongs to a trivial Gaussian fixed point with mean-field critical exponents; the curvature of the second-order transition line T_c(μ) has a negative slope.

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group analysis of the two-flavor quark-meson model, previously applied by Schaefer and collaborators (Schaefer 1999, 2005, 2006a), to finite temperature and chemical potential, enabling a nonperturbative determination of the chiral phase diagram beyond mean-field approximations. The proper-time RG approach used here is an approximation to the exact ERG flow of Wetterich (1993) and is related to earlier work by Floreanini (1995) and Liao (1996). The results enable future studies of the influence of additional flow terms that would make the PTRG flow exact, as well as more precise determinations of the tricritical point location in the QCD phase diagram.
