# Semi-inclusive electroproduction of hidden-charm and double-charm hadronic molecules

> The semi-inclusive electroproduction of exotic hadrons, including the $T_{cc}$, $P_{cs}$, and hidden-charm baryon-antibaryon states, is explored under the assumption that they are $S$-wave hadronic molecules of a pair of charmed hadrons. We employ the Monte Carlo event generator Pythia to produce the hadron pairs and then bind them together to form hadronic molecules. With the use of such a production mechanism, the semi-inclusive electroproduction rates are estimated at the order-of-magnitude level. Our results indicate that a larger number of $P_{cs}$ states and $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ molecules can be produced at the proposed electron-ion colliders in China (EicC) and in the US (EIC). The results also suggest that the $T_{cc}$ states and other hidden-charm baryon-antibaryon states can be searched for at EIC. Besides, the potential 24-GeV upgrade of the Continuous Beam Accelerator Facility at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility can play an important role in the search for the hidden-charm tetraquark and pentaquark states due to its high luminosity.

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive electroproduction rates of exotic hadrons (Tcc, Pcs, and hidden-charm baryon-antibaryon states) are estimated at the order-of-magnitude level under the assumption that these states are S-wave hadronic molecules of a pair of charmed hadrons, using a Monte Carlo event generator to produce the hadron pairs which then bind through final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors employed the Monte Carlo event generator Pythia to simulate the short-distance production of charmed hadron pairs in ep collisions, then used a factorized amplitude approach where the hadron pairs bind together through final-state interactions to form hadronic molecules. The effective coupling constants for the molecular states were extracted from the T matrix of low-energy scattering by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a contact interaction kernel, using a Gaussian regulator with cutoff Lambda in the range 0.5-1.0 GeV. For the 24-GeV CEBAF upgrade, cross sections were calculated at higher electron energies (60, 80, 100, and 150 GeV) and then extrapolated down to 24 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Tcc+ cross section at EicC: 0.3 x 10^-3 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 1.2 x 10^-3 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- Tcc+ cross section at EIC: 0.1 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 0.5 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- Pcs (Xi_c Dbar, J=1/2) cross section at EicC: 0.1 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 1.6 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- Pcs (Xi_c Dbar, J=1/2) cross section at EIC: 1.8 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 30 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- Lambda_c anti-Lambda_c molecule cross section at EicC: 0.3 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 3.0 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- Lambda_c anti-Lambda_c molecule cross section at EIC: 9.6 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 110 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- X(3872) cross section at CEBAF (24 GeV): 1.3 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 5.5 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- Zc(3900)0 cross section at CEBAF (24 GeV): 22.9 pb (Lambda=0.5 GeV) to 82.4 pb (Lambda=1.0 GeV)
- Estimated Tcc+ events: 18-72 at EicC and 3 x 10^4 - 1.5 x 10^5 at EIC with integrated luminosities of 60 fb^-1 and 300 fb^-1 respectively
- Estimated Lambda_c anti-Lambda_c molecule events: 2 x 10^4 - 2 x 10^5 at EicC and 3 x 10^6 - 3 x 10^7 at EIC

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular production mechanism previously applied to X(3872) and Pc states (Yang 2021) to new systems including double-charm Tcc states, Pcs states, and hidden-charm baryon-antibaryon molecules. The approach differs from earlier vector-meson-dominance model calculations for exclusive photoproduction (Huang 2013, Wang 2015, Karliner 2015) by using open-charm hadron pair production as the primary mechanism, motivated by the finding of Du (2020) that near-threshold J/psi photoproduction is dominated by Lambda_c Dbar(*) channels. The results provide production rate estimates for future experiments at EicC, EIC, and the proposed 24-GeV CEBAF upgrade, enabling searches for these exotic hadronic molecules.
