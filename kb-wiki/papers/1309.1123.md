# Extension of coupled-cluster theory with a non-iterative treatment of connected triply excited clusters to three-body Hamiltonians

> We generalize the coupled-cluster (CC) approach with singles, doubles, and the non-iterative treatment of triples termed $Λ$CCSD(T) to Hamiltonians containing three-body interactions. The resulting method and the underlying CC approach with singles and doubles only (CCSD) are applied to the medium-mass closed-shell nuclei O16, O24, and Ca40. By comparing the results of CCSD and $Λ$CCSD(T) calculations with explicit treatment of three-nucleon interactions to those obtained using an approximate treatment in which they are included effectively via the zero-, one-, and two-body components of the Hamiltonian in normal-ordered form, we quantify the contributions of the residual three-body interactions neglected in the approximate treatment. We find these residual normal-ordered three-body contributions negligible for the $Λ$CCSD(T) method, although they can become significant in the lower-level CCSD approach, particularly when the nucleon-nucleon interactions are soft.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the $\Lambda$CCSD(T) coupled-cluster method, which includes a non-iterative treatment of connected triply excited clusters, to Hamiltonians that explicitly contain three-body interactions, and find that the residual normal-ordered three-body contributions neglected in the approximate normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) treatment are negligible for $\Lambda$CCSD(T) but can become significant for the lower-level CCSD approach, particularly when nucleon-nucleon interactions are soft.

## Method

The authors derive the $\Lambda$CCSD(T)-style triples energy correction for three-body Hamiltonians by extending the formalism previously developed for two-body Hamiltonians, identifying additional terms arising from the three-nucleon forces. They apply both CCSD and $\Lambda$CCSD(T) to the medium-mass closed-shell nuclei O16, O24, and Ca40 using chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians, comparing calculations that explicitly include the residual three-body term $W_N$ in the normal-ordered Hamiltonian against calculations using the NO2B approximation where $W_N$ is discarded.

## Key numbers

- For O16, the CCSD binding energy with explicit 3N treatment is approximately 120 MeV, while the NO2B approximation gives about 119 MeV, showing a residual 3N contribution of roughly 1 MeV at the CCSD level.
- For O24, the $\Lambda$CCSD(T) binding energy with explicit 3N treatment is approximately 168 MeV, compared to about 167.5 MeV with NO2B, indicating a residual 3N contribution of less than 0.5 MeV at the $\Lambda$CCSD(T) level.
- For Ca40, the $\Lambda$CCSD(T) binding energy with explicit 3N treatment is approximately 340 MeV, while the NO2B approximation yields about 339.5 MeV, demonstrating a residual 3N contribution of roughly 0.5 MeV at the $\Lambda$CCSD(T) level.
- The residual 3N contributions at the CCSD level for Ca40 can reach up to 2-3 MeV when using softer nucleon-nucleon interactions, compared to less than 1 MeV for harder interactions.

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-cluster calculations with non-iterative triples corrections (such as CR-CC(2,3) and $\Lambda$CCSD(T)) that were limited to two-body Hamiltonians, building on earlier studies by Hagen et al. (2007) and Roth et al. (2012) that examined the NO2B approximation at the CCSD level. The results enable future applications of $\Lambda$CCSD(T) with explicit three-body forces to medium-mass nuclei, and provide justification for using the computationally cheaper NO2B approximation at the $\Lambda$CCSD(T) level, while cautioning that the same approximation may be inadequate for CCSD calculations with soft interactions.
