# Effects of Compression and Collective Expansion on Particle Emission from Central Heavy-Ion Reactions

> Conditions under which compression occurs and collective expansion develops in energetic reactions of heavy nuclei, are analyzed, together with their effects on emitted light baryons and pions. Within transport simulations, it is shown that shock fronts perpendicular to beam axis form in head-on reactions. The fronts separate hot compressed matter from normal. As impact parameter increases, the angle of inclination of the fronts relative to beam axis decreases, and in-between the fronts a weak tangential discontinuity develops. Hot matter exposed to the vacuum in directions perpendicular to shock motion (and parallel to fronts), starts to expand sideways, early within reactions. Expansion in the direction of shock motion follows after the shocks propagate through nuclei, but due to the delay does not acquire same strength. Expansion affects angular distributions, mean-energy components, shapes of spectra and mean energies of different particles emitted into any one direction, and further particle yields. Both the expansion and a collective motion associated with the weak discontinuity, affect the magnitude of sideward flow within reaction plane. Differences in mean particle energy components in and out of the reaction plane in semicentral collisions, depend sensitively on the relative magnitude of shock speed in normal matter and speed of sound in hot matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in central heavy-ion reactions, collective expansion develops anisotropically, stronger in directions perpendicular to the beam axis than along it, due to the earlier onset of sideways expansion while shock fronts still propagate along the beam axis; this anisotropy explains observed differences in mean energy components, angular distributions, and particle yields, with the missing energy previously associated with low pion multiplicity identified as collective expansion energy.

## Method

Transport simulations were performed for symmetric heavy-ion reactions (primarily Au+Au) using a model with nucleon, deuteron, A=3 cluster, pion, delta, and N* degrees of freedom, solving Boltzmann-type transport equations with test particles on a lattice. The single-particle energies included an optical potential with scalar and isovector terms, using both soft (K ≈ 200 MeV) and stiff (K ≈ 375 MeV) equations of state. Shock front formation, expansion dynamics, and freeze-out properties were analyzed from the simulated phase-space distributions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon density in head-on Au+Au at 400 MeV/nucleon reaches approximately 2.1 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.160 fm⁻³), falling short of the Rankine-Hugoniot expectation of 2.5 ρ₀
- Transverse velocity slope dv/dr in 400 MeV/nucleon Au+Au reaches a maximum of about 0.040 c/fm at t ≈ 27 fm/c
- Freeze-out density in head-on Au+Au at 400 MeV/nucleon is about 0.2 ρ₀
- Collective transverse energy per nucleon freezes out at approximately 42 MeV in 400 MeV/nucleon Au+Au, constituting about 50% of the kinetic energy in transverse degrees of freedom at freeze-out
- Speed of sound in the central region of 400 MeV/nucleon Au+Au is assessed at cₛ ∼ 0.4 c (with γ = 5/3)
- For incident ³⁶Ar at 65 ± 15 MeV and ⁹⁴Kr at 95 ± 25 MeV on Ag, collective expansion energies of ∼3 MeV/nucleon and ≥7 MeV/nucleon respectively were inferred from fragment mean energies
- For Au+Au at 150 MeV/nucleon, collective expansion energy of ∼18 MeV/nucleon was found in preliminary analysis, later bounded from below at ∼10 MeV/nucleon; at 400 MeV/nucleon, ∼51 MeV/nucleon was found

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier transport model study (Danielewicz, 1992) of central collisions and light-fragment production, providing a more detailed analysis of compression and expansion dynamics. It addresses experimental findings from Schussler et al. (1991, 1993) on fragment mean energies in emulsion reactions and from the FOPI collaboration (Reisdorf, 1993; Jeong, 1994) on Au+Au collisions at GSI, where collective expansion was inferred from fragment spectra. The paper explains the midrapidity out-of-reaction-plane peaking observed by the Plastic Ball collaboration (Gutbrod, 1989) as a continuation of the 90° polar-angle peaking in head-on reactions, and identifies the missing energy from low pion multiplicity (Stock, 1982) with collective expansion energy. The analysis enables quantitative extraction of collective expansion energy from experimental data and establishes relations between beam energy, impact parameter, and the density and entropy in compressed regions.
