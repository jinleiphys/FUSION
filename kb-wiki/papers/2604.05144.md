# Species-dependent viscous corrections at particlization: A novel relaxation time approximation approach

> We assess the effects of a recently proposed generalized relaxation time approximation (RTA) for multi-species relativistic gases within a realistic numerical hybrid framework and study its phenomenological consequences in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions. The novel approximation introduces counter-terms to the collision kernel, allowing for momentum-dependent relaxation times $τ_i(p)$ while preserving local energy-momentum conservation. As a consequence, the resulting first-order viscous corrections $δf_i$ to the phase-space distribution functions depend explicitly on the particle species mass $m_i$. We systematically investigate the impact of these species-dependent corrections on particle production at particlization, focusing on identified hadron yields and transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra obtained from Cooper-Frye sampling. We find that the yields and spectra of light hadrons ($π, K, p$) are significantly affected, leading to modifications of relative particle yields such as the $K/π$ and $p/π$ ratios. We show that these effects persist, albeit with reduced magnitude, after the inclusion of the hadronic cascade stage. In contrast, the impact on inclusive charged-particle observables is strongly reduced due to compensating enhancements and suppressions among different species. This controlled deformation of identified hadron observables, which selectively modifies flavor-sensitive quantities, makes the new prescription particularly well suited for Bayesian inference, as it introduces new sensitivity directions without spoiling existing constraints. Overall, our results demonstrate that species-dependent viscous corrections arising from the generalized RTA can leave significant and observable imprints on identified hadron production and relative yields, while remaining fully consistent with the successful description of bulk collective flow observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a generalized relaxation time approximation (nRTA) with momentum-dependent relaxation times produces species-dependent viscous corrections to phase-space distribution functions that significantly modify identified hadron yields and ratios (K/π, p/π) in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions, while leaving bulk collective flow observables largely unchanged, but only if the hadronic cascade stage is included, which reduces the magnitude of these effects.

## Method

The authors implemented the nRTA viscous correction formulas from Rocha et al. (2025) into the iSS particlization module within a hybrid simulation chain (TRENTo initial conditions, free-streaming, MUSIC hydrodynamics, iSS Cooper-Frye sampling, UrQMD hadronic cascade). They varied the phenomenological parameter γ (controlling the power-law energy dependence of relaxation times τ_i(p) ∝ (E/T)^γ) across values 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5, using existing Bayesian-calibrated parameter sets from JETSCAPE (for Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV) and the Duke group (for p-Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV) without refitting.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the relative viscous correction δN_i/n_eq,i for pions at T = 0.15 GeV increases with γ (from negative values at γ=0 toward less negative at γ=1.5), while for protons the correction is positive and decreases with γ.
- At T = 0.15 GeV, the relative correction δN_i/n_eq,i shows non-monotonic behavior with γ: increases for pions (m_i = 0.138 GeV) and Ω baryons (m_i = 1.672 GeV), but decreases for kaons (m_i = 0.494 GeV) and protons (m_i = 0.938 GeV).
- The total relative viscous correction δN_tot/n_eq is typically negative across temperatures analyzed (0.10–0.17 GeV), with magnitude smaller than individual species corrections; at T ≈ 0.10 GeV it decreases with γ, while at higher temperatures it increases with γ.
- The bulk viscous correction φ_p,i|_bulk for pions at T = 0.15 GeV shows curves crossing near |p| ∼ 0.15 GeV for different γ values.

## Context

This work extends the generalized RTA formalism developed by Rocha et al. (2021, 2025) for multi-species relativistic gases, providing the first full numerical implementation in a realistic hybrid model. It contrasts with the standard Anderson-Witting RTA (γ=0), which forces constant relaxation times and violates conservation laws when momentum dependence is introduced. The results enable future Bayesian inference studies that can constrain the γ parameter using identified hadron observables, while remaining consistent with existing bulk flow constraints from JETSCAPE (2020) and Duke group (2018) calibrations.
