# Remarks on possible local parity violation in heavy ion collisions

> In this note we discuss some observations concerning the possible local parity violation in heavy ion collisions recently announced by the STAR Collaboration. Our results can be summarized as follows (i) the measured correlations for same charge pairs are mainly in-plain and not out of plane, (ii) if there is a parity violating component it is large and, surprisingly, of the same magnitude as the background, and (iii) the observed dependence of the signal on the transverse momentum (p_t) is consistent with a soft boost in p_t and thus in line with expectations from the proposed chiral magnetic effect.

# Remarks on possible local parity violation in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed same-charge correlations are primarily in-plane rather than out-of-plane, contrary to the expectation from the chiral magnetic effect; if a parity-violating component exists, it must be large and of the same magnitude as the background, which the authors suggest may be a coincidence or an indication that the interpretation as local parity violation needs revision.

## Method

The authors decompose the STAR correlation functions into in-plane and out-of-plane components using the relations between the measured correlators ⟨cos(ϕα+ϕβ)⟩ and ⟨cos(ϕα−ϕβ)⟩. They analyze the integrated signal to extract the parity-violating component P and the background terms Bin and Bout, and they model the transverse momentum dependence of the correlated pair distributions using a thermal distribution with temperature T = 0.22 GeV to compare with the STAR data.

## Key numbers

- For same-sign pairs: ⟨sin(ϕα)sin(ϕβ)⟩_same ≃ 0 and ⟨cos(ϕα)cos(ϕβ)⟩_same < 0, indicating correlations are in-plane
- For opposite-sign pairs: ⟨sin(ϕα)sin(ϕβ)⟩_opposite ≃ ⟨cos(ϕα)cos(ϕβ)⟩_opposite > 0
- The parity-violating component satisfies P ≃ −B_out ≃ −B_in, meaning it has the same magnitude as the background
- The momentum shift required by the data is δp+ ≃ 150 MeV
- The thermal distribution uses T = 0.22 GeV, corresponding to average pion transverse momentum ⟨pt⟩ = 0.45 GeV
- The correlated pair distributions are concentrated in the low pt region, pt < 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the STAR Collaboration (2009) measurement of the correlator proposed by Voloshin (2004) for detecting local parity violation via the chiral magnetic effect, originally proposed by Kharzeev et al. (2008). The authors find that the same-charge correlations are in-plane rather than out-of-plane, contradicting the chiral magnetic effect expectation, and note that a cluster model by Wang (2009) may explain the observations. The paper calls for differential measurements of ⟨cos(ϕα−ϕβ)⟩ in transverse momentum and pseudorapidity to distinguish trivial correlations from a possible parity-violating signal.
