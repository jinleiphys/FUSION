# Thermal Conductivity Coefficient from Microscopic Models

> Thermal conductivity of hadron matter is studied using a microscopic transport model, which will be used to simulate ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at different energy densities, namely the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD). The molecular dynamics simulation is performed for a system of light mesons species (pion, rho, kaon) in a box with periodic boundary conditions. The equilibrium state is investigated by studying chemical equilibrium and thermal equilibrium of the system. Particle multiplicity equilibrates with time, and the energy spectra of different light mesons species have the same slopes and common temperatures when thermal equilibrium is reached. Thermal conductivity transport coefficient is calculated from the heat current - current correlations using the Green-Kubo relations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the thermal conductivity transport coefficient of a hadron gas composed of light mesons (pion, rho, kaon) can be calculated using the UrQMD microscopic transport model combined with Green-Kubo relations, but the results are limited to the hadronic temperature range of 100 MeV to 160 MeV, as above 160 MeV the system begins to behave like a quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of a hadron gas containing pions, rhos, and kaons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions using the UrQMD 3.3 model. The system was initialized with fixed energy densities ranging from 0.1 to 0.5 GeV/fm³, zero baryon number density, and a volume of 1000 fm³, running 200 events per energy density. Chemical and thermal equilibrium were verified by monitoring particle number densities and energy spectra, then the thermal conductivity coefficient was extracted from heat current-current correlations using the Green-Kubo formalism.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium time for all meson species at energy density ε = 0.3 GeV/fm³ was t = 22 fm/c
- Equilibrium time for all meson species at energy density ε = 0.4 GeV/fm³ was t = 32 fm/c
- Thermal temperature extracted from Boltzmann distribution at ε = 0.2 GeV/fm³ was T = 118.3 MeV
- Thermal temperature extracted from Boltzmann distribution at ε = 0.3 GeV/fm³ was T = 150.1 MeV
- Temperature dependence of thermal conductivity followed κ ∼ T²·⁸⁸
- Temperature dependence of the product κT followed κT ∼ T³·⁸⁸
- Saturation of thermal conductivity occurred below T = 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of transport coefficients in hadronic matter, which has been well documented for shear and bulk viscosity (Muronga, 2004; Gorenstein, 2008), but poorly documented for thermal conductivity, especially using the UrQMD model. The results are compared with those from Muroya (2007) who used the URASIMA model with baryon number density, and with Torres (2012) who used Effective Field Theory. The study enables future investigations of how thermal conductivity is affected by adding baryon number density and different meson species, and provides input for dissipative fluid dynamical models of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
