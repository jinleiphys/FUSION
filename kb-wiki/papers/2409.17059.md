# Finite- and infinite-volume study of $DDπ$ scattering

> We develop a comprehensive framework for extracting the pole position and properties of the doubly-charmed tetraquark $T_{\rm cc}^+(3875)$ from lattice QCD data using the relativistic three-particle formalism. This approach incorporates the effect of the one-pion exchange diagram in $DDπ$ and $DD^*$ scattering, making it applicable at energies coinciding with the left-hand cut in the partial-wave projected $DD^*$ amplitude. We present an example application of this framework to existing lattice QCD data at $m_π= 280$ MeV. We solve the integral equations describing the $DDπ$ reaction, use LSZ reduction to determine the corresponding $DD^*$ amplitude, and find the values of the infinite-volume two- and three-body $K$ matrices that lead to agreement with lattice $DD^*$ phase shifts within their uncertainties. Using these $K$ matrices in the three-particle quantization condition, we describe the finite-volume $DD^*$ spectrum and find good agreement with the lattice QCD energies. Our results suggest that, at this pion mass, the tetraquark appears as a pair of subthreshold complex poles whose precise location strongly depends on the value of the $DDπ$ three-particle $K$ matrix.

## Key claim
The paper develops a comprehensive relativistic three-particle formalism for extracting the doubly-charmed tetraquark T_cc^+(3875) pole position from lattice QCD data, incorporating one-pion exchange effects that become critical when the pion mass is heavy enough that the D* meson becomes a D-pi bound state. The authors find that at m_pi = 280 MeV, the tetraquark appears as a pair of subthreshold complex poles whose precise location strongly depends on the value of the DD-pi three-particle K matrix, rather than as a single resonance pole.

## Method
The authors solve relativistic three-body integral equations for DD-pi scattering in the J^P=1+ and J^P=0- channels, using partial-wave projection with non-zero partial waves and non-degenerate masses. They model two-body subchannel interactions (D-pi and D-D) using effective-range expansions tuned to reproduce the D* as a bound state, then apply LSZ reduction to extract the DD* amplitude from the DD-pi solution. The same K matrices are used in the three-particle quantization condition to compute finite-volume energy levels, which are compared to existing lattice QCD data from Padmanath (2022) and Collins (2024).

## Key numbers
- Pion mass used in the example application: m_pi = 280 MeV (from existing lattice QCD data)
- The D* meson mass is determined by the pole condition: m_D* = sqrt(m_pi^2 + q_0^2) + sqrt(m_D^2 + q_0^2), where q_0 is the purely imaginary relative momentum at the bound-state pole
- The two-body phase space factor: rho^{(n)}(sigma_p) = eta_n q_p^* / (8 pi sqrt(sigma_p))
- The two-body K-matrix parametrization uses scattering lengths a_s^{(n)} and effective ranges r_s^{(n)} in the form: [K_{2,s}^{(n)}(sigma_p)]^{-1} = eta_n/(8 pi sqrt(sigma_p) (q_p^*)^{2s}) * (-1/a_s^{(n)} + 1/2 r_s^{(n)} (q_p^*)^2)
- The cutoff function H_i(p) uses sigma_min^{(1)} = (m_D^2 - m_pi^2) and sigma_min^{(2)} = 4(m_D^2 - m_pi^2) as the left-hand cut positions from two-pion exchange

## Context
This work extends the relativistic field theory (RFT) three-particle formalism of Hansen and Sharpe (2014-2015) to the specific case of the T_cc^+ tetraquark, building on the theoretical framework established by Hansen (2024) for handling left-hand cuts from one-pion exchange. It addresses the known failure of two-body approaches like the Luscher formalism and naive effective-range expansions near the left-hand cut, as noted by Du (2023, 2024), and provides a method applicable at any pion mass. The results enable future lattice QCD analyses to reliably extract the tetraquark pole position from finite-volume spectra, connecting results from lattice ensembles with both stable and unstable D* mesons.
