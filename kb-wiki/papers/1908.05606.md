# Coupled Self-Consistent RPA Equations for Even and Odd Particle Numbers. Tests with Solvable Models

> Coupled equations for even and odd particle number correlation functions are set up via the equation of motion method. For the even particle number case this leads to self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) equations already known from the literature. From the equations of the odd particle number case the single particle occupation probabilities are obtained in a self-consistent way. This is the essential new procedure of this work. Both, even and odd particle number cases are based on the same correlated vacuum and, thus, are coupled equations. Applications to the Lipkin model and the 1D Hubbard model give very good results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for obtaining single-particle occupation probabilities in self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) by coupling even and odd particle number equations of motion, where the odd particle number case provides the occupation numbers self-consistently from the same correlated vacuum used for the even particle number SCRPA equations. The authors note that the even particle number equation (SCRPA) is the only point where the approach is not entirely consistent, as the destruction operator does not exactly kill the Coupled-Cluster-Doubles ground state without introducing a generalization, though it kills it to very good approximation.

## Method

The authors set up coupled equations of motion for even and odd particle number correlation functions, where the even particle number case yields standard SCRPA equations and the odd particle number case yields a matrix eigenvalue equation for single-particle occupation probabilities. The even and odd systems are coupled through nonlinear equations that contain both the RPA amplitudes (X, Y) and the single-particle occupation numbers (n_k), requiring iterative solution. The method is tested on two exactly solvable models: the Lipkin model and the 1D Hubbard model (a 6-site ring at half filling with periodic boundary conditions).

## Key numbers

- For the Lipkin model with N=4 particles, the occupation number difference ⟨J₀⟩ and correlation energy are reproduced exactly by the eo-SCRPA approach across coupling strengths up to and slightly beyond the critical value χ_crit = 1
- For the Lipkin model with N=10, the eo-SCRPA results for ⟨J₀⟩ show improvement over the Catara method (labeled "SCRPA" in figures) when compared to the exact solution
- For the Lipkin model first excited state with N=20, the eo-SCRPA approach shows improvement over the Catara method, with the authors hypothesizing the approach becomes exact in the N → ∞ limit
- For the 6-site Hubbard model at half filling, occupation numbers for states with momenta k₆ = -π, k₅ = -2π/3, and k₄ = 2π/3 are shown as functions of U/t from U/t = 0 to U/t = 10
- The ground state energy for the 6-site Hubbard model is shown as a function of U/t from U/t = 0 to U/t = 10

## Context

This work extends the Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) framework previously developed by the authors and collaborators (Jemai 2013, Dukelsky et al. 2005, Schäfer and Schuck), which itself is a sub-product of Time-Dependent Density Matrix (TDDM) theory based on decoupling the BBGKY hierarchy (Schuck and Tohyama). The new method replaces the earlier "Catara method" (Rowe 1968, Catara et al.) for obtaining single-particle occupation probabilities, which the authors note worked well in most cases but failed in particular instances (Jemai 2013). The odd particle number RPA (o-RPA) used here was recently proposed by Tohyama and Schuck (2013) and can be reformulated as a Dyson equation for the single-particle Green's function. The coupled even-odd SCRPA (eo-SCRPA) enables calculation of excitation energies for both even and odd particle number systems, ground state energies via the single-particle Green's function, and transition probabilities.
