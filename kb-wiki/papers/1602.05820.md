# $η$ MAID-2015: update with new data and new resonances

> Recent data for $η$ and $η\prime$ photoproduction on protons obtained by the A2 Collaboration at MAMI are presented. The total cross section for $η$ photoproduction demonstrates a cusp at the energy corresponding to the $η\prime$ threshold. The new data and existing data from GRAAL, CBELSA/TAPS, and CLAS collaborations have been analyzed by an expansion in terms of associated Legendre polynomials. %A Legendre decomposition shows the sensitivity to small partial-wave contributions. The isobar model $η$MAID updated with $η\prime$ channel and new resonances have been used to fit the new data. The new solution $η$MAID-2015 reasonably good describes the data in the photon beam energy region up to 3.7 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents an updated isobar model, ηMAID-2015, that describes η and η′ photoproduction data up to 3.7 GeV, but the model fails to reproduce the magnitude of the beam asymmetry Σ for η′ photoproduction near threshold, suggesting that below-threshold resonances may need to be included using a more realistic approach.

## Method

The authors performed a truncated Legendre polynomial expansion of differential cross sections and polarization observables (T, F, Σ) for γp → ηp and γp → η′p reactions, using data from A2MAMI, CBELSA/TAPS, CLAS, and GRAAL collaborations. They then updated the ηMAID-2003 isobar model by adding 12 new nucleon resonances, updating vector meson couplings, and fitting Breit-Wigner parameters (mass, total width, branching ratios, and helicity amplitudes) to the expanded data set. The fits for the η and η′ channels were performed independently.

## Key numbers

- The N(1895)1/2− resonance parameters were determined as: M = 1896 ± 1 MeV, Γtot = 93 ± 13 MeV, Γηp = (14 ± 3)%, Γη′p = (6.5 ± 2)%, and A1/2 = (−17.4 ± 1.5) × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/².
- The total cross section for η photoproduction shows a cusp at W ∼ 1900 MeV, corresponding to the η′ threshold.
- The model describes data up to a photon beam energy of 3.7 GeV.
- Non-zero A7 and A8 Legendre coefficients point to g-wave contributions above W = 2 GeV for both η and η′ channels.
- Polarization data for η photoproduction were measured below W = 1.9 GeV, showing pd interferences below W = 1.6 GeV and df interferences above W = 1.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier ηMAID-2003 model (developed in 2002) which included 8 resonances and was fitted to data up to 1400 MeV but failed to reproduce new polarization data from Mainz. The updated model incorporates 12 additional resonances and uses the BnGa solution as initial parameter sets, following the approach of the Bonn-Gatchina group. The new solution enables improved description of polarization observables and explains the cusp structure in η photoproduction as a threshold effect from the η′ channel, with future improvements expected from upcoming polarization data from A2MAMI, CBELSA/TAPS, and CLAS collaborations.
