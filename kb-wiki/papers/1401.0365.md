# Progress on Light-Ion Fusion Reactions with Three-Nucleon Forces

> The description of structural and dynamical properties of nuclei starting from the fundamental interaction between nucleons has been a long-standing goal in nuclear physics. The ab initio No-Core Shell Model combined with the Resonating-Group Method (NCSM/RGM) is capable of addressing both structural and reaction properties of light-nuclei. While promising results have already been achieved starting from a two-body Hamiltonian, a truly realistic prediction of nuclear observables requires the treatment of the three-nucleon interaction. Using similarity-renormalization-group evolved two- and three-nucleon interactions, we will present recent applications to n-4He scattering process when accounting for the chiral two- plus three-nucleon interaction versus the chiral two-nucleon interaction. We compare our results to phase shifts obtained from R-matrix analysis of data up to 16 MeV neutron energy, below the d-3H threshold.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a chiral three-nucleon (3N) force, evolved via the Similarity-Renormalization Group (SRG), improves the description of n-4He elastic scattering phase shifts compared to using only a two-nucleon (NN) interaction, but the 2P3/2 resonance energy near 0.78 MeV is still not reproduced correctly, possibly due to missing coupling to the d-3H channel or an insufficient spin-orbit structure of the 3N force.

## Method

The authors applied the ab initio No-Core Shell Model combined with the Resonating-Group Method (NCSM/RGM) to compute n-4He elastic scattering phase shifts and differential cross sections. They used a chiral N3LO NN interaction and a chiral N2LO 3N interaction, both evolved with the SRG method to soften short-range repulsion, and compared results from the NN-only Hamiltonian to those from the NN+3N-full Hamiltonian (including both induced and SRG-evolved chiral 3N forces). The calculations used Nmax=13 harmonic oscillator shells and included the first six excited states of 4He to account for target polarization.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts were computed for incident neutron energies up to 16 MeV, below the d-3H threshold.
- The experimental 2P3/2 resonance energy is 0.78 MeV; both NN-only and NN+3N-full calculations miss this resonance position.
- Calculations used Nmax=13 harmonic oscillator shells, SRG flow parameter lambda=2.0 fm^-1, and HO frequency hbar-Omega=20 MeV.
- Differential cross sections were compared to data at incident neutron energy of 17.6 MeV (from Drosg 1978).
- The first six excited states of 4He were included, corresponding to 24 MeV of excitation energy; the d-3H channel opens experimentally at 17.63 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NCSM/RGM studies of nucleon-4He scattering that used only NN interactions (Quaglioni 2008, Quaglioni 2009) and addresses the need for three-nucleon forces in realistic nuclear reaction predictions, as highlighted by Nollett (2007). The results demonstrate that chiral 3N forces improve the spin-orbit splitting between P-wave phase shifts, but the remaining discrepancies motivate future work using the No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) approach (Baroni 2013) to treat cluster correlations and short-range A-body correlations on the same footing.
