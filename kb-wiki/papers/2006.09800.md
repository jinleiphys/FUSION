# On a quasi-bound state in the $K^- d$ system caused by strong interactions

> It was found that $NN$ potential could influence the results of the quasi-bound state search in the $K^- d$ system, where the corresponding pole is situated close to the threshold. Three-body Faddeev-type calculations of the $\bar{K}NN - πΣN$ system performed with a new model of nucleon-nucleon interaction predict the existence of the quasi-bound $K^- d$ state caused by strong interactions. Its binding energy is small ($1-2$ MeV), while the width is comparable with the width of the $K^- pp$ quasi-bound state ($40-60$ MeV).

## Key claim

Three-body Faddeev-type calculations with a new nucleon-nucleon potential predict a quasi-bound state in the \(K^- d\) system caused purely by strong interactions, but only when the antikaon-nucleon potential has a two-pole structure of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance; the state does not appear with the one-pole phenomenological potential in the full coupled-channel calculation.

## Method

The author solved Faddeev-type three-body AGS equations in momentum representation for the spin-one \(\bar{K}NN\) system with coupled \(\bar{K}NN\) and \(\pi \Sigma N\) channels. Two methods were used: direct search for the pole in the complex energy plane, and the \(1/|{\rm Det}|^2\) method where the pole appears as a bump fitted by a Breit-Wigner formula. The input consisted of separable potentials for \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\), \(NN\), \(\Sigma N\), and \(\pi N\) interactions; the new two-term separable nucleon-nucleon potential (TSN) was fitted to Argonne \(V18\) phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the \(K^- d\) quasi-bound state from coupled-channel calculation with the two-pole phenomenological potential \(V_{\bar{K}N}^{\rm 2,SIDD}\): \(B_{K^- d} = 0.9\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_{K^- d} = 59.4\) MeV.
* Binding energy of the \(K^- d\) quasi-bound state from coupled-channel calculation with the chirally motivated potential \(V_{\bar{K}N}^{\rm Chiral}\): \(B_{K^- d} = 1.3\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_{K^- d} = 41.8\) MeV.
* No quasi-bound state was found in the coupled-channel calculation with the one-pole phenomenological potential \(V_{\bar{K}N}^{\rm 1,SIDD}\).
* Triplet scattering length of the new TSN potential: \(a_{np}^{\rm TSN} = -5.400\) fm; effective range: \(r_{{\rm eff}, np}^{\rm TSN} = 1.744\) fm.
* Singlet scattering length of the new TSN potential: \(a_{pp}^{\rm TSN} = 16.325\) fm; effective range: \(r_{{\rm eff}, pp}^{\rm TSN} = 2.792\) fm.
* Deuteron binding energy from the TSN potential: \(E_{\rm deu} = 2.2246\) MeV.
* For comparison, the \(K^- pp\) quasi-bound state binding energy with the same TSN potential and the chirally motivated model: \(B_{K^- pp} = 29.43\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_{K^- pp} = 46.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of the \(\bar{K}NN\) system (Shevchenko, 2015) where no strong-interaction quasi-bound state was found in \(K^- d\) using the earlier TSA nucleon-nucleon potential. The new TSN potential, fitted to Argonne \(V18\) phase shifts, shifts the pole from slightly above the \(K^- d\) threshold to below it, enabling the state to appear. The result enables future four-body \(\bar{K}NNN\) calculations and distinguishes the strong-interaction quasi-bound state (binding energy \(1-2\) MeV, width tens of MeV) from the atomic kaonic deuterium state (binding energy \(\sim 10\) keV, width \(\sim 1\) keV) studied by Revai (2010).
