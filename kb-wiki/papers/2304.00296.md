# Center-of-mass momentum dependence of short-range correlations with the coarse-grained Granada potential

> The effect of the center of mass motion on the high-momentum distributions of correlated nucleon pairs is studied by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation in nuclear matter with the Granada nucleon-nucleon potential. We show that this coarse-grained potential reduces the problem to an algebraic linear system of five (ten) equations for uncoupled (coupled) partial waves that can be easily solved. The corresponding relative wave functions of correlated pn, pp and nn pairs are computed for different values of their CM momentum. We find that the pn pairs dominate the high-momentum tail of the relative momentum distribution, and that this only depends marginally on center of mass momentum. Our results provide further justification and agreement for the factorization approximation commonly used in the literature. This approximation assumes that the momentum distribution of nucleon pairs can be factorized as the product of the center of mass momentum distribution and the relative momentum distribution.

## Key claim

The authors show that the high-momentum tail of the relative momentum distribution of correlated nucleon pairs depends only marginally on the center-of-mass momentum of the pair, providing further justification for the factorization approximation that separates the momentum distribution into a product of center-of-mass and relative momentum distributions. This result holds for the coarse-grained Granada potential when solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the angular average approximation for the Pauli-blocking operator.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation in nuclear matter using the coarse-grained Granada nucleon-nucleon potential, which reduces the problem to an algebraic linear system of five equations for uncoupled partial waves and ten equations for coupled partial waves. They compute the correlated relative wave functions for proton-neutron (pn), proton-proton (pp), and neutron-neutron (nn) pairs at different values of the center-of-mass momentum, using the angular average of the Pauli-blocking operator. The radial wave functions are obtained by solving integral equations with delta-shell potentials, and the momentum-space wave functions are derived via Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

- The study covers center-of-mass momenta from 0 up to 2 k_F, where k_F is the Fermi momentum
- For K_CM = 0, the angle-averaged Pauli-blocking function is exactly a step function θ(k' - k_F)
- For K_CM = 2 k_F (the maximum allowed CM momentum for two nucleons below the Fermi momentum), the angle-averaged Pauli-blocking function in region (b) is exactly a straight line in k'/k_F with slope 1/2
- The coarse-grained Granada potential uses N_δ = 5 delta-shells for each channel

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ruiz Simo, 2016; Ruiz Simo, 2017) on short-range correlations in the independent pair approximation from the case of zero center-of-mass momentum to non-zero total momentum of the nucleon pair. The angular average approximation for the Pauli-blocking operator follows the approach first proposed by Brueckner (1958) and used by many others including Haftel (1970), Jeukenne (1974), and Muther (1995). The results support the factorization approximation commonly used in the literature for nuclear momentum distributions, which is relevant for calculations of electron-nucleus scattering reactions and nuclear matrix elements.
