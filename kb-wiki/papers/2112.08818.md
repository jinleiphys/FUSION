# Deep Inelastic Scattering in the Dipole Picture at Next-to-Leading Order

> This thesis studies gluon saturation in hadronic matter at high energy by calculating next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to inclusive and diffractive deep inelastic scattering cross sections in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory. We demonstrate that the large soft gluon logarithm is correctly factorized into the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) renormalization group equation by accurately connecting the NLO scattering kinematics to the rapidity scale of the dipole amplitude in the scattering. This brings the perturbative expansion under control and enables us to do precision comparisons between theory and data. We fit the initial condition of the BK evolution equation to HERA inclusive deep inelastic scattering data by combining the NLO accuracy inclusive cross sections with beyond leading order BK evolution prescriptions. This results in the state-of-the-art accuracy comparison between CGC theory and HERA data, and determination of the dipole amplitude initial shape which is a necessary input for all NLO CGC phenomenology. In this introductory part, the effect of the NLO BK equation on the fits is assessed, and an alternative form for the NLO loop correction to the inclusive cross sections is derived which enables the consistent setting of the dipole amplitude rapidity scale in the NLO corrections. Diffraction is studied in this thesis in the CGC formalism, and we calculate the tree-level $q \bar q g$ NLO contribution to the diffractive deep inelastic scattering structure functions where the $q \bar q g$ Fock state scatters off the target and becomes the diffractively produced system. This contribution has previously been known in the literature only in leading $\log(Q^2)$ accuracy valid at large $Q^2$, and only for the structure function $F^D_T$. The $q \bar q g$ contribution to both structure functions $F^D_T$ and $F^D_L$ are presented in full NLO accuracy.

## Key claim

The thesis demonstrates that the large soft gluon logarithm in next-to-leading order (NLO) deep inelastic scattering cross sections is correctly factorized into the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) renormalization group equation by accurately connecting the NLO scattering kinematics to the rapidity scale of the dipole amplitude, but this factorization relies on a consistent setting of the dipole amplitude rapidity scale in the NLO corrections, which is achieved through an alternative form for the NLO loop correction derived in the introductory part.

## Method

The author calculates NLO corrections to inclusive and diffractive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) cross sections within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory using light-front perturbation theory, where the virtual photon state is expanded as a Fock state with light-front wavefunctions. For inclusive DIS, the NLO accuracy cross sections are combined with beyond-leading-order BK evolution prescriptions, and the initial condition of the BK evolution equation is fitted to HERA inclusive DIS data. For diffractive DIS, the tree-level \(q \bar q g\) NLO contribution to the diffractive structure functions \(F^D_T\) and \(F^D_L\) is calculated in full NLO accuracy, where the \(q \bar q g\) Fock state scatters off the target and becomes the diffractively produced system.

## Key numbers

- The quark spin contribution to the proton spin is roughly 30%, with current estimates for quark and gluon contributions at approximately 30-40% and 26-52%, respectively, falling short of producing the proton spin of 1/2.
- The proton mass arises from four contributions: the quark condensate (approximately 9%), the quark energy (approximately 32%), the gluonic field strength (approximately 37%), and the anomalous gluonic contribution (approximately 23%).
- There is a more than 7 sigma disagreement on the charge radius of the proton, originating from a 4% difference between muonic hydrogen spectroscopy and older measurements.
- Approximately 1% of the proton mass comes from the rest masses of the three valence quarks, with the remaining 99% arising from dynamics of quarks and gluons.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory framework for describing gluon saturation in hadronic matter at high energy, building on prior calculations by Beuf (2016, 2017) for NLO inclusive DIS structure functions and by previous authors who had calculated the \(q \bar q g\) contribution to diffractive DIS only in leading \(\log(Q^2)\) accuracy valid at large \(Q^2\) and only for the structure function \(F^D_T\). The results enable precision comparisons between CGC theory and HERA data, and provide the dipole amplitude initial shape necessary for all NLO CGC phenomenology, which will be essential for interpreting upcoming measurements from the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).
