# Late-time attractors in relativistic spin hydrodynamics in Gubser flow

> We investigate the late-time asymptotic solutions and attractor structure of the spin density in minimal causal spin hydrodynamics in Gubser flow. After deriving the differential equation governing the spin density, we obtain its late-time asymptotic solutions and identify both attractors and repellers in the corresponding numerical solutions. We then map these solutions back to flat Minkowski space and find parameter regions where the spin density exhibits a power-law decay. We further show that, when the characteristic length scale of the system is much larger than the proper time, several components of the spin density can decay as slowly as conventional thermodynamic variables in relativistic hydrodynamics. In this regime, the spin density behaves as a hydrodynamic mode governed by the late-time scaling laws of the flow.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in minimal causal spin hydrodynamics under Gubser flow, the spin density can exhibit attractor behavior and decay as slowly as conventional thermodynamic variables at late times, specifically when the characteristic length scale of the system is much larger than the proper time. This contradicts the common expectation that spin density relaxes more rapidly than other macroscopic hydrodynamic fields.

## Method
The authors derive the differential equation governing the spin density component $\hat{S}^{\varphi\eta}$ in minimal causal spin hydrodynamics within the conformally invariant Gubser flow framework, using a Weyl transformation to map from Minkowski spacetime to $dS_3\times\mathbb{R}$ spacetime. They parameterize the transport coefficients $\hat{\tau}_{\phi}^{-1}$ and $8\hat{\gamma}_{s}\hat{\tau}_{\phi}^{-1}\hat{\chi}^{-1}$ as power laws in the variable $w$ (related to proper time) and analyze the late-time asymptotic solutions by introducing an auxiliary function $f(w)$. Numerical solutions are then obtained to identify attractor and repeller branches, and the results are mapped back to flat Minkowski space.

## Key numbers
- The spin density component $\hat{S}^{\varphi\eta}$ (denoted $\hat{S}$) satisfies a second-order differential equation with coefficients involving $\tanh\rho$ and the Weyl-rescaled relaxation time $\hat{\tau}_{\phi} = \tau_{\phi}/\tau$ and transport coefficient $\hat{\gamma}_{s} = \tau^{3}\gamma_{s}$.
- The transport coefficients are parameterized as $\hat{\tau}_{\phi}^{-1} = \alpha w^{\Delta_{1}}$ and $8\hat{\gamma}_{s}\hat{\tau}_{\phi}^{-1}\hat{\chi}^{-1} = \beta w^{\Delta_{2}}$, where $\alpha$, $\beta$, and $\Delta_{1,2}$ are constants.
- The variable $w$ is defined as $w = 1/(1-\tanh\rho)$, and at fixed $\cos\theta=1$, the relation $\tau/L = \sqrt{2w-1}$ holds, so large $w$ corresponds to large proper time $\tau$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of late-time attractors in minimal causal spin hydrodynamics within Bjorken flow (Wang et al., 2024) to the more realistic Gubser flow, which incorporates transverse expansion while preserving boost invariance. The paper addresses the long-standing issue that spin density in canonical spin hydrodynamics (Hattori, 2019; Fukushima, 2020) is expected to relax rapidly, but the results suggest that under certain conditions the spin density can persist to late proper times and potentially influence observables on the freeze-out hypersurface. This enables future studies of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions using more realistic hydrodynamic frameworks.
