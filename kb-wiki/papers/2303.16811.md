# Dynamically screened strongly quantized electron transport in binary neutron-star merger

> We examine electron-transport coefficients in magnetized hot and dense electron-ion plasma relevant in binary neutron star merger simulation. We calculate electrical and thermal conductivities in low density, high temperature, highly magnetized plasma of binary neutron star mergers where quantum oscillatory behavior of electrons emerge. For pronounced thermodynamic effects, we consider zeroth Landau level population of electrons for the calculation of conductivity. We solve Boltzmann equation in presence of magnetic field to obtain the dissipative components of electrical and thermal conductivities. The dissipative coefficients are formulated considering frequency dependent dynamical screening in the quantized electron-ion scattering rate. Numerical estimations show that the effect of dynamical screening of photon propagator on electrical and thermal conductivities is pronounced. We observe that dynamical screening reduces the maxima of both the electrical and thermal conductivities by factors of thirty one and twenty respectively leading to a reduction in the corresponding time scales of these coefficients. The common scaling factor between electrical and thermal conductivity is also observed to follow cubic relationship with temperature violating Wiedemann-Franz law.

## Key claim

Dynamical screening via the Hard-Dense-Loop (HDL) photon propagator reduces the maxima of electrical and thermal conductivities in strongly quantized electron-ion plasma by factors of thirty-one and twenty, respectively, compared to static screening approximations, leading to shorter dissipative time scales in binary neutron star merger simulations.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in a strong magnetic field for electrons confined to the zeroth Landau level, incorporating electron-ion scattering rates computed with HDL-resummed transverse and longitudinal photon propagators. They derived the longitudinal components of the electrical and thermal conductivity tensors for a relativistic, degenerate, magnetized electron-ion plasma, using the magnetic Debye mass appropriate for the strongly quantized regime. Numerical integrations were performed for temperatures around 12 MeV, densities near 10^12 g cm^-3, and magnetic fields up to 10^16 G, considering Fe and Mo as representative ionic species.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical screening reduces the maximum electrical conductivity by a factor of **31** compared to static screening.
- Dynamical screening reduces the maximum thermal conductivity by a factor of **20** compared to static screening.
- The magnetic field considered is up to **10^16 G**.
- The density considered is up to **10^12 g cm^-3**.
- The temperature considered is up to **15 MeV**.
- The critical magnetic field B_c is **4.413 × 10^13 G**.
- The condition for zeroth Landau level population includes T_ce ≈ **1.343 × 10^8 B_12 K** and ρ_B = **7.045 × 10^3 (A/Z) (B_12)^(3/2) g cm^-3**.
- The Wiedemann-Franz law is violated; the ratio κ/σ follows a cubic relationship with temperature rather than a linear one.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of electrical conductivity in warm neutron star crusts by Harutyunyan et al. (2018) and Harutyunyan et al. (2016), which used static screening and did not consider Landau quantization, into the strongly quantized regime relevant for binary neutron star mergers. It also builds on the non-magnetic transport calculations of Alford et al. (2017) by incorporating magnetic field effects and dynamical screening. The results enable more realistic magnetothermal evolution simulations of post-merger objects and provide inputs for general relativistic magnetohydrodynamics codes that currently assume ideal MHD, as the reduced conductivities imply shorter Ohmic decay and thermal equilibration timescales that may compete with the merger timescale.
