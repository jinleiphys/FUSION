# Effects of perturbation for transition operator of double-$β$ decay on nuclear matrix element, effective axial-vector current coupling, and half-life

> We calculate the nuclear matrix element (NME), effective axial-vector current coupling $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$, and half-life of the double-$β$ ($ββ$) decay using the transition operator perturbed by the nuclear interaction. The correction terms for the NME are obtained by extending the hadron sector to a higher order in terms of the Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory. The NME calculations are performed for the neutrinoless $ββ$ ($0νββ$) and the two-neutrino $ββ$ ($2νββ$) decays of $^{136}$Xe. The nuclear wave functions are calculated by the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with the Skyrme, the Coulomb, and the contact pairing interactions. Sufficiently large single-particle valence spaces are used. The correction terms for the NME are comparable with the leading term in absolute value, and the sum of the corrections has the opposite sign to that of the leading term. The $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$'s for the $ββ$ NME are calculated by a few methods depending on the truncation of the NME and the half-life referred to. Similarities are found between some of these $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$'s including those of the $0νββ$ NME. This leads to the conclusion that the value of $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$ can indeed be determined by the perturbed transition operator. It is in a comparable range of the $g_A$ for the $2νββ$ NME. The perturbation effect on the $2νββ$ half-life is discussed by comparing the calculated half-lives with the different $g_A$'s and the NME components.

## Key claim

The perturbation of the double-beta decay transition operator by the nuclear interaction yields correction terms for the nuclear matrix element (NME) that are comparable in absolute value to the leading term but opposite in sign, and the effective axial-vector current coupling \(g_A^\mathrm{eff}\) determined from the perturbed operator falls in a range comparable to that of the two-neutrino double-beta decay NME, though the authors restrict their vertex correction calculations to the simplest terms (those arising from the four-creation part of the residual interaction) and neglect potentially divergent ground-state correlation terms.

## Method

The authors extended the hadron sector of the double-beta decay transition operator to higher order using Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory, deriving first-order correction terms for the compact NME density. They calculated the NME for both neutrinoless and two-neutrino double-beta decays of \(^{136}\)Xe using nuclear wave functions from the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with Skyrme, Coulomb, and contact pairing interactions in sufficiently large single-particle valence spaces. The vertex correction was restricted to the simplest terms using the four-creation part of the residual interaction (\(V_{4\mathrm{c}}\)) with the initial and final states treated as Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov ground states.

## Key numbers

- The effective axial-vector current coupling \(g_A^\mathrm{eff}\) to reproduce the \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay half-life of \(^{136}\)Xe was found to be 0.42 in the authors' previous study, much smaller than the bare value \(g_A = 1.267\) for an isolated nucleon.
- The nuclear root mean square radius used in the NME definition is \(R = 1.2A^{1/3}\) fm.
- The tensor term of the NME is around 10% of the Gamow-Teller NME.
- The candidate nuclei for neutrinoless double-beta decay are distributed in the range of mass number \(A = 50\) to 150.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QRPA study of \(^{136}\)Xe (Terasaki 2019), which concluded that the QRPA is a good approximation for this nucleus but found a strongly quenched \(g_A^\mathrm{eff}\) of 0.42. The perturbation approach addresses the necessity to incorporate changes of nucleon states during the decay due to the nucleon-nucleon interaction, effects that cannot be captured by perturbing only the initial and final states. The study builds on earlier investigations of beta-decay NME perturbation from the 1970s-80s (Arita 1987, Towner 1987) and more recent chiral effective-field theory approaches (Cirigliano 2019, Gysbers 2019, Belley 2021, Coraggio 2024, Castellano 2024), and it enables the determination of \(g_A^\mathrm{eff}\) for the neutrinoless double-beta decay, which has been unknown due to the virtual neutrino momentum being quite different from that in other weak decays.
