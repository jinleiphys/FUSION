# Quark matter under strong electric fields in the Linear Sigma Model coupled with quarks

> In this work we study the influence of external electric field and temperature on the chiral phase transition of Quantum Chromodynamics. We use the two-flavor Linear Sigma Model coupled with quarks (LSMq) in a thermal and electrized medium to evaluate the effective quark mass and the Schwinger pair production. To this end, we apply one-loop correction to the fermionic sector of the model and the simple tree-level approximation in the mesonic contributions. The electric fields strengthen the partial restoration of the chiral symmetry when applied with finite temperature in a crossover transition. The expected decrease of the pseudocritical temperature as a function of the electric field is observed until electric fields reach $eE\approx 13.5 m_π^2$. For stronger electric fields, the effect is the opposite, which is in a very good agreement with previous results obtained with four-point non-renormalizable models, showing that this effect is independent of renormalizability issues. We also show the thermal and electric effects on the behavior of the Schwinger pair production.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the non-monotonic behavior of the pseudocritical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration as a function of electric field, decreasing for weak fields and increasing for strong fields, is independent of whether the effective model is renormalizable or not, contradicting the possibility that this effect was a regularization artifact of non-renormalizable models.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Linear Sigma Model coupled with quarks (LSMq) in Euclidean space, applying a one-loop correction to the fermionic sector while treating the mesonic sector at tree level. They incorporate a constant external electric field and finite temperature using the proper-time formalism and imaginary-time formalism, respectively, and solve the gap equation for the effective quark mass as a function of temperature and electric field strength.

## Key numbers

- The pseudocritical temperature decreases with increasing electric field up to \(eE \approx 13.5 m_\pi^2\); for stronger fields the trend reverses and the pseudocritical temperature increases.
- Model parameters are fixed to vacuum values: pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, sigma meson mass \(m_\sigma = 800\) MeV, and effective quark mass \(M = 300\) MeV.
- The resulting coupling constants are \(g = 3.2258\), \(m^2 = -290600\) MeV\(^2\), \(\lambda = 215.19\), and the renormalization scale \(\Lambda = 181.96\) MeV.
- Schwinger pair production becomes non-negligible around \(eE = 5 m_\pi^2\) and saturates above \(eE = 18 m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry restoration under electric fields performed with the non-renormalizable Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models by Tavares et al. (2018, 2019), which first reported the non-monotonic pseudocritical temperature behavior. The present LSMq results confirm that this effect is not due to regularization issues inherent to non-renormalizable models, and similar behavior was also found in \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory by Loewe et al. (2021). The work enables future inclusion of baryon density effects in the presence of thermal and background electric fields.
