# Empirical Parameterization of Nucleon-Nucleon Elastic Scattering Amplitude at High Beam Momenta for Glauber Calculations and Monte Carlo Simulations

> A parameterization of the nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering amplitude is needed for future experiments with nucleon and nuclear beams in the beam momentum range of 2 -- 50 GeV/c/nucleon. There are many parameterizations of the amplitude at $P_{lab} >$ 25--50 GeV/c, and at $P_{lab} \leq$ 5 GeV/c. Our paper is aimed to cover the range between 5 -- 50 GeV/c. The amplitude is used in Glauber calculations of various cross sections and Monte Carlo simulations of nucleon-nucleon scatterings. Usually, the differential nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering cross sections are described by an exponential expression. Corresponding experimental data on $pp$ interactions at $|t|>$ 0.005 (GeV/c)$^2$ and $|t|\leq$ 0.125 (GeV/c)$^2$ have been fit. We propose formulae to approximate the beam momentum dependence of these parameters in the momentum range considered. The same was done for $np$ interactions at $|t|\leq$ 0.5 (GeV/c)$^2$. Expressions for the momentum dependence of the total and elastic cross sections, and the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the amplitude at zero momentum transfer are also given for $pp$ and $np$ collisions. These results are sufficient for a first approximation of the Glauber calculations. For more exact calculations we fit the data at $|t|>$ 0.005 (GeV/c)$^2$ without restrictions on the maximum value of $|t|$ using an expression based on two coherent exponential. The parameters of the fits are found for the beam momentum range 2 -- 50 GeV/c.

## Key claim
The paper provides empirical parameterizations of the nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering amplitude specifically for the beam momentum range 2–50 GeV/c, filling the gap between existing low-momentum (≤5 GeV/c) and high-momentum (≥25–50 GeV/c) parameterizations. The authors note that the simple exponential form is sufficient for a first approximation, but for more exact calculations a two-exponential expression is required, and they caution that the standard assumption ρ_np = ρ_pp is not correct based on their analysis.

## Method
The authors fit experimental pp differential elastic scattering cross section data at |t| > 0.005 (GeV/c)^2 and |t| ≤ 0.125 (GeV/c)^2 using a standard exponential parameterization, and separately fit np data at |t| ≤ 0.5 (GeV/c)^2. For more exact calculations, they fit pp data at |t| > 0.005 (GeV/c)^2 without an upper |t| restriction using a two-coherent-exponential expression, and for np data they introduce a modified parameterization with an additional constant term to describe the plateau region. They also derive momentum-dependent formulae for total cross sections, elastic cross sections, and the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the amplitude at zero momentum transfer for both pp and np collisions.

## Context
This work extends parameterizations of the nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering amplitude into the intermediate momentum range 2–50 GeV/c, which was previously covered only by partial wave analyses below 5 GeV/c (Ray, 1980) and by high-energy parameterizations above 25–50 GeV/c (Particle Data Group, 2015; Okorokov, 2015). The authors build on the two-exponential approach originally proposed by Kalbfleisch (1972) for antiproton-proton data and independently by Phillips and Barger (1973) for pp data, and apply it to a wider momentum range including EDDA collaboration data (2000) and COSY data (2015). The results enable improved Glauber calculations for studies of exotic nuclei structure, neutron skin measurements, and Monte Carlo simulations of nucleon-nucleon scatterings for future experiments with nucleon and nuclear beams.
