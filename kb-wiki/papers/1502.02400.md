# Direct numerical solution of the coordinate space Balitsky-Kovchegov equation at next to leading order

> We present the first numerical solution to the next to leading order Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation in coordinate space in the large-$N_\mathrm{c}$ limit. In addition to the dipole operator we also solve the evolution of the "conformal dipole" for which the conformal invariance breaking double logarithmic term is absent from the evolution equation. The NLO corrections are shown to slow down the evolution. We show that the solution depends strongly on the details of the initial condition, and that the solution to the equation is not positive definite with all initial conditions relevant for phenomenological applications.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first numerical solution to the next-to-leading order (NLO) Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation in coordinate space, showing that NLO corrections slow down the evolution but that the solution is not positive definite for all initial conditions relevant to phenomenology, particularly those with large anomalous dimension or small initial saturation scale.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the NLO BK evolution equation derived by Balitsky (2008) in the large-Nc limit, using a logarithmic grid in dipole size r and a Runge-Kutta method with adaptive Monte Carlo integration for the four-dimensional NLO integrals. They also solve the evolution equation for the "conformal dipole," which removes the conformal invariance breaking double logarithmic term from the kernel. The initial condition is a McLerran-Venugopalan model modified by an anomalous dimension gamma, and the Balitsky running coupling prescription is used for the kernel K1.

## Key numbers

- The evolution speed of the saturation scale lambda = d ln Qs^2 / dy is significantly reduced by NLO corrections compared to leading order, with the conformal and non-conformal dipoles having comparable speeds after a few evolution steps.
- For initial anomalous dimension gamma = 1.0, the evolution speed partial_y N(r)/N(r) becomes negative and logarithmically decreasing as ~ ln r for r -> 0, leading to instability where the dipole amplitude crosses zero at finite r.
- With initial condition Qs0/Lambda_QCD ~ 19 and gamma = 0.6, the dipole amplitude increases at small dipoles throughout the rapidity interval studied, while for smaller Qs the NLO corrections make the evolution speed negative around the front r ~ 1/Qs.
- The anomalous dimension gamma(r) = d ln N(r)/d ln r^2 grows very large at small dipoles after a few rapidity steps for gamma = 1.0 initial condition, while for gamma = 0.6 the unstable region is not reached within the studied rapidity interval.

## Context

This work extends the leading order BK equation with running coupling corrections (Balitsky 2006) to NLO accuracy, confirming that the same instabilities seen in the NLO BFKL equation with absorptive boundary conditions (Avsar 2011) also appear in the fully nonlinear BK equation in coordinate space. The results show that the NLO BK equation requires resummation of large logarithms similar to those discussed for NLO BFKL (Salam 1998, Ciafaloni 1999, Altarelli 1999, Ciafaloni 2003) before the NLO dipole amplitude can be used in phenomenological applications such as DIS fits (Albacete 2010).
