# Jet Quenching and the p-bar >= pi- Anomaly at RHIC

> PHENIX data on Au+Au at root(s)_= 130 AGeV suggest that p-bar yields may exceed pi- at high p_T > 2 GeV/c. We propose that jet quenching in central collisions suppresses the hard PQCD component of the spectra in central A+A reactions, thereby exposing a novel component of baryon dynamics that we attribute to (gluonic) baryon junctions. We predict that the observed p-bar >= pi- and the p > pi+ anomaly at p_T ~ 2 GeV/c is limited to a finite p_T window that decreases with increasing impact parameter.

## Key claim

The observed excess of antiprotons over pions at transverse momentum above 2 GeV/c in central Au+Au collisions is not a permanent feature of the hadron spectra but is limited to a finite pT window (approximately 2-6 GeV/c), beyond which the ratio is predicted to fall below unity, and the magnitude of this anomaly decreases systematically with increasing impact parameter (decreasing centrality).

## Method

The authors construct a two-component model combining a soft phenomenological component (parameterized with exponential pT distributions that scale with the number of participant nucleons) and a hard perturbative QCD component that is suppressed by jet quenching via non-abelian energy loss computed in the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) formalism. The baryon junction component is introduced as a novel source of baryon and antibaryon production, with an effective string tension approximately three times higher than in conventional string fragmentation, leading to a harder pT spectrum for baryons. The full hadronic spectrum is the sum of these soft and hard components, with the hard PQCD component smoothly turned on at pT around 2 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The PHENIX data show that π0 spectra in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 AGeV are suppressed by a factor of approximately 3-4 relative to perturbative QCD predictions scaled by binary collision density.
- The fitted initial gluon rapidity density required to reproduce the observed suppression is dN^g/dy = 800 ± 100.
- The initial proper gluon density is estimated to be ρg(τ ~ 0.2 fm/c) > 10/fm^3.
- The soft component uses T^π ≈ 220 MeV and T^p ≈ 370 MeV (approximately √3 times T^π) for the mean inverse slopes in central collisions.
- The total charged particle rapidity density for the top 6% central events is taken as dN^ch/dy ≈ 650 from PHOBOS data.
- The baryon junction model predicts dN^B/dy ≈ 10 for Au+Au at √s = 130 AGeV, consistent with STAR measurements showing p̄/p ≈ 0.65 at mid-rapidity.
- The p̄/π^- ratio is predicted to reach a maximum near pT ~ 3-4 GeV/c and decrease below unity for pT ≥ 5-6 GeV/c.
- The p/π^+ ratio is predicted to be approximately 50% larger than p̄/π^- at moderate transverse momenta, and at pT ≥ 6 GeV/c the positive baryon/meson ratio is 2-3 times larger than the negative ratio.

## Context

This work extends the soft+hard dynamical model of Gyulassy, Vitev, and Wang (2001) to include a baryon junction component based on the topological gluon field configurations proposed by Rossi and Veneziano (1977) and developed by Kharzeev (1996) and Vance, Gyulassy, and Wang (1998). The paper contrasts its predictions with boosted thermal source models and hydrodynamic calculations (Kolb, Heinz, Huovinen, Eskola, Tuominen, 2001) which predict a monotonic rise of the baryon/meson ratio with pT, whereas this model predicts a decrease at high pT. The results enable experimental tests through centrality-dependent measurements of p̄/π^- and p/π^+ ratios at pT above 5 GeV/c, complementing baryon number transport measurements by STAR and BRAHMS.
