# The errant life of a heavy quark in the quark-gluon plasma

> In the high-temperature phase of QCD, the heavy quark momentum diffusion constant determines, via a fluctuation-dissipation relation, how fast a heavy quark kinetically equilibrates. This transport coefficient can be extracted from thermal correlators via a Kubo formula. We present a lattice calculation of the relevant Euclidean correlators in the gluon plasma, based on a recent formulation of the problem in heavy-quark effective field theory (HQET). We find a $\approx20%$ enhancement of the Euclidean correlator at maximal time separation as the temperature is lowered from $6T_c$ to $2T_c$, pointing to stronger interactions at lower temperatures. At the same time, the correlator becomes flatter from $6T_c$ down to $2T_c$, indicating a relative shift of the spectral weight to lower frequencies. A recent next-to-leading order perturbative calculation of the correlator agrees with the time dependence of the lattice data at the few-percent level. We estimate how much additional contribution from the $ω\lesssim T$ region of the perturbative spectral function would be required to bring it in agreement with the lattice data at $3.1T_c$.

## Key claim
The Euclidean force-force correlator in the gluon plasma shows a temperature dependence that is consistent with stronger interactions at lower temperatures, but the absolute normalization of the chromo-electric field operator is not yet under non-perturbative control, so the results are preliminary.

## Method
The author performed Monte-Carlo simulations of SU(3) gauge theory using the isotropic Wilson action on lattices of size Nt x Ns^3 with periodic boundary conditions. The heavy-quark effective field theory (HQET) force-force correlator was computed using HYP-smeared links for the color parallel transporters and chromo-electric field, with the normalization fixed by matching to the next-to-leading order perturbative prediction at a reference time t_ref = 1/4T at temperature 3.1T_c.

## Key numbers
- The Euclidean correlator at maximal time separation shows approximately 20% enhancement as temperature is lowered from 6T_c to 2T_c
- At T = 3.1T_c, adding a low-frequency contribution with Delta-kappa/T^3 = 0.352(38) and cutoff Lambda = T brings the perturbative prediction into agreement with lattice data at the two largest t values
- With Lambda = 2T, the required Delta-kappa/T^3 is approximately 0.204(22)
- The lattice data at 3.1T_c agrees with the NLO perturbative time dependence at the few-percent level
- The Sommer scale r_0 T_c = 0.746(7) was used for scale setting

## Context
This work extends the perturbative calculation of Burnier et al. (2010) by providing first lattice results for the HQET force-force correlator in the deconfined phase of SU(3) gauge theory. The results are relevant for understanding heavy quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma, which has been studied phenomenologically at RHIC and is being investigated at LHC. The approach enables future non-perturbative determinations of the momentum diffusion coefficient kappa, which can be compared with weak-coupling results from Moore and Teaney (2004) and Caron-Huot et al. (2007), as well as with holographic results from N=4 SYM theory.
