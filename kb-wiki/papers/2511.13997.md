# Light-Front Transverse Nucleon Charge and Magnetisation Densities

> Nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors obtained using both the three-body and quark + fully-interacting-diquark pictures of nucleon structure are employed to calculate an array of light-front transverse densities for the proton and neutron and their dressed valence-quark constituents, viz. flavour separations of the proton and neutron results. These two complementary descriptions of nucleon structure deliver mutually compatible predictions, which match expectations based on modern parametrisations of available data, where such are available. Amongst other things, it is found that transverse-plane valence $u$- and $d$-quark Dirac radii are practically indistinguishable; but regarding kindred Pauli radii, the $d$ quark value is roughly 10% greater than that of the $u$-quark. Moreover, magnetically, the valence $d$ quark is far more active than the valence $u$ quark, probably because it has much greater orbital angular momentum. Both pictures of nucleon structure agree in predicting that, in a polarised nucleon, the transverse-plane charge densities are no longer rotationally invariant. Instead, for a $+\hat x$ polarised nucleon, positive charge is displaced in the $+\hat y$ direction, with the opposite effect for negative charge.

## Key claim

The two complementary continuum Schwinger method descriptions of nucleon structure (three-body Faddeev equation and quark + fully-interacting-diquark picture) yield mutually compatible predictions for light-front transverse charge and magnetisation densities that match modern data parametrisations where available, though the predictions are parameter-free and not fitted to form factor data.

## Method

The authors solved the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, obtaining nucleon bound-state amplitudes from both a three-body Faddeev equation (involving 128 independent scalar functions) and a quark + fully-interacting-diquark picture. They then computed the nucleon electromagnetic current via the seven-point Schwinger function, using the resulting Dirac and Pauli form factors to construct two-dimensional Fourier transforms that yield transverse densities in the Drell-Yan-West frame.

## Key numbers

- Valence d-quark Pauli radius is roughly 10% greater than that of the u-quark, while their Dirac radii are practically indistinguishable
- Interaction parameters: $\omega = 0.8$ GeV, $\omega D = 0.8$ GeV$^3$, renormalisation point invariant quark current mass $\hat m_u = \hat m_d = 6.04$ MeV (corresponding to one-loop evolved mass 4.19 MeV at $\zeta=2$ GeV)
- Resulting predictions: pion mass $m_\pi = 0.14$ GeV, nucleon mass $m_N = 0.94$ GeV, pion leptonic decay constant $f_\pi = 0.094$ GeV
- Infrared coupling from the interaction: $\alpha_{\mathpzc G}(0)/\pi = 1.45$, gluon mass-scale $m_{\mathpzc G} = 0.54$ GeV (compared to QCD's process-independent effective charge $\hat\alpha(0)/\pi = 0.97(4)$ and gluon mass $\hat m_0 = 0.43(1)$ GeV)
- Data parametrisations cover $0 < Q^2 \lesssim 15$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger method calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors from Yao (2024) and Cheng (2025) by transforming them into transverse-plane densities, following the light-front framework of Miller (2010). It provides parameter-free predictions that can be compared with empirical parametrisations such as those of Ye (2017) and Kelly (2004), and enables future studies of nucleon structure in impact-parameter space, including the deformation of charge densities in transversely polarised nucleons.
