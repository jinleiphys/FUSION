# Polarized $^3$He target and final state interactions in SiDIS

> Jefferson Lab (JLab) is starting a wide experimental program aimed at studying the neutron's structure, with a great emphasis on the extraction of the parton transverse-momentum distributions (TMDs). To this end, Semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SiDIS) experiments on polarized $^3$He will be carried out, providing, together with proton and deuteron data, a sound flavor decomposition of the TMDs. Given the expected high statistical accuracy, it is crucial to disentangle nuclear and partonic degrees of freedom to get an accurate theoretical description of both initial and final states. In this contribution, a preliminary study of the Final State Interaction (FSI) in the standard SiDIS, where a pion (or a Kaon) is detected in the final state is presented, in view of constructing a realistic description of the nuclear initial and final states.

## Key claim

The authors present a preliminary study showing that, despite Final State Interactions (FSI) modifying the effective neutron and proton polarizations inside polarized $^3$He by 10-15%, the standard extraction formula for neutron single-spin asymmetries from $^3$He data remains valid because the products of effective polarization and dilution factor are approximately unchanged.

## Method

The authors compute a distorted spin-dependent spectral function for polarized $^3$He by incorporating Final State Interactions through a Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA), where the Glauber operator includes an effective cross section that depends on longitudinal separation. They compare the resulting effective polarizations and extracted neutron asymmetries (Sivers and Collins) with the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) results, using kinematics relevant to the 12-GeV JLab program.

## Key numbers

- Effective neutron polarization in PWIA: $p_n^{PWIA} = 0.878$; with FSI: $p_n^{FSI} = 0.760$ (a reduction of approximately 13%)
- Effective proton polarization in PWIA: $p_p^{PWIA} = -0.023$; with FSI: $p_p^{FSI} = -0.026$ (a change of approximately 13%)
- The calculations are performed for kinematics at beam energy ${\cal E} = 11$ GeV and $Q^2 \simeq 7.6$ (GeV/c)$^2$, with light-cone momentum fraction $\alpha = 0.86$
- The spectral functions are evaluated for missing momentum $p_{mis}$ and removal energy $E$ values contributing to the SiDIS cross section

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GEA treatment of FSI in spectator SiDIS (Del Dotto et al., 2016) to the standard SiDIS case where a pion or kaon is detected. It builds upon the spin-dependent spectral function formalism for $^3$He developed by Ciofi degli Atti et al., using the AV18 interaction, and the extraction formula previously applied by the JLab Hall A Collaboration (2014) to obtain the first Collins and Sivers moments from a transversely polarized $^3$He target. The results enable the planned high-precision 12-GeV JLab program (experiment E12-09-018) to proceed with confidence that the standard extraction method remains valid despite FSI effects.
