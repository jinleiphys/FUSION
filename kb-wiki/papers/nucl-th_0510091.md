# Coupled-channel study of baryon resonances with charm

> Identifying a zero-range exchange of vector mesons as the driving force for the s-wave scattering of pseudo-scalar mesons off the baryon ground states, a rich spectrum of molecules is formed. We argue that chiral symmetry and large-$N_c$ considerations determine that part of the interaction which generates the spectrum. A bound state with exotic quantum numbers is predicted at mass 2.78 GeV. It couples strongly to the $(\bar D_s N),(\bar D Λ),(\bar D Σ)$ channels. A further charm minus-one system is predicted at mass 2.84 GeV as a result of $(\bar D_s Λ), (\bar D Ξ)$ interactions. The two so far observed s-wave baryons with charm one are recovered. We argue that the $Λ_c(2880)$ is not a s-wave state. In addition to those states we predict the existence of about ten narrow s-wave baryon states with masses below 3 GeV. A triplet of crypto-exotic states decaying dominantly into channels with an $η'$ is obtained with masses 4.24 GeV and 4.44 GeV.

# Coupled-channel study of baryon resonances with charm

## Key claim
The paper predicts a rich spectrum of s-wave baryon resonances with charm ±1 generated by zero-range t-channel exchange of light vector mesons, with the claim that chiral symmetry and large-Nc considerations determine the interaction strength. The authors hedge that the precise values of coupling constants do not affect the major results as long as they range within a factor three of the SU(4)-symmetric estimate.

## Method
The authors construct a coupled-channel scattering kernel for pseudoscalar mesons off ground-state baryons using t-channel vector meson exchange as the driving force, projected onto sectors with conserved isospin, strangeness, and charm. The interaction strengths are constrained by chiral symmetry and large-Nc operator analysis, with SU(4) symmetry used to estimate couplings involving charmed vector mesons. The scattering kernel is solved to identify dynamically generated s-wave resonances with J^P = 1/2^-.

## Key numbers
- Bound state with exotic quantum numbers (C=-1, I=1/2, S=-1) predicted at mass 2.78 GeV, coupling strongly to (D̅_s N), (D̅ Λ), (D̅ Σ) channels
- Charm minus-one system (C=-1, I=0, S=-2) predicted at mass 2.84 GeV from (D̅_s Λ), (D̅ Ξ) interactions
- Λ_c(2593) recovered as narrow state at 2.593 GeV with width 0.05 MeV, coupling to (D N) and (D_s Λ) channels
- Ξ_c(2790) identified as state at 2.793 GeV with width 16 MeV, coupling to (K̅ Σ_c) and (η Ξ'_c) channels
- Crypto-exotic triplet states predicted at masses 4.238 GeV (width 7.3 MeV) and 4.443 GeV (width 9 MeV), decaying dominantly into channels with η'
- Coupling constant g = 10.4 ± 1.4 deduced from D meson decay, compared to g ≃ 5.8 from ρ decay

## Context
This work extends previous coupled-channel studies by Lutz and collaborators (2004, 2005) that described open-charm meson and baryon resonances as hadronic molecular states using leading-order chiral Lagrangians. The paper builds on the conjecture from Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2002, 2004) that meson and baryon resonances not belonging to the large-Nc ground state should be viewed as molecular states, and confirms expectations from Gignoux, Silvestre-Brac, and Richard (1987) and Lipkin (1987) that penta-quark type states exist with charm minus one. The predictions enable experimental tests by SELEX, BELLE, and the FAIR project at GSI, and call for unquenched lattice QCD simulations to verify the existence of the predicted states.
