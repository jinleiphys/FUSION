# Elliptic Flow of Rare High-Momentum Probes in Nuclear Collisions

> In high energy nuclear collisions the leading parton of a jet can change flavor through interactions with the surrounding medium. This can considerably boost the relative yield of rare high momentum particles, like strange quarks and photons. We revisit these jet conversions and discuss implications for the azimuthal asymmetry $v_2$ of rare probes. We predict that the $v_2$ of kaons at RHIC falls significantly below that of pions and protons at high transverse momentum. Experimental information on the relative $v_2$ of strange hadrons could provide constraints on the mean free path of fast quarks and gluons in a quark gluon plasma.

# Elliptic Flow of Rare High-Momentum Probes in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The azimuthal asymmetry $v_2$ of rare high-$p_T$ probes like strange hadrons receives a negative contribution from jet conversions in the quark gluon plasma, leading to a predicted suppression of kaon $v_2$ relative to pion $v_2$ at high transverse momentum; however, the absolute magnitude of this effect depends on the mean free path of fast partons and the authors caution that jet quenching calculations have difficulties explaining the relatively large $v_2$ values observed at high $p_T$ with realistic fireball models.

## Method

The authors model the propagation of jet partons (u, d, s, and gluons) through a quark gluon plasma fireball using Fokker-Planck and rate equations, with elastic perturbative parton cross sections scaled by a K-factor. Jet conversions are implemented by varying the conversion cross section scaling (K=4 with conversions, K=0 without conversions), and after partons exit the fireball they are fragmented into hadrons using AKK fragmentation functions. The elliptic flow $v_2$ of final-state particles is then calculated, with hadrons from recombination added to account for non-jet sources at lower transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV for the 20-30% centrality bin, the $v_2$ of kaons at $p_T$ = 15 GeV/$c$ can be as much as a factor of two smaller than that of pions due to jet conversions.
- The analysis focuses on the high transverse momentum region $p_T >$ 6 GeV/$c$ at RHIC for changes in hadron abundances in the jet fragmentation region.
- Experimental data for $v_2$ of identified hadrons above 6 GeV/$c$ have very large error bars and do not extend beyond 10 GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of jet conversions (Liu and Fries, 2008) which showed that rare high-$p_T$ flavors like strange quarks can be significantly enhanced through coupling to a chemically equilibrated medium. It generalizes the argument of Turbide et al. (2005) that real and virtual photons from jet conversions exhibit negative $v_2$, applying it to strange hadrons. The paper enables experimental constraints on the mean free path of fast quarks and gluons in the quark gluon plasma through measurements of the relative $v_2$ of strange hadrons, and suggests that the mechanism of changed multiplicities in jet cones (Sapeta and Wiedemann, 2007) could be distinguished from the leading-parton flavor change mechanism discussed here.
