# A dispersion relation for the pion-mass dependence of hadron properties

> We present a dispersion relation in the pion-mass squared, which static quantities (nucleon mass, magnetic moment, etc.) obey under the assumption of analyticity in the entire complex $m_π^2$ plane modulo a cut at negative $m_π^2$ associated with pion production. The relation is verified here in a number of examples of nucleon and $Δ$-isobar properties computed in chiral perturbation theory up to order $p^3$. We outline a method to obtain relations for other mass-dependencies, and illustrate it on a two-loop example.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dispersion relation in the pion-mass squared that static hadron properties obey, assuming analyticity in the complex \(m_\pi^2\) plane except for a cut along the negative axis associated with pion production; the relation is verified for nucleon and \(\Delta\)-isobar properties computed in chiral perturbation theory up to order \(p^3\), but requires subtractions when the loop integrals are ultraviolet divergent.

## Method

The authors derive a dispersion relation \(f(m_\pi^2) = -\frac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\infty}^0 dt \, \frac{\mathrm{Im}\, f(t)}{t-m_\pi^2+i0^+}\) for static quantities, based on the analytic structure of chiral loops. They verify the relation by computing the imaginary parts of one-loop chiral perturbation theory expressions for the nucleon mass, nucleon magnetic moments, and nucleon polarizabilities, then showing that the dispersion integral reproduces the renormalized loop results. For the \(\Delta\)-isobar mass, the integration contour extends to \(t = \Delta^2\) (with \(\Delta = M_\Delta - M_N\)) due to the physical pion-production threshold. They also demonstrate direct computation of absorptive parts using hyperspherical coordinates and a two-loop sunset diagram example.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: the order-\(p^3\) self-energy \(\overline{\Sigma}^{(3)}_N(t)\) is given by \(-\frac{3 g_A^2 M_N^3}{(4\pi f_\pi)^2} \big( \tau^{3/2} \sqrt{1-\frac{1}{4}\tau} \, \arccos(\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\tau}) + \frac{1}{4}\tau^2 \ln \tau \big)\) with \(\tau = t/M_N^2\), using \(M_N \simeq 939\) MeV, \(g_A \simeq 1.267\), \(f_\pi \simeq 92.4\) MeV.
- \(\Delta\)-isobar width: \(\Gamma_\Delta = -2\,\mathrm{Im}\,\Sigma_\Delta \simeq 115\) MeV, obtained from the imaginary part of the \(\pi N\) loop contribution to the \(\Delta\) self-energy.
- Magnetic polarizability of the proton at order \(p^3\): the expression \(\beta^{(3)}_p(t)\) is given by an integral involving \(e^2 g_A^2/(192\pi^3 f_\pi^2 M_N)\) with \(e \simeq \sqrt{4\pi/137}\).
- Two-loop sunset diagram: the imaginary part is \(\mathrm{Im}\, \tilde{J}(t) = \theta(-t)\,\pi\,[ -2t/\varepsilon + t(-7 + (2+t)\ln(-t)) - (1-t)^2\ln(1-t) + \mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)]\) in dimensional regularization with \(d = 4-2\varepsilon\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of mass-dispersion relations originally proposed by Fubini (1968) and de Alfaro (1968) for scattering amplitudes, and later developed by Gordon (1970), Murtaza (1970), and O'Donnell (1972). The authors provide the first explicit verification of such relations within chiral perturbation theory, testing them on nucleon and \(\Delta\)-isobar properties. The relation can serve as a consistency constraint for chiral extrapolation formulas used to connect lattice QCD results at larger quark masses to the physical pion mass, and as a computational technique for higher-loop calculations where the imaginary part is simpler to obtain than the full result.
