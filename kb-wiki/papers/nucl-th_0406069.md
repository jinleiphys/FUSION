# Three-pair final-state interaction in the pp->ppηreaction close to threshold

> We present a three-body formalism describing final-state interaction effects. The three-body enhancement factor is derived by expanding the complete three-particle wave function in hyperspherical harmonics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-body formalism for final-state interactions in the \(pp\to pp\eta\) reaction, claiming that close to threshold the \(p-p\) interaction strongly dominates while the \(\eta-p\) interaction gives almost negligible contribution to the calculated effective mass spectra, with the caveat that this conclusion depends on the assumption that the s-wave amplitude dominates because it is proportional to the huge FSI enhancement factor while p-wave amplitudes are not.

## Method

The author derives a three-body enhancement factor by expanding the complete three-particle wave function in hyperspherical harmonics, treating all three pair-wise interactions on equal footing. The enhancement factor is obtained as the limit of the ratio of the full three-body wave function to the plane wave as the hyperradius \(\rho\) goes to zero, using the \(L=0\) channel where \(K=0\) implies \(\ell = \lambda = 0\). The radial wave functions are computed numerically by solving coupled differential equations with boundary conditions at small \(\rho\) and matching to asymptotic Coulomb wave functions at large \(\rho\) to determine the Jost matrix.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy \(Q = 15.5\) MeV is used for comparison with experimental data from Moskal (2004).
- The \(\eta p\) scattering length might be as large as about 1 fm, which is one order of magnitude smaller than the pp scattering length.
- The number of channels \(N\) for a given truncation \(K_{max}\) is given by \(N = \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{2} K_{max} + 1)(\frac{1}{2} K_{max} + 2)\).
- The Coulomb barrier penetration factor is \(C_0(\eta)^2 = 2\pi\eta/[\exp(2\pi\eta) - 1]\).

## Context

This work extends the two-body final-state interaction formalism of Fermi, Watson, and Migdal (1950s) to the three-body case for the \(pp\to pp\eta\) reaction, addressing discrepancies in earlier models that only included pp FSI. The paper builds on the hyperspherical harmonics method developed for Borromean systems by the HH collaboration (1990s) and uses Raynal-Revai coefficients for coordinate transformations. The results enable discrimination between s-wave and p-wave production models through polarization experiments, as suggested by Nakayama (2004), where the s-wave model predicts vanishing polarization observables while any non-zero analyzing power would reveal higher partial waves.
