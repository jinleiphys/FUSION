# A-dependence of weak nuclear structure functions

> Effect of nuclear medium on the weak structure functions $F_2^A(x,Q^2)$ and $F_3^A(x,Q^2)$ have been studied using charged current (anti)neutrino deep inelastic scattering on various nuclear targets. Relativistic nuclear spectral function which incorporate Fermi motion, binding and nucleon correlations are used for the calculations. We also consider the pion and rho meson cloud contributions calculated from a microscopic model for meson-nucleus self-energies. Using these structure functions, $F_i^A/F_i^{proton}$ and $F_i^A/F_i^{deuteron}$(i=2,3, A=$^{12}C$, $^{16}O$, $CH$ and $H_{2}O$) are obtained.

## Key claim

The nuclear medium effects on weak structure functions \(F_2^A\) and \(F_3^A\) differ between nuclear targets and between the two structure functions, with the difference between base results (Fermi motion, binding, correlations) and full calculations (including shadowing, anti-shadowing, and meson cloud contributions) reaching 10-12% for \(F_2^A\) and 4-6% for \(F_3^A\) at low Bjorken \(x\), reducing to about 1% at higher \(x\).

## Method

The authors computed nuclear weak structure functions \(F_2^A\) and \(F_3^A\) for charged-current (anti)neutrino deep inelastic scattering on \(^{12}\text{C}\), \(^{16}\text{O}\), \(\text{CH}\), and \(\text{H}_2\text{O}\) targets using a relativistic nuclear spectral function that incorporates Fermi motion, binding, and nucleon correlations, with a local-density approximation applied to finite nuclei. They included target mass corrections, shadowing/anti-shadowing effects, and pion and rho meson cloud contributions from a microscopic model for meson-nucleus self-energies, using CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions at next-to-leading order (NLO). Ratios \(F_i^A/F_i^{\text{proton}}\) and \(F_i^A/F_i^{\text{deuteron}}\) (i=2,3) were obtained, with deuteron structure functions calculated using the same formalism but with the deuteron wave function squared instead of the nuclear spectral function.

## Key numbers

- The difference between base results and full calculations for \(F_2^A\) is 10-12% at low \(x\), reducing to 1% at higher \(x\).
- The difference between base results and full calculations for \(F_3^A\) is 4-6% at low \(x\), reducing to 1% at higher \(x\).
- Ratios \(F_i^A/F_i^{\text{proton}}\) and \(F_i^A/F_i^{\text{deuteron}}\) were presented for \(A = ^{12}\text{C}, ^{16}\text{O}, \text{CH}, \text{H}_2\text{O}\) at NLO, with the specific numerical values shown in figures (not extracted in text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous field-theoretical approach using relativistic spectral functions (Haider et al., 2011; 2012) to weak structure functions for neutrino-nucleus scattering, complementing studies of electromagnetic structure functions. The calculations are directly relevant to the MINER\(\nu\)A experiment at Fermilab, which measures neutrino-nucleus cross sections on various nuclear targets in the 1-20 GeV energy region, and may aid in the analysis of other proposed (anti)neutrino experiments using nuclear targets.
