# Relativistic Pseudospin Symmetry and the Structure of Nuclear States

> We show that a natural explanation for characteristic features (angular momentum and radial quantum numbers) of pseudospin doublets and intruder levels in nuclei can be obtained by combining the relativistic attributes of pseudospin symmetry with known properties of Dirac bound states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the characteristic angular momentum and radial node structure of pseudospin doublets and intruder levels in nuclei can be explained by combining the relativistic origin of pseudospin symmetry with known properties of Dirac bound states, even though exact pseudospin symmetry cannot support bound states and must be broken in real nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyze the radial wave functions (upper component G and lower component F) of spherically symmetric Dirac Hamiltonians with scalar and vector potentials typical for nuclear mean fields. They derive node-counting relations between G and F for different values of the Dirac quantum number κ, and then show that in the pseudospin symmetry limit (V_S = -V_V), the lower components of the two states in a pseudospin doublet become equal, which forces the observed node structure of the upper components. The analysis uses the product GF and its derivative to determine node patterns.

## Key numbers

- The scalar and vector potential parameters used in the example calculation: α_S = -358 MeV, α_V = 292 MeV, R = 7 fm, a = 0.6 fm, tuned to the neutron spectra of ²⁰⁸Pb.
- For κ < 0 states: the number of internal nodes of the lower component equals the number of internal nodes of the upper component (n_F = n_G).
- For κ > 0 states: the number of internal nodes of the lower component exceeds that of the upper component by one (n_F = n_G + 1).
- Bound states with no nodes in either component (n_F = n_G = 0) can occur only for κ < 0 (j = ℓ + 1/2), not for κ > 0 (j = ℓ - 1/2).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic interpretation of pseudospin symmetry developed by Ginocchio (1997) and by Arima and collaborators, which showed that pseudospin symmetry originates from an SU(2) symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian when the scalar and vector potentials are equal and opposite. The paper provides a unified explanation for the empirical pseudospin doublet pattern observed in nuclear shell models (e.g., the (2p₃/₂, 1f₅/₂) doublet) and for the special status of intruder levels such as 0g₉/₂, 0h₁₁/₂, and 0i₁₃/₂. The results enable a deeper understanding of why pseudospin symmetry is necessarily broken in real nuclei while still producing near-degenerate doublets, and they connect the relativistic node-counting rules directly to the non-relativistic shell-model quantum numbers.
