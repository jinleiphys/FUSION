# The mean transverse momentum of ultracentral heavy-ion collisions: A new probe of hydrodynamics

> We predict that the mean transverse momentum of charged hadrons $\langle p_t\rangle$ rises as a function of the charged-particle multiplicity in ultracentral nucleus-nucleus collisions. We explain that this phenomenon has a simple physical origin and represents an unambiguous prediction of the hydrodynamic framework of heavy-ion collisions. We argue that the relative increase of $\langle p_t \rangle$ is proportional to the speed of sound squared $c_s^2$ of the quark-gluon plasma. Based on the value of $c_s^2$ from lattice QCD, we expect $\langle p_t\rangle$ to increase by approximately $18$ MeV between 1\% and 0.001\% centrality in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV.

## Key claim

The mean transverse momentum of charged hadrons in ultracentral heavy-ion collisions is predicted to rise with charged-particle multiplicity, and this rise is proportional to the speed of sound squared of the quark-gluon plasma; the prediction is parameter-free and does not rely on any specific model, but assumes that the centrality-defining observable is proportional to the entropy on an event-by-event basis.

## Method

The authors use the TRENTo Monte Carlo generator of initial conditions with the p=0 prescription (entropy density proportional to sqrt(T_A T_B)) to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, tuning the fluctuation parameter k to match the multiplicity distribution measured by ALICE. They then derive an analytic model using a Bayesian procedure to relate the entropy density to the charged-particle multiplicity, and use the relation c_s^2 = d ln<p_t> / d ln N_ch to predict the rise of <p_t> based on the speed of sound from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The predicted increase of <p_t> between 1% and 0.001% centrality in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV is approximately 18 MeV.
- Between 1% and 0.1% centrality, <p_t> is predicted to increase by 8.4 MeV.
- Between 0.1% and 0.01% centrality, <p_t> is predicted to increase by 5.6 MeV.
- Between 0.01% and 0.001% centrality, <p_t> is predicted to increase by 4.1 MeV.
- The speed of sound squared used is c_s^2 = 0.252, taken from lattice QCD at an effective temperature T = 222 MeV.
- The baseline <p_t> value left of the knee is p_t0 = 682 MeV, measured by ALICE in the 0-5% centrality range.
- The knee of the entropy distribution is at S_knee = 33800 (in V0 amplitude units) with sigma = 1140.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework of heavy-ion collisions by predicting a new phenomenon in ultracentral collisions that does not involve anisotropic flow, complementing earlier work by Gardim (2019) on the relationship between <p_t> and temperature. The prediction contradicts the observed flatness of <p_t> in wide centrality bins reported by ALICE (2018), but the authors argue the rise is only expected in the ultracentral region beyond the knee of the multiplicity distribution. The analysis enables a direct experimental determination of the speed of sound from the variation of <p_t> with multiplicity, complementing extractions from the variation of <p_t> with collision energy.
