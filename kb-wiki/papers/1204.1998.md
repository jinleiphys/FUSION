# Nuclear modification of high-p_T hadron spectra in p+A collisions at LHC

> Multiple parton scatterings in high-energy p+A collisions involve multi-parton correlation inside the projectile and color coherence of multiple jets which will lead to nuclear modification of final hadron spectra relative to that in p+p collisions. Such modification of final hadron spectra in p+A collisions is studied within HIJING 2.1 model which includes initial parton shadowing, transverse momentum broadening, parton flavor and momentum correlation inside the projectile through flavor and momentum conservation and fragmentation of multiple jets. They are shown to modify the partonic flavor content of final jets and momentum spectra of final hadrons in p+A collisions at the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

Multiple parton correlations inside the projectile nucleon (due to valence quark number conservation) and hadronization of multiple-jet systems from multiple scatterings can lead to suppression of final hadrons at large \(p_{T}\) in \(p+A\) collisions at LHC, in addition to the well-studied effects of parton shadowing and transverse momentum broadening.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING 2.1 Monte Carlo model, which combines perturbative QCD for hard parton scatterings (with a transverse momentum cut-off \(p_0\)) and string excitations for soft interactions. They implemented initial parton shadowing via an impact-parameter-dependent nuclear modification factor, transverse momentum (\(k_T\)) broadening via a Gaussian kick per binary nucleon-nucleon scattering, and enforced valence quark number conservation across multiple independent hard scatterings. Final-state hadronization was treated via the Lund string model, and the results were compared to an alternative "de-coherent hard scattering" (DHC) option that decouples hard and soft interactions, and to independent fragmentation.

## Key numbers

*   The \(k_T\)-broadening width is parameterized as \(\langle k_{T}^{2}\rangle = 0.14\log(\sqrt{s}/{\rm GeV})-0.43 \;{\rm GeV}^{2}\).
*   The gluon shadowing parameter was set to \(s_{g}=0.28\) to describe charged hadron rapidity distributions in \(d+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200\) GeV and \(p+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN}=5.0\) TeV.
*   The nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) for charged hadrons in \(p+A\) collisions is predicted to show a Cronin enhancement peak at around 4 GeV/\(c\), with no apparent energy dependence of the peak position between RHIC and LHC energies.
*   In central \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, the HIJING 2.1 calculation without jet quenching (using Lund string fragmentation) produces a suppression of final hadron spectra at large \(p_{T}\) that leaves no room for additional suppression by jet quenching, as compared to data from ALICE and CMS.

## Context

This work extends studies of cold nuclear matter effects in \(p+A\) collisions, such as parton shadowing (Eskola 1998, Vitev 2002) and the Color Glass Condensate model (McLerran 1993, Kharzeev 2003), by incorporating multi-parton correlations and multiple-jet hadronization within the HIJING framework (Wang 1991). The results enable experimental tests in \(p+Pb\) collisions at LHC to distinguish between \(k_T\)-broadening (which predicts a fixed peak position) and gluon saturation models. The finding that Lund string fragmentation of multiple-jet systems in heavy-ion collisions leaves no room for jet quenching suggests that an independent fragmentation mechanism (e.g., parton recombination) is needed for deconfined systems, and that \(p+A\) collisions can serve as a precursor to study hadronization in the quark-gluon plasma.
