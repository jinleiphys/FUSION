# Hadronic resonance production and interaction in partonic and hadronic matter in EPOS3 with and without the hadronic afterburner UrQMD

> We study the production of hadronic resonances and their interaction in the partonic and hadronic medium using the EPOS3 model, which employs the UrQMD model for the description of the hadronic phase. We investigate the centrality dependence of the yields and momentum distributions for various resonances (rho(770),K(892),phi(1020),Delta(1232),Sigma(1385),Lambda(1520),Xi(1530) and their antiparticles) in Pb-Pb collisions at sNN= 2.76 TeV. The predictions for K(892) and phi(1020) will be compared with the experimental data from the ALICE collaboration. The observed signal suppression of the K(892) with increasing centrality will be discussed with respect to the resonance interaction in the hadronic medium. The mean transverse momentum and other particle ratios such as phi(1020)/p and Omega/phi(1020) will be discussed with respect to additional contributions from the hadronic medium interactions.

## Key claim

The EPOS3 model with the UrQMD hadronic afterburner provides a fair description of the observed centrality-dependent suppression of the K*(892) resonance yield in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, but the model gives less suppression in central collisions than was experimentally observed by ALICE.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS3 event generator, which combines a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic evolution of a quark-gluon plasma core with a corona of hadrons from string decays, and coupled it to the UrQMD hadronic cascade model to describe the hadronic phase. They generated approximately 800,000 Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV with UrQMD turned on and 450,000 events with UrQMD turned off, then compared the transverse momentum spectra, yields, and particle ratios of seven hadronic resonances (rho(770), K*(892), phi(1020), Delta(1232), Sigma(1385), Lambda(1520), Xi(1530)) across multiple centrality intervals. Resonances were flagged as reconstructable only if neither decay product underwent a re-scattering in the hadronic medium.

## Key numbers

- The hadronisation temperature used in EPOS3 is T_H = 166 MeV, with a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of eta/s = 0.08
- The K*(892) is suppressed by at least 50% in the low-pT region (pT < 1 GeV/c) in central collisions when comparing EPOS3 with UrQMD ON versus OFF
- The phi(1020) meson has a lifetime of 46.26 fm/c, compared to the fireball lifetime of approximately 10-15 fm/c, leading to a maximum signal loss of only about 10% in the most central collisions
- The mean transverse momentum (mT) increases by 200-300 MeV/c for most resonances and the proton due to interactions in the hadronic phase
- The kinetic freeze-out temperature in central collisions from blast-wave fits is T_kin = 90 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of resonance production using the UrQMD model (Bleicher 1999, Bass 1998) and the EPOS framework (Werner 2010-2014) to systematically separate the contributions of the partonic and hadronic phases to measured resonance yields. The results are compared to experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration (2015) on K*(892) and phi(1020) production in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The study confirms the interpretation that the observed suppression of K*(892) relative to K- in central collisions is due to re-scattering of decay products in the hadronic phase, while the longer-lived phi(1020) is largely unaffected. The predictions for non-measured resonances (rho(770), Delta(1232), Sigma(1385), Lambda(1520), Xi(1530)) provide testable expectations for future experimental analyses, and the centrality-dependent behavior of the proton/phi ratio demonstrates the importance of hadronic interactions for describing proton momentum spectra.
