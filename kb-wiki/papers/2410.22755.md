# Nucleon-charmonium interactions from lattice QCD

> We present a realistic lattice QCD study on low-energy $N$-$J/ψ$ and $N$-$η_c$ interactions based on (2+1) flavor configurations with nearly physical pion mass $m_π=146$ MeV. The interactions, extracted from the spacetime correlations of nucleon and charmonium system by using the HAL QCD method, are found to be attractive in all distances and possess a characteristic long-range tail consistent with the two-pion exchange potential. The resulting $S$-wave scattering lengths are $0.30(2)\left(^{+0}_{-2}\right)$ fm, $0.38(4)\left(^{+0}_{-3}\right)$ fm, and $0.21(2)\left(^{+0}_{-1}\right)$ fm for spin-$3/2$ $N$-$J/ψ$, spin-$1/2$ $N$-$J/ψ$, and spin-$1/2$ $N$-$η_c$, respectively. Our results are orders of magnitude larger than those from the photoproduction experiments assuming the vector meson dominance. Our findings may provide deeper understanding of the nonperturbative QCD phenomena ranging from the origin of nucleon mass to the in-medium $J/ψ$ mass modification as well as the properties of hidden-charm pentaquark states.

## Key claim

The low-energy nucleon-charmonium interactions are attractive at all distances and yield scattering lengths orders of magnitude larger than those inferred from photoproduction experiments assuming vector meson dominance, but the results are obtained under the condition that the HAL QCD method is used to extract the potential from lattice correlation functions at nearly physical pion mass.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations on a 96^4 lattice with lattice spacing a ≈ 0.0846 fm and nearly physical pion mass mπ = 146 MeV, using the Iwasaki gauge action and nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quark action. The N-J/ψ and N-ηc interactions were extracted from spacetime correlations of nucleon and charmonium systems using the HAL QCD method, which converts the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitude into an energy-independent nonlocal potential. The leading-order local potential was obtained from the normalized correlation function R(r,t) at t/a = 13-15, and scattering phase shifts were computed by solving the Schrödinger equation with a three-range Gaussian fit to the potential.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: mπ = 146.4(4) MeV (lattice) compared to 138.0 MeV (experimental)
* Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.0846 fm (a⁻¹ ≈ 2333 MeV) on a 96⁴ lattice volume corresponding to La ≈ 8.1 fm
* N-J/ψ (spin-3/2) S-wave scattering length: a₀ = 0.30(2)(⁺⁰₋₂) fm
* N-J/ψ (spin-1/2) S-wave scattering length: a₀ = 0.38(4)(⁺⁰₋₃) fm
* N-ηc (spin-1/2) S-wave scattering length: a₀ = 0.21(2)(⁺⁰₋₁) fm
* Two-pion-exchange fit parameter α = 22(2) MeV·fm² for N-J/ψ (spin-3/2), 23(3) MeV·fm² for N-J/ψ (spin-1/2), and 16(2) MeV·fm² for N-ηc, extracted in the range 0.9 < r < 1.8 fm
* J/ψ mass shift in nuclear matter under linear density approximation: δmJ/ψ ≈ 19(3) MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρnm = 0.17 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies that were limited by quenched approximation (Yokokawa 2006, Kawanai 2010), heavy pion mass mπ ≈ 700 MeV (Sugiura 2019), or large uncertainties leading to effectively zero scattering length (Liu 2008, Skerbis 2018). The results are orders of magnitude larger than scattering lengths inferred from J/ψ photoproduction data assuming vector meson dominance (Pentchev 2020) and are comparable to values from QCD multiple expansion methods (Kaidalov 1992, Brodsky 1997, Sibirtsev 2005) and QCD sum rules (Hayashigaki 1998). The findings enable improved predictions for in-medium J/ψ mass modification, hidden-charm pentaquark properties, and charmonium-nucleus bound states, and could be checked by future femtoscopic correlation measurements of p-J/ψ.
