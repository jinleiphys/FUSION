# Lattice Calculation of Short-Range Contributions to Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay $π^-\toπ^+ ee$ at Physical Pion Mass

> Neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decays provide an excellent probe for determining whether neutrinos are Dirac or Majorana fermions. The short-range matrix elements associated with the $π^- \to π^+ ee$ process contribute at leading order in the $0νββ$ decay channel $nn \to ppee$ through pion exchange between nucleons. However, current lattice calculations show notable discrepancies in predicting these short-range contributions. To address this issue, we perform a lattice QCD calculation of the $π^- \to π^+ ee$ matrix elements using domain wall fermion ensembles at the physical pion mass generated by the RBC and UKQCD Collaborations. To mitigate contamination from around-the-world effects, we develop a new method to reconstruct and subtract them directly from lattice data. We then perform a nonperturbative renormalization using the RI/SMOM scheme. Compared with previous studies, this work reduces the uncertainties in the matrix elements and provides an independent cross-check that helps to reconcile the discrepancies among previous lattice calculations.

## Key claim
This lattice QCD calculation of the short-range contributions to the π⁻ → π⁺ ee process at physical pion mass reduces uncertainties compared to previous studies and helps reconcile a factor-of-two discrepancy between two prior lattice results (Nicholson 2018 and Detmold 2022), though the authors note that the most likely source of the discrepancy appears to be an overall normalization factor.

## Method
The authors compute bare matrix elements of five four-quark operators (O₁, O₂, O₃, O′₁, O′₂) using domain wall fermion ensembles at physical and unphysical pion masses generated by the RBC/UKQCD Collaboration. To mitigate around-the-world effects from pion backward propagation under periodic boundary conditions, they develop a new subtraction method that reconstructs and removes these effects directly from lattice data. The matrix elements are renormalized nonperturbatively using the RI/SMOM scheme in both (γ_μ,γ_μ) and (slashed q, slashed q) schemes, with perturbative matching to the MS-bar scheme at μ = 3 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Bag parameter B_π^{MS-bar}(μ=3 GeV) for operator O₃: this work gives 0.421(23) (from Nicholson 2018) versus 0.197(18) (converted from Detmold 2022), showing a factor-of-two discrepancy that this work addresses
- Bare matrix element B_π^{lat} for ensemble 24IH1: 0.5072(30) (this work), compared to 0.5075(31) from Aoki (2010) and 0.2544(21) from Detmold (2022), giving a ratio of 1.994(20)
- Bare matrix element B_π^{lat} for ensemble 32IH1: 0.4691(42) (this work), compared to 0.4721(26) from Aoki (2010) and 0.2299(27) from Detmold (2022), giving a ratio of 2.041(30)
- Pion mass for physical ensembles: 139.6(2) MeV (48I ensemble) and 139.2(3) MeV (64I ensemble)
- Lattice spacing: a⁻¹ = 1.7295(38) GeV (48I) and 2.3586(70) GeV (64I)
- ΔE_{ππ} = 1.445(55) MeV extracted from the two-point function in ensemble 48I

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements, specifically addressing the factor-of-two discrepancy between Nicholson (2018) and Detmold (2022) for the short-range π⁻ → π⁺ ee contribution. The new subtraction method for around-the-world effects provides an independent cross-check that helps reconcile these previous results. The calculation uses the same renormalization coefficients as those used in BSM kaon mixing studies by Boyle (2024), and the bare matrix elements for O₃ agree with the well-established bag parameter calculations from Aoki (2010), supporting the normalization used. This enables more reliable inputs for chiral effective field theory calculations of the nn → ppee decay channel.
