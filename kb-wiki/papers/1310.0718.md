# Some Intensive and Extensive Quantities in High-Energy Collisions

> We review the evolution of some statistical and thermodynamical quantities measured in difference sizes of high-energy collisions at different energies. We differentiate between intensive and extensive quantities and discuss the importance of their distinguishability in characterizing possible critical phenomena of nuclear collisions at various energies with different initial conditions.

# Some Intensive and Extensive Quantities in High-Energy Collisions

## Key claim
The distinguishability between intensive and extensive statistical-thermodynamical quantities in high-energy nuclear collisions is essential for characterizing possible critical phenomena, and this distinction depends on both the collision energy and the system size (pp vs. AA collisions), with the difference between proton ratios in pp and AA collisions disappearing at very high center-of-mass energies.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental data on charged-particle multiplicity per participating nucleon, antiproton-to-proton ratios, elliptic flow coefficients, and inverse slope parameters from transverse mass spectra across multiple collision systems (pp, pA, AA) and energies spanning from AGS through SPS, RHIC, and LHC. The data are compared to predictions from the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model and other statistical-thermal models to assess how intensive versus extensive quantities behave with varying energy and system size.

## Key numbers
- Integrated elliptic flow v₂ in central heavy-ion collisions (20-30% centrality) shows about a 30% increase in magnitude at √sNN = 2.76 TeV compared to Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Integrated elliptic flow is corrected for pT cutoff of 0.2 GeV/c, with estimated correction magnitude of 12 ± 5% based on Therminator calculations
- Hagedorn temperature from direct thermal photons: 304 ± 51 MeV (ALICE) and 221 ± 19 MeV (PHENIX)
- Inverse slope parameter T for K⁺ and K⁻ mesons in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions shows a plateau at SPS energies, preceded by a steep rise at AGS energies and followed by a further increase at RHIC
- pp collisions simulated by PYTHIA, PHOJET, and EPOS at 900 and 7000 GeV yield integrated v₂ coefficients varying from 10% to 15%, attributed solely to non-flow correlations

## Context
This review extends the statistical-thermal model approach dating back to Fermi (1950), Pomeranchuk (1951), and Hagedorn (1965), and builds on the distinction between intensive and extensive quantities introduced by Tolman. The work compares experimental results from multiple collaborations (AGS, SPS, RHIC, LHC, ALICE, PHENIX, STAR) and shows that HRG models describe hadronization at large energies well, with the condition S(√s,T) = 7(4/π²)VT³ appearing valid across all energies from AGS to LHC. The paper enables future systematic measurements to confirm whether the Hagedorn temperature variation with system size and energy is genuine, and highlights that no microscopic Monte Carlo models currently describe anisotropic flow in elementary hadron-hadron interactions.
