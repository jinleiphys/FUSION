# Dynamical Model for Meson Production off Nucleon and Application to Neutrino-Nucleus Reactions

> I explain the Sato-Lee (SL) model and its extension to the neutrino-induced pion production off the nucleon. Then I discuss applications of the SL model to incoherent and coherent pion productions in the neutrino-nucleus scattering. I mention a further extension of this approach with a dynamical coupled-channels model developed in Excited Baryon Analysis Center of JLab.

## Key claim
The Sato-Lee (SL) model provides a unitary description of pion production that treats resonant and non-resonant mechanisms on the same footing, and its extension to the weak sector shows good consistency with neutrino-nucleon data, but the model's application to neutrino-nucleus scattering requires careful incorporation of nuclear effects such as initial-state correlations, Pauli blocking, final-state interactions, and medium effects on Delta propagation.

## Method
The author describes the Sato-Lee (SL) model, which starts from phenomenological Lagrangians and derives an effective Hamiltonian via unitary transformation. The model separates the pion photoproduction amplitude into non-resonant (Born diagrams, t-channel rho and omega exchange, crossed Delta term) and resonant (bare Delta vertex) contributions, with the Delta self-energy and dressed vertices computed from the pion-nucleon scattering amplitude obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The extension to neutrino-induced pion production replaces the electromagnetic current with the weak vector-minus-axial-vector current, where the vector current is obtained from the isovector part of the electromagnetic current by isospin rotation, and the axial-vector N-Delta transition is parametrized with form factors.

## Key numbers
- The axial-vector N-Delta transition form factor uses a dipole form with mass parameter M_A = 1.02 GeV.
- The coupling g_ANDelta(0) is related to the nucleon axial coupling g_A using the nonrelativistic constituent quark model.
- The correction factor R(Q^2) = (1 + a Q^2) e^{-b Q^2} is assumed to be the same as that used for the gamma N to Delta form factor, determined from pion electroproduction data.
- The model is applied to neutrino-nucleus scattering on a carbon-12 target, with differential cross sections shown for nu_e + C-12 -> e- pi X at lepton scattering angle theta = 10 degrees as a function of final pi N invariant mass W.

## Context
This work extends the Sato-Lee (SL) model, originally developed for pion photoproduction off the nucleon (Sato and Lee, 2005), to the weak sector for neutrino-induced pion production. The model is applied to incoherent and coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering, building on prior work by Nakamura and collaborators (2009) for quasi-free Delta excitation and by the same group for coherent pion production. The approach contrasts with most previous works that considered only resonant mechanisms, and it enables more complete treatment of neutrino-nucleus reactions in the few-GeV region relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments.
