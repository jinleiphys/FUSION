# Higher order fluctuations of conserved charges in heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the effect of the global charge conservation on the cumulants of conserved charges in relativistic heavy ion collisions in a finite rapidity window by studying the time evolution of cumulants in the hadronic medium. It is argued that the global charge conservation does not affect the experimental result of the net-electric charge fluctuation observed by ALICE, because of the finite diffusion distance of charged particles in the hadronic stage.

# Higher order fluctuations of conserved charges in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The global charge conservation (GCC) effect on net-electric charge fluctuations observed by ALICE is almost negligible, because the diffusion distance of charged particles in the hadronic stage is too small for boundary effects to reach the experimental rapidity window. This conclusion holds specifically for the ALICE measurement with rapidity window Δη = 1.6 and total rapidity length η_tot = 8 at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.

## Method
The authors model the time evolution of conserved charge fluctuations in the hadronic medium using a diffusion master equation with reflecting boundaries at η = ±η_tot/2, describing particles hopping between discrete rapidity cells. They solve for the second and fourth order cumulants of the net charge Q(net)(Δη, τ) in a finite rapidity window, using initial conditions from an equilibrated free classical gas in a finite volume that respects global charge conservation. The key control parameter is the dimensionless diffusion length T = d(τ)/η_tot, where d(τ) is the average diffusion length of each Brownian particle.

## Key numbers
- Total rapidity length η_tot = 8, taken from the pseudo-rapidity dependence of charged-particle yield at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (ALICE)
- ALICE rapidity window Δη = 1.6, giving Δη/η_tot = 0.2
- The condition for GCC effects to be visible is (η_tot − Δη)/2 ≲ d(τ), where d(τ) is the diffusion length
- Second order cumulant ⟨(Q(net)(Δη))²⟩_c vanishes at Δη/η_tot = 1 (Δη = η_tot) for all T, as a trivial consequence of GCC

## Context
This work extends the analysis of global charge conservation effects on fluctuations, previously estimated by Bleicher (2000) under the assumption of final-state equilibration, to include nonequilibrium evolution in the hadronic stage. The authors show that the stronger suppression observed by ALICE cannot be explained by GCC alone, supporting the interpretation by Asakawa (2000) and Kitazawa (2013) that the suppression reflects survival of primordial fluctuations from the deconfined medium. The results enable quantitative separation of GCC effects from genuine nonequilibrium signals in higher-order cumulant measurements at RHIC and LHC.
