# Thermal production of charm quarks in heavy ion collisions at Future Circular Collider

> By solving the rate equation in an expanding quark-gluon plasma, we study thermal production of charm quarks in central Pb+Pb collisions at the Future Circular Collider. With the charm quark production cross section taken from the perturbative QCD at the next-to-leading order, we find that charm quark production from the quark-gluon plasma can be appreciable compared to that due to initial hard scattering between colliding nucleons.

# Thermal production of charm quarks in heavy ion collisions at Future Circular Collider

## Key claim
Thermal production of charm quarks from the quark-gluon plasma in central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 39 TeV can be appreciable compared to initial hard scattering, but the enhancement is conditional on the QGP formation time τ0, ranging from 21% to 45% for τ0 between 0.6 and 0.2 fm/c.

## Method
The authors solve a rate equation for charm quark number density in a boost-invariant, longitudinally expanding QGP, using the thermal charm production rate from next-to-leading-order pQCD cross sections parameterized from Zhang (2007). The QGP evolution is modeled via entropy conservation with an ideal gas plus resonance gas equation of state including a bag constant, and the initial charm yield is estimated from PYTHIA extrapolation of pp cross sections to 39 TeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature at center of QGP: approximately 935 MeV, dropping below 400 MeV after τ = 2.6 fm/c
- Transverse energy dET/dy = 6447 GeV for central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 39 TeV (with τr = 5 fm)
- Initial charm quark number from primordial collisions: dNc/dy = 48, based on extrapolated cross section dσppc̄c/dy = 1.57 mb at √s = 39 TeV
- Thermal charm enhancement relative to initial production: 21% to 45% for τ0 ranging from 0.6 to 0.2 fm/c
- For comparison, enhancement at √sNN = 5.5 TeV is 6% to 16%
- Ratio ρc/ρceq at center never exceeds 0.42 for τ < 2.6 fm, indicating annihilation is far less important than production

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of thermal heavy quark production at LHC energies by Levai (1994, 1997), Zhang (2007), and Uphoff (2010), who found only small effects at lower collision energies. The authors use the same rate equation approach as Zhang (2007) but apply it to the much higher energy of the proposed Future Circular Collider (√sNN = 39 TeV). The results enable future studies of how enhanced charm yields affect charmonium production and nuclear modification factors at FCC energies, as noted by Zhou (2016).
