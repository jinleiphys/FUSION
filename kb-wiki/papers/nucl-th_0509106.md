# Universal scaling of the rapidity dependent elliptic flow and the perfect fluid at RHIC

> The pseudo-rapidity dependence of the elliptic flow at various excitation energies measured by the PHOBOS Collaboration in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is one of the surprising results that has not been explained before in terms of hydrodynamical models. Here we show that these data are in agreement with theoretical predictions based on perfect fluid hydrodynamics. We also show that these PHOBOS data satisfy the universal scaling relation predicted by the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model, based on exact solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow measured by PHOBOS in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, which had resisted description by hydrodynamical models, is actually consistent with perfect fluid hydrodynamics when described by the Buda-Lund model, provided that the fit parameters are dominated by the space-time eccentricity and longitudinal source elongation, while other parameters (temperature, temperature gradient, transverse flow) can be fixed without affecting the fit quality.

## Method

The authors used the ellipsoidal generalization of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model, which describes an expanding ellipsoid with principal axes X, Y, Z and their expansion rates, to derive an analytic universal scaling law for elliptic flow v2 as a function of a scaling variable w. They performed a saddle-point integration over transverse momentum to obtain a formula directly comparable to the PHOBOS v2(η) data, then fitted this formula to the data at four center-of-mass energies (19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV) with the space-time eccentricity ε and longitudinal elongation Δη as the essential free parameters.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 19.6 GeV: ε = 0.294 ± 0.029, Δη = 1.70 ± 0.25, χ²/NDF = 1.8418/11
- At √sNN = 62.4 GeV: ε = 0.349 ± 0.008, Δη = 2.16 ± 0.05, χ²/NDF = 20.1388/13
- At √sNN = 130 GeV: ε = 0.376 ± 0.005, Δη = 2.46 ± 0.04, χ²/NDF = 34.7935/15
- At √sNN = 200 GeV: ε = 0.394 ± 0.006, Δη = 2.56 ± 0.04, χ²/NDF = 27.4865/15
- Fixed parameters for all fits: T0 = 175 MeV, a = 1.19, ut = 1.64

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model, previously successful in describing single particle spectra and HBT radii from BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR at RHIC (Csorgo 1995, Ster 2003, Mate 2003), to the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow. It contradicts the prior belief that hydrodynamical models could not describe the PHOBOS v2(η) excitation function (Back 2004), and it enables future tests of perfect fluid behavior at large pseudorapidities, showing that the perfect fluid extends far from mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
