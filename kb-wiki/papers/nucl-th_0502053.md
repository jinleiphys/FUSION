# Dynamical baryon resonances from chiral unitarity

> We report on the latests developments in the field of baryonic resonances generated from the meson-baryon interaction in coupled channels using a chiral unitary approach. The collection of resonances found in different strangeness and isospin sectors can be classified into SU(3) multiplets. The Lambda(1405) emerges as containing the effect of two poles of the scattering amplitude and various reactions that might preferentially select one or the other pole are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is not a single state but contains two poles of the scattering amplitude in the complex plane, and that different production reactions may preferentially select one pole over the other, leading to observable differences in the resonance's mass and width.

## Method
The authors construct a coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering model using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell amplitudes and unitarity constraints. The driving kernel is derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, with SU(3) coefficients encoded in the chiral Lagrangian, and the loop function $G_l$ is regularized either by a cut-off (630 MeV) or by dimensional regularization with subtraction constants. The model is applied to strangeness sectors $S=-1$, $S=-2$, and $S=0$, and the resulting poles of the T-matrix in the second Riemann sheet are identified with known baryon resonances.

## Key numbers
- $\Lambda(1405)$ pole positions: $z_R = 1390 - i66$ MeV (wider, couples mostly to $\pi\Sigma$) and $z_R = 1426 - i16$ MeV (narrower, couples mostly to $\bar{K}N$)
- $\Lambda(1670)$ pole: $z_R = 1680 - i20$ MeV, with coupling $\mid g_{K\Xi}\mid^2 = 12$
- $\Sigma(1620)$ pole: $z_R = 1579 - i274$ MeV, with coupling $\mid g_{K\Xi}\mid^2 = 12$
- $S=-2$ resonance pole: $z_R = 1605 - i66$ MeV, identified with $\Xi(1620)$
- $N(1535)$ total decay width: $\Gamma \simeq 110$ MeV, divided into $\Gamma_\pi \simeq 43$ MeV and $\Gamma_\eta \simeq 67$ MeV
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, with average meson decay constant $f = 1.123 f_\pi$
- Cut-off for $S=-1$ sector: 630 MeV
- Branching ratio $\gamma = \Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \pi^+ \Sigma^-) / \Gamma(K^- p \rightarrow \pi^- \Sigma^+)$: model gives 2.32, experiment gives $2.36 \pm 0.04$

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson-meson scattering (Oller and Oset, 1999) and meson-baryon scattering (Oller and Meissner, 2000) to systematically generate baryon resonances across all strangeness sectors. The two-pole nature of the $\Lambda(1405)$ was anticipated in earlier chiral models (Oller and Meissner, 2000; Garcia-Recio et al., 2002) and in the cloudy bag model (Fink et al., 1990). The classification of dynamically generated resonances into SU(3) multiplets (Jido et al., 2003) provides a unified framework for understanding these states. The results enable experimental searches for the two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles in reactions such as $K^- p \to \gamma \Lambda(1405)$, $\gamma p \to K^* \Lambda(1405)$, and $\pi^- p \to K^0 \pi \Sigma$, and allow assigning $J^P = 1/2^-$ to the $\Xi(1620)$.
