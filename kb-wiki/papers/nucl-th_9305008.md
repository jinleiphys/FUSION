# Effective Kaon Mass in Baryonic Matter and Kaon Condensation

> The effective kaon mass in dense baryonic matter is calculated based on PCAC, current algebra and the Weinberg smoothness hypothesis. The off-shell behavior of the K-N scattering amplitudes is treated consistently with PCAC, and the effects of the subthreshold K-N resonances are also included. The effective kaon mass is found to depend crucially on the K-N sigma term. Since the current estimates of K-N Sigma term are very uncertain, we discuss various scenarios treating K-N Sigma term as an input parameter; for certain values of K-N Sigma a collective mode of a hyperon-particle-nucleon-hole state appears at high densities, possibly leading to kaon condensation.

## Key claim

The effective kaon mass in dense baryonic matter depends crucially on the K-N sigma term, which is very uncertain; for certain values of this sigma term a collective mode of a hyperon-particle-nucleon-hole state appears at high densities, possibly leading to kaon condensation.

## Method

The authors calculate the effective kaon mass in dense baryonic matter using PCAC, current algebra, and the Weinberg smoothness hypothesis to treat the off-shell behavior of K-N scattering amplitudes consistently. They parametrize the smooth part of the Cheng-Dashen amplitudes as a second-order polynomial in kaon energy-momentum, determine coefficients from experimental data combined with PCAC constraints (including Adler's consistency condition and the sigma term at the Weinberg point), and include the effects of subthreshold K-N resonances via Born terms with derivative couplings. The in-medium kaon self-energy is computed in the impulse approximation treating nuclear matter as a Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

- The K-N sigma term estimates from the literature range from -370 +/- 110 MeV (Thompson) to 493 +/- 716 MeV (Di Claudio et al.), with values including 480(+110/-600) MeV (Reya) and 175 +/- 890 MeV (Martin and Violini)
- The kaon decay constant used is f_K = 113.6 MeV
- The s-wave scattering lengths are a_{K^+p} = -0.33 fm and a_{K^+n} = -0.15 fm (Martin)
- The K-N sigma term is treated as an unknown parameter ranging from -400 MeV to 600 MeV
- The coefficients B_p^{(+)}, B_n^{(+)}, C_p^{(-)}, C_n^{(-)}, D_p^{(+)}, D_p^{(-)}, D_n^{(+)}, D_n^{(-)} are given in units of m_K^{-3} (specific numerical values listed in Eq. M of the paper)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of s-wave pion condensation (Yabu et al., 1993) to the kaon system, supporting the critique by Delorme, Ericson and Ericson (1992) that meson-baryon scattering amplitudes from the Kaplan-Nelson approach lack known off-shell structures. The results demonstrate that the established positive value of the pi-N sigma term (45-60 MeV) becomes repulsive for off-shell pions near the soft-pion limit, precluding s-wave pion condensation, and similar behavior holds for K^+. The existence of subthreshold resonances in the K^- channel introduces hyperon-particle-nucleon-hole bound states analogous to Migdal's pi_s^+ states in the pi-N system, which may enable kaon condensation at high densities.
