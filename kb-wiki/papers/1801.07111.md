# Relativistic effects in ab-initio electron-nucleus scattering

> The electromagnetic responses obtained from Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations are based on realistic treatments of nuclear interactions and currents. The main limitations of this method comes from its nonrelativistic nature and its computational cost, the latter hampering the direct evaluation of the inclusive cross sections as measured by experiments. We extend the applicability of GFMC in the quasielastic region to intermediate momentum transfers by performing the calculations in a reference frame that minimizes nucleon momenta. Additional relativistic effects in the kinematics are accounted for employing the two-fragment model. In addition, we developed a novel algorithm, based on the concept of first-kind scaling, to compute the inclusive electromagnetic cross section of $^4$He through an accurate and reliable interpolation of the response functions. A very good agreement is obtained between theoretical and experimental cross sections for a variety of kinematical setups. This offers a promising prospect for the data analysis of neutrino-oscillation experiments that requires an accurate description of nuclear dynamics in which relativistic effects are fully accounted for.

# Relativistic effects in ab-initio electron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) approach, which is inherently nonrelativistic, can be extended to intermediate momentum transfers (up to |q|=700 MeV) by performing calculations in the active nucleon Breit (ANB) frame and using the two-fragment model for relativistic kinematics, but residual frame dependence in the transverse channel indicates that higher-order relativistic corrections in the current operator are still missing.

## Method

The authors computed the longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions of 4He using GFMC with the Argonne v18 NN potential and Illinois-7 three-nucleon force, then studied the frame dependence of these responses by boosting results from different reference frames back to the laboratory frame. They applied the two-fragment model to account for relativistic kinematics in a relativistically correct way, and developed a novel interpolation algorithm based on first-kind scaling using a nonrelativistic scaling variable ψ'_{nr} to compute inclusive double-differential cross sections from response functions calculated at discrete momentum transfers of 300, 400, 500, 600, and 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers studied: |q| = 300, 400, 500, 600, and 700 MeV for the response functions
- Initial electron beam energies for cross section comparisons: 0.3 GeV to 1.1 GeV (specifically 961 and 1080 MeV shown in figures)
- Fermi momentum used in scaling variable: p_F = 180 MeV
- Binding energy shift in scaling variable: E_s = 15 MeV
- At E_e = 961 and 1080 MeV with θ_e = 37.5°, Q^2 ≥ 3 GeV^2 at the quasielastic peak
- Longitudinal response at |q|=300 MeV shows good agreement between GFMC and LIT results; discrepancies become more pronounced at |q|=500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the GFMC approach of Lovato (2015, 2016) to higher momentum transfers by incorporating the relativistic treatment previously applied to 3He using the Lorentz Integral Transform method by Efros and collaborators (2005, 2010, 2011). The two-fragment model was originally introduced by Efros (2005) for realistic nuclear dynamics calculations. The scaling-based interpolation algorithm exploits the first-kind scaling features of GFMC responses analyzed by Rocco (2017). This enables ab-initio calculations of inclusive electron-4He cross sections needed for neutrino-oscillation experiments such as MINERvA, T2K, NOvA, and DUNE, where the relativistic Fermi gas model used in event generators has proven inadequate.
