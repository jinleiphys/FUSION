# Thermostatistics of deformed bosons and fermions

> Based on the q-deformed oscillator algebra, we study the behavior of the mean occupation number and its analogies with intermediate statistics and we obtain an expression in terms of an infinite continued fraction, thus clarifying successive approximations. In this framework, we study the thermostatistics of q-deformed bosons and fermions and show that thermodynamics can be built on the formalism of q-calculus. The entire structure of thermodynamics is preserved if ordinary derivatives are replaced by the use of an appropriate Jackson derivative and q-integral. Moreover, we derive the most important thermodynamic functions and we study the q-boson and q-fermion ideal gas in the thermodynamic limit.

# Thermostatistics of deformed bosons and fermions

## Key claim

The paper claims that the entire structure of thermodynamics is preserved for q-deformed bosons and fermions if ordinary derivatives and integrals are replaced by the Jackson derivative and q-integral, respectively, but this holds only under the assumption that the density matrix and partition function remain undeformed, which implicitly amounts to an unmodified Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy structure.

## Method

The authors start from the symmetric q-deformed oscillator algebra for bosons (κ=1) and fermions (κ=-1) with a real deformation parameter q, defined by the commutation relation cc† − κ q c†c = q−N. They compute the mean occupation number from the grand canonical partition function using the cyclic property of the trace, then express it as an infinite continued fraction via standard series-to-continued-fraction methods. Thermodynamic functions are derived by replacing ordinary derivatives with the Jackson derivative and ordinary integrals with q-integrals, and the specific heat is computed in the thermodynamic limit using the q-deformed functions h_n^κ(z,q).

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter q is real and the symmetric algebra is invariant under q ↔ q−1; numerical calculations are shown for q = 0.8, 0.7 (and their inverses 1/0.8, 1/0.7).
- The q-integral approximation is stated to be valid for 0.6 < q < 1.4.
- For q-bosons, the fugacity z_B must satisfy z_B < 1/q for q > 1 and z_B < 1 for q < 1; the stricter condition for the existence of the Jackson derivative gives z_B < z_q where z_q = q−2 for q > 1 and z_q = q² for q < 1.
- The specific heat C_v λ³/(g_κ V) is plotted as a function of fugacity for bosons above the critical condensation point and for fermions, with the modification increasing with the deformation parameter, especially in the fermion case.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of q-deformed thermostatistics (Lavagno and Swamy, 2000, 2002, 2007) by consistently employing q-integrals for the first time in thermodynamic calculations, rather than only q-derivatives. The paper contrasts its approach with nonextensive Tsallis statistics and superstatistics, where the deformation originates from generalized entropy assumptions rather than from deformed quantum algebras. The results enable future study of complex many-body systems with effective interactions, such as nuclear many-body interactions as discussed by Sviratcheva et al., and may connect to nonanalytic temperature behavior of specific heat in Fermi liquids beyond weak-coupling limits.
