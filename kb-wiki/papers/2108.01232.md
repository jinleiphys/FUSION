# Exposing minimal composition of Kohn-Sham theory and its extendability

> Reducing the many-fermion problem to a set of single-particle (s.p.) equations, the Kohn-Sham (KS) theory has provided a practical tool to implement \textit{ab initio} calculations of ground-state energies and densities in many-electron systems. There have been attempts to extend the KS theory so that it could describe other physical quantities, or it could be applied to other many-fermion systems. By generalizing and reformulating the KS theory in terms of the 1-body density matrix, we expose the minimal composition of the theory that enables the reduction of the many-fermion problem to the s.p. equations. Based on the reformulation, several basic issues are reconsidered. The $v$- and $N$-representabilities for the KS theory are distinguished from those for the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem. Criteria for the extendability of the KS theory are addressed.

## Key claim

By reformulating Kohn-Sham theory in terms of the 1-body density matrix, the paper exposes that the minimal composition enabling reduction of the many-fermion problem to single-particle equations requires: (i) all principal variables depend solely on the 1-body density matrix, (ii) the energy functional is partitioned into a regular part and an irregular part that is differentiable with respect to the density matrix though not necessarily with respect to the principal variables, and (iii) the irregular part is assumed representable without correlation functions.

## Method

The author generalizes and reformulates Kohn-Sham theory using the 1-body density matrix as the central object, distinguishing the Hohenberg-Kohn representability conditions from those specific to the Kohn-Sham scheme. The energy functional is partitioned into irregular and regular parts, with the irregular part assumed to depend only on the 1-body density matrix and to be differentiable with respect to it. A Kohn-Sham energy functional is then defined using auxiliary variables and Lagrange multipliers, from which the Kohn-Sham equation is derived via variation, yielding a single-particle Hamiltonian whose diagonalization produces Kohn-Sham orbitals and an idempotent density matrix.

## Key numbers

* The Pauli principle imposes the condition \(0 \leq \varrho_{ii} \leq 1\) for any representation of the 1-body density matrix.
* The particle-number condition is \(\mathrm{tr}(\varrho) = N\), where the trace is taken in the single-particle space.
* The idempotency condition \(\varrho^2 = \varrho\) characterizes states corresponding to a single Slater determinant.
* For the local density \(n(\mathbf{r})\), the \(N\)-representability condition requires \(n(\mathbf{r}) \geq 0\) and \(\int d^3r\, n(\mathbf{r}) = N\).

## Context

This work extends the foundational analysis of Kohn-Sham theory beyond the original formulation by Kohn and Sham (1965) and the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (1964), addressing the representability issues raised by Coleman and the regularity problems noted by Capelle and Vignale (2001) and Kvaal et al. (2014). The reformulation provides criteria for judging whether the Kohn-Sham approach can be extended to describe additional physical quantities or applied to other many-fermion systems such as atomic nuclei, where self-consistent mean-field calculations using Skyrme interactions (Skyrme, 1959) and Gogny interactions have been reinterpreted in a DFT context.
