# Searching for 4$α$ linear-chain structure in excited states of $^{16}$O with a covariant density functional theory

> A study of 4$α$ linear-chain structure in high-lying collective excitation states of $^{16}$O with a covariant density functional theory is presented. The low-spin states are obtained by configuration mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected quadrupole deformed mean-field states with generator coordinate method. The high-spin states are determined by cranking calculations. These two calculations are based on the same energy density functional PC-PK1. We have found a rotational band at low-spin with the dominated intrinsic configuration considered to be the one that 4$α$ clusters stay along a common axis. The strongly deformed rod shape also appears in the high-spin region with the angular momentum $13-18\hbar$; however whether the state is pure $4α$ linear chain or not is less obvious than that in the low-spin states.

## Key claim

The authors find a rotational band in low-spin states of ¹⁶O whose dominant intrinsic configuration is consistent with four α clusters aligned along a common axis, and a strongly deformed rod shape also appears in high-spin states with angular momentum 13–18 ħ; however, the paper hedges that whether the high-spin state is a pure 4α linear chain is less obvious than for the low-spin states.

## Method

The low-spin states were obtained by configuration mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected quadrupole deformed mean-field states using the generator coordinate method (GCM+PNAMP), based on the covariant density functional PC-PK1. The high-spin states were determined by cranking calculations with the same energy density functional, solving the Dirac equation in a rotating frame with a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field state with 4α linear-chain structure appears at β = 3.6 with excitation energy 39.1 MeV; after rotational symmetry restoration this energy reduces to 31.5 MeV, and after configuration mixing the 0⁺ state excitation energy is 29.6 MeV.
- The GCM+PNAMP calculation predicts the 0⁺, 2⁺, 4⁺, and 6⁺ states of the linear-chain candidate band at excitation energies 29.6, 30.4, 32.0, and 34.8 MeV, respectively.
- The rms charge radii of these linear-chain candidate states are around 3.9 fm, giving a ratio R_c^J / R_c^(g.s.) ≈ 1.4.
- The intraband E2 transition strengths for the linear-chain candidate band are 561 e²fm⁴ (2⁺ → 0⁺), 874 e²fm⁴ (4⁺ → 2⁺), and 663 e²fm⁴ (6⁺ → 4⁺).
- In the high-spin region, cranking calculations yield convergent solutions with rotational frequencies ω between 2.75 and 4.00 MeV, corresponding to angular momenta from 12.6 ħ to 18.0 ħ.
- The spin-orbit interaction energy SO1 for the configuration at β = 3.6 is about 3.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent mean-field studies of 4α linear-chain structure in ¹⁶O, including relativistic mean-field calculations by Arumugam (2005) and Liu (2012) that found a hyperdeformed state at β ≈ 3.6 with excitation energy 37 MeV, and non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by Bender (2003) that found a 0⁺ state at 32 MeV with 8p-8h character. The results also connect to cranking calculations by Ichikawa (2011) using Skyrme forces, which suggested a linear-chain configuration in the angular momentum range 13–18 ħ with moment of inertia around 60–80 keV and band-head energy 38 MeV. The present work enables future studies of whether the relativistic energy density functional, with its deeper single-nucleon potentials, systematically predicts more pronounced cluster structures than non-relativistic functionals, and whether the linear-chain states found here correspond to the experimentally suggested band at 16.8 MeV (Chevallier 1967) or to the ¹²C(0₂⁺)+α structure proposed by Ohkubo (2010) and Funaki (2012).
