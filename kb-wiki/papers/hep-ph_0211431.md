# A Note on the J/psi Strong Couplings

> In this note we present an evaluation of the couplings JD(*)D(*) and JD(*)D(*)pi in the Constituent Quark Model. These couplings are a crucial ingredient in the calculation of cross sections for the processes pi J/psi -> D(*)Dbar(*), an important background for the J/psi suppression signal in quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors compute the strong couplings \(J D^{(*)} D^{(*)}\) and \(J D^{(*)} D^{(*)}\pi\) using the Constituent Quark Model (CQM), but caution that the results for the \(J D^{(*)} D^{(*)}\pi\) couplings are valid only in the soft-pion limit (pion momenta up to a few hundred MeV), while the threshold for charmed meson pair production requires pion momenta of 700–1000 MeV, so a form factor must be introduced and the computed cross sections should be considered a lower bound.

## Method

The authors evaluate the \(J D^{(*)} D^{(*)}\) vertex via a Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) ansatz, where the left-hand side is a quark-loop diagram in the CQM that yields the Isgur-Wise function \(\xi(\omega)\), and the right-hand side couples the \(J/\psi\) to the charm current using the decay constant \(f_J = 0.405 \pm 0.014\) GeV. For the \(J D^{(*)} D^{(*)}\pi\) couplings, they similarly use a VMD ansatz with a soft-pion insertion on the light quark line, computing a form factor \(\xi^\pi(\omega)\) in the soft-pion limit. The model incorporates heavy quark symmetry (HQET) and chiral \(SU(2)\) symmetry, with ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.25\) GeV, infrared cutoff \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV, and light constituent mass \(m = 0.3\) GeV.

## Key numbers

*   \(g_{JDD} = 8.0 \pm 0.5\) (dimensionless) for on-shell \(D\) mesons, extracted from the flat region of \(g_{JDD}(p_1^2, p_2^2, p^2)\) for \(p^2\) in the range \((0, 4)\) GeV\(^2\).
*   \(g_{JDD\pi} = 125 \pm 15\) GeV\(^{-3}\) for all mesons on the mass shell, obtained by extrapolating the smooth behavior to \(p^2 = m_{J/\psi}^2\).
*   The \(J/\psi\) decay constant used is \(f_J = 0.405 \pm 0.014\) GeV.
*   The parameter \(\Delta_H = m_D - m_c\) is taken in the range \(0.3\)–\(0.5\) GeV.
*   The constant \(Z_H = 2.36\) GeV\(^{-1}\) is used for the plot of \(g_{JDD}\).
*   The polar-diagram estimate of \(g_{JDD\pi}\) gives approximately 393, 196, and 98 GeV\(^{-3}\) for pion momenta \(|q_\pi| = 50, 100, 200\) MeV respectively, using \(g_{D^*D\pi} = 2m_D/f_\pi g\) with \(g = 0.59 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.07\).
*   The form factor introduced to extend the model beyond the soft-pion limit uses a mass parameter \(m_\chi\) in the range 400–600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(J/\psi\) absorption in hot hadronic matter (Cina et al., various years) by providing a new evaluation of the strong couplings needed for the cross sections of \(\pi J/\psi \to D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}\), which are an important background to the \(J/\psi\) suppression signal in quark-gluon plasma. The authors criticize earlier estimates based on \(SU(4)\) symmetry (which conflicts with Heavy Quark Effective Theory) and Vector Meson Dominance (which requires a large extrapolation from \(p^2=0\) to \(m_{J/\psi}^2\)), and note that their result for \(g_{JDD}\) agrees with a QCD Sum Rules calculation (Nardulli et al., 2002). The CQM approach enables a controlled treatment of the off-shell behavior in \(p^2\), \(p_1^2\), and \(p_2^2\), and the computed couplings can be used in future calculations of the full absorption cross sections, though the authors note that the direct \(J D^{(*)} D^{(*)}\pi\) couplings are suppressed relative to polar-diagram contributions in the soft-pion region.
