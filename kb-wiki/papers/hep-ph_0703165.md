# The Status of Vud

> The best value for Vud comes from superallowed 0+ to 0+ nuclear beta transitions, of which thirteen have now been measured with high precision. The current status of these measurements is described, and the result compared with that of measurements from neutron decay and pion beta decay. Future prospects for improvement are discussed.

# The Status of Vud

## Key claim

Superallowed 0+ to 0+ nuclear beta transitions currently provide the most precise determination of Vud, but the dominant uncertainty now comes from the theoretical "inner" radiative correction (ΔVR) rather than from nuclear-structure-dependent corrections or experimental errors; the nuclear result is not yet limited by experiment but will ultimately be limited by the same ΔVR uncertainty that affects all three beta-decay routes.

## Method

The author compiled world data on thirteen superallowed 0+ to 0+ nuclear beta transitions, incorporating more than 125 independent measurements spanning four decades, and applied calculated radiative corrections (δR', δNS) and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections (δC) to extract corrected Ft values. For comparison, the author also evaluated world data for neutron decay (mean life τn and coupling-constant ratio λ) and pion beta decay (branching ratio and mean life) using the same inclusion criteria as for the nuclear decays.

## Key numbers

- Average corrected Ft value for thirteen superallowed transitions: 3073.9(8) s
- Vud from nuclear decays: 0.97378(27)
- Vud from neutron decay (all data included): 0.9766(20)
- Vud from neutron decay (rejecting Serebrov et al. result): 0.9747(18)
- Vud from pion beta decay (using theoretical π+→e+νe(γ) branching ratio): 0.9751(27)
- Vud from pion beta decay (using experimental π+→e+νe(γ) branching ratio): 0.9732(32)
- Constancy of GV confirmed to 1.3 parts in 10^4
- Limit on scalar current: |CS/CV| ≤ 0.0013
- Uncertainty budget for nuclear Vud: ΔVR contributes ±0.00018, nuclear-structure corrections contribute ±0.00015, experimental uncertainties contribute ±0.00007
- Neutron mean life (all data): τn = 882.0(15) s with χ²/N = 5.4
- Neutron coupling-constant ratio: λ = -1.2690(28) with χ²/N = 2.6
- Pion beta-decay branching ratio: 1.040(6) × 10⁻⁸
- Pion mean life: τπ = 2.6033(5) × 10⁻⁸ s

## Context

This work updates the 2005 survey by Hardy and Towner (2005) with eight new publications from 2005-2006, and incorporates the improved calculation of the inner radiative correction by Marciano and Sirlin (2006). The nuclear result is compared with neutron decay measurements (surveyed by Towner and Hardy in 2003) and the PIBETA measurement of pion beta decay by Pocanic et al. (2004). The consistency of corrected Ft values across nuclei from A=10 to A=74 validates the nuclear-structure-dependent corrections and confirms the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis. Future improvements require a more precise calculation of ΔVR, which is common to all three beta-decay routes and currently limits the nuclear result.
