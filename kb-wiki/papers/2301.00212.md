# How Do Constraints of Nuclear Symmetry Energy Reconcile with Different Models?

> By simultaneously describing the data of isospin sensitive nucleonic flow and pion observables, such as $v_2^n/v_2^{ch}$ and $π^-/π^+$, with ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, we got the symmetry energy at flow and pion characteristic densities which are $S(1.2ρ_0)=34\pm 4$ MeV and $S(1.5ρ_0)=36\pm 8$ MeV. Within the uncertainties, the constraints of symmetry energy at characteristic densities are consistent with the previous constraints by using other transport models. The consistency suggests that the reliable constraints on symmetry energy should be presented at the characteristic density of isospin sensitive observables. By using the constraints of symmetry energy at two different characteristic densities, the extrapolated value of $L$ is provided. Within $2σ$ uncertainty, the extrapolated value of $L$ is in $5-70$ MeV which is consistent with the recent combination analysis from PREX-II and astrophyiscs data. Further, the calculations with the constrained parameter sets can describe the data of charged pion multiplicities from S$π$RIT collaboration.

## Key claim

By simultaneously describing isospin-sensitive nucleonic flow and pion observables with the UrQMD model, the authors obtain symmetry energy constraints at characteristic densities that are consistent with previous constraints from other transport models, provided the symmetry energy is evaluated at the characteristic density of each observable rather than extrapolated to normal density.

## Method

The authors used the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 0.4A GeV and Sn+Sn collisions at 0.27A GeV, with 200,000 events per system. They compared calculations to sixteen experimental observables from the ASY-EOS, FOPI, and SπRIT collaborations, including the elliptic flow ratio v₂ⁿ/v₂ᶜʰ, charged pion multiplicities M(π), and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio, performing χ² analyses to determine the favored symmetry energy parameters at the characteristic densities probed by flow (1.2ρ₀) and pions (1.5ρ₀).

## Key numbers

- S(1.2ρ₀) = 34 ± 4 MeV (symmetry energy at flow characteristic density)
- S(1.5ρ₀) = 36 ± 8 MeV (symmetry energy at pion characteristic density)
- Extrapolated slope parameter L is in the range 5–70 MeV (within 2σ uncertainty)
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter K₀ = 231 MeV (fixed in the model)
- The effective mass m*/m = 0.635 (for the Hama-type momentum-dependent interaction used)

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on the symmetry energy from the ASY-EOS collaboration (Russotto 2016) and FOPI collaboration (FOPI 2010) by simultaneously analyzing both flow and pion observables within a single transport model framework. It addresses the model dependence highlighted by the SπRIT collaboration (2021) and the transport model evaluation project (Zhang 2018, Akira 2019, Maria 2021). The consistency of constraints at characteristic densities across different transport models enables more reliable extrapolation of the slope parameter L, with results consistent with the combined analysis from PREX-II and astrophysics data.
