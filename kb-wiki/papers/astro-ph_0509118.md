# Neutrons and antiprotons in ultrahigh energy cosmic rays

> The neutron fraction in the very high energy cosmic rays near the Greisen-Zatsepin-Kuzmin (GZK) cutoff energy is analyzed by taking into account the time dilation effect of the neutron decays and also the pion photoproduction behaviors above the GZK cutoff. We predict a non-trivial neutron fraction above the GZK cutoff and a negligibly small neutron fraction below. However, there should be a large antiproton fraction in the high energy cosmic rays below the GZK cutoff in several existing models for the observed cosmic-ray events above and near the GZK cutoff. Such a large antiproton fraction can manifest itself by the muon charge ratio $μ^+/μ^-$ in the collisions of the primary nucleon cosmic rays with the atmosphere, if there is no neutron contribution. We suggest to use the muon charge ratio as one of the information to detect the composition of the primary cosmic rays near or below the GZK cutoff.

## Key claim

The authors predict a non-trivial neutron fraction in cosmic rays above the GZK cutoff energy due to protons and neutrons converting into each other via charged pion photoproduction with the cosmic microwave background, but this prediction depends on the assumption that the nucleon sources are at distances comparable to or greater than the GZK zone (tens of Mpc). Below the GZK cutoff, the neutron fraction is negligibly small, which the authors use to argue that a measured muon charge ratio can instead reveal a large antiproton fraction from models such as Z-bursts.

## Method

The authors calculate the mean free paths for neutron beta decay (using relativistic time dilation) and for charged pion photoproduction on the cosmic microwave background, using cross sections derived from isospin amplitudes in the chiral quark model. They then compute the equilibrium neutron/proton ratio for nucleons above the GZK cutoff by balancing the conversion rates between protons and neutrons via the reactions p + γ_CMB → π⁺ + n and n + γ_CMB → π⁻ + p. For the antiproton detection proposal, they estimate the muon charge ratio μ⁺/μ⁻ from primary cosmic rays using a parton-model fragmentation approach, where the quark flavor content of the incident hadron determines the relative production of π⁺/π⁻ and K⁺/K⁻ that decay into muons.

## Key numbers

- Neutron beta-decay mean free path at the pion production threshold (1.10 × 10²⁰ eV): l_n = 3.12 × 10²⁴ cm = 1.01 Mpc.
- Neutron beta-decay mean free path at the Δ resonance threshold (2.52 × 10²⁰ eV): l_n = 7.11 × 10²⁴ cm = 2.30 Mpc.
- Mean free path for proton interaction via p + γ_CMB → π⁺ + n: λ_p = 1.21 × 10²⁵ cm = 3.92 Mpc, using σ(p γ → π⁺ n) = 200 μb.
- Mean free path for neutron interaction via n + γ_CMB → π⁻ + p: λ_n = 9.04 × 10²⁴ cm = 2.93 Mpc.
- Equilibrium neutron/proton ratio above the GZK cutoff: λ_n^eff / λ_p ≈ 0.19 at the pion production threshold and 0.33 at the Δ production threshold.
- Muon charge ratio estimates from parton model: μ⁺/μ⁻ ≈ 1.7 for proton, ≈ 0.7 for neutron, ≈ 0.6 for antiproton, and ≈ 1.4 for antineutron.
- Empirical expectation cited: μ⁺/μ⁻ ≈ 1.66 for proton and ≈ 0.695 for neutron (Adair).
- Number density of cosmic microwave background photons: n_γ = 413 photons per cm³.
- Mean energy per CMB photon: ε_γ = 6.35 × 10⁻⁴ eV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of neutron propagation in ultrahigh energy cosmic rays, previously considered by Weiler and others, by combining the time dilation effect with the proton/neutron interconversion via charged pion photoproduction. The authors apply their results to the Z-burst model (Weiler, Fargion and Salis, Gelmini and Kusenko), as well as models using relic metastable superheavy particles (X) and topological defects (Bhattacharjee 1998), which all predict significant antinucleon content. The proposal to use the muon charge ratio to detect antiprotons below the GZK cutoff builds on earlier work by Adair and on muon ratio measurements from experiments such as Akeno and KASCADE, and it suggests a new observable for next-generation cosmic ray facilities.
