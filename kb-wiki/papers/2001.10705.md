# Review of proton and nuclear shape fluctuations at high energy

> Determining the inner structure of protons and nuclei in terms of their fundamental constituents has been one of the main tasks of high energy nuclear and particle physics experiments. This quest started as a mapping of the (average) parton densities as a function of longitudinal momentum fraction and resolution scale. Recently, the field has progressed to more differential imaging, where one important development is the description of the event-by-event quantum fluctuations in the wave function of the colliding hadron. In this Review, recent developments on the extraction of proton and nuclear transverse geometry with event-by-event fluctuations from collider experiments at high energy is presented. The importance of this fundamentally interesting physics in other collider experiments like in studies of the properties of the Quark Gluon Plasma is also illustrated.

## Key claim

This review argues that event-by-event quantum fluctuations in the proton and nuclear wave functions can be extracted from collider data, but the extraction is model-dependent: multiple different fluctuating geometries (such as hot-spot or flux-tube pictures) can reproduce the same HERA data on coherent and incoherent vector meson production, meaning the data constrain the variance of fluctuations but not the unique underlying configuration.

## Method

The review synthesizes results from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, where the dipole-target scattering amplitude is computed using the IPsat parametrization or by solving classical Yang-Mills equations with sampled color charges. Coherent diffractive cross sections (target remains intact) are obtained by averaging the scattering amplitude over target configurations, while incoherent cross sections (target dissociates) are obtained from the variance of the amplitude, as derived from the Good-Walker formalism. The analysis focuses on exclusive vector meson production (especially J/ψ) in deep inelastic scattering at HERA, comparing model calculations with measured t-distributions.

## Key numbers

- HERA electron-proton collisions at center-of-mass energy √s = 318 GeV, yielding photon-proton energies W = 30–300 GeV
- For J/ψ photoproduction at W = 75 GeV, the optimal hot-spot parameters are B_q = 0.7 GeV⁻² (hot-spot size) and B_qc = 3.3 GeV⁻² (width of hot-spot position distribution), with Bjorken-x ≈ 10⁻³
- The coherent cross section t-slope B_p is related to the transverse proton radius as √(2B_p); the first diffractive minimum occurs at t_first dip ~ 1/R² where R is the target size
- The skewedness correction to the cross section is numerically up to ~40%; the real-part correction is ~10% at the cross section level

## Context

This review extends the traditional approach of extracting average parton distribution functions from HERA data (Aaron 2009, Abramowicz 2015) to differential imaging of event-by-event fluctuations. It builds on the Good-Walker formalism (Good 1960) and the IPsat parametrization (Kowalski 2003) fitted to HERA data (Rezaeian 2012, Mantysaari 2018). The work enables future studies at the Electron-Ion Collider (Accardi 2012) and improves interpretation of Quark-Gluon Plasma signatures in small collision systems, where the initial-state geometry is controlled by single-proton fluctuations rather than nuclear shapes.
