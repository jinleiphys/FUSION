# Nucleon form factors for the elastic electron-deuteron scattering at high momentum transfer

> The reaction of the elastic electron-deuteron scattering at high momentum transfer is investigated within the Bethe-Salpeter approach. The relativistic covariant Graz II separable kernel of nucleon-nucleon interactions is used to analyze the deuteron structure functions, form factors and tensor of polarization components. The modern data for the electromagmetic nucleons structure from the double polarization experiments as well as some other models of the nucleon form factors are considered.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter approach with the covariant Graz II separable kernel can describe elastic electron-deuteron scattering up to momentum transfer of 10 (GeV/c)^2, but the choice of nucleon electromagnetic form factor model significantly affects the predicted deuteron structure functions and form factors, especially above Q^2 = 3 (GeV/c)^2 where no single model matches all available experimental data.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron bound state using the relativistic covariant Graz II separable kernel of nucleon-nucleon interactions. They calculated the deuteron electromagnetic current in the relativistic impulse approximation, performing a Wick rotation to evaluate the loop integrals while accounting for pole contributions from the analytic structure of the vertex functions above threshold values Q^2_k. Four different models of nucleon electromagnetic form factors were used as input: the dipole fit (DFF), a modified dipole fit (MDFF1) incorporating JLab polarization data and the Galster parametrization, the nine-resonance Unitary and Analytic (U&A) model, and the relativistic harmonic oscillator model (RHOM).

## Key numbers

- Calculations were performed up to Q^2 = 10 (GeV/c)^2
- Threshold values for the poles of the Graz II kernel vertex functions: Q^2_0 = 0.004 (GeV/c)^2, Q^2_1 = 1.182 (GeV/c)^2, Q^2_2 = 1.736 (GeV/c)^2, Q^2_3 = 3.915 (GeV/c)^2, Q^2_4 = 5.965 (GeV/c)^2
- At Q^2 = 10 (GeV/c)^2, the difference between the U&A model and RHOM results for the structure function A(q^2) reaches about 2 orders of magnitude
- The U&A model shows a node in the structure function B(q^2) at approximately Q^2 = 6.5 (GeV/c)^2, attributed to a node in the proton electric form factor at Q^2 ~ 11-12 (GeV/c)^2
- The MDFF1 model shows a node in the charge form factor F_C(q^2) at Q^2 = 8.5 (GeV/c)^2
- For the structure function B(q^2) at Q^2 = 4.5 (GeV/c)^2, the contribution from poles of the separable kernel becomes crucial
- For the structure function A(q^2), pole contributions hardly reach 25% over the whole interval up to 10 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends previous Bethe-Salpeter calculations by Bondarenko (2008, 2010) on deuteron electrodisintegration and by Bekzhanov (2013) on elastic electron-deuteron scattering, now incorporating the analytic structure of vertex functions at high momentum transfer. The study contrasts with the earlier work of Rupp (1989) by investigating new parametrizations of the proton electric form factor. The results enable future calculations using modern separable kernels of nucleon-nucleon interaction and suggest the need to include relativistic P-states in the deuteron, two-body interaction currents, and off-mass shell nucleon effects to improve agreement with experimental data.
