# Continuum Coupling and Single-Nucleon Overlap Integrals

> The presence of a particle continuum, both of a resonant and non-resonant character, can significantly impact spectroscopic properties of weakly bound nuclei and excited nuclear states close to, and above, the particle emission threshold. In the framework of the continuum shell model in the complex momentum-plane, the so-called Gamow Shell Model, we discuss salient effects of the continuum coupling on the one-neutron overlap integrals and the associated spectroscopic factors in neutron-rich helium and oxygen nuclei. In particular, we demonstrate a characteristic near-threshold energy dependence of the spectroscopic factors for different l-waves. We show also that the realistic radial overlap functions, which are needed for the description of transfer reactions, can be generated by single-particle wave functions of the appropriately chosen complex potential.

# Continuum Coupling and Single-Nucleon Overlap Integrals

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the non-resonant continuum has a crucial impact on spectroscopic factors in weakly bound and unbound nuclei, producing a characteristic near-threshold cusp that follows the Wigner threshold law; this effect is absent in standard closed quantum system shell model calculations and can be as large as the effects from long-range correlations and short-range tensor forces.

## Method

The authors employ the Gamow Shell Model (GSM), a continuum shell model formulated in the complex momentum plane using the Berggren completeness relation, to calculate one-neutron overlap integrals and spectroscopic factors for neutron-rich helium and oxygen isotopes. They compare full GSM results with two simplified schemes: the pole approximation (GSM-p), which omits non-resonant scattering states, and the harmonic oscillator shell model (HO-SM), representing the standard closed quantum system approach. The depth of the Woods-Saxon potential is varied to study the dependence of spectroscopic factors on the one-neutron separation energy across the particle emission threshold.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the [$^5$He(g.s.)⊗p₃/₂]⁰⁺ channel in $^6$He(g.s.) shows a cusp at the one-neutron emission threshold in $^5$He (e_{p₃/₂}=0), with a discontinuous derivative at this point
- Below the 1n threshold in $^5$He, the anomalous component of the spectroscopic factor behaves as (-e_{ℓj})^{ℓ-1/2}; above threshold, the real part behaves as (e_{ℓj})^{ℓ+1/2} and the imaginary part as (e_{ℓj})^{ℓ-1/2}
- For the helium calculations, the maximal momentum value on the contour is k_max=3.27 fm⁻¹, discretized with up to 60 points, yielding precision on energies and widths better than 0.1 keV
- For the oxygen calculations, the maximal momentum on the d₃/₂ contour is k_max=1.5 fm⁻¹, discretized with 45 points, yielding precision on energies and widths of approximately 0.1 keV
- The surface Gaussian interaction for helium uses coupling constants V₀⁽⁰⁾=-403 MeV fm³ and V₀⁽²⁾=-392 MeV fm³, fitted to reproduce ground-state binding energies of $^6$He and $^7$He
- The surface delta interaction for oxygen uses V₀=-700 MeV fm³, fitted to reproduce the two-neutron separation energy of $^{18}$O
- A difference of approximately 25% between spectroscopic factors in HO-SM and GSM-p is observed for e_{p₃/₂}>0

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Michel et al., 2007) of threshold anomalies for ℓ=1 neutron waves to ℓ=2 waves, and compares full GSM results with the pole approximation to isolate the influence of non-resonant continuum. The paper builds on the Gamow Shell Model framework developed in earlier work (Michel et al., 2002; Michel et al., 2004) and connects to the classic Thomas-Ehrman shift and Wigner cusp phenomenon (Wigner, 1948). The results enable improved modeling of transfer reactions for weakly bound and unbound nuclei by showing that realistic radial overlap functions can be generated by single-particle wave functions of an appropriately chosen complex potential that reproduces the complex Q-value of the reaction.
