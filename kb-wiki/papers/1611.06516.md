# A gauge invariant Debye mass for the complex heavy-quark potential

> The concept of a screening mass is a powerful tool to simplify the intricate physics of in-medium test charges surrounded by light charge carriers. While it has been successfully used to describe electromagnetic properties, its definition and computation in QCD is plagued by questions of gauge invariance and the presence of non-perturbative contributions from the magnetic sector. Here we present a recent alternative definition of a gauge invariant Debye mass parameter following closely the original idea of Debye and Hueckel. Our test charges are a static heavy quark-antiquark pair whose complex potential and its in-medium modification can be extracted using lattice QCD. By combining in a generalized Gauss-Law the non-perturbative aspects of quark binding with a perturbative ansatz for the medium effects, we succeed to describe the lattice values of the potential with a single temperature dependent parameter, in turn identified with a Debye mass. We find that its behavior, as evaluated in a recent quenched lattice QCD study, deviates from that in other approaches, such as hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory or from electric field correlators on the lattice. In particular around the phase transition its values tend to zero significantly faster than at weak-coupling.

# A gauge invariant Debye mass for the complex heavy-quark potential

## Key claim
The authors define a gauge invariant Debye mass parameter by combining a generalized Gauss-Law for the non-perturbative vacuum potential with a perturbative (leading-order hard-thermal-loop) permittivity ansatz for the medium, and find that its temperature dependence extracted from quenched lattice QCD deviates from other approaches, in particular, it tends to zero significantly faster around the phase transition than weak-coupling predictions.

## Method
The static heavy-quark potential (real and imaginary parts) is extracted from quenched lattice QCD simulations using a Bayesian spectral reconstruction of the Wilson loop. The vacuum potential at T=105 MeV is parametrized by a Cornell-type ansatz (Coulomb plus linear plus constant). The in-medium modification is modeled by a generalized Gauss-Law for an auxiliary electric field, with the medium introduced via a leading-order hard-thermal-loop permittivity containing a single temperature-dependent Debye mass parameter m_D. This m_D is fixed by fitting the real part of the potential at each temperature above T_C, and then used to postdict the imaginary part.

## Key numbers
- Lattice parameters: β=6.1, ξ_r=4, a_s=0.097 fm, physical box size L=3.1 fm, N_s=32, N_τ ∈ [20…72].
- Temperature range: T ∈ [105…419] MeV = [0.39…1.4] T_C, with T_C=290 MeV.
- Vacuum potential fit parameters (α_S, σ, c) fixed at T=105 MeV.
- Non-perturbative corrections to the perturbative Debye mass expansion: κ_1 = 2.53 ± 0.25, κ_2 = −1.41 ± 0.15 (using scale μ=πT and continuum-corrected m_D).
- Continuum correction uses T_C^phys = 0.271 GeV and σ_phys = 0.173 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the concept of a Debye mass from the original Debye-Hückel theory (Debye and Hückel, 1923) to QCD via the complex heavy-quark potential, going beyond earlier model-potential studies using color singlet free energies (Maezawa 2007, Digal 2005). It contrasts with results from hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (Laine 2006, Beraudo 2007) and from electric field correlators in EQCD (Kajantie 1997, Hart 2000, Laine 2009), finding that m_D extracted from the lattice potential tends to zero faster near T_C and may rise to larger values at high T. The approach enables a systematic connection between in-medium quarkonium binding and a single gauge-invariant screening parameter, with ongoing work extending to full QCD with N_f=2+1 asqtad lattices (Burnier 2015, 2016) and HISQ ensembles.
