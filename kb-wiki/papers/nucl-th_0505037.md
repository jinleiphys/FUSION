# Matter formed at the BNL relativistic heavy ion collider

> We suggest that the "new form of matter" found just above $T_c$ by RHIC is made up of tightly bound quark-antiquark pairs, essentially 32 chirally restored (more precisely, nearly massless) mesons of the quantum numbers of $π$, $σ$, $ρ$ and $a_1$. Taking the results of lattice gauge simulations (LGS) for the color Coulomb potential from the work of the Bielefeld group and feeding this into a relativistic two-body code, after modifying the heavy-quark lattice results so as to include the velocity-velocity interaction, all ground-state eigenvalues of the 32 mesons go to zero at $T_c$ just as they do from below $T_c$ as predicted by the vector manifestation (VM in short) of hidden local symmetry. This could explain the rapid rise in entropy up to $T_c$ found in LGS calculations. We argue that how the dynamics work can be understood from the behavior of the hard and soft glue.

# Matter Formed at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

## Key claim

The "new form of matter" found just above the critical temperature $T_c$ at RHIC is composed of 32 tightly bound, chirally restored (nearly massless) quark-antiquark pairs with the quantum numbers of $\pi$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $a_1$ mesons, rather than a conventional quark-gluon plasma. The authors argue that the transition across $T_c$ is "continuous" in the sense that the same degrees of freedom that emerge from below $T_c$ are found just above $T_c$, though in a disguised form, and that this structure explains the rapid rise in entropy found in lattice gauge simulations.

## Method

The authors take results from lattice gauge simulations (LGS) by the Bielefeld group for the color Coulomb potential, originally calculated for heavy quarks, and modify them to include the velocity-velocity (magnetic) interaction appropriate for light quarks. They feed this potential into a relativistic two-body code based on a hydrogenic model derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, using thermal quark masses from lattice calculations (approximately 1 GeV at $T = 1.5 T_c$) as input. The calculation treats the 32 meson states as degenerate and determines their ground-state eigenvalues as a function of temperature, with the key result that all masses go to zero at $T_c$.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: $T_c = 175$ MeV (updated from earlier value of 140 MeV used in prior work)
- Thermal quark mass at $T = 1.5 T_c$: $m_q \sim 1$ GeV, with similar large masses at nearby temperatures
- Binding energy of quark-antiquark pairs just above $T_c$: approximately 2 GeV, bringing the sum of two thermal quark masses ($2 m_q \sim 2$ GeV) to zero
- RMS radius of bound states just above $T_c$: $\lesssim 0.4$ fm
- Effective coupling constant as $T \to T_c$: $\alpha_s \to \sim 2$; with Casimir factor (4/3) and velocity factor (2), effective coupling $\to \sim 16/3$, corresponding to $g \to \sim 8$
- Width for $\rho \to \pi \pi$ just above $T_c$: estimated as 380 MeV
- At $T = 1.4 T_c$ in quenched LGS: binding energy 0.15 GeV, thermal mass $m_q \sim 1.2$ GeV
- Bare quark mass for $\rho$ meson: $\bar{m} \sim 5$ MeV
- Initial temperature at which RHIC matter is formed: $\sim 2 T_c$

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991), which predicted that hadron masses decrease proportionally to the soft gluon condensate and go to zero at $T_c$, and the vector manifestation (VM) of hidden local symmetry (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003). It builds on lattice gauge calculations by the Bielefeld group (Karsch, Petreczky, Kaczmarek, Zantow) and on the bound-state calculations of Park, Lee, and Brown (2005). The paper contradicts the simple picture of a weakly interacting quark-gluon plasma above $T_c$, instead proposing that the matter is a strongly interacting liquid of massless mesons. It enables future lattice measurements to verify or falsify the prediction that vector meson masses drop to zero while widths vanish more rapidly near $T_c$, and raises theoretical questions about the effect of quark masses on the renormalization group flow to the VM fixed point and the mechanism by which massless mesons cross $T_c$.
