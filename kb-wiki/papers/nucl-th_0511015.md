# Mass number dependence of the Skyrme-force-induced nuclear symmetry energy

> The global mass dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy and its two basic ingredients due to the mean-level spacing and effective strength of the isovector mean-potential is studied within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model. In particular, our study determines the ratio of the surface-to-volume contributions to the nuclear symmetry energy to be ~1.6 and reveals that contributions due to mean-level spacing and effective strength of the isovector mean-potential are almost equal after removing momentum-dependent effects by rescaling them with isoscalar and isovector effective masses, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, the contributions to the nuclear symmetry energy from the mean-level spacing and from the effective strength of the isovector mean-potential are nearly equal, but only after removing momentum-dependent effects by rescaling with isoscalar and isovector effective masses, respectively.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for a restricted set of nuclei (T_z >= 4 for A=20; T_z >= 6 for A=24; and T_z >= 8 for 28 <= A <= 128) to extract the mass-dependent mean-level spacing epsilon-bar(A) and effective isovector mean-potential strength kappa-bar(A). They then rescaled these quantities using isoscalar and isovector effective masses to remove momentum-dependent effects, and analyzed the surface-to-volume ratio of the symmetry energy by fitting the mass dependence of these components.

## Key numbers

* The surface-to-volume ratio of the nuclear symmetry energy is r_S/V ~ 1.6, consistent with the liquid-drop model of Moller et al. (1995).
* The hydrodynamical formula for neutron skin thickness with r_S/V = 1.6 yields deviations of order +/- 3% when compared to microscopic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations.
* The centroid of the histogram for neutron skin thickness deviations indicates that r_S/V should be slightly larger, around r_S/V ~ 1.65.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock symmetry energy (Satula 2003, Satula 2005a, Ban 2005, Satula 2005c) and addresses the controversy in the literature regarding the surface-to-volume ratio of the nuclear symmetry energy, which ranges from 2.0 to 2.8 in Danielewicz (2003) to approximately 1.6 in Moller et al. (1995) and approximately 2 in hydrodynamical-type models (Lipparini 1982). The finding that epsilon-star approximately equals kappa-star after rescaling raises the open question of whether this near-equality is a fundamental property of the nuclear mean field.
