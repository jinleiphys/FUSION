# Event-by-event study of prompt neutrons from 239Pu(n,f)

> Employing a recently developed Monte Carlo model, we study the fission of 240Pu induced by neutrons with energies from thermal to just below the threshold for second chance fission. Current measurements of the mean number of prompt neutrons emitted in fission, together with less accurate measurements of the neutron energy spectra, place remarkably fine constraints on predictions of microscopic calculations. In particular, the total excitation energy of the nascent fragments must be specified to within 1 MeV to avoid disagreement with measurements of the mean neutron multiplicity. The combination of the Monte Carlo fission model with a statistical likelihood analysis also presents a powerful tool for the evaluation of fission neutron data. Of particular importance is the fission spectrum, which plays a key role in determining reactor criticality. We show that our approach can be used to develop an estimate of the fission spectrum with uncertainties several times smaller than current experimental uncertainties for outgoing neutron energies up to 2 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors show that the total excitation energy of the nascent fragments must be specified to within 1 MeV to avoid disagreement with measurements of the mean neutron multiplicity, and that their Monte Carlo approach combined with statistical likelihood analysis can produce a fission spectrum estimate with uncertainties several times smaller than current experimental uncertainties for outgoing neutron energies up to 2 MeV.

## Method

The authors adapted the FREYA Monte Carlo fission event generator to model neutron-induced fission of 240Pu, generating individual fission events from the initial compound nucleus through scission and subsequent neutron evaporation from fully accelerated fragments. They used a maximum-likelihood analysis to estimate model parameters (including tip separation scaling factor and level-density parameter) by comparing calculated observables (mean neutron multiplicity and prompt neutron spectra) against experimental data from the EXFOR/CSISRS database and the ENDF/B-VII evaluation. The mass and charge partitions were selected using fits to fission product yields from England and Rider (1993) combined with charge distributions from Reisdorf et al. (1971), while fragment kinetic energies were determined from Coulomb potential calculations with mass-dependent tip separations fitted to total kinetic energy data from Wagemans et al., Nishio et al., and Tsuchiya et al.

## Key numbers

- The mean neutron multiplicity data from ENDF/B-VII was assigned a 0.5% uncertainty for use in the likelihood analysis.
- The level-density parameter default value is e0 = 7.25 MeV, with a common factor "a" treated as an adjustable model parameter.
- The charge dispersion width used for fragment charge selection is sigma_Z = 0.5, from Reisdorf et al. (1971).
- The tip separation near symmetric fission (A_H = 120) is 7-8 fm, dropping to less than 4 fm for A_H >= 132.
- The constant tip separation value tested was d0 = 4.05 fm.
- The gamma parameter in the level-density formula is gamma = 0.05, from Lemaire et al. (2005).
- The symmetric (super-long) fission component contributes only 1-2 per mille of the total yield.
- The incident neutron energies studied range from 0.5 to 5.5 MeV, below the threshold for second chance fission.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo fission simulation approach of Lemaire et al. (2005), who implemented sequential neutron emission for spontaneous fission of 252Cf and thermal fission of 235U, to the neutron-induced fission of 239Pu(n,f) across a range of incident energies. The authors demonstrate that their event-by-event approach with statistical likelihood analysis can extract neutron correlations not previously obtained from physics-based Monte Carlo simulations, and provides a tool for improving fission neutron data evaluations needed for reactor criticality calculations and nuclear security applications.
