# Kaon and pion femtoscopy at the highest energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) in a hydrokinetic model

> The hydrokinetic approach, that incorporates hydrodynamic expansion of the systems formed in A+A collisions and their dynamical decoupling, is applied to restore the initial conditions and space-time picture of the matter evolution in central Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy. The analysis is based on the detailed reproduction of the pion and kaon momentum spectra and femtoscopic data in whole interval of the transverse momenta studied by both STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The fitting procedure utilizes the two parameters: the maximal energy density at supposed thermalization time 1 fm/c and the strength of the pre-thermal flows developed to this time. The quark-gluon plasma and hadronic gas is supposed to be in complete local equilibrium above the chemical freeze-out temperature $T_{ch}$ = 165 MeV with the equation of states (EoS) at high temperatures as in the lattice QCD. Below $T_{ch}$ the EoS in the expanding and gradually decoupling fluid depends on the composition of the hadron-resonance gas at each space-time point and accounts for decays of resonances into the non-equilibrated medium. A good description of the pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra and interferometry radii is reached at both used initial energy density profiles motivated by the Glauber and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) models, however, at different initial energy densities. The discussion as for the approximate pion and kaon $m_T$-scaling for the interferometry radii is based on a comparison of the emission functions for these particles.

## Key claim

The hydrokinetic model can simultaneously reproduce pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra and femtoscopic radii at top RHIC energy using either Glauber or Color Glass Condensate (CGC) motivated initial energy density profiles, but only if the maximal initial energy density is adjusted separately for each profile; the approximate $m_T$-scaling of the interferometry radii is explained by comparing the emission functions for pions and kaons.

## Method

The authors apply the HydroKinetic Model (HKM) to central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, using two fitting parameters: the maximal energy density at thermalization time $\tau_0=1$ fm/c and the strength of pre-thermal transverse flows parameterized as a linear rapidity profile. The evolution above chemical freeze-out temperature $T_{ch}=165$ MeV uses a lattice QCD-based equation of state with corrections for non-zero baryon, strangeness, and electric charge chemical potentials; below $T_{ch}$ the equation of state is computed dynamically from the evolving hadron-resonance gas composition including resonance decays with an effective width factor $\gamma=0.75$. Particle emission is computed via escape probabilities in the relaxation time approximation, and the model is compared to STAR and PHENIX data for pion and kaon spectra and HBT radii across the full transverse momentum range.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time set to $\tau_0 = 1$ fm/c for starting hydrodynamic evolution
- Chemical freeze-out temperature $T_{ch} = 165$ MeV, with chemical potentials $\mu_B = 29$ MeV, $\mu_S = 7$ MeV, $\mu_E = -1$ MeV, and strangeness suppression factor $\gamma_S = 0.935$
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Au: $R_A \approx 6.37$ fm, $\delta = 0.54$ fm; nucleon-nucleon cross-section $\sigma_{NN} = 51$ mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- For CGC initial conditions: saturation scale $\Lambda_{s0} \approx Q_s \approx 2$ GeV$^2$; effective mass parameter $a_2 = 0.0358$, effective temperature parameter $a_3 = 0.465$
- Initial energy density profiles are cut at $\epsilon(r) \leq 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$ for the corona region excluded from hydrodynamic evolution
- Hadron-resonance gas includes $N = 359$ hadron states with masses up to 2.6 GeV
- Effective decay width factor $\gamma = 0.75$ for resonances containing $u$ and $d$ quarks, corresponding to about 30% recombination
- Boltzmann approximation for equation of state yields relative divergence in energy density no bigger than 3% compared to quantum distributions in the relevant thermodynamic region

## Context

This work extends the HydroKinetic Model previously developed by the same authors (Karpenko and Sinyukov, 2009-2010) to include resonance decays into the expanding chemically non-equilibrated hadronic system and a dynamically computed equation of state. It addresses the long-standing failure of hybrid hydrodynamic-plus-cascade models to simultaneously reproduce pion out-, side-, and long-interferometry radii with hadronic spectra at RHIC, a problem previously solvable only with artificial freeze-out parametrizations such as sudden freeze-out near hadronization temperature (Broniowski, 2008). The results enable discrimination between Glauber and CGC initial conditions through their different required maximal energy densities, and provide a framework for incorporating pre-thermal flow development without requiring early thermalization at times below 1 fm/c.
