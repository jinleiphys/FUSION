# Temperature dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy

> We have studied the properties of A=54 and A=64 isobars at temperatures T \leq 2 MeV via Monte Carlo shell model calculations with two different residual interactions. In accord with empirical indications, we find that the symmetry energy coefficient, b_{sym}, is independent of temperature to within 0.6 MeV for T \leq 1 MeV. This is in contrast to a recent suggestion of a 2.5 MeV increase of b_{sym} for this temperature, which would have significantly altered the supernova explosion scenario.

# Temperature dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy

## Key claim
Monte Carlo shell model calculations show that the symmetry energy coefficient b_sym is constant to within 0.6 MeV for temperatures T ≤ 1 MeV, contradicting a prior suggestion by Donati et al. (1994) that b_sym increases by 2.5 MeV over the same temperature range, which would have significantly altered supernova explosion scenarios.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo shell model calculations for A=54 and A=64 isobar chains at temperatures up to 2 MeV, using two different residual interactions (Brown-Richter for A=54 and KB3 for A=64, with cross-checks). They computed internal energies U from the canonical ensemble and extracted b_sym from the linear dependence of U on (N-Z)^2 for even-even nuclei, also comparing with calculations from experimental low-lying level spectra.

## Key numbers
- b_sym(0) = 21 ± 1.0 MeV for A=54 with KB3 interaction, and 22 ± 0.75 MeV with Brown-Richter interaction, in agreement with the experimental value of 21 MeV
- b_sym = 23 ± 0.7 MeV for A=64, slightly above the experimental value of 21.2 MeV
- The energy splitting between 54Fe and 54Ti changes by Δ = 0.4 ± 0.9 MeV between T=0 and 1 MeV, compared to the 4.4 MeV increase predicted by Donati et al. (1994)
- From empirical level spectra, |b_sym| changes by at most 0.3 MeV for A=54 as T varies from 0 to 0.7 MeV, and by 0.6 MeV for A=64 as T varies from 0 to 0.6 MeV
- Pairing correlations between like nucleons disappear at T ≈ 1.3 MeV

## Context
This work directly tests the claim by Donati et al. (1994) that the symmetry energy increases by 2.5 MeV between T=0 and 1 MeV due to temperature-dependent effective nucleon mass effects in a mean-field plus quasiparticle random phase approximation approach. The Monte Carlo shell model calculations include correlations beyond the mean field (such as pairing) that are essential for fp-shell nuclei under presupernova conditions. The results imply that the proposed reduction of the supernova core mantle size and the associated 0.5 foe increase in shock energy do not occur, and that the standard presupernova electron capture treatment using b_sym = 30-32 MeV (Baym, Bethe and Pethick, 1971) remains valid for T ≤ 1 MeV.
