# In-medium Production of Kaons at the Mean-Field Level

> The in-medium mass and energy of kaons and antikaons are studied within the Relativistic Mean Field approach and compared with predictions from chiral models by taking care of kaon-nucleon scattering data. Implications for the subthreshold production of kaons and antikaons in heavy-ion collisions are discussed. We find only small corrections due to in-medium effects on the mean-field level for the relevant production processes for kaons. The production of kaons is even less favourable at high density due to repulsive vector interactions. We conclude that one has to go beyond mean-field approaches and take fluctuations and secondary production processes into account to explain the recently measured enhancement of kaon production at subthreshold energies. The situation is different for antikaons where in-medium effects strongly enhances their production rates. We also see strong in-medium modifications of the annihilation processes of antikaons and Lambda's which might be visible in flow measurements. At high density, we predict that the threshold energy for antikaon and Lambda production and annihilation become equal leading to similar numbers of antikaons and Lambda's in the dense zone of a relativistic heavy ion collision.

# In-medium Production of Kaons at the Mean-Field Level

## Key claim

The paper argues that mean-field level in-medium effects cannot explain the experimentally observed enhancement of kaon production at subthreshold energies in heavy-ion collisions; contrary to some earlier claims, kaon production becomes even less favorable at high density due to repulsive vector interactions, and one must invoke fluctuations and secondary production processes beyond mean-field approaches to account for the data. For antikaons, however, in-medium effects strongly enhance production rates, and at high density the threshold energies for antikaon and Lambda production and annihilation become equal, leading to similar numbers of antikaons and Lambdas in the dense zone.

## Method

The authors employ a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model extended to include Lambda hyperons, with coupling constants fixed to hypernuclear data and SU(6) symmetry, and two interaction schemes for kaons: a one-boson exchange model and a chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) approach. The kaon-nucleon coupling constants are fixed to s-wave KN scattering lengths, and the in-medium energies of kaons, antikaons, nucleons, and Lambdas are computed as functions of baryon density using both a soft equation of state (parameter set TM1) and a hard equation of state (parameter set NL-Z). The threshold energies for various production and annihilation processes are then evaluated in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-averaged KN scattering length is a_bar_KN = -0.255 fm, giving a repulsive optical potential depth for kaons of U^KN_opt ≈ +29 MeV at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³.
- The Lambda potential depth in saturated nuclear matter is U_Λ^(N) ≈ -30 MeV.
- The antikaon optical potential from the one-boson exchange model is U^Kbar_opt = -(85 to 100) MeV at normal nuclear density, with the relativistic potential U^Kbar_rel ≈ -(95 to 110) MeV.
- The minimum effective kaon mass in the one-boson exchange model is m_K,min^* ≈ 390 MeV.
- At ρ = 3ρ₀ with the soft EOS, the kaon energy is ω_K = 585 MeV for the RMF model, ω_K = 630 MeV for the coupled channel analysis of Waas et al., and ω_K = 640-670 MeV for ChPT.
- At ρ = 3ρ₀ with the soft EOS, the antikaon energy is ω_Kbar = 217 MeV for the coupled channel analysis, ω_Kbar = 263 MeV for the RMF model, and ω_Kbar = 280-300 MeV for ChPT.
- The vacuum threshold energy for the process NN → NΛK is Q(NΛK) ≈ 671 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of antikaon condensation in neutron stars (Schaffner et al., 1996) to the context of heavy-ion collisions at subthreshold energies. It directly addresses the measured enhancement of kaon production at GSI and the K⁺-puzzle in kaon-nucleus scattering (Weiss et al., 1994). The paper contrasts its findings with earlier dynamical calculations using the RBUU model (Li et al., 1994; Fang et al., 1994) which used a different parametrization of the kaon energy (setting the off-shell term D̃ = 0) and concluded that in-medium effects could explain the data. The results enable future work incorporating fluctuations, secondary production processes (such as πN → ΛK and NΔ → NΛK), and more sophisticated treatments of the Λ(1405) resonance in dense matter, as well as flow measurements of kaons and antikaons as probes of the in-medium potential.
