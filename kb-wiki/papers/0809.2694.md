# Dynamical symmetry of Dirac hydrogen atom with spin symmetry and its connection with Ginocchio's oscillator

> The Dirac hydrogen atom with spin symmetry is shown has a SO(4) symmetry. The generators are derived, and the corresponding Casimir operator leads to the energy spectrum naturally. This type hydrogen atom is connected to a four-dimensional Dirac system with equal scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potential, by the Kustaanheimo-Stiefel transformation with a constraint.

## Key claim

The Dirac hydrogen atom with spin symmetry possesses an SO(4) dynamical symmetry, but only when the scalar and vector potentials are of equal magnitude (SVPEM) and the potential takes the Coulomb form; this symmetry is not geometrical but dynamical, and the paper explicitly states the spin-orbit coupling is the reason dynamical symmetries are broken in the standard Dirac hydrogen atom.

## Method

The authors derived the conserved quantities (generators) of the SO(4) symmetry by solving commutation relations between a proposed operator form and the spin-symmetry Dirac Hamiltonian with a Coulomb potential. They then normalized these generators to obtain two commuting SU(2) algebras, whose Casimir operators yielded the energy spectrum algebraically. Finally, they connected the 3D Dirac hydrogen atom with spin symmetry to a 4D Dirac system with equal scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials (Ginocchio's oscillator) using the Kustaanheimo-Stiefel (KS) transformation with a constraint condition.

## Key numbers

- Energy spectrum of the Dirac hydrogen atom with spin symmetry: \( E^{\pm} = \frac{\pm 4n^2 - k^2}{4n^2 + k^2} M \), where \( n = 2j + 1 = 1, 2, 3, \dots \) and \( k \) is the Coulomb potential parameter.
- In the non-relativistic limit (\( M \rightarrow \infty \)), \( E^{+} \rightarrow M - \frac{k^2}{2n^2} M \), where the second term matches the non-relativistic hydrogen atom result.
- The constraint from the KS transformation requires the 4D harmonic oscillator quantum numbers to satisfy \( n_1 + n_2 = n_3 + n_4 \), confining the total quantum number to even values: \( N + 2 = 2n = 2, 4, 6, \dots \).

## Context

This work extends the known SO(4) dynamical symmetry of the non-relativistic hydrogen atom (Pauli 1926, Lenz 1924) to the relativistic Dirac hydrogen atom under the condition of spin symmetry (equal scalar and vector potentials). It also connects this system to Ginocchio's oscillator (Ginocchio 1997, 1999), which exhibits U(3) symmetry in the Dirac equation with harmonic oscillator potentials under spin or pseudospin symmetry. The results enable further exploration of non-relativistic-like properties (such as dynamical symmetries and KS transformations) in relativistic Dirac systems with SVPEM, which are relevant to antinucleon and nucleon spectroscopy.
