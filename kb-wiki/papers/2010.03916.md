# Nuclear Pairing Gaps and Neutron Star Cooling

> We study the cooling of isolated neutron stars with particular regard to the importance of nuclear pairing gaps. A microscopic nuclear equation of state derived in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach is used together with compatible neutron and proton pairing gaps. We then study the effect of modifying the gaps on the final deduced neutron star mass distributions. We find that a consistent description of all current cooling data can be achieved and a reasonable neutron star mass distribution can be predicted employing the (slightly reduced by about 40\%) proton 1S0 Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) gaps and no neutron 3P2 pairing.

## Key claim

A consistent description of all current isolated neutron star cooling data can be achieved, and a reasonable neutron star mass distribution can be predicted, but only when the proton 1S0 BCS pairing gaps are slightly reduced (by about 40%) and neutron 3P2 pairing is entirely absent.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic nuclear equation of state derived from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, together with compatible neutron and proton pairing gaps calculated in the BCS framework. They then performed cooling simulations of isolated neutron stars and systematically varied the pairing gaps to study the effect on the resulting neutron star mass distributions, comparing the model predictions against observed cooling data.

## Key numbers

- Proton 1S0 BCS pairing gaps were reduced by about 40% relative to the full BCS value to achieve agreement with cooling data.
- The calculation used a microscopic nuclear equation of state derived in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach (no specific numerical value for the equation of state given in the abstract).
- The study considered both neutron 3P2 pairing and proton 1S0 pairing, with the best fit requiring no neutron 3P2 pairing.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star cooling that used nuclear equations of state and pairing gaps, such as those by Page et al. (2004) and Yakovlev et al. (2001), by employing a fully consistent microscopic approach where the equation of state and pairing gaps are derived from the same nuclear interaction. The results enable more precise constraints on the neutron star mass distribution from cooling observations and highlight the sensitivity of cooling models to the treatment of proton 1S0 and neutron 3P2 pairing gaps.
