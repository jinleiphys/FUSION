# Excited light baryons from quark-gluon-level calculations

> The task of mapping and explaining the spectrum of baryons and the structure of these states in terms of quarks and gluons is a longstanding challenge in hadron physics, which is likely to persist for another decade or more. We review the progress made in this topic using a functional method based on Dyson-Schwinger equations. This framework provides a non-perturbative, Poincaré-covariant continuum formulation of Quantum Chromodynamics which is able to extract novel insight on baryon properties since the physics at the hadron level is directly related with the underlying quark-gluon substructure, via convolution of Green functions.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation framework can compute masses for the dressed-quark core of octet and decuplet baryons and their first positive-parity excitations, but these theoretical values are uniformly larger than empirical Breit-Wigner masses because the calculation omits meson-baryon final-state interactions, which typically reduce the mass by roughly 0.25 GeV.

## Method

The authors solve the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation as a homogeneous linear integral equation for the baryon's Faddeev amplitude, treating baryons as three-quark bound-states with soft, fully-interacting diquark correlations. The kernel includes dressed-quark propagators, diquark propagators, and diquark correlation amplitudes, with the quark exchange mechanism ensuring the wave function respects the baryon's fermionic nature. The truncation is constrained by symmetry properties, multiplicative renormalizability, and perturbative limits.

## Key numbers

- Computed masses for ground-state and first positive-parity excitation of octet and decuplet baryons are uniformly larger than empirical Breit-Wigner masses, with the difference attributed to meson-baryon final-state interactions that reduce the mass by roughly 0.25 GeV.
- The theoretical predictions show a response to a coherent ±5% change in the mass-scales appearing in the baryon bound-state equation, indicated by vertical risers on the computed mass points.
- The gluon propagator form factor Δ(q²) saturates in the deep infrared, indicating gluon mass generation, as confirmed by lattice-QCD simulations.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to baryon spectroscopy, building on two decades of studying baryons as three-quark bound-states with diquark correlations (Oettel 1998, Eichmann 2016, Lu 2017, Yin 2019). The results are compared with empirical Breit-Wigner masses from the Particle Data Group (Tanabashi 2018), and the systematic offset is explained by meson-baryon final-state interactions, as previously demonstrated for the nucleon and its parity-partner (Chen 2017). The framework enables future calculations of electromagnetic transition form factors for nucleon resonances, where meson-baryon contributions are expected to be soft and disappear at high momentum transfer, and can be extended to exotic hadrons including tetraquarks, pentaquarks, hybrid mesons, hybrid baryons, and glueballs.
