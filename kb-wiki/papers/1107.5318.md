# Holographic Wilsonian Renormalization and Chiral Phase Transitions

> We explore the role of a holographic Wilsonian cut-off in simple probe brane models with chiral symmetry breaking/restoration phase transitions. The Wilsonian cut-off allows us to define supergravity solutions for off-shell configurations and hence to define a potential for the chiral condensate. We pay particular attention to the need for configurations whose action we are comparing to have the same IR and UV boundary conditions. We exhibit new first and second order phase transitions with changing cut-off. We derive the effective potential for the condensate including mean field and BKT type continuous transitions.

# Holographic Wilsonian Renormalization and Chiral Phase Transitions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a holographic Wilsonian cut-off in probe brane models can define off-shell supergravity configurations and an effective potential for the chiral condensate, revealing new first and second order phase transitions as the cut-off scale is varied; however, the precise mapping between the radial cut-off and a gauge-invariant measure of energy scale in the field theory remains an open problem, so the match to Wilsonian renormalization is imprecise in spirit.

## Method

The authors introduce a radial cut-off in the holographic direction (the radial coordinate on probe D7 or D5 branes in AdS) to define off-shell configurations for the chiral condensate. They use a two-scale cut-off procedure (with cut-offs at epsilon_- and epsilon_+) to ensure all configurations share the same IR boundary condition L'(epsilon_-)=0, then take the limit where the cut-offs coincide. The Euclidean on-shell DBI action integrated from the cut-off to infinity is evaluated as the Wilsonian effective potential for the condensate, with the flat embedding L=0 serving as the reference at V=0.

## Key numbers

- The critical cut-off for the second order chiral symmetry breaking transition in the D3/D7 system with magnetic field B=1 is epsilon_c = 0.4686 B^{1/2}
- The critical exponent extracted from log-log plots of condensate c versus (epsilon_c - epsilon) is 1/2, indicating a mean field transition
- The fitted effective potential near the transition is V_eff(c; epsilon, B) = -4.17(0.4686 - epsilon/B^{1/2}) c^2/B + (-13.98 epsilon/B^{1/2} + 10.81) c^4/B^4
- In the N=2 supersymmetric theory without magnetic field, the analytic solution shows embeddings become complex at rho_c = (2c)^{1/3}, where c is the condensate parameter

## Context

This work extends the holographic Wilsonian renormalization program proposed by Bredberg (2010), Nickel (2010), Heemskerk (2010), Faulkner (2010), and others to explicit probe brane systems with chiral phase transitions. It applies the methodology to the well-studied D3/D7 system (Karch 2002) with magnetic fields (Filev 2007) and chemical potential (Evans 2010, Jensen 2010), as well as the D3/D5 system (Karch 2000, DeWolfe 2001, Erdmenger 2002) which exhibits holographic BKT transitions (Jensen 2010, Evans 2010). The approach enables the derivation of low-energy effective actions near transition points and reveals a Coleman-Weinberg style symmetry breaking mechanism in the strongly coupled regime.
