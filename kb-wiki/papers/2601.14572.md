# Possible Existence of $^3_ϕ$H, $^4_ϕ$H, $^4_ϕ$He, and $^5_ϕ$He Nuclei

> Motivated by recent HAL QCD simulations of the $ϕN$ interaction in the $^4S_{3/2}$ channel and its modification in the $^2S_{1/2}$ channel, we develop a first-principles few-body framework that embeds these potentials into configuration-space Faddeev--Yakubovsky equations. We predict bound $^4_ϕ\mathrm{H}$, $^4_ϕ\mathrm{He}$, and $^5_ϕ\mathrm{He}$ nuclei by performing calculations for $ϕ$-mesic $ϕNNN$ and $ϕNNNN$ systems. Both spin-dependent and spin-independent $ϕN$ interactions are considered, leading to deeply and moderately bound states, respectively. The deeply bound states originate from the strong attraction in the $^2S_{1/2}$ $ϕN$ channel. Coulomb shifts of the binding energies are evaluated. Our findings provide the binding mechanism and demonstrate the importance of short-range $ϕN$ attraction.

# Possible Existence of \(^3_\phi\)H, \(^4_\phi\)H, \(^4_\phi\)He, and \(^5_\phi\)He Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper predicts bound \(\phi\)-mesic nuclei with mass numbers A=3, 4, and 5, but only when the spin-dependent \(\phi N\) interaction in the \(^2S_{1/2}\) channel is strongly enhanced (parameter \(\beta = 6.9\)); with a spin-independent interaction (\(\beta = 1\)), the \(^3_\phi\)H system is only very weakly bound (34 keV above the deuteron binding energy) and the \(\phi NN\) system is unbound.

## Method

The authors solve configuration-space Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for \(\phi NNN\) and \(\phi NNNN\) systems, using the HAL QCD-derived \(\phi N\) potential (a sum of short-range Gaussians and a two-pion-exchange tail) in both the \(^4S_{3/2}\) and \(^2S_{1/2}\) channels, combined with the MT I-III nucleon-nucleon potential. The radial partial amplitudes are expanded in a Lagrange-Laguerre basis using the Lagrange-mesh method, with isospin formalism reducing the number of independent coupled differential equations to 5 for four-body and 16 for five-body systems.

## Key numbers

- \(\phi N\) bound state in the \(^2S_{1/2}\) channel: binding energy 18.361 MeV (QCD-motivated masses, \(\beta=6.9\))
- \(^3_\phi\)H ground state (\(J^\pi=0^-\)): binding energy 51.625 MeV (QCD-motivated masses, \(\beta=6.9\))
- \(^3_\phi\)H excited state (\(J^\pi=1^-\)): binding energy 33.132 MeV (QCD-motivated masses, \(\beta=6.9\))
- \(^4_\phi\)H (\(J^\pi=1/2^-\)): binding energy 55.93 MeV (QCD-motivated masses, \(\beta=6.9\))
- \(^4_\phi\)He (\(J^\pi=1/2^-\)): binding energy 55.15 MeV (QCD-motivated masses, \(\beta=6.9\)); Coulomb shift between \(^4_\phi\)H and \(^4_\phi\)He is 0.68 MeV
- \(^5_\phi\)He (\(J^\pi=1^-\)): binding energy 76.77 MeV (QCD-motivated masses, \(\beta=6.9\), with Coulomb); 77.57 MeV (without Coulomb)
- For \(\beta=1\) (spin-independent): \(^3_\phi\)H binding is only 0.034 MeV above the deuteron binding energy (QCD-motivated masses); \(^4_\phi\)H binding 11.64 MeV; \(^5_\phi\)He binding 40.05 MeV (with Coulomb)
- \(\phi NN\) system: unbound for \(\beta=1\); bound at 18.361 MeV for \(\beta=6.9\) (QCD-motivated masses)

## Context

This work extends previous three-body calculations of \(\phi NN\) systems (BSS, FKVPRD2024, Bel2008, EA24, Wen2025) to four- and five-body \(\phi\)-mesic nuclei for the first time, using the HAL QCD \(\phi N\) potential from Lyu (2022) and its spin-resolved reanalysis by Chizzali (2024). The framework builds on earlier Faddeev-Yakubovsky implementations for hypernuclei (Nogga, 2002; Lazauskas, 2018a, 2018b) and enables future studies of heavier \(\phi\)-nuclear systems and hidden strangeness in nuclear binding, as well as systematic investigation of \(YNNN\) and \(YNNNN\) systems for baryons such as \(\Lambda\), \(\Omega\), and \(\Xi\).
