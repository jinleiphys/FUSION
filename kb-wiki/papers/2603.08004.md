# The Birman-Schwinger operator for the Cornell Hamiltonian

> Quantum Chromodynamics is the theory of strong interactions. It has been shown during the last decades that it describes correctly most of the properties of hadrons at high energy. The most distinctive feature of the theory is the realisation that the elementary particles which composed the known forms of matter, that is to say quarks and gluons, cannot be observed at low energy. In this work we are addressing this specific feature, known as confinement, by performing a rigorous mathematical treatment of the Cornell potential

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Birman-Schwinger operator method can be rigorously applied to the one-dimensional radial Cornell Hamiltonian (linear plus Coulomb potential) to obtain analytic expressions for bound-state energies and eigenfunctions, provided a physical cutoff related to the finite size of particles is introduced to ensure the norm of the remainder operator is less than unity.

## Method

The authors treat the linear confining term as the unperturbed Hamiltonian H0 and the Coulomb term as a perturbation. They solve the unperturbed Schrodinger equation exactly in terms of Airy functions, then construct the Birman-Schwinger integral operator B_E = V^{1/2} (H0 + |E|)^{-1} V^{1/2} for the Coulomb potential V = V_C/r. They prove that B_E belongs to the trace class by showing the diagonal kernel is integrable, then use the Fredholm determinant formalism to extract first-order approximations for the ground state and first excited state energies by splitting the operator into a rank-one part P and a remainder M.

## Key numbers

- The unperturbed energy levels are given by E_n = (hbar^2 c^2 / (2 m c^2))^{1/3} V_l^{2/3} alpha_n, where alpha_n behaves like n^{2/3} as n approaches infinity.
- The first-order correction to the ground state energy is epsilon = (omega / Ai'(-alpha_1)) times the integral over r of (1/r) Ai^2((lambda_0/gamma) r - alpha_n) dr, where omega = (2 m c^2 V_c)/(hbar^2 c^2).
- The condition for the remainder operator norm to be less than unity is omega/epsilon < delta, where delta is the particle radius cutoff.
- An alternative bound gives ||M|| < omega/(alpha_0 delta), where alpha_0 = E_2 - E_1.
- The first-order correction to the first excited state energy is E(2) = E_2 - (omega / Ai'(-alpha_3)) times the integral over r of (1/r) Ai^2((lambda_0/gamma) r - alpha_2) dr.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the Birman-Schwinger method to the one-dimensional Coulomb potential without linear confinement (Fassari et al., 2024). It provides a mathematically rigorous alternative to purely numerical or semianalytical approaches such as the Nikiforov-Uvarov formalism, and to effective Hamiltonian methods used in low-energy QCD (Godfrey and Isgur, 1985). The formalism enables analytic study of the Cornell potential's bound states and is intended for future application to low-energy meson spectra.
