# Chiral approach to nuclear matter: Role of explicit short-range NN-terms

> We extend a recent chiral approach to nuclear matter by including the most general (momentum-independent) NN-contact interaction. Iterating this two-parameter contact-vertex with itself and with one-pion exchange the emerging energy per particle exhausts all terms possible up-to-and-including fourth order in the small momentum expansion. The equation of state of pure neutron matter, $\bar E_n(k_n)$, can be reproduced very well up to quite high neutron densities of $ρ_n=0.5\fmd$ by adjusting the strength of a repulsive $nn$-contact interaction. Binding and saturation of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is a generic feature of our perturbative calculation. Fixing the maximum binding energy per particle to $-\bar E(k_{f0})= 15.3 $MeV we find that any possible equilibrium density $ρ_0$ lies below $ρ_0^{\rm max}=0.191\fmd$. The additional constraint from the neutron matter equation of state leads however to a somewhat too low saturation density of $ρ_0 =0.134 \fmd$. We also investigate the effects of the NN-contact interaction on the complex single-particle potential $U(p,k_f)+i W(p,k_f)$. We find that the effective nucleon mass at the Fermi-surface is bounded from below by $M^*(k_{f0}) \geq 1.4 M$. This property keeps the critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition at somewhat too high values $T_c \geq 21 $MeV. The downward bending of the asymmetry energy $A(k_f)$ above nuclear matter saturation density is a generic feature of the approximation to fourth order. Altogether, there is within this complete fourth-order calculation no "magic" set of adjustable short-range parameters with which one could reproduce simultaneously and accurately all semi-empirical properties of nuclear matter.

# Chiral approach to nuclear matter: Role of explicit short-range NN-terms

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within a complete fourth-order chiral perturbation theory calculation, there is no single set of adjustable short-range parameters that can simultaneously and accurately reproduce all semi-empirical properties of nuclear matter; in particular, the conditions for a good neutron matter equation of state and for good single-particle properties in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter are mutually exclusive.

## Method

The authors extend a previous chiral approach to nuclear matter by including the most general momentum-independent NN-contact interaction parameterized by two strength parameters (gamma_0 and gamma_1) and two isospin-dependent cut-offs (Lambda_0 and Lambda_1). They compute the energy per particle and single-particle potential up to three-loop order (fourth order in small momenta) by iterating the contact vertex with itself and with one-pion exchange, regularizing linear divergences with momentum cut-offs.

## Key numbers

- Neutron matter equation of state reproduced up to neutron density rho_n = 0.5 fm^{-3} using gamma_1 = -0.75 and cut-off Lambda_1 = 0.48 GeV
- Maximum binding energy per particle fixed to -bar E(k_{f0}) = 15.3 MeV yields maximum possible saturation density rho_0^{max} = 0.191 fm^{-3}
- With neutron matter constraint, saturation density becomes rho_0 = 0.134 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear matter compressibility K = 243 MeV for the parameter set constrained by neutron matter
- Effective nucleon mass at Fermi-surface bounded from below by M*(k_{f0}) >= 1.4 M
- Critical temperature of liquid-gas phase transition T_c >= 21 MeV
- Potential depth U(0,k_{f0}) = -27.7 MeV for the neutron-matter-constrained parameter set
- Imaginary single-particle potential at p=0: W(0,k_{f0}) = 43.3 MeV for the neutron-matter-constrained set, and W(0,k_{f0}) = 22.7 MeV for the maximal saturation density case

## Context

This work extends the chiral approach of Lutz et al. (2000) and Kaiser et al. (2002) by including explicit short-range NN-contact terms to fourth order in the small momentum expansion. It addresses the severe phenomenological problems found in the scheme of Lutz et al. (2000), where single-particle properties came out completely unrealistic with a potential depth of only -20 MeV and a negative effective nucleon mass at the Fermi surface. The calculation enables future work to incorporate p^2-dependent NN-contact interactions at fifth order as a possible remedy for the mutually exclusive constraints on neutron matter and single-particle properties.
