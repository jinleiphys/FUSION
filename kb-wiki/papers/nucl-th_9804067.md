# Center-of-mass effects on the quasi-hole spectroscopic factors in the 16O(e,e'p) reaction

> The spectroscopic factors for the low-lying quasi-hole states observed in the 16O(e,e'p)15N reaction are reinvestigated with a variational Monte Carlo calculation for the structure of the initial and final nucleus. A computational error in a previous report is rectified. It is shown that a proper treatment of center-of-mass motion does not lead to a reduction of the spectroscopic factor for $p$-shell quasi-hole states, but rather to a 7% enhancement. This is in agreement with analytical results obtained in the harmonic oscillator model. The center-of-mass effect worsens the discrepancy between present theoretical models and the experimentally observed single-particle strength. We discuss the present status of this problem, including some other mechanisms that may be relevant in this respect.

## Key claim

A proper treatment of center-of-mass motion in variational Monte Carlo calculations for the 16O(e,e'p)15N reaction does not reduce the spectroscopic factor for p-shell quasi-hole states, but rather enhances it by 7%, which worsens the discrepancy between theoretical models and experimentally observed single-particle strength.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations using translationally invariant intrinsic wave functions for both the 16O ground state and the 15N quasi-hole states, with the Argonne v14 two-nucleon interaction and Urbana model VII three-nucleon interaction. They corrected a computational error from a previous report (Rad et al., 1993) that had incorrectly treated center-of-mass effects, and compared their results with analytical harmonic oscillator model predictions and with experimental data from the NIKHEF collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The corrected spectroscopic factor for the p3/2 quasi-hole state with only center-of-mass correlations is S = 1.07, in agreement with the harmonic oscillator model prediction of S_p = 16/15 ≈ 1.07
- The full VMC calculation including dynamical NN correlations gives S_{p3/2} = 0.98 for the p-shell spectroscopic factor
- The experimental spectroscopic factor for the 1/2- ground state in 15N is S_{p1/2}(g.s.) = 0.61, and for the lowest 3/2- state at 6.32 MeV is S_{p3/2}(6.32) = 0.53
- The total experimental p3/2 strength, accounting for fragmentation over three states, gives S_{p3/2} = 0.53/0.87 = 0.61
- The correction factor applied to previous results was (16/15)^{3/2}
- The nonorthogonality and antisymmetrization corrections to the PWIA amplitude under NIKHEF kinematics range from 4×10^{-6} at most negative missing momentum to 6×10^{-3} at most positive missing momentum

## Context

This work corrects an error in the VMC calculations of Rad et al. (1993) and reconciles them with the analytical harmonic oscillator model results of Dieperink and de Forest (1974). The corrected center-of-mass enhancement of 7% worsens the already significant discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental data from the NIKHEF collaboration (Leuschner et al., 1994). The authors note that Green's function calculations by Polls et al. (1995), Muther and Dickhoff (1994), and Geurts et al. (1996) would also be affected by proper center-of-mass treatment, with the latter's result increasing from S_{p3/2}(6.32) = 0.76 to 0.81, still far above the experimental value of 0.53. The work points toward the need for additional physics beyond short-range and tensor correlations, possibly including alpha-cluster components in the wave function.
