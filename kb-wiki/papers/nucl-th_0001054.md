# Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory: Formalism and Application to the Superdeformed Bands in the $A\sim 190$ region

> Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory without and with approximate particle number projection by means of the Lipkin-Nogami method is presented in detail as an extension of Relativistic Mean Field theory with pairing correlations to the rotating frame. Pairing correlations are taken into account by a finite range two-body force of Gogny type. The applicability of this theory to the description of rotating nuclei is studied in detail on the example of superdeformed bands in even-even nuclei of the $A\sim 190$ mass region. Different aspects such as the importance of pairing and particle number projection, the dependence of the results on the parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian and Gogny force etc. are investigated in detail. It is shown that without any adjustment of new parameters the best description of experimental data is obtained by using the well established parameter sets NL1 for the Lagrangian and D1S for the pairing force. Contrary to previous studies at spin zero it is found that the increase of the strength of the Gogny force is not necessary in the framework of Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory provided that particle number projection is performed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory with approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method can describe superdeformed bands in the A~190 mass region without any adjustment of new parameters, using the standard NL1 parameter set for the RMF Lagrangian and D1S for the Gogny pairing force. Contrary to previous studies at spin zero, the authors find that increasing the strength of the Gogny force (beyond the standard f=1.0 factor) is not necessary in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework provided that particle number projection is performed.

## Method

The authors extended Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory by incorporating pairing correlations through a finite-range Gogny-type two-body force (parameter set D1S) in the particle-particle channel, while retaining the relativistic mean field description in the particle-hole channel. They solved the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in one-dimensional cranking approximation, including the spatial components of vector mesons (nuclear magnetism) and implementing approximate particle number projection using the Lipkin-Nogami method based on the Kamlah expansion to order M=2. The theory was applied to study yrast superdeformed bands in even-even nuclei of the A~190 mass region, with systematic investigation of pairing effects, particle number projection, and dependence on the parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian and Gogny force.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of terms proportional to Omega_x^2 to the total energy is typically in the range of ~10-20 keV at the highest rotational frequencies of interest in the A~190 mass region, and can generally be neglected.
- The center-of-mass correction is approximated as E_cm = -3/4 hbar omega_0 = -3/4 * 41 * A^(-1/3) MeV.
- The standard strength factor f=1.0 is used for the Gogny force (parameter set D1S) when particle number projection is performed, in contrast to the factor f=1.15 used in previous Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov studies at spin zero.

## Context

This work extends the Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory developed by Koepf and Ring (1989, 1990, 1993) and Afanasjev, Koepf, and Ring (1996), which had been successfully applied to superdeformed bands in the A~60, A~80, and A~140-150 mass regions without pairing correlations. The present CRHB theory enables the description of pairing correlations in rotating nuclei, which are essential for the A~190 mass region where pairing is not completely quenched even at high spin. The approach builds on the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework of Gonzalez-Llarena et al. (1996) and enables systematic study of superdeformed bands in the A~190 region, addressing questions about identical bands and the treatment of pairing correlations that remained unresolved in previous unpaired calculations.
