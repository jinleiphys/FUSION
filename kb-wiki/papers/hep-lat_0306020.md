# Nonlinear Realization of Chiral Symmetry on the Lattice

> We formulate lattice theories in which chiral symmetry is realized nonlinearly on the fermion fields. In this framework the fermion mass term does not break chiral symmetry. This property allows us to use the Wilson term to remove the doubler fermions while maintaining exact chiral symmetry on the lattice. Our lattice formulation enables us to address non-perturbative questions in effective field theories of baryons interacting with pions and in models involving constituent quarks interacting with pions and gluons. We show that a system containing a non-zero density of static baryons interacting with pions can be studied on the lattice without encountering complex action problems. In our formulation one can also decide non-perturbatively if the chiral quark model of Georgi and Manohar provides an appropriate low-energy description of QCD. If so, one could understand why the non-relativistic quark model works.

## Key claim

The paper constructs lattice theories where chiral symmetry is realized nonlinearly on fermion fields, allowing the Wilson term to remove doubler fermions while maintaining exact chiral symmetry on the lattice, but only for applications where anomalies play no major role, as Wess-Zumino-Witten and Goldstone-Wilczek terms are not constructed on the lattice in this work.

## Method

The authors formulate lattice actions using a Goldstone boson field \(U_x \in SU(N_f)\) and its square root \(u_x = U_x^{1/2}\), which transforms under global chiral rotations via a field-dependent local transformation \(V_x\). A flavor parallel transporter \(\tilde V_{x,\mu}\) is constructed from \(u_x\) and projected onto \(SU(N_f)\) to serve as the lattice analog of the continuum "gauge" field \(v_\mu(x)\). Fermion fields are coupled via this transporter, and a Wilson term is added to remove doublers without breaking the nonlinearly realized chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The Goldstone boson field \(U(x) = \exp[2 i \pi^a(x) T^a / F_\pi]\) lives in the coset space \(G/H = SU(N_f)\), with \(F_\pi\) the pion decay constant.
- For \(N_f = 2\), the singular submanifold where \(u(x)\) is ill-defined occurs at \(U(x) = -\mathbb{1}\), a single point in the \(SU(2)\) group manifold.
- For general \(N_f\), the singular submanifold has dimension \(N_f^2 - 4\) and codimension 3 in the 4-dimensional space-time, so singularities lie on 1-dimensional lines.
- The Wess-Zumino-Witten term prefactor is quantized as the number of colors \(N_c\), with \(Z = \int \mathcal{D}U \exp(-S[U]) \exp(i N_c S_{WZW}[U])\).

## Context

This work extends the continuum nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry developed by Coleman, Wess, and Zumino (1969) and Callan (1969) to the lattice, and builds on the Ginsparg-Wilson relation (1982) and the overlap formulation of Narayanan and Neuberger (1993). The construction avoids the Nielsen-Ninomiya theorem (1981) by using a nonlinear realization where the fermion mass term does not break chiral symmetry, enabling the use of a simple Wilson action. The lattice formulation enables non-perturbative studies of effective field theories for baryons and pions, including the chiral quark model of Georgi and Manohar (1984), and allows simulations of static baryons at non-zero chemical potential without complex action problems.
