# Twice-iterated boson-exchange scattering amplitudes

> We calculate at two-loop order the complex-valued scattering amplitude related to the twice-iterated scalar-isovector boson-exchange between nucleons. In comparison to the once-iterated boson-exchange amplitude it shows less dependence on the scattering angle. We calculate also the iteration of the (static) irreducible one-loop potential with the one-boson exchange and find similar features. Together with the irreducible three-boson exchange potentials and the two-boson exchange potentials with vertex corrections, which are also evaluated analytically, our results comprise all nonrelativistic contributions from scalar-isovector boson-exchange at one- and two-loop order. The applied methods can be straightforwardly adopted to the pseudoscalar pion with its spin- and momentum-dependent couplings to the nucleon.

# Twice-iterated boson-exchange scattering amplitudes

## Key claim

The paper calculates all nonrelativistic contributions from scalar-isovector boson-exchange between nucleons at one- and two-loop order, showing that the twice-iterated boson-exchange amplitude exhibits less dependence on the scattering angle compared to the once-iterated amplitude, under the assumption that the boson is light (mass m much less than nucleon mass M) and couples weakly (coupling constant g ~ 2π m/M).

## Method

The author evaluates two-loop integrals for the twice-iterated scalar-isovector boson-exchange scattering amplitude using perturbative unitarity via the Cutkosky cutting rule, obtaining the imaginary part from a single-integral representation and the real part from an unsubtracted dispersion relation. The iteration of the static irreducible one-loop potential with one-boson exchange is calculated similarly, and the irreducible three-boson exchange potentials and two-boson exchange potentials with one-loop vertex corrections are evaluated analytically using Feynman parameter techniques and spectral function representations.

## Key numbers

- Threshold value of the once-iterated amplitude: G(0,0) = 1/(2m³)
- Threshold value of the twice-iterated amplitude: m⁴ H(0,0) = ln(4/3)
- Threshold value of the iterated one-loop amplitude: m³ J(0,0) = π(1/2 - 2√3/9)
- The real part of the twice-iterated amplitude Re H(p,q) crosses zero at p ≈ 0.58 m
- Isoscalar three-boson exchange potential at q/m = 1: 0.965 of its q=0 value; at q/m = 10: 0.384 of its q=0 value
- Isovector three-boson exchange potential at q/m = 1: 0.957 of its q=0 value; at q/m = 10: 0.289 of its q=0 value
- One-loop two-boson exchange potential at q/m = 1: 0.861 of its q=0 value; at q/m = 10: 0.091 of its q=0 value
- Two-boson exchange potential with vertex corrections (isoscalar) at q/m = 1: 0.927 of its q=0 value; at q/m = 10: 0.275 of its q=0 value
- Two-boson exchange potential with vertex corrections (isovector) at q/m = 1: 0.960 of its q=0 value; at q/m = 10: 0.430 of its q=0 value

## Context

This work extends the algebraic unitarization approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering developed by Oller (2005), who had considered only contact interactions, by providing analytical expressions for multiply iterated boson-exchange amplitudes needed for realistic treatments including pion exchanges. The results complement the known once-iterated pion-exchange amplitude from the NN-potential literature and enable future calculations of NN phase shifts and mixing angles for pseudoscalar pions with spin- and momentum-dependent couplings, which the author states are in progress.
