# Nonlocality Effect in the Alpha decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei with XGBRegressor

> Building on the work of E. L. Medeiros [1] and our previous study [2], we generalize the alpha-nucleus nonlocality effect to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei within the two-potential approach (TPA) framework. The coordinate-dependent parameters introduced by this nonlocality are optimized using an advanced gradient boosting regression model. This improved TPA is applied to calculate the $α$-decay half-lives of 599 nuclei with $Z = 52-118$, yielding a root-mean-square (RMS) deviation that is 74.8$\%$ lower than that of the original TPA. Subsequently, we employ the improved TPA, along with the DZR [3] and MUDL [4]models, to predict alpha-decay half-lives for 142 superheavy nuclei with $Z = 117-120$. The predictions from all three models are in close agreement, with the results from our improved TPA and the DZR model being nearly identical.

# Nonlocality Effect in the Alpha decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei with XGBRegressor

## Key claim
The authors extend the alpha-nucleus nonlocality effect to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei within the two-potential approach (TPA) framework, using an XGBRegressor model to optimize coordinate-dependent mass parameters. The improved TPA reduces the root-mean-square deviation from experimental half-lives by 74.8% compared to the original TPA, though the authors note that the coefficient of determination (R² = 0.56 ± 0.18) indicates only moderate predictive accuracy, and the model performs worse than the original TPA for some individual nuclei.

## Method
The authors incorporate a coordinate-dependent effective mass for the alpha particle, derived from a velocity-dependent potential following the approach of Medeiros (2022), into the TPA framework. The mass parameter ρ_S, which controls the nonlocality strength, is optimized using an XGBoost regression model (XGBRegressor) trained on experimental alpha-decay half-lives from NUBASE2020, with input features including angular momentum, alpha-preformation probability, asymmetry term, and square root of decay energy. The improved TPA is then applied to calculate half-lives for 599 nuclei with Z = 52-118, and predictions for 142 superheavy nuclei with Z = 117-120 are compared with the DZR model of Deng et al. (2020) and the MUDL model of Soylu et al. (2021).

## Key numbers
- RMS deviation reduction: 74.8% lower than the original TPA for 599 nuclei with Z = 52-118
- Standard deviation σ for the improved TPA: 0.46929 (compared to 0.82058 without nonlocality effect)
- XGBRegressor model evaluation metrics: MSE = 2.68 ± 1.43, RMSE = 1.60 ± 0.33, R² = 0.56 ± 0.18 (from 50 repetitions of 5-fold cross-validation)
- Nuclear potential depth parameter V₀ = 192.42 + 31.059 × (N-Z)/A (in MeV)
- Nuclear potential diffuseness a fixed at 0.5958 fm
- Sharp radius R = 1.28 A^(1/3) - 0.76 + 0.8 A^(-1/3) fm
- Nonlocality parameter ΔR = 3.44 fm for R_S = R + ΔR
- Predicted half-lives for 142 superheavy nuclei with Z = 117-120 are tabulated as log₁₀(T₁/₂) in seconds, ranging from approximately -6.9 to +10.9 depending on neutron number

## Context
This work extends the nonlocality treatment of Medeiros (2022) from even-even nuclei to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, building on the authors' prior study (Hu and Wu, 2025) that applied decision tree, random forest, and gradient boosting regression to 198 even-even nuclei. The improved TPA predictions for superheavy nuclei (Z = 117-120) are compared with the DZR formula of Deng et al. (2020) and the MUDL model of Soylu et al. (2021), showing close agreement particularly with DZR. The results suggest N = 184 as a probable neutron magic number, consistent with previous studies. The authors note that future work should incorporate additional physics-informed quantities such as deformation and improve the alpha-preformation probability calculation to enhance predictive reliability.
