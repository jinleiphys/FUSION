# Mean field studies of high-spin properties in the $A\sim 30$ and 60 regions of superdeformation

> The importance of deformation changes and the possible role of proton-neutron pairing correlations on the properties of paired band crossings at superdeformation in the $A\sim 60$ mass region have been analyzed. The present analysis, supported in part by the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations for the SD band in $^{60}$Zn, suggests that when going from $^{60}$Zn to neighboring odd nuclei the properties of paired band crossings are strongly influenced by deformation changes. A number of questions related to the superdeformation in the $A\sim 30$ mass region has been studied with the cranked relativistic mean field theory and the configuration-dependent cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky approach.

## Key claim

The observed jump in the dynamic moment of inertia of the superdeformed band in $^{60}$Zn and its absence in neighboring odd nuclei $^{59}$Cu and $^{61}$Zn cannot be taken as a clear signal of proton-neutron pairing correlations; instead, deformation changes between the nuclei likely play a dominant or at least strongly contributing role, as suggested by the similarity to the $A\sim 150$ region where proton-neutron pairing is absent.

## Method

The authors used cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory and cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory with the NL3 force for the RMF Lagrangian and the D1S Gogny force in the particle-particle channel, including approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method, to study the superdeformed band in $^{60}$Zn. For the $A\sim 30$ region, they employed CRMF theory and the configuration-dependent cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) approach to analyze the high-spin structure of $^{32}$S and neighboring nuclei. The CRHB calculations included fermionic and bosonic states up to shells $N_F=12$ and $N_B=16$ with a basis deformation $\beta_0=0.4$, $\gamma=0^\circ$.

## Key numbers

- The jump in the dynamic moment of inertia $J^{(2)}$ of the SD band in $^{60}$Zn occurs at rotational frequency $\Omega_x \sim 1.0$ MeV.
- The SD band in $^{61}$Zn shows only a small bump at $\Omega_x \sim 1.15$ MeV.
- The calculated ratio of transition quadrupole moments is $Q_t(^{59}\text{Cu})/Q_t(^{60}\text{Zn}) \approx 0.82$.
- In the $A\sim 150$ region for comparison: $Q_t(^{152}\text{Dy})/Q_t(^{150}\text{Gd}) \approx 1.13$, $Q_t(^{151}\text{Tb})/Q_t(^{150}\text{Gd}) \approx 1.06$, $Q_t(^{149}\text{Gd})/Q_t(^{150}\text{Gd}) \approx 0.95$.
- Scaling the Gogny force strength by 10% changes the frequency of the paired band crossing by $\Delta \Omega_x \sim 0.135$ MeV.
- For the SD configuration in $^{32}$S, at high frequencies $\Omega_x \sim 2.5$ MeV, the dynamic moment of inertia is approximately 60% of the kinematic moment of inertia.
- The "maximum" spin of the doubly-magic SD configuration in $^{32}$S is $I_{max}=24\hbar$.
- Nuclear magnetism gives additional binding of 0.907 MeV for $r_{tot}=+1$ configurations and 0.468 MeV for $r_{tot}=-1$ configurations in excited SD bands of $^{32}$S.
- The SD band in $^{36}$Ar approaches termination at $I^\pi=16^+$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of superdeformation in the $A\sim 60$ region, where SD bands were discovered in $^{62}$Zn (Ref. Zn62SD) and $^{36}$Ar (Ref. Ar36), and addresses the debate on whether the observed features in $^{60}$Zn arise from $T=0$ proton-neutron pairing correlations (as suggested in Ref. Zn61) or from deformation changes. The authors compare with the $A\sim 150$ region (e.g., $^{150}$Gd, $^{152}$Dy, $^{151}$Tb, $^{149}$Gd) where similar patterns were explained by consecutive neutron and proton alignments without proton-neutron pairing (Ref. NWJ.89). The results enable future development of CRHB theory to include explicit proton-neutron pairing and motivate exact particle number projection studies in the weak pairing regime.
