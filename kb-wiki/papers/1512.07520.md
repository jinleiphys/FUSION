# The $η^\prime$ transition form factor from space- and time-like experimental data

> The $η^\prime$ transition form factor is reanalyzed in view of the recent BESIII first observation of the Dalitz decay $η^\prime\toγe^+e^-$ in both space- and time-like regions at low and intermediate energies using the Padé approximants method. The present analysis provides a suitable parameterization for reproducing the measured form factor in the whole energy region and allows to extract the corresponding low-energy parameters together with a prediction of its values at the origin, related to $Γ_{η^\prime\toγγ}$, and the asymptotic limit. The $η$-$η^\prime$ mixing is reassessed within a mixing scheme compatible with the large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, with particular attention to the OZI-rule--violating parameters. The $J/ψ$, $Z\toη^{(\prime)}γ$ decays are also considered and predictions reported.

# The $\eta^\prime$ transition form factor from space- and time-like experimental data

## Key claim

The Padé approximant method, previously validated only in the space-like region, can be successfully extended into the time-like region for the $\eta^\prime$ transition form factor up to approximately 760 MeV, despite the presence of a branch cut from the $\pi\pi$ threshold, because the threshold discontinuity is sufficiently smooth (the imaginary part grows as $(s-4m_\pi^2)^{3/2}$) that the $P^L_1(s)$ sequence of Padé approximants remains an excellent approximation tool above the cut.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit of Padé approximants of the type $P^L_1(Q^2)$ to both space-like experimental data (from CELLO, CLEO, BABAR, and L3 collaborations) and new time-like data from the BESIII measurement of the $\eta^\prime\to\gamma e^+e^-$ Dalitz decay, using a $\chi^2$ function that includes the modulus-square of the form factor in the time-like region and optionally constrains the normalization from the two-photon partial width. The $\eta$-$\eta^\prime$ mixing parameters were then reassessed within a large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory framework at next-to-leading order, using the extracted form factor parameters to determine the OZI-rule-violating mixing parameters.

## Key numbers

- $F_{\eta^{\prime}\gamma\gamma}(0) = 0.344(5)(0)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ (statistical and systematic errors), from the combined fit with the two-photon width constraint
- Slope parameter $b_{\eta^{\prime}} = 1.31(4)(1)$, curvature $c_{\eta^{\prime}} = 1.74(9)(3)$, third derivative $d_{\eta^{\prime}} = 2.30(19)(21)$
- Asymptotic limit $\lim_{Q^{2}\to\infty} Q^{2}F_{\eta^{\prime}\gamma^{*}\gamma}(Q^{2}) = 0.254(3)\ \text{GeV}$, in agreement with the BABAR measurement of $0.251(19)(8)\ \text{GeV}$ at $q^{2} = -112\ \text{GeV}^{2}$
- Pole position of the Padé approximant: $\sqrt{s_{p}} = 0.833(14)\ \text{GeV}$ (constrained fit) or $0.831(13)\ \text{GeV}$ (unconstrained fit)
- Predicted $\Gamma_{\eta^{\prime}\to\gamma\gamma} = 4.35(13)\ \text{keV}$ (constrained) and $4.30(33)\ \text{keV}$ (unconstrained), compared to the PDG fitted value of $4.35(14)\ \text{keV}$
- The half-width rule gives an effective pole at $822 \pm 58\ \text{MeV}$, indicating safe applicability of Padé approximants up to about $760\ \text{MeV}$ in the time-like region

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of pseudoscalar transition form factors using Padé approximants (Escribano 2013, Masjuan 2012) by incorporating the first time-like data for the $\eta^\prime$ from BESIII (Ablikim 2015). It builds on the demonstration by Stollenwerk (2011) that the isovector contribution to the transition form factor is a Stieltjes function, for which Padé convergence is mathematically guaranteed in the space-like region and below the production threshold. The improved determination of low-energy parameters enables a more precise extraction of $\eta$-$\eta^\prime$ mixing parameters within the large-$N_c$ chiral perturbation theory framework, and provides predictions for $J/\psi$ and $Z$ radiative decays to $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ mesons. Future data from BESIII, MAMI, and Belle-II are expected to further refine these results.
