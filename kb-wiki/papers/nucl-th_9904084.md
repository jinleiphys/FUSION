# Hot Hypernuclear Matter in the Modified Quark Meson Coupling Model

> Hot hypernuclear matter is investigated in an explicit SU(3) quark model based on a mean field description of nonoverlapping baryon bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of scalar $σ, ζ$ and vector $ω, ϕ$ mesons. The $σ, ω$ mean fields are assumed to couple to the u,d-quarks while the $ζ, ϕ$ mean fields are coupled to the s-quark. The coupling constants of the mean fields with the quarks are assumed to satisfy SU(6) symmetry. The calculations take into account the medium dependence of the bag parameter on the scalar fields $σ, ζ$. We consider only the octet baryons $N,Λ,Σ,Ξ$ in hypernuclear matter. An ideal gas of the strange mesons $K$ and $K^{*}$ is introduced to keep zero net strangeness density. Our results for symmetric hypernuclear matter show that a phase transition takes place at a critical temperature around 180 MeV in which the scalar mean fields $σ, ζ$ take nonzero values at zero baryon density. Furthermore, the bag contants of the baryons decrease significantly at and above this critical temperature indicating the onset of quark deconfinement. The present results imply that the onset of quark deconfinement in SU(3) hypernuclear matter is much stronger than in SU(2) nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the Modified Quark Meson Coupling (MQMC) model, hot hypernuclear matter (including the full baryon octet) undergoes a phase transition at a critical temperature around 180 MeV, where the scalar mean fields sigma and zeta take nonzero values at zero baryon density and the bag constants decrease significantly, indicating the onset of quark deconfinement. The hedge is that this transition is described as "much stronger" than in SU(2) nuclear matter, but the critical temperature is given as "around 180 MeV" rather than a precisely determined value, and the authors note that lattice QCD results with two light quark flavors indicate the transition may be a rapid crossover rather than a true phase transition.

## Method

The authors extended the MQMC model to hot hypernuclear matter by introducing scalar zeta and vector phi meson fields coupled to the s-quark, in addition to the sigma and omega fields coupled to u,d-quarks, with coupling constants satisfying SU(6) symmetry. They considered the full baryon octet (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) in symmetric hypernuclear matter, introduced an ideal gas of K and K* mesons to enforce zero net strangeness density, and solved the self-consistency equations for the mean fields, baryon effective masses, and bag parameters at finite temperature and baryon density. The medium dependence of the bag parameter was included by coupling it exponentially to the scalar fields sigma and zeta.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the phase transition: around 180 MeV (stated as "around 180 MeV" in the abstract, with the text noting the rapid decrease in effective masses begins above 150 MeV and the sigma field shows dramatic increase at about 150 MeV)
- Free nucleon bag radius: R0 = 0.60 fm
- Free bag parameter: B0^(1/4) = 188.1 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter saturation density: rho_B0 = 0.17 fm^(-3)
- Current quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV
- Baryon masses reproduced at experimental values: M_N = 939 MeV, M_Lambda = 1157 MeV, M_Sigma = 1193 MeV, M_Xi = 1313 MeV
- Meson masses used: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_zeta = 975 MeV, m_phi = 1020 MeV, m_K = 495 MeV, m_K* = 892 MeV
- Vector meson coupling constant: g_omega^2/4pi = 5.24
- Scalar bag coupling constant: (g_sigma^B)^2/4pi = 3.69

## Context

This work extends the MQMC model, previously applied to normal (SU(2)) nuclear matter by Jin and Jennings (1996) and to hot nuclear matter by Zakout and Jaqaman (1999), to hypernuclear matter including strange baryons. It builds on the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and later developed by Saito and Thomas (1994) and others. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations by Gottlieb et al. (1997) and Bernard et al. (1997), which found a transition temperature around 140 MeV for two light quark flavors at zero baryon density. The paper enables future studies of the phase transition to quark-gluon plasma in systems with strangeness, and the authors note that the interaction of kaons with the meson fields will be considered in future work.
