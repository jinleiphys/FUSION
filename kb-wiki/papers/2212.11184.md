# Non-strange quark stars within resummed QCD

> The recently developed resummation technique known as {\it renormalization group optimized perturbation theory} (RGOPT) is employed in the evaluation of the EoS describing non-strange cold quark matter at NLO. Inspired by recent investigations, which suggest that stable quark matter can be made only of up and down quarks, the mass-radius relation for two flavor pure quark stars is evaluated and compared with the predictions from perturbative QCD (pQCD) at NNLO. This comparison explicitly shows that by being imbued with renormalization group properties, and a variational optimization procedure, the method allows for an efficient resummation of the perturbative series. Remarkably, when the renormalization scale is chosen so as to reproduce maximum mass stars with M=$2-2.3M_\odot$, one obtains a mass-radius curve compatible with the masses and radii of the pulsars PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, and the compact object HESS J1731-347. Moreover, the scale dependence of the EoS (and mass-radius relation) obtained with the RGOPT is greatly improved when compared to that of pQCD. This seminal application to the description of quark stars shows that the RGOPT represents a robust alternative to pQCD when describing compressed quark matter.

## Key claim

The renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) at next-to-leading order (NLO) can describe non-strange (two-flavor) quark stars whose mass-radius curves are compatible with observational data from pulsars PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, and HESS J1731-347, but only when the renormalization scale is chosen to reproduce maximum mass stars in the range M = 2–2.3 solar masses.

## Method

The authors adapted the RGOPT, previously developed for three-flavor QCD, to the two-flavor case with massless up and down quarks at zero temperature and finite chemical potential. They constructed a thermodynamically consistent equation of state (EoS) by adding a correction term to the pressure to account for the density-dependent renormalization scale, then solved the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for non-strange pure quark stars under charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- For the NLO RGOPT with renormalization scale coefficient X = 2 (central value), the maximum quark star mass is M_max = 1.03 M_sun with radius R_max = 6.30 km and central baryon density 11.9 rho_0 (where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}).
- For the NLO RGOPT with X = 4, the maximum mass is M_max = 2.47 M_sun with radius R_max = 15.5 km and central baryon density 2.90 rho_0.
- For the NLO RGOPT with X = 1, the maximum mass is M_max = 0.53 M_sun with radius R_max = 3.20 km and central baryon density 32.8 rho_0.
- For NNLO pQCD with X = 2, the maximum mass is M_max = 1.68 M_sun with radius R_max = 11.0 km and central baryon density 4.94 rho_0.
- For NNLO pQCD with X = 4, the maximum mass is M_max = 5.65 M_sun with radius R_max = 30.7 km and central baryon density 0.77 rho_0.
- For NNLO pQCD with X = 1, the maximum mass is M_max = 0.51 M_sun with radius R_max = 3.19 km and central baryon density 33.5 rho_0.
- The baryon chemical potential at zero pressure for RGOPT (X = 2) is mu_B = 1.46 GeV with baryon density 2.2 rho_0; for pQCD (X = 2) it is mu_B = 1.16 GeV with baryon density 0.7 rho_0.

## Context

This work extends the RGOPT method, previously applied to vacuum QCD and thermal field theories by Kneur et al. (2011, 2013, 2015, 2019), to the description of compact stars composed of non-strange quark matter, motivated by the suggestion of Holdom et al. (2017) that stable quark matter may consist only of up and down quarks. The results are compared with NNLO perturbative QCD calculations by Fraga et al. (2001, 2004) and Kurkela et al. (2009), showing that RGOPT dramatically reduces the renormalization scale dependence that plagues pQCD. This enables future studies of quark stars and hybrid stars with a resummation method that retains RG properties while remaining perturbatively controlled.
