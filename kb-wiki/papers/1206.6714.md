# Nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes and backward electroproduction of pions

> Baryon to meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), non-diagonal matrix elements of the nonlocal three quark operator between a nucleon and a meson state, extend the concept of generalized parton distributions. These non-perturbative objects which encode the information on three quark correlations inside the nucleon may be accessed experimentally in backward meson electroproduction reactions. We suggest a general framework for modelling nucleon to pion (pi N) TDAs employing the spectral representation for pi N TDAs in terms of quadruple distributions. The factorized Ansatz for quadruple distributions with input from the soft-pion theorem for pi N TDAs is proposed. It is to be complemented with a D-term like contribution from the nucleon exchange in the cross channel. We present our estimates of the unpolarized cross section and of the transverse target single spin asymmetry for backward pion electroproduction within the QCD collinear factorization approach in which the non-perturbative part of the amplitude involves pi N TDAs. The cross section is sizable enough to be studied in high luminosity experiments such as J-lab@12GeV and EIC.

# Nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes and backward electroproduction of pions

## Key claim

Backward pion electroproduction can be described within QCD collinear factorization using nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), and the predicted unpolarized cross section is large enough to be measured at JLab@12GeV and the EIC, provided the factorized description holds in the valence region where the transverse target single spin asymmetry is predicted to be sizable.

## Method

The authors construct a two-component model for πN TDAs using a spectral representation in terms of quadruple distributions, with a factorized Ansatz that takes input from the soft-pion theorem at ξ=1 (where TDAs reduce to combinations of nucleon distribution amplitudes) and a D-term-like contribution from u-channel nucleon exchange. They then compute the leading-order (in αs and 1/Q) hard scattering amplitude for backward pion electroproduction by evaluating 21 diagrams, and use this to estimate the unpolarized transverse cross section and the single transverse target spin asymmetry for π+ and π0 production off protons.

## Key numbers

- Unpolarized cross section estimates are presented for Q² = 10 GeV² and u = -0.5 GeV², with magnitude in nb/sr as a function of xB, using the CZ solution for nucleon DAs as phenomenological input.
- The unpolarized transverse cross section scales as 1/Q⁸, compared to the 1/Q⁴ behavior in the conventional hard meson production regime.
- The single transverse target spin asymmetry is computed for Q² = 10 GeV² and u = -0.5 GeV² as a function of xB, and is predicted to be sizable in the valence region.

## Context

This work extends the concept of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) to baryon-to-meson transition distribution amplitudes, first considered by Frankfurt et al. (1999). The backward kinematics regime is complementary to the conventional generalized Bjorken limit where GPDs apply (Collins et al., 1996). The modelling strategy is analogous to Radyushkin's double distribution Ansatz for GPDs, but adapted to the TDA case where no forward limit (ξ=0) exists; instead the soft-pion theorem at ξ=1 provides the normalization. The results suggest that backward pion electroproduction can be studied at JLab@12GeV and the EIC (Boer et al., 2011), and first experimental results are expected soon.
