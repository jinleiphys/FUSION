# Quark Matter in a Parallel Electric and Magnetic Field Background: Chiral Phase Transition and Equilibration of Chiral Density

> In this article we study spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking for quark matter in the background of static and homogeneous parallel electric field $\bm E$ and magnetic field $\bm B$. We use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a local kernel interaction to compute the relevant quantities to describe chiral symmetry breaking at finite temperature for a wide range of $E$ and $B$. We study the effect of this background on inverse catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking for $E$ and $B$ of the same order of magnitude. We then focus on the effect of equilibration of chiral density, $n_5$, produced dynamically by axial anomaly on the critical temperature. The equilibration of $n_5$, a consequence of chirality flipping processes in the thermal bath, allows for the introduction of the chiral chemical potential, $μ_5$, which is computed self-consistently as a function of temperature and field strength by coupling the number equation to the gap equation, and solving the two within an expansion in $E/T^2$, $B/T^2$ and $μ_5^2/T^2$. We find that even if chirality is produced and equilibrates within a relaxation time $τ_M$, it does not change drastically the thermodynamics, with particular reference to the inverse catalysis induced by the external fields, as long as the average $μ_5$ at equilibrium is not too large.

## Key claim

The paper claims that even when a nonzero chiral density is dynamically produced and equilibrates via chirality-flipping processes in a parallel electric and magnetic field background, it does not drastically change the thermodynamics of the chiral phase transition, specifically, the inverse catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking induced by the fields persists, as long as the average chiral chemical potential at equilibrium remains small compared to temperature.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a local interaction kernel and a proper-time regularization scheme to compute the quark gap equation and thermodynamic potential at finite temperature in the presence of static, homogeneous, parallel electric and magnetic fields. They compute the equilibrium chiral density from the Schwinger pair production rate combined with a relaxation time approximation for chirality-flipping processes, then solve the gap equation and the number equation self-consistently within an expansion in \(E/T^2\), \(B/T^2\), and \(\mu_5^2/T^2\) to obtain the chiral chemical potential and its effect on the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration at \(E=B=0\) is \(T_c \approx 166\) MeV.
* For a pure electric field of \(eE = 8 m_\pi^2\), the critical temperature is lowered compared to the zero-field case; for a pure magnetic field of \(eB = 8 m_\pi^2\), the critical temperature is increased.
* The relaxation time for chirality is assumed to be \(\tau_M = c / M_q\), where \(M_q\) is the constituent quark mass and \(c\) is a dimensionless parameter; the equilibrium chiral density is \(n_5^{\mathrm{eq}} = \frac{q_f^2 (eE)(eB)}{2\pi^2} e^{-\frac{\pi M^2}{|q_f eE|}} \tau_M\).
* The ratio of equilibrium chiral densities for u and d quarks is \(\frac{n_{5u}^\mathrm{eq}}{n_{5d}^\mathrm{eq}} = \frac{q_u^2}{q_d^2} e^{-\frac{\pi M^2}{|eE|}\left(\frac{1}{q_u} - \frac{1}{|q_d|}\right)}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry breaking in external electromagnetic fields, such as those by Klevansky (1992), Suganuma (1990), and Babansky (1997), which examined inverse catalysis at zero temperature, by computing the critical temperature for chiral restoration at finite temperature. It also builds on studies of systems with finite chiral density and chiral chemical potential, such as those by Ruggieri (2011, 2016) and Braguta (2015, 2016), by self-consistently computing the chiral chemical potential from dynamically produced chirality rather than introducing it by hand. The results enable future studies of the chiral phase diagram with dynamically generated chirality, particularly in heavy-ion collision contexts where strong parallel electric and magnetic fields may arise.
