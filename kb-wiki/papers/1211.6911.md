# Dynamical entropy of dense QCD states

> We discuss dense states of QCD matter formed in high-energy hadronic and heavy-ion collisions from the point of view of statistical physics of non-equilibrium processes. For this sake, we first propose a formulation of the dynamical entropy of dense QCD states in the "saturation regime" leading to a color glass condensate (CGC). The statistical physics description amounts to describe the modification of the color correlation length with energy as a compression process for which non equilibrium thermodynamic properties are applicable. We derive an expression of the dynamical entropy in terms of the rapidity evolution of the unintegrated gluon distributions in the colliding nuclei, verifying suitable positivity and irreversibility properties. We extend this approach to the initial pre-equilibrium (glasma) state of an heavy-ion collision. It allows for a definition of the initial entropy before the evolution towards the hydrodynamic regime as a function of the glasma correlation length and an overlap parameter characterizing the low-momentum spectrum of the glasma state. This initial entropy, by extension to the N=4 SYM theory, is then matched as the key input parameter to the strong coupling evaluation of thermalization towards the hydrodynamic regime based on the AdS/CFT correspondence. It thus allows to cast a bridge between the weak and strong coupling phases of an heavy-ion reaction.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "dynamical entropy" for dense QCD states in the saturation regime that does not assume thermodynamic equilibrium, unlike prior macroscopic approaches. This entropy is defined microscopically from the rapidity evolution of unintegrated gluon distributions and is shown to match the macroscopic entropy derived from gluon production in dense-dilute collisions, but only under the condition that geometric scaling holds.

## Method

The author identifies the rapidity evolution of the color glass condensate (CGC) as a non-equilibrium compression process, where the color correlation length shrinks with increasing energy. Using the Hatano-Sasa identity from statistical physics, the dynamical entropy is defined as the relative entropy between gluon momentum probability distributions at two different rapidities. This formalism is applied to Gaussian models of unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs), specifically a generalization of the Golec-Biernat Wusthoff (GBW) model, to compute the entropy density for a CGC state and extended to the initial glasma state of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

* The dynamical entropy per gluon degree of freedom for Gaussian CGC models with overlap parameter κ is Σ = κ{(R₁²/R₂² − 1) − log(R₁²/R₂²)}, where R₁ and R₂ are the initial and final color correlation lengths, and the inequality is strict for Y₂ > Y₁.
* For the GBW model (κ = 2) in proton-proton collisions, the total entropy density per unit transverse area and rapidity scales as (1/A_T)(dS/dy) = [2μ(N_c²−1)/(π²α_s N_c)] Q₂² at large Q₂²/Q₁², where μ is a parameter counting effective gluonic degrees of freedom.
* Matching the microscopic dynamical entropy to the macroscopic entropy of Kutak (2011) requires μ ≡ 3π/2, a pure number characterizing the average number of gluons inside a colorless cluster of the CGC medium.
* For a cyclic compression-expansion process (R₁ → R₂ → R₁), the overall entropy balance is ΔΣ^circle = κ(R₁²−R₂²)²/(R₁²R₂²), which for R₁ ≫ R₂ scales as κ R₁²/R₂², demonstrating strong irreversibility.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic thermodynamic approach to CGC entropy proposed by Kutak (2011), which assumed thermal equilibrium and used the GBW model for gluon production in dense-dilute collisions. The author provides a microscopic statistical physics foundation based on the Hatano-Sasa identity, which does not require equilibrium assumptions. The dynamical entropy is then connected to the AdS/CFT-based thermalization scenario of Janik (2007) and Przemek (2010) by defining the initial entropy of the glasma state as a function of the glasma correlation length and an overlap parameter, thereby bridging the weak-coupling CGC phase and the strong-coupling hydrodynamic regime in heavy-ion collisions.
