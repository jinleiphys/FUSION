# Asymptotic perfect fluid dynamics as a consequence of AdS/CFT

> We study the dynamics of strongly interacting gauge-theory matter (modelling quark-gluon plasma) in a boost-invariant setting using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Using Fefferman-Graham coordinates and with the help of holographic renormalization, we show that perfect fluid hydrodynamics emerges at large times as the unique nonsingular asymptotic solution of the nonlinear Einstein equations in the bulk. The gravity dual can be interpreted as a black hole moving off in the fifth dimension. Asymptotic solutions different from perfect fluid behaviour can be ruled out by the appearance of curvature singularities in the dual bulk geometry. Subasymptotic deviations from perfect fluid behaviour remain possible within the same framework.

## Key claim

Perfect fluid hydrodynamics emerges at large proper times as the unique nonsingular asymptotic solution of the nonlinear Einstein equations in the bulk, but subasymptotic deviations from perfect fluid behaviour remain possible within the same framework.

## Method

The authors impose boost invariance, transverse translational and rotational symmetry, and conformal symmetry on the gauge-theory energy-momentum tensor, parametrizing its components by a single function f(τ) ∼ τ^{-s}. Using Fefferman-Graham coordinates and holographic renormalization, they solve the vacuum Einstein equations with negative cosmological constant order by order in the holographic coordinate z, then take the large-τ limit to obtain analytic asymptotic solutions for the dual metric. They evaluate the curvature scalar R^{μναβ}R_{μναβ} to test for physical singularities in the bulk geometry.

## Key numbers

- The perfect fluid case corresponds to s = 4/3, giving f(τ) = e₀/τ^{4/3} and a temperature scaling T(τ) ∼ τ^{-1/3}.
- The free streaming case corresponds to s = 1, giving f(τ) = \tilde{e}/τ.
- The curvature scalar for the perfect fluid reaches a finite maximum of R^{μναβ}R_{μναβ} = 112 at v = 1/∜3, starting from a boundary value of 40.
- For s ≠ 4/3, the curvature scalar diverges at v = Δ(s)^{-1/4}, where Δ(s) = √[(3s² − 8s + 8)/24].

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT approach to time-dependent, boost-invariant settings inspired by Bjorken (1983) hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions. It builds on holographic renormalization methods developed by Skenderis (2002) and on earlier static black hole solutions of Balasubramanian and Kraus (1999) and Myers (1999). The result shows that the requirement of nonsingular bulk geometry selects perfect fluid behaviour asymptotically, ruling out free-streaming or other power-law behaviours with s ≠ 4/3, and opens the door to studying subasymptotic viscous corrections and the collision of shockwave geometries (Emparan, 2001) as models of heavy-ion reactions.
