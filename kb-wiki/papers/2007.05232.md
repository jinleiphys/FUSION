# Extended quantum diffusion approach to reactions of astrophysical interests

> The quantum diffusion approach is extended to low energy fusion (capture) reactions of light- and medium-mass nuclei. The dependence of the friction parameter on bombarding energy is taken into account. A simple analytic expression is obtained for the capture probability at extreme sub-barrier energies. The calculated cross-sections are in a good agreement with the experimental data. The fusion excitation functions calculated within the quantum diffusion and WKB approaches are compared and presented in the astrophysical $S$-factor representation.

## Key claim

The quantum diffusion approach, previously successful for heavy-ion fusion near and below the Coulomb barrier, is extended to light- and medium-mass nuclei at extreme sub-barrier energies by introducing an energy-dependent friction coefficient and a new method for determining the inverted-oscillator frequency, yielding a simple analytic expression for the capture probability that becomes applicable at energies 2–3 MeV below the barrier.

## Method

The authors treat the relative motion of colliding nuclei as a single collective coordinate coupled to internal excitations, described by a non-Markovian quantum master equation. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated using a double-folding formalism with density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction and quadrupole deformation effects. The friction coefficient is made coordinate-dependent by setting it proportional to the square of the nuclear force gradient, and the internal excitation bandwidth is related to the friction via an exponential relation; both quantities are evaluated at the external turning point, which depends on bombarding energy. The capture probability is obtained from an analytic expression derived in the low-temperature limit, and the capture cross section is summed over angular momenta and averaged over orientation angles for deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the constant friction coefficient at the barrier position, the value used is ℏλ_b = ℏλ(R=R_b) = 2 MeV, with a corresponding constant internal excitation bandwidth ℏγ_b = ℏγ(R=R_b) = 32 MeV.
- The parameter k₁ in the relation between γ and λ is defined as k₁ = α/√(μω²_b) with α = (π/2) MeV¹/² fm⁻¹.
- The astrophysical S-factor for all studied reactions exhibits a maximum at E_c.m. ≈ (2/3)V_b, where V_b is the Coulomb barrier height for spherical interacting nuclei.
- For the ¹²C+¹²C reaction, the astrophysical S-factor maximum is found around E_c.m. = 3.5–4 MeV based on recent experimental data from Jiang (2018).
- Variation of the normalization parameter λ_b by a factor of 2 changes the calculated capture cross sections by less than 5%.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously developed by Sargsyan, Adamian, Antonenko, and collaborators (2012–2016) for heavy-ion fusion near and below the Coulomb barrier to light- and medium-mass nuclei at extreme sub-barrier energies relevant for stellar nucleosynthesis. The approach is applied to fusion reactions involving carbon, oxygen, and silicon isotopes that drive nucleosynthesis in novae, supernovae, and stellar burning, complementing earlier experimental studies by Jiang (2018), Fang (2017), and Montagnoli (2017). The calculated S-factors show a maximum at about two-thirds of the Coulomb barrier height, which if confirmed by further measurements would reduce previously predicted astrophysical reaction rates at the lowest energies.
