# Energy, angular momentum and pressure force distributions inside nucleons

> We review some of the recent developments regarding mass, angular momentum and pressure forces inside hadrons. These properties are all encoded in the energy-momentum tensor of the system, which is described at the non-perturbative level in terms of gravitational form factors. Similarly to electromagnetic form factors, Fourier transforms of gravitational form factors allow one to map out the distribution of the above mechanical properties in position space, providing a whole new way of studying in detail the internal structure of hadrons.

## Key claim
The paper reviews recent developments in mapping mass, angular momentum, and pressure force distributions inside nucleons using gravitational form factors (GFFs), but notes that relativistic corrections plague three-dimensional Breit-frame distributions, making two-dimensional light-front distributions a more reliable alternative.

## Method
The author reviews the parametrization of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) for spin-1/2 and spin-1 targets in terms of gravitational form factors, and describes how Fourier transforms of these GFFs yield spatial distributions of energy density, radial and transverse pressure, and angular momentum. The analysis uses the Breit frame for three-dimensional distributions and light-front coordinates for two-dimensional distributions, with a multipole parametrization of nucleon GFFs and a scalar diquark model providing illustrative examples.

## Key numbers
- The energy density in the nucleon is almost equally shared between quark and gluon contributions, with total isotropic pressure integrating to zero as required by balance equations.
- For spin-1 targets, the forward matrix element of the EMT reduces to a form involving GFFs at t=0: the partial proper energy is U_a = [G^a_1(0) - 1/2 G^a_8(0)] M, and the isotropic pressure-volume work is W_a = [1/2 G^a_8(0) + 1/6 G^a_7(0)] M.
- The total angular momentum sum rule for spin-1/2 targets is sum_a [A_a(0) + B_a(0)] = 1, where A_a(t) and B_a(t) are GFFs.

## Context
This review extends the work of Polyakov (2002, 2018) on defining spatial distributions of mechanical properties via Fourier transforms of GFFs, and builds on Ji (1996) who connected GPDs to GFFs and derived the angular momentum sum rule. It enables future studies of hadron mass and spin at the spatial distribution level, and suggests that stability conditions from pressure forces may provide new insights into confinement, motivating further constraints from exclusive experiments and Lattice QCD.
