# Size Effects in Heavy Ions Fragmentation

> Rise-Plateau Caloric curves for different Heavy Ion collisions have been obtained, in the range of experimental observations. Limit temperature decreases when the residual size is increased, in agreement with recent theoretical analysis of experimental results reported by other Collaborations. Besides, promptly emitted particles influence on temperature plateau is shown. LATINO binary interaction semiclassical model is used to reproduce the inter-nucleonic forces via Pandharipande Potential and fragments are detected with an Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm.

## Key claim

The paper shows that "rise-plateau" caloric curves can be obtained from dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions, and that the limit temperature decreases when the residual size increases, in agreement with recent experimental analyses by Natowitz et al. The authors caution that this result depends on properly identifying promptly emitted particles and the fragmentation time using persistence coefficients.

## Method

The authors used the LATINO semiclassical molecular dynamics model with a Pandharipande binary interaction potential to simulate central collisions of Ag+Ag droplets. Fragments were identified using the Minimum Spanning Tree in Energy (MSTE) algorithm, and the fragmentation time was determined using microscopic persistence coefficients. Temperature was computed from the kinetic energy of nucleons in the participant region using kinetic gas theory, while excitation energy was calculated from the energy deposited in the residual after removing promptly emitted particles.

## Key numbers

- Projectile kinetic energy range: from E_beam = 800 MeV in the center-of-mass system, with about two hundred collisions per projectile energy
- Energy conservation maintained at least to 0.05% using the Verlet algorithm
- The biggest fragment after the coalescent stage reaches approximately 80% to 90% of the total mass
- Promptly emitted particles (PEPs) are defined as unbound light clusters with mass less than or equal to 4 detected at time t_pre that remain unbound at any posterior time
- The energy remaining in the system after prompt emission shows a saturation behavior, indicating a limit to the energy that can be transferred in a collision

## Context

This work extends previous molecular dynamics studies of caloric curves in uniformly excited systems by Strachan and Dorso (2000) and contained systems by Sugawa and Horiuchi, but applies the approach to actual colliding nuclear systems. The results confirm the experimental analysis by Natowitz et al. showing that limit temperature decreases with increasing residual size. The study also addresses the influence of promptly emitted particles on the temperature plateau, a complication absent in contained or infinite systems. This enables future studies on the role of collision dynamics in forming the plateau characteristic of the coexistence region for different residual sizes.
