# The QCD Pomeron in Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions: II. Heavy Quark Production

> The heavy quark production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions is investigated, in particular we focus on the results from the coherent interactions given by the two-photon process. One addresses the heavy quark total cross sections at photon level considering the saturation model and the BFKL dynamics in the color dipole picture. The corresponding cross sections at nuclear level are presented. It is verified that the QCD dynamics implies an enhancement of the cross section in comparison with previous calculations.

## Key claim

The QCD dynamics (saturation model and BFKL approach) implies an enhancement of the heavy quark production cross section in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions by approximately a factor of two compared to previous calculations that considered only the quark-box diagram (QPM) and resolved contributions, but the analysis would not allow to discriminate between the distinct dynamical approaches at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors calculated the total cross sections for heavy quark pair production (charm and bottom) in two-photon collisions using the color dipole picture, employing two models for the dipole-dipole cross section: the saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1999) and a leading-order BFKL dipole formulation. They then folded these photon-level cross sections with the effective two-photon luminosity for ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at LHC energies (sqrt(s)=5500 GeV) to obtain the nuclear-level cross sections, including contributions from both single heavy quark pair production and double heavy quark pair production.

## Key numbers

- For inclusive charm pair production in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5500 GeV, the cross section is enhanced by approximately a factor of 1.8 compared to predictions from Klein and Vogt (previous work), with the uncertainty from the gamma-gamma luminosity treatment estimated at about 15%.
- For inclusive bottom pair production at LHC energies, the deviation between saturation and BFKL approaches reaches up to 10%, and the predictions are a factor of two larger than those obtained by Klein and Vogt.
- The double charm pair production cross section is two orders of magnitude smaller than the inclusive charm pair production cross section, with a difference of about 20% between saturation model and BFKL predictions.
- At RHIC energies, the maximal two-photon energy W_gamma_gamma is about 6 GeV, where the QCD Pomeron effects are not important and results are similar to those of Klein and Vogt.
- At LHC energies, the two-photon energy can reach up to W=160 GeV for ultraperipheral collisions.
- The charm mass was taken as m_c=1.3 GeV and the bottom mass as m_b=4.5 GeV; using m_c=1.2 GeV increases the cross section by approximately 25%.
- The two-photon cross sections are at least 1000 times smaller than the photoproduction cross section, making experimental separation very hard.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of QCD Pomeron effects in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions previously initiated by the same authors for double diffractive J/Psi production (Goncalves and Machado, 2003). It complements the detailed study of heavy quark production in two-photon collisions by Klein and Vogt, who considered only the quark-box diagram and resolved contributions, by including the dipole-dipole interaction that becomes important at LHC energies where W_gamma_gamma can reach 200 GeV. The results motivate further studies of mass and transverse momentum distributions to discriminate between photon-Pomeron and two-photon processes, and show that double heavy quark pair production may be competitive with photoproduction background since the latter requires multiple interactions that are suppressed by a factor of about 1000.
