# Time-reversed particle-vibration loops and nuclear Gamow-Teller response

> The nuclear response theory for charge-exchange modes in the relativistic particle-vibration coupling approach is extended to include for the first time particle-vibration coupling effects in the ground state of the parent nucleus. In a framework based on the effective meson-nucleon Lagrangian, we investigate the role of such complex ground-state correlations in the description of Gamow-Teller transitions in $^{90}$Zr in both (p,n) and (n,p) channels. The particle-vibration coupling effects are calculated without introducing new parameters. We find that this new correlation mechanism is fully responsible for the appearance of the strength in the (n,p) branch. Comparison of our results to the available experimental data shows a very good agreement up to excitation energies beyond the giant resonance region when taking into account a phenomenological admixture of the isovector spin monopole transitions. The parent-daughter binding-energy differences are also greatly improved by the inclusion of the new correlations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including particle-vibration coupling effects in the ground state of the parent nucleus (GSC$_{PVC}$) is fully responsible for the appearance of Gamow-Teller strength in the (n,p) branch of $^{90}$Zr, and that these correlations greatly improve the parent-daughter binding-energy differences, but the good agreement with experimental data up to excitation energies beyond the giant resonance region requires also a phenomenological admixture of the isovector spin monopole transitions.

## Method

The authors extended the proton-neutron relativistic time-blocking approximation (pn-RTBA) to include, for the first time, particle-vibration coupling effects in the ground state of the parent nucleus, using a framework based on the effective meson-nucleon Lagrangian with the NL3* parametrization. They calculated Gamow-Teller transition strength distributions in $^{90}$Zr for both (p,n) and (n,p) channels by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with additional backward-going diagrams that introduce complex ground-state correlations, without introducing new parameters. The phonon spectrum included natural-parity neutral phonons from $0^+$ to $6^+$ and all-parity charge-exchange phonons from $0^\pm$ to $7^\pm$ with excitation energy up to 20 MeV, and single-particle states were limited to a window of 50 MeV around the Fermi levels.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy difference BE($^{90}$Zr) - BE($^{90}$Nb): experimental value 6.893 MeV; pn-RRPA gives 6.160 MeV; pn-RTBA without GSC$_{PVC}$ gives 2.760 MeV; pn-RTBA with GSC$_{PVC}$ gives 5.430 MeV.
- Binding energy difference BE($^{90}$Zr) - BE($^{90}$Y): experimental value 1.496 MeV; pn-RRPA gives 2.200 MeV; pn-RTBA without GSC$_{PVC}$ gives -1.090 MeV; pn-RTBA with GSC$_{PVC}$ gives 1.880 MeV.
- The peak around 4.5 MeV in the GT$_+$ channel appears mainly due to transitions from proton-$1g_{9/2}$ to neutron-$1g_{7/2}$ with absolute transition density 0.347, and from proton-$2p_{3/2}$ to neutron-$2p_{1/2}$ with absolute transition density 0.182.
- The hybrid operator parameter $\alpha$ was adjusted to $9.1 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-2}$ for the GT$_+$ branch and $7.5 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-2}$ for the GT$_-$ branch.
- The Ikeda sum rule violation was 1.23% in $^{90}$Zr with a cut-off energy of 50 MeV on the nucleon states entering the PVC mechanism.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic particle-vibration coupling approach for charge-exchange modes, which previously included PVC in the description of daughter nucleus states but not in the parent ground state (Marketin 2012, Litvinova 2014, Robin 2016, Robin 2018). It builds on the non-relativistic framework of Kamerdzhiev (1994, 1997) who showed that such ground-state correlations from PVC could be consistently included for electromagnetic transitions. The results enable more accurate predictions for beta-decay and electron-capture rates in astrophysical simulations, and could improve RPA-based approaches for neutrinoless double-beta decay calculations, where accurate description of ground-state correlations in both branches is critical.
