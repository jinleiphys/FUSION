# Measurement of spin observables in the quasi-free np-> {pp}_s pi- reaction at 353 MeV

> The transverse spin correlations A_{x,x} and A_{y,y} have been measured in the pol{d} pol{p} -> p_spec {pp}_s pi- reaction at COSY-ANKE at 353 MeV per nucleon. Here {pp}_s denotes a proton-proton pair with low excitation energy, which is dominantly in the 1S0 state. By measuring three protons in the final state it was possible to extract events where there was a spectator proton p_spec so that the reaction could be interpreted in terms of quasi-free pol{n} pol{p} -> {pp}_s pi-. The proton analyzing power in this reaction was also deduced from this data set by averaging over the polarization of the deuteron beam. The values of A_y^p were shown to be consistent with a refined analysis of our earlier results obtained with a polarized proton incident on a deuterium target. Taking these data in combination with our earlier measurements of the differential cross sections and analyzing powers in the pol{p} p -> {pp}_s pi^0 reaction, a more robust partial wave decomposition was achieved. Three different acceptable solutions were found and the only way of resolving this ambiguity without further theoretical input would be through a measurement of the mixed spin-correlation parameter A_{x,z}.

## Key claim

The transverse spin correlations A_{x,x} and A_{y,y} were measured in the quasi-free np -> {pp}_s pi- reaction at 353 MeV per nucleon, and the proton analyzing power A_y^p was also extracted; however, three distinct acceptable partial-wave solutions were found, and the ambiguity can only be resolved experimentally through a measurement of the mixed spin-correlation parameter A_{x,z}.

## Method

The experiment was performed at COSY-ANKE using a polarized deuteron beam incident on a polarized hydrogen gas target in inverse kinematics, with the spectator proton detected in the forward detector system. Events were selected by requiring three protons in the final state from the dp -> p_spec {pp}_s pi- reaction, with the diproton excitation energy cut E_pp < 3 MeV to isolate the 1S0 state. The beam and target polarizations were calibrated using the simultaneously measured np -> d pi0 reaction, and the spin-correlation parameters were extracted from the azimuthal dependence of the pion production cross section.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 353 MeV per nucleon
- Excitation energy cut: E_pp < 3 MeV for selecting 1S0 diproton events
- Resolution of diproton excitation energy: sigma(E_pp) < 0.3 MeV
- Beam polarizations: P_up = +61 +/- 4%, P_down = -50 +/- 3% (measured at injection energy of 75.6 MeV)
- Target proton polarizations: above 90% achieved with the atomic beam source
- Luminosities: up to approximately 10^29 cm^-2 s^-1
- Target density: approximately 10^13 atoms/cm^2 from the storage cell
- Stored beam intensity: 3-12 x 10^9 deuterons per cycle

## Context

This work extends the COSY-ANKE program of complete measurements of NN -> {pp}_s pi reactions at low energy, following earlier measurements of differential cross sections and analyzing powers in the polarized p p -> {pp}_s pi0 reaction (Tsirkov et al., 2012) and the quasi-free polarized p n -> {pp}_s pi- reaction (Dymov et al., 2012). The results enable a more robust partial wave decomposition of both I=1 and I=0 production amplitudes, building on earlier pioneering measurements at TRIUMF (Hahn et al., 1999; Duncan et al., 1998) and the CELSIUS measurements showing large pion d-wave contributions (Bilger et al., 2001). The residual three-solution ambiguity in the partial wave analysis can only be resolved by a future measurement of A_{x,z}.
