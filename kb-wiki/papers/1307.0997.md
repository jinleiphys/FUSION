# Faddeev-type calculation of three-body nuclear reactions including core excitation

> The core excitation, being an important reaction mechanism, so far is not properly included in most calculations of three-body nuclear reactions. We aim to include the excitation of the core nucleus using an exact Faddeev-type framework for nuclear reactions in the three-body (core + neutron + proton) system. We employ Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for the three-particle transition operators and solve them in the momentum-space framework. The Coulomb interaction is included via the method of screening and renormalization. We calculate elastic, inelastic, and transfer reactions involving 10Be and 24Mg nuclear cores. Important effects of the core excitation are found, often improving the description of the experimental data. In the neutron transfer reactions the core excitation effect is by far not just a simple reduction of the cross section by the respective spectroscopic factor. This indicates that widely used extraction of the spectroscopic factors from the ratio of the experimental and theoretical transfer cross sections is unreliable approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that core excitation effects in three-body nuclear reactions (core + neutron + proton) can be included exactly using Faddeev-type AGS equations, and shows that in neutron transfer reactions the core excitation effect is not simply a reduction of the cross section by the spectroscopic factor, indicating that extracting spectroscopic factors from the ratio of experimental and theoretical transfer cross sections is an unreliable approach.

## Method

The authors solve Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for three-particle transition operators in momentum space, with the Coulomb interaction included via the method of screening and renormalization. The Hilbert space is extended to include both the core ground state (A) and excited state (A*) sectors, coupled by the core-nucleon interaction. Calculations are performed for elastic, inelastic, and transfer reactions involving 10Be and 24Mg cores, using several nucleon-core interaction models: a single-particle (SP) model neglecting core excitation, a rotational model with core excitation (CX), and a hybrid model (CXn) where only the neutron-core interaction includes excitation.

## Key numbers

- Core excitation energy: 3.368 MeV for 10Be, 1.369 MeV for 24Mg
- For 10Be: deformation parameter beta_2 = 0.67, deformation length delta_2 = 1.664 fm
- For 24Mg: two calculations with beta_2 = 0.4 (delta_2 = 1.352 fm) and beta_2 = 0.6 (delta_2 = 2.028 fm)
- For 11Be ground state (1/2+): spectroscopic factor 0.855 for s-wave neutron coupled to 0+ core ground state; binding energy -0.504 MeV
- For 11Be excited state (1/2-): spectroscopic factor 0.786 for p-wave neutron coupled to 0+ core ground state; binding energy -0.184 MeV
- In d + 10Be elastic scattering at large angles (Theta_cm > 80 degrees), core excitation reduces cross section by a factor of 2 to 5
- For transfer to 11Be ground state at Ed = 41 MeV, the ratio R_CX reaches 0.47, significantly deviating from the spectroscopic factor of 0.855
- For transfer to 11Be excited state (1/2-), deviations from spectroscopic factor are moderate, up to 10%

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-type AGS framework previously applied to three-nucleon systems with Delta-isobar excitation (Deltuva 2003, 2005) and to A+n+p systems with inert cores (Deltuva 2007, 2009) by now including dynamic core excitation. It contrasts with simpler approaches like DWBA and ADWA that assume core excitation effects simply reduce transfer cross sections by spectroscopic factors, and with XCDCC calculations (Summers 2006, 2007) that gave contradicting results for halo nucleus breakup. The method enables rigorous testing of dynamic models with core excitation and can be extended with more sophisticated nonlocal potentials and deformed optical potentials.
