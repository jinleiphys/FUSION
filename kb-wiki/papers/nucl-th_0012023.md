# Diquarks and Density

> We describe aspects of the role that diquark correlations play in understanding baryon structure and interactions. The significance of diquarks in that application motivates a study of the possibility that dense hadronic matter may exhibit diquark condensation; i.e., quark-quark pairing promoted by a quark chemical potential. A Gorkov-Nambu-like gap equation is introduced for QCD and analysed for 2-colour QCD (QC2D) and, in two qualitatively different truncations, for QCD itself. Among other interesting features, we illustrate that QC2D with massive fermions undergoes a second-order transition to a superfluid phase when the chemical potential exceeds 0.5 m.pi. In the QCD application we illustrate that the sigma= <bar-q q> .neq. 0 phase, which determines the properties of the mass spectrum at zero temperature and chemical potential, is unstable with respect to the superfluid phase when the chemical potential exceeds approx. 2 sigma, and that at this point the diquark gap is large, approx. 0.5 sigma. The superfluid phase survives to temperatures greater than that expected in the core of compact stars.

## Key claim

Diquark condensation (quark-quark Cooper pairing) is possible in dense hadronic matter, and in QCD the superfluid phase becomes favored over the chiral-symmetry-breaking phase when the chemical potential exceeds approximately twice the scale of the quark condensate, with the diquark gap reaching about half that scale at the transition point.

## Method

The authors introduce a Gorkov-Nambu-like gap equation for QCD using a bispinor formalism that incorporates both the usual quark propagator and anomalous off-diagonal elements representing diquark pairing. They analyze this equation for 2-colour QCD (QC2D) using a dressed-rainbow truncation with a contact interaction model for the gluon propagator, and for QCD itself using both rainbow and vertex-corrected truncations.

## Key numbers

- In QC2D with massive fermions, the critical chemical potential for the second-order transition to the superfluid phase satisfies μ_c = m_π/2.
- For QCD, the chiral-symmetry-breaking phase (σ = −⟨q̄q⟩^{1/3} ≠ 0) becomes unstable with respect to the superfluid phase when the chemical potential exceeds approximately 2σ.
- At the transition point in QCD, the diquark gap is approximately σ/2.
- The zero-temperature bag constant for QC2D at μ=0 is B_B(0) = B_Δ(0) = (0.092 η̂)^4, where η̂ is the model mass scale.
- For QC2D with m=0, the vector meson mass is (1/√2)η̂.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of diquark correlations in baryon structure (Hecht, Roberts, Schmidt) and early explorations of diquark condensation using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Kahana) and rainbow-ladder truncation of the quark DSE (Bailin and Love, 1984). The results enable further investigation of superfluid quark matter in compact star cores, where temperatures are low enough for the superfluid phase to survive, and connect to lattice-QCD studies of diquark masses and the Pauli-Gürsey symmetry of QC2D.
