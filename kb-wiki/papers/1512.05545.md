# Hadron Structure and Spectrum from the Lattice

> Lattice calculations for hadrons are now entering the domain of resonances and scattering, necessitating a better understanding of the observed discrete energy spectrum. This is a reviewing survey about recent lattice QCD results, with some emphasis on spectrum and scattering.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations for hadron structure and spectrum are now entering the domain of resonances and scattering, but results still require careful control of systematic effects such as excited-state contamination, finite volume, and extrapolation to physical pion masses, with many current calculations performed at higher-than-physical pion masses.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of hadron properties using Euclidean correlation functions on a discretized space-time lattice. Hadron masses and energies are extracted from the exponential decay of two-point correlation functions, while structure information (form factors, axial charge, spin contributions) comes from three-point functions with current insertions. For scattering and resonances, the Lüscher method relates the discrete finite-volume energy spectrum to infinite-volume phase shifts, extended to coupled channels and moving frames.

## Key numbers

- Axial isovector coupling \(g_A/g_V\) from lattice calculations: all results slightly below the experimental value 1.2723(23); the most likely cause is excited-state contamination
- Disconnected contribution to isoscalar matrix elements: O(7%) (Abdel-Rehim 2013)
- Light quark spin contribution: \(\Delta u + \Delta d = 0.35(6)\) (Alexandrou 2013, Yang 2015, Constantinou 2014)
- Strange quark spin contribution: \(\Delta s = -0.02(1)\) (Chambers 2015)
- Total quark spin sum: \(\Delta \Sigma = 0.33(7)\), in agreement with COMPASS (2007) value 0.33(3)(5)
- Dirac charge radius \(r_1\) from lattice: LHPC (Green 2014) at \(m_\pi = 149\) MeV gives 0.498(55) fm\(^2\); Mainz CLS (Capitani 2015) at \(m_\pi = 193\) MeV gives 0.501(42) fm\(^2\); ETMC (Abdel-Rehim 2015) at \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV gives 0.398(126) fm\(^2\); experimental values are 0.640(9) fm\(^2\) (ep scattering) and 0.578(2) fm\(^2\) (muonic hydrogen)
- Predicted \(B_{s0}\) bound state mass: 5.711(13)(19) GeV (Lang 2015)
- Predicted \(B_{s1}\) bound state mass: 5.750(17)(19) GeV (Lang 2015)
- Weakly coupled \(B_{s1}^0\) state at 5.831(9)(6) GeV, close to experimental 5.8288(4) GeV (Lang 2015)

## Context

This review surveys lattice QCD results up to September 2015, extending the work of the Flavor Lattice Averaging Group (FLAG) compilation (Aoki 2013) and recent reviews by Constantinou (2014) and Zanotti (2015). It covers milestones such as the determination of hadron masses by Durr (2008) and excited spectra by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Edwards 2011, 2012), and the first coupled-channel scattering simulations by Dudek (2014) for \(\pi K - \eta K\) and Wilson (2015) for \(\pi\pi, K\overline{K}\). The review enables future work on multi-channel scattering, three-hadron decays, and precision structure calculations at physical pion masses.
