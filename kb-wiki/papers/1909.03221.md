# Fusion reactions in the $^9$Be + $^{197}$Au system above the Coulomb barrier

> The cross sections of complete fusion and incomplete fusion for the $ ^{9} $Be + $ ^{197} $Au system, at energies not too much above the Coulomb barrier, were measured for the first time. The online activation followed by offline $γ$-ray spectroscopy method was used for the derivation of the cross sections. A slightly higher value of ICF/TF ratio has been observed, compared to other systems reported in the literature with $ ^{9} $Be beam. The experimental data were compared with coupled channel calculations without taking into account the coupling of the breakup channel, and experimental data of other reaction systems with weakly bound projectiles. A complete fusion suppression of about 40\% was found for the $ ^{9} $Be + $ ^{197} $Au system, at energies above the barrier, whereas the total fusion cross sections are in agreement with the calculations.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first measurement of complete fusion (CF) and incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections for the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system at energies above the Coulomb barrier, finding a CF suppression of about 40% relative to coupled-channel calculations that omit breakup and transfer channels, while total fusion (TF) cross sections agree with those calculations.

## Method
Cross sections were measured using an online stacked-foil activation method followed by offline $\gamma$-ray spectroscopy with HPGe detectors. The $^{9}$Be beam at 50.4 MeV bombarded five $^{197}$Au targets, and evaporation residues from CF ($^{201,202,203}$Bi from xn channels) and ICF ($^{199,200}$Tl from $\alpha$xn channels) were identified by their characteristic $\gamma$-ray energies, half-lives, and branching ratios. Experimental data were compared with coupled-channel calculations using the S\~ao Paulo potential and the universal fusion function (UFF) methodology.

## Key numbers
- CF suppression of about 40% for the $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system at energies above the Coulomb barrier, relative to coupled-channel calculations without breakup coupling.
- ICF/TF ratios of 0.37 at 48.9 MeV, 0.37 at 47.9 MeV, 0.38 at 46.9 MeV, and 0.42 at 45.7 MeV (lab energies).
- Experimental CF cross sections: 441.63$\pm$39.59 mb at 48.9 MeV, 421.95$\pm$36.06 mb at 47.9 MeV, 377.01$\pm$30.04 mb at 46.9 MeV, and 304.29$\pm$32.21 mb at 45.7 MeV.
- Barrier parameters from the S\~ao Paulo potential: $R_B$ = 11.35 fm, $V_B$ = 37.50 MeV, $\hbar\omega$ = 4.44 MeV.

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of fusion suppression with weakly bound $^{9}$Be projectiles, following prior measurements on targets such as $^{144}$Sm (Gomes et al., 2006), $^{169}$Tm (Zhang et al., 2015), $^{181}$Ta (Zhang et al., 2014), $^{186}$W (Fang et al., 2013), $^{187}$Re (Zhang et al., 2014), and $^{208}$Pb (Dasgupta et al., 1999, 2004). The observed CF suppression of about 40% is slightly larger than the 30-35% suppression seen in $^{9}$Be + $^{208}$Pb and $^{6,7}$Li + $^{209}$Bi systems. The results enable further investigation of the mass dependence of CF hindrance and the role of breakup versus transfer mechanisms, which remains controversial as noted by recent studies (Cubiss et al., 2019; Phys. Rev. Lett. 122, 042503; Phys. Rev. C 97, 021601).
