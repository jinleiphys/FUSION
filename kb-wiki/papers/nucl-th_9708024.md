# S-matrix studies of resonances in A=3,4,5,6, and 12 nucleon systems

> Resonances of certain light nuclei are explored by studying the complex pole structures of the scattering matrices. Among other results we predict the existence of three-neutron and three-proton resonances, a small spin-orbit splitting in the low-lying He-5 and Li-5 states and the nonexistence of the soft dipole resonance in He-6.

# S-matrix studies of resonances in A=3,4,5,6, and 12 nucleon systems

## Key claim
The authors predict the existence of three-neutron and three-proton resonances, a small spin-orbit splitting in the low-lying ⁵He and ⁵Li states, and the nonexistence of the soft dipole resonance in ⁶He, but these results depend on the use of complex-energy S-matrix pole analysis rather than conventional real-energy resonance prescriptions, which can yield different parameters especially for broad states.

## Method
The authors used a microscopic Resonating Group Model (RGM) with two- or three-cluster dynamics, employing 0s harmonic oscillator shell-model wave functions for cluster internal states and treating relative motions with few-body physics. For two-cluster systems, they performed direct analytic continuation of the S matrix to complex energies; for three-cluster systems, they applied the complex scaling method. They also extracted S-matrix poles from R-matrix fits to experimental data by analytically continuing the S matrix to complex energies (the extended R-matrix method), using the Minnesota effective NN interaction throughout.

## Key numbers
- Three-neutron (³n) resonance: J^π = 3/2⁺ state at E = 14 MeV with Γ = 13 MeV width
- Three-proton (³p) mirror state: E = 15 MeV with Γ = 14 MeV width
- ⁴He 0⁺₂ state from RGM: E_r = 93 keV, Γ = 390 keV
- ⁴He 0⁺₂ state from extended R-matrix: E_r = 114 keV, Γ = 392 keV
- The spin-orbit splitting of the 3/2⁻ and 1/2⁻ states in ⁵He is ruled out as larger than 2 MeV based on pole trajectory behavior

## Context
This work extends prior S-matrix pole studies of light nuclei by applying both microscopic RGM calculations and model-independent extended R-matrix analysis to systems from A=3 to A=12. The three-neutron resonance prediction follows from pion double charge exchange experiments on ³He. The ⁶He soft dipole resonance nonexistence result confirms later calculations in different models and a recent experiment (both cited as "soft" and "soft1" in the text). The ⁰⁺₂ state of ¹²C is unambiguously shown for the first time in a microscopic model to be a three-alpha resonance, which has implications for stellar nucleosynthesis. The work enables future use of complex S-pole prescriptions as the standard for specifying resonance parameters, particularly for broad states where real-energy methods give inconsistent results.
