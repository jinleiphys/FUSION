# Multiplicities and $p_T$ spectra in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions from a next-to-leading order improved perturbative QCD + saturation + hydrodynamics model

> We bring the EKRT framework, which combines perturbative QCD (pQCD) minijet production with gluon saturation and hydrodynamics, to next-to-leading order (NLO) in pQCD as rigorously as possible. We chart the model uncertainties, and study the viability and predictive power of the model in the light of the RHIC and LHC measurements in central $A+A$ collisions. In particular, we introduce a new set of measurement functions to define the infrared- and collinear-safe minijet transverse energy, $E_T$, in terms of which we formulate the saturation. We update the framework with the EPS09 NLO nuclear parton distributions (nPDFs), and study the propagation of the nPDF uncertainties into the computed $E_T$, saturation scales and the final-state multiplicities. The key parameters, which need to be fixed using the measurements, are identified, and their correlation is discussed. We convert the saturated minijet $E_T$ into QCD-matter initial conditions for longitudinally boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics. We compute the charged-particle multiplicities and identified bulk hadron $p_T$ spectra in 5% most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb at the LHC. We obtain an encouragingly good agreement with the experimental data, simultaneously at RHIC and LHC, showing that the approach has a definite predictive power.

## Key claim

The authors reformulate the EKRT framework to define gluon saturation in terms of transverse energy rather than gluon number, which allows a rigorous NLO pQCD computation; the model achieves simultaneous agreement with RHIC and LHC data without tuning parameters separately for each energy, but the proportionality constant K_sat and the measurement function parameter beta must be fixed using experimental data.

## Method

The authors compute the minijet transverse energy E_T in central A+A collisions at NLO pQCD using collinear factorization with EPS09 NLO nuclear parton distributions and CTEQ6M PDFs, defining infrared- and collinear-safe measurement functions with a new parameter beta that controls the minimum E_T accepted in the rapidity window. They impose a geometric saturation criterion where the computed E_T equals K_sat * R_A^2 * p_0^3 * Delta Y, solve for the saturation momentum p_sat, and use the resulting E_T and p_sat to set initial conditions (tau_0 = 1/p_sat) for longitudinally boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics with the s95p-PCE-v1 equation of state and T_f = 120 MeV freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb, 0-5% central): with K_sat = 1 and beta = 0.75, the model matches the average measured charged-particle multiplicity perfectly (within the experimental error band from ALICE and CMS data)
- At RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au, 0-5% central): with the same K_sat = 1 and beta = 0.75, the model agrees very nicely with the average measured multiplicity (from PHENIX, STAR, and BRAHMS data)
- The parameter beta can reduce the produced E_T by as much as a factor of approximately 2 when varied from 0 to 0.9
- The chemical freeze-out temperature used in the equation of state is T_chem = 150 MeV; kinetic freeze-out temperature is T_f = 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the original EKRT framework (Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, Tuominen, 2000) which combined LO pQCD minijet production with gluon saturation and hydrodynamics, and which predicted a multiplicity scaling dN/dy proportional to A^0.92 * s_NN^(0.19-0.20) that agreed surprisingly well with first LHC data from ALICE (Aamodt, 2010). The authors address five open questions from earlier EKRT setups (ERRT, 2001; EHNRR, 2005) including the problematic definition of gluon number at NLO, the role of K_sat, and the use of only LO EKS98 nuclear PDFs. The improved framework enables systematic studies of nPDF uncertainties (using EPS09 error sets) and provides a baseline for future inclusion of viscous hydrodynamics.
