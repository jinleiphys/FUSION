# Classification of three-body quantum halos

> The different kinds of behaviour of three-body systems in the weak binding limit are classified with specific attention to the transition from a true three-body system to an effective two-body system. For weakly bound Borromean systems approaching the limit of binding we show that the size-binding energy relation is an almost universal function of the three s-wave scattering lengths measured in units of a hyperradial scaling parameter defined as a mass weighted average of two-body equivalent square well radii. We explain why three-body halos follow this curve and why systems appearing above reveal two-body substructures. Three-body quantum halos 2-3 times larger than the limit set by zero hypermoment are possible.

# Classification of three-body quantum halos

## Key claim

Three-body quantum halos can be 2–3 times larger than the limit set by zero hypermoment (K=0) wavefunctions, contradicting earlier expectations that halos would appear along the K=0 curve. The size-binding energy relation for weakly bound Borromean systems approaching the binding limit is an almost universal function of the three s-wave scattering lengths measured in units of a hyperradial scaling parameter, but systems appearing above this universal curve reveal two-body substructures rather than genuine three-body behavior.

## Method

The authors classify three-body systems in the weak binding limit using hyperspherical coordinates and a hyperradial scaling radius ρ₀ defined as a mass-weighted average of two-body equivalent square well radii. They compute the mean square radius versus binding energy for schematic systems restricted to K=0 wavefunctions and for realistic calculations of ¹¹Li and hypertriton (³_ΛH), varying two-body s-wave scattering lengths to map transitions between Borromean, tango, and unbound regions. The scaling radius ρ₀ is used to produce dimensionless scaling plots that compare systems with different masses and interaction ranges.

## Key numbers

- The hypertriton (³_ΛH) has a Λ-particle bound by about 0.14 MeV in a tango configuration where the neutron and proton are almost in a deuteron configuration.
- For the realistic ¹¹Li point, the scaled binding energy is 0.13 in the dimensionless units mBρ₀²/ħ².
- The average scattering lengths a_av for the four cases in the weak binding limit vary from 4 fm to 20 fm, while the ratio a_av/ρ₀ varies between 1.7 and 4.2.
- Three-body quantum halos can be a factor of 2–3 larger than expected from the K=0 curve.

## Context

This work extends earlier classification attempts for halo states by Riisager (1994), Jensen and Riisager (2000), and Nielsen et al. (2001), and refines the scaling analysis of Fedorov et al. (1994) which had concluded that halos would appear along the K=0 curve. The paper clarifies the transition from three-body to effective two-body behavior as binding potentials are changed, building on the classification of Borromean systems by Zhukov et al. (1993), Goy et al. (1995), and Moszkowski (2000), and tango systems by Robson (1999). The results enable experimental classification of realistic three-body halos using measurable quantities, and show that K=0 wavefunctions are insufficient for describing the weak binding limit due to coherent contributions from multiple subsystems.
