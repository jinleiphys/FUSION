# Parton Distribution Benchmarking with LHC Data

> We present a detailed comparison of the most recent sets of NNLO PDFs from the ABM, CT, HERAPDF, MSTW and NNPDF collaborations. We compare parton distributions at low and high scales and parton luminosities relevant for LHC phenomenology. We study the PDF dependence of LHC benchmark inclusive cross sections and differential distributions for electroweak boson and jet production in the cases in which the experimental covariance matrix is available. We quantify the agreement between data and theory by computing the chi2 for each data set with all the various PDFs. PDF comparisons are performed consistently for common values of the strong coupling. We also present a benchmark comparison of jet production at the LHC, comparing the results from various available codes and scale settings. Finally, we discuss the implications of the updated NNLO PDF sets for the combined PDF+alphaS uncertainty in the gluon fusion Higgs production cross section.

## Key claim

The paper provides a detailed benchmarking of NNLO parton distribution functions (PDFs) from five major collaborations (ABM, CT, HERAPDF, MSTW, and NNPDF) against LHC data, finding that all sets can describe the 2010 ATLAS inclusive jet data with chi-squared per data point less than 1 when correlated systematic uncertainties are included, but that the current precision of the data and the size of theoretical scale uncertainties limit the ability to discriminate between different PDF sets.

## Method

The authors compared NNLO PDF sets from ABM, CT, HERAPDF, MSTW, and NNPDF by computing chi-squared values for LHC benchmark cross sections (electroweak boson and jet production) using experimental covariance matrices where available, with consistent values of the strong coupling constant alpha_s. For inclusive jet production at 7 TeV, they compared NLO predictions from three independent computer programs (APPLgrid, FastNLO, and MEKS) using various scale choices, and evaluated the impact of correlated systematic uncertainties through a nuisance parameter formalism with 88 sources of correlated errors.

## Key numbers

- For ATLAS W/Z production at NNLO with alpha_s=0.117, chi-squared values ranged from 1.125 (CT10) to 3.194 (MSTW08)
- For ATLAS inclusive jet data (R=0.4) at NNLO with alpha_s=0.119, chi-squared per data point values were: NNPDF2.3 0.937, MSTW08 0.935, CT10 1.016, ABM11 0.959, HERAPDF1.5 1.011
- The best-fit luminosity shift parameter for ATLAS jet data ranged from -1.12 (ABM11) to -2.36 (HERAPDF1.5) standard deviations
- At low pT (below 200 GeV), the scale uncertainty of NLO predictions was comparable to or larger than the PDF uncertainties from CT10
- The four NLO programs (FastNLO, MEKS1, MEKS2, APPLgrid) agreed to within 1% at the largest pT values, with differences up to 4% at low pT due to scale choice

## Context

This work extends previous PDF benchmarking studies by providing a consistent comparison of the most recent NNLO PDF sets from ABM, CT, HERAPDF, MSTW, and NNPDF collaborations, using common values of alpha_s and the same experimental data. The study enables future PDF determinations by documenting the systematic uncertainties from scale choices and jet algorithm implementations that must be controlled, and by showing that the 2010 ATLAS jet data cannot yet discriminate between PDF sets due to the large correlated systematic uncertainties. The paper builds on earlier work by the CTEQ and MSTW groups on jet production in PDF fits, and on the development of the FastNLO and APPLgrid fast interpolation frameworks.
