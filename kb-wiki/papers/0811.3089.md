# Fluctuations of the $K/π$ Ratio in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions: Statistical and Transport Models

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the kaon to pion number ratio in nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied within the statistical hadron-resonance gas model (SM) for different statistical ensembles and in the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach. We find that the HSD model can qualitatively reproduce the measured excitation function for the $K/π$ ratio fluctuations in central Au+Au (or Pb+Pb) collisions from low SPS up to top RHIC energies. Substantial differences in the HSD and SM results are found for the fluctuations and correlations of the kaon and pion numbers. These predictions impose a challenge for future experiments.

## Key claim

The HSD transport model can qualitatively reproduce the measured excitation function for K/π ratio fluctuations in central Au+Au (or Pb+Pb) collisions from low SPS up to top RHIC energies, but substantial differences between HSD and statistical model results for the fluctuations and correlations of kaon and pion numbers impose a challenge for future experiments rather than confirming either approach.

## Method

The authors computed event-by-event fluctuations of the kaon to pion number ratio using the statistical hadron-resonance gas model (SM) in three different ensembles (grand canonical, canonical, and micro-canonical) and compared these to results from the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach for central nucleus-nucleus collisions at center-of-mass energies from 6.27 GeV to 200 GeV. The SM calculations used chemical freeze-out parameters determined by the average energy per particle condition and a parametrized baryon chemical potential, while HSD simulations were performed for central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions with impact parameter b=0.

## Key numbers

- At √s_NN = 200 GeV in the GCE statistical model: ω_π = 1.519, ω_K = 1.086, ρ_Kπ = 0.112
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV in the MCE statistical model: ω_π = 0.835, ω_K = 0.950, ρ_Kπ = -0.180
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV in HSD: ω_π = 5.838, ω_K = 2.765, ρ_Kπ = 0.634, with ⟨N_π⟩ = 5204.0 and ⟨N_K⟩ = 861.77
- At √s_NN = 6.27 GeV in HSD: ω_π = 0.961, ω_K = 1.107, ρ_Kπ = -0.091, with ⟨N_π⟩ = 612.03 and ⟨N_K⟩ = 43.329
- The chemical freeze-out temperature at √s_NN = 200 GeV is T = 163.7 MeV with μ_B = 23.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of event-by-event fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions, following the first measurement of K/π ratio fluctuations by the NA49 Collaboration and subsequent measurements by STAR. The authors compare statistical model predictions (building on prior work by Begun and Koch) with the HSD transport approach (previously applied to charged hadron multiplicity fluctuations by HSDpp and urqmdpp). The results show that HSD predicts strongly increasing scaled variances with energy, unlike the finite limiting values in statistical models, and that the correlation parameter ρ_Kπ changes sign from negative to positive with increasing energy in HSD, which is not seen in any statistical ensemble. Future experimental data at RHIC energies will be needed to distinguish between these scenarios and shed light on the equilibration pattern achieved in heavy-ion collisions.
