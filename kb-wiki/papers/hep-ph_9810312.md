# Nuclear bound states of $ω$ mesons

> Models based on chiral SU(3)_L \otimes SU(3)_R symmetry and vector meson dominance suggest an attractive potential for the $ω$-meson in a nuclear medium. We discuss the feasibility of producing nuclear bound states of $ω$-mesons using $(d,^3He)$ and pion induced reactions on selected nuclear targets.

# Nuclear bound states of $\omega$ mesons

## Key claim

Models based on chiral SU(3) symmetry and vector meson dominance suggest an attractive potential for the $\omega$ meson in nuclear matter, which could support quasi-bound $\omega$-nuclear states; however, these states would have short lifetimes with widths comparable to their binding energies, and the feasibility of observing them depends on the specific production reaction and the magnitude of the in-medium mass shift (predicted to be about 15% at normal nuclear matter density).

## Method

The authors calculate the $\omega N$ scattering amplitude $T_{\omega N}(E)$ using an effective Lagrangian based on chiral SU(3) symmetry, incorporating constraints from measured $\pi^- p \to \omega n$ cross sections and evaluating uncertainties from $\omega N \leftrightarrow \rho N$ coupled-channel dynamics (two input sets A and B). They translate this into a complex, energy-dependent local $\omega$-nuclear optical potential via the local density approximation, solve the Klein-Gordon equation for $\omega$ mesons in nuclei with Fermi-type density distributions, and compute cross sections for $(d,^3He)$ transfer reactions and $\pi^-$-induced $\omega$ production using distorted wave impulse approximation with eikonal distortion factors.

## Key numbers

- Free $\omega$ meson mass: $m_\omega = 0.78$ GeV
- Free $\omega$ meson decay width: $\Gamma_\omega^{(0)} = 8.4$ MeV at $E=m_\omega$
- Normal nuclear matter density: $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- Calculated $\omega N$ scattering length (set A): $a_{\omega N} = (1.6 + i0.3)$ fm
- Predicted in-medium mass shift: $\Delta m_\omega(\rho)/m_\omega \simeq -0.2 \, \rho/\rho_0$ (about 15% downward at $\rho=\rho_0$)
- Predicted in-medium $\omega$ decay width at $\rho=\rho_0$: $\Gamma_{\rm eff} \simeq 40$ MeV
- Real part of $\omega$-nuclear potential at nuclear center: $\Re U \sim -(100-150)$ MeV $\cdot \rho/\rho_0$ at $E \sim m_\omega$
- Binding energies for $^6_\omega He$ (1s state): $-49$ MeV, width: 32 MeV
- Binding energies for $^{11}_\omega B$ (1s state): $-66$ MeV, width: 38 MeV
- Binding energies for $^{26}_\omega Mg$ (1s state): $-83$ MeV, width: 42 MeV
- Binding energies for $^{39}_\omega K$ (1s state): $-87$ MeV, width: 42 MeV
- Free $(d+p \to ^3He+\omega)$ cross section at $T_d \simeq 4$ GeV: approximately $0.5$ $\mu$b/sr
- Deuteron-nucleon cross section used: $\sigma_{dN} \simeq 83$ mb
- $^3He$-nucleon cross section used: $\sigma_{^3He N} \simeq 126$ mb
- Pion-nucleon cross section used: $\sigma_{\pi N} \simeq 35$ mb
- Free $\omega \to e^+e^-$ branching ratio: $7.15 \times 10^{-5}$
- Free $\pi^- p \to n \omega$ cross section: 1-2 mb (c.m., almost isotropic)

## Context

This work extends earlier in-medium QCD sum rule analyses (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992) and the scaling hypothesis known as "Brown-Rho scaling" (Brown and Rho, 1991) by providing explicit calculations of $\omega$-nuclear bound states with realistic widths. It contrasts with the $\rho$ meson case, where the large vacuum width (about 150 MeV) and further in-medium broadening make bound states unlikely. The predictions are in qualitative agreement with the findings of a prior study (ref. 9 in the paper) but differ quantitatively because the present potential is strongly energy dependent, supporting more quasi-bound states. The work enables experimental searches for $\omega$-nuclear bound states via $(d,^3He)$ reactions at GSI (as proposed by Hayano et al., 1997) and pion-induced reactions, providing specific cross section estimates and spectral predictions for light nuclei such as $^7Li$.
