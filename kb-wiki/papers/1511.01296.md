# First measurement of several $β$-delayed neutron emitting isotopes beyond N=126

> The $β$-delayed neutron emission probabilities of neutron rich Hg and Tl nuclei have been measured together with $β$-decay half-lives for 20 isotopes of Au, Hg, Tl, Pb and Bi in the mass region N$\gtrsim$126. These are the heaviest species where neutron emission has been observed so far. These measurements provide key information to evaluate the performance of nuclear microscopic and phenomenological models in reproducing the high-energy part of the $β$-decay strength distribution. In doing so, it provides important constraints to global theoretical models currently used in $r$-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurements of beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities for several neutron-rich isotopes beyond N=126, but the authors explicitly note that these nuclei are not the actual r-process waiting-point nuclei at N=126, which remain inaccessible; rather, the measured isotopes are their direct neighbours, providing constraints on theoretical models used for r-process calculations.

## Method

The experiment was performed at GSI using the fragment separator (FRS) with a 1 GeV/u primary uranium-238 beam impinging on a beryllium target. Fragments were identified event-by-event using time-projection chambers, ionization chambers, and time-of-flight detectors, then implanted into a stack of double-sided silicon strip detectors (SIMBA). Neutrons were detected using the BELEN detector array of 30 helium-3 tubes embedded in polyethylene, and beta-decay half-lives and neutron branching ratios were extracted via binned maximum-likelihood analysis of implant-beta and implant-beta-neutron time correlations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron detection efficiency of BELEN: 40(2)% from thermal neutrons up to 1 MeV, decreasing to 35% at 5 MeV
- Half-life of thallium-214: 11(2) s with neutron emission probability 27(11)%
- Half-life of thallium-215: 10(4) s with neutron emission probability 4.6(4.6)%
- Half-life of thallium-216: 6(3) s with neutron emission probability upper limit <11.5%
- Half-life of mercury-210: 64(12) s with neutron emission probability 2.2(2.2)%
- Half-life of mercury-211: 26(8) s with neutron emission probability 6.3(6.3)%
- Half-life of thallium-211: 76(18) s with neutron emission probability 2.2(2.2)%
- Half-life of thallium-212: 31(8) s with neutron emission probability 1.8(1.8)%
- Half-life of thallium-213: 24(4) s with neutron emission probability 7.6(3.4)%
- Previously reported neutron branching ratio of thallium-210: 7(+7-4) x 10^-5
- Previously reported neutron branching ratio of lanthanum-150: 2.7(3)%

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of beta-delayed neutron emission into the heavy mass region beyond N=126, where previously only thallium-210 had a measured neutron branching ratio (Rusinov 1957, Stetter 1961). The results test three global theoretical models used in r-process nucleosynthesis calculations: FRDM+QRPA (Moller 2003), KTUY (Koura 2005), and RHB+RQRPA (Marketin 2015), finding that no single model performs satisfactorily on both sides of the N=126 shell closure. The data support the RHB+RQRPA model's predictions for r-process abundances around the third peak at A=195, and the authors call for further measurements of beta-decay properties and spectroscopy in the A~200 region to improve theoretical models.
