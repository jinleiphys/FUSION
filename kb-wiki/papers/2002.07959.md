# Photon Self-energy in Magnetized Chiral Plasma from Kinetic Theory

> We study the photon self-energy in magnetized chiral plasma by solving the response of electromagnetic field perturbations in chiral kinetic theory with Landau level states. With lowest Landau level approximation and in collisionless limit, we find solutions for three particular perturbations: parallel electric field, static perpendicular electric and magnetic field, corresponding to chiral magnetic wave, drift state and tilted state, from which we extract components of photon self-energy in different kinematics. We show no solution is possible for more general field perturbations. We argue this is an artifact of the collisionless limit: while static solution corresponding to drift state and tilted state can be found, they cannot be realized dynamically without interaction between Landau levels. We also discuss possible manifestation of side-jump effect due to both boost and rotation, with the latter due to the presence of background magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the collisionless limit and under the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation, solutions for the photon self-energy in magnetized chiral plasma can only be found for three specific electromagnetic field perturbations (parallel electric field, static perpendicular electric field, and static perpendicular magnetic field), and they argue that the impossibility of finding solutions for more general perturbations is an artifact of the collisionless limit rather than a fundamental limitation.

## Method

The authors solve the chiral kinetic equations with Landau level states to obtain the induced current response to weak electromagnetic field perturbations, working in the strong magnetic field regime and collisionless limit. They expand the induced current density order by order in gradients and solve the resulting homogeneous and inhomogeneous equations for the LLL approximation. The momentum integrals of the solutions yield the retarded photon self-energy components via the relation \(\delta J^\mu(q) = \Pi^{\mu\nu}_R(q) \delta A_\nu(q)\).

## Key numbers

* Photon self-energy for parallel electric field (chiral magnetic wave): \(\Pi^{00}(q_0,q_3) = \Pi^{30}(q_0,q_3) = -\frac{e^3 B}{(2\pi)^2} \frac{q_3}{q_0+i\epsilon+q_3}\) and \(\Pi^{03}(q_0,q_3) = \Pi^{33}(q_0,q_3) = \frac{e^3 B}{(2\pi)^2} \frac{q_0}{q_0+i\epsilon+q_3}\), independent of temperature and chemical potential.
* Photon self-energy for static perpendicular electric field (drift state): \(\Pi^{00}(q_M) = \Pi^{30}(q_M) = -\frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^2}\left(eB + \frac{\pi^2 T^2}{3} + \mu^2\right)\) and \(\Pi^{M0}(q_M) = -\frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^2} i\epsilon^{MN} q_N \mu\).
* Photon self-energy for static perpendicular magnetic field (tilted state): \(\Pi^{03}(q_M) = \Pi^{33}(q_M) = -\frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^2}\left(\frac{\pi^2 T^2}{3} + \mu^2\right)\) and \(\Pi^{M3}(q_M) = -\frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^2} i\epsilon^{MN} q_N \mu\).
* The chiral magnetic current from the tilted state: \(J^M = \frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^2} \mu B_M\).

## Context

This work extends field-theoretic studies of photon self-energy in magnetized chiral media (Danielsson 1995, Chao 2014, Fukushima 2015, Chao 2016) by using chiral kinetic theory with Landau level states (Hattori 2016, Sheng 2018, Lin 2019) as an alternative approach. The results for the chiral magnetic wave agree with field-theoretic results in the LLL approximation (Fukushima 2011) up to an overall factor of \(e^{-q_T^2/B}\). The static self-energy components from the drift state and tilted state coincide with components of the self-energy in chiral medium without background magnetic field (Akamatsu 2013) and give rise to the chiral magnetic effect (Son 2012). The work enables future studies of dynamical realizations of drift and tilted states by including collisions between Landau levels, and further investigation of the chiral plasma instability (Akamatsu 2013).
