# Chiral corrections to kaon-nucleon scattering lengths

> We calculate the threshold T-matrices of kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering to one loop order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. To that order the complex-valued isospin-1 $\bar KN$ threshold T-matrix can be successfully predicted from the isospin-0 and 1 $KN$ threshold T-matrices. As expected perturbation theory fails to explain the isospin-0 $\bar KN$ threshold T-matrix which is completely dominated by the nearby subthreshold $Λ^*(1405)$-resonance. Cancelations of large terms of second and third chiral order are observed as they seem to be typical for SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations. We also give the kaon and eta loop corrections to the $πN$ scattering lengths and we investigate $πΛ$ scattering to one-loop order. The second order s-wave low-energy constants are all of natural size and do not exceed 1 GeV$^{-1}$ in magnitude.

# Chiral corrections to kaon-nucleon scattering lengths

## Key claim

One-loop SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory can successfully predict the complex-valued isospin-1 antikaon-nucleon threshold T-matrix from the isospin-0 and 1 kaon-nucleon threshold T-matrices, but perturbation theory fails for the isospin-0 antikaon-nucleon channel because it is completely dominated by the nearby subthreshold Lambda*(1405) resonance.

## Method

The author calculates threshold T-matrices for kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering to one-loop order (O(q^3)) in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, using dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction. The low-energy constants C_0 and C_1 are adjusted to reproduce the empirical kaon-nucleon threshold T-matrices from Martin's dispersion relation analysis, and these values are then used to predict the antikaon-nucleon threshold T-matrices via crossing symmetry relations. Kaon and eta loop corrections to pion-nucleon scattering lengths and pion-Lambda scattering are also computed to one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- Empirical kaon-nucleon threshold T-matrices from Martin: T^(0)_KN = 0.4 fm, T^(1)_KN = -6.3 fm
- Empirical antikaon-nucleon threshold T-matrices from Martin: T^(0)_KNbar = (-32.6 + 13.0 i) fm, T^(1)_KNbar = (7.1 + 11.5 i) fm
- Predicted antikaon-nucleon threshold T-matrices at one-loop: T^(0)_KNbar = (30.4 + 6.2 i) fm, T^(1)_KNbar = (7.1 + 10.4 i) fm
- Low-energy constants extracted from kaon-nucleon data: C_0 = 0.12 fm = 0.60 GeV^{-1}, C_1 = 0.41 fm = 2.09 GeV^{-1}
- Second order s-wave low-energy constants do not exceed 1 GeV^{-1} in magnitude
- Predicted pion-Lambda phase-shift at the Xi-mass: delta_{pi Lambda} = -2.8 degrees
- Resonance parameters from unitarized scattering length approximation: mass W_0 = 1416.4 MeV, width Gamma = 25.2 MeV
- Chiral expansion contributions for T^(1)_KN: leading order -7.63 fm, second order +7.83 fm, third order -6.54 fm

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) chiral perturbation theory framework previously applied to pion-nucleon scattering by BKM (1990s) and the complete fourth-order analysis of low-energy pion-nucleon scattering by Fettes and Meissner (2000). The calculation demonstrates that the isospin-1 antikaon-nucleon amplitude can be predicted from kaon-nucleon data, while the isospin-0 channel requires non-perturbative methods such as those used by Siegel and Weise (1988), Oset and Ramos (1998), or Oller and Meissner (2000) to generate the Lambda*(1405) resonance. The results enable future fully relativistic calculations and higher-order O(q^4) studies in the heavy baryon framework to quantify corrections to these one-loop predictions.
