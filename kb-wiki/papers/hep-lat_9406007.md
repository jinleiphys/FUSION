# $πNN$ and Pseudoscalar Form Factors from Lattice QCD

> The $πNN$ form factor $g_{πNN}(q^2)$ is obtained from a quenched lattice QCD calculation of the pseudoscalar form factor $g_P(q^2)$ of the proton with pion pole dominance. We find that $g_{πNN}(q^2)$ fitted with the monopole form agrees well with the Goldberger-Treiman relation and is much preferred over the dipole form. The monopole mass is determined to be $0.75 \pm 0.14 {\rm GeV}$ which shows that $g_{πNN}(q^2)$ is rather soft. The extrapolated $πN$ coupling constant $g_{πNN} = 12.7 \pm 2.4$ is quite consistent with the phenomenological values. We also compare $g_{πNN}(q^2)$ with the axial form factor $g_A(q^2)$ to check the pion dominance in the induced pseudoscalar form factor $h_A(q^2)$ vis à vis chiral Ward identity.

## Key claim

The πNN form factor gπNN(q²) is much better described by a monopole form than a dipole form when the Goldberger-Treiman relation at q²=0 is used as a constraint, yielding a monopole mass of 0.75 ± 0.14 GeV that is considerably softer than values typically used in nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The authors performed a quenched lattice QCD calculation on 24 gauge configurations with a 16³ × 24 lattice at β = 6.0, computing two-point and three-point correlation functions for the proton with Wilson fermions at three light quark masses (κ = 0.154, 0.152, 0.148). The pseudoscalar form factor gP(q²) was extracted from ratios of these correlation functions, and the πNN form factor gπNN(q²) was obtained via the pion pole dominance relation gP(q²) = Gπ gπNN(q²)/(mπ² - q²), with Gπ determined from the pion two-point function.

## Key numbers

- Monopole mass ΛπNN = 0.75 ± 0.14 GeV from fitting gπNN(q²) to the monopole form
- Extrapolated πN coupling constant gπNN = 12.7 ± 2.4 at q² = mπ²
- Goldberger-Treiman relation prediction at q²=0: gπNN(0) = 12.66 ± 0.04 (from gA(0) = 1.2573 ± 0.0028 and fπ = 93.15 ± 0.11 MeV)
- Monopole extrapolation gives gπNN(0) = 12.2 ± 2.3, consistent with the Goldberger-Treiman relation
- Dipole fit gives gπNN(0) = 10.8 ± 1.3, falling outside the Goldberger-Treiman prediction
- Dipole mass from dipole fit: 1.32 ± 0.17 GeV
- Lattice scale a⁻¹ = 1.74 ± 0.10 GeV set by fixing the nucleon mass to its physical value
- Pion decay constant from lattice: fπ = 89.8 ± 4.5 MeV
- Quark masses studied: approximately 120, 200, and 370 MeV (corresponding to κ = 0.154, 0.152, 0.148)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice calculations of nucleon axial and electromagnetic form factors (Liu, Dong, Draper, and Wilcox, 1994; Wilcox, Draper, and Liu, 1992) to the pseudoscalar sector, addressing a long-standing controversy over the shape and slope of the πNN form factor. The result supports the softer form factor (ΛπNN around 0.8 GeV) suggested by analyses of the Goldberger-Treiman relation discrepancy (Cheng and Silbar, 1981) and the ppπ⁰ versus pnπ⁺ coupling discrepancy (Thomas, 1989), as well as skyrmion models (Li and Liu, 1987), rather than the harder form factors (ΛπNN from 1.3 to 2.5 GeV) used in realistic NN potentials. The soft form factor has implications for NN potential studies, three-body forces, and processes involving πN coupling, and the authors note that future work should expand the lattice volume to access smaller momentum transfers and include dynamical fermion effects.
