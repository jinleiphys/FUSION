# Emission of neutron-proton and proton-proton pairs in neutrino scattering

> We use a recently developed model of relativistic meson-exchange currents to compute the neutron-proton and proton-proton yields in $(ν_μ,μ^-)$ scattering from $^{12}$C in the 2p-2h channel. We compute the response functions and cross sections with the relativistic Fermi gas model for different kinematics from intermediate to high momentum transfers. We find a large contribution of neutron-proton configurations in the initial state, as compared to proton-proton pairs. In the case of charge-changing neutrino scattering the 2p-2h cross section of proton-proton emission ({\it i.e.,} np in the initial state) is much larger than for neutron-proton emission ({\it i.e.,} two neutrons in the initial state) by a $(ω,q)$-dependent factor. The different emission probabilities of distinct species of nucleon pairs are produced in our model only by meson-exchange currents, mainly by the $Δ$ isobar current. We also analyze other effects including exchange contributions and the effect of the axial and vector currents.

## Key claim

The authors find that in charged-current neutrino scattering on carbon-12, the 2p-2h cross section for proton-proton emission (originating from initial neutron-proton pairs) is much larger than for neutron-proton emission (originating from initial two-neutron pairs), with the ratio being around 5-6 near the maximum but dependent on kinematics; this dominance arises solely from meson-exchange currents, mainly the Delta isobar current, and not from short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors compute the five response functions (CC, CL, LL, T, T') and double differential cross sections for the 2p-2h channel in charged-current neutrino scattering on carbon-12 using a relativistic Fermi gas model with a fully relativistic meson-exchange current model that includes pion-in-flight, seagull, pion-pole, and Delta(1232) excitation diagrams. They separate the contributions for final-state proton-proton and neutron-proton pairs by evaluating the elementary two-body matrix elements with distinct isospin combinations, including both direct and exchange (interference) contributions, and integrate over the phase space of the two ejected nucleons for various momentum transfers from 100 to 2000 MeV/c and neutrino energies of 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The pp/np ratio for the transverse response at q=400 MeV/c is about a factor of 6, compared to at most a factor of 3 in the short-range correlation model of Van (2016).
* The MEC 2p-2h transverse response at q=400 MeV/c is one order of magnitude larger than the short-range correlation response of Van (2016).
* The interference (exchange) contribution reduces the pp emission by about 20% and the np emission by about a factor of 1/2.
* The 2p-2h contribution is roughly 20% of the total cross section for typical peak neutrino energies of 1 GeV.
* The pp/np ratio is around 5-6 near the maximum of the cross section for a wide range of neutrino energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic meson-exchange current model (Simo 2016) from electron scattering to neutrino scattering, and provides a theoretical quantification of the separate isospin channels that was missing in event generators like NUWRO (Nie 2016), which assumed a fixed 95% probability for initial np pairs based on electron scattering data from Shneor (2007). The results enable more accurate modeling of two-nucleon knockout events in neutrino oscillation experiments, particularly for asymmetric nuclei like argon-40, iron-56, and lead-208, and can be folded with neutrino fluxes for comparison with experiments such as MINERvA and ArgoNeuT.
