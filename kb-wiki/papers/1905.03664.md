# Characterization of vorticity in pygmy resonances and soft-dipole modes with two-nucleon transfer reactions

> The properties of the two-quasiparticle-like soft E1-modes and PDR have been and are systematically studied with the help of inelastic and electromagnetic experiments which essentially probe the particle-hole components of these vibrations. It is shown that further insight in their characterisation can be achieved with the help of two-nucleon transferreactions, in particular concerning the particle-particle components of the modes, in terms of absolute differential cross sections which take properly into account successive and simultaneous transfer mechanisms corrected for non-orthogonality, able to reproduce the experimental findings at the 10% level. The process $^9$Li$(t,p)^{11}$Li(1$^-$) is discussed, and absolute cross sections predicted.

## Key claim

Two-nucleon transfer reactions can provide complementary insight into the particle-particle components of pygmy dipole resonances and soft E1 modes, beyond what is accessible through inelastic and electromagnetic probes that primarily test particle-hole components. The authors predict that absolute differential cross sections for the reaction \(^9\)Li\((t,p)^{11}\)Li(\(1^-\)) can be calculated with sufficient accuracy (at the 10% level) to assess the role of ground-state correlations, provided that successive and simultaneous transfer mechanisms are properly treated and non-orthogonality corrections are included.

## Method

The authors performed QRPA calculations of the full dipole response of \(^{11}\)Li using a separable dipole-dipole interaction of self-consistent strength in a two-quasiparticle basis with energies up to 50 MeV, with the continuum discretised by placing the system in a spherical box of radius 40 fm. They then calculated absolute differential cross sections for the two-nucleon transfer reaction \(^9\)Li\((t,p)^{11}\)Li(\(1^-\)) using the code COOPER, employing renormalised single-particle wave functions and spectroscopic amplitudes from nuclear field theory. The calculations were benchmarked against the inverse reaction \(^{11}\)Li\((p,t)^9\)Li(gs) using detailed balance, and the dipole strength function was constructed by broadening individual dipole states with a Lorentzian function.

## Key numbers

* The two-neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Li is \(S_{2n} = 0.380\) MeV, compared to 6.094 MeV for \(^9\)Li.
* The radius of \(^{11}\)Li is 4.6 fm, compared to 2.7 fm for \(^9\)Li.
* The soft E1 mode in \(^{11}\)Li has a centroid of 0.75 MeV and FWHM \(\Gamma = 0.5\) MeV, carrying 6.2% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn energy-weighted sum rule.
* The experimental integrated cross section for \(^{11}\)Li\((p,t)^9\)Li(gs) at 3.3 MeV/A is \(\sigma_{exp}(gs) = 5.7 \pm 0.9\) mb, while the theoretical value is \(\sigma_{th}(gs) = 6.1\) mb (a deviation of about 7%).
* The predicted integrated two-neutron transfer cross section for the soft dipole mode is \(\sigma(1^-) = 0.13\) mb, compared to \(\sigma(gs) = 0.51\) mb for the ground state transition in the \(^9\)Li\((t,p)^{11}\)Li reaction at a triton bombarding energy of 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear field theory approach previously applied to halo nuclei by Broglia, Barranco, Potel, and Vigezzi (2016) and by Barranco, Broglia, and collaborators (2001) on the structure of \(^{11}\)Li as a single Cooper pair. The paper builds on experimental studies of the soft E1 mode by Kanungo (2015) and Nakamura (2006), and on two-nucleon transfer measurements by Tanihata (2008). The results enable future experimental tests using active-target and inverse-kinematics experiments to probe the interplay between particle-hole and particle-particle ground-state correlations in dipole low-energy strength, and open the question of whether magnetic moment measurements could further characterise the associated transition densities and velocity fields.
