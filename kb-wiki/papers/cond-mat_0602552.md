# Spin-orbit interaction in quantum dots in the presence of exchange correlations

> We discuss the problem of spin-orbit interaction in a 2D chaotic or diffusive quantum dot in the presence of exchange correlations. Spin-orbit scattering breaks spin rotation invariance, and in the crossover regime between different symmetries of the spin-orbit coupling, the problem has no closed solution. A conventional choice of a many-particle basis in a numerical diagonalization is the set of Slater determinants built from the single-particle eigenstates of the one-body Hamiltonian (including the spin-orbit terms). We develop a different approach based on the use of a good-spin many-particle basis that is composed of the eigenstates of the universal Hamiltonian in the absence of spin-orbit scattering. We introduce a complete labelling of this good-spin basis and use angular momentum algebra to calculate in closed form the matrix elements of the spin-orbit interaction in this basis. Spin properties, such as the ground-state spin distribution and the spin excitation function, are easily calculated in this basis.

## Key claim

The paper develops a good-spin many-particle basis for numerically diagonalizing the Hamiltonian of a 2D chaotic or diffusive quantum dot with spin-orbit interaction and exchange correlations, applicable in crossover regimes between different spin-orbit symmetries where no closed-form solution exists.

## Method

The authors construct a many-particle basis from eigenstates of the universal Hamiltonian (which includes exchange interaction but no spin-orbit coupling), labeling states by orbital occupations, total spin quantum numbers, and intermediate spin quantum numbers represented by a tree graph. They express the spin-orbit Hamiltonian as irreducible tensor operators of rank 1 and use angular momentum algebra (Wigner-Eckart theorem, 6j symbols) to calculate matrix elements of the spin-orbit terms in closed form, transforming between normal and canonical coupling schemes via exchange operations.

## Key numbers

- The exchange interaction constant used in prior work is \(J_s = 0.3 \Delta\), where \(\Delta\) is the mean single-particle level spacing.
- The mean single-particle level spacing in the middle of the spectrum is \(\Delta = \pi a / \sqrt{2N}\), where \(N\) is the dimension of the random matrix and \(a^2/2\) is the variance of off-diagonal matrix elements.
- The spin-orbit crossover parameters are defined as \(x_\perp = \alpha_\perp \sqrt{N}\) and \(x_\parallel = \alpha_\parallel \sqrt{2N}\), where \(\alpha_\perp\) and \(\alpha_\parallel\) are coupling parameters.
- The crossover parameters relate to physical energy scales via \(x_\perp^2 = \epsilon_\perp / \Delta\) and \(x_\parallel^2 = \epsilon_\parallel / \Delta\), with \(\epsilon_\perp\) and \(\epsilon_\parallel\) determined by spin-orbit scattering lengths \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2\) and dot dimensions.
- The multiplicity of states with given total spin \(S\) and \(q\) singly-occupied levels is \(d_q(S) = \binom{q}{S+q/2} - \binom{q}{S+1+q/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the universal Hamiltonian approach of Alhassid (2000) and Aleiner et al. (2002) for chaotic quantum dots to include spin-orbit interaction in crossover regimes, where the Hamiltonian is no longer universal. The good-spin basis formalism enables calculation of spin properties such as ground-state spin distributions and spin excitation functions, and can be applied to other problems involving exchange correlations, such as a quantum dot with a Kondo impurity as studied by Murthy (2005).
