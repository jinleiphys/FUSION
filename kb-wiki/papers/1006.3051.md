# Synchrotron radiation by fast fermions in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the synchrotron radiation of gluons by fast quarks in strong magnetic field produced by colliding relativistic heavy-ions. We argue that due to high electric conductivity of plasma, time variation of the magnetic field is slow and estimate its relaxation time. We calculate the energy loss due to synchrotron radiation of gluons by fast quarks. We find that the typical energy loss per unit length for a light quark at LHC is a few GeV per fm. This effect alone predicts quenching of jets with $p_\bot$ up to about 20 GeV. We also show that the spin-flip transition effect accompanying the synchrotron radiation leads to a strong polarization of quarks and leptons with respect to the direction of the magnetic field. Observation of the lepton polarization may provide a direct evidence of existence of strong magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Synchrotron radiation of gluons by fast quarks in the strong magnetic field produced in heavy-ion collisions can account for jet quenching at LHC with transverse momentum up to about 20 GeV, but only under the condition that the magnetic field is approximately constant during the radiation process, which the author argues is justified by the high electric conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author solves the Dirac equation for a relativistic fermion in a homogeneous constant magnetic field and generalizes the QED synchrotron radiation calculation to gluon radiation by replacing the electromagnetic coupling with the strong coupling. The time dependence of the magnetic field is estimated by solving the diffusion equation for the magnetic field in a conducting medium, using the electric conductivity from lattice QCD calculations. The energy loss per unit length is computed from the gluon radiation spectrum using the invariant parameter χ, and the spin-flip transition probability (Sokolov-Ternov effect) is applied to quarks and leptons.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field at LHC: eB ≈ 15 m_π²/ħ (from Kharzeev (2007) and Skokov (2009))
- Magnetic field at RHIC: eB ≈ m_π²/ħ (from Kharzeev (2007))
- Energy loss per unit length for a u quark with p_T = 10 GeV at LHC: 1.3 GeV/fm
- Energy loss per unit length for a u quark with p_T = 10 GeV at RHIC: 0.2 GeV/fm
- Energy loss per unit length for a light quark with p_T = 15 GeV at LHC: 1.7 GeV/fm
- Energy loss per unit length for a light quark with p_T = 15 GeV at RHIC: 0.27 GeV/fm
- Characteristic relaxation time of the magnetic field due to plasma conductivity: τ ≈ 2.2 fm (T/T_c)² for R ≈ 5 fm
- Adiabaticity parameter γ for u quarks at RHIC: 0.1–0.2
- Adiabaticity parameter γ for d quarks at RHIC: 0.5–0.8
- Maximal spin asymmetry from Sokolov-Ternov effect: A_m = 8/(5√3) ≈ 92%
- Characteristic time for d quark (p_⊥ = 1 GeV, η=φ=0 at RHIC) to achieve maximal asymmetry: τ = 6×10⁻⁸ fm

## Context

This work extends the QED synchrotron radiation formalism (Berestetsky (1982), Jackson (1975)) to QCD gluon radiation in the strong magnetic fields predicted for heavy-ion collisions by Kharzeev (2007) and Skokov (2009). It provides a new mechanism for jet energy loss that is complementary to the standard parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma (Gyulassy (1993), Baier (1994)). The predicted lepton polarization asymmetry offers a direct experimental signature for the existence of strong magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions, which could be tested at RHIC and LHC.
