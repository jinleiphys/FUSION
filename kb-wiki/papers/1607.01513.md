# Charge redistribution from anomalous magnetovorticity coupling

> We investigate novel transport phenomena in a chiral fluid originated from an interplay between a vorticity and strong magnetic field, which induces a redistribution of vector charges in the system and an axial current along the magnetic field. The corresponding transport coefficients are obtained from an energy-shift argument for the chiral fermions in the lowest Landau level (LLL) due to a spin-vorticity coupling and also from diagrammatic computations on the basis of the linear response theory. Based on consistent results from the both methods, we observe that the transport coefficients are proportional to the anomaly coefficient and are independent of temperature and chemical potential. We therefore speculate that these transport phenomena are connected to quantum anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the interplay between vorticity and a strong magnetic field in a chiral fluid induces a local redistribution of vector charge and an axial current along the magnetic field, with transport coefficients proportional to the anomaly coefficient and independent of temperature and chemical potential, suggesting a connection to quantum anomaly, though the authors hedge that this is a speculation based on consistent results from two methods.

## Method

The authors derived the transport coefficients using two complementary approaches: first, an energy-shift argument for chiral fermions in the lowest Landau level (LLL) due to spin-vorticity coupling, and second, a diagrammatic computation based on the Kubo formula within linear response theory, where the retarded Green's function of the vector current and energy-momentum tensor was evaluated at one-loop order using LLL-projected fermion propagators in a constant external magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The induced vector charge density is given by Δj⁰_V = q_f (C_A/2) (B · ω), where C_A = 1/(2π²) is the anomaly coefficient, q_f is the fermion electric charge, B is the magnetic field, and ω is the vorticity.
- The induced axial current is Δj_A = |q_f| (C_A/2) (B · ω) B̂, where B̂ is the unit vector along the magnetic field.
- The transport coefficient λ obtained from the Kubo formula is λ = (C_A/2) q_f B, confirming the result from the energy-shift argument.
- The identity f₀(0) + f̄₀(0) = 1 holds for the Fermi-Dirac distributions at zero energy, making the result independent of temperature T and chemical potential μ.

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous transport phenomena in chiral fluids, specifically the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) by Vilenkin (1980) and Kharzeev et al. (2007) and the Chiral Vortical Effect (CVE) by Kharzeev et al. (2007) and Banerjee et al. (2008), which treated magnetic field and vorticity separately. The paper goes beyond the conventional gradient expansion used in anomalous hydrodynamics by Son and Surowka (2009) to include the coupling between strong magnetic field and vorticity, which was previously dropped as a higher-order effect. The results enable the incorporation of these new terms into anomalous magnetohydrodynamics and open avenues for studying charge redistribution in heavy-ion collisions and Weyl/Dirac semimetals.
