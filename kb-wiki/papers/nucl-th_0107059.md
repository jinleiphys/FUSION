# Quark Model study of the NN*(1440) components on the deuteron

> We present a calculation of the deuteron wave function including $NN$, $ΔΔ$, and $NN^*$(1440) channels. All the transition potentials, as well as the direct $NN$ potential, have been obtained from the same underlying quark model using standard techniques available in the literature. The calculated weights for the different channels are in agreement with previous theoretical estimations.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the deuteron wave function including \(NN\), \(\Delta\Delta\), and \(NN^*(1440)\) channels using a consistent constituent quark model, finding that the \(NN^*(1440)\) components have probabilities on the order of \(10^{-2}\%\), which are small compared to the \(\Delta\Delta\) channels but a factor of 10 larger than some higher \(\Delta\Delta\) partial waves; the abstract's claim of agreement with previous theoretical estimations is hedged by noting that the result is a factor of 10 lower than a specific meson-exchange calculation by Rost.

## Method

The authors derived baryon-baryon potentials (\(NN\), \(\Delta\Delta\), and \(NN^*\)) from a constituent quark model including one-gluon exchange and Goldstone-boson exchange interactions, using the Resonating Group Method for \(NN\) and \(\Delta\Delta\) and the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for the \(NN^*\) transition potentials. The deuteron binding energy and wave function were then obtained by solving the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation in momentum space on a 48-point Gauss-Legendre mesh per channel, including all channels with deuteron quantum numbers: \(^3S_1^{NN}\)–\(^3D_1^{NN}\), \(^3S_1^{NN^*}\)–\(^3D_1^{NN^*}\), and \(^3S_1^{\Delta\Delta}\)–\(^3D_1^{\Delta\Delta}\)–\(^7D_1^{\Delta\Delta}\)–\(^7G_1^{\Delta\Delta}\).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy fixed to -2.2246 MeV for all calculations by fine-tuning quark model parameters.
- \(NN\) channel probabilities: \(^3S_1 = 95.17\%\), \(^3D_1 = 4.53\%\) when all channels (\(NN\), \(\Delta\Delta\), \(NN^*\)) are included.
- \(NN^*(1440)\) channel probabilities: \(^3S_1 = 0.027\%\), \(^3D_1 = 0.024\%\).
- \(\Delta\Delta\) channel probabilities: \(^3S_1 = 0.13\%\), \(^3D_1 = 0.0036\%\), \(^7D_1 = 0.12\%\), \(^7G_1 = 0.0062\%\).
- Deuteron root-mean-square radius \(r_m = 1.986\) fm, asymptotic S-wave amplitude \(A_S = 0.8944\) fm\(^{-1/2}\), and asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta = 0.0250\) for the full calculation.

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model studies of the \(NN\) interaction by Oka, Faessler, and others, which found negligible contributions from \(\Delta\Delta\) and hidden color components, and by Valcarce et al. (1995) who demonstrated the importance of tensor coupling to \(N\Delta\) configurations. The calculation contradicts the meson-exchange estimate of Rost, who found 0.16% for the \(NN^*\) channels, and agrees with the upper estimate of Glozman et al. (\(<10^{-2}\)% for \(NN^*\) and \(<10^{-3}\)% for \(\Delta\Delta\)). The results enable more precise studies of baryon resonance effects in few-nucleon systems using a consistent quark-model framework.
