# Nuclear Binding Effects in Relativistic Coulomb Sum Rules

> We extend the formulation of relativistic Coulomb sum rules to account for the average effects of nuclear binding on the initial and final states of ejected nucleons. Relativistic interactions are included by using a Dirac representation adapted from a vector-scalar field theory. The scalar field reduces the effective nucleon mass $M^*$ and increases the relativistic effects of recoil and Fermi motion. We consider two models for the off-shell behavior of the nuclear electromagnetic current, and demonstrate that the sum rule is accurate for applications to data over the interesting range of $M^*$ and three momentum $q$. We further indicate that the form of the sum rule is sufficiently general to accommodate a broad class of off-shell form factor models.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic Coulomb sum rule (RCSR) to include average nuclear binding effects via a mean-field scalar potential that reduces the effective nucleon mass \(M^*\), but the derivation requires that the photon-energy dependence of the electromagnetic current be factorable, which is satisfied exactly only for certain off-shell form factor models (Model G) and approximately for others (Model F) after a specific replacement for the energy variable.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Coulomb sum rule using a Dirac plane-wave basis adapted from quantum hadrodynamics (QHD-I), where nucleons move in uniform scalar and vector mean fields. They expand the field operators in this "\(M^*\)-basis," apply the nucleons-only approximation to remove antinucleon contributions, and use closure over spacelike final states to separate one-body and two-body terms. Two off-shell models for the nucleon electromagnetic vertex are considered: Model F assumes the Dirac and Pauli form factors \(F_1(Q^2), F_2(Q^2)\) are unchanged in the medium, while Model G assumes the Sachs form factors \(G_E(Q^2), G_M(Q^2)\) are unchanged, leading to a modified Gordon identity with \(M^*\). The one-body term is evaluated via a moment expansion of the relativistic recoil factor in powers of the nucleon momentum \(\mathbf{p}\).

## Key numbers

- The effective nucleon mass is defined as \(M^* \equiv M - g_s\phi\), where \(g_s\phi\) is the scalar mean-field potential from QHD-I.
- In Model G, the recoil function \(r_\sigma(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{q})\) is identical to the free PWIA result but with the replacement \(M \rightarrow M^*\).
- In Model F, the effective Sachs form factors at \(\mathbf{p}=0\) are given by \(\epsilon_\sigma^*(\mathbf{q})\) and \(\mu_\sigma^*(\mathbf{q})\) as explicit functions of \(M^*\), \(M\), and \(E_{\mathbf{q}}^* = \sqrt{\mathbf{q}^2 + M^{*2}}\).
- The kinematic variable \(\tilde\tau^*\) is defined as \(\tilde\tau^* \equiv [\mathbf{q}^2 - (E_{\mathbf{p}+\mathbf{q}}^* - E_{\mathbf{p}}^*)^2] / 4M^{*2}\).
- The Coulomb sum \(\Sigma(\mathbf{q})\) is integrated over \(\omega\) from \(\omega_{\rm el}^+\) to \(q\), with the upper limit \(q\) restricting to spacelike four-momenta.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Coulomb sum rule derived by Koltun and Ferree (1994) in a free PWIA to include mean-field binding effects from QHD-I, following the approach of Serot and Walecka (1986). It addresses the same off-shell form factor sensitivity studied by de Forest (1983) and by Chinn and Picklesimer (1995), and it generalizes earlier Fermi-gas calculations by Matsui (1981) and by Do Dang and Van Giai (1984) that used a reduced effective mass. The reformulation enables extraction of the two-body correlation function \(C(\mathbf{q})\) from measured longitudinal \((e,e')\) responses at CEBAF and other high-energy facilities, provided the off-shell model dependence is controlled.
