# Temperature oscillations and sound waves in hadronic matter

> Recent high energy CERN LHC experiments on transverse momenta distributions of produced particles seem to show the existence of some (small but persistent) log-periodic oscillation in the ratios $R = σ_{data}\left( p_T\right)/σ_{fit}\left( p_T\right)$. We argue that they can provide us with so far unnoticed information on the production process, which can be interpreted as the presence of some kind of sound waves formed during the collision process in the bulk of the produced high density matter.

# Wiki Page: Temperature oscillations and sound waves in hadronic matter

## Key claim

The small but persistent log-periodic oscillations observed in the ratio of measured transverse momentum distributions to their power-law fits in LHC data can be interpreted as evidence for sound waves formed in the bulk of produced high-density matter during the collision process. The authors argue that these oscillations are not artifacts of measurement or detector details, as they appear across all experiments and energies where the transverse momentum range reaches 200 GeV, and become stronger in nuclear collisions with increasing centrality.

## Method

The authors start from the Tsallis distribution used to fit transverse momentum spectra and allow the temperature parameter T to oscillate log-periodically as a function of transverse momentum p_T, described by T(p_T) = a + b sin[c ln(p_T + d) + e]. They then perform a Fourier transform of this oscillating temperature to obtain T(r) as a function of distance r in the plane perpendicular to the collision axis. By solving the wave equation for a cylindrical source with a wave number K(r) = alpha/r, they show that the resulting solution yields log-periodic oscillations in rT'(r), which they interpret as acoustic waves in the hadronic medium.

## Key numbers

- Amplitude of temperature oscillations relative to mean: b/a ~ 3% (ratio of oscillatory to constant component)
- For p+p collisions at 7 TeV: rT'(r) = 5.1 sin[(2π/3.2) ln(r) + 0.42]
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV: rT'(r) = 18.53 sin[(2π/3.68) ln(r) - 0.51]
- Ratio of sound velocities: c_Pb+Pb / c_p+p ≈ 1.15
- Ratio of multiplicity fluctuations: varpi_p+p / varpi_Pb+Pb ≈ 1.32 (compared to independently measured 1.29 ± 0.04 for negatively charged hadrons)
- Fit parameters for p+p: a = 0.143, b = 0.0045, c = 2.0, d = 2.0, e = -0.4
- Fit parameters for Pb+Pb: a = 0.131, b = 0.017, c = 1.7, d = 0.05, e = 0.98
- Regular oscillatory structure visible for r up to ~0.1 fm, essentially disappears by r ~ 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2015-2016) where log-periodic oscillations in transverse momentum distributions were attributed to discrete scale invariance or complex power indices. The Fourier transform approach was inspired by Hindusi (2016), who used similar methods to study temperature fluctuations in non-equilibrated systems described by the Boltzmann transport equation. The results connect to hydrodynamical models of strongly interacting matter at RHIC and LHC, where evidence for a "perfect fluid" and Mach shock waves from hard partons traversing the medium has been reported (HydroWaves, various authors). The observed higher sound speed in nuclear collisions compared to proton-proton collisions is consistent with measurements from the NA61/SHINE collaboration at SPS energies, and the relation between sound velocity and multiplicity fluctuations follows from statistical mechanics (Hill, Balescu). The work enables further investigation of the space-time structure of collisions through Fourier analysis of temperature oscillations, and suggests that the medium in both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions is inhomogeneous with position-dependent density and sound velocity.
