# On the exact solution for the Schrödinger equation

> For almost 75 years, the general solution for the Schrödinger equation was assumed to be generated by an exponential or a time-ordered exponential known as the Dyson series. We study the unitarity of a solution in the case of a singular Hamiltonian and provide a new methodology that is not based on the assumption that the underlying space is $L^{2}(\mathbb{R})$. Then, an alternative operator for generating the time evolution that is manifestly unitary is suggested, regardless of the choice of Hamiltonian. The new construction involves an additional positive operator that normalizes the wave function locally and allows us to preserve unitarity, even when dealing with infinite dimensional or non-normed spaces. Our considerations show that Schrödinger and Liouville equations are, in fact, two sides of the same coin and together they provide a unified description for unbounded quantum systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard Dyson-series (time-ordered exponential) solution to the Schrodinger equation is not manifestly unitary for singular Hamiltonians, and proposes an alternative time-evolution operator that is always unitary by incorporating an additional positive operator that normalizes the wave function locally, without assuming the underlying space is L2(R).

## Method

The author examines the unitarity of the standard exponential and time-ordered exponential (Dyson series) solutions for the Schrodinger equation in the case of a singular Hamiltonian. A new operator construction is introduced that includes an extra positive operator to locally normalize the wave function, ensuring manifest unitarity regardless of the Hamiltonian's properties. The analysis shows that this construction unifies the Schrodinger and Liouville equations into a single framework for describing unbounded quantum systems.

This work challenges the long-standing assumption, dating back to Dyson (1949), that the time-ordered exponential (Dyson series) provides the general solution to the Schrodinger equation. The paper extends the mathematical foundations of quantum mechanics by addressing the unitarity problem for singular Hamiltonians, which is not covered by standard treatments.
