# Probing the two-quasiparticle $K^π=8^+$ isomeric structure and enhanced stability in the proton drip-line nuclei

> Stimulated by recent experimental discoveries [{Phys. Lett. B \textbf{847}, 138310 (2023)} and {Phys. Rev. Lett. \textbf{132}, 072502 (2024)}], two-quasiparticle $K^π=8^+$ isomeric structure (related to the neutron $h_{9/2}$ and $f_{7/2}$ orbitals) in $^{160}_{76}$Os$_{84}$ that lies at the two-proton drip line has been studied by means of the configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface calculations. Calculated results indicate that, for such an isomer, the excitation energy can be well reproduced and its oblate shape can be enhanced by the polarization effects of the two high-$K$ orbits. Comparing with experimental data, two sets of the widely used Woods-Saxon parameters, especially, the spin-orbit coupling one, are evaluated and argued. It is found that, considering the uncertainty of the spin-orbit coupling strength, the energy crossing or inversion of the $h_{9/2}$ and $f_{7/2}$ neutrons can occur, which may lead to three kinds of different evolution-trends of two-quasiparticle excitation energies with the changing quadrupole deformation $β_2$. With decreasing spin-orbit coupling interaction, the structure of the $K^π=8^+$ isomeric state will evolute from $νh_{9/2}f_{7/2}$ ($ν9/2^-[505] \otimes 7/2^-[503]$) to the mixing of $νh_{9/2}f_{7/2}$ and $νh_{9/2}^2$ ($ν9/2^-[505] \otimes 7/2^-[514]$) to $νh_{9/2}^2$, indicating that its structural probes is still of interest and an arbitrary assignment may be risky. The related theoretical calculations and experimental evidences e.g., the transition properties, are desirable. In addition, similar to that in superheavy nuclei, it is suggested that the stability inversion between high-$K$ isomeric states and ground states might occur in this proton drip-line mass region, e.g., in the hitherto unknown nucleus $^{162}_{78}$Pt$_{84}$.

## Key claim

The two-quasiparticle $K^\pi=8^+$ isomeric state in the proton drip-line nucleus $^{160}$Os can be described by configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface calculations, but the intrinsic structure of the isomer may evolve from $\nu h_{9/2}f_{7/2}$ to $\nu h_{9/2}^2$ depending on the uncertain spin-orbit coupling strength, making an arbitrary assignment of the configuration risky.

## Method

Configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface (PES) calculations were performed in a three-dimensional deformation space ($\beta_2, \gamma, \beta_4$) using the macroscopic-microscopic Strutinsky method with a deformed Woods-Saxon mean-field Hamiltonian. The approximate particle-number conserved Lipkin-Nagami treatment of pairing was employed, and the unpaired neutron orbitals were tracked and blocked at each deformation lattice point to specify the two-quasiparticle configuration. Two sets of widely-used Woods-Saxon parameters (universal and Stockholm-I) were evaluated, with the Stockholm-I set selected for the main calculations based on its better reproduction of single-particle energies in $^{146}$Gd.

## Key numbers

- The calculated excitation energy of the $K^\pi=8^+$ isomer in $^{160}$Os is 1.885 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.844(18) MeV from Briscoe (2023).
- The ground state of $^{160}$Os has a calculated quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 = 0.047$ with $\gamma = 41^\circ$, while the $K^\pi=8^+$ isomer has $\beta_2 = 0.071$ with $\gamma = 60^\circ$ (oblate shape).
- The experimental half-life of the $8^+$ isomeric state in $^{160}$Os is $41^{+15}_{-9}$ $\mu$s, and the ground-state half-life is $97^{+97}_{-32}$ $\mu$s (Briscoe 2023).
- For the hitherto unknown nucleus $^{162}$Pt, the calculated excitation energy of the $K^\pi=8^+$ state is 2.029 MeV, with the isomer having $\beta_2 = 0.062$ and $\gamma = 60^\circ$.
- The optimal spin-orbit coupling strength $\lambda$ extracted from the $1h$ orbital in $^{146}$Gd is approximately 29, while from the $3p$ orbital in $^{132}$Sn it is approximately 14, indicating a large uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of two-quasiparticle $K^\pi=8^+$ isomers along the $N=84$ isotonic chain, following experimental discoveries in $^{154}$Yb (Zhang 1993), $^{156}$Hf (Joss 2017), $^{158}$W (Seweryniak 2005), and the recent identification of $^{160}$Os (Briscoe 2023; Yang 2024). The calculations evaluate the performance of the universal and Stockholm-I Woods-Saxon parameter sets (Cwiok 1987; Bhagwat 2010) and suggest that stability inversion between high-$K$ isomeric states and ground states, similar to that observed in superheavy nuclei such as $^{270}$Ds (Hofmann 2001) and $^{252}$Rf (Khuyagbaatar 2025), might occur in the proton drip-line mass region, motivating future experimental searches in $^{162}$Pt.
