# Microscopic Study of (p,$γ$) Reactions in Mass Region A=110-125

> Low energy proton capture reactions have been studied in statistical model using the semi-microscopic optical potential in the mass range A=110-125. Nuclear density obtained from relativistic mean field calculation has been folded with nucleon-nucleon interaction to obtain the optical potential. Theoretical results have been compared with experimental measurements to normalize the potential. Results have also been compared with a standard calculation available in the literature.

## Key claim

The semi-microscopic optical potential, obtained by folding a density-dependent M3Y interaction with nuclear densities from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations and normalized by a single global factor of 0.9 on the real part, can reasonably describe experimental astrophysical S-factors for (p,γ) reactions in the mass range A=110-125, except for a few cases such as the reaction on ¹¹⁹Sn.

## Method

Nuclear density profiles for stable and unstable nuclei in the A=110-125 region were calculated using the relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian density FSU Gold in coordinate space assuming spherical symmetry, with pairing treated under the BCS approximation. The density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) interaction was folded with these densities to obtain the real part of the optical potential, which was then multiplied by a factor of 0.9; the imaginary part was taken identical to the real part. This potential was implemented in the TALYS1.4 code for Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations, using the Constant Temperature Fermi Gas model for level densities and Goriely's Hybrid model for the gamma strength function, with width fluctuation correction included.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical charge radii for stable isotopes in the mass region A=110-125 agree with experimental values to within a relative difference of less than 0.5%.
- Theoretical binding energies for the same nuclei show a maximum error of 0.2% compared to experimental values from Audi et al.
- The Gamow window for the proton capture reactions considered ranges from 1.8 to 4.5 MeV for temperatures around 2-3 GK.
- The calculated proton capture reaction rates are larger than the NON-SMOKER rates by a factor of approximately 2-3 in most cases, over the temperature range 1.5-4 GK.

## Context

This work extends a previous microscopic approach applied to the mass region A=55-100 (Gangopadhyay et al., Chakraborty et al., Dutta et al.) to the heavier mass region A=110-125, which contains several p-nuclei such as ¹¹³In, ¹¹²,¹¹⁴,¹¹⁵Sn, ¹²⁰Te, and ¹²⁴,¹²⁶Xe. The calculated reaction rates are compared with the standard NON-SMOKER calculations of Rauscher et al., and the authors suggest that the effect of the present rates on p-process network calculations should be studied.
