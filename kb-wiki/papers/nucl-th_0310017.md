# Lagrangians for invariant sub-spaces of the squared Pauli-Lubanski vector

## Key claim

The authors present an alternative to the Rarita-Schwinger framework for describing higher-spin states that avoids the Velo-Zwanziger problem of acausal propagation and complex energies in electromagnetic fields, but only for the specific case of the parity-singlet “has-been” spin-3/2 sector (the τ₁ = 15/4 subspace) and without producing pure-spin propagators.

## Method

The authors construct the covariant projector onto the τ₁ = 15/4 invariant subspace of the squared Pauli-Lubanski vector in the 16-dimensional product space Ψ = A ⊗ ψ (four-vector times Dirac spinor), rather than using the traditional Rarita-Schwinger tensor-spinor ψ_μ with separate space-time and spinor indices. They derive the corresponding Lagrangian and propagator for this sector, then minimally couple an electromagnetic field, compute the determinant of the resulting wave equation using Mathematica, and extract the energy-momentum dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The projector onto the parity-singlet subspace is given by P^(15/4)(p) = -1/3 [ (1/m²) \widetilde{W}² + 3/4 (1₄ ⊗ 1₄) ], with eigenvalue τ₁ = 15/4 for the squared Pauli-Lubanski vector.
- The complementary projector P^(3/4)(p) = 1₁₆ - P^(15/4)(p) projects onto two states of opposite parity with τ₁ = τ₀ = 3/4.
- The propagator for the τ₁ = 15/4 sector is S^(15/4)(p) = [2 S·S - (4/m²) G·G - 3 (1₄ ⊗ 1₄)] / [12 (p² - m²)].
- In the presence of a magnetic field B_z oriented along the z axis, the energy-momentum dispersion relation is E² = (p_z - e B_z)² + m², showing no complex energies.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kirchbach (1997) and Kirchbach and Ahluwalia (2002) on the Lorentz multiplet structure of baryon spectra and the spacetime structure of massive gravitinos. It directly addresses the Velo-Zwanziger problem (Velo and Zwanziger, 1969), where the standard Rarita-Schwinger framework for higher-spin fields yields imaginary energies and acausal propagation when coupled to electromagnetic fields. The formalism enables a pathology-free covariant description of the propagating “has-been” spin-3/2⁻ piece of the polar vector spinor, though the authors note they did not produce pure-spin propagators.
