# Three regimes of QCD

> While the QCD Lagrangian as the whole is only chirally symmetric, its electric part has larger chiral-spin SU(2)_{CS} and SU(2N_F) symmetries. This allows separation of the electric and magnetic interactions in a given reference frame. Artificial truncation of the near-zero modes of the Dirac operator results in the emergence of the SU(2)_{CS} and SU(2N_F) symmetries in hadron spectrum. This implies that while the confining electric interaction is distributed among all modes of the Dirac operator, the magnetic interaction is located at least predominantly in the near-zero modes. Given this observation one could anticipate that above the pseudocritical temperature, where the near-zero modes of the Dirac operator are suppressed, QCD is SU(2)_{CS} and SU(2N_F) symmetric, which means absence of deconfinement in this regime. Solution of the N_F=2 QCD on the lattice with a chirally symmetric Dirac operator reveals that indeed in the interval Tc - 3Tc QCD is approximately SU(2)_{CS} and SU(2N_F) symmetric which implies that degrees of freedom are chirally symmetric quarks bound by the chromoelectric field into color-singlet objects without the chromomagnetic effects. This regime is referred to as a Stringy Fluid. At larger temperatures this emergent symmetry smoothly disappears and QCD approaches the Quark-Gluon Plasma regime with quasifree quarks. The Hadron Gas, the Stringy Fluid and the Quark-Gluon Plasma differ by symmetries, degrees of freedom and properties.

# Three regimes of QCD

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD matter in the temperature interval between the pseudocritical temperature Tc (~155 MeV) and approximately 3Tc is not a Quark-Gluon Plasma but a distinct "Stringy Fluid" regime where degrees of freedom are chirally symmetric quarks bound by the chromoelectric field into color-singlet objects without chromomagnetic effects, based on the emergence of SU(2)CS and SU(2NF) symmetries observed in lattice simulations.

## Method

The author analyzes spatial and temporal meson correlators from lattice QCD simulations of Nf=2 QCD with a chirally symmetric Domain Wall Dirac operator (JLQCD ensembles) at temperatures from 220 MeV to 960 MeV. The symmetry breaking parameter κ is defined as the ratio of splitting within the SU(2)CS multiplet to the distance between multiplets, and is evaluated at zT=2 to quantify the emergence and disappearance of chiral-spin symmetry across temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature Tc ~ 155 MeV (from lattice QCD with physical quark masses)
- SU(2)CS and SU(4) symmetries observed in the temperature interval Tc - 3Tc (approximately 155 - 465 MeV)
- Symmetry breaking parameter κ < 5% for all ensembles with T ~ 220 - 500 MeV, evaluated at zT=2
- At T ~ 960 MeV (5.5 Tc), full QCD correlators nearly coincide with free quark correlators
- Lattice simulations performed at temperatures T = (220 - 960) MeV
- At T = 220 MeV (1.2 Tc) and T = 380 MeV (2.2 Tc), multiplet structures of all U(1)A, SU(2)R × SU(2)L, SU(2)CS and SU(4) symmetries are observed
- Between T ~ 500 MeV and T ~ 660 MeV, κ increases to values of order ~1

## Context

This work extends the author's prior discovery (Glozman, 2015) that artificial truncation of near-zero Dirac modes at zero temperature produces SU(2)CS and SU(4) symmetries in hadron spectra, which was observed on JLQCD gauge configurations (Denissenya et al., 2015). The paper challenges the standard picture that QCD matter above Tc is a Quark-Gluon Plasma, instead proposing three distinct regimes: Hadron Gas (below Tc), Stringy Fluid (Tc - 3Tc), and Quark-Gluon Plasma (above ~3Tc). This reinterpretation is enabled by lattice results from JLQCD collaborations (Rohrhofer et al., 2017, 2019) showing that the emergent symmetries persist in full QCD at finite temperature without mode truncation. The findings suggest that experimental observations of perfect fluidity at RHIC and LHC may be explained by the Stringy Fluid regime rather than a strongly coupled QGP.
