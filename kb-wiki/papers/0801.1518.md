# On the excluded space in applications of Feshbach projection formalism

> Various model applications in nuclear structure and reactions have been formulated starting with the Feshbach projection formalism. In recent studies a truncated excluded space has been enumerated to facilitate calculation and identify a convergence in expansions within that truncation. However, the effect of any remainder must be addressed before results from such can be considered physical.

# On the excluded space in applications of Feshbach projection formalism

## Key claim
Even when a truncated excluded space (Q) is enumerated to convergence in Feshbach-based calculations, the effect of any remainder space (R) must be addressed before results can be considered physical; errors from neglecting the remainder arise already at second order in the coupling expansion, not only at higher orders.

## Method
The authors extend the standard Feshbach two-space (P and Q) projection formalism to a three-space decomposition (P, Q, and R), where Q is the enumerable excluded space and R is the remainder. They derive the coupled Schrodinger equations for the three projection operators and obtain the effective P-space Hamiltonian including couplings through both Q and R spaces, showing that the R-space contributions enter at second order in the coupling expansion.

## Key numbers
- The measured B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) in 12C is 7.77 e²fm⁴; calculations with the Zheng et al. interaction give 5.45 e²fm⁴ in a 0ℏω space and 6.9 e²fm⁴ in a complete (0+2)ℏω no-core shell model space.
- The super-deformed 0+ state in 12C lies at 7.6 MeV excitation energy, suggesting 4-particle correlations must be included in the shell model.
- Polarization charges of typically 0.5e are required when restricting the P space to 0ℏω for valence nucleons in the shell model.

## Context
This work extends the Feshbach projection formalism (Feshbach 1958, 1962) used in the no-core shell model (Navratil and Barrett 1996), the continuum shell model (Volya and Zelevinsky 2006), and multi-channel algebraic scattering theory (Amos 2003). The authors identify that truncated Q-space calculations, such as those for 12C using G-matrix interactions (Navratil and Barrett 1996, 1998, 2000; Hayes 2003), show convergence to the enumerated Q-space values but remain far from empirical results, particularly for systems exhibiting alpha-clustering. The three-space formalism enables assessment of whether neglected remainder-space couplings cause errors at second order, motivating the need to identify dominant states in the excluded spaces.
