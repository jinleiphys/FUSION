# High-density symmetry energy from subthreshold hyperon production in heavy-ion collisions

> The hyperon dynamics in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energy has been investigated within the quantum molecular dynamics transport model. The isospin and momentum dependent hyperon-nucleon potential and the threshold energy correction on the hyperon elementary cross section are included in the model. It is found that the high-density symmetry energy is dependent on the isospin ratios $Σ^{-}/Σ^{+}$ and $Ξ^{-}/Ξ^{0}$, in particular in the domain of high kinetic energies. The isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions influences the neutron/proton ratio in the high-density region. The $Σ^{-}/Σ^{+}$ ratio depends on the stiffness of symmetry energy, in particular at the beam energy below the threshold value (E$_{th}$=1.58 GeV), i.e., the kinetic energy spectra of the single ratios, excitation functions and energy spectra of the double ratios in the isotopic reactions of $^{108}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn, $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn, $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn. The double strangeness ratio $Ξ^{-}/Ξ^{0}$ weakly depends on the symmetry energy because of the hyperon-hyperon collision mainly contributing the $Ξ$ production below the threshold energy (E$_{th}$ = 3.72 GeV).

## Key claim

The high-density symmetry energy can be constrained by the isospin ratios Σ⁻/Σ⁺ and Ξ⁻/Ξ⁰ from hyperon production in heavy-ion collisions, but only the Σ⁻/Σ⁺ ratio is sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy; the Ξ⁻/Ξ⁰ ratio shows only a weak dependence because Ξ production below threshold energy (E_th = 3.72 GeV) is dominated by hyperon-hyperon collisions rather than primary nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Method

The author used the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model to simulate heavy-ion collisions of isotopic tin systems (¹⁰⁸Sn + ¹¹²Sn, ¹¹²Sn + ¹¹²Sn, ¹²⁴Sn + ¹²⁴Sn, and ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn) at beam energies near and below the hyperon production thresholds. The model includes isospin- and momentum-dependent hyperon-nucleon potentials, threshold energy corrections on hyperon elementary cross sections, and different stiffness parameters (γ_s = 0.3, 0.5, 1, 2, plus a supersoft case) for the symmetry energy, with corresponding slope parameters L ranging from 24 MeV to 139 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for Σ production is E_th = 1.58 GeV; for Ξ production it is E_th = 3.72 GeV.
- The slope parameter L for the symmetry energy takes values of 24 MeV (supersoft), 42 MeV (γ_s = 0.3), 53 MeV (γ_s = 0.5), 82 MeV (γ_s = 1), and 139 MeV (γ_s = 2).
- All symmetry energy parameterizations cross at saturation density ρ₀ with a value of 31.5 MeV.
- The Λ-nucleon optical potential at saturation density is -32 MeV; the Ξ-nucleon potential is -16 MeV.
- The Σ optical potential uses isoscalar V₀ = 14.8 MeV and isovector V₁ = 67.8 MeV, fitted to chiral EFT calculations with a cutoff of 600 MeV.
- The neutron/proton ratio in the ¹²⁴Sn + ¹²⁴Sn reaction at 1A GeV has an average value of 1.48 for the reaction system.

## Context

This work extends prior transport-model studies of hyperon production in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., by the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck and quantum molecular dynamics approaches) and addresses the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star physics, where the appearance of hyperons softens the equation of state and reduces the maximum neutron star mass below observed values (e.g., PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ and J0740+6620 at 2.14⁺⁰·¹⁰₋₀·₀₉ M⊙). The results suggest that double ratios of Σ⁻/Σ⁺ from isotopic reactions at 1A GeV are promising observables for constraining the high-density symmetry energy, and they motivate future experiments at facilities such as HIAF (China), NICA (Russia), and J-PARC (Japan).
