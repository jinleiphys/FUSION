# Correspondence between Color Glass Condensate and High-Twist Formalism

> The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory and the collinear factorization at high-twist (HT) are two well-known frameworks describing perturbative QCD multiple scatterings in nuclear media. It has long been recognized that these two formalisms have their own domain of validity in different kinematics regions. Taking direct photon production in proton-nucleus collisions as an example, we clarify for the first time the relation between CGC and HT at the level of a physical observable. We show that the CGC formalism beyond shock-wave approximation, and with the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal interference effect is consistent with the HT formalism in the transition region where they overlap. Such a unified picture paves the way for mapping out the phase diagram of parton density in nuclear medium from dilute to dense region.

# Correspondence between Color Glass Condensate and High-Twist Formalism

## Key claim
The authors show for the first time that the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, when extended beyond the shock-wave approximation to include sub-eikonal longitudinal phase factors and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) interference effect, is exactly consistent with the high-twist (HT) collinear factorization formalism at twist-4 level for direct photon production in proton-nucleus collisions. This matching holds in the transition region where both formalisms overlap, specifically at finite momentum fraction x where the naive small-x limit of CGC alone would not reproduce the full HT result.

## Method
The authors compute direct photon production in proton-nucleus collisions using both the CGC effective theory (with hybrid factorization) and the generalized collinear factorization at twist-4. They expand the CGC light-like Wilson line in powers of the gauge field to recover sub-eikonal longitudinal phase factors, and include triple-scattering diagrams (such as the central-cut diagram) that are normally omitted in the eikonal approximation but become non-negligible at finite x due to incomplete LPM destructive interference. The resulting expressions are expanded in inverse powers of the photon transverse momentum and matched term-by-term to the leading-twist and twist-4 collinear factorization results.

## Key numbers
- The leading-twist hard partonic cross-section for single scattering q+g → γ+q is given by H^(2) = (e_q^2 α_em α_s / N_c) ξ^2 [1+(1-ξ)^2] / p_{γ⊥}^4, where ξ = p_γ^- / (x_q P_p^-).
- The CGC result after collinear expansion in the small-x limit yields a term (2π)^2 α_s / N_c * (4 ξ^2 / p_{γ⊥}^2) * T_{gg}(x,0,0) as the twist-4 contribution, establishing the relation between the dipole distribution and the twist-4 gluon-gluon correlation function.
- The photon formation time is τ_γ = 2 x_q P_p^- ξ (1-ξ) / (p_{γ⊥} - ξ l_⊥)^2, which controls the LPM interference condition τ_γ ≫ Δy^- for coherent cancellation.
- The matching condition is demonstrated for p_{γ⊥} > Q_s, where Q_s is the saturation scale (typical transverse momentum from multiple scattering).

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing question about the relationship between the CGC effective theory (McLerran, Venugopalan, 1994) and the high-twist collinear factorization approach (Qiu, Sterman, 1990), which were previously understood to have separate domains of validity at small-x and moderate-x respectively. It extends earlier efforts to connect CGC to collinear factorization through sub-eikonal corrections (Altinoluk, 2014-2021; Chirilli, 2018-2021; Balitsky, 2015-2019) and semi-classical approaches (Boussarie, 2020-2023), but provides the first explicit matching at the level of a physical observable including the LPM effect. The result enables systematic calculation of perturbative hard parts across the full phase diagram of parton density in nuclear matter, from dilute to dense regimes, applicable to future measurements at RHIC, LHC, and the Electron-Ion Collider.
