# Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance in Mo-100: TDHF calculations and ground state deformation

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonance in molybdenum-100 has been measured in recent alpha scattering experiments and its strength function extracted. By performing time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme effective interactions, we are able to reproduce the gross features of the observed strength. The details of the structure are found to be dependent on the ground state shape. We use a measure of fit to determine which ground state shape correlates with the observed strength and find evidence for triaxiality in this nucleus.

## Key claim
The authors show that the isoscalar giant monopole resonance strength function in Mo-100 depends on the ground state shape, and they find quantitative evidence for triaxiality with shape parameters around β₂=0.28, γ=30°, based on a weighted error measure rather than the earlier visual comparison alone.

## Method
Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations with the SLy4 Skyrme effective interaction were performed using a triaxial TDHF code with shape constraints to prepare ground states at various deformations. An isoscalar monopole excitation was applied to each shape-constrained ground state, and the strength function was obtained from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent expectation value of the ISM operator. Each theoretical strength function was compared to experimental data from Howard (2020) using a weighted error formula that incorporates experimental uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- The ground state shape parameters tested were (β₂,γ) = (0.17,0°), (0.2,0°), (0.2,30°), and (0.28,30°).
- The weighted error W was lowest for the shape (β₂=0.28, γ=30°), confirming this as the best fit among the deformations tried.
- The weighted error formula is W = √[(1/N) × Σ Δ_i⁻¹(S_{i,e}−S_{i,t})² / Σ Δ_i⁻¹], where Δ_i is the experimental error at each data point.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier TDHF study (Shi 2023) by replacing visual comparison with a quantitative weighted error measure, reinforcing the conclusion that Mo-100 has a triaxial ground state. It builds on experimental measurements at RCNP Osaka (Howard 2020) showing ISM strength in open-shell isotopes near A=90, and on RPA analyses (Colò 2020) that linked deformation to a two-peaked structure via monopole-quadrupole coupling. The weighted fit approach provides a robust basis for future optimization studies of ground state deformation from giant resonance data.
