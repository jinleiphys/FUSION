# Drawing insights from pion parton distributions

> A symmetry-preserving continuum approach to the two valence-body bound-state problem is used to calculate the valence, glue and sea distributions within the pion; unifying them with, inter alia, electromagnetic pion elastic and transition form factors. The analysis reveals the following momentum fractions at the scale $ζ_2:=2\,$GeV: $\langle x_{\rm valence} \rangle = 0.48(3)$, $\langle x_{\rm glue} \rangle = 0.41(2)$, $\langle x_{\rm sea} \rangle = 0.11(2)$; and despite hardening induced by the emergent phenomenon of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, the valence-quark distribution function, ${q}^π(x)$, exhibits the $x\simeq 1$ behaviour predicted by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). After evolution to $ζ=5.2\,$GeV, the prediction for ${q}^π(x)$ matches that obtained using lattice-regularised QCD. This confluence should both stimulate improved analyses of existing data and aid in planning efforts to obtain new data on the pion distribution functions.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving continuum approach to the two valence-body bound-state problem, the authors obtain parameter-free predictions for the pion's valence, glue, and sea parton distributions that unify with electromagnetic form factor calculations; the valence-quark distribution exhibits the (1-x)^2 behavior predicted by QCD at x near 1, despite hardening from dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and after evolution to 5.2 GeV matches lattice-regularised QCD results.

## Method

The authors compute the pion's valence-quark distribution using a corrected expression for the virtual-photon-pion forward Compton scattering amplitude that restores basic symmetries, with dressed light-quark propagators and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes obtained from numerical solutions of integral equations using a renormalisation-group-invariant current-quark mass of 6.7 MeV. They compute the first six Mellin moments directly, use the Schlessinger point method to extend to the first eleven moments, and reconstruct the distribution function at the hadronic scale ζ_H = m_α = 0.30 GeV, defined by the infrared saturation of QCD's process-independent effective charge. Leading-order QCD evolution with this same running coupling then yields predictions at ζ_2 = 2 GeV and ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Momentum fractions at ζ_2 = 2 GeV: ⟨x_valence⟩ = 0.48(3), ⟨x_glue⟩ = 0.41(2), ⟨x_sea⟩ = 0.11(2)
- Large-x exponent at ζ_2: β(ζ_2) = 2.38(9)
- Large-x exponent at ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV: β(ζ_5) = 2.66(12)
- Momentum fractions at ζ_5: ⟨x⟩^π_g = 0.45(1), ⟨x⟩^π_sea = 0.14(2)
- Low-order moments at ζ_2: ⟨x⟩_u^π = 0.24(2), ⟨x^2⟩_u^π = 0.098(10), ⟨x^3⟩_u^π = 0.049(07)
- Low-order moments at ζ_5: ⟨x⟩_u^π = 0.21(2), ⟨x^2⟩_u^π = 0.076(9), ⟨x^3⟩_u^π = 0.036(5)
- Renormalisation-group-invariant current-quark mass: m̂_u = m̂_d = 6.7 MeV, corresponding to one-loop evolved mass m^{ζ_2=2 GeV} = 4.6 MeV
- Hadronic scale: ζ_H = m_α = 0.30 GeV, with Λ_QCD ≈ 0.23 GeV

## Context

This work extends the continuum analysis of Hecht (2000) by using realistic numerical solutions for propagators and amplitudes rather than algebraic models, and corrects the handbag-diagram representation as identified by Chang (2014). The results unify with prior continuum calculations of charged-pion elastic and neutral-pion transition form factors by Chang (2013), Raya (2015, 2016), Gao (2017), and Ding (2018). The prediction at ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV agrees with the lattice-regularised QCD result of Sufian (2019) and with the E615 Drell-Yan data from Conway (1989) after the next-to-leading-order reanalysis including soft-gluon resummation by Aicher (2010). This confluence enables improved analyses of existing pion Drell-Yan data and supports planning for tagged deep-inelastic scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab and other facilities.
