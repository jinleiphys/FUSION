# Magnetic dipole moments adjacent to doubly-magic nuclei in self-consistent mean-field theory with realistic spin-isospin and tensor forces

> Magnetic dipole ($M1$) moments in nuclei neighboring the doubly-magic core are investigated by the self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) approaches that allow for the breaking of the time-reversal symmetry. By the SCMF calculations with the M3Y-P6 interaction, which keeps realistic spin-isospin and tensor channels, the $M1$ moments are well reproduced, particularly those in the nuclei adjacent to $jj$-closed magicity. The results are in better agreement with the data than those with the Gogny-D1S interaction, slightly better than those of UNEDF1 supplemented by a spin-isospin channel adjusted to the $M1$ moments themselves, and comparable to the shell-model results with the chiral effective-field-theory ($χ$EFT) interaction. Analyses via quadrupole moments, occupation numbers and the lowest-order perturbation theory elucidate the cooperative effects of quadrupole deformation and spin correlation on the displacement from the Schmidt values, which has been known in terms of the quenching of the spin matrix elements. It is shown that a significant portion of the spin correlation is carried by the spin-isospin and tensor channels in the effective interaction. However, while agreement is remarkable at $^{131}$Sn$^m$, $^{133}$Sn and $^{209}$Pb, discrepancies remain at the $Z=\mathrm{odd}$ nuclei $^{133}$Sb, $^{207}$Ti and $^{209}$Bi, as in the $χ$EFT-based shell-model results.

## Key claim

The M3Y-P6 interaction, which retains realistic spin-isospin and tensor channels from the bare nucleonic interaction, reproduces magnetic dipole moments near doubly-magic nuclei better than the Gogny-D1S interaction and slightly better than UNEDF1 with an adjusted spin-isospin channel, but discrepancies remain at the Z-odd nuclei 133Sb, 207Ti and 209Bi, similar to the chiral effective-field-theory shell-model results.

## Method

Hartree-Fock calculations were performed using the M3Y-P6 semi-realistic interaction and the Gogny-D1S phenomenological interaction, employing the Gaussian expansion method with axial symmetry, parity symmetry, and RPT-symmetry imposed while allowing time-reversal symmetry breaking. The magnetic dipole moments were computed from the self-consistent mean-field solutions using the ratio formula mu^MF = J * <mu_0^(1)> / <J_z>, with J taken from the observed ground state or metastable state. For comparison, results from the J-projected calculations of Ref. [SDBG22] and the chiral effective-field-theory shell-model results of Ref. [MCSB24] were also examined.

## Key numbers

- Landau-Migdal parameter g'_0 = 0.970 for M3Y-P6, compared to 0.631 for D1S and 1.7 for UNEDF1 (at saturation point)
- Landau-Migdal parameter g_0 = 0.272 for M3Y-P6, compared to 0.466 for D1S and 0.4 for UNEDF1
- The M3Y-P6 interaction reproduces magnetic dipole moments well at 131Sn^m, 133Sn and 209Pb, with discrepancies remaining at 133Sb, 207Ti and 209Bi
- For nuclei adjacent to 16O and 40Ca, all self-consistent mean-field results yield negligibly small Delta-mu values, consistent with the ls-closure property
- The harmonic-oscillator parameter used for estimating single-particle quadrupole moments was hbar-omega = 41.2 A^{-1/3} MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic dipole moments in nuclei near doubly-magic cores using self-consistent mean-field theory, building on prior investigations of core polarization and spin quenching by Towner (1987), Arima et al. (1988), and Li and Meng (2018). The M3Y-P6 interaction, developed by Nakada (2013), keeps the central channel of the one-pion exchange potential and the tensor force from the G-matrix unmodified, unlike most phenomenological interactions such as Gogny-D1S. The results are compared with the J-projected calculations of Ref. [SDBG22] using D1S and UNEDF1, and with the chiral effective-field-theory shell-model results of Ref. [MCSB24]. The findings enable further investigation of how spin-isospin and tensor channels in effective interactions affect spin properties in finite nuclei, with implications for extrapolating spin properties to astrophysical environments.
