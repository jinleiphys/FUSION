# Factorization in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> In this talk, we discuss the factorization of the logarithms of energy in the Color Glass Condensate framework.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, the logarithms of energy that appear at next-to-leading order in the Color Glass Condensate framework can be factorized into separate JIMWLK evolution equations for each nucleus, provided the soft gluon radiation responsible for these logarithms is emitted before the collision, at a time when the projectiles are spacelike separated.

## Method

The author analyzes inclusive gluon and quark spectra at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, where incoming nuclei are described as collections of color charges \(\rho(\mathbf{x}_\perp)\) with a probability distribution \(W[\rho]\). The NLO corrections are expressed in terms of functional derivatives with respect to the initial classical fields on a surface \(\Sigma\), and the logarithmic dependence on the cutoff is extracted and shown to separate into the JIMWLK Hamiltonians \(\mathcal{H}_1\) and \(\mathcal{H}_2\) for the two nuclei individually.

## Key numbers

- The NLO correction to the dipole scattering amplitude in DIS is proportional to \(\ln(\Lambda_0^- / \Lambda_1^-)\) times the JIMWLK Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{H}\) acting on the LO amplitude, where \(\Lambda_0^-\) and \(\Lambda_1^-\) are longitudinal momentum cutoffs.
- The NLO correction to the inclusive gluon spectrum in nucleus-nucleus collisions separates as \(\ln(\Lambda^+) \mathcal{H}_1 + \ln(\Lambda^-) \mathcal{H}_2\), where \(\Lambda^+\) and \(\Lambda^-\) are the cutoffs for the two nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the factorization of energy logarithms from Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) off a single saturated projectile, where the JIMWLK equation governs the evolution of the source distribution \(W[\rho]\), to the case of two colliding saturated nuclei. The causality argument and the explicit calculation for the gluon spectrum show that the same JIMWLK distributions describe each nucleus independently, enabling resummation of leading logarithms in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The extension to quark production is presented as work in progress, with the NLO formula for the quark spectrum containing additional fermionic terms that must be shown to also factorize through the JIMWLK Hamiltonian.
