# Radiative energy loss of relativistic charged particles in absorptive media

> We determine the energy loss spectrum per time-interval of a relativistic charge traversing a dispersive medium. Polarization and absorption effects in the medium are modelled via a complex index of refraction. We find that the spectrum amplitude becomes exponentially damped due to absorption mechanisms. Taking explicitly the effect of multiple scatterings on the charge trajectory into account, we confirm results obtained in a previous work.

## Key claim
The energy loss spectrum per time-interval of a relativistic charge in a dispersive medium becomes exponentially damped due to absorption mechanisms, but this classical Abelian treatment is restricted to small ω ≪ E and requires improvement before firm quantitative conclusions can be drawn for heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors derived the negative differential work (energy loss spectrum) from Maxwell's equations for linear dispersive media, modeling polarization and absorption via a complex index of refraction n(ω) = n_r(ω) + i n_i(ω). They evaluated the three-momentum integrals by contour integration, then introduced a specific charge trajectory with multiple scatterings modeled as small deflection angles, performing stochastic averaging over deflection angles to obtain the averaged energy loss spectrum per time-interval.

## Key numbers
- The exponential damping factor in the spectrum amplitude is exp[-ω |n_i(ω)| Δr], where n_i(ω) is the imaginary part of the complex index of refraction and Δr is the spatial separation between charge positions at times t and t'.
- For the specific charge trajectory with multiple scatterings, the averaged quantity ⟨I₂⟩ = q̂ t̄²/E², where q̂ is defined as q̂ = ⟨θ(τ)²⟩ E²/(2τ), representing one half of the mean transverse momentum transfer squared per unit time, and E is the charge energy.
- The small deflection angle approximation requires q̂ t̄/(3E²) ≪ 1, which is naturally satisfied when t̄ is smaller than or of the order of the formation time.
- For the complex index of refraction ansatz n(ω)² = 1 - m²/ω² + 2iΓ/ω, radiation cannot be emitted for ω < m, where m is an effective mass and Γ is a finite width.

## Context
This work extends the classical treatment of Landau and Pomeranchuk (1953) for radiative energy loss in vacuum to absorptive media, confirming results from Bluhm (2011) with more detailed derivation. It provides an Abelian approximation for color charge dynamics in a quark-gluon plasma, contrasting with approaches using hard-thermal-loop gluon self-energies by Thoma (1991) and Djordjevic (2003) where emitted gluons have n_i(ω) = 0. The results enable future work incorporating finite medium size and interference between medium-induced radiation and initial bremsstrahlung from charge production.
