# Nuclear Charge Radius of $^{12}$Be

> The nuclear charge radius of $^{12}$Be was precisely determined using the technique of collinear laser spectroscopy on the $2s_{1/2}\rightarrow 2p_{1/2, 3/2}$ transition in the Be$^{+}$ ion. The mean square charge radius increases from $^{10}$Be to $^{12}$Be by $δ<r_{\rm c}^2>^{10,12} = 0.69(5) \fm^{2}$ compared to $δ<r_{\rm c}^2>^{10,11} = 0.49(5) \fm^{2}$ for the one-neutron halo isotope $^{11}$Be. Calculations in the fermionic molecular dynamics approach show a strong sensitivity of the charge radius to the structure of $^{12}$Be. The experimental charge radius is consistent with a breakdown of the N=8 shell closure.

## Key claim

The nuclear charge radius of $^{12}$Be increases relative to $^{10}$Be, consistent with a breakdown of the N=8 shell closure and a dominant $(sd)^2$ intruder configuration in the ground state, but the interpretation depends on the mixing between $p^2$ and $(sd)^2$ configurations in fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) calculations, with the spin-orbit force strength adjusted phenomenologically to match separation energies.

## Method

Collinear and anticollinear laser spectroscopy was performed on the $2s_{1/2}\rightarrow 2p_{1/2, 3/2}$ transition (D1 and D2 lines at 313 nm) in Be$^{+}$ ions produced at ISOLDE/CERN. The 40 keV ion beam was superimposed with copropagating and counter-propagating laser beams, and Doppler-tuning was applied via post-acceleration voltage; an ion-photon coincidence technique suppressed background from laser stray light. The transition rest-frame frequency was extracted from the product of the collinear and anticollinear frequencies, and isotope shifts relative to $^{9}$Be were converted to changes in mean square charge radius using calculated mass-shift values and the electronic field-shift factor $F = -17.02$ MHz/fm$^2$.

## Key numbers

- Mean square charge radius increase from $^{10}$Be to $^{12}$Be: $\delta\langle r_{\rm c}^2\rangle^{10,12} = 0.69(5)$ fm$^2$
- Mean square charge radius increase from $^{10}$Be to $^{11}$Be: $\delta\langle r_{\rm c}^2\rangle^{10,11} = 0.49(5)$ fm$^2$
- Absolute rms charge radius of $^{12}$Be: $R_{\rm c}(^{12}{\rm Be}) = 2.502(16)$ fm (relative uncertainty less than 1%)
- Reference radius of $^{9}$Be used for absolute scale: $R_{\rm c}(^{9}{\rm Be}) = 2.519(12)$ fm from elastic electron scattering (Jan72)
- Two-neutron separation energy of $^{12}$Be: $S_{2n} = 3.67$ MeV (Aud03)
- FMD calculated two-neutron separation energy: $S_{2n} = 3.81$ MeV
- FMD calculated $B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+)$: $8.75$ $e^2$fm$^4$, compared to experimental $8.0(3.0)$ $e^2$fm$^4$ (Ima09)
- FMD calculated $M(E0; 0_1^+ \rightarrow 0_2^+)$: $0.90$ $e$fm$^2$, compared to experimental $0.87(3)$ $e$fm$^2$ (Shi07)

## Context

This work extends previous laser spectroscopy measurements on beryllium isotopes $^{7-11}$Be reported by Noe09 and Zak10, which had been unable to measure $^{12}$Be due to low production rates. The result is compared with FMD calculations using a UCOM(SRG) interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential, with a phenomenological spin-orbit strength factor $\eta$ adjusted from 1.9 (for $^{9}$Be) to 2.2 (for $^{12}$Be) to simulate the role of three-body forces. The measured charge radius increase supports a breakdown of the N=8 shell closure with about 70% $(sd)^2$ intruder admixture in the ground state, consistent with other observables such as the lifetime of the isomeric $0^+$ state and quadrupole transition strengths to the first $2^+$ state.
