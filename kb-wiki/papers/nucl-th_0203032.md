# On the polarization effects in (p,n) reactions between the A=48 isobarical states

## Key claim

The isotopical dependence of spin-orbit splitting (neutron splitting larger than proton splitting in N>Z nuclei) previously discovered in heavy nuclei near doubly magic 208Pb and 132Sn is confirmed in polarization effects from charge-exchange (p,n) reactions on 48Ca, but only when surface absorption (α=0) is assumed and the spin-orbit parameters from low-energy spectroscopy are used without energy dependence.

## Method

The author uses the Lane model (isospin-invariant optical potential) with spin-orbit parameters V_ls = 33.2 MeV·fm² and β_ls = -0.6, previously determined from single-particle spectra in Isakov (2000), to calculate the analyzing power A for the 48Ca(p,n)48Sc reaction leading to the 0⁺ (6.67 MeV) isoanalogue state at 134 MeV incident proton energy. The calculations employ the Born approximation with an imaginary absorption term in the optical potential, comparing volume absorption (α=1), surface absorption (α=0), and mixed cases (α=0.5), as well as testing energy-dependent versus energy-independent spin-orbit parameters.

## Key numbers

- V_0 = -51.5 MeV, r_0 = 1.27 fm, V_ls = 33.2 MeV·fm², a ≈ 0.6 fm, β = 1.39, β_ls = -0.6 (parameters from Isakov (2000) used in the Lane potential)
- Incident proton energy E = 134 MeV (from Andersen et al. (1986) experiment)
- Calculated differential cross section at zero angle for 48Ca(p,n)48Sc* (I.A.S.) on unpolarized protons: ≈ 7.7 mb/sr (with α=0)
- Measured cross section at zero angle from Andersen et al. (1985): ∼ 7 mb/sr
- Theoretical prediction from microscopic theory (Andersen et al. 1985): ∼ 7.5 mb/sr
- Energy dependence of central potential: V_0 = V_0' (1 - 0.0058·E) with V_0' = -52 MeV
- Absorption term: W_V (MeV) = -3.3 · (1 + 0.03·E)

## Context

This work extends the findings of Isakov (2000) on isotopical dependence of spin-orbit splitting in heavy nuclei (208Pb, 132Sn) to the A=48 region, where experimental single-particle data are incomplete due to fragmentation. The analysis uses the Lane model (Lane 1962) rather than the microscopic DWIA approach used by Andersen et al. (1986) for the same 48Ca(p,n) reaction data. The results enable validation of the β_ls parameter from nuclear spectroscopy using charge-exchange reaction data, and demonstrate that surface absorption (with (1-α) ≥ ∼0.5) is required to describe polarization effects, while energy dependence of the spin-orbit parameter degrades agreement with experiment.
