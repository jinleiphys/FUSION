# Localization and clustering in the nuclear Fermi liquid

> Using the framework of nuclear energy density functionals we examine the conditions for single-nucleon localization and formation of cluster structures in finite nuclei. We propose to characterize localization by the ratio of the dispersion of single-nucleon wave functions to the average inter-nucleon distance. This parameter generally increases with mass and describes the gradual transition from a hybrid phase in light nuclei, characterized by the spatial localization of individual nucleon states that leads to the formation of cluster structures, toward the Fermi liquid phase in heavier nuclei. Values of the localization parameter that correspond to a crystal phase cannot occur in finite nuclei. Typical length and energy scales in nuclei allow the formation of liquid drops, clusters, and halo structures.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the transition from cluster structures in light nuclei to the Fermi liquid phase in heavier nuclei can be characterized by a dimensionless localization parameter α, defined as the ratio of the spatial dispersion of single-nucleon wave functions to the average inter-nucleon distance, with the condition that a crystal phase (α ≲ 0.8) cannot occur in finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculated the localization parameter α from self-consistent ground-state solutions of nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs) for N=Z nuclei and ²⁰⁸Pb, using both the non-relativistic SLy4 and relativistic DD-ME2 functionals. They also derived an approximate analytic expression for α using a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential, with the nuclear radius and potential depth determined from the same EDFs.

## Key numbers

- The localization parameter α ≈ 0.67 A¹/⁶ for a typical potential depth V₀ = 70 MeV, using the liquid drop parameterization R = r₀A¹/³ with r₀ = 1.25 fm
- For self-consistent calculations with DD-ME2: α = 0.97 for ²⁰Ne, 0.95 for ²⁴Mg, 0.96 for ²⁸Si, 0.96 for ³²S, and 1.31 for ²⁰⁸Pb
- For self-consistent calculations with SLy4: α = 0.99 for ²⁰Ne, 1.00 for ²⁴Mg, 0.99 for ²⁸Si, 0.99 for ³²S, and 1.28 for ²⁰⁸Pb
- Average α values from DD-ME2 for all occupied orbitals: 0.94 for ¹⁶O, 1.02 for ²⁰Ne, 1.02 for ²⁴Mg, 1.08 for ⁴⁰Ca, 1.22 for ⁹⁰Zr
- The transition from localized clusters to liquid state occurs for nuclei with A ≈ 30
- The effective nucleon mass is 0.70 m for SLy4 and 0.66 m for DD-ME2, changing α by a factor (m/m*)¹/⁴ ≈ 1.1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ebran et al., 2012) showing that the confining nuclear potential determines localization and clustering, and builds on Mottelson's quantality parameter Λ for characterizing the quantum liquid versus crystal transition in infinite systems. The localization parameter α provides a mass-dependent alternative to the quantality parameter, which was limited by its lack of nuclear size dependence. The results enable a unified description of liquid drop, cluster, and halo phases in nuclei, and are relevant for understanding the transition from nuclear matter to Wigner crystals in neutron star crusts, as studied by Baym (1971), Lorenz (1993), and Watanabe (2000).
