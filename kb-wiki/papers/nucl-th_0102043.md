# Quark model predictions for $K^*$ photoproduction on the proton

> The photoproduction of $K^*$ vector mesons is investigated in a quark model with an effective Lagrangian. Including both baryon resonance excitations and {\it t}-channel exchanges, observables for the reactions $γp\to K^{*0}Σ^+$ and $γp\to K^{*+}Σ^0$ are predicted, using the SU(3)-flavor-blind assumption of non-perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical predictions for nucleon resonance excitations in $K^{*0}$ and $K^{*+}$ photoproduction on the proton, using a quark model with an effective Lagrangian under the SU(3)-flavor-blind assumption. The predictions are conditional on adopting quark-meson vector and tensor couplings derived from $\omega$ meson photoproduction, with the caveat that no experimental data for $K^*$ photoproduction exist yet to constrain these parameters.

## Method

The authors employed a quark model with an effective Lagrangian for the $K^*$-$qq$ vertex, including both baryon resonance excitations (via $s$- and $u$-channel diagrams) and $t$-channel exchanges. Low-lying resonances within the $n \le 2$ harmonic oscillator shells were included explicitly, while higher-mass resonances with $n>2$ were treated as degenerate by summing over states with the same mass and width. The $t$-channel kaon exchange was introduced with pseudoscalar coupling for the $K\Sigma N$ vertex and the $K^*K\gamma$ coupling determined from $K^*$ radiative decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Quark-meson vector coupling $a = -2.8$ and tensor coupling $b = -5.9$, adopted from $\omega$ meson photoproduction (Zhao, 2001)
- $K^{*0} \to K^0 + \gamma$ partial decay width: 116 keV, giving $g_{K^{*0} K^0\gamma} = 1.134$
- $K^{*+} \to K^+ + \gamma$ partial decay width: 50 keV, giving $g_{K^{*+} K^+\gamma} = 0.744$
- $K\Sigma N$ coupling: $g_{K^+\Sigma^0 p}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 1.2$ (from Bennhold et al., 1999)
- Harmonic oscillator parameter for $t$-channel kaon exchange form factor: $\alpha_k = 290$ MeV
- Quark masses: $m_s = 500$ MeV, $m_u = m_d = 330$ MeV
- Predicted cross sections for $K^*$ photoproduction are smaller by at least an order of magnitude compared to $K$ or $\rho$ and $\omega$ photoproduction

## Context

This work extends the quark model approach previously applied to $\omega$ and $\rho$ meson photoproduction (Zhao, Li, and Bennhold, 1998; Zhao, 2001) to the strangeness sector, specifically $K^*$ vector meson production. It complements studies of kaon photoproduction (Bennhold et al., 1999; Feuster and Mosel, 1999) and builds on predictions for $N^* \to K^* Y$ decays by Capstick and Roberts (1998). The calculations provide baseline predictions for forthcoming exclusive measurements at JLAB and ELSA, and the sensitivity of $K^{*0}$ production to $t$-channel $K^0$ exchange may offer an additional constraint on the $K\Sigma N$ coupling.
