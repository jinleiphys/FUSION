# From superdeformation to extreme deformation and clusterization in the N~Z nuclei of the A~40 mass region

> A systematic search for extremely deformed structures in the N~Z nuclei of the A~40 mass region has been performed for the first time in the framework of covariant density functional theory. At spin zero such structures are located at high excitation energies which prevents their experimental observation. The rotation acts as a tool to bring these exotic shapes to the yrast line or its vicinity so that their observation could become possible with future generation of $γ-$tracking (or similar) detectors such as GRETA and AGATA. The major physical observables of such structures (such as transition quadrupole moments as well as kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia), the underlying single-particle structure and the spins at which they become yrast or near yrast are defined. The search for the fingerprints of clusterization and molecular structures is performed and the configurations with such features are discussed. The best candidates for observation of extremely deformed structures are identified. For several nuclei in this study (such as $^{36}$Ar), the addition of several spin units above currently measured maximum spin of $16\hbar$ will inevitably trigger the transition to hyper- and megadeformed nuclear shapes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that extremely deformed (hyperdeformed and megadeformed) nuclear shapes in N~Z nuclei of the A~40 mass region, which lie at high excitation energies at zero spin and are thus unobservable, can be brought to the yrast line or its vicinity by rotation, potentially enabling their observation with next-generation gamma-ray tracking detectors such as GRETA and AGATA; however, this requires a modest increase in spin beyond the currently measured maximum of 16ℏ in several nuclei such as ³⁶Ar.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic search for extremely deformed structures using covariant density functional theory (CDFT) in the cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) framework with the NL3* functional, neglecting pairing correlations. They calculated configurations for even-even N=Z and N=Z+2 nuclei (S, Ar, Ca, Ti, Cr) and the odd-odd nucleus ⁴²Sc, tracing rotational bands from low spin up to termination where possible, and analyzed single-particle structure, density distributions, transition quadrupole moments, and moments of inertia. Configurations were classified as superdeformed (SD), hyperdeformed (HD), or megadeformed (MD) based on their calculated transition quadrupole moments relative to a normalized reference value derived from the experimental SD band in ⁴⁰Ca.

## Key numbers

- The experimental SD band in ⁴⁰Ca has a measured transition quadrupole moment Q_t^exp = 1.8^{+0.35}_{-0.29} e b (used as reference point).
- The yrast SD configuration [4,4] in ⁴⁰Ca is only yrast at spin I=22ℏ; it is located above the yrast line at lower spin.
- In ⁴⁰Ca, the semi-axis ratios of density distributions are: SD [4,4] at I=12ℏ gives 2.05; HD [41,41] at I=24ℏ gives 2.27; MD [42,42] at I=25ℏ gives 2.90.
- In ³⁶Ar, the currently measured maximum spin is 16ℏ; the paper states that adding several spin units above this will trigger the transition to hyper- and megadeformed shapes.
- In ⁴⁰Ca, the [1,1] configuration has β₂~0.16 and γ~-24° at I=4ℏ and terminates at I=10⁺; the [2,2] configuration has β₂~0.32 and γ~-30° at I=10ℏ and is expected to terminate at I_max=20ℏ.
- In ⁴⁰Ca, the [3,3] configuration terminating at I=18ℏ has β₂~0.47 and γ~21° at I=12ℏ.
- The HD classification requires Q_t exceeding Q_t^norm(Z,A) by approximately 50%; MD classification requires Q_t approximately twice Q_t^norm(Z,A) or higher.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superdeformation in the A~40 mass region, such as the experimental observation of SD bands in ⁴⁰Ca (Ref. Ca40-PRL.01), ³⁶Ar (Ref. 36Ar-SD.PRL.00), and other nuclei, as well as theoretical studies using cranked Hartree-Fock with Skyrme forces (Refs. YM.00, IMYM.02) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Ref. 40Ca-AMD.07). It goes beyond prior work by systematically searching for hyperdeformed and megadeformed structures across multiple nuclei using covariant density functional theory, and by identifying the specific spins at which these extremely deformed configurations become yrast or near-yrast. The results enable future experimental searches with gamma-ray tracking detectors like GRETA and AGATA, and provide predictions for transition quadrupole moments and moments of inertia that can be compared with future data.
