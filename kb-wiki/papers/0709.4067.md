# Resolution of the Proton Spin Problem

> A number of lines of investigation into the structure of the nucleon have converged to the point where we believe that one has a consistent explanation of the well known proton spin crisis.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the proton spin crisis is resolved not by large gluon polarization, but by three well-known non-perturbative QCD effects that convert quark spin into orbital angular momentum: relativistic valence quark motion, one-gluon-exchange hyperfine interactions, and the pion cloud. The authors hedge that this explanation works only if one accepts that pion cloud and gluon exchange corrections can be combined without double counting, a concern resolved by recent lattice QCD analyses showing pion effects contribute 50 MeV or less of the 300 MeV nucleon-Delta mass difference.

## Method

The authors combine three established corrections to the naive quark model prediction for the fraction of proton spin carried by quarks: (1) relativistic motion of valence quarks in a confining potential, which reduces the spin content by a factor of about 0.65; (2) one-gluon-exchange hyperfine interactions that excite p-wave anti-quark intermediate states, reducing the spin by about 0.15; and (3) the pion cloud of the nucleon, treated in the Cloudy Bag Model (CBM), which gives a multiplicative reduction factor of about 0.70-0.80. These corrections are applied sequentially to the naive quark model value of approximately 0.65 to obtain a final quark spin fraction consistent with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The modern experimental value of the quark spin sum is Σ_inv = 0.33 ± 0.03 (stat.) ± 0.05 (syst.)
- The relativistic reduction factor for valence quarks in the bag model is B = Ω/3(Ω-1) ≈ 0.65, which reduces g_A from 5/3 to about 1.09
- The one-gluon-exchange correction reduces the quark spin fraction by 0.15, i.e., Σ → Σ - 3G
- The pion cloud correction yields a multiplicative factor between 0.70 and 0.80 for the range of probabilities P_Nπ = 0.20-0.25 and P_Δπ = 0.05-0.10
- Combining all three corrections gives Σ between 0.35 (P_Nπ = 0.25, P_Δπ = 0.05) and 0.40 (P_Nπ = 0.20, P_Δπ = 0.10)
- The most recent RHIC measurements of inclusive π0 jets are best fit with ΔG = 0, and Bianchi reported ΔG/G ≈ 0.08 at Pacific-SPIN07

## Context

This work extends the original European Muon Collaboration (EMC) discovery (Ashman 1987) that the quark spin fraction was near zero (14 ± 9 ± 21%), and contradicts the subsequent hypothesis that large gluon polarization (Efremov 1988, Altarelli 1988, Bodwin 1989, Bass 1991) could explain the deficit. The paper builds on earlier non-perturbative suggestions by Myhrer and Thomas (1988), Schreiber (1988), and Hogaasen (1988) that orbital angular momentum from valence quarks, p-wave anti-quarks excited by one-gluon exchange, and the pion cloud could account for the missing spin. The analysis is enabled by lattice QCD results from Young (2002) showing that pion effects contribute at most 50 MeV to the nucleon-Delta mass difference, allowing the combination of pion cloud and gluon exchange corrections without double counting. This resolution motivates the experimental program for measuring generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at the 12 GeV Upgrade at Jefferson Lab.
