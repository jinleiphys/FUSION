# Universality in bosonic dimer-dimer scattering

> Bosonic dimer-dimer scattering is studied near the unitary limit using momentum-space equations for the four-particle transition operators. The impact of the Efimov effect on the dimer-dimer scattering observables is explored and a number of universal relations is established with high accuracy. The rate for the creation of Efimov trimers via dimer-dimer collisions is calculated.

## Key claim

Bosonic dimer-dimer scattering near the unitary limit exhibits universal, log-periodic behavior that is independent of short-range interaction details, but the universal relations are only achieved with high accuracy when considering sufficiently large atom-atom scattering lengths (aΛ/2 ranging from 10³ to 10⁶) and sufficiently high Efimov trimer index n (n ≥ 3), with residual dependence on the potential form factor dropping below 0.015% for n = 4.

## Method

The author solved the Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for the four-particle transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave framework, using a rank-1 separable two-boson potential acting only in the S wave, with two different choices of the potential form factor (c₂ = 0 and c₂ = -9.17) to verify independence from short-range details. Higher angular momentum states with l_y, l_z ≤ 3 were included for the relative motion in 1+2, 1+3, and 2+2 subsystems, and the calculations were performed for atom-atom scattering lengths aΛ/2 ranging from 10³ to 10⁶ to achieve the universal limit.

## Key numbers

- a_{n,1}^{dd} / a_{n}^{dd} = 0.3235(1) (ratio of tetramer-dimer-threshold intersection to dimer-dimer/atom-trimer threshold intersection)
- a_{n,2}^{dd} / a_{n}^{dd} = 0.99947(2) (ratio for the shallow tetramer)
- a_{n}^{dd} / a_{n}^{d} = 6.789(1) (ratio of dimer-dimer/atom-trimer threshold intersection to atom-dimer threshold intersection)
- The Efimov factor e^{π/s₀} ≈ 22.694, giving the log-periodic scaling of A_{dd}/a and r_{dd}/a as functions of a
- The semi-analytical result lim_{n→∞} a_{n}^{d} √(m b_n^u) ≈ 0.0707645 from Braaten (2006) was reproduced with better than 0.015% accuracy at n = 4

## Context

This work extends the AGS-based approach previously applied to bosonic atom-trimer scattering by Deltuva (2010, 2011) to the dimer-dimer scattering problem, providing significantly higher accuracy than the earlier adiabatic hyperspherical calculations by D'Incao et al. (2009), which gave ratios differing by 1 to 9% from the present results. The universal relations established here enable precise predictions for the creation of Efimov trimers via dimer-dimer collisions, and the calculated trimer creation rate β_{dd} shows quantitative differences from D'Incao et al. (2009), particularly in its temperature dependence, partly due to the inclusion of D-wave contributions. The results are directly applicable to future experiments in the universal regime where finite-range corrections become negligible, unlike the existing experimental data from Ferlaino et al. (2008) which operate at smaller a values where such corrections are non-negligible.
