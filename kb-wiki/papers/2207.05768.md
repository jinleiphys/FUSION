# Generalized parton distributions through universal moment parameterization: zero skewness case

> We present a global analysis program for the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) based on conformal moment expansion. We apply the strategy of universal moment parameterization to fit both the collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs) from phenomenology and generalized form factors from lattice calculations, and show that the parameterization is flexible enough to accommodate these constraints. In addition, we can also fit direct lattice calculations of GPDs from large-momentum effective theory. In this work we focus on the analysis of $t$-dependent PDFs which correspond to GPDs in the $ξ\to 0$ limit. The strategy also applies to the $ξ\not =0$ region with extra parameters, and therefore can be fitted to experimental observables in the future. With a demonstrative example of fitted GPDs, we exhibit the quark transverse angular momentum densities of the proton as well as the impact parameter space distributions of quarks in both unpolarized and transversely polarized protons.

## Key claim

The authors present a global analysis program for generalized parton distributions (GPDs) based on conformal moment expansion, but the work is limited to the zero-skewness case (\(\xi \to 0\)) and serves as a demonstrative example rather than a complete phenomenological extraction; the strategy is claimed to be extendable to non-zero skewness with extra parameters for future fits to experimental observables.

## Method

The authors parameterize GPDs through their conformal moments, which are expanded in powers of the skewness parameter \(\xi\) with a cut-off \(k_{\text{cut}}\) for the small-\(\xi\) region. The leading moments at zero momentum transfer are fixed by forward parton distribution functions (PDFs) from global fits using a standard ansatz \(f(x) = \sum_i N_i x^{-\alpha_i} (1-x)^{\beta_i}\), while the \(t\)-dependence of the generalized form factors \(\mathcal{F}_{j,k}(t)\) is modeled via Regge trajectory extrapolation. The GPDs are reconstructed from the moments using the Mellin-Barnes integral resummation technique, and the parameterization is fitted to both collinear PDF phenomenology and lattice QCD calculations of generalized form factors and direct GPDs from large-momentum effective theory (LaMET).

## Key numbers

- The minimum momentum transfer for a given skewness is \(|t|_{\text{min}} = 4 \xi^2 M^2 / (1-\xi^2)\) with nucleon mass \(M = 0.938\) GeV.
- The PDF ansatz uses Mellin moments of the form \(\mathcal{F}_{j,0}(t=0) = \sum_i N_i B(j+1-\alpha_i, 1+\beta_i)\), where \(B\) is the Euler beta function.
- The conformal moment expansion for quarks uses Gegenbauer polynomials \(C_j^{(3/2)}(x/\xi)\) with weight function \(\rho_C(x) = 1-x^2\).
- The Mellin-Barnes integral reconstruction is demonstrated with a reference PDF \(f(x) = 18.6 x^{-0.2} (1-x)^{3.5}\), showing that the method reproduces the PDF when \(x \gg \xi\) with \(\xi\) serving as a regulator.

## Context

This work extends the Kumerički-Müller (KM) model (Mueller 2005, Kumericki 2009) by generalizing the moment expansion to include both valence and sea quark distributions, whereas the KM model parameterized only sea distributions for DVCS phenomenology. The universal moment parameterization (GUMP) program aims to combine constraints from PDF global fits (e.g., CTEQ, Hou 2019), lattice QCD calculations of generalized form factors (LHPC 2007, Hagler 2009, Shanahan 2018), and direct LaMET calculations of GPDs (Alexandrou 2020, Constantinou 2020, Lin 2020, Lin 2021) to solve the inverse problem in GPD reconstruction. The approach enables future extraction of 3D nucleon structure, including quark transverse angular momentum densities and impact parameter space distributions.
