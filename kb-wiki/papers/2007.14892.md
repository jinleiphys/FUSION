# Simple relativistic quark models

> A class of phenomenological relativistic models of hadronic systems motivated by QCD that have dual representations as models of mesons and nucleons or quarks and gluons is investigated. These models are designed to provided qualitative insight into the role of sea quarks in hadronic structure and reactions. The model assumption is that the Hamiltonian can be divided into two parts; one that involves degrees of freedom in the same connected local and global color singlet and the remaining interactions that allow the connected local and global color singlets to interact. The first class of interactions results in infinite towers of bare "particles" with hadronic quantum numbers. All but a finite number of these remain stable when the second class of interactions is included. The model interactions are expressed in terms of sub-hadronic degrees of freedom, which determine the bare hadronic spectrum and the interactions involving the bare hadrons in terms of a small number of sub-hadronic model parameters. As a first test, this paper considers the simplest case of mesons that interact via a string-breaking interaction. One virtue of this model is that all of the bare meson masses and eigenfunctions can be computed analytically. In addition, the string breaking interaction leads to production vertices that can also be computed analytically. The relativistic wave functions have a light-front kinematic symmetry. The goal is to find a simple relativistic quantum mechanical model based on sub-hadronic degrees of freedom that can provide an efficient, qualitatively consistent description of hadronic masses, lifetimes, cross sections, sea quark effects, and electromagnetic properties. The simplicity of the model makes it a potentially useful tool to study the impact of sea quarks on hadronic structure and reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a class of phenomenological relativistic quark models where the Hamiltonian is split into two parts: one describing confined connected color singlets (bare hadrons) and another allowing these singlets to interact via string-breaking vertices; all production vertices can be computed analytically, making the model a potentially useful tool for studying sea quark effects, though the authors explicitly state this initial investigation does not include flavor fine-structure interactions needed for realistic applications.

## Method

The authors construct a Poincaré-invariant model using light-front dynamics, where bare meson states are treated as confined quark-anti-quark pairs interacting via a confining potential, and the string-breaking interaction couples one bare meson to two bare mesons. All bare meson masses, eigenfunctions, and string-breaking vertex matrix elements are computed analytically using light-front irreducible representations of the Poincaré group and Melosh rotations to relate light-front and canonical spins. The model parameters are constituent quark masses and a single coupling constant that determines both the confining interaction and the string-breaking vertices.

## Key numbers

- The bare meson masses and eigenfunctions are computed analytically for the confining interaction, with explicit formulas given for the wave functions in terms of light-front momentum fractions ξ = p_q⁺/P⁺ and transverse momentum k_⟂
- The string-breaking vertex matrix elements are computed analytically for all bare meson states, with the derivation provided in Appendices I and II
- The light-front dispersion relation is p⁻ = (m² + p_⟂²)/p⁺, which defines the on-shell condition for all particles in the model
- The rest four-vector has light-front components p⁺ = p⁻ = m for a particle of mass m at rest

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of quark models motivated by QCD, including the flux tube model and constituent quark models by Micu (1969), the Yaouanc group (1970s), Isgur (1983), and many others cited in the paper. The novel contribution is that all meson vertices can be computed analytically, enabling efficient investigation of sea quark effects and high-lying states, with explicit formulas for wave functions that facilitate calculations. The model is designed to be fully relativistic with light-front kinematic symmetry, making it applicable to light quarks and large momentum transfers, and it provides a dual description in terms of both QCD and hadronic degrees of freedom that can be constrained by both lattice QCD and hadronic phenomenology.
