# Probing Multi-Strange Dibaryon with Proton-Omega Correlation in High-energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> Two-particle intensity correlation between the proton ($p$) and the Omega-baryon ($Ω$) in high-energy heavy ion collisions is studied to unravel the possible spin-2 $pΩ$ dibaryon recently suggested by lattice QCD simulations. The ratio of correlation functions between small and large collision systems, $C_{\rm SL}(Q)$, is proposed to be a new measure to extract the strong $pΩ$ interaction without much contamination from the Coulomb attraction. Relevance of this quantity to the experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions is also discussed.

# Probing Multi-Strange Dibaryon with Proton-Omega Correlation in High-energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The ratio of the proton-Omega (pΩ) correlation function between small and large collision systems, C_SL(Q), is proposed as a new measure to extract the strong pΩ interaction without much contamination from the Coulomb attraction, provided that the spin-2 pΩ dibaryon suggested by lattice QCD simulations exists. The authors note that the quantitative answer on the dibaryon will be obtained only by on-going physical point simulations.

## Method

The authors model the NΩ interaction using three potentials (V_I, V_II, V_III) derived from lattice QCD data at heavy quark masses (m_π=875 MeV, m_K=916 MeV), fitted to an attractive Gaussian core plus an attractive (Yukawa)^2 tail. They solve the Schrödinger equation for the pΩ system in the spin-2 (^5S_2) channel with elastic scattering and the spin-1 (^3S_1) channel with complete absorption into octet-octet channels, including the Coulomb interaction. The correlation function C(Q) is computed using a static spherical source model and an expanding source model with 1-dimensional Bjorken expansion, and the ratio C_SL(Q) between source sizes of 2.5 fm and 5 fm is evaluated.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD simulations used heavy quark masses with m_π = 875 MeV and m_K = 916 MeV (Etminan 2014)
- Binding energies for spin-2 pΩ potentials without Coulomb: V_I no bound state, V_II = 0.05 MeV, V_III = 24.8 MeV
- Binding energies with Coulomb: V_I no bound state, V_II = 6.3 MeV, V_III = 26.9 MeV
- Scattering lengths without Coulomb: V_I = -1.0 fm, V_II = 23.1 fm, V_III = 1.60 fm
- Scattering lengths with Coulomb: V_I = -1.12 fm, V_II = 5.79 fm, V_III = 1.29 fm
- Effective ranges without Coulomb: V_I = 1.15 fm, V_II = 0.95 fm, V_III = 0.65 fm
- Bohr radius of pΩ system: a_B = 45 fm (reduced mass μ = 601 MeV)
- Source sizes considered: R_p = R_Ω = 2.5 fm (small system) and 5 fm (large system)
- For expanding source: τ_p = 3 fm, τ_Ω = 2 fm (peripheral); τ_p = 20 fm, τ_Ω = 10 fm (central)
- Temperature: T_p,Ω = 164 MeV for peripheral; T_p = 120 MeV, T_Ω = 164 MeV for central

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the spin-2 NΩ interaction (Etminan 2014) and the spin-0 H-dibaryon (Jaffe 1977, Inoue 2010, Beane 2010) to experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the formalism of two-particle momentum correlations for non-identical hadrons (Lednicky 1982, Bauer 1993) and previous analyses of ΛΛ correlations (Morita 2014). The proposed C_SL(Q) ratio enables experimental searches at RHIC and LHC for the S=-3 dibaryon, complementing existing H-dibaryon searches (Adamczyk 2015, Adam 2016).
