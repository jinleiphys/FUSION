# Low-Energy Theorems for QCD at Finite Temperature and Chemical Potential

## Key claim
The low-energy theorems originally derived by Novikov et al. for QCD at zero temperature and density are shown to hold at finite temperature and chemical potential, provided that the operators \(T\partial/\partial T\) and \(\mu\partial/\partial \mu\) are appropriately included in the relations; the generalization includes non-zero quark masses and the quark sector, extending prior work that covered only the pure glue sector at finite temperature.

## Method
The authors start from the QCD partition function in the imaginary time formalism and derive the grand potential \(\Omega\). Using dimensional analysis and renormalization group invariance, they express \(\Omega/V\) as a function of the scale \(\Lambda\), temperature \(T\), chemical potential \(\mu\), and (for massive quarks) the renormalization-group-invariant mass \(\hat{m}\). By taking derivatives with respect to \(1/g_0^2\) and applying the renormalization group, they obtain relations connecting the trace of the energy-momentum tensor \(\theta^\mu_\mu = \mathcal{E} - 3P\) to derivatives of \(\Omega/V\) with respect to \(T\) and \(\mu\). They then iterate the procedure to produce connected \(n\)-point correlation functions of \(\theta^\mu_\mu\) and generalize to arbitrary renormalization-group-invariant operators. A perturbative example is worked out for \(SU(N)\) gauge theory at high \(T\) or \(\mu\) using the two-loop pressure.

## Key numbers
- For \(SU(N)\) gauge theory with one quark flavor, the two-loop perturbative pressure is given by:  
  \(P = \frac{\pi^2}{45}(N^2-1)T^4 + N\left( \frac{7\pi^2 T^4}{180} + \frac{\mu^2 T^2}{6} + \frac{\mu^4}{12\pi^2} \right) - \frac{\pi (N^2-1)}{144}\alpha_s(\xi)\left( (4N+5)T^4 + \frac{18}{\pi^2}\mu^2 T^2 + \frac{9}{\pi^4}\mu^4 \right)\).
- The perturbative result for the \(n\)-th derivative relation is:  
  \(\left( T\frac{\partial}{\partial T} + \mu\frac{\partial}{\partial \mu} - 4 \right)^n \langle \theta^\mu_\mu \rangle = \frac{b}{288}(N^2-1) \alpha_s^{n+2}(\xi) \left( \frac{-b}{2\pi} \right)^n (n+1)! \left( (4N+5)T^4 + \frac{18}{\pi^2}\mu^2 T^2 + \frac{9}{\pi^4}\mu^4 \right)\), where \(b = (11N-2N_f)/3\).
- For \(N=3\) and one flavor in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme, the next-order \(\beta_s\)-function correction introduces a factor of order \((1+1.4\alpha_s n)\) to the above expression.

## Context
This work extends the low-energy theorems of Novikov et al. (SVZ+N) from zero temperature and density to finite temperature and chemical potential, and generalizes the finite-temperature pure-glue results of Kapusta et al. (KET) by including the quark sector and non-zero quark masses. The derived relations provide constraints on effective theories and lattice gauge calculations at finite \(T\) and \(\mu\), and the perturbative example gives a benchmark for model calculations of the connected correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor.
