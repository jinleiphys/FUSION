# Symmetry Tests within the Standard Model and Beyond from Nuclear Muon Capture

> Precision measurements in nuclear muon capture on the proton and $^3$He allow for tests of the Standard Model for the strong and electroweak interactions, complementary to those achieved in high energy experiments. The present situation and future prospects are reviewed, emphasizing where renewed efforts could prove to be rewarding in exploring ever further beyond the confines of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

Precision measurements in nuclear muon capture on the proton and ³He provide tests of the Standard Model that are complementary to high-energy experiments, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the most precise tests are currently limited by theoretical uncertainties in the nuclear axial form factor \(F_A(q^2_1)\) and that a 4.2\(\sigma\) discrepancy exists between the theoretical prediction for the pseudoscalar form factor \(g_P\) and a recent radiative muon capture measurement.

## Method

The paper reviews the formalism of muon capture on the proton and ³He using the elementary particle model (EPM) approach, where hadronic matrix elements are parametrized by form factors including vector (\(g_V\), \(g_M\)), axial (\(g_A\), \(g_P\)), and second-class (\(g_S\), \(g_T\)) currents. It analyzes the sensitivity of the statistical capture rate on ³He and the singlet capture rate on hydrogen to these form factors and to beyond-Standard-Model effective couplings, using existing experimental data and projected 1% precision measurements to derive constraints.

## Key numbers

- Statistical capture rate on ³He to the triton channel: \(\lambda_{\rm stat}^{\rm exp} = 1496 \pm 4\) s\(^{-1}\) (0.3% precision), compared to theoretical prediction \(\lambda_{\rm stat}^{\rm theor} = 1497 \pm 12\) s\(^{-1}\)
- Theoretical prediction for the pseudoscalar form factor on the proton: \(g_P(q^2_0) = 8.44 \pm 0.23\) (2.7% precision), with \(g_P(q^2_0)/g_A(0) = 6.70 \pm 0.18\)
- Existing experimental value from ordinary muon capture on the proton: \(g_P(q^2_0)/g_A(0) = 6.9 \pm 1.5\) (22% precision)
- Radiative muon capture measurement: \(g_P(q^2_0)/g_A(0) = 9.8 \pm 0.8\) (8% precision), disagreeing with theory by 4.2\(\sigma\)
- Preliminary triton vector analysing power: \(A_v^{\rm exp} = 0.63 \pm 0.09^{+0.11}_{-0.14}\), compared to theoretical prediction \(A_v^{\rm theor} = 0.524 \pm 0.006\)
- Nuclear PCAC test from ³He capture: \(F_P/F_P^{\rm PCAC} = 1.004 \pm [0.076-0.132][{\rm exp}:0.023]\)
- From a 1% precise measurement of the singlet capture rate on hydrogen, projected determination of \(g_P = 8.44 \pm [0.50][{\rm exp}:0.46]\) (5.9% precision)
- \(e-\mu\) universality test from ³He: \(|h^V_{LL}/f^V_{LL}| = 0.9998 \pm [0.0042-0.0071][{\rm exp}:0.0013]\)
- Compositeness scale limits from ³He capture: \(\Lambda^V_{-+} > [4.9-3.8][{\rm exp}:8.4]\) TeV (90% C.L.) and \(\Lambda^T_{+-} > [9.3-7.2][{\rm exp}:15.9]\) TeV (90% C.L.)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on precision muon capture tests initiated by the ³He capture rate measurement of Ackerbauer et al. (1998) and the radiative muon capture measurement of Jonkmans et al. (1996), which produced a 4.2\(\sigma\) conflict with the chiral perturbation theory prediction for \(g_P\) from Bernard et al. (1998). The paper shows that a forthcoming 1% measurement of the singlet capture rate on hydrogen, as proposed by Andreev et al. and by the H collaboration, would resolve this discrepancy and provide competitive constraints on left-right symmetric models, contact interactions, and leptoquark couplings that are complementary to those from high-energy experiments.
