# Fixed points of the SRG evolution and the on-shell limit of the nuclear force

> We study the infrared limit of the similarity renormalization group (SRG) using a simple toy model for the nuclear force aiming to investigate the fixed points of the SRG evolution with both the Wilson and the Wegner generators. We show how a fully diagonal interaction at the similarity cutoff $λ\rightarrow 0$ may be obtained from the eigenvalues of the hamiltonian and quantify the diagonalness by means of operator norms. While the fixed points for both generators are equivalent when no bound-states are allowed by the interaction, the differences arising from the presence of the Deuteron bound-state can be disentangled very clearly by analyzing the evolved interactions in the infrared limit $λ\to 0$ on a finite momentum grid. Another issue we investigate is the location on the diagonal of the hamiltonian in momentum-space where the SRG evolution places the Deuteron bound-state eigenvalue once it reaches the fixed point. This finite momentum grid setup provides an alternative derivation of the celebrated trace identities, as a by product. The different effects due to either the Wilson or the Wegner generators on the binding energies of $A=2,3,4$ systems are investigated and related to the ocurrence of a Tjon-line which emerges as the minimum of an avoided crossing between $E_α= 4 E_t - 3 E_d$ and $E_α= 2 E_t $. All infrared features of the flow equations are illustrated using the toy model for the two-nucleon $S$-waves.

## Key claim

The SRG evolution with both Wilson and Wegner generators drives the Hamiltonian to a diagonal form in the infrared limit λ→0, but the specific ordering of eigenvalues at the fixed point depends on the generator choice and differs when bound states are present; the Wegner generator's ordering uniquely satisfies Levinson's theorem in the energy-shift approach, while the Wilson generator produces a different ordering that does not.

## Method

The authors solved the SRG flow equations numerically on a finite momentum grid for a toy model of the two-nucleon S-wave interaction, using both the Wilson generator (Gs=T) and the Wegner generator (Gs=H^D_s). They analyzed the infrared fixed points by linearizing the discrete flow equations near the diagonal limit and studied the eigenvalue ordering induced by each generator. The binding energies of A=2,3,4 nuclear systems were computed as functions of the SRG cutoff to examine the Tjon-line correlation.

## Key numbers

- The SRG cutoff λ is related to the flow parameter s by s=λ^{-4}, with λ having dimensions of momentum.
- The off-diagonal matrix elements decay as e^{-s(ε_n-ε_m)(E_n-E_m)} near the fixed point for the Wilson generator.
- The Tjon-line emerges from an avoided crossing between E_α = 4E_t - 3E_d and E_α = 2E_t, where E_d, E_t, and E_α are binding energies of the deuteron, triton, and alpha particle respectively.
- The errors in reaching the infrared fixed point scale as O[e^{-min(s ε_n^2)}].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on the infrared limit of SRG evolution (Arriola 2014) where a connection to Levinson's theorem was established, and their earlier prediction of the Tjon-line (Arriola 2013). It contrasts with the approach of Kukulin and collaborators (2009), who used finite momentum grids for few-body problems but made an erroneous implementation of state ordering. The results enable a clearer understanding of how SRG generators produce inequivalent effective interactions in the on-shell limit, with implications for nuclear many-body calculations using softened potentials.
