# Nuclear incompressibility: An analytical study on leptodermous expansion

> A comparative study of the liquid-drop model (LDM) type expansions of energy $E$ and compression modulus $K_A$ is made within the energy density formalism using Skyrme interactions. As compared to the energy expansion, it is found that, in the pure bulk mode of density vibration, the LDM expansion of $K_A$ shows an anomalous convergence behaviour due to {\it pair \ effect}. A least squares fit analysis is done to estimate the minimum error, one would expect even with synthetic data due to the inherent nature of the LDM expansion of $K_A$ as well as the narrow range of accessible mass number $A$, in the values of the various coefficients. The dependence of the higher-order coefficients like curvature and Gauss curvature on the coupling $β_c$ between the bulk and surface parts of the monopole vibrations is analytically studied. It is shown that the $K_A -$ expansion including the dynamical effect ( $A-$ dependence of $β_c$ ) shows an `up-turn' behaviour below mass number about 120, suggesting the inapplicability of the LDM expansion of $K_A$ over this mass region.

## Key claim

The liquid-drop model (LDM) expansion of the finite nuclear compression modulus $K_A$ shows an anomalous convergence behavior due to a "pair effect" where higher-order terms cancel pairwise, and including the dynamical $A$-dependence of the coupling parameter $\beta_c$ produces an "up-turn" in $K_A$ below mass number about 120, suggesting the LDM expansion of $K_A$ is inapplicable in that mass region.

## Method

The authors derived analytical LDM expansions for both energy $E/A$ and compression modulus $K_A$ within the energy density formalism using Skyrme interactions (SkM$^*$, SkA, and S3), with the density parametrized as a Fermi function. They calculated the exact numerical values of $E/A$ and $K_A$ for symmetric and asymmetric nuclei including Coulomb effects, then performed least-squares fits to these "synthetic data" to extract the LDM coefficients and study their convergence and reliability.

## Key numbers

- For the SkA force, the volume compressibility coefficient $K_v$ extracted from a 4-parameter fit including Coulomb and asymmetry effects showed a discrepancy of about 4% compared to the exact value.
- The surface compressibility coefficient $K_s$ from the same fit showed a discrepancy of about 12% compared to the exact value.
- The ratio $|K_s/K_v|$ decreased as $\beta_c$ decreased from 0 to -1/2, with the scaling model result $K_s \simeq -K_v$ well satisfied for $\beta_c = -1/3$.
- For nuclei below about $A=150$, the Gauss curvature term $K_o A^{-1}$ overshoots the curvature term $K_c A^{-2/3}$ in magnitude.
- The LDM expansion of $K_A$ shows an "up-turn" behavior below mass number about 120 when dynamical effects (the $A$-dependence of $\beta_c$) are included.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear incompressibility using the LDM approach, particularly following the analyses of Sharma et al. (1988, 1989) who determined $K_v$ to be about 300 MeV, and Shlomo and Youngblood (1993) who found that the complete data set on breathing modes (about 46 nuclei, $28 \le A \le 232$) could not determine $K_\infty$ to better than a factor of about 1.7 (200-350 MeV). The study also builds on the hydrodynamical work of Brack and Stocker (1983) showing that the coupling parameter $\beta_c$ between bulk and surface parts of density vibrations depends sensitively on mass number $A$, with values ranging from -0.23 for $^{208}$Pb to -0.84 for $^{90}$Zr and extreme values for lighter nuclei like $^{48}$Ca ($\beta_c = -24$) and $^{40}$Ca ($\beta_c = +9.5$). The results enable a better understanding of the limitations of LDM expansions for extracting nuclear matter incompressibility from finite-nucleus data.
