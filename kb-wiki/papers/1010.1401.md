# Is Upsilon(10580) really Upsilon(4S)?

> We analyse electron-positron data for b-bbar production published by the BABAR Collaboration, in the invariant-mass interval delimited by the B-Bbar and Lambda(b)-Lambda(b) thresholds. In particular, we describe the Upsilon(10580) enhancement, not as a b-bbar resonance, but rather as a threshold phenomenon due to the opening of the B-Bbar decay channel and enhanced by the Upsilon(2D) bound-state pole not far below this threshold. The same data provide evidence for the true Upsilon(4S) resonance, which we find at 10.735 GeV with a width of 38 MeV. At higher energies, two more known Upsilon resonances are observed by us in the data and classified. The vital role played in our analysis by the universal confinement frequency omega is again confirmed, via a comparison with the charmonium spectrum.

## Key claim

The Upsilon(10580) enhancement is not the Upsilon(4S) b-bbar resonance, but rather a threshold phenomenon caused by the opening of the B-Bbar decay channel, enhanced by the nearby Upsilon(2D) bound-state pole; the true Upsilon(4S) resonance is instead found at 10.735 GeV with a width of 38 MeV.

## Method

The authors analyzed electron-positron annihilation data for b-bbar production published by the BABAR Collaboration, covering the invariant-mass interval between the B-Bbar and Lambda_b-Lambda_b thresholds. They employed the harmonic-oscillator approximation of the Resonance-Spectrum Expansion (HORSE) to describe the data, separating contributions into a non-interfering background, nonresonant threshold enhancements, and three Breit-Wigner resonances (Upsilon(4S), Upsilon(3D), and Upsilon(5S)) that interfere with the threshold enhancements but not with the background.

## Key numbers

- True Upsilon(4S) resonance found at 10.735 GeV with a width of 38 MeV
- Upsilon(2D) bound-state pole located about 60 MeV below the B-Bbar threshold (10.558 GeV)
- Universal confinement frequency omega = 190 MeV used in the analysis, confirmed via comparison with the charmonium spectrum
- Quenched b-bbar confinement levels: degenerate 3S-2D pair at 10.493 GeV, degenerate 4S-3D pair at 10.873 GeV
- Upsilon(2D) central mass at 10.492 GeV, just 1 MeV below the b-bbar confinement level

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (van Beveren and Rupp, 2009) where they argued that the enhancement above B-Bbar threshold is better described by a wave function with dominant B-Bbar component rather than a b-bbar pole. It contradicts the standard assignment of Upsilon(10580) as Upsilon(4S) that was supported by the Godfrey-Isgur quark model (1985), which predicted Upsilon(4S) at 10.63 GeV. The analysis also addresses discrepancies between BABAR results for Upsilon(10860) and Upsilon(11020) and earlier measurements by CUSB (1985) and CLEO (1985), and enables a unified description of bottomonium and charmonium spectra using the same universal confinement frequency.
