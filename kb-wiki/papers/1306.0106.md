# Collision Energy Evolution of Elliptic and Triangular Flow in a Hybrid Model

> While the existence of a strongly interacting state of matter, known as 'quark-gluon plasma' (QGP), has been established in heavy ion collision experiments in the past decade, the task remains to map out the transition from the hadronic matter to the QGP. This is done by measuring the dependence of key observables (such as particle suppression and elliptic flow) on the collision energy of the heavy ions. This procedure, known as 'beam energy scan', has been most recently performed at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). Utilizing a Boltzmann+hydrodynamics hybrid model, we study the collision energy dependence of initial state eccentricities and the final state elliptic and triangular flow. This approach is well suited to investigate the relative importance of hydrodynamics and hadron transport at different collision energies.

## Key claim
Using a Boltzmann+hydrodynamics hybrid model, the authors show that the experimentally observed near-constancy of elliptic flow across collision energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV can be reproduced because hadron/string pre-equilibrium dynamics compensate for the shortened hydrodynamic phase at lower energies; however, this compensation does not hold for triangular flow, which drops to near zero below 10 GeV in the model, in qualitative disagreement with preliminary STAR data.

## Method
The study uses a (3+1)-D ideal hydrodynamic model coupled to the UrQMD hadronic cascade. Initial conditions are generated by UrQMD, with energy, momentum, and baryon number densities mapped onto the hydro grid at a starting time t_start = max{2R/sqrt(gamma_CM^2-1), 0.5 fm}. The hydrodynamic evolution uses the SHASTA algorithm with a chiral Polyakov-loop equation of state, and particlization occurs when the energy density falls below 2 epsilon_0 (epsilon_0 = 146 MeV/fm^3), corresponding to a switching temperature of about 154 MeV at top RHIC energy. After particlization via the Cooper-Frye formula, final-state hadronic rescatterings and decays are handled by UrQMD.

## Key numbers
- The switching energy density for particlization is 2 epsilon_0, where epsilon_0 = 146 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to T approx 154 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.
- In midcentral collisions (b = 8.2-9.4 fm), the contribution of hadronic rescatterings to v_2 is about 10%.
- In most central collisions, integrated v_3 increases from 0.005 to above 0.01 with increasing collision energy.
- In midcentral collisions (b = 6.7-8.2 fm), integrated v_3 rises from approx 0 at sqrt(s_NN) < 10 GeV to approx 0.015-0.02 for sqrt(s_NN) >= 19.6 GeV.
- The starting time t_start drops from 3.22 fm at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV to 1.23 fm at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends hybrid model studies of collective flow (Petersen 2008) to a beam energy scan from 7.7 to 200 GeV, addressing the experimental observation by STAR (Adamczyk 2012) that inclusive charged hadron v_2 shows little dependence on collision energy. The results enable future refinement of model parameters (particlization energy density, viscous effects) to improve agreement with kaon spectra and differential v_2(p_T), and highlight a need to understand why preliminary STAR data show non-zero v_3 at low energies where the hybrid model predicts near-zero triangular flow.
