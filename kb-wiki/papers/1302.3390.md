# Elastic and quasi-elastic electron scattering off nuclei with neutron excess

> We present theoretical predictions for electron scattering on oxygen and calcium isotopic chains. The calculations are done within the framework of the distorted-wave Born approximation and the proton and neutron density distributions are evaluated adopting a relativistic Dirac-Hartree model. We present results for the elastic and quasi-elastic cross sections and for the parity-violating asymmetry. As a first step, the results of the models are tested in comparison with some of the data available for elastic and quasi-elastic scattering on 16O and 40Ca nuclei. Then, the evolution of some nuclear properties is investigated as a function of the neutron number. We also present a comparison with the parity-violating asymmetry parameter obtained by the PREX Collaboration on 208Pb and give a prediction for the future experiment CREX on 48Ca.

## Key claim

The authors present theoretical predictions for elastic and quasi-elastic electron scattering on oxygen and calcium isotopic chains, showing that the evolution of nuclear properties with neutron number can be studied within the distorted-wave Born approximation using relativistic Dirac-Hartree densities, but the results are tested only against available data for the stable nuclei 16O and 40Ca, not for the exotic isotopes.

## Method

The calculations employ the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) for elastic scattering and the relativistic Green's function model for inclusive quasi-elastic scattering, with proton and neutron density distributions obtained from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using the DD-ME2 parameterization. For open-shell nuclei, the constant gap approximation with empirical pairing gaps from the 5-point formula is used. The parity-violating asymmetry is computed from the interference of photon and Z0 exchange in elastic and quasi-elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- PREX collaboration measured A_pv = 0.656 ± 0.060(stat) ± 0.014(syst) ppm for 208Pb, corresponding to R_n - R_p = 0.33 +0.16/-0.18 fm
- The Fermi constant used is G_F ≈ 1.16639 × 10^-11 MeV^-2
- The Weinberg angle is sin^2Θ_W ≈ 0.23
- The electric form factor of the proton uses Λ = 4.27 fm^-1
- Nuclear matter saturation density is ρ_sat = 0.152 fm^-3
- Bare meson masses used: m_ω = 783 MeV and m_ρ = 763 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical treatments of electron scattering off exotic nuclei (Garrido 1999, Ershov 2005, Khan 2007, Roca-Maza 2008, Giusti 2011) by providing systematic predictions along oxygen and calcium isotopic chains. The calculations are compared with the PREX measurement on 208Pb (Abrahamyan 2012) and provide predictions for the future CREX experiment on 48Ca, enabling tests of the dependence of neutron skin on atomic mass number.
