# Longitudinal spin polarization in a thermal model

> We use a thermal model with single freeze-out to determine longitudinal polarization of $Λ$ hyperons emitted from a hot and rotating hadronic medium. We consider the top RHIC energies and use the model parameters determined in the previous analyses of particle spectra and elliptic flow. Using a direct connection between the spin polarization tensor and thermal vorticity, we reproduce earlier results which indicate a quadrupole structure of the longitudinal component of the polarization three-vector with an opposite sign compared to that found in the experiment. We further use only the spatial components of the thermal vorticity in the laboratory system to define polarization and show that this leads to the correct sign and magnitude of the quadrupole structure. This procedure resembles a non-relativistic connection between the polarization three-vector and vorticity employed in other works. In general, our results bring further evidence that the spin polarization dynamics in heavy-ion collisions may be not directly related to the thermal vorticity. The additional material explains the construction of the hydrodynamicaly consistent gradients of fluid velocity and temperature in thermal models with the help of the perfect-fluid equations of motion.

## Key claim

Using a thermal model with single freeze-out, the authors show that the longitudinal spin polarization of Λ hyperons has a quadrupole structure whose sign depends on the definition of the spin polarization tensor: using the full thermal vorticity gives the opposite sign to experiment, while using only the spatial components of thermal vorticity in the laboratory frame (projected thermal vorticity) yields the correct sign and magnitude, suggesting that spin polarization dynamics may not be directly related to thermal vorticity.

## Method

The authors used the single freeze-out thermal model with parameters previously fitted to PHENIX data at √sNN = 130 GeV for three centrality classes (0–15%, 15–30%, 30–60%). They calculated thermal vorticity on the freeze-out hypersurface using perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations to determine temperature gradients, then computed the mean Pauli-Lubański four-vector for Λ hyperons at midrapidity. Two cases were compared: (1) spin polarization tensor equal to thermal vorticity, and (2) spin polarization tensor defined using only the spatial components of thermal vorticity in the laboratory frame (projected thermal vorticity).

## Context

This work extends the single freeze-out model of Broniowski and Florkowski (2001) to spin polarization, using parameters from Florkowski (2004) that described PHENIX particle spectra and elliptic flow. It confirms the discrepancy found by Becattini (2017) between thermal-vorticity-based predictions and STAR experimental data (2018) for longitudinal polarization sign. The projected vorticity approach resembles non-relativistic treatments by Voloshin and provides a path toward understanding whether spin polarization dynamics decouples from thermal vorticity, as suggested by the spin hydrodynamics framework of Florkowski (2017).
