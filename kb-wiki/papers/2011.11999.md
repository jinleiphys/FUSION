# Conundrums at Finite Density

> Extending the successes of lattice quantum chromodynamics(QCD) at zero as well as nonzero temperatures to nonzero density is extremely desirable in view of the quest for the QCD phase diagram both theoretically and experimentally. It turns out though to give rise to some conundrums whose resolution may assist progress in this exciting but difficult area, and should therefore be sought actively.

# Conundrums at Finite Density

## Key claim

Extending lattice QCD to nonzero baryon density introduces several conceptual conundrums beyond the well-known sign problem, including divergent terms in the energy and number densities that persist in the continuum limit and conflicts with universality when those divergences are removed by modifying the lattice action.

## Method

The paper reviews the lattice formulation of QCD at finite chemical potential, examining how the chemical potential is introduced by multiplying forward and backward timelike links by functions f(μa) and g(μa). It analyzes the resulting divergences in the free quark gas energy density and number density, compares different prescriptions (linear, exponential, and relativistic forms) for these link factors, and examines numerical simulations of quark number susceptibilities on lattices with N_T = 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12 at temperatures 1.25 T_c and 2 T_c. The paper also reviews studies of the Overlap and Domain Wall fermion formulations at finite density, and analyzes eigenvalue distributions from simulations of QCD at nonzero isospin density and two-colour QCD.

## Key numbers

- Free quark energy density contains terms c_0 a^{-4} + c_1 μ^2 a^{-2} + c_3 μ^4 + c_4 μ^2 T^2 + c_5 T^4, with the μ-dependent a^{-2} divergence persisting after vacuum subtraction
- Free quark number density contains terms d_0 a^{-3} + d_1 μ a^{-2} + d_3 μ^3 + d_4 μ T^2 + d_5 T^3, with the μ-dependent a^{-2} divergence persisting after vacuum subtraction
- For the exponential choice f(μa) = exp(μa) and g(μa) = exp(-μa), the coefficients c_1 = 0 = d_1, eliminating μ-dependent divergences
- For the relativistic choice f(μa) = (1 + μa)/√(1 - μ^2 a^2) and g(μa) = (1 - μa)/√(1 - μ^2 a^2), the coefficients c_1 = 0 = d_1
- The condition f·g = 1 eliminates μ-dependent divergences for all such choices
- Quark number susceptibility simulations at T/T_c = 1.25 and 2.0 with m/T_c = 0.1 on N_T = 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 lattices show c_2 ≃ 0, confirming no additional non-perturbative divergent contribution beyond free theory subtraction
- Continuum extrapolated susceptibility results coincide with earlier results obtained with the exp(±aμ) action

## Context

This talk extends the author's prior work on lattice QCD at finite density, including the original derivation of the chemical potential on the lattice (Bilic and Gavai) and the proof that f·g = 1 eliminates μ-dependent divergences (Gavai, 1985). It builds on numerical simulations by Gavai and Gupta showing the non-perturbative removal of divergences, and on Gavai and Sharma (2015) demonstrating that these divergences exist in continuum theory with a momentum cutoff. The paper contrasts the linear μ-action (which preserves current conservation and allows a canonical partition function) with the exponential and relativistic actions (which eliminate divergences but break current conservation on the lattice). It discusses the Bloch-Wettig prescription for Domain Wall fermions at finite density, which breaks chiral symmetry, versus the chirally invariant Overlap action proposed by Gavai and Sharma (2012), which retains exact chiral symmetry but reintroduces the linear μ-form with its divergences. The topology results extend observations from isospin QCD and two-colour QCD (studied by multiple groups) that chiral symmetry restoration at finite density appears decoupled from changes in the eigenvalue spectrum and topological susceptibility, in contrast to the high-temperature case where zero modes fall exponentially.
