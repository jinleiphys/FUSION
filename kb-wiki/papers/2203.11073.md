# Questioning the 239Pu(n,2n)238Pu cross section shape above emission threshold

> In the light of the JEF(F) European project longstanding story according to the determination of the most exact shape of the 239Pu(n, 2n)238Pu reaction cross section and a recent measurement by Meot et al., this paper aims to shed another light on this topic by bringing new theoretical feedback. To achieve this goal, the AVXSF-LNG computer program has been upgraded to model second-chance reactions using its decay-probability module and, then chained to the TALYS-ECIS06 nuclear reaction system of codes. Present diligent calculation of the (n,2n) cross section over the energy range from the threshold to the onset of third-chance fission at about 12 MeV, suggests that current evaluations under-estimate the 239Pu(n,2n) cross section below 10 MeV; under-estimation of the order of 7% relatively to the JEFF-3.1 evaluation. On this ground, we propose an upward correction to the normalization of the measurement by Meot et al. Correction factor of about 1.24 with a maximum uncertainty on present fitted model estimated to 11.6%. Latter value is extracted from a sensitivity analysis of the calculation route to the level density model that is selected for the non-equilibrated residual nucleus and to alternative choices we can make in terms of neutron fission cross section measurement references for the 238Pu and 239Pu target nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper argues that current nuclear data evaluations (specifically JEFF-3.1) underestimate the 239Pu(n,2n)238Pu cross section below 10 MeV by about 7%, and proposes an upward correction factor of approximately 1.24 to the normalization of the recent measurement by Meot et al. (2021), with the caveat that this correction carries a maximum uncertainty of 11.6% derived from sensitivity to the choice of level density model and neutron fission cross section references.

## Method
The authors upgraded the AVXSF-LNG computer program with a decay-probability module to model second-chance reactions, then chained it to the TALYS-ECIS06 nuclear reaction system of codes. They performed coupled-channel optical model calculations using ECIS-06 for the 239Pu target, computed preequilibrium neutron emission within TALYS, and used the Hauser-Feshbach statistical theory with width fluctuation corrections in AVXSF-LNG to calculate the compound nucleus decay probabilities. A Monte Carlo treatment of fission channel fluctuations was employed for accurate average cross sections, and the calculation route was validated against measured neutron-induced cross sections for the 238Pu and 239Pu systems over the energy range from 1 keV to 12 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Underestimation of the 239Pu(n,2n) cross section below 10 MeV: approximately 7% relative to the JEFF-3.1 evaluation
- Proposed upward correction factor for the Meot et al. (2021) measurement normalization: 1.24
- Maximum uncertainty on the fitted model: 11.6%, extracted from sensitivity analysis to level density model choice and alternative neutron fission cross section references
- Energy range of the calculation: from the (n,2n) threshold to the onset of third-chance fission at about 12 MeV

## Context
This work extends the longstanding JEF(F) European project effort to determine the exact shape of the 239Pu(n,2n)238Pu reaction cross section, following the recent measurement by Meot et al. (2021) that confirmed disagreement between prior measurements and the JEFF-3.3 evaluation near threshold. The paper follows the "full model" methodology of Romain et al. (2016) for the JEFF-3.3 evaluation, and addresses the conflict highlighted by Fort et al. (1997) between the Frehaut et al. (1980) measurement and integral measurements from the PROFIL program. The results enable a renormalization of the Meot et al. (2021) data and provide theoretical support for upward revision of the evaluated cross section below 10 MeV, which has implications for nuclear data libraries and applications requiring accurate plutonium reaction rates.
