# Properties of effective interactions and the excitation of 6- states in 28Si

> Cross-section and analyzing power data from (p,p') scattering to the 6- states at 11.58 and 14.35 MeV in 28Si, taken with energies of 80, 100, 134, and 180 MeV protons, have been analyzed using a distorted wave approximation with microscopically defined wave functions. The results, taken in conjunction with an analysis of an M6 electron scattering form factor, suggest that the two states exhaust respectively, ~50% and ~60% of the strengths of isoscalar and isovector particle-hole excitations from the ground state. The energy variation of data also suggests that the non-central components of the effective interactions at 80 and 100 MeV may need to be enhanced.

## Key claim
The two 6- states in 28Si at 11.58 and 14.35 MeV exhaust approximately 50% and 60% of the isoscalar and isovector particle-hole strengths respectively, but this conclusion is conditional on the 134 and 180 MeV data being well reproduced by the model while the 80 and 100 MeV data are not, suggesting the non-central components of the effective interaction may need enhancement at lower energies.

## Method
The authors used a distorted wave approximation (DWA) with microscopically defined wave functions from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using the SkXcsb interaction and Woods-Saxon (WS) single-particle wave functions. They analyzed proton inelastic scattering cross sections and analyzing powers at 80, 100, 134, and 180 MeV, combined with an M6 electron scattering form factor analysis, using a g-folding model with effective NN interactions derived from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone solutions.

## Key numbers
- The 14.35 MeV isovector 6- state exhausts ~60% of the isovector particle-hole strength, based on scaling the pure particle-hole calculation by 0.36 to match the M6 electron scattering form factor and by 0.41 to match the (p,p') cross sections
- The 11.58 MeV isoscalar 6- state exhausts ~50% of the isoscalar particle-hole strength, based on scaling the pure particle-hole calculation by 0.26 to match the (p,p') cross sections at 134 and 180 MeV
- The spectroscopic amplitude for the pure particle-hole excitation is 2.238, derived from shell occupancies (0d5/2 occupancy 4.623, 0f7/2 occupancy from fractional occupancy)
- The M6 form factor data cover momentum transfer from 1 to nearly 3 fm-1

## Context
This work extends the methodology previously applied to 4- states in 16O by Amos et al. (2005) to the 6- states in 28Si, where five known 6- levels exist between 11 and 15 MeV excitation rather than the two expected from a pure stretched particle-hole scheme. The results enable future studies of effective NN interaction tensor components at lower energies (80 and 100 MeV), where the current model fails to reproduce the data, and suggest that additional data from scattering below 100 MeV are needed to determine any necessary adjustments.
