# Effective quantum kinetic theory for spin transport of fermions with collsional effects

> We systematically derive the collision term for the axial kinetic theory, a quantum kinetic theory delineating the coupled dynamics of the vector/axial charges and spin transport carried by the massive spin-1/2 fermions traversing a medium. We employ the Wigner-function approach and propose a consistent power-counting scheme where the axial-charge distribution function, a non-conserved quantity for massive particles, is accounted as the first-order quantity in the $ \hbar $ expansion, while the vector-charge distribution function the zeroth-order quantity. This specific power-counting scheme allows us to organize a reduced $ \hbar$ expansion for the collision term and to formally identity the spin-diffusion effect and the spin-polarization effect at the same order. We confirm that the obtained collisional axial kinetic theory smoothly reduces to the chiral kinetic theory in the massless limit, serving as a consistency check. In the absence of electromagnetic fields, we further present the simplified axial kinetic equations suitable for tracking dynamical spin polarization of heavy and light fermions, respectively. As an application to the weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma at high temperature, we compute the spin-diffusion term for massive quarks within the leading-log approximation. The formal expression for the first-order terms provides a path toward evaluation of the spin polarization effect in quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper systematically derives collision terms for the axial kinetic theory of massive spin-1/2 fermions, proposing a power-counting scheme where the axial-charge distribution function is treated as a first-order quantity in the \(\hbar\) expansion while the vector-charge distribution function is zeroth-order, which allows the spin-diffusion and spin-polarization effects to appear at the same order in the collision term.

## Method

The authors employ the Wigner-function approach and the Kadanoff-Baym equations to derive master equations for the Wigner functions decomposed in the Clifford algebra basis. They propose a consistent \(\hbar\)-counting scheme where \(\mathcal{V}^{\mu} \sim \mathcal{O}(\hbar^0)\) and \(\mathcal{A}^{\mu} \sim \mathcal{O}(\hbar)\), then solve the reduced master equations to obtain the scalar kinetic equation (SKE) and axial-vector kinetic equation (AKE) with collision terms. The collision term is decomposed into a classical part responsible for spin diffusion and a quantum part contributing to spin polarization from spin-orbit interaction.

## Key numbers

* The axial-charge distribution function \(\mathcal{A}^{\mu}\) is assigned to \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar^1)\) while the vector-charge distribution function \(\mathcal{V}^{\mu}\) is assigned to \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar^0)\) in the \(\hbar\) expansion.
* The spin-diffusion term for massive quarks in weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma at high temperature is computed within the leading-log approximation.
* The free-streaming part of the axial kinetic theory was previously established up to \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar)\) in Hattori (2019).

## Context

This work extends the free-streaming axial kinetic theory developed by Hattori (2019) for massive fermions by systematically including collisional effects, which had not been incorporated in prior studies. It builds on the chiral kinetic theory (CKT) framework for massless fermions developed by Son and Stephanov (2012) and Hidaka (2016), and addresses the need for non-equilibrium corrections to spin polarization beyond the thermal vorticity model of Becattini (2013). The formalism enables future evaluation of the spin polarization effect in quantum chromodynamics and provides a path toward computing the quantum corrections that require self-energies with polarized gluons.
