# Generalized Ensemble Theory with Non-extensive Statistics

> The non-extensive canonical ensemble theory is reconsidered with the method of Lagrange multipliers by maximizing Tsallis entropy, with the constraint that the normalized term of Tsallis' $q-$average of physical quantities, the sum $\sum p_j^q$, is independent of the probability $p_i$ for Tsallis parameter $q$. The self-referential problem in the deduced probability and thermal quantities in non-extensive statistics is thus avoided, and thermodynamical relationships are obtained in a consistent and natural way. We also extend the study to the non-extensive grand canonical ensemble theory and obtain the $q$-deformed Bose-Einstein distribution as well as the $q$-deformed Fermi-Dirac distribution. The theory is further applied to the generalized Planck law to demonstrate the distinct behaviors of the various generalized $q$-distribution functions discussed in literature.

## Key claim

By imposing the constraint that the normalized term of Tsallis' q-average, the sum ∑ p_j^q, is independent of the probability p_i, the self-referential problem in the deduced probability and thermal quantities in non-extensive statistics is avoided, and thermodynamical relationships are obtained in a consistent and natural way.

## Method

The authors reconsider the non-extensive canonical ensemble theory using the method of Lagrange multipliers to maximize Tsallis entropy, adding an extra constraint that ∂/∂p_i ∑ p_j^q = 0, which treats the sum ∑ p_j^q as a p_i-independent constant C_q. They then extend this approach to the grand canonical ensemble to derive q-deformed Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac distribution functions, and apply the results to the generalized Planck law.

## Key numbers

- The generalized q-deformed Bose-Einstein distribution is given by: n̄_l = (1/(e_{q'}^{α'+β'ε_l} - 1))^q, where q' = 2-q, and the '+' sign gives the result for fermions and '-' for bosons.
- For the generalized Planck law, the blackbody photon energy density per unit volume is: D_q(ν) = (8πT³)/(c³h²) × x³/(e_{q'}^x - 1)^q, where x ≡ βhν.
- The generalized Stefan-Boltzmann constant is given by: σ_q = (8π)/(c³h³) ∫₀^∞ x³/(e_{q'}^x - 1)^q dx, with the classical result at q=1 being σ ≈ 6.49394.
- In the limit q → 1, the derived q-BED approximates to n̄_{BE}(ω) ≈ 1/((e_{q'}^{α'+β'ω})^q - 1), which agrees with the result of Beck (2000).
- When (1-q) is not small, the relation n̄_l = (n̄'_l)^q holds at the same energy level l, recovering the distribution of Buyukkilic (1993, 1995) and Teweldeberhan (2003, 2005).

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive statistical mechanics framework based on Tsallis entropy (Tsallis, 1988) by resolving the self-referential problem present in earlier formulations by Curado (1991), Martinez (2000), and Ferri (2005). The derived q-deformed Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac distributions reconcile the conflicting expressions in the literature, such as those from Buyukkilic (1993, 1995) and Beck (2000), by showing that both can be recovered as limiting cases. The application to the generalized Planck law enables future comparisons of different q-distribution functions in blackbody radiation and high-energy particle production.
