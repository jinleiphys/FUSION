# QCD Sum Rules Description of Nucleons in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We calculate the nucleon parameters in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter using the QCD sum rules. The nucleon self-energies are expressed in terms of the in-medium values of QCD condensates. The simple approximate expressions for the self-energies are obtained in terms of these condensates. Relation between successive inclusion of the condensates and the meson-exchange picture of the nucleon interaction with medium is analyzed. The values of the self-energies and of the symmetry energy agree with those obtained by the methods of nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nucleon self-energies and the symmetry energy in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using QCD sum rules in the gas approximation (keeping only terms linear in density), finding that the results agree with those obtained by traditional nuclear physics methods, but the approach requires model-dependent input for the isovector scalar nucleon expectation value ζ_N and for the four-quark condensates.

## Method

The authors use the Borel-transformed QCD sum rules for the nucleon polarization operator in asymmetric nuclear matter, including operator product expansion (OPE) terms up to dimension d=4 (local vector, scalar, and gluon condensates) and the leading 1/q² contributions from nonlocal vector condensates and four-quark condensates. The in-medium condensates are expressed in terms of nucleon densities ρ_n and ρ_p and the asymmetry parameter β = (ρ_n − ρ_p)/(ρ_p + ρ_n), with the nucleon expectation values for the scalar condensate κ_N obtained from the πN sigma term and ζ_N taken from the perturbative chiral quark model (PCQM). The sum rules are solved for the vector self-energy Σ_v, the Dirac effective mass m*, and the effective scalar self-energy Σ*_s, with the continuum threshold W²_m and effective residue λ*²_m determined self-consistently.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ (used as reference density for the gas approximation).
* Vacuum nucleon mass: m = 0.94 GeV.
* Vacuum nucleon residue: λ²₀ = 1.9 GeV⁶; vacuum continuum threshold: W²₀ = 2.2 GeV².
* Borel mass window for matching: 0.8 GeV² < M² < 1.4 GeV².
* Nucleon expectation value of the gluon condensate: g_N = ⟨N|(α_s/π) G²|N⟩ ≈ −(8/9) m.
* PCQM value for the isovector scalar nucleon expectation value: ζ_N = 0.54.
* First moments of the nonlocal vector distribution functions: ξᵘ = −0.24, ξᵈ = −0.09, ξ = ξᵘ + ξᵈ = −0.33.
* Vector self-energy in symmetric matter at ρ₀: Σ_v(ρ₀, 0) (value not given in truncated text; the text states it agrees with nuclear physics methods).
* Symmetry energy: stated to agree with nuclear physics methods (numerical value not given in truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rules approach for nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter, developed by Cohen, Furnstahl, Griegel, and others (1991–1995), to the isospin-asymmetric case. It uses the same current and Borel transform technique as Ioffe (1981) for the vacuum nucleon, and incorporates the nonlocal condensate formalism of Drukarev and Levin (1991) and the four-quark condensates computed in the perturbative chiral quark model by Gutsche et al. (2000). The results enable future extensions beyond the gas approximation to include nonlinear density effects and provide a QCD-based foundation for the symmetry energy and isovector nucleon self-energies used in nuclear structure and neutron-star calculations.
