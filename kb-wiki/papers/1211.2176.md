# Improvement of multichannel amplitudes for the pion-pion scattering using the dispersion relations

> The multichannel S- and P-wave amplitudes for the pion-pion scattering, constructed requiring analyticity and unitarity of the S-matrix and using the uniformization procedure, are elaborated using the dispersion relations with imposed crossing symmetry condition. The amplitudes are modified in the low-energy region to improve their consistency with experimental data and the dispersion relations. Agreement with data is achieved for both amplitudes from the threshold up to 1.8 GeV and with dispersion relations up to 1.1 GeV. Consequences of the applied modifications, e.g. changes of the S-wave lowest-pole positions, are presented.

## Key claim

The multichannel S- and P-wave pion-pion scattering amplitudes, originally constructed using uniformization without crossing symmetry, can be improved in the low-energy region by re-fitting parameters to simultaneously satisfy experimental data and Roy-like dispersion relations, achieving consistency with dispersion relations up to 1.1 GeV and with data up to 1.8 GeV.

## Method

The authors started with three-channel S- and P-wave amplitudes from the uniformization procedure of YuSu (2010) and modified them by replacing the near-threshold region with a generalized power-series expansion in pion momentum, using fixed scattering lengths and slope parameters from dispersive analyses. They then re-fitted the resonance pole positions (for f0(500), f0(980), and ρ(770)), matching energies, and background parameters by minimizing a χ² that included contributions from experimental phase shifts and inelasticities in all coupled channels plus a term enforcing consistency with once-subtracted Roy-like dispersion relations coupling S and P waves.

## Key numbers

- χ²/n.d.f. improved from 2.36 (extended amplitudes) to 1.29 (re-fitted amplitudes)
- The dispersion-relations contribution to χ² dropped from 571 to 66 after re-fitting
- For the f0(500) resonance on sheet II, the pole shifted from (617 − i 554) MeV to (474 − i 298) MeV, changing the σ meson mass from 829 MeV to 560 MeV and width from 1108 MeV to 596 MeV
- For f0(980) on sheet II, the pole shifted from (1013 − i 31) MeV to (1000 − i 22) MeV, giving mass 1000 MeV and width 45 MeV, consistent with PDG values of 990 ± 20 MeV and 50^{+20}_{-12} MeV
- The ρ(770) pole positions changed by less than 1%
- The S-wave background phase shift at the ππ threshold became negative with a value a_{11} = −0.091

## Context

This work extends the multichannel uniformization approach of YuSu (2010) by incorporating crossing symmetry via Roy-like dispersion relations, which were not included in the original amplitude construction. The improved f0(500) pole position agrees well with the result from chiral perturbation theory and Roy-like equations by Colangelo et al. (2011), (441^{+16}_{-8} − i(272^{+9}_{-12.5})) MeV, resolving a major discrepancy between the uniformization and dispersive approaches. The method enables future analyses to produce three-channel ππ amplitudes that simultaneously satisfy analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry constraints from threshold up to 1.1 GeV.
