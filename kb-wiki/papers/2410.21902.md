# Volume fluctuations affect on transport and thermodynamic coefficients in p-Pb systems

> We use A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to simulate the event-by-event p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.02 TeV. To study the space-time volume fluctuations, two different definitions of radius have been introduced in the calculation: One is weigh of event-by-event charged multiplicity (noted as set I), and the other one is weigh of energy density in a single event (notes as set II). Based on calculations of sets I and II, the transport and thermodynamic coefficients, such as the speed of sound squared, shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and mean free path are both dependent on the radius and local temperature. By comparing results of sets I and II, we found that these transport and thermodynamic coefficients differed significantly in the results. These results imply that the transport and thermodynamic properties of the medium in small collisions significantly depend on the space-time volume fluctuations.

## Key claim

The transport and thermodynamic coefficients (speed of sound squared, shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and mean free path) in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV depend significantly on which definition of system radius is used, implying that space-time volume fluctuations strongly affect these properties in small collision systems, but the kinetic theory approach cannot completely suppress non-flow effects.

## Method

The A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model was used to simulate event-by-event p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV with impact parameter b=0 fm. Two different radius definitions were introduced: set I uses the event-by-event charged multiplicity as a weight for the radius, while set II uses the local energy density in a single event as a weight. Transport and thermodynamic coefficients were re-extracted from the parton evolution stage using kinetic theory formulas for shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, speed of sound squared, and mean free path, with volume cut-off methods applied to study the effects of volume fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The transform temperature (chemical freeze-out temperature for partons achieving hadronization) is approximately 0.08 GeV in set II.
- For set II, the shear viscosity eta/s shows a peak close to R approximately 1 fm, indicating highest multiplicity generated in that radius range.
- The speed of sound squared c_s^2 decreases smoothly with increasing radius, with qualitatively similar behavior to the Chiral Mean Field model (Pal 2024).
- The mean free path lambda shows a minimum crossover temperature of 0.08 GeV in set II.
- For set I, shear viscosity eta/s is constant for small radius and decreases for R greater than 4 fm.
- The volume cut-off parameter P_cut was varied from 0.1 to 0.4 in both sets.

## Context

This work extends studies of transport properties in small collision systems, where similar collective flow observations have been found in p+Pb collisions at LHC (Khachatryan 2015) and in p+Au, d+Au, and He-3+Au collisions at RHIC (Aidala 2019, Nagle 2018). The results are compared with calculations from the Chiral Mean Field model (Pal 2024), NJL model (Bhattacharyya 2013), PNJL model (Zhao 2020), and Lattice QCD results (Bazavov 2009). The study enables future work to separate non-flow effects from genuine volume fluctuations in small collision systems, which is necessary for reliable determination of QGP properties using data-driven techniques such as Bayesian analysis (Nijs 2021, Bernhard 2019, Everett 2021).
