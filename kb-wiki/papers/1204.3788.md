# Role of Vector Interaction and Axial Anomaly in the PNJL Modeling of the QCD Phase Diagram

> Effects of a flavor singlet vector interaction in the Polyakov - Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model are studied in combination with the axial U(1) breaking Kobayashi - Maskawa - 't Hooft interaction. Using a consistent cutoff scheme we investigate the QCD phase diagram and its dependence on the vector coupling strength $g_v$. We find that the first order chiral phase transition at moderate baryon chemical potentials and its critical point, a generic feature of most NJL-type models without vector coupling, disappear for sufficiently large values of $g_v$ that are consistent with lattice QCD results at imaginary chemical potential. The influence of non-zero $g_v$ on the curvature of the crossover boundary in the $T-μ$ plane close to $μ= 0$ is also examined.

# Role of Vector Interaction and Axial Anomaly in the PNJL Modeling of the QCD Phase Diagram

## Key claim

The first-order chiral phase transition and its critical point, which appear generically in PNJL models without vector coupling, disappear for sufficiently large vector coupling strengths (g_v >= 0.38 G) that are consistent with lattice QCD results at imaginary chemical potential; the existence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram is therefore strongly disfavored when realistic vector and U(1)_A breaking interactions are included.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model at mean field level, incorporating a flavor singlet vector interaction with variable coupling strength g_v and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) interaction for axial U(1) breaking. They solve the self-consistent gap equations for the chiral condensates and Polyakov loop, using a consistent three-momentum cutoff scheme (Lambda = 750 MeV) where the NJL interaction is turned off above the cutoff to avoid unphysical behavior and ensure the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The critical vector coupling strength at which the first-order transition disappears is g_v^crit ~ 0.38 G, where G is the scalar-pseudoscalar coupling strength.
- The critical KMT coupling strength below which the first-order transition disappears is K_c ~ 0.71 K_0, where K_0 is the vacuum value.
- The curvature of the crossover boundary near mu = 0, defined by T_c(mu)/T_c(0) = 1 - kappa (mu^2/T_c^2), approaches kappa ~ 0.06 for sufficiently large g_v, consistent with lattice QCD results.
- The three-momentum cutoff used is Lambda = 750 MeV, with current quark masses m_u = m_d = 3.6 MeV and m_s = 87 MeV.
- The Polyakov loop effective potential uses T_0 = 270 MeV with parameters a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_4 = -1.75.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Asakawa (1989), Kitazawa (2002), Fukushima (2008), and others who noted the sensitivity of the critical point to vector couplings and axial anomaly terms in NJL-type models. The authors show that with g_v >= 0.4 G, the first-order chiral transition region no longer overlaps with the nuclear liquid-gas coexistence region calculated using in-medium chiral effective field theory by Fiorilla (2011), resolving an unphysical conflict. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star equations of state, where repulsive vector interactions help support two-solar-mass neutron stars as observed by Demorest (2010).
