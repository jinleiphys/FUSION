# Electromagnetic Form Factors and Charge Radii of Pseudoscalar and Scalar Mesons: A Comprehensive Contact Interaction Analysis

> We carry out a comprehensive survey of electromagnetic form factors of all light, heavy and heavy-light ground-state pseudoscalar and scalar mesons. Our analysis is based upon a Schwinger-Dyson equations treatment of a vector $\times$ vector contact interaction. It incorporates confinement and ensures axial vector and vector Ward-Takahashi identities are satisfied along with the corresponding corollaries such as the Goldberger-Treiman relations. The algebraic simplicity of the model allows us to compute the form factors at arbitrarily large virtualities of the probing photon momentum squared with relative ease. Wherever possible and insightful, we compare our results for the electromagnetic form factors and the charge radii with those obtained earlier through Schwinger-Dyson equations, lattice and with experimental observations available. We also comment on the scope and shortcomings of the model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a symmetry-preserving vector × vector contact interaction (CI) model, solved via Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDEs), can provide a comprehensive and algebraically simple description of electromagnetic form factors (EFFs) and charge radii for all light, heavy, and heavy-light ground-state pseudoscalar (PS) and scalar (S) mesons, but the authors explicitly note that the resulting EFFs are "harder" than those from full QCD predictions, meaning the model's simplicity comes at the cost of quantitative accuracy at large momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors employed a rainbow-ladder truncation of the SDEs using a momentum-independent gluon propagator (contact interaction), regularized in a symmetry-preserving manner to ensure axial-vector and vector Ward-Takahashi identities are satisfied. They solved the gap equation to obtain momentum-independent dressed quark masses for u, d, s, c, and b quarks, then solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) to obtain the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes (BSAs) for PS and S mesons. The EFFs were computed from the triangle diagram using the dressed quark propagators and the quark-photon vertex, with the algebraic simplicity of the CI allowing calculations at arbitrarily large photon virtualities.

## Key numbers

* Dressed quark masses: M_u = 0.367 GeV, M_s = 0.53 GeV, M_c = 1.52 GeV, M_b = 4.75 GeV, obtained from the gap equation with current quark masses m_u = 0.007 GeV, m_s = 0.17 GeV, m_c = 1.08 GeV, m_b = 3.92 GeV.
* The infrared gluon mass scale is fixed at m_g = 500 MeV, and the infrared cutoff is Lambda_IR = 0.24 GeV.
* The effective coupling for light quarks (u,d,s) is hat_alpha_IR = 4.57, while for bottomonium (b bbar) it is hat_alpha_IR = 0.035, with the ultraviolet regulator varying from Lambda_UV = 0.905 GeV (light quarks) to 7.159 GeV (bottomonium).
* The PS meson masses computed from the BSE include: pi (u dbar) at 0.14 GeV, K (u sbar) at 0.49 GeV, D (c ubar) at 1.86 GeV, B (u bbar) at 5.35 GeV, and eta_c (c cbar) at 2.95 GeV.
* The S meson masses include: sigma (u dbar) at 1.22 GeV, kappa (u sbar) at 1.33 GeV, D_0* (c ubar) at 2.32 GeV, B_0* (u bbar) at 5.50 GeV, and chi_c0 (c cbar) at 3.35 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the contact interaction approach previously applied to the pion by Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2010) and to vector mesons by Roberts et al. (2011) to a comprehensive survey of all ground-state pseudoscalar and scalar mesons. It provides a benchmark for comparison with more sophisticated SDE treatments (e.g., Maris and Tandy, 2000; Chang et al., 2013), lattice QCD results (e.g., Shultz et al., 2015; Alexandrou et al., 2021), and experimental data (e.g., NA7 collaboration, 1986; Jefferson Lab Fpi collaboration, 2000, 2006). The results enable future studies of diquark correlations in baryons, since the BSE for scalar and pseudoscalar diquarks is identical to that for the corresponding mesons up to color and charge factors, and the computed form factors can be used in quark-diquark models of nucleons and other baryons.
