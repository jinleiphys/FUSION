# Mirror and triplet energy differences in $sd$-shell nuclei using microscopic interactions with isospin-symmetry breaking effects

> In this study, we developed and tested two different isospin symmetry-breaking (ISB) versions of the microscopic DJ16A interaction. Starting with the isospin symmetric DJ16A interaction, we introduced two different Coulomb interactions- Coulomb-CD and Coulomb-w/SRC- along with phenomenological charge symmetry breaking (CSB) and charge independence breaking (CIB) effects. Then, we employed these interactions to calculate $b$- and $c$-parameters of the isobaric multiplet mass equation for $|T_z| = 1/2$ and $|T_z| = 1$ nuclei across the $sd$-shell. Our results indicate that the DJ16A$^\dagger$ interaction provides the most accurate $b$-parameter predictions between the two DJ16A-based interactions. Additionally, we explored mirror energy differences (MEDs) in low-energy spectra around $A = 20$ and demonstrated that large MEDs are primarily associated with high occupancies of the $1s_{1/2}$ orbital. Furthermore, $E2$ transition strengths were calculated using both DJ16A-based ISB interactions agreed with the experimental data, with minimal ISB effects observed on these transitions. Overall, the DJ16A$^\dagger$ interaction serves as a complementary set to the newly developed USD-family interactions, USDC, and USDCm and can be further tested for other mirror nuclei across the $sd$-shell to study nuclear structure properties and ISB effects in nuclear $β$-decay.

## Key claim

The DJ16A$^\dagger$ interaction, which incorporates Coulomb interactions (Coulomb-CD or Coulomb-w/SRC) along with phenomenological charge symmetry breaking (CSB) and charge independence breaking (CIB) effects tuned to reproduce experimental $b(A=17)$, $b(A=18)$, and $c(A=18)$, provides the most accurate $b$-parameter predictions among the two DJ16A-based interactions tested, though it still shows a root-mean-square deviation of 98 keV for $|T_z|=1/2$ doublets compared to 81 keV for the USDCm interaction.

## Method

The authors started with the isospin-symmetric microscopic DJ16A interaction and added two different Coulomb interactions (Coulomb-CD and Coulomb-w/SRC) along with phenomenological CSB and CIB interactions of Yukawa form with a radial cutoff at 0.45 fm. They constructed six sets of ISB interactions by combining these components, with the final tuned sets (DJ16A$^\dagger$ and DJ16A$^*$) adjusted to reproduce experimental $b(A=17)$, $b(A=18)$, and $c(A=18)$ by modifying proton single-particle energies and reducing the CIB strength by factors of 0.43 and 0.39, respectively. Shell-model calculations were then performed for $|T_z|=1/2$ and $|T_z|=1$ nuclei across the $sd$-shell to compute $b$- and $c$-parameters of the isobaric multiplet mass equation, mirror energy differences in low-energy spectra around $A=20$, and $E2$ transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The $b_{rms}$ for $|T_z|=1/2$ doublets (A=17 to 39) is 98 keV for DJ16A$^\dagger$, 174 keV for DJ16A$^*$, and 81 keV for USDCm.
- The $b_{rms}$ for $|T_z|=1$ triplets (A=18 to 38) is 92 keV for DJ16A$^\dagger$, 155 keV for DJ16A$^*$, and 77 keV for USDCm.
- The experimental $c(A=18)$ is 352 keV; DJ16A$^\dagger$ and DJ16A$^*$ reproduce this after reducing CIB strength by factors of 0.43 and 0.39, respectively.
- The experimental MED of the 11/2$^+$ state in the $^{19}$Ne/$^{19}$F mirror pair is -208.4(18) keV; calculated values are -320 keV (DJ16A$^\dagger$), -339 keV (DJ16A$^*$), and -227 keV (USDCm).
- The experimental MED of the 1/2$^+_1$ state in the $^{21}$Na/$^{21}$Ne mirror pair is -370.37(43) keV.
- The experimental MEDs of the 5/2$^+_3$ and 3/2$^+_2$ states in $^{21}$Na/$^{21}$Ne are -231.54(84) keV and -216.63(85) keV, respectively; DJ16A$^\dagger$ gives -115 keV and -100 keV for these states.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic DJ16A interaction (Sarma et al., 2023) by adding isospin symmetry-breaking effects, complementing the USD-family interactions USDC and USDCm (Magilligan and Brown, 2020) that have been used to study ISB effects in $sd$-shell nuclei. The results enable further testing of ISB effects in mirror nuclei across the $sd$-shell for nuclear structure studies and $\beta$-decay calculations, and the authors note that their DJ16A-based interactions fail to reproduce the correct ground state for $A=25$ ($^{25}$Mg-$^{25}$Al) and $A=24$ mirror pairs, where USDCm succeeds.
