# Soliton-potential interaction in the nonlinear Klein-Gordon model

> The interaction of solitons with external potentials in nonlinear Klein-Gordon field theory is investigated using an improved model. The presented model has been constructed with a better approximation for adding the potential to the Lagrangian through the metric of background space-time. The results of the model are compared with another model and the differences are discussed.

# Soliton-potential interaction in the nonlinear Klein-Gordon model

## Key claim

The interaction of solitons with external potentials in nonlinear Klein-Gordon field theory is studied using two different models for incorporating the potential into the Lagrangian; the general behavior is similar between models, but the details of soliton reflection from potential wells differ qualitatively depending on which model is used, with model 1 predicting reflection windows that model 2 does not exhibit for the NKG soliton.

## Method

Two models are compared: Model 1 adds the external potential to the equation of motion by writing the coupling parameter as λ(x) = 1 + v(x) in the field potential U(φ) = λ(x)(½αφ² − ¼βφ⁴ + ⅙γφ⁶). Model 2 incorporates the potential through the metric of background space-time, using g⁰⁰(x) ≈ 1 + v(x) and g¹¹(x) ≈ −1/(1+v(x)) for a smooth, slowly varying weak potential. Numerical simulations use a 4th-order Runge-Kutta method for time derivatives and finite differences for space derivatives, with grid spacing h = 0.01, 0.02, or 0.001 and time steps of h/4, employing the one-soliton solution φ(x,t) = √[(2α/β)(1 ± tanh(√(α/(a²−u²))(x−x₀−ut)))] as the initial condition.

## Key numbers

- Soliton solution parameters: α = β = 1, a = 1, u = 0.5, x₀ = 0 used for the kink and antikink solutions shown in Figure 1.
- Potential form used in simulations: v(x) = a e^(−b(x−c)²), with a controlling strength, b controlling width, and c controlling center.
- For potential barrier v(x) = 0.5 e^(−4x²), critical velocity separates reflection (uᵢ < u_c) from transmission (uᵢ > u_c); the critical velocity differs between models due to different effective masses (M_eff1 = 1 versus M_eff2 = (1+v(x))²).
- For potential well v(x) = −0.5 e^(−4x²), escape velocity is defined as the initial speed for which asymptotic speed is null at infinity; escape velocities differ between models due to greater energy loss from radiation in model 2.
- In model 1, soliton reflection from potential well v(x) = −0.4 e^(−4x²) occurs within the narrow initial velocity window 0.062 ≤ uᵢ ≤ 0.065; no such reflection window was found in model 2 for any potential depth tested (a = −0.2, −0.3, −0.5, −0.6, −0.7, −0.8).

## Context

This work extends studies of soliton-defect interactions in nonlinear Klein-Gordon models, which have applications to pion form factors, hadronic atoms, Josephson junctions, superfluid current disruption, shallow water waves, and Hawking radiation from black holes (references i1–i6). The two models compared here build on prior approaches: model 1 follows the method of adding potential through λ(x) = 1 + v(x) as used in references i11–i13, while model 2 uses the metric-based approach from references i14–i17. The finding that NKG solitons in model 2 do not exhibit reflection from potential wells contradicts behavior seen in Sine-Gordon and φ⁴ models, where such reflection does occur in the metric-based model. The results enable further investigation of soliton scattering on defects using model 2 for other nonlinear field theories to develop a more comprehensive understanding of soliton behavior.
