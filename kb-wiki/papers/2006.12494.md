# Variation of Single-Particle Energies: Scaling Behavior for the Spectrum of $^{48}$Cr

> We perform shell model calculations for $^{48}$Cr using GXPF1A and FPD6 interactions by varying single particle energies. We find a scaling behavior which leads to the finding that many different sets of interactions with vastly different single particle energies can give almost identical results for the yrast spectrum of $^{48}$Cr

# Variation of Single-Particle Energies: Scaling Behavior for the Spectrum of $^{48}$Cr

## Key claim
Shell model calculations for $^{48}$Cr using the GXPF1A interaction show that many different sets of interactions with vastly different single-particle energies can give nearly identical yrast spectra, provided a scaling factor is applied; however, the ratios E(J)$_{\Delta}$/E(J) are not exactly equal across all J states, meaning the scaling is approximate rather than perfect.

## Method
Shell model calculations were performed for $^{48}$Cr using the GXPF1A interaction (and compared with FPD6) by systematically varying the single-particle energies of the 0$f_{7/2}$, 1$p_{3/2}$, 0$f_{5/2}$, and 1$p_{1/2}$ orbitals. In CASE-1, the splitting of pair A ($p_{3/2} - f_{7/2}$) was kept constant while pair B ($p_{1/2} - f_{5/2}$) was shifted by an amount $\Delta$ (both positive and negative). In CASE-2, pair C ($f_{5/2} - f_{7/2}$) was kept constant while pair D ($p_{1/2} - p_{3/2}$) was shifted by $\Delta$, holding spin-orbit splittings constant. The yrast energies for even J states from 0 to 16 and B(E2) transition strengths for J to J-2 were computed for each $\Delta$ value.

## Key numbers
* GXPF1A single-particle energies (MeV relative to 0$f_{7/2}$): 0$f_{7/2}$ = 0 (-8.6240), 1$p_{3/2}$ = 2.9447, 0$f_{5/2}$ = 7.2411, 1$p_{1/2}$ = 4.4870
* FPD6 single-particle energies (MeV relative to 0$f_{7/2}$): 0$f_{7/2}$ = 0 (-8.3876), 1$p_{3/2}$ = 1.8942, 0$f_{5/2}$ = 6.4910, 1$p_{1/2}$ = 3.9093
* For CASE-1 with $\Delta$ = 20 MeV, the $2^+$ energy is 0.669 MeV (original GXPF1A: 0.788 MeV); the $16^+$ energy is 10.623 MeV (original: 12.805 MeV)
* For CASE-1 with $\Delta$ = 20 MeV, B(E2: $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$) = 191 $e^2$fm$^4$ (original: 249 $e^2$fm$^4$)
* Ratio E(J)$_{\Delta}$/E(J) for $\Delta$ = 1 ranges from 0.968 (J=12) to 0.983 (J=4); for $\Delta$ = 20 ranges from 0.812 (J=10) to 0.901 (J=4)
* Renormalization factor of 1.2 applied to the $\Delta$ = 20 spectrum yields energies within approximately 0.1 MeV of the original GXPF1A spectrum for most states (e.g., $2^+$: 0.802 MeV vs. 0.788 MeV; $16^+$: 12.748 MeV vs. 12.805 MeV)

## Context
This work extends systematic shell-model studies of $^{48}$Cr performed by E. Caurier et al. and others using the ANTOINE code in the PF shell. It builds on prior calculations by K. Hara et al., F. Brandolini et al., Z.C. Gao et al., and R.A. Herrera et al. The finding of approximate scaling behavior implies that fitting only the yrast spectrum of $^{48}$Cr cannot uniquely determine the single-particle energies, as many combinations of two-body matrix elements and single-particle energies yield nearly equivalent results. This enables a cautionary perspective for phenomenological shell-model fitting: additional observables beyond the yrast band are needed to constrain the single-particle energies uniquely.
