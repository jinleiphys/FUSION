# Super Efimov effect of resonantly interacting fermions in two dimensions

> We study a system of spinless fermions in two dimensions with a short-range interaction fine-tuned to a p-wave resonance. We show that three such fermions form an infinite tower of bound states of orbital angular momentum l=\pm1 and their binding energies obey a universal doubly exponential scaling E_3^{(n)}\propto\exp(-2e^{3πn/4+θ}) at large n. This "super Efimov effect" is found by a renormalization group analysis and confirmed by solving the bound state problem. We also provide an indication that there are l=\pm2 four-body resonances associated with every three-body bound state at E_4^{(n)}\propto\exp(-2e^{3πn/4+θ-0.188}). These universal few-body states may be observed in ultracold atom experiments and should be taken into account in future many-body studies of the system.

# Super Efimov effect of resonantly interacting fermions in two dimensions

## Key claim
Three spinless fermions in two dimensions with a short-range interaction tuned to a p-wave resonance form an infinite tower of bound states with orbital angular momentum l=±1, whose binding energies obey a universal doubly exponential scaling at large n. This "super Efimov effect" is derived from a renormalization group analysis and confirmed by solving the bound state problem, but the nonuniversal constant θ in the scaling law depends on the initial conditions of the couplings at the ultraviolet scale and is defined modulo 3π/4.

## Method
The authors performed a renormalization group (RG) analysis of an effective field theory for spinless fermions and l=±1 composite bosons, deriving RG equations for the two-body, three-body, and four-body couplings. They also solved coupled integral equations for the three-fermion scattering T-matrix using a model Hamiltonian with a separable p-wave potential regularized by a Gaussian form factor, both analytically within a leading-logarithm approximation and numerically.

## Key numbers
- Three-body binding energies obey the scaling E₃⁽ⁿ⁾ ∝ exp(-2e^{3πn/4+θ}) at large n, where the period is 3π/4 ≈ 2.35619.
- Four-body resonances in the l=±2 channels occur at E₄⁽ⁿ⁾ ∝ exp(-2e^{3πn/4+θ-0.188}), with the shift of -0.188 relative to the three-body binding energies.
- Numerical solutions for the lowest six three-body binding energies yield λₙ = ln ln(Λ/κₙ) values: λ₀ = 0.5632, λ₁ = 2.770, λ₂ = 5.078, λ₃ = 7.430, λ₄ = 9.785, λ₅ = 12.141, with successive differences approaching 2.35619 (3π/4) at large n.
- The p-wave resonance condition is achieved by tuning the bare two-body coupling to v₀ = 2π/Λ², where Λ is the momentum cutoff.

## Context
This work extends the concept of the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) to a new regime: three spinless fermions in two dimensions at a p-wave resonance, whereas the original Efimov effect involves three bosons in three dimensions at an s-wave resonance. The authors note that the Efimov effect does not take place in two dimensions or with p-wave interactions (Lim 1980, Nishida 2011, Nishida 2012). The predicted four-body resonances associated with each three-body bound state resemble the pair of four-body resonances found in the usual Efimov effect (Hammer 2007, von Stecher 2009). These universal few-body states should be taken into account in future many-body studies beyond mean-field approximation, particularly for the topological quantum phase transition across a p-wave Feshbach resonance (Read 2002, Gurarie 2005), and may be observed in ultracold atom experiments with p-wave Feshbach resonances (Chin 2010) through measurements of three-body or four-body recombination atom loss (Kraemer 2006, Ferlaino 2009).
