# $J/ψ$ Gluonic Dissociation Revisited : I. Fugacity, Flux And Formation Time Effects

> We revisit the standard treatment [Xu, Kharzeev, Satz and Wang, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 53}, 3051 (1996)] of $J/ψ$ suppression due to gluonic bombardment in an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma. Effects arising from gluon fugacity, relative $g-ψ$ flux, and $ψ$ meson formation time are correctly incorporated in the formulation of the gluon number density, velocity-weighted cross section, and the survival probability. Our new formulae are applied to numerically study the pattern of $J/ψ$ suppression in the central rapidity region at RHIC/LHC energies. The temperature and transverse momentum dependence of our graphs have noticeable differences from those of Xu et al.

# J/ψ Gluonic Dissociation Revisited: I. Fugacity, Flux And Formation Time Effects

## Key claim

The standard treatment of J/ψ suppression by gluonic bombardment in an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma (Xu et al., 1996) contains three inaccuracies, incorrect treatment of gluon fugacity, relative flux evaluated in the wrong frame, and neglect of J/ψ formation time, which when corrected cooperatively reduce the amount of J/ψ suppression (i.e., increase the survival probability) compared to the earlier results, with the relative difference between the two approaches being about 5-6% for the thermal-averaged rate but more pronounced at high transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors reformulate the gluon number density using the full Bose-Einstein distribution with fugacity λ_g ≤ 1 (rather than the factorized approximation of Xu et al.), compute the thermal-averaged gluon-J/ψ dissociation rate entirely in the J/ψ rest frame (where relative flux v_rel = c), and incorporate the J/ψ formation time τ_F ≈ 0.89 fm/c into the lower limit of the time integration for the survival probability. The temporal evolution of temperature and fugacities is obtained by solving the master rate equations for Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using initial conditions from HIJING Monte Carlo simulations at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon fugacity at RHIC(1): λ_g = 0.05; at LHC(1): λ_g = 0.124 (Table 1)
- Initial gluon number density at RHIC(1): n_g^Xu = 2.11 fm^{-3} (Xu procedure) vs. n_g^Our = 1.76 fm^{-3} (our proposal); at LHC(1): n_g^Xu = 17.34 fm^{-3} vs. n_g^Our = 14.66 fm^{-3} (Table 1)
- The relative difference between n_g^Xu and n_g^Our is of order 1/8 ≈ 12% (Section 2)
- The relative difference between the thermal-averaged rates ⟨Γ^Xu⟩ and ⟨Γ^Our⟩ is not more than about 5-6% (Section 3)
- J/ψ formation time in the c̄c barycentric frame: τ_F ≈ 0.89 fm/c (Section 4)
- Initial thermalization time: t_i = τ_0 ≈ 0.5 fm/c (Section 2)
- Freeze-out temperature: T(t_life) = 200 MeV (Section 2)
- Initial temperature at RHIC(1): T = 0.55 GeV; at LHC(1): T = 0.82 GeV (Table 1)

## Context

This paper extends and corrects the work of Xu, Kharzeev, Satz and Wang (1996) on J/ψ suppression via gluonic dissociation in an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma. The corrections address three approximations in the earlier treatment: the replacement of gluon fugacity by unity in the denominator of the Bose-Einstein distribution, the evaluation of relative flux in the fireball frame while the cross section was in the ψ rest frame, and the neglect of J/ψ formation time. The results enable more accurate predictions of J/ψ survival probability at RHIC and LHC energies, and the authors note that transverse hydrodynamic expansion (not included here) is under investigation for a future publication. The work also relates to the competing mechanism of J/ψ enhancement via dynamical regeneration (Thews, year not specified in text).
