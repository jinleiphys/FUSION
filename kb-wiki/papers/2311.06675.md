# Dilepton production at next-to-leading order and intermediate invariant-mass observables

> The thermal QCD dilepton production rate is calculated at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling and at finite baryon chemical potential. The two-loop virtual photon self-energy is evaluated using finite temperature field theory and combined consistently with the self-energy in the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal regime. We present new results for a dense baryonic plasma. The rates are then integrated using (3+1)-dimensional fluid-dynamical simulations calibrated to reproduce hadronic experimental results obtained at RHIC at energies ranging from those of the Beam Energy Scan to $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 200$ GeV. We elaborate on the ability for dileptons to relay information about the plasma baryonic content and temperature.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of the thermal QCD dilepton production rate at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling that includes a finite baryon chemical potential, and then integrates these rates into (3+1)-dimensional fluid-dynamical simulations to predict observable dilepton yields at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. The authors emphasize that the presence of nonzero baryon chemical potential modifies quark and antiquark distributions and shifts the thermal masses controlling screening effects, but they restrict their analysis to equilibrium thermal rates and leave off-equilibrium corrections from viscosity and diffusion for future work.

## Method

The authors computed the two-loop virtual photon self-energy using finite-temperature field theory in D=4-2ε dimensions, evaluating the imaginary parts of master integrals generalized to nonzero baryon chemical potential. They combined these strict NLO results with resummed contributions in the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal regime using a matching procedure that subtracts the expanded LPM result to avoid double counting, then integrated the resulting rates using (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) with initial conditions from a Monte Carlo Glauber model, followed by hadronic afterburner simulations using UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass at finite chemical potential is given by \(m_D^2 = g^2 \left[ \left(\frac12 N_f + N_c\right) \frac{T^2}{3} + N_f \frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2} \right]\)
- The asymptotic quark mass at finite chemical potential is \(m_\infty^2 = g^2 \frac{C_F}{4} \left( T^2 + \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2} \right)\)
- The hydrodynamic evolution was run until a freeze-out energy density of \(e_{\rm fo} = 0.26\) GeV/fm\(^3\)
- The analysis focuses on the invariant mass region 1 GeV < M < 3 GeV where QGP manifestations are expected to be prevalent
- The hydrodynamical simulations were calibrated to reproduce hadronic experimental results at RHIC for beam energies from 7.7 GeV to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV
- At beam energy 7.7 GeV, a significant portion of fluid cells satisfies \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 3\) within \(|\eta_s| < 1\)

## Context

This work extends the NLO perturbative calculation of dilepton emission rates, previously established at zero baryon chemical potential by Jackson et al. (2019, 2022) and Bala et al. (2022), to nonzero baryon chemical potential relevant for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and future NA60+ experiments at CERN SPS. The authors build on the formalism for combining NLO and LPM contributions originally advocated by Ghisoiu et al. (2014), and on the hydrodynamic modeling framework of Denicol et al. (2018) and Du et al. (2023). The new rates enable more accurate interpretation of dilepton measurements from baryon-rich systems, allowing extraction of information about the plasma's baryonic content and temperature from the intermediate invariant-mass region.
