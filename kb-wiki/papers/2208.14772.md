# Extraction of the Coulomb Sum Rule, Transverse Enhancement, and Longitudinal Quenching from an Analysis of all Available e-$^{12}$C and e-$^{16}$O Cross Section Data

> We report on a phenomenological analysis of all available electron scattering data on ${\rm ^{12}C}$ (about 6600 differential cross section measurements) and on ${\rm ^{16}O}$ (about 250 measurements) within the framework of the quasielastic (QE) superscaling model (including Pauli blocking). All QE and inelastic cross section measurements are included down to the lowest momentum transfer $\bf q$ (including photo-production data). We find that there is enhancement of the transverse QE response function ($R_T^{QE}$) and quenching of the QE longitudinal response function ($R_L^{QE}$) at low $\bf q$ (in addition to Pauli blocking). We extract parameterizations of a $multiplicative$ low $\bf q$ "Longitudinal Quenching Factor" and an $additive$ "Transverse Enhancement" contribution. Additionally, we find that the excitation of nuclear states contribute significantly (up to 30\%) to the Coulomb Sum Rule $SL({\bf q})$. We extract the most accurate determination of $SL({\bf q})$ to date and find it to be in reasonable agreement with recent theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the most accurate determination of the Coulomb Sum Rule $SL({\bf q})$ to date for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, finding that nuclear state excitations contribute significantly (up to 30%) to the sum rule, and that the extracted $SL({\bf q})$ is in disagreement with random phase approximation (RPA) based calculations but in reasonable agreement with "First Principle Green's Function Monte Carlo" calculations.

## Method

The authors performed a phenomenological analysis of all available electron scattering data on $^{12}$C (about 6600 differential cross section measurements) and $^{16}$O (about 250 measurements) within the framework of the quasielastic (QE) superscaling model including Pauli blocking. They extracted parameterizations of a multiplicative low $\bf q$ "Longitudinal Quenching Factor" for the longitudinal QE response function $R_L^{QE}$ and an additive "Transverse Enhancement" contribution for the transverse QE response function $R_T^{QE}$, while also modeling the electro-excitation of nuclear states with excitation energies up to 55 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear excitations contribute up to 29% to $SL({\bf q})$ at $\bf q$ = 0.22 GeV for $^{12}$C
- The total contribution of all excitations to $SL({\bf q})$ is largest (approximately 0.29) at $\bf q$ = 0.22 GeV
- The Fermi momentum parameter $K_F$ = 0.228 GeV for $^{12}$C
- The Pauli suppression factor coefficients: $k_0$ = 0.3054, $k_1$ = 0.7647, $k_2$ = -0.2768, $k_3$ = 0.0328 for $x = {\bf q}/K_F$ less than 2.5
- The Longitudinal Quenching Factor $F^{L}_{quench}({\bf q})$ is unity for $x > 3.75$ and zero for $x < 0.35$
- The transverse enhancement parameterization uses $W \approx 0.88$ GeV and $W \approx 1.2$ GeV for the Gaussian centers
- The analysis includes QE data down to $Q^2$ = 0.01 GeV$^2$ ($\bf q$ = 0.1 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the 2012 fit by Bosted and Mamyan by including QE data at all values of $Q^2$ down to 0.01 GeV$^2$ and updating nuclear elastic and excitation form factors, superscaling parameters, and nucleon form factor parameterizations. The extracted Coulomb Sum Rule for $^{12}$C disagrees with the Cloet 2016 RPA calculation but agrees with the Lovato 2016 "First Principle Green's Function Monte Carlo" and Mihaila 2000 Coupled-Clusters calculations; for $^{16}$O it agrees with Sobczyk 2020 Coupled-Cluster and Mihaila 2000 calculations. The parameterizations enable improved modeling of cross sections in Monte Carlo event generators for both electron and neutrino scattering experiments.
