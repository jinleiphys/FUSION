# Equation of state of a hot-and-dense quark gluon plasma: lattice simulations at real $μ_B$ vs. extrapolations

> The equation of state of the quark gluon plasma is a key ingredient of heavy ion phenomenology. In addition to the traditional Taylor method, several novel approximation schemes have been proposed with the aim of calculating it at finite baryon density. In order to gain a pragmatic understanding of the limits of these schemes, we compare them to direct results at $μ_B>0$, using reweighting techniques free from an overlap problem. We use 2stout improved staggered fermions with 8 time-slices and cover the entire RHIC BES range in the baryochemical potential, up to $μ_B/T=3$.

## Key claim

The paper directly tests several extrapolation schemes for the QCD equation of state at finite baryon density against direct lattice results obtained via phase reweighting (which is free of an overlap problem) at real \(\mu_B>0\), finding that the Taylor expansion to order \(\hat{\mu}_B^8\) and the \(\hat{n}_L / \hat{n}_L^{\rm{SBL}}\) shifting resummation to order \(\lambda_4\) both accurately describe the data up to \(\hat{\mu}_B = 3\), while the exponential resummation scheme shows poor convergence above \(\hat{\mu}_B^2 \approx 4\).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using 2stout improved staggered fermions with physical quark masses on \(16^3 \times 8\) lattices (aspect ratio \(LT=2\)) at temperatures from 145 MeV to 240 MeV. They obtained direct results at finite baryon chemical potential (\(\mu_B/T\) up to 3) via phase reweighting from the phase quenched ensemble (finite isospin chemical potential), which avoids the overlap problem, and cross-checked these against reweighting from \(\mu_B=0\). They then compared these direct results to several extrapolation schemes: the Taylor expansion in \(\hat{\mu}_B\) up to order \(\hat{\mu}_B^8\), two sigmoid-shifting resummations (the \(\hat{n}_L/\hat{\mu}_B\) method and the \(\hat{n}_L/\hat{n}_L^{\rm{SBL}}\) method) fitted to imaginary chemical potential data, and the exponential resummation of the reweighting factor truncated at various orders.

## Key numbers

- Direct phase reweighting results cover the range \(0 \leq \hat{\mu}_B \leq 3\), which includes the entire RHIC Beam Energy Scan range.
- The phase factor expectation value \(\langle e^{i\theta} \rangle_{PQ}\) never drops below 0.1 in any ensemble and is always more than \(10\sigma\) away from zero.
- The Taylor expansion to order \(\hat{\mu}_B^4\) systematically underestimates \(n_L\) below 150 MeV and overestimates it above 150 MeV.
- Including the \(p_6\) term, the Taylor method agrees with direct data up to \(\hat{\mu}_B=3\) at \(T=160\) MeV and up to \(\hat{\mu}_B \approx 1.2\) at \(T=170\) MeV.
- Including the \(p_8\) term, the Taylor expansion agrees with direct data at all studied temperatures up to \(\hat{\mu}_B = 3\).
- The exponential resummation scheme shows bad convergence from \(\hat{\mu}_B^2 \approx 4\) for all temperatures.
- The \(\hat{n}_L / \hat{n}_L^{\rm{SBL}}\) shifting method to order \(\lambda_4\) accurately describes the direct data in the range \(0 \leq \hat{\mu}_B \leq 3\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the equation of state at finite baryon density, which have relied on extrapolations from zero or imaginary chemical potential (e.g., Borsanyi et al. (2012), Bazavov et al. (2017), Borsanyi et al. (2018)). It directly tests the convergence of the Taylor expansion and several recently proposed resummation schemes (the sigmoid-shifting methods of Borsanyi et al. (2021, 2022) and the exponential resummation of Mondal et al. (2021)) against reliable direct results. The findings enable phenomenological applications in heavy ion collisions by establishing the range of validity of these approximation schemes up to \(\hat{\mu}_B = 3\), and point toward the need to extend such direct calculations to lower temperatures and higher chemical potentials to study the transition line and the conjectured critical endpoint.
