# Double strangeness $Ξ^{-}$ production as a probe of nuclear equation of state at high densities

> Double strangeness $Ξ^{-}$ production in Au+Au collisions at 2, 4, and 6 GeV/nucleon incident beam energies is studied with the pure hadron cascade version of a multi-phase transport model. It is found that due to larger nuclear compression, the model with the soft equation of state (EoS) gives larger yields of both single strangeness ($K^{+}$ and $Λ+Σ^{0}$) and double strangeness $Ξ^{-}$. The sensitivity of the double strangeness $Ξ^{-}$ to the EoS is evidently larger than that of $K^{+}$ or $Λ+Σ^{0}$ since the phase-space distribution of produced $Ξ^{-}$ is more compact compared to those of the single strangeness. The larger sensitivity of the yields ratio of $Ξ^{-}$ to the EoS from heavy and light systems is kept compared to that of the single strangeness. The study of $Ξ^{-}$ production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions provides an alternative for the ongoing heavy-ion collision program at facilities worldwide for identifying the EoS at high densities, which is relevant to the investigation of the phase boundary and onset of deconfinement of dense nuclear matter.

# Double strangeness Ξ⁻ production as a probe of nuclear equation of state at high densities

## Key claim

The double strangeness Ξ⁻ particle, produced mainly via multi-step collisions of two single strangeness hadrons in the high-density region of heavy-ion collisions, shows 3–4 times greater sensitivity to the nuclear equation of state (EoS) than single strangeness particles like K⁺ or Λ+Σ⁰, but this enhanced sensitivity is demonstrated only within the pure hadron cascade version of the AMPT model and depends on the assumption that the mean field coupling to strange hadrons does not significantly affect the result.

## Method

The authors used the pure hadron cascade version of a multi-phase transport (AMPT-HC) model, which includes hadronic mean-field potentials for nucleons, baryonic resonances, and strange hadrons, with the nucleon potential parameterized by a density-dependent form using soft (k₀ = 200 MeV) and stiff (k₀ = 300 MeV) equations of state. They added strangeness exchange reactions (K̄ + Y ↔ π + Ξ), isospin-averaged Y + Y ↔ N + Ξ reactions, and Y + N → N + Ξ + K channels to model Ξ⁻ production, then simulated central Au+Au and C+C collisions at beam energies of 2, 4, and 6 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- At 2 GeV/nucleon in central Au+Au collisions, the maximum sensitivity to the EoS (measured as the increase of the pₜ-differential yield) is 24% for K⁺, 32% for Λ+Σ⁰, and 100% for Ξ⁻.
- The sensitivity of double strangeness Ξ⁻ production to the EoS is about 3–4 times that of single strangeness Λ+Σ⁰ or K⁺ at 2 GeV/nucleon.
- The ratio of Ξ⁻ yields from Au+Au over C+C systems shows 35–37% sensitivity to the EoS across 2 to 6 GeV/nucleon, compared to 20–14% for K⁺ and 20–11% for Λ+Σ⁰.
- The fraction of Ξ⁻ in the central cell (r ≤ 2 fm) of the overlap volume is usually more than twice the fraction of K⁺ or Λ+Σ⁰ at 2 GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends prior studies connecting single strangeness kaon production to the nuclear EoS (Fuchs 2001, Fuchs 2006, Di Toro) and builds on experimental measurements of Ξ⁻ production in heavy-ion collisions over the last twenty years (Exp 2003, Exp 2009, Exp 2015). The study enables the use of double strangeness Ξ⁻ yields and their ratios from heavy and light collision systems as an alternative probe for the high-density EoS, relevant to ongoing programs at CBM and HADES at GSI/FAIR, BM@N and MPD at NICA, CEE at HIAF, J-PARC-HI, and STAR at RHIC BES-II.
