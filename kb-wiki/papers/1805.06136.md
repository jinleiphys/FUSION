# Isoscalar neutron-proton pairing and SU(4)-symmetry breaking in Gamow-Teller transitions

> The isoscalar neutron-proton pairing is thought to be important for nuclei with equal number of protons and neutrons but its manifestation in structure properties remains to be understood. We investigate the Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions for the f7/2-shell nuclei in large-scale shell-model calculations with the realistic Hamiltonian. We show that the isoscalar T=0, J=1+ neutron-proton pairing interaction plays a decisive role for the concentration of GT strengths at the first-excited 1+ state in 42Sc, and that the suppression of these strengths in 46V, 50Mn, and 54Co is mainly caused by the spin-orbit force supplemented by the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. Based on the good reproduction of the charge-exchange reaction data, we further analyze the interplay between the isoscalar and isovector pairing correlations. We conclude that even for the most promising A=42 nuclei where the SU(4) isoscalar-isovector-pairing symmetry is less broken, the probability of forming an isoscalar neutron-proton pairing condensation is less than 60% as compared to the expectation at the SU(4)-symmetry limit.

## Key claim

The isoscalar T=0, J=1 neutron-proton pairing interaction is essential for the concentration of Gamow-Teller strength at the first-excited 1+ state in 42Sc, but even in this most favorable case the probability of forming an isoscalar neutron-proton pairing condensate is less than 60% of the SU(4)-symmetry limit.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for f7/2-shell nuclei (A=42-54) using the realistic PMMU Hamiltonian, which includes isovector J=0 and J=2 pairing, isoscalar J=1 pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and monopole interactions. The calculations used the pf-shell model space with a spin-orbit splitting of 6.5 MeV between f7/2 and f5/2, and a quenching factor q=0.74 for GT strengths. The authors also constructed a simplified isoscalar-isovector-pairing Hamiltonian with a control parameter x to study the competition between pairing phases.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar pairing strength g1^0 = 12.5 MeV reproduces the B(GT) data and lowest 1_1+ excitation energy in 42Sc
- The SU(4) symmetry limit corresponds to a constant GT strength B(GT) = 6q^2 ≈ 3.29 for all A=42-54
- For A=54, about 2/3 of the SU(4) value is suppressed by the spin-orbit force
- The critical point for ground-state inversion between 0_1+ and 1_1+ occurs at x_c = 0.17 for 42Sc and around x_c = 0.20 for the other nuclei
- At x=0 (SU(4) symmetry limit), the overlap of the isoscalar condensation state with the 1_1+ state of 42Sc is |⟨1_1+|P10⟩|^2 = 0.57 (57%)
- The overlap |⟨0_1+|P01⟩|^2 = 0.99 for the isovector condensation state in 42Sc at x=0
- The lowest 1_1+ excitation energy in 42Sc is 0.611 MeV experimentally

## Context

This work extends the experimental observations of Fujita et al. (2014, 2015) on low-energy super GT states in 42Sc and the systematic GT strength distributions across f7/2-shell N=Z nuclei. It confirms the earlier conclusion of Bertsch (2010) that spin-orbit splitting suppresses GT strengths and breaks SU(4) symmetry, while also showing that the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction is largely responsible for the small B(GT) values in 46V and 50Mn. The results enable future application of realistic shell-model calculations to SU(4) symmetry in double-beta decay, as discussed by Ferreira (2017).
