# Narrowing the Gap Between Theory and Evaluations: Angular Momentum Distributions in Fission Fragments

> We present a microscopic framework for predicting angular momentum distributions over the full range of fission fragment masses and charges. For the neutron-induced fission of $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu, the obtained distributions exhibit a pronounced sawtooth pattern in average values, reveal a substantial isobaric dependence, and reproduce experimental photon multiplicities without adjustable parameters. These results demonstrate that microscopic theory is gradually becoming quantitatively competitive with phenomenological models.

## Key claim

Microscopic theory can now predict angular momentum distributions across the full range of fission fragment masses and charges for neutron-induced fission of 235U and 239Pu, reproducing experimental photon multiplicities without adjustable parameters, though the model disregards K ≠ 0 rotational components and neglects intrinsic excitations of scission configurations beyond nuclear deformation.

## Method

The authors used the HFBTHO computational framework with Skyrme energy density functionals (SkM* EDF) and a mixed volume-surface contact pairing force to generate scission configurations in the even-even compound systems 236U* and 240Pu*. They applied projection techniques to extract angular momentum, neutron number, and proton number distributions for each fission fragment, then weighted these by configuration probabilities obtained from the time-dependent generator coordinate method with the Gaussian overlap approximation (TDGCM+GOA) using the FELIX package. The final distributions were used as inputs to the CGMF statistical reaction code to compute photon multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- For 236U* at En = 1 MeV, angular momentum distributions were calculated for 381 even-even fission fragments; for 240Pu* at En = 1 MeV, distributions were calculated for 412 even-even fission fragments
- The 1σ spread within isobaric chains near the most probable fragmentation (AH ≈ 136-140) can be as large as ±3ℏ
- Total photon multiplicity dropped from 6.28 (phenomenological) to 6.08 (microscopic), a difference within typical experimental margin of error
- Neutron multiplicities dropped from 2.86 to 2.80 when using microscopic distributions
- The model considered 384 scission configurations in 236U* and 404 in 240Pu*, yielding 1444 and 1601 fission fragments respectively

## Context

This work extends recent theoretical studies of fission fragment angular momentum by Bulgac (2021), Marević (2021), Scamps (2022), Randrup (2021), and Stetcu (2021), which were largely focused on a narrow range of fragment masses around the most probable fragmentation. The results provide microscopic angular momentum distributions that can replace the simple phenomenological models currently used in statistical reaction theory codes such as CGMF (Talou 2021), FREYA (Verbeke 2018), and FIFRELIN (Litaize 2015), potentially improving predictive power for nuclei where experimental data are scarce.
