# Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models on a 3D mesh: II. Time-reversal symmetry breaking

> Models based on nuclear energy density functionals can provide access to a multitude of observables for thousands of nuclei in a single framework with microscopic foundations. Such models can rival the accuracy of more phenomenological approaches, but doing so requires adjusting parameters to thousands of nuclear masses. To keep such large-scale fits feasible, several symmetry restrictions are generally imposed on the nuclear configurations. One such example is time-reversal invariance, which is generally enforced via the Equal Filling Approximation (EFA). Here we lift this assumption, enabling us to access the spin and current densities in the ground states of odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei and which contribute to the total energy of such nuclei through so-called "time-odd" terms. We present here the Skyrme-based BSkG2 model whose parameters were adjusted to essentially all known nuclear masses without relying on the EFA, refining our earlier work [G. Scamps et al., EPJA 57, 333 (2021), arXiv:2011.07904]. Moving beyond ground state properties, we also incorporated information on the fission barriers of actinide nuclei in the parameter adjustment. The resulting model achieves a root-mean-square (rms) deviation of (i) 0.678 MeV on 2457 known masses, (ii) 0.027 fm on 884 measured charge radii, (iii) 0.44 MeV and 0.47 MeV, respectively, on 45 reference values for primary and secondary fission barriers of actinide nuclei, and (iv) 0.49 MeV on 28 fission isomer excitation energies. We limit ourselves here to a description of the model and the study the impact of lifting the EFA on ground state properties such as binding energies, deformation and pairing, deferring a detailed discussion of fission to a forthcoming paper.

## Key claim

The BSkG2 model achieves a global description of nuclear masses, charge radii, and fission barriers without relying on the Equal Filling Approximation (EFA) for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, but the authors note that the vibrational correction term crucial for fission barrier accuracy was adjusted separately and that a detailed discussion of fission is deferred to a forthcoming paper.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a three-dimensional Cartesian Lagrange mesh using the MOCCa code, lifting the assumption of time-reversal invariance to allow non-zero spin and current densities in odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei. They imposed three plane-reflection symmetries (z-signature, y-time-simplex, and parity) but not time-reversal symmetry, and included a phenomenological vibrational correction term with three adjustable parameters to control fission barrier properties. The model parameters were adjusted to essentially all known nuclear masses and to reference values for fission barriers of actinide nuclei, with the time-odd coupling constants for spin terms left as free parameters while current and time-odd spin-orbit terms were fixed by Galilean invariance.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square (rms) deviation of 0.678 MeV on 2457 known nuclear masses
- rms deviation of 0.027 fm on 884 measured charge radii
- rms deviation of 0.44 MeV on 45 reference values for primary fission barriers of actinide nuclei
- rms deviation of 0.47 MeV on 45 reference values for secondary fission barriers of actinide nuclei
- rms deviation of 0.49 MeV on 28 fission isomer excitation energies

## Context

This work extends the Brussels-Montréal (BSk) family of Skyrme energy density functional models, specifically refining the earlier BSkG1 model of Scamps et al. (2021) by lifting the Equal Filling Approximation and incorporating fission barrier data into the parameter adjustment. Unlike most prior global calculations that employ the EFA to avoid time-odd terms (as in the work of Pototzky et al. 2010 and Afanasjev et al. 2010), BSkG2 provides a consistent treatment of time-reversal symmetry breaking for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei. The model enables future studies of fission dynamics and nuclear structure for nuclei far from stability, with the authors noting that a detailed analysis of fission properties will be presented in a separate publication.
