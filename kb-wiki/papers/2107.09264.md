# Study on higher moments of net-charge multiplicity distributions using a multiphase transport model

> The moments and moment products of conserved charges are believed to be sensitive to critical fluctuations, which have been adopted in determining the QCD critical point. Using a dynamical multiphase transport model, we reproduce the centrality and energy dependences of moments and moment products of net-charge multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions measured by the Beam Energy Scan program at the RHIC. No non-monotonic energy dependence is observed. We infer that the moment products develop during the dynamical evolution of heavy-ion collisions. The observed difference based on the expectation of the Poisson baseline indicates a positive two-particle correlation between positively and negatively charged particles, which can arise from different dynamical processes at different stages. Therefore, to adopt moments and moment products of net-charge multiplicity distributions in determining the QCD critical point of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, it is essential to take the dynamical evolution.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with strict charge conservation, the authors reproduce the centrality and energy dependences of net-charge moments measured by STAR, but find no non-monotonic energy dependence in any moment product. They argue that the observed deviations from a Poisson baseline arise from positive two-particle correlations between positively and negatively charged particles that develop dynamically across different evolution stages, implying that dynamical evolution must be accounted for before moments can be used to locate the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors simulate Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies (7.7–200 GeV) using the string-melting version of the AMPT model, which includes initial conditions from HIJING, parton cascade with a fixed 3 mb cross section, quark coalescence hadronization, and hadronic rescatterings with strict electric-charge conservation. They compute the mean (M), standard deviation (σ), skewness (S), and kurtosis (κ) of the net-charge multiplicity distribution for charged particles with 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and |η| < 0.5, applying centrality-bin-width correction (CBWC) and centrality-resolution correction (CRC) analogous to the experimental procedure of Luo (2011, 2013). They also isolate the moment products at four evolution stages (initial, after parton cascade, after coalescence, after hadronic rescatterings) and compare them to a Poisson baseline to extract correlation contributions.

## Key numbers

- Seven beam energies simulated: √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV.
- For most central (0–5%) collisions, σ²/M shows an exponential increase with decreasing beam energy; Sσ decreases with increasing energy; κσ² is approximately constant at unity across all energies.
- For peripheral (70–80%) collisions, σ²/M is larger than for central collisions at the same energy.
- After CBWC, Sσ values are systematically smaller than without CBWC; the difference is largest at low energies (e.g., 7.7 GeV) and diminishes at high energies (200 GeV).
- κσ² with CBWC is approximately unity (within statistical errors) for all centralities and energies.
- The difference ΔK₂ = K₂^(Poisson) – K₂^(netq) is always positive, indicating a positive two-particle correlation between positively and negatively charged particles at all stages.
- At 7.7 GeV, Sσ shows a clear stage evolution: it decreases after parton cascade, increases after coalescence, and decreases again after hadronic rescatterings; at 200 GeV, Sσ remains near zero at all stages.

## Context

This work extends the STAR measurements of net-charge moments (Adamczyk, 2014) by providing a dynamical transport-model interpretation, complementing static thermal approaches such as lattice QCD and the hadron resonance gas model (Braun-Munzinger, 2003; Garg, 2013; Mishra, 2016). The finding that moment products develop through parton cascade, coalescence, and hadronic rescatterings, and that resonance decays in the hadronic phase are a major source of the positive two-particle correlation, implies that static critical-point searches must be augmented with dynamical evolution models. The study also clarifies the role of centrality-bin-width and centrality-resolution corrections, which are most important at low energies and for peripheral collisions.
