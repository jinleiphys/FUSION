# Gauging the three-nucleon system

> We show how to attach an external photon to a system of three strongly interacting nucleons described by three-body scattering equations. Our method involves the idea of gauging the scattering equations themselves, and results in electromagnetic amplitudes where the external photon is effectively coupled to every part of every strong interaction diagram in the model. Current conservation is therefore implemented in the theoretically correct fashion. In this way we obtain the expressions needed to calculate all possible electromagnetic processes of the three-nucleon system including the electromagnetic form factors of the three-body bound state, pd -> pd gamma, gamma He3 -> pd, gamma He3 -> ppn, etc. As the photon is coupled everywhere in the strong interaction model, a unified description of the NNN-gamma NNN system is obtained. An interesting aspect of our results is the natural appearance of subtraction terms needed to avoid the overcounting of diagrams. We have also applied the gauging of equations method to the four-dimensional description of the piNN system which, because of the possibility of pion absorption, has overcounting problems all of its own, and is therefore much more complicated than the NNN case discussed here. Despite these complications, the gauging procedure itself is very simple and leads easily to a unified description of the piNN-gamma piNN system.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that attaching an external photon to a three-nucleon system can be achieved by "gauging" the scattering equations themselves, yielding electromagnetic amplitudes where the photon couples to every part of every strong interaction diagram. The method naturally produces subtraction terms that prevent overcounting of diagrams, a problem that the authors claim was present in earlier four-dimensional calculations by Rupp and Tjon (1988, 1992).

## Method

The authors work within the covariant framework of quantum field theory and start from the three-nucleon Green function expressed in terms of its disconnected part and the kernel. They apply a "gauging of equations" procedure to the Green function equation and to the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for three identical nucleons, obtaining expressions for the gauged Green function and the gauged AGS amplitude. The input to the AGS formulation is given in terms of two-body t-matrices and their gauged counterparts, avoiding the need for explicit two-body potentials.

## Key numbers

- The three-nucleon electromagnetic vertex function is given by $\Gamma^\mu=\sum_{i=1}^3 \left(\Gamma_i^\mu D_{0i}^{-1}+v_i^\mu d_i^{-1} -v_i\Gamma_i^\mu \right)$, where the subtraction term $-v_i\Gamma_i^\mu$ naturally prevents overcounting.
- The gauged AGS Green function for three identical particles is expressed as $\tZ^\mu=\tZ d_3^{-1}d_3^\mu +\tZ\left( D_{03}^{-1}D_{03}^\mu D_{03}^{-1}d_3^{-1} +d_3^{-1}t_3^\mu{\cal P}\right) \tZ$.

## Context

This work extends the gauging of equations method previously applied by the authors to hadronic processes (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1995, unpublished) to the three-nucleon system. It provides a unified framework for calculating all electromagnetic processes of the three-nucleon system, including form factors of the three-body bound state and reactions such as pd -> pd gamma and gamma He3 -> pd. The authors note that earlier four-dimensional calculations of the three-nucleon system by Rupp and Tjon (1988, 1992) contained overcounting because they lacked the subtraction terms that emerge naturally from the gauging procedure.
