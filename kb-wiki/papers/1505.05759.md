# Electroproduction of the $N^\ast(1535)$ nucleon resonance in QCD

> Following the 12 GeV upgrade, a dedicated experiment is planned with the Hall B CLAS12 detector at Jefferson Lab, with the aim to study electroproduction of nucleon resonances at high photon virtualities up to $Q^2 = 12$ GeV$^2$. In this work we present a QCD-based approach to the theoretical interpretation of these upcoming results in the framework of light-cone sum rules that combine perturbative calculations with dispersion relations and duality. The form factors are thus expressed in terms of $N^\ast(1535)$ light-front wave functions at small transverse separations, called distribution amplitudes. The distribution amplitudes can therefore be determined from the comparison with the experimental data on form factors and compared to the results of lattice QCD simulations. The results of the corresponding next-to-leading order calculation are presented and compared with the existing data. We find that the form factors are dominated by the twist-four distribution amplitudes that are related to the $P$-wave three-quark wave functions of the $N^\ast(1535)$, i.e. to contributions of orbital angular momentum.

## Key claim

The authors present a QCD-based approach using light-cone sum rules (LCSRs) to interpret upcoming experimental data on electroproduction of the N*(1535) resonance at high photon virtualities, finding that the form factors are dominated by twist-four distribution amplitudes related to P-wave three-quark wave functions (orbital angular momentum contributions), rather than the leading-twist contributions that dominate in simpler perturbative QCD factorization.

## Method

The authors calculate the correlation function of the electromagnetic current with a nucleon interpolating current between the N*(1535) state and vacuum, then match the operator product expansion (OPE) representation to a dispersion relation with quark-hadron duality. The form factors G1(Q2) and G2(Q2) are expressed in terms of N*(1535) distribution amplitudes (DAs) of increasing twist, using next-to-leading order (NLO) coefficient functions for twist-three and twist-four DAs and leading order for twist-five and twist-six, with the same coefficient functions as for the nucleon but with the N* mass replacing the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- The planned experiment at Jefferson Lab with CLAS12 detector will study electroproduction at photon virtualities up to Q2 = 12 GeV2
- The continuum threshold is set at s0 = (1.5 GeV)2, Borel parameter M2 = 2 GeV2, and factorization scale μF2 = 2 GeV2
- The ratio of twist-three and twist-four couplings fN*/λ1N* = 0.027(2) from lattice calculations (Braun 2014)
- The ratio of normalization constants λ1N/λ1N* = 0.633 from lattice calculations (Braun 2014)
- The dipole formula parameter a = 0.71 GeV2 used for normalization
- The LCSR fit parameters for twist-four DAs: η10 = 0.00, η11 = 0.94 (fit to form factors); η10 = -0.29, η11 = 0.23 (fit to helicity amplitudes)
- The lattice results for leading-twist shape parameters at μ2 = 2 GeV2: φ10 = 0.28(12), φ11 = -0.86(10), φ20 = 1.7(14), φ21 = -2.0(18), φ22 = 1.7(26)
- The LCSR is reliable for Q2 ≥ 2 GeV2; mass corrections become very large below this value

## Context

This work extends the LCSR approach previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Braun 2001, Braun 2006, Anikin 2013) to the N*(1535) resonance, motivated by the planned 12 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Lab and the CLAS12 experiment (Aznauryan 2012). The approach provides a direct relation between form factors and distribution amplitudes that can be compared with lattice QCD results (Braun 2014) and enables extraction of quark distribution information for nucleon resonances, going beyond the mass spectrum that is typically the focus of lattice calculations. The method can be extended to other spin-1/2 resonances of both parities, including the N*(1650) and the Roper resonance.
