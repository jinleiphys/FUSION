# Plasma Instabilities in an Anisotropically Expanding Geometry

> We study (3+1)D kinetic (Boltzmann-Vlasov) equations for relativistic plasma particles in a one-dimensionally expanding geometry motivated by ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We set up local equations in terms of Yang-Mills potentials and auxiliary fields that allow simulations of hard-(expanding)-loop (HEL) dynamics on a lattice. We determine numerically the evolution of plasma instabilities in the linear (Abelian) regime and also derive their late-time behavior analytically, which is consistent with recent numerical results on the evolution of the so-called melting color-glass condensate. We also find a significant delay in the onset of growth of plasma instabilities which are triggered by small rapidity fluctuations, even when the initial state is highly anisotropic.

# Plasma Instabilities in an Anisotropically Expanding Geometry

## Key claim

Plasma instabilities in a one-dimensionally expanding geometry (motivated by heavy-ion collisions) overcome the diluting effects of expansion and grow exponentially at late times, but their onset is significantly delayed when triggered by small rapidity fluctuations, even for highly anisotropic initial states, such that for realistic RHIC parameters the instability may not become important unless initial parton densities are much larger than currently assumed.

## Method

The authors generalize the anisotropic hard-loop effective theory to a boost-invariantly expanding background by transforming the Boltzmann-Vlasov and Yang-Mills equations to proper-time/rapidity coordinates. They introduce auxiliary fields W_α that depend only on velocity (not momentum scale) and derive integro-differential equations for the linear (Abelian) regime. These are solved both analytically at late times via expansion around τ' = τ and numerically using a leap-frog algorithm with discretized memory integrals, for modes with longitudinal wave number ν in the gauge A^τ = 0.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass parameter μ = (1/8) m_D^2 π τ_iso, where m_D is the isotropic Debye mass.
- For a purely gluonic system with saturation scale Q_s, μ/Q_s ≈ 0.182 for gluon liberation factor c = 0.5, and ≈ 0.505 for c = 2 ln 2.
- At late times, transverse gauge fields grow as Ã^i(τ) ~ τ^{1/4} exp(2 √(μ τ)) for large ν ≫ 1.
- For τ_0/τ_iso = 100 (high initial anisotropy, ξ ~ 10^4) and c = 0.5, the time τ_10 when the first mode grows by a factor of 10 is τ_10/τ_0 = 50.
- For τ_0/τ_iso = 100 and c = 2 ln 2, τ_10/τ_0 = 21.
- For the isotropic initial case τ_0/τ_iso = 1, τ_10/τ_0 = 260 (c = 0.5) and 95 (c = 2 ln 2).
- For τ_0/τ_iso = 1000, τ_10/τ_0 = 49 (c = 0.5) and 20 (c = 2 ln 2).
- Ideal-hydrodynamic fits to RHIC data indicate plasma lifetime less than 5 fm/c ≈ 30 τ_0; LHC plasma lifetimes might exceed 7 fm/c ≈ 100 τ_0.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hard-loop effective theory of Rebhan (2004) and Arnold (2005) from stationary plasmas to a longitudinally expanding (boost-invariant) geometry, building on the Kasner-type metric used in earlier classical Yang-Mills simulations by Romatschke (2005). The late-time analytic result Ã^i ~ τ^{1/4} exp(2√(μτ)) is consistent with numerical simulations of the melting color-glass condensate by Romatschke (2005). The paper prepares the ground for full nonlinear lattice simulations of non-Abelian plasma instabilities with expansion, which would address the fate of the energy cascade found in stationary hard-loop simulations by Arnold (2005). The numerical results suggest that plasma instabilities from small rapidity fluctuations may be relevant at LHC but marginal at RHIC unless the gluon liberation factor is significantly larger than the value c ≈ 0.5 from Krasnitz (1998).
