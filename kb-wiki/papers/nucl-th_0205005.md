# Control of Quantum Systems

> A quantum system subject to external fields is said to be controllable if these fields can be adjusted to guide the state vector to a desired destination in the state space of the system. Fundamental results on controllability are reviewed against the background of recent ideas and advances in two seemingly disparate endeavors: (i) laser control of chemical reactions and (ii) quantum computation. Using Lie-algebraic methods, sufficient conditions have been derived for global controllability on a finite-dimensional manifold of an infinite-dimensional Hilbert space, in the case that the Hamiltonian and control operators, possibly unbounded, possess a common dense domain of analytic vectors. Some simple examples are presented. A synergism between quantum control and quantum computation is creating a host of exciting new opportunities for both activities. The impact of these developments on computational many-body theory could be profound.

## Key claim

The paper reviews sufficient conditions for global controllability of a quantum system on a finite-dimensional manifold of an infinite-dimensional Hilbert space, but only when the Hamiltonian and control operators possess a common dense domain of analytic vectors; the authors note that complete controllability on the full unit sphere is not attainable if the Lie algebra generated by the operators has finite dimension.

## Method

The authors employ Lie-algebraic methods from classical bilinear control theory, analyzing the skew-Hermitian counterparts of the drift Hamiltonian and control operators. They define three Lie algebras (A, B, C) from these operators and their commutators, and apply Nelson's Theorem to guarantee the existence of an analytic domain. The HTC Corollary 1 provides sufficient conditions for strong analytic controllability on a finite-dimensional manifold M, requiring that the Lie algebra B be an ideal in C and that the tangent space associated with C have constant finite dimension.

## Key numbers

- The Lie algebra A generated by the operators has dimension d, which is finite; the system is not analytically controllable on the full unit sphere if d is finite (HTC Corollary 2, No-Go Theorem).
- For the harmonic oscillator example, dim A(phi) = dim B(phi) = dim C(phi) = d = 3 for all phi in the analytic domain.
- For the rigid rotor example, the system evolves on a (2J+1)-dimensional manifold, where J is a non-negative integer and M_J ranges from -J to +J.

## Context

This work extends classical controllability results from systems engineering (Chow, Sussmann, Brockett) to the quantum domain, building on earlier efforts by Huang, Tarn, and Clark (HTC). It connects two previously disparate fields: laser control of chemical reactions (Brumer, Rabitz, Rice) and quantum computation (Lloyd, Deutsch, DiVincenzo). The paper enables further development of tailored controllability concepts for technologically important problems, including quantum computation over continuous variables as explored by Lloyd and Braunstein, and opens the door for computational many-body theory to contribute to the design of control fields.
