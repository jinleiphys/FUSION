# Hadronic structure on the light-front IV: Heavy and light Baryons

> This work is a continuation in our series of papers, that addresses quark models of hadronic structure on the light front. The chief focus of this paper is the quantum-mechanical solution of the three-quark model Hamiltonian, describing baryons. In Jacobi coordinates, we use a harmonic oscillator basis for the transverse directions. For the longitudinal momentum fractions $x_i$, the pertinent basis follows from quantum mechanics in a "triangular cup" potential, which we solve exactly. We calculate the masses and light front wave functions for the flavor symmetric $\frac 32^+$ baryons $bbb,ccc,sss,uuu$.

## Key claim

This paper presents a quantum-mechanical solution of the three-quark model Hamiltonian on the light front for flavor-symmetric baryons, but the authors note that the string-based Y and V models over-predict the three-quark forces at large distances, while the so-called "Ansatz A" (with half the binary string interaction) reproduces the forces for an equilateral triangle, though this agreement is crude and does not hold for all geometries.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Hamiltonian for three-quark systems using Jacobi coordinates to eliminate spurious center-of-mass motion, employing a harmonic oscillator basis for the transverse directions and an exact solution of quantum mechanics in a "triangular cup" potential for the longitudinal momentum fractions. They compute static three-quark potentials from a dense instanton liquid model and compare them to lattice data from Koma (2017) and to predictions from three string models (Y-model, V-model, and Ansatz A). The confining part of the Hamiltonian is treated using an einbein trick to linearize the square-root kinetic term, reducing the problem to a six-dimensional spherical oscillator.

## Key numbers

- For the equilateral triangle geometry, the Y-model predicts a force of dV/dx = sqrt(6)*sigma, the V-model predicts sqrt(8)*sigma, and the A-model predicts (3/2)*sqrt(2)*sigma; the lattice data give a ratio sigma/sigma_Y of approximately 0.854, while sigma/sigma_A is approximately 1.
- The instanton size used is rho = 1/3 fm, with density parameter kappa = pi^2 n_{I+anti-I} rho^4 = 1 (dense instanton liquid).
- The empirical string tension used is sigma = (0.4 GeV)^2, deduced from the Regge slope alpha-prime.
- For the Y-model, the rescaling factor between model A and model Y string tensions is sqrt(3)/2, approximately 0.866.
- The lattice site size in the Koma (2017) data is a = 0.123 fm.

## Context

This work is the fourth in a series by Shuryak and Zahed (2021-2022) that addresses quark models of hadronic structure on the light front, extending their previous treatment of mesons to baryons. It compares three-quark static potentials from the instanton liquid model to lattice simulations by Koma (2017) and to earlier non-relativistic constituent quark models such as that of Isgur and Karl (1979) and its update for heavy quarks (2007). The paper enables future calculations of flavor-asymmetric baryons with diquark correlations and provides a framework for computing light-front wave functions that can be used to study partonic observables and sea antiquark distributions.
