# Baryon Stopping in Heavy-Ion Collisions at E_{lab}= 2--160 GeV/nucleon

> It is argued that the experimentally observed baryon stopping may indicate (within the present experimental uncertainties) a non-monotonous behaviour as a function of the incident energy of colliding nuclei. This can be quantified by a midrapidity reduced curvature of the net-proton rapidity spectrum. The above non-monotonous behaviour reveals itself as a "zig-zag" irregularity in the excitation function of this curvature. The three-fluid dynamic calculations with a hadronic equation of state (EoS) fail to reproduce this irregularity. At the same time, the same calculations with an EoS involving a first-order phase transition into the quark-gluon phase do reproduce this "zig-zag" behaviour, however only qualitatively.

# Baryon Stopping in Heavy-Ion Collisions at E_lab = 2–160 GeV/nucleon

## Key claim
The experimentally observed baryon stopping may indicate a non-monotonous behaviour as a function of incident energy, quantified by a midrapidity reduced curvature of the net-proton rapidity spectrum, but only within the present experimental uncertainties. Three-fluid dynamic calculations with an equation of state involving a first-order phase transition into the quark-gluon phase reproduce this "zig-zag" irregularity only qualitatively, while purely hadronic calculations fail.

## Method
The author analyzed existing experimental data on proton (AGS) and net-proton (SPS) rapidity distributions from central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, fitting them with a two-source thermal model to extract fit parameters. The midrapidity reduced curvature C_y was computed from these fits and compared to predictions from three-fluid dynamics (3FD) simulations using both a hadronic equation of state and one with a first-order phase transition into the quark-gluon phase (Gibbs construction).

## Key numbers
- Incident energy range: E_lab = 2–160 GeV/nucleon (center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) from approximately 2.7 to 17.3 GeV)
- The biased fit for 80A GeV data gave a normalization of 330 net-protons, and for 158A GeV data gave 400 net-protons, both exceeding the total proton number in colliding nuclei (=164)
- For central (5%) Au+Au collisions at AGS energies, calculations used impact parameter b = 2 fm; for central (5%) Pb+Pb at E_lab = 158A GeV, b = 2.4 fm; for central (7%) Pb+Pb at 20A–80A GeV, b = 3 fm
- The difference between C_y values at 10A GeV and 20A GeV is two to three times larger than the methodical uncertainty estimated from two different experiments at 10A GeV
- The 3FD calculation without coalescence (fragment production) reveals a monotonous decrease of y_cm (dN/dy)_cm beginning from sqrt(s) = 2.7 GeV
- Parton-parton cross sections in the quark-gluon phase are approximately twice as large as those in the hadronic phase

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of baryon stopping within the three-fluid dynamics model (Ivanov et al., 2005; 2007) using updated NA49 experimental data at SPS energies. The observed "zig-zag" irregularity in the curvature excitation function coincides with previously observed anomalies for other hadron-production properties at low SPS energies (Gazdzicki, 1998; Alt, 2007). The failure of the first-order phase transition scenario to quantitatively reproduce the data suggests that either the Gibbs and Maxwell constructions are inappropriate for the fast dynamics of heavy-ion collisions (Randrup, 2009; Voskresensky, 2009) or the phase transition is not of first order. The results motivate further experimental measurements at FAIR (GSI) and NICA (Dubna), as well as from the low-energy-scan program at RHIC.
