# Ab Initio Theory of Light-ion Reactions

> The exact treatment of nuclei starting from the constituent nucleons and the fundamental interactions among them has been a long-standing goal in nuclear physics. Above all nuclear scattering and reactions, which require the solution of the many-body quantum-mechanical problem in the continuum, represent a theoretical and computational challenge for ab initio approaches. After a brief overview of the field, we present a new ab initio many-body approach capable of describing simultaneously both bound and scattering states in light nuclei. By combining the resonating-group method with the ab initio no-core shell model, we complement a microscopic cluster technique with the use of realistic interactions and a microscopic and consistent description of the clusters. We show results for neutron and proton scattering on light nuclei, including p-7Be and n-8He. We also highlight the first results of the d-3He and d-3H fusion calculations obtained within this approach.

# Ab Initio Theory of Light-ion Reactions

## Key claim
The authors present a new ab initio many-body approach (NCSM/RGM) that can simultaneously describe bound and scattering states in light nuclei throughout the p-shell, but the results for nucleon-4He phase shifts show that the 2P3/2 resonance is positioned at higher energy than experiment due to the neglect of three-nucleon interactions, and the deuteron-induced fusion calculations remain preliminary with strong sensitivity to the treatment of deuteron polarization.

## Method
The method combines the resonating-group method (RGM), a microscopic cluster technique, with the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM). Cluster eigenstates are first obtained via NCSM calculations using similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials, then the localized parts of the Hamiltonian and norm kernels in the RGM integro-differential coupled-channel equations are represented in the harmonic oscillator (HO) basis using the same HO frequency and model-space size as the cluster wave functions. For p-shell nuclei, the approach uses a Slater-determinant single-particle HO basis for the target and a Jacobi basis for the projectile, with importance-truncated NCSM (IT-NCSM) employed to reach larger model spaces (up to Nmax=16 for A<7).

## Key numbers
- n-4He phase-shift convergence demonstrated up to Nmax=17 HO excitations, with Nmax=17 and Nmax=15 curves lying on top of each other
- For p-4He scattering, calculations compared to experimental data at proton laboratory energies Ep = 12, 14.32, and 17 MeV
- Integrated elastic n-3H cross section at 14 MeV obtained from scaled NCSM/RGM calculation: 0.94 barn
- For p-7Be scattering, the lowest 2+ resonance (corresponding to 8B ground state) lies at about 200 keV above threshold (experimental 8B is bound by 137 keV)
- Calculated S-wave scattering length for p-7Be: a02 = -10.2 fm, close to experimental value -7(3) fm
- For n-8He scattering, a resonance in the 1/2- P-wave found at about 1.6 MeV (experimental value about 1.3 MeV)
- Calculated n-8He S-wave scattering length: a0 ~ -2 fm
- For 3He(d,p)4He fusion, calculations used SRG NN interaction with Lambda=1.5 fm-1 in HO model spaces up to Nmax=13 and HO frequency hbar-Omega=14 MeV

## Context
This work extends ab initio scattering calculations beyond the A=4 systems previously treated by Faddeev, Faddeev-Yakubovski, hyperspherical harmonics, and Lorentz integral transform methods (Marcucci 2009, Lazauskas 2009, Deltuva 2007). The first ab initio scattering calculation for A>4 was performed within the Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) approach for n-alpha scattering using the AV18 NN potential. The NCSM/RGM approach fills the gap between A=4 and A=17 systems, enabling calculations of reactions important for astrophysics (such as 7Be(p,gamma)8B relevant to solar neutrino flux) and fusion energy applications (d-3H and d-3He reactions). The approach enables future inclusion of three-nucleon forces and extension to three-nucleon projectiles and three-body continuum.
