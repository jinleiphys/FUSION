# QCD Thermodynamics and Neutral Pion in a Uniform Magnetic Field: Finite Volume Effects

> We address finite volume effects of lattice QCD calculations in background magnetic fields. Using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, volume effects are calculated for thermodynamic quantities: the chiral condensate, pressure anisotropy, and magnetization. The neutral pion effective action in a finite volume is additionally derived. For these charge neutral observables, volume and source averaging are shown to capitalize on magnetic periodicity, which is the remnant translational invariance of the finite-volume theory. For a fixed magnetic field strength, certain volume and source averaged quantities are independent of the size of the lattice transverse to the magnetic field. Despite this simplifying feature, finite volume corrections to the magnetic field dependence of the chiral condensate and neutral pion magnetic polarizability can be non-negligible. The pressure anisotropy at fixed magnetic flux, moreover, appears acutely sensitive to the lattice volume.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, the authors show that finite volume effects on QCD thermodynamics in a uniform magnetic field can be non-negligible even for charge-neutral observables, and that volume and source averaging capitalize on magnetic periodicity to remove certain transverse area effects at fixed magnetic field strength, but the pressure anisotropy at fixed magnetic flux remains acutely sensitive to lattice volume.

## Method

The authors compute finite volume corrections to the chiral condensate, free energy, magnetization, and pressure anisotropy using chiral perturbation theory in a finite Euclidean volume with magnetic periodic boundary conditions. They construct the charged pion propagator in a uniform magnetic field using the method of magnetic periodic images, evaluate the coincident propagator, and derive the neutral pion effective action in finite volume. Volume averaging for thermodynamic quantities and source averaging for the neutral pion two-point function are applied to exploit the remnant \(\mathbb{Z}_{N_\Phi}\) translational invariance.

## Key numbers

- For a cubic volume with \(m_\pi L = 3\) and flux quanta \(N_\Phi = 1, 2, 3\), the local chiral condensate exhibits oscillatory behavior with single, double, and triple periodicity, respectively, as shown in contour plots of \(R(x_\perp)\).
- In asymptotically large volumes (\(m_\pi L \gg 1\)) with fixed \(N_\Phi\) (so that \(QB/m_\pi^2 \ll 1\)), the leading asymptotic behavior of the finite volume correction to the chiral condensate is \(R(x_\perp) = - \left[ \frac{5}{2} + \cos(2\pi N_\Phi x_1/L) + \cos(2\pi N_\Phi x_2/L) \right] \frac{m_\pi^2}{F_\pi^2} \frac{e^{-m_\pi L}}{(2\pi m_\pi L)^{3/2}} + \cdots\).
- Volume averaging the asymptotic formula reduces the finite volume effect by a factor of \(5/9\) compared to the case of vanishing magnetic field.
- For the pressure anisotropy at fixed magnetic flux, a substantial finite volume effect is found for the lowest flux quanta even at \(m_\pi L = 4\).

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of QCD properties in magnetic fields in infinite volume (Shushpanov 1997, Cohen 2007, Tiburzi 2008, Andersen 2012, Adhikari 2021) to finite volume, addressing the systematic finite volume effects that arise in lattice QCD calculations in background magnetic fields (D'Elia 2010, Bali 2012, Bruckmann 2013, Bonati 2013, Endrodi 2015). The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to quantify and correct for finite volume artifacts in calculations of the chiral condensate, magnetization, pressure anisotropy, and neutral pion magnetic polarizability, particularly when using volume and source averaging techniques.
