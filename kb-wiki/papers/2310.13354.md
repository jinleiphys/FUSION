# Tetraquark bound states in constituent quark models: benchmark test calculations

> We investigate the tetraquark bound states that are manifestly exotic using three distinct few-body methods: Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), Resonating Group Method (RGM), and Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC). We refer to manifestly exotic states that do not involve a mixture with the conventional mesons through the creation and annihilation of $n\bar{n}$, where $n=u, d$. Our calculations are conducted with two types of quark models: the pure constituent quark model featuring one-gluon-exchange interactions and confinement interactions, and the chiral constituent quark model, supplemented by extra one-boson-exchange interactions. This study represents a comprehensive benchmark test of various few-body methods and quark models. Our findings reveal the superiority of GEM over RGM and DMC methods based on present implements for the tetraquark bound states. Additionally, we observe a tendency for the chiral quark model to overestimate the binding energies. We systematically explore the fully, triply, doubly, and singly heavy tetraquark states with $J^P=0^+,1^+,2^+$, encompassing over 150 states in total. We successfully identify several bound states, including $[cc\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^{P}=1^{+}}^{I=0}$, $[bb\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^{P}=1^{+}}^{I=0}$, $[bc\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^{P}=0^{+},1^{+},2^{+}}^{I=0}$, $[bs\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^{P}=0^{+},1^{+}}^{I=0}$, $[cs\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^{P}=0^{+}}^{I=0}$, and $[bb\bar{n}\bar{s}]_{J^{P}=1^{+}}$, all found to be bound states below the dimeson thresholds.

## Key claim

The study finds that the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) is superior to the Resonating Group Method (RGM) and Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) for tetraquark bound state calculations based on the present implementations, while noting that the chiral constituent quark model tends to overestimate binding energies compared to the pure constituent quark models.

## Method

The authors employed three distinct few-body methods, Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), Resonating Group Method (RGM) in momentum space, and Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC), to solve the four-body Schrödinger equation for tetraquark systems. They used two types of nonrelativistic constituent quark models: the pure constituent quark model (PCQM) with one-gluon-exchange and confinement interactions (using the AL1 and AP1 parameter sets from Semay and Silvestre-Brac), and the chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) from the Salamanca group (SLM) that includes additional one-boson-exchange interactions. The calculations systematically explored over 150 tetraquark states with quantum numbers J^P = 0^+, 1^+, 2^+ across fully, triply, doubly, and singly heavy configurations.

## Key numbers

- For [cc\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^P=1^+}^{I=0} with the SLM model, GEM gives a binding energy of -189.6 MeV relative to the DD* threshold, while RGM gives -5.9 MeV and DMC gives -139.1 MeV with the same model
- For [bb\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^P=1^+}^{I=0} with the AL1 model, GEM gives -151.6 MeV relative to the \bar{B}\bar{B}* threshold, with an excited bound state at -0.70 MeV
- For [bc\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^P=2^+}^{I=0} with the AL1 model, GEM gives -2.9 MeV relative to the D*\bar{B}* threshold
- The GEM basis test shows that using only diquark-antidiquark spatial wave functions (Ψ_E) makes the [bc\bar{n}\bar{n}]_{J^P=2^+}^{I=0} state unbound, while the full basis (Ψ_A) gives -2.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends benchmark testing approaches previously applied to four-nucleon systems (Kamada, 2001) to the tetraquark sector, where previous predictions from different quark models showed significant divergence, for example, the [cc\bar{n}\bar{n}] state with I(J^P)=0(1^+) had predictions ranging from deeply bound states at -300 MeV to unbound states above the DD* threshold. The study enables systematic comparison of different few-body methods and quark models, identifying GEM as the most reliable variational method for tetraquark bound states while noting that the RGM and DMC implementations require further development. The identified bound states below dimeson thresholds provide candidates for experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb, BESIII, and CMS.
