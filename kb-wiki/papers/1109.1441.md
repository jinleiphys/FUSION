# Reexamination of phenomenological two-photon exchange corrections to the proton form factors and e+-p scattering

> We extract the two-photon exchange (TPE) contributions to electron--proton elastic scattering using two parametrizations and compare the results to different phenomenological extractions and direct calculations of the TPE effects. We find that many of the extractions give similar results, and highlight the common assumptions and the impact of not including such assumptions. We provide a simple parametrization of the TPE contribution to the unpolarized cross section, along with an estimate of the fit uncertainties and the uncertainties associated with the assumptions made in the extraction. We look at the contributions as extracted from various e--p elastic scattering observables, and make predictions for ratio $R^{e^{+} e^{-}}$ of positron-proton to electron-proton elastic scattering cross sections.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) contributions extracted from electron-proton elastic scattering data using different phenomenological parametrizations yield similar results for the form factor ratio \(G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}\) when they assume a nearly-linear correction in \(\varepsilon\) and negligible TPE contributions to polarization transfer data, but the extracted values of \(G_{Mp}\) and the TPE amplitude \(a(Q^2)\) depend on whether the parametrization forces the TPE correction to vanish at \(\varepsilon=0\) or at \(\varepsilon=1\).

## Method

The authors performed global fits to world unpolarized electron-proton elastic scattering cross section data from Refs. [Andivahis (1994), Walker (1994), Christy (2004), Qattan (2005), Litt (1970), Berger (1971), Bartel (1973)] using two empirical parametrizations of the TPE contribution to the reduced cross section \(\sigma_R\): the QA parametrization (linear in \(\varepsilon\), vanishing at \(\varepsilon=0\)) and the BK parametrization (proportional to \(1-\varepsilon\), vanishing at \(\varepsilon=1\)). The ratio \(G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}\) was fixed to the polarization transfer parametrization of Gayou (2002), and the TPE parameters and \(G_{Mp}\) were extracted at fixed \(Q^2\) values; the results were compared to previous phenomenological extractions (Arrington (2005), Alberico (2009)) and to hadronic TPE calculations (Blunden (2005)).

## Key numbers

- For the BK parametrization at \(Q^2 = 2.50\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) from Andivahis (1994): \((G_{Mp}/\mu_p G_D)^2 = 1.147 \pm 0.010\), \((G_{Ep}/G_D)^2 = 0.531 \pm 0.004\), and the TPE parameter \(a(Q^2) = (-1.57 \pm 0.88) \times 10^{-2}\).
- For the QA parametrization at \(Q^2 = 2.50\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) from Andivahis (1994): \((G_{Mp}/\mu_p G_D)^2 = 1.111 \pm 0.013\), \((G_{Ep}/G_D)^2 = 0.514 \pm 0.006\), and the TPE parameter \(f(Q^2) = (0.07 \pm 0.04) \times 10^{-2}\).
- The global fit to the \(Q^2\) dependence of the BK parameter yields \(a(Q^2) = -0.0191\sqrt{Q^2} \pm 0.0014\sqrt{Q^2} \pm 0.003\) for \(1.5 < Q^2 < 4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), with reduced \(\chi^2\) values between 1.04 and 1.10 for several functional forms.
- The TPE parameter \(a(Q^2)\) from the BK fit is at the few percent level and increases in magnitude with \(Q^2\); for example, at \(Q^2 = 4.10\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) from Qattan (2005): \(a(Q^2) = (-4.77 \pm 0.78) \times 10^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological TPE analysis of Qattan and Alsaad (2011) by including additional data sets (Bartel (1973), Litt (1970), Berger (1971)) and comparing the QA and BK parametrizations to previous extractions by Arrington (2005) and Alberico (2009). The results provide a simple parametrization of the TPE contribution to the unpolarized cross section and predictions for the ratio \(R_{e^{+} e^{-}}\) of positron-proton to electron-proton elastic scattering cross sections, which can be tested by upcoming experiments (e.g., VEPP-3, JLab E07-005). The analysis highlights that the extracted TPE corrections are consistent across methods that assume a nearly-linear \(\varepsilon\) dependence, but the low-\(Q^2\) behavior remains poorly constrained and may require including TPE contributions to polarization data.
