# Competition between fusion-fission and quasifission processes in the $^{32}$S+$^{182,184}$W reactions

> The angular distributions of fission fragments for the $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W reaction at center-of-mass energies of 118.8, 123.1, 127.3, 131.5, 135.8, 141.1 and 144.4 MeV were measured. The experimental fission excitation function is obtained. The fragment angular anisotropy ($\mathcal{A}_{\rm exp}$) is found by extrapolating the each fission angular distributions. The measured fission cross sections of the $^{32}$S+$^{182,184}$W reaction are decomposed into fusion-fission, quasifission and fast fission contributions by the dinuclear system model. The total evaporation residue excitation function for the $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W reaction calculated in the framework of the advanced statistical model is in good agreement with the available experimental data up to about $E_{\rm c.m.}\approx 160$ MeV. The theoretical descriptions of the experimental capture excitation functions for both reactions and quantities $K_0^2$, $<\ell^2>$ and $\mathcal{A}_{\rm exp}$ which characterize angular distributions of the fission products were performed by the same partial capture cross sections at the considered range of beam energy.

## Key claim

The measured fission cross sections for the $^{32}$S+$^{182,184}$W reactions can be decomposed into fusion-fission, quasifission, and fast fission contributions using the dinuclear system (DNS) model, but the decomposition remains ambiguous because the mass and angular distributions of quasifission and fusion-fission products can overlap, with the degree of overlap depending on beam energy and mass asymmetry.

## Method

The authors measured angular distributions of fission fragments for the $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W reaction at seven center-of-mass energies using Si detectors and Si strip detectors in coincidence mode at the HI-13 tandem accelerator. They extracted the capture cross sections, fragment angular anisotropy $\mathcal{A}_{\rm exp}$, and variance $K_0^2$ by fitting the angular distributions. The experimental fission cross sections were then decomposed into fusion-fission, quasifission, and fast fission contributions using the DNS model, which calculates the competition between complete fusion and quasifission by evaluating the potential energy surface and decay widths for the dinuclear system. The evaporation residue cross sections were calculated using an advanced statistical model that accounts for the deexcitation cascade of the compound nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Capture cross sections for $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W: 0.04 mb at $E_{\rm c.m.}=118.8$ MeV, 2.35 mb at 123.1 MeV, 22.97 mb at 127.3 MeV, 81.01 mb at 131.5 MeV, 132.27 mb at 135.8 MeV, 189.33 mb at 141.1 MeV, and 237.06 mb at 144.4 MeV.
- Fragment angular anisotropy $\mathcal{A}_{\rm exp}$ for $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W: 1.51 at $E_{\rm c.m.}=118.8$ MeV, 2.16 at 123.1 MeV, 2.27 at 127.3 MeV, 2.74 at 131.5 MeV, 3.06 at 135.8 MeV, 3.28 at 141.1 MeV, and 3.80 at 144.4 MeV.
- Variance $K_0^2$ for $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W: 114.71 at $E_{\rm c.m.}=118.8$ MeV, 124.35 at 123.1 MeV, 132.09 at 127.3 MeV, 140.01 at 131.5 MeV, 148.67 at 135.8 MeV, 157.35 at 141.1 MeV, and 155.09 at 144.4 MeV.
- Angular momentum at which the fission barrier disappears: $\ell_{\rm B}=68$ for $^{214}$Th and $\ell_{\rm B}=70$ for $^{216}$Th, calculated using the rotating finite range model by Sierk (1986).
- The theoretical evaporation residue excitation function for $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W agrees with experimental data from Back (1985) up to $E_{\rm c.m.}\approx 160$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of fusion-fission and quasifission competition in reactions forming thorium compound nuclei, building on prior measurements by Back (1985) for the $^{32}$S+$^{184}$W reaction and by Keller (1986) for the $^{32}$S+$^{182}$W reaction at higher energies. The DNS model approach follows the framework developed by Nasirov (2005), Fazio (2004, 2005), and Adamian (2003) for analyzing the hindrance to complete fusion in massive nuclear collisions. The results enable more reliable decomposition of fission-like yields into fusion-fission and quasifission components, which is essential for predicting optimal reaction conditions for superheavy element synthesis.
