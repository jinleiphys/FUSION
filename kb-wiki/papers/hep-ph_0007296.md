# Exotic Mesons, Theory and Experiment

> In this talk I summarize the status of exotic mesons, including both theoretical expectations and experimental candidates. The current experimental candidates are ``spin-parity exotics''; since these are most often considered possible hybrid mesons, the theoretical discussion will be mainly concerned with hybrids. The exotic meson candidates discussed are the surprisingly light pi_1(1400) and pi_1(1600).

# Exotic Mesons, Theory and Experiment

## Key claim

The two experimental exotic meson candidates, π₁(1400) and π₁(1600), have properties that differ significantly from theoretical expectations for hybrid mesons, particularly in their masses and decay branching fractions; they may instead be another kind of non-qq̅ state or even a misinterpreted nonresonant scattering effect.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical expectations for exotic mesons from the bag model, flux tube model, lattice gauge theory (LGT), and QCD sum rules, then compares these predictions to experimental results from the VES collaboration, BNL experiment E852, and the Crystal Barrel collaboration. The experimental analyses use partial wave analysis of meson production in π⁻ p and other reactions to extract resonant signals in exotic Jᴾᶜ channels, particularly the 1⁻⁺ wave in ηπ, η′π, ρπ, and b₁π final states.

## Key numbers

- Bag model predicts lowest hybrid multiplet masses of ≈ 1.5 GeV, with exotic 1⁻⁺ state included
- Flux tube model (Isgur, Kokoski, Paton) estimates lightest hybrid multiplet mass at 1.9(1) GeV
- Barnes, Close, Swanson improved flux tube calculation gives 1.8–1.9 GeV for lightest hybrid multiplet
- LGT (MILC, UKQCD) gives 1⁻⁺ exotic mass ≈ 2.0(1) GeV
- NRQCD predicts 1⁻⁺ b-bbar hybrid at ≈ 10.99(1) GeV and c-cbar hybrid at ≈ 4.39(1) GeV
- π₁(1600) mass: VES reports 1.61(2) GeV; BNL E852 reports 1.593 ± 0.008 +0.029/−0.047 GeV
- π₁(1600) width: VES reports 0.29(3) GeV; BNL E852 reports 0.168 ± 0.020 +0.150/−0.012 GeV
- π₁(1600) relative branching fractions (VES): b₁π ≡ 1, η′π = 1.0 ± 0.3, ρπ = 1.6 ± 0.4
- π₁(1400) mass: BNL E852 reports 1.370 ± 0.016 +0.050/−0.030 GeV; Crystal Barrel reports 1.400 ± 0.02 ± 0.020 GeV
- π₁(1400) width: BNL E852 reports 0.385 ± 0.040 +0.065/−0.105 GeV; Crystal Barrel reports 0.310 ± 0.050 +0.050/−0.030 GeV
- QCD sum rule (Chetyrkin and Narison) gives 1⁻⁺ mass ≈ 1.6–1.7 GeV, with πη′ partial width ≈ 3 MeV and πρ partial width ≈ 300 MeV
- Flux tube model predicts π₁(2000) total width ≈ 450 MeV with b₁π dominant at 170 MeV and ρπ only 5–20 MeV

## Context

This talk from MESON2000 reviews the status of exotic mesons following decades of theoretical work on multiquark systems (Jaffe, 1970s), bag model hybrids (Barnes and Close, 1980s; Chanowitz and Sharpe, 1980s), and flux tube model hybrids (Isgur, Kokoski, Paton, 1980s; Barnes, Close, Swanson, 1990s). The experimental candidates π₁(1400) and π₁(1600) challenge the flux tube model prediction that the lightest 1⁻⁺ exotic hybrid should lie near 1.9–2.0 GeV and decay predominantly to S+P wave meson pairs (b₁π, f₁π) rather than to ρπ or ηπ. The paper calls for further experimental study, particularly of the ρπ channel in photoproduction at Jefferson Lab, and notes that the heavy quark sector (charmonium and bottomonium hybrids) offers clean LGT predictions that could be tested by high-statistics scans of R.
