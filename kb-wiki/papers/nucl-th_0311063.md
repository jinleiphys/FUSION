# 6He+6He clustering of 12Be in a microscopic algebraic approach

> The norm kernel of the A=12 system composed of two 6He clusters, and the L=0 basis functions (in the SU(3) and angular momentum-coupled schemes) are analytically obtained in the Fock--Bargmann space. The norm kernel has a diagonal form in the former basis, but the asymptotic conditions are naturally defined in the latter one. The system is a good illustration for the method of projection of the norm kernel to the basis functions in the presence of SU(3) degeneracy that was proposed by the authors. The coupled-channel problem is considered in the Algebraic Version of the resonating-group method, with the multiple decay thresholds being properly accounted for. The structure of the ground state of 12Be obtained in the approximation of zero-range nuclear force is compared with the shell-model predictions. In the continuum part of the spectrum, the S-matrix is constructed, the asymptotic normalization coefficients are deduced and their energy dependence is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper analytically constructs the norm kernel and basis functions for the \(^{12}\)Be system treated as two \(^{6}\)He clusters, but the dynamical calculations of the ground state and continuum are performed only in the approximation of zero-range nuclear force, with potential matrix elements fitted to reproduce key experimental data rather than derived from a fully microscopic interaction.

## Method

The authors used the Algebraic Version of the Resonating-Group Method (AVRGM) in the Fock–Bargmann space to analytically obtain the norm kernel for the \(A=12\) system of two \(^{6}\)He clusters. They constructed \(L=0\) basis functions in both the \(SU(3)\) scheme and the angular momentum-coupled ("physical") scheme, resolved the \(SU(3)\) degeneracy for the \((2k,2)\) representation by solving a set of linear equations for the mixing angle \(\alpha(k)\), and computed the eigenvalues of the norm kernel. The coupled-channel problem was then treated with zero-range nuclear force, with the Hamiltonian matrix elements fitted to reproduce experimental data, and the \(S\)-matrix was constructed for the continuum part of the spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The eigenvalues of the norm kernel for the \((2k+4,0)\) representation range from 0.2134 at \(k=5\) to 0.9411 at \(k=10\).
* The eigenvalues for the \((2k-4,4)\) representation range from 0.4609 at \(k=3\) to 0.9979 at \(k=10\).
* The eigenvalues for the \((2k-2,0)\) representation are 1.1111 at \(k=2\) and 0.9959 at \(k=3\), approaching 0.9999 at \(k=10\).
* For the degenerate \((2k,2)\) representation, the two eigenvalues \(\Lambda_{(2k,2)_1}\) and \(\Lambda_{(2k,2)_2}\) at \(k=3\) are 0.8313 and 0, respectively; at \(k=10\) they are 0.9986 and 0.9807.
* The mixing angle \(\cos\alpha(k)\) for the degenerate \((2k,2)\) basis functions is 0.8700 at \(k=3\) and 0.9366 at \(k=10\), approaching 1 as \(k\rightarrow\infty\).
* The minimal number of oscillator quanta allowed by the Pauli principle for the \(L=0\) states is \(n=2k=4\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic version of the resonating-group method to systems with open \(p\)-shell clusters, following earlier norm kernel calculations by Hecht et al. and Fujiwara et al. for alpha-conjugated systems and the \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{6}\)Li system. The study was motivated by experimental observations by Freer et al. and Saito of \(^{12}\)Be states decaying through \(^{6}\)He+\(^{6}\)He and \(^{8}\)He+\(^{4}\)He channels, and by calculations in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-En'yo, 2003) and a quasi-microscopic coupled-channel model (Ito). The analytical basis functions and norm kernel eigenvalues enable future fully microscopic calculations of the \(^{6}\)He+\(^{6}\)He scattering and the continuum spectrum of \(^{12}\)Be, including proper treatment of multiple decay thresholds.
