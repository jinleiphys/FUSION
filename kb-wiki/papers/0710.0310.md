# Shell model description of the 14C dating beta decay with Brown-Rho-scaled NN interactions

> We present shell model calculations for the beta-decay of the 14C ground state to the 14N ground state, treating the states of the A=14 multiplet as two 0p holes in an 16O core. We employ low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions derived from the realistic Bonn-B potential and find that the Gamow-Teller matrix element is too large to describe the known lifetime. By using a modified version of this potential that incorporates the effects of Brown-Rho scaling medium modifications, we find that the GT matrix element vanishes for a nuclear density around 85% that of nuclear matter. We find that the splitting between the (J,T)=(1+,0) and (J,T)=(0+,1) states in 14N is improved using the medium-modified Bonn-B potential and that the transition strengths from excited states of 14C to the 14N ground state are compatible with recent experiments.

# Shell model description of the 14C dating beta decay with Brown-Rho-scaled NN interactions

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller matrix element for the 14C beta decay to 14N, which is too large when calculated with the free-space Bonn-B potential, vanishes when using a Brown-Rho-scaled (medium-modified) version of the same potential at a nuclear density around 85% that of nuclear matter, thereby reproducing the anomalously long half-life of 5730 years.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations for the A=14 multiplet treating the states as two 0p holes in a 16O core, using a low-momentum interaction (Vlow-k) derived from the Bonn-B potential. They incorporated hadronic medium modifications via Brown-Rho scaling, where the masses of the rho and omega mesons and the form factor cutoff were reduced according to m*/m = Lambda*/Lambda = 1 - 0.15 n/n0, while the pion mass was kept unchanged. The effective interaction was derived using the folded-diagram formalism including hole-hole irreducible diagrams up to second order in Vlow-k, and the wavefunctions were obtained in a jj-coupling basis and transformed to LS-coupling to compute the Gamow-Teller matrix element.

## Key numbers

- The experimental half-life of 14C is 5730 years, corresponding to a Gamow-Teller matrix element of approximately 2 x 10^-3.
- The Gamow-Teller matrix element calculated with the free-space Bonn-B potential (n/n0 = 0) is -0.615.
- At a nuclear density of 75% of nuclear matter (n/n0 = 0.75), the Gamow-Teller matrix element is -0.065; at nuclear matter density (n/n0 = 1.0), it becomes +0.074, crossing zero near 85% of nuclear matter density.
- The splitting between the (J^pi,T) = (1+,0) and (0+,1) states in 14N is 2.31 MeV experimentally; the calculation with the medium-modified potential improves agreement with this value.
- The single-particle energy splitting epsilon = E(p1/2^-1) - E(p3/2^-1) was taken as 6.3 MeV from the experimental excitation energy of the first 3/2- state in 15N.
- The decimation momentum cutoff for Vlow-k was chosen as Lambda approximately 2.0 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell model studies of the 14C beta decay by Zamick and collaborators (2004) and by Jancovici and Talmi (1954), who showed that a strong tensor force is required to suppress the Gamow-Teller matrix element. The paper builds on the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991) and previous Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Rapp and collaborators (1998) that used the same medium-modified Bonn-B potential to describe nuclear matter saturation. The results suggest that the in-medium reduction of the tensor force, primarily through the dropping rho meson mass, is responsible for the decay suppression, and that this effect would traditionally be incorporated through three-nucleon forces in ab initio calculations.
