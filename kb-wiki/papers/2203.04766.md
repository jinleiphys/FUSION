# Relativistic second-order dissipative spin hydrodynamics from the method of moments

> We derive relativistic second-order dissipative fluid-dynamical equations of motion for massive spin-1/2 particles from kinetic theory using the method of moments. Besides the usual conservation laws for charge, energy, and momentum, such a theory of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics features an equation of motion for the rank-3 spin tensor, which follows from the conservation of total angular momentum. Extending the conventional method of moments for spin-0 particles, we expand the spin-dependent distribution function near local equilibrium in terms of moments of the momentum and spin variables. We work to next-to-leading order in the Planck constant $\hbar$. As shown in previous work, at this order in $\hbar$ the Boltzmann equation for spin-1/2 particles features a nonlocal collision term. From the Boltzmann equation, we then obtain an infinite set of equations of motion for the irreducible moments of the deviation of the single-particle distribution function from local equilibrium. In order to close this system of moment equations, a truncation procedure is needed. We employ the "14+24-moment approximation", where "14" corresponds to the components of the charge current and the energy-momentum tensor and "24" to the components of the spin tensor, which completes the derivation of the equations of motion of second-order dissipative spin hydrodynamics. For applications to heavy-ion phenomenology, we also determine dissipative corrections to the Pauli-Lubanski vector.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete set of second-order dissipative fluid-dynamical equations for massive spin-1/2 particles, but the derivation relies on a novel power-counting scheme where the fluid vorticity scale can be much smaller than the hydrodynamic gradient scale, provided the ratio of the nonlocality scale to the vorticity scale is of the same order as the Knudsen number.

## Method

The authors extend the method of moments from spin-0 kinetic theory to spin-1/2 particles by expanding the spin-dependent distribution function in irreducible moments of both momentum and spin four-vector, working to next-to-leading order in the Planck constant. Starting from the Boltzmann equation with a nonlocal collision term derived in previous work, they obtain an infinite set of equations of motion for the irreducible moments of the deviation from local equilibrium. To close this system, they employ a "14+24-moment approximation," where 14 moments correspond to the charge current and energy-momentum tensor components and 24 moments correspond to the components of the rank-3 spin tensor, using the Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen pseudo-gauge.

## Key numbers

* The spin tensor contains 24 dynamical degrees of freedom: 3 from the spin-energy tensor, 3 from the spin-pressure tensor, 9 from the spin-diffusion tensor, and 9 from the spin-stress tensor.
* The total number of dynamical degrees of freedom in the truncated system is 14+24 = 38.
* The nonlocality shift in the collision term is of order Delta ~ hbar/m, where m is the particle mass, and the semiclassical expansion requires Delta much smaller than the vorticity length scale ell_vort.
* The power-counting scheme sets Delta/ell_vort ~ Kn, where Kn is the Knudsen number defined as the mean free path divided by the hydrodynamic length scale.

## Context

This work extends the conventional method of moments for relativistic hydrodynamics developed by Denicol, Koide, and Rischke (2012) to include spin degrees of freedom, building on the quantum kinetic theory for spin-1/2 particles with nonlocal collisions established by Weickgenannt, Speranza, and Rischke (2020, 2021). The derivation provides a foundation for second-order dissipative spin hydrodynamics applicable to heavy-ion phenomenology, where the Pauli-Lubanski vector (the observable for hadron spin polarization) can now be computed including dissipative corrections, potentially addressing the unresolved discrepancy between theoretical models and longitudinal-polarization data from the STAR and ALICE collaborations.
