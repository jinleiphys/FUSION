# Double beta decay and the proton-neutron residual interaction

> The validity of the pn-QRPA and -RQRPA descriptions of double beta decay transition amplitudes is analyzed by using an exactly solvable model. It is shown that the collapse of the QRPA is physically meaningful and that it is associated with the appearance of a state with zero energy in the spectrum. It is shown that in the RQRPA this particular feature is not present and that this approach leads to finite but otherwise spurious results for the double beta decay transition amplitudes near the point of collapse.

## Key claim

The collapse of the QRPA (quasiparticle random phase approximation) for double beta decay transition amplitudes is physically meaningful and correlates with the appearance of a state with zero energy in the exact spectrum, while the RQRPA (renormalized QRPA) avoids this collapse but produces finite but spurious results for the transition matrix elements near the collapse point.

## Method

The authors constructed an exactly solvable schematic model Hamiltonian with a single j-shell (j=19/2), pairing terms for protons and neutrons, and a charge-dependent residual interaction including particle-hole and particle-particle channels, treated in the monopole (J=0) limit. They compared exact solutions of this model with results from the QRPA, the RQRPA, and an approximate quasiparticle Hamiltonian, computing excitation energies and double beta decay transition amplitudes as functions of the particle-particle strength parameter κ.

## Key numbers

- The model used j = 19/2, N = 20 total particles, Tz ranging from 1 to 5, with single-particle energies e_p = 0.7 MeV and e_n = 0.0 MeV, pairing strengths G_p = G_n = 0.2 MeV, and particle-hole strength χ = 0.025 MeV.
- The QRPA collapses when 4κ/G ≈ 1.4, where the excitation energy of the lowest 0+ state in the odd-odd intermediate nucleus goes to zero.
- The double beta decay transition amplitudes M_{2ν} were evaluated with Δ = 0.5 MeV, summing over intermediate states in the double-odd nucleus.
- The QRPA eigen-energy formula is E_QRPA = [(2ε + λ₁)² - 4λ₂²]^{1/2}, becoming imaginary when 2λ₂ > 2ε + λ₁.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Vog86, Eng87, Civ87, and Mut89 on the QRPA description of double beta decay, specifically addressing the collapse problem noted for the ββ_{2ν} decay of ¹⁰⁰Mo. It directly tests the RQRPA reformulation of Cat94 and Toi95, showing that while the RQRPA avoids the collapse, its finite results near the collapse point are spurious because it misses the zero-energy state present in the exact solution. The model enables a clear benchmark for evaluating higher-order approximations beyond the quasiboson approximation.
