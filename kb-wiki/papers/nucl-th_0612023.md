# Quantum Shape-Phase Transitions in Finite Nuclei

> Quantum shape-phase transitions in finite nuclei are considered in the framework of the interacting boson model. Critical-point Hamiltonians for first- and second-order transitions are identified by resolving them into intrinsic and collective parts. Suitable wave functions and finite-N estimates for observables at the critical-points are derived.

## Key claim

Quantum shape-phase transitions in finite nuclei are studied within the interacting boson model (IBM) by resolving critical-point Hamiltonians into intrinsic and collective parts, with the explicit recognition that finite-N effects modify the idealized infinite-N predictions of geometric models such as E(5) and X(5).

## Method

The author constructs critical-point Hamiltonians for first- and second-order shape-phase transitions by separating each Hamiltonian into an intrinsic part (which has the equilibrium condensate as an exact zero-energy eigenstate and reproduces the critical energy surface) and a collective part (composed of kinetic terms involving Casimir operators of O(3), O(5), and O(6)). Suitable wave functions are obtained by angular-momentum projection from intrinsic coherent states, and finite-N estimates for observables are derived by evaluating matrix elements in these projected states with the deformation parameter β chosen at the global minimum of the projected energy surface.

## Key numbers

- For the first-order critical-point with high barrier (β₀=1.3, h=0.1) and N=10: E(4⁺₁)=3.32 (c₃-term), 3.28 (c₅-term), 2.81 (c₆-term) in units of E(2⁺₁)=1; B(E2; 4⁺₁→2⁺₁)=1.40 (c₃ and c₅ terms), 1.46 (c₆ term) in units of B(E2; 2⁺₁→0⁺₁)=1.
- For the first-order critical-point with low barrier (U(5)-SU(3) critical Hamiltonian with ε/κ=38.25, N=10): E(4⁺₁)=2.43, E(6⁺₁)=4.29, E(0⁺₂)=2.64 in units of E(2⁺₁)=1; B(E2; 4⁺₁→2⁺₁)=1.61, B(E2; 0⁺₂→2⁺₁)=0.78 in the same units.
- For the high-barrier case with c₆-term: E(4⁺₁)=2.81, E(6⁺₁)=5.43, E(0⁺₂)=4.56, compared to X(5) predictions of 2.91, 5.45, and 5.67 respectively.
- The barrier height for the low-barrier U(5)-SU(3) critical Hamiltonian is h≈10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the geometric approach to nuclear shape-phase transitions, specifically the E(5) model of Iachello (2000) for second-order transitions and the X(5) model of Iachello (2001) for first-order transitions, by incorporating finite-N effects within the algebraic IBM framework. Prior IBM studies by Dieperink and Scholten (1980) and Iachello and Zamfir (2004) identified specific dynamical symmetry chain transitions (U(5)-SU(3), U(5)-O(6), O(6)-SU(3)), but the present work provides a general method for constructing critical-point Hamiltonians not restricted to particular forms. The results enable systematic finite-N calculations of spectra and electromagnetic transitions at critical points, which are essential for interpreting data on stable and unstable nuclei as functions of proton and neutron number.
