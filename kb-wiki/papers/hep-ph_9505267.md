# Effective Action for the Quark-Meson Model

> The scale dependence of an effective average action for mesons and quarks is described by a nonperturbative flow equation. The running couplings lead to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. We argue that for strong Yukawa coupling between quarks and mesons the low momentum physics is essentially determined by infrared fixed points. This allows us to establish relations between various parameters related to the meson potential. The results for $f_π$ and $\VEV{\olpsiψ}$ are not very sensitive to the poorly known details of the quark--meson effective action at scales where the mesonic bound states form. For realistic constituent quark masses we find $f_π$ around $100\MeV$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for strong Yukawa coupling between quarks and mesons, low-momentum physics is essentially determined by infrared fixed points, making results for the pion decay constant $f_\pi$ and the chiral condensate $\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle$ insensitive to the poorly known details of the quark-meson effective action at the scale where mesonic bound states form.

## Method

The authors employ nonperturbative flow equations for the effective average action $\Gamma_k$, which describes the scale dependence of couplings for mesons and quarks as the infrared cutoff $k$ is lowered from a transition scale $k_\phi \approx 600\text{ MeV}$ to zero. They truncate the effective action to include a meson potential, kinetic terms, and a Yukawa coupling, then derive and solve the flow equations for the running couplings, mass term, and vacuum expectation value, including both quark and meson fluctuations. The evolution is integrated from the symmetric regime (where the potential minimum is at the origin) into the spontaneously broken regime where chiral symmetry breaking occurs.

## Key numbers

* For realistic constituent quark masses, the pion decay constant $f_\pi$ is found around $100\text{ MeV}$ (the experimental value is $93\text{ MeV}$).
* In a simplified model with $h(0) = 2m_q/f_\pi \approx 6.5$ and $h(k_\phi)$ between 12 and 100, the predicted $f_\pi$ ranges from $83$ to $100\text{ MeV}$.
* The transition scale $k_\phi$ is taken as $630\text{ MeV}$ with scalar mass $\bar{m}(k_\phi) = 120\text{ MeV}$ from prior work (EW94-1).
* The renormalized Yukawa coupling at the transition scale is large, with $h^2(k_\phi) \approx 180$ for a typical choice of wave function renormalization $Z_\phi(k_\phi) \approx 1/180$.
* The chiral condensate $\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle_0$ is related to the vacuum expectation value $\sigma_0$ via $\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle_0 = (2\bar{m}^2(k_\phi) Z_\psi(k_\phi) / \bar{h}(k_\phi)) \sigma_0$.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of exact flow equations for the average action previously applied to QCD-inspired models with four-quark interactions (EW94-1) and generalizes it to a systematic study of the quark-meson model including both quark and meson fluctuations. The approach aims to compute low-energy parameters like $f_\pi$ from short-distance QCD parameters, complementing lattice gauge theory simulations and chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1979, Gasser and Leutwyler 1982). The results enable future studies of the temperature dependence of $f_\pi$ and the chiral condensate, as well as applications to hadronization models in high-energy scattering.
