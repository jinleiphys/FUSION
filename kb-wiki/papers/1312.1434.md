# Folding model analysis of the inelastic $ﾎｱ+^{12}$C scattering at medium energies, and the isoscalar transition strengths of the cluster states of $^{12}$C

> The complex optical potential and inelastic form factor given by the folding model for the $ﾎｱ+^{12}$C scattering are used to calculate the ($ﾎｱ,ﾎｱ'$) cross sections for the known isoscalar states of $^{12}$C in an elaborate coupled channel approach. The strengths of the form factors for these states are then fine tuned against the ($ﾎｱ,ﾎｱ'$) data to deduce the corresponding $Eﾎｻ$ transition strengths. A significant $E2$ transition strength has been obtained for the $2^+_2$ state from the present analysis of the ($ﾎｱ,ﾎｱ'$) data measured at $E_ﾎｱ=240$ and 386 MeV. The $Eﾎｻ$ transition strengths of the $0^+_2,¥ 3^-_1,¥ 0^+_3,$ and $1^-_1$ states were also carefully deduced, and some difference from the results of earlier analyses has been found and qualitatively understood.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a significant $E2$ transition strength can be assigned to the $2^+_2$ state of $^{12}$C from the folding model plus coupled channel analysis of ($¥alpha,¥alpha'$) data at $E_¥alpha=240$ and 386 MeV, despite the state being strongly hindered by the nearby $3^-_1$ and $0^+_3$ excitations; the deduced $B(E2)$ value depends on the assumed total width of the $2^+_2$ state, with the best-fit value $0.6¥pm 0.1¥ e^2$fm$^4$ differing from the photodissociation result of $1.57¥pm 0.14¥ e^2$fm$^4$.

## Method

The complex optical potential and inelastic form factors for $¥alpha+^{12}$C scattering were calculated using the double-folding model with the complex CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and nuclear transition densities from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations. The ($¥alpha,¥alpha'$) cross sections for the isoscalar states of $^{12}$C were computed in a coupled channel approach using the code ECIS97, and the form factor strengths were fine-tuned against the experimental data at $E_¥alpha=240$ and 386 MeV to deduce the corresponding $E¥lambda$ transition strengths.

- $M(E0;0^+_2¥rightarrow 0^+_1)$ best-fit value: $4.5¥pm 0.5¥ e$fm$^2$ from the present CC analysis at 240 MeV, compared to $3.7¥pm 0.2¥ e$fm$^2$ from John (2003) and $5.4¥pm 0.2¥ e$fm$^2$ from Strehl (1970)

## Context

This work extends the folding model analysis of Khoa (2011) for the $2^+_2$ state of $^{12}$C using the ($¥alpha,¥alpha'$) data at 240 MeV from John (2003) and the high-precision data at 386 MeV from Itoh (2011), incorporating AMD transition densities from Kanada-En'yo (2007) rather than earlier RGM densities. The results enable a more accurate determination of the $E2$ strength of the $2^+_2$ state, which is crucial for understanding the rotational band built upon the Hoyle state as originally suggested by Morinaga (1956), and the analysis shows that proper treatment of the exit channel optical potential and higher-order coupling effects can resolve discrepancies between hadron scattering and electron scattering determinations of transition strengths.
