# Strangeness Production in low energy Heavy Ion Collisions via Hagedorn Resonances

> A novel, unorthodox picture of the dynamics of heavy ion collisions is developed using the concept of Hagedorn states. A prescription of the bootstrap of Hagedorn states respecting the conserved quantum numbers baryon number B, strangeness S, isospin I is implememted into the GiBUU transport model. Using a strangeness saturation suppression factor suitable for nucleon-nucleon-collisions, recent experimental data for the strangeness production by the HADES collaboration in Au+Au and Ar+KCl is reasonable well described. The experimental observed exponential slopes of the energy distributions are nicely reproduced. Thus, a dynamical model using Hagedorn resonance states, supplemented by a strangeness saturation suppression factor, is able to explain essential features (multiplicities, exponential slope) of experimental data for strangeness production in nucleus-nucleus collisions close to threshold.

## Key claim

A dynamical transport model using Hagedorn resonance states, supplemented by a strangeness saturation suppression factor \(\gamma_s = 0.3\), can explain essential features (multiplicities and exponential slopes) of strangeness production in nucleus-nucleus collisions close to threshold, but the \(\phi/K^-\) ratio is overestimated (0.85 for Au+Au and 0.77 for Ar+KCl) compared to HADES data (0.52 and 0.37 respectively), indicating that the model produces too many \(\phi\) mesons.

## Method

The authors implemented a microcanonical bootstrap of Hagedorn states with conserved baryon number \(B\), strangeness \(S\), and isospin \(I\) into the GiBUU transport model, replacing most of its default hadronic interactions. Hagedorn states are formed from nucleon-nucleon collisions and can gather additional energy by picking up further nucleons, acting as energy reservoirs; their creation and decay are governed by detailed balance with a single radius parameter \(R = 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}\). A strangeness saturation suppression factor \(\gamma_s = 0.3\) (fitted to p+p data) rescales cross sections and decay widths for processes involving \(s\bar{s}\) quark pairs, with an additional factor \(\gamma_\phi = \gamma_s^2\) for \(\phi\) meson production.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature \(T_H \sim 165\ \mathrm{MeV}\) derived from fits to the mass degeneracy spectrum using a fixed radius parameter \(R = 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}\) (yielding \(\sigma = 31\ \mathrm{mb}\), \(V = 4.2\ \mathrm{fm}^3\)).
- Strangeness saturation suppression factor \(\gamma_s = 0.3\) fitted to p+p collision data from compilations by Gazdzicki (1995, 1996).
- Calculated \(\phi/K^-\) ratio for Au(1.23 AGeV)Au (0-40% centrality): \(0.85 \pm 0.11\) (HADES data: \(0.52 \pm 0.16\)).
- Calculated \(\phi/K^-\) ratio for Ar(1.76 AGeV)KCl (minimum bias): \(0.77 \pm 0.06\) (HADES data: \(0.37 \pm 0.13\)).
- Default GiBUU calculation gives \(\phi/K^- \sim 0.13\) for Au+Au and \(\sim 0.11\) for Ar+KCl.
- Approximately 33% of produced \(\phi\) mesons are reabsorbed; 60% of all \(\phi\) interactions involve \(B=1\) Hagedorn states.
- For vanishing baryo-chemical potential, the fraction of particles with \(|S|=1\) is 35-45% for masses \(M > 2\ \mathrm{GeV}\); multistrange content reaches 25% (\(|S|=2\)), 15% (\(|S|=3\)), and 10% (\(|S|>3\)) at \(M = 10\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical bootstrap approach previously developed by Beitel, Gallmeister, and Greiner (2014, 2016, 2017) using quantum numbers \((B,S,Q)\) in UrQMD, now reformulated with isospin \(I\) for computational efficiency and implemented in GiBUU. It directly addresses HADES data for Au+Au at 1.23 AGeV (Adamczewski-Musch et al., 2017; Schuldes, PhD thesis) and Ar+KCl at 1.76 AGeV (Agakishiev et al., 2009), where traditional transport models struggle to explain the measured \(\phi/K^-\) ratio of 0.4-0.5 and thermal slope parameters \(T_{\mathrm{eff}} = 70-100\ \mathrm{MeV}\). The idea of using higher mass resonances for these data was previously explored by Steinheimer et al. (2015), but the present work provides a consistent bootstrap framework. The approach enables dynamical calculations of heavy ion collisions with Hagedorn states and can be compared to thermal freeze-out curves from Andronic et al. (2005, 2008).
