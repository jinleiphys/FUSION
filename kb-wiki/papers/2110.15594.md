# Removing center of mass effects in response function and sum rule calculations based on the harmonic oscillator basis

> Response functions are at the heart of any comparison of theory with experiment in studies of the nuclear dynamics with electroweak probes. Calculations performed in the laboratory frame often suffer from center of mass contaminations that need to be removed. By confining the system in a harmonic oscillator, we derive a set of analytical formulas to subtract the center of mass effects from calculations of response functions and associated sum rules. After a general analytical derivation, we first deal specifically with the longitudinal response function appearing in electron scattering and provide expressions for the center of mass correcting functions. Next, we present a proof of principle study for the case of the electric dipole sum rules in a two-body problem with a numerical implementation of our formalism. These steps pave the way to applying the proposed method to heavier nuclei in the future.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytical formulas to subtract center-of-mass (CoM) contaminations from response functions and sum rules calculated in the laboratory frame using a harmonic oscillator basis, provided the ground state and response functions are computed with sufficiently high accuracy. The method is presented as a proof of principle for a two-body system, with the condition that it works best for the harmonic oscillator basis and that the external operator factorizes into a product of an intrinsic and a CoM part.

## Method

The authors assume the nuclear Hamiltonian has been solved in the laboratory frame using single-particle coordinates and a harmonic oscillator basis, with an added CoM harmonic oscillator potential to ensure factorization of the wave function. They then derive recursive relations connecting the total response function \(R(\omega)\) and sum rules \(S_n\) (calculated in the laboratory frame) to the intrinsic response function \(R^{\rm I}(\omega)\) and intrinsic sum rules \(S_n^{\rm I}\), using analytically calculable CoM transition probabilities \(K_N^{\rm CoM}\) and the harmonic oscillator frequency \(\Omega\). The method is applied to the longitudinal response function in electron scattering and to electric dipole sum rules in a two-body system.

## Key numbers

* The CoM excitation energies are quantized as \(E - E_0 = N \hbar \Omega\), with \(N = 0, 1, 2, \ldots\) and \(N = 2 N_r + J\), where \(J \leq N\).
* The CoM ground state wave function is given by \(\Psi_{0 00}^{\rm CoM} ({\bf R}_{\rm CoM}) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{4\pi b_{\rm CoM}^{3} \Gamma \left(3/2 \right)}} e^{- \frac{R_{\rm CoM}^{2}}{2b_{\rm CoM}^{2}}}\), with \(b_{\rm CoM} = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{ A m_N \Omega}}\).
* The intrinsic sum rule of order zero is obtained as \(S_0^{\rm I} = S_0 / \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} K_m^{\rm CoM}\).
* The intrinsic sum rule of order one is \(S_1^{\rm I} = \frac{1}{\sum_{m=0}^{\infty} K_m^{\rm CoM}} \left[ S_1 - \Omega S_0^{\rm I} \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} m K_m^{\rm CoM} \right]\).

## Context

This work extends earlier treatments of the CoM problem in nuclear many-body theory, such as the Lawson method (Gloeckner and Lawson, 1974) and approaches for no-core shell model (Roth, 2009) and coupled-cluster theory (Mihaila and Heisenberg, 1999; Hagen et al., 2009), which focused primarily on ground-state properties. The paper provides an analytical framework for removing CoM effects from response functions and sum rules, which are essential for comparing theory with electroweak scattering experiments. The formalism enables future applications to heavier nuclei, where using Jacobi coordinates becomes impractical due to antisymmetrization difficulties for systems with \(A > 7\).
