# Comments on the electromagnetic decay of $Λ(1520)$

> The electromagnetic decay processes of excited hyperon states are a very sensitive probe of the structure of hyperons. We will argue that the recent measurements of electromagnetic decay rates indicate that the wave functions of the hyperon ground states should contain sizeable components of excited quark states (configuration mixing). Flavor-SU(3) is a broken symmetry and it appears that the hyperon wave functions should preferably be written in a $uds$-basis, where only the light $u$ and $d$ quarks are symmetrized, and not in the usual SU(6)-basis.

## Key claim

The measured radiative decay rate of Λ(1520) → γ Λ(1116), Γγ = 167 ± 43 +26−12 keV, is consistent with non-relativistic quark model predictions that include configuration mixing in the hyperon ground state wave function, but only when the ground state Λ(1116) is described in the uds-basis (where only the light u and d quarks are symmetrized) rather than the usual SU(6)-basis, and when the ground state contains sizeable admixtures of excited quark states.

## Method

The author compares the measured radiative decay width Γγ of Λ(1520) to predictions from several quark models: non-relativistic quark models (NRQM) in both the SU(6)-basis and the uds-basis, a relativistic constituent quark model (RCQM), the Bonn constituent quark model (Bonn-CQM), the MIT bag model, and the chiral/cloudy bag model. The comparison focuses on the coefficients a, b, and c describing the flavor-multiplet composition of the Λ(1520) wave function (Eq. 3) and on whether the Λ(1116) ground state is taken as a pure SU(6) state (Eq. 1) or as a configuration-mixed state (Eq. 2) derived from the Isgur-Karl treatment of the color hyperfine interaction.

## Key numbers

*   The measured decay rate for Λ(1520) → γ Λ(1116) is Γγ = 167 ± 43 +26−12 keV (CLAS collaboration, 2005).
*   The NRQM in the SU(6)-basis predicts Γγ ≈ 96–100 keV (Dharmawadana et al., 1983; Kaxiras et al., 1985; Copley et al., 1969).
*   The NRQM in the uds-basis, using the configuration-mixed Λ(1116) wave function, predicts Γγ = 154 keV (Kaxiras et al., 1985).
*   The MIT bag model predicts Γγ = 46 keV (Kaxiras et al., 1985).
*   The chiral/cloudy bag model predicts Γγ = 32 keV (Umino and Myhrer, 1991, 1993).
*   The relativistic constituent quark model (RCQM) predicts Γγ = 215 keV (Warns et al., 1991).
*   The Bonn constituent quark model (Bonn-CQM) predicts Γγ = 258 keV (Van Cauteren et al., 2006).
*   The photon energy in this decay is Eγ ≈ 400 MeV.
*   In the bag model, the quark energy differences are Ep3/2 − Es1/2 ≈ 230 MeV and Ep1/2 − Es1/2 ≈ 350 MeV (for a bag radius ≈ 1 fm).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Isgur-Karl non-relativistic quark model (Isgur and Karl, 1977, 1978) and the uds-basis approach to hyperon structure, contrasting it with bag model predictions (Kaxiras et al., 1985; Umino and Myhrer, 1991, 1993). The measured rate resolves a long-standing experimental discrepancy between the earlier result of Bertini et al. (1984, 1987), which found Γγ < 50 keV, and the results of Mast et al. (1968) and Antipov et al. (2004). The analysis enables future experimental determination of other radiative decay rates from excited baryon states and highlights the need to resolve the theoretical problem that quark models predict the Λ(1405) and Λ(1520) to be nearly degenerate, suggesting that Λ(1405) may contain a dominant meson-baryon component as originally proposed by Dalitz.
