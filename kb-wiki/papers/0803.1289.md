# Reply on ``Comment on neutrino-mixing interpretation of the GSI time anomaly''by C. Giunti, arXiv:0801.4639 [nucl-th]

> Here we reply critically to the comments by Giunti (arXiv:0801.4639 [nucl-th]) and justify our explanation of the experimentally observed periodic interference term in the rate of the K-shell electron capture decay of the H-like ions 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ as a neutrino-flavour mixing.

# Reply on "Comment on neutrino-mixing interpretation of the GSI time anomaly" by C. Giunti

## Key claim
The authors defend their interpretation that the periodic modulation observed in the electron-capture decay rate of H-like ions 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ at GSI arises from neutrino-flavor mixing, arguing that Giunti's proposed wave function for the final-state neutrino violates standard time-dependent perturbation theory and fails to produce an asymptotic detectable neutrino state.

## Method
The authors analyze the electron-capture decay amplitude using time-dependent perturbation theory to first order in the Fermi constant, defining the decay amplitude as a coherent sum over massive neutrino states (ν₁, ν₂, ν₃) rather than as an incoherent sum weighted by the Pontecorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata (PMNS) mixing matrix elements. They apply the same coherent-sum formalism to the π⁺ → μ⁺ + ν decay to test consistency with known experimental data. The authors also examine the asymptotic behavior (t → ∞) of Giunti's proposed neutrino wave function using plane-wave approximations and delta-function limits.

## Key numbers
- Period of the observed oscillation in the EC-decay rate: T_d ≈ 7 sec, with amplitude a_EC = 0.20(2) (from GSI data)
- Lorentz factor of stored ions: γ = 1.43
- For the π⁺ decay test: theoretical decay rate λ_π⁺ = 2.49 × 10⁻¹⁴ MeV, compared with experimental value λ_π⁺(exp) = 2.53 × 10⁻¹⁴ MeV (PDG 2006)
- Calculated oscillation periods for π⁺ decay: T₂₁ = 1.11 × 10⁻³ sec, T₃₁ = 2.04 × 10⁻⁴ sec, T₃₂ = 2.04 × 10⁻⁴ sec (using Δm²₃₁ ≈ Δm²₃₂ = 2.40 × 10⁻³ eV²)
- π⁺ lifetime: τ_π⁺ = 2.60 × 10⁻⁸ sec (PDG 2006)
- Mixing angle used: θ₁₂ = 33.9 degrees (PDG 2006), with θ₁₃ = 0

## Context
This reply addresses the critique by Giunti (2008) who argued that neutrino mixing cannot explain the GSI time anomaly because the electron-capture decay rate should involve an incoherent sum over massive neutrino channels, producing no interference term. The authors extend their earlier work (Ivanov et al., 2008) and contrast their approach with standard neutrino oscillation formalism (Grimus, 2001; PDG 2006). They also reference GSI experimental data (GSI1, GSI2) on EC and β⁺ decay ratios of H-like and He-like ions. The calculation of π⁺ decay rate is presented as a consistency check showing that the coherent-sum formalism reproduces the known pion lifetime. The paper enables further debate on whether neutrino-flavor mixing can produce observable interference effects in weak decay rates when the final-state neutrino is not detected.
