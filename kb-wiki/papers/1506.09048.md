# Spectra and Scattering of Light Lattice Nuclei from Effective Field Theory

> An effective field theory is used to describe light nuclei, calculated from quantum chromodynamics on a lattice at unphysically large pion masses. The theory is calibrated at leading order to two available data sets on two- and three-body nuclei for two pion masses. At those pion masses we predict the quartet and doublet neutron-deuteron scattering lengths, and the alpha-particle binding energy. For $m_π=510~$MeV we obtain, respectively, $^4a_{\rm nD}=2.3\pm 1.3~$fm, $^2a_{\rm nD}=2.2\pm 2.1~$fm, and $B_α^{}=35\pm 22~$MeV, while for $m_π=805~$MeV $^4a_{\rm nD}=1.6\pm 1.3~$fm, $^2a_{\rm nD}=0.62\pm 1.0~$fm, and $B_α^{}=94\pm 45~$MeV are found. Phillips- and Tjon-like correlations to the triton binding energy are established. Higher-order effects on the respective correlation bands are found insensitive to the pion mass. As a benchmark, we present results for the physical pion mass, using experimental two-body scattering lengths and the triton binding energy as input. Hints of subtle changes in the structure of the triton and alpha particle are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that pionless effective field theory (EFT), which omits explicit pion degrees of freedom, remains applicable for describing light nuclei (A ≤ 4) at unphysically large pion masses up to 805 MeV, provided the low-energy constants are recalibrated to lattice QCD data at each pion mass, a regime where the pion mass exceeds the Delta-nucleon mass difference and where nuclei are much more bound than in the physical world.

## Method

The authors calibrate a leading-order (LO) pionless EFT Hamiltonian, containing two-nucleon contact interactions and a single three-nucleon contact force, to lattice QCD binding energies of the dineutron, deuteron, and triton at two unphysical pion masses (510 MeV and 805 MeV). They then solve the Schrödinger equation for the alpha particle and for neutron-deuteron scattering using two complementary few-body methods: the effective-interaction hyperspherical-harmonic (EIHH) method and the refined resonating-group method (RGM). The theory is also benchmarked at the physical pion mass using experimental two-body scattering lengths and the triton binding energy as input.

## Key numbers

- For m_π = 510 MeV: quartet neutron-deuteron scattering length ⁴a_nD = 2.3 ± 1.3 fm, doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length ²a_nD = 2.2 ± 2.1 fm, and alpha-particle binding energy B_α = 35 ± 22 MeV.
- For m_π = 805 MeV: quartet neutron-deuteron scattering length ⁴a_nD = 1.6 ± 1.3 fm, doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length ²a_nD = 0.62 ± 1.0 fm, and alpha-particle binding energy B_α = 94 ± 45 MeV.
- Lattice QCD input binding energies at m_π = 510 MeV: dineutron 7.4 ± 2 MeV, deuteron 11.5 ± 2 MeV, triton 20.3 ± 4.5 MeV (Yamazaki 2012).
- Lattice QCD input binding energies at m_π = 805 MeV: dineutron 15.9 ± 4 MeV, deuteron 19.5 ± 5 MeV, triton 53.9 ± 10.7 MeV (Beane 2012).
- Physical pion mass benchmark uses experimental scattering lengths: inverse singlet np scattering length −8.31 fm⁻¹, inverse triplet np scattering length 36.4 fm⁻¹ (Dumbrajs 1983).

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach previously applied to binding energies of A ≤ 6 nuclei at m_π = 805 MeV by Barnea (2013) to a second pion mass (510 MeV) and to scattering observables (neutron-deuteron scattering lengths) for the first time. The results establish Phillips-like correlations (doublet nD scattering length versus triton binding energy) and Tjon-like correlations (alpha binding energy versus triton binding energy) at unphysical pion masses, with higher-order effects on these correlation bands found insensitive to the pion mass. This enables future lattice QCD calculations to test these predictions and provides a benchmark for extending chiral EFT to lower pion masses once lattice data reaches sufficiently small quark masses.
