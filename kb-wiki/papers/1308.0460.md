# Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay transition matrix elements within mechanisms involving light Majorana neutrinos, classical Majorons and sterile neutrinos

> In the PHFB model, uncertainties in the nuclear transition matrix elements for the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $\ ^{94,96}$Zr, $^{98,100}$Mo, $^{104}$Ru, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd isotopes within mechanisms involving light Majorana neutrinos, classical Majorons and sterile neutrinos are statistically estimated by considering sets of sixteen (twenty-four) matrix elements calculated with four different parametrization of the pairing plus multipolar type of effective two-body interaction, two sets of form factors and two (three) different parameterizations of Jastrow type of short range correlations. In the mechanisms involving the light Majorana neutrinos and classical Majorons, the maximum uncertainty is about 15% and in the scenario of sterile neutrinos, it varies in between approximately 4 (9)%--20 (36)% without(with) Jastrow short range correlations with Miller-Spencer parametrization, depending on the considered mass of the sterile neutrinos.

## Key claim

Within the PHFB model, the uncertainty in nuclear transition matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay is statistically estimated to be at most about 15% for mechanisms involving light Majorana neutrinos and classical Majorons, while for sterile neutrino mechanisms the uncertainty ranges from approximately 4% to 20% (without Jastrow short-range correlations) or 9% to 36% (with Miller-Spencer Jastrow short-range correlations), depending on the sterile neutrino mass.

## Method

The authors employed the projected-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model to calculate nuclear transition matrix elements for the 0+ to 0+ ground-state transition of neutrinoless double-beta decay in 94,96Zr, 98,100Mo, 104Ru, 110Pd, 128,130Te, and 150Nd. They generated sets of sixteen (for light Majorana neutrino and classical Majoron mechanisms) or twenty-four (for sterile neutrino mechanism) matrix elements by varying four parametrizations of the pairing-plus-multipolar effective two-body interaction, two sets of form factors for finite nucleon size, and two or three parametrizations of Jastrow-type short-range correlations, then computed the mean and standard deviation to quantify uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- For light Majorana neutrino and classical Majoron mechanisms: maximum uncertainty in nuclear transition matrix elements is about 15%.
- For sterile neutrino mechanism without Jastrow short-range correlations: uncertainty varies between approximately 4% and 20%, depending on sterile neutrino mass.
- For sterile neutrino mechanism with Miller-Spencer Jastrow short-range correlations: uncertainty varies between approximately 9% and 36%, depending on sterile neutrino mass.
- For 100Mo with PQQ1 parametrization, the total NTME |M(0nu)| ranges from 5.8716 (with SRC1) to 7.6736 (with SRC3 and closure energy A/2), depending on form factor set and short-range correlation choice.
- For 100Mo with PQQ1 parametrization and FNS1, the total NTME |M(0nu)| is 6.8305 without short-range correlations.

## Context

This work extends previous PHFB calculations by Rath et al. (2010, 2012, 2013) and Chan et al. (2009) to include systematic uncertainty estimates for multiple decay mechanisms across a range of isotopes. It provides statistical uncertainties that can be compared with QRPA-based uncertainty analyses by Rodin et al. (2003, 2006) and Suhonen et al. (2005), and with the spread of results across different nuclear models as compiled by Bahcall et al. (2004) and Avignone et al. (2005). The results enable more reliable extraction of neutrino mass parameters from future experimental half-life measurements by providing quantified theoretical error bars.
