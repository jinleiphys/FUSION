# Tetrahedral and Triangular Deformations of $Z=N$ Nuclei in Mass Region $A \sim 60-80$

> We study static non-axial octupole deformations in proton-rich $Z=N$ nuclei, $^{64}$Ge, $^{68}$Se, $^{72}$Kr, $^{76}$Sr, $^{80}$Zr and $^{84}$Mo, by using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculation with no restrictions on the nuclear shape. The calculation predicts that the oblate ground state in $^{68}$Se is extremely soft for the $Y_{33}$ triangular deformation, and that in $^{80}$Zr the low-lying local minimum state coexisting with the prolate ground state has the $Y_{32}$ tetrahedral deformation.

## Key claim

The Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculation predicts that the oblate ground state in $^{68}$Se is extremely soft for the $Y_{33}$ triangular deformation, and that in $^{80}$Zr the low-lying local minimum state coexisting with the prolate ground state has the $Y_{32}$ tetrahedral deformation, but these are predictions from a mean-field model with no experimental confirmation yet for most of these nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations using the Skyrme III interaction on a 3D Cartesian mesh with a 1 fm grid spacing inside a spherical box of radius 12 fm, imposing no symmetry restrictions on the nuclear shape. They generated five initial states with different quadrupole deformations (ranging from oblate to prolate) and added octupole distortion of $\alpha_{3m}=0.1$ for all m, then solved for the ground and local minimum states in six even-even $Z=N$ nuclei: $^{64}$Ge, $^{68}$Se, $^{72}$Kr, $^{76}$Sr, $^{80}$Zr, and $^{84}$Mo.

## Key numbers

- $^{80}$Zr: the second minimum state shows the largest octupole deformation of $\beta_3 = \beta_{32} = 0.24$ (tetrahedral $Y_{32}$ deformation) with no quadrupole deformation; the energy gain from the spherical solution is 0.71 MeV
- $^{68}$Se: the oblate ground state has $\beta_3 = \beta_{33} = 0.15$ (triangular $Y_{33}$ deformation) with quadrupole deformation $\beta = 0.25, \gamma = 60^\circ$
- $^{80}$Zr: the energy difference between spherical and strongly prolate deformed solutions reduces from 1.61 MeV to 0.90 MeV when including the tetrahedral deformation
- $^{76}$Sr: the oblate local minimum has $\beta_3 = \beta_{33} = 0.16$ and the spherical minimum has $\beta_3 = \beta_{32} = 0.12$
- $^{84}$Mo: the spherical local minimum has $\beta_3 = \beta_{30} = 0.13$ (axial octupole)
- $^{72}$Kr: the oblate local minimum has $\beta_3 = \beta_{33} = 0.05$ and the prolate minimum has $\beta_3 = \beta_{31} = 0.03$
- $^{64}$Ge: the ground state is triaxial with $\beta = 0.27, \gamma = 25^\circ$ and negligible octupole deformation ($\beta_3 = 0.01$)

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of non-axial octupole deformations (Eichler and Faessler 1970; Leander and Larsson 1975) and the shell correction method calculations by Nazarewicz et al. (1984) and Skalski (1991), which considered only axially symmetric octupole deformations or limited combinations of non-axial parameters. The predictions of tetrahedral deformation in $^{80}$Zr and triangular deformation in $^{68}$Se provide specific candidates for experimental searches using E3 transition measurements and characteristic rotational band structures, potentially enabling the first observation of static non-axial octupole deformations in medium-mass nuclei.
