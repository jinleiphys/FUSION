# Fusion and quasi-fission dynamics in nearly-symmetric reactions

> Some nearly-symmetric fusion reactions are systematically investigated with the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model. By introducing two-body inelastic scattering in the Fermi constraint procedure, the stability of an individual nucleus and the description of fusion cross sections at energies near the Coulomb barrier can be further improved. Simultaneously, the quasi-fission process in $^{154}$Sm+$^{160}$Gd is also investigated with the microscopic dynamics model for the first time. We find that at energies above the Bass barrier, the fusion probability is smaller than $10^{-5}$ for this reaction, and the nuclear contact-time is generally smaller than $1500$ fm/c. From the central collisions of Sm+Gd, the neutron-rich fragments such as $^{164,165}$Gd, $^{192}$W can be produced in the ImQMD simulations, which implies that the quasi-fission reaction could be an alternative way to synthesize new neutron-rich heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, with a new two-body inelastic scattering step added to the Fermi constraint procedure, reduces spurious nucleon emission and improves fusion cross-section predictions at near-barrier energies; for the nearly-symmetric reaction 154Sm+160Gd at energies above the Bass barrier, the fusion probability is smaller than 10^-5, and quasi-fission fragments may offer an alternative route to neutron-rich heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD model (version v2.2), in which each nucleon is a Gaussian wave packet propagated by a mean field derived from a Skyrme-type effective interaction (parameter set IQ3a). A Fermi constraint procedure was modified to include two-body inelastic scattering (momentum transfer when |p1-p2| > pF = 260 MeV/c, with transfer factor ft = 5×10^-6) in addition to elastic scattering, to reduce spurious nucleon emission. Fusion probabilities were obtained by simulating events at each center-of-mass energy and impact parameter, counting events where the center-to-center distance falls below the compound-nucleus radius; for 154Sm+160Gd, 10^5 events were generated at Ec.m. = 440 MeV and b = 1 fm to extract the fusion probability.

## Key numbers

- Spurious emitted nucleons at t = 2000 fm/c reduced from 1.1 to 0.56 for 92Zr and from 2.6 to 1.75 for 132Sn (reductions of 50% and 33%, respectively) when comparing ImQMD-v2.2 to v2.1.
- For 154Sm+160Gd at Ec.m. = 440 MeV (1.1 times the Bass barrier of 393 MeV) and b = 1 fm, the fusion probability is less than 10^-5 based on 10^5 simulation events.
- Nuclear contact-time for 154Sm+160Gd is generally smaller than 1500 fm/c.
- Production probabilities at t = 2000 fm/c for neutron-rich fragments from central collisions (b = 1 fm) of 154Sm+160Gd: 2.5×10^-3 for 164Gd, 10^-3 for 165Gd, and 5×10^-4 for 192W.
- The smallest distance Rmin between two nuclei in back-angle quasi-elastic scattering is about 2 fm larger than the sum of charge radii at Ec.m. = Bm.p., and about 1 fm larger at Ec.m. = 1.1 Bm.p..

## Context

This work extends the ImQMD model (previously tested on asymmetric reactions like 16O-induced fusion by Wang (2014)) to nearly-symmetric systems and to the quasi-fission process in 154Sm+160Gd for the first time. It addresses the large discrepancy in predicted fusion probabilities for such systems between different fusion-by-diffusion models (e.g., Choudhury and Gupta (2014) versus Cap et al. (2014), which differed by 13 orders of magnitude for 154Sm+154Sm). The results suggest that quasi-fission reactions could be used to synthesize neutron-rich heavy nuclei (mass region 165 ≤ A ≤ 205) that are difficult to produce via traditional fusion, enabling future mass measurements of nuclei like 164,165Gd and 192W.
