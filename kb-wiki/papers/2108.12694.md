# Quantal diffusion description of isotope production by multinucleon transfer mechanism in ${}^{48}\text{Ca}+{}^{238}\text{U}$ collisions

> As an extension of previous work, we calculate the production cross-section of heavy neutron-rich isotopes by employing the quantal diffusion description to ${}^{48} \text{Ca} + {}^{238} \text{U}$ collisions. The quantal diffusion is deduced from stochastic mean-field approach, and transport properties are determined in terms of time-dependent single-particle wave functions of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. As a result, the approach allows for prediction of production cross-sections without any adjustable parameters. The secondary cross-sections by particle emission are calculated with the help of the statistical GEMINI++ code.

# Quantal diffusion description of isotope production by multinucleon transfer mechanism in 48Ca+238U collisions

## Key claim

The quantal diffusion approach, derived from the stochastic mean-field (SMF) method and using time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) single-particle wave functions, can predict production cross-sections of heavy neutron-rich isotopes without any adjustable parameters, provided the bombarding energy is close to the fusion barrier and the detector acceptance range is accounted for. The authors note that TDHF calculations are not very reliable for determining fusion probability at this energy, so they assume near-central collisions lead to fusion and determine fission probability using the GEMINI++ code.

## Method

The authors employ the quantal diffusion description based on the SMF approach, where transport coefficients (drift and diffusion) for neutron and proton transfer are determined entirely from TDHF time-dependent single-particle wave functions. Coupled Langevin equations for fluctuations in neutron and proton numbers of projectile-like fragments are solved, with drift coefficient derivatives obtained via Einstein relations using a two-parabola approximation of the potential energy surface in the (N,Z)-plane. Primary fragment probability distributions are given as correlated Gaussians, and secondary cross-sections after particle emission are calculated using the statistical GEMINI++ code for target-like fragments with proton numbers Z=64-80.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: E_c.m. = 193.1 MeV (fusion barrier located around E_bar = 193.8 MeV)
- Initial angular momentum range for detector acceptance: ℓ = 38-56 ħ (tip geometry only)
- Reduced curvature parameters: α = 0.13 (iso-vector direction), β = 0.05 (iso-scalar direction)
- Normalization constant for primary yield: η = 214
- Critical angular momentum for fusion: ℓ_c = 5 ħ
- Neutron variances σ_NN² range from 115.7 (ℓ=38 ħ) to 91.6 (ℓ=56 ħ)
- Proton variances σ_ZZ² range from 50.5 (ℓ=38 ħ) to 40.2 (ℓ=56 ħ)
- Mass dispersions σ_AA² range from 234.0 (ℓ=38 ħ) to 183.9 (ℓ=56 ħ)
- Production cross-sections for heavy neutron-rich isotopes of mercury, gold and platinum with neutron numbers around 125-130 are on the order of several micro barns

## Context

This work extends previous quantal diffusion calculations by Ayik et al. (2018) that computed primary fragment mass yields for the same 48Ca+238U system, now providing isotope-specific cross-sections for Z=64-80 and incorporating fusion-fission contributions. The approach contrasts with phenomenological models such as the di-nuclear system model of Adamian et al. (2010) and Langevin-type stochastic models of Zagrebaev et al. (2008-2012), which require adjustable parameters. The predicted cross-sections for heavy neutron-rich isotopes are reported to be much larger than estimates given by Adamian et al. (2010). The results enable comparison with experimental data from Kozulin et al. (2012, 2014) and guide future searches for neutron-rich nuclei near the N=126 shell closure.
