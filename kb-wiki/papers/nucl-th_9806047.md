# Nucleus-Nucleus Bremsstrahlung from Ultrarelativistic Collisions

> The bremsstrahlung produced when heavy nuclei collide is estimated for central collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Soft photons can be used to infer the rapidity distribution of the outgoing charge. An experimental design is outlined.

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung spectrum from central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is a sensitive indicator of the degree of charge stopping, but the measurement requires discriminating between different stopping scenarios using the angular distribution of soft photons (10 keV to 3 MeV) at several angles between 2 and 10 degrees, rather than measuring the total radiated energy which is impractical at a collider.

## Method

The authors compute classical bremsstrahlung from the time-dependent currents of projectile and target nuclei before collision and the outgoing charged particles after collision, using a flat rapidity distribution model and the LEXUS linear extrapolation of nucleon-nucleon scattering to nucleus-nucleus collisions. They evaluate the photon energy and angular distribution for central Au+Au collisions at 100 GeV per nucleon in the center-of-mass frame, integrating over photon energies from 10 keV to 3 MeV to match a proposed detector design. The detector uses germanium crystals at four polar angles (3, 5, 7, and 9 degrees) with collimation and magnetic sweeping to reduce backgrounds, and a plug-based subtraction method to measure the background from secondary interactions.

## Key numbers

- Photon energies of interest: 10 keV to 3 MeV, matching the detector acceptance.
- Peak of bremsstrahlung angular distribution at RHIC: approximately 1 degree.
- Bremsstrahlung concentrated within 10 degrees of the beam line.
- For a flat rapidity distribution at 3 degrees, the pi0 decay background is smaller than the expected bremsstrahlung rate by a factor of 4 x 10^4 (based on HIJET calculations).
- Detector live time estimated at 85% with the proposed collimation and magnet scheme.
- Detector size: less than 1 cm^3 at 4 meters from the interaction point.
- Required running time: 300 hours at the initially anticipated RHIC luminosity of 1 central event per second.
- Validity of classical bremsstrahlung formula: for photon energies less than 4 to 20 MeV, corresponding to collision timescales of 10 to 50 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical study of nucleus-nucleus bremsstrahlung by Kapusta (intended for Bevalac energies of 250-2100 MeV per nucleon) and the later study by Bjorken and McLerran aimed at higher energies, correcting a sign error in their Lorentz transformation that had led to an expectation of GeV-range photons instead of the MeV-range found here. It also builds on the work of Dumitru, McLerran, Stocker and Greiner (using the RQMD model), who proposed studying charge stopping via total radiated energy, but the authors show that angular measurements are more practical at RHIC. The paper enables experimental determination of charge stopping and nuclear transparency at RHIC using a relatively modest detector, providing a method to distinguish between full stopping, 50% stopping, and minimal stopping scenarios.
