# Recent progress in the electroweak structure of light nuclei using quantum Monte Carlo methods

> Nuclei will play a prominent role in searches for physics beyond the Standard Model as the active material in experiments. In order to reliably interpret new physics signals, one needs an accurate model of the underlying nuclear dynamics. In this review, we discuss recent progress made with quantum Monte Carlo approaches for calculating the electroweak structure of light nuclei. We place particular emphasis on recent $β$-decay, muon capture, $0νββ$-decay, and electron scattering results.

## Key claim
Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electroweak observables in light nuclei (A <= 12) show that the long-standing "g_A quenching" problem is primarily explained by many-body correlations in the nuclear wave functions rather than by an artificial effective axial coupling, with two-body current corrections contributing only 2-3% in most cases. However, the sign of these two-body corrections can depend on the specific chiral interaction model used, as seen in the A=10 system where the NV2+3-Ia* model gives a negative correction while other models give positive corrections.

## Method
The authors review quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations combining variational Monte Carlo (VMC) trial wave functions with Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) propagation to solve the many-body Schrodinger equation for light nuclei. Two families of nuclear Hamiltonians and associated electroweak currents are employed: the phenomenological Argonne v18 (AV18) two-body potential with Urbana IX or Illinois-7 three-nucleon forces and meson-exchange currents, and the Norfolk chiral effective field theory (NV2+3) two- and three-body interactions with consistently derived chiral electroweak currents up to N3LO. Matrix elements are computed using mixed estimates where one wave function is GFMC-propagated and the other is variational, with corrections applied for off-diagonal transitions.

## Key numbers
- Two-body currents contribute up to 40% of the magnetic moments of nuclei with A <= 9 in calculations using chi-EFT currents with the AV18+IL7 potential
- Gamow-Teller matrix elements calculated using only the LO single-nucleon axial current operator with fully correlated QMC wave functions show few percent-level agreement with experimental data without artificial quenching
- Two-body current corrections enhance Gamow-Teller matrix elements by 2% to 3% in the hybrid approach combining AV18+IL7 interactions with chi-EFT currents
- The AV18 two-nucleon interaction was fit to np and pp scattering data at lab energies E_lab <= 350 MeV achieving chi^2/datum = 1.1
- The NV2 potential uses cutoff parameters [R_L, R_S] = [1.2 fm, 0.8 fm] for model class (a) and [1.0 fm, 0.7 fm] for model class (b)

## Context
This review extends the work of Schiavilla et al. (2002), Pastore et al. (2017), and King et al. (2020) on beta decay in light nuclei, and Gysbers et al. (2019) who performed No-Core Shell Model calculations of the same phenomena. The results enable reliable interpretation of precision beta-decay experiments searching for physics beyond the Standard Model, including tests of CKM matrix unitarity which currently shows a 3-sigma tension with the Standard Model expectation. The work also supports the goals of the Nuclear Theory for New Physics topical collaboration in providing robust ab initio evaluations for experimental searches.
