# Wilson loop calculation in QGP using non-supersymmetric AdS/CFT

> Previously, two of the present authors obtained the decoupling limit and the corresponding throat geometry of non-supersymmetric D3 brane solution of type IIB string theory. In analogy with the supersymmetric case, it describes the gravity dual of a non-supersymmetric gauge theory with QCD-like properties such as running coupling and confinement (or mass gap) in certain range of its parameters. In this paper, we consider a `black' version of the non-supersymmetric D3 brane solution in the decoupling limit and use this gravity background to holographically compute the expectation value of a time-like Wilson loop which, in turn, is related to the potential of a heavy quark-antiquark pair. By boosting the gravity solution along one of the brane directions and placing the pair at an arbitrary orientation with this direction, we numerically obtain the variation of the screening length as well as the potential with velocity, its orientation with respect to the direction of motion and other parameters of the theory. Remarkably enough, our results are in qualitative agreement with those obtained holographically in supersymmetric gauge theories indicating that these features are quite robust and universal as they are insensitive to the presence of any supersymmetry in the theory. The physical interpretations of the variations with respect to the other parameters of the theory, not observed in supersymmetric theory, have also been given.

## Key claim
The authors numerically compute the screening length and potential of a heavy quark-antiquark pair moving through a hot, non-supersymmetric plasma using a holographic gravity dual, and find qualitative agreement with results from supersymmetric gauge theories, suggesting these features are robust and insensitive to supersymmetry. The hedge is that the agreement is only qualitative, and the non-supersymmetric theory introduces additional parameters (δ, δ₁) whose effects are not present in the supersymmetric case.

## Method
The authors take a "black" version of the non-supersymmetric D3 brane solution in the decoupling limit, boost the gravity solution along one brane direction, and place a fundamental string probe whose endpoints on the boundary represent a heavy quark-antiquark pair at an arbitrary orientation θ to the boost direction. They compute the thermal expectation value of a time-like Wilson loop via the Nambu-Goto action, extract the dipole separation length ℓ and the QQ̄ potential E from the minimal world-sheet area, and numerically solve the resulting integral equations to obtain variations with velocity, orientation, and the theory parameters δ and δ₁.

## Key numbers
- Temperature of the "black" non-susy D3 brane near extremality: T = [(-2δ)^(1/4) / √γ] × (1 / (π ρ₀ sinh θ)), where δ must be negative for real temperature.
- For δ = -2 and γ = 2, the solution reduces to the ordinary black D3 brane with temperature T = 1/(π ρ₀ cosh θ) → 1/(π ρ₀ sinh θ) near extremality.
- The free energy per unit D3 brane volume is proportional to -T⁴ × (2/|δ|), where 0 < |δ| ≤ 2; δ = -2 corresponds to ordinary black D3 brane, and |δ| < 2 gives more negative free energy (more stable).
- The IR cut-off in the boosted metric occurs at u_c = u₀ (tanh^(4/δ) η - 1)^(-1/4), where tanh η = v.

## Context
This work extends the holographic Wilson loop calculations of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006, 2007) for N=4 SYM plasma to a non-supersymmetric gauge theory with QCD-like properties (running coupling, confinement). The non-supersymmetric D3 brane decoupling limit was previously developed by Nayek and Roy (2015, 2016), and the "black" version follows work by Bai (2006) and Lu (2007). The results enable future studies of heavy quark observables in holographic models that more faithfully represent real-world QGP, which is not supersymmetric, and raise open questions about the dynamical stability of the non-susy D3 brane solution via quasi-normal mode analysis.
