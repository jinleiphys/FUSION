# A note on Ξ_c(3055)^+ and Ξ_c(3123)^+

> Babar Collaboration announced two new excited charmed baryons $Ξ_c(3055)^+$ and $Ξ_c(3123)^+$. We study their strong decays assuming they are D-wave states. Some assignments are excluded by comparing our numerical results with the experimental values of the total widths of $Ξ_c(3055)^+$ and $Ξ_c(3123)^+$. We also suggest some possible decay modes, which will be helpful to determine the properties of $Ξ_c(3055)^+$ and $Ξ_c(3123)^+$.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the newly observed charmed baryons Ξ_c(3055)^+ and Ξ_c(3123)^+ are likely D-wave states, but this assignment is conditional: some specific D-wave quantum-number assignments are excluded by comparing calculated total widths with experimental values, while others remain possible.

## Method
The authors used the ^3P_0 quark-pair-creation model, previously applied to S-wave, P-wave, D-wave, and radially excited charmed baryons in their earlier work (Liu, 2007), to compute strong decay widths of Ξ_c(3055)^+ and Ξ_c(3123)^+ under various D-wave assignments. They adopted standard model parameters: γ = 13.4 for the quark-pair strength, harmonic-oscillator wave-function parameters α_ρ = α_λ = 0.6 GeV for the baryons, and meson size parameters R = 2.1 GeV⁻¹ for π and K mesons and R = 2.3 GeV⁻¹ for the D meson. The calculated total widths were compared to the experimental values to rule out incompatible assignments.

## Key numbers
- Mass of Ξ_c(3055)^+: 3054.2 ± 1.2 ± 0.5 MeV/c²
- Total width of Ξ_c(3055)^+: 17 ± 6 ± 11 MeV/c²
- Mass of Ξ_c(3123)^+: 3122.9 ± 1.3 ± 0.3 MeV/c²
- Total width of Ξ_c(3123)^+: 4.4 ± 3.4 ± 1.7 MeV/c²
- Quark-pair creation strength γ = 13.4 (dimensionless, used as universal parameter in the ^3P_0 model)
- Harmonic-oscillator parameters for baryon wave functions: α_ρ = α_λ = 0.6 GeV
- Meson size parameter R = 2.1 GeV⁻¹ for π and K mesons; R = 2.3 GeV⁻¹ for the D meson

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of charmed baryon strong decays by Liu (2007) using the ^3P_0 model, which previously addressed S-wave, P-wave, D-wave, and radially excited states including Λ_c(2880,2940)^+, Ξ_c(2980,3077)^+, and Ω_c(2768)^0. The analysis specifically tests D-wave assignments for the two new states reported by the Babar Collaboration (2007), complementing the earlier radial-excitation study of Ξ_c(3077)^+. The results enable future experimental searches to determine the quantum numbers of Ξ_c(3055)^+ and Ξ_c(3123)^+ by measuring forbidden decay modes and branching-fraction ratios among channels such as Ξ_c^0π^+, Ξ_c'(0)π^+, Ξ_c^{*0}π^+, Σ_c^{++}K^-, Σ_c^{*++}K^-, Λ_c^+K^0, and D^+Λ.
