# Insensitivity of the Yrast Spectra of Even-Even Nuclei to the T=0 two-body interaction matrix elements

> Calculations of the spectra of various even-even nuclei in the fp shell ($^{44}$Ti, $^{46}$Ti, $^{48}$Cr, and $^{50}$Cr) are performed with two sets of two-body interaction matrix elements. The first set consists of the matrix elements of the FPD6 interaction. The second set have the same T=1 two-body matrix elements as the FPD6 interaction, but all the T=0 two-body matrix elements are set equal to zero. Despite the drastic differences between the two interactions, the spectra they yield are very similar and indeed it is difficult to say which set gives a better fit to experiment. That the results for the yrast spectra are insensitive to the presence or absence of T=0 two-body matrix elements is surprising because the only bound two nucleon system has T=0, namely the deuteron. Also there is the general folklore that T=0 matrix elements are responsible for nuclear collectivity. Electric quadrupole transition rates are also examined. It is found that the reintroduction of T=0 matrix elements leads to an enhancement of B(E2)'s for lower spin transitions but in some cases for higher spin transitions one gets another surprising result that there is a small suppression.

## Key claim

The yrast spectra of even-even nuclei in the fp shell (44Ti, 46Ti, 48Cr, 50Cr) are surprisingly insensitive to the presence or absence of T=0 two-body interaction matrix elements, despite the fact that the T=0 channel contains the largest matrix elements in the FPD6 interaction and is responsible for the only bound two-nucleon system (the deuteron). The authors qualify that while spectra are similar, B(E2) transition rates show sensitivity, with enhancement for lower spins but suppression for some higher-spin transitions.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for the yrast states of 44Ti, 46Ti, 48Cr, and 50Cr using two sets of two-body matrix elements in the full fp shell. The first set used the complete FPD6 interaction; the second set (T0FPD6) retained all T=1 matrix elements from FPD6 but set all T=0 two-body matrix elements to zero. The resulting excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates were compared between the two interactions and against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 46Ti, the ratio of B(E2) values (T0FPD6/FPD6) ranges from 0.370 for the 4 to 6 transition to 0.703 for the 0 to 2 transition, showing enhancement when T=0 matrix elements are reintroduced.
- For 48Cr, the B(E2) ratio (T0FPD6/FPD6) for the 14 to 16 transition is 1.021, and for the 16 to 18 transition is 1.249, indicating suppression of B(E2) for higher spins when T=0 matrix elements are reintroduced.
- For 50Cr, the B(E2) ratio (T0FPD6/FPD6) for the 8 to 10 transition is 1.486, showing suppression of B(E2) when T=0 matrix elements are reintroduced at the backbending region.
- For 44Ti, the B(E2) ratio (T0FPD6/FPD6) ranges from 0.303 for the 4 to 6 transition to 0.736 for the 10 to 12 transition.
- Odd-spin excitation energies in 46Ti come down by about 1 MeV when T=0 matrix elements are set to zero.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of 44Ti (Robinson and Zamick, earlier papers) where the insensitivity was first observed, and follows the approach of Satula et al. (earlier work) who used a similar modification of effective interactions to study Wigner energies. The results challenge the general folklore that T=0 matrix elements are responsible for nuclear collectivity, and suggest that studying T=0 pairing by removing the channel entirely may be more fruitful than separating a pairing interaction from the rest of the Hamiltonian, as argued by Satula and Wyss (earlier work). The findings enable future experimental searches for odd-spin states in these nuclei to distinguish between the two interactions.
