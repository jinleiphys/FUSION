# Propagation of uncertainties in the nuclear DFT models

> Parameters of the nuclear density functional theory (DFT) models are usually adjusted to experimental data. As a result they carry certain theoretical error, which, as a consequence, carries out to the predicted quantities. In this work we address the propagation of theoretical error, within the nuclear DFT models, from the model parameters to the predicted observables. In particularly, the focus is set on the Skyrme energy density functional models.

## Key claim

The propagation of theoretical error from model parameters to predicted observables in nuclear DFT can be quantified using a linearized covariance matrix approach, but this procedure can only assess uncertainties within the model itself and cannot estimate errors from model deficiencies, as evidenced by systematic residuals near doubly-magic nuclei.

## Method

The author reviews the formalism for propagating uncertainties in Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) models, where the covariance matrix is computed in a linearized least-squares approximation around the minimum of the χ² function. The standard deviation of any predicted observable is then calculated from the covariance matrix and the partial derivatives of that observable with respect to model parameters, using the UNEDF0 parameterization as the primary example. Numerical derivatives are typically required, and the weights in the χ² function are set according to expected model accuracy rather than experimental error bars.

## Key numbers

- The r.m.s. deviation of UNEDF0 for binding energies over the even-even mass table was 1.4 MeV, and for charge radii 0.017 fm, consistent with the weights used in optimization (2 MeV for binding energies, 0.02 fm for charge radii).
- The PREX experiment determined the neutron skin thickness in ²⁰⁸Pb as r_ns = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm, which is the most model-independent measurement available.
- Current Skyrme-like functionals reproduce empirical single-particle data with a typical r.m.s. deviation of roughly 1 MeV.
- Experimental mass measurements with Penning traps can reach sub-keV accuracy, orders of magnitude more precise than the roughly 1 MeV accuracy of present mean-field models.

## Context

This work extends the covariance analysis previously performed for UNEDF0 (Kortelainen 2010), UNEDF1 (Kortelainen 2012), and UNEDF2 (Kortelainen 2014) parameterizations, as well as similar studies by Gao (2013) on error propagation in semi-magic nuclei and by Kortelainen (2013) on neutron skin thickness uncertainties. The results enable future EDF optimization schemes to include sensitivity analysis as a standard procedure, and highlight that planned experiments such as PREX-II and CREX could help constrain the poorly determined isovector parameters that dominate theoretical uncertainties in neutron-rich nuclei.
