# Color Superconductivity and Tsallis Statistics

> The generalized non-extensive statistics proposed by Tsallis have been successfully utilized in many systems where long range interactions are present. For high density quark matter an attractive long range interaction arising from single gluon exchange suggests the formation of a diquark condensate. We study the effects on this color superconducting phase for two quark flavors due to a change to Tsallis statistics. By numerically solving the gap equation we obtain a generalization of the universality condition, $\frac{2ϕ_{0}}{T_{C}}\approx 3.52$ and determine the temperature dependence of the gap. For the Tsallis parameter $q\approx 1$ the specific heat is exponential becoming more linear as q increases. This suggests that for larger values of q s-wave color superconductors behave like high $T_c$ superconductors rather than weak superconductors.

## Key claim

By replacing Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics with Tsallis non-extensive statistics in the gap equation for two-flavor color superconductivity, the universality condition generalizes to \(2\phi_0/(qT_C) \approx 3.52\) with no appreciable deviation from 3.52 for reasonable values of the Tsallis parameter \(q\), and the specific heat transitions from exponential to linear behavior as \(q\) increases.

## Method

The authors start from the Rischke-Pisarski formalism for color superconductivity in the weak-coupling regime, using the hard dense loop approximation and the soft integral form of the gap equation. They replace the standard Fermi-Dirac distribution with the generalized Tsallis Fermi distribution \(f_q = 1/[1+(q-1)\epsilon/T]^{1/(q-1)}+1\) and numerically solve the resulting gap equation at the Fermi surface for the critical temperature \(T_C\) and the temperature-dependent gap \(\phi(T)\), using a running QCD coupling with \(\Lambda_{QCD}=400\) MeV and a cutoff \(\delta \approx m_g\).

## Key numbers

- The generalized universality condition \(2\phi_0/(qT_C) \approx 3.52\) holds for the Tsallis parameter \(q\) values studied, with no appreciable deviation from 3.52.
- The running coupling constant is given by \(g^2 = (8\pi^2/\beta_0) / \ln(\mu^2/\Lambda_{QCD}^2)\) with \(\Lambda_{QCD}=400\) MeV.
- The gluon mass scale is \(m_g^2 = N_f g^2 \mu^2/(6\pi^2) + (N_C + N_f/2) g^2 T^2/9\).
- For the numerical solutions, the coupling is taken as \(g \approx 0.39\).
- One specific Tsallis parameter value used in the temperature dependence study is \(q = 1.178\).

## Context

This work extends the Rischke-Pisarski formalism for color superconductivity (Pisarski 1999) and the numerical studies of the 2SC gap equation by Zakout et al. (2002) by introducing Tsallis non-extensive statistics. The results suggest that for larger values of the Tsallis parameter \(q\), s-wave color superconductors behave more like high-\(T_c\) superconductors rather than weak (BCS-like) superconductors, as previously noted for high-\(T_c\) BCS superconductors by Uys et al. (2001). This enables future experimental differentiation between Tsallis and Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics in dense quark matter, particularly through measurements of the specific heat.
