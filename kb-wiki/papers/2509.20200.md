# Tensor Spin Polarization Induced by Curved Freeze-Out Hypersurface

> We investigate how the curvature of the freeze-out hypersurface polarizes massive vector bosons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Starting from the Proca Lagrangian and using the Wigner function formalism, we perform a systematic gradient expansion to obtain a covariant spin-polarization tensor expressed in terms of hydrodynamic fields and the curvature tensor of the freeze-out hypersurface. Analytic results for Bjorken and Gubser flows show that curvature anisotropy generates a non-zero tensor polarization. For $ϕ$ meson, we estimate the curvature contribution to its spin alignment as $ δΘ_{yy} \sim -10^{-4} $ to $ -10^{-3} $. We also find that the curvature contribution grows as the system size decreases, suggesting that spin polarization measurements in small systems may provide a clean probe of this geometric effect.

## Key claim

The curvature of the freeze-out hypersurface generates a non-zero tensor spin polarization for massive vector bosons at leading order in the gradient expansion, which is one order lower than the contributions from purely hydrodynamic fields. The effect is estimated to produce a spin alignment contribution for the phi meson of order δΘ_yy ∼ -10^{-4} to -10^{-3}, and grows as the system size decreases.

## Method

Starting from the Proca Lagrangian for massive vector bosons and using the Wigner function formalism, the authors perform a systematic gradient expansion of the local equilibrium density operator on a curved freeze-out hypersurface. They evaluate the first-order corrections to the Wigner function by computing connected correlators of the energy-momentum tensor and spin tensor with the Wigner operator, expanding the hypersurface geometry using a curvature tensor B^{μν} defined from the normal vector of the freeze-out surface. The resulting covariant spin-polarization tensor is expressed in terms of hydrodynamic fields (thermal current β_μ and spin potential Ω_{ρσ}) and the curvature tensor, and is then evaluated analytically for Bjorken and Gubser flow profiles.

## Key numbers

- For the φ meson, the curvature contribution to its spin alignment is estimated as δΘ_{yy} ∼ -10^{-4} to -10^{-3}
- The curvature contribution grows as the system size decreases, suggesting small collision systems may provide a cleaner probe of this geometric effect
- The paper provides analytic results for both Bjorken and Gubser flows, though specific numerical values for these flow profiles are not given in the truncated text

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector meson spin alignment (Zhang 2024, Yang 2024) that assumed the freeze-out hypersurface is mostly a hyperplane, thereby missing geometric effects from non-trivial hypersurface shapes. The curvature-induced tensor polarization appears at first order in gradients, one order lower than the purely hydrodynamic contributions calculated in those earlier works. The results suggest that spin alignment measurements in small collision systems, where curvature effects are enhanced, could serve as a clean probe of this geometric mechanism, complementing existing explanations for vector meson spin alignment such as strong φ mean fields (Sheng 2019, 2022, 2023), strong gluonic fields (Kumar 2023, Yang 2024), and critical fluctuations (Chen 2024).
