# Reply to 'Comment on: 'Non-perturbative finite T broadening of the rho meson and dilepton emission in heavy ion-collisions''

> Nota bene: the numerical calculation underlying Version 1 of this reply and the original work Phys.Rev. C71:064903,2005 contains a basic numerical error (wrong factor in the self-energy formulas of Phys. Rev.C71:064903,2005). This renders the numerical results presented there and in Version 1 of the reply invalid and enforces a careful reinvestigation of Phys. Rev.C71:064903,2005 and the reply. Calculations for an erratum of Phys. Rev. C71:064903,2005 are in progress.

## Key claim

The numerical calculation underlying the original work Phys.Rev. C71:064903,2005 and Version 1 of this reply contains a basic numerical error (wrong factor in the self-energy formulas), which renders all numerical results presented there invalid and enforces a careful reinvestigation.

## Method

The authors identify a basic numerical error in the self-energy formulas used in their original work Phys.Rev. C71:064903,2005 and in Version 1 of this reply. They announce that calculations for an erratum of the original paper are in progress, and that a corrected version of the reply manuscript is to be submitted.

## Key numbers

- No valid numerical results are presented; the paper reports that all numerical results from the original work and Version 1 of the reply are invalid due to a wrong factor in the self-energy formulas.

## Context

This reply addresses concerns raised by Riek, van Hees and Knoll regarding the application of Phi-derivable resummation schemes to the study of the broadening of the rho spectral function due to the pi-rho interaction in the original work by Ruppert and Renk (2005). The paper does not present new results but instead announces that an erratum is in progress, leaving the status of the original claims unresolved pending the corrected calculation.
