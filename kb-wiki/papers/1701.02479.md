# Euclidean to Minkowski Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and observables

> We propose a method to reconstruct the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in Minkowski space given the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter amplitude -- or alternatively the Light-Front wave function -- as input. The method is based on the numerical inversion of the Nakanishi integral representation and computing the corresponding weight function. This inversion procedure is, in general, rather unstable, and we propose several ways to considerably reduce the instabilities. In terms of the Nakanishi weight function, one can easily compute the BS amplitude, the LF wave function and the electromagnetic form factor. The latter ones are very stable in spite of residual instabilities in the weight function. This procedure allows both, to continue the Euclidean BS solution in the Minkowski space and to obtain a BS amplitude from a LF wave function.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to reconstruct the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in Minkowski space from the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter amplitude or the Light-Front wave function by numerically inverting the Nakanishi integral representation, but the inversion is an ill-posed problem requiring regularization (specifically Tikhonov regularization) to suppress instabilities; the resulting observables (electromagnetic form factors and Light-Front wave functions) remain stable despite residual instabilities in the extracted weight function.

## Method

The authors discretize the Nakanishi integral representation for the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (a two-dimensional Fredholm equation of the first kind) and for the Light-Front wave function (a one-dimensional equation) by expanding the unknown weight function in Gegenbauer polynomials and validating at Gaussian quadrature points. They solve the resulting linear systems using Tikhonov regularization (replacing the equation \(AC=B\) by \((A^\dagger A + \epsilon I)C = A^\dagger B\)) to suppress the oscillatory instabilities that arise from the extremely small eigenvalues of the kernel. The method is tested on an analytically solvable toy model and then applied to a dynamical case of two spinless particles (mass \(m=1\)) interacting via one-boson exchange (boson mass \(\mu=0.5\)) forming a bound state of total mass \(M=1.0\).

## Key numbers

* In the toy model, the determinant of the discretized matrix drops from \(\sim 10^{-35}\) for \(N=11\) points to \(\sim 10^{-59}\) for \(N=14\) points, demonstrating the ill-conditioned nature of the problem.
* For the toy model with \(N=16\) and no regularization, the extracted weight function at \(x=0\) gives \(g(0)\approx -10^3\) instead of the exact value \(g(0)=1\); for \(N=32\) with no regularization, \(g(0)\approx -10^7\) instead of \(g(0)=1\).
* For the toy model with \(N=16\) and Tikhonov regularization parameter \(\epsilon=10^{-10}\), the numerical solution coincides with the exact one within better than 1% precision.
* For the dynamical OBE case (masses \(m=1\), \(\mu=0.5\), \(M=1.0\)), the weight function \(g_E\) extracted from the Euclidean Bethe-Salpeter amplitude using \(N_x=4, N_z=2\) and \(\epsilon=10^{-10}\) reproduces the input Euclidean amplitude up to a normalization factor \(c_2=0.88\).

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi integral representation approach previously used by Kusaka (1997), Frederico (2012), and Frederico-Salme-Viviani (2014) to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space for simple kernels like one-boson exchange. It directly addresses the instability problem noted in the authors' earlier work (Carbonell et al., 2016) by applying Tikhonov regularization, and it enables the calculation of Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and electromagnetic form factors from Euclidean solutions (including those from lattice QCD calculations) without requiring direct analytic continuation. The method also provides a link between Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front Dynamics formalisms, allowing cross-calculation of amplitudes and wave functions between the two approaches.
