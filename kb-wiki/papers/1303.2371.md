# Electric Dipole Moments of Nucleons, Nuclei, and Atoms: The Standard Model and Beyond

> Searches for the permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs) of molecules, atoms, nucleons and nuclei provide powerful probes of CP violation both within and beyond the Standard Model (BSM). The interpretation of experimental EDM limits requires careful delineation of physics at a wide range of distance scales, from the long-range atomic and molecular scales to the short-distance dynamics of physics at or beyond the Fermi scale. In this review, we provide a framework for disentangling contributions from physics at these disparate scales, building out from the set of dimension four and six effective operators that embody CP violation at the Fermi scale. We survey existing computations of hadronic and nuclear matrix elements associated with Fermi-scale CP violation in systems of experimental interest, and quantify the present level of theoretical uncertainty in these calculations. Using representative BSM scenarios of current interest, we illustrate how the interplay of physics at various scales generates EDMs at a potentially observable level.

## Key claim

The paper provides a comprehensive framework for interpreting electric dipole moment (EDM) searches by connecting physics across multiple distance scales, from atomic and molecular scales up to the Fermi scale and beyond, while emphasizing that the theoretical uncertainty in hadronic and nuclear matrix elements currently limits the ability to pinpoint the underlying CP-violation mechanism.

## Method

The authors construct a systematic effective field theory approach that starts from dimension-four and dimension-six CP-violating operators at the Fermi scale, then runs and matches these operators down through hadronic, nuclear, and atomic scales. They compile existing calculations of hadronic and nuclear matrix elements using chiral perturbation theory, lattice QCD, QCD sum rules, and quark models, and provide benchmark values with quantified theoretical uncertainties for the neutron EDM, nuclear Schiff moments, and atomic EDM observables.

## Context

This review extends and systematizes earlier work on EDM phenomenology, building on the effective operator framework of Grzadkowski (2010) and the chiral symmetry considerations of Baluni (1978). It provides a unified treatment that connects the strong CP problem (motivating Peccei-Quinn symmetry and axions as reviewed by Kim in 2008) to BSM scenarios, while quantifying theoretical uncertainties that previous reviews by Pospelov (2005), Ellis (2008), and Dzuba (2012) did not systematically address. The framework enables future work to combine experimental EDM limits from multiple systems (neutron, diamagnetic atoms, paramagnetic atoms, molecules) to disentangle contributions from different CPV sources.
