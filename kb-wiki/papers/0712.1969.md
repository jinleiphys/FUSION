# Delta-excitations and the three-nucleon force

> We study the three-nucleon force in chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta-resonance degrees of freedom. We show that up to next-to-next-to-leading order, the only contribution to the isospin symmetric three-nucleon force involving the spin-3/2 degrees of freedom is given by the two-pion-exchange diagram with an intermediate delta, frequently called the Fujita-Miyazawa force. We also analyze the leading isospin-breaking corrections due to the delta. For that, we give the first analysis of the delta quartet mass splittings in chiral effective field theory. The charge-symmetry breaking three-nucleon force due to an intermediate delta excitation is small, of the order of a few keV.

## Key claim

Up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta-resonance degrees of freedom, the only non-vanishing isospin-symmetric three-nucleon force contribution involving spin-3/2 degrees of freedom is the two-pion-exchange diagram with an intermediate Delta (the Fujita-Miyazawa force); the charge-symmetry breaking three-nucleon force due to an intermediate Delta excitation is estimated to be small, of the order of a few keV.

## Method

The authors employed the small scale expansion (SSE) version of chiral effective field theory, ordering irreducible multi-nucleon diagrams according to Weinberg's power counting with an expansion parameter Q/Lambda that includes pion four-momenta, the pion mass, baryon three-momenta, and the nucleon-Delta mass splitting. They evaluated Delta contributions to the three-nucleon force up to NNLO, analyzed the Delta quartet mass splittings from strong and electromagnetic contributions using effective Lagrangians with spurion methods, and worked out the leading isospin-breaking three-nucleon force contributions from intermediate Delta excitations, eliminating the proton-neutron mass difference via field redefinitions following the method of Friar (2004).

## Key numbers

- The Delta mass splittings are parameterized by two parameters: delta m_Delta^1 = -5.3 +/- 2.0 MeV and delta m_Delta^2 = -1.7 +/- 2.7 MeV from one fit; an alternative fit gives delta m_Delta^1 = -3.9 MeV and delta m_Delta^2 = 0.3 +/- 0.3 MeV.
- The average Delta mass is taken as 1233 MeV, consistent with the Particle Data Group estimate of 1231 to 1233 MeV and the determination of Arndt (2006) of 1233.4 +/- 0.4 MeV.
- The coefficients for the 2pi-exchange three-nucleon force in the theory without explicit Delta at order Q^3 are a = -0.70 M_pi^{-1}, b = -2.34 M_pi^{-3}, d = -0.89 M_pi^{-3}; in the theory with explicit Delta at order Q^2 (fit 1) they are a = 0, b = -1.70 M_pi^{-3}, d = -0.42 M_pi^{-3}.
- The charge-symmetry breaking three-nucleon force due to an intermediate Delta excitation contributes to the three-nucleon binding energy at the level of a few keV.
- The chiral limit value of the N-Delta splitting is approximately 330 MeV, and the nucleon mass in the chiral limit is approximately 890 MeV, giving a chiral limit Delta mass of about 1220 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of three-nucleon forces by including explicit Delta-resonance degrees of freedom, building on the foundational work of Fujita and Miyazawa (1957) and the meson-theoretical approaches such as the Tucson-Melbourne, Brazilian, and Urbana-Illinois three-nucleon forces reviewed by Gloeckle. It provides the first quantitative analysis of Delta quartet mass splittings in chiral effective field theory including leading electromagnetic corrections, extending the chiral extrapolation formulae for strong splittings given by Tiburzi (2005). The results enable improved calculations of three-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory with explicit Deltas and allow comparison with the Delta-less theory, showing that both versions yield similar values for the 2pi-exchange three-nucleon force coefficients at NNLO when the same pion-nucleon scattering data are used to determine the low-energy constants.
