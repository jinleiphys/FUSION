# Effects of a parallel electromagnetic field in three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> In this work, we explore the ground state of QCD system and the relevant collective modes in a parallel electromagnetic (EM) field within the effective three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. From the features of neutral chiral condensates, three critical EM fields are identified in our study: $e\bar{I}_2^{c_i}~(i=1,2,3)$. Moreover, competition between QCD and QED anomalies is found: the negativeness of $π_{\rm d}^*$ and $π_{\rm s}^*$ is a signal of QCD anomaly dominance for all the flavors, and the positiveness of $π_{\rm s}^*$ beyond $e\bar{I}_2^{c_1}$ indicates QED anomaly dominance for strange quark. For the lowest-lying neutral collective modes, the masses of $Π$ and $H$ modes reduce to zero around the critical EM fields and the scalar-pseudoscalar components are exchanged between $Σ$ and $H$ modes, as can be seen in the crossing structure.

## Key claim

The paper identifies three critical parallel electromagnetic field strengths at which chiral condensates and collective modes undergo qualitative changes, and finds that the sign of the strange-quark pion condensate switches from negative to positive beyond the first critical field, indicating a transition from QCD anomaly dominance to QED anomaly dominance for the strange quark.

## Method

The authors use the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including the 't Hooft determinant term to account for the QCD U_A(1) anomaly, and study the ground state in a parallel electromagnetic field (E parallel to B). They solve the six coupled gap equations for scalar and pseudoscalar condensates of u, d, and s quarks, and compute the 6x6 polarization function matrix in the random phase approximation to obtain the pole masses of the neutral collective modes.

## Key numbers

- Three critical electromagnetic field strengths: eI2^c1 = (0.278 GeV)^2, eI2^c2 = (0.523 GeV)^2, eI2^c3 = (0.677 GeV)^2
- The transition at eI2^c1 is weak first order, at eI2^c2 is second order, and at eI2^c3 is strong first order
- Model parameters: m0u = m0d = 5.5 MeV, m0s = 140.7 MeV, Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G Lambda^2 = 1.835, K Lambda^5 = 12.36

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor NJL studies by Cao (2015) and Wang (2018) to the more realistic three-flavor case, where the 't Hooft determinant automatically incorporates the QCD U_A(1) anomaly. The results enable comparison of QCD and QED anomaly effects on pseudoscalar condensations, and suggest that anomalous pseudoscalar condensations beyond the chiral magnetic effect may be observable in heavy-ion collisions where parallel electromagnetic fields are relevant, as discussed by Zhao (2019).
