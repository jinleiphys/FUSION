# Probing proton transition momentum in neutron-rich matter

> Around the nuclear Fermi momentum, there is a transition of nucleon momentum distribution n(k) in nuclear matter, i.e., from a constant to the $1/k^{4}$ nucleon momentum distribution. While nowadays the transition momentum of minority in asymmetric matter is rarely studied and thus undetermined. In the framework of the IBUU transport model, proton transition momentum in nuclei is first studied. It is found that the transition momentum of proton is sensitive to the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio as well as the energetic photon production in neutron-rich nuclear reaction. This result may push the study of how the proton momentum is distributed in neutron-rich matter forward and help us to better understand the dynamics of both neutron-rich nuclear reactions and neutron stars.

# Probing proton transition momentum in neutron-rich matter

## Key claim
The transition momentum where the proton momentum distribution changes from constant to a 1/k⁴ tail in neutron-rich matter is not simply the proton Fermi momentum; the π⁻/π⁺ ratio and energetic photon production in heavy-ion collisions are sensitive to whether the proton high-momentum tail starts from the proton Fermi momentum or from the majority neutron Fermi momentum.

## Method
The author used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock initial density distributions and a nucleon momentum distribution that includes a high-momentum tail reaching about 2.75 times the local Fermi momentum, with 20% of nucleons in the HMT. Two scenarios were compared: case A where the proton 1/k⁴ tail starts at the proton Fermi momentum, and case B where it starts at the majority neutron Fermi momentum. The model included isospin- and momentum-dependent single nucleon potentials, pion production through NN⇌NΔ and Δ⇌Nπ processes, and energetic photon production from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung via the one boson exchange model.

## Key numbers
- 20% of nucleons in nuclei are correlated and reside in the high-momentum tail (HMT), consistent with prior work.
- The HMT extends to about 2.75 times the local Fermi momentum.
- In ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reactions at 300 MeV/nucleon incident beam energy, changing the proton HMT starting point from the proton Fermi momentum to the majority neutron Fermi momentum causes a decrease in the π⁻/π⁺ ratio of nearly 20%.
- The value E_kin^p - E_kin^n changes from about -2 to 2 MeV in matter with proton proportion x_p = 39.4%, and the change is more evident in more neutron-rich matter with x_p = 19.7%.
- In ⁴⁸Ca+¹²⁴Sn reactions at 45 MeV/nucleon, more energetic photons are produced when the proton HMT starts from the majority neutron Fermi momentum, especially in peripheral collisions.
- In ⁴⁰Ca+¹⁰⁰Sn reactions at 45 MeV/nucleon (equal numbers of neutrons and protons), changing the proton HMT starting point has no effect on energetic photon production.

## Context
This work extends the study of nucleon short-range correlations (SRC) and the high-momentum tail in asymmetric nuclear matter, building on the n-p dominance picture established by experiments (Hen 2014, Science 2014) showing that neutron-proton correlated pairs dominate. It addresses a question that microscopic theories such as the ladder self-consistent Green function approach (Rios 2009) and Brueckner theory with three-body force (Wang 2013) cannot answer: the minority transition momentum in asymmetric matter. The results suggest that ongoing pion measurements at RIBF at RIKEN in Japan for ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn at 300 MeV/nucleon can test the proton transition momentum, and the findings have implications for neutron star dynamics including cooling, proton superfluidity, and equation of state stiffness.
