# Electromagnetic $N-Δ(1232)$ transitions within the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics

> The electromagnetic $N-Δ(1232)$ transition amplitudes are calculated using the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics. The relativistic effects incorporated in the electromagnetic matrix elements give a good description of the transition amplitudes to the $Δ(1232)$ resonance, reproducing well the $Q^2$ behaviour of the data, apart from the low $Q^2$ one.

## Key claim

The point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics, combined with wave functions from a relativistic hyper-central constituent quark model, reproduces the transverse electromagnetic transition amplitudes to the Δ(1232) resonance well for momentum transfers Q² > 1 GeV², but the description fails at low Q² where quark-antiquark pair production mechanisms are expected to dominate.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a relativistic mass operator for the three-quark system using a variational method in momentum space, with a hyper-central potential including a linear confining term, a Coulomb-like term, and a hyperfine interaction. They then compute the electromagnetic N-Δ(1232) transition amplitudes using the point-form spectator impulse approximation, where the current operator is taken as a single-particle Dirac current and the velocity states are constructed via Bakamjian-Thomas method to keep Lorentz transformations kinematic.

## Key numbers

- The calculation reproduces the transverse transition amplitudes for Q² > 1 GeV², as shown in comparison with data from Azn and MAID analyses.
- The paper reports preliminary results for the transverse amplitudes A₁/₂ and A₃/₂ of the N-Δ(1232) transition; no explicit numerical values for these amplitudes are given in the text.
- The mass operator includes a hyperfine interaction term M_hyp and a linear confining term α x with a Coulomb-like term -τ/x, but the specific values of the parameters τ and α are not provided in the text.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic hyper-central potential model calculations of baryon electromagnetic properties, previously applied to transition form factors by Santopinto and Giannini, by incorporating a fully relativistic treatment of both the wave functions and the electromagnetic matrix elements. The point-form approach has been previously employed for nucleon form factors by Graz group and for pion and deuteron form factors by Desplanques and collaborators. The results enable future calculations of transition amplitudes to other low-lying nucleon resonances such as S₁₁(1535) and D₁₃(1520), and point toward the need for unquenched quark model calculations to address the low-Q² region.
