# Charge-Dependent Directed Flow in Symmetric Nuclear Collisions

> The directed flow ($v_1$) of identified hadrons ($π^{\pm}, K^{\pm}, p, \bar{p}, ϕ, Λ$, and $\barΛ$) is studied in symmetric nuclear collisions (O+O, Cu+Cu, Ru+Ru, Au+Au, and U+U) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV using the string-melting version of a multiphase transport model with improved quark coalescence. The mid-rapidity $v_1$-slope ($dv_1/dy$) and its charge-dependent splitting ($Δdv_1/dy$) between particles and anti-particles are investigated as a function of nuclear mass number ($A$) and collision centrality in both low-$p_\mathrm{T}$ (0.2$-$2.0 GeV/$c$) and high-$p_\mathrm{T}$ (2.0$-$5.0 GeV/$c$) regions. At low-$p_\mathrm{T}$, the $v_1$-slope shows weak system-size dependence, while at high-$p_\mathrm{T}$ strong system-size dependence is found and it becomes negative with nuclear mass number, reflecting the hard-soft asymmetry in particle production. The charge-dependent splitting $Δdv_1/dy$ reveals a striking baryon-meson dichotomy: baryon pairs ($p-\bar{p}$ and $Λ-\barΛ$) exhibit significant splitting that grows with system size, whereas meson pairs ($π^+-π^-$ and $K^+-K^-$) show minimal splitting. The effect of final state hadronic interactions on the $v_1$-slope is found to be negligible confirming that it is primarily generated during the partonic phase and coalescence process. A comparison of the AMPT results with measurements from the STAR experiment at RHIC in Au+Au collisions establish the transported quark contribution as a baseline for the observed charge-dependent $v_1$ splitting, on top of which electromagnetic field effects must be considered.

## Key claim
The charge-dependent splitting of directed-flow slope between baryons and anti-baryons in symmetric nuclear collisions at 200 GeV arises primarily from transported-quark dynamics in the AMPT model without electromagnetic fields, establishing a baseline on top of which electromagnetic field effects must be added to match STAR data; the splitting grows with system size for baryon pairs but remains minimal for meson pairs.

## Method
The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with improved quark coalescence to simulate O+O, Cu+Cu, Ru+Ru, Au+Au, and U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They extracted the mid-rapidity directed-flow slope (dv1/dy) by fitting v1(y) with a linear function in |y| < 1.0 for identified hadrons (pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, anti-p, phi, Lambda, anti-Lambda) in both low-pT (0.2-2.0 GeV/c) and high-pT (2.0-5.0 GeV/c) ranges, and computed the charge-dependent splitting Delta(dv1/dy) between particle-antiparticle pairs as a function of nuclear mass number and collision centrality.

## Key numbers
- The v1-slope at low pT (0.2-2.0 GeV/c) shows weak system-size dependence across O+O, Cu+Cu, Ru+Ru, Au+Au, and U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, with positive slopes for mesons and baryons and negative slopes for anti-baryons.
- At high pT (2.0-5.0 GeV/c), the v1-slope becomes increasingly negative with nuclear mass number, reaching maximum negative values for Au+Au and U+U collisions.
- The charge-dependent splitting Delta F for baryon pairs (p-anti-p and Lambda-anti-Lambda) increases with system size and saturates for the largest systems, while meson pairs (pi+-pi- and K+-K-) show minimal splitting that remains nearly constant across all systems.
- The effect of hadronic cascade time (0.4 fm/c versus 30 fm/c) on the v1-slope is found to be negligible for all particle species.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study on system-size dependence of v1 for inclusive charged hadrons (Bairathi and Nayak, 2025) using the same AMPT-SM model. It directly addresses recent STAR measurements (STAR Collaboration, 2023) of Delta(dv1/dy) for pions, kaons, and protons in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, where a sign change from central to peripheral collisions was attributed to electromagnetic field effects. The AMPT results provide a transported-quark baseline that must be combined with electromagnetic field calculations (Sandeep and collaborators, 2024) to achieve quantitative agreement with data. The predictions for small systems (O+O and Cu+Cu) serve as benchmarks for upcoming experimental measurements to isolate baryon-transport contributions from electromagnetic field effects.
