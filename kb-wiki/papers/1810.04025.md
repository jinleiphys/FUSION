# Analysis of multinucleon transfer reactions involving spherical and statically deformed nuclei using a Langevin-type approach

> An increasing interest in multinucleon transfer processes in low-energy deep inelastic (damped) collisions of heavy ions appeared in recent years is due, in part, to by the possibility of using them as a method of production of heavy neutron-enriched nuclei. Possible promising projectile-target combinations include nuclei deformed in the ground state (e.g., actinides). Mutual orientations of such the nuclei in the entrance channel of the reaction may significantly influence the reaction dynamics. Available experimental data on multinucleon transfer reactions with statically deformed as well as spherical heavy nuclei 144Sm + 144Sm, 154Sm + 154Sm, 160Gd + 186W, and 208Pb + 208Pb/238U have been analyzed within the developed model. A good agreement of the calculated quantities with the corresponding experimental data is reached. Special attention in the paper is paid to analysis of production possibility of the neutron-enriched isotopes of heavy and superheavy elements in multinucleon transfer processes in the 238U + 238U/248Cm/254Es collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Langevin-type dynamical model, extended to handle statically deformed nuclei, can reproduce experimental data for multinucleon transfer reactions involving both spherical and deformed systems, but the mutual orientation of deformed nuclei in the entrance channel strongly affects reaction dynamics only at near-barrier energies; these orientational effects disappear at energies well above the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed eight-dimensional Langevin model (Karpov, 2017) to include the rotational degrees of freedom of statically deformed nuclei. The potential energy is calculated as a time-dependent mixture of diabatic and adiabatic potentials, with an exponential relaxation from axially asymmetric to axially symmetric configurations using a relaxation time of 10⁻²¹ s. The model considers four limiting initial mutual orientations (tip-to-tip, side-to-side, tip-to-side, side-to-tip) and averages cross sections over these orientations, then applies a statistical model for de-excitation of primary fragments via neutron evaporation and sequential fission.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time for restoration of axial symmetry in the potential energy was set to τ_sym = 10⁻²¹ s.
- For the ¹⁵⁴Sm + ¹⁵⁴Sm reaction at E_c.m. = 485 MeV, the ground-state deformation of ¹⁵⁴Sm is β₂ = 0.27.
- For the ¹⁶⁰Gd + ¹⁸⁶W system, the calculated Coulomb barriers for different orientations range from V_C = 412 MeV (tip-to-tip) to V_C = 492 MeV (side-to-side).
- For the ²⁰⁸Pb + ²⁰⁸Pb reaction at E_c.m. = 726 and 786 MeV, the sequential fission rates were found to be 11% and 16% experimentally, compared to calculated values of 10% and 21%.
- The experimental charge resolution for the Sm+Sm reactions was 1.6 units (FWHM); for the Pb+Pb reactions it was 2.5 units (FWHM); for the Gd+W reaction the energy and mass resolutions were 12 MeV and 3 units (FWHM).
- The paper reports that neutron-enriched isotopes of heavy actinides can be produced with cross sections exceeding 1 μb in actinide collisions.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based dynamical model of Karpov (2017), which was previously applied to spherical nuclei, to handle statically deformed nuclei such as actinides. It builds on experimental studies of multinucleon transfer reactions by Hildenbrand (1983), Tanabe (1980), and Kozulin (2017), and on theoretical predictions by Zagrebaev and Greiner (2007, 2008) regarding production of neutron-rich nuclei. The results enable quantitative predictions for production of heavy transuranium nuclei in collisions of actinides, showing that while synthesis of new superheavy elements is unlikely due to exponential drops in cross section with atomic number, several neutron-enriched isotopes of heavy actinides may be reachable.
