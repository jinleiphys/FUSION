# Strange Isosinglet Versus Octet Scalar Axial Current

> The flavor SU(3)_f group symmetry of QCD is systematically considered as the heavy c quark limit of SU(4)_f. Within that scheme we argue that the U(1)_A anomaly prevents Gell-Mann's choice for the realization of the su(4)_f (and thereby the su(3)_f) algebra for the particular case of the neutral axial currents. Rather, Weyl's choice for the SU(4)_f generators has to be used which leads to a neutral strong axial current having same structure (up to a constant factor) as the weak one.

# Strange Isosinglet Versus Octet Scalar Axial Current

## Key claim

The U(1)_A anomaly prevents Gell-Mann's choice for the su(4)_f (and thereby su(3)_f) algebra from being used for neutral axial currents; instead, Weyl's choice for the SU(4)_f generators must be used, leading to a neutral strong axial current with the same structure (up to a constant factor) as the weak axial current. The claim is conditional on the heavy c quark limit of SU(4)_f, where the charm quark mass is taken to approximately 1.5 GeV to suppress c quark effects while preserving anomaly freedom.

## Method

The author systematically considers SU(3)_f flavor symmetry as the heavy c quark limit of SU(4)_f, then compares two realizations of the su(4)_f algebra: Gell-Mann's choice and Weyl's choice. The electromagnetic vector current is decomposed in the Weyl basis to show that the QCD symmetry in the neutral sector reduces to SU(2)_ud ⊗ SU(2)_cs ⊗ U(1)_V. The analysis is extended to axial vector currents, where the U(1)_A anomaly is shown to forbid constructing an octet axial current with a well-defined chiral limit under Gell-Mann's algebra, while Weyl's choice yields a current identical in structure to the weak axial current.

## Key numbers

- The charm quark mass scale Λ_c is taken as approximately 1.5 GeV, chosen to suppress c quark effects on the 1 GeV mass scale while preserving anomaly freedom.
- The mixing angle ε for the η meson in the heavy c quark limit is approximately -45.4 degrees, describing the mixing of Weyl states (c̄c - s̄s) and (ūu + d̄d)/√2.
- The gradient ηN coupling constant f_{ηNN} at tree level is given by f_{ηNN} = √(3/2) × Δs / κ_s(0^-), where Δs is the fraction of proton helicity carried by the strange quark sea and κ_s(0^-) is the dimensionless coupling of the strange quarkonium component.
- The η weak decay constant f_η is approximately 1.1 times the pion decay constant f_π (noted as an empirical observation).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of flavor symmetry realizations by comparing Gell-Mann's and Weyl's choices for the su(4)_f algebra, building on the standard textbook treatment of SU(4)_f generators (Itzykson, cited as Ref. [Itz]) and the Gell-Mann-Nishijima relation. The paper contradicts the conventional interpretation of the η meson as the octet Goldstone boson, instead arguing that the U(1)_A anomaly forces the η to be treated as an approximate "strange" Goldstone boson. This enables a new understanding of the ηN coupling in terms of the strange quark sea contribution Δs rather than the octet axial coupling g_A^(8), consistent with prior work by Kirchbach and Wirzba (cited as Ref. [KiWe]) and with data analyses of η photoproduction (Titov and Kamalov, cited as Ref. [TiKa]), pp̄ collisions (Kroll, cited as Ref. [Kroll]), and NN/NY phase shifts (Reuber, cited as Ref. [Reuber]). The paper also references chiral perturbation theory work by Savage (cited as Ref. [Sav]) on loop corrections and vertex renormalization effects from a_0(980)πN triangular diagrams.
