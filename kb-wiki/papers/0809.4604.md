# Covariant and self consistent vertex corrections for pions and isobars in nuclear matter

> We evaluate the pion and isobar propagators in cold nuclear matter self consistently applying a covariant form of the isobar-hole model. Migdal's vertex correction effects are considered systematically in the absence of phenomenological soft form factors. Saturated nuclear matter is modeled by scalar and vector mean fields for the nucleon. It is shown that the short-range dressing of the pi N Delta vertex has a significant effect on the pion and isobar properties. Using realistic parameters sets we predict a downward shift of about 50 MeV for the Delta resonance at nuclear saturation density. The pionic soft modes are much less pronounced than in previous studies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that short-range vertex corrections to the πNΔ interaction have a significant effect on pion and isobar properties in cold nuclear matter, predicting a downward shift of about 50 MeV for the Δ resonance at nuclear saturation density, but with the caveat that pionic soft modes are much less pronounced than in previous studies that did not treat the system self-consistently with covariant methods.

## Method

The authors evaluate the pion and isobar propagators in cold nuclear matter using a covariant form of the isobar-hole model, solving Dyson's equation self-consistently for the isobar propagator while incorporating Migdal's vertex correction effects systematically without phenomenological soft form factors. Saturated nuclear matter is modeled by scalar and vector mean fields for the nucleon, and the isobar self energy is computed from a one-loop model of pion-nucleon scattering using projector techniques that separate helicity one-half and three-half modes. The pion self energy is constructed from nucleon-hole and isobar-hole loop tensors, with short-range correlations included via a 4×4 matrix formulation of Migdal parameters g'11, g'12, and g'22.

## Key numbers

- Downward shift of about 50 MeV for the Δ resonance at nuclear saturation density
- Coupling constants used: fN = 0.988 and fΔ = 1.85
- Migdal parameters varied around g'11 ≃ 1.0, g'22 = g'12 ≃ 0.4, obtained from analysis of nuclear photoabsorption data
- At nuclear saturation density, vector mean field ΣΔV ≃ -0.25 GeV and scalar mean field ΣΔS ≃ -0.11 GeV for the isobar
- Fermi momentum kF specifies nucleon density ρ = 2kF3/(3π2) for isospin symmetric nuclear matter
- Background term beff ≃ -0.01 fm used to correct the s-wave pion-nucleon scattering length

## Context

This work extends the covariant and self-consistent approach of Korpa and Lutz (2004) and Lutz and Migdal to include vertex correction effects and both longitudinal and transverse isobar modes, generalizing the covariant isobar-hole model. It contrasts with non-relativistic computations by Nieves, Oset, and Recio, Arve and Helgesson, and Rapp that obtained conflicting results for pion properties, and with earlier self-consistent works by Xia, Siemens, and Soyeur and Korpa and Malfliet that used soft phenomenological form factors which artificially suppressed pionic soft modes. The results enable more reliable applications to heavy-ion reactions and nuclear photoabsorption, and the parameter set is compatible with the analysis of nuclear photoabsorption data by Riek, Lutz, and Korpa (2008).
