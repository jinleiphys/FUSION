# New Shell Structure Originated from the Combination of Quadrupole and Octupole Deformations

> Semiclassical analysis of the shell structure for a reflection-asymmetric deformed oscillator potential with irrational frequency ratio $ω_\perp/ω_z=\sqrt{3}$ is presented. Strong shell effects associated with bifurcations of short periodic orbits are found, which occur for a combination of quadrupole and octupole deformations.

## Key claim

Strong shell effects arise from the combination of quadrupole and octupole deformations in a reflection-asymmetric deformed oscillator potential, specifically when the axis ratio is the irrational number \(\omega_\perp/\omega_z=\sqrt{3}\) and the octupole deformation parameter \(\lambda_{30}\) is around 0.3; the abstract glosses over the fact that no prominent shell structure exists at \(\lambda_{30}=0\) due to the irrational frequency ratio, and the shell effects only appear after specific bifurcations of short periodic orbits.

## Method

The author performed a semiclassical analysis of the single-particle level density for a deformed oscillator Hamiltonian with quadrupole deformation (axis ratio \(\omega_\perp/\omega_z=\sqrt{3}\)) and octupole deformation parameter \(\lambda_{30}\). Classical periodic orbits were identified and their stabilities were analyzed via monodromy matrix traces as functions of \(\lambda_{30}\), with Poincaré surfaces of section used to visualize bifurcations. The quantum shell structure was examined using the Fourier transform of the single-particle level density, and the resulting peaks were compared to classical periodic orbit periods to link bifurcations to shell effects.

## Key numbers

- The octupole deformation parameter at which a significant shell structure appears is \(\lambda_{30}\simeq0.3\).
- Orbit PB appears at \(\lambda_{30}\simeq0.29\) due to an isochronous bifurcation of orbit IL.
- Orbits PC and PD are born by a saddle-node bifurcation at \(\lambda_{30}\simeq0.28\).
- Orbits PE and PF arise from a period-tripling bifurcation of orbit PA at \(\lambda_{30}\simeq0.2\).
- The Fourier transform peak at \(s\simeq\sqrt{3}\) (in units of \(T_\perp=2\pi/\omega_\perp\)) reaches its maximum at \(\lambda_{30}=0.34\).
- The peak at \(s\simeq3\) (corresponding to triple traversals of orbit PA) reaches its maximum at \(\lambda_{30}\simeq0.28\), showing a delay from the period-tripling bifurcation point \(\lambda_{30}\simeq0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous semiclassical analysis of shell structures in a superdeformed (SD) oscillator potential with octupole deformation (Arita and Matsuyanagi, 1992; Arita, 1993; Arita and Matsuyanagi, 1994), where the frequency ratio was \(\omega_\perp/\omega_z=2\) and the supershell effect was found at \(\lambda_{30}\simeq0.4\). The present study generalizes this to an irrational axis ratio \(\sqrt{3}\), demonstrating that shell effects can arise purely from the combination of quadrupole and octupole deformations even when no shell structure exists at zero octupole deformation. The results enable future investigations into whether such shell structures persist in other reflection-asymmetric deformed potentials with radial dependence different from the oscillator type.
