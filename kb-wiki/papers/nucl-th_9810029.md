# J/Ψproduction in πN collisions

> We calculate the total cross section for $πN \to J/ΨN$ based on $ρ$-meson exchange. On the basis of this calculation we predict a maximal reaction rate arising from the OZI-violating $J/Ψ{\leftrightarrow}ρπ$ vertex in tree-level. Our estimate for the maximum reaction rate is still one order of magnitude smaller than the existing predictions which were based on the other OZI-violating mechanisms.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the total cross section for πN → J/ΨN via ρ-meson exchange and find that even their maximal estimate (obtained without a form factor at the Jρπ vertex) is still about one order of magnitude smaller than existing predictions based on other OZI-violating mechanisms, such as the duality-violating model, a generalized Veneziano model, and the analogy to the π⁻p → φn reaction.

## Method

The authors compute the total cross section for πN → J/ΨN using tree-level ρ-meson exchange, with effective Lagrangians for the ρNN and Jρπ vertices. The Jρπ coupling constant is determined from the measured J/Ψ → ρπ partial width (31.12 MeV) using both a narrow resonance approximation and an integral over the ρ-meson width, yielding nearly identical values. The sensitivity of the results is tested by applying a monopole form factor at the Jρπ vertex with different cutoff parameters (Λ = 1 GeV, Λ = 2 GeV, and no form factor).

## Key numbers

- The Jρπ coupling constant is g_{Jρπ} = 6.68 × 10⁻³ from the narrow resonance approximation, and g_{Jρπ} = 6.78 × 10⁻³ from the full width treatment.
- The predicted maximum cross section for π⁻p → J/Ψn is 140 pb at a pion laboratory momentum of 12 GeV/c, obtained without a form factor at the Jρπ vertex.
- The ρNN coupling constants used are g²_{ρNN}/4π = 0.74 and κ = f_{ρNN}/g_{ρNN} = 6.1.
- The cutoff parameter for the ρNN form factor is Λ = 920 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of OZI-violating J/Ψ production in πN collisions, specifically contrasting with predictions from Bolzan et al. (duality-violating model, 1.0–2.5 nb), Kodaira and Sasaki (generalized Veneziano model), and Berger and Sorencsen (analogy to π⁻p → φn). The authors note that exclusive J/Ψ production in πN reactions has never been observed, and the only constraints come from upper limits extracted from πA collisions assuming a linear A-dependence. The calculation provides a benchmark for future experimental searches and can be generalized to other pseudoscalar + nucleon → vector meson + nucleon reactions (e.g., πN → ωN, πN → ρN, πN → φN, KN → K*N) by replacing coupling constants and form factors.
