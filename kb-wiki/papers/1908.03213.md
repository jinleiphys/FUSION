# Gravitational Waves from Holographic Neutron Star Mergers

> We simulate the merger of binary neutron stars and analyze the spectral properties of their gravitational waveforms. For the stars we construct hybrid equations of state (EoSs) with a standard nuclear matter EoS at low densities, transitioning to a state-of-the-art holographic EoS in the otherwise intractable high density regime. Depending on the transition density the characteristic frequencies in the spectrum produced from the hybrid EoSs are shifted to significantly lower values as compared to the pure nuclear matter EoS. The highest rest-mass density reached outside a possible black hole horizon is approximately $1.1 \cdot 10^{15}$ g/cm$^3$, which for the holographic model is below the density of the deconfined quark matter phase.

# Gravitational Waves from Holographic Neutron Star Mergers

## Key claim
The characteristic frequencies in the gravitational wave power spectral density from binary neutron star mergers are shifted to significantly lower values when using hybrid equations of state that incorporate a holographic model for high-density nuclear matter, compared to a pure nuclear matter equation of state. However, the highest rest-mass density reached outside a possible black hole horizon is approximately 1.1 x 10^15 g/cm^3, which for the holographic model is below the density of the deconfined quark matter phase, meaning the simulations do not explore the phase transition to quark matter.

## Method
The authors construct hybrid equations of state by combining the SLy nuclear matter equation of state at low densities with a holographic V-QCD equation of state at high densities, matching them at densities between 1.5 and 2 times nuclear saturation density. They then perform fully general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of equal-mass binary neutron star mergers using the Einstein Toolkit and WhiskyTHC codes, extracting gravitational waveforms via the Newman-Penrose formalism and computing power spectral densities to identify characteristic post-merger frequency peaks.

## Key numbers
- Highest rest-mass density reached outside a possible black hole horizon: approximately 1.1 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- Nuclear saturation density: 2.04 x 10^14 g/cm^3
- For the hybrid EoS with b=10.5 and M=1.3 solar masses: f1 = 1.93 kHz, f2 = 2.53 kHz, f3 = 3.77 kHz
- For the pure SLy EoS with M=1.3 solar masses: f1 = 2.21 kHz, f2 = 3.19 kHz, f3 = 4.24 kHz
- For the hybrid EoS with b=10.6 and M=1.3 solar masses: f1 = 2.15 kHz, f2 = 2.80 kHz, f3 = 3.70 kHz (with an ambiguous peak at 4.06 kHz)
- Maximum masses for hybrid EoSs range from 2.00 to 2.25 solar masses depending on the matching density
- Radii for 1.4 solar mass neutron stars range from 12.0 to 13.0 km for the hybrid EoSs
- Tidal deformability for 1.4 solar mass neutron stars ranges from 370 to 680 for the hybrid EoSs, compared to 300 for SLy

## Context
This work extends the study of gravitational wave signatures from neutron star mergers by Takami (2014) and others, who analyzed traditional nuclear matter equations of state, by introducing a holographic model (V-QCD) for the high-density regime where QCD is strongly coupled and lattice methods are inaccessible. The hybrid equations of state provide an explicit realization of the scenario proposed by Annala (2019) where neutron stars without quark matter cores require speeds of sound well above the conformal value of c_s^2 = 1/3. The results enable future comparison with gravitational wave observations from advanced LIGO and the Einstein Telescope, potentially distinguishing between different equations of state through the characteristic post-merger frequency peaks.
