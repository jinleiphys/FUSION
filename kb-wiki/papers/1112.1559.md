# Open heavy flavor at RHIC and LHC in a partonic transport model

> Heavy quarks are a unique probe to study the medium produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Within the partonic transport model Boltzmann approach to multi-parton scatterings (BAMPS) the production, energy loss and elliptic flow of heavy quarks are investigated in 3+1 dimensional simulations of the quark gluon plasma evolution. With only binary interactions between heavy quarks and particles from the medium calculated within perturbative QCD, the results on elliptic flow and the nuclear modification factor are not compatible with experimental data from RHIC and LHC. However, if the binary cross section is multiplied with K=4 both the elliptic flow and the nuclear modification factor are simultaneously described at RHIC and also LHC. Furthermore, preliminary results are presented that the implementation of radiative processes leads to a stronger suppression which agrees well with the measured nuclear modification factor at RHIC without the need of any K factor.

## Key claim
Binary pQCD interactions alone cannot simultaneously describe the elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor of heavy quarks at RHIC and LHC; only by multiplying the binary cross section by an artificial factor K=4 do the results match both observables, but preliminary inclusion of radiative processes reproduces the RHIC nuclear modification factor without any K factor.

## Method
The authors used the partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings) to simulate the 3+1 dimensional evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, solving the Boltzmann equation for on-shell partons with pQCD interactions. Heavy quarks (charm and bottom) were produced via gg -> Q Qbar, and their hadronization to D and B mesons used Peterson fragmentation, with subsequent decay to electrons simulated by Pythia. Binary cross sections for gQ -> gQ were multiplied by a factor K=4 to match data, and in separate calculations radiative processes gQ -> gQg were added using a Gunion-Bertsch matrix element.

## Key numbers
- Binary cross sections multiplied by K=4 simultaneously describe both elliptic flow v2 and nuclear modification factor R_AA of heavy flavor electrons at RHIC for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=8.2 fm.
- With K=4, the same parameters also describe D meson v2 and R_AA at LHC in Pb+Pb collisions, and muon R_AA at forward rapidity at LHC.
- Preliminary results including radiative processes gQ -> gQg reproduce the R_AA of heavy flavor electrons at RHIC without any K factor, agreeing well with data especially at large transverse momentum pT.

## Context
This work extends the BAMPS model (Xu 2004, Xu 2007) to heavy flavor, addressing the puzzle that heavy quarks show suppression and elliptic flow comparable to light quarks despite the expected dead cone effect (Dokshitzer 2001). The need for K=4 in binary-only calculations indicates missing higher-order contributions, which the preliminary radiative results suggest may resolve the discrepancy. The approach enables future detailed study of radiative heavy quark energy loss in a full space-time QGP evolution, contrasting with earlier work that debated collisional versus radiative energy loss (Armesto 2005, van Hees 2005, Moore 2004, Mustafa 2004, Wicks 2005, Adil 2006, Peigne 2008, Gossiaux 2008, Alberico 2011, Cao 2011, Young 2011).
