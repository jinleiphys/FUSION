# Entropy Production During Hadronization of a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We revisit the physical pictures for the hadronization of quark-gluon plasma, concentrating on the problem of entropy production during processes where the number of degrees of freedom is seemingly reduced due to color confinement. Based on observations on Regge trajectories we propose not having an infinite tower of hadronic resonances. We discuss possible entropy production mechanisms far from equilibrium in terms of stochastic dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the entropy problem during hadronization, where the number of degrees of freedom appears to decrease due to color confinement, can be resolved if the hadronic resonance spectrum is finite rather than infinite, based on observations of Regge trajectories that flatten and terminate at a mass around 2.5–3 GeV.

## Method

The authors analyze the connection between the Hagedorn exponential mass spectrum and non-linear Regge trajectories, proposing that the prefactor in the Hagedorn formula depends on the slope of the Regge trajectory. They examine three models for Regge trajectories: a sum of square-root thresholds (model I), a simplified version with a single expanded threshold (model II), and a rational function form with multiple thresholds (model III), fitting these to meson resonance masses and decay widths. Separately, they develop a statistical framework where the number of degrees of freedom fluctuates according to a negative binomial distribution, deriving the resulting single-particle energy spectra and connecting the Tsallis-Pareto parameter q to the second scaled factorial moment of the multiplicity distribution.

## Key numbers

- The exponential increase in the density of hadronic resonances slows down around m ≈ 2.5–3 GeV, indicating termination of the resonance spectrum.
- The ρ trajectory model in Eq. (BK) includes thresholds at masses: 0.28 GeV (ππ), 0.988 GeV (K K̅), 1.88 GeV (N N̅), with the highest threshold at 2.63 GeV where the real part of the trajectory becomes flat.
- The heaviest strongly interacting stable particle considered is bottomium with s_m = 9.391 GeV, which would set the highest possible threshold.
- The phase space volume ratio for p=1 and p=2 norms gives r_n = n (ε/E) (1 − ε/E)^(n−1), while the p=∞ (hypercubic) case gives r_n = (ε/E) (1 − ε/E)^(n−1).
- For a Poisson-distributed number of particles, the average phase space ratio becomes (ε/T) exp(−ε/T) with T = E/⟨n⟩.
- For a negative binomial distribution, the average phase space ratio becomes (ε/T) (1 + ε/(kT))^(−k−1), which resembles a Tsallis-Pareto distribution with q = 1 + 1/k.
- The temperature is given by T = E/⟨n⟩ and the q parameter by q = ⟨n(n−1)⟩/⟨n⟩².

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of the "entropy problem" in hadronization, where statistical models like the ALCOR model (ALCOR, ALCOR SPS) have previously addressed entropy balance by including excited baryon octet and decuplet states. The paper contradicts the assumption of an infinite Hagedorn spectrum (Hagedorn, 1965) and instead proposes a finite resonance spectrum based on Regge trajectory analysis, building on prior work by Burak (Burak, Burak A, Burak B) and the DAMA/LIBRA experiment constraints. The stochastic dynamics approach with fluctuating degrees of freedom enables a natural interpretation of the Tsallis-Pareto parameter q in terms of multiplicity fluctuations, which can be tested against single hadron p_T spectra from high-energy hadronic collisions.
