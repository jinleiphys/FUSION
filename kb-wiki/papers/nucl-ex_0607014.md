# Results from the Analysis of Crystal Ball Meson Production Measurements at BNL

> The Crystal Ball spectrometer, with its nearly complete angular coverage, is an efficient detector of photon and neutron final states. While installed in the C6 beamline of the Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS) of Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL), this feature was used in a series of precise measurements of reactions with all-neutral final states. Here we concentrate on the analysis of data from the pion-induced reactions: pi- p --> gamma n, pi- p --> pi0 n, pi- p --> eta n, and pi- p --> pi0 pi0 n.

## Key claim
The Crystal Ball measurements provide precise cross sections with broad angular coverage for several pion-induced reactions with all-neutral final states, but the analysis reveals that the extracted parameters (such as the eta-n scattering length and resonance branching fractions) depend significantly on the choice of K-matrix formalism used in the partial-wave analysis.

## Method
The Crystal Ball detector, covering 94% of 4pi with 672 NaI crystals, was used in the C6 beamline of the AGS at BNL to measure differential cross sections for pi- p -> gamma n, pi- p -> pi0 n, pi- p -> eta n, and pi- p -> pi0 pi0 n. Data were analyzed using partial-wave analyses including a coupled-channel formalism to simultaneously fit pi N elastic scattering and eta n production data, employing both a standard K-matrix approach and a Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix for comparison.

## Key numbers
- The Crystal Ball detector covers 94% of 4pi solid angle using 672 NaI crystals
- Charge-exchange reaction (pi- p -> pi0 n) measured from threshold to T_pi- = 625 MeV, with two regions analyzed: 64 to 212 MeV and 524 to 625 MeV
- Eta n production measured from threshold to T_pi- = 620 MeV
- Extracted N(1520) eta n branching fraction: 0.08 to 0.16%
- Eta n scattering length extracted from near-threshold data: (1-1.15) + i(0.3-0.4) fm
- Inverse pion photoproduction (pi- p -> gamma n) measured at 18 values of T_pi- from 136 to 621 MeV, corresponding to E_gamma from 285 to 769 MeV
- Pi0 pi0 n reaction measured from 160 MeV to 625 MeV
- Breit-Wigner parameters from SP06 fit: N(1440) P11 mass 1485.0 +/- 1.2 MeV, width 284 +/- 18 MeV; N(1520) D13 mass 1514.5 +/- 0.2 MeV, width 103.6 +/- 0.4 MeV; N(1535) S11 mass 1547.0 +/- 0.7 MeV, width 188.4 +/- 3.8 MeV

## Context
This work extends the partial-wave analyses FA02 and SP06 by incorporating new Crystal Ball data that provide significantly more precise cross sections than previous measurements. The results enable improved determination of low-lying baryon resonance parameters, particularly for the N(1535) and N(1520) resonances through their eta n couplings, and the analysis reveals that different K-matrix formalisms (such as those used by Green and Wycech versus Chew-Mandelstam approaches) yield different results for the eta n scattering length. The future program moves the Crystal Ball detector to MAMI for photonuclear physics with tagged polarized photons and polarized targets.
