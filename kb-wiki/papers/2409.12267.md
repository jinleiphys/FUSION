# Systematic study of cross section for proton-induced reactions on Neodymium up to 65 MeV using TALYS-1.96 code

> Systematic study of theoretical estimation of nuclear cross section of charged particle induced reactions on rare earth nuclei has been carried out. The production cross section of the $^{150,149,148,146,144,143,141}$Pm and $^{149,147}$Nd nuclei were calculated theoretically via proton induced reaction on neodymium using TALYS (version 1.96) code in the default mode, with different combination of nuclear models as well as using adjusted nuclear model parameters from reaction threshold to the proton energy upto 65 MeV. The theoretically computed results were compared with the experimental results taken from EXFOR database and literature, reported by various research groups. Moreover, the effect of the various Level Density Models (LDMs), Preequilibrium Models (PEs), Optical Model Potentials (OMPs), gamma Strength Functions ($γ$SFs) in the cross section calculation were taken into consideration. The present theoretical analysis will help to understand the theory of nuclear reaction models and improve the evaluated nuclear data libraries.

# Systematic study of cross section for proton-induced reactions on Neodymium up to 65 MeV using TALYS-1.96 code

## Key claim

The TALYS-1.96 code in default mode shows several discrepancies with experimental cross section data for proton-induced reactions on neodymium, but agreement improves significantly when specific combinations of nuclear models (level density models, optical model potentials, preequilibrium models, and gamma strength functions) are used together with adjusted diffuseness and radius parameters of the proton optical model potential.

## Method

The author calculated production cross sections for 150,149,148,146,144,143,141Pm and 149,147Nd via proton-induced reactions on neodymium using the TALYS-1.96 code from reaction threshold up to 65 MeV. Calculations were performed using six different level density models (Constant Temperature, Back-shifted Fermi Gas, Generalized Superfluid, and three microscopic models by Goriely and Hilaire) in default mode and then with various combinations of optical model potentials, preequilibrium models, and gamma strength functions. The theoretical results were compared with experimental data from the EXFOR database reported by Olkowsky et al. (1961), Lebeda et al. (2012, 2014), Banerjee et al. (2015), Yang et al. (2015), and Tarkanyi et al. (2017).

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy range: from reaction threshold up to 65 MeV
- For 150Nd(p,x)150Pm reaction: Constant Temperature model reproduced data well up to 10 MeV; beyond 35 MeV and up to 45 MeV it matched Yang et al. (2015) data; from 45-65 MeV it matched Tarkanyi et al. (2017) data
- For 150Nd(p,x)149Pm reaction: excitation functions from all models peak at around 15 MeV
- Experimental D0 value for 150Nd from RIPL-3 database: 0.165 +/- 0.015 eV; theoretical D0 values ranged from 2.39 eV (Goriely-Hilaire Gogny force) to 11.13 eV (Goriely model)
- For fitting 150Nd(p,x)150Pm data: diffuseness parameter av of proton OMP reduced by 20% from default value
- For fitting 150Nd(p,x)149Pm data: diffuseness parameter av reduced by 10% from default value
- For natNd(p,x)144,143Pm reactions: radius parameter rv reduced by 10% from default value
- For natNd(p,x)141Pm data: rv decreased by 30% and av increased by 20%
- For natNd(p,x)149,147Nd reactions: rv reduced by 10% and av reduced by 20% from default values

## Context

This work extends systematic theoretical studies of charged particle-induced reactions on rare earth nuclei, motivated by applications in Accelerator-Driven System design, medical radioisotope production (particularly 149Pm and 140,141Nd for therapeutic purposes), and background estimation for neutrinoless double beta decay experiments (SNO+, DCBA, SuperNEMO) using 150Nd. The study compares TALYS-1.96 calculations with experimental data from Olkowsky et al. (1961), Lebeda et al. (2012, 2014), Banerjee et al. (2015), Yang et al. (2015), and Tarkanyi et al. (2017), and with TENDL-2023 evaluated data. The results enable improved evaluated nuclear data libraries and better understanding of how level density models, optical model potentials, preequilibrium models, and gamma strength functions affect cross section calculations for proton-induced reactions on neodymium isotopes.
