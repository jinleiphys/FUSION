# A Possible Solution to the Difficulty in the Interpretation of Deuteron Compositeness

> We study the theoretical structure of compositeness with explicit energy dependence, and find a possible explanation for the difficulty in the interpretation of compositeness of deuteron. Compositeness of deuteron is calculated as larger than one in many methods like weak-binding limit. Even though it is widely assumed that the energy dependence in interaction always comes from other states, which we call surjective interpretation, we find that the outcome of deuteron may suggest a violation of surjective interpretation. We directly perform numerical and perturbative calculations of deuteron compositeness. It is concluded that if the energy dependent part of interaction contributes to attraction, compositeness is likely to be enhanced from unity. We discuss the indications of this outcome and the model dependence of compositeness. We propose a straightforward extension and a thorough revise on the formalism of compositeness with field theory considerations.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the difficulty in interpreting deuteron compositeness (which is often calculated as larger than unity) may arise from a violation of the "surjective interpretation", the assumption that all energy dependence in the interaction originates from other physical states, rather than from a fundamental flaw in the compositeness concept itself.

## Method
The authors review the formalism of compositeness and elementariness from Schrödinger and Lippmann-Schwinger equations, extending it to explicitly energy-dependent potentials. They prove that elementariness should be positive when energy dependence stems from physical states (one-body, two-body, or three-body) with constant interactions. They then perform numerical and perturbative calculations of deuteron compositeness using simple models (constant interaction, momentum expansion, one-pion exchange potential) with parameters fixed by scattering length, effective range, and binding energy.

## Key numbers
- Compositeness of deuteron calculated with a constant interaction: 1.000 (binding energy 1.477 MeV)
- Compositeness with a momentum-dependent interaction ($c_0 + c_1 q^2$): 1.479 (binding energy 2.232 MeV)
- Compositeness with $c_0 + c_1 q^2 + c_2 q^4$: 1.727 (binding energy 2.423 MeV)
- Compositeness with constant plus one-pion exchange potential (OPEP): 1.870 (binding energy fixed to 2.200 MeV)
- Compositeness with $c_0 + c_1 q^2$ plus OPEP: 1.427 (binding energy fixed to 2.200 MeV)
- Compositeness with D-channel and constant interaction: 1.133 (binding energy fixed to 2.200 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the compositeness formalism developed by Sekihara et al. (2015) and Weinberg (1963, 1965), who first proposed compositeness to distinguish whether deuteron is a composite state of nucleons or an elementary particle. The paper directly addresses the persistent problem that deuteron compositeness exceeds unity in the weak-binding limit and in many model calculations. The findings suggest that attractive energy-dependent corrections in the interaction systematically enhance compositeness beyond unity, implying that the current formalism may require revision with field-theoretic considerations to properly interpret deuteron structure. The work enables future development of a more complete compositeness formalism that accounts for intrinsic energy dependence in interactions.
