# Assocative Photoproduction of Roper Resonance and $ω$-Meson

> Associative photoproduction of $ω$-meson and $N^{*}(1440)$ on nucleons, $γ+N\toω+N^{*}(1440)$, in the near threshold region is investigated in a framework employing effective Lagrangians. Besides $π$-exchange in t-channel, baryon exchanges, i.e. N- and $N^{*}$-exchanges, in the s- and u-channels are also taken into account in calculations of differential cross-section and beam asymmetry. Important inputs of this model are the vector and tensor coupling constants of $ωNN^{*}(1440)$-vertex, which are assumed to be equal to the values of these couplings for $ωNN$ vertex. Using our previous estimation of $ωNN$ coupling constants obtained from a fit to available experimental data on photoproduction of $ω$ meson in the near threshold region, we produce the necessary numerical predictions for different observables in $γ+N\toω+N^{*}(1440)$. Numerical results shows that at low $|$t$|$ dominant contribution comes from t-channel $π$-exchange while effects of nucleon and $N^{*}(1440)$ pole terms can be seen at large $|t|$. Our predictions for the differential cross section and beam asymmetry for the processes $γ+N\toω+N^{*}(1440)$, where N is proton and neutron at $E_γ=2.5$ GeV are presented with zero width approximation and also with the inclusion of width effects of $N^{*}(1440)$.

# Associative Photoproduction of Roper Resonance and ω-Meson

## Key claim

The authors propose that the vector and tensor coupling constants of the ωNN*(1440) vertex can be assumed equal to those of the ωNN vertex, enabling predictions for differential cross sections and beam asymmetry in γ+N→ω+N*(1440) near threshold; however, the model's validity is conditional on the absence of experimental data for these processes, and the results are presented as a first approach rather than a definitive determination.

## Method

The reaction γ+N→ω+N*(1440) is modeled using effective Lagrangians with tree-level diagrams including π-exchange in the t-channel and nucleon (N) and Roper resonance (N*) exchanges in the s- and u-channels, without Pomeron exchange. The ωNN*(1440) coupling constants are taken equal to ωNN couplings obtained from a fit to experimental data on ω photoproduction on nucleons. Differential cross sections and beam asymmetry Σ are calculated at Eγ=2.5 GeV for both proton and neutron targets, using three sets of fitted coupling constants, and width effects of the N*(1440) are included via a Breit-Wigner parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Roper resonance full width: ΓtotN*(1440)(M0N*) = 350 MeV, with partial width ΓN*(1440)→π0p(M0N*) = 76 MeV.
- Photon energy for all predictions: Eγ = 2.5 GeV.
- Three sets of fitted ωNN* coupling constants (with χ² values): Set 1: gVωNN* = -1.4, gTωNN* = 0.4, χ² = 2.2; Set 2: gVωNN* = 0.5, gTωNN* = 0.1, χ² = 1.6; Set 3: gVωNN* = -0.01, gTωNN* = 0.6, χ² = 1.9.
- Cut-off parameters: Set 1 uses ΛπNN* = 0.7 GeV and Λωπγ = 0.77 GeV; Sets 2 and 3 use ΛπNN* = 0.5 GeV and Λωπγ = 1.0 GeV.
- At -t = 0.36 GeV², the differential cross section for γp→ωN*(1440)→ωπ0p is about 20 times smaller than the zero-width approximation result.
- For the proton target, beam asymmetry |Σ| ≤ 0.1 for Sets 1 and 2, while for Set 3 it reaches nearly 0.25 at |t| = 1.2 GeV².
- For the neutron target with Set 1, beam asymmetry is positive for |t| ≤ 0.6 GeV² and negative at larger |t|.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of associative vector meson and N*(1440) production near threshold, specifically the π- and σ-exchange model of Ref. [R20] (2001), by adding nucleon and Roper resonance exchanges in the s- and u-channels to enable nonzero polarization observables. The approach is analogous to studies of elastic vector meson photoproduction (e.g., Refs. [R10-R19]) but adapted for the Roper resonance channel. The results enable future experimental tests once data become available for γN→N*(1440)ω, and the model can be compared with the one-pion exchange model of Ref. [R20] which is valid only for |t| ≤ 0.5-0.6 GeV².
