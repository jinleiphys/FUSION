# A Model for BCS-Type Correlations in Superscaling

> Using ideas from BCS descriptions of systems of fermions, a covariant extension of the relativistic Fermi gas model is presented as a way to incorporate correlation effects in nuclei. The model is developed for the BCS nuclear ground state and for final states consisting of a single plane-wave nucleon plus a BCS recoiling daughter nucleus. The nuclear spectral function is obtained and from it the superscaling function for use in treating high-energy quasielastic electroweak processes. Interestingly, this model has the capability to yield the asymmetric tail seen in the experimental scaling function.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant extension of the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model using BCS-type correlations, claiming that this model can produce the asymmetric tail seen in the experimental superscaling function, but only within a narrow range of the correlation parameter \(\beta\) near a critical value \(\beta_{\rm crit}\), below which the nuclear system becomes strongly disrupted.

## Method

The authors construct a BCS-inspired ground state for the target nucleus and final states consisting of a single plane-wave nucleon plus a BCS recoiling daughter nucleus, assuming on-shell nucleons and exact factorization. They parametrize the BCS occupation coefficients \(v^2(k)\) with a three-parameter Fermi-Dirac-like function, fix the parameters via normalization and stability conditions, and compute the nuclear spectral function and the resulting superscaling function for the longitudinal electromagnetic response.

## Key numbers

* The critical value of the correlation parameter is \(\beta_{\rm crit} = 0.017\) c/MeV, below which the momentum distribution becomes strongly extended beyond the Fermi sphere.
* The Fermi momentum used in the calculations is \(k_F = 228\) MeV/c, a value appropriate for \(^{12}\)C.
* The onset of first-kind scaling occurs at momentum transfers of order 500 MeV/c at the quasielastic peak, but away from the peak only at quite large momentum transfers of order 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the RFG model of Alberico et al. (1988) and the superscaling analyses of Donnelly and Sick (1999) and Maieron et al. (2002) by incorporating correlation effects beyond the degenerate Fermi gas description. The model aims to improve the description of quasielastic electroweak processes for neutrino-nucleus cross sections, but the authors note that the scaling of the first kind is reached from below in the scaling region, contrary to experimental findings.
