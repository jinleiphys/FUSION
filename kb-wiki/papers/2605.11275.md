# Freeze-out model of light nuclei formation in heavy-ion collision transport

> Cluster production plays an important role in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate beam energies, where light nuclei contribute substantially to final-state yields and to other observables that are used to infer the nuclear equation of state. In this letter, we propose a new approach for clustering that combines dynamical transport and thermal cluster production for mid-rapidity particles. The resulting hybrid coarse-graining model matches nucleon and light-cluster descriptions at freeze-out while properly accounting for thermal non-uniformity and collective transport in the hot, strongly interacting systems created in heavy-ion collisions. To illustrate the capabilities of this model, yields at 4~fm impact parameter, spectra and elliptic flows at 7.4~fm ($20\text{--}30\%$ centrality) are predicted at mid-rapidity for semi-peripheral Au$+$Au collisions at an incident energy of $E_\text{lab}=1.23~A\mathrm{GeV}$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid coarse-graining freeze-out (HCGF) model that combines dynamical transport simulations with thermal cluster production at a single chemical freeze-out step, claiming that this approach can reproduce experimental yields, spectra, and elliptic flows for light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions, but only for mid-rapidity particles and under the condition that local chemical equilibrium is reached at a specific low freeze-out density.

## Method

The authors use the UrQMD 4.0 transport model with a momentum-dependent mean-field potential to simulate semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV. At the point where the local baryon density reaches the freeze-out density n_fr = 0.02 fm^-3 (approximately 1/8 of saturation density), they match the transport-derived energy density, baryon density, isospin density, and strangeness density to those of an ideal nucleon-and-cluster gas (INCG) using four coupled equations, solving for local temperature and chemical potentials. From these equilibrium quantities, they compute phase-space distributions of nucleons and light clusters (p, n, d, t, ^3He, ^4He) using the Cooper-Frye formalism, averaging over 2x10^5 events to suppress fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out density: n_fr = 0.02 fm^-3, approximately 1/8 of saturation density
- Incident energy: E_lab = 1.23 AGeV for Au+Au collisions
- Impact parameters: 4 fm for yields, 7.4 fm (20-30% centrality) for spectra and elliptic flows
- Average freeze-out temperature for nucleon-only model: 24 MeV; for model including clusters: 30 MeV
- Majority of particles near mid-rapidity freeze-out at t_fr ≈ 30 fm/c
- Isospin density approximation: n_I3 = -0.07 n_B (n_Q = 0.43 n_B)
- Strangeness density set to n_S = 0 fm^-3
- Coarse-graining volume: (0.5 fm)^3

## Context

This work extends the chemical freeze-out concept commonly used in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Huovinen (2012), Andronic (2017)) to intermediate-energy collisions at facilities such as FRIB, RIBF-RIKEN, FAIR, and GANIL. It addresses the difficulty of predicting cluster production in transport models, where most approaches rely on post-processing methods like coalescence or simulated annealing (e.g., Botvina (2020), Coci (2024)), rather than dynamical cluster treatment. The HCGF model enables thermodynamically consistent predictions of yields, spectra, and elliptic flows for light clusters that can be directly compared with experimental data from HADES and other experiments, with future extensions planned for excited states, decays, and out-of-equilibrium corrections via DNMR or Chapman-Enskog expansions.
