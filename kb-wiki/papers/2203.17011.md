# Assessing the ultracentral flow puzzle in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions

> An outstanding problem in heavy-ion collisions is the inability for models to accurately describe ultra-central experimental flow data, despite that being precisely the regime where a hydrodynamic description should be most applicable. We reassess the status of this puzzle by computing the flow in ultra-central collisions obtained from multiple recent Bayesian models that were tuned to various observables in different collision systems at typical centralities. While central data can now be described with better accuracy than in previous calculations, tension with experimental observation remains and worsens as one goes to ultra-central collisions. Tuning the model parameters cannot remove this tension without destroying the fit at other centralities. As such, new elements are likely needed in the standard modeling of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

While modern Bayesian-tuned hydrodynamic models can describe heavy-ion flow data at typical centralities with better accuracy than previous calculations, tension with experimental data remains and worsens as one moves to ultra-central collisions (0-1% centrality), and tuning model parameters cannot remove this tension without destroying the fit at other centralities.

## Method

The authors computed two-particle flow harmonics v_n{2} in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions using four different state-of-the-art hybrid hydrodynamic models (Duke, JETSCAPE, Trajectum 1, and Trajectum 2) that had been previously constrained by Bayesian parameter estimation using data from non-ultra-central collisions. For Trajectum 2, they used a sampling of the full posterior distribution (20 random samples) to estimate systematic uncertainty, while for the other models they used the maximum a posteriori (MAP) parameter values. They compared the predicted v_2, v_3, and v_4 in centrality bins down to 0-0.02% against measurements from the ALICE and CMS collaborations.

## Key numbers

- At 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, the discrepancy between model predictions and ALICE data for v_2 does not exceed 10% in the most central bins, but the centrality dependence deviates from the experimental trend starting at the 1-2% centrality bin
- At 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, the experimental elliptic flow continues to decrease with centrality for all measured bins down to 0-0.02% centrality, while the model predictions become almost constant
- The ratio v_3{2}/v_2{2} in experimental data continues to increase with decreasing centrality down to 0-0.02% centrality, reaching parity between v_3 and v_2, a feature not seen in any simulation
- The maximum bulk viscosity ζ/s(T) from the JETSCAPE MAP is more than a factor 5 higher than any of the other analyses

## Context

This work extends the long-standing ultra-central flow puzzle first noted by Luzum (2012) and Roland (2013), where hydrodynamic models universally fail to simultaneously describe v_2 and v_3 in ultra-central collisions. Previous attempts to resolve the puzzle included studies of MC-Glauber and MC-KLN initial conditions by Shen (2015), repulsive nucleon-nucleon correlations by Qian (2016), the magma model by Gelis (2019) and Snyder (2020), sub-nucleonic fluctuations by Loizides (2016), and octupole deformation of lead nuclei by Carzon (2020), none of which achieved simultaneous description of flow harmonics. This work is the first to reassess the puzzle using Bayesian-constrained models that show excellent agreement at typical centralities, demonstrating that the puzzle persists even with state-of-the-art simulations and that new physical elements beyond parameter tuning are likely needed in the standard modeling of heavy-ion collisions.
