# Two- and three-meson scattering amplitudes with physical quark masses from lattice QCD

> We study systems of two and three mesons composed of pions and kaons at maximal isospin using four CLS ensembles with $a\approx 0.063\;$fm, including one with approximately physical quark masses. Using the stochastic Laplacian-Heaviside method, we determine the energy spectrum of these systems including many levels in different momentum frames and irreducible representations. Using the relativistic two- and three-body finite-volume formalism, we constrain the two and three-meson K matrices, including not only the leading $s$ wave, but also $p$ and $d$ waves. By solving the three-body integral equations, we determine, for the first time, the physical-point scattering amplitudes for $3ŽĆ^+$, $3K^+$, $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^+ K^+$ and $K^+ K^+ ŽĆ^+$ systems. These are determined for total angular momentum $J^P=0^-$, $1^+$, and $2^-$. We also obtain accurate results for $2ŽĆ^+$, $ŽĆ^+ K^+$, and $2K^+$ phase shifts. We compare our results to Chiral Perturbation Theory, and to phenomenological fits.

## Key claim

For the first time, the physical-point three-meson scattering amplitudes for \(3\pi^+\), \(3K^+\), \(\pi^+\pi^+ K^+\) and \(K^+ K^+ \pi^+\) systems are determined from lattice QCD, including amplitudes with total angular momentum \(J^P=0^-\), \(1^+\), and \(2^-\), though the results are obtained indirectly by constraining two- and three-meson K matrices from finite-volume energies and then solving integral equations, rather than from direct infinite-volume measurements.

## Method

The authors used four CLS ensembles with lattice spacing \(a\approx 0.063\) fm, including one ensemble (E250) with approximately physical quark masses (\(M_\pi \approx 130\) MeV, \(M_K \approx 500\) MeV). They computed finite-volume energy spectra for two- and three-meson systems (pions and kaons at maximal isospin) using the stochastic Laplacian-Heaviside (sLapH) method and the generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) to extract energy levels. The relativistic two- and three-body finite-volume formalism (RFT formalism) was then applied to constrain the two- and three-meson K matrices, including \(s\), \(p\), and \(d\) partial waves, and the three-body integral equations were solved to obtain infinite-volume scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

*   Pion mass on the E250 ensemble: \(M_\pi = 130\) MeV (from \(a M_\pi = 0.04217(20)\) with \(a \approx 0.063\) fm).
*   Kaon mass on the E250 ensemble: \(M_K = 500\) MeV (from \(a M_K = 0.159170(93)\) with \(a \approx 0.063\) fm).
*   Lattice spacing: \(a \approx 0.063\) fm (consistent with determinations from decay constants and baryon masses).
*   Spatial extent of the E250 ensemble: \(L/a = 96\), giving \(M_\pi L = 4.049(19)\) and \(M_K L = 15.2803(90)\).
*   Number of configurations used for the E250 ensemble: \(N_{\rm cfg} = 505\).
*   Number of eigenvectors of the covariant Laplacian retained for E250: \(N_{\rm ev} = 1536\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same collaboration (Blanton 2021, Draper 2023) that analyzed three-meson systems at heavier-than-physical quark masses, and is one of only two studies (alongside Fischer 2020) to examine the \(2\pi^+\) and \(3\pi^+\) systems at the physical point. The results enable first-principles comparisons with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions for multi-meson interactions, and provide benchmark scattering amplitudes for systems that lack resonances, making them ideal for validating multiparticle methods. The determination of three-meson amplitudes with \(J^P=1^+\) and \(2^-\) is a new feature not present in prior work.
