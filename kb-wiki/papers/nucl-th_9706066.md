# Two-phonon background for the double giant resonance

> The direct excitation of a "sea" of two-phonon configurations above 15 MeV from the ground state is considered. It is concluded that these configurations form a flat physical background in photoexcitation cross sections and Coulomb excitation in heavy ion reactions at relativistic energies. The contribution of the background to the total cross section in Coulomb excitation of Pb-208 in the DGDR energy region is about 15%. This shows that It should be considered while drawing out conclusions on anharmonicities effects for the DGDR.

## Key claim

The direct excitation of a "sea" of two-phonon configurations from the ground state forms a flat physical background in photoexcitation cross sections and Coulomb excitation in heavy ion reactions at relativistic energies; this background contributes about 15% to the total cross section in Coulomb excitation of Pb-208 in the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) energy region and should be considered when drawing conclusions on anharmonicity effects for the DGDR.

## Method

The authors used the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) to calculate photoexcitation cross sections of two-phonon 1- states built from phonons with multipolarities from 0 to 9, including both natural and unnatural parity phonons, for nuclei 136Xe and 208Pb. For Coulomb excitation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, they applied second-order perturbation theory within the semiclassical approach of Winther and Alder, coupling two-phonon DGDR states with about 7000 three-phonon states to describe the DGDR properties in 208Pb, and calculated the one-step direct excitation of two-phonon 1- states in first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Total photoexcitation cross section of all two-phonon 1- states in the energy region 20-35 MeV: 511 mb for 136Xe and 423 mb for 208Pb
- Photoexcitation cross section of the single-phonon GDR: 2006 mb for 136Xe and 2790 mb for 208Pb
- Cross section for direct excitation of two-phonon 1- states in the 208Pb (640 A.MeV) + 208Pb reaction integrated over 20-35 MeV: 50.3 mb
- Experimental cross section in the DGDR region for the 208Pb (640 A.MeV) + 208Pb reaction: 380 mb (Boretzky 1996)
- Taking into account the two-phonon 1- background results in a visual shift of the DGDR centroid by -200 keV and a 16% increase of the "DGDR" width
- The reported experiment/theory enhancement for 208Pb is about 30% (Boretzky 1996)
- The reported value n=1.8(3) for the dependence on target charge in the DGDR region for 208Pb (Boretzky 1996) allows for some contribution of one-step transitions

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Catara, Chomaz, and Van Giai (1992) and Ponomarev and Voronov (1992) on direct excitation of two-phonon states at high energies, which had omitted many two-phonon configurations. The calculations show that the two-phonon background appreciably removes the disagreement between experiment and theory for both photo-neutron data and Coulomb excitation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, particularly addressing the 30% underprediction of the DGDR cross section for 208Pb reported by Boretzky (1996). The results enable more accurate extraction of DGDR properties and anharmonicity effects from experimental data, and suggest that the interference between one- and two-phonon configurations may play a more important role for 136Xe, requiring further study.
