# Pre-equilibrium photon production in QCD Kinetic Theory

> We use QCD kinetic theory to compute photon production in the chemically equilibrating Quark-Gluon Plasma created in the early stages of high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We show that the photon spectrum radiated from an attractor evolution satisfies a simple scaling form in terms of the specific shear viscosity $η/s$ and entropy density $dS/dζ\sim {\scriptstyle \left(Tτ^{1/3}\right)^{3/2}}_\infty$. We confirm the analytical predictions with numerical kinetic theory simulations. We use the extracted scaling function to compute the pre-equilibrium photon contribution in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\,\text{TeV}$ 0-20% PbPb collisions. We demonstrate that our matching procedure allows for a smooth switching from pre-equilibrium kinetic to thermal hydrodynamic photon production.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium photon spectrum from a chemically equilibrating quark-gluon plasma obeys a universal scaling form in terms of the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) and the scaled transverse momentum \(\bar{\mathbf{p}}_T = (\eta/s)^{1/2} p_T / (T\tau^{1/3})_\infty^{3/2}\), but this scaling breaks down at high transverse momentum because those photons are produced at very early times when the system is too far from equilibrium for an attractor description to apply.

## Method

The authors used leading-order QCD kinetic theory to compute photon production from all relevant processes (elastic pair annihilation, Compton scattering, inelastic pair annihilation, and Bremsstrahlung) in a boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous expanding quark-gluon plasma. Elastic in-medium scattering matrix elements were modified with an isotropic screening regulator, and inelastic splitting rates included the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Midgdal effect via effective vertex resummation in the AMY formalism. The time-integrated photon spectrum was rewritten in terms of the scaled time variable \(\tilde{w} = \tau T/(4\pi\eta/s)\) and the energy attractor \(\mathcal{E}(\tilde{w})\), yielding a universal scaling function \(\mathcal{N}_\gamma(\tilde{w},\bar{\mathbf{p}}_T)\) that was then applied to experimental data by matching to hydrodynamic initial conditions.

## Key numbers

* For coupling strengths \(\lambda = g^2 N_C = 5, 10, 20\), the shear viscosity to entropy density ratios are \(\eta/s = 2.716, 0.994, 0.385\) respectively.
* For the same couplings, the ideal photon production constants are \(\tilde{C}_\gamma^\text{ideal} = 0.580, 1.019, 1.791\).
* The pre-equilibrium photon contribution was computed for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\,\text{TeV}\) PbPb collisions at 0-20% centrality, using \(\eta/s = 0.08\) as an external parameter for the matching to hydrodynamics.
* The pre-equilibrium photon yield is comparable to the prompt photon yield between \(p_T \sim 2.5\,\text{GeV}\) and \(p_T \sim 7\,\text{GeV}\), and becomes subdominant above \(p_T \sim 7\,\text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hydrodynamic attractors in QCD kinetic theory (Du 2020) and pre-equilibrium dilepton production (Coquet 2021) to the case of real photons. The universal scaling formula enables the first direct comparison of pre-equilibrium photon production with experimental data from ALICE (2015) for PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV. The matching procedure allows smooth switching from pre-equilibrium kinetic theory to thermal hydrodynamic photon production, and the authors suggest that a similar scaling analysis for dileptons could provide direct information about the pre-equilibrium phase of heavy-ion collisions.
