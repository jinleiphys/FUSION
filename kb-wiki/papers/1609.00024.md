# Thermal chiral vortical and magnetic waves: new excitation modes in chiral fluids

> In certain circumstances, chiral (parity-violating) medium can be described hydrodynamically as a chiral fluid with microscopic quantum anomalies. Possible examples of such systems include strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma, liquid helium 3He-A, neutron stars and the Early Universe. We study first-order hydrodynamics of a chiral fluid on a vortex background and in an external magnetic field. We show that there are two previously undiscovered modes describing heat waves propagating along the vortex and magnetic field. We call them the Thermal Chiral Vortical Wave and Thermal Chiral Magnetic Wave. We also identify known gapless excitations of density (chiral vortical and chiral magnetic waves) and transverse velocity (chiral Alfven wave). We demonstrate that the velocity of the chiral vortical wave is zero, when the full hydrodynamic framework is applied, and hence the wave is absent and the excitation reduces to the charge diffusion mode. We also comment on the frame-dependent contributions to the obtained propagation velocities.

# Thermal chiral vortical and magnetic waves: new excitation modes in chiral fluids

## Key claim
The authors claim the discovery of two previously unknown propagating heat-wave modes in chiral fluids, the thermal chiral vortical wave and the thermal chiral magnetic wave, but only under the condition that the background charge density is nonzero (bar n ≠ 0); when the background charge density is zero, the chiral vortical wave claimed in prior literature (Jiang 2015) is absent and reduces to ordinary charge diffusion.

## Method
The authors study first-order relativistic hydrodynamics of a chiral fluid with a single U(1) charge and U(1)^3 triangle anomaly, linearizing the conservation equations around equilibrium on a vortex background (constant vorticity Omega) or in an external magnetic field B. They perform a Fourier transform assuming the wave vector k is parallel to Omega or B, derive dispersion relations from the determinant of the linearized system, and identify propagating modes by extracting the real part of omega proportional to k.

## Key numbers
- For the thermal chiral vortical wave (bar n ≠ 0, fl p = 0): propagation speed v_Omega = 2 Omega [ (C bar mu bar s^2 bar T)/(bar n^2 c_p) - (c_T bar T)/(bar n c_p)(2 bar w - 3 bar mu bar n) ] - (2 bar mu Omega)/(bar w)(c_T bar T^2 + (C/3) bar mu^2), with diffusion coefficient D_T = (sigma bar w^2)/(bar n^2 bar T c_p).
- For the chiral magnetic wave (bar n = 0): propagation speed v_chi = (C B)/chi - (c_T bar T^2 B)/(2 bar w), with damping rate D_chi = sigma/chi.
- For the thermal chiral magnetic wave (bar n ≠ 0, fl p = 0): propagation speed v_chi^T = (B bar T)/(bar n^2 c_p)(c_T + C bar s^2) - (B)/(2 bar w)(c_T bar T^2 + C bar mu^2), with diffusion coefficient D_T = (sigma bar w^2)/(bar n^2 bar T c_p).
- For the chiral Alfven wave: propagation speed v_CAW = (xi B)/(2 bar w), where xi is the chiral vortical coefficient.
- For the chiral vortical wave (bar n = 0): the linear term in k vanishes (speed = 0), leaving only charge diffusion omega = -i (sigma/chi) k^2.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic study of anomalous transport in chiral fluids, building on the framework of Son and Surowka (2009) and Neiman and Oz (2010) for constitutive relations with anomaly-induced terms. It corrects the claim of a propagating chiral vortical wave by Jiang et al. (2015), showing that wave is absent in the full hydrodynamic treatment and reduces to charge diffusion. The new thermal chiral vortical and magnetic waves complement the known chiral magnetic wave (Kharzeev and Yee 2010) and chiral Alfven wave (Yamamoto 2015). The results enable future studies of heat transport in rotating or magnetized chiral systems such as quark-gluon plasma, neutron stars, and Weyl semimetals, and the authors propose extending the analysis to systems with two charges (vector and axial).
