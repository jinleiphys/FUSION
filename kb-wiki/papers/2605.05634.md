# Relativistic mean-field study of the neutron star inner crust using the asymmetric finite difference method

> The ground-state properties of neutron-rich nuclear clusters in the inner crust of neutron stars are investigated within the Wigner-Seitz approximation using a relativistic mean-field framework. The radial Dirac equations are solved with an asymmetric finite-difference scheme, by which the hermiticity is preserved and spurious states are eliminated. Calculations are performed for representative Wigner-Seitz cells employing TM1-based interactions with different symmetry-energy slope parameters $L$, as well as a parametrization with a larger nucleon effective mass. It is found that the binding energy per nucleon decreases systematically with increasing $L$, while a larger effective mass leads to further reduction, particularly at higher densities. Quantum shell effects, which are absent in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, give rise to oscillatory density distributions and modify neutron properties. Within the Wigner-Seitz cell, the resulting neutron root-mean-square radius and chemical potential are shown to be sensitive to both $L$ and the effective nucleon mass, underscoring their important roles in determining the microscopic structure of the neutron-star inner crust.

## Key claim

The binding energy per nucleon of neutron-rich clusters in the inner crust decreases systematically with increasing symmetry-energy slope parameter \(L\), and a larger effective nucleon mass leads to further reduction, particularly at higher densities; however, the authors note that in realistic inner-crust calculations the \(\beta\)-equilibrium condition should be imposed and the energetically optimal configuration would generally depend on \(L\), so the influence of \(L\) is reflected not only in the energy variation of a given configuration but also in the possible change of the equilibrium composition.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Dirac equations for nucleons within the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation using an asymmetric finite-difference (AFD) scheme, which preserves hermiticity and eliminates spurious states without introducing artificial terms such as the Wilson mass. Calculations are performed for nine representative Wigner-Seitz cell configurations (ranging from \(^{180}\)Zr to \(^{1800}\)Sn) using TM1-based interactions with different symmetry-energy slope parameters \(L = 40, 60, 80, 110.8\) MeV, as well as the TM1m* parametrization with a larger nucleon effective mass (\(M^*/M \approx 0.79\) versus the standard TM1 value of \(M^*/M \approx 0.63\)). For comparison, the same configurations are also computed within the Thomas-Fermi (TF) approximation, which neglects quantum shell effects.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for Layer 1 (\(^{180}\)Zr, \(\rho = 0.0016 \rho_0\) with \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)): \(-4.958\) MeV (AFD, \(L=40\)), \(-5.168\) MeV (AFD, TM1 with \(L=110.8\)), and \(-5.062\) MeV (AFD, TM1m* with \(L=40\)).
- Binding energy per nucleon for Layer 9 (\(^{1800}\)Sn, \(\rho = 0.12 \rho_0\)): \(5.226\) MeV (AFD, \(L=40\)), \(2.277\) MeV (AFD, TM1 with \(L=110.8\)), and \(3.828\) MeV (AFD, TM1m* with \(L=40\)).
- Neutron root-mean-square radius for Layer 1: \(24.591\) fm (AFD, \(L=40\)), \(17.622\) fm (AFD, TM1 with \(L=110.8\)), and \(19.413\) fm (AFD, TM1m* with \(L=40\)).
- Neutron root-mean-square radius for Layer 9: \(15.250\) fm (AFD, \(L=40\)), \(14.755\) fm (AFD, TM1 with \(L=110.8\)), and \(15.108\) fm (AFD, TM1m* with \(L=40\)).
- The TM1m* interaction features a larger effective mass ratio \(M^*/M \approx 0.79\) compared to the standard TM1 value of \(M^*/M \approx 0.63\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field study of neutron-star inner crust matter beyond the semiclassical Thomas-Fermi approximation used in earlier works such as Shen (1998) and Shchechilin (2024), by incorporating quantum shell effects via the asymmetric finite-difference method developed by Zhang (2022). The results enable systematic investigation of how the symmetry-energy slope \(L\) and effective nucleon mass influence the microscopic structure of inner-crust nuclei, which is relevant for interpreting experimental constraints from the PREX-II experiment (favoring \(L > 100\) MeV) and astrophysical observations of neutron stars and gravitational waves (suggesting \(L \approx 40\)–\(80\) MeV). The approach can be extended in future work to include pairing correlations and \(\beta\)-equilibrium conditions for a more realistic description of the inner crust.
