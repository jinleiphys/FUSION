# New-physics signatures via $C\!P$ violation in $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^0μ^+μ^-$ and $η^\prime\toημ^+μ^-$ decays

> In this work we investigate the prospect of observing new-physics signatures via $C\!P$ violation in $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^0μ^+μ^-$ and $η^\prime\toημ^+μ^-$ decays at the REDTOP experiment. We make use of the SMEFT to parametrise the new-physics $C\!P$-violating effects and find that the projected REDTOP statistics are not competitive with respect to nEDM experiments. This reasserts the $η\toμ^+μ^-$ process as the most promising channel to find $C\!P$-violation at this experimental facility.

## Key claim

Using SMEFT to parametrize new-physics CP-violating effects, the projected REDTOP statistics for the semileptonic decays η(′)→π0μ+μ− and η′→ημ+μ− are not competitive with respect to nEDM experiments, reasserting the η→μ+μ− process as the most promising channel for finding CP-violation at this facility.

## Method

The authors used the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) to parametrize new-physics CP-violating effects via dimension-six operators involving quarks and leptons, specifically the ℓedq and ℓequ operators. They computed the required hadronic matrix elements for scalar currents within large-Nc chiral perturbation theory at NLO, including isospin-breaking effects, and constructed longitudinal and transverse muon polarization asymmetries to quantify CP-violating signals. The sensitivity to Wilson coefficients was estimated by combining the theoretical asymmetries with projected REDTOP event statistics.

## Key numbers

- REDTOP projected statistics: 5×10^12 η/yr and 5×10^10 η′/yr, with assumed reconstruction efficiencies of approximately 20%
- Statistical SM backgrounds at 1σ: σ(η→π0μ+μ−) = 1.35×10−2, σ(η′→π0μ+μ−) = 0.105, σ(η′→ημ+μ−) = 0.354
- REDTOP sensitivity to Im cℓequ(1)2211 from η→π0μ+μ− longitudinal asymmetry: 0.0695
- REDTOP sensitivity to Im cℓedq2211 from η→π0μ+μ− longitudinal asymmetry: 0.0720
- REDTOP sensitivity to Im cℓedq2222 from η→π0μ+μ− longitudinal asymmetry: 0.686
- nEDM upper bounds: Im cℓequ(1)2211 ≤ 0.001, Im cℓedq2211 ≤ 0.002, Im cℓedq2222 ≤ 0.02
- η→μ+μ− REDTOP sensitivity: Im cℓequ(1)2211 = 0.007, Im cℓedq2211 = 0.007, Im cℓedq2222 = 0.005
- η-η′ mixing angle φ23 = (41.5 ± 0.5)°
- Isospin-breaking parameters: ε12 = (2.4 ± 1.0)%, ε13 = (2.5 ± 0.9)%
- Scalar resonance mass MS = 980 MeV
- B0 = 2.64+0.11−0.42 GeV at renormalization scale μ = 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sanchez-Puertas (2018) on CP-violating muon polarization observables in η leptonic decays to the semileptonic channels η(′)→π0μ+μ− and η′→ημ+μ−, which require dedicated hadronic matrix element calculations. The results contrast with the η→μ+μ− decay studied in Sanchez-Puertas (2018), which evades nEDM bounds and should receive highest priority at REDTOP. The study enables experimental prioritization for the proposed REDTOP experiment (Gatto 2016, Gatto 2019) by showing that the semileptonic channels are not competitive for CP-violation searches using muon polarimetry.
