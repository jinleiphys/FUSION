# Signatures of Dark Matter Scattering Inelastically Off Nuclei

> Direct dark matter detection focuses on elastic scattering of dark matter particles off nuclei. In this study, we explore inelastic scattering where the nucleus is excited to a low-lying state of 10-100 keV, with subsequent prompt de-excitation. We calculate the inelastic structure factors for the odd-mass xenon isotopes based on state-of-the-art large-scale shell-model calculations with chiral effective field theory WIMP-nucleon currents. For these cases, we find that the inelastic channel is comparable to or can dominate the elastic channel for momentum transfers around 150 MeV. We calculate the inelastic recoil spectra in the standard halo model, compare these to the elastic case, and discuss the expected signatures in a xenon detector, along with implications for existing and future experiments. The combined information from elastic and inelastic scattering will allow to determine the dominant interaction channel within one experiment. In addition, the two channels probe different regions of the dark matter velocity distribution and can provide insight into the dark halo structure. The allowed recoil energy domain and the recoil energy at which the integrated inelastic rates start to dominate the elastic channel depend on the mass of the dark matter particle, thus providing a potential handle to constrain its mass.

# Signatures of Dark Matter Scattering Inelastically Off Nuclei

## Key claim

Inelastic scattering of dark matter particles off odd-mass xenon isotopes, where the nucleus is excited to a low-lying state of 10-100 keV with subsequent prompt de-excitation, can produce observable signatures that are comparable to or can dominate the elastic scattering channel for momentum transfers around 150 MeV, depending on the WIMP mass and the specific isotope. The inelastic channel is favored over the elastic one at momentum transfers above 36-76 MeV (corresponding to nuclear recoil energies of 5-24 keV) for WIMP masses of 25-500 GeV, but only for spin-dependent interactions, as the inelastic channel is dominated by spin-dependent WIMP-nucleon couplings.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the ANTOINE code with the GCN5082 interaction in a valence space on top of a 100Sn core for 129Xe and 131Xe, incorporating chiral effective field theory WIMP-nucleon currents including both one-body and two-body currents up to order Q^3. They calculated inelastic structure factors for spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus scattering, then computed differential and integrated recoil spectra using the standard halo model with a Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distribution, accounting for detector resolution and quenching effects in liquid xenon detectors.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies: 39.6 keV for the 3/2+ state in 129Xe, 80.2 keV for the 1/2+ state in 131Xe
- Half-lives of excited states: 0.97 ns for 129Xe, 0.48 ns for 131Xe
- Natural abundances: 26.4% for 129Xe, 21.2% for 131Xe
- Inelastic structure factors at p=0 are suppressed by a factor of 10 for 129Xe and more than two orders of magnitude for 131Xe compared to elastic structure factors
- For u=1-2 (p=125-175 MeV), inelastic structure factors become comparable to elastic ones
- Minimal and maximal recoil energies for inelastic scattering off 129Xe with a 100 GeV WIMP: 1.1 keV and 285 keV
- Minimal and maximal recoil energies for inelastic scattering off 131Xe with a 100 GeV WIMP: 5.4 keV and 244 keV
- Inelastic channel dominates elastic channel above E_vis = 5 keV (25 GeV WIMP), 9 keV (50 GeV), 12 keV (100 GeV), 19 keV (250 GeV), and 24 keV (500 GeV) for natural xenon
- Inelastic scattering not possible for 129Xe below 14 GeV WIMP mass, nor for 131Xe below 31 GeV
- Sensitivity to inelastic spin-dependent WIMP-nucleon cross sections: 3.8x10^-36 cm^2 (XENON100) and 1.3x10^-33 cm^2 (XMASS) for a 130 GeV WIMP
- Gamma energy resolution: sigma/E = 9% at 40 keV, sigma/E = 6.5% at 80 keV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of inelastic dark matter scattering by Toivanen (2008, 2009) using improved shell-model interactions and larger valence spaces, and builds on the elastic structure factor calculations for xenon by Menendez and Klos. The authors find significant differences from Toivanen (2009), where the inelastic structure factor for 129Xe was always decreasing and the suppression at p=0 for 131Xe was an order of magnitude smaller. The results enable xenon-based experiments such as LUX, LZ, XENON1T, XMASS, and DARWIN to search for inelastic scattering signatures as a complementary channel to elastic scattering, potentially allowing determination of the spin-dependent nature of WIMP-nucleon interactions and providing a handle on the WIMP mass and dark matter halo velocity distribution.
