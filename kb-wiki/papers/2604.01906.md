# Collective quantum tunneling with time-dependent generator coordinate method

> Inspired by the work of McGlynn and Simenel [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 102}, 064614 (2020)], this study investigates the quantum tunneling of two interacting distinguishable particles in two potential wells. We first benchmark the system by reproducing key established results: the exact quantum solution and the spurious self-trapping effect that arises in the real-time mean-field dynamics for strong interactions. To exactly capture the tunneling dynamics, we apply the time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) to the model. Numerical simulations demonstrate that the TDGCM, by utilizing the real-time mean-field states as generator states, successfully overcomes the self-trapping effect, yielding tunneling dynamics in excellent agreement with the exact solution. Furthermore, we explore the expectation values of the generator coordinates from the correlated TDGCM many-body wave function. While different methods for calculating expectation values show consistent results in some cases, significant discrepancies are observed in others, providing critical insights into the emergence of collective and single-particle behaviors in interacting systems. This work also verifies the TDGCM as a robust framework for describing collective quantum tunneling and opens avenues for its application to more complex and realistic systems.

## Key claim

The time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM), using real-time mean-field states as generator states, successfully reproduces exact quantum tunneling dynamics for two interacting distinguishable particles in a double-well potential, overcoming the spurious self-trapping effect that plagues real-time mean-field theory for interaction strengths \(|\mu| > 2\).

## Method

The authors benchmark a two-particle, two-well model with exact diagonalization and real-time mean-field (Hartree) dynamics, then apply the TDGCM by constructing generator states from the real-time mean-field product states parameterized by the collective coordinate \(\theta\) (population imbalance). The time-dependent Griffin-Hill-Wheeler equation is solved for the weight functions \(g(\theta,t)\), and the expectation value of the left-well number operator \(N_L(t)\) is computed from the correlated TDGCM many-body wave function and compared with exact and mean-field results.

## Key numbers

- Self-trapping in real-time mean-field dynamics occurs when \(|\mu| > 2\), independent of the sign of the interaction strength \(\mu\).
- For \(\mu = 4\) (strong interaction), real-time mean-field fails completely (self-trapping), while TDGCM with three generator states reproduces the exact tunneling dynamics.
- The exact tunneling rate is \(\eta = (\sqrt{4\alpha^2 + \mu^2} - |\mu|)/\pi\), which decreases as \(\mu\) increases (for \(\mu > 0\)).
- A numerical regularization threshold \(\epsilon = 10^{-10}\) is used for eigenvalues of the norm kernel to ensure numerical stability.

## Context

This work extends the benchmark study of McGlynn and Simenel (2020), who first demonstrated the self-trapping effect in real-time mean-field dynamics for the same two-well model and proposed an imaginary-time mean-field approach as a remedy. The present paper validates TDGCM as an alternative beyond-mean-field framework that overcomes self-trapping without requiring imaginary-time evolution, and it opens the door to applying TDGCM to more complex nuclear tunneling problems such as heavy-ion collisions and fission dynamics, where the method has been developed by groups such as Regnier et al. (2020) and others.
