# Enhanced Strange Particle Yields - Signal of a Phase of Massless Particles ?

> The yields of strange particles are calculated with the UrQMD model for p,Pb(158 AGeV)Pb collisions and compared to experimental data. The yields are enhanced in central collisions if compared to proton induced or peripheral Pb+Pb collisions. The enhancement is due to secondary interactions. Nevertheless, only a reduction of the quark masses or equivalently an increase of the string tension provides an adequate description of the large observed enhancement factors (WA97 and NA49). Furthermore, the yields of unstable strange resonances as the Lambda_star(1520) resonance or the phi meson are considerably affected by hadronic rescattering of the decay products.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of strange particle yields in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, which grows with strangeness content and reaches a factor of ~15 for Omega baryons, cannot be explained by hadronic rescattering alone in the UrQMD model; only an ad hoc reduction of constituent quark masses to current quark masses (or equivalently an increase of the string tension to 3 GeV/fm) reproduces the experimental data, suggesting a possible signal of chiral symmetry restoration or a phase of massless particles.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, which incorporates hadronic and string degrees of freedom, to simulate p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV. Strange baryon yields (Lambda, Xi, Omega) were computed as a function of centrality and rapidity, and compared to WA97 and NA49 experimental data. The model's strangeness suppression factor gamma_s was modified either by reducing constituent quark masses (m_q=10 MeV, m_s=230 MeV) or by increasing the effective string tension (kappa=3 GeV/fm), both yielding gamma_s ≈ 0.65 compared to the default gamma_s ≈ 0.3.

## Key numbers

- Default strangeness suppression factor: gamma_s ≈ 0.3, from constituent quark masses m_q=0.3 GeV and m_s=0.5 GeV with string tension kappa=1 GeV/fm
- Reduced masses/enhanced string tension yields gamma_s ≈ 0.65, from m_q=10 MeV, m_s=230 MeV or kappa=3 GeV/fm
- Enhancement factors at midrapidity for default UrQMD (values in brackets for reduced masses/enhanced string tension): Lambda ≈ 1.5 (2), Xi^- ≈ 2 (6), Omega^- ≈ 5 (13)
- Omega enhancement factor can reach 15 for the reduced masses/enhanced string tension case
- Approximately 80% of Omega baryons are produced in secondary meson-baryon collisions, 20% in baryon-baryon collisions; about 15% of produced Omega's are reabsorbed
- Reconstructable fraction of Lambda*(1520) decays: ≤ 0.5 (about 1.2 per event out of 2.4 total decays)
- Reconstructable fraction of phi meson decays via K+K-: ≤ 0.75 (approximately 25% reduction due to kaon rescattering)
- Approximately 40% of phi mesons scatter elastically; about 40% of phi's are produced via late resonant K+K- → phi scattering

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangeness enhancement as a QGP signature (Rafelski, 1982) and directly compares to WA97 and NA49 experimental data (Andersson et al., 1998; Sikler et al., 1999). The finding that hadronic rescattering alone cannot explain the observed enhancement factors contradicts purely hadronic explanations and supports the interpretation that reduced quark masses (motivated by chiral symmetry restoration) or enhanced string tension (from overlapping color fields) may signal the transition to a deconfined phase. The results enable future studies of how resonance reconstruction distortions affect experimental measurements of Lambda*(1520) and phi mesons, and qualitatively explain the different inverse slope parameters observed by NA49 (phi → K+K-) versus NA50 (phi → mu+mu-).
