# Effective Transport Equations for non-Abelian Plasmas

> Starting from classical transport theory, we derive a set of covariant equations describing the dynamics of mean fields and their statistical fluctuations in a non-Abelian plasma in or out of equilibrium. A general procedure is detailed for integrating-out the fluctuations as to obtain the effective transport equations for the mean fields. In this manner, collision integrals for Boltzmann equations are obtained as correlators of fluctuations. The formalism is applied to a hot non-Abelian plasma close to equilibrium. We integrate-out explicitly the fluctuations with typical momenta of the Debye mass, and obtain the collision integral in a leading logarithmic approximation. We also identify a source for stochastic noise. The resulting dynamical equations are of the Boltzmann-Langevin type. While our approach is based on classical physics, we also give the necessary generalizations to study the quantum plasmas. Ultimately, the dynamical equations for soft and ultra-soft fields change only in the value for the Debye mass.

## Key claim

Starting from classical transport theory for non-Abelian plasmas, the authors derive effective transport equations for mean fields by integrating out fluctuations, obtaining collision integrals in a leading logarithmic approximation and identifying a stochastic noise source; the resulting Boltzmann-Langevin equations are valid for a hot non-Abelian plasma close to equilibrium, with the caveat that the derivation assumes a small gauge coupling and a small plasma parameter.

## Method

The authors begin with the microscopic Wong equations for classical point particles carrying non-Abelian color charges and introduce a phase-space distribution function. They perform a Gibbs ensemble average to separate mean fields from statistical fluctuations, then derive exact dynamical equations for both the mean quantities and the fluctuations. For a hot plasma close to equilibrium, they apply a second-moment approximation and a small-coupling expansion, explicitly integrating out fluctuations with momenta of order the Debye mass to obtain the collision integral and noise term in a leading logarithmic approximation.

## Key numbers

* The collision integral is obtained in a **leading logarithmic approximation** for a hot non-Abelian plasma close to equilibrium.
* Fluctuations with typical momenta of the **Debye mass** are integrated out explicitly.
* The dynamical equations for soft and ultra-soft fields change only in the value for the **Debye mass** when generalizing from classical to quantum plasmas.
* The formalism applies to SU(N) gauge groups, with the number of color charges being **N²-1** for SU(N).

## Context

This work extends the Klimontovitch approach to classical kinetic theory for Abelian plasmas to the non-Abelian case, treating color charges as dynamical variables and consistently handling the nonlinearities of non-Abelian gauge interactions. It corrects and generalizes earlier attempts by Selikhov, Gyulassy and Shuryak, and Markov to derive similar kinetic equations, which were not fully consistent. The resulting effective theory for ultra-soft modes matches the findings of Bodeker (1998), Arnold (1998), and Blaizot and Iancu (1998), and opens the door for applications to out-of-equilibrium non-Abelian plasmas, including quark-gluon plasma physics and electroweak baryogenesis scenarios.
