# Towards a dynamical understanding of the non-$D \bar D$ decay of $ψ(3770)$

> We investigate the $ψ(3770)$ non-$D\bar{D}$ decays into $VP$, where $V$ and $P$ denote vector and pseudoscalar mesons, respectively, via OZI-rule-evading intermediate meson rescatterings in an effective Lagrangian theory. By identifying the leading meson loop transitions and constraining the model parameters with the available experimental data for $ψ(3770)\to J/ψη$, $ϕη$ and $ρπ$, we succeed in making a quantitative prediction for all $ψ(3770)\to VP$ with $BR_{VP}$ from 0.41% to 0.64%. It indicates that the OZI-rule-evading long-range interactions are still playing a role in $ψ(3770)$ strong decays, and could be a key towards a full understanding of the mysterious $ψ(3770)$ non-$D\bar{D}$ decay mechanism.

## Key claim

The non-$D\bar D$ decays of $\psi(3770)$ into vector-pseudoscalar (VP) meson pairs can be quantitatively explained by intermediate charmed meson rescatterings (OZI-rule-evading long-range interactions), with the sum of all VP branching ratios predicted to lie between 0.41% and 0.64%, contingent on the interference phase between the meson-loop and short-range (SOZI) amplitudes.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian theory for $\psi(3770)\to VP$ decays via intermediate $D\bar D$ and $D\bar D^*+c.c.$ meson loops, evaluating both $t$-channel (meson exchange) and $s$-channel ($\psi^\prime$-$\psi(3770)$ mixing) loop diagrams. Model parameters are constrained by fitting to the experimental branching ratios for $\psi(3770)\to J/\psi\eta$, $\phi\eta$, and the upper limit for $\rho\pi$, using a dipole form factor with a cutoff parameter $\alpha=1.73$ fixed from the $J/\psi\eta$ channel. The short-range OZI-allowed (SOZI) contributions are included with a relative phase $\delta$ and a flavor-blind coupling $g_S$, determined from the $\phi\eta$ and $\rho\pi$ data.

## Key numbers

- Sum of all $\psi(3770)\to VP$ branching ratios predicted between 0.41% and 0.64%, depending on the interference phase $\delta$ between meson-loop and SOZI amplitudes.
- $BR(\psi(3770)\to J/\psi\eta) = 9.0 \times 10^{-4}$ (fixed to central experimental value from PDG 2008), with the $t$-channel loop contributing $8.44 \times 10^{-4}$ and the $s$-channel contributing $0.13 \times 10^{-4}$.
- $BR(\psi(3770)\to \phi\eta) = 3.1 \times 10^{-4}$ (fixed to central experimental value from PDG 2008).
- $BR(\psi(3770)\to \rho\pi) = 24.0 \times 10^{-4}$ (total), with $t$-channel loop contributing $34.45 \times 10^{-4}$ and SOZI contributing $8.53 \times 10^{-4}$, constrained by the experimental upper limit $< 0.24\%$ at 90% C.L. from BES (Ablikim 2005).
- $BR(\psi(3770)\to K^{*+}K^- + c.c.) = 8.91 \times 10^{-4}$, $BR(\psi(3770)\to K^{*0}\bar K^0 + c.c.) = 9.90 \times 10^{-4}$.
- $BR(\psi(3770)\to \phi\eta^\prime) = 3.78 \times 10^{-4}$, $BR(\psi(3770)\to \omega\eta) = 4.69 \times 10^{-4}$, $BR(\psi(3770)\to \omega\eta^\prime) = 0.39 \times 10^{-4}$.
- $BR(\psi(3770)\to J/\psi\pi^0) = 4.4 \times 10^{-4}$ (isospin-violating channel).
- Extracted $\psi^\prime$-$\psi(3770)$ mixing angle $\phi \simeq 4.57^\circ$ from the $s$-channel amplitude.
- Form factor cutoff parameter $\alpha = 1.73$ (with $\Lambda = m_{ex} + \alpha \Lambda_{QCD}$, $\Lambda_{QCD}=0.22$ GeV).
- SOZI coupling $g_S^{\rho^0\pi^0} = 0.085$, relative phase $\delta = -66^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of $\psi(3770)$ non-$D\bar D$ decays beyond the $\psi(2S)$-$\psi(1D)$ mixing scheme of Ding (1991) and Rosner (2001), demonstrating that intermediate charmed meson rescatterings (rather than simple mixing) dominate the VP decay channels. The predictions are testable at BES-III (Asner 2008) and complement the contemporaneous work of Liu (2009), who focused on $D\bar D$ rescattering in an on-shell approximation for $J/\psi\eta$, $\rho\pi$, and $J/\psi\pi\pi$. The results enable a systematic search for exclusive non-$D\bar D$ decays to clarify the discrepancy between CLEO (Besson 2005) and BES (Ablikim 2006-2008) measurements of the total non-$D\bar D$ branching ratio.
