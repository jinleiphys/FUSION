# How accurately can the Extended Thomas-Fermi method describe the inner crust of a neutron star?

> We perform a systematic comparison between the results obtained by solving fully self-consistently the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations, and those obtained using the semi-classical Extended Thomas-Fermi method, for various Wigner-Seitz cells within the inner crust of a neutron star. The lack of pairing correlations in the semi-classical approach leads to a large discrepancy between the two approaches. This discrepancy is well beyond the error of the quantum-mechanical calculation, and is related to spurious shell effects in the neutron gas.

## Key claim

The Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) method can reproduce Hartree-Fock results for inner crust Wigner-Seitz cells within their numerical error bars only when pairing correlations are absent; when pairing is neglected in the ETF approach, the discrepancy with full Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations reaches approximately 200 keV per particle, an order of magnitude larger than the estimated Hartree-Fock error.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations self-consistently for spherically symmetric Wigner-Seitz cells using the SLy4 functional for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta interaction for pairing, with Dirichlet-Neumann mixed boundary conditions to discretize continuum states. They then performed Extended Thomas-Fermi calculations by fitting modified Fermi-Dirac functions to the quantal densities and using the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion truncated at second order to obtain kinetic and spin-current densities, omitting pairing correlations in the semi-classical approach. The comparison was done for a series of cells with fixed proton number Z=50 and varying baryonic density, using box sizes of 50, 60, and 80 fm.

## Key numbers

- For a box size of R_B=60 fm, the average error in Hartree-Fock calculations due to continuum discretisation is ⟨δe⟩≈20 keV per particle.
- At very low densities (ρ_B≤0.002 fm⁻³), the discrepancy between ETF and Hartree-Fock methods reaches approximately 100-150 keV per particle.
- The discrepancy between ETF and full Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations reaches a maximum of ≈220 keV per particle when pairing is active.
- For R_B=50 fm the constant discrepancy at high densities is ≈30 keV; for R_B=60 fm it is ≈20 keV; for R_B=80 fm it falls to ≈15 keV.

## Context

This work extends systematic comparisons of semi-classical and quantal methods, which have been routinely performed for finite nuclei (Bartel 2002), to the inner crust of neutron stars for the first time while controlling for different aspects of the calculations. The results contradict the assumption that the ETF method can be reliably used without pairing for inner crust studies, and enable future work combining ETF with Gaussian Process Emulation (Shelley 2018) to perform full equation of state analysis including temperature effects, provided pairing correlations are incorporated for neutrons (Schuck 2013, Pearson 2015).
