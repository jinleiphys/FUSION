# On the possibility of generating a 4-neutron resonance with a {\boldmath $T=3/2$} isospin 3-neutron force

> We consider the theoretical possibility to generate a narrow resonance in the four neutron system as suggested by a recent experimental result. To that end, a phenomenological $T=3/2$ three neutron force is introduced, in addition to a realistic $NN$ interaction. We inquire what should be the strength of the $3n$ force in order to generate such a resonance. The reliability of the three-neutron force in the $T=3/2$ channel is exmined, by analyzing its consistency with the low-lying $T=1$ states of $^4$H, $^4$He and $^4$Li and the $^3{\rm H} + n$ scattering. The {\it ab initio} solution of the $4n$ Schrödinger equation is obtained using the complex scaling method with boundary conditions appropiate to the four-body resonances. We find that in order to generate narrow $4n$ resonant states a remarkably attractive $3N$ force in the $T=3/2$ channel is required.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a narrow four-neutron resonance, as suggested by a recent experimental result, can be generated by introducing a phenomenological three-neutron force with total isospin T=3/2, but finds that such a force must be remarkably attractive to produce narrow resonant states, raising questions about its consistency with known nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors solved the four-neutron Schrödinger equation using the complex scaling method with boundary conditions appropriate for four-body resonances, employing both the Gaussian expansion method and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations. They supplemented the realistic AV8' nucleon-nucleon interaction with a phenomenological isospin-dependent three-nucleon force, treating the attractive strength parameter of the T=3/2 component as a free parameter while fixing the T=1/2 component to reproduce binding energies of 3H, 3He, and 4He. The reliability of the T=3/2 three-neutron force was examined by analyzing its consistency with low-lying T=1 states of 4H, 4He, and 4Li, and with 3H + n scattering.

## Key numbers

* Critical strength of the T=3/2 three-nucleon force required to bind the 4n system at E = -1.07 MeV for the Jπ=0+ state: W1(0)(T=3/2) = -36.14 MeV
* Critical strength for Jπ=2+ state: W1(0)(T=3/2) = -38.05 MeV
* Critical strength for Jπ=2- state: W1(0)(T=3/2) = -58.37 MeV
* For comparison, the T=1/2 three-nucleon force strength is W1(T=1/2) = -2.04 MeV
* In the energy range suggested by the recent measurement (E_R = 0.83 ± 0.65 ± 1.25 MeV), the calculated maximum decay width is Γ = 0.6 MeV, compared to the experimental upper limit of Γ = 2.6 MeV
* For the Jπ=0+ state with W1(T=3/2) = -28 MeV, a narrow resonance was found at E_res = 3.65 - 0.66i MeV
* For the Jπ=0+ state with W1(T=3/2) = -21 MeV, a broad resonance was found at E_res = 5.88 - 2.85i MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of multineutron systems by Lazauskas (2003) and Lazauskas and Carbonell, which used Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and found no narrow tetraneutron resonances. It responds to experimental claims of a possible tetraneutron resonance by Kisamori (2015) from the 4He(8He,8Be)4n reaction. The study also builds on Green's function Monte Carlo calculations by Pieper (2003) using AV18 and Illinois three-nucleon forces, which suggested a possible broad resonance at E_R = 2 MeV. The results enable future investigations into whether T=3/2 three-nucleon forces of the required strength are compatible with the broader nuclear chart, particularly neutron-rich helium isotopes.
