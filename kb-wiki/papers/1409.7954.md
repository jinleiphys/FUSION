# Deep sub-threshold $Ξ$ and $Λ$ production in nuclear collisions with the UrQMD transport model

> We present results on deep sub threshold hyperon production in nuclear collisions, with the UrQMD transport model. Introducing anti-kaon+baryon and hyperon+hyperon strangeness exchange reactions we obtain a good description of experimental data on single strange hadron production in Ar+KCl reactions at $E_{lab}=1.76$ A GeV. We find that the hyperon strangeness exchange is the dominant process contributing to the $Ξ^-$ yield, however remains short of explaining the $Ξ^-/Λ$ ratio measured with the HADES experiment. We also discuss possible reasons for the discrepancy with previous studies and the experimental results, finding that many details of the transport simulation may have significant effects on the final $Ξ^-$ yield.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model, extended with anti-kaon+baryon and hyperon+hyperon strangeness exchange reactions, reproduces single strange hadron yields in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76 A GeV but still underestimates the measured Ξ⁻/Λ ratio by roughly a factor of 10, even though hyperon+hyperon exchange is the dominant Ξ⁻ production channel.

## Method

The authors implemented parametrized cross sections for K⁻+N ↔ π+Y reactions (fitted to experimental data) and isospin-dependent cross sections for Y+Y ↔ N+Ξ reactions (from a gauged SU(3) Lagrangian in Born approximation) into the UrQMD v3.4 hadronic transport model. They simulated Ar+KCl collisions at Elab = 1.76 A GeV with impact parameter b < 5 fm and Au+Au collisions at Elab = 1.23 A GeV with b < 9.5 fm, comparing the resulting strange particle ratios to HADES experimental data and a thermal model fit.

## Key numbers

- Ξ⁻/Λ ratio from UrQMD is roughly a factor of 10 smaller than the HADES measurement for Ar+KCl at Elab = 1.76 A GeV.
- Increasing the Y+Y ↔ N+Ξ cross sections by a factor of 2 leads to only a 10% increase in the Ξ⁻/Λ ratio.
- The Ξ⁻/Λ ratio appears nearly constant across the two beam energies investigated (Elab = 1.76 A GeV and 1.23 A GeV), while the K⁻/π ratio shows a clear beam energy dependence.
- The parametrized ΛΛ → Ξ⁻p cross section is given as (37.15/2) × (p_N/p_Λ) × (√s − √s₀)⁻⁰·¹⁶ mb.

## Context

This work extends previous transport model studies of sub-threshold strangeness production, particularly the RVUU model calculation by Li et al. (2012) which used the same hyperon+hyperon exchange cross sections but obtained a higher Ξ/Λ ratio. The results are compared to HADES experimental data (Agakishiev et al., 2009, 2010) and a thermal fit (Agakishiev et al., 2010; Wheaton and Cleymans, 2004). The study identifies that details of the transport simulation, such as whether Λ production occurs directly or through resonance decays and the use of test particles, significantly affect the final Ξ⁻ yield, suggesting that the discrepancy with experiment may arise from these systematic uncertainties rather than missing reaction channels.
