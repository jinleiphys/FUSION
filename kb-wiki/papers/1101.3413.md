# The absolute mass of neutrino and the first unique forbidden beta-decay of 187Re

> The planned rhenium beta-decay experiment MARE might probe the absolute mass scale of neutrinos with the same sensitivity as the tritium beta-decay experiment KATRIN, which will start data taking in 2011 and will proceed for five years. We present the energy distribution of emitted electrons for the first unique forbidden beta-decay of 187Re. It is found that the p-wave emission of electron dominates over the s-wave. By assuming mixing of three neutrinos the Kurie function for the rhenium beta-decay is derived. It is shown that the Kurie plot near the endpoint is within a good accuracy linear in the limit of massless neutrinos like the Kurie plot of the superallowed beta-decay of 3H.

## Key claim

The Kurie plot near the endpoint of the first unique forbidden beta-decay of 187Re is, within good accuracy, linear in the limit of massless neutrinos, similar to the superallowed beta-decay of tritium, but this linearity holds only because the p-wave electron emission dominates over the s-wave by a factor of about 10^4.

## Method

The authors derived the theoretical spectral shape of the electron energy distribution for the first unique forbidden beta-decay of 187Re to the ground state of 187Os, considering both s1/2 and p3/2 electron emission channels. They included finite nuclear size effects and relativistic Fermi functions F0 and F1, then constructed the Kurie function assuming three-neutrino mixing with normal mass hierarchy. The partial decay rates for the two electron partial waves were computed numerically to determine their relative contributions.

## Key numbers

- The Q-value of the 187Re beta-decay is approximately 2.47 keV.
- The isotopic abundance of 187Re is 62.8%.
- About 10^4 times more p3/2-state electrons are emitted compared with s1/2-state electrons in the 187Re beta-decay.
- The maximal momentum of the electron is approximately 49.3 keV, while the maximal momentum of the neutrino is approximately 2.5 keV.
- The ratio p_e^2 F1(Z,E_e)/F0(Z,E_e) is approximately constant at 0.11 m_e^2 near the endpoint.
- The current best upper bound on the effective neutrino mass m_beta from Mainz and Troitsk experiments is less than 2.2 eV.
- The KATRIN experiment aims for a sensitivity of about 0.2 eV.
- The MARE I experiment might reach a statistical sensitivity of 4 eV after 3 years of data taking.
- The previous rhenium experiments (Genova and Milano/Como groups, MIBETA and MANU) achieved a sensitivity of m_beta less than 15 eV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the 187Re beta-decay spectrum beyond the superallowed approximation used for tritium decay, which was the basis for the KATRIN experiment (KATRIN collaboration, 2011). The results are directly applicable to the planned MARE experiment (MARE collaboration), which aims to challenge the KATRIN sensitivity goal of 0.2 eV. The analysis shows that despite being a first unique forbidden transition, the 187Re decay near the endpoint behaves similarly to the superallowed tritium decay studied by the Mainz and Troitsk groups (Otten, 2008), enabling a direct comparison of neutrino mass sensitivities between the two experimental approaches.
