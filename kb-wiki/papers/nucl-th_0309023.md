# Pentaquark baryon production from photon-neuteron reactions

> Extending the hadronic Lagrangians that we recently introduced for studying pentaquark $Θ^+$ baryon production from meson-proton, proton-proton, and photon-proton reactions near threshold to include the anomalous interaction between $γ$ and $K^*K$, we evaluate the cross section for $Θ^+$ production from photon-neutron reactions, in which the $Θ^+$ was first detected in the SPring-8 experiment in Japan and the CLAS experiment at Thomas Jefferson National Laboratory. With empirical coupling constants and form factors, and assuming that the decay width of $Θ^+$ is 20 MeV, the predicted cross section is found to have a peak value of about 280 nb, which is substantially larger than that for $Θ^+$ production from photon-proton reactions.

## Key claim

The cross section for pentaquark \(\Theta^+\) production from photon-neutron reactions is predicted to have a peak value of about 280 nb, which is substantially larger than the 40 nb peak from photon-proton reactions; this result depends on assuming a \(\Theta^+\) decay width of 20 MeV and using empirical coupling constants and monopole form factors with a cutoff parameter of 0.75 GeV.

## Method

The authors extended a hadronic model based on gauged SU(3) flavor symmetry, previously used for meson-nucleon, proton-proton, and photon-proton reactions, by including the anomalous parity interaction between the photon and the \(K^*K\) system. They evaluated the cross sections for \(\Theta^+\) production from photon-neutron reactions with two-body (\(\gamma n \to K^-\Theta^+\)), three-body (\(\gamma n \to \pi^0 K^-\Theta^+\) and \(\gamma n \to \pi^- \bar K^0\Theta^+\)), and four-body final states (involving \(K^*\) and \(\rho\) mesons), using monopole form factors at interaction vertices and coupling constants determined from empirical decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Peak cross section for \(\gamma n \to K^-\Theta^+\): about 280 nb at photon energy \(E_\gamma \sim 2.2\) GeV.
- Cross section for \(\gamma n \to K^{*-}\Theta^+\) (three-body final states): about 50 nb above \(E_\gamma = 3\) GeV.
- Cross sections for four-body final states: a few nb for both \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 K^{*-}(\pi^- \bar K^{*0})\Theta^+\) and \(\gamma n \to \rho^0 K^-(\rho^- \bar K^0)\Theta^+\).
- Coupling constant \(g_{KN\Theta} = 4.4\) derived from \(\Gamma_\Theta = 20\) MeV and \(m_\Theta = 1.54\) GeV.
- Coupling constants for anomalous \(\gamma KK^*\) interaction: \(g_{\gamma K^0 K^{*0}} = 0.0388\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(g_{\gamma K^\pm K^{*\pm}} = 0.0257\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- For \(K^{*-}\) exchange with \(g_{K^*N\Theta} = 4.4\), the cross section for \(\gamma n \to K^-\Theta^+\) can reach 160 nb at \(E_\gamma \ge 3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hadronic model calculations for \(\Theta^+\) production from meson-nucleon, proton-proton, and photon-proton reactions (Liu, Ko, 2003) to photon-neutron reactions, motivated by the first detection of the \(\Theta^+\) in the SPring-8 and CLAS experiments. The predicted cross section for \(\gamma n \to K^-\Theta^+\) (280 nb) is an order of magnitude larger than the corresponding photon-proton reaction (40 nb), and the authors note that including \(K^{*-}\) exchange could increase the cross section by about 50%. The results are sensitive to the cutoff parameter in the form factors; increasing it from 0.75 GeV to 1.5 GeV would increase all near-threshold cross sections by an order of magnitude. The paper also notes that the large cross section measured in the SAPHIR experiment at ELSA is difficult to reconcile if the \(\Theta^+\) width is less than 1 MeV as suggested by Arndt (2003).
