# Renormalization in Self-Consistent Approximations schemes at Finite Temperature I: Theory

> Within finite temperature field theory, we show that truncated non-perturbative self-consistent Dyson resummation schemes can be renormalized with local counter-terms defined at the vacuum level. The requirements are that the underlying theory is renormalizable and that the self-consistent scheme follows Baym''s $Φ$-derivable concept. The scheme generates both, the renormalized self-consistent equations of motion and the closed equations for the infinite set of counter terms. At the same time the corresponding 2PI-generating functional and the thermodynamical potential can be renormalized, in consistency with the equations of motion. This guarantees the standard $Φ$-derivable properties like thermodynamic consistency and exact conservation laws also for the renormalized approximation schemes to hold. The proof uses the techniques of BPHZ-renormalization to cope with the explicit and the hidden overlapping vacuum divergences.

## Key claim

The paper proves that any truncated non-perturbative self-consistent Dyson resummation scheme following Baym's Phi-derivable concept can be renormalized using only local, temperature-independent counterterms defined at the vacuum level, provided the underlying theory is renormalizable. The proof relies on BPHZ renormalization techniques to handle both explicit and hidden overlapping vacuum divergences that arise from the self-consistent propagator being involved in divergent loops.

## Method

The authors use the BPHZ (Bogoliubov-Parasiuk-Zimmermann) forest formula to systematically subtract vacuum subdivergences from self-consistent diagrams in phi-fourth theory at finite temperature. They expand the finite-temperature self-energy around the vacuum self-energy and decompose the matter part of the propagator to isolate divergent vacuum structures, showing that the divergent piece of the linear-in-matter self-energy satisfies a Bethe-Salpeter ladder equation whose kernel is the second functional derivative of the Phi functional with respect to the propagator at zero temperature. The renormalization proceeds by solving this vacuum Bethe-Salpeter equation for the complete four-point function Lambda, then using it to define the renormalized self-energy, with all counterterms determined by closed recursive equations at the vacuum level.

## Key numbers

* The superficial degree of divergence for a diagram in phi-fourth theory is delta = 4 - E, where E is the number of external lines; only diagrams with 0, 2, or 4 external legs are divergent.
* The vacuum four-point function Lambda has divergence degree 0 (logarithmically divergent), while the remaining self-energy piece Sigma^{(r)} has divergence degree -2 and the remaining propagator piece G^{(r)} has divergence degree -6.
* The renormalization conditions for the on-shell scheme are: Gamma^{(4,vac)}(s,t,u=0) = lambda/2, Sigma^{(vac)}(p^2=m^2)=0, and partial_{p^2} Sigma^{(vac)}(p^2=m^2)=0.

## Context

This work extends the Phi-derivable approximation framework originally formulated by Baym (1962) and the 2PI effective action approach of Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis (1974) to include full renormalization at finite temperature. It addresses a gap left by earlier work such as Bielajew and Serot (1984) who investigated renormalization of self-consistent two-point self-energy loops at zero temperature but finite density, and by subsequent studies that circumvented renormalization by neglecting real parts of self-energies or using symmetry-violating cutoffs. The results enable consistent treatment of both real and imaginary parts of self-energies in applications such as chiral hadronic models of QCD phase transitions and hard thermal loop approaches to QCD, with numerical solutions for the self-consistent sunset diagram level presented in a companion paper (van Hees and Knoll, 2001, part II).
