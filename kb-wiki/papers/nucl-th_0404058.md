# Causal Diffusion and the Survival of Charge Fluctuations in Nuclear Collisions

> Diffusion may obliterate fluctuation signals of the QCD phase transition in nuclear collisions at SPS and RHIC energies. We propose a hyperbolic diffusion equation to study the dissipation of net charge fluctuations. This equation is needed in a relativistic context, because the classic parabolic diffusion equation violates causality. We find that causality substantially limits the extent to which diffusion can dissipate these fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that using a causal (hyperbolic) diffusion equation instead of the classic parabolic diffusion equation substantially limits the dissipation of net charge fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions, meaning that anomalous fluctuation signals from the QCD phase transition may survive to be detected even after hadronic diffusion.

## Method

The authors derive a causal diffusion equation (the Telegraph equation) from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, then generalize it to relativistic fluids with longitudinal Bjorken flow. They apply the Van Kampen master equation framework to obtain evolution equations for the net-charge correlation function, and compute how diffusion broadens the rapidity width of fluctuations for both constant and time-dependent transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For constant coefficients, the classic diffusion rapidity width increase is ΔV = (2D/τ_o)(1 - τ_o/τ), while causal diffusion gives a slower increase; for α = τ_o/τ_d = 10, the causal and classic results are within 20% for τ > 1.4 τ_o.
- Hadronic charge diffusion coefficient D ≈ 2 fm and relaxation time τ_d ≈ 6 fm from a hadronic transport model.
- For hadronization at τ_o ∼ 6–12 fm and freeze-out τ_f < 20 fm, the relevant range is τ/τ_o < 3, with α = τ_o/τ_d in the range 1 < α < 2.
- The quark-gluon plasma fluctuation benchmark is Nν_qgp ≈ -3, while hadronic resonance gas estimates range from Nν ≈ -1 to -1.7.

## Context

This work extends the classic diffusion treatment of charge fluctuations by Shuryak and Stephanov (2001) and Bower and Gavin (2002), correcting the causality violation inherent in the parabolic diffusion equation. The causal formulation is needed because the relativistic fluid produced in nuclear collisions involves rapid changes in an inhomogeneous environment, as previously noted by Muronga (2002) and Teaney (2003) for related causal fluid equations. The paper enables more reliable predictions for whether net-charge fluctuation signals of the QCD phase transition can survive hadronic diffusion at RHIC and SPS energies.
