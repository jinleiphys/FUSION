# X, Y and Z States

> Many new states in the charmonium mass region were recently discovered by BaBar, Belle, CLEO-c, CDF, D0, BESIII, LHCb and CMS Collaborations. We use the QCD Sum Rule approach to study the possible structure of some of these states.

# X, Y and Z States

## Key claim

Using QCD Sum Rule calculations, the authors find that the X(3872) and Y(4260) can only be explained as mixed states containing both a charmonium (c-cbar) component and a four-quark component, while the Zc(3900) is consistent with a pure tetraquark configuration; however, the X(5568) cannot be described as a scalar tetraquark state within the same approach, and the Zc(4025) is better explained as a D*-Dbar* bound state rather than a new resonance above threshold.

## Method

The authors employ the QCD Sum Rule (QCDSR) method, evaluating two-point and three-point correlation functions at both the quark level (operator product expansion) and the hadronic level, then matching the two descriptions via Borel transformation and sum rule analysis. For each state, interpolating currents are constructed corresponding to different possible structures: tetraquark (diquark-antidiquark), molecular (meson-meson), or mixed charmonium-plus-four-quark configurations, and the resulting masses and decay widths are extracted from the sum rules.

## Key numbers

- X(3872) mass from diquark-antidiquark current: M_X = (3.92 ± 0.13) GeV
- X(3872) mass from molecular D-Dbar* current: M_X = (3.87 ± 0.07) GeV
- X(3872) decay width for X → J/ψ ππ from pure four-quark current: Γ = (50 ± 15) MeV, much larger than the experimental upper limit of 1.2 MeV
- X(3872) decay width with mixing angle α = 9° ± 4°: Γ(X → J/ψ (nπ)) = (9.3 ± 6.9) MeV, compatible with experimental upper limit
- Y(4260) mass from mixed charmonium-tetraquark current with mixing angle θ = (53.0 ± 0.5)°: m_Y = (4.26 ± 0.13) GeV
- Y(4260) partial width for Y → J/ψ ππ: Γ ≈ (4.1 ± 0.6) MeV, compared to experimental total width Γ_exp ≈ (95 ± 14) MeV
- Zc(3900) total width from tetraquark current: Γ = (63.0 ± 18.1) MeV, in agreement with BESIII value (46 ± 22) MeV and Belle value (63 ± 35) MeV
- Zc(3900) coupling to J/ψ π: 3.89 ± 0.56 GeV, giving partial width 29.1 ± 8.2 MeV
- Zc(3900) coupling to D Dbar*: 2.5 ± 0.3 GeV, giving partial width 3.2 ± 0.7 MeV per channel
- Ratio Γ(Zc → D Dbar*) / Γ(Zc → π J/ψ) = 0.22 ± 0.12
- Zc(4025) mass from D*-Dbar* molecular current: 3950 ± 100 MeV for J^P = 0+, 1+, or 2+ assignments
- X(5568) mass from scalar tetraquark current: m_X = (6.39 ± 0.10) GeV, not in agreement with the D0 Collaboration's reported mass

## Context

This work extends the QCD Sum Rule approach previously applied to conventional mesons by Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (1979) and to baryons by Ioffe (1981) and Chung et al. (1982), now applied to the exotic charmonium-like states discovered by BaBar, Belle, CLEO-c, CDF, D0, BESIII, LHCb and CMS collaborations. The paper builds on earlier QCDSR studies of individual states (Nielsen et al. 2014, Dias et al. 2012) and addresses the ongoing debate between molecular versus tetraquark interpretations, as well as the mixed charmonium-plus-four-quark picture proposed by Maiani (2005) and others. The results enable future work to focus on decay width calculations as the key discriminant between theoretical models, since mass predictions alone are insufficient to distinguish different structural interpretations.
