# Photons from Pb-Pb Collisions at CERN SPS

> High energy photon emission rate from matter created in Pb + Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies is evaluated. The evolution of matter from the initial state up to freeze-out has been treated within the framework of (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamic expansion. We observe that the photon spectra measured by the WA98 experiment are well reproduced with hard QCD photons and photons from a thermal source with initial temperature ~ 200 MeV. The effects of the spectral changes of hadrons with temperature on the photon emission rate and on the equation of state are studied. Photon yield for Au + Au collisions at RHIC energies is also estimated.

## Key claim

The photon spectra measured by the WA98 experiment are well reproduced by a model that includes both hard QCD photons and photons from a thermal source with an initial temperature of approximately 200 MeV, but the authors note that it is difficult to distinguish whether this thermal source is a quark-gluon plasma or a hadronic phase with strong medium modifications of hadron masses.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, starting from an initial thermalization time of 1 fm/c. They calculated photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma phase using a rate that includes two-loop contributions, and from the hadronic phase using reactions among π, ρ, ω, η, and a₁ mesons. The effects of temperature-dependent hadron masses (using the universal scaling hypothesis with exponent λ=1/2) were incorporated both in the photon emission rates and in the equation of state, and the hard QCD photon contribution was computed using perturbative QCD with intrinsic k_T effects.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_i = 196 MeV for the QGP scenario, derived from dN/dy = 700 and τ_i = 1 fm/c with g_QGP = 37
- Initial temperature T_i = 205 MeV for the pure hadronic scenario with scaling hypothesis
- Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV assumed for the first-order phase transition
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 120 MeV
- Nuclear thickness T_AA(b=0) = 220/fm² for Pb+Pb at impact parameter b=0
- Intrinsic k_T distribution width ⟨k_T²⟩ = 0.9 GeV² used for hard QCD photons
- K-factor = 2 for higher-order QCD corrections
- Initial radial velocity parameter v_0 = 0.2 reproduces WA98 data for the QGP scenario
- For RHIC predictions: initial temperature T_i = 300 MeV and thermalization time τ_i = 0.5 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of WA98 photon data by consistently including both hard QCD photons (with intrinsic k_T effects) and thermal photons from a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion, addressing omissions in earlier studies by Prc1 (neglected transverse expansion and hard photons), Cywong (neglected thermal contribution), and Kg and Dyp (neglected thermal shift of hadronic masses). The paper shows that either QGP formation or strong in-medium modifications of hadron masses (as in the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis) can reproduce the data, and it provides predictions for Au+Au collisions at RHIC where the quark matter contribution becomes substantial.
