# Polarization Response Functions in Kaon Electroproduction

> The electroproduction of kaons on the nucleon is shown to be an ideal tool to extract electromagnetic form factors of strange mesons and hyperons. Longitudinal n(e,e'K0)Lambda cross sections are found to be sensitive to the K0 form factor, while sensitivity to the Lambda magnetic form factor can be seen in recoil-beam double polarization observables. The KK*γtransition form factor could be extracted by measuring transverse response functions in the K-Sigma channels.

## Key claim

Kaon electroproduction on the nucleon is shown to be an ideal tool to extract electromagnetic form factors of strange mesons and hyperons, but the paper emphasizes that this cannot be done model-independently like a Chew-Low extrapolation; rather, the extraction requires information from TJNAF kaon photoproduction experiments to reduce model dependency and obtain accurate quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors employed a kaon electroproduction model from prior work (Cota et al.) and extended it to the (e,e'K^0) channels using methods from Mart et al. They calculated response functions for various polarization configurations (beam, target, and recoil) using the general formalism for pseudoscalar meson electroproduction, which yields 36 different response functions. The sensitivity of these response functions to different form factor models was studied by comparing predictions from light-cone quark (LCQ) models, quark-meson vertex (QMV) models, vector meson dominance (VMD), and chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) for the K^0 form factor, and from VMD-based calculations for the Lambda and KK*gamma form factors.

## Key numbers

- The K^0 form factor has a peak around -q^2 approximately 1 GeV^2 for the LCQ, QMV, and VMD models, though the heights differ between models.
- The longitudinal cross section for n(e,e'K^0)Lambda computed with the LCQ-model K^0 form factor is almost 50% larger than the calculation with no K^0 pole.
- The chiPT prediction for the K^0 form factor is only valid for -q^2 less than 0.3 GeV^2.
- The zero in the charged K+K*+gamma transition form factor occurs around q^2 = -2.6 GeV^2, but is not visible since cross sections are already very small at these momentum transfers.

## Context

This work extends prior kaon electroproduction models (Cota et al., Mart et al.) to neutral kaon channels and proposes methods to extract strange hadron form factors that are otherwise inaccessible due to the lack of stable hyperon targets. The paper builds on earlier studies of hyperon form factors using SU(3) symmetry relations and VMD (Williams et al., 1997), and it enables future experiments at TJNAF (now Jefferson Lab) using CLAS in Hall B to measure double polarization observables in kaon electroproduction. The analysis of upcoming TJNAF kaon photoproduction experiments (Schu et al.) is identified as necessary to reduce model dependency and determine the relevant resonances and coupling constants.
