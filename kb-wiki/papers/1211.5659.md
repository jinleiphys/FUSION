# First Direct Double-Beta Decay Q-value Measurement of 82Se in Support of Understanding the Nature of the Neutrino

> In anticipation of results from current and future double-beta decay studies, we report a measurement resulting in a 82Se double-beta decay Q-value of 2997.9(3) keV, an order of magnitude more precise than the currently accepted value. We also present preliminary results of a calculation of the 82Se neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element that corrects in part for the small size of the shell model single-particle space. The results of this work are important for designing next generation double-beta decay experiments and for the theoretical interpretations of their observations.

## Key claim

The paper reports a direct Penning trap measurement of the 82Se double-beta decay Q-value of 2997.9(3) keV, which is nearly an order of magnitude more precise than the previously accepted value of 2996(2) keV from the 2003 atomic mass evaluation. The authors also present a preliminary shell-model calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element that, when corrected for the limited single-particle space, increases from 2.64 to 3.56, narrowing the gap with other theoretical approaches.

## Method

The authors used the Low-Energy Beam and Ion Trap (LEBIT) Penning trap mass spectrometer at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory to measure the cyclotron frequency ratio between singly charged ions of 82Se and its double-beta decay daughter 82Kr, produced simultaneously from a plasma ion source. Cyclotron frequencies were measured using the Time-of-Flight ion-cyclotron resonance detection technique with excitation times of 500 ms and 750 ms, and the frequency ratio was determined from 110 individual ratio measurements across four runs. For the nuclear matrix element calculation, the authors used diagrammatic perturbation theory with a low-momentum interaction derived from chiral effective field theory to construct an effective two-body neutrinoless double-beta decay operator that includes contributions from single-particle levels outside the valence shell.

## Key numbers

- 82Se double-beta decay Q-value: 2997.9(3) keV, measured directly via Penning trap mass spectrometry
- Previous Q-value from AME2003: 2996(2) keV, an order of magnitude less precise
- Cyclotron frequency ratio R_LEBIT = 1.000 039 290(4)(5) for 82Kr+ to 82Se+
- Phase space factor for neutrinoless double-beta decay of 82Se: G_0nu = 2.848(1) x 10^-14 yr^-1
- Shell model nuclear matrix element (standard): 2.64; corrected shell model NME: 3.56; QRPA NME: 5.19
- Lower limit range for 82Se neutrinoless double-beta decay half-life: 5.0 x 10^24 to 3.7 x 10^25 years, derived from current upper limits of effective Majorana mass (140-380 meV from EXO-200)
- Projected sensitivity for SuperNEMO: effective neutrino mass as low as 60-85 meV could be detected at 90% confidence level

## Context

This work provides the first direct high-precision Q-value measurement for 82Se, which is the only double-beta decay candidate among those currently employed in experiments that had not previously been measured via Penning trap mass spectrometry. The improved Q-value and phase space factor are critical for the SuperNEMO experiment, which plans to observe 100-200 kg of 82Se for five years with a projected half-life sensitivity of 1-2 x 10^26 years. The corrected shell model nuclear matrix element calculation, which builds on earlier exploratory work by Engel and Hagen, represents an important step toward producing a true ab initio matrix element and helps reconcile the factor-of-two discrepancy between shell model and QRPA calculations for 82Se.
