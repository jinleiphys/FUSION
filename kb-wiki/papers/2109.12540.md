# Effects of $\bm\la$ hyperons on the deformations of even-even nuclei

> The deformations of multi-$\la$ hypernuclei corresponding to even-even core nuclei ranging from $^8$Be to $^{40}$Ca with 2, 4, 6, and 8 hyperons are studied in the framework of the deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach. It is found that the deformations are reduced when adding 2 or 8 $\la$ hyperons, but enhanced when adding 4 or 6 $\la$ hyperons. These differences are attributed to the fact that $\la$ hyperons are filled gradually into the three deformed $p$ orbits, of which the [110]1/2$^-$ orbit is prolately deformed and the degenerate [101]1/2$^-$ and [101]3/2$^-$ orbits are oblately deformed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding 2 or 8 Λ hyperons to even-even core nuclei generally reduces nuclear deformation, while adding 4 or 6 Λ hyperons enhances it; this non-monotonic behavior depends on the order in which hyperons fill the three deformed p orbits, which is itself determined by the shape (prolate or oblate) of the core nucleus.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations in axially symmetric cylindrical coordinates, using the SLy5 Skyrme force for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the NSC97f+EmpC parameterization for the ΛN and ΛΛ energy-density functionals. The optimal quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ was determined by minimizing the total energy for each hypernucleus, with pairing treated in a BCS approximation using a density-dependent δ interaction.

## Key numbers

- The average Λ binding energy ⟨BΛ⟩ for multi-Λ hypernuclei decreases with increasing hyperon number; for 8-Λ hypernuclei, ⟨BΛ⟩ is close to that of experimental single-pΛ hypernuclei.
- For p-shell nuclei, the pairing strength was set to V₀ = -410 MeV fm³ for both neutrons and protons.
- For heavier nuclei, V₀ was taken as -999 MeV fm³ for neutrons and -1146 MeV fm³ for protons.
- The energy differences between prolate and oblate local minima in Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes are smaller than 2 MeV, characterizing them as nuclei with shape-coexistence.
- The core nuclei studied range from ⁸Be to ⁴⁰Ca, with hyperon numbers n = 2, 4, 6, and 8.

## Context

This work extends the study of multi-Λ hypernuclei beyond the relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations of Tanimura (2019), which examined only 2, 4, and 8 Λ hyperons in a few isotopes (⁸Be, ²⁰Ne, ²⁸Si) and did not address the case of 6 Λ hyperons. The present SHF analysis covers a broader range of even-even nuclei from ⁸Be to ⁴⁰Ca and reveals that the deformation enhancement for 4 or 6 Λ hyperons is a general feature, attributed to the filling order of the deformed [110]1/2⁻ (prolate) and [101]1/2⁻/[101]3/2⁻ (oblate) p orbits. The results enable future experimental tests of multi-Λ hypernuclei to refine the ΛN and ΛΛ interactions used in mean-field models.
