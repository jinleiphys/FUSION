# Phase Space Description of the Leading Order Quark and Gluon Production from a Space-Time Dependent Chromofield

> We derive source terms for the production of quarks and gluons from the QCD vacuum in the presence of a space-time dependent external chromofield A_{cl} to the order of S^{(1)}. We found that the source terms for the parton production processes A_{cl} -> q\bar{q} and A_{cl},A_{cl}A_{cl} -> gg also include the annihilation processes q\bar{q} -> A_{cl} and gg -> A_{cl},A_{cl}A_{cl}. The source terms we derive are applicable for the description of the production of partons with momentum p larger rhan gA which itself must be larger than Λ_{QCD}. We observe that these source terms for the production of partons from a space-time dependent chromofield can be used to study the production and equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma during the very early stages of an ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

The paper derives source terms for quark-antiquark and gluon pair production from a space-time dependent chromofield to first order in the S-matrix expansion, but these source terms are only applicable for partons with momentum p larger than gA, which itself must be larger than Λ_QCD, meaning the results are restricted to a specific kinematic regime where the coupling is weak enough for perturbative methods to be valid.

## Method

The authors use the background field method of QCD, splitting the gauge field into a classical background chromofield A_cl and a quantum field representing produced partons. For quarks, they compute the S-matrix element for the process A_cl → q\bar{q} from the interaction Lagrangian, then extract the source term by Fourier transforming the probability and expressing it in phase-space form. For gluons, they compute contributions from diagrams with one and two couplings to the background field (A_cl → gg and A_cl, A_cl → gg), subtract ghost contributions to remove unphysical polarizations, and combine all terms to obtain a gauge-invariant source term.

## Key numbers

- The source term for quark production contains a factor g^2/(2(2π)^6 ω) multiplied by integrals over the chromofield and Bessel functions K_0 and K_1, with the Bessel function argument m√(-(x-x_2)^2)
- For the special case of a purely time-dependent field A^{a3}(t) = A_in e^{-|t|/t_0}, the quark source term becomes 16 α_S/(2π)^2 (A_in)^2 e^{2iωt} e^{-|t|/t_0} [t_0/(1+4ω^2 t_0^2)] (m_T^2/ω^2), where m_T^2 = m^2 + k_T^2
- The gluon source term for a time-dependent field contains contributions proportional to 3g^2/4, 6ig^3/(4k^0), and g^4/(64(k^0)^2) multiplying various field combinations and structure constants

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parton production from constant chromoelectric fields used in color flux-tube models (Schwinger 1951; Casher 1979) to the more realistic case of space-time dependent fields, which arise naturally when the field starts producing partons and the transport equations include acceleration, collision, and color precession terms. The derived source terms are intended for use in relativistic non-abelian transport equations (Nayak 1999; Heinz 1999) to study the production and equilibration of quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and could complement the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran 1994; Raju 1995) which describes parton production via classical radiation rather than vacuum polarization.
