# Hybrid stars with large quark cores within the parity doublet model and modified NJL model

> Using the parity doublet model (PDM) for hadronic matter and a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter, we investigate the potential existence of two- and three-flavor quark matter in neutron star cores. Both models respect chiral symmetry, and a sharp first-order phase transition is implemented via Maxwell construction. We find stable neutron stars with quark cores within a specific parameter space that satisfies current astronomical observations. Typical neutron stars with masses around $1.4 \ M_\odot$ may possess deconfined quark matter in their centers. The hybrid star scenario with a two-flavor quark core offers enough parameter space to allow the neutron stars with large quark cores exceeding $\sim 1\ M_\odot$, and allow the early deconfinement position before $2\ ρ_0$, where $ρ_0$ is the nuclear saturation density. The observations of gravitational wave event GW170817 suggest a relatively large chiral invariant mass $m_0=600\ \rm MeV$ in the PDM for scenarios involving three-flavor quark matter cores. The maximum mass of the hybrid star with a quark core is found to be approximately $2.2\ M_\odot$ for both two- or three-flavor quark matter in their centers.

# Hybrid stars with large quark cores within the parity doublet model and modified NJL model

## Key claim

Stable hybrid neutron stars with quark cores exceeding ~1 solar mass can exist within a specific parameter space that satisfies current astronomical observations, but only when the chiral invariant mass m0 in the parity doublet model is 500 or 600 MeV (not 700 MeV, which cannot support 2 solar mass stars) and when the Fierz mixing parameter α and vacuum pressure B are appropriately tuned.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid star equations of state by combining the parity doublet model (PDM) for hadronic matter with a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter, where the NJL model includes both the original interaction and its Fierz-transformed counterpart weighted by a parameter α. A sharp first-order phase transition between hadronic and quark phases was implemented via Maxwell construction, with the deconfinement transition determined by the intersection of pressure-versus-baryon-chemical-potential curves. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then solved for spherically symmetric static stars using the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust EOS, PDM for the outer core, and modified NJL for the quark core.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with quark cores: approximately 2.2 solar masses for both two-flavor and three-flavor quark matter in their centers.
- Quark cores can exceed ~1 solar mass in the two-flavor scenario.
- Early deconfinement can occur before 2 ρ0, where ρ0 is nuclear saturation density.
- Typical neutron stars with masses around 1.4 solar masses may possess deconfined quark matter in their centers.
- Gravitational wave event GW170817 observations suggest a relatively large chiral invariant mass m0 = 600 MeV in the PDM for scenarios involving three-flavor quark matter cores.
- Pure hadronic EOS with m0 = 700 MeV cannot support observed massive neutron stars with 2 solar masses.
- PDM parameters: m0 values of 500 MeV and 600 MeV were used for relatively stiff EOS.
- Modified NJL parameters for (2+1)-flavor model: mu = 3.4 MeV, ms = 104 MeV, Λ = 1330 MeV, G' = 1.51 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻², K = 2.75 × 10⁻¹⁴ MeV⁻⁵.
- Modified NJL parameters for two-flavor model: mu = 3.3 MeV, Λ = 1330 MeV, G' = 2.028 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻².
- Vacuum pressure B^(1/4) = 120 MeV was used as a representative value.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Yuan et al., 2024) on first-order phase transitions in neutron stars, where large quark cores generated by early transition had not been found. It contrasts with earlier hybrid star studies that used relativistic mean-field models for the hadronic phase (such as Schertler et al., 1999; Pagliara et al., 2016; Cierniak et al., 2022) or meta-models (Ferraro et al., 2020), by employing the parity doublet model which respects chiral symmetry. The results enable future investigations into whether the Gibbs construction with finite surface tension would yield intermediate results between the Maxwell construction (infinite surface tension) used here and the crossover scenario (zero surface tension) studied in their earlier work.
