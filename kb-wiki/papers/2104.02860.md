# Thermodynamics of pairing transition for Odd-A nuclei

> The hot nucleus $^{171}$Yb is investigated by the covariant density functional theory with the PC-PK1 effective interaction. The thermodynamic quantities are evaluated with the canonical ensemble theory. The pairing correlations is treated by the shell-model-like approach, in which the particle numbers are conserved strictly and in which the blocking effect is handled exactly. An S-shaped heat capacity versus temperature of $^{171}$Yb appears. It has been studied in terms of the blocking effect, the single-particle levels, the pairing gap, and defined seniority components, and compared to the heat capacity of $^{172}$Yb. The pairing transition from the superfluid state to the normal state can result in the S-shaped heat capacity of $^{172}$Yb where the one-pair-broken and two-pair-broken states dominate, while the single-particle level structure near the Fermi surface is associated with the S-shaped heat capacity of $^{171}$Yb. For odd-A nuclei, although the one-pair-broken and two-pair-broken states still contribute, the pairing gap and the pairing transition is relatively weak. The S-shaped heat capacity could be affected due to the blocking of the single-particle level near the Fermi surface.

## Key claim

The S-shaped heat capacity observed in the odd-A nucleus $^{171}$Yb arises not primarily from a pairing phase transition (as in even-even $^{172}$Yb) but from the single-particle level structure near the Fermi surface, with the blocking effect weakening the pairing gap and pairing transition.

## Method

The covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the PC-PK1 effective interaction was used to obtain single-particle levels for $^{171}$Yb and $^{172}$Yb. Pairing correlations were treated by the shell-model-like approach (SLAP), which conserves particle number strictly and handles the blocking effect exactly for odd-A nuclei. Thermodynamic quantities were evaluated using canonical ensemble theory from the excitation energies and level densities obtained after diagonalizing the pairing Hamiltonian in multi-particle configuration (MPC) space.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing strength $G_{\rm n} = 0.19$ MeV and proton pairing strength $G_{\rm p} = 0.22$ MeV, with energy cutoff $E_c = 30$ MeV.
- For $^{172}$Yb, the S-shaped heat capacity first inflection point occurs at $T \sim 0.17$ MeV and the second inflection point at $T \sim 0.3$ MeV; the critical temperature $0.3$ MeV is half of the zero-temperature pairing gap ($\sim 0.6$ MeV).
- For $^{171}$Yb neutrons, the first inflection point of the S-shaped heat capacity appears at $T \sim 0.3$ MeV and the second inflection point at $T \sim 0.6$ MeV; the zero-temperature pairing gap is $\sim 0.3$ MeV, about half that of $^{172}$Yb.
- The MPC space dimensions are about $2\times10^6$ (neutrons) and $6\times10^5$ (protons) for $^{171}$Yb, and $6\times10^5$ (neutrons) and $6\times10^5$ (protons) for $^{172}$Yb.
- The energy level span for neutrons around the Fermi surface is 3.5 MeV, and for protons is 5.2 MeV; 15 single-particle levels and 8 pairs of valence particles are included for both neutrons and protons.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing transition thermodynamics from even-even nuclei (e.g., $^{162}$Dy in Liu (2015)) to odd-A nuclei using CDFT+SLAP, which overcomes particle-number violation issues in finite-temperature BCS and HFB approaches (e.g., Esashika (2005), Gambacurta (2013)). The results are compared to experimental heat capacity data for $^{171,172}$Yb from Schiller (2001). The method enables microscopic analysis of seniority components and blocking effects in odd-A systems, clarifying that the S-shaped heat capacity in odd-A nuclei is governed by single-particle level structure rather than a pairing phase transition.
