# Research on the halo in $^{31}$Ne with complex momentum representation method

> Halo is one of the most interesting phenomena in exotic nuclei especially for $^{31}$Ne, which is deemed to be a halo nucleus formed by a $p-$wave resonance. However, the theoretical calculations don't suggest a $p-$wave resonance using the scattering phase shift approach or complex scaling method. Here, we apply the complex momentum representation method to explore resonances in $^{31}$Ne. We have calculated the single-particle energies for bound and resonant states together with their evolutions with deformation. The results show that the $p-$wave resonances appear clearly in the complex momentum plane accompanied with the $p-f$ inversion in the single-particle levels. As it happens the $p-f$ inversion, the calculated energy, width, and occupation probabilities of major components in the level occupied by valance neutron support a $p-$wave halo for $^{31}$Ne.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the complex momentum representation (CMR) method reveals $p$-wave resonances (specifically $2p_{3/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, and $2d_{5/2}$) in $^{31}$Ne that were missed by prior scattering phase shift and complex scaling calculations, and that these resonances support a $p$-wave halo structure for $^{31}$Ne, but only under the condition that the $p$-$f$ inversion occurs in the single-particle levels and that the valence neutron occupies either the 1/2[310] orbit (for deformation $0.20 \leq \beta_2 \leq 0.29$) or the 3/2[321] orbit (for $0.40 \leq \beta_2 \leq 0.59$).

## Method

The authors applied the complex momentum representation (CMR) method to solve the Schrodinger equation for a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit and deformation terms, using a coupled-channel expansion in momentum space. The integral equation was discretized via Gauss-Legendre quadrature on a finite momentum grid ($k_{max}=4.0$ fm$^{-1}$, $N_l=120$ grid points) and solved as a symmetric matrix eigenvalue problem, yielding bound and resonant states simultaneously. The method was validated by checking contour independence of resonant states in the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

* The one-neutron separation energy of $^{31}$Ne is $S_n \simeq 0.33$ MeV (from Audi 2003).
* The deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.2$ was used for calculations of the 1/2[310] resonant state, and $\beta_2 = 0.5$ for the 3/2[321] weakly bound state.
* The occupation probability of the $p_{3/2}$ component in the 1/2[310] level is over 64% over the range of deformation considered.
* The occupation probability of the $p_{3/2}$ component in the 3/2[321] level is over 44% for $\beta_2 \geq 0.40$.
* The momentum integration grid used $k_{max}=4.0$ fm$^{-1}$ with $N_l=120$ Gauss-Legendre quadrature points and $N_c=8$ coupled channels.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $^{31}$Ne halo structure that used the scattering phase shift approach (Hamamoto 2010) and complex scaling method (Liu 2012), both of which failed to identify the $2p_{3/2}$ resonance. The CMR method successfully obtains broad resonances ($2p_{3/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, $2d_{5/2}$) that were missed previously, revealing the $p$-$f$ inversion in single-particle levels. The results support earlier conclusions from reaction cross section analyses (Nakamura 2009; Takechi 2012; Minomo 2011, 2012; Sumi 2012) and particle-rotor model calculations (Urata 2011, 2012) that $^{31}$Ne has a $p$-wave halo with spin parity $3/2^-$, and provide specific Nilsson orbits (1/2[310] and 3/2[321]) that could host the valence neutron. The calculated radial-momentum probability distributions for these two orbits offer predictions for knockout reaction experiments to distinguish between them.
