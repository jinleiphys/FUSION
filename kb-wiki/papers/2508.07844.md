# Thermo-coalescence model for Light Nuclei production in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We employ a hybrid approach to describe the light nuclei production mechanism where the nucleons are assumed to be thermally produced, and are allowed to form light nuclei using a coalescence prescription. In this approach, we first fit transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) distribution of nucleons using hydro-inspired boost-invariant blast-wave model. The extracted parameters are then used to describe the deuteron $p_{T}$ spectra, along with two additional parameters that characterize the coalescence prescription employed in this study. We refer this combined approach as ``thermo-coalescence model'' and it is designed to study the deuteron production and describe the experimental measurements. In this work, we analyze the measured $p_{T}$ distribution of protons and deuterons from Pb-Pb collisions at the ALICE Collaboration at LHC. We also evaluate the $p_{T}$-integrated deuteron yields using this approach and compare with experimental measurements. A Bayesian inference framework is employed to determine the best-fit parameters of the thermo-coalescence model. Finally, we estimate the traditionally used experimental coalescence parameter ($B_{A}$) within our framework in order to establish a connection between our model and the conventional coalescence approach commonly used to relate experimental data with theoretical descriptions of light nuclei production.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a "thermo-coalescence model" that combines a thermal blast-wave description of nucleons with a coalescence prescription for deuteron formation, claiming that this hybrid approach can simultaneously describe proton and deuteron transverse momentum spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, but only when the blast-wave parameters are first fixed from proton data and then the coalescence parameters are allowed to vary within narrow ranges around those values.

## Method

The authors first fit proton transverse momentum spectra using a boost-invariant blast-wave model with five free parameters (kinetic freeze-out temperature, maximum transverse flow velocity, exponent of the velocity profile, freeze-out radius, and freeze-out time). They then use these extracted parameters to compute deuteron spectra via a two-nucleon coalescence formalism based on Wigner functions, introducing two additional parameters (alpha controlling the deuteron wave function shape and C_D for overall yield normalization). A Bayesian inference framework with Gaussian Process Regression emulators is employed to determine best-fit parameters and their uncertainties for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV across multiple centrality classes.

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to unify thermal and coalescence approaches for light nuclei production, specifically building on the coalescence formalism of Fries (2008) and the blast-wave model of Schnedermann (1993). It differs from prior studies by Zhu (2017) and Wang (2023, 2024) by treating all five blast-wave parameters as free in a Bayesian fit without assuming space-momentum correlations in the nucleon phase-space distribution. The model addresses the "snowballs in hell" paradox raised by Braun-Munzinger (1995) and Mrowczynski (2020), where loosely bound deuterons appear to form at temperatures around 156-160 MeV that far exceed their 2.2 MeV binding energy.
