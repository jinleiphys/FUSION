# Exploring the decay probability of neutron-rich superheavy nuclei

> The modes of decay for the even-even isotopes of superheavy nuclei of Z = 118 and 120 with neutron number $160 \leq N \leq 204$ are investigated in the framework of the axially deformed relativistic mean field model. The asymmetry parameter $η$ and the relative neutron-proton asymmetry of the surface to the center ($R_η$) are estimated for the ground state density distributions of the nuclei. We suggest that the resulting asymmetry parameter $η$ and the relative neutron-proton asymmetry $R_η$ of the density play a crucial role in the preformation factor of the decay half life.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that the relative neutron-proton asymmetry at the surface compared to the center of the nucleus, quantified by the parameter \(R_{\eta}\), plays a crucial role in the preformation factor of decay half-lives for neutron-rich superheavy nuclei, and that extreme neutron richness at the surface could make \(\beta^-\)-decay a predominant mode instead of \(\alpha\)-decay or fission.

## Method

The axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the NL3* interaction parameter set was used to calculate ground state properties of even-even isotopes of Z = 118 and 120 with neutron numbers \(160 \leq N \leq 204\). Pairing correlations were treated using a constant strength scheme with BCS equations. The asymmetry parameter \(\eta = (\rho_n - \rho_p)/(\rho_n + \rho_p)\) was computed from the proton and neutron density distributions, and the relative neutron-proton asymmetry \(R_{\eta} = \eta_s/\eta_c\) (ratio of surface to center asymmetries) was estimated for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for \(^{278-322}\)118 range from 1966.5 MeV (\(^{278}\)118) to 2233.7 MeV (\(^{322}\)118).
- Binding energies for \(^{280-324}\)120 range from 1962.54 MeV (\(^{280}\)120) to 2239.09 MeV (\(^{324}\)120).
- The quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) for Z=118 isotopes ranges from 0.258 (\(^{278}\)118) to 0.776 (\(^{316}\)118), with most values indicating prolate deformation.
- The relative neutron-proton asymmetry \(R_{\eta}\) for Z=118 increases from 2.51 (\(^{278}\)118) to 5.13 (\(^{322}\)118).
- The relative neutron-proton asymmetry \(R_{\eta}\) for Z=120 increases from 2.51 (\(^{280}\)120) to 4.63 (\(^{324}\)120).
- The central region (0-5 fm) has \(\eta \sim 0.2\), which changes to \(\sim 0.8\) at the surface region (5-8 fm).
- Maximum total density is \(\rho \sim 0.18\) fm\(^{-3}\) and minimum is \(\rho \sim 0.001\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of superheavy nuclei using the RMF model, which has been applied successfully to both \(\beta\)-stable and \(\beta\)-unstable regions including superheavy nuclei (Bhuyan 2012, 2015). The study addresses the question of where the island of stability is centered, with the magic proton number predicted differently by various approaches: macro-microscopic calculations (Meldner 1966, Nilsson 1969) predict Z=114, while fully microscopic approaches predict Z=120 (Beiner 1974, Bhuyan 2012) or Z=126 (Cwiok 1996), with N=184 as a nearly universal neutron magic number. The results suggest that \(\beta^-\)-decay could become a predominant decay mode for neutron-rich superheavy nuclei, which may inform future experimental synthesis efforts and studies of the r-process of nucleosynthesis.
