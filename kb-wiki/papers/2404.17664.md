# The properties of $ϕ(2170)$ and its three-body nature

> We summarize the results we obtained for the partial decay widths of $ϕ(2170)$ into two-body final states formed by a $\bar K$ and a Kaonic resonance, like $K(1460)$, $K_1(1270)$, as well as to final states constituted by a $ϕ$ and an $η/η^\prime$ mesons. The results obtained are compared with the values extracted from experimental data on the corresponding branching ratios, which were determined by the BESIII collaboration. A reasonable agreement is found, which together with the previous reproduction of the mass, width, and cross section for the process $e^+ e^-\to ϕf_0$ strongly indicates the molecular nature of $ϕ(2170)$ as a $ϕK\bar K$ system.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\phi(2170)$ meson is likely a molecular state (a $\phi K\bar K$ three-body system) rather than a conventional $s\bar s$, hybrid, or tetraquark state, based on the reasonable agreement between calculated and experimental branching ratios for its decays into $\bar K$ plus a kaonic resonance or $\phi$ plus $\eta/\eta^\prime$, but the agreement depends strongly on which model is used for the kaonic resonances $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$, and the experimental uncertainties are large.

## Method

The authors calculate partial decay widths of $\phi(2170)$ into $\bar K K_R$ (with $K_R = K(1460), K_1(1270), K_1(1400)$) and $\phi\eta/\phi\eta^\prime$ using a model where $\phi(2170)$ is a $\phi f_0(980)$ bound state generated from Faddeev equations for the $\phi K\bar K$ system. The decay amplitudes involve triangular loop integrals with vertices described by coupling constants obtained from previous chiral and coupled-channel studies; the tensor integrals are evaluated analytically in the rest frame of $\phi(2170)$ using Cauchy's theorem, with form factors (Lorentz or Gaussian) and cutoffs around 1000 MeV to regularize the three-momentum integration. For $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$, three different models (A, B, C) are compared, and for the $\eta-\eta^\prime$ mixing, mixing angles between $-15^\circ$ and $-22^\circ$ are tested.

## Key numbers

- Ratio $B_1 \equiv \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+(1460)K^-) / \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1400)K^-)$: Model B gives $0.62 \pm 0.20$, Model C gives $0.11 \pm 0.04$; experiment gives $0.64 \pm 0.92$ (Solution 1) and $0.03 \pm 0.04$ (Solution 2).
- Ratio $B_2 \equiv \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+(1460)K^-) / \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1270)K^-)$: Model A (both poles) gives $1.3 \pm 0.4$, Model B gives $16 \pm 6$, Model C (Solution $\mathbb{S}_1$) gives $1.2 \pm 0.4$; experiment gives $0.40 \pm 0.54$ (Solution 1) and $0.02 \pm 0.03$ (Solution 2).
- Ratio $B_3 \equiv \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1270)K^-) / \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1400)K^-)$: Model B gives $0.04 \pm 0.01$, Model C (Solution $\mathbb{S}_3$) gives $2.40 \pm 0.40$; experiment gives $1.62 \pm 1.38$ (Solution 1) and $1.55 \pm 0.19$ (Solution 2).
- Ratio $R_{\eta/\eta^\prime} \equiv \Gamma(\phi_R \to \phi\eta) / \Gamma(\phi_R \to \phi\eta^\prime)$: for mixing angle $\beta = -22^\circ$ with model LII, $2.80 \pm 0.86$; experiment gives $0.034^{+0.018}_{-0.011}$ (Solution I) and $1.42^{+0.58}_{-0.48}$ (Solution II) from BESIII (2021, 2020), and $0.013 \pm 0.007$ (Solution I) and $2.4 \pm 0.4$ (Solutions III, IV) from Belle (2022).
- The experimental products $\mathcal{B}r[\phi_R \to K^+(1460)K^-] \Gamma^{e^+e^-}_R = 3.0 \pm 3.8$ eV, and for $K^+_1(1400)K^-$: $4.7 \pm 3.3$ eV (Solution 1) or $98.8 \pm 7.8$ eV (Solution 2), from BESIII (2020).

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of $\phi(2170)$ first proposed by Martinez Torres et al. (2008), where Faddeev equations for the $\phi K\bar K$ system generated a state with mass and width matching $\phi(2170)$ and reproduced the $e^+e^- \to \phi f_0(980)$ cross section from BaBar. The present paper tests this model against recent BESIII data (2020, 2021) on branching ratios to $\bar K K_R$ and $\phi\eta/\phi\eta^\prime$, which had been found inconsistent with $s\bar s$, hybrid, or tetraquark descriptions. The results enable future discrimination between molecular and alternative models for $\phi(2170)$, and also probe the nature of the kaonic resonances $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$, specifically, whether $K_1(1270)$ is a molecular state from pseudoscalar-vector dynamics as in Geng et al. (2006) or a conventional $K_{1A}$-$K_{1B}$ mixing state as in Palomar et al. (2003).
