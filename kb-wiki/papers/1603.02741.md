# Baryon Octet Electromagnetic Form Factors in a confining NJL model

> Electromagnetic form factors of the baryon octet are studied using a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model which utilizes the proper-time regularization scheme to simulate aspects of colour confinement. In addition, the model also incorporates corrections to the dressed quarks from vector meson correlations in the t-channel and the pion cloud. Comparison with recent chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results shows a remarkable level of consistency. For the charge radii we find the surprising result $r_{E}^p < r_{E}^{Σ^+}$ and $|r_{E}^n| < |r_{E}^{Ξ^0}|$, whereas the magnetic radii have a pattern largely consistent with a naive expectation based on the dressed quark masses.

## Key claim

The NJL model with proper-time regularization, vector meson dressing, and pion cloud corrections reproduces the chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results for baryon octet electromagnetic form factors with remarkable consistency, but the authors note that systematic errors at the level of 10% may remain from lattice artifacts and the chiral extrapolation technique.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equation in the static approximation for each baryon octet member using an SU(3) NJL model with proper-time regularization to simulate confinement. The quark-photon vertices are dressed by including vector meson correlations in the t-channel via the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, and pion loop corrections are added for light quarks. The model parameters are fit to reproduce the pion decay constant, pion and rho masses, and the physical Delta++ and nucleon masses.

## Context

This work extends the NJL model framework developed by Cloet et al. (2014) for nucleon electromagnetic form factors to the full baryon octet, and builds on earlier NJL model calculations of axial charges in baryon octet beta decays by Carrillo-Serrano et al. (2014). The results are compared with chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD simulations by Shanahan et al. (2014) on two different lattice volumes, and the model predictions for charge radii show the surprising ordering r_E^p less than r_E^{Sigma+} and |r_E^n| less than |r_E^{Xi0}|, which differs from naive expectations based on dressed quark masses.
