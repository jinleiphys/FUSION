# Response Function of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The charge longitudinal response function is examined in the framework of the random-phase approximation in an isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter where proton and neutron densities are different. This asymmetry changes the response through both the particle-hole interaction and the free particle-hole polarization propagator. We discuss these two effects on the response function on the basis of our numerical results in detail.

## Key claim

The charge longitudinal response function of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter is modified by two competing effects: the change in the effective particle-hole interaction and the change in the free particle-hole polarization propagator; these two effects are opposite in sign and comparable in magnitude, so both must be accounted for simultaneously to correctly describe nuclei with neutron excess.

## Method

The authors calculated the charge longitudinal response function within the random-phase approximation (RPA) for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, using the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the Reid soft core potential to obtain the effective interaction (g-matrix). They expanded both the effective interaction and the Lindhard function in powers of the asymmetry parameter epsilon, and separately evaluated the contributions from the asymmetry in the interaction and in the particle-hole propagator. Numerical calculations were performed at momentum transfer q = 2.0 fm^-1 using proton and neutron Fermi momenta corresponding to the asymmetry of 48Ca.

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry parameter epsilon = -0.213, corresponding to proton Fermi momentum k_F^p = 1.20 fm^-1 and neutron Fermi momentum k_F^n = 1.39 fm^-1 (matching the average asymmetry in 48Ca).
- The asymmetry in the interaction (R_V) yields a 6% reduction of the Coulomb sum rule compared to symmetric nuclear matter.
- The asymmetry in the Lindhard function (R_Pi) yields a 3% enhancement of the Coulomb sum rule compared to symmetric nuclear matter.
- The full response (R) yields a 3% reduction of the Coulomb sum rule compared to symmetric nuclear matter.
- Momentum transfer used in calculations: q = 2.0 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear response functions in asymmetric matter by Stringari and Lipparini (1987) and Alberico et al. (1987, 1989), which considered only the different phase space available for protons and neutrons. The authors incorporate the additional effect of the asymmetry on the effective particle-hole interaction, building on their own previous work (Cheon and Takayanagi, 1992). The results enable more accurate modeling of quasielastic electron scattering from medium and heavy nuclei with neutron excess, such as those ranging from 40Ca to 208Pb, and clarify that future studies of N not equal to Z nuclei must treat both asymmetry effects simultaneously.
