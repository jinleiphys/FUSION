# Strange Asymmetries in the Nucleon Sea

> Relativistic corrections to the strange axial form factor are evaluated within a light-cone formalism, taking into account effects due to the breaking of Lorentz covariance associated with the use of one-body currents. Similar corrections are known to also be needed for the magnetic form factor, and we discuss the importance of these for recent data on strange electromagnetic form factors from the HAPPEX Collaboration at Jefferson Lab. The strange vector form factors, the strange axial charge, and the asymmetries in strange quark distributions are shown to be consistently correlated within the chiral cloud model.

## Key claim

Relativistic corrections to the strange axial form factor, arising from Lorentz covariance violation when using one-body currents in light-cone models, are an order of magnitude larger than the uncorrected result and bring the chiral cloud model prediction into agreement with empirical data; the strange vector form factors, strange axial charge, and strange-antistrange asymmetries are consistently correlated within the chiral cloud model only when soft kaon-hyperon-nucleon form factors are used.

## Method

The authors formulated the strange axial current on the light-cone using the covariant approach of Karmanov et al., which introduces an explicit dependence on the light-cone orientation vector n^mu and generates an unphysical form factor b_1^S that must be subtracted. They evaluated the strange axial charge g_A^S and the uncorrected charge tilde-g_A^S within a chiral cloud model where strangeness resides in kaon-hyperon (K Lambda) components of the nucleon wave function, using a monopole form factor with cut-off mass Lambda to parameterise the KNY vertex. The results were compared with data from the HAPPEX and SAMPLE Collaborations on strange electromagnetic form factors and with CCFR data on the strange-antistrange asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- HAPPEX Collaboration result: G_E^S + r G_M^S = 0.023 ± 0.048 at Q^2 = 0.48 GeV^2, with r ≈ 0.4
- SAMPLE Collaboration result: G_M^S = +0.23 ± 0.44 at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2
- Deep-inelastic scattering world average: Delta s = -0.10 ± 0.04 at scale 10 GeV^2 in the MSbar scheme
- The chiral cloud model with Lambda = 1 GeV gives an average kaon multiplicity in the nucleon of approximately 6%
- Varying the dipole axial form factor mass M_A between 1.086 ± 0.015 GeV and 1.012 ± 0.032 GeV yields g_A^S between 0 and -0.21 ± 0.10

## Context

This work extends the light-cone chiral cloud model analysis of Melnitchouk and Malheiro (1997) from strange magnetic form factors to the strange axial charge, applying the Lorentz covariance restoration prescription of Karmanov and Mathiot (1996). The results are consistent with the SAMPLE measurement (Mueller et al., 1997) and the HAPPEX data (Aniol et al., 1998), and with the strange-antistrange asymmetry measured by the CCFR Collaboration (Bazarko et al., 1995). The paper enables a unified description of strangeness observables across elastic and deep-inelastic experiments, and identifies the need for future Jefferson Lab measurements of G_E,M^S over a range of Q^2 to further clarify the role of non-perturbative strangeness.
