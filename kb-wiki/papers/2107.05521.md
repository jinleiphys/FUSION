# Polarization effects at finite temperature and magnetic field

> We study the screening of four-quark interaction by the ring diagram in an effective chiral quark model. This entails a medium dependent coupling which naturally reduces the chiral transition temperature in a class of models, and is capable of generating an inverse magnetic catalysis at finite temperature. These results are the first coherent description of inverse magnetic catalysis, anchored to a reliable field-theoretical basis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that screening of the four-quark interaction by the ring diagram provides a natural mechanism to reduce the chiral transition temperature in effective chiral quark models and can generate inverse magnetic catalysis at finite temperature, but the numerical demonstration uses an approximation where the screened potential is evaluated at a fixed average quark mass rather than solved fully self-consistently.

## Method

The authors start from an effective chiral quark model with an instantaneous, color-diagonal interaction potential and derive the gap equation for dynamical quarks. They implement in-medium dressing of the four-quark coupling by the static, finite-temperature part of the polarization tensor (electric mass) computed from the ring diagram, and extend the model to include coupling to the Polyakov loop via a statistical confinement scheme. The electric mass is evaluated both at zero and finite magnetic field, with the magnetic field case including contributions from Landau levels.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition temperature in standard PNJL models is approximately 220 MeV, compared to the lattice QCD result of approximately 156.5 MeV.
- The model parameters used are: cutoff Lambda = 1.076 GeV, G_NJL * Lambda^2 = 4.232, current quark mass m = 5 MeV.
- In vacuum, the model gives: pion decay constant f_pi = 92.9 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 137.8 MeV, chiral condensate per flavor = -(250 MeV)^3.
- The electric mass in the massless limit at high temperature (M -> 0) is approximately (1/2) * N_f * T^2/3.
- For the lowest Landau level at high temperature with massless quarks, the electric mass per flavor is (1/2) * |e_f| * B / (2 * pi^2).
- The electric mass is evaluated at fixed quark mass M = 0.18 GeV for the 2-flavor case.

## Context

This work extends the approach of screening by ring diagrams, previously applied in condensed matter physics (Gell-Mann and Brueckner, 1957) and in QCD (Kapusta, 2006), to effective chiral quark models. It addresses the known problem that PNJL models overestimate the chiral transition temperature, which is typically fixed by ad hoc rescaling of the deconfinement temperature parameter (as done by shift1, shift2, mediumG1). The results provide a theoretical justification for introducing a B-dependent coupling, as motivated in mediumG1 and mediumG2, and enable future work incorporating momentum-dependent dressing functions, dynamical gluons, and lattice computations of the gluon Debye mass at low temperature with finite magnetic field.
