# Probing the mass effect of heavy quark jets in high-energy nuclear collisions

> The production of heavy quark (HQ) jets provides a new arena to address the mass effect of jet quenching in heavy-ion physics. This paper presents a theoretical study of HQ jet yield suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and focuses on the energy loss of HQ jets produced by different mechanisms. The p+p baseline is carried out by the SHERPA generator, and the jet-medium interactions are described by the SHELL transport model, which considers the elastic and inelastic partonic energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). In p+p collisions, our numerical results indicate that the HQ jets from gluon splitting ($g \rightarrow Q$-jet) give the dominant contribution at high $p_T$, and it shows more dispersive structures than the HQ-initiated one ($Q \rightarrow Q$-jet). In nucleus-nucleus collisions, our calculations are consistent with the inclusive and b-jet $R_{AA}$ recently measured by the ATLAS collaboration, which suggests a remarkable manifestation of the mass effect of jet energy loss. As a result of the dispersive substructure, the $g \rightarrow Q$-jet will lose more energy than the $Q \rightarrow Q$-jet in the QGP. Due to the significant contribution of $g \rightarrow c$-jet, the $R_{AA}$ of c-jet will be comparable or even smaller than that of inclusive jet. To experimentally distinguish the $g \rightarrow Q$-jet and $Q \rightarrow Q$-jet, we propose the event selection strategies based on their topological features and test the performances. By isolating the $c \rightarrow c$-jet and $b \rightarrow b$-jet, the jets initiated by heavy quarks, we predict that the order of their $R_{AA}$ are in line with the mass hierarchy of energy loss. Future measurements on the $R_{AA}$ of $Q \rightarrow Q$-jet and $g \rightarrow Q$-jet will provide a unique chance to test the flavor/mass dependence of energy loss at the jet level.

# Probing the mass effect of heavy quark jets in high-energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of charm jets can be comparable to or even smaller than that of inclusive jets at high $p_T$, contrary to the naive expectation that heavier quarks lose less energy; this arises because a large fraction of charm jets come from gluon splitting ($g \rightarrow c$-jet), which has a more dispersive substructure and loses more energy in the quark-gluon plasma than charm-initiated jets ($c \rightarrow c$-jet).

## Method

The authors use the SHERPA event generator at NLO+PS to compute the p+p baseline for heavy quark jet production, and the SHELL transport model based on modified Langevin equations with both collisional and radiative energy loss to simulate jet-medium interactions in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV. The jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ is fixed by a $\chi^2$ fit to ATLAS inclusive-jet and b-jet $R_{AA}$ data, yielding $q_0 = 0.9$ GeV$^2$/fm with $\chi^2 = 1.29$. The authors categorize heavy quark jets into $Q \rightarrow Q$-jet (heavy quark initiated) and $g \rightarrow Q$-jet (gluon splitting) and propose event selection strategies based on the number of heavy quarks inside the jet cone and the azimuthal separation of jet pairs.

## Key numbers

* The $\chi^2$ fit of ATLAS inclusive-jet and b-jet $R_{AA}$ data gives the optimal value $q_0 = 0.9$ GeV$^2$/fm with $\chi^2 = 1.29$.
* In p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV, the fractional contribution of gluon splitting ($g \rightarrow Q$-jet) increases with jet $p_T$ and becomes the dominant mechanism for both c-jet and b-jet production in the range $12 < p_T < 400$ GeV.
* For b-jet at lower $p_T$, the flavor excitation mechanism contributes above 50% while gluon splitting contributes less than 20%.
* The purity of the selected $g \rightarrow Q$-jet sample is above 0.9 for both c-jet and b-jet; the purity of the selected $Q \rightarrow Q$-jet sample is above 0.8 for c-jet and 0.9 for b-jet.
* For jet cone size R = 0.4, the c-jet $R_{AA}$ becomes smaller than that of inclusive jet at $p_T > 150$ GeV in central $0-10\%$ Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching from single heavy-flavor hadrons (ALICE, CMS, ATLAS, 2015-2021) to heavy quark jets, addressing the mass effect at the jet level. It builds on the SHELL transport model previously applied to light- and heavy-flavor jets (Dai 2018, Wang 2019-2024, Li 2022-2024) and uses the higher-twist approach for inelastic energy loss (Guo 2000, Zhang 2003, Majumder 2009). The results are consistent with ATLAS (2022) measurements of inclusive and b-jet $R_{AA}$ and with similar findings from the LIDO model (Ke 2020). The proposed selection strategies to isolate $Q \rightarrow Q$-jet and $g \rightarrow Q$-jet enable future experimental tests of flavor/mass dependence of energy loss at the jet level, which has been challenging due to the mixture of production mechanisms in heavy quark jet samples.
