# Influence of the single-particle structure on the nuclear surface and the neutron skin

> We analyze the influence of the single-particle structure on the neutron density distribution and the neutron skin in Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes. The nucleon density distributions are calculated in the Hartree-Fock+BCS approach with the SLy4 Skyrme force. A close correlation is found between the quantum numbers of the valence neutrons and the changes in the position and the diffuseness of the nuclear surface, which in turn affect the neutron skin thickness. Neutrons in the valence orbitals with low principal quantum number and high angular momentum mainly displace the position of the neutron surface outwards, while neutrons with high principal quantum number and low angular momentum basically increase the diffuseness of the neutron surface. The impact of the valence shell neutrons on the tail of the neutron density distribution is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quantum numbers (principal quantum number n and orbital angular momentum l) of valence neutrons directly determine whether the neutron skin thickness grows primarily through outward displacement of the neutron surface (bulk contribution) or through increased surface diffuseness (surface contribution), with the specific correlation being that low-n–high-l orbitals displace the surface outward while high-n–low-l orbitals increase the diffuseness.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme force for even-even isotopes of Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb. They fitted the resulting neutron and proton density profiles to two-parameter Fermi distributions to extract half-density radii and surface diffuseness parameters, then decomposed the neutron skin thickness into bulk and surface contributions using the equivalent sharp radius formalism developed in their prior work (Warda 2010, Centelles 2010).

## Key numbers

- The neutron surface diffuseness a_n for Sn isotopes takes values 0.526 fm for 132Sn (magic N=82), 0.815 fm for 154Sn (mid-shell), and 0.720 fm for 176Sn (near drip line)
- The 3p orbitals in Sn isotopes have mean square radii of 65–90 fm2 when they begin to be populated at N=90, compared to 30–50 fm2 for most other valence orbitals
- The two-neutron drip lines found are 68Ca, 78Ni, 122Zr, 176Sn, and 266Pb
- Pairing strengths used were V0n=300 MeV and V0p=360 MeV with a 1 ħω pairing window

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nuclear charge density evolution (Scattering 2008, Scattering 2013) and their earlier decomposition of neutron skin into bulk and surface parts (Warda 2010, Centelles 2010). It provides a microscopic explanation for the non-linear behavior of neutron skin thickness with neutron excess that had been observed in mean-field calculations, where local minima appear at magic numbers and maxima at mid-shell nuclei. The results enable more precise predictions of neutron density distributions for future parity-violating electron scattering experiments and for constraining the symmetry energy equation of state.
