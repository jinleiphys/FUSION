# Neutron-Neutron Fusion

> The neutron-neutron fusion process, $nn\to deν$, at very low neutron energies is studied in the framework of pionless effective field theory that incorporates dibaryon fields. The cross section and electron energy spectrum for this process are calculated up to next-to-leading order. We include the radiative corrections of ${\cal O}(α)$ calculated for the one-body transition amplitude. The precision of our theoretical estimates is found to be governed essentially by the accuracy with which the empirical values of the neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range are currently known. Also discussed is the precision of theoretical estimates of the transition rates of related electroweak processes in few-nucleon systems.

## Key claim

The cross section for neutron-neutron fusion ($nn\to de\nu$) at very low energies can be calculated with about 4% accuracy using pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields, but the precision is dominated by the existing uncertainties in the measured values of the neutron-neutron scattering length $a_0^{nn}$ and effective range $r_0^{nn}$, not by the theoretical framework itself.

## Method

The authors employed pionless effective field theory (dEFT) that incorporates explicit dibaryon fields for the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ channels, calculating the $nn\to de\nu$ amplitude up to next-to-leading order (NLO). They included ${\cal O}(\alpha)$ radiative corrections for the one-body transition amplitude and fixed the two low-energy constants $e_V^R$ (from neutron beta-decay data) and $l_{1A}$ (using results from EFT* calculations of tritium beta-decay). The cross section and electron energy spectrum were computed for ultra-cold neutrons with relative velocity $v\sim 10$ m/s in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for $nn$-fusion at $v\sim 10$ m/s: $\sigma = (38.6 \pm 1.5) \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$
- Cross section integrated over electron energies above the neutron beta-decay endpoint ($E_e > \delta_N = 1.29$ MeV): $\sigma_{cut} = (30.2 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-40}$ cm$^2$
- Maximum electron energy: $E_e^{max} \simeq 3.52$ MeV (deuteron binding energy plus neutron-proton mass difference)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: $a_0^{nn} = -18.5 \pm 0.4$ fm
- Neutron-neutron effective range: $r_0^{nn} = 2.80 \pm 0.11$ fm
- Low-energy constant $l_{1A} = -0.33 \pm 0.03$
- Radiative correction parameter: $\frac{\alpha}{2\pi}e_V^R = (2.01 \pm 0.40) \times 10^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT), previously used for $np\to d\gamma$ capture by Ando and Hyun (2004), to the weak-interaction process $nn\to de\nu$. The $nn$-fusion reaction is closely related to the $pp$-fusion process ($pp\to de^+\nu$) and neutrino-deuteron reactions studied by Park et al. (2003) and Ando et al. (2003) using the EFT* hybrid approach. The same formalism can be applied with essentially no change to high-precision calculations of $pp$-fusion and $\nu d$ cross sections, which are important for astrophysics and neutrino physics.
