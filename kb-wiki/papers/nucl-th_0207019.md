# The Axial-Vector Current in Nuclear Many-Body Physics

> Weak-interaction currents are studied in a recently proposed effective field theory of the nuclear many-body problem. The Lorentz-invariant effective field theory contains nucleons, pions, isoscalar scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) fields, and isovector vector ($ρ$) fields. The theory exhibits a nonlinear realization of $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ chiral symmetry and has three desirable features: it uses the same degrees of freedom to describe the axial-vector current and the strong-interaction dynamics, it satisfies the symmetries of the underlying theory of quantum chromodynamics, and its parameters can be calibrated using strong-interaction phenomena, like hadron scattering or the empirical properties of finite nuclei. Moreover, it has recently been verified that for normal nuclear systems, it is possible to systematically expand the effective lagrangian in powers of the meson fields (and their derivatives) and to reliably truncate the expansion after the first few orders. Here it is shown that the expressions for the axial-vector current, evaluated through the first few orders in the field expansion, satisfy both PCAC and the Goldberger--Treiman relation, and it is verified that the corresponding vector and axial-vector charges satisfy the familiar chiral charge algebra. Explicit results are derived for the Lorentz-covariant, axial-vector, two-nucleon amplitudes, from which axial-vector meson-exchange currents can be deduced.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory (EFT) of quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) with a nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry can simultaneously satisfy the partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC) condition, the Goldberger–Treiman relation, and the chiral charge algebra, overcoming limitations of earlier linear-sigma-model approaches where these constraints could not be simultaneously met.

## Method

The authors start from a Lorentz-invariant effective lagrangian containing nucleons, pions, and isoscalar scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) fields plus isovector vector (rho) fields, organized by naive dimensional analysis (NDA) and truncated at terms with index nu <= 4. They derive the axial-vector current via Noether's theorem for terms with one derivative of the pion field (nu = 2) and for terms with bilinear derivatives of the pion field (nu = 3), then compute one- and two-body matrix elements, the pion-production amplitude on a single nucleon, and verify PCAC and the Goldberger–Treiman relation explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f_pi appears in the definition of the chiral field U = exp(i tau * pi / f_pi) and in the pionic lagrangian terms.
- The axial coupling constant g_A enters as a free parameter in the lagrangian, allowing the Goldberger–Treiman relation to be satisfied at tree level without field rescaling.
- The sigma meson mass is roughly 500 MeV, used to simulate mid-range NN attraction from correlated two-pion exchange.
- The rho meson mass m_rho appears in the rho-pion coupling term proportional to g_{rho pi pi} * (2 f_pi^2 / m_rho^2).
- The expansion index nu = d + n/2 + b, where d is the number of derivatives, n the number of nucleon fields, and b the number of non-Goldstone boson fields; the lagrangian is truncated at nu <= 4 for normal nuclear densities.

## Context

This work extends the QHD effective field theory framework developed by Furnstahl, Serot, and collaborators (1997) to include axial-vector currents, building on earlier attempts by Ananyan (1998) using a linear sigma model that could not simultaneously satisfy PCAC, the Goldberger–Treiman relation, and the chiral charge algebra. The approach contrasts with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) models by Rho, Park, and others (1991-1996) that use only pions and nucleons, and with hard-pion models that require the a_1 meson as a chiral partner of the rho. The results enable calculation of axial-vector meson-exchange currents (AXC) in nuclear many-body systems using parameters calibrated to strong-interaction phenomena.
