# Surface tension of hot and dense quark matter under strong magnetic fields

> We study the surface tension of hot, highly magnetized three flavor quark matter droplets, focusing specifically on the thermodynamic conditions prevailing in neutron stars, hot lepton rich protoneutron stars and neutron star mergers. We explore the role of temperature, baryon number density, trapped neutrinos, droplet size and magnetic fields within the multiple reflection expansion formalism (MRE), assuming that astrophysical quark matter can be described as a mixture of free Fermi gases composed by quarks $u$, $d$, $s$, electrons and neutrinos, in chemical equilibrium under weak interactions. We find that the total surface tension is rather unaffected by the size of the drop, but is quite sensitive to the effect of baryon number density, temperature, trapped neutrinos and magnetic fields (specially above $eB \sim 5 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{GeV}^2$). Surface tensions parallel and transverse to the magnetic field span values up to $\sim$ 25 MeV/fm$^2$. For $T \lesssim 100$ MeV the surface tension is a decreasing function of temperature but above 100 MeV it increases monotonically with $T$. Finally, we discuss some astrophysical consequences of our results.

## Key claim

The surface tension of hot, magnetized quark matter is sensitive to baryon number density, temperature, trapped neutrinos, and strong magnetic fields (especially above \(eB \sim 5 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{GeV}^2\)), but is rather unaffected by droplet size; the abstract's claim that surface tension decreases with temperature below 100 MeV and increases above 100 MeV holds only for fixed baryon number density and neutrino chemical potential.

## Method

The authors employ the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) formalism to compute the surface tension of finite-size quark matter droplets in a strong magnetic field, treating quark matter as a mixture of free Fermi gases of \(u\), \(d\), \(s\) quarks, electrons, and neutrinos in chemical equilibrium under weak interactions. They impose local charge neutrality and consider three astrophysical scenarios: cold deleptonized neutron stars (\(T=1\) MeV, \(\mu_{\nu_e}=0\)), hot lepton-rich protoneutron stars (\(T=30\) MeV, \(\mu_{\nu_e}=100\) MeV), and binary neutron star mergers (\(T=100\) MeV, \(\mu_{\nu_e}=200\) MeV). The surface tension is computed separately for directions parallel and transverse to the magnetic field, with magnetic field strengths \(eB_{low}=5\times10^{-3}\) GeV\(^2\) and \(eB_{high}=5\times10^{-2}\) GeV\(^2\), and droplet sizes parameterized by \(V/S = 10\) fm, 100 fm, and \(\infty\).

## Key numbers

- Total surface tension in parallel and transverse directions spans values up to \(\sim 25\) MeV/fm\(^2\) for baryon number densities around ten times nuclear saturation density.
- For baryon number densities around nuclear saturation density \(n_0\), surface tension is of the order of a few MeV/fm\(^2\).
- The contribution of \(u\) and \(d\) quarks is no higher than 3 MeV/fm\(^2\) for the range of baryon number densities of interest.
- The contribution of electrons is below \(\sim 0.2\) MeV/fm\(^2\).
- For \(u\) quarks, the parallel surface tension for \(V/S=10\) fm is decreased relative to the bulk case by \(\sim 10\%\) for \(eB_{low}\) and by \(\sim 20\%\) for \(eB_{high}\).
- The surface tension is sensitive to magnetic fields above \(eB \sim 5 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- For \(T \lesssim 100\) MeV the surface tension decreases with temperature; above 100 MeV it increases monotonically with \(T\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study of magnetized quark matter surface tension (Lugones and Grunfeld, 2017) to finite temperature and trapped neutrinos, focusing on astrophysical conditions in neutron stars, protoneutron stars, and neutron star mergers. The results are consistent with earlier calculations that give surface tensions below \(\sim 30\) MeV/fm\(^2\) (Berger, 1987; Pinto, 2012; do Carmo, 2013; Fraga, 2018; Lugones and Grunfeld, 2017), in contrast to significantly larger results above \(\sim 100\) MeV/fm\(^2\) obtained by other authors (Bugaev; Lugones, 2011; Lugones, 2013). The findings enable more realistic modeling of quark-hadron phase transitions in compact stars, particularly regarding whether the interface is a sharp discontinuity or a mixed phase, and inform the possibility of quark matter nucleation in protoneutron stars and merger remnants.
