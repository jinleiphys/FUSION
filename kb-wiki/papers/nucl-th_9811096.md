# Two-point correlation function with pion in QCD sum rules

> Within the framework of the conventional QCD sum rules, we study the pion two-point correlation function, $i\int d^4x e^{iq\cdot x} < 0| T J_N(x) {\bar J}_N(0)|π(p)>$, beyond the soft-pion limit. We construct sum rules from the three distinct Dirac structures, $i γ_5 \notp, i γ_5, γ_5 σ_{μν} {q^μp^ν}$ and study the reliability of each sum rule. The sum rule from the third structure is found to be insensitive to the continuum threshold, $S_π$, and contains relatively small contribution from the undetermined single pole which we denote as $b$. The sum rule from the $i γ_5$ structure is very different even though it contains similar contributions from $S_π$ and $b$ as the ones coming from the $γ_5 σ_{μν} {q^μp^ν}$ structure. On the other hand, the sum rule from the $i γ_5 \notp$ structure has strong dependence on both $S_π$ and $b$, which is clearly in constrast with the sum rule for $γ_5 σ_{μν} {q^μp^ν}$. We identify the source of the sensitivity for each of the sum rules by making specific models for higher resonance contributions and discuss the implication.

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi N}\) extracted from QCD sum rules depends on which Dirac structure of the two-point correlation function with pion is used, and the sum rule from the \(\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu\) structure is the most reliable because it is insensitive to the continuum threshold \(S_\pi\) and contains relatively small contributions from the unknown single pole term \(b\), whereas the other two structures show strong dependence on these parameters.

## Method

The authors computed the OPE for the correlation function \(i\int d^4x e^{iq\cdot x} \langle 0| T J_N(x) \bar{J}_N(0)|\pi(p)\rangle\) beyond the soft-pion limit, keeping terms up to first order in the pion momentum \(p_\mu\). They constructed three separate sum rules from the Dirac structures \(i\gamma_5 \not{p}\), \(i\gamma_5\), and \(\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu\), matching the OPE side to a phenomenological ansatz containing a double pole, an unknown single pole term, and a continuum modeled by a step function starting at threshold \(S_\pi\). To understand the differences between the sum rules, they modeled the contributions from higher resonances (\(N^*\)) using effective Lagrangians for positive- and negative-parity baryon resonances in both pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling schemes.

## Key numbers

- The twist-4 parameter \(\delta^2\) is taken as \(\delta^2 \sim 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) (the paper notes that Ref. [krippa] used \(\delta^2 = 0.35\) GeV\(^2\), which is larger).
- The mixed condensate parameter is taken as \(m_0^2 \sim 0.8\) GeV\(^2\).
- In the sum rule for the \(i\gamma_5\) structure, the numerical factor in the first OPE term is \(0.0785 E_0(x_\pi) M^4 / \pi^2\), and the second term has a factor \(-0.314 \times (1/24) \langle \alpha_s/\pi \mathcal{G}^2 \rangle\).
- The fourth term in the OPE for the \(i\gamma_5 \not{p}\) structure (from the quark condensate squared) is about 4 times larger than the third term (from the gluon condensate).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum-rule calculation of the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi N}\) beyond the soft-pion limit, which was previously used by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) and by Birse and Krippa (1996). The authors identify mistakes in the calculation of Birse and Krippa (1996) and show that their sum rule for the \(i\gamma_5 \not{p}\) structure has different threshold factors. The paper builds on the observation by Jin and Tang (1995) that certain Dirac structures in baryon sum rules are more reliable due to cancellation of parity excitations. The results enable a scheme-independent determination of \(g_{\pi N}\) from the \(\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu\) structure, and suggest that future work should clarify the connection between this approach and the external axial-field sum rules of Belyaev and Kogan (1984) for the nucleon axial charge \(g_A\).
