# Transverse Momentum Distribution and Elliptic Flow of Charged Hadrons in $U$+$U$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=193$ GeV using HYDJET++

> Recent experimental observations of the charged hadron properties in $U+U$ collisions at $193$ GeV contradict many of the theoretical models of particle production including two-component Monte Carlo Glauber model. The experimental results show a small correlation between the charged hadron properties and the initial geometrical configurations (e.g. body-body, tip-tip etc.) of $U+U$ collisions. In this article, we have modified the Monte Carlo HYDJET++ model to study the charged hadron production in $U+U$ collisions at $193$ GeV center-of-mass energy in tip-tip and body-body initial configurations. We have modified the hard as well as soft production processes to make this model suitable for $U+U$ collisions. We have calculated the pseudorapidity distribution, transverse momentum distribution and elliptic flow distribution of charged hadrons with different control parameters in various geometrical configurations possible for $U+U$ collision. We find that HYDJET++ model supports a small correlation between the various properties of charged hadrons and the initial geometrical configurations of $U+U$ collision. Further, the results obtained in modified HYDJET++ model regarding $dn_{ch}/dη$ and elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) suitably matches with the experimental data of $U+U$ collisions in minimum bias configuration.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the HYDJET++ model, after modifications to incorporate deformed Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles for uranium nuclei, supports only a small correlation between charged hadron properties (multiplicity and elliptic flow) and the initial geometrical configurations (tip-tip vs. body-body) of U+U collisions at 193 GeV, consistent with experimental observations that contradict earlier theoretical expectations of a large correlation.

## Method

The authors modified the Monte Carlo HYDJET++ event generator by transforming the deformed Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile from spherical to cylindrical polar coordinates to handle tip-tip and body-body configurations of uranium nuclei. They generated one million events for each centrality class and each configuration, calculating pseudorapidity distributions, transverse momentum distributions, and elliptic flow coefficients from the superposition of soft (thermal hydrodynamic) and hard (multi-parton fragmentation) components, with the soft part using Bjorken boost-invariant hydrodynamics and the hard part based on the PYQUEN partonic energy loss model.

## Key numbers

- Peak value of charged hadron pseudorapidity density (dn_ch/deta) is around 900 in most central (0-5%) tip-tip collisions and around 60 in most peripheral (45-50%) tip-tip collisions, an increment of almost 15 times from peripheral to central.
- The dn_ch/deta at midrapidity in most central U+U collisions is larger than that of most central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
- In most central tip-tip collisions, the soft (hydro) part of the multiplicity is approximately 3 times larger than the hard (jet) part.
- The parameters for the temperature and baryon chemical potential fit at zero impact parameter are: a = 1.290 +/- 0.113 GeV, b = 0.28 +/- 0.046 GeV^-1, c = 0.170 +/- 0.1 GeV, d = 0.169 +/- 0.02 GeV^-1, e = 0.015 +/- 0.01 GeV^-3.
- The maximum possible number of participants N_part(0) for both configurations is shown as a function of b/R_A, where R_A = R(1 + beta_2 Y_20 + beta_4 Y_40) with R_0 = 1.15 fm and deformation parameters beta_2 and beta_4 from Refs. Loizides and Shou (2015).

## Context

This work extends the HYDJET++ model to deformed uranium-uranium collisions, addressing the contradiction between earlier theoretical predictions (e.g., Bjorn (2014), Sergei (2010)) of large multiplicity and elliptic flow differences between tip-tip and body-body configurations and experimental observations from PHENIX (Pandit, 2014) showing only small correlations. The modified model enables further study of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) by providing a tool to subtract background elliptic flow in central U+U collisions, as proposed by John (2013) and Bjorn (2014). The results are compared with AMPT model calculations from Rihan (2012) and experimental data from PHENIX (Adare, 2016) and STAR (Abelev, 2006).
