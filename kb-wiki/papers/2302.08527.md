# Neutrino Structure Functions from GeV to EeV Energies

> The interpretation of present and future neutrino experiments requires accurate theoretical predictions for neutrino-nucleus scattering rates. Neutrino structure functions can be reliably evaluated in the deep-inelastic scattering regime within the perturbative QCD (pQCD) framework. At low momentum transfers ($Q^2 \le {\rm few}$ GeV$^2$), inelastic structure functions are however affected by large uncertainties which distort event rate predictions for neutrino energies $E_ν$ up to the TeV scale. Here we present a determination of neutrino inelastic structure functions valid for the complete range of energies relevant for phenomenology, from the GeV region entering oscillation analyses to the multi-EeV region accessible at neutrino telescopes. Our NNSF$ν$ approach combines a machine-learning parametrisation of experimental data with pQCD calculations based on state-of-the-art analyses of proton and nuclear parton distributions (PDFs). We compare our determination to other calculations, in particular to the popular Bodek-Yang model. We provide updated predictions for inclusive cross sections for a range of energies and target nuclei, including those relevant for LHC far-forward neutrino experiments such as FASER$ν$, SND@LHC, and the Forward Physics Facility. The NNSF$ν$ determination is made available as fast interpolation LHAPDF grids, and can be accessed both through an independent driver code and directly interfaced to neutrino event generators such as GENIE.

## Key claim

The NNSFν determination provides a unified description of neutrino inelastic structure functions from GeV to EeV energies by combining a machine-learning parametrization of experimental data with perturbative QCD calculations, but the low-Q grid is only validated for Q between 0.01 GeV and 22 GeV and x between 10⁻⁵ and 1, while the high-Q grid covers Q from 2 GeV to 10 TeV and x from 10⁻⁹ to 1, with a matching prescription at Q_thr = 22 GeV for x ≥ 10⁻³.

## Method

The authors trained neural networks with three independent inputs (x, Q², A) on experimental neutrino structure function data, supplemented by theoretical constraints from the nNNPDF3.0 nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) evaluated at NLO in QCD using the YADISM code. The low-Q grid (Q ≤ 22 GeV) contains 200 Monte Carlo replicas representing the probability density of fitted structure functions, while the high-Q grid (Q ≥ 2 GeV) tabulates direct YADISM calculations with nNNPDF3.0 replicas plus scale variations for missing higher order uncertainties. The two grids are combined using a threshold matching procedure, and inclusive cross sections are evaluated by integrating the structure functions over the relevant kinematic regions.

## Key numbers

- Low-Q grid valid for Q between 0.01 GeV and 22 GeV and x between 10⁻⁵ and 1
- High-Q grid valid for Q between 2 GeV and 10 TeV and x between 10⁻⁹ and 1
- Low-Q grid contains N_rep = 200 Monte Carlo replicas plus a central set
- High-Q grid contains 210 members: 200 nNNPDF3.0 replicas plus 9 scale variation replicas plus a central set
- Matching threshold Q_thr = 22 GeV for switching between low-Q and high-Q grids
- For neutrino energies E_ν ≲ 1 TeV, the cross section is dominated by the low-Q grid
- For neutrino energies E_ν ≳ 100 PeV, the cross section is dominated by the high-Q grid
- For E_ν = 1 EeV, the bulk of the cross section contribution comes from x ≃ 10⁻⁵ and Q ≃ 100 GeV
- Nuclear modification ratios for lead show suppression up to 25% in the sea quark region (x ≲ 0.01) compared to free-nucleon baseline at Q = 10 GeV

## Context

This work extends the NNPDF methodology (NNPDF4.0 for protons, nNNPDF3.0 for nuclei) to neutrino structure functions, providing a data-driven alternative to the Bodek-Yang model for low-Q² regions where perturbative QCD has large uncertainties. The NNSFν determination enables improved event rate predictions for current and future neutrino experiments including FASERν, SND@LHC, the Forward Physics Facility, and ultra-high energy neutrino telescopes, and is made available as LHAPDF grids that can be interfaced with event generators such as GENIE. The YADISM code used for the perturbative calculations has been benchmarked against APFEL and QCDNUM, and the nuclear PDF comparisons with EPPS21 (Eskola 2021) show good agreement within uncertainties.
