# Charmed baryons and their interactions

> In this proceedings report, we discuss unique features of charmed, or in general heavy, baryons with one heavy quark. A well and long-term known phenomena, the distinction of the two modes of the rho and lambda type of a three-quark system is revisited. The difference of these modes may be tested in the production and decay reactions of the baryons which may be tested in the future experiments at J-PARC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in charmed baryons (one heavy quark plus two light quarks), the mass difference between the heavy and light quarks kinematically separates the two internal excitation modes (the \(\lambda\) mode, where the heavy quark moves relative to the light diquark, and the \(\rho\) mode, where the two light quarks move relative to each other), and that this separation is a universal kinematic effect independent of the details of the confining potential.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic quark model with a harmonic oscillator potential to derive the frequencies of the \(\lambda\) and \(\rho\) modes as functions of the heavy quark mass \(M\). They then employ a Regge model, calibrated on strangeness production data (\(\pi N \to K^* \Lambda\)), to predict cross sections for the charm production reaction \(\pi N \to D^* Y_c\) at J-PARC energies (pion beam around 20 GeV). Production rates for various excited charmed baryon states are computed using harmonic oscillator wave functions and a one-body operator for the \(D^*\) Reggeon exchange mechanism.

## Key numbers

*   The \(\lambda\) mode frequency is \(\omega_\lambda = \sqrt{\frac{M+2m}{M}} \omega\) and the \(\rho\) mode frequency is \(\omega_\rho = \sqrt{3} \omega\), where \(\omega = \sqrt{k/m}\); when \(M > m\), \(\omega_\lambda < \omega_\rho\).
*   The total cross section for \(\pi N \to D^* \Lambda_c\) at a laboratory momentum of about 13 GeV is predicted to be about 10 nb, which is the same order as the BNL upper limit of about 7 nb.
*   For charmed baryon production at \(k_\pi^{Lab} = 20\) GeV, the production rate \({\cal R}(B_c)\) for the \(\Lambda(1/2^-)\) state is 0.93 (relative to the ground state \(\Lambda(1/2^+)\)), and for the \(\Lambda(3/2^-)\) state it is 1.75, both larger than the ground state rate of 1.0.
*   For strange baryon production at \(k_\pi^{Lab} = 4.2\) GeV, the production rate \({\cal R}(B_s)\) for the \(\Lambda(1/2^-)\) state is only 0.07 relative to the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(\rho\) and \(\lambda\) mode separation, previously noted in the context of the \(\Lambda\)-\(\Sigma\) inversion in \(J^P = 5/2^-\) states by Copley (1979), to charmed baryons. It provides theoretical predictions for the J-PARC E50 experiment, which aims to produce charmed baryons using a 20 GeV pion beam. The Regge model approach is adapted from successful descriptions of strangeness production reactions (Dahl 1967, Crennell 1972). The paper enables future experimental tests of the predicted production rates and decay patterns, particularly the low-momentum pion decays that can probe chiral dynamics between constituent quarks.
