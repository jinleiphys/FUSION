# Structure of odd-odd Cs isotopes within the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

> The spectroscopic properties of the odd-odd isotopes $^{124-132}$Cs have been studied within the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional framework. Major ingredients to build the interacting boson-fermion-fermion Hamiltonian, such as the ($β,γ$)-deformation energy surfaces for the even-even core nuclei $^{124-132}$Xe as well as single-particle energies and occupation probabilities of the odd nucleons, have been computed microscopically with the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method. A few coupling constants of the boson-fermion and residual neutron-proton interactions are fitted to reproduce with a reasonable accuracy the experimental excitation energy of the low-lying levels of the odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei. The method is applied to describe the low-energy low-spin spectra of the odd-odd Cs nuclei and the band structures of higher-spin higher-energy states, mainly based on the $(νh_{11/2})^{-1}\otimesπh_{11/2}$ configuration. Many of those odd-odd Cs nuclei have been identified as candidates for exhibiting chiral doublet bands.

## Key claim

The interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2) based on Gogny-D1M energy density functional calculations can describe the low-energy spectra of odd-odd Cs isotopes, but only after fitting a few coupling constants of the boson-fermion and residual neutron-proton interactions to reproduce experimental excitation energies of low-lying levels in the odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, and with the caveat that for nuclei near shell closure (¹³⁰Cs and ¹³²Cs) the excitation energies are overestimated by up to a factor of three.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to obtain (β,γ)-deformation energy surfaces for the even-even core nuclei ¹²⁴⁻¹³²Xe, as well as single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the odd nucleons. These quantities were used as microscopic input to determine the interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian for the even-even cores and most parameters of the boson-fermion coupling terms in the IBFFM-2 Hamiltonian. The remaining free parameters (strengths Γ_ρ, Λ_ρ, A_ρ for boson-fermion interactions and u_D, u_T for the residual neutron-proton interaction) were fitted to reproduce experimental low-lying energy levels of odd-mass Xe and Cs nuclei and odd-odd Cs isotopes. The IBFFM-2 Hamiltonian was then diagonalized in a basis coupling neutron and proton boson angular momenta with odd neutron and proton angular momenta.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²⁴Xe, the IBM-2 calculation reproduces the O(6)-like pattern with R₄/₂ = E(4⁺₁)/E(2⁺₁) ≈ 2.5
- The residual neutron-proton interaction parameters were fixed at u_D = 0.7 MeV and u_T = 0.02 MeV for all considered nuclei and both parities
- For the positive-parity states in ¹²⁸,¹³⁰,¹³²Cs, the Γ_π parameter for the h₁₁/₂ orbital was modified to values of 2.60, 2.40, and 3.00 MeV respectively (compared to 1.00, 1.20, and 1.20 MeV for negative-parity states) to bring the higher-spin states based on the (νh₁₁/₂)⁻¹⊗πh₁₁/₂ configuration to an excitation energy of approximately 0.5 MeV
- The 1⁺₁ ground state of ¹²⁴Cs has about 50% contribution from the odd neutron hole in the 3s₁/₂ orbital
- For ¹²⁶Cs, the lowest negative-parity state is predicted to be 6⁻₁ with 47% contribution from the [ (νh₁₁/₂)⁻¹⊗πg₇/₂ ]^(J=8⁻) configuration
- For ¹³⁰Cs, the two experimental 2⁺ states around 100 keV excitation energy are overestimated by a factor of three in the calculation

## Context

This work extends the IBFFM-2 framework previously applied to odd-odd Au isotopes by Nomura et al. (2019) to the A≈130 mass region, where chiral doublet bands have been observed in odd-odd Cs nuclei (most notably ¹²⁸Cs by Koike et al. (2004) and Grodner et al. (2006, 2018)). The method provides a computationally feasible alternative to large-scale shell model calculations (Caurier et al., 2005) and symmetry-projected generator coordinate method approaches for heavy odd-odd nuclei, enabling systematic studies of spectroscopic properties across isotopic chains. The results enable future investigations of chiral doublet bands and β-decay properties in this mass region, building on previous phenomenological IBFFM studies by Brant et al. (2004) and shell model calculations by Yoshinaga et al. (2006) and Higashiyama et al. (2005, 2007, 2013).
