# Three-body model calculation of the 2$^+$ state in $^{26}$O

> We discuss the energy of the excited 2$^+$ state in the unbound nucleus $^{26}$O using the three-body model of $^{24}$O+$n$+$n$. This model fully takes into account the continuum effects as well as the dineutron correlation of the valence neutrons. The present calculation yields the energy of 1.354 MeV, which is slightly smaller than the unperturbed energy, 1.54 MeV. The energy shifts for the ground and the 2$^+$ states with respect to the unperturbed energy suggest that 0$^+$ and the 2$^+$ states in $^{26}$O show a typical spectrum well described by a short-range pairing interaction acting on two valence nucleons in the same orbit.

## Key claim

The three-body model calculation predicts the 2⁺ state in ²⁶O at about 1.35 MeV, slightly below the unperturbed energy of 1.54 MeV (twice the d₃/₂ single-particle resonance energy in ²⁵O), contrary to most other theoretical calculations which yield values above 1.54 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed a three-body model of ²⁴O+n+n, using a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential for the valence neutrons (adjusted to reproduce the 770 keV d₃/₂ resonance in ²⁵O) and a density-dependent contact pairing interaction between the valence neutrons. The decay energy spectrum for a given angular momentum I was computed using the correlated Green's function constructed from the unperturbed Green's function and the pairing interaction, fully including continuum effects.

## Key numbers

- The 2⁺ state in ²⁶O appears at 1.354 MeV, with an energy shift ΔE = -0.186 MeV relative to the unperturbed energy of 1.54 MeV.
- The 0⁺ ground state appears at 0.148 MeV, with an energy shift ΔE = -1.392 MeV relative to the unperturbed energy.
- The ratio of energy shifts ΔE_{I=0}/ΔE_{I=2} = 7.48 from the three-body model, compared to the single-j model prediction of 5.
- When the pairing interaction is adjusted to give a ground state energy of 5 keV (instead of 148 keV), the 2⁺ state appears at 1.338 MeV, showing little sensitivity to the nn interaction strength.
- The single-particle d₃/₂ resonance energy in ²⁵O used as input is 770 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body model study of the ²⁶O ground state (Hagino and Sagawa, 2014) to the excited 2⁺ state. It contradicts shell model calculations using USDA and USDB interactions (Brown and Richter, 2006) which predict 2⁺ energies of 1.9 and 2.1 MeV, as well as an ab-initio calculation with chiral NN and 3N forces predicting 1.6 MeV, and a continuum shell model calculation (Volya and Zelevinsky, 2006) predicting 1.8 MeV. The result enables experimental verification at the SAMURAI facility at RIKEN to test the validity of nuclear models near the neutron drip line.
