# Structure of isomeric states in 66As and 67As

> Strong residual correlations between neutrons and protons in N ~ Z systems can lead to unusual structure. Using the spherical shell model, we show that a low-excitation shape isomer can occur in the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 66As. This extends the picture of shape coexistence beyond even-even nuclei. Furthermore, it is demonstrated that in 66As and in the N=Z+1 nucleus 67As, a new type of isomer, which we term j-isomer, can be formed. The underlying mechanism for the isomerism formation is structure change in the isomeric states, which involves either an alignment of a neutron-proton pair from the high-j intruder orbitals, or a simultaneous occupation of these neutron and proton high-j orbitals.

## Key claim

Using the spherical shell model, the authors predict a low-excitation shape isomer (a \(3^+\) state) in the odd-odd \(N=Z\) nucleus \(^{66}\)As, and identify a new class of isomer, termed a "\(j\)-isomer," in both \(^{66}\)As and \(^{67}\)As, where the isomerism arises from a structure change involving high-\(j\) intruder orbitals rather than from an energy barrier or selection rules.

## Method

The authors performed spherical shell-model calculations using an extended \(P+QQ\) Hamiltonian in the \(pf_{5/2}g_{9/2}\) model space, diagonalized on a spherical basis. They analyzed wavefunctions by calculating expectation values of nucleon numbers, spin, and isospin in the \(pf\) and \(g_{9/2}\) subspaces, and computed spectroscopic quadrupole moments and reduced transition probabilities (\(B(E2)\) and \(B(E3)\)) to identify states with suppressed electromagnetic decays.

## Key numbers

- In \(^{66}\)As, the calculated \(B(E2:3_1^+ \rightarrow 1_1^+)\) is only 1.3 Weisskopf units (W.u.), much smaller than the 16 W.u. for \(B(E2:3_2^+ \rightarrow 1_1^+)\).
- In \(^{66}\)As, the calculated \(B(E2:9_1^+ \rightarrow 7_1^+)\) is 0.023 W.u., reproducing the experimental value of 0.044(6) W.u. for the 8.2(5) \(\mu\)s isomeric state.
- In \(^{66}\)As, the calculated spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the \(3_1^+\) state is +46.2 \(e\) fm\(^2\), opposite in sign to the -21.0 \(e\) fm\(^2\) of the \(1_1^+\) state, indicating oblate versus prolate shape.
- In \(^{67}\)As, the calculated \(B(E3)\) values from the \(9/2_1^+\) state to the \(5/2_1^-\), \(3/2_1^-\), \(7/2_1^-\), and \(7/2_2^-\) states are 0.057, 0.074, 0.001, and 0.010 W.u., respectively, consistent with the 12(2) ns lifetime of the \(9/2^+\) isomer.
- In \(^{66}\)As, the \(9_1^+\) state has \(\langle n_{g9/2}^\pi \rangle = \langle n_{g9/2}^\nu \rangle \approx 1.04\) and \(J_{g9/2} \approx 8.93\), indicating a complete \(g_{9/2}\) one-proton-one-neutron alignment.

## Context

This work extends the study of shape coexistence, previously observed in even-even \(N=Z\) nuclei such as \(^{72}\)Kr (Bouchez et al.) and predicted in \(^{68}\)Se (Sun et al.), to the odd-odd nucleus \(^{66}\)As. It also introduces the concept of a \(j\)-isomer, distinct from the three established classes of isomers (shape isomers, spin traps, and \(K\)-isomers) discussed in Nature (2001), and proposes that such isomers are unique to \(N \approx Z\) systems. The results enable future comparisons between spherical and deformed-basis shell models (e.g., the projected shell model of Hara and Sun) for nuclei with \(A>70\).
