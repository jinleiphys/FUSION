# How Threshold Effects in Spectroscopic Factors Influence Heavy-Ion Knockout Reactions

> A two-decade-old puzzle in heavy-ion one-nucleon knockout reactions is the strong correlation between the reduction factor $R_s=σ_{\rm exp}/σ_{\rm th}$ and the Fermi surface asymmetry $ΔS$. Theoretical cross sections typically rely on spectroscopic factors (SFs) from shell model (SM) calculations, which neglect continuum coupling effects. Here, we employ the Gamow shell model (GSM), which explicitly incorporates continuum coupling, to compute SFs for $p$-shell nuclei and predict corresponding theoretical cross sections. Systematic calculations demonstrate that using GSM-derived SFs substantially reduces discrepancies between theoretical and experimental results. This improvement is particularly significant for deeply bound nucleon knockout in nuclei near the dripline, where traditional SM-based calculations fall short. As a result, using GSM SFs, the ratio $R_s$ exhibits no pronounced dependence on $ΔS$. Furthermore, both the ratio of GSM SFs to SM SFs and their corresponding reaction cross sections ratios exhibit a strong $ΔS$ dependence. We have also compared GSM SFs and cross sections with those from the no-core shell model calculations, giving a similar pronounced sensitivity to $ΔS$. Detailed analysis attributes these correlations to threshold effects for SFs in weakly bound systems. Overall, incorporating continuum coupling via GSM enhances the reliability of SF predictions for exotic, weakly bound nuclei and provides key insights toward resolving the enduring puzzle in heavy-ion knockout reactions from a nuclear structure perspective.

## Key claim

Using spectroscopic factors (SFs) from the Gamow shell model (GSM), which explicitly incorporates continuum coupling, substantially reduces the long-standing correlation between the reduction factor \(R_s = \sigma_{\rm exp}/\sigma_{\rm th}\) and the Fermi surface asymmetry \(\Delta S\) in heavy-ion one-nucleon knockout reactions, particularly for deeply bound nucleon knockout in nuclei near the dripline where traditional shell model calculations overestimate SFs.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow shell model (GSM) to compute spectroscopic factors for \(p\)-shell nuclei, explicitly accounting for continuum coupling effects that are neglected in traditional shell model (SM) calculations. These GSM-derived SFs were used as nuclear structure inputs in the CNOK code based on the Glauber model to calculate theoretical one-nucleon removal cross sections for reactions on a \(^9\)Be target at 100 MeV/nucleon. Nuclear densities and root-mean-square radii for the reaction calculations were obtained from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using four Skyrme interactions (SLY4, SLY5, SIII, and SKM*), with final cross sections averaged over these interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron knockout reaction \(^8\)C\((0^+)\) to \(^9\)C\((3/2^-)\), the GSM spectroscopic factor for the \(p_{3/2}\) partial wave is 0.374, compared to the SM value of 0.866 and the NCSM value of 0.699, with an effective single-neutron separation energy of 14.225 MeV and \(\Delta S = 12.925\).
- For the neutron knockout reaction \(^9\)C\((3/2^-)\) to \(^{10}\)C\((0^+)\), the GSM spectroscopic factor for the \(p_{3/2}\) partial wave is 0.973, compared to the SM value of 1.735 and the NCSM value of 1.323, with an effective single-neutron separation energy of 21.284 MeV and \(\Delta S = 17.277\).
- For the proton knockout reaction \(^7\)Be\((3/2^-)\) to \(^8\)B\((2^+)\), the GSM spectroscopic factor for the \(p_{3/2}\) partial wave is 0.812, compared to the SM value of 1.008 and the NCSM value of 0.772, with an effective single-proton separation energy of 0.136 MeV and \(\Delta S = -12.690\).
- For the neutron knockout reaction \(^7\)He\((3/2^-)\) to \(^8\)He\((0^+)\), the GSM spectroscopic factor for the \(p_{3/2}\) partial wave is 3.251, compared to the SM value of 3.009 and the NCSM value of 3.317, with an effective single-neutron separation energy of 2.535 MeV and \(\Delta S = -22.281\).

## Context

This work addresses a two-decade-old puzzle first identified in experiments at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (NSCL) in 2004, where the reduction factor \(R_s\) in heavy-ion knockout reactions showed a strong dependence on the Fermi surface asymmetry \(\Delta S\). Previous studies using traditional shell model SFs (e.g., PhysRevC.77.044306, PhysRevC.90.057602) found that nuclei with large \(\Delta S\) values exhibited small \(R_s\) values, often around 30%, but the underlying cause remained unresolved. By incorporating continuum coupling via the Gamow shell model, the authors demonstrate that threshold effects in spectroscopic factors for weakly bound systems are the key nuclear structure mechanism driving this correlation, and that using GSM SFs removes the pronounced \(R_s\)-\(\Delta S\) dependence. This enables more reliable predictions of knockout cross sections for exotic, weakly bound nuclei near the dripline.
