# Bulk Viscosity of Relativistic $npeμ$ Matter in Neutron-Star Mergers

> We discuss the bulk viscosity of hot and dense $npeμ$ matter arising from weak-interaction direct Urca processes. We consider two regimes of interest: (a) the neutrino-transparent regime with $T\leq T_{\rm tr}$ ($T_{\rm tr}\simeq 5÷10$ MeV is the neutrino-trapping temperature); and (b) the neutrino-trapped regime with $T\geq T_{\rm tr}$. Nuclear matter is modeled in relativistic density functional approach with density-dependent parametrization DDME2. The maximum of the bulk viscosity is achieved at temperatures $T \simeq 5÷6$ MeV in the neutrino-transparent regime, then it drops rapidly at higher temperatures where neutrino-trapping occurs. As an astrophysical application, we estimate the damping timescales of density oscillations by the bulk viscosity in neutron star mergers and find that, e.g., at the oscillation frequency $f=10$ kHz, the damping will be very efficient at temperatures $4\leq T\leq 7$ MeV where the bulk viscosity might affect the evolution of the post-merger object.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of hot and dense npeμ matter from direct Urca weak-interaction processes reaches a maximum at temperatures around 5-6 MeV in the neutrino-transparent regime, then drops rapidly at higher temperatures where neutrino trapping occurs; the damping of density oscillations in neutron-star mergers by this viscosity is predicted to be very efficient at temperatures between 4 and 7 MeV for oscillation frequencies around 10 kHz.

## Method

The authors computed the bulk viscosity arising from direct Urca weak-interaction processes in npeμ matter, considering both the neutrino-transparent regime (T ≤ Ttr, with Ttr ≈ 5-10 MeV) and the neutrino-trapped regime (T ≥ Ttr). Nuclear matter was modeled using a relativistic density functional approach with the density-dependent parametrization DDME2. The damping timescales of density oscillations in neutron-star mergers were then estimated from the calculated bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino-trapping temperature Ttr ≈ 5-10 MeV, marking the transition between the two regimes studied.
- Maximum bulk viscosity occurs at temperatures T ≈ 5-6 MeV in the neutrino-transparent regime.
- At oscillation frequency f = 10 kHz, damping by bulk viscosity is very efficient for temperatures 4 ≤ T ≤ 7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bulk viscosity in neutron-star matter by focusing on the direct Urca processes in npeμ matter and using a relativistic density functional (DDME2) rather than non-relativistic models. The results enable more realistic modeling of the post-merger evolution of neutron-star mergers, where the temperature-dependent bulk viscosity can significantly affect the damping of density oscillations and thus the gravitational-wave signal. Prior work on bulk viscosity in neutron-star matter has typically considered either non-relativistic approaches or different composition regimes.
