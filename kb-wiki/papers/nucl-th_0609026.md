# Low Densities Instability of Relativistic Mean Field Models

> The effects of the symmetry energy softening of the relativistic mean field (RMF) models on the properties of matter with neutrino trapping are investigated. It is found that the effects are less significant than those in the case without neutrino trapping. The weak dependence of the equation of state on the symmetry energy is shown as the main reason of this finding. Using different RMF models the dynamical instabilities of uniform matters, with and without neutrino trapping, have been also studied. The interplay between the dominant contribution of the variation of matter composition and the role of effective masses of mesons and nucleons leads to higher critical densities for matter with neutrino trapping. Furthermore, the predicted critical density is insensitive to the number of trapped neutrinos as well as to the RMF model used in the investigation. It is also found that additional nonlinear terms in the Horowitz-Piekarewicz and Furnstahl-Serot-Tang models prevent another kind of instability, which occurs at relatively high densities. The reason is that the effective sigma meson mass in their models increases as a function of the matter density.

## Key claim

The effects of symmetry energy softening on matter with neutrino trapping are less significant than in matter without neutrino trapping, and the predicted critical density for dynamical instability is insensitive to both the number of trapped neutrinos and the specific relativistic mean field (RMF) model used, contrary to the strong model dependence found in the neutrinoless case.

## Method

The authors extended the dynamical stability analysis of uniform matter from Carr et al. (2003) by including muon contributions and all possible mixings of vector and isovector contributions caused by nonlinear terms in the effective relativistic mean field (ERMF) model. They calculated the longitudinal polarization matrix and meson propagators in the limit q0 → 0, then solved the determinant condition det[1 - D_L(q) Π_L(q, q0=0)] ≤ 0 to find the critical density ρ_c for five parameter sets (NL3, Z271, Z271*, G2, G2*) with and without neutrino trapping at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Without neutrino trapping: ρ_c^G2 = 0.052 fm⁻³, ρ_c^G2* = 0.060 fm⁻³, ρ_c^Z271 = 0.066 fm⁻³, ρ_c^Z271* = 0.082 fm⁻³, ρ_c^NL3 = 0.049 fm⁻³
- With neutrino trapping: ρ_c^G2 = (0.084-0.086) fm⁻³, ρ_c^G2* = (0.083-0.086) fm⁻³, ρ_c^Z271 = (0.085-0.086) fm⁻³, ρ_c^Z271* = (0.087-0.088) fm⁻³, ρ_c^NL3 = (0.081-0.084) fm⁻³
- For the NL3 parameter set, an additional instability appears at ρ_c/ρ_0 ≈ 6.5 for q = 2 MeV
- With only neutron contribution and q fixed at 30 MeV, ρ_c/ρ_0 ≈ 1.7 for all parameter sets
- For G2* with Y_le = 0.3, adding protons gives ρ_c/ρ_0 = 0.580; adding electrons further shifts to ρ_c/ρ_0 = 0.430

## Context

This work extends the dynamical stability analysis of Carr et al. (2003) and Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) by including muon contributions and all mixing terms from the ERMF model of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1996). The results show that neutrino trapping raises critical densities and makes them insensitive to the symmetry energy, in contrast to the strong correlation found by Carr et al. (2003) for neutrinoless matter. The findings enable more realistic modeling of protoneutron star crust boundaries and supernova matter, where neutrino trapping is present.
