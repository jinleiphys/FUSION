# Anisotropic flow in fixed-target $^{208}$Pb+$^{20}$Ne collisions as a probe of quark-gluon plasma

> The System for Measuring Overlap with Gas (SMOG2) at the LHCb detector enables the study of fixed-target ion-ion collisions at relativistic energies ($\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\sim100$ GeV in the centre-of-mass). With input from \textit{ab initio} calculations of the structure of $^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne, we compute 3+1D hydrodynamic predictions for the anisotropic flow of Pb+Ne and Pb+O collisions, to be tested with upcoming LHCb data. This will allow the detailed study of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation as well as experimental tests of the predicted nuclear shapes. Elliptic flow ($v_2$) in Pb+Ne collisions is greatly enhanced compared to the Pb+O baseline due to the shape of $^{20}$Ne, which is deformed in a bowling-pin geometry. Owing to the large $^{208}$Pb radius, this effect is seen in a broad centrality range, a unique feature of this collision configuration. Larger elliptic flow further enhances the quadrangular flow ($v_4$) of Pb+Ne collisions via non-linear coupling, and impacts the sign of the kurtosis of the elliptic flow vector distribution ($c_2\{4\}$). Exploiting the shape of $^{20}$Ne proves thus an ideal method to investigate the formation of QGP in fixed-target experiments at LHCb, and demonstrates the power of SMOG2 as a tool to image nuclear ground states.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that elliptic flow ($v_2$) in fixed-target Pb+Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=68.5$ GeV will be enhanced by more than 20% compared to Pb+O collisions across a broad centrality range, specifically because the bowling-pin deformation of $^{20}$Ne is fully resolved by the large $^{208}$Pb nucleus. This enhancement serves as a test of both quark-gluon plasma formation and the predicted nuclear shape of $^{20}$Ne, but the authors note that the hydrodynamic model parameters have not yet been validated by a Bayesian analysis of RHIC Beam Energy Scan data.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with lattice QCD-based equation of state, coupled to the UrQMD hadron cascade model for the final state. Initial nucleon configurations for $^{20}$Ne and $^{16}$O were obtained from ab initio Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) and Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) calculations, while $^{208}$Pb was modeled with a Woods-Saxon distribution. For each nuclear structure scenario, 200k minimum bias events were generated for both Pb+Ne and Pb+O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=68.5$ GeV, with oversampling to obtain $10^6$ hadrons per hydro-surface.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=68.5$ GeV for fixed-target collisions at LHCb
- Woods-Saxon parameters for $^{208}$Pb: $R=6.62$ fm, $a=0.55$ fm
- Woods-Saxon parameters for spherical $^{20}$Ne: $R=2.8$ fm, $a=0.57$ fm
- Woods-Saxon parameters for spherical $^{16}$O: $R=2.61$ fm, $a=0.51$ fm
- Elliptic flow enhancement in central Pb+Ne collisions relative to Pb+O: more than 20%
- Quadrangular flow enhancement in central Pb+Ne collisions relative to Pb+O: as much as 20%
- Hydrodynamic switching temperature: local energy density below 0.45 GeV/fm$^3$
- Natural neon isotopic abundance: 90.48% $^{20}$Ne, 0.27% $^{21}$Ne, 9.25% $^{22}$Ne

## Context

This work extends the program of using asymmetric collisions to probe nuclear structure, building on previous studies of deformed nuclei in heavy-ion collisions by Giacalone (2018) and Mehrabpour (2023). It provides the first theoretical predictions for anisotropic flow in fixed-target Pb+Ne and Pb+O collisions at LHCb's SMOG2 detector, complementing recent LHCb measurements of J/Psi and D0 production in Pb+Ne collisions. The results enable future Bayesian analyses of hydrodynamic parameters in these intermediate-sized systems and allow experimental tests of ab initio nuclear structure predictions for $^{20}$Ne and $^{16}$O from PGCM and NLEFT calculations.
