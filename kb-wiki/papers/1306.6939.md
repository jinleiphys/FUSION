# Nuclear σ-terms and Scalar-Isoscalar WIMP-Nucleus Interactions from Lattice QCD

> It has been argued that the leading scalar-isoscalar WIMP-nucleus interactions receive parametrically enhanced contributions in the context of nuclear effective field theories. These contributions arise from meson-exchange currents (MECs) and potentially modify the impulse approximation estimates of these interactions by 10--60%. We point out that these MECs also contribute to the quark mass dependence of nuclear binding energies, that is, nuclear σ-terms. In this work, we use recent lattice QCD calculations of the binding energies of the deuteron, He-3 and He-4 at pion masses near 500 MeV and 800 MeV, combined with the experimentally determined binding energies at the physical point, to provide approximate determinations of the σ-terms for these light nuclei. For each nucleus, we find that the deviation of the corresponding nuclear σ-term from the single-nucleon estimate is at the few percent level, in conflict with the conjectured enhancement. As a consequence, lattice QCD calculations currently indicate that the cross sections for scalar-isoscalar WIMP-nucleus interactions arising from fundamental WIMP interactions with quarks do not suffer from significant uncertainties due to enhanced meson-exchange currents.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the conjectured 10–60% enhancement of scalar-isoscalar WIMP-nucleus interactions from meson-exchange currents is not supported by lattice QCD data; instead, the deviation of nuclear σ-terms from the single-nucleon estimate is at the few percent level, though this conclusion is based on finite-difference estimates at heavy pion masses (∼325 MeV and ∼658 MeV) and assumes negligible curvature between adjacent pion masses.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD calculations of the binding energies of the deuteron, ³He, and ⁴He at pion masses near 510 MeV and 806 MeV, combined with experimental binding energies at the physical point (mπ ≈ 138 MeV), to compute nuclear σ-terms via the relation σZ,N = (mπ/2)(d/dmπ)E(gs)Z,N. They formed finite differences between adjacent pion masses to estimate the derivative of binding energies with respect to pion mass, and compared the resulting nuclear σ-terms to the impulse approximation contribution AσN, where the nucleon σ-term was extracted from an empirical linear fit to lattice QCD nucleon mass data.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Prezeau (2003), who argued for parametrically enhanced meson-exchange current contributions to scalar-isoscalar WIMP-nucleus interactions, by directly computing nuclear σ-terms from lattice QCD. The results contradict the conjectured enhancement and support the standard hierarchy where nuclear corrections to the impulse approximation are at the few-percent level, consistent with expectations from chiral effective field theory. This enables more reliable calculations of WIMP-nucleus scattering cross sections for dark matter direct detection experiments such as XENON, CDMS, and DAMA, and reduces the theoretical uncertainty in interpreting conflicting experimental results across different nuclear targets.
