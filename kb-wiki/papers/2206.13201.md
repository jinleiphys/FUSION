# Potential energy surface and formation of superheavy nuclei with the Skyrme energy-density functional

> Within the framework of Skyrme energy-density functional theory, the nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated and potential energy surface is obtained with different effective forces for accurately estimating the formation cross sections of superheavy nuclei in massive fusion reactions. The width and height of the potential pocket are influenced by the Skyrme effective forces SkM, SkM$^{\ast}$, SkP, SIII, Ska and SLy4, which correspond to the different equation of state for the isospin symmetry nuclear matter. It is found that the nucleus-nucleus potential is associated with the collision orientation and Skyrme parameters. More repulsive nuclear potential is pronounced with increasing the incompressible modulus of nuclear matter. The available data in the fusion-evaporation reaction of $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U are nicely reproduced with the SkM$^{\ast}$ parameter by implementing into the dinuclear system model.

## Key claim

The Skyrme energy-density functional approach, when implemented with the SkM* parameter set, reproduces experimental data for the fusion-evaporation reaction 48Ca+238U, but the accuracy depends on the specific Skyrme force chosen, with the incompressible modulus of nuclear matter (the "stiffness" of the equation of state) directly affecting the repulsive nuclear potential and thus the width and depth of the potential pocket.

## Method

The nucleus-nucleus potential was calculated using the Skyrme energy-density functional with the frozen-density (sudden) approximation, employing six different Skyrme parameter sets (SkP, SkM, SkM*, SLy4, Ska, SIII). The resulting potential was then used as input to the dinuclear system (DNS) model, where the formation cross section of superheavy nuclei was computed by solving master equations for nucleon transfer coupled to the potential energy surface, with the survival probability of the compound nucleus estimated via the Weisskopf statistical theory.

## Key numbers

- The incompressible modulus K_infinity for the six Skyrme forces ranges from 199 MeV (SkP) to 352 MeV (SIII), with SkM* having K_infinity = 217 MeV.
- Coulomb barriers for the 48Ca+238U reaction calculated with different Skyrme forces lie in the range 185-205 MeV, compared to the proximity potential value of 198.5 MeV and the static barrier of 187 MeV from the quantum molecular dynamics model.
- The diffuseness coefficients a_i for the Woods-Saxon density profiles are taken as 0.55-0.65 fm, with saturation densities rho_01i = 0.06-0.09 fm^{-3}.
- The entrance mass asymmetry for 48Ca+238U is eta_i = -0.664.

## Context

This work extends the application of Skyrme energy-density functionals, previously used for nuclear structure and nuclear matter properties, to the calculation of nucleus-nucleus potentials for superheavy element synthesis. It contrasts with the double-folding method using the Migdal force (Adachi 1996) and the phenomenological proximity potential (Myers 2000), showing that the SkM* force gives better agreement with the Dubna experimental data (Oganessian 2004) for the 48Ca+238U reaction than the standard Migdal approach. The results enable more systematic predictions for the formation of superheavy nuclei around the "island of stability" using neutron-rich radioactive beams at facilities such as RIBF, SPIRAL2, FRIB, and HIAF.
