# Subleading D-like Three-Nucleon Interactions

> We consider subleading contributions to the three-nucleon force from tree-level diagrams involving a single-pion exchange and a contact interaction between two nucleons, which appear at fifth order in the chiral expansion. We show that the corresponding D-like three-nucleon potential depends on 16 low-energy constants, which need to be determined from few-body data. Assuming that their numerical values are governed by the intermediate Δ(1232) excitation mechanism, the considered three-nucleon force can be approximated using 4 low-energy constants that parametrize the short-range nucleon-nucleon to nucleon-Δ transition amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper derives the complete set of subleading D-like three-nucleon interactions at fifth order (N⁴LO) in chiral effective field theory, showing that the potential depends on 16 low-energy constants (LECs) F₁,…,F₁₆, but that under the resonance saturation hypothesis for the Δ(1232) excitation mechanism, this can be approximated using only 4 LECs that parametrize the short-range nucleon-nucleon to nucleon-Δ transition amplitude.

## Method

The authors construct the subleading one-pion-contact three-nucleon force at N⁴LO using three independent methods: direct parametrization of the NN→NNπ transition amplitude in terms of relative momenta, construction of the non-relativistic πNN Lagrangian with three derivatives using reparametrization invariance, and non-relativistic reduction of the covariant Lagrangian. After imposing parity, time-reversal, rotational invariance, hermiticity, and antisymmetrization via the Pauli principle, the 42 candidate operators are reduced to 17 independent structures, one of which can be absorbed into redefinitions of the lower-order LECs D and E_i, leaving 16 independent LECs F₁,…,F₁₆.

## Key numbers

* The subleading D-like three-nucleon potential at N⁴LO depends on **16** low-energy constants F₁,…,F₁₆.
* Under the Δ(1232) resonance saturation assumption, the potential can be approximated using **4** low-energy constants that parametrize the short-range NN→NΔ transition amplitude.
* The leading one-pion-contact 3NF appears at order Q³ (N²LO) and depends on a single LEC **D**.
* The purely short-range subleading 3NF at N⁴LO depends on **13** unknown LECs E_i (Girlanda, 2011).

## Context

This work extends the systematic derivation of three-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory beyond the leading one-pion-contact contribution at N²LO (van Kolck, 1994; Epelbaum, 2002) and the purely short-range subleading 3NF at N⁴LO (Girlanda, 2011). The subleading D-like interactions fill a gap in the N⁴LO three-nucleon force, where loop contributions (Bernard, 2007, 2011; Krebs, 2012, 2013) and contact terms have been studied but the one-pion-contact corrections remained unexplored. This enables future consistent regularization of three-nucleon forces using the gradient flow method (Krebs, 2023) and may help resolve discrepancies between theory and nucleon-deuteron scattering data when combined with the E_i terms (Girlanda, 2018; Epelbaum, 2019; Witala, 2022).
