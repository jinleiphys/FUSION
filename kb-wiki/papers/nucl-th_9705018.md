# Hadron Physics from the Global Color Model of QCD

> We review recent progress in modeling the quark-gluon content of mesons and their low-energy interactions through the Global Color Model field theory. An emphasis is placed on techniques that are shared with the approach based on truncations of the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD. In contrast to most other field theory models for QCD degrees of freedom in hadron physics, this approach directly deals with the derived intrinsic space-time extent of the meson modes in their role as field variables and can accommodate confinement as well as dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry. Various theoretical techniques and approximations found useful in this approach are described. Selected applications reviewed here include the properties and interactions of the Goldstone bosons, interaction vertex functions, low energy chiral observables, electromagnetic interactions and form factors, and transition form factors. Some initial considerations of Vector Meson Dominance and pion loop processes are discussed.

## Key claim

The Global Color Model (GCM) provides a QCD-based framework for meson physics that, unlike point-coupling models such as the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, naturally accommodates confinement and the finite intrinsic space-time extent of meson modes through a distributed effective gluon two-point function, while the NJL limit forces the same coupling strength in the ultraviolet as in the infrared and cannot match perturbative QCD.

## Method

The GCM action is defined in Euclidean metric with quarks interacting via a color current-current coupling mediated by a phenomenological gluon two-point function \(D_{\mu\nu}(x-y)\), constrained to match perturbative QCD in the ultraviolet. Using Fierz reordering identities, the four-fermion interaction is rewritten in terms of bilocal currents \(\bar{q}(x)\Lambda^\theta q(y)\), and auxiliary bilocal boson fields \({\cal B}^\theta(x,y)\) are introduced via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to eliminate the current-current term in favor of a Yukawa coupling. The quarks are then integrated out, yielding a completely bosonized action whose classical (saddle-point) configuration generates a quark self-energy satisfying the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation, and whose quadratic fluctuations define meson propagators and Bethe-Salpeter bound states.

## Key numbers

* The Fierz reordering for color SU(3) yields the identity \(\lambda^a_{ij} \lambda^a_{kl} = C^c_{il} C^c_{kj}\) with \(C^c = ( \frac{4}{3}{\bf 1}, \frac{i}{\sqrt{3}}\lambda ^{a} )\) for color singlet plus octet channels.
* In the point-coupling (NJL) limit with \(D(x-y) \rightarrow \tilde G \delta(x-y)\), the scalar and vector coupling constants are related as \(G_S = \frac{2}{9} \tilde G\) and \(G_V = \frac{1}{9} \tilde G\), giving \(G_S = 2 G_V\).
* The rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark self-energy yields the momentum-dependent amplitudes \(A(p^2)\) and \(B(p^2)\) from integrals over \(D(p-q)\) with the dressed quark propagator denominator \(q^2 A^2(q^2) + (B(q^2)+m)^2\).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the Global Color Model initiated by Cahill and Roberts (1985) and Praschifka et al. (1987a), and is closely related to the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to non-perturbative QCD reviewed by Roberts and Williams (1994). The GCM provides a systematic framework for deriving meson properties, interaction vertices, and form factors from an underlying quark-gluon dynamics, in contrast to purely phenomenological hadronic field theories. The paper reviews applications to Goldstone bosons, low-energy chiral observables, electromagnetic form factors of the pion and kaon, vector meson dominance, and pion loop processes, and it enables future work correlating many hadronic parameters with far fewer sub-hadronic parameters through a consistent quark-gluon basis for hadron interaction vertices.
