# Comparative study of three-nucleon force models in nuclear matter

> We calculate the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the microscopic many-body Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach and employing the Argonne V18 (AV18) nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential supplemented with two different three-nucleon force models recently constructed to reproduce the binding energy of $^3$H, $^3$He and $^4$He nuclei as well as the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length. We find that none of these new three-nucleon force models is able to reproduce simultaneously the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter and the properties of three- and four-nucleon systems.

## Key claim

None of the new three-nucleon force models (TM' or N2LOL) that were recently constructed to reproduce the binding energies of 3H, 3He, and 4He as well as the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length is able to simultaneously reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of the energy per particle for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the Argonne V18 (AV18) nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented with two different three-nucleon force models (TM' and N2LOL). The three-nucleon forces were reduced to effective density-dependent two-body forces by averaging over the coordinates of one nucleon, using a central correlation function approximated by a Heaviside step function with cutoff radius 0.6 fm, and then added to the bare NN interaction in the BHF self-consistent scheme.

## Key numbers

- Saturation point with AV18 alone (no three-nucleon force): rho_0 = 0.23 fm^-3, E/A = -16.43 MeV
- For TM1' interaction at empirical saturation density (0.16 fm^-3): Delta E = +4.9 MeV (repulsive shift)
- For TM3' interaction at empirical saturation density: Delta E = +6.8 MeV
- For TM1' interaction at twice empirical saturation density (0.32 fm^-3): Delta E = +20.9 MeV
- For TM3' interaction at twice empirical saturation density: Delta E = +28.1 MeV
- For N2LOL1 interaction at empirical saturation density: Delta E = -1.4 MeV (attractive shift)
- For N2LOL1 interaction at twice empirical saturation density: Delta E = -2.9 MeV
- Saturation density for TM1': 0.12 fm^-3; for TM2': 0.11 fm^-3; for TM3': 0.11 fm^-3
- Saturation density for N2LOL1: 0.26 fm^-3; for N2LOL2: 0.25 fm^-3; for N2LOL3: 0.26 fm^-3; for N2LOL4: 0.25 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy at empirical saturation density for all models lies within the experimental range: 29.0-32.7 MeV, with slope parameter L in range 40.5-61.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of three-nucleon forces from few-nucleon systems (where the Pisa group had previously fitted TM' and N2LOL parameters to reproduce 3H, 3He, and 4He binding energies and the n-d doublet scattering length) to infinite nuclear matter. The results contradict the expectation that a single three-nucleon force parametrization can simultaneously describe both finite nuclei and nuclear matter saturation, confirming a long-standing tension noted in earlier work such as Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988). The findings enable future studies that may need to include additional exchange contributions or use momentum-space formulations as in Hebeler et al. or Holt et al. to resolve this discrepancy.
