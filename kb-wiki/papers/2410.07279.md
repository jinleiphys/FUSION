# Re-investigation of neutron capture by 84Kr and 86Kr in the s-process nucleosynthesis

> The thermonuclear reaction rates and Maxwellian averaged cross sections (MACS) for the 84Kr(n,γ) 85Kr and 86Kr(n,γ) 87Kr processes were examined using the statistical model code Talys v1.96. The effects of nuclear level densities (NLDs) on Maxwellian averaged cross sections and neutron capture rates are examined both quantitatively and qualitatively. The present Talys based MACS and radiative capture rates for 84Kr(n,γ) 85Kr and 86Kr(n,γ) 87Kr processes agree well with the earlier reported findings. The statistical models nuclear properties (level density and gamma ray strength) were fine tuned to reproduce the existing experimental nuclear data.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Maxwellian averaged cross sections (MACS) and neutron capture rates for 84Kr(n,γ)85Kr and 86Kr(n,γ)87Kr computed with Talys v1.96 agree well with earlier experimental data, but only after fine-tuning the nuclear level density and gamma-ray strength function models; the agreement is not a blind prediction but depends on selecting the Constant Temperature Model as the best-fitted level density model.

## Method

The authors used the statistical model code Talys v1.96, based on Hauser-Feshbach theory, to compute neutron capture cross sections, Maxwellian averaged cross sections (MACS), and radiative capture rates for 84Kr(n,γ)85Kr and 86Kr(n,γ)87Kr. They employed six different nuclear level density models (CTM, BSFM, GSM, SHFB, GHFB, TGHFB) with a fixed temperature-dependent Relativistic Mean Field radiative strength function, and compared the computed MACS against experimental data from Walter, Tomyo, Kappeler, Tessler, and Mutti. The neutron energy range was 0.01–100 keV, and the MACS were evaluated at the standard s-process thermal energy of kT = 30 keV.

## Key numbers

- For 84Kr(n,γ)85Kr at kT = 30 keV, the computed MACS values are 37.87 mb (CTM), 34.080 mb (GHFB), and 34.46 mb (TGHFB), compared to experimental values of 34 ± 2.8 mb (Tessler), 33 ± 2 mb (Mutti), 37.3 ± 4.3 mb (Walter), and 32.133 ± 2.36 mb (Tomyo).
- For 86Kr(n,γ)87Kr at kT = 30 keV, the computed MACS with the GHFB level density model is 4.56 mb, compared to experimental values of 3.5 ± 0.3 mb (Kappeler) and 3.8 ± 0.7 mb (Kappeler).
- The radiative capture rate for 84Kr(n,γ)85Kr at T9 = 0.1 is 6.48 × 10^6 cm^3 mol^−1 s^−1, while for 86Kr(n,γ)87Kr at the same temperature it is 9.77 × 10^5 cm^3 mol^−1 s^−1.
- The radiative capture rate for 84Kr(n,γ)85Kr at T9 = 1.0 is 5.67 × 10^6 cm^3 mol^−1 s^−1, and for 86Kr(n,γ)87Kr at T9 = 1.0 it is 7.74 × 10^5 cm^3 mol^−1 s^−1.

## Context

This work extends previous experimental and theoretical studies of neutron capture on krypton isotopes relevant to the s-process of nucleosynthesis, specifically the measurements by Walter (1986), Tomyo (2005), Kappeler (1990), Tessler (2020), and Mutti (2005). The authors confirm that 86Kr (with magic neutron number N = 50) acts as a bottleneck in the s-process due to its small capture cross section, consistent with earlier findings. The computed MACS and rates provide updated nuclear data for s-process nucleosynthesis simulations in AGB stars and massive stars, particularly for the weak s-process component (mass numbers 56–90).
