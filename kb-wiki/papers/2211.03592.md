# Progress on Brussels-Skyrme atomic mass models on a grid: stiff neutron matter equation of state

> We report here the current developments on the Brussels-Skyrme-on-a-Grid (BSkG) atomic mass models. In comparison with our previous models, BSkG3 improves the infinite nuclear matter (INM) properties which opens its applications to neutron stars. The results presented here show that BSkG3 preserve the excellent agreement with experimental nuclear masses and radii, together with fission barriers of actinides obtained by BSkG1 and BSkG2, while the nuclear matter properties are considerably improved.

## Key claim

BSkG3 improves the infinite nuclear matter properties of the Brussels-Skyrme-on-a-Grid family, specifically producing a stiff neutron matter equation of state at high densities, while maintaining the excellent agreement with experimental nuclear masses, radii, and fission barriers achieved by BSkG1 and BSkG2.

## Method

The authors fitted a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mass model using extended Skyrme forces that include density-dependent generalizations of the usual t1 and t2 terms (t4 and t5 terms), following the approach of the Brussels-Montréal Skyrme series. A committee of Multi-Layer Neural Networks was used as an emulator to reduce computational cost during parameter adjustment, and the model was fitted to essentially all experimental masses and five-point neutron mass-staggering data, with a constraint on the neutron matter equation of state at high density. The pairing treatment was guided by ab-initio calculations, and the parameters of the collective correction to fission barriers were adjusted as a final step.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation for 2457 known masses of AME2020 (nuclei with Z, N >= 8): sigma_BSkG3(M) = 0.693 MeV, compared to 0.741 MeV for BSkG1 and 0.678 MeV for BSkG2
- Root-mean-square deviation for 884 measured charge radii: sigma_BSkG3(R_c) = 0.0250 fm, compared to 0.0239 fm for BSkG1 and 0.0274 fm for BSkG2
- Root-mean-square deviation for 45 reference primary fission barrier heights: sigma_BSkG3(E_I) = 0.38 MeV, compared to 0.88 MeV for BSkG1 and 0.44 MeV for BSkG2
- Saturation density n0 = 0.158 fm^-3 for BSkG3 (same as BSkG1 and BSkG2)
- Symmetry energy coefficient J = 31.0 MeV for BSkG3, compared to 32.0 MeV for both BSkG1 and BSkG2
- Symmetry energy slope L = 51.4 MeV for BSkG3, compared to 51.7 MeV for BSkG1 and 53.0 MeV for BSkG2
- Incompressibility K_v = 244.6 MeV for BSkG3, compared to 237.8 MeV for BSkG1 and 237.5 MeV for BSkG2
- Isoscalar effective mass M*_s/M = 0.86 for BSkG3 (same as BSkG1 and BSkG2)

## Context

This work extends the Brussels-Skyrme-on-a-Grid family of mass models (BSkG1 from Scamps (2021) and BSkG2 from Ryssens (2022)) by incorporating the t4 and t5 density-dependent terms from the Brussels-Montréal Skyrme series (Chamel (2009), Goriely (2016)) to stiffen the neutron matter equation of state. The improved infinite nuclear matter properties, particularly the stiff high-density behavior comparable to the LS2 equation of state, enable applications to neutron star structure and the description of heavy pulsars, while the accurate nuclear structure properties support applications in r-process nucleosynthesis and neutron star crust composition.
