# Localization of pairing correlations in nuclei within relativistic mean field models

> We analyze the localization properties of two-body correlations induced by pairing in the framework of relativistic mean field (RMF) models. The spatial properties of two-body correlations are studied for the pairing tensor in coordinate space and for the Cooper pair wave function. The calculations are performed both with Relativistic-Hatree-Bogoliubov (RHB) and RMF+Projected-BCS (PBCS) models and taking as examples the nuclei $^{66}$Ni, $^{124}$Sn and $^{200}$Pb. It is shown that the coherence length have the same pattern as in previous non-relativistic HFB calculations, i.e., it is maximum in the interior of the nucleus and drops to a minimum in the surface region. In the framework of RMF+PBCS we have also analysed, for the particular case of $^{120}$Sn, the dependence of the coherence length on the intensity of the pairing force. This analysis indicates that pairing is reducing the coherence length by about 25-30 $\%$ compared to the RMF limit.

# Localization of pairing correlations in nuclei within relativistic mean field models

## Key claim

Pairing correlations reduce the coherence length in nuclei by about 25-30% compared to the relativistic mean field (RMF) limit, but the coherence length's spatial pattern, maximum in the nuclear interior and minimum in the surface region, is qualitatively the same as in previous non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations, with the caveat that quantitative differences arise for nuclei near the neutron drip line where the relativistic mean field binds low-angular-momentum states more strongly than non-relativistic models.

## Method

The authors performed Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) and RMF+Projected-BCS (PBCS) calculations for the nuclei ⁶⁶Ni, ¹²⁴Sn, and ²⁰⁰Pb using the DD-ME2 relativistic mean-field parametrization and the Gogny D1S pairing interaction. The pairing tensor in coordinate space and the Cooper pair wave function were expressed in relative and center-of-mass coordinates via Brody-Moshinsky transformations, and the coherence length was computed as a function of the center-of-mass coordinate from the second moment of these two-body correlation functions. For ¹²⁰Sn, the pairing force intensity was scaled by various factors to study the dependence of the coherence length on pairing strength within the RMF+PBCS approach.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁶Ni: average coherence length from pairing tensor is 4.05 fm (RHB) and 4.49 fm (RMF+PBCS); average Cooper pair coherence length is 3.90 fm (RHB) and 4.35 fm (RMF+PBCS); mean neutron distance is 1.86 fm.
- For ¹²⁴Sn: average coherence length from pairing tensor is 4.85 fm (RHB) and 5.69 fm (RMF+PBCS); average Cooper pair coherence length is 4.48 fm (RHB) and 5.16 fm (RMF+PBCS); mean neutron distance is 1.84 fm.
- For ²⁰⁰Pb: average coherence length from pairing tensor is 6.81 fm (RHB) and 7.31 fm (RMF+PBCS); average Cooper pair coherence length is 6.33 fm (RHB) and 6.83 fm (RMF+PBCS); mean neutron distance is 1.82 fm.
- Pairing reduces the coherence length by 25-30% compared to the RMF limit for ¹²⁰Sn: in the bulk from about 6.5 fm to 4.5 fm, and the minimum from about 3.3 fm to 2.6 fm.
- The minimum coherence length in the surface region is of order 2.5-3 fm for the three nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic HFB studies of pairing localization by Matsuo (2001), Pillet et al. (2007, 2010), Vinas et al., and Pastore et al. to the relativistic mean-field framework, using both RHB and particle-number-projected BCS approaches. The results confirm the generic pattern of the coherence length found in non-relativistic calculations but reveal that for drip-line nuclei such as ⁸⁴Ni, the relativistic mean field confines the 3s₁/₂ state more strongly than HFB, leading to a smaller coherence length than predicted non-relativistically. The analysis of the coherence length's dependence on pairing strength for ¹²⁰Sn shows that the weak sensitivity observed in prior HFB work (Pillet 2010, Pastore) arises from cancellations among contributions from different single-particle states, not from finite-size effects alone. The study enables future investigations of pairing regimes in nuclei and comparisons with two-nucleon transfer reaction data.
