# Viscosity Sum Rules at Large Scattering Lengths

> We use the operator product expansion (OPE) and dispersion relations to obtain new model-independent "Borel-resummed" sum rules for both shear and bulk viscosity of many-body systems of spin-1/2 fermions with predominantly short range S-wave interactions. These sum rules relate Gaussian weights of the frequency-dependent viscosities to the Tan contact parameter C(a). Our results are valid for arbitrary values of the scattering length a, but receive small corrections from operators of dimension larger than 5 in the OPE, and can be used to study transport properties in the vicinity of the infinite scattering length fixed point. In particular, we find that the exact dependence of the shear viscosity sum rule on scattering length is controlled by the function C(a). The sum rules that we obtain depend on a frequency scale w that can be optimized to maximize their overlap with low-energy data.

## Key claim

The paper derives new model-independent "Borel-resummed" sum rules for shear and bulk viscosity of spin-1/2 fermions with short-range S-wave interactions, valid for arbitrary scattering length but receiving small corrections from operators of dimension greater than 5 in the operator product expansion (OPE). The exact dependence of the shear viscosity sum rule on scattering length is controlled by the Tan contact parameter C(a), and the sum rules depend on a frequency scale ω₀ that can be optimized to maximize overlap with low-energy data.

## Method

The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) to determine the ω→∞ asymptotic behavior of the Feynman (time-ordered) two-point correlator of the traceless part of the stress tensor, working to all orders in the coupling constant but to leading order (dimension Δ=4) in the operator expansion. They then employ Borel resummation of the OPE combined with dispersion relations (Kramers-Kronig) to derive sum rules that relate Gaussian-weighted integrals of the frequency-dependent shear and bulk viscosities to the Tan contact parameter C(a). The OPE is constructed by taking matrix elements between one-particle and two-particle states, using the exact 2→2 scattering amplitude in d spatial dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity sum rule relates Gaussian weights of Re η(ω) to the Tan contact parameter C(a), with the exact scattering length dependence given by C(a)
- The bulk viscosity expressions vanish as a⁻² in the limit of infinite scattering length (a→∞)
- The OPE result for M^{xy,xy}(ω→∞, q=0) contains the four-fermion contact operator O₄ = ψ₁†ψ₂†ψ₁ψ₂, with coefficient proportional to [(-mω-iε)^{d/2-1} + (mω-iε)^{d/2-1}] in d spatial dimensions
- The results are accurate up to calculable corrections from operators of dimension Δ>5 (e.g., three-body contact operators and finite range effects)

## Context

This work extends the Tan relations (Tan 2005, 2008) and the OPE-based derivation of universal relations by Braaten and Platter (2008) to transport properties of unitary Fermi gases. The asymptotic shear viscosity tail agrees with the previously obtained expression in Enss, Haussmann, and Zwerger (2011), while the asymptotic bulk viscosity result is new. The sum rule methodology follows the approach of Goldberger and Rothstein (2010) for the structure factor, itself based on the QCD sum rule technology of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979). The results enable model-independent tests of transport properties near the unitary fixed point using cold atom experiments, with the free parameter ω₀ allowing optimization for comparison with low-energy data.
