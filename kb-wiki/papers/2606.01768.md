# Semileptonic Decays of $Λ\to p \ell^{-} \barν_{\ell}$ in the Light-Front Dynamics

> We investigate the exclusive semileptonic decays of $Λ\to p \ell^{-} \barν_{\ell}~(\ell=e,μ)$ within the Standard Model using the light-front quark model. The transition form factor behaviors of $Λ\to p$ are obtained from the effective treatment of nonvalence contributions in addition to the valence ones in the Drell-Yan-West frame due to the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. Based on these form factors, we obtain that the branching ratios of $Λ\to p e^{-} \barν_{e}$ and $p μ^{-} \barν_μ$, including nonvalence contributions, are around $8.32\times 10^{-4}$ and $1.31\times 10^{-4}$, which are consistent with the latest measurements from the BESIII Collaboration, respectively. Our results indicate that nonvalence contributions can play a non-negligible role in the semileptonic baryon decays within the light-front framework.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the branching ratios of $\Lambda\to p e^{-} \bar{\nu}_{e}$ and $\Lambda\to p \mu^{-} \bar{\nu}_{\mu}$ computed in the light-front quark model, including nonvalence contributions, are around $8.32\times 10^{-4}$ and $1.31\times 10^{-4}$, respectively, and that these values are consistent with the latest BESIII measurements. The hedge is that nonvalence contributions play a non-negligible role in these semileptonic baryon decays within the light-front framework, and the consistency depends on including those contributions.

## Method

The authors employ the light-front quark model (LFQM) with a quark-diquark picture for baryons, treating the $\Lambda$ and proton as bound states of an active quark and a spectator diquark. They compute the six transition form factors $f_{1,2,3}(q^2)$ and $g_{1,2,3}(q^2)$ for the $\Lambda\to p$ weak transition in the timelike region ($q^2>0$) using the Drell-Yan-West frame, separating the contributions into valence (ordinary) and nonvalence (higher Fock state) parts via an effective treatment based on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. The form factors are then used to calculate the differential decay widths and integrated branching ratios for the electron and muon channels.

## Key numbers

* Branching ratio of $\Lambda\to p e^{-} \bar{\nu}_{e}$: $8.32\times 10^{-4}$ (including nonvalence contributions)
* Branching ratio of $\Lambda\to p \mu^{-} \bar{\nu}_{\mu}$: $1.31\times 10^{-4}$ (including nonvalence contributions)
* Form factor values at $q^2=0$: $f_1(0)=1.305^{+0.015}_{-0.038}$, $f_2(0)=-1.139^{+0.013}_{-0.032}$, $f_3(0)=-0.917^{+0.144}_{-0.182}$, $g_1(0)=0.924^{+0.012}_{-0.024}$, $g_2(0)=-0.269^{+0.115}_{-0.101}$, $g_3(0)=-7.487^{+1.377}_{-1.169}$
* Input constituent quark masses: $m_{u,d}=0.25$ GeV, $m_s=0.48$ GeV, diquark mass $m_{[qq']}=0.5$ GeV
* Gaussian shape parameters: $\beta_{u[qq]}=0.34\pm0.02$ GeV, $\beta_{s[qq]}=0.38\pm0.02$ GeV
* Effective hadronic scale for form factor parameterization: $M_{pole}=1.115$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the light-front quark model treatment of semileptonic baryon decays by incorporating nonvalence (higher Fock state) contributions, which were previously tested mainly in mesonic systems by the same group (BS1, BS2, BS3). The results are compared with the latest BESIII measurements (BESIIIX, BESIIIV) and with lattice QCD predictions (Nonst), showing good agreement for $g_1/f_1$ but a slightly larger $f_2/f_1$ ratio. The work enables future applications of the LFQM with nonvalence contributions to other semileptonic baryon decays and provides a framework for testing the Standard Model through precision measurements of hyperon decays.
