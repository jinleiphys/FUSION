# Charmonium production from nonequilibrium charm and anticharm quarks in quark-gluon plasma

> Parameterizing the charm and anticharm quark momentum distributions by the Tsallis distribution, we study the nonequilibrium effect on the charmonium production rate in a quark-gluon plasma up to the next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD. We find that nonequilibrium charm and anticharm quarks suppress the charmonium production rate compared to that from equilibrated ones. We further show that the suppression factor calculated with the charm quark relaxation time, which has been frequently used in the literature, is close to our results.

## Key claim

Using a Tsallis distribution to parameterize nonequilibrium charm and anticharm quark momentum distributions, the authors find that such nonequilibrium distributions suppress the charmonium production rate in quark-gluon plasma compared to the rate from fully equilibrated charm and anticharm quarks, and that the suppression factor estimated from the charm quark relaxation time (frequently used in the literature) is close to their results.

## Method

The authors calculated the charmonium production rate from charm and anticharm quark recombination using transition amplitudes up to next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, including medium effects on the J/psi wavefunction and binding energy from a screened Cornell potential. They parameterized the charm and anticharm quark momentum distributions with the Tsallis distribution (where lambda = q-1) and used a parton cascade model based on elastic scattering with light quarks and gluons to study how these distributions approach equilibrium over time in a QGP at fixed temperature.

## Key numbers

* The charm quark relaxation time in a QGP of temperature 1.5 T_c (with T_c = 170 MeV) has an approximate value of about 3.2 fm/c.
* For the initial charm quark spectra at mid-rapidity (|y| <= 1) from PHYTIA simulations, the Tsallis distribution parameters were (T, lambda) = (650 MeV, 0.013) for p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (RHIC) and (680 MeV, 0.033) for sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV (LHC).
* After 1 relaxation time in a QGP of T = 1.5 T_c, the fitted Tsallis parameters were (T, lambda) = (190 MeV, 0.078) for RHIC and (140 MeV, 0.107) for LHC.
* Assuming the QGP lifetime equals the charm quark relaxation time, the J/psi production rates in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC are suppressed by 40-50% and 35-45%, respectively, compared to those from completely thermalized charm and anticharm quarks.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on charmonium regeneration in QGP (Zhao 2007, Song 2010, Song 2011) that used a simple relaxation factor R(tau) = 1 - exp[-(tau - tau_0)/tau_rel] to account for nonequilibrium charm quarks. The authors provide a more rigorous calculation using the Tsallis distribution to describe the nonequilibrium charm and anticharm quark momentum distributions, validating the earlier relaxation factor approach. This enables more accurate modeling of charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, where the fraction of regenerated charmonia becomes dominant.
