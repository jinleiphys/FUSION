# Diquark condensation in dense adjoint matter

> We study SU(2) lattice gauge theory at non-zero chemical potential with one staggered quark flavor in the adjoint representation. In this model the fermion determinant, although real, can be both positive and negative. We have performed numerical simulations using both hybrid Monte Carlo and two-step multibosonic algorithms, the latter being capable of exploring sectors with either determinant sign. We find that the positive determinant sector behaves like a two-flavor theory, with the chiral condensate rotating into a two-flavor diquark condensate for mu>m_pi/2, implying a superfluid ground state. Good agreement is found with analytical predictions made using chiral perturbation theory. In the `full' model there is no sign of either onset of baryon density or diquark condensation for the range of chemical potentials we have considered. The impact of the sign problem has prevented us from exploring the true onset transition and the mode of diquark condensation, if any, for this model.

## Key claim
The positive determinant sector of SU(2) adjoint lattice gauge theory with one staggered flavor behaves like a two-flavor theory, with the chiral condensate rotating into a two-flavor diquark condensate for chemical potential \(\mu > m_\pi/2\); however, in the full model including negative determinant configurations, there is no sign of either baryon density onset or diquark condensation for the range of chemical potentials studied, and the sign problem prevents exploration of the true onset transition.

## Method
The authors performed numerical simulations of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with one staggered quark flavor in the adjoint representation on a \(4^3\times8\) lattice at \(\beta=2.0\) for quark masses \(m=0.1, 0.05, 0.01\). They used both the hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) algorithm, which samples only positive determinant configurations, and the two-step multibosonic (TSMB) algorithm, which can explore sectors with either determinant sign, to study fermionic observables (chiral condensate, fermion density, pion mass, pion susceptibility) and gauge observables (plaquette, Wilson loops, Polyakov loop) as functions of chemical potential \(\mu\). Diquark condensates were measured via partially quenched measurements with a diquark source term \(j\), extrapolated to \(j=0\) using a fit form \(A + B j^{1/3} + C j\).

## Key numbers
- Onset transition in the positive determinant sector occurs at \(\mu_o \approx 0.35\) for \(m=0.1\), consistent with \(\mu_o = m_\pi/2\) where \(m_{\pi0} = 0.7322(6)\) at \(\mu=0\).
- At \(\mu=0.37\) for \(m=0.1\), the fraction of configurations with negative determinant is \(p_- = 0.22\), and the average reweighting factor (including sign) is \(\langle r \rangle = 0.45(2)\).
- The chiral condensate in the positive determinant sector follows \(\langle \bar\psi\psi \rangle / \langle \bar\psi\psi \rangle_0 = 1/x^2\) for \(x > 1\) (where \(x = 2\mu/m_{\pi0}\)), with good agreement up to \(x \simeq 2\).
- At \(\mu=0.37\) for \(m=0.1\), the quark number density is \(n \simeq 3.6\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and energy density \(\epsilon \simeq 1270\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3}\) in physical units assuming \(a \simeq 0.22\ \text{fm}\).
- The diquark condensate extrapolation at \(\mu=0.36\) yields \(A = 0.062(15)\) for the fit \(\langle qq_3(j) \rangle = A + B j^{1/3} + C j\).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of two-color QCD with adjoint quarks by Hands et al. (2000) and builds on chiral perturbation theory predictions by Kogut et al. (2000) for theories with diquark Goldstone modes. The positive determinant sector results agree with \(\chi\)PT predictions for a two-flavor theory, while the full \(N=1\) theory shows no onset up to \(\mu=0.4\), contrasting with the behavior seen in two-color QCD with fundamental staggered quarks by Kogut et al. (2001). The results enable future studies of the sign problem in dense matter and the search for a true onset transition or color superconducting phase at higher chemical potential, though the sign problem becomes severe at \(\mu=0.4\) and beyond.
