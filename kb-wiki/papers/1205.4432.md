# An analysis of charged particles spectra in events with different charged multiplicity

> The shapes of invariant differential cross section for charged particle production as function of transverse momentum measured in $pp$ collisions by the STAR detector are analyzed. The spectra shape varies with the event charged multiplicity changing. To describe this and several other recently observed effects a simple qualitative model for hadroproduction mechanism was proposed.

# An analysis of charged particles spectra in events with different charged multiplicity

## Key claim
The relative contribution of an exponential (Boltzmann-like) term to the charged particle transverse momentum spectrum in pp collisions decreases with increasing event charged multiplicity, consistent with a qualitative model where valence quark radiation produces the exponential component and Pomeron exchange produces a power-law component. The observed trend is present but "largely smeared by the multiplicity fluctuations due to very limited rapidity window" in the STAR data.

## Method
The authors fit invariant differential cross sections for charged particle production as a function of transverse momentum (PT) in pp collisions at STAR with a sum of an exponential term and a power-law (Tsallis-like) term. They extract the ratio R of the power-law term contribution to the full parameterization integrated over PT^2, and study how this ratio varies with the visible charged event multiplicity measured in the central rapidity interval (-0.5 < y < 0.5).

## Key numbers
- The parameterization used is dσ/(PT dPT) = A_e exp(-E_Tkin/T_e) + A / (1 + PT^2/(T^2 n))^n, where E_Tkin = sqrt(PT^2 + M^2) - M and M is the produced hadron mass.
- The ratio R = (A n T) / (A n T + A_e (2 M T_e + 2 T_e^2)(n-1)) quantifies the relative contribution of the power-law term.
- The exponential contribution dominates charged particle spectra in baryon-baryon collisions while it is "completely missing" in gamma-gamma interactions.
- The exponential contribution is "characteristic for charged pion production" and "much less pronounced in kaon or proton (antiproton) production spectra."

## Context
This work extends the authors' previously proposed unified approach (Bylinkin and Rostovtsev, 2011) for describing particle production spectra as a sum of exponential and power-law components. It tests a specific prediction derived from the AGK cutting rules (Abramovsky, Gribov, Kancheli, 1973), which state that charged multiplicity in hadronic interactions is proportional to the number of Pomerons involved. The analysis uses STAR data to confirm the predicted decrease of exponential contribution with rising multiplicity, though the effect is smeared by the limited rapidity acceptance. The results enable future tests of additional predictions, including rapidity-dependent behavior in photon-proton collisions at HERA and dominance of exponential contributions in the proton fragmentation region.
