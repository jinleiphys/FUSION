# Validity of the linear coupling approximation in heavy-ion fusion reactions at sub barrier energies

> The role of higher order coupling of surface vibrations to the relative motion in heavy-ion fusion reactions at near-barrier energies is investigated. The coupled channels equations are solved to all orders, and also in the linear and the quadratic coupling approximations. Taking $^{64}$Ni + $^{92,96}$Zr reactions as examples, it is shown that all order couplings lead to considerably improved agreement with the experimentally measured fusion cross sections and average angular momenta of the compound nucleus for such heavy nearly symmetric systems. The importance of higher order coupling is also examined for asymmetric systems like $^{16}$O + $^{112}$Cd, $^{144}$Sm, for which previous calculations of the fusion cross section seemed to indicate that the linear coupling approximation was adequate. It is shown that the shape of the barrier distributions and the energy dependence of the average angular momentum can change significantly when the higher order couplings are included, even for systems where measured fusion cross sections may seem to be well reproduced by the linear coupling approximation.

## Key claim

The linear coupling approximation, which expands the coupling potential to first order in the deformation parameter, is inadequate for reproducing experimental fusion cross sections, average angular momenta, and barrier distributions in heavy-ion fusion reactions; higher-order couplings (up to all orders) are essential even for asymmetric systems where the linear approximation had previously been considered sufficient.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels equations for heavy-ion fusion reactions including finite excitation energies of vibrational states, without expanding the nuclear coupling potential in powers of the deformation parameter. They compared calculations using linear coupling, quadratic coupling (up to second order), and full-order coupling for the nuclear interaction, while treating the Coulomb coupling in the linear approximation after verifying that higher-order Coulomb terms produce negligible effects (less than 0.2% change in fusion cross section). The calculations were performed for the nearly symmetric systems ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹²,⁹⁶Zr and the asymmetric systems ¹⁶O + ¹¹²Cd, ¹⁴⁴Sm, using Woods-Saxon nuclear potentials and collective model form factors for surface vibrations.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹²Zr and ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹⁶Zr: the product Z_P Z_T is 1120; linear coupling calculations considerably underestimate fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies, while full-order coupling agrees very well with experimental data.
- For ¹⁶O + ¹¹²Cd: the product Z_P Z_T is 384; the coupling strength is several times smaller than for the symmetric systems, yet higher-order couplings produce non-negligible changes in fusion cross sections and average angular momenta.
- For ¹⁶O + ¹⁴⁴Sm: the deformation parameter β₃ = 0.205 for the octupole vibration at 1.81 MeV; higher-order couplings transfer strength from the lower barrier to the higher barrier and lower the energies of both barriers in the barrier distribution.
- For ⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni test case: second-order Coulomb coupling changes the fusion cross section by only about 0.2% over the energy range from about 10 MeV below to 10 MeV above the Coulomb barrier.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Esbensen and Landowne (1987) who showed that second-order coupling improves agreement for nickel isotope reactions, and by Rowley, Satchler, and Stelson (1991) who introduced the barrier distribution method. It addresses the failure of conventional linear-coupling coupled-channels calculations for heavy nearly symmetric systems noted by Vandenbosch (1992) and others, and demonstrates that full-order coupling resolves these discrepancies. The results enable more reliable interpretation of high-precision fusion data and barrier distributions, and indicate that standard coupled-channels codes should be revised to include couplings to all orders.
