# The LPM Effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: $1/N_c^2$ corrections

> An important question concerning in-medium high-energy parton showers in a quark-gluon plasma or other QCD medium is whether consecutive splittings of the partons in a given shower can be treated as quantum mechanically independent, or whether the formation times for two consecutive splittings instead have significant overlap. Various previous calculations of the effect of overlapping formation times have either (i) restricted attention to a soft bremsstrahlung limit, or else (ii) used the large-$N_c$ limit (where $N_c{=}3$ is the number of quark colors). In this paper, we make a first study of the accuracy of the large-$N_c$ limit used by those calculations of overlap effects that avoid a soft bremsstrahlung approximation. Specifically, we calculate the $1/N_c^2$ correction to previous $N_c{=}\infty$ results for overlap $g \to gg \to ggg$ of two consecutive gluon splittings $g \to gg$. At order $1/N_c^2$, there is interesting and non-trivial color dynamics that must be accounted for during the overlap of the formation times.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the $1/N_c^2$ corrections to previous $N_c=\infty$ results for overlapping formation time effects in purely gluonic $g \to gg \to ggg$ double splitting, finding that these corrections are comparable to the parametric estimate of $O(1/N_c^2) \sim 10\%$ when extrapolated to $N_c=3$, rather than being anomalously large or small.

## Method

The authors compute the $1/N_c^2$ corrections to the large-$N_c$ limit of overlapping formation time effects for $g \to ggg$ by expanding the SU($N$) color dynamics of the 4-gluon effective Hamiltonian in powers of $1/N$, using the $\hat q$ approximation for a static, homogeneous medium. They obtain analytic integral expressions for both "sequential diagram" and "crossed diagram" contributions, with three time integrals evaluated numerically, focusing exclusively on the purely gluonic process to isolate $1/N_c^2$ corrections from $1/N_c$ effects that would arise from quark pair production.

## Key numbers

- The parametric estimate for $1/N_c^2$ corrections at $N_c=3$ is $1/9 \sim 10\%$, which serves as the benchmark for whether the computed corrections are large, small, or comparable
- The $1/N_c^2$ corrections are computed for the process $g \to ggg$ with overlapping formation times, using the $\hat q$ approximation with $\hat q$ treated as fixed (not expanded in $1/N_c$)
- The relevant color singlet subspace for 4-gluon states in SU($N$) with $N>3$ is 6-dimensional, reducing to 5-dimensional for SU(3) where the $|\bm{0}\rangle$ representation decouples

## Context

This work extends the program of Arnold (2020) and Arnold (2021) that analyzed overlapping formation time effects for $g \to ggg$ in the large-$N_c$ limit, and uses the color formalism developed by Arnold (2022) for finite $N_c$. The calculation addresses whether the $N_c=\infty$ approximation used in those prior calculations is reasonable for the physical case $N_c=3$, and enables future work to incorporate these corrections into in-medium parton shower simulations for quark-gluon plasma. The paper leaves out direct 4-gluon vertex contributions studied by Arnold (2021) and effective 4-gluon vertices from light-cone gauge, whose $N_c=\infty$ contributions are not yet fully computed.
