# Spin of ground state baryons

> We calculate the quark spin contribution to the total angular momentum of flavor octet and flavor decuplet ground state baryons using a spin-flavor symmetry based parametrization method of quantum chromodynamics. We find that third order SU(6) symmetry breaking three-quark operators are necessary to explain the experimental result Sigma_1=0.32(10). For spin 3/2 decuplet baryons we predict that the quark spin contribution is Sigma_3=3.93(22), i.e. considerably larger than their total angular momentum.

# Spin of ground state baryons

## Key claim

Third-order SU(6) symmetry-breaking three-quark operators are necessary to explain the experimental result for the quark spin contribution to the proton spin; for flavor octet baryons the three-quark term cancels the one-quark term to yield Σ₁=0.35(12), while for flavor decuplet baryons the three-quark term enhances the one-quark contribution to give Σ₃=3.93(22), considerably larger than the total angular momentum of 3/2.

## Method

The authors use a general parametrization method based on broken SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry, originally developed by Morpurgo (1989), to formally map QCD matrix elements onto matrix elements in a basis of pure three-quark states with orbital angular momentum L=0. They decompose the flavor singlet axial vector current into one-quark and three-quark operators in spin-flavor space, with no two-quark contribution allowed by symmetry, and determine the unknown constants A and C from experimental deep inelastic scattering and hyperon beta-decay data for Δu and Δd.

## Key numbers

- Experimental quark spin contribution to the proton: Σ₁_exp = 0.32(10), from combined deep inelastic scattering and hyperon beta-decay data (Abe et al., 1998)
- Extracted one-quark operator constant: A = 1.15(7)
- Extracted three-quark operator constant: C = 0.08(1)
- Calculated quark spin contribution for flavor octet baryons: Σ₁ = 0.35(12), compared to the additive quark model value of 1
- Predicted quark spin contribution for flavor decuplet baryons: Σ₃ = 3.93(22), compared to the additive quark model value of 3
- Experimental spin fractions from deep inelastic scattering: Δu = 0.83 ± 0.03, Δd = -0.43 ± 0.03, Δs = -0.09 ± 0.04

## Context

This work extends the broken SU(6) parametrization method previously applied by the same authors to baryon electromagnetic moments and baryon-meson couplings (Buchmann and Henley, 2008). It contradicts the naive constituent quark model prediction that quark spins alone account for the full baryon spin, and resolves the "proton spin crisis" by showing that three-quark operators, which effectively describe quark-antiquark and gluon degrees of freedom, are essential. The prediction for decuplet baryon spin is new and not considered by other authors; the results enable future work on separating quark orbital angular momentum and gluon contributions to baryon total angular momentum.
