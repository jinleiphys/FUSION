# The Heavy Quark Potential as a Function of Shear Viscosity at Strong Coupling

> We determine finite temperature corrections to the heavy-quark (static) potential as a function of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in a strongly coupled, large-$N_c$ conformal field theory dual to five-dimensional Gauss-Bonnet gravity. We find that these corrections are even smaller than those predicted by perturbative QCD at distances relevant for small bound states in a deconfined plasma. Obtaining the dominant temperature and viscosity dependence of quarkonium binding energies will require a theory where conformal invariance is broken in such a way that the free energy associated with a single heavy quark is not just a pure entropy contribution.

## Key claim

The finite-temperature correction to the heavy-quark potential in a strongly coupled conformal field theory depends on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, but these corrections are even smaller than those predicted by perturbative QCD at distances relevant for small bound states in a deconfined plasma; obtaining the dominant temperature and viscosity dependence of quarkonium binding energies will require a theory where conformal invariance is broken.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence with a five-dimensional Gauss-Bonnet gravity dual to a strongly coupled, large-\(N_c\) conformal field theory. They compute the heavy-quark potential from the regularized Nambu-Goto action of a U-shaped string in the Gauss-Bonnet black brane background, expanding the result at small \(LT\) (where \(L\) is the quark separation and \(T\) the temperature) to obtain the leading finite-temperature correction as a function of the Gauss-Bonnet coupling \(\lambda_{GB}\), which is related to \(\eta/s\).

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in Gauss-Bonnet gravity is \(\eta/s = (1/(4\pi))(1 - 4\lambda_{GB})\), valid to all orders in \(\lambda_{GB}\).
* Causality in the boundary theory requires \(\lambda_{GB} \leq 9/100\), which implies \(4\pi\,\eta/s \geq 16/25\).
* The leading finite-temperature correction to the heavy-quark potential at small \(LT\) is proportional to \((LT)^4 / [\eta' (1+\eta')^3]\), where \(\eta' \equiv \sqrt{4\pi\,\eta/s}\).
* The ground state energy shift for quarkonium in the Gauss-Bonnet theory is of order \((T/M_Q)^4 \times 1/(\lambda^2 \eta'^4)\), with a numerical constant \(C \approx 3 \times 10^7\).
* The free energy of a single heavy quark is \(F_Q = -(\sqrt{\lambda}/(1+\eta'))\,T\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the heavy-quark potential in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (Maldacena 1998, Rey 1998) by incorporating curvature-squared corrections to the gravity dual, following the approach of Brigante (2007) and Kats (2007) who showed that \(\eta/s\) can violate the viscosity bound in such theories. The results are compared to resummed perturbative QCD (Dumitru 2009), where the screening corrections scale as \((m_D L)^2\) rather than \((LT)^4\). The authors note that lattice QCD data (Mocsy 2005) indicate a non-vanishing potential at infinite separation, which cannot be captured by conformal theories; constructing a gravity dual with broken conformal invariance (along the lines of Andreev 2006) would enable a more complete description of quarkonium binding energies.
