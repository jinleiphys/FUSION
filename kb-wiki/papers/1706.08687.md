# Precision Theoretical Analysis of Neutron Radiative Beta Decay to Order "O(α^2/π^2)"

> In the Standard Model (SM) we calculate the decay rate of the neutron radiative beta decay to order "O(α^2/π^2 ~ 10^{-5})", where "α$"is the fine--structure constant, and radiative corrections to order "O(α/π~ 10^{-3})". The obtained results together with the recent analysis of the neutron radiative beta decay to next-to-leading order in the large proton-mass expansion, performed by Ivanov et al. Phys. Rev. D95, 033007 (2017), describe recent experimental data by the RDK II Collaboration (Bales et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 116, 242501 (2016)) within 1.5 standard deviations. We argue a substantial influence of strong low-energy interactions of hadrons coupled to photons on the properties of the amplitude of the neutron radiative beta decay under gauge transformations of real and virtual photons.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the neutron radiative beta decay rate to order O(α²/π²) and radiative corrections to order O(α/π) in the Standard Model, but they acknowledge that the contributions from Feynman diagrams where real or virtual photons couple to hadronic blocks cannot be calculated in a model-independent way and require a special analysis that is deferred to a future publication.

## Method

The authors computed the amplitude and decay rate of the neutron radiative beta decay n → p + e⁻ + ν̄_e + γ using the Standard Model effective V-A weak interaction Lagrangian with axial coupling constant λ = -1.2750(9), including one-loop virtual photon exchanges to order O(α/π) and two-real-photon final states to order O(α²/π²). They worked to leading order in the large proton mass expansion, used the physical gauge for real photons where ε^*_λ'(k) = (0, ε⃗^*_λ'(k⃗)), and followed Sirlin's approach (1967) to separate gauge-invariant from gauge-non-invariant parts of the Feynman diagrams, absorbing constant gauge-non-invariant contributions into renormalization of the Fermi coupling constant G_F and the axial coupling constant λ.

## Key numbers

- The axial coupling constant used is λ = -1.2750(9), taken from Abele (2008).
- The Fermi coupling constant is G_F = 1.1664 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV⁻², and the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element is |V_ud| = 0.97417(21), from PDG (2016).
- The theoretical branching ratios from the tree-approximation analysis by Ivanov et al. (2017) were BR_βγ = 3.04 × 10⁻³ for photon energies 14 keV ≤ ω ≤ 782 keV and BR_βγ = 5.08 × 10⁻³ for 0.4 keV ≤ ω ≤ 14 keV, which agreed with the RDK II Collaboration experimental values of 3.35(16) × 10⁻³ and 5.82(66) × 10⁻³ within 2 and 1.2 standard deviations, respectively.
- The new results including radiative corrections to order O(α/π) describe the RDK II experimental data (Bales et al., 2016) within 1.5 standard deviations.

## Context

This work extends the tree-approximation analysis of neutron radiative beta decay by Ivanov et al. (2017) and the earlier calculations by Gaponov and Khafizov (1996), Bernard et al. (2004), and Ivanov et al. (2013), which neglected the contributions of Feynman diagrams where photons are emitted or absorbed by hadronic blocks. The authors follow the approach of Sirlin (1967) for handling radiative corrections to neutron beta decay, but note that unlike the case of ordinary beta decay, the diagrams with hadronic photon couplings in radiative beta decay cannot be shown to be energy-independent using current algebra, so a model-dependent analysis of these contributions is planned for a future publication.
