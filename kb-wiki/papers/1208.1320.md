# Deconfinement to Quark Matter in Neutron Stars - The Influence of Strong Magnetic Fields

> We use an extended version of the hadronic SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model that also includes quarks to study hybrid stars. Within this approach, the degrees of freedom change naturally as the temperature/density increases. Different prescriptions of charge neutrality, local and global, are tested and the influence of strong magnetic fields and the anomalous magnetic moment on the particle population is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that strong magnetic fields (up to ~10^19 G) in hybrid neutron stars can significantly alter the particle population, enhancing charged particle densities and producing spin polarization effects, but that the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) has only a modest net effect on total particle densities despite strongly affecting individual spin-state populations.

## Method

The authors employed an extended SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model in the mean-field approximation, incorporating both hadronic and quark degrees of freedom with a Polyakov loop potential modified to depend on baryon chemical potential. They imposed charge neutrality under both local and global prescriptions, and introduced a magnetic field in the z-direction that varies with baryon chemical potential, with Landau level quantization for charged particles and AMM splitting for all baryons.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic field: B_surf = 69.25 MeV^2 = 10^15 G (at baryon chemical potential μ_B = 938 MeV)
- Central magnetic field considered: B_c = 5×10^5 MeV^2 = 7.22×10^18 G
- Conversion factor: 1 MeV^2 = 1.44×10^13 G
- The effective magnetic field inside the star reaches only about 70% of the central value B_c
- The magnetic field parameterization uses a = 2.5 and b = -4.08×10^-4 (with μ_B in MeV)
- The highest observed surface magnetic field on magnetars is ~10^15 G
- Estimated possible central magnetic fields range from B = 10^18 to 10^20 G

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid star model (Dexheimer 2009) by incorporating strong magnetic fields and comparing local versus global charge neutrality prescriptions. It builds on prior studies of magnetic field effects in neutron stars by Bandyopadhyay (1997), Mao (2001), and Rabhi (2009), and complements similar analyses using different models by Menezes (2009, 2008) and others (2011). The results enable future calculations of magnetic field effects on chiral symmetry restoration and quark deconfinement at finite temperatures, contributing to a more complete understanding of the QCD phase diagram.
