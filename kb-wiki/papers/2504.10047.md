# Vibrational structure and symmetry in $^{110-116}$Cd

> We show that a vibrational interpretation and good U(5) symmetry are maintained for the majority of low-lying normal states in $^{110,112,114,116}$Cd isotopes, consistent with the empirical data. The observed deviations from this paradigm are properly treated by an interacting boson model Hamiltonian which breaks the U(5) symmetry in selected non-yrast states, while securing a weak mixing with coexisting SO(6)-like intruder states. The results demonstrate the relevance of the U(5) partial dynamical symmetry notion to this series of isotopes.

# Vibrational structure and symmetry in $^{110-116}$Cd

## Key claim
The authors show that a vibrational interpretation and good U(5) symmetry are maintained for the majority of low-lying normal states in $^{110,112,114,116}$Cd, but only when the symmetry is treated as partial (U(5)-PDS) rather than exact; the observed deviations from vibrational behavior are confined to selected non-yrast states and can be explained by weak mixing with coexisting SO(6)-like intruder states, without abandoning the spherical-vibrator picture.

## Method
The authors construct an interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) Hamiltonian combining a U(5) partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) Hamiltonian for the normal configuration (N bosons) with a quadrupole-plus-rotational Hamiltonian for the intruder configuration (N+2 bosons), connected by a mixing term. The U(5)-PDS Hamiltonian is built by adding to the standard U(5) dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian terms that annihilate a specific subset of basis states (class-A states with $n_d=\tau$), preserving exact U(5) symmetry for those states while allowing mixing in others. The model is applied to $^{110-116}$Cd with boson numbers N=7,8,9,8 respectively, and the calculated spectra and E2 transition rates are compared to experimental data.

## Context
This work addresses the so-called "Cd problem" identified by Garrett (2008) and Heyde and Wood (2011), where cadmium isotopes near neutron mid-shell showed unexpected deviations from the traditional spherical-vibrator interpretation and U(5) dynamical symmetry. Previous attempts by Garrett (2008, 2010, 2012) to explain discrepancies in E2 decays using strong mixing between vibrational and intruder states proved unsuccessful, leading some to abandon the spherical-vibrational picture in favor of multiple shape coexistence. The present approach extends the partial dynamical symmetry concept previously applied to SU(3)-DS by Leviatan (1996, 2013, 2016, 2020) and SO(6)-DS by Leviatan (2002), Ramos (2009), and Kremer (2014) to the U(5) chain of the IBM. The results enable a unified description of both normal spherical states and deformed intruder states in the cadmium chain, demonstrating that the vibrational interpretation can be maintained through the U(5)-PDS framework with weak normal-intruder mixing.
