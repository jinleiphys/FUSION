# Nuclear chart in covariant density functional theory with dynamical correlations: From Oxygen to Tin

> Nuclear masses of even-even nuclei with the proton number $8\leq Z\leq 50$ (O to Sn isotopes) from proton drip line to neutron drip line are investigated using the triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with the relativistic density functional PC-PK1, and the dynamical correlation energies (DCEs) associated with the rotational motion and the quadrupole shape vibrational motion are taken into account by the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) method. The root-mean-square deviation with respect to the experimental masses reduces from 2.50 MeV to 1.59 MeV after the consideration of DCEs. The inclusion of DCEs has little influence on the position of drip lines, and the predicted numbers of bound even-even nuclei between proton and neutron drip lines from O to Sn isotopes are respectively 569 and 564 for the cases with and without DCEs.

## Key claim

The inclusion of dynamical correlation energies (DCEs) from the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian method reduces the root-mean-square deviation of calculated nuclear masses from experimental values from 2.50 MeV to 1.59 MeV for even-even nuclei from Oxygen to Tin, but the abstract's claim of improvement is qualified by the fact that the DCEs are systematically larger than those from previous triaxial RMF+BCS calculations, leading to a larger rms deviation (1.47 MeV) for the 228 nuclei in common with that prior work (which achieved 1.23 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed large-scale deformation-constrained triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the PC-PK1 density functional in the particle-hole channel and a finite-range separable pairing force (G = -728 MeV) in the particle-particle channel, solving the RHB equation in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis. The resulting quasiparticle energies and wave functions were used to compute mass parameters, moments of inertia, and collective potentials for the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH), from which the dynamical correlation energy was defined as the energy difference between the lowest mean-field state and the 0₁⁺ state of the 5DCH.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation with respect to experimental masses: 2.50 MeV without DCEs, reduced to 1.59 MeV after including DCEs (for even-even nuclei with 8 ≤ Z ≤ 50 from proton to neutron drip line).
- Predicted number of bound even-even nuclei between proton and neutron drip lines from O to Sn: 569 without DCEs, 564 with DCEs.
- For the 228 nuclei with 8 ≤ Z ≤ 50 in common with Lu (2015), the rms deviation in the present work is 1.47 MeV, compared to 1.23 MeV in that prior study.
- The rms deviation between the DCEs from triaxial RHB and those from triaxial RMF+BCS is 0.53 MeV.
- Calculated DCEs range from 0 to 5 MeV, varying mainly in the region of 2.0 to 4.0 MeV.
- 15 nuclei are predicted to have triaxial deformation (γ ≠ 0°, 60°), mostly in Ge, Mo, and Ru isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the systematic inclusion of dynamical correlation energies in covariant density functional theory from the known-mass region (studied by Zhang (2014) and Lu (2015) using axial RMF+BCS and triaxial RMF+BCS, respectively) to the full nuclear landscape from proton to neutron drip lines for O through Sn isotopes. It uses the Bogoliubov transformation for pairing rather than the BCS approximation used in those prior works, which leads to systematically larger DCEs and enables comparison with drip-line predictions from spherical RCHB (Xia, 2018) and axial RHB (Agbemava, 2014). The results provide a foundation for building a complete nuclear mass table including triaxial degrees of freedom and dynamical correlations.
