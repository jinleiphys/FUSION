# Nuclear medium effects in Drell-Yan process

> We study nuclear medium effects in Drell-Yan processes at small target x using quark parton distribution functions and nucleon structure functions for a bound nucleon calculated in a microscopic nuclear model which takes into account the effect of Fermi motion, nuclear binding and nucleon correlations through a relativistic spectral function. The contributions of $π$ and $ρ$ mesons, target mass corrections and nuclear shadowing are also included. The results are compared with the theoretical and experimental results. The model is able to successfully explain the low target x results of E772 and E866 Drell-Yan experiments and is applicable to the forthcoming experimental analysis of E906 Sea Quest experiment at Fermi Lab.

## Key claim
The paper claims that nuclear medium effects in Drell-Yan processes at small target x (x_t > 0.1) can be successfully described by a microscopic model incorporating Fermi motion, nuclear binding, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions, and beam energy loss, but only when the energy loss parameter β is treated as a free parameter (set to 0.04) to achieve agreement with E772 and E866 data.

## Method
The authors calculate Drell-Yan cross sections for proton-induced lepton pair production on nuclear targets (Be, C, Ca, Fe, W) using a relativistic nucleon spectral function derived from the Lehmann representation of the Dirac propagator in nuclear matter, with local density approximation to describe finite nuclei. They convolve CTEQ6.6 nucleon parton distribution functions with the spectral function to obtain bound nucleon PDFs, add contributions from π and ρ meson clouds using in-medium meson propagators, and model beam energy loss via an exponential attenuation of beam parton energy with a constant fractional loss parameter β in a Glauber-type approach.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear structure effects suppress the Drell-Yan yield by 16-18% in the region 0.1 < x_t < 0.3
- Mesonic contributions increase the Drell-Yan ratio by approximately 18% for ¹²C, 25% for ⁴⁰Ca, 35% for ⁵⁶Fe, and 45% for ¹⁸⁴W in the same x_t range
- Beam energy loss with β=0.04 suppresses the yield by approximately 12% for ¹²C, 20% for ⁴⁰Ca, 25% for ⁵⁶Fe, and 35% for ¹⁸⁴W
- Calculations use Q² > 16 GeV² for E772 kinematics and Q² > 5 GeV² for SeaQuest kinematics at E_Lab = 120 GeV
- The deuteron effect on the ratio R is about 2%

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous microscopic nuclear model, which successfully described deep inelastic scattering of charged leptons and neutrinos from nuclei (Marco et al., 1996; Sajjad Athar et al., various years), to the Drell-Yan process. It addresses the longstanding difficulty of providing a consistent description of both DIS and Drell-Yan data with the same nuclear PDFs, as noted by Eskola et al. (2009) and Scheinbein et al. (2008). The results are compared with theoretical calculations by Close et al. (1984), Berger and Coester (1984), Jung and Miller (1990), and Brown et al. (1993), and are applicable to the forthcoming E906 SeaQuest experiment at Fermilab.
