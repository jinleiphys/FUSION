# Microscopic determination of the nuclear incompressibility within the non-relativistic framework

> The nuclear incompressibility $K_\infty$ is deduced from measurements of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) in medium-heavy nuclei, and the resulting value turns out to be model dependent. Since the considered nuclei have neutron excess, it has been suggested that the model dependence is due to the different behaviour of the symmetry energy in different models. To clarify this issue, we make a systematic and careful analysis based on new Skyrme forces which span a wide range of values for $K_\infty$, for the value of the symmetry energy at saturation and for its density dependence. By calculating, in a fully self-consistent fashion, the ISGMR centroid energy in $^{208}$Pb we reach, for the first time within the non-relativistic framework, three important conclusions: (i) the monopole energy, and consequently the deduced value of $K_\infty$, depend on a well defined parameter related to the shape of the symmetry energy curve and called $K_{sym}$; (ii) Skyrme forces of the type of SLy4 predict $K_\infty$ around 230 MeV, in agreement with the Gogny force (previous estimates using Skyrme interactions having been plagued by lack of full self-consistency); (iii) it is possible to build forces which predict $K_\infty$ around 250 MeV, although part of this increase is due to our poor knowledge of the density dependence and effective mass.

## Key claim

The nuclear incompressibility \(K_\infty\) extracted from the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) in \(^{208}\)Pb is not uniquely determined within the non-relativistic Skyrme framework; rather, the deduced value depends on the density dependence of the symmetry energy (parameterized by \(K_{sym}\)) and on the exponent \(\alpha\) of the Skyrme density-dependent term, so that values between 230–240 MeV and 250 MeV are both possible depending on the choice of force and on whether Coulomb exchange and center-of-mass corrections are included.

## Method

The authors constructed two families of new Skyrme effective interactions, one with density dependence exponent \(\alpha = 1/6\) (following the SLy4 protocol) and another with \(\alpha = 0.3563\) (matching the force SK255 of Agrawal et al. (2003)). For each family, they varied \(K_\infty\) (230, 240, 250 MeV for the first set; 250, 260, 270 MeV for the second) and the symmetry energy at saturation \(J\) (26–40 MeV and 28–42 MeV, respectively), while fitting ground-state observables (binding energies, charge radii, spin-orbit splitting, surface energy) of selected nuclei. They then calculated the ISGMR centroid energy \(E_{-1} = \sqrt{m_1/m_{-1}}\) in \(^{208}\)Pb using fully self-consistent constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF), which yields the inverse energy-weighted sum rule \(m_{-1}\) with an accuracy of \(\pm 3\%\) or better.

## Key numbers

- The experimental ISGMR centroid energy in \(^{208}\)Pb is \(14.17 \pm 0.28\) MeV (Youngblood et al., 1999).
- For the \(\alpha = 1/6\) force set, the preferred \(K_\infty\) is 230–240 MeV; for example, the force with \((K_\infty, J) = (230\ \text{MeV}, 28\ \text{MeV})\) reproduces the experimental monopole energy.
- For the \(\alpha = 0.3563\) force set, a value of \(K_\infty\) around 250 MeV (e.g., 250 MeV with \(J = 30\) MeV) can reproduce the experimental monopole energy, in agreement with the result of Agrawal et al. (2003).
- The difference in the extracted \(K_\infty\) between the two force families arises from: a change in the surface term of about 8 MeV, a more negative \(K_{sym}\) contributing about 5 MeV, and the neglect of Coulomb exchange and center-of-mass corrections (which lowers the monopole energy by about 150 keV, corresponding to about 5 MeV in \(K_\infty\)).
- The global error bar on \(K_\infty\) from experimental and theoretical uncertainties is \(\pm 12\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Colo and Van Giai (2004), which showed that fully self-consistent Skyrme calculations yield \(K_\infty \approx 235\) MeV, consistent with the Gogny force result, and resolves the discrepancy with the earlier non-self-consistent Skyrme estimates of 210–220 MeV. It directly addresses the claim of Agrawal et al. (2003) that a Skyrme force with \(K_\infty = 255\) MeV can fit the ISGMR in \(^{208}\)Pb, and shows that such a result is possible only by adopting a different density dependence (\(\alpha = 0.3563\)) and by omitting Coulomb exchange and center-of-mass corrections. The paper also contrasts with relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations (e.g., Vretenar et al., 2003; Piekarewicz, 2002), which predict \(K_\infty\) around 250–270 MeV, and concludes that the non-relativistic framework does not provide a unique value of \(K_\infty\) unless the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the treatment of certain terms in the functional are better constrained.
