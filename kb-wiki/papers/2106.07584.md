# Scaling in the short-time approximation

> We briefly review the concept of scaling and how it occurs in quasielastic electron and neutrino scattering from nuclei, and then the particular approach to scaling in the short-time approximation. We show that, while two-nucleon currents do significantly enhance the transverse electromagnetic response, they do not spoil scaling, but in fact enhance it. We provide scaling results obtained in the short-time approximation that verify this claim. The enhanced scaling is not ``accidental'' -- as claimed in Ref.~\cite{Benhar:2020jye} -- but rather reflects the dominant role played by pion exchange interactions and currents in the quasielastic regime.

## Key claim

While two-nucleon currents significantly enhance the transverse electromagnetic response in quasielastic scattering, they do not spoil scaling but in fact enhance it; this enhanced scaling is not "accidental" as claimed by Benhar (2020), but rather reflects the dominant role of pion exchange interactions and currents in the quasielastic regime.

## Method

The authors applied the short-time approximation (STA), a path-integral-based method that calculates electroweak response functions from one- and two-body off-diagonal density matrices and single- and two-nucleon currents. They computed transverse scaling functions for ^4He at various momentum transfers, first using only single-nucleon current operators and then including two-nucleon currents (primarily those due to pion exchange). The scaling properties of the STA response functions were then compared across momentum transfers to assess the quality of scaling.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal and transverse scaling functions extracted from ^12C data differ in magnitude by approximately 40% for momentum transfers in the range q = 400–600 MeV/c across the entire quasielastic region (citing Benhar, Day, and Sick (2008)).
- The momentum distribution in the range of k ≈ 2 fm^{-1} is dominated by the two-nucleon pion-exchange interaction (citing Schiavilla (2006) and Cruz-Torres (2019)).

## Context

This work extends the short-time approximation approach developed by Pastore (2020) and directly contradicts the claim by Benhar (2020) that the observed scaling in the presence of two-nucleon currents is "accidental." The paper demonstrates that the same pion-exchange dynamics governs both two-nucleon currents and two-nucleon interactions, making the enhanced scaling a natural consequence rather than a coincidence. This enables improved descriptions of quasielastic electron and neutrino scattering from nuclei using a unified scaling framework that accommodates two-nucleon current contributions.
