# Hydrodynamic Modeling of Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We review progress in the hydrodynamic description of heavy-ion collisions, focusing on recent developments in modeling the fluctuating initial state and event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations. We discuss how hydrodynamics can be used to extract information on fundamental properties of quantum-chromo-dynamics from experimental data, and review successes and challenges of the hydrodynamic framework.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, when combined with realistic fluctuating initial state models and event-by-event simulations, has become the standard framework for extracting transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma from heavy-ion collision data, but that precision determination of the temperature dependence of quantities like the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio remains an open challenge requiring further theoretical development.

## Method

The authors review the historical development of hydrodynamic modeling in heavy-ion collisions, from ideal hydrodynamics through second-order Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics to recent anisotropic and third-order approaches. They discuss the theoretical framework including conservation equations, the equation of state from lattice QCD, viscous corrections to particle distribution functions, and the coupling of hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade afterburners like UrQMD for the late dilute stage of the collision.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio extracted from flow measurements: $0.07 \leq \eta/s \leq 0.43$ (effective value at the typical time when flow is built up)
* AdS/CFT prediction for strongly coupled $N=4$ super Yang-Mills: $\eta/s = 1/4\pi$
* Pseudocritical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration from HotQCD: $T_c = 154 \pm 8$ (stat.) $\pm 1$ (sys.) MeV
* Pseudocritical temperature from Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration: $T_c = 155 \pm 3$ (stat.) $\pm 3$ (sys.) MeV
* HotQCD results systematically larger than Wuppertal-Budapest by approximately $25\%$ above $T_c$
* Switching temperature for hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade: $T = 170$ MeV
* For RHIC: 100 MUSIC events sampled 100 times each; for LHC: 1000 MUSIC events sampled 10 times each

## Context

This review extends the work of Kolb (2003), Huovinen (2003), and Hirano (2002) on ideal hydrodynamics, and the viscous hydrodynamic developments of Romatschke (2007), Luzum (2008), and Song (2008). It builds on the AdS/CFT calculation of Kovtun (2004) for the viscosity bound and the fluctuating initial state concepts from Alver (2006, 2010). The review enables future precision extractions of temperature-dependent transport coefficients by identifying open questions about early thermalization, the equation of state at finite baryon density, and the proper treatment of viscous corrections to distribution functions.
