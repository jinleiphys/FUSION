# A first step in the nuclear inverse Kohn-Sham problem: from densities to potentials

> Nuclear Density Functional Theory (DFT) plays a prominent role in the understanding of nuclear structure, being the approach with the widest range of applications. Hohenberg and Kohn theorems warrant the existence of a nuclear Energy Density Functional (EDF), yet its form is unknown. Current efforts to build a nuclear EDF are hindered by the lack of a strategy for systematic improvement. In this context, alternative approaches should be pursued and, so far, an unexplored avenue is that related to the inverse DFT problem. DFT is based on the one-to-one correspondence between Kohn-Sham (KS) potentials and densities. The exact EDF produces the exact density, so that from the knowledge of experimental or {\it ab initio} densities one may deduce useful information through reverse engineering. The idea has already been proven to be useful in the case of electronic systems. The general problem should be dealt with in steps, and the objective of the present work is to focus on testing algorithms to extract the Kohn-Sham potential within the simplest ansatz from the knowledge of the experimental neutron and proton densities. We conclude that while robust algorithms exist, the experimental densities present some critical aspects. Finally, we provide some perspectives for future works.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that algorithms originally developed for electronic systems can extract Kohn-Sham potentials from experimental nuclear densities, but the experimental densities present critical aspects that limit the reliability of the inversion, particularly in the nuclear interior and at large radii.

## Method

The authors implemented two independent inversion methods to extract the Kohn-Sham potential from given neutron and proton densities: an iterative procedure (vLB method) based on the algorithm of van Leeuwen and Baerends, and a constrained variational (CV) method that minimizes the kinetic energy subject to the density constraint using the IPOPT library. Both methods were first tested on theoretical Hartree-Fock densities generated with the SkX Skyrme interaction for the doubly-magic nuclei ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, then applied to experimental charge densities from electron scattering and neutron densities from antiprotonic atom data.

## Key numbers

- The ratio m*/m in ²⁰⁸Pb lies between 0.92 and 1 for protons and between 1 and 1.08 for neutrons, indicating mild non-locality for the SkX interaction.
- The convergence parameter γ in the vLB method was set to 1 MeV, which led to consistent results.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter used for starting guesses was ℏω = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV.
- The convergence condition for the vLB method was set as ΔU^(k) < α, where ΔU^(k) is the maximum absolute variation of the potential between iterations.

## Context

This work represents the first attempt to address the inverse Kohn-Sham problem in nuclear physics, following similar approaches that have proven useful in electronic systems as reviewed by Jensen and Wasserman (2018). The paper extends the methodology of van Leeuwen and Baerends (1994) and the constrained variational approach from the same review, applying them to nuclear densities for the first time. The results enable future work to reconstruct the nuclear energy density functional from the extracted potentials, potentially using ab initio calculations of neutron drops to provide density paths for the van Leeuwen-Baerends reconstruction formula.
