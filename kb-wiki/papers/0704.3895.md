# SPI observations of the diffuse 60Fe emission in the Galaxy

> Gamma-ray line emission from radioactive decay of 60Fe provides constraints on nucleosynthesis in massive stars and supernovae. The spectrometer SPI on board INTEGRAL has accumulated nearly three years of data on gamma-ray emission from the Galactic plane. We have analyzed these data with suitable instrumental-background models and sky distributions to produce high-resolution spectra of Galactic emission. We detect the gamma-ray lines from 60Fe decay at 1173 and 1333 keV, obtaining an improvement over our earlier measurement of both lines with now 4.9 sigma significance for the combination of the two lines. The average flux per line is (4.4 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-5} ph cm^{-2} s^{-1} rad^{-1} for the inner Galaxy region. Deriving the Galactic 26Al gamma-ray line flux with using the same set of observations and analysis method, we determine the flux ratio of 60Fe/26Al gamma-rays as 0.148 \pm 0.06. The current theoretical predictions are still consistent with our result.

## Key claim

The paper reports a detection of gamma-ray lines from radioactive decay of 60Fe in the Galaxy with 4.9 sigma significance, but the signal is only marginally significant (below 3 sigma) in each of the four individual spectra, and the result depends on assuming that the sky distribution of 60Fe matches that of 26Al as measured by COMPTEL.

## Method

The authors analyzed 2.5 years of data from the SPI spectrometer on the INTEGRAL spacecraft, covering December 2002 to August 2005. They constructed instrumental background models using tracers such as GEDSAT rates and a 60Co radioactivity build-up model, then fitted the data with a maximum-likelihood method combining background models with a spatial model for sky emission based on the COMPTEL 26Al map. Separate fits were performed for single-detector events and multiple-detector events at both the 1173 keV and 1333 keV 60Fe lines, producing four independent spectra that were then combined.

## Key numbers

* Detection significance of 4.9 sigma for the combination of the two 60Fe lines (1173 and 1333 keV) from the combined spectrum
* Average flux per line: (4.4 ± 0.9) × 10^{-5} ph cm^{-2} s^{-1} rad^{-1} for the inner Galaxy region
* Flux ratio of 60Fe/26Al gamma-rays: 0.148 ± 0.06 (14.8 ± 6.0%)
* For single events: line flux of (4.2 ± 1.6) × 10^{-5} ph cm^{-2} s^{-1} rad^{-1} at 1173 keV and (3.5 ± 1.5) × 10^{-5} ph cm^{-2} s^{-1} rad^{-1} at 1333 keV
* For multiple events: line flux of (5.8 ± 1.9) × 10^{-5} ph cm^{-2} s^{-1} rad^{-1} at 1173 keV and (5.2 ± 2.1) × 10^{-5} ph cm^{-2} s^{-1} rad^{-1} at 1333 keV
* Upper limits for 60Fe gamma-rays from Cygnus and Vela regions: ~1.1 × 10^{-5} ph cm^{-2} s^{-1} (2 sigma)

## Context

This work improves upon earlier 60Fe measurements from RHESSI (Smith 2004) with 2.6 sigma significance and from the first year of SPI data (Harris 2005) with ~3 sigma significance, providing the most significant detection to date. The measured 60Fe/26Al flux ratio of 0.148 ± 0.06 is consistent with theoretical predictions from Timmes (1995), Prantzos (2004), and Limongi and Chieffi (2006), and the result enables further constraints on nucleosynthesis models for massive stars and core-collapse supernovae, as well as the question of whether 60Fe and 26Al share common production sites.
