# Revisiting the U_A(1) problems

> We survey various U_A(1) problems and attempt to resolve the two puzzles related to the eta mesons that have experimental verification. Specifically, we first explore the Goldstone structure of the eta and eta' mesons in the context of eta-eta' mixing using ideas based on QCD. Then we study the eta decays eta->3pi0, eta'->3pi0 and eta'->eta pi pi. Finally we arrive at essentially the same picture in the dynamical scheme based on consistently coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter integral equations. This chirally well-behaved bound-state approach clarifies the distinction between the usual axial-current decay constants and the gamma gamma decay constants in the eta-eta' complex. Allowing for the effects of the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking in the quark-antiquark annihilation, leads to the improved eta-eta' mass matrix.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the two U_A(1) problems related to the eta mesons can be resolved through a consistent treatment of eta-eta' mixing, using both phenomenological input from meson decays and a dynamical Schwinger-Dyson/Bethe-Salpeter approach, with the caveat that the gluon axial anomaly contribution to the singlet mass is parameterized rather than calculated from first principles.

## Method

The authors first analyze the Goldstone boson structure of the eta and eta' mesons using eta-eta' mixing in the nonstrange-strange basis, fitting the annihilation strength parameter beta and the quark mass ratio X from the diagonalized mass matrix. They then compute the eta to 3pi0, eta' to 3pi0, and eta' to eta pi pi decay rates using PCAC consistency, the Coleman-Glashow quark tadpole for the Delta I=1 transition, and a linear sigma model for the scalar meson pole contributions. Finally, they show consistency with a coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach that incorporates dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and reproduces the gamma gamma decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

*   Eta mass: 547 MeV; eta' mass: 958 MeV (from PDG data).
*   Eta_8 octet mass from Gell-Mann-Okubo formula: approximately 567 MeV.
*   Eta_0 singlet mass from trace relation: approximately 947 MeV.
*   Annihilation strength beta = 0.28 GeV^2; quark mass ratio X = 0.78 (from diagonalization of the mass matrix).
*   Nonstrange-strange mixing angle phi = 41.9 degrees (from kinematic QCD mixing relation).
*   Predicted eta to 3pi0 decay rate: 377 eV, compared to experimental value 380 +/- 36 eV.
*   Predicted eta' to 3pi0 decay rate: 377 eV, compared to experimental value 313 +/- 58 eV.
*   Predicted eta' to eta pi pi decay rate: 130 keV, compared to experimental value 131 +/- 8 keV.
*   Constituent quark mass from SD-BS approach: M_{u,d}(0) = 375 MeV (with explicit breaking bare mass 3.1 MeV, giving m_pi = 140.4 MeV); M_s(0) = 610 MeV.
*   Best fit mixing angle from gamma gamma decays in SD-BS approach: theta = -12 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of eta-eta' mixing and U_A(1) problems previously addressed by Glashow, Weinberg, Crewther, 't Hooft, and Witten, and provides a unified treatment using both phenomenological mass matrix methods and the dynamical Schwinger-Dyson/Bethe-Salpeter approach of Jain and Munczek (1993). The results are consistent with the two-mixing-angle scheme of Feldmann, Kroll, and Stech (1998-1999) and with the empirical mixing angle determinations from decays. The paper enables a consistent description of the eta meson complex that reconciles the Goldstone boson nature of the eta with the nonvanishing singlet mass required by the U_A(1) anomaly, and provides a framework for computing hadronic decay rates that agree with experimental data.
