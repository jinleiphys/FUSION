# An Investigation of the Bound State Solutions of the Klein-Gordon Equation for the Generalized Woods-Saxon Potential in Spin Symmetry and Pseudo-spin Symmetry Limits

> Recently, scattering of a Klein-Gordon particle in the presence of mixed scalar-vector generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential was investigated for the spin symmetric and the pseudo-spin symmetric limits in one spatial dimension. In this manuscript, the bound state solutions of the Klein-Gordon equation with mixed scalar-vector generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential are examined analytically within the framework of spin and pseudo-spin symmetry limits. We prove that the occurrence of bound state energy spectrum exists only in the spin symmetric limit, while in the pseudo-spin symmetric limit, the bound state spectrum does not exist. Besides the theoretical proof, the Newton-Raphson numerical methods are used to calculate the bound state energy spectra of a neutral Kaon particle, confined in a generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential, energy well constituted with repulsive or attractive surface interactions, for the spin and pseudo-spin symmetric limits, respectively. Numerical results are consistent with the non-existence of the bound state energy spectrum in the pseudo-spin symmetric limit.

## Key claim

The paper proves analytically that bound state solutions of the Klein-Gordon equation for the generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential exist only in the spin symmetry limit, while in the pseudo-spin symmetry limit the bound state spectrum does not exist, contradicting any expectation that both limits would support bound states.

## Method

The author solves the time-independent Klein-Gordon equation in one spatial dimension for the generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential under spin symmetry and pseudo-spin symmetry limits, using a coordinate transformation to map the problem onto a hypergeometric differential equation. The bound state conditions are derived from requiring real wave numbers for confinement, and the quantized energy spectra are obtained by applying continuity conditions at the potential center and using the Newton-Raphson method for numerical evaluation. The analysis is performed for a neutral Kaon particle confined in potential wells with both repulsive and attractive surface interactions.

## Key numbers

- Neutral Kaon rest mass energy: \(m_0c^2 = 497.648\ \text{MeV}\)
- For a potential with repulsive surface effects (\(W = 2 m_0c^2\), \(V_0 = m_0c^2/2\), \(\alpha = 1\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(L = 6\ \text{fm}\)), the bound state energies range from \(E_0 = 33.962\ \text{MeV}\) to \(E_8 = 457.746\ \text{MeV}\) for nine states
- For the same repulsive surface parameters but \(V_0 = m_0c^2\), sixteen bound states are found with energies from \(E_0 = -410.270\ \text{MeV}\) to \(E_{15} = 486.799\ \text{MeV}\)
- For \(V_0 = 3m_0c^2/2\) with repulsive surface, twenty-one bound states are found with energies from \(E_0 = -493.730\ \text{MeV}\) to \(E_{20} = 463.891\ \text{MeV}\)
- The surface barrier height for the repulsive case with \(V_0 = m_0c^2/2\) and \(W = 2 m_0c^2\) is \(139.963\ \text{MeV}\) (calculated as \(9/32\ m_0c^2\))

## Context

This work extends the author's previous scattering study of Klein-Gordon particles with the generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential (Lutfuoglu, 2018) to bound state solutions, and builds on the spin symmetry and pseudo-spin symmetry framework established by Ginocchio (1997, 1999) and Meng et al. (2013) for relativistic mean field theory. The result that bound states are absent in the pseudo-spin symmetry limit is a new finding that contrasts with the existence of bound states in the spin symmetry limit, and it enables future studies of confinement conditions for scalar particles in nuclear potentials with surface interactions.
