# Electromagnetic field evolution in relativistic heavy-ion collision and its effect on flow of particles

> We compute the electromagnetic fields generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using the iEBE-VISHNU framework. We calculated the incremental drift velocity from the possible four sources of the electric force (coulomb, Lorentz, Faraday, and Plasma-based) on the particles created. The effect of this external electromagnetic field on the flow harmonics of particles was investigated, and we found out that the flow harmonics values get suppressed and rouse in a non-uniform fashion throughout the evolution. More precisely, a maximum of close to three percent increase in elliptic flow was observed. We also found mass more dominant factor than charges for the change in flow harmonics due to the created electromagnetic field. On the top of that, the magnetic field perpendicular to the reaction plane is found to be sizable while the different radial electric forces were found to cancel out each other. Finally, we found out that the inclusion of an electromagnetic field affects the flow of particles by suppressing or rising it in a non-uniform fashion throughout the evolution.

## Key claim

The inclusion of electromagnetic field evolution in the iEBE-VISHNU framework suppresses or raises flow harmonics in a non-uniform fashion throughout the evolution, with a maximum of close to three percent increase in elliptic flow observed; the effect is mass-dependent rather than charge-dependent, and the different radial electric forces (Coulomb, Lorentz, Faraday, and plasma-based) largely cancel each other out.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid framework with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, (2+1)-d viscous Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics (VISHNew with s95p-v1-PCE equation of state), and a Cooper-Frye particle sampler (iSS) with UrQMD afterburner to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV. They solved Maxwell's equations with four constant electrical conductivity values (0.023, 0.4, 0.66, and 1.1 fm^-1) to compute electromagnetic field evolution, then calculated the incremental drift velocity from the force balance equation (including drag) and added it to the hydrodynamic flow velocity before particle sampling.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s) = 2.76 ATeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Centrality: 20-30% (impact parameter range 6.24 fm < b < 9.05 fm)
- Electrical conductivity values used: sigma = 0.023 fm^-1, 0.4 fm^-1, 0.66 fm^-1, and 1.1 fm^-1
- Maximum observed elliptic flow increase: close to 3% (2.7% for pions at low momentum)
- Proton elliptic flow suppressed up to 2.37% from its initial value; at lower momentum suppressed to 1.5% of initial value
- Pion elliptic flow raised by 2.7% at lower momentum
- Triangular flow change (Delta V_3) for neutron, xsi, antiproton, lambda, and anti kaon is on the order of 10^-4

## Context

This work extends the approach of Shen (2018) by extending the drift velocity calculation for more charge types and evaluating effects for different identified particles (protons, pions, kaons, lambda, xsi). It confirms the inhomogeneous electromagnetic field structure found by Deng (2012) using the HIJING model, and agrees with results from Zhong (2014) on flow harmonics changes for heavier particles. The study enables future work to replace the constant electrical conductivity assumption with a temperature-dependent function and to include additional contributors to elliptic flow change in functional form.
