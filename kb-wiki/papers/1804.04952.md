# Crust breaking and the limiting rotational frequency of neutron stars

> The limiting rotational frequency of neutron stars may be determined by the strength of their crusts. As a star spins up from accretion, centrifugal forces will cause the crust to fail. If the crust breaks unevenly, a rotating mass quadrupole moment will radiate gravitational waves (GW). This radiation can prevent further spin up and may be a promising source for continuous GW searches. We calculate that for a breaking strain (strength) of neutron star crust that is consistent with molecular dynamics simulations, the crust may fail at rotational frequencies in agreement with observations.

## Key claim

The limiting rotational frequency of neutron stars may be set by the strength of the crust, with the crust predicted to fail at rotational frequencies of 300-700 Hz for a breaking strain of order 0.1, consistent with the maximum observed spin frequencies; however, the crust of very compact stars or most isolated neutron stars may never break at all.

## Method

The authors model the neutron star as a two-component homogeneous spheroid with a solid crust of uniform density on top of an incompressible fluid core, following the formalism of Franco (2000). They solve for the displacement field in the crust using boundary conditions at the outer and inner crust boundaries, then compute the strain tensor and the strain angle (difference between largest and smallest eigenvalues) to determine when the local strain reaches the critical breaking strain. Four different equations of state are used to bracket uncertainties in neutron star structure: HLPSsoft, HLPSstiff, IU-FSU, and IU-FSUmax, which predict different radii and crust thicknesses for a given stellar mass.

## Key numbers

- For a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star with the HLPSstiff equation of state and breaking strain sigma = 0.1, the crust will never break if the star is born with rotational frequency less than or equal to 460 Hz (spin period greater than or equal to 2.17 ms).
- The maximum initial frequency for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star whose crust never fails from slowing down ranges from 460 Hz (HLPSstiff) to 769 Hz (HLPSsoft) for sigma = 0.1.
- For accreting neutron stars in the IU-FSU model with sigma = 0.1, a 1.4 solar mass star requires spin-up from 781 Hz to 947 Hz before the crust fails when fully replaced.
- The required breaking strain for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star to reach the maximum observed frequency of 716.36 Hz lies between 0.008 and 0.089.
- The transverse sound speed in most of the crust is approximately 10^6 m/s.
- The crustal mass ranges from about 0.01 solar masses for compact stars to 0.04 solar masses for stars with thick crusts.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of quaking neutron stars from Franco (2000) to consider the much larger breaking strain (sigma approximately 0.1) found in molecular dynamics simulations by Horowitz (2009) and Chugunov (2010), which is orders of magnitude larger than the earlier estimates of 10^-4 to 10^-2 used by Cutler (2002). The crust-breaking mechanism proposed here is distinct from Bildsten's (1998) idea of deformations from nonuniform electron captures and does not require a temperature gradient. The results suggest that accreting neutron stars with failed crusts could be promising sources for continuous gravitational wave searches with Advanced LIGO, VIRGO, and third-generation detectors.
