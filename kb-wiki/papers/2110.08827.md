# Nature of $X(2370)$

> We address the nature of the $X(2370)$ resonance observed in the $J/ψ$ radiative decays, $J/ψ\rightarrowγK^{+} K^{-}η'$, $J/ψ\rightarrowγK_S K_Sη'$ and $J/ψ\rightarrowγπ^{+}π^{-}η'$. By studying the invariant mass spectra we confirm that decays of the $X(2370)$ into three pseudo-scalars are well described by an effective chiral Lagrangian. We extract the branching ratio of $J/ψ\to X(2370)γ$ and show that it is an order of magnitude larger compared to the glueball production rate predicted by lattice QCD. This indicates that $X(2370)$ is not likely to be a glueball candidate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $X(2370)$ resonance is not likely to be a glueball, based on the finding that the extracted branching ratio for $J/\psi \to \gamma X(2370)$ is approximately one order of magnitude larger than the pure glueball production rate predicted by quenched lattice QCD. This conclusion depends on the assumption that the effective chiral Lagrangian description of the $X(2370)$ decays into three pseudoscalars is correct and that the lattice QCD prediction for the glueball production rate is reliable.

## Method

The authors construct decay amplitudes for $J/\psi \to \gamma X(2370) \to \gamma PPP$ (where $PPP$ represents three pseudoscalar mesons) using an effective chiral Lagrangian that couples the $X(2370)$ to nonets of scalar and pseudoscalar mesons. The branching ratios ${\rm Br}[X \to PPP]$ are computed from these amplitudes, and then combined with experimental measurements of ${\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma X \to \gamma PPP]$ from BESIII data to extract ${\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma X]$ via the relation ${\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma X] = {\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma X \to \gamma PPP] / {\rm Br}[X \to PPP]$. Two analyses (Sol. I and Sol. II) are performed with different treatments of the scalar meson decays into two pseudoscalars, and a combined fit of branching ratios and invariant mass spectra is also carried out.

## Key numbers

*   Mass of $X(2370)$ from $J/\psi \to \gamma \pi^+ \pi^- \eta'$: $M = 2376.3 \pm 8.7({\rm stat})^{+3.2}_{-4.3}({\rm syst})$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 83 \pm 17({\rm stat})^{+44}_{-6}({\rm syst})$ MeV (BESIII, 6.4$\sigma$ significance).
*   Mass of $X(2370)$ from $J/\psi \to \gamma \bar{K}K\eta'$: $M = 2341.6 \pm 6.5({\rm stat}) \pm 5.7({\rm syst})$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 117 \pm 10({\rm stat}) \pm 8({\rm syst})$ MeV (BESIII, 8.3$\sigma$ significance).
*   Lattice QCD prediction for the pseudoscalar glueball mass: $M_G \simeq 2395 \pm 14$ MeV (Gui 2019).
*   Extracted ${\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma X]$ from the analysis: $2.87 \pm 0.68 \times 10^{-3}$ (Sol. I) and $3.95 \pm 0.71 \times 10^{-3}$ (Sol. II).
*   Lattice QCD prediction for the pure glueball production rate: ${\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma G] = 0.231 \pm 0.090 \times 10^{-3}$ (Gui 2019).
*   Phenomenological estimate for a mixed glueball-$q\bar{q}$ state: ${\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma X] = 0.487 \pm 0.143 \times 10^{-3}$.
*   Combined fit of mass spectra yields ${\rm Br}[J/\psi \to \gamma X] = 3.26 \pm 0.81 \times 10^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies (Eshraim 2012, Qin 2017) that considered the $X(2370)$ as a possible pseudoscalar glueball candidate by incorporating production characteristics from $J/\psi$ radiative decays. It directly compares the experimentally extracted branching ratio for $J/\psi \to \gamma X(2370)$ with the pure glueball production rate from quenched lattice QCD calculations (Gui 2019, Morningstar 1999, Chen 2005). The result contradicts the glueball interpretation and suggests the $X(2370)$ has a dominant $q\bar{q}$ component. This enables future experimental studies by BESIII and BelleII to further investigate the nature of the $X(2370)$ through measurements of its decay branching ratios.
