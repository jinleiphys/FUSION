# (ΩΩ)_{0^+} dibaryon productions in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy \sqrt {s_{NN}} =130GeV

> Based on the measured transverse mass spectra of π^-, K^- and \bar p at the RHIC energy \sqrt {s_{NN}} =130GeV, di-omega productions from baryon-baryon reactions in hadronic matter are studied. Results about the (ΩΩ)_{0^+} total number per event show that the deeply bound state (ΩΩ)_{0^+} can be observed at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The deeply bound dibaryon state (ΩΩ)_{0+} can be observed at RHIC energies, but the predicted yield per central Au+Au collision at √sNN = 130 GeV is very small, of the order 10^{-9} to 10^{-7}, requiring large event accumulations for detection.

## Method

The authors used measured transverse mass spectra of π^-, K^- and antiprotons from RHIC to constrain hadron distributions via the Cooper-Frye formula, hydrodynamic equations, and baryon number conservation. They then calculated (ΩΩ)_{0+} production from direct baryon-baryon reactions (Ω+Ω → (ΩΩ)_{0+}+γ or +η) and indirect processes involving the intermediate (NΩ)_{2+} dibaryon (Ω+(NΩ)_{2+} → (ΩΩ)_{0+}+N), using cross sections from the chiral SU(3) quark model. The Ω^- yield at RHIC was estimated by scaling CERN-SPS data using the antiproton ratio between the two energies.

## Key numbers

- Total (ΩΩ)_{0+} number per event ranges from 6.63×10^{-9} to 6.35×10^{-7} depending on the assumed transverse velocity profile (Eqs. 12-14).
- Direct electromagnetic process (Ω+Ω → (ΩΩ)_{0+}+γ) contributes N1 = 9.29×10^{-10} to 1.13×10^{-8} per event.
- Direct mesonic process (Ω+Ω → (ΩΩ)_{0+}+η) contributes N2 = 2.27×10^{-9} to 2.92×10^{-8} per event.
- Indirect process (Ω+(NΩ)_{2+} → (ΩΩ)_{0+}+N) contributes N5 = 3.43×10^{-9} to 5.95×10^{-7} per event.
- The (ΩΩ)_{0+} binding energy in vacuum is about 100 MeV.
- The ratio C = dN_{Ω^-,SPS}/dy divided by dN_{pbar,SPS}/dy is approximately 0.01.
- Freeze-out temperature T = 0.1 GeV is common for π^-, K^- and antiprotons.
- Average transverse velocities for the three parametrizations are approximately 0.48c, 0.67c, and 0.83c.
- Hadronic matter freeze-out time τ_fo = 10 fm/c with transverse radius R(τ_fo) = 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends the search for dibaryon bound states from hadron-hadron collisions to ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, following the prediction of a deeply bound (ΩΩ)_{0+} state in the chiral SU(3) quark model by Yu, Zhang, and collaborators (1999-2001). It builds on earlier dibaryon studies including Jaffe's H dibaryon prediction (1977) and the AGS Experiment 896 search in Au+Au collisions. The results enable experimental searches for the (ΩΩ)_{0+} dibaryon at RHIC, though the predicted yields are extremely small and require high statistics. The authors note that in-medium effects on the binding energy and cross sections remain to be explored in future work.
