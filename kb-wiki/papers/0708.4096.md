# Non-Abelian Strings in High Density QCD: Zero Modes and Interactions

> The most fundamental strings in high density color superconductivity are the non-Abelian semi-superfluid strings which have color gauge flux tube but behave as superfluid vortices in the energetic point of view. We show that in addition to the usual translational zero modes, these vortices have normalizable orientational zero modes in the internal space, associated with the color-flavor locking symmetry broken in the presence of the strings. The interaction among two parallel non-Abelian semi-superfluid strings is derived for general relative orientational zero modes to show the universal repulsion. This implies that the previously known superfluid vortices, formed by spontaneously broken U(1)_B, are unstable to decay. Moreover, our result proves the stability of color superconductors in the presence of external color gauge fields.

## Key claim

The paper shows that non-Abelian semi-superfluid strings in high-density QCD possess normalizable orientational zero modes in the internal space, and that the static force between two such parallel strings is universally repulsive and independent of their relative orientation, implying that previously known superfluid vortices formed by spontaneously broken U(1)_B are unstable to decay.

## Method

The authors construct a Ginzburg-Landau Lagrangian for an N x N matrix field Phi representing the pairing gap in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, with symmetry G = SU(N)_C x SU(N)_F x U(1)_B. They derive the cylindrically symmetric single-string solution and its gauge field configuration, then obtain the general string with arbitrary orientation in the CP^{N-1} internal space via a flavor SU(2)_F rotation. Using the Abrikosov ansatz for two well-separated parallel strings, they compute the interaction energy density by subtracting the individual string energies from the total configuration energy and integrate to obtain the force.

## Key numbers

* The kinetic energy density at large distance for a single non-Abelian string is F_kin = 1/(N rho^2), which is 1/N of that of the global U(1)_B string.
* The interaction energy density between two strings is F(rho,theta,a) = +/- (2/N) * [(-a^2 + rho^2) / (a^4 + rho^4 - 2 a^2 rho^2 cos(2 theta))].
* The force per unit length between two strings is f = +/- (2 pi)/(N a) for a << L -> infinity, where the upper sign is for string-string (repulsive) and the lower sign for string-anti-string (attractive).

## Context

This work extends the study of non-Abelian strings in supersymmetric QCD (Auzzi 2003, Eto 2006) and in superstring theory (Hanany 2003) to the context of high-density color superconductivity. It contradicts the expectation from global non-Abelian strings (Nakano, Nitta, Matsuura 2007) where the static force depends on orientation; here the force is orientation-independent. The result enables the conclusion that the superfluid vortices of U(1)_B breaking found by Forbes (2001) and Iida (2002) are unstable to decay into N non-Abelian strings, and it proves the stability of color superconductors in the presence of external color gauge fields regardless of whether they are type I or II.
