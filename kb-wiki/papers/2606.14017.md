# Meson molecules in strong magnetic fields: non-monotonic evolution of the charged pion and kaon energies

> In strong magnetic fields, charged quarks occupy the lowest Landau level, leading to an effective dimensional reduction of hadronic dynamics. This dimensional reduction naturally generates a hierarchy of scales, separating fast intra-meson quark dynamics from slow collective meson motion; this motivates a Born-Oppenheimer description of meson-meson systems. Our Born-Oppenheimer analysis shows that the infrared behavior is controlled by the interplay between dimensional reduction and the structure of the meson-meson interaction, leading to three distinct regimes: scattering-dominated, molecular, and compact multiquark states. Charged pseudoscalar mesons such as $π_+$ and $K_+$ provide a particularly interesting realization of this framework, as their lattice spectra at large magnetic fields suggest the emergence of loosely bound states near the boundary between scattering and molecular regimes. Our results suggest that strong magnetic fields provide a useful laboratory for exploring the emergence and classification of hadronic bound states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strong magnetic fields cause an effective dimensional reduction of two-meson dynamics, which promotes the formation of mesonic molecules even when the inter-meson attraction is weak; the authors claim this mechanism provides a natural explanation for the non-monotonic energy evolution of charged pions and kaons observed in lattice QCD, but the analysis is performed within a non-relativistic quark model and the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, not from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The author constructs a non-relativistic quark model Hamiltonian for neutral and charged mesons in a strong magnetic field, treating the transverse dynamics as quenched due to the lowest Landau level dominance. A Born-Oppenheimer separation is applied to two-meson systems, where the fast intra-meson quark dynamics are solved first, and the slow collective meson motion is reduced to an effective one-dimensional problem along the magnetic field direction. The resulting one-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation is then analyzed to determine the conditions for bound state formation.

## Key numbers

* The QCD scale is given as Lambda_QCD approximately 200-300 MeV.
* The finite-B energies of constituent mesons show an energy reduction by approximately 30% at eB approximately 1 GeV^2, taken from lattice calculations by Bali (2017) and Ding (2026).
* The lattice simulation for pi_+ uses a zero-field pion mass m_pi^{B=0} approximately 220 MeV, from Ding (2020).
* The transverse effective mass M_B for neutral mesons scales as M_B approximately B / Lambda_QCD for a Coulomb-like short-distance potential.

## Context

This work extends the study of hadronic molecules and compact multiquark states, which have been extensively investigated in the heavy-quark sector (Guo 2017, Esposito 2016, Lebed 2016), into the regime of strong magnetic fields. The author argues that previous lattice simulations of charged pions and kaons in strong magnetic fields (Ding 2020, Ding 2026) show puzzling non-monotonic energy behavior that cannot be explained by a simple q-qbar picture (Taya 2014, Kojo 2012), and that the molecular interpretation proposed here resolves this puzzle. The framework enables systematic classification of hadronic states in strong magnetic fields into scattering-dominated, molecular, and compact multiquark regimes, and may have implications for understanding exotic hadrons at zero magnetic field.
