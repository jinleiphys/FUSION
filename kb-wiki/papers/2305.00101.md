# Chiral symmetry restoration in a rotating medium

> We study the nature of the chiral symmetry restoration within the Yukawa model with spontaneous symmetry breaking. We work with scalar and fermion fields which are subject to the effects of a rotating system. In this work, we show the derivation of the scalar field propagator in a rotating medium using the Fock-Schwinger proper-time method. We compute analytically the effective potential in the high-temperature approximations, including the contribution of the ring diagrams to account for the plasma screening properties. We study the chiral transition as we vary the angular velocity $Ω$, the boson self-coupling $λ$ and the fermion-boson coupling $g$. We show that the critical temperature for the restoration of chiral symmetry always starts with decreasing behaviour, until it reaches a minimum and from there when increasing $Ω$, we observe $T_c$ increases monotonically. In all the phase transition lines in the $T-Ω$ plane reported, we obtain that the rotating effects are able to change the order of the phase transition.

## Key claim
The critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration in a rotating Yukawa model always first decreases with increasing angular velocity Ω, reaches a minimum, and then increases monotonically; the rotating effects can change the order of the phase transition, but this behavior depends on the specific values of the boson self-coupling λ and the fermion-boson coupling g.

## Method
The authors derived the scalar field propagator in a rotating medium using the Fock-Schwinger proper-time method, solving the Klein-Gordon equation for bosons rigidly rotating inside a cylinder with radius R (satisfying RΩ < 1 for causality). They computed the effective potential analytically in the high-temperature approximation, including one-loop boson and fermion contributions and ring diagram resummation to account for plasma screening. The vacuum expectation value was used as the order parameter, and counterterms were introduced to preserve tree-level vacuum properties.

## Key numbers
- For λ = 0.4 and g = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6: the critical temperature Tc decreases abruptly from Ω = 0 GeV to Ω = 0.01 GeV, where a first-order phase transition occurs; for Ω > 0.01 GeV the transition is second-order.
- For λ = 1.0 and g = 0.4, 0.6, 0.8: Tc decreases from Ω = 0 GeV to Ω = 0.1 GeV (minimum), with first-order transition in that decreasing region; for Ω > 0.1 GeV Tc increases monotonically.
- For λ = 1.5 and g = 0.4, 0.6, 0.8: Tc decreases up to Ω = 0.15 GeV, with two disconnected second-order regions (Ω = 0 to 0.05 GeV and Ω > 0.15 GeV) and first-order transition in between.
- The boson self-energy at leading order in high-T expansion is Π = λ T²/4 + g² (T²/12 + Ω²/(16π²)).

## Context
This work extends studies of the QCD phase diagram under extreme conditions, specifically incorporating rotation effects into the Yukawa model with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. It builds on prior work using the Fock-Schwinger proper-time method for rotating systems (Iablokov 2019, 2020) and fermion propagators in rotating media (Ayala 2021). The results complement lattice QCD studies at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials (Kogut 2002, Bazavov 2017) and effective model calculations of rotation effects on phase transitions (Chernodub 2016, Jiang 2016, Zhang 2020). The findings enable future investigations of how vorticity influences the chiral transition in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star interiors.
