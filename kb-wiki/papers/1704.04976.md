# The onset of fluid-dynamical behavior in relativistic kinetic theory

> In this proceedings we discuss recent findings regarding the large order behavior of the Chapman-Enskog expansion in relativistic kinetic theory. It is shown that this series in powers of the Knudsen number has zero radius of convergence in the case of a Bjorken expanding fluid described by the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. This divergence stems from the presence of non-hydrodynamic modes, which give non-perturbative contributions to the Knudsen series.

## Key claim
The Chapman-Enskog expansion in powers of the Knudsen number has zero radius of convergence for a Bjorken-expanding fluid described by the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, because the series cannot be well behaved when the Knudsen number approaches zero due to the presence of non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Method
The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a longitudinally expanding system of massless particles undergoing Bjorken flow, using the method of moments to derive an infinite hierarchy of coupled differential equations for dimensionless moments of the distribution function. They then performed the Chapman-Enskog expansion directly on these moment variables as a power series in the Knudsen number (proportional to tau_R/tau) and determined the coefficients order by order from algebraic recurrence relations.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of hydrodynamic emergence in rapidly evolving kinetic systems, building on prior studies of Bjorken flow in kinetic theory by Baym (1984) and Florkowski et al. (2013). The divergence of the gradient expansion was previously found in strongly coupled plasmas without a kinetic description by Heller et al. (2013) and Buchel et al. (2016). The authors propose a generalized series with nontrivial tau-dependent coefficients that includes non-perturbative contributions from non-hydrodynamic modes, which was numerically solved and showed excellent agreement with the exact Boltzmann solution in Denicol (2016). This enables future investigation of the domain of validity of hydrodynamic approximations at moderate to large Knudsen numbers.
