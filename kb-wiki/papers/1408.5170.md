# Structure of Krypton isotopes calculated with symmetry conserving configuration mixing methods

> Shape transitions and shape coexistence in the $^{70-98}$Kr region are studied in a unified view with state-of-the-art beyond self-consistent mean field methods based on the Gogny D1S interaction. Beyond mean field effects are taken into account through the exact angular momentum and particle number restoration and the possibility of axial and non-axial shape mixing. The results of the low-lying properties of these isotopes are in good agreement with the experimental data when the triaxial degree of freedom is included. Shape transitions from axial-oblate ($^{70-72}$Kr) to triaxial-prolate ($^{74-78}$Kr) and from spherical-triaxial ($^{86-92}$Kr) to axial-oblate ($^{94-98}$Kr) ground states are obtained. Additionally, low-lying $0^{+}$ excited states and quasi-gamma bands are found showing the richness of the collective structure in this region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the previously observed failure of axial symmetry-conserving configuration-mixing (SCCM) calculations to reproduce the correct ground-state ordering in neutron-deficient Kr isotopes is due to the omission of the triaxial degree of freedom, not to a deficiency of the underlying effective interaction. The triaxial SCCM calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction yield good agreement with experiment, whereas the axial-only calculations do not.

## Method

The authors employ the symmetry-conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) method, which combines exact angular-momentum and particle-number projection with the generator coordinate method (GCM) for axial and triaxial quadrupole shapes. Intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) type states are obtained via variation after particle-number projection (PN-VAP) with constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameters (β₂, γ), using the Gogny D1S energy density functional. The Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equations are solved for each angular momentum I to obtain the nuclear states and their collective wave functions.

## Key numbers

* The PN-VAP potential energy surfaces are computed for isotopes ⁶⁸⁻⁹⁸Kr, with contour lines separated by 1 MeV (solid) and 0.25 MeV (dashed).
* The GCM calculations use a regular triangular mesh in the triaxial plane including N_GCM = 60 HFB-type states, expanded in a single-particle basis with nine major spherical harmonic oscillator shells.
* The number of integration points for Euler angles are N_a = 8, N_b = N_c = 16, and for the gauge angle N_φ = 9, chosen to ensure convergence of projected matrix elements to nominal values I(I+1), N, and Z = 36.
* The computing time for each nucleus was approximately 48,000 hours on a single processor at the Prometheus facility at GSI.
* The lowest experimental 3⁻ state in ⁷²Kr is found at 1.85 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior beyond-mean-field studies of Kr isotopes that used axial-only SCCM with Skyrme SLy6 (Bender et al., 2006) and relativistic PC-PK1 (2013) interactions, which failed to reproduce the correct level ordering for ⁷⁴⁻⁷⁸Kr. It also contrasts with five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) calculations using Gogny D1S (2007, 2009) and relativistic (2013) interactions, which included triaxiality but relied on a Gaussian overlap approximation and lacked exact particle-number restoration. The present results are consistent with recent triaxial SCCM calculations for ⁷⁶Kr using a relativistic functional (2014) and with interacting boson model (IBM) calculations mapped to Gogny D1M (2013) for neutron-rich isotopes. The work enables systematic studies of shape transitions and coexistence across the full ⁷⁰⁻⁹⁸Kr chain using a fully microscopic, self-consistent method without Gaussian overlap approximations.
