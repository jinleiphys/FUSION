# Discovery of Quark-Gluon-Plasma: Strangeness Diaries

> We look from a theoretical perspective at the new phase of matter, quark-gluon plasma (QGP), the new form of nuclear matter created at high temperature and pressure. Here I retrace the path to QGP discovery and its exploration in terms of strangeness production and strange particle signatures. We will see the theoretical arguments that have been advanced to create interest in this determining signature of QGP. We explore the procedure used by several experimental groups making strangeness production an important tool in the search and discovery of this primordial state of matter present in the Universe before matter in its present form was formed. We close by looking at both the ongoing research that increases the reach of this observable to LHC energy scale $pp$ collisions, and propose an interpretation of these unexpected results.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strangeness production and strange antibaryon enhancement served as the decisive experimental signature for the discovery of quark-gluon plasma (QGP), but the author presents this as a personal retrospective ("diary") rather than a definitive proof, acknowledging that the interpretation of strangeness data at LHC energy scale pp collisions remains an open question requiring further interpretation.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical development of the strangeness signature of QGP from the 1970s onward, tracing how three key arguments, lower energy threshold for strange quark pair production in QGP (2m_s ≈ 300 MeV versus 700 MeV in hadronic reactions), increased strangeness density in QGP (n_s ≈ 0.3 fm^{-3} versus ≈ 0.1 fm^{-3} in hadron gas), and enhanced anti-strangeness relative to anti-up and anti-down quarks in baryon-rich QGP, were developed and then applied by experimental groups at CERN-SPS and later at LHC. The paper incorporates unpublished personal records, earlier arXiv manuscripts, and verifiable references to reconstruct the history of the strangeness approach.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark pair production threshold in QGP: 2m_s ≈ 300 MeV, compared to 700 MeV for the conventional hadronic reaction p + p → p + Λ + K⁺
- Strange quark density in QGP: n_s = n_anti-s ≈ 0.3 fm^{-3}, compared to ≈ 0.1 fm^{-3} in a hadron gas
- QGP formation temperature range: T = 160–250 MeV, as achievable in current experiments
- Universe QGP epoch: at age about 20 μs after the Big Bang
- Proton radius: on the order of 10^{-13} cm
- Lifetime of the current "iced vacuum" state: 1.5 × 10^{10} years (age of the universe)
- Duration of the "melted" vacuum state after the Big Bang: 10^{-6} seconds

## Context

This review extends the author's decades-long work on strangeness as a QGP signature, building on foundational papers by Rafelski (1980, 1982) and collaborations with Jean Letessier, Berndt Müller, and others. It contrasts the strangeness approach with the earlier Lee-Wick (1974) collapsed nuclei model that dominated the BEVALAC program, and with the hadronic cascade models that predict opposite trends for strange antibaryon yields. The work enables continued interpretation of strangeness data from CERN-SPS through LHC energies, including unexpected results in pp collisions that challenge purely hadronic explanations.
