# Two-body problem of impurity atoms in dipolar Fermi gas

> The polarized dipolar Fermi gas shows exotic properties at low temperatures, characterized by an axially-deformed Fermi surface and anisotropic single-particle energy, due to the long-range and anisotropic nature of dipole-dipole interaction. In cold-atom experiments such a system has been realized, e.g., in degenerate gas of Er and Dy atoms. In the case that non-dipolar impurity atoms are introduced in such system, they undergoes an induced interaction mediated by the density fluctuations of the background dipolar Fermi gas. We derive the induced interaction potential to the single-loop order of fluctuations and show that it becomes indeed an anisotropic Ruderman-Kittel-Kasuya-Yosida-type potential which preserves the axial symmetry around the polarization axis. We then solve the two-body problem of impurity atoms interacting via the anisotropic potential and figure out the dependence of bound state and scattering properties on the parameters of dipolar Fermi gas.

## Key claim

The induced interaction between non-dipolar impurity atoms in a polarized dipolar Fermi gas is an anisotropic Ruderman-Kittel-Kasuya-Yosida-type potential that preserves axial symmetry around the polarization axis, and the two-body bound state and scattering properties depend on the deformation parameter β of the Fermi surface, though the critical coupling for bound state formation shows only weak β dependence.

## Method

The authors derive the induced interaction potential between two impurity atoms to single-loop order using the static density-density correlation function of the background dipolar Fermi gas, which is described in the Hartree-Fock approximation with an axially-deformed Fermi surface parameterized by β and λ. They solve the Schrödinger equation for the relative motion of two impurity atoms in cylindrical coordinates with finite-range boundary conditions, expanding the wave function in Fourier modes for the z-direction and Bessel functions for the radial direction. The scattering amplitude is evaluated in the Born approximation, and partial-wave analysis is performed to study angular momentum mixing.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling constant for the first bound state (l_z=0) is G_crit = 0.488 for β=1.0, 0.485 for β=0.9, 0.4843 for β=0.8, and 0.4847 for β=0.7 (Table I).
- The s-wave scattering length in the low-energy limit is a_s = -g^2 m_22 m_1 k_F / (4π^3 λ^2), where m_22 = m_2/2 is the reduced mass of two impurity atoms.
- The dimensionless coupling constant G is defined as G = (2m_22/k_F^2) g^2 m_1 k_F^4/(16π^3 λ^2).
- The scattering amplitude at k=0.2k_F and β=0.8 shows forward scattering dominance with contour values ranging from approximately 1.99 to 1.97 in units of m_22 g^2/(2π) (Fig. 5).
- The parameter λ^2 is approximately 1.00037 for Dy atoms with a peak density of 4×10^13 cm^-3.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Fermi polarons in dipolar Fermi gases (Nishimura 2021) by focusing on the two-body effective interaction between impurity atoms rather than single-particle properties. It builds on the Hartree-Fock description of the deformed Fermi surface in dipolar Fermi gases (Miyakawa 2008, Sogo 2009, Ronen 2010) and the anisotropic RKKY interaction framework (Ruderman 1954, Kasuya 1956, Yosida 1957). The results provide the basis for understanding few-body correlations of minority particles in majority fermion systems (Camacho-Guardian 2018, Moriya 2021, Tajima 2022, Muir 2022) and can be applied to experimental mixtures such as Dy-K (Ravensbergen 2018, 2020), Cr-Li (Ciamei 2022), and Er-Li (Schäfer 2022, 2023).
