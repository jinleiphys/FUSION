# Comparing phenomenological recipes with a microscopic model for the electric amplitude in strangeness photoproduction

> Corrections to the Born approximation in photo-induced strangeness production off a proton are calculated in a semi-realistic microscopic model. The vertex corrections and internal contributions to the amplitude of the $γp \to K^+ Λ$ reaction are included on the one-loop level. Different gauge-invariant phenomenological prescriptions for the modification of the Born contribution via the introduction of form factors and contact terms are discussed. In particular, it is shown that the popular minimal-substitution method of Ohta corresponds to a special limit of the more realistic approach.

## Key claim

The popular minimal-substitution method of Ohta (1989), which predicts no modification to the electric amplitude \(A_2\) when form factors are introduced, corresponds to a special limit of a more realistic microscopic model, specifically, the limit where the mass of the exchanged scalar meson becomes very large, causing vertex corrections and 4-point loop contributions to cancel exactly.

## Method

Vertex corrections and internal (4-point) contributions to the \(\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda\) amplitude were calculated at the one-loop level using an effective Lagrangian model with \(SU(3)_{flavor}\) symmetry, including scalar and pseudo-scalar mesons. The loop corrections were used to extract form factors and contact terms in a gauge-invariant framework, and the resulting electric amplitude \(A_2(s,t)\) was compared with predictions from Ohta's minimal-substitution method and the Davidson-Workman (2001) phenomenological recipe.

## Key numbers

* The \(\sigma\)-meson mass was taken as \(m_\sigma = 0.76\) GeV and \(m_\sigma = 1.0\) GeV in the loop calculations; the \(f_0\) meson mass was \(m_{f_0} = 0.993\) GeV.
* Coupling constants from the Nijmegen model: \(g_{\sigma pp} = 16.90\), \(g_{\sigma \Lambda \Lambda} = 9.84\), \(g_{K^+ p \Lambda} = -14.113\), \(g_{f_0 pp} = -2.97\), \(g_{f_0 \Lambda \Lambda} = -9.10\).
* At \(s = 3\) GeV\(^2\) (corresponding to photon lab energy \(k_{lab} \approx 1.1\) GeV), the microscopic \(s\)-channel form factor \(F_s(s)\) is larger than the phenomenological form factor by a factor of 30.
* The kaon weak-decay constant was \(f_K = 113\) MeV; the kaon mass was \(\mu_k\) and the lambda mass \(M_\Lambda\) are used in the kinematics.
* At \(m_\sigma \approx 1\) GeV, the total correction to \(A_2\) is maximal; at \(m_\sigma\) about 3–4 GeV, the correction becomes practically negligible.

## Context

This work extends the study of loop corrections in meson photoproduction, previously addressed for pions by Bos et al. (1992), to the strangeness sector. It directly compares the Ohta (1989) minimal-substitution method and the Davidson-Workman (2001) phenomenological recipe for restoring gauge invariance in the presence of form factors. The results show that the choice of prescription for the electric amplitude \(A_2\) strongly affects the extracted baryon resonance properties, as noted by Janssen et al. (2002), and the microscopic calculation provides a benchmark for judging which phenomenological approach is more realistic for a given reaction mechanism.
