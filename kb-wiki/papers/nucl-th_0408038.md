# RPA Vector Meson Leptonic Widths

> Recent J = 0 (para) baryonium interpretations of the BES narrow resonance data near the e^-e^+ -> p anti-p threshold suggests the existance of ortho baryonium. To assist future searches we study J = 1 states, especially vector meson leptonic decays, and report RPA calculations for both light and heavy mesons using a Coulomb-gauge QCD-inspired model. Since the phi(1880) is the only missing model state, other discovered J = 1 particles in this region are ortho baryonium candidates.

## Key claim
The paper reports Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for vector meson leptonic widths using a Coulomb-gauge QCD-inspired model, and proposes that any discovered J=1 particles in the 1800 MeV region other than the missing φ(1880) are ortho baryonium candidates. The authors hedge that their predicted masses are only in rough agreement with data since a fit was not attempted and quark masses were not fine-tuned.

## Method
The authors diagonalize an effective QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge using the chiral symmetry preserving Random Phase Approximation (RPA) to compute vector meson wavefunctions. They evaluate the transition form factor g(p) from quark-antiquark annihilation diagrams, then compute the leptonic width Γ = 2α_EM g²/(3M²). The calculation includes both S-wave and D-wave components and uses a QCD confining potential with scale connected to Λ_QCD along with a transverse gluon exchange hyperfine interaction.

## Key numbers
- ρ(770): calculated Γ_ee = 4.7 keV (experimental 6.85 keV)
- ω(782): calculated Γ_ee = 0.50 keV (experimental 0.60(2) keV)
- φ(1019): calculated Γ_ee = 1.1 keV (experimental 1.26(2) keV)
- J/ψ(3097): calculated Γ_ee = 3.8 keV (experimental 5.2(1) keV)
- ψ(3686): calculated Γ_ee = 2.0 keV (experimental 2.2(2) keV)
- Υ(9460): calculated Γ_ee = 0.45 keV (experimental 1.32(5) keV)
- φ(1880): predicted Γ_ee = 0.45-0.55 keV (model mass 1790 MeV)
- Weinberg sum rule check: left side 1.758 MeV², right side 1.754 MeV²
- Phenomenological estimate for φ(1880): Γ_ee = 0.7(3) keV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous RPA calculations (Llanes-Estrada and Cotanch, 2004) to vector meson leptonic widths, providing a field-theoretical alternative to potential quark models such as Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Ebert, Faustov and Galkin (2003). The paper addresses the recent BES narrow resonance data near the e⁻e⁺ → p p̅ threshold, which suggests para baryonium (J=0) and implies ortho baryonium (J=1) partners. The results enable future searches for baryonium states at the proposed DAΦNE upgrade to 2 GeV, where the missing φ(1880) should be distinguishable by its kaon decay modes.
