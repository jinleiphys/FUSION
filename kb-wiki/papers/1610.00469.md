# Signatures of shape phase transitions in odd-mass nuclei

> Quantum phase transitions between competing ground-state shapes of atomic nuclei with an odd number of protons or neutrons are investigated in a microscopic framework based on nuclear energy density functional theory and the particle-plus-boson-core coupling scheme. The boson-core Hamiltonian, as well as the single-particle energies and occupation probabilities of the unpaired nucleon, are completely determined by constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations for a specific choice of the energy density functional and paring interaction, and only the strength parameters of the particle-core coupling are adjusted to reproduce selected spectroscopic properties of the odd-mass system. We apply this method to odd-A Eu and Sm isotopes with neutron number $N \approx 90$, and explore the influence of the single unpaired fermion on the occurrence of a shape phase transition. Collective wave functions of low-energy states are used to compute quantities that can be related to quantum order parameters: deformations, excitation energies, E2 transition rates and separation energies, and their evolution with the control parameter (neutron number) is analysed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that shape phase transitions in odd-mass nuclei can be described microscopically using a framework combining nuclear energy density functional theory with the particle-plus-boson-core coupling scheme, but the strength parameters of the particle-core coupling must be adjusted separately for each nucleus to reproduce selected spectroscopic data, meaning the method is not fully parameter-free.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) mean-field calculations using the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force to generate deformation energy surfaces for even-even Sm core nuclei. These energy surfaces were mapped onto the expectation value of an interacting boson model Hamiltonian in the boson coherent state to determine the boson Hamiltonian parameters, while single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the unpaired fermion were obtained from the same mean-field calculation at zero deformation. The resulting interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) Hamiltonian was diagonalized numerically in a spherical basis, with the strength parameters of the fermion-boson coupling adjusted to reproduce ground-state spins and lowest excitation energies for each odd-mass nucleus separately.

## Key numbers

- The boson core nuclei studied were $^{146-154}$Sm, corresponding to boson numbers from 7 to 11 (fermion pairs outside doubly-magic $^{132}$Sn)
- The average deformation $\overline{\beta}$ increases to approximately 0.35 for the heavier isotopes
- The energy ratio $E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ in the U(5), X(5) and SU(3) limits are 2.00, 2.91 (k=1), 2.79 (k=2) and 3.33, respectively
- The ground-state spin changes at N=90 in Eu isotopes for negative parity, and at N=89 in odd-A Sm for both parities
- The fermion effective charge was fixed at $e_F=1.0$ $e$b

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quantum phase transitions in even-even nuclei (Cejnar 2010, Niksic 2007) to odd-mass systems, which had primarily been investigated using algebraic methods (Boyukata 2010, Iachello 2011, Petrellis 2011) and geometrical models (Zhang 2013a, 2013b). The method builds on the authors' previously developed framework (Nomura 2016) for describing odd-mass nuclei and applies it to the well-known spherical-to-axially-deformed shape transition in the rare earth region around N=90, where Sm nuclei provide an outstanding example of a first-order QPT. The analysis enables future systematic studies of how the unpaired fermion influences the location and nature of shape phase transitions in odd-mass systems across the nuclear chart.
