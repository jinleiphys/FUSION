# Semi-inclusive deep inelastic lepton scattering off complex nuclei

> It is shown that in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) of electrons off a complex nucleus A, the detection, in coincidence with the scattered electron, of a nucleus (A-1) in the ground state, as well as of a nucleon and a nucleus (A-2), also in the ground state, may provide unique information on several long standing problems, such as : i) the nature and the relevance of the final state interaction in DIS; ii) validity of the spectator mechanism in DIS; iii) the medium induced modifications of the nucleon structure function; iv) the origin of the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off complex nuclei, with detection of either a recoiling (A-1) nucleus or a nucleon plus an (A-2) nucleus in the ground state, can provide unique information on final state interactions, the spectator mechanism, medium modifications of nucleon structure functions, and the EMC effect, but the authors emphasize that experimental investigation of these processes represents a difficult task, and the results depend on the validity of the impulse approximation and the specific model (x-rescaling or Q²-rescaling) used for the bound nucleon structure function.

## Method

The authors derive cross sections within the impulse approximation (spectator model) for two semi-inclusive processes: A(e,e'(A-1))X, where DIS occurs on a mean-field nucleon and the recoiling (A-1) nucleus is detected with low momentum and low excitation energy; and A(e,e'N₂(A-2))X, where DIS occurs on a high-momentum nucleon from a correlated pair, and both the partner nucleon N₂ and the (A-2) nucleus are detected. The cross sections are expressed in terms of one-body and two-body nuclear spectral functions, with the nucleon structure function treated either via x-rescaling (energy conservation at the hadronic vertex) or Q²-rescaling (medium-modified QCD evolution). The authors analyze ratios of cross sections for different nuclei and kinematic conditions to isolate the effects of nucleon momentum distributions and test the spectator mechanism.

## Key numbers

- At Q² ≃ 5 GeV², the kinematic factor K^A for A=4 differs from the free nucleon factor K^N by only 5%.
- For the ratio R(|P_{A-1}|) in the Bjorken limit, the P_{A-1}-dependence of the quantity z₁^(A)F₂^(N/A)(x_Bj/z₁^(A))/z₁^(A')F₂^(N/A')(x_Bj/z₁^(A')) is at most of order 5%, so the dependence of R on |P_{A-1}| is entirely provided by the momentum distributions.
- For a complex nucleus like ⁴⁰Ca at θ = 90°, z₁^(A) ≃ 1, making the ratio R^A independent of |P_{A-1}| in the x-rescaling model; at θ = 180°, z₁^(40) ≃ 1 - |P_{A-1}|/M, producing strong |P_{A-1}| dependence.
- For the deuteron at θ = 90°, z₁^(2) ≃ 1 - |P_{A-1}|²/(2M²), giving a strongly |P_{A-1}|-dependent ratio in the x-rescaling model.

## Context

This work extends the spectator model (impulse approximation) previously applied to the deuteron process D(e,e'N)X (reviewed by Frankfurt and Strikman) and to complex nuclei via A(e,e'N)X (Ciofi degli Atti and Simula) by proposing two new semi-inclusive channels: A(e,e'(A-1))X and A(e,e'N₂(A-2))X. The paper is motivated by discussions on the feasibility of an electron-ion collider (HERA, GSI) where detection of nuclear fragments from DIS could be possible. The authors build on the nuclear spectral function formalism of Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators, and compare two models for medium modifications: the x-rescaling model (based on energy conservation) and the Q²-rescaling model of Close (based on modified QCD evolution with a larger confinement radius for bound nucleons). The work enables experimental tests of whether final state interactions are small in semi-inclusive DIS (as suggested by calculations of FSI and data from Adams) and whether the spectator mechanism holds for complex nuclei, which would allow extraction of modified nucleon structure functions and investigation of the EMC effect's origin.
