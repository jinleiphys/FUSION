# Massive Neutron Stars with Hadron-Quark Transition Core : phenomenological approach by "3-window model"

> By a new approach introducing a "3-window model" and constructing phenomenologically an equation of state for the hadron-quark (HQ) transition region, possible maximum mass of neutron stars (NSs) is discussed. It is found that neutron stars (NSs) with HQ transition core are able to have a mass exceeding two-solar mass, consistent with massive NSs recently observed.

## Key claim
Neutron stars with a hadron-quark transition core can exceed two solar masses, reaching up to (2.2–2.4) solar masses, provided that quark degrees of freedom set in at rather low densities (1.5–2 times nuclear density) and the quark matter equation of state is stiff. This result is not guaranteed by the Gibbs or Maxwell construction, which would soften the equation of state; the 3-window model avoids that softening by phenomenological interpolation.

## Method
The authors divide the equation of state into three density regimes: pure hadronic matter (H-EOS) up to a lower boundary density ρ_H, a hadron-quark transition region (HQ-EOS) between ρ_H and ρ_Q, and pure quark matter (Q-EOS) above ρ_Q. The H-EOS is taken from a G-matrix-based effective interaction for neutron-star matter including nucleons, hyperons (Λ, Σ⁻), and leptons, with nuclear incompressibility κ = 250 MeV. The Q-EOS is from a (2+1)-flavor NJL model with a vector interaction strength g_v. The HQ-EOS is constructed by a 5th-degree polynomial interpolation that matches energy density, pressure, and sound velocity at both boundaries, subject to physical constraints (p ≥ 0, ∂p/∂ρ ≥ 0, v_s ≤ c).

## Key numbers
- Maximum masses of hybrid stars reach 2.36 M_⊙ for parameters (x_H, x_Q) = (1.5, 8.5) with g_v/G_S = 0, and 2.21 M_⊙ for (1.5, 11) with g_v/G_S = 0.5.
- For (x_H, x_Q) = (1.5, 11) and g_v/G_S = 0, the maximum mass is 1.79 M_⊙, showing sensitivity to the transition density window.
- Radii of maximum-mass configurations range from 10.2 km to 11.4 km across the parameter sets.
- Central densities ρ_c/ρ_0 range from 5.32 to 7.25 (where ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density).
- The hadron-quark transition onset density is taken as (1.5–2)ρ_0.

## Context
This work addresses the "Hyperon Crisis" identified in earlier studies (Takatsuka et al., prior works), where hyperon mixing softens the equation of state so severely that maximum masses fall to 1.1–1.2 M_⊙, contradicting the 1.44 M_⊙ of PSR1913+16 and the later 2 M_⊙ observations. The 3-window model extends the authors' preceding HQ crossover picture (Masuda, Hatsuda, Takatsuka, 2013–2015) by providing a phenomenological interpolation that does not rely on Gibbs or Maxwell constructions. The results enable future work on whether the HQ transition or universal three-body forces (Takatsuka, 2015) is the more viable resolution to the hyperon crisis.
