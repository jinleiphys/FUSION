# Spin susceptibility of neutron matter at zero temperature

> The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method is applied to compute the spin susceptibility and the compressibility of neutron matter at zero temperature. Results are given for realistic interactions which include both a two-body potential of the Argonne type and the Urbana IX three-body potential. Simulations have been carried out for about 60 neutrons. We find an overall reduction of the spin susceptibilty by about a factor 3 with respect to the Pauli susceptibility for a wide range of densities. Results for the compressibility of neutron matter are also presented and compared with other available estimates obtained for semirealistic nucleon-nucleon interactions by using other techniques.

## Key claim

The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method shows that strong correlations from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions reduce the spin susceptibility of neutron matter by up to about a factor of 3 relative to the Pauli susceptibility, across a wide range of densities; this reduction is largely independent of the three-body force and of the spin-orbit component of the two-body potential.

## Method

The authors applied the AFDMC method to simulate systems of approximately 60 neutrons in a periodic box at zero temperature, using the Reid v6 potential and the Argonne v6' and v8' two-body potentials plus the Urbana IX three-body potential (collectively AU6' and AU8'). They computed the spin susceptibility by applying a small external magnetic field to the system and extracting the second derivative of the ground-state energy with respect to the field, using finite differences from simulations at fixed spin asymmetry Jz = 50 and at the tangent field b0 for each density. The compressibility was obtained from a polynomial fit to the AFDMC energies at zero polarization and zero field as a function of density.

## Key numbers

* Spin susceptibility ratio chi/chi_F = 0.37(1) at rho = 1.25 rho_0 for the AU6' interaction, compared to 0.50 from earlier Correlated Basis Function (CBF) calculations with the Reid6 potential (Jackson 1992).
* Spin susceptibility ratio chi/chi_F = 0.33(1) at rho = 2.0 rho_0 for AU6', and 0.30(1) at rho = 2.5 rho_0 for AU6'.
* Spin susceptibility ratio chi/chi_F = 0.36(1) at rho = 1.25 rho_0 for the Reid6 interaction, about 25% smaller than the CBF result of 0.50 from Jackson (1992).
* Compressibility ratio K/K_F = 0.47(3) at rho = 1.25 rho_0 for AU6', compared to 0.45 from CBF theory (Fabrocini 2001, private communication) and 0.71 from Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) for the AU18 interaction.
* Compressibility ratio K/K_F = 0.21(3) at rho = 2.0 rho_0 for AU6', and 0.14(3) at rho = 2.5 rho_0.
* Time step Delta_tau = 5 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-1} was sufficient for convergence.
* The tangent field b_0 = 53 MeV at rho = 2.0 rho_0 for J_z0 = 50; b_0 = 39 MeV at rho = 1.25 rho_0.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of neutron matter Landau parameters by Backmann and Kallman (1973) using Brueckner theory with the full Reid potential, and by Jackson, Krotscheck, Meltzer, and Smith (1992) using CBF theory with the Reid6 potential. The AFDMC results show that those earlier perturbative methods overestimated the spin susceptibility by up to 25% at rho = 1.25 rho_0, and that Skyrme-type models (Reddy, Prakash, Lattimer, and Pons 1999) predict much smaller values of the Landau parameter G0, leading to instability toward ferromagnetic ground states at densities above 2 rho_0. The strong reduction of the spin susceptibility found here has direct implications for neutrino mean free paths in dense matter, and the results enable more reliable calculations of Gamow-Teller transition rates in supernovae and proto-neutron star studies.
