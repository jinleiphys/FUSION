# Field theory Lagrangian approach to nuclear structure

> We show that in the search of a unified mean field description of finite nuclei and of nuclear and neutron matter even at high densities, the relativistic nuclear model derived from effective field theory and density functional theory methods constitutes a competitive framework. The model predicts a soft equation of state, owing to the additional meson interaction terms, consistently with the results of the microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and recent experimental observations from heavy ion collisions. In finite systems, after inclusion of the pairing correlations, the model is able to describe both stable and exotic nuclei. We address two examples at the limits of the nuclear landscape. On the one hand, we analyze the giant halo effect and the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in very neutron-rich Zr isotopes. On the other hand, we discuss the structure of superheavy nuclei with double shell closures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the relativistic mean field model derived from effective field theory (E-RMF) provides a unified framework for describing finite nuclei and nuclear/neutron matter at high densities, but this success is conditional on the inclusion of additional meson self-interactions and cross-couplings beyond those in standard RMF models, and the model still requires improvement in the isovector sector for neutron matter.

## Method

The authors employ the E-RMF energy density functional derived from a chiral effective Lagrangian truncated at fourth order in the expansion parameter, using the G1 and G2 parameter sets fitted to ground-state observables of five doubly-magic nuclei. For finite nuclei, they solve the Dirac equation in coordinate space iteratively with the Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields, and include pairing correlations through a modified BCS approach that accounts for quasibound levels via centrifugal barriers. Infinite nuclear and neutron matter calculations are performed in the uniform limit where gradient terms vanish.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility modulus: K = 215 MeV for both G1 and G2 parameter sets, compared to K = 271 MeV for the NL3 set
- Effective mass at saturation: M*_infinity/M ~ 0.65 for E-RMF sets, compared to ~ 0.60 for standard RMF parameter sets
- Neutron chemical potential in Zr isotopes beyond N=82: ranges from -0.89 MeV for ^124Zr to practically zero in ^140Zr
- Binding energies of neutron-rich Zr isotopes: range from 943.7 MeV in ^124Zr to 951.9 MeV in ^140Zr
- Single-particle energy of the 3p_3/2 level in Zr isotopes: runs from -0.16 MeV in ^124Zr to -0.77 MeV in ^140Zr
- Contribution to total neutron rms radius from 2f_7/2, 3p_3/2, and 2f_5/2 states: 0.59, 0.50, and 0.06 fm^2 in ^124Zr versus 2.63, 1.80, and 1.53 fm^2 in ^140Zr

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear structure developed by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1998) and applies it to both infinite matter and exotic nuclei, contrasting with standard RMF models like NL3 from Lalazissis et al. (1997) that include only scalar self-interactions. The E-RMF predictions for the equation of state agree with microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and recent heavy-ion collision data, while standard RMF models deviate at high densities. The study of giant halo effects in Zr isotopes builds on earlier relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations by Meng et al. (1998) and enables predictions for superheavy nuclei with double shell closures and for collective excitations near the neutron drip line.
