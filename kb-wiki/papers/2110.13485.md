# Vorticity and $Λ$ polarization in the microscopic transport model PACIAE

> We study the behavior of four types of vorticities in non-central Au+Au collisions at energies $\sqrt{S_{NN}}$ = 5--200 GeV using a microscopic transport model PACIAE. The results show that the vorticities decay faster at lower energy and smaller impact parameter. The non-monotonic dependence of the initial vorticities on the collision energies is reconfirmed and the turning point is 10-15 GeV for different vorticities. The global $Λ$ polarization at energies $\sqrt{S_{NN}}$ = 7.7--200 GeV is reproduced by introducing an energy density freeze-out criterion.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the initial vorticities in non-central Au+Au collisions have a non-monotonic dependence on collision energy, with a turning point between 10 and 15 GeV depending on the vorticity type, and reproduces the global Lambda polarization measured by STAR using an energy density freeze-out criterion in the PACIAE transport model, though the model does not implement an explicit phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE (based on PYTHIA 6.4) to simulate non-central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5-200 GeV. They employed a generalized coarse-graining method with cell size 0.5 x 0.5 x 0.5 fm^3 and time slice 0.5 fm/c to construct fluidized fields from particle positions and momenta, averaging over more than 10^4 events per condition. Four types of vorticities (non-relativistic, kinematic, temperature, and thermal) were computed from the partonic stage, and Lambda hyperons were collected from the hadronization stage using an energy density freeze-out criterion of about 0.1 GeV/fm^3 to calculate polarization.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 5-200 GeV for vorticities; 7.7-200 GeV for Lambda polarization
- Turning point for initial vorticities: approximately 10 GeV for NR-vorticity, K-vorticity, and thermal vorticity; approximately 15 GeV for T-vorticity
- Cell size used in coarse-graining: 0.5 x 0.5 x 0.5 fm^3 with time slice 0.5 fm/c
- Energy density freeze-out criterion: about 0.1 GeV/fm^3
- Impact parameter for polarization calculations: b = 7 fm, corresponding to 20%-50% centrality bin of STAR experiments
- Number of events generated per condition: more than 10^4

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vorticity and polarization in heavy-ion collisions using transport models such as AMPT (Jiang 2016, Li 2017, Xia 2018) and UrQMD (Karpenko 2017, Vitiuk 2019), as well as hydrodynamic models like PICR (Csernai 2013, Becattini 2013, Xie 2016-2021) and ECHO-QGP (Karpenko 2016, Becattini 2021). The non-monotonic dependence of initial vorticities on collision energy was previously reported by Deng (2020) using UrQMD and IQMD, though with a different turning point around 3-5 GeV. The reproduction of STAR Lambda polarization data (STAR 2017) using PACIAE enables future studies of local polarization, polarization of different particles, and different collision systems within this transport framework.
