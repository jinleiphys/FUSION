# Solar Neutrinos: What Next?

> I summarize the current state of solar neutrino research and then give my answer to the question: What should we do next?

## Key claim

The combined standard model (standard solar model plus standard electroweak theory) fails to fit the observed solar neutrino rates at approximately the 20σ level, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the standard solar model predictions for neutrino fluxes are accurate; the discrepancies could in principle be resolved by new neutrino physics such as oscillations.

## Method

Bahcall compared the predicted event rates from the BP98 standard solar model (which includes element diffusion, updated nuclear reaction rates, OPAL opacities, and improved neutrino cross sections) with the measured rates from the chlorine, Kamiokande, SAGE, GALLEX, and SuperKamiokande experiments. He computed χ² for the fit to the three experimental rates (chlorine, gallium weighted average, and SuperKamiokande) combining quadratically the theoretical solar model, experimental, and neutrino cross section uncertainties. He also performed a model-independent analysis allowing the pp, ⁷Be, and ⁸B fluxes to vary freely subject only to the solar luminosity constraint, and examined the best-fit neutrino oscillation solutions in the two-neutrino approximation.

## Key numbers

- χ² for the combined standard model fit to the three experimental rates (chlorine, gallium, SuperKamiokande) is 61, equivalent to approximately a 20σ discrepancy
- Chlorine experiment measured rate: 2.56 ± 0.23 SNU, compared to predicted 7.7⁺¹·²₋₁·₀ SNU (factor of three less)
- Gallium experiments average observed rate: 73 ± 5 SNU, compared to predicted 129⁺⁸₋₆ SNU; the pp and pep neutrinos alone account for 72.4 SNU of the predicted rate
- The ⁸B neutrino contribution to the chlorine rate inferred from SuperKamiokande normalization is 2.8 ± 0.1 SNU, which exceeds the total observed chlorine rate of 2.56 ± 0.23 SNU
- The rms fractional difference between calculated and measured sound speeds from helioseismology is 1.1 × 10⁻³ over 0.05R⊙ to 0.95R⊙, and 0.7 × 10⁻³ in the solar core (0.05R⊙ to 0.25R⊙)
- Best-fit oscillation solutions: SMA (Δm² = 5 × 10⁻⁶ eV², sin²2θ = 5 × 10⁻³), LMA (Δm² = 2 × 10⁻⁵ eV², sin²2θ = 0.8), LOW (Δm² = 8 × 10⁻⁸ eV², sin²2θ = 0.96), VAC (Δm² = 8 × 10⁻¹¹ eV², sin²2θ = 0.7)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing solar neutrino problem first identified by Davis (1968) and Bahcall (1968), where the chlorine experiment measured a rate three times lower than predictions. The paper establishes three distinct solar neutrino problems: the absolute rate discrepancy in chlorine, the incompatibility between chlorine and water Cherenkov experiments (pointed out by Bethe and Bahcall in 1990), and the missing ⁷Be neutrinos in gallium experiments. The work enables the interpretation of upcoming experiments including SNO, BOREXINO, and KamLAND as long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments, and demonstrates that solar model explanations are ruled out by helioseismological measurements showing sound speed agreement to 0.1% rms.
