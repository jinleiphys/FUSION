# J/$ψ$ production and suppression in nuclear collisions

> In terms of a new QCD factorization formula for J/$ψ$ production, we calculate the J/$ψ$ suppression in nuclear collisions by including the multiple scattering between the pre-J/$ψ$ partonic states and the nuclear medium. We find agreement with all data on J/$ψ$ suppression in hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, except a couple of points (the ``second drop'') at the highest $E_T$ bins of the new NA50 data.

## Key claim
The authors' QCD factorization model for J/ψ production, which includes multiple scattering between pre-J/ψ partonic states and the nuclear medium, agrees with all data on J/ψ suppression in hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions except for a couple of data points (the "second drop") at the highest transverse energy bins of the new NA50 data.

## Method
The authors derive a QCD factorization formula for J/ψ production that separates short-distance production of pre-J/ψ c-cbar partonic states from long-distance transition to physical J/ψ mesons via transition distributions D_{[c-cbar]→J/ψ}. They calculate J/ψ suppression in nuclear collisions by including multiple soft spectator interactions between the pre-J/ψ c-cbar pairs and the nuclear medium, which increase the relative momentum between the charm and anticharm quarks and reduce the phase space for J/ψ formation. The model uses a single parameter ε², defined as the average gain of invariant mass per unit medium length, fixed by the total suppression in hadron-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers
- The c-cbar pairs are produced at a distance scale r_H ≤ 1/2M_c ∼ 1/15 fm, much smaller than the J/ψ size
- The invariant mass of the produced quark pair satisfies m_{c-cbar} ≥ 2M_c ∼ 3 GeV
- The suppression of spectator interactions in hard collisions scales as ρ_N L/[(2M_c)² + q_T²] with medium density ρ_N and medium length L
- Without nuclear medium, the length L is of order the hadron radius R ∼ 1 fm
- The model is consistent with all existing J/ψ suppression data except a couple of points at the highest E_T bins of the new NA50 data

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier QCD factorization formalism for J/ψ production (Qiu and Zhang, 2001) and their model of J/ψ suppression in nuclear collisions (Qiu, Vary, and Zhang, 2001). The approach contrasts with the Glauber formula, which predicts a straight line on a semi-log plot of σ_AB versus effective medium length L(A,B) and is inconsistent with the strong J/ψ suppression observed in Pb-Pb collisions by NA50 (2000). The model connects to both the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) model and the color evaporation (CE) model, which correspond to different assumptions for the transition distributions D_{[c-cbar]→J/ψ}, and enables decisive tests of J/ψ production mechanisms through polarization measurements at large transverse momentum.
