# $ΔΔ$ dibaryon structure in extended chiral SU(3) quark model

> The structure of $ΔΔ$ dibaryon is studied in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model in which vector meson exchanges are included. The effect from the vector meson fields is very similar to that from the one-gluon exchange (OGE) interaction. Both in the chiral SU(3) quark model and in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, the resultant mass of the $ΔΔ$ dibaryon is lower than the threshold of the $ΔΔ$ channel but higher than that of the $ΔNπ$ channel.

# ΔΔ Dibaryon Structure in Extended Chiral SU(3) Quark Model

## Key claim
The ΔΔ dibaryon (deltaron) has a mass lower than the ΔΔ threshold but higher than the ΔNπ threshold in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and its extended version with vector meson exchanges, meaning the state is bound against decay to two Δ baryons but is not narrow enough to be easily detected experimentally. The vector meson exchanges produce binding energies very similar to those from the one-gluon exchange (OGE) interaction, despite the two models having fundamentally different short-range quark-quark interaction mechanisms.

## Method
The resonating group method (RGM) was used to solve a coupled-channel bound-state equation including ΔΔ and hidden color (CC) channels, with both L=0 and L=2 partial waves. The Hamiltonian includes confinement (quadratic form), OGE, and chiral field couplings (scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector meson exchanges), with parameters determined by fitting the deuteron binding energy and NN scattering phase shifts. Two parameter sets were used for the extended model: set I with no tensor coupling of vector mesons (f_chv/g_chv=0) and set II with tensor coupling (f_chv/g_chv=2/3).

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of deltaron in chiral SU(3) quark model: 48.3 MeV (ΔΔ+CC channels, L=0+2)
- Binding energy of deltaron in extended chiral SU(3) quark model set I: 84.0 MeV (ΔΔ+CC channels, L=0+2)
- Binding energy of deltaron in extended chiral SU(3) quark model set II: 70.4 MeV (ΔΔ+CC channels, L=0+2)
- Root-mean-square radius of deltaron in extended chiral SU(3) quark model set I: 0.76 fm (ΔΔ+CC channels, L=0+2)
- Root-mean-square radius of deltaron in extended chiral SU(3) quark model set II: 0.78 fm (ΔΔ+CC channels, L=0+2)
- OGE coupling constant α_s reduced from 0.785 (chiral SU(3) model) to 0.086 (set I) and 0.159 (set II) in the extended model
- σ meson mass: 595 MeV (chiral SU(3) model), 535 MeV (set I), 547 MeV (set II)
- Cut-off mass Λ = 1100 MeV for all mesons

## Context
This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model of Zhang et al. (1997) by including vector meson exchanges, following the approach of Glozman and Riska (1996) who also replaced OGE with vector meson coupling to explain baryon spectra. The deltaron was previously studied by Yazaki (1987) using cluster models with OGE, by Wang et al. (1992) using the quark delocalization model (finding binding energies of 320-390 MeV), and by Yuan and Zhang et al. (1999) in the chiral SU(3) quark model. The present results show that the deltaron binding energy remains in the tens of MeV range regardless of whether OGE or vector meson exchange dominates the short-range interaction, and the mass remains above the ΔNπ threshold, suggesting the deltaron is unlikely to be a narrow-width dibaryon detectable in experiment.
