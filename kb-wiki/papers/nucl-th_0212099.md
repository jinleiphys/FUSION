# Hadrons with charmed quarks in matter

> We investigate the $D{\bar D}$ decay width of excited charmonium states at finite nuclear density with simultaneous modification of both $D$ and ${\bar D}$ mesons in nuclear matter. The strongest effect is found for the $Ψ^{\prime}$ meson. The medium modification can be detected by dilepton spectroscopy as substantial $Ψ^{\prime}$ broadening and anomalous $Ψ^{\prime}$ absorption.

## Key claim
The in-medium modification of both \(D\) and \(\bar D\) meson masses, as predicted by the Quark Meson Coupling model, leads to a substantial increase in the \(D\bar D\) decay widths of excited charmonium states, with the strongest effect found for the \(\Psi^{\prime}\) meson; the effect is measurable via dilepton spectroscopy as a broad peak near the \(\Psi^{\prime}\) pole or as anomalous \(\Psi^{\prime}\) absorption in nuclear matter.

## Method
The author calculates the in-medium \(D\bar D\) decay widths of \(\Psi^{\prime\prime}(3770)\), \(\Psi^{\prime}(3686)\), and \(\chi_{c2}(3556)\) charmonium states using the in-medium \(D\) and \(\bar D\) masses from the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model. Two approaches are compared: a phase-space-only estimate assuming density-independent coupling constants, and the \(^3P_0\) model where the coupling constants themselves depend on the in-medium meson masses. The \(\Psi^{\prime}D\bar D\) coupling is estimated via the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model using the radiative \(\Psi^{\prime}\to e^+e^-\) decay width.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), the mass splitting between \(D\) and \(\bar D\) mesons is about 160 MeV (QMC model).
- The downward shift of the \(D\bar D\) threshold at \(\rho_0\) is \(\simeq -100\) MeV.
- The vacuum decay width of \(\Psi^{\prime\prime}(3770) \to D\bar D\) is 23.6 MeV; at \(\rho_0\) the in-medium width saturates at \(\Gamma_{\Psi^{\prime\prime}\to D\bar D} \simeq 90\) MeV (about 3.8 times larger than in vacuum).
- The vacuum total width of \(\Psi^{\prime}(3686)\) is 0.277 MeV; at \(\rho_0\) the in-medium \(\Psi^{\prime}\to D\bar D\) width is \(\simeq 70\) MeV (about 250 times larger than the vacuum total width).
- The \(\Psi^{\prime}\) decay channel into \(D\bar D\) opens at nuclear density \(\rho \simeq 0.05\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The \(\chi_{c2}(3556)\) charmonium level crosses the \(D\bar D\) threshold at a nuclear density of about 0.28 fm\(^{-3}\) (1.75\(\rho_0\)); at \(\rho \simeq 3\rho_0\) its \(D\bar D\) decay width is about 11 MeV, compared to its vacuum total width of 2 MeV.
- The \(\Psi^{\prime}D\bar D\) coupling constant from VMD is 12.84; the \(\Psi^{\prime\prime}D\bar D\) coupling from VMD is 19.94.

## Context
This work extends the proposal of Sibirtsev (2002) that in-medium \(D\) meson modification can be identified via enhanced subthreshold open charm production in \(\bar p A\) annihilation, and the QCD sum rule estimate of Hayashigaki (2000) predicting a similar downward shift of the \(D\bar D\) threshold. It also relates to studies of charmed mesic nuclei by Tsushima (2001) and \(J/\Psi\) suppression in heavy ion collisions by Sibirtsev (2002). The results agree with the most recent findings of Ko (2002) that both the mass and width of \(\Psi^{\prime\prime}(3770)\) depend significantly on the \(D\) meson mass. The work enables experimental searches for in-medium charmonium modification via dilepton spectroscopy in \(AA\) and \(\bar p A\) interactions.
