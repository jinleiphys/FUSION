# Quantum field theory from classical statistics

> An Ising-type classical statistical model is shown to describe quantum fermions. For a suitable time-evolution law for the probability distribution of the Ising-spins our model describes a quantum field theory for Dirac spinors in external electromagnetic fields, corresponding to a mean field approximation to quantum electrodynamics. All quantum features for the motion of an arbitrary number of electrons and positrons, including the characteristic interference effects for two-fermion states, are described by the classical statistical model. For one-particle states in the non-relativistic approximation we derive the Schrödinger equation for a particle in a potential from the time evolution law for the probability distribution of the Ising-spins. Thus all characteristic quantum features, as interference in a double slit experiment, tunneling or discrete energy levels for stationary states, are derived from a classical statistical ensemble. Concerning the particle-wave-duality of quantum mechanics, the discreteness of particles is traced back to the discreteness of occupation numbers or Ising-spins, while the continuity of the wave function reflects the continuity of the probability distribution for the Ising-spins.

## Key claim

An Ising-type classical statistical model with a specific causal and local time-evolution law for the probability distribution of Ising-spins can reproduce all quantum features of Dirac fermions in external electromagnetic fields, including multi-particle interference effects, within a mean-field approximation to quantum electrodynamics.

## Method

The author constructs a lattice model with discrete Ising-spins (occupation numbers 0 or 1) on a three-dimensional lattice, with four or eight species corresponding to Majorana or Dirac spinors. The time evolution of the probability distribution is formulated via a real Grassmann functional integral, from which a classical wave function (real square roots of probabilities up to signs) is extracted. The evolution law is specified as rotations of this wave function vector, preserving positivity and normalization, and the continuum limit yields the Dirac equation for free fermions and the Schrödinger equation for one-particle states in the non-relativistic approximation.

## Key numbers

- The model uses \(N_s = 4\) species of Ising-spins for Majorana/Weyl spinors and \(N_s = 8\) for Dirac spinors.
- The lattice has \(L^3/8\) points (with \(L\) even), yielding \(2^{(N_s L^3/8)}\) classical states.
- The space-time lattice is hypercubic bcc with nearest-neighbor distance \(2\Delta\); time step \(\epsilon = \Delta\).
- The continuum limit action is \(S = \int_{t,x} \{\psi_\gamma \partial_t \psi_\gamma - \psi_\gamma (T_k)_{\gamma\delta} \partial_k \psi_\delta\}\) with real \(4\times4\) matrices \(T_k\).
- The Dirac matrices \(\gamma^\mu\) obey \(\{\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu\} = 2\eta^{\mu\nu}\) with metric signature \((-1,1,1,1)\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior program (Wetterich, 2010-2011) mapping Ising-type models to quantum fermions, previously developed for massless Majorana spinors in four dimensions. It builds on an earlier approach (Wetterich, 2010) that derived the Schrödinger equation from a modified Liouville equation for phase-space densities, but which was cumbersome for multi-particle situations. The present construction enables a simpler, direct description of multi-fermion states and their dynamics, and provides a concrete classical statistical model from which all standard quantum phenomena (interference, tunneling, discrete energy levels) can be derived.
