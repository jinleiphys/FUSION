# Wobbling Motion in the multi-bands crossing region: dynamical coupling mode between high- and low-K states

> We analyze a mechanism of coupling of high- and low-$K$ bands in terms of a dynamical treatment for nuclear rotations, i.e.,wobbling motion. The wobbling states are produced through the generator coordinate method after angular momentum projection ({\it GCM-after-AMP}), in which the intrinsic states are constructed through fully self-consistent calculations by the 2d-cranked (or tilted-axis-cranked) HFB method . In particular, the phenomena of ``signature inversion'' and ``signature splitting'' in the t-band (tilted rotational band) are explained in terms of the wobbling model. % Our calculations will be compared with new data for in-band E2 transition rates in $^{182}$Os, which may shed light on the mechanism of the anomalous K=25 isomer decay, directly to the yrast band.

# Wobbling Motion in the Multi-Bands Crossing Region: Dynamical Coupling Mode Between High- and Low-K States

## Key claim

The authors propose that signature inversion and signature splitting observed in the t-band (tilted rotational band) of nuclei in the A ≈ 180 region can be explained as a wobbling motion, a dynamical coupling mode between low-K and high-K states, rather than requiring a static triaxial shape. The wobbling states are produced through the generator coordinate method after angular momentum projection (GCM-after-AMP), and the mechanism relies on inter-band interactions between even-I members of the t-band and members of the s-band, while odd-I members remain unperturbed.

## Method

The authors construct intrinsic states using fully self-consistent 2d-cranked (tilted-axis-cranked) HFB calculations with a pairing-plus-Q·Q residual interaction. Wobbling states are then generated as superpositions of these tilted-axis-cranked HFB states via the generator coordinate method after angular momentum projection (GCM-after-AMP), where the tilt angle θ and angular momentum J serve as generator coordinates. The GCM wave functions f^I_K(θ) are obtained variationally, and angular momentum projection is performed using the operator P̂^I_MK.

## Key numbers

- The multi-band crossing region occurs at I ≈ 14 ħ in the A ≈ 180 rare-earth nuclei.
- In ¹⁸⁴Os, the t-band has a K^π = 10⁺ band head; in ¹⁸²Os, the t-band has a K^π = 8⁺ band head.
- The K^π = 25⁺ isomer in ¹⁸²Os has a half-life of T₁/₂ = 130 ns.
- The authors present calculations for I = 14 ħ (before crossing) and I = 16 ħ (after crossing) showing the overlap ⟨HFB(θ,J)|P̂^I†_KK|Wbbl^I_M⟩.

## Context

This work extends the wobbling motion concept originally introduced by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) for triaxial nuclei, and builds on the theoretical framework by Kerman and Onishi (1981) based on the time-dependent variational model for three-dimensional nuclear rotations. The paper addresses systematic observations of signature splitting and signature inversion in even-even nuclei of the A ≈ 180 region, including ¹⁸⁰⁻¹⁸⁴W and ¹⁸²⁻¹⁸⁶Os, and connects to experimental measurements by Podolyák et al. (2001) comparing transition rates between K^π = 8⁺ and K^π = 8⁻ bands in ¹⁸²Os. The results enable interpretation of the anomalous K = 25 isomer decay directly to the yrast band in ¹⁸²Os, and suggest that wobbling motion can enhance backbending where typical rotation-alignment is suppressed due to neutron Fermi level location.
