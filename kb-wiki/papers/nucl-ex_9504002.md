# Candidate Events in a Search for Muon Antineutrino to Electron Antineutrino Oscillations

> A search for $\nuebar$'s in excess of the number expected from conventional sources has been made using the Liquid Scintillator Neutrino Detector, located 30 m from a proton beam dump at LAMPF. A $\nuebar$ signal was detected via the reaction $\nuebar\,p \rightarrow e^{+}\,n$ with $e^+$ energy between 36 and $60\mev$, followed by a $γ$ from $np\rightarrow dγ$ ($2.2\mev$). Using strict cuts to identify $γ$'s correlated with positrons results in a signal of 9 events, with an expected background of $2.1 \pm 0.3$. A likelihood fit to the entire $e^+$ sample yields a total excess of $16.4^{+9.7}_{-8.9}\pm 3.3$ events, where the second uncertainty is systematic. If this excess is attributed to neutrino oscillations of the type $\numubar\rightarrow\nuebar$, it corresponds to an oscillation probability of ($0.34^{+0.20}_{-0.18}\pm 0.07$)\%.

## Key claim

The LSND experiment observes an excess of electron antineutrino-like events above conventional background, which could be interpreted as evidence for muon antineutrino to electron antineutrino oscillations, but the result is presented as candidate events with a statistical significance of less than 10^{-3} probability of being a fluctuation, not as a definitive discovery.

## Method

The Liquid Scintillator Neutrino Detector (LSND) at LAMPF, located 30 m from a proton beam dump, searched for electron antineutrinos via the reaction nu_e_bar p -> e+ n, requiring a positron with energy between 36 and 60 MeV followed by a 2.2 MeV gamma from neutron capture on a proton (np -> d gamma). A likelihood ratio R was constructed from the spatial separation, time difference, and number of phototube hits for the gamma candidate to distinguish correlated from accidental coincidences. The analysis compared beam-on data with beam-off data collected between accelerator pulses to determine the excess.

## Key numbers

- Signal of 9 beam-on events with R>30 in the 36-60 MeV positron energy range, with an expected background of 2.1 +/- 0.3 events
- Likelihood fit to the full positron sample yields a total excess of 16.4^{+9.7}_{-8.9} +/- 3.3 events (statistical and systematic uncertainties)
- Oscillation probability of (0.34^{+0.20}_{-0.18} +/- 0.07)% if attributed to nu_mu_bar -> nu_e_bar oscillations
- Total calculated nu_mu_bar decay-at-rest flux of 3.75 x 10^{13} nu/cm^2 at the detector center, with 7% uncertainty
- Electron energy resolution of approximately 7% and angular resolution of approximately 12 degrees
- Neutron capture gamma detection efficiency of 63% with the R>30 cut having 23% efficiency for events with a recoil neutron

## Context

This paper reports the first candidate events from the LSND experiment, extending earlier searches for neutrino oscillations at LAMPF by experiments E225 (Krakauer 1991) and E645 (Freedman 1993). The observed excess is not in conflict with these previous limits but some of the allowed oscillation parameter region is excluded by the KARMEN experiment at ISIS, the E776 experiment at BNL (Borodovsky 1992), and the Bugey reactor experiment (Achkar 1995). The result motivated further data collection and analysis by LSND to confirm or refute the oscillation interpretation, and later became a key piece of evidence for the LSND anomaly that drove the development of the MiniBooNE experiment.
