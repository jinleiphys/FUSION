# Effective field theory investigations of the XYZ puzzle

> Quantum Chromodynamics, the theory of strong interactions, predicts several types of bound states. Among them are mesons ($q\bar{q}$) and baryons ($qqq$), which have been the only states observed in experiments for years. However, in the last decade, many states that do not fit this picture have been observed at $B$-factories (BaBar, Belle and CLEO), at $τ$-charm facilities (CLEO-c, BESIII) and also at proton-proton colliders (CDF, D0, LHCb, ATLAS, CMS). There is growing evidence that at least some of the new charmonium- and bottomonium-like states, the so-called XYZ mesons, are new forms of matter such as quark-gluon hybrids, mesonic molecules or different arrangements of tetraquarks, pentaquarks... Effective Field Theories (EFTs) have been constructed for heavy-quark-antiquark bound states, but a general study of the XYZ mesons within the same framework has not yet been done. The scope of this conference proceedings is to discuss the possibilities we have in developing novel EFTs that, characterizing the conventional quarkonium states, facilitate also the systematic and model-independent description of the new exotic matter, in particular, the hybrid mesons.

## Key claim

Effective Field Theories (EFTs) have been successfully constructed for conventional heavy quarkonium states, but no general EFT framework yet exists for the XYZ mesons; this conference proceedings discusses the possibilities for developing novel EFTs that, while characterizing conventional quarkonium, also enable a systematic and model-independent description of exotic matter, particularly hybrid mesons.

## Method

The paper reviews the existing nonrelativistic EFTs for conventional heavy quarkonium, Nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the scale \(m\) and potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) for the scale \(mv\), and examines how the dynamical situation changes for XYZ states near or above open-flavour thresholds. It then focuses on heavy quarkonium hybrids as a concrete example, describing how the matching between NRQCD and pNRQCD yields the hybrid static energies at short distances via the octet potential plus a gluelump mass, with higher-order corrections from the multipole expansion and \(1/m\) contributions partially computed by Berwein et al. (2015).

## Key numbers

- The Belle Collaboration discovered an enhancement at \(3872\,{\rm MeV}\) in the \(\pi^{+}\pi^{-}J/\psi\) invariant mass spectrum, the \(X(3872)\) state.
- Bottomonium has \(v^{2} \sim 0.1\) and charmonium has \(v^{2}\sim 0.3\), satisfying the nonrelativistic condition \(v\ll1\).
- The hierarchy of scales for heavy quarkonium is \(m \gg p \sim 1/r \sim mv \gg E \sim mv^2\).

## Context

This work extends the successful EFT descriptions of conventional heavy quarkonium (NRQCD from Caswell (1985) and Bodwin (1994); pNRQCD from Pineda (1997) and Brambilla (1999)) into the domain of XYZ exotic states, which have been observed at B-factories (BaBar, Belle, CLEO), tau-charm facilities (CLEO-c, BESIII), and proton-proton colliders (CDF, D0, LHCb, ATLAS, CMS). The paper specifically builds on the pNRQCD characterization of hybrid static energies initiated by Berwein et al. (2015), and aims to enable a quantitative, model-independent EFT description of hybrid mesons and other exotic matter, supporting upcoming experiments such as LHCb@LHC and PANDA@FAIR.
