# Charged Kaon multiplicities of Semi-inclusive DIS off the deuteron target

> We investigate the results of the charged kaon multiplicities off the deuteron target from HERMES and COMPASS experiments. The discrepancy of the two data sets cannot be explained by different $Q^2$ values. Furthermore we examine the empirical parametrization of the fragmentation functions, DSS2017, and find that the agreement between the theoretical predictions and the HERMES data is less satisfactory as claimed.

## Key claim

The authors find that the DSS2017 fragmentation function parametrization, which was claimed to describe both HERMES and COMPASS charged kaon multiplicity data well simultaneously, actually shows less satisfactory agreement with the HERMES data than claimed, with discrepancies reaching up to 40% in the middle-\(x\) region.

## Method

The authors applied the next-to-leading-order (NLO) formula for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) using the DSS2017 fragmentation functions together with MMHT and NNPDF parton distribution functions (PDFs) to compute charged kaon multiplicities off the deuteron target. They compared the theoretical predictions at the precise \(Q^2\) values of each HERMES and COMPASS data point, both before and after integrating over the \(z\) range, and examined the agreement in linear scale rather than logarithmic scale.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the theoretical prediction using DSS2017 and the HERMES data reaches **40%** in the middle-\(x\) region with \(Q^2\) between **2–4 GeV\(^2\)**.
- For the \(K^{+}\) case, theoretical curves are larger than the HERMES data by about **10%** when \(z\) is smaller than **0.35**, across all data points at different \(x\) and \(Q^2\) values.
- The HERMES data points span \(x\) from **0.0033** to **0.452** and \(Q^2\) from **1.1931 GeV\(^2\)** to **10.2355 GeV\(^2\)**; the COMPASS data points span \(x\) from **0.0085** to **0.2502** and \(Q^2\) from **1.1709 GeV\(^2\)** to **20.0857 GeV\(^2\)**.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LO analysis (Yang 2015) which found that extracting strange-quark PDFs from kaon multiplicities depends crucially on the choice of fragmentation functions. The study directly tests the DSS2017 parametrization (de Florian 2017), which updated the earlier DSS2007 set by including COMPASS data (Adolph 2016). The results challenge the claim that DSS2017 describes both HERMES and COMPASS data well, and the authors plan to extend the analysis to COMPASS data and other hadron multiplicities to help resolve the discrepancy between the two experiments.
