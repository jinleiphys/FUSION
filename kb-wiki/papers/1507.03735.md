# Constituent-quark model description of triply heavy-baryon nonperturbative lattice QCD data

> This paper provides results for the spectra of triply charmed and bottom baryons based on a constituent quark model approach. We take advantage of the assumption that potential models are expected to describe triply heavy baryons to a similar degree of accuracy as the successful results obtained in the charmonium and bottomonium sectors. The high precision calculation of the ground state and positive and negative parity excited states recently reported by nonperturbative lattice QCD provides us with a unique opportunity to confront model predictions with data. This comparison may also help to build a bridge between two difficult to reconcile lattice QCD results, namely, the lattice SU(3) QCD static three-quark potential and the recent results of nonperturbative lattice QCD for the triply heavy-baryon spectra.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a simple Cornell-like potential (Coulomb plus linear confinement) can reproduce the nonperturbative lattice QCD spectra of triply bottom baryons, but for triply charm baryons an additional spin-spin interaction is needed to obtain a reliable charm quark mass; the Coulomb and confining strengths required are larger than those derived from SU(3) lattice QCD static three-quark potentials.

## Method

The authors solved the nonrelativistic three-body Schrodinger equation for triply heavy baryons using three independent numerical techniques: generalized Gaussians variational approach, hyperspherical harmonics, and Faddeev equations. They fitted the parameters of a Cornell-like potential (Coulomb term plus linear confinement term, with optional spin-spin term) to reproduce the ground state and excited state energies of the bbb and ccc systems reported in nonperturbative lattice QCD calculations by Meinel (2012) and Padmanath et al. (2013, 2014).

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters for the bbb and ccc systems: Coulomb strength A = 0.1875, linear confinement strength B = 0.1374 GeV^2
- For the bbb system, the bottom quark mass used was m_b = 4.655 GeV
- For the ccc system without spin-spin term, an unrealistic charm quark mass m_c = 2.050 GeV was needed; with spin-spin term, m_c = 1.6 GeV and r_0 = 0.28 fm gave good agreement
- Excitation energies from the best fit (bbb system): E_2(3/2^+) - E_1(3/2^+) = 469 +/- 14 MeV (lattice), E_3(3/2^+) - E_1(3/2^+) = 587 +/- 18 MeV (lattice), E_1(1/2^-) - E_1(3/2^+) = 335.5 +/- 9.5 MeV (lattice)
- Excitation energies from the best fit (ccc system): E_2(3/2^+) - E_1(3/2^+) = 554 +/- 31 MeV (lattice), E_3(3/2^+) - E_1(3/2^+) = 667 +/- 13 MeV (lattice), E_1(1/2^-) - E_1(3/2^+) = 357 +/- 9 MeV (lattice)
- The Q\bar Q Coulomb strength from charmonium/bottomonium phenomenology is a ~ 0.51-0.52, giving A/a < 1/2 for the baryon case

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach that successfully described charmonium and bottomonium spectra (Quigg 1979, Eichten 1980, 2008) to triply heavy baryons. It directly addresses the discrepancy noted by Vijande (2015) between the static three-quark potential from SU(3) lattice QCD (Takahashi 2002) and the nonperturbative lattice QCD results for bbb and ccc baryons (Meinel 2010, 2012; Padmanath 2013). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to use constituent quark model predictions for guidance, while also providing constraints on the functional form and parameter space of potential models for heavy baryons.
