# Parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear forces

> Parity-violating and time-reversal conserving (PVTC) and parity-violating and time-reversal-violating (PVTV) forces in nuclei form only a tiny component of the total interaction between nucleons. The study of these tiny forces can nevertheless be of extreme interest because they allow to obtain information on fundamental symmetries using nuclear systems. The PVTC interaction derives from the weak interaction between the quarks inside nucleons and nuclei and the study of PVTC effects opens a window on the quark-quark weak interaction. The PVTV interaction is sensitive to more exotic interactions at the fundamental level, in particular to strong CP violation in the Standard Model Lagrangian, or even to exotic phenomena predicted in various beyond-the-Standard-Model scenarios. The presence of these interactions can be revealed either by studying various asymmetries in polarized scattering of nuclear systems, or by measuring the presence of non-vanishing permanent electric dipole moments of nucleons, nuclei and diamagnetic atoms and molecules. In this contribution, we review the derivation of the nuclear PVTC and PVTV interactions within various frameworks. We focus in particular on the application of chiral effective field theory, which allows for a more strict connection with the fundamental interactions at the quark level. We investigate PVTC and PVTV effects induced by these potential on several few-nucleon observables, such as the longitudinal asymmetry in proton-proton scattering and radiative neutron-proton capture, and the electric dipole momentsof the deuteron and the trinucleon system.

# Parity- and Time-Reversal-Violating Nuclear Forces

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (χEFT) provides a systematic and model-independent framework for deriving parity-violating time-reversal-conserving (PVTC) and parity-violating time-reversal-violating (PVTV) nuclear forces, but the convergence of χEFT appears problematic for contributions proportional to the ππNN low-energy constants (LECs) c_i due to the large values of those coefficients; including the Δ(1232) as an explicit degree of freedom may improve convergence.

## Method

The authors review the derivation of PVTC and PVTV nuclear interactions within three frameworks: phenomenological boson exchange models (particularly the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein model), pionless effective field theory, and chiral effective field theory (χEFT). They apply the χEFT-derived potentials to calculate selected few-nucleon observables including the longitudinal asymmetry in proton-proton scattering, radiative neutron-proton capture, and electric dipole moments (EDMs) of the deuteron and trinucleon systems (³H and ³He), using the nuclear Schrödinger equation with potentials expanded up to N²LO for PVTV and NLO/N²LO for PVTC interactions.

## Key numbers

- The PVTC NN potential at NLO contains six LECs (five contact operators plus the PVTC pion-nucleon coupling constant h¹_π); at N²LO, five additional LECs appear
- The PVTV χEFT interaction depends on 11 coupling constants
- The discrepancy between using N²LO and N⁴LO parity-conserving time-reversal-conserving (PCTC) potentials for EDM calculations of light nuclei is approximately 5%
- Current experimental upper bound on the neutron EDM: |d_n| < 3.0 × 10⁻¹³ e fm (Baker 2006, Afach 2015)
- Current experimental upper bound on the proton EDM (inferred from ¹⁹⁹Hg): |d_p| < 2.0 × 10⁻¹² e fm (Graner 2016)
- Current experimental upper bound on the electron EDM: |d_e| < 1.1 × 10⁻¹⁶ e fm (Andreev 2018)
- Expected size of the nucleon EDM from the CKM mechanism in the Standard Model: |d_N^CKM| ~ 10⁻¹⁸ e fm
- Proposed storage ring experiments aim to reach an accuracy of ~ 10⁻¹⁶ e fm for light nuclei EDMs
- The expected enhancement factor for PVTV effects in heavy nuclei from polarized neutron transmission can be as large as 10⁶

## Context

This review extends the line of work on parity-violating nuclear forces that began with the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) model (Desplanques 1979) and later χEFT formulations by Zhu (2004) and de Vries (2013). It provides a comprehensive comparison between the meson-exchange approach, pionless EFT, and χEFT frameworks for both PVTC and PVTV interactions. The work enables future experimental programs at facilities such as the Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) and storage ring experiments to extract the LECs from measurements of EDMs in light nuclei (²H, ³H, ³He) and from polarized scattering observables, which together could identify the fundamental sources of CP violation beyond the Standard Model.
