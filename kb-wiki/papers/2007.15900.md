# Three identical bosons: Properties in non-integer dimensions and in external fields

> Three-body systems that are continuously squeezed from a three-dimensional (3D) space into a two-dimensional (2D) space are investigated. Such a squeezing can be obtained by means of an external confining potential acting along a single axis. However, this procedure can be numerically demanding, or even undoable, especially for large squeezed scenarios. An alternative is provided by use of the dimension $d$ as a parameter that changes continuously within the range $2\leq d \leq 3$. The simplicity of the $d$-calculations is exploited to investigate the evolution of three-body states after progressive confinement. The case of three identical spinless bosons with relative $s$-waves in 3D, and a harmonic oscillator squeezing potential is considered. We compare results from the two methods and provide a translation between them, relating dimension, squeezing length, and wave functions from both methods. All calculations are then possible entirely within the simpler $d$-method, but simultaneously providing the equivalent geometry with the external potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the evolution of three-body bound states under progressive confinement from 3D to 2D can be accurately computed using a simpler method where the spatial dimension \(d\) is treated as a continuous parameter between 2 and 3, provided a translation between \(d\) and the squeezing length of an external harmonic oscillator potential is established. The equivalence holds exactly only for harmonic oscillator two-body interactions and is approximate for more realistic short-range potentials.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method in two parallel approaches: one with the spatial dimension \(d\) as a continuous parameter (2 ≤ d ≤ 3) and no external potential, and another in 3D with an explicit harmonic oscillator squeezing potential along the z-axis. For the \(d\)-method, they restrict to relative s-waves to avoid complications with spherical harmonics in non-integer dimensions. They compare the resulting energies and wave functions for three identical spinless bosons interacting via Gaussian and Morse two-body potentials, and establish a mapping between the dimension parameter \(d\) and the harmonic oscillator length \(b_{ho}\) by equating the energies from both methods for the case of harmonic oscillator two-body interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the Gaussian potential A\(_g\) (strength \(S = -2.86\)): 3D two-body binding energy \(E_{3D}^{(2bd)} = -3.301 \times 10^{-3}\) in units of \(\hbar^2/mb^2\), 3D scattering length \(a_{3D} = 18.122\) in units of \(b\)
- For the Gaussian potential B\(_g\) (strength \(S = -14.50\)): 3D two-body binding energy \(E_{3D}^{(2bd)} = -5.075\) in units of \(\hbar^2/mb^2\), 3D scattering length \(a_{3D} = -0.201\) in units of \(b\)
- For the Morse potential A\(_m\) (strength \(S = 0.95\)): 3D two-body binding energy \(E_{3D}^{(2bd)} = -3.751 \times 10^{-3}\) in units of \(\hbar^2/mb^2\), 3D scattering length \(a_{3D} = 18.122\) in units of \(b\)
- For the Morse potential B\(_m\) (strength \(S = 3.34\)): 3D two-body binding energy \(E_{3D}^{(2bd)} = -0.665\) in units of \(\hbar^2/mb^2\), 3D scattering length \(a_{3D} = -0.201\) in units of \(b\)
- The relation between dimension \(d\) and squeezing frequency \(\omega\) for harmonic oscillator two-body interactions: \(d = 2 + \sqrt{1 + \omega^2/\omega_{pp}^2} - \omega/\omega_{pp}\), where \(\omega_{pp}\) is the two-body oscillator strength
- The relation between \(d\) and the harmonic oscillator length \(b_{ho}\): \(\omega/\omega_{pp} = b_{pp}^2/b_{ho}^2 = -(d-1)(d-3)/[2(d-2)]\), where \(b_{pp}\) is the two-body oscillator length

## Context

This work extends the \(d\)-dimensional method previously developed for two-body systems by Garrido and Jensen (2019) to three-body systems, providing a computationally simpler alternative to explicit 3D calculations with external confining potentials. The approach builds on the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method formulated for arbitrary dimensions by Nielsen et al. (2001) and Jensen et al. (2004). The results enable efficient computation of three-body bound state properties under arbitrary confinement without the numerical difficulties that arise in direct 3D calculations for large squeezing, and provide a framework for interpreting \(d\)-dimensional wave functions as deformed 3D wave functions through a scaling parameter.
