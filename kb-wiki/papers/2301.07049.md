# Many-body neutrino flavor entanglement in a simple dynamic model

> Dense neutrino gases form in extreme astrophysical sites, and the flavor content of the neutrinos likely has an important impact on the subsequent dynamical evolution of their environment. Through coherent forward scattering among neutrinos, the flavor content of the gas evolves under a time-dependent potential which can be modeled in a quantum many-body formalism as an all-to-all coupled spin-spin interaction. This two-body potential generically introduces entanglement and greatly complicates the study of these systems. In this work we study the evolution of the quantum many-body problem as well as the typically employed mean-field approximation to it for a small number of neutrinos ($N = 16$). We consider randomly chosen one- and two-body couplings in the Hamiltonian, and the resulting evolution of several initial product states. We subsequently compare many-body and mean-field predictions for one-body observables, and we consider one- and two-body entanglement to assess under what conditions the many-body and mean-field predictions are likely to disagree. Except for a special category of prototypical initial conditions, we find that the typically employed mean-field approximation is insufficient to capture the evolution of one-body operators in the systems we consider. We also observe a loss of coherence in one- and two-body trace-reduced subsystems which suggests that the evolution may be well approximated as a classical mixture of separable states.

# Many-body neutrino flavor entanglement in a simple dynamic model

## Key claim

Except for fully polarized initial product states (all neutrinos in the same flavor), the mean-field approximation fails to capture the evolution of one-body operators for a system of 16 neutrinos with randomly chosen one- and two-body couplings; the many-body evolution instead produces final states whose few-neutrino reduced density matrices are well described by statistical (thermal) distributions.

## Method

The authors model the flavor evolution of 16 neutrinos using an all-to-all coupled Heisenberg spin Hamiltonian with a time-dependent neutrino-neutrino interaction strength, adopting a two-flavor approximation where each neutrino is an SU(2) spinor. They compare the exact quantum many-body evolution against the mean-field approximation for two classes of two-body couplings (uniform and random angular couplings) and for both fully polarized and partially polarized initial product states, quantifying disagreement via one-body observables, entanglement entropy, and trace distances between reduced density matrices.

## Key numbers

- System size: N = 16 neutrinos
- Reference vacuum oscillation frequency: ω₀ ≈ 1 km⁻¹ for a 10 MeV neutrino
- Initial neutrino-neutrino coupling strength: μ(t=0) = 80 ω₀
- Geometric parameters for the time-dependent coupling: R_ν = 32.2 ω₀⁻¹, r₀ = 210.64 ω₀⁻¹
- Vacuum mixing angle: θ_vac = 0.584
- For the partially polarized initial condition, neutrinos with ω_i > 12 ω₀ are initialized as ν_τ flavor states

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutrino flavor evolution in dense astrophysical environments (core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers) using Bethe-Ansatz techniques for integrable models with uniform couplings (Birol 2018, Cervia 2019, Patwardhan 2021). It contradicts the expectation that mean-field treatments are generally sufficient, showing that for non-integrable (random coupling) Hamiltonians, the many-body system undergoes dephasing and relaxation to a statistical ensemble, unlike the integrable uniform-coupling case. The results motivate further investigation of semi-classical approaches beyond mean-field (Lacroix 2022) and connections to dynamical phase transitions in static models (Roggero 2021, 2022).
