# Attractor for 1+1D viscous hydrodynamics with general rapidity distribution

> Attractor, supposed to be one of the possible answer for the early applicability of hydrodynamics in the evolution with different initial conditions, has attracted great attention to the fast decreasing of degrees of freedom in heavy ion collision. We found attractor behaviors for 1+1D viscous hydrodynamics with general rapidity distribution based on MIS theory. Meanwhile, we also observe that a rapid expansion in the fluid velocity is essential for a rapid early time attractor.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a novel attractor exists for 1+1D viscous hydrodynamics with general rapidity distributions, but only when the expansion rate of the fluid is close to that of Bjorken flow; a rapid expansion in the fluid velocity is essential for a rapid early-time attractor.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the Müller–Israel–Stewart (MIS) viscous hydrodynamic equations in 1+1 dimensions, assuming homogeneity in the transverse plane but allowing non-trivial rapidity dependence. They initialize the hydro profile using a parametrization inspired by an asymmetric analytical solution (Shi 2022), varying the asymmetry parameter \(a\), the initial flow parameter \(f_\zeta\), and the initial shear parameter \(f_\pi\). The attractor is studied by constructing Lorentz-invariant dimensionless quantities \(\tilde{w} \equiv T/\theta\) and \(\varphi \equiv 1 + u^\mu \partial_\mu T/(\theta T)\), and comparing numerical solutions to the attractor equation originally derived by Heller and Spalinski (2015) with the replacement \(f \to \varphi\) and \(w \to \tilde{w}\).

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity coefficient is set to \(C_\eta = 1/(4\pi)\) and the relaxation time coefficient to \(C_\tau = (2 - \ln 2)/(2\pi)\), from \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super Yang–Mills theory.
- The initial time unit parameter is fixed to \(t_0 = 0.1\,\tau_i\) and the initial energy density to \(\varepsilon_i = \tau_i^{-4}\).
- The asymmetry parameter \(a\) is varied over \(\{1.1, 1.2, 1.3\}\); the initial flow parameter \(f_\zeta\) over \(\{0, 0.5, 1\}\); and the initial shear parameter \(f_\pi\) over \(\{0, 0.2, 0.4\}\).
- The Navier–Stokes limit gives \(\varphi_\mathrm{NS}(\tilde{w}) = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{4C_\eta}{9\tilde{w}}\).
- The attractor for pressure anisotropy is given by \(A^{P}(\tilde{w}) = \frac{3 - 4\varphi(\tilde{w})}{2\varphi(\tilde{w}) - 1}\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of hydrodynamic attractors, first found for (0+1)D boost-invariant systems by Heller and Spalinski (2015), to (1+1)D systems with general rapidity distributions. It builds on the "temporal shift" generalization of Bjorken flow introduced by Shi (2022) and Chen (2023), and addresses the speculation by Kurkela (2019) that longitudinal expansion, rather than interaction, is essential for attractor behavior. The results enable the study of hydrodynamization in more realistic heavy-ion collision scenarios where boost-invariance is broken, and provide a Lorentz-invariant framework for defining attractor quantities that can be applied to systems with arbitrary spatial dependence.
