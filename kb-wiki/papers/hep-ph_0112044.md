# Electromagnetic form factors in the light-front formalism and the Feynman triangle diagram: spin-0 and spin-1 two-fermion systems

> The connection between the Feynman triangle diagram and the light-front formalism for spin-0 and spin-1 two-fermion systems is analyzed. It is shown that in the limit q+ = 0 the form factors for both spin-0 and spin-1 systems can be uniquely determined using only the good amplitudes, which are not affected by spurious effects related to the loss of rotational covariance present in the light-front formalism. At the same time, the unique feature of the suppression of the pair creation process is maintained. Therefore, a physically meaningful one-body approximation, in which all the constituents are on their mass-shells, can be consistently formulated in the limit q+ = 0. Moreover, it is shown that the effects of the contact term arising from the instantaneous propagation of the active constituent can be canceled out from the triangle diagram by means of an appropriate choice of the off-shell behavior of the bound state vertexes; this implies that in case of good amplitudes the Feynman triangle diagram and the one-body light-front result match exactly. The application of our covariant light-front approach to the evaluation of the rho-meson elastic form factors is presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for spin-0 and spin-1 two-fermion systems, in the limit q⁺ = 0, the electromagnetic form factors can be extracted uniquely from the "good" amplitudes (μ = + and μ = y) which are free from spurious effects due to loss of rotational covariance in the light-front formalism, and that the Feynman triangle diagram matches exactly the one-body light-front result for these amplitudes when the bound-state vertex off-shell behavior is chosen appropriately.

## Method

The authors analyze the Feynman triangle diagram for spin-0 and spin-1 two-fermion systems using a covariant light-front approach based on a null four-vector ω that defines the null-plane direction. They introduce ω-dependent bound-state vertex functions that depend only on the free mass Mᵢ₀ of the constituent pair, which suppresses endpoint contributions and eliminates the Z-graph in the limit ω·q = 0. The trace calculations for the triangle diagram are performed and compared with the standard light-front formalism, identifying which current components (the "good" amplitudes) are free from instantaneous propagation terms and spurious ω-dependent structures.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Frederico, Pace, and Salme (1990s) on the Feynman triangle diagram for spin-0 and spin-1 systems, who found that the Z-graph contributed to both current components for spin-1 systems. The authors show that the conclusion of those works depended on the procedure for extracting form factors from current amplitudes. The paper provides a covariant formulation that enables a consistent one-body approximation in the limit q⁺ = 0, establishing a link with potential models through the radial wave functions wᵢ(k). The approach is applied to the ρ-meson elastic form factors for comparison with non-covariant light-front calculations of Cardarelli et al. (1995).
