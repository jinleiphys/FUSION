# Response function of strongly interacting Fermi gas in a virial expansion

> The dynamic response functions of strongly interacting fermion gas in homogeneous space are investigated in a virial expansion to second order. The density response function exhibits transition from atomic to molecular response, as the interaction strength increases and the system undergoes BCS-BEC crossover. The qualitative features of density and spin response agree with measurements from the Bragg spectroscopy experiments. The virial response is exact at low density and high temperature, therefore providing a benchmark for many-body response.

## Key claim

The virial expansion to second order for the dynamic response functions of a strongly interacting Fermi gas in homogeneous space is exact at low density and high temperature (small fugacity), providing a benchmark for many-body response; however, the qualitative agreement with Bragg spectroscopy experiments is limited to high temperatures (around \(T_F\) and above), and at unitarity the density response shows a double-peak structure only when the temperature is lowered to \(T = T_F\), where the fugacity is 0.49 and higher-order corrections may be sizable.

## Method

The dynamic density and spin response functions for a homogeneous, spin-unpolarized Fermi gas are computed using a virial expansion to second order in the fugacity \(z = e^{\mu/T}\). The formalism separates the response into center-of-mass and relative-motion contributions, using \(S\)-wave scattering states on the BCS side and additionally bound-state contributions on the BEC side, with the non-interacting piece subtracted to obtain finite results. The response functions are evaluated for a large momentum transfer \(q = 3k_F\) and at temperatures \(T = 2T_F\) and \(T = 3T_F\), with fugacity values kept small (largest \(z = 0.25\)) to justify the truncation.

## Key numbers

- Largest fugacity in the results is \(z = 0.25\) (BCS side \(1/k_Fa = -1\) and \(T/T_F = 2\)), indicating higher-order corrections are small.
- At unitarity and \(T = T_F\), the fugacity is \(z = 0.49\), so results at that point should be viewed with caution due to expected sizable higher-order corrections.
- At unitarity and \(T = 3T_F\), the fugacity is about 0.13, so higher-order terms are expected to contribute at about 10–20% of the second-order term.
- The dynamic response functions are calculated with a large momentum transfer \(q = 3k_F\) (and also \(q = 4.5k_F\) in one case).
- The static response functions \(S_{\uparrow\uparrow}(q=3k_F)\) and \(S_{\uparrow\downarrow}(q=3k_F)\) are shown as functions of \(1/k_Fa\) at \(T = 3T_F\).

## Context

This work extends the virial expansion for response functions, previously developed for trapped Fermi gases by Liu and Hu (2010, 2012), to the homogeneous case, providing compact closed-form expressions. The results qualitatively agree with Bragg spectroscopy experiments by Veeravalli et al. (2008) and the spin-response experiment (2012), though those experiments were performed at much lower temperatures in the superfluid phase. The formalism can be applied to other strongly interacting many-body systems at low density and high temperature, such as neutron matter and nuclear matter in supernova, and may be related to trapped-gas results via a local density approximation.
