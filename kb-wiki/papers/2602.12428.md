# Correlated and uncorrelated Monte Carlo neutron capture rate variations in weak $\textit{r}$-process simulations

> Reliable predictions of weak rapid neutron capture ($\textit{r}$-process) abundances require a systematic treatment of nuclear physics uncertainties, especially neutron capture rates far from stability. We employ new neutron capture rates from cross sections calculated with Yet Another Hauser-Feshbach Code ($\texttt{YAHFC}$) using an uncertainty-quantified Koning-Delaroche potential modified for use on neutron-rich systems. Using these rates as a baseline, we perform Monte-Carlo studies with independent rate variations (uncorrelated Monte Carlo) and find correlations between specific neutron capture rates and the resulting elemental abundances for the three weak r-process scenarios: two separate simulations of neutron star merger remnant accretion disks and a simulation of a magnetorotational supernova. We discuss the underlying nuclear dynamics that give rise to these correlations and the role of astrophysical conditions in them. We demonstrate how reducing the uncertainty in these rates would improve the prospects for conducting precision $\textit{r}$-process studies in the future. We additionally present a correlated Monte Carlo study, which incorporates the full covariance matrix that describes the relationships between individual neutron capture rates that arise from an uncertainty-quantified optical potential. We find that the magnitude of the uncertainty in the abundance pattern is similar to that produced by an uncorrelated Monte Carlo that employs only the on-diagonal components of the covariance matrix. We show how correlations restructure how the abundances co-vary, but do not necessarily decrease the overall uncertainty envelope.

# Correlated and uncorrelated Monte Carlo neutron capture rate variations in weak r-process simulations

## Key claim
The overall magnitude of uncertainty in the predicted elemental abundance pattern from correlated Monte Carlo simulations (using the full covariance matrix from an uncertainty-quantified optical potential) is similar to that from uncorrelated Monte Carlo simulations (using only diagonal uncertainties), but the correlations restructure how abundances co-vary rather than shrinking the total uncertainty envelope.

## Method
The authors calculated neutron capture rates using the Yet Another Hauser-Feshbach Code (YAHFC) with an uncertainty-quantified Koning-Delaroche optical model potential modified for neutron-rich systems (KDUQEF). They performed Monte Carlo studies with independent rate variations (uncorrelated) and with variations incorporating the full covariance matrix from the KDUQEF optical potential (correlated), applied to three weak r-process astrophysical scenarios: two neutron star merger remnant accretion disk simulations (Lund+24 and Metzger & Fernandez 14) and one magnetorotational supernova simulation (Reichert+21). They identified neutron capture rates most strongly correlated with elemental abundances using Pearson correlation coefficients and examined the nuclear flow pathways underlying these correlations.

## Key numbers
- Reducing neutron capture rate uncertainties by a factor of 5 for the 35 most consequential rates resulted in approximately 30% to 65% reductions in abundance pattern uncertainties for elements in the range Z = 36 to 54.
- The correlated Monte Carlo simulations used 5000 samples (set 3B) compared to uncorrelated simulations (set 3A) of the same size.
- Pearson correlation coefficients with magnitude greater than 0.30 were used to identify neutron capture rates correlated with elemental abundances; for example, $^{94}$Rb(n,gamma) showed correlation with Mo abundance of rho = 0.61 in the MF14 scenario and rho = 0.50 in the Lund+24 scenario.
- The rate $^{100}$Y(n,gamma) showed anticorrelation with Mo abundance of rho = -0.45 in MF14 and rho = -0.56 in Reichert+21 scenarios.

## Context
This work extends previous sensitivity studies of neutron capture rates in the weak r-process by Surman (2014) and Vescovi+2022, and applies Monte Carlo rate variation methods previously used for (alpha,n) reactions in weak r-process conditions (2020PhRvC.101e5807B, 2022ApJ...935...27P). The paper takes a first step toward a complete account of nuclear physics uncertainties in weak r-process models by focusing on the optical model potential, for which systematic uncertainties have been established, while noting that future work must include correlations from level densities and gamma-ray strength functions. The results enable improved constraints for neutron capture rates relevant to the r-process and demonstrate that correlated Monte Carlo methods can be effectively applied to nucleosynthesis calculations.
