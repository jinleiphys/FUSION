# Heavy ion jet physics studies using precision jet substructure and quark-gluon jet classification

> In the first talk I discuss the usefulness of jet grooming for testing jet quenching mechanisms, and I present a calculation of soft-drop jet mass distribution in proton-proton and heavy ion collisions. In the second talk I discuss the comprehensive studies of quark and gluon jet substructure as a way to search for qualitatively new jet modification signatures. I emphasize the importance of precise understanding of jet substructure as well as powerful analysis tools in future heavy ion jet physics studies, with direct applications to hard probes using heavy flavors and hadronically-decaying electroweak bosons.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of soft-drop jet mass distributions in proton-proton and heavy ion collisions using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), but the results are preliminary and do not yet include non-perturbative effects, so the calculated jet mass is systematically lower than measured values.

## Method

The authors use SCET to factorize the cross section for groomed jet mass distributions, defining a process-independent groomed jet mass function that captures soft-collinear radiation inside jets. They incorporate medium modification via medium-induced splitting functions calculated using SCET with Glauber gluon interactions, and apply the soft-drop grooming procedure to remove soft, wide-angle radiation. The calculation achieves next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy with resummation performed through renormalization group evolution.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive charged jet mass distributions calculated at NLL accuracy for 2.76 TeV PbPb and 5.02 TeV pPb collisions, compared with ALICE measurements
- Inclusive jet mass over transverse momentum (m/pT) distribution calculated for 5.02 TeV pp collisions, compared with ATLAS measurements
- Soft drop jet mass distribution calculated at NLO and NLL+NLO for 5.02 TeV pp collisions, compared with PYTHIA simulations
- Jet mass distribution in Z+jet events calculated at NLL for 7 TeV pp collisions, compared with CMS measurements

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based framework for jet substructure calculations developed by Li (2012), Chien (2012, 2014, 2015) to include medium modification effects in heavy ion collisions. The calculation provides a baseline for future precision jet substructure studies and enables systematic tests of jet quenching mechanisms by comparing groomed jet mass distributions across different hard scattering processes (inclusive jets, photon+jet, Z+jet events). The framework can be applied to hard probes using heavy flavors and hadronically-decaying electroweak bosons.
