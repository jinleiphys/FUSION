# Color Neutrality and the Gluon Distribution in a Very Large Nucleus

> We improve the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the gluon distribution functions in very large nuclei by imposing the condition that the nucleons should be color neutral. We find that enforcing color neutrality cures the infrared divergences in the transverse coordinates which are present in the McLerran-Venugopalan model. Since we obtain well-defined expressions for the distribution functions, we are able to draw unambiguous conclusions about various features of the model. In particular, we show that the gluon distribution functions in the absence of quantum corrections behave as 1/x to all orders in the coupling constant. Furthermore, our distribution functions exhibit saturation at small transverse momenta. The normalization of the distribution function we obtain is not arbitrary but specified in terms of the nucleon structure. We derive a sum rule for the integral of the gluon distribution function over transverse momenta, and show that the non-Abelian contributions serve only to modify the shape of the transverse momentum distribution. We obtain a relatively simple expression for the mean value of the transverse momentum-squared. The connection between the McLerran-Venugopalan model and the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi equation is discussed quantitatively. Finally, we illustrate our results in terms of a simple nuclear model due to Kovchegov.

## Key claim

The authors improve the McLerran-Venugopalan model by imposing color neutrality of nucleons, which cures the infrared divergences in transverse coordinates that plague the original model; this allows well-defined expressions for gluon distribution functions and unambiguous conclusions about their properties, though the results depend on the specific form chosen for the charge-density correlator that parameterizes confinement effects.

## Method

The authors modify the McLerran-Venugopalan model by replacing the purely local transverse charge-density correlator \(\mathcal{D}(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}') = \delta^2(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}')\) with a general correlator that enforces color neutrality of nucleons at some length scale. They compute the classical Yang-Mills vector potential in light-cone gauge from a source current \(J^+ = \rho(x^-,\mathbf{x})\), using a Gaussian ensemble for the charge density with the modified transverse correlator, then evaluate the gluon number density via the two-point vector potential correlation function and its Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

- The gluon distribution functions behave as \(1/x_F\) to all orders in the coupling constant when the charge-density correlator is Gaussian and local in the longitudinal coordinate.
- The condition for dropping the second term in the correlation function requires \(\Omega = N_c \chi / (16\pi \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2) \gg e\), which for the model described implies a nucleus with of order \(10^5\) nucleons.
- The mean transverse momentum-squared is computed to order unity, with the leading \(Q^2\) behavior linear in the number of nucleons and logarithmic corrections quadratic in the charge per unit transverse area.

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) by addressing its infrared divergence problem, which previously required cutting off transverse integrations at \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^{-1}\) as done by Jalilian-Marian et al. (1997). The color neutrality condition incorporates confinement effects parameterized by the charge-density correlator, similar in spirit to approaches by Mueller (1990) and others, but examined in greater detail. The results enable quantitative connections to the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations and provide a foundation for renormalization group treatments of small-\(x\) gluon distributions.
