# Matrix elements of the Argonne v18 potential

> We discuss two approaches to the calculation of matrix elements of the Argonne v18 potential. The first approach is applicable in the case of a single-particle basis of harmonic-oscillator wave functions. In this case we use the Talmi transformation, implemented numerically using the Moshinsky transformation brackets, to separate the center-of-mass and relative coordinates degrees of freedom. Integrals involving the radial part of the potential are performed using Gauss-Hermite quadrature formulas, and convergence is achieved for sets of at least 512 points. We validate the calculation of matrix elements of the Argonne v18 potential using a second approach suitable for the case of an arbitrary functional form of the single-particle wave functions. When the model space is represented in terms of harmonic-oscillator wave functions, results obtained using these two approaches are shown to to be identical within numerical accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper presents two complementary numerical approaches for computing matrix elements of the Argonne v18 potential, and demonstrates that when the single-particle basis consists of harmonic-oscillator wave functions, the results from both methods are identical within numerical accuracy, but the second approach is designed to handle arbitrary functional forms of single-particle wave functions where the Moshinsky-bracket method becomes computationally prohibitive.

## Method

The first approach uses the Talmi transformation implemented via Moshinsky transformation brackets to separate center-of-mass and relative coordinates in a harmonic-oscillator basis, with radial integrals evaluated using Gauss-Hermite quadrature. The second approach calculates matrix elements directly in a basis of single-particle wave functions of arbitrary functional form, expanding the radial part as a linear combination of harmonic-oscillator wave functions to enable Gaussian quadrature. Both methods are applied to the seven basic operator types of the Argonne v18 potential (central, spin-spin, tensor, spin-orbit, ℓ², ℓ²(σ_i·σ_j), and (ℓ·S)²) and their isospin-dependent counterparts.

## Key numbers

- Convergence of Gauss-Hermite quadrature for radial integrals is achieved for sets of at least 512 points.
- The Argonne v18 potential was fit to the Nijmegen NN scattering database containing 1787 pp and 2514 np data in the range 0–350 MeV, with a χ² per datum of 1.09.
- The strong interaction part of the potential contains 18 operator terms: 14 charge-independent operators (7 basic operators and their τ_i·τ_j counterparts), 3 charge-dependent operators (T_ij, (σ_i·σ_j)T_ij, S_ij T_ij), and 1 charge-asymmetric operator (τ_zi + τ_zj).

## Context

This work extends the methodology for computing two-body matrix elements of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, specifically the Argonne v18 potential (Wiringa et al., 1995), which is an updated version of the Argonne v14 potential. The harmonic-oscillator approach using Moshinsky brackets follows the Talmi transformation technique (Talmi, 1952) and its numerical implementation (Moshinsky, 1959). The second, more general approach enables calculations in bases where the single-particle wave functions have exponential rather than Gaussian asymptotic behavior, which is relevant for ab initio studies of medium and large nuclear systems where harmonic-oscillator bases may converge slowly. This enables future work using linear combinations of harmonic-oscillator wave functions or different functional representations for hole and particle states.
