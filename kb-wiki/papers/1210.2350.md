# Quark matter equation of state and stellar properties

> In this paper we study strange matter by investigating the stability window within the QMDD model at zero temperature and check that it can explain the very massive pulsar recently detected. We compare our results with the ones obtained from the MIT bag model and see that the QMDD model can explain larger masses, due to the stiffening of the equation of state.

# Quark matter equation of state and stellar properties

## Key claim

The QMDD (quark mass density-dependent) model can explain the very massive pulsar PSR J1614-2230 (mass 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙) as a quark star, but only when the stability window is analyzed using equal quark chemical potentials (symmetric matter) rather than the charge-neutral β-equilibrium conditions usually applied to stars; the model systematically yields larger maximum masses than the MIT bag model due to a stiffer equation of state.

## Method

The authors computed the equation of state for strange quark matter at zero temperature using the QMDD model, where quark dynamical masses scale inversely with baryon number density (parameter C) plus a current strange quark mass m0s, and compared results with the MIT bag model (fixed quark masses and bag constant B). They determined the stability window by requiring two-flavor quark matter to be unstable (energy per baryon > 930 MeV) and three-flavor strange matter to be stable (energy per baryon ≤ 930 MeV), then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations for quark stars.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ (Shapiro delay measurement from 2010)
- Stability condition threshold: energy per baryon ≤ 930 MeV (iron binding energy), or 934 MeV when finite size effects are included
- For the QMDD model with m0s = 150 MeV, the stability border moves from C = 80.1 MeV/fm³ to 79.2 MeV/fm³ when finite size effects are considered
- For the MIT bag model with m0s = 150 MeV, the stability border moves from B^{1/4} = 155.1 MeV to 155.8 MeV when finite size effects are considered
- QMDD model can describe compact objects more massive than the MIT model, but fails to describe pulsars with low radii

## Context

This work extends the QMDD model approach originally proposed by Fowler (1981) and applied to strange matter by Chakrabarty (1991), and addresses the thermodynamic consistency issues raised by Lugones (1995) and Peng (2000). The paper specifically tests whether the QMDD model can accommodate the high-mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest, 2010), which previous MIT bag model calculations could not explain. The results enable future studies of protoquark stars at finite temperature, where the free energy per baryon must be used instead of energy per baryon.
