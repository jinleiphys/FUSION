# Exclusive $J/ψ$ photo-production on nuclei

> Motivated by the recent experimental developments, the Pom-CQM model of the $γ+ N \to J/ψ+ N$ reaction of Lee et al. [Eur. Phys. J. A. 58, 252 (2022)] and Sakinah et al. [Phys. Rev. C. 109, 065204 (2024)] has been applied to predict the exclusive $J/ψ$ photo-production on nuclei ($A$). Within the multiple scattering theory, the calculations have been performed by including the impulse amplitude $T^{\rm IMP}_{J/ψA,γA}$ and the $J/ψ$-nucleus final state interaction (FSI) amplitude $T^{\rm FSI}_{J/ψA,γA}$. For the deuteron target, $T^{\rm IMP}_{J/ψd,γd}$ is calculated exactly using the wave function generated from the realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. It is found that, near the threshold region, the $J/ψ$ photo-production cross sections depend sensitively on the $d$-state of the deuteron wave function. The FSI amplitude $T^{\rm FSI}_{J/ψA,γA}$ is calculated using the first-order optical potential constructed from the $J/ψ$-$N$ scattering amplitude generated from the employed Pom-CQM model. It turns out that the FSI has significant effects in the large momentum-transfer region. By using the conventional fixed scatter approximation (FSA) and the nuclear form factors from the variational Monte-Carlo (VMC) calculations of Lonardoni et al. [Phys. Rev.C. 96, 024326 (2017)], the cross sections of the $J/ψ$ photo-production on ${^4\rm He}$, ${^{16}\rm O}$, and ${^{40}\rm Ca}$ are also predicted for future experimental investigations at JLab and EIC.

## Key claim

The Pom-CQM model, previously fitted to JLab data for the proton, predicts that exclusive $J/\psi$ photo-production on nuclei is sensitive to the nuclear structure, particularly the deuteron $d$-state near threshold, and that final-state interactions (FSI) are significant in the large momentum-transfer region, but the fixed-scatter approximation (FSA) is only valid at small momentum transfer and fails near threshold.

## Method

The authors applied the Pom-CQM model of the $\gamma + N \to J/\psi + N$ reaction within multiple scattering theory, calculating the impulse amplitude $T^{\rm IMP}_{J/\psi A,\gamma A}$ exactly for the deuteron using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne-V18) and including relativistic kinematics via Lorentz boosts. For heavier nuclei (${^4\rm He}$, ${^{16}\rm O}$, ${^{40}\rm Ca}$), they used the fixed-scatter approximation (FSA) with nuclear form factors from variational Monte-Carlo (VMC) calculations, and computed the $J/\psi$-nucleus final-state interaction (FSI) amplitude $T^{\rm FSI}_{J/\psi A,\gamma A}$ from a first-order optical potential built from the $J/\psi$-$N$ scattering amplitude of the Pom-CQM model.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section of $\gamma d \to J/\psi d$: relativistic effects cause results about an order of magnitude larger than nonrelativistic ones at $E_\gamma = 6$ GeV in the region $-t \geqslant 1.8$ GeV$^2$.
* Deuteron form factors $F_0(t)$ and $F_2(t)$ from the Argonne-V18 potential: $F_2(t)$ peaks in the region where $F_0(t)$ has a minimum, and at large $-t$, $F_2(t)$ is larger than $F_0(t)$.
* Differential cross sections $d\sigma/dt$ for $\gamma d \to J/\psi d$ at $E_\gamma = 6$ GeV: results using only the $s$-wave part of the deuteron wave function are much smaller than full results in the large $-t$ region.
* The FSA is a good approximation only in the small $-t$ region; it is not valid at $E_\gamma = 6$ GeV (near threshold) but is reasonable at $E_\gamma = 8$ and $10$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Pom-CQM model of Lee et al. (2022) and Sakinah et al. (2024) from the proton to nuclear targets, motivated by upcoming experiments at JLab and the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). It builds on the multiple scattering theory of Watson (1960), Kerman (1959), and Landau (1973), and on the authors' previous work on $\gamma + {^4\rm He} \to \phi + {^4\rm He}$ (Kim et al., 2021) which accounted for LEPS data. The predictions for ${^4\rm He}$, ${^{16}\rm O}$, and ${^{40}\rm Ca}$ are intended for future experimental investigations at JLab and EIC, and the work explores how nuclear short-range correlations can be probed by $J/\psi$ photo-production, as suggested by Hatta (2019).
