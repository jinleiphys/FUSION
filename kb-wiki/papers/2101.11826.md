# Testing isospin symmetry breaking in ab initio nuclear theory

> In this work we present the first steps towards benchmarking isospin symmetry breaking in ab initio nuclear theory for calculations of superallowed Fermi $β$-decay. Using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group, we calculate b and c coefficients of the isobaric multiplet mass equation, starting from two different Hamiltonians constructed from chiral effective field theory. We compare results to experimental measurements for all T=1 isobaric analogue triplets of relevance to superallowed $β$-decay for masses A=10 to A=74 and find an overall agreement within approximately 250 keV of experimental data for both b and c coefficients. A greater level of accuracy, however, is obtained by a phenomenological Skyrme interaction or a classical charged-sphere estimate. Finally, we show that evolution of the valence-space operator does not meaningfully improve the quality of the coefficients with respect to experimental data, which indicates that higher-order many-body effects are likely not responsible for the observed discrepancies.

## Key claim

The paper finds that *ab initio* VS-IMSRG calculations reproduce the overall magnitude of the IMME b and c coefficients for T=1 triplets within about 250 keV of experiment, but that a phenomenological Skyrme interaction or a classical charged-sphere estimate actually yields better accuracy, and that IMSRG evolution does not meaningfully improve the coefficients relative to bare interactions.

## Method

The authors used the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) at the IMSRG(2) truncation level to compute binding energies for all members of T=1, J^π=0^+ isobaric analogue triplets from A=10 to A=74. They started from two chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (1.8/2.0 (EM) and N2LO_sat) and varied the normal-ordering reference state among four prescriptions to estimate truncation errors. Binding energies were extrapolated to the infinite basis limit from harmonic-oscillator calculations at e_max=8,10,12, then converted to mass excesses to extract the IMME b and c coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Overall agreement between VS-IMSRG and experiment for both b and c coefficients is within approximately 250 keV.
- The classical charged-sphere estimate (Woods-Saxon density, radius R=r0 A^{1/3} with r0=1.2 fm, diffuseness a=0.524 fm) reproduces the general trend of the b coefficients; the Skyrme SKX interaction reproduces finer details.
- For the sd shell, consistently evolved Coulomb operator accounts for 1/3 to 1/2 of the magnitude of the c coefficient.
- The b coefficient from the charged-sphere estimate is b = -3e^2/(5r0) A^{2/3} with r0=1.2 fm; the c coefficient from the same estimate is c = 3e^2/(5r0) A^{-1/3}.
- The EM interaction predicts charge radii approximately 3-5% below experiment.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to test isospin symmetry breaking with non-empirical methods, which used many-body perturbation theory in the sd-shell (Holt 2013, Gallant 2014, Brodeur 2017) or NN-only interactions in the p- and pf-shells (Caurier 2002, Ormand 2017), and found deficiencies attributed to neglected three-nucleon forces or perturbative convergence. The present study uses nonperturbative VS-IMSRG with consistent NN+3N forces, but finds that IMSRG evolution does not systematically improve IMME coefficients over bare interactions, and that deviations near shell closures are due to the IMSRG(2) truncation. The results indicate that parallel improvements in nuclear forces and many-body methods are needed before *ab initio* ISB corrections can achieve the confidence required for superallowed beta-decay tests of the Standard Model.
