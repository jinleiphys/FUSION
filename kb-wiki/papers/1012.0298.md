# Non-Abelian plasma instabilities: SU(3) vs. SU(2)

> We present the first 3+1 dimensional simulations of non-Abelian plasma instabilities in gauge-covariant Boltzmann-Vlasov equations for the QCD gauge group SU(3) as well as for SU(4) and SU(5). The real-time evolution of instabilities for a plasma with stationary momentum-space anisotropy is studied using a hard-loop effective theory that is discretized in the velocities of hard particles. We find that the numerically less expensive calculations using the group SU(2) essentially reproduce the nonperturbative dynamics of non-Abelian plasma instabilities with higher rank gauge groups provided the mass parameters of the corresponding hard-loop effective theories are the same. In particular we find very similar spectra for the turbulent cascade that forms in the strong-field regime, which is associated with an approximately linear growth of energy in collective fields. The magnitude of the linear growth however turns out to increase with the number of colors.

# Non-Abelian plasma instabilities: SU(3) vs. SU(2)

## Key claim
The paper presents the first 3+1 dimensional simulations of non-Abelian plasma instabilities for the QCD gauge group SU(3) (and also SU(4) and SU(5)), and finds that the numerically cheaper SU(2) calculations essentially reproduce the nonperturbative dynamics of higher-rank gauge groups provided the mass parameters of the hard-loop effective theories are the same; however, the magnitude of the linear growth of energy in collective fields increases with the number of colors, scaling approximately proportional to N_c.

## Method
The authors solve gauge-covariant Boltzmann-Vlasov equations (hard-loop effective theory) discretized on a 3+1 dimensional cubic lattice, with the unit sphere of hard-particle velocities discretized in cylindrical coordinates (N_z × N_φ = 20 × 16 = 320 velocity directions per lattice site). They simulate the real-time evolution of instabilities for a stationary oblate momentum-space anisotropy (ξ = 10) for gauge groups SU(2), SU(3), SU(4), and SU(5), using spatial lattices from 32^3 to 128^3 and initializing with small random seed fields in the auxiliary W fields.

## Key numbers
- Anisotropy parameter ξ = 10 (oblate momentum-space distribution) used for all simulations.
- Unstable modes exist for longitudinal momenta 0 < |k_z| < μ = 1.072 m, where m is the mass parameter.
- The mode with largest growth rate γ_* = 0.12 m occurs at k_⊥ = 0 and k_z = k_* = 0.398 m.
- The asymptotic mass of propagating transverse gluons is m_∞ = 0.447 m.
- Lattice spacings range from a m_∞ = 0.5656 to 0.1414, corresponding to a/(λ_*/2) = 0.16 to 0.04 in terms of the wavelength of maximal growth.
- Initial total energy density of SU(3) is larger than SU(2) by a factor of 8/3 (ratio of gluon numbers).
- The power-law spectrum in the turbulent cascade has spectral index ν ≈ 2, with an inferred error bar of 1.8 ≲ ν ≲ 2.4.
- The ratio of the time derivative of energy densities for SU(3), SU(4), and SU(5) relative to SU(2) scales approximately with N_c (horizontal lines at 3/2, 4/2, and 5/2 for scaling by number of colors).

## Context
This work extends previous 3+1 dimensional hard-loop simulations of non-Abelian plasma instabilities, which had been performed only for SU(2) by Arnold et al. (2005) and Rebhan et al. (2005), and earlier effectively 1+1 dimensional SU(3) calculations by Rebhan et al. (2005). The results confirm the existence of a turbulent cascade with spectral index ν ≈ 2 found in SU(2) by Arnold et al. (2005), and show that this behavior persists for higher-rank gauge groups. The approximately N_c scaling of the linear growth rate in the non-Abelian regime is a new finding. The work enables quantitative predictions for the role of non-Abelian plasma instabilities in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, where the QCD gauge group SU(3) is physically relevant, and provides a benchmark for classical-statistical simulations such as those by Berges et al. (2008) which found a different late-time spectral index of ν = 4/3.
