# A systematic study of Zr and Sn isotopes in the Relativistic Mean Field theory

> The ground-state properties of Zr and Sn isotopes are studied within the relativistic mean field theory. Zr and Sn isotopes have received tremendous attention due to various reasons, including the predicted giant halos in the neutron-rich Zr isotopes, the unique feature of being robustly spherical in the region of $^{100}$Sn $\sim$ $^{132}$Sn and the particular interest of Sn isotopes to nuclear astrophysics. Furthermore, four (semi-) magic neutron numbers, 40, 50, 82 and 126, make these two isotopic chains particularly important to test the pairing correlations and the deformations in a microscopic model. In the present work, we carry out a systematic study of Zr and Sn isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line with deformation effects, pairing correlations and blocking effects for nuclei with odd number of neutrons properly treated. A constrained calculation with quadrupole deformations is performed to find the absolute minimum for each nucleus on the deformation surface. All ground-state properties, including the separation energies, the odd-even staggerings, the nuclear radii, the deformations and the single-particle spectra are analyzed and discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with a zero-range δ-force in the BCS pairing channel, implemented in a deformed Harmonic-Oscillator basis, can reproduce ground-state properties of Zr and Sn isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line, but the expansion method in the Harmonic-Oscillator basis cannot fully reproduce the sudden increase of neutron radii in halo nuclei, which would require solving the RMF equations in coordinate space.

## Method

The authors performed deformed RMF+BCS calculations using the TMA and NL3 parameter sets, with a zero-range δ-force in the particle-particle channel to account for continuum effects near the drip lines. A constrained calculation with quadrupole deformations was performed to find the absolute minimum on the deformation surface for each nucleus, and a blocking method without breaking time-reversal symmetry was adopted for nuclei with odd numbers of neutrons. The Harmonic-Oscillator basis expansion method was used with 20 shells for TMA calculations and 14 shells for NL3 calculations.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength: V₀ = 341.1 MeV fm³ for Zr isotopes and V₀ = 310.0 MeV fm³ for Sn isotopes, obtained by fitting experimental odd-even staggerings with a cut-off E_max − λ ≤ 8.0 MeV
- For ¹³²Sn, increasing the number of shells N from 14 to 20 changes total binding energy B by about 0.2%, rms charge radii R_c by 0.1%, and rms neutron radii R_n by 0.3%
- For ¹³⁰Zr, increasing N from 14 to 20 changes B by about 0.6%, R_c by 0.1%, and R_n by 0.7%; the ground state of ¹³⁰Zr has deformation β₂ₘ = −0.203
- The two-neutron drip-line nucleus for Sn isotopes is predicted to be ¹⁷⁶Sn by calculations with both TMA and NL3 parameter sets
- The double neutron separation energy goes to about −2.5 MeV for ¹⁷⁸Sn from about 1.4 MeV for ¹⁷⁶Sn
- The difference between R_n and R_p (neutron skin) reaches 0.9 fm for ¹⁴⁰Zr and 0.8 fm for ¹⁷⁶Sn
- The spin-orbit splitting between 2d₅/₂ and 2d₃/₂ states is around 1.8 MeV in the present calculations
- The single-particle energy of the 1s₁/₂ state increases about 10.0 MeV from −62.0 MeV in ¹⁰⁰Sn to −52.0 MeV in ¹⁷⁶Sn

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of Zr and Sn isotopes by including deformation effects, pairing correlations, and blocking effects for odd-neutron nuclei simultaneously, going beyond spherical assumptions used in earlier RCHB calculations by Meng (2002) and resonant RMF+BCS calculations by Sandulescu (2003). The results are compared with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) by Moller (1995) and the Hartree-Fock+BCS method by Goriely (2001). The study enables systematic predictions of ground-state properties for exotic nuclei near the drip lines, including the possible existence of one-neutron halos in neutron-rich Zr isotopes and the magicity of Z=50 in the neutron-rich region.
