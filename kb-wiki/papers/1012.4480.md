# Higgs Production with a Central Jet Veto at NNLL+NNLO

> A major ingredient in Higgs searches at the Tevatron and LHC is the elimination of backgrounds with jets. In current H -> WW -> lnulnu searches, jet algorithms are used to veto central jets to obtain a 0-jet sample, which is then analyzed to discover the Higgs signal. Imposing this tight jet veto induces large double logarithms which significantly modify the Higgs production cross section. These jet-veto logarithms are presently only accounted for at fixed order or with the leading-logarithmic summation from parton-shower Monte Carlos. Here we consider Higgs production with an inclusive event-shape variable for the jet veto, namely beam thrust Tau_cm, which has a close correspondence with a traditional p_T jet veto. Tau_cm allows us to systematically sum the large jet-veto logarithms to higher orders and to provide better estimates for theoretical uncertainties. We present results for the 0-jet Higgs production cross section from gluon fusion at next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic order (NNLL), fully incorporating fixed-order results at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). At this order the scale uncertainty is 15-20%, depending on the cut, implying that a larger scale uncertainty should be used in current Tevatron bounds on the Higgs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the 0-jet Higgs production cross section from gluon fusion, when defined using a beam thrust cut as a proxy for a central jet veto, can be computed at next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic (NNLL) order with full next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) fixed-order corrections, and that the resulting theoretical scale uncertainty is 15-20% depending on the cut, larger than the 7% uncertainty used in current Tevatron analyses.

## Method

The authors use the event-shape variable beam thrust (T_cm) to implement a central jet veto in Higgs production via gluon fusion, exploiting a factorization theorem in soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) that separates the cross section into hard, beam, and soft functions. They compute the singular part of the differential beam thrust spectrum at NNLL order by evaluating each function at its natural scale and evolving via renormalization group equations, then add the nonsingular NNLO corrections from fixed-order calculations using the FEHiP program. The correspondence between beam thrust cuts and traditional p_T jet veto cuts is established through leading-logarithmic matching and validated at NNLO.

## Key numbers

- The scale uncertainty of the NNLL+NNLO 0-jet cross section is 15-20%, depending on the cut value
- At leading order, the beam thrust cross section scales as sigma(T_cm^cut) proportional to (1 - (alpha_s C_A/pi) ln^2(T_cm^cut/m_H) + ...) while the p_T jet veto cross section scales as sigma(p_T^cut) proportional to (1 - (2 alpha_s C_A/pi) ln^2(p_T^cut/m_H) + ...)
- The correspondence T_cm^cut = m_H (p_T^cut/m_H)^(sqrt(2)) yields NNLO cross sections differing by ≤2% at the Tevatron and ≤7% at the LHC across the range of interesting cuts
- Using Pythia 8 simulations for m_H = 165 GeV at the LHC, a signal-to-background ratio of one is achieved with cuts T_cm < 31 GeV, p_T^max < 32 GeV for |eta| < 2.5, and p_T^max < 33 GeV for |eta| < 4.8

## Context

This work extends fixed-order calculations of the jet-vetoed Higgs cross section at NNLO by Catani (2001), Anastasiou (2004, 2008, 2009), and Grazzini (2008), which did not resum the large double logarithms induced by the jet veto. It improves upon parton-shower Monte Carlo approaches such as MC@NLO, POWHEG, Pythia, and Herwig, which only provide leading-logarithmic summation for these logarithms. The beam thrust variable was introduced by Stewart (2009) and enables systematic resummation to higher orders; the results enable more reliable theoretical uncertainty estimates for Tevatron and LHC Higgs searches in the H→WW channel, and suggest that the 7% uncertainty used in Tevatron analyses should be increased.
