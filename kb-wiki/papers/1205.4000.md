# Big Bang nucleosynthesis with a non-Maxwellian distribution

> The abundances of light elements based on the big bang nucleosynthesis model are calculated using the Tsallis non-extensive statistics. The impact of the variation of the non-extensive parameter $q$ from the unity value is compared to observations and to the abundance yields from the standard big bang model. We find large differences between the reaction rates and the abundance of light elements calculated with the extensive and the non-extensive statistics. We found that the observations are consistent with a non-extensive parameter q = 1 + 0.05 - 0.12, indicating that a large deviation from the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (q = 1) is highly unlikely.

## Key claim

The authors find that Big Bang nucleosynthesis observations are consistent with a non-extensive parameter \(q = 1^{+0.05}_{-0.12}\), indicating that a large deviation from the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (\(q=1\)) is highly unlikely, but the quoted uncertainty range shows that the data cannot exclude modest deviations.

## Method

The authors replaced the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distribution in BBN reaction-rate calculations with the Tsallis non-extensive distribution, parameterized by a real number \(q\). They computed the modified Gamow energy distributions and reaction rates for key charged-particle reactions (e.g., \(^2\)H(d,p)\(^3\)H, \(^7\)Li(p,\(\alpha\))\(^4\)He) and neutron-induced reactions (e.g., p(n,\(\gamma\))d, \(^7\)Be(n,p)\(^7\)Li) by numerically integrating the rate integral with the non-extensive distribution, using polynomial and Breit-Wigner fits to experimental S-factor data. They then compared the resulting light-element abundances to observational constraints to infer allowed values of \(q\).

## Key numbers

- The non-extensive parameter consistent with observations is \(q = 1^{+0.05}_{-0.12}\) (asymmetric uncertainty).
- For the reaction \(^2\)H(d,p)\(^3\)H at \(T_9 = 1\), the Gamow energy window width is \(\Delta E = 0.2368\,T_9^{5/6}\) MeV, and for \(q=2\) the kinetic energy cutoff occurs at \(E_{\mathrm{max}} = k_B T / (q-1) = 0.086\) MeV (86 keV) at \(T_9=1\).
- The neutron lifetime used is \(\tau_n = 878.5 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.3\) s.
- The baryon-to-photon ratio from WMAP is \(\eta = 6.16 \pm 0.15 \times 10^{-10}\).
- The number of light neutrino families from LEP is \(N_\nu = 2.9840 \pm 0.0082\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of non-Maxwellian distributions in nuclear astrophysics (e.g., Degl'Innocenti (1998), Coraddu (1999), and the Tsallis statistics framework of Tsallis (1988)) by applying the non-extensive formalism specifically to Big Bang nucleosynthesis. The authors note that previous work on CMB temperature fluctuations (Bernui (2007)) found \(q = 1.045 \pm 0.005\) for the photon distribution, and that attempts to solve the lithium problem have invoked various forms of new physics (Fields (2011)). The present results show that deviations from \(q=1\) worsen the lithium problem rather than solving it, and they set observational constraints on the Tsallis parameter from BBN.
