# Photoproduction of Theta+ on the nucleon and deuteron

> Photoproduction of the pentaquark particle Theta^+ on the nucleon has been studied by using an isobar and a Regge model. Using the isobar model, total cross sections around 100 nb for the gamma n --> K- Theta+ channel and 400 nb for the gamma p --> {\bar K}0 Theta+ process are obtained. The inclusion of the K* intermediate state yields a substantially large effect, especially in the gamma p --> {\bar K}0 Theta+ process. The Regge approach predicts smaller cross sections, i.e., less than 100 nb (20 nb) for the process on the neutron (proton). By using an elementary operator from the isobar model, cross sections for the process on a deuteron are predicted.

# Photoproduction of Theta+ on the nucleon and deuteron

## Key claim

The isobar model predicts total cross sections of approximately 100 nb for neutron targets and 400 nb for proton targets, but the Regge model predicts significantly smaller cross sections (less than 100 nb for neutron, less than 20 nb for proton), suggesting the isobar model may overestimate realistic cross sections, especially at higher energies, which could explain why high-energy experiments have not observed the Theta+.

## Method

The authors constructed an isobar model using tree-level Feynman diagrams with nucleon, Theta+, K, K*, and K1 intermediate states for the reactions gamma n -> K- Theta+ and gamma p -> anti-K0 Theta+, incorporating hadronic form factors via the Haberzettl prescription. They also developed a Regge model by replacing Feynman propagators with Regge propagators for the K* and K1 trajectories, and used the elementary operator from the isobar model to predict cross sections for Theta+ photoproduction on the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for gamma n -> K- Theta+ in the isobar model: approximately 100 nb
- Total cross section for gamma p -> anti-K0 Theta+ in the isobar model: approximately 400 nb
- Regge model prediction for gamma n -> K- Theta+: less than 100 nb
- Regge model prediction for gamma p -> anti-K0 Theta+: less than 20 nb
- Theta+ decay width used in calculation: 1 MeV (based on PDG value of 0.9 +/- 0.3 MeV)
- Coupling constant g_{K Theta N} / sqrt(4 pi) = 0.39
- Theta+ magnetic moment from chiral soliton calculation: mu_Theta = 0.82 mu_N, giving kappa_Theta = 0.35
- Vector coupling G^V_{K* Theta N} / (4 pi) = 8.72 x 10^{-2}
- Axial vector coupling G^V_{K1 Theta N} / (4 pi) = -7.64 x 10^{-3}
- Hadronic form factor cut-off used for deuteron calculation: Lambda = 0.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous isobar model studies of Theta+ photoproduction by Yu and Ji (2004) that used only Born terms and obtained cross sections spanning from several nanobarns to almost one microbarn, depending on unknown parameters such as Theta+ width, parity, and hadronic form factor cut-off. The inclusion of K* intermediate states increases cross sections by more than an order of magnitude compared to Born-only calculations, while K1 exchange is found to be negligible. The comparison between isobar and Regge models follows the approach used for K Lambda and K Sigma photoproduction by Mart (2003), where Regge models work well at higher energies but can deviate by up to 50% in the resonance region. The deuteron predictions enable comparison with future experimental searches using deuteron targets.
