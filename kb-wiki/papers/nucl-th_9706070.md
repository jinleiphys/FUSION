# Thermodynamic properties of a simple, confining model

> We study the equilibrium thermodynamics of a simple, confining, DSE-model of 2-flavour QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential. The model has two phases: one characterised by confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking; and the other by their absence. The phase boundary is defined by the zero of the vacuum-pressure difference between the confined and deconfined phases. Chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement are coincident with the transition being of first order, except for $μ=0$, where it is second order. Nonperturbative modifications of the dressed-quark propagator persist into the deconfined domain and lead to a dispersion law modified by a dynamically-generated, momentum-dependent mass-scale. This entails that the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for the bulk thermodynamic quantities is attained only for large values of temperature and chemical potential.

## Key claim

In a simple, confining Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model of two-flavour QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential, deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration are coincident, with a first-order transition for any nonzero chemical potential and a second-order transition only at zero chemical potential; the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for bulk thermodynamic quantities is attained only for large values of temperature and chemical potential because nonperturbative modifications of the dressed-quark propagator persist into the deconfined domain.

## Method

The authors study a DSE model defined by an infrared-dominant, contact-interaction dressed-gluon propagator (the Munczek-Nemirovsky model) generalised to finite temperature, which reduces the gap equation to an algebraic equation. They calculate the pressure from the tree-level auxiliary-field effective action, defining the phase boundary by the zero of the vacuum-pressure difference (bag constant) between the Nambu-Goldstone (confined, chiral-symmetry-broken) and Wigner (deconfined, chiral-symmetric) solutions. For the deconfined phase, they numerically evaluate the quark pressure and fit a modified, temperature-independent dispersion law to account for the momentum-dependent dressing of the quark propagator.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature at zero chemical potential is \(T_c^0 = 0.159\,\eta\); using \(\eta = 1.06\) GeV (from fitting pion and rho masses), this corresponds to \(T_c^0 = 0.170\) GeV.
* The critical chemical potential at zero temperature is \(\mu_c^0 = 0.3\) GeV.
* The bag constant at zero temperature and chemical potential is \({\cal B}(0,0) = (0.102\,\eta)^4 = (0.109\ {\rm GeV})^4\).
* At \(\bar T \sim 0.3 \sim 2 \bar T_c^0\) or \(\bar \mu \sim 1.0 \sim 3 \bar\mu_c^0\), the quark pressure is only half of the free particle (Stefan-Boltzmann) pressure.
* The dynamically generated mass-scale in the dispersion law for deconfined quarks is \(\kappa(0,0) \simeq 0.6 \sim 2 \bar\mu_c^0\).

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of QCD thermodynamics at finite temperature and chemical potential, building on the Munczek-Nemirovsky model (Munczek and Nemirovsky, 1983) and the finite-temperature generalisation of Blaschke et al. (1996). The results are qualitatively consistent with more sophisticated DSE models (Blaschke et al., 1996) and with lattice QCD simulations at finite temperature (Karsch, 1997), showing a slow approach to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. The model enables a straightforward analysis of nonzero chemical potential, where lattice simulations are not tractable, and provides a framework for calculating all bulk thermodynamic quantities in the deconfined domain.
