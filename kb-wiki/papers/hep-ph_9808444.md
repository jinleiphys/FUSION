# An Effective Field Theory Approach to Color Superconductivity at High Quark Density

> We investigate the Fermi Liquid theory of high density QCD. Using the renormalization group (RG), we determine the behavior of effective fermion interactions near the Fermi surface. At sufficiently high densities the matching between the Fermi Liquid theory and QCD can be accomplished in perturbation theory and a reliable calculation of Cooper pair formation performed, modulo the existence of a magnetic screening mass for the gluon. The presence of a chemical potential leads to different RG flows for sub-components of the effective interactions which would ordinarily be linked by Lorentz invariance. We also study instanton-induced operators and find that near the Fermi surface they are likely to be subdominant relative to operators induced by perturbative gluon exchange. We discuss the implications of our results for the phase structure of QCD at finite baryon density and temperature.

# An Effective Field Theory Approach to Color Superconductivity at High Quark Density

## Key claim

The paper argues that at sufficiently high quark densities, the matching between Fermi Liquid theory and QCD can be performed perturbatively, allowing a reliable calculation of Cooper pair formation, but this conclusion depends on the existence of a magnetic screening mass for the gluon, which is assumed rather than proven. The presence of a chemical potential causes different renormalization group flows for sub-components of the effective interactions that would ordinarily be linked by Lorentz invariance.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory of quarks near the Fermi surface using Wilsonian renormalization group methods, integrating out gluons to obtain four-fermion operators. They perform matching between QCD and the effective theory at tree level via one-gluon exchange, averaging the momentum-dependent form factor over all relative orientations of incoming and outgoing quark momenta to project out the zero angular momentum component. The one-loop beta functions for the four-fermion couplings are then derived and solved to determine which channels develop Landau poles as the Fermi surface is approached.

## Key numbers

- The density of states at the Fermi surface in lowest order approximation is N = μ²/2π²
- The Debye screening mass for the temporal gluon component scales as M₀² ~ g²μ²
- The magnetic screening mass for spatial gluon components scales as Mᵢ² ~ g⁴μ²
- The condensate size is given by Δ = Λ_UV exp(-1 / N G_{\bar{3}}(0) (1+3x)), where x > 1 represents the ratio of magnetic to electric screening masses
- For μ = 300 MeV and m_s = 150 MeV, the Fermi momentum mismatch between strange and non-strange quarks is Δp_F ≈ 40 MeV
- The ratio of instanton to gluonic couplings near the Fermi surface scales as κ_a(t) / G⁰_{\bar{3}LL}(t) ∝ (1 + NG(0)(1+3x)t)^{1/2}, showing instanton suppression

## Context

This work extends the Fermi Liquid theory approach to relativistic, non-Abelian gauge theories, building on the renormalization group formulation of Fermi Liquid theory (FLRG) and earlier gap equation calculations by Bailin and Love (1984). The results are qualitatively consistent with those earlier gap equation calculations and with instanton-based analyses of color superconductivity. The paper enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon density and temperature, particularly the transition between the high-density color superconducting phase and the low-density phase exhibiting chiral symmetry breaking.
