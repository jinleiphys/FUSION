# Multipole decomposition of tensor interactions of fermionic probes with composite particles and BSM signatures in nuclear reactions

> A multipole decomposition of a cross-section is a useful tool to simplify the analysis of reactions due to their symmetry properties. By using a new approach to decompose antisymmetric tensor-type interactions within the multipole analysis, we introduce a general mathematical formalism for working with tensor couplings. This allows us to present a general tensor nuclear response, which is particularly useful for ongoing beta-decay experiments looking for physics beyond the Standard Model, as well as other exotic particle scatterings off nuclei, e.g., in dark matter direct detection experiments. Using this method, beyond the Standard Model operators identify with the known Standard Model operators, eliminating the need for calculations of additional matrix elements. We present in detail BSM expressions useful for beta-decay experiments and give an exemplary application for 6He beta-decay, although the formalism is easily generalizable for calculating other exotic scattering reactions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general multipole decomposition for antisymmetric tensor-type interactions of fermionic probes with nuclei, showing that at leading order the beyond-Standard-Model (BSM) tensor nuclear response operators are proportional to the well-known Standard Model axial-vector multipole operators, thereby eliminating the need for calculating new nuclear matrix elements for BSM tensor contributions.

## Method

The authors decompose antisymmetric tensor currents into vector-like objects by separating temporal-spatial and spatial-only components, then apply standard vector multipole analysis using tensor spherical harmonics and the identity relating plane-wave expansions to vector spherical harmonics. They derive the tensor nuclear current from the single-nucleon matrix element using a non-relativistic expansion, express the resulting multipole operators (longitudinal, electric, and magnetic) in terms of the known SM axial-vector multipole operators, and apply the formalism to beta-decay rate expressions including forbidden transitions.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer in dark matter direct detection is \(q \sim 100\ \text{MeV}\), compared to typical beta-decay momentum transfer of a few MeV (1–4 MeV).
- The non-relativistic expansion parameter is \(\epsilon_{\text{NR}} \sim P_{\text{fermi}}/m_N \approx 0.2\).
- The recoil expansion parameter is \(\epsilon_{\text{recoil}} \sim q/m_N \approx 0.002\) for an endpoint of \(\approx 2\ \text{MeV}\).
- The tensor nuclear charge \(g_T\) has similar magnitude to the SM axial-vector charge \(g_A\).
- The pseudoscalar charge is \(g_P = 349(9)\).
- The scalar nuclear charge \(g_S = g_V (M_n - M_p)/(m_d - m_u) \approx 0.8\)–\(1.2\).
- For the simplest BSM operator, a TeV-scale new physics implies \(\epsilon_T \sim 10^{-3}\).
- The expansion parameter \(\epsilon_{qr} \sim qR \approx 0.01 A^{1/3}\) for an endpoint of \(\approx 2\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the multipole analysis framework, previously applied to vector and axial-vector weak interactions (Walecka, 1975) and to symmetric tensor couplings for gravitational radiation (Thorne, 1980), to antisymmetric tensor interactions relevant for BSM searches. It provides a general formalism for tensor couplings in beta-decay experiments and dark matter direct detection, complementing earlier studies of tensor couplings in beta-decay (Jackson, Treiman and Wyld, 1957; Morita, 1953) that were limited to low momentum transfer approximations. The method enables calculation of BSM signatures in forbidden transitions and the Fierz interference term without new nuclear matrix elements, and is applicable to ongoing experiments searching for TeV-scale new physics.
