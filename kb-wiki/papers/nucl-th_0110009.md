# Hydrodynamical analysis of hadronic spectra in the 130 GeV/nucleon Au+Au collisions

> We study one-particle spectra and a two-particle correlation function in the 130 GeV/nucleon Au+Au collisions at RHIC by making use of a hydrodynamical model. We calculate the one-particle hadronic spectra and present the first analysis of Bose-Einstein correlation functions based on the numerical solution of the hydrodynamical equations which takes both longitudinal and transverse expansion into account appropriately. The hydrodynamical model provides excellent agreement with the experimental data in the pseudorapidity and the transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons, the rapidity dependence of anti-proton to proton ratio, and almost consistent result for the pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions. Our numerical solution with simple freeze-out picture suggests the formation of the quark-gluon plasma with large volume and low net-baryon density.

# Hydrodynamical analysis of hadronic spectra in the 130 GeV/nucleon Au+Au collisions

## Key claim

The hydrodynamical model with a first-order phase transition and simple freeze-out picture provides excellent agreement with experimental data for pseudorapidity and transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons and the rapidity dependence of the antiproton-to-proton ratio, but yields outward and longitudinal HBT radii that are slightly larger than the experimental data, suggesting the need for a more complicated freeze-out mechanism.

## Method

The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equations for a perfect fluid with baryon number conservation, using a bag model equation of state with a first-order phase transition between a quark-gluon plasma phase and a hadronic resonance gas phase. Initial conditions at proper time τ=1.0 fm/c are parameterized as Gaussian distributions for energy density and net baryon number density, with Bjorken's scaling solution used for the initial longitudinal flow velocity and no initial transverse flow. Particle spectra are calculated via the Cooper-Frye formula at a constant freeze-out temperature, including resonance decays from ρ, ω, η, K*, and Δ.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density: 6.0 GeV/fm³, corresponding to a temperature of 229 MeV
- Maximum initial net baryon density: 0.125 fm⁻³
- Freeze-out temperature: 125 MeV
- Mean chemical potential at freeze-out: 76.1 MeV
- Mean transverse flow velocity at |η|<0.1: 0.509c
- Lifetime of QGP phase: 2.92 fm/c
- Lifetime of mixed phase: 12.61 fm/c
- Total lifetime of fluid: 18.94 fm/c
- Net baryon number: 131
- Longitudinal extension of hot matter (ση+η0): 2.47, about 2.3 times larger than at SPS
- Average energy density at η=0 transverse plane: 3.9 GeV/fm³
- Total energy of fluid: 25290 GeV, 99% of total collision energy
- Freeze-out time duration: 5-7 fm/c, longer than the SPS case

## Context

This work extends previous (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical analyses by Kolb (2001) and Zschiesche (2001) that used Bjorken's ansatz for the beam direction, and the (3+1)-dimensional work by Hirano (2001) on anisotropic flow, by focusing on central collisions with cylindrical symmetry and presenting the first analysis of Bose-Einstein correlation functions at RHIC using a full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model. The results enable comparison with SPS data and suggest that the quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC has much lower baryon density, slightly higher energy density, and several times larger longitudinal extension than at SPS, though the discrepancy in HBT radii points to the need for further investigation of freeze-out dynamics and source opaqueness.
