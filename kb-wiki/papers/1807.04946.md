# Relativistic hydrodynamics with spin

> A newly proposed framework of perfect-fluid relativistic hydrodynamics for particles with spin 1/2 is briefly reviewed. The hydrodynamic equations follow entirely from the conservation laws for energy, momentum, and angular momentum. The incorporation of the angular-momentum conservation requires that the spin polarization tensor is introduced. It plays a role of a Lagrange multiplier conjugated to the spin tensor. The space-time evolution of the spin polarization tensor depends on the specific form chosen for the spin tensor.

## Key claim

The paper presents a framework for perfect-fluid relativistic hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles where the spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) is introduced as a Lagrange multiplier conjugate to the spin tensor, but the space-time evolution of this tensor depends on the specific form chosen for the spin tensor, and the relation \(\omega_{\mu\nu} = \varpi_{\mu\nu}\) (equality with thermal vorticity) is expected to require a dissipative spin-orbit interaction that is missing in the current framework.

## Method

The authors start from local equilibrium distribution functions for particles and antiparticles expressed in terms of Dirac bispinors and an exponential containing the spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) and the Dirac spin operator \(\Sigma^{\mu\nu}\). They derive the energy-momentum tensor, charge current, and entropy current from these distributions, then impose conservation of energy-momentum (\(\partial_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = 0\)) and angular momentum (\(\partial_\lambda J^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = 0\)). The conservation of angular momentum, with a symmetric energy-momentum tensor, reduces to separate conservation of the spin tensor \(S^{\lambda,\mu\nu}\), which yields the equation \(u^\lambda \partial_\lambda \bar{\omega}^{\mu\nu} = 0\) for the rescaled spin polarization tensor, providing five independent equations that close the system together with the background hydrodynamic equations.

## Key numbers

* The spin polarization parameter \(\zeta = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{1}{2} \omega_{\mu\nu} \omega^{\mu\nu}}\) is interpreted as the ratio of the spin chemical potential \(\Omega\) to temperature \(T\), i.e., \(\zeta = \Omega/T\).
* The normalization condition for the rescaled spin polarization tensor is \(\bar{\omega}_{\mu\nu} \bar{\omega}^{\mu\nu} = 2\).
* The spin tensor is given by \(S^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = \frac{w u^\lambda}{4 \zeta} \omega^{\mu\nu}\), where \(w\) is the spin density.

## Context

This work extends the framework of Becattini (2013) for local equilibrium distribution functions with spin to a full hydrodynamic description, motivated by the first positive measurements of \(\Lambda\)-hyperon spin polarization by the STAR collaboration (2017, 2018). The approach enables numerical simulations of spin-polarized fluid dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, as shown in preliminary simulations by the authors, and opens the question of how the definition of the spin tensor (different forms used by Becattini (2013) and deGroot (1980)) affects the formulation of hydrodynamics. The framework currently lacks dissipative spin-orbit interaction, which Montenegro (2017) suggests would lead to an asymmetric energy-momentum tensor and the equality of spin polarization with thermal vorticity.
