# Precession Mode on High-K Configurations: Non-Collective Axially-Symmetric Limit of Wobbling Motion

> We have studied the precession mode, the rotational excitation built on the high-$K$ isomeric state, in comparison with the recently identified wobbling mode. The random-phase-approximation (RPA) formalism, which has been developed for the nuclear wobbling motion, is invoked and the precession phonon is obtained by the non-collective axially-symmetric limit of the formalism. The excitation energies and the electromagnetic properties of the precession bands in $^{178}$W are calculated, and it is found that the results of RPA calculations well correspond to those of the rotor model; the correspondence can be understood by an adiabatic approximation to the RPA phonon. As a by-product, it is also found that the problem of too small out-of-band $B(E2)$ in our previous RPA wobbling calculations can be solved by a suitable choice of the triaxial deformation which corresponds to the one used in the rotor model.

## Key claim

The precession mode built on high-K isomers can be understood as the non-collective axially-symmetric limit of the wobbling motion formalism, and the random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations reproduce the rotor model results for excitation energies and electromagnetic transitions, provided the model parameters are calculated appropriately; the previously reported problem of too small out-of-band B(E2) values in RPA wobbling calculations is resolved by using a triaxial deformation parameter consistent with the density distribution rather than the Nilsson potential.

## Method

The authors applied the RPA formalism developed by Marshalek for wobbling motion, taking the non-collective axially-symmetric limit (gamma = 60 degrees or -120 degrees in the Lund convention) to describe precession phonons. They used the Nilsson potential as a mean field with appropriate deformation parameters and pairing gap parameters, with a symmetry-restoring separable residual interaction that contains no adjustable parameters. The RPA calculations were performed for eleven high-K isomers in 178W, ranging from four-quasiparticle to ten-quasiparticle configurations, and the results were compared with the rotor model through an adiabatic approximation.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies of one-phonon precession modes were calculated for eleven high-K isomers in 178W, with four exceptions (K^pi = 18-, 25+, 28-, 29+) where the calculated precession energies were too small due to overestimation of proton contributions from the pi[541]1/2- Nilsson state originating from the pi h9/2 high-j decoupled orbit
- The out-of-band to in-band B(E2) ratio for wobbling phonons depends on the gamma parameter approximately as B(E2)_out/B(E2)_in proportional to tan^2(gamma+30 degrees), with the difference between gamma = 10 degrees and gamma = 20 degrees being about a factor of two
- Using gamma(Nils) = 30 degrees in the Nilsson potential (corresponding to gamma(dens) approximately 20 degrees in the density distribution) brings the RPA B(E2) ratio up to the correct magnitude, compared to the previous result with gamma(Nils) = 20 degrees which gave values too small by about 1/2 to 1/3

## Context

This work extends the microscopic RPA formalism for wobbling motion developed by Marshalek (1979) to the precession mode, building on the earlier RPA precession formalism of Andersson et al. (1981) and the rotor model studies of high-K isomers in 178W by Cullen et al. (1999). The results show that the RPA can describe three-dimensional rotational motion at the same level as the rotor model, and the correspondence is understood through an adiabatic approximation studied by Kurasawa (1980, 1982). The resolution of the B(E2) discrepancy raises a fundamental question about why Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations do not provide enough triaxiality to explain the measured B(E2) ratio of the wobbling phonon band in Lu nuclei, where wobbling phonon spectra have been identified up to two-phonon excitations by Odegard et al. (2001) and Jensen et al. (2004).
