# Direct calculation of parton distributions in momentum space from lattice QCD

> Coulomb-gauge quasi-parton distributions can be computed directly in momentum space on a finite lattice, enabled by the commutativity of their renormalization and Fourier transform. This approach removes the formal inverse problem in coordinate-space methods. Our momentum-space pion quasi-distributions agree with coordinate-space results Fourier transformed with asymptotic extrapolation, indicating that the formal inverse problem in the latter is not a concern at this volume. We further extend the framework to higher dimensions and obtain the first 3D image of the pion directly from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Coulomb-gauge quasi-parton distributions can be computed directly in momentum space on a finite lattice, removing the formal inverse problem present in coordinate-space methods; the authors show that at their lattice volume the inverse problem is not a practical concern, as momentum-space results agree with coordinate-space results Fourier transformed with asymptotic extrapolation.

## Method

The authors construct momentum-space Coulomb-gauge quasi-parton distribution operators on the lattice and simulate them directly in a boosted pion state, using a Wilson-clover action for valence quarks on a 48^3 x 144 MILC ensemble with pion mass approximately 310 MeV and lattice spacing 0.06 fm. They extract ground-state matrix elements using the Lanczos method and compare the resulting quasi-PDFs with those obtained from Fourier transforming coordinate-space matrix elements with asymptotic extrapolation. The quasi-PDFs are renormalized in the MSbar scheme and matched to lightcone PDFs at next-to-leading order, and the formalism is extended to three dimensions to compute quasi-TMDs.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass m_pi approximately 310 MeV on a lattice with volume 48^3 x 144 and spacing a approximately 0.06 fm.
* Pion momenta P_z = 2.2 GeV and 2.6 GeV used for quasi-PDF calculations.
* Renormalization factor Z_C^{MSbar} = 0.9215(4) determined on the same lattice.
* Asymptotic exponential decay parameter M approximately 0.6 GeV extracted from fits to coordinate-space matrix elements.
* Finite-volume correction estimate: 2e^{-Lambda L} < 0.04 for m_pi L > 4, with Lambda > m_pi.
* Coordinate-space Fourier transform cutoff z_cut = 0.54 fm used for asymptotic extrapolation fits, with chi^2/d.o.f. < 0.2.
* Endpoint regions x < 0.18 and x > 0.82 where perturbation theory becomes unreliable (alpha_s > 0.5).

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework (Ji 2013, Ji 2014, Ji 2020) by introducing a momentum-space approach that avoids the inverse problem inherent in coordinate-space Fourier transforms, which has been a focus of recent methodological studies (Dutrieux 2025, Chen 2025, Xiong 2025, Chu 2025, Dutrieux 2025, Ling 2025). The Coulomb-gauge correlator approach builds on proposals by Gao (2023) and Zhao (2023) and enables the first direct lattice calculation of a 3D hadron image, complementing existing TMD calculations in transverse-coordinate space (Bollweg 2025) and providing a framework for cross-validation with coordinate-space methods at no additional computational cost.
