# Global Optical Potential for the Elastic Scattering of $^{6}$He at Low Energies

> A set of global optical potential has been derived to describe the interactions of $^{6}$He at low energies. The elastic scattering angular distribution data measured so far for many systems, ranging from $^{12}$C to $^{209}$Bi, have been considered within the framework of the optical model in order to find a global potential set to describe the experimental data consistently. We report that very good agreement between theoretical and experimental results has been obtained with small $χ^{2}/N$ values by using the derived potential set. The reaction cross section and volume integrals of the potentials have been deduced from the theoretical calculations for all studied systems at relevant energies.

## Key claim

The authors derive a global optical potential set that consistently describes the elastic scattering of \(^{6}\)He across a wide range of target nuclei (from \(^{12}\)C to \(^{209}\)Bi) at low energies, but they note that the potential parameters require small modifications (e.g., adjusting the imaginary depth by \(\pm 5\) MeV) to fit experimental data exactly for some heavy-target cases.

## Method

The authors performed optical model calculations using a total effective potential composed of Coulomb, centrifugal, and nuclear terms, where the nuclear potential was taken as the sum of a Woods-Saxon square-shaped real part and a Woods-Saxon shaped imaginary part. They systematically varied the real and imaginary radius parameters (\(r_V\) and \(r_W\)) on a grid from 0.5 to 2.0 fm in steps of 0.1 fm, searching for the best \(\chi^2\) fit to elastic scattering angular distribution data for each system. From the best-fit parameters, they derived empirical formulas for the depth of the real and imaginary potentials as functions of the laboratory energy of \(^{6}\)He and the charge and mass numbers of the target.

## Key numbers

- Real potential depth formula: \(V_0 = 110.1 + 2.1 \frac{Z_T}{A_T^{1/3}} + 0.65 E\) (where \(E\) is the laboratory energy of \(^{6}\)He in MeV).
- Imaginary potential depth formula: \(W_0 = 6.0 + 0.48 \frac{Z_T}{A_T^{1/3}} - 0.15 E\).
- Fixed radius parameters: \(r_V = 0.9\) fm and \(r_W = 1.50\) fm; fixed diffuseness parameters: \(a_V = a_W = 0.7\) fm.
- For \(^{6}\)He+\(^{27}\)Al at 9.5, 11.0, 12.0, and 13.4 MeV, the reaction cross sections from the new potential differ from the Sao Paulo Potential results by about 200 mb.
- For \(^{6}\)He+\(^{64}\)Zn, the reaction cross section from the new potential is comparable to the phenomenological results of 380\(\mp\)60 mb at 10.0 MeV and 1450\(\mp\)130 mb at 13.6 MeV, with a difference of around 10\%.
- For \(^{6}\)He+\(^{197}\)Au at 27 MeV, the new potential gives a reaction cross section of 1925 mb; for \(^{6}\)He+\(^{208}\)Pb at 27 MeV, it gives 1892 mb.

## Context

This work extends prior phenomenological and microscopic analyses of \(^{6}\)He elastic scattering, which had previously used potentials adapted from \(^{4}\)He, \(^{6}\)Li, or \(^{7}\)Li (e.g., Smith et al., Milin et al.) or the Sao Paulo Potential (Benjamim et al., 2007), but lacked a consistent global parameterization. The derived global potential set enables systematic optical model calculations for \(^{6}\)He reactions without requiring system-specific parameter tuning, and the authors suggest it could be extended to describe scattering observables of other halo-type nuclei.
