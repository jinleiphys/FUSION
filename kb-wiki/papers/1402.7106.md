# Operator evolution for ab initio nuclear theory

> The past two decades have seen a revolution in ab initio calculations of nuclear properties. One key element has been the development of a rigorous effective interaction theory, applying unitary transformations to soften the nuclear Hamiltonian and hence accelerate the convergence as a function of the model space size. For consistency, however, one ought to apply the same transformation to other operators when calculating transitions and mean values from the eigenstates of the renormalized Hamiltonian. Working in a translationally-invariant harmonic oscillator basis for the two- and three-nucleon systems, we evolve the Hamiltonian, square-radius and total dipole strength operators by the similarity renormalization group (SRG). The inclusion of up to three-body matrix elements in the 4He nucleus all but completely restores the invariance of the expectation values under the transformation. We also consider a Gaussian operator with adjustable range and find at short ranges an increased contribution from such induced three-body terms.

# Operator evolution for ab initio nuclear theory

## Key claim
For the first time, the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution of operators beyond the Hamiltonian is performed including up to induced three-body terms, and the invariance of expectation values under the transformation is tested as a function of the SRG flow parameter. The inclusion of three-body matrix elements in the 4He nucleus all but completely restores the invariance, but does not fully eliminate dependence on the evolution parameter due to missing induced four-body terms.

## Method
The authors work in a translationally-invariant harmonic oscillator basis for the two- and three-nucleon systems, evolving the Hamiltonian, square-radius operator, and total dipole strength operator via the similarity renormalization group (SRG). They compute the unitary transformation matrix from the eigenvectors of the Hamiltonian before and after SRG evolution, then apply the same transformation to the operators. Expectation values are calculated using ground state wavefunctions from the no-core shell model (NCSM) for 3H and 4He, using Hamiltonians derived from chiral effective field theory with three levels of operator evolution: bare (unevolved), evolved in the two-body space, and evolved in the three-body space.

## Key numbers
- 3H RMS radius calculations are converged to within less than 0.1% using model space truncations of N_A2max = 300 for the A=2 space and N_A3max = 46 for the A=3 space, with hbar-Omega = 20 MeV.
- 4He calculations use N_A2max = 300, N_A3max = 40, and N_A4max = 18, with hbar-Omega = 28 MeV, converged to within less than 0.1% for both RMS radius and total dipole strength.
- The SRG parameter lambda is varied from 1.5 fm^{-1} to 3.0 fm^{-1} for both 3H and 4He calculations.
- For the Gaussian operator study, the relative three-body contribution to renormalization increases from approximately 25% at range a_0 = 0.2 fm to 50% at a_0 = 1.6 fm, for lambda values of 1.5, 2.0, and 2.5 fm^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends previous SRG studies that focused on Hamiltonian evolution (Jurgenson 2009, Jurgenson 2011) and earlier operator renormalization studies using the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation (Stetcu 2005) and momentum-space SRG (Anderson 2010). The authors demonstrate that consistent operator evolution is necessary for accurate calculation of observables such as electric dipole transitions, which are important for nuclear polarizabilities (Stetcu 2009) and radiative capture reactions like 7Be(p,gamma)8B (Adelberger 2011, Navratil 2011). This work enables future calculations of transition strengths and cross-sections in heavier systems (A=5-12) by providing a framework for evolving non-scalar operators and transforming the translationally invariant three-body operators into single-particle Slater determinant basis states.
