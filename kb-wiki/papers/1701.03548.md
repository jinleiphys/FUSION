# Chiral phase structure of three flavor QCD at vanishing baryon number density

> We investigate the phase structure of QCD with 3 degenerate quark flavors as function of the degenerate quark masses at vanishing baryon number density. We use the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks on lattices with temporal extent $N_{t}=6$ and perform calculations for six values of quark masses, which in the continuum limit correspond to pion masses in the range $80~{\rm MeV} \lesssim m_π \lesssim 230~$MeV. By analyzing the volume and temperature dependence of the chiral condensate and chiral susceptibility we find no direct evidence for a first order phase transition in this range of pion mass values. Relying on the universal scaling behaviors of the chiral observables near an anticipated chiral critical point, we estimate an upper bound for the critical pion mass, $m_π^c \lesssim$ 50 MeV, below which a region of first order chiral phase transition is favored.

## Key claim

The authors find no direct evidence for a first order chiral phase transition in three flavor QCD for pion masses down to 80 MeV, and estimate an upper bound for the critical pion mass of \(m_\pi^c \lesssim 50\) MeV, below which a first order transition region is favored. This bound is derived from universal scaling behaviors of chiral observables near an anticipated chiral critical point, not from direct observation of a first order transition.

## Method

The study uses lattice QCD with the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action and a tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on \(N_t = 6\) lattices, simulating three degenerate quark flavors at six quark mass values. The chiral condensate and disconnected chiral susceptibility are analyzed as functions of temperature (via the gauge coupling \(\beta = 10/g^2\)) and volume, with spatial extents \(N_s = 10, 12, 16, 24\). The authors examine volume dependence of peak heights, histograms of the chiral condensate, and Binder cumulants to search for signs of a first order transition, and then use scaling relations for the Z(2) universality class to estimate the critical pion mass.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass range explored: \(80\ \mathrm{MeV} \lesssim m_\pi \lesssim 230\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (continuum limit estimates from six bare quark masses \(0.0009375 \le m_q \le 0.0075\)).
* Upper bound on critical pion mass: \(m_\pi^c \lesssim 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Mixing parameter \(B\) between temperature-like and magnetic-field-like scaling variables: \(-0.038 \lesssim B \le 0\).
* Peak height of disconnected chiral susceptibility at the lightest quark mass (\(m_q = 0.0009375\), \(m_\pi \approx 80\ \mathrm{MeV}\)) on \(24^3 \times 6\) lattice: \(\chi_{q,\mathrm{disc}}^c / T^2 = 649(28)\).
* Pseudo-critical gauge coupling for the lightest quark mass on \(24^3 \times 6\): \(\beta_c = 5.854(7)\).

## Context

This work extends prior lattice studies of the three-flavor chiral phase transition using staggered fermions (standard, p4fat3, asqtad, 2stout actions) on coarse lattices, where critical pion mass estimates ranged from about 300 MeV down to 70 MeV (e.g., deForcrand (2003), Karsch (2001), Schmidt (2002)). It also relates to studies of two-flavor QCD (Bonati (2014), Philipsen (2016)) and to (2+1)-flavor QCD with fixed strange quark mass (Endrodi (2007), Ding (2015)). The results provide a tighter upper bound on the critical pion mass using the HISQ action, which has better taste symmetry than previous actions, and enable future continuum extrapolations needed to determine whether a first order region exists at physically relevant quark masses.
