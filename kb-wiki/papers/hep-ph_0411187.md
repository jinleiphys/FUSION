# Transition from Baryonic to Mesonic Freeze-Out

> The recently discovered sharp peak in the K+/pi+ ratio is discussed in the framework of the statistical model. In this model a rapid change is expected as the hadronic gas undergoes a transition from a baryon-dominated to a meson-dominated gas. The transition happens at sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.16 GeV, temperature T = 140 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 410 MeV. The maximum in the Lambda/pi ratio is well reproduced by the statistical model, but the change in the K+/pi+ ratio is much less pronounced than the one observed by the NA49 collaboration. Further experimental tests are proposed to clarify the nature of the transition. In particular, the maxima expected in the statistical model for the Xi/pi and Omega/pi ratios occur at slightly higher beam energies than the maxima for the Lambda/pi and K+/pi+ ratios.

# Transition from Baryonic to Mesonic Freeze-Out

## Key claim

The sharp peak ("horn") observed by the NA49 collaboration in the K+/pi+ ratio in relativistic heavy-ion collisions corresponds precisely to a transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated hadronic gas in the statistical model, but the model cannot reproduce the sharpness of the observed peak; the model produces only a mild maximum in this ratio, while it does reproduce the Lambda/pi maximum well.

## Method

The authors calculated particle ratios along the chemical freeze-out curve using the statistical hadron gas model, separating contributions of baryons and mesons to the total entropy density as a function of center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN). They determined the transition point where baryon and meson entropy contributions cross, and computed the predicted maxima for Lambda/pi, K+/pi+, Xi-/pi+, and Omega-/pi+ ratios at different beam energies.

## Key numbers

- Transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated hadronic gas occurs at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.2 GeV, temperature T = 140 MeV, and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 410 MeV
- Statistical model predicts Lambda/<pi> maximum at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.1 GeV with maximum value 0.052
- Statistical model predicts Xi-/pi+ maximum at sqrt(s_NN) = 10.2 GeV with maximum value 0.011
- Statistical model predicts K+/pi+ maximum at sqrt(s_NN) = 10.8 GeV with maximum value 0.22
- Statistical model predicts Omega-/pi+ maximum at sqrt(s_NN) = 27.0 GeV with maximum value 0.0012

## Context

This work extends the statistical model predictions of Cleymans et al. (1993) and Becattini et al. (2001) for particle ratios in heavy-ion collisions, and addresses the "horn" structure reported by the NA49 collaboration (Gazdzicki, 2004). The authors argue that the observed maxima in various strange baryon-to-pion ratios occur at different beam energies in the statistical model, in contrast to what would be expected from a genuine deconfinement phase transition, which would produce changes at a single energy. The paper proposes further experimental tests using multi-strange baryon ratios (Xi/pi and Omega/pi) to distinguish between the statistical hadron gas explanation and a critical point interpretation as suggested by Stock (2004) and Wilczek (2004).
