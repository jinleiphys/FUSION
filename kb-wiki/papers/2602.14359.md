# Effects of the symmetry energy slope on magnetized neutron stars

> In this work, we study the effect of the symmetry slope on the observables of weakly and strongly magnetized neutron stars within the chaotic magnetic field approximation. We investigate the impact of the symmetry energy slope in the equation of state, as well as on the observables of neutron stars, by calculating their masses, radii, redshifts, tidal deformabilities, and fundamental-mode gravitational-wave frequencies. We show that the effect of the magnetic field is strong on low mass stars, producing a softer equation of state and correspondingly lower values of radii. Furthermore, the magnetic field also causes a significant drop in the dimensionless tidal parameter even when the effects on the radii are small. At the end of the paper, we discuss the effects of the magnetic field in neutron stars' universal relations.

## Key claim
The symmetry energy slope \(L\) and strong magnetic fields both soften the equation of state at low densities, but the magnetic field's effect on the dimensionless tidal parameter \(\Lambda\) is larger than its effect on radii, especially for low-mass stars; the authors find that for a 1.0 \(M_\odot\) star, the radius difference \(\Delta R\) between weakly and strongly magnetized cases reaches 0.24 km for \(L = 92\) MeV but only 0.06 km for \(L = 44\) MeV, while the tidal parameter \(\Lambda\) can change by over 800 (nearly 20%) for the same mass.

## Method
The authors used the L3\(\omega\rho\) parametrization of quantum hadrodynamics, fixing five saturation properties and leaving the symmetry energy slope \(L\) as a free parameter (44, 60, 76, and 92 MeV). They employed the chaotic magnetic field approximation to treat magnetic fields isotropically, with a density-dependent profile (\(B_0 = 10^{15}\) G for weak fields, \(B_0 = 3.1 \times 10^{18}\) G for strong fields), and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the equations for tidal deformability and nonradial oscillations to compute masses, radii, redshifts, tidal parameters, and fundamental-mode gravitational-wave frequencies.

## Key numbers
* For a 1.0 \(M_\odot\) star with \(L = 92\) MeV, the radius difference between weakly and strongly magnetized cases is \(\Delta R = 0.24\) km; for \(L = 44\) MeV, \(\Delta R = 0.06\) km.
* For the canonical 1.4 \(M_\odot\) star, the dimensionless tidal parameter \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) ranges from 501 (weak field, \(L = 60\) MeV) to below 500 for strong fields with \(L = 44\) and 76 MeV; the GW170817 constraint is \(70 < \Lambda_{1.4} < 580\).
* The redshift \(Z\) varies by up to 0.115 between different slopes, but the magnetic field changes \(Z\) by less than 0.005 in all cases.
* For a 1.0 \(M_\odot\) star, the absolute change in \(\Lambda\) due to a strong magnetic field can exceed 800, corresponding to variations up to almost 20%.
* The maximum mass supported by the L3\(\omega\rho\) model is \(2.30\,M_\odot\).
* The PSR J0740+6620 constraint (mass \(2.08 \pm 0.07\,M_\odot\), radius \(11.41\) km \(< R < 13.69\) km) is satisfied for all values of \(L\) and both magnetic field strengths.

## Context
This work extends previous studies on the role of the symmetry energy slope in neutron star structure (Lopes et al., 2022; Lopes et al., 2024) by incorporating strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars, using the chaotic magnetic field approximation (Zeldovich, 1960s; Lopes et al., 2015; Flores et al., 2020). It shows that the tidal parameter \(\Lambda\) is more sensitive to magnetic fields than radii or redshifts, which may help future gravitational-wave detections (e.g., GW170817 from Abbott et al., 2017) discriminate between magnetized and non-magnetized equations of state. The results also confirm that the monopole approximation (Chatterjee et al., 2019) is valid for strong fields in the core, enabling the use of standard TOV equations.
