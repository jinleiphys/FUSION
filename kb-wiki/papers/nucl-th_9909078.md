# Higher Dimension Operators and Chiral Symmetry Breaking in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

> We point out that adding higher dimension operators to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model can stabilize chiral symmetry breaking and resolve the issue raised by Kleinert and Van den Bossche that chiral symmetry breaking cannot occur for $N_c \le N_c^{\rm cr}$ due to large quantum fluctuations of the composite fields.

## Key claim

Adding higher dimension operators (HDOs) to the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model can increase the stiffness (pion decay constant squared) enough to suppress quantum fluctuations of mesons, thereby preventing the chiral symmetry restoration that Kleinert and Van den Bossche (1999) argued would occur for \(N_c \le N_c^{\rm cr}\) (including the physical case \(N_c=3\)).

## Method

The authors start from the two-flavor NJL model with a four-fermion contact interaction and introduce HDOs via auxiliary meson fields, specifically adding a term proportional to \((\partial_\mu \sigma)^2 + (\partial_\mu \pi_a)^2\) to the Lagrangian. They compute the wave function normalization \(Z(\rho^2)\) and the effective potential \(V(\rho^2)\) in the large \(N_c\) limit, then derive the stiffness \(\beta = Z(\rho_0^2) \rho_0^2\) and compare it to the critical stiffness \(\beta_{\rm cr}\) from the meson-loop gap equation. The analysis is extended to a general class of HDOs parameterized by a polynomial \(K\) and additional potential terms.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for chiral symmetry breaking in the original NJL model is \(g_{\rm cr} = 2\pi^2\).
- The critical stiffness for chiral symmetry restoration is \(\beta_{\rm cr} = \Lambda^2 / (4\pi^2)\).
- For \(N_c = 3\), the condition \(\beta < \beta_{\rm cr}\) (leading to no chiral symmetry breaking) is satisfied when \(\rho_0^2 / \Lambda^2 < e^{-1}\).
- Adding a HDO with coefficient \(c = 0.7/g\) can shift the critical number of colors to \(N_c^{\rm cr} < 3\).

## Context

This work addresses the claim by Kleinert and Van den Bossche (1999) that quantum fluctuations of composite mesons in the NJL model restore chiral symmetry for \(N_c \le N_c^{\rm cr}\), which would invalidate the model as a low-energy description of QCD for \(N_c=3\). The authors extend earlier discussions of HDOs in the NJL model by Suzuki (1990) and Hasenfratz et al. (1991), showing that such operators can stabilize chiral symmetry breaking. The results enable the NJL model to remain viable for phenomenological applications by treating the stiffness and sigma meson mass as free parameters determined by the HDOs.
