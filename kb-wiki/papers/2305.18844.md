# Forward-backward correlations with the $Σ$ quantity in the wounded constituent framework at LHC energies

> $Σ$ is a new correlation measure, quite recently introduced to heavy-ion physics. This measure, defined in the independent source model as a strongly intensive quantity, is expected to be free of the effects of system volume and volume fluctuations. This paper discusses the forward-backward correlation quantified with the $Σ$ observable calculated in the framework of the wounded nucleon model (WNM) and wounded quark model (WQM). Findings show that the wounded constituent approach outperforms the commonly used heavy-ion Monte Carlo generators, such as HIJING, AMPT or EPOS, by accurately describing the experimental data on FB correlations with $Σ$ measured by ALICE in Xe--Xe reactions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$=5.44 TeV and in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$= 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. This paper demonstrates that $Σ$ can be a unique tool for determining the fragmentation function of a wounded constituent in a symmetric nucleus-nucleus collision. However, in the wounded constituent framework, it is no longer a strongly intensive quantity.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the wounded nucleon model (WNM) and wounded quark model (WQM) can accurately describe ALICE data on forward-backward correlations measured with the Sigma observable in Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb collisions, outperforming Monte Carlo generators such as HIJING, AMPT, or EPOS. However, within the wounded constituent framework, Sigma is no longer a strongly intensive quantity as it was in the independent source model.

## Method

The author calculated the forward-backward correlation quantified by the Sigma observable within the wounded nucleon model (WNM) and wounded quark model (WQM) frameworks. These calculations were compared to experimental data from ALICE for Xe-Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. The wounded constituent approach was evaluated against commonly used heavy-ion Monte Carlo generators including HIJING, AMPT, and EPOS.

## Key numbers

* Xe-Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV: Sigma observable data from ALICE were used for comparison with WNM and WQM calculations.
* Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: ALICE data on Sigma were used to test the wounded constituent framework.
* Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: ALICE data on Sigma were used to test the wounded constituent framework.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Sigma correlation measure, recently introduced to heavy-ion physics, by testing it within the wounded nucleon and wounded quark models. The findings contradict the performance of established Monte Carlo generators such as HIJING, AMPT, and EPOS, which the wounded constituent approach outperforms in describing ALICE data. The paper demonstrates that Sigma can serve as a unique tool for determining the fragmentation function of a wounded constituent in symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions, though the loss of strong intensity in this framework raises open questions about volume fluctuation handling.
