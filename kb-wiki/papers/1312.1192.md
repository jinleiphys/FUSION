# Charge asymmetry in high-energy $μ^+μ^-$ photoproduction in the electric field of a heavy atom

> The charge asymmetry in the differential cross section of high-energy $μ^+μ^-$ photoproduction in the electric field of a heavy atom is obtained. This asymmetry arises due to the Coulomb corrections to the amplitude of the process (next-to-leading term with respect to the atomic field). The deviation of the nuclear electric field from the Coulomb field at small distances is crucially important for the charge asymmetry. Though the Coulomb corrections to the total cross section are negligibly small, the charge asymmetry is measurable for selected final states of $μ^+$ and $μ^-$. We further discuss the feasibility for experimental observation of this effect.

## Key claim

The charge asymmetry in high-energy muon pair photoproduction off heavy atoms is measurable (up to tens of percent) for selected final states, even though Coulomb corrections to the total cross section are negligibly small; the asymmetry arises from the next-to-leading quasiclassical correction (proportional to η³) and depends crucially on the deviation of the nuclear electric field from a pure Coulomb field at small distances.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for μ⁺μ⁻ photoproduction in the electric field of a heavy atom using the quasiclassical approximation, with the first quasiclassical correction taken into account. They express the wave functions via the asymptotics of the Green function of the squared Dirac equation and compute the matrix elements using perturbation theory, keeping terms up to third order in the atomic potential V(r). The antisymmetric part of the cross section (dσ_a ∝ η³) is obtained in the region where the perpendicular momentum transfer Q_⊥ is much smaller than the muon transverse momenta, and the nuclear form factor is included via the Fourier transform of the atomic potential.

- Muon Compton wavelength λ_μ = 1/m = 1.87 fm; nuclear radius R = 7.3 fm for gold and 7.2 fm for lead.
- For the form factor approximation F₀(Q²) = 1/(1+Q²/Λ²), Λ ≈ 80 MeV for heavy nuclei; the function ℱ(Q) ≈ 1 − 6Q/(πΛ) at Q ≪ Λ.

## Context

This work extends the study of Coulomb corrections in high-energy lepton pair photoproduction, previously investigated for e⁺e⁻ pairs by Bethe and Maximon (1954), Davies, Bethe, and Maximon (1954), and by Lee, Milstein, and Strakhovenko (2004, 2012). The authors show that for muon pairs, unlike electron pairs, the nuclear form factor strongly suppresses the Coulomb corrections to the total cross section but not the charge asymmetry, which is sensitive to the nuclear charge distribution. The proposed measurement can be conducted jointly with the APEX experiment (JLab E12-10-009) searching for dark photons, and could also be used to test lepton universality by comparing electron and muon production.
