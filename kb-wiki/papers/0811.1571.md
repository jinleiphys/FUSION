# Transport Coefficients of Hadronic Matter near $T_c$

> A hadron resonance gas model including all known particles and resonances with masses $m<2$ GeV and an exponentially rising density of Hagedorn states for $m>2$ GeV is used to obtain an upper bound on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s\approx1/(4π)$, of hadronic matter near $T_c$. We found a large trace anomaly and small speed of sound near $T_c$, which agree well with recent lattice calculations. We comment on the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio close to $T_c$.

## Key claim

The paper uses a hadron resonance gas model that includes an exponentially rising density of Hagedorn states for masses above 2 GeV to obtain an *upper bound* on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of hadronic matter near the critical temperature, showing it can be as low as the KSS bound of 1/(4π), but only if interactions between Hagedorn states and standard hadrons are neglected.

## Method

The authors construct a hadron resonance gas model combining all known hadrons and resonances with masses below 2 GeV from the Particle Data Group with an exponentially increasing density of Hagedorn states for masses above 2 GeV, using a specific mass spectrum ρ_HS(m) = A exp(m/T_H) / (m² + m₀²)^(5/4) with T_H = T_c = 196 MeV. Repulsive interactions among hadrons are included via an excluded-volume approach with an effective MIT bag constant B^(1/4) = 0.34 GeV. The shear viscosity is computed within kinetic theory, treating Hagedorn states non-relativistically and using their decay widths to estimate mean free paths, while the bulk viscosity is estimated via the Euclidean correlator of the energy-momentum tensor trace using a specific ansatz for the spectral density.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature used in the model is T_c = 196 MeV.
- The speed of sound squared near T_c is c_s² ≈ 0.09 when Hagedorn states are included, compared to c_s² ≈ 0.25 without them.
- The Hagedorn state mass spectrum parameters are m₀ = 0.5 GeV and A = 0.5 GeV^(3/2).
- The excluded-volume parameter is B^(1/4) = 0.34 GeV.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio upper bound from Hagedorn states approaches η/s ≈ 1/(4π) near T_c.
- The maximum Hagedorn state mass used in the main calculations is M_max = 20 GeV, with checks up to 80 GeV.
- The bulk viscosity estimate uses ω₀ = 1 GeV as the energy scale parameter.

## Context

This work extends prior hadron resonance gas calculations of η/s by Itakura (2007) and Gorenstein (2007), which did not include the exponentially rising Hagedorn spectrum, and shows that including these heavy states can lower η/s to the KSS bound near T_c. The model reproduces lattice results from Cheng (2007) for the trace anomaly and speed of sound, and suggests that hadronic matter near the phase transition may be more opaque to jets than previously thought. The results enable future studies of how the Hagedorn spectrum affects transport coefficients in heavy-ion collision simulations and motivate further investigation of the relationship between decay widths and momentum transport cross sections for heavy resonances.
