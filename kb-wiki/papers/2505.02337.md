# Dynamics of light nuclei produced in the massive transfer reactions

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system (DNS) model by implementing the cluster transfer into the dissipation process, we systematically investigated the energy spectra and the angular distribution of the preequilibrium clusters (n, p, d, t, $^{3}$He, $α$, $^{6,7}$Li, $^{8,9}$Be) in the massive transfer reactions of $^{12}$C+$^{209}$Bi, $^{14}$N+$^{159}$Tb, $^{14}$N+$^{169}$Tm, $^{14}$N+$^{181}$Ta, $^{14}$N+$^{197}$Au, $^{14}$N+$^{209}$Bi, $^{58,64,72}$Ni+$^{198}$Pt near the Coulomb barrier energies. It is found that the neutron emission is the most probable in comparison with the charged particles and the $α$ yields are comparable with the hydrogen isotopes in magnitude. The preequilibrium clusters are mainly produced from the projectile-like and target-like fragments in the evolution of dinuclear system. The kinetic energy spectra manifest the Boltzmann distribution and the Coulomb potential influences the structure. The preequilibrium clusters follows the angular distribution of multinucleon transfer fragments.

## Key claim

The dinuclear system (DNS) model, extended to include cluster transfer in the dissipation process, reproduces the energy spectra and angular distributions of preequilibrium clusters (n, p, d, t, 3He, α, 6,7Li, 8,9Be) in massive transfer reactions near the Coulomb barrier, with the caveat that the model assumes local equilibrium of the DNS fragments and ignores quasi-fission and fission of heavy fragments in the dissipation process.

## Method

The authors extended the DNS model by incorporating cluster transfer into the master equation that governs the time evolution of fragment distribution probabilities, including terms for transfer of neutrons, protons, deuterons, tritons, 3He, and α particles. The emission probabilities of preequilibrium clusters were calculated via the uncertainty principle using Weisskopf evaporation widths, with kinetic energy spectra sampled by Monte Carlo methods using a Watt spectrum for neutrons and a Boltzmann distribution for charged particles. Angular distributions were computed using a deflection function method that combines Coulomb and nuclear deflection contributions.

## Key numbers

- For 12C+209Bi at Elab=73 MeV: neutron cross section σn = 2.90×10−1 mb, proton σp = 2.58×10−6 mb, deuteron σd = 3.83×10−9 mb, triton σt = 4.06×10−8 mb, 3He σ3He = 7.58×10−20 mb, α σα = 4.54×10−6 mb
- For 14N+159Tb at Elab=115 MeV: σn = 1.45×10−1 mb, σp = 1.29×10−6 mb, σd = 1.92×10−9 mb, σt = 2.03×10−8 mb, σ3He = 3.79×10−20 mb, σα = 2.27×10−6 mb
- For 14N+209Bi at Elab=115 MeV: σn = 9.05×10−3 mb, σp = 8.08×10−8 mb, σd = 1.20×10−10 mb, σt = 1.27×10−9 mb, σ3He = 2.37×10−9 mb, σα = 1.42×10−7 mb
- For 58Ni+198Pt at Elab=170.2 MeV: σn = 1.11×10−4 mb, σp = 7.65×10−5 mb, σα = 2.96×10−9 mb
- For 64Ni+198Pt at Elab=181.4 MeV: σn = 6.94×10−6 mb, σp = 4.85×10−6 mb, σα = 1.85×10−10 mb
- For 72Ni+198Pt at Elab=176.0 MeV: σn = 1.74×10−6 mb, σp = 1.21×10−6 mb, σα = 4.62×10−11 mb
- The emission probabilities of α and hydrogen isotopes are comparable, about 3 to 4 orders of magnitude lower than neutrons, but much larger than 3He
- The peak of the kinetic energy spectrum for hydrogen isotopes is about 10 MeV
- The time step for emission probability calculations was Δt = 0.5×10−22 s for 12C and 14N induced reactions, and Δt = 0.25×10−22 s for Ni isotope induced reactions

## Context

This work extends the DNS model previously developed by the Lanzhou group (Feng et al., 2005-2010) for fusion-evaporation and multinucleon transfer reactions, now incorporating cluster transfer to describe preequilibrium emission. It builds on experimental measurements of preequilibrium cluster emission from RIKEN (Ut1980) for 14N-induced reactions and from IMP/HIRFL (Jin1980) for 12C+209Bi, and the calculated kinetic energy spectra show good agreement with those experimental data. The results enable future studies of cluster structure in nuclei and the formation mechanism of clusters in nuclear reactions, particularly relevant for the HIAF facility in China.
