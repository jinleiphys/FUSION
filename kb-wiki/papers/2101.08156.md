# Extensive Studies of the Neutron Star Equation of State from the Deep Learning Inference with the Observational Data Augmentation

> We discuss deep learning inference for the neutron star equation of state (EoS) using the real observational data of the mass and the radius. We make a quantitative comparison between the conventional polynomial regression and the neural network approach for the EoS parametrization. For our deep learning method to incorporate uncertainties in observation, we augment the training data with noise fluctuations corresponding to observational uncertainties. Deduced EoSs can accommodate a weak first-order phase transition, and we make a histogram for likely first-order regions. We also find that our observational data augmentation has a byproduct to tame the overfitting behavior. To check the performance improved by the data augmentation, we set up a toy model as the simplest inference problem to recover a double-peaked function and monitor the validation loss. We conclude that the data augmentation could be a useful technique to evade the overfitting without tuning the neural network architecture such as inserting the dropout.

## Key claim

The deep learning inference method, when combined with observational data augmentation that adds noise fluctuations corresponding to measurement uncertainties, can infer the neutron star equation of state from mass-radius data while accommodating a weak first-order phase transition, though the method's prior dependence arises implicitly from the uniform distribution of sound-speed parameters rather than from an explicit Bayesian prior.

## Method

The authors generated random equations of state by assigning average squared sound speeds \(c_{s,i}^2\) in five density segments between \(\varepsilon_0 = 150\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\) and \(8\varepsilon_0 = 1200\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\), using SLy4 for the low-density crust. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for each generated EoS to obtain mass-radius curves, then augmented the training data by adding noise fluctuations corresponding to observational uncertainties. A deep feedforward neural network was trained to perform the inverse mapping from mass-radius data points back to the EoS parameters, and its performance was compared against conventional polynomial regression.

## Key numbers

- The density range for EoS parametrization was \([\varepsilon_0,\ 8\varepsilon_0]\) with \(\varepsilon_0 = 150\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\) and \(\varepsilon_5 = 1200\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\), partitioned into five segments at \((\varepsilon_0, \varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_3, \varepsilon_4, \varepsilon_5) = (150, 227, 345, 522, 792, 1200)\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\).
- The squared sound speed parameters \(c_{s,i}^2\) were drawn from a uniform distribution within \(\delta \le c_{s,i}^2 < 1-\delta\) with \(\delta = 0.01\).
- The saturation density of nuclear matter is \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \text{(nucleons)}/\text{fm}^3\), and the central baryon density in neutron stars may reach 5-10 times \(n_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous deep learning approach to EoS inference (Fujimoto, 2017; Fujimoto, 2019) by adding comprehensive analyses including data augmentation and comparison with polynomial regression. It provides a discriminative modeling alternative to Bayesian inference methods (Ozel, 2010; Steiner, 2010; Raithel, 2016) and Gaussian process approaches (Landry, 2018; Essick, 2019), offering complementary tools that can help assess systematic uncertainties in EoS extraction from neutron star observations.
