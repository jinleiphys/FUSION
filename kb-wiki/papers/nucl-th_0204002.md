# (^{8})B breakup in elastic and transfer reactions

> We have studied the transfer reaction $^{14}$N($^7$Be,$^8$B)$^{13}$C at (\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{lab}}=84) MeV, paying special attention to the effects of the coupling to the continuum in the exit channel. Using the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) formalism, we find that these effects are important for the description of the elastic scattering observables. However, for the transfer process, differences between the predictions of the differential cross section within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) and the CDCC-Born approximation (CDCC-BA) are found to be negligible. This result supports the use of the DWBA method as a reliable tool to extract the $S_{17}(0)$ factor in this case.

## Key claim

The coupling to the continuum of \(^{8}\)B is important for describing elastic scattering observables in the exit channel, but for the transfer process \(^{14}\)N(\(^{7}\)Be,\(^{8}\)B)\(^{13}\)C at 84 MeV, the differences between the DWBA and CDCC-Born approximation predictions for the differential cross section are negligible, supporting the use of DWBA as a reliable tool to extract the \(S_{17}(0)\) factor in this specific case.

## Method

The authors used the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) formalism to describe the \(^{8}\)B+\(^{13}\)C elastic scattering at 78.4 MeV, discretizing the \(^{8}\)B continuum into energy bins up to 9 MeV with \(s\), \(p\), and \(d\) partial waves. For the transfer reaction, they compared the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) with the CDCC-Born approximation (CDCC-BA), where the exact wavefunction in the exit channel was approximated by the CDCC wavefunction and the transfer step was treated in Born approximation.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{8}\)B proton separation energy is 0.137 MeV, motivating the study of continuum coupling effects.
- The CDCC calculation used \(N=10\) bins of equal energy width covering the continuum from 0 to 9 MeV, with maximum total angular momentum \(L_{\mathrm{max}}=100\) and matching radius of 40 fm.
- The DWBA calculation using the renormalized double folding potential with \(N_r=0.366\) differed from the full CDCC-BA calculation by only 5% at the maximum of the angular distribution for the transfer reaction at 84 MeV.
- At 160 MeV, the CDCC-BA and DWBA transfer cross sections differed by less than 1% at angles up to 30 degrees; at 40 MeV, differences of around 8% were found at the maximum of the angular distribution.
- The CDCC elastic scattering for \(^{8}\)B+\(^{13}\)C at 78.4 MeV could be reproduced by an optical model calculation using a renormalized double folding potential with complex renormalization constants \(N_r=0.427\) and \(N_i=0.883\), compared to the previously proposed \(N_r\simeq 0.366\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the transfer reaction \(^{14}\)N(\(^{7}\)Be,\(^{8}\)B)\(^{13}\)C previously measured and analyzed within DWBA by Azhari et al. (1999) to extract the astrophysical \(S_{17}\) factor for the capture reaction \(^{7}\)Be+\(p\rightarrow ^{8}\)B. The study addresses concerns raised by Mukhamedzhanov and Timofeyuk (1998) about systematic errors in the asymptotic normalization coefficient method, and complements the work of Nunes and Thompson (2001) which assessed the importance of target inelastic excitations. The results enable continued use of the DWBA method for ANC extractions from this specific reaction, while suggesting that continuum coupling effects may need evaluation for other reactions involving loosely bound nuclei at lower energies.
