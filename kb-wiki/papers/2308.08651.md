# Finite System Size Correction to the Effective Coupling in $ϕ^4$ Scattering

> We compute and explore numerically the finite system size correction to NLO $2\to2$ scattering in massive scalar $ϕ^4$ theory. The derivation uses "denominator regularization" (instead of the usual dimensional regularization) on a spacetime with spatial directions compactified to a torus, with characteristic lengths not necessarily of equal size. We determine a useful analytic continuation of the generalized Epstein zeta function to isolate the usual UV divergence. Self-consistently, the renormalized finite system size correction reduces to zero as the system size goes to infinity and, further, satisfies the optical theorem. One of our checks of unitarity leads to a generalization of a number theoretic result from Hardy and Ramanujan. Precise numerical exploration of the finite system size correction to the amplitude and coupling when two spatial dimensions are finite requires the exploitation of the analytic structure of the finite system size result via a dispersion relation. We find that the finite system size scattering amplitude exhibits "geometric" bound states. Even away from these bound states, the finite system size correction to the effective coupling can be large.

## Key claim

The finite system size correction to the NLO $2\to2$ scattering amplitude in massive scalar $\phi^4$ theory can be large for systems with two compact spatial directions, even away from "geometric bound states," but the correction is modest for systems with only one finite spatial direction at moderate and larger momenta and lengths.

## Method

The authors compute the NLO $2\to2$ scattering amplitude using "denominator regularization" on a spacetime with spatial directions compactified to a torus, with characteristic lengths not necessarily equal. They derive an analytic continuation of the generalized Epstein zeta function to isolate the UV divergence, apply a modified $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ renormalization scheme, and evaluate the finite system size correction numerically using dispersion relation techniques for the $s$ channel contribution in systems with two finite dimensions.

## Key numbers

- Finite system size corrections to thermodynamic quantities such as pressure and entropy can be $\sim 40\%$ for systems the size of proton-proton collisions, and on the order of $10\%$ for systems the size of mid-central nucleus-nucleus collisions (citing Mogliacci (2018) and Kitazawa (2019)).
- The NLO contribution to the amplitude in infinite volume satisfies the optical theorem with $2\,\mathrm{Im}\,\mathcal{M} = \frac{\lambda^2}{16\pi}\sqrt{1-\frac{4m^2}{s}}\,\theta(s-4m^2) + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^3)$.
- The beta function for $\phi^4$ theory is $\beta = \frac{3\lambda^2}{(4\pi)^2}$, yielding the running coupling $\lambda(\mu) = \frac{\lambda(\mu_0)}{1+\lambda(\mu_0)\frac{3}{(4\pi)^2}\ln\mu_0/\mu}$.
- The denominator regularization procedure yields a finite part of $-1$ compared to the $-\gamma_E+\ln4\pi$ from dimensional regularization, with the same $1/\epsilon$ divergence and $\ln\mu^2/\Delta^2$ dependence.

## Context

This work extends the study of finite system size effects in thermal field theory, following Mogliacci (2018) who showed that finite size corrections in a free massless scalar theory with Dirichlet boundary conditions can mimic the temperature dependence of QCD thermodynamics, and Horowitz (2021) who inserted finite size corrections to the coupling into hard thermal loop QCD calculations. The paper provides a step toward computing finite system size corrections to the QCD trace anomaly, which would impact the extraction of shear and bulk viscosities from hydrodynamic simulations of small collision systems at the LHC.
