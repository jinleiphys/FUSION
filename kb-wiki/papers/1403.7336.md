# Systematic investigation of low-lying dipole modes using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> Systematic investigations of the electric dipole (E1) modes of excitation are performed using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) theory. The Cb-TDHFB is able to describe dynamical pairing correlations in excited states of nuclear systems. We apply the method to the real-time calculation of linear response in even-even nuclei with Skyrme functionals. Effects of shell structure, neutron skin, deformation, and neutron chemical potential (separation energy) are studied in a systematic way. This reveals a number of characteristic features of the low-energy E1 modes. We also find a universal behavior in the low-energy E1 modes for heavy neutron-rich isotopes, which suggests the emergence of decoupled E1 peaks beyond N = 82.

## Key claim

The paper claims that systematic Cb-TDHFB calculations reveal a universal behavior in low-energy E1 modes for heavy neutron-rich isotopes, suggesting the emergence of decoupled E1 peaks beyond N=82, but this conclusion is qualified by the restriction to nuclei with neutron separation energies larger than 2.0 MeV due to the BCS-like treatment of pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors performed real-time linear-response calculations of the electric dipole (E1) strength function using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) theory with Skyrme energy density functionals (primarily SkM$^*$). The ground state was prepared by HF+BCS using the imaginary-time method, then a weak impulsive E1 field was applied and the time evolution was computed in a three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate-space representation up to T=10 MeV$^{-1}$. The E1 strength function was obtained from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent dipole moment, and the low-lying strength was quantified by the "PDR fraction" defined as the ratio of energy-weighted E1 strength below 10 MeV to the total energy-weighted sum rule.

## Key numbers

* PDR fraction for $^{132}$Sn calculated with SkM$^*$ is less than 2%, while relativistic calculations with DD-ME2, FSUGold, and NL3 give more than 4%, and experimental data suggest about 4%
* Maximum gradient of PDR fraction with respect to neutron skin thickness, $(df_\textrm{\sc pdr}/d\Delta r_\textrm{rms})_\textrm{max}\approx 0.26$ fm$^{-1}$, observed for Ni isotopes at N>50 and N>82
* Time step $dt = 0.0005$ MeV$^{-1}$ and total evolution time $T=10$ MeV$^{-1}$ used in calculations
* Smoothing parameter $\Gamma = 1$ MeV applied to the strength function
* Perturbation strength $\eta = 3\times10^{-5}$ (e fm)$^{-1}$ for the E1 operator
* Model space: (R,d) = (12, 0.8) fm for Z $\leq$ 20, and (R,d) = (15, 1) fm for 28 $\leq$ Z $\leq$ 50, giving about 8,000 3D mesh points

## Context

This work extends the systematic investigation of low-energy dipole modes previously performed with HF+RPA by Inakura et al. (2011) for nuclei up to Z=40, now including pairing correlations via the Cb-TDHFB method and extending to heavier nuclei up to Z=50. The study confirms the qualitative features found in the earlier HF+RPA work regarding the role of weakly-bound low-$\ell$ orbits in enhancing low-energy E1 strength, but shows that pairing correlations smooth out irregular deformation changes that appeared in the HF+RPA results for heavy isotopes. The paper also addresses the discrepancy between non-relativistic Skyrme calculations and relativistic mean-field predictions for the PDR strength in $^{132}$Sn, comparing SkM$^*$ and SkI3 functionals. The results enable future investigations of the correlation between PDR strength and neutron skin thickness across a wider range of nuclei, and suggest that decoupled E1 peaks may emerge beyond N=82.
