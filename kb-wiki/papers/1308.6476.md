# Dense baryonic matter in conformally-compensated hidden local symmetry: Vector manifestation and chiral symmetry restoration

> We find that, when the dilaton is implemented as a (pseudo-)Nambu-Goldstone boson using a conformal compensator or "conformon" in a hidden gauge symmetric Lagrangian written to $O(p^4)$ from which baryons arise as solitons, namely, skyrmions, the vector manifestation and chiral symmetry restoration at high density predicted in hidden local symmetry theory --- which is consistent with Brown-Rho scaling --- are lost or sent to infinite density. It is shown that they can be restored if in medium the behavior of the $ω$ field is taken to deviate from that of the $ρ$ meson in such a way that the flavor $U(2)$ symmetry is strongly broken at increasing density. The hitherto unexposed crucial role of the $ω$ meson in the structure of elementary baryon and multibaryon systems is uncovered in this work. In the state of half-skyrmions to which the skyrmions transform at a density $n_{1/2}^{} \gtrsim n_0^{}$ (where $n_0^{}$ is the normal nuclear matter density), characterized by the vanishing (space averaged) quark condensate but nonzero pion decay constant, the nucleon mass remains more or less constant at a value $\gtrsim$ 60 \% of the vacuum value indicating a large component of the nucleon mass that is not associated with the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. We discuss its connection to the chiral-invariant mass $m_0^{}$ that figures in the parity-doublet baryon model.

## Key claim

When a dilaton field is introduced as a conformal compensator into the hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian, the vector manifestation (VM) and chiral symmetry restoration at high density predicted by HLS theory are lost or pushed to infinite density; they can be restored only if the behavior of the omega meson deviates from that of the rho meson in medium such that flavor U(2) symmetry is strongly broken at increasing density.

## Method

The authors construct a conformally-invariant HLS Lagrangian up to O(p^4) by introducing a dilaton field as a conformal compensator, with baryons arising as skyrmion solitons. They study single skyrmion properties and dense skyrmion matter on a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal, using parameters fixed by matching to the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model with empirical values of the pion decay constant and vector meson mass. Two versions of the dilaton-coupled HLS are considered: dHLS-I where the dilaton couples equally to rho and omega mesons, and dHLS-II where the dilaton couples to the rho sector but not to the omega mass term.

## Key numbers

- The soliton mass in the dilatonless HLS model is 1184 MeV, about 300 MeV above the observed nucleon mass
- The skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition occurs at a density n_{1/2} greater than or approximately equal to n_0 (normal nuclear matter density, approximately 0.16 fm^{-3})
- In the half-skyrmion phase, the nucleon mass remains more or less constant at a value greater than or approximately 60% of its vacuum value
- The empirical values used to fix parameters are f_pi = 92.4 MeV and m_omega = m_rho = 775.5 MeV
- The binding energy at the minimum density n_min in the dilatonless HLS crystal is approximately 100 MeV per baryon, larger than the empirical value of approximately 16 MeV per baryon

## Context

This work extends previous skyrmion studies using HLS with rho and omega mesons (Ma et al. 2012, 2013) and addresses tensions with the vector manifestation predicted by Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho 1991). The dilaton implementation follows the Freund-Nambu mechanism for scale symmetry breaking and connects to the parity-doublet baryon model of Detar and Kunihiro (1989), where a chiral-invariant mass m_0 appears. The results enable further study of how chiral symmetry restoration and the vector manifestation fixed point can be realized in dense baryonic matter, and raise questions about the role of the omega meson in the structure of elementary baryons and multibaryon systems.
