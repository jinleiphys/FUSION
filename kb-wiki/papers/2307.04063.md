# Symmetry energy and neutron star properties constrained by chiral effective field theory calculations

> We investigate the nuclear symmetry energy and neutron star properties using a Bayesian analysis based on constraints from different chiral effective field theory calculations using new energy density functionals that allow for large variations at high densities. Constraints at high densities are included from observations of GW170817 and from NICER. In particular, we show that both NICER analyses lead to very similar posterior results for the symmetry energy and neutron star properties when folded into our equation-of-state framework. Using the posteriors, we provide results for the symmetry energy and the slope parameter, as well as for the proton fraction, the speed of sound, and the central density in neutron stars. Moreover, we explore correlations of neutron star radii with the pressure and the speed of sound in neutron stars. Our 95\% credibility ranges for the symmetry energy $S_v$, the slope parameter $L$, and the radius of a 1.4$\msun$ neutron star, $R_{1.4}$, are $S_v=(30.6\text{--}33.9)$\,MeV, $L=(43.7\text{--}70.0)$\,MeV, and $R_{1.4}=(11.6\text{--}13.2)$\,km. Our analysis for the proton fraction shows that larger and/or heavier neutron stars are more likely to cool rapidly via the direct Urca process. Within our equation-of-state framework a maximum mass of neutron stars $M_{\rm max}>2.1\msun$ indicates that the speed of sound needs to exceed the conformal limit.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when NICER mass-radius constraints from both the Amsterdam (Riley et al.) and Illinois/Maryland (Miller et al.) analyses are folded into a chiral EFT-informed equation-of-state framework, the resulting posterior distributions for the symmetry energy and neutron star properties are very similar, despite the different mass-radius posteriors from the two NICER groups.

## Method

The authors constructed a Bayesian analysis using a prior of 100,000 energy density functionals (EDFs) fit to chiral EFT neutron matter calculations from many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) at N3LO, with symmetric nuclear matter properties fixed to empirical values. They incorporated astrophysical likelihoods from the GW170817 tidal deformability and from both NICER analyses (PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620) to obtain posterior distributions for the EOS, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to extract neutron star properties and symmetry energy parameters.

## Key numbers

* 95% credibility range for symmetry energy: S_v = (30.6–33.9) MeV
* 95% credibility range for slope parameter: L = (43.7–70.0) MeV
* 95% credibility range for radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_1.4 = (11.6–13.2) km
* Prior 95% range for R_1.4 (d=3): (9.87–13.19) km, narrowing to posterior (11.57–13.17) km with Riley et al. analysis and (11.65–13.23) km with Miller et al. analysis
* For d=3, maximum mass reaches up to 2.23 solar masses in the EOS ensemble
* Prior mean values: ⟨S_v, L⟩ = (31.96, 51.70) MeV; posterior mean with Riley et al.: (32.23, 56.33) MeV; with Miller et al.: (32.31, 57.31) MeV
* 72% of prior EOSs have maximum mass > 2.0 solar masses; 97% (Riley) and 98% (Miller) of posterior EOSs exceed 2.0 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the EDF framework introduced by Huth et al. (2021) by incorporating chiral EFT neutron matter constraints from Drischler et al. (2019) and applying Bayesian inference with GW170817 and NICER data. It confirms and refines the symmetry energy and radius ranges found by Essick et al. (2021) using a different Gaussian process extension, and shows that both NICER analyses (Riley et al. 2019,2021 and Miller et al. 2019,2021) yield consistent posteriors when combined with nuclear physics priors. The results enable more precise predictions for neutron star cooling via the direct Urca process and for the speed of sound in dense matter.
