# Real-time Nonequilibrium Dynamics in Hot QED Plasmas

> The quantum kinetics of photons is studied directly in real time by implementing the dynamical renormalization group. In contrast to conventional approach, the dynamical renormalization group method consistently includes off-shell (energy non-conserving) effects and accounts for time-dependent collisional kernel. To lowest order we find that in the relaxation time approximation the semihard photon distribution function relaxes with a power law.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, in the relaxation time approximation, the semihard photon distribution function in a hot QED plasma relaxes with a power law rather than an exponential, but this result is conditional on the use of the dynamical renormalization group to resum secular terms that otherwise invalidate the perturbative solution at large times.

## Method

The author sets up an initial value problem for the semihard photon number operator in real time using Heisenberg equations of motion, then computes the time derivative of the distribution function perturbatively in powers of the electromagnetic coupling constant e using nonequilibrium Feynman rules and real-time propagators. The resulting perturbative solution contains secular (logarithmically growing) terms at large times, which are resummed by implementing the dynamical renormalization group to obtain a renormalization-group-improved quantum kinetic equation. The kinetic equation is then solved in the relaxation time approximation for a slightly off-equilibrium mode.

## Key numbers

* For semihard photons with momentum satisfying \(eT \ll k \ll T\) (where \(e \ll 1\) is the electromagnetic coupling constant), the time-integrated photon production rate at large times behaves as \(\int^t_{t_0} dt'\, \Gamma^<_k(t') = \frac{e^2 T^3}{6k^3}\left\{\ln\left[2k(t-t_0)\right]+\gamma-1\right\} + \mathcal{O}[1/k(t-t_0)]\), where \(\gamma = 0.577\ldots\) is Euler's constant.
* The power-law relaxation of a slightly off-equilibrium mode is given by \(\delta n_{\bf k}(t) = \delta n_{\bf k}(t_0)\,[k(t-t_0)]^{-e^2 T^2/6k^2}\) for \(k(t-t_0) \gg 1\).
* The lifetime of a deconfined quark-gluon plasma phase is estimated to be of order \(10-20\,{\rm fm}/c\) with an overall freeze-out time of order \(100\,{\rm fm}/c\), based on energy deposited at BNL RHIC energies \(\sqrt{s}\sim 200\,A\;{\rm GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical renormalization group approach previously developed by Boyanovsky, de Vega, and collaborators (Boyanovsky et al., 2000) to the specific case of photon kinetics in hot QED plasmas. It contrasts with conventional approaches that use the Wigner transform and the assumption of completed collisions (Geiger, 1990s; Danielewicz, 1980s), which lead to energy-conserving delta functions and time-independent rates. The method enables the study of direct photon production from a quark-gluon plasma of finite lifetime, where off-shell (energy non-conserving) effects from the finite system lifetime are phenomenologically important.
