# Azimuthal asymmetries in lepton-pair production at a fixed-target experiment using the LHC beams (AFTER)

> A multi-purpose fixed-target experiment using the proton and lead-ion beams of the LHC was recently proposed by Brodsky, Fleuret, Hadjidakis and Lansberg, and here we concentrate our study on some issues related to the spin physics part of this project (referred to as AFTER). We study the nucleon spin structure through $pp$ and $pd$ processes with a fixed-target experiment using the LHC proton beams, for the kinematical region with 7 TeV proton beams at the energy in center-of-mass frame of two nucleons $\sqrt{s}=115$ GeV. We calculate and estimate the $\cos2ϕ$ azimuthal asymmetries of unpolarized $pp$ and $pd$ dilepton production processes in the Drell--Yan continuum region and at the $Z$-pole. We also calculate the $\sin(2ϕ-ϕ_S)$, $\sin(2ϕ+ϕ_S)$ and $\sin2ϕ$ azimuthal asymmetries of $pp$ and $pd$ dilepton production processes with the target proton and deuteron longitudinally or transversally polarized in the Drell--Yan continuum region and around $Z$ resonances region. We conclude that it is feasible to measure these azimuthal asymmetries, consequently the three-dimensional or transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (3dPDFs or TMDs), at this new AFTER facility.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that azimuthal asymmetries in Drell-Yan dilepton production at the proposed AFTER fixed-target experiment using LHC 7 TeV proton beams (center-of-mass energy sqrt(s)=115 GeV) are measurable, which would enable access to transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs); however, the feasibility depends on the specific kinematic cuts employed, such as a rapidity cut of [-4.8, 1] where the momentum fraction in the polarized target nucleon is largest.

## Method

The authors computed the cos2phi azimuthal asymmetry for unpolarized pp and pd Drell-Yan processes, and the sin(2phi-phi_S), sin(2phi+phi_S), and sin2phi asymmetries for single-polarized processes, using the TMD factorization framework at leading twist. They employed the Boer-Mulders function h1_perp extracted from previous unpolarized pp and pd Drell-Yan data (Zhu 2006, Zhu 2008; Zhang 2008; Lu 2009), with a parametrization using a Gaussian transverse momentum dependence and a parameter omega constrained by the positivity bound (0.48 less than omega less than 2.1, with central value omega=1). Calculations were performed for both the Drell-Yan continuum region (Q from 2 GeV to 30 GeV) and the Z-pole region, including both gamma* and Z boson contributions, with the rapidity integrated in the cut [-4.8, 1].

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy for two nucleons: sqrt(s)=115 GeV, using 7 TeV LHC proton beams
- Rapidity cut applied: [-4.8, 1], chosen as the easiest region for measurements
- Parameter omega range from positivity bound: 0.48 less than omega less than 2.1, with central value omega=1 used for calculations
- Q range for Drell-Yan continuum calculations: 2 GeV to 30 GeV
- Specific Q values shown for x_F-dependent asymmetries: Q=2 GeV (low Q region) and Q=5 GeV (mid Q region)

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Brodsky, Fleuret, Hadjidakis and Lansberg (2012) for a fixed-target experiment using LHC beams (AFTER) by providing detailed calculations of azimuthal asymmetries relevant to spin physics. The study builds on the Boer-Mulders effect framework (Boer 1999) for explaining the cos2phi asymmetry observed by NA10 (Falciano 1986, Guanziroli 1987) and Fermilab E615 (Conway 1989), and the Sivers function framework for single spin asymmetries observed in pp^uparrow to pi X (Adams 1991, Bravar 1996). The results enable future experimental extraction of TMDs including the Boer-Mulders function, transversity, and the pretzelosity distributions at the proposed AFTER facility.
