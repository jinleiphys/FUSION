# Nucleon-pair truncation of the shell model for medium-heavy nuclei

> Background: Computationally tractable models of atomic nuclei is a long-time goal of nuclear structure physics. A flexible framework which easily includes excited states and many-body correlations is the configuration-interaction shell model (SM), but the exponential growth of the basis means one needs an efficient truncation scheme, ideally one that includes both deformation and pairing correlations. Purpose: We propose an efficient truncation scheme of the SM: starting from a pair condensate variationally defined by Hartree-Fock single-particle states and the particle-number conserved Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (NBCS) approximation, we carry out projection of states with good angular momentum. Methods: After generating Hartree-Fock single-particle states with Kramers degeneracy in a SM space, we optimize the pair amplitudes in the NBCS by minimizing the energy, and then use linear algebra projection (LAP) of states with good angular momentum. Both NBCS and LAP are computationally fast. Results: Our calculations yield good agreement with full configuration-interaction SM calculations for low-lying states of transitional and rotational nuclei with axially symmetric and triaxial deformation in medium- and heavy-mass regions: $^{44,46,48}$Ti, $^{48,50}$Cr, $^{52}$Fe, $^{60,62,64}$Zn, $^{66,68}$Ge, $^{68}$Se, and $^{108,110}$Xe. We predict low-lying states of $^{112-114}\textrm{Ba}$ and $^{116-120}\textrm{Ce}$, nuclei difficult to reach by large-scale SM calculations. Conclusions: Both pair correlation and the configuration mixing between different intrinsic states play a key role in reproducing collectivity and shape coexistence, demonstrating the utility of this truncation scheme of the SM to study transitional and deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The proposed particle-number conserved BCS (NBCS) with angular momentum projection (PNBCS) provides a computationally fast truncation scheme of the shell model that reproduces low-lying states of transitional and deformed nuclei, but the scheme deteriorates for spherical nuclei dominated by monopole pairing and single-particle splittings, where a multi-reference extension with broken-pair configurations would be needed.

## Method

The authors start with a Hartree-Fock calculation in a shell-model basis enforcing Kramers degeneracy, then construct a pair condensate using the particle-number conserved BCS (NBCS) formalism where collective pairs are built from time-reversed HF single-particle states. The pair amplitudes are optimized by minimizing the energy, and states with good angular momentum are projected out using the linear algebra projection (LAP) method, which is faster than traditional quadrature approaches. For open-shell nuclei with both valence protons and neutrons, the energy minimization includes a proton-neutron interaction term in the iterative determination of pair structure coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the generalized seniority scheme in deformed orbits (PhysRevC.96.034313, PhysRevC.99.014302) from identical-particle systems to open-shell nuclei with both protons and neutrons, adding angular momentum projection. It builds on the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) of the shell model (npa3, npaodd) and the projected shell model (psm, PhysRevC.77.061305), but offers faster computation than projected HFB (SCHMID2004565, PhysRevC.92.064310) or general pair condensate approaches (PhysRevC.105.034317, lu2022nuclear). The method enables predictions for nuclei such as $^{112-114}$Ba and $^{116-120}$Ce that are difficult to reach with large-scale shell-model calculations, and the authors note that a multi-reference PNBCS with broken-pair configurations could improve results for spherical nuclei.
