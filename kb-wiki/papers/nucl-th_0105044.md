# Multiphase transport model for heavy ion collisions at RHIC

> Using a multiphase transport model (AMPT) with both partonic and hadronic interactions, we study the multiplicity and transverse momentum distributions of charged particles such as pions, kaons and protons in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies. Effects due to nuclear shadowing and jet quenching on these observables are also studied. We further show preliminary results on the production of multistrange baryons from the strangeness-exchange reactions during the hadronic stage of heavy ion collisions.

# Multiphase transport model for heavy ion collisions at RHIC

## Key claim
The AMPT model, when constrained by SPS data, reproduces the PHOBOS charged-particle pseudorapidity distribution in central Au+Au collisions at 130A GeV only if significant nuclear shadowing is included and jet quenching is weak (dE/dx ≤ 1 GeV/fm). The observed enhancement of multistrange baryons at SPS can be largely explained by hadronic strangeness-exchange reactions alone, but further enhancement at RHIC would signal partonic-stage strange quark production.

## Method
The AMPT model combines HIJING for initial parton and string production, Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for partonic scatterings, Lund string fragmentation for hadronization (with an average proper formation time of 0.7 fm/c), and a relativistic transport model (ART) for hadronic interactions. The authors tuned the model to SPS data by adding popcorn baryon-antibaryon production, K* resonance channels, and adjusting Lund splitting parameters; they then applied the same parameters to RHIC collisions and added strangeness-exchange reactions (K N → π Y, and assumed identical cross sections for K Λ → π Ξ and K Ξ → π Ω).

## Key numbers
- Central Au+Au collisions at 130A GeV: total charged particle pseudorapidity distribution from AMPT (solid curve) is consistent with PHOBOS data (full circle) for b ≤ 3 fm.
- Jet quenching of dE/dx = 1 GeV/fm (dashed curves) increases total charged multiplicity above the default AMPT result.
- At SPS (√s = 17A GeV, central Pb+Pb, b ≤ 3 fm): midrapidity yields from AMPT are K⁺ = 28, K⁻ = 19, Λ = 12, Ξ⁻ = 1.1, Ω⁻ = 0.19, with ratios Ξ⁻/Λ = 0.093 and Ω⁻/Ξ⁻ = 0.16.
- At RHIC (√s = 130A GeV, central Au+Au): midrapidity yields are K⁺ = 50, K⁻ = 45, Λ = 13, Ξ⁻ = 2.0, Ω⁻ = 0.43, with ratios Ξ⁻/Λ = 0.16 and Ω⁻/Ξ⁻ = 0.22.
- With enhanced strange quark production (strange quarks at same probability as light quarks in Lund fragmentation): RHIC yields become K⁺ = 68, K⁻ = 61, Λ = 13, Ξ⁻ = 5.1, Ω⁻ = 1.9, with ratios Ξ⁻/Λ = 0.38 and Ω⁻/Ξ⁻ = 0.37.

## Context
This work extends the HIJING model (used for initial conditions) and the ART hadronic transport model (developed by the same group) by adding a partonic cascade stage (ZPC) and strangeness-exchange reactions to form a unified multiphase description. It directly addresses the question raised by Rafelski (1982) of whether enhanced multistrange baryon production is a quark-gluon plasma signal; the authors show that hadronic strangeness-exchange can largely explain the SPS enhancement observed experimentally, but that further enhancement at RHIC would indicate partonic contributions. The results enable quantitative discrimination between hadronic and partonic sources of strangeness in future RHIC data, and provide a baseline for interpreting jet quenching and nuclear shadowing effects.
