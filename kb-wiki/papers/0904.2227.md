# Low-Energy Universality in Atomic and Nuclear Physics

> An effective field theory developed for systems interacting through short-range interactions can be applied to systems of cold atoms with a large scattering length and to nucleons at low energies. It is therefore the ideal tool to analyze the universal properties associated with the Efimov effect in three- and four-body systems. In this "progress report", we will discuss recent results obtained within this framework and report on progress regarding the inclusion of higher order corrections associated with the finite range of the underlying interaction.

## Key claim

The short-range effective field theory (EFT) provides a model-independent framework for analyzing universal properties of few-body systems with large two-body scattering length, including the Efimov effect in three- and four-body systems, but the leading-order results are only valid in the zero-range limit and require a three-body counterterm for renormalization.

## Method

The author develops a short-range EFT for non-relativistic particles interacting through short-range potentials, using a Lagrangian with contact interactions and an auxiliary dimeron field to simplify three-body calculations. The two-body scattering amplitude is resummed non-perturbatively at leading order to reproduce the pole structure for large scattering length, while three-body observables are obtained by solving the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation with a three-body force included to achieve cutoff-independent results. Higher-order corrections from finite range effects are treated perturbatively by expanding the two-body propagator in powers of the effective range.

## Key numbers

- Two-body binding energy for large positive scattering length: \(E_D = 1/(m a^2)\) (with \(\hbar=1\))
- The three-body force coupling \(H(\Lambda)\) is periodic in the cutoff \(\Lambda\) with period \(\exp(\pi/s_0) \approx 22.7\), where \(s_0 \approx 1.00624\)
- In the limit of infinite scattering length, Efimov trimer binding energies follow a geometric spectrum: \(E_T^{(n)} = (e^{-2\pi/s_0})^{n-n_*} \kappa_*^2 / m\), where \(\kappa_*\) is the binding wavenumber of the trimer labeled by \(n_*\)
- The scattering length at which an Efimov trimer crosses the dimer-atom threshold is \(a_* \approx 0.0798 \,\kappa_*^{-1}\)
- For the unitary limit (\(a \rightarrow \infty\)), the leading-order binding energy shift from next-to-leading order effective range corrections is zero: \(E_{T,{\rm LO}}^{(n)} - E_{T,{\rm NLO}}^{(n)} = 0\) for all \(n\) and \(\gamma=0\)

## Context

This progress report reviews and extends work on universal few-body physics using short-range EFT, building on the foundational three-body calculations of Bedaque, Hammer and van Kolck (1998-1999) and the earlier discovery of the Efimov effect by Efimov (1970). The framework enables systematic inclusion of finite-range corrections beyond the zero-range limit, which was previously addressed only partially by Efimov (1991, 1993) using the hyperspherical formalism. The approach provides model-independent constraints for nuclear few-body calculations, such as the Phillips line correlation between particle-dimer scattering length and three-body binding energy, and can be extended to electroweak observables relevant to nuclear astrophysics.
