# Study of the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleons in the timelike region

> The electromagnetic form factors $G_{\rm E}$ and $G_{\rm M}$ of the proton and neutron in the timelike region are extracted in a study of the processes $e^+ e^-\to \bar{p}p$ and $e^+ e^-\to \bar{n}n$. The reaction amplitude is evaluated within the distorted wave Born approximation, with the interaction of the antinucleon-nucleon ($\bar{N}N$) pair taken into account. The latter is constructed within $SU(3)$ chiral effective field theory up to the next-to-leading order. An excellent description of the $e^+ e^-\to \bar{N}N$ data in the energy region from the $\bar{N}N$ threshold up to center-of-mass energies $E_{\rm cm}=2.2$~GeV is achieved. Results for the electromagnetic form factors $G_{\rm E}, G_{\rm M}$, $G_{\rm E}/G_{\rm M}$, and the subtracted effective form factors, $G_{\rm osc}$, are provided. These can be helpful for further studies of the properties of the nucleons.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic form factors of the proton and neutron in the timelike region are extracted from threshold up to 2.2 GeV by including the antinucleon-nucleon final-state interaction via chiral effective field theory, achieving an excellent description of the data; the analysis specifically addresses whether the phase difference observed in the effective form factors at higher energies persists in the individual electric and magnetic form factors near threshold.

## Method

The reaction amplitude for e+e- to antinucleon-nucleon was evaluated within the distorted wave Born approximation, where the antinucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude was obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for an interaction potential derived from SU(3) chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order. The potential includes one-boson exchange, two-boson exchange, and contact terms, with the low-energy constants fixed by fitting to phase shifts of the coupled 3S1-3D1 partial wave and experimental data on antinucleon-nucleon cross sections and differential cross sections. The bare production vertex parameters for proton and neutron were then determined by fitting to the e+e- to antinucleon-nucleon data.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers center-of-mass energies from the antinucleon-nucleon threshold (approximately 1.88 GeV) up to E_cm = 2.2 GeV.
* A phase difference in the oscillations of the effective form factors, Delta D = |D_p - D_n| = (125 plus or minus 12) degrees, was previously observed by the BESIII collaboration in the energy range E_cm between 2.00 and 3.08 GeV.
* The fine-structure constant used in the Sommerfeld-Gamow factor is alpha = 1/137.036.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of baryon electromagnetic form factors using chiral effective field theory combined with the distorted wave Born approximation, which had been applied to reactions such as e+e- to antinucleon-nucleon (Haidenbauer 2014), e+e- to anti-Lambda-Lambda (Haidenbauer 2016), and e+e- to Sigma+Sigma- (Haidenbauer 2020). The analysis directly addresses the experimental finding by the BESIII collaboration (2021) of a phase difference between proton and neutron effective form factors, and aims to determine whether this phenomenon holds near threshold where final-state interactions are essential. The results provide individual electric and magnetic form factors for both proton and neutron that can be used for further studies of nucleon internal structure.
