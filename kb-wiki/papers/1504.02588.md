# Anomalous decay and scattering processes of the eta meson

> We amend a recent dispersive analysis of the anomalous $η$ decay process $η\toπ^+π^-γ$ by the effects of the $a_2$ tensor meson, the lowest-lying resonance that can contribute in the $πη$ system. While the net effects on the measured decay spectrum are small, they may be more pronounced for the analogous $η'$ decay. There are nonnegligible consequences for the $η$ transition form factor, which is an important quantity for the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment. We predict total and differential cross sections, as well as a marked forward-backward asymmetry, for the crossed process $γπ^-\toπ^-η$ that could be measured in Primakoff reactions in the future.

## Key claim

The paper amends a previous dispersive analysis of the anomalous decay η → π⁺π⁻γ by including the a₂(1320) tensor meson, the lowest-lying resonance in the πη system; while the net effects on the measured decay spectrum are small (less than 1% deviation in the whole decay region), they become more pronounced for the analogous η' decay and have nonnegligible consequences for the η transition form factor, which is important for the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment.

## Method

The authors calculate the tree-level contribution of the a₂(1320) tensor meson to η → π⁺π⁻γ using effective Lagrangians for tensor-meson–pseudoscalar and tensor-meson–photon couplings, with coupling constants fixed from experimental decay widths. They then unitarize the amplitude by including pion–pion P-wave rescattering dispersively via an Omnès representation, solving an inhomogeneous discontinuity relation that preserves unitarity in the t-channel in the presence of left-hand cuts approximated by the a₂ resonance. The resulting amplitude is fitted to KLOE decay data and then crossed to predict observables for the scattering process γπ⁻ → π⁻η.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly low-energy theorem gives F_{ηππγ} = 5.65 GeV⁻³ in the SU(3) chiral limit at vanishing momenta.
- The a₂ coupling constants extracted from experimental widths are |g_T| = (28.1 ± 1.4) MeV from a₂ → πη and |c_T| = (0.060 ± 0.004) GeV⁻¹ from a₂ → πγ.
- The slope parameter α_Ω from fitting the KLOE data without a₂ effects is (1.52 ± 0.06) GeV⁻²; with a₂ effects included, it becomes (1.42 ± 0.06) GeV⁻².
- The isovector slope of the η transition form factor b_η^(I=1) is reduced from {2.04…2.22} GeV⁻² (without a₂) to {1.90…2.04} GeV⁻² (with a₂), a reduction of about 7% for a cutoff Λ² ≈ 1 GeV².
- The predicted peak cross section for γπ⁻ → π⁻η is about (12 ± 2) μb.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of η → π⁺π⁻γ by Stollenwerk (2012), which assumed no left-hand cuts and used a linear polynomial times the pion vector form factor. The inclusion of the a₂(1320) resonance provides the first well-established left-hand-cut structure and a characteristic breakdown scale in the πη invariant mass squared above which πη resonances dominate the cross section. The results enable future measurements of γπ⁻ → π⁻η in Primakoff reactions (currently pursued by the COMPASS Collaboration) and improve the dispersive analysis of the η transition form factor, which is a crucial input for the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment.
