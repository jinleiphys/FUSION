# When gluons go odd: how classical gluon fields generate odd azimuthal harmonics for the two-gluon correlation function in high-energy collisions

> We show that, in the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework, odd azimuthal harmonics of the two-gluon correlation function with a long-range separation in rapidity are generated by the higher-order saturation corrections in the interactions with the projectile and the target. At the very least, the odd harmonics require three scatterings in the projectile and three scatterings in the target. We derive the leading-order expression for the two-gluon production cross section which generates odd harmonics: the expression includes all-order interactions with the target and three interactions with the projectile. We evaluate the obtained expression both analytically and numerically, confirming that the odd-harmonics contribution to the two-gluon production in the saturation framework is non-zero.

## Key claim

Odd azimuthal harmonics of the two-gluon correlation function with long-range rapidity separation are generated by higher-order saturation corrections requiring at least three scatterings in both the projectile and the target, contrary to the even harmonics produced by the leading-order "glasma graphs" which only involve double interactions.

## Method

The authors derived the leading-order expression for the two-gluon production cross section that generates odd harmonics by including all-order interactions with the target via Wilson lines and three interactions with the projectile, expanding the gluon production amplitude to the first two non-trivial orders (order g and order g^3) in the interaction with the projectile. They evaluated this expression both analytically using the Golec-Biernat–Wusthoff (GBW) approximation for the target interaction and numerically with all-orders target interactions, computing the resulting odd harmonic coefficients and the odd part of the two-gluon correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order gluon production amplitude M1 is of order g (the strong coupling), while the first correction M3 is of order g^3, as shown in the power counting expansion in terms of as^2 * A1^(1/3) where A1 is the projectile atomic number.
- The two-gluon production cross section is expanded as (1/as^2) * [(as^2 * A1^(1/3))^2 * h1(as^2 * A2^(1/3)) + (as^2 * A1^(1/3))^3 * h2(as^2 * A2^(1/3)) + ...], where h1 generates only even harmonics and h2 generates odd harmonics.
- The analytical evaluation using the GBW approximation yields a non-zero result for the odd-harmonics contribution, confirming that saturation dynamics generates non-trivial odd harmonics.

## Context

This work extends the "glasma graphs" approach of Dumitru (2008) and the subsequent saturation corrections in the target by Kovner (2012) and Kovchegov (2012), which only produced even azimuthal harmonics v_{2n}^2{2}. It addresses the conundrum that odd harmonics observed at RHIC and LHC (Abelev 2012, Chatrchyan 2013, Aad 2014) could not be explained by the existing saturation framework. The calculation builds on the order-g^3 amplitude results of Chirilli (2015) and the projectile saturation corrections proposed by McLerran (2016), enabling future phenomenological applications of the saturation/CGC approach to di-hadron correlation functions with odd harmonics.
