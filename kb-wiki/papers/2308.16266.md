# Distilling the essential elements of nuclear binding via neural-network quantum states

> In pursuing the essential elements of nuclear binding, we compute ground-state properties of atomic nuclei with up to $A=20$ nucleons, using as input a leading order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian. A variational Monte Carlo method based on a new, highly-expressive, neural-network quantum state ansatz is employed to solve the many-body Schrödinger equation in a systematically improvable fashion. In addition to binding energies and charge radii, we accurately evaluate the magnetic moments of these nuclei, as they reveal the self-emergence of the shell structure, which is not a priori encoded in the neural-network ansatz. To this aim, we introduce a novel computational protocol based on adding an external magnetic field to the nuclear Hamiltonian, which allows the neural network to learn the preferred polarization of the nucleus within the given magnetic field.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a simple leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian, combined with a neural-network quantum state ansatz, can reproduce binding energies, charge radii, and magnetic moments of nuclei up to A=20 with few-percent accuracy, but only after adjusting the three-body force regulator to R3=1.1 fm to prevent overbinding observed with the original R3=1.0 fm choice.

## Method
The authors solve the many-body Schrodinger equation using a variational Monte Carlo method with a hidden-nucleon neural-network quantum state ansatz augmented by equivariant backflow transformations based on a message-passing neural network. They employ a leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian with a repulsive three-body force, and introduce a novel three-stage protocol involving an external magnetic field to project the wave function onto specific orbital angular momentum states for magnetic moment calculations.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of nuclei up to A=20 show deviations of only a few percent from experimental values, with 12C and 15O underbound by about 0.6 MeV per nucleon
- Three-body force regulator set to R3=1.1 fm (increased from 1.0 fm) to prevent overbinding of 16O and heavier nuclei
- Magnetic field strength Bz=2.5 MeV used in the projection protocol for 7Li
- Magnetic moments computed using mu_S=0.8798 mu_N and mu_V=4.0759 mu_N for the spin contributions
- 6He, 8Li, 8B, 9C, and 17F are unstable against breakup into smaller clusters

## Context
This work extends the search for "essential elements of nuclear binding" initiated by Lu (2018), who showed that an SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonian with non-local three-body force could model nuclei up to A=40. It uses the leading-order pionless EFT Hamiltonian "o" developed by Schiavilla (2021) and builds on the hidden-nucleon neural-network quantum state framework introduced by Lovato (2022). The results enable systematic improvement of simple nuclear Hamiltonians and demonstrate that neural-network quantum states can learn nuclear shell structure without explicit encoding, while the magnetic moment protocol opens the door to studying electromagnetic properties of larger nuclei beyond the reach of Green's function Monte Carlo (limited to A<=12) and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo methods.
