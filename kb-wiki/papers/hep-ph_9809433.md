# Non-perturbative computation of gluon mini-jet production in nuclear collisions at very high energies

> At very high energies, in the infinite momentum frame and in light cone gauge, a hard scale proportional to the high parton density arises in QCD. In an effective theory of QCD at small $x$, this scale is of order $α_Sμ$, where $μ$ is simply related to the gluon density at higher rapidities. The ab initio real time evolution of small $x$ modes in a nuclear collision can be described consistently in the classical effective theory and various features of interest can be studied non-perturbatively. In this paper, we discuss results from a real time lattice computation of the production of gluon jets at very high energies. At very large transverse momenta, $k_t\geq μ$, our results match the predictions from pQCD based mini-jet calculations. Novel non-perturbative behaviour of the small $x$ modes is seen at smaller momenta $k_t\sim α_Sμ$. Gauge invariant energy-energy correlators are used to estimate energy distributions evolving in proper time.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a non-perturbative lattice computation of gluon mini-jet production in nuclear collisions matches perturbative QCD predictions at large transverse momenta ($k_t \geq \mu$) while revealing novel non-perturbative behavior at smaller momenta ($k_t \sim \alpha_S\mu$), with the caveat that the computation is restricted to SU(2) gauge theory and assumes boost invariance.

## Method

The authors performed real-time lattice simulations of classical Yang-Mills equations in 2+1 dimensions (assuming boost invariance) using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian coupled to an adjoint scalar field, with initial conditions provided by Weizsacker-Williams fields for the colliding nuclei. The lattice equations of motion were solved numerically for SU(2) gauge theory on lattices ranging from $10\times 10$ to $160\times 160$ sites, with gauge-invariant energy-energy correlators used to estimate energy distributions evolving in proper time. Results were averaged over Gaussian random color charge sources with variance $g^4\mu^2$.

## Key numbers

- Simulations performed for $g^2\mu$ values ranging from 0.018 to 0.2
- Lattice sizes from $10\times 10$ to $160\times 160$ sites (measured in lattice spacing units)
- At large transverse momenta $k_t \geq \mu$, results match perturbative QCD mini-jet predictions
- Non-perturbative behavior observed at smaller momenta $k_t \sim \alpha_S\mu$
- The infrared cutoff scale in the classical Yang-Mills case is $\lambda = \alpha_S\mu$, giving $L(k_t,\lambda) = \log(k_t^2/\lambda^2)$ for the infrared divergent function

## Context

This work extends the classical effective theory approach to nuclear collisions developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Kovner, McLerran and Weigert (1995), who formulated the problem as collisions of non-Abelian Weizsacker-Williams fields and obtained perturbative solutions. The non-perturbative lattice computation enables study of the infrared region where perturbative approaches break down, and can provide initial conditions for hydrodynamic evolution models such as those of Bjorken (1983) and Eskola, Kajantie and Ruuskanen (1997). The results also connect to the Gunion and Bertsch (1982) quantum bremsstrahlung formula for gluon radiation.
