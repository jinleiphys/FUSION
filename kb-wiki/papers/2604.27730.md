# Non-Gaussian hydrodynamic fluctuations in an expanding relativistic fluid

> We consider non-equilibrium evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuations in a hydrodynamic system undergoing a boost-invariant expansion described by Bjorken flow. We derive the evolution equations for two- and three-point velocity correlators using the effective field theory framework and present analytical solutions for them. We show that the average Landau frame is better suited for studying non-Gaussian fluctuations of velocity when relativistic effects are important. In the Bjorken background, the average Landau frame corresponds to the density frame. We demonstrate that the three-point correlators depend nonlinearly on the non-equilibrium dynamics of the two-point functions, and exhibit non-trivial effects such as memory. The importance of these effects in the context of the search for the QCD critical point via fluctuations is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper derives deterministic evolution equations for non-Gaussian (three-point) velocity and energy-density correlators in a boost-invariant expanding fluid, showing that the average Landau frame (which for Bjorken flow coincides with the density frame) avoids singular time-derivative terms that appear in the standard Landau frame when treating higher-order correlators. The three-point correlators depend nonlinearly on the two-point functions and exhibit memory effects, but the analysis remains within the hydrodynamic regime where fluctuations are parametrically small.

## Method
The authors use the effective field theory (EFT) approach to fluctuating hydrodynamics within the Schwinger–Keldysh framework, expanding the effective Lagrangian to cubic order in fluctuations around a Bjorken background. They derive the Schwinger–Dyson equations for equal-time two- and three-point correlation functions of energy density and momentum density fluctuations, obtaining deterministic evolution equations that are solved analytically. The analysis is performed in the density frame (equivalent to the average Landau frame for Bjorken flow), where noise terms are purely spatial and no singular time derivatives appear.

## Key numbers
- The small parameter controlling fluctuation magnitude is \(\epsilon \equiv (l/\xi)^3\), where \(\xi\) is the correlation length and \(l\) the wavelength; in the hydrodynamic limit \(\epsilon \ll 1\).
- The \(N\)-point correlator scales as \(\epsilon^{[N/2]}\) and the connected \(N\)-point correlator scales as \(\epsilon^{N-1}\).
- For Bjorken flow with constant speed of sound \(c_s\), the background temperature evolves as \(T(\tau)=T_0(\tau_{\rm in}/\tau)^{c_s^2}\) and energy density as \(\varepsilon(\tau)=\varepsilon_0(\tau_{\rm in}/\tau)^{1+c_s^2}\).
- The entropy density decreases as \(s(\tau)=s_0(\tau_{\rm in}/\tau)\).
- The dissipative coefficients in the density frame to leading order are \(D_1=2\eta\), \(D_2=\zeta+\eta/3\), \(D_3=4\eta\), \(D_4=\zeta-2\eta/3\), \(D_5=\zeta+4\eta/3\).

## Context
This work extends the hydro-kinetic approach to non-Gaussian fluctuations, which was previously developed for Gaussian fluctuations in Bjorken flow by Akamatsu (2015) and generalized to arbitrary backgrounds by An et al. (2019, 2020, 2022). It uses the EFT formulation of fluctuating hydrodynamics (Kovtun 2014, Crossley 2015, Glorioso 2017) and the density-frame formalism of Armas (2021). The analytical results are intended as input for the maximum-entropy freeze-out framework (Pradeep 2022, Karthein 2025, Basar 2026) to convert hydrodynamic fluctuations into experimentally accessible hadronic fluctuations, particularly for the search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions.
