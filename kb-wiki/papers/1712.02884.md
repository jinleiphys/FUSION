# Parton correlations in double parton scattering

> Double parton scattering events are directly sensitive to the correlations between two partons inside a proton and can answer fundamental questions on the connections between the proton constituents. In this chapter, the different types of possible correlations, our present knowledge of them, and the processes where they are likely to be important, are introduced and explained. The increasing integrated luminosity at the LHC and the refinements of the theory of double parton scattering, lead to interesting prospects for measuring, or severely constraining, two-parton correlations in the near future.

# Parton correlations in double parton scattering

## Key claim

Double parton scattering (DPS) events can directly probe correlations between two partons inside a proton, but the present knowledge of these correlations is limited to model calculations and theoretical bounds; the increasing luminosity at the LHC and refinements of DPS theory create prospects for measuring or severely constraining two-parton correlations in the near future, though no experimental extraction has yet been achieved.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of double parton scattering, introducing the double parton distributions (dPDFs) that encode the probability to find two partons with longitudinal momentum fractions x1, x2 at a relative transverse distance y inside the proton. They classify correlations into kinematic correlations (between x1, x2 and y) and quantum-number correlations (color, spin, flavor, fermion number), and survey results from model calculations (MIT bag model, light-front constituent quark models, dressed-quark models, valon models) and perturbative QCD evolution studies to assess the expected size and scale dependence of each correlation type.

## Key numbers

- The effective cross section sigma_eff extracted by different collaborations varies in the range 2–20 mb, with large error bars, depending on final states and center-of-mass energies (Aaboud 2016).
- For gluons at x ~ 10^-3, HERA measurements of gamma p -> J/psi p indicate <b^2> = const + 4 alpha' log(1/x) with alpha' ~ 0.15 GeV^-2 = (0.08 fm)^2 (Chekanov 2002, Aktas 2005).
- Color correlations are expected to be small for processes above Q_i^2 ~ 100 GeV^2 due to Sudakov suppression.
- In double D0 meson production at LHCb, longitudinally polarized gluons can contribute up to 50% of the unpolarized cross section (Echevarria 2015).
- The LF model calculation of same-sign W boson pair production yields a DPS cross section in line with previous estimates using a constant sigma_eff, but predicts a dependence on longitudinal variables (Ceccopieri 2017).

## Context

This chapter extends the theoretical work on DPS factorization (Diehl 2017, Paver 1982) and model calculations of dPDFs (Chang 2012, Rinaldi 2013, Rinaldi 2014, Broniowski 2013, Kasemets 2016) by providing a comprehensive review of correlation types and their expected phenomenological impact. It enables future experimental searches at the LHC to target specific channels (same-sign double-W production, double D0 production) and phase-space regions where correlations are most pronounced, and motivates further development of the combined SPS-DPS formalism (Diehl 2017, Buffing 2017) for differential measurements.
