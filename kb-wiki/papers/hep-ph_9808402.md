# Unresolved Questions in J/psi Production and Propagation in Nuclei

> In order to understand the J/psi suppression arising from the possible occurrence of the quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, it is necessary to have a comprehensive picture how the J/psi and its precursors are produced, what their properties after production are, and how the J/psi and its precursors propagate inside nuclear matter. There are unresolved questions in the descriptions of J/psi production and propagation. We outline some of these questions and discuss the approaches for their resolution.

## Key claim

The Color-Octet Model (COM) for J/psi production faces several unresolved discrepancies with experimental data, particularly regarding cross sections in gamma-proton reactions at HERA, the energy dependence of color-octet matrix elements between fixed-target and Tevatron measurements, the underproduction of chi_1 relative to chi_2 states, and the predicted polarization of produced J/psi mesons.

## Method

The paper reviews the Color-Evaporation Model, Color-Singlet Model (CSM), and Color-Octet Model (COM) for J/psi production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, then examines unresolved questions by comparing model predictions to experimental data from CDF at the Tevatron, HERA gamma-proton reactions, and fixed-target experiments. The author analyzes the propagation of J/psi precursors through nuclear matter using the Two-Gluon Model of the Pomeron (TGMP) to calculate absorption cross sections for color-singlet and color-octet states, and discusses both incoherent and coherent admixture models for precursor states in proton-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- Color-octet matrix element combination for J/psi from fixed-target data: ⟨O₈^J/ψ(¹S₀)⟩ + (7/m_c²)⟨O₈^J/ψ(³P₀)⟩ = 3.0 × 10⁻² GeV³
- Color-octet matrix element combination for ψ' from fixed-target data: ⟨O₈^ψ'(¹S₀)⟩ + (7/m_c²)⟨O₈^ψ'(³P₀)⟩ = 0.5 × 10⁻² GeV³
- Color-octet matrix element combination for J/psi from CDF at √s=1.8 TeV: ⟨O₈^J/ψ(¹S₀)⟩ + (3/m_c²)⟨O₈^J/ψ(³P₀)⟩ = 6.6 × 10⁻² GeV³
- Color-octet matrix element combination for ψ' from CDF at √s=1.8 TeV: ⟨O₈^ψ'(¹S₀)⟩ + (3/m_c²)⟨O₈^ψ'(³P₀)⟩ = 1.8 × 10⁻² GeV³
- Fixed-target values are a factor of 4 (for J/psi) and 7 (for ψ') smaller than Tevatron values if ⟨O₈(¹S₀)⟩ = ⟨O₈(³P₀)⟩/m_c²
- CSM predicts λ ~ 0.5 for J/psi decay angular distribution, but experimental data give λ ~ 0 for J/psi and ψ' production in pi-W collisions at 252 GeV and 125 GeV
- CSM predicts chi_1/chi_2 ratio of 0.067 for pi-N collision at 300 GeV, compared to experimental data of ~0.70
- Predicted B₁chi₁/B₂chi₂ = 0.13 versus observed ~1.4 for pi-N collision at 185 GeV and 300 GeV
- Pair production time tau_pair ~ 1/(2m_c) = 0.07 fm/c; evolution time tau_evol ~ 1/Lambda_QCD = 0.5 fm/c in J/psi rest frame
- Color-octet total cross section sigma_8 is typically 30-60 mb
- About 30.5% of observed J/psi comes from feeding from chi states, about 7.5% from psi' decay

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of J/psi suppression as a quark-gluon plasma probe, following the work of Matsui and Satz (1986) and the observation of anomalous suppression in Pb-Pb collisions by Gonin (1996) and Lourenco (1996). It reviews the inadequacies of the Color-Singlet Model (CSM) as documented by Bodwin, Braaten, and collaborators (1995-1997) and the development of the Color-Octet Model (COM) by Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1995). The paper identifies specific unresolved questions that must be addressed before J/psi suppression can be reliably interpreted as a quark-gluon plasma signature, and suggests that accurate measurements of J/psi production in pA collisions across a large set of nuclei could help determine the nature of the precursor state and its absorption properties.
