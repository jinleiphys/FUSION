# Determination of the masses and decay widths of the well-established $s$ and $p$ baryon resonances below $2$ GeV

> This study appertains to the extraction of estimates for the masses and for the partial decay widths to pion-nucleon final states of the well-established (four-star) $s$ and $p$ baryon resonances below $2$ GeV. These estimates are exclusively obtained from the data contained in the recent compilation by the Particle Data Group. Only the $N$ ($S=0,I=1/2$) and the $Δ$ ($S=0,I=3/2$) states are considered, where $S$ and $I$ denote strangeness and total isospin, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper extracts estimates for the masses and partial decay widths to pion-nucleon final states of four-star N and Delta baryon resonances below 2 GeV, using only data from the Particle Data Group (PDG) compilation (2020). The author notes that the PDG recommendations are not obtained from formal statistical procedures, and the results here are more precise on almost all occasions, except for the Breit-Wigner width of the N(1895) where the data cluster into two distinct sets.

## Method

The author performed robust statistical analyses (using Andrews and Tukey weight functions) on the 229 observations for N resonances and 80 observations for Delta resonances listed in the PDG compilation. Prior to optimization, the median uncertainty in each dataset replaced any individual uncertainty smaller than that median, to reduce the influence of overly precise but potentially unreliable datapoints. Outliers were identified by both robust methods, and the trimmed datasets were not subjected to standard chi-squared or cumulative distribution function processing due to data sparseness.

## Key numbers

- N(1440) Breit-Wigner mass: 1422.2^{+9.5}_{-9.4} MeV (PDG range: 1410-1470 MeV)
- N(1535) branching fraction to pi N: 39.0 ± 2.4% (PDG range: 32-52%)
- N(1650) Breit-Wigner width: 130.0^{+9.0}_{-8.8} MeV (PDG range: 100-150 MeV)
- N(1895) Breit-Wigner width: 440^{+89}_{-91} MeV (PDG range: 80-200 MeV), the largest discrepancy, due to data clustering around 109 MeV and 394 MeV
- N(1900) branching fraction to pi N: 3.0 ± 1.9% (PDG range: 2-20%)
- Delta(1600) Breit-Wigner mass: 1654 ± 29 MeV (PDG range: 1500-1640 MeV)
- Delta(1620) Breit-Wigner width: 130.8^{+9.1}_{-8.9} MeV (PDG range: 110-150 MeV)
- Delta(1910) branching fraction to pi N: 15.1^{+2.1}_{-2.0}% (PDG range: 15-30%)

## Context

This work extends the methodology previously applied to scalar-isoscalar and vector-isovector mesons in Matsinos (2020), now applied to baryon resonances for use in the ETH model (Goudsmit 1994, Matsinos 2017), a pion-nucleon interaction model based on s-, u-, and t-channel Feynman graphs. The results provide more precise estimates than the PDG ranges for the masses and decay widths needed to fix the s- and u-channel contributions of higher baryon resonances in that model, and the author notes that additional data are required to resolve the N(1895) width discrepancy.
