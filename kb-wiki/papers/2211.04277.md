# Medium modification of singly heavy baryons in a pion-mean field approach

> We investigate how the masses of singly heavy baryons undergo changes in nuclear matter. The mass spectrum of the singly heavy baryons was successfully described in a pion-mean field approach even with isospin symmetry breaking, based on which we extend the investigation to the medium modification of the singly heavy baryons. Since all dynamical parameters were determined by explaining the mass spectrum of the SU(3) light and singly heavy baryons in free space, we can directly implement the density-dependent functionals for the dynamical parameters, of which the density dependence was already fixed by reproducing the bulk properties of nuclear matter and medium modification of the SU(3) light baryons. We predict and discuss the density dependence of the masses of the singly heavy baryons.

## Key claim

The masses of singly heavy baryons (charmed and bottom) decrease in symmetric nuclear matter, but in asymmetric nuclear matter the mass shifts depend on the quark content of the baryon: baryons with more down quarks than up quarks experience a smaller decrease or even an increase due to Pauli repulsion from the excess of down quarks in the medium.

## Method

The authors use a pion-mean field approach (chiral quark-soliton model) in which a singly heavy baryon consists of \(N_c-1\) valence light quarks bound by a pion mean field, with the infinitely heavy quark treated as a static color source. They implement density-dependent functionals for the dynamical parameters (classical mass, moments of inertia, symmetry-breaking terms) that were previously fixed by reproducing bulk properties of nuclear matter and the medium modification of SU(3) light baryons in Ghim et al. (2021). The masses of the baryon antitriplet (\(\overline{\bm{3}}\)) and sextet (\(\bm{6}\)) with spin 1/2 and 3/2 are then evaluated at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\) for symmetric matter, asymmetric matter (\(\delta=1\)), and strange matter (\(\chi=0.15\)).

## Key numbers

- Volume energy of symmetric nuclear matter: \(a_V = -16\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from semi-empirical Bethe-Weizsacker formula)
- Compressibility of nuclear matter: \(K_0 \simeq (290\pm 70)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (range from various frameworks) or \(K_0 \sim (240\pm 20)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from giant monopole resonance data)
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{sym}}(\lambda=1) = 32\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Slope parameter: \(L_{\mathrm{sym}} = 60\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Density-dependent parameters: \(C_{\mathrm{cl}} = -0.0561\), \(C_1 = 0.6434\), \(C_2 = -0.1218\), \(C_{\mathrm{num}} = 65.60\), \(C_{\mathrm{den}} = 0.60\)
- In symmetric nuclear matter at \(\lambda=1\), \(\Lambda_c\) mass shifts from \(2272.84\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (free space) to \(2268.71\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(\Sigma_c^{++}\) shifts from \(2445.67\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(2372.37\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- In asymmetric matter (\(\delta=1\)), \(\Xi_c^+\) mass becomes \(2411.19\ \mathrm{MeV}\) while \(\Xi_c^0\) becomes \(2533.94\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (compared to free-space values \(2475.20\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(2478.18\ \mathrm{MeV}\) respectively)
- In strange matter (\(\chi=0.15\)), \(\Lambda_c\) mass becomes \(2264.49\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(\lambda=1\)
- For bottom baryons in symmetric matter at \(\lambda=1\): \(\Lambda_b\) shifts from \(5599.30\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(5595.17\ \mathrm{MeV}\); \(\Sigma_b^+\) shifts from \(5801.24\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(5729.83\ \mathrm{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the pion mean-field approach previously applied to SU(3) light baryons in nuclear matter by Ghim et al. (2021) to singly heavy baryons, using the same density-dependent parameters without additional fitting. It builds on the successful description of singly heavy baryon masses in free space by Yang et al. (2016) and subsequent works. The results enable predictions for how charmed and bottom baryons behave in dense nuclear environments, relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments and neutron star physics, and can be compared with earlier studies using relativistic mean-field theory (Tan 2004), the quark-meson coupling model (Tsushima 2002, 2003), QCD sum rules (Wang 2011, 2012; Azizi 2016), and the SU(3) Skyrme model with bound-state approach (Won 2021).
