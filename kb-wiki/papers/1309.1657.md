# Influence of multiple in-medium scattering processes on the momentum imbalance of reconstructed di-jets

> Experimental data measured in sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC show a significant enhancement of events with an unbalanced pair of reconstructed jet momenta in comparison with p+p collisions. This enhancement of momentum imbalance is supposed to be caused by the different momentum loss of the initial back-to-back di-partons by scatterings within the created dense medium. For investigating the underlying partonic momentum loss we employ the on-shell transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach for Multi-Parton Scattering) for full heavy-ion collisions, which numerically solves the 3+1D Boltzmann equation based on 2->2 as well as inelastic 2<->3 scattering processes, together with PYTHIA initial state conditions for the parton showers. Due to the employed test-particle approach jet reconstruction within BAMPS events is not trivial. We introduce a method that nevertheless allows the microscopic simulation of the full evolution of the shower particles, recoiled medium particles, and the underlying bulk medium in one common microscopic framework. With this method it is possible to investigate the role of the medium recoil for the momentum imbalance A_J while using well-established background subtraction algorithms. Due to the available particle information in configuration as well as momentum space within BAMPS, it is additionally possible to reproduce the entire evolution of the reconstructed jets within the medium. With this information we investigate the sensitivity of the jet momentum loss from the difference in the partonic in-medium path lengths.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the momentum imbalance of reconstructed di-jets in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies can be reproduced by the BAMPS transport model using only elastic and inelastic pQCD scattering processes without considering the further evolution of recoiled medium partons, but the authors qualify that including such recoil evolution shifts the imbalance distribution toward lower values, meaning that background subtraction procedures are essential for meaningful comparison with experimental data.

## Method

The authors employ the on-shell partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach for Multi-Parton Scattering), which solves the 3+1D relativistic Boltzmann equation using pQCD-based 2→2 and 2↔3 scattering processes with an improved Gunion-Bertsch matrix element and a running coupling. Parton showers are generated using PYTHIA 6.4.25 with initial state radiation and primordial kt switched off, then embedded into offline-recorded BAMPS heavy-ion collision events at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions with impact parameter b_mean=3.4 fm. Jet reconstruction is performed using the anti-kt algorithm with distance parameter R=0.3, and detector effects are modeled via Gaussian smearing of jet momenta with a width parameter c=2.3 determined by fitting to CMS pp data.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC
- Impact parameter: b_mean=3.4 fm, corresponding to 0-10% centrality class
- Leading jet trigger condition: p_t;leading > 120 GeV
- Subleading jet trigger condition: p_t;subleading > 30 GeV
- Jet rapidity requirement: |y_Trigger;Jets| < 2.0
- Azimuthal angle difference requirement: Delta_phi_Trigger;Jets > 2pi/3
- Jet reconstruction distance parameter: R=0.3
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon=0.6 GeV per cubic fm, corresponding to T_c=175 MeV
- LPM parameter: X_LPM=0.3, fixed based on R_AA of neutral pions at RHIC
- Smearing parameter: c=2.3, determined by fitting PYTHIA to CMS pp data at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV
- Minimum transverse momentum for initial hard process: hat_p_t > 100 GeV
- PYTHIA hadronic mass scale: Q_0=1 GeV
- Number of smearing repetitions per event: N_smeared=1000

## Context

This work extends the BAMPS framework previously used for R_AA and elliptic flow studies (Uphoff, 2014) to the analysis of reconstructed jet momentum imbalance, complementing analytic pQCD calculations (Qin, 2010; Casalderrey-Solana, 2011; He, 2011) and other Monte-Carlo event generators that rely on hydrodynamic backgrounds (Young, 2011; Renk, 2012; Zapp, 2013). The key advantage over these approaches is that BAMPS treats both the shower particles and the bulk medium microscopically within the same framework, enabling investigation of medium recoil effects that are typically neglected or approximated in models using macroscopic hydrodynamic backgrounds. This enables future studies of how recoiled medium particles that undergo further scatterings can contribute to reconstructed jets and thereby affect the interpretation of jet energy loss measurements.
