# Light-cone sum rule approach for Baryon form factors

> We present the state-of-the-art of the light-cone sum rule approach to Baryon form factors. The essence of this approach is that soft Feynman contributions are calculated in terms of small transverse distance quantities using dispersion relations and duality. The form factors are thus expressed in terms of nucleon wave functions at small transverse separations, called distribution amplitudes, without any additional parameters. The distribution amplitudes, therefore, can be extracted from the comparison with the experimental data on form factors and compared to the results of lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The light-cone sum rule (LCSR) approach provides the most direct connection between baryon form factors and distribution amplitudes (DAs) without additional parameters, but the asymptotic regime where perturbative QCD descriptions are correct is postponed to very high \(Q^2\) far beyond current experiments, so the method relies on a light-cone expansion in baryon DAs of increasing twist using dispersion relations and duality.

## Method

The correlation function of an electromagnetic current and an interpolating nucleon operator is expanded around the light-cone using the operator product expansion, with matrix elements expressed in terms of baryon distribution amplitudes of increasing twist. This representation is matched via dispersion relations and quark-hadron duality to a hadronic representation that separates the nucleon or \(N^*\) contribution from higher states, and a Borel transformation suppresses higher-state contributions while introducing an artificial Borel parameter \(M^2\). Next-to-leading order corrections for twist 3 and 4, off-light-cone corrections, and Wandzura-Wilczek contributions to higher twist DAs were included to enable quantitative extraction of DA shape parameters from experimental form factor data.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic form factor \(G_M^p(Q^2)\) and ratio \(G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2)\) were fitted in the interval \(1 < Q^2 < 8.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- Neutron magnetic form factor \(G_M^n(Q^2)\) comes out about 20 to 30% too low compared to data.
- For the \(N^*(1535)\), LCSRs become unstable for \(Q^2 < 2\) GeV\(^2\); fits were performed for \(Q^2 \ge 1.7\) GeV\(^2\).
- The nucleon DA normalization \(f_N/\lambda_1 = -0.17\) was fixed before fitting (ABO1 and ABO2 models), a factor of about two larger than the lattice result of \(-0.083(6)\) and \(-0.075(5)\).
- Leading twist DA shape parameters for the nucleon: \(\varphi_{10} = 0.05\), \(\varphi_{11} = 0.05\) (fixed); second-order parameters \(\varphi_{20} = 0.075(15)\), \(\varphi_{21} = -0.027(38)\), \(\varphi_{22} = 0.17(15)\) for ABO1 at scale \(\mu^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the \(N^*\), the ratio \(\lambda_1^{N^*}/\lambda_1^N = 0.633\) and \(f_{N^*}/\lambda_1^{N^*} = 0.027\) were fixed to lattice values.
- Power corrections to meson transition form factors can reach \(\sim 20\%\) at large \(Q^2 \sim 40\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the LCSR approach previously applied to meson decays (Braun, 1999) to baryon form factors, building on the framework for nucleon and \(N^*\) form factors developed by Braun et al. (2001, 2006). It provides the first quantitative extraction of nucleon and \(N^*\) distribution amplitudes from experimental data, with results consistent with lattice QCD simulations (Braun, 2008; lattice 2013; Braun, 2014). The extracted DAs show a first hint of diquark symmetry from a QCD calculation, though broken by renormalization scale effects. Future work includes axial form factors, \(\Lambda_{c(b)} \to N^*\) transitions (Emmerich, 2016), extension to the Roper resonance, and calculation of \(m_{N(N^*)}^2\) corrections at NLO to improve the Pauli-like form factors \(F_2\) and \(G_2\).
