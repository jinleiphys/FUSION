# Study of the multiplicity of hadrons on nuclei

> The improved two-scale model with symmetric Lund distribution function is used to perform the fit to the semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data on nuclear targets of HERMES experiment at DESY. The ratio of hadron multiplicity on nuclear target to the deuterium one is chosen as observable, as usually. Some peculiarities of the fit are discussed. The two-parameter's fit gives satisfactory agreement with the data. The results of the present and previous fits are compared.

## Key claim

The improved two-scale model (ITSM) with the symmetric Lund distribution function, when fitted to HERMES semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data on nuclear targets, yields results for the fitted parameters and chi-squared values that are close to those obtained with the standard Lund distribution function, provided the sum over hadron ranks is truncated at \(N_{had} \ge 5\).

## Method

The author performed a two-parameter fit of the improved two-scale model (ITSM) to the ratio of hadron multiplicity on nuclear targets (helium, neon, krypton, xenon) to that on deuterium, using 332 experimental points from the HERMES experiment. The symmetric Lund scaling function was used to construct the fragmentation functions, and the distribution of constituent formation lengths was calculated via a recursion equation truncated to a finite sum over hadron ranks (up to \(N_{had}=10\)). The fit tuned the initial string-nucleon cross section \(\sigma_q\) and a coefficient \(c\), using three different nuclear density functions (NDF) and four versions of the string-nucleon cross section \(\sigma^{str}\) from prior work.

## Key numbers

- Minimum reduced chi-squared \(\hat{\chi}^2 = 0.54\) was obtained for \(\sigma^{str}(10)\) and NDF 2, with fitted parameters \(\sigma_q = 2.34 \pm 0.14\) mb and \(c = 0.088 \pm 0.01\).
- For \(\sigma^{str}(8)\) and NDF 2, the fit gave \(\sigma_q = 1.88 \pm 0.15\) mb, \(c = 0.163 \pm 0.011\), and \(\hat{\chi}^2 = 0.71\).
- For \(\sigma^{str}(9)\) and NDF 2, the fit gave \(\sigma_q = 0.00 \pm 0.02\) mb, \(c = 0.088 \pm 0.009\), and \(\hat{\chi}^2 = 0.66\).
- For \(\sigma^{str}(7)\) and NDF 2, the fit gave \(\sigma_q = 0.00 \pm 0.01\) mb, \(c = 0.336 \pm 0.011\), and \(\hat{\chi}^2 = 1.32\).
- The fit results stabilized for sums over hadron ranks with \(N_{had} \ge 5\); the final fit used \(N_{had} = 10\).

## Context

This work extends the previous fit by Grigoryan and collaborators (Akopov et al., 2010) using the improved two-scale model (ITSM) to HERMES SIDIS data, but now employing the symmetric Lund distribution function, which could not be included in the earlier fit due to the impossibility of analytic summation over hadron ranks. The results are compared directly with those from the standard Lund distribution function and the leading hadron approximation from that prior work. The study enables further investigation of quark propagation and hadronization in nuclear matter using the ITSM framework, and shows that the leading hadron approximation gives accidentally good agreement that worsens when more realistic hadron multiplicities are included.
