# Recalibration of the binding energy of hypernuclei measured in emulsion experiments and its implications

> The $Λ$ separation energy for $Λ$-hypernuclei, denoted $B_Λ$, measured in 1967, 1968, and 1973 are recalibrated using the current best mass estimates for particles and nuclei. The recalibrated $B_Λ$ are systematically larger (except in the case of $^6_Λ$He) than the original published values by about 100 keV. The effect of this level of recalibration is very important for light hypernuclei, especially for the hypertriton. The early $B_Λ$ values measured in 1967, 1968, and 1973 are widely used in theoretical research, and the new results provide better constraints on the conclusions from such studies.

# Recalibration of the binding energy of hypernuclei measured in emulsion experiments and its implications

## Key claim
The Λ separation energy BΛ for light hypernuclei measured in emulsion experiments from 1967, 1968, and 1973 are systematically too low by about 100 keV when recalculated with modern particle and nuclear masses, except for 6ΛHe where the correction goes in the opposite direction. The recalibration is most consequential for the hypertriton (3ΛH), whose corrected BΛ of 0.27 ± 0.08 MeV is substantially larger than the widely-cited 1973 value of 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV and approaches the 2019 STAR measurement of 0.41 ± 0.12 ± 0.11 MeV.

## Method
The authors recalculated the Q0 values (the mass difference between the hypernuclear core plus Λ hyperon and the sum of decay daughter masses) for each hypernucleus and decay channel using 2019 masses from the Particle Data Group and the Atomic Mass Data Center, then compared these to the Q0 values that would have been used in the original 1967, 1968, and 1973 publications based on the mass tables available at those times. The difference ΔQ0 between modern and historical Q0 values was applied as a correction to the originally published BΛ values for Λ-hypernuclei with mass numbers A = 3–15, without reweighting or averaging the multiple decay channels due to unresolved systematic uncertainties in the emulsion technique.

## Key numbers
- Recalibrated 3ΛH BΛ = 0.27 ± 0.08 (stat.) MeV, compared to the original 1973 value of 0.13 ± 0.05 (stat.) MeV
- Recalibrated BΛ values are systematically larger than original values by about 100 keV, except for 6ΛHe
- The systematic correction of about 100 keV arises mainly from the difference between modern and historical Λ hyperon mass values
- For 4ΛH, the recalibrated values are closer to the A1 collaboration (2016) measurement of 2.157 ± 0.005 (stat.) ± 0.077 (syst.) MeV than the original 1973 values
- For 7ΛHe, recalibrated values approach the HKS collaboration (2016) measurement of 5.55 ± 0.10 (stat.) ± 0.11 (syst.) MeV
- For 7ΛLi, recalibrated values approach the FINUDA collaboration (2009) measurement of 5.85 ± 0.13 (stat.) ± 0.11 (syst.) MeV
- The difference in BΛ between 1968 and 1973 measurements for the same hypernuclei can be as large as 0.50 ± 0.17 (stat.) MeV, exceeding the claimed systematic error of 0.04 MeV

## Context
This work extends the legacy of emulsion-based hypernuclear binding energy measurements from the 1960s and 1970s (Bohm et al. 1966, 1967, 1968, 1973) by correcting them for the improved mass values now available from the PDG and AMDC. The recalibration is motivated by the 2019 STAR collaboration measurement of the hypertriton BΛ, which was significantly larger than the 1973 value that had been used as a standard input in theoretical studies. The corrected values provide better constraints for chiral effective field theory calculations addressing the overbinding problem of 5ΛHe (as in the work of Contessi et al. 2018), for models of hyperon-nucleon three-body forces affecting neutron star maximum mass predictions (as in the work of Lonardoni et al. 2015), and for understanding the shortened lifetime of 3ΛH relative to the free Λ. The authors note that further progress requires new high-precision measurements from facilities such as JLab, MAMI, FAIR, J-PARC, and the upcoming HIAF in China.
