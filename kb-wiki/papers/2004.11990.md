# Threshold Resummation for Hadron Production in the Small-$x$ Region

> We study the single hadron inclusive production in the forward rapidity region in proton-nucleus collisions. We find the long-standing negative cross section at next-to-leading-order (NLO) is driven by the large negative threshold logarithmic contributions. We established a factorization theorem for resumming these logarithms with systematically improvable accuracy within the color glass condensate formalism. We demonstrate how the threshold leading logarithmic accuracy can be realized by a suitable scale choice in the NLO results. The NLO spectrums with the threshold logarithms resummed remain positive and impressive agreements with experimental data are observed.

## Key claim

The long-standing negative cross section at next-to-leading-order (NLO) for single hadron production in forward proton-nucleus collisions is driven by large negative threshold logarithmic contributions, and resumming these logarithms at leading logarithmic accuracy (LL_thr.) keeps the NLO predictions positive and yields impressive agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors derived an all-order factorization theorem within the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism using soft-collinear-effective theory (SCET) with additional interactions between quarks/gluons and the CGC Wilson line. They identified the threshold logarithms in the partonic cross section, which become large when the hadron transverse momentum increases in the forward rapidity region, and resummed them at leading logarithmic accuracy. The threshold resummation was realized by a suitable dynamical choice of the rapidity scale X_f that minimizes the exponent in the evolution kernel, avoiding the need for explicit evolution while maintaining all-order resummation.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC d+Au collisions with sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and y_h = 2.2, the NLO cross section becomes negative for p_h,perp > 5 GeV, where threshold logarithms become overwhelmingly dominant and the ratio R between NLO threshold contribution and full NLO result approaches one.
- The threshold logarithm contribution is enormous, exceeding 100% of the LO magnitude, requiring resummation.
- The NLO+LL_thr. predictions remain positive for the entire p_h,perp spectrum at both RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, y_h = 2.2 and 3.2) and LHC (p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, 1.5 < y_h < 1.8), with uncertainty bands obtained by varying X_f by a factor of 2 around its central value.

## Context

This work resolves the long-standing negative cross section problem in NLO calculations for pA -> hX within the CGC formalism, first identified by Chirilli (2011) and studied extensively by Stasto (2013), Kang (2014), Altinoluk (2014), Watanabe (2015), Ducloue (2016), Iancu (2016), Xiao (2018), and Liu (2019). The approach extends the threshold resummation techniques developed by Sterman (1986) and Becher (2007) to the small-x CGC framework, and is analogous to the resummation of collinear logarithms in the NLO BK equation that solved instability problems (Beuf 2014, Iancu 2015, Lappi 2016, Ducloue 2019). The results enable reliable phenomenological applications at RHIC and LHC, including global fitting studies of CGC models beyond LO, and are expected to be applicable to other small-x processes at the future Electron Ion Collider.
