# Thermalization in Weakly Coupled Nonabelian Plasmas

> We investigate how relativistic, nonabelian plasmas approach equilibrium in a general context. Our treatment is entirely parametric and for small Yang-Mills coupling $α$. First we study isotropic systems with an initially nonequilibrium momentum distribution. We consider both the case of initially very high occupancy and initially very low occupancy. Then we consider systems which are anisotropic. We consider both weak anisotropy and large anisotropy, and allow the occupancy to be parametrically large or small. Writing the typical momentum of an initial excitation as Q and the final temperature as T, full equilibration occurs in a time t ~ α^{-2}/T for T > Q, and t ~ α^{-2} Q^{1/2} T^{-3/2} for T < Q, unless the initial system is sufficiently anisotropic and T > α^{2/3} Q, in which case equilibration occurs somewhat faster, t ~ α^{-13/7} Q^{5/7} T^{-12/7} (or α^{-2}/T if that is longer).

# Thermalization in Weakly Coupled Nonabelian Plasmas

## Key claim

Full equilibration of a weakly coupled nonabelian plasma occurs parametrically on a timescale that depends on the ratio of the initial typical momentum Q to the final temperature T, and on the degree of initial anisotropy; for isotropic underoccupied systems the equilibration time is longer than the characteristic thermal equilibration time by a factor of α^{c/8}, where c < 0 parameterizes the initial occupancy as f ∼ α^{-c}.

## Method

The authors perform a parametric power-counting analysis of the Boltzmann equation for gauge bosons, identifying the dominant physical processes (elastic scattering, inelastic splitting/joining, and plasma instabilities) at each stage of evolution. They classify initial conditions by two parameters: occupancy c (where f ∼ α^{-c}) and anisotropy d (where the angular spread or variation is α^{-d}), then determine which processes control the dynamics and estimate the timescales for the system to evolve toward thermal equilibrium. For isotropic systems they track the evolution of the momentum distribution's cutoff scale and effective temperature; for anisotropic systems they identify regions in the (c,d) plane where different physics dominates, including Nielsen-Olesen instabilities, plasma instabilities, and LPM-suppressed bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- For isotropic overoccupied systems (0 < c < 1): equilibration time t_eq ∼ α^{-2 + c/4} Q^{-1} ∼ α^{-2} T^{-1}, where T ∼ α^{-c/4} Q is the final temperature.
- For isotropic underoccupied systems (c < 0): equilibration time t_eq ∼ α^{-2 + 3c/8} Q^{-1}, which is longer than the thermal equilibration time by α^{c/8}.
- For isotropic systems with extreme overoccupancy (c > 1): the Nielsen-Olesen instability converts the system to a new state with Q_new ∼ α^{(1-c)/4} Q and f_new ∼ α^{-1} (c = 1) on a timescale t ∼ α^{(c-1)/4} Q^{-1} (up to logarithms).
- For anisotropic systems with T > α^{2/3} Q: equilibration time t_eq ∼ max(α^{-2} T^{-1}, α^{-13/7} Q^{5/7} T^{-12/7}).
- For anisotropic systems with T < α^{2/3} Q: equilibration time t_eq ∼ α^{-2} Q^{1/2} T^{-3/2}.
- The momentum diffusion coefficient for the initial overoccupied distribution: q̂_elast ∼ α^{2-2c} Q^3.
- The large-angle elastic scattering time for the initial overoccupied distribution: t_large angle ∼ α^{-2+2c} Q^{-1}.

## Context

This work provides a comprehensive parametric classification of thermalization in weakly coupled nonabelian plasmas, extending prior studies that focused on specific regimes. For isotropic underoccupied systems it builds on the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier et al. (2001), while for anisotropic systems it incorporates plasma instabilities first studied by Weibel (1959) and Mrówczyński (1993). The results enable parametric estimates for early-universe cosmology scenarios (reheating after inflation, electroweak baryogenesis) and for the early stages of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the authors note they will apply these results to Bjorken-expanding systems in a future publication.
