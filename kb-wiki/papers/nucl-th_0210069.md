# np-nh bands in the N=28 isotones

> The existence of n-particle n-hole deformed yrare bands in the N=28 isotones is explored using full pf-shell diagonalizations and the Lanczos Strength Function method. We find different 2p-2h and 4p-4h collective bands that, when allowed to mix, more often disappear. Only the 2p-2h yrare band in Cr-52 and the 4p-4h yrare band in Ni-56 survive, and only in this latter case, due to the reduced density of 2p-2h states, can the band be seen as a gamma-cascade.

## Key claim

The authors find that when full configuration mixing is allowed, most 2p-2h and 4p-4h collective bands in N=28 isotones disappear; only the 2p-2h yrare band in Cr-52 and the 4p-4h yrare band in Ni-56 survive, and only in the Ni-56 case can the band be observed as a gamma-cascade due to the reduced density of 2p-2h states.

## Method

The authors performed full pf-shell diagonalizations using the KB3G interaction, employing the Lanczos algorithm via the codes ANTOINE (m-scheme) and NATHAN (J-coupled scheme). They used a three-step procedure: first, diagonalizations restricted to configurations with fixed numbers of particle-hole excitations (2p-2h and 4p-4h) to search for collective bands; second, the Lanczos Strength Function method to immerse the bandhead state in the full space and test whether it survives mixing; third, for surviving bands, repeated application of the quadrupole operator with the Lanczos Strength Function method to build the physical band.

## Key numbers

- In Cr-52, the 2p-2h bandhead at 2.43 MeV (theoretical) has 46% 2p-2h content, 26% 3p-3h, and 18% 4p-4h; the experimental 0+_2 is at 2.65 MeV.
- In Cr-52, the 2p-2h yrare band shows B(E2) values up to 331 e^2 fm^4 (J=8 to J=6 transition), with spectroscopic quadrupole moments of -39.2 e fm^2 for J=4,6,8.
- In Cr-52, the energy ratios approximate a rigid rotor: Delta E(4+)/Delta E(2+) = 3 (rigid rotor 3.33), Delta E(6+)/Delta E(4+) = 1.9 (2.1), Delta E(8+)/Delta E(6+) = 1.65 (1.71).
- In Fe-54, the 2p-2h bandhead represents 43% of the 0+_2 at 2.77 MeV (theoretical), with experimental 0+_2 at 2.56 MeV.
- In Ni-56, the 4p-4h band shows B(E2) values of 439 e^2 fm^4 (J=4 to J=2) and 467 e^2 fm^4 (J=6 to J=4).
- In Ti-50, the 2p-2h bandhead survives at 61% in the full space 0+_2 at 4.08 MeV (theoretical), with experimental at 3.87 MeV.
- The full pf-shell m-scheme dimension for Cr-52 calculations was 45 million; for Fe-54 at t=8 truncation, the m-scheme dimension was 177 million.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical search for deformed yrare bands in N=28 isotones previously carried out by Mizusaki and co-workers using the Monte Carlo shell model method (Mizusaki, 2001). The authors find that the 4p-4h band in Fe-54 claimed by Mizusaki based on variation-after-projection cranked Hartree-Fock calculations does not survive full mixing, casting doubt on that result. The study enables future experimental searches by identifying that only the 2p-2h band in Cr-52 and the 4p-4h band in Ni-56 are physically meaningful, though the Cr-52 band likely decays via M1 transitions to other 2p-2h states rather than appearing as a gamma-cascade.
