# New Relations for Coefficients of Fractional Parentage--the Redmond Recursion Formula with Seniority

> We find a relationship between coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp) obtained on the one hand from the principal parent method and on the other hand from a seniority classification. We apply this to the Redmond recursion formula which relates $n \to n+1$ cfp's to $n-1 \to n$ cfp's where the principal parent classification is used. We transform this to the seniority scheme. Our formula differs from the Redmond formula inasmuch as we have a sum over the possible seniorities for the $n \to n+1$ cfp's, whereas Redmond has only one term.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new version of the Redmond recursion formula for coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp) that uses the seniority classification scheme instead of the principal-parent scheme; their formula includes a sum over all possible seniorities of the (n+1)-particle system on the left-hand side, whereas the original Redmond formula has only one term.

## Method

The authors start from the principal-parent Redmond recursion formula, which relates n → n+1 cfp's to n-1 → n cfp's, and transform it into the seniority scheme by using a relation that connects the overcomplete set of principal-parent cfp's to the complete orthonormal set of seniority-classified cfp's. They verify the new relation numerically for specific cases, such as j=9/2 and j=15/2 three-particle systems, and apply it to derive formulas for the number of states of given angular momentum in a (j^3) configuration and to generalize earlier results on isospin and angular momentum for mixed neutron-proton systems.

## Key numbers

* For j=15/2, J=15/2, J0=2, J1=2: the squared principal-parent cfp equals 0.153945; the sum of squares of seniority cfp's (v=1: 0.172516, v=3 alpha=1: 0.153452, v=3 alpha=2: 0.317231) gives 0.153945, verifying the relation.
* For j=9/2, J=9/2, J0=2, J1=4: the left-hand side of the relation equals -0.00677596; the right-hand side sum (v=1: 0.288675 × 0.387598, v=3: -0.181166 × 0.654463) gives -0.006776.
* For n=3, j=9/2, J0=9/2 v0=3, J1=11/2 v1=3, Is=2: the left-hand side sum over vs (vs=2: -0.164495, vs=4: -0.708592) equals -0.873087, matching the right-hand side sum over J2 (J2=2: -0.119633, J2=4: -0.789733, J2=6: +0.199694, J2=8: -0.163415) which also equals -0.873087.
* For the same case with v0=1 and vs=3: lhs = rhs = 0.78674.
* The number of states with Is=j for three identical particles in a single j shell is given by [(2j+3)/6] (largest integer less than that value), and the number with Is=j+1 is [j/3].

## Context

This work extends the original Redmond recursion formula (Redmond 1954) from the principal-parent classification to the seniority classification introduced by Racah (1943, 1949). The new relation enables calculations for systems where states of a given angular momentum occur more than once, which was previously limited to j ≤ 7/2 in earlier work by Zamick, Mekjian, and Lee (2004). The results connect to the Bayman-Lande scheme (1966) for seniority cfp's and to formulas for the number of states derived by Rosensteel and Rowe (2003) using quasispin, by Ginocchio and Haxton (1993), and by Zhao and Arima (2004). The work enables generalization of isospin-angular momentum relations to higher j values and provides conditions for orthogonality of states with different isospin in mixed neutron-proton systems.
