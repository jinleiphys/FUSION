# Elastic energy loss with respect to the reaction plane in a Monte-Carlo model

> We present a computation of $π^0$ nuclear modification factor with respect to the reaction plane in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, based on a Monte-Carlo model of elastic energy loss of hard partons traversing the bulk hydrodynamical medium created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find the incoherent nature of elastic energy loss incompatible with the measured data.

# Elastic energy loss with respect to the reaction plane in a Monte-Carlo model

## Key claim

The incoherent nature of elastic energy loss, as implemented in a microscopical Monte-Carlo model with a realistic hydrodynamic background, is incompatible with the measured PHENIX data for the π⁰ nuclear modification factor as a function of reaction plane angle in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The authors find that a purely elastic energy loss framework cannot reproduce the observed pathlength dependence, confirming that any contribution of elastic energy loss must be rather small (on the order of 10% or less of the total energy loss).

## Method

The authors model elastic energy loss of hard partons via incoherent 2→2 pQCD processes, with scattering partners sampled from a thermal medium described by (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics using the SHASTA algorithm and an equation of state from Laine and Schröder (2006). Hard partons are initiated from LO pQCD single-jet production spectra using CTEQ6L1 parton distribution functions, propagated through the medium in small time steps with scattering rates computed in the local fluid rest frame, and the highest-energy final-state parton is followed after each collision. The resulting medium-modified parton distributions are hadronized into π⁰ using Kniehl-Kramer-Pötter fragmentation functions to compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA(φ) in six bins of angle relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- Collision system: Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Strong coupling constant α_s = 0.5 and mass parameter s_m = 1.0 were used to tune the model
- Initial time τ₀ = 0.17 fm for the hydrodynamic evolution
- Freeze-out temperature T_dec = 160 MeV, chosen to reproduce measured pion p_T spectra up to p_T ≈ 2 GeV
- Minimum transverse momentum for neutral pions analyzed: P_T_min ≥ 5.0 GeV
- Rapidity window for averaging: [-0.35, 0.35], corresponding to PHENIX acceptance
- Initial rapidity range for hard partons: y_min = -1 to y_max = 1
- Simulation ends at T = 165 MeV, when the system is considered fully transformed into hadron gas
- Effective temperature formula uses quark-gluon plasma degrees of freedom g_Q = 95/2 (gluon DOF g_g = 16, quark DOF g_q = 6, N_f = 3)

## Context

This work extends the Monte-Carlo model of elastic energy loss presented in Auvinen (2009) and directly tests the phenomenological finding of the ElasticPhenomenology study (2009) that elastic energy loss contributions above 10% are ruled out by pathlength-dependent observables. The results contradict microscopic calculations by Ruppert (2005) and the MARTINI collaboration that found large elastic contributions under the assumption of perturbatively interacting partonic degrees of freedom. The finding that an ideal fluid description (zero mean free path) works for the bulk medium while an infinite mean free path (ideal parton gas) assumption fails for hard parton interactions suggests the medium degrees of freedom are not well described as quasi-free partons. The authors note that the implications for heavy-quark energy loss remain unclear because elastic energy loss is generically more efficient for heavy quarks than light quarks, and they plan to investigate this in future work.
