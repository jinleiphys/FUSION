# Algebraic Approach to Baryon Structure

> We present an algebraic approach to the internal structure of baryons in terms of three constituents. We investigate a collective model in which the nucleon is regarded as a rotating and vibrating oblate top with a prescribed distribution of charges and magnetization. We contrast the collective and single-particle descriptions of baryons and compare the predictions of the model with existing data on masses, electromagnetic elastic and transition form factors and strong decays widths.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algebraic framework based on a U(7) spectrum generating algebra that encompasses both single-particle and collective dynamics for baryon structure, but the abstract's claim of comparing predictions with data is hedged: the collective model's mass spectrum fit (r.m.s. deviation of 39 MeV) is comparable to quark potential models, yet masses alone cannot distinguish between single-particle and collective dynamics; distinguishing them requires examining electromagnetic and strong couplings.

## Method

The authors construct a U(7) spectrum generating algebra for the spatial dynamics of three constituents, combined with SU_sf(6) ⊗ SU_c(3) spin-flavor-color structure, to describe baryon wave functions. For the collective model, nonstrange baryons are treated as rotations and vibrations of an oblate-top string configuration, with a prescribed charge and magnetization distribution along the string. The transition operators for electromagnetic and strong processes are mapped onto the U(7) algebra, and their matrix elements are evaluated in the collective wave functions, then folded with a probability distribution g(β) = β² e^{-β/a} / 2a³ to obtain form factors.

## Key numbers

- Mass spectrum fit for 3* and 4* nucleon and delta resonances yields an r.m.s. deviation of 39 MeV.
- For SU_sf(6) symmetric case: scale parameter a = 0.232 fm; constituent mass m_u = m_d = 0.336 GeV; μ = μ_p = 2.793 μ_N (= 0.126 GeV⁻¹).
- For SU_sf(6) broken case: a_u = 0.230 fm, a_d = 0.257 fm (Δ = 0.2); m_u = 0.338 GeV, m_d = 0.322 GeV; μ_u = 2.777 μ_N (= 0.126 GeV⁻¹), μ_d = 2.915 μ_N (= 0.133 GeV⁻¹).
- Strong decay fit parameters: g = 1.164 GeV⁻¹, h = -0.094 GeV⁻¹; scale parameter a = 0.232 fm (same as from electromagnetic couplings).
- Proton charge radius: ⟨r²⟩_E^p = 12a².
- Elastic form factors follow dipole form: G_E^p = 1/(1 + k²a²)²; G_M^p/μ_p = G_M^n/μ_n = 1/(1 + k²a²)².
- Magnetic moments under spin-flavor symmetry: μ_p = μ, μ_n = -2μ/3.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to baryon structure previously developed by the authors (Leviatan and Bijker, 1996) and collaborators (Iachello), contrasting the collective oblate-top model with the nonrelativistic quark model (NRQM) and relativized quark model (RQM). The paper enables future experimental tests at CEBAF and MAMI of the predicted stretching effect on helicity amplitudes and the unusual η decay widths, particularly for the N(1535)S₁₁ resonance, which may indicate configurations beyond the q³ model space.
