# Infrared length scale and extrapolations for the no-core shell model

> We precisely determine the infrared (IR) length scale of the no-core shell model (NCSM). In the NCSM, the $A$-body Hilbert space is truncated by the total energy, and the IR length can be determined by equating the intrinsic kinetic energy of $A$ nucleons in the NCSM space to that of $A$ nucleons in a $3(A-1)$-dimensional hyper-radial well with a Dirichlet boundary condition for the hyper radius. We demonstrate that this procedure indeed yields a very precise IR length by performing large-scale NCSM calculations for $^{6}$Li. We apply our result and perform accurate IR extrapolations for bound states of $^{4}$He, $^{6}$He, $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li. We also attempt to extrapolate NCSM results for $^{10}$B and $^{16}$O with bare interactions from chiral effective field theory over tens of MeV.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model (NCSM) truncation of the A-body Hilbert space by total energy is equivalent to confining A nucleons in a 3(A-1)-dimensional hyper-radial well with a Dirichlet boundary condition, and equating the intrinsic kinetic energies yields a precise infrared (IR) length scale L_eff that is more accurate than the previously used single-particle IR length L_2 for extrapolating bound-state energies.

## Method

The authors computed the lowest eigenvalue of the intrinsic kinetic energy operator in the NCSM basis by diagonalizing small matrices (dimension 5-20) in a hyperspherical harmonic basis, exploiting the block-diagonal structure in total oscillator quanta. They equated this eigenvalue to the lowest eigenvalue of the kinetic energy in a 3(A-1)-dimensional hyper-radial infinite well with Dirichlet boundary conditions, yielding the IR length L_eff = b * X_{1,scL} / sqrt(T_{1,scL}(N_max)), where X_{1,scL} is the first zero of a Bessel function and T_{1,scL} is the dimensionless NCSM kinetic energy eigenvalue. They validated this IR scale by performing large-scale NCSM calculations for 6Li and then applied exponential extrapolations E(L) = E_inf + a exp(-2 kappa_inf L) to ground-state energies of several p-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 4He(0+): extrapolated energy E_inf = -27.59 MeV from model spaces with N_max_tot <= 10, compared to benchmark E_ref = -27.76 MeV and variational minimum E_var_min = -27.51 MeV; kappa_inf = 0.87 fm^-1
- For 6He(0+): E_inf = -26.92 MeV from N_max_tot <= 14, compared to E_ref = -27.13 MeV and E_var_min = -26.12 MeV; kappa_inf = 0.49 fm^-1
- For 6Li(1+): E_inf = -30.17 MeV from N_max_tot <= 14, compared to E_ref = -30.27 MeV and E_var_min = -29.18 MeV; kappa_inf = 0.49 fm^-1
- For 7Li(3/2-): E_inf = -37.14 MeV from N_max_tot <= 17, compared to E_var_min = -36.11 MeV; kappa_inf = 0.50 fm^-1
- For 10B(3+): E_inf = -56.29 MeV from N_max_tot <= 16, compared to E_var_min = -54.24 MeV; kappa_inf = 0.50 fm^-1
- For 16O(0+): E_inf = -116.75 MeV from N_max_tot <= 20, compared to coupled-cluster benchmark E_ref = -130.1 MeV and E_var_min = -109.77 MeV; kappa_inf = 0.47 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the IR extrapolation framework developed by Coon et al. (2012) and Furnstahl et al. (2012, 2014) for harmonic oscillator basis truncations, and the precise IR length determination by More et al. (2013) for single-particle systems and by Furnstahl et al. (2014b) for Cartesian product spaces. The key advance is deriving the correct IR length for the NCSM's total-energy truncation, which differs from the single-particle L_2 used in prior NCSM extrapolations by Soma et al. (2013), Jurgenson et al. (2013), Saaef et al. (2013), and Roth et al. (2013). The results enable more reliable extrapolations of NCSM ground-state energies for nuclei up to 16O, though further progress requires better understanding of combined UV and IR correction terms.
