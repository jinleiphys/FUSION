# beta-decay in neutron-deficient Hg, Pb, and Po isotopes

> The effect of nuclear deformation on the energy distributions of the Gamow-Teller strength is studied in neutron-deficient Hg, Pb, and Po even isotopes. The theoretical framework is based on a self-consistent deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing correlations between like nucleons in BCS approximation and residual spin-isospin interactions treated in the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation. After a systematic study of the Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the low excitation energy region, relevant for beta-decay, we have identified the best candidates to look for deformation signatures in their beta-decay patterns. beta+ half-lives and total Gamow-Teller strengths B(GT+) and B(GT-) are analyzed as well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Gamow-Tower strength distributions in neutron-deficient Hg, Pb, and Po isotopes exhibit distinct, shape-dependent patterns that can serve as deformation signatures, but the relative energies of the coexisting shape minima are highly sensitive to the choice of Skyrme force and pairing treatment, meaning the predicted ground-state shape may be altered by these choices.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy4 force, including BCS pairing correlations with fixed gap parameters from experimental masses, and then applied proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) with separable spin-isospin residual interactions. The particle-hole coupling strength was fixed to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance in 208Pb, and the particle-particle strength was taken from global parametrizations. The Gamow-Teller strength distributions were calculated at the equilibrium deformations for each isotope, and half-lives were computed using experimental QEC values with quenching factors.

## Key numbers

- The particle-hole coupling strength used was chi_ph(GT) = 0.08 MeV, fixed by comparison to the experimental GT resonance in 208Pb at 19.2 MeV.
- The particle-particle coupling strength used was kappa_pp(GT) = 0.02 MeV.
- The quenching factor applied was A^2 = [0.77 (g_A/g_V)_free]^2.
- For Hg isotopes, half-lives agreed with experiment within a factor of 2, except for 192Hg (QEC = 0.765 MeV) which deviated considerably.
- For Pb isotopes, half-lives agreed with experiment within a factor of 2-3.
- For Po isotopes, only 198Po and 200Po had meaningful half-life comparisons, agreeing within a factor of 2.
- The Ikeda sum rule values ranged from 3(N-Z) = 60 in 180Hg to 3(N-Z) = 114 in 206Po, fulfilled to within a few percent in all calculations.
- The maximum QEC value among the studied isotopes was QEC = 5.352 MeV in 180Hg.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on Pb isotopes (Moreno et al., 2005) using the Sk3 force to the neighboring Hg, Pb, and Po isotopic chains using the SLy4 force, which was designed for improved isospin properties away from stability. The results are consistent with earlier mean-field calculations by Bengtsson (1980s), Smirnova (2000s), and Niksic (2002) showing shape coexistence in this region, and with experimental observations of shape coexistence from Julin (2000s) and Andreyev (2000s). The paper identifies 184-188Hg, 188-192Pb, and 198,200Po as the best candidates for experimental verification of deformation signatures in beta-decay patterns at facilities such as ISOLDE/CERN and FAIR.
