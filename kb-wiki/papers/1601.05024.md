# Loosening up the Skyrme model

> We consider the Skyrme model with the addition of extra scalar potentials that decrease the classical binding energies of the Skyrmions to about the 3% level -- without altering the pion mass -- if we insist on keeping platonic symmetries that are usually possessed by Skyrmions. A side effect of the potentials under consideration is the smaller size of the 1-Skyrmion resulting in a smaller moment of inertia and in turn a larger spin contribution to the energy upon semi-classical quantization. After taking into account the quantum contributions we find total binding energies at the 6% level.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adding a scalar potential of the form \(V_2 \propto (1 - \frac{1}{2}\text{Tr}[U])^2\) to the Skyrme model can lower classical binding energies to near the 3% level while preserving the platonic symmetries of Skyrmions, but only if the coefficient \(m_2\) is kept below a threshold where the symmetries break; after including semi-classical quantization, total binding energies rise to about the 6% level.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the Skyrme model field equations on a finite-difference lattice with a fourth-order stencil, using relaxation cooling from rational map initial conditions for baryon numbers \(B = 1,2,3,4,5\). They scan a slice of parameter space spanned by the coefficients \(m_2\) and \(m_4\) of the potentials \(V_2\) and \(V_4\), with the pion mass either zero or set to the physical value \(m_1 = 1/4\) (in rescaled units). For each solution they compute the classical energy, the relative classical binding energy \(\delta_B = 1 - E_B/(B E_1)\), and the root-mean-square baryon-charge radius, then fit these to experimental nuclear binding energies and charge radii.

## Key numbers

* Classical relative binding energy for \(B=4\) reaches below 4% on the \(m_4=0\) branch (using only \(V_2\)), compared to about 6% on the \(m_2=0\) branch (using only \(V_4\)), before platonic symmetries break.
* After semi-classical quantization including spin and isospin contributions, total binding energies are at the 6% level.
* The optimal point in parameter space for preserving platonic symmetries is at \((m_2, m_4) \sim (0.7, 0)\).
* The numerically integrated baryon number for the solutions has an accuracy in the range \([0.019\%, 0.16\%]\), with an average around \(0.052\%\).
* The \(B=1\) Skyrmion energy is computed with accuracy better than \(2.7 \times 10^{-4}\).
* The pion mass is set to \(m_1 = 1/4\) in rescaled units, corresponding to \(m_\pi = 1\) in normal Skyrme units.

## Context

This work extends the "lightly bound Skyrme model" of Gillard, Harland, and Speight (2015), which used the \(V_4\) potential to produce low binding energies but at the cost of replacing platonic Skyrmion symmetries with a face-centered-cubic (FCC) lattice of spheres. The present paper shows that the \(V_2\) potential can lower binding energies further while retaining the cubic \(B=4\) Skyrmion and the alpha-particle clustering that is phenomenologically important for nuclear cluster models (Freer, 2007). The results enable future work on quantizing the zero modes of these Skyrmions and comparing rotational bands (e.g., for the Hoyle state in carbon-12, as studied by Lau and Manton, 2014) to experimental data, though the degeneracy of nearly degenerate configurations in the lightly bound regime remains an open problem.
