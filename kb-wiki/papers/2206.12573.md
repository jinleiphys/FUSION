# Shear induced polarization: Collisional contributions

> It has been realized that thermal shear plays a similar role as thermal vorticity in polarizing spin of particles in heavy ion collisions. We point out that shear has a fundamental difference that it leads to particle redistribution in the medium. The redistribution gives rise to an additional contribution to spin polarization through the self-energy, which is parametrically the same order as the one considered so far in the literature. The self-energy contribution is in general gauge dependent. We introduce double gauge links stretching along the Schwinger-Keldysh contour to restore gauge invariance. We also generalize the straight path to adapt to the Schwinger-Keldysh contour. We find another contribution associated with the gauge link, which is also parametrically the same order. We illustrate the two contributions with a massive probe fermion in massless QED plasma with shear. A modest suppression of spin polarization is found from the combined contributions when the probe fermion has momentum much greater than the temperature.

## Key claim

Shear flow in heavy ion collisions produces an additional contribution to spin polarization beyond the previously considered derivative term, arising from particle redistribution in the medium and from gauge link corrections; both contributions are parametrically the same order as the derivative term, and their combined effect yields a modest suppression of spin polarization for a massive probe fermion in massless QED plasma when the probe momentum is much greater than the temperature.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation for massless QED plasma with shear flow at leading-logarithmic order to determine the off-equilibrium redistribution of fermions and photons, parametrized by functions \(\chi(p)\) and \(\gamma(p)\). They then compute the self-energy contribution to the axial component of the Wigner function for a massive probe fermion, using Coulomb scattering as the dominant process in the heavy probe limit \(m \gg eT\). To restore gauge invariance, they introduce double gauge links along the Schwinger-Keldysh contour and evaluate the additional gauge link contribution to spin polarization, also at leading-logarithmic order.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic solution for the fermion redistribution function at large momentum is \(\chi(p \to \infty)/(2\pi)^3 = (1/(e^4 \ln e^{-1})) \cdot (3(1+2N_f)\beta^2 p^2)/(4\pi^2 N_f^2)\), where \(\beta = 1/T\) and \(N_f\) is the number of fermion flavors.
* The photon redistribution function is given by \((\gamma_p - \chi_p)/(2\pi)^3 = (1/(e^4 \ln e^{-1})) \cdot (2\beta^2)/(\pi^2 N_f) \cdot p^2 \cdot (1+\tilde{f}_p)/(1-f_p)\), where \(f_p\) and \(\tilde{f}_p\) are equilibrium fermion and photon distributions.
* The shear viscosity computed from the approximate solution reproduces results from Arnold (2003) with errors of about 1% for \(N_f=1\) and about 3% for \(N_f=2\).
* The self-energy contribution to the axial Wigner function involves integrals \(I_2\) and \(I_3\) (Eqs. labeled Is in the text) containing terms such as \(\pi^2 \cosh^{-2}(\beta p_0/2) \cdot ((15p^4-87p^2p_0^2+72p_0^4)\ln((p_0-p)/(p_0+p)) + 8p^5/p_0 - 126p^3p_0 + 144pp_0^3)/(72\beta)\).
* The gauge link contribution involves integrals \(J_1\) through \(J_4\) (Eqs. labeled Js), with \(J_1 = 8\pi\beta^2 p^3/p_0\) and \(J_2 = -8\pi\beta^2 p^5/p_0^3\).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of spin polarization in heavy ion collisions beyond the free-theory analysis of Liu (2021) and Becattini (2021), which treated thermal shear similarly to thermal vorticity. The authors show that shear fundamentally differs from vorticity because it redistributes particles in the medium, an effect captured by the collisional quantum kinetic theory framework reviewed by Hidaka (2022). The calculation uses the Boltzmann equation approach of Arnold (2000, 2002, 2003) for transport coefficients and builds on the Wigner function formalism of Yang (2020) and Lin (2021). The results enable more complete phenomenological modeling of Lambda hyperon local polarization, which currently shows a sign puzzle compared to experimental data from STAR (2019).
