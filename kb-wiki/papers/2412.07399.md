# Toward a Direct Measurement of Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry at J-PARC E16 via Density-induced Chiral Mixing

> The degeneracy of chiral partners is an ideal signal for measuring the restoration of the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry in QCD. In this work, we investigate the observability of the $ϕ$ - $f_1(1420)$ degeneracy in the J-PARC E16 experiment, which measures di-electrons emitted from 30 GeV pA collisions. We for this purpose make use of an effective Lagrangian approach, which naturally incorporates the broken charge-conjugation symmetry in nuclear matter and the ensuing anomaly-induced mixing between vector and axial-vector mesons, to compute the spectral function relevant for the experimental measurement. The real-time dynamics of the pA collision is obtained from a transport simulation. Including experimental background and resolution effects on top of that, we find that a signal of the $ϕ$ - $f_1(1420)$ mixing can be observed around 2.5 $σ$ with the Run2 statistics planned for the J-PARC E16 experiment with an ideal mixing strength.

## Key claim

The authors find that a signal of the density-induced chiral mixing between the $\phi$ and $f_1(1420)$ mesons can be observed at approximately 2.5 sigma significance with the Run2 statistics of the J-PARC E16 experiment, but only under the assumption of an ideal (large) mixing strength; for smaller, more realistic mixing strengths the signal is not statistically significant.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach incorporating anomaly-induced chiral mixing between vector and axial-vector mesons at finite baryon density, computing the in-medium spectral function of the $\phi$ meson including the modified kaon cloud and partial chiral symmetry restoration (30% at normal nuclear density). The real-time dynamics of 30 GeV pA collisions are simulated using the Parton-Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach to obtain the density and momentum distributions of decaying $\phi$ mesons, and experimental background is estimated using the JAM Monte Carlo event generator and Geant4 detector simulation, with final spectra convolved with the J-PARC E16 mass resolution of 8 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The mixing strength parameter $c$ is estimated to range from 0.1 GeV (from the Wess-Zumino-Witten action) to 1.0 GeV (from holographic QCD) at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0$.
- The $\phi$ meson vacuum mass is 1.02 GeV and the $f_1(1420)$ vacuum mass is 1.42 GeV.
- The $\phi$ meson effective width in nuclear matter is approximately 40 MeV at $s=m_\phi^2$.
- The $f_1(1420)$ vacuum width is 54.9 MeV.
- The chiral mixing signal can be observed at around 2.5 sigma with Run2 statistics (69k $\phi$ meson yields with Cu targets) and an ideal mixing strength.
- The mass resolution of the J-PARC E16 spectrometer is 8 MeV for $\phi$ mesons with momenta below 1 GeV.
- The kaon and anti-kaon masses in nuclear matter at normal density are $m_K^*=510$ MeV and $m_{\bar{K}}^*=380$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of density-induced chiral mixing developed in holographic QCD (PhysRevLett.99.141602) and the Wess-Zumino-Witten approach (Harada 2009), and applies it specifically to the J-PARC E16 experiment, which aims to resolve contradictory results from earlier experiments such as KEK-PS E325 (which observed a 3% $\phi$ mass shift) and JLab CLAS (which observed no shift). The study enables a direct test of chiral symmetry restoration in cold nuclear matter through the observation of chiral partner degeneracy, which cannot be explained by non-chiral medium interactions, and provides a concrete experimental strategy for the upcoming J-PARC E16 Run2.
