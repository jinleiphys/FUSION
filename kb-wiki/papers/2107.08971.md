# Properties of Hybrid Stars with Density Dependent Bag Model

> The phenomena of deconfinement of hadronic matter into quark matter at high density, relevant to hybrid star (HS) cores, is studied in the present work. The effective chiral model describes the pure hadronic phase while for the quark phase the MIT bag model is chosen with density dependent bag pressure. Phase transition is achieved using Maxwell construction. The effect of variation of the asymptotic value of the bag pressure ($B_{as}$) is analyzed w.r.t to the mass and radius of the HSs. The presence of hyperons in the hadronic sector also has significant effect on the choice of the value of $B_{as}$. Both the hadronic composition and the choice of $B_{as}$ significantly affect the stability of the star. The gross structural properties of the resultant HS are calculated in static condition and compared with the various constraints on them from different observational and empirical perspectives. The static properties like the maximum gravitational mass of the HS, obtained with $B_{as}$=80 MeV fm$^{-3}$, is consistent with the limits imposed from the observational analysis of PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620. The estimates of $R_{1.4}$ and $R_{1.6}$ of HSs are found to be within the range prescribed from GW170817 analysis. Also the $M-R$ solutions of the HSs are in excellent agreement with the recently obtained NICER data for PSR J0030+0451. The results of maximum surface redshift, obtained with hybrid equation of state, satisfy the constraints from 1E 1207.4-5209 and RX J0720.4-3125. The work is also extended to obtain the tidal deformation properties of the HSs. The obtained value of $Λ_{1.4}$ is consistent with the bound obtained from GW170817 data analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a density-dependent bag pressure in the MIT bag model, rather than a constant bag pressure, produces hybrid star configurations whose maximum gravitational mass, radius, and tidal deformability are consistent with multiple observational constraints from pulsars PSR J0348+0432, PSR J0740+6620, the GW170817 merger event, and NICER data for PSR J0030+0451, but only for specific values of the asymptotic bag pressure parameter \(B_{as}\).

## Method

The authors describe the hadronic phase using an effective chiral model, including nucleons and optionally hyperons, and the quark phase using the MIT bag model with a density-dependent bag pressure \(B(\rho)\) that follows a Gaussian form from Burgio et al. (2002). A first-order hadron-quark phase transition is implemented via Maxwell construction, assuming a sufficiently high surface tension at the interface. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain static hybrid star properties, and tidal deformability is computed via perturbation of the static metric in the Regge-Wheeler gauge.

## Key numbers

* Maximum gravitational mass of hybrid star with density-dependent bag pressure \(B_{as}=80\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\): \(2.03 M_{\odot}\) with radius 12.67 km.
* Maximum gravitational mass of hybrid star with constant bag pressure \(B=80\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\): \(1.98 M_{\odot}\) with radius 12.51 km.
* Maximum gravitational mass for \(B_{as}=70\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\): \(1.90 M_{\odot}\) with radius 12.83 km.
* Maximum gravitational mass for \(B_{as}=60\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\): \(1.77 M_{\odot}\) with radius 13.00 km.
* Radius of a 1.4 \(M_{\odot}\) hybrid star (\(R_{1.4}\)): 13.00 km for all \(B_{as}\) values considered.
* Radius of a 1.6 \(M_{\odot}\) hybrid star (\(R_{1.6}\)): 13.04 km for all \(B_{as}\) values considered.
* Dimensionless tidal deformability for a 1.4 \(M_{\odot}\) hybrid star (\(\Lambda_{1.4}\)): 355.10 for all \(B_{as}\) values considered.
* Maximum surface redshift for \(B_{as}=80\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\): 0.39.
* Maximum surface redshift for \(B_{as}=70\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\): 0.38.
* Maximum surface redshift for \(B_{as}=60\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\): 0.34.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using the effective chiral model (Sen et al., 2018, 2019, 2020) by incorporating a density-dependent bag pressure following Burgio et al. (2002) and Bordbar et al. (2015), rather than a constant bag constant. The results are compared against observational constraints from massive pulsars (Antoniadis et al., 2013; Cromartie et al., 2019), the GW170817 gravitational wave event (Abbott et al., 2017), and NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 (Riley et al., 2019; Miller et al., 2019). The work enables future studies of hybrid star properties with more realistic density-dependent quark matter models and can be extended to rotating hybrid stars or to include perturbative QCD corrections.
