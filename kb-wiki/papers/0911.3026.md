# A comparative analysis of statistical hadron production

> We perform a systematic comparison of the statistical model parametrization of hadron abundances measured in high energy pp, AA and e+e- collisions. The basic aim of the study is to test if the quality of the description depends on the nature of the collision process. In particular, we want to see if nuclear collisions, with multiple initial interactions, lead to "more thermal" average multiplicities than elementary pp collisions or e+e- annihilation. Such a comparison is meaningful only if it is based on data for the same or similar hadronic species and if the analyzed data has quantitatively similar errors. When these requirements are maintained, the quality of the statistical model description is found to be the same for the different initial collision configurations.

## Key claim

When comparing statistical model fits to hadron abundances across pp, AA, and e+e- collisions using matched data sets with similar hadronic species and comparable experimental errors, the quality of the statistical model description is the same for all initial collision configurations, contradicting claims that nuclear collisions produce "more thermal" average multiplicities than elementary collisions.

## Method

The authors performed statistical hadronization model fits to midrapidity densities from STAR pp and Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and to full phase space multiplicities from LEP e+e- collisions at sqrt(s)=91.25 GeV, using a canonical ensemble formulation for small systems (pp, e+e-) and a grand-canonical formulation for large systems (Au-Au). They compared chi-squared per degree of freedom values and average relative deviations between model and data, and also performed a controlled comparison by rescaling experimental errors to equalize relative uncertainties across data sets.

## Key numbers

- Temperature from pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: T = 170.1 +/- 3.5 MeV
- Temperature from Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: T = 168.5 +/- 4.0 MeV
- Temperature from e+e- collisions at sqrt(s)=91.25 GeV: T = 164.7 +/- 0.9 (stat) +/- 1.9 (sys) MeV
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_S for pp: 0.569 +/- 0.031
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_S for Au-Au: 0.932 +/- 0.040
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_S for e+e-: 0.656 +/- 0.0096 (stat) +/- 0.021 (sys)
- Chi-squared/dof for full pp fit: 15.6/14 (approximately 1.11)
- Chi-squared/dof for Au-Au fit with standard sample: 22.2/8 (approximately 2.78)
- Chi-squared/dof for e+e- fit with standard sample: 41.5/9 (approximately 4.61)
- Average relative deviation of 12 common species between pp and Au-Au: 12.5% for pp, 11.7% for Au-Au
- Average experimental error in e+e- data: 5.7%; in Au-Au data: 10%

## Context

This work extends the long-standing observation that hadron abundances in high-energy collisions follow a thermal pattern with a universal temperature around 160-170 MeV, initially attributed to Hagedorn's limiting temperature and now associated with the QCD quark-hadron transition temperature. The authors directly address claims by previous studies (such as the work referenced as pbm) that nuclear collisions show better agreement with thermal models than elementary collisions, and they demonstrate that such conclusions arise from comparing data sets with different species selections and error sizes. The analysis enables future work to focus on understanding the universal thermal behavior of hadronization itself, rather than searching for differences in thermalization among collision systems.
