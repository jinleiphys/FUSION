# Sensitivity of pp-Bremsstrahlung to Meson-Exchange Currents

> We present pp-bremsstrahlung cross section calculations at the $π$-production threshold to search for kinematics that best differentiate model-dependent descriptions of the NN $t$-matrix and accentuate the contributions of isoscalar meson-exchange currents. Existing optimization procedures are shown to be completely unreliable due to the neglect of meson-exchange currents and phase space variations. We show that phase-space hides the most important differences in model $t$-matrices, but that improved data will be critical to further investigations of exchange currents.

## Key claim

The paper argues that pp-bremsstrahlung cross sections near the π-production threshold cannot reliably distinguish between different NN-interaction models because phase space suppresses the cross section in precisely the regions where off-shell t-matrix differences are largest, and that existing optimization procedures based on nucleon-pole dominance neglect both phase space variations and meson-exchange currents.

## Method

The authors performed fully relativistic meson-baryon model calculations of pp-bremsstrahlung at Elab=280 MeV, including nucleon-pole and rescatter contributions (impulse approximation) plus perturbative ρπγ, ωπγ, ρηγ, ωηγ and NΔγ(π,ρ) meson-exchange currents. They used wave functions from the Paris, Nijmegen, Bonn B and RuhrPot NN-interactions, with coupling constants fixed to experimental values and SU(6) relations, and scanned coplanar and non-coplanar reaction geometries to identify kinematics where exchange currents are enhanced and model differences are visible.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed at Elab=280 MeV, below the π-production threshold
- Coupling constants used: gρπγ=0.55, gωπγ=2.03, gρηγ=1.39, gωηγ=0.31, gNΔπ=28.85, and ΓΔ=115 MeV
- Impulse approximation rescatter amplitudes represent a correction of less than 15% to the dominant nucleon-pole contributions
- For coplanar geometry with θ3=28.0° and θ4=27.8°, exchange currents enhance the cross section near θγ=75° by about 50%
- A comparable calculation by Jetter and Fearing (1994) found a similar enhancement of about 45% at the same kinematics
- An experiment at Elab≈294 MeV with θ3=θ4=6.0°±1.2° suffered from large phase space variations over detector acceptance angles

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Eden et al. (1993) that found collective discrepancies with TRIUMF data, and contradicts the optimization procedures of Fearing (1979) and Brown and Anthony (1991) that relied on nucleon-pole dominance. The authors show that the widely-used approach of maximizing photon energies to probe off-shell t-matrix differences fails because phase space suppresses the relevant regions, and that meson-exchange currents contribute significantly where phase space is large. The paper recommends shifting experimental emphasis to coplanar geometries with larger proton scattering angles (0≤θ3≤25° and θ4≈20°) to investigate isoscalar exchange currents, and suggests that the current focus on highly non-coplanar geometries should be reconsidered.
