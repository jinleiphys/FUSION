# Finite baryon and isospin chemical potential in AdS/CFT with flavor

> We investigate the thermodynamics of a thermal field theory in presence of both a baryon and an isospin chemical potential. For this we consider a probe of several D7-branes embedded in the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background. We determine the structure of the phase diagram and calculate the relevant thermodynamical quantities both in the canonical and in the grand canonical ensemble. We discuss how accidental symmetries present reflect themselves in the phase diagram: In the case of two flavors, for small densities, there is a rotational symmetry in the plane spanned by the baryon and isospin density which breaks down at large densities, leaving a Z_4-symmetry. Finally, we calculate vector mode spectral functions and determine their dependence on either the baryon or the isospin density. For large densities, a new excitation forms with energy below the known supersymmetric spectrum. Increasing the density further, this excitation becomes unstable. We speculate that this instability indicates a new phase of condensed mesons.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the thermodynamics of a thermal field theory with both baryon and isospin chemical potentials using holographic duality, finding that for large densities a new excitation forms below the known supersymmetric spectrum which becomes unstable, potentially indicating a new phase of condensed mesons; however, the authors explicitly state this is a speculation rather than a confirmed result.

## Method

The authors embed a probe of several D7-branes in the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background with an U(N_f) gauge field, which is conjectured to be dual to a thermal quantum field theory with isospin and baryon charge. They determine the phase diagram structure and calculate thermodynamic quantities in both the canonical and grand canonical ensembles, and compute vector mode spectral functions from gauge field fluctuations on the branes to identify mesonic quasiparticle resonances.

## Key numbers

- The diffusion coefficient approaches D = 1/(2πT) in the limits m → ∞ and m → 0, coinciding with the result for R-charges in N = 4 SYM
- For finite baryon density with μ^B ≈ M_c (renormalized quark mass in the plasma), the meson spectrum approaches the supersymmetric spectrum M_n/(2πT) = m√((n+1)(n+2)/2) with m = 2M_q/(√λ T)
- The new excitation forms at an energy below M = 2πT m for finite isospin density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of D7-brane probes in AdS-Schwarzschild backgrounds that investigated baryon chemical potential effects (Kobayashi 2006) and isospin chemical potential effects (Erdmenger 2007). The phase transition along lines of equal densities |d^B| = |d^I| near T = 0 is reminiscent of a corresponding transition found in two-color QCD (Splittorff 2000). The results enable further investigation of mesonic excitation spectra and hydrodynamic properties in holographic models with multiple chemical potentials, and suggest connections to vector meson condensation similar to that studied by Aharony (2007).
