# New Calculations for Phase Space Factors Involved in Double Beta Decay

> We present new results for the phase space factors involved in double beta decay for beta-beta- transitions to ground states and excited 0+1 states, for isotopes of experimental interest. The Coulomb distortion of the electron wave functions is treated by solving numerically the Dirac equation with inclusion of the finite nuclear size and electron screening effects, and using a Coulomb potential derived from a realistic proton density distribution in the daughter nucleus. Our results are compared with other results from literature, obtained in different approximations, and possible causes that can give differences are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors present new calculations of phase space factors (PSF) for double beta decay that improve upon previous work by using a Coulomb potential derived from a realistic proton density distribution in the daughter nucleus, rather than a uniform charge distribution; the resulting PSF values differ from earlier calculations by up to approximately 32%, with the largest differences for heavier nuclei.

## Method

The electron wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation numerically, including finite nuclear size and electron screening effects. The Coulomb potential is derived from a realistic proton density distribution obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with a Woods-Saxon potential and using BCS occupation amplitudes. The phase space factors for both 2νββ and 0νββ decay modes are computed for transitions to ground states and excited 0⁺₁ states for twelve isotopes of experimental interest.

## Key numbers

- For 2νββ decay to ground states, the present PSF values range from 2.53×10⁻²² yr⁻¹ (¹²⁸Te) to 3.42×10⁻¹⁷ yr⁻¹ (¹⁵⁰Nd)
- For 2νββ decay to excited 0⁺₁ states, the present PSF values range from 3.26×10⁻²⁴ yr⁻¹ (²³⁸U) to 3.99×10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹ (¹⁵⁰Nd)
- For 0νββ decay to ground states, the present PSF values range from 5.55×10⁻¹⁶ yr⁻¹ (¹²⁸Te) to 6.20×10⁻¹⁴ yr⁻¹ (¹⁵⁰Nd)
- For 0νββ decay to excited 0⁺₁ states, the present PSF values range from 1.08×10⁻¹⁶ yr⁻¹ (¹¹⁰Pd) to 2.70×10⁻¹⁴ yr⁻¹ (¹⁵⁰Nd)
- Relative differences between the present 2νββ PSF and those of Kotila and Iachello (2012) are within 13% for most nuclei, with the exception of ²³⁸U where differences exceed 100%
- Relative differences between the present 0νββ PSF and those of Kotila and Iachello (2012) are within 5.6% for ground-state transitions, with the exception of ²³⁸U (7.4%)
- The differences between using a uniform charge distribution and a realistic proton density distribution are within 5% for both decay modes

## Context

This work extends the calculations of phase space factors by Kotila and Iachello (2012), who used exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and screening effects but a uniform charge distribution. The authors compare their results with earlier non-relativistic calculations by Doi et al. (1985-1993) and by Suhonen and Civitarese (1998), finding that the improved treatment of the Coulomb potential produces differences up to 32% for heavier nuclei. The new PSF values enable more precise extraction of nuclear matrix elements and beyond-Standard-Model parameters from double beta decay experiments, and can improve the determination of the g_pp parameter in QRPA calculations.
