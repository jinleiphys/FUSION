# On colliding ultrarelativistic nuclei on a transverse lattice

> We argue that the classical evolution of small x modes in the collision of two ultrarelativistic nuclei is described on a transverse lattice by the Kogut--Susskind Hamiltonian in 2+1-dimensions coupled to an adjoint scalar field. The initial conditions for the evolution are provided by the non--Abelian Weizsäcker--Williams fields which constitute the classical parton distributions in each of the nuclei. We outline how lattice techniques developed for real time simulations of field theories in thermal equilibrium can be used to study non--perturbatively, thermalization and classical gluon radiation in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The classical evolution of small x modes in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions can be described non-perturbatively on a transverse lattice using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian in 2+1 dimensions coupled to an adjoint scalar field, provided one assumes approximate boost invariance of the fields and a weak rapidity dependence of the color charge density.

## Method

The authors derive a lattice Hamiltonian from the QCD action in the forward light cone using proper time and space-time rapidity coordinates, imposing the Schwinger gauge and assuming boost invariance. They discretize the resulting 2+1 dimensional Yang-Mills Hamiltonian coupled to an adjoint scalar field, obtaining the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian on a transverse lattice. Initial conditions for the lattice fields are provided by the non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams fields of the two nuclei before collision, discretized via SU(2) matrices at each lattice site. The lattice equations of motion are then integrated using a leapfrog algorithm that respects Gauss' laws exactly.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative approach is valid for modes with transverse momenta \(k_t >> \alpha_S \sqrt{\chi}\), where \(\chi\) is the total color charge squared per unit area integrated from the rapidity of interest to the beam rapidity.
- A natural infrared cutoff for perturbative gluon distributions is estimated at \(k_t \sim \alpha_S \sqrt{\chi}\).
- The initial condition for the adjoint scalar field on the lattice is given by \(p_j|_{\tau=0} = -i\sum_{\hat n}\left([{\cal U}^1_{j,{\hat n}},{\cal U}^2_{j,{\hat n}}] + [{\cal U}^1_{j-{\hat n},{\hat n}},{\cal U}^2_{j-{\hat n},{\hat n}}]\right)\).
- The lattice plaquette term yields \(1 - \frac{1}{2}U_\Box \rightarrow \frac{a^4}{2} B^\nu B^\nu\) in the limit of vanishing lattice spacing \(a\).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) for small x modes in large nuclei and the Kovner-McLerran-Weigert formulation (1995) of nuclear collisions as the collision of Weizsäcker-Williams fields. It enables non-perturbative studies of classical gluon radiation and thermalization in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, going beyond the perturbative solutions of Kovner, McLerran and Weigert (1995), Gyulassy (1997), and others that are infrared singular. The approach provides a framework to compute energy densities, gluon multiplicity distributions, and initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations, and can be checked against perturbative results in the Abelian limit of large transverse momenta.
