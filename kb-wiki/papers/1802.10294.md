# Nuclear dynamics and particle production near threshold energies in heavy-ion collisions

> Recent progress of the quantum molecular dynamics model for describing the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions is viewed, in particular the nuclear fragmentation, isospin physics, particle production and in-medium effect, hadron-induced nuclear reactions, hypernucleus etc. The neck fragmentation in Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions is investigated for extracting the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities. The isospin effects, in-medium properties and the behavior of high-density symmetry energy in medium and high energy heavy-ion collisions are thoroughly discussed. The hypernuclide dynamics formed in heavy-ion collisions and in hadron induced reactions is analyzed and addressed in the future experiments at the High-Intensity heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF).

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress of the quantum molecular dynamics model (specifically the LQMD model developed by the Lanzhou group) for describing heavy-ion collisions, but the abstract's claim of "thoroughly discussing" isospin effects and high-density symmetry energy is qualified by the statement that the high-density behavior of symmetry energy is "not well understood" and that constraints come from observables emitted from the high-density regime.

## Method

The LQMD transport model represents each nucleon as a Gaussian wave packet and evolves the system via Hamilton's equations of motion under a self-consistently generated mean-field potential derived from a Skyrme-type energy-density functional. The model includes dynamics of resonances (Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535)), hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi, Omega), and mesons (pi, eta, K, anti-K, rho, omega) through hadron-hadron collisions, Pauli-blocking, resonance decays, mean-field potentials, and threshold energy corrections. In-medium cross sections are scaled by effective masses, and hadron optical potentials are computed from chiral Lagrangian approaches, dispersion relations, and relativistic mean-field models with light-quark counting rules for hyperons.

- Anti-hyperon potentials at saturation density: -436 MeV for anti-Lambda, -218 MeV for anti-Xi

## Context

This review extends the body of work on quantum molecular dynamics models developed by various groups, including the ImQMD model by the CIAE group for fusion-fission dynamics, the IQMD model incorporating isospin degree of freedom (Hartnack 1998, Chiba 1998), and the CoMD model with phase-space constraints (Papa 2001). The LQMD model specifically builds on earlier work by Feng (2013, 2015) and has been applied to medium and high energy heavy-ion collisions, antiproton induced reactions, and meson-nucleus dynamics. The paper discusses how observables from heavy-ion collisions can constrain the high-density symmetry energy, which is relevant for understanding neutron star structure and mergers as studied by Abbott (2017) and Li (2017), and connects to planned experiments at HIAF, FAIR, RIKEN, and other facilities.
