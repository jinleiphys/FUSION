# Eta photoproduction

> We present a combined analysis of the new eta photoproduction data for total and differential cross sections, target asymmetry and photon asymmetry. Using basic assumptions, this allows a model-independent extraction of the $E_{2-}$ and $M_{2-}$ multipoles as well as resonance parameters of the $D_{13}(1520)$ state. At higher energy, we show that the photon asymmetry is extremely sensitive to small multipoles that are excited by photons in the helicity 3/2 state. These could be, e.g., the $F_{15}(1680)$, the $F_{17}(1990)$, or the $G_{17}(2190)$ resonances.

## Key claim

The paper presents a combined analysis of new eta photoproduction data that allows a model-independent extraction of the $E_{2-}$ and $M_{2-}$ multipoles and resonance parameters of the $D_{13}(1520)$ state, but only under the assumptions that the phase difference between electric and magnetic multipoles is neglected, the analysis is restricted to a truncated multipole representation, and the phase of the $E_{0+}$ multipole is known from a Breit-Wigner parametrization.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit of total and differential cross sections, target asymmetry, and photon asymmetry data for the reaction $p(\gamma,\eta)p$ using a multipole expansion truncated to $s$-, $p$-, and $d$-waves, valid up to 1 GeV photon lab energy. They expressed the three observables in terms of angle-independent coefficients ($a$, $b$, $c$, $d$, $e$, $f$) that were fitted to experimental data at various energies, then used these coefficients to extract the $d$-wave multipoles $E_{2-}$ and $M_{2-}$ and the helicity 3/2 multipole $B_{2-}=E_{2-}+M_{2-}$. Single-energy fits were combined with a polynomial fit to the energy dependence of the coefficients to produce a global energy-dependent solution.

## Key numbers

- $M^* = (1549 \pm 8)$ MeV for the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance mass from a Breit-Wigner fit to the $a$ coefficient
- $\Gamma_R = (202 \pm 35)$ MeV for the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance width
- $|E_{0+}| = 16.14 \times 10^{-3}/m_\pi^+$ for the absolute value of the $s$-wave multipole at threshold
- $A_{3/2} = (+166 \pm 5) \times 10^{-3} \text{GeV}^{-1/2}$ for the $D_{13}(1520)$ resonance helicity 3/2 coupling
- $A_{3/2} = (+133 \pm 12) \times 10^{-3} \text{GeV}^{-1/2}$ for the $F_{15}(1680)$ resonance helicity 3/2 coupling
- $A_{3/2} = (+86 \pm 60) \times 10^{-3} \text{GeV}^{-1/2}$ for the $F_{17}(1990)$ resonance helicity 3/2 coupling
- $A_{3/2} = 81$ to $180 \times 10^{-3} \text{GeV}^{-1/2}$ for the $G_{17}(2190)$ resonance helicity 3/2 coupling

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of eta photoproduction that focused on the dominant $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance (Krusche 1995, Tiator 1994) by using new polarization data from ELSA (Bock 1998) and GRAAL (Hourany 1998) to access the previously difficult-to-discern $D_{13}(1520)$ contribution. The analysis reveals an unexpected phase shift between the $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ resonances that contradicts predictions from the authors' own isobar model (Knoechlein 1995) and from coupled-channel calculations (Kaiser 1997, Feuster 1997), suggesting that either the $S_{11}(1535)$ is heavily distorted or may be a completely different phenomenon. The method enables future determination of eta branching ratios for higher resonances such as $F_{15}(1680)$, $F_{17}(1990)$, and $G_{17}(2190)$ down to values well below 0.1%.
