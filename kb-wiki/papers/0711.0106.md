# Equation of state in the inner crust of neutron stars: discusion of the unbound neutron states

> In this paper, we calculate the stable Wigner-Seitz (W-S) cells in the inner crust of neutron stars and we discuss the nuclear shell effects. A distinction is done between the shell effects due to the bound states and those induced by the unbound states, which are shown to be spurious. We then estimate the effects of the spurious shells on the total energy and decompose it into a smooth and a residual part. We propose a correction to the Hartree-Fock binding energy in Wigner-Seitz cell (HF-WS).

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shell effects induced by the discretization of unbound neutron states in the Wigner-Seitz (W-S) approximation are spurious, and that these spurious effects introduce fluctuations in the total energy of up to about 300 keV per nucleon, which can only be partially reduced by a proposed smooth correction.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction to determine the stable Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust of neutron stars, dividing the crust into 11 density zones following the approach of Negele and Vautherin (1973). To isolate the spurious shell effects from unbound neutrons, they simulated a homogeneous neutron gas in a W-S cell by removing all proton states and varied the W-S radius from 10 to 50 fm at constant neutron density, fitting the smooth part of the resulting energy fluctuations to a universal function of density and radius.

## Key numbers

- The spurious shell effects from discretized unbound states introduce fluctuations in the binding energy up to about 300 keV per nucleon for small W-S radii (around 20 fm).
- The smooth part of the spurious shell effect is fitted by the function f(rho_unb, R_WS) = 89.05 (rho_unb/rho_0)^0.1425 R_WS^{-2}, where R_WS is in fm.
- After subtracting the smooth correction, the residual fluctuations in the binding energy are of the order of 50 keV per nucleon.
- In the HF calculation for zone N_zone=4, the difference between the first and second energy minima is about 5 keV per nucleon; with pairing correlations (HFB), the first minimum becomes deeper by about 34 keV per nucleon.
- The maximum pairing gap in neutron matter used in the calculations is about 3 MeV, located at k_F = 0.85 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent mean-field modeling of the inner crust pioneered by Negele and Vautherin (1973) and later developed by Baldo (2005) and others, by clarifying the distinction between physical shell effects from bound states and spurious shell effects from the discretization of unbound states in the W-S approximation. The paper builds on band theory calculations by Chamel (2007), which showed that the W-S approximation is justified only if the temperature is larger than about 100 keV or if quantities are averaged over a scale of about 100 keV around the Fermi surface. The proposed correction (HF-WS) is insufficient to fully remove spurious effects, and the authors identify the need for future work including pairing correlations and finite temperature to reduce residual fluctuations.
