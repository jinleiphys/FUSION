# A (p/E) Calculation of Strong Pionic Decays of Baryons

> Strong pionic decays of baryons are studied in a non-relativistic quark model framework via a convergent (p/E) expansion of the transition operator. Results are compared to the ones obtained within a more conventional (p/m) expansion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a convergent (p/E) expansion of the transition operator for strong pionic decays of baryons, within a non-relativistic quark model framework, provides a more sound theoretical basis than the conventional (p/m) expansion, but only for a limited set of low-lying resonances; for higher excitations, neither expansion is justified.

## Method

The authors calculated strong pionic decay widths of non-strange baryons using two quark potential models: a two-body potential (V^(2)) and a three-body potential (V^(2)+V^(3)). They constructed the transition operator using both a (p/m) expansion and a (p/E) expansion (where E = sqrt(p^2 + m^2)), and compared the convergence of the two approaches. Additionally, they employed a ^3P_0 quark pair creation model to incorporate the pion's q-qbar structure, and developed a "relativized" version (R^3P_0) by substituting relativistic expressions into the ^3P_0 operator.

## Key numbers

- For the (p/E) expansion, a first-order treatment is justified for only five cases: Δ(1232) → Nπ, N(1520) → Nπ, N(1535) → Δπ, N(1650) → Δπ, and N(1700) → Nπ.
- The root mean square radius of the pion is reproduced using a gaussian parameter R_A^2 = 8 GeV^{-2}.
- The small core size in the V^(2) model gives ⟨r^2⟩^{1/2} ≈ 0.47 fm, making quark speeds close to 1 with (p/m) ≳ 1.
- A standard dipole type form factor for the pion-quark vertex gives corrections to the widths of at most 20%.
- The mixing angle from a tensor potential evaluation is ϑ ≈ -40°.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Cano 1995) of the effective transition operator for B → B'π decays, which emphasized the role of pion structure and relativistic effects. The results contradict the adequacy of simple (p/m) expansions for many decays and support the need for a consistent treatment of both relativistic effects and the q-qbar structure of the pion. The paper enables future work to incorporate these elements simultaneously, and notes that other treatments (Stancu 1988) using a ^3P_0 scheme achieve reasonable fits to a wider range of data but with a different philosophy.
