# Nucleon excited states on the lattice

> We study the pion-nucleon system in s-wave in the framework of lattice QCD in order to gain new information on the nucleon excited states. We perform simulations for $n_f =2$ mass degenerate light quarks at a pion mass of 266 MeV. The results show that including the two-particle states drastically changes the energy levels. The variational analysis and the distillation approach play an important role in the extraction of the energy levels. The phase shift analysis allows to extract information on the resonance nature of the observed states.

## Key claim

Including explicit two-particle (pion-nucleon) interpolators in the operator set drastically changes the extracted energy levels for the negative-parity nucleon spectrum, and the resulting levels can be interpreted via Luscher's formula to extract resonance parameters, but the work is performed at an unphysical pion mass of 266 MeV where the Neta channel is not open.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with n_f = 2 mass-degenerate light quarks using a tree-level improved Wilson-Clover action on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with lattice spacing a = 0.12 fm and pion mass 266 MeV. They computed the Euclidean cross-correlation matrix for a set of interpolators including both 3-quark nucleon operators and (4+1)-quark Npi operators, using the distillation method to evaluate the required diagrams. A variational analysis was applied to extract energy levels, and Luscher's formula was used to relate the finite-volume energy levels to the continuum phase shift.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi = 266 MeV
* Lattice spacing: a = 0.12 fm
* Lattice volume: V = 16^3 x 32
* Nucleon ground state mass from 3-quark interpolators: m_N = 1068(6) MeV
* Resonance mass extracted from Breit-Wigner fit to the first resonance: m_R = 1.678(99) GeV
* Number of configurations used: 280

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of nucleon excited states that previously used only 3-quark interpolators (Mahbub 2009, Dudek 2010, Bulava 2010, Engel 2010, Engel 2012), which produced a lower state below the physical N*(1535) mass. It follows the approach of recent mesonic resonance studies (Lang 2011, Aoki 2011, Feng 2010, Mohler 2012) by explicitly including two-particle interpolators. The results enable future studies of coupled-channel systems in lattice QCD and demonstrate that meson-baryon interpolators are necessary for a reliable picture of the negative-parity nucleon spectrum.
