# Rotational properties in even-even superheavy $^{254-258}$Rf nuclei based on total-Routhian-surface calculations

> High-spin yrast structures of even-even superheavy nuclei $^{254-258}$Rf are investigated by means of total-Routhian-surface approach in three-dimensional ($β_2, γ, β_4$) space. The behavior in the moments of inertia of $^{256}$Rf is well reproduced by our calculations, which is attributed to the $j_{15/2}$ neutron rotation-alignment. The competition between rotationally aligned $i_{13/2}$ proton and $j_{15/2}$ neutron is discussed. High-spin predictions are also made for its neighboring isotopes $^{254,258}$Rf.

# Rotational properties in even-even superheavy $^{254-258}$Rf nuclei

## Key claim

The total-Routhian-surface calculations reproduce the moments of inertia of $^{256}$Rf well, attributing the upbending to rotation-alignment of a $j_{15/2}$ neutron pair, but the paper hedges that the competition between $i_{13/2}$ proton and $j_{15/2}$ neutron alignment is "competitive to some extent" and that the predictive power of the model in the superheavy region requires testing because preconceived knowledge about densities and single-particle potentials fades away towards superheavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed total-Routhian-surface (TRS) calculations in three-dimensional ($\beta_2, \gamma, \beta_4$) deformation space using a Woods-Saxon potential with the Lipkin-Nogami pairing approach, including both monopole and doubly stretched quadrupole pairings. The nuclear system was cranked around the x-axis at given rotational frequencies, and the resulting cranked-Lipkin-Nogami equation was solved self-consistently for each deformation lattice point. Equilibrium deformations were determined by minimizing the calculated TRS at fixed rotational frequency.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state $\beta_2$ deformations for $^{254-258}$Rf are approximately 0.24, consistent with previous results from Moller (1995) and Sobiczewski (2001).
- A rapid decrease of $\beta_2$ appears after a critical rotational frequency of approximately 0.3 MeV, indicating the band crossing frequency.
- In $^{254}$No, two 2-quasiproton isomeric states are reported: a $K^\pi = 3^+$ state at 0.988 MeV and a $K^\pi = 8^-$ state at 1.296 MeV (Herzberg, 2006).
- The Strutinsky shell correction used a smoothing range $\gamma = 1.20\hbar\omega_0$, where $\hbar\omega_0 = 41/A^{1/3}$ MeV, with a correction polynomial of order $p=6$.

## Context

This work extends TRS calculations, previously successful in medium and heavy mass nuclei, to the superheavy rutherfordium isotopes $^{254-258}$Rf, following the first high-spin data reported for $^{256}$Rf by Greenlees (2012). The calculations reproduce the experimental moments of inertia for $^{256}$Rf and predict rotational properties for $^{254,258}$Rf, which await future experimental confirmation. The study builds on prior work by Frauendorf (1981), Simon (1980, 1982), and AlKhudair (2009) on alignment properties of $i_{13/2}$ protons and $j_{15/2}$ neutrons in actinides and transfermium nuclei, and enables predictions for high-spin behavior in the superheavy region where spectroscopic data remain scarce.
