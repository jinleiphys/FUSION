# On semi-classical pion production in heavy ion collisions

> In high energy heavy ion collisions the pion multiplicity is large, and one might expect that pions are radiated semi-classically. The axial symmetry of the collision and approximately zero isotopic spin of the colliding nuclei result then in peculiar isotopic spin -- azimuth correlations of the produced pions. These correlations are easy to test -- and should be tested.

## Key claim

In high-energy heavy ion collisions with large pion multiplicity, the pion field may be treated semi-classically; the axial symmetry of the collision and approximate isospin symmetry of the colliding nuclei then produce specific correlations between the isospin and azimuthal angle of the produced pions, which are independent of the detailed dynamics and can be tested experimentally.

## Method

The authors use a Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann formalism to write the N-pion production amplitude as a functional integral over pion fields and sources. They evaluate this integral in the saddle-point (WKB) approximation, assuming the source is large enough that a classical pion field develops, and impose axial symmetry and isospin symmetry to restrict the form of the saddle-point field to a "hedgehog" configuration. They then compute inclusive one- and two-pion cross sections by integrating over the isospin orientation of the classical field using Haar measure and averaging formulas for SO(3) rotations.

## Key numbers

* The average multiplicity ⟨N⟩ is given by the integral of |F_i(k)|^2 over phase space, which equals ℱ_{ii}(R_μ=0) (Eq. multiplicity).
* The dispersion relation ⟨N(N-1)⟩ = ⟨N⟩^2 holds for all pions together, indicating a Poisson distribution for the total multiplicity (Eq. dispersion).
* For pions at zero rapidity (k_1^∥ = k_2^∥ = 0), the ratio (4σ^(1) - 3σ^(2)) / (2σ^(2) + σ^(3)) = cos²(φ₁ - φ₂) (Eq. ratio1).
* For π⁺π⁻ pairs at zero rapidity, (σ^tot dσ^{π⁺π⁻}/(d𝐤₁ d𝐤₂) - (9/10) dσ^{π⁺}/(d𝐤₁) dσ^{π⁻}/(d𝐤₂)) / (dσ^{π⁺}/(d𝐤₁) dσ^{π⁻}/(d𝐤₂)) = (3/10) cos²(φ₁ - φ₂) (Eq. ratio2).

## Context

This work extends the idea of a classical pion field in heavy ion collisions, previously discussed by Anselm (1988), Bjorken (1993), and Krzwicki (1993), and follows recent investigations of chiral symmetry restoration by Wilczek (1993). The predicted isospin-azimuth correlations are distinct from the well-studied Bose-Einstein correlations and provide a testable signature of semi-classical pion radiation, independent of the detailed dynamics of the collision. The results enable experimental searches for such correlations in central heavy ion collisions at high energies.
