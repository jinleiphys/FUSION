# Self-consistent description of nuclear compressional modes

> Isoscalar monopole and dipole compressional modes are computed for a variety of closed-shell nuclei in a relativistic random-phase approximation to three different parametrizations of the Walecka model with scalar self-interactions. Particular emphasis is placed on the role of self-consistency which by itself, and with little else, guarantees the decoupling of the spurious isoscalar-dipole strength from the physical response and the conservation of the vector current. A powerful new relation is introduced to quantify the violation of the vector current in terms of various ground-state form-factors. For the isoscalar-dipole mode two distinct regions are clearly identified: (i) a high-energy component that is sensitive to the size of the nucleus and scales with the compressibility of the model and (ii) a low-energy component that is insensitivity to the nuclear compressibility. A fairly good description of both compressional modes is obtained by using a ``soft'' parametrization having a compression modulus of K=224 MeV.

## Key claim

Self-consistency in the relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA), using a residual particle-hole interaction identical to the one that generates the mean-field ground state, and including negative-energy states via a nonspectral approach, is sufficient to guarantee both the conservation of the vector current and the complete decoupling of the spurious isoscalar-dipole strength (center-of-mass translation) from the physical response, without recourse to artificial cutoffs or truncations.

## Method

The author computed the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) and isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) for several closed-shell nuclei using a relativistic RPA based on three parametrizations of the Walecka model with scalar self-interactions. A nonspectral approach was used to compute the nucleon propagator, treating bound and continuum states on equal footing and automatically including negative-energy states. The residual particle-hole interaction was kept fully consistent with the mean-field interaction, and the RPA response was obtained by iterating the lowest-order polarization to all orders via Dyson’s equation.

## Key numbers

- The original linear Walecka model gave a nuclear compression modulus of K = 545 MeV, which was reduced to K = 224 MeV by including scalar self-interactions.
- A “soft” parametrization with a compression modulus of K = 224 MeV provided a fairly good description of both compressional modes.
- The cumulative violation of the vector current in the Feynman-density part of the polarization for the timelike component at q = q′ was shown to equal the mass number A of the nucleus (e.g., A = 208 for ²⁰⁸Pb).

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic RPA calculations (Ma97, Ma99, Pi00, Vr00) by incorporating scalar self-interactions and emphasizing strict self-consistency, in contrast to nonrelativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus RPA approaches (Co99, Co00) and earlier semi-empirical methods (Bl99). The author explicitly addresses criticisms by Shlomo and Sanzhur (2000) regarding approximations in RPA implementations, showing that the relativistic formalism avoids neglecting two-body Coulomb and spin-orbit terms, momentum-dependent approximations, artificial cutoffs, and smearing dependence. The results enable more reliable extraction of the nuclear compression modulus from GMR and ISGDR measurements, with implications for the equation of state of neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions.
