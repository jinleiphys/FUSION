# Clues to the nature of the Delta^*(1700) resonance from pion- and photon-induced reactions

> We make a study of the (pi^- p --> K^0 pi^0 Lambda), (pi^+ p --> K^+ pi^+ Lambda), (K^+\bar{K}^0 p), (K^+ pi^+ Sigma^0), (K^+ pi^0 Sigma^+), and (eta pi^+ p) reactions, in which the basic dynamics is given by the excitation of the Delta^*(1700) resonance which subsequently decays into (K Sigma^*(1385)) or (Delta(1232) eta). In a similar way we also study the (gamma p --> K^0 pi^+ Lambda), (K^+ pi^- Sigma^+), (K^+ pi^+ Sigma^-), (K^0 pi^0 Sigma^+), and (eta pi^0 p) related reactions. The cross sections are proportional to the square of the coupling of Delta^*(1700) to (Sigma^*K), (Delta eta) for which there is no experimental information but which is provided in the context of coupled channels chiral unitary theory where the Delta^*(1700) is dynamically generated. Within present theoretical and experimental uncertainties one can claim a global qualitative agreement between theory and experiment. We provide a list of items which need to be improved in order to make further progress along these lines.

## Key claim

The global qualitative agreement between theory and experiment across multiple pion- and photon-induced reactions, with cross sections spanning nearly two orders of magnitude, supports the hypothesis that the Delta*(1700) resonance is dynamically generated in coupled channels chiral unitary theory, but the authors caution that the agreement is only qualitative within present theoretical and experimental uncertainties, and they provide a list of items needing improvement.

## Method

The authors calculated tree-level diagrams where the Delta*(1700) resonance is excited by an incoming pion or photon and subsequently decays into K Sigma*(1385) or Delta(1232) eta, with the Sigma*(1385) or Delta(1232) then decaying into the final meson-baryon states. The couplings of Delta*(1700) to Sigma*K and Delta eta were taken from previous coupled channels chiral unitary theory calculations, while the pi N Delta* coupling was extracted from experimental branching ratios using a d-wave vertex with Blatt-Weisskopf penetration factors. The model was applied to six pion-induced reactions (pi- p -> K0 pi0 Lambda, pi+ p -> K+ pi+ Lambda, K+ Kbar0 p, K+ pi+ Sigma0, K+ pi0 Sigma+, eta pi+ p) and five photon-induced reactions (gamma p -> K0 pi+ Lambda, K+ pi- Sigma+, K+ pi+ Sigma-, K0 pi0 Sigma+, eta pi0 p).

## Key numbers

* The Delta*(1700) width is taken as 300 +/- 100 MeV, with a pi N branching ratio of 15 +/- 5%, yielding a d-wave coupling g^(d)_(pi N Delta*) = 0.94 +/- 0.20.
* The couplings of Delta*(1700) to coupled channels from chiral unitary theory are: g_(Delta pi) = 1.0, g_(Sigma* K) = 3.4, and g_(Delta eta) = 2.2.
* For the pi- p -> K0 pi0 Lambda reaction, data exist at sqrt(s) = 2020 MeV (about 320 MeV above the Delta*(1700) peak) and at lower energies around sqrt(s) = 1930-1980 MeV.
* The pi+ p -> K+ pi+ Lambda reaction data extend from about sqrt(s) = 1800 MeV upward, with the theoretical prediction more than a factor of two larger than data at around sqrt(s) = 1900 MeV.
* The pi+ p -> eta pi+ p cross section is a factor of fifty larger than for pi+ p -> K+ pi0 Sigma+ or pi+ p -> K+ pi+ Sigma0 below p_lab = 1670 MeV.
* For the gamma p -> K0 pi0 Sigma+ and gamma p -> eta pi0 p reactions, the cross sections have been reduced by about 30 percent with respect to previous calculations due to a more realistic Delta(1700) width and a small fraction of rho N decay width in d-wave.

## Context

This work extends the coupled channels chiral unitary approach previously applied to dynamically generated resonances such as the Lambda(1405) (Kaiser 1995, Kaiser 1996, Oller 2000, Jido 2003) and the N*(1535) (Nacher 1999), and specifically builds on calculations of the Delta*(1700) couplings to Sigma*K and Delta eta from Lutz (2004) and decuplet studies (2005). The authors note that a previous model by Hyodo (2003) based on Lambda(1405) excitation predicted a cross section of 6 microbarns for the K0 pi0 Lambda channel compared to the experimental 104 +/- 8 microbarns, because that model lacked the Sigma*(1385) resonance. The present work enables further experimental tests of the dynamically generated nature of the Delta*(1700), particularly through the gamma p -> K0 pi0 Sigma+ reaction being analyzed at ELSA (NSTAR05 workshop).
