# Study of the $^7Be(p,γ)^8B$ and $^7Li(n,γ)^8Li$ capture reactions using the shell model embedded in the continuum

> We apply the realistic shell model which includes the coupling between many-particle (quasi-)bound states and the continuum of one-particle scattering states to the spectroscopy of mirror nuclei: $^8$B and $^8$Li, as well as to the description of low energy cross sections (the astrophysical S factors) in the capture reactions:$^7Be(p,γ)^8B$ and $^7Li(n,γ)^8Li$.

## Key claim

The Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), which couples realistic shell-model many-particle states to the one-particle scattering continuum via a residual zero-range nucleon-nucleon interaction, can simultaneously describe the spectroscopy and low-energy capture cross sections of the mirror nuclei \({}^{8}\)B and \({}^{8}\)Li, but the agreement for the width of the \(J^{\pi}=3_{1}^{+}\) state in \({}^{8}\)B remains poor (discrepancy by at least a factor of 5) and the calculated width of the \(J^{\pi}=1_{1}^{+}\) state is only about half the experimental value for the standard spin-exchange parameter.

## Method

The authors construct the \(Q\) subspace from shell-model states generated with the Cohen-Kurath interaction and the \(P\) subspace from one-nucleon scattering states built on low-lying states of the residual nuclei \({}^{7}\)Be and \({}^{7}\)Li. They solve coupled-channel equations for the continuum and inhomogeneous equations for the decay of quasi-bound states, using a zero-range residual force with a spin-exchange term to couple the two subspaces. The single-particle potential is determined self-consistently by iteratively including the diagonal part of the residual coupling, and the resulting wave functions are used to compute the astrophysical \(S\)-factors for both capture reactions.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of \({}^{8}\)B (\(J^{\pi}=2_{1}^{+}\)) has the proton \(1p_{3/2}\) single-particle orbit bound at \(-137\) keV in the self-consistent potential for the \(2^{+}\) state.
- The root-mean-squared radius of the proton \(1p_{3/2}\) orbit in the self-consistent potential for \(J^{\pi}=2^{+}\) is \(4.228\) fm, nearly identical to \(4.239\) fm in the equivalent Saxon-Woods potential.
- The energy splitting of the \(p_{3/2}\) and \(p_{1/2}\) proton orbitals in the self-consistent potential is \(0.868\) MeV for \(J^{\pi}=2^{+}\) states and \(2.849\) MeV for \(J^{\pi}=1^{+}\) states, compared to \(3.369\) MeV in the initial potential.
- The strength of the residual interaction is set to \(V_{12}^{(0)} = 650\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), with the optimal range between 600 and 700 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\).
- The ground state energy relative to the proton emission threshold changes by about 100 keV due to coupling to the \({}^{7}\)Be ground state.
- The neutron \(1p_{3/2}\) and \(1p_{1/2}\) orbits in \({}^{8}\)Li are placed at \(-13.02\) MeV and \(-11.16\) MeV respectively, with r.m.s. radius \(\sim 2.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the Continuum Shell Model (CSM) approach of Bartz et al. (various years) by using a realistic shell-model Hamiltonian for the \(Q\) subspace and a self-consistent determination of the average single-particle potential, addressing the description of weakly bound exotic nuclei near the drip lines. The simultaneous study of the mirror reactions \({}^{7}\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\({}^{8}\)B and \({}^{7}\)Li(n,\(\gamma\))\({}^{8}\)Li aims to reduce theoretical ambiguities that have plagued earlier calculations by Tombrello, Aurdal, Robertson, Descouvemont, Csoto, Brown, and Nunes (various years), and is motivated by the solar neutrino problem (Bahcall, various years) and primordial nucleosynthesis in inhomogeneous big-bang models (Applegate, Fuller, Malaney, Terasawa, various years). The results enable future extensions to include multi-nucleon decay channels following the approach of Balashov et al.
