# Structure of $^{71-78}$Ga isotopes in $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ and $fpg_{9/2}$ spaces

> We have performed comprehensive set of shell model calculations for Ga isotopes including high-spin states with three different effective interactions. This work will add more information in the earlier work by Cheal {\it et al.} for odd-even Ga isotopes [ Phys. Rev. Lett. 104, 252502 (2010)] and Mané {\it et al.} for odd-odd Ga isotopes [ Phys. Rev. C 84, 024303 (2011)], where only few excited states are studied in $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space. For lighter isotopes $fpg$ interaction is better and for heavier isotopes jj44b is quantitatively better than JUN45. These results show that limitation of existing interactions and calling for further improvements to predict nuclear structure properties of Ga isotopes.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations for Ga isotopes using three different effective interactions (JUN45, jj44b, and fpg) show that no single interaction reproduces all observed states across the isotopic chain: the fpg interaction works better for lighter isotopes (e.g., 71Ga), jj44b is quantitatively better for heavier isotopes (e.g., 73,75Ga), and all existing interactions have limitations that call for further improvements to predict nuclear structure properties in this region.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for 71-78Ga isotopes in two model spaces: the f5/2pg9/2 space (with 56Ni core) using the JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions, and the fpg9/2 space (with 48Ca core) using the fpg interaction of Sorlin et al. (2002). For the fpg9/2 space, a truncation was imposed allowing up to three particle excitations from the f7/2 orbital to the upper fp orbitals for protons and from the upper fp orbitals to the g9/2 orbital for neutrons. All calculations were performed using the ANTOINE code on a 20-node cluster.

## Key numbers

- For 71Ga, the 9/2+ band head is predicted at 2478 keV (JUN45), 1922 keV (jj44b), and experimentally observed at 1493 keV; the 13/2+ state is predicted within 50 keV of experiment by the fpg interaction.
- For 73Ga, the 1/2- state is predicted at 219 keV (JUN45), 91 keV (jj44b), and 505 keV (fpg), while experiment shows a 1/2-,3/2- doublet near ground state with the 3/2- at approximately 0.8 keV.
- For 75Ga, the 9/2+ band head is predicted at 2206 keV (JUN45), with experimental value at 1510 keV; the 13/2+ is at 2726 keV (JUN45) versus 2088 keV experiment.
- For 77Ga, the 13/2+ is predicted at 2724 keV (JUN45) and 2345 keV (jj44b).
- B(E2) values for 71Ga: 5/2- -> 3/2- transition is 6 e2fm4 (JUN45) and 5 e2fm4 (jj44b); 7/2- -> 5/2- is 222 e2fm4 (JUN45) and 209 e2fm4 (jj44b).
- B(E2) values for 73Ga: 9/2- -> 5/2- is 42 e2fm4 (JUN45) and 81 e2fm4 (jj44b); 13/2- -> 9/2- is 194 e2fm4 (JUN45) and 270 e2fm4 (jj44b).
- Quadrupole moment of 71Ga: +0.106(3) eb (experiment), +0.166 eb (fpg); for 75Ga: -0.285(17) eb (experiment), -0.338 eb (fpg).
- Magnetic moment of 71Ga: +2.56227(2) mu_N (experiment), +2.198 mu_N (fpg); for 73Ga: +0.209(2) mu_N (experiment), +0.039 mu_N (fpg).
- Occupancy of g9/2 orbital in 71Ga: 0.53 (JUN45), 0.83 (jj44b); in 77Ga first 9/2+ state: 0.96 (JUN45), 0.053 (jj44b).

## Context

This work extends the earlier shell-model studies of Cheal et al. (2010) for odd-even Ga isotopes and Mane et al. (2011) for odd-odd Ga isotopes, which only examined a few excited states below 1 MeV in the f5/2pg9/2 space. The inclusion of the f7/2 orbital in the fpg9/2 model space, as proposed by Cheal et al. (2010), enables better reproduction of quadrupole moments and magnetic moments, particularly the correct sign for 73Ga. The results show that the energy level systematics of Ga isotopes are quite similar to those in Cu isotopes, and the limitations of existing interactions call for further theoretical developments to produce a suitable interaction for neutron-rich nuclei in this "active-two-shell" region.
