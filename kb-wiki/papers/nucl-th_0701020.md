# Shell Model and Mean-Field Description of Band Termination

> We study nuclear high-spin states undergoing the transition to the fully stretched configuration with maximum angular momentum I_max within the space of valence nucleons. To this end, we perform a systematic theoretical analysis of non-fully-stretched I_max-2 and I_max-1 f_{7/2}^n seniority isomers and d_{3/2}^{-1} f_{7/2}^{n+1} intruder states in the A~44 nuclei from the lower-fp shell. We employ two theoretical approaches: (i) the density functional theory based on the cranked self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method, and (ii) the nuclear shell model in the full sdfp configuration space allowing for 1p-1h cross-shell excitations. We emphasize the importance of restoration of broken angular momentum symmetry inherently obscuring the mean-field treatment of high-spin states. Overall good agreement with experimental data is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that mean-field (cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock) descriptions of unfavored-signature terminating states in A~44 nuclei suffer from dramatic violation of rotational symmetry even for nearly spherical shapes, and that approximate angular momentum projection methods are necessary to bring the calculated energy differences between I_max and I_max-1 states into agreement with experiment and shell-model results.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic theoretical analysis of f_{7/2}^n seniority isomers and d_{3/2}^{-1} f_{7/2}^{n+1} intruder states in A~44 nuclei (20 ≤ Z ≤ N ≤ 24) using two complementary approaches: (i) the density functional theory based on the cranked self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (CHF) method with SLy4 and SkO functionals modified in their time-odd spin couplings, and (ii) the nuclear shell model in the full sdfp configuration space allowing for 1p-1h cross-shell excitations using the FPD6 interaction. For the CHF treatment of I_max-1 states, the authors developed two approximate angular momentum projection methods (A and B) to remove spurious components arising from the broken rotational symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The mean empirical energy difference ΔE ≡ E(I_max) - E₁(I_max-1) for d_{3/2}^{-1}f_{7/2}^{n+1} intruder states, averaged over all nuclei considered, is approximately 0.990 MeV.
- For the lowest CHF solutions corresponding to π̄-type configurations, the calculations yield average ΔE values of approximately 0.330 MeV (SLy4) and 0.730 MeV (SkO).
- Angular momentum correction method A shifts the CHF curves upward by δΔE ≈ 0.090 MeV (SLy4) and δΔE ≈ 0.140 MeV (SkO); method B gives δΔE ≈ 0.210 MeV (SLy4) and δΔE ≈ 0.270 MeV (SkO).
- The correlation energy in I_max-2 states, mainly associated with quadrupole effects, is calculated to be large, around 2 MeV.
- The mean particle-hole ΔJ_z = -2 excitation energy calculated in HF-SLy4 lies approximately 2 MeV below the data.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Zdunczuk et al. (2005) and Stoitcheva et al. (2006) on fully-stretched high-spin states in the A~44 mass region, which demonstrated that energy differences between terminating configurations are sensitive probes of time-odd spin couplings and spin-orbit strength in Skyrme functionals. The present paper systematically analyzes the non-fully-stretched I_max-2 and I_max-1 states, showing that approximate angular momentum projection is essential for mean-field descriptions of unfavored-signature terminating states, and that the agreement between shell-model calculations and experiment is excellent for both normal-parity and intruder configurations. The results enable further refinement of Skyrme energy density functionals, particularly their time-odd terms, and provide benchmarks for understanding the transition from collective rotation to single-particle motion at band termination.
