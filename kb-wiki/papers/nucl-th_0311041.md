# Adiabatic Heavy Ion Fusion Potentials for Fusion at Deep Sub-barrier Energies

> The fusion cross sections from well above barrier to extreme sub-barrier energies have been analysed using the energy (E) and angular momentum (L) dependent barrier penetration model ({\small{ELDBPM}}). From this analysis, the adiabatic limits of fusion barriers have been determined for a wide range of heavy ion systems. The empirical prescription of Wilzynska and Wilzynski has been used with modified radius parameter and surface tension coefficient values consistent with the parameterization of the nuclear masses. The adiabatic fusion barriers calculated from this prescription are in good agreement with the adiabatic barriers deduced from {\small{ELDBPM}} fits to fusion data. The nuclear potential diffuseness is larger at adiabatic limit, resulting in a lower $\hbarω$ leading to increase of "logarithmic slope" observed at energies well below the barrier. The effective fusion barrier radius and curvature values are anomalously smaller than the predictions of known empirical prescriptions. A detailed comparison of the systematics of fusion barrier with and without L-dependence has been presented.

## Key claim

The energy and angular momentum dependent barrier penetration model (ELDBPM) provides a consistent description of fusion cross sections from above barrier to extreme sub-barrier energies, and the adiabatic fusion barriers derived from experimental data agree with a modified Wilzynska-Wilzynski prescription only when using radius parameter and surface tension coefficient values consistent with nuclear mass parameterizations, rather than the original values used by Wilzynska and Wilzynski (2001).

## Method

The authors analyzed fusion excitation functions for 26 heavy ion systems with Z_p Z_t values from 400 to 1100 using the ELDBPM, which treats the effective fusion barrier as linearly decreasing with effective radial kinetic energy between sudden and adiabatic limits. They derived adiabatic fusion barriers (V_1) from experimental data and compared them to barriers calculated from the Wilzynska-Wilzynski prescription using two different sets of radius parameter (r_0) and surface tension coefficient (gamma) values. The analysis also included fits using the energy-dependent but angular-momentum-independent EDBPM and high-energy fits using the CCFUS program.

## Key numbers

- For the 58Ni+58Ni system, the ELDBPM best fit values are R_b = 8.75 fm and hbar-omega = 2.75 MeV, lower than the Wilzynska-Wilzynski prescription predictions
- For the 60Ni+89Y system, the ELDBPM best fit values are R_b = 8.0 fm and hbar-omega = 2.50 MeV
- The diffuseness parameters from the modified prescription range from 0.65 to 0.9 fm, compared to 0.90-1.20 fm from the original Wilzynska-Wilzynski prescription
- The percentage deviations of adiabatic barrier heights (V_1) from the modified prescription are within 2% for all systems studied
- The percentage deviations of sudden barrier heights (V_2) from the Vaz et al. (1981) prescription are up to 6%
- For the 36S+58Ni system, the ELDBPM yields R_b = 8.0 fm and hbar-omega = 4.0 MeV
- For the Ar+Sm systems, the optimized hbar-omega values are around 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jiang et al. (2002), who reported unexpected behavior of fusion cross sections at extreme sub-barrier energies, and Hagino et al. (2003), who attributed the increasing logarithmic slope to large diffuseness parameters. The authors show that the ELDBPM, originally developed by Mohanty et al. (1990, 1992), reproduces the deep sub-barrier fusion data and that the adiabatic barriers from the Wilzynska-Wilzynski prescription (2001) require modified radius and surface tension parameters consistent with the Moller and Nix (1995) mass compilation. The results enable systematic determination of adiabatic fusion barriers for heavy ion systems and provide a framework for understanding the isotopic dependence of fusion barriers.
