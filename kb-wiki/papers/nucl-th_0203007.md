# Charge and stability of strange quark matter at finite temperature

> By means of the quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model, it is found that the negative charge and the higher strangeness fraction are in favor of the stability of strange quark matter at finite temperature. A critical baryon number density n_Bc has been found. The strange quark matter is unstable when n_B < n_Bc. The charge and strangeness fraction dependence of stability for strange quark matter is addressed.

## Key claim

Using the quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model, the authors find that negative electric charge and a higher strangeness fraction both favor the stability of strange quark matter at finite temperature, but only above a critical baryon number density \(n_{Bc}\); below this density the system is unstable regardless of charge or strangeness.

## Method

The authors employ the QMDTD model, in which the bag constant \(B\) is a function of temperature (\(B(T)=B_0[1-(T/T_c)^2]\) for \(0\leq T\leq T_c\)), and the quark masses depend on both baryon number density and temperature. They treat infinite strange quark matter as a gas of noninteracting quarks and electrons, solve the coupled equations for chemical equilibrium (including weak processes that convert light quarks to strange quarks), and determine the critical baryon number density \(n_{Bc}\) below which no self-consistent solution exists. The stability is then mapped as a function of temperature, charge density \(Q\), and strangeness fraction.

## Key numbers

* Critical baryon number density for neutral strange quark matter at \(T=100\) MeV: \(n_{Bc}=8.28\times 10^{-3}\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* At this critical point, the strange quark fraction \(F_s=0.107\) (nonzero).
* For charge density \(Q=-0.2\ e\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) at \(T=100\) MeV, the critical density drops to \(n_{Bc}=5.02\times 10^{-4}\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* The bag constant at zero temperature is \(B_0=170\ \text{MeV}\ \text{fm}^{-3}\); the current strange quark mass is \(m_{s0}=150\ \text{MeV}\); the deconfinement critical temperature is \(T_c=170\ \text{MeV}\).
* At \(T=100\) MeV, the stable charge density range for strange quark matter is \(0.001\ e\ \text{fm}^{-3} > Q > -0.322\ e\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* In the QMDTD model, \(n_{Bc}=0\) at \(T=T_c=170\) MeV; in the QMDD model (constant \(B\)), \(n_{Bc}\) never reaches zero even above \(T_c\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier zero-temperature studies of strange quark matter stability, particularly the conflicting conclusions from the MIT bag model (Jaffe and co-workers, who found slightly positive charge; Greiner and co-workers, who found highly negative charge) and the QMDD model (Peng and co-workers, 1999). The authors use their own previously developed QMDTD model (Zhang and Su, 2001) to incorporate finite temperature effects, finding that negative charge and higher strangeness fraction stabilize strange quark matter, in agreement with Greiner and co-workers (1997) but now generalized to finite temperature. The results provide stability windows that may guide searches for strange quark matter in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments.
