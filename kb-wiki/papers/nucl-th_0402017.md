# Lepton interferometry in relativistic heavy ion collisions - a case study

> We propose intensity interferometry with identical lepton pairs as an efficient tool for the estimation of the source size of the expanding hot zone produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions. This can act as a complementary method to two photon interferometry. The correlation function of two electrons with the same helicity has been evaluated for RHIC energies. # Lepton interferometry in relativistic heavy ion collisions - a case study

## Key claim

Intensity interferometry with identical lepton pairs can serve as a complementary tool to two-photon interferometry for estimating the source size of the expanding hot zone in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the thermal shift of the rho meson mass has negligible effects on the extracted HBT radii, making lepton interferometry insensitive to in-medium vector meson modifications.

## Method

The authors evaluated the two-electron correlation function for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies using spin-dependent source functions for electrons produced from quark-antiquark annihilation and pion annihilation, with the invariant amplitudes calculated via standard field-theoretic techniques. A (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic model with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion was employed to evolve the system from an initial quark-gluon plasma phase through a mixed phase to a hadronic gas phase in a first-order phase transition scenario, and the correlation functions were parametrized with Gaussian forms to extract HBT radii.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_i = 264\) MeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, derived from a particle multiplicity per unit rapidity of 1100 and an initial time of 0.6 fm/c
- Critical temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 120\) MeV used in the hydrodynamic evolution
- HBT radii extracted for the sum (QGP+Mixed+Hadrons) phase: \(R_{inv} = 3.2\) fm, \(R_{out} = 6.0\) fm, \(R_{side} = 3.2\) fm, \(R_{long} = 3.3\) fm
- HBT radii for individual phases: QGP (\(R_{out}=3.5\) fm, \(R_{side}=3.5\) fm, \(R_{long}=0.6\) fm), Mixed (\(R_{out}=4.3\) fm, \(R_{side}=3.2\) fm, \(R_{long}=1.8\) fm), Hadron (\(R_{out}=6.5\) fm, \(R_{side}=3.0\) fm, \(R_{long}=3.0\) fm)
- The ratio \(R_{out}/R_{side} \sim 2\) for the sum phase, consistent with a first-order phase transition scenario

## Context

This work extends prior studies of two-photon interferometry by the same group (Alam et al., 2003) by incorporating spin-dependent source functions for lepton pairs, and it complements the extensive body of hadron HBT interferometry measurements at RHIC (NA35, HB7, HB8 collaborations) and theoretical work by Csorgo and collaborators. The finding that lepton interferometry is insensitive to in-medium vector meson mass modifications contrasts with the sensitivity of dilepton invariant mass distributions (Rapp, 1999; Alam et al., 2001), and the results enable future comparisons with two-photon correlation measurements already attempted by the WA98 collaboration.
