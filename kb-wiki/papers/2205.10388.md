# Magnetic dipole moments as a strong signature for $α$-clustering in even-even self-conjugate nuclei

> We investigate the magnetic dipole moments in even-even self-conjugate nuclei from ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ to ${}^{44}\mathrm{Ti}$. For the latter, the measured gyromagnetic factors of excited states turn out to assume the same value of $g \approx + 0.5$ within statistical errors. This peculiar feature can be interpreted on the basis of collective excitations of $α$-clusters. Analogously, the behaviour of the same observable is studied for all isotopes obtained by adding one or two neutrons to the considered self-conjugate nuclei. It is found that for the $N = Z + 1$ isotopes the $α$-cluster structure hardly contributes to the observed negative g- factor value, corroborating molecular $α$-cluster models. The addition of a further neutron, however, restores the original $α$-cluster g-factors, except for the semi-magic isotopes, in which the deviations from $g \approx + 0.5$ can be associated with the relevant shell closures. Secondly, we analyze the same observable in the framework of a macroscopic $α$-cluster model on a finite lattice of side length $L$. We focus on the discretization effects induced in the magnetic dipole moments of the $2_1^+$ and the $3_1^-$ states of ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ at different values of the lattice spacing $a$.

## Key claim

The measured gyromagnetic factors of excited states in even-even self-conjugate nuclei from 12C to 44Ti all cluster around g ≈ +0.5 within statistical errors, which the authors interpret as evidence for collective α-cluster excitations, though they acknowledge that small deviations (up to 10%) exist for some nuclei such as 16O and 24Mg that can be attributed to isospin mixing and meson-exchange current corrections.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental g-factor values for low-lying excited states of even-even N=Z nuclei from 8Be to 44Ti and compared them with shell-model calculations using USDB, WBT, and FPD6 interactions, as well as with the macroscopic α-cluster prediction g = 2mp/mα ≈ +0.5034. They extended the analysis to N=Z+1 and N=Z+2 isotopes, comparing experimental g-factors with Schmidt estimates and shell-model predictions to assess the contribution of α-clustering versus single-particle degrees of freedom. Additionally, they performed lattice calculations of magnetic dipole moments for the 2₁⁺ and 3₁⁻ states of 12C using a macroscopic α-cluster model on a finite cubic lattice to study discretization effects.

## Context

This work extends the Ikeda diagram concept (Ikeda 1968) of α-clustering emerging near breakup thresholds to magnetic dipole moments, providing a new observable signature for α-cluster structure in even-even self-conjugate nuclei. The authors fill a gap noted in earlier experimental work, such as the measurement of the 3₁⁻ state g-factor in 16O by Ashery et al. (1984), where α-cluster interpretations were marginalized. The results corroborate molecular α-cluster models for N=Z+1 nuclei and enable future tests of the macroscopic α-cluster picture, particularly for states near α-decay thresholds such as the 3₁⁻ and 2₂⁺ states of 12C and the 3₅⁻ and 5₁⁺ states of 16O, where even closer agreement with the g ≈ +0.5 prediction is expected.
