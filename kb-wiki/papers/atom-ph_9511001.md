# On the Use of Quantum Algebras in Rotation-Vibration Spectroscopy

> A two-parameter deformation of the Lie algebra u$_2$ is used, in conjunction with the rotor system and the oscillator system, to generate a model for rotation-vibration spectroscopy of molecules and nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a phenomenological model for simultaneously describing rotation and vibration spectra of molecules and nuclei using a two-parameter deformation of the Lie algebra u₂, but the authors explicitly state this work constitutes only a "first step" and that a second important step involving difference equations and quantum inverse scattering methods remains to be made.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian from two dynamical systems: a non-rigid rotor with the quantum algebra U_qp(u₂) and an anharmonic oscillator with the chain U_sr(u₂) ⊃ u₁. They diagonalize this Hamiltonian on a subspace spanned by rotation-vibration state vectors |n,v,j,m⟩, where the coupling between rotational and vibrational parts is achieved by setting the quantum number j₀ equal to the vibrational quantum number v. The resulting energy eigenvalues are expressed in terms of four quantum algebra parameters (q, p, s, r), which are then reduced to three real parameters β, γ, and τ under Hermiticity constraints.

## Key numbers

- The energy eigenvalues are given by E(n,v,j) = E_rovib (qp)^{v−j} [[j]]_qp [[j+1]]_qp + E_vib [[2v]]_sr [[2(n−v)]]_sr + E₀, where [[X]]_qp = (q^X − p^X)/(q−p) and [[X]]_sr = (s^X − r^X)/(s−r)
- Under the parameterization q = p* = exp(β cos γ) exp(+iβ sin γ) and s = r* = exp(τ cos γ) exp(+iτ sin γ), the energy becomes E(n,v,j) = E_rovib exp[(2v−1)β cos γ] {sin(jβ sin γ) sin[(j+1)β sin γ] / sin²(β sin γ)} + E_vib exp[(2n−2)τ cos γ] {sin(2vτ sin γ) sin[2(n−v)τ sin γ] / sin²(τ sin γ)} + E₀
- The limiting case γ = π/2 corresponds to decoupled rotational and vibrational parts, where only coefficients Y_0k and Y_l0 in the Dunham-type expansion are non-vanishing

## Context

This work extends previous one-parameter quantum algebra models for rotation spectra of nuclei (Iwao, 1990; Raychev et al., 1990) and rotation-vibration spectra of diatomic molecules (Bonatsos et al., 1991; Pan, 1993), as well as a two-parameter model applied to superdeformed nuclei (Barbier et al., 1994). The limiting case E_vib = 0 with q = p⁻¹ = e^{iβ} recovers the U_q(su₂) rotor model for nuclear rotational spectroscopy, while the case E_rovib = 0 with q = p⁻¹ = s = r⁻¹ = e^{iτ} recovers the U_q(u₂) ⊃ o₂ model for molecular vibrational spectra. The model enables future work on difference equations and quantum inverse scattering methods for understanding the dynamics of the U_qp(u₂) × U_sr(u₂) system.
