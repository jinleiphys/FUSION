# A pure S-wave covariant model for the nucleon

> Using the manifestly covariant spectator theory, and modeling the nucleon as a system of three constituent quarks with their own electromagnetic structure, we show that all four nucleon electromagnetic form factors can be very well described by a manifestly covariant nucleon wave function with zero orbital angular momentum. Since the concept of wave function depends on the formalism, the conclusions of light-cone theory requiring nonzero angular momentum components are not inconsistent with our results. We also show that our model gives a qualitatively correct description of deep inelastic scattering, unifying the phenomenology at high and low momentum transfer. Finally we review two different definitions of nuclear shape and show that the nucleon is spherical in this model, regardless of how shape is defined.

## Key claim

Using a manifestly covariant spectator theory, the authors show that all four nucleon electromagnetic form factors can be described by a nucleon wave function with zero orbital angular momentum, but they hedge that this conclusion depends on the formalism: the concept of "wave function" differs between the covariant spectator approach and light-cone theory, so the light-cone requirement of nonzero angular momentum components is not inconsistent with their results.

## Method

The nucleon is modeled as a system of three constituent quarks with their own electromagnetic structure, described by a manifestly covariant wave function built from scalar and vector diquark components in the covariant spectator formalism. The electromagnetic form factors are calculated using the relativistic impulse approximation, where the diquark is placed on its mass shell and the four-dimensional loop integral reduces to an integration over the three-momentum of the on-shell spectator diquark. The quark electromagnetic current includes both Dirac and Pauli terms with isoscalar and isovector combinations, and the nucleon body form factor factorizes from the quark form factors in the final expressions for \(G_E\) and \(G_M\).

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment: \(\mu_p = 1 + \kappa \simeq 3\) (with \(\kappa \sim 2\) from the simplest assumption \(\kappa_+ = \kappa_- = \kappa\))
- Extracted isoscalar and isovector anomalous moments: \(\kappa_+ = 3(\mu_p + \mu_n) - 1 = 1.639\), \(\kappa_- = \frac{3}{5}(\mu_p - \mu_n) - 1 = 1.823\)
- Extracted quark anomalous moments: \(\kappa_u = 1.778\), \(\kappa_d = 1.915\)
- The ratio \(R = G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}\) is predicted to be zero at \(Q^2 \simeq 2\) (GeV)\(^2\) in the naive case where \(f_{1+}=f_{1-}=f_1\) and \(f_{2+}=f_{2-}=f_2=\kappa f_1\) with \(\kappa \simeq 2\)
- The body form factor normalization condition: \(B(0) = \int_k \psi^2 = 1\)

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator formalism previously applied to the three-nucleon system by Gross (1969, 1982) and Stadler (1997), and builds on the wave function approach of Gross and Agbakpe (2006). It directly addresses the light-cone argument by Brodsky (1997) that the Pauli form factor \(F_2\) must be zero unless the wave function contains orbital angular momentum components \(L>0\). The model enables a unified description of nucleon electromagnetic form factors at both low and high momentum transfer, and provides a qualitatively correct description of deep inelastic scattering, bridging the phenomenology across momentum scales.
