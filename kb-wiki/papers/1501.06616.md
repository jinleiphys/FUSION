# (In-)Significance of the Anomalous Magnetic Moment of Charged Fermions for the Equation of State of a Magnetized and Dense Medium

> We investigate the effects of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) in the equation of state (EoS) of a system of charged fermions at finite density in the presence of a magnetic field. In the region of strong magnetic fields (eB>m^2) the AMM is found from the one-loop fermion self-energy. In contrast to the weak-field AMM found by Schwinger, in the strong magnetic field region the AMM depends on the Landau level and decreases with it. The effects of the AMM in the EoS of a dense medium are investigated at strong and weak fields using the appropriate AMM expression for each case. In contrast with what has been reported in other works, we find that the AMM of charged fermions makes no significant contribution to the EoS at any field value.

## Key claim

The anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of charged fermions makes no significant contribution to the equation of state of a magnetized and dense medium at any field value, contrary to claims in prior works that used the Schwinger AMM in the strong-field region where it is not valid.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop fermion self-energy in a constant magnetic field using Ritus's method to obtain the AMM separately for each Landau level in the strong-field limit (eB > m²), finding it depends on the Landau level index and decreases with it. They then calculated the thermodynamic potential including the AMM in both the strong-field and weak-field approximations, incorporating the renormalized vacuum contribution, zero-temperature finite-density term, and thermal term, and compared the AMM contribution against the Maxwell magnetic pressure and matter pressure.

## Key numbers

- Critical field for electrons: B_c^(e) = 4.4 × 10¹³ Gauss
- Critical field for u and d quarks (m_u = m_d = 5 MeV/c²): B_c^(u,d) = 4.4 × 10¹⁵ Gauss
- Critical field for protons (938 MeV/c²): B_c^(p) = 1.6 × 10²⁰ Gauss
- For the first Landau level at strong field, the AMM is T₁ ≈ m (α/8π) ln²(m²/2eB)
- For the second Landau level at strong field, T₂/T₁ ≈ 0.0668 for m̂ = 0.1
- The leading vacuum contribution in the strong-field approximation is Ω_vac^(S(R)) = -(α B²/6π) ln(eB/m²) + (eB/4π²) T₁² [ln(2eB/m²) + 2]

## Context

This work extends the calculation of the AMM in strong magnetic fields originally studied by Jancovici (year not given) and corrects inconsistencies in the treatment by Dittrich (year not given), who used the Schwinger AMM at arbitrary field strengths. The paper contradicts findings in works by Broderick (year not given) and others (EoS-AMM references) that reported significant AMM effects on the equation of state, showing those results arose from using the weak-field Schwinger AMM in the strong-field region and from neglecting the Maxwell magnetic pressure and vacuum contributions. This enables reliable calculations of magnetized neutron star equations of state without including AMM effects.
