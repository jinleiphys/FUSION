# Van der Waals forces and Photon-less Effective Field Theories

> In the ultra-cold regime Van der Waals forces between neutral atoms can be represented by short range effective interactions. We show that universal low energy scaling features of the underlying vdW long range force stemming from two photon exchange impose restrictions on an Effective Field Theory without explicit photons. The role of naively redundant operators, relevant to the definition of three body forces, is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that universal low-energy scaling features of van der Waals forces (originating from two-photon exchange) impose restrictions on an Effective Field Theory without explicit photons, and that a perfect matching between the van der Waals effective range and the EFT effective range for any scattering length is not possible.

## Method

The author analytically computes the effective range expansion for a pure \(C_6/r^6\) van der Waals potential, obtaining a universal scaling relation for the effective range \(r_0\) in terms of the van der Waals length \(R\) and the scattering length \(\alpha_0\). This relation is then compared to the effective range derived from a momentum-space EFT with contact interactions (up to \(C_2\) and including a redundant operator \(C_2'\)), solved via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a sharp cutoff \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

* The van der Waals length is defined as \(R = (M C_6/\hbar^2)^{1/4}\).
* For a pure \(C_6/r^6\) potential, the effective range scales as \(r_0/R = 1.395 - 1.333 (R/\alpha_0) + 0.6373 (R/\alpha_0)^2\).
* For many homonuclear diatomic systems, the dimensionless coefficients for higher-order corrections are \(g_1 \sim 10^{-2}\) and \(g_2 \sim 10^{-4}\).
* Causality arguments give a shortest possible range for an effective potential of \(r_c > 0.6 R\).
* For large scattering lengths (\(\alpha_0 \gg R\)), a square well potential yields \(M C_0^{\rm SW}/R = -14.41\) and \(M C_2^{\rm SW}/R^3 = 2.80\); a delta-shell potential yields \(M C_0^{\rm DS}/R = -13.15\) and \(M C_2^{\rm DS}/R^3 = 2.40\).
* For the EFT with \(C_2=0\) and \(\alpha_0 \gg R\), matching gives \(\Lambda R = 0.91\) and \(M C_0/R = -21.6\).
* For the EFT with \(C_2 \neq 0\) and \(\alpha_0/R = 10\), a stability plateau occurs at \(\Lambda \sim \pi/(2R)\), giving \(M C_0/R \sim -15\) and \(M C_2/R^3 \sim 2\).
* Off-shell two-body unitarity is violated for \(\Lambda R \ge 4\).
* Minimizing the difference between the EFT and van der Waals effective ranges gives a reasonable cutoff range of \(\Lambda R = 1.6-1.8 \sim \pi/2\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic effective range result for a pure \(C_6/r^6\) potential from Gao (1998) and PhysRevA.59.1998, and the more general scaling analysis of Cordon (2009) and Cordon (2010). It also builds on the EFT framework for ultra-cold atoms reviewed by Braaten (2004) and Platter (2009). The paper shows that the universal two-body scaling of van der Waals forces imposes restrictions on EFT without explicit photons, distinguishing between naively unitarily equivalent Hamiltonians, and these limitations are expected to affect the EFT analysis of three-body forces.
