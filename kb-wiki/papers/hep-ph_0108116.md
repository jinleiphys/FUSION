# New light on electromagnetic corrections to the scattering parameters obtained from experiments on pionium

> We calculate the electromagnetic corrections needed to obtain isospin invariant hadronic pion-pion s-wave scattering lengths a^0, a^2 from the elements a_cc, a_0c of the s-wave scattering matrix for the (π^+ π^-, π^0 π^0) system at the π^+ π^- threshold. These elements can be extracted from experiments on pionium. Our calculation uses energy independent hadronic pion-pion potentials that satisfactorily reproduce the low-energy phase shifts given by two-loop chiral pertur- bation theory. We also take into account an important relativistic effect whose inclusion influences the corrections considerably.

# New light on electromagnetic corrections to the scattering parameters obtained from experiments on pionium

## Key claim

The electromagnetic corrections needed to extract isospin-invariant hadronic pion-pion s-wave scattering lengths a^0 and a^2 from the experimentally accessible scattering matrix elements a_cc and a_0c (obtainable from pionium lifetime and level shift measurements) are calculated using a potential model that includes a relativistic factor f in the coupled Schrödinger equations; the authors find that the true hadronic mass of the pion in the isospin-invariant situation is very close to the neutral pion mass μ_0, and that the total electromagnetic correction Δ_{0c}(μ_0) is -0.055(5), which is sufficiently precise for the DIRAC experiment's planned accuracy.

## Method

The authors construct energy-independent hadronic pion-pion potentials V^0 and V^2 that reproduce the low-energy s-wave phase shifts from two-loop chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for both the charged pion mass μ_c and the neutral pion mass μ_0, using coupled relativised Schrödinger equations (RSEs) that include a relativistic factor f_c and f_0. These potentials are then inserted into a two-channel RSE model for the physical (π^+π^-, π^0π^0) system that includes the Coulomb potential for extended charge distributions, and the K-matrix elements a_cc and a_0c at the π^+π^- threshold are obtained by numerical integration and extrapolation. The electromagnetic corrections Δa_cc(μ) and Δ_{0c}(μ) are defined as the differences between the hadronic quantities (computed from the potentials with a common hadronic mass μ) and the physical scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic scattering lengths from two-loop ChPT: a^0(μ_c) = 0.3055 fm, a^0(μ_0) = 0.2930 fm, a^2(μ_c) = -0.0632 fm, a^2(μ_0) = -0.0614 fm
- Hadronic scattering lengths from the authors' potentials: a^0(μ_c) = 0.3004 fm, a^0(μ_0) = 0.2892 fm, a^2(μ_c) = -0.0632 fm, a^2(μ_0) = -0.0613 fm
- Physical scattering parameters from the full RSE calculation: a_cc = 0.1773 fm, a_0c = -0.1726 fm, a_00 = 0.0576 fm
- Electromagnetic corrections with hadronic mass μ_c: Δa_cc(μ_c) = 0.0018 fm, Δ_{0c}(μ_c) = -0.0068
- Electromagnetic corrections with hadronic mass μ_0 (the true corrections): Δa_cc(μ_0) = -0.0052 fm, Δ_{0c}(μ_0) = -0.0436
- Lowest-order ChPT calculation (Knecht and Urech) gives Δa_cc(μ_0) = -0.0139 fm, Δ_{0c}(μ_0) = -0.0533
- One-loop ChPT calculation gives Δ_{0c}(μ_0) = -0.0625
- Recommended total electromagnetic correction: Δ_{0c}(μ_0) = -0.055(5)
- Predicted pionium lifetime from combined results: τ = 2.9 fs
- Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler (2001) give a^0 - a^2 = 0.265(4) in units of μ_c^{-1}, leading to a_{0c}^h(μ_0) = -0.170(3) fm

## Context

This work extends and improves upon earlier potential-model calculations of electromagnetic corrections for pionium by Gashi et al. (Refs. [2,3] in the paper), which used more primitive double-square-well hadronic potentials and omitted the relativistic factors f_c and f_0. The inclusion of these relativistic factors, previously found important for the analogous (π^-p, π^0n) system, significantly influences the corrections. The results are compared with the field-theoretic ChPT calculations of Knecht and Urech (Ref. [17]), which treat electromagnetic effects via the low-energy expansion; the potential model provides a more precise determination of the Coulomb part of the correction, while the ChPT approach gives a more reliable estimate of the mass-difference correction. The work enables the DIRAC experiment at CERN to extract the hadronic scattering length a_{0c}^h(μ_0) from the measured pionium lifetime with an accuracy limited primarily by experimental error, and it emphasises that true hadronic information must be obtained with the neutral pion mass μ_0 as the hadronic mass parameter.
