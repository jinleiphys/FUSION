# Quantum expression for the electrical conductivity of massless quark matter and of the hadron resonance gas in the presence of a magnetic field

> We have studied the classical and quantum expressions of electrical conductivity and their numerical estimation in the presence of a magnetic field for hadron resonance gas (HRG) and massless quark matter. Classical results of transport coefficients of HRG matter in the presence of a magnetic field were studied previously by Dash et al. [Phys. Rev. D 102, 016016 (2020)] using the standard relaxation time approximation in the Boltzmann equation. In the same reference, the transition from isotropic transport coefficients to anisotropic coefficients in the presence of a magnetic field was also estimated for massless and HRG matter. This led to an upper limit or Stefan-Boltzmann (SB) type limit to the nonperturbative domain transition of transport coefficients. In a similar context, the present work has concentrated on the classical to quantum transition of HRG transport from the domain of high temperature and low magnetic field to that of low temperature and high magnetic field. We have also compared the quantum modification of HRG results with that of massless quark matter, where we observed an opposite trend. A similar kind of quantum effect is also noticed between mesons and baryons due to their different particle distribution functions. Despite the fact that HRG contains both mesons and baryons, Landau quantization of its net magnetothermodynamic phase space reveals meson- or boson-dominated quantum modification. That is why the quantum modification of HRG results reveals the opposite trend from that of massless quark matter, which faces fermionic quantum modification.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for both massless quark matter and hadron resonance gas (HRG) in a magnetic field, the classical (Boltzmann) expressions for electrical conductivity fail in the low-temperature, high-magnetic-field domain, while the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation also fails for the magnetic field strengths expected at RHIC and LHC (eB = 1-10 m_pi^2); a full Landau-level summation is required. The quantum modification of HRG results shows an opposite trend to that of massless quark matter because HRG is dominated by bosonic (meson) contributions rather than fermionic ones.

## Method

The authors derived classical (CL) expressions for the parallel (sigma_zz) and perpendicular (sigma_xx) components of electrical conductivity using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) in the Boltzmann equation, then extended them to quantum (QM) expressions by replacing the 3-momentum integration with a sum over Landau levels and a 1-dimensional momentum integral along the magnetic field direction. For the HRG model, they summed contributions from charged mesons (spin 0 and 1, treated as bosons) and charged baryons (spin 1/2 and 3/2, treated as fermions), using appropriate dispersion relations and spin degeneracy factors for each Landau level. They compared the normalized conductivity sigma/(tau_c T^2) as a function of temperature T and magnetic field eB for both massless quark matter and HRG matter.

## Key numbers

* For massless quark matter, the classical parallel conductivity is sigma_parallel = 8e^2 * zeta(2)/(3 pi^2) * tau_c T^2, giving a constant normalized value sigma_parallel/(tau_c T^2) as a reference (Stefan-Boltzmann-type limit).
* The magnetic field at RHIC for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is of order 10^19 Gauss, and at LHC for Pb-Pb collisions it is eB ~ 10^20 Gauss, which is much larger than Lambda_QCD^2 (approx 2 x 10^18 Gauss, where Lambda_QCD approx 0.2 GeV).
* For massless QGP at T = 0.150 GeV, the Landau quantization effect becomes noticeable beyond eB = 1 m_pi^2, and the LLL approximation is not valid within the range eB = 1-10 m_pi^2.
* The perpendicular conductivity for massless quark matter is sigma_perp = 12 * zeta(2)/(3 pi^2) * tau_c T^2 * sum_f (e_f^2 / (1 + (tau_c e_f B / (3T))^2)), where e_f = +2/3 e, -1/3 e, -1/3 e for u, d, s quarks.

## Context

This work extends the classical calculations of anisotropic transport coefficients in a magnetic field by Dash et al. (2020), who used the RTA Boltzmann equation for HRG and massless quark matter, by incorporating Landau quantization. It also goes beyond prior quantum calculations by Hattori et al. (2016), Kurian et al. (2017, 2018), and Rath et al. (2019) that used only the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation, which is shown to be insufficient for RHIC/LHC magnetic field strengths. The results enable more accurate modeling of electrical conductivity in heavy-ion collisions, which determines the lifetime of the magnetic field produced in such collisions, and provide a benchmark for future non-perturbative calculations.
