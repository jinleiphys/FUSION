# The One-Boson-Exchange Potential Model Approach

> A review is given of the present situation in YN scattering. Special attention is given to the handling of SU(3) in the various meson exchanges. The importance of the almost always ignored contribution of the Pomeron is reiterated.

## Key claim

This review argues that SU(3) flavor symmetry provides the essential theoretical framework for constructing hyperon-nucleon (YN) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) potential models from the well-known nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, but only if the NN model itself is constructed from the outset with SU(3) generalization in mind, a condition that excludes many popular NN models such as the Paris potential.

## Method

The authors survey the available YN scattering data (35 selected datapoints from experiments prior to 1971, plus hyperfragment data) and review the Nijmegen group's program of constructing NN potential models (hard-core, soft-core, and extended soft-core) that are explicitly built with SU(3) symmetry to enable generalization to YN and YY channels. They detail the SU(3) classification of meson nonets (pseudoscalar, vector, scalar) and baryon octets, the mixing angles for neutral mesons, the treatment of the Pomeron as a repulsive short-range contribution, and the phenomenological handling of the inner region via form factors or hard/soft cores.

## Key numbers

- The selected YN scattering dataset contains 35 datapoints, with 12 for elastic Λp scattering (momentum range 120–330 MeV/c, kinetic energy 6.5–50 MeV), 4 for Σ⁺p elastic (145–175 MeV/c, 9–13 MeV), and 6 each for Σ⁻p elastic, Σ⁻p→Σ⁰n, and Σ⁻p→Λn (142–168 MeV/c, 9–12 MeV).
- The ratio at rest r_R = Σ⁰/(Σ⁰+Λ) = 0.468(10) from stopped Σ⁻ capture on protons.
- For the Nijmegen soft-core model Nijm93, χ²/datapoint = 1.87 for all NN data below T_L = 350 MeV; the extended soft-core ESC model gives χ²/datapoint = 1.16 (17–350 MeV).
- The π⁰pp coupling constant is f_p² = 0.075, corresponding to g² = 13.56.
- The pseudoscalar mixing angle from the linear Gell-Mann-Okubo formula is θ_ps = −23°, from the quadratic formula θ_ps = −10.1°, with experimental value ∼ −20°.
- The vector meson ideal mixing angle is θ_v = 35.26°.
- The Nijmegen determination of (f/g)_ρ = 4.1 (close to the vector meson dominance value of 3.7).
- The scalar meson κ has mass M = 887 MeV (Svec, 1992); the predicted mass of the f₀′ (ε) from the diquark model is M = 690 MeV.

## Context

This paper reviews the Nijmegen group's long-standing program (de Swart 1971, 1988; Nagels 1975, 1978, 1979) of constructing NN potentials that are suitable for SU(3) generalization to YN and YY interactions, in contrast to the Paris potential (Lacombe 1980) which cannot be extended because it relies on πN scattering data unavailable for hyperons. The work extends earlier hard-core models (Nijmegen D and F) to soft-core models (Nijm78, Nijm89) and the recent extended soft-core ESC model, and emphasizes the previously neglected Pomeron contribution (de Swart 1988) as essential for obtaining reasonable ω-coupling constants and canceling large ε-contributions. The review enables future construction of YN and YY potentials from the ESC model and the Reid-like potentials (NijmI, NijmII, Reid93) that achieve χ²/datapoint = 1.03.
