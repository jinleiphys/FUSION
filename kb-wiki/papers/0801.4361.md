# Initial condition for hydrodynamics, partonic free streaming, and the uniform description of soft observables at RHIC

> We investigate the role of the initial condition used for the hydrodynamic evolution of the system formed in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and find that an appropriate choice motivated by the models of early-stage dynamics, specifically a simple two-dimensional Gaussian profile, leads to a uniform description of soft observables measured in the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). In particular, the transverse-momentum spectra, the elliptic-flow, and the Hanbury-Brown--Twiss correlation radii, including the ratio R_out/R_side as well as the dependence of the radii on the azimuthal angle (azHBT), are properly described. We use the perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with a realistic equation of state based on lattice calculations and the hadronic gas at high and low temperatures, respectively. We also show that the inclusion of the partonic free-streaming in the early stage allows to delay the start of the hydrodynamical description to comfortable times of the order of 1 fm/c. Free streaming broadens the initial energy-density profile, but generates the initial transverse and elliptic flow. The data may be described equally well when the hydrodynamics is started early, or with a delay due to partonic free-streaming.

## Key claim
A simple two-dimensional Gaussian initial energy-density profile, motivated by early-stage partonic dynamics, allows perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with a realistic equation of state to simultaneously describe transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii at RHIC, thereby resolving one of the "RHIC puzzles." The inclusion of partonic free-streaming can delay the start of hydrodynamics to times of order 1 fm/c without spoiling the agreement with data, provided the free-streaming time is not too large.

## Method
The authors use boost-invariant, inviscid (perfect-fluid) hydrodynamics with an equation of state combining lattice QCD results above 170 MeV and a hadron gas below 170 MeV, with a smooth crossover interpolation. The initial energy-density profile in the transverse plane is taken as a two-dimensional Gaussian whose width parameters (a and b) are matched to the results from the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo for each centrality class. For the free-streaming scenario, massless partons formed at τ₀ = 0.25 fm propagate along straight lines until a later proper time τ, where the energy-momentum tensor is computed and Landau matching is used to obtain the initial conditions for hydrodynamics. The hydrodynamic evolution runs until freeze-out at T_f = 145 MeV, after which hadrons and resonances are generated via the Cooper-Frye formalism and simulated with THERMINATOR.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamics: τ₀ = 0.25 fm (early start) or τ = 1 fm (with free-streaming delay)
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 145 MeV
- Equation of state crossover temperature: 170 MeV
- Shape parameters for centrality 0-5%: a = 2.65 fm, b = 2.90 fm
- Shape parameters for centrality 20-30%: a = 1.94 fm, b = 2.52 fm
- Shape parameters for centrality 20-40%: a = 1.78 fm, b = 2.45 fm
- Entropy conservation satisfied at relative level of 10⁻⁵ or better
- All soft observables (spectra, v₂, HBT radii) described within 10% or better

## Context
This work addresses the "RHIC puzzles" identified by Heinz (2002), Hirano (2004), Lisa (2005), and Huovinen (2006), where large elliptic flow values favored long evolution times while HBT radii indicated short times. The authors show that a Gaussian initial profile, rather than the Glauber-like profiles used in earlier hydrodynamic calculations, resolves this tension. The partonic free-streaming approach extends ideas from Sinyukov (2006) and Gyulassy (2007). The results enable future studies incorporating viscosity, departure from boost invariance, and more detailed early-stage dynamics from the Color Glass Condensate framework.
