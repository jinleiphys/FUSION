# Hard Thermal Loops and Beyond in the Finite Temperature World-Line Formulation of QED

> We derive the hard thermal loop action for soft electromagnetic fields in the finite temperature world-line formulation at imaginary time, by first integrating out the hard fermion modes from the microscopic QED action. Further, using the finite T world-line method, we calculate all static higher order terms in the soft electromagnetic field. At high T, the leading non-linear terms are independent of the temperature and, except for a term quartic in the time component of the vector potential, they cancel exactly against the vacuum contribution. The remaining T-dependent non-linear terms become more strongly suppressed by the temperature as the number of soft fields increases, thus making the expansion reliable. Applications of this method to other theories and problems at the soft scale are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors derive the hard thermal loop (HTL) effective action for soft electromagnetic fields in QED using the finite-temperature world-line formulation at imaginary time, and further compute all static higher-order nonlinear terms; the leading nonlinear terms at high temperature are independent of temperature and cancel against the vacuum contribution except for a term quartic in the time component of the vector potential, with the remaining temperature-dependent nonlinear terms becoming more strongly suppressed as the number of soft fields increases.

## Method

The authors split the fermion and gauge fields into hard and soft momentum components at a scale Λ satisfying eT ≪ Λ ≪ T, integrate out the hard fermion modes from the microscopic QED action, and rewrite the resulting one-loop determinant as a quantum-mechanical path integral in the finite-temperature imaginary-time world-line formalism. They then expand the world-line path integral in powers of the soft external gauge field to obtain the quadratic HTL effective action, and generalize the method to arbitrary order N in the static case to compute all higher-order nonlinear terms.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass: m_D² = e²T²/6, derived from the A₄-A₄ quadratic term in the HTL effective action
- Free fermion contribution to the effective action at T>0: -7π²T⁴V₄/180, where V₄ is the four-volume
- The A₄-A₄ quadratic term yields the result m_D² ∫ d⁴z [A₄(z)]², with m_D² = e²T²/6
- The spatial A_k-A_j quadratic term gives a contribution that cancels a magnetic mass-like term of e²T²/12, leaving a gauge-invariant HTL result proportional to e²T²/6

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature world-line formulation developed by McKeon (1992) and Sato (1993) to derive the HTL effective action, which was originally obtained diagrammatically by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and through effective Lagrangian approaches by Taylor and Wong (1990) and by Pisarski (1993). The method enables systematic computation of all static higher-order nonlinear terms beyond HTL, which are relevant for light-light scattering and photon splitting studies as investigated by Gies (2000) and by Brandt and Frenkel (1998, 1999), and the authors note that the world-line formalism provides an intuitive connection between classical kinetic theory and quantum field theory.
