# Contact representation of short range correlation in light nuclei studied by the High-Momentum Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> The high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (HMAMD) is a new promising framework with significant analytical simplicity and efficiency inherited from its antisymmetrized molecular dynamics in describing the high momentum correlations in various nuclear states. In the aim of further improving the numerical efficiency for the description of nucleon-nucleon correlation, we introduce a new formulation by including a new Gaussian weighted basis of high momentum pairs in the HMAMD wave function, with which very rapid convergence is obtained in numerical calculation. It is surprising that the very high-momentum components in the new HMAMD basis are found to be almost equivalent to the contact representation of the nucleon-nucleon pairs with very small nucleon-nucleon distance. The explicit formulation for the contact term significantly improves the numerical efficiency of the HMAMD method, which shows the importance of the contact correlation in the formulation of light nuclei.

# Contact representation of short range correlation in light nuclei studied by the High-Momentum Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

## Key claim

The very high-momentum components of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlation in light nuclei can be represented almost exactly by a contact term where two nucleons occupy the same spatial position, approximated by a delta function in their relative coordinate. This equivalence holds under the numerical condition that the product of the Gaussian range parameter ν and the variational parameter β satisfies ν × β ≈ 0.5, which emerges variationally rather than being imposed.

## Method

The authors introduce a new Gaussian-weighted basis into the High-Momentum Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (HMAMD) framework, where the shift parameter D of high-momentum nucleon pairs is integrated over with a Gaussian weight exp(−D²/β). They apply this formulation to the ³H nucleus using the Argonne V4′ central potential, which includes strong short-range repulsion, and compare results with the previous discrete HMAMD approach. The Gaussian parameter β is treated as a variational parameter and optimized for each calculation.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of ³H with AMD+Discrete+Gaussian wave function: −7.38 MeV, converging to the AMD+Discrete result of −7.40 MeV from Myo (2018)
- Optimized Gaussian parameter β = 2.22 fm² for AMD+Discrete+Gaussian wave function; β = 2.27 fm² for AMD+Gaussian wave function
- The product ν × β ≈ 0.5 holds for the optimized parameters (ν = 0.22 fm⁻², β = 2.22 fm² gives ν × β = 0.488)
- The width σ of the Gaussian in the relative coordinate of the contact pair is σ = 0.33 fm
- Number of basis states reduced from 15 (AMD+Discrete) to 8 (AMD+Discrete+Gaussian)
- Discrete HMAMD bases with D_z from 1 to 6 fm are sufficient when combined with the Gaussian basis, compared to D_z from 1 to 14 fm previously
- AMD basis alone gives energy +15.6 MeV; AMD+Gaussian gives −1.76 MeV

## Context

This work extends the HMAMD framework developed by Myo (2017, 2018) and the Tensor-Optimized AMD (TOAMD) approach by Myo (2015, 2017). The contact representation found here parallels the generalized contact theory of Weiss (2018), where nuclear wave functions are factorized in relative and center-of-mass coordinates at short distances. The new formulation enables future ab initio calculations with the AV8′ interaction (including both tensor and short-range repulsion) with significantly reduced computational cost, as the basis number is nearly halved compared to the discrete HMAMD method.
