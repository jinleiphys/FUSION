# Magnetic Moments of the Octet Baryons in the Colour-Dielectric Model

> Baryon magnetic moments are calculated in the colour-dielectric model with pion and kaon loops. The only free parameter of the model is determined from the nucleon isoscalar radius, and all SU(3) symmetry breaking, including that in the quark sector, is determined by mesonic masses and decay constants. Good agreement with experiment is obtained for the ratios of the magnetic moments, but the inclusion of kaons does not improve the results. The results obtained in this approach are significantly better than any that have been obtained in hedgehog-based models.

## Key claim

The colour-dielectric model, with pion and kaon loops included perturbatively, achieves good agreement with experimental ratios of octet baryon magnetic moments, but the inclusion of kaons does not improve the results and actually decreases the SU(3) splittings slightly. The results are significantly better than any obtained in hedgehog-based models, though the absolute scale of magnetic moments carries an uncertainty of up to 30% from the nucleon mass scale.

## Method

The authors calculate baryon magnetic moments in the colour-dielectric model using three-flavour quarks coupled to a scalar chiral singlet χ field, with perturbative inclusion of pion, kaon, and η meson loops in the cloudy bag formalism. The only free parameter (the χ-field mass Mχ) is determined by fitting the nucleon isoscalar charge radius, and all SU(3) symmetry breaking is governed by physical meson masses and decay constants (fK/fπ and mK). The magnetic moment receives three contributions: bare quark coupling, photon coupling to mesons in flight, and photon coupling to quarks with a virtual meson in flight, all calculated to second order in perturbation theory with wavefunction renormalisation.

## Key numbers

- The χ-field mass Mχ is determined by fitting the nucleon isoscalar charge radius rI, which ranges over 0.74 < rI < 0.85 fm experimentally, with values from 0.72 to 1.0 fm considered.
- The ratio of strange to non-strange quark coupling constants is cg = 2fK/fπ - 1 = 1.44 from experimental decay constants, with an enhanced value of 1.58 also studied.
- The bare soliton energy for the flavour-symmetric case is E = 1906 MeV for p=1 and E = 1773 MeV for p=2.
- The absolute proton magnetic moment varies from 1.96 at rI = 0.72 fm to 2.61 at rI = 1.0 fm (in nuclear magnetons).
- The best fit shows a maximum deviation of 0.27 and an average deviation of only 0.11 nuclear magnetons from experimental magnetic moments.
- The nucleon mass calculated from the model is 1227 MeV, about 30% above the experimental value.

## Context

This work extends the colour-dielectric model calculations of McGovern (1995) for octet and decuplet mass spectra to the magnetic moments of the same baryons. It directly parallels the cloudy bag model calculations of Theberge and Thomas (1982), but uses strict second-order perturbation theory with bare (soliton) masses rather than physical masses, and includes kaon and η loops in addition to pions. The results enable comparison with hedgehog-based models (Skyrme and related approaches by Chemtob, Nyman, Oh, and Park) which have all failed to improve on the additive quark model, and show that the colour-dielectric model performs better than the cloudy bag model with the same perturbative treatment.
