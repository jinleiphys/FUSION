# Coupled-cluster theory for strong entanglement in nuclei

> Atomic nuclei can exhibit shape coexistence and multi-reference physics that enters in their ground states, and to accurately capture the ensuing correlations and entanglement is challenging. We address this problem by applying single-reference coupled-cluster theory based on spherical and deformed reference states and the tailored coupled-cluster method. The latter combines configuration interaction to capture static correlations with coupled-cluster theory for dynamic correlations. We compute the atomic nuclei $^{12}$C, $^{28}$Si, and $^{56}$Ni and find that the tailored coupled-cluster method and the single-reference approach based on a deformed Hartree-Fock state yield the most accurate results.

## Key claim

The tailored coupled-cluster method and single-reference coupled-cluster theory based on a deformed (axially symmetric) Hartree-Fock state yield the most accurate results for nuclei with strong static correlations, but the tailored method requires a sufficiently large active space to capture deformation effects, and its results can overbind when the active space is too small.

## Method

The authors applied single-reference coupled-cluster theory using both spherical and deformed (axially symmetric) Hartree-Fock reference states, and the tailored coupled-cluster (TCC) method which combines full configuration interaction in an active space (to capture static correlations) with coupled-cluster theory for the external space (to capture dynamic correlations). They computed ground-state energies of ¹²C, ²⁸Si, and ⁵⁶Ni using shell-model Hamiltonians (GXPF1A for ⁵⁶Ni) and chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions (NNLO_opt and 1.8/2.0(EM)), with coupled-cluster approximations up to CCSDT-3 and CCSDT-1.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵⁶Ni with the GXPF1A interaction at ΔG = -2 MeV, the deformed Hartree-Fock state captures 5 MeV more binding than the spherical one; deformed CCSD captures about another 3 MeV; deformed CCSDT-1 is about 1 MeV away from full configuration interaction.
- For ⁵⁶Ni at ΔG = -2 MeV, the correlation energy from deformed and spherical coupled cluster is 3.4 and 4.5 MeV, respectively.
- For ¹²C with the NNLO_opt interaction, about 7 MeV of binding energy are still lacking at the CCSDT-3 result compared to experiment.
- For ²⁸Si with the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction, the deformed reference yields a gain of about 3 MeV in binding energy over the spherical reference.
- For ²⁸Si with the NNLO_opt interaction, calculations based on a deformed reference gain about 10 MeV in binding.

## Context

This work extends the study of static correlations in nuclei beyond symmetry-breaking approaches, following the shell-model coupled-cluster work of Sun (2018, 2021) and the observation by Horoi (2007) that single-reference coupled-cluster fails for sufficiently strong static correlations. The tailored coupled-cluster method, previously applied in quantum chemistry by Stolarczyk (1994) and Kinoshita (2005a), is here applied to nuclear physics for the first time for these nuclei. The results enable future hybrid quantum-classical algorithms and show that single-reference methods with deformed references can be competitive with more complex multi-reference approaches, while also identifying the need for larger active spaces to capture deformation in nuclei like ²⁸Si.
