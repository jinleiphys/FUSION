# Probabilistic picture of in-medium jet evolution

> We briefly discuss the recently developed probabilistic picture for in-medium jet evolution that is driven by independent multiple scatterings and branchings. These are controlled by the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$. In this framework, large radiative corrections to $p_\perp$-broadening of partons in the jet, enhanced by a double logarithm (DL) of the medium size $L$, are recovered. We argue that these non-local corrections are universal and can be reabsorbed in a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter without spoiling the probabilistic picture. As a consequence, we find that for large media, the mean radiative energy loss result scales as $L^{2+γ}$, where the anomalous dimension $γ=2\sqrt{α_sN_c/π}$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that large double-logarithmic radiative corrections to in-medium jet evolution, which initially appear to spoil the probabilistic picture of independent multiple scatterings and branchings, are universal and can be reabsorbed into a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\), thereby preserving the probabilistic framework.

## Method

The author analyzes radiative corrections to the 2-point function (describing transverse momentum broadening) and to the 3-point function (describing the BDMPS radiative spectrum) in a QCD medium modeled by a classical random field with instantaneous correlations. By showing that the logarithmic divergence in the gluon fluctuation time \(\tau\) allows the fluctuation to be treated as effectively instantaneous, the radiative correction is absorbed into a redefinition of the dipole cross section and hence of \(\hat q\). A renormalization group equation for \(\hat q(\tau, \k^2)\) is then derived to resum the double-logarithmic corrections to all orders.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order BDMPS mean energy loss scales as \(\langle\omega\rangle \sim \alpha_s C_R \,\hat q\, L^2\).
* The renormalized jet quenching parameter at double-logarithmic accuracy is \(\hat q_\text{R}(t) \simeq \hat q \left(1 + \frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2\pi} \ln^2 \frac{t}{\tau_0}\right)\).
* After resummation, the quenching parameter scales as \(L^\gamma\) with anomalous dimension \(\gamma = 2\sqrt{\alpha_s N_c / \pi}\).
* The resummed mean radiative energy loss scales as \(L^{2+\gamma}\) for large media.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Zakharov formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation (BDMPS, Zakharov) by incorporating radiative corrections to transverse momentum broadening first identified by Wu (2011) and Liou, Mueller, and Wu (2013). It enables a systematic resummation of double-logarithmic corrections to jet quenching observables, showing that the probabilistic picture of independent emissions (BDIM) remains valid after renormalization of \(\hat q\). The resulting energy loss scaling \(L^{2+\gamma}\) interpolates between the standard weak-coupling result \(L^2\) and the strong-coupling result \(L^3\) from \({\cal N}=4\) SYM theory (Hatta, 2007).
