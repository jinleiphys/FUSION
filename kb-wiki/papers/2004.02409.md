# Solitons in nuclear time-dependent density functional theory

> The soliton existence in sub-atomic many-nucleon systems is discussed. In many nucleon dynamics represented by the nuclear time-dependent density functional formalism, much attention is paid to energy and mass dependence of the soliton existence. In conclusion, the existence of nuclear soliton is clarified if the temperature of nuclear system is from 10 to 30 MeV. With respect to the mass dependence $^{4}$He and $^{16}$O are suggested to be the candidates for the self-bound states exhibiting the property of nuclear soliton.

## Key claim
The paper claims that nuclear solitons exist in sub-atomic many-nucleon systems described by time-dependent density functional theory, but only if the nuclear system temperature lies in the range from 10 to 30 MeV. The claim is conditional on this specific temperature window, and the nuclei 4He and 16O are suggested as candidate self-bound states that may exhibit soliton properties.

## Method
The author analyzes the mathematical structure of nuclear time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) with Skyrme-type effective interactions, comparing it to known soliton equations such as the nonlinear Schrodinger equation and Korteweg-de Vries equation. The similarity between the TDDFT master equation and the nonlinear Schrodinger equation is established by examining the dominance of the t0 and t3 Skyrme parameter terms in the binding energies of light nuclei, particularly 4He and 8He. The existence of two-soliton solutions in one-dimensional bosonic models with delta-force interactions is reviewed as supporting evidence for soliton behavior in the nuclear context.

## Key numbers
- The temperature range for nuclear soliton existence is from 10 to 30 MeV.
- The experimental binding energy of 4He is 28.30 MeV and of 8He is 31.40 MeV.
- With only the t0 term, the calculated binding energy of 4He is approximately 1000 MeV; adding the t3 term reduces it to 63.80 MeV; including the t1 term further reduces it to 27.71 MeV; the full calculation including all terms gives 27.73 MeV.
- The t0 and t3 terms dominate the interaction part of the TDDFT with the SV-bas model at approximately 63.9 percent.
- The Skyrme parameter set SV-bas includes t0 = -1879.640018 MeV·fm^3, t1 = 313.7493427 MeV·fm^5, t2 = 112.6762700 MeV·fm^5, t3 = 12527.38921 MeV·fm^(3+3α), W0 = 124.6333000 MeV·fm^5, and α = 0.3.

## Context
This work extends the concept of solitons from classical and quantum field theory into nuclear many-body physics, building on the mathematical framework of nonlinear Schrodinger and Korteweg-de Vries equations studied by Zabusky and Kruskal (1965) and earlier work on solitary waves by Scott-Russel (1848). The paper connects to the nuclear TDDFT formalism developed by Dirac (1930) and Engel (1975), and specifically uses the Skyrme interaction parameterization from Vautherin and Brink (1972) with the SV-bas parameter set from Klupfel (2009). The findings enable future investigation of nuclear soliton properties in heavy-ion collisions and potential applications in nuclear engineering for beam preservation and nuclear waste transmutation.
