# Multi-channel analog of the effective-range expansion

> Similarly to the standard effective range expansion that is done near the threshold energy, we obtain a generalized power-series expansion of the multi-channel Jost-matrix that can be done near an arbitrary point on the Riemann surface of the energy within the domain of its analyticity. In order to do this, we analytically factorize its momentum dependencies at all the branching points on the Riemann surface. The remaining single-valued matrix functions of the energy are then expanded in the power-series near an arbitrary point in the domain of the complex energy plane where it is analytic. A systematic and accurate procedure has been developed for calculating the expansion coefficients. This means that near an arbitrary point in the domain of physically interesting complex energies it is possible to obtain a semi-analytic expression for the Jost-matrix (and therefore for the S-matrix) and use it, for example, to locate the spectral points (bound and resonant states) as the S-matrix poles.

## Key claim

The authors derive a generalized power-series expansion of the multi-channel Jost matrix that can be performed near an arbitrary complex energy point on the Riemann surface, not only near the threshold energy as in the standard effective-range expansion. The expansion is made possible by analytically factorizing the momentum dependencies at all branching points, leaving single-valued matrix functions that can be expanded in a power series.

## Method

The authors start from the coupled-channel radial Schrödinger equation and reformulate it as a system of first-order differential equations for the Jost matrices using the variable-phase approach. They then factorize the odd-power momentum dependencies from the Jost matrix elements, transforming to new matrices \(\tilde{\mathcal{A}}\) and \(\tilde{\mathcal{B}}\) that are single-valued functions of energy. These single-valued matrices are expanded in a power series of \((E-E_0)\) around an arbitrary complex energy \(E_0\), and the full Jost matrix is reconstructed by multiplying back the analytic momentum factors.

## Key numbers

* The Riemann surface for an \(N\)-channel problem consists of \(2^N\) sheets, each corresponding to a different combination of signs of the \(N\) channel momenta.
* The Riccati-Bessel function series representation is given by \(j_\ell(kr) = (kr/2)^{\ell+1} \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n \sqrt{\pi}}{\Gamma(\ell+3/2+n)n!} (kr/2)^{2n}\).
* The Riccati-Neumann function series representation is given by \(y_\ell(kr) = (2/kr)^{\ell} \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n+\ell+1}}{\Gamma(-\ell+1/2+n)n!} (kr/2)^{2n}\).

## Context

This work extends the single-channel generalized effective-range expansion (Rakityansky 2009) to multi-channel problems, and generalizes earlier multi-channel effective-range approaches (Ross 1961, Ross and Shaw 1961, Nath 1964, Kermode 1965, Sprung 1965, Rosenberg 1971) that were limited by adherence to cotangent-phase-shift formulations. The method enables locating bound and resonant states as poles of the S-matrix by expanding the Jost matrix near arbitrary complex energies, and provides a systematic procedure for calculating expansion coefficients without the singularities that plague the variable-phase approach for potentials supporting bound states.
