# Role of Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion on the crust of magnetars within the nuclear energy density functional theory

> Magnetic fields of order $10^{15}$ G have been measured at the surface of some neutron stars, and much stronger magnetic fields are expected to be present in the solid region beneath the surface. The effects of the magnetic field on the equation of state and on the composition of the crust due to Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion are studied. Both the outer and inner crustal regions are described in a unified and consistent way within the nuclear-energy density functional theory.

## Key claim
The paper claims that Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion significantly alters the composition and equation of state of magnetar crusts, but these effects are most prominent in the strongly quantizing regime where electrons occupy only the lowest Landau-Rabi level; the effects become negligible at densities above approximately 0.01 fm^-3, where the equation of state matches that of unmagnetized neutron stars.

## Method
The authors calculated the equilibrium composition and equation of state of both the outer and inner crusts of magnetars using the nuclear energy density functional theory with the BSk24 functional. For the outer crust, they minimized the Gibbs free energy per nucleon at fixed pressure using experimental atomic masses from the 2016 Atomic Mass Evaluation supplemented by the HFB-24 mass model. For the inner crust, they employed the fourth-order extended Thomas-Fermi method with Strutinsky integral theorem (ETFSI) and proton shell and pairing corrections, minimizing the energy per nucleon at fixed mean baryon number density, with electron contributions modified to account for Landau-Rabi quantization.

## Key numbers
- Surface density for B_star=3000: mean baryon number density at stellar surface n_s = 3.72 x 10^-6 fm^-3
- Neutron-drip pressure for B_star=3000: P_drip = 1.74 x 10^-3 MeV fm^-3, compared to 4.87 x 10^-4 MeV fm^-3 in the absence of magnetic field
- Neutron-drip density for B_star=3000: n_drip = 4.45 x 10^-4 fm^-3, compared to 2.56 x 10^-4 fm^-3 in the absence of magnetic field
- For B_star=1000, the maximum pressure for the 56Fe layer is P_max = 2.00 x 10^-7 MeV fm^-3 with maximum density n_max = 2.63 x 10^-6 fm^-3
- The characteristic magnetic field strength B_rel = 4.41 x 10^13 G
- The lowest Landau-Rabi level is fully populated at electron Fermi energy mu_e = 39.6 MeV for B_star=3000
- The inner crust equation of state matches the unmagnetized case at densities above approximately 0.01 fm^-3

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same group (Chamel et al., 2012; Chamel et al., 2017) on magnetar outer crusts by incorporating updated experimental atomic masses from the 2016 Atomic Mass Evaluation and extending calculations to the inner crust using the same nuclear functional BSk24. It contradicts earlier Thomas-Fermi calculations by Nandi et al. (2011) that predicted significant inner crust effects only at extremely high fields (B_star=10^4) but neglected nuclear shell effects. The results enable more accurate modeling of magnetar seismic frequencies and cooling, as these properties are sensitive to crustal composition.
