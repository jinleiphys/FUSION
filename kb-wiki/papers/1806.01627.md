# Two-photon exchange correction in elastic lepton-proton scattering

> We present the dispersion relation approach based on unitarity and analyticity to evaluate the two-photon exchange contribution to elastic electron-proton scattering. The leading elastic and first inelastic $πN$ intermediate state contributions are accounted for in the region of small momentum transfer $Q^2 < 1~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ based on the available data input. The novel methods of analytical continuation allow us to exploit the MAMI form factor data and the MAID parameterization for the pion electroproduction amplitudes as input in the calculation. The results are compared to the recent CLAS, VEPP-3 and OLYMPUS data as well as to the full two-photon exchange correction in the near-forward approximation, which is based on the Christy and Bosted unpolarized structure functions fit. Additionally, predictions are given for a forthcoming muon-proton scattering experiment.

## Key claim

The dispersion relation approach, which accounts for elastic and pion-nucleon intermediate states, provides a data-driven evaluation of the two-photon exchange correction to elastic lepton-proton scattering at low momentum transfer (Q² < 1 GeV²), but the description of experimental data still shows a 2-3 sigma difference for some data points even after including the πN contribution.

## Method

The author uses fixed-Q² dispersion relations based on unitarity and analyticity to evaluate the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction as a sum of contributions from elastic and πN intermediate states. The imaginary parts of TPE amplitudes are obtained from experimental input: MAMI form factor data for the elastic contribution and the MAID2007 parameterization for pion electroproduction amplitudes. For the unphysical region at larger Q², the author performs analytical continuation by fitting the Q² dependence of the imaginary parts and extrapolating, with the theoretical error estimated from the difference between four-parameter and six-parameter fits. A separate near-forward calculation approximates the hadronic part as unpolarized doubly-virtual Compton scattering using the Christy-Bosted fit for unpolarized structure functions.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed for momentum transfer Q² < 1 GeV²
- At Q² = 0.05 GeV² and Q² = 0.25 GeV², the total TPE correction is compared with the Feshbach result, Born TPE, and the empirical fit from the A1 Collaboration
- The dispersive integral for πN contribution is evaluated entirely from the physical region for Q² ≤ 0.624 GeV²
- The boundary between physical and unphysical regions for the analytical continuation example occurs at Q²_ph ≈ 0.329 GeV²
- For the MUSE experiment, the TPE correction in muon-proton scattering is 2-3 times smaller than the corresponding correction in electron-proton scattering
- The inelastic contributions in the near-forward approximation are an order of magnitude below the resulting TPE for muon-proton scattering

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Tomalak (2015) and Borisyuk (2008) by including both elastic and πN intermediate states, and provides the first estimates of the TPE correction for the forthcoming MUSE muon-proton scattering experiment. The results are compared with experimental data from the CLAS, VEPP-3, and OLYMPUS collaborations, as well as with the empirical TPE fit from the A1 Collaboration (Bernauer, 2013). The calculation enables more precise extraction of the proton magnetic radius and form factors from elastic scattering data, and provides input for the analysis of muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting measurements by the CREMA, FAMU, and J-PARC collaborations.
