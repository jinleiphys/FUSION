# Nuclear modification of $B_c$ mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions based on a linear Boltzmann transport model

> The nuclear modification factor ($R_\mathrm{AA}$) of $B_c$ mesons in high-energy nuclear collisions provides a novel probe of heavy quark interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Based on a linear Boltzmann transport model that incorporates both Yukawa and string types of interactions between heavy quarks and the QGP, we study the production and evolution of heavy quarks and $B_c$ mesons within the same framework. A $B_c$ bound state dissociates while one of its constituent heavy quarks scatters with the QGP with momentum transfer greater than its binding energy. The medium-modified charm and bottom quarks can recombine into $B_c$ mesons, and the medium-modified bottom quarks can also fragment to $B_c$ mesons. We find that most primordial $B_c$ mesons generated from the initial hard collisions dissociate inside the QGP. The production of $B_c$ mesons is primarily driven by the recombination mechanism at low transverse momentum and fragmentation at high transverse momentum. The string interaction dominates over the Yukawa interaction in the nuclear modification of $B_c$ mesons. The participant number dependence of the $B_c$ meson $R_\mathrm{AA}$ is determined by the complicated interplay between the heavy quark yield, energy loss, and the QGP volume. We obtain a reasonable description of the $R_\mathrm{AA}$ of $B_c$ mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, and provide predictions for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV.

# Nuclear modification of $B_c$ mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions based on a linear Boltzmann transport model

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor ($R_\mathrm{AA}$) of $B_c$ mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV is primarily driven by recombination of medium-modified charm and bottom quarks at low transverse momentum and by bottom quark fragmentation at high transverse momentum, with the non-perturbative string interaction dominating over the perturbative Yukawa interaction in the nuclear modification, but the authors note that most primordial $B_c$ mesons from initial hard collisions dissociate inside the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors extended the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model, previously developed for open heavy quarks and jets, to bound states of heavy quarks by incorporating both Yukawa (perturbative) and string (non-perturbative) interactions between heavy quarks and the quark-gluon plasma. The dissociation of $B_c$ mesons was treated via the quasifree dissociation method, where a bound state dissociates when a constituent heavy quark scatters with a thermal parton with momentum transfer exceeding the binding energy; the medium-modified charm and bottom quarks then recombine into $B_c$ mesons via an instantaneous coalescence model, while bottom quarks can also fragment to $B_c$ mesons using a fragmentation function fitted to CMS data. The QGP medium background was generated using the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc.

## Key numbers

- The dissociation temperature of $B_c(1S)$ is 420 MeV and of $B_c(1P)$ is 260 MeV, extracted from in-medium spectral functions evaluated using the T-matrix method.
- The fragmentation function normalization constant $N=0.002$ was determined from CMS data at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, indicating approximately 0.3% of bottom quarks fragment to $B_c$ mesons.
- The Yukawa coupling strength was set to $\alpha_\mathrm{s}=0.27$ and the string tension to $\sigma=0.45$ GeV$^2$, with screening masses parameterized as $m_d=a+bT$ ($a=0.20$ GeV, $b=2.0$) and $m_s=\sqrt{a_s+b_s T}$ ($a_s=0$, $b_s=0.10$ GeV).
- The charm and bottom quark masses were set to $m_c=1.27$ GeV and $m_b=4.19$ GeV, with the hadronization temperature $T_h=165$ MeV.
- The branching fractions applied were $B(B_c^- \to J/\psi \mu^- \bar{\nu}) \approx 1.95\%$ and $B(J/\psi \to \mu^+ \mu^-) \approx 5.96\%$, with a $p_\mathrm{T}$ shift $p_\mathrm{T}^{\mu\mu\mu}\approx 0.85\, p_\mathrm{T}^{B_c}$.

## Context

This work extends the LBT model (Cao 2016, Luo 2023) previously applied to open heavy quarks and jets, now incorporating bound states of heavy quarks. It builds on prior transport model studies of $B_c$ mesons by Zhao (2022) and Wu (2023), which used Boltzmann equation and kinetic-rate equation approaches respectively, and on the statistical hadronization model by Zhao (2024). The instantaneous coalescence model extends earlier work on single-heavy-quark hadron formation (Fries 2008, Greco 2003, Cao 2019). The results provide a simultaneous description of the nuclear modification of both single-heavy-quark mesons and $B_c$ mesons, enabling future comparisons with CMS data at LHC energies and predictions for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies.
