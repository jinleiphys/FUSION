# Selfconsistent calculations of spectral densities in the O(N) model: improving the Hartree-Fock approximation by including nonzero decay widths

> We study the linear sigma model with O(N) symmetry at nonzero temperature in the framework of the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism. Extending the set of two-particle irreducible diagrams by adding sunset diagrams to the usual Hartree-Fock contributions, we derive a new approximation scheme which extends the standard Hartree-Fock approximation by the inclusion of nonzero decay widths. In this approximation, in-medium modifications of the meson masses as well as decay and collisional broadening effects are selfconsistently taken into account. As compared to the standard Hartree-Fock approximation, the temperature for the chiral symmetry restoring phase transition is lowered by \~20%. For large temperatures, the spectral densities of the sigma-meson and the pion become degenerate, as required for the chirally symmetric phase.

## Key claim

By extending the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action to include sunset diagrams alongside the usual Hartree-Fock double-bubble diagrams, the authors derive a self-consistent approximation scheme that incorporates nonzero decay widths for mesons; the chiral symmetry restoring phase transition temperature is lowered by approximately 20-25% compared to the standard Hartree-Fock approximation, but this result depends on the chosen ultraviolet cutoff and the neglect of the real parts of the sunset self-energy diagrams.

## Method

The authors apply the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism to the O(N) linear sigma model at nonzero temperature, truncating the sum of 2PI diagrams to include both double-bubble (Hartree-Fock) and sunset topologies. They rewrite the Dyson-Schwinger equations as self-consistent integral equations for the spectral densities of the sigma-meson and pion, where the imaginary parts of the self-energies arise solely from the sunset diagrams and are computed using the spectral densities themselves. The real parts of the self-energies are taken only from the tadpole (Hartree-Fock) contributions, neglecting the real parts of the sunset diagrams, and ultraviolet divergences are regulated with a momentum cutoff.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry restoring phase transition temperature is lowered by approximately 20-25% compared to the standard Hartree-Fock approximation.
* The vacuum pion mass is taken as M_pi = 139.5 MeV and the scalar condensate (pion decay constant) as sigma = f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
* The sigma-meson vacuum mass is set to M_sigma = 600 MeV.
* The numerical grid uses a spacing of 10 MeV in both energy and momentum directions.

## Context

This work extends the Hartree-Fock approximation previously studied in chiral effective theories such as the O(N) and U(N_f) models by Baym (1977), Bochkarev (1995), Roh (1996), Amelino-Camelia (1997), Petropoulos (1998), Lenaghan (1999, 2000), and Roder (2003). It addresses the limitation that the Hartree-Fock approximation yields only real, momentum-independent self-energies and thus cannot describe finite particle lifetimes. The inclusion of nonzero decay widths enables the self-consistent calculation of spectral densities with collisional broadening, which is necessary for a realistic description of in-medium meson properties. The approach enables future studies of how finite-width effects modify transport coefficients and the nature of the chiral phase transition in effective models of QCD.
