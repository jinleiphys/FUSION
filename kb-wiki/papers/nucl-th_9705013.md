# Spin-Isospin Structure and Pion Condensation in Nucleon Matter

> We report variational calculations of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, using the new Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. At the equilibrium density of 0.16 fm^-3 the two-nucleon densities in symmetric nuclear matter are found to exhibit a short-range spin-isospin structure similar to that found in light nuclei. We also find that both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter undergo transitions to phases with pion condensation at densities of 0.32 fm^-3 and 0.2 fm^-3, respectively. Neither transtion occurs with the Urbana v14 two-nucleon interaction, while only the transition in neutron matter occurs with the Argonne v14 two-nucleon interaction. The three-nucleon interaction is required for the transition to occur in symmetric nuclear matter, whereas the the transition in pure neutron matter occurs even in its absence. The behavior of the isovector spin-longitudinal response and the pion excess in the vicinity of the transition, and the model dependence of the transition are discussed.

## Key claim

Variational calculations using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions show that both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter undergo transitions to phases with pion condensation, but the transition in symmetric nuclear matter requires the three-nucleon interaction to occur, whereas the transition in pure neutron matter occurs even without it; the transitions are model-dependent, as they do not occur with the Urbana v14 interaction and only the neutron matter transition occurs with the Argonne v14 interaction.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations using hypernetted chain summation techniques (VCS, previously denoted FHNC-SOC) with a wavefunction consisting of symmetrized products of pair correlation operators acting on a Fermi gas wavefunction. The correlation operators were determined from Euler-Lagrange equations minimizing the two-body cluster contribution, with three variational parameters (α, d_c, d_t) optimized by energy minimization. Many-body cluster contributions were calculated using chain summation methods, and second-order two-particle two-hole contributions from correlated basis perturbation theory were added perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.16 fm^-3
- Transition density for pion condensation in symmetric nuclear matter: 0.32 fm^-3
- Transition density for pion condensation in pure neutron matter: 0.2 fm^-3
- Minimum energy calculated for symmetric nuclear matter with A18+IX Hamiltonian: -12 MeV (compared to empirical value of -16 MeV)
- Contribution of Model IX three-nucleon interaction exceeds that of Model VII by 8.2 MeV to SNM energy and 4.5 MeV to PNM energy at ρ=0.32 fm^-3
- For U14 Hamiltonian at k_F=1.33 fm^-1: ⟨V^2π⟩ = -3.49 MeV, ⟨V^R⟩ = 3.99 MeV
- For A18+IX Hamiltonian at k_F=1.33 fm^-1: ⟨V^2π⟩ = -3.60 MeV, ⟨V^R⟩ = 6.33 MeV
- For PNM with A18 interaction alone (no three-nucleon force), the transition occurs at approximately 3.5 ρ_0 (0.56 fm^-3)

## Context

This work extends the variational calculations of Wiringa, Fiks and Fabrocini (WFF, 1988) who found a transition in PNM at ρ∼0.24 fm^-3 with Argonne v14 but not with Urbana v14, and found no evidence of a similar transition in SNM. The present study uses the improved Argonne v18 interaction (Wiringa, Stoks and Schiavilla) and Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction (Pudliner et al.), whose parameters were determined from exact Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei. The results confirm the earlier suggestion by WFF that the high-density phase includes pion condensation, and show that the Fujita-Miyazawa two-pion exchange three-nucleon interaction is necessary for the transition in symmetric nuclear matter. The findings have implications for neutron star cooling and evolution, as noted by Pethick.
