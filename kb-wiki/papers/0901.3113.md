# Deep inelastic scattering and factorization in the 't Hooft Model

> We study in detail deep inelastic scattering in the 't Hooft model. We are able to analytically check current conservation and to obtain analytic expressions for the matrix elements with relative precision O(1/Q^2) for 1-x >> β^2/Q^2. This allows us to compute the electron-meson differential cross section and its moments with 1/Q^2 precision. For the former we find maximal violations of quark-hadron duality, as it is expected for a large N_c analysis. For the latter we find violations of the operator product expansion at next-to-leading order in the 1/Q^2 expansion.

## Key claim

The paper analytically checks current conservation and obtains analytic expressions for deep inelastic scattering matrix elements in the 't Hooft model with relative precision O(1/Q^2) for 1-x >> beta^2/Q^2, finding that the operator product expansion is violated at next-to-leading order in the 1/Q^2 expansion due to non-local matrix elements arising from constructive interference between non-analytic oscillating terms.

## Method

The authors compute the full non-perturbative forward Compton scattering amplitude in the 't Hooft model (two-dimensional QCD in the large N_c limit) using the light-cone quantization frame and the semiclassical (WKB) solution of the 't Hooft equation for highly excited meson states. They expand the forward Compton tensor in the deep Euclidean domain with 1/Q^2 precision and compare this expansion to what would be obtained from the operator product expansion, identifying contributions that cannot be expressed as local operator matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The relative precision of the analytic expressions for matrix elements is O(1/Q^2) for the kinematic region 1-x >> beta^2/Q^2
- The bound state mass spectrum for large excitations is M_n^2 = pi^2 beta^2 n + (m_{i,R}^2 + m_{j,R}^2) ln n + 3pi^2/4 + C(m_{i,R}^2/beta^2) + C(m_{j,R}^2/beta^2) + O(1/n)
- The boundary-layer integrals yield int_0^inf dxi phi_i(xi)/xi = pi beta/m_i and int_0^inf dxi phi_i(xi) = pi m_i/beta at leading order
- The 1/n corrections to the integral int_0^1 dx phi_n^{ij}(x)/x include terms proportional to (m_{i,R}^2 + m_{j,R}^2)/(2n pi^2 beta^2) and (m_i m_j)/(n pi^2 beta^2)(-1)^n

## Context

This work extends previous tests of the operator product expansion in the 't Hooft model, which had been checked at lowest order for the vacuum polarization and DIS off a meson by Callan, Coote, and Gross (1976) and Einhorn (1976). The authors had previously checked the OPE numerically at next-to-leading order for the vacuum polarization in Mondejar and Pineda (2008). The present paper finds violations of the OPE at next-to-leading order in the 1/Q^2 expansion for DIS, which contradicts the expectation that the OPE should hold at this order. This result enables further study of quark-hadron duality violations in processes where perturbative factorization techniques typically neglect such effects entirely.
