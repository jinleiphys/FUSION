# Branching ratios for deexcitation processes of daughter nuclei following invisible dinucleon decays in $^{16}$O

> Various theories beyond the standard model of particle physics predict the existence of baryon number violating processes resulting in nucleon decay. When occurring within an atomic nucleus, such a decay will be followed by secondary decays of the daughter nucleus unless its ground state is directly populated. In this paper, we estimate branching ratios for processes associated with dinucleon decays of the $^{16}$O nucleus. To this end, we use a simple shell model for the ground state of $^{16}$O. For decays from the 1$s_{1/2}$ configuration, which result in highly excited states in the daughter nucleus, we employ a statistical model with the Hauser-Feshbach theory. Our analysis indicates that the branching ratio for gamma-ray emission in the energy range between 5 and 9 MeV, which is relevant to low-threshold water Cherenkov experiments such as SNO+, is 4.53%, 35.7%, and 20.2% for the $nn$, $pp$, and $pn$ decays in $^{16}$O, respectively. In particular, emission of 6.09 MeV and 7.01 MeV gamma-rays from $^{14}$C, and 6.45 MeV and 7.03 MeV gamma-rays from $^{14}$N, have branching ratios as large as 10.9%, 20.1%, 7.73% and 8.90%, respectively.

## Key claim

The branching ratios for gamma-ray emission in the 5–9 MeV energy range following invisible dinucleon decays in 16O depend strongly on the decay channel (nn, pp, or pn), with the pp channel producing the largest branching ratio in that region (35.7%) due to discrete gamma-ray lines from bound states in 14C, while the nn channel yields only 4.53% in the same energy range because its excited daughter states predominantly decay via proton emission rather than gamma emission.

## Method

The authors used a simple shell model for the 16O ground state, with nucleons occupying the 1s1/2, 1p3/2, and 1p1/2 orbitals. For dinucleon decays from the 1p1/2 and 1p3/2 configurations, they assigned experimentally known decay properties of the daughter nuclei (14O, 14C, 14N). For decays from the 1s1/2 configuration, which produce highly excited daughter states, they employed the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model implemented in the TALYS code, using a Breit-Wigner strength distribution with width Γ = 7 MeV and the Gilbert-Cameron level density.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ejiri (1993) and Kamyshkov (2003), who calculated branching ratios for single-nucleon decay modes in 16O, to the previously unstudied case of dinucleon decays (nn, pp, and pn) in the same nucleus. The results are directly relevant to low-threshold water Cherenkov experiments such as SNO+, which can search for invisible nucleon decay in 16O using the 5–9 MeV gamma-ray window where backgrounds are low. The branching ratios provided here, combined with those from earlier single-nucleon decay studies, enable experimental collaborations to set lower limits on the lifetimes for invisible dinucleon decay modes.
