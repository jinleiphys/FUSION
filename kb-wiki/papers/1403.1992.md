# "Buddha's Light" of Cumulative Particles

> We show analytically that in the cumulative particles production off nuclei multiple interactions lead to a glory-like backward focusing effect. Employing the small phase space method we arrived at a characteristic angular dependence of the production cross section $dσ\sim 1/ \sqrt {π- θ}$ near the strictly backward direction. This effect takes place for any number $n\geq 3 $ of interactions of rescattered particle, either elastic or inelastic (with resonance excitations in intermediate states), when the final particle is produced near corresponding kinematical boundary. Such a behaviour of the cross section near the backward direction is in qualitative agreement with some of available data. Explanation of this effect and the angular dependence of the cross section near $θ\sim π$ are presented for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors show analytically that multiple interaction processes (MIP) in cumulative particle production off nuclei produce a glory-like backward focusing effect, with the cross section scaling as \(d\sigma \sim 1/\sqrt{\pi - \theta}\) near the strictly backward direction, but only for processes with \(n \geq 3\) interactions and when the final particle is produced near the corresponding kinematical boundary.

## Method

The authors employ the small phase space method, developed previously in Kopeliovich (1982) and Kopeliovich (1985), to calculate the probability of multiple interaction processes leading to cumulative particle production. They expand the angular variables (azimuthal deviations \(\varphi_k\) and polar deviations \(\vartheta_k\)) around the optimal kinematics where all scattering angles equal \(\theta/N\), integrate the resulting quadratic forms, and derive the phase space integrals analytically for both light particle rescattering (e.g., pions) and nucleon rescattering cases.

## Key numbers

- The angular dependence of the production cross section near the strictly backward direction is \(d\sigma \sim 1/\sqrt{\pi - \theta}\).
- For elastic rescatterings of light particles, the maximal final energy after \(N\) interactions is \(\omega_N^{max} \simeq N (2m/\theta^2) + m/(6N)\) (Eq. 2.7), valid for \(N \geq 2\).
- For nucleon rescattering, the maximal final momentum is \(k_N^{max} \simeq N (2m/\theta^2) - m/(3N)\) (Eq. 2.13), with preasymptotic corrections twice as large as for light particles.
- Normal Fermi motion of nucleons inside the nucleus (\(p_F^{max}/m \simeq 0.27\)) widens the kinematical boundaries for MIP by about 40% (Eq. 2.14).
- The formation time of the first produced particle in cumulative processes is \(\tau_1^{form} \sim 1/m\) (Eq. 2.24), indicating quasiclassical character.
- For the deuteron, the intermediate mass at the absolute kinematical boundary is \(M_1^D(max) \sim E_0 m/2\) (Eq. 2.18).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of multiple interaction processes leading to cumulative particle production, first investigated by Kondratyuk and Kopeliovich (1979) for pion production off deuterons and later by Kopeliovich (1982, 1985) for nucleon production. The backward focusing effect (called "Buddha's light" or nuclear glory) was observed experimentally at JINR (Dubna) by Baldin (1977) and at ITEP (Moscow) by Leksin's group (foc-2), and the present paper provides the first analytical proof of its existence and the explicit angular dependence near \(\theta = \pi\). The results enable quantitative comparison with existing data and clarify that the effect is an intrinsic property of the MIP mechanism, not requiring exotic nuclear structure explanations.
