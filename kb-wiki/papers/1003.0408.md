# The NJL model of dense three-flavor matter with axial anomaly: the low temperature critical point and BEC-BCS diquark crossover

> We study the QCD phase structure in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, incorporating the interplay between the chiral and diquark condensates induced by the axial anomaly. We demonstrate that for an appropriate range of parameters of the model, the interplay leads to the low temperature critical point in the phase structure predicted by a previous Ginzburg-Landau analysis. We also show that a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of diquark molecules emerges in the intermediate density region, and as a result, a BEC-BCS crossover is realized with increasing quark chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the axial anomaly-induced coupling between chiral and diquark condensates in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model produces a low-temperature critical point in the QCD phase diagram, but only for sufficiently large chiral-diquark coupling strength \(K'\) exceeding a critical value (e.g., \(K'_c \approx 4.1 K\) for massless quarks and \(K'_c \approx 3.8 K\) for quarks with mass 5.5 MeV).

## Method

The authors employ the three-flavor NJL model with a six-fermion Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) interaction and an additional six-fermion term coupling chiral and diquark condensates, which breaks \(\U(1)_A\) symmetry. They work at the mean-field level, linearizing the interactions to obtain the thermodynamic potential as a function of the chiral condensate \(\chi\) and diquark condensate \(s\), then numerically minimize this potential to map the phase diagram in the \((\mu, T)\)-plane for two parameter sets (massless and finite-mass quarks) with varying \(K'\) strength.

## Key numbers

* For massless quarks (case I), the critical chiral-diquark coupling is \(K'_c = 4.1 K\) (with \(K\Lambda^5 = 12.36\) and cutoff \(\Lambda = 602.3\) MeV), above which the first-order chiral transition becomes a crossover.
* For finite-mass quarks with \(m_q = 5.5\) MeV (case II), the critical coupling is \(K'_c = 3.8 K\).
* At \(T=0\) in case I, the BEC-BCS crossover line \(\mu = M(\mu,T)\) ends at \(\mu = 286.6\) MeV; in case II, it ends at \(\mu = 297.8\) MeV.
* The onset of BEC at \(T=0\) in case II occurs at \(\mu = 279.2\) MeV, determined by the condition \(M_D - 2\mu = 0\).
* The vacuum dynamical quark mass is 355.2 MeV (case I) and 367.6 MeV (case II); the chiral condensate is \(-240.4\) MeV (case I) and \(-241.9\) MeV (case II).

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of Hatsuda, Yamamoto, and collaborators (2006, 2007), which predicted a low-temperature critical point in three-flavor QCD based on model-independent arguments, by confirming its existence within a concrete NJL model with realistic parameters. The BEC-BCS crossover of diquark pairing, previously discussed in nonrelativistic condensed matter by Eagles (1969), Leggett (1980), and Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink (1985), and in relativistic systems by Abuki (2002), Nishida and Abuki (2005), and others, is shown here to be induced by the axial anomaly. The results enable future studies of the transition from diquark matter to nuclear matter, and extensions incorporating charge neutrality, \(\beta\)-equilibrium, and the Polyakov loop for confinement effects.
