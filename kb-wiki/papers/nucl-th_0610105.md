# Universal Properties of the Four-Body System with Large Scattering Length

> Few-body systems with large scattering length have universal properties that do not depend on the details of their interactions at short distances. We study the universal bound state properties of the four-boson system with large scattering length in an effective quantum mechanics approach. We compute the four-body binding energies using the Yakubovsky equations for positive and negative scattering length. Moreover, we study the correlation between three- and four-body energies and present a generalized Efimov plot for the four-body system. These results are useful for understanding the cluster structure of nuclei and for the creation of weakly-bound tetramers with cold atoms close to a Feshbach resonance.

## Key claim

The four-body system with large scattering length can be renormalized at leading order without a four-body parameter, using only two-body and three-body contact interactions, but this conclusion is restricted to cutoff variations within a factor of approximately 22.7 where the number of three-body bound states remains constant.

## Method

The authors solved the Yakubovsky equations for four identical bosons using an effective quantum mechanics approach with Gaussian-regulated contact interactions. The two-body coupling was fixed from the dimer binding energy (for positive scattering length) or from the scattering length (for negative scattering length), while the three-body coupling was determined by fixing one three-body binding energy. The four-body binding energies were then computed for both positive and negative scattering lengths without introducing any four-body parameter.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor for the three-body system is approximately 22.7, corresponding to exp(π/s₀) where s₀ ≈ 1.00624
- The constant c in the limit cycle expression for the three-body coupling is 0.074 ± 0.003
- Away from thresholds, the ground state four-body binding energy is related to the deepest three-body state by a factor of approximately 5
- Away from thresholds, the excited state four-body binding energy is related to the deepest three-body state by a factor of approximately 1.01
- Subsequent three-body states differ in binding energy by factors of approximately 515 at infinite scattering length

## Context

This work extends the study of Platter (2004) on four-boson systems with large positive scattering length to include negative scattering lengths and to map out the full scattering length dependence of the four-body spectrum in a generalized Efimov plot. It addresses a controversy with Yamashita et al. (2006), who found sensitivity to a four-body subtraction constant and argued a four-body parameter should enter at leading order. The results enable predictions for weakly-bound tetramers in cold atom experiments near Feshbach resonances and for understanding cluster structure in nuclei, and are consistent with Monte Carlo calculations by Hannah and Blume (2006) for up to 10 bosons.
