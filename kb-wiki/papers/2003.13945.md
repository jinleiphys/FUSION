# Topological term, QCD anomaly, and the eta' chiral soliton lattice in rotating baryonic matter

> We study the ground states of low-density hadronic matter and high-density color-flavor locked color superconducting phase in three-flavor QCD at finite baryon chemical potential under rotation. We find that, in both cases under sufficiently fast rotation, the combination of the rotation-induced topological term for the eta' meson and the QCD anomaly leads to an inhomogeneous condensate of the eta' meson, known as the chiral soliton lattice (CSL). We find that, when baryon chemical potential is much larger than isospin chemical potential, the critical angular velocity for the realization of the eta' CSL is much smaller than that for the pi_0 CSL found previously. We also argue that the eta' CSL states in flavor-symmetric QCD at low density and high density should be continuously connected, extending the quark-hadron continuity conjecture in the presence of the rotation.

## Key claim

In three-flavor QCD at finite baryon chemical potential under sufficiently fast rotation, a new type of chiral soliton lattice (CSL) of the eta' meson is realized, and when baryon chemical potential is much larger than isospin chemical potential, the critical angular velocity for this eta' CSL is much smaller than that for the pi_0 CSL found previously by Huang et al. (2017).

## Method

The authors constructed low-energy effective theories for two regimes: chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for low-density hadronic matter, and an effective theory for the high-density color-flavor locked (CFL) phase. They derived a topological term for the eta' meson from the chiral vortical effect (CVE) matching condition, combined it with the QCD anomaly (U(1)_A anomaly) and quark mass terms, and analytically solved the resulting equations of motion to find the ground-state configurations and critical angular velocities for the eta' CSL.

## Key numbers

- Critical angular velocity for eta' CSL in the chiral limit: Omega_eta'^0 = (8 pi f_eta' sqrt(6a)) / mu_B^2, where a is the strength of the U(1)_A anomaly and f_eta' is the eta' decay constant.
- Critical angular velocity for eta' CSL with finite quark masses: Omega_eta' = (6 pi f_eta'^2 / mu_B^2) * integral from 0 to 2pi of dtheta sqrt(A_1 sin^2(theta/2) + A_2 sin^2(3theta/2)), with A_1 = 4B tr(M)/(3f_eta'^2) and A_2 = 2a/(3f_eta'^2).
- Critical angular velocity for pi_0 CSL: Omega_pi_0 = (8 pi m_pi f_pi^2) / (mu_B |mu_I|), showing Omega_eta' << Omega_pi_0 when mu_B >> |mu_I|.
- In the CFL phase, the decay constants at high density are f_tilde_pi^2 = (21-8 ln 2)/18 * mu_B^2/(18 pi^2) and f_tilde_eta'^2 = mu_B^2/(24 pi^2), with velocities v_tilde_pi^2 = v_tilde_eta'^2 = 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the previous finding of a pi_0 CSL in two-flavor QCD at finite mu_B and mu_I under rotation by Huang et al. (2017) to the eta' meson in three-flavor QCD at finite mu_B alone. It also connects to the broader literature on CSLs in chiral magnets (Dzyaloshinsky, 1964; Togawa et al., 2012), cholesteric liquid crystals (de Gennes), and QCD in magnetic fields (Brauner et al., 2016). The authors argue that the eta' CSL states at low and high density should be continuously connected, extending the quark-hadron continuity conjecture of Schafer and Wilczek (1999), Hatsuda et al. (2006), and Yamamoto et al. (2007) to rotating systems.
