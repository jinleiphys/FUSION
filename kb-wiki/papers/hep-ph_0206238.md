# Thermal rho and sigma mesons from chiral symmetry and unitarity

> We study the temperature evolution of the rho and sigma mass and width, using a unitary chiral approach. The one-loop pion-pion scattering amplitude in Chiral Perturbation Theory at finite temperature is unitarized via the Inverse Amplitude Method. Our results predict a clear increase with T of both the rho and sigma widths. The masses decrease slightly for high T, while the rho-pion-pion coupling increases. The rho behavior seems to be favored by experimental results. In the sigma case, it signals chiral symmetry restoration.

# Thermal rho and sigma mesons from chiral symmetry and unitarity

## Key claim

Using only chiral symmetry and unitarity (via the Inverse Amplitude Method applied to one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory at finite temperature), the thermal widths of the rho and sigma mesons increase with temperature while their masses decrease slightly, but the analysis is limited to temperatures where the dilute gas approximation holds (roughly T < 200 MeV for the rho and T < 180 MeV for the sigma), and the results assume the resonances are at rest with the thermal bath.

## Method

The one-loop ππ scattering amplitude in Chiral Perturbation Theory at finite temperature was unitarized via the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM), which corresponds to the [1,1] Padé approximant of the chiral expansion in powers of momenta, masses, or temperature over fπ². The thermal amplitude was analytically continued to the complex plane to find the pole positions of the ρ (I=J=1) and σ (I=J=0) resonances in the second Riemann sheet, using the thermal phase space σ_T(s) = σ(s)[1 + 2 n_B(√s/2)] where n_B is the Bose-Einstein distribution.

## Key numbers

- At T=0, the ρ mass M₀ = 770 MeV and width Γ₀ = 159 MeV from the real-axis IAM analysis (δ₁₁ = 90° condition)
- At T=0, the ρ pole position gives M₀ = 755 MeV and Γ₀ = 152 MeV from the complex-plane continuation
- At T=0, the ρππ coupling g₀ ≈ 6.2 from the narrow resonance approximation
- At T = 100 MeV, the ρ mass from the real-axis IAM is M₁₀₀ ≈ 775.5 MeV (a slight increase from T=0)
- At T = 50 MeV, the ρππ coupling ratio g₅₀/g₀ ≈ 0.9991 (a slight decrease)
- The validity limit for the ρ is set by 2 n_B(M_ρ/2) < 1, giving T < 300 MeV, but results are shown only up to 200 MeV where 2 n_B(M_ρ/2) ≈ 0.3
- The validity limit for the σ is T < 180 MeV from the condition 2 n_B(M_σ/2) ≈ 1
- The one-loop SU(2) ChPT validity is estimated to reach about T ≈ 150 MeV, but unitarization extends this range

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature ππ scattering amplitude calculated to one loop in Chiral Perturbation Theory by the same authors (Dobado et al., 2002) by unitarizing it via the IAM, which at T=0 successfully reproduces scattering data up to 1 GeV and generates ρ and σ poles (IAM by Dobado et al., Truong, and others). The results agree with earlier model calculations showing increasing ρ width from stimulated emission (Pisarski, Weldon, 1993-1995) and small mass changes (Dey et al., 1990; Gale and Kapusta, 1991), but provide a first-principles chiral treatment without introducing the ρ as an explicit degree of freedom. The decreasing σ mass with temperature is interpreted as a signal of chiral symmetry restoration, consistent with expectations from chiral symmetry (Bochkarev and Kapusta, 1996). The work enables more rigorous treatment of dilepton spectra in heavy ion collisions, where the flatness observed by HELIOS and CERES collaborations has been attributed to ρ modification in hot hadron gas.
