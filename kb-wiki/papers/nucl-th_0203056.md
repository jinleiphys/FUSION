# Continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation and the time dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach

> Quadrupole excitations of neutron-rich nuclei are analyzed by using the linear response method in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA). The QRPA response is derived starting from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations. The residual interaction between the quasiparticles is determined consistently from the two-body force used in the HFB equations, and the continuum coupling is treated exactly. Calculations are done for the neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. It is found that pairing correlations affect the low-lying states, and that a full treatment of the continuum can change the structure of the states in the giant resonance region.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first continuum QRPA calculations where the single-particle spectrum and the residual interaction are both determined from the same effective two-body force, but the residual interaction in the particle-hole channel is approximated by its Landau-Migdal limit rather than being fully derived from the Skyrme force, breaking exact self-consistency and requiring renormalization of the interaction to eliminate the spurious center-of-mass state.

## Method

The authors derive the QRPA response function in coordinate space from the small-amplitude limit of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations, obtaining a generalized Bethe-Salpeter equation for the QRPA Green function. The ground state is calculated using the continuum HFB approach with a Skyrme interaction (SLy4) for the mean field and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force, and the QRPA Green function is constructed using real-energy solutions of the continuum HFB spectrum. The residual interaction in the particle-hole channel is approximated by its Landau-Migdal form, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved numerically for quadrupole excitations of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Pairing interaction parameter V0 = -415.73 MeV.fm^3 with a quasiparticle cutoff energy of 50 MeV, using density parameter rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3 and alpha = 1.5
- The spurious J^pi = 1^- state comes out at zero energy for a renormalization factor alpha = 0.80
- Energy-weighted sum rules are satisfied within 1% to 5%
- The first 2^+ state in 24O is located at 4.0 MeV, compared to 5.0 MeV in a previous calculation by Ma et al. (2001) using a Woods-Saxon potential
- The B(E2) value for 24O is predicted smaller than those of lighter isotopes, supporting the 2s1/2 subshell closure effect
- The Mn/Mp ratio for 24O is more than twice the N/Z value

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA approach of Bertsch (1975), Liu and Giai (1976), and Giai and Sagawa (1981) to include pairing correlations within a fully self-consistent framework, building on earlier attempts by Hamamoto and Sagawa (1982) and Ma et al. (2001) that treated the continuum only partially or used inconsistent ground states. The calculations enable systematic studies of giant resonances and low-lying states in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, and the results for oxygen isotopes show that a full continuum treatment is necessary for giant resonances while low-lying states are sensitive to pairing correlations.
