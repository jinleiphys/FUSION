# New angular momentum coupling method based on Wigner rotation theory

> We present a new method for constructing the total angular momentum of many-nucleon states. We find that the restrictions imposed by the fermion antisymmetry on the total state are fully absorbed into the single-j space when the broken rotational symmetry of the product state is restored by angular momentum projection. For different j-shells, any total angular momentum that obeys the selection rule is allowed, just as for non-identical particles. The method based on this reorganization is conceptually different from the traditional J and m schemes and may help to improve the efficiency of angular momentum coupling in nuclear many-body calculations.

# New angular momentum coupling method based on Wigner rotation theory

## Key claim

The restrictions imposed by fermion antisymmetry on the total angular momentum of many-nucleon states are fully absorbed into the single-j space when broken rotational symmetry is restored by angular momentum projection; for different j-shells, any total angular momentum obeying the selection rule is allowed as if coupling were performed on non-identical particles, which the authors claim is conceptually different from traditional J and m schemes.

## Method

The authors use angular momentum projection (AMP) operators to extract good total angular momentum components from product states (Slater determinants) that break rotational symmetry. They prove a theorem showing that the matrix element of the projection operator for multi-shell product states factorizes into a sum over products of single-shell projection matrix elements multiplied by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, using the block-diagonal structure of the rotation operator matrix in the many-body basis. The method is demonstrated for single-j and multi-j systems, with the rotation operator matrix elements computed as determinants of submatrices of the Wigner d-function.

## Key numbers

- For a single j=7/2 shell with 4 fermions, the product state basis has dimension C(8,4)=70
- The record dimension for Hamiltonian matrices in large-scale shell model calculations is reported as 10^10 or larger (citing Mizusaki 2010, Brown and Rae 2014, Shimizu 2020)
- For a fully-occupied j-shell, the determinant of the d-function matrix equals 1, showing the state has J=0
- For a fully-paired state, the angular momentum distribution integral vanishes for odd J due to symmetry of the Legendre polynomial d^J_00(beta)=P_J(cos(beta)) about beta=pi/2

## Context

This work extends the tradition of angular momentum projection used in deformed mean-field calculations (Ring and Schuck, Hara and Sun) and the Projected Shell Model (Sun 2016, Hara 1991) to the spherical shell model basis, where angular momentum projection had not been considered as a practical coupling tool. The method aims to replace the traditional J-scheme using Coefficients of Fractional Parentage (Talmi 1963) and to improve upon the m-scheme used in Large Scale Shell Model calculations (Caurier 2005), which requires diagonalizing matrices up to dimension 10^9 for medium-mass nuclei. The approach may enable more efficient treatment of dense highly-excited states needed for reaction calculations, beta-decay with high Q values, and nuclear fission calculations, where spin distribution (Egidy 2009, Grimes 2016, Stetch 2014) remains an unsolved problem.
