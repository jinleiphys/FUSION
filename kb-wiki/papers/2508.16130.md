# Transport coefficients of hot and dense matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The transport coefficients are known as the measure of system interactions, as well as the dynamical input of the hydrodynamic evolution equations of an expanding system created in the relativistic heavy ion collisions. In the current analysis, they have been evaluated for strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma system as well as hot hadronic media. The medium effects for an interacting QCD system have been introduced through a quasiparticle model. The same for a hadronic system has been captured via the in-medium self-energy correction using thermal field theory. The resulting behavior of the transport coefficients shows significant modification with respect to the estimations made in vacuum or using ideal equation of state.

## Key claim
Transport coefficients for both strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma and hot hadronic matter are evaluated using medium-modified approaches, and the results show significant modification compared to vacuum or ideal equation-of-state estimates, with the actual hedge being that the quasiparticle model and thermal field theory corrections introduce temperature-dependent enhancements that depend on the specific interaction model used.

## Method
The author employs the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation solved via both the Chapman-Enskog gradient expansion technique and Grad's 14-moment method to extract shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and thermal conductivity. For the hadronic sector, medium effects are introduced through one-loop self-energy corrections to the propagator using thermal field theory, while for the QGP sector, the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) is used to incorporate lattice QCD equation-of-state effects into the single-particle distribution functions.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s in hot hadronic matter is enhanced by thermal medium effects at both values of nucleonic chemical potential, with the enhancement shown as a function of temperature T (exact numerical values not provided in the truncated text).
- The elliptic flow v2 of hadrons in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC is explained using η/s ~ 2 × (1/4π) (from cited prior work Luzum (2008)).
- The proper time evolution of temperature is shown for different η/s ratios with and without the EQPM, with the EQPM effect being more prominent at larger times (smaller temperatures) (exact numerical values not provided in the truncated text).
- Pressure anisotropy PL/PT = (P - φ)/(P + φ/2) is plotted as a function of proper time τ for different η/s values, showing disagreement between BAMPS (using ultrarelativistic EOS P = ε/3) and the quasiparticle model at large η/s.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in both QGP (cited as QGPViscosity) and hot hadronic matter (cited as HadronViscosity) by incorporating medium effects that were previously neglected in vacuum or ideal equation-of-state calculations. The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly by providing temperature-dependent transport coefficients that account for the phase transition or crossover region between QGP and hadronic matter, as motivated by the work of Csernai (2006) showing η/s has a minimum near the transition.
