# Chiral condensates and size of the sigma term

> The in-medium chiral condensate is studied with a new approach which has the advantage of no need for extra assumptions on the current mass derivatives of model parameters. It is shown that the pion-nucleon sigma term is 9/2 times the average current mass of light quarks, if quark confinement is linear. Considering both perturbative and non-perturbative interactions, the chiral condensate decreases monotonously with increasing densities, approaching to zero at about 4 fm^{-3}.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pion-nucleon sigma term is exactly 9/2 times the average current mass of light quarks, but only under the condition that quark confinement is linear (confinement exponent Z = 1). The in-medium chiral condensate approaches zero at about 4 fm^{-3} when both perturbative and non-perturbative interactions are considered.

## Method

The author develops a new approach to calculate the in-medium chiral condensate using an equivalent mass formalism, where the interacting quark system is mapped onto a free system with a density-dependent equivalent mass m, avoiding the need for current mass derivatives of model parameters. The equivalent mass is expanded as a Laurent series in the effective Fermi momentum p_f, with a non-perturbative term a_{-1}/p_f representing linear confinement and a perturbative term a_1 p_f linked to the QCD running coupling alpha_s. The baryon number density is related to the effective Fermi momentum through thermodynamic relations, and the chiral condensate ratio is then computed from Eq. (qc2) by solving the coupled equations for m and p_f.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum chiral condensate magnitude: |<q̅q>_0| ≈ (225 MeV)^3
- The chiral condensate approaches zero at a baryon density of about 4 fm^{-3}
- For linear confinement (Z = 1), the pion-nucleon sigma term σ_N = (9/2) m_0 ≈ 34 MeV, using m_0 ≈ 7.5 MeV as the average current mass of u/d quarks
- The confinement parameter a_{-1} is chosen so that the maximum QCD running coupling alpha_s does not exceed 1 at all densities, and the energy per baryon for two-flavor case is no less than 930 MeV
- The perturbative coefficient a_1 is given by a_1 = sqrt(2α_s / (π - 2α_s))

## Context

This work extends the author's previous approach (Peng 2002, Peng 2003) for calculating in-medium chiral condensates by clarifying the thermodynamic relation between effective Fermi momentum and density, which was not clearly investigated in those earlier papers. The method avoids the difficulty of the Feynman-Hellmann theorem approach (Cohen 1992) which requires knowledge of current mass derivatives of model parameters. The result enables calculation of the full density behavior of the chiral condensate and provides a relation between the sigma term and the confinement exponent that can be tested against lattice simulations of linear confinement and against the model-independent nuclear matter result of Cohen (1992).
