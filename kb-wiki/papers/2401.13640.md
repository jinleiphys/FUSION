# Relativistic meson-exchange currents in semi-inclusive lepton scattering

> We assess the impact of two-particle--two-hole excitations on the semi-inclusive electron scattering process (e,e'p) using a fully relativistic nuclear model calculation that precisely incorporates antisymmetrization. The calculation encompasses all contributions involving the exchange of a single pion and the excitation of a Delta resonance. Our results are compared with (e,e'p) data on carbon at kinematics where two-nucleon emission dominates. This work represents an essential step towards the microscopic computation of the two-particle--two-hole contribution to semi-inclusive neutrino reactions, crucial in the analysis of neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully relativistic microscopic calculation of two-particle–two-hole (2p2h) meson-exchange currents for the semi-inclusive electron scattering process (e,e'p), but the agreement with data is conditional on kinematics: it is "quite satisfactory" in the dip region between the quasi-elastic and Delta peaks, yet the model overestimates data at higher momentum transfer (Q²) and shows discrepancies at lower proton angles where quasi-elastic contamination is larger.

## Method

The calculation uses a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) framework with antisymmetrized two-body currents that include pion-in-flight, seagull (contact), and Delta-resonance diagrams, all derived from a non-linear sigma model Lagrangian. The semi-inclusive hadronic tensor is evaluated via a five-dimensional Monte Carlo integration over hole momenta and angles, with the detected proton fixed and the second nucleon (proton or neutron) unobserved. The model employs a Fermi momentum k_F = 228 MeV/c and an energy shift E_s^{2p2h} = 40 MeV for carbon-12, with the Hoeler parametrization for the electromagnetic nucleon form factor.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum for carbon-12: k_F = 228 MeV/c, with energy shift E_s = 20 MeV for the quasi-elastic peak and E_s^{2p2h} = 40 MeV (twice the QE shift) for the 2p2h calculation
- The analyzed data correspond to very low Q² values, below 0.1 GeV²
- For the kinematics at electron energy ε = 478 MeV, energy transfer ω = 263 MeV, momentum transfer q = 303 MeV/c, and proton angle θ_p = 38°, the longitudinal response is negligible at roughly 1% of the transverse response
- The pp channel contribution is about a factor of 2 smaller than in the non-relativistic calculation of Ryckebusch (1994)
- The Delta contribution alone represents roughly half of the total 2p2h response, with the purely pionic and π-Δ interference terms being almost equal

## Context

This work extends the inclusive relativistic antisymmetrized 2p2h calculation of De Pace (2003) to the semi-inclusive (e,e'p) channel, providing the first microscopic relativistic computation of these contributions. The results are compared with NIKHEF data from Ryckebusch (1994) and Kester (1995) and MIT-Bates data from Baghaei (1989), showing qualitative agreement with the non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculation of Ryckebusch (1994) but with differences in the 2p2h peak position and pp final-state strength. This work enables the future microscopic computation of 2p2h contributions to semi-inclusive neutrino reactions, which are currently simulated in event generators using approximate methods based on inclusive calculations, and the authors state that calculations for neutrino cross sections are in progress.
