# Investigation of Hot QCD Matter: Theoretical Aspects

> This lecture presents an overview of the status of the investigation of the properties of the quark-gluon plasma using relativistic heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). It focuses on the insights that have been obtained by the comparison between experimental data from both facilities and theoretical calculations.

# Investigation of Hot QCD Matter: Theoretical Aspects

## Key claim
The quark-gluon plasma produced at LHC energies is less strongly coupled than that produced at RHIC, as indicated by a 60% higher average shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio at LHC (0.20) compared to RHIC (0.12), though each value carries systematic uncertainties of at least 50%.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical frameworks for hot QCD matter including lattice gauge theory for static thermodynamic properties, viscous hydrodynamics for bulk evolution, perturbative QCD for jet quenching, and holographic AdS/CFT methods for transport coefficients. It compares experimental data from RHIC and LHC with these theoretical calculations to extract properties such as the shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio, jet quenching parameter, and color screening length.

## Key numbers
- Quasi-critical temperature where chiral susceptibilities peak: T_c ≈ 155 MeV
- Initial temperature in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV): exceeds 300 MeV
- Initial temperature in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (√s_NN = 2.76 TeV): at least 30-40% higher than at RHIC
- Lower bound on shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio: η/s ≥ (4π)^(-1) ≈ 0.08
- Average η/s in Au+Au at top RHIC energy: 0.12
- Average η/s in Pb+Pb at LHC: 0.20
- Suppression factor R_AA for hadrons with transverse momenta around 10 GeV/c: approximately 0.2 at both RHIC and LHC
- Quarkonium state radii: approximately 0.1 fm for Υ ground state to almost 1 fm for ψ'

## Context
This lecture review extends the line of work comparing RHIC and LHC heavy ion data to theoretical models, building on lattice QCD calculations by Borsanyi (2010, 2013) and Bazavov (2011) for the equation of state, and on viscous hydrodynamics frameworks by Romatschke (2009) and Song (2010, 2011). The analysis of η/s using event-by-event fluctuations by Gale (2012, 2013) is highlighted as the most complete study to date. The paper identifies open questions including the transition scale between weak and strong coupling in the quark-gluon plasma, the temperature dependence of transport coefficients, and the role of recombination in quarkonium production.
