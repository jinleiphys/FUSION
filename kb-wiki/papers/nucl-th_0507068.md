# Probing the density dependence of the symmetry potential in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> Based on the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, the effects of the density-dependent symmetry potential for baryons and of the Coulomb potential for produced mesons are investigated for neutron-rich heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies. The calculated results of the $Δ^-/Δ^{++}$ and $π^{-}/π^{+}$ production ratios show a clear beam-energy dependence on the density-dependent symmetry potential, which is stronger for the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio close to the pion production threshold. The Coulomb potential of the mesons changes the transverse momentum distribution of the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio significantly, though it alters only slightly the $π^-$ and $π^+$ total yields. The $π^-$ yields, especially at midrapidity or at low transverse momenta and the $π^-/π^+$ ratios at low transverse momenta, are shown to be sensitive probes of the density-dependent symmetry potential in dense nuclear matter. The effect of the density-dependent symmetry potential on the production of both, K$^0$ and K$^+$ mesons, is also investigated.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of the $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio to the density-dependent symmetry potential is strongest near the pion production threshold and is largely reduced at beam energies above about $1.5A$ GeV; the Coulomb potential of mesons significantly alters the transverse momentum distribution of this ratio but only slightly changes the total pion yields.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model (version 1.3) with a hard ($K=300$ MeV) or soft ($K=200$ MeV) Skyrme-type equation of state, without momentum dependence, to simulate central ($b=0-2$ fm) and near-peripheral ($b=5-8$ fm) collisions of $^{132}$Sn+$^{132}$Sn at beam energies $E_{\rm b}=0.5A$ and $1.5A$ GeV. They implemented three phenomenological density-dependent symmetry potentials (F05 with $\gamma=0.5$, F15 with $\gamma=1.5$, and Fa3 with a critical density $a=3$) and switched the Coulomb potential of mesons on or off to isolate its effect on pion and kaon observables.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy coefficient $S_0=34$ MeV adopted, with a range $32\ {\rm MeV} \leq S_0 \leq 36\ {\rm MeV}$ from isovector GDR data in $^{208}$Pb.
- At $E_{\rm b}=0.5A$ GeV, the neutron/proton collision number ratio at midrapidity is $\sim 82\%$; at $E_{\rm b}=1.5A$ GeV it increases to $\sim 95\%$.
- For $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb at $E_{\rm b}=0.8A$ GeV and $b=7-9$ fm, the $K^0/K^+$ ratio is about $1.25$ for the F15 symmetry potential and about $1.4$ for the Fa3 symmetry potential.
- The $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio at low transverse momenta ($p_t^{\rm cm}=0.1\ {\rm GeV}/c$) is identified as a suitable probe of the density-dependent symmetry potential.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of isospin effects in heavy ion collisions by Gaitanos et al. (2004) and B.-A. Li et al. (2005), who studied the $\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}$ ratio using the IBUU04 model and found reduced sensitivity at beam energies above $2A$ GeV. The present study uses the UrQMD model to systematically examine the beam-energy dependence, the role of the Coulomb potential of mesons, and the phase-space regions where pions are most sensitive to the symmetry potential. It also explores $K^0/K^+$ ratios as an additional probe, following earlier work on $\Sigma^{-}/\Sigma^{+}$ ratios by Li et al. (2005). The results enable experimental searches at facilities such as FAIR (GSI) and RIA to focus on low-transverse-momentum pions near midrapidity at beam energies close to the pion production threshold.
