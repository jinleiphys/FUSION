# Dead-cone searches in heavy-ion collisions using the jet tree

> We explore the possibility of using the dead cone of heavy quarks as a region of the Lund plane where medium-induced gluon radiation can be isolated and characterised. The filling of the dead cone by medium-induced gluons is expected to be the result of the interplay between the minimum angle of such radiation due to transverse momentum broadening and the dead-cone angle. Since the measurement of a fully corrected Lund plane in heavy-ion collisions is currently challenging, we propose to use jet grooming techniques to identify a particular splitting in the jet tree that is both perturbative and sensitive to the dead-cone effect. To that end, we propose a new jet substructure groomer, dubbed Late-$k_t$, that selects the most collinear splitting in a QCD jet above a certain transverse momentum cutoff $k_{t,\rm{cut}}$. The role of $k_{t,\rm{cut}}$ is to guarantee perturbative splittings, while selecting the most collinear splitting enhances the sensitivity to mass effects. As a proof of concept, we study the angular distribution of the splitting tagged by Late-$k_t$ both analytically and with Monte Carlo simulations. First, we derive the logarithmic resummation structure in vacuum and demonstrate its capability to distinguish between inclusive and heavy-flavoured jets. Next, we extend the calculation for in-medium jets and show that medium-induced emissions lead to an enhancement of collinear emissions below the dead cone angle. Numerically, we demonstrate an excellent resilience of Late-$k_t$ against uncorrelated thermal background, thus confirming this observable as a potential candidate to unveil medium dynamics around the dead cone regime.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the angular distribution of the most collinear splitting selected by their new Late-$k_t$ groomer can isolate medium-induced gluon radiation in the dead-cone region of heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions, but only for bottom-initiated jets where the dead-cone angle is sufficiently larger than the minimum angle set by transverse momentum broadening; for charm jets, the dead-cone angle is too small for this effect to be observable.

## Method

The authors introduce a jet substructure groomer called Late-$k_t$ that, after reclustering a jet with the Cambridge-Aachen algorithm, selects the splitting with the smallest opening angle among all primary-branch splittings whose transverse momentum exceeds a cutoff $k_{t,\rm{cut}}$. They derive the logarithmic resummation structure of the groomed angular distribution in vacuum at modified leading-logarithmic accuracy, and extend the calculation to in-medium jets using the BDMPS-Z framework with the full massive-quark medium-induced gluon spectrum from Armesto (2003). Monte Carlo simulations with Pythia8 are used to study the groomer's resilience against hadronisation and thermal background.

## Key numbers

* Dead-cone angle definition: $\theta_0 \equiv m_Q / E$, where $m_Q$ is the quark mass and $E$ its energy.
* For charm jets, the quark mass is set to $m_c = 1.4$ GeV; for bottom jets, $m_b = 4.8$ GeV.
* The transverse momentum cutoff is set to $k_{t,\rm{cut}} = 1$ GeV for the Monte Carlo study, with values of 0.5, 1, and 2 GeV explored analytically.
* Jets are reconstructed with anti-$k_t$ algorithm using jet radius $R = 0.2$ at $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV.
* The medium diffusion parameter range explored is $2 < \hat{q}L < 8$ GeV$^2$.
* The ATLAS track-based Lund plane measurement reports splitting angles down to 0.005 radians.

## Context

This work extends the dead-cone measurement technique successfully used by ALICE (2021) in proton-proton collisions for charm jets, which relied on iterative declustering of D-meson-tagged jets, to the heavy-ion environment where a full Lund plane measurement is not yet available. The proposed Late-$k_t$ groomer is contrasted with Soft Drop (Dasgupta 2013, Larkoski 2014) and an "Early-$k_t$" variant, and the authors note that colour coherence effects (Mehtar-Tani 2010, 2011; Casalderrey-Solana 2011) can produce similar collisional enhancements in inclusive jets, requiring careful comparison between heavy-flavour and inclusive jets. The work enables future experimental searches for medium-induced radiation in the dead-cone regime using jet grooming techniques at the LHC.
