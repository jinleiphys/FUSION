# Asymptotic behavior of pion form factors

> We consider the electromagnetic and transition pion form factors. Using dispersion relations we simultaneously describe both the hadronic, time-like region and the asymptotic region of large energy-momentum transfer. For the latter we propose a novel mechanism of Regge fermion exchange.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the asymptotic behavior of pion form factors at large momentum transfer is dominated by Regge fermion exchange rather than by perturbative QCD, and that hadronic (resonance) contributions remain significant even at the highest available energies, providing about 50% of the transition form factor at the largest measured values of \(-s\).

## Method

The authors use dispersion relations to connect the time-like (hadronic) and space-like (asymptotic) regions of the pion electromagnetic and transition form factors. They model the low-energy region with Breit-Wigner parametrizations of the \(\rho(770)\) and \(\omega(782)\) resonances, and introduce a novel mechanism for the high-energy region based on reggeized quark exchange, where the quark Regge trajectory is related to meson trajectories from \(\pi\pi\) scattering. They fit the available data with a single free parameter for the transition form factor and five parameters for the electromagnetic form factor, including the magnitude of the \(q\bar q\) continuum.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\)
- \(\omega\) resonance coupling: \(c^{\omega}_{\pi\gamma} = 0.493\) (obtained from \(g_{\omega\pi\gamma} = 1.81\) and \(g_\omega = 17.1\))
- \(\rho\) resonance coupling: \(c^{\rho}_{\pi\gamma} = 0.613\) (obtained from \(g_{\rho\pi\gamma} = 0.647\) and \(g_\rho = 4.96\))
- Quark Regge trajectory: \(\alpha_q(t) \approx 0.75 + 0.45\, t/\text{GeV}^2\) (derived from \(\alpha_\rho(t)\))
- Available data on the pion electromagnetic form factor range up to \(|s| \lesssim 10\ \text{GeV}^2\)
- BaBar data for the transition form factor extend to \(-s \approx 40\ \text{GeV}^2\)
- The asymptotic contribution to \(\text{Im}\,F_{\pi\gamma}\) behaves as \((s/\text{GeV}^2)^{\alpha_q(0) - 3/2}\)
- The product \(|s\,F_{2\pi}(s)|\) is predicted to rise asymptotically roughly as \(s^{1/4}\)

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach to pion form factors by incorporating a Regge fermion exchange mechanism, addressing the discrepancy between perturbative QCD predictions and the BaBar data on the \(\pi^0\) transition form factor (Aubert 2009b). The authors build on the pQCD framework of Efremov and Radyushkin (1980), Duncan and Mueller (1979), and Lepage and Brodsky (1979), and the Regge formalism of Fadin and Sherman (1977). The results enable a unified description of both form factors across all energy scales and predict a rising behavior of \(|s\,F_{2\pi}(s)|\) at large \(|s|\), which can be tested by future experiments at higher momentum transfers.
