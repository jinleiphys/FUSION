# Heavy baryon/meson ratios in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Heavy baryon/meson ratios $Λ_c/D^0$ and $Λ_b/\bar{B}^0$ in relativistic heavy ion collisions are studied in the quark coalescence model. For heavy baryons, we include production from coalescence of heavy quarks with free light quarks as well as with bounded light diquarks that might exist in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma produced in these collisions. Including the contribution from decays of heavy hadron resonances and also that due to fragmentation of heavy quarks that are left in the system after coalescence, the resulting $Λ_c/D^0$ and $Λ_b/\bar{B}^0$ ratios in midrapidity ($|y|\le 0.5$) from central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV are about a factor of five and ten, respectively, larger than those given by the thermal model, and about a factor of ten and twelve, respectively, larger than corresponding ratios in the PYTHIA model for $pp$ collisions. These ratios are reduced by a factor of about 1.6 if there are no diquarks in the quark-gluon plasma. The transverse momentum dependence of the heavy baryon/meson ratios is found to be sensitive to the heavy quark mass, with the $Λ_b/\bar{B}^0$ ratio being much flatter than the $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio. The latter peaks at the transverse momentum $p_T^{} \simeq 0.8$ GeV but the peak shifts to $p_T^{} \simeq 2$ GeV in the absence of diquarks.

# Heavy baryon/meson ratios in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The $\Lambda_c/D^0$ and $\Lambda_b/\bar{B}^0$ ratios in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV are enhanced by about a factor of ten and twelve, respectively, compared to PYTHIA model predictions for $pp$ collisions, but this enhancement depends critically on the assumption that scalar light diquarks exist in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma; without diquarks, the ratios are reduced by a factor of about 1.6.

## Method

The authors used a quark coalescence model to compute heavy baryon and meson production from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma fire cylinder, including both three-quark coalescence and diquark-heavy-quark coalescence for $\Lambda_Q$ baryons. They incorporated contributions from decays of heavy hadron resonances ($D^*$, $\Sigma_Q$, $\Sigma_Q^*$, and excited $\Lambda_Q$ states) and from fragmentation of heavy quarks not consumed by coalescence, using heavy quark momentum distributions parameterized from RHIC data and pQCD with energy loss corrections.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio in central Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV (midrapidity $|y|\le 0.5$): about a factor of 5 larger than the thermal model prediction and about a factor of 10 larger than the PYTHIA $pp$ value of 0.13
- $\Lambda_b/\bar{B}^0$ ratio in same collisions: about a factor of 10 larger than the thermal model prediction and about a factor of 12 larger than the PYTHIA $pp$ value of 0.7
- Ratios reduced by a factor of about 1.6 if no diquarks exist in the quark-gluon plasma
- $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio peaks at transverse momentum $p_T \simeq 0.8$ GeV; peak shifts to $p_T \simeq 2$ GeV in the absence of diquarks
- Thermal model gives $\Lambda_c/D^0 \simeq 0.27$ and $\Lambda_b/\bar{B}^0 \simeq 0.86$ including higher resonances
- Coalescence model (diquark case) gives relative production fractions: $\Lambda_c = 0.378$, $D^0 = 0.282$; $\Lambda_b = 0.385$, $\bar{B}^0 = 0.044$
- Charm quark number in central Au+Au: $N_c \simeq 9.23$; bottom quark number: $N_b \simeq 0.035$
- Light quark numbers in fire cylinder volume $V=1000$ fm$^3$: $N_u = N_d \approx 245$, $N_s \approx 149$ at effective temperature $T_{\rm eff}=200$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the diquark-based enhancement mechanism proposed by Lee et al. (2007) for $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratios, which was motivated by the possibility of quasi-bound diquark states in quark-gluon plasma at temperatures between $T_C$ and $4T_C$ as suggested by Shuryak and Zahed (2003-2004). The study addresses the suggestion by Sorensen (2005) and by Martinez-Garcia et al. (2007) that a $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio of about 1 could produce an additional 20-25% suppression of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^e$ for electrons from heavy meson decays, beyond collisional energy loss. The results enable quantitative predictions for future measurements of heavy baryon/meson ratios at RHIC and LHC, which could serve as a diagnostic for diquark existence in the quark-gluon plasma.
