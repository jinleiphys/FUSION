# Thermodynamic inequalities for nuclear rotation

> The strictly reversible, thermodynamically equilibrium nature of the free rotation of a body makes it possible to obtain a number of bounds on the rotational characteristics within individual rotational bands of nonspherical nuclei. As a result, the bounds between which the possible values of the critical spin $J_c$ lie can be expressed exclusively in terms of a restricted number of the experimentally most accessible data on the lower phase $J<J_c$ for a given nuclide. The bounds are tested on the ground-state rotational bands (yrast lines) of even-even nuclei, in which the corresponding phase transition (backbending) has already been observed experimentally. For nuclei with pronounced non-sphericity, all the bounds are invariably confirmed. For the ground-state rotational bands for which the phase transition point $J =J_c$ has not yet been reached, predictions are made for the corresponding values of $J_c^{min}$ and, especially, $J_c^{max}$. The specific features of excited rotational bands, and also the bands of odd nuclei are discussed.

# Thermodynamic inequalities for nuclear rotation

## Key claim

The paper derives thermodynamic bounds on the critical spin \(J_c\) (the backbending phase transition point) in rotational bands of nonspherical nuclei, using only experimentally accessible data from the lower phase \(J < J_c\). The bounds are rigorously valid only for nuclei with pronounced nonspherical shape; for nuclei that may be nearly spherical in the ground state, violations of the inequalities can occur, suggesting possible first-order phase transition behavior or shape changes.

## Method

The authors treat nuclear rotation as a thermodynamically reversible, equilibrium phenomenon and apply the concept of minimal work to derive inequalities relating energy, angular momentum, angular velocity, and moment of inertia. For the lower phase (\(J < J_c\)), they use the monotonic increase of the moment of inertia with spin (empirically observed) and the inequality \(\hbar J / \Omega_m < I_0\) (rigid-body moment of inertia). For the upper phase (\(J > J_c\)), they use the pole behavior of the moment of inertia near the Curie point and the integral relation \(\int_{J_c}^\infty (1/I_0 - 1/I) dJ = 0\). The lower bound \(J_c^{min}\) is obtained from the intersection of the lower-phase energy curve with the parabola \(\hbar^2 J^2 / I_0\); the upper bound \(J_c^{max}\) is obtained from the condition that the derivative \(dE_m/dJ\) at \(J_c^{max}\) equals \(\hbar^2 J_c^{max} / I_0\). These bounds are tested against experimental data for ground-state rotational bands (yrast lines) of even-even nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic approach to nuclear rotation developed by the same authors in earlier papers (Nosov and Kamchatnov, Refs. 1 and 2), which introduced the concept of a Curie point for rotational bands and the pole behavior of the moment of inertia. The present paper provides practical bounds for the critical spin \(J_c\) that can be computed from limited experimental data on the lower phase, enabling predictions for nuclei where backbending has not yet been observed. The method is tested against the compilation of Sayer et al. (Ref. 7) and later data from Refs. 8-16 and the review by Lieder and Ryde (Ref. 17). The results enable predictions for nuclei such as \(^{152}\)Gd, \(^{156}\)Gd, \(^{178}\)W, \(^{176}\)Os, and \(^{238}\)U, where experiments have nearly reached the phase transition point. The approach also generalizes to excited rotational bands and odd nuclei, though the authors note that non-monotonicity of the moment of inertia in excited bands can introduce uncertainty.
