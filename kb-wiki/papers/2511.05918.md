# Search for shape-isomers in the Pt-Hg-Pb region

> Potential energy surfaces of nine even-even isotopes of Pt, Hg, and Pb around $^{186}$Pt are evaluated within a macroscopic-microscopic model based on the Lublin-Strasbourg-Drop macroscopic energy and the microscopic energy obtained using the Yukawa-folded mean-field potential to establish the Strutinski shell corrections and the pairing correlation energy through the BCS approach with a monopole pairing force. The rapidly converging Fourier-over-Spheroid shape parametrization is used to describe nuclear deformations. The stability of the identified shape isomeric states with respect to non-axial and higher-order deformations is investigated. It is also found that in the description of non-axial deformations special attention needs to be devoted to the orientation of the triaxial shape. For the example of the $^{186}$Hg nucleus, where three prolate shape-isomeric states are found, it is shown that the potential energy surface obtained in our model is close to the one obtained in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with the Gogny energy-density functional.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shape-isomeric states in Pt, Hg, and Pb isotopes around 186Pt can be reliably identified using a macroscopic-microscopic model with the Fourier-over-Spheroid shape parametrization, but only if a symmetrization procedure accounting for all possible orientations of the deformed nuclear shape is included; without this orientation degree of freedom, many shape isomers would be missed.

## Method

The authors compute potential energy surfaces for nine even-even isotopes of Pt, Hg, and Pb using the macroscopic-microscopic Lublin-Strasbourg-Drop (LSD) model for the macroscopic energy, with microscopic shell corrections from a Yukawa-folded mean-field potential and BCS pairing with a monopole pairing force. Nuclear deformations are described using the Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) parametrization in a four-parameter space (elongation, non-axiality, left-right asymmetry, and neck degree of freedom), and the resulting surfaces are projected onto the traditional Bohr (β,γ) plane after a symmetrization procedure that minimizes energy across the three 120° γ-sectors. For 186Hg, the results are compared with self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1S force.

## Key numbers

- In 182Pt, 184Pt, and 186Pt, a barrier of approximately 0.5 MeV separates the prolate ground-state minimum (β ≈ 0.17, γ = 0°) from an oblate isomeric state (γ = 60°) at slightly larger β.
- In 184Hg, a shallow shape isomer appears at {β ≈ 0.46, γ ≈ 28°} around 6 MeV above the ground state; the energy gain from the symmetrization procedure at this deformation is about 1.5 MeV.
- In 186Pb, a shape isomer at β ≈ 0.36 along the oblate axis (γ = 60°) is found only because the energy gain from symmetrization reaches 1.5 MeV at that deformation and 2.5 MeV at larger β.
- In the HFB calculation for 186Hg, three prolate local minima are found at Q20 ≈ 21.0, 34.4, and 70.5 b, corresponding to β = 0.56, 0.79, and 1.13 in the macroscopic-microscopic model, with energies about 3.0, 6.5, and 15.3 MeV above the ground state, respectively.
- In the macroscopic-microscopic model for 186Hg, similar minima are found at β = 0.38, 0.71, and 1.01 with energies 2.3, 6.6, and 8.9 MeV above the ground state.
- The pairing strength parameter is g0 = 0.28 ħω0 for both protons and neutrons, where ħω0 = 41 MeV/A1/3.

## Context

This work extends previous macroscopic-microscopic studies of shape coexistence in the Z ≈ 82 region, particularly the earlier study by Pomorski et al. (2020) which used the original Fourier deformation parameters without the symmetrization procedure; the authors state that the shape isomers found here would have been missed in that earlier approach. The results are compared with self-consistent HFB calculations using the Gogny D1S force (Berger et al., 1984), showing good qualitative agreement for 186Hg. The study enables future investigations of other isotopic chains near Z ≈ 82 and in other mass regions to test the predictive power of the symmetrized potential energy surface approach against experimental data.
