# v4 for identified particles at RHIC from viscous hydrodynamics

> Using ideal and viscous hydrodynamics, the ratio of azimuthal moments v4/(v2)^2 is calculated for pions, protons, and kaons in sqrt{s}=200 A*GeV Au+Au collisions. For any value of viscosity here is little dependence on particle species. Ideal hydrodynamics and data show a flat curve as a function of pt. Adding viscosity in the standard way destroys this flatness. However, it can be restored by replacing the standard quadratic ansatz for delta f (the viscous correction to the distribution function at freeze-out) with a weaker momentum dependence.

## Key claim

Using viscous hydrodynamics, the ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) for pions, protons, and kaons in \(\sqrt{s}=200\) A*GeV Au+Au collisions shows little dependence on particle species for any value of viscosity, but the standard quadratic ansatz for the viscous correction \(\delta f\) destroys the flat \(p_t\) dependence seen in ideal hydrodynamics and data; this flatness can be restored by replacing that ansatz with a weaker momentum dependence.

## Method

The author performed ideal and viscous hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at RHIC, using both Glauber-type and Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC)-type initial conditions. The ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) was computed from the Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula, with the viscous correction \(\delta f\) to the equilibrium distribution function implemented either via the standard quadratic ansatz or a linear (weaker momentum dependence) ansatz. Results were compared for pions, protons, and kaons as a function of transverse momentum \(p_t\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\).

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) from ideal hydrodynamics approaches roughly 1/2 at large \(p_t\), with a correction that behaves as \(1/p_t\).
- A freeze-out temperature of \(T_f = 140\) MeV gives a flat dependence of \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) on \(p_t\), matching experimental data.
- Viscous simulations with \(\eta/s = 0.16\) and the standard quadratic ansatz for \(\delta f\) produce a significant change in the \(p_t\) dependence of \(v_4/(v_2)^2\), destroying the flat shape.
- Data from STAR (40-50% centrality) and PHENIX (45-50% centrality) show a ratio closer to 1, which can be partially explained by event-by-event fluctuations that increase the ratio by a factor of 1.38.

## Context

This work extends the analytic prediction of Borghini and Ollitrault (2005) that \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) should approach 1/2 at large \(p_t\) in ideal hydrodynamics, and confirms that ideal hydrodynamics with a freeze-out temperature near 140 MeV reproduces the flat \(p_t\) dependence seen in data. The results contradict the standard quadratic ansatz for \(\delta f\) used in viscous hydrodynamics, which introduces a strong \(p_t\) dependence and centrality dependence not seen experimentally. This enables future work to constrain the correct form of the viscous correction \(\delta f\) and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) by comparing to identified particle data, as detailed in Luzum (2010).
