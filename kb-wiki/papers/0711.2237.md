# $Σ$-nuclear spin-orbit coupling from two-pion exchange

> Using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory we calculate the density-dependent complex-valued spin-orbit coupling strength $U_{Σls}(k_f)+ i W_{Σls}(k_f)$ of a $Σ$ hyperon in the nuclear medium. The leading long-range $ΣN$ interaction arises from iterated one-pion exchange with a $Λ$ or a $Σ$ hyperon in the intermediate state. We find from this unique long-range dynamics a sizeable ``wrong-sign'' spin-orbit coupling strength of $U_{Σls}(k_{f0}) \simeq -20$ MeVfm$^2$ at normal nuclear matter density $ρ_0 = 0.16 $fm$^{-3}$. The strong $ΣN\to ΛN$ conversion process contributes at the same time an imaginary part of $W_{Σls}(k_{f0}) \simeq -12$ MeVfm$^2$. When combined with estimates of the short-range contribution the total $Σ$-nuclear spin-orbit coupling becomes rather weak.

## Key claim
Using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, the leading long-range contribution from iterated one-pion exchange generates a sizeable "wrong-sign" spin-orbit coupling strength of approximately -20 MeVfm² for a Σ hyperon at normal nuclear matter density, but when combined with estimates of the short-range contribution the total Σ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling becomes rather weak.

## Method
The density-dependent complex-valued spin-orbit coupling strength of a Σ hyperon in nuclear matter was calculated from iterated one-pion exchange diagrams with Λ or Σ hyperons in the intermediate state, using SU(3) chiral perturbation theory. The spin-orbit coupling strength was expressed as a derivative of the Σ-nuclear potential with respect to the squared ΣΛ mass splitting parameter Δ², and the resulting loop integrals were evaluated analytically. The short-range contribution was estimated by relating it to the known nucleonic spin-orbit coupling strength via a coefficient C_ls, with guidance from QCD sum rule predictions.

## Key numbers
- Real part of Σ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling strength at normal nuclear matter density: U_Σls(k_f0) ≃ -20 MeVfm² (specifically -19.9 MeVfm² from the sum of calculated terms).
- Imaginary part of Σ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling strength at normal nuclear matter density: W_Σls(k_f0) ≃ -12 MeVfm² (specifically -11.7 MeVfm² from the sum of calculated terms).
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (corresponding to Fermi momentum k_f0 = 263 MeV).
- Threshold density for Pauli blocking to become active in the imaginary part: ρ_th = 0.072 fm⁻³ = 0.45 ρ₀.
- Short-range Σ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling strength estimate at saturation density: U_Σls(k_f0)^(sh) ≃ 18.6 MeVfm² (using C_ls ≃ 1 and U_Nls(k_f)^(sh) = 30 MeVfm² ρ/ρ₀).
- In the chiral limit (m_π = 0): U_Σls(k_f0) + i W_Σls(k_f0) = (-25.0 - 13.0 i) MeVfm².

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory framework previously applied to calculate Λ hyperon mean-fields and spin-orbit coupling (Kaiser, 2007) and the complex Σ-nuclear mean-field (Kaiser, 2007). It provides a novel explanation for a weak Σ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling through cancellation between long-range contributions from iterated one-pion exchange and short-range contributions, contrasting with earlier quark model and one-boson exchange models that predicted strong Σ spin-orbit coupling (Pirner, 1979; Bouyssy, 1984). The results enable a consistent theoretical description of hyperon-nucleus interactions but note that experimental verification is difficult due to the repulsive Σ-nucleus optical potential precluding rich spectroscopy of heavy Σ-hypernuclei.
