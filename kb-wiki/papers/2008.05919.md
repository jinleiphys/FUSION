# Microscopic derivation of density functional theory for superfluid systems based on effective action formalism

> Density-functional theory for superfluid systems is developed in the framework of the functional renormalization group based on the effective action formalism. We introduce the effective action for the particle-number and nonlocal pairing densities and demonstrate that the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem for superfluid systems is established in terms of the effective action. The flow equation for the effective action is then derived, where the flow parameter runs from $0$ to $1$, corresponding to the non-interacting and interacting systems. From the flow equation and the variational equation that the equilibrium density satisfies, we obtain the exact expression for the Kohn-Sham potential generalized to including the pairing potentials. The resultant Kohn-Sham potential has a nice feature that it expresses the microscopic formulae of the external, Hartree, pairing, and exchange-correlation terms, separately. It is shown that our Kohn-Sham potential gives the ground-state energy of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory by neglecting the correlations. An advantage of our exact formalism lies in the fact that it provides ways to systematically improve the correlation part.

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact microscopic derivation of density functional theory (DFT) for superfluid systems using the effective action formalism combined with the functional renormalization group (FRG), establishing that the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem holds for superfluid systems with spontaneous symmetry breaking when the effective action is defined for both particle-number and nonlocal pairing densities, though the proof requires an infinitesimally small but nonzero external pairing field to break the degeneracy and ensure a unique variational determination of the equilibrium density.

## Method

The authors construct the generating functional for correlation functions of particle-number and nonlocal pairing densities, then define the effective action via a Legendre transformation. They introduce a flow parameter lambda from 0 to 1 that adiabatically switches on the two-body interaction, derive the flow equation for the effective action by differentiating with respect to lambda, and obtain the exact expression for the Kohn-Sham potential generalized to include pairing potentials from the flow equation combined with the variational equation for equilibrium densities.

## Key numbers

- The flow parameter lambda runs from 0 to 1, corresponding to the non-interacting and interacting systems respectively.
- The inverse temperature is denoted by beta, with the zero-temperature limit taken as beta approaches infinity.
- The Kohn-Sham potential is shown to reproduce the ground-state energy of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory when correlations are neglected.

## Context

This work extends the FRG-DFT formalism previously developed by Yokota et al. (2018) for normal systems to superfluid systems with spontaneous symmetry breaking. It builds upon the effective action approach for composite fields by Fukuda et al. (1994) and Valiev et al. (1997), and generalizes the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem for superfluid systems originally demonstrated by Oliveira et al. (1988). The formalism enables systematic improvement of the correlation part of the energy density functional beyond the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation, and provides a foundation for ab-initio calculations of superfluid nuclear systems.
