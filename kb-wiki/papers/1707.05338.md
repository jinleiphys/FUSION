# Thermal excitation spectrum from entanglement in an expanding quantum string

> A surprising result in $e^+ e^-$ collisions is that the particle spectra from the string formed between the expanding quark-antiquark pair have thermal properties even though scatterings appear not to be frequent enough to explain this. We address this problem by considering the finite observable interval of a relativistic quantum string in terms of its reduced density operator by tracing over the complement region. We show how quantum entanglement in the presence of a horizon in spacetime for the causal transfer of information leads locally to a reduced mixed-state density operator. For very early proper time $τ$, we show that the entanglement entropy becomes extensive and scales with the rapidity. At these early times, the reduced density operator is of thermal form, with an entanglement temperature $T_τ=\hbar/(2πk_B τ)$, even in the absence of any scatterings.

# Thermal excitation spectrum from entanglement in an expanding quantum string

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent thermal hadron spectra observed in e+e- collisions arise from quantum entanglement between observable and unobservable regions of an expanding string, rather than from frequent scatterings. This thermal-like state emerges at very early proper times τ, where the reduced density operator takes a thermal form with entanglement temperature T_τ = ħ/(2π k_B τ), but only under the condition that the system is effectively conformal at those early times (Mτ → 0).

## Method

The authors model the expanding string formed between a quark-antiquark pair using the bosonized Schwinger model (1+1-dimensional QED with a massless Dirac fermion), which maps to a free massive scalar field theory. They compute the reduced density matrix and entanglement entropy for a rapidity interval A = (-Δη/2, Δη/2) at fixed Bjorken time τ by tracing over the complement region, exploiting the fact that Gaussian density matrices remain Gaussian under partial tracing. The entanglement entropy is evaluated using a c-function approach for massive scalar fields and free massive Dirac fermions, with numerical results for the entropy density dS/dΔη as a function of Mτ.

## Key numbers

- Entanglement temperature: T_τ = ħ/(2π k_B τ), derived for very early proper time τ
- For the conformal limit (Mτ → 0), the entanglement entropy density dS/dΔη approaches a plateau at 1/6 (the asymptotic conformal value, dashed line in Fig. 2 and Fig. 3)
- For the bosonized massless Schwinger model, the entanglement entropy for large rapidity intervals (Δη ≫ 1) is S = (c/6)[Δη + 2 ln(τ)] + const, where c is the conformal central charge
- The conformal central charge c = 1/3 for a real massless scalar boson (c/3 = 1/3)
- For the 't Hooft model estimate with N_c = N_f = 3 plus two transverse scalar fields, the estimated conformal charge is c = N_c × N_f + 2 = 11
- Measured charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη ≈ 2-4 for e+e- collision energies between √s = 14 GeV and √s = 206 GeV
- Estimated entropy per charged particle S/N_ch = 7.2 for a hadron resonance gas, giving dS/dη ≈ 14-28 for the above energy range

## Context

This work addresses the longstanding puzzle that hadron spectra in e+e- collisions appear thermal despite insufficient scatterings for thermalization, a problem discussed by Becattini (1995, 2009, 2010), Castorina (2007), Andronic (2008), and Fischer (2016). The authors extend the Schwinger model framework of Andersson (1983) and Catani (1991) to show that entanglement across a Minkowski spacetime horizon produces thermal-like states, analogous to but distinct from Hawking radiation (which involves event horizons) and the Unruh effect (which involves acceleration and a space-dependent temperature). The result enables a new interpretation of statistical hadronization models and suggests that quantum entanglement may also explain early thermalization observed at RHIC and the LHC, as well as thermal-like states in cold atom experiments after sudden quenches.
