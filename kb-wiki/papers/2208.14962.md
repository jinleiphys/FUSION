# Masses of Heavy Quarkonium states in magnetized matter -- effects of PV mixing and (inverse) magnetic catalysis

> We study the in-medium masses of the heavy quarkonium (charmonium and bottomonium) states in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter in presence of an external magnetic field. The mass modifications of the heavy quarkonia are obtained from the medium modifications of a scalar dilaton field, $χ$, calculated within a chiral effective model. The dilaton field is introduced in the model through a scale invariance breaking logarithmic potential, and, simulates the gluon condensates of QCD. Within the chiral effective model, the values of the dilaton field along with the scalar (isoscalar, $σ(\sim \langle \bar u u \rangle +\langle \bar d d \rangle)$, isoscalar $ζ(\sim \langle \bar s s \rangle$) and isovector $δ(\sim \langle \bar u u\rangle - \langle\bar d d \rangle)$) fields, are solved from their coupled equations of motion. These are solved accounting for the effects of the Dirac sea (DS) as well as anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of the nucleons. The Dirac sea contributions are observed to lead to enhancement (reduction) of the quark condensates (through $σ$ and $ζ$ fields) with increase in magnetic field, an effect called the (inverse) magnetic catalysis. The magnetic field effects on the masses of the heavy quarkonia include the mixing of the pseudoscalar (spin 0) and vector (spin 1) states (PV mixing), as well as, the effects from (inverse) magnetic catalysis. These effects are observed to be significant for large values of the magnetic field. This should have observable consequences on the production of the heavy quarkonia and open heavy flavour mesons, resulting from ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy ion collision experiments, where the created magnetic field can be huge.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of charmonium and bottomonium states in magnetized isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter are modified by two competing effects: pseudoscalar-vector (PV) mixing and the (inverse) magnetic catalysis arising from Dirac sea contributions of nucleons, with the inclusion of anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of nucleons reversing the magnetic catalysis trend at nuclear matter saturation density.

## Method

The mass modifications of heavy quarkonia are obtained from the medium change of a scalar dilaton field, which simulates the gluon condensates of QCD, within a chiral effective model. The dilaton field and the scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta) are solved from their coupled equations of motion, accounting for Dirac sea contributions through summation of nucleonic tadpole diagrams and including the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons. The PV mixing effects are incorporated using an effective Lagrangian for charmonium states (with coupling fitted from radiative decay widths) and a spin-mixing Hamiltonian for bottomonium states.

## Key numbers

- For J/psi and eta_c at nuclear matter saturation density (rho_B = rho_0) and isospin asymmetry eta=0, PV mixing leads to substantial rise (drop) in the mass of J/psi^|| (eta_c) meson, with the effect much larger at high magnetic field values when AMMs are not considered
- For the excited charmonium state psi(1D), the mixing with eta_c(2S) along with Dirac sea contributions leads to significant modifications that should affect the decay width of psi(1D) -> D anti-D
- For the bottomonium state Upsilon(4S), the PV mixing shows opposite trend at higher magnetic field values (eB > 7 m_pi^2) for rho_B = rho_0 in symmetric nuclear matter (eta=0), because the effective mass of Upsilon(4S) becomes smaller than that of eta_b(4S) within the chiral effective model
- The isospin asymmetry parameter eta=0.5 produces very small dependence on the masses compared to the effects of (inverse) magnetic catalysis and PV mixing

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonium in-medium masses using the chiral effective model (Amarv Dmeson Tprc, Amarvepja, AM DP upsilon) by incorporating both Dirac sea contributions and PV mixing in the presence of magnetic fields. The magnetic catalysis effect at zero density and the inverse magnetic catalysis at saturation density when AMMs are included builds on findings from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Preis, Menezes) and Walecka model studies (Haber, Arghya, Balicm). The results enable predictions for heavy quarkonium production in ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy ion collision experiments at LHC and RHIC, where the created magnetic field can be huge.
