# Isospin Lattice Gas Model and Nuclear-Matter Phase Diagram and Pressure-Volume Isotherms

> We study a cubic lattice gas model for nuclear matter where each lattice site can be either occupied, by one proton or one neutron, or unoccupied. A nearest-neighbor interaction of the form $ - \sum_{<ij>} J_{ij}τ_{zi} τ_{zj}$ is assumed. Our model is an isospin-1 Ising model, with ${τ_z}$ = (1,0,-1) representing respectively (proton, vacancy, neutron). A kinetic-energy term has been included in our model. Under the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation our model exhibits the existence of a dense phase (liquid-like) and a rare phase (gas-like). The nuclear-matter p-v isotherms given by our model are discussed.

# Isospin Lattice Gas Model and Nuclear-Matter Phase Diagram and Pressure-Volume Isotherms

## Key claim

Under the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation, a cubic lattice gas model with an isospin-1 Ising Hamiltonian and an included kinetic-energy term exhibits a liquid-gas phase transition for symmetric nuclear matter, with a critical temperature of 18.4 MeV. The model produces pressure-volume isotherms that are similar to van der Waals isotherms but without metastable states in the coexistence region, thus requiring no Maxwell construction.

## Method

The authors constructed a cubic lattice gas model where each site can be occupied by a proton (τz = 1), a neutron (τz = -1), or be vacant (τz = 0), with a nearest-neighbor interaction of the form -Σ<ij> Jij τzi τzj using two interaction parameters (Js for pp and nn pairs, Jd for pn pairs). A kinetic-energy term proportional to ρ^(2/3) was added, guided by the Fermi gas model. The grand partition function was evaluated using the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation with Stirling's formula, and the thermodynamic properties were obtained by extremizing the free energy with respect to the vacancy fraction R and the proton-neutron asymmetry S.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: Tc = 18.4 MeV
- Interaction parameter C2 = 125 MeV, estimated from average potential energy in nuclear matter of about -40 MeV
- Kinetic parameter κ = 131.037 MeV, obtained from the condition α = (2/3)^(1/3) where α = 5κ/(6C2)
- Lattice spacing assumed: 1.5 fm
- Nucleon mass used for kinetic energy estimate: 940 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Yang lattice gas model (Lee and Yang, 1952) from atomic systems to nuclear matter by introducing two types of nucleons (proton and neutron) via an isospin-1 Ising model. It contrasts with standard many-body approaches to the nuclear liquid-gas transition such as Hartree-Fock approximation (Vautherin, 1984; Jaqaman et al., 1983), real-time Green's function methods (Su et al., 1987, 1989), and ring-diagram summation (Yang et al., 1987), which use continuous space-time rather than a lattice. The inclusion of a kinetic-energy term is a key new ingredient compared to earlier lattice gas models (Huang, 1963) and the authors' own previous unsuccessful attempt without it (Su et al., 1990). The model enables future investigations using more accurate methods such as Bethe-Peierls approximation or Monte Carlo simulations.
