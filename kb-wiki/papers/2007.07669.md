# Bayesian inference of nuclear symmetry energy from measured and imagined neutron skin thickness in $^{116,118,120,122,124,130,132}$Sn, $^{208}$Pb, and $^{48}$Ca

> The neutron skin thickness $Δr_{np}$ in heavy nuclei has been known as one of the most sensitive terrestrial probes of the nuclear symmetry energy around $\frac{2}{3}$ of the saturation density $ρ_0$ of nuclear matter. Existing neutron skin data mostly from hadronic observables suffer from large uncertainties and their extraction from experiments are often strongly model dependent. While waiting eagerly for the promised model-independent and high-precision neutron skin data for $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca from the parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX-II and CREX at JLab as well as MREX at MESA), within the Bayesian statistical framework using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model we infer the posterior probability distribution functions (PDFs) of the slope parameter $L$ of the nuclear symmetry energy at $ρ_0$ from imagined $Δr_{np}(^{208}$Pb$)=0.15$, 0.20, and 0.30 fm as well as $Δr_{np}(^{48}$Ca$)=0.12$, 0.15, and 0.25 fm, with different $1σ$ error bars. The results are compared with the PDFs of $L$ inferred using the same approach from the available $Δr_{np}$ data for Sn isotopes from hadronic probes. They are also compared with results from a recent Bayesian analysis of the radius and tidal deformability data of canonical neutron stars from GW170817 and NICER. The neutron skin data for Sn isotopes gives $L=45.5^{+26.5}_{-21.6}$ MeV surrounding its mean value or $L=53.4^{+18.6}_{-29.5}$ MeV surrounding its maximum {\it a posteriori} value, respectively, with the latter smaller than but consistent with the $L=66_{-20}^{+12}$ MeV from the neutron star data within their 68\% confidence intervals. In order to provide additionally useful information on $L$ extracted from the $Δr_{np}$ of Sn isotopes, the experimental error bar of $Δr_{np}(^{208}$Pb) should be at least smaller than 0.06 fm aimed by some current experiments.

## Key claim

Using a Bayesian statistical framework with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, the authors infer that the neutron skin thickness data for Sn isotopes alone give a slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy of 45.5^{+26.5}_{-21.6} MeV (mean) or 53.4^{+18.6}_{-29.5} MeV (maximum a posteriori), which is smaller than but consistent with the L = 66_{-20}^{+12} MeV from neutron star data within 68% confidence intervals; however, the extraction of L at saturation density from neutron skin data remains model dependent because the data primarily constrain the slope of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities around 0.10 fm^{-3}.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model with the MSL0 interaction, varying three isovector parameters (symmetry energy E_sym^0, slope parameter L, and isovector effective mass m_v^*/m) uniformly within prior ranges of 25-35 MeV, 0-120 MeV, and 0.5-1, respectively. They calculated the posterior probability distribution functions (PDFs) of L from real neutron skin thickness data of seven Sn isotopes (^{116,118,120,122,124,130,132}Sn) and from imagined neutron skin thickness values for ^{208}Pb (0.15, 0.20, and 0.30 fm with 1-sigma error bars of 0.02, 0.04, and 0.06 fm) and ^{48}Ca (0.12, 0.15, and 0.25 fm with 1-sigma error bars of 0.01 and 0.02 fm), using the Markov-Chain Monte Carlo approach with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm.

## Key numbers

- From Sn isotope neutron skin data: L = 45.5^{+26.5}_{-21.6} MeV (mean) or L = 53.4^{+18.6}_{-29.5} MeV (maximum a posteriori) at 68% confidence level.
- From neutron star data (GW170817 and NICER): L = 66_{-20}^{+12} MeV at 68% confidence level.
- For imagined ^{208}Pb data with Delta r_np = 0.20 +/- 0.02 fm: L = 65.2^{+26.4}_{-15.6} MeV (mean) or L = 74.8^{+16.8}_{-25.2} MeV (MAP).
- For imagined ^{208}Pb data with Delta r_np = 0.15 +/- 0.02 fm: L = 35.6^{+19.1}_{-25.8} MeV (mean) or L = 35.5^{+19.2}_{-25.7} MeV (MAP).
- For imagined ^{48}Ca data with Delta r_np = 0.15 +/- 0.01 fm: L = 30.8^{+9.8}_{-30.8} MeV (mean) or L = 16.3^{+24.3}_{-16.3} MeV (MAP).
- The neutron skin data for Sn isotopes determine L(rho^* = 0.10 fm^{-3}) = 43.7^{+5.3}_{-5.3} MeV at 68% confidence level.
- To provide additional useful information on L from Sn data, the experimental error bar of Delta r_np(^{208}Pb) should be at least smaller than 0.06 fm.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian analyses of nuclear symmetry energy from neutron star observations (Xie 2019) and from traditional chi-squared fitting of Sn isotope neutron skin data (MSL0 interaction, 2010). It directly addresses questions posed by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010) about what novel information new measurements bring and how they reduce uncertainties. The results enable quantitative predictions for how upcoming parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX-II, CREX at JLab, and MREX at MESA) will improve constraints on the symmetry energy slope parameter L, showing that Delta r_np(^{208}Pb) = 0.17-0.18 fm with an error bar of about 0.02 fm would yield a PDF of L compatible with Sn data, while error bars larger than 0.06 fm would provide negligible improvement.
