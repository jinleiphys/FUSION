# Chiral corrections to the Adler-Weisberger sum rule

> The Adler-Weisberger sum rule for the nucleon axial-vector charge, $g_A$, offers a unique signature of chiral symmetry and its breaking in QCD. Its derivation relies on both algebraic aspects of chiral symmetry, which guarantee the convergence of the sum rule, and dynamical aspects of chiral symmetry breaking---as exploited using chiral perturbation theory---which allow the rigorous inclusion of explicit chiral symmetry breaking effects due to light-quark masses. The original derivations obtained the sum rule in the chiral limit and, without the benefit of chiral perturbation theory, made various attempts at extrapolating to non-vanishing pion masses. In this paper, the leading, universal, chiral corrections to the chiral-limit sum rule are obtained. Using PDG data, a recent parametrization of the pion-nucleon total cross-sections in the resonance region given by the SAID group, as well as recent Roy-Steiner equation determinations of subthreshold amplitudes, threshold parameters, and correlated low-energy constants, the Adler-Weisberger sum rule is confronted with experimental data. With uncertainty estimates associated with the cross-section parameterization, the Goldberger-Treimann discrepancy, and the truncation of the sum rule at $\mathcal{O}(M_π^4)$ in the chiral expansion, this work finds $g_A = 1.248 \pm 0.010 \pm \ 0.007 \pm 0.013$.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading chiral corrections to the Adler-Weisberger sum rule, which originally held only in the chiral limit, and finds that the corrected sum rule yields g_A = 1.248 with uncertainties from the cross-section parameterization, the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, and truncation of the chiral expansion at O(M_pi^4).

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory to compute the leading universal chiral corrections to the chiral-limit Adler-Weisberger sum rule, expressed as the "Adler-Weisberger discrepancy" Delta_AW at O(M_pi^3). They construct a parametrization of the pion-nucleon total cross-section difference sigma^- across four energy regions: threshold (effective range expansion), resonance region (SAID partial-wave analysis), transition region (PDG data interpolation), and Regge region (power-law fit to PDG data). The corrected sum rule is then evaluated using Roy-Steiner equation determinations of subthreshold amplitudes and low-energy constants, treating it as a nonlinear equation for g_A.

## Key numbers

* g_A = 1.248 +/- 0.010 +/- 0.007 +/- 0.013, where uncertainties are from the cross-section parametrization, the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, and truncation of the chiral expansion at O(M_pi^4)
* The effective range expansion for 2 sigma^- at threshold gives 3.56(14) mb - 2 q_cm^2 * 86(3) mb/GeV^2, where q_cm is in GeV
* The Regge-region fit for 2 sigma^- above k = 3.3 GeV gives 5.76(2) k^{-0.459(1)} mb, with k in GeV
* The subthreshold coefficient d_00^- from this paper's parametrization is 1.50(3) M_pi^{-2}, compared to 1.53(2) from Hohler (1983) and 1.41(1) from Roy-Steiner equations
* The AW discrepancy Delta_AW is 0.257(36) using this paper's parametrization, contributing 20.2% to the g_A calculation

## Context

This work extends the original Adler-Weisberger sum rule derivations by Adler (1965) and Weisberger (1965), which obtained the sum rule only in the chiral limit and lacked chiral perturbation theory for extrapolating to physical pion masses. The paper uses modern Roy-Steiner equation analyses by Hoferichter et al. (2015) for subthreshold amplitudes and low-energy constants, and the SAID partial-wave analysis by Workman et al. (2012) for the resonance region. The results enable rigorous, controlled uncertainty estimates for the axial-vector coupling constant from the Adler-Weisberger sum rule within chiral perturbation theory, and demonstrate that the sum rule's convergence follows directly from chiral symmetry rather than Regge model assumptions.
