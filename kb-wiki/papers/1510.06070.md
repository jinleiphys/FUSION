# Jacobi no-core shell model for p-shell nuclei

> We introduce an algorithm to obtain coefficients of fractional parentage for light $p$-shell nuclei. The coefficients enable to use Jacobi coordinates in no-core shell model calculations separating off the center-of-mass motion. Fully antisymmetrized basis states are given together with recoupling coefficients that allow one to apply two- and three-nucleon operators. As an example, we study the dependence on the harmonic oscillator frequency of $^3$H, $^4$He, $^6$He, $^6$Li and $^7$Li and extract their binding and excitation energies. The coefficients will be made openly accessible as HDF5 data files.

## Key claim
The paper introduces an algorithm to obtain coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp) for light p-shell nuclei, enabling the use of Jacobi coordinates in no-core shell model calculations while fully separating off center-of-mass motion. The authors demonstrate that once antisymmetrized basis states are obtained, individual binding energy calculations are not computationally expensive because the states are independent of the interaction and the harmonic oscillator frequency.

## Method
The authors construct fully antisymmetrized harmonic oscillator basis states in Jacobi coordinates for A-body systems by recursively diagonalizing the antisymmetrization operator, starting from A=3. They derive recoupling coefficients that allow separation of two-nucleon and three-nucleon clusters from the A-body system using Talmi-Moshinsky brackets and angular momentum recoupling. As a test, they calculate binding and excitation energies for 3H, 4He, 6He, 6Li, and 7Li using SRG-evolved chiral interactions, studying the dependence on the harmonic oscillator frequency.

## Context
This work extends the Jacobi-coordinate no-core shell model approach of Navratil (2000) from s-shell to p-shell nuclei, overcoming the antisymmetrization difficulty that previously forced most calculations into the m-scheme basis. The method enables exact separation of center-of-mass motion and avoids expensive on-the-fly transformations of interaction matrix elements from relative to single-particle coordinates, which is especially relevant for high-order chiral interactions including three-nucleon and potentially four-nucleon forces. The approach enables future applications of three-nucleon interactions within this scheme and allows benchmarking against Faddeev-Yakubovsky or coupled-cluster calculations.
