# Normal mode analysis within relativistic massive transport

> In this paper, we address the normal mode analysis on the linearized Boltzmann equation for massive particles in the relaxation time approximation. One intriguing feature of massive transport is the coupling of the secular equations between the sound and heat channels. This coupling vanishes as the mass approaches zero. By utilizing the argument principle in complex analysis, we determine the existence condition for collective modes and find the onset transition behavior of collective modes previously observed in massless systems. We numerically determine the critical wavenumber for the existence of each mode under various values of the scaled mass. Within the range of scaled masses considered, the critical wavenumbers for the heat and shear channels decrease with increasing scaled mass, while that of the sound channel exhibits a non-monotonic dependence on the scaled mass. In addition, we analytically derive the dispersion relations for these collective modes in the long-wavelength limit. Notably, kinetic theory also incorporates collisionless dissipation effects, known as Landau damping. We find that the branch cut structure responsible for Landau damping differs significantly from the massless case: whereas the massless system features only two branch points, the massive system exhibits an infinite number of such points forming a continuous branch cut.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in relativistic massive transport within the relaxation time approximation, the sound and heat channels become coupled in the secular equations, unlike the massless case where they decouple; this coupling vanishes only in the limit of zero mass, and the authors find that the critical wavenumber for the existence of collective modes in the sound channel exhibits a non-monotonic dependence on the scaled mass, while the heat and shear channels show monotonic decreases.

## Method

The authors linearize the relativistic Boltzmann equation for massive particles around global equilibrium, adopting a novel relaxation time approximation that preserves collision invariance by retaining the five zero eigenmodes and approximating all nonzero eigenvalues with the smallest one. They perform a Fourier transform to obtain a dimensionless equation, then derive secular equations for the fluctuation amplitudes of conserved charge densities, which separate into three block-diagonal sectors: a coupled sound-heat channel (3x3 determinant) and a shear channel (degenerate 1x1). Using the argument principle from complex analysis, they determine the existence conditions for collective modes by analyzing the winding number of the secular determinant in the complex frequency plane, and numerically compute critical wavenumbers for various values of the scaled mass parameter z = m/T.

## Key numbers

* The critical wavenumber for the heat and shear channels decreases with increasing scaled mass across the range of scaled masses considered.
* The critical wavenumber for the sound channel exhibits a non-monotonic dependence on the scaled mass, unlike the monotonic behavior in the heat and shear channels.
* The massless system features only two branch points in the branch cut structure responsible for Landau damping, while the massive system exhibits an infinite number of such points forming a continuous branch cut.
* The paper defines the scaled mass as z = m/T, where m is the particle mass and T is the temperature, and uses the dimensionless wavenumber kappa = sqrt(-kappa_alpha kappa^alpha)/T and frequency omega = (k dot u)/T.

## Context

This work extends the normal mode analysis of Romatschke (2015) and Hu (2023) from massless to massive relativistic transport within the relaxation time approximation. The authors find that the sound-heat coupling, which was absent in the massless RTA studies, emerges naturally in massive systems and is not an artifact of the approximation. The analysis enables future investigations into how particle mass affects the onset of hydrodynamic behavior and the transition between collective mode regimes, and provides a framework for comparing with retarded correlation function calculations in massive transport such as those by Hataei (2025) and Bajec (2025).
