# Axial-vector dominance predictions in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering

> The axial form factor plays a crucial role in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, but the error of the theoretical cross section due to uncertainties of $G_A$ remains to be established. Reversely, the extraction of $G_A$ from the neutrino nucleus cross section suffers from large systematic errors due to nuclear model dependencies, while the use of single parameter dipole fits underestimates the errors and prevents an identification of the relevant kinematics for this determination. We propose to use a generalized axial-vector-meson-dominance (AVMD) in conjunction with large-$N_c$ and high energy QCD constrains to model the nucleon axial form factor, as well as the half width rule as an a priori uncertainty estimate. The minimal hadronic ansatz comprises the sum of two monopoles corresponding to the lightest axial-vector mesons being coupled to the axial current. The parameters of the resulting axial form factor are the masses and widths of the two axial mesons as obtained from the averaged PDG values. By applying the half width rule in a Monte Carlo simulation, a distribution of theoretical predictions can then be generated for the neutrino-nucleus quasielastic cross section. We test the model by applying it to the $(ν_μ,μ)$ quasielastic cross section from $^{12}$C for the kinematics of the MiniBooNE experiment. The resulting predictions have no free parameters. We find that the relativistic Fermi gas model globally reproduces the experimental data, giving $χ^2/ \# bins = 0.81$. A $Q^2$-dependent error analysis of the neutrino data shows that the uncertainties in the axial form factor $G_A(Q^2)$ are comparable to the ones induced by the a priori half width rule. We identify the most sensitive region to be in the range $0.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 0.6 \,{\rm GeV}^2$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a theoretically motivated axial-vector-meson-dominance (AVMD) model for the nucleon axial form factor, with uncertainties estimated via the half-width rule, can predict the MiniBooNE quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross section without fitting to neutrino data, achieving a global \(\chi^2/\# \text{bins} = 0.81\). The hedge is that this agreement holds only within the relativistic Fermi gas model, and the authors note that systematic discrepancies remain at the edges of the kinematic phase space, suggesting limitations of the nuclear model rather than the axial form factor.

## Method

The authors construct the nucleon axial form factor \(G_A(Q^2)\) as a product of two monopoles corresponding to the lightest axial-vector mesons (\(a_1\) and \(a_1'\)), with masses and widths taken from PDG averages. They apply the half-width rule in a Monte Carlo simulation to generate a distribution of axial form factors, using Gaussian distributions for the meson masses with variances equal to half their widths. This form factor is then inserted into a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model for \(^{12}\)C to compute the flux-averaged double-differential charged-current quasielastic cross section for the MiniBooNE kinematics, with no free parameters fitted to neutrino data.

## Key numbers

- Global goodness-of-fit: \(\chi^2 / \# \text{bins} = 0.81\) for 137 bins, comparing AVMD predictions to MiniBooNE data without any fitting.
- Axial meson masses used: \(m_{a_1} = 1.230\) GeV and \(m_{a_1'} = 1.647\) GeV, with widths \(\Gamma_{a_1} = 0.425\) GeV and \(\Gamma_{a_1'} = 0.254\) GeV.
- Most sensitive \(Q^2\) region for axial form factor determination: \(0.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 0.6\) GeV\(^2\).
- Fermi momentum of the RFG model: \(k_F = 225\) MeV.
- When fitting the two axial masses to MiniBooNE data (with normalization factor \(\lambda=0.96\) and energy shift \(Q_b=17\) MeV), the minimum yields \(\chi^2/\nu = 0.31\) at \(m_{a_1} = m_{a_1'} = 1293\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) inspired meson dominance approach of Masjuan, Ruiz Arriola, and Broniowski (2013) to neutrino-nucleus scattering, providing a theoretically grounded alternative to the conventional dipole axial form factor parameterization. It contrasts with analyses such as Nieves et al. (2012), which fit a dipole axial mass to MiniBooNE data including nuclear effects like RPA correlations and meson exchange currents, achieving \(\chi^2/\nu = 0.24\) but with questionable statistical significance. The AVMD approach enables a priori uncertainty estimates for the axial form factor without relying on neutrino data fitting, and identifies the \(Q^2\) range most sensitive for future extractions of \(G_A\) from neutrino experiments.
