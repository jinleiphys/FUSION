# The Bose-Einstein correlation function $C_2(Q)$ from a Quantum Field Theory point of view

> We show that a recently proposed derivation of Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) by means of a specific version of thermal Quantum Field Theory (QFT), supplemented by operator-field evolution of the Langevin type, allows for a deeper understanding of the possible coherent behaviour of the emitting source and a clear identification of the origin of the observed shape of the BEC function $C_2(Q)$. Previous conjectures in this matter obtained by other approaches are confirmed and have received complementary explanation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Bose-Einstein correlation function \(C_2(Q)\) derived from a thermal Quantum Field Theory with Langevin-type operator evolution yields a form identical to that obtained by Weiner (1989) via quantum statistical methods, but differs from the formula of Akkelin, Lednicky, and Sinyukov (2002) because the two approaches define coherence differently: the present work introduces coherence as a property of the fields (via a constant external force \(P\) in the Langevin equation), whereas Akkelin et al. (2002) divide the source into separate coherent and chaotic subsources.

## Method

The authors model the hadronizing system as a heat bath of damped oscillators at temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), with a single selected boson described by an annihilation operator \(b(\vec{k},t)\). The time evolution of this operator follows a Langevin equation containing a stochastic Langevin force, a heat-bath term, and a constant external force \(P\). The two-particle correlation function \(C_2(Q)\) is computed from thermal averages of the Fourier-transformed stationary solution, and the delta functions arising from the infinite, uniform reservoir are replaced by smearing functions \(\Omega(Q\cdot r)\) to account for the finite space-time extent of the source.

## Key numbers

* The correlation function \(C_2(Q)\) reaches its maximum at \(Q=0\) and falls to its asymptotic value \(C_2 = 1\) at large \(Q\), already at \(Q \sim 1\) GeV/c.
* For the illustrative plots, the source size parameter is set to \(r = 1\) fm and the coherence parameter \(\alpha = 0.2\) (corresponding to chaoticity \(p = 0.8\) in the Weiner (1989) notation).
* The parameter \(\alpha\) takes values \(0, 0.25, 1, 4\) in the second figure, corresponding to chaoticity parameters \(p = 1.0, 0.8, 0.5, 0.2\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the thermal QFT approach of Kozlov (2002) and the Langevin evolution formalism of Kozlov (1998, 2001) to derive the Bose-Einstein correlation function. It confirms and provides a complementary explanation for the quantum statistical results of Weiner (1989), while explicitly contrasting them with the coherent/chaotic subsource model of Akkelin, Lednicky, and Sinyukov (2002). The approach enables future generalization to multiple particle species, resonance production, and final-state interactions, which are left for later work.
