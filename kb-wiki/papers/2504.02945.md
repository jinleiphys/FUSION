# Is the central compact object in HESS J1731-347 a hybrid star with a quark core? An analysis with the constant speed of sound parametrization

> In this work, we investigate the possibility of the compact object in HESS J1731-347 with $M=0.77_{-0.17}^{+0.20}M_{\odot}$ and $R=10.4_{-0.78}^{+0.86} km$ to be a hybrid star with quark matter in the inner core. The observation of this low mass compact star dictates the use of softer equation of state which on the contrary cannot explain the massive compact stars. This poses a new challenge for the astrophysicists in their search for the equation of state of compact objects. The hybrid equations of state are constructed using the IUFSU parametrization based on the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory for the hadronic part and the generic constant speed of sound parametrization (CSS) for the phase transition to quark matter.The CSS framework is characterized by three key parameters, namely the transition density $(ρ_{tr})$, the energy jumps $(Δ\varepsilon)$ and the speed of sound $(C_s)$. Here, our primary aim is to investigate the influence of individual CSS parameters on the formation of a object of small mass and radius compatible with HESS J1731-347 parameters. We have also examined the effect of hadronic parameters such as effective mass, symmetry energy, and the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation densities on the formation of this compact object.Finally our analysis suggests that, within a 1$σ$ credible level, HESS J1731-347 aligns with the scenario of a stable hybrid star with early deconfinement and higher energy gap

## Key claim

The paper claims that the central compact object in HESS J1731-347, with mass and radius estimates at the 1-sigma credible level, can be described as a stable hybrid star with a quark core, but only under specific conditions: early deconfinement (low transition density) and a higher energy gap between hadronic and quark phases.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state using the IUFSU parametrization of the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory for the hadronic phase and the constant speed of sound (CSS) parametrization for the quark phase, with a sharp first-order phase transition via Maxwell construction. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations, systematically varying the three CSS parameters (transition density, energy density jump, and speed of sound) as well as hadronic parameters (effective mass, symmetry energy, and slope of symmetry energy at saturation). The resulting hybrid star configurations are compared against observational constraints from HESS J1731-347, PSR J0740+6620, and GW170817.

## Key numbers

- Mass of HESS J1731-347: M = 0.77 (+0.20, -0.17) solar masses at 1-sigma credible level
- Radius of HESS J1731-347: R = 10.4 (+0.86, -0.78) km at 1-sigma credible level
- For transition density 0.25 fm^-3 and C_s^2 = 0.60, hybrid branch satisfies HESS J1731-347 constraints for energy gaps from 100 to 350 MeV fm^-3
- For transition density 0.30 fm^-3, the critical energy gap for disconnected hybrid branch is Delta_epsilon_cr = 270.37 MeV fm^-3; HESS J1731-347 twin star configuration requires energy gap above this value
- For transition density 0.30 fm^-3, the mass constraint restricts energy gap to approximately 300-750 MeV fm^-3, while the radius constraint restricts it to approximately 350-525 MeV fm^-3
- For transition density 0.40 fm^-3, energy gaps of 900 and 1100 MeV fm^-3 satisfy constraints at 1-sigma level, and 500 MeV fm^-3 at 2-sigma level
- Hadronic sector at transition density 0.30 fm^-3: transition energy density = 293.46 MeV fm^-3, transition pressure = 20.48 MeV fm^-3, mass = 0.8247 solar masses, radius = 12.156 km

## Context

This work extends previous studies of HESS J1731-347 that have considered it as a strange quark star (Horvath 2023, Rather 2023, Gholami 2024), a hybrid star with early deconfinement (Laskos-Patkos 2023, Mariani 2024), or a dark matter-admixed neutron star (Hong 2024, Pal 2024). The analysis uses the CSS parametrization (Alford 2013, Tsaloukidis 2022, Laskos-Patkos 2023) rather than more complex quark models like the MIT bag model or NJL model. The results enable future studies to constrain the CSS parameters and the hadron-quark phase transition properties using the combined observations of low-mass and high-mass compact stars, and suggest that HESS J1731-347 could be a twin star with a hadronic counterpart.
