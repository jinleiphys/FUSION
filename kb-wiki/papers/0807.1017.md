# String Free Energy, Hagedorn and Gauge/String Duality

> We give examples of modeling the string free energy whose behavior mimics that of QCD: a power-law at high temperature and an exponential decrease at low temperature. Although the effective description is in terms of strings, no limiting temperature exists, as expected for a crossover.

# String Free Energy, Hagedorn and Gauge/String Duality

## Key claim
The paper proposes two distinct string-based models whose free energy reproduces the QCD behavior of a power-law at high temperature and an exponential decrease at low temperature, with no limiting (Hagedorn) temperature, consistent with a crossover rather than a phase transition. The key hedge is that the effective description is in terms of strings, but the models are phenomenological and the full string dual of QCD remains unknown.

## Method
The first model uses an ensemble of strings with quantized tensions, where the free energy is a sum over string species with different Regge slopes; a temperature-dependent lower bound on the summation index suppresses long strings that cause the Hagedorn divergence. The second model uses a warped AdS-Schwarzschild black hole geometry (metric of AdS5 times a compact space), where a single ten-dimensional temperature below the Hagedorn temperature gives rise to many different four-dimensional temperatures via the warping, so the four-dimensional temperature can become arbitrarily large without the string theory side exceeding the Hagedorn temperature.

## Key numbers
- Hagedorn temperature: \( T_H = 1/(4\pi\sqrt{\alpha'}) \)
- For the quantized tension model with polynomial \(\mathcal{P}_k(n) = a_k n^k\), the high-temperature free energy scales as \( F \sim T^{2\gamma + 2(\delta+1)/k} \), with parameters chosen as \(\delta = -1\) and \(\gamma = 2\)
- For the warped geometry model, the free energy density scales as \( f \sim T^4 \), matching the QCD Stefan-Boltzmann behavior
- The black hole horizon radius is \( r_T = \pi R^2 T \), giving the lower integration limit in the warped geometry free energy expression

## Context
This work extends the approach of Polchinski and Strassler (2002) for hard scattering amplitudes in warped geometry, and the quantized tension string models of Andreev and Siegel (2004) for scattering amplitudes with Regge poles and parton behavior. It addresses the puzzle that lattice QCD shows an analytic crossover rather than a phase transition at zero baryon chemical potential (cross, 2006; Stephanov, 2004), requiring a string description valid at both low and high temperatures. The paper enables future work on constructing a complete string dual of QCD that reproduces both the high-temperature power-law and the low-temperature exponential behavior, as well as incorporating logarithmic scaling violations and parton structure.
