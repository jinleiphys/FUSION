# Bubble dynamics in a strong first-order quark-hadron transition

> We investigate the dynamics of a strong first-order quark-hadron transition driven by cubic interaction via homogeneous bubble nucleation in the Friedberg-Lee model. The one-loop effective thermodynamics potential of the model and the critical bubble profiles have been calculated at different temperatures and chemical potentials. By taking the temperature and the chemical potential as the variables, the evolutions of the surface tension, the typical radius of the critical bubble and the shift in the coarse-grained free energy in the presence of a nucleation bubble are obtained and the limit on the reliability of the thin-wall approximation is also addressed accordingly. Our results are compared to those obtained for a weak first-order quark-hadron phase transition, especially the spinodal decomposition is relevant.

## Key claim

The paper investigates bubble nucleation dynamics in a strong first-order quark-hadron phase transition using the Friedberg-Lee model, finding that the surface tension exhibits a non-monotonic behavior with temperature and chemical potential, first increasing to a maximum then decreasing, which differs from weak first-order transitions where surface tension drops to zero at a spinodal temperature. The authors establish that the thin-wall approximation breaks down when the temperature deviates sufficiently from the critical temperature, with the turning point of surface tension serving as a landmark for this breakdown.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop effective thermodynamic potential of the Friedberg-Lee model at finite temperature and chemical potential, then numerically solve the nonlinear ordinary differential equation for the critical bubble profile with boundary conditions requiring the field to approach the false vacuum at infinity and have zero derivative at the origin. From these saddle-point solutions, they calculate the surface tension, typical critical bubble radius, and the shift in coarse-grained free energy as functions of temperature and chemical potential, comparing exact numerical results with the thin-wall approximation.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential: \(T_c \simeq 119.8\) MeV, where the two minima of the effective potential become degenerate
- Critical chemical potential at fixed temperature \(T = 50\) MeV: \(\mu_c \simeq 256.4\) MeV
- Maximum surface tension at zero chemical potential: \(\Sigma(T) \simeq 7.38\) MeV/fm\(^2\) at \(T \simeq 109\) MeV
- At \(T \simeq 114.5\) MeV and zero chemical potential, \(\Delta F_b/T \simeq 1\), marking the transition where nucleation rate becomes strongly suppressed
- At fixed temperature \(T = 50\) MeV, when \(\mu \simeq 231\) MeV, \(\Delta F_b/T \simeq 1\)
- The turning point for thin-wall approximation validity occurs at \(\mu \simeq 230\) MeV for fixed temperature \(T = 50\) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of weak first-order quark-hadron phase transitions using the linear sigma model coupled to quarks (Scavenius 2000, Bessa 2008) to the strong first-order regime using the Friedberg-Lee model, which provides a zero-temperature potential barrier. The results enable quantitative studies of bubble nucleation dynamics relevant to neutron star astrophysics with quark cores and gravitational wave production from cosmological phase transitions, particularly beyond the thin-wall approximation that is widely adopted in the literature.
