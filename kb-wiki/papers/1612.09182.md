# Soft modes in the proton-neutron pairing channel as precursors of deuteron condensate in N=Z nuclei

> Relativistic nuclear response theory is formulated for the proton-neutron pairing, or deuteron transfer, channel. The approach is based on the meson-nucleon Lagrangian of Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and advances the relativistic field theory to connect consistently the high-energy scale of heavy mesons, the medium-energy range of the pion and the low-energy domain of emergent collective vibrations (phonons) in a parameter-free way. Mesons and phonons build up the in-medium nucleon-nucleon interaction in spin-isospin transfer channels, in particular, the phonon-exchange part takes care of the leading-order retardation effects. In this framework, we explore $J^π = 0^+$ and $J^π = 1^+$ channels of the nuclear response to the proton-neutron pair removal and addition in $^{56}$Ni and $^{100}$Sn with a special focus on the lowest (soft) modes as precursors of deuteron condensate and candidates for being the mediators of the proton-neutron pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dynamical proton-neutron pairing correlations, mediated by the exchange of collective vibrations (phonons) built from the same meson-exchange interaction, can produce soft (low-energy) modes in the Jπ = 0⁺ and Jπ = 1⁺ pair-transfer channels of N=Z nuclei, acting as precursors of a deuteron condensate; however, the authors hedge that the isoscalar (T=0, J=1) channel does not show strong collectivity sufficient to form a condensate, consistent with experimental evidence against such a condensate in finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors formulate a relativistic nuclear response theory for the proton-neutron particle-particle (pairing) channel, based on the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) meson-nucleon Lagrangian with the NL3* parameter set. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the time blocking approximation (pn-pp-RTBA), which includes both static meson-exchange interactions (ρ-meson, pion, and a Landau-Migdal term) and dynamical retardation effects from coupling to isoscalar phonons (J ≤ 6, Ω ≤ 20 MeV) computed within the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA). The strength functions for Jπ = 0⁺ and Jπ = 1⁺ pair removal and addition are computed for the doubly magic N=Z nuclei ⁵⁶Ni and ¹⁰⁰Sn, with a truncation on two-particle energies at |εp + εn| ≤ 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵⁶Ni Jπ = 0⁺ addition modes: the meson-exchange interaction and the particle-vibration coupling each introduce approximately 2 MeV downward shifts of the lowest solutions, while the corresponding shifts in the removal sector are twice smaller (approximately 1 MeV each).
- For ⁵⁶Ni Jπ = 1⁺ removal modes: the meson-exchange interaction shifts the lowest solution downward by about 1.5 MeV, and the particle-vibration coupling shifts it further downward by about 0.5 MeV.
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn Jπ = 0⁺ removal modes: the meson-exchange interaction shifts the lowest solution downward by about 2 MeV, and the particle-vibration coupling shifts it further downward by about 1 MeV.
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn Jπ = 1⁺ addition modes: the meson-exchange interaction shifts the lowest solution downward by about 1.5 MeV, and the particle-vibration coupling shifts it further downward by about 0.5 MeV.
- The phonon space is truncated to isoscalar modes with multipolarities Jμ ≤ 6, frequencies Ωμ ≤ 20 MeV, and reduced transition probabilities exceeding 5% of the maximal one for the given multipolarity.
- A finite imaginary part Δ = 200 keV is used in the energy variable for representation of the strength functions.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic time blocking approximation (RTBA) framework, previously developed for particle-hole channels (Litvinova, Ring, Tselyaev 2007) and for spin-isospin response (Marketin, Litvinova, Vretenar et al. 2012; Robin, Litvinova 2016), to the proton-neutron particle-particle (pairing) channel. It addresses the long-standing question of proton-neutron pairing in N=Z nuclei, where previous work by Frauendorf (2014) concluded that experimental evidence supports isovector (T=1, J=0) pair vibrational excitations as precursors of condensate but speaks against an isoscalar (T=0, J=1) deuteron condensate. The approach enables a parameter-free description of dynamical pairing correlations mediated by phonon exchange, in contrast to phenomenological proton-neutron pairing used in proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) calculations by Niksic, Marketin, Vretenar et al. (2005) and Engel, Bender, Dobaczewski et al. (1999).
