# Nuclear Potential with Two Pion Exchange

> We carefully calculate the nucleon-nucleon interaction due to two pion exchange processes by properly evaluating the corresponding Feynman diagrams. In the estimation, we have made no approximation, and instead, we carry out the numerical integrations of three Feynman parameters. It is found that the two pion exchange potential gives rise to the attractive force which corresponds to the effective scalar meson with its mass of $m_s\simeq 4.7 m_π$ and its strength of ${g_s^2\over 4π}\simeq 1.45 $. There is a strong isospin dependence of $(\bmτ_1\cdot \bmτ_2)^2 $ which cannot be simulated by the one boson exchange model calculations.

# Nuclear Potential with Two Pion Exchange

## Key claim
The two-pion exchange Feynman diagram, evaluated without approximation via numerical integration of three Feynman parameters, produces an attractive nucleon-nucleon potential that can be represented as an effective scalar meson exchange with mass \(m_s \simeq 4.7 m_\pi\) and strength \(g_s^2/4\pi \simeq 1.45\), but only up to momentum transfer \(\boldsymbol{q}^2 \simeq (6 m_\pi)^2\) and with a small increase (up to \(\sim 20\%\)) in these parameters as a function of \(\boldsymbol{q}^2\).

## Method
The authors evaluated the fourth-order two-pion exchange Feynman diagram for nucleon-nucleon scattering by introducing three Feynman parameters \(x, y, z\) and performing the four-momentum integration analytically, leaving a three-dimensional numerical integration over these parameters. They then fit the resulting T-matrix to a Yukawa-type form \(T(\boldsymbol{q}) \propto -(\boldsymbol{\tau}_1\cdot\boldsymbol{\tau}_2)^2 / (\boldsymbol{q}^2 + m_s^2)\) in the non-relativistic limit, using the static approximation \(q_0^2 \ll \boldsymbol{q}^2\) and taking the \(\pi NN\) coupling constant as \(g_\pi^2/4\pi \simeq 8\).

## Key numbers
- Effective scalar meson mass from two-pion exchange: \(m_s \simeq 4.7 m_\pi \simeq 650\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Effective scalar coupling strength: \(g_s^2/4\pi \simeq 1.45\) (with \(g_\pi^2/4\pi \simeq 8\)).
- For the \(T=0\) (isospin-singlet) NN channel, the effective sigma meson parameters are \(m_\sigma \simeq 650\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(g_\sigma^2/4\pi \simeq 13\).
- Phenomenological values from OBEP fits to NN scattering data (Machleidt, 1989; Machleidt et al., 1987): \(m_\sigma \simeq 615\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(g_\sigma^2/4\pi \simeq 11.7\).
- The fit parameter \(A\) in the T-matrix expression is \(A \simeq 0.57\).
- The T-matrix representation is valid up to \(\boldsymbol{q}^2 \simeq (6 m_\pi)^2\).

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing problem of the medium-range attraction in the nucleon-nucleon potential, which in one-boson-exchange models (OBEP) is artificially simulated by a scalar meson that does not exist in nature. Prior attempts to explain this attraction via second-order one-pion exchange with intermediate \(\Delta\)-resonance states (Holinde, 1981; Val, 1984) were found insufficient. The authors show that the two-pion exchange process, without approximation, yields an effective scalar interaction whose mass and coupling constant agree quantitatively with the phenomenological sigma meson parameters determined from NN scattering data by Machleidt (1989) and Machleidt et al. (1987). The result also reveals a strong isospin dependence proportional to \((\boldsymbol{\tau}_1\cdot\boldsymbol{\tau}_2)^2\) that cannot be reproduced by one-boson-exchange models, explaining the empirical observation that proton-neutron interactions are much stronger than those between identical nucleons. This work enables a re-examination of the one-boson-exchange potential with new constraints on the \(\pi NN\) coupling constant.
