# Nucleon Structure using lattice QCD

> A review of recent nucleon structure calculations within lattice QCD is presented. The nucleon excited states, the axial charge, the isovector momentum fraction and helicity distribution are discussed, assessing the methods applied for their study, including approaches to evaluate the disconnected contributions. Results on the spin carried by the quarks in the nucleon are also presented.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure, but the abstract's claim that results "are presented" glosses over the fact that the authors explicitly neglect disconnected contributions when extracting individual quark spin contributions, and that the axial charge results systematically underestimate the experimental value, with the source of this discrepancy still under investigation.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon three-point correlation functions on ensembles of gauge configurations generated with twisted mass fermions (TMF) at multiple lattice spacings and pion masses, extracting ground-state matrix elements from plateau regions of appropriate ratios. For the nucleon spectrum, they use a five-dimensional basis of interpolating fields to extract excited states. The isovector quantities (axial charge, momentum fraction, helicity moment) are obtained from connected diagrams only, while the isoscalar quantities needed for individual quark spin contributions are computed neglecting disconnected contributions, with stochastic techniques described for future inclusion of those terms.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial charge gA: all lattice results underestimate the experimental value as the pion mass approaches its physical value; results shown use Nf=2, Nf=2+1+1 TMF, Nf=2+1 DWF, hybrid, Nf=2 clover, and Nf=2+1 clover formulations
- Isovector momentum fraction ⟨x⟩_{u-d}: lattice results converge toward the physical point at near-physical pion mass
- Isovector helicity moment ⟨x⟩_{Δu-Δd}: similar convergence trend as the momentum fraction
- Nucleon spectrum: first excited state in positive parity (Roper) from lattice data is higher than the experimental Roper mass even at pion masses near the physical value; negative parity channel gives the experimental S_{11}(1535) mass
- Quark spin contributions: total spin carried by u- and d-quarks J^{u+d} ~ J^u since J^d ~ 0; quarks account for about half of the proton spin
- Disconnected contribution to isoscalar axial matrix element: expected to be of order 10% based on the plateau data shown

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD nucleon structure program by Alexandrou et al. (2010, 2011, 2012) and compares results across multiple fermion discretizations including twisted mass, clover, domain wall, and hybrid actions. The work enables systematic investigation of the long-standing discrepancy between lattice calculations and experiment for the nucleon axial charge, and provides a baseline for future calculations that will include disconnected contributions and use near-physical pion masses with large volumes. The spin decomposition results are compared with experimental data and with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory extrapolations as described in Alexandrou et al. (2013).
