# Coherent and incoherent $\mathrm{J/}ψ$ photonuclear production in an energy-dependent hot-spot model

> In a previous publication, we have presented a model for the photoproduction of $\mathrm{J/}ψ$ vector mesons off protons, where the proton structure in the impact-parameter plane is described by an energy-dependent hot-spot profile. Here we extend this model to study the photonuclear production of $\mathrm{J/}ψ$ vector mesons in coherent and incoherent interactions of heavy nuclei. We study two methods to extend the model to the nuclear case: using the standard Glauber-Gribov formalism and using geometric scaling to obtain the nuclear saturation scale. We find that the incoherent cross section changes sizably with the inclusion of subnucleonic hot spots, and that this change is energy dependent. We propose to search for this behavior by measuring the ratio of the incoherent to coherent cross section at different energies. We compare the results of our model to results from RHIC and from the Run 1 at the LHC finding a satisfactory agreement. We also present predictions for the LHC at the new energies reached in Run 2. The predictions include $\mathrm{J/}ψ$ production in ultra-peripheral collisions, as well as the recently observed photonuclear production in peripheral collisions.

# Coherent and incoherent J/ψ photonuclear production in an energy-dependent hot-spot model

## Key claim
The incoherent photonuclear cross section for J/ψ production changes sizably when subnucleonic hot spots are included in the nuclear profile, and this change is energy-dependent; the ratio of incoherent to coherent cross section develops a dependence on energy (decreasing as energy increases) that could be observed at RHIC and LHC.

## Method
The authors extend their earlier energy-dependent hot-spot model for J/ψ photoproduction off protons (Cepila 2016) to nuclear targets using two approaches: the Glauber-Gribov formalism (GG) and geometric scaling (GS) to obtain the nuclear saturation scale. The dipole-nucleus cross section is computed for two nuclear profile models, one with nucleons only and one with subnucleonic hot spots whose number increases with decreasing x, and the cross sections for coherent and incoherent photonuclear production are obtained via the Good-Walker formalism. All parameters were fixed from fits to proton-target data from HERA and the LHC, with no new free parameters introduced for the nuclear case.

## Key numbers
- Coherent photonuclear cross section off lead at x=0.015 (PHENIX Au-Au data): measured 5.7 ± 2.3 (stat) ± 1.2 (syst) μb; GS-hs model predicts 6.9 μb, GG-hs predicts 10.4 μb
- Incoherent photonuclear cross section off lead at x=0.015 (PHENIX Au-Au data): measured 3.6 ± 1.4 (stat) ± 0.7 (syst) μb; GS-hs model predicts 4.5 μb, GG-hs predicts 2.4 μb
- For the GG approach, numerical precision for coherent cross section is ≈2% and for incoherent cross section ≈13% (using 200 configurations)
- For the GS approach, 10000 profile configurations were used, achieving percent-level numerical precision
- The 70-90% centrality class in Pb-Pb collisions corresponds to impact parameter range (13.05, 14.96) fm at Run 1 energies (√sNN = 2.76 TeV) and (13.1, 15.0) fm at Run 2 energies (√sNN = 5.02 TeV)
- The virtuality of the quasi-real photon is taken as Q² = 0.05 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the energy-dependent hot-spot model of Cepila (2016), which described exclusive and dissociative J/ψ photoproduction off protons, to nuclear targets, following earlier work by Mantysaari (2016, 2017) who used a fixed-number hot-spot model for proton and nuclear targets. The key new element is the energy evolution of the hot-spot number, which produces an energy-dependent ratio of incoherent to coherent cross sections. The model predictions agree with ALICE and PHENIX data without new free parameters, and the authors provide predictions for LHC Run 2 energies. The results support the existence of subnucleonic degrees of freedom in nuclei and their energy evolution, and future measurements at RHIC and LHC with smaller uncertainties can test this prediction.
