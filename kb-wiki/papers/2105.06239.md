# Hybrid stars with large strange quark cores constrained by GW170817

> We analyze the impact of several multi-quark interaction channels on the properties of hybrid stars. Four and eight vector interactions were included in the $SU(3)$ NJL model to describe the quark matter, and all vector like interactions were investigated. The hybrid stars were built starting from an hadronic EoS that satisfy presently accepted nuclear matter properties and considering the quark model constrained by the vacuum properties of several light mesons. The interplay between the 8-quark vector interaction and the 4-quark isovector-vector interaction determine the size of the quark core and its strangeness content. The inclusion of a isovector-vector interaction is essential to obtain large $s$-quark contents and a smaller central speed of sound, although this interaction pushes the onset of quarks to larger densities. It is observed that low mass stars, with a mass below 1.4$M_\odot$, may contain a quark core but with small strangeness content. It is shown that the presently existing mass and tidal deformability constraints from NS observations allow for the existence of hybrid stars with a large strangeness content and large quark cores for binary mass ratios $q=M_2/M_1\gtrsim 0.85$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that hybrid neutron stars with large strange quark cores are compatible with the GW170817 tidal deformability constraints, but only for binary mass ratios \(q = M_2/M_1 \gtrsim 0.85\); the inclusion of a 4-quark isovector-vector interaction is essential to obtain large strangeness content and a smaller central speed of sound, although this interaction pushes the onset of quarks to higher densities.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by combining the DDME2 hadronic relativistic mean-field model with an SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter that includes four-quark and eight-quark vector interaction channels, plus isovector-vector interactions. A Maxwell construction was used for the first-order phase transition between hadronic and quark phases, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star masses reach up to approximately \(2.1 M_\odot\) for certain parameter combinations, satisfying the \(2 M_\odot\) pulsar constraint.
- Quark core masses up to \(1.53 M_\odot\) are obtained for large values of the eight-quark vector coupling \(\chi_{\omega\omega}\) with \(\chi_\omega = \chi_\rho = 0\).
- The onset of quark matter in low-mass stars occurs below \(1.4 M_\odot\), but with small strangeness content.
- The squared speed of sound at the center of the maximum-mass star reaches values close to \(c^2\) (i.e., \(v_s^2 \approx 1\)) for the largest \(\chi_{\omega\omega}\) values when \(\chi_\rho = 0\).
- Nuclear saturation density is taken as \(n_0 = 0.155\) fm\(^{-3}\); central densities of maximum-mass stars reach up to several times \(n_0\) depending on couplings.
- The NJL model parameters reproduce vacuum meson masses: \(m_{\pi^\pm} = 139.6\) MeV, \(m_{K^\pm} = 493.7\) MeV, \(m_{\eta'} = 957.8\) MeV, and decay constants \(f_{\pi^\pm} = 92.0\) MeV, \(f_{K^\pm} = 96.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ferreira, Pereira, and Providência (2020) that analyzed hybrid stars with four-quark and eight-quark vector-isoscalar interactions, now incorporating all vector interaction channels including isovector-vector terms. It builds on earlier NJL-based hybrid star studies by Benic et al. (2014) and Pereira et al. (2016), and addresses the tension between stiff equations of state required for \(2 M_\odot\) pulsars and softer equations of state favored by GW170817 tidal deformability constraints. The results enable future work on discriminating hybrid stars with large strange quark cores from purely hadronic stars using multimessenger observations, particularly for asymmetric binary mergers.
