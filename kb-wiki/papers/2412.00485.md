# Measurement of $p$-$Ξ^{-}$ ($\bar{p}$-$\barΞ^{+}$) Correlation Function in Isobar and Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV with the STAR Detector

> Understanding the strong interactions between baryons, especially hyperon-nucleon ($Y$-$N$) interactions, is crucial for comprehending the equation-of-state (EoS) of the nuclear matter and inner structure of neutron star. In these proceedings, we present the measurements of $p$-$Ξ^{-}$ ($\bar{p}$-$\barΞ^{+}$) correlation functions with high statistics in Isobar (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr) and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV by the STAR experiment. With the Lednický-Lyuboshitz approach, the source size and strong interaction parameters of $p$-$Ξ^{-}$ ($\bar{p}$-$\barΞ^{+}$) pairs are extracted.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first extraction of strong interaction parameters (scattering length and effective range) for \(p\)-\(\Xi^{-}\) (and \(\bar{p}\)-\(\bar{\Xi}^{+}\)) pairs from experimental data, finding that the interaction is shallowly attractive. The results are consistent across three different collision systems (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, Au+Au) at the same energy, but the extracted parameters carry statistical and systematic uncertainties that place the scattering length in a range rather than a single precise value.

## Method
The STAR experiment measured two-particle correlation functions for \(p\)-\(\Xi^{-}\) pairs in Isobar (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr) and Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\) = 200 GeV, using three centrality bins (0-10%, 10-40%, 40-80%). The correlation functions were fitted simultaneously with the Lednický-Lyuboshitz (LL) model, which assumes a static spherical Gaussian source and an S-wave scattering amplitude with Coulomb interaction, using a Bayesian method to extract the source size (\(R_G\)), spin-averaged scattering length (\(f_0\)), and effective range (\(d_0\)). Residual correlations from \(p\)-\(\Xi(1530)\) were removed, and contributions from other pairs were neglected.

## Key numbers
- The spin-averaged scattering length \(f_0\) for \(p\)-\(\Xi^{-}\) pairs is determined in the range [0.57, 0.90] fm across all collision systems, indicating a shallow attractive interaction.
- The extracted source size \(R_G\) for \(p\)-\(\Xi^{-}\) pairs decreases monotonically from central to peripheral collisions, following a linear trend as a function of charged-particle rapidity density (\(dN_{ch}/d\eta\)).
- The strong interaction parameters (\(f_0\), \(d_0\)) from Isobar and Au+Au collisions are consistent within 1\(\sigma\) statistical uncertainty.
- The UrQMD + HALQCD simulation results are consistent with the experimental data within 1\(\sigma\) for both the correlation functions and the extracted parameters.

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic measurements of hyperon-nucleon interactions to the strangeness \(S = -2\) sector, specifically \(p\)-\(\Xi^{-}\) pairs, which are relevant for understanding the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars and as a potential decay channel for the H-dibaryon. The results are compared with theoretical predictions from the HALQCD Collaboration (Lattice QCD) and with transport model calculations from UrQMD coupled with Lattice QCD potentials. The extracted parameters provide the first experimental constraints on \(p\)-\(\Xi^{-}\) strong interactions, enabling future comparisons with theoretical models of baryon-baryon interactions and the equation-of-state of dense nuclear matter.
