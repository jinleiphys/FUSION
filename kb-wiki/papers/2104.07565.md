# Two Particles with Zero-Range Interaction in a Magnetic Field

> Energy levels are investigated for two charged particles possessing an attractive, momentum-independent, zero-range interaction in a uniform magnetic field. A transcendental equation governs the spectrum, which is characterized by a collective Landau-level quantum number incorporating both center-of-mass and relative degrees of freedom. Results are obtained for a system of one charged and one neutral particle, with the interaction chosen to produce a bound state in vanishing magnetic field. Beyond deriving the weak-field expansion of the energy levels, we focus on non-perturbative aspects. In the strong-field limit, or equivalently for a system in the unitary limit, a single bound level with universal binding energy exists. By contrast, excited states are resonances that disappear into the continuum as the magnetic field is raised beyond critical values. A hyperbola is derived that approximates the number of bound levels as a function of the field strength remarkably well.

## Key claim

The paper derives the energy levels for two charged particles with an attractive zero-range interaction in a uniform magnetic field, but the detailed numerical results are obtained only for the special case of one charged and one neutral particle, with the interaction strength chosen to produce a bound state in zero field. The abstract's claim of "a single bound level with universal binding energy" in the strong-field limit applies only to the ground state (n=0), while excited states become resonances that disappear at critical field strengths.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for two particles interacting via a contact potential in a uniform magnetic field, using a regulator-dependent running coupling that is renormalized by matching to the zero-field binding energy. For the case of one charged and one neutral particle, they compute the restricted non-interacting Green's function projected onto states of good pseudo-momentum, which becomes diagonal in the basis of Hermite functions characterized by a collective Landau-level quantum number n. The spectrum is determined by a transcendental equation that can be inverted to give the magnetic field as a function of binding energy through a one-dimensional integral.

## Key numbers

* For equal masses (m1 = m2) in the unitary limit (E0 → 0), the dimensionless ground-state binding energy is χ(0)∞ = 0.081588, where χ is the binding energy in units of QB/(2μ).
* For a light charged particle (x → 0) in the unitary limit, the binding energy attains its largest value χ(0)∞ = 0.605444.
* For the first excited level (n=1) with equal masses, the critical magnetic field at which the level reaches threshold is bc = 3.87554, where b = QB/(-2μE0).
* The approximate formula for the critical field of high-lying levels (equal masses) is bc = 4/(2n-1) [1 + O(n^{-2})], which works at 3% for n=1 and 0.6% for n=2.
* The number of bound levels as a function of magnetic field is approximated by N = 2/b + 3/2.
* The first-order correction to the unitary-limit binding energy for equal masses is χ(1)∞ = 0.388235, and the second-order correction is χ(2)∞ = 0.567853.

## Context

This work extends the general results of Avron, Herbst, and Simon (1978) on charged particles with translationally invariant interactions in magnetic fields to the specific case of a zero-range interaction, motivated by pionless effective field theory for few-nucleon systems as developed by Beane (2000), Bedaque (2002), Epelbaum (2008), and Hammer (2019). The results enable future treatment of the two-nucleon system in magnetic fields, which is relevant to lattice QCD calculations of light nuclei in external fields (Detmold 2015, Beane 2015, Chang 2015) that suggest an analogue of Feshbach resonances in strongly interacting systems.
