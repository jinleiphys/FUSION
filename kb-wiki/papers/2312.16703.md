# QCD material parameters at zero and non-zero chemical potential from the lattice

> Using an eighth-order Taylor expansion in baryon chemical potential, we recently obtained the (2+1)-flavor QCD equation of state (EoS) at non-zero conserved charge chemical potentials from the lattice. We focused on strangeness-neutral, isospin-symmetric QCD matter, which closely resembles the situation encountered in heavy-ion collision experiments. Using this EoS, we present here results on various QCD material parameters; in particular we compute the specific heat, speed of sound, and compressibility along appropriate lines of constant physics. We show that in the entire range relevant for the beam energy scan at RHIC, the specific heat, speed of sound, and compressibility show no indication for an approach to critical behavior that one would expect close to a possibly existing critical endpoint.

# QCD material parameters at zero and non-zero chemical potential from the lattice

## Key claim

Using an eighth-order Taylor expansion in baryon chemical potential, the authors compute the specific heat, speed of sound, and compressibility for (2+1)-flavor QCD matter along lines of constant physics relevant to heavy-ion collisions, and find that in the entire range relevant for the RHIC beam energy scan, these material parameters show no indication of critical behavior that would be expected near a possible critical endpoint.

## Method

The authors use high-statistics lattice QCD data sets with a HISQ action for (2+1)-flavor QCD, with degenerate light quarks and a strange quark mass tuned to m_s/m_l = 27 (or 20 for temperatures above 180 MeV). They impose strangeness-neutral (n_S = 0) and isospin-symmetric (n_Q/n_B = 0.5) conditions, then compute material parameters analytically from cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations rather than through numerical interpolation, which reduces statistical uncertainties. The results are compared to hadron resonance gas (HRG) model calculations using the QMHRG2020 resonance list.

## Key numbers

- The pseudocritical temperature T_pc = 156.5(1.5) MeV is taken from HotQCD (2018) as the reference scale.
- Nuclear matter density n_0 = 0.16/fm^3 is used as the reference density.
- Lattice calculations use aspect ratio N_sigma/N_tau = 4 for all data sets.
- The isentropic speed of sound c_s^2 shows no indication of going to zero within the surveyed range of s/n_B down to s/n_B = 10.
- At mu_B = 0, the isovolumetric heat capacity C_V agrees with previous HotQCD results from 2014.
- The isentropic speed of sound overlaps with the estimate from Gardim (2019) extracted from hydrodynamic simulations using experimental data.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of material parameters at mu_B = 0 by Gavai (2004), Borsanyi (2013), and HotQCD (2014) to non-zero baryon chemical potential. It improves upon the authors' own earlier computation of the speed of sound in Bollweg (2022) by using analytic formulas in terms of cumulants rather than numerical interpolation, yielding more controlled uncertainties. The results enable future searches for the hypothesized critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram and can be compared to experimental data from the RHIC beam energy scan. The authors note that calculations of the thermal expansion coefficient and isobaric heat capacity are in progress, and continuum extrapolations for 6th- and 8th-order cumulants remain a long-term goal.
