# Constraining the equation of state of hybrid stars using recent information from multidisciplinary physics

> At the ultra-high densities existing in the core of neutron stars, it is expected that a phase transition from baryonic to deconfined quark matter may occur. Such a phase transition would affect the underlying equation of state (EoS) as well as the observable astrophysical properties of neutron stars. Comparison of EoS model predictions with astronomical data from multi-messenger signals then provides us an opportunity to probe the behaviour of dense matter. In this work, we restrict the allowed parameter space of EoS models in neutron stars for both nucleonic (relativistic mean field model) and quark matter (bag model) sectors by imposing state-of-the-art constraints from nuclear calculations, multi-messenger astrophysical data and perturbative QCD (pQCD). We systematically investigate the effect of each constraint on the parameter space of uncertainties using a cut-off filter scheme, as well as the correlations among the parameters and with neutron star astrophysical observables. Using the constraints, we obtain limits for maximum NS mass, maximum central density, as well as for NS radii and tidal deformability. Although pQCD constraints are only effective at very high densities, they significantly reduce the parameter space of the quark model. We also conclude that astrophysical data supports high values of the bag parameter B and disfavors the existence of a pure quark matter core in hybrid stars.

## Key claim

The authors find that astrophysical data supports high values of the bag parameter B and disfavors the existence of a pure quark matter core in hybrid stars, though pQCD constraints are only effective at very high densities not reached in neutron star interiors and therefore only weakly affect the observable neutron star properties.

## Method

The authors generate hybrid star equations of state by combining a Relativistic Mean Field model for nucleonic matter with the MIT Bag model for quark matter, constructing the mixed phase via Gibbs construction. They apply a cut-off filter scheme to constrain the eight-dimensional parameter space (six nuclear saturation parameters plus two quark model parameters) using chiral effective field theory calculations at low densities, multi-messenger astrophysical data (maximum mass of 2.01 solar masses from PSR J0740+6620 and tidal deformability limit from GW170817), and perturbative QCD constraints at very high densities. Correlations among model parameters and neutron star observables are evaluated using Pearson's correlation coefficient.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass range after all constraints: 2.01 to 2.67 solar masses
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star after all constraints: 11.4 to 13.5 km
- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star after all constraints: 247 to 720
- Central density range for maximum mass configurations after all constraints: 0.69 to 1.23 fm^-3
- Central baryon chemical potential range for maximum mass configurations after all constraints: 1.33 to 1.97 GeV
- About 23 percent of the 100,000 initial parameter sets satisfy CEFT and physical constraints
- 53 percent of CEFT-compatible EoSs pass astrophysical constraints; 52 percent pass pQCD constraints; 28 percent pass both

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian-like cut-off filter scheme previously applied to pure nucleonic matter by Ghosh and Chatterjee (2022) to include quark degrees of freedom via the MIT Bag model. It builds on model-independent studies using piecewise polytropes or Chebyshev polynomials by Kurkela et al. (2014) and Annala et al. (2020) that combined pQCD with astrophysical observations, but here the parameters are directly linked to microscopic quark matter properties. The results enable future investigations of correlations between nuclear and quark model parameters with neutron star observables, and the finding that astrophysical data disfavors pure quark matter cores provides a testable prediction for upcoming NICER and gravitational-wave observations.
