# Off-shell pairing correlations from meson-exchange theory of nuclear forces

> We develop a model of off-mass-shell pairing correlations in nuclear systems, which is based on the meson-exchange picture of nuclear interactions. The temporal retardations in the model are generated by the Fock-exchange diagrams. The kernel of the complex gap equation for baryons is related to the in-medium spectral function of mesons, which is evaluated nonperturbatively in the random phase approximation. The model is applied to the low-density neutron matter in neutron star crusts by separating the interaction into a long-range one-pion-exchange component and a short-range component parametrized in terms of Landau Fermi liquid parameters. The resulting Eliashberg-type coupled nonlinear integral equations are solved by an iterative procedure.We find that the self-energies extend to off-shell energies of the order of several tens of MeV. At low energies the damping of the neutron pair correlations due to the coupling to the pionic modes is small, but becomes increasingly important as the energy is increased. We discuss an improved quasiclassical approximation under which the numerical solutions are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper develops a model of off-mass-shell pairing correlations in neutron matter based on meson-exchange theory, finding that while the damping of neutron pair correlations due to coupling to pionic modes is small at low energies, it becomes increasingly important as energy increases; the self-energies extend to off-shell energies of several tens of MeV, but the model relies on an improved quasiclassical approximation and neglects vertex corrections beyond short-range correlation dressing.

## Method

The authors construct a model where the retarded pairing interaction is mediated by dynamical pions, with the kernel of the complex gap equation related to the in-medium spectral function of mesons evaluated nonperturbatively in the random phase approximation (RPA). The interaction is separated into a long-range one-pion-exchange component and a short-range component parametrized by Landau Fermi liquid parameters, and the resulting Eliashberg-type coupled nonlinear integral equations for the complex gap function and wave-function renormalization are solved iteratively using an improved quasiclassical approximation.

## Key numbers

- The density regime considered is (0.002 - 0.5) ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is the nuclear saturation density
- The pion Compton wavelength is m_π⁻¹ = 1.4 fm
- The self-energies extend to off-shell energies of the order of several tens of MeV
- The momentum transfer integration for the effective pairing kernel K(ω) runs over 0 ≤ q ≤ 2p_F for particles constrained to their Fermi surface

## Context

This work extends the Eliashberg theory of boson-exchange superconductors (Eliashberg, 1960) to nuclear systems, where the characteristic energies of off-shell excitations are not small compared to the Fermi energy, unlike in electron-phonon systems where the Migdal theorem applies. The model differs from previous treatments of retarded pairing in nuclear systems (Sedrakian, 2002; Terasaki, 2002; Kamerdzhiev, 2002) by focusing on pionic modes rather than density fluctuations or phonons of the nuclear Coulomb lattice, and it contrasts with potential-model approaches by treating mesons dynamically rather than in the static limit. The approach enables future calculations of off-shell pairing gaps in neutron star crusts that include retardation effects missing in standard BCS treatments.
