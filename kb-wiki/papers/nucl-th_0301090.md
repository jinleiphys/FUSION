# Electromagnetic Meissner effect in spin-one color superconductors

> It is shown that color-superconducting quark matter, where quarks of the same flavor form Cooper pairs with spin one, exhibits an electromagnetic Meissner effect. This is in contrast to spin-zero color superconductors where Cooper pairs consist of quarks with different flavors.

## Key claim

Spin-one color superconductors (where quarks of the same flavor form Cooper pairs) can exhibit an electromagnetic Meissner effect, but whether they do depends on the specific phase of the condensate: the polar phase shows no Meissner effect for a single flavor, while the color-spin locked (CSL) phase always shows a Meissner effect.

## Method

The authors computed the Meissner mass matrix (the zero-energy, zero-momentum limit of the spatial components of the polarization tensor) for gluons and photons in one-loop approximation at zero temperature, for two specific phases of a spin-one color superconductor: the polar phase and the CSL phase. They also analyzed the pattern of symmetry breaking of the local gauge symmetries SU(3)_c × U(1)_em by identifying which residual gauge groups leave the gap matrix invariant.

## Key numbers

* For the polar phase, gluons 1, 2, and 3 have zero Meissner mass (in units of g^2 μ^2/(6π^2)), indicating an unbroken SU(2)_c subgroup.
* For the polar phase, the new photon (a superposition of the original photon and the eighth gluon) has zero Meissner mass, confirming no electromagnetic Meissner effect for a single flavor.
* For the CSL phase, all eight gluons and the photon have nonzero Meissner masses; the photon mass squared is 6 q^2 in units of e^2 μ^2/(6π^2), where q is the quark electric charge.
* The gap in spin-one color superconductors is estimated to be 20 - 400 keV, with a critical temperature of order 10 - 400 keV.
* The critical magnetic field for the transition to the normal conducting state is estimated to be of order 10^16 Gauss, much larger than typical neutron star magnetic fields of order 10^12 Gauss.
* The ratio of penetration depth to coherence length is of order φ/(e μ) ~ 10^{-3} [100 MeV/(e μ)], indicating type-I superconductivity.

## Context

This work extends the study of electromagnetic properties of color superconductors beyond the spin-zero 2SC and CFL phases (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1998; Alford, Schäfer 2002), where no electromagnetic Meissner effect was found. The results show that spin-one color superconductors can exhibit a Meissner effect, making them electromagnetically distinct from spin-zero phases. This enables the possibility that neutron star cores composed of spin-one color-superconducting quark matter would be type-I superconductors, which could explain observations of pulsar precession periods (Link 2003) that are incompatible with type-II superconducting matter.
