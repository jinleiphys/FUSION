# Pygmy dipole resonance in exotic nuclei

> The evolution of the PDR strength with the neutron excess is investigated in Sn isotopic and N=82 isotonic chains with regard to its possible connection with the neutron skin thickness. For this purpose a recently proposed method incorporating both HFB and multi-phonon QPM theory is applied. Analysis of the corresponding neutron and proton dipole transition densities is presented.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the total strength of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones is closely correlated with the size of the neutron (or proton) skin, but this correlation is established only for the specific theoretical framework combining HFB and multi-phonon QPM theory, and the results are limited to the calculated chains.

## Method
The authors combined a fully microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) description for nuclear ground states with the multi-phonon Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) for excited states. They performed QRPA calculations on Sn isotopes from A=112 to A=132 and on N=82 isotones to obtain low-lying one-phonon dipole states. They then analyzed the neutron and proton dipole transition densities for different excitation energy regions to characterize the PDR and its relation to the neutron skin thickness.

## Key numbers
- In Sn isotopes (A=112-132) and N=82 nuclei, low-lying one-phonon dipole states are found at excitation energies E* = 6-7.5 MeV, with almost pure neutron structure.
- At E* ≤ 8 MeV in Sn isotopes and N=82 nuclei, the dipole transition densities show in-phase proton-neutron oscillation in the interior but only neutron contribution at the surface.
- In the most exotic nucleus 100Sn, a state with proton structure is found at E* = 8.3 MeV, indicating a possible proton PDR.
- The total PDR strength increases as the relative difference between neutron and proton rms radii (δr) increases, across the mass range 100-132Sn and N=82 nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the earlier studies of the PDR in neutron-rich nuclei (PLB2004, Rez, Paar, Volz) by applying a combined HFB and multi-phonon QPM approach to systematically investigate the correlation between PDR strength and neutron skin thickness. The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted correlation between PDR strength and skin thickness in Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones, and suggest the possibility of a proton PDR in light Sn isotopes near 100Sn. The calculations are compared with available data from Volz, Gov, and Ozel.
