# Pseudogauge dependence of the spin polarization and of the axial vortical effect

> The mean spin polarization vector of spin 1/2 particles in a relativistic fluid at local thermal equilibrium (LTE) in different pseudogauges (PGs), i.e., with different choices for the decomposition of orbital and spin angular momentum, is obtained. The spin polarization obtained in the canonical PG differs from the one obtained in the Belinfante PG. It is found that this difference can not be written by replacing the thermal vorticity with the spin potential in the usual polarization formula. Other PG choices affect the contribution of thermal shear to the spin polarization. Therefore, the choice of a PG affects the predictions for the polarization measured in heavy-ion collisions. In general, it is shown that the Wigner function of a noninteracting Dirac field at LTE is affected by the PG transformations. Explicit expressions of the mean axial current are also calculated and it is found that even the axial vortical effect conductivity depends on the PG.

## Key claim

The spin polarization of spin-1/2 particles in a relativistic fluid at local thermal equilibrium depends on the chosen pseudogauge (decomposition of orbital and spin angular momentum), and this difference cannot be removed by simply replacing thermal vorticity with the spin potential in the standard polarization formula; the axial vortical effect conductivity also depends on the pseudogauge in systems out of global equilibrium.

## Method

The author derives the local thermal equilibrium statistical operator for a free Dirac field using Zubarev's method, starting from the Belinfante pseudogauge and then applying pseudogauge transformations to obtain operators for the canonical, de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW), Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen (HW), and Callan-Coleman-Jackiew (CCJ) pseudogauges. Using linear response theory, the Wigner function is evaluated at first order in thermodynamic gradients for each pseudogauge, from which the mean spin polarization vector and mean axial current are computed.

## Key numbers

* The spin polarization in the canonical pseudogauge differs from the Belinfante result by a term proportional to (varpi_{rho sigma} - Omega_{rho sigma}), where varpi is thermal vorticity and Omega is the spin potential (Eq. 4 in the text).
* The GLW and HW pseudogauges yield a spin polarization proportional to the spin potential Omega_{rho sigma} alone, with no explicit dependence on thermal vorticity or thermal shear.
* The axial vortical effect conductivity for a massless free Dirac field at global equilibrium is W^A = T^3/6 + (mu^2)/(2 pi^2) T, where T is temperature and mu is chemical potential.
* At global equilibrium, the spin polarization depends only on thermal vorticity and is pseudogauge-independent, with the same form as the Belinfante result but with constant varpi_{rho sigma}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pseudogauge dependence in relativistic spin hydrodynamics previously discussed by Becattini (2018) and Speranza (2020), who showed that pseudogauge transformations affect the local thermal equilibrium statistical operator but not the global equilibrium one. The paper provides explicit formulas for spin polarization in different pseudogauges, which were previously unreported, and shows that the spin-thermal shear coupling found by Becattini (2021) and Liu (2021) is affected by the pseudogauge choice. This enables quantitative assessment of how theoretical predictions for lambda hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions depend on the angular momentum decomposition used, which is relevant for resolving the discrepancy between hydrodynamic predictions and experimental data from the STAR collaboration (2019).
