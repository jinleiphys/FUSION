# Unversal Features of the Order-Parameter Fluctuations

> We discuss the universal scaling laws of order parameter fluctuations in any system in which the second-order critical behavior can be identified. These scaling laws can be derived rigorously for equilibrium systems when combined with the finite-size scaling analysis. The relation between order parameter, criticality and scaling law of fluctuations has been established and the connexion between the scaling function and the critical exponents has been found.

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal scaling laws for order-parameter fluctuations, derived rigorously for equilibrium second-order phase transitions using finite-size scaling, also hold for off-equilibrium systems (such as the fragmentation-inactivation binary process) provided the system exhibits self-similarity at criticality.

## Method

The authors derive scaling laws for the probability distribution of the order parameter starting from Widom's scaling hypothesis for the free energy density and Fisher-Barber finite-size scaling, using the hyperscaling and Rushbrooke relations to connect critical exponents. They then test these scaling predictions numerically on two models: the bond percolation model in 3D (an equilibrium system) and the fragmentation-inactivation binary (FIB) model (an off-equilibrium system), plotting the scaled probability distributions for various lattice sizes or initial cluster masses.

## Key numbers

- In 3D bond percolation at the critical bond activation probability \(p_{cr}=0.2482\), the scaling function \(\Phi(z_{(1)})\) for the order parameter \(\eta = S_{max}/N\) shows good superposition for lattice sizes \(N=10^3\) and \(N=14^3\).
- For the percolation model at \(p=0.35 > p_{cr}\) (liquid phase), the second scaling law with \(\delta=1/2\) is observed.
- In percolation at \(p_{cr}\), the probability distribution of \(m = M_1 - S_{max}\) obeys delta-scaling with \(\delta \simeq 0.80\), compared to the analytical prediction \(1/(\tau-1) = 0.84\).
- In the critical FIB model with \(p_F=0.875\) and \(a=0\), the anomalous dimension is \(g=0.75\); for initial sizes \(N=2^{10}\) and \(N=2^{14}\), the distribution of \(N-M_0\) shows delta-scaling with \(\delta \simeq 0.73\) (close to \(g=0.75\)).
- In the critical FIB model with \(p_F=0.7\) and \(a=0\), the anomalous dimension is \(g=0.4\).
- In the shattered phase of the FIB model (\(a=0, b=-1, \tau=4\)), the order parameter distribution follows the second scaling with \(\delta=1/2\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling analysis of Fisher and Barber (1972) and the Widom scaling hypothesis to the probability distribution of order-parameter fluctuations, establishing a direct connection between the scaling function and the critical free energy density. The results for the FIB model build on prior studies of the fragmentation-inactivation binary process by the same authors (Botet and Ploszajczak, 1995, 1996) and on the relation between KNO scaling and phase transitions studied by Antoniou and collaborators. The approach enables determination of combinations of critical exponents from small systems and is proposed for applications in strong interaction physics and condensed matter physics.
