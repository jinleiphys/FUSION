# Bulk viscosity from neutron decays to dark baryons in neutron star matter

> The existence of a dark baryon that mixes with the neutron leads to the possibility of neutron decay into a dark sector. Such dark decays have been studied as possibly relevant for the neutron decay anomaly and for their potential impacts on neutron stars. The most popular formulation is a dark sector consisting of a dark baryon $χ$ and a dark scalar $ϕ$, where a neutron in vacuum decays 1% or less of the time via the channel $n\rightarrow χ+ϕ$. In this work, we consider the effect of this additional neutron decay channel on transport in neutrons star mergers. We find that the neutron dark decay rate in medium is quite slow, and thus the dark baryons modify the dense matter equation of state in a way that decreases the Urca bulk viscosity by, at most, a factor of 2-3. However, if the neutron dark decay was to occur more rapidly, then the bulk viscosity at merger temperatures of tens of MeV would be strongly enhanced, potentially rapidly damping oscillations in merger environments and therefore providing a signature of slowly equilibrating matter in the merger.

## Key claim

The paper finds that if neutron dark decay into a dark baryon and dark scalar occurs at the 1% branching ratio suggested by the neutron decay anomaly, the in-medium decay rate is so slow that the Urca bulk viscosity in neutron star merger matter is decreased by at most a factor of 2-3; however, if the dark decay coupling were larger, the bulk viscosity at merger temperatures of tens of MeV would be strongly enhanced, potentially damping oscillations and providing a signature of slowly equilibrating matter.

## Method

The authors model dense matter using the IUF-II relativistic mean field equation of state for npe matter, adding a dark baryon χ with repulsive self-interaction mediated by a dark vector meson and a massless dark scalar φ that escapes the system. They calculate the neutron dark decay rate in medium using the nucleon width approximation, compute the Urca and dark decay rates as functions of density and temperature, and derive the bulk viscosity for a system with two coupled neutron decay channels (standard beta decay and dark decay).

## Key numbers

- The vacuum neutron dark decay rate required for a 1% branching ratio is Γ_n,dark = 1.138 × 10^{-5} s^{-1} (based on bottle method lifetime τ_bottle = 878.4 ± 0.5 s)
- The coupling g_φ that yields a 1% branching ratio for m_χ = 938 MeV and m_φ = 0 is g_φ ≈ 1.7 × 10^{-13}
- The dark baryon self-repulsion must satisfy G' ≥ 44 fm^2 to allow neutron stars of at least 2.0 M_⊙
- The beam method neutron lifetime is τ_beam = 888.0 ± 2.0 s, while the bottle method gives τ_bottle = 878.4 ± 0.5 s, a tension over 4σ
- The 95% confidence upper bound on the dark branching ratio is 0.27% from Czarnecki, Marciano, and Sirlin (2018), updated to 0.15% by Dubbers et al. (2018)
- The stability of ⁹Be requires m_χ + m_φ > 937.900 MeV

## Context

This work extends studies of dark baryon effects on neutron stars by Baym et al. (2018), McKeen et al. (2018), and Motta et al. (2018), and builds on the bulk viscosity calculations for npe matter in merger conditions by Alford et al. (2017, 2019, 2023). It corrects a flawed bulk viscosity calculation by Shirke et al. (2023) for the multi-channel decay system and provides the first full calculation of both the in-medium neutron dark decay rate and the resulting bulk viscosity. The results enable future neutron star merger simulations to include dark sector effects on damping of density oscillations and gravitational wave signals.
