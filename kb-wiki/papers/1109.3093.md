# The Fermion Sign Problem at Finite Density, and Large Nc Orbifold Equivalence

> The study of QCD at finite baryon density is severely hampered by the so-called fermion sign problem. As a result, we have no known first principles approach to study nuclear matter, or neutron stars from QCD. On the surface, the large Nc limit does not seem to simplify matters. In this limit, however, one can exploit dualities that exist between strongly coupled gauge theories. Our focus will be on some rather novel orbifold equivalences that have recently been discovered at finite density. These equivalences relate strongly coupled theories plagued by a sign problem, to strongly coupled theories free of sign problems. As a result, such dualities give deeper insight into the nature of the sign problem and possibly provide a way to simulate QCD at finite density in the large Nc limit.

## Key claim

The orbifold equivalence between $SO(2N_c)$ gauge theory (which has no sign problem at finite baryon density) and $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory (which does have a sign problem) holds only for chemical potentials $\mu_B < \frac{1}{2} m_\pi$, because beyond this threshold spontaneous baryon number violation in the parent theory breaks the discrete symmetry used in the projection.

## Method

The authors establish an orbifold equivalence by projecting $SO(2N_c)$ gauge theory with fundamental matter onto its $\mathbb{Z}_2$-invariant subsector, where the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ transformation combines a specific $SO(2N_c)$ rotation with a $U(1)_B$ phase factor. They then analyze the low-energy effective chiral Lagrangian for $SO(2N_c)$ theory, treating the chemical potential, fermion mass, and four-fermion deformation terms as perturbations about the chiral limit, to determine the phase diagram and the region where the equivalence remains valid.

## Key numbers

* The orbifold equivalence between $SO(2N_c)$ and $SU(N_c)$ gauge theories at finite baryon density is valid for $\mu_B < \frac{1}{2} m_\pi$; beyond this threshold the equivalence breaks down due to spontaneous $U(1)_B$ breaking.
* In $SO(2N_c)$ gauge theory with $N_f$ light quarks, the spontaneously broken $SU(2N_f)$ flavor symmetry produces $N_f(2N_f+1)-1$ Goldstone modes, consisting of $N_f^2-1$ pseudoscalar pions and $N_f(N_f+1)$ baryonic pions.
* Lattice simulations of QCD at finite baryon number have reached baryon numbers $N_B = 3$ and $4$ (citing Beane (2009) and Yamazaki (2009)).

## Context

This work extends the concept of orbifold equivalence, previously applied in string theory by Kachru (1998) and in supersymmetric gauge theories by Bershadsky (1998) and Schmaltz (1998), to the finite-density sign problem in large $N_c$ QCD. The approach is inspired by the use of double-trace deformations to preserve center symmetry in Eguchi-Kawai volume reduction, as developed by Unsal (2008) and tested in lattice simulations by Bringoltz (2009). The results enable future lattice studies of deformed $SO(2N_c)$ gauge theory to indirectly probe properties of large $N_c$ QCD at finite baryon density, though practical challenges remain in implementing the four-fermion deformations without reintroducing a sign problem.
