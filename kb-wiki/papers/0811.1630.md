# Variational Approach to the Chiral Phase Transition in the Linear Sigma Model

> The chiral phase transition at finite temperature is investigated in the linear sigma model, which is regarded as a low energy effective theory of QCD with three momentum cutoff, in the variational method with the Gaussian approximation in the functional Schroedinger picture. It is shown that the Goldstone theorem is retained and the meson pair excitations are automatically included by taking into account the linear response to the external fields. It is pointed out that the behavior of chiral phase transition depends on the three-momentum cutoff, which leads to the careful treatment of the problem.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the chiral phase transition in the O(4) linear sigma model can be either second order or first order depending on the value of the three-momentum cutoff parameter, meaning the order of the transition is not uniquely determined within the model but requires careful treatment of the cutoff.

## Method

The authors used a time-dependent variational approach with Gaussian wave functionals in the functional Schroedinger picture, extended to finite temperature via the Matsubara imaginary-time formalism. They incorporated linear response to external fields to derive polarization tensors for meson pair excitations, which allowed them to compute the pion and sigma meson masses as poles of the full propagator. The model was treated as a low-energy effective theory of QCD with a three-momentum cutoff parameter Lambda to regulate ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass at zero temperature: 140 MeV, used as a realistic parameterization.
* Sigma meson mass at zero temperature: 500 MeV, used as a realistic parameterization.
* Chiral condensate at zero temperature: 93 MeV, used as a realistic parameterization.
* Cutoff parameter Lambda = 650 MeV yields a second-order or crossover chiral phase transition with monotonic behavior of the order parameter.
* Cutoff parameters Lambda = 700, 900, and 1100 MeV produce increasingly steep transitions; beyond a certain value the order parameter becomes multi-valued, indicating first-order phase transition.
* With Lambda = 650 MeV and an unrealistically low zero-temperature pion mass of 70 MeV, the order parameter becomes multi-valued and the transition becomes first order.

## Context

This work extends the variational approach with linear response developed by Tsue, Vautherin, and Matsui (2000) from zero temperature to finite temperature in the O(4) linear sigma model. It contrasts with earlier treatments such as the optimized perturbation theory of Chiku and Hatsuda (1998), which also found second-order transitions with realistic pion mass and first-order transitions in the chiral limit, and with the self-consistent Hartree approximation of Rischke and Muller (1998). The results enable further study of how the cutoff choice affects predictions for chiral symmetry restoration in heavy-ion collision contexts at RHIC and LHC.
