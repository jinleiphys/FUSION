# In-Medium Effects on Charmonium Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions is investigated within a kinetic theory framework incorporating in-medium properties of open- and hidden-charm states in line with recent QCD lattice calculations. A continuously decreasing open-charm threshold across the phase boundary of hadronic and quark-gluon matter is found to have important implications for the equilibrium abundance of charmonium states. The survival of $J/ψ$ resonance states above the transition temperature enables their recreation also in the Quark-Gluon Plasma. Including effects of chemical and thermal off-equilibrium, we compare our model results to available experimental data at CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC energies. In particular, earlier found discrepancies in the $ψ'/ψ$ ratio can be resolved.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that in-medium modifications of open-charm and charmonium states, inferred from lattice QCD, can be incorporated into a kinetic rate equation to provide a continuous treatment of charmonium dissociation and regeneration across the hadron-to-QGP phase transition, resolving earlier discrepancies in the ψ'/ψ ratio at SPS energies.

## Method

The authors model in-medium charm properties using lattice QCD results: a continuously decreasing open-charm threshold across the phase boundary, and survival of J/ψ resonances above the critical temperature. They implement these into kinetic rate equations for J/ψ, ψ', and χ states, using a schematic thermal fireball model for the temperature and volume evolution of heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, including chemical and thermal off-equilibrium effects via a charm-quark fugacity and a thermal relaxation time correction.

## Key numbers

- The typical light-quark mass reduction at the critical temperature is Δm_q(T_c) ≈ 140 MeV.
- The in-medium charm-quark mass above T_c is m_c* ≈ 1.6–1.7 GeV.
- The thermal J/ψ width is ~100 MeV at T ≈ 250 MeV.
- The nuclear absorption cross section used for initial charmonia is σ_abs = 4.4 mb.
- The minimal radius for the correlation volume is r_0 ≈ 1.2 fm, and the average relative speed of c and anti-c quarks is ⟨v_c⟩ ≈ 0.5c.
- The uncertainty band for the charm-hadron mass reduction corresponds to 80 MeV < Δm_q(T_c) < 250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier "two-component" model (Grandchamp and Rapp, 2001) by replacing the ad-hoc statistical production at hadronization with a microscopic rate-equation treatment. It improves upon the kinetic approach of Thews et al. (2001), which only considered J/ψ in the QGP phase using vacuum binding energy, and upon transport calculations by Zhu et al. (2002) and Bratkovskaya et al. (2003) that do not invoke an equilibrium phase transition. The approach enables a continuous treatment of charmonium production across the phase transition and resolves the previously found discrepancy in the ψ'/ψ ratio at SPS.
