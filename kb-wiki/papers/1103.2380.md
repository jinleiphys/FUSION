# Elliptic flow in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions and 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions: insights from viscous hydrodynamics + hadron cascade hybrid model

> Using the newly developed hybrid model VISHNU which connects viscous hydrodynamics with a hadron cascade model, we study the differential and integrated elliptic flow v_2 at different centrality bins for 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions and 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions. We find that the average Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) specific shear viscosity eta/s slightly increases from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) to Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. However, a further study assuming different temperature dependencies for (eta/s)_QGP shows that one cannot uniquely constrain the form of (eta/s)_QGP(T) by fitting the spectra and v_2 alone. Based on our current understanding, the question on whether the QGP fluid is more viscous or more perfect in the temperature regime reached by LHC energies is still open.

## Key claim

The average QGP specific shear viscosity η/s slightly increases from RHIC to LHC energies, but one cannot uniquely constrain the functional form of (η/s)_QGP(T) by fitting spectra and v₂ alone, leaving open whether the QGP fluid is more viscous or more perfect at LHC temperatures.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid model VISHNU, which couples (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) for the QGP phase with the microscopic hadronic transport model UrQMD for the hadronic phase, with a switching temperature T_sw = 165 MeV near the QGP phase transition. They employed reaction-plane averaged MC-KLN initial conditions with Navier-Stokes initial conditions for the shear viscous pressure tensor, and used the equation of state s95p-PCE based on recent lattice results. The model was tuned to reproduce charged hadron multiplicities and pT spectra at both RHIC and LHC energies, then used to compute differential and integrated elliptic flow v₂ for various centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity density at LHC is about a factor of 2.2 higher than at RHIC (dN/dη ≈ 690 for central 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions; dN/dη ≈ 1600 for central 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions)
- Initial temperature of the QGP fireball at lower LHC energies is about 30% larger than at top RHIC energies
- With constant η/s = 0.16, VISHNU fits STAR v₂(pT){4} data from 0 to 2 GeV/c across multiple centrality bins
- To fit ALICE v₂(pT) data at pT > 0.5 GeV/c, η/s must be increased to 0.20–0.24
- Integrated v₂ fits require η/s ≈ 0.16 at RHIC and η/s ≈ 0.20 at LHC (using pT cuts 0.15–2 GeV/c for RHIC and 0.2–5 GeV/c for LHC)
- Two different functional forms of (η/s)_QGP(T) were found that simultaneously fit STAR and ALICE v₂(pT) at 30–40% centrality, with minimum values below 0.16 and maximum values above 0.24

## Context

This work extends earlier purely hydrodynamic analyses of ALICE data by Luzum (2010), Bozek (2011), and Schenke (2011), and an ideal hydro + hadron cascade approach by Hirano (2010), by using the viscous hybrid model VISHNU that correctly describes dissipative effects in both QGP and hadronic stages. The finding that a larger average η/s is needed at LHC than at RHIC is qualitatively consistent with expectations from weakly coupled QGP calculations by Csernai (2006) and lattice simulations by Meyer (2007), both showing η/s increasing with temperature. However, the inability to uniquely constrain (η/s)(T) from spectra and v₂ alone means that future work must incorporate additional observables or theoretical constraints to resolve whether the QGP is more viscous or more perfect at LHC temperatures.
