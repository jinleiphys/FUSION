# Effective Range Corrections in Few-Body Systems with large Scattering Length

> The effective field theory with contact interactions alone is a powerful tool to compute low-energy observables for three-body systems with large scattering length. Recent calculations including effective range corrections are discussed and results are presented.

# Effective Range Corrections in Few-Body Systems with large Scattering Length

## Key claim
The effective field theory with contact interactions alone (CEFT) can describe three-body observables up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) without including an energy-dependent three-body force at that order, contrary to previous power-counting assumptions. This is achieved by using a subtraction formalism that shifts the energy-dependent three-body force down by one order, improving predictive power.

## Method
The author applies a non-relativistic effective field theory with contact interactions only, using a Lagrangian with two-body (C0) and three-body (D0) contact terms. A subtraction formalism is employed that trades the three-body force in the kernel of the Faddeev equation for explicit three-body observables in the inhomogeneity of the integral equation, allowing calculations up to NNLO without an additional three-body counterterm at that order.

## Key numbers
- For the ⁴He trimer using the TTY potential with atom-dimer scattering length a_Ad = 1.205 γ⁻¹: ground state binding energy B₃^(0) = 89.38 B₂ and excited state B₃^(1) = 1.737 B₂ at NNLO, compared to Roudnev's results of B₃^(0) = 96.33 B₂ and B₃^(1) = 1.738 B₂.
- The ⁴He atom-atom scattering length is a₂ ∼ 100 Å, while the van der Waals range R ≈ 10 Å, giving an EFT expansion parameter R/a₂ ≈ 0.1.
- For the triton: binding energy B_t^(LO) = 8.08 MeV, B_t^(NLO) = 8.19 MeV, and B_t^(NNLO) = 8.54 MeV when matched to the neutron-deuteron scattering length a_nd = 0.65 fm.

## Context
This work extends the CEFT framework previously applied to few-body systems with large scattering length by Bedaque (1998), Braaten (2004), and Platter (2005). It directly addresses the power-counting question for three-body forces raised in calculations by Bedaque (2002) and Griesshammer (2004). The results enable improved predictions for three-body observables in both atomic helium systems and the three-nucleon system, with the formalism applicable to any short-range interaction at low energies. The author notes that previous NNLO calculations by Bedaque (2002) required an additional three-body counterterm that is shown to be unnecessary in this subtraction formalism.
