# Magnetic moments and electromagnetic radii of nucleon and $Δ(1232)$ in an extended GBE model

> We derive the exchange currents of pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar mesons from Feynman diagrams, and use them to calculate the magnetic form factors of nucleon and $Δ(1232)$. The magnetic moments and electromagnetic radii are obtained by using those form factors and the parameters determined from the masses of nucleon and $Δ(1232)$. We find the magnetic moments and electromagnetic radii of nucleon and $Δ(1232)$ can be produced very well in the extended Goldstone-boson-exchange model (GBE) in which all of pseudoscalar, vector and scalar meson nonet are included. The magnetic moments of $Δ(1232)$ are closer to experiment values and results from lattice calculation than the results obtained by the model without other mesons except for pion and sigma.

## Key claim

The extended Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) model, which includes all pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson nonets, can reproduce the magnetic moments and electromagnetic radii of the nucleon and Δ(1232) very well, with the magnetic moments of Δ(1232) showing improvement over models that include only pion and sigma mesons, though the N→Δ transition magnetic moment remains underestimated compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors derived exchange currents for pseudoscalar (π, η, η'), vector (ρ, ω, φ), and scalar (σ) mesons from Feynman diagrams, then calculated magnetic form factors for the nucleon and Δ(1232) using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with harmonic-oscillator confinement. Parameters were determined by fitting the masses of the nucleon and Δ(1232), and the magnetic moments and electromagnetic radii were extracted from the form factors at zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment: 2.830 μ_N (experiment: 2.792847337 μ_N)
- Neutron magnetic moment: -1.897 μ_N (experiment: -1.91304272 μ_N)
- Δ⁺⁺ magnetic moment: 5.599 μ_N (experiment: 6.14±0.51 μ_N by LOPEZCATRO and 4.52±0.50±0.45 μ_N by BOSSHARD; lattice QCD: 4.99±0.56 μ_N)
- Δ⁺ magnetic moment: 2.799 μ_N (experiment: 2.7⁺¹.⁰₋₁.₃(stat.)±1.5(syst.)±3(theor) μ_N; lattice QCD: 2.49±0.47 μ_N)
- N→Δ⁺ transition magnetic moment: 2.585 μ_N (experiment: 3.642±0.019±0.085 μ_N)
- Proton charge radius: 0.751 fm (experiment: 0.870 fm)
- Neutron charge radius: 0.340 fm (experiment: 0.341 fm)
- Proton magnetic radius: 0.867 fm (experiment: 0.855 fm)
- Neutron magnetic radius: 0.885 fm (experiment: 0.873 fm)
- Constituent quark mass: 313 MeV (assumed as one third of nucleon mass)
- Harmonic-oscillator parameter b: 0.609 fm
- Gluon-quark coupling constant α_s: 0.8584

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Buchmann et al. (2002), who considered only gluon, pion, scalar meson, and confinement exchanges, by including the full nonet of pseudoscalar and vector mesons in the Goldstone-boson-exchange framework developed by Glozman and Riska (1996). The results show that vector meson contributions are non-negligible at the quark level, reducing the gluon exchange strength and improving agreement with experimental Δ magnetic moments and lattice QCD results from the Lattice collaboration (2003). The parameter-independent relation r²_Δ = r²_p - r²_n found in Buchmann et al. (2002) remains valid after inclusion of additional mesons.
