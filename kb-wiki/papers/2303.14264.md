# SU(2) Gauge Theory in $2+1$ Dimensions on a Plaquette Chain Obeys the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis

> We test the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) for 2+1 dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory. By considering the theory on a chain of plaquettes and truncating basis states for link variables at $j=1/2$, we can map it onto a quantum spin chain with local interactions and numerically exactly diagonalize the Hamiltonian for reasonably large lattice sizes. We find energy level repulsion in momentum sectors with no remaining discrete symmetry. We study two local observables made up of Wilson loops and calculate their matrix elements in the energy eigenbasis, which are shown consistent with the ETH.

## Key claim

The 2+1 dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory on a plaquette chain, with the electric basis truncated at \(j=1/2\), obeys the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) for the majority of eigenstates, as demonstrated by exponential scaling of diagonal matrix element fluctuations with system size and vanishing off-diagonal matrix elements at large energy differences. The claim is qualified by the truncation at \(j=1/2\) and the restriction to a one-dimensional chain of plaquettes with periodic boundary conditions and no topologically nontrivial gauge flux.

## Method

The Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian for 2+1 dimensional SU(2) gauge theory on a chain of plaquettes was mapped onto a quantum spin chain with local interactions by truncating the electric basis at \(j=1/2\) and applying Gauss's law constraints. The resulting Hamiltonian (Eq. 5 in the text) was exactly diagonalized for lattice sizes up to \(N=19\) using translational symmetry to block-diagonalize into momentum sectors. Level spacing statistics were computed in each momentum sector, and matrix elements of two local observables (1-plaquette and 2-plaquette Wilson loop operators) were calculated in the energy eigenbasis to test both the diagonal and off-diagonal parts of the ETH.

## Key numbers

- Level spacing distributions in nonzero momentum sectors for \(N=19\) (each sector containing 27594 states) exhibit Wigner-Dyson statistics with level repulsion, fitted to the Wigner surmise \(P_{\rm ws}(\Delta E) = a(\Delta E)^b \exp[-c (\Delta E)^2]\).
- The zero momentum sector for \(N=19\) shows Poisson-like statistics fitted to \(P_{\rm p}(\Delta E) = a \exp(-b\Delta E)\), attributed to remaining parity symmetry.
- The coupling was set to \(ag^2=1.2\) for numerical studies.
- For the diagonal ETH test, the average magnitude of the difference between eigenstate expectation values and the microcanonical proxy (using 21 nearest eigenstates) decreases exponentially with system size \(N\) for both 1-plaquette and 2-plaquette operators (Fig. 3 in the text).
- For the off-diagonal ETH test with \(N=17\) and energy window \(E=1 \pm 10^{-5}\), the magnitudes of off-diagonal matrix elements \(|\langle m | O_i | n \rangle|\) decrease toward zero as \(\omega = E_n - E_m\) increases, with the lowest and highest eigenenergies being approximately \(-24.76\) and \(16.69\) respectively.

## Context

This work provides the first direct test of the ETH for a non-Abelian gauge theory, extending previous ETH studies on quantum spin chains and nonintegrable systems (Deutsch 1991, Srednicki 1994, Rigol et al. 2008) to the context of relativistic gauge theories. The mapping onto a spin chain is similar to approaches used by Hayata and Hidaka (2021) and Rahman et al. (2022). The results enable future studies of thermalization in non-Abelian gauge theories on larger lattices, including cases with higher \(j\) truncations, SU(3) gauge groups, fermionic matter, and two-dimensional plaquette arrays where the simple mapping fails due to the Mandelstam constraint.
