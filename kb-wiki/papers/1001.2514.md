# Solving the radial Dirac equations: a numerical odyssey

> We discuss, in a pedagogical way, how to solve for relativistic wave functions from the radial Dirac equations. After an brief introduction, in Section II we solve the equations for a linear Lorentz scalar potential, V_s(r), that provides for confinement of a quark. The case of massless u and d quarks is treated first, as these are necessarily quite relativistic. We use an iterative procedure to find the eigenenergies and the upper and lower component wave functions for the ground state and then, later, some excited states. Solutions for the massive quarks (s, c, and b) are also presented. In Section III we solve for the case of a Coulomb potential, which is a time-like component of a Lorentz vector potential, V_v(r). We re-derive, numerically, the (analytically well-known) relativistic hydrogen atom eigenenergies and wave functions, and later extend that to the cases of heavier one-electron atoms and muonic atoms. Finally, Section IV finds solutions for a combination of the V_s and V_v potentials. We treat two cases. The first is one in which V_s is the linear potential used in Sec. II and V_v is Coulombic, as in Sec. III. The other is when both V_s and V_v are linearly confining, and we establish when these potentials give a vanishing spin-orbit interaction (as has been shown to be the case in quark models of the hadronic spectrum).

# Solving the Radial Dirac Equations: A Numerical Odyssey

## Key claim

The paper presents a pedagogical numerical method for solving the radial Dirac equations for bound-state wave functions, demonstrating that a "shoot and match" iterative procedure using a generalized Newton-Raphson method can successfully find eigenenergies and wave functions for linear scalar potentials (quark confinement), Coulomb vector potentials (hydrogen-like atoms), and mixed potentials, with the caveat that the method's convergence depends on defining relative gaps rather than absolute differences when the system becomes more non-relativistic.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled first-order ordinary differential equations for the upper and lower radial components g(r) and f(r) of the Dirac wave function by integrating both outward from a small radius epsilon and inward from a large asymptotic radius x_max, then matching at an intermediate point x_match. A two-parameter generalized Newton-Raphson method iteratively adjusts the energy eigenvalue and the outward boundary condition amplitude a_0 to drive the relative gaps in g and f at the match point to zero, using numerically computed partial derivatives. The integrations are performed using Runge-Kutta methods implemented in Mathematica notebooks.

## Key numbers

- For the linear scalar potential with massless u and d quarks (kappa^2 = 0.9 GeV/fm, r_0 = 0.705 fm): ground state 1s energy E = 0.306 GeV (dimensionless tilde_E = 0.727102)
- For the 2s 1/2 excited state with same potential: E = 0.809 GeV (tilde_E = 1.91897)
- For the 2p 3/2 state: E = 0.940 GeV (tilde_E = 2.23003)
- For the 2p 1/2 state: E = 1.002 GeV (tilde_E = 2.37846), giving a spin-orbit splitting of 62 MeV between the two 2p states
- For massive quarks in the linear scalar potential: strange quark (m_s = 0.3445 GeV) gives E_s = 0.486 GeV; charm quark (m_c = 1.803 GeV) gives E_c = 1.667 GeV; bottom quark (m_b = 5.298 GeV) gives E_b = 5.007 GeV
- The hydrogen atom ground state binding energy is 13.6 eV, with mu = sqrt(1 - tilde_E^2) approximately 0.00730, comparable to alpha = 1/137

## Context

This work extends the numerical approach to solving the Dirac equation for quark confinement models, building on the GMSS potential of Goldman, Maltman, Stephenson, and Schmidt (GMSS) and the non-relativistic Cornell potential of CorPot. The authors note that Paris and Soares de Castro and Franklin (JF) discussed analytic solutions to these equations, while this paper adopts a numerical approach. The method enables calculation of relativistic quark model wave functions for mesons as q-qbar states, and the authors indicate that including a color Coulomb-like vector potential from one-gluon exchange should prevent too-rapid fall-off of quark-quark interaction energies as quark masses increase, which is a matter for a separate paper. The suppression of spin-orbit interaction in the meson spectrum, attributed by Page, Goldman, and Ginocchio (PGG) to cancellation between scalar and vector potentials with the same linear slope, is revisited in the mixed-potential section.
