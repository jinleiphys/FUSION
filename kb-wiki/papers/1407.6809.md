# The influence of vacuum polarization on the Sherman function during elastic electron-nucleus scattering

> The change of the transverse spin asymmetry in the scattering of highly-relativistic spin-polarized electrons from nuclei by vacuum polarization effects is calculated within the Uehling approximation. Results are provided for 1 - 100 MeV electrons scattering from 31P, 64Zn, 152Sm, 197Au and 207,208Pb at backward angles.

# The influence of vacuum polarization on the Sherman function during elastic electron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
Vacuum polarization, calculated within the Uehling approximation, changes the Sherman function (transverse spin asymmetry) for elastic electron-nucleus scattering by approximately 0.3–0.4% at 3.5 MeV beam energy and by less than 1% for energies below 40 MeV, but these estimates carry a numerical accuracy of about 1% below 40 MeV that deteriorates to 10% or more near 100 MeV due to computational challenges at high energies and backward angles.

## Method
The Dirac equation was solved numerically using the RADIAL Fortran package for electrons in the combined nuclear potential plus the Uehling potential, which approximates vacuum polarization to all orders in the nuclear field but only to linear order in Zα. The Sherman function S was computed from a phase-shift analysis of the resulting scattering states for spin-zero nuclei (64Zn, 152Sm, 208Pb) and spin-1/2 nuclei (31P, 207Pb, 197Au) at backward scattering angles, and the relative change dS = S(V_VP)/S(V_T) − 1 was evaluated by comparing results with and without the Uehling potential.

## Key numbers
- For 197Au at 3.5 MeV and θ = 164°: S(V_VP) = −0.45408, S(V_T) = −0.45247, giving dS = 0.36%
- For all nuclei investigated at 3.5 MeV and backward angles: dS = 0.3–0.4%
- For energies below 40 MeV: dS ≲ 1% irrespective of nuclear species
- Numerical accuracy of dS estimates: about 1% below 40 MeV, deteriorating to 10% or more near 100 MeV
- Beam energy range investigated: 1–100 MeV
- Scattering angles investigated: up to 178° and 164° (for 197Au)

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of QED corrections to electron scattering, noting that self-energy effects (not calculated here) tend to be of opposite sign and roughly twice the size of vacuum polarization effects, as reported by Shabaev (2002) for radiative recombination. The study is motivated by planned high-precision measurements at the S-DALINAC in Darmstadt and the MAMI accelerator in Mainz, as well as the future MESA facility, where percent-level or better accuracy in beam polarization measurements using Mott and Compton polarimeters (Taro et al., 2013; Baunack et al., 2013) will require inclusion of QED corrections. The results enable experimentalists to correct Sherman function predictions for vacuum polarization when determining beam polarization, and identify spin-0 nuclei as preferred targets to avoid complications from magnetic scattering at energies above 40 MeV.
