# Single Electron Transverse Momentum and Azimuthal Anisotropy Distributions: Charm Hadron Production at RHIC

> Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is a basic gauge field theory to describe strong interactions. Lattice QCD calculations predict a phase transition from hadronic matter to a deconfined, locally thermalized Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) state at high temperature and small baryon density. Plenty of exciting results from RHIC experiments in the first three years have demonstrated that a hot dense matter with strong collective motion which cannot be described with hadronic degrees of freedom was created at RHIC. Charm quarks are believed to be mostly created from initial gluon fusion in heavy ion collisions. Since they are massive, charm hadrons are proposed to be ideal probes to study the early stage dynamics in heavy ion collisions. We provide here an indirect measurement of charm semi-leptonic decay. Single electron transverse momentum ($p_T$) distributions from 200 GeV \dAu, \pp collisions and 62.4 GeV \AuAu collisions, and single electron azimuthal anisotropy ($v_2$) from 62.4 GeV \AuAu collisions are presented. (Abridged)

# Single Electron Transverse Momentum and Azimuthal Anisotropy Distributions: Charm Hadron Production at RHIC

## Key claim

Charm quarks, produced primarily through initial gluon fusion, can serve as probes of early-stage dynamics in heavy ion collisions, and measuring the transverse momentum and elliptic flow of electrons from their semi-leptonic decays provides an indirect but feasible way to infer charm quark flow and test whether light quarks have reached thermal equilibrium at RHIC.

## Method

The author presents measurements of single electron (non-photonic) transverse momentum distributions from 200 GeV d+Au and p+p collisions and 62.4 GeV Au+Au collisions, along with single electron azimuthal anisotropy (v2) from 62.4 GeV Au+Au collisions. The analysis uses a topological method to reject electrons from photon conversions in detector material, while the remaining dominant background from pi0 Dalitz decays is subtracted statistically. The electron v2 is measured for pT above 2 GeV/c, where electrons from charm decays carry most of the saturated v2 of the parent charm hadrons, allowing discrimination between scenarios where charm quarks flow similarly to light quarks or do not flow at all.

## Key numbers

- The initial Bjorken energy density in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is approximately 5 GeV/fm^3 (with formation time tau ~ 1 fm/c), well above the expected critical energy density epsilon_c ~ 1 GeV/fm^3
- Approximately 73 +/- 6 (+12/-26) GeV of the initial 100 GeV per participant is deposited and available for excitations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- The total charm cross section per nucleon-nucleon collision at RHIC energy is measured as sigma_ccbar^NN = 1.4 mb
- The ratio sigma_J/psi / sigma_ccbar is on the order of 10^-2 and shows a slight decrease from low energies up to RHIC energy
- The open charm spectrum fit parameters in d+Au collisions are: dN/dy = 0.0265 +/- 0.0035, <pT> = 1.32 +/- 0.08 GeV/c, and n = 8.3 +/- 1.2
- A power law fit to total charm cross section data yields power n ~ 1.9 +/- 0.2

## Context

This work extends the first charm production measurements at RHIC by the PHENIX collaboration at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV in Au+Au collisions (PHENIX, 2002), which showed charm production obeys binary collision scaling and is consistent with PYTHIA calculations. The measurements enable tests of pQCD calculations for heavy flavor production at RHIC energies, where NLO pQCD calculations with standard parameters (m_c = 1.2 GeV/c^2, mu_F = mu_R = 2m_c) underpredict the measured cross sections, while calculations with mu_R = m_c reproduce the 200 GeV data but miss other energy points. The results also provide input for statistical coalescence models of J/psi production, where using the measured sigma_ccbar^NN = 1.4 mb as input would predict at least a factor of 3 increase above binary scaling in J/psi yield in central Au+Au collisions, which appears inconsistent with the upper limit from PHENIX measurements.
