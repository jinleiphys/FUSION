# Search for the QCD Critical Point by Higher Moments of Net-proton Multiplicity Distributions at STAR

> We present the measurements of the higher moments of the net-proton multiplicity distributions for Au+Au collisions from the first phase of the beam energy scan program at RHIC. The measurements are carried out at midrapidity ($|y|<0.5$) and within the transverse momentum range $0.4<p_{T}<0.8$ {\gevc} by STAR. The moment products, $\KV$ and $\SD$, which are related to the ratios of various order baryon number susceptibilities and sensitive to the correlation length, are applied to search for the QCD critical point. We report the centrality and energy dependence of the moment products ({\KV} and {\SD}), results are compared with Poisson expectations. The experiment data are also compared with transport model calculation to understand the non-critical physics effects.

## Key claim

The moment products kappa sigma^2 and S sigma of net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions show deviations from Poisson expectations that depend on collision energy and centrality, but the paper hedges that the signature of the QCD critical point could be small due to the limited correlation length and dynamical evolution of the system, requiring comparison with QCD calculations that include dynamical evolution.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured event-by-event net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 to 200 GeV from the first phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. Protons and anti-protons were identified using ionization energy loss (dE/dx) in the Time Projection Chamber at midrapidity (|y|<0.5) and within transverse momentum 0.4<p_T<0.8 GeV/c. The cumulants C1 through C4 were extracted from the net-proton distributions, corrected for finite centrality bin width effects, and used to form the moment products kappa sigma^2 = C4/C2 and S sigma = C3/C2, which are compared to Poisson (Skellam distribution) expectations and to UrQMD transport model calculations.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Acceptance: midrapidity |y|<0.5 and transverse momentum range 0.4<p_T<0.8 GeV/c
- The kappa sigma^2 and S sigma values normalized to Poisson expectations are below unity for sqrt(s_NN) above 11.5 GeV and above unity for 7.7 GeV in Au+Au collisions
- For sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV, the peripheral collision results for normalized S sigma are above unity
- The UrQMD model calculations show a monotonic decrease of the moment products with decreasing beam energy

## Context

This work extends the search for the QCD critical point using higher moments of conserved quantities, following theoretical proposals that moment products like kappa sigma^2 and S sigma are related to ratios of baryon number susceptibilities from Lattice QCD and are sensitive to the correlation length near the critical point. The results are compared to UrQMD transport model calculations to understand non-critical physics effects such as baryon number conservation and hadronic scattering. The data enable future comparisons with QCD-based calculations that include dynamical evolution, and motivate the second phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program with higher statistics at energies below 19.6 GeV and at 15 GeV.
