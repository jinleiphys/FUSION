# Quantifying jet modifications with substructure

> The striking suppression and modification patterns that are observed in jet observables measured in heavy-ion collisions with respect to the proton-proton baseline have the potential to constrain the spatio-temporal branching process of energetic partons in a dense QCD medium. The mechanism of jet energy loss is intricately associated with medium resolution of jet substructure fluctuations. This naturally affects the behavior of the suppression of jets at high-pT, inducing an explicit dependence on jet scales. In this contribution, we review recent work on using the insight from multi-parton quenching to calculate leading-logarithmic corrections to the single-inclusive jet spectrum, and discuss its impact on a wide range of observables, including jet substructure.

## Key claim

The paper argues that higher-order corrections to jet suppression in heavy-ion collisions, which are typically neglected, become logarithmically enhanced due to a mismatch between real and virtual emissions induced by the medium, and that these corrections can be resummed into a novel "collimator" function that multiplies the standard single-parton quenching factor. The key qualifier is that this enhancement occurs only in a specific phase-space region where the decoherence time of the dipole is shorter than the medium length, and the formation time is shorter than the decoherence time.

## Method

The authors perform a QCD calculation at leading-logarithmic accuracy that resums higher-order corrections to the single-inclusive jet spectrum in heavy-ion collisions. They expand the jet suppression factor R_jet in the strong coupling constant, compute the first-order correction involving a quark-gluon splitting, and factorize the quenching of the total color charge from the quenching of the color-singlet dipole. The resummation of all orders is achieved by exponentiating the first-order correction in the strong quenching regime, using the large-N_c approximation and the decoherence parameter to define the relevant phase space.

## Key numbers

* The decoherence time scale is given by t_d ~ (hat_q * theta^2)^{-1/3}, where hat_q is the jet transport coefficient and theta is the dipole angle.
* The critical angle for decoherence is theta_c ~ (hat_q * L^3)^{-1/2}, where L is the medium length.
* The single-quark quenching factor in the soft-scattering approximation is Qc_q(pT) = exp[-2 * abar * L * sqrt(pi * n * hat_q / pT)], where abar = alpha_s * C_R / pi.
* The first-order correction in the strong quenching regime yields a term proportional to -abar * log(R/theta_c) * [log(pT/omega_c) + (2/3)*log(R/theta_c)], where omega_c ~ hat_q * L^2.
* The resummed collimator function in the strong quenching regime is Cc(pT,R) = exp[-abar * log(R/theta_c) * (log(pT/omega_c) + (2/3)*log(R/theta_c))].

## Context

This work extends the standard jet quenching paradigm based on single-parton energy loss, as formulated by Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (1997) and Zakharov (1997), by incorporating higher-order corrections that account for the medium's resolution of jet substructure fluctuations. It builds on earlier Monte-Carlo studies by Milhano and Zapp (2016) and Casalderrey-Solana, Gulhan, Milhano, Pablos, and Rajagopal (2017) that highlighted the importance of fragmentation fluctuations, and provides a first-principles QCD calculation that resums logarithmically enhanced corrections. The results enable a theoretically controlled phenomenological analysis of jet suppression data and can be directly applied to substructure observables such as the quenching of groomed subjets.
