# Semisuper Efimov effect of two-dimensional bosons at a three-body resonance

> Wave-particle duality in quantum mechanics allows for a halo bound state whose spatial extension far exceeds a range of the interaction potential. What is even more striking is that such quantum halos can be arbitrarily large on special occasions. The two examples known so far are the Efimov effect and the super Efimov effect, which predict that spatial extensions of higher excited states grow exponentially and double exponentially, respectively. Here, we establish yet another new class of arbitrarily large quantum halos formed by spinless bosons with short-range interactions in two dimensions. When the two-body interaction is absent but the three-body interaction is resonant, four bosons exhibit an infinite tower of bound states whose spatial extensions scale as $R_n\sim e^{(πn)^2/27}$ for a large $n$. The emergent scaling law is universal and is termed a semisuper Efimov effect, which together with the Efimov and super Efimov effects constitutes a trio of few-body universality classes allowing for arbitrarily large quantum halos.

## Key claim

When two-body interactions are absent but the three-body interaction is resonant in the s-wave channel, four spinless bosons in two dimensions exhibit an infinite tower of bound states whose spatial extensions scale as \(R_n\sim e^{(\pi n)^2/27}\) for large \(n\), a new universal scaling law termed the semisuper Efimov effect that disappears if a two-body interaction is present.

## Method

The author analyzed a model Hamiltonian for spinless bosons in two dimensions with a separable three-body potential and no two-body interaction, solving the three-body scattering T matrix to define the three-body resonance condition. The four-body bound state problem was then formulated as a Skornyakov–Ter-Martirosyan-type integral equation for the residue function, which was solved analytically in the low-energy limit using a leading-logarithm approximation and confirmed numerically for the \(\ell=0\) partial-wave channel. An effective field theory with boson and trimer fields was constructed to demonstrate universality via renormalization group analysis of the two-body, three-body, and four-body couplings.

## Key numbers

- Four-body binding energies follow \(E_n \propto e^{-2(\pi n+\theta)^2/27}\Lambda^2\) for large \(n\), where \(\theta\) is a nonuniversal constant defined modulo \(\pi\).
- The asymptotic spacing between successive bound states satisfies \(\lim_{n\to\infty}(\sqrt{\ln\Lambda/\kappa_n}-\sqrt{\ln\Lambda/\kappa_{n-1}})=\pi/\sqrt{27}\approx0.604600\).
- Numerical results for the 80th bound state give \(\ln\Lambda/\kappa_{80}=2484.10\) with the spacing converging to 0.604881.
- The three-body resonance is achieved by tuning the coupling constant to \(v_0=2\pi^2/\Lambda^2\).

## Context

This work extends the known Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970) for three particles in three dimensions and the super Efimov effect (Nishida, 2013) for three fermions in two dimensions, completing the trio of universal scaling laws for arbitrarily large quantum halos. The semisuper Efimov effect fills the gap between exponential (Efimov) and double-exponential (super Efimov) growth rates, and together with the results for bosons in one dimension (Nishida, 2010) and three dimensions (Efimov, 1970), it completes the classification of universal scaling laws for spinless bosons in all dimensionality. The finding enables experimental searches in ultracold atom systems where vanishing two-body but strong three-body interactions can be realized, and it opens the question of whether even more exotic scaling laws (semihyper or hyper Efimov effects) might exist.
