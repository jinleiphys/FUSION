# Gravitational wave from rotating neutron star

> Using the nuclear equation of states for a large variety of relativistic and non-relativistic force parameters, we calculate the static and rotating masses and radii of neutron stars. From these equation of states, we also evaluate the properties of rotating neutron stars, such as rotational and gravitational frequencies, moment of inertia, quadrupole deformation parameter, rotational ellipcity and gravitational wave strain amplitude. The estimated gravitational wave strain amplitude of the star is found to be $\sim 10^{-23}$.

## Key claim

Using 20 different nuclear equation of state (EOS) models (13 non-relativistic Skyrme and 7 relativistic mean field parameter sets), the authors calculate that the gravitational wave strain amplitude from rotating neutron stars is on the order of approximately 10^{-23}, but this result depends on the specific EOS and the assumed breaking strain of the crust.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static neutron stars and use the rotational neutron star (RNS) code for rotating configurations, with pressure and energy density inputs from 13 Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) and 7 Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) parameter sets. From the maximum mass and radius of each model, they compute the quadrupole moment, ellipticity, moment of inertia, and gravitational wave strain amplitude using standard relations, assuming two possible values for the breaking strain of the crust (10^{-2} and 10^{-3}).

## Key numbers

- Gravitational wave strain amplitude estimated to be approximately 10^{-23} (order-of-magnitude estimate, no specific value per EOS given in the truncated text)
- Maximum mass of pulsar J1614-2230 used as reference: (1.97 ± 0.04) solar masses (Demorest 2010)
- Upper limit of gravitational wave amplitude for pulsar PSR I1603-7202 observed to be approximately 2.6 × 10^{-25} (from prior work cited in the paper)
- Ellipticity of pulsar PSRI2124-3358 found to be less than 10^{-6} (from prior work cited in the paper)
- Kepler frequency formula used: ν_max = 1.22 × 10^3 (M/M_sun)^{1/2} (R/10 km)^{-3/2} Hz
- Quadrupole moment relation: Φ_{22} = 2.4 × 10^{38} g cm^2 (σ_max/10^{-2}) (R/10 km)^{6.26} (1.4 M_sun/M)^{1.2}
- Moment of inertia formula: I_{zz} = 9.2 × 10^{44} g cm^2 (M/1.4 M_sun) (R/10 km)^2 [1 + 0.7 (M/1.4 M_sun)(10 km/R)]

## Context

This work extends prior neutron star mass measurements from Taylor (1989) for PSR 1913+16 (M = 1.144 M_sun) and the more massive pulsar J1614-2230 from Demorest (2010), showing that softer EOS models (such as the SU(2) chiral model and the LNS Skyrme parameter set) fail to reproduce the observed 2.0 M_sun threshold. The study enables comparison of gravitational wave predictions across 20 different EOS models, providing a theoretical basis for interpreting future detections from LIGO, GEO600, and LISA, and identifies which parameter sets (particularly G1 and G2 from Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1996) simultaneously satisfy experimental flow data from Danielewicz (2002) and the high-mass neutron star constraint.
