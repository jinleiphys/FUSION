# Polarized fermions in the unitarity limit

> We consider a polarized Fermi gas in the unitarity limit. Results are calculated analytically up to next-to-leading order in an expansion about d=4 spatial dimensions. We find a first order transition from superfluid to normal phase. The critical chemical potential asymmetry for this phase transition is delta_mu_c= 2/(mu epsilon)*(1-0.467ε), where epsilon=4-d is the expansion parameter and 'mu' is the average chemical potential of the two fermion species. Stability of the superfluid phase in the presence of supercurrents is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper finds a first-order phase transition from superfluid to normal phase in a polarized Fermi gas at unitarity, but the authors note that next-to-leading order corrections are sizable and the presence of a gapless superfluid phase cannot be excluded, especially given that the epsilon expansion may have poor convergence at higher orders.

## Method

The authors use an analytic expansion around d=4 spatial dimensions (epsilon = 4-d) to study a polarized Fermi gas in the unitarity limit, where the scattering length diverges. They construct an effective lagrangian with fermions weakly coupled to bosons, perform a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, and compute the thermodynamic potential up to next-to-leading order in epsilon to determine the critical chemical potential asymmetry for the superfluid-to-normal phase transition. The stability of the superfluid phase in the presence of supercurrents is also analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential asymmetry at next-to-leading order: delta_mu_c = (2 mu / epsilon) * (1 - 0.467 epsilon), where epsilon = 4-d is the expansion parameter and mu is the average chemical potential
- At epsilon = 1 (d=3), the critical chemical potential is delta_mu_c = 1.06 mu
- The superfluid gap at zero asymmetry: chi_0 = (2 mu / epsilon) * [1 + (3*C_hat - 1 + log 2) * epsilon] where C_hat ≈ 0.14424
- The gap at the critical chemical potential: Delta_c = 0.244 mu (with epsilon set to 1)
- The supercurrent instability window in terms of H = -(Delta_0 - delta_mu)/Delta_0: H_min = -0.08 and H_max = 0.63 from numerical results in d=4

## Context

This work extends the epsilon expansion method of Nishida and Son (2006) from unpolarized to polarized Fermi gases at unitarity, and the results agree with the independent work of Nishida and Son (2006) on polarized systems. The authors note that subsequent work by Arnold et al. (2006) found that the next-to-next-to-leading order correction to the ground state energy of an unpolarized Fermi liquid is large, destroying the apparent convergence observed at next-to-leading order, which suggests the epsilon expansion needs improvement through combination with the d=2+epsilon expansion and Pade approximants. The results are relevant to recent experiments with polarized atomic systems near Feshbach resonances by Zwierlein et al. (2005, 2006) and Partridge et al. (2005).
