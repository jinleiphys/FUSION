# Rapidity dependence of global polarization in heavy ion collisions

> We use a geometric model for the hadron polarization with an emphasis on the rapidity dependence. It is based on the model of Brodsky, Gunion, and Kuhn and that of the Bjorken scaling. We make predictions for the rapidity dependence of the hadron polarization in the collision energy range 7.7-200 GeV by taking a few assumed forms of the parameters. The predictions can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the rapidity dependence of global hadron polarization in heavy ion collisions using a geometric model, but the predictions depend on several assumed forms of parameters (such as the rapidity dependence of the coefficient kappa and average transverse momentum) that are not uniquely determined by existing mid-rapidity data.

## Method

The authors extend the Brodsky-Gunion-Kuhn (BGK) model for hadron rapidity distributions to nucleus-nucleus collisions, combining it with Bjorken scaling to compute the average longitudinal momentum and local orbital angular momentum as functions of rapidity and in-plane position. They calculate the quantity d ln f(Y,x)/dYdx (the derivative of the logarithm of the normalized rapidity distribution with respect to both rapidity and in-plane coordinate) using both hard sphere and Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions, then relate this quantity to hadron polarization through a proportionality constant kappa constrained by STAR data at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon model yields d ln f(Y,x)/dYdx at Y=0 of 0.0374 fm^-1 for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and 0.0876 fm^-1 at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV, with impact parameter b=1.2R_A (20-50% centrality)
- The hard sphere model yields d ln f(Y,x)/dYdx at Y=0 of 0.0471 fm^-1 at 200 GeV and 0.121 fm^-1 at 7.7 GeV for the same conditions
- The constant kappa_0 used to fit mid-rapidity polarization data is 8.4 for the Woods-Saxon model and 6.4 for the hard sphere model in the energy range 7.7-62.4 GeV
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear density uses parameters a=0.54 fm and R_A=6.98 fm for Au-197 nuclei, with normalization constant C_0=0.131 fm^-3
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section ranges from 42.0 mb at 200 GeV to 30.6 mb at 7.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends a previous geometric model by Gao et al. (2007) for hadron polarization in heavy ion collisions, which was based on the BGK model of Brodsky, Gunion, and Kuhn (1977) and Bjorken scaling. The predictions are motivated by the STAR collaboration's measurements of Lambda and anti-Lambda global polarization at mid-rapidity (Adam et al., 2018; STAR Collaboration, 2017) and by upcoming STAR forward upgrades that will enable polarization measurements in the rapidity range [2.5,4] for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV starting in 2023. The results can be tested by future experiments to distinguish between different assumptions about the rapidity dependence of the polarization mechanism.
