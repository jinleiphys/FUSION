# Inclusive Ridge Distributions in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The formation of ridges induced by semihard scattering in nuclear collisions is included in the description of single-particle distributions for both pion and proton at low transverse momenta. The ridge component is characterized by an azimuthal dependent factor that is derived in the study of the ridge structure in two-particle correlation distributions involving triggers. It is shown that the inclusive ridge can reproduce the observed data on $v_2(p_T)$ if the base component underlying the ridge has no azimuthal dependence. A common description of pion and proton spectra is given in the recombination model that can smoothly join the low- and intermediate-$p_T$ regions. All the important properties of single-particle distributions in those regions can be satisfactorily described in this approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the azimuthal anisotropy (elliptic flow, \(v_2\)) of pions and protons at low transverse momentum (\(p_T < 2\) GeV/c) can be reproduced without hydrodynamics by attributing all \(\phi\)-dependence to a "ridge" component arising from semihard (minijet) scattering near the nuclear surface, provided the underlying "base" component has no azimuthal dependence.

## Method

The single-particle distribution \(\rho_1^h(p_T,\phi,b)\) is separated into a \(\phi\)-independent base \(B^h(p_T,b)\) and a ridge component \(R^h(p_T,\phi,b)\) proportional to a calculable surface segment \(S(\phi,b)\) derived from the geometry of the initial elliptical overlap region. The thermal parton recombination model is used to write the \(p_T\) spectra of pions and protons as exponentials with a common inverse slope \(T\) for the inclusive distribution and a lower slope \(T_B\) for the base; the ridge then carries the difference. The elliptic flow \(v_2^h(p_T,b)\) is expressed in terms of the average \(\langle \cos 2\phi \rangle_S\) from \(S(\phi,b)\) and a \(p_T\)-dependent factor \(Z(p_T) = e^{p_T/\tilde T} - 1\), with \(\tilde T\) determined by \(T\) and \(T_B\).

## Key numbers

- Inverse slope of inclusive spectra: \(T = 0.283\) GeV, determined from the proton spectrum at 20-30% centrality for \(p_T < 2\) GeV/c.
- Inverse slope of the base component: \(T_B = 0.253 \pm 0.003\) GeV, extracted from fitting \(v_2^h\) vs. transverse kinetic energy \(E_T\) for both pions and protons at 20-30% centrality.
- Effective inverse slope of the ridge component \(\bar R_0(p_T)\) for \(p_T > 1\) GeV/c: \(T' = 0.326\) GeV, giving \(\tilde T = 2.39\) GeV.
- Gaussian width between semihard parton and ridge particle azimuthal angles: \(\sigma = 0.33\).
- Resonance contribution to pion spectrum parameterized with \(T_0 = 0.45\) GeV and \(u_0 = 3.416\) for 20-30% centrality.
- Normalization factors for spectra: \({\cal N}_\pi(N_{part}) = 0.516 N_{part}^{1.05}\), \({\cal N}_p(N_{part}) = 0.149 N_{part}^{1.18}\), \(u_0(N_{part}) = 2.8 + 0.003 N_{part}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous recombination-model studies (Hwa and Yang, 2003; Hwa and Zhu, 2010) to the low-\(p_T\) region below 2 GeV/c, bridging the gap between hydrodynamics (which works at very low \(p_T\)) and the intermediate-\(p_T\) region where thermal-shower recombination dominates. It contrasts with standard viscous hydrodynamics descriptions of elliptic flow (e.g., Kolb and Heinz, 2003) by attributing azimuthal anisotropy to semihard scattering rather than pressure gradients, and it connects the inclusive ridge to triggered ridge phenomena observed by STAR (Abelev et al., 2009) and PHENIX (Adare et al., 2007). The approach enables a unified description of pion and proton spectra and \(v_2\) without invoking hydrodynamics, and it predicts similar \(v_2\) behavior at LHC energies.
