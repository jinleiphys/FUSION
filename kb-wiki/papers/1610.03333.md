# Strange quark asymmetry in the proton in chiral effective theory

> We perform a comprehensive analysis of the strange-antistrange parton distribution function (PDF) asymmetry in the proton in the framework of chiral effective theory, including the full set of lowest order kaon loop diagrams with off-shell and contact interactions, in addition to the usual on-shell contributions previously discussed in the literature. We identify the presence of $δ$-function contributions to the $\bar s$ PDF at $x=0$, with a corresponding valence-like component of the $s$-quark PDF at larger $x$, which allows greater flexibility for the shape of $s-\bar s$. Expanding the moments of the PDFs in terms of the pseudoscalar kaon mass, we compute the leading nonanalytic behavior of the number and momentum integrals of the $s$ and $\bar s$ distributions, consistent with the chiral symmetry of QCD. We discuss the implications of our results for the understanding of the NuTeV anomaly and for the phenomenology of strange quark PDFs in global QCD analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral effective theory, including the full set of lowest-order kaon loop diagrams with off-shell and contact interactions, generates a strange-antistrange PDF asymmetry in the proton that is consistent with phenomenological constraints, with the presence of delta-function contributions to the anti-strange PDF at x=0 providing greater flexibility for the shape of the asymmetry than previously assumed.

## Method

The authors compute strange and antistrange parton distribution functions in the proton using chiral SU(3) effective theory, expanding the chiral Lagrangian to second order in the pseudoscalar fields to obtain kaon rainbow, bubble, tadpole, and Kroll-Ruderman (contact) interaction diagrams. They match twist-two quark operators to hadronic operators in the effective theory, derive convolution formulas for the PDFs in terms of nucleon-to-hadron splitting functions and valence distributions in the hadronic constituents, and regularize the loop integrals using Pauli-Villars regularization to preserve chiral and gauge symmetries.

## Key numbers

- The second moment of the strange-antistrange asymmetry, S^- = integral dx x (s(x) - s̄(x)), is constrained to be in the range consistent with phenomenological analyses, with previous experimental extractions cited as S^- = (-2.7 ± 1.3) × 10^{-3} from NuTeV (leading order) and S^- = (1.96 ± 1.43) × 10^{-3} from a next-to-leading order analysis at Q² = 16 GeV²
- The phenomenological analysis of Bentz et al. (2010) gave S^- = (0 ± 2) × 10^{-3} at Q² = 16 GeV²
- Perturbative three-loop effects from Catani et al. (2004) gave S^- ≈ -0.5 × 10^{-3}
- Previous kaon cloud model calculations gave S^- = 0.143 × 10^{-3} (Cao and Signal, 2003) for KΛ and KΣ states alone, and S^- = -0.135 × 10^{-3} with inclusion of K* mesons
- Alwall and Ingelman (2004) found S^- = 1.65 × 10^{-3} from KΛ fluctuations
- Ding et al. (2005) found S^- ≈ (4-9) × 10^{-3} using an effective chiral quark model
- Wakamatsu (2005) obtained S^- = (2.5-5.5) × 10^{-3} from an SU(3) chiral quark soliton model
- Hobbs et al. (2015) found S^- = (-1 to +5) × 10^{-3} using a scalar tetraquark spectator model

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory approach previously applied to the d̄-ū asymmetry by Salamu et al. (2015) and the preliminary SU(3) analysis by Wang et al. (2016), providing full details of the kaon loop calculation with Pauli-Villars regularization. The results enable more reliable predictions for the strange-antistrange asymmetry that can help resolve the NuTeV anomaly regarding the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio and sin²θW extraction, while also informing global QCD analyses that currently use restrictive parametrizations for the s-s̄ difference.
