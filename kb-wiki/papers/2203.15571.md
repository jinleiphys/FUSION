# Transport coefficients of quasi-particle models within a new relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann equation

> We investigate the transport properties of a kinetic theory model that is tuned to describe the thermodynamic properties of QCD at zero chemical potential using a new formulation of the relaxation time approximation. In contrast to previous approaches, the latter is constructed to preserve the fundamental properties of the collision term of the Boltzmann equation for any energy-dependence of the relaxation time. A novel choice of matching conditions is implemented to ensure that the background mean-field depends only on the temperature even when the system is out of equilibrium. We provide a consistent analysis of how the transport coefficients of relativistic Navier-Stokes theory vary with the energy dependence of the relaxation time. We also show that the entropy production of this theory is consistent with the second law of thermodynamics and verify that it is independent of the matching conditions employed. We used this fact to calculate the matching independent combination of transport coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a new relaxation time approximation (RTA) for the Boltzmann equation, which preserves the fundamental properties of the collision term for any energy-dependence of the relaxation time, can be consistently applied to a quasiparticle model with temperature-dependent masses tuned to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics. The transport coefficients of relativistic Navier-Stokes theory are shown to vary significantly with the energy-dependence parameter gamma, and the entropy production is verified to be independent of the matching conditions employed.

## Method

The authors construct a quasiparticle kinetic theory where the temperature-dependent mass M(T) and background field B(T) are determined by solving the lattice QCD equation of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration (Borsanyi 2010) using Bessel function relations. They implement a new relaxation time approximation for the collision term that includes projection operators to preserve energy-momentum conservation for any energy-dependence of the relaxation time, parametrized as tau_R = t_R (E_p/T)^gamma. The Chapman-Enskog expansion is then performed to first order in gradients to obtain the shear viscosity and bulk viscosity coefficients, with the particular solution for the non-equilibrium distribution function found by expanding in an irreducible orthogonal polynomial basis.

## Key numbers

- The trace anomaly parametrization uses parameters h0 = 0.1396, h1 = -0.1800, h2 = 0.0350, f0 = 2.76, f1 = 6.79, f2 = -5.29, g1 = -0.47, g2 = 1.04, and h3 = 0.01, where t = T/(0.2 GeV)
- The degeneracy factor is set to g = (pi^4/180)[4(N_c^2 - 1) + 7 N_c N_f] with N_c = N_f = 3
- The relaxation time energy-dependence is parametrized as tau_R = t_R (E_p/T)^gamma, with gamma = 1/2 cited for QCD effective kinetic theories (dusling 2010) and gamma = 1 for scalar field theories (calzetta 1988)
- Results are shown for temperatures T = 50 MeV, 150 MeV, and 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the new relaxation time approximation developed by Rocha (2021) to describe systems with vanishing net-charge and temperature-dependent particle masses, generalizing previous quasiparticle kinetic theory approaches by Romatschke (2011) and Alqahtani (2015). The method enables consistent calculation of transport coefficients for QCD matter using an effective kinetic description that reproduces lattice thermodynamics, and the frame-invariant combination of transport coefficients derived from entropy production provides a robust prediction for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
