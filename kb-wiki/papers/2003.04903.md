# The QCD Axion at Finite Density

> We show how the properties of the QCD axion change in systems at finite baryonic density, such as neutron stars. At nuclear saturation densities, where corrections can be reliably computed, we find a mild reduction of the axion mass and up to an order of magnitude enhancement in the model-independent axion coupling to neutrons. At moderately higher densities, if realized, meson (kaon) condensation can trigger axion condensation. We also study the axion potential at asymptotically large densities, where the color-superconducting phase of QCD potentially leads to axion condensation, and the mass of the axion is generically several orders of magnitude smaller than in vacuum due to the suppressed instantons. Several phenomenological consequences of the axion being sourced by neutron stars are discussed, such as its contribution to their total mass, the presence of an axionic brane, or axion-photon conversion in the magnetosphere.

## Key claim

At nuclear saturation densities, where chiral perturbation theory remains reliable, the QCD axion's mass is mildly reduced and its model-independent coupling to neutrons can be enhanced by up to an order of magnitude, because the accidental cancellation present in vacuum is spoiled at finite density.

## Method

The authors use the QCD chiral Lagrangian at finite baryonic chemical potential to compute the axion potential and its couplings to nucleons around nuclear saturation density, where the chiral expansion remains under control. They also analyze the axion potential in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase at asymptotically large densities using a perturbative instanton expansion, and study the possibility of kaon condensation triggering axion condensation at intermediate densities.

## Key numbers

* Axion mass reduction at nuclear saturation density: corrections of order \(n/\Lambda_\chi f_\pi^2 \approx 20\% \, (n/n_0)\), with \(n_0 \approx 0.16 \, {\rm nucleons}/{\rm fm}^3\) (nuclear saturation density) and \(\Lambda_{\chi} \approx 700 \ {\rm MeV}\) (chiral Lagrangian cutoff).
* Enhancement of the model-independent axion coupling to neutrons: up to an order of magnitude (factor of \(O(10)\)) at nuclear saturation densities.
* Axion mass in vacuum: \(m_a \approx m_\pi f_\pi/f_a \approx 5 \, \mu{\rm eV} (10^{12} \ {\rm GeV}/f_a)\).
* In the CFL phase at asymptotically large densities, the axion mass is generically several orders of magnitude smaller than in vacuum due to instanton suppression scaling as \(1/\mu_q^8\).

## Context

This work provides the first comprehensive study of how QCD axion properties change with baryonic chemical potential, extending the vacuum analysis of di Cortona et al. (2015) to finite density systems such as neutron stars. It builds on the discussion of axion condensation by Hook (2017) for non-standard axions and on the analysis of meson condensation in QCD by Son and Stephanov (2000). The results enable future studies of axion phenomenology in neutron stars, including contributions to stellar mass, axionic brane formation, and axion-photon conversion in magnetospheres.
