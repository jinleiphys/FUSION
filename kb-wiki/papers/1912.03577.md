# Systematically Localizable Operators for Quantum Simulations of Quantum Field Theories

> Correlations and measures of entanglement in ground state wavefunctions of relativistic quantum field theories are spatially localized over length scales set by the mass of the lightest particle. We utilize this localization to design digital quantum circuits for preparing the ground states of lattice scalar quantum field theories. Controlled rotations that are exponentially localized in their position-space extent are found to provide exponentially convergent wavefunction fidelity. These angles scale with the correlation between sites and the classical two-point correlation function, as opposed to the more localized mutual information or the hyper-localized negativity. We anticipate that further investigations will uncover quantum circuit designs with controlled rotations dictated by the measures of entanglement. This work is expected to impact quantum simulations of systems of importance to nuclear physics, high-energy physics, and basic energy sciences research.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that controlled rotations in quantum circuits for preparing ground states of lattice scalar field theories can be exponentially localized in position-space extent, with the rotation angles scaling with the classical two-point correlation function rather than the more localized mutual information or hyper-localized negativity, enabling exponentially convergent wavefunction fidelity under systematic truncation.

## Method

The authors construct digital quantum circuits for ground state preparation of free and interacting lattice scalar field theories by decomposing the Gaussian wavefunction into controlled rotations between pairs of lattice sites. They analyze the spatial localization of these rotation angles by relating them to the correlation matrix K and its inverse, and demonstrate that the angles decay exponentially with site separation at a rate set by the scalar mass. The method is validated through numerical calculations on one-dimensional lattices with periodic boundary conditions, examining both nearest-neighbor and smeared finite-difference gradient operators.

## Key numbers

- For a free lattice scalar field theory with dimensionless mass \(\hat m = 0.3\) on \(N = 30\) spatial sites, the two-point correlation function \(\langle \phi_n \phi_0 \rangle\) decays as \(e^{-\hat m n}/\sqrt{\hat m n}\) at large separations in the continuum limit
- The mutual information between two lattice sites falls as \(e^{-2 \hat m j}\) for large separations, exponentially faster than the two-point correlation function which falls as \(e^{-\hat m j}\)
- The negativity between two sites vanishes for all but nearest neighbors when using a nearest-neighbor finite-difference gradient operator (\(s_1\)), and extends only to the range of the gradient operator when using a smeared operator (\(s_3\)) defined over sites separated by up to distance \(\hat r = 3\)

## Context

This work builds upon the foundational quantum simulation frameworks of Jordan, Lee and Preskill (2011-2017) for lattice field theories, and extends recent studies of digitization artifacts by Klco and collaborators (2018-2019) and Somma (2016). The results connect to the broader literature on entanglement in quantum field theories, including Srednicki (1993) on entanglement entropy in scalar fields and Casini (2009) on exponential decay of mutual information. The systematic localization approach enables truncations of quantum circuits with quantifiable fidelity loss, which is expected to impact quantum simulations of systems relevant to nuclear physics, high-energy physics, and basic energy sciences research.
