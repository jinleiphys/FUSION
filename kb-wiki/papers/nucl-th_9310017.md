# Properties of $^{8}$Be and $^{12}$C deduced from the folding--potential model

> The $α$--$α$ differential cross sections are analyzed in the optical model using a double--folded potential. With the knowledge of this potential bound and resonance--state properties of $α$--cluster states in $^{8}$Be and $^{12}$C as well as astrophysical S--factors of $^{4}$He($α$,$γ$)$^{8}$Be and $^{8}$Be($α$,$γ$)$^{12}$C are calculated. $Γ_γ$--widths and B(E2)--values are deduced.

# Properties of $^{8}$Be and $^{12}$C deduced from the folding–potential model

## Key claim

The double-folding potential model, with a single normalization factor fitted to elastic $\alpha$–$\alpha$ scattering data, reproduces the ground-state resonance energy and width of $^{8}$Be and, when separately renormalized for each state, describes the $\alpha$–$^{8}$Be cluster states in $^{12}$C; the authors assume the broad 10.3 MeV level in $^{12}$C is the $2_2^+$ state despite its adopted $0^+$ assignment in the literature.

## Method

The $\alpha$–$\alpha$ differential cross sections were analyzed in the optical model using a double-folded potential derived from nuclear charge distributions folded with an energy- and density-dependent NN interaction, with a single renormalization factor $\lambda = 1.656$ fitted to data from $E_{\rm lab} \approx 7$–35 MeV. The same folding procedure was then applied to the $\alpha$–$^{8}$Be system, with separate normalization factors $\lambda$ fitted to the bound and resonance-state energies of $^{12}$C. Astrophysical $S$-factors for $^{4}$He($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{8}$Be and $^{8}$Be($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{12}$C were calculated in the direct capture model using the same potentials.

## Key numbers

- $^{8}$Be ground state: calculated resonance energy $E_{\rm R}^{\rm th} = 92.1$ keV (experimental $92.12 \pm 0.05$ keV); calculated $\alpha$-width $\Gamma_{\alpha}^{\rm calc} = 6.86$ eV (experimental $6.8 \pm 1.7$ eV)
- $^{8}$Be $2^+$ state: calculated energy $2.78$ MeV (experimental $3.13 \pm 0.03$ MeV); calculated $\alpha$-width $1.3 \times 10^6$ eV (experimental $1.5 \pm 0.2 \times 10^6$ eV)
- $^{8}$Be $4^+$ state: calculated energy $11.02$ MeV (experimental $11.5 \pm 0.3$ MeV); calculated $\alpha$-width $>3 \times 10^6$ eV (experimental $\approx 3.5 \times 10^6$ eV)
- $^{12}$C $0_2^+$ state at 7.654 MeV: calculated $\alpha$-width $7.5$ eV (experimental $8.3 \pm 1.0$ eV)
- $^{12}$C $2_2^+$ state at 10.3 MeV: calculated $\alpha$-width $\approx 2.0 \times 10^6$ eV (experimental $3.0 \pm 0.7 \times 10^6$ eV)
- $\Gamma_{\gamma}$-width for $^{8}$Be $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$: $8.4$ meV; B(E2) = 75 W.u.
- $\Gamma_{\gamma}$-width for $^{12}$C $0_2^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+$: $4.1$ meV; B(E2) = 8.8 W.u.
- $\Gamma_{\gamma}$-width for $^{12}$C $2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_2^+$: $17$ meV; B(E2) = 100 W.u.
- $\alpha$-particle spectroscopic amplitudes for $^{8}$Be: ${\cal S}({\rm g.s.}) = 1.48$, ${\cal S}(2^+) = 1.46$, ${\cal S}(4^+) = 1.39$

## Context

This work extends the folding-potential approach of Kobos (1984) and Oberhummer (1991) to simultaneously describe $\alpha$–$\alpha$ scattering and $\alpha$–cluster states in $^{8}$Be and $^{12}$C, providing a unified framework for the triple-alpha process. The calculated $\Gamma_{\gamma}$-widths and B(E2) values enable improved reaction-rate determinations for the triple-alpha process, building on earlier work by Langanke (1983) on bremsstrahlung models. The assumption that the 10.3 MeV level in $^{12}$C is $2^+$ rather than $0^+$ follows suggestions from experimental results by Jacquot (1974) and microscopic calculations by Fujiwara (1980), and if confirmed would resolve a long-standing ambiguity in the nuclear structure of $^{12}$C.
