# Does the QCD plasma contain propagating gluons?

> Comparison of two appropriately chosen screening masses of colour singlet operators in the pure glue QCD plasma indicates that at sufficiently high temperature it contains a weakly-interacting massive quasi-particle with the quantum numbers of the electric gluon. Still in the deconfined phase, but closer to T_c, the same mass ratio is similar to that at zero temperature, indicating that the propagating modes are more glueball-like, albeit with a lower scale for the masses. We observe a continuity between these two regimes.

## Key claim

The ratio of screening masses in the $A_2^{--}$ and $A_1^{++}$ sectors approaches the perturbative value of 3/2 for temperatures above about $1.25T_c$, indicating the presence of weakly interacting massive quasi-particles with electric gluon quantum numbers, but closer to $T_c$ the same ratio resembles the zero-temperature value, suggesting more glueball-like propagating modes.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of pure gauge $SU(3)$ on $N_t=4$ lattices with spatial sizes up to $24^3$, using the Wilson action. They measured screening masses in the $A_1^{++}$ (scalar) and $A_2^{--}$ sectors using Wilson loops, the real part of the Wilson line, and the imaginary part of the Wilson line, extracting masses via variational procedures and controlling for tunneling effects through finite-size scaling and phase identification.

## Key numbers

- At $T/T_c=0.97$, the $A_1^{++}$ screening mass is $m(T)/T_c=3.41\pm0.08$; at $T/T_c=0.89$, it is $m(T)/T_c=3.52\pm0.07$
- The zero-temperature scalar glueball mass at similar lattice spacing gives $m(T=0)/T_c=3.93\pm0.05$
- The ratio $m(T)/m(T=0)\sim0.8$ in the scalar channel near $T_c$
- At $T=T_c$ in the deconfined phase, $m(T_c)/T_c\approx1$
- The perturbative expansion coefficients for the Debye screening mass are $b=2.46\pm0.15$ and $c=-0.49\pm0.15$
- $T_c/\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}=1.15\pm0.05$

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of screening masses in the QCD plasma, particularly building on the approach of screening correlators classified by the $D_4\times Z_2(T)\times Z_2(C)$ symmetry group (Bernd, 2000; Saumen, 2001). The results are compatible with earlier screening mass measurements from point-to-point Wilson-line correlations (Olaf, 2001) and with zero-temperature glueball mass ratios (Peardon, 1999). The findings enable a direct connection between screening properties and the nature of propagating modes in the plasma, showing a smooth crossover from glueball-like excitations near $T_c$ to weakly interacting electric gluons at higher temperatures, which may also explain the behavior of the entropy density (Entropy, 2001).
