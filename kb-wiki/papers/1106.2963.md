# Spin Scissors Mode and the Fine Structure of M1 States in Nuclei

> The coupled dynamics of low lying modes, including the scissors mode, and various giant quadrupole resonances are studied with the help of the Wigner Function Moments method generalized to take into account spin degrees of freedom. Equations of motion for collective variables are derived on the basis of Time Dependent Hartree-Fock equations in the model of harmonic oscillator with spin orbital mean field potential plus quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction. Introducing spin allows one to consider new types of nuclear collective motion where the nucleons with spin 'up' oscillate against nucleons with spin 'down'.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the experimentally observed group of M1 peaks in the energy interval 2-4 MeV corresponds to two different types of collective motion: the "standard" scissors mode and a new "spin scissors mode" where nucleons with spin up oscillate against nucleons with spin down, though this conclusion is based on a model that includes only the spin-orbit interaction as the spin-dependent mean-field term, omitting other spin-dependent interactions.

## Method

The authors generalized the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method, previously based on Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations, to include spin degrees of freedom. They derived equations of motion for collective variables using a harmonic oscillator model with a spin-orbit mean-field potential plus a separable quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction, then linearized these equations for the K^π = 1^+ scissors mode and solved them in the small-amplitude approximation.

## Key numbers

- The earlier WFM method without spin reproduced rare-earth nuclei scissors mode energies with approximately 10% accuracy, but B(M1) factors remained about two times too high compared to experiment.
- The experimentally observed group of M1 peaks lies in the energy interval 2-4 MeV.
- The spin-orbit coupling strength η appears in the equations with factors of ℏ/2, ℏ/√2, and ℏ²/8 in the Poisson bracket expansions.

## Context

This work extends the WFM method previously applied to the scissors mode by Balbutsev, Molodtsova, and Schuck, which had been improved by switching from TDHF to TDHFB equations to include pair correlations (Malov, Urban). The earlier calculations reproduced energies with ~10% accuracy but overestimated B(M1) factors by about a factor of two. The present generalization to include spin degrees of freedom aims to resolve this discrepancy by allowing some M1 transition strength to shift to the 5-10 MeV region where a spin-nature 1+ resonance is observed. The results enable future inclusion of other spin-dependent interactions beyond the spin-orbit term.
