# Note on the Time Dependent Variational Approach with Quasi-Spin Squeezed State for Pairing Model

> A simple many-fermion system in which there exists N identical fermions in a single spherical orbit with pairing interaction is treated by means of the time-dependent variational approach with a quasi-spin squeezed state with the aim of going beyond the time-dependent Hartree-Fock or Bogoliubov theory. It is shown that the ground state energy is reproduced well analytically in this approach.

## Key claim

The time-dependent variational approach with a quasi-spin squeezed state reproduces the exact ground state energy of the pairing model analytically up to order \(1/\Omega\) under a small-\(|Y|^2\) expansion, provided a consistency condition between the canonical angle variables is satisfied; this goes beyond the \(su(2)\)-coherent state (BCS) approximation, which misses the \(2/\Omega\) term.

## Method

The authors treat \(N\) identical fermions in a single spherical orbit with a pairing interaction, using the \(su(2)\) quasi-spin algebra. They construct a quasi-spin squeezed state on top of the \(su(2)\)-coherent state (BCS vacuum) by applying an exponential of the square of a boson-like operator built from Bogoliubov-transformed fermions. The time-dependent variational principle yields canonical equations of motion for the action-angle variables \((n_X, \theta_X)\) and \((n_Y, \theta_Y)\), where \(n_Y\) represents quantum fluctuations. The ground state energy is obtained by imposing the consistency condition \(\dot{\theta}_Y = 2\dot{\theta}_X\) and minimizing the energy expectation value under a small-\(n_Y\) expansion.

## Key numbers

* Exact ground state energy: \(E_0 = \epsilon N - \frac{1}{4} G N \Omega \left(2 - \frac{N}{\Omega} + \frac{2}{\Omega}\right)\) (Eq. 1-7), where \(\Omega = j+1/2\) is half the degeneracy.
* \(su(2)\)-coherent state (BCS) ground state energy: \(E = \epsilon N - \frac{1}{4} G N \Omega \left(2 - \frac{N}{\Omega} + \frac{N}{\Omega^2}\right)\) (Eq. 1-12), missing the exact \(2/\Omega\) term.
* Quasi-spin squeezed state ground state energy (large \(N\), large \(\Omega\) limit): \(H_{\text{sq}} = \epsilon N - \frac{1}{4} G N \Omega \left(2 - \frac{N}{\Omega} + \frac{2}{\Omega} + O(1/(N\Omega), 1/\Omega^2, 1/N^2)\right)\) (Eq. 3-31), reproducing the exact \(2/\Omega\) term.
* Numerical example: parameters \(G=1.2\), \(\epsilon=1.0\), \(\Omega=2N=8\); initial values \(n_X(0)=1.3\), \(\theta_X(0)=0.0\), \(n_Y(0)=0.35\), \(\theta_Y(0)=0.0\) (Figs. 1 and 2).

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous time-dependent variational study of the Lipkin model with a quasi-spin squeezed state (Tsue and Akaike, 2004) to the pairing model, where the exact ground state energy is known analytically (Kerman, 1961; Lawson and Macfarlane, 1965). The quasi-spin squeezed state goes beyond the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) or Bogoliubov (TDHB) theory, which corresponds to the \(su(2)\)-coherent state. The analytical reproduction of the exact energy up to \(1/\Omega\) clarifies the role of quantum fluctuations included in the squeezed state and enables a systematic large-\(N\) (or \(\hbar\)) expansion for pairing-type many-fermion systems.
