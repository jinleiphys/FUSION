# Possibility to study pentaquark states $P_{c}(4312), P_{c}(4440)$, and $P_{c}(4457)$ in $γp\rightarrow J/ψp$ reaction

> Inspired by the observation of the pentaquark states $P_{c}(4312),P_{c}(4440)$ and $P_{c}(4457)$ at LHCb, photoproduction of these three $P_{c}$ states via the interaction $γp\rightarrow J/ψp$ is investigated in an effective Lagrangian approach. The $t$-channel Pomeron exchange diffractive process is considered as the main background for the $J/ψ$ photoproduction. The numerical results show that the theoretical cross section, which is calculated by assuming a branching ratio $Br[P_{c}\rightarrow J/ψp]\simeq 3\%$, is consistent with the existing experimental data of the $ γp\rightarrow J/ψp$ process. With such a branching ratio, if experimental precision reaches 0.1 nb within a bin of 100 MeV for photon energy, two peaks are expected to be obviously observed in the $J/ψ$ photoproduction. To observe the two-peak structure from $P_{c}(4440)$ and $ P_{c}(4457)$, higher precision, about 0.1nb/10 MeV, is required to distinguish two close pentaquarks. If the physical branching ratio is larger, the requirement of experimental precision will be reduced. The differential cross sections for reaction $γp\rightarrow J/ψp$ are also present. It is found that the $t$-channel Pomeron exchange provides a sharp increase at extreme forward angles and gives a sizable contribution at most energy points, while the contributions from the $s$-channel $P_{c}$ exchanges play important roles at threshold energies. The experimental measurement of the $γp\rightarrow J/ψp$ process in the near-threshold energy region around $E_{γ}\simeq 9.4-10.5$ GeV is suggested, and is accessible at CEBAF@JLab and COMPASS.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the three pentaquark states \(P_{c}(4312), P_{c}(4440), P_{c}(4457)\) can be observed in the photoproduction reaction \(\gamma p \rightarrow J/\psi p\) if the experimental precision reaches 0.1 nb within a 100 MeV bin for photon energy, but only if the branching ratio \(Br[P_{c}\rightarrow J/\psi p]\) is at least about 3%; if the physical branching ratio is larger, the required precision is reduced.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams to model the \(s\)-channel production of the three \(P_{c}\) states and the \(t\)-channel Pomeron exchange as the dominant background. The coupling constants for the \(\gamma N P_{c}\) and \(P_{c} \psi N\) vertices were derived from the decay widths of the \(P_{c}\) states into \(J/\psi p\) using vector meson dominance (VMD), assuming branching ratios of 3% and 10%. The total and differential cross sections were then calculated as functions of photon beam energy and scattering angle.

## Key numbers

- The masses and widths of the three pentaquark states are taken from LHCb data: \(P_{c}(4312)\) mass = \(4311.9 \pm 0.7_{-0.6}^{+6.8}\) MeV, width = \(9.8 \pm 2.7_{-4.5}^{+3.7}\) MeV; \(P_{c}(4440)\) mass = \(4440.3 \pm 1.3_{-4.7}^{+4.1}\) MeV, width = \(20.6 \pm 4.9_{-10.1}^{+8.7}\) MeV; \(P_{c}(4457)\) mass = \(4457.3 \pm 0.6_{-1.7}^{+4.1}\) MeV, width = \(6.4 \pm 2.0_{-1.9}^{+5.7}\) MeV.
- Assuming \(Br[P_{c}\rightarrow J/\psi p] \simeq 3\%\), the coupling constants are: \(g_{P_{c}\psi N} = 0.06\) for \(P_{c}(4312)\), 0.08 for \(P_{c}(4440)\), and 0.036 for \(P_{c}(4457)\); the electromagnetic couplings \(eh\) or \(eh_{1}\) are 0.0014, 0.0018, and 0.0008 respectively.
- To observe the \(P_{c}(4312)\) as a distinct peak, an experimental precision of 1 nb per 0.1 GeV bin is required; to resolve the two-peak structure of \(P_{c}(4440)\) and \(P_{c}(4457)\), a higher precision of 0.1 nb per 10 MeV bin is needed.
- The suggested photon energy range for measurement is \(E_{\gamma} \simeq 9.4 - 10.5\) GeV, corresponding to center-of-mass energies near threshold.
- The \(J/\psi \rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\) partial width used is 5.547 keV, giving \(e/f_{\psi} \simeq 0.027\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hidden-charm pentaquark photoproduction, such as those by Wang (2015) and Huang (2013), which focused on the earlier \(P_{c}(4450)\) and \(P_{c}(4380)\) states observed by LHCb in 2015. The new LHCb results (Aaij 2019) split the former \(P_{c}(4450)\) into two narrow states, \(P_{c}(4440)\) and \(P_{c}(4457)\), and added the \(P_{c}(4312)\), motivating a reanalysis. The calculations suggest that experiments at CEBAF at Jefferson Lab (using GlueX and CLAS12 detectors) and at COMPASS can search for these states in the near-threshold region, and that measurement of the photon beam asymmetry \(\Sigma_{\gamma}\) can help clarify the role of the pentaquarks.
