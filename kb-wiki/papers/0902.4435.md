# Three-particle correlation from glasma flux tubes

> We compute three particle correlations in the Glasma flux tube model of high energy heavy ion collisions. We obtain a simple geometrical picture of these correlations; when convoluted with final state radial flow, it results in distinct predictions for the near side three particle correlation in central heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The three-particle correlation in the Glasma flux tube model is proportional to the square of the ratio of the flux tube area to the system area, yielding a simple geometric picture; this result is derived analytically for large transverse momentum \(Q_S \ll p_\perp, q_\perp, l_\perp\) and is conjectured to hold at lower momentum as well, though the numerical coefficient may differ.

## Method

The authors computed the three-gluon correlation function in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework by evaluating the classical disconnected diagrams for gluon production from Glasma flux tubes, averaging over color sources using a Gaussian (MV model) weight functional. They then applied a radial boost to the correlation function to model the effect of transverse flow, using a blast-wave parametrization with transverse velocity \(V_r\) extracted from PHENIX data.

## Key numbers

* The three-particle correlation coefficient \(\kappa_3 \sim 0.3\) for the expression \(C_3 = \kappa_3 \frac{1}{S_\perp^2 Q_S^4} \langle dN/dy_p d^2\mathbf{p}_\perp \rangle \langle dN/dy_q d^2\mathbf{q}_\perp \rangle \langle dN/dy_l d^2\mathbf{l}_\perp \rangle\).
* The two-particle correlation coefficient \(\kappa_2 \sim 0.4\) for \(C_2 = \kappa_2 \frac{1}{S_\perp Q_S^2} \langle dN/dy_p d^2\mathbf{p}_\perp \rangle \langle dN/dy_q d^2\mathbf{q}_\perp \rangle\).
* The correlation strength scales as \(C_3 \propto 1/N_{\rm part}^2\), where \(N_{\rm part} \sim S_\perp Q_S^2\).
* The maximum of the boosted angular correlation occurs at \(\Delta\phi_{pq} = \Delta\phi_{pl} = 0\) and the minimum at \(\Delta\phi_{pq} = 2\pi/3\), \(\Delta\phi_{pl} = 4\pi/3\).
* The saturation scale is related to the color charge density by \(Q_S \sim 0.6\, g^2 \mu_A\).

## Context

This work extends the Glasma flux tube model previously applied to two-particle ridge correlations by Dumitru et al. (2008) and Gavin et al. (2008), which quantitatively reproduced STAR data on the near-side ridge. The three-particle correlation predictions are motivated by first experimental studies from STAR (Netrakanti et al., 2008). The results enable tests of the flux tube geometry via centrality dependence and provide a method to extract the average radial flow independently of the non-perturbative coefficient \(\kappa_3\).
