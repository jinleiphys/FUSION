# Quark model with chiral-symmetry breaking and confinement in the Covariant Spectator Theory

> We propose a model for the quark-antiquark interaction in Minkowski space using the Covariant Spectator Theory. We show that with an equal-weighted scalar-pseudoscalar structure for the confining part of our interaction kernel the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is preserved and our model complies with the Adler-zero constraint for pi-pi-scattering imposed by chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors propose a quark-antiquark interaction model in Minkowski space using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) and show that an equal-weighted scalar-pseudoscalar structure for the confining part of the interaction kernel preserves the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity and satisfies the Adler-zero constraint for pi-pi scattering, but only in the chiral limit of vanishing bare quark mass and vanishing vertex momentum.

## Method

The authors construct a general Lorentz structure for the quark-antiquark interaction kernel in CST, including scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor components with weight parameters lambda_i and kappa_i. They impose the condition that the scalar and pseudoscalar weights are equal (lambda_S = lambda_P) and then verify that the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity (AV-WTI) is satisfied by the dressed quark propagator from the CST Dyson equation. They then demonstrate that the sum of all diagrams contributing to pi-pi scattering vanishes in the chiral limit by using the AV-WTI to replace the pion vertex function with the axial vertex and exploiting the decoupling of the confining kernel from the chiral-limit equations.

## Key numbers

* The condition lambda_S = lambda_P (equal weights for scalar and pseudoscalar confining terms) is required for the AV-WTI to be preserved.
* The linear-confining potential satisfies the CST generalization of the non-relativistic condition V_L(r=0)=0, given by the integral equation: integral (d^3 k / E_k) V_LR(p +/- hat k) = 0, where E_k = sqrt(m^2 + vector k^2).
* The Adler zero is demonstrated in the chiral limit m_0 -> 0 (vanishing bare quark mass) and P -> 0 (vanishing vertex momentum).

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory approach to quark-antiquark interactions, building on prior work by Gross (1991) and Gross and Riska (1987, 1993, 1996). It addresses the challenge of making confining forces consistent with spontaneous chiral-symmetry breaking, similar to the approach of PhysRevD.47.1145 for a different formalism. The model enables future calculations of hadronic observables in Minkowski space, particularly form factors in the timelike region, and provides a framework for studying the interplay between confinement and chiral symmetry in light mesons.
