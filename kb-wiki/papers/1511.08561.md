# Hybrid Stars using the Quark-Meson Coupling and Proper Time NJL Models

> Background: At high density deconfinement of hadronic matter may occur leading to quark matter. The immense densities reached in the inner core of massive neutron stars may be sufficient to facilitate the transition. Purpose: To investigate a crossover transition between two phenomenological models which epitomise QCD in two different regimes, while incorporating the influence of quark degrees of freedom in both. Method: We use the Hartree-Fock quark-meson coupling model and the proper time regularised three flavour NJL model to describe hadronic and quark matter, respectively. Hybrid equations of state are obtained by interpolating the energy density as a function of total baryonic density and calculating the pressure. Results: Equations of state for hadronic, quark and hybrid matter and the resulting mass versus radius curves for hybrid stars are shown, as well as other relevant physical quantities such as species fractions and the speed of sound in matter. Conclusions: The observations of massive neutron stars can certainly be explained within such a construction. However, the so-called thermodynamic correction arising from an interpolation method can have a considerable impact on the equation of state. The interpolation dependency of and physical meaning behind such corrections require further study.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a crossover (rather than first-order) transition from hadronic to quark matter can explain the existence of massive neutron stars near two solar masses, finding that such an explanation is possible but that the thermodynamic correction arising from the interpolation method used to construct the hybrid equation of state has a considerable impact and requires further study.

## Method

The authors use the Hartree-Fock quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to describe hadronic matter and the proper time regularised three-flavour Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to describe quark matter. Hybrid equations of state are obtained by interpolating the energy density as a function of total baryonic density and then calculating the pressure, rather than constructing a first-order phase transition via Maxwell or Gibbs constructions.

## Key numbers

* The QMC model parameters are fixed at saturation density in symmetric nuclear matter, with the sigma meson cut-off mass explored in the range 0.9 to 1.3 GeV.
* Massive hybrid stars with masses near 2 solar masses (M_NS ~ 2 M_sun) could be produced provided the quark matter equation of state is stiff enough and the transition occurs at moderately low density (rho ~ 3 rho_0, where rho_0 is nuclear saturation density).
* The Hartree-Fock QMC model includes the full vertex structure for vector mesons, including both Dirac and Pauli tensor terms, for all octet baryons.

## Context

This work extends the crossover-type transition approach recently investigated by Masuda et al. (2012) using several hadronic models and the three-momentum regularised NJL model, and complements work by Miyatsu et al. (2015) which used a different variation of the Hartree-Fock QMC model with a bag model and a Gibbs construction for a first-order transition. The novelty is the incorporation of quark degrees of freedom in the hadronic phase through the QMC model and the use of a covariantly regularised NJL model for deconfined quark matter, which enables investigation of whether the crossover construction can reconcile exotic degrees of freedom with observations of massive neutron stars by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013).
