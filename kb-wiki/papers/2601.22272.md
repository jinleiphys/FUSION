# Excited-state uncertainties in lattice-QCD calculations of multi-hadron systems

> Excited-state effects lead to hard-to-quantify systematic uncertainties in lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) spectroscopy calculations when computationally accessible imaginary times are smaller than inverse excitation gaps, as often arises for multi-hadron systems with signal-to-noise problems. Lanczos residual bounds address this by providing two-sided constraints on energies that do not require assumptions beyond Hermiticity, but often give very conservative systematic uncertainty estimates. Here, a more-constraining set of gap bounds is introduced for hadron spectroscopy. These bounds provide tighter constraints whose validity requires an explicit assumption about an energy gap. Exactly solvable lattice field theory correlators are used to test the utility of residual and gap bounds at finite and infinite statistics. Two-sided bounds and other analysis methods are then applied to a high-statistics LQCD calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering at $m_π\sim 800$ MeV. Generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) and Lanczos energy estimators are compatible when applied to the same correlator data, but analyses including different interpolating operators show statistically significant inconsistencies. However, two-sided bounds from all operators are consistent. Under the assumption that the number of energy levels below $NΔ$ and $ΔΔ$ thresholds is the same as for non-interacting nucleons, gap bounds are sufficient to constrain nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes at phenomenologically relevant precision. Lanczos methods further reveal that energy-eigenstate estimates from previously studied asymmetric correlators have not converged over accessible imaginary times. Nevertheless, data-driven examples demonstrate why assumptions are required to draw conclusions about the natures of two-nucleon ground states at these masses.

## Key claim

The paper introduces "gap bounds" as a more constraining alternative to Lanczos residual bounds for quantifying excited-state systematic uncertainties in LQCD spectroscopy, but these tighter bounds require an explicit assumption about the energy gap to unresolved states (the "no-missing-states assumption") rather than being assumption-free like residual bounds.

## Method

The authors apply Lanczos methods to compute residual bounds and newly introduced gap bounds on energy eigenvalues from lattice QCD correlation functions, testing these bounds against exactly solvable scalar field theory correlators and then applying them to a high-statistics LQCD calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering at a pion mass of approximately 800 MeV. The study uses quark propagators from 2.12 × 10^7 sources and compares Lanczos energy estimators with generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) analyses of symmetric correlator matrices, as well as with asymmetric correlator analyses that have historically been used in two-nucleon spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

- The LQCD dataset uses quark propagators from 2.12 × 10^7 sources, an order of magnitude more measurements than previous studies of spectroscopy in two-baryon systems
- The calculation is performed at m_π ∼ 800 MeV
- Only results with t ≥ 2 are included in analyses to avoid contact-term complications
- Residual bounds lead to percent-level constraints on two-nucleon energies but are insufficiently precise to determine scattering phase shifts
- Gap bounds are sufficient to constrain nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes at phenomenologically relevant precision under the assumption that the number of energy levels below NΔ and ΔΔ thresholds is the same as for non-interacting nucleons

## Context

This work extends the Lanczos residual bound approach introduced by Wagman (2024) and Hackett (2024) to LQCD correlator analysis, introducing gap bounds as a compromise between assumption-free residual bounds and more model-dependent systematic uncertainty estimates. The study addresses long-standing discrepancies between asymmetric correlator analyses (Fukugita 1994, Beane 2006, NPLQCD 2011) that favor bound states in two-nucleon systems at unphysical quark masses, and correlator matrix analyses (Francis 2018, Amarasinghe 2021, Horz 2020) that do not find evidence of such bound states. The results demonstrate that GEVP and Lanczos energy estimators are compatible when applied to the same correlator data, but analyses including different interpolating operators show statistically significant inconsistencies, while two-sided bounds from all operators remain consistent.
