# $η\to π^0 γγ$ decay within unitarized chiral perturbation theory

> We improve the calculations of the $η\to π^0 γγ$ decay within the context of meson chiral lagrangians. We use a chiral unitary approach for the meson-meson interaction, thus generating the $a_0(980)$ resonance and fixing the longstanding sign ambiguity on its contribution. This also allows us to calculate the loops with one vector meson exchange, thus removing a former source of uncertainty. In addition we ensure the consistency of the approach with other processes. First, by using vector meson dominance couplings normalized to agree with radiative vector meson decays. And, second, by checking the consistency of the calculations with the related $γγ\to π^0 η$ reaction. We find an $η\to π^0 γγ$ decay width of $0.47\pm 0.10$ eV, in clear disagreement with published data but in remarkable agreement with the most recent measurement.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the $\eta\to \pi^0 \gamma\gamma$ decay width using a chiral unitary approach that dynamically generates the $a_0(980)$ resonance and includes vector meson dominance contributions with couplings normalized to experimental radiative vector meson decays, finding a width of $0.47\pm 0.10$ eV that disagrees with older published data but agrees with the most recent measurement.

## Method

The authors combine tree-level vector meson dominance (VMD) diagrams with a chiral unitary approach that resums meson-meson scattering via the Bethe-Salpeter equation using lowest-order chiral perturbation theory amplitudes, thereby dynamically generating the $a_0(980)$ resonance. They normalize the $VP\gamma$ couplings to reproduce experimental branching ratios of radiative vector meson decays ($\rho\to\pi^0\gamma$, $\omega\to\pi^0\gamma$, $\phi\to\eta\gamma$, $K^{*}\to K\gamma$, etc.) and include resummed chiral loops from kaon intermediate states, VMD loop diagrams with vector meson exchange, and anomalous $\gamma\to 3M$ vertex loops. The consistency of the approach is checked against the crossed-channel $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^0\eta$ reaction.

## Key numbers

- $\Gamma(\eta\to\pi^0\gamma\gamma) = 0.47 \pm 0.10$ eV (final result, including all contributions and uncertainties)
- Tree-level VMD contribution alone: $0.30 \pm 0.06$ eV (with normalized couplings); $0.57$ eV (with universal couplings)
- Chiral loops from Eq. (tNPA629) contribute $0.011$ eV to the total width but increase the rate by $30\%$ when added coherently to tree-level VMD
- The $a_0(980)$ resonance tail increases the decay rate from $0.47$ eV to $0.48$ eV
- VMD loop contributions increase the decay rate by $0.02$ eV
- Previous experimental value (PDG): $\Gamma = 0.84 \pm 0.18$ eV; most recent measurement (Nefkens, preliminary): $\Gamma = 0.42 \pm 0.14$ eV

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations of $\eta\to\pi^0\gamma\gamma$ by Ametller et al. (1991), which suffered from uncertainties in the sign and size of the $a_0(980)$ contribution and from uncalculated VMD loop diagrams. The authors use the unitarized chiral approach developed by Oller et al. (1997) for meson-meson scattering and $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^0\eta$, which dynamically generates the $a_0(980)$ resonance without free parameters. The approach is consistent with successful descriptions of related radiative decays such as $\phi\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma$, $\omega\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma$, and $\rho\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma$ by Bramon et al. (2001) and Palomar et al. (2001). The result enables precise tests of the chiral unitary framework through improved measurements of the $\eta\to\pi^0\gamma\gamma$ decay width and the $\gamma\gamma$ invariant mass distribution.
