# The apparent Coulomb reacceleration of neutrons in electrodissociation of the deuteron

> We demonstrate that the final state $p$-$n$ interaction in the reaction of electrodissociation of the deuteron at large $Q^{2}$ in a static external field leads to the apparent reacceleration of neutrons. The shift of the neutron velocity from the velocity of the deuteron beam is related to the quantum-mechanical forward-backward asymmetry of the missing momentum distribution in the $^2H(e,e'p)n$ scattering.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the apparent reacceleration of neutrons in electrodissociation of the deuteron at large momentum transfer squared \(Q^{2}\) arises from the final-state proton-neutron interaction, not from higher-order Coulomb field effects; the sign of the velocity shift depends on the sign of the real part of the forward proton-neutron scattering amplitude, allowing both apparent deceleration and acceleration.

## Method

The authors calculate the average missing momentum distribution \(W(\vec{p}_{m})\) for the \(^{2}\mathrm{H}(e,e'p)n\) reaction in the laboratory frame, using the Glauber theory to describe the final-state interaction (FSI) between the proton and neutron at high kinetic energy (\(T_{\mathrm{kin}} \gtrsim 0.5\) GeV). They relate the shift of the neutron velocity from the deuteron beam velocity to the forward-backward asymmetry of the missing momentum distribution, deriving an analytic expression for the average velocity shift \(\Delta v_{z}\) in terms of the neutron-proton forward scattering amplitude parameters (total cross section \(\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}\), ratio of real to imaginary part \(\rho\), and diffraction slope \(b_{o}^{2}\)). Numerical calculations are performed using the realistic Bonn potential wave function for the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for neutron-proton scattering is \(\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}(np) \sim 40\) mb at \(T_{\mathrm{kin}} \gtrsim 1.0\) GeV.
- The ratio of the real to imaginary part of the forward elastic scattering amplitude is \(\rho \sim -0.4\) at \(T_{\mathrm{kin}} \gtrsim 1.0\) GeV.
- The diffraction slope parameter is \(b_{o} \sim 0.5\) fm.
- The estimated velocity shift of neutrons is \(\Delta v_{z} \sim 0.0026\) (dimensionless, in units of \(c\)).
- The deuteron radius is \(R_{D} \sim 2\) fm.
- The Glauber approximation is valid for \(Q^{2} \gtrsim 1\) GeV\(^{2}\) (corresponding to \(T_{\mathrm{kin}} \gtrsim 0.5\) GeV).
- The departure of the integrated missing momentum distribution from unity due to FSI attenuation is small, \(\sim 7\%\).

## Context

This work extends the quantum-mechanical treatment of Coulomb reacceleration effects previously discussed for the dissociation of \(^{11}\mathrm{Li}\) nuclei (experiment [1] and theoretical treatments [2,3]), contradicting the classical interpretation of deceleration and acceleration in the Coulomb field. The authors build on the well-understood final-state interaction formalism for \(^{2}\mathrm{H}(e,e'p)n\) reactions (Jeschonnek et al. [8]) and the Glauber theory description of nucleon-nucleon scattering at high energies (reviewed by [11]). The results enable direct measurement of the forward-backward asymmetry of the missing momentum distribution in traditional \(^{2}\mathrm{H}(e,e'p)n\) experiments, though the authors note that measuring the velocity shift in Coulomb dissociation of relativistic deuterons is not feasible at large \(Q^{2}\) due to masking by diffraction dissociation induced by strong nuclear interactions.
