# Removal of the Energy Dependence from the Resolvent-like Energy-Dependent Interactions

> The spectral problem $(A + V(z))ψ=zψ$ is considered with $A$, a self-adjoint Hamiltonian of sufficiently arbitrary nature. The perturbation $V(z)$ is assumed to depend on the energy $z$ as resolvent of another self-adjoint operator $A':$ $V(z)=-B(A'-z)^{-1}B^{*}$. It is supposed that operator $B$ has a finite Hilbert-Schmidt norm and spectra of operators $A$ and $A'$ are separated. The conditions are formulated when the perturbation $V(z)$ may be replaced with an energy-independent ``potential'' $W$ such that the Hamiltonian $H=A +W$ has the same spectrum (more exactly a part of spectrum) and the same eigenfunctions as the initial spectral problem. The orthogonality and expansion theorems are proved for eigenfunction systems of the Hamiltonian $ H=A + W $. Scattering theory is developed for $H$ in the case when operator $A$ has continuous spectrum. Applications of the results obtained to few-body problems are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for a spectral problem with an energy-dependent perturbation of resolvent form, \(V(z) = -B(A' - z)^{-1}B^{*}\), one can replace \(V(z)\) with an energy-independent potential \(W\) such that the Hamiltonian \(H = A + W\) shares a part of the spectrum and the same eigenfunctions as the original problem, provided the spectra of \(A\) and \(A'\) are separated and the Hilbert-Schmidt norm of \(B\) satisfies \(\|B\|_2 < \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{dist}\{\sigma(A), \sigma(A')\}\).

## Method

The author considers a two-channel Hamiltonian \(\mathbf{H}\) acting on a direct sum of Hilbert spaces \(\mathcal{H} = \mathcal{H}_1 \oplus \mathcal{H}_2\), with channel Hamiltonians \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) and coupling operators \(B_{12}\) and \(B_{21}\). Reducing the spectral problem to one channel yields an energy-dependent perturbation \(V_\alpha(z) = -B_{\alpha\beta}(A_\beta - z)^{-1}B_{\beta\alpha}\). The energy dependence is removed by solving the nonlinear operator equation \(H_\alpha = A_\alpha + V_\alpha(H_\alpha)\), where \(V_\alpha(Y)\) is defined via an integral over the spectral measure of \(A_\beta\). Existence and uniqueness of solutions are established using a contracting mapping argument under the condition \(\|B_{\alpha\beta}\|_2 < \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{dist}\{\sigma(A_1), \sigma(A_2)\}\). The resulting operators \(H_\alpha\) are shown to correspond to parts of \(\mathbf{H}\) acting in invariant subspaces.

## Key numbers

- The condition for solvability of the basic equation is \(\|B_{\alpha\beta}\|_2 < \frac{1}{2} d_0\), where \(d_0 = \operatorname{dist}\{\sigma(A_1), \sigma(A_2)\} > 0\).
- The Hilbert-Schmidt norm of the coupling operator must satisfy \(\|B_{\alpha\beta}\|_2 < d_0 \min\{ \frac{\delta}{1+\delta^2}, \frac{1}{1+\delta} \}\) for a given \(\delta > 0\) in the more general existence theorem.
- The estimate \(\| (A_\alpha + B_{\alpha\beta} Q_{\beta\alpha} - \mu)^{-1} \| \leq C_{\alpha\beta} / (1 + |\mu|)\) holds with \(C_{\alpha\beta} \sim 1/(d_0 - \|B_{\alpha\beta} Q_{\beta\alpha}\|)\) when the spectra are separated and the number of intervals in the continuous spectrum is finite.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier studies (Motovilov, 1991–1994) on removing energy dependence from resolvent-like interactions, and it connects to the method of constructing invariant subspaces for self-adjoint operators developed by Malyshev and Minlos (1980s). The results enable the construction of effective energy-independent cluster Hamiltonians for few-body problems, as previously applied to atoms adsorbed on metal surfaces (Motovilov and collaborators, 1994). The paper also notes overlap with the work of Adamyan and Langer (1995) on rational operator-valued functions, though the methods differ.
