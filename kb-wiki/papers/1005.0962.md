# Can the nuclear symmetry potential at supra-saturation densities be negative?

> In the framework of an Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, for the central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reaction at an incident beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon, effect of nuclear symmetry potential at supra-saturation densities on the pre-equilibrium clusters emission is studied. It is found that for the positive symmetry potential at supra-saturation densities the neutron to proton ratio of lighter clusters with mass number $A\leq3$ ($(n/p)_{A\leq3}$) is larger than that of the weighter clusters with mass number $A>3$ ($(n/p)_{A>3}$), whereas for the negative symmetry potential at supra-saturation densities the $(n/p)_{A\leq3}$ is \emph{smaller} than that of the $(n/p)_{A>3}$. This may be considered as a probe of the negative symmetry potential at supra-saturation densities.

## Key claim

For the central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon, the sign of the symmetry potential at supra-saturation densities (positive vs. negative) reverses the ordering of the neutron-to-proton ratio of light clusters ($A \leq 3$) relative to heavier clusters ($A > 3$), providing a potential probe of a negative symmetry potential at high density, but the author notes that different isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections may affect the results.

## Method

The author used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent mean-field (MDI) interaction, varying the parameter $x$ to produce either a positive ($x=-2$, super-stiff symmetry energy) or negative ($x=1$, super-soft symmetry energy) symmetry potential at supra-saturation densities, while keeping the sub-saturation symmetry potential fixed at $x=0$. For the central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon, the neutron-to-proton ratio $(n/p)$ of pre-equilibrium clusters was computed using two cluster recognition methods: a density cut-off ($\rho_c = 1/8 \rho_0$) and a phase-space coalescence method ($P_0 = 263$ MeV/c, $R_0 = 3$ fm), with nucleons having kinetic energy $E_{\text{kin}}/\text{nucleon} \geq 20$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The maximal compressed density reached in the central $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon is about $2.5\rho_0$, where $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- For the negative symmetry potential ($x=1$), the integrated $(n/p)_{A\leq3}$ is 1.44 and $(n/p)_{A>3}$ is 1.65 using the density cut-off method, and 1.44 and 1.55 using the coalescence method.
- For the positive symmetry potential ($x=-2$), the integrated $(n/p)_{A\leq3}$ is 1.48 and $(n/p)_{A>3}$ is 1.35 using the density cut-off method, and 1.49 and 1.42 using the coalescence method.
- The reaction system's overall $n/p$ is 1.49.
- Changing the density cut-off $\rho_c$ from $1/5\rho_0$ to $1/10\rho_0$ shifts quantitative results by about 3%.
- Changing the coalescence parameter $P_0$ from 263 MeV/c to 258 MeV/c shifts results by not more than 0.3%.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin fractionation in heavy-ion collisions from sub-saturation densities (where the symmetry energy is constrained, e.g., by Shetty (2007), Piekarewicz (2008), and Li (2007)) to supra-saturation densities, where the symmetry potential sign is uncertain. The finding that the ordering of $(n/p)$ between light and heavy clusters reverses with the sign of the symmetry potential at high density contradicts the conventional expectation from lower-energy isospin fractionation (e.g., Baran (2005), Chomaz (2004), Li (2000)) where the gas phase is always more neutron-rich. The author notes that recent comparisons between IBUU calculations and FOPI data favor a soft symmetry energy ($x=1$) at supra-saturation densities (Xiao (2009)), and this work provides a specific experimental test to confirm or rule out a negative symmetry potential at those densities.
