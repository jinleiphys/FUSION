# Large Amplitude motion with a stochastic mean-field approach

> In the stochastic mean-field approach, an ensemble of initial conditions is considered to incorporate correlations beyond the mean-field. Then each starting pont is propagated separately using the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock equation of motion. This approach provides a rather simple tool to better describe fluctuations compared to the standard TDHF. Several illustrations are presented showing that this theory can be rather effective to treat the dynamics close to a quantum phase transition. Applications to fusion and transfer reactions demonstrate the great improvement in the description of mass dispersion.

# Large Amplitude motion with a stochastic mean-field approach

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, which replaces a single TDHF trajectory with an ensemble of mean-field evolutions from randomly sampled initial conditions, can describe fluctuations and spontaneous symmetry breaking near quantum phase transitions, but only up to a time that depends on the interaction strength.

## Method

The authors start from an initial one-body density matrix in canonical form and generate an ensemble of initial densities by adding random Gaussian fluctuations to the occupation numbers, with variances given by the quantal fluctuation formula. Each initial density is then propagated independently using the standard Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation of motion, keeping the density matrix components constant along each trajectory. For the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, the SMF approach is formulated directly in the collective space of quasi-spin operators, where initial conditions for the spin components are sampled according to Gaussian probabilities with prescribed first and second moments.

## Key numbers

- For the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model with N=40 particles, the critical interaction strength for symmetry breaking is χ > 1, where χ = V(N-1)/ε.
- The initial sampling variances for the quasi-spin components in the Lipkin model are given by: σ²(j_x) = σ²(j_y) = 1/(4N) = 1/160.
- The ⁴⁰Ca + ⁹⁰Zr reaction is studied at center-of-mass energy E_cm = 97 MeV for central collisions.
- Mass dispersion variances are computed as a function of time using σ²_AA(t) ≈ 2 ∫₀ᵗ D_AA(s) ds, where D_AA is the diffusion coefficient for mass exchange.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach previously developed by Lacroix (2004) and Ayik (2008) to describe transport properties in fusion reactions and dynamics near quantum phase transitions. The SMF method addresses the known limitations of standard TDHF, which severely underestimates fluctuations of one-body observables and cannot describe spontaneous symmetry breaking (Blaizot 1986, Ring 1980). The approach enables extraction of transport coefficients for dissipation and fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions around the Coulomb barrier, as demonstrated in applications to ⁴⁰Ca + ⁹⁰Zr reactions by Ayik (2009), Washiyama (2009), and Yilmaz (2011).
