# Neutron scattering from 208Pb at 30.4 and 40.0 MeV and isospin dependence of the nucleon optical potential

> Analysis of data involving nuclei far from stability often requires optical potential (OP) for neutron scattering. Since neutron data is seldom available, while proton scattering data is more abundant, it is useful to have estimates of the difference of the neutron and proton optical potentials. This information is contained in the isospin dependence of the nucleon OP. Here we attempt to provide it for the nucleon-208Pb system. The goal of this paper is to obtain accurate n+208Pb scattering data, and use it, together with existing p+208Pb and 208Pb(p,n)208$Bi*_{IAS} data, to obtain an accurate estimate of the isospin dependence of the nucleon OP at energies in the 30-60 MeV range. Cross sections for n+208Pb scattering were measured at 30.4 and 40.0 MeV, with a typical relative (normalization) accuracy of 2-4% (3%). An angular range of 15 to 130 degrees was covered using the beam-swinger time of flight system at Michigan State University. These data were analyzed by a consistent optical model study of the neutron data and of elastic p+208Pb scattering at 45 MeV and 54 MeV. These results were combined with a coupled-channel analysis of the 208Pb(p,n) reaction at 45 MeV, exciting the 0^+ isobaric analog state in 208Bi. The new data and analysis give an accurate estimate the isospin impurity of the nucleon-208Pb OP at 30.4 MeV, caused by the Coulomb correction to the proton OP. The corrections to the real proton OP given by the CH89 global systematics was found to be only few percent, while for the imaginary potential it was over 20% at the nuclear surface. Based on the analysis of the measured elastic p+208Pb data at 40 MeV, a Coulomb correction of similar strength and shape was also predicted for the p+208Pb OP at energy around 54 MeV.

# Neutron scattering from 208Pb at 30.4 and 40.0 MeV and isospin dependence of the nucleon optical potential

## Key claim

The Lane formalism for the isospin dependence of the nucleon optical potential is accurate to within about 4-5% for the real central potential, but the isospin impurity becomes much larger (above 20%) for the imaginary part of the optical potential at the nuclear surface, based on a consistent analysis of neutron and proton scattering on 208Pb combined with charge exchange data.

## Method

Cross sections for elastic n+208Pb scattering were measured at 30.4 and 40.0 MeV using the beam-swinger time-of-flight system at Michigan State University, covering an angular range of 15 to 130 degrees. The data were analyzed by a consistent optical model study of the neutron data together with elastic p+208Pb scattering at 45 and 54 MeV, combined with a coupled-channel analysis of the 208Pb(p,n) reaction at 45 MeV exciting the 0+ isobaric analog state in 208Bi. The CH89 global parametrization was used as the starting point, with the Coulomb correction to the proton incident energy determined by matching diffraction patterns of neutron and proton angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron scattering cross sections measured at 30.4 and 40.0 MeV with typical relative accuracy of 2-4% and normalization accuracy of 3%
- Coulomb correction to proton incident energy determined as Delta E_C = 14.6 MeV for 45 MeV protons and Delta E_C = 14.2 MeV for 54 MeV protons, compared to the CH89 value of 19 MeV
- Ratio of Coulomb correction to real potential strength: Delta V_v/V_v = 4.5%
- Ratio of Coulomb correction to volume imaginary potential strength: Delta W_v/W_v = 20.3%
- Ratio of Coulomb correction to surface imaginary potential strength: Delta W_s/W_s = 20.6%
- Isovector potential strengths from CH89 at 45 MeV: epsilon V_1 = 2.77 +/- 0.17 MeV, epsilon W_1 = 2.05 +/- 0.13 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the isospin dependence of the nucleon optical potential by Becchetti and Greenlees (1969), Varner et al. (1991, CH89), and Koning and Delaroche (2003), which relied mainly on comparisons across different nuclei or simple treatments of Coulomb corrections. Prior to this measurement, detailed elastic n+208Pb data were only available at energies up to 26 MeV, which did not overlap with precise proton data. The results confirm the trend found in a global dispersive optical model analysis by Mueller et al. (2011), which showed different asymmetry dependences between protons and neutrons for the surface imaginary potential. The accurate neutron data and consistent analysis enable improved predictions of the nucleon optical potential for unstable nuclei near the dripline, where large isospins make accurate knowledge of the isospin dependence critical.
