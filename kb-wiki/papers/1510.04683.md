# Magnetic response of baryon properties in a skyrmion model

> An axially symmetric ansatz is proposed to investigate the properties of baryon in a uniform magnetic field. The baryon number is shown to be conserved, while the baryon shape is stretched along the magnetic field. It is found that with increasing magnetic field strength, the static mass of the baryon first decreases and then increases, while the size of the baryon first increases and then decreases. Finally, in the core part of the magnetar, the equation of state strongly depends on the magnetic field, which modifies the mass limit of the magnetar.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an axially symmetric skyrmion ansatz to study baryon properties in a uniform magnetic field, finding that the baryon mass first decreases and then increases with field strength, while the baryon shape is stretched along the magnetic field direction; the baryon number is always conserved, contrary to an earlier claim by Eto (2011).

## Method

The author constructs a skyrmion model with even and odd intrinsic parity terms, including the Wess-Zumino-Witten action, and introduces an axially symmetric ansatz with deformation parameters c_rho and c_z that break the SO(3) spatial symmetry to SO(2) in a magnetic field. The soliton mass and root-mean-square radii for baryon number density and energy density are computed numerically as functions of the magnetic field strength |eB|, using standard Skyrme parameters (m_pi = 138 MeV, f_pi = 108 MeV, g = 4.84). The parameter c_z is varied to minimize the soliton mass for each magnetic field value.

## Key numbers

- The baryon number is always normalized to exactly 1, independent of magnetic field strength.
- The transition from spherical to axially symmetric shape occurs at |eB| = 0.027 GeV^2.
- At |eB| ~ 11 m_pi^2, the baryon mass reaches a minimum, decreasing by about 20% compared to the vacuum mass.
- At |eB| ~ 3 m_pi^2, the RMS radius <R^2>_B^{1/2} has a maximum, increasing by about 43% over the vacuum value.
- At |eB| ~ 2 m_pi^2, the RMS radius <R^2>_E^{1/2} has a maximum, increasing by about 10% over the vacuum value.
- At |eB| ~ 2 m_pi^2, the ratio <R_z^2>_B^{1/2} / <R_x^2>_B^{1/2} is about 1.9.
- For magnetar core fields (|eB| ~ 10^-2 GeV^2), the baryon density decreases by about 28% and the energy density decreases by about 14% compared to vacuum.

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion approach of Skyrme (1962) and Adkins (1983) to include magnetic field effects, correcting an earlier incorrect result by Eto (2011) regarding baryon number non-conservation. The findings enable improved modeling of magnetar equations of state beyond the mean-field approximation, with implications for predicting magnetar mass limits; the author suggests that skyrmion crystal or BPS skyrmion approaches could provide more precise predictions for magnetar EOS.
