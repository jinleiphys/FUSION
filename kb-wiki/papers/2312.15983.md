# Effects of center-of-mass correction and nucleon anomalous magnetic moments on nuclear charge radii

> Effects of the center-of-mass correction together with the nucleon electromagnetic form factors on the nuclear charge radius are systematically studied with a relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. Both one- and two-body parts of the CM correction are taken into account. It is found that the one- and two-body CM corrections, and the spin-orbit effect originating from the nucleon anomalous magnetic moments are all of the same order in magnitude, and that they give sizable impacts on the charge radius from light to heavy nuclei.

# Effects of center-of-mass correction and nucleon anomalous magnetic moments on nuclear charge radii

## Key claim

The one-body and two-body center-of-mass corrections and the spin-orbit effect from nucleon anomalous magnetic moments are all of the same order of magnitude and give sizable impacts on nuclear charge radii from light to heavy nuclei, but the agreement with experimental data after including all corrections is mixed: it improves for He, C, and Pb isotopes while deteriorating for Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn isotopes relative to the uncorrected DDME2 functional.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DDME2 parameter set for the particle-hole channel and Gogny D1S interaction for the particle-particle channel, imposing spherical symmetry and solving in radial coordinate space. They compute the center-of-mass correction to the mean-squared proton radius using both one-body and two-body operators, and include the spin-orbit contribution to the charge density arising from the nucleon anomalous magnetic moments (kappa_p = 1.793, kappa_n = -1.913). The finite-size effects of the nucleon are added as constant shifts using experimental proton and neutron charge radii.

## Key numbers

- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_p = 1.793; neutron anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_n = -1.913
- Experimental proton charge radius: (0.841 fm)^2; experimental neutron charge radius: -0.116 fm^2
- Finite-size constant term in the charge radius formula: 0.588 + 0.011*(N/Z) fm^2
- For He isotopes, the CM1 correction values: -0.999 fm^2 for ^4He, -0.237 fm^2 for ^6He, +0.0688 fm^2 for ^8He
- The two-body to one-body CM correction ratio in a harmonic-oscillator approximation: Delta_p^(CM2)/Delta_p^(CM1) = -N_bar/(N_bar+2), where N_bar is the harmonic-oscillator quantum number of the highest-occupied major shell
- Nonrelativistic approximation estimates for the spin-orbit contribution: -0.0422n fm^2 for ^(4+n)He, -0.0211n fm^2 for ^(16+n)O, -0.0127n fm^2 for ^40Ca (underestimates RHB results by a factor of approximately 2)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of center-of-mass corrections from binding energy (recently treated by Colò (2023) with focus on the two-body operator part) to charge radii, and combines them with the spin-orbit contribution from anomalous magnetic moments that has been discussed since Bertozzi (1972) and more recently by Ong (2010), Kurasawa and Suzuki (2000, 2019), and Naito et al. (2023). The approach connects to the approximate projection method of Schmid and Reinhard (1991) via a harmonic-oscillator model, showing equivalence for heavy nuclei but not for light or weakly-bound systems. The results enable systematic assessment of whether existing energy density functionals fitted without these corrections (such as DDME2, which was fitted to charge radii using r_ch = sqrt(<r^2>_p + (0.8 fm)^2) with BCS calculations) remain adequate when these sizable corrections are included.
