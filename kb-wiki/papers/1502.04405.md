# Effective theory for the non-rigid rotor in an electromagnetic field: Toward accurate and precise calculations of E2 transitions in deformed nuclei

> We present a model-independent approach to electric quadrupole transitions of deformed nuclei. Based on an effective theory for axially symmetric systems, the leading interactions with electromagnetic fields enter as minimal couplings to gauge potentials, while subleading corrections employ gauge-invariant non-minimal couplings. This approach yields transition operators that are consistent with the Hamiltonian, and the power counting of the effective theory provides us with theoretical uncertainty estimates. We successfully test the effective theory in homonuclear molecules that exhibit a large separation of scales. For ground-state band transitions of rotational nuclei, the effective theory describes data well within theoretical uncertainties at leading order. In order to probe the theory at subleading order, data with higher precision would be valuable. For transitional nuclei, next-to-leading order calculations and the high-precision data are consistent within the theoretical uncertainty estimates. We also study the faint inter-band transitions within the effective theory and focus on the $E2$ transitions from the $0^+_2$ band (the "$β$ band") to the ground-state band. Here, the predictions from the effective theory are consistent with data for several nuclei, thereby proposing a solution to a long-standing challenge.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent effective field theory (EFT) for electric quadrupole (E2) transitions in deformed nuclei, claiming that the approach describes both strong intra-band and faint inter-band transitions within theoretical uncertainty estimates, but notes that data with higher precision would be needed to probe the theory at subleading order.

## Method

The authors construct an effective theory for axially symmetric deformed nuclei based on emergent symmetry breaking from SO(3) to SO(2), treating the orientation of the symmetry axis as the low-energy degree of freedom. They couple the non-rigid rotor to electromagnetic fields through minimal coupling of gauge potentials at leading order and gauge-invariant non-minimal couplings at subleading order, deriving transition operators that are consistent with the Hamiltonian. The power counting provides theoretical uncertainty estimates, and the approach is tested on homonuclear molecules (N2, H2) before application to rotational and transitional nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach for deformed nuclei developed by Papenbrock (2011), Zhang (2013), and Papenbrock (2014) to include electromagnetic couplings, addressing a long-standing challenge in collective models where inter-band E2 transitions from the 0⁺₂ band (the "β band") to the ground-state band are overpredicted by factors of 2 to 10 (Garrett 2001, Aprahamian 2004, Rowe 2010). The approach differs from the Bohr Hamiltonian and interacting boson model by providing consistent treatment of Hamiltonians and currents with theoretical uncertainty estimates, and it enables systematic improvement through the power counting while avoiding parameter proliferation.
