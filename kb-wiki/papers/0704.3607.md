# Low density expansion and isospin dependence of nuclear energy functional: comparison between relativistic and Skyrme models

> In the present work we take the non relativistic limit of relativistic models and compare the obtained functionals with the usual Skyrme parametrization. Relativistic models with both constant couplings and with density dependent couplings are considered. While some models present very good results already at the lowest order in the density, models with non-linear terms only reproduce the energy functional if higher order terms are taken into account in the expansion.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the non-relativistic limit of relativistic nuclear mean-field models can be mapped onto a Skyrme-like energy functional at low densities, but the quality of the mapping depends on the specific relativistic model: models with constant couplings (QHD-II) reproduce the Skyrme functional already at lowest order in density, while models with non-linear sigma and omega terms require higher-order terms in the density expansion to match the Skyrme form.

## Method

The authors take the non-relativistic limit of several relativistic mean-field models (QHD-II, NL3, TM1, NLδ, TW, DDHδ) by expanding the single-particle energy ε_i^* ≈ M_i^* + p^2/(2M_i^*) and the scalar density ρ_{s_i} ≈ ρ_i − τ_i/(2M_i^{*2}), then express the relativistic energy functional in terms of the same local densities (ρ, ρ_3, τ, τ_3) used in the Skyrme model. They compare the resulting approximate binding energy, isoscalar interaction term B₁, isovector interaction term B₃, and effective masses between the relativistic models and four Skyrme parametrizations (SIII, SLy230a, NRAPR, LNS) at subsaturation densities.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear models (Bao-li, 1997; Chomaz, 2004; Ring, 1997) by directly comparing the energy functionals at the level of their density dependence rather than just saturation properties. The analysis shows that the isovector channel, particularly the symmetry energy slope and the proton-neutron effective mass splitting, exhibits the largest discrepancies between model classes, with some Skyrme forces (SLy230a) predicting M_n^* < M_p^* in neutron-rich matter while others (LNS, NRAPR) predict the opposite. The results enable future work to constrain the isospin dependence of the nuclear energy functional using experimental data from radioactive beam facilities, as discussed in Baran (2005) and the correlation between neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy slope found by Bao-an (2007) and Skin07.
