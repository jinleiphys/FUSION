# Jet quenching test of the QCD matter created at RHIC and the LHC needs opacity-resummed medium induced radiation

> After almost two decades of investigation, jet quenching has become a fundamental tool to study the properties of the QCD matter produced in high-energy nuclear collisions. Despite the large progress in both experimental and theoretical tools, several unknowns remain to be solved. In particular the systematics of the QCD matter parameters extracted at different energies and centralities is still puzzling. On the other hand, a lot of attention has been put in the last few years to a correct resummation of the multiple scatterings that the particles of the jet suffer when traversing the QCD matter. Present formalisms have substantially improved the multiple-soft or single hard scattering approximations, common in the last twenty years. We show here that medium parameters extracted with full resummed medium-induced gluon radiation spectra significantly differ from these two extreme approximations, providing a natural systematics for different collision energies and centralities, with no inconsistencies. For the case of the full resummation this ensures that the density of scattering centers as a function of the temperature qualitatively agree with the equation of state computed in lattice QCD.

## Key claim
The authors show that using a full opacity-resummed (all-order) medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum, rather than the common single-hard (GLV) or multiple-soft (BDMPS) approximations, resolves the long-standing systematic discrepancy of a factor K ≈ 1.3–2 in extracted QGP opacity between RHIC and LHC energies, and yields a number density whose temperature dependence qualitatively agrees with lattice QCD results.

## Method
The authors numerically implement an exact resummation of multiple scatterings in the opacity expansion for medium-induced gluon radiation, using a Debye-screened potential and a Monte Carlo evaluation of the gluon emission amplitude. They fit the single free parameter, the initial number density ρ₀ of scattering centers, to the nuclear modification factor R_AA^h data for pions across multiple collision systems (CuCu, AuAu, PbPb, XeXe) at energies from 200 GeV to 5.44 TeV, using a simplified one-dimensional Bjorken expansion and HBT-measured QGP lifetimes to fix all other medium parameters.

## Key numbers
- For the most central PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the fit yields ρ(τ₀) ≈ 56 fm⁻³, with α_s ≈ 0.28, μ_d ≈ 1.1 GeV, τ₀ ≈ 0.6 fm/c, and τ_f ≈ 9.8 fm/c.
- For the most central AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the initial temperature is T₀ ≈ 362 MeV and the fitted density is ρ(τ₀) ≈ 21 fm⁻³.
- Using the single-hard (N=1) approximation, a deviation factor of K = 1.22 in ρ/T³ is found between the hottest RHIC plasma and the most central PbPb at 2.76 TeV, translating to K = 1.29 in the transport coefficient q̂.
- The QGP lifetime parameterization is τ_f = (0.87 ± 0.01) × (dN_ch/dη)^{1/3} fm/c, deviating less than 10% from RHIC and LHC freeze-out predictions at T_f ≈ 120 MeV.
- For the most central PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the initial temperature is fixed at T₀ ≈ 470 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the opacity-resummation formalism of Feal (2018) and addresses the "energy puzzle" identified by the JET Collaboration (2014), where a factor K ≈ 1.3–2 discrepancy in extracted opacity was found between RHIC and LHC energies. The authors show that the single-hard (GLV) approximation of Gyulassy (2000) and the multiple-soft (BDMPS) approximation of Baier (1997) both produce systematic deviations in the extracted density, while the full resummation yields a ρ/T³ scaling that qualitatively matches the lattice QCD equation of state from the Wuppertal collaboration (2016). This enables a consistent framework for extracting QGP color opacity from jet observables without energy-dependent inconsistencies.
