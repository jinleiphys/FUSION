# QCD bound states in motion

> I consider the frame dependence of QCD bound states in the presence of a confining, spatially constant gluon field energy density. The states are quantized at equal time in $A^0=0$ (temporal) gauge. I derive the frame dependence of the wave functions, and demonstrate the Lorentz covariance of the electromagnetic (transition) form factors for states of any spin. The wave functions of $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$ states with CM momentum $P \neq 0$ are considered in some detail, verifying their local normalizability and the expected frame dependence of the bound state energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that QCD bound states quantized at equal time in temporal gauge (A⁰=0) have the correct Lorentz covariance for their electromagnetic form factors, despite the frame dependence of the Hamiltonian, a property that has not been verified before for strongly bound states with a confining potential.

## Method

The author introduces the QCD scale through a homogeneous solution of Gauss' law in temporal gauge, which adds a spatially constant gluon field energy density characterized by a universal scale parameter λ. This yields an instantaneous confining potential that is linear for qq̅ states at zeroth order in αₛ. The bound state equation is solved for J^PC = 0⁻⁺ states with center-of-mass momentum P ≠ 0, and the frame dependence of the wave functions is derived from infinitesimal boosts combined with time translations generated by the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The confining potential is V(r) = λ² r, where λ is a universal scale parameter (no numerical value given in the text).
- The Cornell-type potential from the instantaneous interaction is V_qq̅(|x₁−x₂|) = λ²|x₁−x₂| − C_F αₛ/|x₁−x₂|, with C_F = 4/3 for QCD.
- The asymptotic behavior of the radial wave function F₁(r→∞) is F₁(r→∞) ≃ N r^(−1−i m²/V′) exp[i(M−V)²/4V′], where V′ = λ².
- The bound state mass M satisfies E = √(P² + M²) for the boosted state, as required by Poincaré covariance.

## Context

This work extends the bound state method developed in Hoyer (2021) for QCD in temporal gauge, which introduced a confining potential through a homogeneous solution of Gauss' law. It addresses the dynamic realization of boost covariance for equal-time bound states, complementing light-front quantization approaches (Brodsky 1997, Burkardt 1995) where boosts are kinematic but rotation symmetry is dynamical. The frame dependence of atomic form factors was previously studied in QED (Brodsky 1968, PhysRev.176.1523), and the present work provides the analogous non-trivial check for QCD bound states with confinement.
