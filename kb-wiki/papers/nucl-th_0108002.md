# The mean energy, strength and width of triple giant dipole resonances

> We investigate the mean energy, strength and width of the triple giant dipole resonance using sum rules.

## Key claim

The excitation operator for the triple giant dipole resonance (TGDR) must be modified from the naive choice \( D^{3} \) to \( O^{(3)} = D^{3} - D \frac{\langle 0 | D^{4} | 0 \rangle}{\langle 0 | D^{2} | 0 \rangle} \) to obtain physically meaningful sum-rule results; the naive choice yields unphysical strength and mean energy values.

## Method

The authors use sum-rule moments (zeroth, first, and second) of the strength distribution of appropriately defined excitation operators to derive model-independent expressions for the mean energy, strength, and width of multiple giant dipole resonances. They then apply the Tomonaga model (a schematic boson-expansion method) to evaluate these moments explicitly for the single, double, and triple giant dipole resonances, comparing the results from the modified TGDR operator with those from the naive \( D^{3} \) operator. Finally, they derive model-independent relations between mean energies and strengths under the assumption that charge-exchange components are absent in the nuclear interaction.

## Key numbers

- In the Tomonaga model, the total strengths satisfy \( S_{0}^{(2)} = 2 \, S_{0}^{(1)\,2} \) and \( S_{0}^{(3)} = 6 \, S_{0}^{(1)\,3} \).
- In the Tomonaga model, the mean energies satisfy the harmonic limit: \( \bar{\omega}_{2} = 2\bar{\omega}_{1} \) and \( \bar{\omega}_{3} = 3\bar{\omega}_{1} \).
- With the naive choice \( \tilde{O}^{(3)} = D^{3} \), the Tomonaga model gives \( \tilde{S}_{0}^{(3)} = 15 \, S_{0}^{(1)\,3} \) and \( \tilde{\omega}_{3} = \frac{9}{5} \bar{\omega}_{1} \), the latter being smaller than the double GDR energy.
- Neglecting quadratic terms and dispersion in \( H_{11} \), the widths satisfy \( \Gamma^{(2)} = \sqrt{2} \, \Gamma^{(1)} \) and \( \Gamma^{(3)} = \sqrt{3} \, \Gamma^{(1)} \); in general, \( \Gamma^{(2)} > \sqrt{2} \, \Gamma^{(1)} \) and \( \Gamma^{(3)} > \sqrt{3} \, \Gamma^{(1)} \).
- Under the no-charge-exchange assumption, the first moment for the GDR equals the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn value: \( S_{1}^{(1)} = \frac{\hbar^{2}}{2m} \frac{NZ}{A} \).

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach previously applied to the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) by Kuriyama (1996) and Bohr and Mottelson (1975) to the triple giant dipole resonance (TGDR). It corrects the naive operator choice that would otherwise yield unphysical results, and provides model-independent relations that can constrain experimental searches for the TGDR, which were underway at the time (Emling, 2000; Frascaria, 1999). The method is generalizable to multiple giant resonances of other multipolarities, such as quadrupole resonances.
