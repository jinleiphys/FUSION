# On higher order and anisotropic hydrodynamics for Bjorken and Gubser flows

> We study the evolution of hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic moments of the distribution function using anisotropic and third-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics for systems undergoing Bjorken and Gubser flows. The hydrodynamic results are compared with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation with a collision term in relaxation time approximation. While the evolution of the hydrodynamic moments of the distribution function (i.e. of the energy momentum tensor) can be described with high accuracy by both hydrodynamic approximation schemes, their description of the evolution of the entropy of the system is much less precise. We attribute this to large contributions from non-hydrodynamic modes coupling into the entropy evolution which are not well captured by the hydrodynamic approximations. The differences between the exact solution and the hydrodynamic approximations are larger for the third-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics than for anisotropic hydrodynamics, which effectively resums some of the dissipative effects from anisotropic expansion to all orders in the anisotropy, and are larger for Gubser flow than for Bjorken flow. Overall, anisotropic hydrodynamics provides the most precise macroscopic description for these highly anisotropically expanding systems.

## Key claim

While both third-order Chapman-Enskog and anisotropic hydrodynamics describe the evolution of the energy-momentum tensor with high accuracy for Bjorken and Gubser flows, their description of entropy evolution is much less precise, with anisotropic hydrodynamics providing the most precise macroscopic description overall, especially for Gubser flow where deviations from the exact solution are larger.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) exactly for systems with Bjorken and Gubser symmetries. They then compared these exact solutions to two approximate hydrodynamic schemes: third-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics, which expands the distribution function perturbatively in gradients, and anisotropic hydrodynamics using the \(P_L\) matching scheme, which non-perturbatively resums dissipative effects from anisotropic expansion. The comparison focused on the evolution of both hydrodynamic moments (energy-momentum tensor) and non-hydrodynamic moments (entropy) of the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- For Bjorken flow, the third-order Chapman-Enskog shear evolution equation has transport coefficients \(\lambda = 38/21\) and \(\chi = 72/245\) (dimensionless).
- For Gubser flow, the third-order Chapman-Enskog shear evolution equation has transport coefficients \(\hat{\lambda} = 46/21\) and \(\hat{\chi} = 72/245\) (dimensionless).
- The local scalar expansion rate for Gubser flow is \(\hat{\theta} \equiv 2\tanh\rho\).
- The relaxation time is given by \(\tau_r = 5\bar{\eta}/T \equiv c/T\), where \(\bar{\eta} \equiv \eta/s\) is the specific shear viscosity.

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of hydrodynamic approximations to exact RTA Boltzmann solutions for Bjorken flow (Florkowski 2013, Denicol 2014) and Gubser flow (Denicol 2014, Molnar 2016, Martinez 2017) by presenting for the first time the solution of third-order Chapman-Enskog evolution equations for Gubser flow and a detailed analysis of entropy evolution across all approximations. The finding that entropy production is a sensitive discriminator between hydrodynamic approximations, exhibiting larger deviations than hydrodynamic observables, enables future work to use entropy as a stringent test for hydrodynamic models and highlights the significant contribution of non-hydrodynamic modes to entropy production.
