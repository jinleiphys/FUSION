# Emergent properties of nuclei from ab initio coupled-cluster calculations

> Emergent properties such as nuclear saturation and deformation, and the effects on shell structure due to the proximity of the scattering continuum and particle decay channels are fascinating phenomena in atomic nuclei. In recent years, ab initio approaches to nuclei have taken the first steps towards tackling the computational challenge of describing these phenomena from Hamiltonians with microscopic degrees of freedom. This endeavor is now possible due to ideas from effective field theories, novel optimization strategies for nuclear interactions, ab initio methods exhibiting a soft scaling with mass number, and ever-increasing computational power. This paper reviews some of the recent accomplishments. We also present new results. The recently optimized chiral interaction NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ is shown to provide an accurate description of both charge radii and binding energies in selected light- and medium-mass nuclei up to $^{56}$Ni. We derive an efficient scheme for including continuum effects in coupled-cluster computations of nuclei based on chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, and present new results for unbound states in the neutron-rich isotopes of oxygen and calcium. The coupling to the continuum impacts the energies of the $J^π= {1/2}^-,{3/2}^-,{7/2}^-,{3/2}^+$ states in $^{17,23,25}$O, and - contrary to naive shell-model expectations - the level ordering of the $J^π= {3/2}^+,{5/2}^+,{9/2}^+$ states in $^{53,55,61}$Ca.

# Emergent properties of nuclei from ab initio coupled-cluster calculations

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ab initio coupled-cluster calculations using chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces can describe emergent nuclear phenomena such as saturation, deformation, and continuum effects, but the accuracy is limited to specific interactions (notably NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ for binding energies and radii up to $^{56}$Ni) and the continuum coupling effects are shown to be significant only for states with small centrifugal barriers, not universally applicable.

## Method

The authors employ coupled-cluster theory with a Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis built from a mixed representation of harmonic-oscillator and Berggren basis states, allowing bound, scattering, and resonant states to be treated on equal footing. For unbound states, they use the particle-attached equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (PA-EOM-CC) method with Berggren bases for selected partial waves, and for deformation studies they use the coupled-cluster effective-interaction (CCEI) method to derive shell-model interactions from chiral interactions. Three-nucleon forces are included by projecting density matrices onto a finite harmonic-oscillator space to avoid computing 3NF matrix elements directly in the Berggren basis.

## Key numbers

- $^{28}$O predicted unbound with respect to $^{24}$O by 2.5 MeV (NNLO$_{\rm sat}$) and 2.9 MeV (N$^3$LO + L(400))
- Inclusion of continuum for the $d_{3/2}$ partial wave adds about 1 MeV of additional binding compared to harmonic oscillator basis
- $^{25}$O ground-state particle unstable at neutron decay energy 770 keV with width 172(30) keV
- $^{26}$O observed at decay energy $150^{+50}_{-150}$ keV with width 5 keV
- $^{25}$O $J^\pi = {3/2}^+$ unbound state at 0.77 MeV (experiment), in good agreement with calculations
- $^{23}$O $J^\pi = {3/2}^+$ unbound state at 1.27 MeV (experiment), in good agreement with calculations
- In $^{25}$O, continuum coupling lowers $J^\pi = {1/2}^-,{3/2}^-$ states by about 2 MeV
- $^{48}$Ca overbound by about 4 MeV with charge radius underestimated by about 0.2 fm for the SRG-evolved interaction
- Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis converged with 30-40 quadrature points and maximum real momentum Re[$k$] = 4-5 fm$^{-1}$
- $^{28}$O predicted unbound by phenomenological shell-model with decay energy 1.44 MeV (Volya 2005)

## Context

This work extends ab initio coupled-cluster calculations to include continuum effects via Berggren bases, building on earlier Gamow-shell-model approaches (Michel 2002, Id Betan 2002) and prior coupled-cluster continuum studies (Hagen 2007, Hagen 2010). The results for oxygen isotopes contrast with shell-model calculations (Otsuka 2010) that explained the oxygen anomaly using 3NFs but without continuum coupling, and with phenomenological Gamow-shell-model predictions (Volya 2005) for $^{28}$O. For calcium isotopes, the work extends earlier coupled-cluster calculations (Hagen 2012) that used schematic density-dependent 3NFs, now employing full chiral 3NFs. The CCEI method for deformation builds on effective shell-model interactions from coupled-cluster theory (Jansen 2014, Bogner 2014) and enables ab initio computation of rotational bands in $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg, comparable to phenomenological USDB interactions (Brown 2006). Future work requires computing transition strengths and effective operators, and extending to heavier neutron-rich nuclei such as $^{79}$Ni where similar level inversions are expected.
