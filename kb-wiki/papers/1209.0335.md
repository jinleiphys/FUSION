# Medium information from anisotropic flow and jet quenching in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Within a multiphase transport (AMPT) model, where the initial conditions are obtained from the recently updated HIJING 2.0 model, the recent anisotropic flow and suppression data for charged hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC center of mass energy of 2.76 TeV are explored to constrain the properties of the partonic medium formed. In contrast to RHIC, the measured centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity dN_ch/deta at LHC provides severe constraint to the largely uncertain gluon shadowing parameter s_g. We find final-state parton scatterings reduce considerably hadron yield at midrapidity and enforces a smaller s_g to be consistent with dN_ch/deta data at LHC. With the parton shadowing so constrained, hadron production and flow over a wide transverse momenta range are investigated in AMPT. The model calculations for the elliptic and triangular flow are found to be in excellent agreement with the RHIC data, and predictions for the flow coefficients v_n(p_T, cent) at LHC are given. The magnitude and pattern of suppression of the hadrons in AMPT are found consistent with the measurements at RHIC. However, the suppression is distinctly overpredicted in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy. Reduction of the QCD coupling constant alpha_s by ~30% in the higher temperature plasma formed at LHC reproduces the measured hadron suppression.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with HIJING 2.0 initial conditions can simultaneously describe anisotropic flow and bulk hadron production at both RHIC and LHC energies, but the same QCD coupling constant that works at RHIC overpredicts jet quenching at LHC; a reduction of the strong coupling constant by approximately 30% in the higher-temperature LHC plasma is required to reproduce the measured hadron suppression.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT model with HIJING 2.0 initial conditions, including parton-parton elastic scatterings via the ZPC parton cascade and final hadron transport via ART. They constrained the gluon shadowing parameter s_g by fitting the measured centrality dependence of charged hadron pseudorapidity density at LHC, then computed anisotropic flow coefficients v_n and the nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadrons at both RHIC and LHC energies. For the LHC suppression calculations, they tested both the RHIC-tuned QCD coupling constant alpha_s = 0.33 and a reduced value alpha_s = 0.24 derived from the estimated initial temperature.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron pseudorapidity density at LHC: dN_ch/dη (|η|<0.5) = 1601 ± 60 for 0-5% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (ALICE data)
- HIJING 2.0 without final-state interactions predicts dN_ch/dη = 1775 ± 3 at LHC and 706 ± 5 at RHIC for central collisions
- Parton-parton elastic scattering cross section used: sigma ≈ 1.5 mb, corresponding to alpha_s = 0.33 and screening mass μ = 3.226 fm^{-1}
- Initial temperature estimates: T_i ≈ 320 MeV at RHIC and T_i ≈ 436 MeV at LHC for 0-5% central collisions at proper time τ_i = 1 fm/c
- Reduced coupling constant at LHC: alpha_s ≈ 0.24, corresponding to sigma ≈ 0.76 mb at the higher LHC temperature
- Gluon shadowing parameter constrained to s_g ≈ 0.17 at LHC and s_g = 0.10-0.17 at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model framework (Lin et al., 2005) by incorporating the updated HIJING 2.0 initial conditions (Deng and Wang, 2010) to simultaneously describe bulk hadron production, anisotropic flow, and jet quenching at both RHIC and LHC energies. The finding that the QCD coupling constant must be reduced at LHC temperatures is consistent with thermal suppression of alpha_s and contradicts the expectation that the denser LHC medium would cause stronger suppression; instead the LHC medium appears more transparent (Horowitz, 2011). The results enable future studies of temperature-dependent coupling in transport models and provide predictions for flow coefficients v_n(p_T, centrality) at LHC that can be tested against ALICE data.
