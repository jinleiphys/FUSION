# Van der Waals Excluded Volume Model for Lorentz Contracted Rigid Spheres

> Conventional cluster and virial expansions are generalized to momentum dependent inter-particle potentials. The model with Lorentz contracted hard core potentials is considered, e.g. as hadron gas model. A Van der Waals-type model with a temperature dependent excluded volume is derived. Lorentz contraction effects at given temperature are stronger for light particles and make their effective excluded volume smaller than that of heavy ones.

# Van der Waals Excluded Volume Model for Lorentz Contracted Rigid Spheres

## Key claim

The conventional Van der Waals excluded volume model for hadron gases can be generalized to include Lorentz contraction of moving hard-core particles, resulting in a temperature-dependent excluded volume that is always smaller than the static value; the effect is stronger for lighter particles (e.g., pions) than for heavier ones (e.g., nucleons) at the same temperature.

## Method

The authors generalize the standard cluster and virial expansions to momentum-dependent inter-particle potentials, then apply this formalism to a gas of classical Boltzmann particles with Lorentz-contracted hard-core potentials. The second virial coefficient \(a_2(T)\) is computed as a function of temperature by integrating over momentum-dependent minimal distances between two contracting spheres, and the resulting temperature-dependent excluded volume is inserted into the Van der Waals equation of state in the grand canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers

- At \(T \cong 150\) MeV, the excluded volume \(a_2\) for a nucleon gas (\(m \cong 939\) MeV) decreases by 10% relative to its nonrelativistic value \(4v_{\rm o}\).
- At the same temperature \(T \cong 150\) MeV, the excluded volume \(a_2\) for pions (\(m \cong 140\) MeV) shrinks by almost a factor of 2 relative to \(4v_{\rm o}\).
- The analytical approximation for the function \(f(T) = a_2(T)/(4v_{\rm o})\) is given by \(f(T) = c + (1-c) \rho_s(T)/\phi(T)\) with \(c = (1 + 74/(9\pi))^{-1}\), valid to within a few percent for all temperatures.
- The asymptotic behavior of \(f(T)\) is \(1 - O(T/m)\) for \(T \ll m\) and \(c + O(m/T)\) for \(T \gg m\).
- The pion gas density is calculated for \(r_{\rm o} = 0.5\) fm, \(\mu = 0\), \(g = 3\), comparing ideal gas, constant excluded volume, and Lorentz-contracted excluded volume models.

## Context

This work extends the standard Van der Waals excluded volume model previously used to describe hadron yields in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (Yen 1999, Stoecker 1999) by incorporating Lorentz contraction of moving hadrons, a problem discussed earlier by Kapusta (1983) and Zimanyi (1995). The authors criticize the approach of Zimanyi (1995) for confusing the excluded volume parameter with the proper volume of an individual particle. The derived temperature-dependent excluded volume provides a natural explanation for the smaller effective pion hard-core radius introduced phenomenologically by Rischke (1997) and Yen (1997) to fit hadron yield data, and enables more accurate modeling of hadron gas thermodynamics at temperatures typical for high-energy nuclear collisions (120-170 MeV).
