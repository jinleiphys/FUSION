# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and Quark Deconfinement beyond Mean Field in a Magnetized PNJL Model

> We study chiral symmetry restoration and quark deconfinement beyond mean field approximation in a magnetized PNJL model. The feedback from mesons to quarks modifies the quark coupling constant and Polyakov potential. As a result, the separate critical temperatures for the two phase transitions at mean field level coincide and the magnetic catalysis becomes inverse magnetic catalysis, when the meson contribution is included.

## Key claim

When meson quantum fluctuations are included beyond mean field in a magnetized PNJL model, the separate critical temperatures for chiral symmetry restoration and quark deconfinement coincide, and the magnetic catalysis effect seen at mean field level becomes inverse magnetic catalysis, consistent with lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The author extends a two-flavor PNJL model in chiral limit by including meson degrees of freedom (neutral pions and sigma mesons) as quantum fluctuations above the mean field, constructed via random phase approximation. The meson thermodynamic contribution is expanded around the mean field solution and absorbed into an effective quark coupling constant \(G'(T,B)\) and an effective Polyakov potential \({\cal U}'\), which are then used to solve the coupled gap equations for the dynamical quark mass and Polyakov loop at finite temperature and magnetic field. A covariant Pauli-Villars regularization is employed to handle ultraviolet divergences while preserving causality.

## Key numbers

- In vacuum (\(T=B=0\)), the model parameters are fixed by fitting \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV and \(\langle \bar\psi\psi \rangle = (-250\ \text{MeV})^3\), yielding \(G = 7.79\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\Lambda = 1127\) MeV with \(N=3\) regulated masses.
- At mean field level with vanishing magnetic field, the chiral restoration and deconfinement critical temperatures are both around \(T_c \simeq 220\) MeV.
- Numerical calculations are performed for magnetic fields \(eB \ge 15 m_\pi^2\) to avoid significant charged pion contributions.
- The effective coupling constant satisfies \(G' < G\) at all temperatures and magnetic fields, and at fixed temperature \(G'\) decreases with increasing \(B\).
- Beyond mean field, the chiral critical temperature \(T_\chi\) decreases with increasing magnetic field (inverse magnetic catalysis), while the Polyakov loop shows its maximum change at \(T_\chi\), so the deconfinement temperature \(T_\Phi\) coincides with \(T_\chi\).

## Context

This work extends the method of including meson feedback in the NJL model (Mao and Zhuang, 2015) to the PNJL model, addressing the discrepancy between mean-field model predictions (magnetic catalysis) and lattice QCD results (inverse magnetic catalysis) reported by Bali et al. (2012) and others. The study shows that meson fluctuations remove the separation of chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures seen in mean-field PNJL calculations (Fukushima, 2008; Ratti et al., 2006), and reproduces the inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice simulations. This enables a unified description of both phase transitions in strong magnetic fields relevant for compact stars and heavy-ion collisions.
