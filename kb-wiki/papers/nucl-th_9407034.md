# Chiral perturbation in dense matter and meson condensation controversy

> An outstanding problem in the study of possible kaon condensation is the striking discrepancy between the results of chiral perturbation theory and those of the PCAC-plus-current-algebra approach. I discuss here what causes this discrepancy and what needs to be done to solve the problem. In addition, I point out the importance of examining the validity of the non-relativistic approximation universally employed in the existing treatments of kaon condensation.

# Chiral Perturbation in Dense Matter and Meson Condensation Controversy

## Key claim

The striking discrepancy between chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and the PCAC-plus-current-algebra approach to kaon condensation arises from the neglect of higher-order density terms (of order rho^2) in the effective Lagrangian, and from the universal but questionable use of the non-relativistic approximation rho_s ~ rho. Neither approach can be preferred without a systematic treatment of these effects.

## Method

The paper compares two effective Lagrangians for pion-nucleon interactions: L_1 (lowest-order ChPT) and L_2 (constructed to reproduce the PCAC-based amplitude). It computes the effective pion mass m_pi^* in nuclear matter using the mean-field approximation for each Lagrangian, showing that L_1 yields m_pi^*^2 = m_pi^2 - rho * sigma_piN / f^2 while L_2 yields a different expression that saturates at m_pi / sqrt(2) as rho -> infinity. The author then applies a field redefinition to L_2 and expands in density to show the two Lagrangians differ at O(rho^2). A Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation of a linear sigma model Hamiltonian is used to derive a corrected critical density that accounts for the distinction between scalar density rho_s and baryon density rho.

## Key numbers

- For the ChPT-based Lagrangian L_1, the critical density for pion condensation is given by rho_c = m_pi^2 f^2 / sigma_piN (equation eQd).
- For the PCAC-based approach, the effective pion mass m_pi^*(2) approaches m_pi / sqrt(2) as rho -> infinity, making condensation highly unlikely.
- Using the non-relativistic approximation rho_s ~ rho, the factor kappa = <E^*(k)/m_N^*> is ~1.02 at rho = rho_0 and ~1.05 at rho = 3 rho_0 for m_N^* = m_N.
- For s-wave pion condensation with sigma_piN ~ 50 MeV, the relativistic correction to rho_c is modest; for kaon condensation with sigma_KN ~ 500 MeV, the factor m_N / (m_N - eta) ~ 2 even in free space, giving a sizable effect.

## Context

This work addresses the unresolved discrepancy between ChPT predictions of kaon condensation at rho_c = 2-3 rho_0 (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986) and the PCAC-based results of Yabu et al. (1993) that found no condensation. The paper extends the earlier analysis of Campbell, Dashen and Manassah (1975) on relativistic effects in pion condensation, and critiques the heavy-fermion ChPT calculations of Brown, Lee, Rho and Thorsson (1994) and Lee, Jung, Min and Rho (1994) for neglecting multiple-fermion terms. It calls for a systematic in-medium chiral counting formalism that includes O(rho^2) terms and relativistic corrections, which would be necessary to resolve the kaon condensation controversy and has broader implications for dense nuclear matter theory.
