# Effective interaction dependence of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter

> The liquid-gas phase transition for homogeneous symmetric nuclear matter is studied in the mean-field approximation. Critical properties are computed using a comprehensive group of Skyrme and Gogny forces in an effort to elucidate the effective interaction dependence of the results. Analytical models for the thermodynamical and critical properties are discussed and compared to an extensive set of mean-field data. In agreement with these models, a tight correlation is found between the flashing and the critical points. Accurate predictions for the critical temperature, based on saturation properties, can only be obtained after the density dependence of the effective mass is properly taken into account. While the thermodynamical properties coming from different mean-fields do not follow a law of corresponding states, the critical exponents for all the mean-fields have been found to be the same. Their values coincide with those predicted by the Landau mean-field theory of critical phenomena.

## Key claim

The liquid-gas phase transition properties in symmetric nuclear matter are strongly dependent on the effective interaction used in mean-field calculations, but a tight correlation exists between the flashing and critical points across different forces; accurate predictions for the critical temperature based on saturation properties require proper inclusion of the density dependence of the effective mass.

## Method

The liquid-gas phase transition for homogeneous symmetric nuclear matter was studied using the finite-temperature self-consistent Hartree-Fock approximation in the mean-field framework. Critical properties were computed for 39 Skyrme force parameterizations and 8 Gogny force parameterizations by solving the coupled equations for the momentum distribution, mean-field, and chemical potential, then determining the critical point from the conditions where the first and second derivatives of pressure with respect to density vanish. The flashing point was found from the simultaneous conditions of zero pressure and zero pressure derivative.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperatures for the four representative interactions span a range from 14 to 23 MeV, with ZR1 and SLy9 giving the highest and lowest values respectively
- The degeneracy parameter at the liquid-gas phase transition is approximately d ~ 1/3, corresponding to T ~ 15 MeV and density ~ 0.08 fm^-3
- For the Jaqaman model, the critical compressibility factor gamma_c = p_c/(T_c rho_c) lies between 1/4 and 1/3 for Skyrme forces with alpha = 0-1
- The ratio of critical to flashing temperature in the Jaqaman model is T_c/T_f ~ 1.3, with rho_c/rho_f between 1/sqrt(e) and 2/3

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of the liquid-gas phase transition by providing a systematic comparison across a comprehensive set of Skyrme and Gogny forces, building on the saturation property analyses of Stone (2003) and the finite-temperature mean-field formalism of Bonche et al. (1984). The results enable future work connecting critical properties to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and astrophysical applications, particularly the onset of instabilities in neutron star crusts as studied by Margueron et al. (2004) and Ducoin et al. (2006). The finding that critical exponents are universal and match Landau mean-field theory predictions provides a benchmark for beyond-mean-field approaches such as those of Baldo et al. (2004) and Rios et al. (2008).
