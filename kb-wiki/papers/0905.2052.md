# Integral relations for three-body continuum states with the adiabatic expansion

> Application of the Hyperspherical Adiabatic expansion to describe three-body scattering states suffers the problem of a very slow convergence. Contrary to what happens for bound states, a huge number of hyperradial equations has to be solved, and even if done, the extraction of the scattering amplitude is problematic. In this paper we show how to obtain accurate scattering phase shifts using the Hyperspherical Adiabatic expansion. To this aim two integral relations, derived from the Kohn Variational Principle, are used. The convergence of this procedure is as fast as for bound states.

# Integral relations for three-body continuum states with the adiabatic expansion

## Key claim
The Hyperspherical Adiabatic (HA) expansion method can describe three-body elastic scattering with convergence as fast as for bound states, but only when two integral relations derived from the Kohn Variational Principle are used to extract the phase shift; without these relations, the direct extraction of phase shifts from the asymptotic form of the HA wave function converges extremely slowly even with many adiabatic channels.

## Method
The authors solve the coupled hyperradial equations of the HA expansion for three identical spinless bosons and for nucleon-nucleon systems, obtaining the wave function in the interaction region. They then apply two integral relations derived from the Kohn Variational Principle, one for the coefficient A and one for the coefficient B, whose ratio gives a second-order estimate of the scattering phase shift. These integrals involve the wave function multiplied by the potential and the asymptotic functions F_ST and a regularized G_ST, and converge rapidly because the integrands vanish outside the interaction region.

## Key numbers
- For a three-boson system with a Gaussian potential (V0 = -51.5 MeV, r0 = 1.6 fm), the ground state binding energy converges to -9.7574 MeV with 10 HA basis states, and the excited state converges to -0.4816 MeV with 10 HA states.
- At E = -0.1 MeV, the phase shift from the integral relations converges to 73.179 degrees with 20 HA basis states, while the direct asymptotic extraction gives only 72.72 degrees with 40 states and extrapolates to 72.8-72.9 degrees.
- The converged phase shift from the integral relations (73.179 degrees) agrees with the HH expansion result of 73.180 degrees using 120 basis elements.
- For the MT-III potential in the S=3/2, T=0 state, the phase shift at 0.2 MeV converges to -28.294 degrees with 16 HA states, at 1.0 MeV to -55.863 degrees, and at 2.0 MeV to -71.473 degrees, all matching HH results.

## Context
This work addresses the slow convergence problem of the HA expansion for scattering states identified in Barletta (2009), where direct extraction of phase shifts from the asymptotic form requires many channels. The method extends the Kohn Variational Principle approach used in two-body scattering by Holt (1972) to the three-body case. The integral relations are general and can be applied to wave functions obtained from other methods, such as the HH expansion used by Kievsky (2008) or Quantum Monte Carlo calculations like those of Nollett for n-alpha scattering. This enables accurate scattering calculations for systems with hard-core interactions (e.g., ultracold atoms as studied by Esry and Nielsen) and for A>4 nuclear systems where the HA method is otherwise difficult to apply.
