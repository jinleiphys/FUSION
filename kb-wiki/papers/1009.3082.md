# Neutrino reactions via neutral and charged current by Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation(QRPA)

> We developed the quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) for the neutrino scattering off even-even nuclei via neutral current (NC) and charged cur- rent (CC). The QRPA has been successfully applied for the βand ββdecay of relevant nuclei. To describe neutrino scattering, general multipole transitions by weak interactions with a finite momentum transfer are calculated for NC and CC reaction with detailed formalism. Since we consider neutron-proton (np) pairing as well as neutron-neutron (nn) and proton-proton (pp) pairing correlations, the nn + pp QRPA and np QRPA are combined in a framework, which enables to describe both NC and CC reactions in a consistent way. Numerical results for ν-^{12}C, -^{56}Fe and -^{56}Ni reactions are shown to comply with other theoretical calculations and reproduce well available experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors develop a quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) framework that combines neutron-neutron (nn) plus proton-proton (pp) QRPA with neutron-proton (np) QRPA to describe both neutral current (NC) and charged current (CC) neutrino-nucleus reactions consistently; the numerical results for nu-12C, nu-56Fe, and nu-56Ni are shown to comply with other theoretical calculations and reproduce available experimental data, though the paper notes that previous RPA and QRPA calculations typically overestimated data by a factor of 4 to 5.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian with a one-boson-exchange potential (Bonn group) and perform a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) transformation to quasi-particles, including np pairing as well as nn and pp pairing correlations. They then solve a QRPA equation that couples all three pairing channels to generate excited states of the compound nucleus, and compute multipole transition operators for weak interactions (Coulomb, longitudinal, electric, and magnetic) with finite momentum transfer. Cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering via NC and CC are calculated using the standard weak interaction formalism, with Coulomb distortion of outgoing leptons treated by both the Fermi function and the effective momentum approach (EMA).

## Key numbers

- For the inclusive reaction 12C(nu_e, e-)12N*, experimental data range from 3.3 to 6.7 in units of 10^-42 cm^2 (including error bars).
- For the exclusive reaction 12C(nu_e, e-)12N_g.s., experimental data range from 6.07 to 10.4 in units of 10^-42 cm^2 for the DAR neutrino.
- For the exclusive reaction 12C(nu, nu')12C (15.11 MeV), experimental data range from 8.5 to 12.3 in the same unit for the DAR neutrino.
- The mono-energetic nu_mu from pion decay at rest is fixed at 29.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QRPA approach previously applied by the authors to beta and double-beta decay (Cheoun 1993) to neutrino-nucleus scattering, addressing the need for consistent treatment of both NC and CC reactions. It builds on earlier shell model calculations (Suzuki 2006, Volpe 2000) and RPA/QRPA calculations (Volpe 2000, Ring 2008), which typically overestimated data by a factor of 4-5, as well as continuum RPA calculations (Kolbe 1995, Kolbe 2003, Ryck 2002) that include quasi-elastic contributions. The framework enables future network calculations for nuclear abundances in supernova neutrino processes and can be applied to the weak rapid process (Wana 2006).
