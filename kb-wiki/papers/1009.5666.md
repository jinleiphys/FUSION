# Short Range Correlations and the EMC Effect

> This paper shows quantitatively that the magnitude of the EMC effect measured in electron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at intermediate $x_B$, $0.35\le x_B\le 0.7$, is linearly related to the Short Range Correlation (SRC) scaling factor obtained from electron inclusive scattering at $x_B\ge 1.$. The observed phenomenological relationship is used to extract the ratio of the deuteron to the free $pn$ pair cross sections, the DIS cross section for a free neutron, and $F_2^n/F_2^p$, the ratio of the free neutron to free proton structure functions. We speculate that the observed correlation is because both the EMC effect and SRC are dominated by the high virtuality (high momentum) nucleons in the nucleus.

# Short Range Correlations and the EMC Effect

## Key claim
The magnitude of the EMC effect in electron deep inelastic scattering at intermediate Bjorken x (0.35 ≤ x_B ≤ 0.7) is linearly related to the Short Range Correlation (SRC) scale factor obtained from inclusive electron scattering at x_B ≥ 1. The authors speculate that this correlation arises because both phenomena are dominated by high-virtuality (high-momentum) nucleons in the nucleus, but they do not claim a causal mechanism.

## Method
The authors compared published EMC slopes dR_EMC/dx (the slope of the per-nucleon DIS cross section ratio of nucleus A to deuterium over 0.35 ≤ x_B ≤ 0.7) with SRC scale factors a_2(A/d) derived from inclusive (e,e') cross section ratios at x_B ≥ 1.5 for nuclei from deuterium through iron. They performed a linear fit of the EMC slopes versus the SRC scale factors, then used the resulting linear relationship to extract the deuteron in-medium correction (IMC) and the free neutron structure function F_2^n.

## Key numbers
- The linear relationship between EMC slope and SRC scale factor is: -dR_EMC/dx = (a_2(A/d) - 1) × (0.079 ± 0.006)
- The deuteron IMC slope (the EMC slope extrapolated to a_2 = 0) is |dR_IMC(d)/dx| = 0.079 ± 0.006
- The deuteron to free pn pair cross section ratio decreases linearly from 1 to 0.97 over 0.3 ≤ x_B ≤ 0.7, a depletion of 0.031 ± 0.004
- The SRC scale factor for deuterium relative to ^3He is 0.508 ± 0.025; for ^4He it is 1.93 ± 0.14; for ^12C it is 2.41 ± 0.17; for ^56Fe it is 2.83 ± 0.18
- The combined EMC slopes (weighted average of Seely (2009) and Gomez (1994) data) range from -0.070 ± 0.029 for ^3He to -0.496 ± 0.051 for ^108Ag

## Context
This work extends the observation by Higinbotham et al. (2010) of a possible correlation between the EMC effect and SRC, providing a quantitative linear relationship. It builds on high-precision EMC data from Seely (2009) that suggested the EMC effect is a local density effect, and on SRC scaling measurements from Egiyan (2003, 2006) and Fomin (2007) at Jefferson Laboratory. The extracted free neutron structure function F_2^n/F_2^p disfavors models predicting d/u → 0 at x_B = 1 (e.g., Melnitchouk (1996) and references therein) and enables model-independent extraction of parton distribution functions important for collider and neutrino oscillation experiments.
