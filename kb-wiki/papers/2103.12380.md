# Estimation of initial state structures in high energy heavy-ion collisions using Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

> In high-energy heavy-ion collisions, structures in the initial collision zone are a matter of intense investigation, both from theory and experimental points of view. A large number of models have been developed to represent the initial state of the collision including Glauber model, Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) among others. Another important aspect of the study is to investigate proper observables that will be sensitive to the initial collision zone. In this work, we have discussed a formalism to implement the spatial clusters at the partonic level in the string melting version of the AMPT model for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. These clusters are then propagated through the AMPT hadronization scheme. The Principal Component Analysis (PCA) has been used on the $η$, $ϕ$ and $p_T$ distributions of the produced charged particles and the eigenvalues have been compared before and after the implementation of the clustering. It is found that for all these three different distributions, all the prominent PCA modes have shown sensitivity to the clustering. A centrality dependent study has also been performed on those eigenvalues.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the first few eigenvalues from Principal Component Analysis (PCA) of the $\eta$, $\phi$, and $p_T$ distributions of produced charged particles are sensitive to the presence of partonic-level spatial clusters in the initial state of PbPb collisions, even though the inclusive distributions of the produced particles show no visible structures from these clusters.

## Method

The authors implemented spatial clustering of partons in the string-melting version of the AMPT model for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. Partons were grouped into clusters by bringing them closer in position space according to a cluster radius parameter R and a distance scaling factor df, and thermal momenta were assigned according to an exponential distribution with temperature parameter T. The clustered partons were then propagated through the AMPT hadronization scheme via coalescence, and PCA was applied to the $\eta$, $\phi$, and $p_T$ distributions of the final charged particles to obtain eigenvalues, which were compared before and after clustering.

## Key numbers

- PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, with up to 0.2 million minimum bias events generated using AMPT string-melting version.
- Cluster radius parameters used: R = 2 fm and R = 5 fm, with distance scaling factors df = 0.05 and df = 0.1; maximum cluster radii after scaling were 0.5 fm for R=5 fm, df=0.1.
- Temperature parameters for thermal parton momentum assignment: T = 200 MeV/c and T = 400 MeV/c.
- PCA performed with 20 bins for $\eta$ (range -1 to +1), 20 bins for $p_T$ (range 0 to 5 GeV/c), and 50 bins for $\phi$ (range -$\pi$ to +$\pi$).
- For T = 200 MeV, all eigenvalues for $\eta$ and $p_T$ distributions lie above the no-clustering reference; for position clustering alone, eigenvalues lie below the reference.

## Context

This work extends studies of initial state geometry in heavy-ion collisions, where models such as the Glauber model and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) are commonly used, and where flow parameters ($v_n$) are known to correlate with initial eccentricities ($\epsilon_n$) as shown by Alver (2010) and Liu (2019). The paper applies PCA, previously used by Bhalerao (2014) and Mazeliauskas (2015) to extract flow fluctuations from azimuthal distributions, to instead probe partonic clustering in the initial state. The approach enables event-by-event identification of events with partonic domains based on PCA eigenvalues, and could be combined with sophisticated cluster-finding algorithms for further substructure analysis.
