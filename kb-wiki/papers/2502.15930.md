# Yu-Shiba-Rusinov states in color superconducting quark matter

> The high-density region of the QCD phase diagram displays an intricate competition between color superconductivity and the QCD Kondo effect due to color exchange in quark matter containing a single heavy quark impurity. We explore the characteristic impurity-induced superconducting subgap states arising in such systems by generalizing the surrogate model solver, recently considered in the context of condensed matter physics [Phys. Rev. B 108, L220506 (2023)]. The method consists of approximating the full superconducting bulk by only a small number of effective levels whose parameters are chosen so as to best reproduce the Matsubara frequency dependence of the impurity-bulk hybridization function. We numerically solve a surrogate QCD Kondo model describing a quantum impurity color-exchange coupled to a two-color two-flavor superconducting bulk. The results directly indicate the presence of multiple phase transitions as the coupling of the impurity to the bulk is increased, due to the interplay between various overscreened states. The methods introduced here are straightforward enough to be extended to more realistic QCD scenarios.

# Yu-Shiba-Rusinov states in color superconducting quark matter

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Yu-Shiba-Rusinov subgap states arise in color superconducting quark matter containing a single heavy quark impurity, and that the surrogate model solver (SMS) can efficiently capture the resulting phase diagram. The authors hedge that their results are obtained for a simplified two-color two-flavor toy model with a single quark chirality, and that the thin-shell approximation for the hybridization function introduces only mild quantitative effects.

## Method

The authors generalize the surrogate model solver (SMS) from condensed matter physics (Baran, 2023) to Kondo-type exchange-coupled impurities. They replace the continuous superconducting bulk with a small number of effective discrete levels (L-tilde = 1, 2, 3) whose parameters are fitted to reproduce the Matsubara frequency dependence of the impurity-bulk hybridization function. They then numerically solve the resulting surrogate QCD Kondo model by exact diagonalization (for L-tilde = 1) and by density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) with maximum bond dimension 5000 (for L-tilde = 1, 2, 3).

## Key numbers

* The surrogate model parameters for D = 10Δ are: for L-tilde = 1, γ₀ = 0.9618; for L-tilde = 2, γ₁ = 1.2642, ξ̃₁ = 1.3099; for L-tilde = 3, γ₀ = 0.508, γ₁ = 1.8454, ξ̃₁ = 2.7546 (all in units of Δ).
* The DMRG calculations used a maximum bond dimension of 5000 and an energy convergence threshold of 10⁻⁴ Δ.
* The zero-bandwidth (L-tilde = 1) model yields analytical quasiparticle energies E(N_qp)/Δ = N_qp(1 − J/4) − J/2 for N_qp ≥ 1, with a special degeneracy point at J = 4.
* The L-tilde = 1 subgap state energy for the standard BCS Kondo model (Appendix) reads (E_o − E_e)/Δ = 1 − 3J/4.

## Context

This work extends the surrogate model solver approach of Baran (2023), originally developed for Anderson impurities in condensed matter BCS superconductors, to the QCD Kondo effect in high-density quark matter. It goes beyond the classical spin approximation used by Kanazawa (2016) for studying subgap spectra in color superconducting systems, and provides a complementary perspective to mean-field studies of the QCD Kondo effect (e.g., Yasui, 2024) and recent exact solutions (Kattel, 2024). The method is straightforward enough to be extended to more realistic QCD scenarios involving full SU(3) color symmetry and density-dependent coupling.
