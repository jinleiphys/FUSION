# Narrow resonances and short-range interactions

> Narrow resonances in systems with short-range interactions are discussed in an effective field theory (EFT) framework. An effective Lagrangian is formulated in the form of a combined expansion in powers of a momentum Q << Lambda--a short-distance scale--and an energy difference delta epsilon = |E-epsilon_0| << epsilon_0--a resonance peak energy. At leading order in the combined expansion, a two-body scattering amplitude is the sum of a smooth background term of order Q^0 and a Breit-Wigner term of order Q^2 (delta epsilon)^{-1} which becomes dominant for delta epsilon <~ Q^3. Such an EFT is applicable to systems in which short-distance dynamics generates a low-lying quasistationary state. The EFT is generalized to describe a narrow low-lying resonance in a system of charged particles. It is shown that in the case of Coulomb repulsion, a two-body scattering amplitude at leading order in a combined expansion is the sum of a Coulomb-modified background term and a Breit-Wigner amplitude with parameters renormalized by Coulomb interactions.

# Narrow resonances and short-range interactions

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) for narrow low-lying resonances in two-body systems with short-range interactions, where the resonance width scales as Q^3 and the peak energy scales as Q^2 relative to a small momentum Q. The EFT is valid only when the energy difference from the resonance peak, δϵ = |E−ϵ₀|, is much smaller than the peak energy ϵ₀ itself, and the combined expansion in Q/Λ and δϵ/ϵ₀ yields a leading-order amplitude that separates into a smooth background term and a Breit-Wigner term that dominates within a narrow energy window δϵ ≲ Q^3.

## Method

The author constructs an effective Lagrangian containing a four-point contact coupling C₀ for background scattering and a dimeron field Φ with Yukawa coupling g and residual mass Δ to generate the resonance. Power counting rules assign C₀ ∼ Q⁰, g ∼ Q, and Δ ∼ Q², with δϵ ≲ Q³. The two-body s-wave T-matrix is computed via a loop expansion; the dimeron contribution requires summation to all orders when |E−Δ| ∼ Q³, yielding a Breit-Wigner amplitude. For charged particles, Coulomb interactions are included by replacing plane-wave external lines with Coulomb functions and using a Coulomb-dressed loop integral evaluated with power-divergence subtraction (PDS) regularization, following the approach of Kong and Ravndal.

## Key numbers

- Resonance width scales as Γ ∼ Q³, while peak energy scales as ϵ₀ ∼ Q², giving Γ ≪ ϵ₀.
- In the toy model with a δ-shell barrier, the dimensionless barrier strength α ∼ Q⁻¹, and the resonance width parameter γ ∼ Q², with Γ = 2γ√ϵ₀ ∼ Q³.
- The flux delay at resonance is ∂δ₀(ϵ₀)/∂E ∼ Q⁻³, compared to Q⁻² for broad resonances.
- For the α-α system cited as an example, the s-wave resonance has ϵ₀ ≈ 92 keV and Γ ≈ 5.6 eV in the center-of-mass frame (from Higa, Hammer, and van Kolck).
- The Coulomb-modified loop integral I^C₀ contains a term −(M/4π)(μ − (2/a_B)ln(μ a_B √π/2) + (3C_E−2)/a_B + (2/a_B)h(η) + i k C²_η), where a_B is the Bohr radius, C_E = 0.5772 is the Euler constant, and C²_η is the Sommerfeld factor.

## Context

This work extends EFT methods previously applied to shallow bound and virtual states (broad resonances) in nucleon-nucleon scattering (Kaplan, Savage, Wise; van Kolck) to the case of narrow resonances where the width is parametrically smaller than the peak energy. The toy model with a δ-shell barrier generalizes earlier work by Massmann on resonances in δ-function potentials. For charged particles, the EFT builds on the Coulomb-modified effective range expansion developed by Kong and Ravndal for nucleon-nucleon interactions, and on the halo EFT approach of Higa, Hammer, and van Kolck for α-α scattering. The formalism enables systematic description of narrow Feshbach resonances in ultracold alkali atoms and low-energy α-α scattering via the ⁸Be isotope.
