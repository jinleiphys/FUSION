# Next-to-leading order Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with resummation

> We solve the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation at next-to-leading order accuracy including a resummation of large single and double transverse momentum logarithms to all orders. We numerically determine an optimal value for the constant under the large transverse momentum logarithm that enables including a maximal amount of the full NLO result in the resummation. When this value is used the contribution from the $α_s^2$ terms without large logarithms is found to be small at large saturation scales and at small dipoles. Close to initial conditions relevant for phenomenological applications these fixed order corrections are shown to be numerically important.

## Key claim

The resummed next-to-leading order (NLO) Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, with an optimally chosen constant \(C_\text{sub}=0.65\) inside the resummed single transverse logarithm, yields a stable evolution where the fixed-order \(\alpha_s^2\) terms are small at large saturation scales and small dipoles, but these same fixed-order corrections are numerically important near phenomenologically relevant initial conditions.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the NLO BK equation in the large-\(N_c\) limit, including the Balitsky running coupling prescription and a resummation of both double transverse logarithms (DLA) and single transverse logarithms (STL) to all orders. The constant \(C_\text{sub}\) in the STL resummation factor \(K_\text{STL}\) was tuned to \(0.65\) by requiring that the subtraction term \(K_\text{sub}\) reproduces the small-\(r\) limit of the other NLO kernels (\(K_1^\text{fin}\), \(K_2\), \(K_f\)) as accurately as possible. The evolution was performed for dipole amplitudes starting from a resummed McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model initial condition, and the contributions from the resummation and the fixed-order \(\alpha_s^2\) terms were separated.

## Key numbers

* The optimal constant in the single transverse logarithm resummation was determined to be \(C_\text{sub}=0.65\), which makes the ratio of the subtraction term contribution to the total NLO contribution close to unity over a wide range of parent dipole sizes at the initial condition.
* The resummed NLO BK equation evolves roughly \(30\%\) slower than the leading-order running-coupling BK equation at very large saturation scales (e.g., \(\qs/\Lambda_\text{QCD} \approx 19\) and \(\qs/\Lambda_\text{QCD} \approx 66\) after 10 units of rapidity).
* At the initial condition with \(\qs \sim 1\ \text{GeV}\), the fixed-order \(\alpha_s^2\) terms significantly increase the evolution speed of the saturation scale; this effect becomes negligible at larger saturation scales later in the evolution.
* The resummed evolution remains stable (positive evolution speed at small dipoles) for initial conditions with anomalous dimensions \(\gamma = 0.8, 1.0, 1.2\), whereas the unresummed NLO BK equation becomes unstable for \(\gamma \gtrsim 0.8\dots 1\).

## Context

This work extends the resummation scheme for large transverse logarithms developed by Iancu et al. (2015) for the BK equation by including the full fixed-order \(\alpha_s^2\) terms from the NLO BK equation derived by Balitsky (2008). It directly follows the authors' previous numerical solution of the NLO BK equation (Lappi and Mantysaari, 2015), which showed that the unresummed equation is unstable and cannot be applied to phenomenology. The results enable the next step of combining this resummed NLO BK evolution with NLO photon impact factors to compute structure functions and test the NLO Color Glass Condensate picture against precise HERA deep inelastic scattering data.
