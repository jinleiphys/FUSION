# Three-nucleon forces in exotic open-shell isotopes

> Advances in the self-consistent Green's function approach to finite nuclei are discussed, including the implementation of three-nucleon forces and the extension to the Gorkov formalism. We report results on binding energies in the nitrogen and fluorine isotopic chains, as well as spectral functions of 22O. The application to medium-mass open-shell systems is illustrated by separation energy spectra of two argon isotopes, which are compared to one-neutron removal experiments.

# Three-nucleon forces in exotic open-shell isotopes

## Key claim
Three-nucleon forces (3NF) are crucial for a consistent description of nitrogen, oxygen, and fluorine drip lines, and the Gorkov extension of self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory can successfully address open-shell systems in the medium-mass region, though the ADC(2) truncation used for argon isotopes may miss coupling to collective fluctuations that would compress calculated spectra.

## Method
The authors implemented three-nucleon forces in the SCGF approach via one- and two-body effective interactions derived from contractions of the three-body term with correlated density matrices, and extended the formalism to Gorkov theory to handle pairing correlations in open-shell nuclei. They used chiral effective field theory two- and three-body forces at N³LO and N²LO respectively, evolved to low momentum scales by free-space similarity renormalization group (SRG), and solved the Dyson equation with the self-energy truncated at the algebraic diagrammatic construction to third order (ADC(3)) for closed-shell systems and ADC(2) for the Gorkov open-shell calculations.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energies of nitrogen and fluorine isotopes: calculations performed with chiral interactions evolved to λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ by SRG, with full two-plus-three-body Hamiltonians yielding results close to experiment and correctly describing the drip line.
- One-neutron removal separation energies for ³⁴Ar: calculated 22.4 MeV versus experimental 17.07 MeV.
- One-neutron removal separation energies for ³⁶Ar: calculated 15.3 MeV versus experimental 15.25 MeV.
- Relative variations of about 1% were found when assessing model-space convergence and dependence on the SRG renormalization scale λ_SRG for oxygen isotopes.

## Context
This work extends prior SCGF calculations on closed-shell nuclei (Barbieri 2009) by implementing three-nucleon forces (Cipollone 2013) and applying the Gorkov formalism (Soma 2011, Soma 2013) to open-shell systems. The results for oxygen isotopes agree with other ab initio calculations (Hergert 2013), and the argon separation energy spectra are compared to neutron transfer experiments by Lee (2010) at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. The method enables ab initio description of full isotopic chains in the medium-mass region, with future work needed to extend the Gorkov self-energy to ADC(3) and include coupling to collective fluctuations.
