# Odd-even binding effect from random two-body interactions

> Systematic odd-even binding energy differences in finite metallic particles are usually attributed to mean-field orbital energy effects or to a coherent pairing interaction. We show analytically and numerically that a purely random two-body Hamiltonian can also give rise to an odd-even staggering. We explore the characteristics of this chaotic mechanism and discuss distinguishing features with respect to the other causes of staggering. In particular, randomness-induced staggering is found to be a smooth function of particle number, and the mechanism is seen to be largely insensitive to the presence of a magnetic field.

# Odd-even binding effect from random two-body interactions

## Key claim

Purely random two-body interactions, without any coherent pairing or mean-field orbital effects, can produce odd-even staggering in ground-state binding energies of finite fermionic systems. The randomness-induced staggering is a smooth function of particle number and is largely insensitive to magnetic fields, which distinguishes it from staggering caused by mean-field or BCS pairing mechanisms.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian for finite fermionic systems (metallic grains or quantum dots) consisting of a mean-field single-particle term plus random two-body interactions in the spin-0 and spin-1 channels, with coupling strengths C0 and C1. The random two-body matrix elements were drawn from the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) for time-reversal symmetric systems and from the Gaussian unitary ensemble (GUE) for systems with broken time-reversal symmetry. Ground-state energies were computed numerically for systems with M=10 single-particle orbitals using the sparse matrix solver ARPACK, with ensemble averages taken over 200 runs. Analytical expressions for the trace of H² in different spin sectors were derived to explain the staggering mechanism.

## Key numbers

- For pure spin-0 random interactions in the dilute limit (N ≪ M), the empirical pairing gap scales as Δ(N) = (−1)^N (3/(2N²)) |E(N)|, where E(N) is the ground-state energy of the N-body system.
- For pure spin-1 random interactions, a critical density ρ_crit = (5−2√3)/13 ≈ 0.118 exists below which no staggering occurs and above which staggering appears proportional to |E(N)|/N².
- Numerical simulations used M=10 single-particle orbitals with ensemble averages from 200 runs; the largest matrices had dimension 63504.
- For the mean-field case with Wigner-distributed level spacings, the average pairing gap for even N is Δ(N) = (1/(MN)) |E(N)|, which is smaller than the interaction-induced gap in the dilute limit.

## Context

This work extends the study of random-interaction ensembles from nuclear physics (Johnson, Bertsch, Dean 1998; Kusnezov 2000; Bijker and Frank 2000) to finite metallic particles and quantum dots, where odd-even staggering had previously been attributed exclusively to mean-field orbital energy effects (de Heer 1993) or BCS pairing (Black, Ralph, Tinkham 1996). The paper shows that random two-body interactions alone produce staggering, and that the smoothness of the staggering envelope as a function of particle number can discriminate this chaotic mechanism from mean-field effects. The results enable experimental distinction between pairing mechanisms in small metallic grains and quantum dots, and connect to studies of J=0 ground-state dominance in random-interaction shell models (Johnson, Bertsch, Dean 1998) and singlet ground-state spin preferences in quantum dots (Jacquod and Stone 2000).
