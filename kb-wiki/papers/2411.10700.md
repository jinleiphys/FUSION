# Direct correlation between the near-proton-emission threshold resonance in $^{11}$B and the branching ratio of beta-delayed proton emission from $^{11}$Be

> Background: Beta-delayed proton emission from neutron halo nuclei $^{11}$Be represents a rare decay process. The existence of the narrow resonance near the proton-emission threshold in $^{11}$B explains its unexpectedly high probability. However, the accurate value of the branching ratio remains challenging to determine. Purpose: We aim to provide a microscopic potential model to determine the branching ratio for beta-delayed proton emission from $^{11}$Be. We focus on quantifying the influence of the narrow resonance near the proton emission threshold on the result of the branching ratio. Method: We employ the Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation within the potential model to obtain the branching ratio. We derive the single-particle potentials for the halo neutron and the emitting proton from the Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation with minimal adjustment. As the resonance position is tightly linked to the potential depth, we can demonstrate quantitatively how variations in its location impact the outcome. Result: Slight variations in the resonance position significantly impact the branching ratio, with the upper limit reaching the order of $10^{-5}$. Conclusion: Experimental determination of the resonance energy, particularly whether it lies below $200$ keV, is crucial for determining the value of the branching ratio.

# Direct correlation between the near-proton-emission threshold resonance in \(^{11}\)B and the branching ratio of beta-delayed proton emission from \(^{11}\)Be

## Key claim

The branching ratio for beta-delayed proton emission from \(^{11}\)Be is strongly and directly correlated with the precise energy of a narrow s-wave single-particle resonance near the proton-emission threshold in \(^{11}\)B; variations of a few tens of keV in the resonance position change the branching ratio by orders of magnitude, with the upper limit reaching \(10^{-5}\), but the exact value depends on whether the resonance lies below or above 200 keV.

## Method

The authors employ a Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation within a potential model to obtain the branching ratio. Single-particle potentials for the halo neutron (initial bound state) and the emitting proton (final scattering state) are derived from Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations using the SkM*, SAMi-ISB, SLy4, and SAMi forces, with two overall scaling parameters adjusted to reproduce the experimental neutron separation energy (501 keV) and the resonance location. The overlap radial integral between the bound neutron wave function and the scattering proton wave function is computed, and the differential decay probability per unit time is integrated over the available Q-value of 281 keV, incorporating Fermi and Gamow-Teller transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Be is \(S_n = 501\) keV, and the total energy available in the decay is \(Q = 281\) keV.
- For the resonance located at 182 keV, the branching ratio is \(9 \times 10^{-6}\) (averaged across Skyrme forces: \(8.91 \times 10^{-6}\) to \(9.05 \times 10^{-6}\)) with a spectroscopic factor \(S_F = 0.51\).
- For the resonance at 171 keV, the branching ratio is approximately \(13 \times 10^{-6}\) (range \(12.9 \times 10^{-6}\) to \(13.2 \times 10^{-6}\)).
- For the resonance at 211 keV, the branching ratio is approximately \(2.9 \times 10^{-6}\) (range \(2.86 \times 10^{-6}\) to \(2.90 \times 10^{-6}\)).
- The experimentally estimated branching ratio of \(8.6 \times 10^{-6}\) from Ayyad et al. (2019) corresponds to a resonance at 197 keV; the upper limit of \(2.2 \times 10^{-6}\) from Riisager et al. (2020) and Sokolowska et al. (2024) corresponds to a resonance at 217 keV in the calculation.
- The single-particle resonance widths \(\Gamma_p\) computed with the SLy4 force are 4.738 keV at \(E_R = 171\) keV, 6.249 keV at \(E_R = 182\) keV, and 11.905 keV at \(E_R = 211\) keV.
- The \(\log(ft)\) values computed with SLy4 are 2.621, 2.628, and 2.659 for resonances at 171, 182, and 211 keV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach of Baye and Tursunov (2011) for beta-delayed proton emission from \(^{11}\)Be by incorporating the recently discovered near-threshold resonance in \(^{11}\)B reported by Ayyad et al. (2022) and Lopez-Saavedra et al. (2022). It provides a microscopic Skyrme Hartree-Fock basis for the single-particle potentials, improving upon earlier phenomenological treatments. The results directly address the discrepancy between the branching ratio of \(8.6 \times 10^{-6}\) measured by Ayyad et al. (2019) and the upper limit of \(2.2 \times 10^{-6}\) reported by Riisager et al. (2020) and Sokolowska et al. (2024), showing that both values can be accommodated depending on the precise resonance energy. The work enables future experiments at facilities such as FRIB to resolve the puzzle by measuring the resonance energy with precision better than the current uncertainties of \(\pm 20\) keV and \(\pm 40\) keV.
