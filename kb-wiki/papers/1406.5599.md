# Covariant equations for the tetraquark and more

> We derive covariant equations for a system of two quarks and two antiquarks where the effect of quark-antiquark annihilation is taken into account. In our approach, only pair-wise interactions are retained, while all possibilities of overcounting are excluded by (i) keeping terms in the kernel that are consistent with a meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark substructure, and (ii) introducing 4-body equations with a novel structure that specifically avoids the generation of overcounted terms. The resulting tetraquark bound state equations are given for the case of general two-body interactions, and for the specific case of separable interactions that lead to a description of the tetraquark in terms of meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark degrees of freedom where the effects of quark-antiquark annihilation is included. The inclusion of $2q2\bar{q}$- and $q\bar{q}$-channel coupling in our approach enables a wide variety of applications of our equations to other processes within the $2q2\bar{q}$ system, and to other 2-particle plus 2-antiparticle systems.

## Key claim

The paper derives covariant four-body equations for a system of two quarks and two antiquarks that include the effect of quark-antiquark annihilation, which was omitted in prior work. The authors achieve this by introducing a novel structure for the coupled-channel equations that avoids overcounting of Feynman diagrams, but only under the approximation that linear terms in the two-body t-matrices are neglected, consistent with a meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark substructure picture.

## Method

The authors start from the exact four-body Green function and its Dyson equation, then decompose the kernel into pairwise interactions. They separate the two-body t-matrices into connected parts (scattering) and disconnected parts (annihilation into vacuum). To avoid double-counting generated by the disconnected annihilation kernels, they split the four-body amplitude into two coupled components and introduce modified equations where the annihilation kernels only couple to the connected parts of the amplitude. The final bound-state equations are given for general two-body interactions and then specialized to separable interactions that describe meson, diquark, and antidiquark degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

* The paper contains no explicit numerical results; it is a formal derivation of equations.

## Context

This work extends the covariant four-body equations of Khvedelidze and Kvinikhidze (KK), which were valid only for systems without annihilation (e.g., four quarks), to the two-quark two-antiquark system where quark-antiquark annihilation is present. It directly addresses the approach of Heupel, Eichmann, Popovici and Fischer (HEPF), who used the KK equations for tetraquarks but neglected annihilation effects. The derived equations enable future studies of tetraquark, meson-molecule, and hybrid states, as well as applications to other two-particle plus two-antiparticle systems such as two nucleons and two antinucleons.
