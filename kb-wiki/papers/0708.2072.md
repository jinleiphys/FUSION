# Hadronic Resonances from Lattice QCD

> The determination of the pattern of hadronic resonances as predicted by Quantum Chromodynamics requires the use of non-perturbative techniques. Lattice QCD has emerged as the dominant tool for such calculations, and has produced many QCD predictions which can be directly compared to experiment. The concepts underlying lattice QCD are outlined, methods for calculating excited states are discussed, and results from an exploratory Nucleon and Delta baryon spectrum study are presented.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of the Nucleon and Delta baryon spectra in the quenched approximation at an unphysically high pion mass of approximately 700 MeV show that the first excited even-parity states (corresponding to the Roper and P33(1600) resonances) lie above the first odd-parity band, in contrast to the experimental ordering.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 12^3 x 48 anisotropic lattice with spatial spacing a_s ~ 0.1 fm and anisotropy a_s/a_tau ~ 3.0. They constructed a basis of interpolating operators using quark field smearing and gauge link smearing to improve signal quality, then applied the variational method to a correlator matrix to extract multiple excited states. The resulting energy levels were subduced from continuum J^p quantum numbers onto the discrete lattice spin-parity irreducible representations of the O_h^D group.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass of approximately 700 MeV used in the exploratory quenched study
* Lattice volume of 12^3 x 48 with spatial lattice spacing a_s ~ 0.1 fm and temporal anisotropy a_s/a_tau ~ 3.0
* 200 quenched configurations were analyzed
* The first excited even-parity states (Roper and P33(1600) analogs) were found above the first odd-parity band, in contrast to the experimental ordering where the Roper lies below the odd-parity band

## Context

This work extends the program of the Lattice Hadron Physics Collaboration (LHPC) to calculate the QCD hadron spectrum from first principles, building on operator construction methods described by Bulava (2007) and Morningstar (2004). The exploratory quenched results show discrepancies with experimental spectra from the Particle Data Group, particularly in the ordering of the Roper resonance relative to odd-parity states, which may be due to quenching effects, the unphysical quark mass, finite-volume effects, or limitations of the three-quark operator basis. Ongoing unquenched runs at multiple volumes and pion masses aim to resolve these issues and move toward a first-principles determination of the QCD resonance spectrum.
