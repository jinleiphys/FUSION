# Coulomb-nuclear dynamics in the weakly-bound 8Li breakup

> A detailed study of total, Coulomb and nuclear breakup cross sections dependence on the projectile ground-state binding energy $\varepsilon_b$ is presented, by considering the $^8$Li+$^{12}$C and $^8$Li+$^{208}$Pb breakup reactions. To this end, apart from the experimental one-neutron separation energy of $^8$Li nucleus ($\varepsilon_b=2.03$~MeV), lower values of $\varepsilon_b$ down to $\varepsilon_b=0.01$~MeV, are also being considered. It is shown that all breakup processes become peripheral as $\varepsilon_b\to 0.01$ MeV, which is understood as due to the well-known large spacial extension of ground-state wave functions associated to weakly-bound projectiles. The Coulomb breakup cross section is found to be more strongly dependent on $\varepsilon_b$ than the nuclear breakup cross section, such that the Coulomb breakup becomes more significant as $\varepsilon_b$ decreases, even in a naturally nuclear-dominated reaction. This is mainly due to the long-range nature of the Coulomb forces, leading to a direct dependence of the Coulomb breakup on the electromagnetic transition matrix. It is also highlighted the fact that the nuclear absorption plays a minor role for small binding when the breakup becomes more peripheral.

# Coulomb-nuclear dynamics in the weakly-bound 8Li breakup

## Key claim
The Coulomb breakup cross section becomes dominant over the nuclear breakup cross section as the projectile ground-state binding energy decreases toward 0.01 MeV, even in reactions that are naturally nuclear-dominated (such as 8Li+12C), due to the long-range nature of Coulomb forces and the increasing peripherality of the breakup process.

## Method
The authors used the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) formalism with the Fresco code to calculate total, Coulomb, and nuclear breakup cross sections for 8Li+12C and 8Li+208Pb reactions. They artificially varied the 8Li ground-state binding energy from the experimental value of 2.03 MeV down to 0.01 MeV while keeping the projectile mass and charge fixed, separating Coulomb and nuclear contributions via a weak-coupling approximation.

## Key numbers
- Experimental one-neutron separation energy of 8Li: εb = 2.03 MeV; artificially reduced values down to εb = 0.01 MeV were also considered
- For 8Li+12C at Elab = 14 MeV and 24 MeV, elastic scattering shows weak dependence on εb in the range 0.4 MeV ≤ εb ≤ 2.03 MeV, but becomes significant for εb ≤ 0.08 MeV
- For 8Li+208Pb at Elab = 36 MeV and 60 MeV, the Coulomb breakup cross section exceeds the nuclear breakup cross section by about three orders of magnitude as εb decreases toward 0.01 MeV
- The 8Li ground-state root-mean-square radius is √⟨r²⟩ = 2.39 fm at εb = 2.03 MeV, in agreement with reported values of 2.39 ± 0.05 fm (2015Fan) and 2.37 ± 0.02 fm (1988Tanihata)
- For εb → 2.03 MeV, nuclear absorption reduces the breakup cross section by about one order of magnitude; for εb → 0.01 MeV, nuclear absorption becomes negligible

## Context
This work extends previous studies on Coulomb-nuclear dynamics in weakly-bound projectile breakup, particularly building on the analysis of 8Li+208Pb by Mukeru (2020) which considered critical angular momentum for complete fusion. The study systematically varies binding energy for a single projectile (8Li) to avoid the shortcomings of comparing different projectiles with different ground-state structures, as done in earlier works by Rangel (2016), Lei (2016), and Mukeru (2015, 2020). The results enable better understanding of how Coulomb-nuclear interference and nuclear absorption evolve with binding energy, with implications for fusion suppression mechanisms and the validity of the Coulomb dissociation method.
