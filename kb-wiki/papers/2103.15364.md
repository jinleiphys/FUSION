# Electrical conductivity of strongly magnetized dense quark matter -- possibility of quantum hall effect

> We have pointed out the possibility of quantum Hall effect or quantum patterns of transportation in a degenerate strongly magnetized quark matter, which might be expected inside a highly dense compact star. An anisotropic pattern of electrical conductivity and resistivity tensor in classical and quantum cases is explored by considering cyclotron motion and Landau quantization respectively. With increasing magnetic field, classical to quantum transitions are realized through enhanced/reduced resistivity/conductivity with jumping pattern. Considering QCD relaxation time scale of 10 fm, $eB\approx (1-4) m_π^2$ might be considered as strong magnetic field for massless and degenerate quark matter with quark chemical potential $μ\approx 0.2-0.4$ GeV. Beyond these threshold ranges of magnetic field, perpendicular motion of quarks might be stopped and 3 $\rightarrow$ 1 dimensionally reduced conduction picture might be established.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that in degenerate, massless quark matter at zero temperature and high chemical potential (μ ≈ 0.2–0.4 GeV), a quantum Hall effect pattern may appear when the magnetic field exceeds a threshold of eB ≈ (1–4) m_π², beyond which perpendicular conduction stops and the system undergoes a 3D to 1D dimensional reduction. The claim is hedged as a "possibility" and a "toy-level estimation" for quark matter inside neutron stars, not a confirmed prediction.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) to derive analytical expressions for the electrical conductivity tensor components (σ_xx, σ_xy, σ_zz) and resistivity tensor components (ρ_xx, ρ_xy, ρ_zz) for massless up and down quark matter. They first obtain classical expressions using cyclotron motion, then incorporate Landau quantization by replacing the continuous phase space with a sum over Landau levels and modifying the energy dispersion. The degenerate (T = 0) limit is taken by replacing the Fermi-Dirac distribution with a step function, and the sum over Landau levels is regularized using Hurwitz and Riemann zeta functions.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field range considered is eB ≈ (1–4) m_π², where m_π is the pion mass, for quark chemical potential μ ≈ 0.2–0.4 GeV.
- The QCD relaxation time scale is taken as τ_c = 10 fm.
- The threshold magnetic field B_0 for dimensional reduction is approximately m_π² for μ = 0.2 GeV and 4 m_π² for μ = 0.4 GeV, beyond which l_max = 1 and perpendicular motion stops.
- The maximum Landau level l^Q_max is given by Integer[(μ² – m²)/(2 e_Q B)], which varies from 1 to 10 in the range e_Q B ≈ (0.1–1) m_π² for μ = 0.2 GeV and (1–5) m_π² for μ = 0.4 GeV.
- The longitudinal conductivity in the lowest Landau level (l = 0) regime for massless quarks is σ^{zz}_{QM} = Σ_Q g e_Q² (|e_Q| B_0 τ_c^Q)/(2π²).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of electrical conductivity in magnetized quark matter from the high-temperature, low-density regime relevant to heavy ion collisions (e.g., Dey et al. (2020), Hattori et al. (2017)) to the opposite domain of zero temperature and high density, relevant to neutron star cores as described by Hatsuda et al. and Kurkela et al. It builds on classical anisotropic conductivity models for neutron stars (e.g., Sedrakian et al., Kerbikov) and earlier quantum treatments using Landau quantization (e.g., Lifshitz and Kosevich). The results enable future studies of quantum Hall patterns at the quark-hadron phase transition density and calculations of other transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, thermal conductivity) via the Kubo formalism.
