# Nucleon momentum distributions in asymmetric nuclear matter

> Nucleon momentum distributions at various densities and isospin-asymmetries for nuclear matter are investigated systematically within the extended Bruecker-Hartree-Fock approach.The shapes of the normalized momentum distributions varying with $k/k_{F}$ are practically identical, while the density and isospin dependent magnitude of the distribution is directly related to the depletion of the Fermi sea. Based on these properties, a parameterized formula is proposed with the parameters calibrated to the calculated result.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the shapes of normalized nucleon momentum distributions in asymmetric nuclear matter are practically identical across a range of densities and isospin asymmetries when plotted as a function of \(k/k_F\), while the magnitude is directly related to the depletion of the Fermi sea. This universality is presented as a finding within the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) approach, with the caveat that the parameterized formula is calibrated to these specific calculations and may not be definitive due to approximations in the mass operator.

## Method

The authors employed the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) approach, calculating the mass operator up to second order in the hole-line expansion using the Argonne \(V18\) two-body interaction supplemented with a microscopic three-body force (TBF). The momentum distribution \(n(k)\) was obtained from the spectral function via the Green's function formalism, and the depletion parameter \(\chi\) (the fraction of nucleons above the Fermi momentum) was computed for various densities (0.50\(\rho_0\) to 2.5\(\rho_0\)) and isospin asymmetries (\(\beta = 0.0\) to 0.5). A parameterized formula for the momentum distribution was then fitted to these numerical results, with the depletion parameter expressed as a function of density and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The empirical saturation density of nuclear matter is \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The normalized momentum distribution below the Fermi momentum is fitted as \(n_{<}^{\tau}(k)/n(0) = 1.00329 - 0.02876x - 0.09053x^{7}\), where \(x = k/k_F^{\tau}\).
- The normalized momentum distribution above the Fermi momentum is fitted as \(n_{>}^{\tau}(k)/n(1.05k_F^{\tau}) = 3.548e^{-1.799x} + 52.2e^{-4.2766x^{2}}\), where \(x = k/k_F^{\tau}\).
- The depletion parameter \(\chi\) is parameterized as \(\chi(\rho,\beta) = 0.1669[1 + \lambda(0.1407\frac{\rho}{\rho_0} - 0.7296)\beta] \times [1 + 2.448e^{-4.1854\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}} + 0.1382(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0})^{1.5}]\), where \(\lambda = 1\) for neutrons and \(\lambda = -1\) for protons.
- The formula is stated to be reliable for densities \(0.1\rho_0 < \rho < 3.0\rho_0\) and isospin asymmetries \(\beta \in (-0.5, 0.5)\).

## Context

This work extends previous EBHF calculations of nucleon momentum distributions in symmetric nuclear matter (Bhf4) to asymmetric nuclear matter, and simplifies the parameterization to a single expression valid across densities and asymmetries. It contrasts with the SCGF approach (md4, scg4) which found similar exponential damping at high momentum, and with the Tan's relation \(k^{-4}\) which the authors argue is less suitable for nuclear matter. The parameterized formula enables applications in transport models for heavy-ion reactions, such as the IBUU model, and can be used in local density approximation for finite nuclei. The authors note that the results depend on the chosen interaction (Argonne \(V18\)) and that higher-order terms in the mass operator may alter the depletion.
