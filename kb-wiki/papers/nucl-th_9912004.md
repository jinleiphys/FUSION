# Many-body corrections to the nuclear anapole moment II

> The contribution of many-body effects to the nuclear anapole moment were studied earlier in [1]. Here, more accurate calculation of the many-body contributions is presented, which goes beyond the constant density approximation for them used in [1]. The effects of pairing are now included. The accuracy of the short range limit of the parity violating nuclear forces is discussed.

# Many-body corrections to the nuclear anapole moment II

## Key claim

The leading approximation (LA) used in previous calculations overestimates the core polarization contribution to the nuclear anapole moment by approximately a factor of 2, and the accuracy of the short-range limit of parity-violating nuclear forces is state-dependent rather than constant across nuclei.

## Method

The authors solved the random-phase approximation (RPA) equations for the effective radial fields of the anapole moment operators, going beyond the constant density approximation and the leading approximation used in their prior work (Dmitriev and Telitsin, 1997). They included exact calculations of the core polarization contribution using Woods-Saxon wave functions, incorporated pairing effects via Bogolyubov factors and a modified particle-hole propagator, and evaluated the normalization factors \(W_\pi\) and \(W_\rho\) that relate the zero-range effective parity-non-conserving (PNC) interaction to finite-range meson exchange forces.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{133}\)Cs, the direct contribution of the two-particle effective PNC interaction (diagram IIa) to the anapole moment is \(\delta\kappa_{F_w} = -0.015\) for the "best values" of the coupling constants, compared to the single-particle value \(\kappa_s = 0.32\) (less than 5% contribution).
- For \(^{205}\)Tl, the direct PNC contribution is \(\delta\kappa_{F_w} = 0.003\); for \(^{209}\)Bi, \(\delta\kappa_{F_w} = 0.023\); for \(^{207}\)Pb, \(\delta\kappa_{F_w} = 0.0085\).
- The spin part of the anapole moment for \(^{205}\)Tl is \(\kappa_s = 0.37\) in the present RPA calculation, compared to \(\kappa_s = 0.35\) from a shell model calculation (Auerbach and Brown, 1999).
- The pairing gap values used for \(^{133}\)Cs are \(\Delta_p = 0.95\) MeV for protons and \(\Delta_n = 1.05\) MeV for neutrons (from M.S. et al., 1967).
- The normalization factor \(W_\pi\) is approximately 0.16 near the Fermi surface but reaches \(W_\pi \sim 0.4 - 0.5\) for \(1s_{1/2}\) states at lower energy.
- The proton-neutron constant of the effective spin-orbit residual interaction is \(U_{ls}^{pn} = U_{ls}^{np} = 134\) MeV·fm\(^5\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RPA calculation (Dmitriev and Telitsin, 1997) by removing the constant density approximation and including pairing effects, motivated by the first measurement of the cesium anapole moment (Wood et al., 1997). The improved calculation shows that the leading approximation overestimates core polarization effects, and that the normalization factors \(W_\pi\) and \(W_\rho\) used to relate zero-range PNC forces to finite-range meson exchange forces (from Desplanques et al., 1980; and earlier work by the authors) are state-dependent, not constant as previously assumed. The results are compared with shell model calculations (Auerbach and Brown, 1999; Haxton et al., 1989) and show that the extracted weak coupling constants \(H^1_\pi\) and \(H^0_\rho\) from \(^{133}\)Cs and \(^{205}\)Tl data remain inconsistent with each other, raising questions about the reliability of nuclear anapole moment theory. The work enables more accurate extraction of PNC coupling constants from future anapole moment measurements and highlights the need for experiments sensitive to the neutron PNC potential constant \(g_n\).
