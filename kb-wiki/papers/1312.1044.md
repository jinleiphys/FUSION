# Microscopic study of chiral rotation in odd-odd A $\sim$ 100 nuclei

> A systematic study of the doublet bands observed in odd-odd mass $\sim$ 100 is performed using the microscopic triaxial projected shell model approach. This mass region has depicted some novel features which are not observed in other mass regions, for instance, it has been observed that two chiral bands cross diabatically in $^{106}$Ag. It is demonstrated that this unique feature is due to crossing of the two 2-quasiparticle configurations having different intrinsic structures. Further, we provide a complete set of transition probabilities for all the six-isotopes studied in this work and it is shown that the predicted transitions are in good agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The diabatic crossing of the two chiral bands observed in \(^{106}\)Ag at spin \(I=14\) is demonstrated to arise from the crossing of two distinct 2-quasiparticle configurations with different intrinsic structures, rather than from a change in chiral geometry alone.

## Method

The authors employed the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, constructing a basis of one-neutron and one-proton quasiparticle configurations on a triaxial Nilsson+BCS vacuum. Three-dimensional angular-momentum projection was applied to restore rotational symmetry, and the shell model Hamiltonian (including quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing interactions) was diagonalized in the projected basis for six odd-odd nuclei in the \(A \sim 100\) region.

## Key numbers

- The diabatic crossing of the yrast and partner bands in \(^{106}\)Ag occurs at spin \(I=14\).
- For \(^{104}\)Ag, the crossing of the two lowest bands is predicted to occur at \(I=18\).
- Axial deformation parameters \(\beta\) used range from 0.149 (\(^{104}\)Ag) to 0.237 (\(^{106}\)Rh); triaxial deformation parameters \(\gamma\) range from \(30^\circ\) to \(34^\circ\) across the six isotopes.
- Monopole-pairing force constants are given by \(G_M^\nu = [20.12 - 13.13 (N-Z)/A] A^{-1}\) and \(G_M^\pi = 20.12 A^{-1}\); quadrupole-pairing strength is \(G_Q = 0.16 G_M\).
- Effective charges used: \(e^\pi = 1.5e\), \(e^\nu = 0.5e\); spin g-factors: \(g_s^\pi = 5.586 \times 0.85\), \(g_s^\nu = -3.826 \times 0.85\).

## Context

This work extends the TPSM approach, previously applied to \(^{128}\)Cs in the \(A \sim 130\) region (Sheikh et al., 2012) and to Er isotopes (Sheikh et al., 2011), to systematically study chiral doublet bands in the \(A \sim 100\) region. It resolves a puzzle noted in the experimental study of \(^{106}\)Ag (Joshi et al., 2004) regarding the diabatic band crossing, confirming the earlier conjecture that the yrast and partner bands have different quasiparticle configurations. The results enable future microscopic investigations of chiral rotation in other mass regions and provide a complete set of transition probabilities for comparison with new experimental data.
