# Sequential Clusterization of Light Nuclei and Hypernuclei in Heavy-Ion Collisions within a Wigner Function Coalescence Framework

> We investigate the formation of light nuclei and hypernuclei in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3~\mathrm{GeV}$ within a coalescence framework embedded in the microscopic N-body Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) transport model. The Wigner phase-space distributions employed in the coalescence calculation are constructed from realistic $N$-body wave functions obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation in the hyperspherical harmonics formalism, providing a solid and parameter-free description of nuclear clusters and hypernuclei. By comparing calculated rapidity distributions with STAR data, we extract species-dependent coalescence times, revealing a non-universal formation pattern among different clusters. The resulting yields and kinematic distributions of light nuclei and hypernuclei are systematically analyzed and shown to be sensitive to the underlying wave-function structure and formation time. In addition, we explore cluster-nucleon formation channels for $A=4$ systems. These additional channels improve the description of ${}^{4}\mathrm{He}$ and ${}^{4}_Λ\mathrm{H}$ yields and help address the underestimation of $A=4$ cluster production in theoretical approaches. Finally, we provide predictions for heavier hypernuclei, including ${}^{5}_Λ\mathrm{He}$ and ${}^{5}_{ΛΛ}\mathrm{He}$, which are of interest for future experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the coalescence time for cluster formation in heavy-ion collisions is species-dependent, with more compact and strongly bound clusters like \({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\) and \({}^{4}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\) forming earlier (around 40-80 fm/\(c\)) than loosely bound clusters like deuterons, tritons, and \({}^{3}\mathrm{He}\) (around 70-100 fm/\(c\)), and that including cluster-nucleon formation channels for \(A=4\) systems improves agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors embed a Wigner-function coalescence framework within the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) transport model for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3\) GeV. The Wigner phase-space distributions are constructed from realistic \(N\)-body wave functions obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation numerically in a hyperspherical harmonics basis, rather than using the common Gaussian approximation. The coalescence procedure is applied at different evolution cutoff times \(t_{\mathrm{coal}}\) (ranging from 20 to 145 fm/\(c\)), and the resulting yields and rapidity distributions are compared with STAR experimental data to extract species-dependent coalescence times via \(\chi^2/\mathrm{NDF}\) minimization.

## Key numbers

- The preferred coalescence time for deuterons, tritons, and \({}^{3}\mathrm{He}\) is approximately 80-100 fm/\(c\) based on \(\chi^2/\mathrm{NDF}\) minima, with matched coalescence times from yield intersections in the range 70-96 fm/\(c\).
- For \({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\) including all channels (direct four-body plus \({}^{3}\mathrm{He}+n\) and \(t+p\)), the preferred coalescence time is approximately 50 fm/\(c\) from \(\chi^2/\mathrm{NDF}\) analysis, with a matched coalescence time of \(57 \pm 4\) fm/\(c\).
- The direct four-body channel alone for \({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\) gives a minimum \(\chi^2/\mathrm{NDF}\) of 18.2 at \(t_{\mathrm{coal}} \approx 40\) fm/\(c\), while including cluster-nucleon channels reduces the minimum to 1.5 at \(t_{\mathrm{coal}} \approx 50\) fm/\(c\).
- For \({}^{3}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\), the preferred coalescence time is approximately 125 fm/\(c\) from \(\chi^2/\mathrm{NDF}\) analysis, with a matched coalescence time of 114 fm/\(c\).
- For \({}^{4}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\), the direct four-body channel gives a matched coalescence time of \(40^{+10}_{-13}\) fm/\(c\), while the multi-channel calculation (including \({}^{3}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}+n\)) gives \(66^{+14}_{-13}\) fm/\(c\).
- More than 99% of protons and \(\Lambda\) hyperons freeze out before 145 fm/\(c\) in the PHQMD calculation.

## Context

This work extends the Wigner-function coalescence approach by replacing the common Gaussian approximation for cluster wave functions (used in SatoYazaki (1981) and ScheiblHeinz (1999)) with realistic few-body wave functions from hyperspherical harmonics, reducing model arbitrariness. It addresses the known underestimation of \({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\) production in coalescence models, as reported by ALICE (2023) and Liu (2024), by introducing cluster-nucleon formation channels for \(A=4\) systems. The results enable future experimental measurements of heavier hypernuclei such as \({}^{5}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\) and \({}^{5}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\mathrm{He}\) by providing predictions for their yields and kinematic distributions.
