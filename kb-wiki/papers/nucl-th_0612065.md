# Nuclear polarizability of helium isotopes in atomic transitions

> We estimate the nuclear polarizability correction to atomic transition frequencies in various helium isotopes. This effect is non-negligible for high precision tests of quantum electrodynamics or accurate determination of the nuclear charge radius from spectroscopic measurements in helium atoms and ions. In particular, it amounts to $28(3)$ kHz for 1S-2S transition in 4He+.

# Nuclear polarizability of helium isotopes in atomic transitions

## Key claim

The nuclear polarizability correction to atomic transition frequencies in helium isotopes is non-negligible for high-precision QED tests and charge radius determinations, but the authors find that in most cases the correction is smaller than current experimental precision; the dominant approximation is the electric dipole interaction, which may not always be valid, though higher-order polarizabilities are estimated to be below 1 kHz.

## Method

The nuclear polarizability correction is calculated using a weighted electric dipole polarizability derived from the B(E1) strength distribution, which is obtained either from experimental photoabsorption cross sections or from theoretical three-body model calculations. For ⁶He, the B(E1) distribution comes from Coulomb breakup data and a three-body Schrödinger equation calculation; for ⁴He, it is extracted from total photoabsorption cross section measurements; for ³He, theoretical photodisintegration cross sections from two different realistic NN potential calculations (CD Bonn + three-body force and AV18 + UrbanaIX) are used.

## Key numbers

- ⁴He weighted polarizability: 2.07(20) fm³ = 3.6(4)×10⁻⁸ m⁻³
- ⁴He polarizability correction to 1S-2S transition in ⁴He⁺: 28(3) kHz
- ³He weighted polarizability: 3.56(36) fm³ = 6.2(6)×10⁻⁸ m⁻³
- ³He polarizability correction to 1S-2S transition in ³He⁺: 48(5) kHz
- ⁶He weighted polarizability: 24.7(5.0) fm³ = 4.3(9)×10⁻⁷ m⁻³
- ⁶He polarizability correction to 2³S₁-3³P₂ transition: 0.015(3) MHz
- Ratio of polarizability to finite nuclear size correction for ⁶He: 0.35%
- Ratio of polarizability to finite size correction for ⁴He: 4.6×10⁻⁴
- Separation energies: S₂ₙ(⁶He) = 0.975 MeV, Sₚ(⁴He) = 19.8 MeV, Sₙ(⁴He) = 20.6 MeV, Sₚ(³He) = 5.49349 MeV, Sₙ(³He) = 7.71804 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to muonic helium (Friar, 1977) and to hydrogen-deuterium isotope shifts (Huber, 1998) to the helium isotope chain ³He, ⁴He, and ⁶He. The results enable improved charge radius determinations from planned high-precision measurements of the 1S-2S transition in He⁺ (Hansch group) and provide necessary corrections for future muonic helium experiments, though current experimental precision for ⁶He isotope shifts (Wang, 2004) is about four times larger than the polarizability correction.
