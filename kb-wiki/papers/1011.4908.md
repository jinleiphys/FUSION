# Single and Double Photonuclear Excitations in Pb+Pb Collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Cross sections are calculated for single and double photon exchange in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The particle production is simulated with the DPMJET event generator. Large cross sections are found for particle production around mid-rapidity making these processes an important background to hadronic nuclear interactions at both the trigger and analysis levels.

## Key claim
Cross sections for single and double photon exchange in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC are large enough to constitute an important background to hadronic nuclear interactions, but the calculations rely on the DPMJET event generator and the photon spectrum is calculated in impact parameter space rather than using a nuclear form factor, which would give cross sections 30-185% higher depending on the minimum photon energy.

## Method
The authors used the Weizsäcker-Williams method to compute the equivalent photon flux in impact parameter space, with a Glauber model providing the probability of no hadronic interaction. The photonuclear cross section was calculated using the DPMJET Monte Carlo event generator version 3.0, which is based on the two-component dual parton model with Gribov-Glauber treatment of nuclear shadowing. Six samples of 500,000 events each were generated for single and double excitation with minimum photon energies of 6, 1000, and 10000 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Total photoproduction cross section for photon energies above 6 GeV is about 3 times larger than the total hadronic cross section in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Cross section for at least one photoproduced charged particle within |eta| < 1 is about 4 barns, roughly 50% of the total hadronic cross section
- Photonuclear cross section sigma_gamma_A(k) increases monotonically from 8.1 to 12.9 mb over the photon energy range 6 GeV to 100 TeV
- Interaction probability has a maximum of approximately 15% for k_min = 6 GeV in grazing collisions at impact parameter approximately 16 fm
- Mean transverse momentum of photoproduced particles at mid-rapidity is <p_T> approximately 450 MeV/c
- Photon spectrum cutoff occurs for k > gamma/R approximately 120 TeV in the rest frame of the target nucleus
- Using a nuclear form factor instead of impact parameter cut gives cross sections 30% higher for k_min = 6 GeV, 73% higher for k_min = 1000 GeV, and 185% higher for k_min = 10000 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC (Baltz 1998, Baltz 2002) and lower energies (reviewed by Bertulani 2005), which focused on exclusive production of single vector mesons or dilepton pairs, to inclusive photoproduction at LHC energies. The results enable experimentalists to account for these processes as background to hadronic nuclear interactions at the trigger and analysis levels, and suggest that if photonuclear events can be separated using rapidity gaps, they could provide information on nuclear parton distribution functions through processes like gamma+gluon fusion to c-cbar pairs with cross sections near 1 barn at full LHC energy (Klein 2002).
