# Isovector part of nuclear energy density functional from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces

> A recent calculation of the nuclear energy density functional from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces is extended to the isovector terms pertaining to different proton and neutron densities. An improved density-matrix expansion is adapted to the situation of small isospin-asymmetries and used to calculate in the Hartree-Fock approximation the density-dependent strength functions associated with the isovector terms. The two-body interaction comprises of long-range multi-pion exchange contributions and a set of contact terms contributing up to fourth power in momenta. In addition, the leading order chiral three-nucleon interaction is employed with its parameters fixed in computations of nuclear few-body systems. With this input one finds for the asymmetry energy of nuclear matter the value $A(ρ_0) \simeq 26.5\,$MeV, compatible with existing semi-empirical determinations. The strength functions of the isovector surface and spin-orbit coupling terms come out much smaller than those of the analogous isoscalar coupling terms and in the relevant density range one finds agreement with phenomenological Skyrme forces. The specific isospin- and density-dependences arising from the chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions can be explored and tested in neutron-rich systems.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous calculation of the nuclear energy density functional from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces to the isovector terms, finding that the asymmetry energy of nuclear matter at saturation density is approximately 26.5 MeV, which is about 20% smaller than empirical determinations, and that the strength functions of the isovector surface and spin-orbit coupling terms are much smaller than their isoscalar counterparts.

## Method

The author uses the improved density-matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner (2009) adapted to small isospin asymmetries, and calculates in the Hartree-Fock approximation the density-dependent strength functions for the isovector terms. The two-body interaction is the N$^3$LOW chiral nucleon-nucleon potential (with cutoff scale $\Lambda = 414$ MeV) comprising long-range multi-pion exchange and contact terms up to fourth power in momenta, supplemented by the leading order (N$^2$LO) chiral three-nucleon interaction with parameters $c_E$, $c_D$, $c_1$, $c_3$, $c_4$ fixed from few-body systems. The contributions are computed separately for finite-range pion-exchange, zero-range contact, and three-body interactions, then summed to obtain the total strength functions.

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry energy of nuclear matter at saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$: $A(\rho_0) \simeq 26.5$ MeV (compatible with empirical values of $35 \pm 2$ MeV)
- Kinetic energy contribution to asymmetry energy at saturation: $A_{\rm kin}(\rho_0) = 11.8$ MeV
- Slope parameter of asymmetry energy at saturation: $L = 3\rho_0 A'(\rho_0) = 76$ MeV (compatible with $L \simeq 100$ MeV from Blaizot (1980))
- Three-body contribution to asymmetry energy at saturation: $4.0$ MeV
- One-pion exchange contribution to asymmetry energy at $\rho_0$: $-4.4$ MeV
- Strength function $G_d(\rho)$ from two-body finite-range interactions: $-9.9$ MeV fm$^5$
- Isovector spin-orbit strength $G_{so}(\rho_0/2)$ after correction: $2.63$ MeV fm$^5$ (compared to $6.19$ MeV fm$^5$ before correction)
- Skyrme parameterization band for isovector surface strength: $G_\nabla^{(\rm Sk)} = -(11 \pm 5)$ MeV fm$^5$
- Isoscalar surface strength from Skyrme forces: $F_\nabla^{(\rm Sk)} \simeq 75$ MeV fm$^5$
- Skyrme isovector spin-orbit strength: $G_{so}^{(\rm Sk)} = W_0/4 \simeq 30$ MeV fm$^5$

## Context

This work extends the calculation of the nuclear energy density functional from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces by Kaiser (2012) to isospin-asymmetric systems, building on the improved density-matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet and Bogner (2009) and the earlier microscopically constrained functional of Gebremariam, Bogner and Duguet (2010). The results enable testing of the specific isospin- and density-dependences arising from chiral interactions in neutron-rich nuclei and long chains of stable isotopes, while also showing that the Hartree-Fock approximation works reasonably well for the asymmetry energy (unlike for the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter, where second-order corrections are essential as shown by Achim (2011) and Hebeler (2011)).
