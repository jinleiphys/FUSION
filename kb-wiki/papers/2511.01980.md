# Incoherent Particle Production in Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this paper, we present the first complete treatment of incoherent photon-initiated production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, focussing on the dilepton and diphoton final states. In the former case we compare to the ATLAS measurement of dielectron production and find that our predictions match the data very well. In the latter case we show that this contribution is too small to explain the observed broad in diphoton acoplanarity background to the purely coherent light-by-light scattering signal. We in addition consider a new `quark emission' topology, which while naively might be expected to dominate the incoherent diphoton production channel, is in fact found to be kinematically suppressed. Finally, we revisit QCD-initiated diphoton production and issue of theoretical uncertainties in this case. We find that this again cannot explain the observed size of the higher acoplanarity background. The production mechanism leading to this background to light-by-light production, and the reason for its observed enhancement in comparison to the dilepton case, therefore remains unclear.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete treatment of incoherent photon-initiated production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, but finds that this mechanism cannot explain the observed broad acoplanarity background to light-by-light scattering, leaving the production mechanism for this background unclear.

## Method

The author calculates incoherent photon-initiated production cross sections for dilepton and diphoton final states in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions, using a formalism that accounts for photon emission from individual nucleons within the ion (both elastic and inelastic), Pauli blocking effects, and the survival factor probability of no additional ion-ion inelastic interactions. The calculation is implemented in the SuperChic Monte Carlo generator and compared to ATLAS data on dielectron production, then extended to light-by-light scattering including a new "quark emission" topology and a revisit of QCD-initiated diphoton production.

## Key numbers

- Incoherent to coherent cross section fraction for dielectron production with |y_ee|<0.8, 10 < m_ee < 20 GeV: predicted 0.039 (AnAn), 0.12 (0nXn), 0.17 (XnXn); ATLAS measured 0.043±0.0026, 0.099±0.006, 0.13±0.01 respectively
- Pauli blocking reduces the elastic incoherent cross section by a factor of approximately 3-5
- Incoherent inelastic contribution to light-by-light scattering is expected at the sub-percent level after accounting for kinematic cuts, significantly lower than the background observed by ATLAS and CMS
- The "quark emission" topology for incoherent diphoton production is kinematically suppressed relative to the signal-like incoherent contribution, despite a naive enhancement factor of approximately 1/α²

## Context

This work extends the treatment of photon-initiated production in ultraperipheral collisions from purely coherent processes (where both ions act coherently as photon sources, enhanced by Z⁴) to include incoherent contributions where individual nucleons within the ion emit photons. It provides the first complete theoretical model for comparison to the ATLAS measurement of dielectron production (ATLAS 2022), finding good agreement. The results contradict the suggestion by Klusek-Gawenda (2025) that incoherent photon-initiated production could explain the light-by-light scattering background, and show that QCD-initiated production as calculated in Harland-Lang (2018) also cannot explain the observed background size, leaving the production mechanism for the higher acoplanarity tail in light-by-light scattering as an open question.
