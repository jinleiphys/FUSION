# System Energies for Pentaquark Family Using Thomas-Fermi Quark Model

> This study calculates the system energies of families of pentaquarks, $uudc\bar{c}$, starting from 5 quarks up to 70 using Thomas-Fermi statistical quark model. The model assumes spherical symmetry with the particles continuously distributed with varying densities and boundaries. The particles interact with Coulombic forces and a discontinuity in the light quark density function is energetically favored. Total energies of the system are calculated for each multiquark system and compared to determine family stability characteristics. A remarkable stability for multiquark combinations with multiples of four pentaquarks, which we term an icosaquark, is identified. The first icosaquark system is found to have lowest energy per quark, suggesting stability of a system consisting of four charm, four anticharm and twelve light quarks.

## Key claim

The Thomas-Fermi statistical quark model predicts that multiquark systems composed of multiples of four pentaquarks (termed "icosaquarks") exhibit enhanced stability, with the first icosaquark system (20 quarks: four charm, four anticharm, and twelve light quarks) showing the lowest energy per quark among all systems studied from 5 to 70 quarks.

## Method

The authors applied the Thomas-Fermi quark model to systems of $uudc\bar{c}$ pentaquarks, treating quarks as continuously distributed spherical shells with Coulombic interactions at zero temperature. They divided the analysis into two cases: Case I for a single pentaquark ($\eta = 1$) and Case II for multiple pentaquark pockets ($\eta = 2$ to 14, corresponding to 10 to 70 quarks), with three distinct radial regions for light quarks, charm quarks, and anticharm quarks. Consistency conditions derived from the Thomas-Fermi equations were solved numerically using Mathematica's NDSolve to obtain proportionality constants between the Thomas-Fermi density functions and the boundaries $x_2$, $\bar{x}_2$, and $x_1$ for each system size.

## Key numbers

- The first icosaquark system ($\eta = 4$, 20 quarks) has the lowest energy per quark among all systems studied from $\eta = 1$ to $\eta = 14$ (5 to 70 quarks).
- For $\eta = 1$, the proportionality constants are $\beta_{1\bar{2}} = 0.27530$, $\beta_{2\bar{2}} = 0.96216$, and $\gamma_{12} = 0.39542$.
- For $\eta = 4$, the proportionality constants are $\beta_{12} = 0.22047$, $\beta_{\bar{2}2} = 0.82371$, and $\gamma_{1\bar{2}} = 0.45567$.
- The study covers $\eta$ values from 1 to 14, corresponding to systems of 5 to 70 quarks.

## Context

This work extends the previous Thomas-Fermi quark model study by Giri et al. (2021), which investigated the structure of the single $uudc\bar{c}$ pentaquark system and established that three distinct radial regions are needed to describe the quark distributions. The present paper generalizes that approach to families of pentaquarks and identifies the icosaquark as a particularly stable configuration, which motivates future inclusion of spin degrees of freedom in the Thomas-Fermi model to explore the origin of this stability.
