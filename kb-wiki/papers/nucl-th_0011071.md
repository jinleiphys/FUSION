# Low lying $qqqq\bar q$ states in the baryon spectrum

> The coupling to light mesons leads to large widths and shifts of the energy of the excited states in the baryon spectrum from the predictions of the constituent quark model with three valence quarks. This coupling may be modelled by admixtures of sea-quark $qqq(q\bar q)^n$ configurations in the resonances. A schematic flavor and spin dependent interaction model, similar to the one which reproduces the low lying part of the experimental baryon spectrum in the valence quark model, is shown to bring the lowest $qqqq\bar q$ states with L=1 and positive parity below the states with L=0. Because of the partial overlap with the corresponding $qqq$ states this suggests that most of the low lying states in the baryon resonance spectrum have substantial $qqqq\bar q$ admixtures.

## Key claim

The authors show that a schematic flavor- and spin-dependent interaction model, similar to that used to reproduce the low-lying baryon spectrum with three valence quarks, can bring the lowest $qqqq\bar q$ states with orbital angular momentum $L=1$ and positive parity below the $L=0$ states, suggesting that most low-lying baryon resonances have substantial $qqqq\bar q$ admixtures. The key hedge is that this conclusion depends on the specific choice of the hyperfine interaction parameter $C_{\chi}=21$ MeV and the harmonic oscillator parameter $\omega_0=250$ MeV, and that the ordering of positive and negative parity states for $\Lambda$-, $\Sigma$-, and $\Xi$-like states is very sensitive to the value of $C_{\chi}$.

## Method

The authors construct a translationally invariant harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian for the $qqqq\bar q$ system with a flavor- and spin-dependent hyperfine interaction $H_{\chi}=-C_{\chi}\sum_{i<j}\boldsymbol{\lambda}_i^F\cdot\boldsymbol{\lambda}_j^F \boldsymbol{\sigma}_i\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_j$, where the hyperfine interaction between quarks and antiquarks is taken to be negligible. They classify the eigenstates by their $SU(6)$ flavor-spin symmetry and $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry of the four-quark subsystem, then add the antiquark to obtain states with the quantum numbers of nucleon, $\Delta$, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$, and $\Omega^-$ resonances. The confining constant $C$ is determined from the three-quark oscillator parameter $\omega_0=250$ MeV, giving $\omega=\sqrt{5/6}\,\omega_0\approx 228$ MeV for the $qqqq\bar q$ system, and the hyperfine strength $C_{\chi}=21$ MeV is adjusted to allow $qqqq\bar q$ admixtures in the lowest nucleon resonances.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator parameter for the three-quark baryon system is $\omega_0=250$ MeV, determined from the empirical splitting between the N(1440) resonance and the nucleon.
- The splitting between $L=1$ and $L=0$ bands in the $qqqq\bar q$ system is $\omega=\sqrt{5/6}\,\omega_0\approx 228$ MeV.
- The hyperfine interaction constant is set to $C_{\chi}=21$ MeV.
- The constituent quark mass is taken as $m=340$ MeV and the constant $V_0=-269$ MeV.
- The lowest positive parity $qqqq\bar q$ state for the nucleon, with symmetry $\lbrack 4\rbrack_{FS}\lbrack 22\rbrack_{F}\lbrack 22\rbrack_{S}$, has an energy of 1365 MeV, matching the real part of the pole position of the N(1440) resonance.
- The lowest positive parity $qqqq\bar q$ state for the $\Lambda$ hyperon, with the same symmetry, has an energy of 1485 MeV.
- The lowest positive parity $qqqq\bar q$ state for the $\Sigma$ hyperon, with the same symmetry, also has an energy of 1485 MeV.
- The $\Delta(1620)$, ${1\over2}^-$ resonance has a predicted $qqqq\bar q$ admixture from a $\lbrack 31\rbrack_{FS}\lbrack 31\rbrack_{F}\lbrack 31\rbrack_{S}$ multiplet at 1613 MeV.
- The $\Lambda(1405)$, ${1\over2}^-$ state is suggested to have a $qqqq\bar q$ component from a low-lying flavor singlet state.
- The difference between strange and light constituent quark masses is taken as $\delta m=m_s-m_u=120$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model by incorporating sea-quark $qqqq\bar q$ configurations to model the coupling to mesonic channels, which leads to large widths and energy shifts of baryon resonances. It builds on the earlier observation by Jaffe (1977) that $(q\bar q)^2$ states in the meson sector have energies close to excited $q\bar q$ states, and on the bound state version of the topological soliton model (Skyrme model) as developed by Blom et al. (1998) and Hor et al. (1999), which predicts similar spectra for strange hyperons. The results enable a systematic interpretation of baryon resonances as having significant five-quark admixtures, particularly for states like the N(1440), N(1535), $\Delta(1600)$, $\Lambda(1405)$, and $\Lambda(1520)$, and provide a framework for understanding the large widths and peculiar decay patterns (e.g., strong $\eta$ decays) of these resonances.
