# Spontaneous parity and charge-conjugation violations at real isospin and imaginary baryon chemical potentials

> The phase structure of two-flavor QCD is investigated at real isospin and imaginary quark chemical potentials by using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. In the region, parity symmetry is spontaneously broken by the pion superfluidity phase transition, whereas charge-conjugation symmetry is spontaneously violated by the Roberge-Weiss transition. The chiral (deconfinement) crossover at zero isospin and quark chemical potentials is a remnant of the parity (charge-conjugation) violation. The interplay between the parity and charge-conjugation violations are analyzed, and it is investigated how the interplay is related to the correlation between the chiral and deconfinement crossovers at zero isospin and quark chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The chiral and deconfinement crossovers at zero isospin and quark chemical potentials are remnants of spontaneous parity violation (from pion superfluidity) and spontaneous charge-conjugation violation (from the Roberge-Weiss transition), respectively, and the correlation between these two exact phase transitions at finite isospin and imaginary quark chemical potentials underlies the coincidence of the chiral and deconfinement crossovers at zero chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors used the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and its extension, the entanglement PNJL (EPNJL) model, to study two-flavor QCD at real isospin chemical potential and imaginary quark chemical potential. They solved the mean-field thermodynamic potential under stationary conditions for the chiral condensate, pion condensate, Polyakov loop, and its conjugate, and analyzed the phase structure as functions of temperature, isospin chemical potential, and the imaginary quark chemical potential parameter Theta.

## Key numbers

- The PNJL model yields pseudocritical temperatures of T_sigma = 216 MeV for chiral restoration and T_Phi = 173 MeV for deconfinement at zero chemical potentials, when defined by peak positions.
- The EPNJL model yields coincident pseudocritical temperatures T_sigma = T_Phi = 173 MeV at zero chemical potentials.
- The NJL sector parameters reproduce the pion decay constant f_pi = 93.3 MeV and the pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV at vacuum.
- The Polyakov potential parameter T_0 is set to 212 MeV in the PNJL model and to 190 MeV in the EPNJL model.
- The entanglement vertex parameters are alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.2 in the EPNJL model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the EPNJL model (Sakai 2011) which showed that chiral and deconfinement crossovers coincide at zero chemical potentials, and their earlier finding (Kouno 2011) that parity restoration and charge-conjugation violation coincide at theta = Theta = pi. The paper enables future lattice QCD checks of the model predictions, since lattice QCD has no sign problem at real isospin and imaginary quark chemical potentials, unlike at finite theta. The analysis connects the exact phase transitions (pion superfluidity and Roberge-Weiss) to the crossover phenomena observed at zero chemical potentials, addressing the debate on whether chiral and deconfinement transitions coincide (Borsanyi 2010).
