# The critical temperature of nuclear matter and fragment distributions in multifragmentation of finite nuclei

> The fragment production in multifragmentation of finite nuclei is affected by the critical temperature of nuclear matter. We show that this temperature can be determined on the basis of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) by analyzing the evolution of fragment distributions with the excitation energy. This method can reveal a decrease of the critical temperature that, e.g., is expected for neutron-rich matter. The influence of isospin on fragment distributions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of nuclear matter can be determined by analyzing the evolution of fragment mass distributions with excitation energy within the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), specifically by finding the minimum of the power-law exponent τ; however, the extracted value depends on the isospin of the source and the method is most sensitive for low critical temperatures.

## Method

The authors performed SMM calculations for a gold nucleus (A₀=197, Z₀=79) at a freeze-out density of one-third normal nuclear density, varying the critical temperature T_c as a free parameter (10, 18, and 30 MeV). They extracted the power-law exponent τ from the mass distribution of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, A=5-40) and plotted its evolution with excitation energy, identifying the minimum τ (τ_min) and the corresponding temperature (T_min). Additional calculations were performed for ¹²⁴Sn and ¹²⁴La nuclei to study isospin effects.

## Key numbers

- Standard critical temperature implemented in SMM: T_c = 18 MeV, consistent with many theoretical studies.
- For T_c = 18 MeV, the minimum τ parameter occurs at excitation energy around 5-6 MeV and temperature around 5-6 MeV.
- For T_c = 10 MeV and 30 MeV, the values of τ_min and T_min are essentially different from the T_c = 18 MeV case.
- Experimental break-up excitation energies extracted from Au reactions vary within 3.8 to 4.5 MeV/nucleon, with τ in the range from 2.12 to 2.18.
- Interpolation of SMM calculations for Au sources fits experimental values if T_c is in the range between 18 and 22 MeV.
- τ_min is lower than the extracted experimental τ by around 15%.
- A small critical exponent τ ≈ 1.88 is reported for multifragmentation of Kr nuclei (lower N/Z ratio than Au).
- A critical temperature T_c ≈ 16.6 MeV was extracted from analysis of break-up temperatures in another study (Natowitz).
- A temperature T_c ≈ 20 MeV was obtained in the experiment of Karnaukhov.

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of Bondorf et al. (1995) by explicitly using the surface tension dependence on T/T_c to extract the nuclear matter critical temperature from finite-nuclei data. It contrasts with other multifragmentation models (e.g., Gross, Randrup) that do not incorporate nuclear matter properties explicitly. The method enables future experiments with radioactive beams to investigate the predicted decrease of T_c for neutron-rich matter, relevant for supernova explosions and neutron star formation.
