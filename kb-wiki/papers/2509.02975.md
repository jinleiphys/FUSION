# Lee--Yang edge singularities in Nonlocal Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> We investigate the QCD phase diagram and the associated Lee--Yang edge singularities using the two-flavor nonlocal Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model extended to complex chemical potential. There exists a strong correlation between the chiral phase transition and the structure of the effective potential in the complex order parameter plane, serving as a criterion to differentiate crossover from first-order transitions. Typically, the Lee--Yang edge singularities can be understood as a generalization of the critical end-point (CEP) between crossover and first-order transitions, where the positive Nambu phase and the Wigner phase coalesce. We further analyze the scaling behavior near the CEP by extracting the critical exponent associated with the Lee--Yang singularities. Additionally, we confirm that the extrapolation of the Lee--Yang edge singularity trajectories provides an effective method of determining the CEP location, even at a small real chemical potential. This provides a viable method for exploring regions of the QCD phase diagram that remain inaccessible to lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The Lee-Yang edge singularities (LYEs) can be understood as a generalization of the critical end-point (CEP) where the positive Nambu phase and the Wigner phase coalesce, and the extrapolation of LYE trajectories provides an effective method for determining the CEP location even at small real chemical potential, contingent on the analytic continuation of the thermodynamic potential into the complex chemical potential plane.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nNJL) model with an exponential form factor (FA scheme) and mean-field approximation, extending the thermodynamic potential to complex chemical potential. They analyzed the structure of the effective potential in the complex order parameter plane by tracking the solutions of the real and imaginary parts of the gap equation, identifying shape-shifting lines that separate different potential surface topologies. The critical exponent associated with the Lee-Yang singularities was extracted from the scaling behavior near the CEP.

## Key numbers

- CEP location: T_CEP = 90.10 MeV, mu_q,CEP = 188.73 MeV
- Crossover curvature parameter: kappa = 0.01708(2), consistent with prior 2-flavor DSE results of kappa = 0.0175(7) (Gao 2020) and kappa = 0.0160(1) (Pawlowski 2014)
- Model parameters: G*Lambda^2 = 20.65, m_q = 5.74 MeV, Lambda = 752 MeV
- Pseudo-critical temperature at vanishing chemical potential: T_c(0) = 127.7 MeV
- At T = 80 MeV and mu = 200 MeV, the positive Nambu solution appears at sigma = 399.7 MeV; first-order phase transition occurs at mu = 216.45 MeV; the positive Nambu solution vanishes at mu = 226.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of Lee-Yang edge singularities from lattice QCD approaches (such as those by Clarke 2023 and Schmidt 2025) and effective model studies (Basar 2021, Wan 2024) to the nonlocal NJL framework, which incorporates momentum-dependent quark interactions motivated by Dyson-Schwinger equations and lattice QCD results (Parappilly 2006). The proposed shape-shifting line method for locating LYEs and the CEP provides a complementary approach to Taylor expansion and resummation techniques (Mondal 2022), and could enable exploration of QCD phase diagram regions inaccessible to lattice QCD due to the sign problem (de Forcrand 2002, Aarts 2009).
