# Parton coalescence and antiproton/pion anomaly at RHIC

> Coalescence of minijet partons with the partons from the quark-gluon plasma formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions is suggested as the mechanism for production of hadrons with intermediate transverse momentum. The resulting enhanced antiproton and pion yields at intermediate transverse momentum gives a plausible explanation for the observed large antiproton to pion ratio. With further increasing momentum, the ratio is predicted to decrease and approach the small value given by independent fragmentations of minijet partons after their energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Coalescence of minijet partons with thermal partons from the quark-gluon plasma can explain the observed large antiproton-to-pion ratio at intermediate transverse momentum (around 2–4 GeV/c) in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but the ratio is predicted to decrease at higher transverse momentum and approach the small value from independent fragmentation. The agreement with experimental data depends on the assumed inverse slope parameter for intermediate-transverse-momentum thermal antiprotons.

## Method

The authors used a coalescence model to compute hadron production from recombination of minijet partons (from perturbative QCD with energy loss) with thermal partons from a quark-gluon plasma at temperature T=180 MeV. Minijet partons with transverse momentum above 1.75 GeV/c were taken from an improved pQCD calculation with effective opacity L/λ=3.5, while QGP partons below that cutoff were treated as thermal. The coalescence integrals for pions (quark+antiquark) and antiprotons (three antiquarks) were evaluated using uniform spatial distributions and a momentum-space cutoff Δp=0.24 GeV/c, with gluons converted to quark-antiquark pairs via the ALCOR model.

## Key numbers

- Central Au+Au collisions at √s=200 AGeV, 0–10% centrality
- Minijet parton momentum cutoff: p0=1.75 GeV/c
- QGP temperature: T=180 MeV; QGP volume: V=730 fm³
- Total transverse energy per unit rapidity from thermal and minijet partons: about 570 GeV
- Minijet partons contribute only about 10% of the total transverse energy
- Coalescence enhances intermediate-transverse-momentum pion yields by a factor of about 1.7 and antiproton yields by a factor of about 2.2
- Inverse slope parameters for thermal hadrons: 240 MeV for pions, 340 MeV for antiprotons (low pT); alternative values of 240 MeV and 300 MeV explored for intermediate-pT antiprotons
- Momentum cutoff in coalescence: Δp=0.24 GeV/c, corresponding to pion size Δx≈0.85 fm
- Probability factors: gπ+=1/36 for π⁺; g\bar p=1/108 for antiproton

## Context

This work extends the coalescence/recombination approach previously used in the ALCOR model (ALCOR), MICOR model (MICOR), and the AMPT model (AMPT) to describe hadronization of partonic matter. It specifically builds on the idea of coalescence of hard minijet partons with soft QGP partons introduced by Lin (Lin) to predict flavor ordering of elliptic flow. The paper addresses the antiproton/pion anomaly observed by PHENIX (ppi), which had previously been attributed to baryon junctions by Vitev (Vitev), to parton recombination by Hwa (Hwa), and to recombination of QGP partons with high effective temperature by Fries (Fries). The mechanism predicts that the antiproton-to-pion ratio will decrease at high transverse momentum, providing a testable signature that would further support the formation of quark-gluon plasma.
