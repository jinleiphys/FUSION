# Trajectory of neutron$-$neutron$-^{18}C$ excited three-body state

> The trajectory of the first excited Efimov state is investigated by using a renormalized zero-range three-body model for a system with two bound and one virtual two-body subsystems. The approach is applied to $n-n-^{18}$C, where the $n-n$ virtual energy and the three-body ground state are kept fixed. It is shown that such three-body excited state goes from a bound to a virtual state when the $n-^{18}$C binding energy is increased. Results obtained for the $n-^{19}$C elastic cross-section at low energies also show dominance of an $S-$matrix pole corresponding to a bound or virtual Efimov state. It is also presented a brief discussion of these findings in the context of ultracold atom physics with tunable scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The first excited Efimov state of the neutron-neutron-¹⁸C system transitions from a bound state to a virtual state (not a resonance) as the neutron-¹⁸C binding energy is increased, consistent with the behavior previously established for three equal-mass particles, contrary to a suggestion in earlier work that a resonance might appear.

## Method

The authors used a renormalized zero-range three-body model with coupled spectator-function equations for the n-n-¹⁸C system, fixing the n-n virtual energy at -143 keV and the ²⁰C ground-state energy at -3.5 MeV. They analytically continued the bound-state equations to the second Riemann sheet through the n-¹⁹C elastic scattering cut to locate virtual-state poles, and independently solved the scattering equations to find poles of the S-matrix in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- The n-n virtual state energy is fixed at -143 keV.
- The three-body ground-state energy of ²⁰C is fixed at -3.5 MeV.
- The n-¹⁸C binding energy of 160 ± 110 keV is the experimental value for ¹⁹C.
- Three-body bound states occur when |E_¹⁹C| is approximately smaller than 170 keV; virtual states occur when |E_¹⁹C| is larger than 170 keV.
- For n-¹⁸C bound at -150 keV, the excited Efimov bound state has energy E* = -150.12 keV relative to the n-¹⁸C threshold.
- For n-¹⁸C bound at -180 keV, the virtual state has energy E* = -180.12 keV relative to the n-¹⁸C threshold.
- For n-¹⁸C bound at -500 keV, the ²⁰C virtual energy is E* = -568.73 keV.
- The effective range expansion gives σ(k_i) ≈ 2741 keV / (E(k_i) - E*) barn for the n-¹⁹C elastic cross-section near threshold.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Efimov-state trajectories from equal-mass systems, studied by Adhikari et al. (2000) using the Amado model, to the unequal-mass halo-nucleus system n-n-¹⁸C. It contradicts a suggestion by Amorim et al. (2004) that a resonance might appear near threshold in ²⁰C, showing instead that the excited Efimov state becomes virtual, consistent with the equal-mass case. The results enable predictions for low-energy n-¹⁹C elastic scattering cross-sections and suggest observable effects in ultracold atom experiments with tunable scattering lengths, where the crossing of an Efimov state from bound to virtual could dramatically affect atom-dimer scattering and condensate stability.
