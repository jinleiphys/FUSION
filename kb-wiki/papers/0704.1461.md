# Trajectory of neutron$-$neutron$-^{18}C$ excited three-body state

> The trajectory of the first excited Efimov state is investigated by using a renormalized zero-range three-body model for a system with two bound and one virtual two-body subsystems. The approach is applied to $n-n-^{18}$C, where the $n-n$ virtual energy and the three-body ground state are kept fixed. It is shown that such three-body excited state goes from a bound to a virtual state when the $n-^{18}$C binding energy is increased. Results obtained for the $n-^{19}$C elastic cross-section at low energies also show dominance of an $S-$matrix pole corresponding to a bound or virtual Efimov state. It is also presented a brief discussion of these findings in the context of ultracold atom physics with tunable scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors used a renormalized zero-range three-body model with coupled spectator-function equations for the n-n-¬Ļ‚ĀłC system, fixing the n-n virtual energy at -143 keV and the ¬≤‚ĀįC ground-state energy at -3.5 MeV. They analytically continued the bound-state equations to the second Riemann sheet through the n-¬Ļ‚ĀĻC elastic scattering cut to locate virtual-state poles, and independently solved the scattering equations to find poles of the S-matrix in the complex energy plane.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Efimov-state trajectories from equal-mass systems, studied by Adhikari et al. (2000) using the Amado model, to the unequal-mass halo-nucleus system n-n-¬Ļ‚ĀłC. It contradicts a suggestion by Amorim et al. (2004) that a resonance might appear near threshold in ¬≤‚ĀįC, showing instead that the excited Efimov state becomes virtual, consistent with the equal-mass case. The results enable predictions for low-energy n-¬Ļ‚ĀĻC elastic scattering cross-sections and suggest observable effects in ultracold atom experiments with tunable scattering lengths, where the crossing of an Efimov state from bound to virtual could dramatically affect atom-dimer scattering and condensate stability.
