# Chiral corrections in hadron spectroscopy

> We show that the implementation of chiral symmetry in recent studies of the hadron spectrum in the context of the constituent quark model is inconsistent with chiral perturbation theory. In particular, we show that the leading nonanalytic (LNA) contributions to the hadron masses are incorrect in such approaches. The failure to implement the correct chiral behaviour of QCD results in incorrect systematics for the corrections to the masses.

## Key claim

The implementation of chiral symmetry via Goldstone boson exchange in the constituent quark model of Glozman and Riska yields incorrect leading nonanalytic (LNA) contributions to hadron masses, particularly for the Δ baryon, and therefore the systematics of the mass corrections are wrong.

## Method

The authors compare the spin-isospin matrix elements of the pion-exchange interaction used by Glozman and Riska with those required by chiral perturbation theory (χPT) for the nucleon and Δ(1232). They compute the LNA contributions arising from the linear term in the expansion of the Yukawa potential and contrast these with the LNA contributions from one-loop pion self-energy diagrams in χPT, including explicit Δ degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleon, the spin-isospin matrix element from the Glozman-Riska interaction is 30, while χPT requires 25.
- For the Δ, the spin-isospin matrix element from the Glozman-Riska interaction is 6, while χPT requires 25.
- In the limit where ΔM = 0, the ratio of total N and Δ self-energies from the correct projection method is 57 : 33.
- The LNA contribution to the nucleon mass in χPT is given by M_N^{LNA} = - (3 / (32 π f_π^2)) g_A^2 m_π^3, with f_π ~ 93 MeV and g_A = 1.26.

## Context

This paper extends the critique of the Glozman-Riska approach (Glozman, Riska and collaborators, late 1990s) to hadron spectroscopy, which replaced one-gluon-exchange with Goldstone boson exchange. The work builds on earlier chiral bag model calculations (Jaffe, 1970s; Mulders and Thomas, 1980s; Cloudy Bag Model) and large N_c χPT (Dashen and Manohar, 1990s). It enables a correct re-analysis of hadron masses that includes pion exchange between all quarks with projection onto bare baryon states, as in the Cloudy Bag Model.
