# Cross sections of removal reactions populating weakly-bound residual nuclei

> In many instances, single nucleon removal reactions from neutron-proton asymmetric projectile nuclei populate final states in the residual nuclei that are very weakly bound. Familiar examples include neutron removal reactions from neutron-rich $^{11}$Be and $^{12}$Be, the latter populating the well-known $1/2^+$ halo ground-state and $1/2^-$ excited-state of $^{11}$Be - both states less than 1 MeV from the first neutron-decay threshold. Numerous additional examples arise in reactions of asymmetric $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei. The importance of this weak residue binding upon calculated single-nucleon removal reaction cross sections is quantified by means of model calculations that neglect or include the dissociation degree of freedom of the residual nuclei. The calculated removal-reaction cross sections for two representative $p$-shell projectiles indicate that an explicit treatment of these residue break-up effects is unnecessary and that the differences between the break-up and no break-up calculations are small provided a consistent description of the residue structure and density is used.

## Key claim

The paper argues that explicitly including the break-up degree of freedom of weakly-bound residual nuclei in single-nucleon removal reaction calculations changes the computed cross sections by only 0.3–0.4%, provided the no-break-up calculations use a consistent description of the residue structure and density; this contradicts earlier suggestions that break-up effects would cause significant reductions.

## Method

The author performed eikonal-model calculations for single-nucleon removal reactions at 100 MeV/nucleon on a ⁹Be target, comparing two approaches for the residue-target S-matrix: (i) a static-density optical limit (OL) calculation using the residue one-body density, and (ii) a few-body calculation that explicitly includes the two-body (core + proton) cluster structure and break-up of the weakly-bound residues ⁸B and ⁹C. The residues were described as 1p₃/₂ Woods-Saxon eigenstates with their experimental separation energies, and the core and target densities were taken as Gaussians with rms radii from systematics.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹C(−p) at 100 MeV/nucleon on ⁹Be: few-body (including break-up) single-particle cross section = 61.25 mb; density-based (neglecting break-up) = 61.47 mb; a difference of 0.3–0.4%.
- For ¹⁰C(−n) at 100 MeV/nucleon on ⁹Be: few-body single-particle cross section = 21.44 mb; density-based = 21.51 mb; a difference of 0.3–0.4%.
- The ⁸B residue has a proton separation energy of 0.137 MeV; the ⁹C residue has a proton separation energy of 1.3 MeV.
- The ⁹C(−p) reaction removes a weakly-bound proton with Sₚ = 1.3 MeV; the ¹⁰C(−n) reaction removes a strongly-bound neutron with Sₙ = 21.3 MeV.
- Few-body and density-based residue-target reaction cross sections for ⁸B on ⁹Be: 851.8 mb and 856.1 mb, respectively.
- Few-body and density-based residue-target reaction cross sections for ⁹C on ⁹Be: 866.2 mb and 868.0 mb, respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier higher-energy analyses by Al-Khalili and Tostevin (1996) and Al-Khalili, Tostevin, and Thompson (1996) that showed few-body effects reduce reaction cross sections for extended systems, and it directly addresses an early suggestion by Lewes (year not given) that break-up channels would increase absorption and reduce removal cross sections. The paper enables future removal-reaction analyses to safely use standard density-based optical-limit calculations for weakly-bound residues, provided the residue density is consistently computed, without needing explicit few-body treatments.
