# A new approach to the complex-action problem and its application to a nonperturbative study of superstring theory

> Monte Carlo simulations of a system whose action has an imaginary part are considered to be extremely difficult. We propose a new approach to this `complex-action problem', which utilizes a factorization property of distribution functions. The basic idea is quite general, and it removes the so-called overlap problem completely. Here we apply the method to a nonperturbative study of superstring theory using its matrix formulation. In this particular example, the distribution function turns out to be positive definite, which allows us to reduce the problem even further. Our numerical results suggest an intuitive explanation for the dynamical generation of 4d space-time.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new "factorization method" for Monte Carlo simulations of systems with a complex action that completely removes the so-called overlap problem, but the authors acknowledge that the method does not solve the complex-action problem entirely because determining the correction factor becomes increasingly difficult as system size increases.

## Method

The method uses a factorization property of distribution functions: the full distribution function ρ_i(x) for an observable factorizes as ρ_i(x) = (1/C) ρ_i^(0)(x) w_i(x), where ρ_i^(0)(x) is the distribution from the model omitting the imaginary part of the action and w_i(x) is a correction factor obtained by constrained Monte Carlo simulations. The correction factor w_i(x) is evaluated by simulating a system with a harmonic potential V(λ_i) = (1/2)γ(λ_i - ξ)^2 that forces the simulation to sample the important region for the full model, thereby eliminating the overlap problem. For the specific application to the type IIB matrix model in D=6, the authors use a low-energy effective theory to reduce computational effort from O(N^6) to O(N^3).

## Key numbers

- For D=6, the model without the phase was studied up to N=768; for D=10, up to N=512 using the low-energy effective theory
- The correction factor w_i(x) for fixed x decreases as e^{-α N^2} at large N, where α may depend on x
- For the 4th eigenvalue at N=128, the peak at x>1 is dominant with area A_l ~ 4.5 × 10^{-3} compared to A_s ~ 5.0 × 10^{-4} for the peak at x<1
- For the 5th eigenvalue at N=128, the two peaks have comparable areas: A_s ~ 2.0 × 10^{-3} and A_l ~ 3.8 × 10^{-3}
- The function (1/N) f_i^(0)(x) shows scaling behavior for N=16, 32, 64, 128

## Context

This work extends the study of the type IIB matrix model proposed by Ishibashi, Kawai, Kitazawa and Tsuchiya (1997) as a nonperturbative definition of superstring theory. Previous work by Ambjorn et al. (2000) and Nishimura and Vernizzi (2000) studied the model omitting the phase and found isotropic space-time, while the phase was shown to play a crucial role in dynamical reduction of space-time dimensionality. The method enables future studies of complex-action systems in finite density QCD, Chern-Simons theories, and systems with topological terms, and the authors report successful application to a Random Matrix Theory for finite density QCD in a separate paper.
