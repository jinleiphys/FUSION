# Coupled-channel effects in hadron-hadron correlation functions

> Two-particle momentum correlation functions as measured in heavy ion collisions or in high-energetic proton-proton collisions are studied. Special emphasis is put on systems like $ΛΛ$ or $K^-p$ where effects from the coupling to other channels could be relevant. In both cases other channels open at relatively low momenta or are already open at the reaction threshold. To have a solid basis, realistic coupled-channel interactions for $ΛΛ-ΞN-ΛΣ-ΣΣ$ and $πΛ-πΣ-\bar KN$ are utilized in the actual calculations. It is found that the opening of the $ΞN$ channel leaves a trace in the $ΛΛ$ correlation function that could be detectable in experiments. Should the proposed $H$-dibaryon be located close to or below the $ΞN$ it will have a very pronounced effect. The presence of open channels in systems like $Ξ^- p$ or $K^-p$ does influence the correlation functions significantly at low momenta and will certainly complicate any dedicated analysis.

## Key claim

Coupled-channel effects, specifically the opening of the $\Xi N$ channel approximately 25 MeV above the $\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold, leave a detectable trace in the $\Lambda\Lambda$ correlation function; however, the influence of these open channels is significant only at low momenta and will complicate any dedicated analysis, particularly for systems like $\Xi^- p$ or $K^-p$ where channels are already open at the reaction threshold.

## Method

The author computed two-particle momentum correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formalism, solving the Schrodinger equation with realistic coupled-channel interactions derived from SU(3) chiral effective field theory for the $\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi N-\Lambda\Sigma-\Sigma\Sigma$ system and from a meson-exchange potential for the $\pi\Lambda-\pi\Sigma-\bar K N$ system. Wave functions for all coupled channels were obtained from half-off-shell T-matrices via Fourier-Bessel transforms, and the correlation functions were evaluated assuming a static spherical Gaussian source with radius R = 1.2 fm, with physical masses used throughout to account for threshold splittings such as $\Xi^0 n$ versus $\Xi^- p$ and $K^- p$ versus $\bar K^0 n$.

## Key numbers

* $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length $a_0 = -0.66$ fm and effective range $r_0 = 5.05$ fm for the NLO (600) chiral interaction in the $^1S_0$ partial wave.
* The $\Xi N$ channel threshold lies approximately 23 MeV ($\Xi^0 n$) and 28 MeV ($\Xi^- p$) above the $\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold, corresponding to $\Lambda\Lambda$ c.m. momenta of about 161 MeV/c and 178 MeV/c, respectively.
* The $\Sigma N$ channel threshold lies about 77 MeV above the $\Lambda p$ threshold.
* The $\bar K^0 n$ threshold lies about 5 MeV above the $K^- p$ threshold.
* The $\Xi N$ scattering length in the $^1S_0$ $I=0$ channel is $a = -10.89 - i\,14.91$ fm; in the $^1S_0$ $I=1$ channel $a = 0.34$ fm with $r = -7.07$ fm.
* The $\Sigma\Sigma$ scattering length in the $^1S_0$ $I=2$ channel is $a = -1.83$ fm with $r = 6.05$ fm.
* The $\Xi N$ $^3S_1$ $I=0$ scattering length is $a = -0.62$ fm with $r = 1.00$ fm; the $^3S_1$ $I=1$ scattering length is $a = 0.02$ fm with $r = 1797$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for two-particle momentum correlations, previously applied to single-channel systems like $pp$ and $K^+p$ by Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1982) and others, to explicitly include coupled-channel effects in systems where inelastic thresholds lie close to the energy region of interest. The calculations use realistic interactions from chiral effective field theory by Haidenbauer et al. (2013, 2016) and meson-exchange potentials by the Julich group (1992, 2011), enabling predictions for ongoing and planned measurements of $\Lambda\Lambda$ correlations by ALICE and STAR collaborations and $\Xi^- p$ correlations by the HADES collaboration. The results demonstrate that the presence of an $H$-dibaryon bound state near the $\Xi N$ threshold would produce a dramatic enhancement in the $\Lambda\Lambda$ correlation function that is ruled out by existing data, and that measurements of $\Xi^- p$ correlations could distinguish between weakly attractive and strongly attractive $\Xi N$ interactions.
