# Mass hierarchy in identified particle distributions in proton-lead collisions

> We study the mass dependence for identified-particle average transverse momentum and harmonic flow coefficients in proton-lead (p-Pb) collisions, recently measured at the LHC. The collective mechanism in the p-Pb system predicts a specific mass ordering in these observables: the growth of the average transverse momentum with the particle mass and a mass splitting of the elliptic flow coefficient, i.e., smaller differential elliptic flow of protons than pions for p_T<2 GeV. This provides an opportunity to distinguish between the collective scenario and the mechanism based on the initial gluon dynamics in the evolution of the p-Pb system.

## Key claim

The collective hydrodynamic mechanism in p-Pb collisions predicts a specific mass ordering in identified-particle observables (growth of average transverse momentum with particle mass and mass splitting of elliptic flow), which can distinguish this scenario from alternative explanations based on initial gluon dynamics; however, the hydrodynamic description is quantitatively reliable only for the most central collisions where the system is large enough to sustain collective expansion.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with shear viscosity η/s=0.08 and hydro ignition time τ=0.6 fm/c, using Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions with a compact source prescription. Statistical hadronization via the Cooper-Frye formula was applied at a constant freeze-out temperature T_f=150 MeV, and the results were compared to LHC data on identified-particle average transverse momentum, elliptic flow v_2, and triangular flow v_3 for pions, kaons, and protons.

## Key numbers

- Average transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ for identified particles shows mass hierarchy (protons > kaons > pions) in hydrodynamic calculations, matching experimental data from Abelev (2013) across centrality ranges 0-60%
- Elliptic flow v_2 for charged particles in central collisions (N_part ≥ 18, 0-3% centrality): model gives 0.057 vs CMS value 0.063 at ⟨N_trk⟩=154.5
- Triangular flow v_3 for charged particles in central collisions: model gives 0.023 vs CMS value 0.02 at ⟨N_trk⟩=154.5
- For peripheral collisions (⟨N_trk⟩=45), model v_2=0.061 vs CMS 0.043, and model v_3=0.020 vs CMS 0.006, showing breakdown of hydrodynamic description
- Freeze-out temperature T_f=150 MeV reproduces the mass splitting magnitude in p-Pb, higher than the lower freeze-out temperature used in A-A collisions
- Mass splitting of v_2 for identified particles is observed for p_T < 2 GeV, with smaller differential elliptic flow of protons than pions
- Triangular flow v_3 shows inverted mass ordering for p_T < 500 MeV due to resonance decays

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach previously applied to p-Pb collisions by Bozek (2011) and Bozek (2012) to identified-particle observables, directly addressing the challenge posed by Bzdak and Skokov (2013) that p-Pb average transverse momentum cannot be explained by superposition of p-p production. The results contradict the HIJING model (Deng 2010) which has no collective flow and cannot reproduce the measured mass splitting. The paper enables experimental discrimination between the collective hydrodynamic scenario and the alternative gluon saturation/partonic dynamics approach of Kovner (2010) and Dusling (2012), with the key prediction being the mass splitting pattern in identified-particle elliptic and triangular flow coefficients.
