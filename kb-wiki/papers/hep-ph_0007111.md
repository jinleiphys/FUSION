# Photoproduction of Charmonia and Total Charmonium-Proton Cross Sections

> Elastic virtual photoproduction cross sections gamma^*p -> J/psi(psi')p and total charmonium-nucleon cross sections for J/psi, psi' and chi states are calculated in a parameter free way with the light-cone dipole formalism and the same input: factorization in impact parameters, light-cone wave functions for the gamma^* and the charmonia, and the universal phenomenological dipole cross section which is fitted to other data. The charmonium wave functions are calculated with four known realistic potentials, and two models for the dipole cross section are tested. Very good agreement with data for the cross section of charmonium photoproduction is found in a wide range of s and Q^2. The inclusion of the Melosh spin rotation increases the psi' photoproduction rate by a factor 2-3 and removes previously observed discrepancies in the psi' to J/psi ratio in photoproduction. We also calculate the charmonium-proton cross sections whose absolute values and energy dependences are found to correlate strongly with the sizes of the states.

## Key claim

The authors calculate elastic photoproduction cross sections and total charmonium-nucleon cross sections in a parameter-free manner using the light-cone dipole formalism, but the results depend on which of four realistic potentials and two dipole cross section models are chosen; the best agreement with data requires potentials with charm quark mass near 1.5 GeV (BT and LOG) rather than 1.8 GeV (COR and POW), and the inclusion of Melosh spin rotation increases the psi' photoproduction rate by a factor of 2-3, resolving previous discrepancies in the psi' to J/psi ratio.

## Method

The authors use the light-cone dipole formalism with factorization in impact parameters, where the forward amplitudes for gamma*p -> J/psi(psi')p and psi-p elastic scattering are expressed as integrals over the transverse separation r_T of the c-cbar pair, weighted by the photon and charmonium light-cone wave functions and the universal phenomenological dipole cross section sigma_qqbar(r_T,s). Charmonium wave functions are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with four known potentials (Cornell, Buchmuller-Tye, logarithmic, and power-law), then boosted to the infinite momentum frame using a standard procedure that includes the Melosh spin rotation. Two parameterizations of the dipole cross section (GBW and KST) are tested, and the real part of the amplitude is included via the derivative analyticity relation.

## Key numbers

- J/psi photoproduction cross section at sqrt(s)=90 GeV with BT potential and GBW dipole: 52.01 nb (37.77 nb without spin rotation)
- J/psi photoproduction cross section at sqrt(s)=90 GeV with COR potential and GBW dipole: 23.13 nb (17.07 nb without spin rotation)
- Ratio R = sigma(psi')/sigma(J/psi) at sqrt(s)=90 GeV with BT potential and GBW dipole: 0.147 (0.075 without spin rotation)
- Ratio R = sigma(psi')/sigma(J/psi) at sqrt(s)=90 GeV with COR potential and GBW dipole: 0.168 (0.099 without spin rotation)
- Slope parameter B = 4.73 GeV^{-2} used for the photoproduction cross section formula
- GBW dipole cross section saturates at 23.03 mb for large r_T
- KST dipole cross section has sigma_0(s) = 23.6 (s/s_0)^{0.08} (1 + 3/8 * r_0^2(s)/<r^2_ch>) mb with s_0 = 1000 GeV^2 and <r^2_ch> = 0.44 fm^2
- Charm quark masses used: m_c = 1.84 GeV (Cornell), 1.48 GeV (Buchmuller-Tye), 1.5 GeV (logarithmic), 1.8 GeV (power-law)

## Context

This work extends the light-cone dipole approach previously applied by Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1991) and by Hoyer et al. (1988) to charmonium photoproduction, but improves upon those calculations by using realistic charmonium wave functions from four different potentials and by including the Melosh spin rotation that had been overlooked in earlier work. The authors also compare with the multichannel analysis of Hufner and Kopeliovich (1998) which showed that vector dominance model predictions for charmonium-nucleon cross sections are unreliable. The calculations enable extraction of total charmonium-proton cross sections from photoproduction data, which are needed for interpreting charmonium suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions as a probe of quark-gluon plasma formation.
