# Long-Range Interactions of Small Color Dipoles

> We study the scattering of small color dipoles (e.g., heavy quarkonium states) at low energies. We find that even though the couplings of color dipoles to the gluon field can be described in perturbation theory, at large distances the interaction becomes totally non--perturbative. The structure of the scattering amplitude, however, is fixed by the (broken) chiral and scale symmetries of QCD; the leading long--distance contribution arises from the correlated two--pion exchange. We use the spectral representation technique to evaluate both perturbative and non-perturbative contributions to the scattering amplitude. Our main result is the sum rule which relates the overall strength of the non--perturbative interaction between color dipoles to the energy density of QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

Even though the couplings of small color dipoles (such as heavy quarkonium) to the gluon field can be described in perturbation theory, at large distances the interaction becomes totally non-perturbative; the leading long-distance contribution arises from correlated two-pion exchange, and the overall strength of the non-perturbative interaction is related to the energy density of the QCD vacuum via a sum rule.

## Method

The authors use the Wilson operator product expansion to write the scattering amplitude of two small color dipoles in terms of the correlator of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. They then employ a spectral representation technique to evaluate both perturbative (two-gluon exchange) and non-perturbative (two-pion exchange) contributions to the scattering amplitude. The non-perturbative contribution is computed using low-energy theorems from broken scale and chiral symmetries, with the pion scalar form factor obtained from experimental ππ phase shifts via the Omnes-Muskhelishvili equation.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative potential between two color dipoles is proportional to \(R^{-7}\), with the explicit result \(V(R) = - g^4 (\bar d_2 a_0^2/\epsilon_0)^2 (23)/(8\pi^3) (1/R^7)\).
- The vacuum energy density of QCD is \(\epsilon_{\rm vac} = (1/4) \langle \theta_\mu^\mu \rangle \simeq - (0.24\ \mbox{GeV})^4\).
- For the \(J/\psi\) system, using \(\epsilon_0 = 2 M_D - M(J/\psi) = 642\ \mbox{MeV}\) and \(m = 1.5\ \mbox{GeV}\), the authors obtain \(\alpha_s = 0.87\) and \(a_0 = 0.20\ \mbox{fm}\).
- The large-distance non-perturbative potential from uncorrelated two-pion exchange behaves as \(V^{\pi\pi}(R) \rightarrow -(\bar d_2 a_0^2/\epsilon_0)^2 (4\pi^2/b)^2 (3/2) (2 m_\pi)^4 (m_\pi^{1/2})/((4\pi R)^{5/2}) e^{-2 m_\pi R}\).
- The sum rule relating the integrated non-perturbative potential to the vacuum energy density is \(\int d^3{\bf R} (V(R) - V^{pt}(R)) = -(\bar d_2 a_0^2/\epsilon_0)^2 (4\pi^2/b)^2 \cdot 16 |\epsilon_{\rm vac}|\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of onium-onium scattering by Peskin (1979) to include non-perturbative effects at large distances, and builds on low-energy theorems from scale and chiral symmetry breaking by Novikov, Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1980) and by Voloshin and Zakharov (1980). The results show that pion clouds dominate the long-range interactions of heavy quark systems, implying that lattice QCD simulations of heavy quarkonia must go beyond the quenched approximation. The approach may also be relevant to high-energy scattering, as suggested earlier by Anselm and Gribov (1972).
