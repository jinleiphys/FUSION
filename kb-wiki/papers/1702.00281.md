# The stifness of the supranuclear equation of state (once again)

> We revisit the present status of the stiffness of the supranuclear equations of state, particularly the solutions that increase the stiffness in the presence of hyperons, the putative transition to a quark matter phase and the robustness of massive compact star observations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the existence of neutron stars with masses near or above 2 solar masses is now firmly established, the stiffness required to accommodate these masses may be barely achievable in hyperonic equations of state, and if hyperons are present with sufficient repulsion, the formation of a quark matter core becomes almost impossible.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field model (σ-ω-ρ-δ-φ) including the full baryonic octet with derivative couplings to parametrize many-body forces, varying the key parameter ζ to control stiffness. They then construct Maxwell phase transitions to a cold quark matter phase described by the MFTQCD model, which uses a mean-field approximation with a dynamical gluon mass and a vacuum term fixed at 90 MeV/fm³. The resulting equations of state are integrated into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to generate theoretical non-rotating stellar sequences and compute maximum masses.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230: mass 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ from Shapiro delay measurement (Demorest et al.)
- PSR J0348+0432: mass 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ from optical observations of the companion (Antoniadis et al.)
- Vela X-1 (HMXB): mass 2.12 ± 0.16 M⊙ (Falanga et al.)
- 4U 1700-37: mass 1.96 ± 0.19 M⊙ (Falanga et al.)
- PSR J1748-2021B in NGC 6440: mass 2.74 ± 0.21 M⊙, noted as needing confirmation
- Bimodal mass distribution peaks at 1.37 M⊙ and 1.73 M⊙ (Valentim, Rangel and Horvath); updated by Ozel et al. to 1.39 M⊙ and 1.8 M⊙ with a possible maximum mass of 2.15 M⊙
- The σ-ω-ρ-δ-φ model uses ζ values from 4 × 10⁻² to 8.5 × 10⁻²; the MFTQCD model uses ξ values from 1.5 × 10⁻⁴ to 7.5 × 10⁻⁴ with B_MFTQCD = 90 MeV/fm³
- The maximum mass from the hyperonic equations of state is barely above 2 M⊙ for a limited set of parameters

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the "hyperon puzzle" first identified when high-mass neutron star observations (Demorest et al. 2010; Antoniadis et al. 2013) contradicted the softening effect of hyperons predicted by earlier calculations. The paper builds on the specific hyperonic model of Gomes et al. and the MFTQCD quark matter model of Franzon et al., and contrasts with equations of state that omit hyperons, which can easily reach masses above 2 M⊙. The results suggest that if hyperons are present with sufficient repulsion, quark matter cores may not form, leaving the alternative that strange quark matter might compose entire compact objects if it is the true ground state of strong interactions.
