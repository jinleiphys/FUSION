# Fitting two nucleons inside a box: exponentially suppressed corrections to the Luscher's formula

> Scattering observables can be computed in lattice field theory by measuring the volume dependence of energy levels of two particle states. The dominant volume dependence, proportional to inverse powers of the volume, is determined by the phase shifts. This universal relation (\Lu's formula) between energy levels and phase shifts is distorted by corrections which, in the large volume limit, are exponentially suppressed. They may be sizable, however, for the volumes used in practice and they set a limit on how small the lattice can be in these studies. We estimate these corrections, mostly in the case of two nucleons. Qualitatively, we find that the exponentially suppressed corrections are proportional to the {\it square} of the potential (or to terms suppressed in the chiral expansion) and the effect due to pions going ``around the world'' vanishes. Quantitatively, the size of the lattice should be greater than $\approx(5 {fm})^3$ in order to keep finite volume corrections to the phase less than $1^\circ$ for realistic pion mass.

## Key claim

The exponentially suppressed corrections to Lüscher's formula for two nucleons in a finite box are proportional to the *square* of the potential (or to terms suppressed in the chiral expansion), and the effect from pions traveling "around the world" vanishes at first order; the lattice volume must be greater than approximately (5 fm)³ to keep finite volume corrections to the phase shift below 1° for realistic pion mass.

## Method

The authors derived a finite-volume generalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, separating the singular on-shell part (which gives the power-law volume dependence of Lüscher's formula) from the regular part. They defined a finite-volume K-matrix (𝕂) that includes the exponentially suppressed corrections (ESC) via an F-matrix term, and solved the resulting integral equations numerically using chiral effective field theory potentials for the ¹S₀ nucleon-nucleon channel, including one-pion exchange and contact interactions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volume should be greater than ≈(5 fm)³ to keep finite volume corrections to the phase less than 1° for realistic pion mass (mπ = 138.0 MeV).
- For the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) only, the leading ESC term is proportional to (gA⁴ mπ M²)/(32π fπ⁴) × e^(-√n mπ L)/(√n mπ L) × (2 + √n mπ L), where gA = 1.26, fπ = 132 MeV, and M is the nucleon mass.
- For realistic potentials at mπ = 138 MeV, the ESC do not follow a simple e^(-mπ L) behavior; the characteristic scale for the exponential decay is ≈260 MeV when using the k value from Lüscher's formula.
- For mπ = 250 MeV, the ESC show little difference from the mπ = 138 MeV case when the k value from Lüscher's formula is used.

## Context

This work extends the universal Lüscher formula (Lüscher 1986, 1991) by computing the exponentially suppressed corrections that distort the relation between energy levels and phase shifts in finite-volume lattice calculations. It builds on the one-loop calculation for two pions in the I=2 channel (pipi_fv, 2005) and provides estimates needed for the first unquenched lattice QCD calculations of nucleon-nucleon phase shifts (NPLQCD collaboration, 2006). The results enable lattice practitioners to determine the minimum box size required for reliable extraction of nucleon-nucleon scattering parameters, and provide a framework for extrapolating to the higher pion masses used in current lattice simulations.
