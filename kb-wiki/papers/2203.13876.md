# Mapping out the thermodynamic stability of a QCD equation of state with a critical point using active learning

> The Beam Energy Scan Theory (BEST) collaboration's equation of state (EoS) incorporates a 3D Ising model critical point into the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) equation of state from lattice simulations. However, it contains 4 free parameters related to the size and location of the critical region in the QCD phase diagram. Certain combinations of the free parameters lead to acausal or unstable realizations of the EoS that should not be considered. In this work, we use an active learning framework to rule out pathological EoS efficiently. We find that checking stability and causality for a small portion of the parameters' range is sufficient to construct algorithms that perform with $>$96% accuracy across the entire parameter space. Though in this work we focus on a specific case, our approach can be generalized to any EoS containing a parameter space-class correspondence.

## Key claim
Active learning can classify thermodynamically stable and causal realizations of the BEST collaboration's QCD equation of state with >96% accuracy across the full parameter space, using only a small portion of the parameter range for training. The approach generalizes to any equation of state with a parameter space-class correspondence, though the paper only demonstrates this for the specific BEST EoS formulation.

## Method
The authors generated realizations of the BEST collaboration's equation of state by varying four free parameters (mu_BC, alpha_diff, w, rho) that control the mapping of a 3D Ising model critical point onto QCD variables. They labeled each realization as acceptable, unstable, or acausal by checking thermodynamic stability conditions (positivity of pressure, entropy, baryon density, second-order baryon susceptibility, heat capacity) and causality (speed of sound squared between 0 and 1) across the entire T-mu_B plane. They trained random forest, K-nearest-neighbor, and support vector machine classifiers on either the four input parameters or a PCA-reduced version of the pressure, using both random sampling and margin-based active learning query strategies.

## Key numbers
- Active learning classifiers achieved >96% accuracy across the entire parameter space
- The parameter grid used step sizes of 20 MeV for mu_BC (range 220-420 MeV), 0.5 for w and rho (range 0.1-10.0), and 5 degrees for alpha_diff (range -180 to 180 degrees)
- The pressure grid covered T from 30 to 800 MeV and mu_B from 0 to 450 MeV with 1 MeV steps, yielding a 451x771 table
- Two PCA components accounted for over 99% of the variance in the pressure data
- Initial training sets contained 350 labeled realizations; test sets contained approximately 20,000 labeled realizations
- Queries consisted of k=200 samples per iteration
- Training stopped at 10,000 samples for RF and KNN, and at 2,500 samples for SVM

## Context
This work extends the BEST collaboration's equation of state framework from Parotto (2018) and Karthein (2021), which incorporated a 3D Ising model critical point into lattice QCD results. Previous work by Mroczek (2020) studied the effects of a critical point on fourth-order baryon number susceptibility. The active learning approach is novel for heavy-ion collision applications, though active learning was previously used in nuclear theory by Sarkar (2021) and in high-energy physics by Caron (2019). This enables efficient filtering of pathological EoS realizations for use in hydrodynamic simulations at BES-II energies, and the method can be generalized to other parameterized equations of state.
