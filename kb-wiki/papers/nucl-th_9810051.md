# Phase space density and chiral symmetry restoration in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The effect of altered hadron masses is studied for its effect with regard to final-state hadronic observables. It is shown that the final phase space densities of pions and kaons, which can be inferred experimentally, are sensitive to in-medium properties of the excited matter at earlier stages of the collision, but that the sensitivity is significantly moderated by interactions that change the effective numbers of pions and kaons during the latter part of the collision.

## Key claim

The final phase space densities of pions and kaons, which can be inferred experimentally, are sensitive to in-medium properties of the excited matter at earlier stages of the collision, but the sensitivity is significantly moderated by interactions that change the effective numbers of pions and kaons during the latter part of the collision.

## Method

The authors model the chemical development of a kinetically equilibrated hadron gas that expands under a time scale relevant for relativistic heavy ion collisions, using phenomenologically motivated chemical rates. They include 26 mass states from the baryon octet and decuplet, pseudoscalar meson nonet, and vector meson nonet, and integrate the time evolution of chemical potentials from an initial temperature of 160 MeV to breakup at 110 MeV using a simplified temperature profile of dT/dt = -6.5 MeV/(fm/c). Cross sections for pion and strangeness production are estimated using step-function parameterizations motivated by measured inelastic pp and pπ cross sections, with σ0 values of 25 mb (baryon-baryon), 20 mb (baryon-meson), and 15 mb (meson-meson).

## Key numbers

- Initial energy densities in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS (160A GeV) and Au+Au at Brookhaven AGS (11A GeV) are in the neighborhood of a few GeV/fm³
- Characteristic expansion times are approximately 5 fm/c when T=160 MeV and approximately 20 fm/c when the system is near breakup at T≈110 MeV
- For the SPS case (baryon-to-pion ratio 0.2) with mass reduction factor below 0.5, the generated chemical potential approaches 100 MeV when number-changing rates are neglected, but is reduced to near 50 MeV when rates are included
- For the AGS case (baryon-to-pion ratio 1.0) with mass reduction factor below 0.5, the generated chemical potential approaches 150 MeV when number-changing rates are neglected
- The resulting μK surpasses 100 MeV even when rates are included, with strangeness phase space density nearly doubled compared to the μK=0 case
- Chemical potentials can be inferred from hadronic measurements to the accuracy of ±30 MeV
- Preliminary measurements of the Λ̄/p̄ ratio by E864 yielded values above unity, approximately double or triple the calculations presented here

## Context

This work extends studies of chiral symmetry restoration effects in heavy ion collisions, particularly following the observation of altered ρ meson properties in dilepton measurements at the SPS (Li, Ko, and Brown, 1995; Siemens and Chin, 1985; Chanfray, Rapp, and Wambach, 1996). The authors build on chemical equilibration studies by Song and Koch (1997) for a simple meson gas, and on the concept of effective chemical potentials for pions and kaons introduced by Rafelski (1996). The results enable experimental extraction of chemical potentials from two-particle correlations and spectra, and provide boundary conditions for modeling earlier stages of collisions, including dilepton production from ππ annihilation which would scale as exp(2μπ/T).
