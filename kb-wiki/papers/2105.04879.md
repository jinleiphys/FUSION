# Model investigations of the correlation between the mean transverse momentum and anisotropic flow in shape-engineered events

> The correlation between the event mean-transverse momentum $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$, and the anisotropic flow magnitude $v_n$, $ρ(v^{2}_{n},[p_{T}])$, has been argued to be sensitive to the initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions. We use simulated events generated with the AMPT and EPOS models for Au+Au at $\sqrt{\textit{s}_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, to investigate the model dependence and the response and sensitivity of the $ρ(v^{2}_{2},[p_{T}])$ correlator to collision-system size and shape, and the viscosity of the matter produced in the collisions. We find good qualitative agreement between the correlators for the string melting version of the AMPT model and the EPOS model. The model investigations for shape-engineered events as well as events with different viscosity ($η/s$), indicate that $ρ(v^{2}_{2},[p_{T}])$ is sensitive to the initial-state geometry of the collision system but is insensitive to sizable changes in $η/s$ for the medium produced in the collisions. These findings suggest that precise differential measurements of $ρ(v^{2}_{2},[p_{T}])$ as a function of system size, shape, and beam-energy could provide more stringent constraints to discern between initial-state models and hence, more reliable extractions of $η/s$.

## Key claim

The correlation coefficient ρ(v²₂,[pT]) is sensitive to the initial-state geometry of the collision system but is insensitive to sizable changes in the specific shear viscosity η/s over the range 0.1–0.3, based on AMPT and EPOS model simulations for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (version 2.26t9b) with three parameter sets, varying string melting on/off and η/s values of 0.1 and 0.3, and the EPOS model (3+1D viscous hydrodynamics with UrQMD afterburner) to simulate minimum bias Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They computed ρ(v²₂,[pT]) using a three-subevent cumulant method to mitigate non-flow effects, with charged hadrons in the range 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |η| < 1.0, and applied event-shape engineering via selections on the reduced flow vector q₂ to probe sensitivity to collision geometry.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 4.0 million events generated with AMPT Set-1 (η/s = 0.1, string melting off), 5.0 million with Set-2 (η/s = 0.1, string melting on), 3.0 million with Set-3 (η/s = 0.3, string melting on), and 0.3 million with EPOS for Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- The AMPT model varied η/s over the range 0.1–0.3 by adjusting the screening mass μ from 2.26 to 4.2 fm⁻¹ at a fixed temperature Ti = 378 MeV and αs = 0.47.
- The ratio v₂{4}/v₂{2} was insensitive to model choice and parameter sets to within approximately 5%, indicating flow fluctuations are eccentricity-driven.
- For shape-engineered events in 10–50% centrality, the ρ(v²₂,[pT]) values showed a quadratic dependence on q₂%, while √c_k/⟨pT⟩ showed q₂%-independence.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the ρ(v²_n,[pT]) correlator proposed by Bozek (2016) and Giacalone (2020) as a probe of initial-state geometry, and follows initial measurements by the ATLAS Collaboration (2019) for p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The findings suggest that precise differential measurements of ρ(v²₂,[pT]) as a function of system size, shape, and beam energy could provide constraints to discern between initial-state models such as AMPT and EPOS, enabling more reliable extractions of η/s from future experimental data at RHIC and LHC.
