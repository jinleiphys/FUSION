# Neutron-neutron scattering length from the reaction gamma d --> pi^+ nn employing chiral perturbation theory

> We discuss the possibility to extract the neutron-neutron scattering length a_{nn} from experimental spectra on the reaction gamma d --> pi^+ nn. The transition operator is calculated to high accuracy from chiral perturbation theory. We argue that for properly chosen kinematics, the theoretical uncertainty of the method can be as low as 0.1 fm.

# Neutron-neutron scattering length from the reaction γd → π⁺nn employing chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The neutron-neutron scattering length aₙₙ can be extracted from differential cross sections of the reaction γd → π⁺nn with a theoretical uncertainty as low as 0.1 fm, provided that kinematic configurations are chosen where the quasi-free pion production is suppressed (specifically at θᵣ = 90°). This uncertainty estimate is based on comparing leading-order calculations with complete calculations up to order χ⁵/² in chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The transition operator for γd → π⁺nn was calculated up to order χ⁵/² in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), where χ ∼ mπ/MN ∼ 1/7, with half-integer powers arising from two- and three-body unitarity cuts. Nucleon-nucleon wave functions from the N²LO chiral interaction with spectral function regularization (cutoff Λ̃ varied 500–700 MeV, Λ varied 450–600 MeV) were employed for consistency. The theoretical uncertainty was quantified by defining a function S analogous to a χ² measure, comparing the momentum spectrum at leading order (baseline) against spectra including all higher-order contributions up to χ⁵/² and different wave function choices, then matching the resulting spread to an equivalent shift Δaₙₙ.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical uncertainty Δaₙₙ ≤ 0.1 fm for the extracted neutron-neutron scattering length, achieved with a momentum cutoff pᶜᵘᵗ = 30 MeV and kinematics θᵣ = 90°, θπ = 0°.
- At Q = 5 MeV excess energy with pᶜᵘᵗ = 30 MeV, the uncertainty reduces to 0.07 fm; the function α(pᶜᵘᵗ) takes values α(pₘₐₓ) = 41 fm⁻² and α(30 MeV) = 33 fm⁻².
- The central value used for the uncertainty estimate is aₙₙ⁽⁰⁾ = −18.9 fm; the signal from varying aₙₙ by ±1 fm is clearly distinguishable from the theoretical error band.
- Existing experimental values cited: aₙₙ = −16.1 ± 0.4 fm from nd → pnn (Huhn et al.), aₙₙ = −18.7 ± 0.6 fm from nd → pnn (Gonzalez Trotter et al.), aₙₙ = −18.5 ± 0.3 fm from π⁻d → γnn (Howell et al.), and aₚₚ = −17.3 ± 0.4 fm (Wiringa et al.) after electromagnetic corrections.
- The analysis is valid for excess energies Q ≤ 20 MeV; the maximum value of the expansion parameter χQ = kπ/mπ at Q = 20 MeV is about 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of γd → π⁺nn from Lensky et al. (2005) by focusing specifically on extracting aₙₙ from the final-state interaction region of the momentum spectrum. It provides a systematic uncertainty estimate that was lacking in the earlier approach of Laget (1978), which gave a similar leading-order expression. The method is complementary to extractions from π⁻d → γnn, studied by Phillips et al. (2000, 2002) using ChPT, and from nd → pnn (Huhn et al., Gonzalez Trotter et al.). The theoretical uncertainty of 0.1 fm is comparable to that claimed for those reactions, and the authors note that a complete N³LO calculation could reduce the uncertainty by an order of magnitude. The results enable a new experimental determination of aₙₙ from photoproduction experiments, which could help resolve the current sign ambiguity in Δa = aₚₚ − aₙₙ.
