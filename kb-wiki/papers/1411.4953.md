# The effective QCD phase diagram and the critical end point

> We study the QCD phase diagram on the plane of temperature T and quark chemical potential mu, modelling the strong interactions with the linear sigma model coupled to quarks. The phase transition line is found from the effective potential at finite T and mu taking into accounts the plasma screening effects. We find the location of the critical end point (CEP) to be (mu^CEP/T_c,T^CEP/T_c) sim (1.2,0.8), where T_c is the (pseudo)critical temperature for the crossover phase transition at vanishing mu. This location lies within the region found by lattice inspired calculations. The results show that in the linear sigma model, the CEP's location in the phase diagram is expectedly determined solely through chiral symmetry breaking. The same is likely to be true for all other models which do not exhibit confinement, provided the proper treatment of the plasma infrared properties for the description of chiral symmetry restoration is implemented. Similarly, we also expect these corrections to be substantially relevant in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The critical end point (CEP) of the QCD phase diagram can be located at \((\mu^{\text{CEP}}/T_c, T^{\text{CEP}}/T_c) \sim (1.2, 0.8)\) using the linear sigma model with quarks, provided that plasma screening effects are properly included via ring diagram resummation. This location lies within the region found by lattice-inspired calculations, even though the model does not include confinement.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop effective potential for the linear sigma model coupled to quarks at finite temperature \(T\) and quark chemical potential \(\mu\), including ring diagram contributions to account for plasma screening. The boson self-energy \(\Pi\) is computed in the high-temperature limit with finite chemical potential, and the fermion contribution to the effective potential is obtained by solving a second-order differential equation with boundary conditions at zero mass. The phase transition line is determined by finding where the effective potential develops degenerate minima, and the CEP is identified as the point where the transition changes from second order (at small \(\mu\)) to first order (at larger \(\mu\)).

## Key numbers

- CEP location: \((\mu^{\text{CEP}}/T_c, T^{\text{CEP}}/T_c) \sim (1.2, 0.8)\), where \(T_c\) is the (pseudo)critical temperature at \(\mu = 0\).
- For the parameter set with vacuum sigma mass \(m_\sigma^{\text{vac}} = 300\) MeV: \(\lambda = 0.2\), \(g = 0.71\).
- For the parameter set with \(m_\sigma^{\text{vac}} = 400\) MeV: \(\lambda = 0.86\), \(g = 1.1\).
- The relation between model parameters at \(T_c\) is given by \(a = T_c \sqrt{\lambda/2 + N_f N_c g^2 / 6}\), with \(T_c \simeq 170\) MeV for two-flavor massless quarks.
- Lattice values cited for \(T_c\) (2+1 flavors): MILC collaboration \(T_c = 169(12)(4)\) MeV; RBC-Bielefeld \(T_c = 192(7)(4)\) MeV; Wuppertal-Budapest \(T_c = 147(2)(3)\) MeV; HotQCD \(T_c = 154(9)\) MeV and later \(T_c = 155(1)(8)\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model treatment of the QCD phase diagram by including plasma screening effects beyond mean field, following the approach previously used to describe inverse magnetic catalysis by Ayala et al. (2014). The results contradict the expectation that models without confinement (such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and Polyakov-loop extended NJL model, cited as NJL, pNJL, Chqm, pChqm) necessarily place the CEP at locations inconsistent with lattice-inspired calculations. The finding that proper screening can yield a CEP in the region \((\mu/T_c, T/T_c) \sim (1.0-1.4, 0.9-0.95)\) from mathematical extensions of lattice techniques (Sharma, cited as Sharma) enables further exploration of chiral symmetry restoration effects in the QCD phase diagram, and suggests that similar screening corrections may be relevant for determining the CEP in full QCD.
