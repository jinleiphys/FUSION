# Quantum Lévy Processes and Fractional Kinetics

> Exotic stochastic processes are shown to emerge in the quantum evolution of complex systems. Using influence function techniques, we consider the dynamics of a system coupled to a chaotic subsystem described through random matrix theory. We find that the reduced density matrix can display dynamics given by Lévy stable laws. The classical limit of these dynamics can be related to fractional kinetic equations. In particular we derive a fractional extension of Kramers equation.

## Key claim

The quantum evolution of a wavepacket coupled to a chaotic background (modeled by random matrix theory) can produce reduced density matrices that follow Lévy stable laws, with the specific Lévy exponent α determined by the short-distance statistical correlations of the background's energy levels. The classical limit of these quantum Lévy processes yields a fractional extension of Kramers equation, where the fractional derivatives act on momenta rather than coordinates.

## Method

The authors couple a test particle to a chaotic environment described by a parametric random matrix Hamiltonian, where the background's chaotic properties are encoded in a correlation function G(X) that scales as 1 - |X|^α at short distances. Using an influence functional formalism, they integrate out the random matrix bath to derive an effective evolution equation for the reduced density matrix of the test particle. They then solve this equation for the diagonal density matrix (spatial probability distribution) and take the classical limit via the Wigner transform to obtain a fractional kinetic equation in phase space.

## Key numbers

- The Lévy exponent α is restricted to the range 0 < α ≤ 2, where α = 2 corresponds to Gaussian processes
- The spatial probability distribution P(X,t) = ρ(X,X,t) is expressed as a convolution of Lévy distributions with time-dependent coefficients a(t) = Γ^↓/((α+1)ħ) (ħ/(M X_0))^α t^(α+1) and b(t) = σ^2/2 + ħ^2/(8M^2σ^2) t^2
- For 1 < α < 2 in the long-time limit, the density asymptotically approaches a Lévy distribution: ρ(X,X,t) → a(t)^(-1/α) L_α(a(t)^(-1/α) X)
- The crossover momentum scale between Gaussian and Lévy behavior is k_c = [a(t)/b(t)]^(1/(2-α)) ∝ t^((α-1)/(2-α))
- For α = 2 with constant level density (β = 0), the dynamics can produce turbulent-like diffusion with ⟨X^2⟩ ∼ t^3
- The generalized friction coefficient is γ_α = β Γ^↓ ħ α / (2 M X_0^α)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration that turbulent diffusion can arise in quantum systems (Kusnezov, Bulgac, DoDang 1997) by showing that Lévy processes emerge from a general chaotic background, not just specific models. It contrasts with the Caldeira-Leggett approach (1983) by assuming the background is chaotic rather than thermal. The paper provides a microscopic foundation for phenomenological fractional kinetic equations previously proposed by Fogedby (1994), Zaslavsky (1994), and Jumarie (1992), and enables the systematic construction of fractional kinetic theory in more than one dimension with well-defined transport coefficients.
