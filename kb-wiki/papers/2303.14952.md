# Universal tetramer limit-cycle at the unitarity limit

> We demonstrate that a four-boson limit-cycle independent of the Efimov one appears in Hamiltonian systems at the unitary limit. The model interaction contains two-, three- and four-body short-range potentials, which disentangle the interwoven three- and four-boson cycles, for the universal trimer and tetramer energy levels, respectively. The limit-cycle associated with the correlation between the energies of two successive universal tetramer levels for fixed weakly bound trimer is found to be largely model independent. This is a universal manifestation of an independent four-boson scale associated with a cycle beyond the Efimov one.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a four-boson limit-cycle, independent of the Efimov three-boson cycle, appears in Hamiltonian systems at the unitary limit when a four-body short-range potential is introduced to disentangle the interwoven three- and four-boson cycles; the correlation between successive universal tetramer energies for a fixed weakly bound trimer is found to be largely model independent, though finite-range corrections cause deviations from zero-range predictions.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation variationally for three and four bosons using correlated Gaussian basis functions optimized with the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM). The Hamiltonian includes two-body Gaussian potentials tuned to the unitarity limit (two-body binding energy B2=0), with three- and four-body Gaussian potentials used to independently control the three- and four-body energy scales. The four-body strength V4 is varied while tracking universal tetramer states through avoided crossings to construct correlation functions between successive tetramer energy levels.

## Key numbers

- Two-body strength set to V2 = -2.684005 hbar^2/mr2^2 to achieve the unitarity limit (B2=0)
- Three-body binding energy B3 = 0.238451 hbar^2/mr2^2 when V3=0 (no three-body force)
- Three-body binding energy B3 = 0.04001 hbar^2/mr2^2 when V3=30 hbar^2/mr2^2 and r3=r2
- Four-body potential range r4=r2 for the first calculation, r4=2r2 for the second calculation
- Avoided crossing observed at V4 approximately -323 hbar^2/mr2^2 for the N=3 state
- The Efimov geometric scaling factor exp(2pi/s0) is approximately 515 for three bosons

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Hadizadeh et al. (2011) that a new limit-cycle beyond the Efimov one could appear in four-boson systems at the unitarity limit, which was obtained by solving regularized Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with zero-range interactions. The results agree with those of Hadizadeh et al. (2011) and Deltuva (2012) when accounting for range corrections, and contradict earlier EFT findings by Platter et al. (2004) and Stecher et al. (2009) that did not observe an independent four-boson cycle. The work enables future searches for interwoven limit-cycles in N>=5 boson systems and suggests that many-body forces may manifest in cold-atom experiments near narrow Feshbach resonances.
