# Deeply bound pionic atoms from the $(γ,p)$ reaction in nuclei

> We study the $(γ,p)$ reaction on $^{208}Pb$ leading to $^{207}Pb$ with a bound pion attached to it in the lowest 1s or 2p pionic levels. The reaction can be made recoilless to optimize the production cross section but we must choose a bit higher photon energy to overcome the Coulomb barrier in the proton emission. The cross sections obtained are easily measurable and can be larger than 50 per cent of the background from inclusive ($γ$,p). This makes it a clear case for the detection of the pionic atom signals, converting this reaction into a practical tool to produce deeply bound pionic atoms.

## Key claim

The $(\gamma,p)$ reaction on $^{208}Pb$ can produce deeply bound pionic atoms in $^{207}Pb$ with cross sections large enough to be clearly measurable, provided the photon energy is increased to about 170 MeV (above the recoilless condition of 155 MeV) to overcome the Coulomb barrier for the emitted proton.

## Method

The authors calculated the double differential cross section for the reaction $\gamma + ^{208}Pb \rightarrow p + (^{207}Pb \cdot \pi^-_b)$ using the Kroll-Ruderman term of the elementary $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ amplitude under nearly recoilless kinematics. They included distortion of the outgoing proton wave via a proton-nucleus optical potential and a finite-size Coulomb potential, and summed over final states corresponding to a bound pion (1s or 2p) coupled to a neutron hole state in $^{207}Pb$. The background from inclusive $(\gamma,p)$ was estimated by extrapolating experimental data at higher photon energies.

## Key numbers

- The optimal incident photon energy for the largest signals is $k = 170$ MeV, while the recoilless condition occurs at $k = 155$ MeV.
- The formation cross section for the largest signal (2p pionic state) is about 20 $\mu b/sr/MeV$ after including distortion effects, which reduce the plane-wave result by about 30%.
- The background from inclusive $(\gamma,p)$ at $k = 170$ MeV and a proton angle of 52$^\circ$ is estimated to be 32 $\mu b/sr/MeV$, about double the signal peak cross section.
- The pionic 1s state formation appears at a proton kinetic energy $T_p = 29 \sim 30$ MeV.
- For an experimental resolution of 200 keV FWHM, the strength at the peak of the p-wave states is reduced by about 20% compared to the 50 keV resolution case.

## Context

This work extends the successful detection of deeply bound pionic states in Pb isotopes via the $(d,^3He)$ reaction (Toki, 1988) and the $(\pi^-,\gamma)$ reaction (Juan, 1990) by proposing a new production method using a primary photon beam. The $(\gamma,p)$ reaction offers recoilless kinematics similar to the $(d,^3He)$ reaction but with the practical advantage of using photons, and the calculated cross sections are comparable to those already measured in high-precision $(\gamma,p)$ experiments (Arends, 1990). The results enable a practical tool for producing deeply bound pionic atoms at existing low-energy photon facilities, and the signal-to-background ratio could be further improved using delayed fission techniques previously applied to $\Lambda$ hypernuclei (Hyp, 1990).
