# On the implementation of NLO high energy factorization in single inclusive forward hadron production

> Single inclusive particle production cross sections in high energy hadron collisions at forward rapidity are an important benchmark process for the CGC picture of small x QCD. Recent calculations of this process have not led to a stable perturbative expansion for this quantity at high transverse momenta. We consider the quark channel production cross section using the new rapidity factorization procedure proposed by Iancu et al. We show that for fixed coupling one does indeed obtain a physically meaningful cross section which is positive and reduces in a controlled way to previous leading order calculations. We also consider a running coupling that depends on the transverse momentum of the produced particle. This gives a stable result which, however, is not fully consistent with previous leading order calculations that use a coordinate space running coupling.

# On the implementation of NLO high energy factorization in single inclusive forward hadron production

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that implementing the rapidity factorization procedure proposed by Iancu et al. (2016) for the NLO quark channel cross section yields a positive, physically meaningful result at fixed coupling, but that introducing a momentum-space running coupling creates a mismatch with the coordinate-space running coupling used in the BK evolution, preventing full consistency between the manifestly positive and subtracted formulations.

## Method

The authors numerically implement the NLO quark channel single inclusive hadron production cross section in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) hybrid formalism, using the new rapidity factorization scheme of Iancu et al. (2016) where the dipole correlators are evaluated at a rapidity scale X(ξ) = x_g/(1-ξ) that depends on the integration variable ξ. They compute separately the N_c and C_F contributions to the quark multiplicity, using the MSTW2008 NLO parton distribution functions for the projectile quark, and solve the leading order Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation numerically for the target dipole amplitude, first with fixed coupling α_s=0.2 and an MV initial condition, then with the Balitsky (2006) coordinate-space running coupling prescription and an MV^e initial condition fitted to HERA data by Lappi and Mäntysaari (2013).

## Key numbers

- Fixed coupling calculation uses α_s = 0.2, with center-of-mass energy √s = 500 GeV and rapidity y = 3.2; the kinematic limit where x_p = 1 - x_g/x_0 is reached at k_⊥ ≈ 17.5 GeV for x_0 = 0.01
- The MV initial condition for fixed coupling uses Q_s0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2 and Λ_QCD = 0.241 GeV
- The MV^e initial condition for running coupling uses Q_s0^2 = 0.06 GeV^2, C^2 = 7.2, and e_c = 18.9, with Λ_QCD = 0.241 GeV and the coordinate-space coupling frozen at 0.7 at large |r|
- The CXY approximation (with dipole correlators evaluated at X(ξ)=x_g and ξ integration upper limit set to 1) becomes negative for the LO+N_c contribution around k_⊥ ∼ 6 GeV at fixed coupling, and for the full NLO result above k_⊥ ∼ 10 GeV
- For the momentum-space running coupling, the parameter C_mom^2 = 10^3 gives disagreement between the LO limits of the unsubtracted and subtracted formulations smaller than 30% at both small and large k_⊥

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the negativity problem in NLO forward particle production first identified in the numerical implementation by Stasto, Xiao, and Yuan (2013) of the Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2011, 2012) factorization framework. The authors build on their own earlier analysis (Ducloué, Lappi, Zhu, 2016) attributing the problem to the subtraction procedure for factorizing soft divergences into BK evolution, and implement the manifestly positive formulation proposed by Iancu et al. (2016). The results enable future phenomenological applications at LHC energies, but the running coupling mismatch identified here must be resolved before practical comparisons to experimental data can be made.
