# Causality and Stability Conditions of a Conformal Charged Fluid

> In this paper, I study the conditions imposed on a normal charged fluid so that the causality and stability criteria hold for this fluid. I adopt the newly developed General Frame (GF) notion in the relativistic hydrodynamics framework which states that hydrodynamic frames have to be fixed after applying the stability and causality conditions. To my purpose, I take a charged conformal matter in the flat and $3+1$ dimension to analyze better these conditions. The causality condition is applied by looking to the asymptotic velocity of sound hydro modes at the large wave number limit and stability conditions are imposed by looking to the imaginary parts of hydro modes as well as the Routh-Hurwitz criteria. By fixing some of the transports, the suitable spaces for other ones are derived. I have observed that in a dense medium with finite $U(1)$ charged chemical potential $μ_0$, negative values for transports appear and the second law of thermodynamics has not ruled out the existence of such values. Sign of scalar transports are not limited by any constraints and just a combination of vector transports is limited by the second law of thermodynamic. Also numerically it is proved that the most favorable region for transports $\tildeγ_{1, 2}$, coefficients of the dissipative terms of the current, is of negative values.

## Key claim
For a conformal charged fluid with finite U(1) chemical potential, the second law of thermodynamics does not rule out negative values for scalar transport coefficients, and the most favorable region for the dissipative current coefficients \(\tilde{\gamma}_{1,2}\) is numerically found to be negative values, a result that challenges the usual expectation that transport coefficients must be non-negative.

## Method
The author studies linearized hydrodynamic modes around a local rest frame for a conformal charged fluid in 3+1 flat spacetime, using the General Frame (GF) approach where hydrodynamic frames are not fixed a priori. Causality is imposed by examining the asymptotic group velocity of sound modes in the large wave-number limit, while stability is enforced by requiring non-positive imaginary parts of the modes and applying the Routh-Hurwitz criterion. Five of the nine independent transport parameters are fixed, and the allowed regions for the remaining four are derived for both a hyper-dense medium (\(\mu_0 \gg T_0\)) and a finite-density medium with two values of \(\mu_0/T_0\).

## Key numbers
- For shear modes, the critical wave number is \(k_c = w_0 / (2 \sqrt{\eta \theta_1})\), where \(w_0 = \epsilon_0 + p_{id}\) is the enthalpy density.
- Stability of shear modes requires \(\eta \geq 0\) and \(\theta_1 \geq 0\) from the Routh-Hurwitz criterion applied to \(\Omega^2 \theta_1 + \Omega w_0 + \eta k^2 = 0\).
- The paper studies two regimes: hyper-dense (\(\mu_0 \gg T_0\)) and finite density with two specific values of \(\mu_0/T_0\) (numerical values of these ratios are not given in the truncated text).
- The most favorable region for the dissipative current coefficients \(\tilde{\gamma}_{1,2}\) is numerically found to be negative values (exact numerical bounds not provided in the truncated text).

## Context
This work extends the General Frame (GF) approach of Kovtun (2019) from uncharged conformal fluids to charged conformal fluids with finite U(1) chemical potential. It contrasts with the Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) framework, which introduces second-order terms phenomenologically; the GF approach instead respects frame freedom without adding extra degrees of freedom beyond temperature, chemical potential, and fluid velocity. The results enable future studies of charged fluid hydrodynamics in regimes relevant to the QCD phase diagram, particularly at finite baryon density, and open the question of whether negative transport coefficients are physically admissible in relativistic hydrodynamics.
