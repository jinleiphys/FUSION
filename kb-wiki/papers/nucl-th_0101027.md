# g-Factors and the Interplay of Collective and Single-Particle Degrees of Freedom in Superdeformed Mass-190 Nuclei

> Interplay of collective and single-particle degrees of freedom is a common phenomenon in strongly correlated many-body systems. Despite many successful efforts in the study of superdeformed nuclei, there is still unexplored physics that can be best understood only through the nuclear magnetic properties. We point out that study of the gyromagnetic factor (g-factor) may open a unique opportunity for understanding superdeformed structure. Our calculations suggest that investigation of the g-factor dependence on spin and particle number can provide important information on single-particle structure and its interplay with collective motion in the superdeformed mass-190 nuclei. Modern experimental techniques combined with the new generation of sensitive detectors should be capable of testing our predictions.

# g-Factors and the Interplay of Collective and Single-Particle Degrees of Freedom in Superdeformed Mass-190 Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors argue that g-factors in superdeformed mass-190 nuclei deviate significantly from the simple rigid-rotor estimate g = Z/A, and that measuring g-factors as a function of both spin and particle number can reveal the interplay between collective rotation and single-particle alignment. However, current experimental uncertainties are too large (by a factor of 2-3) to distinguish the predicted variations or test the underlying theory.

## Method

The authors used the projected shell model (PSM) with a separable-force Hamiltonian and a configuration space of 4 major shells per nucleon type (N=4-7 for neutrons, N=3-6 for protons). The many-body wave function was constructed as a superposition of angular-momentum-projected multi-quasiparticle states including the quasiparticle vacuum, 2-quasineutron, 2-quasiproton, and 4-quasiparticle configurations, with basis deformation fixed at epsilon_2 = 0.45. g-factors were computed directly from the full many-body wave function without semiclassical separation of collective and single-particle parts.

## Key numbers

- The g-factor for $^{194}$Hg reaches a value of 0.41 (approximately Z/A) at spin I = 44.
- The basis deformation was fixed at epsilon_2 = 0.45 for all nuclei calculated.
- Free values for gyromagnetic ratios were used: g_l^pi = 1, g_s^pi = 5.586 x 0.75; g_l^nu = 0, g_s^nu = -3.826 x 0.75.
- The dimension of the quasiparticle basis was about 100.
- Experimental uncertainty must be reduced by a factor of at least 2-3, and data extended to at least spin states I = 36-42, for meaningful comparison.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic description of yrast superdeformed bands in the mass-190 region using the projected shell model (Sun 1997). It contrasts with the rigid-rotor picture (g = Z/A) and with Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations by Perries et al. (1997), which gave spin-averaged g-factors but did not capture the spin-dependent behavior from high-j pair alignment. The paper directly addresses the first direct g-factor measurement in the superdeformed well of $^{194}$Hg by Mayer et al., noting that current error bars are too large to distinguish between competing theoretical predictions. The results suggest that accurate g-factor measurements could resolve the competition between Coriolis antipairing and high-j orbital alignment, and enable tests of microscopic structure in superdeformed nuclei beyond what is possible from gamma-ray energies or transition quadrupole moments alone.
