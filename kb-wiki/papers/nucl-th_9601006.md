# Rho Mesons in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter: A Renormalization Group Approach

> The running of the $ρ\bar N N$ couplings in presence of nuclear matter is studied, and the relevant $β$-functions are shown to be different for $ρ^0$ and $ρ^+$, when the nuclear medium is asymmetric.

# Rho Mesons in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter: A Renormalization Group Approach

## Key claim

The running of the ρNN couplings with four-momentum transfer q² is shown to be different for ρ⁰ and ρ⁺ mesons when the nuclear medium is asymmetric, due to the breaking of isospin symmetry in the nucleon sector (Mₚ* ≠ Mₙ*); this effect vanishes for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian with vector ρNN coupling and compute the one-loop self-energy diagrams for ρ⁰ and ρ⁺ mesons in nuclear matter, using the nucleon propagator modified by a Fermi sphere (Pauli blocking). They extract the wavefunction renormalization constants Z₃ for each meson from the divergent parts of the polarization tensors, then derive the β-functions and the running of the couplings αᵥ⁰ and αᵥ⁺ with q² using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The β-function for ρ⁰ is β(gᵥ⁰) = (gᵥ⁰)³/(6π²), while for ρ⁺ it is β(gᵥ⁺) = (gᵥ⁺)³/(6π²) × [1 − 3(Mₚ* − Mₙ*)²/(2q²)].
- Taking gᵥ⁰ = 2.67 at μ = 0.77 GeV (the ρ⁰ mass in vacuum, from the Bonn group estimate), the model with ρ⁰ and nucleon behaves like a free field theory from q² ≈ 50 GeV (the Landau pole).
- The running of αᵥ⁰ is given by 1/αᵥ⁰(Q²) = 1/αᵥ⁰(μ²) − (2/(3π)) ln(Q²/μ²).
- The running of αᵥ⁺ is given by 1/αᵥ⁺(Q²) = 1/αᵥ⁺(μ²) − (2/(3π))[1 − 3(Mₚ* − Mₙ*)²/(2q²)] ln(Q²/μ²).

## Context

This work extends the study of dense nuclear matter properties (Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux; Serot and Walecka) and the role of ρ mesons in dilepton spectroscopy from heavy ion collisions (DLS Collaboration). It shows that the ρNN coupling runs with momentum transfer analogously to QED, but with a matter-induced difference between neutral and charged ρ mesons in asymmetric media. The results enable further study of low-energy hadronic dynamics and the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter, though the authors note that the Landau pole lies outside the physically interesting region of stable collective excitations.
