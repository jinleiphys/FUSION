# Single photon production induced by (anti)neutrino neutral current scattering on nucleons and nuclear targets

> We review our theoretical approach to neutral current photon emission on nucleons and nuclei in the few-GeV energy region, relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments. These reactions are dominated by the weak excitation of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance but there are also important non-resonant contributions. We have also included terms mediated by nucleon excitations from the second resonance region. On nuclei, Pauli blocking, Fermi motion and the in-medium $Δ$ resonance broadening have been taken into account for both incoherent and coherent reaction channels. With this model, the number and distributions of photon events at the MiniBooNE and T2K experiments have been obtained. We have also compared to the NOMAD upper limit at higher energies. The implications of our findings and future perspectives are discussed.

# Single photon production induced by (anti)neutrino neutral current scattering on nucleons and nuclear targets

## Key claim

The authors present a microscopic model for neutral-current photon (NCγ) production on nucleons and nuclei in the few-GeV region, finding that the Δ(1232) resonance dominates but non-resonant nucleon-pole terms and the N(1520) resonance also contribute significantly; crucially, the model predicts that NCγ emission from single-nucleon currents is insufficient to explain the low-energy excess observed by MiniBooNE in both neutrino and antineutrino modes, despite good agreement with MiniBooNE's in-situ background estimate.

## Method

The hadronic current for NCγ on nucleons is constructed from Feynman diagrams including direct and crossed baryon-pole terms (nucleon, Δ(1232), N(1440), N(1520), N(1535)) and t-channel pion exchange, with vector form factors from MAID helicity amplitudes and axial form factors from a dipole parametrization fitted to BNL/ANL deuterium data. For nuclear targets, the incoherent cross section is computed using a local density approximation with a 1p1hγ hadronic tensor that incorporates Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and in-medium Δ resonance broadening, while the coherent channel is evaluated by summing amplitudes over nucleons weighted by nuclear density distributions.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) contribution to the nucleon cross section is about 2.5 times larger than the nucleon-pole (NP+CNP) contribution at Eν(ν̄) ~ 1.5 GeV
- Nuclear medium corrections reduce the incoherent cross section on 12C by approximately 30% in the full model (Fermi motion + Pauli blocking + Δ in-medium modification)
- Coherent NCγ cross sections on 12C are about a factor 10-15 smaller than the incoherent ones
- For the T2K experiment at Super-Kamiokande (6.57 × 10^20 POT in ν mode), the predicted total number of NCγ events before efficiency corrections is 0.421 ± 0.051, with the uncertainty from C^A_5(0) = 1.00 ± 0.11
- The ratio σ(NCγ, W < 1.6 GeV)/σ(νμ A → μ⁻ X) at Eν = 25 GeV is approximately 0.8 × 10⁻⁴, safely below the NOMAD upper limit of 4.0 × 10⁻⁴ at 90% CL
- A photon energy cut of Eγ ≥ 140 MeV is applied in all phase-space integrals

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of NCγ reactions beyond the earlier model of Hill (2009), which studied the reaction on nucleons with hadronic degrees of freedom and treated coherent emission via a scalar nucleus with a form factor, and beyond the chiral effective field theory approach of Serot and Zhang (2012) that was phenomenologically extended to intermediate energies. The model improves upon these by including N(1440), N(1520), and N(1535) resonance contributions, a more complete treatment of nuclear medium effects for both incoherent and coherent channels, and direct application to MiniBooNE, T2K, and NOMAD experimental conditions. The results enable more accurate background estimates for νe appearance searches and motivate investigation of multinucleon mechanisms (2p2hγ) or heavy neutrino decay scenarios as possible explanations for the MiniBooNE low-energy excess.
