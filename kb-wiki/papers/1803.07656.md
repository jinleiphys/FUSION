# The puzzling two-proton decay of $^{67}$Kr

> Two-proton (2$p$) radioactivity is a rare decay mode found in a few proton-unbound nuclei. The $2p$-decay lifetime and properties of emitted protons carry invaluable information on nuclear structure in the presence of low-lying proton continuum. The recently measured $2p$ decay of $^{67}$Kr [Phys. Rev. Lett. 117, 162501 (2016)] turned out to be unexpectedly fast. Since $^{67}$Kr is expected to be a deformed system, we investigate the impact of deformation effects on the $2p$ radioactivity. We apply the recently developed Gamow coupled-channel framework, which allows for a precise description of three-body systems in the presence of rotational and vibrational couplings. This is the first application of a three-body approach to a two-nucleon decay from a deformed nucleus. We show that deformation couplings significantly increase the $2p$ decay width of $^{67}$Kr; this finding explains the puzzling experimental data. The calculated angular proton-proton correlations reflect a competition between $1p$ and $2p$ decay modes in this nucleus.

# The puzzling two-proton decay of $^{67}$Kr

## Key claim

Deformation couplings significantly increase the two-proton decay width of $^{67}$Kr, explaining why the experimentally measured partial lifetime (20 ± 11 ms) is much shorter than earlier theoretical predictions. The effect arises because oblate deformation of the $^{65}$Se core opens up the 1/2[321] Nilsson orbital with large $\ell=1$ component for valence protons, replacing the higher-$\ell$ $f_{5/2}$ or 9/2[404] configurations that would otherwise dominate.

## Method

The authors extended the Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) framework to include core excitations, treating the $^{65}$Se core as either vibrational (at small deformations) or rotational (at large quadrupole deformations). The three-body core+$p$+$p$ wave function was expanded in hyperspherical harmonics with the hyperradial part expanded in the Berggren basis, allowing simultaneous treatment of bound and continuum states. The proton-core interaction used a Woods-Saxon potential with central, spin-orbit, and Coulomb terms, with Pauli-forbidden states removed via a supersymmetric transformation method.

## Key numbers

- Measured $2p$ decay energy of $^{67}$Kr: 1690 ± 17 keV (Goigoux 2016)
- Measured partial $2p$ lifetime of $^{67}$Kr: 20 ± 11 ms (Goigoux 2016)
- Calculated half-life for $^{48}$Ni (spherical benchmark): 14 ms, compared to experimental values of $8.4^{+12.8}_{-7}$ ms (Dossat 2005) and $3^{+2.2}_{-1.2}$ ms (Pomorski 2014)
- At deformation $\beta_2 \approx -0.3$, the calculated $2p$ ground-state half-life of $^{67}$Kr is 24 ms, consistent with experiment
- For small deformations ($|\beta_2| \le 0.1$) with vibrational coupling, the predicted half-life exceeds 218 ms, over an order of magnitude larger than experiment
- The $1p$ decay width of $^{67}$Kr estimated by the core-proton model is $8.6 \times 10^{-20}$ MeV, of the same order as the $2p$ decay width
- Dominant $(S_{12},\ell_x,\ell_y)$ configurations in $^{67}$Kr: 59% (0,0,0) and 27% (1,1,1)

## Context

This work extends the GCC framework previously introduced by Wang (2017) to include core excitations, and applies it for the first time to a two-nucleon decay from a deformed nucleus. The puzzlingly fast decay of $^{67}$Kr was first reported by Goigoux (2016), who suggested configuration mixing and/or deformation in the daughter $^{65}$Se as possible explanations; an alternative explanation involving competition between two-body and three-body decay channels was proposed by Grigorenko (2017). The present results show that deformation effects naturally resolve the discrepancy, and the calculated angular correlations indicate competition between $1p$ and $2p$ decay modes in $^{67}$Kr, unlike the purely $2p$ character of $^{48}$Ni decay. This work enables future studies of three-body decays from deformed nuclei and demonstrates the sensitivity of $2p$ lifetimes to the proton-proton interaction strength and the $\ell$ content of valence proton configurations.
