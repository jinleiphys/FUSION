# Scalar-Isovector Delta-Meson in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory and the Structure of Neutron Stars with a Quark Core

> In the framework of the relativistic mean-field theory, we have considered the equation of state of superdense nuclear matter, taking into account an effective scalar-isovector delta-meson field. The effect of the delta-meson field on the characteristics of a Maxwell-type quark phase transition has been studied. The quark phase is described with the aid of the improved version of the MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology) bag model, in which interactions between the u, d, s quarks inside the bag are taken into account in the one-gluon exchange approximation. For different values of the bag parameter B, series of neutron star models with a quark core have been built. Stability problems for neutron stars with an infinitesimal quark core are discussed. An estimate is obtained for the amount of energy released in a catastrophic transformation of a critical neutron star to a star with a finite-size quark core.

## Key claim

Inclusion of the scalar-isovector delta-meson field in the relativistic mean-field theory increases the stiffness of the nuclear matter equation of state and modifies the parameters of a Maxwell-type first-order phase transition to quark matter, but the effect is conditional on the value of the bag parameter B in the MIT bag model.

## Method

The authors constructed the equation of state of superdense nuclear matter using relativistic mean-field theory with sigma, omega, rho, and delta mesons, solving the field equations self-consistently for beta-equilibrium npe matter. The quark phase was described by an improved MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange interactions among u, d, s quarks, using quark masses mu=5 MeV, md=7 MeV, ms=150 MeV, and strong coupling constant alpha_s=0.5. They applied the Maxwell construction for the first-order phase transition between nuclear and quark matter, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star models with quark cores for bag parameter B values from 60 to 120 MeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density n0 = 0.153 fm^-3, binding energy per baryon f0 = -16.3 MeV, compressibility modulus K = 300 MeV, asymmetry energy E_sym^(0) = 32.5 MeV, and effective mass ratio gamma = m_N*/m_N = 0.78
- For B = 60 MeV/fm^3 with delta meson: transition pressure P0 = 2.11 MeV/fm^3, nuclear density n_N = 0.1207 fm^-3, quark density n_Q = 0.2831 fm^-3, density jump parameter lambda = 2.327
- For B = 69.3 MeV/fm^3 with delta meson: transition pressure P0 = 13.84 MeV/fm^3, nuclear density n_N = 0.2241 fm^-3, quark density n_Q = 0.3504 fm^-3, lambda = 1.504
- For B = 80 MeV/fm^3 with delta meson: transition pressure P0 = 37.95 MeV/fm^3, nuclear density n_N = 0.3276 fm^-3, quark density n_Q = 0.4525 fm^-3, lambda = 1.278
- For B = 100 MeV/fm^3 with delta meson: transition pressure P0 = 93.30 MeV/fm^3, nuclear density n_N = 0.4746 fm^-3, quark density n_Q = 0.6497 fm^-3, lambda = 1.213
- For B = 120 MeV/fm^3 with delta meson: transition pressure P0 = 143.9 MeV/fm^3, nuclear density n_N = 0.5729 fm^-3, quark density n_Q = 0.8165 fm^-3, lambda = 1.24
- Purely nucleon EOS gives maximum neutron star mass ~2.2 solar masses; with quark core this decreases to ~1.75 solar masses
- For B = 60 MeV/fm^3: catastrophic transformation yields a star of mass ~0.24 solar masses and radius ~16.77 km transforming to radius ~13.95 km with quark core mass ~0.087 solar masses and core radius ~4.38 km, releasing energy E_conv ~ 4 x 10^43 J
- Critical density jump parameter for stability: lambda_cr = 3/2

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field studies of Kubis and Kutschera (2003) and earlier work by Alaverdyan on the role of the delta meson in asymmetric nuclear matter, specifically investigating its effect on the quark deconfinement phase transition in neutron stars. The results enable future studies of mixed-phase formation (as noted by Glendenning, 1992) and provide constraints on the bag parameter B from neutron star stability criteria, showing that B must lie between 69.3 and 90 MeV/fm^3 for stable hybrid stars with arbitrarily small quark cores, while B below 69.3 MeV/fm^3 leads to catastrophic transformations with large energy release.
