# Beyond the Schwinger boson representation of the su(2)-algebra. I -- New boson representation based on the su(1,1)-algebra and its related problems with application

> With the use of two kinds of boson operators, a new boson representation of the su(2)-algebra is proposed. The basic idea comes from the pseudo su(1,1)-algebra recently given by the present authors. It forms a striking contrast to the Schwinger boson representation of the su(2)-algebra which is also based on two kinds of bosons. This representation may be suitable for describing time-dependence of the system interacting with the external environment in the framework of the thermo field dynamics formalism, i.e., the phase space doubling. Further, several deformations related to the su(2)-algebra in this boson representation are discussed. On the basis of these deformed algebra, various types of time-evolution of a simple boson system are investigated.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new boson representation of the su(2)-algebra, constructed from two kinds of boson operators, that is specifically designed for application to the phase space doubling formalism in thermo field dynamics, in contrast to the Schwinger boson representation which is unsuitable for this purpose; the representation is valid only within a finite subspace of the infinite-dimensional su(1,1) space, where the su(2)-generators are expressed as functions of the su(1,1)-generators and a parameter related to the subspace dimension.

## Method

The authors start from the Schwinger boson representation of the su(1,1)-algebra, defined by operators \({\hat T}_+ = {\hat a}^*{\hat b}^*\), \({\hat T}_- = {\hat b}{\hat a}\), and \({\hat T}_0 = \frac{1}{2}({\hat a}^*{\hat a} + {\hat b}^*{\hat b} + 1)\). They then construct a pseudo su(1,1)-algebra by restricting the infinite-dimensional su(1,1) space to a finite subspace with a maximum weight state, introducing a cutoff parameter \(t_m = C_m + 1 - t\). Using the same subspace and the su(1,1) generators, they define new operators \({\hat {\cal S}}_{\pm,0}\) that satisfy the su(2) commutation relations, with the magnitude operator \({\hat {\cal S}} = \frac{1}{2}({\hat T}_m - {\hat T})\) and the explicit forms \({\hat {\cal S}}_+ = {\hat a}^*{\hat b}^* \cdot \sqrt{C_m - {\hat b}^*{\hat b}} \cdot (\sqrt{{\hat b}^*{\hat b} + 1 + \epsilon})^{-1}\) and \({\hat {\cal S}}_0 = \frac{1}{2}({\hat a}^*{\hat a} + {\hat b}^*{\hat b}) - \frac{1}{2}C_m\).

## Key numbers

* The su(2) magnitude operator is given by \({\hat {\cal S}} = \frac{1}{2}({\hat a}^*{\hat a} - {\hat b}^*{\hat b}) + \frac{1}{2}C_m\), where \(C_m\) is a parameter related to the maximum number of bosons.
* The maximum value of the su(1,1) quantum number \(t\) is \(\mu = \frac{1}{2}(C_m + 1)\), which for the fermion pairing model becomes \(\mu = \Omega_0 + \frac{1}{2}\) where \(2\Omega_0\) is the number of single-particle states.
* The total fermion number operator is \({\hat N} = 2{\hat T}_0 - 1 = {\hat a}^*{\hat a} + {\hat b}^*{\hat b}\), and the seniority operator is \({\hat \nu} = 2{\hat T} - 1 = -{\hat a}^*{\hat a} + {\hat b}^*{\hat b}\).
* The expectation value of the intrinsic Hamiltonian \(\langle {\hat H}_{\rm intr} \rangle_\tau = \omega \langle {\hat b}^*{\hat b} \rangle_\tau\) varies in the range \(2t - 1 \leq \langle {\hat b}^*{\hat b} \rangle_\tau \leq t_m + t - 1\) for the pseudo su(1,1)-algebra.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous construction of the pseudo su(1,1)-algebra (Tsue, Providencia, da Providencia, Yamamura, 2014, referred to as (A) in the text), which was used to describe a deformed Cooper pair obeying the su(2)-algebra in many-fermion systems. The new boson representation of the su(2)-algebra is designed to be suitable for the thermo field dynamics formalism (Umezawa, 1993) and the phase space doubling approach, which has been applied to describe damped and amplified harmonic oscillators (Refs. 5, 6, 7 in the paper). This representation enables the investigation of time-dependent behavior of boson systems interacting with an external environment, where the operator \(({\hat b}^*{\hat b} - {\hat a}^*{\hat a})\) is a constant of motion, and the energy of the intrinsic system can exhibit periodic time dependence rather than monotonic damping or amplification.
