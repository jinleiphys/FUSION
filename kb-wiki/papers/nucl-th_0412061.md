# Modified Dihadron Fragmentation Functions in Hot and Nuclear Matter

> Medium modification of dihadron fragmentation functions due to gluon bremsstrahlung induced by multiple partonic scattering is studied in both deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off large nuclei and high-energy heavy-ion collisions within the same framework of twist expansion. The modified fragmentation functions for dihadrons are found to follow closely that of single hadrons leading to a weak nuclear suppression of their ratios as measured by HERMES in DIS experiments. Meanwhile, a moderate medium enhancement of the near-side correlation of two high transverse momentum hadrons with increasing centrality is found in heavy-ion collisions because of the trigger bias and the increase in parton energy loss with centrality. Successful comparisons between theory and experiment for multi-hadron observables in both confining and deconfined environments offers comprehensive evidence for partonic energy loss as the mechanism of jet modification in dense matter.

## Key claim

The medium modification of dihadron fragmentation functions follows that of single hadron fragmentation functions closely, leading to a weak nuclear suppression of their ratios in DIS off nuclei but a moderate medium enhancement of near-side two-hadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions due to trigger bias and increased parton energy loss with centrality.

## Method

The authors derived medium-modified dihadron fragmentation functions within the framework of twist expansion, calculating the next-to-leading twist contributions from multiple partonic scattering and induced gluon bremsstrahlung. For DIS off nuclei, the modified splitting functions depend on the quark-gluon correlation function \(T^A_{qg}\) in the nucleus, with a single parameter \(\tilde{C}=0.006\) GeV\(^2\) determined from previous single fragmentation function studies. For heavy-ion collisions, the formalism was extended by replacing the nuclear correlation function with a parton-gluon correlation function \(T^M\) in a quark-gluon plasma, and the associated hadron yields were computed using a factorized cross-section with nuclear parton distribution functions and nuclear thickness functions.

## Key numbers

- \(\tilde{C}=0.006\) GeV\(^2\): the single parameter in the modified dihadron fragmentation functions, determined from previous single fragmentation function studies in DIS off nuclei (HERMES kinematics).
- Nuclear radius \(R_A = 1.12 A^{1/3}\): used in the Gaussian nuclear distribution estimate of the quark-gluon correlation function \(T^A_{qg}\).
- Trigger hadron threshold \(p_T^{\text{trig}} > 8\) GeV: used in the heavy-ion collision analysis for associated hadron yields in \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.
- Associated hadron transverse momentum ranges: 6 GeV \(< p_T^{\text{assoc.}} < p_T^{\text{trig.}}\) and 4 GeV \(< p_T^{\text{assoc.}} < 6\) GeV: used in the comparison with experimental data from Adams (2006).
- Factor \(K \simeq 2\): accounts for higher order corrections in the hard parton cross section, identical to that used for single inclusive spectra.

## Context

This work extends the twist-expansion formalism for single fragmentation functions developed by Guo and Wang (2000) and Wang (2003) to dihadron fragmentation functions, and builds on the DGLAP evolution equations for dihadron fragmentation functions derived by Majumder, Wang, and Wang (2004). The predictions are compared to HERMES experimental data on DIS off Nitrogen and Krypton nuclei (Dinezza, 2004) and to STAR data on \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (Adams, 2006). The successful comparisons provide comprehensive evidence for partonic energy loss as the mechanism of jet modification in both confining and deconfined matter, and enable further tests of the factorized pQCD formalism for jet-like observables in heavy-ion collisions.
