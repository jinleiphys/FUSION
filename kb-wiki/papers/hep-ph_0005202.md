# Weak hyperon decays in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory: Renormalization and applications

> The complete renormalization of the weak Lagrangian to chiral order q^2 in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory is performed using heat kernel techniques. The results are compared with divergences appearing in the calculation of Feynman graphs for the nonleptonic hyperon decay Lambda -> p pi^- and an estimate for the size of the counterterm contributions to the s-wave amplitudes in nonleptonic hyperon decays is given.

## Key claim

The paper performs the complete renormalization of the weak Lagrangian to chiral order \(q^2\) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the abstract's claim of a full renormalization is hedged: the work focuses on divergences relevant to nonleptonic and radiative hyperon decays, neglecting weak decays into two or more pions, and provides only an estimate (not a precise determination) of counterterm contributions to s-wave amplitudes.

## Method

The authors use heat kernel techniques to isolate the divergent parts of the one-loop generating functional for the weak baryon-meson Lagrangian in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, extending prior work by Muller and Meissner (1999) in the strong sector. They compute the irreducible tadpole, self-energy, and eye-graph contributions to the divergences, then compare these with explicit Feynman diagram calculations for the nonleptonic decay \(\Lambda \rightarrow p \pi^-\). The renormalized low-energy constants are decomposed into scale-dependent and divergent parts, with beta functions derived to cancel the poles in dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The divergent one-loop functional is expressed as \(\Sigma_{\text{weak}}^{ab,\text{div}}(y,y) = \frac{1}{(4\pi F_0)^2} \frac{1}{\epsilon} [\hat{\Sigma}_{\text{tad}}^{ab} + \hat{\Sigma}_{\text{self}}^{ab} + \hat{\Sigma}_{\text{eye}}^{ab}]\), where \(\epsilon = 4-d\) and \(F_0\) is the pseudoscalar decay constant in the chiral limit.
- For the p-wave of \(\Lambda \rightarrow p \pi^-\), the irreducible tadpole contributions are \(P^a = -\frac{i}{4F_0^2}(d-f)L(\mu)(3M_\pi^2 + 6M_K^2 + 3M_\eta^2)\) and \(P^b = \frac{i}{4F_0^2}\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(d+3f)L(\mu)(3M_\pi^2 + 6M_K^2 + 3M_\eta^2)\), where \(L(\mu)\) is the divergent loop function.
- For the s-wave, the tadpole contribution is \(S^a = \frac{1}{24\sqrt{3}F_0^3}(d+3f)L(\mu)(5M_\pi^2 + 16M_K^2 + 9M_\eta^2)\), and the sum of diagrams 2c and 2d gives \(S^c + S^d = -\frac{1}{4\sqrt{3}F_0^3}(d+3f)L(\mu)(2M_\pi^2 + M_K^2)\).
- The pion Z-factor divergence is \(\sqrt{Z_\pi} = -\frac{1}{F_0^2}L(\mu)\frac{2}{3}(2M_\pi^2 + M_K^2)\) plus a finite term.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization program of the strong baryon-meson sector by Muller and Meissner (1999) to the weak interaction, providing the complete divergence structure for the weak Lagrangian at chiral order \(q^2\). It corrects an earlier treatment by Borasoy and Holstein (1999) that did not correctly include the pion Z-factor in s-wave divergences. The results enable future calculations of nonleptonic and radiative hyperon decays at one-loop order with full counterterm renormalization, and the derived beta functions allow estimation of higher-order counterterm contributions to s-wave amplitudes, potentially clarifying the long-standing s-wave/p-wave puzzle in nonleptonic hyperon decays.
