# Gluon-decay cascade in AA collisions at the LHC and saturation

> We extract the energy-dependence of the gluon-jet decay cascade from e^+e^- annihilation data and show that this energy-dependence has power-low behavior for the average transverse momentum of jet bigger than 1 GeV. We show that the observed different power-law energy-dependence of hadron multiplicity in AA compared to pp collisions at the LHC is due to the enhanced gluon-decay effect before hadronization. This effect is more important for AA collisions where the saturation scale is larger than 1 GeV. Here we confront our published predictions in arXiv:1102.2385 for the rapidity distribution of the charged hadron production with the recently released data from the CMS collaboration in AA collisions and show that our predictions is in perfect agreement with the data.

# Gluon-decay cascade in AA collisions at the LHC and saturation

## Key claim
The different power-law energy-dependence of charged hadron multiplicity in AA collisions compared to pp collisions at the LHC is explained by an enhanced gluon-decay cascade effect before hadronization, but this effect only becomes significant when the saturation scale exceeds 1 GeV and the average transverse momentum of the jet is above 0.85-1 GeV.

## Method
The author extracted the energy-dependence of the gluon-jet decay cascade from experimental data on gluon jets in e+e- annihilation, then incorporated this into a Color Glass Condensate (CGC)/saturation framework using kT factorization for gluon production convolved with the gluon-to-hadron fragmentation function. The hadron multiplicity was calculated as a convolution of gluon jet production yield (from kT factorization) and the average multiplicity of hadrons in a gluon jet (from Modified Leading Logarithmic Approximation, MLLA), with the saturation scale Qs as the only relevant dimensional scale.

## Key numbers
- The energy-dependence of mean charged particle multiplicity of gluon-jet follows ⟨N_h^Gluon⟩ ∝ E_jet^δ with δ = 0.6-0.7 for E_jet ≥ 0.85-1 GeV, extracted from e+e- annihilation data
- For pp collisions: dN_h/dη ∝ Q_s^2 ∝ s^0.11 (using λ ≈ 0.22)
- For AA collisions: dN_h/dη ∝ Q_s^2 × (Q_s)^0.65 ∝ s^0.145
- The kT factorization accounts for s^0.11 of the energy-dependence, while the MLLA gluon-decay cascade adds only about s^0.036 extra effect
- Prediction for dN_AA/dη at midrapidity for 0-5% Pb+Pb collisions at 5.5 TeV: 1897 ± 133
- The gluon-decay cascade contributes about 20-25% to the multiplicity in AA collisions at the LHC
- Prediction that (2/N_par)(dN_AA/dη) for 5.5 TeV AA collisions at midrapidity is about 1/(0.87 ± 0.06) times larger than at 2.76 TeV

## Context
This work extends the CGC/saturation approach by incorporating gluon-decay cascade effects beyond the leading-log approximation in kT factorization, addressing the unexpected different power-law energy behavior of charged hadron multiplicities in AA versus pp collisions observed at the LHC by the CMS and ALICE collaborations. The author confronts predictions from arXiv:1102.2385 with CMS data and finds perfect agreement. The results contrast with KLN-type approaches (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi) which predict different scaling of average transverse momentum with multiplicity; the author shows that STAR data at RHIC favors the current model over KLN-type predictions. This work enables further study of how fragmentation processes can be accommodated within the CGC/saturation framework and addresses the open problem of entropy production at the early stage of heavy-ion collisions.
