# Unitary correlation in nuclear reaction theory

> We prove that the amplitudes for the (d,p), (d,pn) and (e,e'p) reactions determining the asymptotic behavior of the exact scattering wave functions in the corresponding channels are invariant under unitary correlation operators while the spectroscopic factors are not. Moreover, the exact reaction amplitudes are not parametrized in terms of the spectroscopic factors and cannot provide a tool to determine the spectroscopic factors.

# Unitary correlation in nuclear reaction theory

## Key claim

The exact amplitudes for (d,p), (d,pn), and (e,e'p) reactions are invariant under finite-range unitary correlation operators (UCOs), while spectroscopic factors are not invariant; consequently, exact reaction amplitudes cannot be used to determine spectroscopic factors, contrary to the long-standing assumption in nuclear reaction theory.

## Method

The authors prove invariance by rewriting reaction amplitudes in a surface-integral formulation using kinetic energy operators rather than potentials, then applying Green's theorem to transform volume integrals into surface integrals. They show that at sufficiently large separation distances between the outgoing particle and the residual nucleus, the unitary correlation operator reduces to unity, making the surface integral insensitive to short-range correlations. The proof is carried out explicitly for (d,p) reactions in post-form, for (d,pn) breakup in prior-form, and for (e,e'p) reactions in prior-form.

## Key numbers

- The short-range repulsive core in the NN interaction operates at distances of approximately 1–1.5 fm, which is the range where the unitary correlation operator g(ij) shifts nucleons away from the classically forbidden region.
- About 70–80% of the contribution to the (d,p) reaction amplitude comes from the nuclear exterior, which suppresses the sensitivity of the DWBA amplitude to UCOs.
- The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) C_{An}^{B} determines the amplitude of the tail of the radial overlap function I_{A}^{B}(r) ~ C_{An}^{B} * exp(-kappa * r_{nA}) / r_{nA} as r → ∞.

## Context

This work extends the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) developed by Feldmeier and collaborators to nuclear reaction theory, proving that short-range NN correlations do not affect exact reaction amplitudes. It contradicts the conventional interpretation of phenomenological spectroscopic factors extracted from (d,p) reactions (Lee 2006), (e,e'p) reactions (Kramer), and nucleon knockout reactions (Gade), which show systematic reductions compared to independent-particle shell model predictions. The paper enables a clearer separation between reaction mechanism effects and nuclear structure information, showing that spectroscopic factors must be calculated from overlap functions rather than extracted from reaction data.
