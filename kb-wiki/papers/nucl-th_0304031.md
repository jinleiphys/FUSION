# Oblique-Basis Calculations for $^{44}$Ti

> The spectrum and wave functions of $^{44}$Ti are studied in oblique-basis calculations using spherical and SU(3) shell-model states. Although the results for $^{44}$Ti are not as good as those previously reported for $^{24}$Mg, due primarily to the strong spin-orbit interaction that generates significant splitting of the single-particle energies that breaks the SU(3) symmetry, a more careful quantitative analysis shows that the oblique-basis concept is still effective. In particular, a model space that includes a few SU(3) irreducible representations, namely, the leading irrep (12,0) and next to the leading irrep (10,1) including its spin S=0 and 1 states, plus spherical shell-model configurations (SSMC) that have at least two valence nucleons confined to the $f_{7/2}$ orbit -- the SM(2) states, provide results that are compatible with SSMC with at least one valence nucleon confined to the $f_{7/2}$ orbit -- the SM(3) states.

# Oblique-Basis Calculations for $^{44}$Ti

## Key claim
The oblique-basis method, which combines spherical shell-model configurations (SSMC) with a few SU(3) irreducible representations, works effectively for $^{44}$Ti even though the strong spin-orbit interaction in the lower $pf$-shell breaks SU(3) symmetry; however, in contrast to the $sd$-shell case where SU(3) is dominant, here the spherical shell model is dominant and SU(3) plays a secondary but still important role.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for $^{44}$Ti using the KB3 interaction, combining spherical shell-model configurations (SSMC) with SU(3) basis states. The SU(3) basis included the leading irrep (12,0) and the next-to-leading irrep (10,1) with spin S=0 and S=1 states, while the SSMC bases were truncated by restricting valence nucleons to the $f_{7/2}$ orbit (SM(n) spaces, where n indicates the number of valence particles allowed anywhere in the full $pf$-shell). The oblique-basis results were compared to pure SSMC calculations and to the full $pf$-shell space (4000 dimensions).

## Key numbers
- The full $pf$-shell model space for $^{44}$Ti has 4000 dimensions (100%).
- The SM(2) space (at least two valence nucleons confined to $f_{7/2}$) has 1908 dimensions (47.7% of full space); adding the SU(3) irreps (12,0) and (10,1) increases this to approximately 49.8% of the full space.
- The SM(3) space (at least one valence nucleon confined to $f_{7/2}$) has 3360 dimensions (84% of full space).
- The ground-state energy for the SM(2)+(12,0)&(10,1) oblique basis is -13.76 MeV, compatible with the pure SM(3) result of -13.74 MeV.
- Adding the two SU(3) irreps to the SM(1) basis (14.5% of full space) lowers the ground-state energy by 0.85 MeV, while increasing the space by only 2.1% (from 14.5% to 16.6%).
- SM(2)-type calculations yield 80% overlap with exact (full shell-model) states; SM(3) calculations yield overlaps greater than 97%.
- The SU(3)-type calculated eigenstates often have less than 40% overlap with exact results.

## Context
This work extends the oblique-basis method previously applied successfully to $^{24}$Mg by Gueorguiev et al. (2003), where SU(3) symmetry is good in the $sd$-shell following Elliott's SU(3) model. For the lower $pf$-shell, the strong spin-orbit interaction breaks SU(3) symmetry, as discussed by Gueorguiev et al. in prior work on SU(3) and LS coupling in $pf$-shell nuclei. The results demonstrate that the oblique-basis approach remains effective in a regime where spherical shell-model configurations dominate, enabling calculations that achieve results comparable to much larger model spaces (84% of full space) using only about 50% of the full space. This enables future applications of the oblique-basis method to heavier $pf$-shell nuclei where full shell-model calculations become computationally prohibitive.
