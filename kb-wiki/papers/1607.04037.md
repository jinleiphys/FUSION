# Production of $ΛΛ$ and $\bar{Λ\text{n}}$ in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV within a covariant coalescence model

> We study the production of $ΛΛ$ and $\overline{Λ\text{n}}$ exotic states in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at LHC via both hadron and quark coalescence within a covariant coalescence model with a blast-wave-like parametrization for the phase-space configurations of constituent particles at freezeout. In the hadron coalescence, the two states are considered as molecular states while they are considered as six-quark states in the quark coalescence.For $\overline{Λ\text{n}}$, we find that the yields of both molecular and six-quark states are much larger than the experimental upper-limits. For $ΛΛ$, while the molecule-state yield is much larger than the experimental upper-limits, the six-quark-state yield could be lower than the upper-limits. The higher molecule-state yields are mainly due to the large contribution of short-lived strong resonance decays into (anti-)nucleons and (anti-)$Λ$ which can significantly enhance the molecule-state yields of $ΛΛ$ and $\overline{Λ\text{n}}$ via hadron coalescence. Our results suggest that the current experimental measurement at LHC cannot exclude the existence of the $ΛΛ$ as an exotic six-quark state, and if $ΛΛ$ is a six-quark state, it is then on the brink of being discovered.

# Production of ΛΛ and anti-Λn in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV within a covariant coalescence model

## Key claim

While the abstract states that the six-quark-state yield of ΛΛ could be lower than experimental upper limits, the actual condition is that this holds only for specific choices of cluster size (root-mean-square radius) and decay branching ratio; the six-quark-state ΛΛ yield is consistent with experimental upper limits even assuming a decay branching ratio as large as 0.7, which is larger than the preferred value of 64%, but the molecular-state yields of both ΛΛ and anti-Λn are much larger than experimental upper limits across almost the entire range of decay branching ratios.

## Method

The authors used a covariant coalescence model with a blast-wave-like parametrization for the phase-space configurations of constituent particles at freezeout, implementing multi-dimensional Monte-Carlo integration of the overlap between the cluster Wigner function and the constituent particle phase-space distribution. For hadron coalescence, ΛΛ and anti-Λn were treated as molecular states formed from Λ-Λ and anti-Λ-anti-n coalescence, respectively, using freezeout configurations of nucleons and Λ extracted from fits to measured spectra of protons, Λ, deuterons, 3He, and 3ΛH. For quark coalescence, the states were treated as six-quark states (uuddss for ΛΛ, anti-u anti-d anti-d anti-u anti-d anti-s for anti-Λn), with quark freezeout configurations extracted from fits to measured transverse momentum spectra of p, Λ, φ, Ξ-, and Ω- in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For hadron coalescence with binding energy 1 MeV, the yield (dN/dy at y=0) of anti-Λn is 14.79 x 10^-2 and of ΛΛ is 2.51 x 10^-2, using freezeout configurations FOPb-N (T=121.1 MeV, ρ0=1.215, R0=19.7 fm, τ0=15.5 fm/c, Δτ=1.0 fm/c, ξ_H=3.72) and FOPb-Λ* (T=123.4 MeV, ρ0=1.171, R0=16.7 fm, τ0=13.1 fm/c, Δτ=1.0 fm/c, ξ_H=13.64).
- For quark coalescence with harmonic oscillator frequency w_s=0.078 GeV, the root-mean-square radii are 1.4 fm for anti-Λn and 1.35 fm for ΛΛ, with corresponding yields of 10.6 x 10^-3 and 9.11 x 10^-4, respectively, using quark freezeout configuration FOPb-Q (T=154 MeV, ρ0=1.08, R0=13.6 fm, τ0=11.0 fm/c, Δτ=1.3 fm/c, ξ_u=1.02, ξ_s=0.89).
- The yield of six-quark-state ΛΛ ranges from 2.35 x 10^-4 to 1.95 x 10^-3 for root-mean-square radii from 0.5 to 5 fm, while the yield of six-quark-state anti-Λn ranges from 22.4 x 10^-4 to 20.0 x 10^-3 over the same radius range.
- The measured Λ multiplicity is related to the primordial yield by N_Λ(measured) = 7.44 N_Λ(1115), with electromagnetic decay of Σ(1192) contributing about 16.4% of the total yields.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model predictions of Cho et al. (2011) for H-dibaryon production at RHIC and LHC energies, which used a significantly simplified approximate analytic formula, by implementing a full covariant coalescence model with realistic freezeout configurations. The results contradict the conclusion from the ALICE measurement (Adam et al., 2016) that the experimental upper limits exclude the existence of ΛΛ and anti-Λn bound states, showing instead that the six-quark-state ΛΛ cannot be excluded. The finding that molecular-state yields are much larger than experimental limits is consistent with the conclusion from the HypHI Collaboration (Rappold et al., 2013) that anti-Λn is unlikely to exist as a bound state. The work enables future experimental searches at LHC to focus on the six-quark-state interpretation of ΛΛ, suggesting it is on the brink of being discovered.
