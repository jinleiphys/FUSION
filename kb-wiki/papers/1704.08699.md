# Fluid Dynamics far from Local Equilibrium

> Fluid dynamics is traditionally thought to apply only to systems near local equilibrium. In this case, the effective theory of fluid dynamics can be constructed as a gradient series. Recent applications of resurgence suggest that this gradient series diverges, but can be Borel-resummed, giving rise to a hydrodynamic attractor solution which is well defined even for large gradients. Arbitrary initial data quickly approaches this attractor via non-hydrodynamic mode decay. This suggests the existence of a new theory of far-from-equilibrium fluid dynamics. In this work, the framework of fluid dynamics far from local equilibrium for conformal system is introduced, and the hydrodynamic attractor solutions for rBRSSS, kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, and strongly-coupled N=4 SYM are identified for a system undergoing Bjorken flow.

## Key claim

Fluid dynamics can be generalized to systems far from local equilibrium, where normalized gradients are large, through the existence of a non-analytic hydrodynamic attractor solution; however, far-from-equilibrium fluid dynamics will not describe most far-from-equilibrium solutions because arbitrary initial data typically excites non-hydrodynamic modes that must decay before the attractor is reached.

## Method

The author identifies hydrodynamic attractor solutions for conformal systems undergoing Bjorken flow in three microscopic theories: rBRSSS (a resummed variant of second-order conformal hydrodynamics), kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, and strongly-coupled N=4 SYM via the AdS/CFT correspondence. For each theory, the equations of motion are solved numerically for a range of initial conditions, and the attractor is identified as the unique solution to which all other initial conditions converge after non-hydrodynamic mode decay. The attractor solutions are then characterized by an effective viscosity coefficient eta_B that depends on local gradient strength.

## Key numbers

- For rBRSSS, the pole closest to the origin in the Borel plane is located at tau T = z_0^{-1}, where z_0 = 3/(2 C_pi), giving non-hydrodynamic mode contributions proportional to e^{-z_0 tau T}
- For kinetic theory, the attractor solution can be approximated by (partial ln epsilon)/(partial ln tau) = -(C_pi^2 + 0.744 C_pi (tau T) + 0.21 (tau T)^2)/(C_pi^2 + 0.6 C_pi (tau T) + 0.1575 (tau T)^2), with C_pi = 5 C_eta and C_lambda = 5/7
- For N=4 SYM, the attractor candidate starts at lim_{tau -> 0} (partial ln epsilon)/(partial ln tau) -> -1, with parameters C_eta = 1/(4 pi), C_pi = (2 - ln 2)/(2 pi), C_lambda = 1/(2 - ln 2)
- The effective viscosity eta_B/s tends to zero for large gradient strength, while recovering eta/s for small gradients

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic attractor concept first demonstrated for rBRSSS by Heller and Spalinski (2015) to kinetic theory and strongly-coupled N=4 SYM, showing the attractor is not limited to a single toy model but applies to phenomenologically relevant theories. The framework builds on earlier evidence from numerical experiments by Chesler (2009), Heller (2011), Wu (2011), and others that fluid dynamics can match exact results even when gradient corrections are of order unity. The existence of attractors in kinetic theory and AdS/CFT suggests the possibility of far-from-equilibrium attractors for the particle distribution function and space-time geometry respectively, and enables a self-contained description of out-of-equilibrium systems through Eq. (ffh) with an effective viscosity eta_B.
