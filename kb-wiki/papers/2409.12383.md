# Effective nucleus-nucleus potentials for heavy-ion fusion reactions

> Based on the Skyrme energy density functional and the reaction $Q$-value, we propose an effective nucleus-nucleus potential for describing the capture barrier in heavy-ion fusion processes. The 443 extracted barrier heights are well reproduced with a root-mean-square (rms) error of 1.53 MeV and the rms deviations with respect to 144 TDHF capture barrier heights is only 1.05 MeV. Together with the Siwek-Wilczyński formula in which the three parameters are determined by the proposed effective potentials, the measured capture cross sections at energies around the barriers can be reasonably well reproduced for a series of fusion reactions induced by not only nearly spherical nuclei but also the nuclei with large deformations such as $^{154}$Sm and $^{238}$U. The shallow capture pockets and small values of the average barrier radii play a role in the reduction of the capture cross sections for $^{52,54}$Cr and $^{64}$Ni induced reactions which are related to the synthesis of new super-heavy nuclei.

# Effective nucleus-nucleus potentials for heavy-ion fusion reactions

## Key claim
The proposed effective nucleus-nucleus potential, based on the Skyrme energy density functional and the reaction Q-value, reproduces 443 extracted barrier heights with an rms error of 1.53 MeV, but only when combined with a specific parameterization of the barrier distribution width that includes both Q-value and excitation threshold dependence; the potential is explicitly not applicable at deep sub-barrier energies because the Siwek-Wilczynski formula used does not include barrier penetration effects.

## Method
The authors first calculate frozen nucleus-nucleus potentials using the Skyrme energy density functional with the SkM* parameter set and the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF2) approach under the frozen density approximation. They then introduce an effective potential that reduces the frozen barrier by a factor depending on the distance between the two nuclei, with parameters k=0.027 and s=1.0 fm, and construct a separate expression for the potential after projectile-target contact that depends on the reaction Q-value and the driving potential difference from the dinuclear system model. The capture cross sections are calculated using the Siwek-Wilczynski formula with three parameters (barrier height, average barrier radius, and standard deviation of the Gaussian barrier distribution) determined from the effective potential, where the width W is parameterized as a combination of a Q-value-dependent term and a term depending on mass asymmetry and the energies of the lowest excited states of the reaction partners.

## Key numbers
- Root-mean-square deviation of 1.53 MeV for 443 extracted barrier heights from experimental data
- Root-mean-square deviation of 1.05 MeV for 144 capture barrier heights from TDHF calculations
- Barrier height for 40Ca+144Sm reduced from B0=150.57 MeV (frozen) to VB=143.32 MeV (effective)
- Depth of capture pocket B_cap^D = 1.71 MeV for 70Zn+209Bi, compared to 9.38 MeV for 30Si+238U
- Barrier height for 16O+154Sm is 59.42 MeV, lower than 60.51 MeV for 16O+144Sm by 1.09 MeV
- Average barrier radius R_m = 5.42 fm for 64Ni+238U, compared to 12.71 fm for 48Ca+238U
- Standard deviation W = 3.22 MeV for 64Ni+238U, compared to 4.51 MeV for 30Si+238U

## Context
This work extends the static entrance-channel nucleus-nucleus potential approach of Liu et al. (2008) by incorporating dynamical effects through a Q-value-dependent effective potential, and generalizes the TDHF-based Siwek-Wilczynski formula application of Yao et al. (2024) to reactions with strongly deformed nuclei such as 154Sm and 238U. The proposed potential reproduces the systematic barrier heights extracted by Chen et al. (2023) from 443 datasets and provides a computationally efficient alternative to time-consuming TDHF calculations for systematic studies of heavy-ion fusion reactions, particularly for systems relevant to super-heavy element synthesis such as 64Ni+238U and 54Cr+243Am.
