# Photo-production of lowest $Σ^*_{1/2^-}$ state within the Regge-effective Lagrangian approach

> Since the lowest $Σ^{*}$ state, with quantum numbers spin-parity $J^{P} =1/2^{-}$, is far from established experimentally and theoretically, we have performed a theoretical study on the $Σ^*_{1/2^-}$ photo-production within the Regge-effective Lagrangian approach. Taking into account that the $Σ^*_{1/2^-}$ couples to the $\bar{K}N$ channel, we have considered the contributions from the $t$-channel $K$ exchange diagram. Moreover, these contributions from $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange, $s$-channel nucleon pole, $u$-channel $Σ$ exchange, and the contact term, are considered. The differential and total cross sections of the process $γn \to K^{+}Σ^{*-}_{1/2^-}$ are predicted with our model parameters. The results should be helpful to search for the $Σ^*_{1/2^-}$ state experimentally in future.

## Key claim
The paper predicts the differential and total cross sections for the reaction γn → K⁺Σ*⁻₁/₂⁻, assuming the lowest Σ* state with Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ has a mass of 1480 MeV and a sizeable coupling to the K̄N channel, but the authors note that the precise couplings of this state are still unknown and the results are model-dependent.

## Method
The authors employ a Regge-effective Lagrangian approach, computing scattering amplitudes for t-channel K and K* exchanges (with Regge propagators replacing Feynman propagators), s-channel nucleon pole, u-channel Σ exchange, and a contact term to maintain gauge invariance. They use effective Lagrangian densities for electromagnetic and strong interaction vertices, with coupling constants taken from prior literature, and include form factors for s- and u-channel baryon exchanges with a cutoff parameter Λ = 1.5 GeV. The unpolarized differential cross section is then calculated in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers
- The total cross section for γn → K⁺Σ*⁻₁/₂⁻ reaches a maximum of about 4.3 μb at a photon lab energy Eγ = 2.3 GeV.
- The mass of the Σ(1480) state is taken as M = 1480 ± 15 MeV with width Γ = 60 ± 15 MeV, from the previous Review of Particle Physics.
- The strong coupling g_KNΣ* = 8.74 GeV is taken from Oller (2006), and the transition magnetic moment μ_ΣΣ* = 1.28 is taken from Kim (2021).
- The Regge trajectory for the K meson is α_K(t) = 0.7 GeV⁻² × (t − M_K²), and for the K* meson α_K*(t) = 1 + 0.83 GeV⁻² × (t − M_K*²), with scale factors s₀ᴷ = 3.0 GeV² and s₀ᴷ* = 1.5 GeV².

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical studies of the lowest Σ*₁/₂⁻ state, which has been predicted to lie in the mass range 1380–1500 MeV by various approaches including quark models and unitary chiral approaches (e.g., Oller 2006; Roca 2013; Kamano 2015). The paper specifically builds on the suggestion by Kim (2021) to search for this state in photo-production, and on the analysis of Khemchandani (2018) which found a Σ*₁/₂⁻ state around 1400 MeV. The predicted cross sections are intended to guide future experimental searches at facilities such as CLAS, LEPS, or Crystal Ball, and the authors note that the t-channel K exchange dominates at forward angles while the u-channel Σ exchange contributes at backward angles.
