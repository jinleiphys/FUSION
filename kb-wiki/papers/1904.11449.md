# Dense matter equation of state and neutron star properties from nuclear theory and experiment

> The equation of state of dense matter determines the structure of neutron stars, their typical radii, and maximum masses. Recent improvements in theoretical modeling of nuclear forces from the low-energy effective field theory of QCD has led to tighter constraints on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter at and somewhat above the densities of atomic nuclei, while the equation of state and composition of matter at high densities remains largely uncertain and open to a multitude of theoretical speculations. In the present work we review the latest advances in microscopic modeling of the nuclear equation of state and demonstrate how to consistently include also empirical nuclear data into a Bayesian posterior probability distribution for the model parameters. Derived bulk neutron star properties such as radii, moments of inertia, and tidal deformabilities are computed, and we discuss as well the limitations of our modeling.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Bayesian modeling combining chiral effective field theory calculations of the nuclear equation of state with empirical nuclear data (via Skyrme energy density functionals as a proxy) yields posterior probability distributions for neutron star properties, but the authors caution that the pressure support at very high densities (above 3 times nuclear saturation density) is poorly constrained and the inferred maximum masses should not be interpreted too strongly.

## Method
The authors parametrize the energy density of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter from chiral effective field theory (varying the chiral order from N2LO to N3LO, cutoff from 400-500 MeV, and many-body perturbation theory order from second to third) as expansions in powers of the Fermi momentum. They construct prior probability distributions for the expansion parameters from these microscopic calculations, then incorporate Bayesian likelihood functions derived from 205 Skyrme energy density functionals (for symmetric matter) and from empirical constraints on the symmetry energy J = 31 ± 1.5 MeV together with correlations among symmetry energy parameters (for neutron matter). The neutron star equation of state is constructed under beta equilibrium using a quadratic interpolation between pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, with the inner crust treated via the Wigner-Seitz cell formalism and liquid-drop model.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the radius lies in the range 10.27 km < R1.4 < 12.84 km at 95% credibility
- At 95% credibility, the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star lies in the range 100 < Λ1.4 < 500
- At 95% credibility, the moment of inertia of a 1.338 solar mass neutron star lies in the range 1.03 × 10^45 g cm^2 < I1.338 < 1.51 × 10^45 g cm^2
- The chiral effective field theory equations of state are fitted up to n = 0.32 fm^-3 (and also tested up to n = 0.25 fm^-3)
- Central densities in neutron stars range from about 3-7 n0 for soft equations of state favored by GW170817
- The symmetry energy constraint used is J = 31 ± 1.5 MeV from Lattimer (2013)

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian framework previously developed by the authors (Lim 2018a, 2018b, 2019) for combining chiral effective field theory calculations with nuclear experimental data. It builds on microscopic calculations of the nuclear equation of state from chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2010, Drischler 2014, Holt 2017) and empirical constraints from heavy nuclei (Dutra 2012) and neutron star observations (Steiner 2010, Raithel 2016, 2017). The results are directly comparable to constraints from the GW170817 neutron star merger event (Abbott 2018b) and provide a baseline for future NICER radius measurements and LIGO/Virgo tidal deformability observations to constrain the dense matter equation of state at densities around twice saturation density.
