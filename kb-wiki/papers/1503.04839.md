# Light-front representation of chiral dynamics in peripheral transverse densities

> The nucleon's electromagnetic form factors are expressed in terms of the transverse densities of charge and magnetization at fixed light-front time. At peripheral transverse distances $b = O(M_π^{-1})$ the densities are governed by chiral dynamics and can be calculated model-independently using chiral effective field theory (EFT). We represent the leading-order chiral EFT results for the peripheral transverse densities as overlap integrals of chiral light-front wave functions, describing the transition of the initial nucleon to soft pion-nucleon intermediate states and back. The new representation (a) explains the parametric order of the peripheral transverse densities; (b) establishes an inequality between the spin-independent and -dependent densities; (c) exposes the role of pion orbital angular momentum in chiral dynamics; (d) reveals a large left-right asymmetry of the current in a transversely polarized nucleon and suggests a simple interpretation. The light-front representation enables a first-quantized, quantum-mechanical view of chiral dynamics that is fully relativistic and exactly equivalent to the second-quantized, field-theoretical formulation. It relates the charge and magnetization densities measured in low-energy elastic scattering to the generalized parton distributions probed in peripheral high-energy scattering processes. The method can be applied to nucleon form factors of other operators, e.g. the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key claim

The peripheral transverse densities of the nucleon, computed at leading order in chiral effective field theory, can be exactly represented as overlap integrals of light-front wave functions describing the transition of the nucleon to soft pion-nucleon intermediate states; this representation explains the parametric order of the densities, proves an inequality between the spin-independent and spin-dependent densities, and reveals that the approximate saturation of this inequality is a consequence of the relativistic character of chiral dynamics.

## Method

The authors start from the known Lorentz-invariant chiral EFT result for the isovector nucleon current matrix element at leading order, which involves triangle and contact diagrams with two-pion exchange. They rewrite the Feynman integrals into a form with pseudoscalar πNN coupling and an effective contact term, then perform a three-dimensional reduction in a frame where the momentum transfer is purely transverse, closing the contour around the nucleon pole. The resulting expression is an overlap integral of light-front wave functions defined as the vertex function from the chiral Lagrangian divided by the invariant mass difference of the N → πN transition.

## Key numbers

- The peripheral transverse densities decay exponentially as exp(-2M_π b), where M_π is the pion mass and the range is set by the minimal mass of the exchanged two-pion system.
- The isovector charge density ρ₁^V(b) and the modified magnetization density \widetildeρ₂^V(b) ≡ (∂/∂b)[ρ₂^V(b)/(2M_N)] are of the same order in the chiral expansion and obey the inequality |\widetildeρ₂^V(b)| < ρ₁^V(b), which is approximately saturated (\widetildeρ₂^V(b) ≈ ρ₁^V(b)) at distances b ≳ 1 M_π^{-1}.
- The effective contact term in the current matrix element is proportional to (1 - g_A²), where g_A is the axial coupling constant; for a pointlike Dirac fermion g_A = 1, so this term expresses internal structure and is numerically small.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive calculation of peripheral transverse densities in Granados and Weiss (2013) by providing a first-quantized, particle-based representation using light-front quantization, which was not available in the earlier invariant formulation. The light-front representation connects the charge and magnetization densities measured in low-energy elastic scattering to the generalized parton distributions probed in peripheral high-energy scattering processes, as discussed by Burkardt (2000) and Strikman and Weiss (2003, 2009). The method can be applied to nucleon form factors of other operators, such as the energy-momentum tensor.
