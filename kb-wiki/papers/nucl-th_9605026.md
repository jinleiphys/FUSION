# Pion Transparency in 500 MeV C(pi,pi') Reactions?

> The question whether there is a pion transparency in 500 MeV $C(π, π^{'})$ scatterings is studied using a semiclassical, hadronic transport model. The double differential cross sections of this reaction measured at LAMPF can be largely accounted for, if one uses energy-dependent, anisotropic angular distributions which are fitted to pion-nucleon scattering data for the decay of $Δ(1232)$ and $N^*(1440)$ resonances. The remaining discrepancy between the data and the calculation sets a limit on effects of more exotic processes.

## Key claim

The observed double differential cross sections in 500 MeV C(π,π') reactions can be largely accounted for by a semiclassical hadronic transport model using energy-dependent, anisotropic angular distributions fitted to pion-nucleon scattering data for Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonance decays, but a remaining discrepancy of up to a factor of three in parts of the energy-loss spectra leaves open the possibility of exotic processes such as in-medium modifications of pion-nucleon cross sections.

## Method

The authors used the BUU/ART semiclassical hadronic transport model, originally developed for heavy-ion collisions, to simulate 500 MeV π+ scattering on a carbon target. Pion scatterings were modeled through formation and decay of Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonances, with the resonances forced to decay either isotropically or according to measured energy-dependent pion-nucleon angular distributions expressed as Legendre polynomial expansions. Pion absorption was modeled through two-step two-body processes (π+N→Δ(N*) followed by Δ(N*)+N→N+N) and quasi-deuteron absorption using a parameterized experimental cross section formula, with nucleons initialized using the experimentally measured charge distribution of carbon and local Thomas-Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

- The ratio σ(π+N→π+Δ)/σ(π+N→ρ+N) is approximately constant at 1.7 for center-of-mass energies 1.22 ≤ √sπN ≤ 1.66 GeV
- The quasi-deuteron absorption cross section parameterization uses a = -1.2 mb, b = 3.5 mb·MeV-1/2, c = 7.4×104 mb·MeV-2, d = 5600 MeV2, and ER = 2136 MeV
- The carbon nucleus charge distribution parameters are c = 2.36 fm, z = 0.522 fm, and w = -0.149
- The RQMD 1.08 model underestimates the data at forward angles by a factor of 2-7 and overestimates at large angles by a factor of 2-6 for energy losses between 200 and 300 MeV
- Increasing the pion-nucleon inelastic cross section by a factor of three largely removes the discrepancy at energy losses Tin - Tout ≥ 150 MeV

## Context

This work addresses the question of pion transparency raised by LAMPF experiments (1995) where inclusive (π,π') cross sections at 500 MeV with outgoing pion energies near 200 MeV failed to show the strong depletion predicted by an intranuclear cascade model (Gibbs). The study extends earlier work using the BUU/ART model from heavy-ion collisions to pion-nucleus scattering, and contrasts with the RQMD 1.08 model (John) which showed larger discrepancies. The results suggest that evidence for a 2 fm/c formation time for pions from the (π,2π) process, previously reported by Gibbs, is not yet conclusive, and the remaining discrepancy enables future studies of possible in-medium modifications of hadron properties related to partial restoration of chiral symmetry.
