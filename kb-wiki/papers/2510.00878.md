# Bulk and spectroscopic nuclear properties within an ab initio renormalized random-phase approximation framework

> A modern chiral potential incorporating the three-body force is adopted to investigate bulk properties, spectra, and nuclear responses of closed-(sub)shell nuclei throughout the nuclear chart within a particle-hole (p-h) renormalized random-phase approximation (RRPA) scheme using a Hartree- Fock (HF) single-particle basis. Our analysis shows that all instabilities induced by the quasiboson approximation (QBA) underlying RPA are removed and an overall better consistency with the experiments is achieved for all observables of the investigated nuclei. The residual discrepancies point out the need of going beyond the p-h space.

## Key claim

The renormalized random-phase approximation (RRPA) removes all instabilities induced by the quasiboson approximation (QBA) underlying standard RPA, achieving better consistency with experiment for all observables, but residual discrepancies point to the need of going beyond the particle-hole (p-h) space.

## Method

The authors adopt the chiral potential ΔN²LO_GO(394) incorporating a three-body force, generate a self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) single-particle basis within a harmonic oscillator space up to N_max=14, and solve the eigenvalue equations within Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA), standard RPA, and renormalized RPA (RRPA). The RRPA iteratively solves nonlinear coupled equations that account for ground-state correlations by computing occupation numbers from the one-body density matrix (OBDM) and using a natural orbital basis, rather than replacing the correlated ground state with the uncorrelated HF wavefunction as in the QBA.

## Context

This work extends the renormalized RPA method developed for metal clusters by Catara et al. (1996, 1998) and applied to nuclei by Papakonstantinou et al. (2007) using the UCOM potential, now employing a modern chiral potential with three-body forces. The results are compared with coupled-cluster (CC) calculations from Jiang et al. (2020) and IMSRG(2) calculations using the imsrg++ code by Stroberg, showing comparable accuracy for binding energies and low-lying levels. The approach enables systematic first-principles studies of closed-(sub)shell nuclei across the nuclear chart from ⁴He to ²⁰⁸Pb at moderate computational cost, and the authors suggest future extension to open-shell nuclei via Bogoliubov quasiparticles and to larger configuration spaces via the equation of motion multiphonon method (EMPM) by De Gregorio et al. (2016).
