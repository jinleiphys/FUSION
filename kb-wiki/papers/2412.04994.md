# Excited cluster states: A new source for proton number fluctuations in the high baryon density regime

> We calculate the contribution of the decay products of excited nuclear cluster states to the event-by-event fluctuations of protons in the energy range from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2-5$~GeV within the statistical model. We find that the inclusion of the excited nuclear clusters yields corrections to all cumulant ratios, ranging from 1\% for ratio of second to first-order cumulant to 100\% for the sixth to second order cumulant towards the lowest inspected energy. As expected the contribution of excited cluster states is most important at low energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}<3.5$~GeV and becomes negligible at higher collision energies. Especially in light of the expected ultra-high precision data from CBM at FAIR, this new contribution is important to allow for a quantitative comparison with (potentially later available) lattice QCD or effective model results.

## Key claim

The decay products of excited nuclear cluster states, previously omitted from baseline calculations, produce corrections to proton number fluctuation cumulant ratios that range from 1% for the second-to-first order cumulant ratio up to 100% for the sixth-to-second order cumulant ratio at the lowest inspected collision energy, with the effect becoming negligible above \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.5\) GeV.

## Method

The authors used a grand-canonical statistical hadron resonance gas model, treating excited nuclear clusters as resonances that decay into at most one proton with a given probability. Cumulants of the net-proton number distribution up to sixth order were computed from the cumulant-generating function for resonance decays, using the list of excited cluster states from Vovchenko (2020) and Tilley (1992, 2002). The calculations were performed at chemical freeze-out with temperature and baryochemical potential parametrized as functions of collision energy following Vovchenko (2015), for central Au+Au reactions in the range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2-5\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* Correction to \(\cum{(\delN)}{2}/\cum{N}{}\) (variance over mean) is 1% at the lowest inspected energy.
* Correction to \(\cum{(\delN)}{6}/\cum{(\delN)}{2}\) (hyperkurtosis times variance squared) is 100% at the lowest inspected energy.
* The effect is most important for collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}<3.5\) GeV.
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4\) GeV, the scaling variable \(r_{ij}\) shows the excited cluster states cut up to one sixth from the distance of the cumulant ratio from unity.
* The calculation covers the energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2-5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends baseline fluctuation calculations for the QCD phase diagram search, which previously omitted contributions from high-mass nuclear cluster decays. It builds on the hadron resonance gas approach of Tomasik (2021) and uses the cluster state list from Vovchenko (2020), who showed these states contribute to average yields of light nuclei. The results are directly relevant for interpreting the upcoming ultra-high precision data from the CBM experiment at FAIR, enabling quantitative comparisons with lattice QCD susceptibilities at finite baryon density and effective model results.
