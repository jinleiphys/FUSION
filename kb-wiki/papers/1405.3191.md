# B(E2) Predictions for Even-Even Nuclei in the Differential Equation Model

> We use the recently developed Differential Equation Model for the reduced electric quadrupole transition probability B(E2) for predicting its values for a wide range of even-even nuclides almost throughout the nuclear landscape from Neon to Californium. This is made possible as the principal equation in the model, namely, the differential equation connecting the B(E2) value of a given nucleus with its derivatives with respect to neutron and proton numbers provides two different recursion relations, each connecting three different neighboring even-even nuclei from lower to higher mass numbers and vice-verse. These relations helped us to extrapolate from known to unknown terrain of the B(E2) landscape and thereby facilitate its predictions throughout.

# B(E2) Predictions for Even-Even Nuclei in the Differential Equation Model

## Key claim

The Differential Equation Model (DEM) can predict B(E2) values for even-even nuclei from Neon to Californium with 93% of predictions agreeing with experiment within a factor of two, but the predictions are limited to extrapolations of up to 3 to 5 steps from known data due to accumulation of errors from successive generations of recursion.

## Method

The authors derived two recursion relations from a differential equation connecting the B(E2) value of a nucleus to its partial derivatives with respect to neutron and proton numbers. Using the combined experimental data set of Raman et al. and Pritychenko et al. (325 even-even nuclides from Z=10 to 98), they applied forward and backward recursion relations to generate first-generation predictions, then iterated these predictions with experimental data to obtain second- and third-generation predictions for neighboring unknown nuclei. Uncertainties were propagated through each generation using standard error analysis from the experimental uncertainties of input data points.

## Key numbers

- 325 even-even nuclides from the combined experimental data set of Raman et al. and Pritychenko et al. served as input, spanning Z=10 to 98 (Neon to Californium)
- 251 additional nuclides were predicted going up to 3rd order of extrapolations
- 93% of DEM predictions agreed with experimental values within a factor of two, classified as "excellent" by the yardstick of Raman et al.
- Predictions were confined to extrapolations of 3 to 5 steps on either side of the known data base

## Context

This work extends the Differential Equation Model previously developed by the same authors (Nayak and Pattnaik, 2014) for B(E2) values, which was inspired by the Infinite Nuclear Matter (INM) model's local energy relation that successfully predicted nuclear masses. The DEM predictions show better agreement with experimental trends than the Single-Shell Asymptotic Nilsson Model (SSANM) and the Finite-Range Droplet Model (FRDM), particularly for isotope series with sharply changing nuclear structures. The approach enables systematic filling of gaps in the B(E2) landscape for even-even nuclei where experimental data are unavailable, though the accumulation of uncertainties limits the number of extrapolation steps possible.
