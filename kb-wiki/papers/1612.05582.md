# Triple parton scatterings in high-energy proton-proton collisions

> A generic expression to compute triple parton scattering (TPS) cross sections in high-energy proton-proton (pp) collisions is presented as a function of the corresponding single-parton cross sections and the transverse parton distribution in the proton encoded in an effective parameter $σ_{\rm eff,TPS}$. The value of $σ_{\rm eff,TPS}$ is closely related to the similar effective cross section that characterizes double-parton scatterings, and amounts to $σ_{\rm eff,TPS} = 12.5 \pm 4.5$ mb. Estimates for triple charm ($\rm c\overline{c}$) and bottom ($\rm b\overline{b}$) production in pp collisions at LHC and FCC energies are presented based on next-to-next-to-leading order perturbative calculations for single $\rm c\overline{c},\rm b\overline{b}$ cross sections. At $\sqrt{s}\approx$ 100 TeV, about 15% of the pp collisions produce three $\rm c\overline{c}$ pairs from three different parton-parton scatterings.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generic expression for triple parton scattering (TPS) cross sections in proton-proton collisions, showing that the effective TPS cross section parameter sigma_eff,TPS is numerically very close to the double parton scattering (DPS) effective cross section sigma_eff, with the specific value sigma_eff,TPS = 12.5 ± 4.5 mb, under the assumption that longitudinal and transverse degrees of freedom factorize and that multiparton correlations are absent.

## Method

The authors derive a factorized expression for TPS cross sections as a triple product of single-parton scattering (SPS) cross sections divided by the square of an effective TPS cross section sigma_eff,TPS, which is obtained from the integral of the cube of the hadron-hadron overlap function. They compute sigma_eff,TPS from the measured DPS effective cross section sigma_eff ≈ 15 ± 5 mb using several analytical models for the proton transverse profile (hard-sphere, Gaussian, exponential, and dipole form factor), and then apply the result to estimate triple charm and bottom production cross sections using NNLO perturbative QCD calculations for single heavy-quark production.

## Key numbers

- sigma_eff,TPS = 12.5 ± 4.5 mb, derived from sigma_eff ≈ 15 ± 5 mb with the relation sigma_eff,TPS = k × sigma_eff where k = 0.82 ± 0.11
- At sqrt(s) = 14 TeV (LHC): sigma(ccbar+X) = 7.1 ± 3.5 (scale) ± 0.3 (PDF) mb; sigma(ccbar ccbar ccbar+X) = 0.39 ± 0.28 (total) mb
- At sqrt(s) = 100 TeV (FCC): sigma(ccbar+X) = 25.0 ± 16.0 (scale) ± 1.3 (PDF) mb; sigma(ccbar ccbar ccbar+X) = 16.7 ± 11.8 (total) mb
- At sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: sigma(bbbar+X) = 0.56 ± 0.09 (scale) ± 0.01 (PDF) mb; sigma(bbbar bbbar bbbar+X) = (0.19 ± 0.12) × 10^(-3) mb
- At sqrt(s) = 100 TeV: sigma(bbbar+X) = 2.8 ± 0.6 (scale) ± 0.1 (PDF) mb; sigma(bbbar bbbar bbbar+X) = (24 ± 17) × 10^(-3) mb
- At sqrt(s) ≈ 100 TeV, about 15% of pp collisions produce three ccbar pairs from three different parton-parton scatterings

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of multiparton interactions (MPI) and double parton scattering (DPS) by Calucci and Treleani (2009), Maina (2009), and Snigirev (2016), providing the first quantitative estimates of triple parton scattering rates based on measured DPS effective cross sections. The results enable experimental searches for TPS at the LHC and future colliders such as the FCC, and provide a tool for characterizing backgrounds to rare final states with multiple heavy particles in searches for new physics.
