# Scaling Distributions of Quarks, Mesons and Proton for all $p_T$, Energy and Centrality

> We present the evidences for the existence of a universal scaling behavior of the production of $π^0$ at all transverse momenta in heavy-ion collisions at all centralities and all collision energies. The corresponding scaling behavior of the quarks is then derived just before the quarks recombine with antiquarks to form the pions. The degradation effect of the dense medium on the quark $p_T$ is derived from the scaling distribution. In the recombination model it is then possible to calculate the $p_T$ distributions of the produced proton and kaon, which are scaling also. Experimentally verifiable predictions are made. Implications of the existence of the scaling behavior are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a universal scaling function \(\Phi(z)\) describes \(\pi^0\) production in heavy-ion collisions across all transverse momenta, centralities, and collision energies, but this scaling breaks down at very high \(z\) (low collision energy) due to kinematic constraints from energy conservation, as seen in the 17 GeV data where \(p_T > 3\) GeV/c deviates from the universal curve.

## Method

The authors analyzed preliminary PHENIX data for \(\pi^0\) production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=130\) and 200 GeV) and WA98 data for Pb+Pb collisions at 17 GeV, combining energy scaling at maximum centrality with centrality scaling at fixed energy into a single factorizable scaling factor \(K(s,N)=K(s)K(N)\). Using the recombination model, they derived the quark distribution \(F_q(z)\) from the pion scaling function, then calculated proton and kaon distributions by applying the same model with appropriate recombination functions and valon distributions.

## Key numbers

- Scaling factor for energy: \(K(s)=0.69+1.55\times10^{-3}\sqrt{s}\) (GeV/c), normalized to 1 at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.
- Scaling factor for centrality: \(K(N)=1.226-6.36\times10^{-4}N\), normalized to 1 at \(N=350\).
- Normalization factor: \(A(N_c)=530N_c^{-0.9}\), with \(N_c=0.44N^{1.33}\).
- Fitted scaling distribution: \(\Phi(z)=1200\,(z^2+2)^{-4.8}\left(1+25\,{\rm e}^{-4.5z}\right)\).
- Quark distribution: \(F_q(z)=15\,(z^2+0.47z+0.72)^{-4.25}\).
- Average quark scaling variable: \(\langle z\rangle=0.415\).
- Degradation parameter: \(\lambda=5.35\times 10^{-4}\) for the centrality dependence of quark moments, giving \(\langle x\rangle_{350}/\langle x\rangle_2=0.83\) (17% loss in average quark \(p_T\) at maximum centrality).
- Strange-to-light quark ratio: \(s/q=0.256\) at \(\sqrt{s}=130\) GeV.
- Kaon formation fractions: \(\kappa=0.628\) for \(\bar K\) from \(s\) quarks, \(\bar{\kappa}=0.713\) for \(K\) from \(\bar s\) quarks at 130 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on energy scaling (Paper I) and centrality scaling (Paper II) of \(\pi^0\) production at RHIC, combining them into a unified scaling framework. It enables predictions for proton and kaon transverse momentum distributions that can be tested experimentally, and suggests that the regularity of quark \(p_T\) distributions implies that low-mass particle production is not a sensitive probe for quark-gluon plasma signals, unlike \(J/\psi\) production as discussed by Matsui and Satz (1986). The recombination model approach follows the valon model of Hwa and Yang (1985) and avoids the complexity of parton evolution from hard collisions treated by other groups (e.g., Gyulassy and Wang, 1994; Baier et al., 1997).
