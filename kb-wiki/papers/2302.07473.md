# Parton Decomposition of Nucleon Spin and Momentum: Gluons from Dressed Quarks

> The lowest two Mellin moments of hadronic Generalized Parton Distributions are explored within a model that allows investigation of the inter-related quark and gluon contributions. For light quarks their dynamical connection is strong due to quark dressing. Our principal focus is the angular momentum $J$ of the nucleon. This work employs and extends dynamical insights obtained from our recent model results for quark and gluon momentum fractions $\langle x \rangle_{\rm q/g}$ in both pion and nucleon. The employed model is based on the Rainbow-Ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD. The special case of a 1-loop treatment of a single hadronic quark is used to motivate several insights and obtain initial estimates such as the Wilson line correction to the established Landau gauge model ($ -7\%$ for both $J_{\rm q}$ and $\langle x \rangle_{\rm q}$), and the "binding gluon" contribution to $\langle x \rangle_{\rm g}$ ($\leq 10\%$). We obtain the proton $J$ within 1\% after inclusion of the pion cloud mechanism to produce the sea. The gluon second Mellin moments of the proton reflect similar dynamics; ($\langle x \rangle_{\rm g}$, $J_{\rm g}$) are (26\% , 24\%) at model scale, and (40\%, 38\%) at $2$ GeV. We also find that $J_{\rm tot}$ is shared almost equally between the total orbital and total intrinsic spin contributions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the gluon contributions to nucleon angular momentum and momentum fraction arise predominantly from the dressing of light quarks via dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and that the total angular momentum of the proton can be reproduced within 1% after including the pion cloud mechanism for the sea, with the caveat that the quark contributions require a Wilson line correction of approximately -7% when calculated in Landau gauge.

## Method

The authors employ the Rainbow-Ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) of QCD to compute the second Mellin moments of unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the nucleon, extracting the quark and gluon contributions to angular momentum J and momentum fraction ⟨x⟩ via the Ji sum rule. The approach uses dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter vertices, with the gluon-in-quark contributions generated from the quark dressing mechanism, and includes a 1-loop treatment of a single dressed quark to estimate Wilson line corrections and "binding gluon" contributions.

## Key numbers

- Wilson line correction to Landau gauge quark results: -6.5% for ⟨x⟩_q and -7.5% for J_q, estimated from a 1-loop dynamical calculation with parameters simulating the DSE-RL model at scale μ₀ = 0.64 GeV
- "Binding gluon" contribution to ⟨x⟩_g: ≤ 10%
- Proton gluon second Mellin moments at model scale: (⟨x⟩_g, J_g) = (26%, 24%)
- Proton gluon second Mellin moments at 2 GeV: (⟨x⟩_g, J_g) = (40%, 38%)
- Proton J reproduced within 1% after inclusion of pion cloud mechanism
- Nucleon model scale results at μ₀ = 0.64 GeV: ⟨x⟩_u_v = 0.413, ⟨x⟩_d_v = 0.175, ⟨x⟩_sea = 0.160, ⟨x⟩_g = 0.252
- Nucleon results at √5 GeV: ⟨x⟩_u_v = 0.265, ⟨x⟩_d_v = 0.112, ⟨x⟩_sea = 0.198, ⟨x⟩_g = 0.425 (comparable to NNPDF3.0 values of 0.273, 0.111, 0.175, 0.441 respectively)
- Pion model scale results at μ₀ = 0.9 GeV: 2⟨x⟩_u_v = 0.602, ⟨x⟩_sea = 0.141, ⟨x⟩_g = 0.257
- Pion results at √10 GeV: 2⟨x⟩_u_v = 0.453, ⟨x⟩_sea = 0.152, ⟨x⟩_g = 0.395 (comparable to JAM values of 0.45±0.01, 0.17±0.01, 0.37±0.02 respectively)

## Context

This work extends the DSE-RL approach previously applied to pion and nucleon PDFs by Nguyen (2011), Chang (2014), Chen (2016), Bednar (2018), and Freese (2021) to now include the angular momentum decomposition via GPD second Mellin moments, going beyond the Ward Identity vertex approximation used in earlier studies. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD calculations from Alexandrou (2017, 2020) and global analyses such as NNPDF3.0 from Ball (2014) and JAM from Barry (2018), and provide a framework for interpreting measurements at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron Ion Collider. The finding that J_tot is shared almost equally between total orbital and total intrinsic spin contributions addresses a long-standing question in nucleon spin structure that previous SU(6) modeling approaches could not resolve in a gauge-invariant manner.
