# Sensitivity to multi-phonon excitations in heavy-ion fusion reactions

> Measured cross sections for the fusion of {64}Ni with {64}Ni, {74}Ge, and {100}Mo targets are analyzed in a coupled-channels approach. The data for the {64}Ni target above 0.1 mb are reproduced by including couplings to the low-lying 2^+ and 3^- states and the mutual and two-phonon excitations of these states. The calculations become more challenging as the fusing nuclei become softer and heavier, and excitations to multi-phonon states start to play an increasingly important role. Thus it is necessary to include up to four-phonon excitations in order to reproduce the data for the {64}Ni+{74}Ge system. Similar calculations for {64}Ni+{100}Mo, and also for the symmetric {74}Ge+{74}Ge system, show large discrepancies with the data. Possible ways to improve the calculations are discussed.

## Key claim

Measured fusion cross sections for heavy, soft nuclei can be reproduced by coupled-channels calculations only if multi-phonon excitations up to the four-phonon level are included, but even then the calculations fail for the heaviest systems studied (64Ni+100Mo and 74Ge+74Ge), where large discrepancies remain.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed in the rotating frame (isocentrifugal) approximation, using an empirical proximity-type ion-ion potential with ingoing wave boundary conditions at a radial minimum inside the Coulomb barrier. Couplings to inelastic channels were expressed in terms of radial derivatives of the ion-ion potential up to second order in the deformation amplitudes, and effective multi-phonon states were constructed from known B(E2) transition strengths and energy-weighted sums.

## Key numbers

- For 64Ni+64Ni fusion data above 0.1 mb, the best fit using two-phonon calculations gave chi-squared per point of 10, with an energy shift of 0.27 MeV when including the two-phonon 3- state and mutual 3- excitations.
- For 64Ni+74Ge, including up to four-phonon excitations (2PH-4) improved the fit, requiring an energy shift of -0.57 MeV (equivalent to increasing Delta R from 0.10 to 0.16 fm).
- For 64Ni+100Mo, the best fit (3PH-3 calculation) still gave chi-squared/N = 25 for all data, reduced to 15 when restricting to cross sections above 0.1 mb.
- For 74Ge+74Ge, the 3PH-4 calculation gave chi-squared/N = 17, and the calculation had not converged with respect to multi-phonon excitations.
- The effective three-phonon beta-value for 100Mo was determined as beta_2^C(3ph) = 0.182 with an excitation energy of 1.65 MeV, compared to the harmonic limit of beta_2^C = 0.231.

## Context

This work extends the systematic coupled-channels analysis of heavy-ion fusion reactions from lighter systems (previously studied by Esbensen and Landowne (2000) and others) to heavier, softer nuclei where multi-phonon excitations become increasingly important. The calculations reproduce 64Ni+64Ni data well but fail for 64Ni+100Mo and 74Ge+74Ge, consistent with the suppression of fusion above the Coulomb barrier identified by Newton et al. (2001, 2004) and the hindrance at extreme subbarrier energies. The results enable future work to develop more realistic structure models (such as those by Takigawa and Anh (2001)) and to improve the nuclear interaction at large deformation amplitudes.
