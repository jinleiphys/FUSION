# Nuclear Effects In The F_3 Structure Function For Finite And Asymptotic Q^2

> We study nuclear effects in the structure function F_3 which describes the parity violating part of the charged-current neuitrino nucleon deep inelastic scattering. Starting from a covariant approach we derive a factorized expression for the nuclear structure function in terms of nuclear spectral function and off-shell nucleon structure functions valid for arbitrary momentum transfer Q and in the limit of weak nuclear binding, i.e. when a nucleus can be treated as a non-relativistic system. We develop a systematic expansion of nuclear structure functions in terms of Q^{-2} series caused by nuclear effects ("nuclear twist" series). Basing on this expansion we calculate nuclear corrections to the Gross-Llewellyn-Smith sum rule as well as to higher moments of F_3. We show that corrections to the GLS sum rule due to nuclear effects cancel out in the Bjorken limit and calculate the corresponding Q^{-2} correction. Special attention is payed to the discussion of the off-shell effects in the structure functions. A sizable impact of these effects both on Q^2- and x-dependence of nuclear structure functions is found.

## Key claim
The paper shows that nuclear corrections to the Gross-Llewellyn-Smith (GLS) sum rule for the structure function \(F_3\) cancel exactly in the Bjorken limit (infinite \(Q^2\)), but a finite \(Q^{-2}\) correction remains; the off-shell behavior of the nucleon structure function has a sizable impact on both the \(Q^2\)- and \(x\)-dependence of nuclear \(F_3\), a nuance not captured by the abstract's general statement of cancellation.

## Method
The author uses a covariant impulse approximation to derive a factorized expression for the nuclear structure function \(F_3^A\) in terms of the nuclear spectral function and off-shell nucleon structure functions, valid for arbitrary momentum transfer \(Q\). A non-relativistic expansion of nuclear matrix elements is performed to obtain a generalized convolution equation, which is then expanded systematically in a \(Q^{-2}\) series ("nuclear twist" series). Off-shell effects in the nucleon structure function are modeled using a spectral representation with a single effective mass and a cutoff parameter, constrained by the condition that the GLS integral is not renormalized off-shell.

## Key numbers
- The effective expansion parameter for the nuclear \(Q^{-2}\) series is \(\langle \mathbf{p}^2 \rangle x^2 / Q^2\), where \(\langle \mathbf{p}^2 \rangle\) is the average squared nucleon momentum in the nucleus.
- The off-shell nucleon structure function is modeled with a spectral density \(\rho\) using a cutoff \(\Lambda^2 = 1.2\ \text{GeV}^2\) and exponent \(n=4\), fixed from fits to nucleon data at average \(Q^2 \approx 5-10\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The GLS sum rule for the nucleon is \(S_{\text{GLS}} = 3\) at asymptotic \(Q^2\), and the paper calculates the \(Q^{-2}\) correction to this sum rule due to nuclear effects.

## Context
This work extends the covariant impulse approximation formalism previously applied to the spin-independent structure functions \(F_{1,2}\) (Kulagin 1989, Kulagin et al. 1994) and spin structure functions \(g_{1,2}\) (Kulagin et al. 1995) to the parity-violating structure function \(F_3\) in neutrino deep inelastic scattering. It addresses the lack of theoretical studies on nuclear effects in neutrino DIS, particularly for \(F_3\), which has no analog in charged-lepton scattering. The results enable a reliable extraction of the free-nucleon \(F_3\) from nuclear data (e.g., from iron targets in CCFR 1996) and provide a framework for QCD analyses at finite \(Q^2\).
