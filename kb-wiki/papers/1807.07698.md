# Extracting Nuclear Symmetry Energies at High Densities from Observations of Neutron Stars and Gravitational Waves

> By numerically inverting the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation using an explicitly isospin-dependent parametric Equation of State (EOS) of dense neutron-rich nucleonic matter, a restricted EOS parameter space is established using observational constraints on the radius, maximum mass, tidal polarizability and causality condition of neutron stars (NSs). The constraining band obtained for the pressure as a function of energy (baryon) density is in good agreement with that extracted recently by the LIGO+Virgo Collaborations from their improved analyses of the NS tidal polarizability in GW170817. Rather robust upper and lower boundaries on nuclear symmetry energies are extracted from the observational constraints up to about twice the saturation density $ρ_0$ of nuclear matter. More quantitatively, the symmetry energy at $2ρ_0$ is constrained to $E_{\rm{sym}}(2ρ_0)=46.9\pm10.1$ MeV excluding many existing theoretical predictions scattered between $E_{\rm{sym}}(2ρ_0)=15$ and 100 MeV. Moreover, by studying variations of the causality surface where the speed of sound equals that of light at central densities of the most massive neutron stars within the restricted EOS parameter space, the absolutely maximum mass of neutron stars is found to be 2.40 M$_{\odot}$ approximately independent of the EOSs used. This limiting mass is consistent with findings of several recent analyses and numerical general relativity simulations about the maximum mass of the possible super-massive remanent produced in the immediate aftermath of GW170817.

## Key claim

By numerically inverting the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation with an explicitly isospin-dependent parametric equation of state, the authors extract upper and lower boundaries on nuclear symmetry energies up to about twice saturation density, constraining the symmetry energy at 2ρ₀ to 46.9 ± 10.1 MeV, which excludes many existing theoretical predictions that scatter between 15 and 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors parameterized the energy of symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy separately as functions of density up to (ρ/ρ₀)³ terms, fixing low-density parameters at their most probable values while leaving three high-density parameters (J₀, K_sym, J_sym) free. They then numerically inverted the TOV equation to compute neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, scanning the high-density parameter space to identify which combinations satisfy observational constraints on radius, maximum mass, tidal deformability from GW170817, and the causality condition (speed of sound ≤ speed of light). The crust-core transition was treated self-consistently using the NV and BPS equations of state for the inner and outer crust, respectively.

## Key numbers

* The symmetry energy at twice saturation density is constrained to E_sym(2ρ₀) = 46.9 ± 10.1 MeV.
* The absolutely maximum mass of neutron stars is found to be 2.40 M_⊙, approximately independent of the equation of state used.
* The minimum maximum mass from the observed pulsar PSR J0348+0432 is 2.01 M_⊙ (with uncertainty ±0.04 M_⊙).
* The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter is fixed at K₀ = 230 ± 20 MeV.
* The symmetry energy at saturation density is fixed at E_sym(ρ₀) = 31.7 ± 3.2 MeV with slope L = 58.7 ± 28.1 MeV.
* The crust-core transition density and pressure are restricted to approximately 0.08 fm⁻³ and 0.4 MeV fm⁻³ when using the empirical K_sym-L correlation from Tews et al. (2017).

## Context

This work extends the approach of numerically inverting the TOV equation using an isospin-dependent parametric EOS, first introduced by the authors in Zhang and Li (2018). It directly addresses the long-standing controversy in nuclear physics over the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy, where predictions from over 520 nuclear energy density functionals and many-body theories (including Brueckner Hartree-Fock, Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock, Chiral Effective Field Theory, and Variational Many Body theory) scatter between 15 and 100 MeV at 2ρ₀. The extracted constraints on the symmetry energy and the pressure band are consistent with the LIGO+Virgo Collaborations' improved analyses of the tidal deformability in GW170817 (2018). The limiting mass of 2.40 M_⊙ agrees with findings from electromagnetic signals and numerical general relativity simulations of GW170817 by Margalit and Metzger (2017), Shibata et al. (2017), Rezzolla et al. (2018), Ruiz et al. (2018), and Radice et al. (2018). The results enable future work to further narrow the symmetry energy at densities above 2ρ₀ using additional neutron star observations and to test general relativity against alternative theories of gravity.
