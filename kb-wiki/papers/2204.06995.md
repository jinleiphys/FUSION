# $β^{2}$ corrections to spherical EDF calculations for root-mean-square charge radii

> Root-mean-square charge radii are discussed in terms of spherical Energy Density Functional (EDF) models corrected for quadrupole deformations. Comparisons between experiment and theory are made for the absolute radii of all even-even nuclei, for the isotonic shift between cadmium and tin isotopes, the isotopic shifts of the calcium isotopes and the isotonic shift for nuclei with $ N=28 $. We conclude that the data are well described in this approach, except for the sharp rise just after the neutron magic numbers.

# β² corrections to spherical EDF calculations for root-mean-square charge radii

## Key claim

Spherical Energy Density Functional (EDF) calculations supplemented with β₂² corrections derived from experimental B(E2) values provide a reasonably good description of root-mean-square charge radii for even-even nuclei across a wide range, except for the sharp rise in charge radii observed just after neutron magic numbers (N=28, 50, 82, 132), which remains unexplained by this approach.

## Method

The authors start with spherical EDF calculations using 12 Skyrme functionals (representative sets s8 and s17) with self-consistent exact-pairing calculations based on (J,T)=(0,1) two-body matrix elements from configuration-interaction Hamiltonians. They then apply a β² correction using the Bohr collective model formula ⟨r²⟩ = ⟨r²⟩₀[1 + 5β²/(4π)], where β² is obtained from experimental B(E2,0⁺→2⁺₁) values. For calcium isotopes and N=28 isotones, configuration-mixing calculations using the ZBM2-modified Hamiltonian and GXPF1A Hamiltonian respectively are employed to compute ⟨Q·Q⟩ values for both even-even and odd-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the increase in rms charge radii after magic numbers: ΔRch(⁴⁹Ca–⁴⁸Ca) = 0.015(3) fm; ΔRch(⁹¹Zr–⁹⁰Zr) = 0.015(2) fm; ΔRch(²⁰⁹Pb–²⁰⁸Pb) = 0.009(2) fm
- For the ⁴⁹Ca–⁴⁸Ca difference, spherical EDF calculations give: Sv-min = 0.008 fm; s8 = 0.009 fm; s17 = 0.010 fm; CODF = 0.011 fm; M3Y-P6a = 0.011 fm; Fy(Δr,BCS) = 0.004 fm; Fy(Δr,HFB) = 0.014 fm
- For the β₂² correction formula, the next most important term proportional to β₂³ gives about a 10% change to Eq. (1) for |β| = 0.6
- For ⁴²Ca, the ratio B(E2,0⁺₁→2⁺₂)/B(E2,0⁺₁→2⁺₁) ≈ 0.1
- Effective charges used for configuration-mixing calculations: eₚ = 1.22, eₙ = 0.78

## Context

This work extends the approach of using spherical EDF calculations (Brown 2014, Roca-Maza 2015) corrected for quadrupole deformation via the Bohr collective model (Bohr 1969). It addresses the well-known kinks in charge radii at magic numbers N=28, 50, 82 and Z=28, 50, and specifically examines the isotonic shift between cadmium and tin isotopes (experimental data from 2018 and 2019 compilations). The paper compares results with Fayans-type EDF functionals (Fayans 2000, Saperstein 2011) and the Fy(Δr) parameter set (Reinhard 2017), as well as the M3Y-P6a functional (Nakada 2015). The remaining discrepancy just after magic numbers is suggested to arise from octupole deformations (Nazarewicz 1984) or limitations in standard Skyrme-type EDF functionals, pointing toward the need for EDF models that include octupole degrees of freedom.
