# Scaling Factor Inconsistencies in Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> The modern theory of neutrinoless double beta decay includes a scaling factor that has often been treated inconsistently in the literature. The nuclear contribution to the decay half life can be suppressed by 15-20% when scaling factors are mismatched. Correspondingly, $<m_ν>$ is overestimated.

# Scaling Factor Inconsistencies in Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

## Key claim
The nuclear contribution to the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life can be suppressed by 15–20% when the scaling factor used in the nuclear matrix element (NME) is mismatched with the scaling factor used in the kinematic factor, leading to a corresponding overestimation of the extracted effective neutrino mass ⟨m_ν⟩. This inconsistency arises because some authors combine NME calculated with one value of the nuclear radius parameter r₀ with kinematic factors G⁵₀₁ calculated using a different r₀.

## Method
The author traces how a scaling factor R = r₀ A^{1/3} was introduced into the neutrino potential h₊ to make the NME dimensionless, with a compensating factor 1/R² inserted into the kinematic factor G⁵₀₁(R). By comparing published C₀₀ values (the product |M̃⁰ᵛ|² G⁵₀₁(R)) for ⁷⁶Ge against revised values computed using the correctly matched kinematic factor, the author quantifies the effect of using G⁵₀₁(R=1.2 A^{1/3}) with NME calculated using r₀=1.1 fm. The author also computes unscaled G₀₁ values for several nuclei using r₀ values fit to experimental root-mean-square charge radii.

## Key numbers
- The suppression factor from mismatched scaling is (1.1/1.2)² ≈ 20% for C₀₀ when NME use r₀=1.1 fm but G⁵₀₁ uses R=1.2 A^{1/3}.
- For ⁷⁶Ge, G⁵₀₁(R=1.2 A^{1/3}) = 6.46 × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹ and G⁵₀₁(R=1.1 A^{1/3}) = 7.78 × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹.
- Assuming a half-life of 4 × 10²⁷ yr for ⁷⁶Ge, the spread of predicted ⟨m_ν⟩ values across all methods is 0.022–0.068 eV, unchanged after correction, though individual values are reduced.
- Revised C₀₀ values for ⁷⁶Ge range from 0.34 × 10⁻¹⁴ yr⁻¹ (self-consistent QRPA) to 28.8 × 10⁻¹⁴ yr⁻¹ (VAMPIR).
- Unscaled G₀₁ for ¹⁵⁰Nd varies from 8.719 × 10⁻¹² fm² yr⁻¹ (r₀=1.23 fm) to 8.813 × 10⁻¹² fm² yr⁻¹ (r₀=1.20 fm), showing weak sensitivity to R.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of nuclear matrix element uncertainties in neutrinoless double beta decay, following the formalism of Doi et al. (1985) and the kinematic factor calculations of Pantis et al. (1996). It corrects a compilation originally published in a review by Elliott (2004) and adapted from Civitarese and Suhonen (2003), where several NME calculations had been combined with an inconsistent kinematic factor. The paper enables future comparisons of NME calculations to be performed with consistent scaling, and recommends that authors either omit the scaling factor entirely or clearly state their choice of r₀.
