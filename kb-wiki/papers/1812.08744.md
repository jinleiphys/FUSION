# Ab initio no-core shell model study of $^{18-23}$O and $^{18-24}$F isotopes

> In the present work, we have done a comprehensive study of low-lying energy spectrum for oxygen and fluorine chains using $ab~initio$ no core shell model. We have used inside nonlocal outside Yukawa (INOY) potential, which is a two body interaction but also has the effect of three body forces by short range and nonlocal character. Also, we have performed calculations with N3LO and N2LOopt interactions and compared corresponding results with the experimental data and phenomenological USDB interaction. We have reached up to $N_{max}$=6 for $^{18-21}$O and $^{18-19}$F, $N_{max}$=4 for other oxygen and fluorine isotopes, respectively. We have also discussed the binding energy of oxygen and fluorine chains. Over binding in the ground state (g.s.) energy in neutron rich oxygen isotopes is observed in our largest model space calculations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the INOY (inside nonlocal outside Yukawa) nucleon-nucleon potential, which incorporates three-body force effects through its short-range nonlocal character, can reproduce the neutron drip line at ²⁴O in oxygen isotopes without explicitly including three-nucleon forces, though the abstract's mention of "over binding" in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes qualifies that the largest model space calculations still show systematic overbinding of ground-state energies.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for oxygen (¹⁸⁻²³O) and fluorine (¹⁸⁻²⁴F) isotopes using the INOY potential, as well as the chiral N3LO and N2LOopt interactions for comparison. They used harmonic oscillator basis spaces up to Nmax=6 for ¹⁸⁻²¹O and ¹⁸⁻¹⁹F, and Nmax=4 for the remaining isotopes, with the pANTOINE shell model code adapted for NCSM. Effective interactions were generated via Okubo-Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation at the two-body cluster level, and results were compared with experimental data and the phenomenological USDB interaction.

## Key numbers
- For ²¹O at Nmax=6, the Hamiltonian matrix dimension is approximately 3.36 × 10⁹
- For ¹⁹F at Nmax=6, the Hamiltonian matrix dimension is approximately 1.35 × 10⁹
- The NCSM calculations for ¹⁸O with INOY interaction yield a point-proton radius of approximately 2.12 fm at ħΩ=18 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.68(10) fm
- The N3LO calculations were performed at ħΩ=14 MeV, while N2LOopt calculations used ħΩ=20 MeV
- For ¹⁸F, the N3LO interaction predicts the correct ground state as 1⁺, while INOY and N2LOopt fail to predict the correct ground state

## Context
This work extends previous NCSM calculations for oxygen and fluorine isotopes by reaching larger model spaces (Nmax=6 for several isotopes) than earlier studies, such as those by Barrett (2013) and Roth (2016) who reported NCSM with perturbation approach results for ¹⁸⁻²⁰O. The study builds on the finding by Otsuka (2010) that three-nucleon forces are essential to explain the oxygen neutron drip line at ²⁴O, and tests whether the INOY potential's implicit three-body effects can reproduce this drip line without explicit three-nucleon forces. The results enable future higher-Nmax calculations to improve agreement with experimental spectra, particularly for the N=14 shell gap in ²²O and for fluorine isotopes where INOY shows better performance for heavier neutron-rich systems.
