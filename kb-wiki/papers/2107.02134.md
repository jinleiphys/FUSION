# The anomalous $Zb\bar{b}$ couplings at the HERA and EIC

> To resolve the long-standing discrepancy between the precision measurement of bottom quark forward-backward asymmetry at LEP/SLC and the Standard Model prediction, we propose a novel method to probe the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling by measuring the single-spin asymmetry $A_e^b$ of the polarized lepton cross section in neutral current DIS processes with a $b$-tagged jet at HERA and EIC. Depending on the tagging efficiency of the final state $b$-jet, the measurement of $A_e^b$ at HERA can already partially break the degeneracy found in the anomalous $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling, as implied by the LEP and SLC precision electroweak data. In the first year run of the EIC, the measurement of $A_e^b$ can already break the degeneracy, due to its much larger luminosity and higher electron beam polarization. With enough integrated luminosity collected at the EIC, it is possible to either verify or exclude the LEP data and resolve the $A_{\rm FB}^b$ puzzle. We also discuss the complementary roles between the proposed $A_e^b$ measurement at EIC and the measurement of $gg \to Zh$ cross section at the HL-LHC in constraining the anomalous $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling.

## Key claim

The single-spin asymmetry $A_e^b$ in neutral-current deep-inelastic scattering with a $b$-tagged jet is exclusively sensitive to the vector component of the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling (parameterized by $\kappa_V$), not the axial-vector component ($\kappa_A$), because the dominant contribution arises from $\gamma$-$Z$ interference diagrams with the axial-vector electron coupling $g_A^e$ multiplied by $\kappa_V$. This measurement can break the four-fold degeneracy in the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling implied by LEP and SLC precision electroweak data, but the degree of degeneracy breaking depends on the $b$-jet tagging efficiency and the integrated luminosity available.

## Method

The authors calculate the single-spin asymmetry $A_e^b = (\sigma_{b,+}^{\rm tot} - \sigma_{b,-}^{\rm tot})/(\sigma_{b,+}^{\rm tot} + \sigma_{b,-}^{\rm tot})$ for polarized lepton-proton scattering, where $\sigma_{b,\pm}^{\rm tot}$ is the total inclusive $b$-tagged DIS cross section for right-handed or left-handed lepton beams. They compute the structure functions at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in QCD using the S-ACOT-$\chi$ heavy quark scheme with CT14NNLO parton distribution functions, including contributions from all quark flavors weighted by $b$-jet tagging efficiencies and misidentification probabilities. The analysis uses two benchmark tagging efficiency scenarios: a good scenario ($\epsilon_b=0.7$, $\epsilon_c^b=0.03$, $\epsilon_q^b=0.001$) and a worse scenario ($\epsilon_b=0.5$, $\epsilon_c^b=0.2$, $\epsilon_q^b=0.01$).

## Key numbers

- For HERA with center-of-mass energy $E_{\rm cm}=318$ GeV, the relative error $\delta A_e^b / A_e^b$ is approximately $60\%$ to $80\%$ for each experiment (H1 and ZEUS) with the tagging efficiencies considered.
- For EIC with $E_{\rm cm}=141$ GeV, $P_e=70\%$ polarization, and canonical integrated luminosity $\mathcal{L}=10$ fb$^{-1}$, the SM prediction yields $A_e^b = -0.023$ with $\delta A_e^b/A_e^b = 7.7\%$ for the good tagging scenario, and $A_e^b = -0.014$ with $\delta A_e^b/A_e^b = 6.5\%$ for the worse scenario.
- The minimal EIC luminosity needed to exclude the degeneracy with $\kappa_{V,A}<0$ is $\mathcal{L} > 27$ pb$^{-1}$ for the good tagging scenario and $\mathcal{L} > 214$ pb$^{-1}$ for the worse scenario.
- The minimal EIC luminosity needed to resolve the degeneracy with $\kappa_{V,A}>0$ (specifically the solution $(\kappa_V,\kappa_A)=(1.46,0.67)$) is $\mathcal{L} > 0.5$ fb$^{-1}$ for the good scenario and $\mathcal{L} > 4.0$ fb$^{-1}$ for the worse scenario.
- The minimal EIC luminosity needed to exclude the LEP $A_{\rm FB}^b$ measurement (the solution close to the SM) is $\mathcal{L} > 42.0$ fb$^{-1}$ for the good scenario and $\mathcal{L} > 332.6$ fb$^{-1}$ for the worse scenario.
- To break the remaining degeneracy at HERA with $\kappa_{V,A}>0$ would require much better tagging efficiency: $\epsilon_q^b=0.0005$, $\epsilon_c^b=0.01$, and $\epsilon_b=0.95$.

## Context

This work extends the program to resolve the long-standing $2.1\sigma$ discrepancy between the LEP measurement of $A_{\rm FB}^b$ at the $Z$-pole and the Standard Model prediction (Particle Data Group, 2020). Previous global analyses combining $R_b$, $A_{\rm FB}^b$, and $A_b$ measurements at LEP and SLC yielded four degenerate solutions for the $Zb\bar{b}$ coupling (Choudhury, 2001), and off-$Z$-pole LEP data reduced this to two solutions. The authors previously proposed measuring $gg\to Zh$ at the HL-LHC to probe the axial-vector component (Yan, 2021), which is complementary to the present proposal for probing the vector component via $A_e^b$ at lepton-hadron colliders. The EIC, with its high luminosity and polarization, can break the remaining degeneracy and either verify or exclude the LEP anomaly, while HERA data can partially break the degeneracy by excluding the $\kappa_{V,A}<0$ solutions.
