# Quarkonia in anisotropic hot QCD medium in a quasi-particle model

> The fate of heavy quarkonia states has been investigated in QCD at high temperature when the plasma has a small momentum space anisotropy within a quasi-particle model. A real time static potential has been obtained from a Hard thermal loop expression for the gluon self-energy, by employing the quasi-parton equilibrium distribution functions extracted from hot QCD equations of state of $O(g^5)$ and $O(g^6\ln(1/g))$. Employing the potential, quarkonia melting has been studied. It is found that the equations of state and the momentum anisotropy cause significant modifications in the screening properties of the plasma.

## Key claim

The dissociation temperatures of heavy quarkonia in a small-anisotropy QGP are higher when using realistic hot QCD equations of state (EOS1 at O(g⁵) and EOS2 at O(g⁶ ln(1/g))) than when using an ideal-gas EOS, because the interactions effectively renormalize the partonic charges and reduce the Debye screening. The anisotropy parameter ξ modifies the dissociation temperature by a factor (1 + ξ/9), the same dependence as in the ideal case.

## Method

The authors start from the hard-thermal-loop gluon self-energy for a general momentum distribution, then introduce a small momentum-space anisotropy via the parameter ξ (with ξ = 0 isotropic). They replace the usual ideal Bose/Fermi equilibrium distribution functions with quasi-parton distribution functions extracted from two hot QCD equations of state: EOS1 (O(g⁵)) and EOS2 (O(g⁶ ln(1/g))). Using these, they compute the Debye mass m_D and the real-time static heavy-quark potential in the leading-logarithmic approximation, and then determine the dissociation temperature T_D by equating the binding energy (from the real part of the potential) to the thermal width (from the imaginary part).

## Key numbers

- For pure gauge theory near T ~ 1.5 T_c, the dissociation temperature T_D is ~20%–6% higher than the ideal value for EOS1, and ~12%–5% higher for EOS2.
- In the anisotropic case with non-ideal EOS, the dissociation temperature increases by a factor (1 + ξ/9) relative to the isotropic case; for ξ ~ 1 this is an increase of ~10%.
- The ratio T_D / T_D^I equals μ_d^{-1/3}, where μ_d ≡ m_D / m_D^I; μ_d ≤ 1 and reaches unity only asymptotically.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quarkonia melting in an anisotropic QGP by Laine (2007) from an ideal-gas EOS to realistic hot QCD equations of state (EOS1 from Zhai and Arnold (1994) and EOS2 from Kajantie (2003)), using a quasi-particle model previously developed by Chandra and Ravishankar (2009, 2010). The results show that interactions reduce screening and raise dissociation temperatures, enabling more realistic predictions for quarkonia suppression at RHIC and LHC. The authors note that for larger anisotropies numerical simulations would be required, and suggest future extensions to non-zero baryon density and lattice equations of state with physical quark masses.
