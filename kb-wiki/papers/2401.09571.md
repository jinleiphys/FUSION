# Hadronisation in event generators from small to large systems

> The results of the dynamical core-corona initialisation framework in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energies are presented. We extract the fractions of final hadron yields originating from equilibrated and non-equilibrated matter as functions of multiplicity. We show that the contribution from non-equilibrated matter is non-negligible even in intermediate and central Pb+Pb collisions. The particle production from non-equilibrated matter behaves as a correction on $c_2\{4\}$ that is purely obtained from the equilibrated matter. The result poses a warning on Bayesian parameter estimation with conventional hydrodynamic models. The observed flow coefficients might need a reinterpretation with new dynamical models which incorporate the particle production from non-equilibrated matter.

# Hadronisation in event generators from small to large systems

## Key claim
The dynamical core-corona initialisation framework DCCI2 shows that non-equilibrated matter (corona) contributes non-negligibly to final hadron yields even in intermediate and central Pb+Pb collisions, not only in small systems. This implies that Bayesian parameter estimation using conventional hydrodynamic models, which assume full equilibration, may need reinterpretation because the observed flow coefficient c2{4} is diluted by corona contributions.

## Method
The authors used the DCCI2 framework, which couples Pythia8 (for p+p) or Pythia8 Angantyr (for Pb+Pb) initial parton production with (3+1)-D ideal hydrodynamics for the equilibrated core component. The dynamical core-corona initialisation separates the system based on a phenomenologically-defined mean-free path: partons with low transverse momentum and/or in high-density regions generate QGP fluids, while high-pT partons in low-density regions remain as non-equilibrated corona. Hadronisation proceeds via iS3D sampling from hydrodynamic fields for the core and Lund string fragmentation via Pythia8 for the corona, followed by hadronic rescatterings and resonance decays using JAM.

## Key numbers
- The onset of core dominance occurs at average charged-particle pseudorapidity density ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ~ 20.
- At the highest multiplicity in p+p collisions, the core fraction is only around 50%.
- Simulations were performed for p+p at sqrt(s) = 7 and 13 TeV, and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- The corona contribution to c2{4} stays zero-consistent across the entire Nch range in Pb+Pb collisions, while the core contribution shows negative values.

## Context
This work extends the core-corona picture originally proposed by Werner (2007) into a fully dynamical framework (DCCI2) that can describe both small and large colliding systems. It builds on the authors' prior development of DCCI (Kanakubo 2021, 2022) and directly challenges the assumption underlying Bayesian parameter estimation studies by Pratt (2015) and Bernhard (2016), which used conventional multi-stage hydrodynamic models that neglect non-equilibrated contributions. The results enable future model-to-data comparisons that incorporate non-equilibrium effects for quantitative extraction of QGP properties, and they contribute to the ongoing discussion about QGP signals in small systems raised by Dusling (2015), Nagle (2018), and Schenke (2021).
