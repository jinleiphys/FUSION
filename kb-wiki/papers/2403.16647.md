# Effect of Coriolis Force on Electrical Conductivity Tensor for Rotating Hadron Resonance Gas

> We have investigated the influence of the Coriolis force on the electrical conductivity of hadronic matter formed in relativistic nuclear collisions, employing the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model. A rotating matter in the peripheral heavy ion collisions can be expected from the initial stage of quark matter to late-stage hadronic matter. Present work is focused on rotating hadronic matter, whose medium constituents - hadron resonances can face a non-zero Coriolis force, which can influence the hadronic flow or conductivity. We estimate this conductivity tensor by using the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation. In the absence of Coriolis force, an isotropic conductivity tensor for hadronic matter is expected. However, our study finds that the presence of Coriolis force can generate an anisotropic conductivity tensor with three main conductivity components - parallel, perpendicular, and Hall, similar to the effect of Lorentz force at a finite magnetic field. Our study has indicated that a noticeable anisotropy of conductivity tensor can be found within the phenomenological range of angular velocity $Ω= 0.001-0.02$ GeV and hadronic scattering radius $a=0.2-2$ fm.

## Key claim

The Coriolis force, arising from the rotation of hadronic matter formed in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, generates an anisotropic electrical conductivity tensor with parallel, perpendicular, and Hall components, analogous to the effect of a magnetic field via the Lorentz force, within the phenomenological range of angular velocity \(\Omega = 0.001-0.02\) GeV and hadronic scattering radius \(a = 0.2-2\) fm, when quadratic and higher powers of \(\Omega\) are neglected.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in a rotating frame, using the covariant form with connection coefficients derived from the rotating frame metric. The collision term was treated in the relaxation time approximation, and the deviation from equilibrium was expanded to first order in the electric field. The hadronic medium was modeled using the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, treating all known baryons and mesons as non-interacting particles with hard-sphere cross sections, and the conductivity tensor components were computed by summing over all charged hadron species.

## Key numbers

* Parallel conductivity \(\sigma^{||}\) (equal to the isotropic conductivity in the absence of rotation) for the HRG at \(\mu=0\) is given by \(\sigma^{||}_{\rm HRG} = \sum_{B} \frac{g_B q_B^2}{3T}\int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3}\tau_c \frac{p^2}{E^2}f^0(1-f^0) + \sum_{M} \frac{g_M q_M^2}{3T}\int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3}\tau_c \frac{p^2}{E^2}f^0(1+f^0)\).
* Perpendicular conductivity \(\sigma^{\perp}_{HRG}\) includes the factor \(\frac{\tau_c}{1+(\tau_c/\tau_\Omega)^2}\) in the integrand, where \(\tau_\Omega\) is the characteristic time associated with the angular velocity \(\Omega\).
* Hall conductivity \(\sigma^{\times}_{HRG}\) includes the factor \(\frac{\tau_c(\tau_c/\tau_\Omega)}{1+(\tau_c/\tau_\Omega)^2}\) in the integrand.
* For massless QGP, the parallel conductivity is \(\sigma_{\rm QGP} = \frac{8e^2}{3\pi^2}\tau_c T^2 L_2\), where \(e^2 = 4\pi/137\) in natural units.
* The phenomenological range of angular velocity considered is \(\Omega = 0.001-0.02\) GeV, and the hadronic scattering radius range is \(a = 0.2-2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Dwibedi (2023) from non-relativistic to relativistic kinetic theory for calculating transport coefficients in rotating nuclear matter, and is the first study of Coriolis-induced anisotropy in the electrical conductivity of the HRG system. It parallels previous work by Dash (2020), Dey (2020), and Das (2019) on the role of the Lorentz force in creating anisotropic transport in the HRG under a magnetic field, and enables future comparisons between rotational and magnetic effects on the electromagnetic response of the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter formed in heavy-ion collisions.
