# Neutron Stars and Nuclei in the Modified Relativistic Hartree Approximation

> We have examined the properties of neutron-rich matter and finite nuclei in the modified relativistic Hartree approximation for several values of the renormalization scale, $μ$, around the standard choice of $μ$ equal to the nucleon mass $M$. Observed neutron star masses do not effectively constrain the value of $μ$. However for finite nuclei the value $μ/M=0.79$, suggested by nuclear matter data, provides a good account of the bulk properties with a sigma mass of about 600 MeV. This value of $μ/M$ renders the effective three and four body scalar self-couplings to be zero at 60\% of equilibrium nuclear matter density, rather than in the vacuum. We have also found that the matter part of the exchange diagram has little impact on the bulk properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The modified relativistic Hartree approximation (MRHA) with a renormalization scale of μ/M = 0.79, suggested by nuclear matter data, provides a good account of the bulk properties of finite nuclei with a sigma mass of about 600 MeV, while observed neutron star masses do not effectively constrain the value of μ.

## Method

The authors examined neutron-rich matter and finite nuclei using the modified relativistic Hartree approximation (MRHA), which includes one-loop vacuum corrections with a renormalization scale μ varied around the standard choice μ = M (the nucleon mass). For finite nuclei, they applied the local density approximation with leading derivative expansion corrections to the spherically-symmetric doubly-closed-shell nuclei 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb. For neutron stars, they solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff hydrostatic equilibrium equations using the MRHA equation of state at high densities, matched to the Negele-Vautherin and Baym-Pethick-Sutherland equations of state at lower densities, for charge-neutral matter in beta equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per particle of nuclear matter at saturation: 16 MeV
- Equilibrium nuclear matter density: n0 = 0.16 fm−3
- Symmetry energy coefficient: asymm = 30 MeV
- For μ/M = 0.79, the sigma mass required to fit finite nuclei: approximately 600 MeV
- The effective three and four body scalar self-couplings become zero at a nuclear density of n = 0.092 fm−3 (60% of equilibrium nuclear matter density) for μ/M = 0.79
- Maximum neutron star mass for μ/M = 0.73: consistent with observed limit of (1.85 ± 0.3) M⊙ for 4U0900-40 and (1.44 ± 0.01) M⊙ for PSR 1913+16
- For μ/M = 0.79, spin-orbit splittings in 208Pb are on average 80% of experimental values
- The compression modulus K varies with renormalization scale; values are listed in Table 1 for μ/M = 0.73, 0.79, 1.00, 1.25, and 1.50
- The adiabaticity index Γ for densities n > 2.5n0 lies in the range 2–3 for most renormalization scales, except μ/M = 0.73 where it is significantly larger

## Context

This work extends the modified relativistic Hartree approximation previously applied to nuclear matter by Heide, Rudaz, and Ellis (1993) and by Heide, Rudaz, Prakash, and Ellis (1993) to finite nuclei and neutron stars. The standard Walecka model (Serot and Walecka, 1986) with μ = M (the Chin-Walecka result) gives poorer agreement with finite nuclei data, particularly for charge radii and binding energies. The value μ/M = 0.79 renders the effective three and four body scalar self-couplings zero at finite density rather than in vacuum, which has long been known to be necessary in mean field treatments that fit σ3 and σ4 terms to nuclei (Bouyssy, 1987; Sharma, 1993). The results enable further studies of neutron star cooling via the direct Urca process, which becomes possible when proton fractions exceed 0.11–0.15, and motivate inclusion of exchange contributions and hyperon effects in future work.
