# The islands of shape coexistence within the Elliott and the proxy-SU(3) Models

> A novel dual-shell mechanism for the phenomenon of shape coexistence in nuclei within the Elliott SU(3) and the proxy-SU(3) symmetry is proposed for all mass regions. It is supposed, that shape coexistence is activated by large quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and involves the interchange among the spin-orbit (SO) like shells within nucleon numbers 6-14, 14-28, 28-50, 50-82, 82-126, 126-184, which are being described by the proxy-SU(3) symmetry, and the harmonic oscillator (HO) shells within nucleon numbers 2-8, 8-20, 20-40, 40-70, 70-112, 112-168 of the Elliott SU(3) symmetry. The outcome is, that shape coexistence may occur in certain islands on the nuclear map. The dual-shell mechanism predicts without any free parameters, that nuclei with proton number (Z) or neutron number (N) between 7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112, 146-168 are possible candidates for shape coexistence. In the light nuclei the nucleons flip from the HO shell to the neighboring SO-like shell, which means, that particle excitations occur. For this mass region, the predicted islands of shape coexistence, coincide with the islands of inversion. But in medium mass and heavy nuclei, in which the nucleons inhabit the SO-like shells, shape coexistence is accompanied by a merging of the SO-like shell with the open HO shell. The shell merging can be accomplished by the outer product of the SU(3) irreps of the two shells and represents the unification of the HO shell with the SO-like shell.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel dual-shell mechanism for shape coexistence in nuclei, claiming that shape coexistence is not a universal phenomenon but can only occur within specific, well-defined islands on the nuclear chart, determined by the interplay between harmonic oscillator (HO) shells and spin-orbit (SO) like shells.

## Method

The authors employ the Elliott SU(3) symmetry for HO shells (magic numbers 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112, 168) and the proxy-SU(3) symmetry for SO-like shells (magic numbers 6, 14, 28, 50, 82, 126, 184) to calculate the highest weight SU(3) irreducible representations for each shell type. They then compare the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength, measured by the second-order Casimir operator C2, between the HO and SO-like shells at each nucleon number to identify regions where the SO-like shell interaction dominates, enabling shape coexistence through either particle excitations (light nuclei) or shell merging (medium-mass and heavy nuclei).

## Key numbers

* The HO magic numbers are 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112, 168; the SO-like magic numbers are 6, 14, 28, 50, 82, 126, 184.
* The dual-shell mechanism predicts shape coexistence candidates for proton or neutron numbers in the ranges: 7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112, 146-168.
* The proxy-SU(3) single-particle energy shift is given by epsilon_proxy = (1 - 2*epsilon/3)*hbar*omega, where epsilon is the Nilsson deformation parameter.
* The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in the Elliott model is given by QQ = 4*C2 - 3*L(L+1), with C2 = lambda^2 + mu^2 + lambda*mu + 3*(lambda + mu).
* The deformation parameter beta is related to C2 by beta^2 = (4*pi/(5*A*bar_r^2)^2)*(C2 + 3), with bar_r^2 = 0.87^2*A^(1/3).

## Context

This work extends the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) and the proxy-SU(3) symmetry (Bonatsos et al., 2017) to provide a unified mechanism for shape coexistence across all mass regions. It contradicts the common belief stated in Heyde (2011) that shape coexistence can appear anywhere on the nuclear chart, instead predicting specific islands. The mechanism is compatible with the particle-hole mechanism previously applied in regions like the island of inversion around neutron-rich Mg isotopes, and enables parameter-free predictions for shape coexistence candidates in regions such as the rare earths and lead region, where experimental data from authors like Olaizola (2019) and Siciliano (2020) provide evidence.
