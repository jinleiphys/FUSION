# How do chiral condensates affect color superconducting quark matter under charge neutrality constraints?

> We investigate the effects of the dynamical formation of the chiral condensates on color superconducting phases under the electric and color neutrality constraints at vanishing temperature. We shall show that the phase appearing next to the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase down in density depends on the strength of the diquark coupling. In particular, the gapless CFL (gCFL) phase is realized only in a weak coupling regime. We give a qualitative argument on why the gCFL phase in the weak coupling region is replaced by some other phases in the strong coupling, once the competition between dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the Cooper pair formation is taken into account.

## Key claim

The gapless CFL (gCFL) phase appears as the next phase after the CFL phase only in the weak diquark coupling regime; in stronger coupling, the gCFL phase is replaced by other phases (UQM, g2SC, or 2SC) once the competition between dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and Cooper pair formation is taken into account.

## Method

The authors used a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar coupling \(G_s\) and diquark coupling \(G_d\), imposing electric and color neutrality constraints at zero temperature. They solved the mean-field gap equations for the diquark gaps \(\Delta_\eta\), the constituent quark masses \(M\) and \(M_s\), and the chemical potentials \(\mu_e, \mu_3, \mu_8\) by minimizing the thermodynamic potential, comparing phases with and without dynamical chiral condensates.

## Key numbers

* The CFL-to-gCFL transition occurs at a critical chemical potential \(\mu_* \cong 581.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(\Delta_0 = 25\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (weak coupling).
* The gCFL phase disappears for diquark coupling strengths \(\Delta_0 \gtrsim 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The UQM phase takes over the gCFL phase at \(\mu \cong 558\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(\Delta_0 = 25\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The transition from gCFL to UQM occurs when \(M_s^2/\mu \sim 4\Delta_1(\mu_*)\), compared to \(M_s^2/\mu \sim 5\Delta_1(\mu_*)\) in earlier work where the strange quark mass was treated as a fixed parameter.
* The cutoff used is \(\Lambda = 800\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with the scalar coupling chosen to give a vacuum constituent quark mass of \(400\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in the chiral limit.
* The current strange quark mass is set to \(m_s = 80\ \mathrm{MeV}\); u and d current masses are taken as zero.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gapless color-superconducting phases (gCFL and g2SC) previously studied by Alford, Kouvaris, and Rajagopal (2004) and by Fukushima, Kouvaris, and Rajagopal (2004), which treated the strange quark mass as a fixed parameter. By incorporating dynamical chiral condensation, the authors show that the gCFL phase is fragile and disappears at moderate diquark coupling, in contrast to the earlier fixed-mass results. The findings enable more realistic modeling of quark matter in compact stars and motivate further studies of the competition between chiral and diquark condensates at finite temperature and with possible inhomogeneous phases.
