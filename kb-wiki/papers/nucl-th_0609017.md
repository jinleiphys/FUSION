# High p_T Hadron Correlation and No Correlation

> An overview of the correlation among high-p_T hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions is presented. Emphasis is placed on the physical processes that can quantitatively account for the data on correlations in p_T, ηand ϕon the near and far sides. Predictions are made on processes that have no observable correlations for hadrons produced at intermediate and higher p_T at RHIC and LHC.

# High p_T Hadron Correlation and No Correlation

## Key claim
Whether high-p_T hadrons exhibit correlation or not depends on the hadron species, the medium from which they are produced, and the kinematical region; the conventional assumption that every high-p_T particle belongs to a jet may be invalid for certain cases, including phi and Omega production, forward production, and high-p_T production at LHC.

## Method
The paper reviews calculations based on the recombination model (RM), where hadronization proceeds via thermal-shower parton recombination for intermediate p_T and thermal-thermal recombination for lower p_T, and compares these to data from STAR and BRAHMS on near-side and away-side correlations in p_T, eta, and phi. For the no-correlation cases, the author applies the RM to show that when hard scattering is suppressed (phi/Omega, forward production) or when jet multiplicity is so high that jet cones overlap (LHC), the resulting hadrons are of thermal origin and should exhibit no observable correlation above background.

## Key numbers
- For away-side correlation in Au+Au at 200 GeV, suppression is seen at p_T^assoc > 2 GeV/c and enhancement at p_T^assoc < 2 GeV/c, for 4 < p_T^trig < 6 GeV/c.
- The trigger-normalized fragmentation function D(z_T) on the away side is studied for z_T > 0.35, with p_T^trig in the 8-15 GeV/c range.
- The near-side pedestal in Delta-eta extends over |Delta-eta| less than about 1.2, with Delta-T approximately 15 MeV from the RM calculation.
- For phi and Omega production, the Omega spectrum remains exponential out to p_T approximately 5.5 GeV/c, with thermal s-quarks thermal out to 2 GeV/c at T approximately 0.32 GeV.
- For forward production at eta = 3.2 and sqrt(s) = 62.4 GeV, the p/pi ratio reaches as high as 2 at p_T approximately 2 GeV/c.
- At LHC, for 10 < p_T < 20 GeV/c, the p/pi ratio is predicted to decrease from 20 to 5 for an overlap probability Gamma ~ p_T^{-7}, significantly higher than the one-jet case.

## Context
This review extends the recombination model approach previously applied to single-particle distributions by Hwa and collaborators (2003-2006) and addresses the STAR data on jet correlations from 2005-2006. It contrasts with the conventional fragmentation-based jet tomography approach of Wang (2004) and others, and suggests that the medium-modified fragmentation function may not apply at intermediate p_T where recombination dominates. The predictions for no correlation in phi/Omega production, forward production, and at LHC provide testable alternatives to the standard jet-quenching picture and could guide experimental searches at RHIC and LHC.
