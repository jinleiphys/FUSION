# Charmonium Suppression - Interplay of Hadronic and Partonic Degrees of Freedom

> Last year the E866-group of the Fermilab measured the $x_F$ dependence of $J/Ψ$ and $Ψ'$ suppression in $pA$ collisions. We discuss two of the effects found in that experiment with regard to color coherence effects: the different suppression of the $J/Ψ$ and the $Ψ'$ at $x_F<0$ and the significant suppression of both at large $x_F$. The small $x_F$ regions is dominated by fully formed charmonium states and thus enables us to discuss the formation time and the cross section of the different charmonium states. In the large $x_F$ region the interaction of the charmonium states with nuclear matter has to be described by partonic degrees of freedom, because in that kinematic domain the formation time is much larger than the nuclear radii. The understanding of this region will be crucial for the interpretation of the data of the future heavy ion colliders RHIC and LHC.

# Charmonium Suppression - Interplay of Hadronic and Partonic Degrees of Freedom

## Key claim
The different suppression of J/Ψ and Ψ' at xF < 0 and the significant suppression of both at large xF observed by the E866 experiment can be explained by color coherence effects, but only if one treats the interaction with nuclear matter using hadronic degrees of freedom at small xF and partonic degrees of freedom at large xF, where the formation time exceeds nuclear radii.

## Method
The authors used a semiclassical Gribov-Glauber model to calculate survival probabilities of charmonium states in pA collisions at Elab = 800 GeV. They employed a non-perturbative parametrization σ(b) = C·b² for charmonium-nucleon cross sections, with the constant C calibrated to the pion-nucleon cross section and pion size, and calculated transverse sizes of charmonium states using non-relativistic wave functions from two different sources (Eich and Buch). For the mixed kinematic region where formation time is comparable to nuclear size, they modeled the expanding c-cbar pair using a quantum diffusion prescription where the cross section grows linearly with time until the formation time is reached.

## Key numbers
- Formation time of J/Ψ: t_f(J/Ψ) = 0.3 fm/c · γ (from the energy difference between Ψ' and J/Ψ)
- Formation time of J/Ψ from alternative extraction: t_f(J/Ψ) = 0.44 fm/c · γ, t_f(Ψ') = 0.91 fm/c · γ (from Kharzeev analysis of e⁺e⁻ → Q-Qbar data)
- Hadronic region boundary: t_f < 1.8 fm/c corresponds to xF < -0.3 at Elab = 800 GeV
- Mixed region boundary: 1.8 fm/c < t_f < 2·R_A corresponds to -0.3 < xF < 0 for Be at Elab = 800 GeV
- Non-perturbative J/Ψ-nucleon cross section: 2.5 mb (Eich wave function) or 3.1 mb (Buch wave function)
- Non-perturbative Ψ'-nucleon cross section: 10 mb (Eich) or 12.6 mb (Buch)
- Non-perturbative χ₁₀-nucleon cross section: 3.8 mb (Eich) or 4.6 mb (Buch)
- Non-perturbative χ₁₁-nucleon cross section: 7.6 mb (Eich) or 9.3 mb (Buch)
- Cross section ratio: σ(χ₁₁)/σ(χ₁₀) = 2
- Cross section at production point: σ(z) = 1 mb
- Pion cross section used for calibration: σ(π) = 25 mb with b²(π) = 0.96 fm²
- At xF = 0: formation length l_c = 20.6 · 0.3 fm > 2·R_Be

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the E866 Fermilab experiment (Leitch, 1999) on xF-dependent J/Ψ and Ψ' suppression in pA collisions. The authors build on their earlier work (Gerland, 1998) showing that the pQCD-predicted J/Ψ-nucleon cross section (Radyushkin, 1993) is too small to explain photoproduction data at Elab = 20 GeV (SLAC), and that a non-perturbative contribution dominates at SPS energies. The results are relevant for interpreting future heavy ion data from RHIC and LHC, where the pQCD contribution to charmonium-nucleon cross sections may become significant or dominant. The authors note that their assumption of hard c-cbar production (A-scaling) may be inappropriate at large xF (Duffy, 1998) and that nuclear modifications of parton distribution functions are neglected.
