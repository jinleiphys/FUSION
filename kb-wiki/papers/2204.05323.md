# Asymmetric transverse momentum broadening in an inhomogeneous medium

> Gradient jet tomography in high-energy heavy-ion collisions utilizes the asymmetric transverse momentum broadening of a propagating parton in an inhomogeneous medium. Such broadening is studied within a path integral description of the evolution of the Wigner distribution for a propagating parton in medium. Going beyond the eikonal approximation of multiple scattering, the evolution operator in the transverse direction can be expressed as the functional integration over all classical trajectories of a massive particle with the light-cone momentum $ω$ as its mass. With a dipole approximation of the Wilson line correlation function, evolution with the light-cone time $t$ is determined by the jet transport coefficient $\hat q$ that can vary with space and time. In a uniform medium with a constant $\hat q_0$, the analytical solution to the Wigner distribution becomes a typical drifted Gaussian in both transverse momentum and coordinate with the diffusion width $\sqrt{\hat q_0t}$ and $\sqrt{\hat q_0t^3/3ω^2}$, respectively. In the case of a simple Gaussian-like transverse inhomogeneity with a spatial width $σ$ on top of a uniform medium, the final asymmetrical momentum distribution can be calculated semi-analytically. The transverse asymmetry defined for jet gradient tomography that characterizes the asymmetrical distribution is found to linearly correlate with the initial transverse position of the propagating parton within the domain of the inhomogeneity. It decreases with the parton energy $ω$, increases with the propagation time initially and saturates when the diffusion distance is much larger than the size of the inhomogeneity or $t^3\gg 3ω^2σ^2/\hat q_0$. The transverse momentum broadening due to the inhomogeneity also saturates at late time in contrast to the continued increase with time if the drifted diffusion in space is ignored.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the asymmetric transverse momentum broadening of a parton propagating through an inhomogeneous medium can be described within a path integral formalism beyond the eikonal approximation, and that the resulting transverse asymmetry linearly correlates with the initial transverse position of the parton within the inhomogeneity domain, but only when the diffusion distance is much larger than the size of the inhomogeneity or when \(t^3\gg 3\omega^2\sigma^2/\hat q_0\).

## Method

The authors formulate the transverse diffusion of a propagating parton using a path integral approach for the evolution of the Wigner function, going beyond the eikonal approximation by retaining the \(p_{\perp}^2\) term in the propagator. They employ the dipole approximation to relate the Wilson line correlation function to the jet transport coefficient \(\hat q\), which can vary with space and time. For a uniform medium with constant \(\hat q_0\), the analytical solution is obtained as a drifted Gaussian; for a Gaussian-like transverse inhomogeneity with spatial width \(\sigma\) on top of a uniform medium, the final asymmetrical momentum distribution is calculated semi-analytically by expanding the functional integral to leading order in the inhomogeneity.

## Key numbers

- In a uniform medium with constant \(\hat q_0\), the diffusion width in transverse momentum is \(\sqrt{\hat q_0 t}\) and the diffusion width in transverse coordinate is \(\sqrt{\hat q_0 t^3 / 3\omega^2}\).
- The transverse asymmetry saturates when the condition \(t^3 \gg 3\omega^2\sigma^2/\hat q_0\) is satisfied, where \(\sigma\) is the spatial width of the Gaussian-like transverse inhomogeneity.
- The transverse momentum broadening due to the inhomogeneity saturates at late time, in contrast to the continued increase with time if the drifted diffusion in space is ignored.

## Context

This work extends the gradient jet tomography approach introduced by He (2020) for localizing initial jet production positions in heavy-ion collisions, which was previously studied using a drift-diffusion Boltzmann equation and verified by event-by-event simulations within the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model. The path integral formulation provides a more rigorous QCD-based derivation of the same physics, complementing the earlier Boltzmann approach and enabling future studies of asymmetrical broadening due to a few hard scatterings as investigated by Sadofyev (2021) and Barata (2022).
