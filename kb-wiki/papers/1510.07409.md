# Interaction of antiprotons with nuclei

> We performed fully self-consistent calculations of $\bar{p}$-nuclear bound states using a complex ${\bar p}$-nucleus potential accounting for $\bar{p}$-atom data. While the real part of the potential is constructed within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, the $\bar{p}$ annihilation in the nuclear medium is described by a phenomenological optical potential. We confirm large polarization effects of the nuclear core caused by the presence of the antiproton. The ${\bar p}$ annihilation is treated dynamically, taking into account explicitly the reduced phase space for annihilation from deeply bound states as well as the compressed nuclear density due to the antiproton. The energy available for the products of ${\bar p}$ annihilation in the nuclear medium is evaluated self-consistently, considering the additional energy shift due to transformation from the ${\bar p}N$ system to ${\bar p}$-nucleus system. Corresponding $\bar{p}$ widths in the medium are significantly suppressed, however, they still remain considerable for the $\bar{p}$ potential consistent with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that deeply bound antiprotons cause large polarization and compression of the nuclear core, but the resulting antiproton widths in the medium are significantly suppressed compared to vacuum estimates, yet remain considerable for a potential consistent with antiproton-atom data.

## Method

Fully self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations of antiproton-nuclear bound states were performed using a complex antiproton-nucleus potential. The real part was constructed via G-parity transformation of nucleon-meson couplings scaled by a factor xi, while the imaginary part describing antiproton annihilation was taken from a phenomenological optical potential fitted to antiproton-atom data. The energy available for annihilation products was evaluated self-consistently, accounting for the reduced phase space from deeply bound states, the compressed nuclear density, and the additional energy shift from transforming the antiproton-nucleon system to the antiproton-nucleus system.

## Key numbers

* Antiproton binding energy in the 1s state: 1212.4 MeV in O-16 (TM2 model) and 1107.5 MeV in Pb-208 (TM1 model), with total binding energies of 1259.9 MeV and 2651.2 MeV respectively.
* Nuclear core density reaches approximately 3-4 times normal nuclear density in the central region where the antiproton is localized.
* The scaling factor xi = 0.2 provides an antiproton potential consistent with antiproton-atom data, corresponding to a real potential depth of about 100-300 MeV at normal nuclear density.
* The imaginary part parameter Im b0 = 1.9 fm was adopted from fits to antiproton-atom data.
* Nuclear matter compressibilities: K = 240 MeV in the TW99 model, K = 344 MeV in the TM2 model, and K = 280 MeV in the TM1 model.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Mishustin et al. (2005) and Larionov et al. (2010-2014) on antiproton-nuclear bound states within the RMF approach, which predicted strong binding and large nuclear core compression. The authors improve upon these earlier calculations by performing fully self-consistent dynamical calculations with a complex potential that accounts for antiproton-atom data from Batty et al. (1997) and Mares et al. (2006), and by treating the annihilation energy shift self-consistently following methods recently applied to kaonic atoms and eta-nuclear states. The results enable more reliable predictions for future experiments with antiproton beams at the FAIR facility at GSI.
