# Intrinsic operators for the electromagnetic nuclear current

> The intrinsic electromagnetic nuclear meson exchange charge and current operators arising from a separation of the center-of-mass motion are derived for a one-boson-exchange model for the nuclear interaction with scalar, pseudoscalar and vector meson exchange including leading order relativistic terms. Explicit expressions for the meson exchange operators corresponding to the different meson types are given in detail for a two-nucleon system. These intrinsic operators are to be evaluated between intrinsic wave functions in their center-of-mass frame.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit intrinsic electromagnetic meson-exchange charge and current operators for a two-nucleon system within a one-boson-exchange model, including leading-order relativistic corrections, but these operators are only valid when evaluated between intrinsic wave functions in their center-of-mass frame and rely on a 1/m-expansion that introduces approximations.

## Method

The authors start from Foldy-Wouthuysen (FW) electromagnetic currents obtained via the extended S-matrix method, then separate center-of-mass motion from intrinsic motion using a unitary transformation that introduces boost contributions (kinetic and interaction-dependent). The intrinsic operators are constructed by evaluating FW currents in the Breit frame, adding boost commutators and separation terms, and reorganizing the relativistic parts using an effective momentum transfer to absorb gradient terms. Explicit expressions are given for one-nucleon currents and two-nucleon meson-exchange currents corresponding to scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector meson exchange, with the continuity equation used to verify consistency.

## Key numbers

- The total mass of the two-nucleon system is taken as M = 2m, where m is the nucleon mass.
- The nonrelativistic recoil energy is given by ω_R^(1) = (k·K)/(2M), with M the total mass.
- The effective momentum transfer squared is k_F^2 = k^2 - (k_0^(1))^2 + (ω_fi^(1))^2 - k^4/(4M^2).
- The kinetic boost operator χ_0 for two equal-mass nucleons contains terms of order 1/(16m^2) and 1/(8m^2).
- The relativistic one-nucleon charge density ρ_F^(2) contains terms proportional to 1/(16m^2) and 1/(4m^2).

## Context

This work extends the formal separation of center-of-mass motion from intrinsic motion for relativistic transition operators developed by Adam and Arenhoevel (1995, referred to as "I"), applying it to a specific one-boson-exchange model for the nuclear interaction. The paper builds on the Foldy-Wouthuysen approach and the extended S-matrix method of Adam, Truhlik, and Adamova (1984), and incorporates boost current techniques from Friar (1975) and Friar, Gibson, and Payne (1984). The derived intrinsic operators enable calculations of electromagnetic observables in the center-of-mass frame without frame-dependent complications, and provide a foundation for testing continuity equation satisfaction in relativistic nuclear models.
