# Correlations and effective interactions in nuclear matter

> We performed Self-Consistent Greens Function (SCGF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and effective low-momentum interactions ($V_{low-k}$), which are derived from such realistic NN interactions. We compare the spectral distributions resulting from such calculations. We also introduce a density-dependent effective low-momentum interaction which accounts for the dispersive effects in the single-particle propagator in the medium.

## Key claim

Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) calculations using the effective low-momentum interaction \(V_{low-k}\) reproduce the spectral distribution of single-particle strength from the bare CD-Bonn interaction within an energy window of \(\pm 50\) MeV around the Fermi energy at saturation density, but fail to reproduce high-momentum correlations and overestimate binding energy at high densities unless a density-dependent version \(V_{low-k}(\rho)\) is introduced to account for dispersive quenching of the single-particle propagator.

## Method

The authors performed Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using the bare CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in the complete Hilbert space, and separately using the effective low-momentum interaction \(V_{low-k}\) (cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\)) within a model space defined by that cutoff. The \(V_{low-k}\) interaction was derived via a unitary model operator transformation of the CD-Bonn interaction. A density-dependent effective interaction \(V_{low-k}(\rho)\) was constructed by iteratively incorporating a self-consistent BHF single-particle spectrum into the unitary transformation procedure. The in-medium \(T\)-matrix was solved in partial waves with total angular momentum \(J < 6\), and the spectral function, self-energy, momentum distribution, and energy per particle were computed self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon from Hartree-Fock using \(V_{low-k}\) at \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) ranges from approximately 2 MeV (attractive) to 15 MeV (attractive) over the densities considered (Fermi momenta from about 0.8 to 1.8 fm\(^{-1}\)).
- Self-consistent \(T\)-matrix approach using \(V_{low-k}\) provides additional attraction of 6 MeV per nucleon at density \(0.4 \rho_0\) and 3 MeV per nucleon at \(2 \rho_0\) relative to Hartree-Fock with \(V_{low-k}\).
- Occupation probability at \(k=0\) at saturation density \(\rho_0\): 0.897 for the CD-Bonn \(T\)-matrix calculation, 0.920 for the \(V_{low-k}\) \(T\)-matrix calculation.
- For \(^{16}\)O using CCSD with \(\Lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\): binding energy \(-143.4 \pm 0.4\) MeV for N\(^3\)LO interaction and \(-153.3 \pm 0.4\) MeV for CD-Bonn interaction.

## Context

This work extends the use of \(V_{low-k}\) effective interactions, previously applied in BHF and Hartree-Fock calculations by Kuo et al. (2003) and Bogner et al. (2003), to the more sophisticated SCGF framework. It confirms the earlier BHF finding by Kuckei et al. (2003) that \(V_{low-k}\) overbinds nuclear matter at high densities. The paper introduces a density-dependent \(V_{low-k}(\rho)\) to remedy this, improving agreement with bare-interaction SCGF results. The work enables future studies of three-body forces, which the authors suggest may be needed to fully resolve the remaining discrepancy above saturation density.
