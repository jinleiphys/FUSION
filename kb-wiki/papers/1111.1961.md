# Structure effects in the region of superheavy elements via the $α$-decay chain of $^{293}$118

> The $α$-decay chain of $^{293}$118, first proposed in the Berkeley cold fusion experiment $^{208}$Pb($^{86}$Kr,1n) and now retracted, is calculated by using the preformed cluster model (PCM) of one of us (RKG). Also, the possible branchings of $α$-particles to heavier cluster decays of all the parents in this chain are calculated for the first time. The calculated Q-values, penetrabilities and preformation factors for $α$-decays suggest that the $^{285}$114 nucleus with Z=114, N=171 is a magic nucleus, either due to the magicity of Z=114, or of N=172 or of both. The N=172 is proposed to be a magic number in certain relativistic mean-field calculations, but with Z=120. The calculated cluster decays point to new interesting possibilities of $^{14}$C decay of the $^{281}$112 parent, giving rise to a (reasonably) deformed Z=106, N=161, $^{267}$106 daughter (N=162 being now established as the deformed magic shell) or to a doubly magic $^{48}$Ca cluster emitted from any of the parent nucleus in the $α$-decay chain. Apparently, these are exciting new directions for future experiments.

## Key claim

The calculated α-decay half-lives, Q-values, penetrabilities, and preformation factors for the chain of 293118 suggest that 285114 (Z=114, N=171) is a magic nucleus, possibly due to Z=114, N=172, or both; however, the authors note that N=172 is predicted to be magic only when Z=120 in certain relativistic mean-field calculations, and the experimental data for the α-decay chain itself has been retracted.

## Method

The authors used the preformed cluster model (PCM), which solves a stationary Schrödinger equation in the mass asymmetry coordinate to obtain cluster preformation probabilities, and calculates barrier penetrability via the WKB approximation. The fragmentation potential includes Coulomb, nuclear proximity, and binding energy terms, with binding energies taken from the 1995 Audi-Wapstra compilation and Möller et al. (1995) calculations using the finite-range droplet model (FRDM). The α-decay chain of 293118 was calculated for all parents down to 269106, and heavy cluster decays (including 10Be, 14C, and 48-50Ca) were computed for each parent nucleus in the chain.

## Key numbers

- The fusion cross section for the 208Pb(86Kr,1n) reaction producing 293118 was originally reported as 2.2+2.6-0.8 pb, but subsequent experiments set an upper limit of <0.5 pb.
- The calculated α-decay half-life for 285114 shows a strong peaking (very high stability), with the PCM and GLDM predictions differing by about four orders of magnitude.
- The preformation factor P0 for α in 285114 is smaller by a few orders of magnitude (∼10-9) compared to that for actinides.
- The assault frequency ν0 used in the PCM is approximately 3×1021 s-1.
- The calculated half-lives for 48-50Ca decays lie far below the present experimental limits, which go up to ∼1028 s for nuclei where enough atoms are available.

## Context

This work extends the preformed cluster model (PCM) of Gupta (1988) to the superheavy element region, analyzing the retracted Berkeley cold fusion experiment of Ninov et al. (1999) for 293118. The calculations contradict the predictions of Smolańczuk (1997), whose Q-values guided the original experiment and showed an ever-increasing function of parent charge, which the authors suggest was a cause for the experiment's failure. The results enable future experimental searches for 14C decay of 281112 (pointing to deformed magicity at N=162, as established by Hofmann (2000)) and for doubly magic 48Ca cluster emission from any parent in the chain, as well as cold synthesis of Z=114 via the 76Ge+208Pb reaction proposed by Gupta et al. (1977).
