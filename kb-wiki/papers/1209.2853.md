# Forward dihadron correlations in deuteron-gold collisions with a Gaussian approximation of JIMWLK

> We compute dihadron correlations in forward deuteron-gold or proton-gold collisions. The running coupling BK equation is used to calculate the energy dependence of the dipole cross sections and extended to higher point Wilson line correlators using a factorized Gaussian approximation. Unlike some earlier works we include both the "inelastic" and "elastic" contributions to the dihadron cross section. We show that the double parton scattering contribution is included in our calculation and obtain both an away side peak that roughly agrees with experimental observations and an estimate for the azimuthal angle-independent pedestal. We find that nonlinear effects for momenta close to the saturation scale are clearly visible in the away side peak structure.

## Key claim

The authors compute forward dihadron correlations in deuteron-gold collisions using a factorized Gaussian approximation of JIMWLK evolution, finding that including both "inelastic" and "elastic" contributions enhances the away-side peak by a large factor compared to earlier approximations, but the qualitative features, particularly a strong dependence of the correlation on the ratio of transverse momentum to the saturation scale, remain consistent with previous literature.

## Method

The running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation was solved numerically to obtain the energy dependence of dipole cross sections, with initial conditions from the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model using a nuclear saturation scale obtained by N_bin scaling. Higher-point Wilson line correlators needed for the dihadron cross section were constructed using a factorized Gaussian approximation at large N_c, relating them to the two-point function. The double parton scattering (DPS) contribution, which appears as a logarithmically divergent part in the full dihadron cross section, was identified and subtracted to avoid double counting, then added back separately using double parton distribution functions constructed from CTEQ NLO parton distributions with kinematic constraints.

## Key numbers

- Initial saturation scale for the proton: Q_s0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2 at x_0 = 0.007
- Nuclear saturation scale for minimum bias dAu collisions: Q_s0^2 = 0.72 GeV^2 (from N_bin scaling)
- Nuclear saturation scale for 0-20% centrality dAu collisions: Q_s0^2 = 1.51 GeV^2
- Quark mass used in numerical calculations: m_q = 0.14 GeV
- The nuclear saturation scale ratio Q_sA^2 / Q_sp^2 ≈ 3.6 for minimum bias dAu collisions from N_bin scaling
- The initial nuclear saturation scale used in the calculation of Albacete (2010) was Q_sA^2 ≈ 2.3 Q_sp^2

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculations of dihadron correlations by Marquet (2007) and Albacete (2010) by implementing the Gaussian approximation of JIMWLK evolution proposed by Dumitru (2011) and Iancu (2011), which relates higher-point Wilson line correlators to the two-point function. Unlike the "correlation limit" approach of Dominguez (2011) and Stasto (2011) where the DPS contribution vanishes, this calculation includes the full cross section and explicitly subtracts the DPS contribution to avoid double counting, following the emphasis on DPS importance by Strikman (2010). The results enable future comparisons with LHC proton-lead collision data and provide a framework for studying nonlinear effects at momenta close to the saturation scale.
