# A fully quantal molecular description for the spectra of bosons and fermions in the lowest Landau level

> Through the introduction of a class of appropriate translationally invariant trial wave functions, we show that the strong correlations in the lowest Landau level (LLL) reflect in finite systems the emergence of intrinsic point-group symmetries associated with rotations and vibrations of molecules formed through particle localization. This quantal molecular description is universal, being valid for both bosons and fermions, for both the yrast and excited states of the LLL spectra, and for both low and high angular momenta. "Quantum-fluid" physical pictures associated with Jastrow-type trial functions are shown to be reducible to the molecular description introduced in this paper.

# A fully quantal molecular description for the spectra of bosons and fermions in the lowest Landau level

## Key claim

The strong correlations in the lowest Landau level (LLL) for finite systems reflect the emergence of intrinsic point-group symmetries associated with rotations and vibrations of molecules formed through particle localization, and this quantal molecular description is universal for both bosons and fermions, for both yrast and excited states, and for both low and high angular momenta. The authors claim that "quantum-fluid" physical pictures associated with Jastrow-type trial functions are reducible to this molecular description, including at low angular momenta where quantum-liquid pictures had been thought to apply exclusively.

## Method

The authors constructed translationally invariant trial wave functions called rotating-vibrational molecular (RVM) states, built from a two-step method: first constructing a single Slater determinant (for fermions) or permanent (for bosons) using displaced Gaussian orbitals centered at vertices of regular polygons, then restoring rotational symmetry via angular momentum projection. The RVM basis combines pure rotational states (rotating electron molecules or rotating boson molecules) with vibrational excitations given by symmetric polynomials of the form Q_lambda = sum_i (z_i - z_c)^lambda, where lambda is a prime number greater than 1. The authors diagonalized the interaction Hamiltonian in this RVM basis and compared results to exact diagonalization (EXD) for systems of three and four particles.

## Key numbers

- For three spinless bosons with contact interaction at L=0: energy eigenvalue 1.5000000 in units of g/(pi Lambda^2), where Lambda = sqrt(hbar/(m Omega))
- For three spinless bosons at L=6: ground state energy 0.0000000 (the bosonic Laughlin function for nu=1/2 is equivalent to an RBM state incorporating vibrational correlations)
- For three spinless bosons at L=12: three energy eigenvalues 0.0000000, 0.0000000, and 0.5002441 in the same units
- For four spin-polarized electrons with Coulomb interaction at L=18 (nu=1/3): yrast state energy 1.30572905 in units of e^2/(kappa l_B)
- For four electrons at L=30 (nu=1/5): yrast state energy 0.25084902 e^2/(kappa l_B) per electron, agreeing with EXD within machine precision
- For four electrons at L=18: the pure REM state Phi^REM_18(0,4) has a 0.9294 overlap with the EXD yrast solution
- The RVM basis dimension at L=30 for four electrons is D^TI(L=30)=19, compared to the full EXD space dimension D^EXD(L=30)=169

## Context

This work extends the rotating electron molecule (REM) approach previously introduced by the same authors (Yannouleas and Landman, 2002, 2007) to bosonic systems and to vibrational excitations, providing a unified correlated basis that spans the translationally invariant subspace of the LLL spectrum. The authors show that the Jastrow-Laughlin ansatz (Laughlin, 1983) and composite-fermion trial functions (Jain, 2007) can be expanded in the RVM basis, and that composite-fermion diagonalization (CFD) approaches (Jeon and collaborators, 2004) fall short of machine precision because their basis is not translationally invariant. The work enables a molecular interpretation of states previously considered exclusively as quantum liquids, including the nu=1/3 and nu=1/5 fractional quantum Hall states, and provides a method to compute LLL spectra with machine precision using a much smaller basis than full EXD.
