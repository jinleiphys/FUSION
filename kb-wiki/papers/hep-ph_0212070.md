# Saturation Effects in Hadronic Cross Sections

> We compute total and differential elastic cross sections of high-energy hadronic collisions in the loop-loop correlation model that provides a unified description of hadron-hadron, photon-hadron, and photon-photon reactions. The impact parameter profiles of pp and gamma*p collisions are calculated. For ultra-high energies the hadron opacity saturates at the black disc limit which tames the growth of the hadronic cross sections in agreement with the Froissart bound. We compute the impact parameter dependent gluon distribution of the proton xG(x,Q^2,b) and find gluon saturation at small Bjorken x. These saturation effects manifest S-matrix unitarity in hadronic collisions and should be observable in future cosmic ray and accelerator experiments at ultra-high energies. The c.m. energies and Bjorken x at which saturation sets in are determined and LHC and THERA predictions are given.

## Key claim

The loop-loop correlation model (LLCM) predicts that saturation of the proton opacity at the black disc limit and gluon saturation at small Bjorken x occur at energies and x values far beyond current accelerator reach: the transition to a Froissart-bound-compliant ln^2 growth of the total pp cross section occurs at about sqrt(s) ≈ 10^3 TeV, and gluon saturation at zero impact parameter sets in at x ≲ 10^{-10} for Q^2 ≳ 1 GeV^2, both well above LHC energies and below HERA x ranges.

## Method

The authors compute total and differential elastic cross sections for pp, γ*p, and γγ collisions using the loop-loop correlation model (LLCM), which describes high-energy scattering via the correlation of two light-like Wegner-Wilson loops representing color dipoles. The dipole-dipole scattering amplitude S_DD is computed in the Berger-Nachtmann approach, combining non-perturbative stochastic vacuum model correlations with perturbative two-gluon exchange, with energy dependences ascribed to each component (ε^NP = 0.125, ε^P = 0.73). The impact parameter dependent gluon distribution xG(x,Q^2,|b⊥|) is extracted from the profile function for longitudinally polarized virtual photons via an approximate relation to the longitudinal structure function.

## Key numbers

- The transition from power-like to ln^2 increase of σ^{tot}_{pp}(s) occurs at about sqrt(s) ≈ 10^3 TeV, about two orders of magnitude beyond LHC energy sqrt(s) = 14 TeV.
- The proton opacity saturates at the black disc limit for ultra-high energies sqrt(s) ≳ 10^6 GeV.
- The gluon distribution at zero impact parameter xG(x,Q^2,|b⊥|=0) saturates at x ≲ 10^{-10} for Q^2 ≳ 1 GeV^2, far below HERA (x ≳ 10^{-6}) and THERA (x ≳ 10^{-7}) reach.
- The perturbative energy dependence parameter is ε^P = 0.73 and the non-perturbative parameter is ε^NP = 0.125.
- The LLCM results for dσ^{el}_{pp}/dt are compared to data at sqrt(s) = 23.5, 30.7, 44.7, 63 GeV (CERN ISR), 546 GeV (CERN Sp-pbar-S), and 1.8 TeV (Fermilab Tevatron).

## Context

This work extends the loop-loop correlation model of Shoshi (2002) to provide a unified description of hadron-hadron, photon-hadron, and photon-photon reactions that respects S-matrix unitarity. The saturation effects manifest the Froissart bound (Froissart, 1961) and are consistent with the concept of gluon saturation from gluon fusion processes taming the growth of cross sections at small x, as studied by Mueller (1986) and others. The predictions for LHC and THERA provide testable benchmarks, while cosmic ray experiments (Fly's eye data from Baltrusaitis (1984) and Akeno data from Honda (1993)) may access the ultra-high energy regime where saturation becomes observable.
