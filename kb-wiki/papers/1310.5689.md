# Effect of isospin asymmetry in nuclear system

> The effect of $δ-$ and $ω-ρ-$meson cross couplings on asymmetry nuclear systems are analyzed in the frame-work of an effective Field theory motivated relativistic mean field formalism. The calculations are done on top of the G2 parameter set, where these contributions are absent. We calculate the root mean square radius, binding energy, single particle energy (for the $1^{st}$ and last occupied orbits), density and spin-orbit interaction potential for some selected nuclei and evaluate the $L_{sym}-$ and $E_{sym}-$ coefficients for nuclear matter as function of $δ-$ and $ω-ρ-$meson coupling strengths. As expected, the influence of these effects are negligible for symmetry nuclear system and these effects are very important for systems with large isospin asymmetry.

# Effect of isospin asymmetry in nuclear system

## Key claim

The inclusion of δ-meson and ω-ρ cross couplings in the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) formalism has negligible influence on symmetric nuclear systems but becomes very important for systems with large isospin asymmetry, with the δ-meson coupling strength limited to gδ ≤ 1.5 for finite nuclei and gδ ≤ 7.0 for nuclear matter before convergence fails.

## Method

The authors extended the G2 parameter set of the E-RMF model by adding the scalar-isovector δ-meson and the ω-ρ cross coupling term Λv, then solved the resulting Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently for finite nuclei (⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb) and for infinite nuclear matter at various asymmetry parameters α = (ρn − ρp)/(ρn + ρp). They calculated binding energies, root-mean-square radii, single-particle energies, density distributions, spin-orbit potentials, and the symmetry energy coefficients Esym, Lsym, and Ksym as functions of the coupling strengths.

## Key numbers

- For the G2 parameter set alone (gδ = 0, Λv = 0), symmetric nuclear matter saturates at ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³, BE/A = 16.07 MeV, compressibility K₀ = 215 MeV, and symmetry energy Esym = 36.42 MeV.
- For pure neutron matter with gδ = 7.0, the maximum binding energy is approximately 7 MeV per nucleon.
- The symmetry energy Esym varies from 45.09 MeV (gδ = 0.0) down to 20.04 MeV (gδ = 7.0) at saturation density.
- The slope parameter Lsym decreases from 120.60 MeV (gδ = 0.0) to 55.78 MeV (gδ = 7.0).
- The curvature Ksym increases from −29.28 MeV (gδ = 0.0) to 13.27 MeV (gδ = 7.0).
- For finite nuclei, convergence fails beyond gδ ∼ 1.5; for nuclear matter, instability arises at gδ = 7.0.

## Context

This work extends the E-RMF model of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1996, 1997) by incorporating the δ-meson, which was absent in the original G2 parameter set, and the ω-ρ cross coupling studied by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001). It addresses the need for isovector scalar interactions to produce effective mass splitting between neutrons and protons, which is relevant for neutron-rich systems and drip-line nuclei. The results enable constraints on the δ-meson coupling strength by comparison with heavy-ion collision data on symmetry energy from the HIC collaboration and with non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock predictions (GSkII, Skxs20), and may help resolve the charge radius anomaly of ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca.
