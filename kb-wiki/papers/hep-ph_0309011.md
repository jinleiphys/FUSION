# Two-photon decays of pi^0, eta and eta-prime

> We investigate the decays of pi^0, eta and eta-prime into two photons in an effective U(3) chiral Lagrangian approach without employing large N_c arguments. Tree level and one-loop contributions from the anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten Lagrangian are calculated and the importance of eta--eta-prime mixing is discussed. Unitarity corrections beyond one-loop play an important role for the decays with off-shell photons and are included by employing a coupled channel Bethe-Salpeter equation which satisfies unitarity constraints and generates vector-mesons from composed states of two pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

The authors investigate the two-photon decays of π⁰, η, and η' using an effective U(3) chiral Lagrangian approach without employing large N_c arguments, finding that η-η' mixing contributes at next-to-leading order rather than leading order, which contradicts the usual assumption in large N_c chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors calculate tree-level and one-loop contributions from the anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten Lagrangian, including η-η' mixing effects without large N_c counting rules. Unitarity corrections beyond one-loop are included by employing a coupled channel Bethe-Salpeter equation that satisfies unitarity constraints and generates vector-mesons from composed states of two pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as F_π⁰ = F_π⁺ = 92.4 MeV
- For F_η the authors employ F_η ≈ 1.3 F_π, which follows from an analysis within conventional SU(3) ChPT
- The η-η' mixing angle from the two-photon decays is approximately -20° in the one-mixing-angle scheme (citing earlier work by Donoghue, Holstein, and Lin)
- The tree-level coefficients for the decay amplitudes are: α_π⁰(tree) = 1, α_η(tree) = 1/√3, α_η'(tree) = 2√(2/3) - (16π²/3)(w₁⁽¹⁾ + w₂⁽¹⁾)

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of two-photon decays by Donoghue, Holstein, and Lin (who showed one-loop diagrams do not lead to divergences for on-shell photons) and by Kaiser and Leutwyler (who proposed a two-mixing-angle scheme for pseudoscalar decay constants in large N_c ChPT). The authors also build upon their own earlier work (Borasoy and Nissler) where they showed that even at leading order, η-η' mixing does not obey the one-mixing-angle scheme if large N_c counting rules are not employed. The coupled channel Bethe-Salpeter approach was previously applied successfully to hadronic decays of η and η' by the same authors. This work enables predictions for double Dalitz decays with two off-shell photons, which have not yet been studied experimentally, and may clarify whether double vector-meson dominance holds for the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon and kaon decays.
