# Next-to-next-to-leading power corrections to unpolarized Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering

> Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering (SIDIS) is a key tool for exploring the three-dimensional structure of the nucleon through Transverse Momentum Dependent parton distributions and fragmentation functions. While leading-power contributions to the SIDIS cross-section are well established, next-to-leading (NLP) of order $1/Q$ and next-to-next-to-leading power (NNLP) corrections of order $1/Q^2$ to the hadronic tensor have only recently begun to be systematically investigated. These corrections are essential for the reliable phenomenology and interpretation of modern high-precision data. In recent papers by one of the authors, NNLP corrections to Drell-Yan process were derived using rapidity factorization formalism. In the present work we extend this approach to SIDIS and obtain analytic expressions for the unpolarized structure functions. We derive NNLP corrections that include convolutions of unpolarized distributions, $f_1$, with unpolarized fragmentation functions, $D_1$, and Boer-Mulders functions, $h_1^\perp$, with Collins fragmentation functions, $H_1^\perp$. We compare our results with previous formulations, provide numerical studies, confront our predictions with HERMES and COMPASS measurements, and present predictions for future experiments at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors derive next-to-next-to-leading power (NNLP) corrections of order $1/Q^2$ to the unpolarized SIDIS hadronic tensor using rapidity factorization formalism, obtaining analytic expressions that include convolutions of unpolarized distributions $f_1$ with unpolarized fragmentation functions $D_1$, and Boer-Mulders functions $h_1^\perp$ with Collins fragmentation functions $H_1^\perp$, under the approximation $Q^2 \gg M^2$ and at leading order in $1/N_c$.

## Method

The authors extend the rapidity factorization formalism previously applied to Drell-Yan (Balitsky 2020, 2024) to SIDIS by analytically continuing the Drell-Yan hadronic tensor to negative $q \to -q$, replacing the target hadron mass $M$ with the produced hadron mass $m_h$, and substituting TMD parton distribution functions of the projectile with TMD fragmentation functions according to specific replacement rules. They use QCD equations of motion to convert quark-antiquark-gluon TMDs to quark-antiquark TMDs, achieving gauge completion of the leading-twist result, and obtain expressions for the four unpolarized structure functions $F_{UU,T}$, $F_{UU,L}$, $F_{UU}^{\cos\phi_h}$, and $F_{UU}^{\cos2\phi_h}$.

## Key numbers

* The NNLP corrections are of order $1/Q^2$, while NLP corrections are of order $1/Q$, relative to the leading-power contribution.
* The kinematic prefactors in the cross section are $p_1 = (1-y)/(1-y+\frac12 y^2)$ and $p_3 = (2-y)\sqrt{1-y}/(1-y+\frac12 y^2)$, valid up to corrections suppressed as $1/Q^2$.
* The relation between transverse momenta in the two frames is $\bfPhperp = z_h{\bm k_\perp} + {\bm p_\perp}$ in the photon-hadron frame and $\qT = -{\bm k_T} + {\bm p'_T}$ in the hadron-hadron frame, with the approximation ${\bm k_\perp} = {\bm k_T}$, ${\bm p_\perp} = -z_h {\bm p'_T}$, $\bfPhperp = -z_h \qT$ valid for $Q^2 \gg M^2$.
* The SIDIS cross section is differential in $x$, $y$, $z_h$, $\phi_h$, and $\Phperp^2$, with $\sigma_0 = 2\pi\alpha_{\rm em}^2/(x y Q^2) \cdot (1-y+\frac12 y^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the rapidity factorization formalism for NNLP corrections from Drell-Yan (Balitsky 2020, 2024) to SIDIS, building on the pioneering leading-power and next-to-leading-power hadronic tensor calculations by Mulders and Tangerman (1995) and Kotzinian (1994). It complements recent investigations of NLP corrections by Ebert, Gao, and Stewart (2021), Gamberg et al (2022), and Rodini and Vladimirov (2023), as well as kinematic power corrections studied by Vladimirov (2023) and Piloneta and Vladimirov (2025). The results enable reliable phenomenology for high-precision SIDIS data from HERMES, COMPASS, Jefferson Lab, and the future Electron-Ion Collider, particularly for the longitudinally polarized photon structure function $F_{UU,L}$ and the $\cos\phi_h$ and $\cos2\phi_h$ modulations.
