# Electron Hydrodynamics in Graphene : Experimental and Theoretical Status

> The present work comprehensively reviews electron hydrodynamics in graphene, highlighting both experimental observations and theoretical developments. Key experimental signatures such as negative vicinity resistance, Poiseuille flow, and significant violation of the Wiedemann-Franz (WF) law have been discussed, with special emphasis on Lorenz ratio measurements. In the theoretical direction, recent efforts have focused on developing hydrodynamic frameworks for calculating the thermodynamic and transport coefficients of electrons in graphene. The present work has briefly addressed the theoretical framework adopted by our group.

## Key claim

This paper comprehensively reviews electron hydrodynamics in graphene, covering both experimental signatures and theoretical frameworks, with the authors' own group contributing a relativistic kinetic theory approach for calculating thermodynamic and transport coefficients of the electron-hole fluid. The review emphasizes that while experimental observations such as negative vicinity resistance, Poiseuille flow, and Wiedemann-Franz law violation are well-documented, the interpretation of these signatures as uniquely hydrodynamic remains qualified because ballistic systems can also produce negative vicinity resistance.

## Method

The authors present a relativistic kinetic theory framework for the Dirac fluid regime in graphene, where electrons and holes follow linear dispersion relations analogous to massless relativistic particles with the Fermi velocity replacing the speed of light. They derive macroscopic conservation laws from microscopic energy-momentum and charge conservation, then solve the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation to obtain expressions for electrical conductivity, thermal conductivity, shear viscosity, and the Lorenz ratio. The theoretical results are compared with experimental data from three different measurements of the Lorenz ratio in graphene samples.

## Key numbers

- Lorenz ratio reached approximately 22 times the standard Lorenz number L0 at temperature near 60 K in ultra-pure graphene samples (Crossno et al., 2016)
- Lorenz ratio reached approximately 400 times L0 near the Dirac point at temperature 19 K in ultra-clean graphene samples (Majumdar et al., 2025)
- Lorenz ratio violation up to 1.3 L0 observed via bipolar thermal conductivity measurements (PhysRevX.3.041008, 2013)
- Poiseuille flow visualized in graphene/hBN channels of length 15 micrometers and width 4.7 micrometers (Sulpizio et al., 2019)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio near the holographic lower bound of hbar/(4 pi kB) reported in Majumdar et al. (2025)

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed by Gurzhi (1963) for hydrodynamic electron transport and applies it specifically to graphene, where the Dirac cone band structure enables relativistic fluid behavior. The work builds upon experimental observations of Wiedemann-Franz law violation by Crossno et al. (2016) and Majumdar et al. (2025), and Poiseuille flow visualization by Sulpizio et al. (2019). The theoretical approach using relativistic kinetic theory with dimensional reduction from 3+1 to 2+1 spacetime dimensions provides a framework for calculating transport coefficients that can be tested against future experiments on electron hydrodynamics in two-dimensional materials.
