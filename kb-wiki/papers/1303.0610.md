# QCD sum rule study on the $f_0(980)$ structure as a pure $K \bar{K}$ bound state

> We perform a QCD sum rule analysis for the scalar $f_0(980)$ meson to investigate whether it can be described as a pure bound state of $K$ and $\bar{K}$ mesons. Based on the QCD sum rule with the operators of up to dimension 10 within the operator product expansion, we found that it is hard to treat the $f_0(980)$ as a simple $K\bar{K}$ bound state, which implies that the $f_0(980)$ scalar meson has more complicated structure being mixed states of various configurations.

## Key claim
The QCD sum rule analysis, including operators up to dimension 10, finds that it is hard to treat the $f_0(980)$ as a simple $K\bar{K}$ bound state, implying a more complicated mixed structure. The conclusion is conditional on the absence of any threshold value that simultaneously guarantees positivity of the OPE side and weak Borel-mass dependence of the fitted mass.

## Method
A QCD sum rule (QCDSR) was constructed for the $f_0(980)$ meson assuming a pure $K\bar{K}$ bound state interpolating current. The correlator was computed within the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-10 operators, including contributions from quark condensates, gluon condensate, and mixed condensates, using the factorization hypothesis for higher-dimensional condensates. The sum rule was then analyzed by fitting the mass and checking the Borel stability and pole-to-continuum ratio for various continuum thresholds $s_0$.

## Key numbers
- The standard input values used: $\langle\bar{u}u\rangle = -(0.25)^3$ GeV$^3$, $\langle g_c^2 G^2\rangle = 0.5$ GeV$^4$, $m_s = 0.15$ GeV, $ig_c\langle\bar{u}\sigma\cdot G u\rangle = 0.8$ GeV$^2 \langle\bar{u}u\rangle$, and $f_s = 0.8$ for the ratio of strange to light quark condensates.
- For $s_0 = 1.2$ GeV, the full $L^{\rm OPE}(M)$ (including up to dimension 10) becomes negative in the physical Borel region, contradicting the positive-definite left-hand side of the sum rule.
- For $s_0 = 1.5$ GeV, the fitted mass from the sum rule shows strong dependence on the Borel mass $M$, and the ratio of pole to continuum contributions is very small ($\sim 0.03$).
- For $s_0 = 2.0$ GeV, the fitted mass is about 1.8 GeV, much higher than the $f_0(980)$ mass.

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum rule approach previously applied to test the $\eta\eta$ bound state picture of the $f_0(980)$ by Lee (2011), which also found no support for that pure configuration. It contradicts the phenomenological Lagrangian analysis by Branz, Gutsche, and Lyubovitskij (2008) that claimed consistency of a pure $K\bar{K}$ bound state with decay data, and aligns with the $\pi\pi$ scattering analysis by Shen, Bai, and Li (2012) that rejected the pure $K\bar{K}$ structure. The results motivate future studies of mixed four-quark and two-quark configurations for the $f_0(980)$.
