# Isotropization by shock waves generation in anisotropic hydrodynamics

> Anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) has proven successful in modeling the evolution of quark-gluon matter created in heavy-ion collisions. The hydrodynamic description of quark-gluon plasma has also been widely used to study sound phenomena, such as shock waves. It has recently been shown that initial fluctuations in energy density and supersonic partons can generate fairly strong shock waves. However, significant anisotropic properties of the system due to the rapid longitudinal expansion of matter have not been taken into account in such studies. Moreover, the process of isotropization and its characteristic time-scales were not considered along with the question of the shock waves formation. Previous studies on shock discontinuous solutions in anisotropic hydrodynamics assumed constant anisotropy, leading to flow refraction towards the anisotropy axis and flow acceleration, characteristics of rarefaction waves, indicating limitations in this approach. This paper investigates discontinuous solutions for normal shock waves without flow refraction, introducing two compression parameters for longitudinal and transverse pressures. The resulting analytical solutions, as well as numerical computations, provide an isotropization mechanism of the system.

# Isotropization by shock waves generation in anisotropic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

This paper demonstrates that discontinuous solutions for normal shock waves in anisotropic hydrodynamics, without flow refraction and using two separate compression parameters for longitudinal and transverse pressures, provide a mechanism for isotropization of the quark-gluon plasma system. The degree of isotropization depends strongly on the direction of the shock wave relative to the anisotropy axis, with near-complete isotropization occurring for strong shocks in the transverse direction while significant anisotropy can remain in the longitudinal direction.

## Method

The author derives analytical solutions for normal shock waves in anisotropic hydrodynamics by introducing two compression parameters, sigma_perp and sigma_parallel, for transverse and longitudinal pressures respectively, and imposing the condition of no flow refraction (alpha' = alpha). The matching conditions from energy-momentum conservation across the discontinuity surface are solved analytically for the longitudinal case (normal vector parallel to anisotropy axis, alpha = 0) and transverse case (normal vector perpendicular to anisotropy axis, alpha = pi/2), and numerically for arbitrary polar angles alpha. The solutions relate upstream and downstream flow velocities, anisotropy parameters, and compression parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the longitudinal case (alpha = 0), the minimum downstream anisotropy parameter xi'_min relative to upstream xi approaches 1/3 as xi approaches 0, and decreases monotonically to zero as xi approaches infinity (see Fig. xi_min_alpha_0).
- For the transverse case (alpha = pi/2), the product of upstream and downstream velocities v * v' equals exactly 1/3, matching the isotropic ultrarelativistic solution.
- In the limit of strong shocks (sigma_perp approaching infinity) for any angle alpha except alpha = 0, the upstream velocity v approaches 1 and the downstream velocity v' approaches 1/3, identical to the isotropic ultrarelativistic limit.
- For the longitudinal case, the compression parameter k is restricted to the range (1, k*] where k*(xi) = (-R_perp + sqrt(R_perp(4 R_parallel + 5 R_perp))) / (2 R_parallel), and sigma_perp approaches infinity as k approaches k*.
- For alpha approximately 0.1 (about 5 degrees) or larger, sigma_perp reaches physically acceptable values near the critical point k = k* (Fig. s_crit for xi = 5).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shock discontinuous solutions in anisotropic hydrodynamics by Kovalenko (2022, 2023) that assumed constant anisotropy (xi = xi') across the shock, which led to flow refraction toward the anisotropy axis and flow acceleration characteristic of rarefaction waves. The present paper removes the constant anisotropy assumption and introduces two compression parameters, allowing for isotropization (xi' < xi) in compression shock waves. The mechanism links together the time scales of isotropization and shock wave formation in quark-gluon plasma, which is relevant to understanding early-stage evolution in heavy-ion collisions where previous work by Bouras (2009) showed that for eta/S > 0.2 the formation time of shock waves exceeds the expected QGP lifetime. The author proposes that the two-particle correlation function G(eta_1, eta_2, Delta_phi) used by ALICE (Acharya 2020) for event-by-event transverse momentum fluctuations could be used to study this isotropization mechanism experimentally.
