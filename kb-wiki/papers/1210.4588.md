# MUSIC with the UrQMD Afterburner

> As RHIC is entering the precision measurement era and the LHC is producing a copious amount of new data, the role of 3+1D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics is more important than ever to understand the bulk data as well as providing the background for hard probes. For more meaningful comparison with the experimental data, it is also important that hydrodynamics be coupled to the hadronic afterburner. In this proceeding we report on preliminary results of coupling MUSIC with UrQMD.

## Key claim

Coupling the 3+1D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC with the hadronic afterburner UrQMD yields preliminary results that show improved or comparable descriptions of RHIC and LHC data compared to pure hydrodynamics, but the authors emphasize these are preliminary and require more statistics and further tuning before definitive conclusions can be drawn.

## Method

The authors coupled the 3+1D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC (using the Israel-Stewart formalism and the Kurganov-Tadmor algorithm) with the hadronic kinetic theory model UrQMD, switching from hydrodynamics to the hadronic afterburner at a transition temperature of 170 MeV. For RHIC, 10,000 events were generated by running 100 UrQMD events on each of 100 viscous MUSIC events; for the LHC, 1,000 events were generated by running 10 UrQMD events on each of 100 viscous MUSIC events. The interface between the hyperbolic coordinate system of MUSIC and the Cartesian coordinate system of UrQMD was implemented with full treatment of the freeze-out hypersurface directions, though viscous corrections to the thermal distribution at the switching surface were not yet included.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from hydrodynamics to UrQMD afterburner: \(T_{\rm tr} = 170\,\hbox{MeV}\)
- For RHIC: 10,000 events per centrality bin (100 UrQMD events on each of 100 hydro events)
- For LHC: 1,000 events per centrality bin (10 UrQMD events on each of 100 hydro events)
- Freeze-out temperature for pure MUSIC without afterburner: \(T = 140\,\hbox{MeV}\)
- Results shown for 0-5% most central collisions (momentum distributions) and 10-20% centrality class (elliptic flow \(v_2\) and triangular flow \(v_3\))

## Context

This work extends the 3+1D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics approach of Schenke et al. (2010-2011) by coupling it to a hadronic afterburner, addressing the transition between the hydrodynamic and hadronic kinetic theory phases. It builds on earlier efforts by Petersen et al. (2008) who coupled ideal hydrodynamics with UrQMD, but MUSIC uses a more sophisticated viscous treatment and a hyperbolic coordinate system. The results enable more meaningful comparisons with precision RHIC data and new LHC data, particularly for identified particle spectra and flow harmonics, though the authors note that viscous corrections at the switching surface and better statistics are needed for definitive conclusions.
