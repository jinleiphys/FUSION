# Heavy quark production by a quasi-classical color field in proton-nucleus collisions

> We calculate the inclusive heavy quark production cross section for proton-nucleus collisions at high energies. We perform calculation in a quasi-classical approximation (McLerran-Venugopalan model) neglecting all low-x evolution effects. The derived expression for the differential cross section can be applied for studying the heavy quark production in the central rapidity region at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper derives the inclusive heavy quark production cross section for proton-nucleus collisions at high energies in the quasi-classical approximation (McLerran-Venugopalan model), explicitly neglecting all low-x quantum evolution effects, so the result is applicable only when e^{-1/α_s} ≲ x ≪ 1, corresponding to the central rapidity region at RHIC.

## Method

The calculation uses light-cone perturbation theory in the nucleus rest frame, separating the process into three time-ordered stages: gluon emission by a valence quark, subsequent quark-antiquark pair emission by the gluon, and interaction of the q_v g q q̄ system with the nucleus. The rescatterings of the partonic system in the nucleus are computed for each time ordering using the McLerran-Venugopalan model, with the saturation scale Q_s^2 defined via the gluon distribution function in a nucleon, and the result is exponentiated assuming independent interactions of the proton with individual nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale: Q_s^2 ≃ 2 GeV^2 for the Gold nucleus at x ≃ 0.01
- The derived formula is applicable at center-of-mass energy √s = 200 GeV at RHIC
- In the strong color field limit Q_s ≫ m, the total cross section scales as σ_tot ∼ A^{2/3}
- In the opposite limit Q_s ≪ m, the total cross section scales as σ_tot ∼ A

## Context

This work extends the quasi-classical approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the dipole model of Mueller (1994) to heavy quark production, generalizing the result of Kopeliovich and Tarasov (2000). The derived expression enables numerical calculations of charm production at RHIC, as performed in Kharzeev and Tuchin (2004), and can be used to study whether the formula yields Cronin enhancement of charm production analogous to gluon production. The paper explicitly notes that generalization to include low-x quantum evolution, which becomes important at forward rapidities as indicated by BRAHMS collaboration data, is an important task for future work.
