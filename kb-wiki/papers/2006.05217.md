# Constraining isovector nuclear interactions with giant resonances within a Bayesian approach

> We put a stringent constraint on the isovector nuclear interactions in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model from the centroid energy $E_{-1}$ of the isovector giant dipole resonance in $^{208}$Pb as well as its electric polarizability $α_D$. Using the Bayesian analysis method, $E_{-1}$ and $α_D$ are found to be mostly determined by the nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}$ at about $ρ^\star=0.05$ fm$^{-3}$ and the isovector nucleon effective mass $m_v^\star$ at the saturation density. At $90\%$ confidence level, we obtain $E_{sym}(ρ^\star) = 16.4 ^{+1.0}_{-0.9}$ MeV and $m_v^\star/m = 0.79 ^{+0.06}_{-0.06}$.

# Constraining isovector nuclear interactions with giant resonances within a Bayesian approach

## Key claim
The centroid energy of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in 208Pb and its electric polarizability are most sensitive to the symmetry energy at a subsaturation density of approximately 0.05 fm−3 and the isovector nucleon effective mass at saturation density, rather than directly to the symmetry energy at saturation density or its slope parameter L. At 90% confidence level, the authors obtain Esym(ρ⋆) = 16.4 +1.0/−0.9 MeV and m⋆v/m = 0.79 +0.06/−0.06.

## Method
The authors performed random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations based on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model to compute the IVGDR centroid energy E−1 and electric polarizability αD for 208Pb. They applied Bayesian analysis with Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling using the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm, treating Esym0, L, and m⋆v as free parameters with uniform priors, while fixing the isoscalar effective mass m⋆s/m = 0.83 from the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) excitation energy. The likelihood function was constructed from experimental data for E−1 = 13.46 MeV and αD = 19.6 ± 0.6 fm3.

## Key numbers
- Esym(ρ⋆) = 16.4 +1.0/−0.9 MeV at 90% confidence level, where ρ⋆ = 0.05 fm−3
- m⋆v/m = 0.79 +0.06/−0.06 at 90% confidence level at saturation density
- Esym(ρ⋆) = 16.4 +0.5/−0.7 MeV at 68% confidence level
- m⋆v/m = 0.79 +0.04/−0.04 at 68% confidence level
- m⋆s/m = 0.83, determined from the ISGQR excitation energy Ex = 10.9 MeV in 208Pb
- Neutron-proton effective mass splitting m⋆n−p ≈ 0.084 +0.143/−0.123 δ in normal nuclear matter with isospin asymmetry δ
- Experimental E−1 = 13.46 MeV from photoneutron scatterings
- Experimental αD = 19.6 ± 0.6 fm3 from polarized proton inelastic scatterings

## Context
This work extends previous studies linking IVGDR properties to the symmetry energy (Tri08, Col14) and electric polarizability to the slope parameter L (Roc13b, Roc15, Geb16), while incorporating the finding that both observables are also affected by the isovector effective mass (Zha16, Kon17). The Bayesian approach follows the methodology of Xie19. The result that IVGDR properties are most sensitive to the symmetry energy at about ρ0/3 confirms the conclusion of Zha14 regarding αD. This enables simultaneous extraction of the symmetry energy at subsaturation density and the isovector effective mass, breaking degeneracies that hindered previous analyses using single observables.
