# On the $θ$-angle physics of QCD under pressure: The strange and isospin phase diagram

> We unveil the impact of the $θ$-angle on the QCD phase diagram at nonzero isospin and strangeness chemical potentials for three light flavors and different quark mass ratios. We establish the phase boundaries as well as the nature of the associated phase transitions. The order parameters and the physical spectrum in the different phases are determined. We further elucidate the physics around $θ=π$ where we discover a novel parity-preserving superfluid phase. Finally, we comment on Dashen's phenomenon in the superfluid phases when varying the number of light flavors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the QCD phase diagram at nonzero isospin and strangeness chemical potentials is significantly altered by the topological $\theta$-angle, including the discovery of a novel parity-preserving superfluid phase at $\theta=\pi$ for certain quark mass ratios, but this finding is conditional on the specific regime where the up-down quark mass ratio $\gamma \equiv m/m_s$ exceeds 2.

## Method

The authors use the low-energy chiral Lagrangian for three-flavor QCD, incorporating the $\theta$-angle via Witten variables $\{\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3\}$ and including isospin ($\mu_I$) and strangeness ($\mu_s$) chemical potentials through covariant derivatives. They solve the static equations of motion for the vacuum ansatz in the Normal, Pion, and Kaon phases, then compare the ground state energies to determine phase boundaries and transition orders, working in the limit where the topological susceptibility parameter $a$ is much larger than meson masses.

## Key numbers

* The critical isospin chemical potential for the Normal-to-Pion transition is $\mu^2_{I,cr}(\theta) = m_\pi^2 \cos(\alpha(\theta))$, with minimum $\mu_{I,cr}(\theta=\pi) = m_\pi/\sqrt{2}$ for degenerate masses
* The critical strangeness chemical potential for the Normal-to-Kaon transition is $\mu_{s,cr}(\theta) = m_\pi \sqrt{\cos(\alpha(\theta))} - \mu_I/2$ for degenerate masses
* The quadruple point at $\theta=\pi$ for degenerate masses occurs at $\mu_s = \mu_I/2 = m_\pi/(2\sqrt{2})$
* The special parameter point in the Pion phase where parity is restored is $\mu_I^* = m_\pi^2 / \sqrt{2(2m_K^2 - m_\pi^2)}$, derived from $\mu_I^* = m_\pi \sqrt{\gamma/2}$
* The critical quark mass ratio separating CP-broken from CP-preserving regimes in the Normal phase is $\gamma = 2$, where $\gamma \equiv m/m_s = m_\pi^2/(2m_K^2 - m_\pi^2)$

## Context

This work extends the study of the $\theta$-angle phase diagram from the two-flavor case (Metlitski 2005) to three light flavors with isospin and strangeness chemical potentials, building on the zero-$\theta$ phase diagram established by Son (2000) and Kogut (2001). The findings enable future investigations of axion physics in dense matter, the cosmological Peccei-Quinn phase transition, and strongly interacting beyond-Standard-Model scenarios, while also providing a comparison to the two-color QCD results of Bersini (2022) where different behavior at $\theta=\pi/2$ and $3\pi/2$ was observed.
