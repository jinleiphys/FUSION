# Global dynamical correlation energies in covariant density functional theory: cranking approximation

> The global dynamical correlation energies for 575 even-even nuclei with proton numbers ranging from Z=8 to Z=108 calculated with the covariant density functional theory using the PC-PK1 parametrization are presented. The dynamical correlation energies include the rotational correction energies obtained with the cranking approximation and the quadrupole vibrational correction energies. The systematic behavior of the present correlation energies is in good agreement with that obtained from the projected generator coordinate method using the SLy4 Skyrme force although our values are systematically smaller. After including the dynamical correlation energies, the root-mean-square deviation predicted by the PC-PK1 for the 575 even-even nuclei masses is reduced from 2.58 MeV to 1.24 MeV.

## Key claim

The dynamical correlation energies (rotational plus quadrupole vibrational) calculated with the cranking approximation in covariant density functional theory using the PC-PK1 parametrization systematically improve mass predictions for 575 even-even nuclei, but the authors note that their values are systematically smaller than those obtained from the more exact projected generator coordinate method using the SLy4 Skyrme force.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations using the point-coupling PC-PK1 parametrization for 575 even-even nuclei with proton numbers Z=8 to Z=108. The rotational correction energy was computed using the cranking approximation with the Inglis-Belyaev moment of inertia, and the full dynamical correlation energy (including quadrupole vibrational corrections) was obtained by multiplying the rotational correction by a deformation-dependent factor with parameters taken from prior work. The Dirac equation was solved in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with 14 major shells for Z<60 and 18 major shells for heavier nuclei, and pairing correlations were treated with the BCS method using a density-independent pairing force.

## Key numbers

* The root-mean-square deviation for 575 even-even nuclear masses predicted by PC-PK1 was reduced from 2.58 MeV to 1.24 MeV after including dynamical correlation energies.
* The rotational correction energies for well-deformed nuclei vary between approximately 1.5 MeV and 3 MeV.
* For the mirror nuclei 26Mg and 26Si, rotational correction energies reach up to 4 MeV.
* The rms deviation between the cranking-approximation rotational correction energies and those from the topological Gaussian overlap approximation of angular-momentum-projected calculations (using SLy4) is approximately 0.64 MeV (excluding spherical nuclei).
* After including only rotational correction energies (not full dynamical correlations), the rms deviation was reduced from 2.58 MeV to 1.27 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory mass predictions of Geng et al. (2005) and Zhao et al. (2010) by including dynamical correlation energies that were previously omitted in CDFT mass tables. The approach follows the cranking-approximation methodology used by Goriely et al. (2009) and Chamel et al. (2008) in the nonrelativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, and the results are compared to the projected generator coordinate method calculations of Bender et al. (2006) using the SLy4 force. The improved mass predictions, particularly the nearly isospin-independent deviation from experimental data, suggest the PC-PK1 functional is promising for describing masses of neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis, though the authors note that exact beyond-mean-field calculations (currently in progress) are needed for nuclei where the ground state changes in configuration mixing calculations.
