# A Semiclassical Approach to Fusion Reactions

> The semiclassical method of Alder and Winther is generalized to study fusion reactions. As an illustration, we evaluate the fusion cross section in a schematic two-channel calculation. The results are shown to be in good agreement with those obtained with a quantal Coupled-Channels calculation. We suggest that in the case of coupling to continuum states this approach may provide a simpler alternative to the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels method.

## Key claim
The semiclassical Alder-Winther method, generalized to fusion reactions, yields fusion cross sections in good agreement with quantal Coupled-Channels calculations for a schematic two-channel model, but this agreement does not hold at sub-barrier energies because the classical trajectory does not reach the barrier radius and thus cannot account for the effective barrier lowering that enhances the cross section.

## Method
The authors generalized the semiclassical Alder-Winther method by replacing the Rutherford trajectory with a classical trajectory derived from the ground-state expectation value of the projectile-target interaction, then solving time-dependent Schrödinger equations for the intrinsic degrees of freedom along that trajectory. For fusion, they approximated the fusion probability for each partial wave as the product of the tunneling probability and the probability that the projectile is in a given intrinsic state at closest approach. They tested this approach on a schematic two-channel model mimicking \(^{6}\text{He} + ^{238}\text{U}\) collisions, representing the breakup channel by a single effective channel with an electric dipole form factor.

## Key numbers
- The \(^{6}\text{He}\) breakup threshold energy is \(B = 0.975\) MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used: real depth \(V_0 = -50\) MeV, radius \(r_{0r} = 1.25\) fm, diffuseness \(a_r = 0.65\) fm; imaginary depth \(W_0 = -50\) MeV, radius \(r_{0i} = 1.0\) fm, diffuseness \(a_i = 0.65\) fm.
- The semiclassical results were "very close" to full coupled-channel results at near-barrier energies, but the agreement fails at sub-barrier energies.

## Context
This work extends the semiclassical Alder-Winther method, originally developed for Coulomb excitation (Alder and Winther), to fusion reactions involving weakly bound projectiles where coupling to the continuum is important. It builds on prior CDCC calculations by Hagino (2000) and Diaz-Torres (2002) and on a semiclassical study of \(^{8}\text{B}\) breakup by Maass (2001) that agreed with CDCC results from Nunes and Thompson (1998). The approach offers a simpler alternative to full CDCC calculations for cases involving coupling to continuum states, and the authors suggest it can be extended to treat both complete and incomplete fusion, improving upon the classical three-body model of Hinde (2002).
