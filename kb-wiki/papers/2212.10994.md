# Unobservability of topological charge in nonabelian gauge theory

> We show that the topological charge of nonabelian gauge theory is unphysical by using the fact that it always involves the unphysical gauge field component proportional to the gradient of the gauge function. The removal of Gribov copies, which may break the Becchi-Rouet-Stora-Tyutin symmetry, is irrelevant thanks to the perturbative one-loop finiteness of the chiral anomaly. The unobservability of the topological charge immediately leads to the resolution of the Strong CP problem. We also present important consequences such as the physical relevance of axial $U(1)$ symmetry, the $θ$-independence of vacuum energy, the unphysicalness of topological instantons, and the impossibilities of realizing the sphaleron induced baryogenesis as well as the chiral magnetic effect. The unphysical vacuum angle and the axial $U(1)$ symmetry also imply that the CP phase of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix is the sole source of CP violation of the standard model.

# Unobservability of topological charge in nonabelian gauge theory

## Key claim
The topological charge of nonabelian gauge theory is unphysical because it always involves the longitudinal component of the gauge field, which is unphysical under BRST symmetry; this holds despite Gribov copy ambiguities because the chiral anomaly is perturbatively one-loop finite and infinitesimal field configurations always lie in the fundamental modular region where BRST symmetry is exact.

## Method
The author demonstrates that the topological charge density operator, when expressed as a surface integral, contains a triple product of spatial gauge fields contracted with the Levi-Civita symbol, which is always proportional to the three-dimensional gradient of the gauge function, the longitudinal polarization. Using the Adler-Bardeen theorem to establish perturbative one-loop finiteness of the chiral anomaly, and Glauber's coherent field formulation to argue that infinitesimal field amplitudes avoid Gribov copies, the author concludes that topological charge contributions to observables vanish when summed over all topological sectors.

## Key numbers
- The strong CP problem fine-tuning constraint is $|\theta| < 10^{-10}$, based on null electric dipole moment experimental results.
- The topological charge integral yields an integer change $\Delta n$ of the winding number labeling topological sectors.
- The chiral anomaly is given by $\frac{N_f\alpha_s}{8\pi} F_{\mu\nu,a}\tilde F^{\mu\nu}_a$, where $N_f$ is the number of active fermion flavors.
- The vacuum energy is $O(1)$ in the $1/N_c$ expansion and is claimed to be $\theta$-independent.

## Context
This work proposes a resolution to the Strong CP problem without introducing axions (Peccei 1977) or other new fields, contradicting the standard instanton-based resolution of the $U(1)_A$ problem (t Hooft 1976) and the necessity of a $\theta$-vacuum (Jackiw 1976, Callan 1976). The argument extends the perturbative BRST framework (Becchi 1975, Tyutin 1975) and the Adler-Bardeen theorem (Adler 1969, 1972) to nonperturbative topological sectors, with implications for lattice QCD simulations (Cossu 2013, Brandt 2016, Aoki 2020) and the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008). The paper enables a reassessment of baryogenesis mechanisms, ruling out sphaleron-induced baryogenesis (Manton 1983, Klinkhamer 1984) and requiring explicit baryon number violation (Georgi 1974, Affleck 1984) to explain matter abundance.
