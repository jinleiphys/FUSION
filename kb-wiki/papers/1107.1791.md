# Octet baryon electromagnetic form factors in a relativistic quark model

> We study the octet baryon electromagnetic properties by applying the covariant spectator quark model, and provide covariant parametrization that can be used to study baryon electromagnetic reactions. While we use the lattice QCD data in the large pion mass regime (small pion cloud effects) to determine the parameters of the model in the valence quark sector, we use the nucleon physical and octet baryon magnetic moment data to parameterize the pion cloud contributions. The valence quark contributions for the octet baryon electromagnetic form factors are estimated by extrapolating the lattice parametrization in the large pion mass regime to the physical regime. As for the pion cloud contributions, we parameterize them in a covariant, phenomenological manner, combined with SU(3) symmetry. We also discuss the impact of the pion cloud effects on the octet baryon electromagnetic form factors and their radii.

## Key claim

The paper provides a covariant parametrization of octet baryon electromagnetic form factors by separating valence quark contributions (constrained by lattice QCD data at large pion masses) from pion cloud contributions (parametrized phenomenologically using SU(3) symmetry and physical magnetic moment data), with the hedge that the separation between valence and pion cloud effects is model-dependent and cannot be uniquely defined from experiment alone.

## Method

The authors apply the covariant spectator quark model, describing each octet baryon as a quark-diquark system in an S-wave configuration, and compute the valence quark contributions to the Dirac and Pauli form factors using lattice QCD data at large pion masses to constrain the model parameters. They then add pion cloud contributions using a covariant, phenomenological parametrization based on one-pion loop diagrams, with the coupling strengths determined by the SU(3) symmetry parameter α = D/(D+F) = 0.6 from SU(6) symmetry, and fit the remaining parameters to physical nucleon and octet baryon magnetic moment data.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) symmetry parameter α = 0.6 (from SU(6) quark model) defines the pion cloud strength coefficients: β_Σ = 16/25, β_Λ = 12/25, β_Ξ = 1/25
- The mass parameter M_0 = 1.342 GeV and scalar integral B_0 = -0.127 GeV describe octet baryon masses within 7% accuracy
- The renormalization constants are Z_N = [1 + 3B_1]^{-1}, Z_Λ = [1 + 3β_Λ B_1]^{-1}, Z_Σ = [1 + (2β_Σ + β_Λ)B_1]^{-1}, Z_Ξ = [1 + 3β_Ξ B_1]^{-1}
- At Q^2 = 0, the bare charge e_{0B} equals the dressed charge e_B for each baryon

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to the nucleon (Ramalho, 2010) and decuplet baryons including the Omega (Ramalho, 2010) to the full octet baryon family, using lattice QCD data to constrain valence quark contributions where experimental data are scarce. The approach enables future studies of electromagnetic reactions involving Σ, Λ, and Ξ baryons at finite Q^2, where no experimental data currently exist, and provides a framework for separating valence and meson cloud effects that can be compared with chiral perturbation theory calculations (Perdrisat, 2007) and other phenomenological models.
