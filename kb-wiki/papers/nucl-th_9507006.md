# Delta Excitations in Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> We derive the contribution of $Δ$-h excitations to quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory. We discuss the effect of $Δ$ production on the determination of the axial mass $M_A$ in neutrino scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The paper derives the contribution of Delta-hole (Δ-h) excitations to quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering and shows that these contributions are comparable in size to particle-hole (p-h) excitations, potentially introducing a systematic error of order 0.1 GeV in the extracted axial mass \(M_A\) when data are analyzed assuming only p-h excitations.

## Method

The authors calculate the inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross section using a relativistic mean-field theory with a Fermi gas description of the nucleus, including both p-h and Δ-h contributions to the nuclear polarization tensor. The Δ resonance is treated with a Rarita-Schwinger propagator, and the decay width of the Δ (115 MeV) is included by averaging over the Δ mass with a Breit-Wigner distribution. The calculation is performed for a \(^{16}\)O target with Fermi momentum \(k_F = 225\) MeV, using an axial \(N\Delta\) transition strength \(r_{N\Delta} = 2\) and an axial mass \(M_A = 1.09\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The free Δ decay width used in the calculation is \(\Gamma = 115\) MeV.
- For \(^{16}\)O, the Fermi momentum is taken as \(k_F = 225\) MeV.
- In the mean-field approximation with universal couplings and \(k_F = 225\) MeV, the effective masses are \(M_\Delta^* = 931\) MeV, \(M^* = 638\) MeV, and the vector self-energy is \(V_N = V_\Delta = 239\) MeV.
- Including a finite delta width reduces the peak height of the double differential cross section by about 30 to 35 percent.
- When fitting the \(Q^2\) dependence from 0.3 to 1 GeV\(^2\) of the full calculation (p-h plus Δ-h) with a p-h only model, the extracted \(M_A\) is underestimated by 70 to 90 MeV.
- The theoretical uncertainty on an extracted \(M_A\) could be of order 0.1 GeV, compared to the claimed experimental error of \(\pm 0.03\) (stat) \(\pm 0.02\) (syst) GeV from the BNL experiment (Ahrens et al., 1988).

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic mean-field calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering by Kim et al. (1995), which examined nuclear structure corrections but did not include the Δ resonance, and the nonrelativistic treatment of Δ-h excitations by Singh and Oset (1992). The results show that Δ-h contributions are comparable to p-h contributions at GeV energies, implying that analyses of neutrino scattering data that assume only p-h excitations may extract an incorrect axial mass. The paper enables future experimental analyses to account for Δ contamination and calls for further theoretical work on the branching ratio of Δ decay channels in the nuclear medium.
