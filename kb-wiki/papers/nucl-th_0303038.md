# An Infrared Renormalization Group Limit Cycle in QCD

> Small increases in the up and down quark masses of QCD would tune the theory to the critical renormalization group trajectory for an infrared limit cycle in the three-nucleon system. At critical values of the quark masses, the deuteron binding energy goes to zero and the triton has infinitely many excited states with an accumulation point at the 3-nucleon threshold. The ratio of the binding energies of successive states approaches a universal constant that is close to 515. The proximity of physical QCD to the critical trajectory for this limit cycle explains the success of an effective field theory of nucleons with contact interactions only in describing the low-energy 3-nucleon system.

## Key claim
Small increases in the up and down quark masses of QCD would tune the theory to the critical renormalization group trajectory for an infrared limit cycle in the three-nucleon system; the proximity of physical QCD to this critical trajectory explains the success of an effective field theory of nucleons with contact interactions only in describing the low-energy three-nucleon system.

## Method
The authors used chiral extrapolations from an effective field theory with explicit pions (Epelbaum, Meissner, and Glockle 2002) to compute the inverse scattering lengths 1/a_t and 1/a_s as functions of the pion mass m_pi. They then used the contact effective field theory of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) to calculate the three-body spectrum in the triton channel as a function of m_pi, taking the central values of the error bands for the scattering lengths and approximating the three-body parameter Lambda_* as constant at 189 MeV for small extrapolations.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of binding energies of successive states in the limit cycle approaches a universal constant close to 515, corresponding to a discrete scaling factor lambda_0 = e^{pi/s_0} approximately 22.7, where s_0 approximately 1.00624.
- The physical spin-singlet np scattering length is a_s = -23.8 fm and the spin-triplet np scattering length is a_t = 5.4 fm, both significantly larger than the NN effective range r_0 = 1.8 fm in the spin-triplet channel.
- The critical value m_{pi,t} at which 1/a_t = 0 is predicted to be in the range 170 MeV < m_{pi,t} < 210 MeV, not much larger than the physical pion mass of 138 MeV.
- The three-body parameter Lambda_* = 189 MeV for m_pi = 138 MeV, obtained by taking the triton binding energy as input.
- The deuteron binding energy at physical quark masses is 2.2 MeV; when extrapolated to the chiral limit, the deuteron has a much larger binding energy comparable to the scale 1/(m_N r_0^2) approximately 10 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Efimov effect (Efimov 1971, 1979, 1981) and the effective field theory formulation by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999), which exhibited an ultraviolet RG limit cycle in a contact EFT of nucleons. The authors argue that this ultraviolet limit cycle is not an artifact but a hint of an infrared limit cycle in QCD itself. The result has implications for lattice QCD simulations, which are typically performed at pion masses 2-3 times larger than the physical value and must extrapolate past the region where an RG limit cycle occurs, potentially introducing large extrapolation errors.
