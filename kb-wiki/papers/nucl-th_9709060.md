# Thermodynamic Properties of the SU(2)$_f$ Chiral Quark-Loop Soliton

> We consider a chiral one-loop hedgehog soliton of the bosonized SU(2)$_f$ Nambu & Jona-Lasinio model which is embedded in a hot medium of constituent quarks. Energy and radius of the soliton are determined in self-consistent mean-field approximation. Quasi-classical corrections to the soliton energy are derived by means of the pushing and cranking approaches. The corresponding inertial parameters are evaluated. It is shown that the inertial mass is equivalent to the total internal energy of the soliton. Corrected nucleon and $Δ$ isobar masses are calculated in dependence on temperature and density of the medium. As a result of the self-consistently determined internal structure of the soliton the scaling between constituent quark mass, soliton mass and radius is noticeably disturbed.

# Thermodynamic Properties of the SU(2)$_f$ Chiral Quark-Loop Soliton

## Key claim

The authors show that a chiral one-loop hedgehog soliton of the bosonized SU(2)$_f$ Nambu & Jona-Lasinio model, embedded in a hot medium of constituent quarks, dissolves at temperatures around 180 MeV when the valence quark occupation number is kept fixed to one, but this dissolution does not coincide with the restoration of chiral symmetry, the constituent quark mass is only reduced to half its vacuum value when the soliton dissolves.

## Method

The authors compute the grand canonical potential of the NJL model in the mean-field approximation, splitting the quark determinant into a regularized sea contribution (using Schwinger's proper-time scheme) and a medium contribution that depends on temperature and chemical potential. They restrict the meson fields to the chiral circle and to static, spherically symmetric hedgehog configurations, then solve the self-consistent equations of motion for the chiral angle. Quasi-classical corrections to the soliton energy are derived using pushing and cranking approaches to restore translational and rotational symmetries, with the inertial parameters evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: $M = 420\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (chosen to reproduce the experimental Delta-nucleon splitting)
- Soliton dissolution occurs at temperatures around $180\ \mathrm{MeV}$ when the valence level occupation is fixed to one
- At temperatures below approximately $75\ \mathrm{MeV}$, numerical results become unreliable due to finite box radius effects (box radius $D = 18/M^*$)
- The medium density $\rho_0$ is given in units of normal nuclear density $\rho_{\mathrm{nm}} = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$
- The cut-off parameter $\Lambda$ is related to experimental values of the pion mass and weak pion-decay constant in vacuum

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton approach to baryons (reviewed by Christov (1996) and Alkofer (1996)) by embedding the soliton in a hot and dense medium of constituent quarks, combining the NJL model's description of chiral symmetry restoration (reviewed by Hatsuda (1994)) with the non-topological soliton picture. The approach differs from earlier attempts by Jaminon (1989) and Berger (1996) to replace quark degrees of freedom with nucleonic ones, which led to chiral symmetry restoration already at normal nuclear density. The relation between inertial mass and internal energy extends a corresponding relation for solitons in vacuum derived by Pobylitsa (1992). The work enables study of how the internal structure of baryons changes with temperature and density, though the authors note the mean-field approximation neglects thermal pion fluctuations and that the quark gas is not the true ground state below critical temperature and density.
