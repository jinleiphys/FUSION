# Phenomenology of Jet Quenching in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We derive an analytical expression for the quenching factor in the strong quenching limit where the $p_T$ spectrum of hard partons is dominated by surface emission. We explore the phenomenological consequences of different scaling laws for the energy loss and calculate the additional suppression of the away-side jet.

# Phenomenology of Jet Quenching in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper derives an analytical expression for the jet quenching factor in the strong quenching limit, showing that the high-\(p_T\) parton spectrum is dominated by surface emission and scales with the surface area rather than the volume of the collision zone. The analytical approximation is valid to within 5% when the quenching factor \(Q \le 0.2\), but the author notes that the momentum dependence of the QCD-motivated BDMS energy loss formula does not seem to agree with some RHIC data, and a linear dependence of the average energy loss on \(p_T\) is in better agreement with the data in the \(p_T\) region explored so far.

## Method
The author models the energy loss of an energetic parton traversing a homogeneous, static medium of thickness \(L\) using a general scaling law \(\Delta p_T = \eta p_T^\mu L\), then derives an analytical expression for the quenching factor \(Q(p_T)\) by integrating the quenched parton spectrum over the transverse geometry of a cylindrical collision zone. The analytical result is compared with exact numerical integration and extended to noncentral collisions using a surface-to-volume ratio generalized for impact parameter \(b\). Three scaling laws for energy loss are explored numerically: the Bethe-Heitler (BH) law with \(\mu=1\), a random-walk (RW) law with \(\Delta p_T \propto p_T \sqrt{(L\rho)_{\rm eff}}\), and the BDMS law with \(\Delta p_T \propto L_{\rm eff} \sqrt{\rho p_T / \nu}\), incorporating an expanding medium with density \(\rho({\bf r},\tau) = C \rho_{\rm part}({\bf r}) / (\tau+\tau_0)\).

## Key numbers
- For pions with \(p_T \approx 5\) GeV/c in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon pair, the measured suppression factor \(Q(p_T)\) is about 1/5.
- The unquenched parton spectrum is parameterized as \(dN/d^2p_T = N_0 (1 + p_T/p_0)^{-\nu}\) with power \(\nu \approx 8\) and \(p_0 \approx 1.75\) GeV/c.
- The analytical approximation for the quenching factor, \(Q(p_T) \approx 2(p_0+p_T) / [\pi R \eta (\nu-1) p_T^\mu]\), deviates from the exact integral by less than 5% when \(Q \le 0.2\).
- For the numerical study, the stopping power strength parameters are set to \(\eta=0.06\) (RW), \(\eta=0.017\) (BH), and \(\eta'=1.1\) (BDMS).
- The calculated elliptic flow parameter \(v_2 \le 0.1\) for all three scaling laws is significantly smaller than the measured values \(v_2 \approx 0.2\) for semicentral and peripheral collisions.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for jet quenching developed by Baier et al. (BDMS) and others (Zakharov 1996, Wiedemann 2000, Wang 2001, Gyulassy 2001), providing an analytical tool to connect energy loss models to experimental observables at RHIC. The paper addresses discrepancies between the PHOBOS collaboration's observation that charged hadron yields at \(p_T \approx 4\) GeV/c scale with participant number \(N_{\rm part}\) and the PHENIX collaboration's contradictory findings, suggesting that parton recombination (Fries 2003, Greco) may dominate hadron formation at intermediate \(p_T\) while fragmentation dominates above 6 GeV/c. The results enable experimental tests of the universal relation between same-side and away-side jet suppression factors predicted for the BDMS law, and motivate further investigation of competing energy gain and loss mechanisms in the momentum range explored at RHIC.
