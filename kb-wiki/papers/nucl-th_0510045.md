# A Complex Shell Model Representation Including Antibound States

> A generalization of the Complex Shell Model formalism is presented which includes antibound states in the basis. These states, together with bound states, Gamow states, and the continuum background, represented by properly chosen scattering waves, form a representation where all states are treated on the same footing. Two-particle states are evaluated within this formalism and observable two-particle resonances are defined. The formalism is illustrated in the well known case of $^{11}$Li in its bound ground state and in $^{70}$Ca(gs), which is also bound. Both cases are found to have a halo structure. These halo structures are described within the generalized Complex Shell Model. We investigated the formation of two-particle resonances in these nuclei, but no evidence of such resonances was found.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Complex Shell Model (CXSM) to include antibound (virtual) states in the single-particle basis, alongside bound states, Gamow resonances, and discretized continuum scattering states; the authors find that both \(^{11}\)Li and \(^{70}\)Ca have bound ground states with halo structures, but they report **no evidence** of observable two-particle resonances in either nucleus.

## Method

The authors solve the time-independent Schrödinger equation with outgoing boundary conditions, classifying eigenstates into bound, antibound, decay resonant (Gamow), and capture resonant states. They construct a Berggren representation using a generalized integration contour in the complex energy plane that encloses both Gamow resonances and antibound states, regularizing divergent integrals via complex rotation. Two-particle states are then evaluated using the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) with a separable two-body interaction derived from the derivative of a Woods-Saxon plus Gaussian central potential.

## Key numbers

- \(^{11}\)Li: The \(1s_{1/2}\) single-particle state is an antibound state at energy \((-0.025, 0)\) MeV with wave number \(k_n = (0, -0.033)\) fm\(^{-1}\); the \(0p_{1/2}\) state is a resonance at \((0.240, -0.064)\) MeV (position 240 keV, width 128 keV).
- \(^{11}\)Li: The Woods-Saxon potential parameters are \(V_0 = 39.97\) MeV, \(V_0^{so} = 19.43\) MeV, \(r_0 = r_0^{so} = 1.27\) fm, \(a = a^{so} = 0.67\) fm; the Gaussian potential has \(V_g = 663\) MeV and \(a_g = 0.26\) fm.
- The bound \(0s_{1/2}\) state in \(^{11}\)Li lies at \((-20.61, 0)\) MeV, and the bound \(0p_{3/2}\) state at \((-4.525, 0)\) MeV.
- For the \(n+^9\)Li system, the scattering length is large and negative (\(-17\) fm), indicating the \(1s_{1/2}\) state is antibound.
- The shortest measurable radioactive decay corresponds to a mean life \(T \approx 10^{-12}\) sec and a width \(\Gamma = 6.6 \times 10^{-10}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Complex Shell Model (CXSM) formalism previously developed by the same group (Refs. [rsm, mic, cxsm]) by incorporating antibound states, following a short publication (Ref. [ant]) that first included them. The method is applied to the well-studied halo nucleus \(^{11}\)Li, which has been a test case for models of weakly bound nuclei (reviews by Zhu, Garrido, and Suzuki), and to the neutron drip-line nucleus \(^{70}\)Ca. The inclusion of antibound states enables a more complete description of halos in exotic nuclei and allows future studies of two-particle resonances in systems where such states may be physically meaningful.
