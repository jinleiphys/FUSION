# Axial-Current Matrix Elements in Light Nuclei from Lattice QCD

> I present results from the first lattice QCD calculations of axial-current matrix elements in light nuclei, performed by the NPLQCD collaboration. Precision calculations of these matrix elements, and the subsequent extraction of multi-nucleon axial-current operators, are essential in refining theoretical predictions of the proton-proton fusion cross section, neutrino-nucleus cross sections and $ββ$-decay rates of nuclei. In addition, they are expected to shed light on the phenomenological quenching of $g_A$ that is required in nuclear many-body calculations.

## Key claim

The first lattice QCD calculations of axial-current matrix elements in light nuclei yield results consistent with experiment and phenomenology, but only under the assumption of mild quark-mass dependence of the correlated two-nucleon contributions, an assumption supported by analogous behavior observed in the radiative capture process \(np\rightarrow d\gamma\).

## Method

The NPLQCD collaboration employed axial background fields to generate light-quark propagators, which were then used to compute axial-current matrix elements in light nuclei at a pion mass of \(m_\pi\sim 805\) MeV. The calculations were performed at unphysical light-quark masses and a single spacetime volume with one discretization, and the results were extrapolated to the physical point using effective field theory (EFT). The leading matrix element for low-energy proton-proton fusion was calculated to constrain the higher-order isovector counterterm \(L_{1A}\) in pionless EFT, while the Gamow-Teller matrix element for tritium \(\beta\)-decay was also determined.

## Key numbers

- Gamow-Teller matrix element for tritium \(\beta\)-decay: GT = 0.979(03)(10), compared to the experimental value GT\(^{\rm expt}\) = 0.9511(13).
- S-factor for pp-fusion (without electromagnetism and isospin breaking): \(\Lambda(0) = 2.6585(06)(72)(25)\), compared to the currently accepted value \(\Lambda(0) = 2.652(2)\).
- Counterterm \(L_{1A}\) in pionless EFT: \(L_{1A}=3.9(0.1)(1.0)(0.3)(0.9)~{\rm fm}^3\).
- All calculations were performed at a pion mass of \(m_\pi\sim 805\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the NPLQCD collaboration's prior lattice QCD calculations of light nuclei binding energies and magnetic interactions (Beane 2012, Beane 2014, Chang 2015) to axial-current matrix elements, which are essential for refining theoretical predictions of proton-proton fusion, neutrino-nucleus cross sections, and \(\beta\beta\)-decay rates. The results enable future precision calculations of axial-current form factors of light nuclei and constrain the multi-nucleon axial-current operators needed for nuclear many-body calculations using EFT techniques, building on the few-body analyses of Barnea (2013), van Kolck (2015), and Kirscher (2015).
