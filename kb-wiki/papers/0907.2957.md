# Shell-model Hamiltonian from self-consistent mean-field model: $N=Z$ nuclei

> We propose a procedure to determine the effective nuclear shell-model Hamiltonian in a truncated space from a self-consistent mean-field model, e.g., the Skyrme model. The parameters of pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interaction with monopole force are obtained so that the potential energy surface of the Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS calculation is reproduced. We test our method for $N=Z$ nuclei in the $fpg$- and $sd$-shell regions. It is shown that the calculated energy spectra with these parameters are in a good agreement with experimental data, in which the importance of the monopole interaction is discussed. This method may represent a practical way of defining the Hamiltonian for general shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a procedure to determine the parameters of a shell-model Hamiltonian (pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interaction with monopole force) by fitting the potential energy surface of a Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS calculation, rather than by empirical fitting to experimental spectra. The method is tested only for \(N=Z\) nuclei in the \(fpg\)- and \(sd\)-shell regions, and the authors note that the single-particle energies in the shell-model calculation are still taken from experiment, not from the self-consistent mean-field model.

## Method

The authors start with a constrained Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS calculation (using the SIII parameter set for \(^{68}\)Se and SLy4 for \(^{28}\)Si) to generate potential energy surfaces on the \(\beta\)-\(\gamma\) plane. They then define a shell-model Hamiltonian with a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole plus monopole (\(PQQM\)) form and adjust its few parameters (pairing strength \(g_0\), quadrupole strength \(\chi\), and monopole strengths) so that the expectation value of the shell-model Hamiltonian in a constrained Hartree-Fock state reproduces the Skyrme Hartree-Fock potential energy surface. Shell-model calculations are performed in the \(fpg\)-shell space (with a \(^{56}\)Ni core) and the \(sd\)-shell space (with a \(^{16}\)O core), using experimental single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

* For the \(fpg\)-shell space, the extracted force strengths are \(g_{0}=0.270(64/A)\) and \(\chi=0.222(64/A)^{5/3}/b^{4}\), with the \(T=1\) monopole force strength \(V_{m}(f_{5/2},p_{1/2};T=1)=-0.25\) MeV.
* For the \(sd\)-shell space, the extracted force strengths are \(g_{0} = 0.50\) and \(\chi=4.158A^{-2/3}/b^{4}\), with monopole interaction strengths \(V_{m}(d_{5/2},d_{3/2};T=1)=-0.20\) MeV and \(V_{m}(s_{1/2},s_{1/2};T=1)=1.0\) MeV.
* For \(^{68}\)Se, the Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation with SIII yields two separate minima at oblate and prolate sides with deformation \(\beta\approx 0.24\).
* For \(^{28}\)Si, the Skyrme Hartree-Fock potential energy surface has a minimum at the oblate side with deformation \(\beta\approx 0.33\), corresponding to the experimental spectroscopic quadrupole moment \(Q_{s}=16\) \(e\)fm\(^{2}\).
* For \(^{68}\)Se, the calculated \(B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow 0_{1}^{+})\) yields \(\beta\sim 0.26\), consistent with the experimental estimation \(\beta\sim 0.27\) by Fischer et al. and 0.30 by Jenkins et al.
* For \(^{28}\)Si, the calculated \(B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow 0_{1}^{+})\) is 55.7 \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\), compared to the experimental value of 66.7 \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of mapping self-consistent mean-field results onto a simpler Hamiltonian, following the earlier proposal by Alhassid, Bertsch, and collaborators for mapping onto a shell-model Hamiltonian with monopole pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, and the more recent work by Nomura et al. (2008) for mapping onto the interacting boson model. The authors use the \(PQQM\) model, which had been previously applied phenomenologically to \(N\approx Z\) nuclei by Hasegawa (2001) and Kaneko (2002). The method enables the derivation of a shell-model Hamiltonian from a microscopic mean-field theory, potentially allowing systematic shell-model calculations for heavier nuclei where empirical fitting is difficult, and could be applied to the Projected Shell Model.
