# Effect of the repulsive core in the proton-neutron potential on deuteron elastic breakup cross sections

> The role of the short-range part (repulsive core) of the proton-neutron ($pn$) potential in deuteron elastic breakup processes is investigated. A simplified one-range Gaussian potential and the Argonne V4' (AV4') central potential are adopted in the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method. The deuteron breakup cross sections calculated with these two potentials are compared. The repulsive core is found not to affect the deuteron breakup cross sections at energies from 40 MeV to 1 GeV. To understand this result, an analysis of the peripherality of the elastic breakup processes concerning the $p$-$n$ relative coordinate is performed. It is found that for the breakup processes populating the $pn$ continua with orbital angular momentum $\ell$ different from 0, the reaction process is peripheral, whereas it is not for the breakup to the $\ell=0$ continua (the s-wave breakup). The result of the peripherality analysis indicates that the whole spatial region of deuteron contributes to the s-wave breakup.

## Key claim

The short-range repulsive core of the proton-neutron potential does not affect deuteron elastic breakup cross sections on a $^{58}$Ni target at incident energies from 40 MeV to 1 GeV, but this insensitivity arises for different reasons depending on the partial wave: for d-wave and g-wave breakup the process is peripheral (sensitive only to the asymptotic tail of the deuteron wave function), whereas for s-wave breakup the process is non-peripheral and probes the whole spatial region of the deuteron, which smears out the difference between wave functions with and without a repulsive core.

## Method

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method was used to calculate deuteron elastic breakup cross sections on a $^{58}$Ni target. Two proton-neutron interactions were compared: the Argonne V4' (AV4') central potential, which contains a short-range repulsive core, and a one-range Gaussian potential (1G-av4) fitted to reproduce the same deuteron binding energy (2.24 MeV) and root-mean-square radius (2.01 fm) as AV4'. The nucleon-target distorting potentials were obtained by folding the Melbourne g-matrix interaction with the target density. To test peripherality, six additional one-range Gaussian potentials with deuteron rms radii from 1.7 fm to 2.2 fm were constructed, and the ratio $f = \sigma_{\rm bu} / b^2$ (breakup cross section divided by the square of the asymptotic normalization coefficient) was examined for each partial-wave component.

## Key numbers

- At 80 MeV on $^{58}$Ni, the total breakup cross section is 106.7 mb with AV4' and 105.3 mb with 1G-av4; the difference in each partial-wave component (s-wave, d-wave, g-wave) is less than 2%.
- At 1 GeV on $^{58}$Ni, the total breakup cross section is 40.8 mb with AV4' and 40.9 mb with 1G-av4.
- The s-wave component at 80 MeV is 27.3 mb (AV4') vs 27.0 mb (1G-av4); at 1 GeV it is 10.5 mb (AV4') vs 10.6 mb (1G-av4).
- The d-wave component at 80 MeV is 58.4 mb (AV4') vs 57.5 mb (1G-av4); at 1 GeV it is 27.9 mb for both potentials.
- The g-wave component at 80 MeV is 21.0 mb (AV4') vs 20.7 mb (1G-av4); at 1 GeV it is 24.3 mb for both potentials.
- The one-range Gaussian potentials used for the peripherality study have depths $V_0$ ranging from 31.76 MeV to 280.23 MeV and ranges $a$ from 0.687 fm to 2.492 fm, producing deuteron rms radii from 1.70 fm to 2.20 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier comparison by Iseri (1991) between one-range Gaussian and Reid soft core potentials for deuteron elastic scattering, which found differences mainly in polarization observables rather than cross sections. The present study focuses specifically on the central part of the interaction using the AV4' potential and examines breakup cross sections systematically from 40 MeV to 1 GeV. The peripherality analysis using the ratio $f = \sigma_{\rm bu} / b^2$ follows the idea of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method. The results show that deuteron breakup reactions are not a sensitive probe of the short-range repulsive core of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, except possibly for s-wave breakup where the whole spatial region contributes; this motivates further investigation into whether a breakup process that selectively probes $r$ larger than 1 fm could reveal the repulsive core effect.
