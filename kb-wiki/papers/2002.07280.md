# A covariant model for the decuplet to octet Dalitz decays

> In the last years it became possible to measure in HADES the dilepton decays of several baryons. The baryon dilepton decays provide information about the electromagnetic structure of the baryons in the timelike region. In the present work, we study the $B^\prime \to e^+ e^- B$ decays, where $B^\prime$ is a baryon decuplet member and $B$ is a baryon octet member. Our calculations are based on the covariant spectator quark model, where the contribution of the quark core is complemented with an $SU(3)$ contribution from the pion cloud. The pion cloud contribution prove to be relevant in the range of study. We present predictions for the $Σ^0 (1385) \to e^+ e^- Λ(1116)$ and $Σ^{+}(1385) \to e^+ e^- Σ^+(1193)$ decays, which may be tested at HADES in a near future. Predictions for the remaining decuplet baryon Dalitz decays are also presented. We conclude that different orders of magnitudes are expected for the baryon decuplet Dalitz decay widths, according to the quark content of the baryons. We also conclude that the dependence of the transition form factors on the square momentum transfer ($q^2$) is important for some transitions.

# Covariant model for decuplet to octet Dalitz decays

## Key claim
The covariant spectator quark model, which combines a three-quark core with an SU(3)-based pion cloud contribution, can predict the Dalitz decay widths of decuplet baryons to octet baryons in the timelike region, with the pion cloud contribution proving relevant across the range of study. The predictions for the Σ⁰(1385) → e⁺e⁻ Λ(1116) and Σ⁺(1385) → e⁺e⁻ Σ⁺(1193) decays may be tested at HADES in the near future, but the model's accuracy depends on the assumption that the magnetic dipole form factor dominates and that quadrupole contributions are negligible.

## Method
The author extended the covariant spectator quark model, previously developed for spacelike electromagnetic transitions, to the timelike region by converting the momentum transfer variable (q² = -Q²) and generalizing the dependence on the invariant mass W of the γ*B system. The magnetic dipole transition form factor G_M was decomposed into a valence quark contribution (from a quark-diquark S-wave configuration) and a pion cloud contribution (calibrated by the γ*N → Δ(1232) transition and extended via SU(3) symmetry). The Dalitz decay widths were then computed by integrating the differential decay rate over the dilepton invariant mass from the electron-pair threshold (4m_e²) to the kinematic limit (W - M_B)².

## Key numbers
- The Δ(1232) Dalitz decay was recently analyzed at HADES based on the pp → pp e⁺e⁻ channel, and the results were compared with the author's previous estimates (Timelike2).
- The physical masses used for decuplet baryons are: M_Δ = 1.232 GeV, M_Σ* = 1.385 GeV, and M_Ξ* = 1.533 GeV.
- The octet baryon masses used are: M_N = 0.939 GeV, M_Λ = 1.116 GeV, M_Σ = 1.192 GeV, and M_Ξ = 1.318 GeV.
- The radial wave function parameters for the decuplet baryons are α₁ = 0.3366 and α₂ = 0.1630, determined from lattice QCD simulations of electromagnetic form factors for pion masses larger than 350 MeV.
- The octet baryon radial wave function parameters are β₁ = 0.0532, β₂ = 0.809, β₃ = 0.603, and β₄ = 0.381.
- The fine-structure constant α ≃ 1/137 is used in the decay width calculations.
- The valence quark contribution to G_M is proportional to 1/Q⁴ for very large Q², consistent with perturbative QCD estimates.

## Context
This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to the γ*N → Δ(1232) transition in the timelike region (Timelike, Timelike2), to all decuplet-to-octet baryon electromagnetic transitions. The model complements calculations based on nonrelativistic and relativistic quark models (Kaxiras 1985, Darewych 1983, Sahoo 1995, Wagner 1998, Bijker 2000, Keller 2012), Dyson-Schwinger equations (Alepuz 2016, 2018), lattice QCD (Leinweber 1993), QCD sum rules (Aliev 2006, Wang 2009), Skyrme and soliton models (Schat 1995, Haberichter 1997, Kim 2020), chiral perturbation theory, and large N_c limit (Butler 1993, Arndt 2004, Holmberg 2018, Lebed 2011). The predictions enable experimental tests at HADES, where feasibility studies on Σ(1385), Λ(1404), and Λ(1520) Dalitz decays are in progress (Lalik 2019, Rathod 2020, HADES 2020), and may guide future measurements at FAIR.
