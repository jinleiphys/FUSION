# Jet Quenching Parameter via Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET)

> We analyze the transverse momentum broadening in the absence of radiation of an energetic parton propagating through quark-gluon plasma via Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET). We show that the probability for picking up transverse momentum k_\perp is given by the Fourier transform of the expectation value of two transversely separated light-like path-ordered Wilson lines. The subtleties about the ordering of operators do not change the \hat q value for the strongly coupled plasma of N=4 SYM theory.

## Key claim

The probability for an energetic parton to acquire transverse momentum \(k_\perp\) while propagating through quark-gluon plasma is given by the Fourier transform of the expectation value of two transversely separated light-like path-ordered Wilson lines; however, the operator ordering in this expectation value is path ordering rather than time ordering, though this subtlety does not change the \(\hat q\) value for the strongly coupled plasma of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory.

## Method

The authors formulate transverse momentum broadening of a hard parton in the absence of radiation using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), identifying the relevant gluon exchanges as "Glauber gluons" with momenta scaling as \(p \sim Q(\lambda^2, \lambda^2, \lambda)\) where \(\lambda = T/Q \ll 1\). They compute the probability distribution \(P(k_\perp)\) via the optical theorem and the \(S\)-matrix, evaluating the forward scattering amplitude in the limit \(Q \gg k_\perp^2 L \sim \hat q L^2\) where the parton trajectory remains approximately straight. The result is expressed as the Fourier transform of the expectation value of two light-like Wilson lines separated in the transverse plane, which is then used to compute \(\hat q\) for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory via the AdS/CFT correspondence, following the Schwinger-Keldysh contour prescription of Skenderis and Van Rees (2008).

## Key numbers

- The jet quenching parameter for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory at strong coupling: \(\hat q = \frac{\pi^{3/2}\Gamma(\frac{3}{4})}{\Gamma(\frac{5}{4})}\sqrt{\lambda} T^3\), where \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling and \(T\) is the temperature.
- The small dimensionless ratio in the problem: \(\lambda \equiv T/Q \ll 1\), where \(Q\) is the hard parton energy and \(T\) the medium temperature.
- The condition for the straight-line trajectory approximation: \(Q \gg k_\perp^2 L \sim \hat q L^2\).

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based approach to jet quenching initiated by Idilbi and Majumder (2008), who first identified the role of Glauber gluons in this context. The result for \(P(k_\perp)\) as the Fourier transform of Wilson line expectation values had been obtained previously by Casalderrey-Solana and Salgado (2007) and by Liang, Wang and Zhou using different methods. The authors revisit the strong-coupling calculation of \(\hat q\) in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory reported by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006), confirming that the operator-ordering subtlety does not change their result, and thereby solidifying the AdS/CFT determination of \(\hat q\). This formulation enables a systematically improvable, factorized description of jet quenching that can ultimately include radiation effects beyond leading order.
