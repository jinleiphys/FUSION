# Monopole, quadrupole and pairing: a shell model view

> The three main contributions to the nuclear Hamiltonian - monopole, quadrupole and pairing - are analyzed in a shell model context. The first has to be treated phenomenologically, while the other two can be reliably extracted from the realistic interactions. Due to simple scaling properties, the realistic quadrupole and pairing interactions eliminate the tendency to collapse of their conventional counterparts, while retaining their basic simplicity.

# Monopole, quadrupole and pairing: a shell model view

## Key claim

The realistic multipole part of the nuclear Hamiltonian (quadrupole and pairing) can be reliably extracted from realistic interactions and, when expressed in normalized form with universal coupling constants, eliminates the tendency to collapse that plagues conventional pairing-plus-quadrupole models, while the monopole part must be treated phenomenologically and is responsible for saturation and shell structure.

## Method

The author separates the nuclear Hamiltonian into a monopole part (which reproduces average energies of configurations and must be fitted phenomenologically) and a multipole part (which contains all other terms). The multipole Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the E and e representations using the KLS realistic interaction in spaces of one and two major oscillator shells. The dominant eigenvalues in the e representation are identified with normalized pairing (Gamma=01) and quadrupole (gamma=20) operators, whose coupling constants are universal and scale with the oscillator frequency.

## Key numbers

- The normalized pairing coupling constant is |E^01|/hbar omega_0 = g' = 0.32, with hbar omega_0 = 9 MeV.
- The normalized quadrupole coupling constant is |e^20|/hbar omega_0 = kappa' = 0.216.
- For one major shell, the conventional pairing strength G is approximately 19.51/(A^{1/3} A_mf^{2/3}) MeV, where A_mf is approximately (2/3)(p_f + 3/2)^3.
- For one major shell, the conventional quadrupole strength chi'/2 is approximately 216/(A^{1/3} A_mf^{4/3}) MeV.
- The oscillator frequency is given by hbar omega approximately 39/rho, where rho = A^{1/3}(1 - (2T/A)^2) e^{(3.5/A)}.
- The collective monopole Hamiltonian W = sum_p (m_p / sqrt(D_p))^2, with D_p = (p+1)(p+2), cancels the kinetic energy to order A^{1/3}.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Dufour and Zuker 1996, 1999) on separating monopole and multipole Hamiltonians. It addresses the long-standing problem of collapse in the conventional pairing-plus-quadrupole model (Baranger and Kumar 1968, Bes and Sorensen 1969), where adding more oscillator shells causes unbounded energy gain. The normalized operators with universal coupling constants provide a solution that retains the simplicity of the original model while ensuring proper shell effects scaling as A^{1/3}. The work enables large-scale shell model calculations using realistic interactions without readjusting coupling constants for each nucleus.
