# Prompt Neutron Multiplicity Distributions Inferred from $γ$-ray and Fission Fragment Energy Measurements

> We propose a novel method to extract the prompt neutron multiplicity distribution, $P(ν)$, in fission reactions based on correlations between prompt neutrons, $γ$ rays, and fragment kinetic energy arising from energy conservation. In this approach, only event-by-event measurements of the total $γ$-ray energy released as a function of the total kinetic energy (TKE) of the fission fragments are performed, and no neutron detection is required. Using the $\texttt{CGMF}$ fission event generator, we illustrate the method and explore the accuracy of extracting the neutron multiplicity distribution when taking into account the energy resolution and calibration of the energy measurements. We find that a TKE resolution of under 2 MeV produces reasonably accurate results, independent of typical $γ$-ray energy measurement resolution.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the prompt neutron multiplicity distribution \(P(\nu)\) can be extracted from event-by-event measurements of total \(\gamma\)-ray energy versus total kinetic energy (TKE) of fission fragments, without directly detecting neutrons, provided the TKE resolution is better than 2 MeV; the method's accuracy is largely independent of typical \(\gamma\)-ray energy resolution.

## Method

The study uses the \(\texttt{CGMF}\) Monte Carlo Hauser-Feshbach fission event generator to produce two-dimensional densities \(\rho(E_\gamma^\mathrm{tot},\mathrm{TKE}|\nu_i)\) for each fixed total neutron multiplicity \(\nu_i\). A gradient-descent \(\chi^2\) minimization then fits the superposition of these \(\nu\)-specific distributions to the full calculated \(\rho(E_\gamma^\mathrm{tot},\mathrm{TKE})\) to extract the weights \(P(\nu)\). The method is tested for \(^{252}\mathrm{Cf}\) spontaneous fission and for neutron-induced fission of \(^{235}\mathrm{U}\) and \(^{239}\mathrm{Pu}\) at thermal and 4.0 MeV incident energies, including the effects of Gaussian smearing of TKE and systematic shifts.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{252}\mathrm{Cf}\)(sf) with no energy resolution, the relative errors on the first three factorial moments are \(\varepsilon_1 = 4\times10^{-6}\), \(\varepsilon_2 = 3\times10^{-7}\), \(\varepsilon_3 = 4\times10^{-5}\).
- With a TKE resolution of \(\delta_\mathrm{TKE}=2\) MeV, the extracted \(\overline{\nu}\) is determined within 1.5% of the nominal \(\texttt{CGMF}\) value for \(^{252}\mathrm{Cf}\)(sf).
- For \(\delta_\mathrm{TKE}=2\) MeV, the first three factorial moments stay within 10% of the nominal \(\texttt{CGMF}\) values for \(^{252}\mathrm{Cf}\)(sf) and for \(^{239}\mathrm{Pu}+n\) at thermal and 4.0 MeV; for \(^{235}\mathrm{U}+n\) at thermal, the moments also remain within 10% at this resolution.
- For \(\delta_\mathrm{TKE}=2\) MeV, the extracted \(P(\nu)\) for \(^{252}\mathrm{Cf}\)(sf) is within \(\sim 20\%\) of the true distribution.
- When the \(\gamma\)-ray energy resolution \(\delta_{\mathrm{E}_\gamma^\mathrm{tot}}\) is below 0.5 MeV, there is no difference in the extracted \(P(\nu)\) compared to the direct \(\texttt{CGMF}\) distribution.
- Varying model inputs (Brosa parameterization of \(Y(A,\mathrm{TKE})\), spin parameter \(\alpha\) from 1.7 to 1.5, excitation-energy sharing ratio \(R_T\) to 1.1 and 1.2) changes \(\overline{\nu}\) by no more than 3% and the peak of \(P(\nu)\) by less than 25%.

## Context

This work extends the use of energy-conservation correlations between prompt \(\gamma\) rays and fission fragment kinetic energy, previously exploited in measurements by Pleasonton (1972) and by Rusev (2017) with the DANCE detector, to infer the neutron multiplicity distribution without neutron detection. The method addresses the scarcity of direct neutron multiplicity measurements above thermal energies, where only one measurement exists for \(^{235}\mathrm{U}\), \(^{238}\mathrm{U}\), and \(^{239}\mathrm{Pu}\) (Zucker, 1986). It enables access to correlated fission observables from a single experimental setup and provides a test of fission models such as \(\texttt{CGMF}\) and the Brosa parameterization (Brosa, 1990).
