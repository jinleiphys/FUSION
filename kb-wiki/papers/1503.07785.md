# Reconciling Nuclear and Astrophysical Constraints

> In view of new constraints put forth by recent observations and measurements in the realm of astrophysics and nuclear physics, we update the non-linear realization of the sigma model as to reflect such constraints. By doing this, we obtain new equations of state that may be used to describe neutron stars. Such equations of state are obtained by investigating different ways by which the vector mesons self-interact. Furthermore, we also investigate the role played by the delta mesons in the model. As a result, we are able to develop equations of state that are in better agreement with data, such as nuclear compressibility and slope of the symmetry energy at saturation, star masses, radii, and cooling profiles.

## Key claim

By tuning the vector-meson self-interaction terms and the scalar-isovector delta meson coupling within a chiral SU(3) sigma model, the authors identify parameter sets that simultaneously satisfy recent nuclear physics constraints (narrow ranges for symmetry energy and its slope at saturation) and astrophysical constraints (neutron star masses near 2.0 solar masses with radii around 12 km), but only for specific coupling schemes that include a strong omega-rho mixing term.

## Method

The authors update the hadronic chiral mean-field (CMF) model by testing four chirally invariant coupling schemes (C1-C4) for vector-meson self-interactions, varying the coupling constants g4, g_omega, and g_rho while keeping the scalar sector fixed. They also vary the coupling strength g_delta of the scalar-isovector delta meson from 0 to 15, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star masses and radii, and perform thermal evolution simulations including direct Urca process thresholds and nucleon pairing.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density constrained to 29.0 MeV < E_sym < 32.7 MeV and its slope L to 40.5 MeV < L < 61.9 MeV, taken from Lattimer (2012) as guideline
- Best-fit parameter set C1 yields: compressibility K = 271.41 MeV, E_sym = 29.00 MeV, L = 54.00 MeV, maximum star mass M = 2.05 M_sun, radius R = 12.56 km
- Best-fit parameter set C1 with E_sym = 32.70 MeV yields: L = 59.05 MeV, M = 2.04 M_sun, R = 12.49 km
- Best-fit parameter set C4 yields: K = 292.91 MeV, M = 2.14-2.15 M_sun, R = 12.18-12.26 km
- Bare nucleon mass M0 = 0 MeV for schemes C1-C3, M0 = 150 MeV for scheme C4
- Delta meson coupling strength ratio (g_delta/m_delta)^2/(g_sigma/m_sigma)^2 ~ 0.3 at g_delta = 11.50

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) sigma model framework of Dexheimer (2008) and Schurhoff (2010) by incorporating recent constraints from massive neutron star measurements (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) and improved radius estimates (Psaltis 2013, Guillot 2013, Lattimer 2013, Heinke 2014). The approach follows the idea of Steiner (2012), Dutra (2014), and Bizarro (2015) to study different vector-meson self-interactions, and investigates the delta meson role previously studied by Kubis (1997), Liu (2001), and Menezes (2004). The results enable systematic comparison of equations of state that simultaneously satisfy nuclear saturation properties, neutron star mass-radius constraints, and cooling observations such as Cassiopeia A.
