# Shell structure of weakly-bound and resonant neutrons

> The systematic change of shell structure in both weakly bound and resonant neutron one-particle levels in nuclei towards the neutron drip line is exhibited, solving the coupled equations derived from the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space with the correct asymptotic behaviour of wave functions for $r \rightarrow \infty$. The change comes from the behaviour unique in the one-particle motion with low orbital angular momenta compared with that with high orbital angular momenta. The observed deformation of very neutron-rich nuclei with $N \simgeq 20$ in the island of inversion is a natural result of this changed shell structure, while a possible deformation of neutron-drip-line nuclei with $N \approx 51$, which are not yet observed, is suggested.

## Key claim
The systematic change of shell structure in weakly-bound and resonant neutron one-particle levels towards the neutron drip line is driven by the unique behavior of low orbital angular momentum (low-ℓ) components, which are pushed down relative to high-ℓ levels as binding energy decreases; this change provides a natural explanation for observed deformation in the island of inversion (N ≳ 20) and suggests possible deformation in neutron-drip-line nuclei with N ≈ 51 that have not yet been observed.

## Method
The author solved coupled equations derived from the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space with correct asymptotic wave function behavior for r → ∞, using axially symmetric quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potentials. For bound levels, standard eigenvalue problems were solved; for positive-energy levels, all eigenphases for a given Ω^π were calculated using the eigenphase formalism, and one-particle resonant levels were identified when an eigenphase increased through π/2 as energy increased. The Woods-Saxon parameters were diffuseness a = 0.67 fm, radius R = r₀ A^{1/3} with r₀ = 1.27 fm, and depth depending on neutron excess.

## Key numbers
- For the stable nucleus ²⁵Mg (S_n = 7.3 MeV) at β = 0: calculated one-particle resonant energies are ε_res(p₃/₂) = +0.31 MeV and ε_res(f₇/₂) = +0.32 MeV, showing the N = 28 energy gap disappears in low-energy resonance spectra.
- For the neutron-drip-line nucleus ³¹Ne (S_n = 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV) at β = 0: the f₇/₂ one-neutron resonance is found at 2.04 MeV, while no p₃/₂ or p₁/₂ resonance defined by eigenphase formalism is obtained; the f₅/₂ level is at 9.50 MeV.
- For neutron-drip-line nuclei with N ≈ 51 (e.g., ⁷⁵Cr) at β = 0: the very weakly bound 3s₁/₂ level is almost degenerate with (slightly lower than) the 2d₅/₂ level; resonant levels are ε_res(1g₇/₂) = +3.44 MeV and ε_res(1h₁₁/₂) = +5.48 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies using the eigenphase formalism (Hamamoto 2005, 2006) to systematically show how shell structure changes for weakly-bound and resonant neutrons, contrasting with the traditional Nilsson diagram based on modified oscillator potentials used by Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The results explain the observed deformation of very neutron-rich nuclei with N ≳ 20 in the island of inversion, as seen in ³¹Ne and ³³Mg, and suggest that N ≈ 51 nuclei such as ⁷⁵Cr may also be deformed if they lie inside the neutron drip line. The change in shell structure is fundamentally different from that produced by tensor forces between protons and neutrons using harmonic-oscillator wave functions, as discussed elsewhere at the meeting.
