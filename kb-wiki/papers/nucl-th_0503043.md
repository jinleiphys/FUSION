# Electromagnetic properties of ground and excited state pseudoscalar mesons

> The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity places constraints on particular properties of every pseudoscalar meson. For example, in the chiral limit all pseudoscalar mesons, except the Goldstone mode, decouple from the axial-vector current. Nevertheless, all neutral pseudoscalar mesons couple to two photons. The strength of the π_n^0 γγcoupling, where n=0 denotes the Goldstone mode, is affected by the Abelian anomaly's continuum contribution. The effect is material for n \neq 0. The γ* π_n γ* transition form factor, T_{π_n}(Q^2), is nonzero for all n, and T_{π_n}(Q^2) \approx (4π^2/3) (f_{π_n}/Q^2) at large Q^2. For all pseudoscalars but the Goldstone mode, this leading contribution vanishes in the chiral limit. In this instance the ultraviolet power-law behaviour is 1/Q^4 for n \neq 0, and we find numerically T_{π_1}(Q^2) \simeq (4π^2/3) (-<\bar q q>/Q^4). This subleading power-law behaviour is always present. In general its coefficient is not simply related to f_{π_n}. The properties of n \neq 0 pseudoscalar mesons are sensitive to the pointwise behaviour of the long-range piece of the interaction between light-quarks.

## Key claim

The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity forces the leptonic decay constant of every pseudoscalar meson except the Goldstone mode to vanish in the chiral limit, which means that for excited pseudoscalar mesons (n ≥ 1) the leading 1/Q² contribution to the γ*π_nγ* transition form factor vanishes in the chiral limit, leaving a subleading 1/Q⁴ behaviour whose coefficient is not simply related to f_{π_n}.

## Method

The authors employ QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in a rainbow-ladder truncation, which is the first term in a systematic, Poincaré covariant and symmetry preserving truncation scheme. They solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar mesons to obtain masses and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and compute the two-photon coupling via renormalised triangle diagrams. The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is used to derive exact constraints on the coupling of pseudoscalar mesons to two photons, and the asymptotic behaviour of the transition form factor is analysed at large momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The π(1300) has a width of 200 to 600 MeV; the π(1800) has a width of 207 ± 13 MeV
- For the ground state pion, the continuum contribution to the anomaly modifies the result g_{π₀⁰γγ} = 1/(2 f_{π₀}) by less than 2%
- At large Q², the transition form factor behaves as T_{π_n}(Q²) ≈ (4π²/3)(f_{π_n}/Q²) for all n, but for n ≠ 0 this leading term vanishes in the chiral limit
- For n = 1 in the chiral limit, the subleading behaviour is T_{π₁}(Q²) ≃ (4π²/3)(-⟨q̄q⟩/Q⁴)
- The Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation (f_{π₀}⁰)² m_{π₀}² = -2 m(ζ) ⟨q̄q⟩_ζ⁰ is a corollary of the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of pseudoscalar mesons (Krassnigg 2003, Maris and Roberts 1997, Maris, Roberts, and Tandy 1998) to excited states and their electromagnetic properties, providing a unified theoretical framework for the entire pseudoscalar trajectory including the π(140), π(1300), and π(1800). The results contradict constituent-quark model interpretations that treat these states simply as radial excitations on a Q̄Q n¹S₀ trajectory (Anisovich, Norbury), and instead show that excited pseudoscalar meson properties are sensitive to the long-range part of the light-quark interaction. The analysis enables future distinction between radial excitations and hybrid mesons through the pointwise behaviour of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes.
