# Comment on "Measurement of the $Q^2$ Dependence of the Deuteron Spin Structure Function $g_1$ and its Moments at Low $Q^2$ with CLAS"

> We argue that the recently published CLAS results on the deuteron spin polarizability $γ_0$ [Adhikari {\it et al.}, Phys.\ Rev.\ Lett.\ {\bf 120}, 062501 (2018)], as well as their comparisons with chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT), are misleading. In reality, the deuteron polarizability is larger by 4 orders of magnitude, as demonstrated here by a novel calculation in pionless EFT. The CLAS paper, on the other hand, presents only a tiny correction to it, based on a partial evaluation of a sum rule for $γ_0$. The sum rule is assumed to have the same form as for the nucleon; we argue it does not. Moreover, their "test of $χ$PT" tacitly involves assumptions which, as we demonstrate, may not be valid at the claimed accuracy.

## Key claim

The CLAS results on the deuteron spin polarizability γ₀ (Adhikari et al., 2018) are misleading because they present only a tiny correction to the actual deuteron polarizability, which is larger by 4 orders of magnitude; the CLAS paper's comparison with chiral perturbation theory (χPT) involves assumptions that may not be valid at the claimed accuracy, and the sum rule used for γ₀ is incorrectly assumed to have the same form as for the nucleon.

## Method

The authors performed a leading-order calculation of the deuteron polarizability γ₀ in pionless effective field theory (π̸EFT) extended to finite Q², showing results that are 4 orders of magnitude larger than the CLAS values. They also evaluated the breakup channel contribution at Q²=0 using the helicity-difference cross sections of Arenhövel et al. (2004), and derived the correct sum rule for a spin-1 target, which includes a term involving the deuteron anomalous magnetic moment and anomalous quadrupole moment that is absent for spin-1/2 targets.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron polarizability γ₀ from the CLAS paper is presented in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, which is 4 orders of magnitude smaller than the authors' pionless EFT estimate.
- At Q²=0, the breakup channel contribution to γ₀ is roughly -0.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, based on the helicity-difference cross sections of Arenhövel et al. (2004).
- The deuteron electromagnetic moment term in the correct sum rule, using empirical values κ_d ≃ -0.143, ς_d ≃ 13.5, and M_d ≃ 1.8756 GeV, amounts to -0.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴.

## Context

This comment directly challenges the CLAS results of Adhikari et al. (2018) on the deuteron spin polarizability γ₀, arguing that the published values represent only a small contribution rather than the full polarizability. The work extends the pionless EFT calculation of Ji (2003) to finite Q² and corrects the sum rule formulation, which differs from the nucleon case due to the deuteron's spin-1 nature. The authors also critique the χPT comparisons in the CLAS paper, noting that the single-nucleon calculations of Bernard (2012) and Lensky (2014) used there neglect the breakup channel and other deuteron-specific effects. This enables a proper theoretical framework for future experimental extractions of the deuteron polarizability.
