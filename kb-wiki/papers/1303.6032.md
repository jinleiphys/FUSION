# Toward Construction of the Unified Lepton-Nucleus Interaction Model from a Few Hundred MeV to GeV Region

> Next generation neutrino oscillation experiments will need a quantitative understanding of neutrino-nucleus interaction far better than ever. Kinematics covered by the relevant neutrino-nucleus interaction spans wide region, from the quasi-elastic, through the resonance region, to the deeply inelastic scattering region. The neutrino-nucleus interaction in each region has quite different characteristics. Obviously, it is essential to combine different expertise to construct a unified model that covers all the kinematical region of the neutrino-nucleus interaction. Recently, several experimentalists and theorists got together to form a collaboration to tackle this problem. In this contribution, we report the collaboration's recent activity and a goal in near future.

## Key claim

The collaboration aims to construct a unified lepton-nucleus interaction model covering the kinematic region from a few hundred MeV to GeV, but the paper reports only the current status of baseline models for quasi-elastic, resonance, and deeply inelastic scattering regions, with the actual unified model still under development.

## Method

The collaboration develops separate baseline models for three kinematic regions: quasi-elastic (QE) scattering using nuclear spectral functions, resonance (RES) region using a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model that solves a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation with meson-exchange potentials and bare N* excitation mechanisms, and deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) region. For the forward neutrino-induced meson productions, the Partially Conserved Axial Current (PCAC) hypothesis is applied to relate matrix elements with those of pion-induced meson production using the DCC model for πN → X reactions.

## Key numbers

- The DCC model analysis includes fitting about 20,000 data points for π(γ)N → πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ reactions simultaneously up to W = 2.1 GeV (total energy)
- The branching fraction for γ emission following CC and NC QE processes is significant at ∼ 40%
- The structure function F₂(Q²=0) is shown as a function of total energy W for neutrino-induced meson productions, with πN production dominant up to W = 1.5 GeV, above which ππN production becomes comparable

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels approach previously applied to meson production reactions (Nakamura et al., various years) to neutrino-induced reactions, and addresses the need identified at the NuInt12 Workshop for spectral function approaches rather than Fermi-Gas models for QE calculations. The collaboration, formed at the J-PARC branch of KEK Theory Center, aims to enable next-generation neutrino oscillation experiments (such as T2K and atmospheric neutrino experiments) to achieve quantitative understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions at the level of 5% or better, particularly for CP-violation and mass hierarchy measurements.
