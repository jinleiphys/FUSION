# Gravitational $p \to Δ^+ $ transition form factors in chiral perturbation theory

> The gravitational form factors of the transition from the proton to the $Δ^+$ resonance are calculated to leading one-loop order using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of chiral perturbation theory. We take into account the leading electromagnetic and strong isospin-violating effects. The loop contributions to the transition form factors are found to be free of power-counting violating pieces, which is consistent with the absence of tree-level diagrams at the considered order. In this sense, our results can be regarded as predictions of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The gravitational form factors for the proton-to-Δ⁺ transition are calculated to leading one-loop order in chiral perturbation theory, and the results are free of power-counting-violating pieces, making them predictions of the theory; however, the calculation relies on empirical input for the mass splittings between Δ resonance states, which is currently poorly known.

## Method

The authors use a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of chiral perturbation theory with pions, nucleons, photons, and Δ resonances coupled to an external gravitational field, employing the small-scale expansion (ϵ-counting) up to third chiral order. They compute 25 one-loop diagrams for strong isospin-breaking effects and additional diagrams for electromagnetic radiative corrections, applying the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to handle ultraviolet divergences. The matrix element of the energy-momentum tensor for the p → Δ⁺ transition is parameterized in terms of five gravitational transition form factors, and the leading isospin-violating effects from both strong interactions (mass differences within iso-multiplets) and electromagnetic interactions are included.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass difference used: M_π⁺ = 0.140 GeV, M_π⁰ = 0.135 GeV.
- The proton and neutron masses used: m_p = 0.938 GeV, m_n = 0.940 GeV.
- The Δ⁺ mass used: m_Δ = 1.232 GeV; the Δ⁰ mass used: 1.233 GeV; the Δ⁺⁺ mass used: 1.231 GeV.
- The axial coupling constant g_A = 1.289, the pion-nucleon-Δ coupling g = 1.35, and the pion decay constant F = 0.092 GeV.
- The electromagnetic coupling e = 0.303 and the low-energy constant d₃⁽²⁾ = 2.72 GeV⁻¹.
- The diagrams with radiative corrections give smaller contributions than those with pion loops, consistent with power-counting estimates.

## Context

This work extends the study of gravitational form factors from stable nucleons and Δ resonances (Alharazin 2020, Alharazin 2022) to the transition between them, building on the parametrization of the p → Δ⁺ transition matrix element introduced by Kim (2022). The results enable future connections to transition generalized parton distributions and experimental access via deeply virtual Compton scattering or hard exclusive meson production, complementing earlier calculations using QCD light-cone sum rules (Ozdem 2022) and the large-N_c limit of QCD (Kim 2023).
