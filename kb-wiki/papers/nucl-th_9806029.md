# Effective Field Theories For Nuclei And Dense Matter

> A recent development on the working of effective field theories in nuclei and in dense hadronic matter is discussed. We consider two extreme regimes: One, dilute regime for which fluctuations are made on top of the matter-free vacuum; two, dense systems for which fluctuations are treated on top of the "vacuum" defined at a given density, with masses and coupling constants varying as function of matter density ("Brown-Rho scaling"). Based on an intricate -- as yet mostly conjectural -- connection between the in-medium structure of chiral Lagrangian field theory which is a beautiful effective theory of QCD and that of Landau Fermi liquid theory which is an equally beautiful and highly successful effective theory of many-body systems, it is suggested that a chiral Lagrangian with Brown-Rho scaling in the mean field is equivalent to Fermi-liquid fixed point theory. I make this connection using electroweak and strong responses of nuclear matter up to nuclear matter density and then extrapolating to higher densities encountered in heavy-ion collisions and compact stars.

# Effective Field Theories For Nuclei And Dense Matter

## Key claim
The paper suggests that a chiral Lagrangian with Brown-Rho scaling in the mean field is equivalent to Fermi-liquid fixed point theory, but this connection is described as "intricate -- as yet mostly conjectural." The claim is that what is usually attributed to a change in the QCD vacuum may instead be understood in terms of many-body interactions on top of the matter-free vacuum, at least up to a certain density below the chiral phase transition.

## Method
The author develops effective field theories for two regimes: dilute systems where fluctuations are made on top of the matter-free vacuum, and dense systems where fluctuations are treated on top of a density-dependent vacuum with masses and coupling constants varying as a function of matter density (Brown-Rho scaling). For the dilute regime, a chiral Lagrangian is truncated to next-to-leading order (NLO) and applied to two-nucleon systems by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a four-point vertex kernel. For dense matter, the connection between chiral Lagrangian field theory and Landau Fermi liquid theory is made by analyzing electroweak and strong responses of nuclear matter, using the Landau-Migdal formula for the convection current and comparing it with predictions from chiral and scale invariance of QCD.

## Key numbers
- For np scattering in the $^1S_0$ channel at a fixed center-of-mass momentum of $p = 68.5$ MeV, the NLO calculation shows cutoff independence for $\Lambda \gsim 150$ MeV, with the cutoff chosen at $\Lambda_{Z=1} \simeq 172$ MeV.
- The chiral perturbation theory cross-section for radiative np capture ($n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma$) is $\sigma_{ChPT} = 334 \pm 3$ mb, in agreement with the experimental value $\sigma_{exp} = 334.2 \pm 0.5$ mb.
- The $S(0)$ factor for the solar proton fusion process ($p+p\rightarrow d+e^+ +\nu_e$) from chiral perturbation theory to NNLO is $S(0)_{ChPT} = 4.05 (1\pm 0.012) \times 10^{-25}$ MeV-b, consistent with the helioseismology constraint $3.25 \lsim S(0)/(10^{-25}{\rm MeV-b}) \lsim 4.59$.
- The Brown-Rho scaling parameter at nuclear matter density is $\Phi(\rho_0) \approx 0.78$, extracted from the orbital gyromagnetic ratio $\delta g_l^p = 0.23 \pm 0.03$ for the proton.
- The Landau mass of the nucleon at nuclear matter density from this scaling is $m_N^\star (\rho_0)/m_N \simeq 0.70$, agreeing with the QCD sum-rule result $0.69^{+0.14}_{-0.07}$.
- The BR scaling function is given by $\Phi (\rho) \approx (1+0.28\rho/\rho_0)^{-1}$.
- The QCD sum-rule calculation of the $\rho$ meson in medium gives $m_\rho^\star (\rho_0)/m_\rho = 0.78 \pm 0.08$.
- The BR scaling Lagrangian at tree order predicts a $K^{-}$-nuclear attractive potential of $\sim 190$ MeV at nuclear matter density, consistent with the experimental analysis giving $185 \pm 15$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the application of effective field theories from particle physics to nuclear many-body systems, building on chiral perturbation theory approaches by Weinberg, van Kolck, and others, and on Landau Fermi liquid theory as applied to condensed matter systems by Polchinski. The paper connects Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) to Fermi-liquid fixed point theory, and applies the framework to heavy-ion dilepton data from the CERES collaboration and to kaon condensation in compact stars. The results enable a systematic mean-field description of dense matter up to and beyond nuclear matter density, with the open question of how to formulate higher-order corrections beyond the mean-field approximation and how to describe the crossover from hadronic Fermi liquid to quark Fermi liquid at higher densities.
