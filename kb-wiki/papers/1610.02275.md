# Prompt photon production and photon-hadron jet correlations with POWHEG

> We present a calculation of direct photon production at next-to-leading order of QCD and a matching of this calculation with parton showers using POWHEG BOX. Based on simulations with POWHEG+PYTHIA, we perform a detailed phenomenological analysis of PHENIX data on prompt photon production and photon-hadron jet correlations in pp collisions at RHIC, considerably improving the description of these data with respect to previous calculations, and we suggest additional interesting analyses.

## Key claim

The authors present a next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD calculation of direct photon production matched to parton showers using POWHEG BOX, claiming that this approach considerably improves the description of PHENIX data on prompt photon production and photon-hadron jet correlations in pp collisions at RHIC compared to previous calculations, though the improvement is conditional on the inclusion of enhanced QED radiation and the modeling of photon fragmentation through an interleaved QCD+QED parton shower.

## Method

The authors computed the spin- and colour-averaged cross sections for direct photon production at LO (processes qqbar → γg and qg → γq) analytically using FormCalc 8.4 and checked against MadGraph 5 and the literature. Virtual one-loop corrections were computed with FORM in D=4-2ε dimensions, reduced using the Passarino-Veltman procedure, and renormalized in the MSbar scheme, with infrared divergences canceled against integrated Catani-Seymour dipoles computed with AutoDipole 1.2.3. The NLO calculation was matched to parton showers using POWHEG BOX version 2, with modifications to handle photon radiation off quarks and an artificial enhancement of QED radiation by a constant factor c > 1 in the Sudakov form factor, compensated by reweighting events.

## Key numbers

- PHENIX pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV were used as the baseline for phenomenological analysis
- A Born suppression factor with kT_supp = 100 GeV and exponent i = 3 was used by default, with checks using kT_supp = 10 GeV and i = 4 showing independence at sufficiently large pT
- Only about 2% of events in a pp run at √s = 200 GeV contain a photon, with the remainder being QCD Born configurations
- Effective temperatures of T = 304 ± 58 MeV and T = 309 ± 64 MeV were previously extracted from ALICE data at pT ∈ [0.8;2.2] GeV and pT ∈ [1.5;3.5] GeV, respectively, with a power-law (pT^-4) behaviour for pT > 4 GeV
- A smaller effective temperature of T = 221 ± 27 MeV had previously been measured in lower-energy Au-Au collisions at RHIC

## Context

This work extends previous NLO calculations of prompt photon production using JETPHOX (Aurenche 2006) and the POWHEG approach previously applied to di-photon production as a Higgs boson background (D'Errico 2011) and as an electroweak correction to single-W production (Barze 2012, 2014). The method enables more realistic kinematic distributions for photon-hadron and photon-jet correlations, particularly in the regions of vanishing photon-hadron transverse momentum pT^γh → 0 and back-to-back azimuthal angle Δφ → π, where LO and NLO calculations produce unrealistic δ-functions or divergences. This provides an improved baseline for heavy-ion collision analyses at RHIC and the LHC, where thermal photon spectra and jet quenching effects are studied.
