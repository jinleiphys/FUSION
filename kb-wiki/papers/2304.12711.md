# Random model of flow decorrelation

> The collective flow generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions fluctuates from event to event. The fluctuations lead to a decorrelation of flow vectors measured in separate bins in phase space. These effects have been measured in experiments and observed in numerical simulations in hydrodynamic models. We present a simple random model of flow decorrelation in pseudorapidity. Analytical expressions for the flow factorization breaking coefficients for flow vectors, flow vector magnitudes, and flow angles are derived. The model explains the relations between different factorization breaking coefficients found in experimental data and model simulations. In particular, it is found that the flow angle decorrelation constitutes about one half of the total flow vector decorrelation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple random model where the flow vector in a small pseudorapidity bin is written as a sum of the overall flow (averaged over the whole event) and a random vector component, and shows that this model can explain qualitatively the relations between different factorization breaking coefficients observed in experimental data and hydrodynamic model simulations, with the specific finding that flow angle decorrelation constitutes about one half of the total flow vector decorrelation.

## Method

The authors decompose the flow in a small pseudorapidity bin as V_i = V + K_i, where V is the average flow over the whole acceptance region and K_i is a random component, assuming independence of the directions of the random component and the average flow. They derive analytical expressions for the flow factorization breaking coefficients for flow vectors, flow vector magnitudes, and flow angles by expanding to second order in the small random component delta. The analytical results are compared with numerical simulations from a 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

* The approximation that the random component is independent of the average flow works well for triangular flow and elliptic flow in central collisions, with |langle V K*rangle| less than 0.05 times langle K K*rangle.
* For elliptic flow in semicentral collisions, the approximation is still acceptable with |langle V K*rangle| approximately 0.13 times langle K K*rangle.
* The flow angle decorrelation constitutes about one half of the total flow vector decorrelation, with the relation [1 - rho_psi^(m)] approximately equal to [1 - rho_v^(m)] approximately equal to [1 - rho_V^(m)]/2.
* The factorization breaking coefficient for the mth power of the flow vector can be approximated as [rho_V^(1)]^m for fluctuation-dominated flow (triangular flow and elliptic flow in central collisions).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of flow decorrelation in pseudorapidity observed in hydrodynamic models (Bozek 2010, Gardim 2012) and measured in experiments (CMS 2015, ATLAS 2017, ALICE 2022). The random model provides a simple analytical understanding of relations between different factorization breaking coefficients that were previously only observed in numerical simulations, including the finding that flow angle decorrelation equals flow magnitude decorrelation (Bozek 2018). The model enables experimental estimates of flow angle decorrelation for odd m and explains why decorrelation is strongest in events with the smallest overall flow.
