# Transverse Energy-Energy Correlators in the Color-Glass Condensate at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We investigate the transverse energy-energy correlators (TEEC) in the small-$x$ regime at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Focusing on the back-to-back production of electron-hadron pairs in both $ep$ and $eA$ collisions, we establish a factorization theorem given in terms of the hard function, quark distributions, soft functions, and TEEC jet functions, where the gluon saturation effect is incorporated. Numerical results for TEEC in both $ep$ and $eA$ collisions are presented, together with the nuclear modification factor $R_A$. Our analysis reveals that TEEC observables in deep inelastic scattering provide a valuable approach for probing gluon saturation phenomena. Our findings underscore the significance of measuring TEEC at the EIC, emphasizing its efficacy in advancing our understanding of gluon saturation and nuclear modifications in high-energy collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transverse energy-energy correlators (TEEC) in deep inelastic scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider can serve as a valuable probe of gluon saturation, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of kinematic region (back-to-back lepton-hadron production at small azimuthal angle difference) and on the particular saturation models (GBW and rcBK) used for the numerical predictions.

## Method

The authors derive a transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization theorem for TEEC in the back-to-back limit, expressing the cross section in terms of hard functions, quark distributions, soft functions, and TEEC jet functions. They incorporate gluon saturation by expanding the TMD quark distribution at small Bjorken-x in terms of the dipole gluon distribution using the Color-Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, employing both the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) model and the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation. Numerical predictions are then computed for both ep and eA collisions at the EIC, including the nuclear modification factor R_A for gold nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy for electron-proton collisions: sqrt(s) = 140 GeV
- Lepton rapidity chosen: y_e = -2
- Lepton transverse momenta considered: p_T^e = 2, 4, 6 GeV, corresponding to probed x values between 2 x 10^{-3} and 8.5 x 10^{-3}
- GBW model parameters: lambda = 0.29, x_0 = 3 x 10^{-4}, S_perp = 1/2 x 23 mb for proton targets
- Nuclear modification factor R_A shows modifications on the order of 15% to 20% in the small tau region for both rcBK and GBW models
- Gold nucleus with atomic mass A = 197 used for nuclear targets
- Non-perturbative Sudakov parameters for TEEC jet function: g_0^e = 0.226 GeV^{1/2}, g_1^e = 0.463 GeV, g_2^e = 0.033 GeV^2
- Parameter c for nuclear saturation scale chosen in range 0.5 < c < 1.0

## Context

This work extends the TEEC formalism developed by Li et al. (2020) for deep inelastic scattering to incorporate gluon saturation effects in the small-x regime, building on the Color-Glass Condensate framework of McLerran and Venugopalan (1993-1994) and the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation. The paper enables future experimental measurements at the EIC to discriminate between different saturation models (GBW versus rcBK), as the numerical results for TEEC observables can differ by a factor of two between these models, particularly at low transverse momentum.
