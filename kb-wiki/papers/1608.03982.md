# Search for doubly-heavy dibaryons in a quark model

> We study the stability of hexaquark systems containing two heavy quarks and four light quarks within a simple quark model. No bound or metastable state is found. The reason stems on a delicate interplay between chromoelectric and chromomagnetic effects. Our calculation provides also information about anticharmed pentaquarks that are seemingly unbound in simple quark models.

## Key claim

No bound or metastable doubly-heavy dibaryon state of the form $(qqqqQQ')$ is found in a simple quark model, despite the possibility that chromoelectric and chromomagnetic effects might cooperate to produce binding. The negative result stems from a conflict between these two effects: chromoelectric dynamics favors the heavy-heavy pair in a color $\bar 3$ state and maximizes strength on heavy-light pairs, while chromomagnetic effects are optimized when the light sector receives the largest color strengths.

## Method

The authors solved the six-body Schrödinger equation using a Gaussian expansion method with Jacobi coordinates, constructing spin-color basis states from three diquark subsystems $(qq)(qq)(QQ')$ with color $\bar 3$ or $6$ and spin 0 or 1, enforcing full antisymmetrization. They used a Hamiltonian with a pairwise potential containing a Coulomb-plus-linear confining term (chromoelectric) and a smeared Gaussian spin-spin interaction (chromomagnetic), with parameters fitted to reproduce baryon masses. The variational calculation was performed both for single spin-color channels and for coupled channels, and was cross-checked using alternative bases that explicitly describe two separated baryons with a relative Gaussian wave function.

## Key numbers

- Baryon masses used for thresholds (in GeV): $qqQ(\Sigma)=1.372$, $qqQ'(\Lambda)=1.258$, $qqq(\Sigma)=1.461$, $QQ'q(\Sigma)=1.109$ (Table I).
- Lowest dissociation threshold for the $J^P=0^+$ six-quark state: $QQ'q(\Sigma)+qqq(\Sigma)=2.570$ GeV; upper threshold: $qqQ(\Sigma)+qqQ'(\Lambda)=2.630$ GeV (Table II).
- Coupled-channel six-quark energy for $J^P=0^+$: 2.767 GeV, above both thresholds (Table II).
- In the infinite heavy-quark mass limit ($M=10$ GeV, $m=0.4$ GeV): six-quark energy 2.326 GeV, between the thresholds $QQ'q+qqq=2.162$ GeV and $qqQ+qqQ'=2.477$ GeV, still above the lowest threshold.

## Context

This work extends the search for doubly-heavy multiquarks beyond the well-studied $(\bar q \bar q QQ)$ tetraquark system, where Janc and Rosina (2004) and Vijande et al. (2007) found stability for $(\bar u \bar d cc)$. The authors show that the $(qqqqQQ')$ configuration, unlike the tetraquark, cannot coherently combine chromoelectric and chromomagnetic attraction, and they explicitly contrast their result with molecular approaches (Lee 2011, Meguro 2011, Li and Zhu 2012, Oka 2013, Huang 2014, Carames 2015) that have suggested possible bound states via tensor couplings or channel coupling. The calculation also implies that anticharmed pentaquarks $(qqqq \bar Q)$ are unbound in simple quark models, contradicting earlier chromomagnetic counting arguments by Gignoux et al. (1987) and Lipkin (1987).
