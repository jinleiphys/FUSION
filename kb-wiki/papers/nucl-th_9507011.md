# Large-basis shell model studies of light nuclei with a multi-valued G-matrix effective interaction

> Large-basis shell model studies of low-lying excitations in light nuclei from 4He to 7Li have been performed with a multi-valued G-matrix effective interaction, as recently suggested by Haxton et al.. Calculations were performed relative to the vacuum (``no core") using very large, separable model spaces containing all excitations with unperturbed energies up to 8\hbarΩ. Using G matrices derived from a new Nijmegen potential, we achieve a very satisfactory description of these excitations.

# Large-basis shell model studies of light nuclei with a multi-valued G-matrix effective interaction

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a multi-valued G-matrix effective interaction, which includes a dependence on the total oscillator quanta of spectator nucleons (N^spectators_sum), produces significant shifts in calculated excitation energies for light nuclei from 4He to 7Li, lowering states dominated by 1ħΩ and 2ħΩ configurations relative to 0ħΩ states and improving agreement with experiment. The authors caution that neglecting this spectator dependence in present-day multi-shell calculations can lead to unattractive consequences, including the apparent need for unrealistic single-particle energies.

## Method

The authors performed large-basis, no-core shell model calculations using full multi-ħΩ model spaces containing all excitations with unperturbed energies up to 8ħΩ, with the Hamiltonian expressed relative to the vacuum. They evaluated the Brueckner G-matrix with a spectator-dependent Pauli operator Q(N^spectators_sum), using the non-relativistic version of the new Nijmegen potential (NijmII) with a harmonic oscillator basis parameter ħΩ fixed at 14 MeV, and included a phenomenological starting-energy shift Δ to account for omitted higher-order contributions. The calculations were carried out using the Many-Fermion-Dynamics Shell-Model code for 4He, 5He, 6Li, and 7Li, with model spaces ranging from 4ħΩ to 8ħΩ depending on the nucleus and parity.

## Key numbers

- For 4He, the binding energy obtained is 26.3 MeV using Δ = -55 MeV; the first excited 0+ state (predominantly 2ħΩ) is obtained at 21.8 MeV excitation energy, 1.6 MeV higher than experiment.
- For 4He, the ground state wave function is 70% |0ħΩ⟩, 14% |2ħΩ⟩, 9% |4ħΩ⟩, 3% |6ħΩ⟩, and 4% |8ħΩ⟩; the first excited 0+ state is 8% |0ħΩ⟩, 61% |2ħΩ⟩, 15% |4ħΩ⟩, 13% |6ħΩ⟩, and 3% |8ħΩ⟩.
- For 4He, the 0_2+ state is lowered by 11.9 MeV from its excitation energy in a previous calculation by the same group (Zheng et al., 1993) and by 4.3 MeV from a later calculation (Zheng et al., 1994).
- For 5He, the first excited state (1/2-) is obtained at 2.47 MeV excitation energy; the 3/2+ state at 16.75 MeV experimentally is reproduced at 19.06 MeV.
- For 5He, the energy splitting ΔE between the 1/2- and 3/2- ground state is 2.47 MeV in the 6ħΩ calculation, decreasing from 2.81 MeV in the 0ħΩ calculation.
- For 5He, the lowest 2ħΩ state (3/2-) is obtained at 12.01 MeV excitation energy, dramatically decreased from 21.5 MeV in a previous 4ħΩ single-G calculation (Zheng et al., 1994).
- For 6Li, the 0+ isospin triplet state is obtained at 3.79 MeV excitation energy, close to the experimental value of 3.56 MeV.
- For 6Li, the ground state magnetic dipole moment is calculated as 0.840 μ_N (experimental: 0.822 μ_N); the quadrupole moment is -0.067 e·fm² (experimental: -0.082 e·fm²).
- For 7Li, the first excited state (1/2-) is obtained at 0.46 MeV excitation energy (experimental: 0.48 MeV).
- For 7Li, the ground state quadrupole moment is -2.37 e·fm² (experimental: -4.06 e·fm²); the rms point charge radius is 2.06 fm (experimental: 2.29 fm).
- Diagonal G-matrix elements for 6Li shift by up to 3.3 MeV when N^spectators_sum dependence is properly treated.

## Context

This work extends the multi-ħΩ shell model approach previously developed by the same group (Zheng et al., 1993, 1994) by incorporating the spectator-dependent G-matrix formalism suggested by Haxton et al. (1993), and by extending model spaces to include excitations up to 8ħΩ. The results show that neglecting the N^spectators_sum dependence in earlier large-space calculations led to excited states being obtained at energies several MeV too high, and that the inclusion of high-lying configurations (up to 6ħΩ and 8ħΩ) is essential for convergence of wave functions dominated by 1ħΩ and 2ħΩ components, consistent with findings by Ceuleneer et al. (1994) using a modified Sussex interaction. The work enables more reliable predictions for light nuclei using realistic NN potentials in large model spaces, though the authors note that for 7Li the two-body G-matrix approximation may be inadequate for the model spaces they can handle, and that effective many-body interactions may be needed.
