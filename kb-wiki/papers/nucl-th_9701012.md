# Soft transverse expansion in Pb(158 AGeV) on Pb collisions: preequilibrium motion or 1st order phase transition?

> Transverse expansion of centrally produced matter in Pb on Pb collisions at beam energies around 158 AGeV appears to be rather `soft'. Two possible reasons -- an extended preequilibrium stage and a first order phase transition from a quark-gluon-plasma into hadronic matter -- are discussed. The softening of transverse expansion caused by preequilibrium dynamics is estimated with the aid of the transport model RQMD which does not contain a first order phase transition. It is found that the anisotropy of transverse flow in non-central reactions is very different in the preequilibrium and hydrodynamic scenarios even if the latter are based on a strong 1st order transition.

## Key claim

The softening of transverse expansion in Pb(158 AGeV) on Pb collisions could be caused either by an extended preequilibrium stage or by a first-order phase transition from a quark-gluon plasma into hadronic matter; the anisotropy of transverse flow in non-central reactions is very different between the preequilibrium and hydrodynamic scenarios even when the latter includes a strong first-order transition.

## Method

The author used the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) transport model, which does not contain a first-order phase transition, to simulate approximately 300 Pb(160 AGeV) on Pb collisions at impact parameter b=6 fm. The time evolution of the event-averaged local transverse pressure and energy density was evaluated in the collision center, and the azimuthal asymmetry of transverse hadron momenta was calculated as a function of time. These results were compared to boost-invariant hydrodynamic calculations by Ollitrault using equations of state with and without a first-order phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The crossing time of two Pb nuclei in an observer frame with CMS rapidity is 1.4 fm/c at 160 AGeV.
- The rapidity dispersion from the spreading of longitudinal velocities amounts to approximately 1.1 units for Pb(159 AGeV)+Pb collisions.
- Initially, the energy density is close to 3 GeV/fm^3 in the center of the collision region.
- The transverse pressure is considerably softened for a time interval of about 4 fm/c due to preequilibrium dynamics.
- The preequilibrium stage lasts for approximately 5 fm/c when taking prehadronic and hadronic evolution together.
- After equilibration, the ratio p/e approaches values around 1:6, expected for the hadronic system in kinetic and chemical equilibrium.
- The final asymmetry parameter alpha from RQMD is smaller than the hydrodynamic pi gas value by a factor of 3.84, and smaller than the QGP values by factors of 1.74 (critical temperature 150 MeV) or 2.84 (critical temperature 200 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of transverse expansion dynamics presented in Sorge (1996) which discussed corrections to ideal hydrodynamic evolution in later dilute stages, and complements the hydrodynamic studies of Ollitrault (1992) on flow anisotropy in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus reactions. The paper enables experimental distinction between preequilibrium softening and phase transition scenarios by measuring elliptic flow in non-central collisions, with the NA49 collaboration having already reported preliminary data on elliptic transverse energy flow patterns for non-central Pb(158 AGeV) on Pb reactions.
