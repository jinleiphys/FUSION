# Dynamics of entanglement in expanding quantum fields

> We develop a novel real-time approach to computing the entanglement between spatial regions for Gaussian states in quantum field theory. The entanglement entropy is characterized in terms of local correlation functions on space-like Cauchy hypersurfaces. The framework is applied to explore an expanding light cone geometry in the particular case of the Schwinger model for quantum electrodynamics in 1+1 space-time dimensions. We observe that the entanglement entropy becomes extensive in rapidity at early times and that the corresponding local reduced density matrix is a thermal density matrix for excitations around a coherent field with a time dependent temperature. Since the Schwinger model successfully describes many features of multiparticle production in $e^+ e^-$ collisions, our results provide an attractive explanation in this framework for the apparent thermal nature of multiparticle production even in the absence of significant final state scattering.

## Key claim

The authors develop a real-time method to compute entanglement entropy for Gaussian states in quantum field theory, and apply it to the Schwinger model in 1+1 dimensions. They find that the entanglement entropy becomes extensive in rapidity at early times and that the local reduced density matrix is thermal with a time-dependent temperature, providing an explanation for apparent thermalization in multiparticle production without final-state scattering.

## Method

The authors compute entanglement entropy from local correlation functions on space-like Cauchy hypersurfaces using a real-time formalism for Gaussian states. They apply this framework to an expanding light cone geometry in the Schwinger model (QED in 1+1 dimensions), which is bosonized following Coleman (1975) to map the theory onto a massive scalar field with mass m = e/√π. The entanglement entropy and relative entropy are evaluated using the replica method and expressed in terms of two-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger model bosonizes to a massive scalar with mass m = e/√π, where e is the gauge coupling.
- The bosonized Lagrangian for the massive Schwinger model includes a cosine term with prefactor m e exp(γ)/(2π^(3/2)), where γ is the Euler constant.
- The vacuum angle θ appears in the bosonized Lagrangian as cos(2√π φ + θ) but drops out as an irrelevant overall factor in the massless case.

## Context

This work extends the real-time approach to entanglement in quantum field theory, building on prior work by Bombelli (1986), Srednicki (1993), and Callan and Wilczek (1994) on geometric entropy. The results provide a framework for understanding thermal behavior in e+e- collisions without final-state interactions, complementing the statistical hadronization models of Becattini (1995) and the Lund string fragmentation model of Andersson (1983). The method enables future studies of entanglement dynamics in expanding geometries relevant to heavy-ion collisions and early-universe cosmology.
