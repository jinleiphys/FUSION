# The GDH Sum Rule and Related Integrals

> The spin structure of the nucleon resonance region is analyzed on the basis of our phenomenological model MAID. Predictions are given for the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule as well as generalized integrals over spin structure functions. The dependence of these integrals on momentum transfer is studied and rigorous relationships between various definitions of generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integrals and spin polarizabilities are derived. These results are compared to the predictions of chiral perturbation theory and phenomenological models.

# The GDH Sum Rule and Related Integrals

## Key claim
The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for the proton is satisfied within uncertainties when the phenomenological model MAID is combined with an asymptotic contribution from deep inelastic scattering, yielding a total of (-202 ± 10) μb versus the sum rule prediction of -204 μb; however, the neutron prediction is off by about 60 μb (25%), indicating that the sum rule is not yet fully validated for both nucleons.

## Method
The authors used their phenomenological model MAID to compute helicity-dependent photoabsorption cross sections σ_{3/2} and σ_{1/2} for one-pion, two-pion, and eta production channels, decomposing the cross sections into multipoles (E_{l±}, M_{l±}, L_{l±}) and integrating over energy. They evaluated the Q²-dependent generalized GDH integrals I_A, I_B, I_C, and I_1, and derived model-independent relations connecting their slopes at Q²=0 to the forward spin polarizability γ₀ and the longitudinal-transverse polarizability δ₀. Results were compared to chiral perturbation theory predictions at O(p³) and O(p⁴) and to the models of Anselmino et al. (1989), Burkert et al. (1992), and Soffer and Teryaev (1993).

## Key numbers
- GDH sum rule prediction for the proton: -204 μb; MAID plus asymptotic contribution gives (-202 ± 10) μb.
- Neutron prediction from MAID is off from the sum rule by about 60 μb (25%).
- Forward spin polarizability γ₀ for the proton: (-0.70 ± 0.10) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, with the experimental value in the resonance region (200 MeV < ν < 800 MeV) being (-1.71 ± 0.09) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴.
- Slope of I₁ at Q²=0 from MAID: 4.4 GeV⁻²; from the vector dominance model of Anselmino et al. (1989): 3.4 GeV⁻².
- Slope of I_B from MAID: approximately -4 (GeV/c)⁻² after adding the asymptotic contribution from HERMES data; chiral perturbation theory at O(p⁴) gives -6.6 GeV⁻² (Ji et al., 1999) or -12.6 GeV⁻² (Gellas et al., 2000) depending on the definition of γ₀.
- Zero-crossing of I₁ occurs around Q² = 0.4 (GeV/c)².
- Minimum of I_A occurs at Q² = 0.5 (GeV/c)², in qualitative agreement with Scholten and Korchin (1999).
- Chiral perturbation theory at O(p³) predicts I_A' = I_C' and I₁' = 0; at O(p⁴), I_A' is given by Eq. (27) with coupling constants g_A = 1.26, f_π = 92.4 MeV, m = 938 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV, κ_V = 3.71, κ_S = -0.12.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological analysis of the GDH sum rule and its Q²-dependent generalizations, building on the earlier model of Burkert et al. (1992) and the vector dominance parametrization of Anselmino et al. (1989). The authors compare their results to chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard et al. (1993) at O(p³) and Ji et al. (1999) at O(p⁴), highlighting ambiguities in separating pole terms from polarizabilities in heavy baryon ChPT. The analysis enables future experimental tests at MAMI, ELSA, and Jefferson Lab, particularly for the two-pion contribution and the neutron sum rule, and clarifies the need for consistent definitions of generalized GDH integrals when comparing theory and experiment.
