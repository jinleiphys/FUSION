# Renormalizing the zero point energy in dense QCD

> We analyze the zero point energy in a dense matter of quarks or hadrons with particular attention on the renormalization of the UV divergences. Besides divergences removable by the vacuum subtraction and counter terms, there are also UV divergences associated with non-perturbative modifications of quark bases appearing in the in-medium propagators. The latter would remain after the self-energies and vertices are renormalized, unless a proper set of medium contributions is included at a given truncation. We use the formalism of the two particle irreducible action to clarify how the UV divergences are assembled to cancel. An example is given for the thermodynamic potentials with mesons as composite particles whose zero point energies apparently diverge but can be cancelled by the quark self-energy contributions. Important applications of this work are quark matter with hadronic correlations which may be realized at the core of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in dense QCD, UV divergences in the zero point energy arising from non-perturbative modifications of quark bases (which survive after standard vacuum subtraction and counter-term renormalization) can be systematically cancelled by including a proper set of medium contributions at a given truncation, specifically using the two-particle-irreducible (2PI) action formalism.

## Method

The author uses the 2PI (Luttinger-Ward) action formalism for quark propagators at finite chemical potential, decomposing the renormalized thermodynamic potential into a part associated with the change of quark bases (I_ΔS) and a part associated with the chemical potential shift (I_Δμ). The UV structure of I_ΔS is analyzed by expanding around a reference in-medium propagator built from vacuum-optimized quark bases (S^μ_∥), showing that the most dangerous quadratic divergences cancel when the quark self-energy contributions from the Φ-functional are consistently included alongside the single-particle terms. An explicit example is given for mesonic two-body correlations, where the quadratic divergence from meson zero-point energies is cancelled by corresponding quark self-energy contributions.

## Key numbers

* The pressure relevant for neutron star phenomenology is at most ∼ 1 GeV/fm³, governed by the quark chemical potential μ and the QCD scale Λ_QCD.
* The quartic UV divergence in the zero point energy scales as ∼ Λ_UV⁴ (Λ_UV: UV cutoff).
* Quadratic divergences scale as ∼ (M²_med − M²_vac) Λ² and ∼ μ² Λ².
* For a class of propagators with asymptotic behavior S⁻¹ − (S^μ_∥)⁻¹ ∼ (Slash{p}) Δ²/p², the energy shift I_ΔS is of order ∼ Δ⁴ ln Λ_UV.
* The meson zero-point energy difference between medium and vacuum contributes a quadratic divergence ∼ (m²_* − m²_vac) Λ_UV².

## Context

This work extends the renormalization program for the 2PI action, previously developed for scalar field theories at finite temperature by van Hees and Knoll (2001) and refined by Blaizot et al. (2003), to fermion theories with finite chemical potential. It addresses a gap in the literature where the renormalizability of fermion theories at finite μ had not been explicitly discussed. The formalism enables consistent beyond-mean-field computations for dense quark matter with hadronic correlations, relevant for neutron star cores where hadronic-to-quark matter transitions may be crossover-like, as suggested by Baym et al. (2017) and Masuda et al. (2012). It also provides a microscopic quark-description framework to understand the systematic cancellations observed in nuclear many-body forces, e.g., in the work of Akmal et al. (1998).
