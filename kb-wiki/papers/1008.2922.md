# Universal Relations for Fermions with Large Scattering Length

> The behavior of fermions with two spin states that interact with a large scattering length is constrained by universal relations that hold for any state of the system. These relations involve a central property of the system called the contact, which measures the number of pairs of fermions with different spins that have small separations. The contact controls the thermodynamics of the system as well as the large-momentum and high-frequency tails of correlation functions. This review summarizes the current theoretical and experimental status of these universal relations.

## Key claim
The behavior of fermions with two spin states interacting via a large scattering length is constrained by universal relations that hold for any state of the system, provided the scattering length is large compared to the range of interactions and all other relevant length scales (such as interparticle spacing, thermal de Broglie wavelength, and trap length) are also large compared to that range.

## Method
The universal relations were derived by Shina Tan using boundary conditions on the many-body Schrödinger wavefunction for otherwise noninteracting particles, where the large scattering length enters through the boundary conditions. An alternative derivation uses quantum field theory methods, specifically renormalization and the operator product expansion, which facilitates the systematic inclusion of corrections from nonzero range of interactions. The contact, a central property measuring the number of pairs of fermions with different spins at small separations, is extracted from tails of momentum distributions, short-distance density-density correlators, or adiabatic derivatives of energy with respect to scattering length.

## Key numbers
- The tail of the momentum distribution for each spin state behaves as \(n_\sigma(\bm{k}) \longrightarrow C/k^4\) in the scaling region \(|a|^{-1} \ll k \ll r_0^{-1}\), where \(C\) is the contact with dimension (length)\(^{-1}\).
- The adiabatic relation: \((\mathrm{d}E/\mathrm{d}a^{-1})_S = -\hbar^2 C/(4\pi m)\), where the derivative is taken at fixed entropy.
- For a weakly-bound dimer (two atoms, \(a>0\)): binding energy \(E_\text{dimer} = -\hbar^2/(m a^2)\) and contact \(C_\text{dimer} = 8\pi/a\).
- In the BCS limit (\(a \to 0^-\)) for a balanced homogeneous gas: contact density \(\mathcal{C} \longrightarrow 4\pi^2 n^2 a^2\).
- In the unitary limit (\(a \to \pm\infty\)): contact density \(\mathcal{C} \longrightarrow (2\zeta/5\pi)(3\pi^2 n)^{4/3}\) with \(\zeta \approx 0.95\) from fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo.
- In the BEC limit (\(a \to 0^+\)): contact density \(\mathcal{C} \longrightarrow 4\pi n/a\).
- For the strongly-imbalanced homogeneous gas in the BCS limit: \(\mathcal{C} \longrightarrow 16\pi^2 n_1 n_2 a^2\); in the BEC limit: \(\mathcal{C} \longrightarrow 8\pi n_2/a\).
- The high-temperature limit of the contact density for a balanced gas: \(\mathcal{C} \longrightarrow 8\pi^2 \hbar^2 n^2/(m k_B T)\).

## Context
This review by Braaten (2010) summarizes the theoretical and experimental status of universal relations for two-spin-state fermions with large scattering length, originally derived by Tan (2005, 2008) and known as the Tan relations. The work extends earlier results on universal properties of particles with large scattering length (Braaten, 2004) and provides a framework that has enabled subsequent calculations of the contact for balanced and imbalanced homogeneous gases using methods such as Luttinger-Ward self-consistent formalism (Haussmann, Punk, and Zwerger, 2009), diagrammatic Monte Carlo (Prokof'ev and Svistunov, 2007), and variational methods (Punk, Dumitrescu, and Zwerger, 2009). The universal relations connect thermodynamic variables to tails of correlation functions and have been tested experimentally in ultracold atom systems, particularly through measurements of the contact and rf spectroscopy.
