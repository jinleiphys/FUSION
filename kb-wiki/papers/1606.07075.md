# Strange Quark Magnetic Moment of the Nucleon at Physical Point

> We report a lattice QCD calculation of the strange quark contribution to the nucleon's magnetic moment and charge radius. This analysis presents the first direct determination of strange electromagnetic form factors including at the physical pion mass. We perform a model-independent extraction of the strange magnetic moment and the strange charge radius from the electromagnetic form factors in the momentum transfer range of $0.051 \,\text{GeV}^2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 1.31 \,\text{GeV}^2 $. The finite lattice spacing and finite volume corrections are included in a global fit with $24$ valence quark masses on four lattices with different lattice spacings, different volumes, and four sea quark masses including one at the physical pion mass. We obtain the strange magnetic moment $G^s_M(0) = - 0.064(14)(09)\, μ_N$. The four-sigma precision in statistics is achieved partly due to low-mode averaging of the quark loop and low-mode substitution to improve the statistics of the nucleon propagator. We also obtain the strange charge radius $\langle r_s^2\rangle_E = -0.0043 (16)(14)\,$ $\text{fm}^2$.

# Strange Quark Magnetic Moment of the Nucleon at Physical Point

## Key claim
This lattice QCD calculation reports the first direct determination of strange electromagnetic form factors at the physical pion mass, obtaining a nonzero strange magnetic moment with four-sigma statistical precision. The result depends on controlling excited-state contamination through a combined two-state fit, including finite lattice spacing and volume corrections via a global fit across 24 valence quark masses on four ensembles.

## Method
The authors compute disconnected insertion (DI) three-point correlation functions for the strange vector current on four (2+1)-flavor domain wall fermion ensembles (24I, 32I, 48I, 32ID) with lattice spacings from 0.0828 fm to 0.1431 fm. They extract Sachs form factors from ratios of three-point to two-point nucleon correlation functions using a combined fit (CF) that simultaneously fits the standard ratio and the summed ratio to control excited-state contamination. The momentum transfer range covered is 0.051 GeV^2 to 1.31 GeV^2, and the Q^2 dependence of the form factors is modeled with a model-independent z-expansion fit to extract G^s_M(0) and the strange charge radius.

## Key numbers
- Strange magnetic moment: G^s_M(0) = -0.064(14)(09) μ_N (statistical and systematic uncertainties combined as -0.064(17) μ_N)
- Strange charge radius: ⟨r_s^2⟩_E = -0.0043(16)(14) fm^2 (combined as -0.0043(21) fm^2)
- G^s_M(Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2) = -0.037(10)(05) μ_N
- Lowest Q^2 values used: 0.051 GeV^2 for the 48I ensemble and 0.073 GeV^2 for the 32ID ensemble
- Four lattice ensembles used: 24I (a=0.1105 fm, m_π=330 MeV), 32I (a=0.0828 fm, m_π=300 MeV), 48I (a=0.1141 fm, m_π=139 MeV), 32ID (a=0.1431 fm, m_π=171 MeV)

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of strange form factors by the same group (Liu et al., quenched; Doi et al., 2+1 flavor at heavy pion masses) and by Green et al. (2015) who obtained G^s_M(0) = -0.022(8) μ_N at m_π = 317 MeV. It also improves upon indirect model-dependent extractions such as Shanahan et al. (2014) who found G^s_M(0) = -0.07(3) μ_N using linear extrapolation. The results provide a first-principles lattice prediction at the physical point that can be compared with parity-violating electron scattering experiments (SAMPLE, HAPPEX, G0, A4 collaborations) and global analyses of world data, enabling more precise determination of weak form factors when combined with experimental inputs.
