# Quark-Antiquark Bound States within a Dyson-Schwinger Bethe-Salpeter Formalism

> Pion and kaon observables are calculated using a Dyson-Schwinger Bethe-Salpeter formalism. It is shown that an infrared finite gluon propagator can lead to quark confinement via generation of complex mass poles in quark propagators. Observables, including electromagnetic form factors, are calculated entirely in Euclidean metric for spacelike values of bound state momentum and final results are extrapolated to the physical region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an infrared-finite gluon propagator (Gaussian form) can produce quark confinement through the generation of complex mass poles in the quark propagator, contrary to the common assumption that only infrared-singular gluon propagators can confine quarks. The authors qualify that this is shown specifically within the Dyson-Schwinger Bethe-Salpeter formalism using a dressed quark-gluon vertex that preserves the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator functions A(p) and B(p) in Euclidean metric using an iterative numerical method, with a gluon propagator consisting of an infrared Gaussian term plus an ultraviolet perturbative QCD term. They then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar meson bound states by computing eigenvalues for spacelike bound state momenta (P^2 less than 0) and extrapolating the results to the physical timelike region using polynomial fits. The confinement test applies a spectral density analysis to the Euclidean quark propagator to determine whether real or complex poles exist when continued to Minkowski metric.

## Key numbers

- Quark confinement test: For coupling strength G = 1.97 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻², the average distance a quark can travel before hadronization is 0.39 Fermi, compared to pion radius 0.66 Fermi and kaon radius 0.53 Fermi
- Model parameters: G = 1.97 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻², σ = 750 MeV, Λ_QCD = 225 MeV, τ = 3
- Current quark masses used: m_u,d = 3 MeV, m_s = 60 MeV (with m_s/m_u ratio within acceptable limits from Gasser and Leutwyler)
- For weak coupling G = 1 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻², the quark propagator shows a real pole at approximately 110 MeV (unconfined case)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger Bethe-Salpeter formalism previously applied by Roberts, Jain, and Munczek to meson observables, but introduces a dressed quark-gluon vertex (Ball-Chiu vertex) while maintaining the chiral limit condition originally established by Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (1961). The approach enables calculation of electromagnetic form factors entirely in Euclidean metric without assuming analyticity of the quark propagator functions, and the confinement test method follows the procedure of H. R. W. (no specific year given). The results enable future calculations of meson observables using infrared-finite gluon propagators, which are simpler to handle numerically than singular forms like 1/q⁴.
