# Statistical description with anisotropic momentum distributions for hadron production in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The various experimental data at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies on hadron particle yields for central heavy ion collisions are investigated by employing a generalized statistical density operator, that allows for a well-defined anisotropic local momentum distribution for each particle species, specified by a common streaming velocity parameter. The individual particle ratios are rather insensitive to a change in this new intensive parameter. This leads to the conclusion that the reproduction of particle ratios by a statistical treatment does not imply the existence of a fully isotropic local momentum distribution at hadrochemical freeze-out, i.e. a state of almost complete thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim

The reproduction of hadron particle ratios by a statistical treatment does not imply the existence of a fully isotropic local momentum distribution at hadrochemical freeze-out; the particle ratios are rather insensitive to changes in a new intensive streaming velocity parameter, meaning that a state of almost complete thermal equilibrium is not required to explain the data.

## Method

The authors constructed a generalized statistical density operator for a noninteracting hadron resonance gas (masses up to 2 GeV) that includes a Lagrange multiplier v representing a common streaming velocity, which deforms the local momentum distribution longitudinally. They fixed temperature T and baryochemical potential μ_B to values from prior statistical model fits (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1995-2001) and then varied v from zero to near the speed of light to test the sensitivity of hadron particle ratios to this anisotropy. The analysis was applied to central heavy ion collision data at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, the p/π⁺ ratio shows the largest deviation when varying v, changing by about 6%.
- The ṗ/p and K⁺/K⁻ ratios vary by about 1% or less when v is varied.
- For Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV (GSI energies), the relative velocity v_rel at maximum compression was found to be approximately 0.6 c in prior transport model work (Fuchs et al., cited in text).
- The velocity parameter v=0.4 c is used as an illustrative example for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, showing the momentum distribution elongation.

## Context

This work extends the standard statistical (thermal) model approach of Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995-2001) by relaxing the assumption of local isotropic momentum distributions, which those authors had used to argue for near-complete thermal equilibrium at chemical freeze-out. The study is motivated by microscopic transport model results (Bravina et al., Sollfrank et al., and Schenke et al., 2000) that show incomplete local momentum equilibration at intermediate and late reaction stages. The finding that particle ratios are insensitive to the anisotropy parameter implies that the standard extraction of total particle number densities and energy densities at chemical freeze-out becomes uncertain, and it underlines the need for detailed microscopic transport models to understand heavy ion collisions.
