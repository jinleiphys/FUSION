# Neutron stars in a Skyrme model with hyperons

> Available Skyrme parametrizations with hyperons are examined from the point of view of their suitability for applications to neutron stars. It is shown that the hyperons can attenuate or even remove the problem of ferromagnetic instability common to (nearly) all Skyrme parametrizations of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. At high density the results are very sensitive to the choice of the Lambda-Lambda interaction. The selected parameter sets are then used to obtain the resulting properties of both cold neutron stars and hot protoneutron stars. The general features known from other models are recovered.

## Key claim

Hyperons can attenuate or even remove the ferromagnetic instability that is common to nearly all Skyrme parametrizations of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, but this effect is highly sensitive to the choice of the Lambda-Lambda interaction at high density, and the actual presence and position of the ferromagnetic pole depends on the specific combination of NN, N-Lambda, and Lambda-Lambda parameter sets.

## Method

The author tested 43 NN Skyrme forces in combination with 13 parametrizations of the N-Lambda interaction and 4 options for the Lambda-Lambda interaction, imposing conditions of beta-equilibrium and electric charge conservation in neutron star matter. The criterion for ferromagnetic instability was evaluated via the determinant of the inverse magnetic susceptibility matrix, expressed in terms of Landau parameters in the spin S=1 channel, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation was solved to obtain mass-radius relations for both cold neutron stars and hot protoneutron stars with trapped neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- Threshold for Lambda hyperon formation occurs between 1.7 n_sat and 4 n_sat depending on parameter choice, in agreement with other Brueckner-Hartree-Fock or relativistic mean field calculations
- For the SLy10 NN force with LY-I N-Lambda interaction, the hyperon threshold is at 2.719 n_sat with proton fraction Y_p = 0.041
- For the SkI3 NN force with LY-I N-Lambda interaction, the hyperon threshold is at 1.864 n_sat with proton fraction Y_p = 0.126
- The ferromagnetic transition in npe matter without hyperons occurs at 3.055 n_sat for SLy10, 3.078 n_sat for SkI3, 2.140 n_sat for SkI5, and no pole for SV
- The SV NN interaction is the only one among all tested that does not give a ferromagnetic instability in npe matter in beta equilibrium
- Maximum masses of neutron stars without hyperons are of order 2 M_sun to 2.4 M_sun for the four selected NN forces
- The speed of sound reaches c_s^2=1 at densities of approximately 8-11 n_sat for the SLL3 Lambda-Lambda parametrization, 13-16 n_sat for SLL2, and above 20 n_sat for SLL1

## Context

This work extends the study of Rikovska Stone et al. (2003), who tested 87 parameter sets for the NN force in neutron star calculations, by including the Lambda hyperon. It builds on the hyperonic Skyrme parametrizations of Balberg and Gal (1997), who included all hyperons but provided only the unpolarized energy density, and uses the N-Lambda parametrizations of Lanskoy and Yamamoto (1997) and Fernandez et al. (1989) along with the Lambda-Lambda parametrizations of Lanskoy (1998). The results enable calculation of neutrino-baryon scattering rates in hot protoneutron stars via the axial response function in the random phase approximation, presented in a separate paper by the same author (2004).
