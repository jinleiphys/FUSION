# Masses and Radii of the Nuclei with N>=Z in an Alpha-Cluster Model

> In the framework of a recently developed alpha-cluster model a nucleus is represented as a core (alpha-cluster liquid drop with dissolved excess neutron pairs in it) and a nuclear molecule on its surface. From analysis of experimental nuclear binding energies one can find the number of alpha-clusters in the molecule and calculate the nuclear charge radii. It was shown that for isotopes of one Z with growing A the number of alpha-clusters in the molecule decreases to three, which corresponds to the nucleus 12C for even Z and 15N for odd Z, and the specific density of the core binding energy ρgrows and reaches its saturation value. In this paper it is shown that the value ρ=2.55 MeV/fm^3 explains the particular number of excess neutrons in stable nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the binding energies of stable and beta-stable nuclei with N>=Z can be described by a simple formula where the core binding energy is proportional to the volume of core alpha-clusters with a single fitted specific density ρ=2.55 MeV/fm³, but only under the condition that the outside molecule is always ¹²C (or ¹⁵N for odd Z) and the core contains exactly Nα−3 alpha-clusters.

## Method

The author represents each nucleus as a core (an alpha-cluster liquid drop containing dissolved excess neutron pairs) plus a nuclear molecule on its surface, where the molecule consists of a fixed number of alpha-clusters (3 for even Z, 3.5 for odd Z). The core binding energy is calculated as the product of the number of core alpha-clusters, the volume per alpha-cluster (vα=4/3πrα³ with rα=1.595 fm), and the fitted specific density ρ. The total binding energy is then the sum of the core binding energy, the binding energy of the ¹²C or ¹⁵N molecule, minus the Coulomb interaction energy between the molecule and the core. ## Key numbers

- Specific density of core binding energy ρ = 2.55 MeV/fm³ (the single fitting parameter)
- Volume per core alpha-cluster vα = 43.34 MeV (from vα × ρ)
- Binding energy of ¹²C molecule Eml = 92 MeV; binding energy of ¹⁵N molecule Eml = 115 MeV
- Charge radius of an alpha-cluster in the core rα = 1.595 fm (obtained from independent analysis of experimental charge radii)
- Radius of ⁴He nucleus r_⁴He = 1.71 fm (experimental value)
- For the distance Rα between molecule and core: using empirical values for Z<24 and formula Rα=2.168(Nα−4)^(1/3) for Z≥24, the fit gives ρ=2.56 MeV/fm³ with rms deviation δ=17 MeV for stable nuclei with Z,Z1≤83; using Rα = Rcore + Rml gives ρ=2.54 MeV/fm³ with δ=15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster model developed in the author's prior work (Nie, 2009) which represented nuclei as a core (alpha-cluster liquid drop with dissolved excess neutron pairs) and a nuclear molecule on the surface. The paper provides new evidence that the specific density of core binding energy saturates at ρ=2.55 MeV/fm³ and that stable nuclei have exactly three alpha-clusters (or three and a half for odd Z) in the surface molecule, corresponding to ¹²C or ¹⁵N. This enables prediction of the number of excess neutrons in stable nuclei and explains why experimental charge radii of isotopes with the same Z decrease with growing A. The model contradicts the standard liquid-drop model approach by treating the nucleus as having a fixed molecular structure on the surface rather than a uniform distribution of nucleons.
