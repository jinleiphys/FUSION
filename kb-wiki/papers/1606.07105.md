# Magnetic hyperfine structure of the ground-state doublet in highly charged ions $^{229}$Th$^{89+,87+}$ and the Bohr-Weisskopf effect

> The magnetic hyperfine (MHF) structure of the $5/2^+$(0.0 eV) ground state and the low-lying $3/2^+$(7.8 eV) isomeric state of the $^{229}$Th nucleus in highly charged ions Th$^{89+}$ and Th$^{87+}$ is calculated. The distribution of the nuclear magnetization (the Bohr-Weisskopf effect) is accounted for in the framework of the collective nuclear model with the wave functions of the Nilsson model for the unpaired neutron. The deviations of the MHF structure for the ground and isomeric states from their values in the model of point-like nuclear magnetic dipole are calculated. The influence of the mixing of the states with the same quantum number $F$ on the energy of sublevels is studied. Taking into account the mixing of states, the probabilities of the transitions between the components of MHF structure are found.

## Key claim

The magnetic hyperfine (MHF) structure of the ground-state doublet in highly charged ions ²²⁹Th⁸⁹⁺ and ²²⁹Th⁸⁷⁺ is calculated, accounting for the Bohr-Weisskopf effect via the collective nuclear model with Nilsson-model wave functions; the abstract's claim of "deviations... from their values in the model of point-like nuclear magnetic dipole" is hedged by the finding that the reduction is only 3% for the ground state and 6% for the isomeric state, and that mixing of states with the same quantum number F significantly modifies the sublevel energies and transition probabilities.

## Method

The electron wave functions for the 1s₁/₂ state in Th⁸⁹⁺ and the 2s₁/₂ state in Th⁸⁷⁺ were obtained by solving the Dirac equation in the Coulomb potential of a spherical nucleus with homogeneous charge distribution, using ab initio density functional theory (DFT) with LDA and GGA (PBE) exchange-correlation functionals. The Bohr-Weisskopf effect was incorporated by computing the interaction energy between the electron current and the vector potential from the distributed nuclear magnetization, using the collective nuclear model with Nilsson-model wave functions for the unpaired neutron in the 5/2⁺[633] ground state and 3/2⁺[631] isomeric state. Mixing of the two F=2 sublevels (from the ground and isomeric states) was treated via a two-state mixing formalism, and radiative transition probabilities between hyperfine sublevels were calculated including the electron spin-flip and nuclear transition operators.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field at the ²²⁹Th nucleus: 112 MT for Th⁸⁹⁺ (1s₁/₂ configuration) and 20 MT for Th⁸⁷⁺ ((1s₁/₂)²(2s₁/₂)¹ configuration).
- Large radial component at the origin: g₁s₁/₂(0) = 8.46×10⁻³ for Th⁸⁹⁺; g₂s₁/₂(0) = 3.57×10⁻³ for Th⁸⁷⁺ (in units of R₀⁻³/², with R₀ = 1.2 A¹/³ fm).
- Bohr-Weisskopf reduction of hyperfine splitting: 3% for the 5/2⁺ ground state and 6% for the 3/2⁺ isomeric state, relative to the point-nucleus model.
- Mixing coefficient b: 0.048 for Th⁸⁹⁺ and 0.0083 for Th⁸⁷⁺.
- Energy of the M1 mixing interaction E_M1: 0.4 eV for Th⁸⁹⁺ and 0.07 eV for Th⁸⁷⁺.
- Radiative widths and half-lives for transitions in Th⁸⁹⁺: transition 1 (3/2⁺,1→3/2⁺,2) Γ = 6.2×10⁻¹⁷ eV, τ = 7.4 s; transition 2 (5/2⁺,3→5/2⁺,2) Γ = 1.1×10⁻¹⁴ eV, τ = 42 ms; transition 3 (3/2⁺,1→5/2⁺,2) Γ = 3.2×10⁻¹⁴ eV, τ = 14 ms; transition 4 (3/2⁺,2→5/2⁺,3) Γ = 2.2×10⁻¹⁴ eV, τ = 21 ms; transition 5 (3/2⁺,2→5/2⁺,2) Γ = 2.0×10⁻¹⁵ eV, τ = 0.23 s.
- Radiative widths and half-lives for transitions in Th⁸⁷⁺: transition 1 Γ = 3.8×10⁻¹⁹ eV, τ = 20 min; transition 2 Γ = 4.6×10⁻¹⁷ eV, τ = 10 s; transition 3 Γ = 8.0×10⁻¹⁶ eV, τ = 0.57 s; transition 4 Γ = 7.6×10⁻¹⁶ eV, τ = 0.61 s; transition 5 Γ = 5.2×10⁻¹⁷ eV, τ = 8.7 s.
- Radiative width for the bare nuclear M1 isomeric transition (3/2⁺→5/2⁺): Γ = 3×10⁻¹⁹ eV, corresponding to τ = 26 min.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the magnetic hyperfine structure in ²²⁹Th ions by Wycech (1993) and Karpeshin (1998), who considered only the Th⁸⁹⁺ ion, by providing the first calculation for Th⁸⁷⁺ and by incorporating the Bohr-Weisskopf effect using Nilsson-model wave functions rather than simpler point-nucleus or Fermi-contact approximations. The results enable experimental studies of the hyperfine structure of ²²⁹Th ions in storage rings (as proposed by Geissel (1992), Klaf (1994), Radon (1997), and Ma (2015)), and can be used for precise determination of the nuclear magnetic moments of the ground and isomeric states, which is relevant for the development of a nuclear clock (Peik 2003, Campbell 2012) and tests of fundamental symmetries (Flambaum 2006, Berengut 2009).
