# $D^{\pm}D^{*\mp}$ Hadronic Atom as a Key to Revealing the $X(3872)$ Mystery

> The $X(3872)$, whose mass coincides with the $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold, is the most extended hadron object. Since its discovery in 2003, debates have never stopped regarding its internal structure. We propose a new object, the $X$ atom, which is the $D^\pm D^{*\mp}$ composite system with positive charge parity and a mass of $(3879.89\pm0.07)$ MeV, formed mainly due to the Coulomb force. We show that a null signal of the $X$ atom can be used to put a lower limit on the binding energy of the $X(3872)$. From the current knowledge of the $X(3872)$ properties, the production rate for the $X$ atom relative to the $X(3872)$ in $B$ decays and at hadron colliders should be at least $1\times10^{-3}$. New insights into the $X(3872)$ will be obtained through studying the $X$ atom.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a new object, the $X$ atom (a $D^\pm D^{*\mp}$ Coulomb bound state with positive charge parity and mass $(3879.89\pm0.07)$ MeV), can be used to set a lower limit on the binding energy of the $X(3872)$ if a null signal for the $X$ atom is observed; the production rate of the $X$ atom relative to the $X(3872)$ should be at least $1\times10^{-3}$ based on current $X(3872)$ properties, but this bound depends on the assumption that isospin symmetry holds for the short-distance production amplitudes.

## Method

The authors construct a nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) Lagrangian for the coupled-channel system of $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ and $D^+D^{*-}$ with positive charge parity, including Coulomb interactions and a contact term $C_0$ for the strong interaction. They compute the $T$-matrix for $S$-wave scattering, incorporating the infinite sum of Coulomb bound states through the exact Coulomb propagator, and derive the pole positions and effective couplings for both the $X(3872)$ and the $X$ atom. The production rates in $B$ decays and $pp$ collisions are factorized into short-distance amplitudes (related by isospin symmetry) and long-distance effective couplings, yielding a ratio that depends only on the $X(3872)$ binding energy.

## Key numbers

- $X$ atom ground-state binding energy: $\text{Re}\,E_{A1} = 22.92$ keV (Coulomb binding energy $E_1 = 25.81$ keV with strong interaction correction)
- $X$ atom mass: $(3879.89\pm0.07)$ MeV
- $X$ atom total decay width: $(89.2\pm1.8)$ keV (includes $D^{*\pm}$ width $\Gamma_{D^{*+}} = (83.4\pm1.8)$ keV and strong interaction contribution)
- Bohr radius of $D^\pm D^{*\mp}$ Coulomb bound state: $r_c = 27.86$ fm
- $X(3872)$ binding energy relative to $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold: $\delta = (0.00\pm0.18)$ MeV
- Predicted minimum production ratio $R_\Gamma \simeq R_\sigma \gtrsim 1\times10^{-3}$ for $X$ atom relative to $X(3872)$
- Lower bound relation: $\delta \simeq 0.25\ \text{eV} / R_\Gamma^2$

## Context

This work extends the study of hadronic atoms (previously limited to light-hadron systems such as pions, kaons, and protons, as reviewed by Gasser (2007) and Gasser (2009)) to a system involving charm quarks, where the strong interaction is nonperturbative due to the nearby $X(3872)$ resonance. The approach builds on the NREFT factorization framework developed by Braaten (2005) and others for $X(3872)$ production, and the method for measuring the $X(3872)$ binding energy at PANDA proposed by Guo (2019). The $X$ atom provides a new observable that can be searched for in existing LHCb data (Aaij 2020) and at future facilities like PANDA and LHC upgrades, potentially resolving debates about the $X(3872)$ internal structure.
