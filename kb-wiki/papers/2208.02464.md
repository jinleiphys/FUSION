# Exploring medium mass nuclei using effective chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions

> In the past decades, it has been shown that the three-body force is necessary for a fully microscopic understanding of nuclear many-body systems, and thus efficient schemes for storing and utilizing three-body matrix elements have been developed. However, three-body matrix elements still demand a lot of memory and/or storage and it readily exceeds the limitation of the state-of-the-art supercomputers. We explore the possibility of mimicking the effects of three-body forces in medium-mass region, especially lower $pf$ shell, by density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interactions combining with a modern family of chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction and an uncertainty quantification on the coupling constants. We found that some effective nucleon-nucleon potentials give decent description of ground state energies, but also systematic underestimation in charge radii and deficiencies relevant to the $pf$-shell region.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions, derived from three-nucleon forces (3NF) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), can mimic the effects of explicit 3NFs in medium-mass nuclei without introducing full three-body matrix elements. The authors find that while some effective NN potentials give decent descriptions of ground-state energies, they produce systematic underestimation in charge radii and deficiencies in the lower pf-shell region, indicating that the structure of 3NF is richer than what a density-dependent NN interaction at NNLO can capture.

## Method

The authors combine a modern chiral NN interaction up to N4LO (the Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk or EMN interaction) with a density-dependent effective NN force derived from 3NF at NNLO, which they call "2n3n." They calibrate five low-energy constants (LECs) for the 2n3n terms (c̃₁, c̃₃, c̃₄, c_D, c_E) using Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling with ground-state energies of ⁴⁰,⁴⁸,⁵²,⁵⁴Ca and ⁵⁶Ni as data, employing Hartree-Fock many-body perturbation theory (HF-MBPT) for fast evaluation. They then apply the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) at the IMSRG(2) truncation level and valence-space IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) to derive shell-model effective interactions for the pf shell, performing calculations with the newly developed Julia package NuclearToolkit.jl and the KSHELL code.

## Key numbers

- The NN interaction uses cutoff Λ = 500 MeV and spectral function regularization cutoff Λ_SFR = 700 MeV
- The Fermi momentum for the density-dependent interaction is fixed at k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹, corresponding to empirical normal nuclear density ρ = 0.166 fm⁻³
- IMSRG(2) ground-state energies for ⁴⁰Ca range from -344.348 MeV (parameter set C) to -351.855 MeV (MAP*), compared to the experimental value of -342.052 MeV
- IMSRG(2) charge radii for ⁴⁰Ca range from 2.8608 fm (set C) to 2.9068 fm (set B), compared to the experimental value of 3.4776 fm
- IMSRG(2) ground-state energy for ⁵⁶Ni ranges from -471.810 MeV (set B) to -476.886 MeV (set A), compared to the experimental value of -483.996 MeV
- For lighter nuclei (⁴He, ¹⁶O), binding energies are underestimated by about 10%; for heavier nuclei (⁹⁰Zr, ¹³²Sn), binding energies are overestimated by about 10%

## Context

This work extends the approach of using density-dependent NN forces from 3NF, previously applied by Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2013) and Kohno (2013), to modern N4LO chiral NN interactions. The study contrasts with calculations that include explicit 3NFs, such as the "EM 1.8/2.0" interaction (Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk, 2017) which gives better descriptions of 2+ systematics in Ca and Ni isotopes, and the VS-IMSRG results of Stroberg (2017) using consistently evolved NN+3NF interactions. The findings enable future work on sub-leading density-dependent NN forces derived at higher orders (Kaiser, 2018-2020) and motivate more detailed multi-angle analyses of chiral potentials and many-body methods in medium-mass nuclei.
