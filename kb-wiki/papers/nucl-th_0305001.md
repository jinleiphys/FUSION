# Intermediate energy Coulomb excitation as a probe of nuclear structure at radioactive beam facilities

> The effects of retardation in the Coulomb excitation of radioactive nuclei in intermediate energy collisions (Elab ~100 MeV/nucleon) are investigated. We show that the excitation cross sections of low-lying states in 11Be, {38,40,42}S and {44,46}Ar projectiles incident on gold and lead targets are modified by as much as 20% due to these effects. The angular distributions of decaying gamma-rays are also appreciably modified.

# Intermediate energy Coulomb excitation as a probe of nuclear structure at radioactive beam facilities

## Key claim

Retardation and recoil corrections modify Coulomb excitation cross sections of low-lying nuclear states by as much as 20% at intermediate beam energies around 100 MeV/nucleon, and also appreciably alter the angular distributions of decaying gamma rays, contrary to the simpler non-relativistic or purely relativistic treatments that neglect one of these effects.

## Method

The authors developed a semiclassical Coulomb excitation formalism that uses a modified Rutherford trajectory with the half-distance of closest approach scaled by the Lorentz factor (a = a0/γ), and incorporates the full relativistic electromagnetic propagator. They computed orbital integrals for electric and magnetic multipolarities using the code COULINT, and applied the formalism to calculate excitation cross sections and gamma-ray angular distributions for 11Be, 38,40,42S, and 44,46Ar projectiles incident on gold and lead targets at intermediate energies.

## Key numbers

- Excitation cross sections of low-lying states in 11Be, 38,40,42S, and 44,46Ar projectiles on gold and lead targets are modified by as much as 20% due to retardation effects at Elab ~ 100 MeV/nucleon
- For γ = 1.1 (corresponding to approximately 100 MeV/nucleon), the orbital integrals I(E2,μ) for a deflection angle of 10° differ substantially from the non-relativistic (γ = 1) values; for example, at ζ = 0.1, I(E2,2) = 1.105(-3) for γ = 1.1 versus 8.675(-4) for γ = 1
- The adiabacity parameter condition for intermediate energies is ξ(R) = ωfiR/(γv) < 1, allowing excitation of states up to 10-20 MeV at bombarding energies around and above 100 MeV/nucleon
- For low excitation energies (Ex ~ 1 MeV) at high energies, the gamma-ray angular distribution coefficients become independent of ζ or ξ: a2E1 = 1, a2E2 = -2, a4E2 = -0.25

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Coulomb excitation theory of Alder and Winther (1975) for low-energy collisions and the relativistic treatment of Winther and Alder (1979) to the intermediate energy regime (30-200 MeV/nucleon) where both recoil and retardation effects are significant. The formalism bridges the gap between the non-relativistic Rutherford-trajectory approach and the relativistic straight-line approximation, and is directly applicable to experiments at radioactive beam facilities such as the MSU facility where reactions on 38,40,42S and 44,46Ar have been studied at about 40 MeV/nucleon (Schatz et al., 1996). The results enable more accurate extraction of electromagnetic transition matrix elements from Coulomb excitation measurements at intermediate energies.
