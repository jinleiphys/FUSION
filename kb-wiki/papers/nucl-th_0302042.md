# Electromagnetic interaction in chiral quantum hadrodynamics and decay of vector and axial-vector mesons

> The chiral invariant QHD-III model of Serot and Walecka is applied in the calculation of some meson properties. The electromagnetic interaction is included by extending the symmetry of the model to the local U(1) \times SU(2)_{R} \times SU(2)_{L} group. The minimal and nonminimal contributions to the electromagnetic Lagrangian are obtained in a new representation of QHD-III. Strong decays of the axial-vector meson, a_{1} \to πρ, a_{1} \to πσ, and the electromagnetic decays ρ\to ππγ, a_{1} \to πγand ρ\to πγare calculated. The low-energy parameters for the π-πscattering are calculated in the tree-level approximation. The effect of the auxiliary Higgs bosons, introduced in QHD-III in order to generate masses of the vector and axial-vector mesons via the Higgs mechanism, is studied as well. This is done on the tree level for π-πscattering and on the level of one-loop diagrams for the a_{1} \to πγdecay. It is demonstrated that the model successfully describes some features of meson phenomenology in the non-strange sector.

## Key claim

The chiral QHD-III model, when extended to include electromagnetic interactions via a local U(1) × SU(2)R × SU(2)L symmetry and after proper diagonalization of the pion and Higgs fields, can successfully describe several meson decays and low-energy π-π scattering parameters, though the mass of the σ meson and the Higgs particles remain unconstrained and the Higgs mass is taken to infinity in the calculations.

## Method

The authors extended the chiral QHD-III model of Serot and Walecka by gauging the symmetry to U(1) × SU(2)R × SU(2)L to include electromagnetic interactions, deriving both minimal and nonminimal contributions to the EM Lagrangian. They removed the a1-π and ρ-H mixing terms using a gauge-fixing procedure in the unitary gauge, following the method of Pre99, and expressed the Lagrangian in terms of the physical pion field. Strong decays (a1 → πρ, a1 → πσ) and electromagnetic decays (ρ → ππγ, a1 → πγ, ρ → πγ) were calculated at tree level and one-loop level, and low-energy π-π scattering parameters were computed in the tree-level approximation.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson mass mρ is related to the strong coupling constant and vacuum expectation value via mρ = (1/2)gρu.
- The a1 meson mass squared is given by ma² = mρ² + gρ²v² = gρ²(v² + u²/4).
- The mixing angle between the Z and π fields is defined by tan θπ = 2v/u, with the physical pion field related by π = (mρ/ma)π̃.
- The gyromagnetic ratio for the ρ and a1 mesons is 2 in units of e/2mρ and e/2ma respectively.

## Context

This work extends the QHD-III model of Serot and Walecka (1992) by incorporating electromagnetic interactions, building on the earlier gauged linear σ model (GLSM) developed by Lee (1968) and elaborated by Gasiorowicz and Geffen (1969). The method for removing the a1-π mixing follows the alternative gauge-fixing approach suggested by Pre99, which avoids the complicated momentum-dependent vertices that arise from the field redefinition method used in earlier works such as Ko94 and Urb01. The results enable further calculations of meson properties within a renormalizable chiral framework and provide a basis for studying the role of Higgs bosons in hadronic phenomenology.
