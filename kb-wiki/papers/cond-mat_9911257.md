# Phase Transitions in "Small" systems

> Traditionally, phase transitions are defined in the thermodynamic limit only. We discuss how phase transitions of first order (with phase separation and surface tension), continuous transitions and (multi)-critical points can be seen and classified for small systems. Boltzmann defines the entropy as the logarithm of the area W(E,N)=e^S(E,N) of the surface in the mechanical N-body phase space at total energy E. The topology of the curvature determinant D(E,N) of S(E,N) allows the classification of phase transitions without taking the thermodynamic limit. The first calculation of the entire entropy surface S(E,N) for the diluted Potts model (ordinary (q=3)-Potts model plus vacancies) on a 50*50 square lattice is shown. The regions in {E,N} where D>0 correspond to pure phases, ordered resp. disordered, and D<0 represent transitions of first order with phase separation and ``surface tension''. These regions are bordered by a line with D=0. A line of continuous transitions starts at the critical point of the ordinary (q=3)-Potts model and runs down to a branching point P_m. Along this line \nabla D vanishes in the direction of the eigenvector v_1 of D with the largest eigen-value λ_1\approx 0. It characterizes a maximum of the largest eigenvalue λ_1. This corresponds to a critical line where the transition is continuous and the surface tension disappears. Here the neighboring phases are indistinguishable. The region where two or more lines with D=0 cross is the region of the (multi)-critical point. The micro-canonical ensemble allows to put these phenomena entirely on the level of mechanics.

## Key claim

Phase transitions of first order, continuous transitions, and (multi)-critical points can be classified for "small" systems (where entropy is not extensive) by the topology of the curvature determinant of the micro-canonical entropy surface, without taking the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors performed micro-canonical Monte Carlo simulations of the diluted (q=3)-Potts model on a 50x50 square lattice with periodic boundaries, covering the entire energy-density and particle-number-density phase space with a mesh of about 1000 knots. At each knot they sampled approximately 2x10^8 events to obtain histograms of the probability distribution, from which local derivatives of the entropy S(E,N) were extracted. A multi-step iterative procedure with fourth-order regression fits was used to obtain a uniform covering of the entropy surface and its derivatives up to third order.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 50x50 square lattice (L=50)
- Number of knots in the mesh: approximately 1000, with spacings of Δe=0.08 and Δn=0.04
- Events per knot: approximately 2x10^8
- Critical energy of the ordinary (q=3)-Potts model at zero vacancy concentration: e_c ≈ 1.58 (from Baxter (1973) and Pathria (1972))
- Ground-state energy of the diluted Potts model: e_0(n) = -2n
- Maximum entropy (random configuration) energy: e_max(n) = -2n^2/q with q=3
- Critical point of the diluted (q=1)-Potts model (analogous to Ising lattice gas): e_c ≈ -0.853, n_c = 0.5

## Context

This work extends the micro-canonical approach to phase transitions previously applied to systems with one thermodynamic degree of freedom, such as nuclear fragmentation (Gross 1995) and atomic clusters (Gross 1997), to systems with two thermodynamic degrees of freedom (energy and particle number). The method contrasts with the conventional Yang-Lee theory (Lee 1952) which requires the thermodynamic limit, and with Hill's (1964) thermodynamic treatment of small systems. The results enable the localization of (multi)-critical points in finite systems and demonstrate that the grand-canonical ensemble loses information about the region of phase coexistence, as Gibbs (1902) had warned.
