# Transient spin modes from relaxational axial kinetic theory

> We study the dynamics of spin mode by solving the axial kinetic equations under the relaxation time approximation in the presence of dissipative sources. We find transient spin modes in response to electric field with spacetime inhomogeneity, fluid acceleration and shear. To the lowest order in spatial momentum $k$, we find the responses to electric field and acceleration can be interpreted as retarded response to temporal variations of magnetic field and vorticity respectively. The response to shear occurs at $O(k^2)$ and can be reduced to retarded response to spatial variation of vorticity. Beyond lowest order, the responses to all three sources are non-local with branch cut in the dispersions. We argue that the non-locality is a consequence of the quasi-particle picture underlying the kinetic description. We also analyze spin transport equation taking into account spin response to temporal and spatial variations of vorticity. We find the corrections turn the original first order spin transport equation into a third order one (or a second order one in the homogeneous limit). The change in order of transport equation is a consequence of non-local nature of the responses, suggesting possible breakdown of gradient expansion in spin hydrodynamics for microscopic theories with quasi-particles.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that transient spin modes in response to electric field, fluid acceleration, and shear are all reducible to retarded responses to temporal and spatial variations of magnetic field and vorticity, but only at lowest order in spatial momentum; beyond that order, the responses become non-local with branch cuts in the dispersion, suggesting a possible breakdown of gradient expansion in spin hydrodynamics for microscopic theories with quasi-particles.

## Method

The authors solve the axial kinetic equations under the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a probe fermion subject to electromagnetic and hydrodynamic sources, using Fourier transform to obtain the spin density response in momentum space. They perform phase space integration in the massless limit to derive analytic expressions for the transient spin modes, then expand in small spatial momentum \(k\) to extract local retarded responses and analyze the non-local structure from branch cuts in the complex frequency plane.

## Key numbers

- Spin response to electric field at lowest order in \(k\): \(\widetilde{J}_5^i(\omega,k\to 0) = \frac{T\ln(1+e^{\mu/T})}{6\pi^2}\frac{i(6i\tau+5\tau^2\omega)}{(i+\tau\omega)^2}\epsilon^{ijk}ik_j \tilde{E}_k\)
- Combined particle and anti-particle response to electric field: \(J_5^i(t,x) = -\frac{\mu}{3\pi^2}\int dt'\theta(t')\left(\frac{t'}{\tau}+5\right)e^{-t'/\tau}\partial_0 B_i(t-t',x)\)
- Spin response to shear at \(O(k^2)\): \(\widetilde{J}_5^i(\omega,k\to 0) = -\frac{2i\mathcal{C}\tau^2}{15(i+\tau\omega)^3}k^2\epsilon^{ijk}\hat{k}_j\hat{k}_l\tilde{\sigma}_{lk}\) with \(\mathcal{C} = \frac{T^2}{6} + \frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2}\)
- Spin response to acceleration at lowest order: \(\widetilde{J}_5^i(\omega,k\to 0) = -\frac{\mathcal{C}\tau}{12(i+\tau\omega)^2}\widetilde{\partial_0\Omega^i}\)
- Branch cut location in complex \(\omega\) plane: \(\omega \in (-k - i/\tau, k - i/\tau)\)

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions beyond the static and homogeneous limit used in field theory approaches (Liu 2021, Becattini 2021) and quantum kinetic theory (Hidaka 2017). It addresses the discrepancy between global spin polarization data (STAR 2017) and local polarization measurements (STAR 2019), where spin-shear coupling gives the correct sign but previous treatments assumed instantaneous relaxation. The results enable construction of higher-order spin transport equations and suggest that the gradient expansion in spin hydrodynamics (Hongo 2021) may break down for quasi-particle theories, with the spin transport equation rising from first order to third order when including temporal and spatial variations of vorticity.
