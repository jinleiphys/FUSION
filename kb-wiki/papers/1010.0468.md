# Spin structure of the pion from the instanton vacuum

> We investigate the spin structure of the pion within the framework of the nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum. We first evaluate the tensor form factors of the pion for the first and second moment (n=1,2) and compare it with the lattice data. Combining the tensor form factor of the pion with the electromagnetic one, we determine the impact-parameter dependent probability density of transversely polarized quarks inside the pion. It turns out that the present numerical results for the tensor form factor as well as those for the probability density are in good agreement with the lattice data. We also discuss the distortion of the spatial distribution of the quarks in the transverse plane inside the pion.

## Key claim

The nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum, with no adjustable free parameters, reproduces the lattice QCD results for the pion tensor form factors and the resulting probability density of transversely polarized quarks, provided the model results are evolved from the model scale (approximately 0.6 GeV) to the lattice scale (2 GeV) using leading-order evolution equations.

## Method

The authors compute the tensor form factors \(B_{10}(Q^2)\) and \(B_{20}(Q^2)\) of the pion using the nonlocal chiral quark model derived from the instanton vacuum, which has only two fixed parameters: the average instanton size \(\bar{\rho} \approx 1/3\) fm and the average inter-instanton distance \(\bar{R} \approx 1\) fm. The matrix elements are evaluated in Euclidean space using the Breit frame, and the results are Fourier-transformed to impact-parameter space to obtain the probability density of transversely polarized quarks inside the pion. The model results are evolved from the normalization point \(\mu_0 \approx 0.6\) GeV to \(\mu = 2\) GeV using leading-order evolution to compare with lattice data from the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration (Brommel, 2007).

## Key numbers

- Tensor form factor at zero momentum transfer after evolution to \(\mu = 2\) GeV: \(B_{10}(0) = 0.216\) and \(B_{20}(0) = 0.032\).
- Monopole mass for the electromagnetic form factor: \(M = 0.738\) GeV, compared to the lattice result \(M = (0.727 \pm 0.016)\) GeV and the experimental value \(M = (0.714 \pm 0.004)\) GeV.
- Squared charge radius of the pion: \(\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.456\) fm\(^2\), compared to the lattice result \(\langle r^2 \rangle = (0.441 \pm 0.019)\) fm\(^2\).
- Average transverse shift of the polarized probability density: \(\langle b_y \rangle = 0.152\) fm, compared to the lattice result \(\langle b_y \rangle = (0.151 \pm 0.024)\) fm.
- Pole masses from a \(p\)-pole parametrization with \(p=1.6\): \(m_{p_1} = 0.762\) GeV and \(m_{p_2} = 0.864\) GeV, compared to lattice values \(m_{p_1} = (0.756 \pm 0.095)\) GeV and \(m_{p_2} = (1.130 \pm 0.265)\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of pion structure using the instanton vacuum framework, previously applied to the electromagnetic form factor by Nam (2007), to the tensor form factors and transversity. It directly confronts the lattice QCD results of the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration (Brommel, 2007) and complements earlier theoretical studies using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Broniowski (2010). The results enable a parameter-free description of the pion's spin structure and provide a benchmark for future experimental probes of pion transversity, such as in Drell-Yan processes or semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering.
