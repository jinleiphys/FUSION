# QCD sum rules for the $Δ$ isobar in neutron matter

> We study the properties of the $Δ$ isobar in the symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter using the QCD sum rules approach based on the energy dispersion relation. Allowing for different continuum thresholds for the polarization tensors with different dimensions, we find stable masses for the $Δ$ in both the vacuum and the medium. Compared to the nucleon case, we find that the vector repulsion is smaller for the $Δ$ while the scalar attraction is similar (75 MeV vector repulsion and 150 MeV scalar attraction in the symmetric matter). The smaller vector repulsion can be understood using the Pauli principle and a constituent quark model. Also the isospin dependence of the quasiparticle energy, which mainly comes from the vector self energy, is quite weak. We also allow for an explicit $π-N$ continuum contribution to the polarization function, but find its effect to be minimal. Phenomenological consequences of our results are discussed.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule analysis finds that in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, the vector repulsion for the Δ isobar is smaller (75 MeV) than for the nucleon, while the scalar attraction is similar (200 MeV), with the total mass shift of Δ⁺⁺ being a reduction of 125 MeV; the result depends on allowing different continuum thresholds for polarization tensors of different dimensions and on treating four-quark condensates in greater detail.

## Method

The authors used the QCD sum rules approach based on the energy dispersion relation, employing the Ioffe-type interpolating current η⁽²⁾_μ = ε_abc (u_a^T C γ_μ u_b) u_c for the Δ isobar in the (1/2,1) ⊕ (1,1/2) chiral representation. They performed the operator product expansion (OPE) up to four-quark condensates, applied Borel sum rules with a weighting function (ω − Ē_q) exp(−ω²/M²), and extracted the scalar and vector self-energies by matching the OPE side to a phenomenological side describing a quasi-Δ isobar state in the medium. The continuum thresholds were treated separately for the scalar and vector invariants (s⁰_s = (1.4 GeV)² and s⁰_q = (2.0 GeV)²), and the analysis was extended to asymmetric nuclear matter by allowing different densities for protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- In symmetric nuclear matter at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ = (110 MeV)³, the vector repulsion for the Δ is 75 MeV and the scalar attraction is 200 MeV, giving a total mass reduction of 125 MeV for Δ⁺⁺.
- In neutron matter at normal nuclear density, the vector repulsion is 45 MeV and the scalar attraction is 190 MeV, giving a total mass reduction of 145 MeV for Δ⁺⁺.
- The vacuum mass of the Δ isobar extracted from the sum rule is m_Δ ≃ 1.15 GeV.
- The Borel window used for the sum rule analysis is 1.5 GeV² ≤ M² ≤ 2.0 GeV², with the optimal region near M² ≃ 1.8 GeV².
- The continuum thresholds are set to s⁰_s = (1.4 GeV)² for the scalar invariant and s⁰_q = (2.0 GeV)² for the vector invariant.
- The estimated error from varying s⁰_s, s⁰_q, and the factorization parameter k₁ is 22% of the vacuum mass.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of the Δ isobar in nuclear matter by Jin (1994) and Johnson (1995) by allowing different continuum thresholds for different polarization tensors and treating four-quark condensates in greater detail, and by Azizi (2016) by applying the approach to asymmetric nuclear matter. The results are consistent with self-consistent many-body calculations by Korpa (2003, 2008) that predicted a downward shift of about 50 MeV for the Δ at saturation density, though the present work finds larger shifts. The smaller vector repulsion for the Δ compared to the nucleon is explained using the Pauli principle and a constituent quark model, and the results have implications for the neutron star equation of state, as large changes in Δ properties would soften the equation of state and influence the maximum allowed neutron star mass.
