# Production of fragments and hyperfragments in antiproton-nucleus collisions

> Formation mechanism of fragments with strangeness in collisions of antiprotons on nuclei has been investigated within the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport approach combined with a statistical model (GEMINI) for describing the decays of excited fragments. Production of strange particles in the antiproton induced nuclear reactions is modeled within the LQMD model, in which all possible reaction channels such as elastic scattering, annihilation, charge exchange and inelastic scattering in antibaryon-baryon, baryon-baryon and meson-baryon collisions have been included. A coalescence approach is developed for constructing hyperfragments in phase space after de-excitation of nucleonic fragments. The combined approach could describe the production of fragments in low-energy antiproton induced reactions. Hyperfragments are formed within the narrower rapidities and lower kinetic energies. It has advantage to produce heavier hyperfragments and hypernuclides with strangeness s=-2 (double-$Λ$ fragments) and s=1 ($\overlineΛ$-fragments) in antiproton induced reactions.

# Production of fragments and hyperfragments in antiproton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model combined with the GEMINI statistical decay code can describe fragmentation reactions induced by low-energy antiprotons, and the approach predicts that antiproton-nucleus collisions have an advantage for producing heavier hyperfragments and hypernuclides with strangeness s=-2 (double-Λ fragments) and s=1 (anti-Λ fragments), though the production cross sections for these exotic species are small (maximal cross sections at the level of 0.01 mb for s=-2 and 1 μb for s=1).

## Method
The LQMD transport model was used to simulate antiproton-nucleus collisions, including all relevant reaction channels such as elastic scattering, annihilation, charge exchange, and inelastic scattering in antibaryon-baryon, baryon-baryon, and meson-baryon collisions. Primary fragments were constructed in phase space using a coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff P0 = 200 MeV/c and relative distance cutoff R0 = 3 fm for nucleonic fragments (5 fm for hyperfragments). The de-excitation of excited primary fragments was then described using the GEMINI statistical decay code.

## Key numbers
- Antiproton-nucleus optical potential strength at normal nuclear density: V_opt(p=0, ρ=ρ0) = -655 MeV from G-parity approach, but scaled to -164 MeV using factor ξ=0.25 to match antiproton-nucleus scattering data.
- Antihyperon potential strengths at saturation density: -436 MeV for anti-Λ and -218 MeV for anti-Ξ.
- Coalescence parameters: relative momentum cutoff P0 = 200 MeV/c; relative distance cutoff R0 = 3 fm for nucleonic fragments and 5 fm for hyperfragments.
- Maximal cross sections for hyperfragments in p-bar + 63Cu and p-bar + 181Ta at 4 GeV/c: s=-1 (Λ-hyperfragments) at the level of 1 mb; s=-2 (double-Λ fragments) at the level of 0.01 mb; s=1 (anti-Λ fragments) at the level of 1 μb.
- Threshold momentum for direct anti-N N → anti-Λ Λ reaction: 1.439 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends prior intranuclear cascade (INC) model studies of antiproton-nucleus collisions (Cugnon 1990) and the GiBUU transport model (Larionov 2009) by providing a self-consistent description of dynamical evolution and fragmentation. The combined LQMD+GEMINI approach reproduces mass yield data from the LEAR facility at CERN (Lozano 2001; Jastrzebski 1993). The results suggest that antiproton beams at the future PANDA facility could produce double-Λ hypernuclei and anti-Λ hypernuclei, which are difficult to produce in heavy-ion collisions or proton-induced reactions, enabling studies of Λ-Λ and anti-Λ-nucleon interactions that remain poorly understood.
