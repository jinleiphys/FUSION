# Realistic Calculations of Exclusive $A(e,e'p)X$ Processes off Few-Body Nuclei

> The exclusive processes 2H(e,e'p)n, 3He(e,e'p)^2H, and 3He(e,e'p)(pn), have been calculated using realistic few-body wave functions and treating final state interaction effects within a generalized eikonal approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a generalized eikonal approach, which relaxes the Glauber frozen approximation, can treat final state interaction (FSI) effects in exclusive \(A(e,e'p)X\) processes off few-body nuclei using realistic wave functions, and that by a proper choice of kinematics, FSI effects might be appreciably reduced, allowing the details of the ground-state few-body wave function to be investigated.

## Method

The authors computed the differential cross section for the exclusive processes \(^2H(e,e'p)n\), \(^3He(e,e'p)^2H\), and \(^3He(e,e'p)(pn)\) in the one-photon-exchange approximation. They used realistic few-body wave functions from the AV18 interaction and treated FSI effects by evaluating Feynman diagrams for single and double rescattering within a generalized eikonal approach that includes a factor \(\Delta_0\) to account for the recoil momentum of the third nucleon, releasing the frozen approximation of the standard Glauber approach.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^2H(e,e'p)n\) process, FSI effects lead to better agreement with experimental data from JLAB and SLAC across a range of missing momentum \(p_m\) and azimuthal angle \(\phi\).
- For the \(^3He(e,e'p)(np)\) process, at missing momentum \(p_m \geq 440\) MeV/c and missing energy \(E_m \geq 10\) MeV, the PWIA predictions would directly relate to the three-body wave function, but FSI between the struck proton and the \((np)\) pair almost entirely exhausts the cross section in the perpendicular kinematics of the JLAB experiment.
- The factor \(\Delta_0\) in the generalized eikonal approach scales as \(\Delta_0 \sim (q_0/|{\bf q}|) E_{m}\), and its effects increase with removal energy but do not appreciably distort the Glauber result in most cases considered.

## Context

This work extends the standard non-relativistic Schrodinger approach, which is practical at low energies but becomes impractical at high energies due to the sharp increase in partial waves and possible nucleon excitations (Gloeckler, Pisa group). The calculations use the AV18 interaction for wave functions and compare with experimental data from JLAB (Ulmer, 2003), SLAC (Bulten, 1995), and Saclay (1990s). The approach enables future investigation of ground-state few-body wave functions through quasi-elastic exclusive processes, particularly by choosing kinematics that reduce FSI effects, as also seen in the \(^3He(e,e'2p)n\) process.
