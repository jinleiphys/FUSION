# Ab initio short-range-correlation scaling factors from light to medium-mass nuclei

> High-energy scattering processes, such as deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and quasielastic (QE) scattering provide a wealth of information about the structure of atomic nuclei. The remarkable discovery of the empirical linear relationship between the slope of the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect in DIS and the short-range-correlation (SRC) scaling factors $a_2$ in QE kinematics is naturally explained in terms of scale separation in effective field theory. This explanation has powerful consequences, allowing us to calculate and predict SRC scaling factors from ab initio low-energy nuclear theory. We present ab initio calculations of SRC scaling factors for a nucleus $A$ relative to the deuteron $a_2(A/d)$ and relative to $^3\rm He$ $a_2(A/^3\rm He)$ in light and medium-mass nuclei. Our framework further predicts that the EMC effect and SRC scaling factors have minimal or negligible isovector corrections.

# Ab initio short-range-correlation scaling factors from light to medium-mass nuclei

## Key claim
The empirical linear correlation between the EMC effect slope and SRC scaling factors is explained by scale separation in effective field theory, and this framework allows ab initio calculation of SRC scaling factors from low-energy nuclear theory. The authors predict that isovector corrections to both the EMC effect and SRC scaling factors are minimal or negligible, at the level of O((N-Z)/A N_c) suppression with N_c=3.

## Method
The authors used Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) for nuclei up to A=12 and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) for larger systems, both with local chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) with coordinate-space cutoffs R0=1.0 fm and R0=1.2 fm. Three-nucleon forces included two-pion-exchange contributions plus contact terms V_D and V_E, with two parametrizations for V_E (Eτ and E1). The SRC scaling factors were extracted from ratios of two-body central densities at small internucleon separation, exploiting the scheme and scale independence of these ratios.

## Key numbers
- SRC scaling factor a2(4He/d) = 3.60 ± 0.10 (statistical) ± 0.15 (systematic) for the local chiral N2LO interaction with R0=1.0 fm and Eτ 3N parametrization
- SRC scaling factor a2(12C/d) = 4.75 ± 0.10 (statistical) ± 0.20 (systematic) for the same interaction
- SRC scaling factor a2(16O/d) = 5.20 ± 0.15 (statistical) ± 0.40 (systematic) for the same interaction
- SRC scaling factor a2(40Ca/d) = 5.80 ± 0.20 (statistical) for the simplified AV4'+UIX_c potential
- SRC scaling factor a2(6He/d) = 3.90 ± 0.15 (statistical) for the local chiral N2LO interaction with R0=1.0 fm and Eτ 3N parametrization
- SRC scaling factor a2(6Li/d) = 4.10 ± 0.15 (statistical) for the same interaction
- The EMC effect slope -dR_EMC/dx is linearly correlated with a2-1, with the fit constrained to pass through the deuteron point (-dR_EMC/dx=0, a2=1)

## Context
This work extends the EFT framework of Chen and Detmold (2004) that predicted factorization of x and A dependence in nuclear structure functions, and the empirical discovery by Weinstein et al. (2010) and Hen et al. (2012) of the linear correlation between EMC effect slope and SRC scaling factors. The calculations use local chiral EFT interactions developed by Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) and three-nucleon forces from Tews et al. (2015) and Lynn et al. (2015, 2017). The results enable predictions for future experiments at Jefferson Lab and other facilities, particularly for 6He, 6Li, 16O, and 40Ca, and provide a framework for connecting low-energy nuclear structure to high-energy scattering observables.
