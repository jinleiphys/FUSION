# Core-excitation effects in three-body breakup reactions studied using the Faddeev formalism

> Previous studies of $(d,p)$ reactions in three-body (proton, neutron, nuclear core) systems revealed a nontrivial effect of the core excitation: the transfer cross section cannot be factorized into the spectroscopic factor and the single-particle cross section obtained neglecting the core excitation. This observable, up to a kinematic factor, is the angular distribution of the core nucleus in the $(p,d)$ reaction. The study of the core excitation effect for the most closely related observable in the $(p,pn)$ three-body breakup, i.e., the core angular distribution, is aimed in the present work. Breakup of the one-neutron halo nucleus in the collision with the proton is described using three-body Faddeev-type equations extended to include the excitation of the nuclear core. The integral equations for transition operators are solved in the momentum-space partial-wave representation. Breakup of 11Be nucleus as well as of model $A=11$ $p$-wave nuclei is studied at beam energies of 30, 60, and 200 MeV per nucleon. Angular and momentum distributions for the 10Be core in ground and excited states is calculated. In sharp contrast to $(p,d)$ reactions, the differential cross section in most cases factorizes quite well into the spectroscopic factor and the single-particle cross section. Due to different reaction mechanisms the core excitation effect in the breakup is very different from transfer reactions. A commonly accepted approach to evaluate the cross section, i.e., the rescaling of single-particle model results by the corresponding spectroscopic factor, appears to be reliable for breakup though it fails in general for transfer reactions.

## Key claim

In sharp contrast to transfer reactions, the differential cross section for three-body breakup reactions factorizes quite well into the spectroscopic factor and the single-particle cross section, making the rescaling of single-particle model results by the spectroscopic factor reliable for breakup even though it fails for transfer reactions.

## Method

The three-body system of proton, neutron, and nuclear core was treated using Faddeev-type integral equations extended to include excitation of the nuclear core in an extended Hilbert space with ground and excited states. The integral equations for transition operators were solved in the momentum-space partial-wave representation, with partial waves up to orbital momenta of 5, 5, and 8 for the neutron-core, proton-neutron, and proton-core pairs respectively, and total angular momentum up to 55. The breakup operator was obtained as a quadrature involving elastic and transfer operators, and the semi-inclusive differential cross sections for the core were calculated by integrating over the unobserved neutron-proton pair states.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the 10Be(0+) component in 11Be(1/2+) bound state: SF(0+) = 0.854
- Spectroscopic factor for the 10Be(2+) component in 11Be(1/2+): SF(2+) = 0.146
- Core excitation energy for 10Be: Δm_C = 3.368 MeV
- Deformation parameter for rotational quadrupole deformation: β2 = 0.67, deformation length δ2 = 1.664 fm
- Neutron separation energy for 11Be(1/2+): Sn = 0.504 MeV
- For the 11Be(1/2+) breakup at E/A = 60 MeV, the deviation D of the ratio R from unity stays below 10% in magnitude for the core angular distribution, while for the (p,d) transfer reaction it reaches almost 50% at forward angles
- For the 11Be(1/2+) breakup, the CX effect for the ground-state core integrated cross section (D̅) ranges from -2.3% to -3.4% depending on beam energy and optical potential
- For the p-wave model nucleus with Sn = 5.0 MeV at E/A = 60 MeV, the difference between CX and rescaled SP results at the peak of the transverse momentum distribution is 8.5%, reducing to 4.0% at E/A = 200 MeV
- Integrated three-body breakup cross section for 11Be(1/2+) at E/A = 60 MeV: σ(0+) = 67.1 mb, σ(2+) = 14.9 mb, total σ = 82.0 mb (using KD optical potential)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of core excitation effects in transfer reactions using the Faddeev formalism by Deltuva (2013, 2015, 2016, 2017) to the closely related (p,pn) three-body breakup reaction. It contrasts with earlier distorted-wave impulse approximation studies by Moro (2012) and extended continuum discretized coupled channels (XCDCC) calculations by Summers (2007) and Diego (2014, 2017), which found important core excitation effects for resonant breakup. The present work demonstrates that the factorization of cross sections into spectroscopic factors and single-particle results, which fails for transfer reactions, is reliable for breakup reactions, enabling the use of the simpler single-particle rescaling approach for analyzing breakup data.
