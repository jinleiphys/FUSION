# Crossover equation of state based on color-molecular-dynamics

> The equation of State for dense matter is studied with color molecular dynamics, in which hadron matter and quark matter are automatically distinguished only from quark color state. The quark-quark interactions are optimized to be consistent with saturation properties: symmetric energy, $L-$parameter, and incompressibility around nuclear density. In the calculations, the degrees of freedom of colors are solved at each numerical step, although the flavors are fixed as up or down quarks. The resultant mass-radius relations also satisfy the observational constraints such as the gravitational wave observations, NICER, and ``{\it the two-solar mass observations}" of neutron stars. In this model with the allowed parameter range, deconfined quark matter appears in the core of neutron stars via crossover. Although the current constraints from the observations are not enough to conclude whether quark matter appears at high-density region, our method would help to understand high-density material properties inside neutron stars in the future.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their color-molecular-dynamics (CMD) model, which automatically distinguishes hadron and quark matter from quark color states, produces equations of state consistent with observational constraints on neutron star mass-radius relations, but they acknowledge that the study remains at a qualitative level and current observations are insufficient to conclude whether deconfined quark matter appears at high density.

## Method

The authors perform color molecular dynamics simulations where constituent quarks with fixed up or down flavors and time-dependent color degrees of freedom are evolved via classical equations of motion derived from a Hamiltonian containing relativistic kinetic energy, a color confining potential, one-gluon exchange, meson-exchange potentials (sigma, omega, rho), and a phenomenological Pauli potential. The ground state at zero temperature is obtained through frictional cooling in a periodic box of 6 fm size, and the resulting energies per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter (ud matter) and neutron matter (udd matter) are fitted with sigmoid regression functions to construct the equation of state under charge-neutral and beta-equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 (normalization factor used in regression)
- Constituent quark mass m = 361.8 MeV, determined to reproduce nucleon mass M = 938 MeV
- String tension kappa = 0.75 GeV fm^-1 and QCD fine structure constant alpha_s = 1.25 for the color interaction
- Meson-quark coupling constants: g_omega = 5.46, g_sigma = 3.23, g_rho = 8.19
- Nonlinearity parameters: epsilon_omega = 0.20, epsilon_sigma = -0.13
- Gaussian wave packet width L_q = 0.37 fm; effective meson widths L_omega = 0.75 fm, L_sigma = 1.35 fm, L_rho = 1.30 fm
- Pauli potential parameters: q0 = 2.46 fm, p0 = 240 MeV, Cp = 131 MeV

## Context

This work extends the color-molecular-dynamics approach of Maruyama and Hatsuda (2000) and Akimura et al. (2005) by including relativistic kinetic energy and time-dependent color evolution, and by adding nonlinear quark-quark repulsions to satisfy astrophysical constraints. The authors compare their equation of state to the DD2 equation of state by Typel et al. and to the crossover equations of state constructed by Kojo et al. (QHC21A) which interpolate between chiral effective field theory nuclear equations of state and quark matter at high density. The method enables future study of thermodynamic quantities such as thermal conductivity that are difficult to obtain from purely interpolative approaches.
