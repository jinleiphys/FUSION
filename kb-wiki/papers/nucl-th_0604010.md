# TDHF fusion calculations for spherical+deformed systems

> We outline a formalism to carry out TDHF calculations of fusion cross sections for spherical + deformed nuclei. The procedure incorporates the dynamic alignment of the deformed nucleus into the calculation of the fusion cross section. The alignment results from multiple E2/E4 Coulomb excitation of the ground state rotational band. Implications for TDHF fusion calculations are discussed. TDHF calculations are done in an unrestricted three-dimensional geometry using modern Skyrme force parametrizations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism to incorporate the dynamic alignment of a deformed nucleus, caused by multiple E2/E4 Coulomb excitation of the ground state rotational band, into TDHF calculations of fusion cross sections for spherical+deformed systems, rather than assuming all relative orientations occur with equal probability.

## Method

The authors perform a semiclassical Coulomb excitation calculation for the deformed nucleus along the incoming branch of the Rutherford trajectory to determine the orientation probability at a finite internuclear distance (about 15 fm), where the TDHF run is initialized. They then carry out unrestricted 3D TDHF calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme parametrization for fixed initial orientations, and integrate the orientation-weighted fusion probability over impact parameter and Euler angles to obtain the total fusion cross section.

## Key numbers

- For the system $^{64}$Ni + $^{162}$Dy at $E_{cm}=265$ MeV in a central collision, the alignment ratio at $R=13.2$ fm is $dP(\beta=90^\circ)/d\Omega \ / \ dP(\beta=0^\circ)/d\Omega = 1.54$.
- For the system $^{16}$O + $^{22}$Ne at $E_{cm}=95$ MeV in a central collision, the alignment ratio at $R=14$ fm is $dP(\beta=90^\circ)/d\Omega \ / \ dP(\beta=0^\circ)/d\Omega = 1.007$.
- For $^{16}$O + $^{22}$Ne at $E_{cm}=95$ MeV, the maximum impact parameters for fusion are 6.35 fm ($\beta=60^\circ$), 6.55 fm ($\beta=70^\circ$), 6.83 fm ($\beta=80^\circ$), and 6.87 fm ($\beta=90^\circ$).
- For $^{16}$O + $^{22}$Ne at $E_{cm}=95$ MeV, the differential fusion cross sections $d\sigma_{fusion}/sin\beta d\beta d\alpha$ are 633 mb/sr ($\beta=60^\circ$), 673 mb/sr ($\beta=70^\circ$), 732 mb/sr ($\beta=80^\circ$), and 741 mb/sr ($\beta=90^\circ$).

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF collision studies that were limited to spherical systems or used axial geometry with the rotating frame approximation, which could not address orientation dependence. The authors' new unrestricted 3D TDHF code (Umar and Oberacker, 2006) enables these calculations. The formalism enables future TDHF studies of heavy-ion fusion with deformed nuclei, particularly for heavier systems where dynamical alignment is significant, and for reactions relevant to superheavy element synthesis.
