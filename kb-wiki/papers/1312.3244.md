# Chiral density wave in nuclear matter

> Inspired by recent work on inhomogeneous chiral condensation in cold, dense quark matter within models featuring quark degrees of freedom, we investigate the chiral density-wave solution in nu- clear matter at zero temperature and nonvanishing baryon number density in the framework of the so-called extended linear sigma model (eLSM). The eLSM is an effective model for the strong interaction based on the global chiral symmetry of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). It contains scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons as well as baryons. In the latter sector, the nucleon and its chiral partner are introduced as parity doublets in the mirror assignment. The eLSM simultaneously provides a good description of hadrons in vacuum as well as nuclear matter ground-state properties. We find that an inhomogeneous phase in the form of a chiral density wave is realized, but only for densities larger than 2.4 ρ0, where ρ0 is the nuclear matter ground-state density.

## Key claim
The authors find that an inhomogeneous chiral density wave phase is realized in nuclear matter within the extended linear sigma model (eLSM), but only for densities larger than 2.4 times the nuclear matter ground-state density, meaning the nuclear matter ground state itself remains homogeneous.

## Method
The authors employ the eLSM, an effective hadronic model based on chiral symmetry that includes scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons as well as nucleons and their chiral partners in the mirror assignment. They make a chiral density wave Ansatz for the condensates, where the sigma and neutral pion fields vary spatially as cosine and sine functions respectively, and minimize the mean-field effective potential with respect to the condensate amplitudes and the wave number, using parameters previously fixed to reproduce nuclear matter ground-state properties.

## Key numbers
- The onset of the inhomogeneous chiral density wave phase occurs at a chemical potential of 973 MeV, corresponding to a density of 2.4 times the nuclear matter ground-state density (2.4 ρ0).
- At the nuclear matter ground state (chemical potential 923 MeV), the chiral condensate takes values of 154.3 MeV (vacuum) and 149.5 MeV (nuclear matter), with a local minimum at 38.3 MeV corresponding to the inhomogeneous phase.
- At the transition to the chiral density wave phase, the wave number f jumps to 389.5 MeV, giving a modulation wavelength of approximately 1.5 fm.
- The compressibility of nuclear matter in the model is K = 197.0 MeV.
- A mixed phase of homogeneous and inhomogeneous matter exists between 2.4 ρ0 and 10.4 ρ0.

## Context
This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensation from quark models (such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studied by Nickel (2009) and Carignano (2012)) to a hadronic model that simultaneously describes vacuum hadron properties and nuclear matter ground-state properties. The result contrasts with earlier approaches without nucleon-nucleon tensor forces, which found inhomogeneous condensation already in the nuclear matter ground state. The onset density of 2.4 ρ0 is similar to the value of 2 ρ0 found in skyrmion matter by Ma (2013), and the work enables future studies of more general inhomogeneous phases and the inclusion of strange degrees of freedom at higher densities.
