# A solvable model for spin polarizations with flow-momentum correspondence

> We present an analytically solvable model based on the blast-wave picture of heavy-ion collisions with flow-momentum correspondence. It can describe the key features of spin polarizations in heavy-ion collisions. With the analytical solution, we can clearly show that the spin polarization with respect to the reaction plane is governed by the directed flow, while the spin polarization along the beam direction is governed by the ellipticity in flow and in transverse emission area. There is a symmetry between the contribution from the vorticity and from the shear stress tensor due to the flow-momentum correspondence. The solution can be improved systematically by perturbation method.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first analytically solvable model for spin polarizations in heavy-ion collisions, based on a modified blast-wave model with flow-momentum correspondence, which can capture the key patterns of both global and local spin polarization observed in experiments, but only under the condition that the parameters \(\alpha_{1}\), \(\rho_{2}\), and \(\epsilon\) are treated as small perturbations relative to \(\rho_{0}\).

## Method

The authors construct a modified blast-wave model (MBWM) with a flow velocity profile that includes a term \(\alpha_{1}\eta\cos\phi_{b}\) for directed flow and a term \(\rho_{2}\cos(2\phi_{b})\) for elliptic flow, along with an elliptic emission area parameterized by \(\epsilon\). Using the Boltzmann distribution under the flow-momentum correspondence (setting \(\eta=Y\) and \(\phi_{b}=\phi_{p}\)), they derive analytical expressions for the spin polarization vectors \(P^{y}\) and \(P^{z}\) from the thermal vorticity and shear stress tensor, and compute these as functions of \(\phi_{p}\) and \(p_{T}\) by integrating over the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

* Directed flow parameter \(\alpha_{1} = -0.05\) is extracted by fitting \(\Lambda/\overline{\Lambda}\) data in 10-40% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.
* For 30-40% central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the model parameters are: \(R = 9\) fm, \(T = 104\) MeV, \(\rho_{0} = 0.894\), \(\rho_{2} = 0.036\), \(\epsilon = 0.085\), \(\alpha_{1} = -0.05\), and \(\tau_{f} = 5.1\) fm/c.
* The decay parameters of \(\Lambda\) and \(\overline{\Lambda}\) are chosen as \(\alpha_{\Lambda} = -\alpha_{\overline{\Lambda}} = 0.732\).
* For \(P^{z}\) as a function of \(\phi_{p}\) in 20-60% central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the shear contribution is the dominant while the vorticity contribution has an opposite sign relative to the data.
* For \(P_{H} \equiv -P^{y}\), the vorticity is the dominant source and the shear contribution is negligible.

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave model previously used by the STAR collaboration (STAR, 2019) to explain spin polarization along the beam direction, but improves upon it by providing a fully relativistic, analytically solvable framework that can also describe global spin polarization. The model addresses the "sign puzzle" of longitudinal polarization by including contributions from both thermal vorticity and the shear stress tensor, consistent with results from hydrodynamic models by Becattini (2021), Fu (2021), and Yi (2021). The analytical solution enables systematic improvements via perturbation methods and provides a clear demonstration that \(P^{y}\) is governed by directed flow while \(P^{z}\) is governed by elliptic flow and the ellipticity of the transverse emission area.
