# Delta-isobar relativistic meson exchange currents in quasielastic electron scattering

> We study the role of the $Δ$-isobar current on the response functions for high energy inclusive quasielastic electron scattering from nuclei. We consider a general Lagrangian which is compatible with contact invariance and perform a fully relativistic calculation in first-order perturbation theory for one-particle emission. The dependence of the responses upon off-shell parametrizations is analyzed and found to be mild. A discussion of scaling behaviour and a comparison with various non-relativistic approaches are also presented.

## Key claim

The Delta-isobar meson exchange current contribution to the transverse nuclear response in quasielastic electron scattering is destructive (reducing the total from the purely nucleonic answer) and becomes negligible at high momentum transfers above 2 GeV/c, while its effect on the longitudinal response is always negligible; however, the authors note that the net effect of all meson exchange currents (including pionic and Delta contributions) in the 1p-1h sector is almost vanishing at high momentum transfers due to cancellation between the two types.

## Method

The authors performed a fully relativistic calculation in first-order perturbation theory for one-particle emission, using a Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model with a general Lagrangian for the gamma-N-Delta vertex compatible with contact invariance. They computed the particle-hole matrix elements of the Delta current for both forward- and backward-going diagrams, including an energy-dependent width for the Delta resonance decay, and evaluated the longitudinal and transverse response functions for momentum transfers up to 3 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- At q = 0.5 GeV/c, the net effect of the MEC(Delta) contributions on the transverse response is 19% reduction relative to the pure RFG result.
- At q = 1 GeV/c, the net effect of the MEC(Delta) contributions on the transverse response is 18% reduction.
- At q = 2 GeV/c, the net effect of the MEC(Delta) contributions on the transverse response is 10% reduction.
- At q = 3 GeV/c, the net effect of the MEC(Delta) contributions on the transverse response is 4% reduction.
- The Fermi momentum used was k_F = 237 MeV/c, representative of a typical sd-shell nucleus.
- The monopole form factor cutoff was Lambda = 1300 MeV.
- The Delta resonance width at resonance was taken as Gamma_0 = 120 MeV.
- The coupling constants used were: f = sqrt(4*pi*0.08), f_{pi N Delta} = 4*0.564, G_1 = 4.2, G_2 = 4, G_3 = 1.
- The transverse response shows insensitivity to off-shell parameter variations, with the effect being at most 6-7%.
- Second-kind scaling violations of about 14% are predicted at the quasielastic peak when comparing k_F = 200 MeV/c with k_F = 250 MeV/c; for real nuclei such as C-12 versus Au-197, an effect of roughly 8% is predicted.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic calculations of pionic correlations and meson exchange currents in quasielastic electron scattering (Amaro et al., 2002a,b,c) by including the Delta-isobar current contributions that were neglected in those earlier studies. The results are compared with non-relativistic approaches (Alberico et al., 1990) and with the Peccei Lagrangian approach (Peccei, 1969), and the analysis of scaling behavior connects to the superscaling analyses of Donnelly et al. (1999a,b) and Maieron et al. (2002). The work enables future studies of 2p-2h contributions that may cancel the Delta-induced scaling violations, and provides a framework for testing Delta off-shell parameterizations against high-momentum-transfer electron scattering data.
