# Exploring the Phase Structure and Thermodynamics of QCD

> We put forward a Polyakov-loop extended quark meson model, where matter as well as glue fluctuations are taken into account, cf. [1]. The latter are included via a Polyakov-loop potential. Usually such a glue potential is based on Yang-Mills lattice data only. We show that a parametrisation of unquenching effects as proposed in [2], together with the inclusion of fluctuations via the functional renormalisation group [3,4], accounts for the relevant dynamics. This is demonstrated by a comparison of order parameters and thermodynamic observables to recent lattice results at vanishing chemical potential, where we find very good agreement.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Polyakov-loop extended quark meson (PQM) model, which includes both unquenching effects via a simple rescaling of the reduced temperature in the glue potential and fluctuations via the functional renormalisation group (FRG), can reproduce lattice QCD results for order parameters and thermodynamic observables at vanishing chemical potential, provided the open parameter \(T_{\rm cr}^{\rm glue}=210\) MeV is fixed by a comparison to the lattice pressure.

## Method

The authors use a \((2+1)\)-flavour Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model, coupling the chiral quark-meson sector to a Polyakov-loop potential. Unquenching effects are incorporated by rescaling the reduced temperature of the Yang-Mills-based Polyakov-loop potential using the relation \(t_{\rm YM}(t_{\rm glue}) \approx 0.57\, t_{\rm glue}\) from Haas (2013). Quantum and thermal fluctuations in the matter sector are included via the functional renormalisation group (FRG) flow equation for the effective potential, solved from an ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda=1\) GeV to the infrared. The resulting thermodynamic potential is used to compute the pressure, interaction measure, and order parameters, which are compared to lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations.

## Key numbers

* Chiral critical temperature from FRG: \(T_\chi = 172\) MeV, compared to lattice results of \(157\pm3\) MeV (Wuppertal-Budapest) and \(154\pm9\) MeV (HotQCD).
* Deconfinement critical temperature from FRG: \(T_d = 163\) MeV.
* The open parameter in the glue potential is fixed to \(T_{\rm cr}^{\rm glue}=210\) MeV by comparison to lattice pressure.
* The rescaling relation between reduced temperatures is \(t_{\rm YM}(t_{\rm glue}) \approx 0.57\, t_{\rm glue}\).
* The ultraviolet cutoff is set to \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV.
* The FRG results agree with lattice data up to approximately \(1.3\) times the critical temperature.

## Context

This work extends mean-field analyses of the PQM model by Haas (2013) that first applied the unquenching rescaling, by now including quantum and thermal fluctuations via the FRG. It builds on first-principles FRG calculations of the glue potential in pure Yang-Mills theory and 2-flavour QCD by Braun (2007, 2009) and Fister (2013), which revealed the shape similarity that motivates the rescaling. The results benchmark the model at zero chemical potential against lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2010, 2013) and HotQCD (Bazavov 2011, 2012), enabling future extension to the full QCD phase diagram at finite chemical potential.
