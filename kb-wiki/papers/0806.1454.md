# Quark-Model Identification of Baryon Ground and Resonant States

> We present a new classification scheme of baryon ground states and resonances into SU(3) flavor multiplets. The scheme is worked out along a covariant formalism with relativistic constituent quark models and it relies on detailed investigations of the baryon spectra, the spin-flavor structure of the baryon eigenstates, the behaviour of their probability density distributions as well as covariant predictions for mesonic decay widths. The results are found to be quite independent of the specific types of relativistic constituent quark models employed. It turns out that a consistent classification requires to include also resonances that are presently reported from experiment with only two-star status.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new classification of baryon ground states and resonances into SU(3) flavor multiplets, arguing that a consistent scheme requires including resonances with only two-star experimental status (not just three- or four-star states) and that the classification is largely independent of which specific relativistic constituent quark model is employed.

## Method

The authors solved the eigenvalue problem of the Poincare-invariant mass operator using the stochastic variational method for two different relativistic constituent quark models: a Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) model and a one-gluon-exchange (OGE) model. They analyzed the spin-flavor content of each eigenstate using flavor projection operators, examined spatial probability density distributions from the wave functions, and compared covariant predictions for mesonic decay widths (calculated via the point-form spectator model) to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon ground state shows a probability density distribution peaked at a root-mean-square radius of about 0.3 fm
- The GBE RCQM produces a theoretical mass of 1805 MeV for the J^P = 1/2^+ Xi state identified with the Xi(1690) resonance
- The GBE RCQM produces a J^P = 1/2^+ Lambda singlet at 1799 MeV, while the OGE RCQM produces it at 1957 MeV
- The Lambda(1405) singlet has an octet admixture of about one third (33%)
- The Lambda(1600) has a singlet contribution of 4%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous covariant studies of mesonic decay widths (Melde 2005, Melde 2006, Sengl 2007) which found that relativistic results systematically underestimate experimental data, in contrast to earlier nonrelativistic or relativized calculations by Stancu (1989), Capstick (1993), and others. The classification differs from the PDG assignments (which largely follow Samios 1974) and from the scheme by Guzey and Polyakov (2005), particularly regarding the identification of Sigma(1560) and Sigma(1620) as octet states rather than assigning Sigma(1750) to the lowest J^P = 1/2^- octet. The results enable a consistent multiplet classification that includes two-star resonances and provide benchmarks for further explorations of baryon structure.
