# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory for odd-mass nuclei with a time-odd constraint and application to deformed halo nuclei

> We show that the lowest-energy solution of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation has the even particle-number parity as long as the time-reversal symmetry is conserved in the HFB Hamiltonian without null eigenvalues. Based on this finding, we give a rigorous foundation of a method for solving the HFB equation to describe the ground state of odd-mass nuclei by employing a time-reversal anti-symmetric constraint operator to the Hamiltonian, where one obtains directly the ground state as a self-consistent solution of the cranked-HFB-type equation. Numerical analysis is done for the neutron-rich Mg isotopes with a reasonable choice for the operator, and it is demonstrated that the anomalous increase in the matter radius of $^{37}$Mg is well described when the last neutron occupies a low angular-momentum orbital in the framework of the nuclear energy-density-functional method, revealing the deformed halo structure.

## Key claim

The authors prove that the lowest-energy solution of the HFB equation always has even particle-number parity when time-reversal symmetry is conserved in the HFB Hamiltonian and no eigenvalues are zero; this provides a rigorous foundation for describing odd-mass nuclei by adding a time-odd constraint operator to the Hamiltonian, so that the ground state of an odd-mass nucleus is obtained directly as a self-consistent solution of a cranked-HFB-type equation rather than by the usual blocking procedure.

## Method

The authors derive a general relation between the particle-number parity, the Pfaffian of the HFB Hamiltonian, and the quasiparticle energies, showing that the number parity of the lowest-energy state equals the sign of the Pfaffian of the HFB Hamiltonian when time-reversal symmetry holds. They then implement a time-reversal anti-symmetric constraint operator in the HFB framework and solve the resulting cranked-HFB-type equations self-consistently using a non-relativistic Skyrme-type energy-density functional with time-odd fields, applied to neutron-rich Mg isotopes near the drip line.

## Key numbers

* The matter radius of \(^{37}\)Mg shows an anomalous increase that is well described when the last neutron occupies a low angular-momentum orbital, revealing a deformed halo structure.
* The number parity of the lowest-energy state is given by \(\pi_N^{\rm L.E.} = \sgn \operatorname{pf} \mathcal{H}_{\mathcal{X}}\), where \(\mathcal{H}_{\mathcal{X}}\) is the \(\mathcal{X}\)-transformed HFB Hamiltonian.
* The determinant of the Bogoliubov transformation matrix \(\mathcal{W}\) equals the number parity: \(\det \mathcal{W} = (-1)^{N_1}\), where \(N_1\) is the seniority number (number of unpaired particles).

## Context

This work extends the method initiated by Bertsch et al. (2009) for describing odd-particle systems under a time-odd constraint, providing a rigorous mathematical foundation based on the relationship between time-reversal symmetry and particle-number parity in HFB theory. It enables systematic, unified DFT calculations for odd-mass nuclei across the nuclear chart, including weakly-bound deformed halo nuclei such as \(^{37}\)Mg, which were previously observed experimentally by Takechi et al. (2014) and Kobayashi et al. (2014).
