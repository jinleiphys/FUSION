# Nucleon momentum distribution of $^{56}\text{Fe}$ from the axially deformed relativistic mean-field model with nucleon--nucleon correlations

> Nucleon momentum distribution (NMD), particularly its high-momentum components, is essential for understanding the nucleon--nucleon ($ NN $) correlations in nuclei. Herein, we develop the studies of NMD of $^{56}\text{Fe}$ from the axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. Moreover, we introduce the effects of $ NN $ correlation into the RMF model from phenomenological models based on deuteron and nuclear matter. For the region $ k<k_{\text{F}} $, the effects of deformation on the NMD of the RMF model are investigated using the total and single-particle NMDs. For the region $ k>k_{\text{F}} $, the high-momentum components of the RMF model are modified by the effects of $ NN $ correlation, which agree with the experimental data. Comparing the NMD of relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field models, the relativistic effects on nuclear structures in momentum space are analyzed. Finally, by analogizing the tensor correlations in deuteron and Jastrow-type correlations in nuclear matter, the behaviors and contributions of $ NN $ correlations in $^{56}\text{Fe}$ are further analyzed, which helps clarify the effects of the tensor force on the NMD of heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by introducing nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlation effects into the axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) model via two phenomenological methods (light-front dynamics and local density approximation), the calculated nucleon momentum distribution (NMD) of 56Fe can reproduce experimental data in the high-momentum region (k > kF), but only after the mean-field RMF results are corrected by these external correlation models.

## Method

The authors first calculated the NMD of 56Fe using the axially deformed RMF model with the NL3* parameter set, obtaining both total and single-particle momentum distributions for spherical (β=0.00) and deformed (β=0.17 and β=0.50) configurations. They then introduced NN correlation effects using two phenomenological approaches: the light-front dynamics (LFD) method, which rescales high-momentum components from the deuteron wave function, and the local density approximation (LDA) method, which incorporates Jastrow-type correlations from nuclear matter calculations. The RMF results were compared with non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations for 16O to analyze relativistic effects.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of 56Fe in the axially deformed RMF model has deformation parameter β = 0.17, where the binding energy is lowest.
- For the LFD method, the scaling factor CA for A > 12 nuclei lies in the range 4.0 < CA < 5.0, based on Ref. Ciofi degli Atti (1996).
- For the LDA method, the Jastrow correlation parameter α has a reasonable range 1.1 fm−1 < α < 1.7 fm−1, corresponding to wound parameter values 0.1 < κdir < 0.3.
- The high-momentum region (k > kF) contains approximately 20% of nucleons but approximately 70% of nuclear kinetic energy, based on prior work by Ohen (2014) and Polls (1994).
- Tensor correlations dominate the NMD in the range 1.5 fm−1 to 3.0 fm−1, based on prior ab initio calculations by Wiringa (2014) using AV18+UX potentials.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of NMD from non-relativistic mean-field models, specifically the Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations by Deguerra (1991) for Nd isotopes and Gaidarov (2009) for deformed nuclei, into the relativistic framework. The paper also builds on the LFD method developed by Antonov (2002) and the LDA method from Stringari (1990) and Flynn (1984) for incorporating NN correlations. The results enable future systematic studies of NMD in heavy deformed nuclei using relativistic models and help clarify the role of tensor forces versus short-range repulsion in the high-momentum components of heavy nuclei.
