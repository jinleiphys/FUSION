# The Representation Transformation of Multiquark Wave Functions

> It is shown that the representation transformations of multiquark wave functions between different coupling schemes are just the Racah coefficients of the permutation group. The transformation coefficients between the flavor-spin (FS) and the color-spin (CS) schemes are obtained. As an example, the expansion of the physical bases in terms of symmetry bases in the CS scheme are given for the interesting cases $YIJ = 201, 210, 000$.

## Key claim

The representation transformation coefficients between the flavor-spin (FS) and color-spin (CS) coupling schemes for multiquark wave functions are shown to be exactly the Racah coefficients of the permutation group S(n), up to a known phase factor, rather than requiring separate calculation of SU(n) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Method

The authors derive the transformation coefficients between the FS and CS coupling schemes by expressing the six-quark wave functions in each scheme using permutation group Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, then equating the two expansions. They compute the required Racah coefficients of S(n) for n up to 6 using a genealogical method based on existing S(n) ⊃ S(n-1) isoscalar factors from Chen et al. (1991), starting from trivial S(2) Racah coefficients. The transformation coefficients are then applied to expand physical bases (two-baryon cluster states) in terms of symmetry bases in the CS scheme for specific quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- For (YIJ)=(210): the NN physical basis expands into CS symmetry bases with coefficients −√(1/9), √(1/2), −√(1/4), −√(1/36), √(1/9), and 0 for the respective symmetry labels [ν][μ]β[f] = 1 9 3, 3 5 3, 3 4 3, 3 9₁ 3, 3 9₂ 3, and 3 11 3
- For (YIJ)=(201): the NN physical basis expands with coefficients −√(1/9), √(9/20), −√(16/45), √(1/36), −√(1/18), and 0 for symmetry labels 1 8 5, 3 3 5, 3 6 5, 3 8 5, 3 7 5, and 3 10 5
- For (YIJ)=(000): the ΛΛ physical basis expands with coefficients −√(3/40), √(1/40), √(27/80), and −√(27/160) for symmetry labels 1 9 3, 1 4 8, 3 5 3, and 3 4 3
- The Racah coefficients of S(6) are tabulated as squared values with signs, including entries such as 16/25, 9/25, 1/5, 4/5, and many others for various coupling combinations

## Context

This work extends the representation transformation study of Stancu (1989), who treated the u,d 2-flavor world for cases (YIJ)=(201) and (200), to the full u,d,s 3-flavor world. The authors reproduce Stancu's Tables VII and VIII but identify a phase inconsistency in Stancu's Table IX, showing that certain symmetry labels (such as [21111]) cannot appear in NN couplings due to the Littlewood rule. The results enable systematic calculation of dibaryon properties by transforming between the physical basis (which has definite dibaryon content asymptotically) and the symmetry basis (which is convenient for fractional parentage expansion techniques), building on the FS-scheme tables of Harvey (1981) and Ping, Wang, and Goldman (1994, 1995).
