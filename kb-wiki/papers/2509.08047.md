# Imaging the Jet-Induced Medium Response with Energy Correlators

> Quark-gluon plasma (QGP), when viewed at length scales of order the inverse of its temperature, behaves as a strongly-coupled liquid. However, when it is probed with sufficiently high momentum transfer, asymptotic freedom mandates the presence of quark- and gluon-like quasi-particles. High energy partons within jets can trigger these high-momentum exchanges, making jets valuable probes for revealing the presence of such quasi-particles. Such elastic scatterings are implemented in the Hybrid Model, where a jet parton that scatters is deflected, kicking a medium parton, which recoils. Before and after a scattering, as one and then both partons propagate through the medium, they lose energy and momentum, exciting wakes in the QGP droplet. We use two-point and three-point energy-energy correlators (EECs) to reveal the relevant angular regions at which (modified) parton showers and wakes in the QGP each dominate, offering a new way with which to visualize and constrain the corresponding dynamics. We compare our calculations to recent CMS and ALICE measurements of two-point EECs of charged-particle tracks in jets produced in PbPb collisions. We show that our calculations are closest to the experimental measurements when elastic scattering is included and when the elastically scattered recoil-partons produce their own wakes. We also propose a new variant of the measurement that is especially sensitive to jet wakes.

## Key claim

The Hybrid Model calculations of two-point energy-energy correlators (EECs) in PbPb collisions are closest to CMS and ALICE experimental measurements only when both elastic scatterings of jet partons off quasi-particles in the medium and the hydrodynamic wakes excited by the recoiling partons are included; excluding either component degrades agreement with data.

## Method

The authors implement the Hybrid Model, which combines perturbative parton showers from PYTHIA8 with a holographic energy loss formula for partons propagating through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and a Cooper-Frye prescription for the resulting hydrodynamic wakes. They additionally include 2-to-2 elastic (Molière) scatterings between jet partons and medium quasi-particles, where the deflected jet parton and the recoiling medium parton each subsequently lose energy and excite their own wakes. Two-point and three-point energy-energy correlators (EECs) are computed from charged-particle tracks in simulated jets and compared to CMS and ALICE measurements of PbPb/pp ratios.

## Key numbers

- PbPb/pp ratio of two-point EECs calculated for inclusive jets with 120 < pT < 140 GeV and R = 0.4, using charged-particle tracks with pT > 1 GeV, compared to CMS data.
- PbPb/pp ratio of two-point EECs calculated for charged-particle jets with 80 < pT < 100 GeV and R = 0.2, using charged-particle tracks with pT > 1 GeV, compared to ALICE data.
- Proposed extended EEC measurement uses a lower track-pT threshold of 150 MeV and extends the RL-axis to 2R (twice the jet radius).
- Three-point EEECs calculated for anti-kt R = 0.8 jets with 140 < pT < 240 GeV, projected onto shape coordinates (x,y) for two RL ranges: 0.2 < RL < 0.3 and 0.6 < RL < 0.7.

## Context

This work extends the Hybrid Model framework developed by Casalderrey-Solana (2014, 2016), Bossi (2024), and Kudinoor (2025), which previously used holographic energy loss and wake implementations. The inclusion of elastic scatterings follows the approach of Hulcher (2022). The paper compares directly to recent CMS (2025) and ALICE (preliminary) measurements of two-point EECs in PbPb collisions. The three-point EEEC analysis builds on Bossi (2024) to provide a shape-dependent probe that separates angular scale from triangular topology, enabling future experimental measurements that can distinguish between wake-dominated and elastic-scattering-dominated regions of phase space.
