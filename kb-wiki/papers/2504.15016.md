# Probing high-order deformation effects in neutron-deficient nuclei $^{246,248}$No with improved potential-energy-surface calculations

> The high-order deformation effects in even-even $^{246,248}$No are investigated by means of pairing self-consistent Woods-Saxon-Strutinsky calculations using the potential-energy-surface (PES) approach in an extended deformation space $(β_2, β_3,β_4,β_5,β_6,β_7, β_8)$. Based on the calculated two-dimensional-projected energy maps and different potential-energy curves, we find that the highly even-order deformations have an important impact on both the fission trajectory and energy minima, while the odd-order deformations, accompanying the even-order ones, primarily affect the fission path beyond the second barrier. Relative to the light actinide nuclei, nuclear ground state changes to the superdeformed configuration but the normally-deformed minimum, as the low-energy shape isomer, may still be primarily responsible for enhancing nuclear stability and ensuring experimental accessibility in $^{246,248}$No. Our present investigation indicates the nonnegligible impact of high-order deformation effects along the fission valley and will be helpful for deepening the understandings of different deformation effects and deformation couplings in nuclei, especially in this neutron-deficient heavy-mass region.

## Key claim
The paper finds that high-order even deformations (β₆, β₈) significantly affect both the fission trajectory and energy minima in ²⁴⁶,²⁴⁸No, while odd-order deformations (β₃, β₅, β₇) primarily influence the fission path beyond the second barrier. The abstract's claim that the normally-deformed minimum "may still be primarily responsible for enhancing nuclear stability" is hedged: the ground state actually shifts to a superdeformed configuration, with the normally-deformed minimum acting as a low-energy shape isomer.

## Method
Pairing self-consistent Woods-Saxon-Strutinsky calculations were performed using the potential-energy-surface (PES) approach in an extended axially symmetric deformation space (β₂, β₃, β₄, β₅, β₆, β₇, β₈). The macroscopic energy was computed from a liquid-drop model, while microscopic shell corrections used the Strutinsky method with a sixth-order Hermite polynomial and pairing correlations were treated via the Lipkin-Nogami method. Calculations sampled (25, 13, 13, 7, 5, 5, 5) grid points for each deformation parameter respectively, with a step size of 0.05, and two-dimensional projected energy maps and potential-energy curves were generated by minimizing over remaining deformation degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁴⁶No: normally-deformed minimum at (β₂=0.234, β₄=0.041, β₆=-0.017, β₈=-0.005) with energy -5.82 MeV; superdeformed minimum at (β₂=0.679, β₄=0.045, β₆=-0.005, β₈=-0.007) with energy -7.08 MeV.
- For ²⁴⁸No: normally-deformed minimum at (β₂=0.242, β₄=0.033, β₆=-0.021, β₈=-0.007) with energy -6.33 MeV; superdeformed minimum at (β₂=0.679, β₄=0.037, β₆=-0.003, β₈=-0.007) with energy -7.12 MeV.
- The smoothing parameter γ = 1.20 ħω₀ was used, where ħω₀ = 41/A¹/³ MeV.
- Basis sets with N ≤ 12 oscillator shells for protons and N ≤ 14 for neutrons were used for diagonalization.

## Context
This work extends previous PES calculations by the same group that considered deformation spaces up to (β₂, β₃, β₄, β₅) (Wang 2012, Li 2023, Song 2023, Wei 2024) to include β₆, β₇, and β₈. The results agree with ground-state deformations from Moller et al. (1995) who used a finite-range droplet model with deformations up to β₆. The study addresses conflicting predictions about fission stability: Lopez-Martens (2022) reported that fission half-lives drop by over five orders of magnitude between ²⁵²No and ²⁵⁴No, suggesting extremely short half-lives for neutron-deficient isotopes, while Rogov (2024) using a cluster model found ²⁴⁷,²⁴⁸No relatively stable. The present calculations enable deeper understanding of deformation couplings in the neutron-deficient heavy-mass region and may guide experimental searches for ²⁴⁶No.
