# Continuum-discretized coupled-channels method for four-body breakup reactions

> Development of the method of CDCC (Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels) from the level of three-body CDCC to that of four-body CDCC is reviewed. Introduction of the pseudo-state method based on the Gaussian expansion method for discretizing the continuum states of two-body and three-body projectiles plays an essential role in the development. Furthermore, introduction of the complex-range Gaussian basis functions is important to improve the CDCC for nuclear breakup so as to accomplish that for Coulomb and nuclear breakup. A successful application of the four-body CDCC to $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV is reported.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the extension of the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method from three-body to four-body breakup reactions, enabled by the pseudo-state method based on Gaussian expansion, and reports successful application to $^6$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV, but notes that Coulomb breakup was neglected in this specific application and was treated separately in later work.

## Method

The authors developed four-body CDCC by discretizing the continuum states of three-body projectiles (such as $^6$He = $n+n+^4$He) using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) to obtain bound and pseudo-states. For two-body projectiles, they introduced complex-range Gaussian basis functions to improve the description of highly oscillatory continuum wave functions needed for Coulomb breakup. The coupled-channel equations were solved using double-folding potentials constructed from the DDM3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction, with an imaginary part scaled by a single parameter $N_I$ fitted to elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- $^6$He ground state energy: $-0.98$ MeV for the $0^+$ state, and $0.72$ MeV for the $2^+$ resonance state, calculated using the Bonn A potential with adjusted $n-\alpha$ potential depth
- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 18 MeV: optimum imaginary potential parameter $N_I = 0.5$, same as for $^6$Li scattering at various energies
- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 229.8 MeV: optimum imaginary potential parameter $N_I = 0.3$
- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 229.8 MeV: convergence required pseudo-states with excitation energies $\epsilon_{nI} < 25$ MeV, with 44 ($0^+$) and 64 ($2^+$) pseudo-states in the sufficient case (b)
- For $^6$He+$^{12}$C at 18 MeV: pseudo-states with $\epsilon_{nI} < 12$ MeV were satisfactory
- Computation time for all bound and pseudo-state wave functions of $^6$He in case (c) (60 $0^+$ and 85 $2^+$ states): 10 minutes on FUJITSU VPP5000
- Bound and pseudo-states below 100 MeV satisfied 99.9% of the energy-weighted cluster sum-rule limit for monopole, dipole and quadrupole transitions

## Context

This work extends the three-body CDCC method developed by the same group for nuclear breakup of two-body projectiles (such as $^6$Li = $\alpha+d$) to four-body CDCC for three-body projectiles like $^6$He and $^{11}$Li. The pseudo-state method using Gaussian basis functions was previously tested for two-body projectiles in Kamimura et al. (2003) and showed agreement with the momentum-bin method. The four-body CDCC was later extended to include Coulomb breakup in a separate application to $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering at 19.0 and 22.5 MeV. The work enables systematic analysis of reactions induced by Borromean nuclei and, with the proposed hybrid eikonal-CDCC method, opens the door to treating Coulomb and nuclear breakup at intermediate energies for astrophysical applications such as determining the $S_{17}(0)$ astrophysical factor from $^8$B dissociation.
