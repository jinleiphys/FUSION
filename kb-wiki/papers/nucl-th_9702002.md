# Hadrons in Dense Matter

> Physical reason behind the mass-shift of vector mesons ($ρ$, $ω$, $ϕ$) in nuclear matter is discussed in the Walecka model and in QCD sum rules. Using analytic formulas for the mass-shift valid at low densities, it is shown that the energy dependent part of the sels-energy (wave function renomalization) in medium is a main source for the negative mass-shift, while the energy independent part (the plasma frequency) has relatively a small effect. Future experiments in GSI ($π^- + A \to X + ω$ ) and KEK-PS ($p + A \to X + ϕ$ ) to detect the twin peak structure of vector mesons in $e^+e^-$ spectrum are reviewed. Also, it is proposed a new possible experiment using a $S (strangeness) = 1$ vector meson $K^{*}(892)$ with its radiative decay $K^{*+}(892) \to K^+ + γ$.

## Key claim

The negative mass shift of vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) in nuclear matter at low density is primarily driven by the energy-dependent part of the self-energy (wave function renormalization, parameter B), while the energy-independent part (the plasma frequency, parameter A) has a relatively small effect; this conclusion depends on the validity of a low-density expansion and a smooth approximation for the self-energy.

## Method

The paper analyzes the vector meson propagator at rest in nuclear matter using a low-density expansion of the self-energy Σ(ω²) ≈ A + B ω², then derives approximate dispersion relations and pole shifts. Two theoretical frameworks are applied: the Walecka model, where A and B arise from particle-hole and nucleon-antinucleon excitations respectively, and QCD sum rules, where the pole shift is extracted from finite energy sum rules using density-dependent condensates such as the quark condensate and 4-quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- In the Walecka model, the approximate ω-meson pole shift formula is m*² ≈ m² + A [1 − (4m²)/(3π m_σ²) (g_σ²/4π)], where A = g_ω² ρ/M; the expansion neglects terms of O(m²/4M²) ≈ 15% or higher.
- From QCD sum rules, the linear fit for ρ and ω mesons gives m*_ρ,ω / m_ρ,ω = 1 − (0.16 ± 0.06) (ρ/ρ₀).
- From QCD sum rules, the linear fit for the φ meson gives m*_φ / m_φ = 1 − (0.023 ± 0.011) (ρ/ρ₀).
- An alternative Monte Carlo error analysis by Jin and Leinweber found m*_ρ,ω / m_ρ,ω = 1 − (0.22 ± 0.08) (ρ/ρ₀) and m*_φ / m_φ = 1 − (0.01 ± 0.01) (ρ/ρ₀).
- The bag model combined with the Walecka model by Saito and Thomas gives m*_ρ,ω / m_ρ,ω ≈ 1 − 0.09 (ρ/ρ₀).
- The approximate formula from QCD sum rules is valid only up to (0.2–0.3)ρ₀, with non-negligible non-linearity below ρ₀.
- The K*(892) meson has a total width of 50 MeV (corresponding to 3.9 fm⁻¹) and a radiative branching ratio Br(K⁺γ) = 1 × 10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson mass shifts in the Walecka model by Saito, Maruyama and Soutome, and by Kurasawa and Suzuki, and develops the QCD sum rule approach first formulated by Hatsuda and Lee. The predictions are consistent with the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis and with later Monte Carlo error analyses by Jin and Leinweber. The paper enables experimental searches for twin peak structures in lepton pair spectra at GSI (HADES with π⁻ + A → X + ω), KEK-PS (E325 with p + A → X + φ), and proposes a new experiment using the radiative decay K*⁺(892) → K⁺ + γ.
