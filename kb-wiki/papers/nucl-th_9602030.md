# Chaoticity and Dissipation of Nuclear Collective Motion in a Classical Model

> We analyze the behavior of a gas of classical particles moving in a two-dimensional "nuclear" billiard whose multipole-deformed walls undergo periodic shape oscillations. We demonstrate that a single particle Hamiltonian containing coupling terms between the particles' motion and the collective coordinate induces a chaotic dynamics for any multipolarity, independently on the geometry of the billiard. The absence of coupling terms allows us to recover qualitatively the "wall formula" predictions. We also discuss the dissipative behavior of the wall motion and its relation with the order-to-chaos transition in the dynamics of the microscopic degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a classical model of non-interacting particles in a multipole-deformed billiard, where the particles and the oscillating wall are coupled via a Hamiltonian that conserves total energy, induces chaotic single-particle dynamics for any multipolarity (L=0, 2, 3), contradicting the earlier claim by Blocki et al. that chaos appears only for L > 2. The dissipation of collective motion is present for all multipolarities, but the mechanism differs between monopole and higher modes.

## Method

The authors simulate a gas of 30 classical non-interacting nucleons (mass m = 938 MeV) moving in a two-dimensional potential well with an oscillating multipole-deformed surface described by R(θ) = R₀(1 + α P_L(cosθ)). The Hamiltonian couples particle motion to the collective coordinate α via a steep Woods-Saxon potential, and Hamilton's equations are solved with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm (time step 1 fm/c). Initial particle positions and momenta are drawn from a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution at T = 36 MeV, and the wall is initially perturbed by δα = 0.3 from equilibrium. Fractal correlation dimension D₂ of scatter plots quantifies the transition from regular to chaotic motion.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass m = 938 MeV; potential depth V₀ = 1500 MeV; diffuseness a = 0.01 fm
- Billiard radius R₀ = 6 fm; wall oscillation frequency Ω = 0.05 c/fm, giving wall period τ_w ≈ 125.66 fm/c
- Single-particle period τ_p ≈ 43.3 fm/c (from most probable speed v = √(2T/m) at T = 36 MeV)
- Number of particles A = 30; Inglis mass factor η = 1 for monopole (L=0), η = 10 for quadrupole (L=2) and octupole (L=3)
- Initial wall perturbation δα = 0.3; ensemble average over 1000 events
- Fractal correlation dimension D₂ evolves from 1 (regular) to 2 (fully chaotic) for all multipolarities
- For L=2 and L=3, two dissipative regimes appear: a fast rise in gas excitation energy lasting about 250 fm/c, followed by saturation
- In the uncoupled case, dissipation rates (slope b) depend strongly on multipolarity; in the coupled case, slopes are identical for L=2 and L=3

## Context

This work extends the classical billiard model of Blocki et al. (1978) and the "wall formula" predictions, which found no heating for quadrupole deformations and attributed dissipation to chaotic motion only for higher multipolarities. It also contrasts with the semiclassical Vlasov calculations of Bauer et al. (1990), which showed regular undamped collective motion coexisting with weakly chaotic single-particle dynamics. The key advance is that including coupling between particles and the oscillating wall (conserving total energy) produces chaotic dynamics and dissipation for all multipolarities, suggesting that one-body dissipation mechanisms may be more universal than previously thought. The authors note that their model involves real particle-wall collisions absent in mean-field calculations, and plan future studies to explore consequences for nuclear damping.
