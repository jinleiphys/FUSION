# Nuclear modification of leading hadrons and jets within a virtuality ordered parton shower

> The event generator based on the higher-twist energy loss formalism -- Modular All Twist Transverse-scattering Elastic-drag and Radiation (MATTER) -- is further developed and coupled to a hydrodynamic model for studying jet modification in relativistic nuclear collisions. The probability of parton splitting is calculated using the Sudakov form factor that is constructed by a combination of vacuum and medium-induced splitting functions; and the full parton showers are simulated, including both energy-momentum and space-time evolutions of all jet partons. With the assumption that partons below a virtual scale of 1 GeV is absorbed by the medium, this framework is able to provide a reasonable description of the nuclear modification of both leading hadrons and jets at high transverse momentum at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the CERN Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

The MATTER event generator, based on the higher-twist energy loss formalism, provides a reasonable description of nuclear modification of both leading hadrons and jets at high transverse momentum at RHIC and the LHC, but only under the assumption that partons below a virtual scale of 1 GeV are absorbed by the medium.

## Method

The authors developed a Sudakov-type parton shower model (MATTER) where parton splitting probabilities are calculated using a Sudakov form factor constructed from a combination of vacuum and medium-induced splitting functions. The medium-induced part is adopted from higher-twist energy loss calculations and treated as a perturbation on the vacuum part. The full parton showers are simulated including both energy-momentum and space-time evolutions, with the MATTER generator coupled to a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model (VISHNEW) for studying jet modification in relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers

- Partons below a virtual scale of 1 GeV are assumed to be absorbed by the medium
- Gluon transport coefficient values used: $\hat{q}_0 = 1.8$ GeV$^2$/fm (GW) or 3.0 GeV$^2$/fm (AZZ) at RHIC, and 0.9 GeV$^2$/fm (GW) or 1.5 GeV$^2$/fm (AZZ) at the LHC, scaled at $s_0 = 96$ fm$^{-3}$
- Quark transport coefficient $\hat{q}_q/T^3$ around 3.1 (GW) or 5.2 (AZZ) at RHIC and 1.6 (GW) or 2.6 (AZZ) at the LHC
- Minimum allowed virtuality set as $t_\mathrm{min} = 1$ GeV$^2$
- Starting time of QGP evolution set as $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm
- Specific shear viscosity $\eta/s = 0.08$ used to tune the hydrodynamic model
- Nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections: 42 mb at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200$ GeV and 64 mb at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV
- LO pQCD cross sections multiplied by a $K$-factor of 1.7

## Context

This work extends semi-analytical DGLAP evolution equation approaches to incorporate non-collinear splittings of finite energy partons and their effects on daughter parton trajectories through the medium. It also extends medium modification of energetic partons to high virtuality scale, a region neglected by on-shell transport models such as LBT (Cao 2017) and MARTINI (Schenke 2009). The MATTER framework is designed to be part of a multi-stage approach within the JETSCAPE collaboration (Cao 2017), where highly virtual partons first evolve in MATTER and then smoothly transition to transport models at low virtuality, enabling more complete descriptions of jet observables.
