# Quarkyonic matter in lattice QCD at strong coupling

> We study the phase diagram of quark matter at finite temperature and density in the strong coupling lattice QCD with one species of unrooted staggered fermions including finite coupling ($1/g^2$) effects for color SU($N_c$). We find that we may have partially chiral restored medium density matter at $N_c=3$, which would correspond to the quarkyonic matter suggested at large $N_c$.

## Key claim

The authors find that partially chiral restored medium-density matter, which would correspond to the quarkyonic matter suggested at large \(N_c\), may appear at \(N_c=3\) in strong coupling lattice QCD when next-to-leading order (\(1/g^2\)) effects are included and the quark number density is treated as an additional order parameter alongside the chiral condensate.

## Method

The authors perform a strong coupling expansion of lattice QCD with one species of unrooted staggered fermions, including next-to-leading order (\(1/g^2\)) plaquette effects for color SU(\(N_c\)). They introduce an extended Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to handle the new interaction terms, which allows them to introduce the quark number density as an auxiliary field order parameter. The effective potential is then evaluated in the mean-field approximation using the \(1/d\) expansion, and the phase diagram is determined by solving the stationary conditions for the chiral condensate and the quark number density simultaneously.

## Key numbers

* At \(6/g^2 = 4.5\) for \(N_c=3\) in the chiral limit, the highest temperature of the first order phase transition boundary decreases in the NLO-A and NLO-B treatments.
* The second order critical chemical potential \(\mu_c^{(2nd)}\) at \(T=0\) overtakes the first order one at \(6/g^2 \simeq 3.5\) in the NLO-C treatment.
* The quark number density \(\rho_q\) at low temperature is given by \(\rho_q/N_c = 2\sinh[N_c\tilde{\mu}/T] / (X_{N_c} + 2\cosh[N_c\tilde{\mu}/T])\), which tends to 0 when \(E_q > \tilde{\mu}\) and to \(N_c\) when \(E_q < \tilde{\mu}\).
* In the NLO-B and NLO-C treatments, the condition for medium density matter is found to be equivalent to \(\rho_q = (\mu - E_q)/\beta_{\tau}\), where \(\beta_{\tau} = d/N_c^2 g^2\).

## Context

This work extends the strong coupling lattice QCD approach of previous studies (e.g., Bilic (year), Jolicoeur (year), and Ohnishi (2007)) by including next-to-leading order \(1/g^2\) effects and introducing the quark number density as an explicit order parameter. The results support the quarkyonic matter scenario proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (year) for large \(N_c\), now demonstrated at \(N_c=3\) in a lattice-based framework. This enables future studies of the phase diagram evolution with finite coupling effects, and may help resolve the baryon mass puzzle noted by Bringoltz (year) and others, where the chiral transition occurs before nuclear matter is formed in the strong coupling limit.
