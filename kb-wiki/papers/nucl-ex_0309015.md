# Hard scattering at RHIC: Experimental review

> The most significant results on hard scattering processes in Au+Au, p+p, and d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV obtained after 3 years of operation at the BNL Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) are summarized. Hadron production at high transverse momentum (p_T) in central Au+Au collisions shows very different properties (production yields, flavor composition, azimuthal correlations, ...) in comparison to p+p, d+Au, and peripheral Au+Au collisions, and this fact constitutes arguably the most interesting outcome of the experimental program so far. Four main topics are covered in this report: (i) the depletion of high p_T inclusive hadron spectra, (ii) the ``anomalous'' baryon-meson composition at intermediate p_T's, (iii) the disappearance of away-side azimuthal (jet) correlations, and (iv) the large value of collective azimuthal (elliptic flow) correlations. Additionally, the observed high p_T ``Cronin enhancement'' in d+Au collisions will be discussed. A succinct comparison of experimental data to theoretical models is also provided.

## Key claim

The high transverse momentum hadron production in central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV shows substantial differences compared to p+p, d+Au, and peripheral Au+Au collisions, with the observed suppression patterns and particle composition anomalies pointing to strong medium effects in a deconfined QCD medium, though alternative interpretations based on initial-state gluon saturation or final-state hadronic reinteractions remain possible.

## Method

The four RHIC experiments (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR) measured inclusive high-pT hadron spectra, identified particle yields, and azimuthal correlations in Au+Au, p+p, and d+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV over three years of operation. The nuclear modification factor R_AA was computed as the ratio of Au+Au to p+p invariant yields scaled by the nuclear overlap function T_AB, and the centrality dependence was studied via the ratio of central to peripheral yields scaled by N_coll (R_cp). Azimuthal correlation measurements extracted jet fragmentation parameters and elliptic flow coefficients v_2.

## Key numbers

- High-pT inclusive charged hadron and π0 yields in central Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV are suppressed by a factor of 4–5 compared to p+p and peripheral Au+Au yields scaled by T_AB (or N_coll)
- The baryon-to-meson ratio p/π ≈ 1 in central Au+Au at intermediate pT (2–4 GeV/c) is 4–5 times larger than the perturbative p/π ≈ 0.1–0.3 ratio observed in p+p and e+e- collisions
- Mean jet fragmentation transverse momentum ⟨|j⊥y|⟩ = 373 ± 16 MeV/c in p+p at √s = 200 GeV
- Average parton transverse momentum ⟨|k⊥y|⟩ = 725 ± 34 MeV/c in p+p at √s = 200 GeV
- The extracted √⟨|p⊥²|⟩_pair = 1.82 ± 0.85 GeV/c from p+p azimuthal correlations
- The scaling exponent n = 6.3 ± 0.6 for xT scaling of π0 yields in Au+Au collisions
- The high-pT suppression in central Au+Au becomes approximately constant at R_AA ≈ 0.2 for pT = 5–10 GeV/c
- The critical energy density for QGP formation from lattice QCD is ε ≈ 0.7 ± 0.3 GeV/fm³
- The Bjorken energy density estimate for the 40–60% centrality bin is ε_Bj ≈ 1 GeV/fm³

## Context

This experimental review summarizes the first three years of RHIC operations, extending the study of hard scattering in heavy-ion collisions from CERN-SPS (Pb+Pb at lower energies, where Cronin enhancement R_AA > 1 was observed by WA98) and CERN-ISR (α+α collisions) to the much higher energy of √s_NN = 200 GeV at RHIC. The observed high-pT suppression (jet quenching) is interpreted within the framework of medium-induced radiative energy loss models (BDMPS, GLV) requiring large initial gluon densities dN^g/dy ≈ 1000, transport coefficients q̂_0 ≈ 3.5 GeV/fm², or effective energy losses dE/dx ≈ 14 GeV/fm, while the anomalous baryon enhancement at intermediate pT is explained by quark recombination models. The results enable quantitative constraints on the properties of the dense QCD medium produced in heavy-ion collisions and provide the baseline for future measurements at LHC energies.
