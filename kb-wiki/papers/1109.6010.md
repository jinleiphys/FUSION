# Jet Physics from Static Charges in AdS

> Soft interactions with high-energy jets are explored in radial coordinates which exploit the approximately conformal behavior of perturbative gauge theories. In these coordinates, the jets, approximated by Wilson lines, become static charges in Euclidean AdS. The anomalous dimension of the corresponding Wilson line operator is then determined by the potential energy of the charges. To study these Wilson lines we introduce a "conformal gauge" which does not have kinetic mixing between radial and angular directions, and show that a number of properties of Wilson lines are reproduced through relatively simple calculations. For example, certain non-planar graphs involving multiple Wilson lines automatically vanish. We also discuss the linear growth of the charges' imaginary potential energy with separation, and a relationship between Wilson line diagrams and Witten diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper shows that by mapping high-energy jets (approximated by Wilson lines) to static charges in Euclidean Anti-deSitter (AdS) space via radial coordinates, many properties of Wilson line anomalous dimensions, including pairwise structure and the vanishing of certain non-planar graphs, can be understood through simple classical energy calculations in AdS, exploiting the approximate conformal invariance of perturbative gauge theories.

## Method

The authors introduce radial coordinates where the Minkowski metric becomes a product space R × AdS, with the dilatation operator mapping to the Hamiltonian in AdS. They define a "conformal gauge" that eliminates kinetic mixing between radial and angular components of the gauge field, and use this to compute the anomalous dimension of multi-Wilson line operators by calculating the classical potential energy of static charges in AdS. The method is applied to one-loop and two-loop calculations, and a relationship between Wilson line diagrams and Witten diagrams is discussed.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop anomalous dimension for Wilson lines is given by Γ = −(α_s/π) Σ_{i<j} T_i·T_j ((β_{ij} − iπ) coth β_{ij} − 1), where β_{ij} is the cusp angle defined by cosh β_{ij} = n_i·n_j/|n_i||n_j|.
* In the lightlike limit n_i² → 0, the anomalous dimension becomes linear in the cusp angles: Γ = −Σ_{i<j} Γ^{ij}(α_s) β_{ij} + …, with β_{ij} → ∞.
* For massless jets at one loop, the anomalous dimension is Γ = (α_s/π) Σ_{i<j} T_i·T_j ln(μ²/(n_i·n_j Λ²)) + …, where Λ is an infrared regulator scale.
* The geodesic distance between two static charges in AdS equals the cusp angle β_{12}, as derived from the spatial metric d s²_AdS = dβ² + sinh²β dΩ₂².

## Context

This work extends the use of radial quantization techniques previously applied to high-spin operators in Alday (2007) and Belitsky (2003), and provides a geometric framework for understanding properties of Wilson line anomalous dimensions that were previously established through direct perturbative calculations, such as the pairwise structure conjectured by Becher (2009), Gardi (2009), and Chiu (2009). The conformal gauge introduced here enables simpler calculations of multi-loop contributions and suggests a connection between Wilson line diagrams and Witten diagrams in AdS/CFT, potentially enabling new computational techniques for jet physics in QCD and N=4 SYM.
