# Role of nuclear and electromagnetic fragmentation in the charge-changing reactions of 18O on carbon and lead targets at around 370 MeV/nucleon

> Charge-changing cross sections (CCCSs) of 18O on carbon (C) and lead (Pb) targets have been measured with an uncertainty of less than 4% at around 370MeV/nucleon. We evaluate the contributions of nucleon-nucleon (NN) and electromagnetic (EM) interactions to CCCSs by considering the direct proton removal process, the charged particle evaporation (CPE) after neutron removal, and the EM excitation. We conclude that the CPE accounts for 12.3% and 5% of CCCSs on C and Pb, respectively. Only less than 1% of CCCSs of 18O is attributed to the EM excitation. Further investigation of projectiles from 18O to 197Au on C, silver (Ag) and Pb targets at 300 and 900MeV/nucleon show that the contribution of EM to CCCSs on Ag and Pb increases with projectile mass numbers and incident energies, and can reach 10% for 197Au on Pb at 900MeV/nucleon. In contrast, the EM contribution to CCCS is negligible for all projectiles on C at both energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that charged particle evaporation (CPE) after neutron removal accounts for 12.3% and 5% of the charge-changing cross sections (CCCSs) of 18O on carbon and lead targets, respectively, while electromagnetic excitation contributes less than 1% at around 370 MeV/nucleon; however, the electromagnetic contribution becomes significant (up to 10.5%) for heavy projectiles like 197Au on lead at 900 MeV/nucleon.

## Method

The CCCSs of 18O on natural carbon and natural lead targets were measured using the transmission method at the External Target Facility of the Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou, with beam energies of 372(1) MeV/nucleon and 378(1) MeV/nucleon at the target midpoints. The contributions were decomposed into three components: direct proton removal calculated with the zero-range optical-limit Glauber model using harmonic-oscillator density distributions for 18O and 12C and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock densities for lead isotopes; charged particle evaporation after neutron removal computed using a maximum excitation energy of 42 MeV within the Fermi gas model; and electromagnetic dissociation evaluated using the equivalent photon method with E1 and E2 virtual photon spectra and photonuclear cross sections from quasiparticle random-phase approximation and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- CCCS of 18O on natural carbon at 372(1) MeV/nucleon: 863(21) mb (experimental), with calculated contributions of 764 mb (direct), 106 mb (CPE), and 0.2(19) mb (EM)
- CCCS of 18O on natural lead at 378(1) MeV/nucleon: 3386(137) mb (experimental), with calculated contributions of 3169 mb (direct), 169 mb (CPE), and 9.2(49) mb (EM)
- CPE accounts for 12.3% of CCCS on carbon and 5% on lead targets
- EM contribution to CCCS is less than 1% for 18O on both targets at around 370 MeV/nucleon
- EM contribution reaches 6.7% for 197Au on lead at 300 MeV/nucleon and 10.5% at 900 MeV/nucleon
- EM contribution is negligible for all projectiles on carbon targets at both 300 and 900 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends previous CCCS measurements on light targets (Yamaguchi 2011, Zhao 2023) by providing the first systematic evaluation of electromagnetic dissociation contributions to CCCS on heavy targets below 1 GeV/nucleon. The finding that CPE resolves the longstanding discrepancy between Glauber model predictions and experimental data (Estrad 2014, Bagchi 2019, Tanaka 2022) is confirmed for both light and heavy targets, while the negligible EM contribution on carbon targets validates the common practice of using carbon targets for proton radius determinations (Terashima 2014, Zhang 2024). The results enable more accurate extraction of nuclear structure information from CCCS measurements on heavy targets and provide benchmarks for reaction models used in medical physics and space science applications.
