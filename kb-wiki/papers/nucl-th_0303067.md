# Relativistic predictions of spin observables for exclusive proton knockout reactions

> Within the framework of the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA), we investigate the sensitivity of complete sets of polarization transfer observables for exclusive proton knockout from the 3s$_{1/2}$, 2d$_{3/2}$ and 2d$_{5/2}$ states in $^{208}$Pb, at an incident laboratory kinetic energy of 202 MeV, and for coincident coplanar scattering angles ($28.0^{\circ}$, $-54.6^{\circ}$), to different distorting optical potentials, finite-range (FR) versus zero-range (ZR) approximations to the DWIA, as well as medium-modified meson-nucleon coupling constants and meson masses. Results are also compared to the nonrelativistic DWIA predictions based on the Schrödinger equation.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first relativistic and nonrelativistic predictions of complete sets of polarization transfer observables for exclusive proton knockout, but the authors caution that no published spin observable data other than analyzing power exist for the reaction kinematics studied, so the predictions serve to demonstrate the discriminatory power of such measurements rather than to validate a specific model.

## Method
The authors employed the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using both finite-range (FR) and zero-range (ZR) approximations for the NN interaction, with scattering wave functions generated from global Dirac optical potentials. They compared these relativistic predictions to nonrelativistic Schrodinger-based DWIA calculations using the THREEDEE code of Chant and Roos, and investigated sensitivity to nuclear medium modifications by applying the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture with reductions of meson-nucleon coupling constants and meson masses ranging from 0% to 20%.

## Key numbers
- Incident laboratory kinetic energy: 202 MeV for proton knockout from 3s$_{1/2}$, 2d$_{3/2}$, and 2d$_{5/2}$ states in $^{208}$Pb
- Coincident coplanar scattering angles: ($28.0^{\circ}$, $-54.6^{\circ}$)
- Nuclear distortion reduces unpolarized triple differential cross section to about 5% of its plane wave value for $^{208}$Pb
- Differences in optical potential parameter sets translate to an uncertainty of 10% in unpolarized cross sections
- Effective mean densities for the knockout reactions estimated between 0.08 and 0.15 of saturation density
- For values of $\xi = \chi < 0.8$ (i.e., greater than 20% reduction), both FR-DWIA and ZR-DWIA models fail to reproduce experimental analyzing power data

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous relativistic DWIA analysis of analyzing power data (Hillhouse 2003) for the same reaction system, where they found that relativistic ZR predictions suggested free NN scattering amplitudes were adequate while FR predictions implied 10% to 20% medium modifications were essential. The paper also addresses the failure of nonrelativistic Schrodinger-based DWIA predictions reported by Ne02 to reproduce the 3s$_{1/2}$ and 2d$_{3/2}$ analyzing powers. The predictions enable future experimental measurements of complete polarization transfer observables to discriminate between relativistic and nonrelativistic dynamics and to resolve ambiguities about nuclear medium effects on the NN interaction.
