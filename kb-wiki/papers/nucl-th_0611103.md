# Scaling of Anisotropic Flows in Intermediate Energy and Ultra-relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Anisotropic flows ($v_2$ and $v_4$) of hadrons and light nuclear clusters are studied by a partonic transport model and nucleonic transport model, respectively, in ultra-relativistic and intermediate energy heavy ion collisions. Both number-of-constituent-quark scaling of hadrons, especially for $ϕ$ meson which is composed of strange quarks, and number-of-nucleon scaling of light nuclear clusters are discussed and explored for the elliptic flow ($v_2$). The ratios of $v_4/v_2^2$ of hadrons and nuclear clusters are, respectively, calculated and they show different constant values which are independent of transverse momentum. The above phenomena can be understood, respectively, by the coalescence mechanism in quark-level or nucleon-level.

## Key claim
Anisotropic flow scaling (number-of-constituent-quark for hadrons, number-of-nucleon for light nuclear clusters) holds in both ultra-relativistic and intermediate energy heavy ion collisions, but the ratio \(v_4/v_2^2\) differs between the two regimes: approximately 0.5 for nucleonic-level coalescence versus 3/4 for mesons and 2/3 for baryons in partonic-level coalescence, with the caveat that the measured charged-hadron ratio of about 1.2 at RHIC exceeds the simple coalescence prediction.

## Method
The study used two transport models: the AMPT model with string melting (parton scattering cross section 10 mb) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, and the IQMD model for \({}^{86}\)Kr + \({}^{124}\)Sn collisions at \(E_{\text{beam}}/A = 25\) MeV/u with impact parameter 7–10 fm. Anisotropic flows \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) were computed for hadrons (including \(\phi\) mesons) and for light nuclear clusters (A=1–4), and scaling with number of constituent quarks or number of nucleons was tested. Coalescence mechanisms at the quark level (for hadrons) and nucleon level (for clusters) were used to interpret the scaling.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV: \(\phi\)-meson \(v_2\) is non-zero for \(p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\) and significant for \(p_T > 2\) GeV/\(c\), comparable to \(K^+ + K^-\).
- For charged hadrons in Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the ratio \(v_4/v_2^2\) is approximately 1.2 (STAR data).
- For nuclear fragments in \({}^{86}\)Kr + \({}^{124}\)Sn at 25 MeV/u, \(v_4/v_2^2\) is approximately 0.5 for nucleons and for A=2 and A=3 clusters.
- In the AMPT model, parton anisotropic flows satisfy \(v_n(p_t) \sim v_2^{n/2}(p_t)\).
- The IQMD simulation used 50,000 events; freeze-out occurs around 120 fm/\(c\), with results extracted at 200 fm/\(c\).
- The nuclear mean field parameters: \(\alpha = -124\) MeV, \(\beta = 70.5\) MeV, \(\gamma = 2.0\) (hard EOS, incompressibility \(K=380\) MeV), \(C_{\text{sym}} = 32\) MeV.
- Cluster coalescence criteria: \(r_{\text{min}} \leq 3.5\) fm in coordinate space and \(p_{\text{min}} \leq 300\) MeV/\(c\) in momentum space.

## Context
This work extends the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow observed at RHIC (Adams et al., 2004) to include the \(\phi\) meson, and applies the same scaling concept to light nuclear clusters at intermediate energies using a nucleon-level coalescence model. It contrasts with earlier studies of directed flow mass dependence (Huang et al., 1996; Kunde et al., 1997) and provides the first theoretical exploration of \(v_4\) in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions. The results enable future experimental measurements of \(v_4/v_2^2\) for nuclear fragments to test whether the coalescence picture holds across energy scales, and highlight the need for improved coalescence models to match the RHIC charged-hadron ratio of 1.2.
