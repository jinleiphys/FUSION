# The nucleon properties in finite temperature and density with Gaussian fluctuations

> We investigate the properties of nucleons at finite temperature and density using a two-flavor quark meson model with Gaussian fluctuations that extend beyond the mean-field approximation. Our findings suggest that Gaussian fluctuations lead to a non-monotonic behavior of the nucleon mass as a function of temperature and density, which may play an important role in the study of the hadronization process of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Moreover, we observe an increase in the nucleon radius due to Gaussian fluctuations, suggesting an effective repulsive force akin to the Casimir effect, as observed in the gold-bromobenzene-silica system. This study offers new insights into how temperature, density, and quantum fluctuations affect the structure and properties of nucleons under extreme conditions.

## Key claim

Gaussian fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation cause the nucleon mass to exhibit a non-monotonic behavior as a function of temperature and density, and increase the nucleon radius more rapidly than mean-field predictions, suggesting an effective repulsive force analogous to the Casimir effect observed in the gold-bromobenzene-silica system.

## Method

The authors used a two-flavor quark meson model with a chiral soliton approach, embedding the soliton into a hot and dense homogeneous background described by a thermal effective potential. They calculated the thermodynamic potential including Gaussian fluctuations by decomposing meson fields into vacuum expectation values plus fluctuations, then solved the coupled radial soliton field equations in spherical coordinates numerically at various temperatures and chemical potentials. The nucleon mass and root mean square radius were extracted from the soliton solutions after subtracting the thermal vacuum contribution.

## Key numbers

- Critical End Point (CEP) located at T_c = 137 MeV, mu_c = 255 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV
- Constituent quark mass m_q = 500 MeV, sigma mass m_sigma = 1200 MeV
- Coupling constant g approximately 5.28, lambda approximately 82.1
- Nucleon "experimental" mass of 1086 MeV used for parameter tuning (an equal mixture of Delta and N)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon properties within the quark meson model that relied on the mean-field approximation (Birse 1984, Birse 1984, Zhang 2015, Jin 2015, Li 2018, Wang 2023). The inclusion of Gaussian fluctuations follows the approach of Mocsy (2004) for incorporating meson fluctuations beyond mean-field theory. The results show that the phase diagram changes from only first-order transitions (as in mean-field) to a combination of first-order and crossover transitions, similar to findings by Skokov (2010) who included fermion vacuum fluctuations. This work enables re-evaluation of hadronization simulations in heavy-ion collisions where hadron masses are typically treated as fixed.
