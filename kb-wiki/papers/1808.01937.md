# Proof of NRQCD Color Octet Factorization of P-Wave Heavy Quarkonium Production In Non-Equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC

> Recently we have proved NRQCD color octet factorization of S-wave and P-wave heavy quarkonia production at all orders in coupling constant in QCD in vacuum at high energy colliders in Eur. Phys. J. C76 (2016) 448 and in arXiv:1807.04722 [hep-ph] respectively. In this paper we extend this to prove NRQCD color octet factorization of P-wave heavy quarkonium production in non-equilibrium QCD at RHIC and LHC at all orders in coupling constant. This proof is necessary to study the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proves that NRQCD color octet factorization for P-wave heavy quarkonium production holds at all orders in the coupling constant in non-equilibrium QCD, extending previous proofs that were limited to QCD in vacuum. This proof is necessary for studying quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, but the paper does not claim that the quark-gluon plasma is definitively in non-equilibrium; rather, it argues that the plasma may be in non-equilibrium from beginning to end, making the non-equilibrium proof essential.

## Method

The proof uses the closed-time path (CTP) integral formalism to describe non-equilibrium QCD, combined with the background field method and SU(3) pure gauge background fields to handle infrared divergences. The author considers a color octet heavy quark-antiquark pair in a P-wave configuration interacting with a light-like eikonal line, and demonstrates factorization by performing gauge transformations on the path integral and showing that the non-perturbative correlation function becomes independent of the background field. The analysis is carried out at all orders in the coupling constant using the path integral formulation rather than fixed-order diagrammatic methods.

## Key numbers

- The temperature of the quark-gluon plasma is greater than or approximately 200 MeV (greater than approximately 10^12 K).
- The energy density of the quark-gluon plasma is greater than or approximately 2 GeV/fm^3, compared to approximately 0.15 GeV/fm^3 for normal nuclear matter.
- RHIC collides gold nuclei at center-of-mass energy per nucleon sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, with total energy 39.4 TeV.
- LHC collides lead nuclei at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV (second run), with total energy 1044.16 TeV.
- The hadronization time scale in QCD is approximately 10^(-24) seconds.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous proofs of NRQCD color octet factorization for S-wave heavy quarkonium production (Nayak, 2016, Eur. Phys. J. C76 448) and P-wave production in vacuum (Nayak, 2018, arXiv:1807.04722) to the non-equilibrium QCD environment relevant for heavy-ion collisions. The proof is motivated by the observation that the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC may remain in non-equilibrium throughout its evolution, making equilibrium-based calculations (such as finite-temperature lattice QCD used by Matsui and Satz for Debye screening) inapplicable. This enables future theoretical studies of heavy quarkonium production as a probe of non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma, building on the NRQCD color octet mechanism that has successfully explained experimental data from Tevatron, RHIC, and LHC in proton-proton collisions.
