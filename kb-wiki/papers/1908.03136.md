# QCD in the heavy dense regime for general $N_c$: On the existence of quarkyonic matter

> Lattice QCD with heavy quarks reduces to a three-dimensional effective theory of Polyakov loops, which is amenable to series expansion methods. We analyse the effective theory in the cold and dense regime for a general number of colours, $N_c$. In particular, we investigate the transition from a hadron gas to baryon condensation. For any finite lattice spacing, we find the transition to become stronger, i.e. ultimately first-order, as $N_c$ is made large. Moreover, in the baryon condensed regime, we find the pressure to scale as $p\sim N_c$ through three orders in the hopping expansion. Such a phase differs from a hadron gas with $p\sim N_c^0$, or a quark gluon plasma, $p\sim N_c^2$, and was termed quarkyonic in the literature, since it shows both baryon-like and quark-like aspects. A lattice filling with baryon number shows a rapid and smooth transition from condensing baryons to a crystal of saturated quark matter, due to the Pauli principle, and is consistent with this picture. For continuum physics, the continuum limit needs to be taken before the large $N_c$ limit, which is not yet possible in practice. However, in the controlled range of lattice spacings and $N_c$-values, our results are stable when the limits are approached in this order. We discuss possible implications for physical QCD.

## Key claim

The authors find that in the heavy dense regime of lattice QCD, for any finite lattice spacing, the transition from a hadron gas to baryon condensation becomes stronger and ultimately first-order as the number of colours $N_c$ is made large, and the pressure in the baryon condensed regime scales as $p\sim N_c$ through three orders in the hopping expansion, consistent with the conjectured quarkyonic matter phase.

## Method

The authors use an effective three-dimensional lattice theory derived from the standard Wilson action via combined strong coupling and hopping (inverse quark mass) expansions, valid for heavy quarks. They analytically evaluate the free energy density for general $N_c$ in the strong coupling limit to order $\mathcal{O}(\kappa^4)$ in spatial hoppings, using character expansion methods and integration formulas for $SU(N_c)$ group integrals. Thermodynamic functions including pressure, baryon number density, and energy density are then computed for varying $N_c$ values, with asymptotic analysis performed for large $N_c$.

## Key numbers

- The pressure in the baryon condensed regime scales as $p\sim N_c$ through three orders in the hopping expansion, compared to $p\sim N_c^0$ for a hadron gas and $p\sim N_c^2$ for a quark gluon plasma.
- For $N_f=1$ in the strong coupling limit, the static determinant gives $z_0 = 1 + (N_c+1)h_1^{N_c} + h_1^{2N_c}$, where the prefactor $N_c+1$ reflects spin degeneracy of baryons with $s=N_c/2$.
- For $h_1<1$ (below onset), the leading order pressure scales as $a^4 p_{\mathrm{LO}} \sim (1/N_\tau) N_c h_1^{N_c}$ for large $N_c$.
- For $h_1>1$ (above onset), the leading order pressure scales as $a^4 p_{\mathrm{LO}} \sim (2/N_\tau) \ln(h_1) N_c$ for large $N_c$.
- The saturation density in the static strong coupling limit is $2N_c$ quarks per flavour per lattice site, reflecting the Pauli principle.

## Context

This work extends the effective lattice theory approach previously developed for $SU(3)$ (Fromm et al., 2012; Bind et al., 2016) to general $N_c$, establishing contact with continuum large $N_c$ QCD considerations by 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979). The authors directly test the conjectured existence of quarkyonic matter proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007), which predicts a phase with pressure scaling $p\sim N_c$ and both baryon-like and quark-like aspects. The results enable future studies of the continuum limit for physical QCD, though the continuum limit must be taken before the large $N_c$ limit, which is not yet practically possible.
