# Resonances in chiral unitary approaches

> The extension of chiral theories to the description of resonances, via the incorporation of unitarity in coupled channels, has provided us with a new theoretical perspective on the nature of some of the observed excited hadrons. In this contribution some of the early achievements in the field of baryonic resonances are reviewed, the recent evidence of the two-pole nature of the Lambda(1405) is discussed and results on charmed baryon resonances are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is not a single state but has a two-pole nature, with the two poles having different masses, widths, and coupling patterns; this claim is supported by the observation that the $K^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ reaction produces a peak at 1420 MeV with a narrow width, while the $\pi^- p \to K^0 \pi \Sigma$ reaction produces a peak at 1395 MeV with a broader width, and these different shapes can be interpreted as evidence for the two poles.

## Method

The authors review chiral unitary approaches that extend chiral perturbation theory to higher energies by incorporating unitarity in coupled channels via the N/D method. The scattering amplitudes for meson-baryon interactions are constructed from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, and the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved on-shell to generate resonances dynamically. For the charm sector, the model uses a $t$-channel vector meson exchange mechanism that respects both chiral symmetry for light mesons and heavy-quark symmetry for charmed mesons, with the interaction supplemented by a scalar-isoscalar attraction term.

## Key numbers

* The two poles of the $\Lambda(1405)$ have masses around 1390 MeV and 1425 MeV; the lower pole has a width of about 130 MeV and couples mostly to $\pi \Sigma$, while the higher pole has a width of about 30 MeV and couples mostly to $\bar{K} N$.
* In the $K^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ reaction at a kaon laboratory momentum of 687 MeV/c, the $\Lambda(1405)$ peak appears at $M_I^2 = 2.02$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to $M_I = 1420$ MeV, with a pure resonant width of $\Gamma = 38$ MeV after background removal.
* The $\Lambda_c(2593)$ resonance is generated as a $DN$ $S$-wave molecular state with a binding energy of approximately 200 MeV and a width of approximately 3 MeV (model A) or approximately 5 MeV (model B).
* In the $C=1$, $I=1$ sector, the model generates a resonance near 2800 MeV with a width of around 30 MeV, which may correspond to the $\Sigma_c(2800)$ observed by the Belle Collaboration with a width of around 60 MeV (measured with more than 50% error).
* The $S=-2$ sector produces a pole at $1605 + i65$ MeV, identified with the $\Xi(1620)$ resonance.

## Context

This work reviews and extends the chiral unitary approach to baryon resonances, building on earlier studies that generated the $\Lambda(1405)$ dynamically from meson-baryon interactions (Kaiser 1995, Oset and Ramos 1998, Oller and Meissner 2000). The two-pole nature of the $\Lambda(1405)$ was first predicted in these chiral unitary models (Oller 2000, Garcia-Recio 2002, Jido 2003), and the present paper provides evidence from the $K^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ reaction (Magas 2005) that confirms this prediction. The extension to the charm sector follows the work of Hofmann and Lutz (2005) and Mizutani (2006), and the in-medium studies of $D$ mesons build on earlier work by Tolos (2004, 2005). The results enable future experimental tests of the two-pole nature of the $\Lambda(1405)$ in other reactions and provide a framework for understanding charmed baryon resonances and $D$ meson properties in nuclear matter.
