# The Proximal Chiral Phase Transition

> We consider the form of the chiral symmetry breaking piece of the effective potential in the linear sigma model. Surprisingly, it allows for a second local minimum at both zero and finite temperature. Even though chiral symmetry is not exact, and therefore is not restored in a true phase transition at finite temperature, this second minimum can nevertheless mimic many of the effects of a first order phase transition. We derive a lower limit on the height of the second minimum relative to the global minimum based on cosmological considerations; this limit is so weak as to be practically nonexistent. In high energy nuclear collisions, it may lead to observable effects in Bose-Einstein interferometry due to domain walls and to coherent pion emission.

## Key claim

The linear sigma model's effective potential can possess a second local minimum at both zero and finite temperature when the chiral symmetry breaking term includes a quadratic component in addition to the usual linear term, even though chiral symmetry is not exact and therefore cannot be restored in a true thermodynamic phase transition. This second minimum can mimic many effects of a first order phase transition, with cosmological constraints on the relevant parameters being so weak as to be practically nonexistent.

## Method

The authors analyze the linear sigma model with a generalized symmetry-breaking potential that includes both linear and quadratic terms in the sigma field, parametrized by coefficients epsilon_1 and epsilon_2. They compute the effective potential at zero temperature and at finite temperature using the one-loop high-temperature approximation, then derive conditions for the existence of a second minimum at theta = pi. They determine domain wall solutions using the sine-Gordon equation and calculate the critical bubble size for unstable walls, applying cosmological horizon arguments to constrain the parameters.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 140 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 94.5 MeV
- Sigma meson mass chosen as m_sigma = 1 GeV for definiteness
- Critical temperature for chiral restoration in the chiral limit: T_c = sqrt(2) f_pi, approximately 134 MeV
- Lower bound on epsilon_1 from cosmology: epsilon_1 > 3 x 10^{-13} MeV^3
- For epsilon_2 = 0.8 m_pi^2, critical bubble radius R_c approximately 4.5 m_pi^{-1}, with wall energy about 14 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) by Bjorken, Kowalski and Taylor (1993) and Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), which assumed only a linear symmetry-breaking term. It also relates to investigations by Gavin, Gocksch and Pisarski (1994) on DCC domain sizes and by Gavin and Muller (1994) on domain annealing. The paper enables new phenomenological studies of domain wall formation in heavy ion collisions, particularly for Bose-Einstein interferometry signatures of shell-like pion sources as studied by Pratt (1986), and suggests a possible mechanism for Centauro cosmic ray events.
