# Exploring the $Σ^+ p$ interaction by measurements of the correlation function

> The spin-dependent components of the fundamental hyperon-nucleon interactions are largely unknown. We show that under reasonable assumptions, a measurement of the $Σ^+ p$ correlation function in high-energy proton-proton or heavy-ion collisions combined with cross section data allows to separate the singlet and the triplet $S$-wave contributions.

## Key claim

The authors show that measuring the Σ⁺p correlation function in high-energy proton-proton or heavy-ion collisions, when combined with existing cross-section data, can separate the spin-singlet and spin-triplet S-wave contributions to the hyperon-nucleon interaction, but only under the condition that the interaction in one spin state is attractive and in the other is repulsive.

## Method

The authors compute the two-particle momentum correlation function C(k) for Σ⁺p using the Koonin–Pratt formalism with a Gaussian source function, solving the Schrödinger equation for the full scattering wave functions including Coulomb interaction. They compare results from two chiral effective field theory potentials (NLO19 and a simulated NLO(sim) with adjusted low-energy constants) that produce different combinations of singlet and triplet scattering lengths, and contrast the correlation function predictions with existing Σ⁺p elastic cross-section data from Eisele (1971).

## Key numbers

- The NLO19(600) potential gives a Coulomb-modified singlet scattering length a^c_s = -3.62 fm and triplet scattering length a^c_t = 0.47 fm; the NLO(sim) potential gives a^c_s = -2.39 fm and a^c_t = 0.80 fm.
- For a source radius R = 1.2 fm (typical of high-energy pp collisions at ALICE), the correlation function shows a pronounced difference between the two potentials in the momentum range 25 to 75 MeV/c.
- For a source radius R = 2.5 fm (typical of heavy-ion collisions at STAR), the signal is smaller and requires higher-precision data.
- The total cross-section χ² for both NLO19(600) and NLO(sim) is 0.4, meaning both reproduce the existing cross-section data equally well.

## Context

This work extends the use of correlation functions beyond the Λp system studied by STAR (Adams, 2006) and ALICE (Acharya, 2019; Acharya, 2021), where spin separation was not possible because singlet and triplet contributions add with the same statistical weight. The authors specifically target the Σ⁺p system, where lattice QCD calculations (Beane, 2012; Nemura, 2018) and chiral EFT potentials (Haidenbauer, 2013; Haidenbauer, 2019) predict an attractive singlet and repulsive triplet interaction. A successful measurement would constrain the Σ⁻n interaction via charge symmetry, with implications for neutron-star equation-of-state models where the role of Σ⁻ hyperons remains unsettled (Bombaci, 2016; Fabbietti, 2021).
