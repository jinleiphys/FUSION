# Neutron-proton interaction in rare-earth nuclei: Role of tensor force

> We investigate the role of the tensor force in the description of doubly odd deformed nuclei within the framework of the particle-rotor model. We study the rare-earth nuclei 174Lu, 180Ta, 182Ta, and 188Re using a finite-range interaction, with and without tensor terms. Attention is focused on the lowest K=0 and K=1 bands, where the effects of the residual neutron-proton interaction are particularly evident. Comparison of the calculated results with experimental data evidences the importance of the tensor-force effects.

## Key claim

The tensor force is an essential ingredient for explaining the odd-even shift (Newby shift) in K=0 bands and the odd-even staggering in K≠0 bands of doubly odd deformed rare-earth nuclei, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of a finite-range Gaussian interaction with parameters determined by Boisson (1976); a zero-range delta interaction fails to reproduce these effects regardless of parameter variation.

## Method

The authors performed complete Coriolis band-mixing calculations within the particle-rotor model for the doubly odd deformed nuclei 174Lu, 180Ta, 182Ta, and 188Re. They used a finite-range Gaussian neutron-proton interaction with and without tensor terms, adopting the parameter values of Boisson (1976), and compared the calculated spectra for the lowest K=0 and K=1 bands against experimental data from Headly (1998) and others.

## Key numbers

- For the K=0 band in 174Lu, the central part of the Gaussian force contributes only 1 keV to the Newby shift matrix element, while the tensor part contributes -26 keV, giving a total of -27 keV.
- For the K=0 band in 182Ta, the central contribution is 3 keV and the tensor contribution is 19 keV, giving a total matrix element of 22 keV.
- For the K=0 band in 188Re (p 9/2[514] n 9/2[505]), the central contribution is -4 keV and the tensor contribution is -60 keV, giving a total of -64 keV.
- For the K=0 band in 188Re (p 5/2[402] n 5/2[503]), the central contribution is 0 keV and the tensor contribution is 47 keV, giving a total of 47 keV.
- The calculated Gallagher-Moszkowski splittings for 174Lu, 182Ta, and 188Re agree with experiment to within less than 50 keV in all but one case, where the discrepancy is 101 keV (but the experimental value has an uncertainty of 57 keV).
- A delta interaction with spin-spin strength v1 = -0.9 MeV gives a Newby shift matrix element of 22 keV for the 174Lu K=0 band, which has the wrong sign compared to experiment.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of 176Lu (Covello, 1997) to four additional doubly odd deformed nuclei, confirming that the tensor force is essential for describing Newby shifts and odd-even staggering in K=0 and K=1 bands. The results contradict the common practice of using zero-range forces (e.g., Reviol, 1999) and support the earlier findings of Boisson (1976) and Jones (1971) regarding the importance of tensor contributions. The study enables future investigations of more complex band-mixing situations, such as doubly decoupled bands, where the tensor force should not be neglected.
