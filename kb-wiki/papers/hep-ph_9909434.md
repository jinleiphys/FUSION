# Signals of the quark-gluon plasma in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> flatex qm99.tex, 2 files XIV International Conference on `Ultra-Relativistic Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions' Torino, Italie 1999-05-10 1999-05-15 May 10-15, 1999 [SPhT-T99/103]

## Key claim

While several anomalies in nucleus-nucleus collision data (J/Psi suppression, low-mass dilepton excess, strangeness enhancement) are consistent with quark-gluon plasma formation, the author argues it is "a bit premature" to definitively associate these anomalies with plasma production, as no unique signature has been established and theoretical understanding remains incomplete.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data from BNL/AGS and CERN/SPS heavy-ion collisions, comparing measured observables (transverse energy distributions, particle ratios, collective flow, interferometry) to theoretical predictions from lattice QCD, statistical models, and hadronic transport calculations. Specific anomalies in J/Psi production, dilepton spectra, and strangeness yields are examined against baseline expectations from nucleon-nucleon collisions and nuclear absorption models.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at AGS: approximately 1.3 GeV/fm^3 (from Bjorken formula with dE_T/deta = 200 GeV for central Au+Au, tau_0 = 1 fm/c)
- Energy density at SPS: approximately 3 GeV/fm^3 (from Bjorken formula with dE_T/deta = 450 GeV for central Pb-Pb, tau_0 = 1 fm/c)
- Critical temperature in pure SU(3) gauge theory: T_c approximately 264 MeV (determined with accuracy of a few percent)
- Critical temperature with dynamical quarks: T_c approximately 150-200 MeV
- Energy density at 2T_c: about 85% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit value
- J/Psi absorption cross section in nuclear matter: sigma_abs approximately 6 to 7 mb
- Energy per particle on the freeze-out line: about 1 GeV

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of QCD phase transitions and heavy-ion collisions established by lattice calculations (Boyd 1996, Laermann 1996) and earlier phenomenological work (Matsui and Satz 1986 on J/Psi suppression; Rafelski 1982 and Koch 1986 on strangeness enhancement). It summarizes the state of the field just before RHIC operations began, identifying open questions about whether observed anomalies at SPS energies constitute evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation. The paper enables future work by highlighting the need for systematic studies across different system sizes, beam energies, and centrality selections to distinguish plasma signatures from conventional hadronic effects.
