# Four-body dynamics in 6Li elastic scattering

> We analyze 6Li elastic scattering in a wide range of incident energies (Ein), assuming the n + p + alpha + target four-body model and solving the dynamics with the four-body version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). Four-body CDCC well reproduces the experimental data with no adjustable parameter for 6Li + 209Bi scattering at Ein = 24-50 MeV and 6Li + 208Pb scattering at Ein = 29-210 MeV. In the wide Ein range, 6Li breakup is significant and provides repulsive corrections to the folding potential. As an interesting property, d breakup is strongly suppressed in 6Li-breakup processes independently of Ein. We investigate what causes the d-breakup suppression.

## Key claim

Four-body CDCC reproduces experimental elastic scattering data for 6Li on 209Bi (24–50 MeV) and 208Pb (29–210 MeV) with no adjustable parameter, and the deuteron-breakup suppression previously observed near the Coulomb barrier is confirmed to persist across the entire energy range where 6Li breakup itself is significant.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation for the n + p + α + target system using the four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC). The internal structure of 6Li was constructed with the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) using three sets of Jacobi coordinates, the KKNN interaction for n-α and p-α, the Bonn-A interaction for n-p, and a three-body force tuned to reproduce the experimental binding energy and root-mean-square radius of 6Li. The model space included the ground state and discretized continuum states of 1+, 2+, and 3+ up to 10 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- 6Li ground state binding energy: −3.69 MeV (calculated) versus −3.6989 MeV (experimental)
- 6Li ground state matter root-mean-square radius: 2.43 fm (calculated) versus 2.44 ± 0.07 fm (experimental)
- d-α probability in the 6Li ground state: 0.70 (spectroscopic overlap 0.83), consistent with experimental estimate 0.85 ± 0.04
- Number of npα-dominant breakup states in the model space: 140; number of dα-dominant breakup states: 15
- Energy range of 6Li + 209Bi scattering analyzed: 24–50 MeV; 6Li + 208Pb scattering: 29–210 MeV
- Coulomb-barrier energy for 6Li + 209Bi: approximately 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous four-body CDCC analysis of 6Li + 209Bi near the Coulomb barrier (Watanabe 2012) to a much wider energy range and to a 208Pb target. It resolves the long-standing problem noted by Satchler and Love (1979) that double-folding potentials fail for weakly-bound projectiles like 6Li, by showing that four-body breakup effects provide repulsive corrections to the folding potential. The finding that d-breakup suppression is universal enables future effective three-body (d + α + target) descriptions of 6Li scattering, provided the d-target potential is taken as a single-folding potential without implicit d-breakup effects.
