# Solving the Richardson equations close to the critical points

> We study the Richardson equations close to the critical values of the paring strength g_c where the occurrence of divergencies preclude numerical solutions. We derive a set of equations for determining the critical g values and the non-collapsing pair energies. Studying the behavior of the solutions close to the critical points, we develop a procedure to solve numerically the Richardson equations for arbitrary coupling strength.

## Key claim

The paper derives a set of equations that allow one to determine *a priori* all critical values of the pairing strength \(g_c\) (where the Richardson equations become singular) and the corresponding non-collapsing pair energies, enabling stable numerical solutions near these points rather than relying on heuristic interpolation.

## Method

The authors transform the Richardson equations near a critical point by introducing symmetric sums \(S_p = \sum_{\alpha \in C_k} (2\eta_k - e_\alpha)^p\) for the \(M_k\) pair energies that collapse to \(2\eta_k\). They expand the singular terms as geometric series, derive a linear homogeneous system for the \(S_p\) at leading order, and obtain a determinantal condition (Eq. 8a) whose solutions yield the critical \(g_c\) values together with the non-collapsing pair energies from the remaining Richardson equations (Eq. 8b). They then compute derivatives at \(g_c\) to construct linear initial guesses for solving the equations at nearby \(g\) values.

## Key numbers

* For a \(6 \times 6\) lattice with 36 fermions (half filling, \(M=18\)), the cluster condition gives \(M_4 = 5\) pair energies collapsing at the single-particle level \(j=4\) (\(\varepsilon_4 = -1\)).
* First negative critical \(g\) values for the cluster at \(2\varepsilon_4 = -2\): \(g_c = -0.0384565\) (energy \(-47.6184\)) and \(g_c = -0.0391412\) (energy \(-49.5405\)).
* For the ground state, a critical point occurs at \(g_c = 0.170878\) corresponding to collapse at level \(j=2\) (\(2\varepsilon_2 = -6\)) with a cluster of \(M_2 = 5\) pair energies.

## Context

This work extends the exact Richardson solution of the pairing Hamiltonian, originally developed by Richardson (1963), by addressing the numerical instability that arises at critical pairing strengths where pair energies become equal to \(2\eta_k\). It improves upon the nonlinear transformation approach of Rombouts et al. (2004), which could cross critical regions but could not predict the critical \(g\) values themselves. The method enables systematic numerical solution of the Richardson equations for arbitrary coupling strength, with applications to ultrasmall metallic grains and Richardson-Gaudin integrable models.
