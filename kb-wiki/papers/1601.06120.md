# Fast spinning strange stars: possible ways to constrain interacting quark matter parameters

> For a set of equation of state (EoS) models involving interacting strange quark matter, characterized by an effective bag constant (B_eff) and a perturbative QCD corrections term (a_4), we construct fully general relativistic equilibrium sequences of rapidly spinning strange stars for the first time. Computation of such sequences is important to study millisecond pulsars and other fast spinning compact stars. Our EoS models can support a gravitational mass (M_G) and a spin frequency at least up to approximately 3.0 solar mass and approximately 1250 Hz respectively, and hence are fully consistent with measured M_G and spin frequency values. This paper reports the effects of B_eff and a_4 on measurable compact star properties, which could be useful to find possible ways to constrain these fundamental quark matter parameters, within the ambit of our EoS models. We confirm that a lower B_eff allows a higher mass. Besides, for known M_G and spin frequency, measurable parameters, such as stellar radius, radius-to-mass ratio and moment of inertia, increase with the decrease of B_eff. Our calculations also show that a_4 significantly affects the stellar rest mass and the total stellar binding energy. As a result, a_4 can have signatures in evolutions of both accreting and non-accreting compact stars, and the observed distribution of stellar mass and spin and other source parameters. Finally, we compute the parameter values of two important pulsars, PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J1748-2446ad, which may have implications to probe their evolutionary histories, and for constraining EoS models.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the first fully general relativistic equilibrium sequences of rapidly spinning strange stars using interacting quark matter equations of state, and shows that the perturbative QCD corrections parameter \(a_4\) significantly affects stellar rest mass and binding energy even though it has minimal effect on the mass-radius relation, potentially allowing observational constraints on both the effective bag constant \(B_{\rm eff}\) and \(a_4\) within the chosen model framework.

## Method

The authors used a modified MIT bag model equation of state for strange quark matter that includes perturbative QCD corrections up to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\), characterized by an effective bag constant \(B_{\rm eff}\) and a parameter \(a_4\) (with \(a_4 = 1\) corresponding to an ideal relativistic Fermi gas). They solved the Einstein field equations for rapidly rotating relativistic stars using the Green's function approach and a multi-iteration numerical scheme to obtain self-consistent two-dimensional equilibrium solutions, from which they computed gravitational mass, rest mass, equatorial radius, spin frequency, moment of inertia, and other stellar properties for sequences at fixed rest mass, fixed gravitational mass, and fixed spin frequency.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational masses in the static (non-spinning) limit: 2.09 \(M_\odot\) for EoS 1 (\(B_{\rm eff}^{1/4} = 138\) MeV, \(a_4 = 0.80\)), 2.07 \(M_\odot\) for EoS 2 (\(B_{\rm eff}^{1/4} = 138\) MeV, \(a_4 = 0.61\)), and 2.48 \(M_\odot\) for EoS 3 (\(B_{\rm eff}^{1/4} = 125.5\) MeV, \(a_4 = 0.80\))
- The equations of state can support spin frequencies up to approximately 1250 Hz and gravitational masses up to approximately 3.0 \(M_\odot\)
- For the maximum mass configuration in the static limit, the stellar radius is 3.74 \(r_g\) (where \(r_g = GM_G/c^2\)) and the innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO) is approximately 7 km (specific value from text truncated)
- The strange quark mass was fixed at \(m_s = 100\) MeV, with \(m_u = m_d = 0\) and \(m_e = 0\)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of non-spinning strange stars using the modified bag model (Fra01, Alf05, weis11) and spinning strange stars using other SQM equations of state (Bombacietal2000) by constructing the first fully general relativistic equilibrium sequences of rapidly rotating strange stars with interacting quark matter. The results enable future observational constraints on the fundamental QCD parameters \(B_{\rm eff}\) and \(a_4\) through measurements of compact star properties such as mass, spin frequency, radius, and moment of inertia, and may help probe the evolutionary histories of specific pulsars like PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J1748-2446ad.
