# Light Neutron-Rich Hypernuclei from the Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model

> We explore the systematics of ground-state and excitation energies in singly-strange hypernuclei throughout the helium and lithium isotopic chains --- from $^5_Λ$He to $^{11}_Λ$He and from $^7_Λ$Li to $^{12}_Λ$Li --- in the ab initio no-core shell model with importance truncation. All calculations are based on two- and three-baryon interaction from chiral effective field theory and we employ a similarity renormalization group transformation consistently up to the three-baryon level to improve the model-space convergence. While the absolute energies of hypernuclear states show a systematic variation with the regulator cutoff of the hyperon-nucleon interaction, the resulting neutron separation energies are very stable and in good agreement with available data for both nucleonic parents and their daughter hypernuclei. We provide predictions for the neutron separation energies and the spectra of neutron-rich hypernuclei that have not yet been observed experimentally. Furthermore, we find that the neutron drip lines in the helium and lithium isotopic chains are not changed by the addition of a hyperon.

## Key claim

The neutron drip lines in the helium and lithium isotopic chains are not changed by the addition of a hyperon, contrary to the naive expectation that the hyperon would provide additional binding to stabilize more neutron-rich isotopes. This conclusion is hedged by the fact that the absolute energies show a systematic variation with the regulator cutoff of the hyperon-nucleon interaction, while the neutron separation energies are robust.

## Method

The authors used the importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) for hypernuclei, including Lambda and Sigma hyperons explicitly, with a Hamiltonian based on two- and three-baryon interactions from chiral effective field theory. They employed a similarity renormalization group transformation consistently up to the three-baryon level to improve model-space convergence, and computed the four lowest states up to Nmax=12 with a basis frequency of hbar-Omega=20 MeV for hypernuclei throughout the helium and lithium isotopic chains.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 7_Lambda-He: -35.33(6) MeV for hbar-Omega=20 MeV with the 700 MeV/c cutoff
- Ground-state energy of 7_Lambda-He: -35.43(7) MeV for hbar-Omega=16 MeV and -35.17(10) MeV for hbar-Omega=24 MeV
- The 600 MeV/c cutoff overbinds helium hypernuclei by about 2 MeV relative to experiment
- Neutron separation energy of 9_Lambda-He: approximately 3.6 MeV, compared to 2.3 MeV in 8-He
- Neutron separation energy of 12_Lambda-Li: 1.2(4) MeV for the 700 MeV/c cutoff and 1.0(4) MeV for the 600 MeV/c cutoff
- The 600 MeV/c cutoff puts the 8_Lambda-He ground state 0.26(6) MeV below the 7_Lambda-He + n threshold

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ab initio IT-NCSM calculations for hypernuclei (Wirth 2014) to neutron-rich systems, and is the first systematic full ab initio treatment of these hypernuclei with chiral interactions. While some hypernuclei from these isotopic chains have been considered in a microscopic shell model by Millener (2012) and Gal (2013), this work provides predictions for neutron separation energies and spectra of neutron-rich hypernuclei that have not yet been observed experimentally, and enables future experimental searches at facilities like FAIR, FRIB, and RIBF.
