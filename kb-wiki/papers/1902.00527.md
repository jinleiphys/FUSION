# Enhancements and suppressions of CP violating effect in the nucleons, nuclei, and atoms

> The electric dipole moment (EDM) is a very sensitive probe of CP violation beyond the standard model, and it as been measured in many systems such as atoms, neutrons, etc. The EDM of composite systems may be sensitive to several elementary level CP violating processes, but the theoretical evaluations of the CP violation at different physical (atomic, nuclear, hadronic, elementary) hierarchies are required to unveil them. In this context, we are particularly interested in which CP violating processes are enhanced in a given system, or vice versa. In this proceedings contribution, we will give an overview of the enhancement and suppression of CP violation in processes contributing to the EDMs of composite systems.

## Key claim

This proceedings contribution provides an overview of how CP-violating effects are enhanced or suppressed across different physical scales (elementary, hadronic, nuclear, atomic) in composite systems, with the goal of relating experimental electric dipole moment (EDM) measurements to underlying new physics beyond the standard model. The author emphasizes that the sensitivity to specific CP-violating processes is highly system-dependent, and that quantitative evaluations at all hierarchies are required to disentangle new physics contributions.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework connecting elementary-level CP-odd operators (quark EDM, chromo-EDM, Weinberg operator, four-fermion interactions) to the EDMs of composite systems through renormalization group evolution, chiral effective field theory, and nuclear structure calculations. The discussion covers the relativistic enhancement of the electron EDM in heavy atoms and polar molecules, the chiral enhancement of the nucleon EDM from quark chromo-EDM and CP-odd four-quark interactions, the generation of CP-odd nuclear forces from pion-nucleon couplings, and the calculation of nuclear Schiff moments for diamagnetic atoms. The standard model contribution via the CKM matrix is also estimated.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement factor of the electron EDM is more than 500 times for the Tl atom and about 900 times for Fr.
- The effective electric field in polar molecules reaches 23 GV/cm for YbF and -79 GV/cm for ThO.
- The leading perturbative QCD evolution from 1 TeV to 1 GeV yields: isoscalar quark mass suppression factor 0.5; isoscalar quark EDM suppression factor 0.8; isoscalar chromo-EDM mixing coefficient -0.8; Weinberg operator suppression factor 0.16 with mixing terms -0.14 and +0.08.
- The nucleon pseudoscalar densities at leading chiral order are ⟨p|ūiγ₅u|p⟩ = 180 and ⟨p|d̄iγ₅d|p⟩ = -170.
- The nuclear spin matrix elements are ⟨¹²⁹Xe|σ_n|¹²⁹Xe⟩ ≈ 0.2 and ⟨¹⁹⁹Hg|σ_n|¹⁹⁹Hg⟩ ≈ -0.4, with 30% accuracy.
- The standard model contribution to the nucleon EDM is d_N ∼ 10⁻³² e cm, with CP-odd nuclear couplings Ḡ_π^(0,1) ∼ 10⁻¹⁷.
- The standard model CP-odd electron-nucleon interaction C_N^SP ∼ 10⁻¹⁷.
- The octupole deformed nuclei (²²⁵Ra, ²²³Rn) may show Schiff moment enhancement by more than three orders of magnitude.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for interpreting EDM measurements across multiple systems, building on the foundational work of Schiff (1963) on screening in neutral atoms, the parity doubling mechanism described by Labzovskii, Sushkov, and Kozlov (1994-1995) for polar molecules, and the chiral perturbation theory approach of Crewther et al. (1979) for nucleon EDMs. The work connects to ongoing experimental programs including neutron EDM measurements (Baker), atomic EDM experiments on ¹⁹⁹Hg (Graner), and proposed nuclear EDM measurements in storage rings (JEDI, Anastassopoulos). The author identifies that further quantitative studies are needed, particularly for the Weinberg operator contribution to the nucleon EDM and for the Schiff moments of heavy nuclei where theoretical uncertainties may exceed 100%.
