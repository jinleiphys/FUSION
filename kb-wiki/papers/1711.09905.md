# Vector boson tagged jets and jet substructure

> In these proceedings, we report on recent results related to vector boson-tagged jet production in heavy ion collisions and the related modification of jet substructure, such as jet shapes and jet momentum sharing distributions. $Z^0$-tagging and $γ$-tagging of jets provides new opportunities to study parton shower formation and propagation in the quark-gluon plasma and has been argued to provide tight constrains on the energy loss of reconstructed jets. We present theoretical predictions for isolated photon-tagged and electroweak boson-tagged jet production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV at the LHC, addressing the modification of their transverse momentum and transverse momentum imbalance distributions. Comparison to recent ATLAS and CMS experimental measurements is performed that can shed light on the medium-induced radiative corrections and energy dissipation due to collisional processes of predominantly quark-initiated jets. The modification of parton splitting functions in the QGP further implies that the substructure of jets in heavy ion collisions may differ significantly from the corresponding substructure in proton-proton collisions. Two such observables and the implication of tagging on their evaluation is also discussed.

# Vector boson tagged jets and jet substructure

## Key claim

Vector boson-tagged jet production and jet substructure modification in Pb+Pb collisions can be described by a perturbative QCD framework that accounts for both radiative and collisional energy losses, provided the jet-medium coupling strength is taken in the range g=2.0 to g=2.2. The tagging preferentially selects quark jets with approximately 80% purity at high transverse momentum, and the modification of tagged jet substructure differs substantially from that of inclusive jets.

## Method

The authors start from Pythia 8 leading-order p+p baseline cross sections for vector boson+jet production, then embed these in a heavy ion collision geometry using an optical Glauber model with inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections of 70 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. Medium modification is implemented via probability distributions for fractional energy loss from medium-induced multiple gluon emission, using splitting functions from soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon interactions (SCET_G), where only the fraction of energy redistributed outside the jet cone contributes to jet suppression. For jet substructure, the groomed momentum sharing distribution is evaluated using symmetrized splitting functions that include both vacuum and medium-induced components, and differential jet shapes are computed from the transverse energy profile within reconstructed jets.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair: sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section: sigma_in=70 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- Jet-medium coupling strengths used: g=2.0 and g=2.2, values that describe inclusive light hadron and jet suppression data
- Quark jet purity from gamma or Z0 tagging: approximately 80% at high pT
- For Z0+jet momentum imbalance shift Delta< x_JZ > in 0-30% central Pb+Pb collisions with pT_J>30 GeV: theoretical predictions range from 0.022 (pT_Z=40-50 GeV, g=2.0) to 0.119 (pT_Z=80-120 GeV, g=2.2); CMS data range from 0.061+/-0.059 to 0.124+/-0.052
- Jet shape calculations use jet pT>100 GeV and pseudorapidity 0.3<|eta|<2.0
- Groomed soft-drop jet parameters: beta=0, z_cut=0.1, Delta R_12>0.1
- Jet radius parameters used: R=0.3 and R=0.4

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative QCD studies of vector boson tagged jet production in heavy ion collisions by Dai (2012) and Neufeld (2012), and complements calculations using the Boltzmann transport model by Wang (2013), the JEWEL event generator by Kunnawalkam Elayavalli (2016), and the hybrid strong/weak coupling model by Casalderrey-Solana (2015). The theoretical predictions are compared to recent ATLAS and CMS experimental measurements at the LHC, including data from Sirunyan (2017), CMS (2016), and ATLAS (2016). The jet substructure calculations build on earlier work on jet shapes by Vitev (2008) and on groomed momentum sharing using the soft drop procedure by Larkoski (2014) and Dasgupta (2013). The results enable future experimental studies of gamma-tagged and Z0-tagged jet substructure to distinguish between inclusive and tagged jet modification patterns.
