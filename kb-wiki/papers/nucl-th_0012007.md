# Quark model derivation of a NN* (1440) potential

> We derive a $NN^*$(1440) potential from a non-relativistic quark-quark interaction and a chiral quark cluster model for the baryons. By making use of the Born-Oppenheimer approximation we examine the most important features of this interaction in comparison to those obtained from meson-exchange models.

## Key claim

The paper derives an \(NN^*\)(1440) potential from a non-relativistic quark-quark interaction using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, but the authors note that the Roper resonance is treated as a stable particle and its width would need to be implemented through coupling to the continuum for dynamical applications.

## Method

The authors start from a quark-quark potential consisting of confining, one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange, and one-sigma exchange terms, with parameters fixed from the \(NN\) case. They use harmonic oscillator wave functions for the nucleon and the Roper resonance (the latter as a radial excitation) and compute the baryon-baryon potential as the expectation value of the six-quark system energy minus the self-energy of the two clusters, including quark-exchange contributions via the antisymmetrizer.

## Key numbers

- The \(NN^*\)(1440) probability on the deuteron was found to be 0.003% for the \(^3S_1^{NN^*(1440)}\) component and 0.024% for the \(^3D_1^{NN^*(1440)}\) component, much lower than the \(\Delta\Delta\) components (approximately 0.25%).
- The one-pion exchange (OPE) interaction determines the very long-range behavior for interbaryon distances R > 4 fm; OPE together with one-sigma exchange (OSE) are responsible for the long-range part (1.5 fm < R < 4 fm).

## Context

This work extends previous quark-model derivations of \(NN \rightarrow N\Delta\) and \(N\Delta \rightarrow N\Delta\) potentials by Valcarce et al. (1995) to the \(NN^*\)(1440) system, providing a parameter-free prediction from the quark level rather than relying on meson-exchange models with adjusted coupling constants. The results enable future studies of reactions such as elastic \(\pi d\) scattering above the Roper threshold and deuteron breakup into \(NN^*\) channels, and the authors note that channels with odd \(f\) (e.g., \(^1S_0\) with \(T=0\)) exist for \(NN^*\) that have no counterpart in the \(NN\) system.
