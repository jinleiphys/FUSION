# Photoabsorption in nuclei

> We calculate the total photoabsorption cross section and cross sections for inclusive pion and eta photoproduction in nuclei in the energy range from 300 MeV to 1 GeV within the framework of a semi-classical BUU transport model. Besides medium modifications like Fermi motion and Pauli blocking we focus on the collision broadening of the involved resonances. The resonance contributions to the elementary cross section are fixed by fits to partial wave amplitudes of pion photoproduction. The cross sections for $NR \to NN$, needed for the calculation of collision broadening, are obtained by detailed balance from a fit to $NN \to NNπ$ cross sections. We show that a reasonable collision broadening is not able to explain the experimentally observed disappearance of the $D_{13}$(1520)-resonance in the photoabsorption cross section on nuclei.

# Photoabsorption on Nuclei

## Key claim
The authors show that a reasonable collision broadening of nucleon resonances, calculated within a semi-classical BUU transport model, is not able to explain the experimentally observed disappearance of the $D_{13}$(1520)-resonance in the total photoabsorption cross section on nuclei, contrary to earlier suggestions that collision widths of order 300 MeV might be responsible.

## Method
The authors use a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to calculate total photoabsorption cross sections and inclusive pion and eta photoproduction cross sections on nuclei ($^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca, $^{208}$Pb) for photon energies from 300 MeV to 1 GeV. Resonance contributions to the elementary $\gamma N$ cross section are fixed by fits to partial-wave amplitudes of pion photoproduction, and cross sections for $NR \to NN$ are obtained by detailed balance from fits to $NN \to NN\pi$ cross sections. Medium modifications applied include Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and collision broadening of the $P_{33}(1232)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, and $S_{11}(1535)$ resonances.

## Key numbers
- At nuclear density $\rho_0$, the collision width from $N\Delta \to NN$ at the $\Delta(1232)$ pole (M = 1.232 GeV) is about 25 MeV.
- At about 100 MeV above the $\Delta(1232)$ pole, the in-medium width has grown by about 50 MeV, mainly due to $N\Delta \to N\Delta$ scattering.
- Collision widths at the poles of the $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$ resonances are only of order 20–40 MeV.
- Fermi motion alone damps the $\Delta$-peak by about 100 $\mu$b per nucleon on $^{40}$Ca.
- Pauli blocking further decreases the $\Delta$-peak by about 100 $\mu$b per nucleon.
- Applying a $\Delta$-potential of $U_{\Delta} = -30 \, \rho/\rho_0$ MeV shifts the $\Delta$-peak to higher energies and decreases it by about 70 $\mu$b per nucleon.
- On $^{208}$Pb, the calculated $\pi^0$ production cross section overestimates experimental data in the $\Delta$-region by about 20\%.
- On $^{12}$C, the calculated eta photoproduction cross section at higher energies overestimates experiment by about 20\%; on $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb the agreement is very good.

## Context
This work extends the BUU transport model, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Wolf et al., Teis et al.) and pion-nucleus reactions (Engel et al.), to photonuclear reactions in the resonance region. The calculations contradict the suggestion by Kondratyuk et al. (1994) and Alberico et al. that a collision width of 300 MeV for the $D_{13}(1520)$ could explain its disappearance in nuclear photoabsorption. The results enable future investigation of alternative in-medium effects, such as a strong coupling of the $D_{13}$ to the $N\rho$ channel combined with a downward mass shift of the $\rho$-meson in the nuclear medium, or medium modifications of the $\gamma N \to N\pi\pi$ process.
