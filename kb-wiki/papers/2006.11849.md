# The nature of short-ranged correlations in nuclear systems

> We apply ideas of the parquet-diagram and optimized Fermi-hypernetted chain methods to determine the short-range structure of the pair wave function in neutron matter and compare these with Bethe-Goldstone results and those of low-order variational calculations. It is shown that the induced interaction, describing the exchange of density, spin, and tensor fluctuations, has a profound influence on the short-ranged structure of the pair wave function and, hence, on effective interactions in neutron matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the induced interaction, describing the exchange of density, spin, and tensor fluctuations, has a profound influence on the short-ranged structure of the pair wave function in neutron matter, and that this effect is particularly strong because the bare spin-singlet interaction is close to having a bound state (S-wave scattering length -18.7 fm), making the pair wave function very sensitive to small changes in the interaction.

## Method

The authors apply parquet-diagram summation methods, generalized to fermions, to compute the short-range structure of the pair wave function in neutron matter. They solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation with an energy-independent effective induced interaction derived from the static structure function, and compare results from fully state-dependent calculations with state-independent (central-only) approximations and with calculations omitting the induced interaction entirely. The calculations use the Reid v6 interaction at a Fermi momentum of 1.0 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers

- S-wave scattering length of the spin-singlet Reid interaction: -18.7 fm (indicating the two-body interaction is close to developing a bound state)
- Fermi momentum used in the calculations: KF = 1.0 fm^{-1}
- The Fermi-liquid parameter F0^a is positive and of order 1, consistent with previous calculations (PLB2491990, EPL171992, PhysRevC.87.014338)

## Context

This work extends the parquet-diagram and optimized Fermi-hypernetted chain (FHNC-EL) methods, previously applied to bosonic nucleons by Smith and Jackson (SmithSpin) and to fermions by the same authors (v3eos), to study operator-dependent correlations in neutron matter. It contrasts with the Bethe-Goldstone equation and low-order constrained variational (LOCV) methods of Pandharipande and Bethe (PandharipandeBethe), which include only minimal many-body effects and have given rather different answers. The results enable improved calculations of pairing properties and response functions in neutron matter, though the importance of non-parquet diagrams (corresponding to commutator diagrams in the wave function) remains an open issue, especially when core sizes differ across interaction channels as in the Reid and Argonne interactions.
