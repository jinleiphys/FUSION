# Transition Density and Pressure at the Inner Edge of Neutron Star Crusts

> Using the nuclear symmetry energy that has been recently constrained by the isospin diffusion data in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions, we have studied the transition density and pressure at the inner edge of neutron star crusts, and they are found to be 0.040 fm$^{-3}$ $\leq ρ_{t}\leq 0.065$ fm$^{-3}$ and 0.01 MeV/fm$^{3}$ $\leq P_{t}\leq 0.26$ MeV/fm$^{3}$, respectively, in both the dynamical and thermodynamical approaches. We have also found that the widely used parabolic approximation to the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter gives significantly higher values of core-crust transition density and pressure, especially for stiff symmetry energies. With these newly determined transition density and pressure, we have obtained an improved relation between the mass and radius of neutron stars.

# Transition Density and Pressure at the Inner Edge of Neutron Star Crusts

## Key claim

Using the nuclear symmetry energy constrained by isospin diffusion data from intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, the transition density and pressure at the inner edge of neutron star crusts are determined to be 0.040 fm⁻³ ≤ ρₜ ≤ 0.065 fm⁻³ and 0.01 MeV/fm³ ≤ Pₜ ≤ 0.26 MeV/fm³, respectively, in both the dynamical and thermodynamical approaches, but these values depend on the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy and are significantly lower than those obtained from the parabolic approximation to the equation of state.

## Method

The authors employed the momentum-dependent MDI interaction, based on the modified finite-range Gogny effective interaction, which was previously used to analyze isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions. They applied both the dynamical approach, which includes density-gradient (surface) terms and Coulomb interactions in the stability condition for homogeneous npe matter, and the thermodynamical approach, which considers only bulk stability conditions. The transition density and pressure were computed as functions of the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy, using both the full equation of state and its parabolic approximation for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Transition density range: 0.040 fm⁻³ ≤ ρₜ ≤ 0.065 fm⁻³, determined from the constrained L values using the full MDI equation of state
- Transition pressure range: 0.01 MeV/fm³ ≤ Pₜ ≤ 0.26 MeV/fm³, from the constrained L values using the dynamical approach with the full MDI equation of state
- Slope parameter L = 86 ± 25 MeV, extracted from isospin diffusion data and used to constrain the symmetry energy
- Neutron drip-out density: approximately 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³ or 0.00024 fm⁻³, cited as a relatively well-determined reference point
- Minimum radius constraint for the Vela pulsar: R ≥ 4.7 + 4.0 M/M⊙ km, derived from the upper bounds ρₜ = 0.065 fm⁻³ and Pₜ = 0.26 MeV/fm³ combined with the glitch constraint ΔI/I > 0.014
- Radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star constrained between 11.9 km and 13.2 km using the full MDI equation of state with x=0 and x=-1
- Previous constraint for Vela pulsar: R ≥ 3.6 + 3.9 M/M⊙ km, obtained by Link et al. (1999) using ρₜ = 0.075 fm⁻³ and Pₜ = 0.65 MeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the determination of neutron star crust-core transition properties by using the nuclear symmetry energy constrained from terrestrial heavy-ion collision experiments, specifically the isospin diffusion data analyzed by Tsang et al. (2004), Chen et al. (2005), and Li et al. (2005). The results contradict earlier estimates that used the parabolic approximation to the equation of state, such as those by Kubis (2007), which gave significantly higher transition densities and pressures, especially for stiff symmetry energies. The improved constraints on ρₜ and Pₜ enable a more stringent mass-radius relation for neutron stars, demonstrated here for the Vela pulsar using the glitch constraint from Link et al. (1999), and provide input for future studies of neutron star structure and pulsar glitch phenomena.
