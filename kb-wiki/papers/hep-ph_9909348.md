# Thermal Dilepton Radiation at Intermediate Masses at the CERN-SpS

> We investigate the significance of thermal dilepton radiation in the intermediate-mass region in heavy-ion reactions at CERN-SpS energies. Within a thermal fireball model for the space-time evolution, the radiation from hot matter is found to dominate over hard 'background' processes (Drell-Yan and open charm) up to invariant masses of about 2 GeV, with a rather moderate fraction emerging from early stages with temperatures $T\simeq 175-200$ MeV associated with deconfined matter. Further including a schematic acceptance for the NA50 experiment we find good agreement with the observed enhancement in the region 1.5 GeV~$<M_{μμ}<$~3 GeV. In particular, there is no need to invoke any anomalous open charm enhancement.

## Key claim

Thermal dilepton radiation from a fireball model can explain the observed excess in the intermediate-mass region (1.5–3 GeV) at CERN-SpS without invoking any anomalous open charm enhancement, with only a moderate fraction (about 25%) originating from the deconfined phase at temperatures T ≃ 175–200 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a thermal fireball model for central Pb(158 AGeV)+Pb collisions, using a trajectory in the baryon-chemical potential/temperature plane based on baryon-number and entropy conservation with a resonance gas equation of state for hadronic matter and an ideal QGP equation of state for the early phase. They computed thermal dilepton production rates using the partonic rate from quark-antiquark annihilation (valid for M ≥ 1.5 GeV via parton-hadron duality), integrated over the space-time evolution of the fireball with cylindrical hydro-type volume expansion. The calculated spectra were folded with a schematic acceptance for the NA50 experiment and compared to data together with Drell-Yan and open charm contributions.

## Key numbers

- Thermal radiation dominates over Drell-Yan and open charm backgrounds up to invariant masses of about 2 GeV in central Pb(158 AGeV)+Pb collisions.
- The initial temperature for the standard calculation was T_i = 192 MeV (corresponding to z_0 = 2.3 fm), cooling to T_fo ≃ 113 MeV within a total fireball lifetime of ∼14 fm/c.
- A calculation with higher initial temperature T_i = 221 MeV (z_0 = 1.5 fm) produced a similar thermal spectrum up to M ≃ 2 GeV, with visible increase only beyond 2 GeV.
- The QGP contribution to the thermal yield constitutes about 25% (slightly more with higher T_i) of the total thermal signal.
- Finite pion chemical potentials (μ_π = 60–80 MeV) increase the thermal yield by 30–40% compared to the μ_π = 0 case.
- The Drell-Yan normalization used a K-factor of 1.5 to comply with p-A data, with isospin corrections for Pb+Pb being as small as 3%.
- The schematic acceptance used E_cut = 12 GeV for single-muon energy thresholds to best reproduce NA50 detector simulations.

## Context

This work extends the same fireball framework previously applied to low-mass dilepton spectra from CERES/NA45 (Rapp and Wambach, 1999) to the intermediate-mass region measured by NA50, providing a consistent picture of dilepton production at CERN-SpS energies. It contradicts earlier suggestions of anomalous open charm enhancement as an explanation for the IMR excess, and differs from the transport model of Li and Gale (1998) which used only hadronic degrees of freedom without addressing possible QGP formation. The results enable further experimental tests including direct D-meson measurements and dedicated NA50 runs with reduced magnetic field to better isolate the thermal signal.
