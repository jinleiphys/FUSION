# Heavy Ion Collisions and the Density Dependence of the Local Mean Field

> We study the effect of the density dependence of the scalar and the vector part of the nucleonic self-energy in Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) on observables like the transversal flow and the rapidity distribution. The stability of nuclei in RQMD is greatly improved if the density dependence is included in the self-energies compared to a calculation assuming always saturation density of nuclear matter. Different approaches are studied: The main results are calculated with self-energies extracted from a Dirac-Brückner-Hartree-Fock G-matrix of a one boson exchange model, i.e. the Bonn potential. These results are compared with those obtained by a generalization of static Skyrme force, with calculations in the simple linear Walecka model and results of the Brückner-Hartree-Fock G-matrix of the Reid soft core potential. The transversal flow is very sensitive to these different approaches. A comparison with the data is given.

## Key claim

Including the full density dependence of the scalar and vector self-energies (beyond a linear one) in Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) greatly improves the stability of simulated nuclei, and the resulting transverse flow is sensitive enough to distinguish between different microscopic forces, with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) results lying closer to experimental data than static Skyrme forces.

## Method

The authors implemented four types of self-energies in RQMD: a static Skyrme force (soft equation of state), the linear Walecka model (QHDI), self-energies from non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations using the Reid soft-core potential, and self-energies from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations using the Bonn potential. The density-dependent coupling constants for the BHF and DBHF cases were fitted as functions of the local scalar density using Lagrange interpolation, then used in a local density approximation within the constrained Hamiltonian dynamics formalism. Simulations of Ar+KCl collisions at 1.8 GeV/A were performed and compared with experimental streamer chamber data from the BEVALAC.

## Key numbers

- Ar+KCl collision simulated at 1.8 GeV/A, with impact parameter range 0 ≤ b ≤ 2.3 fm
- The Gaussian width parameter L = 1.08 fm² was used for the nucleon density distributions
- The constraint weight function used Lc = 8.66 fm²
- DBHF and BHF results for transverse flow lie closer to experimental data than the Skyrme soft EOS, which cannot reach the experimentally measured magnitude of flow at this energy
- Skyrme force provides about 10 percent less Pauli blocking compared with the realistic forces

## Context

This work extends prior non-relativistic QMD simulations that used self-consistent potentials from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock G-matrix elements (Jaenicke 1992, Khoa 1992) into the relativistic domain, and extends earlier RQMD work (Sorge 1989, Maruyama 1991, Lehmann 1995) that had only used static Skyrme forces. The results show that the density dependence of self-energies from DBHF calculations (Brockmann 1984, ter Haar 1987) produces a linear energy dependence in the Schroedinger equivalent potential, yielding a repulsive interaction at high relative momenta that enhances sidewards flow. The authors note that agreement with data might be improved by going beyond a Fermi sphere approximation for the local momentum distributions, using two penetrating Fermi spheres as in Elsenhans (1992).
