# Perturbative QCD Analysis of the Nucleon's Pauli Form Factor F_2(Q^2)

> We perform a perturbative QCD analysis of the nucleon's Pauli form factor $F_2(Q^2)$ in the asymptotically large $Q^2$ limit. We find that the leading contribution to $F_2(Q^2)$ has a $1/Q^6$ power behavior, consistent with the well-known result in the literature. Its coefficient depends on the leading- and subleading-twist light-cone wave functions of the nucleon, the latter describing the quarks with one unit of orbital angular momentum. We also derive at the logarithmic accurary the asymptotic scaling $F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2) \sim (\log^2 Q^2/Λ^2)/Q^2$ which describes recent Jefferson Lab data well.

# Perturbative QCD Analysis of the Nucleon's Pauli Form Factor \(F_2(Q^2)\)

## Key claim

The leading contribution to the nucleon's Pauli form factor \(F_2(Q^2)\) scales as \(1/Q^6\) at asymptotically large momentum transfer, with a coefficient that depends on both leading-twist (twist-three) and subleading-twist (twist-four) light-cone wave functions, the latter describing quarks carrying one unit of orbital angular momentum. The authors also derive the asymptotic scaling \(F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2) \sim (\log^2 Q^2/\Lambda^2)/Q^2\) which they find describes recent Jefferson Lab data unexpectedly well, though they caution that asymptotic PQCD is unlikely to be the dominant contribution at the measured \(Q^2\) values of 3–5 GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative QCD (PQCD) calculation of the nucleon's Pauli form factor \(F_2(Q^2)\) at asymptotically large \(Q^2\) by computing leading-order Feynman diagrams with two hard-gluon exchanges. They expand the hard part of the diagrams in the quark transverse momenta \(\mathbf{k}_i^2/Q^2\) (collinear expansion), isolating the leading contribution from configurations where the initial or final state quarks carry one unit of orbital angular momentum while the other state has zero. The result is expressed in a factorized form involving twist-three and twist-four light-cone distribution amplitudes, with the hard-scattering kernels computed explicitly.

## Key numbers

* The leading power behavior of \(F_2(Q^2)\) is \(1/Q^6\) modulo logarithms, consistent with the helicity-flip suppression relative to \(F_1(Q^2) \sim 1/Q^4\).
* The asymptotic scaling of the ratio is \(F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2) \sim (\log^2 Q^2/\Lambda^2)/Q^2\).
* Using asymptotic wave functions with \(f_N = 5.3 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\lambda_1 = -2.7 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^2\), and \(\Lambda = (0.3\ \text{GeV})^2\), the estimate for \(Q^6 F_2^p(Q^2)\) is roughly \(0.6\ \text{GeV}^6\) for \(Q^2 = 5-20\ \text{GeV}^2\), about 1/3 of the Jefferson Lab data at \(Q^2 = 5\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* The strong coupling evolution yields a factor of \(\alpha_s^{32/(9\beta)}(Q^2)\) for \(F_1(Q^2)\) and \(\alpha_s^{8/(3\beta)}(Q^2)\) for \(F_2(Q^2)\), where \(\beta = 11 - 2n_f/3\).

## Context

This work extends the PQCD factorization formalism for nucleon form factors, previously applied to the helicity-conserving Dirac form factor \(F_1(Q^2)\) by Lepage and Brodsky (1980) and others, to the helicity-flip Pauli form factor \(F_2(Q^2)\). The calculation builds on the classification of light-cone wave functions including orbital angular momentum developed by Ji, Ma, and Yuan (2002, 2003) and the conformal expansion approach of Braun, Fries, Mahnke, and Stein (2000). The result enables future phenomenological analyses of the ratio \(F_2/F_1\) at moderate \(Q^2\), where the authors note that higher-order corrections and higher-twist effects must be included for a realistic comparison with Jefferson Lab data.
