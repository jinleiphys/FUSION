# Maximal Net Baryon Density in the Energy Region Covered by NICA

> There are several theoretical indications that the energy region covered by the proposed NICA accelerator in Dubna is an extremely interesting one. We present a review of data obtained in relativistic heavy ion collisions and show that there is a gap around 10 GeV where more and better precise measurements are needed. The theoretical interpretation can only be clarified by covering this energy region. In particular the strangeness content needs to be determined, data covering the full phase space ($4 π$) would be very helpful to establish the thermal parameters of a possible phase transition.

# Maximal Net Baryon Density in the Energy Region Covered by NICA

## Key claim

The energy region around 11 GeV center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair, which will be covered by the NICA accelerator, is where the highest net baryon density in relativistic heavy ion collisions is reached, and where a transition from a baryon-dominated to a meson-dominated hadronic gas occurs. However, the observed particle ratio variations (such as the "horn" in K+/pi+) are sharper than those calculated in thermal-statistical models, and NICA measurements are needed to clarify this discrepancy.

## Method

The paper reviews existing experimental data from relativistic heavy ion collisions and analyzes them within the thermal-statistical model framework, extracting chemical freeze-out temperatures T and baryon chemical potentials mu_B from particle yield measurements. The freeze-out curve is mapped onto the energy density versus net baryon density plane, and the contributions of mesons and baryons to the entropy density s/T^3 are separated to identify the transition between baryon-dominated and meson-dominated regimes.

## Key numbers

- The transition from a baryon-dominated to a meson-dominated hadronic gas occurs at a temperature T = 151 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 327 MeV, corresponding to an incident energy of sqrt(s_NN) = 11 GeV.
- The K+/pi+ ratio shows a clear maximum in the T-mu_B plane that hugs the chemical freeze-out line, with the largest observed value just barely compatible with this maximum.
- The entropy density divided by temperature cubed, s/T^3, is constant over the whole energy range, with separate contributions from mesons and baryons shown in the analysis.

## Context

This work extends the thermal-statistical model analyses of Cleymans and Satz (1990s) and Becattini (1999) by incorporating more recent data from the following decade, including results from NA49. The paper identifies a gap in measurements around 10-11 GeV that NICA is designed to fill, and connects the observed "horn" in particle ratios to a transition in the hadronic gas composition. The results enable future NICA experiments to test the existence of a quarkyonic phase, as discussed by McLerran (recently), and to determine the thermal parameters of a possible phase transition using full 4pi phase space data.
