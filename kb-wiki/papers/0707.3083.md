# A numerical method of solving time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation with Gogny interaction

> A numerical method to solve time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation is proposed to treat the case with Gogny effective interaction. To check the feasibility of the method, it is applied to oxygen isotope O20 and small amplitude oscillations are calculated. The conservation of the nucleon numbers as well as energy expectation value is demonstrated. The strength distributions of the small amplitude quadrupole oscillations are also shown.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a numerical method for solving the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equation with the Gogny interaction and demonstrates its feasibility on the oxygen isotope 20O, but the results are limited by a small basis space (maximum major shell quantum number N_shell = 4) and the omission of the Coulomb interaction.

## Method

The authors solve the TDHFB equation by expanding the time evolution operator as a power series up to tenth order in the time increment, using a predictor-corrector method at each time step to construct the intermediate TDHFB Hamiltonian. The method is applied to 20O using the Gogny force with the D1 parameter set, with the initial condition given by an impulse-type quadrupole operator applied to the static HFB ground state. The numerical basis consists of three-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenstates with N_shell = 4 and oscillator parameter hbar omega_0 = 16.0 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Time increment c Delta t = 0.2 fm, total time steps = 19000
- Excitation energy of 0.2 MeV is conserved to a good extent in the integration process
- Proton number is conserved within 10^{-11} (protons are in the normal state)
- Neutron number is conserved up to around 10^{-5} (neutrons are in the superconducting state)
- Parameter epsilon = 1.0 x 10^{-3} for the initial quadrupole impulse
- Power series expansions for initial conditions and time evolution are calculated up to N_max = 10
- Low-energy 2+ levels of 20O are located at 1.7 MeV, 4 MeV, 5 MeV, and 10 MeV (experimental values cited from literature)
- The lowest energy peak in the calculated strength distribution is around 3 MeV, and the high energy peak is around 24 MeV

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method to include pairing correlations through the TDHFB formalism, using the Gogny interaction which treats particle-hole and particle-particle channels on equal footing. The results are compared with quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations by Khan and collaborators that include continuum space, and with experimental data on 20O low-energy 2+ levels. The method enables future applications to deformed nuclei and aims to provide a self-consistent framework for studying collective excitations in exotic nuclei where pairing correlations are important.
