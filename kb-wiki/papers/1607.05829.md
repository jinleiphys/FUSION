# Next-to-next-to-leading order Skyrme interaction in nuclear matter: Nuclear bulk quantities at second order in perturbation theory

> We present the explicit form of the next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) Skyrme interaction in momentum space by including the fourth-order gradient potentials to the standard Skyrme interaction. With the N$^2$LO Skyrme interaction, we evaluate the second-order corrections to the nuclear bulk quantities of nuclear matter: equation of state (EoS) of isospin symmetric and pure neutron matter, density-dependent in-medium effective nucleon mass, isospin-asymmetry energy, pressure and incompressibility. These second-order contributions are ultraviolet (UV) divergent due to the zero range character of the interaction and renormalized using the techniques of dimensional regularization (DR) with the minimal subtraction scheme (MS). We adjust the 18 parameters of the interaction by performing a global fit to the nuclear bulk quantities. Besides the too strong dependence $k_F^{12}$ of several second-order corrections, a very good reproduction of a realistic nuclear matter saturation curve with all the nuclear bulk quantities in the density region $0.12<ρ<0.20\;$ fm$^{-3}$ is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by including fourth-order gradient potentials (N²LO Skyrme interaction) and second-order perturbative corrections, a good reproduction of the realistic nuclear matter saturation curve can be obtained in the density region 0.12 < ρ < 0.20 fm⁻³, but only if all nuclear bulk quantities are included in the fitting procedure; otherwise, the effective mass and isospin-asymmetry energy take unreasonable values. The abstract glosses over the fact that several second-order corrections exhibit a too strong k_F¹² dependence, which signals a problem of perturbativity.

## Method

The author extended the standard Skyrme interaction by adding all possible fourth-order gradient terms (s-wave, p-wave, d-wave, and higher-order spin-orbit potentials) in momentum space, resulting in an 18-parameter N²LO Skyrme interaction. Second-order corrections to nuclear bulk quantities (equation of state, effective mass, isospin-asymmetry energy, pressure, incompressibility) were computed analytically using many-body perturbation theory with 2p-2h excitations; ultraviolet divergences arising from the zero-range character were removed using dimensional regularization with the minimal subtraction scheme. The 18 parameters were adjusted via χ² minimization against the SLy5 phenomenological interaction as a benchmark, using 20 reference points in the density range 0.12–0.20 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- The second-order corrections to the equation of state of isospin symmetric nuclear matter scale as k_F¹², k_F¹⁰, and k_F⁸ (from the N²LO part), with the leading term proportional to k_F¹².
- The first-order equation of state for isospin symmetric matter is: E^(1)(k_F) = (3ħ²k_F²)/(10m) + (k_F³)/(4π²) { t₀ + (k_F²/10)[3t₁ + t₂(5+4x₂)] + (3k_F⁴/70)[12t₆ + 3t₇ + 2t₈(5+4x₈)] }.
- The first-order contribution to the effective mass parameter is: R^(1)(k_F) = (M k_F³)/(12π²) { 3t₁ + t₂(5+4x₂) + k_F²[12t₆ + 3t₇ + 2t₈(5+4x₈)] }.
- The second-order contributions to the equation of state of isospin symmetric matter from the N²LO part are: ΔE_Sk4^(2)(k_F) = (M/(ħ²π⁴)) (e₁s k_F¹² + e₂s k_F¹⁰ + e₃s k_F⁸).
- The fit was performed using 18 equidistant reference points for densities from 0.12 fm⁻³ to 0.200 fm⁻³, plus two additional points at 0.159 and 0.161 fm⁻³.
- The errors ΔQ_i in the χ² minimization were chosen equal to 1% of the reference quantities.

## Context

This work extends the beyond-mean-field treatment of nuclear matter using Skyrme interactions, following earlier second-order calculations with the simplified LO Skyrme interaction (Moghrabi 2015, Brenna 2011) and with the standard NLO Skyrme interaction (Orsay group, Kaiser 2013). It builds upon the N²LO pseudo-potential framework of Davesne (2015, 2016) by including non-gauge-invariant fourth-order spin-orbit terms that were previously discarded, and demonstrates that these terms contribute at second order. The work enables future inclusion of N²LO tensor forces and density-dependent terms, and highlights the need for pionic degrees of freedom to overcome the non-perturbativity problem indicated by the k_F¹² dependence.
