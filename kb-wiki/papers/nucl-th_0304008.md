# Phase transitions and critical points in the rare-earth region

> A systematic study of isotope chains in the rare--earth region is presented. For the chains (144-154)Nd, (146-160)Sm, (148-162)Gd, and (150-166)Dy, energy levels, E2 transition rates, and two--neutron separation energies are described by using the most general (up to two--body terms) IBM Hamiltonian. For each isotope chain a general fit is performed in such a way that all parameters but one are kept fixed to describe the whole chain. In this region nuclei evolve from spherical to deformed shapes and a method based on catastrophe theory, in combination with a coherent state analysis to generate the IBM energy surfaces, is used to identify critical phase transition points.

# Phase transitions and critical points in the rare-earth region

## Key claim

The paper claims that a global fit using the most general one- and two-body IBM Hamiltonian, combined with catastrophe theory, can identify critical phase transition points in rare-earth isotope chains, but the specific nuclei previously proposed as critical (such as 150Nd and 152Sm) are not necessarily the closest to criticality; instead, 148Nd and 150Sm are the best candidates, and the identification depends on the Hamiltonian parameter space used.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic least-square fit of excitation energies, E2 transition rates, and two-neutron separation energies for the isotope chains 144-154Nd, 146-160Sm, 148-162Gd, and 150-166Dy using the most general IBM Hamiltonian with up to two-body terms. For each chain, all Hamiltonian parameters except the single-particle energy epsilon_d were kept fixed across isotopes. The energy surfaces were generated using the coherent state formalism, and catastrophe theory was applied to classify phase transitions and locate critical points in the separatrix plane defined by two essential parameters r1 and r2.

## Key numbers

- 148Nd (boson number N=8) and 150Sm (boson number N=9) are the closest to the Maxwell set r13+, indicating proximity to a first-order phase transition critical point.
- 150Nd (N=9) and 152Sm (N=10) are close to the bifurcation set (r2 axis), not the Maxwell set, meaning they are near a region where two minima coexist but not at the critical point itself.
- 152Gd (boson number N=10) is also near the Maxwell set but less clearly than 148Nd and 150Sm.
- For Dy, no isotope is close to a critical point; the transition from spherical to deformed occurs between N=11 and N=12.
- 162Dy (N=15) is close to the Maxwell set but in the region where the denominator of r1 and r2 becomes negative, corresponding to degenerate maxima rather than minima.

## Context

This work extends the systematic IBM studies of rare-earth isotopes by Castanos et al. (1982), Frank et al. (1989), and Lopez-Moreno et al. (1996, 1998), who used similar global fitting approaches with general Hamiltonians. It also contrasts with the consistent Q formalism (CQF) studies by Chou et al. (1997) and Fossion et al. (2002), which used restricted Hamiltonians and identified 150Nd and 152Sm as critical based on the X(5) symmetry criteria of Iachello (2001). The paper shows that the general Hamiltonian approach yields different critical candidates than the CQF approach, and it enables future work to identify experimentally measurable signatures that unequivocally signal criticality, as the authors note that catastrophe theory provides a definite criterion but no direct experimental observable.
