# Dependence of asymmetries for charge distribution with respect to the reaction plane on initial energy in heavy ion collisions

> In the paper two combinations of correlators are defined in order to investigate the evolution of possible $\mathcal{P/CP}$ invariance violation in strong interactions with initial energy for heavy ion collisions. These combinations correspond to absolute and relative asymmetry of distribution of electrically charge particles with respect to the reaction plane in heavy ion collisions. Energy dependence of the parameters was derived from data of STAR and ALICE experiments. Significant decreasing both absolute and relative asymmetry is observed at energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 20$ GeV. This feature agrees qualitatively with other results of stage-I beam energy scan program in STAR experiment. General behavior of dependence of absolute asymmetry on initial energy agree reasonably with behavior of similar dependence of Chern -- Simons diffusion rate calculated at different values of external Abelian magnetic field. The observed behavior of understudying parameters vs energy can be considered as indication on possible transition to predominance of hadronic states over quark-gluon degrees of freedom in the mixed phase created in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The paper defines two asymmetry parameters (absolute and relative) for the azimuthal distribution of electric charges with respect to the reaction plane, and finds that both decrease significantly at center-of-mass energies below 20 GeV per nucleon pair, which is interpreted as a possible indication of a transition from quark-gluon to hadronic dominance in the mixed phase, rather than a definitive measurement of local P/CP violation.

## Method

The author defines an absolute asymmetry \(A_a\) and a relative asymmetry \(A_r\) from experimental correlators \(\langle \mathbf{K}_{\alpha\beta}^{\mathrm{E}}\rangle\) measured by STAR and ALICE collaborations. These correlators are sensitive to the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and possible local strong P/CP violation. The energy dependence of these asymmetries is derived from published and preliminary STAR data for Au+Au, U+U, and Cu+Cu collisions, and from ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions, and compared to the Chern-Simons diffusion rate \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{CS}}\) computed from holographic AdS/CFT correspondence for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma in the strong coupling regime.

## Key numbers

- Absolute asymmetry \(A_a\) for Au+Au semi-central collisions shows sharp decreasing at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 20\) GeV, with the transition region around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 19.6\) GeV.
- Relative asymmetry \(A_r\) for heavy ions changes dramatically at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 19.6\) GeV, especially for peripheral bins (e.g., 50-60% and 60-70% centrality).
- For Cu+Cu collisions, \(A_a\) values are significantly larger than for Au+Au or U+U at corresponding energies.
- The Chern-Simons diffusion rate \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{CS}}\) at \(B=0\) is proportional to \(T^4\), and curves for \(B=0\), \(B=5T^2\), and \(B=10T^2\) are compared; the \(B=0\) curve coincides with \(B \sim T^2\) for all centralities.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \lesssim 12\) GeV, the phenomenological \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{CS}}\) curves for all considered \(B\) values lie within the uncertainty band from freeze-out temperature parameter errors.

## Context

This work extends the experimental search for local strong P/CP violation via the chiral magnetic effect, first observed by STAR (Abelev 2009) and confirmed by ALICE (Abelev 2012). The author introduces new asymmetry parameters to study the energy dependence of charge separation, building on the theoretical framework of Kharzeev (2008) and the correlator definition of Voloshin (2004). The observed decrease of asymmetries below 20 GeV agrees qualitatively with other results from the STAR beam energy scan program (Mohanty 2011, Aggarwal 2010) and suggests a transition from quark-gluon to hadronic dominance in the mixed phase. The comparison with Chern-Simons diffusion rates from holographic calculations (Son 2002, Basar 2012) provides a theoretical context for interpreting the energy dependence, though the author notes that background contributions to the experimental correlators remain incompletely understood.
