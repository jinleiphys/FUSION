# Rate for Laser-Induced Nuclear Dipole Absorption

> Using the Brink-Axel hypothesis we derive the rate $R$ for nuclear dipole excitation by a laser pulse carrying $N \gg 1$ photons with average energy $\hbar ω_0 \approx 5$ MeV. As expected $R \propto (\hbar ω_0)^3$. The rate is also proportional to the aperure $α$ of the laser pulse. Perhaps less expected is the fact that $R \propto N$, irrespective of the degree of coherence of the laser pulse. The expression for $R$, derived for a nearly stationary laser pulse, is valid also for short times and can, thus, be used in simulations via rate equations of multiple nuclear dipole excitations by a single pulse. The explicit dependence of $R$ on the parameters of the laser pulse and on nuclear parameters given in the paper should help to optimize experiments on laser-nucleus reactions.

## Key claim

Using the Brink-Axel hypothesis, the rate for laser-induced nuclear dipole absorption is proportional to the total number of photons N in the pulse, irrespective of the degree of coherence of the laser pulse, and the derivation assumes a nearly stationary laser pulse with the caveat that the result also holds for short times relevant to rate-equation simulations.

## Method

The authors derived the dipole absorption rate by combining a density-matrix description of the laser pulse (as a wave packet carrying N photons with mean energy ℏω₀ ≈ 5 MeV and energy spread σ ≈ 10 keV) with the Brink-Axel hypothesis for the nuclear response. They expanded the electromagnetic field in orthonormal plane-wave modes in a quantization volume, used the dipole approximation in the long-wavelength limit, applied the Wigner-Eckart theorem for nuclear matrix elements, and summed over final nuclear states using a Lorentzian parametrization of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) with mean energy E_d and spreading width Γ^↓.

## Key numbers

- Mean photon energy ℏω₀ ≈ 5 MeV, with energy spread σ ≈ 10 keV
- GDR mean energy E_d − E_i ≈ 12 MeV for medium-weight nuclei and ≈ 8 MeV for heavy nuclei
- GDR spreading width Γ^↓ ≈ 5 MeV
- The rate R ∝ (ℏω₀)³ and R ∝ N, where N is the total number of photons in the pulse
- The rate is proportional to the aperture α of the laser pulse
- For significant nuclear excitation, N ≈ 10⁸ photons are needed, out of a primary pulse of perhaps ∼10¹¹ or more photons

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of the laser-induced nuclear dipole absorption rate used in Refs. Wei11, Pal14, and Pal15, where a plausible guess for R was employed. The derivation provides explicit dependence on laser-pulse parameters (photon energy, energy spread, photon number, aperture) and nuclear parameters (GDR energy and spreading width), enabling optimization of future experiments at facilities such as the Nuclear Pillar of the Extreme Light Infrastructure in Romania and Gamma Factories at CERN. The approach based on rate equations and nuclear equilibration is contrasted with the standard approach to laser-atom interactions that uses electrical field strength.
