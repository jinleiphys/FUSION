# Constraining the equation of state in neutron-star cores via the long-ringdown signal

> Multimessenger signals from binary neutron star (BNS) mergers are promising tools to infer the largely unknown properties of nuclear matter at densities that are presently inaccessible to laboratory experiments. The gravitational waves (GWs) emitted by BNS merger remnants, in particular, have the potential of setting tight constraints on the neutron-star equation of state (EOS) that would complement those coming from the late inspiral, direct mass-radius measurements, or ab-initio dense-matter calculations. To explore this possibility, we perform a representative series of general-relativistic simulations of BNS systems with EOSs carefully constructed so as to cover comprehensively the high-density regime of the EOS space. From these simulations, we identify a novel and tight correlation between the ratio of the energy and angular-momentum losses in the late-time portion of the post-merger signal, i.e., the ``long ringdown'', and the properties of the EOS at the highest pressures and densities in neutron-star cores. When applying this correlation to post-merger GW signals, we find a significant reduction of the EOS uncertainty at densities several times the nuclear saturation density, where no direct constraints are currently available. Hence, the long ringdown has the potential of providing new and stringent constraints on the state of matter in neutron stars in general and, in particular, in their cores.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a novel, tight correlation between the slope of the radiated gravitational-wave energy versus angular momentum during the late-time "long ringdown" phase of binary neutron star merger remnants and the properties of the equation of state at the highest densities and pressures in neutron-star cores, specifically the central pressure and density of the maximum-mass (TOV) configuration. This correlation is claimed to be stronger than the traditional \(f_2\) peak correlation, but the analysis is limited to smooth equations of state without strong phase transitions.

## Method

The authors performed general-relativistic simulations of binary neutron star mergers using six "golden" equations of state selected via principal-component analysis from a large Gaussian-process ensemble of zero-temperature EOSs conditioned on astrophysical constraints (GW170817 tidal deformability, pulsar masses, NICER mass-radius measurements) and theoretical bounds from chiral effective field theory and perturbative QCD. The simulations used a fixed chirp mass of \(1.18\,M_\odot\) and three mass ratios \(q = 0.7, 0.85, 1.0\), with the cold EOSs augmented by a thermal ideal-gas component with adiabatic index \(\Gamma_\mathrm{th} = 1.75\). The gravitational-wave signal was extracted from approximately 15 ms before merger to 30 ms after, and the slope \(d\hat{E}_\mathrm{GW}/d\hat{J}_\mathrm{GW}\) was measured during the long ringdown window (1–30 ms post-merger) and correlated with the TOV pressure \(p_\mathrm{TOV}\) and density \(n_\mathrm{TOV}\) via a bilinear model.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear saturation density is \(n_{\rm sat} = 0.16\) baryons/fm\(^3\).
* The Pearson correlation coefficient between the long-ringdown slope \(dE_{\rm GW}/dJ_{\rm GW}\) and the TOV pressure \(p_\mathrm{TOV}\) is \(r = 0.877\); with the TOV density \(n_\mathrm{TOV}\) it is \(r = 0.917\).
* For comparison, the correlation between the traditional \(f_2\) peak frequency and \(p_\mathrm{TOV}\) is \(r = 0.792\), and with \(n_\mathrm{TOV}\) it is \(r = 0.865\).
* The bilinear model fit over the time window \(t - t_\mathrm{mer} \in [1, 30]\,{\rm ms}\) yields mean parameters \(\bar{\bm{\beta}} = (1.78, 0.72, -1.44, 1.90, -1.74, -1.14, 3.61)\) with \(p_\mathrm{TOV}\) in GeV/fm\(^3\) and \(n_\mathrm{TOV}\) in fm\(^{-3}\).
* More than 97% of the gravitational-wave amplitude during the long ringdown arises from the \(\ell=2=m\) quadrupole mode.
* The long-ringdown signal is analyzed between approximately 1 and 15 ms after merger.

## Context

This work extends the established approach of using post-merger gravitational-wave spectral peaks (the \(f_1\)-\(f_3\) features) to constrain the neutron-star equation of state, as pioneered by Bauswein (2015), Takami (2015), Rezzolla (2016), and others. The key advance is focusing on the late-time "long ringdown" phase (first noted by Bernuzzi (2015) and Chaurasia (2020)) rather than the full post-merger spectrum, which the authors argue provides a cleaner correlation with the highest-density EOS properties. The results enable future third-generation gravitational-wave detectors (Cosmic Explorer and Einstein Telescope) to constrain matter at densities several times nuclear saturation, where no direct laboratory or observational constraints currently exist. The authors explicitly exclude equations of state with strong phase transitions, leaving that exploration to future work using the approach of Annala (2019) and Altiparmak (2022).
