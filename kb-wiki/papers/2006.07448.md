# Entanglement and correlation in two-nucleon systems

> We examine the mode entanglement and correlation of two fermionic particles. We study the one- and two-mode entropy and a global characteristic, the one-body entanglement entropy. We consider not only angular momentum coupled states with single configuration but use the configuration interaction method. With the help of the Slater decomposition, we derive analytical expressions for the entanglement measures. We show that when the total angular momentum is zero specific single configurations describe maximally entangled states. It turns out that for a finite number of associated modes the one- and two-mode entropies have identical values. In the shell model framework, we numerically study two valence neutrons in the $sd$ shell. The one-body entanglement entropy of the ground state is close to the maximal value and the associated modes have the largest mutual information.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for two-fermion systems with total angular momentum zero, specific single configurations describe maximally entangled states, and that for a finite number of associated modes, the one- and two-mode entropies have identical values; this holds under the condition that the wave function can be expressed in Slater decomposed form using a specific ordering of the modes.

## Method

The authors use the configuration interaction (CI) method within the nuclear shell model to study two valence neutrons in the $sd$ shell, employing the USD and USDB interactions. They derive analytical expressions for entanglement measures, one-mode entropy, two-mode entropy, one-body entanglement entropy, and mutual information, by applying the Slater decomposition (the fermionic analogue of the Schmidt decomposition) to two-fermion wave functions with good total angular momentum. The entanglement measures are computed from the eigenvalues of the one-particle reduced density matrix (OP-RDM) and from the two-mode reduced density matrix constructed using transition operators.

## Key numbers

- For a two-fermion state with total angular momentum $J=0$ and identical orbits ($a_1=a_2=a$), the one-mode entropy of each mode is $S(\rho^{(a,m)}) = h(2/(2j+1))$, where $h(x) = -x\log_2 x - (1-x)\log_2(1-x)$ and $j$ is the total angular momentum of the orbit.
- For $J=0$ and distinct orbits ($a_1 \neq a_2$), the one-mode entropy is $S(\rho^{(a_1,m)}) = S(\rho^{(a_2,m)}) = h(1/(2j+1))$.
- The one-body entanglement entropy for a $J=0$ state with identical orbits is $S^{SP} = (2j+1) h(2/(2j+1))$; for distinct orbits it is $S^{SP} = 2(2j+1) h(1/(2j+1))$.
- The maximum one-body entanglement entropy for a two-fermion state using $d$ modes is $S^{SP}_{\text{max}} = -2 + d \log_2 d - (d-2)\log_2(d-2)$ for $d>2$, achieved when $\lambda_i^2 = 1/(2d)$.
- For the CI wave function of the form $\vert\Psi^{(J=0,M=0)}\rangle = \sum_a A_a \vert\Phi_{aa}^{00}\rangle$, the one-mode entropy of modes $(a,m)$ is $S(\rho^{(a,m)}) = h(|A_a|^2 \cdot 2/(2j_a+1))$, independent of the magnetic quantum number $m$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of entanglement in fermionic systems by Kwaśniewicz et al. (2014, 2016, 2017), which examined von Neumann entropy of one-particle reduced density operators for angular momentum and isospin coupled states, by focusing on mode entanglement rather than particle entanglement and providing explicit analytical formulas. The results enable the quantitative characterization of quantum correlations in nuclear shell-model calculations, building on prior applications of quantum information concepts to atomic physics (Tichy et al., 2011) and chemical bonding (Szalay et al., 2015, 2017), as well as initial nuclear physics studies in the Lipkin model (Tully et al., 2019) and fermionic superconducting systems (Tully et al., 2018).
