# Local electric current correlation function in an exponentially decaying magnetic field

> The effect of an exponentially decaying magnetic field on the dynamics of Dirac fermions in 3+1 dimensions is explored. The spatially decaying magnetic field is assumed to be aligned in the third direction, and is defined by {\mathbf{B}}(x)=B(x){\mathbf{e}}_{z}, with B(x)=B_{0}e^{-ξ x/\ell_{B}}. Here, ξ is a dimensionless damping factor and \ell_{B}=(eB_{0})^{-1/2} is the magnetic length. As it turns out, the energy spectrum of fermions in this inhomogeneous magnetic field can be analytically determined using the Ritus method. Assuming the magnetic field to be strong, the chiral condensate and the \textit{local} electric current correlation function are computed in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation and the results are compared with those arising from a strong homogeneous magnetic field. Although the constant magnetic field B_{0} can be reproduced by taking the limit of ξ-> 0 and/or x-> 0 from B(x), these limits turn out to be singular once the quantum corrections are taken into account.

## Key claim

The limits of vanishing damping factor (xi -> 0) and/or vanishing spatial coordinate (x -> 0) of the exponentially decaying magnetic field B(x) = B0 exp(-xi x / lB) reproduce the constant field B0 at the classical level, but these limits are singular once quantum corrections are taken into account, meaning the chiral condensate and local electric current correlation function in the inhomogeneous field do not reduce to their homogeneous-field counterparts in these limits.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for massive fermions in 3+1 dimensions in the presence of an exponentially decaying magnetic field B(x) = B0 exp(-xi x / lB) aligned along the z-direction, using the Ritus eigenfunction method. They computed the chiral condensate and the local electric current correlation function (local electric susceptibility) in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation, assuming a strong magnetic field, and compared these results with those obtained from a strong homogeneous magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field profile is B(x) = B0 exp(-xi x / lB), where lB = (eB0)^{-1/2} is the magnetic length and xi is a dimensionless damping parameter.
* The energy dispersion relation for an electron in the exponentially decaying magnetic field is E_kappa = sqrt(xi^2 eB0 kappa + p3^2 + m^2), with kappa = s^2 - r^2, where s and r are positive integers with s >= r.
* The energy dispersion relation for an electron in a constant magnetic field B0 is E_p = sqrt(2 eB0 p + p3^2 + m^2), with p = 0, 1, 2, ...
* The chiral condensate in the exponentially decaying magnetic field depends on the dimensionless variable u = (2/xi^2) exp(-xi x / lB), whereas in the homogeneous field it is constant.

## Context

This work extends the study of fermion dynamics in inhomogeneous magnetic fields, following prior work on magnetic quantum dots in graphene by Martino et al. (2007) and the solution of the Dirac equation in exponentially decaying magnetic fields by Handrich (2005) and Ghosh et al. (2009). It also builds on the Ritus eigenfunction method used by Raya et al. (2010) for 2+1 dimensional chiral condensates and by Gies et al. (2011) for computing local vacuum polarization tensors in inhomogeneous fields. The results demonstrate the nonlocal features of the quantum vacuum in spatially decaying magnetic fields and are relevant for both high energy physics and condensed matter physics.
