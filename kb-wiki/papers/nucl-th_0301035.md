# Prediction and formation mechanism of triaxial superdeformed nuclei for $A\sim 80$

> The three dimensional Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations are carried out for 64 nuclei between $70\leq A\leq 90$ to find triaxial superdeformed nuclei. Total of 12 nuclei were predicted to have triaxial superdeformation in which the neutron rotational energy plays a key role and the neutron shell energy plays additional role in the formation of triaxial superdeformed nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that 11 (or 12, counting the known \(^{86}\)Zr) nuclei in the \(A\sim 80\) region have triaxial superdeformation, but only under the specific condition that the rotational frequency is fixed at \(0.1\hbar\omega_0\) and the deformation criteria are \(\varepsilon_2 > 0.35\) and \(10^\circ \leq \gamma \leq 50^\circ\). The abstract's claim of 12 nuclei includes the previously known \(^{86}\)Zr, while the summary states 11 new predictions.

## Method
The authors performed three-dimensional Total Routhian Surface (TRS) calculations for 64 even-even nuclei between \(70 \leq A \leq 90\), using a modified harmonic oscillator (MHO) potential with parameters from Ref. [7]. The total routhian was minimized as a function of quadrupole deformation \(\varepsilon_2\), triaxial deformation \(\gamma\), and hexadecapole deformation \(\varepsilon_4\) at a fixed rotational frequency of \(0.1\hbar\omega_0\) (where \(\omega_0 = 41/\sqrt[3]{A}\) MeV), with the pairing gap set to \(\Delta = 0.9\Delta_{\text{o.e.}}\) from experimental odd-even mass differences.

## Key numbers
- 64 nuclei between \(70 \leq A \leq 90\) were analyzed; 11 nuclei were newly predicted to have triaxial superdeformation (plus the known \(^{86}\)Zr, making 12 total).
- Predicted deformations for \(^{80}\)Kr: \((\varepsilon_2, \gamma, \varepsilon_4) = (0.393, 28.8^\circ, 0.030)\) at \(\omega = 0.1\omega_0\).
- Predicted deformations for \(^{86}\)Zr (known TSD nucleus): \((\varepsilon_2, \gamma) = (0.455, 16.8^\circ)\), consistent with prior work.
- The selection criteria for triaxial superdeformation were \(\varepsilon_2 > 0.35\) and \(10^\circ \leq \gamma \leq 50^\circ\).
- Most predicted TSD nuclei have neutron numbers \(N = 44\) or \(46\); only \(^{90}\)Mo is an exception.
- In \(^{80}\)Kr, at \(\omega = 0.10\hbar\omega_0\), one proton pair and two neutron pairs are broken, with crossings at \(\omega = 0.082\hbar\omega_0\) (protons) and \(\omega = 0.09\hbar\omega_0\) (neutrons).

## Context
This work extends prior TRS calculations in the \(A\sim 160\) region (Ref. [6]) to the \(A\sim 80\) region, where only one TSD nucleus (\(^{86}\)Zr, discovered in 1998) was known experimentally. The authors find that the formation mechanism differs between the two regions: in \(A\sim 160\), neutron shell correction energy controls TSD formation, while in \(A\sim 80\), neutron rotational energy (including quasiparticle angular momentum alignment) plays the key role, with neutron shell energy playing an additional role. The predictions enable experimental searches for new TSD nuclei in the \(N=44,46\) region, such as \(^{72}\)Ni, \(^{74}\)Ni, \(^{76}\)Zn, \(^{76}\)Ge, \(^{78}\)Se, \(^{80}\)Se, \(^{80}\)Kr, \(^{88}\)Mo, \(^{90}\)Mo, and \(^{90}\)Ru.
