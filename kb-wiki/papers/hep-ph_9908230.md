# Nuclear Deep-Inelastic Lepton Scattering and Coherence Phenomena

> This review outlines our present experimental knowledge and theoretical understanding of deep-inelastic scattering on nuclear targets. The emphasis is primarily on nuclear coherence phenomena, such as shadowing, where the key physics issue is the exploration of hadronic and quark-gluon fluctuations of a high-energy virtual photon and their passage through the nuclear medium. New developments in polarized deep-inelastic scattering on nuclei are also discussed, and more conventional binding and Fermi motion effects are summarized. The report closes with a brief outlook on vector meson electroproduction, nuclear shadowing at very large Q^2 and the physics of high parton densities in QCD.

# Nuclear Deep-Inelastic Lepton Scattering and Coherence Phenomena

## Key claim
Nuclear deep-inelastic scattering reveals that nuclear structure functions differ substantially from free-nucleon structure functions across the entire Bjorken-x range, with the most striking coherence phenomenon being nuclear shadowing at x < 0.1, which is interpreted as arising from hadronic and quark-gluon fluctuations of the virtual photon interacting coherently with the nuclear medium. The review emphasizes that while the EMC effect at intermediate x (0.3 < x < 0.8) is well established, the detailed dynamical origin of all nuclear modifications, including shadowing, the small enhancement at 0.1 < x < 0.2, and Fermi motion effects at x > 0.8, remains an active area of theoretical investigation.

## Method
The authors compile and compare experimental data from fixed-target deep-inelastic lepton scattering experiments at CERN (NMC, BCDMS), SLAC (E139, E140), and Fermilab (E665, E772) for nuclear targets ranging from deuterium to lead, analyzing the ratio F2^A / F2^d as a function of Bjorken x and Q^2. They also examine complementary data from Drell-Yan dilepton production, heavy quark (J/psi) production via photon-gluon fusion, and neutrino scattering to separate valence quark, sea quark, and gluon distributions in nuclei, and they discuss polarized deep-inelastic scattering on deuterium and helium-3 targets to extract neutron spin structure functions.

## Key numbers
- At x approximately 0.01, the structure function ratio scales as F2^A / F2^C ~ A^(alpha - 1) with alpha approximately 0.95 (Arneodo 1996).
- High-energy photonuclear cross sections follow sigma_gamma A approximately A^0.92 sigma_gamma N (Weise 1993).
- For carbon and calcium, the enhancement at 0.1 < x < 0.2 amounts to typically 2% (Arneodo 1996).
- The E665 collaboration provides data for F2^Xe / F2^d down to x approximately 2 x 10^(-5), with Q^2 approximately 0.03 GeV^2 at x approximately 6 x 10^(-5) (Adams 1992).
- At x approximately 5 x 10^(-3), typical momentum transfers are Q^2 approximately 1 GeV^2 (Arneodo 1995).
- The ratio of inelastic J/psi production cross sections for Sn relative to carbon is 1.13 plus or minus 0.08 (Amaudruz 1992).
- For the proton structure function at Q^2 approximately 100 GeV^2, sigma_gamma* p ~ (W^2)^Delta with Delta approximately 0.3 (Aid 1996); for real photons, Delta approximately 0.08 (Donnachie 1992).
- The pomeron trajectory intercept is alpha_P(0) approximately 1.08 with slope alpha_P' approximately 0.26 GeV^(-2) (Donnachie 1992, Abe 1994).
- The reggeon trajectory intercept is alpha_R(0) approximately 0.5 with slope alpha_R' approximately 0.9 GeV^(-2) (Donnachie 1992, Apel 1979).
- At Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, the flavor singlet axial matrix element Delta q_0 = 0.23 plus or minus 0.07 (stat) plus or minus 0.19 (sys) (Adeva 1998).
- The Bjorken sum rule is fulfilled at the 5% level (Adeva 1998).
- At x = 2.4 x 10^(-4) and Q^2 = 15 GeV^2, R = sigma_L / sigma_T is approximately 0.5 (Adloff 1997).

## Context
This review synthesizes the experimental program following the EMC collaboration's discovery (Aubert 1983) that the iron structure function differs substantially from deuterium, establishing the systematic mapping of nuclear effects across five kinematic regions: shadowing (x < 0.1), enhancement (0.1 < x < 0.2), the EMC effect (0.3 < x < 0.8), Fermi motion (x > 0.8), and the x > 1 region. The work extends earlier reviews by Arneodo (1994) and Geesaman (1995) by incorporating new data from NMC, E665, and HERA, and by connecting nuclear shadowing to the broader QCD phenomena of high parton densities and diffractive scattering. The review enables future investigations of nuclear gluon distributions via heavy quark production, tests of the QCD evolution of nuclear parton distributions at large Q^2, and studies of the transition from vector meson dominance to partonic descriptions at small x.
