# Density-dependent van der Waals model under the GW170817 constraint

> We propose a density-dependent function for the attractive interaction in the original van der Waals model to correctly describe the flow constraint at the high-density regime of the symmetric nuclear matter. After a generalization to asymmetric nuclear matter, it was also possible to study the stellar matter regime from this new model. The mass-radius relation for neutron stars under $β$-equilibrium is found to agree with recent X-ray observations. The neutron star masses supported against gravity, obtained from some parametrizations of the model, are in the range of $(1.97-2.07)M_{\odot}$, compatible with observational data from the PSR J0348+0432 pulsar. Furthermore, we verify the reliability of the model in predicting tidal deformabilities of the binary system related to the GW170817 neutron star merger event and find a full agreement with the new bounds obtained by the LIGO/Virgo collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors propose a density-dependent van der Waals (DD-vdW) model that, by introducing a power-law density dependence in the attractive interaction parameter, satisfies both the flow constraint for symmetric nuclear matter and the GW170817 tidal deformability bounds, but only for a narrow range of incompressibility values (242.4 MeV ≤ K₀ ≤ 251.5 MeV) and symmetry energy values (25 MeV ≤ J ≤ 35 MeV).

## Method

The authors modify the van der Waals model by making both the attractive parameter a(ρ) and the repulsive parameter b(ρ) density-dependent, with b(ρ) following the Carnahan-Starling excluded volume method and a(ρ) taking the form a/(1+bρ)ⁿ. They calibrate the model to reproduce saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ and binding energy B₀ ≈ 16.0 MeV, then use the flow constraint from Danielewicz (2002) to select parametrizations with K₀ in the allowed range. For asymmetric matter, they add a term dρ₃² to the energy density and adjust d to reproduce symmetry energy values between 25 and 35 MeV. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust EoS to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and compute tidal deformabilities from the Love number formalism.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ and binding energy per particle B₀ ≈ 16.0 MeV (standard nuclear matter values used for calibration)
- Incompressibility range satisfying flow constraint: 242.4 MeV ≤ K₀ ≤ 251.5 MeV
- Maximum density for causality with K₀=251.5 MeV parametrization: ρ/ρ₀ ≤ 4.9
- Neutron star masses from parametrizations compatible with PSR J0348+0432: (1.97-2.07) M⊙
- Incompressibility range for parametrizations producing ~2 M⊙ neutron stars: 247.8 MeV ≤ K₀ ≤ 251.5 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L₀ range: 63.4 MeV ≤ L₀ ≤ 96.5 MeV (for 25 MeV ≤ J ≤ 35 MeV and flow-constrained K₀)
- Maximum dimensionless tidal deformability for canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: Λ₁.₄ = 527 (for K₀=251.5 MeV, J=35 MeV)
- Maximum density for causality with J=25 MeV, K₀=247.8 MeV parametrization: ρ/ρ₀ = 5.66

## Context

This work extends the van der Waals model for nuclear matter previously developed by Vovchenko et al. (2015, 2016) and the Carnahan-Starling excluded volume treatment from Vovchenko et al. (2017). Unlike those earlier models, which violated causality in the density range 2.0 ≤ ρ/ρ₀ ≤ 4.6 of the flow constraint from Danielewicz (2002), the DD-vdW model satisfies both causality and the flow constraint. The model also improves upon the earlier real gas models (Redlich-Kwong-Soave, Peng-Robinson, Clausius-2) studied by Vovchenko et al. (2017), which could not produce K₀ within the accepted range of 220-260 MeV. The results enable future studies of neutron star structure and tidal deformabilities that are consistent with both nuclear matter constraints and gravitational wave observations from GW170817.
