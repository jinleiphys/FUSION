# Nuclear Matter EOS with a Three-body Force

> The effect of a microscopic three-body force on the saturation properties of nuclear matter is studied within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. The calculations show a decisive improvement of the saturation density along with an overall agreement with the empirical saturation point. With the three-body force the symmetry energy turns more rapidly increasing with density, which allows for the direct URCA process to occur in $β$-stable neutron star matter. The influence of the three-body force on the nuclear mean field does not diminish the role of the ground state correlations.

# Nuclear Matter EOS with a Three-body Force

## Key claim
The inclusion of a microscopic three-body force (TBF) within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach decisively improves the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter toward the empirical value, while leaving the saturation energy almost unchanged; however, the TBF's sigma meson mass is treated as a free parameter set to 540 MeV, and the results depend on this choice.

## Method
The authors performed BHF calculations for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the Argonne AV18 two-body interaction, with a microscopic three-body force derived from a meson-exchange current model (including pi-pi, pi-rho, rho-rho, sigma, omega, and rho-pi-gamma contributions). The TBF was reduced to an effective two-body interaction by averaging over the third nucleon's wave function including correlations via the defect function, and the G-matrix was solved self-consistently with the Bethe-Goldstone equation using the continuous choice for the auxiliary potential.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density improved from 0.265 fm^-3 (without TBF) to 0.19 fm^-3 (with TBF), approaching the empirical value
- Saturation energy remained essentially unchanged and already in good agreement with the empirical value
- Compressibility modulus increased from 250 MeV to 265 MeV after including the TBF
- Symmetry energy at saturation density increased from 30.3 MeV to 31.3 MeV, consistent with the empirical value of 30 +/- 4 MeV
- At density 0.4 fm^-3, the proton fraction in beta-equilibrium matter reached 0.1365 with TBF, exceeding the approximate direct URCA threshold of 0.148 at higher densities
- At density 0.5 fm^-3, the proton fraction reached 0.1882 with TBF, compared to 0.1069 without TBF
- At saturation density (0.17 fm^-3), the TBF increased the single-particle potential at the Fermi momentum by only 1.0-1.5 MeV (less than 2% reduction of the attractive mean field)

## Context
This work extends the earlier microscopic TBF approach of Lejeune et al. (circa 1990) by using the more sophisticated Argonne AV18 two-body interaction instead of the Paris force, and by extending calculations to neutron matter for symmetry energy extraction. The results are compared with BHF calculations using the phenomenological Urbana TBF (Burgio et al.) and with relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations (Lee et al.), showing close agreement in symmetry energy despite different theoretical frameworks. The finding that the direct URCA process can occur in neutron stars when TBF is included has implications for neutron star cooling mechanisms, extending the work of Lattimer et al. on URCA thresholds.
