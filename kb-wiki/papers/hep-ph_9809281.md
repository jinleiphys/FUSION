# Comment on the absence of Coulomb effects on $e^+e^-$ pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> In recent works [Segev,Wells; Baltz, McLerran; Eichmann, Reinhardt, Schramm, Greiner] it was claimed that the Coulomb correction to the $e^+e^-$ production cross section in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is absent. We point out that this statement is in obvious contradiction to some well known results. We obtain large Coulomb corrections in the pair production cross section for the RHIC and LHC heavy-ion colliders.

# Comment on the absence of Coulomb effects on $e^+e^-$ pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Contrary to claims by Segev & Wells, Baltz & McLerran, and Eichmann et al. that the Coulomb correction to $e^+e^-$ pair production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is absent, the authors demonstrate that large Coulomb corrections exist and are in contradiction with well-known results from photoproduction. The correction is obtained using a well-grounded approach that sums the whole perturbative series in $Z\alpha$, yielding corrections of 25% for RHIC and 14% for LHC.

## Method
The authors apply the equivalent-photon (Weizsäcker-Williams) method, factorizing the cross section for $e^+e^-$ pair production in heavy-ion collisions into the product of the number of equivalent photons from one nucleus and the photoproduction cross section on the other nucleus. They use the Coulomb-corrected photoproduction cross section from Bethe & Maximon (1954) and sum the full perturbative series in $Z\alpha$ following the approach of Ivanov, Kuraev, Schiller, and Serbo (1998). The Coulomb correction is defined as the difference between the full summed cross section and the Born approximation.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC (Au-Au collisions, $Z=79$, $\gamma=108$): Born cross section $\sigma_{\mathrm{Born}} = 36$ kb; Coulomb correction ratio $\sigma_{\mathrm{Coul}}/\sigma_{\mathrm{Born}} = 25\%$.
- For LHC (Pb-Pb collisions, $Z=82$, $\gamma=3000$): Born cross section $\sigma_{\mathrm{Born}} = 227$ kb; Coulomb correction ratio $\sigma_{\mathrm{Coul}}/\sigma_{\mathrm{Born}} = 14\%$.
- The Born cross section formula: $\sigma_{\mathrm{Born}} = \frac{28}{27\pi} \frac{Z^4\alpha^4}{m_e^2} \left( L^3 - \frac{178}{28} L^2 + \frac{7\pi^2 + 370}{28} L - 13.8 \right)$, where $L = \ln(4\gamma^2)$.

## Context
This comment directly contradicts the conclusions of Segev & Wells (1996), Baltz & McLerran (1996), and Eichmann, Reinhardt, Schramm, & Greiner (1998), who claimed that Coulomb corrections vanish for symmetric heavy-ion collisions. The authors instead recover the well-known 10% Coulomb correction for photoproduction on a Pb target from Bethe & Maximon (1954) and extend it to the two-nucleus case. The results are consistent with earlier work by Ivanov, Kuraev, Schiller, and Serbo (1998) on muon-heavy-nucleus collisions and with the approach of Nordsieck (1954) and Baur & Bertulani (1988) for asymmetric collisions. The large Coulomb corrections imply that the $e^+e^-$ pair production process must be calculated with full Coulomb distortion effects for accurate background estimates and beam lifetime predictions at RHIC and LHC.
