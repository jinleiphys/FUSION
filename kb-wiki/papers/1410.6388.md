# Finite temperature quark-gluon vertex with a magnetic field in the Hard Thermal Loop approximation

> We compute the thermo-magnetic correction to the quark-gluon vertex in the presence of a weak magnetic field within the Hard Thermal Loop approximation. The vertex satisfies a QED-like Ward identity with the quark self-energy. The only vertex components that get modified are the longitudinal ones. The calculation provides a first principles result for the quark anomalous magnetic moment at high temperature in a weak magnetic field. We extract the effective thermo-magnetic quark-gluon coupling and show that this decreases as a function of the field strength. The result supports the idea that the properties of the effective quark-gluon coupling in the presence of a magnetic field are an important ingredient to understand the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon coupling decreases as a function of magnetic field strength in the weak-field, high-temperature regime, but only when the magnetic field is smaller than the square of the temperature and the quark mass is used as an infrared regulator; the decrease reaches about 15%–25% relative to the purely thermal coupling for field strengths up to \(qB \sim T^2\).

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop quark-gluon vertex in the presence of a constant, weak magnetic field using the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation and the imaginary-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory. They employ Schwinger’s proper-time method for the charged fermion propagator, expand to first order in \(qB\), and evaluate the Matsubara sums and momentum integrals in the high-temperature limit, keeping the quark mass as an infrared regulator. The vertex correction is extracted from the QED-like and pure QCD diagrams, and the effective coupling is obtained from the longitudinal vertex components in a back-to-back momentum configuration.

## Key numbers

* The effective thermo-magnetic coupling \(g_{\text{eff}}\) normalized to the purely thermal coupling \(g_{\text{therm}}\) decreases by about 15%–25% for \(qB \sim T^2\) when \(\alpha_s = 0.2\)–\(0.3\).
* The function \(M^2(T,m,qB)\) governing the vertex correction is \(M^2 = \frac{qB}{16\pi^2}\left[\ln(2) - \frac{\pi}{2}\frac{T}{m}\right]\).
* The thermal quark mass is set to \(m_f^2 = \frac{1}{8}g^2 T^2 C_F\) with \(C_F = (N^2-1)/(2N)\) for \(SU(N)\).
* The purely thermal vertex correction gives \(\delta\Gamma^{\text{therm}}_0(p_0) = -\frac{m_f^2}{p_0^2}\gamma_0\) in the static limit \(p \to 0\) with \(p_0 = T\).

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous perturbative studies of magnetic-field-dependent coupling in the Abelian Higgs model and Linear Sigma model (Ayala et al., 2014; Ayala et al., 2014) to QCD at high temperature. It contrasts with strong-field calculations by Ferrer et al. (2010) and provides a weak-field, high-temperature counterpart. The result supports the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon observed in lattice QCD (Fodor et al., 2012; Bali et al., 2012) and enables future calculations of the magnetic-field dependence of the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration.
