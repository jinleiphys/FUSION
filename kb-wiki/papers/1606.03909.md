# Perspective on the origin of hadron masses

> The energy-momentum tensor in chiral QCD, $T_{μν}$, exhibits an anomaly, viz. $Θ_0 :=T_{μμ} \neq 0$. Measured in the proton, this anomaly yields $m_p^2$, where $m_p$ is the proton's mass; but, at the same time, when computed in the pion, the answer is $m_π^2=0$. Any attempt to understand the origin and nature of mass, and identify observable expressions thereof, must explain and unify these two apparently contradictory results, which are fundamental to the nature of our Universe. Given the importance of Poincaré-invariance in modern physics, the utility of a frame-dependent approach to this problem seems limited. That is especially true of any approach tied to a rest-frame decomposition of $T_{μν}$ because a massless particle does not possess a rest-frame. On the other hand, the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) paradigm, connected with a Poincaré-covariant treatment of the continuum bound-state problem, provides a straightforward, simultaneous explanation of both these identities, and also a diverse array of predictions, testable at existing and proposed facilities. From this perspective, $\langle π| Θ_0 |π\rangle =0$ owing to exact, symmetry-driven cancellations which occur between one-body dressing effects and two-body-irreducible binding interactions in any well-defined computation of the forward scattering amplitude that defines this expectation value in the pseudoscalar meson. The cancellation is incomplete in any other hadronic bound state, with a remainder whose scale is set by the size of one-body dressing effects.

# Perspective on the origin of hadron masses

## Key claim

The trace anomaly of the energy-momentum tensor in chiral QCD yields the entirety of the proton mass ($m_p^2$) when evaluated in the proton state, yet yields zero when evaluated in the pion state because the pion is a massless Nambu-Goldstone mode; the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) paradigm, connected with a Poincaré-covariant treatment of the continuum bound-state problem, provides a simultaneous explanation of both results via exact, symmetry-driven cancellations between one-body dressing effects and two-body-irreducible binding interactions in the pion, with incomplete cancellation in other hadrons.

## Method

The paper derives the QCD trace anomaly $T_{\mu\mu} = \beta(\alpha) \frac{1}{4} G^{a}_{\mu\nu}G^{a}_{\mu\nu}$ from the scale transformation properties of the classical action and the introduction of a renormalisation scale via dimensional transmutation. It then evaluates the expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor in one-particle proton and pion states using the equations of motion, obtaining $\langle p|T_{\mu\mu}|p\rangle = m_p^2$ and $\langle \pi|T_{\mu\mu}|\pi\rangle = 0$ in the chiral limit. The resolution is framed using Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) analyses and the Goldberger-Treiman relations derived from the axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identity, which connect the dressed-quark mass function to the pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The proton mass $m_p$ is entirely produced by gluons via the trace anomaly in the chiral limit, as $\langle p(P) | T_{\mu\mu} | p(P) \rangle = m_p^2$.
- The pion mass $m_\pi^2 = 0$ in the chiral limit from the trace anomaly expectation value $\langle \pi(q) | \Theta_0 | \pi(q) \rangle = 0$.
- Explicit Higgs-generated current masses for $u$- and $d$-quarks contribute $f_{u+d} = 6\%$ of the proton mass; the $s$-quark contributes $f_s = 2\%$.
- Charm-quark contributions to the proton mass are estimated as $f_c \approx 0.1\%$, based on scaling from $f_s$ using meson decay constants and masses.
- The mass gap in pure Yang-Mills theory is $\Delta \gtrsim 1.5$ GeV from lattice QCD simulations (McNeile, 2008).
- The length scale for flux tube dissolution in QCD is $r_{\not\sigma} \simeq (1/3)$ fm.
- The emergent length scale from dressed-gluon and -quark propagator studies is $\varsigma \approx 0.5$ fm.
- The renormalisation scale below which coloured two-point functions exhibit an inflection point is $\zeta_c \approx 0.5$ GeV.

## Context

This perspective extends the DCSB paradigm developed through Dyson-Schwinger equation analyses (Cloet, 2013; Roberts, 2015; Horn, 2016) and contrasts with frame-dependent approaches to the energy-momentum tensor decomposition (Ji, 1994; Ji, 1995), which the author argues cannot address the massless pion case because a massless particle has no rest frame. The work also critiques the static potential approach to confinement (Wilson, 1974) as disconnected from real-world QCD with light quarks, citing lattice results (Bali, 2005; Prkacin, 2005) showing flux tube dissolution at $r_{\not\sigma} \simeq 1/3$ fm. The paper enables a unified understanding of hadron masses through Poincaré-covariant bound-state methods and identifies testable predictions for existing and proposed experimental facilities.
