# The massive Thirring / sine-Gordon model with non-zero current density

> This paper determines the zero-temperature equation of state for the massive Thirring / sine-Gordon model. This demonstrates recently derived model-independent upper and lower bounds on the zero-temperature equation of state with fixed number density from systems with a non-zero current density. That approach is potentially valuable as Monte Carlo calculations with a current density avoid the sign problem in the Euclidean formulation. An advantage to illustrating these bounds in the massive Thirring / sine-Gordon model is that the relevant calculations with both a number density and a current density can be done using a Bethe ansatz. For this model, optimal bounds constrain the energy density as a function of number density by a factor of two from above and below at high densities for all choices of couplings. The lower bound becomes exact at low densities, while the upper bound approaches the worst constraint of a factor of 4.90.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that model-independent upper and lower bounds on the zero-temperature equation of state, derived from systems with non-zero current density, constrain the energy density as a function of number density by a factor of two from above and below at high densities for all choices of couplings in the massive Thirring / sine-Gordon model, with the upper bound approaching a factor of 4.90 at low densities and the lower bound becoming exact at low densities.

## Method

The authors use a Bethe ansatz approach to compute the energy density as a function of current density at zero number density in the massive Thirring / sine-Gordon model, generalizing Haldane's method (1982) for finite number density to the finite current density case. They solve an integral equation for the density of states as a function of rapidity, then apply Lorentz boosts to the fixed-current results to obtain upper and lower bounds on the energy density as a function of number density. The bounds are computed for both weak and strong sine-Gordon coupling (κ = 0.001 and κ = 0.99) and compared with the exact equation of state obtained from finite-number-density Bethe ansatz calculations.

## Key numbers

- At high densities, the upper and lower bounds constrain the energy density by a factor of two for all coupling choices.
- At low densities, the optimal upper bound approaches a ratio of approximately 4.90 relative to the exact energy density, derived from the minimum of (2 + β²)/(1 - β²)/β at β = √(2/5) ≈ 0.63.
- The lower bound becomes exact at low densities when the boost parameter β = 1/√2 ≈ 0.7, corresponding to βγ = 1.
- The minimum of the ratio ε(α)/j(α) occurs at α_min = ln(√2 + 1), giving a minimum energy per current of 2 m_s.
- The crossover between low- and high-density regimes is characterized by the breather mass m_b, not the soliton mass m_s.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent bounding approach of Cohen (2025) by providing the first nontrivial quantum field theory illustration of these bounds using the massive Thirring / sine-Gordon model. The approach is potentially valuable because Monte Carlo calculations with a current density avoid the sign problem that plagues lattice QCD calculations at finite baryon density, as noted by Philipsen (2013) and others. The Bethe ansatz calculations build on the foundational work of Bergknoff and Thacker (1978) for the vacuum properties and Haldane (1982) for finite number density, enabling future applications to QCD where direct calculations of the equation of state at zero temperature and nonzero density remain intractable.
