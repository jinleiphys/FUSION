# Nucleonic models at finite temperature with in-medium effective fields

> We perform a calculation of dense and hot nuclear matter where the mean interaction between nucleons is described by in-medium effective fields and where we employ analytical approximations of the Fermi integrals. We generalize a previous work where we have addressed the case of the Fermi gas model with in-medium effective mass. In the present work, we fully treat the in-medium interaction by considering both its contribution to the in-medium effective fields, which can be subsumed by the mass in some cases, and to the potential term. Our formalism is general and could be applied to relativistic and nonrelativistic approaches. It is illustrated for different popular models -- Skyrme, nonlinear, and density-dependent relativistic mean-field models --, but also for the metamodel, and it provides a clear understanding of the in-medium correction to the pressure, which is present in the case of the Skyrme and metamodel but is not for the relativistic ones. For the Fermi integrals, we compare the analytical approximation to the, so-called, ``exact'' numerical calculations in order to quantitatively estimate the accuracy of the approximation.

# Nucleonic models at finite temperature with in-medium effective fields

## Key claim

The authors generalize a previous Fermi gas model with in-medium effective mass (Dutra 2023) to fully treat the in-medium interaction, including both its contribution to effective fields and to the potential term, using analytical approximations of Fermi integrals. The formalism reveals that in-medium corrections to pressure appear in Skyrme and metamodel approaches but are absent in relativistic mean-field models, a distinction the abstract does not explicitly highlight.

## Method

The authors develop a canonical ensemble formalism for hot dense nuclear matter where the Helmholtz free energy density is decomposed into kinetic and potential contributions, with in-medium effective fields treated as density-dependent parameters. They apply this formalism to four phenomenological model classes: Skyrme, nonlinear relativistic mean-field (RMF), density-dependent RMF, and the metamodel, computing thermodynamic quantities using both exact numerical Fermi integrals and the JEL analytical approximation (Johns, Ellis, Lattimer 1996). The scalar density is expressed in terms of kinetic energy density, pressure, and effective mass via the relation n_s,q = (ε*_kin,q - 3p*_kin,q)/m*_q, enabling use of the JEL approximation for relativistic models.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature Fermi gas treatment of Dutra (2023) by incorporating full in-medium interactions beyond effective mass corrections alone. The formalism enables fast calculations using analytical Fermi integral approximations (JEL 1996) for applications in core-collapse supernova simulations (Bethe; Sumiyoshi 2019) and neutron star merger remnants (Shibata 2019). The approach is applicable to both relativistic and nonrelativistic models including Skyrme (Skyrme 1956, 1959; Stone 2007), nonlinear RMF (Boguta and Bodmer 1977; Baoan Li 2008), density-dependent RMF (Typel and Wolter 1999; Gogelein 2008), and the metamodel (Margueron 2018a, 2018b), and is designed to reduce computational time in Bayesian Markov chain Monte-Carlo uncertainty quantification.
