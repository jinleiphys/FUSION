# Pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions in chiral effective field theory: next-to-next-to-leading order contributions

> A complete calculation of the pion-nucleon loops that contribute to the transition operator for $NN\to NNπ$ up-to-and-including next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in chiral effective field theory near threshold is presented. The evaluation is based on the so-called momentum counting scheme, which takes into account the relatively large momentum of the initial nucleons inherent in pion-production reactions. We show that the significant cancellations between the loops found at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the earlier studies are also operative at N$^2$LO. In particular, the $1/m_N$ corrections (with $m_N$ being the nucleon mass) to loop diagrams cancel at N$^2$LO, as do the contributions of the pion loops involving the low-energy constants $c_i$, i=1...4. In contrast to the NLO calculation however, the cancellation of loops at N$^2$LO is incomplete, yielding a non-vanishing contribution to the transition amplitude. Together with the one-pion exchange tree-level operators, the loop contributions provide the long-range part of the production operator. Finally, we discuss the phenomenological implications of these findings. In particular, we find that the amplitudes generated by the N$^2$LO pion loops yield contributions comparable in size with the most important phenomenological heavy-meson exchange amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in chiral effective field theory using the momentum counting scheme, the significant cancellations between pion-nucleon loop diagrams found at NLO are also operative, but the cancellation at N$^2$LO is incomplete, yielding a non-vanishing contribution to the transition amplitude for $NN\to NN\pi$ near threshold.

## Method

The authors performed a complete calculation of all pion-nucleon one-loop diagrams contributing to the $NN\to NN\pi$ transition operator up to N$^2$LO in chiral effective field theory, using the momentum counting scheme that accounts for the large initial nucleon momentum $p = \sqrt{m_N m_\pi}$ inherent in pion-production reactions. They evaluated two sets of loop diagrams, those proportional to $g_A^3$ and those proportional to $g_A$, and analyzed the cancellation patterns among them, separating reducible and irreducible contributions by rewriting the Weinberg-Tomozawa vertex to expose terms that cancel nucleon propagators. The calculations were performed near threshold, focusing on the two independent transition amplitudes $\mathcal{A}$ (for $^3P_0\to ^1S_0s$) and $\mathcal{B}$ (for $^3P_1\to ^3S_1s$).

## Key numbers

* The expansion parameter in the momentum counting scheme is $\chi = p/\Lambda_\chi \simeq 0.4$, where $p = \sqrt{m_N m_\pi}$ is the initial nucleon momentum at threshold and $\Lambda_\chi$ is the chiral symmetry breaking scale of order 1 GeV.
* The N$^2$LO loop contributions yield amplitudes comparable in size with the most important phenomenological heavy-meson exchange amplitudes.
* The $1/m_N$ corrections to loop diagrams cancel at N$^2$LO, as do the contributions of pion loops involving the low-energy constants $c_i$ for $i=1\ldots4$.
* The direct one-nucleon diagrams at LO are about an order of magnitude smaller than the LO rescattering diagram amplitude for $pp\to d\pi^+$, due to momentum mismatch and accidental cancellations from final state interactions.

## Context

This work extends the NLO analysis of Hanhart and Kaiser (2000) and Lensky et al. (2007), who found complete cancellation of NLO loop contributions for both neutral and charged pion production. The paper resolves the puzzle of NLO loop contributions to $pp\to d\pi^+$ by showing that irreducible box diagrams cancel the finite remainder found in earlier studies. The results provide the long-range part of the production operator from pion-nucleon loops, which together with one-pion exchange tree-level operators will be combined with Delta-isobar contributions and short-range contact terms in future work to enable a complete N$^2$LO calculation of $NN\to NN\pi$ observables.
