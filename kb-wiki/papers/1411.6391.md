# Group Theory for Embedded Random Matrix Ensembles

> Embedded random matrix ensembles are generic models for describing statistical properties of finite isolated quantum many-particle systems. For the simplest spinless fermion (or boson) systems with say $m$ fermions (or bosons) in $N$ single particle states and interacting with say $k$-body interactions, we have EGUE($k$) [embedded GUE of $k$-body interactions) with GUE embedding and the embedding algebra is $U(N)$. In this paper, using EGUE($k$) representation for a Hamiltonian that is $k$-body and an independent EGUE($t$) representation for a transition operator that is $t$-body and employing the embedding $U(N)$ algebra, finite-$N$ formulas for moments up to order four are derived, for the first time, for the transition strength densities (transition strengths multiplied by the density of states at the initial and final energies). In the asymptotic limit, these formulas reduce to those derived for the EGOE version and establish that in general bivariate transition strength densities take bivariate Gaussian form for isolated finite quantum systems. Extension of these results for other types of transition operators and EGUE ensembles with further symmetries are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives, for the first time, finite-N formulas for moments up to order four of transition strength densities for systems where both the Hamiltonian and the transition operator are represented by independent embedded Gaussian unitary ensembles (EGUE), establishing that in the asymptotic limit these bivariate transition strength densities take a bivariate Gaussian form for isolated finite quantum systems.

## Method

The author uses the Wigner-Racah algebra of the embedding U(N) Lie algebra to decompose the Hamiltonian (a k-body operator) and the transition operator (a t-body operator) into irreducible tensorial components. By applying the Wigner-Eckart theorem and ensemble averaging over independent EGUE representations for both operators, exact finite-N formulas are derived for the bivariate moments M_PQ of the transition strength density, with P+Q up to 4. Asymptotic results are obtained by taking the limit N to infinity with m/N approaching zero and fixed k and t.

- The ensemble averaged second moment of the Hamiltonian is given by: overline{langle [H(k)]^2 rangle^m} = V^2_H Lambda^0(N,m,k), where Lambda^0(N,m,k) = binom{m}{k} binom{N-m+k}{k}
- The bivariate correlation coefficient xi(m) in the asymptotic limit reduces to: xi(m) rightarrow binom{m}{k}^{-1} binom{m-t}{k}
- The normalized fourth moment mu_{40}(m) in the asymptotic limit becomes: mu_{40}(m) rightarrow 2 + binom{m}{k}^{-1} binom{m-k}{k}
- The normalized moment mu_{31}(m) in the asymptotic limit satisfies: mu_{31}(m) rightarrow xi mu_{40}
- The normalized moment mu_{22}(m) in the asymptotic limit becomes: mu_{22}(m) rightarrow 1 + binom{m}{k}^{-2} binom{m-t}{k}^2 + binom{m}{k}^{-2} binom{m-t-k}{k} binom{m-t}{k}

## Context

This work extends the group theoretical approach previously applied to eigenvalue densities for EGUE ensembles by Kota (2005) and to EGOE versions by French, Kota, and collaborators, providing the first exact finite-N formulas for transition strength densities. The results enable analytical calculation of transition strength distributions in finite quantum many-body systems such as nuclei, atoms, and quantum dots, and establish that the bivariate Gaussian form previously obtained in the asymptotic limit using binary correlation approximation by Mon and French (1975) and others holds generally. The paper also outlines extensions to particle transfer operators and beta-decay-type operators with U(N1) oplus U(N2) embedding algebra, which are relevant for nuclear physics applications.
