# Compton Scattering off Nucleons: Focus on the Nucleonic Low-Energy Degrees of Freedom

> In this sketch, I focus on Physics and formalism behind dynamical polarisabilities, a new tool to test and interpret quantitative predictions about the low-energy degrees of freedom inside the nucleon from the multipoles of Compton scattering for photon energies below 300 MeV. A way to extract them from double-polarised precision experiments is sketched. Predictions from Chiral Effective Field Theory, both on the proton and on the neutron, serve as guideline for forthcoming experiments. Special interest is put on the rôle of the nucleon spin-polarisabilities. For details and a complete list of references, consult Refs. [1,2,3].

## Key claim

Dynamical polarisabilities, defined as energy-dependent multipole parameters extracted from Compton scattering amplitudes, provide a new tool to test and interpret quantitative predictions about the low-energy degrees of freedom inside the nucleon for photon energies below 300 MeV, with the caveat that the separation into structure and non-structure (pole) parts of the scattering amplitudes cannot be answered uniquely.

## Method

The author decomposes the six independent amplitudes of the real Compton scattering T-matrix into pole (point-like nucleon with anomalous magnetic moment) and structure (non-pole) parts, then expands the structure parts in terms of energy-dependent dipole polarisabilities at fixed photon energy using their different angular dependence. Chiral Effective Field Theory (chiEFT) at leading-one-loop order, including pion cloud, Delta(1232) resonance, and two short-distance energy-independent coefficients (delta_alpha_E1, delta_beta_M1), is employed to predict the energy dependence of all six dipole polarisabilities for both proton and neutron. The three free parameters (b1, delta_alpha_E1, delta_beta_M1) are determined by fitting the unexpanded chiEFT amplitude to existing Compton scattering data on the proton.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range considered: below 300 MeV
- Static magnetic dipole polarisability from Delta(1232) contribution: beta_M1^Delta = +[7 to 13] in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- Observed static value: bar_beta_M1 approximately 1.5 in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- Three-parameter fit results for proton: bar_alpha_E = 11.52 +/- 2.43 (10^{-4} fm^3), bar_beta_M = 3.42 +/- 1.70 (10^{-4} fm^3), b1 = 4.66 +/- 0.14
- Two-parameter fit results (using Baldin sum rule): bar_alpha_E = 11.04 +/- 1.36 (10^{-4} fm^3), bar_beta_M = 2.76 +/- 1.36 (10^{-4} fm^3), b1 = 4.67 +/- 0.14
- Short-distance parameters from fit: delta_alpha_E1 = -5.9 +/- 1.4, delta_beta_M1 = -10.7 +/- 1.2
- Baldin sum rule value for proton: bar_alpha_E + bar_beta_M = 13.8 (10^{-4} fm^3)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nucleon Compton scattering beyond the static electric and magnetic polarisabilities (bar_alpha_E, bar_beta_M) that have been the focus of most previous experiments, introducing dynamical polarisabilities as a more complete probe of nucleon structure. The chiEFT predictions are compared with Dispersion Theory results (Olmos 2001) and with the Baldin sum rule, showing good agreement. The paper enables forthcoming double-polarised precision experiments at facilities including TUNL/HIgammmaS, S-DALINAC, MAMI, and MAXlab to extract the energy dependence of all six dipole polarisabilities per nucleon, particularly the poorly known spin-polarisabilities.
