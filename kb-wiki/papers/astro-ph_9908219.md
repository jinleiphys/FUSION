# Charge-conjugation violating neutrino interactions in supernovae

> The well known charge conjugation violating interactions in the Standard Model increase neutrino- and decrease anti-neutrino- nucleon cross sections. This impacts neutrino transport in core collapse supernovae through "recoil" corrections of order the neutrino energy $k$ over the nucleon mass $M$. All $k/M$ corrections to neutrino transport deep inside a protoneutron star are calculated from angular integrals of the Boltzmann equation. We find these corrections significantly modify neutrino currents at high temperatures. This produces a large mu and tau number for the protoneutron star and can change the ratio of neutrons to protons. In addition, the relative size of neutrino mean free paths changes. At high temperatures, the electron anti-neutrino mean free path becomes {\it longer} than that for mu or tau neutrinos.

# Charge-conjugation violating neutrino interactions in supernovae

## Key claim

The well-known charge-conjugation (C) violating interactions in the Standard Model produce differences between neutrino and anti-neutrino cross sections of order the neutrino energy over the nucleon mass (k/M), which significantly modify neutrino transport in protoneutron stars at high temperatures, but only because time-reversal symmetry forbids such differences if the nucleon does not recoil, the effect is therefore a recoil correction.

## Method

The authors expanded neutrino-nucleon cross sections to first order in k/M (equivalently T/M) using the Fermi theory with weak magnetism (F2 term) and calculated the angular moments of the Boltzmann equation for neutrino transport in dense, non-degenerate, non-interacting nucleon matter. They derived analytic expressions for the neutrino number and energy currents including all T/M corrections, separating contributions from recoil kinematics, thermal motion of nucleons, and the C-violating weak magnetism term. The formalism was applied separately to neutral-current scattering (for mu and tau neutrinos) and combined neutral-plus-charged-current interactions (for electron neutrinos and anti-neutrinos).

## Context

This work extends earlier neutrino transport calculations by Schinder (1990) and others (Tubbs and Schramm, 1975; Bruenn and Podsiadlowski, 1987; Sawyer and Soni, 1979; Iwamoto, 1982) that omitted the C-violating weak magnetism term. The authors explicitly note that relativistic calculations by Reddy (1998) implicitly include nu/anti-nu differences to all orders, but the present expansion to order k/M isolates the C-violating effects in a simple analytic form. The results enable quantitative inclusion of C-violation in supernova simulations, with implications for the mu and tau number of protoneutron stars (Horowitz and Li, 1998) and for r-process nucleosynthesis in the neutrino-driven wind (Horowitz and Li, 1999).
