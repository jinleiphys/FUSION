# Spectrum of $S$- and $P$-wave $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}'$ $(\bar{q},\bar{q}' = \bar{u}, \bar{d}, \bar{s})$ systems in a chiral SU(3) quark model

> Inspired by the resonance $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ recently observed by the LHCb Collaboration, we systematically explore the $S$- and $P$-wave $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}'$ $(\bar{q},\bar{q}' = \bar{u}, \bar{d}, \bar{s})$ systems in a chiral SU(3) quark model. The Hamiltonian contains the kinetic energy, the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potential, the confinement potential, and the one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential stemming from the coupling of quark and chiral fields. The Schrödinger equation is solved by use of the variational method with the spacial trial wave functions chosen as Gaussian functions. It is found that the lowest state has a mass $3879$ MeV, isospin and spin-parity $IJ^P=01^+$, and quark constituent $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$, in agreement with the experimentally observed $T_{cc}^+(3875)$. This state is approximately at the calculated $DD^\ast$ threshold, and has a root-mean-square radius about $0.48$ fm. These demonstrates that the $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ can be accommodated as a stable and compact tetraquark sate in the chiral SU(3) quark model. All the other $S$- and $P$-wave $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}'$ $(\bar{q},\bar{q}' = \bar{u}, \bar{d}, \bar{s})$ states lie about one hundred to few hundreds MeV higher than the corresponding meson-meson thresholds, and thus are not suggested to be candidates of stable and compact tetraquark states due to their fall-apart decays to two mesons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the recently observed $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ resonance can be accommodated as a stable and compact tetraquark state within the chiral SU(3) quark model, but only the lowest state with mass 3879 MeV, isospin and spin-parity $IJ^P=01^+$, and quark constituent $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ matches the experimental observation; all other $S$- and $P$-wave $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}'$ states lie above their corresponding meson-meson thresholds and are therefore not candidates for stable compact tetraquarks.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation for $S$- and $P$-wave $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}'$ systems using a variational method with Gaussian trial wave functions. The Hamiltonian includes kinetic energy, one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potential, confinement potential, and one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential from chiral field coupling, with parameters for light quarks taken from a previous fit to baryon masses and nucleon-nucleon scattering (Huang 2018), and charm quark parameters fixed by fitting known charmed meson and baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- The lowest state has mass 3879 MeV, isospin and spin-parity $IJ^P=01^+$, and quark constituent $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$, approximately at the calculated $DD^\ast$ threshold
- This state has a root-mean-square radius of about 0.48 fm
- The experimentally observed $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ has mass $M=3875.1$ MeV $+\delta m$ with $\delta m=-273\pm 61\pm5^{+11}_{-14}$ keV and decay width $\Gamma = 410 \pm 165\pm43^{+18}_{-38}$ keV
- Calculated $D^0$ mass: 1866.9 MeV (PDG: 1864.84$\pm$0.05 MeV)
- Calculated $D^{*+}$ mass: 2011.6 MeV (PDG: 2010.26$\pm$0.05 MeV)
- Calculated $J/\psi(1S)$ mass: 3096.8 MeV (PDG: 3096.9$\pm$0.006 MeV)
- Calculated $\Lambda_c^+$ mass: 2245.4 MeV (PDG: 2286.46$\pm$0.14 MeV)
- Charm quark mass $m_c = 1500$ MeV, OGE coupling $g_c = 0.635$

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model previously applied to baryon ground states and nucleon-nucleon scattering (Huang 2018) to the tetraquark sector. It addresses conflicting results in the literature: some quark model studies (Ebert 2007, Lu 2020, Wang 2022) found no stable doubly-charmed tetraquarks, while others (Vijande 2009, Meng 2020, Deng 2021) found bound states below threshold. The present calculation finds the $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ state with $J^P=1^+$ lies approximately at threshold, supporting a compact tetraquark interpretation rather than a loosely bound molecule. The results enable future studies of decay widths and production mechanisms for the $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ and suggest that searches for other doubly-charmed tetraquarks with different flavor content or quantum numbers are unlikely to yield stable states.
