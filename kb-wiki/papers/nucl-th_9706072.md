# Influence of final state interaction on incoherent η-photoproduction on the deuteron near threshold

> Incoherent $η$-photoproduction on the deuteron is studied for photon energies from threshold up to 720 MeV. The elementary $γN \to ηN$ amplitude is constructed in the frame of a conventional isobar model. Effects of final state interaction are investigated and their role in the cross section as well as in polarization observables is found to be important. Inclusion of such effects leads to a satisfactory agreement with experimental data in the near threshold region. In addition, recent experimental results on the coherent reaction $d(γ,η)d$ are discussed.

# Influence of final state interaction on incoherent η-photoproduction on the deuteron near threshold

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that final state interactions (NN-rescattering and ηN-rescattering) are essential for reproducing experimental cross sections of incoherent η-photoproduction on the deuteron near threshold, where the pure spectator-nucleon model fails. The ratio of isoscalar to proton amplitude α = 0.11 provides the best agreement with data, but this value is inconsistent with the larger α ≈ 0.20–0.26 needed to describe coherent γd → ηd data within impulse approximation, indicating a remaining puzzle.

## Method

The authors construct the elementary γN → ηN amplitude using a nonrelativistic isobar model where only the S11(1535) resonance contributes, with parameters fitted to p(γ,η)p data. For the deuteron reaction γd → ηnp, they employ time-ordered perturbation theory including three contributions: impulse approximation (spectator model), NN-rescattering restricted to the ¹S₀ channel using the Bonn OBEPQ potential, and ηN-rescattering via N* formation. The calculations cover photon energies from threshold up to 720 MeV in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range studied: from threshold up to 720 MeV
- S11(1535) resonance mass: M_N* = 1535 MeV
- Total decay width at resonance: Γ = 160 MeV, with Γ_ηN = 0.5 Γ, Γ_πN = 0.4 Γ, Γ_ππN = 0.1 Γ
- ηNN* coupling constant: g_ηNN* = 2.10
- πNN* coupling constant: g_πNN* = 1.19
- Proton helicity amplitude: A_{1/2}^p = 104 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2}
- Best-fit ratio of isoscalar to proton amplitude: α = 0.11
- Ratio of neutron to proton cross sections corresponding to α = 0.11: σ_n/σ_p = 0.61
- At ω = 720 MeV, the spectator model underestimates backward-angle data by roughly a factor of 3
- NN-rescattering increases the spectator result by about 10% at ω = 720 MeV
- The ratio α extracted from quasifree kinematics: α_qf = 0.09 ± 0.02
- The ratio α needed for coherent reaction in impulse approximation: α_coh = 0.20 ± 0.02

## Context

This work extends previous studies of η-photoproduction on the deuteron by Krusche (1995), Bacci (1969), and others, who established the dominant isovector character of the S11(1535) photoexcitation. The authors build on the spectator-nucleon model used in experiments by Hoffmann (1997) to extract the neutron-to-proton cross section ratio, but show that final state interactions become crucial near threshold. The approach follows similar methodology to Paul (1995) for coherent π⁰-photoproduction on the deuteron and Fix (1997) for η-rescattering effects. The results enable more reliable extraction of elementary neutron amplitudes from deuteron data and highlight the need for further theoretical and experimental work to resolve the discrepancy between coherent and incoherent reactions regarding the isoscalar amplitude.
