# Advances in the field-theoretic understanding of pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions

> We study the production amplitude for the reaction $NN\to NNπ$ up to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory. We show that the irreducible chiral loops at this order exactly cancel those terms that arise from the off-shell parts of the $πN$ rescattering vertex. This cancellation is required for formal consistency of the whole scheme. The net effect of the inclusion of all next-to-leading order loops is to enhance the leading rescattering amplitude by a factor of 4/3 compared to phenomenological studies, bringing its contribution to the cross section for $pp\to dπ^+$ close to the experimental value.

## Key claim

The irreducible chiral loops at next-to-leading order exactly cancel the terms arising from off-shell parts of the πN rescattering vertex, resolving a formal inconsistency in the field-theoretic treatment; the net effect enhances the leading rescattering amplitude by a factor of 4/3 compared to phenomenological studies, bringing the cross section for pp → dπ⁺ close to the experimental value.

## Method

The authors computed the production amplitude for NN → NNπ up to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, using a modified counting scheme where the expansion parameter is χ = √(m_π/M) due to the large momentum transfer at threshold. They identified irreducible pieces in diagrams that appear reducible, arising from the energy dependence of the leading-order \(\bar N N \pi \pi\) vertex, and evaluated their contributions alongside the standard NLO loop diagrams for both pp → ppπ⁰ and pp → dπ⁺ channels.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter for the modified counting scheme is χ = √(m_π/M), where m_π is the pion mass and M is the nucleon mass.
- For pp → ppπ⁰, the sum of NLO loop contributions (diagrams 1b+1c+1d) cancels exactly: A = (g_A³)/(256f_π⁵) × (-2+3-1) × |p| = 0.
- For pp → dπ⁺, the sum of all irreducible NLO contributions (1a1(irr)+1a2(irr)+1b+1c+1d) cancels exactly: A = (g_A³)/(256f_π⁵) × (-3/4-1/4-2+3+0) × |p| = 0.
- The irreducible piece of the first diagram (1a1) for pp → dπ⁺ contributes A = -(3/4) × (g_A³|p|)/(256f_π⁵).
- The on-shell value of the \(\bar N N \pi \pi\) vertex is 2 m_π, replacing the commonly used 3/2 m_π, which enhances the isovector πN-rescattering amplitude by a factor of 4/3.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions by Hanhart (2002) and resolves a problem raised by Gårdestig (2005), who proposed a counterterm at leading order that would violate chiral symmetry. The cancellation of off-shell sensitivity in the NLO loops removes the need for such a counterterm and provides a consistent field-theoretic framework, enabling improved calculations of isospin violation in few-nucleon processes and absorptive corrections to πd scattering.
