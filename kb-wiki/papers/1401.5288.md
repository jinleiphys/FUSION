# Neutron Production Rates by Inverse-Beta Decay in Fully Ionized Plasmas

> Recently we showed that the nuclear transmutation rates are largely overestimated in the Widom-Larsen theory of the so called `Low Energy Nuclear Reactions'. Here we show that unbound plasma electrons are even less likely to initiate nuclear transmutations.

# Neutron Production Rates by Inverse-Beta Decay in Fully Ionized Plasmas

## Key claim
Even assuming the large electron mass renormalization factor (β=20) required by the Widom-Larsen theory, unbound plasma electrons produce neutron capture rates that never approach the bound-electron capture rate derived by the authors, let alone the larger rates claimed by Widom and Larsen; the plasma enhancement saturates due to Debye screening at high densities, making the reaction even less likely than previously estimated.

## Method
The authors compute neutron production rates from inverse-beta decay (e⁻ + p → n + νₑ) in a fully ionized plasma by replacing the bound-electron wavefunction at the origin with the product of electron density and the Sommerfeld enhancement factor, using standard electroweak theory. They derive the Sommerfeld factor for both unscreened Coulomb plasmas (S₀ = 2πα/v) and Debye-screened plasmas (S = a_D / a_B^⋆), then compare the resulting plasma rates to their previously calculated bound-electron rate at β=20. A critical velocity v_crit is defined where the de Broglie wavelength equals the Debye length, determining which Sommerfeld factor applies.

## Key numbers
- Threshold mass renormalization factor required for the reaction: β = m^⋆/m ≥ (m_n − m_p)/m ≈ 2.8
- Reference mass renormalization from Widom-Larsen: β = 20
- Bound-electron capture rate at β=20: Γ_bound = 1.8 × 10⁻³ Hz
- Debye length at T = 5000 K and n_e = 10²⁰ cm⁻³: a_D = 4.87 Å
- Debye mass at same reference point: m_D = 404 eV
- Critical velocity at reference point (β=20, n=10²⁰ cm⁻³, T=5000 K): v_crit = 2.48 × 10⁻⁴
- Thermal velocity at reference point: v_th = 3.6 × 10⁻⁴ (for β=20, T=5000 K)
- Density range considered: 10¹⁴ cm⁻³ ≤ n ≤ 6 × 10²³ cm⁻³
- Density at which Debye length equals Bohr radius: approximately 2.5 × 10²¹ cm⁻³
- Density at which v_crit exceeds v_th: approximately 2 × 10²⁰ cm⁻³

## Context
This paper extends the authors' previous work (Maiani, Polosa, Riquer, 2013) which showed that nuclear transmutation rates in the Widom-Larsen theory of Low Energy Nuclear Reactions (LENR) were largely overestimated for bound electrons. Here they address subsequent arguments by Widom and Larsen (2013) that unbound plasma electrons would produce higher rates due to the Sommerfeld enhancement. The authors find that even with Debye screening considered, plasma rates remain far below the bound-electron rate, consistent with the lack of neutron observation in plasma discharge experiments reported by ENEA. The work rules out plasma-based LENR mechanisms as viable sources of observable neutron production.
