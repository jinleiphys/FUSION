# Application of the weak-binding relation with range correction

> The weak-binding relation is a useful tool to study the internal structure of hadrons from the observable quantities. We introduce the range correction in the weak-binding relation for the system having a sizable magnitude of the effective range, and show that the applicability of the weak-binding relation can be enlarged by the range correction. Thanks to the low-energy universality, the weak-binding relation can be used to study the structure of shallow bound states in any systems with different length scales. We apply the weak-binding relation to actual systems, including hadrons, hypernuclei, and atoms and show the importance of the range correction.

## Key claim

The weak-binding relation, which determines the compositeness (probability of finding two-body scattering states in the bound state wavefunction) from observables, can be applied to a wider range of systems by redefining the correction term's length scale as the larger of the interaction range \(R_{\rm int}\) and the effective-range length scale \(R_{\rm eff}=|r_e|\), rather than using \(R_{\rm int}\) alone.

## Method

The authors modify the weak-binding relation \(a_0 = R [2X/(1+X) + \mathcal{O}(R_{\rm typ}/R)]\) by setting \(R_{\rm typ} = \max\{R_{\rm int}, R_{\rm eff}\}\), where \(R = 1/\sqrt{2\mu B}\) is the binding-energy length scale, \(a_0\) is the scattering length, and \(r_e\) is the effective range. They test the improved relation against an effective-range model with a finite cutoff where the exact compositeness is known (\(X=1\)), and then apply it to six bound systems: the deuteron, \(X(3872)\), the \(N\Omega\) dibaryon, the \(\Omega\Omega\) dibaryon, the hypertriton, and the \({}^{4}{\rm He}\) dimer. For each system, they collect \(a_0\), \(r_e\), binding energy \(B\), and an estimated \(R_{\rm int}\) from the literature, then compute the compositeness \(X\) with uncertainties using both the old (\(R_{\rm typ}=R_{\rm int}\)) and improved (\(R_{\rm typ}=R_{\rm eff}\)) correction terms.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron (\(pn\)): \(a_0 = 5.42\) fm, \(r_e = 1.75\) fm, \(B = 2.22\) MeV; compositeness with improved relation: \(0.738 \leq X \leq 1\).
- \(X(3872)\) (\(D^0\bar{D}^{*0}\)): \(a_0 = 28.5\) fm, \(r_e = -5.34\) fm, \(B = 18\) keV; compositeness with improved relation: \(0.530 \leq X \leq 1\).
- \(N\Omega\) dibaryon (\(n\Omega\)): \(a_0 = 5.30\) fm, \(r_e = 1.26\) fm, \(B = 1.54\) MeV; old relation gives lower bound 1.04 (unphysical), improved relation gives \(0.801 \leq X \leq 1\).
- \(\Omega\Omega\) dibaryon: \(a_0 = 4.6\) fm, \(r_e = 1.27\) fm, \(B = 1.6\) MeV; compositeness with improved relation: \(0.791 \leq X \leq 1\).
- Hypertriton (\(d\Lambda\)): \(a_0 = 16.8\) fm, \(r_e = 2.3\) fm, \(B = 0.13\) MeV; compositeness with improved relation: \(0.745 \leq X \leq 1\).
- \({}^{4}{\rm He}\) dimer: \(a_0 = 189\) Bohr radii, \(r_e = 13.8\) Bohr radii, \(B = 1.30\) mK; compositeness with improved relation: \(0.926 \leq X \leq 1\).

## Context

This work extends the weak-binding relation originally formulated by Weinberg (1965) for the deuteron and later applied to hadrons by Kamiya et al. (2015, 2016). The authors had previously introduced the range correction in Kinugawa and Hyodo (2021a, 2021b). By applying the improved relation to systems with sizable effective ranges, including exotic hadron candidates like \(X(3872)\) and lattice-QCD-predicted dibaryons from HAL QCD (2018) and Gongyo et al. (2017), they show that the range correction is essential for obtaining physically meaningful compositeness values (e.g., for the \(N\Omega\) dibaryon). This enables more reliable structure determination of shallow bound states across hadron, nuclear, and atomic physics.
