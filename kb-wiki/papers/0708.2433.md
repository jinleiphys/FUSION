# Relativistic hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions

> Relativistic hydrodynamics is essential to our current understanding of nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultrarelativistic energies (current experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, forthcoming experiments at the CERN Large Hadron Collider). This is an introduction to relativistic hydrodynamics for graduate students. It includes a detailed derivation of the equations, and a description of the hydrodynamical evolution of a heavy-ion collisions. Some knowledge of thermodynamics and special relativity is assumed.

## Key claim

Relativistic hydrodynamics provides a simple and general framework for describing nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultrarelativistic energies, relying on the single but strong assumption of local thermodynamic equilibrium. The paper presents this as an introductory pedagogical treatment, noting that even if hydrodynamics gives reasonable results, local equilibrium is at best an approximation and "not the end of the story."

## Method

The paper derives the equations of inviscid (ideal-fluid) relativistic hydrodynamics from thermodynamic identities and the assumption of local thermodynamic equilibrium, which implies isotropic properties in the fluid rest frame. It then applies these equations to model the hydrodynamical expansion of matter created in heavy-ion collisions, specifying initial conditions at a fixed proper time using Bjorken's boost-invariant prescription for longitudinal flow and gaussian profiles for the transverse and rapidity distributions of entropy density. The derivation covers baryon number conservation, energy-momentum conservation via the energy-momentum tensor, sound wave propagation, and the role of the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The collision time for two nuclei passing through each other at RHIC is approximately 0.15 fm/c, a factor 100 smaller than the transverse nuclear size R.
- For a central Au-Au collision, the rms transverse widths of the gaussian entropy density profile are sigma_x = sigma_y approximately 3 fm.
- For a non-central Au-Au collision at impact parameter b = 7 fm, sigma_x approximately 2 fm and sigma_y approximately 2.6 fm.
- Rapidity distributions of outgoing particles in symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC are fit by gaussians of width sigma_eta approximately 2.3.
- For a baryonless quark-gluon plasma, the number of degrees of freedom g is approximately 40 (16 for gluons and 24 for light u and d quarks).
- The entropy per particle in a quark-gluon plasma is approximately 4 (3.6 for bosons, 4.2 for fermions).

## Context

This paper is an introduction to relativistic hydrodynamics for graduate students, building on the foundational work of Landau (1953) and the textbook treatment in Landau's course. It extends the Bjorken boost-invariant picture (Bjorken, 1982) and reviews recent applications to heavy-ion collisions covered by Huovinen (2006) and Kolb (2003). The work enables students to understand how hydrodynamical models describe collective flow phenomena observed at RHIC, particularly transverse flow signatures, and to engage with current questions about the conditions for local equilibrium and the role of QCD plasma instabilities (Arnold, 2003).
