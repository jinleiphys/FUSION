# Longitudinal distribution of initial energy density and directed flow of charged particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the origin of the directed flow of charged particles produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Three different initial conditions, Boz$\dot{\textrm{e}}$k-Wyskiel, CCNU and Shen-Alzhrani, of energy density distributions are coupled to the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc, and their effects on the development of the anisotropic medium geometry, pressure gradient and radial flow are systematically compared. By comparing to experimental data at both RHIC and LHC, we find that the directed flow provides a unique constraint on the tilt of the initial medium profile in the plane spanned by the impact parameter and space-time rapidity. Within mid-rapidity, the counter-clockwise tilt is shown to be a crucial source of the positive/negative force by the pressure gradient along the impact parameter ($x$) direction at backward/forward rapidity, which drives a negative slope of the $x$ component of the medium flow velocity with respect to rapidity, and in the end the same feature of the charged particle directed flow.

## Key claim

The directed flow of charged particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is driven by a counter-clockwise tilt of the initial energy density profile in the plane spanned by the impact parameter and space-time rapidity, which produces a negative slope of the medium flow velocity with respect to rapidity; however, this mechanism depends on the specific parametrization of the initial condition, as the Shen-Alzhrani parametrization produces a qualitatively different average pressure gradient at mid-rapidity compared to the Bozek-Wyskiel and CCNU parametrizations.

## Method

Three different initial condition parametrizations (Bozek-Wyskiel, CCNU, and Shen-Alzhrani) for the longitudinal energy density distribution were each coupled to the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc. The time evolution of the average pressure gradient and flow velocity was computed for each parametrization, and the final-state charged particle directed flow was compared to experimental data from Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC.

## Key numbers

* For 5-40% Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the initial time of hydrodynamic evolution tau_0 = 0.6 fm, with overall normalization factor K = 35.5 GeV/fm^3, collision hardness parameter alpha = 0.05, and nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section sigma_NN = 42 mb.
* For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the parameters are tau_0 = 0.6 fm, K = 103.0 GeV/fm^3, alpha = 0.05, and sigma_NN = 64 mb.
* The rapidity plateau width eta_w = 1.3 for Au+Au at 200 GeV and eta_w = 2.0 for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV; the Gaussian decay width sigma_eta = 1.5 for Au+Au and 1.8 for Pb+Pb.
* The freeze-out temperature T_frz = 137 MeV and the shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio eta_v/s = 0.08 were used for all collision systems.
* The model parameter eta_m = 2.8 was used for the Bozek-Wyskiel parametrization, H_t = 2.9 for the CCNU parametrization, and f = 0.15 for the Shen-Alzhrani parametrization in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bozek-Wyskiel parametrization (Bozek, 2010) and the CCNU parametrization (Jiang, 2021) by systematically comparing them with the Shen-Alzhrani parametrization (Shen, 2020; Ryu, 2021) within a single hydrodynamic framework (CLVisc). The study enables future investigations of how the longitudinal tilt of the initial fireball affects other observables such as heavy flavor meson directed flow, and motivates the inclusion of more sophisticated initial velocity profiles beyond the Bjorken approximation.
