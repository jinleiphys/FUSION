# Flavor structure of the unpolarized and longitudinally-polarized sea-quark distributions in the nucleon

> It is now widely recognized that a key to unravel the nonperturbative chiral-dynamics of QCD hidden in the deep-inelastic-scattering observables is the flavor structure of sea-quark distributions in the nucleon. We analyze the flavor structure of the nucleon sea in both of the unpolarized and longitudinally polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) within a single theoretical framework of the flavor SU(3) chiral quark soliton model (CQSM), which contains only one adjustable parameter $Δm_s$, the effective mass difference between the strange and nonstrange quarks. A particular attention is paid to a nontrivial correlation between the flavor asymmetry of the unpolarized and longitudinally polarized sea-quark distributions and also to a possible particle-antiparticle asymmetry of the strange quark distributions in the nucleon. We also investigate the charge-symmetry-violation (CSV) effects in the parton distribution functions exactly within the same theretical framework, which is expected to provide us with valuable information on the relative importance of the asymmetry of the strange and antistrange distributions and the CSV effects in the valence-quark distributions inside the nucleon in the resolution scenario of the so-called NuTeV anomaly in the extraction of the Weinberg angle.

## Key claim

The flavor SU(3) chiral quark soliton model (CQSM), with only one adjustable parameter (the effective strange-nonstrange quark mass difference Δm_s), can simultaneously reproduce the flavor structure of both unpolarized and longitudinally polarized sea-quark distributions, though the model overestimates the strange quark sum s(x)+s̄(x) by roughly a factor of two and the singlet and valence distributions by about 20% due to the neglect of explicit gluon degrees of freedom.

## Method

The author uses the flavor SU(3) chiral quark soliton model (CQSM), a relativistic mean-field theory of quarks with infinitely many Dirac-sea orbitals, to compute unpolarized and longitudinally polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) at the model scale Q²_ini = 0.30 GeV². These model predictions are then evolved to Q² = 2 GeV² using next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD evolution equations and compared with the NNPDF2.1 NLO global fits. The single free parameter Δm_s is fixed to 80 MeV by fitting the shape of the empirical strange quark distribution s(x)+s̄(x).

## Key numbers

- Δm_s = 80 MeV: the effective mass difference between strange and nonstrange quarks, fixed as the only adjustable parameter in the model.
- Q²_ini = 0.30 GeV²: the starting energy scale for QCD evolution used in the CQSM, corresponding to approximately (550 MeV)².
- At Q² = 4 GeV², the empirical quark and gluon momentum fractions from MRST2004 are ⟨x⟩^Q = 0.579 and ⟨x⟩^G = 0.421.
- The model prediction for the strange sum s(x)+s̄(x) overestimates the NNPDF fit by roughly a factor of two; after applying an ad hoc reduction factor of 1/2, the prediction becomes order-of-magnitude consistent with the fit.
- The strange-antistrange asymmetry x S⁻(x) ≡ x [s(x) − s̄(x)] predicted by the CQSM peaks around x ∼ 0.5, consistent with the NNPDF fit in both peak position and absolute magnitude.
- The model overestimates the singlet distribution Σ(x) and the total valence distribution V(x) by about 20% compared to NNPDF fits, attributed to the neglect of gluon degrees of freedom which carry about 20% of nucleon momentum even at the model scale.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies using the flavor SU(2) and SU(3) CQSM (Wakamatsu 1991, 1992, 2003) to simultaneously analyze unpolarized and polarized sea-quark flavor structure, including strange-antistrange asymmetry and charge-symmetry-violation (CSV) effects. The results are compared with the unbiased NNPDF2.1 global fits and with the HERMES semi-inclusive DIS data on strange quark distributions. The analysis aims to clarify the relative importance of s-s̄ asymmetry versus CSV effects in valence-quark distributions for resolving the NuTeV anomaly in the Weinberg angle extraction, a question previously addressed by Sather (1992), Rodionov, Thomas, and Londergan (1994), and Londergan and Thomas (2003). The model predictions are contrasted with meson cloud models (which contain many free parameters) and with the 5-quark intrinsic-sea model of Chang and Peng (2011), which interprets the two-component structure of the HERMES strange data as arising from intrinsic versus extrinsic sea contributions.
