# Is there a bound Lambda-n-n ?

> The HypHI Collaboration at GSI argued recently for a (Lambda-n-n) bound state from the observation of its two-body (t + pi-) weak-decay mode. We derive constraints from several hypernuclear systems, in particular from the A=4 hypernuclei with full consideration of (Lambda N <--> Sigma N) coupling, to rule out a bound (Lambda-n-n).

# Is there a bound Λ-n-n?

## Key claim

The paper argues that a bound Λnn (³Λn) state, as claimed by the HypHI Collaboration based on observation of a t+π⁻ weak-decay signal, is ruled out by constraints from several hypernuclear systems, particularly the A=4 hypernuclei with full consideration of ΛN ↔ ΣN coupling. The authors demonstrate that the ΛN interactions required to bind ³Λn are inconsistent with measured Λp scattering cross sections, the binding energy of ³ΛH, and the 0⁺–1⁺ excitation energy of A=4 hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors solve YNN Faddeev equations with two-body YN input pairwise separable interactions constructed from low-energy YN scattering parameters, using Yamaguchi form factors within rank-one separable interactions. They also employ G-matrix 0sN0sY effective interactions derived from the Nijmegen soft-core interaction model NSC97 to incorporate ΛN ↔ ΣN coupling effects, and perform a series of Λnn Faddeev calculations while varying the nn interaction from SU(4) symmetric values to empirical values for the unbound dineutron.

## Key numbers

- The experimental binding energy of ³ΛH (T=0) is BΛ(³ΛH) = 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV with spin-parity Jᴾ = ½⁺
- The HypHI Collaboration reported a binding energy for the conjectured ³Λn of 0.5 ± 1.1 ± 2.2 MeV with standard deviation σ = 5.4 ± 1.4 MeV
- For r = 2.5 fm, the ΛN scattering length required to bind ³Λn is a = -4.491 fm, giving σΛpᵗᵒᵗ = 953.8 mb at pΛ = 145 MeV/c, compared to the measured σΛpᵗᵒᵗ = 180 ± 22 mb
- For r = 3.5 fm, the ΛN scattering length required to bind ³Λn is a = -5.930 fm, giving σΛpᵗᵒᵗ = 943.1 mb
- When ³Λn is just bound (x = 1.614), the calculated BΛ(³ΛH T=0, J=½⁺) = 0.625 MeV and BΛ(³ΛH T=0, J=³/₂⁺) = 3.772 MeV, far exceeding the experimental 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV
- The 0⁺–1⁺ splitting in A=4 hypernuclei is approximately 1.1 MeV
- The ΛN ↔ ΣN coupling parameters from NSC97e are V̄ΛΣ = 2.96 MeV and ΔΛΣ = 5.09 MeV
- The maximum δBΛ = BΛᵀ⁼¹(½⁺) – BΛᵀ⁼⁰(½⁺) in the SU(4) limit is 0.26 MeV, giving BΛ(³Λn) = 0.39 ± 0.05 MeV relative to a bound dineutron core
- With empirical nn scattering parameters (aₛ = -17.612 fm, rₛ = 2.881 fm), ³Λn becomes unbound

## Context

This work extends the long-standing theoretical consensus, dating back to Downs and Dalitz (1959), that the isotriplet (³Λn, ³ΛHᵀ⁼¹, ³ΛHe) hypernuclei do not form bound states. It contradicts the claim by the HypHI Collaboration (Rap13) for a particle-stable ³Λn. The analysis builds on Faddeev equation studies by Garcilazo (1987), Miyagawa et al. (1995), and Hiyama et al. (2014) using various Nijmegen YN potentials, as well as chiral constituent quark model studies by Garcilazo et al. (2007, 2014) and NLO chiral EFT YN interactions by Gazda et al. (2014). The paper establishes connections between ³Λn and A=4 hypernuclear spectroscopy using the effective interaction framework of Akaishi et al. (2000) and Millener (2008, 2012), demonstrating that a bound ³Λn cannot be accommodated into hypernuclear physics. The work enables future experimental investigations into alternative origins of the t+π⁻ signal observed by HypHI.
