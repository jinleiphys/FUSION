# On the quantum numbers of the $X(1880)$

> We study the properties of the $X(1880)$, the structure around the $\bar{p}p$ threshold that appears in the $3(π^+π^-)$ invariant mass spectrum in the decay process of $J/ψ\to γ3(π^+π^-)$. Nucleon-antinucleon rescattering is taken into account in our analysis, and the decay amplitude of $J/ψ\to γ3(π^+π^-)$ can be obtained by the distorted wave Born approximation. With these amplitudes, we analyze the contributions to the $X(1880)$ from different partial waves. Our analysis suggests that the $X(1880)$ should be isoscalar $0^{-+}$, and it is generated by the threshold behavior.

## Key claim

The X(1880) structure near the \(\bar{p}p\) threshold is most likely an isoscalar \(0^{-+}\) state generated by threshold effects from nucleon-antinucleon rescattering, rather than a glueball or a conventional resonance. The analysis suggests this assignment based on a combined fit to multiple datasets, with the caveat that the isovector \(^1S_0\) wave is a much worse but still possible alternative.

## Method

The authors performed a combined partial wave analysis of three processes: \(J/\psi \to \gamma 3(\pi^+\pi^-)\), \(J/\psi \to \gamma p\bar{p}\), and \(p\bar{p} \to 3(\pi^+\pi^-)\). They used the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) to construct decay amplitudes, with the \(\bar{N}N\) scattering amplitudes taken from chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO). Each of the five lowest partial waves (\(^1S_0\), \(^3P_0\), \(^3P_1\), \(^1D_2\), \(^3P_2\)) in both isoscalar and isovector channels was fitted individually to all datasets, and the fit quality was assessed via \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}\) and p-values.

## Key numbers

* Mass of X(1880): \(M = 1882.1 \pm 1.7 \pm 0.7\) MeV (from BESIII, 2023).
* Width of X(1880): \(\Gamma = 30.7 \pm 5.5 \pm 2.4\) MeV (from BESIII, 2023).
* Best-fit \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}\) for the isoscalar \(^1S_0\) wave: 1.99 (cut-off \(R = 1.0\) fm), compared to 3.30 for the isovector \(^1S_0\) wave and values from 6.74 to 66.18 for all other partial waves.
* p-value for the isoscalar \(^1S_0\) wave: 0.215 (cut-off \(R = 1.0\) fm, after removing three outlier data points); all other partial waves had p-values below \(10^{-7}\).
* \(\bar{N}N\) scattering lengths from ChEFT (Dai, 2017): \(a_{^{11}S_0} = -0.20 - 1.23i\) fm and \(a_{^{31}S_0} = 1.05 - 0.58i\) fm.
* Effective ranges from ChEFT (Dai, 2017): \(r_{^{11}S_0} = -3.98 - 11.03i\) fm and \(r_{^{31}S_0} = 0.65 - 2.08i\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the X(1880) first reported by BESIII (2023) in the \(3(\pi^+\pi^-)\) invariant mass spectrum of \(J/\psi \to \gamma 3(\pi^+\pi^-)\). It uses the \(\bar{N}N\) scattering amplitudes from chiral effective field theory developed by Dai (2017) and the DWBA framework previously applied to related processes by Kang (2015) and Yang (2022). The conclusion that the X(1880) is a threshold cusp rather than a glueball contradicts the glueball interpretation suggested by some lattice QCD studies (Sun, 2018; Sakai, 2023), which predict a pseudoscalar glueball mass around 2.6 GeV. The analysis enables future experimental checks of the quantum number assignment and the threshold nature of the structure.
