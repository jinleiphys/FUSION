# Incoherent Pion Production in Neutrino - Deuteron Reactions

> Within the multiple scattering formulation, the incoherent pion production in neutrino-deuteron reactions at energies near the $Δ(1232)$ resonance is investigated. The calculations include an impulse term and one-loop contributions from nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and pion-nucleon ($πN$) final state interactions. The input amplitudes of $πN$ scattering and electroweak pion production reaction on nucleon are generated from a dynamical model which describes very extensive data of $πN$ scattering and both the electromagnetic and the weak pion production reactions on the nucleon. The $NN$ scattering amplitudes are generated from the Bonn potential. The validity of the calculational procedures is established by giving a reasonably good description of the data of pion photo-production on the deuteron. The constructed model is then applied to predict the cross sections of $ν+ d \rightarrow μ^- + π^+ + n+ p$ and $ν+d \rightarrow μ^-+π^0+p+p$ reactions. The importance of including the $NN$ final state interactions to understand the experimental data of these neutrino-deuteron reactions is demonstrated. Our results strongly suggest that the spectator approximation used in the previous analyses to extract the pion production cross sections on the nucleon from the data on the deuteron is not valid for the $ν+ d \rightarrow μ^-+ π^+ +n+p$, but is a good approximation for $ν+ d \rightarrow μ^-+ π^0 + p+p$.

## Key claim

The spectator approximation used in previous analyses to extract single-pion production cross sections on the nucleon from deuteron data is not valid for the reaction $\nu + d \rightarrow \mu^- + \pi^+ + n + p$, but is a good approximation for $\nu + d \rightarrow \mu^- + \pi^0 + p + p$, based on calculations that include nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon final state interactions within a multiple scattering formulation.

## Method

The authors employed a multiple scattering formulation to calculate incoherent pion production in neutrino-deuteron reactions near the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance, including an impulse term and one-loop contributions from nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and pion-nucleon ($\pi N$) final state interactions. The input amplitudes for $\pi N$ scattering and electroweak pion production on the nucleon were generated from the SL dynamical model, which describes extensive data on $\pi N$ scattering and electromagnetic and weak pion production on the nucleon, while the $NN$ scattering amplitudes were generated from the Bonn potential. The model was first validated by describing data on pion photo-production on the deuteron, then applied to predict cross sections for $\nu + d \rightarrow \mu^- + \pi^+ + n + p$ and $\nu + d \rightarrow \mu^- + \pi^0 + p + p$ reactions.

## Key numbers

* The total cross section of $\gamma + d \rightarrow \pi^0 + n + p$ is greatly reduced when the $np$ final state interaction term is added, while $\pi N$ re-scattering gives negligible contributions; the $np$ re-scattering effects are very large.
* For $\gamma + d \rightarrow \pi^- + p + p$, both the $pp$ and the $\pi N$ final state interactions are weak in this process.
* Predictions are made for incoming muon-neutrino energy $E_{\nu_\mu} = 1$ GeV, with outgoing muon energy $E_{\mu^-} = 550$, 600, 650 MeV and angle $\theta_{\mu^-} = 25$ degrees.

## Context

This work extends the multiple scattering formulation and the SL dynamical model (Sato and Lee, 2005) to neutrino-deuteron reactions, building on previous studies of pion photo-production on the deuteron by Darwish (2003), Fix (2005), Lev (2006), Schwamb (2010), and Tarasov (2011). The results enable more accurate extraction of neutrino-nucleon pion production cross sections from deuteron target data, which is critical for interpreting neutrino-nucleus reaction experiments and determining neutrino properties such as mass hierarchy and CP violation.
