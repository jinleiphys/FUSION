# A theoretical view of practical problems in interferometry

> Interferometry is discussed in terms of the representation of the source. In particular, scale-invariant 1-d hydrodynamics is revisited, and extended to the case of unequal transverse masses. It is argued that that kaon emission occurs over a short time interval. Exact results for models of two- and three-dimensional flow are presented, which exhibit altered scaling laws. Such qualitative trends, together with other observables, are vital if one is to draw conclusions about the source.

## Key claim

The paper argues that interferometric data from heavy-ion collisions cannot be directly inverted to obtain source parameters, and that a "step-by-step" strategy using progressively more complex analytic hydrodynamic models is necessary. The authors claim that kaon emission occurs over a short time interval, based on the absence of scaling violation in NA44 data, but this conclusion depends on the assumption of a sharp, common freeze-out surface for both pions and kaons.

## Method

The authors formulate interferometry at the operator level, expressing one- and two-particle spectra in terms of field operators on a freeze-out hypersurface. They extend the analytic calculation of the two-pion correlation function for scale-invariant 1-d hydrodynamics to the case of unequal transverse masses, using a saddle-point approximation with Boltzmann statistics. They also present exact analytic results for two-dimensional (cylindrical) and three-dimensional (spherical) boost-invariant flow models, deriving closed-form expressions for the correlation functions.

## Key numbers

* For 1-d boost-invariant flow, the longitudinal radius is given by \(R_L = \tau / \cosh y \cdot \sqrt{T_c / m_t}\), where \(\tau\) is the proper time, \(y\) is pair mid-rapidity, \(T_c\) is the critical temperature, and \(m_t\) is the transverse mass.
* For the kaon freeze-out scenario, the authors use \(T_c = 130\) MeV and \(\tau = 30\) fm for sharp freeze-out, and \(\tau_0 = 7\) fm with \(T_0 = 180\) MeV for gradual decoupling (assuming \(\tau T^3 = \text{const}\)).
* For 2-d cylindrical flow, the one-particle spectrum is \(k^0 dN_1/d\vec{k} = 2\pi L \tau^2 T_c e^{-m_z/T_c} / (m_z/T_c) \cdot [m_z/T_c - 1]\), where \(m_z\) is the longitudinal mass.
* For 3-d spherical flow, the one-particle spectrum is \(k^0 dN_1/d\vec{k} = 4\pi \tau^3 T_c K_2(m/T_c)\), where \(K_2\) is the modified Bessel function and \(m\) is the particle mass.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Makhlin and Sinykov (1987) for 1-d boost-invariant hydrodynamics to the case of unequal transverse masses, and provides exact analytic results for 2-d and 3-d flow models that were previously unavailable. The paper addresses controversies in the mathematical formulation of interferometry, particularly the use of Wigner phase-space distributions versus operator methods, following the work of Pratt (1984) and Podgoretskii (1989). The results enable systematic fitting of model parameters \(T_c\) and \(\tau\) directly from correlation function data without intermediate Gaussian approximations, and provide a framework for testing freeze-out dynamics at RHIC energies through combined pion and kaon interferometry.
