# Comment on ``Strangeness enhancement in $p+A$ and S$+A$ interactions at energies near 200 $A$ GeV"

> We argue that the recent analysis of strangeness production in nuclear collisions at 200 $A$ GeV/$c$ performed by Topor Pop {\it et al.} \cite{To:95} is flawed. The conclusions are based on an erroneous interpretation of the data and the numerical model results. The term ``strangeness enhancement" is used in a misleading way.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the analysis of strangeness production in nuclear collisions at 200 \(A\) GeV/\(c\) by Topor Pop et al. is flawed because the HIJING model fails to reproduce \(p+p\) data at the claimed level of accuracy, and the term "strangeness enhancement" is used in a misleading way that conflates model deficiencies with genuine physical effects.

## Method

The authors re-examine the comparison between HIJING model predictions and NA35 experimental data for strange particle yields in \(p+p\), \(p+A\), and \(A+A\) collisions at 200 \(A\) GeV/\(c\). They check the statistical significance of the claimed agreement between HIJING and \(p+p\) data using the published Table I, and evaluate whether the model's failure to reproduce baryon stopping and rapidity distributions invalidates its use as a baseline for extrapolation to nuclear collisions. They also identify that Topor Pop et al. did not apply experimental acceptance cuts to the model calculations, which can lower model predictions by up to a factor of 3.

## Key numbers

- The HIJING model overpredicts \(\langle \Lambda \rangle\) yields in \(p+p\) collisions by 6–7 standard deviations.
- The HIJING model overpredicts \(\langle K^0_S \rangle\) yields in \(p+p\) collisions by 9–10 standard deviations.
- The yields of \(\overline{p}\) and \(\overline{\Lambda}\) in \(p+p\) are overpredicted by HIJING but agree within 3 standard deviations.
- Failure to apply experimental acceptance cuts can lower model points by up to a factor of about 3.
- The claim of strangeness enhancement in \(p+S\) is based on an average of only 2 target nucleons struck by the incident proton.

## Context

This comment directly challenges the analysis of Topor Pop et al. (1995), who claimed to observe strangeness enhancement in light ion interactions using the HIJING model as a baseline. The authors note that previous work by Gazdzicki (1991) and the NA35 collaboration (1994) established that there is no strangeness enhancement in \(p+A\) interactions at 200 GeV/\(c\) based on 8 independent measurements across targets from Mg to Au. The comment clarifies that genuine strangeness enhancement is observed only in central nucleus-nucleus collisions (e.g., S+S and S+Ag at 200 \(A\) GeV/\(c\)), and that no existing microscopic model (including HIJING and VENUS) reproduces this effect. This enables future work to focus on the correct definition of strangeness enhancement via the strangeness suppression factor \(\lambda_S\) or the factor \(E_S\) introduced by the NA35 collaboration (1994), rather than relying on flawed model comparisons.
