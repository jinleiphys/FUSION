# Chiral condensate thermal evolution at finite baryon chemical potential within Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We present a model independent study of the chiral condensate evolution in a hadronic gas, in terms of temperature and baryon chemical potential. The meson-meson interactions are described within Chiral Perturbation Theory and the pion-nucleon interaction by means of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory, both at one loop, and nucleon-nucleon interactions can be safely neglected within our hadronic gas domain of validity. Together with the virial expansion, this provides a systematic expansion at low temperatures and chemical potentials, which includes the physical quark masses. This can serve as a guideline for further studies on the lattice. We also obtain estimates of the critical line of temperature and chemical potential where the chiral condensate melts, which systematically lie somewhat higher than recent lattice calculations but are consistent with several hadronic models. We have also estimated uncertainties due to chiral parameters, heavier hadrons and higher orders through unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Key claim

The chiral condensate melting line in the temperature–baryon chemical potential plane, computed from a model-independent virial expansion combined with Chiral Perturbation Theory and Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory at one loop, lies systematically somewhat higher than recent lattice calculations but is consistent with several hadronic models; the results are valid only within a restricted domain where the virial expansion converges (roughly T < 200 MeV and μ_B < 800 MeV) and nucleon density stays below half saturation density.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic virial expansion to compute the grand canonical potential of a hadronic gas, with meson–meson interactions described by one-loop SU(2) and SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory and pion–nucleon interactions described by one-loop Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory at O(p^3). The chiral condensate was obtained as the derivative of the pressure with respect to the light quark mass, using ChPT expressions for the hadron mass dependence on quark masses; nucleon–nucleon interactions were neglected, limiting the study to dilute hadronic gases.

## Key numbers

- The pion–nucleon sigma term from HBChPT is σ_πN = 40^{+17}_{-21} MeV (using the parameter set from Meissner).
- The c_h coefficients encoding hadron mass dependence on the quark mass are: c_π = 0.9^{+0.2}_{-0.4}, c_K = 0.5^{+0.4}_{-0.7}, c_η = 0.4^{+0.5}_{-0.7}, c_N = 3.6^{+1.5}_{-1.9}.
- At μ_B = 0, the free SU(2) gas melting temperature lies beyond the virial validity region; adding ππ interactions brings it to ~230 MeV, and further adding πN interactions reduces it to 217 MeV.
- At μ_B = 40–50 MeV (the region relevant for Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions), including the πN interaction decreases the melting temperature by roughly 10 MeV compared to the gas without πN interactions.
- The second-order virial expansion reproduces the exact free gas condensate with a relative error ε ≈ 0.05 for T < 200 MeV and μ_B < 800 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent approach combining the virial expansion and Chiral Perturbation Theory, previously applied at zero baryon chemical potential by Gerber and by Dobado and Pelaez, to finite baryon chemical potential. It provides a systematic low-temperature, low-chemical-potential calculation that includes physical quark masses, serving as a guideline for lattice QCD studies at finite density where the fermion sign problem complicates first-principles calculations. The results enable comparison with lattice estimates from de Forcrand and Philipsen and from Tawfik and Toublan, and with hadronic model calculations by Nyffeler and by Tawfik, while also estimating uncertainties from chiral parameters and higher orders via unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory.
