# The rapidity dependence of the average transverse momentum in p+Pb collisions at the LHC: the Color Glass Condensate versus hydrodynamics

> We show that in proton-lead (p+Pb) collisions at the LHC, the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) and hydrodynamics lead to qualitatively different behavior of the average transverse momentum, <p_T>, with the particles rapidity. In hydrodynamics, the <p_T> decreases as one goes from zero rapidity, y=0, to the proton fragmentation region since the number of particles decreases. In contrast, in the CGC the saturation momentum increases as one goes from y=0 to the proton fragmentation region, and so the <p_T> increases. At the LHC, the difference between the two models may be large enough to be tested experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the rapidity dependence of the average transverse momentum \(\langle p_\perp \rangle\) in p+Pb collisions at the LHC can distinguish between the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) and hydrodynamic models, because the CGC predicts an increase in \(\langle p_\perp \rangle\) from midrapidity toward the proton fragmentation region while hydrodynamics predicts a decrease; the difference may be large enough to be tested experimentally, but the CGC results rely on the assumption that the nucleus saturation momentum \(Q_A\) is much larger than the proton saturation momentum \(Q_p\).

## Method

The authors derive the average transverse momentum of gluons in the CGC using the \(k_\perp\)-factorization formalism with the McLerran-Venugopalan model, obtaining an expression proportional to the nucleus saturation momentum \(Q_A\) with logarithmic corrections. They then compute the ratio \(\langle p_\perp \rangle_y / \langle p_\perp \rangle_{y=0}\) by substituting the rapidity dependence of the saturation scales \(Q_A^2 \sim Q_0^2 N_{\text{part}}^{\text{Pb}} e^{\lambda y}\) and \(Q_p^2 \sim Q_0^2 e^{-\lambda y}\) with \(\lambda \approx 0.2\). For the hydrodynamic comparison, they perform 3+1 dimensional event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations with an initial entropy profile that includes an asymmetry between the proton and lead sides, tuned to reproduce experimental pseudorapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- The CGC calculation uses \(\lambda \approx 0.2\) for the rapidity evolution of the saturation momentum.
- The hydrodynamic simulation uses a beam rapidity \(y_{\rm beam} = 8.5\), a longitudinal profile width \(\sigma_\eta = 1.4\), and a plateau edge \(\eta_0 = 2.4\).
- The hydrodynamic results cover three centrality classes: 0-3%, 5-15%, and 40-60%.
- The CGC results are shown for \(N_{\text{part}}^{\text{Pb}}\) values ranging from 10 to 25.
- The average transverse momentum of produced pions in high multiplicity p+Pb collisions is approximately 0.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the debate between CGC and hydrodynamic interpretations of p+Pb collisions at the LHC, following observations of ridge-like correlations (CMS 2012, ALICE 2012, ATLAS 2013) and measurements of elliptic and triangular flow (Bozek 2011, Bzdak 2013). It proposes a new observable, the rapidity dependence of \(\langle p_\perp \rangle\), as a discriminator, complementing earlier proposals such as femtoscopy radii (Bozek 2013, Bzdak 2013) and the mass hierarchy of transverse momentum (Bozek 2013, CMS 2013). The results enable experimental tests at the LHC to determine whether collectivity or saturation dominates the dynamics, and the authors note the possibility that both mechanisms could operate in different rapidity regions.
