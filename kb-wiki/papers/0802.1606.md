# The old and the new of parity-violating two-pion-exchange $NN$ potential

> We consider the parity-violating two-pion-exchange potential obtained from the covariant formalism in the past and the state-of-the-art effective field theory approach. We discuss the behavior of the potential in coordinate space and its application to the parity-violating asymmetry in $\vec{n} p \to d γ$ at threshold.

## Key claim

The parity-violating two-pion-exchange potential from the covariant formalism is finite, has no unknown constant, and is less singular at short distances than the corresponding effective field theory (EFT) term; however, the uncertainty from degrees of freedom integrated out of the model remains significant, and the total correction to the asymmetry from two-pion-exchange and higher-order contributions is at most about 10%.

## Method

The authors compared the parity-violating two-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential derived from the covariant formalism of the 1970s with the state-of-the-art EFT approach. They took the large-nucleon-mass limit of the covariant spectral functions to extract the leading \(1/M\) terms and compared them to the EFT result. They then computed the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in the \(\vec{n} p \to d \gamma\) reaction at threshold using the Argonne v18 potential for the strong interaction, treating the weak coupling \(h^1_\pi\) perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- The covariant \(v_{56}\) contribution to the asymmetry factor \(a_\gamma\) is \(0.0093\); the covariant \(v_{75}\) contribution is \(-0.0040\); their sum is \(0.0053\).
- The leading-order large-mass (LM) \(v_{56}\) contribution to \(a_\gamma\) is \(0.0141\).
- The one-pion-exchange (OPE) contribution to \(a_\gamma\) is \(-0.1120\).
- The total two-pion-exchange and higher-order corrections to \(A_\gamma\) are at most about 10% of the OPE contribution.

## Context

This work extends the earlier covariant formulation of the parity-violating two-pion-exchange potential by Desplanques (1972, 1974) and Pirner (1973) and compares it with the modern EFT approach developed by Zhu et al. (2005) and applied by Hadjimichael et al. (2007) and Liu et al. (2007). The analysis shows that the leading \(1/M\) term from the covariant form is equivalent to the EFT two-pion-exchange term, but the covariant form contains additional higher-order \(1/M\) contributions that are non-negligible. The results enable an indirect estimation of the low-energy constant term in EFT and indicate that a precise measurement of \(A_\gamma\) could determine the weak \(\pi NN\) coupling constant \(h^1_\pi\) with an uncertainty of about 10%.
