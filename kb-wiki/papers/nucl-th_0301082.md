# Charmonium photoproduction

> The available data on $J/Ψ$ photoproduction are analyzed in terms of pomeron exchange, two gluon exchange and photon-gluon fusion models. Allowing the pomeron-quark interaction to be flavour dependent and introducing the soft and hard pomerons it is possible to reproduce the data at $\sqrt{s}{>}$10 GeV and small $|t|$. The two gluon exchange calculations indicate strong sensitivity to the gluon distribution function. The results obtained with the most modern MRST2001 and DL PDF reproduce the forward $J/Ψ$ photoproduction cross section at $\sqrt{s}{>}$10 GeV. The calculations with the photon-gluon fusion model and with MRST2001 and DL PDF are also in reasonable agreement with the data on the total $J/Ψ$ photoproduction cross section. We allocate the $J/Ψ$ photoproduction at low energies and large $|t|$ to the mechanism different from pomeron or two gluon exchanges. We consider that this might be axial vector trajectory exchange that couples to the axial form factor of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper claims that while pomeron exchange models (including both soft and hard pomerons) can reproduce J/Ψ photoproduction data at √s > 10 GeV and small |t|, they fail at low energies (√s < 10 GeV) and large |t|, where the authors propose that axial vector trajectory exchange coupling to the nucleon's axial form factor may be responsible.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic analysis of all available J/Ψ photoproduction data using three theoretical frameworks: pomeron exchange (with soft and hard pomeron trajectories), two-gluon exchange in perturbative QCD, and photon-gluon fusion. For the pomeron exchange model, they allowed the pomeron-quark coupling to be flavour dependent, fitting separate coupling constants for light quarks (β_u = β_d = 2.35 GeV⁻¹ from ω photoproduction) and charm quarks (β_c = 0.45 GeV⁻¹). For the two-gluon exchange and photon-gluon fusion models, they compared calculations using different gluon parton distribution functions (GRV98, MRST2001, CTEQ6, and DL).

## Key numbers

- The J/Ψ photoproduction threshold is E_γ ≈ 8.15 GeV
- The Cornell measurement at 9.0 ≤ E_γ ≤ 11.8 GeV gives dσ/dt = (1.01 ± 0.20) exp[(1.25 ± 0.20)t] nb/GeV², with total cross section σ = 0.48 ± 0.12 nb
- The soft pomeron coupling to charm quark was fitted as β_c = 0.45 GeV⁻¹, while the hard pomeron coupling to charm quark was fitted as β̃_c = 0.05 GeV⁻¹
- The hard pomeron trajectory is α(t) = 1.44 + 0.1t, with intercept α(0) ≈ 1.25 required by the forward cross section data
- The exponential slope b for J/Ψ photoproduction at E_γ < 100 GeV is b = 2.6 ± 0.4 GeV⁻², while at high energies b = 5.1 ± 0.2 GeV⁻²
- The ratio of forward J/Ψ to ω photoproduction cross sections predicted by soft pomeron exchange with flavour-independent coupling is 0.13, while the experimental ratio at √s ≈ 10 GeV is about 0.005
- The charm quark mass used in two-gluon exchange calculations was m_q = 1.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Regge theory analysis of vector meson photoproduction previously applied to ω mesons by Sibirtsev et al. (2002) and to ρ mesons by Donnachie and Landshoff (1992). The authors show that the flavour-independent pomeron-quark coupling assumption fails to simultaneously describe ω and J/Ψ photoproduction, requiring flavour-dependent couplings. The analysis enables future studies of the gluon distribution function through J/Ψ photoproduction at high energies, particularly testing the MRST2001 and CTEQ6 PDFs, and motivates investigation of axial vector exchange mechanisms for J/Ψ production near threshold.
