# ACHILLES: A novel event generator for electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We present a novel lepton-nucleus event generator: ACHILLES, A CHIcagoLand Lepton Event Simulator. The generator factorizes the primary interaction from the propagation of hadrons in the nucleus, which allows for a great deal of modularity, facilitating further improvements and interfaces with existing codes. We validate our generator against high quality electron-carbon scattering data in the quasielastic regime, including the recent CLAS/e4v reanalysis of existing data. We find good agreement in both inclusive and exclusive distributions. By varying the assumptions on the propagation of knocked out nucleons throughout the nucleus, we estimate a component of theoretical uncertainties. We also propose novel observables that will allow for further testing of lepton-nucleus scattering models. ACHILLES is readily extendable to generate neutrino-nucleus scattering events.

# ACHILLES: A novel event generator for electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
ACHILLES reproduces inclusive electron-carbon quasielastic scattering data well across multiple beam energies and angles, but the authors explicitly note that resonance production and meson-exchange currents are not yet implemented, so the code does not describe the second peak in the cross section. The factorization scheme that separates the primary interaction from hadron propagation neglects quantum interference effects, which the authors state are expected to be subdominant but require future investigation.

## Method
The generator factorizes lepton-nucleus scattering into a primary interaction vertex (described by one-body electromagnetic currents and a spectral function from correlated basis function theory) and a semi-classical intranuclear cascade for final-state interactions. The cascade propagates nucleons using relativistic kinematics with stochastic nucleon-nucleon scattering, sampling initial nuclear configurations from quantum Monte Carlo calculations and momenta from a local Fermi gas model. Two nuclear potential options are implemented: a non-relativistic three-parameter fit to infinite nuclear matter single-particle energies (Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini 1988) and a global optical potential fitted to proton-nucleus elastic and reaction cross section data (Cooper 2009).

## Key numbers
- Inclusive electron-carbon cross section at 730 MeV beam energy and 37 degree scattering angle: the quasielastic peak is well described by ACHILLES (data from OConnell 1987)
- Inclusive electron-carbon cross section at 961 MeV and 37.5 degrees: quasielastic peak reproduced (data from Sealock 1989)
- Inclusive electron-carbon cross section at 1300 MeV and 37.5 degrees: quasielastic peak reproduced (data from Sealock 1989)
- Inclusive electron-carbon cross section at 2500 MeV and 15 degrees: quasielastic peak reproduced (data from Zeller 1973)
- Nuclear saturation density used in the non-relativistic potential: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Non-relativistic potential parameters: alpha(rho) = 15.52 (rho/rho_0) + 24.93 (rho/rho_0)^2 MeV, beta(rho) = -116 (rho/rho_0) MeV, Lambda(rho) = 3.29 - 0.373 (rho/rho_0) fm^{-1}
- Optical potential valid for proton energies between 20 and 1040 MeV for several nuclear targets including carbon (Cooper 2009)

## Context
This work extends the intranuclear cascade algorithm developed by Isaacson (2020) by implementing nuclear potentials and validating against the recent CLAS/e4v reanalysis of electron-carbon data (CLAS 2021). It directly addresses the finding that existing event generators do not describe electron-nucleus scattering to the precision required by next-generation experiments such as DUNE (DUNE 2020). The code is designed for modularity and uses the HepMC3 output format (Buckley 2019) to interface with analysis tools like Rivet (Buckley 2010) and Nuisance (Stowell 2016). Future work includes implementing two-nucleon currents, pion production, and quantum interference effects in the cascade.
