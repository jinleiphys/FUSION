# The valence quark, sea, and gluon content of the pion from the parton distribution functions and the electromagnetic form factor

> We present a light-front model calculation of the pion parton distribution functions (PDFs) and the pion electromagnetic form factor. The pion state is modeled in terms of light-front wave functions (LFWFs) for the $q\bar q$, $q\bar q q\bar q$, $q\bar q g$, and $q\bar q gg$ components. We design the LFWFs so that the parameters in the longitudinal and transverse momentum space enter separately in the fit of the pion PDFs and the electromagnetic form factor, respectively. We extract the pion PDFs within the xFitter framework using available Drell-Yan and photon-production data. With the obtained parameters in the longitudinal-momentum space, we then fit the available experimental data on the pion electromagnetic form factor to constrain the remaining parameters in the transverse-momentum space. The results for the pion PDFs are compatible with existing extractions and lattice calculations, and the fit to the pion electromagnetic form factor data works quite successfully. The obtained parametrization for the LFWFs marks a step forward towards a unified description of different hadron distribution functions in both the longitudinal- and transverse-momentum space and will be further applied to a phenomenological study of transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution functions and generalized parton distributions.

## Key claim
The authors present a light-front model of the pion that includes Fock states up to two gluons (q-qbar, q-qbar-q-qbar, q-qbar-g, and q-qbar-gg) and fit the longitudinal-momentum parameters to parton distribution function (PDF) data and the transverse-momentum parameters to electromagnetic form factor data separately, achieving consistency with existing extractions but noting that the sea and gluon contributions are not well constrained by the available experimental data.

## Method
The pion state is expanded in light-front wave functions (LFWFs) for Fock components up to four partons, with the longitudinal and transverse momentum dependencies factorized. The longitudinal parameters are fitted to Drell-Yan data from NA10 and E615 experiments and prompt photon production data from WA70 using the xFitter framework, while the transverse parameters are subsequently fitted to available experimental data on the pion electromagnetic form factor. The model uses Brodsky-Lepage-Huang-type Gaussian functions for the transverse momentum dependence and expansions in Gegenbauer polynomials for the longitudinal momentum dependence.

## Key numbers
- Model scale fixed at mu_0^2 = 0.7225 GeV^2, well below the charm mass threshold m_c^2 = 2.04 GeV^2
- Factorization and renormalization scales set to mu_F = mu_R = 0.8 GeV
- Drell-Yan data from NA10 with pion beam energies E_pi = 194 and 286 GeV, and from E615 with E_pi = 252 GeV
- Prompt photon production data from WA70 with E_pi = 280 GeV
- After cuts: 91 data points from E615, 70 from NA10, total database of 260 points
- Cuts applied: 4.16 GeV < mu < 7.68 GeV, and x_F >= 0

## Context
This work extends light-front model calculations of the pion by including Fock states up to the two-gluon component, which the authors state is the largest basis used so far in such calculations. It builds on prior extractions of pion PDFs from Drell-Yan and photon-production data by groups such as Owens (1984), Sutton (1991), Gluck (1991, 1999), and more recent analyses by Barry (2018, 2021), Bourrely (2018, 2020, 2022), and the JAM collaboration (2022). The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations of pion PDFs by Gao (2022), Karthik (2021), and others. The parametrization is intended to enable future phenomenological studies of transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution functions and generalized parton distributions.
