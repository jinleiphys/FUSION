# The spectrum of $^{18}$O in terms of the multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry

> Background: The experimental study of the $^{6}$Li-transfer reaction and alpha-scattering resulted in a rich spectrum of $^{18}$O, including some strong indication for band structure. Different configurations are proposed for the structural interpretation of different energy domains. Purpose: We intend to investigate whether a uniform description is possible for the complete spectrum, including the high-lying states from scattering measurement. Methods: We apply the multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry for the determination of the shape isomers and for the calculation of the spectrum obtained from different reactions. Results: The shape isomers of $^{18}$O is obtained from the study of the stability and self-consistency of the SU(3) symmetry, which is in the intersection of the shell and different cluster configurations. The $^{14}$C+$^{4}$He spectrum is calculated, and its connection to other configurations is revealed. A very simple Hamiltonian is applied, including only a harmonic oscillator term, a quadrupole interaction and an $L^2$ term. Conclusions: The spectrum (from the ground state up to the high-lying states) can be reasonably well described by a $^{14}$C+$^{4}$He cluster configuration. All the state allows the $^{12}$C+$2n$+$^{4}$He clusterization as well, and has definite shell model content. For the lowest-lying three bands the shell configuration is unique, and the overlap of the shell and the different cluster configurations is 100%.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the complete spectrum of \(^{18}\)O, from the ground state up to high-lying resonances observed in alpha-scattering, can be reasonably well described by a \(^{14}\)C+\(^{4}\)He cluster configuration using a very simple three-parameter Hamiltonian, but this description is qualified by the fact that the model only accounts for states that also allow a \(^{12}\)C+\(2n\)+\(^{4}\)He clusterization and have definite shell model content, with 100% overlap between configurations only for the three lowest-lying bands.

## Method

The authors applied the multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (MUSY) to construct a unified model space for \(^{18}\)O that includes the shell model, the \(^{14}\)C+\(^{4}\)He binary cluster configuration, and the \(^{12}\)C+\(2n\)+\(^{4}\)He configuration. They used a Hamiltonian containing only a harmonic oscillator term, a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction (the second-order Casimir operator of SU(3)), and an \(L^2\) rotational term, with parameters fitted to experimental band structures. Shape isomers were determined via the Stability and self-Consistency of the SU(3) Symmetry (SCS) method, which uses a Nilsson-model calculation to find stable quadrupole shapes.

## Key numbers

* The Hamiltonian parameters fitted to the experimental data are \(\hbar\omega = 2.344\) MeV, \(a = -0.007257\) MeV, and \(d = 0.8572\).
* The ground state shape isomer has U(3) symmetry [8,4,4] with deformation parameters \((\beta, \gamma) = (0.31, 0.0^\circ)\) and axis ratio \(1.3:1.0:1\).
* The prolate shape isomer at \(2\hbar\omega\) excitation has U(3) symmetry [12,4,2] with deformation \((\beta, \gamma) = (0.67, 10.9^\circ)\) and axis ratio \(1.9:1.2:1\).
* The experimental moment of inertia for the \(0^{+}_{1}\) band is \(3.00\ \hbar^{2}/\text{MeV}\), while the theoretical value for the corresponding 0(4,0) band is \(1.93\ \hbar^{2}/\text{MeV}\).
* The distances between the centers of gravity of the clusters are 4.436 fm, 4.691 fm, and 4.997 fm for the [8,4,4], [10,4,3], and [12,4,2] configurations, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM) study of \(^{18}\)O by Lévai (1992) by incorporating a richer experimental dataset, including high-lying resonances from alpha-scattering experiments by Rogachev et al. (roga1, roga2), and by considering the interrelation of shell, \(^{14}\)C+\(\alpha\), and \(^{12}\)C+\(2n\)+\(\alpha\) configurations. The study contradicts the interpretation of von Oertzen et al. (vonOertzen) that a \(4\hbar\omega\) alpha-cluster shape isomer exists, as the SCS method found no such stable shape, and it also addresses the questions raised by Pirrie et al. (pirrie20) regarding the importance of alpha-cluster configurations. The results enable a unified description of the \(^{18}\)O spectrum and provide a framework for calculating alpha spectroscopic factors microscopically.
