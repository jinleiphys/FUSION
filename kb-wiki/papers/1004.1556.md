# Migration of nuclear shell gaps studied in the d(24Ne,p gamma)25Ne reaction

> The transfer of neutrons onto 24Ne has been measured using a reaccelerated radioactive beam of 24Ne to study the (d,p) reaction in inverse kinematics. The unusual raising of the first 3/2+ level in 25Ne and its significance in terms of the migration of the neutron magic number from N=20 to N=16 is put on a firm footing by confirmation of this state's identity. The raised 3/2+ level is observed simultaneously with the intruder negative parity 7/2- and 3/2- levels, providing evidence for the reduction in the N=20 gap. The coincident gamma-ray decays allowed the assignment of spins as well as the transferred orbital angular momentum. The excitation energy of the 3/2+ state shows that the established USD shell model breaks down well within the sd model space and requires a revised treatment of the proton-neutron monopole interaction.

## Key claim

The first 3/2+ state in 25Ne is raised in energy relative to the 5/2+ state, causing an inversion of these levels compared to the isotone 27Mg, which provides firm evidence for the migration of the neutron magic number from N=20 to N=16. This breakdown of the standard USD shell model occurs well within the sd model space and requires a revised treatment of the proton-neutron monopole interaction.

## Method

The authors used a reaccelerated radioactive beam of 24Ne at 10.6 MeV per nucleon incident on a deuterated polythene target to study the (d,p) reaction in inverse kinematics. Protons were detected using the TIARA silicon detector array spanning 85% of 4π, while gamma rays from the 25Ne residues were detected in coincidence using four EXOGAM segmented germanium detectors. Beam-like reaction products were identified using the VAMOS magnetic spectrometer, and the coincident gamma-ray decays allowed spin assignments and determination of transferred orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 10.6 MeV per nucleon for 24Ne ions, with intensity 2 × 10^5 particles/sec
- Target thickness: 1 mg/cm^2 of deuterated polythene (CD)2
- Gamma-ray detection: full-energy peak efficiency estimated at 15% at 1.0 MeV, with Doppler correction resolution of 65 keV FWHM at 1 MeV
- Excited states observed in 25Ne at: 1680 keV (5/2+), 2030 keV (3/2+), 3330 keV (3/2-), and 4030 keV (7/2-), all with ±40 keV uncertainty from the present work
- Spectroscopic factors: ground state 1/2+ S=0.80, 1680 keV 5/2+ S=0.15, 2030 keV 3/2+ S=0.44, 3330 keV 3/2- S=0.75, 4030 keV 7/2- S=0.73, with 20% theoretical uncertainty
- The 3/2+ state in 25Ne lies approximately 250 keV above the 5/2+ state, whereas in the isotone 27Mg the 3/2+ state lies about 700 keV below the 5/2+ state

## Context

This work extends studies of the N=16 magic number emergence in neutron-rich nuclei, following beta-decay studies of 25F by Padgett et al. (2005) and transfer studies of 27Ne by Obertelli et al. (2006) and 23O by Elekes et al. (2007). The firm spin assignments and spectroscopic factors for 25Ne provide quantitative data to test shell model modifications, such as the revised USD interactions (USD-A and USD-B) proposed by Brown et al., that aim to explain the monopole shift of the 0d3/2 orbital. The simultaneous observation of the raised 3/2+ state with intruder negative parity 7/2- and 3/2- levels provides evidence that the opening of the N=16 gap is accompanied by a closing of the N=20 gap.
