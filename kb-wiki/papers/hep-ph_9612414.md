# Cause of Disparity between the Results for the $n\bar{n}$ Transition Problem

> We show that there is a double counting in the standard model of $n\bar{n}$ mixing in the medium, resulting in full cancellation of leading terms. The direct calculation of $n\bar{n}$ transition, annihilation is performed. For lower limit on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time we get $τ_{min} \sim 10^{31}y$.

# Cause of Disparity between the Results for the \(n\bar{n}\) Transition Problem

## Key claim

The standard potential model of \(n\bar{n}\) mixing in a medium contains a double counting that leads to full cancellation of the leading \(\epsilon^2 t^2\) terms; when the process is calculated directly with only annihilation products in the final state, the lower limit on the free-space \(n\bar{n}\) oscillation time is \(\tau_{min} \sim 10^{31}\) years, not the much shorter limits obtained from the potential model.

## Method

The author performs a direct calculation of the two-step process \((nucleus) \rightarrow (\bar{n}-nucleus) \rightarrow (annihilation\; products)\) using a finite-time approach with the exact amplitude \(T_{fi}(t)\) expressed through the antineutron-nucleus decay amplitude \(T_{fi}^{\bar{n}}\). The calculation avoids the infrared singularity that appears in the S-matrix formulation (where the antineutron Green function diverges as \(1/\delta U\)) by working with a finite time interval \(T\) and using the asymptotic value of the annihilation amplitude for times \(\tau \gg 1/\Gamma\).

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on free-space \(n\bar{n}\) oscillation time: \(\tau_{min} \sim 10^{31}\) years, obtained from the inequality \(W(T) < 1\) with nuclear annihilation lifetime \(T = 6.5 \times 10^{31}\) years.
- Annihilation width of the \(\bar{n}\)-nucleus state: \(\Gamma \sim 100\) MeV, corresponding to a characteristic annihilation time \(1/\Gamma \sim 10^{-24}\) seconds.
- The ratio of the direct calculation probability to the potential model probability scales as \(W(T)/W_{pot}(T) \sim \Gamma T\), where \(T \sim 10^{31}\) years.
- For comparison, the free-neutron limit on \(\tau_{n\bar{n}}\) is \(> 10^7\) seconds, and the previous nuclear limit was \(\tau_{n\bar{n}} > 10^8\) seconds \(\sim 1\) year.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous finite-time approach (Nazaruk, 1996) which obtained \(\tau_{min} = 3 \times 10^{31}\) years, and directly addresses the criticism by Dover et al. (1995) who used the potential model with Hamiltonian \(H = -i\Gamma/2\) to refute that result. The paper identifies a fundamental flaw in the standard potential model of \(n\bar{n}\) mixing in the medium (described by Schrodinger equations with self-consistent potentials \(U_n\) and \(U_{\bar{n}}\)): it includes \(\bar{n}\)-nucleus states as final states in the probability calculation, leading to double counting and cancellation of the leading terms. The correct treatment requires fixing only annihilation products in the final state, which enables a consistent lower limit on the free-space oscillation time that is 31 orders of magnitude larger than previous nuclear limits.
