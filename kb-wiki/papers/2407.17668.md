# Resonances and collisional properties of neutron-rich helium isotopes in the adiabatic hyperspherical representation

> This work treats few-body systems consisting of neutrons interacting with a $^{4}{\mathrm{He}}$ nucleus. The adiabatic hyperspherical representation is utilized to solve the $N$-body Schr$\ddot{\mathrm{o}}$dinger equation for the three- and four-body systems, treating both $^{6}{\mathrm{He}}$ and $^{7}{\mathrm{He}}$ nuclei. A simplified central potential model for the $^{4}{\mathrm{He}}-n$ interaction is used in conjunction with a spin-dependent three-body interaction to reproduce $^{6}{\mathrm{He}}$ bound-state and resonance properties as well as properties for the $^{8}{\mathrm{He}}$ nucleus in its ground-state. With this Hamiltonian, the adiabatic hyperspherical representation is used to compute bound and scattering states for both $^{6}{\mathrm{He}}$ and $^{7}{\mathrm{He}}$ nuclei. For the $^{6}{\mathrm{He}}$ system, the electric quadrupole transition between the $0^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ state is investigated. For the $^{7}{\mathrm{He}}$ system, $^{6}{\mathrm{He}}+n$ elastic scattering is investigated along with the four-body recombination process $^{4}{\mathrm{He}}+n+n+n\rightarrow$$^{6}{\mathrm{He}}+n$ and breakup process $^{6}{\mathrm{He}}+n\rightarrow$$^{4}{\mathrm{He}}+n+n+n$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simplified central potential model for the \(^{4}\mathrm{He}-n\) interaction, combined with a spin-dependent three-body interaction, can reproduce bound-state and resonance properties of \(^{6}\mathrm{He}\) and \(^{8}\mathrm{He}\) within the adiabatic hyperspherical representation, but the model deliberately omits spin-orbit coupling and introduces a three-body force to compensate for the resulting extra attraction in the \(j=1/2\) channels.

## Method

The authors solved the \(N\)-body Schrödinger equation for three- and four-body systems using the adiabatic hyperspherical representation, expanding channel functions in a hyperradial explicitly-correlated Gaussian basis. They employed angular-momentum-dependent central two-body potentials for the \(^{4}\mathrm{He}-n\) interaction (with separate \(l=0\), \(l=1\), and \(l\geq2\) components) and a Gaussian \(nn\) interaction, supplemented by a spin-dependent three-body \(^{4}\mathrm{He}-nn\) interaction whose parameters were fitted to reproduce \(^{6}\mathrm{He}\) and \(^{8}\mathrm{He}\) ground-state energies. Bound states were computed via hyperradial coupled-channel calculations including up to 8 channels, and resonances were extracted using the slow-variable discretization method.

## Key numbers

*   \(^{6}\mathrm{He}(0^{+})\) ground-state energy: \(-0.9799\) MeV (8-channel calculation), with an rms matter radius of \(2.5070\) fm.
*   \(^{6}\mathrm{He}(2^{+})\) resonance energy: \(0.8010\) MeV, with a width of \(0.0926\) MeV (8-channel calculation).
*   \(^{4}\mathrm{He}-n\) \(s\)-wave scattering length: \(2.58\) fm; effective range: \(1.38\) fm.
*   \(^{4}\mathrm{He}-n\) resonance in the \(^{2}P_{3/2}\) state: energy \(0.798\) MeV, width \(0.648\) MeV.
*   \(^{8}\mathrm{He}(0^{+})\) ground-state energy used for fitting: \(-3.11\) MeV.
*   Neutron-neutron \(^{1}S_{0}\) scattering length: approximately \(-18.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic hyperspherical treatment of halo nuclei, previously applied to two-neutron halos such as \(^{6}\mathrm{He}\) by Garrido (1997) and Thompson (1998), to systems with three and four neutrons interacting with a \(^{4}\mathrm{He}\) core. The approach provides a framework for studying the \(^{4}\mathrm{He}+n+n+n\) four-body recombination and breakup processes, and it lays groundwork for future five-body calculations of the \(^{4}\mathrm{He}+4n\) system relevant to interpreting the low-energy four-neutron signal observed in experiments by Duer (2022) and Kisamori (2016). The simplified central-potential model with a fitted three-body force enables hyperspherical calculations that would be more difficult with realistic spin-orbit interactions.
