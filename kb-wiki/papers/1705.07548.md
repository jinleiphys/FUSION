# $K^+$-nucleus elastic scattering revisited from perspective of partial restoration of chiral symmetry

> The $K^{+}$ meson properties in the nuclear medium are investigated by considering the wavefunction renormalization as a first step to reveal the in-medium properties of the $K^{+}$ meson in the context of partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The $K^{+}N$ elastic scattering amplitude is constructed using chiral perturbation theory up to the next-to-leading order. Using the constructed amplitude, we calculate the wavefunction renormalization in the Thomas-Fermi approximation. We obtained a good description of the $K^{+}N$ elastic scattering amplitude. The obtained wavefunction renormalization factor suggests the 2 to 6% enhancement of the $K^{+}N$ interaction at the saturation density. We conclude that the wavefunction renormalization could be one of the important medium effects for the $K^{+}$ meson.

## Key claim

The wavefunction renormalization of the in-medium kaon, arising from the energy dependence of the self-energy, could be one of the important medium effects for the $K^{+}$ meson, suggesting a 2 to 6% enhancement of the $K^{+}N$ interaction at saturation density, though this is presented as a first step rather than a definitive explanation.

## Method

The authors constructed the $K^{+}N$ elastic scattering amplitude using chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order, with low-energy constants determined by fitting to $K^{+}p$ differential cross section data at 205 MeV/c and $I=0$ total cross section data between 366 and 799 MeV/c. They then calculated the in-medium $K^{+}$ self-energy using the Thomas-Fermi approximation for nuclear matter, integrating the forward $K^{+}N$ amplitude over nucleon Fermi momentum, and extracted the wavefunction renormalization factor from the energy derivative of the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- The wavefunction renormalization factor suggests a 2 to 6% enhancement of the $K^{+}N$ interaction at saturation density.
- The low-energy constants determined include $b^{I=1} = 1.01 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $d^{I=1} = 4.33 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ from fitting $K^{+}p$ data at $p_{\rm lab} = 205$ MeV/c with $\chi^{2}/N = 0.83$.
- For $I=0$, the parameters are $b^{I=0} = 1.55 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $d^{I=0} = 3.91 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ from fitting total cross section data between $p_{\rm lab} = 366$ and 799 MeV/c with $\chi^{2}/N = 12.0$.
- The baryon mass parameters used include $M_N = 938.9$ MeV/c$^2$, $M_K = 495.6$ MeV/c$^2$, $M_{\Lambda} = 1115.7$ MeV/c$^2$, $M_{\Sigma} = 1193.2$ MeV/c$^2$, and the kaon decay constant $f_K = 110$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of partial restoration of chiral symmetry from the pion-nucleus system, where chiral symmetry is considered to be restored about 30% at saturation density based on deeply bound pionic atoms (Kienle and Yamazaki, 2004) and low-energy pion-nucleus scattering (Friedman, 2004), to the strangeness sector using $K^{+}$ mesons. The paper addresses the previously observed enhancement of the $K^{+}N$ interaction in nuclei, which was found to be 14-34% larger than expected from a simple $T\rho$ approximation (Friedman, 2007), and proposes wavefunction renormalization as a leading-order correction, building on the framework developed for pions by Kaiser and Weise (2003) and Jido et al. (2008). This enables future systematic studies using in-medium chiral perturbation theory to describe $K^{+}$ properties in nuclear matter.
