# QCD Kondo effect: quark matter with heavy-flavor impurities

> We show that the Kondo effect occurs in light quark matter which contains heavy quarks as impurities. We consider a scattering between a heavy-flavor impurity and a light quark near a Fermi surface which is mediated by gluon-exchange interactions. We find that the scattering amplitude has a logarithmic infrared divergence originating from imperfect cancellation between quark-impurity and hole-impurity scatterings in a loop integral, implying the presence of a strongly coupled regime near the Fermi surface. Renormalization group method is used to find the Kondo scale where a running coupling constant hits a Landau pole. Following an illustration by a simple contact-interaction model, we examine gluon-exchange interactions on the basis of high density QCD.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Kondo effect occurs in light quark matter containing heavy quarks as impurities, but only when the non-Abelian color SU(3) interaction causes imperfect cancellation between quark-impurity and hole-impurity scattering in loop integrals; if the interaction were Abelian, the infrared divergence would cancel completely and no Kondo effect would arise.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop scattering amplitude between a heavy-flavor impurity (charm or bottom quark) and a light quark near the Fermi surface, mediated by gluon-exchange interactions in high-density QCD. They apply the renormalization group (RG) method to resum leading logarithmic contributions and determine the Kondo scale where the running coupling constant hits a Landau pole, first using a simple contact-interaction toy model with SU(n) symmetry and then extending to finite-range gluon exchanges with separate treatment of electric (Debye-screened) and magnetic (dynamically screened) components.

## Key numbers

- The Kondo scale from the contact-interaction model is given by \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{K}} = \Lambda_{0} \exp\left(-\cfrac{2}{n\rho_0 \, G(\Lambda_{0})}\right)\), where \(n=3\) for color SU(3), \(\rho_0\) is the density of states at the Fermi surface, and \(G(\Lambda_0)\) is the initial coupling.
- For the magnetic gluon exchange, the energy gap in color superconductivity changes from \(\Delta \propto {\rm e}^{-c/g^2}\) (screening case) to \(\Delta \propto {\rm e}^{-c'/g}\) (unscreening case) with constants \(c\) and \(c'\), showing parametric enhancement from the unscreened magnetic component.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Yasui (2013) that Kondo-like infrared instabilities could appear in QCD matter with heavy impurities, moving beyond the contact-interaction approximation to include realistic finite-range gluon exchanges. The analysis parallels the RG treatment used for color superconductivity by Evans (1998), Son (1998), and Hsu (1999), where the unscreened magnetic gluon component plays a crucial role. The results enable future studies of transport properties and excitations near the Fermi surface in dense quark matter with heavy-flavor impurities.
