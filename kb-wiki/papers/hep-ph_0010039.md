# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and the sigma-meson

> The sigma-meson, which is at best a broad resonance in the vacuum, may suffer a substantial red-shift and appear as a soft and narrow collective mode at finite temperature and baryon densities. The physics behind this softening phenomena and its relation to the partial restoration of chiral symmetry are discussed. Possible experiments in laboratory to test the idea are also summarized.

# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and the sigma-meson

## Key claim

The sigma-meson, which is a broad resonance in vacuum with a width comparable to its mass, may become a soft and narrow collective mode at finite temperature and baryon density due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry, but this effect is conditional on the system approaching the critical point of the chiral phase transition and manifests as a spectral enhancement just above the two-pion threshold rather than as a clean resonance peak.

## Method

The authors analyze the in-medium spectral function of the sigma-meson using the O(4) linear sigma model at the one-loop level, incorporating the coupling between sigma and two pions. They parameterize the reduction of the chiral condensate in nuclear matter through a density-dependent factor Phi(rho) = <sigma>/sigma_0, and compute the spectral function and pi-pi cross section in the I=J=0 channel for various values of this condensate reduction. The density dependence of the quark condensate is derived from an exact QCD theorem involving the pion-nucleon sigma term and nuclear binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate in nuclear matter is reduced by almost 35% already at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}, based on the density expansion of the exact QCD relation.
- The pion-nucleon sigma term used in the condensate calculation is Sigma_{pi N} = 45 +/- 10 MeV.
- The vacuum chiral condensate is taken as <bar{q}q>_0 approx -(225 MeV)^3.
- The reduction factor Phi(rho = rho_0) = <sigma>/sigma_0 at nuclear matter density can take values in the range 0.6 to 0.9 depending on how one relates the sigma field to the quark condensate.
- For the formation of sigma mesic nuclei via (d,t) reactions, the incident deuteron kinetic energy in the laboratory system is estimated to be in the range 1.1 GeV < E < 10 GeV to cover the spectral function in the range 2m_pi < omega < 750 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1985) which first proposed that chiral restoration causes softening (red-shift) of the sigma meson and suppression of its width due to reduced phase space for sigma -> 2pi decay. The analysis builds on further theoretical work (Chiku and Hatsuda, 1998) showing spectral enhancement near the two-pion threshold in the O(4) linear sigma model. The paper connects this theoretical framework to experimental data from the CHAOS collaboration, which observed enhanced pi+pi- yield near the 2pi threshold in nuclear targets, and suggests that standard calculations using nonlinear chiral Lagrangians with pi-N many-body dynamics (Wiringa et al.) cannot simultaneously reproduce the I=0 and I=2 channel cross sections, indicating that partial chiral restoration may be the missing mechanism. The results enable experimental searches using di-pion and di-photon spectra in hadron-nucleus and photon-nucleus reactions, as well as possible detection of dileptons through scalar-vector mixing in matter.
