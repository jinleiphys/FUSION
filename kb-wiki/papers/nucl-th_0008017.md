# Deep inelastic scattering on asymmetric nuclei

> We study deep inelastic scattering on isospin asymmetric nuclei. In particular, the difference of the nuclear structure functions and the Gottfried sum rule for the lightest mirror nuclei, 3He and 3H, are investigated. It is found that such systems can provide significant information on charge symmetry breaking and flavor asymmetry in the nuclear medium. Furthermore, we propose a new method to extract the neutron structure function from radioactive isotopes far from the line of stability. We also discuss the flavor asymmetry in the Drell-Yan process with isospin asymmetric nuclei.

## Key claim

The difference between the nuclear structure functions of mirror nuclei (such as 3He and 3H) can provide significant information on charge symmetry breaking and flavor asymmetry in the nuclear medium, but the authors restrict their study to the region x > 0.1 to keep the discussion simple, noting that nuclear shadowing effects should be small for the isovector nonsinglet part.

## Method

The authors calculated the difference between the nuclear structure functions of 3He and 3H using a spectral function obtained from solving the Faddeev equation with the PEST potential, treating the Coulomb interaction approximately by adjusting the strength of the 1S0 nucleon-nucleon potential to fit the observed binding energies (7.72 MeV for 3He and 8.48 MeV for 3H). For the free nucleon structure function they used the CTEQ5 parametrization, which incorporates the measured flavor asymmetry in the free nucleon. They then extended the analysis to propose methods for extracting the neutron structure function from radioactive isotopes and for studying flavor asymmetry in the nuclear Drell-Yan process using mirror nuclei and single radioactive ions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies used: 7.72 MeV for 3He and 8.48 MeV for 3H
- At Q2 = 10 GeV2, the Gottfried sum for the free nucleon integrated from x = 0.1 is IGN(0.1) = 0.152 (from CTEQ5)
- At Q2 = 10 GeV2, the nuclear Gottfried sum for the 3He-3H pair integrated from x = 0.1 is IGHe,H(0.1) = 0.150
- The experimental value of the Gottfried sum for the free nucleon measured by NMC is IGN(0) = 0.235 ± 0.026 at Q2 = 4 GeV2
- The integrated flavor asymmetry from NMC is ∫01 dx [d̄(x) - ū(x)] = 0.148 ± 0.039
- The naive expectation for the flavor asymmetry ratio from Pauli blocking in a free proton is d̄/ū ≈ 5/4, while in a 6-quark configuration from two overlapping protons it becomes d̄/ū ≈ 2

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear structure functions beyond the singlet case, which has been extensively studied since the EMC measurement (European Muon Collaboration, 1983), to the nonsinglet case using isospin asymmetric nuclei. It builds on the observation of flavor asymmetry in the free nucleon by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC, 1997) and the E866 Drell-Yan experiments (Fermilab, 1998), and on earlier theoretical work by Thomas (1983) on pion cloud contributions to flavor asymmetry. The paper proposes that future experiments at the RIKEN-MUSES project could enable extraction of the neutron structure function from radioactive isotopes and study of flavor asymmetry in nuclei, potentially revealing non-perturbative QCD effects such as modified pion dynamics or 6-quark configurations in the nuclear medium.
