# Radiative Corrections to the PREX and QWEAK Experiments

> The high precision parity violating electron scattering experiments PREX and QWEAK are sensitive to radiative corrections. In this paper we introduce the PREX experiment that measures the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb and discuss coulomb distortion corrections. We then discuss dispersion corrections for QWEAK that aims to measure the weak charge of the proton.

# Radiative Corrections to the PREX and QWEAK Experiments

## Key claim
The parity violating asymmetry measured by PREX is reduced by about 30% due to Coulomb distortion corrections, and the dispersion correction to the weak charge of the proton for QWEAK is large (approximately 7.6% relative contribution) compared to the desired 2% statistical error, requiring accurate inclusion in the interpretation of experimental results.

## Method
The author discusses Coulomb distortion corrections for PREX by numerically solving the Dirac equation for an electron moving in the Coulomb potential of 208Pb (Z=82) and a weak axial vector potential, including distortions to all orders in Zα. For QWEAK, dispersion corrections involving intermediate excited nuclear states are calculated using a model that separates contributions from nucleon resonance excited states and higher energy background excitations, with the isospin decomposition of the background being a key uncertainty.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb distortions reduce the parity violating asymmetry A_pv for 1.05 GeV electrons scattering from 208Pb at five degrees by about 30%
- The dispersion correction ΔR to the weak charge of the proton at QWEAK kinematics (E=1.165 GeV, t=-0.03 GeV^2) is ΔR = [5.39 ± 0.27 (mod. avg.) ± 1.88 (backgr.) +0.58/-0.49 (res.) ± 0.07 (t-dep.)] × 10^-3
- The total weak charge of the proton is Q_W^p = 0.0767 ± 0.0008 ± 0.0020, giving a relative dispersion contribution ΔR/Q_W^p = 7.6 ± 2.8%
- At lower energy (E=0.180 GeV, t=0), the dispersion correction is ΔR = [1.32 ± 0.05 (mod. avg.) ± 0.27 (backgr.) +0.11/-0.08 (res.)] × 10^-3
- The original PREX goal is a 1% (±0.05 fm) constraint on the neutron radius of 208Pb
- Hebeler et al. predict R_n - R_p = 0.17 ± 0.03 fm for 208Pb from chiral perturbation theory calculations

## Context
This work extends the treatment of radiative corrections for two high-precision parity violating electron scattering experiments: PREX, which measures the neutron radius of 208Pb, and QWEAK, which measures the weak charge of the proton. The dispersion correction calculation builds on the initial work by Gorchtein (2009) who first identified the large correction, and is further detailed in Gorchtein, Horowitz, and Ramsey-Musolf (2011). The neutron radius measurement is strongly correlated with the neutron matter equation of state, neutron star radii, and the symmetry energy, as established by prior work including Hebeler et al. (using chiral perturbation theory) and correlations with neutron star properties. The results enable improved interpretation of both experiments and suggest that a lower-energy QWEAK-like experiment would be more cleanly interpretable in terms of the weak charge of the proton.
