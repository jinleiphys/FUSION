# In-medium viscous coefficients of a hot hadronic gas mixture

> We estimate the shear and the bulk viscous coefficients for a hot hadronic gas mixture constituting of pions and nucleons. The viscosities are evaluated in the relativistic kinetic theory approach by solving the transport equation in the relaxation time approximation for binary collisions ($ππ$,$πN$ and $NN$). Instead of vacuum cross-sections usually used in the literature we employ in-medium scattering amplitudes in the estimation of the relaxation times. The modified cross-sections for $ππ$ and $πN$ scattering are obtained using one-loop modified thermal propagators for $ρ$, $σ$ and $Δ$ in the scattering amplitudes which are calculated using effective interactions. The resulting suppression of the cross sections at finite temperature and baryon density is observed to significantly affect the $T$ and $μ_N$ dependence of the viscosities of the system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using in-medium scattering cross-sections (rather than vacuum cross-sections) for pion-pion and pion-nucleon interactions significantly alters the temperature and baryon-density dependence of shear and bulk viscosities in a hot hadronic gas mixture, but this effect is conditional on the specific choice of thermal propagator corrections for the rho, sigma, and Delta resonances.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic transport equation in the relaxation time approximation for a binary hadronic gas mixture of pions and nucleons, considering pi-pi, pi-N, and N-N collisions. Instead of using vacuum cross-sections, they compute in-medium scattering amplitudes by evaluating one-loop self-energy corrections to the rho and sigma meson propagators (for pi-pi scattering) and the Delta baryon propagator (for pi-N scattering) using real-time thermal field theory. The relaxation times for pions and nucleons are then obtained from these in-medium cross-sections, and the shear and bulk viscosities are computed via momentum integrals over the equilibrium distribution functions weighted by the relaxation times.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity increases by a factor of roughly 2-3 when using in-medium cross-sections compared to vacuum cross-sections over the temperature range 100-160 MeV and nucleon chemical potential 200-500 MeV (see Figure 3, upper vs lower curve sets).
- The pi-pi cross-section is suppressed at T=130 MeV and T=160 MeV relative to the vacuum case, with the suppression increasing with temperature (Figure 1).
- The pi-N cross-section is suppressed at finite temperature and nucleon chemical potential, with the suppression increasing for higher T and mu_N values (Figure 2, with mu_N values of 200 and 500 MeV shown).
- The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (eta/s) decreases monotonically with temperature and respects the KSS lower bound of 1/(4 pi) near the transition temperature (Figure 4).
- Bulk viscosity is much smaller than shear viscosity in magnitude, but shows similar qualitative behavior with temperature and chemical potential (Figure 5).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of transport coefficients in a pion gas by Mitra et al. (2012-2013) that used in-medium pi-pi cross-sections, now incorporating nucleons and in-medium pi-N scattering via the Delta resonance to account for finite baryon density. It contrasts with prior work by Prakash et al. (1993), Itakura et al. (2007), and others who used vacuum or parameterized cross-sections extracted from empirical data. The results are intended to provide more realistic inputs for viscous hydrodynamic simulations of the later stages of heavy ion collisions, particularly relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR.
