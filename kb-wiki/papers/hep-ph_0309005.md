# Infrared regularization of baryon chiral perturbation theory reformulated

> We formulate the infrared regularization of Becher and Leutwyler in a form analogous to our recently proposed extended on-mass-shell renormalization. In our formulation, IR regularization can be applied straightforwardly to multi-loop diagrams with an arbitrary number of particles with arbitrary masses.

## Key claim

The authors reformulate the infrared (IR) regularization of Becher and Leutwyler so that the subtraction terms are obtained by expanding loop integrands in small parameters and interchanging summation and integration, rather than by splitting the Feynman parameter integral at z=1. This reformulation makes IR regularization straightforwardly applicable to multi-loop diagrams and diagrams with an arbitrary number of particles with arbitrary masses, including multiple fermion lines and resonances.

## Method

The authors start from the dimensionally regularized one-loop fermion self-energy integral and compare the standard IR regularization decomposition (splitting the Feynman parameter integral at z=1 into an IR singular part I and a remainder R) with their extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme. They show that expanding the integrand of the loop integral in small parameters (pion mass, off-shell momenta) and interchanging summation and integration reproduces exactly the expansion of the IR regular part R. They then generalize this procedure to arbitrary one-loop integrals with one fermion line and multiple pion and fermion propagators, and demonstrate the equivalence for integrals with two nucleon lines and for integrals involving the Delta resonance.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop self-energy integral I_{Nπ}(-p,0) is assigned chiral order Q^{n-3} under standard power counting, where n is the number of space-time dimensions.
- The expansion of the IR regular part R for the self-energy integral is given as: R = -[m^{n-4} Γ(2-n/2)]/[(4π)^{n/2}(n-3)] × [1 - (p²-m²)/(2m²) + (n-6)(p²-m²)²/(4m⁴(n-5)) + (n-3)M²/(2m²(n-5)) + ...].
- For the two-nucleon integral I_{NNπ}, the integrals I_{ij} and J_{ij} both evaluate to [1+(-1)^j]/[(n-4+i)(n-5+j)].
- The integral I_{0π}(-p,0) for the Delta resonance case has an imaginary part in n=4 dimensions given by -(1/16π)(1-M²/p²), which violates power counting.

## Context

This work extends the IR regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) by reformulating it in a manner analogous to the EOMS renormalization scheme of Fuchs, Gegelia, and Scherer (2003). The reformulation addresses the limitation that the original IR regularization was not straightforwardly applicable to multi-loop diagrams or diagrams with multiple fermion lines. The authors verify their method against explicit calculations for pion-nucleon scattering integrals from Becher and Leutwyler (2001) and for two-nucleon diagrams from Goity, Lehmann, and Scherer (2001). The work also discusses implications for including vector mesons (following Fuchs, Gegelia, and Scherer, 2003) and the Delta resonance (following Bernard, Hemmert, and Meißner, 2003), noting that the apparent power-counting violation for the Delta case disappears when the full spin-3/2 propagator decomposition is considered.
