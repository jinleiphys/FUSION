# Evolution of transverse-distance dependent parton densities at large-$x_B$ and geometry of the loop space

> We discuss possible applications of the equations of motion in the generalized Wilson loop space to the phenomenology of the three-dimensional parton distribution functions in the large-$x_B$ approximation. This regime is relevant for future experimental programs to be launched at the (approved) Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade and the (planned) Electron-Ion Collider. We show that the geometrical evolution of the Wilson loops corresponds to the combined rapidity and renormalization-group equation of the transverse-distance dependent parton densities in the large-$x_B$ factorization scheme.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the geometrical evolution of Wilson loops in generalized loop space corresponds to the combined rapidity and renormalization-group equation of transverse-distance dependent parton densities, but only within the large-\(x_B\) factorization scheme and under the assumption that the only allowed shape variations conserve the angles between the sides of the light-cone Wilson rectangle.

## Method

The authors derive an evolution equation for planar light-like Wilson rectangular loops using a Schwinger quantum dynamical principle, where shape variations are generated by an area logarithmic derivative operator. They then apply this loop-space formalism to the soft function that appears in the large-\(x_B\) factorization of transverse-distance dependent (TDD) parton correlation functions, showing that the rapidity derivative \(P^+ d/dP^+\) is dual to the area variation operator \(d/d\ln\sigma\). The resulting equations are integrated to give a complete evolution of TDDs in the large-\(x_B\) region.

## Key numbers

* The Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade will probe the region \(x_B\) from \(\sim 0.1\) up to very close to 1, targeting mainly valence quarks.
* The combined kinematic range of JLab 12 GeV and the planned Electron-Ion Collider will be of the order \(10^{-3} < x_B < 1\) and \(2\ \text{GeV}^2 < Q^2 < 100\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* The rapidity variables are defined as \(Y^\pm = \frac{1}{2} \ln \frac{(N^\pm)^+}{(N^\pm)^-}\), with the limit \(\eta^\pm \to 0\) providing regularization.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(x_B\) factorization approach for collinear PDFs developed by Kroll and collaborators (LargeX_KM) to three-dimensional parton densities, and generalizes the Makeenko-Migdal equations for Wilson loops to cusped loops with light-like segments. The resulting evolution equations coincide with those from the Collins-Soper-Sterman formalism and from SCET-based TMD approaches, but the authors claim their loop-space method yields simpler integration constants. The formalism is directly applicable to phenomenology at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade and the planned Electron-Ion Collider.
