# Quark beta decay in the inhomogeneous chiral phase and cooling of compact stars

> A novel cooling mechanism is proposed for neutron stars, based on the recent development in the studies of the QCD phase diagram. Possible appearance of the inhomogeneous chiral phase makes the quark beta decay without gluonic interaction. An estimate of the neutrino emissivity shows the order of $10^{24-26}(T/10^9)^6$(erg cm$^{-3}$ s$^{-1}$) near the phase boundaries, whose efficiency is comparable with the usual quark cooling or pion cooling, but it works only in the limited density region. These features may give another cooling scenario of neutron stars.

## Key claim
The authors propose that quark beta decay can occur without gluonic interactions in the inhomogeneous chiral phase (dual-chiral-density wave, DCDW) of neutron star matter, but this cooling mechanism works only in the limited density region near the phase boundaries of the DCDW phase, not throughout the entire star.

## Method
The authors calculate the neutrino emissivity from quark beta decay (d → u + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ and u + e⁻ → d + νₑ) in the DCDW phase using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry in the chiral limit. They evaluate the transition matrix element and phase-space integrals near the onset density (where the Fermi surface deformation is weak) and near the termination density (where the Fermi surfaces are strongly deformed into separated spheres), treating quarks as quasiparticles with modified energy spectra due to the spatially varying chiral condensate.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino emissivity near the onset density: ε_DCDW ≈ 6.1 × 10²⁶ (ρ_B/ρ₀)^(2/3) Y_e^(2/3) T₉⁶ erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹, where T₉ = T/10⁹ K and ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³
- Neutrino emissivity near the termination density: ε_DCDW = 2.16 × 10²⁴ (ρ_B/ρ₀)^(1/3) Y_e^(1/3) T₉⁶ erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹
- The DCDW phase exists between onset chemical potential μ_c1 = 0.49Λ and termination chemical potential μ_c2 = 0.53Λ, with Λ = 850 MeV (cut-off parameter)
- Dynamical quark mass at onset: m_c1 = 0.2Λ; at termination: m_c2 = 0.01Λ
- Wave vector magnitude at onset: |q|_c1 = 0.55Λ; at termination: |q|_c2 = 0.8Λ

## Context
This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral phases in QCD, particularly the DCDW phase first proposed by Nakano and Tatsumi (2004), and applies them to neutron star cooling. The mechanism is analogous to pion cooling (Maxwell et al., 1977) but uses the spatially varying chiral condensate to supply momentum at the weak-interaction vertex, relaxing the kinematic constraints that normally suppress direct Urca processes for free quarks (Iwamoto, 1980). This enables a new cooling scenario that could help explain observations of young pulsars like 3C58 and Vela, which show lower surface temperatures than standard cooling models predict, and complements previous work by the authors (Noda et al., 2013) on quark matter in neutron star cores.
