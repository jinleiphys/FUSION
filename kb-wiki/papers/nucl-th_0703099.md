# Upper edge of the neutron star crust : the drip point and around

> A semi-microscopic self-consistent quantum approach developed recently to describe the inner crust structure of neutron stars within the Wigner-Seitz method with the explicit inclusion of neutron and proton pairing correlations is used for finding the neutron drip point which separates the outer and inner crusts. The equilibrium configurations of the crust are examined in vicinity of the drip point and in the upper part of the inner crust, for the density region corresponding to average Fermi momenta $k_{\rm F}{=}0.2 ÷0.5 $fm$^{-1}$.

# Upper edge of the neutron star inner crust: the drip point and around

## Key claim

The neutron drip point density is found to be approximately 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³ when using the full semi-microscopic functional, in agreement with the classic Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) result, but the proton number of the equilibrium nucleus at the drip point is Z=52 rather than the Z=34–38 range reported in prior work by Ruster et al. (2006). This shift arises from the specific isovector properties of the DF3 effective force used in the phenomenological component of the functional.

## Method

The authors apply a semi-microscopic self-consistent quantum approach within the spherical Wigner-Seitz (WS) cell approximation, matching a phenomenological nuclear energy density functional (DF3 by Fayans et al.) to a microscopic functional for neutron matter calculated via Brueckner theory with the Argonne v18 potential. Neutron and proton pairing correlations are included using the P2 model, which applies a many-body suppression factor of 1/2 to the BCS gap. The equilibrium configuration (Z, R_c) for each average density is found by minimizing the total energy per cell under the β-stability condition.

## Key numbers

- Drip point density with pure DF3 functional: ρ_d = 3.30 × 10¹¹ g/cm³, corresponding to k_F^d = 0.181 fm⁻¹
- Drip point density with full semi-microscopic functional: ρ_d = 4.06 × 10¹¹ g/cm³, corresponding to k_F^d = 0.194 fm⁻¹
- Equilibrium proton number at drip point: Z = 52 (with neutron number N ≈ 126) for both functional versions
- For k_F = 0.2 fm⁻¹ (just above drip): equilibrium cell has Z = 52, A = 212, R_c = 57.2 fm, binding energy per nucleon E_B = −0.950 MeV
- For k_F = 0.5 fm⁻¹: equilibrium cell has Z = 46, A = 1020, R_c = 38.6 fm, E_B = 1.625 MeV
- Asymptotic neutron gap values: Δ_as = 0.044 MeV at k_F = 0.2 fm⁻¹; Δ_as = 0.832 MeV at k_F = 0.5 fm⁻¹
- Infinite neutron matter gap (P2 model) at corresponding asymptotic densities: Δ_inf = 0.063 MeV at k_F = 0.2 fm⁻¹; Δ_inf = 0.945 MeV at k_F = 0.5 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the inner crust (Baldo et al., 2005–2006) from the intermediate and bottom regions (k_F = 0.6–1.2 fm⁻¹) up to the drip point, completing the description of the inner crust ground state. The results differ significantly from the classic Negele-Vautherin (NV) calculation (1973), which found Z = 40 for all densities in this region, and from the thermodynamic survey by Ruster et al. (2006), which predicted Z = 34–38 at the drip point. The approach enables future comparison with band-theory calculations (Chamel, 2005–2006) for dynamical properties and provides input for modeling neutron star glitches and cooling.
