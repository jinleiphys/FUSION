# Completing the picture of the Roper resonance

> We employ a continuum approach to the three valence-quark bound-state problem in relativistic quantum field theory to predict a range of properties of the proton's radial excitation and thereby unify them with those of numerous other hadrons. Our analysis indicates that the nucleon's first radial excitation is the Roper resonance. It consists of a core of three dressed-quarks, which expresses its valence-quark content and whose charge radius is 80% larger than the proton analogue. That core is complemented by a meson cloud, which reduces the observed Roper mass by roughly 20%. The meson cloud materially affects long-wavelength characteristics of the Roper electroproduction amplitudes but the quark core is revealed to probes with $Q^2 \gtrsim 3 m_N^2$.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance is the proton's first radial excitation, consisting of a dressed-quark core whose charge radius is 80% larger than the proton's, complemented by a meson cloud that reduces the observed mass by roughly 20%; the quark core is revealed to probes with momentum transfer squared Q^2 greater than approximately 3 times the nucleon mass squared.

## Method

The authors solved the Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation using Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) methods, which incorporate dressed-quark propagators and diquark correlation amplitudes with an explicit connection to QCD. They computed the mass and Faddeev amplitude for both the nucleon ground state and its first J^P = 1/2^+ excited state, then used these solutions to calculate the electromagnetic nucleon-to-Roper transition form factors. The Faddeev equation kernel omitted resonant contributions associated with meson-baryon final-state interactions, so the calculation produces the dressed-quark core of the bound states rather than the fully dressed observable states.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon dressed-quark core mass: 1.18 GeV; Roper dressed-quark core mass: 1.73 GeV (from Eq. eqMasses)
- Bare Roper mass from dynamical coupled channels analysis (Suzuki 2009): 1.76 GeV, agreeing with the computed core mass of 1.73 GeV (from Eq. eqMassesA)
- Charge radius of the Roper core is 80% larger than the proton's charge radius (ratio r_R+/r_p = 1.8); magnetic radius ratio is 1.6
- Scalar diquark probability in both nucleon and Roper cores: 62%; axial-vector diquark probability: 38% (from Eq. Pdiquark)
- Pauli transition form factor at photoproduction point: F2*(0) = -0.65, consistent with experimental values of -0.58 ± 0.02 (Dugger 2009) and -0.62 ± 0.04 (PDG 2014)
- Meson cloud reduces the observed Roper mass by approximately 20% relative to the dressed-quark core

## Context

This work extends the continuum DSE/Faddeev approach previously applied successfully to the nucleon and Delta-baryon elastic and transition form factors (Segovia 2014, Roberts 2015) to the Roper resonance, unifying its properties with those of numerous other hadrons. The results confirm the picture proposed by Suzuki (2009) that the Roper is the proton's first radial excitation with a dressed-quark core shielded by a meson cloud, and contradict the contact-interaction result (Wilson 2011) which found negligible scalar diquark content in the Roper. The analysis enables future lattice QCD simulations and spectral function methods (Chang 2013) to target the entire complex of nucleon, Delta, and Roper properties, and establishes that electroproduction measurements at Q^2 > 3 m_N^2 can serve as incisive probes of quark-gluon dynamics.
