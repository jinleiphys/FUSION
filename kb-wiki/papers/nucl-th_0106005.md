# Study of polarization observables in double pion photoproduction on the proton

> Using a model for two pion photoproduction on the proton previously tested in total cross sections and invariant mass distributions, we evaluate here polarization observables on which recent experiments are providing new information. We evaluate cross sections for spin 1/2 and 3/2, which are measured at Mainz and play an important role in tests of the GHD sum rule. We also evaluate the proton polarization asymmetry $Σ$ which is currently under investigation at GRAAL in Grenoble.

## Key claim

Using a previously tested model for two-pion photoproduction on the proton, the authors evaluate polarization observables (helicity cross sections and the beam asymmetry Sigma) and find that the GDH sum rule integral may not converge, though they caution that their model has only been tested up to 800 MeV and should not be considered conclusive.

## Method

The authors use the model of Nacher et al. (2001), which includes rho production and Delta(1700) excitation, to compute helicity amplitudes for gamma p -> pi+ pi- p and gamma p -> pi+ pi0 n reactions. They adapt their calculations to the DAPHNE detector acceptance at Mainz (three charged particles for the pi+ pi- channel, pi+ with momentum threshold for the pi+ pi0 channel) and evaluate sigma_{1/2}, sigma_{3/2}, the helicity asymmetry, and the beam asymmetry Sigma for various final-state selections and invariant-mass cuts.

## Key numbers

- For gamma p -> pi+ pi- p with 3 charged particles in DAPHNE acceptance, the model reproduces a peak around 700 MeV in sigma_{3/2} and sigma_{1/2}.
- The I^{GDH} integral up to 800 MeV for gamma p -> pi+ pi- p is 0.13; for gamma p -> pi+ pi0 n it is 0.07; for gamma p -> pi0 pi0 p it is 0.02; total from all (gamma,2pi) channels up to 800 MeV is 0.22.
- Extrapolating to 1 GeV, the I^{GDH} contributions are 0.17 (pi+ pi-), 0.09 (pi+ pi0), 0.02 (pi0 pi0); total 0.28, an increase of about 20% over the 800 MeV value.
- For the vector polarizability gamma sum rule up to 800 MeV: 8.6e-5 microbar/MeV^2 (pi+ pi- p), 4.2e-5 microbar/MeV^2 (pi+ pi0 n), 1.3e-5 microbar/MeV^2 (pi0 pi0 p); total 0.00014 microbar/MeV^2.
- For the vector polarizability gamma sum rule up to 1 GeV: 1.0e-4 microbar/MeV^2 (pi+ pi- p), 6.4e-5 microbar/MeV^2 (pi+ pi0 n), 1.7e-5 microbar/MeV^2 (pi0 pi0 p); total 0.00018 microbar/MeV^2.
- The authors estimate the total 2pi contribution to the gamma sum rule lies in the range [0.00018, 0.00030] microbar/MeV^2, with convergence around 2 GeV giving 0.00030 microbar/MeV^2 (believed to be an overestimate).

## Context

This work extends the model of Nacher et al. (2001) for two-pion photoproduction, which improved on the earlier model of Tejedor and Oset (1996) by including rho production and Delta(1700) excitation, to polarization observables. It provides the first theoretical evaluation of helicity cross sections for the GDH sum rule test at Mainz (Lang et al., 2001) and predictions for the beam asymmetry Sigma to be compared with upcoming GRAAL data (Hourany et al., 2001). The results challenge the earlier estimate of Karliner (1973) used in GDH sum rule tests and suggest that the GDH integral may not converge, a possibility previously raised by Drell and Hearn (1966).
