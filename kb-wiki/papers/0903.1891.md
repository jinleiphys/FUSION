# A proposed experimental method to determine $α$-sensitivity of splitting between ground and 7.6 eV isomeric states in Th-229

> The 7.6 eV electromagnetic transition between the nearly degenerate ground state and first excited state in the Th-229 nucleus may be very sensitive to potential changes in the fine-structure constant, $α= e^2/\hbar c$. However, the sensitivity is not known, and nuclear calculations are currently unable to determine it. We propose measurements of the differences of atomic transition frequencies between thorium atoms (or ions) with the nucleus in the ground state and in the first excited (isomeric) state. This will enable extraction of the change in nuclear charge radius and electric quadrupole moment between the isomers, and hence the $α$-dependence of the isomeric transition frequency with reasonable accuracy.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of the 7.6 eV nuclear transition in Th-229 to variation of the fine-structure constant alpha can be determined experimentally by measuring the change in nuclear charge radius and electric quadrupole moment between the ground and isomeric states, rather than relying on uncertain nuclear calculations which give wildly different values for the Coulomb energy difference.

## Method

The authors propose measuring atomic transition frequency differences between thorium atoms or ions with the nucleus in the ground state and in the isomeric state, using the isomeric field shift to extract the change in mean-square charge radius and hyperfine structure measurements to extract the change in electric quadrupole moment. They develop a simple geometric model of the nucleus as a uniform prolate ellipsoid to relate these measurable nuclear parameters to the Coulomb energy difference between isomers, and calculate field-shift constants and hyperfine-structure constants for Th IV, Th III, and Th II using the correlation-potential method and CI+MBPT techniques.

## Key numbers

- The 7.6 eV electromagnetic transition lies between the nearly degenerate ground state (5/2+) and first excited state (3/2+) in Th-229, with an estimated width of about 10^-4 Hz.
- Current limit on alpha variation: dot-alpha/alpha = (-1.6 +/- 2.3) x 10^-17 per year (Rosenband 2008).
- For a Coulomb energy difference of 100 keV and delta-alpha/alpha = 10^-16, the frequency shift delta-omega = 10^-11 eV = 2.4 x 10^3 Hz, which is 4 orders of magnitude larger than limits on atomic transition shifts.
- The ground state root-mean-square radius of Th-229 is 5.6807 +/- 0.0509 fm (Angeli 2004).
- The laboratory quadrupole moment of the ground state is 4.3 +/- 0.9 e b (Stone 2005).
- The change in Coulomb energy is given by: delta-V_C (MeV) = -506 delta-Rms/Rms + 23 delta-Q/Q_0 + 17 delta-z/z.
- For Th IV, the field shift constant for the 7s_{1/2} transition is 146 +/- 4 GHz/fm^2, giving an expected isomeric shift magnitude of 6.3 GHz assuming |delta-Rrms| = 0.004 fm.
- For Th IV, the hyperfine-structure constant B for the 5f_{5/2} state is 740 Q MHz, corresponding to 3180 MHz for Q = 4.3 b.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Flambaum (2006) that the near degeneracy of the Th-229 isomers could provide enhanced sensitivity to fundamental constant variation, and addresses the problem that nuclear calculations give wildly different values for the Coulomb energy difference (Hayes 2007, 2008; He 2008; Litvinova 2009; Flambaum 2009). The proposed method enables experimental determination of the alpha-sensitivity without relying on uncertain nuclear models, and could enable the most sensitive laboratory probe of alpha-variation to date when combined with a nuclear clock based on this transition (Peik 2003).
