# Comment on "Tensor force in doubly odd deformed nuclei"

> An article by Covello, Gargano and Itaco [Phys.Rev. C 56, 3092 (1997)] tries to find evidence for the important role of the residual tensor force between the valence proton and neutron in doubly odd deformed nuclei. It is shown that observable effects discussed by these authors do not fully justify their rather strong conclusions.

# Comment on "Tensor force in doubly odd deformed nuclei"

## Key claim
The observable effects presented by Covello, Gargano and Itaco (1997) as evidence for the important role of the residual tensor force between valence protons and neutrons in doubly odd deformed nuclei do not fully justify their strong conclusions; the particular example of the lowest Kπ=0− band in 176Lu cannot indicate general features, and nearly the same picture is obtained when space-exchange and spin-spin space-exchange forces are assumed to be most important.

## Method
The authors critically analyze the calculations of Covello et al. (1997) and Itaco et al. (1998) for Newby shifts and Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting energies in 174,176Lu, comparing them with their own finite-range calculations using different empirical sets of proton-neutron parameters. They performed preliminary calculations of the 176Lu spectrum assuming 42 low-lying rotational bands of positive parities, including Coriolis interaction, intrinsic rotational contributions, recoil terms, diagonal and non-diagonal ΔK=0 residual proton-neutron mixing, using the same Nilsson potential mean-field parameters as in their previous study (Nosek et al., 1994).

## Key numbers
- Newby shift in 176Lu Kπ=0− {7/2+[404] 7/2−[514]} band: experimental 69.2(0.6) keV; Covello et al. central+tensor Gaussian value 70 keV; present central+tensor Gaussian value 64.8 keV
- Newby shift in 174Lu Kπ=0+ {7/2+[404] 7/2+[633]} band: experimental −40.0(3.2) keV; Covello et al. central+tensor Gaussian value −26 keV; present central+tensor Gaussian value −35.7 keV
- Newby shift in 176Lu Kπ=0+ {7/2−[523] 7/2−[514]} band: experimental −155.5(1.2) keV; present central+tensor Gaussian value −40.7 keV
- Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting for 176Lu Kπ=1+ {9/2−[514] 7/2−[514]} configuration: experimental −220.3(5.1) keV; Covello et al. central+tensor value −154 keV; present central+tensor Gaussian value −200.8 keV
- Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting for 176Lu Kπ=1+ {7/2+[404] 9/2+[624]} configuration: experimental −15.4(3.2) keV; Covello et al. central+tensor value −90 keV; present central+tensor Gaussian value −132.4 keV
- Non-diagonal ΔK=0 mixing matrix elements between Kπ=1+ rotational bands: approximately 50-60 keV
- Admixtures from Coriolis coupling with Newby-shifted Kπ=0+ bands: typically 5-15%

## Context
This Comment challenges the conclusions of Covello, Gargano and Itaco (1997) and Itaco, Covello and Gargano (1998), who claimed clear evidence for tensor force effects based on calculations of Newby shifts and odd-even staggering in 176Lu. The authors argue that the Newby shift in the lowest Kπ=0− band is of central type and does not possess significant tensor contributions, as previously noted by Boisson, Piepenbring and Ogle (1976). They further demonstrate that including ΔK=0 interaction, which was not considered by Covello et al., significantly affects empirical Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting energies and can transfer odd-even staggering between bands. This work enables a more careful re-evaluation of tensor force effects in doubly odd deformed nuclei, suggesting that alternative explanations involving space-exchange forces and non-diagonal mixing remain viable.
