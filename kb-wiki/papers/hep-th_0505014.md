# Artificially induced positronium oscillations in a two-sheeted spacetime: consequences on the observed decay processes

> Following recent theoretical results, it is suggested that positronium (Ps) might undergo spontaneous oscillations between two 4D spacetime sheets whenever subjected to constant irrotational magnetic vector potentials. We show that these oscillations that would come together with o-Ps/p-Ps oscillations should have important consequences on Ps decay rates. Experimental setup and conditions are also suggested for demonstrating in non accelerator experiments this new invisible decay mode.

## Key claim

Positronium (Ps) subjected to a constant irrotational (curl-free) magnetic vector potential may undergo spontaneous oscillations between two 4D spacetime sheets, accompanied by ortho-positronium to para-positronium conversion, leading to an apparent invisible decay mode that modifies the observed decay rate; the effect depends on the coupling constant \(g\) between sheets, which is presently unknown and may require current intensities beyond current technology.

## Method

The authors extend a two-sheeted \(M_4 \times Z_2\) spacetime model, previously developed for fermions via non-commutative geometry and finite-difference approaches, to positronium. They derive a two-sheeted Pauli equation including a coupling term \(\mathbf{H}_{cm}\) proportional to \(g\) and the magnetic vector potential difference between sheets, then solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for o-Ps and p-Ps states coupled by this term, incorporating natural decay constants \(\Gamma_o\) and \(\Gamma_p\) and the hyperfine splitting frequency \(\omega_0\). The resulting decay rate \(\Gamma\) is expressed analytically as a function of the parameter \(\eta = \omega_0/(\kappa\sqrt{2})\) with \(\kappa = g\gamma g_s \mu A\).

## Key numbers

- o-Ps natural decay time: \(1.4 \times 10^{-7}\) s (decay rate \(\Gamma_o \sim 3.57 \times 10^6\) s\(^{-1}\)).
- p-Ps natural decay time: \(1.25 \times 10^{-10}\) s (decay rate \(\Gamma_p \sim 4 \times 10^9\) s\(^{-1}\)).
- Hyperfine structure interval of Ps: \(8.41 \times 10^{-4}\) eV, corresponding to \(\omega_0 \sim 1.28 \times 10^{12}\) s\(^{-1}\).
- Characteristic field-like parameter \(b_0 = \omega_0 m_e/(e\sqrt{2}) \sim 5.14\) T, used to define \(\eta = b_0/b\) with \(b = gA\).
- For the off-diagonal \(\mathbf{H}_c\) term (finite-difference approach), even with \(g \sim 10^3\) m\(^{-1}\) (sheet separation \(\sim 1\) mm), the relative decay rate variation is \(\sim 2 \times 10^{-9}\) for o-Ps and \(\sim 2 \times 10^{-15}\) for p-Ps, far below current experimental accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-sheeted spacetime models for fermions (Sarrazin and Petit, 2004) to positronium, predicting oscillations between sheets that produce an invisible decay mode distinct from mirror-matter models (e.g., Foot and Gninenko, 2000) which rely on photon–mirror-photon kinetic mixing rather than a discrete extra dimension. The proposed effect offers a new experimental signature for extra dimensions, complementing searches for deviations from Newtonian gravity or Kaluza-Klein tower states; if confirmed, it would demonstrate that our spacetime is embedded in a more complex manifold and enable controlled manipulation of positronium decay rates via engineered magnetic vector potentials.
