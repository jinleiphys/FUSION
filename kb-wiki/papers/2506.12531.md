# Exact Renormalization Relation and Binding Energies for Three Identical Bosons

> In the low-energy limit, non-relativistic particles with short-range interactions exhibit universal behavior that is largely independent of microscopic details. This universality is typically described by effective field theory, in which the two-body interaction is renormalized to a single parameter-the scattering length. For systems of identical bosons, the three-body problem reveals the Efimov effect, a novel phenomenon proposed that necessitates the introduction of an additional three-body parameter. However, the exact relation between this three-body parameter, the coupling constants in the effective field theory, and the binding energies of Efimov states remains unresolved. In this Letter, we address this question through a comprehensive analysis of the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation with a finite cutoff. We establish an exact renormalization relation for the three-body parameter and determine its connection to the energies of Efimov bound states. These results are validated through high-precision numerical simulations. We expect our findings to be of fundamental interest across various fields, including atomic, nuclear, condensed matter, and particle physics, and to have broad applications in both few-body and many-body physics.

# Exact Renormalization Relation and Binding Energies for Three Identical Bosons

## Key claim

The authors derive an exact analytical renormalization relation connecting the three-body parameter, the effective field theory coupling constant, and the momentum cutoff for three identical bosons at two-body resonance, correcting a previous approximate result that had been refined numerically to about 0.879; the exact relation yields a value of approximately 0.87866 for the key parameter alpha, with an additional phase shift parameter phi_0 of approximately 0.05281 that was absent in earlier treatments.

## Method

The authors analyze the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation for three-body bound states at two-body resonance (scattering length a_s = infinity), introducing a finite momentum cutoff Lambda. They separate the problem into two regimes: Regime I (high momentum, k >> sqrt(|E|)) for determining the renormalization relation, and Regime II (low momentum, k << Lambda) for determining binding energies. In Regime I, they generalize the Wiener-Hopf method to solve the integral equation by exploiting analyticity properties of the kernel in the frequency domain, performing a factorization of the function F(omega) and applying Liouville's theorem to determine the wavefunction. In Regime II, they take the Lambda to infinity limit and use a change of variables to arcsinh(k/sqrt(-4E/3)) to obtain an exact solution.

## Key numbers

- The universal scaling factor s_0 is approximately 1.00624, determined by solving the equation G(s_0) = lambda where lambda = sqrt(3) pi/8.
- The exact renormalization relation yields alpha approximately 0.87866 and phi_0 approximately 0.05281, compared to the previous approximate value alpha approximately 0.879 with phi_0 = 0.
- The binding energy relation gives s_0 ln(Lambda_*/kappa_*) = -s_0 ln(sqrt(3) e^{-pi/(2s_0)}) mod pi, which evaluates to approximately 1.01807.
- Numerical validation shows discrepancies below 10^{-7} for the renormalization relation, relative error |Delta E_1/E_1| less than or approximately 10^{-3} for the second-lowest bound state, and |Delta E_2/E_2| less than or approximately 10^{-6} for the third-lowest bound state.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering effective field theory approach to three-body physics developed by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998), who derived an approximate analytical renormalization relation assuming the universal log-periodic wavefunction held for all momenta up to the cutoff. Numerical simulations by Braaten, Hammer, and collaborators (2011) later suggested a corrected value of alpha approximately 0.879 with about 10^{-3} accuracy. The present paper provides the exact analytical form including a previously unknown phase shift phi_0, and gives the exact binding energy relation for Efimov states in the low-energy limit. These results enable precise characterization of three-body correlations in systems such as Bose polarons, virial expansion of atomic gases, and universal relations in both few-body and many-body physics.
