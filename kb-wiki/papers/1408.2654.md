# Clustering in nuclear environment

> The properties of few-body clusters (mass number $A \le 4$) are modified if they are immersed in a nuclear medium. In particular, Pauli blocking that reflects the antisymmetrization of the many-body wave function is responsible for the medium modification of light clusters and the dissolution with increasing density. A more consistent description is given with takes also the contribution of correlations in the continuum into account. The relation between cluster formation in warm dense matter and in nuclear structure is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dissolution of light clusters (A ≤ 4) in a nuclear medium is governed by Pauli blocking, but the precise density at which a bound state disappears depends on temperature: at finite temperatures the Mott point occurs when the binding energy reaches zero, whereas at zero temperature the bound state persists until the Fermi energy allows decay into unoccupied free states.

## Method

The author derives an in-medium few-nucleon Schrödinger equation using a thermodynamic Green function approach, incorporating quasiparticle energy shifts and Pauli blocking terms [1 − f(i) − f(j)]. For A=2, a separable Gaussian interaction potential is used to compute the T-matrix and scattering phase shifts for the deuteron channel, with the Pauli blocking treated both in the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (neglecting hole-hole terms) and in the particle-particle RPA (ppRPA) that includes hole-hole contributions. For A=4, a variational approach with the same separable potential (refitted to the α-particle binding energy and radius) is used to compute the in-medium binding energy shift.

## Key numbers

- Free deuteron binding energy and rms radius reproduced with potential parameters λ = −1287.4 MeV fm³ and γ = 1.474 fm⁻¹.
- Critical density for deuteron dissolution at T = 0.1 MeV: n_cr = 0.0016167 fm⁻³ (Tamm-Dancoff blocking).
- Critical density for deuteron dissolution at T = 5 MeV: n_cr = 0.005472 fm⁻³ (Tamm-Dancoff blocking).
- Critical density for deuteron dissolution at T = 0.1 MeV: n_cr = 0.000235476 fm⁻³ (ppRPA blocking).
- Critical density for deuteron dissolution at T = 5 MeV: n_cr = 0.0045349 fm⁻³ (ppRPA blocking).
- Free α-particle binding energy: B⁰_α = 28.3 MeV, reproduced with potential parameters λ = −1449.6 MeV fm³ and γ = 1.152 fm⁻¹.
- α-particle bound state disappears at zero temperature at n_B ≈ 0.03 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium cluster description of Röpke (2011) by explicitly computing scattering phase shifts and the second virial coefficient via a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula, connecting cluster formation in warm dense matter to nuclear structure approaches. The zero-temperature α-particle dissolution density of ≈ 0.03 fm⁻³ agrees with the fully antisymmetrized THSR formalism of Tohsaki and collaborators. The results enable improved equations of state for warm dense matter that include continuum correlations beyond simple bound-state counting, and the comparison between Tamm-Dancoff and ppRPA treatments clarifies the relationship to pairing theory (Gorkov equations) at low temperatures.
