# Quantum Monte-Carlo methods and exact treatment of the two-body problem with Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov states

> In this article, we show that the exact two-body problem can be replaced by quantum jumps between densities written as $D=| Ψ_a \right> \left< Ψ_b |$ where $| Ψ_a \right>$ and $| Ψ_b \right>$ are vacuum for different quasi-particles operators. It is shown that the stochastic process can be written as a Stochastic Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov theory (Stochastic TDHFB) for the generalized density ${\cal R}$ associated to $D$ where ${\cal R}^2 = {\cal R}$ along each stochastic trajectory.

# Quantum Monte-Carlo methods and exact treatment of the two-body problem with Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov states

## Key claim

The exact dynamics of fermions interacting through a general two-body Hamiltonian can be replaced by a stochastic process of quantum jumps between Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov (HFB) states, provided the densities are restricted to a subclass where the generalized density matrix satisfies R^2 = R along each stochastic trajectory. The reformulation is not unique and the selection of this subclass simplifies derivations without restricting exactness.

## Method

The author first defines a subclass of dyadic densities D = |Psi_a><Psi_b| where both states are vacua for different sets of quasiparticle operators, and shows that the generalized density matrix R associated to D satisfies R^2 = R. The action of the two-body Hamiltonian on these densities is decomposed into a mean-field part (leading to a Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov equation for R) and a residual interaction. The residual interaction is expressed as a sum of separable terms in either the particle-hole or particle-particle/hole-hole channels, and stochastic variables are introduced to treat these terms via a Stochastic TDHFB equation for R.

## Key numbers

* The stochastic variables dxi^L_m satisfy variance overline{d xi^L_m d xi^L_{m'}} = delta_{m m'} dt / (2 i hbar) for the particle-hole separable decomposition.
* The stochastic variables dxi^R_m satisfy overline{d xi^R_m d xi^R_{m'}} = -delta_{mm'} dt / (i hbar) for the right-propagator evolution.
* For the particle-particle/hole-hole separable interaction, the stochastic variables deta^L_m satisfy overline{d eta^L_m (d eta^L_{m'})^*} = delta_{m m'} dt / (2 hbar).

## Context

This work extends the Stochastic Mean-Field approach of Juillet and Chomaz (2002) and Lacroix (2005) from Hartree-Fock trial states to Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov states, thereby incorporating pairing correlations into the stochastic framework. It generalizes the earlier stochastic dynamics between BCS states introduced by Montina and Castin (2006). The method offers an alternative to Generator Coordinate Methods (GCM) of Bender, Heenen, and Reinhard (2003) that does not require a priori selection of collective degrees of freedom, though large numerical efforts remain.
