# Investigating the correlations of flow harmonics in 2.76A TeV Pb--Pb collisions

> This proceeding briefly summarizes our recent investigations on the correlations of flow harmonics in 2.76A TeV Pb--Pb collisions with viscous hydrodynamics {\tt VISH2+1}. We calculated both the symmetric cumulants $SC^{v}(m, n)$ and the normalized symmetric cumulants $NSC^{v}(m, n)$, and found $v_{2}$ and $v_{4}$, $v_{2}$ and $v_{5}$, $v_{3}$ and $v_{5}$ are correlated, $v_{2}$ and $v_{3}$, $v_{3}$ and $v_{4}$ are anti-correlated. We also found $NSC^{v}(3, 2)$ are insensitive to the QGP viscosity, which are mainly determined by the initial conditions.

# Investigating the correlations of flow harmonics in 2.76A TeV Pb--Pb collisions

## Key claim
The correlations between different flow harmonics, quantified by symmetric cumulants, are more sensitive to the details of hydrodynamic calculations than the individual flow coefficients alone; the normalized symmetric cumulant NSCv(3,2) is insensitive to the QGP shear viscosity and mainly determined by initial conditions, while NSCv(4,2), NSCv(5,2), and NSCv(5,3) are sensitive to both initial conditions and viscosity.

## Method
The authors used the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model VISH2+1 with longitudinal boost-invariance to simulate 2.76A TeV Pb--Pb collisions. They employed three different initial condition models (MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, and AMPT) and two values of specific shear viscosity eta/s for each (0.08 and 0.20 for MC-Glauber and MC-KLN; 0.08 and 0.16 for AMPT). The hydrodynamic output was converted to final hadron distributions at freeze-out temperature T_dec = 120 MeV via the Cooper-Frye prescription, and symmetric cumulants SCv(m,n) and normalized symmetric cumulants NSCv(m,n) were calculated from the event-by-event flow harmonics.

## Key numbers
- Specific shear viscosity values used: eta/s = 0.08 and 0.20 for MC-Glauber and MC-KLN initial conditions; eta/s = 0.08 and 0.16 for AMPT initial conditions
- Freeze-out temperature: T_dec = 120 MeV
- Collision energy: 2.76A TeV Pb--Pb collisions
- Centrality range for tuning: 0-5% centrality data of dN/deta and pT spectra of pi, K, and p

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical and experimental investigations of flow harmonic correlations, including studies by ALICE (2016) on symmetric cumulants, Aad (2014) on flow vector orientations, and Niemi (2015) on flow correlations. The calculations use the VISH2+1 hydrodynamic framework developed by Song (2007) and Shen (2014), with initial conditions from MC-Glauber, MC-KLN (Drescher 2006, Hirano 2009), and AMPT (Xu 2016). The results show that normalized symmetric cumulants can be used to constrain QGP viscosity for different initial conditions, and that the sign of SCv(4,3) is affected by the nonlinear contribution of epsilon_2^2 to v_4, as described by Gardim (2011) and Teaney (2012).
