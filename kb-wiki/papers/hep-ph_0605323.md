# Manifestation of Instabilities in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models

> We study a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model with two-flavor quark matter in $β$-equilibrium. It turns out that the system develops instabilities in the dispersion relations of the diquark fields, i.e., the velocity squared $v^2$ becomes negative in a certain region of the electron chemical potential. The critical point is the same as that of the chromomagnetic instability. The results imply the existence of spatially inhomogeneous diquark condensates in the genuine vacuum. We also discuss gauge equivalent classes between the inhomogeneous diquark condensates and gluon condensates.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model of two-flavor quark matter in beta-equilibrium, the dispersion relations of diquark fields develop instabilities (negative velocity squared) at the same critical points where chromomagnetic instabilities appear, implying that spatially inhomogeneous diquark condensates, not just gluon condensates, are the genuine ground state.

## Method

The authors calculate the two-point functions for diquark fields in the fermion one-loop approximation within an NJL model with two light quark flavors, neglecting current quark masses and the scalar-pseudoscalar interaction channel. They work in the hard dense loop (HDL) approximation, neglect the color chemical potential mu_8 (which is of order Delta^2/mu), and use the gap equation to eliminate the four-quark coupling constant. The dispersion relations are extracted from the zeros of the two-point functions in the kinematic region where energy and momentum are much smaller than the diquark gap Delta.

## Key numbers

* The velocity squared v^2 for the anti-red and anti-green diquark fields becomes negative for delta-mu > Delta/sqrt(2), where delta-mu is the mismatch between Fermi momenta and Delta is the diquark gap.
* For the anti-blue diquark field, gapless tachyons with v^2 < 0 emerge in the region delta-mu > Delta.
* In the 2SC region (delta-mu < Delta), the anti-blue diquark field phi_b2 has v^2 = 1/3, matching the relativistic density sound wave.
* In the limit delta-mu >> Delta, the two branches of the anti-blue diquark field approach v^2 = 1 (normal) and v^2 = -1/3 (tachyonic).
* For the real part of the anti-blue diquark field (phi_b1), the velocity squared approaches approximately -0.184 in the limit delta-mu >> Delta.

## Context

This work extends the study of chromomagnetic instabilities previously found in two-flavor color superconducting (2SC/g2SC) phases by Huang et al. (2004) and in the gapless color-flavor locked (gCFL) phase by Casalbuoni et al. (2004), Alford et al. (2005), and Fukushima (2005). The results connect to earlier proposals for resolving such instabilities, including the LOFF phase (Alford et al., 2000), mixed phases (Reddy et al., 2004), spontaneous NG current generation (Huang et al., 2005; Hong et al., 2005), and meson supercurrent states (Kryjevski et al., 2005; Schafer et al., 2005). The paper enables future construction of low-energy effective theories that simultaneously reproduce the correct velocities for diquark fields and the Debye/Meissner screening masses after gauging.
