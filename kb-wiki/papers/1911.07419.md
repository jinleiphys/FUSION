# Excitation functions of related parameters from transverse momentum (mass) spectra in high energy collisions

> Transverse momentum (mass) spectra of positively and negatively charged pions, positively and negatively charged kaons, protons and antiprotons produced at mid-(pseudo)rapidity in various collisions at high energies are analyzed in this work. The experimental data measured in central gold-gold, central lead-lead, and inelastic proton-proton collisions by several international collaborations are studied. The (two-component) standard distribution is used to fit the data and extract the excitation function of effective temperature. Then, the excitation functions of kinetic freeze-out temperature, transverse flow velocity, and initial temperature are obtained. In the considered collisions, the four parameters increase with the increase of collision energy in general, and the kinetic freeze-out temperature appears the trend of saturation at the top Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

The effective temperature, kinetic freeze-out temperature, transverse flow velocity, and initial temperature all increase with collision energy in general, but the kinetic freeze-out temperature shows a trend of saturation at the top Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and Large Hadron Collider energies, with the authors noting that the extracted parameters are model dependent and the method is described as "less model dependent" rather than fully model independent.

## Method

The authors fitted transverse momentum and transverse mass spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons at mid-(pseudo)rapidity using the (two-component) standard distribution (Bose-Einstein, Fermi-Dirac, or Boltzmann) to extract the effective temperature. From the effective temperature and the average transverse momentum, they derived the kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity using the multisource thermal model framework, and the initial temperature from the root-mean-square transverse momentum using the color string percolation model. The analysis covered central gold-gold collisions from the Alternating Gradient Synchrotron and Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, central lead-lead collisions from the Super Proton Synchrotron and Large Hadron Collider, and inelastic proton-proton collisions from the Super Proton Synchrotron, Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, and Large Hadron Collider.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperature T for pions in central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV: 162 ± 9 MeV (positive) and 159 ± 10 MeV (negative) from STAR data
- Effective temperature T for protons in central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV: 376 ± 16 MeV (positive) and 372 ± 11 MeV (negative) from STAR data
- Effective temperature T for pions in central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV: 168 ± 4 MeV (positive) and 169 ± 4 MeV (negative) from ALICE data
- Effective temperature T for kaons in central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV: 235 ± 12 MeV (positive) and 240 ± 13 MeV (negative) from ALICE data
- Chemical freeze-out temperature parameterization: T_lim = 0.158 GeV as the limiting or saturation temperature
- Contribution fraction of thermal motion parameterization: k_0 = 0.30 - 0.01 ln(sqrt(s_NN)) where sqrt(s_NN) is in GeV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of particle spectra in high energy collisions by using a method the authors describe as less model dependent than approaches such as the blast-wave model (Schnedermann et al.) or the statistical thermal model (Cleymans et al.). The study covers a wider energy range than prior single-experiment analyses, from 2.7 GeV to 13 TeV, and enables systematic comparison of freeze-out parameters across different collision systems (gold-gold, lead-lead, and proton-proton) and energies. The results can be used to test predictions from the color string percolation model (Braun et al.) and the multisource thermal model (Liu et al.) for the energy dependence of the initial temperature and flow velocity.
