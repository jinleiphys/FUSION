# Reaction rates from Coulomb dissociation: Core excitation effects

> A process, involving the 7Be core excitation in the Coulomb breakup of 8B into p + 7Be has been investigated. From the experimental results recently obtained in RIKEN we have derived the mixing amplitude of the | 7Be(1/2-) X (1p_{3/2}); 2+> configuration in the ground state of 8B. Implications on the evaluation of the S17 at stellar energies are discussed.

# Reaction rates from Coulomb dissociation: Core excitation effects

## Key claim

The mixing amplitude of the $| {}^{7}\text{Be}(1/2^{-}) \otimes \pi(1p_{3/2}); 2^{+}\rangle$ configuration in the ground state of $^{8}\text{B}$ is derived from experimental Coulomb breakup data, and the resulting inelastic contribution to the astrophysical S-factor $S_{17}$ at stellar energies is much larger than previously expected, implying that core excitation must be accounted for in breakup experiments aiming to determine this reaction rate.

## Method

The authors analyzed the Coulomb dissociation of $^{8}\text{B}$ into $p + ^{7}\text{Be}$, considering both the elastic channel (leaving $^{7}\text{Be}$ in its $3/2^{-}$ ground state) and the inelastic channel (populating the $1/2^{-}$ excited state of $^{7}\text{Be}$ at 0.429 MeV). They constructed a two-component ground-state wave function for $^{8}\text{B}$ with a mixing amplitude $\alpha$, used a Woods-Saxon potential (Barker potential parameters: $r_0 = 1.25$ fm, $d = 0.65$ fm) with well depths adjusted to reproduce proton binding energies ($V_{01} = -46.54$ MeV and $V_{02} = -47.93$ MeV for the two components), and calculated E1 transition matrix elements to determine the branching ratio between the two breakup channels. The mixing amplitude was then extracted by comparing the calculated branching ratio to the experimental value of 5% measured at RIKEN.

## Key numbers

- Mixing amplitude $\alpha = 0.36$ for the $| {}^{7}\text{Be}(1/2^{-}) \otimes \pi(1p_{3/2}); 2^{+}\rangle$ configuration in the $^{8}\text{B}$ ground state, derived from the experimental branching ratio of 5% for the inelastic breakup channel.
- Asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) of $\hat{b}_s = 0.75$ and 0.73 for the $\pi(1p_{3/2})_1$ and $\pi(1p_{3/2})_2$ single-particle components, respectively.
- Astrophysical S-factor $S_{17} = 19.5$ eV b for the elastic channel and $S_{17} = 9.6$ eV b for the inelastic channel, both evaluated at a proton energy $E_p = 20$ keV.
- Excitation energy of the $^{7}\text{Be}$ $1/2^{-}$ state: 0.429 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb dissociation method for determining the $^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}$ reaction rate, previously applied by Motobayashi et al. (1994), by incorporating the effect of core excitation in $^{7}\text{Be}$. The analysis uses experimental data from Kikuchi et al. (1997) on the inelastic breakup channel at RIKEN. The derived mixing amplitude $\alpha = 0.36$ is compared to a three-body model calculation by Grigorenko et al. (1998) which gave $\alpha = 0.31$. The results imply that future Coulomb breakup experiments must account for the inelastic channel contribution to reliably extract the $S_{17}$ astrophysical factor.
