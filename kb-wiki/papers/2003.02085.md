# Parametrisations of relativistic energy density functionals with tensor couplings

> The relativistic density functional with minimal density dependent nucleon-meson couplings for nuclei and nuclear matter is extended to include tensor couplings of the nucleons to the vector mesons. The dependence of the minimal couplings on either vector or scalar densities is explored. New parametrisations are obtained by a fit to nuclear observables with uncertainties that are determined self-consistently. The corresponding nuclear matter parameters at saturation are determined including their uncertainties. An improvement in the description of nuclear observables, in particular for binding energies and diffraction radii, is found when tensor couplings are considered, accompanied by an increase of the Dirac effective mass. The equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are studied for all models. The density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, the Dirac effective masses and scalar densities is explored. Problems at high densities for parametrisations using a scalar density dependence of the couplings are identified due to the rearrangement contributions in the scalar self-energies that lead to vanishing Dirac effective masses.

## Key claim

The paper extends relativistic density functionals with minimal density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings to include tensor couplings of nucleons to vector mesons, finding that tensor couplings improve the description of nuclear observables (particularly binding energies and diffraction radii) while increasing the Dirac effective mass, but only when the minimal couplings depend on vector density rather than scalar density, as scalar-density-dependent parametrisations develop problems at high densities where rearrangement contributions cause Dirac effective masses to vanish.

## Method

The authors constructed relativistic energy density functionals starting from a Lagrangian density with nucleon and meson fields, adding tensor coupling terms for the omega and rho mesons to the standard minimal couplings. They considered two types of density dependence for the minimal couplings (vector density dependence and scalar density dependence) and performed self-consistent fits to nuclear observables, allowing the uncertainties in the objective function to be determined during the fitting procedure rather than fixed heuristically. The model parameters were obtained by minimising an objective function that depends on differences between calculated and measured nuclear data, with the uncertainties adjusted self-consistently during the parameter determination.

## Context

This work extends the class of relativistic mean-field models with density-dependent couplings (such as those reviewed by Dutra (2014)) by systematically including tensor couplings of nucleons to omega and rho mesons, which were previously explored in parametrisations like the VT model of Rufa (1988), the G1 and G2 parametrisations of Furnstahl (1996), and the NL-VT1 model of Bender (1999), but without a comprehensive understanding of their role. The study also compares vector density dependence (common in recent EDF parametrisations) with scalar density dependence (discussed in early RMF work by Fuchs (1995) and Lenske (1995)), identifying problems with scalar-density-dependent couplings at high densities. The self-consistent determination of uncertainties during fitting follows the approach of Dobaczewski (2014). The results enable future studies of tensor coupling effects in deformed nuclei and beyond-mean-field calculations, though the authors note that such extensions would require complete refits of the EDF parameters.
