# Constraints on millicharged particles from nuclear gamma-decays

> We consider nuclear gamma decays and $γ$-emitting reactions that can be an efficient source of hypothetical millicharged particles ($χ$). In particular, we revisit the production of millicharged particles in nuclear reactor environment, pointing out that $γ$ cascades from $^{239}$U is an overlooked yet a powerful source of $χ\barχ$ pairs. This leads to an increased flux compared to previous studies. We then apply new estimates of the flux to derive novel limits on the value of millicharge, $\varepsilon = Q_χ/e$, from the electron recoil searched for in a variety of experiments placed in proximity to the reactor cores. The derived limits on $\varepsilon$ are the strongest in the interval of masses $\sim 0.7-2 $\,MeV. We also derive the MCP flux from the Sun and point out potential sensitivity of the low-threshold dark matter search experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear gamma cascades from neutron capture, particularly the overlooked $^{239}$U transitions, produce millicharged particles (MCPs) more efficiently than previously considered, leading to stronger constraints on the millicharge $\varepsilon$ in the mass range $\sim 0.7-2$ MeV, but only under the assumption that the nuclear matrix elements for photon emission do not change significantly when the photon is taken off-shell.

## Method

The authors calculate the ratio of MCP pair production to ordinary gamma emission for electric dipole (E1) and magnetic dipole (M1) nuclear transitions, using the formalism of Pitrou (2019). They apply this to neutron capture reactions in nuclear reactors, specifically the $p(n,\gamma)^2$H M1 transition at 2.223 MeV and two E1 transitions from $^{239}$U at 3.297 MeV and 4.060 MeV, using neutron capture yields from TEXONO (2005) and gamma emission data from the ENSDF database. The differential MCP flux is then combined with the Photo Absorption Ionization (PAI) model for atomic ionization cross sections in germanium detectors to derive count rates, which are compared to the experimental data from the TEXONO collaboration (2018).

## Key numbers

- Neutron yield per second from a reactor: $N_n = 7.8 \times 10^{19} \times \text{Power(GW)}$
- The strongest constraints on $\varepsilon$ are derived in the mass interval $\sim 0.7-2$ MeV
- For a 1 MeV MCP, the limiting millicharge from TEXONO data is $\varepsilon = 2.7 \times 10^{-5}$
- The asymptotic free-electron cross section for MCP detection scales as $\sigma(T>T_0) \simeq \varepsilon^2 \times 2.6 \times 10^{-25} \, \text{cm}^2 \times (\text{MeV}/T_0)$
- For solar MCPs, the total flux at 1 AU is approximately $12 \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1} \times (\varepsilon/10^{-5})^2 \times F(m_\chi)$, where $F=1$ for $m_\chi \ll m_e$
- The energy loss rate for MCPs traveling through the Earth's crust is estimated as $dE/dx = -(0.003-0.03) \, \text{MeV} / 100 \, \text{km} \times (\varepsilon/10^{-5})^2$

## Context

This work extends previous MCP searches at reactors by TEXONO (2018) and CONNIE (2024), which only considered MCP production from $e+\gamma \to e+\chi+\bar\chi$ reactions, limiting their sensitivity to $m_\chi < 0.5$ MeV. By including nuclear de-excitation channels, the authors extend the reach to higher masses. The production mechanism follows the treatment of pair emission by Kroll (1955) and Pitrou (2019). The derived limits are the strongest in the $0.7-2$ MeV range, surpassing constraints from SLAC beam dump experiments (Prinz 1998). The solar MCP flux calculation enables future sensitivity projections for low-threshold dark matter experiments such as Oscura (2023) and DAMIC-M (2025).
