# EMC effect with different oscillator-model parameters $hω$ for different shells by considering difference between proton and neutron structure functions

> The magnitude of binding energy used in the conventional nuclear theory to explain the EMC experimental data, seems to be larger than the one expected. In this paper to get sufficient depletion in the binding energy, different oscillator-model parameters $ \textit{$hω$} $ for different shells and the proton (neutron) structure function that have good agrement with experimental data are used. The extracted results for $^4He, ^{12}C, ^{40}Ca$ and $^{56}Fe$ nuclei show that one can get improved results in medium x ranges by less binding energy.

## Key claim

Using different oscillator-model parameters \(\hbar\omega\) for different nuclear shells (based on their root-mean-square radii) together with separate GRV proton and neutron structure functions yields improved EMC ratios in the medium-\(x\) range while requiring smaller binding energies than the conventional approach, which used a single \(\hbar\omega\) for all shells and a single nucleon structure function.

## Method

The author computes the nuclear structure function \(F_2^A(x)\) via a convolution of nucleon momentum distributions (derived from harmonic-oscillator wave functions) with free GRV leading-order proton and neutron structure functions. For each shell \((n,l)\) in a given nucleus, a distinct \(\hbar\omega\) is calculated from the shell's root-mean-square radius using \(\hbar\omega = 41.33\,(2n+l+3/2)/\langle r^2\rangle_{nl}\) (with \(\langle r^2\rangle_{nl}^{1/2}\) in fm and \(\hbar\omega\) in MeV). The EMC ratio \(R^A_{\mathrm{EMC}}(x) = F_2^A(x)_{\mathrm{per\;nucleon}} / F_2^{^2\mathrm{H}}(x)_{\mathrm{per\;nucleon}}\) is then evaluated for \(^4\mathrm{He}\), \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\), \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\), and \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\).

## Key numbers

* For \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\), the binding energies used are \(\varepsilon_{nl} = -26\) to \(-32\) MeV, compared to \(-40\) MeV used in prior work by Akulinichev et al. and Dunne et al.
* The extracted \(\hbar\omega\) values for different shells in each nucleus are listed in Table 1 (values not reproduced in the truncated text, but the formula \(\hbar\omega = 41.33\,(2n+l+3/2)/\langle r^2\rangle_{nl}\) is given with \(\hbar\omega\) in MeV and \(\langle r^2\rangle_{nl}^{1/2}\) in fm).
* For \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\), the EMC ratio is lowered by about 3 percent in the medium-\(x\) range due to the extra 4 neutrons (neutron structure function being smaller than proton structure function), even with \(\varepsilon_{nl}=0\).
* The results are considered reliable from \(x \sim 0.2\) to \(x \sim 0.7\).

## Context

This work extends the conventional nuclear theory approach of Akulinichev et al. (1985) and Dunne and Thomas (1986), who used a single harmonic-oscillator parameter for all shells and a single nucleon structure function that did not distinguish protons from neutrons. The author also notes that the light-front formalism of Miller and Smith gave results inconsistent with binding effects in both magnitude and nucleon-number dependence. By using shell-dependent \(\hbar\omega\) parameters and separate GRV proton/neutron structure functions, the present calculation achieves improved agreement with EMC data using smaller binding energies, and the results show saturation of the EMC ratio for large \(x\) across different nuclei.
