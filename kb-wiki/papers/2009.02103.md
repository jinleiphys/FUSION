# Electron-capture decay in isotopic transfermium chains from self-consistent calculations

> Weak decays in heavy nuclei with charge numbers Z=101-109 are studied within a microscopic formalism based on deformed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean-field calculations with pairing correlations. The half-lives of beta+ decay and electron capture are compared with alpha-decay half-lives obtained from phenomenological formulas. Transfermium isotopes of Md, No, Lr, Rf, Db, Sg, Bh, Hs, and Mt that can be produced in the frontier of cold and hot fusion-evaporation channels are considered. Several isotopes are identified whose beta+/EC- and alpha-decay half-lives are comparable. The competition between these decay modes opens the possibility of new pathways towards the islands of stability.

## Key claim

The paper identifies specific transfermium isotopes where beta+/EC-decay half-lives are comparable to alpha-decay half-lives, but this competition is conditional on the spin-parity assignment of the odd nucleon in odd-A nuclei; only positive-parity states produce half-lives short enough to compete with alpha decay, while negative-parity states yield half-lives orders of magnitude longer.

## Method

The author performed deformed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with pairing correlations in the BCS approximation using the SLy4 interaction, constructing deformation-energy curves to identify ground-state quadrupole deformations at β₂ ≈ 0.25. Gamow-Teller strength distributions were computed using a deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with residual spin-isospin interactions, and half-lives were obtained by summing allowed GT transitions weighted by phase-space factors for both positron emission and electron capture. Alpha-decay half-lives were estimated from four phenomenological formulas (Parkhomenko-Sobiczewski, Royer, and two Viola-Seaborg parametrizations) using experimental Qα values.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous microscopic study of beta+/EC-decay in superheavy nuclei (Sarriguren, 2019) to the transfermium region (Z=101-109, N=151-168), using a deformed self-consistent approach that goes beyond earlier phenomenological treatments. Prior calculations by Karpov (2012) and Zagrebaev (2012) used only allowed ground-state-to-ground-state transitions with a constant log(ft)=4.7, while Moller (1997) published only half-lives shorter than 100 s using a pnQRPA with folded-Yukawa single-particle Hamiltonian. The identification of isotopes with comparable beta+/EC- and alpha-decay half-lives opens new possible decay pathways toward the predicted islands of stability, complementing the charged-particle evaporation channels studied by Hong (2015, 2016) and the experimental evidence of beta+/EC-decay in ²⁵⁸Db reported by Hessberger (2016).
