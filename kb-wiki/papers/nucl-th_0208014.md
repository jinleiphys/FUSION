# Analysis of directed flow from three-particle correlations

> We present a new method for analysing directed flow, based on a three-particle azimuthal correlation. It is less biased by nonflow correlations than two-particle methods, and requires less statistics than four-particle methods. It is illustrated on NA49 data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-particle azimuthal correlation method for measuring directed flow \(v_1\) that is less biased by nonflow correlations than two-particle methods and requires less statistics than four-particle methods, but it relies on a prior independent measurement of elliptic flow \(v_2\) and yields only the product \((v_1)^2 v_2\), from which \(v_1\) is extracted up to a global sign.

## Method

The method measures a mixed three-particle correlation \(\langle e^{i(\phi_a+\phi_b-2\phi_c)}\rangle \simeq (v_1)^2 v_2\) using a generating function formalism to sum over all particle triplets and remove detector anisotropies. First, the integrated elliptic flow \(v_2\) is obtained from four-particle cumulants of the same event sample, which are free from nonflow effects. Then, the integrated directed flow \(v_1\) is extracted from the three-particle correlation using the measured \(v_2\), and finally differential \(v_1(p_T,y)\) is obtained by restricting one particle to a narrow \((p_T,y)\) bin.

## Key numbers

- Weighted elliptic flow \(v_2\) of charged particles in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 \(A\)GeV is shown as a function of centrality using two-, four-, six-, and eight-particle cumulants; the four-, six-, and eight-particle estimates all agree, supporting freedom from nonflow effects.
- Weighted directed flow \(v_1\) of charged particles is presented as a function of centrality; the statistical uncertainty on the three-particle estimate is barely larger than that on the two-particle value.
- Differential directed flow \(v_1\) of charged pions in midcentral collisions is shown as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity; at high \(p_T\), the three-particle result is consistent with the two-particle value corrected for momentum conservation but significantly lower than the uncorrected one.
- At midrapidity, the three-particle estimate of \(v_1\) vanishes as required by symmetry, while the uncorrected two-particle estimate does not.

## Context

This work extends the cumulant method for flow analysis developed by Borghini (2001) from four-particle correlations to a three-particle mixed correlation, addressing the specific problem that directed flow \(v_1\) is very small at ultrarelativistic energies, making four-particle methods statistically prohibitive. The method is applied to NA49 data on Pb-Pb collisions at 158 \(A\)GeV, where earlier work by Dinh (1999) had shown that nonflow correlations from HBT and momentum conservation are important at SPS energies. It enables reliable differential measurements of directed flow at RHIC and LHC energies where minijet correlations may be large, as noted by Kovchegov (2002).
