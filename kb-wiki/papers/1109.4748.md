# The Lattice QCD Study of the Three-Nucleon Force

> We investigate three-nucleon forces (3NF) from lattice QCD simulations, utilizing the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function to determine two-nucleon forces (2NF) and 3NF on the same footing. Quantum numbers of the three-nucleon (3N) system are chosen to be (I, J^P)=(1/2, 1/2^+) (the triton channel). We consider the simplest geometrical configuration where 3N are aligned linearly with an equal spacing, to reduce the enormous computational cost. Lattice QCD simulations are performed using Nf=2 dynamical clover fermion configurations at the lattice spacing of a = 0.156 fm on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with a large quark mass corresponding to m(π) = 1.13 GeV. We find repulsive 3NF at short distance.

## Key claim

The paper reports a first-principles lattice QCD calculation of three-nucleon forces (3NF) in the triton channel, finding repulsive 3NF at short distance under the specific condition of a heavy pion mass (m_pi = 1.13 GeV) and a linear geometric configuration of the three nucleons.

## Method

The authors used the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function formalism extended to the three-nucleon (3N) system, extracting the 3N wave function from six-point correlators on the lattice. They employed Nf=2 dynamical clover fermion configurations on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with lattice spacing a = 0.156 fm, and restricted the 3N geometry to a linear setup with equal spacing to reduce computational cost. The 3NF were extracted by subtracting two-nucleon forces (2NF), obtained from separate lattice calculations, from the total potential in the 3N Schrodinger equation, using a specific spin-isospin channel (psi_S) that automatically eliminates contributions from parity-odd 2NF.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: a = 0.156 fm, with inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} = 1.269(14) GeV
* Lattice volume: 16^3 x 32, corresponding to (2.5 fm)^3 physical spatial box
* Pion mass: m_pi = 1.13 GeV; nucleon mass: m_N = 2.15 GeV; Delta mass: m_Delta = 2.31 GeV
* Number of configurations: 598, with measurements at 32 source time slices per configuration
* Sink time slice for main results: (t-t_0)/a = 8, with consistency check at (t-t_0)/a = 9
* Energy shift applied: delta_E approximately 5 MeV, with systematic error less than or about 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the NBS wave function approach previously applied to two-nucleon forces by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009) to the three-nucleon system, and follows earlier studies of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions by Nemura (2008), Inoue (2010), Sasaki (2010), and Aoki (2011). The results provide a first-principles lattice QCD determination of 3NF, complementing phenomenological models such as the two-pion exchange model with Delta-excitation pioneered by Fujita and Miyazawa (1957). The observed short-range repulsive 3NF is relevant for understanding the nuclear equation of state at high density in neutron stars, and the authors note that simulations with lighter quark masses are underway to study the quark mass dependence.
