# Short-range interactions and narrow resonances in effective field theory

> We consider the effective field theory (EFT) treatment of two-body systems with narrow resonances. Within this approach, an $s$-wave scattering amplitude can be expanded in powers of a typical momentum scale of a system $Q\ll Λ$, where $Λ$ represents a hard scale of a scattering system and an energy difference $δε=|E-ε_0|\ll ε_0$, where $ε_0$ is a resonance peak energy. It is shown that at leading order in the double expansion a universal form of a two-body scattering amplitude is a sum of a Breit-Wigner term of order $Q^{-1}$, a smooth background term of order $Q^0$, and an interference term of order $Q^0$. The techniques developed in this paper can be used to investigate the properties of narrow resonances that are produced by short-distance dynamics.

# Short-range interactions and narrow resonances in effective field theory

## Key claim
The universal form of a two-body scattering amplitude for narrow resonances at leading order in a double expansion in momentum scale Q and energy difference δε includes a Breit-Wigner term, a smooth background term, AND an interference term of order Q^0, contrary to a prior claim by Gelman (2010) that such interference terms are suppressed by additional powers of Q and δε.

## Method
The author constructs an effective field theory (EFT) Lagrangian for two spin-zero particles of mass M with a dimeron field representing the resonance, containing a four-point coupling C0 for background scattering and a Yukawa-like coupling g for resonance coupling. The power counting assigns C0 ~ Q^0, g ~ Q, resonance energy ε0 ~ Q^2, and energy difference δε ~ Q^3, with the width Γ ~ Q^3 for a narrow resonance. The scattering amplitude is computed by summing Feynman diagrams order by order in the expansion, including all graphs that contribute at each order, and the couplings are fixed by matching to the expanded phenomenological amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The Breit-Wigner term in the amplitude scales as Q^{-1}, the background and interference terms scale as Q^0, and corrections scale as Q
- The resonance width Γ(E) = 2 g'^2 k = 2 g'^2 √(ME), where g' = g√(M/4π) is a dimensionless coupling
- The four-point coupling is fixed as C0 = (4π/M) a_bg, where a_bg is the s-wave background scattering length
- The dimeron coupling is fixed as g^2 = (4π/M)(1/k)(Γ(E)/2)
- The loop integral I0 = -i(M/4π)k scales as Q

## Context
This work corrects and extends the EFT treatment of narrow resonances by Gelman (2010), who neglected interference terms that the author shows are of the same order (Q^0) as the background term. The approach is analogous to the EFT treatment of strongly interacting systems with bound or virtual states developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and van Kolck (1999), but adapted for the weak-coupling limit where a narrow resonance appears. The techniques can be applied to investigate near-threshold states such as the X(3872) particle.
