# Distortions in the negative energy Dirac sea: violation of the Gottfried sum rule and $Δ\bar{u}$ in proton

> We report on recent work concerning the effect which the change in vacuum structure (negative energy Dirac sea), in the presence of a confining scalar field, has on the nucleon structure functions and parton distributions. Using the Dirac equation in 1+1 dimensions, we show that distortions in the Dirac sea are responsible for part of the violation of the Gottfried sum rule -- i.e., part of the flavor asymmetry in the proton sea. Our basic argument is that, even if isospin is an exact symmetry, the presence of a confining potential changes the vacuum structure, and inevitably leads to a violation of SU(2) flavour symmetry in a hadron with a different number of valence $u$ and $d$ quarks. The same mechanism also leads to a prediction for $Δ\bar{u}$ and $Δ\bar{d}$.

## Key claim

The authors argue that distortions in the negative energy Dirac sea caused by a confining scalar potential inevitably produce a flavor asymmetry in the proton sea, even if isospin is an exact symmetry, and that this mechanism accounts for part of the violation of the Gottfried sum rule.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation in 1+1 dimensions for massless fermions in the presence of a static, kink-like scalar potential V(z) = V₀ tanh z. They expand the field operator in two complete orthonormal sets (free plane waves and eigenstates of the potential), compute the overlaps between the positive-energy bound state wave function and free plane wave solutions, and interpret the non-zero overlap with negative-energy free states as the source of sea quark asymmetry in a model proton.

## Key numbers

- The scalar potential strength is chosen as V₀ = 3/2, yielding bound states at energies E₁^(±) = ±√2.
- The bound state wave function for n=1 is given explicitly: χ_(b,1)^(±)(z) = (√2 tanh z sech^(1/2) z, ± sech^(1/2) z)^T.
- The Gottfried sum rule violation is expressed as S_G = 1/3 - (2/3) ∫ dp/(2π) |⟨v_p|χ_(b,1)⟩|², where the integral gives the area under the valence curve for negative free energy.

## Context

This work extends the study of Gottfried sum rule violation, experimentally confirmed by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC), beyond existing explanations such as mesonic (pionic) contributions from Thomas (1983) and Pauli exclusion principle arguments from Signal. The approach is based on nonperturbative vacuum structure changes analogous to the fractional charge phenomenon studied by Jackiw and Rebbi (1976) and others. The authors note that while chiral quark soliton model calculations by Wakamatsu, Diakonov, Goeke, and Weigel have studied vacuum structure self-consistently, the direct connection to structure functions was not easily visible. The mechanism predicts Δū positive and of similar magnitude to d̄ - ū, while Δd̄ is expected to be negative and smaller, providing a way to distinguish this mechanism from the unpolarized pion cloud contribution of Thomas (1983, 2000).
