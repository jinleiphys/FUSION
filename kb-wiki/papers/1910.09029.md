# High Energy Hadron Production, Self-Organized Criticality and Absorbing State Phase Transition

> In high energy nuclear collisions, production rates of light nuclei agree with the predictions of an ideal gas at a temperature $T=155 \pm 10$ MeV. In an equilibrium hadronic medium of this temperature, light nuclei cannot survive. In this contribution, we suggest that the observed behavior is due to an evolution in global non-equilibrium, leading to self-organized criticality and to hadron formation as an absorbing state phase transition for color degrees of freedom. At the confinement point, the initial quark-gluon medium becomes quenched by the vacuum, breaking up into all allowed free hadronic and nuclear mass states, without (or with a very short-live) subsequent formation of thermal hadronic medium.

# High Energy Hadron Production, Self-Organized Criticality and Absorbing State Phase Transition

## Key claim

The observed production yields of light nuclei in high-energy nuclear collisions, which match an ideal gas at T = 155 ± 10 MeV despite the impossibility of nuclear survival in a hot hadron gas, can be explained by a global non-equilibrium evolution leading to self-organized criticality and hadron formation as an absorbing state phase transition for color degrees of freedom, without any subsequent thermal hadronic medium.

## Method

The authors model hadronization as a non-equilibrium phase transition to an absorbing state for color degrees of freedom, using a mean-field evolution equation for color charge density ρ(t) with rates for parton annihilation, parton production, and color neutralization. They then apply the formalism of self-organized criticality, using the Hagedorn bootstrap density of states ρ(m) ~ (1 + m/μ₀)^(-a) exp(m/T_H) as the strength function, and fit the resulting power-law yield N(m) = α[ρ(m)]^(-p) to ALICE data for hadron and light nuclei production in Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- The critical hadronization temperature is T_c = 155 ± 10 MeV, matching the pseudocritical confinement temperature from lattice QCD.
- The hyper-triton root-mean-square size is close to 10 fm, approximately the same size as the whole fireball formed in Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV.
- The energy needed to remove the Λ from the hyper-triton is 130 ± 30 keV.
- The Hagedorn temperature derived from the bootstrap equation with m₀ = m_π ≈ 130 MeV is T_H ≈ 150 MeV.
- The fit to ALICE data using log[(dN/dy)/(2s+1)] ≈ -m(0.43 p / T_H) + A yields p = 0.9 and A = 3.4, with T_H = 155 MeV.
- The critical exponent for the integer partitioning model is p ≈ 1.26.

## Context

This work extends the puzzle identified by Andronic et al. (2018) and the statistical hadronization model (SHM) of Braun-Munzinger et al., where light nuclei yields match thermal predictions despite their binding energies of a few MeV. The authors propose an alternative to the standard thermal hadron gas picture, building on the self-organized criticality framework of Bak et al. (1987) and the absorbing state phase transition formalism of Hinrichsen (2000) and Henkel et al. (2008). The approach enables a non-thermal explanation for the observed power-law mass dependence of hadron yields, suggesting that hydrodynamic flow observed in experiments originates from the earlier deconfined stage rather than from a hadron gas phase.
