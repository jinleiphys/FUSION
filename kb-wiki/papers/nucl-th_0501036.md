# Non-reactive scattering of excited exotic hydrogen atoms

> The Coulomb deexcitation of light exotic atoms in collisions with hydrogen atoms has been studied in the framework of the fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling method for the first time. The calculations of the $l$-averaged cross sections have been performed for $(μp)_n$ and $(μd)_n$ atoms in the states with the principal quantum number $n=3 ÷8$ and relative energies region $E=0.01 ÷100$ eV. The obtained results reveal the new $n$ and $E$ dependences of the Coulomb deexcitation cross sections. The large fraction (up to $\sim$ 36%) of the transition with $Δn > 1$ is also predicted.

# Non-reactive scattering of excited exotic hydrogen atoms

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling calculations of Coulomb deexcitation cross sections for light exotic atoms colliding with hydrogen, revealing that the traditional semiclassical n-dependence (σ ~ n^γ with γ ≥ 2) and 1/E energy dependence are incorrect for low principal quantum numbers n = 3–8, with the actual behavior being approximately 1/√E at low energies and strongly influenced by multi-step (Δn > 1) transitions that were neglected in prior two-state approximations.

## Method

The authors solved the time-independent Schrödinger equation for the four-body system (aμ⁻ + be⁻) using a close-coupling expansion in a basis of total angular momentum eigenstates, with the hydrogen target fixed in its 1s ground state. The coupled radial equations were integrated numerically using the Numerov method, including all open channels with n' ≤ n and all multipoles in the interaction potential up to convergence (t_m = 8–10). Cross sections were computed for (μp)_n and (μd)_n with n = 3–8 at relative collision energies E_cm = 0.01–100 eV, averaging over initial orbital angular momentum substates with statistical weight (2l+1)/n².

## Key numbers

- Coulomb deexcitation cross section σ^CD_3 for (μp)_3 + H at E_cm = 0.01 eV: 4.848 a.u.; at E_cm = 20 eV: 0.012 a.u.
- Coulomb deexcitation cross section σ^CD_6 for (μp)_6 + H at E_cm = 0.01 eV: 6.246 a.u.; at E_cm = 20 eV: 0.033 a.u.
- Fraction of transitions with Δn > 1 for (μp)_6 + H reaches up to 36.8% (at E_cm = 10 eV and 15 eV)
- Fraction of transitions with Δn > 1 for (μp)_4 + H reaches up to 34.9% (at E_cm = 0.05 eV)
- For (μp)_3 + H, the fraction f_3 is only 0.2–0.6% across all energies, indicating Δn > 1 transitions are negligible for n = 3
- For (μd)_6 + H at E_cm = 0.1 eV, the elastic cross section σ_66 converges to 54.31 a.u. with t_m = 10 multipoles, while the dipole-only approximation (t_m = 1) gives 54.70 a.u.
- For (μp)_7 + D at E_cm = 1 eV, including 7 levels gives σ_76 = 0.597360 a.u., while a 2-level approximation gives 1.063412 a.u. (nearly a factor of 2 difference)
- The energy dependence at E_cm ≲ 0.5 eV is approximately 1/√E, in contrast to the 1/E behavior of Bracci and Fiorentini (1981) and the advanced adiabatic approach

## Context

This work extends the quantum-mechanical close-coupling treatment previously applied to elastic scattering and Stark transitions of muonic hydrogen (Korenman et al., 1997) to the Coulomb deexcitation process, which had only been treated with semiclassical methods (Bracci and Fiorentini, 1981), classical-trajectory Monte Carlo (Jensen and Markushin, 1999), or the advanced adiabatic approach (Ponomarev and Solovyov, 1999). The results contradict the long-standing σ ~ n^γ/E dependence used in the extended standard cascade model (ESCM) and show that multi-step deexcitation (Δn > 1) contributes substantially (up to ~37%) for n ≥ 4, which was neglected in two-state approximations. The new cross sections provide a more reliable theoretical input for kinetics calculations of muonic atom cascades and may help resolve the "hot" πp atom puzzle where kinetic energies up to 200 eV were observed experimentally.
