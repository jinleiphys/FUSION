# Exact charged and rotating toroidal black hole in the Einstein $SU(N)$-Skyrme model

> In this paper, we construct an exact solution of the Einstein $SU(N)$-Skyrme model in $D=4$ space-time dimensions describing a charged and rotating black hole with toroidal horizon. Rotation is added by applying an improper coordinate transformation to the known static toroidal black hole with Skyrme hair, while the electric charge is supplemented by considering a $U(1)$ gauge field interacting with Einstein gravity. We perform the thermal analysis in the grand canonical ensemble, explicitly showing the role that the flavor number plays. Some discussions about stability are also considered.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an exact analytical solution for a charged and rotating toroidal black hole in the Einstein $SU(N)$-Skyrme model, but only for the specific case where the Skyrme field takes a particular maximal embedding Ansatz and rotation is added via an improper coordinate transformation that preserves local geometry while making the solution globally stationary.

## Method

The authors start from a known static toroidal black hole with Skyrme hair (Henríquez-Baez et al., 2023) and apply an improper gauge transformation (Lorentz boost) in the $(t,\phi)$ plane to introduce rotation, simultaneously transforming the matter field. Electric charge is added by coupling a $U(1)$ Maxwell field to Einstein gravity with a minimal coupling term. The thermal analysis is performed in the grand canonical ensemble using the Euclidean action approach with appropriate counterterms, and global stability is studied by comparing the Gibbs free energy of the hairy solution to the vacuum rotating toroidal black hole (Lemos, 1995).

## Key numbers

* The trace of the $SU(N)$ generators is $\text{Tr}(T_j T_k) = -\frac{1}{2} a_N \delta_{jk}$ with $a_N = N(N^2-1)/6$, which explicitly depends on the flavor number $N$.
* The Hawking temperature for the charged rotating black hole is $T = -\left(\frac{K\kappa a_N q^2}{16\pi r_+} + \frac{(K\kappa a_N \lambda q^4 + 32Q^2)}{128\pi r_+^3} - \frac{3r_+}{4\pi\ell^2}\right)\sqrt{1-\omega^2}$, where $r_+$ is the horizon radius, $q$ is the hair parameter, $Q$ is the charge parameter, and $\omega$ is the rotation parameter.
* The entropy is $S = \pi^2 r_+^2 / (2\sqrt{1-\omega^2}) = A/4$, satisfying the area law.
* The angular velocity of the black hole is $\Omega = \omega/\ell$, where $\ell^2 = -3/\Lambda$ is the AdS radius.
* The electric charge is $\tilde{Q} = \pi Q / (2\sqrt{1-\omega^2})$.

## Context

This work extends the exact toroidal black hole solutions previously constructed in the Einstein $SU(N)$-Skyrme model (Henríquez-Baez et al., 2023) and the $SU(2)$-non-linear sigma model (Canfora et al., 2013-2018) by adding both rotation and electric charge analytically. The solution generalizes the rotating toroidal black hole first presented by Lemos (1995) to include Skyrme hair, and the charged static solution of Canfora et al. (2013) to include rotation and arbitrary flavor number. The results enable holographic applications using the AdS/CFT correspondence, particularly for modeling the quark-gluon plasma with topologically planar event horizons, and provide a tractable analytical framework for studying hairy black hole thermodynamics with multiple parameters.
