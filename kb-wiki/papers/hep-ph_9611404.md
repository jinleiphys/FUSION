# Baryons in chiral constituent quark model

> Beyond the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking scale light and strange baryons should be considered as systems of three constituent quarks with an effective confining interaction and a flavor-spin chiral interaction that is mediated by the octet of Goldstone bosons (pseudoscalar mesons) between the constituent quarks. One cannot exclude, however, the possibility that this flavor-spin interaction has an appreciable vector- and higher meson exchange component.

# Baryons in chiral constituent quark model

## Key claim
Light and strange baryons beyond the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking scale should be described as three constituent quarks interacting via a flavor-spin chiral interaction mediated by the octet of Goldstone bosons (pseudoscalar mesons), but the author acknowledges that this interaction may also include an appreciable vector- and higher meson exchange component that cannot be excluded.

## Method
The paper develops a chiral constituent quark model where the effective interaction between constituent quarks consists of a confining potential plus a flavor-spin dependent interaction derived from pseudoscalar meson (pion, kaon, eta) exchange. The model is first treated analytically using harmonic oscillator confinement and first-order perturbation theory to extract matrix elements from the baryon spectrum, then validated through numerical three-body Faddeev calculations with linear confinement and the Goldstone boson exchange interaction included to all orders, also incorporating flavor-singlet eta-prime exchange.

## Key numbers
- Quark condensate order parameter: <vacuum|qq|vacuum> ≈ -(240-250 MeV)^3
- N-Delta splitting used to determine constant C_chi = 29.3 MeV
- Oscillator parameter hbar-omega ≈ 250 MeV from mass differences between first excited 1/2+ states and ground states (e.g., N(1440) - N)
- From full fit: hbar-omega = 157.4 MeV, P_00 = 29.3 MeV, P_11 = 45.2 MeV, P_20 = 2.7 MeV, P_22 = -34.7 MeV
- Predicted N,Sigma mass difference: m(1/2+) - m(1/2- - 3/2-) = -102 MeV
- Predicted Lambda mass difference: m(1/2+) - m(1/2- - 3/2-) = 74 MeV
- Faddeev calculations reproduce lowest N and Delta states with deviations not exceeding 3% in the most unfavorable cases

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach by replacing the traditional one-gluon exchange interaction (used in earlier attempts such as those reviewed by Isgur and Karl) with a Goldstone boson exchange interaction motivated by spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD. The model successfully explains three features that the gluon-exchange model fails to address: the different ordering of positive and negative parity states in nucleon versus Lambda spectra, the existence of two-quantum positive parity excitations below one-quantum negative parity excitations, and the absence of strong spin-orbit splittings. The paper cites lattice QCD results by Liu and Dong showing that N-Delta splitting appears in quenched but not valence approximations, supporting meson exchange over gluon exchange as the origin of this splitting. This work enables a unified description of light and strange baryon spectra and connects to the pion-nucleon sigma-term problem discussed by Gasser.
