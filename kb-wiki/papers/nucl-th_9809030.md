# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov description of the deformed ground-state proton emitters

> Ground-state properties of deformed proton-rich odd-Z nuclei in the region $59 \leq Z \leq 69$ are described in the framework of Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory. One-proton separation energies and ground-state quadrupole deformations that result from fully self-consistent microscopic calculations are compared with available experimental data. The model predicts the location of the proton drip-line, the properties of proton emitters beyond the drip-line, and provides information about the deformed single-particle orbitals occupied by the odd valence proton.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the NL3 effective interaction and finite-range Gogny pairing (D1S) can reproduce experimental one-proton separation energies for deformed proton emitters in the region 59 ≤ Z ≤ 69, though the agreement is not perfect for all cases; the calculated value for 145Tm differs from experiment by more than 200 keV.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations self-consistently for odd-Z nuclei, using the NL3 effective interaction for the mean-field Lagrangian and the pairing part of the Gogny force (parameter set D1S) for the pairing channel. Ground states of odd-proton systems were treated with a simple blocking procedure, and the resulting quasiparticle eigenspectrum was transformed into the canonical basis to obtain single-particle energies and occupation probabilities. One-proton separation energies were calculated as the difference between binding energies of neighboring nuclei.

## Key numbers

- 131Eu: calculated proton separation energy of -0.9 MeV, compared to experimental transition energy Ep = 0.950(8) MeV with half-life 26(6) ms; odd proton occupies the 5/2+[413] orbital
- 141Ho: calculated proton separation energy of -0.9 MeV, compared to experimental Ep = 1.169(8) MeV with half-life 4.2(4) ms; odd proton occupies the 7/2-[523] orbital
- 145Tm: calculated proton separation energy of -1.46 MeV, compared to experimental Ep = 1.728(10) MeV; ground-state quadrupole deformation β2 = 0.23 (prolate)
- 147Tm: calculated proton separation energy of -0.96 MeV, compared to experimental Ep = 1.054(19) MeV; ground-state quadrupole deformation β2 = -0.19 (oblate)
- Predicted drip-line nuclei: 124Pr, 129Pm, 134Eu, 139Tb, 146Ho, and 152Tm
- Predicted proton emitters 136Tb and 135Tb with separation energies -0.90 MeV and -1.15 MeV respectively, both with 3/2+[411] proton configuration
- Pr, Pm, Eu, and Tb isotopes show strong prolate deformations with β2 ≈ 0.30 - 0.35

## Context

This work extends the RHB model, previously applied to spherical proton-rich nuclei (Vretenar, 1998) and neutron-rich systems (Lalazissis, 1998), to deformed proton emitters in the rare-earth region. The calculations are compared with experimental data on direct proton decay from Davids (1998) for 131Eu and 141Ho, and from Batchelder (1998) and Sellin (1993) for Tm isotopes. The results enable fully microscopic and self-consistent predictions for the entire proton drip-line in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei from Z=51 to Z=83, going beyond the macroscopic-microscopic mass model of Moller (1995) and the spherical WKB or DWBA approximations used in earlier proton-decay studies.
