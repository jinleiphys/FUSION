# Proposal for measuring new transverse momentum dependent parton distributions $g_{1T}$ and $h_{1L}^\perp$ through semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering

> We calculate $g_{1T}$ and $h_{1L}^\perp$, two of the eight leading twist transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), in the light-cone quark-diquark model. The new TMDs can be measured through semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS). We present predictions of the single and double spin asymmetries related to $g_{1T}$ and $h_{1L}^\perp$ in SIDIS at HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab kinematics respectively.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the two least-studied leading-twist transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), \(g_{1T}\) and \(h_{1L}^\perp\), in the light-cone quark-diquark model and predicts that the associated single and double spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) are significantly larger than those of the pretzelosity asymmetry \(A_{UT}^{\sin(3\phi_h - \phi_S)}\) reported in She (2009), making a transverse momentum cut unnecessary for their measurement.

## Method

The authors computed \(g_{1T}\) and \(h_{1L}^\perp\) using the light-cone quark-diquark model, which treats the spectator system as a scalar or vector diquark and includes the Melosh-Wigner rotation. They then expressed these TMDs in terms of unpolarized parton distribution functions and used two approaches for numerical predictions: approach 1 used the model's own wave functions directly, while approach 2 adopted the CTEQ6L parametrization for unpolarized distributions with a Gaussian transverse momentum form factor. The double spin asymmetry \(A_{LT}^{\cos(\phi_h-\phi_S)}\) and the single spin asymmetry \(A_{UL}^{\sin 2\phi_h}\) were calculated for SIDIS on proton, neutron, and deuteron targets at the kinematics of HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab experiments.

## Key numbers

* The model parameters used are: quark mass \(m_q = 0.33\ \mathrm{GeV}\), scalar diquark mass \(m_S = 0.60\ \mathrm{GeV}\), vector diquark mass \(m_V = 0.80\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and mixing angle \(\theta_0 = \pi/4\).
* The Gaussian width for transverse momentum in approach 2 is \(k_{av}^2 = 0.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* The ratios \(g_{1T}^{(1/2)v}(x) / f_1(x)\) and \(h_{1L}^{\perp(1/2)v}(x) / f_1(x)\) are shown at \(Q^2 = 3.0\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* The asymmetry predictions are presented for HERMES (beam energy 27.6 GeV, \(Q^2 > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), \(x\) range 0.023-0.4), COMPASS (beam energy 160 GeV, \(Q^2 > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)), JLab6 (beam energy 6 GeV, \(Q^2 > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) for proton, \(Q^2\) range 1.3-3.1 \(\mathrm{GeV}^2\) for neutron), and JLab12 (beam energy 12 GeV, \(Q^2 > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the light-cone quark-diquark model calculations of TMDs, following the same method used by She (2009) for the pretzelosity distribution \(h_{1T}^\perp\). The authors note that \(g_{1T}\) and \(h_{1L}^\perp\) are unique among the eight leading-twist TMDs because their corresponding generalized parton distributions vanish due to time-reversal invariance, as discussed by Diehl (2005). The predictions enable future measurements at HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab to probe the quark spin-orbit correlation in the nucleon, with the authors expecting that the asymmetries are comparable to those in Kotzinian (2006) but larger than those in Boffi (2009).
