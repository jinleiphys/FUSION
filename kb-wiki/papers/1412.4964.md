# Meson turbulence at quark deconfinement from AdS/CFT

> Based on the QCD string picture at confining phase, we conjecture that the deconfinement transition always accompanies a condensation of higher meson resonances with a power-law behavior, "meson turbulence". We employ the AdS/CFT correspondence to calculate the meson turbulence for $\mathcal{N}=2$ supersymmetric QCD at large $N_c$ and at strong coupling limit, and find that the energy distribution to each meson level $n$ scales as $n^α$ with the universal scaling $α=-5$. The universality is checked for various ways to attain the quark deconfinement: a static electric field below/around the critical value, a time-dependent electric field quench, and a time-dependent quark mass quench, all result in the turbulent meson condensation with the universal power $α=-5$ around the deconfinement.

## Key claim

The paper conjectures that quark deconfinement always accompanies a condensation of higher meson resonances with a power-law energy distribution ("meson turbulence"), and claims that for the specific theory studied, 𝒩=2 supersymmetric QCD at large N_c and strong coupling, the universal scaling exponent is α = −5, independent of how deconfinement is triggered.

## Method

The authors employ the AdS/CFT correspondence using the D3/D7 brane system to model 𝒩=2 supersymmetric QCD with a single flavor. They solve the D7-brane action in AdS₅×S⁵ background, decompose the brane fluctuations into Kaluza-Klein modes corresponding to meson resonances labeled by level n, and compute the energy distribution ε_n across meson levels for three distinct deconfinement scenarios: a static electric field below/at the Schwinger limit, a time-dependent electric field quench, and a time-dependent quark mass quench.

## Key numbers

- The energy distribution scales as ε_n ∝ (ω_n)^α with α = −5 (specifically measured as −5.01 from numerical fit for the static electric field case).
- The Schwinger limit for the static electric field is E_Sch = 0.5759 m² (in units where R=1).
- Meson resonance masses are given by ω_n = 2√[(n+1)(n+2)] m for n = 0,1,2,...
- The effective QCD string tension at the D7-brane tip is σ_st = (1/(2π l_s²)) [w(ρ=0)²/R² − R²E], which approaches zero near the deconfinement transition.

## Context

This work extends the QCD string picture of confinement (Polyakov 1978) and the holographic description of mesons via the D3/D7 system (Karch and Katz 2002), and is motivated by the Bizon-Rostworowski conjecture (2011) of weak turbulence in AdS gravity. The results enable a new observable, the power-law scaling of meson resonance energies, as a universal signature of deconfinement, applicable to both static and time-dependent transitions, and suggest a connection between deconfinement and Hagedorn-like behavior in string theory.
