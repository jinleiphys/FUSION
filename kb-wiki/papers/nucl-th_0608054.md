# Problems with interpretation of $^{10}$He ground state

> The continuum of $^{10}$He nucleus is studied theoretically in a three-body $^{8}$He+$n$+$n$ model basing on the recent information concerning $^9$He spectrum [Golovkov, \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{76}, 021605(R) (2007)]. The $^{10}$He ground state (g.s.) candidate with structure $[p_{1/2}]^2$ for new g.s. energy of $^9$He is predicted to be at about $2.0-2.3$ MeV. The peak in the cross section associated with this state may be shifted to a lower energy (e.g. $\sim 1.2$ MeV) when $^{10}$He is populated in reactions with $^{11}$Li due to peculiar reaction mechanism. Formation of the low-energy ($E< 250$ keV) ``alternative'' ground state with structure $[s_{1/2}]^2$ is highly probable in $^{10}$He in the case of considerable attraction (e.g. $a<-5$ fm) in the s-wave $^9$He channel, which properties are still quite uncertain. This result either questions the existing experimental low-energy spectrum of $^{10}$He or place a limit on the scattering length in $^9$He channel, which contradicts existing data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the ground state of \(^{10}\)He may not be the previously observed resonance near 1.2–1.7 MeV, but could instead be a very low-energy (\(E < 250\) keV) state with \([s_{1/2}]^2\) structure, provided the s-wave neutron-\(^9\)He interaction is sufficiently attractive (scattering length \(a < -5\) fm). This interpretation either contradicts existing experimental low-energy spectra of \(^{10}\)He or places a limit on the \(^9\)He scattering length that conflicts with current data.

## Method

The authors performed three-body \(^8\)He+\(n\)+\(n\) continuum calculations using the hyperspherical harmonic method, solving an inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation with a source term to simulate different reaction mechanisms. They varied the depths of the \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave \(^8\)He-\(n\) potentials to explore sensitivity to the \(^9\)He spectrum, using both a narrow Gaussian source (simulating transfer reactions) and a broad source derived from the \(^{11}\)Li wavefunction (simulating proton removal from \(^{11}\)Li). The Feshbach reduction technique was employed to achieve convergence with effective basis sizes up to \(K_{\max}=100\).

## Key numbers

- The \([p_{1/2}]^2\) ground state candidate for \(^{10}\)He is predicted at about 2.0–2.3 MeV when the \(^9\)He \(p_{1/2}\) state is at 2 MeV (using \(V^1_c = -4.5\) MeV).
- The peak in the cross section for populating \(^{10}\)He from \(^{11}\)Li may be shifted to \(\sim 1.2\) MeV due to reaction mechanism effects.
- A low-energy "alternative" ground state with \([s_{1/2}]^2\) structure is predicted at \(E < 250\) keV (relative to the \(^8\)He+\(n\)+\(n\) threshold) when the s-wave scattering length in \(^9\)He satisfies \(a < -5\) fm.
- For a specific case with \(a = -15\) fm, the \([s_{1/2}]^2\) state appears at \(E = 4\) keV with \(\Gamma = 0.7\) keV.
- The width-versus-energy curve for the \([s_{1/2}]^2\) state agrees with the earlier prediction by Aoyama (2002) of \(E = 0.05\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 0.21\) MeV.
- The \(d_{5/2}\) state in \(^9\)He is found at 4.8 MeV, consistent with experimental values in the range 4.2–4.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the three-body \(^8\)He+\(n\)+\(n\) model of Korsheninnikov et al. (1993) by incorporating new data on the \(^9\)He spectrum from Golovkov et al. (2007), which placed the \(p_{1/2}\) state at about 2 MeV rather than the previously reported doublet at 1.27 and 2.4 MeV. The study also revisits the prediction by Aoyama (2002) of a near-threshold \([s_{1/2}]^2\) state in \(^{10}\)He, providing observable consequences for reaction cross sections and demonstrating that the reaction mechanism can significantly shift peak positions. The results challenge the interpretation of existing experimental spectra from Korsheninnikov et al. (1994) and Ostrowski et al. (1994), and suggest that future experiments must carefully distinguish between \([s_{1/2}]^2\) and \([p_{1/2}]^2\) configurations.
