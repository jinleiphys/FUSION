# Heavy and light flavor jet quenching at RHIC and LHC energies

> The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model coupled to hydrodynamical background is extended to include transport of both light partons and heavy quarks through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The LBT model includes both elastic and inelastic medium-interaction of both primary jet shower partons and thermal recoil partons within perturbative QCD (pQCD). It is shown to simultaneously describe the experimental data on heavy and light flavor hadron suppression in high-energy heavy-ion collisions for different centralities at RHIC and LHC energies. More detailed investigations within the LBT model illustrate the importance of both initial parton spectra and the shapes of fragmentation functions on the difference between the nuclear modifications of light and heavy flavor hadrons. The dependence of the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ on medium temperature and jet flavor is quantitatively extracted.

## Key claim

The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, when extended to treat both light partons and heavy quarks with elastic and inelastic interactions on the same footing within perturbative QCD, can simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factors of both light hadrons and D mesons across different centralities at RHIC and LHC energies, but only after applying a K factor for jet transport coefficients at low momentum and using a fixed coupling constant (α_s = 0.15) for vertices connecting to thermal partons.

## Method

The authors coupled a Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNew) for RHIC and 2.76 ATeV LHC, and (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (CLVisc) for 5.02 ATeV LHC, with MC Glauber initial conditions. Jet partons were initialized from leading-order pQCD with CTEQ parton distribution functions and EPS09 nuclear shadowing, then evolved through the QGP medium via Monte Carlo sampling of elastic scattering rates (from leading-order matrix elements with Debye screening) and inelastic gluon radiation rates (from the higher-twist formalism). Hadronization used a hybrid fragmentation-plus-coalescence model for heavy quarks and PYTHIA for light partons.

## Key numbers

- Elastic energy loss rates in a static medium at T=300 MeV: 1.54 GeV/fm for gluon, 0.664 GeV/fm for light quark, 0.668 GeV/fm for charm quark, 0.382 GeV/fm for beauty quark (all at initial parton energy 30 GeV)
- Fixed strong coupling constant α_s = 0.15 used for vertices connecting to thermal partons
- QGP formation time τ_0 = 0.6 fm and shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio η/s = 0.08 used in hydrodynamics
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.27 GeV and beauty quark mass m_b = 4.19 GeV
- K factor parameters for low-momentum transport: A_p = 5 and σ_p = 5 GeV
- Debye screening mass μ_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (N_c + N_f/2)/3
- QCD scale Λ = 0.2 GeV for running coupling
- Freeze-out temperature T_c = 165 MeV

## Context

This work extends the LBT model previously developed by the same group (Cao 2016) to include both light and heavy flavor partons simultaneously, and provides a unified description of jet quenching across flavors. It builds on the systematic extraction of the jet quenching parameter by the JET Collaboration (Burke 2013) and addresses the puzzle that D meson and light hadron R_AA values are comparable at RHIC and LHC despite expectations from color and mass dependence of energy loss. The results enable future studies of flavor-dependent jet transport coefficients and their temperature dependence in heavy-ion collisions.
