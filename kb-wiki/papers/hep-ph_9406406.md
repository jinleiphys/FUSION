# Solvability, Consistency and the Renormalization Group in Large-$N_c$ Models of Hadrons

> We establish the following fundamentals about Lagrangian models of meson-baryon interactions in the large-$N_{c}$ limit: 1. Calculating the leading-order contribution to $1$-meson/$2$-baryon Green's functions in the $1/N_c$ expansion involves summing an infinite class of divergent Feynman diagrams. So long as the bare Lagrangian properly obeys all large-$N_c$ selection rules, this all-loops resummation is accomplished exactly by solving coupled classical field equations with a short-distance cutoff. 2. The only effect of the resummation is to renormalize the bare Yukawa couplings, baryon masses and hyperfine baryon mass splittings of the model. 3. In the process, the large-$N_{c}$ renormalization group flow of these bare parameters is completely determined. We conjecture that variants of the Skyrme model emerge as UV fixed points of such flows.

# Solvability, Consistency and the Renormalization Group in Large-$N_c$ Models of Hadrons

## Key claim

The paper establishes that in large-$N_c$ meson-baryon Lagrangian models, the infinite sum of divergent Feynman diagrams contributing at leading order to 1-meson/2-baryon Green's functions can be solved exactly via coupled classical field equations with a short-distance cutoff, provided the bare Lagrangian obeys all large-$N_c$ selection rules. The authors conjecture that variants of the Skyrme model emerge as UV fixed points of the resulting renormalization group flow, but only for a meson parameter space of measure zero, and with the caveat that the continuum limit $\Lambda\rightarrow\infty$ must be taken only after $N_c\rightarrow\infty$, as these limits likely do not commute.

## Method

The authors study generic 2-flavor relativistic hadron Lagrangians conserving C, P, T, and isospin, restricted by five large-$N_c$ consistency conditions: $n$-meson and $n$-meson/2-baryon vertices scale as $N_c^{1-n/2}$, the baryon spectrum consists of an infinite $I=J$ tower degenerate at leading order, hyperfine splittings follow $J(J+1)/2{\cal I}_{\rm bare}$, Yukawa couplings obey the proportionality rule, and meson-baryon couplings obey the $I_t=J_t$ rule. They sum all leading-order Feynman diagrams by switching to the $SU(2)$ collective coordinate basis $|A\rangle$ for the baryon tower, where the Yukawa vertex becomes diagonal, and reducing the problem to solving classical Euler-Lagrange equations for the meson fields with a smeared $\delta$-function baryon source. The renormalized on-shell couplings, masses, and hyperfine splittings are extracted from the asymptotic behavior of the classical hedgehog solution and from evaluating the effective action on the classical configuration.

## Key numbers

- Baryon masses scale as $M_{\rm bare} \sim N_c$ and Yukawa couplings scale as $\sqrt{N_c}$ at leading order.
- The renormalized pion-nucleon coupling constant is given by $g_{\pi}^{\rm ren} = (2/3)\pi f_{\pi}{\cal A}$, where ${\cal A}$ is extracted numerically from the asymptotic form $F(r) \rightarrow {\cal A}(m_{\pi}/r + 1/r^2)e^{-m_{\pi}r}$ of the hedgehog profile.
- Hyperfine baryon mass splittings have the form $J(J+1)/2{\cal I}_{\rm bare}$ with ${\cal I}_{\rm bare} \sim N_c$, preserved under renormalization as $M_{\rm ren}(J) = M_{\rm ren} + J(J+1)/2{\cal I}_{\rm ren}$.
- The ratio $g_{\pi N\Delta}/g_{\pi NN} = 3/2$ is given as an example of the proportionality rule.

## Context

This work extends the summation methods of A. Manohar (1994) to show that large-$N_c$ meson-baryon models are solvable at leading order and that the large-$N_c$ selection rules derived from planar graphs (E. Witten, 1979; G. 't Hooft, 1974), the Skyrme model (Adkins, Nappi, Witten, 1983), the nonrelativistic quark model (R. Dashen, A. Manohar, 1993), and diagrammatic self-consistency (Gervais, Sakita, 1984; M. Mattis, M. Peskin, 1985) are all consistent with effective hadron Lagrangians. The resulting picture of a hard-core baryon dressed by tree-level meson graphs (termed "hard-core Born-ography") is reminiscent of chiral bag models but follows from large-$N_c$ alone without chiral symmetry assumptions. The renormalization group flow analysis opens the possibility of connecting explicit baryon-field models to Skyrme-type soliton models as UV fixed points, with numerical work on the RG flows described as in progress.
