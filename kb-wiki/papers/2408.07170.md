# TMD phenomenology motivated by nonperturbative structures

> This talk summarized work done recently to organize the steps for implementing TMD phenomenology in a way optimized for contexts where the extraction and interpretation of hadronic structures and nonperturbative effects is the primary driving motivation.

## Key claim

The standard bstar/g-function method for organizing TMD phenomenology, while formally exact, introduces practical complications when the primary goal is extracting nonperturbative hadron structure: the bstar function and bmax parameter conflate two physically distinct transition scales, and arbitrarily chosen nonperturbative g-functions can contaminate the perturbative parts, undermining the claimed separation.

## Method

The authors propose a "hadron structure oriented" (HSO) approach that constructs a smooth, continuous parametrization of the TMD pdf in transverse coordinate space that directly interpolates between the perturbative small-b region and the nonperturbative large-b region, with the constant term fixed by imposing the generalized number-density integral relation that connects TMD pdfs to collinear pdfs. The Collins-Soper kernel is treated analogously, and evolution to higher Q is performed using the standard evolution equations. The resulting parametrization can then be transformed into the bstar/g-function form if desired, but now the g-functions automatically satisfy the constraints that the standard approach lacks.

## Key numbers

- bmax is understood to satisfy bmax less than or approximately equal to 1/Q0, where Q0 is the input scale for TMD factorization
- bmin is set to b0/mu_Q0, where b0 = 2 e^{-gamma_E}, to eliminate logarithmic terms in the integral constraint
- The nonperturbative mass parameters m_a and m_b in the example parametrization are expected to be comparable in size to Lambda_QCD
- The power-suppressed errors in the OPE expression are of order (bmax * Lambda_QCD)^a with a > 0, and can be made arbitrarily small by choosing small enough bmax

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous papers Gonzalez-Hernandez (2022), Gonzalez-Hernandez (2023), and Aslan (2024), which introduced the HSO approach. It addresses longstanding issues with the bstar method originally developed by Collins (1981, 1984), where nonperturbative model dependence can affect perturbative calculations; prior work by Qiu (2000) and Grewal (2020) dealt with this by imposing continuity conditions at the perturbative-nonperturbative boundary. The proposed approach enables cleaner extraction of nonperturbative transverse momentum dependence from data by ensuring that the g-functions are constrained by the generalized number-density integral relation, preserving the hadron structure interpretation embodied in the classic "prism" diagram of Lorce (2011).
