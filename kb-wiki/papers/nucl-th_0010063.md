# Poissonian reducibility and thermal scaling within the lattice gas model and molecular dynamics model

> The emission of clusters in the nuclear disassembly is investigated within the framework of isospin dependent lattice gas model and classical molecular dynamics model. As observed in the recent experimental data, it is found that the emission of individual cluster is poissonian and thermal scaling is observed in the linear Arrhenius plots made from the average multiplicity of each cluster. The mass, isotope and charge dependent "emission barriers" are extracted from the slopes of the Arrhenius plots and their possible physical implications are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Poissonian reducibility and thermal scaling of individual fragment emission, previously observed in experimental data where temperature was assumed proportional to the square root of transverse energy, also hold in the isospin-dependent lattice gas model (I-LGM) and classical molecular dynamics (I-CMD) when the true temperature is used directly, but only on the low-temperature side of the Arrhenius plots.

## Method

The author simulated nuclear disassembly using an isospin-dependent lattice gas model (I-LGM) on a cubic lattice at a fixed freeze-out density of about 0.38 rho_0, and an isospin-dependent classical molecular dynamics model (I-CMD) with a repulsive core potential for like nucleons and an attractive potential for unlike nucleons. For each temperature, 1000 events were generated for the medium-size nucleus 129Xe, and the cluster multiplicity distributions for individual mass (A), isotope (ISO), and charge (Z) values were analyzed for Poissonian behavior and for thermal scaling via Arrhenius plots of ln(mean multiplicity) versus 1/T.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out density used in most calculations was 0.38 rho_0, corresponding to a 7^3 cubic lattice for 129Xe, a 6^3 lattice for A_sys = 80, and a 9^3 lattice for A_sys = 274.
- The interaction constant for proton-neutron pairs was epsilon_pn = -5.33 MeV; like-nucleon interactions were set to 0 MeV.
- The ratio sigma_i^2 / <n_i> for clusters with Z >= 2 was close to 1 across the temperature range, confirming Poissonian behavior; protons were the exception.
- The emission barrier for clusters without Coulomb force was fitted by B = c1 * A_i^(2/3) with c1 = 8.469, and by B = c1 * ((A/Z)_fit * Z_i)^(2/3) with (A/Z)_fit = 1.866.
- For I-CMD with Coulomb, the barrier fit used c2 = 12.921 and a fitted residue charge Z_res ~ 41, with r_Coul = 1.22 fm.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Moretto, Beaulieu, Phair, and others (late 1990s) who observed binomial and Poissonian reducibility and thermal scaling in multifragmentation data from heavy-ion collisions, where temperature was inferred from transverse energy. It also complements the theoretical work of Elliott and Moretto (1999) who found similar features in percolation and the Fisher droplet model. The results enable a systematic study of fragment-dependent emission barriers and their dependence on surface energy and Coulomb interaction, and show that the lattice gas model and molecular dynamics without Coulomb produce barriers that scale as Z^(2/3) and are independent of source size and freeze-out density.
