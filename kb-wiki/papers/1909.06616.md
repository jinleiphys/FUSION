# Symmetry energy of super-dense neutron-rich matter from integrating barotropic pressures in neutron stars and heavy-ion reactions

> Within the minimum model of neutron stars (NS) consisting of neutrons, protons and electrons, a new approach is proposed for inferring the symmetry energy of super-dense neutron-rich nucleonic matter above twice the saturation density $ρ_0$ of nuclear matter directly from integrating iteratively barotropic pressures in both neutron stars and heavy-ion reactions. Simultaneously, the proton fraction of NSs at $β$ equilibrium is extracted as a function of baryon density from the same procedure. An application of this approach using the NS pressure from GW170817 and the pressure in cold symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) extracted earlier by analyzing nuclear collective flow data in relativistic heavy-ion collisions provides a useful constraining band for the symmetry energy above $2ρ_0$.

# Symmetry energy of super-dense neutron-rich matter from integrating barotropic pressures in neutron stars and heavy-ion reactions

## Key claim
Within the minimum model of neutron stars consisting of neutrons, protons and electrons, the symmetry energy above twice saturation density can be inferred directly by iteratively integrating the barotropic pressures from neutron stars and heavy-ion reactions, without assuming neutron stars are made of pure neutron matter. The approach simultaneously extracts the proton fraction of neutron stars at beta equilibrium, but the results are limited by the caveat that muons and other particles are not included.

## Method
The authors derive an integral relation connecting the symmetry energy to the difference between the pressure in pure neutron matter and the pressure in symmetric nuclear matter. They then construct an iterative procedure: starting from the assumption that neutron stars are pure neutron matter (proton fraction zero), they compute the symmetry energy via the integral, use that symmetry energy to calculate a new proton fraction from the beta equilibrium condition, and then use that proton fraction to correct the inferred pure neutron matter pressure from the observed neutron star pressure. This cycle repeats until the symmetry energy and proton fraction converge. The inputs are the neutron star pressure from GW170817 (LIGO & VIRGO Collaborations, 2018) and the symmetric nuclear matter pressure from transport model analyses of nuclear collective flow (Danielewicz et al., 2002) at beam energies from about 0.4 to 10 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- The integration starts at $2\rho_0$ using a reference symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0)=55.7$ MeV, taken as the central value from the ASY-EOS band (Russotto et al., 2011; ASY-EOS Collaboration).
- The proton fraction at $2.5\rho_0$ is at least about 10% in neutron stars at beta equilibrium along the lower boundary.
- The symmetry energy at twice saturation density from Bayesian analysis of canonical neutron star radii is $E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2\rho_0)=39.2_{-8.2}^{+12.1}$ MeV at 68% confidence level (Xie and Li, 2019).
- The neutron star pressure from GW170817 is at 90% confidence level; the symmetric nuclear matter pressure from heavy-ion reactions is given as absolute (100%) upper and lower boundaries.

## Context
This work extends efforts to constrain the high-density symmetry energy, which is the most uncertain aspect of the equation of state of dense neutron-rich matter besides possible phase transitions (Li et al., 2008; Steiner et al., 2005; Lattimer and Prakash, 2012; Tsang et al., 2012; Horowitz et al., 2014; Baldo and Burgio, 2016; Oertel et al., 2017; Chen, 2017; Li, 2017; Blaschke and Chamel, 2018; Baiotti and Rezzolla, 2017). Prior approaches using GW170817 to constrain symmetry energy (Most et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2019; Tsang et al., 2019) often assumed neutron stars are pure neutron matter, which is intrinsically inconsistent with extracting the symmetry energy that determines the proton fraction. The present approach enables simultaneous extraction of both symmetry energy and proton fraction, and the resulting constraining band overlaps with that from Bayesian inference using canonical neutron star radii (Xie and Li, 2019). Future work should include muons and other particles beyond the minimum $npe$ model, and narrow the error bands of both the astrophysical and terrestrial pressure inputs.
