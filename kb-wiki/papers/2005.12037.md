# Finite size effect on Dissociation and Diffusion of chiral partners in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> Along with masses of pion and sigma meson modes, their dissociation into quark medium provide a detail spectral structures of the chiral partners. Present article has studied a finite size effect on that detail structure of chiral partners by using the framework of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Through this dissociation mechanism, their diffusions and conductions are also studied. The masses, widths, diffusion coefficients, conductivities of chiral partners are merged at different temperatures in restore phase of chiral symmetry, but merging points of all are shifted in lower temperature, when one introduce finite size effect into the picture. The strengths of diffusions and conductions are also reduced due to finite size consideration.

# Finite size effect on Dissociation and Diffusion of chiral partners in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

## Key claim

The merging temperatures of masses, decay widths, diffusion coefficients, and conductivities for pion and sigma chiral partners are all shifted to lower values when a finite system size (down to 2 fm) is introduced, compared to the infinite-volume case. The strengths of diffusion and conduction are also reduced due to finite size consideration, with the effect becoming significant for system sizes below approximately 4 fm.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a finite volume implemented by imposing a lower momentum cutoff p_min = pi/R, where R is the linear dimension of a cubical volume, treating the system size as a parameter alongside temperature. Meson masses are extracted from the pole of the meson propagator within the random-phase-approximation (RPA), and decay widths for the pi -> Q anti-Q and sigma -> Q anti-Q dissociation channels are obtained from the imaginary part of the one-loop polarization function. The drag coefficients are equated to these decay widths, and the diffusion coefficients and conductivities are then calculated from the drag coefficients using the Einstein relation and the static susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- For R = infinity, the transition temperature is 184 MeV and the Mott temperature is 200 MeV; for R = 2 fm, the transition temperature shifts to 166 MeV and the Mott temperature shifts to 153 MeV.
- For R = infinity, the mass doublet merging temperature is 245 MeV; for R = 2 fm, the mass doublet merging temperature shifts to 223 MeV.
- For R = infinity, the drag coefficient doublet merging temperature is 234 MeV; for R = 2 fm, the drag coefficient doublet merging temperature shifts to 188 MeV.
- For R = infinity, the diffusion coefficient doublet merging temperature is 245 MeV; for R = 2 fm, the diffusion coefficient doublet merging temperature shifts to 195 MeV.
- For R = infinity, the conductivity doublet merging temperature is 236 MeV; for R = 2 fm, the conductivity doublet merging temperature shifts to 193 MeV.
- The model parameters used are: current quark mass m_u = 5.5 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 0.651 GeV, coupling g_S Lambda^2 = 5.04, chiral condensate |<bar-psi_u psi_u>|^(1/3) = 251 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 92.3 MeV, and pion mass m_pi = 139.3 MeV.
- The Mott temperature for R = 2 fm is 153 MeV, which is lower than the transition temperature of 166 MeV; for R = infinity, the Mott temperature of 200 MeV is higher than the transition temperature of 184 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-size studies within the NJL model framework, following the recent work of Ref. (NJLV_2018) on finite-size effects on meson masses, by providing a more complete spectral analysis including dissociation, diffusion, and conduction of chiral partners. The finite volume range considered (50-250 fm^3 from UrQMD simulations to 2000-3000 fm^3 from HBT measurements at RHIC) is motivated by heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC. The results enable future comparisons with other effective models such as the quark-meson model (Braun et al., Fraga et al.) and Polyakov loop extended NJL (Deb et al., Sur et al., Kinkar et al.) for understanding how finite-size effects modify the chiral phase transition and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
