# A tale of two skyrmions: the nucleon's strange quark content in different large N_c limits

> The nucleon's strange quark content comes from closed quark loops, and hence should vanish at leading order in the traditional large $N_c$ (TLNC) limit. Quark loops are not suppressed in the recently proposed orientifold large $N_c$ (OLNC) limit, and thus the strange quark content should be non-vanishing at leading order. The Skyrme model is supposed to encode the large $N_c$ behavior of baryons, and can be formulated for both of these large $N_c$ limits. There is an apparent paradox associated with the large $N_c$ behavior of strange quark matrix elements in the Skyrme model. The model only distinguishes between the two large $N_c$ limits via the $N_c$ scaling of the couplings and the Witten-Wess-Zumino term, so that a vanishing leading order strange matrix element in the TLNC limit implies that it also vanishes at leading order in the OLNC limit, contrary to the expectations based on the suppression/non-suppression of quark loops. The resolution of this paradox is that the Skyrme model does not include the most general type of meson-meson interaction and, in fact, contains no meson-meson interactions which vanish for the TLNC limit but not the OLNC. The inclusion of such terms in the model yields the expected scaling for strange quark matrix elements.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model's apparent paradox, that strange quark matrix elements vanish at leading order in both the traditional large N_c (TLNC) and orientifold large N_c (OLNC) limits, despite quark loops being unsuppressed in the OLNC limit, is resolved by noting that the standard Skyrme model omits meson-meson interaction terms with multiple flavor traces; including such terms yields the expected leading-order scaling of N_c^2 for strange matrix elements in the OLNC limit.

## Method

The authors analyze the N_c scaling of the nucleon's strange quark content in the Skyrme model for both the TLNC and OLNC limits, using collective quantization of SU(3) hedgehog Skyrmions. They compute the strange scalar matrix element fraction X_s for arbitrary N_c in the exact SU(3) flavor limit, and then introduce an additional term S' from chiral perturbation theory (proportional to L_4) to illustrate how meson-meson interactions with multiple flavor traces produce the expected scaling difference between the two limits.

## Key numbers

- X_s = 2(N_c + 4)/(N_c^2 + 10 N_c + 21) for the TLNC limit in the standard Skyrme model, which scales as 2/N_c + O(1/N_c^2) at large N_c.
- For N_c = 3, X_s = 7/30 in the exact SU(3) limit, matching earlier phenomenological calculations.
- The additional term S' contributes σ_s' ∼ N_c^0 in the TLNC limit and σ_s' ∼ N_c^2 in the OLNC limit, as shown in Eq. (sigma').
- Meson vertices scale as Γ_n ∼ N_c^{2-n} for OLNC and Γ_n ∼ N_c^{1-n/2} for TLNC, with the conversion rule N_c^k → N_c^{2k}.
- The Skyrmion mass scales as M ∼ N_c^2 in the OLNC limit.

## Context

This work resolves a paradox in the large N_c behavior of strange quark matrix elements in Skyrme models, extending earlier studies of the OLNC limit by Armoni, Shifman, and Veneziano (2003) and Bolognesi (2005). The analysis shows that the standard Skyrme model, while capturing generic N_c scaling and the contracted SU(2N_f) symmetry derived by Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994), does not include the most general meson-meson interactions; terms with multiple flavor traces, known from chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984), are needed to reproduce the expected quark-loop-driven scaling. This enables future Skyrme-type models to correctly incorporate the distinction between TLNC and OLNC limits for observables sensitive to closed quark loops.
