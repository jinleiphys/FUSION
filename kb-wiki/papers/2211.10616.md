# Effect of the $σ$-cut potential on the properties of neutron stars with or without a hyperonic core

> Motivated by the recent observation of high-mass pulsars ($M \simeq 2 M_{\odot}$), we employ the $σ$-cut potential on the equation of state (EOS) of high-density matter and the properties of neutron stars within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using TM1$^{*}$ parameter set. The $σ$-cut potential is known to reduce the contributions of the $σ$ field, resulting in a stiffer EOS at high densities and hence leading to larger neutron star masses without affecting the properties of nuclear matter at normal saturation density. We also analyzed the effect of the same on pure neutron matter and also on the neutron star matter with and without hyperonic core and compared it with the available theoretical, experimental, and observational data. The corresponding tidal deformability ($Λ_{1.4}$) is also calculated. With the choice of meson-hyperon coupling fixed to hypernuclear potentials, we obtain $\approx 10~\%$ increase in mass by employing the $σ$-cut potential for $f_{s} = 0.6$. Our results are in good agreement with various experimental constraints and observational data, particularly with the GW170817 data.

## Key claim

The sigma-cut potential, applied within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using the TM1* parameter set, produces a stiffer equation of state (EOS) at high densities and increases the maximum neutron star mass by approximately 10% for a cutoff parameter f_s = 0.6, but this effect is conditional on the choice of meson-hyperon coupling constants and is most significant when hyperons are included in the core.

## Method

The authors implemented the sigma-cut potential, a logarithmic term that reduces the scalar sigma-field contribution at high density, within the RMF Lagrangian using the TM1* parameter set. They solved the field equations for sigma, omega, and rho mesons and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for both pure nucleonic matter and matter including the full baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Sigma, Xi). Meson-hyperon couplings were fixed by reproducing hyperon potential depths at saturation density: -28 MeV for Lambda, +30 MeV for Sigma, and -18 MeV for Xi, with scalar coupling x_sigmaH taken as 0.6 and 0.7.

## Key numbers

*   Maximum mass for pure nucleonic matter (npeμ) with TM1*: 2.02 solar masses; with sigma-cut (f_s=0.6): 2.08 solar masses.
*   Maximum mass for hyperonic matter (npeμ+H) with x_sigmaH=0.6: 1.59 solar masses; with sigma-cut (f_s=0.6): 1.74 solar masses (approximately 10% increase).
*   Maximum mass for hyperonic matter (npeμ+H) with x_sigmaH=0.7: 1.69 solar masses; with sigma-cut (f_s=0.6): 1.86 solar masses.
*   Tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses (Lambda_1.4) for pure nucleonic matter: 845.30; with sigma-cut: 860.81.
*   Tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses (Lambda_1.4) for hyperonic matter with x_sigmaH=0.6: 841.74; with sigma-cut: 851.92.
*   Tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses (Lambda_1.4) for hyperonic matter with x_sigmaH=0.7: 831.66; with sigma-cut: 860.81.
*   Radius at 1.4 solar masses (R_1.4) for all TM1* models: approximately 15.1 to 15.2 km.
*   The sigma-cut EOS becomes stiffer than the original TM1* EOS at a number density of 0.28 fm^{-3} for symmetric nuclear matter and at 0.32 fm^{-3} for pure neutron matter.
*   Lambda hyperons appear at approximately 0.34 fm^{-3} for both x_sigmaH values with the sigma-cut potential.

## Context

This work extends the sigma-cut scheme, previously applied to the TM1 parameter set by Zhang (2018) and to kaon condensates by Ma (2022), to the TM1* parameter set which includes additional cross-couplings that soften the EOS at high densities. The results are compared with microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) EOSs (Bonn B, Nijmegen 93, Argonne V18, Urbana UIX, DBHF, APR) and the phenomenological SFHO EOS. The calculated masses and tidal deformabilities are consistent with observational constraints from GW170817, NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the high-mass pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and the GW190814 event. The work enables further exploration of how the sigma-cut mechanism can reconcile the need for stiff EOSs to support 2-solar-mass neutron stars with the softening effects of hyperonic degrees of freedom.
