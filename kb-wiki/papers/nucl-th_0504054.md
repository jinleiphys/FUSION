# The effect of quark exchange in A=3 mirror nuclei and neutron/proton structure functions ratio

> By using the quark-exchange formalism, realistic Faddeev wave functions and Fermi motion effect, we investigate deep inelastic electron scattering from A=3 mirror nuclei in the deep-valence region. The initial valence quarks in put are taken from the GRV's next-to-leading order calculations on $F_2^p(x,Q^2)$ which give very good fit to the available data in the $(x,Q^2)$-plane. It is shown that the free neutron to proton structure functions ratio can be extracted from corresponding EMC ratios for $^3He$ and $^3H$ mirror nuclei by using self-consistent iteration procedure and the results are in good agreement with other theoretical models as well as present available experimental data, especially the one expected form the proposed 11 GeV Jefferson Laboratory.

# The effect of quark exchange in A=3 mirror nuclei and neutron/proton structure functions ratio

## Key claim

The free neutron to proton structure function ratio can be extracted from the EMC ratios of helium-3 and tritium using a self-consistent iteration procedure, with results that agree with other theoretical models and available experimental data, provided the constraint that the ratio of the EMC ratios for the two mirror nuclei is approximately unity (between 0.99 and 1.01) and the nucleon size parameter b does not significantly affect the outcome.

## Method

The authors combined the quark-exchange formalism with realistic Faddeev wave functions (using the Reid soft-core potential) and Fermi motion effects (treated via a convolution approach with harmonic oscillator basis) to calculate the valence quark momentum distributions and structure functions of helium-3 and tritium. The initial valence quark inputs were taken from GRV next-to-leading order parton distributions, with parameters (quark mass m and binding energy epsilon_0) fitted to reproduce the GRV proton valence quark distributions at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2. The neutron to proton structure function ratio was then obtained by solving coupled equations relating the EMC ratios of the two mirror nuclei through a self-consistent iteration procedure, imposing the constraint that the ratio of the helium-3 to tritium EMC ratios is approximately 1.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass m = 165 MeV and binding energy epsilon_0 = 150 MeV (or 250 MeV) used at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, with nucleon size parameter b = 0.8 fm giving the best fit to GRV valence quark distributions.
- RMS radius of 1.95 fm for helium-3 and 1.7 fm for tritium used in the harmonic oscillator Fermi motion calculation.
- The iteration procedure converges after the third iteration, with the constraint R_EMC^(3He/3H)(x,Q^2) set to 1.01.
- The ratio F_2^n/F_2^p at x approaching 1 is compared to the SU(6) symmetry prediction of 2/3, the phenomenological/Regge prediction of 1/4, and the perturbative QCD/quark counting rule prediction of 3/7.
- The EMC ratios for helium-3 and tritium were compared to HERMES helium-3 data at Q^2 approximately 7 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the quark-exchange formalism originally introduced by Hoodbhoy and Jaffe (1987) and previously applied to light nuclei and nuclear matter by Modarres (1994) and to three-nucleon systems by Yazdanpanah and Modarres (1998). It addresses the longstanding difficulty of extracting the free neutron structure function from deuterium data, where nuclear corrections can introduce uncertainties as large as 50% in the d/u or F_2^n/F_2^p ratios, as noted by West (1971). The calculation is motivated by the proposed 11 GeV Jefferson Laboratory experiment using unpolarized tritium targets, as discussed by Afnan et al. (2000), and the results are compared with other theoretical approaches including those of Melnitchouk and Thomas (1996), Bodek et al. (1979), and Whitlow et al. (1992). The work enables improved extraction of the neutron structure function at large x (x greater than 0.7) and can be extended by including three-body connected diagrams, nucleon-nucleon correlations, and improved treatment of Fermi motion within the quark-exchange framework.
