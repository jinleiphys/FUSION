# Susceptibilities of strongly interacting matter in a finite volume

> We investigate possible finite-volume effects on baryon number susceptibilities of strongly interacting matter. Assuming that a hadronic and a deconfined phase both contribute to the thermodynamic state of a finite system due to fluctuations, it is found that the resulting shapes of the net-baryon number distributions deviate significantly from the infinite volume limit for a given temperature $T$ and baryochemical potential $μ_B$. In particular, the constraint on color-singletness for the finite quark-gluon phase contribution leads to a change of the temperature dependence of the susceptibilities in finite volumes. According to the model, the finite-volume effect depends qualitatively on the value of $μ_B$.

## Key claim

Finite-volume effects on baryon number susceptibilities arise from two competing mechanisms: fluctuations of the phase composition (which soften the transition) and the color-singlet constraint on the quark-gluon plasma phase (which shifts the effective critical temperature and sharpens the transition); the net effect depends qualitatively on the value of the baryochemical potential \(\mu_B\).

## Method

The authors extend a schematic two-phase model (Spieles 1998) that assumes a first-order phase transition between a hadronic phase (modeled as a relativistic ideal quantum gas of non-strange hadron resonances with eigenvolume corrections) and a quark-gluon plasma phase (modeled as a relativistic ideal quantum gas of two massless quark flavors and gluons confined in an MIT bag). For a finite total volume \(V\), the probability of each macroscopic configuration (partial volumes of the two phases) is given by the Boltzmann factor of the grand canonical potential, and all intensive thermodynamic quantities are computed as expectation values over these configurations. The color-singlet constraint is implemented via a group-theoretical projection method (Elze 1986) that projects onto color-singlet states and includes finite-size shell corrections.

## Key numbers

*   At \(\mu_B = 0\) and \(V = 1000\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) (typical for one unit of rapidity at RHIC), the effective critical temperature is shifted by only \(\Delta T_C^{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) relative to the infinite-volume limit.
*   For \(V = 100\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) at \(\mu_B = 0\), the susceptibility ratio \(\chi^B_4/\chi^B_2\) reflects a "superheated" hadron gas in the temperature range \(155\ \mathrm{MeV} < T < 165\ \mathrm{MeV}\); for \(V = 50\ \mathrm{fm}^3\), this region extends to \(T \approx 175\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   The infinite-volume critical temperature at \(\mu_B = 0\) is \(T_C^{\infty} \approx 155\ \mathrm{MeV}\); at \(\mu_B = 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\) it is lowered to \(T_C^{\infty} \approx 147\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   The bag constant is fixed to \(B^{1/4} = 215\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to match the chiral transition temperature from lattice QCD.
*   Both model calculations (with and without color-singlet constraint) approach the value \(2/9\) for \(\chi^B_2\) at high temperature, expected for an ideal gas of massless quarks and gluons with two quark flavors.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume model of Spieles (1998) to baryon number susceptibilities, addressing a gap between lattice QCD calculations (which generally assume infinite volume) and heavy-ion experiments (which probe finite volumes). The results contradict the naive expectation that ratios like \(\chi^B_4/\chi^B_2\) cancel volume dependence; instead, the effective equation of state in a finite volume differs from the infinite-matter case, complicating direct comparisons with lattice QCD results (Bazavov 2017, Guenther 2018). The study enables future theoretical work to incorporate the \(T\)-\(\mu\)-\(V\) space rather than the classic \(T\)-\(\mu\) plane when interpreting experimental observables from the RHIC beam energy scan (Luo 2017).
