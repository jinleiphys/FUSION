# Transverse Momentum Dependent PDFs in Chiral Effective Theory

> We develop a theoretical framework to match transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs) onto chiral effective theory operators. In this framework the TMD PDF is expressed as a convolution of TMD hadronic distribution functions, which describe fluctuations of initial states into intermediate hadrons in chiral perturbation theory, and short distance matching coefficients, which are the TMD PDFs of intermediate hadrons in the chiral limit. The various limits of the matching condition are explored and an operator product expansion is applied to the high energy TMD matching coefficients, allowing them to be written in terms of the collinear valence PDFs of intermediate hadrons. As an example, we calculate the isovector TMD hadronic distribution functions for the proton at leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim

The paper develops a theoretical framework to match transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs) onto chiral effective theory operators, but the matching condition for TMDs is presented as a conjecture rather than a rigorous derivation, because unlike collinear PDFs, TMD PDFs lack a moment expansion in terms of local operators that would allow a systematic matching procedure.

## Method

The authors generalize the collinear convolution formalism of Chen and Ji (2001), which expresses collinear PDFs as convolutions of short-distance matching coefficients (valence PDFs of intermediate hadrons in the chiral limit) with hadronic distribution functions from chiral perturbation theory, to the TMD case by introducing transverse separation into both the matching coefficients and the chiral operators. They construct chirally invariant nonlocal operators for pions and nucleons with both light-like and transverse separation, and calculate the isovector TMD hadronic distribution functions for the proton at leading order in the chiral expansion. The matching coefficients are identified with the TMD PDFs of intermediate hadrons in the chiral limit, and an operator product expansion is applied to write the high-energy TMD matching coefficients in terms of collinear valence PDFs.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is given as Lambda_chi ~ 1-2 GeV, while Lambda_QCD ~ 200 MeV, establishing the scale separation used to argue that at small transverse momentum (k_T ~ m_pi ~ Lambda_QCD), the transverse momentum dependence is dominated by the low-energy hadronic fluctuations rather than the matching coefficients.
- The paper calculates the isovector TMD hadronic distribution functions for the proton at leading order in the chiral expansion, but no explicit numerical results for these functions are presented in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory approach previously applied to collinear PDFs by Chen and Ji (2001), Arndt and Savage (2001), and others, and to generalized parton distributions by Moiseeva and Vladimirov (2012), into the domain of TMD PDFs. The only prior study of a TMD PDF in a chiral effective framework was by He, Li, and Vogelsang (2019), who calculated the Sivers function using a nonlocal chiral effective theory with a finite range regulator, whereas this paper uses conventional chiral perturbation theory. The framework enables future calculations of chiral corrections to all eight TMD PDFs in the proton and provides a systematic method to study the low-energy behavior of TMD distributions that can be compared with experimental data and lattice QCD results.
