# Charged hadron spectra and anisotropic flow from the AMPT model with shear viscous transport dynamics simulations at RHIC

> We utilized the AMPT model to simulate the shear viscous transport dynamics of parton matter in Au+Au collisions at a constant specific shear viscosity and varying phase transition temperatures at 200 GeV. The resulting charged hadron spectra and anisotropic flow profiles correspond closely with experimental data. The transverse momentum spectra and longitudinal decorrelations are essentially unaffected by the phase transition temperature. An increase in the phase transition temperature leads to a rise in particle yields at midrapidity, accompanied by a decrease in both elliptic and triangular flows over a range of transverse momenta and pseudorapidities.

# Charged hadron spectra and anisotropic flow from the AMPT model with shear viscous transport dynamics simulations at RHIC

## Key claim

The AMPT model with shear viscous transport dynamics corrected by the Chapman-Enskog method can reproduce charged hadron spectra and anisotropic flow at RHIC with a constant specific shear viscosity $\eta/s=0.08$, but the phase transition temperature $T_{tr}$ has a systematic effect: higher $T_{tr}$ increases particle yields at midrapidity while decreasing both elliptic and triangular flow, whereas transverse momentum spectra and longitudinal decorrelations remain essentially unaffected.

## Method

The authors implemented shear viscous transport dynamics in the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model by using the Chapman-Enskog method to relate the shear viscosity to the partonic scattering cross section and screening mass, enabling parameterized control of $\eta/s$. They simulated Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV with a constant $\eta/s=0.08$ and varied the phase transition temperature, using Gaussian smearing to obtain the spatiotemporal temperature distribution of parton matter and applying the Lund string fragmentation model for hadronization.

## Key numbers

- Specific shear viscosity fixed at $\eta/s=0.08$ (near the KSS bound) for all simulations at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV
- Lund string fragmentation parameters: $a_{L}=0.55$, $b_{L}=0.2$ GeV$^{-2}$; QCD coupling constant $\alpha_{s}=0.33$
- Temperature calculation used cells with width $\Delta x=0.2$ fm, $\Delta y=0.2$ fm, and space-time rapidity $\Delta\eta_{\rm{s}}=0.2$, with time step 0.1 fm/c
- Parton matter assumed massless three-flavor quarks with $g_{B}=52$ degrees of freedom

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT modeling by incorporating shear viscous transport dynamics using the Chapman-Enskog method rather than the Israel-Stewart method, which is better suited for the forward-angle scattering present in the ZPC parton cascade. The results connect to Bayesian parameter estimation studies (Bernhard et al., 2016; Auvinen et al., 2018) that constrain the temperature dependence of shear viscosity at LHC energies, and to prior AMPT studies (Xu and Ko, 2011; Li and Shen, 2018) that examined viscosity effects. The findings enable further investigation of phase transition temperature effects across different collision energies at RHIC and LHC, particularly for understanding how the parton-to-hadron transition influences anisotropic flow development.
