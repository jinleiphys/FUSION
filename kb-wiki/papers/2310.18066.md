# Photon momentum anisotropies from the late stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The photon emission from the late stages of the dynamical evolution of heavy-ion reactions at the highest RHIC and LHC energies is investigated. A comparison between a calculation from hadronic rates from a fluid dynamic evolution down to temperatures of 120 MeV and a full non-equilibrium hadronic transport approach is performed. The photon yields are very similar in both cases while the elliptic flow is slightly smaller in the non-equilibrium scenario. This study is important, since it is crucial to apply the same dynamical evolution model for hadronic and electromagnetic observables.

## Key claim

The photon yields from the late hadronic stage of heavy-ion collisions are very similar whether calculated using equilibrium thermal rates from fluid dynamics or a full non-equilibrium hadronic transport approach, but the elliptic flow is slightly smaller in the non-equilibrium scenario, with differences becoming significant only at very low transverse momenta (below 1 GeV) where no experimental measurements currently exist.

## Method

The authors performed a simplified 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution using the MUSIC code with averaged initial conditions from the T_RENT_O model, switching to hadronic transport (SMASH) at a particlization temperature of 150 MeV. Photon emission from the hadronic stage was calculated in two ways: (1) from the hydrodynamic evolution folded with thermal rates down to temperatures of 140 or 120 MeV, and (2) microscopically within SMASH via mesonic 2↔2 scatterings and meson bremsstrahlung. The comparison was done for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV at a centrality of roughly 10-20% (impact parameter b = 5 fm).

## Key numbers

- Photon yields from non-equilibrium SMASH overlap everywhere with the band from hydrodynamic thermal rates for both Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV; the SMASH spectrum is slightly softer than the equilibrium scenario.
- The elliptic flow from non-equilibrium SMASH and equilibrium MUSIC agree well from a transverse momentum of ~1 GeV upward; at lower transverse momenta the non-equilibrium flow is reduced.
- The equilibrium emission band spans from a lowest temperature of 120 MeV (upper end) to 140 MeV (lower end) for the hydrodynamic calculation.
- The particlization hypersurface was set at a temperature of 150 MeV, with averaged initial conditions obtained at τ = 0.4 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid approach combining viscous hydrodynamics with hadronic transport that has been used successfully for hadronic observables at RHIC and LHC energies (Schenke 2020, Petersen 2014). It directly addresses the challenge posed by PHENIX measurements of direct photon yields and elliptic flow (Adare 2011, 2014, 2015). The study enables future event-by-event hybrid calculations with consistent photon emission from SMASH, including higher-order flow harmonics such as v_3, for comparison with experimental results.
