# Two-Body Triton Photodisintegration and Wigner-SU(4) Symmetry

> We calculate the two-body triton photodisintegration cross section as a function of photon energy to next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) in pionless effective field theory (EFT($π\!\!/$)) and show good agreement with experiment. In addition we calculate the polarization asymmetry $R_C=-0.441(15)$ in cold neutron-deuteron capture to NNLO in EFT($π\!\!/$), in agreement with the experimental value of $R_C=-0.42\pm 0.03$ [M. W. Konijnenberg et al. in Phys. Lett. B 205, 215 (1988)]. We also assess the dependence of $R_C$ on different fits of the two-nucleon magnetic currents. Finally, we consider the impact of Wigner-SU(4) symmetry and demonstrate that starting from the Wigner-SU(4) symmetric limit and including perturbative corrections to the breaking of Wigner-SU(4) symmetry does a good job of describing two-body triton photodisintegration.

# Two-Body Triton Photodisintegration and Wigner-SU(4) Symmetry

## Key claim
The authors calculate the two-body triton photodisintegration cross section to next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) in pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) and show good agreement with experiment, but the Wigner-SU(4) symmetry expansion is applied only to states before the photon emission (the triton vertex function and dibaryon propagators at the bound-state energy), not to the scattering states after the photon, making this a "quasi" Wigner-SU(4) expansion.

## Method
The authors compute the electric dipole (E1) and magnetic dipole (M1) moments for the reaction γ + ³H → n + d using strictly perturbative pionless effective field theory up to NNLO, including two-nucleon magnetic current contact interactions and a three-nucleon magnetic moment counterterm fit to the triton magnetic moment. The E1 and M1 amplitudes are obtained by solving integral equations for the half-off-shell nd scattering amplitude with perturbative corrections from effective range insertions, dibaryon kinetic terms, and three-body forces. For the Wigner-SU(4) analysis, the triton vertex function and dibaryon propagators at the bound-state energy are expanded in powers of the symmetry-breaking parameter δ = (γ_t − γ_s)/2, while the kernel of the integral equation at scattering energies is left unexpanded.

## Key numbers
- Polarization asymmetry in cold neutron-deuteron capture: R_C = −0.441(15) at NNLO in EFT(π̸), compared to the experimental value R_C = −0.42 ± 0.03 from Konijnenberg et al. (1988).
- Triton binding energy used: E_B = −8.481798 MeV.
- Wigner-SU(4) breaking parameter: δ* = (1/2)(1/a^{³S₁} − 1/a^{¹S₀}) ≈ 27 MeV.
- Spin-triplet parameters: γ_t = 45.7025 MeV, Z_t = 1.6908.
- Spin-singlet parameters: γ_s = −7.890 MeV, Z_s = 0.9015.
- Ratio δ_r/ρ = 0.095 ∼ Q², justifying treatment of δ_r as a next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO) correction.

## Context
This work extends the strictly perturbative EFT(π̸) calculation of cold nd capture by Lin and Vanasse (2022) to include the E1 moment and two-body triton photodisintegration, and it provides the first strictly perturbative calculation of γ + ³H → n + d to NNLO. Previous calculations of this process used phenomenological potentials (Efros 1999, Schadow 2000, Skibinski 2002, Yuan 2002, Deltuva 2003) or non-perturbative partial resummation techniques in EFT(π̸) (Sadeghi 2006, 2009). The quasi Wigner-SU(4) expansion builds on the work of Vanasse (2016) on three-nucleon bound states and avoids the complication that low-energy nd scattering states are not well described by the Wigner-SU(4) expansion, enabling a consistent treatment of the E1 moment where the outgoing P-wave states have no three-body force.
