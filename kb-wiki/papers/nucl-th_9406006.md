# Orbital and Spin Magnetic Dipole Strength in a shell model calculation with $ΔN$=$2$ excitations: $^8\mbox{Be}$

> The magnetic dipole strength and energy-weighted strength distribution is calculated in $^8\mbox{Be}$, as well as the separate orbit and spin parts. All $ΔN$=$2$ excitations over and above (and including) the configuration $0s^4$$0p^4$ are included. The interaction has a central, two-body spin-orbit and a tensor part. The energy- independent and energy-weighted {\underline orbital} strength distribution is remarkably insensitive to the presence or absence of the spin-orbit or tensor interaction -not so the spin strength. The energy-weighted strength distribution can be divided into a low enegy and a high energy part. The high energy orbital part is somewhat less but close to the low energy part, in fair agreement with a prediction that they be equal by de Guerra and Zamick and by Nojarov. There is a wide plateau separating the low energy part from the high energy part.

# Orbital and Spin Magnetic Dipole Strength in a shell model calculation with ΔN=2 excitations: ⁸Be

## Key claim

The orbital magnetic dipole strength distribution in ⁸Be is remarkably insensitive to the presence or absence of the spin-orbit or tensor interaction, while the spin strength is highly sensitive; the high-energy orbital energy-weighted strength is 71% of the low-energy part, in fair (not exact) agreement with predictions by de Guerra and Zamick and by Nojarov that they should be equal.

## Method

The authors performed OXBASH shell model calculations for the J=0⁺ T=0 ground state and J=1⁺ T=1 states of ⁸Be, including all ΔN=2 excitations beyond the base 0s⁴0p⁴ configuration. They used a schematic interaction with central, two-body spin-orbit, and tensor parts, varying the spin-orbit and tensor strengths via parameters x and y. They computed B(M1) values for three operator choices: spin+orbit, spin only, and orbit only, using standard g-factors (g_{lπ}=1, g_{lν}=0, g_{sπ}=5.586, g_{sν}=-3.826).

## Key numbers

- For the pure central interaction (x=0, y=0), the dominant orbital state lies at 19.92 MeV with B(M1)=0.61 μ_N², representing 71% of the total orbital strength of 0.87 μ_N².
- With full spin-orbit and tensor interactions (x=1, y=1), the lowest 1⁺ state at 17.96 MeV has orbital B(M1)=0.398 μ_N² and the next state at 20.8 MeV has 0.198 μ_N².
- Summed orbital strengths for (x,y) = (0,0), (0,1), (1,0), (1,1) are respectively 0.75, 0.74, 0.72, and 0.73 μ_N²; corresponding energy-weighted numbers are 20.48, 21.03, 20.05, and 20.56 μ_N² MeV.
- For spin strength with tensor off (y=0): summed strength S=0.12 μ_N² without spin-orbit, tripling to 0.38 μ_N² with spin-orbit; energy-weighted values are 5.3 and 11.6 μ_N² MeV respectively.
- With spin-orbit off (x=0): turning on tensor changes spin S from 0.12 to 0.40 μ_N² and energy-weighted from 5.3 to 21.3 μ_N² MeV.
- The low-lying orbital energy-weighted strength is about 12 μ_N² MeV; the total is 20.56 μ_N² MeV, giving high-lying strength as 71% of low-lying.

## Context

This work extends shell model studies of scissors mode excitations in light deformed nuclei, following earlier theoretical work on N=Z nuclei by Chaves and Poves (1990) for ²⁰Ne. The results test predictions by de Guerra and Zamick (1990) and Nojarov (1990) that high-lying and low-lying energy-weighted orbital strengths should be equal, and contradict the claim by Hamamoto and Nazarewicz (1990) that the high-lying strength should be much larger. The findings enable better understanding of orbital M1 sum rules in strongly deformed systems where complete ΔN=2 calculations are not possible for heavy nuclei.
