# Decoherence and energy loss in QCD cascades in nuclear collisions

> The medium modifications in the properties of QCD cascades are considered. In particular, the changes in the intrajet rapidity distributions due to medium-induced decoherence, collisional losses of cascade gluons and those of final prehadrons are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that medium-induced color decoherence, which destroys angular ordering for decays occurring inside the hot dense fireball, causes a substantial softening of intrajet rapidity distributions even in the absence of any energy loss; this effect is additional to and distinct from the more commonly considered radiative and collisional energy loss mechanisms.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of mass-ordered gluon cascades using a modified version of the JEWEL framework, implementing a 'global constrained' prescription based on PYTHIA. They modeled the spatiotemporal pattern of the cascade using parton lifetimes derived from energy and virtuality, and introduced three medium modifications: (1) decoherence by removing angular ordering constraints for decays inside the medium, (2) collisional energy loss of cascade gluons described by a Gaussian distribution with mean loss per unit length, and (3) collisional energy loss of final prehadrons proportional to their path length through the medium. The simulations tracked the rapidity distributions, multiplicities, and stopping probabilities of final prehadrons as functions of the distance L from the cascade origin to the medium-vacuum interface.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon energy \(E_0 = 100\) GeV with initial invariant mass \(Q_0 = 10\) GeV; the average decay time of such a gluon is \(\tau_0 \sim E_0/Q_0^2 = 1\;\mathrm{GeV}^{-1} \simeq 0.2\) fm.
- For collisional losses, the mean energy loss per unit length was set to \(\mu_{\Delta E} = 1\;\mathrm{GeV/fm}\) with \(\sigma^2_{\Delta E} = 1\;\mathrm{GeV}^2/\mathrm{fm}\).
- At \(L = 5\) fm, more than half of the final prehadrons are stopped inside the fireball when both parton and prehadron collisional losses are included.
- The rapidity distribution at \(L = 0.5\) fm is already significantly different from the vacuum case, and at larger L it practically saturates.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium QCD cascades beyond the radiative energy loss mechanisms implemented in earlier Monte Carlo generators such as PYQUEN (Lokhtin & Snigirev), JEWEL (Zapp et al.), Q-PYTHIA, and Q-HERWIG (all cited in the paper). The authors specifically highlight that color randomization, with a characteristic time \(t_c\) much shorter than the momentum diffusion time \(t_p\) as calculated by Selikhov and Gyulassy (1993), provides a faster decoherence mechanism than rescattering. The results enable future work incorporating medium-induced radiative losses, which the authors state is in progress, and challenge the assumption that most final hadrons form outside the hot fireball.
