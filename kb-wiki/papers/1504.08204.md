# Neutrino Interactions and Long Baseline Physics

## Key claim

Neutrino oscillation parameters cannot be extracted purely from experimental measurements; they require event generators to subtract pion contributions and reconstruct neutrino energy, because quasielastic cross sections on nuclear targets are always entangled with pion production processes and cannot be determined by purely experimental means.

## Method

The author uses the GiBUU transport theoretical framework, which goes beyond Monte Carlo simulations by taking nuclear potentials into account, to calculate event distributions for neutrino-nucleus reactions on an ⁴⁰Ar target in the LBNF beam. The framework decomposes total event rates into contributions from true quasielastic scattering, 2-particle-2-hole excitations, Δ resonance excitations, and deep inelastic scattering, and compares event distributions as functions of true neutrino energy versus energy reconstructed from outgoing lepton kinematics assuming a true quasielastic process.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty width for neutrino energy reconstruction from quasielastic scattering on bound, Fermi-moving neutrons is about 60 MeV for a neutrino energy of about 1 GeV.
- Pion production processes (resonances and deep inelastic scattering) contribute about 2/3 of the total cross section on ⁴⁰Ar in the LBNF beam.
- True quasielastic scattering contributes about 1/3 of the total event rate at the peak of the energy distribution for the LBNF beam on ⁴⁰Ar.
- The event rate vs. reconstructed energy is shifted by more than 500 MeV in its maximum compared to true energy when using events with 0 outgoing pions.
- Restricting the event sample to 0 pions and exactly 1 proton reduces the difference between true and reconstructed energy to at most 100 MeV.
- The axial mass from neutrino experiments on nucleons or deuterons assumes a value of M_A ≈ 1 GeV.
- Recent experimental analyses still use an unphysical axial mass of M_A ≈ 1.25 GeV for describing quasielastic scattering.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of neutrino-nucleus interactions beyond the impulse approximation by incorporating spectral functions (Benhar 1994) and explicit 2-particle-2-hole interactions (Martini 2009, Nieves 2011, Megias 2014). It directly addresses the needs of long-baseline oscillation experiments including T2K, MINOS, NOvA, and future DUNE, showing that energy reconstruction errors from event misidentification distort the oscillation signal. The paper enables improved event generator development by demonstrating that requiring exactly one final-state proton in addition to zero pions provides a cleaner quasielastic signal, a strategy recently exploited by the MINERνA experiment (Walton 2014).
