# Tuning of Efimov states in non-integer dimensions

> The purpose of this paper is to show that, by combining Feshbach resonances with external confining potentials, the energy scale factor of neighboring Efimov states can be tremendously reduced. The Efimov conditions can be reached for systems made of three different particles. For the case of two identical light particles and a heavy particle the energy factor can be reduced by many orders of magnitude, and the Efimov states are in this way more easily reachable experimentally. The equivalence between external potentials and the formulation in terms of non-integer dimensions, $d$, is exploited. The technically simpler $d$-method is used to derive analytic expressions for two-component relative wave functions describing two short-range square-well interacting particles. The two components express one open and one closed channel. The scattering length is obtained after phase shift expansion, providing an analytic form for the Efimov condition. We illustrate the results by means of systems made of $^7$Li, $^{39}$K, and $^{87}$Rb, with realistic parameters. The related pairs of dimension and magnetic field are shown and discussed. The results are universal as they only rely on large-distance properties.

## Key claim

By combining Feshbach resonances with external confining potentials (mapped to non-integer dimensions), the energy scale factor between neighboring Efimov states can be reduced by many orders of magnitude, making Efimov states more experimentally accessible, but only when the two-body scattering lengths for the relevant subsystems can be simultaneously tuned to infinity via the dimension and magnetic field.

## Method

The authors use a two-channel model with square-well potentials in a continuous non-integer dimension \(d\), where the centrifugal barrier \(\ell(\ell+1) = (d-3)(d-1)/4\) accounts for the effect of external confinement. They derive analytic expressions for the two-component radial wave functions (open and closed channels) and obtain the scattering length from a low-energy expansion of the phase shift. The condition for infinite scattering length (critical dimension \(d_E\) and magnetic field \(B_0\)) is then applied to three-body systems with two identical particles (ABB) or three different particles (ABC), using realistic parameters for \(^7\)Li, \(^{39}\)K, and \(^{87}\)Rb.

## Key numbers

- For an ABB system with two heavy particles and one light particle, the energy scale factor between consecutive Efimov states is many orders of magnitude smaller than for two light particles and one heavy particle (Fig. 1, qualitative).
- For two light particles and one heavy particle with both \(a_{AB}=\infty\) and \(a_{BB}=\infty\), the energy scale factor ranges between 250 and 1000 when \(m_B/m_A \ll 1\) (for \(d=3.0, 2.8, 2.6\)).
- For three identical bosons in three dimensions, the energy scale factor is 515 (stated for comparison).
- The confinement length \(b_{ho}\) is related to dimension via \(b_{ho}/r_0 = \sqrt{2(d-2)/[(d-1)(3-d)]}\); for \(b_{ho}/r_0 \approx 1000\) one gets \(d \approx 2.999999\), while for \(d=2.9\) one gets \(b_{ho}/r_0 \approx 3.1\) (corresponding to harmonic frequencies in the tens of MHz, currently out of experimental reach).

## Context

This work extends the \(d\)-method formalism developed by Garrido et al. (2019a, 2019b, 2020, 2021) for describing confinement-induced Efimov effects, and builds on the two-channel square-well model used by Soerensen et al. (2013) and Langmack et al. (2009) for Feshbach resonances in three dimensions. The key advance is the simultaneous tuning of two two-body interactions via both dimension (confinement) and magnetic field, enabling Efimov conditions for systems with three different particles or two identical particles. This opens the possibility of experimentally realizing Efimov states with reduced energy scaling, though the required confinement frequencies (tens of MHz) remain challenging with current technology.
