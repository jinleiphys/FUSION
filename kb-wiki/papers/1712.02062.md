# The density- and isospin- dependent $Δ$-formation cross section and its decay width

> The energy-, density-, and isospin-dependent $Δ$-formation cross section $σ_{Nπ\rightarrow Δ}^*$ and $Δ$-decay width are calculated based on the relativistic BUU approach in which the effective mass splitting of nucleon and $Δ$ baryons in isospin-asymmetric matter is considered by the inclusion of the $δ$ meson exchange in the effective Lagrangian density. With the density-dependent couplings for baryons of F. Hofmann et al., the $σ_{Nπ\rightarrow Δ}^*$ is decreased (increased) moderately with increasing density with (without) the consideration of the density dependent pion effective mass. Meanwhile, if the invariant mass of the system is not far from the $Δ$ pole mass, the $Δ$-decay width is also weakly dependent on density. The mass splitting effect of differently charged nucleon and $Δ$ baryons on $σ_{Nπ\rightarrow Δ}^*$ are found to be more obvious than that of pion mesons but much weaker than the mass splitting in the hard $Δ$ production channel $NN\rightarrow NΔ$. Further, the largest mass-splitting influence is seen in the $π^-p\rightarrow Δ^0$ and $π^+n\rightarrow Δ^+$ channels but not in the production of $Δ^-$ and $Δ^{++}$ isobars.

# The density- and isospin-dependent Δ-formation cross section and its decay width

## Key claim
The density dependence of the Δ-formation cross section σ*_{Nπ→Δ} is moderate (less than 20% variation at twice normal density), and the mass-splitting effect from nucleon and Δ baryons is more obvious than that from pions but much weaker than the mass splitting in the hard Δ production channel NN→NΔ. This weak density dependence contrasts with earlier calculations by Zhang (2017) that showed a larger density dependence, possibly due to short-range correlations not included in the relativistic approach.

## Method
The authors calculated the in-medium Δ-formation cross section σ*_{Nπ→Δ} and Δ-decay width using the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) approach, incorporating effective mass splitting of nucleons and Δ baryons via δ meson exchange in the effective Lagrangian density. They used density-dependent couplings for baryons from Hofmann (2000) and included the density-dependent pion effective mass from the s-wave pion self-energy based on chiral perturbation theory. The six individual isospin channels of the Nπ→Δ process were computed with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for isospin coupling.

## Key numbers
- At the Δ pole mass (m_Δ^0 = 1.232 GeV) and twice normal density (u=2), the enhancement or suppression of σ*_{Nπ→Δ} relative to the free cross section is not more than 16% of the free value.
- At free space, the Δ-decay width Γ_0 at the Δ pole mass is 115 MeV.
- At u=2 and isospin asymmetry α=0.3, the smallest Δ-decay width (from π^-p→Δ^0) is about 109 MeV, while the largest (from π^+n→Δ^+) is about 126 MeV.
- At u=2 and the Δ pole mass, the isospin-dependent ratio R(α)=σ*(α)/σ*(α=0) for individual channels ranges from 0.87 to 1.15, with values: π^-p→Δ^0 = 1.15, π^0p→Δ^+ = 1.05, π^-n→Δ^- = 1.02, π^+p→Δ^{++} = 0.97, π^0n→Δ^0 = 0.94, π^+n→Δ^+ = 0.87.
- The reduced effective nucleon mass m*/m_0 at normal density is 0.55 in this work, compared to 0.6 in Mao (1998).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous RBUU study of the NN→NΔ process (Li 2016) to the Nπ→Δ (soft Δ production) channel, and builds on the formalism for medium-modified cross sections from Mao (1998). The results show that medium effects on the Nπ→Δ cross section are much weaker than those on the NN→NΔ cross section, suggesting that in transport simulations such as the UrQMD model, medium modifications on the hard Δ production channel deserve more attention. The weak density dependence found here differs from the stronger dependence reported by Zhang (2017) using the extended RVUU approach, a discrepancy attributed to short-range correlations (via the Migdal parameter g') that remain an open problem for relativistic calculations.
