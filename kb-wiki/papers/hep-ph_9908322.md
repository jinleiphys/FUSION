# Light-Quark Mesons and Four-Quark Condensates at Finite Temperature

> We propose an analog of the familiar gap equation for the case of four-quark condensates at finite temperature. The condensates of interest correspond to scalar, vector, psudoscalar, axial vector, and tensor Dirac structures. Working with correlators at zero chemical potential without factorization, we arrive at coupled equations for these four-quark condensates and the masses of certain light-quark mesons. We study the temperature dependence of the four-quark condensates and masses; in one of our models, factorization of the four-quark condensates is shown to be increasingly violated as the temperature is increased toward $T_c$. The 2$^{++}$ tensor mesons a$_2$(1320)-f$_2$(1270) are identified as especially sensitive probes of the four-quark condensates.

# Light-Quark Mesons and Four-Quark Condensates at Finite Temperature

## Key claim

The authors propose that four-quark condensates and light-quark meson masses can be determined from coupled QCD sum-rule equations without assuming factorization, and that in one of their models factorization of the four-quark condensates is increasingly violated as the temperature approaches the critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 200\) MeV. The tensor mesons a\(_2\)(1320) and f\(_2\)(1270) are identified as especially sensitive probes of the four-quark condensates, but the authors caution that their results are based on QCD sum-rule models with ad hoc assumptions about the temperature dependence of the continuum, not on rigorous predictions of QCD.

## Method

The authors extend QCD sum rules to finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism, working with correlators at zero chemical potential without applying the factorization approximation for four-quark condensates. They derive coupled equations for five independent four-quark condensates (scalar, vector, pseudoscalar, axial vector, and tensor Dirac structures) and the masses of the corresponding light-quark mesons, including the scalar f\(_0\)(1370)-a\(_0\)(1450), vector \(\rho\)(776)-\(\omega\)(783), axial vector a\(_1\)(1260)-f\(_1\)(1285), axial D\(_1\)(1235), tensor a\(_2\)(1320)-f\(_2\)(1270), and axial tensor H\(_2\)(1670) mesons. The four-quark condensates are obtained from separate sum rules that relate the correlator residues to the vacuum expectation value \(\langle 0|J^\Gamma J^\Gamma|0\rangle\), with the scale fixed phenomenologically to reproduce the factorized values at \(T=0\).

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the phase transition is taken as \(T_c \simeq 200\) MeV.
- The zero-temperature quark condensate value is \(\langle 0|:\bar{q}q:|0\rangle = -(0.25\ \text{GeV})^3\).
- The zero-temperature gluon condensate is \(\langle g_s^2 G^2\rangle \simeq 0.47\ \text{GeV}^4\).
- The strong coupling constant is \(\alpha_s = g_s^2/4\pi \approx 0.7\) at a scale of \(\mu = 200\) MeV.
- The perturbative contribution to the gluon condensate at finite temperature is \(\langle G^2\rangle^{P.T.}_T \simeq (8\pi^2/15) T^5\), which at \(T_c \simeq 200\) MeV is less than one-tenth of the zero-temperature nonperturbative term.
- The scalar meson mixing parameter \(\beta = 0.7\) reproduces the mass \(M^{0^{++}} = 1370\) MeV for the f\(_0\)(1370).
- The quark condensate drops toward zero at a temperature of about 160 MeV at zero chemical potential, based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model extended to finite temperature.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature QCD sum-rule treatment of four-quark condensates originally introduced by Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov (1990s), who investigated the four-quark condensate contributing to the \(\rho\) meson mass without using the factorization approximation. The authors note that subsequent calculations of the \(\rho\) meson mass at finite temperature by other groups (e.g., Dominguez and collaborators, and Furnstahl and collaborators) generally used factorization, while studies at finite density by Furnstahl and Banerjee (1990s) and by Jin and collaborators (1990s) showed that factorization is not valid for hadrons in a medium of nonzero density. The present work is the first attention given to dynamical models of four-quark condensates since the original Bochkarev-Shaposhnikov study, and it enables future experimental tests at RHIC to probe the temperature dependence of four-quark condensates through measurements of tensor meson properties.
