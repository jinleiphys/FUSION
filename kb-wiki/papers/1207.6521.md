# Correlations in Multi-Parton Interactions

> In double parton interactions, the two hard proceses are correlated via double parton densities. We examine the double Drell-Yan process and the impact of such correlations on the differential cross section. In particular correlations between the spins of the interacting quarks can induce correlations between the decay planes of the vector bosons. We investigate upper limits on spin correlations following from positivity of the double parton densities.

# Correlations in Multi-Parton Interactions

## Key claim
Spin correlations between quarks in double parton interactions can induce correlations between the decay planes of vector bosons in double Drell-Yan processes, but these effects are bounded by positivity constraints on the double parton densities; the actual size of such correlations depends on whether the vector bosons are neutral or charged, with neutral currents showing an azimuthal angle dependence that charged currents do not.

## Method
The authors calculate the differential cross section for double Drell-Yan production at leading order, incorporating double parton distributions (DPDs) that account for unpolarized, longitudinally polarized, and transversely polarized quarks. They integrate over transverse boson momenta to obtain collinear DPDs, then organize the DPDs into a spin density matrix in the light-cone helicity basis and derive positivity bounds by requiring the matrix to be positive semi-definite. The cross section is expressed in terms of the polar and azimuthal angles of the leptons in the vector boson rest frames.

## Key numbers
- The cross section for neutral current double Drell-Yan with transversely polarized quarks contains a term proportional to sin²θ₁ sin²θ₂ cos(2(φ₁ − φ₂)) and sin²θ₁ sin²θ₂ sin(2(φ₁ − φ₂)), where θᵢ and φᵢ are the lepton angles in each vector boson rest frame.
- Positivity bounds yield the inequality f_qq ≥ |f_δqδq − y²M²f_δqδqᵗ|, where M is the proton mass and y is the transverse distance between the two hard vertices.
- Additional inequalities from positivity include f_qq + f_ΔqΔq ≥ 2y²M²fᵗ_δqδq and f_qq − f_ΔqΔq ≥ 2f_δqδq.
- For W bosons, the coupling factors for transversely polarized quarks are zero, so transverse spin correlations do not contribute to the cross section for charged currents.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of double parton interactions from the perturbative QCD framework developed by Diehl (2011) and Gaunt (2010), building on earlier work by Mekhfi (1983) on double Drell-Yan processes. The positivity constraints are analogous to those previously derived for generalized parton distributions by Diehl (2005) and for transverse momentum dependent distributions by Bacchetta (1999). The results enable future studies of spin correlation effects in other multi-parton processes such as double dijet production, though with increased complexity due to color structure.
