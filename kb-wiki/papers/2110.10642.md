# Study of one-proton transfer reaction for the $^{18}$O + $^{48}$Ti system at 275 MeV

> Single-nucleon transfer reactions are processes that selectively probe single-particle components of the populated many-body nuclear states. In this context, recent efforts have been made to build a unified description of the rich nuclear spectroscopy accessible in heavy-ion collisions. An example of this multichannel approach is the study of the competition between successive nucleon transfer and charge exchange reactions, the latter being of particular interest in the context of single and double beta decay studies. To this extent, the one-proton pickup reaction $^{48}$Ti($^{18}$O,$^{19}$F)$^{47}$Sc at 275 MeV was measured for the first time, under the NUMEN experimental campaign. Differential cross-section angular distribution measurements for the $^{19}$F ejectiles were performed at INFN-LNS in Catania by using the MAGNEX large acceptance magnetic spectrometer. The data were analyzed within the distorted-wave and coupled-channels Born approximation frameworks. The initial and final-state interactions were described adopting the São Paulo potential, whereas the spectroscopic amplitudes for the projectile and target overlaps were derived from shell-model calculations. The theoretical cross sections are found to be in very good agreement with the experimental data, suggesting the validity of the optical potentials and the shell-model description of the involved nuclear states within the adopted model space.

## Key claim

The one-proton pickup reaction $^{48}$Ti($^{18}$O,$^{19}$F)$^{47}$Sc at 275 MeV was measured for the first time, and the theoretical cross sections calculated within the Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) and Coupled-Channels Born Approximation (CCBA) frameworks, using the Sao Paulo potential and shell-model spectroscopic amplitudes, are found to be in very good agreement with the experimental data, suggesting the validity of the adopted optical potentials and shell-model description within the chosen model space.

## Method

Differential cross-section angular distributions for $^{19}$F ejectiles from the $^{48}$Ti($^{18}$O,$^{19}$F)$^{47}$Sc reaction at 275 MeV were measured using the MAGNEX large acceptance magnetic spectrometer at INFN-LNS in Catania. The data were analyzed within the DWBA and CCBA frameworks using the FRESCO code, with initial and final-state interactions described by the double-folding Sao Paulo potential and spectroscopic amplitudes for projectile and target overlaps derived from shell-model calculations. Background contributions from the $^{27}$Al backing and $^{16}$O component of the TiO$_2$ target were subtracted using separate measurements with $^{27}$Al and WO$_3$ targets.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 275 MeV for the $^{18}$O$^{8+}$ ion beam delivered by the K800 Superconducting Cyclotron.
- Target thickness: 510 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ thick TiO$_2$ target evaporated on a 216 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ thick aluminum foil.
- Angular range covered: between 3 and 15 degrees in the laboratory reference frame for MAGNEX spectrometer operation.
- Energy resolution: approximately 500 keV in full width at half maximum (FWHM) for the $^{27}$Al($^{18}$O,$^{19}$F)$^{26}$Mg reaction measurement.
- Normalization factors for the Sao Paulo potential in DWBA calculations: N$_R$=1.0 and N$_I$=0.78.
- For the $^{48}$Ti target, the reduced transition probability used for the 2$_1$$^+$ state was B(E2; 0$^+$$\rightarrow$2$^+$)= 0.072 e$^2$b$^2$ from Raman.
- For the $^{18}$O projectile, the reduced transition probability used for the 2$_1$$^+$ state was B(E2; 0$^+$$\rightarrow$2$^+$)= 0.0043 e$^2$b$^2$ from Pritychenko.
- For the $^{16}$O target, the reduced transition probability used for the 3$_1$$^-$ state was B(E3; 0$^+$$\rightarrow$3$^-$)= 0.0015 e$^2$b$^3$ from Kibedi.

## Context

This work is part of the NUMEN project, which aims to access information about nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay through heavy-ion induced double charge exchange reactions. The study extends previous work on the $^{18}$O+$^{65}$Cu system by Zagatto (2019) and earlier ($^{18}$O,$^{19}$F) studies on $^{40}$Ca and $^{54}$Fe by Siemssen, which yielded poor descriptions of angular distributions. The present measurement of the one-proton pickup reaction on $^{48}$Ti provides a test of the optical potentials and shell-model spectroscopic information needed to properly describe single charge exchange and double charge exchange mechanisms, as emphasized by Lenske. The results enable future quantitative determination of competing reaction mechanisms in double charge exchange channels relevant to neutrinoless double beta decay studies.
