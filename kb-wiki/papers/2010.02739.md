# Density-dependent nn-potential from subleading chiral three-neutron forces: Long-range terms

> The long-range terms of the subleading chiral three-nucleon force [published in Phys.\,Rev.\,C77, 064004 (2008)] are specified to the case of three neutrons. From these $3n$-interactions an effective density-dependent neutron-neutron potential $V_\text{med}$ in pure neutron matter is derived. Following the division of the pertinent 3n-diagrams into two-pion exchange, two-pion-one-pion exchange and ring topology, all self-closings and concatenations of two neutron-lines to an in-medium loop are evaluated. The momentum and $k_n$-dependent potentials associated with the spin-operators $1,\, \vecσ_1\!\cdot\!\vecσ_2,\, \vecσ_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\, \vecσ_2\!\cdot\!\vec q,\, i( \vecσ_1\!+\!\vecσ_2)\!\cdot \! (\vec q\!\times \! \vec p\,),\, (\vecσ_1\!\cdot\!\vec p\,\vecσ_2\!\cdot\!\vec p+\vecσ_1\!\cdot\!\vec p\,'\, \vecσ_2\!\cdot\!\vec p\,')$ and $ \vecσ_1\!\cdot \! (\vec q\!\times \! \vec p\,)\vecσ_2\!\cdot \! (\vec q\!\times \! \vec p\,)$ are expressed in terms of functions, which are either given in closed analytical form or require at most one numerical integration. The subsubleading chiral 3N-force is treated in the same way. The obtained results for $V_\text{med}$ are helpful to implement the long-range chiral three-body forces into advanced neutron matter calculations.

## Key claim
The paper derives an effective density-dependent neutron-neutron potential \(V_\text{med}\) in pure neutron matter from the long-range terms of the subleading chiral three-neutron force at order N\(^3\)LO, and also treats the subsubleading chiral 3N-force at N\(^4\)LO in the same manner. The results are expressed in terms of functions given in closed analytical form or requiring at most one numerical integration, but the derivation is restricted to on-shell scattering kinematics in the center-of-mass frame and to the case of three neutrons only.

## Method
The long-range terms of the subleading chiral three-nucleon force (Bernard et al., 2008) were specified to three neutrons by substituting isospin operators (\(\vec\tau_i \cdot \vec\tau_j \to 1\), \(\vec\tau_1 \cdot (\vec\tau_2 \times \vec\tau_3) \to 0\)). Self-closings and concatenations of two neutron lines to an in-medium loop were evaluated for three diagram topologies: two-pion exchange, two-pion-one-pion exchange, and ring topology. The resulting momentum- and Fermi-momentum-dependent potentials for spin operators \(1\), \(\vec\sigma_1\cdot\vec\sigma_2\), \(\vec\sigma_1\cdot\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\cdot\vec q\), \(i(\vec\sigma_1+\vec\sigma_2)\cdot(\vec q\times\vec p)\), \((\vec\sigma_1\cdot\vec p\,\vec\sigma_2\cdot\vec p+\vec\sigma_1\cdot\vec p'\,\vec\sigma_2\cdot\vec p')\), and \(\vec\sigma_1\cdot(\vec q\times\vec p)\,\vec\sigma_2\cdot(\vec q\times\vec p)\) were expressed in closed analytical form or as single numerical integrals.

## Key numbers
- Neutron Fermi momentum \(k_n\) is the density variable; the density \(\rho_n = k_n^3 / 3\pi^2\) appears as a factor in self-closing contributions (e.g., eq. (2) and eq. (10)).
- Self-closing from two-pion exchange gives \(V^{(0)}_\text{med} = - [g_A^4 m_\pi k_n^3 / (2(4\pi f_\pi^2)^3)] \, (\vec\sigma_1\cdot\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\cdot\vec q)/(m_\pi^2+q^2)^2 \, (q^2 + 5m_\pi^2/6)\).
- Self-closing from two-pion-one-pion exchange gives \(V^{(0)}_\text{med} = [5g_A^4 (1-g_A^2) m_\pi k_n^3 / (384\pi^3 f_\pi^6)] \, (\vec\sigma_1\cdot\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\cdot\vec q)/(m_\pi^2+q^2)\).
- Self-closing from the ring topology at order \(g_A^4\) yields a central term proportional to \(k_n^3\) with coefficients involving \(m_\pi\) and \(\arctan(q/2m_\pi)\) (eq. (20)).
- Self-closing from the subsubleading \(2\pi^0\)-exchange 3n-force gives \(V^{(0)}_\text{med} = -[g_A^2 k_n^3 / (12\pi^2 f_\pi^4)] \, (\vec\sigma_1\cdot\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\cdot\vec q)/(m_\pi^2+q^2)^2 \, \tilde g_+(0)\).

## Context
This work extends the derivation of density-dependent two-nucleon potentials from chiral three-nucleon forces, previously done for the leading N\(^2\)LO force (Holt et al., 2010) and for the short-range and relativistic \(1/M\)-correction terms at N\(^3\)LO (Kaiser, 2020), to the long-range terms of the subleading three-neutron force. It also treats the subsubleading N\(^4\)LO \(2\pi^0\)-exchange and ring diagrams. The results enable the implementation of long-range chiral three-body forces into advanced neutron matter calculations, complementing the purely numerical approach of the Darmstadt group (Hebeler et al., 2011) which avoids the on-shell restriction.
