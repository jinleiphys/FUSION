# Jet Measurements with Neutral and Di-jet Triggers in Central Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{{s}_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV with STAR

> We present two measurements related to jet production in p+p and central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{{s}_{NN}}$=200 GeV. Firstly, a study of semi-inclusive charged recoil jets coincident with high-$\rm{p_{T}}$ direct-photon and neutral pions, and secondly, the hadron correlations with respect to reconstructed di-jet triggers. Indication of medium effects is observed by comparing $γ$+jet and $π^{0}$+jet measurements. The di-jet+hadron study shows signs that the medium-induced modifications of an imbalanced set of di-jets with "hard cores" primarily affect the recoil jet.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that medium-induced modifications of imbalanced di-jets with "hard cores" (constituent pT > 2 GeV/c) in central Au+Au collisions primarily affect the recoil jet, while the comparison between γ+jet and π⁰+jet measurements shows an indication of medium effects that requires larger statistics and final corrections to draw a strong conclusion.

## Method

Semi-inclusive charged recoil jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with R=0.3 from charged tracks with pT > 0.2 GeV/c, triggered by high-pT direct-photons (enriched via transverse shower profile method) and neutral pions in 0%-10% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. Uncorrelated background jets were subtracted using a mixed-event method, and the distributions were corrected via singular value decomposition unfolding. Separately, di-jets were selected with "hard core" constituents (pT > 2.0 GeV/c) requiring a leading jet pT > 20.0 GeV/c and subleading jet pT > 10.0 GeV/c, and hadron correlations in Δη and Δφ were measured for both trigger and recoil jets.

## Key numbers

- Charged recoil jets reconstructed with jet resolution parameter R=0.3 using charged tracks with pT > 0.2 GeV/c
- Neutral triggers selected within ET range 9 < ET < 30 GeV
- Di-jet selection: leading jet pT > 20.0 GeV/c and subleading jet pT > 10.0 GeV/c
- Di-jet "hard core" constituent cut: pT > 2.0 GeV/c
- Di-jet reconstruction used R=0.4 with anti-kT algorithm
- Online high tower trigger required uncorrected transverse tower energy ET > 5.4 GeV
- Direct-photon purity estimated at 70% in Au+Au collisions and 40% in p+p collisions
- PYTHIA simulation shows π⁰ carries on average 85% of parent parton energy

## Context

This work extends the STAR AJ measurement (Adamczyk 2016) which found that di-jets selected with hard cores were imbalanced in central Au+Au compared to p+p, with lost energy recovered in soft constituents (pT > 0.2 GeV/c) within R=0.4. The γ+jet versus π⁰+jet comparison probes the color-factor dependence of parton energy loss, exploiting that direct-photon triggers do not suffer surface bias and carry approximately the initial recoil parton energy (Wang and Huang, Sarcevic; Wang and Zhu). The di-jet+hadron correlations enable investigation of energy redistribution within the medium, and future measurements with increased statistics from STAR 2014 and 2016 Au+Au data sets will allow differential AJ studies and potential path length control via "jet geometry engineering."
