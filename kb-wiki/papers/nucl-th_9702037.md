# Three-Body Resonances in Framework of the Faddeev Configuration Space Approach

> Algorithm, based on explicit representations for analytic continuation of the T-matrix Faddeev components on unphysical sheets, is worked out for calculations of resonances in the three-body quantum problem. According to the representations, poles of the T--matrix, scattering matrix and resolvent on unphysical sheets, interpreted as resonances, coincide with those complex energy values where appropriate truncations of the scattering matrix have zero as eigenvalue. Scattering amplitudes on the physical sheet, necessary to construct scattering matrix, are calculated on the basis of the Faddeev differential equations. Effectiveness of the algorithm developed is demonstrated for example of searching for resonances in the system $nnp$ and in a model three-boson system.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algorithm for calculating three-body resonances by analytically continuing the Faddeev T-matrix components onto unphysical sheets, where resonances coincide with complex energies at which appropriate truncations of the scattering matrix have zero as an eigenvalue; the method is demonstrated for the \(nnp\) system and a model three-boson system, but the authors find only the known virtual state in \(nnp\) and no excited state of \(^3\)H.

## Method

The authors solve the two-dimensional Faddeev integro-differential equations in configuration space with \((2\rightarrow 2,3)\) asymptotic boundary conditions at complex energies, using a finite-difference approximation with up to 180 grid points in both radial and angular variables and a cut-off radius up to 39 fm. They extract the truncated s-state scattering matrix \(S_{01}(z) = 1 + 2ia_0(z)\) from the elastic \(nd\) scattering amplitude \(a_0(z)\) continued onto the physical sheet, and locate resonances as zeros of \(S_{01}(z)\) on the unphysical sheet \(\Pi_{(0,1)}\). For the \(nnp\) system the Malfliet-Tjon MT I–III potential is used; for the model three-boson system a Gauss-type potential with an additional repulsive Gauss barrier term is employed.

## Key numbers

- Triton bound-state energy \(E_t = -8.55\) MeV (with respect to breakup threshold) found as a pole of \(S_{01}(z)\) on an \(80\times 80\) grid, in good agreement with known MT I–III model results.
- For the \(nnp\) system on a \(180\times 180\) grid, the virtual state resonance energy \(z_{\rm res} = -2.728\) MeV, situated 0.504 MeV below the \(nd\) threshold \(E_d = -2.224\) MeV (MT I–III model); the experimental shift is 0.515 MeV and the separable MT I–III model gives 0.502 MeV.
- In the model three-boson system with the Gauss potential, the barrier amplitude \(V_b\) is varied between 0.85 MeV and 1.5 MeV; for \(V_b \geq 0.95\) MeV the resonance becomes real (turning into a discrete eigenvalue), and at \(V_b \cong 0.97\) MeV the real part of the resonance coincides with the two-boson binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the analytic continuation approach of Motovilov (1995) for three-body resonances to numerical implementation, using the Faddeev differential equations in configuration space as developed by Merkuriev, Gignoux, and Laverne (1976–1985). The results for the \(nnp\) virtual state agree with prior momentum-space Faddeev calculations by Kvitsinsky et al. (1993) and with experimental \(nd\) scattering data, but the authors find no evidence for the excited \(^3\)H resonance reported by other groups. The method enables systematic searches for three-body resonances in systems with exponentially decaying interactions and can be applied to other nuclear or hadronic three-body systems.
