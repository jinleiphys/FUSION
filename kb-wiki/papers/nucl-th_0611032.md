# Compton Scattering on He-3

> We present first calculations for Compton scattering on $^3$He. The objective of the calculation is an extraction of the neutron polarizabilities.

## Key claim

First calculations for Compton scattering on He-3 show that the differential cross-section and double-polarization observables have sizeable sensitivity to neutron polarizabilities, suggesting this is a promising avenue for extracting those polarizabilities, though the results are calculations rather than experimental extractions.

## Method

The irreducible amplitudes for elastic scattering of real photons from the three-nucleon system were ordered and calculated in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBχPT) to O(Q³), using the same amplitudes as those computed by Beane et al. (1999). These amplitudes were then sandwiched between nuclear wavefunctions to obtain the scattering amplitude, from which the differential cross-section and double-polarization observables Δz and Δx were computed.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross-section in the center-of-mass frame calculated at 80 MeV, showing sensitivity to Δβn (neutron magnetic polarizability) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Double-polarization observable Δz shows sensitivity to Δγ1n (neutron spin polarizability) at 120 MeV in the center-of-mass frame, with variations in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Double-polarization observable Δx shows sensitivity to Δγ4n (neutron spin polarizability) at 120 MeV in the center-of-mass frame, with variations in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the approach of Beane et al. (1999) for Compton scattering on few-nucleon systems to the specific case of He-3, motivated by the impossibility of direct neutron polarizability measurements due to the lack of free neutron targets. The calculations enable future experimental programs that could extract neutron polarizabilities from γ-He-3 scattering data, providing an indirect method to access these fundamental neutron properties.
