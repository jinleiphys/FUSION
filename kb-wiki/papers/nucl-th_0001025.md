# Solutions of the pion dispersion equation in the medium: new aspects

> In symmetrical nuclear matter the solutions of pion dispersion equation are investigated in the complex plane of the pion frequency ω. There are three well-known branches of solutions on the physical sheet : sound, pion and isobar, at the matter density less than the critical one ρ<ρ_c. At ρ>ρ_c the fourth branch appears on the physical sheet. For this branch the condition ω^2_c\leq0 (in general case Reω^2_c\leq0) takes place. This points out the instability of the ground state which is possibly related to the pion condensation.

## Key claim

In symmetrical nuclear matter, a fourth branch of solutions to the pion dispersion equation appears on the physical sheet at densities above a critical value (ρ > ρc), where the condition Re ω²c ≤ 0 holds, indicating ground-state instability possibly related to pion condensation; this branch emerges at a Fermi momentum of pF ≥ 283 MeV for the specific parameter set used.

## Method

The author solves the pion dispersion equation ω² − k² − m²π − Π(ω, k, pF) = 0 in the complex plane of the pion frequency ω, using a polarization operator Π that includes scalar (ΠS) and P-wave (ΠP) contributions from πNN and πNΔ interactions. The scalar part is derived from the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation with the scalar quark condensate expanded to first order in density, while the P-wave part sums nucleon-hole and isobar-hole loops with Migdal functions, including finite baryon size form factors and a nonzero isobar width (ΓΔ = 115 MeV in the baseline case). Solutions are traced across physical and unphysical sheets of the complex ω-plane as functions of momentum k and density ρ.

## Key numbers

- The critical Fermi momentum for the appearance of the fourth branch is pF = 283 MeV, corresponding to a critical density ρc ≈ 1.2 ρ0, where ρ0 is the equilibrium density (ρ0 = 2p³F0/3π², pF0 = 268 MeV).
- At the critical density, the momentum values where the condensate branch enters and leaves the physical sheet coincide: k1 = k2 = 1.8 mπ.
- At pF = 290 MeV, the condensate branch lies on the physical sheet in the momentum interval k1 = 1.3 mπ to k2 = 2.3 mπ.
- The sound branch ωs(k) leaves the physical sheet at kf = 0.430 mπ for pF = 290 MeV (0.436 mπ for pF = 268 MeV).
- The isobar branch ωΔ(k) ends on the isobar cut at kf = 5.1 mπ for pF = 268 MeV (4.8 mπ for pF = 290 MeV).
- The pion branch ωπ(k) ends on the physical sheet under the isobar cut at kf = 3.5 mπ for pF = 268 MeV (3.8 mπ for pF = 290 MeV).
- The isobar width in the baseline calculation is ΓΔ = 115 MeV; at ΓΔ = 0 the condensate branch becomes pure imaginary with ω²c ≤ 0.

## Context

This work extends the classic studies of pion condensation by Migdal and collaborators (Migdal, 1978) and the subsequent book by Migdal et al., which concluded that pion condensation manifests weakly at normal density but may influence the equation of state at high densities. The author uses the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation to determine the in-medium pion mass, following the approach of Drukarev and Ryskin (1997) for the scalar quark condensate. The results enable further investigation of the connection between the condensate branch and chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter at critical density, as suggested in Drukarev and Ryskin (1997).
