# Leptophilic Interactions in Nuclear Energy Density Functional Theory

> We develop a unified theoretical framework that embeds a light leptophilic vector boson into nuclear energy density functional (EDF) theory. Starting from an underlying leptophilic gauge interaction, the mediator is integrated out in the static limit, yielding an effective current--current interaction that couples proton and lepton densities. This interaction is incorporated self-consistently into relativistic mean-field equations, defining a leptophilic extension of conventional nuclear EDFs. The resulting leptophilic EDF induces correlated modifications of proton and lepton chemical potentials, directly affecting beta equilibrium in dense matter. In uniform matter, these effects lead to percent-level changes in the proton fraction, symmetry energy, and equation of state within phenomenologically allowed parameter ranges. In finite nuclei, the modified proton mean field generates shifts of $10^{-3}$--$10^{-2}\,\mathrm{fm}$ in neutron-skin thicknesses, comparable to current experimental sensitivities. Our results demonstrate that light leptophilic interactions leave coherent and experimentally accessible imprints on both nuclear structure and dense-matter observables. The framework introduced here provides a controlled and realistic extension of nuclear EDF theory, enabling nuclear systems to serve as laboratories for probing new physics in the leptonic sector.

# Leptophilic Interactions in Nuclear Energy Density Functional Theory

## Key claim

Light leptophilic vector bosons, when embedded self-consistently into relativistic mean-field theory, produce percent-level modifications in the proton fraction, symmetry energy, and equation of state of beta-equilibrated matter, as well as shifts of 10^{-3}–10^{-2} fm in neutron-skin thicknesses, but only within phenomenologically allowed parameter ranges that are compatible with current laboratory and astrophysical constraints.

## Method

The author introduces a new U(1)' gauge interaction coupling to leptons with strength g_ℓ and, via kinetic mixing with hypercharge, to protons with effective coupling g_p ≃ εe. The mediator Z_ℓ is integrated out in the static limit (m_{Z_ℓ} ≫ k_F), yielding a local current–current energy density term that is incorporated into relativistic mean-field equations with σ–ω–ρ meson fields. The coupled mean-field and beta-equilibrium equations are solved self-consistently for uniform matter across baryon densities n_B = 0.5n_0–3n_0 and for finite nuclei including ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb using the DD-ME2 and NL3 hadronic parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Proton fraction Y_p modification reaches the few-percent level for benchmark couplings (m_{Z_ℓ}=10 MeV, g_ℓ=10^{-3}, g_p=10^{-4}) at densities n_B = 0.5n_0–3n_0, where n_0 ≃ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Pressure shifts ΔP/P ∼ 1–5% in beta-equilibrated matter for the same benchmark parameters
- Symmetry-energy slope L changes by ΔL ≃ 2–8 MeV at saturation density
- Neutron-skin thickness shifts Δr_skin ≃ 0.005–0.02 fm for ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb with benchmark couplings
- Leptophilic parameter ranges explored: m_{Z_ℓ}=5–100 MeV, g_ℓ = 10^{-4}–10^{-2}, ε = 10^{-6}–10^{-3}

## Context

This work extends standard relativistic mean-field approaches such as those of Lalazissis (2005) and the DD-ME2 parameter set by embedding a leptophilic gauge sector motivated by anomaly-free U(1)' constructions discussed by Heeck (2014) and kinetic mixing mechanisms from Holdom (1986). The framework enables nuclear structure observables, including neutron-skin thicknesses measured by PREX-II and CREX, to serve as probes of new leptophilic physics, complementing particle-physics and astrophysical searches. The results demonstrate that leptophilic effects are comparable in magnitude to the intrinsic spread among modern EDF parametrizations, motivating future global refits of L-EDF parameters and combined analyses of nuclear and neutron-star observables.
