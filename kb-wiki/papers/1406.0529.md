# Nuclear matter with JISP16 NN interaction

> Saturation properties of the JISP16 NN interaction are studied in symmetric nuclear matter calculations, with special attention paid to the convergence properties with respect to the number of partial waves. We also present results of pure neutron matter calculations with the JISP16 interaction.

## Key claim

The JISP16 NN interaction, when extended to include partial waves up to J=8, produces a saturating nuclear matter equation of state that overbinds and overcompresses nuclear matter relative to empirical saturation, but this overbinding may be correctable through phase-equivalent transformations in higher partial waves without affecting the description of light nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter calculations using many-body perturbation theory with the JISP16 NN interaction, including Hartree-Fock single-particle energies and summing ladder particle-particle contributions to all orders. They extended the JISP16 interaction to higher partial waves (up to J=8) using the J-matrix inverse scattering approach with Nijmegen partial wave analysis as input, and studied convergence with respect to both the number of partial waves and the order of perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The converged energy minimum for symmetric nuclear matter with JISP16 (J≤4) occurs at Fermi momentum k_F ≈ 1.55 fm^{-1} with binding energy ≈ 22.7 MeV per nucleon, both larger than empirical saturation values.
- Convergence of the nuclear matter equation of state with respect to J is achieved when all partial waves with J≤7 are included.
- The JISP16 interaction is defined with truncation in oscillator quanta N=2n+L≤9, allowing definition only in NN partial waves with J≤8.
- The angle-average approximation for the Pauli blocking operator is accurate to ~0.5 MeV per nucleon for soft interactions.
- The JISP16 interaction describes np scattering data with χ²/datum ≈ 1.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear matter saturation properties from soft NN interactions such as V_low-k (Scott 2005, Hebeler 2011) to the JISP16 interaction, which is constructed via the J-matrix inverse scattering approach and fitted to nuclei with A≤16. Unlike V_low-k NN interactions that cause nuclear matter collapse without three-nucleon forces, JISP16 produces saturation at the NN-only level, suggesting that its off-shell properties, adjusted by fitting light nuclei, may simulate some three-body force contributions. The results enable future phase-equivalent modifications of JISP16 to improve nuclear matter saturation while preserving the good description of light nuclei, and demonstrate that the long-held belief that soft NN interactions cannot properly saturate nuclear matter (Bethe 1971) is not strictly true.
