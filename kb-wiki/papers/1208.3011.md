# Anisotropic Flow and Viscous Hydrodynamics

> We report part of our recent work on viscous hydrodynamics with consistent phase space distribution $f(x,\p)$ for freeze out. We develop the gradient expansion formalism based on kinetic theory, and with the constraints from the comparison between hydrodynamics and kinetic theory, viscous corrections to $f(x,\p)$ can be consistently determined order by order. Then with the obtained $f(x,\p)$, second order viscous hydrodynamical calculations are carried out for elliptic flow $v_2$.

# Anisotropic Flow and Viscous Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Viscous corrections to the phase-space distribution function at freeze-out can be consistently determined order-by-order in a gradient expansion based on kinetic theory with relaxation time approximation, but the resulting second-order correction $\delta f_{(2)}$ has such strong $p_T$ dependence (up to $p_T^4$) that the formalism becomes unreliable for $p_T > 1.5$ GeV in the quadratic ansatz limit with conformal equation of state.

## Method

The authors developed a Chapman-Enskog gradient expansion formalism to solve the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation, matching the viscous corrections to the distribution function $\delta f(x,\mathbf{p})$ order-by-order to the second-order BRSSS viscous hydrodynamics equations of motion. They derived the explicit form of $\delta f_{(2)}$ and determined the second-order transport coefficients $\tau_\pi$, $\lambda_1$, $\lambda_2$, and $\lambda_3$ from the constraint that the momentum integral of $\delta f$ reproduces the stress tensor $\pi^{\mu\nu}$. Hydrodynamical simulations were then performed for elliptic flow $v_2$ at RHIC energy using both conformal and lattice equations of state, with smooth initial conditions and a constant freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ is fixed; for a massless gas in the quadratic ansatz limit, $c_0 = \eta/s$.
- Second-order transport coefficients derived: $\eta\tau_\pi = T^2 B_3 / 15$, $\lambda_1 = \eta\tau_\pi [2B_2/(7B_3) + 1]$, $\lambda_2 = -2\eta\tau_\pi$, $\lambda_3 = 0$.
- Freeze-out temperature $T_{fo} = 150$ MeV used in simulations.
- Impact parameter $b = 6.8$ fm for the smooth initial condition calculations.
- For conformal equation of state with quadratic ansatz ($\alpha=0$), the formalism becomes unreliable for $p_T > 1.5$ GeV due to the $p_T^4$ dependence of $\delta f_{(2)}$.

## Context

This work extends the BRSSS second-order viscous hydrodynamics formalism (Baier et al., 2008) by systematically determining the consistent phase-space distribution function for freeze-out through second order in gradients, which had not been previously addressed in the literature. It builds on the fourteen-momentum method of DeGroot (1980) and addresses the concern raised by Dusling (2009) that the quadratic ansatz for $\delta f$ may be inappropriate when radiative energy loss dominates. The formalism reduces the number of free parameters in viscous hydrodynamical calculations, which is significant for the extraction of $\eta/s$ from heavy ion collision data (Shen, 2011). The results enable more reliable extraction of $\eta/s$ from anisotropic flow measurements, particularly for higher-order flow harmonics $v_n$, which will be the subject of future work.
