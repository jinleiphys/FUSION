# Long-Range Forces of QCD

> We consider the scattering of two color dipoles (e.g., heavy quarkonium states) at low energy - a QCD analog of Van der Waals interaction. Even though the couplings of the dipoles to the gluon field can be described in perturbation theory, which leads to the potential proportional to (N_c^2-1)/R^{7}, at large distances R the interaction becomes totally non-perturbative. Low-energy QCD theorems are used to evaluate the leading long-distance contribution \sim (N_f^2-1)/(11N_c - 2N_f)^2 R^{-5/2} exp(-2 μR) (μis the Goldstone boson mass), which is shown to arise from the correlated two-boson exchange. The sum rule which relates the overall strength of the interaction to the energy density of QCD vacuum is derived. Surprisingly, we find that when the size of the dipoles shrinks to zero (the heavy quark limit in the case of quarkonia), the non-perturbative part of the interaction vanishes more slowly than the perturbative part as a consequence of scale anomaly. As an application, we evaluate elastic πJ/ψand πJ/ψ\to πψ' cross sections.

# Long-Range Forces of QCD

## Key claim

The paper claims that the interaction between small color dipoles (such as heavy quarkonium states) at large distances is dominated by non-perturbative effects arising from correlated two-pion exchange, and that in the heavy quark limit the non-perturbative part vanishes more slowly than the perturbative part as a consequence of scale anomaly, a result that holds even though the perturbative coupling of dipoles to gluons can be described by standard methods.

## Method

The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) to express the scattering amplitude of two color dipoles in terms of Wilson coefficients and matrix elements of local operators, specifically the square of the chromo-electric field. They then employ a spectral representation method to evaluate the correlator of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor, matching the perturbative two-gluon contribution at high invariant masses to the non-perturbative two-pion contribution at low invariant masses using low-energy QCD theorems from Voloshin and Zakharov (VZ). The pion scalar form factor is computed via the Omnes-Muskhelishvili equation using experimental pi-pi phase shifts, and the sum rule relating the spectral density integral to the QCD vacuum energy density is imposed to fix the matching scale.

## Key numbers

- Perturbative potential between color dipoles: V_pert(R) ~ -g^4 (N_c^2-1)/R^7, specifically V^pt(R) = -g^4 (d2_bar a_0^2/epsilon_0)^2 (23/(8 pi^3)) (1/R^7) for N=3
- Non-perturbative long-distance potential from correlated two-pion exchange: V^{pi pi}(R) ~ -(d2_bar a_0^2/epsilon_0)^2 (4 pi^2/b)^2 (3/2) (2 m_pi)^4 (m_pi^{1/2}/(4 pi R)^{5/2}) exp(-2 m_pi R), where b = (1/3)(11N_c - 2N_f) = 9 for N_c=3, N_f=3
- QCD vacuum energy density: epsilon_vac ~ -(0.24 GeV)^4 from the Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov (SVZ) sum rule analysis
- Matching scale s_0 where physical and perturbative spectral densities are consistent with both the low-energy theorem and duality: sqrt(s_0) = 2 GeV
- For J/psi: alpha_s(J/psi) = 0.87, Bohr radius a_0 = 0.20 fm, Rydberg energy epsilon_0 = 642 MeV, charm quark mass m = 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of quarkonium-quarkonium interactions by Appelquist and Fischler (1978) and Bhanot and Peskin (1979) to include non-perturbative effects at large distances. It builds on the low-energy theorem of Voloshin and Zakharov (1980) linking the trace of the energy-momentum tensor to two-pion states, and uses the sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) relating spectral densities to the QCD vacuum condensate. The results enable evaluation of elastic pi-J/psi and pi-J/psi to pi-psi' cross sections relevant for J/psi suppression in heavy ion collisions, and suggest that the interaction between very small color dipoles becomes totally non-perturbative in the heavy quark limit.
