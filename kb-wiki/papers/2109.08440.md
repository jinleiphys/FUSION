# Quantum Kinetic Theory with Vector and Axial Gauge Fields

> In this paper we introduce the axial gauge field to the framework of the quantum kinetic theory with vector gauge field in the massless limit. Treating axial-gauge field on an equal footing with the vector-gauge field, we construct a consistent solution to the kinetic equations up to the first order in gradient expansion or equivalently the semi-classical expansion. The intuitive extension of quantum kinetic theory presented in this work provides a natural generalization, and turns out to give rise to the covariant anomaly and the covariant currents. The corresponding consistent currents can be obtained from the covariant ones by adding the Chern-Simons current. We use the consistent currents to calculate various correlation functions among currents and energy-momentum tensor in equilibrium state.

## Key claim

The paper extends the framework of quantum kinetic theory for massless fermions to include an axial gauge field on equal footing with the vector gauge field, constructing a consistent solution to the kinetic equations up to first order in gradient expansion; the resulting covariant currents give rise to the covariant anomaly, and the corresponding consistent currents (obtained by adding the Chern-Simons current) are used to calculate correlation functions in equilibrium.

## Method

The authors start from the Dirac equation for a massless chiral fermion coupled to both vector and axial gauge fields, then derive the quantum kinetic equations for the Wigner function in the collisionless limit under the assumption of constant field strengths. They solve the kinetic equations order by order in a gradient expansion (equivalent to a semiclassical expansion in Planck's constant), obtaining the Wigner function up to first order, and integrate over kinetic momentum to obtain the chiral currents and the canonical energy-momentum tensor. Correlation functions among currents and between currents and the energy-momentum tensor are then computed by taking functional derivatives of the consistent currents with respect to the gauge potentials.

## Key numbers

* The chiral vortical effect coefficient for right/left-handed fermions is \(\xi_s = \frac{s}{12\pi^2}\left(\frac{\pi^2}{\beta^2} + 3\mu_s^2\right)\), where \(s = \pm 1\) and \(\mu_s = \mu + s\mu_5\).
* The chiral magnetic effect coefficient for right/left-handed fermions is \(\xi_{Bs} = \frac{s}{4\pi^2}\mu_s\).
* The vector current anomaly equation is \(\partial_\mu j^\mu = \frac{1}{2\pi^2}(\vec{E}_5 \cdot \vec{B} + \vec{E} \cdot \vec{B}_5)\).
* The axial current anomaly equation is \(\partial_\mu j^\mu_5 = \frac{1}{2\pi^2}(\vec{E} \cdot \vec{B} + \vec{E}_5 \cdot \vec{B}_5)\).
* The two-point correlation function \(\langle \tilde{j}_{\text{cons}}^i(k) \tilde{j}_{\text{cons}}^j(-k) \rangle = \frac{i\mu_5 \epsilon^{ijk} k^k}{2\pi^2}\).
* The three-point correlation function \(\langle \tilde{j}_{\text{cons}}^i(q) \tilde{j}_{5,\text{cons}}^0(k-q) \tilde{j}_{\text{cons}}^j(-k) \rangle = -i\epsilon^{ijk} \frac{k^k}{2\pi^2}\).

## Context

This work generalizes the chiral kinetic theory developed by Son and Stephanov (2012) and others to include axial gauge fields, which were previously treated only in the context of anomalous hydrodynamics by Son and Surowka (2009) and Neiman and Oz (2010). The framework enables the derivation of correlation functions among vector and axial currents and the energy-momentum tensor in equilibrium, providing a kinetic-theory alternative to field-theoretic and holographic calculations by Landsteiner, Megias, and Pena-Benitez (2011). The results also extend the first-order solution of Yang and Lin (2020) to the case with axial gauge fields, and the calculated correlation functions agree with those obtained from field theory and holography.
