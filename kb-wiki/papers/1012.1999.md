# Heavy Quarkonium at finite temperature

> 6 pages, Invited Plenary Talk at the Conference "Quark Confinenement and the Hadron Spectrum IX", Madrid (Spain) August 30-September 3, 2010. To appear in AIP proceedings

## Key claim

The paper argues that quarkonium dissociation in a hot QCD medium is primarily caused by the appearance of a thermal decay width (imaginary part of the potential) rather than by color screening of the real part of the potential, with the caveat that this picture holds in the weak-coupling regime where a hierarchy of scales can be established.

## Method

The author constructs a hierarchy of effective field theories (EFTs) for heavy quarkonium at finite temperature, starting from QCD and integrating out scales sequentially: the heavy quark mass (NRQCD), the inverse distance scale (pNRQCD), and then the temperature scale (pNRQCD_HTL). The real-time evolution of a static quark-antiquark pair is studied in a thermal medium of gluons and light quarks, and the potential, energy levels, and thermal decay width are derived at order m alpha_s^5 for temperatures satisfying the hierarchy m >> m alpha_s >> pi T >> m alpha_s^2 >> m_D.

## Key numbers

* The thermal correction to the color-singlet potential real part includes a term (4π/9) α_s² r T², where r is the quark-antiquark distance and T the temperature.
* The Debye mass is given by m_D² = g² T² (1 + n_f/6), where n_f is the number of light quarks.
* For bottomonium 1S states at LHC, the relevant scales are approximately: m_b ≈ 5 GeV > m_b α_s ≈ 1.5 GeV > πT ≈ 1 GeV > m α_s² ≈ 0.5 GeV ≳ m_D.
* The leading thermal correction to the quarkonium energy is δE_{n,l}^{(thermal)} = (2π/9) α_s² T² a_0 [3n² - l(l+1) + 8/3], where a_0 = 3/(2m α_s) is the Bohr radius.
* The leading thermal width is Γ_{n,l}^{(thermal)} = (4 + 832/(81 n²)) α_s³ T, which is linear in temperature.
* The dissociation temperature scales parametrically as π T_melting ∼ m g^{4/3}.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature EFT framework of NRQCD (Caswell 1985) and pNRQCD (Pineda 1997, Brambilla 1999) to finite temperature, providing a rigorous definition of the quarkonium potential in a hot medium. It contradicts the earlier screening-based dissociation picture of Matsui and Satz (1986) by showing that thermal width from Landau damping and singlet-to-octet breakup dominates over screening. The results enable systematic calculations of bottomonium properties at LHC energies and provide a framework for extending the analysis to nonperturbative regimes.
