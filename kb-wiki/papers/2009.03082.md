# Dielectric Skyrmions

> We consider the dielectric Skyrme model proposed recently, with and without the addition of the standard pion mass term. Then we write down Bogomol'nyi-type energy bounds for both the massless and massive cases. We further show that, except for when taking the strict BPS limit, the Skyrmions are made of 3 orthogonal dipoles that can always be placed in their attractive channel and form bound states. Finally, we study the model numerically and discover that, long before realistic binding energies are reached, the Skyrmions become bound states of well-separated point-particle-like Skyrmions. By going sufficiently close to the BPS limit, we are able to obtain classical binding energies of realistic values compared with experiments.

## Key claim

The dielectric Skyrme model, where coupling constants are promoted to field-dependent functions, can interpolate between the standard Skyrme model (too strongly bound) and a BPS limit (unbound for multi-Skyrmions), and by tuning the freezing parameter \(n_\star\) sufficiently close to but not at the BPS limit, classical binding energies of realistic values compared with experiments can be obtained.

## Method

The author promotes the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\) to a function \(f(n_0)\) that freezes to unity above a threshold \(n_\star\) while keeping the Skyrme coupling \(e(n_0)=1\), then derives Bogomol'nyi-type energy bounds for both the massless and massive (with standard pion mass term) versions. Numerical solutions for multi-Skyrmions are obtained by solving the full partial differential equations of motion, and inter-Skyrmion forces are analyzed via linearization of the hedgehog equation to show that Skyrmions behave as three orthogonal dipoles.

## Key numbers

* The standard (massless) Skyrme model has a 1-Skyrmion energy about 23% above the Faddeev-Skyrme bound of \(E \geq 12\pi^2|B|\).
* Typical binding energies in the standard Skyrme model are of order 10% per nucleon, compared to about 1% for real world nuclei.
* The Bogomol'nyi bound for the massless near-BPS dielectric model is \(E_{\rm BPS} = 12\pi^2|B|\mathcal{F}(n_\star)\), where \(\mathcal{F}(-1)=1\) and \(\lim_{n_\star\to 1^-}\mathcal{F}(n_\star)\to\infty\).
* For the massive case with pion mass parameter \(m\), the combined bound is \(E \geq 12\pi^2|B|\mathcal{G}(n_\star,m)\) with \(a \simeq 0.755\,\mathcal{F}^2(n_\star)/m\).
* The interaction potential between two Skyrmions at separation \(R\) is \(V_{\rm int} \simeq (q^2 e^{-mR}/(16\pi R))\,\widehat{\mathbf{R}}\cdot\mathcal{O}\widehat{\mathbf{R}}\).

## Context

This work extends the dielectric Skyrme model proposed by Adam, Oles, and Wereszczynski (2020), which is a special case of the Ferreira model (2017) where coupling constants are promoted to field-space matrices. It addresses the long-standing binding-energy problem in Skyrme model research, following earlier approaches including the BPS-Skyrme model by Adam, Sanchez-Guillen, and Wereszczynski (2010), the Harland model (2013) with a saturable bound for \(B=1\), and the Gillard-Harland-Speight model (2015). The near-BPS dielectric model enables interpolation between the standard Skyrme model and a BPS theory, allowing systematic exploration of binding energies that could make Skyrmion-based nuclear physics more realistic.
