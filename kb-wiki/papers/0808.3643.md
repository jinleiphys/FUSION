# Bayesian Methods for Parameter Estimation in Effective Field Theories

> We demonstrate and explicate Bayesian methods for fitting the parameters that encode the impact of short-distance physics on observables in effective field theories (EFTs). We use Bayes' theorem together with the principle of maximum entropy to account for the prior information that these parameters should be natural, i.e.O(1) in appropriate units. Marginalization can then be employed to integrate the resulting probability density function (pdf) over the EFT parameters that are not of specific interest in the fit. We also explore marginalization over the order of the EFT calculation, M, and over the variable, R, that encodes the inherent ambiguity in the notion that these parameters are O(1). This results in a very general formula for the pdf of the EFT parameters of interest given a data set, D. We use this formula and the simpler "augmented chi-squared" in a toy problem for which we generate pseudo-data. These Bayesian methods, when used in combination with the "naturalness prior", facilitate reliable extractions of EFT parameters in cases where chi-squared methods are ambiguous at best. We also examine the problem of extracting the nucleon mass in the chiral limit, M_0, and the nucleon sigma term, from pseudo-data on the nucleon mass as a function of the pion mass. We find that Bayesian techniques can provide reliable information on M_0, even if some of the data points used for the extraction lie outside the region of applicability of the EFT.

## Key claim

Bayesian methods with a Gaussian "naturalness prior" for EFT parameters, combined with marginalization over the EFT order \(M\) and the scale \(R\) that encodes the ambiguity in "\({\cal O}(1)\)", yield reliable extractions of low-energy constants even when standard \(\chi^2\) methods are ambiguous, and can provide information on parameters such as the nucleon mass in the chiral limit even when some data lie outside the EFT's region of applicability.

## Method

The authors use Bayes' theorem with a Gaussian likelihood for the data and a Gaussian prior for the EFT parameters derived from the principle of maximum entropy under the constraint that the ensemble average of the sum of squares of the parameters is \((M+1)R^2\). They marginalize over higher-order nuisance parameters, over the EFT order \(M\) (with a uniform prior), and over the scale \(R\) (with a prior uniform in \(\log R\)), to obtain a final posterior pdf for the parameters of interest. They apply this formalism and a simpler "augmented \(\chi^2\)" (which adds a penalty \(\sum_i a_i^2 / R^2\) to the usual \(\chi^2\)) to pseudo-data generated from a toy function and from a chiral perturbation theory expression for the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- For the toy function \(g(x) = (1/2 + \tan(\pi x/2))^2\), the radius of convergence of its Taylor series is \(\rho = 1\); the first five expansion coefficients are approximately \(0.25, 1.57, 2.47, 1.29, 4.06\), with an rms value of about 3.
- Pseudo-data for the nucleon mass as a function of the pion mass are generated in the range \(m = 200\)–\(500\) MeV.
- Bayesian extractions of the nucleon mass in the chiral limit \(M_0\) from pseudo-data generated in different ranges of \(m\) yield consistent results, even for fit windows extending significantly above the pion mass where the \(\chi\)PT form ceases to be valid.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian "constrained curve fitting" methods previously applied to lattice QCD data by Lepage (2001) and Morningstar (2001), and to the static-quark self energy by Trottier et al. (2001), by developing a general strategy for parameter estimation in EFTs based solely on the expectation of naturalness. It also builds on Bayesian model averaging techniques discussed by Guttman (2005) and Rafferty (1997). The paper enables a rigorous accounting of theoretical uncertainty from higher-order EFT terms and from the ambiguity in defining "\({\cal O}(1)\)" parameters, applicable to a wide variety of EFT situations including chiral perturbation theory.
