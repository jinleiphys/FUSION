# Phenomenology of kaonic atoms and other strange hadronic atoms

> Recent optical-potential studies of the phenomenology of kaonic atoms are reviewed. It is shown that the data can be fitted by a complex optical potential with either a relatively shallow attractive component (about -50 MeV at nuclear-matter density), as suggested by a self consistent application of chirally motivated coupled-channels models, or a relatively deep attractive component (about -180 MeV at nuclear-matter density) as suggested by a mean-field extrapolation of a phenomenological low-density expansion. Both classes of these optical potentials, due to their strongly absorptive component, predict similarly structured relatively narrow `deeply bound' kaonic-atom states with widths saturating at about 2 MeV. Possible formation reactions are briefly discussed. The more speculative case for relatively narrow deeply bound kaonic nuclear states is briefly mentioned. Recent works on other strange hadronic atom systems, for the negatively charged Sigma and Cascade hyperons, are also discussed.

# Phenomenology of kaonic atoms and other strange hadronic atoms

## Key claim

The paper reviews that kaonic atom data can be fitted by a complex optical potential with either a relatively shallow attractive component (about -50 MeV at nuclear-matter density) as suggested by chirally motivated coupled-channels models, or a relatively deep attractive component (about -180 MeV at nuclear-matter density) as suggested by a mean-field extrapolation; both classes predict similarly structured narrow "deeply bound" kaonic-atom states with widths saturating at about 2 MeV, but the existence of narrow deeply bound kaonic *nuclear* states remains more speculative and likely faces widths of 30-40 MeV.

## Method

The paper reviews optical-potential fits to X-ray data from kaonic atoms, comparing two approaches: a hybrid relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with a phenomenological density-dependent (DD) imaginary part, and a self-consistent chirally motivated coupled-channels approach incorporating medium modifications. For Sigma and Cascade hyperon atoms, the author discusses density-dependent analyses of atomic shift and width data, and evaluates proposed X-ray measurements for constraining the Xi-nucleus interaction.

## Key numbers

- Re V_opt depth for kaonic atoms: about -180 MeV at nuclear-matter density (rho_0) for the RMF+DD best-fit potential, and about -60 MeV at rho_0 for the self-consistent chirally inspired potential with an added phenomenological component
- Im V_opt depth for kaonic atoms: about 60 MeV in the best-fit RMF+DD potentials
- Widths of deeply bound kaonic atomic states saturate at about 2 MeV
- For the 2p level in kaonic Pb, the width saturates at about 20% of the nominal Im b_0 value of 0.92 fm
- Derived pi-Lambda-Sigma coupling constant: f^2_{pi Lambda Sigma} / (4 pi) = 0.048 +/- 0.005 +/- 0.004
- Xi-nucleus potential depth V_0^(Xi) estimated between 14 and 20 MeV from (K-, K+) counter experiments
- Binding energy of Xi- atomic state in 12C: B_Xi-(12C) = 0.58 +/- 0.14 MeV (from emulsion events, interpreted as 1s capture)
- Binding energy of Xi- atomic state in 14N: B_Xi-(14N) = 0.35 +/- 0.20 MeV (from emulsion events)
- Binding energy of Xi- atomic state in 16O: B_Xi-(16O) = 0.31 +/- 0.23 MeV (from emulsion events)

## Context

This review extends the phenomenological optical-potential analyses of kaonic atoms by Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997) and the self-consistent chirally motivated calculations by Ramos and Oset (2000) and Lutz (1998). It also builds on the discovery of deeply bound pionic atomic states at GSI by Gilg et al. (1999), which demonstrated that such states can be observed via recoilless reactions. The paper enables future experimental searches for deeply bound kaonic atomic states using low-energy K- beams at DAΦNE, and motivates X-ray measurements of Xi- atoms to constrain the Xi-nucleus interaction, which is crucial for assessing whether strange hadronic matter (Schaffner et al., 1993) can exist.
