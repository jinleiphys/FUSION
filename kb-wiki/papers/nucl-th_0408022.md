# Angular analysis of bremsstrahlung in alpha decay

> A new quantum electrodynamical method of calculations of bremsstrahlung spectra in the $α$-decay of heavy nuclei taking into account the angle between the directions of $α$-particle motion (or its tunneling) and photon emission is presented. The angular bremsstrahlung spectra for $^{210}{Po}$ have been obtained for the first time. According to calculations, the bremsstrahlung in the $α$-decay of this nucleus depends extremely weakly on the angle. Taking into account nuclear forces, such dependence is not changed visibly. An analytical formula of the angular dependence of the bremsstrahlung spectra is proposed and gives its harmonic behavior. The extremal values of the angle, at which the bremsstrahlung has maximal and minimal values, has been found.

# Angular analysis of bremsstrahlung in alpha decay

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung intensity in alpha decay of 210Po depends extremely weakly on the angle between the alpha-particle direction and photon emission, and this angular dependence is not visibly changed when nuclear forces are included. The authors propose that the angular dependence follows a harmonic behavior, with the first approximation (l=0) showing no angular dependence at all, and the second approximation (l=1) introducing a cos²β dependence.

## Method

The authors develop a quantum electrodynamical method using perturbation theory to calculate bremsstrahlung spectra in alpha decay, starting from a matrix element for the transition between initial and final states of the compound system (alpha-particle plus daughter nucleus). They introduce a simplified transformation that reduces the angular formalism to a form depending only on the angle β between the alpha-particle direction and photon emission, using spherical wave expansion of the plane wave and separating the integration into an internal region (where nuclear forces act) and an external Coulomb region. The radial integrals are evaluated using Coulomb wave functions, and the convergence problem in the asymptotic region is resolved by reducing all integrals to linear combinations of two convergent radial integrals.

## Key numbers

- For 210Po, the bremsstrahlung from the internal spatial region (inside Rc) is smaller by a factor of 10⁻²² to 10⁻²⁴ compared to bremsstrahlung from the external Coulomb region
- The bremsstrahlung probability in the first approximation (l=0) is given by W_{l=0}(w) = (2/3) N₀ k_f w |J_c(0,w)|², which shows no dependence on angle β
- The bremsstrahlung probability in the second approximation (l=1) is W^{(l=1)}(w,β) = 6 N₀ k_f w |J_c(1,w)|² cos²β
- The total bremsstrahlung probability in the second approximation is W_{l=1}(w,β) = W_{l=0}(w) |1 - N(w) cosβ|², where N(w) = 3i J_c(1,w)/J_c(0,w)
- For reliable values of the first 2-3 digits of the bremsstrahlung spectrum for 210Po, one million oscillations of the sub-integral function must be taken into account

## Context

This work extends earlier quantum electrodynamical approaches to bremsstrahlung in alpha decay, including the multipolar method of Maydanyuk (2003) and the dipole approximation models of Papenbrock (1998) and Takigawa (1999), which only provided angle-averaged spectra. The paper provides the first angular bremsstrahlung spectra for 210Po, enabling comparison with experimental data from D'Arrigo (1994) at 90° and Kasagi (1997) at 25°, which showed differences that motivated angular analysis. The method is designed to be applicable to all existing experimental data for spherical nuclei and can be extended to realistic alpha-nucleus potentials, enabling future extraction of information about tunneling dynamics from bremsstrahlung spectra.
