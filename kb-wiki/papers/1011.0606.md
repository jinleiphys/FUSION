# Correlation Widths in Quantum--Chaotic Scattering

> An important parameter to characterize the scattering matrix S for quantum-chaotic scattering is the width Gamma_{corr} of the S-matrix autocorrelation function. We show that the "Weisskopf estimate" d/(2pi) sum_c T_c (where d is the mean resonance spacing, T_c with 0 <= T_c <= 1 the "transmission coefficient" in channel c and where the sum runs over all channels) provides a very good approximation to Gamma_{corr} even when the number of channels is small. That same conclusion applies also to the cross-section correlation function.

# Correlation Widths in Quantum–Chaotic Scattering

## Key claim

The Weisskopf estimate Gamma_W = d/(2π) Σ_c T_c provides a good approximation to the S-matrix autocorrelation width Gamma_corr not only in the Ericson regime of strongly overlapping resonances (Σ_c T_c >> 1) but also for small numbers of channels and for weakly overlapping resonances, with deviations from unity typically less than 10 percent for Σ_c T_c ≳ 4.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluated the exact analytical expression for the S-matrix autocorrelation function (derived by Verbaarschot (1985) from a Gaussian orthogonal ensemble random-matrix model) using variable transformations from Verbaarschot (1986) and Dietz et al. (2010). For fixed numbers of channels Λ and fixed total transmission T = Σ_c T_c, they generated random sets of transmission coefficients {T_1, ..., T_Λ} and computed the full width at half maximum Gamma_corr of the correlation function C_{12}(ε) for the off-diagonal S-matrix element, comparing it to the Weisskopf estimate Gamma_W.

## Key numbers

- The Weisskopf estimate is Gamma_W = d/(2π) Σ_c T_c, where d is the mean resonance spacing and T_c are transmission coefficients (0 ≤ T_c ≤ 1).
- For case I (non-sorted transmission coefficients), the average ratio Gamma_corr/Gamma_W deviates from unity by more than 10 percent only for T ≲ 8.
- For case II (incident and outgoing channels are the dominant ones), deviations from unity are less than 10 percent unless T ≲ 3.
- For case III (incident and outgoing channels are the most weakly coupled), the average ratio is below unity for all T, with the largest deviations among the three cases.
- For Λ equal transmission coefficients, the ratio Gamma_corr/Gamma_W is very close to unity for all T and Λ shown.
- Root-mean-square deviations of Gamma_corr/Gamma_W from unity are less than 0.1 for all T and Λ considered in cases I and II, and for T ≳ 3 in case III.
- Microwave experiment data (Dietz et al. 2010, 2008) with two dominant channels and 300 weakly coupled absorptive channels gave Gamma_corr/Gamma_W ratios above unity for all T, with deviations around 5 percent for T ≳ 4.
- A superconducting microwave billiard experiment (Alt et al. 1995) with Λ = 3 and T ≈ 0.55 yielded Gamma_corr/Gamma_W ≈ 1.13.

## Context

This work extends the analytical results of Verbaarschot (1985, 1986) and Harney et al. (who showed the Weisskopf estimate works for many weakly coupled channels) by systematically testing the Weisskopf estimate across the full parameter space of channel number and coupling strengths. The results validate the use of the Weisskopf estimate in microwave experiments on quantum-chaotic scattering (Dietz et al. 2010, Schäfer et al. 2010, Alt et al. 1995) and contradict the Moldauer–Simonius sum rule (Moldauer 1969, Simonius 1974) as a predictor of the correlation width when any T_c is close to unity. The findings enable experimentalists to confidently use the simple Weisskopf formula for extracting mean resonance spacings or transmission coefficients from measured correlation widths in compound-nucleus scattering, mesoscopic transport, and microwave billiard experiments.
