# Meson correlation functions at high temperature QCD: $SU(2)_{CS}$ symmetry vs. free quarks

> We report on the progress of understanding spatial correlation functions in high temperature QCD. We study isovector meson operators in $N_f=2$ QCD using domain-wall fermions on lattices of $N_s=32$ and different quark masses. It has previously been found that at $\sim 2T_c$ these observables are not only chirally symmetric but in addition approximately $SU(2)_{CS}$ and $SU(4)$ symmetric. In this study we increase the temperature up to $5T_c$ and can identify convergence towards an asymptotically free scenario at very high temperatures.

## Key claim
At temperatures around 2 T_c, spatial meson correlators exhibit approximate SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetry, but at very high temperatures above 700 MeV the correlators converge toward the free quark limit where SU(2)_CS is broken, indicating that the dynamics near 2 T_c is dominated by chromo-electric interactions rather than free deconfined quarks.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2 mass-degenerate flavors of Möbius domain-wall fermions on 32^3 x N_t lattices, using tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action. They computed spatial correlation functions of local isovector meson bilinears (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, tensor-vector, and axial-tensor-vector) by summing over orthogonal lattice slices in x, y, t directions. The temperature was varied from approximately 1.2 T_c to 5.5 T_c by changing the strong coupling beta and the temporal extent N_t, with quark masses between 2-15 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The (pseudo)critical temperature for the ensembles is T_c = 175 ± 5 MeV
- Temperatures accessed range from T ≈ 1.2 T_c (≈220 MeV) to T ≈ 5.5 T_c (≈960 MeV)
- At T ≈ 380 MeV (≈2.2 T_c), SU(2)_CS symmetry breaking effects remain at the level of 5%
- The free quark limit is approached for interacting quarks at temperatures T > 700 MeV (≈4 T_c)
- Lattice spacings range from 0.113 fm (beta=4.10) to 0.051 fm (beta=4.50)

## Context
This work extends previous findings by Rohrhofer (2017) that spatial correlators at ~2 T_c show approximate SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetry, by pushing the temperature up to ~5.5 T_c. The study builds on earlier low-mode truncation studies by Denissenya (2014, 2015) which revealed these symmetries upon removing near-zero Dirac modes, and on the theoretical framework by Glozman (2014, 2015) showing that chromo-electric interactions are SU(2)_CS invariant while chromo-magnetic interactions break it. The results suggest that at T ~ 2 T_c the system behaves as an SU(2)_CS symmetric stringy fluid rather than free deconfined quarks, with implications for the QCD phase diagram including finite chemical potential as discussed by Glozman (2017).
