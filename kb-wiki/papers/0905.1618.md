# Anomalous enhancements of low-energy fusion rates in plasmas: the role of ion momentum distributions and inhomogeneous screening

> Non-resonant fusion cross-sections significantly higher than corresponding theoretical predictions are observed in low-energy experiments with deuterated matrix target. Models based on thermal effects, electron screening, or quantum-effect dispersion relations have been proposed to explain these anomalous results: none of them appears to satisfactory reproduce the experiments. Velocity distributions are fundamental for the reaction rates and deviations from the Maxwellian limit could play a central role in explaining the enhancement. We examine two effects: an increase of the tail of the target Deuteron momentum distribution due to the Galitskii-Yakimets quantum uncertainty effect, which broadens the energy-momentum relation; and spatial fluctuations of the Debye-Hückel radius leading to an effective increase of electron screening. Either effect leads to larger reaction rates especially large at energies below a few keV, reducing the discrepancy between observations and theoretical expectations.

# Anomalous enhancements of low-energy fusion rates in plasmas: the role of ion momentum distributions and inhomogeneous screening

## Key claim

Either the Galitskii-Yakimets quantum uncertainty broadening of the target deuteron momentum distribution (using an Ion Sphere Model collisional cross section) or spatial fluctuations of the Debye-Hückel radius leading to a modified screening potential can separately reduce the discrepancy between observed low-energy fusion rates in deuterated metal targets and theoretical predictions, but the authors treat these as upper-limit estimates and note that further work is needed for quantitative comparisons to other targets.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluate the thermal average ⟨σv_rel⟩ for D+D fusion in a tantalum matrix, incorporating a Lorentzian-broadened energy-momentum relation (quantum uncertainty effect) with either a Coulomb or Ion Sphere Model collisional cross section. Separately, they apply a modified Debye-Hückel screening potential derived from superstatistics (entropic index q=0) to compute the barrier penetration factor and resulting astrophysical S-factor, comparing both approaches to experimental thick-target yield data from Raiola (2002).

## Key numbers

- Target parameters for deuterated tantalum: density n_D = 0.701×10²² cm⁻³, temperature T = 10°C (k_bT = 0.0244 eV), Wigner-Seitz radius a_ws = 3.24×10⁻⁸ cm, resulting in coupling parameter Γ = 182
- Experimental screening potentials needed to fit data: U_e = 309 eV (Raiola 2002), U_e = 322 eV (Czerski 2001), U_e = 340 eV (Raiola 2002a, Bonomo 2003), range U_e = 210–460 eV (Huke 2007, 2008a), all much larger than the adiabatic limit U_ad = 28 eV
- With the modified Debye-Hückel potential (q=0), a screening potential U_q ≈ 100 eV reproduces the experimental data, three times lower than the standard Debye-Hückel value of U_DH ≈ 300 eV
- The quantum uncertainty effect with Ion Sphere Model cross section (α₁=1) produces rate enhancement below E_b ∼ 10 keV, while the Coulomb cross section case enhances rates only below E_b ∼ 2 keV; experimental enhancement begins below E_b ∼ 4–6 keV
- Bare astrophysical factor used: S(ε) ≈ S₀ = 43 keV b, valid within ≈6% for E_b ≤ 10 keV

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of anomalously large fusion cross sections observed in deuterated metal targets (first reported by Yuki (1998), Kasagi (2002), and others), which cannot be explained by standard electron screening at the adiabatic limit (Salpeter 1969) or by Maxwellian thermal motion (Filippov 2003, Starostin 2003). The quantum uncertainty effect was previously proposed by Starostin (2003, 2005) and Coraddu (2006) using Coulomb collisional cross sections, but those calculations only produced enhancement below ∼2 keV; the present work extends this to higher energies using the Ion Sphere Model for strongly coupled plasmas (Ichimaru 1982, 1993). The modified Debye-Hückel screening follows the superstatistics approach of Quarati and Scarfone (2007) and yields screening potentials consistent with Thomas-Fermi calculations by Saltzmann and Hass (2008) for deuterated copper (163 eV). The authors identify as open questions the determination of the fraction of quasi-free deuterons and the derivation of the entropic index q from the plasma equation of state without free parameters.
