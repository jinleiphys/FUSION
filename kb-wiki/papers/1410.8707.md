# Functional renormalization group study of nuclear and neutron matter

> A chiral model based on nucleons interacting via boson exchange is investigated. Fluctuation effects are included consistently beyond the mean-field approximation in the framework of the functional renormalization group. The liquid-gas phase transition of symmetric nuclear matter is studied in detail. No sign of a chiral restoration transition is found up to temperatures of about 100 MeV and densities of at least three times the density of normal nuclear matter. Moreover, the model is extended to asymmetric nuclear matter and the constraints from neutron star observations are discussed.

## Key claim

The functional renormalization group (FRG) treatment of a chiral nucleon-meson model finds no chiral restoration transition in symmetric nuclear matter up to temperatures of about 100 MeV and densities of at least three times nuclear saturation density, contrary to mean-field calculations that predict a first-order chiral transition at lower densities. For pure neutron matter, the chiral condensate remains nonzero up to densities of about seven times nuclear saturation density, contradicting perturbative chiral effective field theory results that would predict restoration around three times saturation density.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral nucleon-meson (ChNM) Lagrangian with nucleons, pions, sigma, omega, and rho mesons, with parameters fitted to empirical nuclear matter properties. They include fluctuations of nucleons, pions, and the sigma field beyond the mean-field approximation using the functional renormalization group flow equation, starting from an ultraviolet cutoff of 1.4 GeV and flowing to the full quantum effective action. The flow equation sums up pion exchange processes and multi-nucleon forces generated by the nonlinear effective potential, and the resulting equation of state is computed for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at varying temperatures and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy range explored: 29 MeV ≤ E_sym ≤ 33 MeV
- No chiral restoration found up to temperatures of about 100 MeV and densities of at least three times n0 in symmetric nuclear matter
- In pure neutron matter, chiral symmetry remains broken up to densities of about seven times nuclear saturation density
- Ultraviolet cutoff for the FRG flow: Λ = 1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the mean-field analysis of the chiral nucleon-meson model by Floerchinger and Weise (2012) by including fluctuations via the functional renormalization group. The results contradict perturbative in-medium chiral effective field theory calculations by Kaiser (2009) and Krueger and collaborators (2013), which predict chiral restoration in neutron matter around three times saturation density. The equation of state is compared with the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) equation of state (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) and quantum Monte Carlo computations by Armani and collaborators (2011), showing good agreement. The model enables studies of neutron star structure and mass-radius constraints from observations.
