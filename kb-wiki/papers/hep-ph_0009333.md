# Droplet Formation in Quark-Gluon Plasma at Low Temperatures and High Densities

> Considering the low-temperature ($T$) and high-baryon-number-density ($n_B$) region of the QCD phase diagram, we present a model for the first-order phase transition between the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and the recently proposed colour superconducting phase. We study nucleation of a droplet of the superconducting phase within the metastable QGP gas. Numerical results for the activation energy, radius and other physical parameters of the droplets, at various temperatures, densities and gap parameters, are given. We have estimated the latent heat of the phase transition. In the $T-n_B$ plane, we are able to demarcate the region of the superconducting phase.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological model for the first-order phase transition between the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and the colour superconducting phase at low temperatures and high baryon number densities, claiming that droplet formation in this transition has not been discussed previously in the literature. The authors note that their results are relatively stable with respect to changes in baryon number density and temperature, in contrast to the high sensitivity seen in analogous Bose-Einstein condensation studies.

## Method

The authors model a gas of weakly interacting quarks, antiquarks and gluons, treating interactions to lowest order in the strong coupling constant alpha_s (which is set to zero for numerical calculations). They consider a spherical droplet of the colour superconducting phase forming within the metastable QGP gas, where quarks near the Fermi surface form Cooper pairs in the colour antitriplet channel. The activation energy for droplet formation is calculated as the difference between the Helmholtz free energies of the final (superconducting) and initial (QGP) states, with the droplet radius treated as a free parameter. The condition of pressure equality between the droplet and surrounding gas fixes the equilibrium droplet radius.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number density range considered: 0.5 to 3 fm^{-3} (normal nuclear matter density is 0.17 fm^{-3})
- Temperature range considered: 0 to 100 MeV
- Quark chemical potential varies over approximately 250 to 600 MeV; baryon chemical potential varies over approximately 750 to 1800 MeV
- Superconducting gap parameter values considered: 1, 10, 50 and 100 MeV
- For Delta = 100 MeV, the Pippard coherence length xi is approximately 0.6 fm (from xi = 1/(pi * Delta))
- Latent heat of the phase transition for Delta = 100 MeV is approximately 150 to 250 MeV, increasing with baryon number density
- Droplet radius R_d is found to be larger than the Pippard coherence length at low temperatures

## Context

This work extends the study of droplet formation in QCD phase transitions to the previously unexplored QGP to colour-superconducting phase transition at high baryon number densities. It is methodologically similar to the approach of Cote and Kharchenko (1999) for Bose-Einstein condensation in trapped atomic hydrogen, but applied to vastly different temperature and density scales. The paper contrasts with Alford, Rajagopal and Wilczek (1998), who studied droplet formation in the hadronic matter to 2SC phase transition at T=0 (where chiral symmetry is restored inside their droplets), and with Neergaard and Madsen (2000), who studied droplet formation in the QGP to hadronic matter transition at low baryon densities. The results provide numerical support for the phase structure of QCD in the low-temperature, high-density regime relevant to neutron-star cores.
