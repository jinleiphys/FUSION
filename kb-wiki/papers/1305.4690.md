# Does a proton "bubble" structure exist in the low-lying states of 34Si?

> The possible existence of a "bubble" structure in the proton density of $^{34}$Si has recently attracted a lot of research interest. To examine the existence of the "bubble" structure in low-lying states, we establish a relativistic version of configuration mixing of both particle number and angular momentum projected quadrupole deformed mean-field states and apply this state-of-the-art beyond relativistic mean-field method to study the density distribution of the low-lying states in $^{34}$Si. An excellent agreement with the data of low-spin spectrum and electric multipole transition strengths is achieved without introducing any parameters. We find that the central depression in the proton density is quenched by dynamic quadrupole shape fluctuation, but not as significantly as what has been found in a beyond non-relativistic mean-field study. Our results suggest that the existence of proton "bubble" structure in the low-lying excited $0^+_2$ and $2^+_1$ states is very unlikely.

## Key claim

The authors find that dynamic quadrupole shape fluctuations quench the central depression in the proton density of ³⁴Si, but less so than in a prior non-relativistic study using the SLy4 force; they conclude that a proton "bubble" structure in the low-lying excited 0⁺₂ and 2⁺₁ states is "very unlikely."

## Method

The authors develop a relativistic version of the generator coordinate method (GCM) that includes both particle-number projection (PNP) and angular-momentum projection (AMP) of axially deformed quadrupole mean-field states. They apply this beyond-relativistic-mean-field method to ³⁴Si using the point-coupling relativistic effective force PC-PK1, with pairing treated in the BCS approximation. The wave functions of low-lying states are superpositions of projected states with different intrinsic quadrupole deformations β, and the density distributions, electric monopole (E0) and electric quadrupole (E2) transition strengths are computed directly in the laboratory frame without effective charges.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state (0⁺₁) mean-square radius is 3.09 fm; the 0⁺₂ state has 3.15 fm; the 2⁺₁ state has 3.21 fm.
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the 2⁺₁ state is 9.48 e fm², indicating a predominantly oblate deformation.
- The depletion factor F_max for the proton density is 0.27 for the spherical mean-field (β=0.0), 0.22 for the 0⁺₁ state, 0.04 for the 0⁺₂ state, and 0.03 for the L=0 component of the 2⁺₁ state.
- The depletion factor F_sat for the charge density is 0.04 for the spherical mean-field, 0.01 for the 0⁺₁ state, and -0.10 for the 0⁺₂ state.
- The spin-orbit splitting of the 2p₃/₂-2p₁/₂ doublet in the spherical mean-field calculation is reduced from 2.77 MeV (protons) and 2.66 MeV (neutrons) in ³⁶S to 0.73 MeV (protons) and 0.80 MeV (neutrons) in ³⁴Si.

## Context

This work extends the beyond-mean-field study of ³⁴Si by Yao et al. (2012), which used the non-relativistic Skyrme force SLy4 and found stronger quenching of the central density depression. The present relativistic calculation reproduces the recently measured low-spin spectrum and electric multipole transition strengths (Rotaru et al., 2012) without adjustable parameters, giving confidence in the predicted density distributions. The results suggest that the proton "bubble" structure predicted by mean-field calculations (e.g., Todd-Rutel 2004, Khan 2008, Grasso 2009) is largely washed out in the low-lying excited states by shape mixing, and the findings are expected to be testable at future electron-RIB colliders such as SCRIT and ELISe.
