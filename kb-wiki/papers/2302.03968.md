# Study of $X(6900)$ with unitarized coupled channel scattering amplitudes

> In this paper, we study the resonant state $X(6900)$. The scattering amplitudes of coupled channels, $J/ψJ/ψ$-$J/ψψ(2S)$-$J/ψψ(3770)$, are constructed with the interaction of four vector mesons described by effective Lagrangians. The amplitudes are calculated up to one loop, decomposed by partial wave projection, and unitarized by Pad$\acute{e}$ approximation. These amplitudes are fitted to the latest experimental data sets of di-$J/ψ$ and $J/ψψ(2S)$ invariant mass spectra of LHCb, CMS, and ATLAS. High-quality solutions are obtained. With these partial wave amplitudes, we extract the pole parameters of the $X(6900)$. Its quantum number is likely to be $0^{++}$. According to the pole counting rule as well as analysis of the phase shifts of the partial waves, it supports our previous conclusion that the $X(6900)$ prefers to be a compact tetra-quark.

## Key claim

The X(6900) resonance is most likely a compact tetraquark state with quantum number \(0^{++}\), based on the pole counting rule and phase shift analysis of unitarized coupled-channel scattering amplitudes fitted to LHCb, CMS, and ATLAS data, though the authors note that the Padé approximation used for unitarization is not as model-independent as dispersion relations and may introduce fake poles.

## Method

The authors constructed scattering amplitudes for the coupled channels \(J/\psi J/\psi\), \(J/\psi\psi(2S)\), and \(J/\psi\psi(3770)\) using effective Lagrangians for four-vector-meson interactions. Amplitudes were calculated up to one loop, decomposed into partial waves (S-waves \(0^{++}\) and \(2^{++}\), P-waves \(0^{-+}\), \(1^{-+}\), \(2^{-+}\)), and unitarized via the matrix Padé approximation. These amplitudes were fitted to the di-\(J/\psi\) and \(J/\psi\psi(2S)\) invariant mass spectra from LHCb, CMS, and ATLAS to extract the pole parameters of the X(6900).

## Key numbers

- Mass of X(6900) from LHCb (Breit-Wigner fit, no continuum): \(6905 \pm 11 \pm 7\) MeV/\(c^2\); width: \(80 \pm 19 \pm 33\) MeV/\(c^2\)
- Mass of X(6900) from LHCb (with NRSPS continuum): \(6886 \pm 11 \pm 11\) MeV/\(c^2\); width: \(168 \pm 33 \pm 69\) MeV/\(c^2\)
- Mass of X(6900) from CMS: \(6.87 \pm 0.03^{+0.06}_{-0.01}\) GeV; width: \(0.12 \pm 0.04^{+0.03}_{-0.01}\) GeV
- Mass of X(6900) from ATLAS: \(6927 \pm 9 \pm 5\) MeV; width: \(122 \pm 22 \pm 19\) MeV
- Threshold of \(J/\psi\psi(2S)\): 6783 MeV; threshold of \(J/\psi\psi(3770)\): 6867.6 MeV
- \(\chi^2_{\text{d.o.f.}}\) for couple-channel fits: Fit.I (LHCb) = 1.31, Fit.II (CMS) = 1.77, Fit.III (ATLAS) = 2.53

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous amplitude analysis (Zhou 2022) that only used LHCb data, now including new measurements from CMS and ATLAS to test the stability of conclusions. It contrasts with earlier studies such as Dong (2020), who extracted a pole at \(6818^{+28}_{-32} - i142^{+14}_{-10}\) MeV in a two-channel case but found no such pole in a triple-channel case, and Chen (2020), who assigned the X(6900) as a P-wave resonance with quantum numbers \(0^{-+}\) or \(1^{-+}\). The present analysis uses partial wave decomposition and unitarization to determine the quantum number as \(0^{++}\) and supports a compact tetraquark interpretation, enabling future studies of the X(6900) nature via phase shift extraction and S-matrix methods.
