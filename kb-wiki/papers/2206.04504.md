# Searching for the QCD critical point along the pseudo-critical/freeze-out line using Padé-resummed Taylor expansions of cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations

> Using high-statistics datasets generated in (2+1)-flavor QCD calculations at finite temperature we construct estimators for the radius of convergence from an eighth order series expansion of the pressure as well as the number density. We show that the estimator for pressure and number density will be identical in the asymptotic limit. In the vicinity of the pseudo-critical temperature, $T_{pc}\simeq 156.5$~MeV, we find the estimator of the radius of convergence to be $μ_B/T \gtrsim\ 3$ for strangeness-neutral matter. We also present results for the pole structure of the Padé approximants for the pressure at non-zero values of the baryon chemical potential and show that the pole structure of the [4,4] Padé is consistent with not having a critical point at temperatures larger than $135~$MeV and a baryon chemical potential smaller than $μ_B/T \sim \ 2.5$.

## Key claim

Using Padé-resummed Taylor expansions of the pressure and number density from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD, the authors find that the pole structure of the [4,4] Padé approximant is consistent with no critical point existing at temperatures above 135 MeV and baryon chemical potentials below μB/T ∼ 2.5, but they cannot rule out real poles at temperatures below 135 MeV due to large statistical errors.

## Method

The authors performed high-statistics (2+1)-flavor QCD calculations on Nτ=8 lattices with about 1.5 million configurations per temperature, computing conserved charge cumulants up to eighth order. They constructed [2,2] and [4,4] Padé approximants from the Taylor series of the pressure in baryon chemical potential, imposing strangeness-neutral and isospin-symmetric conditions (nS=0, nQ/nB=0.5), and analyzed the pole structure of these approximants in the complex μB-plane across temperatures from 125 to 175 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature: Tpc ≃ 156.5 MeV
- Radius of convergence estimator near Tpc: μB/T ≳ 3 for strangeness-neutral matter
- Temperature range where all [4,4] Padé poles are complex: 135 MeV ≤ T ≤ 165 MeV
- Lower temperature bound for which a critical point is disfavored: T > 135 MeV
- Upper baryon chemical potential bound for which a critical point is disfavored: μB/T ∼ 2.5
- The [4,4] Padé estimator for the radius of convergence yields μB/T ≳ [2.5–4] in the temperature range T ∼ [135–165] MeV

## Context

This work extends the HotQCD collaboration's recent results on Padé resummation of Taylor series in QCD (Bollweg 2022) and uses high-statistics cumulant data from the same collaboration (Bazavov 2020). The results are consistent with model-based expectations (Halasz 1998, Karsch 2019, HotQCD 2019) that the QCD critical point, if it exists, should be searched for at temperatures below the chiral critical temperature (∼135 MeV). The findings enable future searches for the critical point at lower temperatures (T < 135 MeV) and higher baryon densities (μB/T > 2.5), corresponding to beam energies below √s ∼ 7.7 GeV at RHIC.
