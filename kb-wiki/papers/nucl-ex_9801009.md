# Alpha - 5He decaying states and the ground state rotational band of 9Be

> In a measurement of the 9Be(7Li,alpha 7Li)n alpha reaction at E = 52 MeV it is unambigously established for the first time that the 9Be excited states around 6.5 and 11.3 MeV decay into the alpha + 5He channel. This fact may support previous claims that the 11.3 MeV state is also a member of the ground state rotational band.

## Key claim

The paper unambiguously establishes for the first time that the 9Be excited states around 6.5 and 11.3 MeV decay into the alpha + 5He channel, which may support previous claims that the 11.3 MeV state is a member of the ground state rotational band, but the authors note that if the 6.5 MeV structure is a single state, its larger width relative to the 11.3 MeV state would contradict this rotational band assignment.

## Method

The authors measured the 9Be(7Li,alpha 7Li)n alpha reaction at a 7Li beam energy of 52 MeV using silicon surface barrier detector telescopes and ionization chamber plus position-sensitive silicon detector telescopes in coincidence. They analyzed Q-value spectra from 7Li-alpha coincidence data, imposing cuts corresponding to the 5He ground state, to extract the 9Be excitation energy spectrum and identify decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 52 MeV for the 7Li projectile
- Target thickness: 400 microgram per square centimeter self-supported beryllium target
- Excitation energies of observed states: 6.5 MeV and 11.3 MeV in 9Be
- Q-value for 5He ground state decay: -2.5 MeV
- Widths used in Breit-Wigner fit: 6.76 MeV state with Gamma = 1.540 MeV; 11.28 MeV state with Gamma = 0.575 MeV (from compilation reference)
- Theoretical widths from one calculation: 9/2+ state width 2.9 MeV, 7/2- state width 1.2 MeV (ratio 2.4)

## Context

This work extends studies of the 9Be ground state rotational band, where only the 3/2-, 5/2-, and 7/2- members were well established, with controversy about the fourth member around 11-12 MeV. Prior work by Peterson (no year given) had assigned the 11.28 MeV state as 9/2- from alpha inelastic scattering, while electron and proton scattering studies (no authors given, no year) suggested different spin-parity assignments. The results enable future experiments to determine spins and parities of these states and clarify whether they belong to the rotational band, with the authors noting that final conclusions require additional experimental and theoretical results.
