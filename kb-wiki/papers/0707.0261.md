# Photons and Dileptons at LHC

> We discuss real and virtual photon sources in heavy ion collisions and present results for dilepton yields in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC at intermediate and large transverse momentum p_T.

## Key claim

Jet-medium interactions will be a significant source of dileptons at LHC energies, potentially exceeding Drell-Yan yields at intermediate masses up to about 8 GeV, offering a new probe of the fireball's temperature and partonic nature.

## Method

The authors compute dilepton yields from several sources for central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC: Drell-Yan (next-to-leading order pQCD), jet production (leading order), jet-medium photons (Compton scattering/annihilation and medium-induced bremsstrahlung), and thermal radiation from the quark-gluon plasma (all using Hard Thermal Loop resummation). Jet energy loss is calculated using the AMY formalism. The mass and transverse momentum spectra are presented for electron-positron pairs with specific kinematic cuts.

## Key numbers

- Mass spectrum integrated over pT > 8 GeV/c for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, showing yields as a function of invariant mass M
- Transverse momentum spectrum integrated over mass range 0.5 GeV < M < 1 GeV, showing yields as a function of pT
- Jet-medium dileptons are predicted to be as important as or exceeding Drell-Yan yields at intermediate masses up to about 8 GeV
- Calculations use a cut |y_e| < 0.5 for single electron rapidity and y_d = 0 for pair rapidity

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of photon and dilepton production in heavy ion collisions, specifically building on jet-medium photon studies by Fries (2002) and Zakharov (2004), and the AMY energy loss formalism by Turbide (2005). It provides predictions for the LHC that go beyond earlier lower-energy experiments at RHIC, showing that jet-medium dileptons will be more important at LHC energies. The full calculation is presented in Turbide (2006), with direct photon predictions in Turbide (2005).
