# Role of heavy-meson exchange in pion production near threshold

> Recent calculations of $s$-wave pion production have severely underestimated the accurately known $pp\rightarrow ppπ^0$\ total cross section near threshold. In these calculations, only the single-nucleon axial-charge operator is considered. We have calculated, in addition to the one-body term, the two-body contributions to this reaction that arise from the exchange of mesons. We find that the inclusion of the scalar $σ$-meson exchange current (and lesser contributions from other mesons) increases the cross section by about a factor of five, and leads to excellent agreement with the data. The results are neither very sensitive to changes in the distorting potential that generates the $NN$ wave function, nor to different choices for the meson-nucleon form factors. We argue that $pp\rightarrow ppπ^0$\ data provide direct experimental evidence for meson-exchange contributions to the axial current.

# Role of heavy-meson exchange in pion production near threshold

## Key claim

The inclusion of scalar sigma-meson exchange current (along with smaller contributions from other heavy mesons) increases the calculated total cross section for $pp\rightarrow pp\pi^0$ near threshold by about a factor of five, bringing theory into excellent agreement with experimental data without adjusting any parameters to pion-production data. The authors argue this provides direct experimental evidence for meson-exchange contributions to the axial current, but note that the sigma meson is a phenomenological model for intermediate-range attraction rather than a sharp resonance.

## Method

The authors calculated the $s$-wave pion production cross section for $pp\rightarrow pp\pi^0$ in the $^3P_0\rightarrow{}^1S_0$ partial wave using coordinate-space distorted waves from the Bonn one-boson-exchange potential (BPA and BPB), the Reid soft-core potential, and the Reid hard-core potential. They evaluated the one-body axial-charge operator contribution alongside two-body meson-exchange current contributions from sigma, delta, omega, and rho mesons using the same meson parameters that define the distorting potentials, with monopole form factors at all meson-nucleon vertices and including the Coulomb interaction in the final state.

## Key numbers

- The one-body term alone underestimates the experimental $pp\rightarrow pp\pi^0$ total cross section near threshold by approximately a factor of five
- The sigma-meson exchange contribution $J_\sigma$ is about as large as the one-body term $J_1$, and the two contributions add constructively, increasing the cross section by about a factor of four
- The omega-meson contribution is 25-35% of the $J_1$ or $J_\sigma$ contribution
- The initial relative momentum near threshold is about $1.9\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, comparable to the sigma-meson mass of $550\ \text{MeV}$
- The pion rescattering parameter $\lambda_1$ is determined to be $0.001\pm 0.002$ from pionic hydrogen data, consistent with zero
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is $f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 0.075$

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Koltun and Reitan (1966), Miller (1991), Niskanen (1992), and Meyer et al. (1992) that used only the single-nucleon axial-charge operator and severely underestimated the measured cross section. It builds on the suggestion by Lee and Riska (1993) that meson-exchange currents could explain the discrepancy, but uses an explicit one-boson-exchange model for both the $NN$ interaction and the exchange currents rather than a phenomenological operator form. The results enable quantitative tests of meson-exchange current models in the simplest possible nuclear system, where complications from nuclear correlations and wave function uncertainties are minimized, and provide evidence that the relativistic enhancement of the axial charge seen in nuclear beta decay (Townsend, 1992) also appears in pion production.
