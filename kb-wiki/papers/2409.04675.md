# Effect of vector meson spin coherence on the measurements of chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions reflects the local violation of ${\cal P}$ and ${\cal CP}$ symmetries in strong interactions and manifests as electric charge separation along the direction of the magnetic field created by the wounded nuclei. The experimental observables for the CME, such as the $γ_{112}$ correlator, the $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ correlator, and the signed balance functions, however, are also subject to non-CME backgrounds, including those from resonance decays. A previous study showed that the CME observables are affected by the diagonal component of the spin density matrix, the $ρ_{00}$ for vector mesons. In this work, we study the contributions from the other elements of the spin density matrix using a toy model and a multiphase transport model. We find that the real part of the $ρ_{1-1}$ component, $\mathrm{Re}\,ρ_{1-1}$, affects the CME observables in a manner opposite to that of the $ρ_{00}$. All three aforementioned CME observables show a linear dependence on $\mathrm{Re}\,ρ_{1-1}$ in the model calculations, supporting our analytical derivations. The rest elements of the spin density matrix do not contribute to the CME observables. The off-diagonal terms in the spin density matrix indicate spin coherence and may be nonzero in heavy-ion collisions due to local spin polarization or spin-spin correlations. Thus, $\mathrm{Re}\,ρ_{1-1}$, along with $ρ_{00}$, could play a significant role in interpreting measurements in search of the CME.

# Effect of vector meson spin coherence on the measurements of chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The real part of the off-diagonal spin density matrix element Re ρ₁₋₁ for vector mesons affects the three major CME observables (γ₁₁₂ correlator, RΨ₂(ΔS) correlator, and signed balance functions) in a manner opposite to that of the diagonal element ρ₀₀, and its contribution is comparable in magnitude; however, this effect constitutes a non-CME background that must be accounted for when interpreting experimental searches, and the possibility that a finite Re ρ₁₋₁ could itself arise from the CME cannot be ruled out.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the dependence of the γ₁₁₂ correlator, the RΨ₂(ΔS) correlator, and signed balance functions on all elements of the vector meson spin density matrix, using the decay channel ρ → π⁺π⁻. They then verified the analytical results with toy model simulations and with the string-melting version of the multiphase transport model (AMPT) for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with an impact parameter of 8 fm, generating 2 million events per configuration and redistributing decay product momenta according to the spin density matrix.

## Key numbers

- The analytical expression for the ρ-decay contribution to Δγ₁₁₂ in the laboratory frame is Δγ₁₁₂^ρ = f₀ (Nρ/N+N−) [ (3/4)(ρ₀₀ − 1/3) − (1/2) Re ρ₁₋₁ ], where f₀ absorbs Lorentz boost effects.
- The analytical expression for the ρ-decay contribution to Δσ_R² (the variance-based quantifier for the RΨ₂(ΔS) correlator) is Δσ_R² = f₀ (Nρ/N+N−) [ (3/2)(ρ₀₀ − 1/3) − Re ρ₁₋₁ ].
- The sign of the concavity of the RΨ₂(ΔS) distribution is given by S_concavity = Sign[ Re ρ₁₋₁ − (3/2)(ρ₀₀ − 1/3) ]; at ρ₀₀ = 1/3, a negative Re ρ₁₋₁ value corresponds to a concave shape (S_concavity = −1) and a positive value to a convex shape (S_concavity = 1).
- In the AMPT simulations, the spin density matrix elements ρ₀₀ − 1/3 and Re ρ₁₋₁ were each set to −0.2, 0, and 0.2 to test linear dependence; other elements were set to −0.2 to demonstrate their lack of contribution.
- The AMPT simulations used Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with an impact parameter of 8 fm.

## Context

This work extends a prior study by Shen et al. (2023) that showed the diagonal component ρ₀₀ of the vector meson spin density matrix affects CME observables. The present paper demonstrates that the off-diagonal element Re ρ₁₋₁, which indicates spin coherence, also contributes significantly and oppositely to ρ₀₀. The results imply that experimental CME measurements using the γ₁₁₂ correlator (Voloshin, 2004), the RΨ₂(ΔS) correlator (Magdy, 2017), and signed balance functions (Tang, 2019) must account for both ρ₀₀ and Re ρ₁₋₁ backgrounds. The nonzero off-diagonal elements could arise from local spin alignment (Xia, 2020) or short-range spin-spin correlations (Lv, 2024), and the interaction between the CME and spin density matrix elements warrants further theoretical investigation.
