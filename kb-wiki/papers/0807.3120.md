# Stability and Causality in relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics

> The stability and causality of the Landau-Lifshitz theory and the Israel-Stewart type causal dissipative hydrodynamics are discussed. We show that the problem of acausality and instability are correlated in relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics and instability is induced by acausality. We further discuss the stability of the scaling solution. The scaling solution of the causal dissipative hydrodynamics can be unstable against inhomogeneous perturbations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that acausality and instability are correlated in relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics, and that instability is induced by acausality when the theory is examined in a Lorentz-boosted frame, even though in the rest frame the Landau-Lifshitz theory and the Israel-Stewart type causal dissipative hydrodynamics with acausal parameter sets appear stable.

## Method

The authors perform a linear perturbation analysis around the hydrostatic state for 1+1 dimensional massless ideal QGP gas, first in the rest frame and then in a general Lorentz-boosted frame, deriving dispersion relations for both the Landau-Lifshitz (first-order) theory and the Israel-Stewart type causal dissipative (second-order) hydrodynamics. They then analyze the stability of the scaling solution using the Lyapunov direct method, studying homogeneous and inhomogeneous perturbations as functions of the Reynolds number, the dimensionless time parameter, and the wave number.

## Key numbers

- The critical momentum in the Landau-Lifshitz theory is \(k_c = 2\sqrt{\alpha} (\varepsilon + P)/\zeta_0\), where \(\alpha = 1/3\) for the massless ideal gas.
- For the causal dissipative hydrodynamics, the maximum propagation speed is \(v_c = \sqrt{1/b + \alpha}\), and the parameter \(b\) must be smaller than \(3/2\) to be consistent with causality for \(\alpha = 1/3\).
- The numerical simulations use initial temperature \(T = 590\) MeV and initial size \(0.7\) fm, with bulk viscosity parameter \(a = 1\).
- The scaling solution is unstable when \(\hat{\tau} > 1/b\) for \(k = 0\) and \(R_0^{-1} = 1 + \epsilon\), and when \(\hat{\tau} < 1/b\) for \(k = 0\) and \(R_0^{-1} = 1 - \epsilon\).

## Context

This work extends the stability analyses of Hiscock and collaborators (1985-1987) and Kouno et al. (1990), who studied linear perturbations around hydrostatic states and scaling solutions in the Landau-Lifshitz theory. The paper shows that the Israel-Stewart type causal dissipative hydrodynamics, previously considered stable in the rest frame, can become unstable against inhomogeneous perturbations around the scaling solution when the bulk viscosity is large, and that acausal parameter sets lead to instability in boosted frames. This enables a criterion for selecting physically consistent parameter sets in numerical simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
