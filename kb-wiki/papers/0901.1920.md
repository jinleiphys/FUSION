# Spectroscopic Factors in 16O and Nucleon Asymmetry

> The self-consistent Green's functions method is employed to study the spectroscopic factors of quasiparticle states around 16O, 28O, 40Ca and 60Ca. The Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) is used to account for the coupling of particles with collective excitation modes. Results for 16O are reviewed first. The same approach is applied to isotopes with large proton-neutron asymmetry to estimate its effect on spectroscopic factors. The results, based on the chiral N3LO force, exhibit an asymmetry dependence similar to that observed in heavy-ion knockout experiments but weaker in magnitude.

## Key claim

The self-consistent Green's functions method using the Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) with the chiral N3LO force shows that spectroscopic factors for quasiparticle states around 16O, 28O, 40Ca, and 60Ca exhibit a dependence on proton-neutron asymmetry similar to that observed in heavy-ion knockout experiments, but the magnitude of this dependence is significantly weaker than the experimental data suggest.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson equation for the single-particle Green's function using an irreducible self-energy expanded in Feynman-Dyson diagrams, approximated by solving Faddeev equations that couple the particle-hole polarization propagator (via dressed random phase approximation) and the two-particle propagator to single-particle motion. The calculations were performed in a harmonic oscillator basis using a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the Bonn-C potential for 16O and from the chiral N3LO interaction for the asymmetric isotopes, with self-consistency achieved through iteration. For the asymmetric nuclei, self-consistency was implemented only partially following the scheme of Ref. BaH.09.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, the spectroscopic factor for proton removal from the p3/2 orbit is 0.75 of the independent particle model (IPM) value, and for the p1/2 orbit it is 0.77, from self-consistent FRPA calculations including short-range correlations (SRC) and long-range correlations (LRC).
- When the first excited 0+ state in 16O is constrained to its experimental energy, the p3/2 spectroscopic factor reduces to 0.72, with an additional 2.6% strength appearing in satellite fragments near -26.3 MeV missing energy.
- Short-range correlations alone account for approximately a 10% reduction of spectroscopic factors in 16O, yielding values near 0.90 of the IPM value.
- For the most asymmetric isotope studied, 28O with asymmetry parameter alpha = (N-Z)/A approximately 0.43, the FRPA spectroscopic factors for proton and neutron knockout differ by about 11% of the IPM value.
- Experimental spectroscopic factors for 16O from (e,e'p) reactions range from 0.64-0.71 for p1/2 and 0.54-0.61 for p3/2.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Green's functions approach from the benchmark nucleus 16O, previously studied by Barbieri (2002) using the Bonn-C potential, to asymmetric isotopes near the dripline using the chiral N3LO force. The results contradict the strong asymmetry dependence reported from heavy-ion knockout experiments by Hansen (2003) and Gade (2008), showing a much weaker variation with nucleon separation energy. The findings are consistent with dispersive optical model analyses by Charity (2006, 2007) extrapolated to proton-rich calcium isotopes, and with nuclear matter calculations by Frick (2005) that considered only short-range correlations. The work enables future improvements by identifying that realistic two-nucleon forces tend to overestimate giant resonance excitation energies, suggesting that better forces may resolve the discrepancy with knockout data.
