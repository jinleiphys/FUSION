# Meson retardation in deuteron electrodisintegration

> The effect of meson retardation in $NN$-interaction and exchange currents on deuteron electrodisintegration is studied in a coupled channel approach including $NN$-, $N Δ$- and $πd$-channels. It is shown that the influence of retardation depends on the energy regime: Whereas below $π$-threshold calculations with static and retarded operators yield almost identical results, they differ significantly in the $Δ$-region. Especially, the longitudinal and the longitudinal-transverse interference structure functions are strongly affected.

## Key claim

Meson retardation in the NN-interaction and exchange currents has a negligible effect on deuteron electrodisintegration below pion threshold, but above pion threshold it significantly alters the longitudinal and longitudinal-transverse interference structure functions, with the recoil charge contribution to the Coulomb monopole being the dominant source of this effect.

## Method

The authors employed a coupled channel approach including NN-, NΔ-, and πd-channels with retarded one-boson-exchange operators for π, ρ, σ, δ, ω, and η mesons, using the Elster potential as the basis for the retarded NN-interaction. They constructed both a retarded model version ("CC(ret,π,ρ,0)") and a static version ("CC(stat,π,ρ,0)") based on the Bonn OBEPR-potential, then computed the T-matrix for deuteron electrodisintegration using standard multipole decomposition and Siegert's theorem. The γNΔ-transition current was modeled with a dominant M1-transition, with the coupling constant fitted to the M1+^(3/2) multipole of pion photoproduction and parametrized as a function of the πN-subsystem invariant mass.

## Key numbers

- Below π-threshold kinematics: Enp = 66 MeV, q² = 3.87 fm⁻² (Jordan et al., 1996); static and retarded operators yield almost identical results, with only fTT showing distinguishable curves.
- Above π-threshold kinematics (Turck-Chièze et al., 1984): Enp = 179 MeV, q² = 1.66 fm⁻²; fL is enhanced by roughly 45 percent at forward angles due to retardation.
- Δ-region kinematics (Pellegrino et al., 1996): Enp = 280 MeV, q² = 2.47 fm⁻²; retardation effects on fL and fLT are even stronger than at 179 MeV.
- The Δ-resonance mass used in the model: MΔ^res = 1232 MeV.
- The study restricts to kinematics with Q² ≤ 4 fm⁻² where the dipole form factor approximation for the γNΔ-transition is adequate.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled channel approach to NN-scattering and deuteron photodisintegration (Schwamb and Arenhövel, 1998, 2001a, 2001b) to the electrodisintegration case, where independent variation of energy and momentum transfer provides additional insight. The results contradict the static approximation commonly used in conventional approaches (e.g., the Bonn OBEPR-potential of Machleidt et al., 1987) for energies above pion threshold, and the authors note that the approach of Ritz et al. (1997) cannot treat retardation realistically above pion threshold due to its Taylor expansion in p/MN. The work enables improved extraction of nucleon properties like the neutron electric form factor from quasifree kinematics, and identifies the need for a "high precision" NN-interaction above pion threshold as an indispensable ingredient for future theoretical descriptions.
