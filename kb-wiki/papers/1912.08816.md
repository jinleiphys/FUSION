# Drag force to all orders in gradients

> We study the energy loss of a heavy quark slowly moving through an evolving strongly coupled plasma. We use the linearized fluid/gravity correspondence to describe small perturbations of the medium flow with general spacetime dependence. This all order linearized hydrodynamics results in a drag force exerted on a heavy quark even when it is at rest with the fluid element. We show how the general contribution to the drag force can be derived order by order in the medium velocity gradients and provide explicit results valid up to the third order. We then obtain an approximate semi-analytic result for the drag force to all orders in the gradient expansion but linearized in the medium velocity. Thus, the effects of a class of hydrodynamic gradients on the drag force are re-summed, giving further insight into the dissipative properties of strongly coupled plasmas. The all order result allows us to study the drag force in the non-hydrodynamic regime of linear medium perturbations that vary rapidly in space and time.

## Key claim

The authors show that a heavy quark at rest in a strongly coupled plasma experiences a drag force due to linearized hydrodynamic gradients of the medium, and they derive this force to all orders in the gradient expansion for small medium velocity, going beyond the first-order gradient results previously known in the literature.

## Method

The authors use the linearized fluid/gravity correspondence for strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, where the dual description of a heavy quark is a classical string whose endpoint is attached to the AdS boundary. They expand the bulk metric to first order in the amplitude of the 3-dimensional fluid velocity, keeping all orders in gradients, and solve the linearized Nambu-Goto equations for the string profile in this background. The drag force is then extracted from the canonical string momentum flux at the boundary, and the authors also employ a matching procedure that stitches series expansions at the boundary and horizon to obtain an approximate all-order solution.

## Key numbers

* The drag force on a quark at rest up to first order in gradients is given by \(-\frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{\lambda}}\vec{f}^{(0+1)} = (1 + i\omega)\vec{\beta}\), where \(\vec{\beta}\) is the Fourier amplitude of the medium 3-velocity and \(\omega\) is the frequency in units of \(\pi T\).
* The second-order gradient correction to the drag force is \(-\frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{\lambda}}\vec{f}^{(2)} = \frac{1}{8}\left(2(\pi - 4 - 2\log 2)\omega^2 - k^2\right)\vec{\beta} - \frac{3}{8}\vec{k}(k\cdot\vec{\beta})\), where \(k\) is the spatial momentum in units of \(\pi T\).
* The third-order gradient correction is \(-\frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{\lambda}}\vec{f}^{(3)} = -\frac{i\omega^3\vec{\beta}}{24}\left(\pi^2 - 6(\pi - 2)\log 2\right) + \frac{i\omega\left[\vec{k}(k\cdot\vec{\beta}) + 3\vec{\beta}k^2\right]}{96}\left(3\pi - 20 + 6\log 2\right)\).
* The shear viscosity in the static limit is \(\eta_0 = q(\pi T)^3 = q\), and the ratio \(\eta_0/s = 1/(4\pi)\), where \(s = 4\pi q (\pi T)^3 = 4\pi q\) is the entropy density.

## Context

This work extends the first-order gradient results for the drag force on a heavy quark in a flowing holographic plasma, previously studied by Lekaveckas and Rajagopal (2013) and Rajagopal and Sadofyev (2015), to higher orders in the gradient expansion for terms linear in the medium velocity. It uses the linearized fluid/gravity correspondence developed by Bu and collaborators (2014, 2015) to resum a class of hydrodynamic gradients to all orders. The results provide a benchmark for higher-order hydrodynamic effects on probes of the quark-gluon plasma and enable studies of the drag force in the non-hydrodynamic regime where medium perturbations vary rapidly in space and time.
