# Spin tensor and pseudo-gauges: from nuclear collisions to gravitational physics

> The relativistic treatment of spin is a fundamental subject which has an old history. In various physical contexts it is necessary to separate the relativistic total angular momentum into an orbital and spin contribution. However, such decomposition is affected by ambiguities since one can always redefine the orbital and spin part through the so-called pseudo-gauge transformations. We analyze this problem in detail by discussing the most common choices of energy-momentum and spin tensors with an emphasis on their physical implications, and study the spin vector which is a pseudo-gauge invariant operator. We review the angular momentum decomposition as a crucial ingredient for the formulation of relativistic spin hydrodynamics and quantum kinetic theory with a focus on relativistic nuclear collisions, where spin physics has recently attracted significant attention. Furthermore, we point out the connection between pseudo-gauge transformations and the different definitions of the relativistic center of mass. Finally, we consider the Einstein-Cartan theory, an extension of conventional general relativity, which allows for a natural definition of the spin tensor.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the decomposition of relativistic total angular momentum into orbital and spin parts is fundamentally ambiguous due to pseudo-gauge transformations, and that no single decomposition is universally physical; instead, the appropriate choice depends on the specific physical context (e.g., nuclear collisions, gravitational physics), and the spin vector defined via the Pauli-Lubanski operator is pseudo-gauge invariant.

## Method

The authors review the canonical energy-momentum and spin tensors derived from Noether's theorem for the free Dirac field, then systematically apply pseudo-gauge transformations to obtain the Belinfante-Rosenfeld, Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen (HW), de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW), and alternative Klein-Gordon (KG) currents. They analyze the Lorentz transformation properties of the global spin for each choice, relate the spin tensors to the Frenkel condition and the Pauli-Lubanski vector, and discuss the implications for relativistic spin hydrodynamics, quantum kinetic theory, the definition of the relativistic center of mass, and Einstein-Cartan gravity.

## Key numbers

* The canonical spin tensor for the Dirac field is given by \( \hat{S}_C^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = \frac{\hbar}{4} \bar{\psi} \{ \gamma^\lambda, \sigma^{\mu\nu} \} \psi = -\frac{\hbar}{2} \epsilon^{\lambda\mu\nu\alpha} \bar{\psi} \gamma_\alpha \gamma_5 \psi \), where \( \sigma^{\mu\nu} = \frac{i}{2} [\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu] \).
* The Belinfante-Rosenfeld transformation yields a symmetric energy-momentum tensor and a vanishing spin tensor: \( \hat{S}_B^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = 0 \).
* The HW global spin operator is \( \hat{S}^{\mu\nu}_{HW} = \frac{\hbar}{2} \int d^3 x \, \psi^\dagger \sigma^{\mu\nu} \psi + \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \int d^3 x \, \psi^\dagger \gamma^{[\mu} \partial^{\nu]} \psi \), which satisfies the Frenkel condition \( \langle \mathbf{p}, s | \hat{P}_\mu \hat{S}^{\mu\nu}_{HW} | \mathbf{p}, s \rangle = 0 \).
* The relativistic spin vector is defined as \( \hat{S}^\mu = \hat{w}^\mu / m \), where \( \hat{w}^\mu = -\frac{1}{2} \epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} \hat{P}_\nu \hat{J}_{\alpha\beta} \) is the Pauli-Lubanski vector, and its spatial components obey \( [\hat{S}_i, \hat{S}_j] = i\hbar \, \epsilon_{ijk} \hat{S}_k \) for states at rest.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing analysis of angular momentum decomposition in quantum field theory, building on the foundational treatments of Belinfante (1939, 1940), Rosenfeld (1940), and Hehl (1976). It connects the pseudo-gauge ambiguity to the Frenkel theory of relativistic spin and the Pryce (1948) definition of the relativistic center of mass. The paper enables further development of relativistic spin hydrodynamics and quantum kinetic theory for heavy-ion collisions, where spin polarization phenomena have been observed by the STAR Collaboration (2017), and it points toward the Einstein-Cartan extension of general relativity as a framework where a natural spin tensor arises from spacetime torsion.
