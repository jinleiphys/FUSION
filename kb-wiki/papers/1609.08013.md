# Multiplicity fluctuation and correlation of identified baryons in quark combination model

> The dynamical fluctuation and correlation of multiplicity distributions of identified baryons and antibaryons produced by the hadronization of the bulk quark system are systematically studied in quark combination model. Starting from the most basic dynamics of quark combination which are necessary for multiplicity study, we analyze moments (variance, skewness and kurtosis) of inclusive multiplicity distribution of identified baryons, two-baryon multiplicity correlations, and baryon-antibaryon multiplicity correlations after the hadronization of quark system with given quark number and antiquark number. We obtain a series of interesting findings, e.g., binomial behavior of multiplicity moments, coincide flavor dependent two-baryon correlation and universal baryon-antibaryon correlation, which can be regarded as general features of quark combination. We further take into account correlations and fluctuations of quark numbers before hadronization to study their influence on multiple production of baryons and antibaryons. We find that quark number fluctuation and flavor conservation lead to a series of important results such as the negative $p\barΩ^{+}$ multiplicity correlation and universal two-baryon correlations. We also study the influence of resonance decay in order to compare our results with future experimental data in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at LHC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that multiplicity fluctuations and correlations of identified baryons and antibaryons produced via quark combination exhibit general features such as binomial behavior of moments, flavor-dependent two-baryon anti-correlations, and universal baryon-antibaryon correlations, but these results are derived under the condition of a fixed quark system with given quark and antiquark numbers before hadronization, and the inclusion of pre-hadronization quark number fluctuations and resonance decays modifies these baseline predictions.

## Method

The authors used a quark combination model (QCM) with a specific combination algorithm that treats quarks and antiquarks as a one-dimensional sequence in rapidity space, combining neighboring partons into hadrons via a set of probabilistic rules that govern baryon versus meson formation. They calculated moments (variance, skewness, kurtosis) of inclusive multiplicity distributions, two-baryon covariances, and baryon-antibaryon covariances for identified ground-state baryons (octet and decuplet) in flavor SU(3), first for a fixed quark system and then incorporating pre-hadronization quark number fluctuations and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- For a quark system with \(N_q = N_{\bar{q}} = 500\) and flavor ratio \(N_u:N_d:N_s = 1:1:0.43\), the variance of total baryon multiplicity scales as \(\overline{\sigma}^2_B / \overline{N}_B \approx 0.35\) and the skewness scales as \(\overline{S}_B \overline{N}_B^{1/2} \approx 0.37\).
- The baryon formation probability \(P_1\) in the \(qqq\bar{q}\) configuration is set to about 0.04 to reproduce observed baryon yields in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
- The relative covariance between two identified baryons is given at leading order by \(\overline{C}_{B_i B_j} / (\overline{N}_{B_i} \overline{N}_{B_j}) = -\sum_f n_{f,B_i} n_{f,B_j} / N_f + \mathcal{O}(N_q^{-2})\), where \(n_{f,B_i}\) is the number of valence quarks of flavor \(f\) in baryon \(B_i\).
- The baryon-antibaryon relative covariance follows the scaling \(\overline{C}_{B_i \bar{B}_j} / (\overline{N}_{B_i} \overline{N}_{\bar{B}_j}) = \overline{\sigma}^2_B / (\overline{N}_B \overline{N}_{\bar{B}})\) for all identified baryon-antibaryon pairs.

## Context

This work extends the quark combination model (QCM), previously used to reproduce yields, rapidity distributions, and transverse momentum spectra at RHIC and LHC (Song et al. 2013, Wang et al. 2012, 2015), to the study of multiplicity fluctuations and correlations of identified baryons. It provides baseline predictions for future experimental measurements of strange baryons such as \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi^{-}\), and \(\Omega^{-}\) at LHC, complementing existing theoretical studies based on statistical hadronization models (Koch 2008, Tawfik, Gorenstein 2009, Konchakovski 2009) that have mainly focused on pion, kaon, and proton fluctuations. The results enable tests of hadronization dynamics and extraction of pre-hadronization quark correlations from event-by-event baryon multiplicity data.
