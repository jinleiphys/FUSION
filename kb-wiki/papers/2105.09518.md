# Elliptic flow splittings in the Polyakov-looped Nambu-Jona Lasinio transport model

> To incorporate the effect of gluons on the evolution dynamics of the quark matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we extend the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) transport model to include the contribution from the Polyakov loops. Imbedding the resulting pNJL partonic transport model in an extended multiphase transport (extended AMPT) model, we then study the elliptic flow splittings between particles and their antiparticles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC-BES energies. We find that a weak quark vector interaction in the partonic phase is able to describe the elliptic flow splitting between protons and antiprotons in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ to 39 GeV. Knowledge on the quark vector interaction is useful for understanding the equation of state of quark matter at large baryon chemical potentials and thus the location of the critical point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

A weak quark vector interaction in the partonic phase, implemented via the Polyakov-looped Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (pNJL) transport model, is able to describe the elliptic flow splitting between protons and antiprotons in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC-BES energies from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) to 39 GeV, but the study finds that none of the evolution trajectories of the partonic matter passes through the spinodal region or the critical point for the collision energies considered.

## Method

The authors extended the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) transport model to include Polyakov loop contributions, creating a pNJL partonic transport model that was embedded in an extended multiphase transport (extended AMPT) model. The partonic evolution was described by solving transport equations with mean-field potentials from the pNJL model, using the test-particle method with parallel events, and treating parton scatterings via the stochastic method with Pauli blocking factors. The hadronization of partons was achieved by coalescing partons close in phase space into hadrons, followed by hadronic evolution described by a relativistic transport (ART) model.

## Key numbers

* The partonic phase ends when the central energy density drops to about 0.8 GeV/fm\(^3\).
* The specific shear viscosity used was 0.14 in the NJL transport model and 0.06 in the pNJL transport model to reproduce measured \(v_2\) of charged hadrons.
* The parameters for the Polyakov loop effective potential were \(a=664\) MeV and \(b=0.028\Lambda^3\), with \(\Lambda = 750\) MeV/c, giving a simultaneous crossover of chiral restoration and deconfinement around \(T \approx 212\) MeV.
* The reduced vector coupling constant values studied were \(R_V=G_V/G_S=0\), 0.5, and 1.1.
* Partons are generally formed after 0.2 fm/\(c\), with central density peaking around 0.5 fm/\(c\) at higher collision energies; the parton phase ends around 4-5 fm/\(c\).
* The largest peak value of the time component of the vector potential \(U_V^0\) was reached at about \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=11.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group using the NJL-based extended AMPT model (Xu 2014, Xu 2016) to include Polyakov loop effects, which address the known drawback of the NJL model giving a relatively low temperature for the critical point due to the lack of gluon effects. The pNJL model yields a higher critical temperature more comparable with predictions from the Dyson-Schwinger approach (Xin 2014, Fischer 2014) and the functional renormalization group method (Fu 2019, Gao 2020). The constrained quark vector interaction from this study is useful for understanding the equation of state of quark matter at large baryon chemical potentials and thus the location of the QCD critical point.
