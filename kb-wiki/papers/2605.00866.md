# Nuclear geometry driven symmetry plane correlations in OO and Ne--Ne collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Symmetry-plane correlations (SPCs) are key observables sensitive to the medium's transport properties and are driven by participant-plane correlations (PPCs) in the nuclear overlap region. This study explores the possibility of nuclear-geometry-driven SPCs in Oxygen--Oxygen (OO) and Neon--Neon (Ne--Ne) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.36$ TeV using nuclear geometry simulations based on Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) configurations. We investigate $\langle \cos[4(ψ_2 - ψ_4)]\rangle_{\rm GE}$ and $\langle \cos[6(ψ_3 - ψ_6)]\rangle_{\rm GE}$ in OO and Ne--Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.36$ TeV using the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model. We find that Ne--Ne collisions exhibit larger $\langle \cos[4(ψ_2 - ψ_4)]\rangle_{\rm GE}$ values than OO collisions, whereas $\langle \cos[6(ψ_3 - ψ_6)]\rangle_{\rm GE}$ is larger in OO than in Ne--Ne collisions. This behavior indicates a strongly deformed shape of the $^{20}$Ne nucleus and a tetrahedral structure of the $^{16}$O nucleus. We also explore SPCs for events with tip-tip and body-body collision configurations, which further support these findings.

# Nuclear geometry driven symmetry plane correlations in OO and Ne–Ne collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim

Symmetry-plane correlations (SPCs) in Oxygen–Oxygen and Neon–Neon collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV, simulated with the AMPT model using NLEFT and PGCM nuclear configurations, are driven by intrinsic nuclear geometry rather than solely by final-state collective dynamics: Ne–Ne collisions show larger ⟨cos[4(ψ2 − ψ4)]⟩GE than OO collisions, while OO collisions show larger ⟨cos[6(ψ3 − ψ6)]⟩GE than Ne–Ne collisions, indicating a strongly deformed (quadrupole) shape of 20Ne and a tetrahedral (octupole) structure of 16O.

## Method

The study uses the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model in its string-melting configuration to simulate OO and Ne–Ne collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV. Initial nuclear configurations for 16O and 20Ne are taken from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) calculations that incorporate α-cluster parameterizations. Symmetry-plane correlations are quantified using the Gaussian Estimator (GE) for two observables: ⟨cos[4(ψ2 − ψ4)]⟩GE and ⟨cos[6(ψ3 − ψ6)]⟩GE, studied as functions of collision centrality and of the event-shape ratio R = q2/⟨pT⟩, which selects tip-tip versus body-body collision configurations.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 5.36 TeV for both OO and Ne–Ne systems.
- ⟨cos[4(ψ2 − ψ4)]⟩GE is larger in Ne–Ne collisions than in OO collisions for central events, with the ratio Ne–Ne/OO exceeding unity in central collisions and approaching unity in peripheral collisions.
- ⟨cos[6(ψ3 − ψ6)]⟩GE is larger in OO collisions than in Ne–Ne collisions for central events, with the ratio Ne–Ne/OO below unity in central collisions.
- ⟨cos[4(ψ2 − ψ4)]⟩GE increases with q2/⟨pT⟩ percentile class for both systems in 0–10% central collisions, with Ne–Ne values consistently higher than OO values.
- ⟨cos[6(ψ3 − ψ6)]⟩GE shows no significant dependence on q2/⟨pT⟩ percentile class or collision system within uncertainties for 0–10% central collisions.

## Context

This work extends studies of anisotropic flow coefficients in light-ion collisions, particularly the ALICE (2025) and ATLAS (2025) measurements of v2 and v3 ratios in Ne–Ne versus OO collisions that pointed toward quadrupole deformation of 20Ne and tetrahedral structure of 16O. It also builds on theoretical work by Tripathy (2025) on SPCs as probes of nuclear structure and by Giacalone (2024) and Loizides (2025) on implementing NLEFT and PGCM configurations in AMPT. The results establish SPCs as complementary observables to flow coefficients for constraining nuclear geometry, enabling experimental tests with LHC data from OO and Ne–Ne runs.
