# Potential model for nuclear astrophysical fusion reactions with a square-well potential

> The potential model for nuclear astrophysical reactions requires a considerably shallow nuclear potential when a square-well potential is employed to fit experimental data. We discuss the origin of this apparently different behavior from that obtained with a smooth Woods-Saxon potential, for which a deep potential is often employed. We argue that due to the sharp change of the potential at the boundary the radius parameter tends to be large in the square-well model, which results in a large absorption radius. The wave function then needs to be suppressed in the absorption region, which can eventually be achieved by using a shallow potential. We thus clarify the reason why the square-well potential has been able to reproduce a large amount of fusion data.

# Potential model for nuclear astrophysical fusion reactions with a square-well potential

## Key claim

The shallow depth of square-well potentials used to fit heavy-ion fusion data is an artifact of the potential's sharp boundary, not a signature of a repulsive nuclear core; the abrupt change forces a large radius parameter, which in turn requires a shallow potential to suppress the wave function in the absorption region.

## Method

The authors computed fusion cross sections and astrophysical S-factors for the $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O system using a square-well potential with an incoming-wave boundary condition, varying the depth $V_0$, radius $R$, and imaginary-part range $R_i$. They compared these results to calculations with a Woods-Saxon potential and to experimental data from Thomas (1986), and also tested the effect of decoupling the real and imaginary range parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C reaction, the best-fit square-well potential has $V_0 = +5.8$ MeV and $R = 7.50$ fm, yielding a nuclear potential depth of only $V_0 - V_c = 1.1$ MeV (Fowler 1975).
- For $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O, a square-well fit with $V_0 = 9.4$ MeV, $R = 8.13$ fm, and $W_0 = 2.1$ MeV reproduces experimental data equally well as a Woods-Saxon potential with $V_0 = -54.5$ MeV, $R = 6.5$ fm, and $a = 0.45$ fm.
- A shallow Woods-Saxon potential with $V_0 = -4.3$ MeV and $R = 8.13$ fm also fits the $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O data, showing the same shallow-large-radius trade-off as the square-well model.
- Setting the square-well depth to $V_0 = 0$ MeV with $R = 8.13$ fm overestimates the astrophysical S-factor below 10 MeV.
- Decoupling the imaginary range ($R_i = 6.5$ fm) from the real range ($R = 8.13$ fm) requires deepening $V_0$ to 7.0 MeV but yields a less satisfactory fit than the equal-range case.

## Context

This work extends the square-well model of Michaud and Fowler (1970) and Fowler (1975), which had been successfully applied to $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O, and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O reactions but produced anomalously shallow potentials. The authors clarify that this shallowness is a geometric artifact of the square-well's sharp boundary, not evidence for a repulsive core as speculated in earlier work (e.g., Tamagaki 1965, Baye 1982). The result warns that extrapolations of square-well fits to astrophysical energies must be treated with caution, even though the model remains simple and convenient for fitting data.
