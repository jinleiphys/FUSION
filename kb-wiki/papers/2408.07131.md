# Search for baryon junctions in e+A collisions at the Electron Ion Collider

> Constituent quarks in a nucleon are the essential elements in the standard ``quark model" associated with the electric charge, spin, mass, and baryon number of a nucleon. Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) describes nucleon as a composite object containing current quarks (valence quarks and sea (anti-)quarks) and gluons. These subatomic elements and their interactions are known to contribute in complex ways to the overall nucleon spin and mass. In the early development of QCD theory in the 1970s, an alternative hypothesis postulated that the baryon number might manifest itself through a non-perturbative configuration of gluon fields forming a Y-shaped topology known as the gluon junction. In this work, we propose to test such hypothesis by measuring (i) the Regge intercept of the net-baryon distributions for $e$+($p$)Au collisions, (ii) baryon and charge transport in the isobaric ratio between $e$+Ru and $e$+Zr collisions, and (iii) target flavor dependence of proton and antiproton yields at large rapidity, transported from the hydrogen and deuterium targets in $e+p$(d) collisions. Our study indicates that these measurements at the EIC can help determine what carries the baryon number.

# Search for baryon junctions in e+A collisions at the Electron Ion Collider

## Key claim

The authors propose that three specific measurements at the future Electron Ion Collider (EIC), the Regge intercept of net-baryon distributions in e+(p)Au collisions, the isobaric ratio between e+Ru and e+Zr collisions, and target flavor dependence of proton and antiproton yields, can determine whether the baryon number is carried by valence quarks or by a non-perturbative Y-shaped gluon field configuration known as the baryon junction. However, the study uses the BeAGLE and PYTHIA models which incorporate only the valence quark scenario, so the results serve as baseline predictions for that scenario rather than as evidence for or against the baryon junction hypothesis.

## Method

The authors used version 1.03 of the BeAGLE Monte Carlo model, a hybrid framework combining DPMJet, PYTHIA6, PyQM, FLUKA, and LHAPDF5, to simulate electron-nucleus collisions at 10x40 GeV. They generated approximately 0.3 billion events for each collision system (e+p, e+Au, e+Ru, e+Zr, e+p, e+d) and analyzed net-baryon rapidity distributions, isobaric ratios of net-baryon to net-charge differences, and net-proton yields as functions of the hadronic final state mass W.

## Key numbers

- The extracted Regge intercept slope for net-baryon distributions in e+p collisions at 10x40 GeV is α_J = 1.5, which exceeds the theoretical baryon junction expectation of α_J = 0.5 and is consistent with the valence quark scenario implemented in PYTHIA.
- The isobaric ratio R(Isobar) = (B × ΔZ/A)/ΔQ for e+Ru and e+Zr collisions at 10x40 GeV shows values less than unity, consistent with the valence quark stopping scenario where R(Isobar) ≤ 1.0.
- The EMC Collaboration data for μ+p versus μ+d at 280 GeV muon energy on fixed targets shows target flavor independence within uncertainties, while the Lund model and BeAGLE model predict approximately 20% higher proton yield from the proton target compared to the deuteron target.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal by Kharzeev (1996) that baryon number might be carried by a Y-shaped gluon junction rather than by valence quarks, and builds on recent studies by Lewis (2022) and Sinha (2023) suggesting that correlations between stopped baryons and charges can differentiate the two scenarios. The authors provide baseline predictions using the BeAGLE model for the EIC, which is expected to begin operations in the 2030s. The results enable future EIC measurements to test the baryon junction hypothesis by comparing experimental data against these valence-quark-scenario baselines, particularly addressing the puzzle of baryon asymmetry at mid-rapidity observed in heavy-ion collisions by the NA49, BRAHMS, STAR, and ALICE collaborations.
