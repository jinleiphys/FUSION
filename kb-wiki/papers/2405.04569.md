# Baryon-number -flavor separation in the topological expansion of QCD

> Gauge invariance of QCD dictates the presence of string junctions in the wave functions of baryons. In high-energy inclusive processes, these baryon junctions have been predicted to induce the separation of the flows of baryon number and flavor. In this paper we describe this phenomenon using the analog-gas model of multiparticle production proposed long time ago by Feynman and Wilson and adapted here to accommodate the topological expansion in QCD. In this framework, duality arguments suggest the existence of two degenerate junction-antijunction glueball Regge trajectories of opposite $\cal{C}$-parity with intercept close to 1/2. The corresponding results for the energy and rapidity dependence of baryon stopping are in reasonably good agreement with recent experimental findings from STAR and ALICE experiments. We show that accounting for correlations between the fragmenting strings further improves agreement with the data, and outline additional experimental tests of our picture at the existing (RHIC, LHC, JLab) and future (EIC) facilities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the separation of baryon-number and flavor flows in high-energy collisions, predicted from the gauge-invariant structure of baryon wave functions, can be described using a Feynman-Wilson gas model adapted to QCD's topological expansion; the intercept of the leading junction-antijunction glueball Regge trajectory is estimated to be close to 0.26 rather than the originally proposed 0.5, yielding a beam rapidity dependence of baryon stopping that matches the STAR experimental result of \(e^{-0.65 Y/2}\) rather than the earlier prediction of \(e^{-0.42 Y/2}\).

## Method

The authors adapt the Feynman-Wilson gas model of multiparticle production to the topological expansion of QCD, treating inclusive cross sections as grand-canonical partition functions of a gas in rapidity space. For baryon-antibaryon annihilation, they introduce a three-species gas corresponding to the three "pages" of the baryon junction topology, and relate the intercepts of junction-antijunction Regge trajectories to the pressures and inter-species correlations in this gas. The intercept of the leading \(J_0\) trajectory is then extracted by identifying the planar pressure with the Reggeon intercept and the two-species correlation with the Pomeron intercept minus one.

## Key numbers

* The intercept of the leading junction-antijunction Regge trajectory \(\alpha_{\mathbb{J}_0}\) is estimated as \(0.5 - 3 C_{RL} - C_3\), where \(C_{RL} = \alpha_\mathbb{P} - 1 \simeq 0.08\) and \(C_3\) is a three-species correlation; with \(C_3 = 0\) this gives \(\alpha_{\mathbb{J}_0} \simeq 0.26\).
* The beam rapidity dependence of baryon stopping from Eq. (1) is \(e^{(\alpha_{\mathbb{J}_0} + \alpha_\mathbb{P} - 2)Y/2}\); using \(\alpha_{\mathbb{J}_0} \simeq 0.26\) and \(\alpha_\mathbb{P} \simeq 1.08\) yields \(e^{-0.66 Y/2}\), compared to the STAR experimental result of \(e^{-0.65 Y/2}\) from Lewis (2022).
* The soft Pomeron intercept is taken as \(\alpha_\mathbb{P} \simeq 1.08\) with \(\Delta = \alpha_\mathbb{P} - 1 \simeq 0.08\), from the Particle Data Group (2022).
* The original prediction from Rossi (1977) used \(\alpha_{\mathbb{J}_0} \simeq 0.5\), giving a beam rapidity dependence of \(e^{-0.42 Y/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the topological expansion approach to QCD with baryons developed by Rossi and Veneziano (1977), who introduced the baryon junction and predicted junction-antijunction glueball Regge trajectories. It revisits the prediction for baryon stopping made by Kharzeev (1996) using the Mueller-Kancheli approach, updating the intercept of the \(J_0\) trajectory to account for correlations between fragmenting strings. The results are compared to recent baryon stopping data from the STAR experiment at RHIC reported by Lewis (2022), and the framework enables predictions for future experimental tests at RHIC, LHC, JLab, and the EIC.
