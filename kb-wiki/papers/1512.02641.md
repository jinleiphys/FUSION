# Retarded Correlators in Kinetic Theory: Branch Cuts, Poles and Hydrodynamic Onset Transitions

> In this work the collective modes of an effective kinetic theory description based on the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation applicable to gauge theories at weak but finite coupling and low frequencies are studied. Real time retarded two-point correlators of the energy-momentum tensor and the R-charge current are calculated at finite temperature in flat space-times for large N gauge theories. It is found that the real time correlators possess logarithmic branch cuts which in the limit of large coupling disappear and give rise to non-hydrodynamic poles that are reminiscent of quasi-normal modes in black holes. In addition to branch cuts, correlators can have simple hydrodynamic poles, generalizing the concept of hydrodynamic modes to intermediate wavelength. Surprisingly, the hydrodynamic poles cease to exist for some critical value of the wavelength and coupling reminiscent of the properties of onset transitions.

# Retarded Correlators in Kinetic Theory: Branch Cuts, Poles and Hydrodynamic Onset Transitions

## Key claim

The analytic structure of retarded two-point correlators in effective kinetic theory undergoes a qualitative change as a function of coupling: logarithmic branch cuts present at weak coupling disappear in the naive strong-coupling limit and are replaced by non-hydrodynamic poles reminiscent of black hole quasi-normal modes, while the hydrodynamic poles themselves cease to exist at some critical value of wavelength and coupling, resembling an onset transition.

## Method

The author solves the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time (BGK) approximation for large N gauge theories at finite temperature, calculating retarded two-point correlators of the energy-momentum tensor and R-charge current in flat space-time. The relaxation times for momentum relaxation (τ_R) and charge diffusion (τ_D) are taken from weak-coupling microscopic calculations, scaling as τ ∝ 1/(λ² ln λ). The weak-coupling limit is taken as τT → ∞ with ω/T and k/T fixed, while the naive strong-coupling limit is taken as τT → 0 with ω/T and k/T fixed, using Padé approximants to extract the analytic structure.

## Key numbers

- Diffusion constant: D = τ_D/3, with diffusion relaxation time τ_Δ = τ_D
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = τ_R T/5, with shear relaxation time τ_π = τ_R
- Hydrodynamic diffusion pole location: ω₀^(D) = -i (τ_D/3) k² + O(k³)
- Non-hydrodynamic pole locations for R-charge diffusion: ω_{1,2}^(D) = -i/τ_D ± 2k/√15 + O(k²)
- Sound channel hydrodynamic poles: ω_±^(0) = ± c_s k - (2i τ_R k²)/15
- Shear channel hydrodynamic pole: ω₀^(1) = -i τ_R k²/5
- Shear channel non-hydrodynamic poles: ω_{1,2}^(1) = -i/τ_R ± τ_R k √(8/35)
- Spin-2 channel non-hydrodynamic poles: ω_{1,2}^(2) = -i/τ_R ± k √(1/7)

## Context

This work extends the numerical study of Hong (2010) by providing a fully analytic treatment of thermal correlators in kinetic theory, enabling explicit limits of weak coupling, strong coupling, and hydrodynamics. The results confirm the qualitative picture suggested by Hartnoll (2005) that branch cuts at weak coupling give way to poles at strong coupling, and generalize the concept of hydrodynamic modes to intermediate wavelength. The findings enable systematic study of the transition between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic behavior in gauge theories, with implications for understanding transport in strongly coupled systems such as quark-gluon plasmas and cold quantum gases.
