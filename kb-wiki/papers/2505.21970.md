# Constraints on the strength of first-order phase transition and its relation to nucleon mass

> We investigate the constraints on the strength of first-order phase transitions in neutron star matter and its relation to the origin of nucleon mass. By combining the parity doublet model for the hadronic phase, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for quark matter, and the integral constraint framework for intermediate densities, we construct equation of states spanning the full density range relevant to neutron stars. Our approach systematically explores how the chiral invariant mass $m_0$ affects the allowable properties of first-order quark-hadron phase transitions. Through comparison with recent neutron star observations, we establish a inverse correlation between the allowed phase transition strength and the chiral invariant mass. Our results demonstrate a direct connection between fundamental questions about the microscopic origin of nucleon mass and macroscopic neutron star observables, providing a novel astrophysical probe of chiral dynamics and QCD physics under extreme conditions.

## Key claim

The paper establishes an inverse correlation between the allowed strength of first-order quark-hadron phase transitions in neutron star matter and the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\), but this result is conditional on the specific choice of hadronic model (parity doublet model), quark model (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio), and the integral constraint framework for intermediate densities, with the constraints depending on the confidence level (1\(\sigma\) or 2\(\sigma\)) adopted for comparison with observations.

## Method

The authors combine the parity doublet model (PDM) for the hadronic phase at low densities, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the quark phase at high densities, and the integral constraint framework of Komoltsev (2021) to construct equations of state spanning the full density range relevant to neutron stars. They systematically vary the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\) (from 600 MeV upward) and the strength of first-order phase transitions parameterized by the density jump \(\delta n_B\), then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and compare with NICER and LIGO-Virgo observations.

## Key numbers

- For hadronic EOS with \(m_0 = 600\) MeV and matching density \(n_L = 1.5 n_0\), the maximum allowable first-order transition strength at 1\(\sigma\) confidence level is \(\delta n_B \leq 1.12 n_0\) with corresponding energy density jump \(\delta \varepsilon \leq 182\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- At 2\(\sigma\) confidence level for the same parameters, the constraints become \(\delta n_B \leq 1.68 n_0\) with \(\delta \varepsilon \leq 273\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- For \(m_0 = 600\) MeV with \(\delta n_B = 1.5 n_0\), the mass-radius curve violates PSR J0030+0451 constraints at the 1\(\sigma\) level (radius \(R = 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5}\) km for mass \(M = 1.44^{+0.07}_{-0.07} M_\odot\)).
- At \(\delta n_B = 2 n_0\) with \(m_0 = 600\) MeV and \(n_L = 1.5 n_0\), no parameter combinations in the quark phase can support neutron stars with masses over \(2 M_\odot\).
- The low-density boundary is set at \(n_L = 1.5 n_0\) and the high-density boundary at \(\mu_H = 2600\) MeV, \(n_H = 6.47\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(P_H = 3823\) MeV/fm\(^3\) from perturbative QCD.

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model studies of Gao (2022, 2024) and the integral constraint framework of Komoltsev (2021) by directly connecting the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\), a parameter representing the portion of nucleon mass surviving chiral symmetry restoration, to observable neutron star properties. The results contradict the possibility of arbitrarily strong first-order phase transitions and instead show that the allowed transition strength decreases with increasing \(m_0\), providing a novel astrophysical probe of chiral dynamics. This enables future work to use neutron star observations to constrain the microscopic origin of nucleon mass, and suggests that twin star scenarios (Kampfer 1981, Glendenning 1998, Benic 2014) may be ruled out for certain parameter ranges.
