# Pion charge radius from pion+electron elastic scattering data

> With the aim of extracting the pion charge radius, we analyse extant precise pion+electron elastic scattering data on $Q^2 \in [0.015,0.144]\,$GeV$^2$ using a method based on interpolation via continued fractions augmented by statistical sampling. The scheme avoids any assumptions on the form of function used for the representation of data and subsequent extrapolation onto $Q^2\simeq 0$. Combining results obtained from the two available data sets, we obtain $r_π= 0.640(7)\,$fm, a value $2.4\,σ$ below today's commonly quoted average. The tension may be relieved by collection and similar analysis of new precise data that densely cover a domain which reaches well below $Q^2 = 0.015\,$GeV$^2$. Considering available kaon+electron elastic scattering data sets, our analysis reveals that they contain insufficient information to extract an objective result for the charged-kaon radius, $r_K$. New data with much improved precision, low-$Q^2$ reach and coverage are necessary before a sound result for $r_K$ can be recorded.

## Key claim

The authors extract a pion charge radius of \(r_\pi = 0.640(7)\,\)fm from reanalyzing existing NA7 pion+electron elastic scattering data using a form-unbiased interpolation method, a value \(2.4\,\sigma\) below the commonly quoted Particle Data Group average of \(0.659 \pm 0.004\,\)fm. The result is conditional on the specific subset of data points used and the range of interpolation parameters validated by the method; the authors caution that new data reaching below \(Q^2 = 0.015\,\)GeV\(^2\) are needed to resolve the tension.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical Schlessinger Point Method (SPM), a continued-fraction interpolation scheme augmented by statistical bootstrap resampling, to two NA7 Collaboration data sets on the pion elastic form factor \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) covering \(Q^2 \in [0.015,0.144]\,\)GeV\(^2\). For each data set, they generate thousands of interpolating functions from randomly selected subsets of data points, smooth the data with a roughness penalty, and extrapolate to \(Q^2=0\) to extract the radius via \(r_\pi^2 = -6\,dF_\pi/dQ^2|_{Q^2=0}\). The method is validated on synthetic data generated from monopole, dipole, and Gaussian form factors with known radii.

## Key numbers

* Pion charge radius from 1984 NA7 data (Amendolia 1984): \(r_\pi = 0.636 \pm 0.009_{\rm stat}\,\)fm.
* Pion charge radius from 1986 NA7 data (Amendolia 1986): \(r_\pi = 0.648 \pm 0.013_{\rm stat}\,\)fm.
* Combined error-weighted average from both NA7 data sets: \(r_\pi = 0.640 \pm 0.007_{\rm stat}\,\)fm.
* This value is \(2.4\,\sigma\) below the Particle Data Group 2020 average of \(r_\pi = 0.659 \pm 0.004\,\)fm.
* For the charged kaon, the SPM analysis returns a roughness penalty of \(\epsilon = 1\) (maximal smoothing), yielding \(r_K = 0.53\,\)fm from a linear least-squares fit, consistent with the commonly quoted \(r_K = 0.560 \pm 0.031\,\)fm but indicating insufficient data for an objective extraction.

## Context

This work extends the methodology used to resolve the proton radius puzzle (e.g., the PRad collaboration's approach for the proton, Xiong 2019) to the pion, applying a form-unbiased interpolation scheme that avoids the systematic errors inherent in assuming a specific functional form for the form factor. The results contradict the earlier PDG average (Tanabashi 2018) and support a downward shift in the pion radius, while highlighting that available kaon+electron scattering data (Dally 1980, Amendolia 1986) are too sparse and imprecise for a reliable determination of the charged-kaon radius. The analysis enables future high-luminosity experiments at facilities such as the CERN SPS to provide definitive measurements of both pion and kaon charge radii.
