# Exotics from Heavy Ion Collisions

> Discriminating hadronic molecular and multi-quark states is a long standing problem in hadronic physics. We propose here to utilize relativistic heavy ion collisions to resolve this problem, as exotic hadron yields are expected to be strongly affected by their structures. Using the coalescence model, we find that the exotic hadron yield relative to the statistical model result is typically an order of magnitude smaller for a compact multi-quark state, and larger by a factor of two or more for a loosely bound hadronic molecule. We further find that some of the newly proposed heavy exotic states could be produced and realistically measured at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

Using the coalescence model, the yield of an exotic hadron in relativistic heavy ion collisions relative to the statistical model prediction is typically an order of magnitude smaller for a compact multi-quark state and larger by a factor of two or more for a loosely bound hadronic molecule, providing a method to discriminate between these two structural interpretations.

## Method

The authors calculated hadron yields at mid-rapidity using the coalescence model with harmonic oscillator (Gaussian) wave functions, assuming a fire-cylinder model for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV at RHIC. They compared these yields to those from the statistical model, using the ratio R_h = N_h^coal / N_h^stat to classify hadron structures. For compact multi-quark states, the oscillator frequency omega was fixed by fitting normal hadron yields; for hadronic molecules, omega was determined from the radius or binding energy of the molecule.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the statistical model parameters are: V_H = 1908 fm^3, T_H = 175 MeV, mu_B = 20 MeV, mu_S = 10 MeV, and charm fugacity gamma_c = 6.40 to reproduce total charm quark number N_c = 3.
- The coalescence factor for adding an s-wave quark is 0.360, and for a p-wave quark is 0.093, using quark numbers N_u = N_d = 245 and N_s = 150 at hadronization (T = 175 MeV, V = 1000 fm^3).
- For the f_0(980) meson, the measured yield from STAR (preliminary) gives a ratio f_0(980)/rho^0 ~ 0.2, leading to N_f0 ~ 8, compared to the statistical model prediction of ~5.6 and coalescence model results of 3.8 (q-qbar), 0.73 (s-sbar), 0.10 (q-qbar-s-sbar tetraquark), and 13 (K-Kbar molecule).
- For Lambda(1405) as a hadronic molecule, R_h = 1.1 for a small size (omega = 174 MeV) and R_h = 4.9 for a larger size (omega = 20.5 MeV) from a coupled channel analysis.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model approach of Chen (2003) and the statistical model framework of Andronic (2005) to exotic hadrons, proposing heavy ion collisions as a new tool to resolve the long-standing problem of distinguishing hadronic molecules from multi-quark states. The method enables future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC to test the structure of specific exotic candidates such as f_0(980), X(3872), Z+(4430), and Theta+(1530), and can rule out a pure tetraquark configuration for f_0(980) based on preliminary STAR data.
