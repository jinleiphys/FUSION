# Treatment of like-particle pairing with quartets

> The ground state correlations induced by a general pairing Hamiltonian in a finite system of like fermions are described in terms of four-body correlated structures (quartets). These are real superpositions of products of two pairs of particles in time-reversed states. Quartets are determined variationally through an iterative sequence of diagonalizations of the Hamiltonian in restricted model spaces and are, in principle, all distinct from one another. The ground state is represented as a product of quartets to which, depending on the number of particles (supposed to be even, in any case), an extra collective pair is added. The extra pair is also determined variationally. In case of pairing in a spherically symmetric mean field, both the quartets and the extra pair (if any) are characterized by a total angular momentum J=0. Realistic applications of the quartet formalism are carried out for the Sn isotopes with the valence neutrons in the 50-82 neutron shell. Exact ground state correlation energies, occupation numbers and pair transfer matrix elements are reproduced to a very high degree of precision. The formalism also lends itself to a straightforward and accurate description of the lowest seniority 0 and 2 excited states of the pairing Hamiltonian. A simplified representation of the ground state as a product of identical quartets is eventually discussed and found to improve considerably upon the more traditional particle-number projected-BCS approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ground state of a general pairing Hamiltonian for like fermions can be represented to very high precision as a product of distinct four-body correlated structures (quartets), with an extra collective pair added for odd particle numbers, and that this representation is basically exact after sufficient iterative cycles, a result that holds for realistic nuclear systems like Sn isotopes, not just for the exactly-solvable constant-pairing Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors construct quartets as real superpositions of products of two pairs of particles in time-reversed states, then determine them variationally through an iterative sequence of diagonalizations of the Hamiltonian in restricted model spaces. For even numbers of pairs, the ground state is a product of distinct quartets; for odd numbers, an extra collective pair is added and also determined variationally. The procedure is applied to Sn isotopes with valence neutrons in the 50-82 shell, using single-particle energies and pairing strengths from Zelevinsky and Volya, and results are compared to exact solutions obtained via the seniority scheme and SU(2) quasispin algebra.

## Key numbers

- Relative error in ground state correlation energy for Sn isotopes: already at the lowest level of approximation (1 iterative cycle), the approach provides "remarkably good agreement" with exact results; after multiple cycles the agreement becomes "basically exact" (no single numerical error value given for the final result, but the text states progressive improvement toward exactness).
- The dimension of each restricted model space for diagonalization is Omega(Omega-1)/2, where Omega is the number of single-particle levels; for the Sn isotopes with 5 orbitals, this yields spaces of only a few hundred states, compared to the full shell model space of up to 6x10^8 basis states.
- For the identical-quartet approximation: the relative errors in ground state correlation energy differ from the independent-quartet approach by "something like two orders of magnitude" (exact numerical values not provided in the truncated text).
- The particle-number projected-BCS (PBCS) approach shows the worst approximation among the three compared methods, though even PBCS relative errors remain "within a quite acceptable range" (no specific numbers given).

## Context

This work extends the exact quartet representation previously known only for the constant-pairing Hamiltonian (Richardson, 1960s) to general pairing Hamiltonians, using a numerical variational method inspired by an earlier pair-based approach by Sambataro (2012). It improves considerably upon the particle-number projected-BCS approach (Ring, 1980) and upon the distinct-pair formalism of Sambataro (2012). The quartet formalism enables accurate description of seniority 0 and 2 excited states and reduces the computational cost from shell model spaces of up to 6x10^8 basis states to spaces of only a few hundred states. Future work aims to extend the method to proton-neutron pairing with isovector and isoscalar forces.
