# Electric polarizability of nuclei from a longitudinal sum rule

> The nuclear electric polarizability is theoretically analyzed using a sum rule derived from the longitudinal part of the forward Compton amplitude. Beyond the leading dipole contribution, this approach leads to the presence of potential-dependent terms that do not show up in previous analyses. The significance of these new contributions is illustrated by performing an explicit calculation for a proton-neutron system interacting via a separable potential.

## Key claim

The electric polarizability of nuclei receives additional potential-dependent contributions beyond the leading dipole term and the previously known \(\langle \mathbf{r}^2 \rangle\) correction, provided one respects gauge invariance, analyticity, and unitarity by using a longitudinal sum rule before taking the nonrelativistic limit.

## Method

The authors start from an unsubtracted dispersion relation for the longitudinal forward virtual Compton amplitude, which yields a sum rule for the electric polarizability \(\alpha\) in terms of the longitudinal response function \(R_L(|\mathbf{q}|,\omega)\) in the quasi-real photon limit. They expand the charge operator in powers of momentum transfer, perform the integration over excitation energy, and rewrite the result using energy-weighted sum rules expressed as commutators and anticommutators of the multipole operators \(C_n\) with the nuclear Hamiltonian \(H = T + V\). The final expression for \(\alpha\) is then evaluated explicitly for a proton-neutron system interacting via a separable Yamaguchi potential.

## Key numbers

* For the Yamaguchi potential, the electric polarizability up to order \(M^{-1}\) is \(\alpha = \frac{e^2}{4\pi} \left[ \frac{7}{12 M \epsilon_B^2} + \frac{1}{2M^2\epsilon_B} - \frac{3}{16 M^2 \epsilon_B} + \frac{9}{8 M^3} \right]\), where \(\epsilon_B = \beta^2/(2M)\) is the binding energy and \(M\) is the total mass.
* The potential-dependent contribution from the \(m''_1(0)\) term is \(\frac{1}{4} \langle 0|[C_2,[V,C_2]]|0\rangle - \frac{1}{3} \langle 0|[C_1,[V,C_3]]|0\rangle = -\frac{3}{16 M^2 \epsilon_B}\) for the \(Z=1\) case.
* The \(\langle \mathbf{r}^2 \rangle\) term contributes \(\frac{Z}{3 M_A} \langle 0| \sum_{i=1}^Z \mathbf{r}_i^2|0\rangle\) at order \(M_A^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic calculations of Petrun'kin (1964), Ericson and Hufner (1973), and Friar (1975), which only yielded the leading dipole term and the \(\langle \mathbf{r}^2 \rangle\) correction. By using the longitudinal sum rule derived by Bernabeu and Tarrach (1975), the authors show that potential-dependent terms of order \(V\) and \(V^2/M_A\) appear and are of the same order of magnitude as the \(\langle \mathbf{r}^2 \rangle\) term when the potential is treated as order \(1/M\). The result enables future studies of exchange-current contributions to the nuclear electric polarizability for realistic nuclear potentials.
