# Photo-production of Nucleon Resonances and Nucleon Spin Structure Function in the Resonance Region

> The photo-production of nucleon resonances is calculated based on a chiral constituent quark model including both relativistic corrections H{rel} and two-body exchange currents, and it is shown that these effects play an important role. We also calculate the first moment of the nucleon spin structure function g1 (x,Q^2) in the resonance region, and obtain a sign-changing point around Q^2 ~ 0.27 {GeV}^2 for the proton.

# Photo-production of Nucleon Resonances and Nucleon Spin Structure Function in the Resonance Region

## Key claim
The chiral constituent quark model, including both relativistic corrections (spin-orbit and non-additive terms) and two-body exchange currents, improves agreement with experimental photo-production amplitudes for nucleon resonances up to 2 GeV, but the two-body exchange currents have only a small effect on the first moment of the nucleon spin structure function. The sign-changing point for the proton's generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule is found at approximately Q^2 = 0.27 GeV^2, which lies between earlier predictions of 0.22 GeV^2 (Burkert and Ioffe, 1994) and 0.53 GeV^2 (Li and Dong, 1996).

## Method
The authors calculated photo-production helicity amplitudes for all nucleon resonances with masses below 2.0 GeV using a chiral constituent quark model that includes one-gluon, one-pion, one-sigma, and confinement exchange potentials. The total electromagnetic current was constructed as a sum of one-body non-relativistic reduction, spin-orbit, non-additive terms, and two-body exchange currents (gluon, pion-pair, pionic, confinement, and sigma currents), with model wave functions in the standard SU(6) x O(3) basis and parameters from Buchmann et al. (1991). For the spin structure function, the resonant contributions were combined with a non-resonant part modeled after Burkert and Ioffe (1994) using vector meson intermediate states, and the first moment Gamma(Q^2) was computed via Breit-Wigner parametrization of the absorption cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Sign-changing point for the proton's generalized GDH sum rule: Q^2 ~ 0.27 GeV^2
- Photo-production amplitude for P33(1232) proton A_{3/2}: total -182 (units of 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), compared to experimental -255 +/- 8
- Photo-production amplitude for D13(1520) proton A_{3/2}: total 117 (units of 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), compared to experimental 166 +/- 5
- Photo-production amplitude for S11(1535) proton A_{1/2}: total 80 (units of 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), compared to experimental 90 +/- 30
- Photo-production amplitude for F15(1680) proton A_{3/2}: total 104 (units of 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), compared to experimental 133 +/- 12
- Photo-production amplitude for P11(1440) proton A_{1/2}: total -65 (units of 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), compared to experimental -55 +/- 4
- Non-resonant part parameters: Gamma_p(Q^2_m) = 0.237, Gamma_n(Q^2_m) = -0.025, c_p = 1.387, c_n = -2.753

## Context
This work extends the calculations of Meyer et al. (1997), who studied photo-production of resonances below sqrt(s) = 1.6 GeV using two-body exchange currents but neglected relativistic corrections, by including both effects for resonances up to 2.0 GeV. It also extends the work of Close and Li (1990) and Li and Close (1990) by incorporating two-body exchange currents alongside the relativistic corrections. For the spin structure function, the results are compared with the phenomenological model of Burkert and Ioffe (1994) and with chiral perturbation theory predictions from Ji, Kao, and Osborne (2000). The calculation enables more reliable saturation of the GDH sum rule and the first moment of g1 in the resonance region, though the authors note that configuration mixing effects and the q^2 dependence of helicity amplitudes remain to be included in future work.
