# Neutrino potential for neutrinoless double beta decay

> Neutrino potential for neutrinoless double beta decay is studied with focusing on its statistical property. The statistics provide a gross view of understanding amplitude of constitutional components of the nuclear matrix element.

## Key claim

The paper statistically characterizes the neutrino potential part of the nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless double beta decay, showing that the amplitude contributions from different harmonic-oscillator basis states follow a specific hierarchy and that the tensor part involves significant cancellation between positive and negative values, while Fermi and Gamow-Teller parts are always positive and nearly proportional.

## Method

The author computes the neutrino potential matrix elements ⟨n′l′|Hα(√2ρ)|nl⟩ under the closure approximation for massless neutrinos, using harmonic-oscillator basis states with principal quantum numbers n,n′ = 0–3 and orbital angular momenta l,l′ = 0–6. The neutrino potential includes dipole form factors with finite-size parameters ΛV = 850 MeV and ΛA = 1086 MeV, and the closure parameter ⟨E⟩ is fixed to 0.5 MeV. The frequency distribution, ranking of largest contributions, and correlations between Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and tensor parts are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The largest contribution for all three parts comes from the (n l n′ l′) = (0 0 0 0) transition: Fermi value 1.626, Gamow-Teller value 1.488, tensor value 0.2249.
- For the tensor part, the total sum 19.88 results from cancellation between +23.128 and −3.248.
- The average of nonzero components is 0.350 for Fermi, 0.324 for Gamow-Teller, and 0.025 for the tensor part.
- The ten largest contributions cover 45.0% of the total for Fermi, 46.1% for Gamow-Teller, and 10.1% for the tensor part.
- Contributions with l = l′ = 0 cover 27.1% of the total for Fermi and Gamow-Teller parts, and 7.2% for the tensor part.
- The minimum tensor value is −0.0450 for (n l n′ l′) = (0 4 1 0) and (1 0 0 4).
- Numerical integration uses qmax = 2000 MeV and rmax = 10 fm to obtain 3-digit accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay by focusing on the statistical properties of the neutrino potential, complementing large-scale shell-model calculations such as those by Iwata (2015). The results are valid for all double-beta decay candidates within the given oscillator basis and enable systematic understanding of which orbital transitions dominate the amplitude, with the cancellation components of the nuclear matrix element left for future study. The positive correlation between Fermi and Gamow-Teller parts is nontrivial given their different form factors, as noted in prior work by Tomoda (1991), Horoi (2010), and Senkov (2013).
