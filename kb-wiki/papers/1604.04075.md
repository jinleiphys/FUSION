# Atomic Parameters for the $2p^53p~^2[3/2]_2 - 2p^53s~^2[3/2]^o_2$ Transition of Ne I relevant in nuclear physics

> We calculated the magnetic dipole hyperfine interaction constants and the electric field gradients of $2p^53p~^2[3/2]_2$ and $2p^53s~^2[3/2]^o_2$ levels of Ne I by using the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock method. The electronic factors contributing to the isotope shifts were also estimated for the $λ= 614.5$ nm transition connecting these two states. Electron correlation and relativistic effects including the Breit interaction were investigated in details. Combining with recent measurements, we extracted the nuclear quadrupole moment values for $^{20}$Ne and $^{23}$Ne with a smaller uncertainty than the current available data. Isotope shifts in the $2p^53p~^2[3/2]_2 - 2p^53s~^2[3/2]^o_2$ transition based on the present calculated field- and mass-shift parameters are in good agreement with the experimental values. However, the field shifts in this transition are two or three orders of magnitude smaller than the mass shifts, making rather difficult to deduce changes in nuclear charge mean square radii. According to our theoretical predictions, we suggest to use instead transitions connecting levels arising from the $2p^53s$ configuration to the ground state, for which the normal mass shift and specific mass shift contributions counteract each other, producing relatively small mass shifts that are only one order of magnitude larger than relatively large field shifts, especially for the $2p^53s~^2[1/2]^o_1 - 2p^6~^1S_0$ transition.

## Key claim

The authors calculate atomic parameters for the $2p^53p~^2[3/2]_2 - 2p^53s~^2[3/2]^o_2$ transition of Ne I using the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock method, but find that the field shifts in this transition are two or three orders of magnitude smaller than the mass shifts, making it unsuitable for deducing changes in nuclear charge mean square radii. They instead suggest using transitions from the $2p^53s$ configuration to the ground state, where mass shifts are only one order of magnitude larger than relatively large field shifts.

## Method

The authors used the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) method with the GRASP2K package to calculate magnetic dipole hyperfine interaction constants, electric field gradients, and isotope shift parameters for the $2p^53p~^2[3/2]_2$ and $2p^53s~^2[3/2]^o_2$ levels of Ne I. They constructed configuration spaces using an active space approach with single and double excitations from reference configurations, including up to nine layers of virtual orbitals with angular symmetries up to $l=5$, and incorporated the Breit interaction through relativistic configuration interaction calculations. The authors also applied a single- and double-multireference approach to account for higher-order correlation effects between valence orbitals.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear electric quadrupole moment for $^{21}$Ne: 101.44(20) mb, extracted using the calculated electric field gradient $q=-4.681$ a.u. and experimental $B$-factors from Geithner et al. (2005)
- Nuclear electric quadrupole moment for $^{23}$Ne: 141.8(6.0) mb, extracted using the same electric field gradient value
- Field-shift factor for the $2p^53p~^2[3/2]_2 - 2p^53s~^2[3/2]^o_2$ transition: 32.3 MHz/fm$^2$
- Field shift between $^{19}$Ne and $^{20}$Ne for this transition: 0.549 MHz
- Field-shift factors for transitions from $2p^53s$ levels to ground state: 137.13 to 138.34 MHz/fm$^2$
- Mass-shift factor for the $2p^53s~^2[1/2]^o_1 - 2p^6~^1S_0$ transition: -34 GHz u, with normal mass shift -2269 GHz u and specific mass shift 2235 GHz u

## Context

This work extends earlier empirical and approximate theoretical calculations of atomic parameters for Ne I that were used to extract nuclear moments from collinear laser spectroscopy experiments at ISOLDE/CERN (Geithner et al., 2000, 2005, 2008; Marinova et al., 2011). The authors improve upon the finite-element multiconfiguration Hartree-Fock calculations of Sundholm and Olsen (1992) by including electron correlation involving the $1s$ core and relativistic effects including the Breit interaction. The extracted nuclear quadrupole moments for $^{21}$Ne and $^{23}$Ne have smaller uncertainties than previous values from Grosof et al. (1958), Ducas et al. (1972), Sundholm and Olsen (1992), Geithner et al. (2005), and De Rydt et al. (2013). The work enables more accurate extraction of nuclear charge radii by identifying the $2p^53s~^2[1/2]^o_1 - 2p^6~^1S_0$ transition as a better candidate for isotope shift measurements.
