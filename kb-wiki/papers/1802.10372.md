# Safe and free instantons

> We investigate the instanton dynamics of asymptotically safe and free quantum field theories featuring respectively controllable ultraviolet and infrared fixed points. We start by briefly reviewing the salient points about the instanton calculus for pure Yang Mills (YM) and QCD. We then move on to determine the role of instantons within the controllable regime of the QCD conformal window. In this region we add a fermion-mass operator and determine the density of instantons per unit volume as function of the fermion mass. Finally, for the first time, we extend the instanton calculus to asymptotically safe theories.

## Key claim

The paper extends instanton calculus to asymptotically safe gauge theories for the first time, and also applies it to the perturbative (Banks-Zaks) regime of the QCD conformal window, but only within the controllable region where the infrared fixed point is weakly coupled and the analysis relies on a two-loop improved running coupling.

## Method

The authors first review the one-instanton density for pure Yang-Mills and QCD, including the 't Hooft result and the variational approach of Diakonov and Petrov (1983, 1985) for an interacting instanton liquid. They then adapt this formalism to the Banks-Zaks conformal window by using the two-loop beta function (which yields an infrared fixed point) and introducing a fermion-mass operator, computing the instanton density per unit volume as a function of the fermion mass. Finally, they generalize the instanton calculus to asymptotically safe theories, again considering the fermion mass dependence, now affecting the infrared trivial fixed point.

## Key numbers

* For pure Yang-Mills at large \(N_c\), the average instanton size is of order 1 (in units of \(\Lambda_{YM}^{-1}\)) and is quite independent of \(N_c\), while the density of instantons increases as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\).
* The vacuum energy density for pure Yang-Mills in the instanton liquid model is \(\mathcal{E} = -\frac{b}{4} \frac{\langle N \rangle}{V}\), where \(b = \frac{11}{3}N_c - \frac{2}{3}N_f\) and \(\langle N \rangle\) is the average instanton number.
* The topological susceptibility in the pure Yang-Mills instanton liquid is \(\chi_{top} = \langle N \rangle\).
* The two-loop running coupling for QCD uses \(b' = \frac{51}{9}N_c^2 - \frac{19}{3}N_f\).
* The Banks-Zaks expansion parameter \(\epsilon\) measures the distance from the loss of asymptotic freedom; the paper shows an example with \(\epsilon = -1/10\).

## Context

This work extends the instanton calculus from pure Yang-Mills and QCD (pioneered by 't Hooft (1976), Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), and Diakonov and Petrov (1983, 1985)) to the perturbative conformal window of QCD (the Banks-Zaks fixed point, Banks and Zakharov (1981)) and to asymptotically safe theories (Litim and Sannino (2014, 2015)). It enables the study of instanton effects in theories with controllable ultraviolet fixed points, which are relevant for dark and bright extensions of the Standard Model (Sannino (2014), Abel et al. (2017), Pelaggi et al. (2017)).
