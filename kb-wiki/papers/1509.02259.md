# Proton pygmy resonances: predictions for N=20 isotones

> We study theoretically the low-energy electric-dipole response of N=20 isotones. We present results from a quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and a continuum random-phase approximation (CRPA), and we compare them with results for the mirror Z=20 nuclei. According to our analysis, enhanced E1 strength is expected energetically well below the giant dipole resonance in the proton-rich isotones. Large amounts of E1 strength in the asymmetric N=20 isotones are predicted, unlike their equally asymmetric Z=20 mirror nuclei, pointing unambiguously to the role of structural effects such as loose binding. A proton-skin oscillation could develop especially in 46Fe. The proper description of non localized threshold transitions and the nucleon effective mass in mean-field treatments may affect theoretical predictions. We call for systematic theoretical investigations to quantify the role bulk-matter properties, in anticipation of measurements of E1 transitions in proton-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

Enhanced low-energy electric-dipole (E1) strength is predicted in proton-rich N=20 isotones, but the authors qualify that the proper description of non-localized threshold transitions and the nucleon effective mass in mean-field treatments may affect theoretical predictions, and they call for systematic theoretical investigations to quantify the role of bulk-matter properties.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary non-relativistic models: a self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with the Gogny D1S interaction, which includes pairing effects, and a continuum random-phase approximation (CRPA) with the Skyrme SLy4 interaction, which provides a proper treatment of the continuum. They calculated the isoscalar and isovector dipole response for N=20 isotones from ⁴⁰Ca to ⁴⁸Ni and compared results with mirror Z=20 nuclei, analyzing transition densities and energy-weighted sums to identify proton-skin oscillations.

## Key numbers

- Proton separation energies for Z=20,22,...,28 isotones: 8.32, 3.75, 3.03, 1.54, 0.692 MeV respectively, from the 2012 atomic mass evaluation
- Proton skin thickness |R_p-R_n| for ⁴⁸Ni: 0.249 fm (QRPA+D1S) and 0.281 fm (SHF+SLy4); for ⁴⁸Ca: 0.145 fm (QRPA+D1S) and 0.111 fm (SHF+SLy4)
- E1 strength summed up to 13 MeV in ⁴⁸Ni: 0.553 e²fm² (QRPA+D1S); integrated E1 strength below 10 MeV in ⁴⁸Ni: 0.242 e²fm² (CRPA+SLy4)
- Percentage of energy-weighted sum below 10 MeV in ⁴⁸Ni: 1.01% (CRPA+SLy4); in ⁴⁸Ca: 0.11% (CRPA+SLy4)
- IS-LED E1 strength in ⁴⁶Fe: 0.008 e²fm²; in ⁴⁸Ni: 0.009 e²fm² (QRPA+D1S)

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions of proton-skin oscillations in proton-rich N=20 isotones using relativistic RQRPA (PhysRevLett.94.182501, 2005) and Skyrme-CRPA (PPP2005), and contrasts with studies of neutron-rich nuclei where neutron-skin vibrations have been investigated. The results enable future experimental searches at radioactive-ion beam facilities and call for systematic theoretical investigations to disentangle the roles of the symmetry energy, shell effects, and continuum coupling in proton-rich nuclei, building on prior work by PhysRevC.77.024304 (2008) and DaG2012.
