# Antimatter $^4_Λ$H Hypernucleus Production and the $^3_Λ$H/$^3$He Puzzle in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We show that the measured yield ratio $^3_Λ$H/$^3$He ($^3_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$/$^3\overline{\text{He}}$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV can be understood within a covariant coalescence model if (anti-)$Λ$ particles freeze out earlier than (anti-)nucleons but their relative freezeout time is closer at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV than at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The earlier (anti-)$Λ$ freezeout can significantly enhance the yield of (anti)hypernucleus $^4_Λ$H ($^4_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$), leading to that $^4_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$ has a comparable abundance with $^4\overline{\text{He}}$ and thus provides an easily measured antimatter candidate heavier than $^4\overline{\text{He}}$. The future measurement on $^4_Λ$H ($^4_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$) would be very useful to understand the (anti-)$Λ$ freezeout dynamics and the production mechanism of (anti)hypernuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# Antimatter $^4_Λ$H Hypernucleus Production and the $^3_Λ$H/$^3$He Puzzle in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The measured yield ratio $^3_Λ$H/$^3$He (and its antiparticle counterpart) at both RHIC and LHC can be reproduced by a covariant coalescence model only if (anti-)$Λ$ particles freeze out earlier than (anti-)nucleons, with the relative freezeout time difference being smaller at LHC than at RHIC. The earlier (anti-)$Λ$ freezeout enhances $^4_Λ$H production enough that $^4_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$ becomes comparable in abundance to $^4\overline{\text{He}}$, making it a promising heavier antimatter candidate.

## Method
The authors employed a covariant coalescence model with a boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and a Gaussian freezeout time distribution. They extracted freezeout parameters (temperature T, transverse rapidity ρ₀, transverse radius R₀, mean freezeout time τ₀, dispersion Δτ, and fugacity ξ) by fitting measured spectra of protons, deuterons, and $^3$He from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC and from ALICE at LHC, then separately fitted Λ spectra to determine Λ freezeout parameters. To reproduce the $^3_Λ$H/$^3$He ratio, they reduced R₀ and τ₀ for Λ particles (to 71% at RHIC and 85% at LHC of the nucleon values) while keeping T and ρ₀ from direct Λ fits, representing earlier Λ freezeout.

## Key numbers
- Measured $^3_Λ$H/$^3$He ratio: 0.82 ± 0.16 (stat.) ± 0.12 (syst.) for 0-80% centrality Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV (STAR); 0.47 ± 0.10 (stat.) ± 0.13 (syst.) for central 0-10% Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (ALICE)
- Measured $^3_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$/$^3\overline{\text{He}}$ ratio: 0.89 ± 0.28 (stat.) ± 0.13 (syst.) at RHIC; 0.42 ± 0.10 (stat.) ± 0.13 (syst.) at LHC
- With earlier Λ freezeout (FOAu-Λ*), predicted $^4_Λ$H dN/dy = 1.57 × 10⁻⁶ at RHIC and 5.43 × 10⁻⁷ at LHC; $^4_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$ dN/dy = 5.43 × 10⁻⁷ at RHIC and 5.43 × 10⁻⁷ at LHC
- Enhancement factor from earlier Λ freezeout: 10.6 for $^4_Λ$H at RHIC, 4.0 at LHC
- Λ freezeout temperature from direct fits: T = 126.0 MeV at RHIC (FOAu-Λ*), T = 123.4 MeV at LHC (FOPb-Λ*)
- Nucleon freezeout temperature: T = 111.6 MeV at RHIC, T = 121.1 MeV at LHC
- $^3_Λ$H root-mean-square radius: 4.9 fm; $^3$He r_rms: 1.76 fm
- $^4_Λ$H mass: 3922.484 ± 0.01 (stat.) ± 0.09 (syst.) MeV; $^4$He mass: 3727.379 MeV
- $^4_Λ$H lifetime: 192⁻¹⁸⁺²⁰ ps; branching ratio for $^4_Λ$H → $^4$He + π⁻: about 50%

## Context
This work addresses the failure of conventional thermal models (Andronic 2011, Cleymans 2011, Pal 2013, Petran 2013) and earlier coalescence models (Zhang 2010, Xue 2012, Shao 2015) to simultaneously describe the $^3_Λ$H/$^3$He ratio at both RHIC and LHC. The multi-freezeout thermal model (Chatterjee 2014) described RHIC but failed at LHC, while the PACIAE+DCPC model (Chen 2013, She 2015) showed the opposite behavior. The proposed earlier Λ freezeout mechanism resolves this puzzle and predicts that $^4_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$ should be detectable as the next-heaviest antimatter nucleus beyond $^4\overline{\text{He}}$, with comparable or larger yields. Future measurements of $^4_Λ$H and $^4_{\overlineΛ}\overline{\text{H}}$ at RHIC and LHC can test this freezeout scenario and the production mechanism of light antihypernuclei.
