# Antimatter $^4_őõ$H Hypernucleus Production and the $^3_őõ$H/$^3$He Puzzle in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We show that the measured yield ratio $^3_őõ$H/$^3$He ($^3_{\overlineőõ}\overline{\text{H}}$/$^3\overline{\text{He}}$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV can be understood within a covariant coalescence model if (anti-)$őõ$ particles freeze out earlier than (anti-)nucleons but their relative freezeout time is closer at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV than at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The earlier (anti-)$őõ$ freezeout can significantly enhance the yield of (anti)hypernucleus $^4_őõ$H ($^4_{\overlineőõ}\overline{\text{H}}$), leading to that $^4_{\overlineőõ}\overline{\text{H}}$ has a comparable abundance with $^4\overline{\text{He}}$ and thus provides an easily measured antimatter candidate heavier than $^4\overline{\text{He}}$. The future measurement on $^4_őõ$H ($^4_{\overlineőõ}\overline{\text{H}}$) would be very useful to understand the (anti-)$őõ$ freezeout dynamics and the production mechanism of (anti)hypernuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

## Context
This work addresses the failure of conventional thermal models (Andronic 2011, Cleymans 2011, Pal 2013, Petran 2013) and earlier coalescence models (Zhang 2010, Xue 2012, Shao 2015) to simultaneously describe the $^3_őõ$H/$^3$He ratio at both RHIC and LHC. The multi-freezeout thermal model (Chatterjee 2014) described RHIC but failed at LHC, while the PACIAE+DCPC model (Chen 2013, She 2015) showed the opposite behavior. The proposed earlier őõ freezeout mechanism resolves this puzzle and predicts that $^4_{\overlineőõ}\overline{\text{H}}$ should be detectable as the next-heaviest antimatter nucleus beyond $^4\overline{\text{He}}$, with comparable or larger yields. Future measurements of $^4_őõ$H and $^4_{\overlineőõ}\overline{\text{H}}$ at RHIC and LHC can test this freezeout scenario and the production mechanism of light antihypernuclei.
