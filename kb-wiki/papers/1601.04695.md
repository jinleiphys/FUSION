# An Effective Field Theory for Forward Scattering and Factorization Violation

> Starting with QCD, we derive an effective field theory description for forward scattering and factorization violation as part of the soft-collinear effective field theory (SCET) for high energy scattering. These phenomena are mediated by long distance Glauber gluon exchanges, which are static in time, localized in the longitudinal distance, where $|t| \ll s$. In hard scattering, Glauber gluons can induce corrections which invalidate factorization. With SCET, Glauber exchange graphs can be calculated explicitly, and are distinct from graphs with soft, collinear, or ultrasoft gluons. We derive a complete basis of operators which describe the leading power effects of Glauber exchange. Key ingredients include regulating light-cone rapidity singularities and subtractions which prevent double counting. Our results include a novel all orders gauge invariant pure glue soft operator which appears between two collinear rapidity sectors. The 1-gluon Feynman rule for the soft operator coincides with the Lipatov vertex, but it also contributes to emissions with $\ge 2$ soft gluons. Our Glauber operator basis is derived using tree level and one-loop matching calculations from full QCD to SCET. The rapidity RGE yields gluon Reggeization at the amplitude level, and gives the BFKL equation for the soft and collinear functions in the forward scattering cross section. We derive an explicit rule for when eikonalization is valid, and provide a direct connection to the picture of multiple Wilson lines crossing a shockwave. In hard scattering operators Glauber subtractions for soft and collinear loop diagrams ensure that we are not sensitive to the directions for soft and collinear Wilson lines. Conversely, certain Glauber interactions can be absorbed into these soft and collinear Wilson lines by taking them to be in specific directions. We also discuss criteria for factorization violation.

## Key claim

Starting from QCD, the authors derive an effective field theory for forward scattering and factorization violation within soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), where Glauber gluon exchanges are treated as distinct operators that can be systematically separated from soft, collinear, and ultrasoft contributions, with the caveat that the formalism applies at leading power in the forward scattering limit where $|t| \ll s$.

## Method

The authors construct a complete basis of leading-power Glauber exchange operators in both SCET_I and SCET_II by performing tree-level and one-loop matching calculations from full QCD, using dimensional regularization and a rapidity regulator to handle light-cone singularities while preventing double counting between Glauber, soft, and collinear modes. They derive the gauge-invariant pure glue soft operator whose one-gluon Feynman rule coincides with the Lipatov vertex, and show that the rapidity renormalization group equation yields gluon Reggeization at the amplitude level and the BFKL equation for soft and collinear functions in forward scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The Glauber exchange potential at tree level is $V_{\rm G}(q_\perp) = -8\pi\alpha_s(\mu)/\vec q_\perp^2 = 8\pi\alpha_s(\mu)/t$, where $t$ is the Mandelstam variable and $|t| \ll s$
- The forward scattering condition is defined by $|t| \ll s$, where $s$ is the center-of-mass energy squared
- The hierarchy $|t| \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$ is required for perturbative treatment of the forward scattering vertex
- The small-$x$ relation is given by $x \sim |t|/s$ for DIS and Drell-Yan processes

## Context

This work extends the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) framework developed by Bauer et al. (2000-2002) to include Glauber exchange, which had previously been treated only as a background field in SCET_G for heavy-ion collisions (Idilbi 2008, D'Eramo 2010, Ovanesyan 2011-2012, Benzke 2012). The formalism connects to the multi-Wilson line effective field theory for forward scattering constructed by Balitsky (1995-2001) and provides a systematic operator-based method to study both forward scattering resummation via the BFKL equation (Kuraev 1977, Balitsky 1978) and factorization violation in hard scattering processes, enabling future calculations of subleading power corrections and underlying event contributions in hadronic collisions.
