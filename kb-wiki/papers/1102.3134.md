# Stopping in central Pb + Pb collisions at SPS energies and beyond

> We investigate stopping and baryon transport in central relativistic Pb + Pb and Au + Au collisions. At energies reached at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron [sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3-17.3 GeV] and at RHIC (62.4 GeV), we determine the fragmentation-peak positions from the data. The resulting linear growth of the peak positions with beam rapidity is in agreement with our results from a QCD-based approach that accounts for gluon saturation. No discontinuities in the net-proton fragmentation peak positions occur in the expected transition region from partons to hadrons at 6-10 GeV.

## Key claim

The fragmentation-peak positions of net-proton rapidity distributions in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions grow linearly with beam rapidity from SPS to RHIC energies, with no discontinuities in the expected transition region from partons to hadrons at 6–10 GeV center-of-mass energy, indicating that the transition is gradual rather than sudden.

## Method

The authors performed double-gaussian fits to net-proton rapidity distributions from NA49 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (√s_NN = 6.3–17.3 GeV) and BRAHMS data for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (62.4 GeV). They compared the extracted fragmentation-peak positions to an analytical prediction from a QCD-based model that accounts for gluon saturation and geometric scaling, where the peak position depends on beam rapidity through the saturation-scale exponent λ.

## Key numbers

- Peak position at √s_NN = 6.3 GeV: |y_peak| = 0.67 ± 0.10 (beam rapidity |y_beam| = 1.92)
- Peak position at √s_NN = 12.3 GeV: |y_peak| = 1.14 ± 0.15 (|y_beam| = 2.58)
- Peak position at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV: |y_peak| = 1.26 ± 0.25 (from older NA49 data) or 2.00 ± 0.40 (from newer preliminary data)
- Peak position at √s_NN = 62.4 GeV (Au+Au): |y_peak| = 2.63 ± 0.10 (|y_beam| = 4.20)
- Saturation-scale exponent λ = 0.2 gives the best fit to the slope of peak positions versus beam rapidity
- Gluon saturation momentum Q_s ≈ 0.77 GeV at x = 0.01 for λ = 0.2; after rescaling by color factor 9/4, Q_s ≈ 1.2 GeV at x = 0.01

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD-based partonic model (Mehtar-Tani and Wolschin, 2009) from RHIC energies down to SPS energies, testing whether the linear dependence of fragmentation-peak positions on beam rapidity holds at lower energies. The results contradict claims of a non-monotonous curvature anomaly in net-proton distributions at 4–10 GeV from three-fluid dynamic calculations (Ivanov, 2010). The analysis suggests that net-proton fragmentation-peak positions are not well-suited as signatures for the parton-hadron cross-over, and that observables closer to statistical equilibrium, such as strange meson production, may be more promising for detecting the transition.
