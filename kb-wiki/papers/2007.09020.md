# Breakup mechanisms in the 6He+64Zn reaction at near-barrier energies

> New experimental results for the elastic scattering of 6He on 64Zn at incident energies of 15.0 and 18.0 MeV and 4He at 17.5 MeV along with results already published at 10.0 and 13.6 MeV, are presented. Elastic and alpha experimental cross sections are compared with coupled-reaction-channel, continuum-discretized coupledchannel, and DWBA inclusive-breakup models. The large yield of alpha particles observed at all measured energies can be explained by considering a nonelastic breakup mechanism.

## Key claim
The large yield of alpha particles observed in the 6He+64Zn reaction at all measured near-barrier energies can be explained by considering a non-elastic breakup mechanism, not solely by elastic breakup as captured by CDCC calculations.

## Method
Elastic scattering angular distributions for 6He+64Zn at 15.0 and 18.0 MeV and 4He+64Zn at 17.5 MeV were measured using silicon strip detectors and time-of-flight techniques. The data were compared with Optical Model, Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (3-body and 4-body CDCC), Coupled-Reaction-Channel (CRC), and DWBA inclusive-breakup calculations to disentangle elastic breakup from non-elastic breakup contributions.

## Key numbers
- Elastic scattering of 6He on 64Zn measured at incident energies of 15.0 and 18.0 MeV (center-of-target energies 14.85 and 17.9 MeV) and 4He on 64Zn at 17.5 MeV (center-of-target 17.4 MeV)
- 6He beam average intensity of 3x10^6 particles per second
- Target thickness: 530 microgram/cm^2 self-supporting 64Zn foil
- For the 4He+64Zn optical model, the Sao Paulo Potential was used with imaginary part renormalized by factor N_i = 0.78
- For 6He+64Zn optical model, surface imaginary diffuseness a_d approximately 1 fm at all four energies (9.8, 13.5, 14.85, 17.9 MeV)
- In the di-neutron model of 6He, the effective two-neutron separation energy was set to 1.6 MeV to reproduce the tail and rms radius of the 2n+4He wave function
- The 2+ resonance in 6He continuum was placed at excitation energy 1.8 MeV above ground state
- CDCC calculations included 4He-2n relative angular momenta up to l_max = 4 and excitation energies up to 8 MeV
- Four-body CDCC calculations used a THO basis with 86 states truncated at maximum energy 8 MeV
- CRC calculations considered partial waves up to l_f = 5 for the 64Zn-2n relative motion, with 2 MeV bins up to 10 MeV for states above breakup threshold

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 6He-induced reactions on medium-mass targets, particularly the 6He+206Pb system studied by Fernandez-Garcia et al. (2010) and Escrig et al. (2007), where two-neutron transfer was found important for alpha-particle production. The results show that for the 6He+64Zn system, elastic breakup alone (as computed by CDCC) significantly underestimates the measured alpha yields, and non-elastic breakup mechanisms (including two-neutron transfer) must be included via CRC or DWBA inclusive-breakup methods. This enables future work to separate elastic and non-elastic breakup contributions in reactions of halo nuclei with medium-mass targets.
