# Condensation of Cooper Triples

> The condensation of Cooper pairs, originating from the Fermi-surface instability due to a weakly attractive interaction between two fermions, opened a new frontier for exploring many-body physics in interdisciplinary contexts. In this work, we discuss the possible condensation of Cooper triples, which are three-body counterparts of Cooper pairs for three-component fermions with a three-body attraction. Although each composite trimer-like state obeys the Fermi-Dirac statistics, its aggregate can form a condensate at zero center-of-mass momentum in the presence of the internal degrees of freedom associated with the relative momenta of constituent particles of momenta close to the Fermi surface. Such condensation can be regarded as bosonization in infinite-component fermions. We propose a variational wave function for the condensate of Cooper triples in analogy with the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer ground state and obtain the ground-state energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that Cooper triples, three-body bound states of three-component fermions, can form a condensate at zero center-of-mass momentum despite each composite trimer obeying Fermi-Dirac statistics, provided that the internal degrees of freedom associated with relative momenta of constituent particles near the Fermi surface allow macroscopic occupation of an effective single state.

## Method

The authors consider non-relativistic three-component (r,g,b) fermions of equal mass with a purely attractive three-body contact interaction and vanishing two-body interaction. They generalize the Cooper problem to the three-body case by writing a trial wave function for a single three-body state above the Fermi surface and solving the resulting Schrödinger-like equation for the binding energy. They then propose a variational BCS-type wave function for the condensate of Cooper triples, defined as a product over momentum states of the form (u_k + v_k F^†_{k,R0})|0⟩, where F^† creates a trimer with zero center-of-mass momentum via a 2π/3 rotation operator R0, and minimize the ground-state energy to obtain the gap equation.

## Key numbers

- The three-body binding energy in the weak-coupling limit is given by \(E = -3\Lambda \exp\left(-\frac{3}{\rho(0)U_0}\right) < 0\), where \(\Lambda\) is the energy cutoff and \(\rho(0)\) is the density of states at the Fermi level.
- The transition between a Cooper pair and a Cooper triple state occurs at \(G = \frac{2}{3}U_0\) in the weak-coupling limit, where \(G\) is the two-body coupling constant and \(U_0\) is the three-body coupling constant.
- The Cooper pair binding energy is given by \(E_2 = -2\Lambda \exp\left(-\frac{2}{\rho(0)G}\right)\) for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the BCS theory of Cooper pair condensation (Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer, 1957) to three-body bound states in three-component Fermi systems, building on prior studies of Cooper triple formation in the presence of two-body and three-body interactions (Tajima et al., 2014; Kirk and Parish, 2017). The proposed condensation mechanism, which the authors describe as bosonization in infinite-component fermions, is analogous to the treatment of alpha-particle condensation in nuclear matter (Röpke et al., 1998; Senkov et al., 2009) and opens the possibility of studying novel superfluid phases in ultracold atomic gases with tunable three-body interactions, such as mixtures of ⁶Li or ¹⁷³Yb.
