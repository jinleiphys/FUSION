# Triple-X and beyond: hadronic systems of three and more X(3872)

> The $X(3872)$ resonance has been conjectured to be a $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$ charm meson-antimeson two-body molecule. Meanwhile, there is no experimental evidence for larger, few-body compounds of multiple charm meson-antimeson pairs which would resemble larger molecules or nuclei. Here, we investigate such multi-meson states to the extent of what can be deduced theoretically from essentials of the interaction between uncharged $D^{0}$ and $D^{*0}$ mesons. From a molecular $X(3872)$, we predict a $4X$ ($4^{++}$) octamer with a binding energy \mbox{$B_{4X} > 2.08\,{\rm MeV}$,} assuming a $D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$ system close to the unitary limit (as suggested by the mass of the $X(3872)$). If we consider heavy-quark spin symmetry explicitly, the $D^{*0} \bar{D}^{*0}$ ($2^{++}$) system is close to unitarity, too. In this case, we predict a bound $3X$ ($3^{++}$) hexamer with $B_{3X} > 2.29\,{\rm MeV}$ and a more deeply bound $4X$ octamer with $B_{4X} > 11.21\,{\rm MeV}$. These results exemplify with hadronic molecules a more general phenomenon of equal-mass two-species Bose systems comprised of equal number of either type: the emergence of unbound four- and six-boson clusters in the limit of a short-range two-body interaction which acts only between bosons of different species. Finally, we also study the conditions under which a $2X$ ($2^{++}$) tetramer might form.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that bound states of multiple X(3872) meson-antimeson pairs (hexamers and octamers) can exist, but only under specific conditions: either assuming only the X-channel interaction is resonant (yielding a bound 4X octamer), or additionally including heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) which also makes the D*0 D̅*0 channel resonant (yielding both a bound 3X hexamer and a more deeply bound 4X octamer). The 2X tetramer remains unbound unless an additional attractive D0 D̅0 interaction of at least about 20% of the X-channel strength is included.

## Method

The authors model multi-X systems as non-relativistic few-body problems of neutral D0 and D*0 mesons and their antiparticles, using a contact-range potential regularized with a Gaussian cutoff. They solve the Schrödinger equation with the Stochastic-Variational Method (SVM), expanding wave functions in correlated Gaussians, for systems of 2, 3, and 4 X(3872) molecules (corresponding to 4, 6, and 8 mesons respectively). The potential strength is calibrated to put the X(3872) at threshold (zero binding energy), and the analysis is performed both with and without including the D*0 D̅*0 (2++) channel via heavy-quark spin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- 4X octamer binding energy (without HQSS): B4X > 2.08 MeV
- 3X hexamer binding energy (with HQSS): B3X > 2.29 MeV
- 4X octamer binding energy (with HQSS): B4X > 11.21 MeV
- Critical coupling strength for 2X tetramer binding: η* = 0.876(1)
- Minimum relative strength of D0 D̅0 attraction needed for 2X binding: λ* = 0.174(8) (about 20% of X-channel strength)
- Ratio of 4X to 3X binding energies: B4/B3 ~ 4.9
- Meson mass used in calculations: m = 1933.29 MeV (twice the reduced mass of D*0 D̅0)
- Cutoff range explored: Rc = 1.0-2.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the universal few-body physics of resonant two-body interactions (the Efimov effect) from atomic systems (Kraemer 2006) and nuclear physics (Bedaque 1998, 1999) to hadronic molecules composed of charm mesons. It builds on the conjecture that the X(3872) is a shallow D*0 D̅0 molecule (Tornqvist 2003, Voloshin 2003) and on previous studies showing that three-body systems of charm mesons do not exhibit the Efimov effect (Canham 2009). The predictions enable future experimental searches for multi-charm hadronic molecules, particularly given recent observations of double charm-anticharm systems (Aaij 2017, 2020), and demonstrate a novel generalization of Borromean/Brunnian systems where binding appears only for larger clusters (4X octamer) but not smaller ones (2X tetramer, 3X hexamer without HQSS).
