# Exploring the proton spin structure

> Understanding the spin structure of the proton is one of the main challenges in hadronic physics. While the concepts of spin and orbital angular momentum are pretty clear in the context of non-relativistic quantum mechanics, the generalization of these concepts to quantum field theory encounters serious difficulties. It is however possible to define meaningful decompositions of the proton spin that are (in principle) measurable. We propose a summary of the present situation including recent developments and prospects of future developments.

## Key claim

The proton spin decomposition into spin and orbital angular momentum contributions from quarks and gluons is intrinsically ambiguous due to Lorentz and gauge symmetry, but this ambiguity is resolved by recognizing that the experimental configuration determines the natural decomposition, making both kinetic and canonical decompositions meaningful and in principle measurable.

## Method

The paper reviews the two families of proton spin decompositions, kinetic (mechanical) and canonical, and collects the various spin sum rules and relations that connect orbital angular momentum (OAM) to measurable parton distributions, including generalized parton distributions (GPDs), generalized transverse-momentum dependent distributions (GTMDs), and transverse-momentum dependent distributions (TMDs). It also introduces the concept of quark spin-orbit correlation, defines its kinetic and canonical versions, and shows how these can be expressed in terms of twist-2 and twist-3 GPDs and GTMDs, with model comparisons from the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) and the light-front chiral quark-soliton model (LFχQSM).

## Key numbers

- In the LFCQM, the total quark kinetic OAM ⟨L^q_kin⟩ = 0.126, with u-quark contribution 0.071 and d-quark contribution 0.055.
- In the LFχQSM, the total quark kinetic OAM ⟨L^q_kin⟩ = 0.069, with u-quark contribution -0.008 and d-quark contribution 0.077.
- In the LFCQM, the total quark canonical OAM from GTMDs ⟨L^q_can⟩ = 0.126, with u-quark contribution 0.131 and d-quark contribution -0.005.
- In the LFχQSM, the total quark canonical OAM from GTMDs ⟨L^q_can⟩ = 0.069, with u-quark contribution 0.073 and d-quark contribution -0.004.
- From the LSS fit to experimental data at μ² = 1 GeV², the first moment of the u-quark helicity distribution ∫dx H̃^u(x,0,0) = 0.82 and for d-quark = -0.45; the second moment ∫dx x H̃^u(x,0,0) ≈ 0.19 and for d-quark ≈ -0.06.
- From lattice calculations at μ² = 4 GeV² and pion mass m_π = 293 MeV, the first moment ∫dx H̃^u(x,0,0) = 0.82(7) and for d-quark = -0.41(7); the second moment ∫dx x H̃^u(x,0,0) ≈ 0.20 and for d-quark ≈ -0.05.
- The estimated kinetic quark spin-orbit correlation is ⟨C^u_kin⟩ ≈ -0.8 and ⟨C^d_kin⟩ ≈ -0.55, indicating antiparallel quark spin and kinetic OAM.

## Context

This review extends the ongoing debate on proton spin decompositions, which began with the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition (1989) and the Ji decomposition (1996), and addresses the controversies summarized in reviews by Leader (2013) and Wakamatsu (2014). The paper clarifies that the Stueckelberg symmetry identified by Lorcé (2012) makes the decompositions ambiguous, but experimental conditions resolve this. It enables future work on extracting orbital angular momentum from GPDs at twist 2 and 3, direct lattice calculations of OAM, and measurements of the quark spin-orbit correlation, which had escaped attention until recently.
