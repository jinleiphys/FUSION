# Glauber-model analysis of total reaction cross sections for Ne, Mg, Si, and S isotopes with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock densities

> A systematic analysis is made on the total reaction cross sections for Ne, Mg, Si, and S isotopes. The high-energy nucleus-nucleus collision is described based on the Glauber model. Using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method in the three-dimensional grid-space representation, we determine the nuclear density distribution for a wide range of nuclei self-consistently without assuming any spatial symmetry. The calculated total reaction cross sections consistently agree with the recent cross section data on Ne$+^{12}$C collision at 240$A$\,MeV, which makes it possible to discuss the radius and deformation of the isotopes. The total reaction cross sections for Mg$+^{12}$C, Si$+^{12}$C and S$+^{12}$C cases are predicted for future measurements. We also find that the high-energy cross section data for O, Ne, and Mg isotopes on a $^{12}$C target at around 1000\,$A$MeV can not be reproduced consistently with the corresponding data at 240\,$A$MeV.

# Glauber-model analysis of total reaction cross sections for Ne, Mg, Si, and S isotopes with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock densities

## Key claim

The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock densities combined with the Glauber model (Nucleon-Target formalism) reproduce the total reaction cross sections for Ne isotopes on a 12C target at 240 A MeV, but the same theoretical framework cannot consistently reproduce the high-energy cross section data at around 1000 A MeV for O, Ne, and Mg isotopes, suggesting the need for updated experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations in a three-dimensional grid-space representation (0.8 fm mesh, 15 fm spherical box) to obtain self-consistent nuclear density distributions for Ne, Mg, Si, and S isotopes without assuming spatial symmetry, using the SkM* and SLy4 energy functionals. The total reaction cross sections on a 12C target were then calculated using the Nucleon-Target formalism in the Glauber model (NTG approximation), which accounts for multiple scattering effects beyond the optical limit approximation. For odd-A nuclei, the half-filling approximation was adopted, and the ground-state density in the laboratory frame was obtained by angular averaging of the deformed intrinsic state density.

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross section of 12C+12C at 240 A MeV: 790 mb (calculated), compared to experimental interaction cross section 782 ± 10 mb at 250 A MeV
- Total reaction cross section of 12C+12C at 1000 A MeV: 850 mb (calculated), compared to experimental interaction cross section 853 ± 6 mb at 950 A MeV
- Increase in total reaction cross section from N=18 to N=24 for Ne isotopes: 210 mb; for Mg isotopes: 180 mb; for Si isotopes: 140 mb; for S isotopes: 130 mb (all at 240 A MeV on 12C target)
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β for 30Ne: 0.35 (SkM*) versus -0.06 (SLy4)
- Neutron separation energy of 31Ne: approximately 0.33 MeV (experimental) versus about 2 MeV (SkM* calculation)
- Neutron separation energy of 37Mg: approximately 162 keV (experimental)
- Estimated maximum difference between total reaction and interaction cross sections: 100 mb (phenomenological estimate)

## Context

This work extends previous Glauber-model analyses by Horiuchi, Suzuki, Abu-Ibrahim, and Kohama (2007, 2009) that used spherical Woods-Saxon densities for C and O isotopes, now applying the method to the island-of-inversion region where nuclear deformation plays a significant role. The study is motivated by recent systematic measurements of total reaction cross sections in the pf-shell region by Kanungo (2011) and Takechi (2010, new). The results enable future measurements of Mg, Si, and S isotopes to test deformation predictions, and the identified inconsistencies at 1000 A MeV for O, Ne, and Mg isotopes call for both improved experimental data and further theoretical investigation, including studies on proton targets to probe interior density profiles differently than carbon targets.
