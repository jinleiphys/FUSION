# Observation of a near-threshold structure in the $K^+$ recoil-mass spectra in $e^+e^-\to K^+ (D_s^- D^{*0} + D^{*-}_s D^0)$

> We report a study of the processes of $e^+e^-\to K^+ (D_s^- D^{*0} + D^{*-}_s D^0)$ based on $e^+e^-$ annihilation samples collected with the BESIII detector operating at BEPCII at five center-of-mass energies ranging from 4.628 to 4.698 GeV with a total integrated luminosity of 3.7 fb$^{-1}$. An excess over the known contributions of the conventional charmed mesons is observed near the $D_s^- D^{*0}$ and $D^{*-}_s D^0$ mass thresholds in the $K^{+}$ recoil-mass spectrum for events collected at $\sqrt{s}=4.681$ GeV. The structure matches a mass-dependent-width Breit-Wigner line shape, whose pole mass and width are determined as $(3982.5^{+1.8}_{-2.6}\pm2.1)$ MeV/$c^2$ and $(12.8^{+5.3}_{-4.4}\pm3.0)$ MeV, respectively. The first uncertainties are statistical and the second are systematic. The significance of the resonance hypothesis is estimated to be 5.3 $σ$ over the contributions only from the conventional charmed mesons. This is the first candidate of the charged hidden-charm tetraquark with strangeness, decaying into $D_s^- D^{*0}$ and $D^{*-}_s D^0$. However, the properties of the excess need further exploration with more statistics.

## Key claim

The BESIII Collaboration reports the first candidate for a charged hidden-charm tetraquark with strangeness, the Z_cs(3985)^-, observed as an excess in the K^+ recoil-mass spectrum near the D_s^- D^{*0} and D^{*-}_s D^0 mass thresholds at a center-of-mass energy of 4.681 GeV. The significance of the resonance hypothesis is 5.3 sigma over conventional charmed meson contributions, but the authors caution that the properties of the excess need further exploration with more statistics and that other explanations for the near-threshold enhancement remain possible.

## Method

The BESIII Collaboration analyzed e+e- annihilation data at five center-of-mass energies from 4.628 to 4.698 GeV with a total integrated luminosity of 3.7 fb^-1, collected with the BESIII detector at BEPCII. A partial-reconstruction technique was used, reconstructing only the bachelor K^+ and the D_s^- meson (decaying to K^+K^-pi^- or K_S^0 K^-), while the D^{*0} or D^{*-}_s was inferred from the recoil mass. A simultaneous unbinned maximum likelihood fit was performed to the K^+ recoil-mass spectra at all five energy points, modeling the excess as a mass-dependent-width Breit-Wigner resonance and including contributions from nonresonant processes, excited D_s^{**} states, and combinatorial backgrounds estimated from wrong-sign combinations.

## Key numbers

* Pole mass of the Z_cs(3985)^- candidate: (3982.5^{+1.8}_{-2.6} +/- 2.1) MeV/c^2 (statistical and systematic uncertainties, respectively).
* Pole width of the Z_cs(3985)^- candidate: (12.8^{+5.3}_{-4.4} +/- 3.0) MeV (statistical and systematic uncertainties, respectively).
* Significance of the resonance hypothesis over conventional charmed meson contributions: 5.3 sigma.
* Born cross section times branching fraction at sqrt(s)=4.681 GeV: sigma^B * B = (4.4^{+0.9}_{-0.8} +/- 1.4) pb.
* Number of signal events observed at sqrt(s)=4.681 GeV: 85.2^{+17.6}_{-15.6}.
* Total integrated luminosity of the data sample: 3.7 fb^-1.

## Context

This work extends the search for strange partners of the Z_c states, which are nonstrange hidden-charm tetraquark candidates with quark content c c-bar q q-bar' (q = u or d) observed by BESIII, Belle, and CLEO. The Z_cs(3985)^- candidate, with quark content most likely c c-bar s u-bar, is predicted by several theoretical models including tetraquark scenarios (Ebert, 2008; Ferretti, 2020), the D_s D-bar* molecular model (Lee, 2008; Dias, 2013), and the initial-single-chiral-particle-emission mechanism (Chen, 2013). The measured mass is close to the D_s D-bar* and D_s* D-bar mass thresholds, consistent with these predictions. Future studies with larger statistics are needed to perform an amplitude analysis and fully explore the properties of this excess.
