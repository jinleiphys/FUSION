# Bayesian inference favors quark matter in neutron star interiors

> We perform a physics-informed Bayesian analyses of the equation of state of hybrid neutron stars that incorporates color-flavor-locked quark matter modeled by a three-flavor non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio framework with vector repulsion and diquark pairing. Contrary to the model-agnostic Bayesian analyses our scheme allows for distinguishing between the scenarios of neutron stars with quark cores and without them. The used quark model realizes asymptotic conformality at high densities in accordance with perturbative QCD. The hadronic sector is described by the density-dependent relativistic functional DD2Y-T, which satisfies chiral effective field theory constraints and includes hyperonic degrees of freedom. We construct a large set of candidate hybrid EOSs by varying the vector and diquark couplings and apply a Maxwell construction for the quark-hadron phase transition. Observational constraints from recent NICER pulsar mass-radius measurements and tidal deformability from GW170817 are incorporated into the likelihood. Depending on whether the observational data from the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 and the HESS J1731-347 object are included to the analysis or not, the posterior distribution favors vector and diquark couplings around $(η_V,η_D)\simeq (0.82,0.40)$ or $(η_V,η_D)\simeq (0.64,0.36)$, respectively. This corresponds to equations of state that support two-solar-mass neutron stars with superconformal speed of sound and relatively low onset densities for deconfinement. Our findings indicate that the most probable hybrid EOSs are statistically preferred over the purely hadronic baseline. The corresponding probabilities of agreeing with the observational data differ by one or two orders of magnitude depending on the data set used. This suggests that quark cores may exist in all observed neutron stars.

## Key claim

The Bayesian analysis shows that hybrid equations of state (EOSs) with quark cores are statistically preferred over purely hadronic EOSs by one to two orders of magnitude in posterior probability, but this conclusion depends on whether observational data from the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 and the HESS J1731-347 object are included in the analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a physics-informed Bayesian analysis of hybrid neutron star EOSs combining a hadronic sector (DD2Y-T density-dependent relativistic functional with hyperons) with a quark matter sector described by a three-flavor non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with color-flavor-locked pairing and vector repulsion. They generated 31x36 quark EOSs by varying the vector coupling η_V from 0.50 to 1.20 and diquark coupling η_D from 0.20 to 0.50, applied a Maxwell construction for the quark-hadron phase transition, and incorporated observational constraints from NICER pulsar mass-radius measurements and the GW170817 tidal deformability into the likelihood.

## Key numbers

- The posterior distribution favors vector and diquark couplings around (η_V, η_D) ≈ (0.82, 0.40) when including PSR J0952-0607 and HESS J1731-347 data, and (η_V, η_D) ≈ (0.64, 0.36) without those data sets
- The effective bag pressure B_eff = 81.3 MeV/fm^3 for the quark model with vacuum effective quark mass M_{0,k=0} = 400 MeV and chiral condensate |⟨f̄f⟩_0|^(1/3) = 250 MeV
- The quark model parameters are: current quark mass m = 3.5 MeV, momentum scale Λ = 564 MeV, scalar coupling G_S = 9.62 GeV^(-2)
- The onset chemical potential for deconfinement is restricted to μ_B^min = 971 MeV (one nuclear saturation density) and μ_B^max = 1695 MeV (central density of maximum-mass hadronic star)
- 269 quark EOSs out of 31x36 combinations provide a valid Maxwell crossing with positive density jump and TOV-stable quark branches
- The hybrid EOSs support maximum neutron star masses up to about 2.2 M_⊙

## Context

This work extends the physics-informed Bayesian analysis scheme developed by Ayriyan (2024) by using a more realistic three-flavor nonlocal chiral quark model that reaches the conformal limit of QCD at high densities, unlike model-agnostic approaches such as those by Annala (2019), Altiparmak (2022), and Brandes (2022, 2023). The hadronic baseline uses the DD2Y-T functional recently developed by Typel (Shahrbaf, 2022), and the analysis includes new NICER mass-radius constraints for the 1.4 M_⊙ pulsar PSR J0614-3329 by Mauviard (2025). The results enable quantitative comparison of the probability that observed neutron stars contain quark cores versus purely hadronic interiors.
