# Spectroscopic implications from the combined analysis of processes with pseudoscalar mesons

> Contributed paper to XIII International Conference "Selected Problems of Modern Theoretical Physics", Bogoliubov Lab of Theoretical Physics, JINR, Dubna, Russia, 23-27 June, 2008; 26 pages, 7 figures; the scalar part modified, references corrected and added, list of authors modified

## Key claim
The paper claims that a combined, coupled-channel analysis of processes involving pseudoscalar mesons can determine the status, parameters, and SU(3) classification of scalar, vector, and tensor mesonic resonances, but the results depend on which channels are explicitly included (e.g., variant I vs. variant II for the scalar sector) and on the number of resonances assumed in the fit.

## Method
The authors performed a coupled-channel analysis of data on the processes pi pi -> pi pi, K anti-K, eta eta, and eta eta' in the channels with I^G J^PC = 0^+ 0^++ and 0^+ 2^++, and on pi pi scattering in the channel with 1^+ 1^--. They used two approaches: a model-independent method based on analyticity and unitarity (using a uniformizing variable for the 2- and 3-channel cases) and a multichannel Breit-Wigner parametrization of the Jost matrix determinant via the Le Couteur-Newton relations. The S-matrix poles on the Riemann surface were extracted to classify resonances by their coupling to different channels.

## Key numbers
*   For the scalar sector (variant I), the total chi^2/NDF was 345.603/(301-40) ≈ 1.32; for variant II, it was 282.682/(293-38) ≈ 1.11.
*   The f0(980) state was described by a pole on sheet II at (1013.1 ± 4) - i(34.1 ± 6) MeV (variant I) and at (1009.3 ± 3) - i(32 ± 4) MeV (variant II).
*   The f0(1500) mass and total width were calculated as 1544 MeV and 714 MeV (variant I) and 1511.4 MeV and 398 MeV (variant II).
*   For the vector sector (model-independent, five resonances), the rho(770) pole on sheet II was at (765.8 ± 0.6) - i(73.3 ± 0.4) MeV; the calculated mass and total width were 769.3 ± 0.6 MeV and 146.6 ± 0.9 MeV.
*   For the tensor sector (ten states), the f2(1270) mass was 1275.3 ± 1.8 MeV with a total width of approximately 212 MeV; the total chi^2/NDF was 161.147/(168-65) ≈ 1.56.

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel analysis of pi pi scattering and related reactions, building on the model-independent method of KMS96 and the multichannel Breit-Wigner approach. It addresses the problematic scalar sector, where the nature of states like f0(980) and f0(1500) (the latter a glueball candidate) is debated, and the poorly known vector meson spectrum (e.g., the mass of rho(1450) ranges from 1250 to 1582 MeV in the PDG). The results enable a more precise classification of resonances into SU(3) multiplets and provide pole parameters for comparison with other analyses, such as those from antiproton-proton annihilation by Anisovich (2005).
