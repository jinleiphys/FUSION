# $D^*Dπ$ form factor revisited

> The $D^*Dπ$ form factor is evaluated in a QCD sum rule calculation for both $D$ and $π$ off-shell mesons. We study the Borel sum rule for the three-point function of one pseudoscalar, one axial and one vector meson currents. We find that the momentum dependence of the form factors is very different if the $D$ or the $π$ meson is off-shell, but they lead to the same coupling constant in the $D^*Dπ$ vertex.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the $D^*D\pi$ form factor is very different depending on whether the $D$ or the $\pi$ meson is off-shell, but both parametrizations lead to the same coupling constant at the respective poles, yielding $g_{D^*D\pi}=14.0\pm1.5$, which is still smaller than the CLEO experimental value of $17.9\pm0.3\pm1.9$.

## Method

The authors performed a QCD sum rule calculation of the three-point function with one pseudoscalar, one axial, and one vector meson current, evaluating the $D^*D\pi$ form factor for both off-shell $D$ and off-shell $\pi$ mesons. They applied a double Borel transformation to the sum rules, related the Borel parameters in the two- and three-point functions following the HQET prescription of BBG93, and parametrized the QCD sum rule results for the momentum dependence using a monopole form for the off-shell $D$ case and an exponential form for the off-shell $\pi$ case. The coupling constant was extracted by evaluating the form factor at the pole of the off-shell meson ($Q^2=-m_D^2$ for the $D$ off-shell, $Q^2=-m_\pi^2$ for the $\pi$ off-shell).

## Key numbers

* $g_{D^*D\pi}=14.0\pm1.5$, extracted from the combined analysis of both off-shell $D$ and off-shell $\pi$ form factors, with errors reflecting variations in continuum thresholds, parametrizations, and Borel mass relations.
* For the off-shell $D$ meson, the monopole parametrization is $g_{D^*D\pi}^{(D)}(Q^2)=126.1/(Q^2+11.95)$, yielding a coupling constant of $14.9$ at the $D$ pole ($Q^2=-m_D^2$).
* For the off-shell $\pi$ meson, the exponential parametrization is $g_{D^*D\pi}^{(\pi)}(Q^2)=15.5\,e^{-Q^2/1.48}$, yielding a coupling constant of $15.5$ at the pion pole ($Q^2\approx0$).
* Cut-off parameters: $\Lambda_D=3.5$ GeV for the off-shell $D$ meson; $\Lambda_\pi=1.2$ GeV for the off-shell $\pi$ meson.
* For the $B^*B\pi$ vertex: $g_{B^*B\pi}=42.3$ with $\Lambda_B=6.8$ GeV (off-shell $B$); $g_{B^*B\pi}=45.1$ with $\Lambda_\pi=1.3$ GeV (off-shell $\pi$).
* The effective scale-independent coupling $g$ for $B^*B\pi$ is $0.59-0.63$, obtained from $g=(f_\pi/2m_B)g_{B^*B\pi}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule calculation of the $D^*D\pi$ form factor (Navarra, Nielsen, Bracco, 2001) which used only the off-shell pion and a gaussian parametrization yielding $g_{D^*D\pi}=5.7\pm0.4$, a value much smaller than the CLEO measurement. The new approach follows the procedure developed for the $DD\rho$ form factor (Navarra, Nielsen, Bracco, 2001) by evaluating the form factor for both off-shell mesons and using consistent pole extrapolations. The result $g_{D^*D\pi}=14.0\pm1.5$ is in fair agreement with light cone sum rule calculations by Belyaev et al. (1995) and Khodjamirian et al. (2000), but remains below the experimental CLEO value of $17.9\pm0.3\pm1.9$. The finding that the form factor is harder (softer) when the off-shell particle is heavier (lighter) enables improved modeling of heavy-light meson vertices in effective lagrangian approaches.
