# Tetrahedral $4α$ and $^{12}\textrm{C}+α$ cluster structures in $^{16}$O

> We have investigated structures of the ground and excited states of $^{16}\textrm{O}$ with the method of variation after spin-parity projection in the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model combined with the generator coordinate method of $^{12}\textrm{C}+α$ cluster. The calculation reasonably reproduces the experimental energy spectra, $E2$, $E3$, $E4$, $IS1$ transitions, and $α$-decay properties. The formation of 4 $α$ clusters has been confirmed from nucleon degrees of freedom in the AMD model without assuming existence of any clusters. They form "tetrahedral" $4α$- and $^{12}\textrm{C}+α$-cluster structures. The $^{12}\textrm{C}+α$ structure constructs the $K^π=0^+$ band consisting of the $0^+_2$, $2^+_1$, and $4^+_1$ states and the $K^π=0^-$ band of the $1^-_2$, $3^-_2$, and $5^-_1$ states. The $0^+_1$, $3^-_1$, and $4^+_2$ states are assigned to the ground band constructed from the tetrahedral 4$α$ structure. The $0^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ are approximately interpreted as $T_d$ band members with the ideal tetrahedral configuration. The ground state $4α$ correlation plays an important role in enhancement of the $E3$ transition strength to the $3^-_1$. The $4^+_2$ state is not the ideal $T_d$ member but constructed from a distorted tetrahedral $4α$ structure. Moreover, significant state mixing of the tetrahedral $4α$ and $^{12}\textrm{C}+α$ cluster structures occurs between $4^+_1$ and $4^+_2$ states, indicating that the $T_d$ configuration of $4α$ is rather fragile at $J^π=4^+$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ground and excited states of $^{16}$O can be described by a coexistence of tetrahedral $4\alpha$ and $^{12}\textrm{C}+\alpha$ cluster structures, but the ideal $T_d$ tetrahedral configuration is fragile at $J^\pi=4^+$ due to significant state mixing between the $4^+_1$ and $4^+_2$ states, contrary to earlier assignments that placed a single $4^+$ state in the tetrahedral band.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) to obtain intrinsic wave functions for $^{16}$O states from nucleon degrees of freedom without assuming clusters. They then combined these AMD+VAP wave functions with a generator coordinate method (GCM) using $^{12}\textrm{C(AMD)}+\alpha$ cluster configurations at various inter-cluster distances and $^{12}$C orientations, superposing all basis states to obtain the final VAP+$\alpha$GCM wave functions. The effective interactions were the MV1 central force and G3RS spin-orbit force with parameters previously used for $^{12}$C.

## Key numbers

- Calculated binding energy of $^{16}$O: 123.0 MeV (VAP) and 123.5 MeV (VAP+$\alpha$GCM), compared to experimental 127.62 MeV
- Calculated r.m.s. radius: 2.69 fm (VAP) and 2.73 fm (VAP+$\alpha$GCM), compared to experimental 2.55 fm
- Calculated $^{12}\textrm{C}+\alpha$ threshold: 7.6 MeV (VAP) and 8.2 MeV (VAP+$\alpha$GCM), compared to experimental 7.16 MeV
- $B(E3;3^-_1\to 0^+_1)$ = 191 $e^2$fm$^6$ (VAP) and 218 $e^2$fm$^6$ (VAP+$\alpha$GCM), compared to experimental 205 $\pm$ 11 $e^2$fm$^6$
- $B(E4;4^+_1\to 0^+_1)$ = 260 $e^2$fm$^8$ (VAP) and 360 $e^2$fm$^8$ (VAP+$\alpha$GCM), compared to experimental 380 $\pm$ 130 $e^2$fm$^8$
- $B(E2;4^+_1\to 2^+_1)$ = 5.7 $e^2$fm$^4$ (VAP) and 180 $e^2$fm$^4$ (VAP+$\alpha$GCM), compared to experimental 156 $\pm$ 14 $e^2$fm$^4$
- $B(IS1;0^+_1\to 1^-_1)$ = 125 fm$^6$ (4.0% of EWSR) for VAP and 165 fm$^6$ (4.6% of EWSR) for VAP+$\alpha$GCM, compared to experimental 4.2% of EWSR
- Excitation energy of $0^+_2$: 13.1 MeV (VAP) and 9.7 MeV (VAP+$\alpha$GCM), compared to experimental 6.06 MeV
- Calculated binding energy of $\alpha$ particle: 27.8 MeV, compared to experimental 28.30 MeV
- Calculated binding energy of $^{12}$C: 87.6 MeV, compared to experimental 92.16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous AMD+VAP study of $^{16}$O (Kanada-En'yo 2012) by adding $^{12}\textrm{C(AMD)}+\alpha$ GCM configurations to better describe weak-coupling cluster states. It addresses the long-standing problem of assigning $4^+$ states in the tetrahedral $4\alpha$ band, which was debated by Robson (1979), Elliott (1985), and more recently by Bijker and Iachello (2014, 2016) using algebraic approaches. The calculation improves upon earlier microscopic cluster models (Fujiwara 1980, Descouvemont 1993) that overestimated the $0^+_2$ band-head energy by about 16 MeV, and it confirms the tetrahedral $4\alpha$ structure found in first-principle calculations using chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2013). The results enable future studies of how $4\alpha$ cluster correlations affect electromagnetic transitions and $\alpha$-decay properties in $^{16}$O.
