# Quantum van der Waals quarkyonic matter at non-zero isospin asymmetry

> We extend the recently developed quantum van der Waals quarkyonic matter to non-zero isospin asymmetries by utilizing the two-component van der Waals equation with a generalized excluded volume prescription. The isospin dependence of van der Waals interaction parameters is determined by constraints on the symmetry energy, slope of the symmetry energy, and nuclear ground state properties. We find that the speed of sound has a peak for all values of the asymmetry parameter, signifying a transition to quarkyonic matter. The quarkyonic matter onset density is found to have a mild dependence on isospin asymmetry, with specific details influenced by the isospin dependence of the repulsive interactions. We also incorporate leptonic degrees of freedom and explore the neutron star matter equation of state, calculating mass-radius relations and tidal properties of neutron stars. We find that quarkyonic matter supports heavy neutron stars with a maximum mass of at least 2.6 solar masses. We observe quantitatively different behavior for the excluded volume cases of isospin-blind ($b_{n}=b_{pn}$) and isospin-dependent ($b_{n} \neq b_{pn}$) repulsion, the latter being preferred by observational constraints.

## Key claim

The paper extends the quantum van der Waals quarkyonic matter framework to non-zero isospin asymmetry, finding that the speed of sound exhibits a peak for all asymmetry values and that quarkyonic matter can support neutron stars with a maximum mass of at least 2.6 solar masses, with the isospin-dependent repulsion scenario (b_n ≠ b_pn) being preferred by observational constraints.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter as a mixture of nucleons (protons and neutrons) and quarks (u and d) at zero temperature, using a two-component quantum van der Waals equation with a generalized excluded volume prescription. The isospin dependence of van der Waals interaction parameters is calibrated by fitting to the symmetry energy J = 32.5 MeV and its slope L = 58.9 MeV, with calculations performed for van der Waals, Carnahan-Starling, and trivirial excluded volume models. Leptonic degrees of freedom (electrons and muons) are incorporated under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions to compute neutron star matter equations of state, which are then used as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for mass-radius relations and tidal properties.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass of at least 2.6 solar masses supported by quarkyonic matter
- Symmetry energy fixed to J = 32.5 MeV, with slope L = 58.9 MeV for the isospin-dependent repulsion scenario
- For the isospin-blind scenario (b_pn = b_n), the symmetry energy slope is L ≈ 121-122 MeV
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- van der Waals parameters for symmetric matter: a = 329 MeV fm^3, b = 3.42 fm^3
- Carnahan-Starling parameters: a = 347 MeV fm^3, b = 4.43 fm^3
- Trivirial model parameters: a = 349 MeV fm^3, b = 4.28 fm^3
- For the isospin-dependent scenario: a_pn/a_n = 2.48 (vdW), 2.40 (CS), 2.44 (TVM); b_pn/b_n = 1.98 (vdW), 2.01 (CS), 2.08 (TVM)
- For the isospin-blind scenario: a_pn/a_n = 1.57 (vdW), 1.45 (CS), 1.43 (TVM); b_pn/b_n = 1 for all models
- Charge fraction peak occurs at y ≈ 0.07 at ρ_B ≈ 1.4 ρ_0 for the isospin-dependent case
- Charge fraction peak occurs at y ≈ 0.17-0.19 at ρ_B ≈ 2-2.2 ρ_0 for the isospin-blind case (CS and TVM)
- Muon production threshold around ρ_B ≈ 0.8 ρ_0
- Infrared regulator Λ = 200 MeV used for quark density of states

## Context

This work extends the quantum van der Waals quarkyonic matter framework developed by Poberezhnyuk et al. (2023) from isospin-symmetric matter to non-zero isospin asymmetries, building on the dynamical mechanism for generating quarkyonic momentum shell structure proposed by Jeong et al. (2019). The results enable calculations of neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformability that can be compared with observational constraints from pulsars such as PSR J0030-0451, PSR J0740-6620, PSR J0952-0607, and GW190814, and the framework can be extended in future work to explore baryquark or uniform quarkyonic mixtures and to improve the description of nuclear compressibility through density-dependent mean fields.
