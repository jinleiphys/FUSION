# Effects of Landau quantization on neutrino emission and absorption

> Some neutron stars known as magnetars possess very strong magnetic fields, with surface fields as large as $10^{15}\,\rm G$ and internal fields that are possibly stronger. Recent observations of the radio pulsar GLEAM-X J1627 suggest it may have a surface field as strong as $10^{16} \,\rm G$. In the presence of a strong magnetic field, the energy levels of electrons and protons are quantized and the Direct Urca process allows neutron stars to cool rapidly, even at low density. For the case of magnetic fields $B \geq 10^{16}\,\rm G$, we find features in the emissivity due to energy quantization that are not captured by the frequently employed quasiclassical approximation where energy levels are treated as nearly continuous. Resonances can result in amplification of the neutrino emissivity at specific densities compared to a calculation that neglects quantization, particularly at low temperature. These effects are not important for the thermal evolution of an entire neutron star, but may be relevant for phenomena that depend on behavior at specific densities. We present a fully relativistic calculation of the Direct Urca rate in a strong magnetic field using the standard V-A weak Lagrangian incorporating mean field nuclear effects and discuss approaches to the numerical challenge the modified wavefunctions present and a new semi-analytic approximation. These tools are also applicable to calculating neutrino opacities in strong magnetic fields in the ejecta of binary neutron star mergers. We calculate the opacities for neutrinos capturing on free nucleons at sub-saturation densities and temperatures exceeding an MeV. We find an enhancement to capture processes of the lowest energy neutrinos by an order of magnitude or more due to suppression of electron Pauli blocking in the case of capture on neutrons, and from the effect of the nucleon magnetic moments in the case of capture on protons.

## Key claim

The authors find that Landau quantization of electrons and protons in magnetic fields \(B \geq 10^{16}\,\rm G\) produces resonant features in the Direct Urca neutrino emissivity at specific densities that are not captured by the quasiclassical approximation, but these effects are not important for the overall thermal evolution of an entire neutron star and may only be relevant for phenomena that depend on behavior at specific densities.

## Method

The authors perform a fully relativistic calculation of the Direct Urca rate (\(n \rightarrow p + e + \overline{\nu}\) and \(p+e \rightarrow n + \nu\)) in a strong magnetic field using the standard V-A weak Lagrangian with mean field nuclear effects from the IUFSU\(^*\) relativistic mean field model. They compute the emissivity by summing over Landau levels for electrons and protons, using a semi-analytic approximation for levels where the Fermi surface approximation is valid and full numerical integration for the highest few Landau levels where resonances occur. The same formalism is applied to calculate neutrino opacities for capture on free nucleons at sub-saturation densities and temperatures exceeding an MeV.

## Key numbers

* For magnetic fields \(B \geq 10^{16}\,\rm G\), resonances in the emissivity appear at specific densities due to Landau quantization, with amplification compared to calculations neglecting quantization, particularly at low temperature.
* The enhancement to capture processes of the lowest energy neutrinos is by an order of magnitude or more, due to suppression of electron Pauli blocking for capture on neutrons and from nucleon magnetic moments for capture on protons.
* The IUFSU\(^*\) model produces neutron stars with maximum mass \(M_{\rm TOV} \simeq 2 M_\odot\) and radius \(R_{1.4} \simeq 12 \, \mathrm{km}\), with the Direct Urca threshold at approximately \(4 n_{\rm sat}\).
* The semi-analytic approximation is valid when \(\cos \theta_p \gg \sqrt{2M_N T}/n_p^{1/3}\) and \(\cos \theta_e \gg \sqrt{T/n_e^{1/3}}\), with the authors using a threshold of 0.2 for both quantities.
* For \(B = 5 \times 10^{16} \, \mbox{G}\) at \(T = 1\) keV and 100 keV, the semi-analytic approximation and full calculation are indistinguishable in the comparison of the suppression factor \(R_B\).
* For a magnetic field of \(10^{16} \, \mbox{G}\), thermal smearing of proton Landau levels becomes important at \(T \gtrsim 100 \, \mbox{keV}\), while electron Landau level smearing requires \(T \gtrsim 1 \, \mbox{MeV}\) for \(n_e \leq 100\).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the Direct Urca rate in strong magnetic fields by Leinson and Perez, Baiko and Yakovlev (1999), and Yakovlev and Kaminker, which used the quasiclassical approximation treating Landau levels as nearly continuous and were valid for \(B \lesssim 10^{15}\,\rm G\). It also complements work by Duan and Qian (2004, 2005) on neutrino processes in supernovae at lower densities, and by Maruyama et al. (2022) who included mean field and relativistic effects but took the low temperature limit with momenta set to Fermi momenta. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino emission and absorption at specific densities in magnetars with fields above \(10^{16}\,\rm G\) and in binary neutron star merger ejecta where strong magnetic fields are present.
