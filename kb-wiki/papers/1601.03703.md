# Hartree-Fock Many-Body Perturbation Theory for Nuclear Ground-States

> We investigate the order-by-order convergence behavior of many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) as a simple and efficient tool to approximate the ground-state energy of closed-shell nuclei. To address the convergence properties directly, we explore perturbative corrections up to 30th order and highlight the role of the partitioning for convergence. The use of a simple Hartree-Fock solution to construct the unperturbed basis leads to a convergent MBPT series for soft interactions, in contrast to, e.g., a harmonic oscillator basis. For larger model spaces and heavier nuclei, where a direct high-order MBPT calculation in not feasible, we perform third-order calculation and compare to advanced ab initio coupled-cluster calculations for the same interactions and model spaces. We demonstrate that third-order MBPT provides ground-state energies for nuclei up into tin isotopic chain that are in excellent agreement with the best available coupled-cluster results at a fraction of the computational cost.

## Key claim

Hartree-Fock many-body perturbation theory (HF-MBPT) yields convergent perturbation series for nuclear ground-state energies when using soft interactions, in contrast to harmonic-oscillator-based MBPT which diverges; third-order HF-MBPT agrees with coupled-cluster calculations to better than 1% for nuclei up to tin, but the authors caution that convergence depends on the partitioning and there is no formal guarantee of convergence.

## Method

The authors formulated Rayleigh-Schrodinger many-body perturbation theory using Hartree-Fock single-particle states as the unperturbed basis, starting from chiral nucleon-nucleon (N3LO by Entem and Machleidt) plus three-nucleon (N2LO) interactions softened via similarity renormalization group evolution. They computed perturbative corrections up to 30th order for light nuclei (He-4, O-16, O-24) using a recursive scheme, and performed third-order calculations for heavier closed-shell nuclei up to Sn-132 in an e_max=12 truncated model space, comparing results to configuration interaction diagonalizations and coupled-cluster CR-CC(2,3) calculations.

## Key numbers

* For O-16 with SRG parameter alpha=0.08 fm^4, the 30th-order HF-MBPT partial sum gives -122.577 MeV, matching the CI result of -122.577 MeV exactly (N_max=6 model space, hbar Omega=24 MeV)
* For He-4 with alpha=0.08 fm^4, the 30th-order HF-MBPT partial sum is -26.971 MeV versus CI at -26.994 MeV (deviation ~0.08%)
* For O-24 with alpha=0.08 fm^4, the 30th-order HF-MBPT partial sum is -147.489 MeV versus CI at -147.488 MeV (N_max=4 model space)
* Third-order HF-MBPT ground-state energies per nucleon agree with CR-CC(2,3) results to better than 1% for all nuclei studied from He-4 to Sn-132
* The third-order energy correction contributes approximately 0.2 MeV per nucleon to the total binding energy, about 3% of the total for tin isotopes

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of harmonic-oscillator MBPT by Roth et al. (2010) and Langhammer and Roth (2012), which showed that HO-MBPT diverges for almost all cases and required Pade resummation techniques. The authors demonstrate that switching to a Hartree-Fock basis resolves these convergence issues for soft interactions, enabling low-order MBPT as a computationally cheap alternative to coupled-cluster methods (Binder et al., 2014) and in-medium similarity renormalization group approaches. This enables rapid survey calculations of ground-state systematics across medium-mass and heavy nuclei, with straightforward extensions to excited states and open-shell nuclei.
