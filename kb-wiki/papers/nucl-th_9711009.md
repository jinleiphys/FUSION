# Many Body approach to the inclusive $(e,e')$ reaction from the quasielastic to the $Δ$ excitation region

> We have performed a many body calculation of the inclusive (e,e') cross section which runs over the three traditional regions at intermediate energies: the quasielastic peak, the dip region and the delta region. The longitudinal and transverse response functions in the quasielastic peak have also been evaluated. Traditional effects like polarization, meson exchange currents, final state interaction and delta renormalization in the nuclear medium have been included. Meson exchange currents are generated from a model of pion electroproduction on the nucleon which reproduces accurately the experimental data. The inclusive cross section accounts for 1N,2N,3N mechanisms of virtual photon absorption and one pion production. Meson exchange currents associated to the (γ^*,2π) reaction are also accounted for. We obtain good results for the (e,e') cross sections in the whole energy range and for different nuclei. The response functions are also in good agreement with the latest experimental analysis. On the other hand, the method provides the separation of the contribution to the inclusive cross section from different physical channels which is a necessary input to evaluate cross sections like (e,e' N), (e,e' NN), (e,e' π) etc.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a many-body calculation of the inclusive (e,e') cross section, which includes polarization, meson exchange currents, final state interaction, and delta renormalization, can simultaneously reproduce experimental data across the quasielastic peak, dip region, and delta excitation region for different nuclei, provided that the meson exchange currents are generated from a model of pion electroproduction on the nucleon that accurately reproduces experimental data.

## Method

The calculation uses an infinite nuclear matter approach with the local density approximation (LDA) to model finite nuclei. The inclusive cross section is related to the imaginary part of the virtual photon self-energy, and contributions from 1N, 2N, and 3N mechanisms of virtual photon absorption, as well as one-pion production and meson exchange currents from the (gamma*,2pi) reaction, are included. Nucleon propagators are dressed with a realistic self-energy that depends explicitly on energy and momentum, and the delta resonance is treated with a medium-modified width.

## Key numbers

*   The model for the gamma N -> pi N reaction uses a pion mass of m_pi = 139.5 MeV and a delta mass of M_Delta = 1238 MeV.
*   The free decay width of the delta is given by Gamma(s) = (1/(6*pi)) * (f*/m_pi)^2 * (M_N/sqrt(s)) * |k_cm|^3 * Theta(sqrt(s) - M_N - m_pi).
*   The fine structure constant used is alpha = 1/137.036.
*   The longitudinal response from the Saclay data (Meziani et al., Altemus et al., Barreau et al.) showed a reduction of about 20% over simple shell model expectations, according to more recent Bates data on Ca-40 (Kardan et al.).
*   The effective mass of the nucleon in relativistic approaches is approximately M_N* ~ M_N/2.

## Context

This work extends prior many-body calculations of inclusive electron scattering by incorporating a unified treatment of the quasielastic peak, dip region, and delta region, building on earlier work on real photon absorption (Gil et al., 1993). It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the longitudinal and transverse response functions, which was highlighted by the Saclay data (Meziani et al., Altemus et al., Barreau et al.) and later revised by Jourdan (1996). The method provides a framework for evaluating exclusive cross sections like (e,e'N), (e,e'NN), and (e,e'pi) in a forthcoming paper (Gil et al., 1997).
