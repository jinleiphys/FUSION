# Chiral symmetry in strongly interacting matter

> This is a brief summary of topics that were presented as lectures within the programme "New Frontiers in QCD 2010" at the Yukawa Institute of Theoretical Physics in Kyoto. The basic subject is phases and symmetry breaking patterns as they emerge from the approximate chiral symmetry of QCD. Part I focuses on the QCD interface with nuclear physics via chiral effective field theory. This includes nuclear thermodynamics and, in particular, constraints for compressed and hot baryonic matter provided by the density and temperature dependence of the chiral condensate. Part II explores aspects of the QCD phase diagram using a non-local improved version of the Polyakov - Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. A prominent feature of such an approach is the occurence of a dynamical entanglement between chiral and deconfinement crossover transitions. Comparisons with available results from lattice QCD thermodynamics will be made.

## Key claim

This lecture summary argues that while chiral and deconfinement transitions in QCD appear dynamically entangled in both lattice QCD and PNJL model calculations at zero baryon chemical potential, the PNJL model cannot properly describe the low-temperature, finite-density domain of nuclear matter because it uses quark rather than nucleon degrees of freedom, and must be constrained by nuclear matter results from chiral effective field theory.

## Method

Part I uses in-medium chiral perturbation theory with explicit pions, nucleons, and Delta isobars, computing the free energy density up to three-loop order via the Matsubara formalism and applying the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to extract the density and temperature dependence of the chiral condensate. Part II employs a non-local improved version of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with a momentum-dependent dynamical quark mass, coupling quarks to a homogeneous temporal gauge field representing the Polyakov loop, and compares results to lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition: \(T_c \simeq 15\) MeV (symmetric nuclear matter, from in-medium chiral perturbation theory)
- Normal nuclear matter equilibrium density: \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(T = 0\)
- Validity limit of three-loop chiral expansion: up to Fermi momentum \(p_F \lesssim 0.3\) GeV (about twice normal nuclear density)
- Pion decay constant in vacuum: \(f_\pi = 0.09\) GeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_N \simeq 0.05\) GeV
- Pure gauge QCD deconfinement transition temperature: about 270 MeV (first order)
- Current quark masses used in three-flavor PNJL model: \(m_{u,d} \simeq 4\) MeV
- Common transition temperature from two-flavor PNJL model with Polyakov loop coupling: around 0.2 GeV
- Temperature at which chiral condensate approaches non-interacting Fermi gas behavior: about 100 MeV
- Thermal pion contribution to condensate reduction at \(T \simeq 100\) MeV: additional 5% of vacuum value

## Context

This lecture summary extends the author's prior work on in-medium chiral perturbation theory (FKW2005, FKW2010) for nuclear matter thermodynamics and connects it to PNJL model studies of the QCD phase diagram (HRCW2009, HRCW2010). The non-local PNJL approach improves upon earlier local NJL-type models (Fu2003, RTW2006, RRW2007) by eliminating the artificial momentum cutoff and generating a momentum-dependent dynamical quark mass that can be compared with lattice QCD results (Bo2003) and Dyson-Schwinger approaches. The work enables future efforts to consistently combine nuclear matter constraints from chiral effective field theory with the high-temperature success of PNJL models, addressing open questions about the existence and location of the QCD critical point at finite baryon density.
