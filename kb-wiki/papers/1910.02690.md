# Diphoton elastic scattering in UPC at smaller $W_{γγ}$

> We present a study of photon-photon scattering for $W_{γγ} < 5$ GeV. We extend earlier calculations of this cross section for $W_{γγ}>$ 5 GeV into the low mass range where photoproduction of the pseudoscalar mesons $η$(548), $η^{'}(958)$ and other mesonic resonances contribute to the two-photon final states. We consider the dominant background of the two photon final state which arises from $γγ$ decays of photoproduced $π^{0} π^0$-pairs. We discuss how to reduce the background by imposing cuts on different kinematical variables. We present results for ALICE and LHCb kinematics.

## Key claim

The paper extends calculations of photon-photon elastic scattering cross sections into the low invariant-mass region below 5 GeV, where photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons (η, η') contributes significantly to the two-photon final state, and demonstrates that the dominant π⁰π⁰ background can be reduced by imposing cuts on kinematical variables such as transverse momentum, scalar asymmetry, and acoplanarity.

## Method

The authors use an equivalent photon approximation (EPA) in impact parameter space to compute the cross section for Pb+Pb → Pb+Pb γγ and Pb+Pb → Pb+Pb π⁰π⁰ in ultra-peripheral collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV. The signal includes fermionic box diagrams and s-channel resonance contributions (η, η', η_c, χ_c0), while the background is modeled using a multi-component description of γγ → π⁰π⁰ data from Belle and Crystal Ball. Cross sections are evaluated for ALICE (|η_γ| < 0.9) and LHCb (2 < η_γ < 4.5) acceptances, and the effect of cuts on diphoton transverse momentum, scalar asymmetry, and acoplanarity is studied.

## Key numbers

* Total nuclear cross section for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for the box (signal) contribution in the W_γγ = 0–2 GeV range: 4,890 nb (ALICE) and 3,818 nb (LHCb).
* Total nuclear cross section for the π⁰π⁰ background in the W_γγ = 0–2 GeV range: 135,300 nb (ALICE) and 40,866 nb (LHCb).
* Total nuclear cross section for η(548) resonance in the W_γγ = 0–2 GeV range: 722,573 nb (ALICE) and 568,499 nb (LHCb).
* Total nuclear cross section for η'(958) resonance in the W_γγ = 0–2 GeV range: 54,241 nb (ALICE) and 40,482 nb (LHCb).
* Total nuclear cross section for the box contribution for W_γγ > 2 GeV: 146 nb (ALICE) and 79 nb (LHCb).
* Total nuclear cross section for η_c(1S) for W_γγ > 2 GeV: 9 nb (ALICE) and 5 nb (LHCb).
* Total nuclear cross section for χ_c0(1P) for W_γγ > 2 GeV: 4 nb (ALICE) and 2 nb (LHCb).
* Total nuclear cross section for η_c(2S) for W_γγ > 2 GeV: 2 nb (ALICE) and 1 nb (LHCb).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of diphoton elastic scattering (Klusek-Gawenda 2016) from the high-mass region (W_γγ > 5 GeV, where ATLAS and CMS have reported data) down to W_γγ < 5 GeV, where meson resonances become important. The study is motivated by the possibility of measuring γγ → γγ scattering at ALICE and LHCb, which have different acceptances than ATLAS and CMS. The results enable future experimental searches for light-by-light scattering at lower invariant masses, provided that Monte Carlo simulations with detailed detector responses are performed.
