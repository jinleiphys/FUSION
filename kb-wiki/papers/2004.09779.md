# Quadruply charmed baryons as heavy quark symmetry partners of the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$

> Both unitary chiral theories and lattice QCD simulations show that the $DK$ interaction is attractive and can form a bound state, namely, $D^*_{s0}(2317)$. Assuming the validity of the heavy antiquark-diquark symmetry (HADS), the $Ξ_{cc}\bar{K}$ interaction is the same as the $DK$ interaction, which implies the existence of a $Ξ_{cc}\bar{K}$ bound state with a binding energy of $49-64$ MeV. In this work, we study whether a $Ξ_{cc}Ξ_{cc}\bar{K}$ three-body system binds. The $Ξ_{cc}Ξ_{cc}$ interaction is described by exchanging $π$, $σ$, $ρ$, and $ω$ mesons, with the corresponding couplings related to those of the $NN$ interaction via the quark model. We indeed find a $Ξ_{cc}Ξ_{cc}\bar{K}$ bound state, with quantum numbers $J^P=0^-$, $I=\frac{1}{2}$, $S=1$ and $C=4$, and a binding energy of $80-118$ MeV. It is interesting to note that this system is very similar to the well-known $NN\bar{K}$ system, which has been studied extensively both theoretically and experimentally. Within the same framework, we show the existence of a $NN\bar{K}$ state with a binding energy of $35-43$ MeV, consistent with the results of other theoretical works and experimental data, which serves as a consistency check on the predicted $Ξ_{cc}Ξ_{cc}\bar{K}$ bound state.

## Key claim
The paper predicts the existence of a $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ three-body bound state (a quadruply charmed baryon) with quantum numbers $J^P=0^-$, $I=\frac{1}{2}$, $S=1$ and $C=4$, assuming the validity of heavy antiquark-diquark symmetry (HADS) to relate the $\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ interaction to the $DK$ interaction that forms the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$. The prediction carries a hedge: HADS is known to be broken at the level of $25-40\%$ for a charm quark pair, and the authors study uncertainties by varying couplings by $25\%$ from their central values.

## Method
The $\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ interaction is taken from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, with its strength fixed by reproducing the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ as a $DK$ bound state, then mapped to the $\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ system via HADS. The $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}$ interaction is described by a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model exchanging $\pi$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons, with couplings related to the $NN$ interaction via the quark model and HADS. The three-body Schr\"odinger equation for the $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ system is solved using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), with the total wave function expanded in Jacobi coordinates and antisymmetrized for the two identical $\Xi_{cc}$ baryons.

## Key numbers
- $\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ dimer binding energy: $49-64$ MeV (without repulsive core) and $49-63$ MeV (with repulsive core), depending on the cutoff $R_c = 0.5-2.0$ fm.
- $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ trimer binding energy: $79.6-118.4$ MeV (for $R_c = 0.5-2.0$ fm, without repulsive core), with uncertainties from $25\%$ HADS breaking shown in Table 1.
- $NN\bar{K}$ trimer binding energy: $35-43$ MeV, used as a consistency check against prior theoretical and experimental results.
- The $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ is reproduced as a $DK$ bound state with a binding energy of $45$ MeV to fix the cutoff.
- The $\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ scattering length is $2.15$ fm (from Ref. Meng:2018zbl, cited in the text).

## Context
This work extends the heavy antiquark-diquark symmetry (HADS) framework, previously applied to $DK$ and $DDK$ systems, to predict a $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ bound state. It builds on prior predictions of a $\Xi_{cc}\bar{K}$ bound state by Guo (2011) at about $60 \pm 20$ MeV and by Yan (2018) at $50$ MeV, and on studies of the $NN\bar{K}$ system (Yamazaki 2002, Dote 2008, and others) which the authors use as a consistency check. The predicted quadruply charmed baryon is analogous to the $NN\bar{K}$ trimer and, if observed, would provide a test of HADS and the OBE model for doubly charmed baryons.
