# Solution of large scale nuclear structure problems by wave function factorization

> Low-lying shell model states may be approximated accurately by a sum over products of proton and neutron states. The optimal factors are determined by a variational principle and result from the solution of rather low-dimensional eigenvalue problems. Application of this method to sd-shell nuclei, pf-shell nuclei, and to no-core shell model problems shows that very accurate approximations to the exact solutions may be obtained. Their energies, quantum numbers and overlaps with exact eigenstates converge exponentially fast as the number of retained factors is increased.

## Key claim

Low-lying shell model states can be approximated accurately by a sum over products of proton and neutron states, with the optimal factors determined variationally; the method yields energies, quantum numbers, and overlaps with exact eigenstates that converge exponentially fast as the number of retained factors is increased, but the convergence is fastest for ground states and for systems with weak proton-neutron correlations, while excited states converge more slowly unless directly targeted or treated with angular-momentum projection.

## Method

The authors factorize the shell-model wave function as a sum over products of proton and neutron states, with the number of retained factors controlled by a truncation parameter. The optimal factors are determined by iteratively solving coupled generalized eigenvalue problems derived from a variational principle, using the sparse matrix solver ARPACK. The method is implemented in both an m-scheme (preserving axial symmetry) and a jj-coupled scheme (preserving good angular momentum), and applied to sd-shell nuclei with the USD interaction, pf-shell nuclei with the KB3 interaction, and a no-core shell-model calculation for ⁴He.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁴Mg, solving an eigenvalue problem of only 10% of the full m-scheme dimension yields squared overlaps with exact results between 90% and 96% for low-lying states; the ground-state squared overlap exceeds 99% once the dimension exceeds 20% of the full dimension.
- For ⁴⁸Cr (KB3 interaction, exact ground-state energy E₀ = -32.95 MeV, full m-scheme dimension d_max = 1.96 × 10⁶), a calculation with only 8% of the m-scheme dimension (Ω = 391 factors) deviates less than 100 keV from the exact ground-state energy.
- For ⁵⁶Ni (full m-scheme dimension d_max ≈ 1.09 × 10⁹), the ground-state energy is reproduced within 100 keV using an eigenvalue problem with relative dimension d/d_max ≈ 0.1% (Ω = 147 factors).
- For ⁶⁰Fe (full m-scheme dimension d_max ≈ 110 × 10⁶), the ground-state energy is reproduced within 50 keV using an eigenvalue problem with relative dimension d/d_max ≈ 1% (Ω = 352 factors).

## Context

This work extends the factorization method previously introduced by the same authors (Papenbrock et al., 2003) and is related to the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) approach of White (1992), which also exploits the rapid fall-off of singular values in a wave-function factorization. The method contrasts with truncation schemes based on statistical spectroscopy (Horoi et al., 1994, 1999, 2002, 2003) and configuration truncation with extrapolation (Mizusaki and Imada, 2002, 2003), as the factorization directly follows from a variational principle rather than from physically motivated selection rules. The results enable accurate approximations for large-scale shell-model problems where exact diagonalization is infeasible, particularly for upper pf-shell nuclei with m-scheme dimensions exceeding one billion.
