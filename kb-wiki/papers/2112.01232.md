# A transport model study of multiparticle cumulants in $p+p$ collisions at 13 TeV

> Flow-like signals including the ridge structure observed in small collision systems that are similar to those in large collision systems have led to questions about the onset of collectivity in nuclear collisions. In this study, we use the string melting version of a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with or without the sub-nucleon geometry for the proton to study multiparticle cumulants in $p+p$ collisions at 13 TeV. Both versions of the model produce negative $c_{2}\{4 \}$ values at high multiplicities. In addition, the dependence of $c_{2}\{4 \}$ on the parton cross section is non-monotonous, where only a range of parton cross section values leads to negative $c_{2} \{4 \}$. Furthermore, the AMPT model with sub-nucleon geometry better describes the multiplicity dependence of $c_{2} \{4 \}$, demonstrating the importance of incorporating the sub-nucleon geometry in studies of small collision systems.

# A transport model study of multiparticle cumulants in $p+p$ collisions at 13 TeV

## Key claim
The string melting version of the AMPT model, both with and without sub-nucleon geometry for the proton, can produce negative four-particle cumulants $c_{2}\{4\}$ at high multiplicities in $p+p$ collisions at 13 TeV, but only when the parton cross section lies within a limited range (approximately 0.15 to 1.5 mb); quantitatively, no model results with a constant parton cross section can fully reproduce the ATLAS data for either $c_{2}\{2\}$ or $c_{2}\{4\}$.

## Method
The study uses the string melting version of the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model, which includes HIJING initial conditions, Zhang's parton cascade for elastic parton scatterings, quark coalescence hadronization, and the ART hadronic cascade. Two versions are compared: a "normal AMPT" treating the proton as a point particle, and a "3-quark AMPT" that incorporates sub-nucleon geometry by sampling three constituent quarks from a matter distribution $\rho(r) \propto e^{-r/R}$ with $R=0.2$ fm. Multiparticle cumulants $c_{2}\{2\}$ and $c_{2}\{4\}$ are calculated using both the standard cumulant method and the three-subevent cumulant method for reference particles within $0.3 < p_{\rm T} < 3$ GeV/$c$ and $|\eta| < 2.5$, with about 150 million simulated $p+p$ events per case.

## Key numbers
- The parton cross section is given by $\sigma = 4.5\pi\alpha_{s}^{2}/\mu^{2}$, with values tested including 0.01 mb (no scatterings), 0.15 mb, 0.3 mb, 1.5 mb, and 3 mb.
- The $c_{2}\{4\}$ value from the standard cumulant method is negative only when $\sigma$ is within approximately [0.15, 1.5] mb; at $\sigma \simeq 0.15$ mb and $\sigma \simeq 1.5$ mb, $c_{2}\{4\}$ crosses zero for multiplicities around $N_{\rm ch} \sim 130$.
- In the ATLAS data, the sign change of $c_{2}\{4\}$ from positive to negative occurs at $N_{\rm ch} \sim 130$; the 3-quark AMPT model at $\sigma=0.15$ mb or 1.5 mb shows the sign change at $N_{\rm ch} \sim 80$.
- The normal AMPT model at $\sigma=0.15$ or 0.3 mb produces mostly negative $c_{2}\{4\}$ values when $N_{\rm ch} > 32$, while the ATLAS data show the sign change at $N_{\rm ch} \sim 130$.
- For $c_{2}\{2\}$ within the multiplicity range $N_{\rm ch}(p_{\rm T}>0.4~{\rm GeV}/c) \in (40,150)$, the value first increases with parton cross section and then decreases once $\sigma \ge 1.5$ mb.
- The sub-nucleon geometry uses a matter distribution $\rho(r) \propto e^{-r/R}$ with $R=0.2$ fm.

## Context
This work extends studies of flow-like signals in small collision systems, where ridge structures similar to those in large A+A collisions have been observed by ATLAS (2017) and CMS (2015). Prior hydrodynamics-based hybrid model calculations by Zhao (2017) found $c_{2}\{4\} > 0$ in $p+p$ collisions, in contrast to the negative values expected from collective flow. The present study uses the AMPT model, which has been applied to collective flow from small to large systems at RHIC and LHC energies by Bzdak (2014), Ma (2016), and others. The results demonstrate that including sub-nucleon geometry of the proton, following the approach of Zheng (2021), improves agreement with ATLAS data for the multiplicity dependence of $c_{2}\{4\}$, enabling further investigation of the onset of collectivity in small systems.
