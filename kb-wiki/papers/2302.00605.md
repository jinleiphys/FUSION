# The RHIC Cold QCD Program

> The RHIC Cold QCD program has produced a remarkable breadth of physics results and experimental techniques in the exploration of the fundamental structure of strongly interacting matter over the years. In this document, we present highlights of longitudinal and transverse spin physics to date and the 25 years of innovation in accelerator science from the RHIC Spin program. These measurements and techniques will be essential to fully realize the scientific missions of the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) by providing a comprehensive set of measurements in hadronic collisions and laying the foundation for the design of the future EIC.

# The RHIC Cold QCD Program

## Key claim
The RHIC Cold QCD program has produced a broad set of measurements in transverse and longitudinal spin physics that will be essential for the scientific mission of the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), but many results remain preliminary or require further analysis of data from upcoming runs (2022-2024) to reach conclusive tests of key predictions such as the Sivers function sign change.

## Method
The program used polarized proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC at center-of-mass energies of 200 and 510 GeV, with the STAR and PHENIX detectors measuring single-spin asymmetries in hadron, jet, photon, weak boson, and heavy flavor production. Measurements exploited jets and their substructure, dihadron interference fragmentation functions, forward calorimetry, and ultra-peripheral collisions to probe transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distributions and fragmentation functions, as well as collinear twist-3 correlation functions and generalized parton distributions (GPDs).

## Key numbers
- Transverse single-spin asymmetry $A_N$ for forward $\pi^0$ production persists up to $\sqrt{s}=510$ GeV with weak energy dependence, measured at $x_F$ up to 0.8 and $p_T<1$ GeV/$c$ by RHICf at $\eta>6$
- STAR measured Collins asymmetries for charged pions within jets at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV with jet $p_T>9.9$ GeV/$c$, finding forward-scattered asymmetries larger than theoretical predictions from D'Alesio et al. (2017)
- The 2017 STAR $W/Z$ data had approximately 14 times more integrated luminosity than the 2011 dataset, and found smaller $A_N$ asymmetries than suggested by the 2011 data
- STAR observed the first-ever Sivers effect in dijet production from 200 GeV transverse spin data recorded in 2012 and 2015, with results unfolded for individual parton $k_T$
- PHENIX measured direct photon $A_N$ at midrapidity $|\eta|<0.35$ in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, consistent with zero to within 1% across the measured $p_T$ range
- STAR measured $J/\psi$ transverse asymmetry $A_N^{\gamma}$ in ultra-peripheral $p^{\uparrow}Au$ collisions at mean $W_{\gamma p}\approx 24$ GeV from Run-15 data with integrated luminosity 140 nb$^{-1}$, consistent with zero
- Run-24 is projected to provide about 1.2 pb$^{-1}$ for $p^{\uparrow}Au$ UPC measurements, enabling a sizeable reduction of statistical uncertainty
- PHENIX observed strong nuclear $A$ dependence for charged hadron $A_N$ at $1.2<\eta<2.4$ consistent with $A^{-1/3}$ suppression for positive hadrons, while STAR found weaker nuclear dependence for $\pi^0$ at $2.7<\eta<3.8$
- STAR measured forward diffractive EM-jet $A_N$ at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV favoring a non-zero negative asymmetry with 3.3$\sigma$ significance

## Context
This paper reviews the RHIC Cold QCD program's results on transverse spin physics, extending prior SIDIS measurements from HERMES and COMPASS into the high-$Q^2$, low-$x$ regime ($x\sim 0.01$) using hadronic collisions. The measurements of Collins asymmetries in jets, Sivers effects in dijets, and weak boson asymmetries provide tests of TMD factorization, universality, and evolution that are complementary to SIDIS and $e^+e^-$ results from Belle and BaBar. The program directly enables the future EIC by establishing benchmark measurements in hadronic collisions and informing detector design for forward jet detection, while open questions remain regarding the origin of nuclear dependence in single-spin asymmetries and the mechanism behind the large forward $\pi^0$ $A_N$ puzzle.
