# Laser spectroscopy of neutron-rich $^{207,208}$Hg isotopes: Illuminating the kink and odd-even staggering in charge radii across the $N=126$ shell closure

> The mean-square charge radii of $^{207,208}$Hg ($Z=80, N=127,128$) have been studied for the first time and those of $^{202,203,206}$Hg ($N=122,123,126$) remeasured by the application of in-source resonance-ionization laser spectroscopy at ISOLDE (CERN). The characteristic \textit{kink} in the charge radii at the $N=126$ neutron shell closure has been revealed, providing the first information on its behavior below the $Z=82$ proton shell closure. A theoretical analysis has been performed within relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov and non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approaches, considering both the new mercury results and existing lead data. Contrary to previous interpretations, it is demonstrated that both the kink at $N=126$ and the odd-even staggering (OES) in its vicinity can be described predominately at the mean-field level, and that pairing does not need to play a crucial role in their origin. A new OES mechanism is suggested, related to the staggering in the occupation of the different neutron orbitals in odd- and even-$A$ nuclei, facilitated by particle-vibration coupling for odd-$A$ nuclei.

## Key claim

The kink in charge radii at the N=126 neutron shell closure and the odd-even staggering (OES) in its vicinity can be described predominantly at the mean-field level, and pairing does not need to play a crucial role in their origin, contrary to previous interpretations based on non-relativistic functionals.

## Method

In-source resonance-ionization laser spectroscopy was performed at ISOLDE (CERN) using a 1.4-GeV proton beam on a molten-lead target, with mercury isotopes ionized via three sequential atomic transitions and detected by either a Faraday cup or the ISOLTRAP Paul trap and multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer. The measured isotope shifts in the 253.65 nm transition were converted to changes in mean-square charge radii. Theoretical analysis employed spherical relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with the DD-ME2 covariant energy density functional and spherical non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (NR-HFB) calculations with the semi-realistic M3Y-P6a interaction, including a newly developed RHB code enabling blocking of selected single-particle orbitals for odd-A nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The isotope shift for 207Hg relative to 198Hg is -25,790(190) MHz, corresponding to delta<r^2> = 0.503(4){35} fm^2
- The isotope shift for 208Hg relative to 198Hg is -32,030(160) MHz, corresponding to delta<r^2> = 0.624(3){44} fm^2
- The isotope shift for 206Hg relative to 198Hg is -20,930(160) MHz, corresponding to delta<r^2> = 0.409(3){29} fm^2
- The rms deviation between DD-ME2 and experimental binding energies is 1.3 MeV for 198-214Pb and 1.1 MeV for 201-208Hg
- In RHB(DD-ME2) calculations, EGS configurations with a blocked nu 2g9/2 state lie at excitation energies of 137 keV, 122 keV, and 96 keV above ground state configurations with a blocked nu 1i11/2 state in 209,211,213Pb

## Context

This work provides the first measurement of charge radii across N=126 below the Z=82 proton shell closure, extending previous studies of the kink in lead isotopes by Anselment (1986) and bismuth isotopes by Barzakh (2018). The results challenge the interpretation from Gorges (2019) using non-relativistic Skyrme and Fayans functionals, which attributed the kink and OES primarily to gradient terms in pairing interactions. The new data and theoretical analysis enable systematic benchmarking of covariant energy density functionals across the N=126 shell closure and suggest a new OES mechanism related to staggering in neutron orbital occupation between odd and even isotopes, facilitated by particle-vibration coupling.
