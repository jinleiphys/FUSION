# Parity restoration in the Highly Truncated Diagonalization Approach: application to the outer fission barrier of $^{240}$Pu

> The restoration of the parity symmetry has been performed in the framework of the Highly Truncated Diagonalization Approach suited to treat correlations in an explicitly particle-number conserving microscopic approach. To do so we have assumed axial symmetry and used a generalized Wick's theorem due to Löwdin in a projection-after-variation scheme. We have chosen the Skyrme SkM$^*$ energy-density functional for the particle-hole channel and a density-independent delta force for the residual interaction. We have applied this approach in the region of the outer fission barrier of the $^{240}$Pu nucleus. As a result, we have shown that the $K^π = 0^+$ fission isomeric state is statically unstable against intrinsic-parity breaking modes, while the projection does not affect the energy at the top of the intrinsic outer fission barrier. Altogether, this leads to an increase of the height of the outer fission barrier--with respect to the fission isomeric state--by about 350 keV, affecting thus significantly the fission-decay lifetime of the considered fission isomer.

## Key claim

The parity projection of the HTDA correlated wave function increases the height of the outer fission barrier of \(^{240}\)Pu relative to the fission isomeric state by about 350 keV, but only because the \(K^\pi = 0^+\) fission isomeric state is statically unstable against intrinsic-parity breaking modes; the projection does not affect the energy at the top of the intrinsic outer fission barrier, so the barrier-height increase is entirely due to the lowering of the isomeric state's energy after projection.

## Method

The authors performed Highly Truncated Diagonalization Approach (HTDA) calculations using the Skyrme SkM* energy-density functional for the particle-hole channel and a density-independent delta force for the residual interaction, assuming axial symmetry. They constructed correlated wave functions from a particle-hole vacuum plus one-pair excitations, then applied parity projection after variation using Löwdin's generalized Wick theorem to compute projected energies and overlaps. The method was applied to the two-dimensional deformation energy surface (axial quadrupole \(Q_{20}\) and octupole \(Q_{30}\) moments) of \(^{240}\)Pu from before the fission isomeric state to beyond the outer saddle point.

## Key numbers

- The second fission-barrier height in the HTDA calculation is 5.5 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 5.1 MeV (Bjørnholm 1980).
- The parity projection increases the height of the outer fission barrier with respect to the fission isomeric state by about 350 keV.
- The transition between different octupole-deformation behaviors occurs at \(Q_{20} \simeq 65\) b, \(\simeq 105\) b, and \(\simeq 125\) b.
- At \(Q_{20} = 85\) b, the overlap between an intrinsic solution and its parity-transformed wave function drops to about 0.01 for \(Q_{30} \gtrsim 3.5\) \(\mathrm{b^{3/2}}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent calculations by Berger and by Bonneau, Quentin, and Samsoen (BQS) that demonstrated octupole deformation lowers the second fission barrier of actinide nuclei. It also builds on the HTDA method developed by Pillet et al. (2002) and previous applications by Quentin et al. (2007) and Bonneau et al. (2007). The results enable more realistic estimates of fission-decay lifetimes for the fission isomer in \(^{240}\)Pu and provide a framework for parity-projected calculations in other heavy nuclei where octupole correlations are important, complementing the systematic study by Samyn et al. (2005) using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov with separate parity projection.
