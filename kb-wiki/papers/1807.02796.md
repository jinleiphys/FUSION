# Causality and dissipation in relativistic polarizeable fluids

> We analyze the breakdown of causality for the perfect fluid limit in a medium with polarizeability. We show that to restore causality a relaxation term linking vorticity and polarization, analogous to the Israel-Stewart term linking viscous forces and gradients,is required. This term provides a minimum amount of dissipation a locally thermalized relativistic medium with polarizeability must have, independently of its underlying degrees of freedom. For ferromagnetic materials an infrared acausal mode remains, which we interpret as a Banks-Casher mode signaling spontaneous magnetization. With these ingredients, we propose a candidate for a fully causal Lagrangian of a relativistic polarizeable system near the perfect fluid limit.

## Key claim

The paper shows that causality in a relativistic polarizeable fluid requires a relaxation term linking vorticity and polarization, analogous to the Israel-Stewart term for viscosity, which introduces a minimum amount of dissipation that is independent of the underlying microscopic degrees of freedom; for ferrovortetic materials (c > 0) an infrared acausal mode remains, interpreted as a Banks-Casher mode signaling spontaneous magnetization.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian field theory for a relativistic fluid with polarization, extending the formalism of Nicolis et al. (2011) and building on prior work by Montenegro and Torrieri (2016, 2017). They introduce a non-equilibrium polarization tensor Y_{\mu\nu} with a Maxwell-Cattaneo-type relaxation equation (Eq. 1) that asymptotically aligns polarization with vorticity, analogous to the Israel-Stewart treatment of viscous forces. The dispersion relations for longitudinal (sound) and transverse (vortex) modes are derived by linearizing around a hydrostatic background and solving the coupled equations of motion, and the group velocities are analyzed in the ultraviolet (large k) limit to test for causality violations.

## Key numbers

- The causality condition for antiferrovortetic materials (c < 0) is: τ_Y^2 ≥ 8 c χ^2(b_o,0) / [(1 - c_s^2) b_o F'(b_o)], where τ_Y is the relaxation time, χ is the vortical susceptibility, c_s is the speed of sound, b_o is the hydrostatic entropy density, and F'(b_o) is the derivative of the free energy.
- For ferrovortetic materials (c > 0), the asymptotic group velocity for transverse modes is v_g = 1 / sqrt(1 - τ_Y^2 / (B/A)), where B = 4 c χ^2(b_o,0) and A = b_o F'(b_o); causality requires τ_Y^2 / (B/A) < 1.
- For antiferrovortetic materials (c < 0), the asymptotic group velocity for transverse modes is v_g = 1 / sqrt(1 + τ_Y^2 / (B/A)), which is always causal.
- The asymptotic group velocity for longitudinal modes is v_g = sqrt( (c_s^2 τ_Y^2 ∓ (B/A)) / (τ_Y^2 ± (B/A)) ) for c > 0 (upper sign) and c < 0 (lower sign).
- The viscosity over entropy density ratio is bounded by η/s ≥ T τ_Y^{lim}, where τ_Y^{lim} saturates the inequality above.

## Context

This work extends the Lagrangian formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics with polarization developed by Montenegro and Torrieri (2016, 2017), which showed that the perfect fluid limit with polarization leads to acausal dispersion relations due to Ostrogradski instabilities. The paper resolves this by introducing a relaxation term analogous to the Israel-Stewart (1976) treatment of viscous hydrodynamics, using the doubled-variable technique of Galley (2013) and Grozdanov and Polonyi (2015) as formulated by Ushakov (2017). The result provides a bottom-up constraint on dissipation in polarizeable fluids, connecting to the broader question of a universal lower bound on η/s, previously addressed via holography by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005) and via fluctuation arguments by Moore and Sohrabi (2011) and Kovtun et al. (2011). The work enables future calculation of transport coefficients via Kubo formulas for polarization-vorticity correlators and suggests that ferrovortetic materials may exhibit spontaneous magnetization analogous to the Banks-Casher (1980) mechanism.
