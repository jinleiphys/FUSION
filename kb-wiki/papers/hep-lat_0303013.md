# Pressure and non-linear susceptibilities in QCD at finite chemical potentials

> When the free energy density of QCD is expanded in a series in the chemical potential, mu, the Taylor coefficients are the non-linear quark number susceptibilities. We show that these depend on the prescription for putting chemical potential on the lattice, making all extrapolations in chemical potential prescription dependent at finite lattice spacing. To put bounds on the prescription dependence, we investigate the magnitude of the non-linear susceptibilities over a range of temperature, T, in QCD with two degenerate flavours of light dynamical quarks at lattice spacing 1/4T. The prescription dependence is removed in quenched QCD through a continuum extrapolation, and the dependence of the pressure, P, on mu is obtained.

## Key claim

The Taylor coefficients of the free energy density expansion in chemical potential (the non-linear quark number susceptibilities) are prescription-dependent at finite lattice spacing, meaning all extrapolations in chemical potential are ambiguous unless a continuum extrapolation is performed; this prescription dependence vanishes only in the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors compute the non-linear quark number susceptibilities up to fourth order in chemical potential using lattice QCD with two degenerate flavours of staggered quarks at lattice spacing 1/4T. They evaluate these susceptibilities on dynamical configurations with sea quark mass m/Tc=0.1 at temperatures T=Tc, 1.5Tc, 2Tc, and 3Tc, and also perform a continuum extrapolation in quenched QCD using lattices with Nt=4, 8, 10, 12, and 14 at T=1.5Tc, 2Tc, and 3Tc. The prescription dependence is quantified by comparing the standard Hasenfratz-Karsch (HK) exponential prescription with the alternative Bilic-Gavai (BG) prescription.

## Key numbers

- For the critical end-point at Nt=4 in 2+1 flavour QCD, the HK prescription gives mu_E=725±3 MeV while the BG prescription gives mu_E≈692 MeV, yielding an uncertainty about 11 times larger than the statistical errors
- In quenched QCD at T=1.5Tc, the continuum-extrapolated value of chi_uuuu is 0.45(1) and mu_*/T=4.67(4), compared to Nt=4 values of chi_uuuu=1.48(2) and mu_*/T=3.81(2)
- At T=2Tc in quenched QCD, the continuum value of chi_uuuu is 0.49(3) and mu_*/T=4.76(4), while at Nt=6 the values are chi_uuuu=1.01(1) and mu_*/T=4.11(1)
- For an ideal quark gas in the continuum, mu_*/T=sqrt(2)*pi≈4.43
- At RHIC energies mu/Tc=0.06, making Delta P/T^4 negligible; at SPS energies mu/Tc≈0.45 the effects are more significant but still dominated by the leading quadratic term

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers on linear susceptibilities (Gavai and Gupta, 2002-2003) to the non-linear susceptibilities needed for finite-density QCD. It directly addresses the prescription ambiguity in lattice chemical potential first noted by Hasenfratz and Karsch (1983) and Bilic and Gavai (1990). The results show that estimates of the critical end-point from finite-lattice-spacing calculations, such as those by Fodor and Katz (2002), require continuum extrapolation to remove prescription dependence and finite-spacing artifacts of order 20% in mu_*. The continuum results for pressure at finite chemical potential enable reliable comparisons with heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and SPS energies.
