# Nucleon form factors in dispersively improved Chiral Effective Field Theory I: Scalar form factor

> We propose a method for calculating the nucleon form factors (FFs) of $G$-parity-even operators by combining Chiral Effective Field Theory ($χ$EFT) and dispersion analysis. The FFs are expressed as dispersive integrals over the two-pion cut at $t > 4 M_π^2$. The spectral functions are obtained from the elastic unitarity condition and expressed as products of the complex $ππ\rightarrow N\bar N$ partial-wave amplitudes and the timelike pion FF. $χ$EFT is used to calculate the ratio of the partial-wave amplitudes and the pion FF, which is real and free of $ππ$ rescattering in the $t$-channel ($N/D$ method). The rescattering effects are then incorporated by multiplying with the squared modulus of the empirical pion FF. The procedure results in a marked improvement compared to conventional $χ$EFT calculations of the spectral functions. We apply the method to the nucleon scalar FF and compute the scalar spectral function, the scalar radius, the $t$-dependent FF, and the Cheng-Dashen discrepancy. Higher-order chiral corrections are estimated through the $πN$ low-energy constants. Results are in excellent agreement with dispersion-theoretical calculations. We elaborate several other interesting aspects of our method. The results show proper scaling behavior in the large-$N_c$ limit of QCD because the $χ$EFT calculation includes $N$ and $Δ$ intermediate states. The squared modulus of the timelike pion FF required by our method can be extracted from Lattice QCD calculations of vacuum correlation functions of the operator at large Euclidean distances. Our method can be applied to the nucleon FFs of other operators of interest, such as the isovector-vector current, the energy-momentum tensor, and twist-2 QCD operators (moments of generalized parton distributions).

## Key claim

The authors propose a method that combines Chiral Effective Field Theory (χEFT) with dispersion analysis to calculate nucleon form factors, where χEFT is used only for the ratio of the ππ → N\(\bar N\) partial-wave amplitude to the pion form factor (which is free of ππ rescattering), while the rescattering effects are incorporated by multiplying with the squared modulus of the empirical timelike pion form factor, resulting in a marked improvement over conventional χEFT calculations of the spectral functions.

## Method

The spectral function of the nucleon scalar form factor on the two-pion cut (t > 4 Mπ²) is expressed using the elastic unitarity condition as the product of the complex ππ → N\(\bar N\) partial-wave amplitude and the complex-conjugate timelike pion form factor. The ratio of these two functions, J⁰₊(t), is real and free of ππ rescattering effects and is calculated using χEFT at leading order (LO) with relativistic N and Δ degrees of freedom, with higher-order chiral corrections estimated through πN low-energy constants. The rescattering effects are then incorporated by multiplying J⁰₊(t) with the squared modulus of the empirical timelike pion scalar form factor, which contains the full ππ rescattering effects.

## Key numbers

- The two-pion cut threshold is at t = 4 Mπ², where Mπ is the pion mass.
- The method is applicable up to t ≲ 1 GeV², with the main contribution to dispersive integrals coming from t′ ≲ 0.5 GeV².
- The scalar charge radius from empirical spectral functions is ⟨r²⟩σ ∼ 1.6 fm², substantially larger than the proton's charge radius of ∼ 0.65 fm² (Dirac radius).
- The Cheng-Dashen discrepancy Δσ has been obtained at ∼ 14 MeV from empirical spectral functions.
- The sigma term σ(0) is varied in the range 45–59 MeV to estimate N2LO uncertainties.
- The ρ resonance appears at t = 40 Mπ² = 0.77 GeV².

## Context

This work extends prior χEFT calculations of nucleon form factor spectral functions (Gasser 1987, Bernard 1996, Becher 1999, Kubis 2000, Kaiser 2003) which significantly underestimated spectral functions at energies t ∼ 10–50 Mπ² due to strong ππ rescattering. The method builds on the N/D amplitude analysis technique (Chew 1960) and the elastic unitarity condition (Frazer 1960, Frazer 1960, Höhler 1974), and is applied here to the scalar form factor as a testing ground, with results in excellent agreement with dispersion-theoretical calculations (Gasser 1990, Hoferichter 2012). The approach enables predictive dispersive calculations of nucleon form factors based on χEFT input alone and can be extended to other operators such as the isovector-vector current, energy-momentum tensor, and twist-2 QCD operators.
