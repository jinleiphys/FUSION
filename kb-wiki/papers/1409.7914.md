# Relativistic symmetry breaking in light kaonic nuclei

> As the experimental data from kaonic atoms and $K^{-}N$ scatterings imply that the $K^{-}$-nucleon interaction is strongly attractive at saturation density, there is a possibility to form $K^{-}$-nuclear bound states or kaonic nuclei. In this work, we investigate the ground-state properties of the light kaonic nuclei with the relativistic mean field theory. It is found that the strong attraction between $K^{-}$ and nucleons reshapes the scalar and vector meson fields, leading to the remarkable enhancement of the nuclear density in the interior of light kaonic nuclei and the manifest shift of the single-nucleon energy spectra and magic numbers therein. As a consequence, the pseudospin symmetry is shown to be violated together with enlarged spin-orbit splittings in these kaonic nuclei.

## Key claim

The strong attraction between a single $K^{-}$ and nucleons in light kaonic nuclei reshapes scalar and vector meson fields, leading to a remarkable enhancement of nuclear density in the interior and a manifest shift of single-nucleon energy spectra; as a consequence, pseudospin symmetry is violated together with enlarged spin-orbit splittings, but this effect diminishes in heavier nuclei where the $K^{-}$ is pulled outward by outer nucleons.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set to solve the coupled Dirac equations for nucleons and the Klein-Gordon equation for the $K^{-}$ self-consistently in spherical symmetry. The $K^{-}$-nucleon coupling constants were chosen from SU(3) relations, with $g_{\sigma K}$ adjusted to yield a $K^{-}$ binding energy of 100 MeV for $^{40}_{K^{-}}$Ca. The imaginary part of the $K^{-}$ self-energy was included using a simple $t\rho$ form fitted to kaonic atom data.

## Key numbers

* Pseudospin doublet splitting ($2S_{1/2}$ and $1D_{3/2}$) in $^{40}$Ca: 0.79 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{40}$Ca: 11.37 MeV.
* Spin-orbit splitting ($1P_{3/2}$ and $1P_{1/2}$) in $^{40}$Ca: 4.48 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{40}$Ca: 10.63 MeV.
* Pseudospin doublet splitting in $^{34}$S: 3.50 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{34}$S: 12.36 MeV.
* Spin-orbit splitting in $^{34}$S: 7.03 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{34}$S: 15.66 MeV.
* Pseudospin doublet splitting in $^{48}$Ca: -0.17 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{48}$Ca: 4.59 MeV.
* Spin-orbit splitting in $^{48}$Ca: 3.31 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{48}$Ca: 6.13 MeV.
* Pseudospin doublet splitting in $^{90}$Zr: -2.14 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{90}$Zr: 0.73 MeV.
* Spin-orbit splitting in $^{90}$Zr: 1.82 MeV; in $_{K^{-}}^{90}$Zr: 2.88 MeV.
* Central nuclear density in $_{K^{-}}^{40}$Ca reaches up to twice the saturation density.
* Pseudospin doublet splitting in $_{K^{-}}^{40}$Ca increases sharply for $K^{-}$ binding energies above 80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of kaonic nuclei by Mareš et al. (2005, 2006) and others, which focused on $K^{-}$ binding energies and ground-state properties, by specifically examining pseudospin symmetry breaking and spin-orbit splitting changes. The results show that the $K^{-}$ embedment can reshape shell structure and create new magic numbers (2, 6, 16 in $^{42}_{K^{-}}$Ca) through migration of $S_{1/2}$ orbitals and enlarged spin-orbit splittings. This enables future studies of cold dense nuclear systems as an alternative to hot dense matter from heavy-ion collisions, and provides a testbed for understanding pseudospin symmetry breaking mechanisms beyond isospin effects studied by Meng et al. (1998, 1999) and others.
