# Proton form-factor dependence of the finite-size correction to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen

> The measurement of the 2P^{F=2}_{3/2} to 2S^{F=1}_{1/2} transition in muonic hydrogen by Pohl et al. and subsequent analysis has led to the conclusion that the rms radius of the proton differs from the accepted (CODATA) value by approximately 4%, corresponding to a 4.9 sigma discrepancy. We investigate the finite-size effects - in particular the dependence on the shape of the proton electric form-factor - relevant to this transition using bound-state QED with nonperturbative, relativistic Dirac wave-functions for a wide range of idealised charge-distributions and a parameterization of experimental data in order to comment on the extent to which the perturbation-theory analysis which leads to the above conclusion can be confirmed. We find no statistically significant dependence of this correction on the shape of the proton form-factor.

## Key claim

The finite-size correction to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen shows no statistically significant dependence on the shape of the proton electric form-factor, despite investigating a wide range of idealized charge-distributions and a parameterization of experimental data. This rules out the form-factor shape as a source of the 4% discrepancy between the proton rms radius extracted from muonic hydrogen and the CODATA value.

## Method

The authors used an effective Dirac equation for a muon with reduced mass appropriate to the muon-proton system, integrating iteratively to produce converged wave-functions and eigenvalues for the 2S and 2P states. They calculated the finite-size correction to the Lamb shift by comparing eigenvalues obtained with point-Coulomb and finite-Coulomb potentials, where the finite-Coulomb potential was constructed by convolving the point potential with four different charge-distributions: exponential (dipole form-factor), Gaussian, Yukawa (monopole form-factor), and a distribution fitted to experimental Sachs electric form-factor data from Venkat (2010). The calculations were performed at several proton rms radii spanning 0.2 fm around the CODATA and muonic hydrogen values, and polynomial fits of the form ΔE = a⟨r²⟩ + b⟨r²⟩^(3/2) were extracted.

## Key numbers

- The finite-size correction to the Lamb shift at r_p = 0.878 fm for the exponential distribution is -3.9850 meV; for Yukawa -3.9830 meV; for Gaussian -3.9868 meV; for the G_E-fitted distribution -3.9799 meV.
- The extracted proton rms charge-radius using the exponential distribution is 0.84174(67) fm; using Yukawa 0.84194(67) fm; using Gaussian 0.84155(67) fm; compared to the Pohl (2010) value of 0.84184(67) fm and the 2006 CODATA value of 0.8768(69) fm.
- The discrepancy with the 2006 CODATA value is 4.02% for Gaussian, 4.00% for exponential, and 3.98% for Yukawa charge-distributions.
- The Dirac calculation with an exponential distribution agrees with the perturbative expression of Friar (1978) to within 0.05%.
- The numerical accuracy of the eigenvalue calculation is conservatively ±0.5 μeV.

## Context

This work directly addresses claims by De Rujula (2010) that the form-factor shape could significantly affect the proton radius extracted from the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement by Pohl (2010). The authors extend prior perturbative treatments by Borie (2004), Martynenko (2004, 2006), and Pachucki (1999) using a nonperturbative relativistic Dirac approach, and confirm that the shape dependence is negligible. This enables future work to focus on other possible explanations for the proton radius puzzle, such as off-shell corrections to the photon-nucleon vertex noted by Miller (2011).
