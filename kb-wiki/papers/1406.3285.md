# The observation of long-range three-body Coloumb effects in the decay of 16Ne

> The interaction of an $E/A$=57.6-MeV $^{17}$Ne beam with a Be target was used to populate levels in $^{16}$Ne following neutron knockout reactions. The decay of $^{16}$Ne states into the three-body $^{14}$O+$p$+$p$ continuum was observed in the High Resolution Array (HiRA). For the first time for a 2p emitter, correlations between the momenta of the three decay products were measured with sufficient resolution and statistics to allow for an unambiguous demonstration of their dependence on the long-range nature of the Coulomb interaction. Contrary to previous experiments, the intrinsic decay width of the $^{16}$Ne ground state was found to be narrow ($Γ<60$~keV), consistent with theoretical estimates.

## Key claim

For the first time for a two-proton (2p) emitter, correlations between the momenta of the three decay products were measured with sufficient resolution and statistics to unambiguously demonstrate their dependence on the long-range nature of the Coulomb interaction. Contrary to previous experiments, the intrinsic decay width of the 16Ne ground state was found to be narrow (Γ < 80 keV), consistent with theoretical estimates.

## Method

A 57.6-MeV/nucleon 17Ne beam produced via fragmentation of a 170-MeV/nucleon 20Ne primary beam on a 9Be target was used to populate states in 16Ne via neutron knockout reactions on a secondary 9Be target. The three-body decay products (14O + p + p) were detected in the High Resolution Array (HiRA) of 14 ΔE-E Si-CsI(Tl) telescopes covering zenith angles from 2° to 13.9°. The measured momentum correlations were compared to Monte Carlo simulations of a theoretical model solving the inhomogeneous three-body Schrödinger equation with approximate boundary conditions for the three-body Coulomb problem, using the hyperspherical harmonics method with classical trajectory extrapolation at large hyperradii.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state decay energy of 16Ne: ET = 1.466(20) MeV, consistent with the value of 1.466(45) MeV from Burleson (1980).
- 3-σ upper limit on the intrinsic decay width of the 16Ne ground state: Γ < 80 keV, obtained using a Breit-Wigner line shape with target thickness scaling factor ftar = 0.91.
- Previously known peaks at ET = 3.16(2) and 7.60(4) MeV are both assigned as 2+ excited states; a broad structure at ET ∼ 5.0(5) MeV is described as a 1- "soft" excitation.
- Full width at half maximum of the ε distribution in the "Y" Jacobi system: 0.33, compared to 0.41 in the lower-resolution work of Wamers (2014).

## Context

This work fills a gap between studies of the lightest p-shell 2p emitter 6Be (Egorova 2012, Fomichev 2012), where Coulomb effects are minute, and heavier pf-shell emitters like 54Zn (Ascher 2011) and 45Fe (Miernik 2007), which suffer from poor statistics. The high-resolution data on the sd-shell nucleus 16Ne demonstrate that the three-body Coulomb interaction must be considered out to distances far beyond 103 fm, validating the theoretical treatment for future studies of heavier two-proton decay. This enables extraction of nuclear-structure information from correlation observables, as the long-range Coulomb effects must first be accurately determined before the effects of structure and nuclear final-state interactions can be properly probed.
