# Threshold $π^0$ photoproduction in relativistic chiral perturbation theory

> We present a calculation of $π^0$ photoproduction on the proton in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to and including chiral order $q^4$. With the results we analyze the latest $π^0$ photoproduction data in the threshold region obtained at the Mainz Microtron. In the calculation of observables and the fit of the low-energy constants, we take $S$, $P$, and $D$ waves into account. We compare the results for the multipoles with the corresponding single-energy analysis. Furthermore, we also fit the $O(q^4)$ heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation and compare both results. We provide predictions for several polarization observables for future experiments. Finally, we discuss the $β$ parameter of the unitarity cusp which is related to the breaking of isospin symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of neutral pion photoproduction on the proton using manifestly Lorentz-invariant (relativistic) baryon chiral perturbation theory up to chiral order \(q^4\), and finds that the relativistic calculation shows more favorable convergence properties than the heavy-baryon approach, although the heavy-baryon fit yields a slightly better reduced chi-squared value of 1.11 compared to 1.22 for the relativistic fit.

## Method

The authors computed all tree and loop diagrams up to chiral order \(q^4\) using Mathematica and the FeynCalc package, with loop integrals evaluated via LoopTools. They performed a \(\chi^2\) fit of the low-energy constants (LECs) to the latest MAMI data for differential cross sections and photon asymmetries in the threshold region, including \(S\), \(P\), and \(D\) waves in the multipole decomposition, and used a maximum lab-frame photon energy of 165.8 MeV for the fit range. The bootstrap method was employed to estimate parameter errors, and the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme was used to subtract power-counting-violating contributions.

## Key numbers

* Reduced chi-squared \(\chi^2_\text{red} = 1.22\) for the relativistic ChPT fit to MAMI data up to \(E_\gamma^\text{lab} = 165.8\) MeV.
* Reduced chi-squared \(\chi^2_\text{red} = 1.11\) for the heavy-baryon ChPT fit to the same data range.
* LEC \(\tilde{d}_9 := d_8 + d_9 = -2.31 \pm 0.02\) in units of \(\text{GeV}^{-2}\).
* LEC \(\tilde{e}_{48} := e_{48} + e_{67} = -3.0 \pm 0.2\) in units of \(\text{GeV}^{-3}\).
* LEC \(\tilde{e}_{50} := e_{50} + e_{69} = -1.2 \pm 2.1\) in units of \(\text{GeV}^{-3}\).
* LEC \(\tilde{e}_{51} := e_{51} + e_{71} = 2.3 \pm 1.1\) in units of \(\text{GeV}^{-3}\).
* LEC \(\tilde{e}_{112} := e_{112} + e_{113} = -4.4 \pm 2.1\) in units of \(\text{GeV}^{-3}\).
* The \(\beta\) parameter of the unitarity cusp is given as \(\beta = (3.43 \pm 0.08) \times 10^{-3} / M_{\pi^+}\), where \(M_{\pi^+}\) is the charged pion mass.

## Context

This work extends the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of Bernard et al. (1994, 1995, 2001) for neutral pion photoproduction by using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant framework, which includes an infinite number of higher-order corrections that can be important for analytic behavior. The calculation enables improved extraction of low-energy constants and multipoles from the precise MAMI data of Hornidge et al. (2012), and provides predictions for polarization observables that can be tested in future experiments. The results also allow a comparison with the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) model of Kamalov et al. (2000) and a refined determination of the \(\beta\) parameter related to isospin symmetry breaking in the unitarity cusp.
