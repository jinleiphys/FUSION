# Momentum Broadening of a Fast Parton in a Perturbative Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The average transverse momentum transfer per unit path length to a fast parton scattering elastically in a perturbative quark-gluon plasma is related to the radiative energy loss of the parton. We first calculate the momentum transfer coefficient $\hat q$ in terms of a classical Langevin problem and then define it quantum-mechanically through scattering matrix element. After treating the well known case of a quark-gluon plasma in equilibrium we consider an off-equilibrium unstable plasma. As a specific example, we treat the two-stream plasma with unstable modes of longitudinal chromoelectric field. In the presence of the instabilities, $\hat q$ is shown to exponentially grow in time.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the momentum broadening coefficient $\hat q$ in an unstable two-stream quark-gluon plasma grows exponentially in time due to the exponential growth of unstable chromoelectric field modes, a time-dependent effect that is missing in previous steady-state calculations of anisotropic plasmas.

## Method

The authors first derive $\hat q$ for a fast parton in a perturbative quark-gluon plasma using a classical Langevin approach, expressing the transverse momentum broadening through the correlation function of chromodynamic fields. They then compute $\hat q$ quantum-mechanically via the unordered gluon propagator in the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation, recovering the classical result in the limit $n(\omega) \approx T/\omega$. For the unstable case, they apply the Langevin approach to a two-stream plasma with distribution function $f({\bf p}) = (2\pi)^3 n [\delta^{(3)}({\bf p} - {\bf p}_0) + \delta^{(3)}({\bf p} + {\bf p}_0)]$, considering only the contribution from unstable longitudinal chromoelectric field modes.

## Key numbers

- The values of $\hat q$ extracted from experimental data on relativistic heavy-ion collisions vary in the range $0.5-15\;{\rm GeV^2/fm}$ depending on the model.
- The Debye mass for equilibrium plasma is $m_D^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{6} (N_f + 2N_c)$.
- The soft collision rate for equilibrium plasma is approximated as $\frac{d\Gamma_{\rm soft}}{d^2q_T} \approx g^2 \frac{C_R}{(2\pi)^2} \frac{T m_D^2}{q_T^2 (q_T^2+ m_D^2)}$.
- The hard contribution to the collision rate is $\frac{d\Gamma_{\rm hard}}{d^2q_T} \approx g^4 \frac{C_R}{(2\pi)^2} \frac{\rho}{q_T^4}$.
- The instability growth rate $\gamma_{\bf k}$ is defined via $\omega_-({\bf k}) = i \gamma_{\bf k}$ with $\mu^2 \equiv g^2 n / 2 E_{{\bf p}_0}$.
- The unstable mode domain is defined by ${\bf k}^2 ({\bf k} \cdot {\bf u})^2 < 2 \mu^2 ({\bf k}^2 - ({\bf k} \cdot {\bf u})^2)$.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of $\hat q$ for equilibrium plasmas (Arnold 2008, Peigne 2008) and for anisotropic but stationary plasmas (Romatschke 2006, Baier 2008) by explicitly accounting for the time-dependent growth of unstable modes. The authors note that numerical simulations (Dumitru 2007, Schenke 2008) had already indicated sizable contributions from unstable modes to $\hat q$. The formalism developed here, using field strength correlators from Mrowczynski (2008), can be applied to calculate momentum broadening in arbitrary media where such correlators are calculable, and the results suggest that phenomenological consequences for hard parton propagation in heavy-ion collisions (Dumitru 2007, Majumder 2006) may need to incorporate time-dependent instability growth.
