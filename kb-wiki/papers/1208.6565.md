# Off-equilibrium photon production during the chiral phase transition

> In the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions chiral symmetry is restored temporarily. During this so-called chiral phase transition, the quark masses change from their constituent to their bare values. This mass shift leads to the spontaneous non-perturbative creation of quark-antiquark pairs, which effectively contributes to the formation of the quark-gluon plasma. We investigate the photon production induced by this creation process. We provide an approach that eliminates possible unphysical contributions from the vacuum polarization and renders the resulting photon spectra integrable inthe ultraviolet domain. The off-equilibrium photon numbers are of quadratic order in the perturbative coupling constants while a thermal production is only of quartic order. Quantitatively, we find, however, that for the most physical mass-shift scenarios and for photon momenta larger than 1 GeV the off-equilibrium processes contribute less photons than the thermal processes.

## Key claim

The authors show that off-equilibrium photon production from quark-antiquark pair creation during the chiral phase transition yields photon numbers of quadratic order in the coupling constants (compared to quartic order for thermal production), but for photon momenta above 1 GeV and under the most physical mass-shift scenarios, the off-equilibrium contribution is quantitatively smaller than the thermal contribution.

## Method

The authors model the chiral phase transition by introducing a time-dependent scalar background field that couples to fermions via a Yukawa interaction, giving quarks a time-dependent mass that changes from constituent to bare values. They construct an interaction picture that incorporates this Yukawa coupling into the unperturbed Hamiltonian, then compute the photon yield from first-order QED processes (maintaining all orders in the Yukawa coupling) using an in/out formalism where photon numbers are extracted in the limit t → ∞ with an adiabatic switching of the electromagnetic interaction, which eliminates unphysical vacuum polarization contributions and renders the photon spectra ultraviolet-integrable.

## Key numbers

- For photon momenta larger than 1 GeV, off-equilibrium processes contribute less photons than thermal processes for the most physical mass-shift scenarios.
- The off-equilibrium photon numbers are of quadratic order in the perturbative coupling constants, while thermal production is of quartic order.
- The lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions is expected to be of order 5-10 fm/c.
- Photon spectra from earlier approaches (Boyanovski et al.) flattened into a power law decay for photon energies ω_k > 1.5 GeV and dominated over higher-order equilibrium contributions in that domain.
- In earlier work (Wang 2000, Wang 2001), photon spectra decayed as 1/ω_k^3 in the ultraviolet domain, causing logarithmic and linear divergences in total number and energy densities respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Boyanovski et al. (2000, 2001) and Boyanovsky (2003) on photon production from a finite-lifetime quark-gluon plasma, which suffered from unphysical vacuum polarization divergences and non-integrable ultraviolet behavior. The authors address criticisms raised by Fraga et al. (2003, 2004) regarding the consistency of the finite-time approach and the need for proper renormalization, providing a gauge-invariant framework that respects Ward-Takahashi identities. The results enable future studies of photon signatures as probes of both the thermalization time of the quark-gluon plasma and the nature of the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions.
