# Low-Energy $Λ-\p$ Scattering Parameters from the $pp \to pK^+Λ$ Reaction

> Constraints on the spin-averaged $Λp$ scattering length and effective range have been obtained from measurements of the $pp\to pK^+Λ$ reaction close to the production threshold by comparing model phase-space Dalitz plot occupations with experimental ones. The data fix well the position of the virtual bound state in the $Λp$ system. Combining this with information from elastic $Λp$ scattering measurements at slightly higher energies, together with the fact that the hyperdeuteron is not bound, leads to a new determination of the low energy $Λp$ scattering parameters.

## Key claim

The spin-averaged $\Lambda p$ scattering length and effective range are strongly correlated and cannot be determined separately from the $pp\to pK^+\Lambda$ Dalitz plot data alone; however, the position of the virtual bound state pole in the $\Lambda p$ system is well fixed, with the system being unbound by $(7.7^{+6.0}_{-3.0})$~MeV.

## Method

The $pp\to pK^+\Lambda$ reaction was measured at the COSY-11 magnetic spectrometer at COSY-Jülich at six excess energies from 2.7 to 6.7~MeV. The shape of the Dalitz plot at each energy was compared to a model incorporating a Watson-type final-state interaction factor for the $\Lambda p$ system and a Coulomb correction for the $pK^+$ subsystem, using a maximum likelihood method to map confidence contours in the spin-averaged scattering length $\bar{a}$ and effective range $\bar{r}$. The relative normalisation of the cross section as a function of beam energy was not used in the fit to avoid circularity with earlier energy calibration.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda p$ virtual bound state pole position: $\gamma_1 = (-0.45^{+0.15}_{-0.1})$~fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to the average $\Lambda p$ system being unbound by $(7.7^{+6.0}_{-3.0})$~MeV.
- Combined fit with elastic scattering data yields $\bar{a} = -2.0$~fm and $\bar{r} = 1.0$~fm, with $\gamma_1 = -0.41$~fm$^{-1}$ and $\gamma_2 = 2.4$~fm$^{-1}$.
- The data sample consisted of about 2400 events measured at six excess energies from 2.7 to 6.7~MeV.
- The missing mass resolution for $\Lambda$ identification was $\sigma = 0.5$~MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier bubble chamber measurements of $\Lambda p$ elastic scattering (Alexander et al., 1968; Sechi-Zorn et al., 1968; Alexander et al., 1969) and $K^-d\to \pi^-p\Lambda$ studies (Tan, 1970) by reaching lower $\Lambda p$ centre-of-mass momenta inaccessible to those experiments. The results are consistent with phenomenological potentials from the Jülich group (Holzenkamp et al., 1989) and Nijmegen group (Nijmegen, 1990s), but provide tighter constraints on the virtual state pole. The analysis enables future experiments with polarised beam and target to separate the spin-singlet and spin-triplet $\Lambda p$ scattering parameters.
