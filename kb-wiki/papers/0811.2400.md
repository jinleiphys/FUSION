# Solitonic ground states in (color-) superconductivity

> We present a general framework for analyzing inhomogeneous (color-) superconducting phases in mean-field approximation without restriction to the Ginzburg-Landau approach. As a first application, we calculate real gap functions with general one-dimensional periodic structures for a 3+1-dimensional toy model having two fermion species. The resulting solutions are energetically favored against homogeneous superconducting (BCS) and normal conducting phases in a window for the chemical potential difference which is about twice as wide as for the most simple plane-wave ansatz ("Fulde-Ferrell phase"). At the lower end of this window, we observe the formation of a soliton lattice and a continuous phase transition to the BCS phase. At the higher end of the window the gap functions are sinusoidal, and the transition to the normal conducting phase is of first order. We also discuss the quasiparticle excitation spectrum in the inhomogeneous phase. Finally, we compare the gap functions with the known analytical solutions of the 1+1-dimensional theory.

## Key claim

The authors present a general mean-field framework for analyzing inhomogeneous (color-)superconducting phases that is not restricted to the Ginzburg-Landau approximation, and demonstrate that for a 3+1-dimensional toy model with two fermion species, real gap functions with general one-dimensional periodic structures are energetically favored over both homogeneous BCS and normal conducting phases in a window for the chemical potential difference that is about twice as wide as for the simple plane-wave (Fulde-Ferrell) ansatz.

## Method

The authors solve the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations numerically in a momentum-space basis by exploiting Bloch's theorem to block-diagonalize the effective Hamiltonian according to crystal momentum in the Brillouin zone. They employ a Pauli-Villars-like regularization scheme via proper-time representation to consistently regularize the thermodynamic potential for both homogeneous and inhomogeneous phases, and they simplify the Dirac structure using a high-density approximation that neglects non-collinear scattering and antiparticle contributions. The gap functions are determined self-consistently by minimizing the thermodynamic potential for general one-dimensional periodic structures without restricting to a finite number of plane-wave modes.

## Key numbers

* The window for the chemical potential difference δμ where inhomogeneous phases are favored over homogeneous BCS and normal phases is about twice as wide as for the Fulde-Ferrell plane-wave ansatz (no explicit numerical width given in the truncated text).
* At the lower end of this window, a soliton lattice forms with a continuous phase transition to the BCS phase; at the higher end, the gap functions become sinusoidal with a first-order transition to the normal conducting phase.
* The regularization uses a blocking function f(τ) = 1 - 2e^{-τΛ²} + e^{-2τΛ²} with regulator scale Λ, and the regularized quasiparticle energies take the form E_{λ,j}(k) = √(E_λ²(k) + jΛ²) for j = 0,1,2 with coefficients c₀ = c₂ = 1, c₁ = -2.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of crystalline color-superconducting phases that were restricted to the Ginzburg-Landau approximation, such as those by Bowers (2002) and Casalbuoni (2005), where the Ginzburg-Landau functional for the energetically favored two-flavor solution was found to be unbounded from below. The authors overcome this limitation by solving the full mean-field problem, enabling the study of arbitrary superpositions of different wavelengths rather than only finite sets of plane waves with equal-length wave vectors. This framework enables future systematic studies of the QCD phase diagram at high densities, particularly for neutron star interiors where imbalanced Fermi surfaces arise from electric neutrality and strange quark mass effects, as discussed in the reviews by Rajagopal (2000), Alford (2001), and Buballa (2003).
