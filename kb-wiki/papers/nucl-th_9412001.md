# Low-energy theorem for a composite particle in mean scalar and vector fields

> For a relativistic particle moving in the presence of mean scalar and vector fields, the energy at second order in the scalar field is shown to contain two contributions in general. One is a momentum-dependent repulsive interaction satisfying a low-energy theorem pointed out by Wallace, Gross and Tjon. The other does not vanish at zero-momentum and involves a ``polarisability" of the particle by the scalar field. The first of these contributions is independent of the details of the structure of the particle and the couplings of its constituents to the external fields. The appearance of such a piece in the central nucleon-nucleus potential thus would support the existence of strong scalar fields in nuclei, without requiring the use of a Dirac equation for the nucleon.

# Low-energy theorem for a composite particle in mean scalar and vector fields

## Key claim

For a relativistic composite particle moving in mean scalar and vector fields, the second-order scalar-field contribution to the energy contains a universal, momentum-dependent repulsive term that is independent of the particle's internal structure and its constituent couplings, alongside a structure-dependent "polarisability" term that does not vanish at zero momentum. The universal term provides a signature of strong scalar fields in nuclei that does not require assuming a Dirac equation for the nucleon.

## Method

The author derives the general dispersion relation for a composite particle in uniform scalar and vector fields using covariance arguments, expanding the energy to second order in both fields while keeping terms to all orders in momentum. The analysis separates the universal second-order scalar interaction, identified by Wallace, Gross and Tjon (1994), from structure-dependent polarisability contributions by examining the expansion of the nucleon mass and vector coupling in powers of the scalar field.

## Key numbers

- The universal second-order scalar interaction is given by V = (p^2 / (2 epsilon(p)^3)) g_s^2 sigma^2, where epsilon(p) is the free nucleon energy, g_s is the scalar coupling, and sigma is the scalar field strength.
- The scalar field expansion of the nucleon mass is M_tilde(sigma) = M_0 + g_s sigma + (1/2) alpha_s sigma^2 + ..., where M_0 is the vacuum nucleon mass and alpha_s is the scalar polarisability.
- The vector coupling expansion is G_v(sigma) = g_v + alpha_{sv} sigma + ..., where g_v is the vacuum vector coupling and alpha_{sv} is the mixed scalar-vector polarisability.
- The rest energy of the nucleon in the vector field is E* = M_tilde + G_v omega + (alpha_v + alpha_p M_tilde^2)/(2 M_tilde) omega^2, where omega is the vector field strength and alpha_v, alpha_p are vector polarisabilities.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorem of Wallace, Gross and Tjon (1994), who demonstrated the second-order scalar interaction in a specific model with zero-range forces and scalar coupling to the scale anomaly. The paper shows the result is universal and independent of nucleon structure, unlike other medium effects such as axial charge enhancement (Delorme and Towner, 1987; Kirchbach, Riska and Tsushima, 1992) and magnetic moments, which depend on nucleon polarisabilities. The result enables interpretation of momentum-dependent repulsion in the nucleon-nucleus optical potential as evidence for strong scalar fields, though Kleinmann et al. (1994) note that Fock exchange terms can produce similar momentum dependence at low energies.
