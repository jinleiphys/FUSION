# Study of the $η$-nucleus interaction in the $p d \to ^3He η$ reaction near threshold

> A detailed study of the effect of the $η$-nucleus final state interaction (FSI) in the $p d \to ^3$He$ η$ reaction close to threshold is presented. The FSI is incorporated through a T-matrix for $η^3$He elastic scattering, constructed using few body equations. This T-matrix accounts for off-shell and binding effects in $η$-nucleus scattering. The energy dependence of the data on the $p d \to ^3$He$ η$ reaction near threshold is reproduced only after including the FSI. The off-shell and binding effects in $η^3$He scattering are found to be important. Given the uncertainty in the knowledge of the elementary $η$-nucleon interaction, the sensitivity of the $ p d \to ^3$He$ η$ cross section to different prescriptions of the elementary t-matrix, $t_{ηN \to ηN}$ is also discussed.

## Key claim

The rapid energy dependence of the $p d \to ^3$He$\, \eta$ cross section near threshold is reproduced only after including the $\eta$-$^3$He final state interaction (FSI) through an off-shell T-matrix; the off-shell and binding effects in $\eta$-$^3$He scattering are found to be important, and the result is sensitive to the choice of the elementary $\eta N$ t-matrix.

## Method

The authors model the $p d \to ^3$He$\, \eta$ reaction using a two-step mechanism via $NN \to \pi d$ and $\pi N \to \eta N$, with the $\eta$-$^3$He final state interaction incorporated through a T-matrix for $\eta$-$^3$He elastic scattering. This T-matrix is evaluated using few-body equations within a Finite Rank Approximation, which accounts for off-shell and nuclear binding effects. Three different prescriptions of the elementary $\eta N$ t-matrix (Fix et al. (I), Fix et al. (II), and Bhalerao et al.) are used to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The squared amplitude $|f|^2$ for $p d \to ^3$He$\, \eta$ falls by a factor of about 3.75 from threshold to an $\eta$ centre-of-mass momentum of 75 MeV/c (corresponding to 11 MeV above threshold).
- The momentum transfer in the reaction is 900 MeV/c at threshold, reducing to 500 MeV/c by about 1 GeV above threshold.
- The $\eta N$ scattering lengths used are: $a_{\eta N} = (0.75, 0.27)$ fm for Fix et al. (I); $a_{\eta N} = (0.88, 0.41)$ fm for Fix et al. (II); and $a_{\eta N} = (0.28, 0.19)$ fm for Bhalerao et al.
- The $^3$He binding energy used in the FSI calculation is $\varepsilon = -7.718$ MeV.
- Including binding effects reduces the squared amplitude by nearly a factor of 2 for Fix et al. (I), a factor of 1.25 for Fix et al. (II), and negligibly for Bhalerao et al.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the $p d \to ^3$He$\, \eta$ reaction by Wilkin (1993) and Wycech (1993), which incorporated the FSI only approximately through on-shell enhancement factors or scattering lengths. The present calculation uses a more rigorous off-shell T-matrix for $\eta$-$^3$He scattering, finding that the off-shell and binding effects are essential to reproduce the energy dependence of the data. The results enable more reliable extraction of the $\eta N$ scattering length from production data and inform searches for $\eta$-mesic nuclei.
