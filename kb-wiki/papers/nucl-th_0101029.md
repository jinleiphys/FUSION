# On the isospin dependence of the mean spin-orbit field in nuclei

> By the use of the latest experimental data on the spectra of $^{133}$Sb and $^{131}$Sn and on the analysis of properties of other odd nuclei adjacent to doubly magic closed shells the isospin dependence of a mean spin-orbit potential is defined. Such a dependence received the explanation in the framework of different theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The neutron spin-orbit splitting is larger than the proton spin-orbit splitting in nuclei with neutron excess (N > Z), but this difference saturates at a modest value; the isospin dependence cannot introduce major structural changes even in extreme cases of neutron excess, contrary to earlier speculation that it might be a significant factor.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental single-particle energies from nuclei adjacent to doubly magic cores (²⁰⁸Pb, ¹³²Sn, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and extrapolated data for ¹⁰⁰Sn) and extracted spin-orbit splittings from the energy differences between spin-orbit partner states. They performed least-squares fits of a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential with a spin-orbit term containing an isospin-dependent parameter β_ℓs, and also carried out Hartree-Fock calculations using the SIII Skyrme interaction to compare microscopic predictions with the empirical results.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit splitting of the 2d orbit in ¹³²Sn: 1.48 MeV for protons, 1.65 MeV for neutrons (neutron splitting larger by ~10%).
- Spin-orbit splitting of the 2f orbit near ²⁰⁸Pb: 1.93 MeV for protons; after accounting for fragmentation, 2.13 MeV for neutrons (neutron splitting larger).
- Spin-orbit splitting of the 3p orbit near ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.85 MeV for protons, 0.90 MeV for neutrons (neutron splitting larger).
- Effective β_ℓs values extracted from data: −0.55 (2d in ¹³²Sn), −0.60 (2f in ²⁰⁸Pb), −0.27 (3p in ²⁰⁸Pb).
- Theoretical β_ℓs from Dirac phenomenology with Bonn coupling constants: −0.40; with NL2 parameter set: −0.43; with NL1 parameter set: approximately −1.3.
- The difference between neutron and proton splittings in the ¹³²Sn region is about 25% of the saturation value.

## Context

This work extends the systematics of spin-orbit splittings from the well-studied ²⁰⁸Pb region to the neutron-rich ¹³²Sn region using new experimental data on ¹³³Sb and ¹³¹Sn from the authors' earlier work (Isakov et al., 2000). The finding that neutron splittings exceed proton splittings in neutron-rich nuclei confirms a qualitative prediction by earlier authors (ref. 32 in the paper) and is consistent with the isospin dependence derived from the two-body spin-orbit interaction, where the np interaction is half as strong as the nn or pp interaction. The results enable improved parameterizations of the mean-field spin-orbit potential for use in nuclear structure calculations of exotic neutron-rich nuclei, and the saturation of the isospin dependence limits the expected structural changes in nuclei far from stability.
