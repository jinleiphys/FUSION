# Bulk Viscosity of Hot Quark Plasma from Non-Equilibrium Statistical Operator

> We provide a discussion of the bulk viscosity of two-flavor quark plasma, described by the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, within the framework of Kubo-Zubarev formalism. This discussion, which is complementary to our earlier study, contains a new, detailed derivation of the bulk viscosity in the case of multiple conserved charges. We also provide some numerical details of the computation of the bulk viscosity close to the Mott transition line, where the dissipation is dominated by decays of mesons into quarks and their inverse processes. We close with a summary of our current understanding of this quantity, which stresses the importance of loop resummation for obtaining the qualitatively correct result near the Mott line.

## Key claim
The paper provides a detailed derivation of the bulk viscosity of two-flavor quark plasma within the Kubo-Zubarev formalism, emphasizing that loop resummation is essential for obtaining qualitatively correct results near the Mott transition line, where dissipation is dominated by meson decay into quarks and inverse processes.

## Method
The authors employ the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to describe the quark plasma and apply the Kubo-Zubarev non-equilibrium statistical operator formalism to compute the bulk viscosity. They present a new derivation that generalizes the formalism to the case of multiple conserved charges, and perform numerical computations of the bulk viscosity specifically near the Mott transition line.

## Key numbers
- The bulk viscosity is computed for two-flavor quark plasma described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, with numerical results provided close to the Mott transition line where dissipation is dominated by meson decays into quarks and their inverse processes.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Harutyunyan and Sedrakian, previous work) by providing a more detailed derivation of the bulk viscosity in the presence of multiple conserved charges. The results highlight the necessity of loop resummation for correct qualitative behavior near the Mott line, which is relevant for understanding transport properties of hot quark matter in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers. The paper closes with a summary of the current understanding of this quantity, identifying loop resummation as a key open issue for future work.
