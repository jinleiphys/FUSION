# Extraction of the sound velocity from rapidity spectra: Evidence for QGP formation at FAIR/RHIC-BES energies

> We analyze longitudinal pion spectra from \sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2 GeV to \sqrt {s_{\rm NN}}=20 GeV within Landau's hydrodynamical model and the UrQMD hybrid approach. From the measured data on the widths of the pion rapidity spectra, we extract the sound velocity c_s^2 in the dense stage of the reactions. It is found that the sound velocity has a local minimum (indicating a softest point in the equation of state, EoS) at \sqrt{s_{NN}}= 4-9 GeV, an energy range accessible at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) as well as the RHIC-Beam Energy Scan (RHIC-BES). This softening of the EoS is compatible with the formation of a QGP at the onset of deconfinement. The extracted sound velocities are then used to calculate an excitation function for the mean transverse mass of pions from the hybrid model. We find that, above \sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10 GeV, even the lowest c_s^{2} gives a considerably larger <m_T> of pions compared to data.

## Key claim

The sound velocity extracted from pion rapidity spectra shows a local minimum (softest point) in the equation of state at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4-9\) GeV, which is compatible with QGP formation at the onset of deconfinement; however, the exact energy of the minimum depends on the hydrodynamic framework used, and an increased resonance contribution cannot be ruled out as the cause of the softening.

## Method

The authors analyzed longitudinal pion spectra from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2\) GeV to 20 GeV using Landau's hydrodynamical model and the UrQMD hybrid approach. From the measured widths of the pion rapidity distributions, they extracted the sound velocity \(c_s^2\) by analytically inverting Landau's formula and by comparing hybrid model results with different fixed equations of state to experimental data. The extracted sound velocities were then used to calculate the excitation function for the mean transverse mass of pions from the hybrid model.

## Key numbers

- The sound velocity has a local minimum at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4-9\) GeV, indicating a softest point in the equation of state.
- For Landau's model, the softest point is around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6-9\) GeV; for the hybrid model, it is more pronounced and at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4\) GeV.
- The softest point is located in the energy range of \(E_{\rm lab} = 5A-30A\) GeV.
- Above \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10\) GeV, even the lowest \(c_s^2\) gives a considerably larger \(\langle m_T \rangle\) of pions compared to data.
- Statistical errors on the extracted sound velocities are smaller than 3%.

## Context

This work extends the search for the onset of deconfinement at low SPS energies, following observations of non-monotonous structures in the K\(^+/\pi^+\) ratio, transverse momentum excitation functions, and net-proton fluctuation signals (Gazdzicki 2004, Kumar 2011, Luo 2011). The results are compatible with predictions of a mixed phase at the onset of QGP formation (Bleicher 2000, Shuryak 2000, Gorenstein 2003) and with transport simulations suggesting partonic degrees of freedom at \(\sim 7\) GeV (Weber 1998, Bratkovskaya 2004). The findings enable further experimental exploration of this energy domain at FAIR, CERN-SPS, and BNL-RHIC.
