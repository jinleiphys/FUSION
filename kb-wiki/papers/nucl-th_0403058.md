# A microscopic study of the proton-neutron symmetry and phonon structure of the low-lying states in 92Zr

> We studied in a microscopic multiphonon approach the proton-neutron symmetry and phonon structure of some low-lying states recently discovered in 92Zr. We confirm the breaking of F-spin symmetry, but argue that the breaking mechanism is more complex than the one suggested in the original shell model analysis of the data. We found other new intriguing features of the spectrum, like a pronounced multiphonon fragmentation of the states and a tentative evidence of a three-phonon mixed symmetry state.

# A microscopic study of the proton-neutron symmetry and phonon structure of the low-lying states in 92Zr

## Key claim

The paper confirms F-spin symmetry breaking in 92Zr but argues that the breaking mechanism is more complex than suggested by the original shell model analysis, involving a competition between a dominant neutron configuration with small quadrupole matrix elements and a large number of small-amplitude components that act coherently. The authors find tentative evidence for a three-phonon mixed symmetry state, but note this requires confirmation by more complete calculations.

## Method

The authors employed the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM), which uses a multiphonon basis whose phonons are generated in random-phase approximation (RPA), to study the low-lying states of 92Zr. They used a Woods-Saxon potential with a separable two-body Hamiltonian, fitting the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength on the energy and E2 decay of the 2+1 state and the quadrupole pairing constant on the properties of the low-lying isovector 2+ state. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a space spanned by RPA multiphonon states including up to three-phonon configurations.

## Key numbers

- The first 2+1 state is calculated at 1017 keV (experimental: 934 keV) with 92.6% [2+1]RPA one-phonon component; its B(E2) to ground state is 6.5 W.u. (experimental: 6.4(6) W.u.)
- The second 2+2 state is calculated at 1945 keV (experimental: 1847 keV) with 90% [2+2]RPA one-phonon component; its B(E2) to ground state is 2.1 W.u. (experimental: 3.7(8) W.u.)
- The gyromagnetic factor for 2+1 is g = -0.20 (experimental: g_exp = -0.18(1)); for 2+2 the QPM gives g = -0.31 (shell model gives g_SM = 0.9)
- B(M1; 2+2 → 2+1) = 0.68 μN^2 (experimental: 0.46(15) μN^2)
- B(M1; 1+1 → g.s.) = 0.069 μN^2 (experimental: 0.094(4) μN^2)
- For the candidate three-phonon state at 3.263 MeV: B(M1; 2+3.263 → 2+1) = 0.06 μN^2 (computed for a pure three-phonon state; experimental: 0.16(2) μN^2)
- The ratio R_E1 = B(E1;3-1 → 2+2)/B(E1;3-1 → 2+1) is calculated as 4.4 (experimental: 2.7(2))
- Effective charges used: e_p = 1.1, e_n = 0.1; spin-gyromagnetic quenching factor g_s = 0.7

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QPM studies of 94Mo (Lo Iudice and Stoyanov, 2000, 2002) to 92Zr, which is at the border of the F-spin symmetry domain. It directly contradicts the truncated shell model analysis of Werner et al. (2002) and Lise (2000), which used a space of only two neutrons and two protons external to inert cores N_c=50 and Z_c=38, finding that the 2+1 is a pure neutron excitation and the 2+2 is purely isovector. The QPM results show that the severely truncated shell model space excludes many configurations that change the nature of the states. The study enables future experimental tests, particularly measurements of the g-factor of the 2+2 state and observation of additional 4+ states and their E2 decays, which would discriminate between the QPM and shell model descriptions.
