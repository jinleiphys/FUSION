# Nuclear fission reaction simulations in compact stars

> Type-Ia supernovae (SN Ia) are powerful stellar explosions that provide important distance indicators in cosmology. Recently, we proposed a new SN Ia mechanism that involves a nuclear fission chain-reaction in an isolated white dwarf [PRL 126, 1311010]. Here we perform novel reaction network simulations of the actinide-rich first solids in a cooling white dwarf. The network includes neutron-capture and fission reactions on a range of U and Th isotopes with various possible values for U-235 enrichment. We find, for modest U-235 enrichments, neutron-capture on U-238 and Th-232 can breed additional fissile nuclei so that a significant fraction of all U and Th nuclei may fission during the chain-reaction. The resulting large energy release could ignite thermonuclear carbon burning and possibly trigger a SN Ia.

## Key claim
For modest uranium-235 enrichments (fraction of all uranium that is U-235), neutron-capture on U-238 and Th-232 can breed additional fissile nuclei during a fission chain-reaction in a cooling white dwarf, so that a significant fraction of all uranium and thorium nuclei may fission; the resulting energy release could ignite thermonuclear carbon burning and possibly trigger a Type-Ia supernova, but the authors note that hydrodynamical simulations are needed to explicitly verify this.

## Method
The authors performed nuclear reaction network simulations of fission chain-reactions in actinide-rich first solids forming in a cooling white dwarf, using 1 MeV neutron cross sections from the ENDF 2011 data set. The network included neutron-capture and neutron-induced fission reactions on 3 thorium isotopes (Th-232, Th-233, Th-234) and 8 uranium isotopes (U-235 through U-242), plus neutrons and fission fragments, assuming mono-energetic 1 MeV neutrons and no moderation by light nuclei. They computed abundance evolution, heating rates, and final temperatures for various values of uranium enrichment (f_5), U-238 fission cross section, and fission fragment neutron-capture cross section.

## Key numbers
- Initial uranium abundance per baryon: 1.3 × 10^{-3}; initial thorium abundance: 9.51 × 10^{-4}; initial lead abundance: 2.28 × 10^{-3}
- Baryon number density: 6.02 × 10^{31} cm^{-3}
- For Case A (f_5 = 0.14, unmodified U-238 fission cross section 0.014 b): 9.6% of uranium fissions, 0.4% of thorium fissions, total heating S = 0.026 MeV per baryon, final temperature T_f = 1.6 × 10^9 K
- For Case B (f_5 = 0.14, U-238 fission cross section 0.04 b): 95% of uranium fissions, 58% of thorium fissions, S = 0.358 MeV per baryon, T_f = 5.9 × 10^9 K
- For Case C (f_5 = 0.20, U-238 fission cross section 0.014 b): 94% of uranium fissions, 58% of thorium fissions, S = 0.356 MeV per baryon, T_f = 5.9 × 10^9 K
- For Case D (f_5 = 0.14, U-238 fission cross section 0.04 b, fission fragment capture cross section 0.01 b): 89% of uranium fissions, 49% of thorium fissions, S = 0.325 MeV per baryon, T_f = 5.6 × 10^9 K
- The chain-reaction proceeds in less than 10^{-11} s in all cases
- Critical enrichment threshold for chain-reaction: approximately f_5 ≈ 0.12
- U-235 fission cross section at 1 MeV: 1.20 b; U-238 fission cross section at 1 MeV: 0.014 b; U-238 neutron-capture cross section at 1 MeV: 0.13 b
- Average neutrons emitted per fission for U-235: 2.53; for U-238: 2.69

## Context
This work extends the authors' previously proposed new Type-Ia supernova mechanism (Deibel, Caplan, and Horowitz, Physical Review Letters 126, 131101, 2021) involving a fission chain-reaction in an isolated white dwarf, by performing the first reaction network simulations for such a compact star environment. The results provide necessary input for thermal diffusion simulations of carbon ignition, which the authors present in a separate paper. The work contrasts with terrestrial nuclear reactors (which use moderated thermal neutrons) and nuclear weapons (which disassemble rapidly), noting that a white dwarf's degenerate matter allows the system to heat without rapidly expanding, enabling a breeder reaction stage where U-238 and Th-232 capture neutrons to become fissile. The final temperature threshold for carbon ignition (approximately 5 × 10^9 K) is referenced from Timmes and Woosley (1992, Astrophysical Journal 396, 649).
