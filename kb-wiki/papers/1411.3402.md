# Lattice QCD Evidence that the Lambda(1405) Resonance is an Antikaon-Nucleon Molecule

> For almost 50 years the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance has been a mystery. Even though it contains a heavy strange quark and has odd parity, its mass is lower than any other excited spin-1/2 baryon. Dalitz and co-workers speculated that it might be a molecular state of an antikaon bound to a nucleon. However, a standard quark-model structure is also admissible. Although the intervening years have seen considerable effort, there has been no convincing resolution. Here we present a new lattice QCD simulation showing that the strange magnetic form factor of the Lambda(1405) vanishes, signaling the formation of an antikaon-nucleon molecule. Together with a Hamiltonian effective-field-theory model analysis of the lattice QCD energy levels, this strongly suggests that the structure is dominated by a bound antikaon-nucleon component. This result clarifies that not all states occurring in nature can be described within a simple quark model framework and points to the existence of exotic molecular meson-nucleon bound states.

## Key claim

The strange quark contribution to the magnetic form factor of the Lambda(1405) vanishes as the up and down quark masses approach their physical values, which is naturally explained if the state becomes a molecular antikaon-nucleon bound state in that limit; combined with a Hamiltonian effective-field-theory analysis showing antikaon-nucleon dominance, this constitutes strong evidence for the molecular interpretation, though a nontrivial single-particle three-quark component remains present.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on 32^3 x 64 full-QCD ensembles from the PACS-CS collaboration, using five different light quark masses with a fixed strange quark hopping parameter. They calculated the strange quark contribution to the Sachs magnetic form factor of the Lambda(1405) at momentum transfer Q^2 = 0.16(1) GeV^2/c^2 using optimized three-quark interpolating fields that isolate the state. A finite-volume Hamiltonian model with a basis of single-particle and two-particle meson-baryon states (pi Sigma, antikaon-nucleon, K Xi, eta Lambda) was constructed and constrained to the lattice QCD energy levels to interpret the composition of the eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark magnetic form factor of the Lambda(1405) at Q^2 = 0.16(1) GeV^2/c^2 drops by an order of magnitude at the lightest pion mass (156 MeV) compared to heavier quark masses, approaching zero.
- The lightest PACS-CS ensemble provides a pion mass of 156 MeV, slightly above the physical value of 140 MeV.
- The lattice volume is (2.90 fm)^3 with a 32^3 x 64 grid.
- The Hamiltonian model bootstrap error analysis provides a resonance energy of 1.48 (+0.17 / -0.07) GeV in the infinite-volume limit, with the most probable value of 1.41 GeV in agreement with experiment.
- The physical decay width to pi Sigma used to constrain the model is 50 +/- 2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate between the molecular antikaon-nucleon hypothesis proposed by Dalitz and co-workers (1960, 1967) and the standard three-quark model description of the Lambda(1405). The lattice QCD results confirm earlier speculation that the state is dominated by a molecular antikaon-nucleon component, resolving a mystery that has persisted for nearly 50 years. The finding enables future searches for other exotic molecular meson-nucleon bound states and demonstrates that not all hadronic states can be described within a simple quark model framework.
