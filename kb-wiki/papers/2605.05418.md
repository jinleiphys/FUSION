# Axial-vector Current and General Unpolarized Electroweak Single-nucleon Responses

> The present study provides an extension to our recent work on the vector (V) electromagnetic single-nucleon current and associated response functions, both for unpolarized situations and in situations where the target nucleon is polarized. Here the axial-vector (A) single-nucleon current matrix element is developed in detail and the full set of vector and axial-vector currents used to obtain the electroweak VV, AA and VA response functions. Only the unpolarized case is studied in the present work. The general forms for all of these elements are developed together with various approximation schemes in which numerical studies are provided to indicate where these approximations may be expected to be valid. The results of this work provide the basis for a deeper understanding of the roles played by the various single-nucleon form factors in weak interaction reactions on free nucleons and when using the standard ``prescription for nuclear physics'' in reactions involving nucleons in nuclei.

## Key claim

This paper extends the authors' previous work on vector (V) electromagnetic single-nucleon currents to include the axial-vector (A) current, providing the full set of unpolarized electroweak VV, AA, and VA response functions; the development is limited to first-class currents and the unpolarized case, with numerical studies indicating where various approximation schemes (particularly expansions in the nucleon 3-momentum \(p\)) may be expected to be valid.

## Method

The authors explicitly evaluate the spinor matrix elements of the axial-vector current operator \(J^\mu_5\) for on-shell nucleons, writing it in terms of the axial form factor \(G_A\) and induced pseudoscalar form factor \(G_P\) with the assumption of pion pole dominance for \(G_P\). They then construct the full set of symmetric (VV and AA) and antisymmetric (VA) hadronic response functions in the standard charge-longitudinal-transverse decomposition, using the same kinematic variables and coordinate systems as in their prior vector-current work (Donnelly and Jeschonnek, 2024). Numerical calculations are performed using simple dipole parametrizations for the nucleon form factors (with \(M_V \approx 0.84\) GeV, \(M_A\) for the axial dipole, \(g_A = 1.267\)) to test the validity of approximations such as expansions in the dimensionless nucleon momentum \(\eta = p/m_N\) and the neglect of contributions involving the combination \(G_A' \equiv G_A - \tau G_P\).

## Key numbers

* The nucleon mass is defined as \(m_N \equiv (M_p + M_n)/2\).
* The isovector magnetic form factor at \(Q^2 = 0\) is \(G_M^v(0) = 4.71\).
* The axial coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.267\).
* The proton and neutron magnetic moments used are \(\mu_p = 2.79\) and \(\mu_n = -1.91\).
* The vector dipole mass is \(M_V \approx 0.84\) GeV.
* The mass difference correction \(\tilde{\delta} \equiv (M - M')/(M + M')\) is approximately \(\pm 7 \times 10^{-4}\) for charge-changing reactions and is set to zero in the analysis.
* Values of \(\tau\) at the quasielastic peak in neutrino-nucleus studies typically lie above \(\tau \sim 0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the vector-current formalism developed by Donnelly and Jeschonnek (2024) and by Jeschonnek and Donnelly (earlier work) to include the axial-vector current, providing the complete single-nucleon electroweak response functions needed for modeling weak interaction processes. The results enable a deeper understanding of the roles of individual nucleon form factors in charge-changing neutrino and anti-neutrino reactions on free nucleons and in the standard "prescription for nuclear physics" used for reactions on nucleons bound in nuclei; the authors note that the commonly made approximation of ignoring contributions involving \(G_A'\) is excellent for typical kinematics because \(G_A\) is considerably larger than \(G_A'\) for all \(\tau\) except very near zero.
