# Periodic systems in time: double-well potential

> Time analysis of oscillations of a particle between wells in the one-dimensional double-well potential with infinite high outside walls, based on wave packet use and energy spectrum analysis, is presented. For the double-well potential of the form $x^{2}+1/x^{2}$ in the external regions, an exact analytical solution of the energy spectrum is found (by standard QM approach), an analysis of oscillation periodicity is fulfilled, an approach for exact analitical calculation of the oscillation period is proposed (for the first time for the double-well potential).

# Periodic systems in time: double-well potential

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a symmetric double-well potential of the specific form \(x^{2}+B^{2}/x^{2}\) in the external regions, an exact analytical solution for the energy spectrum exists, and that the oscillation period of a particle between the wells can be calculated analytically from the energy spectrum, but only for this particular potential shape, and only under the condition that the system has infinite high outside walls and the energy levels form two independent equidistant series.

## Method

The author solves the stationary Schrödinger equation for a one-dimensional symmetric double-well potential with infinite high outside walls, where the external regions have the form \(x^{2}+B^{2}/x^{2}\). By transforming the equation into a Whittaker form and then into a hypergeometric equation, exact analytical expressions for the energy spectrum and wave functions are obtained. The time-dependent wave function is then constructed as a superposition of stationary states, and the periodicity of the wave function is analyzed using Fourier series methods, leading to formulas for the oscillation period that account for the ground-state energy contribution.

## Key numbers

- Energy spectrum for the double-well potential: \(E_{n}^{\pm} = 2\hbar w \left(\frac{1}{2} \pm \mu\right) + 2\hbar w n\), where \(\mu = \frac{1}{4}\sqrt{1 + \frac{4m^{2}w^{2}B^{2}}{\hbar^{2}}}\) and \(\Delta = 2\hbar w\).
- Oscillation period without ground-state contribution: \(T_{f} = \frac{2\pi\hbar}{\Delta} = \frac{\pi}{w}\).
- Oscillation period with ground-state contribution for each series: \(T^{\pm} = \frac{2\pi\hbar}{E_{0}^{\pm}+\Delta} = \frac{2\pi\hbar}{E_{1}^{\pm}}\).
- Energy splitting between corresponding levels: \(\Delta E = E_{n}^{+} - E_{n}^{-} = \hbar w \sqrt{1 + \frac{4m^{2}w^{2}}{\hbar^{2}} B^{2}}\).
- For a harmonic oscillator example, including the ground state reduces the period by a factor of 2/3: \(T_{new} = \frac{2}{3} T_{old}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of double-well potential oscillations beyond instanton methods (Coleman 1988, Zhou 2000) by providing an exactly solvable model where the energy spectrum can be computed analytically. The approach enables exact calculation of oscillation periods for this specific potential, which was previously not possible for general double-well potentials. The author suggests that supersymmetric quantum mechanics methods (Cooper 1995) could be applied to generalize this periodicity analysis to isospectral potentials, and that the concept of "quasi-cycles" from Maydanyuk (1999) could be extended to quasi-periodical systems.
