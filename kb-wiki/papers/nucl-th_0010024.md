# Contemporary applications of Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We illustrate the contemporary application of Dyson-Schwinger equations using two examples: the calculation of pseudoscalar meson masses, an associated model-independent mass formula and the approach to the heavy-quark limit; and the study of nucleon observables, including a calculation of its mass, $M$, via a covariant Fadde'ev equation and an estimate of pion-loop contributions to $M$.

## Key claim

Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation can provide a unified, model-independent description of pseudoscalar meson masses from the light-quark to the heavy-quark limit, and a covariant Fadde'ev equation with scalar and pseudovector diquark correlations can yield a nucleon mass within approximately 30-40% of the experimental value, with pion-loop corrections contributing only about 1%.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for pseudoscalar mesons using the renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, which preserves the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. For the nucleon, they employed a covariant Fadde'ev equation that treats the nucleon as a bound state of a dressed-quark and a nonpointlike diquark (scalar and pseudovector correlations), with the diquark properties determined from independent studies of the quark-quark scattering matrix. The nucleon mass was then calculated by solving the Fadde'ev equation, and pion-loop contributions to the nucleon mass were estimated using a model DSE for the nucleon self energy with a momentum-dependent pi-NN coupling.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar meson mass evolution for equal current-quark masses: interpolating formula m_H = beta sqrt(X) + gamma X, with beta = 1.04 GeV, gamma = 0.21 GeV, and X = m^zeta / Lambda_QCD, where zeta = 19 GeV and Lambda_QCD = 0.234.
- For a pseudoscalar meson with mass m_H = 1 GeV, the sqrt(X) term provides 86% of the meson's mass, corresponding to a current-quark mass hat m_f = 0.32 Lambda_QCD = 2.3 hat m_s.
- Ratio m^2_H(m_f = 2 m_s) / m^2_H(m_f = m_s) = 2.2, in agreement with lattice simulations.
- Kaon-like u-q trajectory interpolation: m_HK = 0.083 + 0.5 sqrt(X) + 0.31 X (in GeV), reproducing m_D ~ 1.9 GeV and m_B ~ 5.3 GeV with m_c^(1 GeV) ~ 1.1 GeV and m_b^(1 GeV) ~ 4.2 GeV.
- Nucleon mass from Fadde'ev equation with scalar diquark only: M = 1.48 GeV (with r = 1.11); with scalar and pseudovector diquark: M = 0.94 GeV (with r = 0.54, omega_0+ = 0.68 GeV, m_0+ = 0.54 GeV, omega_1+ = 0.40 GeV, m_1+ = 0.69 GeV).
- Pion-loop contribution to nucleon mass: +10 MeV (1% increase), with Lambda_pi = 0.96 GeV for the pi-NN coupling form factor.
- Nucleon observables from impulse approximation (r = 1): proton charge radius r_p^2 = (0.78 fm)^2, neutron charge radius r_n^2 = -(0.34 fm)^2, proton magnetic moment mu_p = 2.82 mu_N, neutron magnetic moment mu_n = -1.62 mu_N, ratio mu_n/mu_p = -0.57, pi-NN coupling g_piNN = 14.6, axial coupling g_A = 1.27.

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach to QCD beyond simple calculations of f_pi and the vacuum quark condensate, building on the model-independent analysis of the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation from Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1998) and the rainbow-ladder truncation scheme of Bender, Roberts, and von Smekal (1996). The nucleon model follows the covariant Fadde'ev equation approach demonstrated by Oettel, Hellstern, Alkofer, and Reinhardt (1998) and the extensive study by Oettel, Alkofer, and von Smekal (2000). The results enable future calculations of quark distribution functions in the nucleon and detailed Fadde'ev-equation-based studies of the Delta resonance, moving beyond the parametrisations used in deep inelastic scattering analyses.
