# Calculation of acceleration effects using the Zubarev density operator

> The relativistic form of the Zubarev density operator can be used to study quantum effects associated with acceleration of the medium. In particular, it was recently shown that the calculation of perturbative corrections in acceleration based on the Zubarev density operator makes it possible to show the existence of Unruh effect. In this paper, we present the details of the calculation of quantum correlators arising in the fourth order of the perturbation theory needed to demonstrate the Unruh effect. Expressions for the quantum corrections for massive fermions are also obtained.

## Key claim

The Zubarev density operator approach, when expanded to fourth order in acceleration, reproduces the Unruh effect for a uniformly accelerated medium observed from an inertial frame in flat Minkowski space, without explicitly invoking event horizons or curved spacetime.

## Method

The authors constructed a perturbation theory in acceleration using the Zubarev density operator for a uniformly accelerated medium with zero vorticity and chemical potential. They calculated the fourth-order corrections to the energy-momentum tensor of the massless Dirac field by evaluating five-point thermal correlators containing four boost operators and one energy-momentum tensor operator, using the finite-temperature Wick theorem and Matsubara frequency summation techniques. The calculation was performed in Euclidean imaginary-time formalism, with the boost operators expressed as spatial integrals of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- Fourth-order coefficient for the energy density component: \(A_2 = -\frac{17}{960\pi^2}\) (dimensionless, for massless Dirac field)
- Fourth-order coefficient for the pressure component: \(A_4 = -\frac{17}{2880\pi^2}\) (dimensionless, for massless Dirac field)
- Fourth-order coefficient for the anisotropic stress component: \(A_6 = 0\) (dimensionless, for massless Dirac field)
- The Unruh temperature relation used: \(T_U = |a|/(2\pi)\) (where \(a\) is acceleration, in units \(\hbar=c=k=1\))

## Context

This work extends the Zubarev density operator approach previously applied to second-order corrections in thermal vorticity by Buzzegoli et al. (2017, 2018) and Becattini et al. (2015). It provides the detailed calculation supporting the earlier claim by Prokhorov et al. (2019) that the Unruh effect can be derived from the Zubarev operator in flat spacetime. The results also connect to the work of Dowker (1994) and Frolov (1987) on cosmic strings with conical singularities, as the Zubarev operator for accelerated media is argued to produce emergent conical geometry. The calculation enables further study of quantum effects in accelerated systems and provides explicit expressions for massive fermion corrections.
