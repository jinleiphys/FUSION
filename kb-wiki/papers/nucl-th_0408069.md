# Relativistic Hartree approach with exact treatment of vacuum polarization for finite nuclei

> We study the relativistic Hartree approach with the exact treatment of the vacuum polarization in the Walecka sigma-omega model. The contribution from the vacuum polarization of nucleon-antinucleon field to the source term of the meson fields is evaluated by performing the energy integrals of the Dirac Green function along the imaginary axis. With the present method of the vacuum polarization in finite system, the total binding energies and charge radii of 16O and 40Ca can be reproduced. On the other hand, the level-splittings in the single-particle level, in particular the spin-orbit splittings, are not described nicely because the inclusion of vacuum effect provides a large effective mass with small meson fields. We also show that the derivative expansion of the effective action which has been used to calculate the vacuum contribution for finite nuclei gives a fairly good approximation.

# Relativistic Hartree Approach with Exact Treatment of Vacuum Polarization for Finite Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the exact treatment of vacuum polarization in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) for finite nuclei can reproduce total binding energies and charge radii of 16O and 40Ca, but the spin-orbit splittings are not described nicely because the inclusion of vacuum effects provides a large effective mass with small meson fields. The derivative expansion of the effective action gives a fairly good approximation to the exact calculation, but this agreement depends on the presence of the sigma meson.

## Method

The vacuum polarization contribution from nucleon-antinucleon fields to the source terms of the meson fields is evaluated by performing energy integrals of the Dirac Green function along the imaginary axis, replacing the summation over infinite negative-energy Hartree basis states. The renormalized baryon and scalar densities are computed by subtracting divergent perturbative contributions (the Uehling-type term for baryon density and corresponding terms for scalar density) from the full vacuum Green function, with the residual finite densities summed over partial-wave contributions up to |kappa_max| = 33 for baryon density and 36-48 for scalar density. The self-consistent RHA iteration solves the Dirac equation for valence nucleons, computes vacuum densities using the same potentials, solves the Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields with both valence and vacuum source terms, and repeats until convergence of the total binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energies and rms charge radii of 16O and 40Ca are reproduced with the parameter set g_sigma = 7.38, g_2 = 7.90, g_3 = 3.20, m_sigma = 458.0 MeV, g_omega = 9.18, and m_omega = 783.0 MeV
- The integral over imaginary energy is performed with upper limits around 20i to 50i GeV, with extrapolation to z_max approaching infinity
- The baryon number conservation check gives Delta B approximately 10^{-3} as a typical value for both 16O and 40Ca
- The Uehling-type contributions rho^{(1)}_{omega,ren} and rho^{(1)}_{sigma,ren} are an order of magnitude smaller than the residual densities, in contrast to QED where the Uehling term dominates

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field (RMF) approach of Walecka (1974) by including the full one-nucleon-loop vacuum polarization for finite nuclei, which had previously only been treated via local-density approximation (Horowitz and collaborators, 1984; Ichii and collaborators, 1988; Fox and collaborators, 1989) or derivative-expansion methods (Perry, 1986; Wasson and collaborators, 1988; Maksymowicz and collaborators, 1999, 2003). The method adapts techniques from QED developed by Gyulassy (1972) and Soff and collaborators (1988) for evaluating vacuum polarization via imaginary-axis Green function integrals. The results confirm that the derivative expansion provides a good approximation for the sigma-omega model, and the work enables future studies of antinucleon binding in nuclei and extensions including tensor coupling of the omega meson or finite pion mean fields to resolve the spin-orbit splitting problem.
