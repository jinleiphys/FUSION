# Doubly-Charged Scalars in the Type-II Seesaw Mechanism: Fundamental Symmetry Tests and High-Energy Searches

> We analyze the sensitivity of low-energy fundamental symmetry tests to interactions mediated by doubly-charged scalars that arise in type-II seesaw models of neutrino mass and their left-right symmetric extensions. We focus on the next generation measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry in Møller scattering planned by the MOLLER collaboration at Jefferson Laboratory. We compare the MOLLER sensitivity to that of searches for charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) and neutrinoless double beta-decay ($0νββ$-decay) as well as present and possible future high-energy collider probes. We show that for the simplest type-II seesaw scenario, CLFV searches have the greatest sensitivity. However, in a left-right symmetric extension where the scale of parity-breaking is decoupled from the $SU(2)_R$-breaking scale, the MOLLER experiment will provide a unique probe of scalar triplet interactions in the right-handed sector for a doubly-charged scalar mass up to $\sim$ 10 TeV and help elucidate the mechanism of $0νββ$-decay.

## Key claim

In the simplest type-II seesaw scenario, charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) searches already provide stronger constraints on the left-handed doubly-charged scalar than the planned MOLLER experiment can achieve; however, in a left-right symmetric extension where parity-breaking is decoupled from the SU(2)_R-breaking scale, MOLLER can uniquely probe right-handed scalar triplet interactions for doubly-charged scalar masses up to approximately 10 TeV, provided the Yukawa coupling remains perturbative.

## Method

The authors computed the contribution of doubly-charged scalars (both left-handed H_L^{++} and right-handed H_R^{++}) to the parity-violating asymmetry in Møller scattering via s-channel exchange, expressed as an effective four-fermion contact interaction. They compared the projected MOLLER sensitivity (0.7 ppb precision on the asymmetry, corresponding to a 2.4% fractional accuracy on the electron weak charge Q_W^e) against existing constraints from CLFV processes (muon to three electrons and muon to electron gamma), neutrinoless double beta decay, and direct collider searches at LEP and LHC. For the minimal type-II seesaw, the Yukawa coupling matrix f_L was fixed by neutrino oscillation data using the relation m_nu = sqrt(2) f_L v_L, with the triplet vev v_L varied between 10^{-3} eV and 10 eV and neutrino parameters scanned within their 2-sigma ranges.

## Key numbers

- MOLLER sensitivity to the ratio of doubly-charged scalar mass to electron Yukawa coupling: M_{H^{++}} / |f_ee| > 3.7 TeV at 95% CL (applies equally to left-handed and right-handed scalars).
- Projected MOLLER precision: parity-violating asymmetry measured to 0.7 ppb overall, electron weak charge Q_W^e determined to 2.4% fractional accuracy.
- Model-independent sensitivity to new four-electron contact interaction scale: Lambda / sqrt(|g_RR^2 - g_LL^2|) approximately 7.5 TeV.
- Current CLFV limits on effective cutoff scale: mu to eee gives M_{H_L^{++}} / sqrt(|(f_L)_ee^dagger (f_L)_e mu|) > 208 TeV; mu to e gamma gives M_{H_L^{++}} / sqrt(|sum_ell (f_L)_mu ell^dagger (f_L)_e ell|) > 61 TeV.
- Current LHC direct mass limits on doubly-charged scalars from dilepton searches at sqrt(s)=13 TeV: roughly 800 GeV for left-handed and 657 GeV for right-handed scalars.
- Electroweak precision constraint on triplet vev: v_L less than or approximately 5 GeV.
- Cosmological limit on sum of neutrino masses: sum m_nu,i < 0.23 eV, used to constrain lightest neutrino mass m_0 between 0 and 0.05 eV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of CLFV and neutrinoless double beta decay constraints on type-II seesaw parameter space (Cirigliano 2004, Abada 2007, Chakrabortty 2012, Awasthi 2015, Barry 2013, Bambhaniya 2015, Borah 2016, Tello 2010) by adding the MOLLER experiment as a probe. The analysis also builds on earlier studies of parity-violating Møller scattering as a probe of BSM scenarios including heavy Z' bosons (Langacker 2008, Li 2009), dark Z bosons (Davoudiasl 2012), and supersymmetric models (Kurylov 2003, Ramsey-Musolf 2006). The results enable future comparisons between low-energy precision measurements and direct searches at future lepton colliders such as CEPC, FCC-ee, ILC, or CLIC, which could surpass MOLLER sensitivity for the entire parameter space of interest in the parity-violating left-right symmetric model.
