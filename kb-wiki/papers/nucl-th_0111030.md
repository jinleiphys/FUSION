# Goldstone Boson's Valence-Quark Distribution

> Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is one of the keystones of low-energy hadronic phenomena. Dyson-Schwinger equations provide a model-independent quark-level understanding and correlate that with the behaviour of the pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. This amplitude is a core element in the calculation of pion observables and combined with the dressed-quark Schwinger function required by DCSB it yields a valence-quark distribution function for the pion that behaves as (1-x)^2 for x~1, in accordance with perturbative analyses. This behaviour can be verified at contemporary experimental facilities.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework yields a valence-quark distribution function for the pion that behaves as (1-x)^2 for x near 1, in agreement with perturbative QCD predictions, but this directly contradicts existing Drell-Yan experimental data which indicate a (1-x) behavior. The author presents this as a "very disturbing" discrepancy that would challenge QCD if the experimental result were verified.

## Method
The author uses a symmetry-preserving truncation scheme for the Dyson-Schwinger equations, employing a renormalisation group improved rainbow approximation for the gap equation. The model's single parameter, along with u/d and s current-quark masses, is tuned to fit experimental values of m_pi, f_pi, and m_K. The valence-quark distribution function is then calculated from the appropriate "handbag diagrams" using the dressed-quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude obtained from this framework.

## Key numbers
- The DSE calculation yields a valence-quark distribution with exponent eta_2 approximately 2, giving (1-x)^2 behavior at x near 1, while Drell-Yan data analysis yields (1-x) behavior
- At resolving scale q_0 = 0.54 GeV, valence quarks with mass 0.3 GeV carry 71% of the pion's momentum
- At q_0 = 2.0 GeV, calculated moments are: <x^2> = 0.098, <x^3> = 0.049; experimental values are <x^2> = 0.10 +/- 0.01, <x^3> = 0.058 +/- 0.004
- The model yields current-quark masses: m_u^(1 GeV) = 5.5 MeV, m_s^(1 GeV) = 124 MeV
- Vacuum quark condensate: -<q qbar>_(1 GeV)^0 = (0.242 GeV)^3; in-pion condensate: -<q qbar>_(1 GeV)^pi = (0.245 GeV)^3
- The interaction tension required for DCSB is sigma^Delta >= 4 GeV^2 ~ 70 Lambda_QCD^2, with a critical threshold of sigma^Delta ~ 0.5 GeV^2 ~ 9 Lambda_QCD^2

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to hadron phenomenology developed by Roberts and collaborators (1997-2001), which successfully described pion electromagnetic form factors and unified light- and heavy-meson masses. The DSE result for the pion's valence-quark distribution directly contradicts the Drell-Yan experimental data from the 1980s, and the paper proposes remeasurement via the Sullivan process at JLAB or a future electron-proton collider. The calculation also provides a quark-level understanding of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and Goldstone's theorem in QCD, connecting to the work of Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1998) on the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity.
