# Possible experimental signature of octupole correlations in the 0$^+_2$ states of the actinides

> $J^π$= 0$^+$ states have been investigated in the actinide nucleus ${}^{240}$Pu up to an excitation energy of 3 MeV with a high-resolution (p,t) experiment at $E_{p}$= 24 MeV. To test the recently proposed $J^π$= 0$^+_2$ double-octupole structure, the phenomenological approach of the spdf-interacting boson model has been chosen. In addition, the total 0$^+$ strength distribution and the $0^+$ strength fragmentation have been compared to the model predictions as well as to the previously studied (p,t) reactions in the actinides. The results suggest that the structure of the 0$^+_2$ states in the actinides might be more complex than the usually discussed pairing isomers. Instead, the octupole degree of freedom might contribute significantly. The signature of two close-lying 0$^+$ states below the 2-quasiparticle energy is presented as a possible manifestation of strong octupole correlations in the structure of the 0$^+_2$ states in the actinides.

## Key claim

The structure of the 0⁺₂ states in actinides may be more complex than the usually discussed pairing isomers, with the octupole degree of freedom contributing significantly; the signature of two close-lying 0⁺ states below the 2-quasiparticle energy is presented as a possible manifestation of strong octupole correlations, but the authors note that large relative (p,t) cross sections do not per se allow identification of double-octupole states because quadrupole structures are predicted in some cases.

## Method

A high-resolution (p,t) experiment was performed at the Q3D magnetic spectrograph of the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratory in Munich, using a 24 MeV proton beam on a highly-enriched ²⁴²Pu target (99.93%, 120 μg/cm² on carbon backing). Angular distributions were measured at nine laboratory angles from 5° to 40° and compared to CHUCK3 code calculations to assign J^π = 0⁺ states. The phenomenological spdf-interacting boson model (IBM) was used to interpret the experimental 0⁺ strength distribution and fragmentation, with the transfer operator including both the standard s-boson removal term and an additional term accounting for negative-parity bosons.

## Key numbers

- 17 states firmly assigned J^π = 0⁺ in ²⁴⁰Pu up to 3 MeV excitation energy, plus 4 tentative assignments
- 0⁺₂ state at 861.2(1) keV with integrated (p,t) cross section 33.69(3) μb, giving relative strength σ(0⁺₂)/σ(gs) = 19.39(2)%
- 0⁺₃ state at 1090.3(1) keV with integrated (p,t) cross section 13.83(2) μb, giving relative strength σ(0⁺₃)/σ(gs) = 7.96(1)%
- Ground state integrated (p,t) cross section: 173.75(7) μb
- Total relative transfer strength: 68.45(8)% (64.57(8)% for firm 0⁺ assignments)
- Energy difference ΔE between 0⁺₂ and 0⁺₃ states: ~230 keV in ²⁴⁰Pu, ~107 keV in ²²⁸Th, ~600 keV in ²³⁰Th and ²³²U
- 2-quasiparticle energy in ²⁴⁰Pu: 2Δ_n ≈ 1090 keV
- IBM predicts relative strengths of 22.17% and 13.12% for the first two excited 0⁺ states
- Experimental B(E1)/B(E2) ratio for 0⁺₂ → 1⁻₁ / 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₁: 13.7(3) × 10⁻⁶ fm⁻² (IBM: 10.4 × 10⁻⁶ fm⁻²)

## Context

This work extends previous (p,t) studies in actinides by Maher (1972), Friedman (1974), and the systematic studies by Wirth (2004), Levon (2009, 2013) in Th and U isotopes. It tests the double-octupole structure proposed for the 0⁺₂ state in ²⁴⁰Pu by Wang (2009) and Jolos (2013), which challenged the traditional pairing isomer interpretation by Ragnarsson and Broglia (1976). The spdf-IBM calculations follow the approach of Zamfir (2001, 2003) and successfully reproduce both hadronic and electromagnetic observables in ²⁴⁰Pu, supporting octupole correlations in the 0⁺₂ state. The results enable future studies of candidate nuclei such as ²²⁴,²²⁶Ra, ²³²Th, ²³⁸U, and ²³⁸Pu, and suggest that remeasurement of (p,t) and (t,p) reactions in state-of-the-art experiments could help discriminate between pairing isomers and double-octupole excitations.
