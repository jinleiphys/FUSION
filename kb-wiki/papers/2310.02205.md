# Stability of Initial Glasma Fields

> A system of gluon fields produced in the earliest phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which is called `glasma', can be described in terms of classical fields. Initially there are chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields along the collision axis. A linear stability analysis of these fields is performed, assuming that the fields are space-time uniform and using the SU(2) gauge group. We apply Milne coordinates and the gauge condition which are usually used in studies of glasma. The chromoelectric field is in the Abelian configuration with the corresponding potential linearly depending on coordinates but the chromomagnetic field is in the nonAbelian configuration generated by the potential of non-commuting components. The chromomagnetic field is found to be unstable and the growth rate of the unstable mode is derived. Our findings are critically debated and confronted with the numerical simulations by Romatschke and Venugopalan who found that the evolving glasma is unstable due to the Weibel instability well-known in electromagnetic plasma.

## Key claim

The chromomagnetic field in the initial glasma state is found to be unstable, but the instability is not the Weibel instability as previously claimed by Romatschke and Venugopalan (2005, 2006); instead, the authors argue that the initial glasma chromomagnetic field is in a nonAbelian configuration, which is physically nonequivalent to the Abelian configuration assumed in prior studies by Iwazaki (2007, 2008) and Fujii (2008, 2009).

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis of space-time uniform chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields using the SU(2) gauge group, Milne coordinates, and the Fock-Schwinger gauge condition (A^τ = 0) typically used in glasma studies. They derive the linearized Yang-Mills equations around the background fields representing the initial glasma state, then analyze the growth of small fluctuations by solving these equations in two special cases: one where the longitudinal fluctuation component a^η_a is set to zero, and another where only transverse fluctuations are considered.

## Key numbers

- The chromomagnetic field B is generated with strength parameter B (dimensionful) and the pre-collision potentials are parametrized as β_{1a}^i = δ^{ix} δ^{a3} (1/λ) √(B/g) and β_{2a}^i = δ^{iy} δ^{a2} λ √(B/g), where λ is a dimensionless parameter.
- The gauge-invariant squared current for the nonAbelian magnetic field configuration is j^μ_a j_{a μ} = -(1/λ^2 + λ^2) g B^3, showing the configurations with different (λ^{-2}+λ^2) are gauge nonequivalent.
- The growth rate of the unstable mode is derived (explicit numerical value not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of uniform chromodynamic field stability (Bazak 2021, 2022) to the specific initial glasma configuration, using Milne coordinates and the standard glasma gauge. It contradicts the interpretation by Romatschke and Venugopalan (2005, 2006) that the glasma instability is the Weibel instability, and also differs from the approach of Iwazaki (2007, 2008) and Fujii (2008, 2009) who assumed both chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields were in Abelian configurations. The results enable a more accurate understanding of the early-time dynamics of glasma in heavy-ion collisions and clarify which instability mechanism is actually at work.
