# Double beta decay in heavy deformed nuclei: what have we learned?

> The pseudo SU(3) approach is used to describe low lying states and BE(2) intensities of rare earth and actinide nuclei which are double beta decay candidates. The double beta half lives of some of these nuclei to the ground and excited states of the final ones are evaluated for both the two and zero neutrino emitting modes. The existence of selection rules which strongly restrict the decays is discussed. These restrictions represent a possible test of the model. Up to now the predictions are in good agreement with the available experimental data.

# Double beta decay in heavy deformed nuclei: what have we learned?

## Key claim
The pseudo SU(3) shell model predicts that two-neutrino double beta decay is strictly forbidden for five specific heavy deformed nuclei (154Sm, 160Gd, 176Yb, 232Th, 244Pu) due to selection rules on occupation numbers and SU(3) irreps, while for the six nuclei where decay is allowed, the predictions agree with available experimental data. The model's selection rules represent a testable condition: if these forbidden decays are observed experimentally, the pseudo SU(3) truncation would need revision through inclusion of pairing or symplectic extensions.

## Method
The pseudo SU(3) shell model was applied to describe low-lying states of rare earth and actinide double beta decay candidates, using a Hamiltonian with spherical Nilsson single-particle terms, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and a residual force for fine-tuning rotational spectra. Occupation numbers for protons and neutrons in normal and abnormal parity states were determined by filling Nilsson levels from below, and the double beta decay matrix elements were evaluated using a summation method that avoids the closure approximation, with wave functions restricted to one active shell per nucleon type and only pseudo spin zero and seniority zero configurations.

## Key numbers
- For 150Nd decaying to 150Sm, the predicted two-neutrino double beta half-life agrees with experimental data; the zero-neutrino matrix element is about a factor four smaller than QRPA estimations (Staudt 1990).
- For 238U, the predicted zero-neutrino half-life (assuming <m_nu> = 1 eV) is at least three orders of magnitude greater than the predicted two-neutrino half-life, confirming the observed decay as the two-neutrino mode.
- The two-neutrino double beta decay of 150Nd to the first excited 0+ state of 150Sm is forbidden by an exact selection rule; the decay to the second excited 0+ state has a predicted half-life four orders of magnitude larger than the ground-state decay.
- The two-neutrino matrix element for decay to the first excited 2+ state of 150Sm is three orders of magnitude smaller than the ground-state matrix element.
- For the six allowed emitters, the predicted zero-neutrino half-lives with <m_nu> = 1 eV are at least three orders of magnitude greater than current experimental lower limits.

## Context
This work extends the pseudo SU(3) calculations of Hirsch (1992) and Castanos (1993) for two-neutrino double beta decay in eleven heavy deformed nuclei, and the zero-neutrino calculations of Hirsch (1994) for six nuclei, by reviewing the formalism and presenting new results for decays to excited states of 150Sm. The predictions contradict earlier speculations by Piepke (1994) and Barabash (1990) that the decay of 150Nd to the first excited 0+ state of 150Sm could have similar intensity to the ground-state decay. The results enable experimental tests of the model through searches for the five predicted forbidden decays, particularly the ongoing search in 244Pu (Moody 1992), and provide nuclear matrix elements needed to extract neutrino mass limits from future experiments on 150Nd, which is a candidate for improving the <m_nu> sensitivity to 0.1 eV.
