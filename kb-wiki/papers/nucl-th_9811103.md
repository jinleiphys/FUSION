# Coulomb Final State Interactions for Gaussian Wave Packets

> Two-particle like-sign and unlike-sign correlations including Coulomb final state interactions are calculated for Gaussian wave packets emitted from a Gaussian source. We show that the width of the wave packets can be fully absorbed into the spatial and momentum space widths of an effective emission function for plane wave states, and that Coulomb final state interaction effects are sensitive only to the latter, but not to the wave packet width itself. Results from analytical and numerical calculations are compared with recently published work by other authors.

## Key claim

The authors show that for Gaussian source models, the wave packet width σ can be fully absorbed into the effective spatial and momentum widths of an emission function for plane wave states, and that Coulomb final state interaction effects depend only on these effective widths, not on σ itself, contradicting the claim by Merlitz and Pelte (1997) that Coulomb distortion becomes unobservable for Gaussian wave packets.

## Method

The authors start from Gaussian one-particle wave packets centered at phase-space points and expand the two-particle state in plane waves, separating center-of-mass and relative coordinates. They incorporate Coulomb final state interactions via the Møller scattering operator, which maps plane waves onto Coulomb scattering wave functions, and then average over a Gaussian source model with spatial width R and momentum width Δ. The resulting two-particle correlator for unlike-sign and like-sign pion pairs is reduced to analytical expressions that depend only on the combined parameters \(\bar{R}^2 = R^2 + \sigma^2/2\) and \(\bar{\Delta}^2 = \Delta^2 + 1/(2\sigma^2)\), and numerical integration is performed for comparison with the plane-wave limit.

## Key numbers

- For unlike-sign pion pairs with R = 3.5 fm, Δ = 84 MeV, and σ = 2.5 fm (the parameters used by Merlitz and Pelte), the Coulomb correlation remains clearly observable even for σ = 14 fm, contradicting the claim of unobservability.
- The limit \(\bar{\Delta} \to \infty\) yields the Koonin formula (1977) for the correlator, which is shown to agree almost exactly with the full Gaussian wave packet calculation for the studied parameters.
- For like-sign pion pairs, the HBT radius parameter is given by \(R_{\rm HBT}^2 = \bar{R}^2 - 1/(4\bar{\Delta}^2)\); for σ = 2.5 fm, the term \(1/(4\bar{\Delta}^2)\) contributes approximately 5% to \(R_{\rm HBT}^2\).
- The correlator for like-sign pairs is more sensitive than for unlike-sign pairs to small changes in the Gaussian width of the phase space density.

## Context

This work extends the standard plane-wave treatment of Coulomb final state interactions in two-particle correlations, as developed by Koonin (1977), Baym and Braun-Munzinger (1996), and others, by explicitly incorporating Gaussian wave packets. It directly contradicts the numerical results of Merlitz and Pelte (1997, 1998), who claimed that Coulomb distortion becomes unobservable for wave packets. The authors show that the Gaussian wave packet formalism and the conventional plane-wave calculations (using the Koonin formula) yield equivalent results when the wave packet width is consistently absorbed into the effective source size, enabling continued use of standard Coulomb correction procedures in experimental HBT analyses.
