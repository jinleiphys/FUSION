# The Functional Derivation of Master Equations

> Master equations describe the quantum dynamics of open systems interacting with an environment. They play an increasingly important role in understanding the emergence of semiclassical behavior and the generation of entropy, both being related to quantum decoherence. Presently we derive the exact master equation for a homogeneous scalar Higgs or inflaton like field coupled to an environment field represented by an infinite set of harmonic oscillators. Our aim is to demonstrate a derivation directly from the path integral representation of the density matrix propagator. Applications and generalizations of this result are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper derives an exact master equation for a homogeneous scalar field (such as a Higgs or inflaton field) coupled to an environment of harmonic oscillators, using a path integral approach that is nonperturbative and does not rely on weak-coupling approximations. The derivation is valid for arbitrary self-interactions of the scalar field, but the final master equation still contains two path integrals that must be evaluated by approximation or in special cases.

## Method
The author starts from the Feynman-Vernon influence functional formalism for a system-plus-environment model where the environment consists of an infinite set of harmonic oscillators with a given spectral density. By splitting the path integral for the density matrix propagator at an intermediate time and expanding to first order in the time step, the time derivative of the reduced density matrix is obtained directly from the path integral representation. The resulting master equation is expressed in terms of noise and dissipation kernels derived from the spectral density of the environment.

## Key numbers
- The spectral density is defined as \( I(\omega) \equiv \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n=1}^N m_n \omega_n^3 \delta(\omega - \omega_n) \), characterizing the environment oscillators.
- For an Ohmic environment (\(k=1\)) in the high-temperature limit \( T \gg \Lambda \ge \omega \), the noise kernel becomes \( \nu(s) = 2\gamma T \delta(s) \) and the dissipation kernel becomes \( \eta(s) = \gamma \delta'(s) \), where \(\gamma\) is the effective coupling constant.
- The resulting master equation for the Ohmic case contains a dissipative term proportional to \(\gamma\) and a diffusive term proportional to \(2\gamma T\).
- An initial "jolt" term appears in the Ohmic master equation: \( -4\pi\gamma \delta(t) \delta'(\Pi) \int d\Phi' \Phi' W(\Phi', 0, 0) \) in the Wigner representation.

## Context
This work extends the Caldeira-Leggett and Unruh-Zurek models of quantum Brownian motion (Grabert, 1988; Unruh and Zurek, 1989) to a homogeneous scalar field with arbitrary self-interactions, using a path integral derivation rather than canonical quantization. The method enables future studies of semiquantum chaos in systems coupled to environments (Blümel and Elze, 1996) and could be generalized to propagating fields for applications such as entropy production in heavy-ion collisions.
