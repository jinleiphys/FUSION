# New limits on intrinsic charm in the nucleon from global analysis of parton distributions

> We present a new global QCD analysis of parton distribution functions, allowing for possible intrinsic charm (IC) contributions in the nucleon inspired by light-front models. The analysis makes use of the full range of available high-energy scattering data for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2 and W^2 > 3.5 GeV^2, including fixed-target proton and deuteron cross sections at lower energies that were excluded in previous global analyses. The expanded data set places more stringent constraints on the momentum carried by IC, with <x>_IC at most 0.5% (corresponding to an IC normalization of ~ 1%) at the 4-sigma level for Delta chi^2 = 1. We also critically assess the impact of older EMC measurements of F_2c at large x, which favor a nonzero IC, but with very large chi^2 values.

## Key claim

The global analysis finds that the momentum fraction carried by intrinsic charm quarks is at most 0.5% at the 4-sigma level (for Delta chi^2 = 1), but this limit depends on including low-Q^2 and low-W^2 fixed-target data that previous analyses excluded, and on the use of a hadronic threshold suppression factor.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis of parton distribution functions using the JR14 framework, including all available high-energy scattering data with Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2 and W^2 >= 3.5 GeV^2, which incorporates fixed-target SLAC proton and deuteron cross sections excluded in earlier analyses. They decomposed the charm structure function into perturbative (photon-gluon fusion) and nonperturbative (intrinsic charm) components, considering several IC models including the BHPS five-quark model and meson-baryon fluctuation models, with target mass corrections and higher twist contributions applied to the low-Q^2 data.

## Key numbers

- Momentum fraction carried by intrinsic charm: <x>_IC < 0.1% at the 1-sigma level for the standard fit without threshold suppression
- With hadronic threshold suppression factor: <x>_IC at most 0.5% at the 4-sigma level (corresponding to an IC normalization of approximately 1%)
- Best fit with threshold suppression: <x>_IC = (0.15 +/- 0.09)% for the full data set
- Total chi^2/N_dat = 1.25 for N_dat = 4296 data points
- EMC F_2^c data alone favor IC at approximately (0.3-0.4)% but give chi^2 = 4.3 per datum for 19 data points
- Charm quark mass: m_c(m_c) = 1.3 GeV in the MSbar scheme

## Context

This work extends the JR14 global fit (Jimenez-Delgado et al., 2014) and contradicts earlier global analyses by Pumplin et al. (2007) and Dulat et al. (2014) that used more stringent Q^2 and W^2 cuts (Q^2 >= 4 GeV^2, W^2 >= 12 GeV^2) and found weaker limits on IC (<x>_IC less than 2-3%). The inclusion of SLAC fixed-target data (SLAC, 1970s-1980s) provides stronger constraints on high-x PDFs, while the analysis also critically assesses the EMC charm data (EMC, 1980s) that have historically been cited as evidence for IC. The results enable future searches for IC at a proposed electron-ion collider, where new precise F_2^c measurements at large x could definitively determine the nonperturbative charm content of the nucleon.
