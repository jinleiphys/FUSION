# Modeling J/psi Properties at Finite Temperature

> We model J/psi using an effective lagrangian approach and calculate its spectral function in a gas of light mesons at finite temperature. We explore the hadronic landscape estimating cross sections for elastic and inelastic channels. Effects of form factors are tested in a general, but consistently gauge invariant manner. Relevance to heavy ion experiments is discussed.

## Key claim
The J/ψ spectral function in a hot hadronic gas is severely broadened and its peak suppressed already at temperatures on the order of the pion mass, but the results depend fairly sensitively on the choice of form factors and on the approximate SU(4) symmetry used to fix coupling strengths.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian based on SU(4) symmetry for pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets, including J/ψ, and introduce interactions via gauge covariant minimal substitution up to order g². They calculate dissociation cross sections for J/ψ + h → D + D* (and related channels) for pions, kaons, and rho mesons, then use these cross sections to compute the in-medium width and spectral function of J/ψ in a thermal gas of π, K, ρ, and K* mesons at finite temperature. Form factors are incorporated as monopole functions with cutoff parameters, and gauge invariance is enforced by a general Lorentz structure for contact graphs.

## Key numbers
- Dissociation cross sections for J/ψ + π, J/ψ + K, and J/ψ + ρ are shown in Fig. 1; bands of uncertainty are estimated to be ~20–50%.
- Elastic J/ψ + h cross sections are of order femtobarns for pions, microbarns for rho mesons, and nanobarns for kaons.
- The spectral function is computed at a temperature of 150 MeV, with a band of uncertainty appearing over the 5 GeV results.
- Form factor cutoffs are tested at fixed values of 2, 3, and 4 GeV, and also with Λᵢ = mᵢ (the mass of the on-shell charmed meson at the vertex).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of vector meson broadening in hot hadronic matter, particularly the ρ meson (Haglin 1995; Rapp and Wambach 1996), to the charmonium sector. It provides hadronic cross sections and spectral functions needed to interpret J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, where purely hadronic comover models have been proposed (J/ψ theory references) but remain uncertain (Kharzeev and Satz 1999). The results enable future comparisons with data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and with QGP-based suppression scenarios (Matsui and Satz 1986; Digal et al. 1998).
