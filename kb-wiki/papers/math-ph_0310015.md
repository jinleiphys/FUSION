# An Algebraic q-Deformed Form for Shape-Invariant Systems

> A quantum deformed theory applicable to all shape-invariant bound-state systems is introduced by defining q-deformed ladder operators. We show these new ladder operators satisfy new q-deformed commutation relations. In this context we construct an alternative q-deformed model that preserve the shape-invariance property presented by primary system. q-deformed generalizations of Morse, Scarf, and Coulomb potentials are given as examples.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a quantum deformed (q-deformed) algebraic framework that applies to all shape-invariant bound-state systems, but the construction preserves the shape-invariance property only for a specific alternative model defined by new ladder operators, not for the standard q-deformed models which break shape invariance.

## Method

The authors define q-deformed ladder operators in terms of the standard shape-invariant creation and annihilation operators using the q-number definition [x]_q = (q^x - q^{-x})/(q - q^{-1}). They construct three distinct q-deformed models: a standard model yielding q-commutation relations, a generalized Q-deformed model using the alternative Q-number definition [x]_Q = (Q^x - 1)/(Q - 1), and a third model that preserves shape invariance by introducing new operators S_±^(q) with a functional constraint on the operator F that depends on the potential parameters. The shape-invariance-preserving model is then applied to Morse, Scarf, and Coulomb potentials as examples.

## Key numbers

- For the Morse potential: R(a_j) = [a_j^2 - a_{j+1}^2] with a_j = b - (λℏ/√(2mV_0))(j - 1/2), where ℏΩ ≡ V_0
- For the Scarf potential: R(a_j) = [a_j^2 - a_{j+1}^2] with a_j = (1/2)(√(8mV_0/(ℏ^2λ^2) + 1) - 2j + 1), where Ω = λ√(V_0/(2m))
- For the Coulomb potential: R(L) = (Z^2 e^4/4)[1/(L+1)^2 - 1/(L+2)^2], where ℏΩ = m(Ze^2)^2/(2ℏ^2)
- The q-deformed Hamiltonian eigenvalues for the shape-invariant model are ℏΩ q^(2R(a_0)) F^2 q^(e_n) [e_n]_q, where e_n = Σ_{k=1}^n R(a_k)

## Context

This work extends the algebraic formulation of shape invariance developed by Balantekin (1998) and the supersymmetric quantum mechanics framework of Witten (1981) and Cooper, Khare, and Sukhatme (1995). It generalizes previous q-deformed harmonic oscillator models (Macfarlane, Biedenharn, Arik and Coon) to all shape-invariant potentials. The results enable the construction of exactly-solvable q-deformed systems that preserve integrability, and can be combined with earlier work by Aleixo, Balantekin, and Ribeiro on coupling shape-invariant systems to two-level systems (generalizing the Jaynes-Cummings model).
