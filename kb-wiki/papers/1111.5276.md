# The generalized fermion-bag approach

> We present a new approach to some four-fermion lattice field theories which we call the generalized fermion bag approach. The basic idea is to identify unpaired fermionic degrees of freedom that cause sign problems and collect them in a bag. Paired fermions usually act like bosons and do not lead to sign problems. A resummation of all unpaired fermion degrees of freedom inside the bag is sufficient to solve the fermion sign problem in a variety of interesting cases. Using a concept of duality we then argue that the size of the fermion bags is small both at strong and weak couplings. This allows us to construct efficient algorithms in both these limits. Using the fermion bag approach, we study the quantum phase transition of the 3D massless lattice Thirrring model which is of interest in the context of Graphene. Using our method we are able to solve the model on lattices as large as $40^3$ with moderate computational resources. We obtain the precise location of the quantum critical point and the values of the critical exponents through this study.

## Key claim

The paper presents the generalized fermion-bag approach as a new Monte Carlo method for four-fermion lattice field theories, claiming it solves the fermion sign problem by resumming only unpaired fermionic degrees of freedom inside a "bag" rather than over the entire volume. The hedge is that the method is efficient only when the bag size remains a small fraction of the thermodynamic volume, which the authors argue occurs at both strong and weak couplings due to a duality relation.

## Method

The authors expand the partition function of a four-fermion lattice field theory in powers of the coupling, expressing each term as a product of correlation functions of chiral condensates. Using the identity Det(D) Det(G[{x}]) = Det(W[{x}]), they show that the weight of a configuration can be computed either as the determinant of a (V-k) × (V-k) matrix (strong-coupling bag of free sites) or as the determinant of a k × k propagator matrix (weak-coupling bag of interaction sites). They implement a Monte Carlo algorithm with bond creation/destruction, bond translation, source translation, and worm updates, using block LU decomposition to achieve O(N_b^3) scaling per sweep where N_b is the number of bonds.

## Key numbers

- Lattice sizes up to 40^3 were simulated with moderate computational resources for the 3D massless lattice Thirring model.
- At the quantum critical point, the fermion bags contain approximately one eighth of the total degrees of freedom (N_b ~ V/8).
- The quantum critical point was located at U_c = 0.2608(2).
- Critical exponents from combined fits: ν = 0.85(1), η = 0.65(1), η_ψ = 0.37(1).
- The combined fit had χ^2/d.o.f. = 1.3.
- For comparison, earlier work by Del Debbio et al. (1995, 1997) found ν = 0.71(4) and ν = 0.60(2) using HMC with fermion masses.

## Context

This work extends the fermion bag approach introduced by Chandrasekharan (2009) by incorporating a strong-weak coupling duality that makes the method efficient at weak couplings, whereas the original approach only worked at strong couplings. The authors apply the method to the 3D massless lattice Thirring model, which is relevant to critical points in Graphene (PhysRevB.84.075123, 2011). Previous Monte Carlo studies by Del Debbio et al. (1995, 1997) and Barbour et al. (1998) used HMC with auxiliary fields and finite fermion masses on smaller lattices, introducing uncontrolled systematic errors from two infrared scales. The new method enables direct simulation with exactly massless fermions and enables extraction of critical exponents without mass extrapolation, while the algorithm shows essentially zero dynamical critical exponent (z ≈ 0) when measured in sweeps.
