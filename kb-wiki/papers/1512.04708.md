# What We Know About the Lambda(1405)

> The current status of the Lambda(1405) resonance in the Kbar N scattering is summarized. It is shown that the precise experimental data and the theoretical developments in chiral SU(3) dynamics enable us to quantitatively understand the physics around the Kbar N threshold. We present the recent theoretical predictions of the pi Sigma spectrum and the investigations of the internal structure of the Lambda(1405).

## Key claim
The Lambda(1405) resonance is consistently described as having a two-pole structure in chiral SU(3) dynamics, but the precise position of the second pole remains uncertain because it lies far from the Kbar N threshold.

## Method
The author reviews meson-baryon coupled-channel scattering calculations using chiral SU(3) dynamics, where the interaction potential is constructed from chiral perturbation theory and the full scattering amplitude is obtained by solving a nonperturbative scattering equation. The next-to-leading order (NLO) analysis incorporates constraints from the SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen shift and width, fitting K- p total cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and the K- p scattering length. The resulting amplitude is extrapolated to the complex energy plane to locate pole positions, and the wavefunction and compositeness of the Lambda(1405) are studied using an equivalent local potential and a generalized weak-binding relation.

## Key numbers
- Kaonic hydrogen 1s level shift: Delta E = 283 +/- 36 (stat) +/- 6 (syst) eV; width: Gamma = 541 +/- 89 (stat) +/- 22 (syst) eV (SIDDHARTA collaboration)
- Best fit chi-squared per degree of freedom: chi^2/dof = 0.96 for the NLO analysis including SIDDHARTA constraint
- Pole 1 position from NLO analysis: z1 = 1424 (+7/-23) - i 26 (+3/-14) MeV
- Pole 2 position from NLO analysis: z2 = 1381 (+18/-6) - i 81 (+19/-8) MeV
- Root mean distance of the Kbar N density distribution: sqrt(<r^2>) = 1.44 fm
- Compositeness X for the Kbar N component from Ikeda (2012): X = 1.2 + i 0.1, with tilde-X = 1.0

## Context
This review extends the chiral SU(3) dynamics framework developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001), and builds on the two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405) identified by Oller (2000) and Jido (2003). The precise SIDDHARTA data (Bazzi, 2011, 2012) enables systematic NLO analyses by Ikeda (2011, 2012), Mai (2012, 2014), and Guo (2012), resolving an inconsistency found in earlier kaonic hydrogen measurements. The results enable future studies of the pi Sigma spectrum in reactions such as K- d -> n pi Sigma (J-PARC E31) and nonleptonic Lambda_c decay (Belle, BESIII), and support the Kbar N molecular structure of the Lambda(1405) over a simple three-quark picture.
