# Testing Non-local Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions in the Four-Nucleon Systems

> The Faddeev-Yakubovski equations are solved in configuration space for the $% α$-particle and n-$^3$H continuum states. We test the ability of nonlocal nucleon-nucleon interaction models to describe 3N and 4N systems.

## Key claim

Non-local nucleon-nucleon interaction models of the Doleschall type, which successfully reproduce three-nucleon binding energies without explicit three-nucleon forces, are extended to the four-nucleon system; the authors test whether these models remain successful for the alpha-particle ground state and n-³H scattering, noting that the n-³H system possesses a resonance at E_cm ≈ 3 MeV that conventional NN+3NF models fail to describe.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space for the four-nucleon system, expanding the FY components K and H in a partial wave basis using j-j coupling and piecewise Hermite spline basis functions. They used four versions of Doleschall non-local potentials (INOY96, INOY03, INOY04, INOY04') which incorporate non-local short-range terms in selected partial waves, with higher partial waves taken from the AV18 potential, and performed calculations both with and without Coulomb repulsion between protons.

## Key numbers

- ³H binding energies from non-local potentials: 8.556 MeV (INOY96), 8.497 MeV (INOY03), 8.476 MeV (INOY04), 8.464 MeV (INOY04'); experimental value is 8.482 MeV
- ³He binding energies from non-local potentials: 7.882 MeV (INOY96), 7.734 MeV (INOY03), 7.711 MeV (INOY04), 7.704 MeV (INOY04'); experimental value is 7.718 MeV
- ΔB = B(³H) - B(³He) from non-local potentials: 0.674 MeV (INOY96), 0.763 MeV (INOY03), 0.765 MeV (INOY04), 0.760 MeV (INOY04'); experimental value is 0.764 MeV
- ⁴He binding energy without Coulomb from non-local potentials: 31.00 MeV (INOY96), 30.04 MeV (INOY03), 29.91 MeV (INOY04), 29.88 MeV (INOY04'); AV18 gives 24.95 MeV
- ⁴He binding energy with Coulomb from non-local potentials: 30.19 MeV (INOY96), 29.24 MeV (INOY03), 29.11 MeV (INOY04), 29.09 MeV (INOY04'); AV18 gives 24.22 MeV, AV18+UIX gives 28.50 MeV, experimental value is 28.30 MeV
- ⁴He rms radius with Coulomb from non-local potentials: 1.358 fm (INOY96), 1.373 fm (INOY03), 1.377 fm (INOY04), 1.376 fm (INOY04'); AV18 gives 1.516 fm, AV18+UIX gives 1.44 fm, experimental value is 1.47 fm
- n-d doublet scattering length from non-local potentials: 0.448 fm (INOY96), 0.523 fm (INOY03), 0.543 fm (INOY04), 0.553 fm (INOY04'); AV18 gives 1.26 fm, AV18+UIX gives 0.595 fm, experimental value is 0.65 ± 0.04 fm
- n-d quartet scattering length from all models: 6.34 fm; experimental value is 6.35 ± 0.02 fm

## Context

This work extends the pioneering studies of Doleschall and collaborators (Doleschall, 1996-2004) from three-nucleon to four-nucleon systems, testing whether non-local NN interaction models that reproduce ³H and ³He binding energies without three-nucleon forces can also describe the alpha-particle and n-³H scattering. The results show that these non-local models overbind the alpha-particle (giving 29-30 MeV versus the experimental 28.30 MeV) and produce rms radii smaller than experiment, in contrast to the AV18+UIX model which gives 28.50 MeV and 1.44 fm; this suggests that non-locality alone cannot fully replace three-nucleon forces in four-nucleon systems, and the n-³H resonance at E_cm ≈ 3 MeV remains a challenge for future work.
