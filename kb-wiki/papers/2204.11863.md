# Transverse charge and current densities in the nucleon from dispersively improved chiral effective field theory

> Background: The transverse densities $ρ_{1, 2}(b)$ describe the distributions of electric charge and magnetic moment at fixed light-front time and connect the nucleon's elastic form factors with its partonic structure. The dispersive representation of the form factors $F_{1, 2}(t)$ expresses the densities in terms of exchanges of hadronic states in the $t$-channel and permits their analysis using hadronic physics methods. Purpose: Compute the densities at peripheral distances $b = \mathcal{O}(M_π^{-1})$, where they are generated predominantly by the two-pion states in the dispersive representation. Quantify the uncertainties. Methods: Dispersively improved chiral effective field theory (DI$χ$EFT) is used to calculate the isovector spectral functions $\textrm{Im}\, F_{1, 2}(t)$ on the two-pion cut. The method includes $ππ$ interactions ($ρ$ resonance) through elastic unitarity and provides realistic spectral functions up to $t \approx$ 1 GeV$^2$. Higher-mass states are parametrized by effective poles and constrained by sum rules (charges, radii, superconvergence relations). The densities $ρ_{1, 2}(b)$ are obtained from their dispersive representation. Uncertainties are quantified by varying the spectral functions. The method respects analyticity and ensures the correct $b \rightarrow \infty$ asymptotic behavior of the densities. Results: Accurate densities are obtained at all distances $b \gtrsim 0.5$ fm, with correct behavior down to $b \rightarrow 0$. The region of distances is quantified where transverse nucleon structure is governed by the two-pion state. The light-front current distributions in the polarized nucleon are computed and discussed. Conclusions: Peripheral nucleon structure can be computed from first principles using DI$χ$EFT. The method can be extended to generalized parton distributions and other nucleon form factors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dispersively improved chiral effective field theory (DIχEFT) can compute the peripheral transverse charge and magnetization densities of the nucleon at distances \(b \gtrsim 0.5\) fm with quantified uncertainties, extending the reach of traditional chiral EFT which was limited to \(b \gtrsim 2\) fm. The hedge is that the method relies on a partial next-to-next-to-leading-order (pN2LO) approximation for the spectral functions and a parametrized high-mass contribution constrained by sum rules, not a complete next-to-next-to-leading-order calculation.

## Method

The authors compute the isovector spectral functions \(\textrm{Im}\, F_{1,2}(t)\) on the two-pion cut using the \(N/D\) method within DIχEFT, which separates the nonperturbative \(\pi\pi\) interactions (taken from the measured pion timelike form factor) from the \(\pi\pi \rightarrow N\bar{N}\) amplitudes computed in chiral EFT at LO, NLO, and pN2LO accuracy. The transverse densities \(\rho_{1,2}(b)\) are then obtained from the dispersive representation as integrals over the spectral functions weighted by modified Bessel functions \(K_0\) and \(K_1\). Higher-mass states above \(t \approx 1\) GeV\(^2\) are parametrized by effective poles constrained by sum rules (charges, radii, superconvergence relations), and uncertainties are quantified by varying the spectral function parameters and the high-mass pole ensemble.

## Key numbers

- The two-pion part of the isovector spectral functions is constructed up to \(t_{\rm max} \approx 1\) GeV\(^2\), with the \(\rho\) resonance at \(t \approx 0.6\) GeV\(^2\).
- Accurate densities are obtained at all distances \(b \gtrsim 0.5\) fm, with correct behavior down to \(b \rightarrow 0\).
- The proton anomalous magnetic moment is \(\kappa^p = 1.793\) (in nuclear magnetons); the neutron anomalous magnetic moment is \(\kappa^n = -1.913\).
- The two-pion threshold is \(t_{\rm thr} = 4 M_\pi^2\); the three-pion threshold for isoscalar FFs is \(t_{\rm thr} = 9 M_\pi^2\).
- The asymptotic behavior of the nucleon FFs at large spacelike \(|t|\) is \(F_1(t) \sim |t|^{-2}\) and \(F_2(t) \sim |t|^{-3}\) (QCD counting rules).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of transverse densities using empirical spectral functions (Miller, 2011) and leading-order DIχEFT (Alarcon, 2017). It improves upon traditional chiral EFT calculations (Gasser, 1987; Bernard, 1996; Kubis, 2000; Kaiser, 2003) which were limited to the near-threshold region and could only compute densities at \(b \gtrsim 2\) fm. The results enable future studies of quark-hadron duality in the transverse densities by matching the EFT description at large distances with quark-model descriptions at \(b \lesssim 1\) fm, and can be extended to generalized parton distributions and other nucleon form factors.
