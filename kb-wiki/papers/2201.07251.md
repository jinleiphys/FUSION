# Empirical capture cross sections for cosmic neutrino detection with $^{151}{\rm \bf Sm}$ and $^{171}{\rm \bf Tm}$

> The nuclei $^{151}$Sm and $^{171}$Tm have been identified as attractive candidates for the detection of the cosmic neutrino background. Both isotopes undergo first-forbidden non-unique beta decays which inhibits a prediction of their spectral shape using symmetries alone and this has, so far, obstructed a definitive prediction of their neutrino capture cross sections. In this work we point out that for both elements the so-called "$ξ$-approximation" is applicable and this effectively reduces the spectral shape to deviate by at most $1\%$ from the one that would arise if beta decays were of the allowed type. Using measured half-lives we extract the relevant nuclear matrix element and predict the neutrino capture cross sections for both isotopes at $1\%$ level, accounting for a number of relevant effects including radiative corrections and the finite size of the nuclei. We obtained $(1.12\pm 0.01)\times 10^{-46}{\rm cm}^2$ for $^{171}$Tm and $(4.77\pm 0.01)\times 10^{-48}{\rm cm}^2$ for $^{151}$Sm. This method is robust as it does not rely on the data points near the end-point of the beta spectrum which may be contaminated by atomic physics effects, namely shake-up and shake-off. Finally, we calculate the target mass which is necessary for cosmic neutrino discovery and discuss several bottlenecks and respective solutions associated to the experimental program. We conclude that the detection of cosmic neutrino background by neutrino capture on $^{151}$Sm and $^{171}$Tm is achievable and free from theoretical limitations but still subject to technical issues that should be further investigated by the experimentalists in the context of the proposed PTOLEMY project.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the ξ-approximation is applicable to the first-forbidden non-unique beta decays of ¹⁵¹Sm and ¹⁷¹Tm, allowing the neutrino capture cross sections to be predicted at the 1% level from measured half-lives, contrary to prior claims that such cross sections could not be predicted from first principles due to the forbidden nature of the transitions.

## Method

The authors use the Behrens-Bühring formalism to show that for heavy nuclei with small Q-values satisfying Q ≪ Zα/R, the beta decay spectrum reduces to the allowed shape up to corrections of order 1/ξ, where ξ = Zα/(QR). They extract the nuclear matrix element by demanding that the measured half-life be reproduced, using the BetaShape software for the Fermi function, finite-size corrections, and radiative corrections, with atomic exchange and mismatch corrections added analytically following Hayen (2017). The cross section uncertainty is estimated by varying a parameter χ that parametrizes sub-dominant 1/ξ corrections to the matrix element.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of candidate isotopes for cosmic neutrino background detection previously considered by Weinberg (1962), Cocco (2007), and the PTOLEMY collaboration, who identified ¹⁷¹Tm as an attractive candidate. The authors address the obstruction raised by Mikulenko (2021) that first-forbidden non-unique transitions prevent definitive cross section predictions, and provide a rigorous theoretical framework for extracting cross sections from half-lives. The results enable experimental programs to consider heavy nuclei as alternatives to tritium, with the key advantage that ¹⁵¹Sm is a common byproduct of spent nuclear fuel with no supply issues, while ¹⁷¹Tm can be produced by irradiating enriched erbium. The method is robust against atomic physics effects (shake-up and shake-off) that could contaminate data near the beta decay endpoint.
