# Holographic Magnetized Chiral Density Wave

> We explore the end point of the helical instability in finite density, finite magnetic field background discussed by Kharzeev and Yee [1]. The nonlinear solution is obtained and identified with the (magnetized) chiral density wave phase in literature. We find there are two branches of solutions, which match with the two unstable modes in [1]. At large chemical potential and magnetic field, the magnetized chiral density wave can be thermodynamically preferred over chirally symmetric phase and chiral symmetry breaking phase. Interestingly, we find an exotic state with vanishing chemical potential at large magnetic field. We also attempt to clarify the role of anomalous charge in holographic model.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the nonlinear endpoint of the helical instability in a finite-density, finite-magnetic-field holographic D3/D7 model corresponds to the magnetized chiral density wave (MCDW) phase, and that this phase can be thermodynamically preferred over both the chirally symmetric and chiral symmetry breaking phases at large chemical potential and magnetic field, though the precision of numerical data does not allow a decisive conclusion on the competition between MCDW and the chiral symmetry breaking phase in some parameter regions.

## Method

The authors use the D3/D7 holographic model in the probe limit, with a black hole background providing finite temperature, and turn on a quark chemical potential via a nonvanishing gauge field component \(A_t(\rho)\) and a constant magnetic field \(F_{xy}=B\). They adopt an ansatz for the MCDW phase where the D7-brane embedding angle \(\theta\) depends only on the radial coordinate \(\rho\) and the angular coordinate \(\phi\) is proportional to the spatial coordinate \(z\) (\(\phi = k z\)), forming a spiral along the magnetic field direction. The equations of motion are derived from the Dirac-Born-Infeld and Wess-Zumino actions, solved numerically from the horizon to the boundary with a fixed quark number density \(n\), and the Gibbs free energy is minimized with respect to the spiral momentum \(k\) to determine the thermodynamically preferred state.

## Key numbers

- At \(B/(\pi T)^2 = 6.5\), the large-\(k\) branch of MCDW phase becomes thermodynamically preferred over the chirally symmetric phase for \(\mu/B^{1/2} \gtrsim 0.35\).
- At \(B/(\pi T)^2 = 9\), the large-\(k\) MCDW phase has lower free energy than the chirally symmetric phase in their overlap region; the chiral symmetry breaking phase exists below a critical value of \(\mu/B^{1/2} \simeq 0.15\).
- At \(B/(\pi T)^2 = 15\), the small-\(k\) branch of MCDW phase always has lower free energy than the chirally symmetric phase; the MCDW phase extends to states with vanishing chemical potential (\(\mu = 0\)) and to states with vanishing density (\(n = 0\)).
- For the largest magnetic field studied, \(B/(\pi T)^2 = 15\), the free energy difference \(\Delta \Omega\) between MCDW and chirally symmetric phases is less than \(1\%\) of \(\Omega_{\chi S}\).
- At \(B/(\pi T)^2 = 15\) and \(\mu/(\pi T) = 1.36\), the free energy density \(\Omega/(V \mathcal{N} B^2)\) versus \(k/B^{1/2}\) shows two branches of MCDW solutions.

## Context

This work extends the linear stability analysis of Kharzeev and Yee (2011), who found an unstable helical mode in a finite-density, finite-magnetic-field holographic background, by constructing the full nonlinear endpoint of that instability. The resulting MCDW phase is analogous to the magnetized chiral density wave studied in effective models by Tatsumi (2014) and others. The paper also clarifies the role of anomalous charge in the holographic model, showing that it varies along the renormalization group flow and vanishes in the infrared and ultraviolet limits, in contrast to effective model treatments where it is constant. The results enable future studies of the full phase diagram including zero-density states, transverse spiral phases (chiral magnetic spiral), and transport properties of the MCDW phase.
