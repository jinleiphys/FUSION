# Exchange terms in the two--nucleon induced non--mesonic weak decay of $Λ$--hypernuclei

> The contribution of Pauli exchange terms to the two--nucleon induced non--mesonic weak decay of $^{12}_Λ$C hypernuclei, $ΛNN\to nNN$ ($N=n$ or $p$), is studied within a nuclear matter formalism implemented in a local density approximation. We have adopted a weak transition potential including the exchange of the complete octets of pseudoscalar and vector mesons as well as a residual strong interaction modeled on the Bonn potential. The introduction of exchange terms turns out to reduce the two--nucleon induced non--mesonic rate by 18% and, jointly with an increase in the one--nucleon induced rate by the same magnitude, reveals to be significant for an accurate determination of the full set of hypernuclear non--mesonic decay widths in theoretical and experimental analyses.

## Key claim

The inclusion of Pauli exchange terms (required by fermion antisymmetrization) reduces the two-nucleon induced non-mesonic decay rate of \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C by 18% and increases the one-nucleon induced rate by 19%, but the abstract's statement of an 18% reduction and a matching increase of the same magnitude glosses over the fact that the one-nucleon increase is actually 19% (not exactly 18%), and the effect on the total non-mesonic width and the \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) ratio is smaller.

## Method

The authors employed a non-relativistic nuclear matter formalism with a local density approximation to calculate the two-nucleon induced weak decay \(\Lambda NN \to nNN\) for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C. The weak transition potential included the exchange of the full octets of pseudoscalar (\(\pi, \eta, K\)) and vector (\(\rho, \omega, K^*\)) mesons, with strong coupling constants from the Nijmegen soft-core interaction NSC97f; the residual strong interaction for ground-state correlations was modeled on a Bonn potential with \(\pi, \rho, \sigma, \omega\) exchange. Pauli exchange terms were evaluated exactly for the dominant \(\Gamma_2^{\rm pp}\) diagrams and approximately for the remaining diagrams using a Landau-Migdal model with fitted \(g'\) and \(g'_\Lambda\) parameters.

## Key numbers

- The total two-nucleon induced decay width for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}\)C including all exchange terms is \(\Gamma_2 = 0.252\) in units of the free \(\Lambda\) decay rate \(\Gamma^0 = 2.52 \times 10^{-6}\) eV.
- The direct-only two-nucleon induced width (without exchange) is \(\Gamma_2 = 0.287\) in the same units, showing an 18% reduction due to exchange terms.
- The one-nucleon induced rate \(\Gamma_1\) increases by 19% when exchange terms are included.
- The ratio \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1\) decreases by 31% due to antisymmetrization.
- For the \(\Gamma_2^{\rm pp}\) subset, the dominant direct term is \(\Gamma_2^{dd'd'd} = 0.232\), while the largest exchange terms are \(\Gamma_2^{dd'd'e} = -0.057\) and \(\Gamma_2^{dd'e'd} = -0.048\) (all in units of \(\Gamma^0\)).
- The isospin channel decomposition of the total \(\Gamma_2\) is \(\Gamma_{np} : \Gamma_{pp} : \Gamma_{nn} = 0.83 : 0.12 : 0.04\) (i.e., \(\Gamma_{np} = 0.210\), \(\Gamma_{pp} = 0.031\), \(\Gamma_{nn} = 0.011\) in units of \(\Gamma^0\)).
- The Landau-Migdal parameters fitted for the approximate exchange terms are \(g' = 0.05\) and \(g'_\Lambda = 0.09\).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic calculations of two-nucleon induced decay by Bauer et al. (2004, 2006, 2008) and Alberico et al. (1991, 2000), which previously considered only direct terms, by including Pauli exchange terms for the first time. The results are relevant for interpreting experimental data from KEK (Bhang et al., 2007) and BNL (Parker et al., 2007), which gave conflicting indications of \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1 \simeq 0.7\) and \(<0.32\), respectively; the present calculation yields \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1 = 0.252/(\Gamma_1)\) (with \(\Gamma_1\) increased by 19% from its direct-only value), enabling more accurate extraction of the \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) ratio from future triple-coincidence experiments at J-PARC.
