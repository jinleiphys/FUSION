# Soft modifications to jet fragmentation in high energy proton-proton collisions

> The discovery of collectivity in proton-proton collisions, is one of the most puzzling outcomes from the two first runs at LHC, as it points to the possibility of creation of a Quark-Gluon Plasma, earlier believed to only be created in heavy ion collisions. One key observation from heavy ion collisions is still not observed in proton-proton, namely jet-quenching. In this letter it is shown how a model capable of describing soft collective features of proton-proton collisions, also predicts modifications to jet fragmentation properties. With this starting point, several new observables suited for the present and future hunt for jet quenching in small collision systems are proposed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a microscopic model of string interactions (the "shoving" model) which reproduces the near-side ridge in proton-proton collisions also predicts modifications to jet fragmentation properties, but that the effect on standard observables like the jet momentum fraction \(z_j\) is geometrically suppressed in realistic event geometries and only becomes visible in a toy geometry that violates causality.

## Method

The author implements the string shoving model in the Pythia event generator, where parallel strings exert transverse forces on each other via an interaction term \(f(d_\perp) = (g\kappa d_\perp/\rho^2)\exp(-d_\perp^2/4\rho^2)\). Events are generated for proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV containing a \(Z\) boson and a hard jet, using both a realistic geometry (where the jet core escapes the dense region before shoving occurs) and a toy geometry (where all strings are treated as expanded at \(\tau=0\)). The model parameters are fixed by fitting the ridge in \(Z\)-tagged events to preliminary ATLAS data, then jet observables are computed with Rivet and FastJet.

## Key numbers

- The shoving model parameter \(g=4\) is chosen to describe the ridge in minimum bias events, as shown in Bierlich (2017).
- Jet mass increases by about 10% for low jet masses in the 40-125 GeV jet-\(p_\perp\) bin when shoving is included, compared to default Pythia.
- The jet cross section \(\sigma_j(R)\) shows a change in the \(R^2\) coefficient \(C\) from \(1.35 \pm 0.03\) (default Pythia) to \(1.49 \pm 0.03\) (Pythia + shoving), a relative increase of about 10%.
- The average hadron mass in the leading jet shows a deviation of about 10% for large jet radius \(R=0.7\) when shoving is included.
- The jet charge distribution width shows deviations up to 40% in the large-\(R\) limit.

## Context

This work extends the string shoving model developed by Bierlich (2014, 2016, 2017) for describing collective effects in proton-proton collisions, which was previously shown to reproduce the near-side ridge in minimum bias events. The paper addresses the open question of why jet quenching (observed in heavy ion collisions) has not been seen in small systems, and proposes new observables for the high-luminosity LHC era. It contrasts the final-state interaction approach with initial-state saturation effects from Color Glass Condensate calculations (Schenke, 2016), and builds on the ATLAS observation of a ridge in \(Z\)-tagged events (ATLAS, 2017) and CMS measurements of jet mass in \(Z\)+jet events (Chatrchyan, 2013).
