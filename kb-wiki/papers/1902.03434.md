# Normalized multi-pion Hanbury Brown and Twiss correlation functions of pion-emitting sources with Bose-Einstein condensation

> Recently, the ALICE collaboration analyzed the three- and four-pion Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlations in Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The measured suppressions of three- and four-pion correlations may originate from a substantial coherence of the particle-emitting sources. In this work we investigate the normalized three- and four-pion HBT correlation functions for evolving pion gas (EPG) sources with Bose-Einstein condensation. We find that the intercepts of the normalized correlation functions at zero relative momentum are sensitive to source condensation and particle momentum. The normalized correlation functions in low average-momentum regions of three and four pions decrease with decreasing temperature and increasing particle number of the source, indicating a dependence of the normalized correlation functions on source condensation. However, this dependence becomes weak in an intermediate average-momentum region because particles with high momenta are likely emitted from excited states incoherently in the EPG model, even if the source has a considerable condensation fraction. For a wide momentum range, the normalized correlation functions for low source temperatures are enhanced at larger relative momenta because of a rapid increase of two-pion chaoticity parameter with increasing particle momentum. We hope the significant enhancement of the normalized four-pion correlation function at high relative momentum will be identified through future analyses of experimental data.

# Normalized multi-pion Hanbury Brown and Twiss correlation functions of pion-emitting sources with Bose-Einstein condensation

## Key claim
The normalized three- and four-pion HBT correlation functions for evolving pion gas (EPG) sources with Bose-Einstein condensation are sensitive to source condensation and particle momentum, but this sensitivity becomes weak in an intermediate average-momentum region because particles with high momenta are likely emitted from excited states incoherently even if the source has a considerable condensation fraction. The authors hope the significant enhancement of the normalized four-pion correlation function at high relative momentum will be identified through future experimental data analyses.

## Method
The authors used an evolving pion gas (EPG) model with Bose-Einstein condensation, where the source is a quasi-static identical-pion gas trapped within a harmonic oscillator mean field, undergoing adiabatic expansion satisfying TV^(gamma-1) = constant. They calculated the normalized three-pion correlation function r3(Q3) and normalized four-pion correlation function r4(Q4) from the one-particle density matrix and ground-state wave functions, then analyzed these functions in different average-transverse-momentum regions (low: 0 < KT3 < 0.16 GeV/c, intermediate: 0.16 < KT3 < 0.3 GeV/c, high: KT3 > 0.3 GeV/c) for various source temperatures (80, 100, 120, 150 MeV) and particle numbers (N = 400, 800, 1200, 1600).

## Key numbers
- Source-size parameter C1 values used: 0.35 and 0.40
- Initial source radius R0 = 6 fm and initial temperature T0 = 170 MeV for hydrodynamical calculation
- Model parameters gamma = 1.627 and alpha = 0.62 from hydrodynamical calculation
- Average phase-space density at p = 100 MeV/c: <f> = 1.509 for parameter set (C1 = 0.40, N = 1200, T = 100 MeV)
- Average phase-space density at p = 500 MeV/c: <f> = 0.006 for parameter set (C1 = 0.40, N = 1200, T = 100 MeV)
- Average phase-space density at average momentum for T = 100 MeV: 0.030 (C1 = 0.40, N = 1200); 0.060 (C1 = 0.40, N = 1600); 0.108 (C1 = 0.35, N = 1200)
- Average phase-space density at average momentum for T = 150 MeV: 0.016 (C1 = 0.40, N = 1200); 0.022 (C1 = 0.40, N = 1600); 0.028 (C1 = 0.35, N = 1200)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous EPG model analyses of multi-pion correlation functions (Bary, Ru, Zhang, 2018) which indicated a source condensation fraction between 16% and 47% for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The normalized three-pion correlation function r3 was originally proposed by Heinz, Zhang, and Sugarbaker to determine source coherence without contamination from resonance decays, and has been used by NA44, WA98, STAR, and ALICE collaborations. The ALICE collaboration (2016) found significant suppression of multi-pion correlations in Pb-Pb collisions not observed in pp and p-Pb collisions. The present results suggest the simple spherical EPG model may capture main characteristics of pion-emitting sources at the LHC, and the authors hope the predicted enhancement of r4 at large relative momentum will be tested experimentally.
