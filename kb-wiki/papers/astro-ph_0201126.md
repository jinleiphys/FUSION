# Strangeness Nucleation in Neutron Star Matter

> We study the transition from npe-type nuclear matter (consisting of neutrons, protons, and electrons) to matter containing strangeness, using a Walecka-type model predicting a first-order kaon-condensate phase transition. We examine the free energy of droplets of K-matter as the density, temperature, and neutrino fraction are varied. Langer nucleation rate theory is then used to approximate the rate at which critical droplets of the new phase are produced by thermal fluctuations, thus giving an estimate of the time required for the new (mixed) phase to appear at various densities and various times in the cooling history of the proto-neutron star. We also discuss the famous difficulty of "simultaneous weak interactions" which we connect to the literature on non-topological solitons. Finally, we discuss the implications of our results to several phenomenological issues involving neutron star phase transitions.

# Strangeness Nucleation in Neutron Star Matter

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nucleation of kaon-condensed matter in proto-neutron stars via thermal fluctuations is strongly suppressed by the presence of electron neutrinos, potentially preventing the transition from npe matter to the mixed phase during the early, hot stages of cooling. This means that a neutron star with central density not too far above the nominal critical density may fail to produce any K-matter during its cooling, possibly existing indefinitely in a metastable state.

## Method

The author uses a Walecka-type relativistic mean-field model (Glendenning and Schaffner, 1998) that predicts a first-order kaon-condensate phase transition, solving the meson field equations of motion for both N-matter (θ=0) and K-matter (θ≠0) phases under constraints of chemical equilibrium, mechanical equilibrium (including surface tension), and overall electric charge neutrality at fixed baryon number density. The free energy of spherical K-matter droplets of varying radii is calculated including bulk, surface, and Coulomb contributions, and Langer nucleation rate theory is applied to estimate the nucleation rate per unit volume as Γ ∼ T⁴ exp(-ΔF(R_crit)/T), from which the expected time for a single nucleation event in a Wigner-Seitz cell is derived.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension chosen: σ = 30 MeV/fm², a value suggested by Glendenning's study of the boundary between the two phases
- At T = 1.0 MeV, the nucleation time is less than one second at baryon densities above approximately n_B ∼ 0.55 fm⁻³, which is about 0.5 n₀ above the critical density
- At T = 0.1 MeV, the expected nucleation time is many times longer than the age of the universe across the entire density range of the mixed phase
- The critical density at zero temperature and zero neutrino fraction is 0.49 fm⁻³
- With an electron neutrino fraction of a few percent, no mixed phase is produced at densities less than 0.60–0.65 fm⁻³
- With an electron neutrino fraction near ten percent, the effective critical density is pushed upward to around 0.80 fm⁻³, several factors of n₀ above the nominal critical density

## Context

This work extends the study of first-order phase transitions in neutron star matter, following previous work on the nucleation of quark matter droplets by Olesen and Madsen (1994), Heiselberg (1995), Iida and Sato (1998), and Shukla et al. (2000). The kaon condensation scenario was first proposed by Kaplan and Nelson (1986), and the mixed-phase description using a Coulomb lattice ("pasta" structures) was developed by Glendenning (1992) and Glendenning and Schaffner (1998). The paper connects the nucleation problem to the literature on non-topological solitons (Q-balls) and identifies the "simultaneous weak interactions" difficulty as a key issue requiring further investigation, since the thermal fluctuations responsible for nucleation must directly involve weak-interaction processes that produce kaons.
