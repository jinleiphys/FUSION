# Effective Field Theory for Neutron-Deuteron Scattering: Higher Partial Waves

> The phase shifts for the higher partial waves in the spin quartet and doublet channel of nd scattering are presented at next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order in an effective field theory in which pions are integrated out. The results agree with both phase shift analyses and potential model calculations.

## Key claim
The paper presents phase shifts for higher partial waves in neutron-deuteron scattering at next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order in a pionless effective field theory, but only for the case where pions are integrated out, reserving the inclusion of pion exchanges for later investigation.

## Method
The author solved the integral equations for neutron-deuteron scattering in the spin quartet (J = 3/2) and doublet (J = 1/2) channels using a pionless effective field theory with a well-defined power counting. Phase shifts were computed at next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order and compared to existing phase-shift analyses and potential model calculations based on the Bonn and av14 potentials.

## Key numbers
- Quartet channel phase shifts for the first four partial waves (S, P, D, F) were computed as functions of center-of-mass energy and shown in figures, with agreement to experimental phase-shift analyses for proton-deuteron scattering.
- Doublet channel phase shifts for the first four higher partial waves (P, D, F, G) were computed as functions of center-of-mass energy and shown in figures.

## Context
This work extends the application of effective field theory to higher partial waves in neutron-deuteron scattering, following earlier EFT treatments that focused on lower partial waves. The results agree with phase-shift analyses for proton-deuteron scattering (Gabbiani, prior work) and with potential model calculations using the Bonn and av14 potentials. The framework enables systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections and external currents, with the doublet S-wave component (related to the triton bound state) left for future study.
