# Final-State Interaction in Electron Scattering by Polarized Nuclei

> The cross section for the reaction $^{39}\vec{\rm K}(e,e'p)^{38}$Ar is computed, and the dependence of the FSI on the initial polarization angles of the nucleus $^{39}$K is analyzed. The results are explained in a semi-classical picture of the reaction. This procedure allows us to find the best initial kinematical conditions for minimizing the FSI.

# Final-State Interaction in Electron Scattering by Polarized Nuclei

## Key claim

The dependence of final-state interactions (FSI) on the initial polarization angles of the nucleus can be understood semi-classically, allowing one to predict the optimal target polarization for either minimizing FSI (for nuclear structure studies) or maximizing FSI (for studying absorptive effects), but only within the specific shell-model and kinematic conditions considered.

## Method

A distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculation was performed for the reaction $^{39}\vec{\rm K}(e,e'p)^{38}$Ar in the extreme shell model, describing the ground state of $^{39}\vec{\rm K}$ as a 100% polarized hole in the $d_{3/2}$ shell of $^{40}$Ca. The ejected proton wave function was obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with an optical potential for positive energies, and the cross section was computed for different nuclear polarization angles at fixed kinematics corresponding to the quasi-elastic peak with $q=500$ MeV/c and in-plane emission. A semi-classical model was then introduced to relate the nuclear transparency to the path length $s$ that the ejected nucleon travels through the nucleus, using the expectation value of the nucleon position in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer: $q = 500$ MeV/c; energy transfer: $\omega = 133.5$ MeV; electron scattering angle: $\theta_e = 30^\circ$
- Missing momentum at the maximum of the momentum distribution: $p \sim 140$ MeV/c
- Mean free path extracted from linear regression of $\log T$ vs. path length $s$: $\lambda = 8.4$ fm (independent of the nuclear radius in the region between $r_{1/2}$ and $r_{1/10}$)
- Estimated decay length of the $\Delta$ resonance: $x \sim 1.7$ fm (from $\hbar c / \Gamma_\Delta \sim 200/120$ fm)

## Context

This work extends earlier DWIA studies of $(e,e'p)$ reactions with polarized medium and heavy nuclei by Boffi (1988), Garrido (1995), and Amaro (1998, 1999), which reported a dependence of FSI effects on polarization angles. The semi-classical orbit picture provides a physical interpretation for those variations and enables predictions for future experiments with polarized targets. The approach also suggests applications to $(e,e'N\pi)$ reactions in the Delta region, where selecting nuclear polarization could help distinguish FSI effects from the Delta electroproduction amplitude in the nuclear medium.
