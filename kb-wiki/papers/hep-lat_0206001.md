# Moments of Isovector Quark Distributions from Lattice QCD

> We present a complete analysis of the chiral extrapolation of lattice moments of all twist-2 isovector quark distributions, including corrections from N pi and Delta pi loops. Even though the Delta resonance formally gives rise to higher order non-analytic structure, the coefficients of the higher order terms for the helicity and transversity moments are large and cancel much of the curvature generated by the wave function renormalization. The net effect is that, whereas the unpolarized moments exhibit considerable curvature, the polarized moments show little deviation from linearity as the chiral limit is approached.

## Key claim

The chiral extrapolation of lattice moments of polarized (helicity and transversity) isovector quark distributions shows little deviation from linearity as the chiral limit is approached, because the large coefficients of the next-to-leading non-analytic terms from Delta pi loops cancel much of the curvature generated by wave function renormalization, even though the Delta resonance formally contributes at higher order in the pion mass.

## Method

The authors performed a complete chiral extrapolation analysis of lattice QCD data for moments (n ≤ 3) of twist-2 isovector quark distributions (unpolarized, helicity, and transversity) from the QCDSF, LHPC-SESAM, and other collaborations. They constructed effective parameterizations incorporating leading non-analytic (LNA) behavior from πN loops and next-to-leading non-analytic (NLNA) behavior from Δπ loops, with hadronic vertices constrained phenomenologically using a finite spatial extension (cutoff) for the pion source, and connected the extrapolation to the heavy quark limit.

## Key numbers

- The LNA coefficient for unpolarized moments is c_LNA = -(1 + 3 g_A^2)/(4π f_π)^2; for helicity moments Δc_LNA = -(1 + 2 g_A^2)/(4π f_π)^2; for transversity moments δc_LNA = -(1 + 4 g_A^2)/[2(4π f_π)^2].
- The best-fit value of the cutoff parameter μ for unpolarized moments is 500 MeV, determined by fitting the first three nontrivial moments to phenomenological values.
- In the heavy quark limit, both Δu(x)-Δd(x) and δu(x)-δd(x) are given by (5/3)δ(x-1/3), leading to constraints Δb_n = 5/3^(n+1) - Δa_n(1 - μ^2 Δc_LNA) and similarly for transversity.
- The NLNA term from the Δ enters as m_π^3/ΔM, with ΔM = M_Δ - M, and its coefficient is roughly three times bigger than the m_π^3 term in the expansion of the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the earlier chiral extrapolation analysis of unpolarized isovector moments by Detmold et al. (2001) to the polarized sector (helicity and transversity), addressing the longstanding discrepancy where linear extrapolations of lattice g_A gave values 10% or more below experiment. The inclusion of Δπ loops resolves the puzzle that including only πN loops made the g_A disagreement worse, as found in preliminary work by Detmold (thesis) and Chen et al. (2001). The approach follows the finite-source-size method of Leinweber et al. (2002) for baryon masses, and enables reliable predictions for transversity moments (where no experimental data exist) from lattice QCD.
