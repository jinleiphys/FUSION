# Corrections to the Neutrinoless Double-Beta-Decay Operator in the Shell Model

> We use diagrammatic perturbation theory to construct an effective shell-model operator for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of 82Se. The starting point is the same Bonn-C nucleon-nucleon interaction that is used to generate the Hamiltonian for recent shell-model calculations of double-beta decay. After first summing high-energy ladder diagrams that account for short-range correlations and then adding diagrams of low order in the G matrix to account for longer-range correlations, we fold the two-body matrix elements of the resulting effective operator with transition densities from the recent shell-model calculation to obtain the overall nuclear matrix element that governs the decay. Although the high-energy ladder diagrams suppress this matrix element at very short distances as expected, they enhance it at distances between one and two fermis, so that their overall effect is small. The corrections due to longer-range physics are large, but cancel one another so that the fully corrected matrix element is comparable to that produced by the bare operator. This cancellation between large and physically distinct low-order terms indicates the importance of a reliable nonperturbative calculation.

## Key claim

The effective shell-model operator for neutrinoless double-beta decay of 82Se, constructed via diagrammatic perturbation theory starting from the Bonn-C interaction, yields a fully corrected nuclear matrix element comparable to the bare operator result, but only because large and physically distinct low-order corrections cancel one another; this cancellation indicates the importance of a reliable nonperturbative calculation.

## Method

The authors used diagrammatic perturbation theory to construct an effective shell-model operator for neutrinoless double-beta decay, starting from the same Bonn-C nucleon-nucleon interaction used to generate the Hamiltonian in recent shell-model calculations. They first summed high-energy ladder diagrams nonperturbatively to account for short-range correlations, producing a renormalized operator M_high, then added low-order diagrams in the G matrix to account for longer-range correlations. The two-body matrix elements of the resulting effective operator were folded with transition densities from a shell-model calculation of 82Se decaying to 82Kr, using the valence space f5/2pg9/2, oscillator parameter b=82^(1/6) MeV, and average energy E-bar = 10.08 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The bare Gamow-Teller matrix element M^0nu_GT for 82Se → 82Kr is 3.33 (dimensionless, from Eq. 1 with nuclear radius R).
- After including only high-energy ladder diagrams (short-range correlations), the matrix element reduces to 3.07 at 4 hbar-omega boundary, a reduction of about 8%.
- After adding ladder and 4-particle-2-hole diagrams, the matrix element increases to 5.39 at 4 hbar-omega boundary, an increase of about 75% relative to the short-range-corrected value.
- The fully corrected matrix element including core-polarization diagrams at 8 hbar-omega boundary is 3.35, marginally larger than the bare value of 3.33.
- The total M_0nu including Fermi term at 8 hbar-omega is 3.95, versus the bare value 3.78.
- For the full operator including form factors, the short-range correlation reduction is less than 3%.

## Context

This work extends the systematic effective-operator techniques previously applied to shell-model Hamiltonians (Caurier et al., 2005) to the neutrinoless double-beta-decay operator for the first time. The results contradict the long-standing phenomenological Jastrow-function approach (Miller and Spencer, 1976) for treating short-range correlations, which the authors find overestimates quenching; their results instead agree with coupled-cluster calculations (Simkovic et al., 2009) using the Argonne V18 potential. The near cancellation between large pairing and neutron-proton correlation effects suggests that the agreement between shell-model and QRPA matrix elements may improve with proper renormalization, but the convergence issues with core-polarization diagrams at high energy (beyond 8 hbar-omega) indicate that a fully nonperturbative treatment is needed for reliable predictions.
