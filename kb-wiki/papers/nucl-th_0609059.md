# Hard Probes at LHC

> This talk gives a theoretical perspective of the physics issues awaiting us when heavy ions will collide in the LHC.

# Hard Probes at LHC

## Key claim
The quark-gluon plasma produced at LHC energies may be qualitatively different from that observed at RHIC, possibly a weakly coupled gaseous plasma rather than the strongly coupled fluid seen at RHIC, but lattice gauge calculations show no evidence for a second transition point above T_c, so this speculation lacks a solid theoretical basis and a conservative expectation is that LHC will produce similar but hotter matter.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical expectations for hard probes in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV at the LHC, comparing predictions from saturation models, classical color field dynamics, and k_T-factorization for initial conditions, and examining the perturbative QCD factorization framework for high-p_T hadrons, jets, heavy quarks, quarkonia, and electromagnetic probes. The author evaluates the readiness of theoretical frameworks developed at RHIC to describe LHC data, focusing on the extraction of the jet quenching parameter q̂ and the modeling of medium-modified fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers
- Charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity for Pb+Pb: dN_ch/dy|_{y=0} ≈ 2000 ± 500
- Initial energy density at τ_i = 0.2 fm/c: ε_i ≈ 200 GeV/fm^3; at τ = 0.5 fm/c: 60 GeV/fm^3
- Equilibrated plasma temperature at these energy densities: 600 MeV and 450 MeV respectively
- Saturation scale estimate for LHC: Q_s ≈ 2 GeV/c
- Charm production increase factor compared to RHIC: 10; bottom production increase factor: 100
- Predicted yields per central Pb+Pb collision: about 100 c-quark pairs and 5 b-quark pairs
- Jet rate at design luminosity: about 20 jets with E_T > 100 GeV per second
- W-boson rate: about 1 per second; Z-boson rate: about 1 every three seconds
- Extracted q̂ values from RHIC data range from 0.5 to 15 GeV^2/fm
- Predicted R_AA for central collisions at LHC varies from about 0.1 at p_T ~ 10 GeV/c to about 0.4 for p_T > 100 GeV/c (Vitev et al.) versus roughly constant R_AA ≈ 0.15 (Eskola et al.)

## Context
This talk reviews the theoretical landscape for the LHC heavy ion program two years before its start, building on the RHIC program that provided evidence for energy densities exceeding the QCD critical density of about 1 GeV/fm^3 and discovered a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma. The paper extends the "yellow book" publications covering nuclear effects on parton distribution functions (Accardi 2004), jets (Accardi 2004), heavy flavors (Bedjidian 2004), and electromagnetic probes (Arleo 2004), and identifies open questions about whether the LHC plasma will be qualitatively different from RHIC's or simply hotter, with the need to fill gaps in theoretical frameworks for heavy quarkonia and to make quantitative predictions using state-of-the-art evolution models.
