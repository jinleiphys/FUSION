# Covariance analysis for Energy Density Functionals and instabilities

> We present the covariance analysis of two successful nuclear energy density functionals, (i) a non-relativistic Skyrme functional built from a zero-range effective interaction, and (ii) a relativistic nuclear energy density functional based on density dependent meson-nucleon couplings. The covariance analysis is a useful tool for understanding the limitations of a model, the correlations between observables and the statistical errors. We show, for our selected test nucleus 208Pb, that when the constraint on a property A included in the fit is relaxed, correlations with other observables B become larger; on the other hand, when a strong constraint is imposed on A, the correlations with other properties become very small. We also provide a brief review, partly connected with the covariance analysis, of some instabilities displayed by several energy density functionals currently used in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the strength of correlations between nuclear observables predicted by energy density functionals depends critically on how tightly a given property is constrained in the fitting protocol: relaxing a constraint on a property increases its correlations with other observables, while imposing a strong constraint reduces those correlations. This behavior is shown for the test nucleus 208Pb.

## Method

The authors performed a covariance analysis of two nuclear energy density functionals: a non-relativistic Skyrme functional (SLy5-min) and a relativistic covariant functional with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (DDME-min1). They computed the curvature matrix from first derivatives of the chi-squared function with respect to model parameters, then derived the covariance and Pearson correlation matrices for both parameters and predicted observables. For the Skyrme functional, they also constructed two variants (SLy5-a and SLy5-b) with modified constraints on the neutron matter equation of state and the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb to study sensitivity of correlations.

## Context

This work extends previous covariance analyses of nuclear energy density functionals performed by Kortelainen (2010), Reinhard (2010, 2013a, 2013b), Fattoyev (2011, 2012), Piekarewicz (2012), Erler (2013), and Dobaczewski (2014). The paper also provides a brief review of instabilities displayed by several currently used energy density functionals, noting that while a direct connection between instabilities and covariance analysis has not been proven, instabilities should serve as a warning when building new functionals. The results enable improved understanding of which observables (such as isovector giant resonances) can best constrain the symmetry energy parameters L and S_2(rho_0), and highlight that additional nuclear matter constraints appear necessary in fitting protocols for relativistic functionals to reduce uncertainties in the isovector channel.
