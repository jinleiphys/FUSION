# Charmed and Bottom Baryons: a Variational Approach based on Heavy Quark Symmetry

> The use of Heavy Quark Symmetry to study bottom and charmed baryons leads to important simplifications of the non-relativistic three body problem, which turns out to be easily solved by a simple variational ansatz. Our simple scheme reproduces previous results (baryon masses, charge and mass radii, $...$) obtained by solving the Faddeev equations with simple non-relativistic quark--quark potentials, adjusted to the light and heavy--light meson spectra. Wave functions, parameterized in a simple manner, are also given and thus they can be easily used to compute further observables. Our method has been also used to find the predictions for strangeness-less baryons of the SU(2) chirally inspired quark-quark interactions. We find that the one pion exchange term of the chirally inspired interactions leads to relative changes of the $Λ_b$ and $Λ_c$ binding energies as large as 90%.

## Key claim

Heavy Quark Symmetry (HQS) simplifies the non-relativistic three-body problem for charmed and bottom baryons enough that a simple variational ansatz reproduces results previously obtained from solving Faddeev equations, but the authors note that the one-pion exchange term of chirally inspired interactions leads to relative changes of the Lambda_b and Lambda_c binding energies as large as 90 percent, indicating that the choice of quark-quark interaction significantly affects the predictions.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic three-body intrinsic Hamiltonian for baryons containing one heavy quark (c or b) and two light quarks (u, d, or s) using a variational wave function with a Jastrow-type spatial form: a product of single-particle s-wave ground states from the heavy-light mean-field Hamiltonian, modified by a linear factor at large distances, multiplied by a light-light correlation function built from a linear combination of Gaussians. They tested five phenomenological quark-quark potentials (BD, AL1, AL2, AP1, AP2) previously fitted to meson spectra, and also constructed a hybrid potential (AL1chi) that uses the SU(2) chirally inspired light-light interaction from Fernandez (1993) combined with the AL1 heavy-light interaction, to study the effects of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in strangeness-less heavy baryons.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev equation calculations of Silvestre-Brac (1996) for heavy baryon masses and static properties, showing that a simpler variational method yields comparable results while providing manageable wave functions for computing further observables. The authors also apply for the first time the SU(2) chirally inspired quark-quark interaction of Fernandez (1993), previously successful in the meson sector, to predict strangeness-less heavy baryon masses, enabling future studies of how spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking affects heavy baryon structure. The method enables straightforward calculation of additional observables such as charge form factors and magnetic moments beyond the masses and radii examined here.
