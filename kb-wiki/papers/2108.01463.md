# Collective states of even-even nuclei in gamma-rigid quadrupole Hamiltonian with Minimal Length under the sextic potential

> In the present paper, we study the collective states of even even nuclei in gamma rigid mode within the sextic potential and the Minimal Length (ML) formalism in Bohr Mottelson model. The eigenvalues problem for this latter is solved by means conjointly of Quasi-Exact Solvability (QES) and a Quantum Perturbation Method (QPM). Numerical calculations are performed for 35 nuclei:(98 108)Ru, (100 102)Mo, (116 130)Xe, (180 196)Pt, (172)Os, (146 150)Nd, (132 134)Ce, (154)Gd, (156)Dy and (150 152)Sm. Through this study, it appears that our elaborated model leads to an improved agreement of the theoretical results with the corresponding experimental data by reducing the rms with a rate going up to 63% for some nuclei. This comes out from the fact that we have combined the sextic potential, which is a very useful phenomenological potential, with the formalism of the ML which is based on the generalized uncertainty principle and which is in turn a quantum concept widely used in quantum physics. Besides, we investigate the effect of ML on energy ratios, transition rates, moments of inertia and a shape phase transition for the most numerous isotopic chains, namely Ru, Xe, Nd and Pt.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining the sextic potential with the Minimal Length (ML) formalism in the gamma-rigid Bohr-Mottelson model improves agreement with experimental data by reducing the root-mean-square deviation by up to 63% for some nuclei, but this improvement is conditional on the smallness of the ML parameter and the validity of the first-order perturbation approximation used.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian in the gamma-rigid mode (gamma fixed to 0 degrees) with a sextic potential, first using Quasi-Exact Solvability (QES) for the unperturbed case (alpha=0), then applying a Quantum Perturbation Method (QPM) to first order in the Minimal Length parameter alpha to obtain corrected energy spectra and wave functions. Numerical calculations are performed for 35 nuclei across multiple isotopic chains, and the model is fitted to experimental data by varying two free parameters (rho and kappa, where kappa = sqrt(a)*alpha is the new ML parameter).

## Key numbers

* The ML parameter kappa takes values of 0, 0.001, 0.01, and 0.1 in the theoretical analysis of energy ratios as a function of rho.
* The rms deviation between theoretical and experimental results is reduced by up to 63% for some nuclei when including the ML formalism.
* The model is applied to 35 nuclei: (98-108)Ru, (100-102)Mo, (116-130)Xe, (180-196)Pt, (172)Os, (146-150)Nd, (132-134)Ce, (154)Gd, (156)Dy, and (150-152)Sm.
* The quasi-exact solvability order k = 2 is used, which reproduces states up to 10+ in the ground band, 6+ in the first beta band, and 2+ in the second beta band.
* For higher values of the parameter rho (rho > 6) with kappa=0, the model reproduces the results of the gamma-rigid prolate harmonic vibrator X(3)-beta^2.

## Context

This work extends the X(3)-Sextic model of Batoul et al. (2021) by incorporating the Minimal Length formalism, which was previously introduced into nuclear structure through the Bohr-Mottelson model in pioneering work cited as reference [b20]. The model builds on prior gamma-rigid solutions including X(3)-MLF (infinite square well potential), X(3)-D-ML (Davidson potential), X(3)-H-ML (Hulthen potential), and X(3)-C-ML (Coulomb potential), all of which are tabulated for comparison. The sextic potential is particularly suited for describing shape phase transitions and critical points, as it can exhibit spherical minima, deformed minima, flat forms, or double minima separated by a barrier depending on the parameter rho. The work enables future application to nuclei near shape phase transition regions and can be extended to higher quasi-exact solvability orders (k > 2).
