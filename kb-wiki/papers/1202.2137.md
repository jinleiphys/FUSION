# Kadomtsev-Petviashvili equation in Relativistic Fluid Dynamics

> The Kadomtsev-Petviashvili (KP) nonlinear wave equation is the three dimensional generalization of the Korteveg-de Vries (KdV) equation. We show how to obtain the cylindrical KP (cKP) and cartesian KP in relativistic fluid dynamics. The obtained KP equations describe the evolution of perturbations in the baryon density in a strongly interacting quark gluon plasma (sQGP) at zero temperature. We also show the analytical solitary wave solution of the KP equations in both cases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Kadomtsev-Petviashvili (KP) nonlinear wave equation, which generalizes the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation to three spatial dimensions, can be derived from relativistic fluid dynamics for perturbations in baryon density in a strongly interacting quark gluon plasma (sQGP) at zero temperature, under the assumption that the perturbations have small amplitude and long wavelength with weak dependence on transverse coordinates.

## Method

The authors apply the reductive perturbation method (RPM) to the relativistic Euler equation and continuity equation for baryon density, using an equation of state derived from QCD that contains derivative terms from the coupling between quarks and massive gluons. They expand the baryon density and velocity components in powers of a small parameter sigma in both cylindrical and cartesian coordinates, then collect terms order by order to derive the cylindrical KP (cKP) and cartesian KP equations. The stretched coordinate transformations are chosen to capture the balance between nonlinearity and dispersion that leads to solitary wave solutions.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound in the sQGP is given by: c_s^2 = [(27g^2 rho_0^2)/(8 m_G^2) + pi^(2/3) rho_0^(4/3)] / [(27g^2 rho_0^2)/(8 m_G^2) + 3 pi^(2/3) rho_0^(4/3)]
- In the limit m_G -> infinity, the speed of sound becomes c_s^2 = 1/3 and the KdV equation reduces to a breaking wave equation: partial rho_1/partial t + c_s partial rho_1/partial x + (2/3) c_s rho_1 partial rho_1/partial x = 0
- The quark degeneracy factor is gamma_Q = 2 (spin) x 3 (flavor) = 6
- The baryon density is related to Fermi momentum by: rho_B = (gamma_Q/(6 pi^2)) k_F^3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous one-dimensional study (Fogaca, Navarra, Ferreira Filho, 2011) where a KdV equation was derived for baryon density perturbations in cold sQGP, now generalizing to three dimensions via the KP equation. The reductive perturbation method follows the formalism developed by Taniuti and others for nonlinear wave propagation in dispersive media. The results enable the study of three-dimensional solitary waves in relativistic heavy ion collisions and compact star interiors, where transverse perturbations in the quark-gluon plasma may generate three-dimensional solitary structures.
