# Observation of the collective flow in proton-proton collisions

> The scenario of a collective expansion of matter created in proton-proton (p-p) collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) is discussed. Assuming a small transverse size and a formation time of 0.1fm/c of the source we observe the build up of a substantial transverse flow in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations. In order to demonstrate the collectivity in p-p collisions we propose to look at the multiplicity dependence of the elliptic flow coefficient. If high multiplicity events originate from azimuthally asymmetric events containing two flux tubes, an observable signal above the statistical fluctuations in the measured elliptic flow could appear.

# Observation of the collective flow in proton-proton collisions

## Key claim
If high-multiplicity events in proton-proton collisions at the LHC originate from azimuthally asymmetric configurations containing two flux tubes (strings), a measurable elliptic flow signal above statistical fluctuations could appear, demonstrating collectivity in these small systems. The claim is conditional on the existence of such two-string events and on the assumption that the fireball has a small transverse size and a formation time of 0.1 fm/c.

## Method
The author performed ideal relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of a small fireball with Bjorken scaling flow in the longitudinal direction and a Gaussian energy density profile (sigma = 0.5 fm) in the transverse plane, using a realistic equation of state and the THERMINATOR statistical emission code for freeze-out and resonance decays. For two-string events, the energy density was taken as the sum of two Gaussian profiles separated by 0.7–0.9 fm, and the elliptic flow coefficient was calculated from the simulated particle distributions using the reaction plane method.

## Key numbers
- Initial formation time: 0.1 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: 140 MeV
- Central energy density for one-string events: epsilon_0 = 63 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to a central temperature of 450 MeV
- Fireball lifetime from hydrodynamic evolution: approximately 2 fm/c
- Average transverse velocity acquired: about 0.5 c
- Charged particle multiplicity in one-string events: 45 ± 7
- Eccentricity of two-string energy distributions: 0.2–0.29 (for separations d = 0.7–0.9 fm)
- Fraction of events assumed to have two strings: 20%
- HBT radii from the hydrodynamic model: approximately 1.5 fm
- For one-string events, sqrt(N) v_2 is approximately 0.95

## Context
This work extends the application of relativistic hydrodynamics from heavy-ion collisions (where collective flow is well established) to the much smaller system of proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. It contrasts with earlier approaches that treated p-p collisions as simple superpositions of independent particle emissions, and it builds on the constituent quark model and string model of particle production (Bialas 1977, Bjorken 1983). The paper proposes a specific experimental signature, the multiplicity dependence of elliptic flow, to distinguish collective expansion from statistical fluctuations, and it notes that a similar idea for eccentricity fluctuations with many small string-like objects appeared in Casalderrey-Solana (2009) after this work was completed. The results enable experimental searches for collectivity in high-multiplicity p-p events at the LHC.
