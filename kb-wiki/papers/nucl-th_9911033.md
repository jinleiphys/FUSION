# Sensitivity of tensor analyzing power in the process $d+p\to d+X$ to the longitudinal isoscalar form factor of the Roper resonance electroexcitation

> The tensor analyzing power of the process $d + p \to d + X$, for forward deuteron scattering in the momentum interval 3.7 to 9 GeV/c, is studied in the framework of $ω$ exchange in an algebraic collective model for the electroexcitation of nucleon resonances. We point out a special sensitivity of the tensor analyzing power to the isoscalar longitudinal form factor of the Roper resonance excitation. The main argument is that the $S_{11}(1535)$, $D_{13}(1520)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ resonances have only isovector longitudinal form factors. It is the longitudinal form factor of the Roper excitation, which plays an important role in the $t-$dependence of the tensor analyzing power. We discuss possible evidence of swelling of hadrons with increasing excitation energy.

## Key claim

The tensor analyzing power T20 in the process d + p → d + X is especially sensitive to the isoscalar longitudinal form factor of the Roper resonance (P11(1440)) excitation, because the other resonances in the 1.4–1.6 GeV mass region (S11(1535), D13(1520), S11(1650)) have only isovector longitudinal form factors and therefore cannot contribute to the isoscalar ratio r = σL/σT that drives the t-dependence of T20.

## Method

The authors study the tensor analyzing power T20 for forward deuteron scattering in the reaction d + p → d + X using an ω-exchange model, where the ω-meson acts as an isoscalar virtual photon via vector dominance. The ratio r = σL/σT of longitudinal to transverse isoscalar photon absorption cross sections is calculated using the helicity amplitudes for nucleon resonance electroexcitation from an algebraic collective model (Bijker, Iachello, Leviatan 1994, 1996) that assumes SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry and describes baryons as Y-shaped string configurations. The t-dependence of T20 is then computed from the deuteron electromagnetic form factors and the resonance contributions, including the effects of finite resonance widths via Breit-Wigner functions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron momentum interval studied: 3.7 to 9 GeV/c
- The ratio r = σL/σT that best fitted earlier data was r = 0.1 (from Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson 1996)
- The scale parameter of the charge distribution in the collective model: a = 0.232 fm
- The constituent mass used: m = 0.336 GeV
- The magnetic moment parameter: μ = 0.127 GeV−1 (g-factor g = 1)
- The longitudinal isoscalar form factor of the Roper resonance has a zero at k² = 1/(2a²)
- The tensor analyzing power T20 takes the value T20 = −1/√8 when r = 0, independent of t
- The theoretical curves for T20 intersect at q ≃ 1.85 fm−1 and q ≃ 5 fm−1 (where q = √(−t))
- The minimum in T20 occurs in the region |t| ≃ 0.35 GeV²
- The vector polarization transfer coefficient K_y^{y'} shows strong sensitivity to r for q ≥ 3 fm−1

## Context

This work extends the earlier ω-exchange model of Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson (1996), which treated the ratio r as a free parameter independent of momentum transfer, by incorporating the t-dependent predictions of the algebraic collective model for nucleon resonance electroexcitation developed by Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994, 1996). The paper shows that hadronic probes with polarized particles, such as p(d,d')X, can provide information on isoscalar form factors of N* excitations that is complementary to inelastic electron-nucleon scattering. The results enable future measurements at Jefferson Lab (proposal E-94-018) to further constrain the transition radius of the Roper resonance and test the hypothesis of hadron swelling with increasing excitation energy through the stretchability parameter ξ.
