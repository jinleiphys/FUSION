# Dynamical transition between two mesons and a tetraquark

> We consider a system composed of two identical light quarks ($qq$) and two identical antiquarks ($\bar Q\bar Q$) that can be linked either as two mesons or as a tetraquark, incorporating quantum correlations between identical particles and an effective many-body potential between particles. We perform a 3-D Monte Carlo simulation of the system, considering the configurations allowed to form: i) Only two mesons, ii) Only tetraquark and iii) two mesons and tetraquark . We characterize each case and determine whether it is energetically more favorable to form a tetraquark or two mesons, as a function of the interparticle separation distance which, for a fixed number of particles, can be identified as a particle density. We determine how the two mesons, which dominate the low density regime, mixes with a tetraquark state as the density increases. Properties like the mean square radius and the two-particle correlation function are found to reflect such transition, and we provide a parameterization of the diquark correlation function in the isolated case. We track the dynamical flipping among configurations to determine the recombination probability, exhibiting the importance of the tetraquark state. We analize the four-body potential evolution and show that its linear behavior is preserved, although the slope can reflect the presence of a mixed state. Results are shown for several light-quarks to heavy-antiquarks mass ratios whenever they are found to be relevant.

# Dynamical transition between two mesons and a tetraquark

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a system of two identical light quarks and two identical heavy antiquarks undergoes a dynamical transition from a two-meson configuration at low density to a tetraquark-dominated mixed state at high density, but only when the interparticle separation (interpreted as particle density) is varied; the tetraquark state is never the ground state at zero density, where its energy is always higher than that of two isolated mesons.

## Method
The authors perform a 3-D Monte Carlo simulation using the String-Flip Model with a linear confining potential, incorporating quantum correlations via a variational wave function that includes a free Fermi gas Slater determinant for identical particles. The system is placed in a cubic box of variable side length to control particle density, and the potential is chosen as the minimum among two possible meson pairings and a tetraquark Steiner-tree configuration. The variational parameter is optimized at each density, and observables are computed using Metropolis importance sampling.

## Key numbers
- For equal masses (m2/m1 = 1), the exact ground state energy of a single meson is 2.33811 (in units where m = k = 1), and the variational energy is 2.3472 (about 0.4% higher).
- The tetraquark energy in the zero-density limit for equal masses is 5.02 ± 0.02, compared to twice the meson energy of 4.6944, confirming the tetraquark is not the ground state at low density.
- The tetraquark mean square radius in the zero-density limit for equal masses is 13.9 ± 0.1 (in the same units).
- The slope of the linear tetraquark potential at zero density is B = 0.84 ± 0.02.
- The meson-meson correlation function at low density shows a separation of 4.66 r/r_M, consistent with the result of Lenz et al.

## Context
This work extends the seminal approach of Lenz et al. (1980s) who studied similar four-quark systems with harmonic potentials, by using a linear confining potential and including density-dependent effects through Monte Carlo simulation. The paper also builds on the String-Flip Model previously applied to dense matter by Piekarewicz and Ayala. The results enable quantitative predictions for tetraquark formation in meson-meson collisions and in WW decay at LEP2 energies, where the spatial separation of produced quarks is around 0.1 fm, and provide parameterizations of diquark correlation functions that can be used in future hadron spectroscopy studies.
