# Intrinsic Glue and Wilson lines within Dressed Quarks

> We construct a quark target model (QTM) to incorporate intrinsic glue into effective low-energy models of QCD, which often contain only quark degrees of freedom. This method guarantees the gauge invariance of observables order-by-order in the strong coupling. The quark and gluon PDFs for the dressed quarks are obtained in the QTM at leading order. We demonstrate gauge invariance of the results by comparing both covariant and light cone gauges, with the former including an explicit Wilson line contribution. A key finding is that in covariant gauges the Wilson line can carry a significant amount of the light cone momentum. With coupling strength $α_s = 0.5$ and dressed quark mass $M_q = 0.4\,$GeV, we find quark and gluon momentum fractions of $\left<x\right>_q = 0.81$ and $\left<x\right>_g = 0.19$, where the Wilson line contribution to the quark momentum fraction is $-0.18$. We use the on-shell renormalization scheme and find that at one-loop this Wilson line contribution does not depend on the covariant gauge but does vanish in light cone gauge as expected. This result demonstrates that it is crucial to account for Wilson line contributions when calculating quantum correlation functions in covariant gauges. We also consider the impact of a gluon mass using the gauge invariant formalism proposed by Cornwall, and combine these QTM results with two quark-level models to obtain quark and gluon PDFs for the pion.

## Key claim

The authors construct a quark target model (QTM) to incorporate intrinsic gluon degrees of freedom into effective low-energy QCD models that typically contain only quark degrees of freedom, demonstrating that in covariant gauges the Wilson line contribution to the quark PDF carries a significant amount of light cone momentum and must be included to satisfy gauge invariance and the momentum sum rule.

## Method

The QTM is formulated by taking the target state to be a dressed quark and computing its quark and gluon PDFs at one-loop order in perturbative QCD (order g²), using both covariant and light cone gauges with explicit Wilson line contributions. The Feynman diagrams for the quark PDF include a triangle diagram and two Wilson line diagrams, while the gluon PDF is given by a single diagram; all expressions are evaluated using Mellin moments and the on-shell renormalization scheme, with proper time regularization using Λ_IR = 240 MeV and Λ_UV = 645 MeV.

## Key numbers

- With coupling strength α_s = 0.5 and dressed quark mass M_q = 0.4 GeV, the quark momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_q = 0.81 and the gluon momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_g = 0.19
- The Wilson line contribution to the quark momentum fraction is −0.18 for α_s = 0.5 and M_q = 0.4 GeV
- The quark field renormalization constant in covariant gauge is Z_2 = 0.9933 at one loop for α_s = 0.5 and M_q = 0.4 GeV
- For α_s = 0.75 with M_q = 0.4 GeV, the gluon momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_g = 0.2861, approximately equal to the gluon momentum fraction found empirically for the pion
- The Mellin moments of the quark PDF become negative starting at s = 19 for the parameters used (M_q = 0.4 GeV, α_s = 0.5)
- Without the Wilson line contribution, the inferred gluon momentum fraction would be ⟨x⟩_g = 0.0133 instead of the correct value 0.1907

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach (Cloet 2013, Eichmann 2016) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Cloet 2014, Freese 2019) by providing a method to directly calculate gluonic observables in effective models that previously only contained quark degrees of freedom. The results demonstrate that previous DSE calculations of PDFs in Landau gauge (Cui 2020, Freese 2021) that ignored Wilson line contributions may have underestimated gluon momentum fractions, and the QTM can be combined with quark-level pion models to obtain quark and gluon PDFs for the pion at the model scale, enabling DGLAP evolution and comparison to experimental data.
