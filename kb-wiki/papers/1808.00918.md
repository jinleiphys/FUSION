# Electron-photon deep inelastic scattering at small x in holographic QCD

> We study the electron-photon deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken variable x in the framework of holographic QCD, employing the Pomeron exchange to describe the involved strong interaction. With the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan Pomeron exchange kernel and appropriate wave functions for the incident and target particles, which are defined in the five-dimensional AdS space, we obtain the photon structure functions. It is shown that our predictions agree with the experimental data measured at LEP and with those derived from a known parameterization of the photon parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their holographic QCD model, using the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel, produces photon structure functions that agree with LEP data and with the GRS parton distribution parameterization, but only after tuning an overall factor to fit the experimental data; the three adjustable parameters of the model were determined from previous nucleon DIS studies, making the results "predictions" only in a qualified sense.

## Method

The authors compute photon structure functions \(F_2^\gamma\) and \(F_L^\gamma\) for electron-photon deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken \(x\) using the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) Pomeron exchange kernel in five-dimensional AdS space. They employ two approaches for the target photon: one using the five-dimensional U(1) vector field wave function for a quasi-real photon, and another using the \(\rho\) meson gravitational form factor from a bottom-up AdS/QCD model to implement vector meson dominance. The density distributions for the probe photon are taken as the wave functions of the five-dimensional U(1) vector field.

## Key numbers

- The photon structure function \(F_2^\gamma(x,Q^2)\) is computed for \(Q^2\) values of 1 and 10 GeV\(^2\) and compared to OPAL data at LEP.
- The ratio \(R_{\gamma/N} = F_2^\gamma(x,Q^2) / [\alpha F_2^N(x,Q^2)]\) is shown for \(Q^2 = 1\) and 10 GeV\(^2\), with nucleon results taken from Watanabe (2013).
- The longitudinal-to-transverse ratio \(R_{L/T} = F_L(x,Q^2) / F_T(x,Q^2)\) is presented for \(Q^2 = 1\) and 10 GeV\(^2\) and as a function of \(Q^2\) at fixed \(x = 10^{-3}\).
- The Bjorken variable is defined as \(x = Q^2 / (Q^2 + W^2 + P^2)\), approximated by \(x \approx Q^2/W^2\) in the kinematic region \(W^2 \gg Q^2 \gg P^2\).

## Context

This work extends the application of holographic QCD with the BPST Pomeron kernel, previously used for nucleon DIS by Brower (2010) and Watanabe (2012, 2013), to the case of photon targets. It builds on the vector meson dominance model and uses the \(\rho\) meson gravitational form factor from Abidin (2008). The results enable future tests at linear colliders such as the International Linear Collider and support further applications of holographic QCD to high-energy scattering processes where perturbative QCD is not applicable.
