# On the definition of electromagnetic local spatial densities for composite spin-$1/2$ systems

> An unambiguous definition of the electromagnetic spatial densities for a spin-1/2 system is proposed and worked out in the zero average momentum frame and in moving frames. The obtained results are compared with the traditional definition of the densities in terms of the three-dimensional Fourier transforms of the electromagnetic form factors in the Breit frame.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an unambiguous definition of electromagnetic spatial densities for spin-1/2 systems by taking the sharp localization limit of wave packets without performing a nonrelativistic expansion, in contrast to the traditional Breit-frame Fourier transform definition which only holds when the Compton wavelength is much smaller than other length scales characterizing the system.

## Method

The authors start from matrix elements of the electromagnetic current operator between momentum eigenstates of a spin-1/2 system, parameterized by Dirac and Pauli form factors F1(q^2) and F2(q^2). They construct normalizable wave-packet states with spherically symmetric profile functions and take the limit R → 0 (sharp localization) using dimensional counting methods, without performing a 1/m expansion. The resulting densities are then generalized to moving frames by applying Lorentz boosts to the wave packets and taking the same sharp localization limit.

## Key numbers

- The Dirac form factor normalization is F1(0) = 1 and the Pauli form factor normalization is F2(0) = κ/m, where κ is the anomalous magnetic moment of the spin-1/2 particle
- The new charge density ρ1(r) is normalized to 1, while the new magnetization density ρ2(r) is normalized to (2/3)κ rather than to the magnetic moment 1 + κ as in the naive definition
- The naive charge density ρ_naive^ch(r) and the new charge density ρ1(r) are both normalized to 1

## Context

This work extends the approach of Epelbaum (2022) from spin-0 systems to spin-1/2 systems, following the conceptual critique by Jaffe (2020) that traditional Breit-frame densities only hold in the static limit of infinitely heavy particles. The paper resolves the issue that for light hadrons whose Compton wavelength is comparable to or larger than their charge radius, the traditional definition becomes problematic. The results connect to two-dimensional transverse densities in the infinite-momentum frame discussed by Burkardt (2002), Miller (2007), and Carlson (2007), and enable unambiguous definitions of electromagnetic spatial densities for nucleons and other light spin-1/2 systems.
