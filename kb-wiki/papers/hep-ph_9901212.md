# Chiral NN interactions in nuclear matter

> We consider an effective field theory of NN system in nuclear medium. The shallow bound states, which complicate the effective field theory analysis and lead to the large scattering length in the vacuum case do not exist in matter. We study whether the chiral expansion of the effective potential can be truncated in such situation. The cutoff regularization is used to render the solution of the Bethe-Goldstone equation finite. We show that the next-to-leading order terms in the chiral expansion of the effective NN potential can indeed be interpreted as corrections so that the truncation of the expansion is justified. However, it is pointed out that it is still useful to treat the problem nonperturbatively since it may allow the consideration of the nuclear systems with the density smaller that the normal nuclear matter one. The possible directions of constructing the chiral theory of NN interaction in medium are outlined.

## Key claim

The shallow bound or virtual NN states that cause large scattering lengths and complicate chiral EFT in vacuum do not exist in nuclear matter due to Pauli blocking, so the chiral expansion of the effective NN potential can be truncated with next-to-leading order terms acting as genuine corrections; however, a nonperturbative treatment remains useful for densities below normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The author solves the Bethe-Goldstone (BG) equation for the G-matrix in nuclear matter using a separable potential with parameters fitted to reproduce the experimental vacuum scattering length and effective radius, then extracts in-medium scattering length and effective radius. Using these in-medium values as "observables," the effective constants \(C_0\) and \(C_2\) from the leading and subleading orders of the chiral NN Lagrangian are determined by matching the chiral expansion of the G-matrix to the effective range expansion, and the consistency of the truncation is judged by the size of the correction to \(C_0\) when going from leading to subleading order.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum scattering length: \(a = -23.71 \pm 0.013\) fm; effective radius: \(r_e = 2.73 \pm 0.03\) fm.
- Separable potential parameters reproducing vacuum data: \(\lambda = 1.92\), \(\beta = 0.77\) fm.
- In-medium scattering length: \(a_m = -1.1\) fm; in-medium effective radius: \(r_m = 1.8\) fm (for Fermi momentum \(p_F = 1.37\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- Cutoff parameter: \(\Lambda = 1.85\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the preferred solution (\(C_2 \sim 0.63\)), the subleading-order constant \(C_0^{(1)}\) is approximately \(0.58\)–\(0.6\) times the leading-order constant \(C_0^{(0)}\), i.e., a correction of about 40%.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach to NN interactions, previously developed for vacuum by Weinberg (1991) and refined by Kaplan et al. (1998) and van Kolck (1998), into the nuclear medium by using the Bethe-Goldstone equation. It contrasts with the vacuum case where Cohen (1997) found large differences between leading and subleading \(C_0\) values, indicating inconsistency. The results suggest that a chiral G-matrix approach may enable future quantitative descriptions of nuclear matter bulk properties, though pions must still be included explicitly and a full renormalization program remains to be carried out.
