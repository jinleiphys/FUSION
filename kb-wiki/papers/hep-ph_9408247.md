# Alignment in Gamma-Hadron Families of Cosmic Rays

> Alignment of main fluxes of energy in a target plane is found in families of cosmic ray particles detected in deep lead X-ray chambers. The fraction of events with alignment is unexpectedly large for families with high energy and large number of hadrons. This can be considered as evidence for the existence of coplanar scattering of secondary particles in interaction of particles with superhigh energy, $E_0 > 10^{16}$ eV. Data analysis suggests that production of most aligned groups occurs low above the chamber and is characterized by a coplanar scattering and quasiscaling spectrum of secondaries in the fragmentation region. The most elaborated hypothesis for explanation of alignment is related to the quark-gluon string rupture. However, the problem of theoretical interpretation of our results still remains open.

## Key claim

Alignment of the main energy fluxes in gamma-hadron families of cosmic rays is observed at a rate significantly above fluctuation background, but only for families with high total gamma-component energy (above 500 TeV) and large hadron multiplicity; the effect is interpreted as evidence for coplanar scattering of secondary particles in interactions with primary energy above about 10^16 eV, though the authors state that a complete theoretical interpretation remains open.

## Method

The authors analyzed 68 gamma-hadron families recorded in deep lead X-ray emulsion chambers of the Pamir Experiment (altitude 4400 m), selecting events with total gamma-component energy ΣEγ ≥ 100 TeV, at least 3 gamma quanta, and at least 1 hadron. For each family, the four most energetic "Energy Distinguished Cores" (EDC), including electromagnetic halos, gamma-clusters, high-energy gamma quanta, and high-energy hadrons, were projected onto a common target plane, and the alignment parameter λ4 (Borisov asymmetry parameter) was computed; events with λ4 ≥ 0.8 were classified as aligned. The observed alignment fractions were compared with predictions from a quasiscaling model without any special asymmetry mechanism and with random-incidence simulations.

## Key numbers

- Fraction of families with alignment for 4 EDC at ΣEγ ≥ 500 TeV: (47 ± 17)%, compared to a fluctuation background level of about 24% for random incident points.
- Fraction of families with alignment for 3 EDC at ΣEγ ≥ 500 TeV: (61 ± 18)%.
- For families with hadron number Nh > 30, the fraction with alignment for 3 EDC reaches (83 ± 37)% and for 4 EDC (67 ± 33)%.
- Average value of ER (energy times distance from energy-weighted center) for events with alignment: 2.1 ± 0.8 GeV·km; for events without alignment: 1.8 ± 0.5 GeV·km.
- Average ratio Σp_t^∥ / Σp_t^⊥ (similar to thrust) for aligned events: ~11; for non-aligned events: ~4.
- Estimated average transverse momentum within the group of 4 EDC: ~1 GeV/c; average transverse momentum perpendicular to the alignment direction: ~0.1 GeV/c.
- Average invariant mass of the group of 4 aligned particles: <M^2> = (60^{+120}_{-60}) GeV^2; for 6 aligned particles: (150 ± 150) GeV^2.
- Combined significance of deviation from background for the two highest energy bins (300–500 TeV and ≥500 TeV) yields a confidence level of about 99% by the χ² criterion.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by the Pamir Collaboration (1985) on aligned multiple halos in superfamilies, which were based on a small number of events with electromagnetic halos in carbon chambers. The present study uses a larger sample from deep lead chambers and introduces the more general concept of Energy Distinguished Cores (EDC) to include hadronic halos, gamma-clusters, and individual high-energy particles, allowing analysis at lower energies and with better statistics. The results contradict predictions from conventional quasiscaling models (e.g., the MSF model) that do not include any special asymmetry mechanism, and they suggest a threshold-like onset of coplanar scattering around 10^16 eV. The authors note that the effect may be related to semi-hard double inelastic diffraction and quark-gluon string rupture (Roizen hypothesis), but they emphasize that no complete theoretical interpretation exists; the work motivates further accelerator tests at FNAL energies and continued cosmic-ray studies to clarify the interaction mechanism.
