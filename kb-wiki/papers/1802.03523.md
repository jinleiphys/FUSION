# Real-time evolution method and its application to 3$α$ cluster system

> A new theoretical method is proposed to describe the ground and excited cluster states of atomic nuclei. The method utilizes the equation-of-motion of the Gaussian wave packets to generate the basis wave functions having various cluster configurations. The generated basis wave functions are superposed to diagonalize the Hamiltonian. In other words, this method uses the real time as the generator coordinate. The application to the $3α$ system as a benchmark shows that the new method works efficiently and yields the result consistent with or better than the other cluster models. Brief discussion on the structure of the excited $0^+$ and $1^-$ states is also made.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new theoretical method (Real-time Evolution Method, REM) that uses the real-time evolution of Gaussian wave packets governed by an equation-of-motion to generate basis wave functions for describing ground and excited cluster states, with the caveat that the method's accuracy for highly excited broad resonances requires an additional r²-constraint to remove contamination from non-resonant wave functions.

## Method

The method applies the time-dependent variational principle to Brink-Bloch wave functions of alpha clusters to derive equations-of-motion for the complex centroid parameters of the Gaussian wave packets. Starting from an initial wave function prepared by imaginary-time evolution at a fixed intrinsic excitation energy (25 MeV), the real-time evolution is solved for up to 6000 fm/c to generate an ensemble of basis wave functions. These basis functions are then superposed after parity and angular momentum projection using the generator coordinate method, with the real time serving as the generator coordinate, and an r²-constraint is applied to separate resonant from non-resonant states.

## Key numbers

- The minimum intrinsic energy obtained by imaginary-time evolution is -74.5 MeV for the 3α system.
- The real-time evolution uses a time step of Δt = 0.02 fm/c with maximal propagation time T_max = 6000 fm/c.
- The rebound condition for alpha clusters is applied when any cluster exceeds R_max = 10.0 fm.
- Basis wave functions with overlap greater than O_max = 0.75 with past wave functions are removed.
- The cut-off radius r²_cut is varied from (5.0 fm)² to (7.0 fm)² for the r²-constraint.
- The ground state energy is -7.6 MeV (from 3α threshold) with radius 2.4 fm.
- The Hoyle state (0⁺₂) energy is 0.3 MeV with radius 3.7 fm.
- The electric monopole transition from ground state to Hoyle state is 6.4 e fm².
- The monopole transition from Hoyle state to 0⁺₃ state is 28 e fm² (6.2 Weisskopf units).

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method (GCM) framework of Hill (1953) and Griffin (1957) by introducing real-time evolution as an alternative to stochastic sampling (Suzuki 1998, Itagaki 2003) and imaginary-time development (Fukuoka 2013) for generating basis wave functions. The method is benchmarked against the 3α system (¹²C) and compared with resonating group method (RGM) results from Fujiwara (1980) and Kamimura (1981) and Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function results from Funaki (2015, 2016). The approach enables future studies of multi-alpha condensates (5α and beyond) and cluster states in neutron-rich isotopes that have been difficult to treat with microscopic models.
