# Diquark Correlations from Nucleon Charge Radii

> We argue that precise measurements of charge and magnetic radii can meaningfully constrain diquark models of the nucleon. We construct properly symmetrized, nonrelativistic three-quark wave functions that interpolate between the limits of a pointlike diquark pair and no diquark correlation. We find that good fits to the data can be obtained for a wide range of diquark sizes, provided that the diquark wave functions are close to those that reduce to a purely scalar state in the pointlike limit. A modest improvement in the experimental uncertainties will render a fit to the charge radii a more telling diagnostic for the presence of spatially correlated quark pairs within the nucleon.

## Key claim

Good fits to the nucleon charge and magnetic radii data can be obtained for a wide range of diquark sizes, provided that the diquark wave functions are close to those that reduce to a purely scalar state in the pointlike limit; however, the current experimental uncertainties do not yet allow a definitive statement on whether small diquark correlations exist within the nucleon.

## Method

The authors constructed completely antisymmetrized, nonrelativistic three-quark wave functions in Jacobi coordinates that interpolate continuously between the limits of a pointlike diquark pair and no diquark correlation. They computed the electric and magnetic form factors and their associated charge radii for both dipole and Gaussian momentum-space wave functions, performing chi-squared fits to the ratios of squared charge radii using data from the review of Hyde-Wright and de Jager (2004).

## Key numbers

- Proton electric charge radius: \( r_{Ep} = 0.895 \pm 0.018 \) fm (from Sick, 2003)
- Proton magnetic radius: \( r_{Mp} = 0.855 \pm 0.035 \) fm (from Sick, 2003)
- Neutron electric charge radius squared: \( r_{En}^2 = -0.119 \pm 0.003 \) fm\(^2\) (from Kopecky et al., 1997)
- Neutron magnetic radius: \( r_{Mn} = 0.87 \pm 0.01 \) fm (from Kubon et al., 2002)
- Best fit for dipole model: \(\alpha/\beta = 0.266\), \(\cos\theta = 0.917\), yielding \( r_{Ep} = 0.895 \) fm, \( r_{Mp} = 0.896 \) fm, \( r_{Mn} = 0.862 \) fm, \( r_{En}^2 = -0.119 \) fm\(^2\)
- Best fit for Gaussian model: \(\alpha/\beta = 0.360\), \(\cos\theta = 0.927\), yielding \( r_{Ep} = 0.895 \) fm, \( r_{Mp} = 0.897 \) fm, \( r_{Mn} = 0.863 \) fm, \( r_{En}^2 = -0.119 \) fm\(^2\)
- In the small-diquark limit, the neutron electric charge radius squared is negative only for \(|\cos\theta| > 1/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.7\)

## Context

This work extends earlier diquark-quark model studies of nucleon form factors by Jakob et al. (1993), which treated the diquark as a distinct state with its own intrinsic form factor, by instead starting from a three-quark state with fully antisymmetrized wave functions that can continuously interpolate between correlated and uncorrelated quark pairs. The results suggest that a fully relativistic extension of this approach is well motivated, and that improved experimental precision on the charge radii could determine whether small diquark solutions are favored without reference to higher-momentum form factor behavior.
