# Magnetic and density effects on the nucleon axial coupling

> Using appropriate QCD finite energy sum rules, we discuss the influence of an external magnetic field and baryonic density on the axial-vector coupling constant $g_{A}$. This scenario corresponds to a magnetar environment. We found that $g_{A}$ decreases both as function of the magnetic field strength and the baryonic density. It turns out that at the nuclear density $ρ_{0}$ the axial-vector coupling takes the value $g_{A}^{*} \approx 0.92$. Although $g_{A}$ decreases in general with the magnetic field intensity, $g_{A}^{*}$ does not change in a relevant way with the magnetic field.

## Key claim

Using QCD finite energy sum rules (FESR) in a magnetar environment, the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) decreases with both baryonic density and magnetic field strength; however, at nuclear density \(\rho_0\) the value \(g_A^* \approx 0.92\) is essentially unchanged by the magnetic field, despite the general quenching of \(g_A\) with field intensity.

## Method

The authors employ double finite energy sum rules (FESR) for a three-point correlator involving proton, axial-vector, and neutron currents, together with two-point nucleon-nucleon FESR for the proton and neutron. They extract the in-medium nucleon-current couplings \(\lambda_p, \lambda_n\) and the continuum thresholds \(s_p, s_n\) as functions of baryonic density \(\rho_B\) and external magnetic field \(B\), using Ioffe’s nucleon interpolating currents and a weak-field expansion for fermion propagators. The axial coupling is then obtained from \(g_A = s_n^3 / (48\pi^4 \lambda_n \lambda_p)\), with the condition that \(s_n < s_p\) always holds.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3} = 1.22\times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{GeV}^3\), the axial-vector coupling is \(g_A^* \approx 0.92\).
* The vacuum value of \(g_A\) is approximately \(1.275\) (used as experimental input).
* The density-dependent quark condensate is \(\langle\bar qq\rangle = \langle\bar qq\rangle_0 [1 - 0.329(\rho_B/\rho_0)]\).
* The density-dependent gluon condensate is \(\langle G^2\rangle = \langle G^2\rangle_0 [1 - 0.066(\rho_B/\rho_0)]\).
* The in-medium nucleon mass is \(m_N^* = m_N [1 - 0.329(\rho_B/\rho_0)]\).
* The quark energy-momentum tensor component is \(\langle \Theta_q\rangle = 2.6\times 10^{-4} (\rho_B/\rho_0)\) and the gluon component is \(\langle \Theta_g\rangle = 6.1\times 10^{-5} (\rho_B/\rho_0)\).
* For \(\rho_B \lesssim 1.25\rho_0\), \(g_A\) decreases with magnetic field; for \(\rho_B \gtrsim 1.25\rho_0\), \(g_A\) increases with magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum-rule studies of nucleon properties in medium, particularly the quenching of \(g_A\) in nuclear matter noted by Rho (1974), Wilkinson (1974), Brown and Rho (1978), and others. It provides the first combined treatment of baryonic density and external magnetic field relevant to magnetar interiors, using the FESR framework previously applied by the same group (Villavicencio et al., 2022). The result that \(g_A^* \approx 0.92\) is insensitive to the magnetic field supports the common assumption that \(g_A^* \lesssim 1\) in neutron-star matter, and enables more reliable estimates of neutrino emissivity via the URCA process in magnetars.
