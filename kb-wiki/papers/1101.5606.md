# QCD in neutron stars and strange stars

> This paper provides an overview of the possible role of Quantum Chromo Dynamics (QDC) for neutron stars and strange stars. The fundamental degrees of freedom of QCD are quarks, which may exist as unconfined (color superconducting) particles in the cores of neutron stars. There is also the theoretical possibility that a significantly large number of up, down, and strange quarks may settle down in a new state of matter known as strange quark matter, which, by hypothesis, could be more stable than atomic nuclei. In the latter case new classes of self-bound, color superconducting objects, ranging from strange quark nuggets to strange quark stars, should exist. The properties of such objects will be reviewed along with the possible existence of deconfined quarks in neutron stars. Implications for observational astrophysics are pointed out.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) may play a central role in neutron stars and strange stars, but the existence of deconfined quark matter in neutron star cores and the hypothesis that strange quark matter could be more stable than atomic nuclei remain theoretical possibilities rather than established facts.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical models for the nuclear equation of state using a variational ansatz that parameterizes the stiffness or softness of matter at high densities, constrained by general relativity, microscopic stability, and causality. It then surveys the theoretical properties of quark matter in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) and non-CFL phases, and examines how strange quark matter nuggets would affect pycnonuclear reaction rates in neutron star crusts.

## Key numbers

- The variational upper limit on neutron star masses is close to 2.9 solar masses, with central densities up to four times nuclear matter density.
- The neutron star PSR J1614-2230 has a mass of 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses and rotates at 3.15 milliseconds; its central density could lie between 2 and 10 times nuclear matter density.
- Nucleons may begin to dissolve at densities between 2 and 10 times nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³).
- The strange quark mass is approximately 150 MeV.
- For ordinary (NCFL) strangelets, the charge is approximately Z ≈ 0.1 m₁₅₀² A for A ≪ 10³, and Z ≈ 8 m₁₅₀² A^(1/3) for A ≫ 10³, where m₁₅₀ ≡ m_s / 150 MeV.
- For CFL strangelets, the charge-to-mass ratio is Z ≈ 0.3 m₁₅₀ A^(2/3).
- The surface electric field of strange quark stars is on the order of 10^(17-19) V/cm, which can increase the stellar mass by up to 15%.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed by Witten (1984) on strange quark matter stability and by Alcock, Farhi, and Olinto (1986, 1988) on strange star properties. It builds on earlier work by Glen (1992) on variational limits for neutron star central densities and by Madsen (2001) on strangelet charge-to-mass ratios. The paper enables future observational searches for signatures of quark deconfinement in pulsar timing and cooling behavior, and motivates further study of pycnonuclear reactions in neutron star crusts as probes of strange quark matter nuggets.
