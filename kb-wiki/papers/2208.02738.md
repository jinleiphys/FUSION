# Surface energy of magnetized superconducting matter in the neutron star cores

> In this paper, an effective field theory for proton superconductor (SC) interacting with neutron superfluid (SF), both with scalar order parameters, is developed and applied to the surface energy (SE) of a magnetized SC body in neutron stars (NS). Essentially, the SE studied here differs from the nuclear SE: here, the proton SF density decays to zero while the total proton density is constant across the surface. Interactions between the condensates are parameterized phenomenologically and their effects determined from calculations of a planar SE as the ranges of parameters are varied. The critical Ginzburg-Landau (GL) parameter $κ_c$ which renders the SE equal to zero is found analytically by noting that in a system with vanishing SE the thermodynamic critical MF is equivalent to the upper critical MF. In the case of weak coupling, $κ_c$ is shown to be a linear function of SF-SF density-density coupling, in agreement with the earlier results based on asymptotic intervortex interactions. Numerical simulations corroborate our analytical predictions. Coupling due to the mixed term arising from a scalar product of gradients of the SF densities, which had been considered in the earlier literature, is seen to have practically no effect on the superconductivity type. However, this coupling does produce a frozen wave packet of the SF neutron density localized at the surface. It is shown that the leading contribution from the gradient coupling arises from a novel mixed quantum pressure term, but still does not affect the planar SE. The present calculations provide an initial map of superconductivity types in the phenomenological effective field theory and will serve as a landmark for future studies, which require microscopic calculations of the coupling parameters introduced here phenomenologically.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the critical Ginzburg-Landau parameter κc which determines the superconductivity type in neutron star matter can be modified by the neutron-proton density-density coupling, but only under the condition that the two condensates have equal critical temperatures and the system is studied near that temperature where the Ginzburg-Landau expansion is valid.

## Method

The author develops a two-component Ginzburg-Landau effective field theory for a proton superconductor interacting with a neutron superfluid, both described by scalar order parameters, and applies it to compute the surface energy of a planar interface between superconducting and normal phases in a magnetic field. The interactions between condensates are parameterized phenomenologically through density-density coupling, gradient coupling (λnp), and momentum coupling (entrainment nnp), and the critical GL parameter κc is found analytically by equating the thermodynamic critical magnetic field to the upper critical magnetic field at the point where surface energy vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The critical GL parameter for a single-component superconductor is κc = 1/√2, while in neutron star matter the GL parameter is around 3 (Baym, Pethick and Pines, 1969)
- The proton fraction in nuclear matter is xp ∼ 5% (Baym, Bethe and Pethick, 1971)
- The ratio of proton to neutron superfluid densities η = np0/nn0 is expected to take values between 0 and +∞
- In the weak coupling regime, κc is shown to be a linear function of the SF-SF density-density coupling

## Context

This work extends the single-component analysis of superconductivity type in neutron stars by Baym, Pethick and Pines (1969) to include interactions between proton and neutron condensates. It provides an independent investigation of parametric transitions between superconductivity types, complementing the vortex-vortex asymptotic interaction approach of Alford and Good (2008). The results provide an initial map of superconductivity types in the phenomenological effective field theory and serve as a landmark for future microscopic calculations of the coupling parameters introduced here phenomenologically.
