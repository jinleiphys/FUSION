# eta-prime Meson Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions Near The Threshold

> The production of $η'$ mesons in the reactions $pp\to ppη'$ and $pn\to pnη' $ at threshold-near energies is analyzed within a covariant effective meson-nucleon theory. The description of cross section and angular distributions of the available data in this kinematical region in the $pp$ channel is accomplished by including meson currents and nucleon currents with the resonances $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{11}(1710)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$. Predictions for the $pn$ channel are given. The di-electron production from subsequent $η'$ Dalitz decay $η' \to γγ^* \toγe^+e^-$ is also calculated and numerical results are presented for intermediate energy and kinematics of possible experiments with HADES, CLAS and KEK-PS.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the available near-threshold data for $\eta'$ production in $pp\to pp\eta'$ can be described by including only the known four-star resonances $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{11}(1710)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$ together with meson conversion currents, contrary to earlier claims that missing resonances are required; however, the authors note that various sets of parameters with different numbers of known and missing resonances can fit the data equally well, meaning the data are too scarce to determine unambiguously the contributing resonances.

## Method

The authors employ a covariant effective meson-nucleon theory with tree-level Feynman diagrams, including nucleon currents, meson conversion currents (via $\omega$ and $\rho$ exchange), and resonance currents from $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{11}(1710)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$. Coupling constants are fixed from known decay widths, SU(3) symmetry, vector meson dominance, and previous analyses, with monopole or dipole form factors at vertices; initial-state interaction is treated as a constant reduction factor ($\zeta=0.277$ for $^1P_1$ and $\zeta=0.243$ for $^3P_0$ waves) and final-state interaction via the Jost function formalism. The Dalitz decay $\eta'\to\gamma e^+e^-$ is then calculated from the same production amplitudes using a factorized cross section formula.

## Key numbers

- Threshold kinetic energy for $\eta'$ production in $NN$ reactions: $T_{kin}\sim 2.41$ GeV
- Reduction factor for initial-state interaction: $\zeta=0.277$ for the $^1P_1$ wave and $\zeta=0.243$ for the $^3P_0$ wave
- Coupling constant $g_{\eta' NN}\simeq 1.42$, taken from Feldmann (2000) and confirmed by CLAS data
- Coupling constants for meson conversion: $g_{\omega\omega\eta'}\simeq 4.90$, $g_{\rho\rho\eta'}\simeq 5.84$
- $\eta'\to\gamma\gamma$ decay width: $\Gamma_{\eta'\to\gamma\gamma}=(4.30\pm 0.15)$ keV, yielding $|f_{\eta'\gamma\gamma}|\simeq 0.0314$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Upper limit on $g_{\eta' NN}$ from pseudo-vector coupling: $g_{\eta' NN}<2$ (Nakayama, 2006)
- Cut-off parameters for resonance form factors: $\Lambda=1.2$ GeV for most vertices, $\Lambda=0.9$ GeV for the $\eta'$ vertex on $S_{11}(1650)$

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of $\eta'$ production in $NN$ reactions by Nakayama (2006, 2007) and photo-production studies by SAPHIR and CLAS collaborations, providing a consistent model that includes only known four-star resonances rather than invoking missing resonances as done by Zhang (2005) and others. The calculations enable predictions for the $pn$ channel and for di-electron production from $\eta'$ Dalitz decay at HADES, CLAS and KEK-PS kinematics, which is relevant for background control in searches for chiral symmetry restoration signals in compressed nuclear matter at FAIR.
