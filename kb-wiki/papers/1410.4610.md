# The proton-neutron symplectic model of nuclear collective motions

> A proton-neutron symplectic model of collective motions, based on the non-compact symplectic group $Sp(12,R)$, is introduced by considering the symplectic geometry of the two-component many-particle nuclear system. The possible classical collective motions are determined by different dynamical groups that can be constructed from the symplectic generators. The relation of the $Sp(12,R)$ irreps with the shell-model classification of the basis states is considered by extending of the state space to the direct product space of $SU_{p}(3) \otimes SU_{n}(3)$ irreps, generalizing in this way the Elliott's $SU(3)$ model for the case of two-component system. The $Sp(12,R)$ model appears then as a natural multi-major-shell extension of the generalized proton-neutron $SU(3)$ scheme which takes into account the core collective excitations of monopole and quadrupole, as well as dipole type associated with the giant resonance vibrational degrees of freedom. Each $Sp(12,R)$ irreducible representation is determined by a symplectic bandhead or an intrinsic $U(6)$ space which can be fixed by the underlying proton-neutron shell-model structure, so the theory becomes completely compatible with the Pauli principle. It is shown that this intrinsic $U(6)$ structure is of vital importance for the appearance of the low-lying collective bands with both the positive and negative parity. The full range of low-lying collective states can then be described by the microscopically based intrinsic $U(6)$ structure, renormalized by coupling to the giant resonance vibrations.

# The proton-neutron symplectic model of nuclear collective motions

## Key claim

The paper introduces a proton-neutron symplectic model based on the non-compact group Sp(12,R) that extends Elliott's SU(3) model to two-component (proton-neutron) systems, claiming that the intrinsic U(6) structure, determined by the underlying shell-model configuration, is essential for describing both positive- and negative-parity low-lying collective bands without mixing multiple symplectic irreps, but only for deformed heavy mass even-even nuclei where the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction dominates.

## Method

The authors construct the Sp(12,R) algebra from one-body bilinear operators in Jacobi coordinates and momenta for a two-component (proton-neutron) many-particle system, separating collective from intrinsic degrees of freedom via the reduction Sp(12m,R) ⊃ Sp(12,R) ⊗ O(m). Irreducible representations of Sp(12,R) are built in a harmonic oscillator basis using boson creation and annihilation operators, with basis states classified by the chain Sp(12,R) ⊃ U(6) ⊃ SU_p(3) ⊗ SU_n(3) ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SO(3), generalizing Elliott's SU(3) model to the direct product space of proton and neutron SU(3) irreps. The proper permutational symmetry of the nuclear wave function is ensured by embedding the symmetric group S_{m+1} in O(m) and further reducing to S_{N1} ⊗ S_{N2}.

## Key numbers

- The Sp(12,R) group has 78 Hermitian generators, given by one-body operators Q_{ij}(α,β), S_{ij}(α,β), L_{ij}(α,β), and T_{ij}(α,β) where α,β = p,n and i,j = 1,2,3.
- The raising operators of Sp(12,R) carry U(1) ⊗ SU(3) quantum numbers N(λ,μ) = 2(2,0) for monopole/quadrupole type and N(λ,μ) = 2(0,1) for dipole type, representing ±2ℏω inter-shell excitations.
- The scalar Sp(12,R) irrep ⟨σ⟩ = 0 contains U(6) irreps including [2], [4], [22], [6], [42], [222], [8], [62], [44], [422], [2222] for increasing oscillator quanta N = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, ...
- The number of bosons operator N is the first Casimir invariant of U(6) and of the combined proton-neutron U(3) group.

## Context

This work extends the one-component Sp(6,R) model of Rosensteel and Rowe (1977) to explicitly include proton-neutron degrees of freedom, generalizing Elliott's SU(3) model (1958) for two-component nuclear systems. The Sp(12,R) model provides a multi-major-shell extension of the generalized proton-neutron SU(3) scheme and incorporates collective excitations of monopole, quadrupole, and dipole type (including giant resonance vibrations). The approach contrasts with the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), which permits only fully symmetric U(6) irreps and thus only axial rotor configurations, whereas the present model allows generic U(6) irreps giving rise to two-axial, axial-triaxial, and two-triaxial rotor configurations. The framework enables simultaneous shell-model description of ground-state bands and excited low-lying collective bands (β, γ, K^π = 0^−, 1^−, 2^−, etc.) in heavy deformed even-even nuclei without mixing different symplectic irreps, unlike the Sp(6,R) approach of Carvalho (1986).
