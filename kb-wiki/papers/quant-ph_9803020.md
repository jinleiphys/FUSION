# Double Spiral Energy Surface in One-dimensional Quantum Mechanics of Generalized Pointlike Potentials

> We analyze the eigenvalue problem of a quantum particle on the line with the generalized pointlike potential of three parameter family. It is shown that the energy surface in the parameter space has a set of singularities, around which different eigenstates are connected in the form of paired spiral stairway. An examplar wave-function aholonomy is displayed where the ground state is adiabatically turned into the second excited state after cyclic rotation in the parameter space. KEYWORDS: one-dimensional system, $δ'$ potential, non-trivial topology in quantum mechanics, exotic wave-function aholonomy

# Double Spiral Energy Surface in One-dimensional Quantum Mechanics of Generalized Pointlike Potentials

## Key claim
The energy surface of a one-dimensional quantum particle with a generalized pointlike potential exhibits a double spiral structure around a singularity in parameter space, where the ground state can be adiabatically transformed into the second excited state after a cyclic parameter rotation. This aholonomy is an approximation based on an expansion neglecting terms of order O(ρ) around the singular point, though the relation connecting states separated by 2π in the angular parameter is exact.

## Method
The author constructs a generalized pointlike potential as the zero-distance limit of three Dirac delta functions with varying strengths, parameterized by four real numbers (α, β, γ, δ) satisfying the constraint αγ − βδ = 1 to ensure self-adjointness. The system is placed in a one-dimensional billiard of length L with a point potential at x = 0 dividing the line into two segments of lengths rL and (1−r)L, and the eigenvalue equation is derived from the matching conditions at the discontinuity. The energy surface is visualized by fixing γ = γ₀ and examining eigenvalues as functions of the two parameters (α, β), with δ determined by the constraint, revealing a singularity at (α*, β*) = (1/γ₀, 0).

## Key numbers
- The singularity in the (α, β) parameter space occurs at (α*, β*) = (1/γ₀, 0), where the energy eigenvalue becomes indefinite.
- The system length is set to L = 1 with r = (√5 − 1)/2 ≈ 0.618034 and γ₀ = −1 for the numerical examples.
- The energy eigenvalues satisfy the approximate relation Eₙ(θ + π) = Eₙ₊₁(θ) and the exact relation Eₙ(θ + 2π) = Eₙ₊₂(θ), where θ is the angular coordinate around the singularity.
- A bound state emerges from negative infinite energy at θ = 0₊, π₊, 2π₊, ... as κ → ∞ when θ → 0₊.

## Context
This work extends the study of Berry phase phenomena (Berry, 1984; Aharonov and Anandan, 1987) and non-Abelian generalizations (Wilczek and Zee, 1984) by identifying a new type of energy surface topology beyond the familiar double-cone structure. The author notes a resemblance to the Bender-Wu singularity found in the complex parameter space of the quartic quantum oscillator (Bender and Wu, 1969), though no direct link is established. The findings enable the search for similar aholonomy structures in other quantum systems, particularly contact interactions on graphs (Exner, 1996) and "homeopathic" quantum systems (Avron and Berger, 1997), with potential applications in quantum dot and heterojunction devices for manipulating wave functions.
