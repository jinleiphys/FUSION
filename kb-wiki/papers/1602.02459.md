# Nuclear effects in deep inelastic scattering and transition region

> We discuss nuclear effects on neutrino-nuclear interactions in a wide kinematical range from shallow to deep inelastic scattering (DIS) region. There is necessity from neutrino communities to have precise neutrino-nucleus cross sections for future measurements on neutrino oscillations and leptonic CP violation. We try to create a model to calculate neutrino cross sections in the wide kinematical range, from quasi-elastic scattering and resonance productions to the DIS. In this article, nuclear modifications of structure functions are mainly discussed, and a possible extension to the $Q^2 \to 0$ region is explained. We also comment on the transition region between baryon resonances and the DIS. There are ongoing experimental efforts on nuclear modifications of structure functions or parton distribution functions such as by pA reactions at RHIC and LHC, Drell-Yan measurements at Fermilab, Miner$ν$a neutrino DIS, and charged-lepton DIS at JLab.

# Nuclear effects in deep inelastic scattering and transition region

## Key claim
Nuclear modifications of parton distribution functions (NPDFs) are needed for precise neutrino-nucleus cross sections, but the nuclear modifications of structure functions in neutrino deep inelastic scattering may differ significantly from those in charged-lepton DIS, particularly in the medium-x depletion, anti-shadowing, and shadowing regions, though this discrepancy is not seen in all global analyses.

## Method
The author reviews the formalism for neutrino-nucleus deep inelastic scattering structure functions (F1, F2, F3) in terms of parton distribution functions, including higher-order alpha_s corrections and the longitudinal-transverse ratio R. Nuclear parton distribution functions are extracted from global chi-squared analyses of world data on nuclear structure function ratios F2^A/F2^D from charged-lepton DIS, Drell-Yan cross-section ratios sigma_pA/sigma_pD, and pA/AA reactions at RHIC and LHC. The extension to low Q^2 (< 1 GeV^2) is handled by separating vector and axial-vector contributions, with the axial-vector part constrained by partial conservation of the axial-vector current (PCAC). The transition region between baryon resonances (W^2 < 4 GeV^2) and DIS is addressed via hadron duality and the Bodek-Yang parametrization.

## Key numbers
- The resonance region is defined by invariant-mass squared M_N^2 < W^2 < 4 GeV^2, and the DIS region by W^2 >= 4 GeV^2.
- The DGLAP evolution requires Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2 or a few GeV^2 for the running coupling to be small enough.
- Nuclear shadowing occurs at x < 0.05, anti-shadowing at 0.1 < x < 0.2, and the EMC effect (depletion) at 0.3 < x < 0.7.
- The average nucleon separation in a nucleus is 2.2 fm, relevant for the shadowing mechanism.
- For calcium-40 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, nuclear modifications are shown with uncertainty bands; the mass number A=40 is larger than carbon (A=12), so modifications are generally larger.
- The Minerova experiment probes the region 0.05 < x < 0.6, including the anti-shadowing range 0.1 < x < 0.2.

## Context
This paper reviews the status of nuclear parton distribution functions for neutrino-nucleus interactions, extending prior work on NPDFs by groups such as Eskola et al., de Florian et al., and the nCTEQ collaboration. It discusses discrepancies between neutrino and charged-lepton DIS nuclear modifications reported by nCTEQ, and notes that the Minerova experiment at Fermilab is ongoing to investigate nuclear modifications in neutrino DIS. The work enables more precise neutrino-nucleus cross-section calculations for neutrino oscillation experiments (e.g., T2K with oxygen targets) and leptonic CP violation measurements. Open questions include the mechanism of anti-shadowing, gluon shadowing at small x, and antiquark modifications at x > 0.2, which may be addressed by the Fermilab-E906 Drell-Yan experiment and LHC p+Pb data.
