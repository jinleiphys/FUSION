# Electroweak reactions with light nuclei

> The investigation of light nuclei with ab-initio methods provides an optimal setting to probe our knowledge on nuclear forces, because the few-nucleon problem can be solved accurately. Nucleons interact not only in pairs but also via many-body forces. Theoretical efforts need to be taken towards the identification of nuclear observables sensitive to the less known many-nucleon forces. Electromagnetic reactions can potentially provide useful information on this. We present results on photo-absorption and electron scattering off light nuclei, emphasizing the role of three-body forces and the comparison with experimental data. On the other hand, reactions induced by weak probes, like neutrino interactions with nucleonic matter, are relevant to astrophysics and can be calculated with few-body techniques. In this case, since often no experiment is possible, ab-initio predictions provide valuable input for astrophysical simulations.

## Key claim

Ab-initio calculations of electroweak reactions on light nuclei can probe three-nucleon forces, but the experimental data for the photo-absorption cross section on 4He are too inconsistent (deviating by up to a factor of two) to allow definitive conclusions about the role of three-body forces, whereas the longitudinal response in electron scattering shows a clear sensitivity to three-nucleon forces that increases at low momentum transfer.

## Method

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method was used to compute inclusive response functions for photo-absorption and electron scattering on 4He and A=3 nuclei, solving an inhomogeneous equation with bound-state boundary conditions to avoid explicit continuum wave functions. The bound-state wave functions and the LIT solutions were expanded in antisymmetrized hyperspherical harmonics (HH) with an effective interaction constructed via the Lee-Suzuki method (EIHH), using the Argonne V18 (AV18) two-nucleon potential with the Urbana IX (UIX) or Tucson-Melbourne (TM') three-nucleon forces, and chiral N3LO two-body and N2LO three-body forces.

## Key numbers

* Total photo-absorption cross section peak on 4He: three-nucleon forces reduce the peak height by less than 10% for both AV18+UIX and chiral N2LO forces.
* Longitudinal response function RL for 4He at q=50 MeV/c: three-nucleon forces quench the strength by about 40% relative to the AV18-only calculation.
* Longitudinal response function RL for 4He at q=200 MeV/c: three-nucleon forces quench the quasi-elastic peak by about 20% (AV18+UIX vs. AV18 only).
* Longitudinal response function RL for 3H at q=174 MeV/c: three-nucleon forces quench the strength by about 10% at energies between 10 and 20 MeV.
* Coulomb sum rule for 4He at q=50 MeV/c: CSR = 0.064 with AV18 only, 0.053 with AV18+UIX (about 20% reduction).
* Coulomb sum rule for 4He at q=500 MeV/c: CSR = 1.884 with AV18 only, 1.871 with AV18+UIX.
* Relativistic Darwin-Foldy correction to RL for 4He at q=450 and 500 MeV/c: decreases the quasi-elastic peak by 5-6%.
* 4He binding energy with AV18+TM' potential: 28.46 MeV (compared to 28.46 MeV for AV18+UIX).

## Context

This work extends ab-initio calculations of electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei using the LIT method, previously applied to systems up to A=7 (Li6, Li7), with the most systematic studies on 4He. The results for the longitudinal response function at low momentum transfer (q=50-200 MeV/c) show that 4He is a more sensitive probe of three-nucleon forces than A=3 nuclei (studied by the same group in earlier work), because the ratio of triplets to pairs doubles from 3H to 4He. The calculations have stimulated new experimental activity at Mainz (Distler) and at Jefferson Laboratory (E05.110 at Hall A) for precise measurements of the longitudinal response at low and high momentum transfers, which could help constrain the low-energy constants of effective field theory three-body forces or discriminate between different nuclear force models.
