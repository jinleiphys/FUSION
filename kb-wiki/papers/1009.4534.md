# QCD Critical Points and Their Associated Soft Modes

> The mean-field level calculation shows that the QCD matter can have multiple critical points incorporating the color superconductivity under charge neutrality constraint due to the repulsive vector interaction; this actually implies that the QCD matter is very soft for a simultaneous formation of diquark and chiral condensates coupled with the baryonic density. Dynamical density fluctuations are analyzed as possible soft modes around the QCD critical point using dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics. It is found that the entropy fluctuation solely gets enhanced while the sound modes due to mechanical density fluctuations are strongly attenuated around the QCD CP, which may suggest a suppression or even total disappearance of Mach cone at the CP.

## Key claim

The mean-field calculation suggests that QCD matter can have multiple critical points when color superconductivity and a repulsive vector interaction are incorporated under charge neutrality, but the number of critical points is sensitive to the ratio of vector to scalar coupling and other model parameters, meaning the phase diagram is not uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors use a nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Lorentzian-type form factor to study the chiral and color-superconducting phase transitions at finite temperature and chemical potential. They impose electric-charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium, and analyze the effect of a repulsive vector-vector interaction on the phase structure. Separately, they compute the dynamical structure factor for density fluctuations using dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics, including the Landau equation and Israel-Stewart equation, to identify soft modes around the QCD critical point.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters are fixed by vacuum quantities: pion mass \(M_\pi = 135\ \text{MeV}\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\), and quark condensate \(-\langle \bar{u}u\rangle^{1/3} \approx 250\ \text{MeV}\).
- For a parameter set giving \(M(p=0) = 367.5\ \text{MeV}\) and standard diquark coupling, the number of critical points changes as \(1 \rightarrow 2 \rightarrow 4 \rightarrow 2 \rightarrow 0\) as the vector coupling \(G_V/G_S\) is increased.
- With \(G_V/G_S = 0.26\) and \(G_D/G_S = 0.75\), four critical points (labeled E, F, G, H) appear in the phase diagram.
- The spectral function is evaluated at wave number \(k = 0.1\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and reduced temperatures \(t = (T - T_c)/T_c = 0.5\) and \(t = 0.1\), showing attenuation of Brillouin peaks and enhancement of the Rayleigh peak.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the QCD phase diagram that included vector interactions (Kitazawa 2002, Fukushima 2008) and charge neutrality (Zhang 2008), showing that multiple critical points can arise when color superconductivity is considered. The analysis of density fluctuations builds on the established result that the QCD critical point belongs to the same universality class as the liquid-gas critical point (Fujii, Son), and uses the relativistic fluid dynamics framework of Minami and Kunihiro (2009). The finding that sound modes are attenuated near the critical point suggests that Mach cone suppression in heavy-ion collisions could serve as a signal for the critical point, though explicit calculations with expanding backgrounds are needed for confirmation.
