# Applying the Density Matrix Expansion with Coordinate-Space Chiral Interactions

> We apply the density matrix expansion (DME) at Hartree-Fock level with long-range chiral effective field theory interactions defined in coordinate space up to next-to-next-to-leading order. We consider chiral potentials both with and without explicit Delta isobars. The challenging algebra associated with applying the DME to three-nucleon forces is tamed using a new organization scheme, which will also facilitate generalizations. We include local regulators on the interactions to mitigate the effects of singular potentials on the DME couplings and simplify the optimization of generalized Skyrme-like functionals.

## Key claim
The authors apply the density matrix expansion (DME) at Hartree-Fock level to long-range chiral effective field theory interactions in coordinate space up to next-to-next-to-leading order, but only include the finite-range parts of the potentials while omitting contact terms and certain intermediate-range three-nucleon contributions that are assumed to be absorbable into a subsequent global refit of Skyrme parameters.

## Method
The DME is applied to coordinate-space chiral potentials for both nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) forces, with and without explicit Delta isobars, using a new organization scheme that simplifies the algebra for 3N forces. The Hartree term is treated exactly while the DME is applied only to the Fock (exchange) terms, and local regulators are included on the interactions to mitigate singular short-distance parts of the chiral potentials. The resulting density-dependent couplings are derived by numerically performing the final integrals in coordinate space rather than analytically.

## Key numbers
- Chiral potentials are considered up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the chiral expansion
- The pion mass m_pi, Delta-N mass splitting M_Delta-N, pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, nucleon axial vector coupling g_A, and N-to-Delta axial vector coupling h_A = 3 g_A / sqrt(2) are used as parameters
- The potentials depend on subleading LECs c_1, c_2, c_3, c_4 from L_piN^(2) and the b_3+b_8 combination from L_piN Delta^(2)
- The OPE potential at LO uses the form factor W_S^(0)(r) = (m_pi^3/(12 pi)) (g_A/(2 f_pi))^2 e^(-x)/x with x = r m_pi
- The NLO form factors involve modified Bessel functions K_0(2x) and K_1(2x) with coefficients depending on g_A^4

## Context
This work extends the program initiated in PhysRevC.82.014305, Gebremariam (2011), and Stoitsov (2010) to incorporate microscopic chiral physics into Skyrme energy density functionals using an improved DME formulation. It differs from the previous momentum-space implementation of Bogner (2011) by working in coordinate space with local regulators, following the approach used in quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Gezerlis (2013, 2014). The new organization scheme for 3N forces enables simpler algebra than the symbolic software approach of Gebremariam (2010), and the inclusion of regulators allows for adiabatic turning on of finite-range physics to enable more stable functional optimization.
