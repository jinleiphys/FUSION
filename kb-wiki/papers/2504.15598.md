# Charge dependent nucleon-nucleon potentials in covariant chiral effective field theory

> The charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions are crucial in understanding the nuclear structure and reaction problems. This work explores the charge-dependent $NN$ interaction in covariant chiral effective field theory. By incorporating the isospin-breaking contributions, we derive the charge-dependent covariant chiral $NN$ potential up to the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). The calculated $np$ and $pp$ phase shifts are in satisfactory agreement with the PWA93 partial wave analysis. Our results contribute to a deeper understanding of the isospin-breaking effects in nuclear forces and provide a solid foundation for future studies of nuclear structure and reactions in the context of a covariant framework.

# Charge dependent nucleon-nucleon potentials in covariant chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
The authors construct charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon potentials within covariant chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), incorporating isospin-breaking effects from pion mass splitting, Coulomb interactions, and charge-dependent contact terms. The calculated np and pp phase shifts agree satisfactorily with the PWA93 partial wave analysis, but the description of the np 1S0 and 3D1 phase shifts deteriorates at NLO due to compensation for the description of pp phase shifts, and the NNLO low-energy constants appear "a bit large" due to the large contribution from the NNLO two-pion exchange.

## Method
The authors derived charge-dependent covariant chiral NN potentials up to NNLO by incorporating isospin-breaking contributions: pion mass splitting in one-pion exchange (Mπ± ≠ Mπ0 at NLO), static one-photon exchange (Coulomb force), and charge-dependent contact terms (at NNLO). They solved the Thompson equation in the LSJ basis with a non-local Gaussian-type regulator (cutoff Λ = 800/900/700 MeV at LO/NLO/NNLO) and fitted 19 isospin-conserving plus 4 isospin-breaking low-energy constants simultaneously to np and pp scattering phase shifts from PWA93 at laboratory energies 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, and 200 MeV for partial waves with total angular momentum J ≤ 2.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon mass M_N = 939 MeV; pion masses Mπ0 = 135 MeV, Mπ± = 139 MeV; pion decay constant fπ = 92.4 MeV; axial coupling constant gA = 1.29
- Low-energy constants for two-pion exchange: c1 = -1.39, c2 = 4.01, c3 = -6.61, c4 = 3.92 (in units of GeV^-1)
- Total χ² for NLO: 70.92 (np: 48.21, pp: 22.71); for NNLO: 22.82 (np: 13.19, pp: 9.63)
- The pp 1S0 partial wave contributed χ² = 17.47 at NLO and 4.06 at NNLO
- Matching radius for Coulomb treatment: R = 12 fm
- The four additional pp contact LECs at NNLO: C_S^pp = -70.41, C_V^pp = -13.94, C_AV^pp = 13.94, C_T^pp = 0.00 (in units of 10^4 GeV^-2)

## Context
This work extends the covariant chiral EFT framework previously developed for np scattering (Xiao 2020, Wang 2021, Lu 2021) to include proton-proton interactions by systematically incorporating isospin-breaking effects. It follows the treatment of isospin-breaking in conventional (non-covariant) chiral EFT as formulated by Epelbaum (2004) and others, including van Kolck (1997), Walzl (2000), and Friar (2003). The charge-dependent potentials enable future studies of isospin-breaking phenomena in nuclear systems such as mirror energy differences, charge-exchange processes, and the symmetry energy of isospin-asymmetric matter. The authors note that implementing a more modern semi-local regulator (Reinert 2017) in the covariant framework is left as future work.
