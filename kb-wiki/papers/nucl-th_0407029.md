# Effect of a finite-time resolution on Schrödinger Cat States in complex collisions

> We study the effect of finite time resolution on coherent superpositions of rotating clockwise and anticlockwise wave packets in the regime of strongly overlapping resonances of the intermediate complex. Such highly excited deformed complexes may be created in binary collisions of heavy-ions, molecules and atomic clusters. It is shown that time averaging reduces the interference fringes acting effectively as dephasing. We propose a simple estimate of the ``critical'' time averaging interval. For the time averaging intervals bigger than the critical one the interference fringes wash out appreciably. This is confirmed numerically. We evaluate minimal energy intervals for measurements of the excitation functions needed to observe the Schrödinger Cat States. These should be easily observable in heavy-ion scattering. Such an experiment is suggested.

## Key claim

Finite time resolution in measurements of the decay of an intermediate complex acts as effective dephasing that reduces interference fringes of Schrödinger Cat States; the effect becomes appreciable only when the time averaging interval exceeds a critical value \(\Delta t_{cr} \simeq T/2I\), where \(T\) is the rotational period and \(I\) is the average spin.

## Method

The authors analytically average the time- and angle-dependent decay intensity \(P(t,\theta)\) of a highly excited intermediate complex over a finite time interval \(\Delta t\), using a constant-weight integration. They then compute the time-averaged intensity numerically for the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{24}\text{Mg}\) system, using parameters extracted from prior analyses of experimental cross-section energy autocorrelation functions (Kun 2001). The ratio of time-averaged to ideal-resolution intensity is evaluated at half a rotational period to quantify the suppression of interference maxima and filling of interference minima.

## Key numbers

- Critical time averaging interval: \(\Delta t_{cr} \simeq T/2I\), where \(T\) is the rotational period and \(I = 14\) is the average spin of the intermediate complex.
- For the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{24}\text{Mg}\) system: \(\hbar\omega = 1.35\) MeV, \(\bar{t} = 2.2 \times 10^{-21}\) sec, \(\beta = 0.01\) MeV, \(d = 3\), and \(I = 14\).
- For \(\Delta t = \Delta t_{cr}\), the interference maximum/minimum ratio is reduced by a factor of about 1.5; for \(\Delta t = 1.5 \times \Delta t_{cr}\), reduction is by a factor of about 2.5; for \(\Delta t = 2 \times \Delta t_{cr}\), reduction is by a factor of about 3.5.
- Minimal energy interval required for measurements: \(\Delta E \simeq \hbar / \Delta t_{cr} = (I/\pi) \hbar \omega \approx 6\) MeV for the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{24}\text{Mg}\) system.
- Required angular resolution: \(\Delta\theta \leq \pi/3I \simeq 4-5\) degrees.

## Context

This work extends the prediction of Schrödinger Cat States in heavy-ion collisions by Kun (2003), which demonstrated stable coherent superpositions of clockwise and anticlockwise rotating wave packets in the regime of strongly overlapping resonances. The present paper addresses the practical experimental question of required time (and thus energy) resolution, which was left open in that prior work. The results enable a concrete experimental search for Schrödinger Cat States in \(^{12}\text{C}+^{24}\text{Mg}\) elastic scattering, where prior measurements of excitation functions (Mermaz 1981; Ghosh 1987) already cover energy intervals larger than the required 6 MeV, making such an experiment feasible with existing techniques.
