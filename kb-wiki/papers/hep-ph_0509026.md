# Dilepton production from hot hadronic matter in nonequilibrium

> The influence of time dependent medium modifications of low mass vector mesons on dilepton yields is investigated within a nonequilibrium quantum field theoretical description on the basis of the Kadanoff-Baym equations. Time scales for the adaption of the spectral properties to changing self energies are given and, under use of a model for the fireball evolution, nonequilibrium dilepton yields from the decay of rho- and omega-mesons are calculated. In a comparison of these yields with those from calculations that assume instantaneous (Markovian) adaption to the changing medium, quantum mechanical memory effects turn out to be important.

# Dilepton production from hot hadronic matter in nonequilibrium

## Key claim

Memory effects from non-Markovian dynamics significantly alter dilepton yields from rho- and omega-meson decay in an expanding fireball, compared to calculations assuming instantaneous (Markovian) adaptation of spectral properties to the changing medium, particularly for scenarios such as Brown-Rho scaling.

## Method

The authors derive a nonequilibrium dilepton production rate from the Kadanoff-Baym equations in the Schwinger-Keldysh real-time formalism, working in the two-time representation to retain full memory effects. They introduce time-dependent medium modifications of vector meson self-energies by hand, solve the equation of motion for the retarded rho-meson propagator, and compute the dynamic dilepton rate via a memory integral over the virtual photon propagator. The results are convolved with a Bjorken-like fireball expansion model to obtain yields for comparison with the Markovian (instantaneous) limit.

## Key numbers

- The typical lifetime of the diluting hadronic phase in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is 5–8 fm/c (citing Rapp 1999).
- A cutoff Lambda of approximately 0.3 GeV is introduced for the free photon propagators to cure numerical divergences from early-time contributions.
- The factor used to renormalize the final result after the cutoff is (omega^2 + Lambda^2)^2 / omega^4.

## Context

This work extends prior nonequilibrium quantum field theory studies of thermalization in phi^4 theory (Juchem 2003) and Yukawa-type systems (Berges 2002) to the phenomenologically relevant case of dilepton production from vector mesons. It directly addresses the question of whether memory effects matter for dilepton observables, which had been treated in earlier schematic models (Rapp 1999) and Monte-Carlo transport simulations (Cassing 1999) under the assumption of instantaneous adaptation. The results enable more accurate extraction of in-medium vector meson properties from experimental dilepton data at SPS, RHIC, and future facilities.
