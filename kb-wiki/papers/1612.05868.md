# Decay mechanism and lifetime of $^{67}$Kr

> The lifetime of the recently discovered $2p$ emitter $^{67}$Kr was recently found considerably below the lower limit predicted theoretically. This communication addresses this issue.Different separation energy systematics are analyzed and different mechanisms for $2p$ emission are evaluated. It is found that the most plausible reason for this disagreement is a decay mechanism of $^{67}$Kr, which is not "true $2p$" emission, but "transition dynamics" on the borderline between true $2p$ and sequential $2p$ decay mechanisms. If this is true, this imposes stringent limits $E_r=1.35-1.42$ MeV on the ground state energy of $^{66}$Br relative to the $^{65}$Se-$p$ threshold.

## Key claim

The observed lifetime of the $2p$ emitter $^{67}$Kr is considerably below the lower limit predicted theoretically in 2003; the most plausible reason is that the decay mechanism is not "true $2p$" emission but a "transition dynamics" on the borderline between true $2p$ and sequential $2p$ decay, which imposes stringent limits on the ground state energy of $^{66}$Br.

## Method

The authors performed refined three-body cluster model calculations (core+$p$+$p$) using the hyperspherical harmonics method, solving the three-body Schrodinger equation with complex energy and outgoing wave boundary conditions. They also employed an improved direct decay model (IDDM), a semianalytical R-matrix approach, to evaluate $2p$ lifetimes and systematic studies. Different separation energy systematics were analyzed, including odd-even staggering and Thomas-Ehrman shift estimates, to evaluate the proton separation energy $S_p$ of $^{67}$Kr and the ground state resonance energy $E_r$ of $^{66}$Br.

## Key numbers

- The measured total lifetime of $^{67}$Kr is $7.4(30)$ ms, and the partial $2p$ lifetime is $20(11)$ ms.
- The $2p$ decay energy is $E_T = 1690 \pm 17$ keV.
- The previous (2003) theoretical lower lifetime limit was $240^{+100}_{-70}$ ms.
- The transition from true $2p$ to sequential $2p$ decay occurs in the $E_r = 1.35-1.42$ MeV range, corresponding to $S_p$ from $-340$ to $-270$ keV.
- For a realistic shell model structure with about 18% $[p^2_{3/2}]$ configuration, the calculated lifetimes agree with experiment only in the narrow range $E_r \sim 1.38-1.58$ MeV.
- The width of the $^{66}$Br ground state at $E_r = 1.36$ MeV is very small, e.g., $\Gamma_r = 1.17$ eV for potential P1.

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between the 2003 three-body model predictions by Grigorenko (2003) and the recent experimental discovery of $^{67}$Kr as a $2p$ emitter by Goigoux (2016). It extends the study of "transition type" decay mechanisms, previously observed in $^{30}$Ar by Mukha (2015) and Golubkova (2016), to a heavier system. The findings enable future experimental studies of $2p$ correlations in $^{67}$Kr using time projection chambers, which could distinguish between true $2p$ and sequential decay mechanisms even with modest counting rates.
