# Suppression of high $p_{T}$ hadrons in $Pb+Pb$ Collisions at LHC

> Nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(p_{T})$ for large transverse momentum pion spectra in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV is predicted within the NLO perturbative QCD parton model. Effect of jet quenching is incorporated through medium modified fragmentation functions within the higher-twist approach. The jet transport parameter that controls medium modification is proportional to the initial parton density and the coefficient is fixed by the RHIC data on suppression of large $p_{T}$ hadron spectra. Data on charged hadron multiplicity $dN_{ch}/dη=1584 \pm 80$ in central $Pb+Pb$ collisions from the ALICE Experiment at the LHC are used to constrain the initial parton density both for determining the jet transport parameter and the 3+1D ideal hydrodynamic evolution of the bulk matter that is employed for the calculation of $R_{PbPb}(p_{T})$ for neutral pions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) for neutral pions in \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, using the ALICE measurement of charged hadron multiplicity \(dN_{ch}/d\eta = 1584 \pm 80\) to constrain the initial parton density. The hedge is that the jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0\) is assumed to scale linearly with the transverse density of final charged hadron multiplicity, with the proportionality constant fixed by RHIC data, so the LHC prediction depends on this extrapolation.

## Method

The authors employ a next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD parton model for initial jet production, with medium-modified fragmentation functions computed via the higher-twist (HT) approach. The jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}\) is taken proportional to the local parton density in a quark-gluon plasma and hadron density in a hadron resonance gas, with its initial value \(\hat{q}_0\) at the center of the medium determined by fitting RHIC data on pion suppression in central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV. A 3+1D ideal hydrodynamic model, initialized using HIJING 2.0 and constrained by the ALICE multiplicity data, provides the space-time evolution of temperature and flow velocity for evaluating \(\hat{q}\) along the jet path.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity density at mid-rapidity in central (0-5%) \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV: \(dN_{ch}/d\eta = 1584 \pm 4\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 76\ (\text{sys.})\) from the ALICE Experiment.
- For central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV, the extracted jet transport parameter times initial time is \(\hat{q}_0 \tau_0 = 0.54-0.63\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For central \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, the extrapolated value is \(\hat{q}_0 \tau_0 = 1.0-1.4\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is \(\tau_0 = 0.6\ \text{fm}/c\).
- The critical temperature for the phase transition is \(T_c = 170\ \text{MeV}\), and the decoupling temperature is \(T^{\text{dec}} = 100\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions for jet quenching at LHC energies (e.g., those summarized in the 2007 CERN Yellow Report by Abreu et al.) by using the first ALICE data on charged hadron multiplicity to constrain the initial parton density and the hydrodynamic evolution. It builds on the higher-twist approach to medium-modified fragmentation functions previously applied to RHIC data by Chen et al. (2010) and uses the HIJING 2.0 model of Deng et al. (2010) for initial conditions. The results enable comparison with upcoming LHC data on \(R_{AA}\) for pions and charged hadrons, and the authors note that contributions from jet-jet parton recombination, not included here, may increase the suppression factor at moderate \(p_T\).
