# Decoherence and the Branching of Chaos-less Classical Trajectory

> This study was started to know mysterious classicality of nuclei. This time, I found a new rule for decoherence. I used a model without chaos. As a result, it was shown that not only the intersection of classical trajectories but also branching of classical trajectories are needed for decoherence. In other words, it was shown that interactions between a main system and environments have to make enough branchings of classical trajectories of the main system for decoherence.

## Key claim

Decoherence requires not only the intersection of classical trajectories but also their branching, where the branching is produced by interactions between the main system and its environment. The author claims this holds even in a model without chaos, contrary to suggestions in prior work that chaos might be necessary for such branching.

## Method

The author constructs a closed three-body system of particles connected by springs with different angular frequencies, plus an external harmonic potential acting on particle-1. The Feynman propagator is obtained numerically, and the reduced density matrix for particle-1 is computed by integrating out particles 2 and 3. Classical trajectories for particle-1 are generated from the \(2^3 = 8\) combinations of initial positions corresponding to the two Gaussian wavepacket centers in each particle's initial Schrödinger cat state, with all initial velocities set to zero.

## Key numbers

- Weak coupling case: coupling constants \(K_{12}=0.01442\), \(K_{31}=0.01732\) between particle-1 and environments; \(K_{23}=1.02236\) between environmental particles. Decoherence does not occur when packets cross.
- Strong coupling case: \(K_{12}=0.1442\), \(K_{31}=0.1732\) (10 times the weak case); \(K_{23}=1.02236\) unchanged. Decoherence occurs when packets cross.
- Masses: \(m_1=1.5\), \(m_2=1.0\), \(m_3=1.0\) (arbitrary units); external spring constant \(K=2.5\); initial packet separation \(d_1=-5.0\), \(d_2=6.0\), \(d_3=7.5\); \(\hbar=1.0\).
- Time evolution shown from \(t=0.505\) to \(t=6.005\) in steps of \(0.5\) for both coupling cases.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous three-body model (Ishikawa, 2014) by adding an external harmonic potential and allowing unequal masses, and contrasts with the Caldeira-Leggett model (Caldeira and Leggett, 1983, 1985) where decoherence does not occur for weakly damped crossing Gaussian packets. The result suggests that the integration over environmental degrees of freedom in constructing the reduced density matrix is itself the quantum counterpart of collecting all classical trajectories, and that chaos (as studied by Zurek and Paz, 1994, and Fujisaki, Miyadera, and Tanaka, 2003) is not required for decoherence. The work aims to explain the classicality observed in finite nuclear systems such as the liquid drop model and TDHF.
