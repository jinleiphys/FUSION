# The position of the quasielastic peak and electron Coulomb distortion in (e,e') scattering

> The position of the quasielastic peak for (e,e') scattering off 208-Pb extracted from a selected data set measured at Saclay is related to a heuristic theoretical description. An analysis of the data shows that the peak position can be described very accurately by a simple equation in the relevant kinematic region where a pronounced peak is observable. The simple findings result in a concluding comment related to recent calculations concerning the Coulomb distortion in (e,e') scattering for heavy nuclei.

## Key claim
The position of the quasielastic peak in (e,e') scattering off 208Pb can be described very accurately by a simple heuristic equation that replaces the nucleon mass with an effective mass parameter (around 0.7-0.8 times the free nucleon mass) and uses an average removal energy, but the effective mass parameter should not be directly interpreted as a physical nucleon effective mass.

## Method
The author extracted quasielastic peak positions from Saclay data for 208Pb at two scattering angles (60° and 143°) across multiple beam energies (140-645 MeV). A least-squares polynomial fit was used to determine each peak position and its uncertainty. The experimental peak positions were then fitted to a heuristic equation that includes an effective nucleon mass parameter and an average removal energy, with the initial electron energy shifted by 20 MeV to account for Coulomb acceleration inside the nucleus.

## Key numbers
- For scattering angle 60°: effective nucleon mass = 721 MeV, average removal energy = 12.8 MeV
- For scattering angle 143°: effective nucleon mass = 662 MeV, average removal energy = 10.0 MeV
- The effective mass parameters are approximately 0.77 and 0.70 times the free nucleon mass (939 MeV)
- The average Coulomb potential for 208Pb is approximately 19 MeV (specifically 18.7 ± 1.5 MeV from experimental determination), not the central value of 25 MeV
- The peak shift between PWBA and EMA for 310 MeV electrons at 143° is 17.7 MeV, not the 23 MeV claimed in prior work by Jin (2005)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of quasielastic peak positions from Saclay data (Zghiche, 1994) and addresses discrepancies in Coulomb distortion calculations between the Ohio group (Jin, 2005) and the Basel group (Aste, 2005). The author shows that the effective momentum approximation (EMA) agrees well with full distorted-wave calculations when using an average Coulomb potential of approximately 19 MeV rather than the central potential value of 25 MeV that had been used in prior work by Rosenfelder (1980) and propagated through the literature. This enables more reliable Coulomb corrections for (e,e') scattering on heavy nuclei.
