# Electroproduction of medium- and heavy-mass hypernuclei

> The DWIA formalism for computing the cross sections in electroproduction of hypernuclei used before for lighter (p-shell and sd-shell) systems is utilized in studying electroproduction on heavier targets such as $^{52}$Cr, and $^{208}$Pb. First, the effects from kaon distortion and kinematics are re-examined and then results for the heavy targets are discussed in view of various forms of the $Λ$N effective interaction, the elementary amplitude, and kinematics. Apparent sensitivity of the hypernucleus excitation spectra to various forms of the $Λ$N effective interaction is shown. Predictions of the excitation spectrum in electroproduction of $^{208}_{~~Λ}$Tl are also given in kinematics of the experiment E12-20-013 in preparation at Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The DWIA formalism previously applied to lighter hypernuclei is extended to medium- and heavy-mass targets (⁵²Cr, ²⁰⁸Pb), showing that the predicted excitation spectra are sensitive to the form of the ΛN effective interaction, but the actual cross-section predictions for the upcoming Jefferson Lab experiment E12-20-013 depend on choices of kaon distortion model, kinematics (including hypernucleus mass shift), and the on-shell approximation for the elementary amplitude.

## Method

The authors compute electroproduction cross sections using the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with an optimal factorization approximation, where the elementary production amplitude is evaluated at an effective proton momentum determined from the two-body energy conservation and the mean proton momentum of the dominant single-particle transition. The nuclear structure is described via Hartree-Fock (HF) with the Daejeon 16 NN interaction plus a phenomenological density-dependent term, and the hypernucleus states are obtained from a Tamm-Dancoff (TDΛ) diagonalization in the space of Λ-particle proton-hole excitations; the ΛN interaction is modeled using various G-matrix potentials (Nijmegen F, Jülich A, chiral LO). Kaon distortion is included via the eikonal approximation with a first-order optical potential using HF nucleon densities, and the hypernucleus mass is shifted by the excitation energy in the kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Kaon distortion suppresses the cross section by 40–60% for ⁴⁰ΛK and 50–80% for ²⁰⁸ΛTl, with stronger suppression for more deeply bound Λ states.
- The hypernucleus mass shift (due to excitation energy) suppresses cross sections by 10–16% for the two example states of ²⁰⁸ΛTl at Eγ = 1.5 GeV (e.g., the state at 11.964 MeV, 8⁻ shows a 16.0% decrease in dσ; the state at 18.201 MeV, 8⁺ shows a 10.6% decrease).
- The HF density for ²⁰⁸Pb gives a mean nuclear radius R_HF = 5.26 fm, compared to the experimental value R_exp = 5.50 fm from electron scattering.
- The Coulomb interaction contribution to kaon-nucleus scattering at 1 GeV/c is estimated at about 3% for ⁴⁰Ca and a few percent for heavier nuclei.
- Fermi motion effects (differences between frozen-proton and frozen-Λ approximations) amount to about 20–30% at small kaon angles for selected states of ²⁰⁸ΛTl at Eγ = 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the DWIA formalism previously applied to p-shell and sd-shell hypernuclei (Bydžovský et al., 2018; 2020) to heavier targets, providing predictions for the planned Jefferson Lab experiments E12-15-008 (on ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca) and E12-20-013 (on ²⁰⁸Pb). It builds on earlier photoproduction studies by Motoba et al. (2010; 2012) and the authors' own analysis of Fermi motion effects in lighter systems. The results enable spectroscopic studies of medium- and heavy-mass hypernuclei beyond the p-shell, testing various ΛN effective interactions (Nijmegen F, Jülich A, chiral LO) against future experimental data.
