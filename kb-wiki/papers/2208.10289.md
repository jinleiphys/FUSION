# Analysis of the interaction between $د•$ meson and nucleus

> In this work, we systematically study the $د•$ meson and nucleus interaction by analyzing and fitting the cross sections of $خ³N$$\rightarrow د•$$N$ ($N$ represent the nucleus) reactions near the threshold. With the help of vector meson dominant model, the distribution of $د•$-$N$ scattering length as a function of energy is presented, and the results show that there is a slight increase in scattering length with increasing energy. Based on this, the average scattering length of $د•$-proton is obtained as $0.10\pm0.01$ fm by combining experimental data and theoretical models. Moreover, the average scattering length of $د•$-deuteron interaction is derived to be $0.014\pm0.002$ fm for the first time. Further, the effect of the momentum transfer $|t_{min}|$ on the $د•$-$N$ scattering length at the threshold is discussed. The obtained results not only provide important theoretical information for a more comprehensive and accurate study of the $د•$-$N$ scattering length, but also provide a basis for future experimental measurements of $د•$ meson production.

This work extends previous studies of vector meson-proton scattering lengths, including those by Strakovsky (2020) who obtained |خ±_د†p|=0.063آ±0.01 fm, the LEPS Collaboration (2007) who obtained |خ±_د†p|=0.15 fm, and Koike (1997) who obtained خ±_د†p=-0.15آ±0.02 fm using QCD sum rules. The work also highlights that the pomeron model gives systematically smaller scattering lengths than the two-gluon exchange model, indicating the latter is more reliable for near-threshold predictions.
