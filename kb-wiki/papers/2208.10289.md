# Analysis of the interaction between $ϕ$ meson and nucleus

> In this work, we systematically study the $ϕ$ meson and nucleus interaction by analyzing and fitting the cross sections of $γN$$\rightarrow ϕ$$N$ ($N$ represent the nucleus) reactions near the threshold. With the help of vector meson dominant model, the distribution of $ϕ$-$N$ scattering length as a function of energy is presented, and the results show that there is a slight increase in scattering length with increasing energy. Based on this, the average scattering length of $ϕ$-proton is obtained as $0.10\pm0.01$ fm by combining experimental data and theoretical models. Moreover, the average scattering length of $ϕ$-deuteron interaction is derived to be $0.014\pm0.002$ fm for the first time. Further, the effect of the momentum transfer $|t_{min}|$ on the $ϕ$-$N$ scattering length at the threshold is discussed. The obtained results not only provide important theoretical information for a more comprehensive and accurate study of the $ϕ$-$N$ scattering length, but also provide a basis for future experimental measurements of $ϕ$ meson production.

# Analysis of the interaction between φ meson and nucleus

## Key claim
The average scattering length of the φ-proton interaction is 0.10±0.01 fm, and the average scattering length of the φ-deuteron interaction is derived for the first time as 0.014±0.002 fm. These values are obtained by combining the vector meson dominance (VMD) model with experimental photoproduction cross sections near threshold, and the authors note that the scattering length shows a slight increase with energy rather than being a single fixed value.

## Method
The authors used the vector meson dominance (VMD) model to relate the total and differential cross sections of γN → φN reactions near threshold to the φ-N scattering length. They employed two theoretical models to predict photoproduction cross sections: a two-gluon exchange model based on lowest-order perturbative QCD and a pomeron exchange model. The free parameters in both models were obtained by global fitting to experimental total and differential cross section data from CLAS and LEPS collaborations. For the φ-deuteron case, the gluon distribution parameters from the φ-proton fit were retained while the slope parameter b₀ was re-fitted to LEPS deuteron data.

## Key numbers
- Average φ-proton scattering length: 0.10±0.01 fm, obtained by combining results from differential and total cross sections
- Average φ-deuteron scattering length: 0.014±0.002 fm, derived for the first time
- From the two-gluon exchange model using differential cross sections: √⟨|α²_φp|⟩ = 0.102±0.011 fm
- From direct extraction using CLAS and LEPS differential data: √⟨|α²_φp|⟩ = 0.106±0.005 fm
- From the two-gluon exchange model using total cross sections: √⟨|α²_φp|⟩ = 0.091±0.010 fm
- From direct extraction using CLAS total cross section data: √⟨|α²_φp|⟩ = 0.096±0.010 fm
- φ-deuteron scattering length from LEPS data: √⟨|α²_φd|⟩ = 0.015±0.002 fm
- φ-deuteron scattering length from two-gluon exchange model at R∈[0,0.66]: 0.014±0.002 fm
- Two-gluon exchange model parameters: A₀=0.36±0.04, A₁=-0.055±0.003, A₂=0.12±0.03, b₀=3.60±0.04 GeV⁻² (for φ-p); b₀=9±0.29 GeV⁻² (for φ-d)
- Pomeron model parameter: β=1.919±0.011 GeV⁻¹

## Context
This work extends previous studies of vector meson-proton scattering lengths, including those by Strakovsky (2020) who obtained |α_φp|=0.063±0.01 fm, the LEPS Collaboration (2007) who obtained |α_φp|=0.15 fm, and Koike (1997) who obtained α_φp=-0.15±0.02 fm using QCD sum rules. The authors note their result lies between these values and satisfies the previously observed proportionality between |α_Vp| and exp(1/m_V) established in their own prior work (Wang 2022). The φ-deuteron scattering length is reported for the first time, and the authors suggest this provides a basis for future experimental measurements at JLab or the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) facilities. The work also highlights that the pomeron model gives systematically smaller scattering lengths than the two-gluon exchange model, indicating the latter is more reliable for near-threshold predictions.
