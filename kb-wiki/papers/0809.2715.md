# Phase transition of the nucleon-antinucleon plasma at different ratios

> We investigate phase transitions for the Walecka model at very high temperatures. As is well known, depending on the parametrization of this model and for the particular case of a zero chemical potential ($ μ$), a first order phase transition is possible \cite{theis}. We investigate this model for the case in which $ μ\ne 0 $. It turns out that, in this situation, phases with different values of antinucleon-nucleon ratios and net baryon densities may coexist. We present the temperature versus antinucleon-nucleon ratio as well as the temperature versus the net baryon density for the coexistence region. The temperature versus chemical potential phase diagram is also presented.

## Key claim

The Walecka model at high temperature exhibits a first-order phase transition only when the antinucleon-nucleon ratio is exactly one (zero chemical potential); for any other fixed ratio, the apparent phase-transition signals (abrupt changes in effective mass and entropy) do not correspond to true phase coexistence under the Gibbs criteria.

## Method

The authors solved the Walecka model in the mean-field, no-sea approximation by self-consistently solving the gap equation for the effective nucleon mass together with the energy density and pressure functionals at finite temperature. They first constrained the antinucleon-nucleon ratio R to fixed values (0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9, and 1.0) and then relaxed the constraint to search for phase coexistence using the Gibbs criteria (equal temperature, pressure, and chemical potential between phases). The coupling constants were fixed to reproduce nuclear matter binding energy (16 MeV) at saturation density (0.15 fm⁻³).

## Key numbers

- For R=1, phase coexistence occurs at T = 183.25 MeV and μ = 0.
- The coexistence region for unconstrained R spans T from 180.1 MeV to 183.25 MeV, with chemical potential μ from 0 to 274 MeV.
- The antinucleon-nucleon ratio at RHIC is approximately 0.65 at T = 174 MeV and μ = 46 MeV (from thermal model fits to STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, BRAHMS data).
- The coupling constants used are C_σ² = 359.35 and C_ω² = 275.12.
- The saturation density is ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ and binding energy is 16 MeV.
- The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in the Walecka model is around 18 MeV.
- No phase coexistence exists if the antinucleon-nucleon ratio is not greater than 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the study of Theis et al. (1983) who found a first-order phase transition in the Walecka model at zero chemical potential (R=1). The authors show that for R ≠ 1, the apparent phase-transition signals do not satisfy Gibbs coexistence criteria, a rare behavior reminiscent of the Kosterlitz-Thouless transition in two dimensions. The results suggest that hadronic models used to describe chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., by Cleymans et al., Braun-Munzinger et al.) may encounter phase coexistence regions depending on the temperature and chemical potential values used to fit experimental data.
