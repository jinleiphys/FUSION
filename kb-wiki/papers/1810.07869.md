# Elliptic flow of transported and non-transported proton in Au+Au collisions within the UrQMD model

> With the framework of UrQMD model, by tracing the number of initial quarks in protons, we study the elliptic flow of transported protons, poduced protons and anti-protons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 39, 200 GeV. The difference of elliptic flow between transported protons and anti-protons is observed. The results of UrQMD model show a good agreement with the STAR results at low energies, but are deviate at high energies. It partly explains the $v_{2}$ difference between proton and anti-proton observed in the Beam Energy Scan program in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider~(RHIC).

## Key claim

By tracing the number of initial quarks in protons within the UrQMD model, the elliptic flow of transported protons (with three initial quarks) is systematically larger than that of anti-protons at low collision energies (7.7 and 11.5 GeV), but the model overestimates the STAR results at 39 GeV, indicating that partonic interactions missing from the model become important at higher energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD model (version 2.3) to simulate Au+Au collisions at four beam energies, with no modifications except for additional output to trace the number of initial quarks in each final-state proton. Protons were classified into four categories based on how many of their three constituent quarks originated from the initial colliding nuclei: 3 initial quarks (transported), 2, 1, or 0 initial quarks (produced). The elliptic flow coefficient v2 was calculated as a function of collision centrality, transverse momentum, and beam energy for each category and compared to anti-protons and to STAR experimental data.

## Key numbers

- At 7.7 GeV, transported protons (3 initial quarks) account for 17.4% of all protons and anti-protons in mid-rapidity (|Y|<1); at 11.5 GeV: 8.3%; at 39 GeV: 1.26%; at 200 GeV: 0.36%.
- At 7.7 GeV, produced protons (0 initial quarks) account for 0.45% of all protons and anti-protons; at 11.5 GeV: 2.4%; at 39 GeV: 15.4%; at 200 GeV: 39.1%.
- Anti-protons account for 0.44% at 7.7 GeV, 2.3% at 11.5 GeV, 18.05% at 39 GeV, and 35.24% at 200 GeV.
- The v2 difference between transported protons (3 initial quarks) and anti-protons is larger than 0 at 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV, and shows strong centrality dependence with the largest difference in mid-central collisions.
- The v2 difference between produced protons (0 initial quarks) and anti-protons is approximately consistent with 0 across all energies and centralities.
- The UrQMD results for the v2 difference between transported protons and anti-protons agree quantitatively with STAR data at 7.7 and 11.5 GeV, but overestimate the STAR results at 39 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the proton/anti-proton v2 splitting observed in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (Adamczyk 2013, 2016) by directly tracing quark content within the UrQMD model. It tests the hypothesis from Dunlop (2011) that transported quarks have larger v2 than produced quarks, and contrasts with explanations based on mean-field potentials in the AMPT model (Xu 2012) or the NJL model (Xu 2013, Song 2012). The results enable future work incorporating partonic interactions into transport models to reproduce the v2 splitting at higher energies (39 GeV and above).
