# Bayesian Inference of the Symmetry Energy and the Neutron Skin in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb from CREX and PREX-2

> Using the recent model-independent determination of the charge-weak form factor difference $ΔF_{\rm CW}$ in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb by the CREX and PREX-2 collaborations together with some well-determined properties of doubly magic nuclei, we perform Bayesian inference of the symmetry energy $E_{\rm sym}(ρ)$ and the neutron skin thickness $Δr_{\rm np}$ of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb within the Skyrme energy density functional (EDF). We find the inferred $E_{\rm sym}(ρ)$ and $Δr_{\rm np}$ separately from CREX and PREX-2 are compatible with each other at $90\%$ C.L., although they are inconsistent at $68.3\%$ C.L. with CREX (PREX-2) favoring a very soft (stiff) $E_{\rm sym}(ρ)$ and rather small (large) $Δr_{\rm np}$. By combining the CREX and PREX-2 data, we obtain a soft symmetry energy around saturation density $ρ_0$ and thinner $Δr_{\rm np}$ of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, which are found to be closer to the corresponding results from CREX alone, implying the PREX-2 is less effective to constrain the $E_{\rm sym}(ρ)$ and $Δr_{\rm np}$ due to its lower precision of $ΔF_{\rm CW}$. Furthermore, we find the Skyrme EDF results inferred by combining the CREX and PREX-2 data nicely agree with the measured dipole polarizabilities $α_D$ in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb as well as the neutron matter equation of state from microscopic calculations. The implications of the inferred soft $E_{\rm sym}(ρ)$ around $ρ_0$ are discussed.

## Key claim

The CREX and PREX-2 measurements of the charge-weak form factor difference in \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{208}\)Pb are compatible with each other at 90% confidence level, although they are inconsistent at 68.3% confidence level, with CREX favoring a very soft symmetry energy and PREX-2 favoring a very stiff one; when combined, the data yield a soft symmetry energy around saturation density that is closer to the CREX-alone result, implying PREX-2 is less effective due to its lower precision.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference within the Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) framework, using the model-independent \(\Delta F_{\rm CW}\) values from CREX and PREX-2 together with well-determined properties (binding energies, charge radii, diffraction radii, surface thicknesses, spin-orbit splittings, neutron-proton Fermi energy differences, and breathing mode energies) of eight doubly magic nuclei. Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm was used to estimate posterior distributions of the ten Skyrme EDF parameters, including the symmetry energy \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho)\) and its slope \(L\), and the neutron skin thickness \(\Delta r_{\rm np}\) of \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Key numbers

*   \(\Delta F_{\rm CW}(q=0.8733\ {\rm fm}^{-1}) = 0.0277 \pm 0.0055\) for \(^{48}\)Ca from CREX.
*   \(\Delta F_{\rm CW}(q=0.3977\ {\rm fm}^{-1}) = 0.041 \pm 0.013\) for \(^{208}\)Pb from PREX-2.
*   From combining CREX and PREX-2 data (B-All): \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0) = 29.1_{-1.8(2.7)}^{+2.1(3.6)}\) MeV and \(L = 17.1_{-22.3(36.0)}^{+23.8(39.3)}\) MeV at 68.3% (90%) C.L.
*   From B-All: \(\Delta r_{\rm np}^{48} = 0.150_{-0.019(0.030)}^{+0.019(0.031)}\) fm and \(\Delta r_{\rm np}^{208} = 0.136_{-0.035(0.056)}^{+0.036(0.059)}\) fm at 68.3% (90%) C.L.
*   From B-All: \(E_{\rm sym}(2\rho_0/3) = 25.4_{-0.6(0.9)}^{+0.7(1.2)}\) MeV at 68.3% (90%) C.L.
*   The CREX-alone analysis gave \(L = -1_{-23(36)}^{+24(41)}\) MeV at 68.3% (90%) C.L.; the PREX-2-alone analysis gave \(L = 68_{-33(54)}^{+32(52)}\) MeV at 68.3% (90%) C.L.
*   The SkREx interaction predicts \(\Delta r_{\rm np}^{48}=0.152\) fm and \(\Delta r_{\rm np}^{208}=0.141\) fm.

## Context

This work addresses the tension between the CREX and PREX-2 results, which had been highlighted in analyses using modern EDFs (Reinhard 2022, Yuksel 2022) and in the CREX collaboration paper (2022). The Bayesian approach extends previous constraints on the symmetry energy from the dipole polarizability \(\alpha_D\) measurements in \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{208}\)Pb at RCNP (Tamii 2011, Birkhan 2016) and from neutron matter equation of state calculations (Tews 2012, Drischler 2020). The inferred soft symmetry energy around saturation density has implications for neutron star core-crust transition density and the location of the neutron-drip line, and the authors provide a new Skyrme interaction (SkREx) that simultaneously reproduces the combined CREX and PREX-2 data, the measured dipole polarizabilities, and microscopic neutron matter EOS.
