# Overview of pion-nucleus interaction at low energies

> Predictions of the existence of well-defined deeply bound pionic atom states for heavy nuclei and the eventual observation of such states by the (d,3He) reaction have revived interest in the pion-nucleus interaction at threshold and in its relation to the corresponding pion-nucleon interaction. Explanation of the 'anomalous' s-wave repulsion in terms of partial restoration of chiral symmetry and/or in terms of energy-dependence effects have been tested in global fits to pionic atom data and in a recent dedicated elastic scattering experiment. The role of neutron density distributions in this context is discussed in detail for the first time.

# Overview of pion-nucleus interaction at low energies

## Key claim
The "anomalous" s-wave repulsion observed in pionic atoms and low-energy pion-nucleus scattering can be fully explained by combining a chiral-motivated density dependence of the isovector interaction with an energy dependence of the isoscalar interaction, but only when neutron density distributions are carefully accounted for; the best fits to global pionic atom data require neutron rms radii that are consistent with other sources only when both effects are included.

## Method
Global fits of the Ericson-Ericson (EE) optical potential to 100 pionic atom data points across the periodic table were performed, scanning over neutron density rms radii parameterized by the neutron-proton difference parameter gamma. The analysis tested three neutron density shapes (skin, halo, and average) and compared conventional potentials with chiral-motivated density dependence of the isovector amplitude b1 and energy-dependent effects, separately and combined. Elastic scattering data for 21.5 MeV pi+ and pi- on Si, Ca, Ni, and Zr were analyzed with the same potential models, with absolute normalization based on parallel measurements of Coulomb scattering of muons.

## Key numbers
- Free pi-N isovector amplitude b1 = -0.088 +/- 0.001 m_pi^-1 (from free pi-N interaction)
- Best fit to pionic atoms with finite range (0.9 +/- 0.1 fm rms) applied only to p-wave part yields gamma close to 1.0 fm for neutron rms radii
- For elastic scattering at 21.5 MeV: conventional model gives b1 = -0.114 +/- 0.006 m_pi^-1 (chi^2=134 for 72 points); chiral model gives b1 = -0.081 +/- 0.005 m_pi^-1 (chi^2=88); energy-dependent model gives b1 = -0.119 +/- 0.006 m_pi^-1 (chi^2=80); combined chiral+energy model gives b1 = -0.083 +/- 0.005 m_pi^-1 (chi^2=88)
- The chiral-motivated density dependence uses b1(rho) = b1(0)/(1-2.3 rho) for sigma=50 MeV, with rho in fm^-3

## Context
This work extends the global fitting approach of Friedman and Gal (2003) and the predictions of deeply bound pionic states by Friedman and Soff (1985), which were first observed by Yamazaki et al. (1996). The analysis tests the chiral-motivated explanation of Weise (2001) and energy-dependent effects from Ericson and Tauscher (1982) against the conventional EE potential of Ericson and Ericson (1966). The results enable consistent treatment of pion-nucleus interactions from threshold to scattering energies, and the neutron density information obtained can be compared with antiprotonic atom analyses of Trzcinska et al. (2001) and Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2005).
