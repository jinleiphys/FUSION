# Neutral current neutrino and antineutrino scattering off the polarized nucleon

> The elastic and inelastic neutral current $ν$ ($\overlineν$) scattering off the polarized nucleon is discussed. The inelastic scattering concerns the single-pion production process. We show that the spin asymmetries' measurement can help to distinguish between neutrino and antineutrino neutral current scattering processes. The spin asymmetries also encode information about a type of target. Eventually, detailed studies of the inelastic spin asymmetries can improve understanding of the resonant-nonresonant pion production mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper shows that measuring target spin asymmetries in neutral current neutrino and antineutrino scattering off polarized nucleons can distinguish between neutrino- and antineutrino-induced processes at energies below about 1 GeV, but this discrimination gradually vanishes at higher beam energies; the asymmetries also encode information about the target nucleon type and the resonant-nonresonant content of single-pion production amplitudes.

## Method

The authors compute target spin asymmetries for neutral current elastic and single-pion production (SPP) scattering of neutrinos and antineutrinos off polarized protons, neutrons, and isoscalar targets. The elastic cross sections use standard vector and axial form factors with dipole parametrization (axial mass M_A = 1 GeV), while the SPP cross sections employ the model of Hernandez et al. (2007) which sums seven amplitudes: two Delta-pole terms and five nonresonant background terms (nucleon pole, crossed nucleon pole, pion in flight, contact term, and pion pole). The spin asymmetries are defined as ratios of total cross sections for target polarization along the beam (longitudinal) and perpendicular to the beam (transverse), computed using FORM for analytic expressions and C++ for numerical integration.

## Key numbers

- For neutrino (antineutrino) energies below approximately 0.7 GeV, the transverse components of target spin asymmetries for elastic and SPP processes differ in sign between neutrino and antineutrino interactions.
- The longitudinal and transverse spin asymmetries for elastic scattering on proton, neutron, and isoscalar targets are computed for energies from 0.01 GeV to 4 GeV, covering supernova and accelerator ranges.
- For neutrino (antineutrino) energies around 5 GeV, the asymmetries converge to fixed values specific to each target type.
- The normal component of the spin asymmetry for SPP processes is of order 10^{-3} and would be difficult to measure.
- The axial form factor uses a dipole parametrization with F_A(0) = 1.2723 and axial mass M_A = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on polarization effects in charged current quasielastic scattering (Graczyk 2019) and charged current single-pion production (Graczyk 2017, 2019, 2021) to the neutral current channel. It builds on earlier work by Jachowicz et al. (2004) who showed that recoil nucleon polarization in neutral current neutrino-nucleus scattering can help distinguish neutrino from antineutrino interactions, and by Bilenky and Christova (2013) who noted sensitivity of recoil nucleon polarization to the axial form factor. The results enable future experimental studies using polarized targets in long-baseline oscillation experiments (T2K, NOvA) and supernova neutrino detection, potentially improving determination of oscillation parameters and the CP violation phase.
