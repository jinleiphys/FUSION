# Signatures of collective behavior in small systems

> We perform 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics calculations of proton-nucleus (pA) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions. Our goal is to understand the apparent collective behavior recently observed in pA collisions and to verify whether the highest multiplicity collision systems can be accurately described as a relativistic fluid. We compare our calculations of flow variables to existing measurements, and demonstrate that hydrodynamics correctly captures the measured trends. We show that our predictions for the pair correlation observable $r_n$ are validated by recent experimental pA measurements, and that our results are sensitive to the granularity of the initial state. We also compare our results with measurements done for nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic simulations can describe a wide range of flow observables in both proton-nucleus (pA) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions, but the same set of model parameters cannot simultaneously describe the pair correlation observable \(r_n\) for both systems, suggesting that a unified hydrodynamic description of pA and AA data remains challenging.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the music code, with initial entropy density profiles generated from a Monte Carlo Glauber model that includes longitudinal anisotropy and local entropy density fluctuations. The hydrodynamic evolution used Israel-Stewart theory for shear stress, with a shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio \(\eta/s = 0\)–\(0.08\), a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV, and a lattice QCD equation of state from Huovinen and Petreczky (2009). They compared their calculations of flow cumulants \(v_n\{2\}\) and \(v_n\{4\}\) and the pair correlation observable \(r_n(p_T^a, p_T^b)\) to experimental data from CMS and ALICE for p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- The initial entropy density profile is set at thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c, with a nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section of \(\sigma_{\rm NN} = 67\) mb.
- The granularity parameter \(\sigma\) (length scale of entropy density fluctuations) is varied in the range \(0.4\)–\(0.8\) fm.
- The shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) is varied in the range \(0\)–\(0.08\).
- The freeze-out temperature is \(T = 150\) MeV.
- For p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, the longitudinal parameters are \(\eta_0 = 2.5\), \(\sigma_\eta = 1.4\), and \(y_{\rm beam} = 8.58\).
- The agreement between hydrodynamic calculations and CMS data for \(v_2\{2\}\) and \(v_2\{4\}\) in p-Pb collisions visibly breaks when the offline multiplicity \(N_{\rm offline} < 50\).
- The CMS data for \(r_2(p_T^a, p_T^b)\) in p-Pb collisions favors the calculation with larger granularity (\(\sigma = 0.4\) fm) over smaller granularity (\(\sigma = 0.8\) fm).

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling, previously successful for large nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at RHIC and LHC, to small proton-nucleus (pA) systems where collective behavior was recently observed by ATLAS (2012), CMS (2012), ALICE (2012), and PHENIX (2013). The authors show that the pair correlation observable \(r_n\) is sensitive to initial-state granularity, unlike traditional flow observables, and that their predictions for \(r_2\) and \(r_3\) were subsequently confirmed by CMS (2014). The inability to describe both pA and AA data with the same parameters motivates future work to resolve this puzzle.
