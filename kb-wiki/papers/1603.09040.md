# Centrality dependence of midrapidity density from GeV to TeV heavy-ion collisions in the effective-energy universality picture of hadroproduction

> The dependence on centrality, or on the number of nucleon participants, of the midrapidity density of charged particles measured in heavy-ion collisions at the collision energy of about 20 GeV at RHIC to the highest LHC energy of 5 TeV is investigated within the recently proposed effective-energy approach. This approach relates multihadron production in different types of collisions by combining, under the proper scaling of the collision energy, the constituent quark picture with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics. The measurements are shown to be well described based on the similarity of multihadron production process in (anti)proton-proton interactions and heavy-ion collisions driven by the centrality-dependent effective energy of participants.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the centrality dependence of charged-particle midrapidity density in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC (about 20 GeV) to LHC (5.02 TeV) is well described by the effective-energy approach, which relates heavy-ion collisions to proton-proton interactions through a centrality-dependent effective energy. The hedge is that the description works best for central and mid-central collisions, with some deviations in the most peripheral collisions at low energy (19.6 GeV) and in the most central collisions at LHC energies, attributed to experimental limitations and missing proton-proton reference data at very high energies.

## Method
The authors apply the effective-energy approach, which combines the constituent quark model with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics, to relate the midrapidity density per participant pair in heavy-ion collisions to that in proton-proton collisions at a scaled center-of-mass energy. The centrality is converted into an effective energy via the relation ε_NN = √s_NN (1 − α), where α is the centrality fraction, and the proton-proton reference is taken at √s_pp = 3 ε_NN. The calculations use existing proton-proton data for midrapidity density and multiplicity, and heavy-ion data for total multiplicity, with power-law and linear-log fits applied where experimental data are unavailable.

## Key numbers
- The analysis covers heavy-ion collision energies from √s_NN = 19.6 GeV (RHIC PHOBOS AuAu) up to √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (LHC ALICE PbPb).
- The effective energy for a given centrality α is defined as ε_NN = √s_NN (1 − α).
- The scaling relation uses √s_pp = 3 ε_NN, i.e., the proton-proton reference energy is three times the effective heavy-ion energy.
- The calculations use a power-law fit for N_ch^pp of the form N_ch^pp = 3.102 s_pp^{0.178} and a linear-log fit ρ_pp = −0.308 + 0.276 ln(s_pp) for √s_pp ≤ 53 GeV, and a CMS power-law fit ρ_pp = −0.402 + s_pp^{0.101} for √s_pp > 53 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the effective-energy approach previously applied to mean multiplicity data (Sarkisyan et al., 2015) and midrapidity density in central collisions (Sarkisyan et al., 2015) to the centrality dependence of midrapidity density, including the newest ALICE data at 5.02 TeV. The approach builds on the constituent quark model and Landau hydrodynamics, and it also relates to the observed similarity between e+e− and pp/pbar-p multiplicity data (L3 Collaboration, 2000) and the universality of hadroproduction across collision systems. The findings suggest a possible change in the multihadron production mechanism at TeV energies, where collisions appear to obey a head-on regime for all centralities, and they enable future tests at higher energies and in different collision types.
