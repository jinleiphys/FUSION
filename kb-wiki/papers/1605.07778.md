# Global analysis of isospin dependent microscopic nucleon-nucleus optical potential in Dirac Bruckner Hartree-Fock approach

> The Microscopic Otical Model Potential is evaluated within a relativistic scheme which provides a natural and consistent relation between the spin-orbit part and the central part of the potential. The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach provides such a microscopic relativistic scheme, which is based on a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction and reproduce the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter without any adjustable parameter. Its solution using the projection technique within the subtracted T-matrix (STM) representation provides a reliable extension to asymmetric nuclear matter, which is important to describe the features of the isospin asymmetric nuclei. Therefore, the present work aims to perform a global analysis of the isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleus MOP based on the DBHF calculation in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. The DBHF is used to evaluate the relativistic structure of the nucleon self-energies in nuclear matter at various densities and asymmetries. The Schrödinger equivalent potentials of finite nuclei are derived from these Dirac components by a local density approximation (LDA). The nucleon-nucleus scattering calculations are carried out for a broad spectrum $n$ and $p$ scattering experiments below 200 MeV with targets ranging from $^{12}$C to $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a microscopic optical potential (MOP) derived from the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, with only a free range factor \(t\) and minor adjustments to scalar and vector potentials at low densities, can satisfactorily reproduce nucleon-nucleus scattering observables across a broad mass range and energy region below 200 MeV, and may therefore have predictive power for unstable nuclei. The hedge is that the DBHF calculations yield reliable results only for densities \(\rho > 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\), requiring extrapolation to lower densities with auxiliary mesh points and enhancement factors (\(f_1, f_2\)) to maintain natural behavior.

## Method

The DBHF approach was used to evaluate the relativistic scalar and vector components of the nucleon self-energy in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at various densities and isospin asymmetries, using the Bonn-B potential for the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. The Schrödinger equivalent central, spin-orbit, and Darwin potentials for finite nuclei were derived from these Dirac components via a local density approximation (LDA) with a finite-range correction (improved LDA, ILDA) using a Gaussian form with range parameter \(t\). Radial nucleon densities for targets from \(^{12}\)C to \(^{208}\)Pb were taken from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1S force, and a \(\chi^2\) assessment system based on the global simulated annealing algorithm (GSA) was developed to optimize the free parameters \(t\) and the low-density Dirac potentials.

## Key numbers

* The range parameter \(t\) was optimized to 1.35 fm for proton scattering (with \(\chi^2/N = 0.29\) over 1028 data points of d\(\sigma\)/d\(\Omega\) in 18 data sets) and 1.45 fm for neutron scattering.
* The DBHF calculations provide reliable results only for densities \(\rho > 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\); auxiliary mesh points were introduced at \(\rho = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\) for real parts and at \(\rho = 0.04\) and 0.06 fm\(^{-3}\) for imaginary parts, with enhancement factors (\(f_1, f_2\)) of (0.86, 1.14).
* The analysis covered targets from \(^{12}\)C to \(^{208}\)Pb for incident energies below 200 MeV, involving about 500 sets of elastic scattering angular distributions, 30 sets of analyzing power angular distributions, and 20 sets of total neutron cross sections for 32 different targets.

## Context

This work extends the preliminary study by Xu et al. (2012) that applied the DBHF-based MOP to \(^{27}\)Al, now performing a global analysis across a wide range of nuclei and energies. It contrasts with non-relativistic approaches such as the Mahaux scheme (Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux, 1977) and the \(g\)-folding method (Amos et al., 2000), which require separate treatment of the spin-orbit potential, whereas the relativistic DBHF scheme provides a natural relation between spin-orbit and central potentials. The results are compared with the widely used phenomenological Koning-Delaroche (KD) global optical potential (Koning and Delaroche, 2003), and the present MOP is intended to enable reliable predictions for unstable nuclei and nuclei beyond the line of \(\beta\) stability.
