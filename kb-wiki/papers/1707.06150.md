# Decays of light mesons triggered by chiral chemical potential

> Light meson ( $π,\ σ,\ a_0$) properties in the environment with chiral imbalance are analyzed with the help of meson effective Lagrangian associated with QCD. New spatial parity violating decays of scalar mesons arise as a result of mixing of $π$ and $a_0$ mesons under influence of chiral charge density. The pion electromagnetic form-factor gets an unusual parity-odd contribution. Pion effective masses depend on their velocities and may vanish in flight. The possible determination of chiral chemical potential in heavy ion collisions based on above mentioned phenomena is discussed.

## Key claim
Light meson properties (π, σ, a₀) are modified in a fireball with chiral imbalance, and new parity-violating decays of scalar mesons arise from π–a₀ mixing induced by a chiral chemical potential μ₅, but the effective pion mass may vanish in flight without causing vacuum instabilities.

## Method
The authors construct an effective meson Lagrangian based on QCD, extended with an axial/chiral chemical potential μ₅ to account for local parity breaking from topological fluctuations. They derive the bilinear terms for π and a₀ mesons, diagonalize the mass matrix to obtain mixed eigenstates, and compute decay widths for exotic radiative decays (a₀⁺ → π⁺γ and a₀⁰ → γγ) using parity-odd Wess-Zumino-Witten vertices.

## Key numbers
- Lagrangian parameters fixed from vacuum scalar meson spectra: λ₁ = 16.4850, λ₂ = −13.1313, c = −4.46874 × 10⁴ MeV², b = 1.61594 × 10⁵ MeV².
- Fireball size L = 5–10 fm for estimating topological charge lifetime.
- Decay widths computed for |q⃗| = 200 MeV and μ₅ = 200 MeV for a₀⁰ → γγ and a₀⁺ → π⁺γ decays.
- Effective masses of π̃, σ, and ã₀ are shown as functions of 3-momentum at various μ₅ values (figures with μ₅ = 200 MeV displayed).

## Context
This work extends the study of local parity breaking in heavy-ion collisions, building on the chiral magnetic effect observed at RHIC (2009) and LHC (2015) and on earlier effective Lagrangian approaches by Andrianov et al. (2014) and Giacosa (2007). It enables experimental searches for parity violation via exotic a₀ decays and πγ scattering, and complements lattice QCD computations using the chiral chemical potential method by Braguta (2016). The authors previously reported (eQCD2016) that vector mesons (ρ, ω) also show polarization-dependent mass splitting, which may explain the anomalous dilepton excess seen in CERES, PHENIX, STAR, NA60, and ALICE experiments.
