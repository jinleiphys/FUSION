# Effects of the magnetic moment interaction between nucleons on observables in the 3N continuum

> The influence of the magnetic moment interaction of nucleons on nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and breakup cross sections and on elastic scattering polarization observables has been studied. Among the numerous elastic scattering observables only the vector analyzing powers were found to show a significant effect, and of opposite sign for the proton-deuteron and neutron-deuteron systems. This finding results in an even larger discrepancy than the one previously established between neutron-deuteron data and theoretical calculations. For the breakup reaction the largest effect was found for the final-state-interaction cross sections. The consequences of this observation on previous determinations of the ^1S_0 scattering lengths from breakup data are discussed.

# Effects of the magnetic moment interaction between nucleons on observables in the 3N continuum

## Key claim
The magnetic moment interaction (MMI) between nucleons produces significant, opposite-sign effects on the vector analyzing powers in proton-deuteron versus neutron-deuteron elastic scattering, enlarging the previously established discrepancy between nd data and theory; however, the MMI corrections to the 1S0 scattering lengths extracted from nd breakup final-state-interaction data are nearly canceled when the MMI is treated consistently at both the NN and 3N levels, so the MMI does not explain the conflicting values of the nn scattering length reported in recent experiments.

## Method
The authors solved the 3N Faddeev equations in momentum space and a partial-wave basis, including the magnetic moment interactions (from the AV18 potential parametrization) in addition to the strong AV18 or CD Bonn NN potentials for the pp, np, and nn subsystems. Calculations were performed for both pd and nd elastic scattering and breakup at several incident energies, with total 3N angular momenta up to J=25/2 and two-nucleon subsystem angular momenta up to j_max=3, using both an approximate "2/3-1/3 rule" for isospin T=1 states and a full treatment including T=3/2 states.

## Key numbers
- At E_n^lab = 3 MeV, the MMI raises the maximum of A_y(theta) and iT_11(theta) in pd scattering by approximately 4%, while reducing the maximum in nd scattering by approximately 3%.
- The largest changes in breakup cross sections reach approximately 10%, located mostly in final-state-interaction (FSI) geometries.
- At E_p^lab = 13 MeV, MMI effects on quasi-free scattering (QFS) configurations are never larger than 2%, and on space-star (SST) configurations are smaller than 1%.
- The relative changes of FSI cross sections for the nn peak are a factor of approximately 2 smaller than for np and pp peaks, corresponding to the ratio mu_p^2 / mu_n^2 = 2.13.
- The MMI correction to the np scattering length extracted from nd breakup is approximately +0.9 to +1.0 fm (depending on configuration), while the correction to the nn scattering length is approximately -0.24 to -0.32 fm.
- In 2N calculations, including MMI increases the magnitude of a_np by -0.779 fm (AV18) or -0.945 fm (CD Bonn), and decreases the magnitude of a_nn by +0.331 fm (AV18) or +0.368 fm (CD Bonn).

## Context
This work extends previous 3N continuum Faddeev calculations (Glöckle et al., 1996) that were restricted to pure strong nuclear forces, going beyond the approximate quasi two-body calculation by Stoks (1998) at 3 MeV which predicted only tiny effects on A_y. The results support the speculation by Neidel et al. (2003) that the difference between low-energy nd and pd A_y data is due to MMI. The findings show that MMI cannot resolve the conflicting values for the nn 1S0 scattering length obtained by González Trotter et al. (1999) and Huhn et al. (2000), nor the discrepancies between theory and data for nd breakup SST and nn QFS cross sections. The work enables reliable extraction of scattering lengths from breakup data provided MMI is treated consistently at both the NN and 3N levels.
