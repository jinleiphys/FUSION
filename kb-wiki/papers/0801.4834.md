# Level crossing of particle-hole and mesonic modes in eta mesic nuclei

> We study eta meson properties in the infinite nuclear matter and in atomic nuclei with an emphasis on effects of the eta coupling to N*(1535)--nucleon-hole modes. The N*(1535) resonance, which dominates the low-energy eta-nucleon scattering, can be seen as a chiral partner of the nucleon. The change of the chiral mass gap between the N* and the nucleon in a nuclear medium has an impact on the properties of the eta-nucleus system. If the N*-nucleon mass gap decreases with a density increase (chiral symmetry restoration) the calculations show the existence of the resonance state at the energy about 60 MeV and two bound eta-nucleus states with the binding energies about -80 MeV. These states can have strong effect on predicted cross sections of the ^12C (gamma,p) ^11B reaction with eta-meson production.

## Key claim

If the N*-nucleon mass gap decreases with increasing nuclear density (chiral symmetry restoration), the calculations predict a resonance state at about 60 MeV and two bound eta-nucleus states with binding energies around -80 MeV, which could strongly affect predicted cross sections of the 12C(gamma,p)11B reaction with eta-meson production.

## Method

The authors constructed the eta meson Green function in nuclear matter using the N*(1535) dominance in eta-nucleon scattering, with the self-energy calculated from N*-nucleon-hole excitations in the gas approximation. They solved the Dyson equation to find the two branches of the eta spectrum in infinite nuclear matter, then studied finite nuclei by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space for the eta-nucleus scattering amplitude using a local density approximation for the self-energy. The formation spectra for the 12C(gamma,p)11B reaction were calculated in the distorted-wave impulse approximation using the Green function method.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum eta mass: m_eta = 547 MeV; vacuum N* mass: m_N* = 1535 MeV; vacuum nucleon mass: m_N = 939 MeV
- Vacuum N* -> eta N decay width: Gamma_N*->etaN = 75 MeV
- eta N scattering length calculated in this work: a_etaN = 0.55 + i 0.39 fm
- For C=0.2 and L=0: two bound states at E1 = (-88.53 - i 11.54) MeV and E2 = (-74.13 - i 15.00) MeV; one resonance at E = (61.02 - i 25.82) MeV on the second Riemann sheet; one virtual state at E = (6.40 - i 0.46) MeV on the first Riemann sheet
- For C=0 and L=0: one bound state at (-22.18 - i 12.88) MeV; one resonance at (3.47 - i 54.78) MeV; another resonance at (31.91 - i 54.79) MeV; virtual state at (6.13 - i 0.69) MeV
- Level crossing occurs at density rho ~ 0.4 rho_0 for k=0 with C=0.2
- Parameter C (strength of chiral restoration at normal density) set to C=0.2, with reasonable range C=0.1-0.3

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of eta bound states in nuclei predicted by Haider and Liu (1986) and subsequent calculations by Kohno (1989), Tsushima (1998), Garcia-Recio (2002), Jido (2002), and Nagahiro (2003, 2005). It differs from previous approaches by explicitly including the energy dependence of the eta optical potential arising from the coupling to N*-nucleon-hole modes, rather than using energy-independent optical potentials. The results enable more realistic predictions for the 12C(gamma,p)11B reaction and other nucleon pickup processes (such as (d,3He) and (pi,p) reactions) that could be used to search for eta mesic nuclei experimentally.
