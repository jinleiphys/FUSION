# A dispersion theoretical approach to the threshold amplitudes of pion photoproduction

> We give predictions for the partial wave amplitudes of pion photoproduction near threshold by means of dispersion relations at fixed t. The free parameters of this approach are determined by a fit to experimental data in the energy range 160 MeV $\le E_γ \le$ 420 MeV. The observables near threshold are found to be rather sensitive to the amplitudes in the resonance region, in particular to the $Δ$ (1232) and $N^*$ (1440). We obtain a good agreement with the existing threshold data for both charged and neutral pion production. Our predictions also agree well with the results of chiral perturbation theory, except for neutral pion production off the neutron.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dispersion relations at fixed t, with parameters fitted to data in the 160–420 MeV range, can predict threshold amplitudes for pion photoproduction, but the approach is limited by the quality of input data and shows a significant discrepancy with chiral perturbation theory for neutral pion production off the neutron.

## Method

The authors used fixed-t dispersion relations for the invariant amplitudes of pion photoproduction, projecting them onto multipole amplitudes via integral equations with known kernels. The real and imaginary parts of the amplitudes were related by unitarity via Watson's theorem below two-pion threshold, with an ansatz based on unitarity used from two-pion threshold up to W = 2 GeV. Ten free parameters (including four coupling parameters for t-channel ρ- and ω-exchange and parameters for homogeneous equation solutions) were fitted to experimental data from MAMI, Bonn, Frascati, and TRIUMF in the energy range 160 MeV ≤ Eγ ≤ 420 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Kroll-Ruderman term prediction for s-wave multipole at threshold: E0+thr(π+n) = 27.6 × 10−3/mπ+, E0+thr(π−p) = −31.7 × 10−3/mπ+ (assuming gπN = 13.4)
- Δ(1232) contribution to E0+(−) determined as 0.09 × 10−3/mπ+, with E1+(3/2) contribution of 0.27 × 10−3/mπ+
- Final threshold value for E0+(pπ0) after iteration: −1.22 × 10−3/mπ
- Final threshold value for E0+(nπ0): +1.19 × 10−3/mπ, compared to ChPT prediction of 2.1 × 10−3/mπ
- From the Panofsky ratio (P = 1.543 ± 0.008) and the authors' threshold amplitude, a1 − a3 = 0.253/mπ, compared to 0.274 ± 0.012/mπ from partial wave analysis (KH80 solution) and 0.288/mπ from pionic atoms
- Preliminary TRIUMF analysis gave E0+thr(π−p) = (−34.6 ± 1.0) × 10−3/mπ+ from angular distributions, but (−32.8 ± 0.7) × 10−3/mπ+ from total cross section extrapolation

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Schwela et al. (1969) and Höhler (1964) to threshold pion photoproduction, addressing the discrepancy between the low energy theorem of de Baenst (1970) and Vainshtein and Zakharov (1972) and experimental data from Saclay (Maz86) and Mainz (Bec90). The results agree well with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard et al. (1991, 1996) for charged pion production and for p-waves, but disagree for neutral pion production off the neutron. The approach enables independent determination of the pion-nucleon scattering length difference a1−a3 and identifies the need for improved neutron data and better understanding of the M1− multipole connected with the Roper resonance.
