# Thermodynamic instabilities in nonlocal NJL models

> It has been recently pointed out, that nonlocal Nambu--Jona-Lasinio models, may present unphysical thermodynamical behavior like negative pressure and oscillating entropy. Here we show how these thermodynamic instabilities can be related to the analytical structure of the poles of the quark propagator in the model. The analysis is carried out for two different regulators and we show, in each case, how the instabilities are related to the pressence of highly unstable poles. We also argue that the softening of these instabilities by the inclusion of the Polyakov loop is related to the effect the latter has on the poles of the propagator.

## Key claim

The paper shows that thermodynamic instabilities (negative pressure, oscillating entropy) in nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nNJL) models are caused by the presence of "ill defined complex poles" in the quark propagator (poles with negative squared mass or decay width comparable to mass), and that the softening of these instabilities by the Polyakov loop arises from new, more stable poles introduced by the loop.

## Method

The authors analyze the poles of the light quark propagator in the mean-field approximation for two regulators: a Gaussian regulator and a fractional Lorentzian regulator. They solve the gap equation for the mean scalar field \(\bar{\sigma}(T)\) (an order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking) and classify poles as real, well-defined complex, or ill-defined complex based on their squared mass and decay width. They then compare the temperature dependence of \(\bar{\sigma}\) when including all poles versus excluding ill-defined complex poles, and examine how the Polyakov loop modifies the pole structure.

## Key numbers

- Parameter set A (Gaussian regulator): \(\Lambda = 687\) MeV, \(m = 6\) MeV, \(G\Lambda^2 = 28.43\), \(\bar{\sigma}_0 = 677.8\) MeV; produces an ill-defined complex pole with negative squared mass and a thermodynamic instability bump near \(T \approx 100\) MeV.
- Parameter set B (Gaussian regulator): \(\Lambda = 1042.2\) MeV, \(m = 4.6\) MeV, \(G\Lambda^2 = 15.08\), \(\bar{\sigma}_0 = 235\) MeV; produces two real poles and no thermodynamic instability.
- For the fractional Lorentzian regulator \(r^2(q_E^2) = 1/[1 + (q_E^2/\Lambda^2)^{3/2}]\), the propagator has four poles: one well-defined complex pole \(\mathcal{M}_1\) and three ill-defined complex poles \(\mathcal{M}_{2,3,4}\).

## Context

This work extends the findings of Blaschke (2013) and Blaschke (2014), who first reported negative pressure and oscillating entropy in nNJL models. It provides a mechanistic explanation for those instabilities in terms of pole structure, building on the nonlocal NJL framework developed by Birse (2001, 2002) and Scoccola (2002). The results enable a new criterion for parameter selection in nNJL models (avoiding ill-defined complex poles) and clarify how the Polyakov loop, as implemented by Yo (2002) and Weise (2001), stabilizes the thermodynamics.
