# Generalized Mesons in Dense QCD

> QCD superconductors in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase support excitations (generalized mesons) that can be described as pairs of particles or holes (rather than particle-hole) around a gapped Fermi surface. In weak coupling and to leading logarithm accuracy the scalar and pseudoscalar excitations are massless and the vector and axial-vector excitations are massive and degenerate. The massless scalar excitations are combined with the longitudinal gluons leading to the Meissner effect in the CFL phase. The mass of the composite vector excitations is close to twice the gap in weak coupling, but goes asymptotically to zero with increasing coupling thereby realizing Georgi's vector limit in cold and dense matter. We discuss the possible mixing of the composite scalar and vector excitations with the gluons, their possible coupling to the modified photons and their decay into light pseudoscalars in the CFL phase. The issue of hidden gauge-symmetry in the QCD superconductor is critically examined. The physical implications of our results on soft dilepton and neutrino emission in cold and dense matter are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

In the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense QCD, the scalar and pseudoscalar generalized mesons are massless to leading logarithm accuracy, while the vector and axial-vector generalized mesons are massive and degenerate; the vector meson mass is close to twice the gap in weak coupling but goes asymptotically to zero with increasing coupling, realizing Georgi's vector limit in cold and dense matter.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for generalized meson excitations (pairs of quasiparticles or quasiholes around a gapped Fermi surface) in the CFL phase, using the Nambu-Gorkov formalism for quark propagators with a gap function. The analysis is performed in weak coupling to leading logarithm accuracy, using a simplified gluon propagator with electric and magnetic screening masses, and the gap equation is solved using logarithmic variables. The scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector vertices are constructed and their masses and form factors are derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the meson rest frame.

## Key numbers

* The scalar and pseudoscalar excitations have mass \(M = 0\) in the chiral limit, as shown by equation (GOLD): \(M = 2G_0 \left(1 - e^{-(\sqrt{3}-\sqrt{3})\pi/h_*}\right)^{\frac12} = 0\).
* The pion decay constant squared is \(F^2 \approx \kappa^2 \mu^2 / (16\pi^2)\) for the generalized pions, with \(\kappa = 4\) matching the axial-vector current normalization.
* The square of the velocity of the Goldstone modes is \(v^2 = F_S^2 / F_T^2 = 1/3\).
* For equal quark masses \(m_u = m_d = m_s \equiv m_q\), the Goldstone mass squared at next-to-leading order is \(M^2 \approx (2^{23} \pi^{10} m_q^2) / (3^4 \sqrt{2} \, g^{11}) \times \exp(-3\sqrt{2}\pi^2 / g)\).
* The Meissner mass for static colored magnetic fields is of order \(m_E \approx g\mu\), not of order \(g G_0\).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic analysis of light Goldstone modes in the CFL phase previously performed by Rho, Wirzba, and Zahed (1999) for generalized pions, now including scalar, vector, and axial-vector excitations. It builds on the effective Lagrangian approach of Hong, Rho, and Zahed (1999) and the gap equation solutions of Son (1999) and Schafer and Wilczek (1999). The results enable calculations of soft dilepton and neutrino emission from cold and dense matter, and critically examine the applicability of hidden gauge symmetry and vector dominance to finite-size composite mesons in the QCD superconductor.
