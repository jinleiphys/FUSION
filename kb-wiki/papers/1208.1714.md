# On chiral corrections to nucleon GPD

> Within the pion-nucleon chiral perturbation theory we derive the leading chiral correction to the nucleon GPD at $ξ=0$. We discuss the difficulties of consideration of nonlocal light-cone operators within the theory with a heavy particle and the methods to solve the difficulties. The consideration of the chiral corrections directly for nonlocal operators allows to resolve the ambiguity of the inverse Mellin transformation. In particular, we show that the mixing between axial and vector GPDs are of order $m_π^2/M_N^2$, which is two orders of magnitude less that it follows from the Mellin moments calculation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the leading chiral correction to nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at zero skewness can be computed directly from nonlocal light-cone operators in covariant chiral perturbation theory, resolving an ambiguity in the inverse Mellin transformation. The mixing between axial and vector GPDs is of order \(m_\pi^2/M_N^2\) (approximately \(2\times10^{-2}\)), which is two orders of magnitude larger than the estimate from Mellin moments calculations.

## Method

The authors construct effective nonlocal light-cone operators for vector and axial currents in pion-nucleon chiral perturbation theory, matching them to QCD operators. They perform one-loop calculations of the five contributing diagrams using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to handle the heavy baryon limit consistently, avoiding the non-commutativity of the non-relativistic limit with the light-cone operator definition. The calculation is done directly in \(x\)-space (longitudinal momentum fraction) rather than through Mellin moments, keeping the light-cone separation parameter \(\lambda\) explicit to preserve proper analytic properties.

## Key numbers

- The mixing between axial and vector GPDs is of order \(m_\pi^2/M_N^2 \approx 2\times10^{-2}\), compared to \(m_\pi^4/M_N^4 \approx 5\times10^{-4}\) from Mellin moments calculations.
- The pion decay constant is \(F_\pi \simeq 93\) MeV.
- The chiral expansion parameter is \(a_\chi = m_\pi^2/\Lambda_\chi^2\) with \(\Lambda_\chi \sim M \sim 4\pi F_\pi \sim 1\) GeV.
- The nucleon mass \(M\) is approximately 1 GeV, and the pion mass \(m_\pi\) is approximately 0.15 GeV, giving \(m_\pi/M \sim 0.15\).
- For the isoscalar case at \(x \sim 0.8\), the chiral correction from the mixing diagram is of order \(10\%\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of nucleon GPDs beyond the Mellin moments approach used in prior studies by Diehl (2006), Ando (2006), and Dorati (2007), which computed moments up to second chiral order. The direct nonlocal operator method resolves the ambiguity in inverse Mellin transformation identified in earlier work on pion GPDs by Kivel (2007, 2008) and Perevalova (2011). The results enable model-independent determination of the quark mass dependence and momentum transfer dependence of nucleon GPDs, particularly the behavior at large impact parameters governed by the pion cloud, and provide a framework for future calculations at non-zero skewness.
