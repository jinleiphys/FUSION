# $ΔI=1$ axial-vector mixing and charge symmetry breaking

> Phenomenological Lagrangians that exhibit (broken) chiral symmetry as well as isospin violation suggest short-range charge symmetry breaking (CSB) nucleon-nucleon potentials with a $\mbox{\boldmath $σ$}_1 \!\cdot\!\mbox{\boldmath $σ$}_2$ structure. This structure could be realized by the mixing of axial-vector ($1^+$) mesons in a single-meson exchange picture. The Coleman-Glashow scheme for $ΔI_{z}=1$ charge symmetry breaking applied to meson and baryon $SU(2)$ mass splittings suggests a universal scale. This scale can be extended to $ΔI=1$ nonstrange CSB transitions $\langle a_1^\circ|H_{em}|f_1\rangle$ of size $-0.005$ GeV$^2$. The resulting nucleon-nucleon axial-vector meson exchange CSB potential then predicts $ΔI=1$ effects which are small.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that axial-vector meson mixing ($a_1$–$f_1$) could realize a short-range charge symmetry breaking (CSB) nucleon-nucleon potential with a $\boldsymbol{\sigma}_1 \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}_2$ structure, but finds that the resulting CSB effects are small and the dimensionless coefficient characterizing the potential is much smaller than unity, indicating this mechanism does not saturate the corresponding term of the phenomenological chiral Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors derive a class III CSB nucleon-nucleon potential from $t$-channel exchange of axial-vector mesons, using the Coleman-Glashow scheme to estimate the $a_1$–$f_1$ mixing matrix element as $-0.005$ GeV$^2$. They compute the potential in the nonrelativistic limit for $S$-wave scattering, estimate the $a_1 N N$ and $f_1 N N$ coupling constants from two approaches (Sudarshan's $SU(4)$ model and axial-vector dominance with lattice QCD input), and calculate the resulting shifts in the $^1S_0$ scattering lengths and the $^3$H–$^3$He binding-energy difference using three different charge-symmetric potential models.

## Key numbers

- $\langle a_1^\circ|H_{em}|f_1\rangle \approx -0.005$ GeV$^2$: the universal scale for $\Delta I=1$ nonstrange CSB transitions, extended from the Coleman-Glashow scheme.
- $g_{a_1pp} \approx 9.4 \pm 1.2$: the $a_1 N N$ coupling constant from axial-vector dominance using lattice QCD values $m_{a_1} = 1250 \pm 80$ MeV and $f_{a_1} = 0.21 \pm 0.02$ GeV$^2$.
- $g_{a_1 N N} g_{f_1 N N} / 4\pi \approx 3.1$: the product of coupling constants from the axial-vector dominance estimate for $m_{a_1}=1250$ MeV.
- $g_{a_1 N N} g_{f_1 N N} / 4\pi \approx 1.7$: the product from Sudarshan's model.
- $g_{f_1 pp} \approx (0.45 \pm 0.025) g_{a_1 pp}$: the $f_1 N N$ coupling from $SU(3)$ analysis with mixing angle $\phi_A \approx 12^\circ$ and $f/d = 0.575 \pm 0.0165$.
- $\Delta a_{\rm exp} \equiv |a_{nn}| - |a_{pp}| \approx +1.1 \pm 0.6$ fm: the experimental difference in singlet scattering lengths.
- $\Delta E_{\rm exp} \equiv (^3{\rm H} - ^3{\rm He}) \approx 76 \pm 24$ keV: the experimental binding-energy difference.
- $\gamma_{\sigma}^{a_1 f_1} \approx -0.03 (\epsilon m_\pi^2 / f_\pi^2 \Lambda^2)$: the dimensionless coefficient from axial-vector mixing, far from ${\cal O}(1)$.

## Context

This work extends the Coleman-Glashow tadpole picture (Coleman and Glashow, 1964) for charge symmetry violation from pseudoscalar and vector mesons to axial-vector mesons, following the universal scale established by Coon and Scadron (1995). The results are compared with the phenomenological chiral Lagrangian analysis of isospin violation by van Kolck et al. (1994) and with specific meson-mixing models such as $\pi$–$\eta$ mixing (Coon et al., 1977) and $\rho$–$\omega$ mixing (Coon and Barrett, 1987) which successfully describe nuclear data. The small contribution from axial-vector mixing suggests that nonresonant $\rho$–$\pi$ exchange between two nucleons should be examined as an alternative mechanism to saturate the short-range CSB term of the chiral Lagrangian.
