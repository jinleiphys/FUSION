# Glauber isovector spin responses for (p,n) reactions at 494 MeV

> The separated isovector spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse responses from a recent (p,n) quasifree experiment at 494 MeV are analyzed in a Glauber theory framework up to two-step contributions. Nuclear correlations are treated in the continuum random phase approximation, accounting for the spreading width of the particle-hole states. A good description of the spin-longitudinal response is achieved, hinting at some evidence of pionic effects in the quasielastic region. The spin-transverse response is underestimated and we show that multistep contributions, while sizable, are not sufficient to provide an explanation of the discrepancy.

# Glauber isovector spin responses for (p,n) reactions at 494 MeV

## Key claim

A Glauber theory framework with continuum random phase approximation (RPA) correlations and spreading width of particle-hole states provides a good description of the spin-longitudinal response in quasifree (p,n) reactions at 494 MeV, hinting at pionic effects; however, the spin-transverse response is underestimated, and multistep contributions, while sizable, are insufficient to explain the discrepancy.

## Method

The author analyzed separated isovector spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse response functions extracted from polarization transfer measurements of (p,n) quasifree scattering on 12C at 494 MeV, using a Glauber theory framework that includes one-step and two-step contributions. Nuclear correlations were treated in the continuum RPA with a g'+pi+rho model for the particle-hole interaction (using g'=0.7) and a phenomenological spreading width for particle-hole states, employing Woods-Saxon wave functions for finite nuclei. The two-step contribution was evaluated through a convolution of two free response functions with an effective number of pairs participating in double scattering.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers at the quasielastic peak: q = 1.2, 1.7, and 2.5 fm^-1, corresponding to scattering angles of 12.5°, 18°, and 27°, respectively
- Effective total NN cross section used: approximately 26 mb
- Effective number of participating neutrons in 12C: approximately 2.3
- Two-step contribution to the spin-longitudinal response: approximately 5% of the total at theta = 27°
- Two-step contribution to the spin-transverse response: 20-30% of the particle-hole response at theta = 27°
- At q = 2.5 fm^-1, the quasielastic peak position is shifted downward by approximately 8 MeV due to relativistic kinematics

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations based on infinite nuclear matter models by Alberico, Ericson, and Molinari (1982) that predicted a large R_L/R_T ratio from pionic effects, and addresses the surprising experimental results from Taddeucci et al. (1994) showing no evidence for pion-induced effects in the ratio. The analysis improves upon the distorted wave Born approximation calculations by Ichimura et al. (1989) that failed to reproduce the momentum dependence and quasielastic peak position, and builds on the author's previous Glauber theory framework (De Pace and Viviani, 1993). The unexplained excess of transverse strength suggests the need for further theoretical work on reaction mechanisms and possibly the inclusion of delta degrees of freedom or two-particle-two-hole excitations.
