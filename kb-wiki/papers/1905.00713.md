# Electromagnetic properties of the $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark

> Experiments with intense photon and electron beams have the potential to provide access to nontrivial properties of the recently discovered $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark including its size, structure, magnetic moment, quadrupole and octupole deformations. In this paper we investigate the sensitivity of ongoing and planned experiments to various properties of the $d^*(2380)$, employing models based on both constituent quark and pion cloud frameworks. Our calculations indicate that for photoinduced reactions on the deuteron the $d^*(2380)$ is predominantly produced from the $D-wave$ component of the deuteron. We confirm earlier findings that the intrinsic quadrupole deformation of the $d^*(2380)$ should be small. We also demonstrate an ability to extract the $d^*(2380)$ magnetic moment and put constrains on the $d^*(2380)$ $M3/E2$ ratio.

# Electromagnetic properties of the $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that photoinduced production of the $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark from a deuteron target proceeds predominantly through the deuteron's D-wave component, and that the intrinsic quadrupole deformation of the $d^*(2380)$ should be small. The authors show that the magnetic moment and the M3/E2 multipole ratio can be extracted from planned double-polarization photoproduction experiments, though the calculations are based on geometrical arguments without inter-particle interactions and are intended as rough estimates.

## Method
The authors employ a pion-cloud model in which the $d^*(2380)$ wavefunction is decomposed into three components: a compact six-quark configuration (A-term), a $\Delta N\pi$ configuration (B-term), and a $NN\pi\pi$ configuration (C-term). They calculate the quadrupole and octupole moments by applying quadrupole and octupole operators to these wavefunction components, using the same formalism previously applied to the $\Delta$ resonance by Buchmann (2001). Transition moments from the deuteron to the $d^*(2380)$ are computed by considering only the deuteron D-wave component, with radial overlap integrals taken from the deuteron wavefunction and the $d^*(\Delta\Delta)$ wavefunction from the Beijing group (Y.B. Dong, 2015).

## Key numbers
- Mass of the $d^*(2380)$: $M_{d^*} = 2380$ MeV; vacuum width $\Gamma = 70$ MeV; quantum numbers $I(J^P) = 0(3^+)$.
- The compact six-quark configuration has radius $R_{d^*}(|6q\rangle) \sim 0.5$ fm and is predicted to be dominant ($\sim 69\%$); the $\Delta\Delta_{S-wave}$ component has radius $\sim 0.8$ fm; the $\Delta\Delta_{D-wave}$ component has radius $\sim 1.4$ fm and is of order $2\%$.
- Photon beam energy for $d^*(2380)$ production from deuteron: $E_\gamma \approx 570$ MeV.
- Calculated $d^*(2380)$ quadrupole moment: $Q_{d^*} \approx 0.21 Q_{\Delta^+} = 0.009$ $e$ fm$^2$, using $Q_{\Delta^+} = -0.043$ $e$ fm$^2$ from Pena (2001).
- Deuteron to $d^*$ transition quadrupole moment: $Q_{d\rightarrow d^*} \sim 0.20$ $e$ fm$^2$, with radial overlap ratio $\langle R_{d^D}|R_{d^*}\rangle / \langle R_{d^D}|R_{d^S}\rangle \approx 0.7$.
- Calculated $d^*(2380)$ octupole moment: $\Omega_{d^*} \approx -1.98 Q_{\Delta^+} \mu_N = 0.0089$ $e$ fm$^3$.
- Deuteron to $d^*$ transition octupole moment: $\Omega_{d\rightarrow d^*} \sim -0.021$ $e$ fm$^3$.
- Predicted $M3/E2$ ratio for $d\rightarrow d^*$: approximately $30\%$, rising to $80\%$ if the $d^*$ magnetic moment is $\mu_{d^*} = 7.6 \mu_N$ as anticipated by the Beijing group (Y.B. Dong, 2015).
- The Beijing group's calculation gives $Q_{d^*} = 0.025$ $e$ fm$^2$ (prolate shape) and $\Omega_{d^*} = -0.00567$ $e$ fm$^3$.

## Context
This work extends the pion-cloud model formalism previously applied to the $\Delta$ resonance by Buchmann (2001) to the newly discovered $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark, which was observed in proton-neutron scattering and pionic fusion reactions by Bashkanov (2013) and others. The calculations provide rough estimates to guide planned double-polarized photoproduction experiments at CLAS (JLab) using a tensor-polarized deuteron target, which aim to extract the $d^*$ magnetic moment and octupole deformation. The results also connect to neutron star physics, where the $d^*(2380)$ magnetic moment may contribute to magnetic field formation, and where the $d^*$ degree of freedom can simultaneously satisfy the mass-radius constraint from the LIGO merger event (Abbott, 2017) and the maximum observed neutron star mass of $\sim 2.17 M_\odot$.
