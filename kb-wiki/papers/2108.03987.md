# Learning Langevin dynamics with QCD phase transition

> In this proceeding, the deep Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are deployed to recognize the order of QCD phase transition and predict the dynamical parameters in Langevin processes. To overcome the intrinsic randomness existed in a stochastic process, we treat the final spectra as image-type inputs which preserve sufficient spatiotemporal correlations. As a practical example, we demonstrate this paradigm for the scalar condensation in QCD matter near the critical point, in which the order parameter of chiral phase transition can be characterized in a $1+1$-dimensional Langevin equation for $σ$ field. The well-trained CNNs accurately classify the first-order phase transition and crossover from $σ$ field configurations with fluctuations, in which the noise does not impair the performance of the recognition. In reconstructing the dynamics, we demonstrate it is robust to extract the damping coefficients $η$ from the intricate field configurations.

## Key claim

Deep convolutional neural networks can accurately classify first-order phase transitions versus crossovers from stochastic Langevin dynamics of the sigma field, and can predict the damping coefficient eta, even when the noise amplitude is large enough to make individual configurations visually dissimilar.

## Method

The authors simulate a 1+1-dimensional Langevin equation for the sigma field derived from a linear sigma model, with a Hubble-like cooling temperature profile and white noise obeying the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. They generate 10,000 event-by-event spatiotemporal field configurations per parameter set, using the final 40 time steps (4 fm duration) after the phase transition has occurred as 40x30 pixel image inputs. A deep CNN is trained to classify the phase order (first-order vs. crossover) and to predict the damping coefficient eta from these configurations.

## Key numbers

- Testing accuracy for phase-order classification reaches 99.9% for noise amplitudes B = 0.5 and 1.0.
- Damping coefficient eta is varied from 1.0 to 5.5 fm^{-1} across the evolution.
- For the crossover scenario, the coefficient of determination R^2 grows from 87% to 93% during training; for the first-order scenario, R^2 grows from 91% to 97%.
- The spatial grid size is dx = 0.2 fm over a range L = 6.0 fm; temporal step size is dt = 0.1 fm/c with total evolution duration up to 16 fm/c.
- Input configurations contain N = 40 x 30 = 1200 pixels per event.
- Training uses events with eta in (1.0-2.5) fm^{-1} and (4.6-5.5) fm^{-1}, with 1000 events per eta-bin.

## Context

This work extends the application of deep CNNs to phase transition recognition in stochastic, non-equilibrium systems, building on prior machine-learning approaches for heavy-ion collisions by Pang (2018) and Du (2020). It specifically addresses the challenge of identifying phase order from noisy Langevin dynamics, which is relevant to the QCD critical-point search discussed by Mukherjee (2015). The method enables future extraction of dynamical parameters from experimental data and could be applied to topological-dependent stochastic processes, such as detecting the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions as attempted by Zhao (2021).
