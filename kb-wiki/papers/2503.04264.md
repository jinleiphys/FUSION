# Towards modeling the short-range interactions of hidden/open charm pentaquark molecular states

> The hadronic $Σ_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ and $Σ_c^{(*)}{D}^{(*)}$ interactions are revisited, with a focus on their short-range parts, motivated by a tension between the interpretations of $P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)$, $P_{c\bar{c}}(4440)$, and $P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)$ in effective field theory (EFT) frameworks and the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model. While the three states can be interpreted as $Σ_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ molecular states within EFT frameworks, this is not feasible in the single-channel OBE model with consistent cutoff. In this work, the possibility to reconcile OBE model with EFTs by refitting the $ρ$-, $ω$- and $σ$-exchange interaction is explored and ruled out. It is pointed out that the problem in OBE arises from the strong short-range spin-dependent one-pion-exchange (OPE) interaction and the fixed signs of other short-range interactions in OBE model also prevent the cancellation. To address this issue, the short-range subtraction strategies within the OBE model are revisited. Two subtraction schemes are explored: removing the delta-function from all interactions and eliminating it only from the pseudoscalar-meson-exchange component. These schemes favor different spin assignments for $P_{c\bar{c}}(4440)$ and $P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)$. Though solving the problem, there is no clear dynamical picture to support the subtraction schemes. We propose a new quark-exchange mechanism motivated by the Pauli principle. Different from the two subtraction schemes in OBE, the quark-exchange mechanism offers an explanation grounded in microscopic dynamics. It is shown that the spin-dependent quark-exchange interaction cancels those from OPE. The differences in the predictions for the spin, isospin, and open-charm partner states of the experimental $P_{c\bar{c}}$ states offer a way to distinguish between the subtracted OBE model and the OBE model with quark-exchange contributions.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model cannot simultaneously reproduce the three observed \(P_{c\bar{c}}\) states (\(P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)\), \(P_{c\bar{c}}(4440)\), \(P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)\)) as \(\Sigma_c \bar{D}^{(*)}\) molecular states with a consistent cutoff, due to the strong short-range spin-dependent one-pion-exchange (OPE) interaction. The authors propose that a quark-exchange mechanism motivated by the Pauli principle can provide a microscopically grounded alternative to ad hoc subtraction schemes for the short-range interactions.

## Method
The authors fit the OBE model parameters (scaling factors \(R_\beta\), \(R_\lambda\), \(R_s\) for \(\rho\)-, \(\omega\)-, and \(\sigma\)-exchange coupling constants) to the experimental binding energies of the three \(P_{c\bar{c}}\) states, using S-wave interactions without coupled-channel effects and with a cutoff varied from 1.0 GeV to 1.4 GeV. They then explore two subtraction schemes (removing the delta-function from all interactions or only from the pseudoscalar-meson-exchange component) and propose a new quark-exchange mechanism where spin-dependent quark-exchange interactions cancel those from OPE.

## Key numbers
- The binding energies used as inputs are: \(\Delta E(P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)) \in \{-0.8, -7.8, -8.7\}\) MeV; \(\Delta E(P_{c\bar{c}}(4440)) \in \{-16.3, -20.3, -25.3\}\) MeV; \(\Delta E(P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)) \in \{-1.1, -5.1, -6.9\}\) MeV.
- The cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) is varied from 1.0 GeV to 1.4 GeV.
- The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 0.130\) GeV, and the \(\Sigma_c \Sigma_c \pi\) coupling constant \(g_1\) is taken as 0.71 from lattice QCD.
- The \(\rho\)-, \(\omega\)-, and \(\sigma\)-meson masses are 0.775 GeV, 0.783 GeV, and 0.600 GeV, respectively.

## Context
This work extends prior studies on the \(P_{c\bar{c}}\) pentaquark states as hadronic molecules, specifically addressing the tension between effective field theory (EFT) frameworks (e.g., Meng (2019), Wang (2019), Pavon Valderrama (2019)) and the OBE model (e.g., Chen (2019), He (2019)). The paper builds on the observation by Liu (2019) that removing short-range delta-function terms in OBE can reconcile the models, but criticizes this approach as lacking a clear dynamical picture. The proposed quark-exchange mechanism offers a new microscopic foundation, and the authors suggest that predictions for open-charm partner states can distinguish between the subtracted OBE model and the quark-exchange model.
