# Inclusive breakup reaction of a two-cluster projectile on a two-fragment target: A genuine four-body problem

> We develop a four-body model for the inclusive breakup of two-fragment halo projectiles colliding with two-fragment targets. In the case of a short lived projectiles, such as halo nuclei, on a deuteron target, the model allows the extraction of the neutron capture cross section of such projectiles. We supply examples.

# Inclusive breakup reaction of a two-cluster projectile on a two-fragment target: A genuine four-body problem

## Key claim

The authors develop a four-body model for inclusive nonelastic breakup (INEB) reactions where both the projectile and target are two-fragment systems, and claim that the inclusive proton spectrum can be decomposed into two distinct energy groups, one from target breakup and one from projectile breakup, allowing extraction of the neutron capture cross section of short-lived radioactive nuclei, but only under the simplifying approximation that the full four-body problem is treated as two separate three-body problems.

## Method

The authors extend the Inclusive Nonelastic Breakup (INEB) reaction theory, previously formulated for three-body (projectile + two-fragment target) and four-body (three-fragment projectile + target) cases, to the genuine four-body case of a two-fragment projectile on a two-fragment target. They treat the process as two independent three-body problems: breakup of the projectile without affecting the target, and breakup of the target without affecting the projectile. The inclusive proton spectrum is written as a sum of terms, each containing a density of states factor multiplied by a medium-modified total reaction cross section for the interacting fragment, with the source functions evaluated using distorted waves and optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Halo proton separation energy in ^8B: 0.137 MeV
- Halo neutron separation energy in ^11Be: 0.501 MeV
- Halo neutron separation energy in ^19C: 0.530 MeV
- Proton separation energy of the deuteron: 2.22 MeV
- Thermal neutron capture cross section of ^135Xe: 2.5 × 10^6 barns
- Lifetime of ^135Xe: 9.8 hours (also stated as 9.6 hours in text)

## Context

This work extends the INEB theory of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (1985) and Hussein and McVoy (1985), as well as the recent four-body treatment for three-fragment projectiles by Carlson, Frederico, and Hussein (2017), to the case where both projectile and target are two-fragment systems. The proposed method is intended to enable extraction of neutron capture cross sections for short-lived radioactive nuclei such as ^135Xe, which are not directly measurable, and could serve as a benchmark for testing the inclusive proton spectrum in reactions of the type d + ^135Xe → p + ^136Xe. The authors note that the formalism requires optical potentials for the participant fragments, which are in principle known from elastic scattering data.
