# Precise study of the Efimov three-particle spectrum and structure functions within variational approach

> A precise study within variational approach of the basic properties of the three-particle spectrum and structure functions with Gaussian potential near the critical coupling constant of interaction where the Efimov effect takes place is carried out. A method is developed to calculate highly excited states with very small binding energies, and numerical analysis is carried out for the ground and three excited energy states. For these states, one-particle density distributions, formfactors, pair correlation functions, and momentum distributions are calculated. It is found that the second excited state has already all the basic features of a level from the infinite Efimov series. An essential asymmetry is found in the position of energy levels with respect to the critical constant point. A halo-type structure is revealed in the one-particle density distributions, and formfactors are shown to have specific dips of finite depth, the number of dips being equal to the number of the state. The behaviour of pair correlation functions and momentum distributions is studied for three-particle states.

# Precise study of the Efimov three-particle spectrum and structure functions within variational approach

## Key claim
The second excited state already exhibits all basic features of a level from the infinite Efimov series, but the energy levels show an essential asymmetry with respect to the critical coupling constant point, the levels extend much farther to the right of the critical point than to the left, contrary to the symmetric picture sometimes assumed in asymptotic treatments.

## Method
The authors solved the three-particle Schrödinger equation with a Gaussian two-body potential using a variational Galerkin method in a Gaussian basis, with special optimization schemes to handle the vastly different energy scales of ground and highly excited states. To prepare initial configurations for the second and third excited states, they used evolution in the coupling constant at fixed zero energy and deformation of the Hamiltonian by changing particle masses, then restored the equal-mass condition stepwise. They calculated one-particle density distributions, formfactors, pair correlation functions, and momentum distributions for the ground state and three excited states.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of neighboring Efimov energy levels for equal masses: λ_E = exp(2π/s₀) ≈ 515.035, where s₀ = 1.0062378
- The critical two-particle coupling constant for the Gaussian potential: g_cr = 2.684005
- The ratio of radii of neighboring halo layers is about √λ_E ≈ 22.7
- The second excited level appears below the two-particle threshold at g ≥ g_cr^(2) = 16.3; the third at g ≥ g_cr^(3) = 30.8; the fourth at g ≥ g_cr^(4) = 32.55; the fifth at g ≥ g_cr^(5) = 50.89
- Angular coefficients for the n-th level near its appearance: k̃₂ ≈ -0.0045, k̃₃ ≈ -0.1, k̃₄ ≈ -0.01, k̃₅ ≈ -0.03
- The two-particle energy near the critical constant: E_(2) ≈ -0.107 (g - g_cr)²
- For the case of different masses (⁴⁰Ca core with two neutrons), λ_E ≈ 262; for a mesoatom system of deuterium and tritium with an electron, λ_E ≈ 1.26
- The momentum distribution density at small momenta grows by (λ_E)^(3/2) ≈ 1.17 × 10⁴ with each increment of the state number

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970, 1971, 1973), the appearance of an infinite series of three-body bound states near threshold when the two-body scattering length is large, by providing the first systematic variational calculation with a local Gaussian potential for excited states beyond the ground and first excited levels. Prior numerical work (e.g., using Faddeev equations with separable potentials) had not systematically treated local potentials for highly excited states. The results confirm the universal scaling properties predicted by asymptotic theory and enable detailed comparison with structure functions for possible experimental observation in three-nucleon systems, three-cluster nuclei, or mesoatomic systems. The asymmetry of levels relative to the critical point and the halo structure of density distributions provide new quantitative predictions for future experimental searches.
