# Searching for the Subatomic Swirls in the CuCu and CuAu Collisions

> Recently the STAR Collaboration discovered the "subatomic swirls", that is, the most vortical fluid flow structures in the quark-gluon plasma produced via the AuAu collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). Published in Nature and featured as a cover story, this discovery attracted significant interest and generated wide enthusiasm. For such an important finding, it is crucial to look for independent evidences of confirmation and to critically test the current interpretation of the global polarization measurement. We suggest that the CuCu and CuAu colliding systems at RHIC provide such opportunity. Interestingly, our calculations reveal that the fluid vorticity in the CuCu or CuAu collision is comparable to that in the AuAu Collision. Surprisingly, we find the computed $Λ$ hyperon polarization effect is stronger in the CuCu and CuAu systems than the AuAu system at the same collisional beam energy and centrality class, with an interesting hierarchy CuCu $>$ CuAu $>$ AuAu due to interplay between $Λ$ production timing and the time evolution of the vorticity. These predictions can be readily tested by experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the Λ hyperon global polarization signal in CuCu and CuAu collisions at RHIC will be stronger than in AuAu collisions at the same beam energy and centrality, forming a hierarchy CuCu > CuAu > AuAu, despite the fluid vorticity magnitude being comparable across all three systems. This counterintuitive result arises from the interplay between the earlier timing of Λ production in smaller systems and the rapid time decay of vorticity.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which includes initial particle production, an elastic parton cascade, quark coalescence for hadronization, and a hadronic cascade. They extracted the local fluid vorticity tensor from the four-velocity field of fluid cells averaged over thousands of events, then computed the Λ hyperon spin 4-vector from the local vorticity at each hyperon's formation location. The same model setup and parameters previously validated against AuAu data (Lin 2014) were applied to CuCu and CuAu collisions across a range of beam energies.

## Key numbers

- The average fluid vorticity ⟨ω_y⟩ for 20-50% centrality CuCu, CuAu, and AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 63 GeV is comparable in magnitude, with AuAu showing slightly larger values (Fig. 3).
- The predicted Λ hyperon polarization P_Λ for 20-50% centrality collisions shows the hierarchy CuCu > CuAu > AuAu across beam energies (Fig. 4).
- The ϕ meson polarization signal (1-3ρ_00) is predicted to be on the order of (2-5) × 10^{-3} for all three systems, also following the CuCu > CuAu > AuAu hierarchy.
- The initial angular momentum in a typical non-central heavy ion collision is about 10^{4-5} ħ.

## Context

This work extends the AMPT-based vorticity studies of Jiang (2016) and Li (2017) for AuAu collisions to CuCu and CuAu systems, providing independent tests of the STAR Collaboration's 2017 discovery of "subatomic swirls" in AuAu collisions at RHIC. The predictions can be directly tested against existing experimental data from RHIC for CuCu and CuAu collisions previously measured by PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR (Adare 2008, Alver 2010, Adare 2015, Adamczyk 2016). The results also enable future quantitative studies of chiral vortical effects in these systems, where the comparable vorticity but differing bulk backgrounds may help disentangle signal from contamination.
