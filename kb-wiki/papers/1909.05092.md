# Influence of isoscalar and isovector pairing on Gamow-Teller transitions for nuclei in the 2p1f shell: A schematic shell model study

> We perform a systematic study of Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions in the 2p1f shell, using the nuclear shell model with two schematic Hamiltonians. The use of the shell model provides flexibility to analyze the role of different proton-neutron pairing modes in the presence of nuclear deformation. The schematic Hamiltonians that are used contain a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction as well as isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) pairing interactions, but differ in the single particle energies. The objective of the work is to observe the behavior of GT transitions in different isoscalar and isovector pairing scenarios, together with the corresponding energy spectra and rotational properties of the parent and daughter nuclei (42Ca -> 42Sc, 44Ca -> 44Sc, 46Ti -> 46V, 48Ti -> 48V). We also treat the rotational properties of 44Ti and 48Cr. All results are compared with experimental data. The results obtained from our models depend on the different scenarios that arise, whether for N = Z or N neq Z nuclei. In the latter case, the presence of an attractive isoscalar pairing interaction imposes a 1+ ground state in odd-odd nuclei, contrary to observations for some of the nuclei considered, and it is necessary to suppress that pairing mode when considering such nuclei. The effect of varying the strength parameters for the two pairing modes is found to exhibit different but systematic effects on energy spectra and on GT transition properties.

## Key claim
The study demonstrates that the presence of an attractive isoscalar (T=0) pairing interaction imposes a 1+ ground state in odd-odd nuclei with N ≠ Z, contrary to observations for some nuclei considered, making it necessary to suppress that pairing mode when dealing with such nuclei; the effect of varying the strength parameters for the two pairing modes exhibits different but systematic effects on energy spectra and on Gamow-Teller transition properties.

## Method
The authors performed systematic shell-model calculations using two schematic Hamiltonians containing a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction plus isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) pairing interactions, differing only in their single-particle energies (Model 1 uses a spin-orbit term; Model 2 uses realistic single-particle energies from the kb3 interaction). Calculations were carried out with the ANTOINE shell-model program for the parent and daughter nuclei of the GT decays 42Ca → 42Sc, 44Ca → 44Sc, 46Ti → 46V, and 48Ti → 48V, plus the rotational properties of 44Ti and 48Cr, systematically varying the pairing strength parameters a (isoscalar) and b (isovector) while keeping the quadrupole strength fixed.

## Key numbers
- Optimal quadrupole strength: χ = -0.065 MeV for both models
- Optimal pairing strengths for N=Z nuclei: a = b = 6 (dimensionless)
- Optimal spin-orbit strength for Model 1: α = 20
- Model 2 single-particle energies: ε(7/2) = 0.0 MeV, ε(3/2) = 2.0 MeV, ε(1/2) = 4.0 MeV, ε(5/2) = 6.5 MeV
- B(GT) values multiplied by quenching factor (0.74)²
- For 42Ca → 42Sc with a=b=6: a single strong peak produced at almost the exact experimental energy
- For 44Ca → 44Sc with b=6 and a=0: Model 1 lowest peak is within a factor of roughly two of the strength of the next two peaks
- For 44Ca → 44Sc with b=6 and a=0: Model 2 lowest excitation is roughly five times more strongly populated than the next few states
- For 48Ti → 48V: Model 2 with optimal parameters obtains the lowest 1+ excitation near 2 MeV in excitation energy

## Context
This work extends earlier schematic shell-model studies by Stu (referenced as [Stu]) that did not consider GT transitions, using a slightly larger quadrupole strength (χ = -0.065 MeV versus the earlier value). The study systematically explores the role of isoscalar versus isovector proton-neutron pairing in the 2p1f shell for A=42-48 nuclei, building on prior work by Stu1, Stu5, and Stu6 that established the importance of pn pairing in N≈Z nuclei. The results enable future studies to incorporate the correct treatment of isoscalar pairing suppression for N≠Z nuclei when modeling GT transitions in this mass region, and highlight the limitations of schematic Hamiltonians in reproducing states such as the 7+ state in 42Sc and the 4+ ground state in 48V.
