# Power expansion for heavy quarkonium production at next-to-leading order in $\rm e^+e^-$ annihilation

> We study heavy quarkonium production associated with gluons in $\rm e^+e^-$ annihilation as an illustration of the perturbative QCD (pQCD) factorization approach, which incorporates the first nonleading power in the energy of the produced heavy quark pair. We show how the renormalization of the four-quark operators that define the heavy quark pair fragmentation functions using dimensional regularization induces "evanescent" operators that are absent in four dimensions. We derive closed forms for short-distance coefficients for quark pair production to next-to-leading order ($α_s^2$) in the relevant color singlet and octet channels. Using non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) to calculate the heavy quark pair fragmentation functions up to $v^4$ in the velocity expansion, we derive analytical results for the differential energy fraction distribution of the heavy quarkonium. Calculations for ${}^3S_1^{[1]}$ and ${}^1S_0^{[8]}$ channels agree with analogous NRQCD analytical results available in the literature, while several color-octet calculations of energy fraction distributions are new. We show that the remaining corrections due to the heavy quark mass fall off rapidly in the energy of the produced state. To explore the importance of evolution at energies much larger than the mass of the heavy quark, we solve the renormalization group equation perturbatively to two-loop order for the ${}^3S_1^{[1]}$ case.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that perturbative QCD (pQCD) factorization at next-to-leading power (NLP) can reproduce the high-energy limit of NRQCD calculations for heavy quarkonium production in e+e- annihilation, but only after including "evanescent" fragmentation functions that vanish in four dimensions and arise from dimensional regularization of four-quark operators.

## Method

The authors calculate short-distance coefficients for heavy quark pair production in e+e- annihilation at next-to-leading order (α_s^2) using the pQCD factorization formalism of Kang et al. (2014), which separates production into leading-power single-parton fragmentation and next-to-leading-power heavy quark pair fragmentation. They derive closed forms for color singlet and octet channels, then combine these with NRQCD fragmentation functions computed up to v^4 in the velocity expansion to obtain analytical differential energy fraction distributions for heavy quarkonium. The renormalization group equation is solved perturbatively to two-loop order for the ^3S_1^[1] channel to study evolution effects at energies much larger than the heavy quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD factorization formalism developed by Kang et al. (2014, 2014) for heavy quarkonium production, which provides a unified framework for leading-power and next-to-leading-power behavior. The analytical results complement existing NRQCD calculations by Ma et al. (2013, 2014) and numerical NLO calculations for both color-singlet and color-octet channels by Ma et al. (2008), Gong et al. (2009), and Zhang et al. (2009). The formalism enables resummation of large logarithms ln(E_H^2/(2m_Q)^2) through evolution equations, going beyond fixed-order NRQCD calculations, and can be applied to Belle experiment data for J/ψ production in e+e- annihilation.
