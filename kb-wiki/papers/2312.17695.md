# Enhancement of deuteron production in jets

> A strong enhancement of deuteron production in jets has been recently observed in proton-proton collisions at LHC. We show that the effect is due to two independent factors: a collimation of jet nucleons and a smallness of nucleon source which is significantly smaller than a deuteron.

# Enhancement of deuteron production in jets

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of deuteron production in jets is caused by two independent factors: the collimation of jet nucleons and the smallness of the nucleon source relative to the deuteron size. The quantitative estimate of the source size depends on whether one uses the Hulthén wave function or a modern nucleon-nucleon potential with hard-core repulsion, which significantly reduces the formation rate and makes further quantification uncertain.

## Method

The author uses the coalescence model, where deuteron yield factorizes into the probability to produce a neutron-proton pair and the probability to form a deuteron via final-state interaction. A simplified analytical momentum distribution of jet nucleons is assumed, with collimation described by a critical zenithal angle theta_c, and the deuteron formation rate is computed from the overlap of a Gaussian source function with the Hulthén deuteron wave function. The coalescence parameter B, defined via Lorentz-invariant yields, is related to the formation rate A as B = 2A/m.

## Key numbers

- For a typical jet radius R = 0.2–0.5, the critical zenithal angle theta_c = 11°–29°.
- Reducing theta_c from 180° (isotropic) to 20° increases the deuteron yield by a factor of 33.
- The maximal formation rate A_max = 20.2 fm^{-3}, corresponding to a source much smaller than the deuteron.
- The maximal coalescence parameter B_max = 0.33 GeV^2.
- The experimentally measured coalescence parameter for jet deuterons is B = 0.13 ± 0.07 GeV^2 (ALICE 2022).
- From this B value, the source size of jet deuterons is r_0 = 0.3 ± 0.2 fm.
- The coalescence parameter for deuterons in underlying events is about ten times smaller, giving r_0 = 1.3 ± 0.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model analysis of deuteron production in jets observed by ALICE (2020, 2022) in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV. The earlier data had been compared with two variants of the coalescence model using PYTHIA 8 (Skands 2014) for nucleon momentum distributions, one with a sharp coalescence momentum cutoff and one with energy-dependent cross sections (Dal 2015). The present note provides a simple analytical demonstration that both collimation and source size contribute independently to the enhancement. The author notes that using a modern nucleon-nucleon potential such as Argonne v18 (Wiringa 1994) significantly reduces the formation rate (Mahlein 2023), making it too small to describe jet deuteron production, which points to the need for a relativistic treatment of the deuteron structure at small distances.
