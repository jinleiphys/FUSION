# Inclusive electron scattering off $^{12}$C, ${}^{40}$Ca, and ${}^{40}$Ar: effects of the meson exchange currents

> The scattering of electrons on carbon, calcium, and argon targets are analyzed using an approach that incorporates the contributions to the electromagnetic response functions from the quasielastic (QE), inelastic processes, and two-particle and two-hole meson exchange current ($2p-2h$ MEC). This approach describes well the whole energy spectrum of data at very different kinematics. It is shown that the accuracy of the $(e,e')$ cross section calculations in the region between the QE and delta-resonance peaks, where the $2p-2h$ MEC contribution reaches its maximum value, depends on the momentum transfer $|\q|$ and at $|\q|>500$ MeV the calculated and measured cross sections are in agreement within the experimental uncertainties.

## Key claim

The RDWIA+MEC+RES approach, which combines quasielastic, two-particle-two-hole meson exchange current (2p-2h MEC), and inelastic contributions, describes the inclusive electron scattering cross sections on carbon, calcium, and argon across the full energy spectrum, but the accuracy in the dip region between the QE and delta-resonance peaks depends on momentum transfer, with agreement within experimental uncertainties only for momentum transfers above 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated inclusive (e,e') double-differential cross sections for 12C, 40Ca, and 40Ar using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) for quasielastic scattering, a parameterization of exact relativistic Fermi gas calculations for 2p-2h meson exchange current contributions, and empirical fits to proton and neutron inelastic structure functions for the resonance and inelastic region. The RDWIA calculations included final state interactions via a relativistic optical potential and short-range NN-correlations from high-momentum nucleon distributions. The MEC response for calcium and argon was obtained by rescaling the carbon parameterization according to the method of Megias (2014).

## Key numbers

- For carbon at momentum transfer below 340 MeV, theoretical predictions overestimate data by 30-50% at the QE peak
- The ratio R^C_dip (calculated/measured cross section at the dip minimum) increases from 0.7 at |q|_dip ≈ 250 MeV to approximately 1 at |q|_dip ≈ 500 MeV
- The 2p-2h MEC contribution δ_MEC for carbon decreases from 65% at |q|_dip ≈ 250 MeV to 42% at |q|_dip ≈ 500 MeV, and to 20% at |q|_dip ≈ 1000 MeV
- For calcium, R^Ca_dip increases from 0.7 at |q|_dip = 350 MeV to approximately 1 at |q|_dip > 500 MeV
- The 2p-2h MEC contribution for calcium decreases from 68% at |q|_dip = 300 MeV to 38% at |q|_dip = 600 MeV
- The observed effect in per-nucleon cross section ratios (Ca/C and Ar/C) in the QE peak region is approximately 15%, higher than experimental errors
- The accuracy of the inclusive cross section calculation in the dip region improves from 35% at |q|_dip ≈ 250 MeV to 10% at |q|_dip ≥ 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA+MEC+RES approach previously applied to carbon by Butkevich and collaborators to calcium and argon targets, using recent JLab data from Dai (2019) for validation. The study is motivated by the need to improve nuclear models for neutrino oscillation experiments such as T2K, NOvA, and DUNE, where 2p-2h MEC contributions are a significant source of systematic uncertainty in neutrino energy reconstruction. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutrino-nucleus scattering in the vector sector of the electroweak interaction, particularly for the DUNE experiment which will use both liquid argon and scintillator detectors.
