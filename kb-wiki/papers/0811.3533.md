# Meson supercurrents and the Meissner effect in the Sakai-Sugimoto model

> The Sakai-Sugimoto model provides a holographic description for chiral symmetry breaking. We use this model to investigate chirally broken phases in an external magnetic field at finite isospin and baryon chemical potentials. The equations of motion for the bulk gauge fields are solved analytically and the free energy is computed from the Yang-Mills and Chern-Simons contributions to the D8 brane action. In the case of a neutral pion condensate, a magnetic field is found to induce nonzero gradients of the Goldstone boson fields corresponding to meson supercurrents. A charged pion condensate, on the other hand, expels the magnetic field due to the Meissner effect. Upon comparing the Gibbs free energies of these two phases we find that the rotation of the chiral condensate into a charged pion condensate for finite isospin chemical potentials is partially undone by switching on a magnetic field, and we determine the critical magnetic field which removes the charged pion condensate in a first-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The Sakai-Sugimoto model predicts that a magnetic field induces meson supercurrents (nonzero gradients of Goldstone boson fields) in the neutral pion condensate phase, while the charged pion condensate expels the magnetic field via the Meissner effect; the rotation of the chiral condensate into a charged pion condensate at finite isospin chemical potential is partially undone by switching on a magnetic field, with a first-order phase transition at a critical field strength.

## Method

The authors solved the equations of motion for the bulk U(2) gauge fields analytically in the confined (chirally broken) phase of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, using the Yang-Mills and Chern-Simons actions on D8 branes with a background magnetic field and finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials. They computed the Gibbs free energy for both neutral and charged pion condensate phases, comparing them to determine the energetically preferred phase as a function of magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the deconfinement phase transition is \( T_c = M_{\rm KK}/(2\pi) \), independent of chemical potential.
- The parameter \(\alpha\) governing the Chern-Simons coupling is \(\alpha = 27\pi/(2\lambda)\), where \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling.
- The Kaluza-Klein mass \(M_{\rm KK}\) sets the energy scale, with the compactified dimension radius given by \(2\pi/M_{\rm KK}\).
- The magnetic field components are related to the electromagnetic field by \(\hat{\cal B} = e{\cal B}_{\rm em}/6\) and \({\cal B} = e{\cal B}_{\rm em}/2\) for up and down quarks with charges \(2/3\,e\) and \(-1/3\,e\).

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of chiral symmetry breaking in the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2004, 2005) to include external magnetic fields, building on earlier investigations of magnetic field effects in holographic models by Filev (2007), Albash (2007), and Erdmenger (2007). The results enable future studies of the phase diagram of dense quark matter in strong magnetic fields, relevant for neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collisions, and complement previous work using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Gusynin, 1994, 1995) and chiral perturbation theory (Agasian, 2001; Cohen, 2007) that were restricted to vanishing chemical potentials.
