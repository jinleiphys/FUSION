# Unexpected transparency in the scattering of fragile $^{6}$Li and $^{6}$He Nuclei

> It is found that the scattering of the fragile nucleus $^6$Li from $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O is unexpectedly transparent. It is shown that the internal-wave contribution is significantly large in the scattering, which suggests that some transparency could persist in the scattering involving the fragile nucleus $^6$He.

## Key claim
The scattering of the fragile nucleus \(^{6}\)Li from \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O is unexpectedly transparent, with a significantly large internal-wave contribution; this suggests that some transparency could persist in the scattering involving the fragile nucleus \(^{6}\)He, although the claim for \(^{6}\)He is a suggestion based on similarity rather than a direct observation.

## Method
The authors analyzed elastic scattering angular distributions for \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{16}\)O and \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{12}\)C using optical model potentials with a squared Woods-Saxon form factor for both real and imaginary parts. They decomposed the calculated scattering amplitudes into internal-wave and barrier-wave components, and into farside and nearside components, to identify Airy minima and quantify the internal-wave contribution. For \(^{6}\)He+\(^{12}\)C at 18 MeV, they applied the same potential obtained from the \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{12}\)C analysis and performed the same decomposition.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{16}\)O at \(E_L\) = 29.8 MeV, a broad dip at \(\theta_{c.m.} \simeq 100^\circ\) is identified as an Airy minimum of second order (A2).
- For \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{16}\)O, the optical potential parameters used are \(V_0 = 316.2\) MeV, \(R_R = 3.319\) fm, \(a_R = 0.7\) fm, \(R_I = 5.822\) fm, \(a_I = 0.613\) fm; \(W_0\) is adjusted to values between 7 and 16 MeV for incident energies \(E_L\) = 13, 25.7, 29.8, 36, and 48 MeV.
- For \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{12}\)C, systematic angular distributions were analyzed for incident energies ranging from a few MeV to 318 MeV.
- For \(^{6}\)He+\(^{12}\)C, elastic scattering data at \(E_L = 18\) MeV extend up to \(\theta_{c.m.} \simeq 85^\circ\).
- In the coupled-channel calculation of \(^{12}\)C(\(^{6}\)Li,\(^{6}\)Li')\(^{12}\)C\(^*\) (\(J^\pi = 2^+\), \(E_x = 4.44\) MeV) inelastic scattering at \(E_L = 24\) and 30 MeV, the rise of cross sections beyond \(\theta_{c.m.} = 80^\circ\) comes entirely from internal-wave contributions.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear rainbow scattering, previously observed in systems involving magic nuclei such as \(\alpha\)+\(^{16}\)O, \(\alpha\)+\(^{40}\)Ca (Michel 1998), and \(^{16}\)O+\(^{16}\)O (Khoa 2000), to fragile projectiles like \(^{6}\)Li and \(^{6}\)He. The finding that a weakly bound projectile can still exhibit transparency and Airy structure contradicts the expectation that strong absorption from breakup effects would suppress such features. The results enable future measurements of \(^{6}\)He+\(^{16}\)O and \(^{6}\)He+\(^{12}\)C scattering at higher energies and larger angles to determine whether transparency persists, and could allow determination of the \(^{6}\)He-nucleus potential for systems like \(^{6}\)He+\(^{16}\)O, \(^{6}\)He+\(^{15}\)N, and \(^{6}\)He+\(^{14}\)C.
