# Pseudoscalar glueball mass: a window on three-gluon interactions

> In pure-glue QCD, gluon-gluon scattering in the $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$ channel is described by a very simple equation, especially if one considers just the leading contribution to the scattering kernel. Of all components in this kernel, only the three-gluon vertex, $V_{μνρ}$, is poorly constrained by contemporary analyses; hence, calculations of $0^{-+}$ glueball properties serve as a clear window onto the character and form of $V_{μνρ}$. This is important given that many modern calculations of $V_{μνρ}$ predict the appearance of an infrared suppression in the scalar function which comes to modulate the bare vertex after the nonperturbative resummation of interactions. Such behaviour is a peculiar prediction; but we find that such suppression is essential if one is to achieve agreement with lattice-QCD predictions for the $0^{-+}$ glueball mass. It is likely, therefore, that this novel feature of $V_{μνρ}$ is real and has observable implications for the spectrum, decays and interactions of all QCD bound-states.

## Key claim

The mass of the $0^{-+}$ glueball, computed from the leading contribution to the gluon-gluon scattering kernel, is highly sensitive to the infrared behavior of the three-gluon vertex; agreement with lattice-QCD predictions requires a marked infrared suppression (zero-crossing) in the scalar function modulating the bare vertex, a feature that is essential for obtaining a realistic mass and is likely a real physical effect.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a $0^{-+}$ glueball in pure-glue QCD, using a ladder-like kernel built from a process-independent effective charge, an algebraic Ansatz for the gluon propagator (featuring a dynamical mass $m_g$), and an algebraic Ansatz for the three-gluon vertex that multiplies the bare vertex by a factor $R^\pm = {\mathpzc f}(k_\pm^2){\mathpzc f}(q_\pm^2){\mathpzc f}(Q^2)$, where ${\mathpzc f}(k^2) = 1 - \exp(-k^2/\omega_{3g}^2)$ controls the infrared suppression. The single free parameter $\omega_{3g}$ was varied to map the glueball mass as a function of the suppression scale, and the results were compared to lattice-QCD benchmarks.

## Key numbers

- The lattice-QCD prediction for the $0^{-+}$ glueball mass is $M_{0^{-+}} = 2.56(13)\,$GeV (Chen, 2005).
- The computed glueball mass matches the lattice prediction at $\omega_{3g} \approx 1.9\,$GeV when using the gluon propagator of Eq. (GluonProp) with $m_g = 0.6\,$GeV.
- A critical value $\omega_{3g}^c = 0.87\,$GeV exists: for $\omega_{3g} < \omega_{3g}^c$ the glueball becomes tachyonic, and at $\omega_{3g} = \omega_{3g}^c$ it appears as a composite zero mode.
- Using an alternative gluon propagator (Stingl, 1985), the lattice mass is recovered at $\omega_{3g} \approx 1.7\,$GeV.
- The effective charge parameters include $D\omega = (0.96\,{\rm GeV})^3$, $\omega = 0.5\,$GeV, $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}=0.234\,$GeV.

## Context

This work extends continuum bound-state studies of glueballs (Dudal, 2010; Meyers, 2012; Sanchis-Alepuz, 2015; Meyer, 2015) by focusing on the $0^{-+}$ channel, where the Bethe-Salpeter equation is simplest and the three-gluon vertex is the only poorly constrained element. It directly tests predictions from modern three-gluon vertex analyses (Aguilar, 2013; Blum, 2014; Eichmann, 2014; Athenodorou, 2016; Duarte, 2016; Boucaud, 2017; Corell, 2018) that show an infrared suppression, and it explains why earlier work (Meyers, 2012) required an unrealistically small coupling to obtain a realistic mass. The results enable future studies to use the $0^{-+}$ glueball as a test-bed for vertex Ansatze and to extend the analysis to other glueball channels, potentially leading to a unified understanding of glueball structure and the existence problem for quantum Yang-Mills theories.
