# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung

> The splitting processes of bremsstrahlung and pair production in a medium are coherent over large distances in the very high energy limit, which leads to a suppression known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. We analyze the case when the coherence lengths of two consecutive splitting processes overlap, which is important for understanding corrections to standard treatments of the LPM effect in QCD. Previous authors have analyzed this problem in the case of overlapping double bremsstrahlung where at least one of the bremsstrahlung gluons is soft. Here we show how to generalize to include the case where both splittings are hard. A number of techniques must be developed, and so in this paper we simplify by (i) restricting attention to a subset of the interference effects, which we call the "crossed" diagrams, and (ii) working in the large-$N_c$ limit. We first develop some general formulas that could in principle be implemented numerically (with substantial difficulty). To make more analytic progress, we then focus on the case of a thick, homogeneous medium and make the multiple scattering approximation (also known as the $\hat q$ or harmonic approximation) appropriate at high energy. We show that the differential rate $dΓ/dx\,dy$ for overlapping double bremsstrahlung of gluons with momentum fractions $x$ and $y$ can then be reduced to the calculation of a 1-dimensional integral, which we perform numerically. [Though this paper is unfortunately long, our introduction is enough for getting the gist of the method.]

## Key claim

The paper develops formalism for computing the LPM effect in QCD when the coherence lengths of two consecutive bremsstrahlung splittings overlap, generalizing previous work that only treated the case where at least one radiated gluon is soft; however, the authors restrict to a subset of interference diagrams (the "crossed" diagrams) and work in the large-\(N_c\) limit, so the results are not yet a complete calculation of the double-splitting rate.

## Method

The authors reduce the problem of overlapping double bremsstrahlung in a medium to effective two-dimensional non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, where the number of "particles" in the quantum mechanics problem equals the number of high-energy partons present in each time interval between splittings. Using the property that the sum of effective masses vanishes (\(\sum m_i = 0\)), they reduce the 4-particle quantum mechanics problem (for the interval between the two splittings) to a 2-particle problem. They then specialize to a thick, homogeneous medium and apply the multiple scattering (harmonic oscillator or \(\hat q\)) approximation, which allows them to reduce the differential rate \(d\Gamma/dx\,dy\) for overlapping double bremsstrahlung of gluons with momentum fractions \(x\) and \(y\) to a single one-dimensional numerical integral over the duration \(\Delta t\) of the 4-particle evolution.

## Key numbers

* The differential rate for single splitting in a thick, homogeneous medium is given by \(d\Gamma/dx = (\alpha_s/\pi\sqrt{2})\, P(x)\, |\Omega_0|\), where \(\Omega_0 = \sqrt{ -i\hat q_A/(2E) \left( -1 + 1/(1-x) + 1/x \right) }\) for \(g \to gg\) splitting.
* For \(y \ll x \ll 1\), the crossed contribution to the double-splitting rate scales as \([d\Gamma/dx\,dy]_{\text{crossed}} \propto \alpha_s^2 \ln(y/x) / (x^{3/2} y)\), which when integrated over \(y\) gives a double-log correction \(\propto \alpha_s^2 \log^2\).
* In the limit of small \(y\) and fixed \(x\), the crossed diagrams give a more divergent contribution scaling as \([d\Gamma/dx\,dy]_{\text{crossed}} \propto \alpha_s^2 \ln(y/x) / (x y^{3/2})\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of overlapping formation times in QCD bremsstrahlung beyond the soft-gluon limit treated by Blaizot and Mehtar-Tani (2013), Iancu (2014), and Wu (2014), who performed explicit calculations for \(y \ll x \ll 1\) to leading-log order. The paper develops methods for general \(x\) and \(y\) without requiring either to be small, and goes beyond leading logarithms within the multiple-scattering approximation. The results enable future computation of the full double-splitting rate (including the remaining "uncrossed" diagrams and virtual corrections) and may inform the treatment of overlapping formation times in Monte Carlo generators such as JEWEL.
