# Hadronic structure on the light-front VII. Pions and kaons and their partonic distributions

> This work is a continuation in our series of papers, that addresses quark models of hadronic structure on the light front, motivated by the QCD vacuum structure and lattice results. The spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry on the light front, is shown to parallel that in the rest frame, where the non-local instanton induced $^\prime$t Hooft interaction plays a central role. By rewriting this interaction solely in terms of the good component of the fermionic field, a scalar chiral condensate emerges in the mean-field approximation, which is identical to the one obtained in the rest frame. The pions and kaons emerge as deeply bound Goldstone modes in the chiral limit, with the scalar-isoscalar sigma meson mode as a threshold state with zero binding. We explicitly derive the light front distribution amplitudes (DAs) and partonic functions (PDFs) for these mesons. The DAs and PDFs are in good agreement with those extracted from the QCD instanton vacuum in the rest frame, using the large momentum effective theory (LaMET). The QCD evolved DAs and PDFs compare well with available measurements, as well as recent lattice results.

## Key claim

The spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry on the light front is shown to parallel that in the rest frame, with the non-local instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction producing a scalar chiral condensate in the mean-field approximation that is identical to the one obtained in the rest frame, provided careful treatment of the quark zero-mode form factors is included.

## Method

The authors decompose the fermionic field into good and bad components along the light cone directions, eliminate the non-propagating bad component as a constraint, and rewrite the 't Hooft multi-flavor interactions from the QCD instanton liquid model (ILM) solely in terms of the good component. In the mean-field approximation using 1/Nc counting, they derive the light front Hamiltonian, solve the gap equation for the constituent quark mass, and diagonalize the Hamiltonian in the two-body sector to obtain light front wavefunctions, distribution amplitudes (DAs), and parton distribution functions (PDFs) for pions and kaons.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass M is obtained from the gap equation with critical coupling g_S,RIV^cr = 2.981 π² ρ², where ρ is the instanton size of about 1/3 fm.
* The packing fraction of the instanton-antiinstanton ensemble is κ_{I+Ī} = 2π²ρ⁴/R⁴ ≈ 0.1, with mean tunneling density 1/R⁴ ≈ 1 fm⁻⁴.
* The zero mode zone bandwidth is |Δλ| ∼ ρ²/R³ ∼ 20 MeV.
* The 't Hooft coupling strength for Nf=3 is G_Hooft = (n_{I+Ī}/2) (4π²ρ³)³ (1/m*_u ρ)(1/m*_d ρ)(1/m*_s ρ), with effective quark masses m*_q.

## Context

This work continues a series by Shuryak and Zahed (2021-2022) that exports the successes of the QCD instanton liquid model from space-like to light-front formulations, extending earlier analyses of chiral symmetry breaking on the light front using NJL-type models by Bentz (1999), Itakura (2000), and Naito (2004). The results enable direct computation of partonic distributions for pseudoscalar mesons from a semi-classical QCD vacuum model, with DAs and PDFs that can be QCD evolved and compared to experimental measurements and lattice results from LaMET approaches.
