# Final state effects on charge asymmetry of pion elliptic flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Within a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with the string melting mechanism and imported initial electric quadrupole moment, the difference between the elliptic flow of positive and negative pions is calculated. The slope parameter $r$ of the linear dependence of $Δv_{2}=v_{2}(π^{-})-v_{2}(π^{+})$ on $A_{ch}=(N^{+}-N^{-})/(N^{+}+N^{-})$ is yielded owing to the parton cascade, which converts the initial electric quadrupole distribution into the final charge-dependent elliptic flow. The slope parameter $r$ is found to be increased by the hadronization given by the coalescence, and decreased by the resonance decays. The slope parameter $r$ is very sensitive to both the initial electric quadrupole percentage and centrality bin and consequently a helpful constraint on the quadrupole moment of the chiral magnetic wave is obtained for Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The slope parameter \(r\) of the linear dependence of \(\Delta v_{2}=v_{2}(\pi^{-})-v_{2}(\pi^{+})\) on charge asymmetry \(A_{ch}\) is sensitive to both the initial electric quadrupole percentage and centrality bin, but the measured value is an end product of the full dynamical evolution where parton cascade, coalescence hadronization, and resonance decays each modify \(r\) in opposite directions.

## Method

The AMPT model with string melting was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200\) GeV. An initial electric quadrupole moment was artificially introduced by switching the positions of a fraction of \(u\) (or \(\bar{d}\)) quarks with \(\bar{u}\) (or \(d\)) quarks in the initial state. The evolution proceeded through parton cascade (elastic cross section 10 mb), coalescence hadronization, and hadronic resonance decays (with hadronic scatterings turned off to conserve charge), and the slope parameter \(r\) was extracted from linear fits of \(\Delta v_{2}\) versus \(A_{ch}\) for pions with \(0.15 < p_T < 0.5\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta| < 1\).

## Key numbers

* The slope parameter \(r\) for pions after the full evolution with a 2% initial quadrupole percentage in the 30-40% centrality bin is \(3.34 \pm 0.56\), which describes the experimental preliminary trend of \(r \sim 3\).
* After parton cascade alone (quark level), the slope parameter is \(r = 5.90 \pm 0.58\); after coalescence hadronization it increases to \(r = 9.95 \pm 1.13\); after resonance decays it decreases to \(r = 3.34 \pm 0.56\).
* For the 30-40% centrality bin, the initial quadrupole percentage is estimated to be 1-2%; for the 0-10% most central bin it is 0%; for the 50-60% bin it is 2-3%; for the 70-80% bin the percentage is uncertain because the partonic evolution is too short or weak.

## Context

This work extends the study of the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) signal by incorporating final state interactions in a dynamical transport model, whereas previous theoretical studies used anomalous hydrodynamics (Hongo 2013) or analytic arguments (Yee and Yin 2013; Taghavi and Wiedemann 2013). The results show that the slope parameter \(r\) is not a direct measure of the anomalous transport alone but is significantly modified by hadronization and resonance decays, providing a constraint on the initial electric quadrupole moment of the CMW for Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy.
