# Comprehending Particle Production in Proton+Proton and Heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Particle production mechanisms in proton+proton and heavy-ion collisions at the LHC energies are discussed. An attempt is made to connect p+p to heavy-ion collisions in understanding the final state observables. New emerging phenomena like Multipartonic Interactions (MPI) and Color Reconnection (CR) mechanism are important in understanding heavy-ion like behaviors in high-multiplicity p+p events. Particle spectra, Multipartonic Interactions, Strangeness enhancement, Nuclear modification factor etc. are few of the aspects, which are discussed in this presentation.

# Comprehending Particle Production in Proton+Proton and Heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim
High-multiplicity proton+proton collisions at LHC energies may exhibit signatures of deconfinement, such as strangeness enhancement and collectivity, comparable to those seen in heavy-ion collisions, but the paper reviews evidence rather than establishing a definitive causal link, and the question of whether a deconfined phase is actually formed in p+p systems remains open.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental data from the ALICE experiment at the LHC on transverse momentum spectra, strangeness enhancement, and nuclear modification factors in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions. It compares the applicability of Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (for A+A) versus non-extensive Tsallis distributions (for p+p) to describe particle spectra, and discusses the roles of Multipartonic Interactions (MPI) and Color Reconnection (CR) in PYTHIA8 simulations. The author also presents a formalism using the Boltzmann Transport Equation in Relaxation Time Approximation with a non-extensive initial p+p distribution as input to explain Pb+Pb observables.

## Key numbers
- LHC collision energies reach energy densities higher than the lattice QCD prediction for deconfinement transition of 1 GeV/fm³, both in peripheral heavy-ion and p+p collisions.
- The nuclear modification factor (R_AA) and elliptic flow (v₂) of φ-mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV are explained by the BTE-RTA formalism with non-extensive initial conditions.
- Strangeness enhancement is quantified as yield ratios of strange and multi-strange particles to (π⁺+π⁻) as a function of charged particle multiplicity density (dN_ch/dη) at mid-rapidity |y| < 0.5, as measured by ALICE.

## Context
This review extends the discussion of QGP-like signatures, previously established in central nucleus-nucleus collisions (Heiselberg, 2000), into the domain of high-multiplicity p+p collisions at the LHC. It connects to work by Tripathy (2017) on R_AA and elliptic flow in Pb+Pb collisions, and to Thakur (2017) on charmonia production in p+p collisions. The paper enables future investigation of whether deconfinement can be observed in small systems, and whether a unified description of particle production from p+p to A+A collisions is achievable via non-extensive statistics and transport approaches.
