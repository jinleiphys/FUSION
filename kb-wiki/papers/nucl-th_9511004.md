# Coulomb potential from a particle in uniform ultrarelativistic motion

> The Coulomb potential produced by an ultrarelativistic particle (such as a heavy ion) in uniform motion is shown in the appropriate gauge to factorize into a longitudinal Dirac delta function of (z - t) times the simple two dimensional potential solution in the transverse direction. This form makes manifest the source of the energy independence of the interaction.

# Coulomb potential from a particle in uniform ultrarelativistic motion

## Key claim

The Coulomb potential produced by an ultrarelativistic particle in uniform motion can be gauge-transformed into a form that factorizes into a longitudinal Dirac delta function of (z - t) times a simple two-dimensional potential in the transverse direction, but this factorization is valid only when ignoring terms of order ln(gamma)/gamma^2 and for impact parameters that are not too large.

## Method

Starting from the Lienard-Wiechert potential for a moving charged particle, the author applies a previously known gauge transformation (Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser, 1991) that removes the large positive and negative time contributions as well as the gamma dependence. In the limit of large gamma with v=1, the author integrates the gauge-transformed potential over the longitudinal coordinate to obtain a closed-form expression for the transverse potential, and verifies that this result matches the multipole decomposition previously derived.

## Key numbers

- The gauge transformation removes contributions of order ln(gamma)/gamma^2, establishing the accuracy of the delta-function approximation to that order
- The resulting transverse potential takes the form V(rho) = -alpha Z (1 - alpha_z) ln[(b - rho)^2 / b^2], where b is the impact parameter, Z is the charge of the moving ion, and alpha is the fine structure constant
- The expression is valid from the smallest relevant impact parameters just outside nuclear touching throughout the region where b is not too large and the interaction is strong enough to be non-perturbative

## Context

This work extends the multipole decomposition approach of Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser (1991) by showing that their formula implicitly contains a much simpler cylindrically symmetric expression. The result builds on the gauge transformation technique previously used by Toshima and Eichler (1990) to address difficulties with interactions that decay slowly as 1/t. The author suggests that this reduction from three dimensions to two dimensions might make direct solution of the time-dependent Dirac equation a viable alternative to coupled channels calculations for electron-positron pair production induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ions, though the result has no obvious application to conventional angular momentum algebra methods used in coupled channels calculations (Rumrich et al., 1991; Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser, 1993, 1994).
