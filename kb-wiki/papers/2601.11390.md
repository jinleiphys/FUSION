# The unfinished picture of low-energy antineutron interactions: open issues and hints for future research possibilities

> This report examines the open questions that remain unsolved following the measurements with antineutrons ($\bar n$) as probes conducted up to the 1990s at the LEAR facility at CERN. It also presents suggestions for possible new experiments at a future, upgraded AD complex, which can potentially provide access to new areas of physics.

# The unfinished picture of low-energy antineutron interactions: open issues and hints for future research possibilities

## Key claim

While antineutron beams offer cleaner strong-force measurements than antiproton beams due to the absence of Coulomb interactions, several unresolved puzzles from LEAR-era data (1980s–1990s), including a dip-bump structure in the total cross section at 65–80 MeV/c and systematic discrepancies between measured antineutron-nucleus annihilation cross sections and optical-potential model predictions, remain unexplained and demand new experiments at a future upgraded AD complex at CERN.

## Method

The paper reviews historical antineutron production techniques, focusing on the charge-exchange (CEX) reaction $\bar{p}p \rightarrow \bar{n}n$ used at LEAR's OBELIX experiment, where antineutron momentum was reconstructed iteratively from time-of-flight measurements accounting for antiproton energy loss in the production target. The author then compares OBELIX total, annihilation, and elastic cross section data with earlier measurements from Armstrong et al. (1987) and Banerjee et al. (1985), and evaluates modern optical-potential models by Friedman et al. (2014) against the existing antineutron-nucleus annihilation data on various nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- Antineutron total cross section data from OBELIX cover the 50–400 MeV/c range with total error ~10%
- Armstrong et al. measured $\beta\sigma_{ann} = (40 \pm 3)$ mb at 22 MeV/c and $(32 \pm 5)$ mb at 43 MeV/c
- Banerjee et al. reported $\sigma_{ann}(\bar{n}p) = (55.4 \pm 2.2)$ mb in the 500–800 MeV/c range
- OBELIX annihilation cross section data carry a normalization error as large as 7%
- D-wave contributes $(4.7 \pm 0.6)\%$ to $\bar{n}p$ annihilation up to 200 MeV/c
- The isospin ratio $r = \sigma_T(I=0)/\sigma_T(I=1) = 2.5 \pm 0.4$ at 70 MeV/c, compared to $1.1 \pm 0.1$ at 300 MeV/c
- Banerjee et al. reported $\sigma_{ann}(\bar{p}p) = 77.9 \pm 0.6$ mb (500–800 MeV/c) for comparison
- Antineutron-nucleus annihilation cross sections follow an $A^{2/3}$ scaling with 2% accuracy
- Backward-emitted antineutrons from CEX at 200 MeV/c antiprotons have a cross section of $(4.7 \pm 1.9)$ $\mu$b/sr
- The X(1835) resonance mass is $(1836.5 \pm 3.0(stat) ^{+5.6}_{-2.1}(syst))$ MeV/c^2 with width $(190 \pm 9(stat) ^{+3.8}_{-3.6}(syst))$ MeV
- OBELIX analyzed 35,118 events in $\bar{n}p \rightarrow 2\pi^+ \pi^-$ over 50–300 MeV/c
- OBELIX selected 241 exclusive events in $\bar{n}p \rightarrow K^+ K^- \pi^+$
- OBELIX selected 687 events in $\bar{n}p \rightarrow K^0_S K^0_S \pi^+$ over 50–400 MeV/c
- Current limit on free neutron-antineutron oscillation period: $\tau_{\bar{n}n} > 8.6 \times 10^8$ s (90% CL) from ILL (1994)
- Super-Kamiokande limit on oscillation period from $^{16}$O: $4.7 \times 10^8$ s (90% CL)

## Context

This review extends the analysis of antineutron interactions measured at LEAR by experiments including OBELIX (PS201), PS178, and PS199, and compares them with antiproton data from ASACUSA and earlier bubble-chamber experiments by Banerjee et al. (1985). The paper identifies specific discrepancies, such as the failure of the Friedman et al. (2014) optical-potential model to reproduce antineutron-nucleus annihilation cross sections, and the unresolved dip-bump structure in the elastic cross section at 65–80 MeV/c, that motivate future measurements at a slow-extraction upgraded AD facility. New experiments could resolve the isospin dependence of annihilation dynamics, clarify the nature of near-threshold structures like the X(1835) observed by BES and BESIII, and provide the antineutron-nucleus scattering lengths needed for next-generation neutron-antineutron oscillation searches using ultracold neutrons.
