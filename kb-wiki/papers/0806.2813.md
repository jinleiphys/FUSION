# Self-consistent calculations within the Green's function method including particle-phonon coupling and the single-particle continuum

> The Green's function method in the \emph{Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation} is applied to nuclear excitations in $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb. The calculations are performed self-consistently using a Skyrme interaction. The method combines the conventional RPA with an exact single-particle continuum treatment and considers in a consistent way the particle-phonon coupling. We reproduce not only the experimental values of low- and high-lying collective states but we also obtain fair agreement with the data of non-collective low-lying states that are strongly influenced by the particle-phonon coupling.

## Key claim

The Green's function method in the Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (QTBA), applied self-consistently with a Skyrme interaction and an exact treatment of the single-particle continuum, reproduces experimental low- and high-lying collective states and achieves fair agreement for non-collective low-lying states in $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb, but only because the phonon coupling compresses the too-wide Hartree-Fock single-particle spectrum; without this compression, the non-collective states deviate strongly from experiment.

## Method

The authors solve the RPA equations in coordinate space using the QTBA, which incorporates particle-phonon coupling into both the single-particle propagator and the particle-hole interaction. The mean field is calculated within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach using the SLy4 Skyrme parametrization, and the single-particle continuum is treated exactly rather than via discretization. The phonon vertices and energy-dependent corrections to the interaction are derived from the RPA wave functions of the phonons, introducing no new parameters beyond the Skyrme force.

## Key numbers

* In $^{208}$Pb, the four lowest $3^{-}$ states: RPA gives 3.25, 5.96, 6.18, 6.65 MeV; QTBA gives 2.43, 4.53, 4.96, 5.13 MeV; experiment gives 2.61, 4.05, 4.25, 4.70 MeV.
* In $^{208}$Pb, the four lowest $5^{-}$ states: RPA gives 4.13, 5.06, 5.48, 5.92 MeV; QTBA gives 3.30, 4.07, 4.52, 4.99 MeV; experiment gives 3.20, 3.71, 3.96, 4.13 MeV.
* In $^{208}$Pb, the four lowest $2^{+}$ states: RPA gives 5.33, 6.20, 6.42, 7.12 MeV; QTBA gives 4.39, 5.26, 5.96, 6.43 MeV; experiment gives 4.08, 4.93, 5.56, 5.72 MeV.
* In $^{208}$Pb, the four lowest $4^{+}$ states: RPA gives 5.59, 6.17, 6.77, 8.05 MeV; QTBA gives 4.61, 5.17, 5.52, 7.18 MeV; experiment gives 4.32, 4.91, 5.22, 5.56 MeV.
* In $^{132}$Sn, low-lying collective states with full continuum: $2^{+}$ at 3.70 MeV (QTBA) vs. 4.04 MeV experiment; $3^{-}$ at 4.60 MeV (QTBA) vs. 4.35 MeV experiment; $4^{+}$ at 4.20 MeV (QTBA) vs. 4.42 MeV experiment.
* Giant dipole resonance in $^{132}$Sn: RPA gives E = 14.3 MeV, Gamma = 3.4 MeV; QTBA gives E = 13.8 MeV, Gamma = 4.0 MeV; with isovector force increased by 10% (QTBA*), E = 16.1 MeV, Gamma = 4.5 MeV; experiment gives E = 16.1(7) MeV, Gamma = 4.7(2.1) MeV.
* Giant dipole resonance in $^{208}$Pb: RPA gives E = 12.4 MeV, Gamma = 2.7 MeV, sigma = 986 mb; QTBA gives E = 11.8 MeV, Gamma = 5.3 MeV, sigma = 552 mb; with isovector force increased by 6% (QTBA*), E = 13.4 MeV, Gamma = 5.3 MeV, sigma = 544 mb; experiment gives E = 13.4 MeV, Gamma = 4.1 MeV, sigma = 640 mb.
* Giant isoscalar quadrupole resonance in $^{208}$Pb: RPA gives E = 13.04 MeV, Gamma = 2.38 MeV; QTBA gives E = 12.63 MeV, Gamma = 3.30 MeV; experiment gives E = 11.0(0.2) MeV, Gamma = 3.3(0.3) MeV.
* Giant isovector quadrupole resonance in $^{208}$Pb: RPA gives E = 22.2 MeV, Gamma = 4.00 MeV; QTBA gives E = 22.4 MeV, Gamma = 4.78 MeV; experiment gives E = 22.5(1.0) MeV, Gamma = 6.0(3.0) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent QTBA calculations (Avdeenkov et al., 2004) by replacing the discretized continuum with an exact single-particle continuum treatment, which removes the arbitrariness in configuration-space size and significantly improves agreement for low-lying collective states in $^{132}$Sn. The results confirm microscopically the phenomenological analysis of Mahaux et al. on the effective mass in nuclei and the role of phonon coupling in compressing the single-particle spectrum. The method enables quantitative predictions for unstable neutron-rich nuclei where the continuum treatment is essential, and suggests that a small renormalization of the SLy4 parametrization could yield an optimal force for self-consistent calculations including phonon degrees of freedom.
