# Electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in chiral perturbation theory including vector mesons

> We calculate the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon up to fourth order in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral perturbation theory with vector mesons as explicit degrees of freedom. A systematic power counting for the renormalized diagrams is implemented using both the extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme and the reformulated version of infrared regularization. We analyze the electric and magnetic Sachs form factors, $G_E$ and $G_M$, and compare our results with the existing data. The inclusion of vector mesons results in a considerably improved description of the form factors. We observe that the most dominant contributions come from tree-level diagrams, while loop corrections with internal vector meson lines are small.

## Key claim

Including vector mesons as explicit degrees of freedom in chiral perturbation theory up to fourth order yields a considerably improved description of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, but the results with and without vector mesons are completely equivalent up to that order; the improvement comes from re-summing higher-order contributions (beyond \(q^4\)) that are reorganized by the vector-meson degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors calculated the electric and magnetic Sachs form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) of the proton and neutron up to order \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral perturbation theory, adding \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\phi\) mesons as explicit fields. They used both the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme and the reformulated version of infrared regularization to implement a systematic power counting for all diagrams, including those with vector mesons inside loops. The low-energy constants were fixed using the proton electric and magnetic radii and the neutron magnetic radius from Hammer (2003), and the vector-meson coupling constants were taken from Ecker (1989) and from dispersion relations of Mergell (1995) and Hammer (2003).

## Key numbers

- Proton electric radius: \(r_E^p = 0.848\ \text{fm}\) (used as input)
- Proton magnetic radius: \(r_M^p = 0.857\ \text{fm}\) (used as input)
- Neutron magnetic radius: \(r_M^n = 0.879\ \text{fm}\) (used as input)
- Vector-meson coupling constants (dimensionless unless noted): \(f_\rho = 0.10\), \(f_\omega = 0.03\), \(f_\phi = 0.05\), \(g_\rho = 4.0\), \(g_\omega = 42.8\), \(g_\phi = -20.6\), \(G_\rho = 13.0\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(G_\omega = 0.96\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(G_\phi = -3.3\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\)
- The form factors were calculated and compared with data in the momentum transfer range \(0\ \text{GeV}^2 \le Q^2 \le 0.4\ \text{GeV}^2\)
- The description improved considerably for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\) compared to the calculation without vector mesons

## Context

This work extends the earlier \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) calculation of nucleon form factors without vector mesons by Fuchs (2003) and the calculation with vector mesons by Kubis (2000) that could not treat vector mesons inside loops. By using the EOMS scheme and reformulated infrared regularization, the authors were able to include all diagrams up to \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) with vector mesons, showing that loop contributions with internal vector meson lines are small and that the dominant effects come from tree-level pole diagrams. This places the traditional vector-meson dominance model on a more systematic effective-field-theory footing and enables controlled higher-order corrections beyond the present calculation.
