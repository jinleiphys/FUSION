# Why Color-Flavor Locking is Just like Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> We review how a classification into representations of color and flavor can be used to understand the possible patterns of symmetry breaking for color superconductivity in dense quark matter. In particular, we show how for three flavors, color-flavor locking is precisely analogous to the usual pattern of chiral symmetry breaking in the QCD vacuum.

# Why Color-Flavor Locking is Just like Chiral Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim

Color-flavor locking for three flavors is precisely analogous to the usual pattern of chiral symmetry breaking in the QCD vacuum, but only after one recognizes that the color sextet admixture in the condensate is a red herring and concentrates on the color anti-triplet field; the analogy holds because both order parameters are $3 \times 3$ matrices that condense proportionally to the identity matrix, giving all quarks the same gap or constituent mass.

## Method

The authors classify possible diquark condensates by their representations under $SU(3)_c$ color and $SU(N_f)_f$ flavor, constrained by Fermi statistics which requires the condensate to be symmetric under simultaneous interchange of color and flavor indices. They construct an effective Lagrangian for the color anti-triplet field $\phi$ and color sextet field $\chi$, including a six-point operator $\det(\phi)^* \, \text{tr}(\phi \chi \phi)$ that is marginal in the renormalization-group sense but becomes linear in $\chi$ when $\phi$ condenses with nonzero determinant, thereby forcing a nonzero $\chi$ expectation value even when its mass-squared term is positive. The analysis is extended to $2+1$ flavors (up, down, strange) using analogous operators.

## Key numbers

- For $N_f = 2$ flavors, the maximum of the gap ratio is $\phi_0/\mu \simeq 0.13$, occurring at a coupling $g \simeq 4$ where $\alpha_s = g^2/4\pi \sim 1$, assuming the undetermined prefactor $b_0' = 1$.
- The ratio of the transition temperature to the gap is $T_c/\phi_0 \sim 1.13$, which is twice the BCS value.
- The gap decreases with increasing $N_f$ as $1/N_f^{5/2}$, assuming $b_0'$ does not depend significantly on $N_f$.
- For $2+1$ flavors, the pseudo-Goldstone boson from the strange quark mass term has mass $\sim 100$ MeV, while the pseudo-Goldstone boson for parity breaking in the instanton-free regime has mass linear in the up and down quark masses, $\sim 5$ or $10$ MeV, giving a relaxation time $\sim 10-20$ fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the classification of color-superconducting condensates developed by Pisarski and Rischke (1999) and provides a transparent explanation for the color-flavor locked condensate found by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998). The analogy to chiral symmetry breaking was noted in earlier work by the same authors and by Schafer and Wilczek (1999), but the present paper gives a detailed effective-Lagrangian derivation showing why the color sextet component necessarily appears for three flavors but not for two. The analysis enables future study of intermediate-density phases where the color sextet channel might become attractive, and suggests observable parity-violating signals in heavy-ion collisions if cool, dense quark matter is produced in an instanton-free regime.
