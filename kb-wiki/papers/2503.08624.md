# Light-by-Light scattering in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions: Estimating inelastic contributions

> The current state-of-the-art theoretical estimations lead to cross-sections for $AA \to γγAA$ which are somewhat smaller than the measured ones by the ATLAS and CMS Collaborations, which motivates the searching and calculation of subleading corrections disregarded in these previous studies. In this paper, we estimate for the first time the contribution of inelastic channels to the Light-by-Light (LbL) scattering in ultraperipheral collisions of heavy ions (UPHICs), in which one or both of the incident nuclei dissociate ($A A \to γγX Y$ where $X, Y = A, A'$) due to the photon emission. These new mechanisms are related to extra emissions that are rather difficult to identify at the LHC and may be misinterpreted as enhanced $γγ\to γγ$ scattering compared to the Standard Model result. We include processes of coupling of photons to individual nucleons (protons and neutrons) in addition to coherent coupling to the whole nuclei (called standard approach here). Both elastic (nucleon in the ground state) and inelastic (nucleon in an excited state) in the couplings of photons to nucleons are taken into account. The inelastic nucleon fluxes are calculated using CT18qed photon in nucleon PDFs. The inelastic photon fluxes are shown and compared to standard photon fluxes in the nucleus. In addition, we show the ratio of the inelastic corrections to the standard contribution as a function of diphoton invariant mass and photon rapidity difference. We find that for the ATLAS acceptance region the inelastic corrections grow with $M_{γγ}$ and rapidity difference. Our results indicate that the inelastic contributions can be of the order of 20-40 \% of the traditional (no nuclear excitation) predictions. Uncertainties due to factorization scale choice are quantified.

## Key claim

The inelastic contributions to Light-by-Light scattering in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions, where one or both nuclei dissociate due to photon emission, can be of the order of 20-40% of the traditional elastic-only predictions, which may help resolve the tension where state-of-the-art theoretical cross-sections are somewhat smaller than those measured by ATLAS and CMS.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross-section for Light-by-Light scattering in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV by summing elastic, semi-elastic, and inelastic contributions. The elastic photon flux for the nucleus used the Weizsacker-Williams distribution with a sharp cutoff at the nuclear radius, while the inelastic photon flux for the nucleus was taken as the incoherent sum of proton and neutron photon distributions from the CT18qed parametrization, including both elastic (nucleon in ground state) and inelastic (nucleon in excited state) nucleon contributions. The elementary gamma gamma -> gamma gamma cross-section was calculated including fermionic loops, and the hard scale was set to the diphoton invariant mass M_gamma_gamma.

## Key numbers

* The inelastic contributions are of the order of 20-40% of the traditional (no nuclear excitation) predictions for the ATLAS acceptance region.
* The analysis considered PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with ATLAS kinematic cuts: photon transverse momentum p_t > 2.5 GeV, photon rapidity range -2.4 < y < 2.4, and diphoton invariant mass M_gamma_gamma > 5 GeV.
* The ratio R_inel (sum of elastic, semi-elastic, and inelastic over elastic only) grows with M_gamma_gamma and rapidity difference y_diff = y_1 - y_2.
* The uncertainty band due to factorization scale choice was calculated by varying the scale between M_gamma_gamma^2/2 and 2 M_gamma_gamma^2.

## Context

This work extends the standard approach to Light-by-Light scattering in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, which previously assumed only coherent photon coupling to the whole nucleus, by including for the first time inelastic channels where photons couple to individual nucleons. The study is motivated by the tension between experimental data from ATLAS (2020) and CMS and theoretical predictions that remain somewhat smaller even after including next-to-leading order corrections to gamma gamma -> gamma gamma scattering (AH:2023kor, AH:2023ewe). The results enable improved modeling of the Standard Model background for searches for beyond-Standard-Model physics and could be implemented in Monte Carlo generators such as Superchic, which has recently incorporated semi-elastic processes for pp collisions (Harland-Lang:2022jwn) and ion dissociation in UPCs (Harland-Lang:2023ohq).
