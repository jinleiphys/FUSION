# Medium-induced radiation with vacuum propagation in the pre-hydrodynamics phase

> The recent discovery of the potential of jet quenching observables to constrain the initial stages after a heavy-ion collision makes imperative to have a better understanding of the process of medium-induced radiation before the formation of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and its impact on observables at high-$p_T$. In this work, we generalize the BDMPS-Z framework for medium-induced radiation to account for additional emissions occurring before the creation of the QGP. For simplicity, we assume that during the pre-hydrodynamics phase the hard parton propagates as in vacuum. This set-up, allows us to isolate the contribution from the additional initial radiation by comparing with the usual scenarios in which the emitter is created inside the medium but with different starting points. Using both a numerical implementation of the fully resummed emission spectrum and the usual analytical approximations, we find that replacing an initial slab of the medium by vacuum yields to a significant reduction of the emission spectrum for low radiated gluon energies, while the high-energy tails remain largely unmodified. Finally, we assess the effect of replacing the initial medium by vacuum propagation on the single-inclusive particle suppression $R_{AA}$ and high-$p_T$ azimuthal asymmetry $v_2$. Our findings indicate that considering vacuum propagation prior to hydrodynamization leads to an increase in the $v_2$, thus corroborating the importance of the treatment of jet quenching in the initial stages for the correct description of both observables.

## Method

The authors derive the fully resummed BDMPS-Z medium-induced radiation spectrum for generic production time τ_p and medium formation time τ_m, then split the t-integration at τ_m to isolate the contribution from initial vacuum propagation. They numerically evaluate the energy distribution for a static "brick" medium with Yukawa (Gyulassy-Wang) elastic scattering, comparing three scenarios: (A) τ_p = τ_m = 0, (B) τ_p = τ_m > 0, and (C) τ_m > τ_p = 0. They also compute the first opacity (GLV) limit and the harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation for the same scenarios, and assess the effect on the nuclear modification factor R_AA and high-p_T azimuthal asymmetry v_2 for 20-30% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For a brick with n_0L = 5 and τ_m/L = 0.1, the fully resummed spectrum with vacuum propagation (case C) lies between the τ_p = τ_m = 0 and τ_p = τ_m = 0.1L cases, with differences only noticeable for very small ω/ω̄_c (left panel, Fig. 1).
- For τ_m/L = 0.3 with n_0L = 5, the differences between case C and the τ_m = 0 case grow, and the two curves agree only in the high-energy tail (Fig. 2).
- The GLV spectrum without kinematic constraint (Eq. 3.15) shows that for very large radiated gluon energies (ω ≫ ω̄_m), the initial part of the propagation does not affect the spectrum, yielding identical spectra for emitters created inside or outside the medium.
- The strong coupling is fixed to α_s = 0.3 and the quark Casimir to C_F = 4/3 throughout the numerical evaluations.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z framework (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) for medium-induced radiation to include emissions before QGP formation, motivated by findings from Andres (2019) and Zigic (2019) that present energy loss implementations need strong suppression of jet quenching in the pre-hydrodynamic phase to simultaneously describe R_AA and high-p_T v_2. The authors compare with scenarios where the emitter is created inside the medium at different starting points (Renk 2010, Armesto 2009, Andres 2016, Andres 2017), and their results corroborate the importance of treating jet quenching in the initial stages for correct description of both observables. The work enables more realistic modeling of jet quenching that accounts for the full evolution of the system from the initial hard scattering through the QGP phase.
