# On the pi pi continuum in the nucleon form factors and the proton radius puzzle

> We present an improved determination of the $ππ$ continuum contribution to the isovector spectral functions of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors. Our analysis includes the most up-to-date results for the $ππ\to\bar N N$ partial waves extracted from Roy-Steiner equations, consistent input for the pion vector form factor, and a thorough discussion of isospin-violating effects and uncertainty estimates. As an application, we consider the $ππ$ contribution to the isovector electric and magnetic radii by means of sum rules, which, in combination with the accurately known neutron electric radius, are found to slightly prefer a small proton charge radius.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved determination of the ππ continuum contribution to the isovector spectral functions of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, finding that when combined with the accurately known neutron electric radius, the results slightly prefer a small proton charge radius, but the authors hedge that the ππ contribution alone does not definitively resolve the proton radius puzzle, and the analysis is limited to energies below 1 GeV where 4π intermediate states are neglected.

## Method

The authors compute the isovector spectral functions for the electric and magnetic nucleon form factors using the unitarity relation that connects them to the ππ → N̅N P-wave partial waves (f⁺ and f⁻) and the pion vector form factor FπV. The ππ → N̅N partial waves are extracted from Roy-Steiner equations for πN scattering, using the most up-to-date subthreshold parameters and three variants of ππ P-wave phase shifts (from Bern, Madrid-Cracow, and a variant including ρ′ and ρ″ effects). The pion vector form factor is fit to experimental data from BaBar, KLOE, and BESIII below √t = 1 GeV using an Omnès representation with ρ-ω mixing, ensuring consistency with the same ππ phase shifts used in the Roy-Steiner analysis. Isospin-violating corrections from proton-neutron mass differences in Born terms, subthreshold parameters, and ρ-ω mixing are evaluated and found to be negligible except for the ρ-ω mixing treatment, which is included in the final spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- The ππ contribution to the isovector electric radius sum rule: ⟨r²E⟩v = 0.418(32) fm² with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV, and 0.405(36) fm² with cutoff Λ = 2mN
- The ππ contribution to the isovector magnetic radius sum rule: ⟨r²M⟩v = 1.83(10) fm² with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV, and 1.81(11) fm² with cutoff Λ = 2mN
- The ππ contribution to the isovector electric form factor normalization at t=0: GEv(0) = 0.76(8) with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV, compared to the exact value of 0.5
- The ππ contribution to the isovector magnetic form factor normalization at t=0: GMv(0) = 3.34(25) with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV, compared to the exact value of 2.35
- The πN coupling constant used: gc²/(4π) = 13.7(0.2) from Baru (2011)
- The ω → π⁺π⁻ branching fraction: 1.53⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀·₁₃%

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of nucleon form factors originally developed by Höhler (1976, 1983) and Mergell (1995), and later refined by Hammer (2003) and Belushkin (2005, 2006), by updating the ππ → N̅N partial waves using modern Roy-Steiner equation solutions from Hoferichter (2015) and consistent pion vector form factor fits to recent BaBar, KLOE, and BESIII data. The improved spectral functions enable more reliable extraction of the proton charge radius from electron-proton scattering data, addressing criticisms by Lee (2015) regarding the lack of systematic uncertainties and isospin-violating corrections in previous analyses. The results also find applications in improving the nucleon-pole-term contribution to bremsstrahlung in proton-nucleus scattering and in transverse charge density calculations.
