# Explanation of the RHIC pt-spectra in a single-freeze-out model

> The pt-spectra of hadrons measured at RHIC are very well described in a model which assumes that the chemical and thermal freeze-outs occur simultaneously. The model calculation includes all hadronic resonances and uses a simple parametrization of the freeze-out hypersurface.

# Explanation of the RHIC pt-spectra in a single-freeze-out model

## Key claim
The transverse momentum spectra of hadrons measured at RHIC can be described simultaneously with hadron yield ratios using a model where chemical and thermal freeze-out occur at the same time, requiring only four parameters (two thermodynamic and two geometric). The model reproduces spectra across multiple hadron species including pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, phi mesons, and hyperons, but the geometric parameters obtained are too low compared to measured HBT radii, requiring future excluded-volume corrections.

## Method
The authors combine a thermal model for hadron yields with a Cooper-Frye freeze-out formalism using a constant proper-time hypersurface (tau = constant) and Hubble-like hydrodynamic flow. All hadronic resonances are included, and all cascade decays are treated exactly in a semi-analytic fashion. The thermodynamic parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu_B) are fixed by fitting to nine independent hadron yield ratios, while the geometric parameters (proper time tau and maximum transverse radius rho_max) are determined from the spectral shapes.

## Key numbers
- Temperature T = 165 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 41 MeV from fit to 9 independent hadron yield ratios at RHIC
- For minimum-bias data: tau = 5.55 fm and rho_max = 4.50 fm
- For most central collisions: tau = 7.66 fm and rho_max = 6.69 fm
- Resonance decays produce an effective cooling of the spectrum by 30-40 MeV, reducing the inverse slope
- The chemical freeze-out temperature is close to 170 MeV in different approaches, while the apparent temperature from spectral analysis is typically not more than 130 MeV

## Context
This work extends the thermal model approach used for hadron yield studies (references 1-10 in the paper) by adding a hydrodynamic freeze-out description, generalizing the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1983). The single-freeze-out assumption is supported by RHIC HBT measurements showing R_out/R_side close to unity, indicating short pion emission times, and by the antibaryon puzzle (Rapp, 2001) where the absence of significant p-pbar annihilation suggests no long period between chemical and thermal freeze-out. The model enables future calculations of elliptic flow and rapidity dependence, and the authors note that excluded-volume corrections (references to earlier thermal model work) could resolve the discrepancy between fitted geometric parameters and measured HBT radii.
