# NICER view on holographic QCD

> The holographic models for dense QCD matter work surprisingly well. A general implication seems that the deconfinement phase transition dictates the maximum mass of neutron stars. The nuclear matter phase turns out to be rather stiff which, if continuously merged with nuclear matter models based on effective field theories, leads to the conclusion that neutron stars do not have quark matter cores in the light of all current astrophysical data. We comment that as the perturbative QCD results are in stark contrast with strong coupling results, any future simulations of neutron star mergers incorporating corrections beyond ideal fluid should proceed cautiously. For this purpose, we provide a model which treats nuclear and quark matter phases in a unified framework at strong coupling.

## Key claim
Holographic QCD models, when matched to traditional nuclear matter equations of state at low densities and constrained by all current astrophysical data, imply that neutron stars do not contain quark matter cores, contrary to some recent claims. The deconfinement phase transition, rather than the central density, dictates the maximum mass of neutron stars in this framework.

## Method
The author uses the V-QCD holographic model, a bottom-up approach based on string theory in the Veneziano limit (large number of colors Nc and flavors Nf with fixed ratio), extrapolated to finite baryon density and low temperature. A hybrid equation of state is constructed by matching the holographic nuclear matter phase to traditional nuclear matter models from effective field theory at a matching density of n ~ 1.2-2 n_s, then solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to compute mass-radius relations and applying astrophysical constraints from NICER, LIGO/Virgo, and pulsar mass measurements.

## Key numbers
- Matching density between nuclear matter models and holographic V-QCD: n ~ 1.2-2 n_s (nuclear saturation density)
- Constrained neutron star radius for 1.4 solar masses: 12.0 km ≤ R(1.4 M_sun) ≤ 12.8 km
- Tidal deformability constraint from LIGO/Virgo: 70 ≤ Λ(1.4 M_sun) ≤ 580
- Minimum neutron star mass constraint from observed pulsars: M ≥ 2 M_sun
- NICER bound used: R(2 M_sun) > 12.2 km
- Representative V-QCD(APR) curve matched at n_tr = 1.6 n_s emerges as a particularly realistic equation of state

## Context
This work extends the V-QCD holographic framework developed by Jarvinen (2011) and applied to finite density by Alho (2012, 2013), building on earlier holographic studies of deconfinement by Hoyos (2016). The results contradict the claims of Annala (2019) that neutron stars may contain quark matter cores, arguing instead that the polytropic index of holographic nuclear matter reaches very small values before the deconfinement transition, which those authors misidentify as quark matter. The paper enables future neutron star merger simulations with a unified treatment of nuclear and quark matter phases at strong coupling, and provides predictions for gravitational wave peak frequencies testable by LIGO/Virgo.
