# Mass inequality for the quark propagator

> We show that for any gauge-fixing scheme with positive semi-definite functional integral measure, the inverse correlation length of the quark propagator is bounded below by one-half the pion mass.

## Key claim

For any gauge-fixing scheme with a positive semi-definite functional integral measure, the inverse correlation length of the quark propagator (the dressed quark mass at zero momentum) is bounded below by one-half the pion mass; the inequality holds under the conditions of γ5-Hermiticity, exact flavor symmetry, and an even number of degenerate quark flavors.

## Method

The authors prove a general inequality by considering the pion correlation function and the quark correlator in Euclidean QCD with a gauge-fixed action. Using the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality, they relate the absolute value of the quark correlator to the square root of the absolute value of the pion correlation function, and then extract the inverse correlation lengths in the limit of large Euclidean time separation.

## Key numbers

- The dressed quark mass at zero momentum, M(0), from Dyson-Schwinger studies is cited as being between 170 MeV and 500 MeV.
- Quenched lattice results using Landau and Laplacian gauge with overlap fermions and improved staggered fermions yield M(0) between 250 and 500 MeV.
- The proven inequality states: [M(0)]_f ≥ (1/2)[m_π]_f, where the subscript f denotes the gauge-fixed scheme.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Weingarten (1983), who proved that the pion must have the lowest energy of all meson states for an even number of degenerate quark flavors. The new inequality provides a rigorous lower bound on the infrared quark mass in terms of the pion mass, applicable to all gauge-fixing schemes used in recent lattice simulations. It enables further studies of the dependence of the dressed quark mass on gauge-fixing scheme, lattice spacing, quark masses, and quenching artifacts, and can be tested against Dyson-Schwinger equation results and lattice simulations.
