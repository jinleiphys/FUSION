# Zipf's Law in the Liquid Gas Phase Transition of Nuclei

> Zipf's law in the field of linguistics is tested in the nuclear disassembly within the framework of isospin dependent lattice gas model. It is found that the average cluster charge (or mass) of rank $n$ in the charge (or mass) list shows exactly inversely to its rank, i.e., there exists Zipf's law, at the phase transition temperature. This novel criterion shall be helpful to search the nuclear liquid gas phase transition experimentally and theoretically. In addition, the finite size scaling of the effective phase transition temperature at which the Zipf's law appears is studied for several systems with different mass and the critical exponents of $ν$ and $β$ are tentatively extracted.

# Zipf's Law in the Liquid Gas Phase Transition of Nuclei

## Key claim
The average charge (or mass) of nuclear clusters of rank n in the charge (or mass) list follows an inverse proportionality to its rank (Zipf's law) specifically at the phase transition temperature of nuclear disassembly, as demonstrated within the isospin dependent lattice gas model. This criterion is proposed as a novel signature to identify the nuclear liquid gas phase transition, though the author notes that the extracted critical exponents are sensitive to the chosen effective phase transition temperature and largest cluster size.

## Method
The study employed an isospin dependent lattice gas model (I-LGM) with nucleons placed on a three-dimensional cubic lattice via Monte Carlo Metropolis sampling, using isospin-dependent nearest-neighbor interactions (repulsive for like-nucleons, attractive for unlike-nucleons) to reproduce nuclear binding energies. Cluster identification used a Coniglio-Klein type prescription where two nucleons belong to the same cluster if their relative kinetic energy is less than their interaction energy. For each system, 1000 events were simulated at a freeze-out density of 0.38 ρ₀, and the average charge of clusters for each rank was computed across events to test the Zipf relation Zₙ ∝ n⁻¹.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²⁹Xe, the slope parameter λ of the Zₙ ∝ n⁻¹ relation equals 1.00 at T = 5.5 MeV, identifying the phase transition temperature; λ = 5.77 at T = 3 MeV, λ = 3.65 at T = 4 MeV, λ = 1.53 at T = 5 MeV, λ = 0.80 at T = 6 MeV, and λ = 0.56 at T = 7 MeV.
- Finite size scaling of the effective phase transition temperature T_A for systems with the same N/Z as ¹²⁹Xe but A = 80, 274, 500, 830, and 1270 (lattice sizes 6³, 9³, 11³, 13³, and 15³) yields an extrapolated critical temperature T_C = 5.846 ± 0.013 MeV for infinite nuclear matter.
- The critical exponent ν extracted from finite size scaling is ν = 0.348 ± 0.023, approaching the 3D Ising/liquid gas class value (ν = 0.6) rather than the 3D percolation class (ν = 0.9).
- The ratio β/ν extracted from the scaling of the largest cluster size at the phase transition point is β/ν = 1.105 ± 0.039, close to the classical value.

## Context
This work extends the application of Zipf's law, previously known in linguistics and observed in percolation processes by Watanabe (1996), to nuclear multifragmentation as a novel signature of the liquid gas phase transition. The study builds on prior lattice gas model calculations by Pan and Das Gupta (1995, 1998) and Campi and Krivine (1997), and complements existing phase transition observables such as the power-law parameter τ, the second moment S₂, and intermediate mass fragment multiplicity N_imf. The finite size scaling analysis enables extrapolation to infinite nuclear matter and tentative extraction of critical exponents, potentially allowing comparison with experimental data from multifragmentation studies by the ALADIN, INDRA, MSU, TAMU, EOS, and GSI collaborations.
