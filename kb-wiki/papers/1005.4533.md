# Radiatively Induced Non Linearity in the Walecka Model

> We evaluate the effective potential for the conventional linear Walecka non perturbatively up to one loop. This quantity is then renormalized with a prescription which allows finite vacuum contributions to the three as well as four 1PI Green's functions to survive. These terms, which are absent in the standard relativistic Hartree approximation, have a logarithmic energy scale dependence that can be tuned so as to mimic the effects of phi3 and phi4 type of terms present in the non linear Walecka model improving quantities such as the compressibility modulus and the effective nucleon mass, at saturation, by considering energy scales which are very close to the nucleon mass at vanishing density.

## Key claim

The authors show that by using a renormalization prescription that preserves finite, scale-dependent contributions to the three- and four-point 1PI Green's functions (while eliminating them for lower-order functions), one can mimic the effects of explicit phi^3 and phi^4 terms in the nonlinear Walecka model, improving the compressibility modulus and effective nucleon mass at saturation without introducing new coupling parameters.

## Method

The authors evaluate the effective potential for the linear Walecka model nonperturbatively at one loop using Matsubara's imaginary time formalism at zero temperature. They regularize divergent vacuum integrals using dimensional regularization with the modified minimal subtraction (overline MS) scheme, then apply a hybrid renormalization prescription that eliminates vacuum contributions to the zero-, one-, and two-point functions (so that M, m_s, and m_v represent physical vacuum masses) while retaining only the scale-dependent leading-logarithmic parts of the three- and four-point functions. This yields effective running couplings kappa_eff and lambda_eff that vanish at Lambda_overlineMS = M (recovering the Relativistic Hartree Approximation) and vary with scale otherwise.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.193 fm^{-3}; binding energy per nucleon: BE = -15.75 MeV (fitted values, from Walecka (1974))
* Fermi momentum at saturation: k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1} (280.20 MeV)
* Meson masses used: m_s = 512 MeV, m_v = 783 MeV
* Nucleon mass: M = 939 MeV
* Best LHA results obtained at energy scales Lambda_overlineMS approximately 0.92 M to 0.98 M (i.e., roughly 864-920 MeV)
* At Lambda_overlineMS = M, the RHA result is recovered (M*_sat ~ 0.72 M, K ~ 470 MeV)
* In the MRHA of Heide and Rudaz, the optimum scale is about 35% smaller than M (i.e., ~610 MeV), whereas in LHA the optimum scale is only about 5% smaller than M

## Context

This work extends the approach of Heide and Rudaz (1994) (the Modified Relativistic Hartree Approximation, MRHA), who first showed that retaining scale dependence in vacuum contributions could improve nuclear matter properties without explicit nonlinear meson couplings. The authors' Logarithmic Hartree Approximation (LHA) differs from MRHA by restricting the scale dependence to only the leading-logarithmic terms that naturally arise in dimensional regularization, rather than including additional linear terms in the cubic coupling. The LHA also differs from the standard Relativistic Hartree Approximation of Chin (1977) and from the nonlinear Walecka model of Boguta and Bodmer (1977), which introduces explicit phi^3 and phi^4 terms with parameters b and c. The approach enables a simpler tuning of the compressibility modulus and effective mass while keeping the energy scale close to the nucleon mass, unlike MRHA which requires scales about 35% lower.
