# Trace initial interaction from final state observable in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> In order to trace the initial interaction in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision in all azimuthal directions, two azimuthal multiplicity-correlation patterns -- neighboring and fixed-to-arbitrary angular-bin correlation patterns -- are suggested. From the simulation of Au + Au collisions at 200 GeV by using the Monte Carlo models RQMD with hadron re-scattering and AMPT with and without string melting, we observe that the correlation patterns change gradually from out-of-plane preferential one to in-plane preferential one when the centrality of collision shifts from central to peripheral, meanwhile the anisotropic collective flow v_2 keeps positive in all cases. This regularity is found to be model and collision energy independent. The physics behind the two opposite trends of correlation patterns, in particular, the presence of out-of-plane correlation patterns at RHIC energy, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that two azimuthal multiplicity-correlation patterns (neighboring and fixed-to-arbitrary angular-bin correlations) can reveal the initial interaction geometry in relativistic heavy ion collisions, even when the standard elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) remains positive. The key hedge is that the out-of-plane correlation pattern observed at RHIC energies is not due to the initial squeeze-out effect (as at lower energies) but instead arises from the eccentricity of the initial overlap zone, and that this pattern coexists with in-plane flow.

## Method

The authors simulated Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV using three Monte Carlo models: RQMD with hadron re-scattering, AMPT with string melting, and AMPT without string melting. They divided the full \(2\pi\) azimuthal angle into 50 equal bins and computed two correlation functions: the neighboring angular-bin correlation \(C_{m,m+1}\) and the fixed-to-arbitrary angular-bin correlation \(C_{m_0,m}\), where \(m_0\) was fixed at angles corresponding to \(\phi \approx 0, \pi/4, \pi/2\). The centrality dependence of these patterns was studied across several centrality ranges, and additional simulations at 60 GeV and 5 GeV were performed with RQMD to test energy dependence.

## Key numbers

* Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV: 249,824 events from RQMD, 204,004 events from AMPT with string melting, and 686,278 events from AMPT without string melting were generated.
* The azimuthal range \(2\pi\) was partitioned uniformly into 50 equal-size angular bins.
* In peripheral collisions (centrality 40–60%), the correlation patterns showed a \(\cos^2\phi\)-like shape; in near-central collisions (centrality 0–20%), they showed a \(\sin^2\phi\)-like shape.
* The out-of-plane correlation pattern was observed at RHIC energy (200 GeV) and also at 60 GeV and 5 GeV in RQMD simulations, demonstrating energy independence.
* The anisotropic collective flow \(v_2\) remained positive in all cases across all models and energies (200 GeV, 60 GeV, 5 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of azimuthal correlations beyond the standard two-particle correlation function used by PHENIX (2002) and the anisotropic flow parameter \(v_2\) introduced by Sorge (1997). The authors build on their own prior development of spatial-dependent correlation patterns (Wu et al., 2005) and apply them for the first time to relativistic heavy ion collisions. The results contradict the expectation that out-of-plane correlations should vanish at RHIC energies due to the rapid departure of spectator nucleons (Liu, Panitkin, and Xu, 1999), and instead suggest that the initial eccentricity of the overlap zone can produce such patterns even when \(v_2\) is positive. The work enables future experimental searches for these out-of-plane correlation patterns at RHIC, which would provide direct information on pre-thermalization interactions and their competition with collective expansion.
