# Photo-production of Bound States with Hidden Charms

> The photo-production of $J/Ψ$-$^3He$ bound state ($[^3He]_{J/Ψ}$) on a $^4He$ target has been investigated using the impulse approximation. The calculations have been performed using several $γ+N \rightarrow J/Ψ+N$ models based on the Pomeron-exchange and accounting for the pion-exchange mechanism at low energies. The $J/Ψ$ wavefunctions in $[^3He]_{J/Ψ}$ are generated from various $J/Ψ$-nucleus potentials which are constructed by either using a procedure based on the Pomeron-quark coupling mechanism or folding a $J/Ψ$-N potential ($v_{J/Ψ,N}$) into the nuclear densities. We consider $v_{J/Ψ,N}$ derived from the effective field theory approach, Lattice QCD, and Pomeron-quark coupling mechanism. The upper bound of the predicted total cross sections is about $0.1 - 0.3$ pico-barn. We also consider the possibility of photo-production of a six quark-$J/Ψ$ bound state ($[q^6]_{J/Ψ})$ on the $^3He$ target. The Compound Bag Model of $NN$ scattering and the quark cluster model of nuclei are used to estimate the $[q^6]$-N wavefunction in $^3He$ by imposing the condition that the calculated $^3He$ charge form factor must be consistent with what is predicted by the conventional nuclear model. The upper bound of the predicted total cross sections of $γ+ ^3He \rightarrow [q^6]_{J/Ψ} +N$ is about 2 - 4 pico-barn, depending on the model of $γ+N \rightarrow J/Ψ+N$ used in the calculations. Our results call for the need of precise measurements of $γ+p \rightarrow J/Ψ+p$ and also the $γ+^2H\rightarrow J/Ψ+n + p $ reactions near the threshold.

## Key claim

The photo-production cross sections for $J/\Psi$-$^3He$ bound states on a $^4He$ target are predicted to have an upper bound of about 0.1–0.3 picobarn, but this result depends sensitively on the choice of $J/\Psi$-nucleus potential and the model for the $\gamma+N\rightarrow J/\Psi+N$ reaction, with the binding energy of the $[^3He]_{J/\Psi}$ system varying from 19.86 MeV down to 0.04 MeV across different parameter sets.

## Method

The authors used the impulse approximation to calculate the cross section for $\gamma+^4He\rightarrow [^3He]_{J/\Psi}+N$, employing several models for the elementary $\gamma+N\rightarrow J/\Psi+N$ amplitude: a Pomeron-exchange model (with parameters adjusted to fit data up to $W=300$ GeV), a two-gluon exchange model, and a two-gluon-plus-three-gluon exchange model from Brodsky et al. (1990). The $J/\Psi$ wavefunctions in the bound state were generated from Yukawa-type $J/\Psi$-$^3He$ potentials constructed either via a Pomeron-quark coupling procedure or by folding a $J/\Psi$-N potential (derived from effective field theory, Lattice QCD, or Pomeron-quark coupling) into the nuclear density of $^3He$. For the six-quark-$J/\Psi$ bound state $[q^6]_{J/\Psi}$, the Compound Bag Model of $NN$ scattering and the quark cluster model were used to estimate the $[q^6]$-N wavefunction in $^3He$, constrained by the $^3He$ charge form factor.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound of predicted total cross section for $\gamma+^4He\rightarrow [^3He]_{J/\Psi}+n$: 0.1–0.3 picobarn, depending on the model.
- Upper bound of predicted total cross section for $\gamma+^3He\rightarrow [q^6]_{J/\Psi}+N$: 2–4 picobarn, depending on the $\gamma+N\rightarrow J/\Psi+N$ model.
- Binding energies for $[^3He]_{J/\Psi}$ from the Pomeron-quark coupling model: 19.86 MeV (with $\beta_{u/d}=1.85$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\beta_c=1.85$ GeV$^{-1}$), 3.27 MeV ($\beta_{u/d}=2.05$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\beta_c=1.21$ GeV$^{-1}$), and 0.04 MeV ($\beta_{u/d}=2.05$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\beta_c=0.84$ GeV$^{-1}$).
- Binding energy for $[^3He]_{J/\Psi}$ from the folding model using parameters of Brodsky et al. (1990): 1.62 MeV.
- Binding energies for $[^{12}C]_{J/\Psi}$ from the Pomeron-quark coupling model: 280.0 MeV, 165.0 MeV, and 67.0 MeV for the same three parameter sets.
- Binding energy for $[^{12}C]_{J/\Psi}$ from the folding model: 7.0 MeV.
- Pomeron-exchange model parameters used for predictions: $\alpha_0=1.25$, $\beta_c=0.84$ GeV$^{-1}$ (PM model).
- $J/\Psi$-N scattering lengths from prior work: -0.24 fm (Brodsky and Miller, based on effective field theory), -0.05 fm (Kaidalov and Volkovitsky), -0.09 fm (Lattice QCD by Kawanai and Saski), and -8.83 fm (Pomeron-exchange model of Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of $J/\Psi$-nucleus bound states, following the Pomeron-exchange approach of Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond (1990) and the effective field theory results of Luke, Manohar, and Savage (1992). It also builds on the suggestion by Brodsky, Chudakov, Hoyer, and Laget (2004) that $J/\Psi$ could interact strongly with six-quark components in nuclei. The results call for precise measurements of $\gamma+p\rightarrow J/\Psi+p$ and $\gamma+^2H\rightarrow J/\Psi+n+p$ near threshold to refine the reaction models, and enable future experimental searches for these exotic bound states at facilities such as those discussed in the workshop referenced by the authors.
