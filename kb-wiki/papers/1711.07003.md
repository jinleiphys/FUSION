# Baryon interactions from lattice QCD with physical masses --- strangeness $S=-1$ sector ---

> We present our recent results of baryon interactions with strangeness $S=-1$ based on Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) correlation functions calculated from lattice QCD with almost physical quark masses corresponding to $(m_π,m_K)\approx(146,525)$ MeV and large volume $(La)^4=(96a)^4\approx$ (8.1 fm)$^4$. In order to perform a comprehensive study of baryon interactions, a large number of NBS correlation functions from NN to $ΞΞ$ are calculated simultaneously by using large scale computer resources. In this contribution, we focus on the strangeness $S=-1$ channels of the hyperon interactions by means of HAL QCD method. Four sets of three potentials (the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ central, $^3S_1-^3D_1$ tensor, and the $^1S_0$ central potentials) are presented for the $ΣN - ΣN$ (the isospin $I=3/2$) diagonal, the $ΛN - ΛN$ diagonal, the $ΛN \rightarrow ΣN$ transition, and the $ΣN - ΣN$ ($I=1/2$) diagonal interactions. Scattering phase shifts for $ΣN$ $(I=3/2)$ system are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary lattice QCD results for hyperon-nucleon interactions in the strangeness S=-1 sector at nearly physical quark masses, but the authors explicitly caution that the phase shifts and mixing angles should be regarded as preliminary due to large statistical fluctuations that require future analysis with larger statistics.

## Method

The authors computed Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) correlation functions from Nf=2+1 lattice QCD gauge configurations on a 96^4 lattice with almost physical quark masses. Using the HAL QCD method, they extracted leading-order (LO) potentials (central and tensor) for the S=-1 baryon-baryon channels via the velocity expansion of the non-local potential, then parametrized these potentials with analytic functional forms to solve the Schrodinger equation for scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters: (m_pi, m_K) approximately (146, 525) MeV; lattice spacing a approximately 0.085 fm; physical volume (La)^4 approximately (8.1 fm)^4
- 207 gauge configurations were used, with 96 wall sources per configuration, at every 10 trajectories
- The single baryon effective mass plateaux for N, Lambda, and Sigma start from time slice t-t0 approximately 14
- For the Sigma N (I=3/2) system: the central potential in the ^3S1-^3D1 channel shows repulsive core for r less than approximately 1 fm
- The phase shift bar-delta_0 in the ^3S1-^3D1 channel of Sigma N (I=3/2) shows repulsive interaction at time slices t-t0=9-11
- The phase shift in the ^1S0 channel of Sigma N (I=3/2) shows attractive interaction on average

## Context

This work extends earlier HAL QCD method results reported at LATTICE 2008, LATTICE 2009, and LATTICE 2011 (Nemura 2012) that used heavier quark masses and smaller lattice volumes, and also extends studies by Beane (2012) on the Sigma- n channel. The results are qualitatively consistent with phenomenological approaches such as those by Fujiwara (1996), Arisaka (2000), and Haidenbauer (2013), and with the flavor SU(3) limit results from Inoue (2011). The calculations enable future quantitative studies of hypernuclear binding and hyperonic matter in neutron stars, where recent observations of neutron stars heavier than 2 solar masses (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) challenge hyperonic equations of state.
