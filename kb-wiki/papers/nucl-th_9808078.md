# Bose-Einstein Correlations and the Equation of State of Nuclear Matter in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Experimental spectra of the CERN/SPS experiments NA44 and NA49 are fitted while using four different equations of state of nuclear matter within a relativistic hydrodynamic framework. For the freeze-out temperatures, $T_f = 139$ MeV and $T_f = 116$ MeV, respectively, the corresponding freeze-out hypersurfaces and Bose-Einstein correlation functions for identical pion pairs are discussed. It is concluded, that the Bose-Einstein interferometry measures the relationship between the temperature and the energy density in the equation of state of nuclear matter at the late hadronic stage of the fireball expansion. It is necessary, to use the detailed detector acceptances in the calculations for the Bose-Einstein correlations.

## Key claim
Bose-Einstein correlation functions for identical pion pairs are sensitive to the relationship between temperature and energy density in the equation of state at the late hadronic stage, but cannot by themselves determine whether a phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma occurs.

## Method
Experimental single-particle momentum spectra from 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions measured by the NA44 and NA49 collaborations were fitted using the relativistic hydrodynamic simulation code HYLANDER-C with four different equations of state (EOS-I, EOS-II, EOS-III, EOS-Ib). Cooper-Frye freeze-out was applied, and Bose-Einstein correlation functions for identical pion pairs were calculated with full simulation of the NA44 detector acceptance.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperatures used: T_f = 139 MeV (for all four EOS) and T_f = 116 MeV (for EOS-I only)
- Critical temperature for EOS-I: T_c = 200 MeV with critical energy density epsilon_c = 3.2 GeV/fm^3
- Critical temperature for EOS-II: T_c = 160 MeV with critical energy density epsilon_c ≈ 1.5 GeV/fm^3
- Freeze-out energy densities at T_f = 139 MeV: epsilon_f = 0.292 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-I), 0.126 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-II), 0.130 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-III)
- Freeze-out energy density for EOS-Ib at T_f = 139 MeV and EOS-I at T_f = 116 MeV: epsilon_f = 0.127 GeV/fm^3
- Errors in inverse widths and overall strengths of correlation functions can reach 25% if detector acceptance is not fully simulated

## Context
This work extends the few prior studies addressing the role of the equation of state in intensity interferometry, notably Rischke et al. (1996). It uses the same hydrodynamic framework as Schlei et al. (1997, 1998) but adds Bose-Einstein correlation analysis to the previously published single-particle spectra fits. The finding that BEC alone cannot discriminate a QGP phase transition contradicts earlier hopes that these correlations would serve as a direct QGP signature, and instead points to the need for combined analysis of single-particle spectra and correlation functions.
