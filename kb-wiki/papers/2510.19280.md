# The universal size compression effect of nucleon pair in finite nuclei

> We systematically investigate the size evolution of the di-neutron (2n), di-proton (2p), and deuteron (d) in 6He, 14Be, 17B, 6Be, 17Ne, and 6Li using microscopic calculations. Remarkably, all nucleon pairs exhibit a universal size compression at the nuclear surface, regardless of their species and binding energies. These features correspond to the BCS- and BEC-like nucleon pairs, which recent experimental techniques can further investigate.

## Key claim
All three types of nucleon pairs (di-neutron, di-proton, and deuteron) exhibit a universal size compression at the nuclear surface, regardless of their species and binding energies; the effect is predicted for di-protons and deuterons where it has not yet been measured experimentally.

## Method
The authors used the beta-constrained antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (beta-AMD) method to obtain wave functions of the mother and daughter nuclei, then calculated the reduced width amplitude (RWA) as a function of both the radius between the residue nucleus and the nucleon pair and the internal size of the pair. From the two-dimensional RWA, they extracted the root-mean-square distance of the di-nucleon at each radial position, using the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with a reduced spin-orbit strength for the lightest systems.

## Key numbers
- Minimum RMS distance of the di-neutron: 1.8 fm for 6He, 1.4 fm for 14Be, and 1.5 fm for 17B, all occurring at approximately 3 fm radius.
- Minimum RMS distance of the di-proton: 1.9 fm for 6Be and 1.8 fm for 17Ne.
- Final RMS distance of the deuteron in 6Li after compression: 2.94 fm, consistent with the RMS radius of an individual 2H calculated with the same framework.
- Binding energies reproduced: 6He calculated at -31.70 MeV (experiment -29.27 MeV), 14Be at -69.38 MeV (experiment -69.92 MeV), 17B at -89.61 MeV (experiment -89.59 MeV), 6Be at -28.95 MeV (experiment -26.92 MeV), 17Ne at -115.23 MeV (experiment -112.89 MeV), 6Li at -36.95 MeV (experiment -31.99 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the surface localization effect previously established for di-neutrons in Borromean nuclei such as 11Li by Hagino (2007), Kubota (2020), and Corsi (2023) to di-protons and deuterons, demonstrating universality across pair species. The results predict that the same experimental technique used for di-neutron measurements via (p,pn) knockout reactions can confirm the size compression of di-protons and deuterons, and suggest that the mechanism may be related to the formation of alpha clusters in nuclei as discussed by Y. Wang (2024).
