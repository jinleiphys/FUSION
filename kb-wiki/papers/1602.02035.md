# PDFs from nucleons to nuclei

> I review recent progress in the extraction of unpolarized parton distributions in the proton and in nuclei from a unified point of view that highlights how the interplay between high energy particle physics and lower energy nuclear physics can be of mutual benefit to either field. Areas of overlap range from the search for physics beyond the standard model at the LHC, to the study of the non perturbative structure of nucleons and the emergence of nuclei from quark and gluon degrees of freedom, to the interaction of colored probes in a cold nuclear medium.

## Key claim

Global QCD fits can serve as a tool to connect high-energy particle physics with lower-energy hadronic and nuclear physics, but the paper emphasizes that this interplay is most fruitful when nuclear corrections (such as binding, Fermi motion, and offshellness in deuteron targets) are systematically included and controlled, rather than avoided by kinematic cuts.

## Method

The paper reviews recent global QCD fits of unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the proton and for nuclei, focusing on the CTEQ-JLab (CJ) collaboration's approach. The CJ fits systematically include target mass corrections (proportional to \(M^2/Q^2\)), higher-twist corrections (proportional to \(\Lambda^2/Q^2\)), and nuclear effects in deuteron targets using a smearing approach with a nuclear spectral function. The fits utilize data from deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) on proton and deuteron targets, as well as \(W\) and \(Z\) boson production data from the Tevatron and LHC, to constrain the \(d/u\) quark ratio at large \(x\) and to study nuclear dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The CJ fits constrain the \(d/u\) quark ratio up to \(x \approx 0.85\), and the extrapolation indicates \(d/u(x=1) \lesssim 0.3\), excluding several non-perturbative model predictions.
- The ratio of strange to light sea quarks extracted from LHC \(W + c\) data is \(k = 2s/(\bar u + \bar d) \approx 1\), compared to \(k \approx 0.4\) from fits using only dimuon data from neutrino-nucleus scattering.
- Using a \(W^2 > 3\) GeV\(^2\) cut on DIS data (instead of \(W^2 = 12.5\) GeV\(^2\)) reduces the relative uncertainty in the extracted \(u\) and \(d\) quark PDFs at large \(x\), as shown in the CJ12 fit.
- The \(F_2(D)/F_2(p+n)\) ratio extracted in the CJ12 and MMHT14 fits agrees in size and shape.

## Context

This work extends the earlier large-\(x\) PDF studies by Alekhin et al. (2000, 2012) and Martin et al. (2003), and builds on the CTEQ-JLab collaboration's series of fits (CJ12, CJ15). The paper highlights that the CJ approach, which includes nuclear corrections and a flexible \(d\)-quark parametrization, has been partially confirmed by recent CT14, NNPDF3.0, and MMHT14 fits, though those still use large invariant mass cuts that limit their reach in \(x\). The review enables future combined fits of proton and nuclear data to improve precision in calculations for beyond-the-standard-model searches at the LHC, and to study the EMC effect, short-range correlations in nuclei, and charm quark propagation in cold nuclear matter.
