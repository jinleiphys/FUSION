# Jet tomography in heavy-ion collisions -- Challenges, Results, and Open Problems

> Over the past 30 years, jet observables have proven to provide important information about the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions. I review the challenges, results, and open problems of jet physics in heavy-ion collisions, discussing the main ideas as well as some most recent results focussing on two major jet observables, the nuclear modification factor and the high-pT elliptic flow.

## Key claim

Jet tomography in heavy-ion collisions is not as straightforward as medical X-ray tomography because there is no controlled flow of penetrating particles, no established dynamical theory of energy loss, and a non-moving, non-fluctuating background medium is absent; despite these challenges, the paper argues that a pQCD-based scenario with a running jet-medium coupling describes the measured nuclear modification factor and high-pT elliptic flow within uncertainties, but a conformal AdS/CFT scenario is ruled out by the rapid rise of the measured RAA(pT).

## Method

The paper reviews the main ideas and recent results for two major jet observables, the nuclear modification factor (RAA) and the high-pT elliptic flow (v2), in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It describes the BBMG model, a generic jet-energy loss model based on an ansatz dE/dτ = -κ E^a τ^z T^(2+z-a) with fluctuations and background flow fields, which interpolates between pQCD-based (linear path-length dependence, z=1) and AdS/CFT-inspired (squared path-length dependence, z=2) energy loss algorithms. The model is coupled to state-of-the-art hydrodynamic and parton cascade background media, and results are cross-checked with the CUJET model based on pQCD calculations up to 10 orders in opacity.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification factor for pions in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is RAA ∼ 0.2, significantly below 1.
- Pre-LHC runs at RHIC indicated that the measured RAA and high-pT v2 can only be described simultaneously if a squared path-length dependence, dE/dx ∼ τ^2, is considered.
- At LHC energies, the RAA showed an unexpected similarity to RHIC measurements in the region 10 < pT < 20 GeV, indicating a surprising QGP-transparency.
- The rapid rise of the nuclear modification factor at LHC energies rules out any model with dE/dx ∼ E^(a>1/3); a=1/3 is the lower bound of the falling-string scenario, while a=0 is referred to as a pQCD scenario.
- A pQCD-based scenario with parameters a=0, z=1, c=3 describes the measured RAA and high-pT v2 within uncertainties if a running jet-medium coupling is considered.
- The high-pT v2 from various models is up to a factor of 2 too small compared to data.
- The reference point chosen for all scenarios in the SHEE study is RAA(pT=10 GeV)=0.185.
- The jet-medium coupling κ(E^2,T) includes non-perturbative effects around the phase transition at Tc ∼ 0.16 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on jet quenching and jet tomography that began with the start of RHIC in 2000, where studies of azimuthal distributions in Au+Au collisions showed jet suppression compared to p+p or d+Au collisions (Adams 2003, Adare 2008). The paper discusses how pre-LHC runs at RHIC (Adare 2010) indicated a squared path-length dependence pointing to AdS/CFT-like energy loss, but first LHC data ruled out conformal AdS/CFT and favored pQCD with a running coupling. The BBMG model (Betz 2011, Betz 2012, Betz 2014) is used to explore jet physics, and the SHEE approach (Noronha-Hostler 2016) couples hydrodynamic v-USPhydro code to BBMG to study the impact of background fluctuations on high-pT v2. The paper concludes that a key open problem is finding a new observable to break the degeneracy between pQCD and AdS/CFT scenarios.
