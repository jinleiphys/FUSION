# Perturbative Calculation of Excluded Volume Effect for Nuclear Matter in a Relativistic Mean-field Approximation

> Considering the finite volume of nucleons, a Lagrangian density is given. The first order self-energy of the nucleon and the equation of state of nuclear matter are calculated in the framework of relativistic mean-field approximation. Our results indicate the effects of the volume of nucleons are not negligible.

## Key claim

The finite volume of nucleons (excluded volume effect) produces a non-negligible repulsive contribution to the equation of state of nuclear matter, but the perturbative treatment used in this work is only reliable up to about twice saturation density, beyond which the model's validity is uncertain.

## Method

The authors incorporate the excluded volume effect into a relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian density by replacing the nucleon field operator with a rescaled field that accounts for the volume occupied by nucleons, using a nucleon radius of 0.63 fm. They treat the excluded volume term as a perturbation (since the nucleon volume occupies only about 10% of space at saturation density) and compute the first-order self-energy of the nucleon, then solve self-consistently for the effective mass, scalar density, and vector density to obtain the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon radius taken as 0.63 fm, corresponding to nucleon volume occupying about 10% of space at saturation density
- Saturation density for the ITP model: ρ₀ = 0.145 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = 16.727 MeV
- Compression modulus at saturation: K = 288.65 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation: m*/m = 0.695
- Coupling constants for the ITP model: g_σ = 12.30, g_ω = 6.45, g₂ = -17.30 fm⁻¹, g₃ = 28.00

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to include finite nucleon volume effects in nuclear matter, such as the geometric approach of Zhang (1995) and Zhang and Zhang (1995), and the QRZ parameter set of Sun et al. (2001). The authors compare their results with the standard NL3 parameter set of Lalazissis, Koenig, and Ring (1997), which treats nucleons as point-like particles. The perturbative Lagrangian approach enables a systematic inclusion of excluded volume effects, but the authors caution that the model is not suitable for densities much above 2ρ₀, leaving open the question of how to treat the excluded volume effect at higher densities.
