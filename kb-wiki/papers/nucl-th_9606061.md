# Microscopic description of light unstable nuclei with the stochastic variational method

> The structure of the light proton and neutron rich nuclei is studied in a microscopic multicluster model using the stochastic variational method. This approach enables us to describe the weakly bound nature of these nuclei in a consistent way. Applications for various nuclei $^{6-9}$Li, $^7$Be, $^8$B, $^9$C, $^{9-10}$Be, $^{9-10}$B presented. The paper discusses the relation of this model to other models as well as the possible extension for p and sd shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The stochastic variational method (SVM) applied to a microscopic multicluster model can describe the weakly bound nature of light unstable nuclei consistently, but the accuracy depends on reproducing separation energies as small as 100 keV and on properly treating the asymptotic behavior of the wave function.

## Method

The authors use a microscopic multicluster model with relative (Jacobi) coordinates and correlated Gaussian basis functions, where the spatial part of each basis function is a Gaussian multiplied by spherical harmonics for the angular part. The nonlinear parameters of the basis functions are selected stochastically by random sampling from a preset domain, accepting a new basis element only if the energy gain exceeds a preset threshold, and this process is repeated until convergence. The effective nucleon-nucleon interaction used is the Minnesota potential (central and spin-orbit parts), with exact treatment of Coulomb interaction between protons and full antisymmetrization.

## Key numbers

- The ground state energy of $^9$Be is calculated as -1.431 MeV (experimental: -1.5735 MeV), with matter radius 2.50 fm, proton radius 2.39 fm (experimental: 2.37 ± 0.01 fm), and neutron radius 2.58 fm.
- The quadrupole moment of $^9$Be is calculated as 5.13 e fm² (experimental: 5.3 ± 0.3 e fm²), and the magnetic moment is -1.169 μ_N (experimental: -1.1778 ± 0.0009 μ_N).
- The $B(E2; 5/2^- → 3/2^-)$ transition in $^9$Be is calculated as 22.0 W.u. (experimental: 24.4 ± 1.8 W.u.), and $B(M1; 5/2^- → 3/2^-)$ is 0.229 W.u. (experimental: 0.30 ± 0.03 W.u.).
- The $B(E1; 1/2^+ → 3/2^-)$ transition in $^9$Be is calculated as 0.24 W.u. (experimental: 0.22 ± 0.09 W.u.).
- The quadrupole moment of $^9$C is predicted to be -5.04 e fm², and of $^7$Be to be -6.11 e fm².
- The neutron skin thickness is about 0.4 fm in $^8$Li and $^9$Li, while the proton skin thickness is 0.5 fm in $^8$B and $^9$C, compared to 0.8 fm in $^6$He and $^8$He.
- The matter size of the 0⁺ state of $^6$Li at 3.563 MeV excitation energy is calculated to be 2.73 fm, which is 0.2 fm larger than the $^6$He size.
- For the $^9$B spectrum, a missing 1/2⁻ state is predicted at 2.43 MeV excitation energy with a width of 1.0 MeV (experimental location at 2.83 MeV from a $^9$Be(p,n) reaction).
- The log$ft$ value for the β decay of $^9$Li to the $^9$Be ground state is calculated as 5.60 (experimental: about 5.31).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic multicluster approach previously applied to $^{6-8}$He (Varga, Suzuki, and Ogawa, earlier papers) to a wider range of light unstable nuclei including $^{6-9}$Li, $^7$Be, $^8$B, $^9$C, $^{9-10}$Be, and $^{9-10}$B. The method contrasts with phenomenological three-body models that assume a stable core (e.g., $^9$Li+n+n for $^{11}$Li) and sacrifice exact antisymmetrization, and with shell model approaches that struggle with the extended asymptotic behavior of halo nuclei. The results enable future studies of $^{10}$Be, $^{11}$Be, and $^{11}$Li, and suggest that the 3.563 MeV 0⁺ state of $^6$Li has a halo-like structure that may explain anomalies in inelastic proton and pion scattering data.
