# Three-loop HTLpt thermodynamics at finite temperature and chemical potential

> We calculate the three-loop thermodynamic potential of QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential(s) using the hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) reorganization of finite temperature and density QCD. The resulting analytic thermodynamic potential allows us to compute the pressure, energy density, and entropy density of the quark-gluon plasma. Using these we calculate the trace anomaly, speed of sound, and second-, fourth-, and sixth-order quark number susceptibilities. For all observables considered we find good agreement between our three-loop HTLpt calculations and available lattice data for temperatures above approximately 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) thermodynamic potential for QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential yields good agreement with available lattice data for all observables considered, but only for temperatures above approximately 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the three-loop thermodynamic potential of QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential using the hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) reorganization, which systematically shifts the perturbative expansion from an ideal gas of massless particles to a gas of massive quasiparticles. They expanded the resulting sum-integrals analytically in powers of m_D/T and m_q/T, keeping terms through O(g^5), and renormalized the result using vacuum, Debye mass, fermion mass, and coupling constant counterterms. From the analytic thermodynamic potential, they computed the pressure, energy density, entropy density, trace anomaly, speed of sound, and second-, fourth-, and sixth-order quark number susceptibilities.

## Key numbers

* The three-loop HTLpt calculations show good agreement with lattice data for temperatures above approximately 300 MeV.
* The thermodynamic potential is expressed in terms of dimensionless variables hat_m_D = m_D/(2 pi T) and hat_mu = mu/(2 pi T).
* The pure-glue NNLO thermodynamic potential contains terms proportional to hat_m_D^3, with coefficient -15/4 in the leading term.
* The quark contribution includes terms proportional to (1 + 12 hat_mu^2) and (1 + 120/7 hat_mu^2 + 240/7 hat_mu^4) for equal chemical potentials.
* The result reduces to the correct O(g^5) perturbative result when expanded in a strict power series in g.

## Context

This work extends previous HTLpt calculations at three loops from zero chemical potential (3loopqcd1, 3loopqcd2, 3loopqcd3) to finite chemical potential, and generalizes the equal-chemical-potential result first presented in Haque et al. (2013) to flavor-dependent chemical potentials. The HTLpt approach provides an alternative to lattice QCD, which suffers from the sign problem at finite baryon chemical potential, and improves upon the poor convergence of strict weak-coupling perturbation theory (Vuorinen 2003, 2004) by treating soft momenta more carefully. The results enable phenomenological studies of the quark-gluon plasma at finite density relevant for beam energy scans at RHIC and future FAIR experiments.
