# Effect of mesonic off-shell correlations in the PNJL equation of state

> We study the meson contribution to the equation of state of the 2-flavor PNJL model, including the full momentum dependence of the meson polarization loops. Within the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach, we demonstrate that the contribution from the quark-antiquark continuum excitations in the spacelike region $ω^2 - q^2 < 0$, i.e. the Landau damping, leads to an increase of the pressure for temperatures $\gtrsim 0.8\,T_c^χ$ and a significant meson momentum cut-off dependence in the mesonic pressure and the QCD trace anomaly. We investigate the dependence of the results on the choice of the Polyakov-loop potential parameter $T_0$. From the dependence of the mesonic pressure on the current quark mass, by means of the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, we evaluate the contribution of the pion quasiparticle gas and Landau damping to the chiral condensate.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the full momentum dependence of meson polarization loops, specifically the Landau damping (LD) region with spacelike momenta (omega^2 - q^2 < 0), leads to a significant increase in the pressure for temperatures above approximately 0.8 times the chiral pseudocritical temperature, contrary to the common practice of excluding this region in similar models.

## Method

The authors employed the 2-flavor Polyakov-loop improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model at finite temperature and zero baryon chemical potential, using the mean-field approximation with mesonic fluctuations treated via the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach. They calculated the meson pressure from quark-antiquark scattering phase shifts in the pion and sigma channels, including the full frequency and momentum dependence of the polarization operators, and evaluated the contribution from the Landau damping region separately from the quasiparticle pole contribution.

## Key numbers

* The Landau damping contribution to the pressure becomes significant for temperatures greater than approximately 0.8 times the chiral pseudocritical temperature (T_c^chi).
* Varying the threshold in the LD region between 1 and 2 times the quark 3-momentum cutoff Lambda (where Lambda regularizes the PNJL vacuum pressure) produced a sizeable 4-fold enhancement of the LD pressure for temperatures above T_c^chi.
* The inclusion of the LD contribution can lower the chiral pseudocritical temperature T_c^chi by several MeV, depending on the choice of the LD cutoff parameter Lambda^LD.
* The analytic expression for the LD polarization loop in the non-relativistic limit contains a temperature exponent exp(-sqrt((2m)^2 + q^2) / (2T)), with the large-momentum tail regulated by twice the medium temperature.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model studies of Ratti et al. (2005) and Blaschke et al. (2016) by including the previously neglected Landau damping contribution to the mesonic pressure, which was excluded in similar models by Yamazaki et al. (2013) and Torres-Rincon et al. (2017). The results may explain the kinks in the pressure seen in the preprint version of Roessner et al. (2007) that were absent in the published version where only the quasipole contribution was retained. The findings are expected to apply beyond mean-field approaches to include 1/N_c corrections to the quark propagator, and generalization to N_f = 2 + 1 with additional meson degrees of freedom is anticipated.
