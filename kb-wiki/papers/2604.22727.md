# Ab initio short-range nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We present converged ab initio calculations of short-range neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay nuclear matrix elements for the key experimental isotopes $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{130}$Te and $^{136}$Xe. Starting from different nuclear forces derived from chiral effective field theory, we apply the in-medium similarity renormalization group to obtain an effective valence-space Hamiltonian along with consistently transformed $0νββ$-decay operators. We then obtain a range of values for the matrix elements that is consistent with, but generally smaller than, those from phenomenology. Finally, we combine our results with current limits from $0νββ$-decay searches to obtain constraints for the sterile-neutrino mixing-mass parameter space when considering the inclusion of a fourth sterile neutrino.

## Key claim

The paper presents converged ab initio calculations of short-range neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for four key isotopes, finding values that are consistent with but generally smaller than phenomenological estimates, with the caveat that the results depend on the choice of operator renormalization scheme and regulator cutoff, with differences between schemes reaching up to 50%.

## Method

The authors employ the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) starting from chiral effective field theory nuclear forces (N3LOLNL and DeltaGO interactions) to obtain effective valence-space Hamiltonians and consistently transformed decay operators. They compute six short-range nuclear matrix elements (Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and Tensor transitions with various parity components) for 76Ge, 82Se, 130Te, and 136Xe using harmonic-oscillator basis truncations, then diagonalize the Hamiltonian with the KSHELL code. The resulting matrix elements are combined with experimental likelihoods from LEGEND-200, CUORE, EXO-200, and KamLAND-Zen to derive Bayesian constraints on sterile-neutrino mixing parameters.

## Key numbers

- Short-range NMEs for 76Ge, 82Se, 130Te, and 136Xe are converged by emax=12 with model-space truncation error smaller than the less than 15% uncertainty from the choice of reference state (parent versus daughter nucleus)
- Difference between the "SRG/Loc. reg." and "Bare/Dipole" operator renormalization schemes can be up to 50% for short-range NMEs
- Ab initio NMEs reduce the overall spread by a factor of 4 compared to nuclear models when using a conservative 450-550 MeV cutoff range for transition operators
- For the N3LOLNL interaction, calculations use a harmonic-oscillator frequency of 15 MeV; for the DeltaGO interaction, 12 MeV
- The SRG evolution scale is lambda=2.0 fm-1 for the N3LOLNL interaction

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements from light systems and 48Ca (Yao 2020, Belley 2021, Novario 2021) and the first rigorous uncertainty analysis for 76Ge (Belley 2024) to short-range contributions in four key experimental isotopes. The results enable constraints on sterile-neutrino mixing-mass parameter space that are competitive with collider experiments (ATLAS 2025, CMS 2018-2024) and PMNS unitarity bounds (Blennow 2023), while the dominant uncertainty remains from unknown low-energy constants such as g1piN, which is taken to span plus or minus 10-1/2 to plus or minus 101/2.
