# Contour integral method for thermal and quantal fluctuations

> The partition function by means of the static path approximation (SPA) plus the random-phase approximation (RPA) treatment can be written as a contour integral form without solving the RPA equations for a separable interaction. This method is an efficient way to evaluate numerically the partition function for realistic calculations. As an illustration, we adopt the pairing model at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The partition function in the static path approximation (SPA) plus random-phase approximation (RPA) treatment can be written as a contour integral form without solving the RPA equations for a separable interaction, but the method breaks down at very low temperature when the condition β|Ω| < 2π is violated and the formalism may include contributions from unstable mean-fields.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian with a separable multipole-multipole interaction and use the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to write the partition function as a functional integral over auxiliary fields. They separate the fields into static and fluctuating parts, perform the Gaussian integral over the fluctuation coefficients, and obtain the RPA correction factor C_RPA. They then introduce a meromorphic function f(z) = det(1 - χR(iz)) whose zeros are the RPA frequencies Ω_ν and poles are the particle-hole energies ε_kl, and apply Cauchy's theorem to express the thermal RPA energy E_RPA as a closed contour integral that avoids solving the RPA eigenvalue equations directly. The method is illustrated for a monopole pairing Hamiltonian with number parity projection, using single-particle energies from a deformed Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy from exact Richardson solution: E_gs = -74.48 MeV
- SPA+CIR value at β = 10 MeV^{-1}: E = -75.24 MeV
- Pairing force strength: G = 20/A MeV
- Deformation parameter: δ = 0.23 (from experimental B(E2) value of ¹⁸⁴W)
- 50 doubly degenerate single-particle levels taken outside ¹³²Sn core
- SPA+RPA and SPA+CIR results deviate from SPA above T = 0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the contour integral representation for the RPA correlation energy at zero temperature, previously developed by Donau (2004) and Shimizu (2005), to finite temperature systems. The method addresses the computational difficulty of evaluating the RPA partition function for deformed or heavy nuclei, where solving the full set of RPA equations at each temperature is time-consuming. The approach enables efficient numerical calculation of thermal properties such as heat capacity and level density, and the authors demonstrate that the S-shape heat capacity behavior observed experimentally in ¹⁶²Dy, ¹⁶⁶Er, and ¹⁷²Yb (Schiller 2001, Melby 2001) can be reproduced. The method is applicable to more realistic interactions such as pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions.
