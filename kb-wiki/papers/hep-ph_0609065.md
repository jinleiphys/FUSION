# Aspects of Strangeness -1 Meson-Baryon Scattering

> We consider meson-baryon interactions in S-wave with strangeness -1. This is a sector populated by plenty of resonances interacting in several two-body coupled channels. We consider a large set of experimental data, where the recent experiments are remarkably accurate. This requires a sound theoretical description to account for all the data and we employ Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory up to and including O(p^2). The spectroscopy of our solutions is studied within this approach, discussing the rise from the pole content of two Λ(1405) resonances and of the Λ(1670), Λ(1800), Σ(1480), Σ(1620) and Σ(1750). We finally argue about our preferred fit.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (UCHPT) up to and including O(p^2) can simultaneously describe a large set of S-wave strangeness -1 meson-baryon scattering data and the DEAR measurement of kaonic hydrogen, but only for a specific class of fits (A-type); the alternative B-type fits reproduce the scattering data but disagree with the DEAR kaonic hydrogen shift and width.

## Method

The authors employ Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (UCHPT) for S-wave meson-baryon scattering with strangeness -1, constructing the interaction kernel K from chiral perturbation theory up to O(p^2) and inserting it into the coupled-channel unitarization formula T = [1 + K g]^{-1} K. They fit the resulting amplitudes to 97 data points including K^-p elastic and charge-exchange cross sections, hyperon production cross sections, threshold ratios, and the DEAR kaonic hydrogen measurement, while also constraining the fit by computing pion-nucleon observables at O(p^2) in baryon SU(3) CHPT.

## Key numbers

- DEAR measurement of kaonic hydrogen 1s level: shift ΔE = 193 ± 37 (stat) ± 6 (syst) eV, width Γ = 249 ± 111 (stat) ± 39 (syst) eV
- Threshold ratios: γ = 2.36 ± 0.04, R_c = 0.664 ± 0.011, R_n = 0.189 ± 0.015
- A-type fit K^-p scattering length: a_{K^-p} = (-0.49 + i 0.44) fm for σ_{πN} = 20 MeV
- B-type fit K^-p scattering length: a_{K^-p} = (-1.01 + i 1.03) fm for σ_{πN} = 20 MeV
- A-type fit I=0 poles: Λ(1405) at 1414 - i 23 MeV (2RS), Λ(1670) at 1676 - i 10 MeV (3RS), Λ(1800) at 1825 - i 49 MeV (5RS)
- A-type fit I=1 poles: Σ(1750) at 1720 - i 18 MeV (4RS), broad structure around 1.6 GeV corresponding to Σ(1620)
- Isospin violation in kaonic hydrogen shift: 14% for A-type fits, 30% for B-type fits

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous UCHPT studies (Oller et al., 2005; Oller et al., 2006) by including new precise data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration for K^-p → ηΛ and π^0π^0Σ^0 reactions, which could not be reproduced by earlier fits. The results address the tension between the DEAR kaonic hydrogen measurement and K^-p scattering data previously noted by other groups (Borasoy et al., 2005), showing that fits compatible with both exist. The spectroscopy results confirm the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) resonance and provide pole positions for the Λ(1670), Λ(1800), Σ(1480), Σ(1620), and Σ(1750) resonances, with the A-type fits showing better agreement with PDG values than the B-type fits.
