# Diquarks, Pentaquarks and Dibaryons

> We explore the connection between pentaquarks and dibaryons composed of three diquarks in the framework of the diquark model. With the available experimental data on H dibaryon, we estimate the Pauli blocking and annihilation effects and constrain the $P=-$ pentaquark $SU(3)_F$ singlet mass. Using the $Θ^+$ pentaquark mass, we estimate $P=-$ dibaryon mass.

## Key claim

The paper explores the connection between pentaquarks and dibaryons within the diquark model, arguing that the Pauli blocking and annihilation effects are essential to reconcile the low mass of the Θ⁺ pentaquark with the higher mass constraints on the H dibaryon; the author estimates that a negative-parity pentaquark SU(3)ₓ singlet is likely low-lying, while a negative-parity dibaryon is probably 40–80 MeV above the ΛΛ threshold.

## Method

The author uses the diquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek, where two scalar diquarks combine with an antiquark to form pentaquarks, and three scalar diquarks form dibaryons. Mass formulas are constructed including terms for diquark masses, orbital excitation energy (E_L), Pauli blocking energies (E_pb^(L=0) and E_pb^(L=1)), and annihilation energies (E_ann and E'_ann). The Pauli blocking energy for S-wave diquarks is estimated from experimental constraints on the H dibaryon mass, and the annihilation energies are taken from literature estimates to derive masses for negative-parity pentaquark and dibaryon states.

## Key numbers

- H dibaryon lower bound: M_H ≥ 2224 MeV, from doubly Λ hyper-nuclei experiments (BNL, KEK).
- Pauli blocking energy for S-wave diquarks: E_pb^(L=0) ≈ 215 MeV, derived from the H dibaryon mass constraint.
- Orbital excitation energy: E_L ≈ 240 MeV.
- Annihilation energy estimates: E_ann ≈ (50–100) MeV, E'_ann ≈ (10–30) MeV.
- Pauli blocking energy for P-wave diquarks: E_pb^(L=1) ≈ (20–60) MeV, from the Θ⁺ mass formula.
- Negative-parity pentaquark SU(3)ₓ singlet mass: M_1 = (1402–1542) MeV.
- Negative-parity dibaryon mass (Λ₆ type): M_Λ₆ = (2270–2310) MeV, which is (40–80) MeV above the ΛΛ and ΞN thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the diquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) for pentaquarks to dibaryons composed of three diquarks, building on Jaffe's original H dibaryon proposal (1977). The author uses experimental constraints on the H dibaryon from doubly Λ hyper-nuclei experiments (BNL, KEK) to estimate Pauli blocking effects, and incorporates annihilation estimates from Bicudo (2003) and Zhu, Zhang, and Yuan (2004). The results suggest a low-lying negative-parity pentaquark singlet that could be searched for in future experiments, and a negative-parity dibaryon state that may be observable at RHIC.
