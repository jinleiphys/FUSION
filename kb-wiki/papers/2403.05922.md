# Skewed generalized parton distributions of proton from basis light-front quantization

> We obtain all the leading-twist quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) inside the proton at nonzero skewness within the basis light-front quantization framework. We employ the light-front wave functions of the proton from a light-front quantized Hamiltonian in the valence Fock sector consisting of a three-dimensional confinement potential and a one-gluon exchange interaction with fixed coupling. We find that the qualitative behaviors of our GPDs are similar to those of other theoretical calculations. We further examine the GPDs within the boost-invariant longitudinal coordinate, $σ=\frac{1}{2} b^- P^+$, which is identified as the Fourier conjugate of the skewness. The GPDs in the $σ$-space show diffraction patterns, which are akin to the diffractive scattering of a wave in optics.

## Key claim

The authors obtain all leading-twist quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton at nonzero skewness using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ), but only within the valence Fock sector and the DGLAP domain (ξ < x < 1), meaning the results do not include contributions from quark-antiquark pair creation or annihilation that would appear in the ERBL region (−ξ < x < ξ).

## Method

The authors solve a light-front effective Hamiltonian for the proton in the valence three-quark Fock space, which includes a three-dimensional confinement potential and a one-gluon exchange interaction with fixed coupling. The resulting light-front wave functions (LFWFs), expanded in a basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator functions for transverse degrees of freedom and discretized plane waves for longitudinal momentum, are used to compute the overlap integrals that define all eight leading-twist GPDs (chiral-even H, E, H̃, Ẽ and chiral-odd H_T, E_T, H̃_T, Ẽ_T) at nonzero skewness. The GPDs are then Fourier-transformed with respect to the skewness variable ξ to obtain distributions in the boost-invariant longitudinal coordinate σ = ½ b⁻ P⁺.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed with basis truncation parameters N_max = 10 and K = 16.5, corresponding to a low-resolution scale μ₀² ≈ 0.195 GeV²
- GPDs shown at fixed momentum transfer t = −0.5 GeV² for the chiral-even and chiral-odd distributions as functions of x and ξ
- GPDs in longitudinal position space shown at fixed t = −1.0 GeV² for x = {0.2, 0.4, 0.6}, with ξ_max ≈ 0.47 for t = −1.0 GeV²
- The upper integration limit ξ_f for the Fourier transform is ξ_f = min{x, ξ_max}, giving ξ_f = 0.2 for x = 0.2, ξ_f = 0.4 for x = 0.4, and ξ_f = 0.47 for x = 0.6

## Context

This work extends previous BLFQ studies of proton GPDs at zero skewness (Kaur 2023) to nonzero skewness, which is essential because experimental probes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering always access ξ ≠ 0. The results show qualitative agreement with other theoretical approaches including the bag model (Ji 1997), constituent quark model (Boffi 2002, 2003), light-front quark-diquark model (Mondal 2015, 2017), and other phenomenological models (Freese 2020). The diffraction patterns observed in the longitudinal position space are similar to those previously seen in the DVCS amplitude (Brodsky 2006) and in GPDs from other models (Chakrabarti 2008, Manohar 2010, Kumar 2015). This work enables future comparisons with experimental data from the Electron-Ion Collider and the 12 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Lab.
