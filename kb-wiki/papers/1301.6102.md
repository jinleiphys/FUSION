# Medium-Induced QCD Cascade: Democratic Branching and Wave Turbulence

> We study the average properties of the gluon cascade generated by an energetic parton propagating through a quark-gluon plasma. We focus on the soft, medium-induced emissions which control the energy transport at large angles with respect to the leading parton. We show that the effect of multiple branchings is important. In contrast with what happens in a usual QCD cascade in vacuum, medium-induced branchings are quasidemocratic, with offspring gluons carrying sizable fractions of the energy of their parent gluon. This results in an efficient mechanism for the transport of energy toward the medium, which is akin to wave turbulence with a scaling spectrum $\sim 1/\sqrtω$. We argue that the turbulent flow may be responsible for the excess energy carried by very soft quanta, as revealed by the analysis of the dijet asymmetry observed in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Medium-induced QCD branchings are quasidemocratic (offspring gluons carry sizable fractions of parent energy), unlike vacuum cascades, and this leads to a turbulent energy flow toward the medium with a scaling spectrum ∼ 1/√ω that may explain the excess of very soft quanta seen in LHC dijet asymmetry data.

## Method

The authors study the average gluon spectrum D(x,τ) generated by an energetic parton traversing a quark-gluon plasma, using a probabilistic branching process where successive medium-induced gluon emissions are treated as independent. They derive an evolution equation for D(x,τ) based on the BDMPSZ elementary branching rate, solve it analytically for a simplified kernel via Laplace transform, and analyze the energy flow using both perturbative and nonperturbative methods.

## Key numbers

- The branching time scale is τ_br = √[z(1-z)ω/^q_eff], with ^q_eff ≡ ^q [1-z(1-z)] and ^q the jet quenching parameter.
- The maximum energy for a single gluon is ω_c = ^q L²/2; multiple branchings become important when zω ≲ ω_s ≡ ᾱ² ω_c, where ᾱ ≡ α_s N_c/π.
- For the simplified kernel, the exact solution is D₀(x,τ) = (τ/√x (1-x)^{3/2}) exp[-π τ² (1-x)].
- The total energy stored in the spectrum for the simplified kernel is ℰ₀(τ) = exp(-πτ²).
- The turbulent flow energy in physical units is ℰ_flow = υ ᾱ² ω_c, with υ = 4.96 for the full kernel.
- Using ω_c = 40 GeV (^q = 1 GeV²/fm, L ≈ 4 fm) and ᾱ² ≈ 0.1, ℰ_flow ≈ 20 GeV.
- CMS data show the energy imbalance is compensated by an excess of about 25 GeV, with about 80% carried by quanta with p_T ≤ 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPSZ mechanism for medium-induced radiation (Baier et al., 1997; Zakharov, 1997) by incorporating multiple branchings, building on the independent-emission approximation established by Blaizot et al. (2012). It contrasts with standard DGLAP vacuum cascades, where branchings are asymmetric and energy remains in the spectrum. The results provide a theoretical basis for the dijet asymmetry observed by ATLAS (Aad et al., 2010) and CMS (Chatrchyan et al., 2011, 2012), and enable quantitative predictions for the angular distribution of soft energy in jet quenching phenomenology.
