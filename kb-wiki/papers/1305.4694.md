# Glauber gluons in pion-induced Drell-Yan processes

> We point out that the existence of Glauber gluons in the $k_T$ factorization theorem can account for the violation of the Lam-Tung relation, namely, the anomalous lepton angular distribution, observed in pion-induced Drell-Yan processes. Radiations, that balance the lepton-pair transverse momentum, cause the responsible spin-transverse-momentum correlation in the Glauber-gluon background. It is argued that the Glauber effect is significant in the pion due to its unique role of being a Nambu-Goldstone boson and a $q\bar q$ bound state simultaneously. This mechanism is compared to other resolutions in the literature by means of vacuum effects and Boer-Mulders functions. We propose to discriminate the above resolutions by measuring the $p\bar p$ Drell-Yan process at GSI and J-PARC.

# Glauber gluons in pion-induced Drell-Yan processes

## Key claim

The violation of the Lam-Tung relation observed in pion-induced Drell-Yan processes can be explained by Glauber gluons in the $k_T$ factorization theorem, but only at low lepton-pair transverse momentum $q_T$ and low invariant mass $Q$, where neither scale is large enough to cause power suppression of the Glauber divergence; the effect is argued to be significant specifically for pions due to their dual nature as Nambu-Goldstone bosons and $q\bar q$ bound states.

## Method

The authors compute the parton-level hard kernels for the process $\bar q(p_1)+q(p_2)\to \ell^-\ell^+ + g(k_3)$ in the Collins-Soper frame, including a Glauber phase factor $\exp(iS_e)$ that arises from factorizing Glauber divergences into a universal nonperturbative phase. The hard kernels are convoluted with parton distribution functions for the pion and proton, using the on-shell condition for the final-state gluon to relate the momentum fractions $x_1$ and $x_2$, and the angular coefficients $\lambda$, $\mu$, and $\nu$ are extracted from the resulting cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The Glauber phase values $S_e = 1.1$ and $S_e = 1.2$ produce predictions for $\lambda$, $\nu$, and the deviation $1-\lambda-2\nu$ that agree well with the NA10 data.
- The analysis uses $\sqrt{s} = 194$ GeV and $Q = 8$ GeV as typical values, following Brandenburg-Nachtmann-Mirza (1993).
- The data considered come from the region $4.05$ GeV $< Q < 8.5$ GeV and $q_T < 3$ GeV, where neither $Q$ nor $q_T$ is high enough to suppress the Glauber effect.
- The deviation $1-\lambda-2\nu$ and $\nu$ are proportional to $s_1^2 = q_T^2/(Q^2+q_T^2)$, so they vanish at $q_T = 0$ naturally.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-gluon mechanism previously applied to resolve puzzles in heavy-quark decays (the $B\to\pi\pi$, $B\to\pi K$, and $D\to\pi\pi$ puzzles) by Li and collaborators (2011, 2012) to the Drell-Yan process. It offers an alternative to two existing resolutions: the vacuum effect proposed by Brandenburg, Nachtmann, and Mirza (1993, 2005), which is flavor-blind, and the Boer-Mulders functions proposed by Boer (1999), which rely on the valence/sea distinction of anti-quarks. The authors propose discriminating these mechanisms by measuring the $p\bar p$ Drell-Yan process at GSI and J-PARC: the Boer-Mulders proposal predicts strong violation of the Lam-Tung relation in $p\bar p$ (since the anti-quark in $\bar p$ is valence), while the Glauber-gluon proposal predicts no violation (since $\bar p$ is not a Nambu-Goldstone boson).
