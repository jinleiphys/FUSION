# Covariant kinetic theory and transport coefficients for Gribov plasma

> Gribov quantization is a method to improve the infrared dynamics of Yang-Mills theory. We study the thermodynamics and transport properties of a plasma consisting of gluons whose propagator is improved by the Gribov prescription. We first construct thermodynamics of Gribov plasma using the gauge invariant Gribov dispersion relation for interacting gluons. When the Gribov parameter in the dispersion relation is temperature dependent, one expects a mean field correction to the Boltzmann equation. We formulate covariant kinetic theory for the Gribov plasma and determine the mean-field contribution in the Boltzmann equation. This leads to a quasiparticle like framework with a bag correction to pressure and energy density which mimics confinement. The temperature dependence of the Gribov parameter and bag pressure is fixed by matching with lattice results for a system of gluons. Finally we calculate the temperature dependence of the transport coefficients, i.e., bulk and shear viscosities.

## Key claim

The paper formulates, for the first time, a covariant kinetic theory for a gluon plasma whose propagator is improved by the Gribov prescription, and calculates the temperature dependence of shear and bulk viscosities. The key condition is that the Gribov parameter in the dispersion relation is taken to be temperature dependent, which introduces a mean-field correction to the Boltzmann equation and leads to a quasiparticle framework with a bag correction to pressure and energy density.

## Method

The authors first construct the equilibrium thermodynamics of the Gribov plasma using the gauge-invariant Gribov dispersion relation for gluons, which has two complex energy poles. They then formulate a covariant kinetic theory by deriving the Boltzmann equation with a mean-field term arising from the temperature-dependent Gribov parameter, using a relaxation-time approximation for the collision kernel. The temperature dependence of the Gribov parameter and the bag function is fixed by matching the entropy density to lattice QCD results for a pure gluon system. Finally, using a Chapman-Enskog-like iterative solution, they compute the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients from the first-order correction to the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- The scaled Gribov parameter, \(\gamma_G/T\), decreases with temperature and tends to saturate to a constant value at very high temperatures; near \(T_c\) its value is nearly half of that obtained in Coulomb gauge calculations by Begun (2016).
- The scaled shear viscosity coefficient, \(\eta/(s\,\tau_R\,T_c)\), increases with temperature; the covariant gauge result is slightly smaller than the Coulomb gauge result of Begun (2016) and the effective mass quasiparticle model of Czajka (2017).
- The scaled bulk viscosity coefficient, \(\zeta/(s\,\tau_R\,T_c)\), has a peak near \(T_c\) and then decreases; the peak value from the covariant gauge calculation is in numerical agreement with the Coulomb gauge result of Begun (2016).
- The equilibrium pressure from the fit matches lattice results from Borsanyi (2012) at the level of 1-2% accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger approach to finite temperature, which was previously applied to thermodynamics by Fukushima (2013) and to boost-invariant kinetic theory by Florkowski (2015) and Begun (2016). It provides the first covariant kinetic theory formulation for Gribov plasma, enabling the calculation of transport coefficients in a framework that can be extended to include quark degrees of freedom and finite chemical potential, and to derive second-order dissipative hydrodynamics as done by Czajka (2017) for temperature-dependent quasiparticle masses.
