# Helicity Evolution at Small $x$: the Single-Logarithmic Contribution

> We calculate single-logarithmic corrections to the small-$x$ flavor-singlet helicity evolution equations derived recently in the double-logarithmic approximation. The new single-logarithmic part of the evolution kernel sums up powers of $α_s \ln(1/x)$, which are an important correction to the dominant powers of $α_s \ln^2(1/x)$ summed up by the double-logarithmic kernel at small values of Bjorken $x$ and with $α_s$ the strong coupling constant. The single-logarithmic terms arise separately from either the longitudinal or transverse momentum integrals. Consequently, the evolution equations we derive employing the light-cone perturbation theory simultaneously include the small-$x$ evolution kernel and the leading-order polarized DGLAP splitting functions. We further enhance the equations by calculating the running coupling corrections to the kernel.

## Key claim

The paper derives single-logarithmic corrections to the small-x flavor-singlet helicity evolution equations, which sum powers of \(\alpha_s \ln(1/x)\) as a subleading correction to the previously known double-logarithmic terms \(\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x)\); the single-logarithmic terms arise separately from either longitudinal or transverse momentum integrals, and the resulting evolution equations simultaneously contain the small-x evolution kernel and the leading-order polarized DGLAP splitting functions.

## Method

The authors used light-cone perturbation theory in the \(A^- = 0\) gauge to compute the lowest-order splitting wave functions for \(q \to qG\), \(G \to q\bar{q}\), and \(G \to GG\) processes. From these wave functions they constructed polarized splitting kernels, subtracted the double-logarithmic contributions to avoid double-counting, and assembled the single-logarithmic transverse (SLA\(_T\)) kernel. They then combined this with the previously known single-logarithmic longitudinal (SLA\(_L\)) kernel and the double-logarithmic kernel, and further incorporated running coupling corrections by disentangling ultraviolet-divergent transverse integrals.

## Key numbers

* The double-logarithmic approximation for large-\(N_c\) gives the small-\(x\) intercepts \(\alpha_h^q = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \sqrt{\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2\pi}}\) for the flavor-singlet quark helicity distribution and \(\alpha_h^G = \frac{13}{4\sqrt{3}} \sqrt{\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2\pi}}\) for the gluon helicity distribution (Eq. \eqref{alphas}).
* For large-\(N_c \& N_f\), the oscillation frequency of the quark helicity distribution is approximated by \(\omega_q \approx \frac{0.66 (N_f/N_c)}{1 + 0.3795 (N_f/N_c)} \sqrt{\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2\pi}}\) (Eq. \eqref{omega_fit}).
* The current experimental values of the quark and gluon spin contributions are \(S_q (Q^2 = 10\ \text{GeV}^2) \approx 0.15 \div 0.20\) integrated over \(0.001 < x < 1\) and \(S_G (Q^2 = 10\ \text{GeV}^2) \approx 0.13 \div 0.26\) integrated over \(0.05 < x < 1\).

## Context

This work extends the double-logarithmic helicity evolution equations derived by Kovchegov, Pitonyak, Sievert, and Cougoulic (2015-2019) by adding the next-order single-logarithmic corrections, analogous to how next-to-leading-logarithmic corrections improve the unpolarized Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) and Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution. The resulting equations enable more precise predictions for helicity parton distribution functions at small Bjorken \(x\), which are needed to constrain the small-\(x\) contribution to the proton spin puzzle and to interpret data from the future Electron-Ion Collider.
