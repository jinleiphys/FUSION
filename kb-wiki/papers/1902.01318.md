# Probing gravity at sub-femtometer scales through the pressure distribution inside the proton

> Recently, a measurement of the pressure distribution experienced by the quarks inside the proton has found a strong repulsive (positive) pressure at distances up to 0.6 femtometers from its center and a (negative) confining pressure at larger distances. In this paper we show that this measurement puts significant constraints on modified theories of gravity in which the strength of the gravitational interaction on microscopic scales is enhanced with respect to general relativity. We consider the particular case of Eddington-inspired Born-Infeld gravity, showing that strong limits on $κ$, the only additional parameter of the theory with respect to general relativity, may be derived from the quark pressure measurement ($|κ| \lsim 10^{-1} \, {\rm m^5 \, kg^{-1} \, s^{-2}}$). Furthermore, we show how these limits may be significantly improved with precise measurements of the first and second moments of the pressure distribution inside the proton.

## Key claim

The recent measurement of the quark pressure distribution inside the proton can constrain modified theories of gravity where the gravitational interaction is enhanced on microscopic scales, specifically Eddington-inspired Born-Infeld (EiBI) gravity, but the derived limits are conservative because the analysis assumes that EiBI gravity does not affect the determination of the physical pressure distribution using virtual Compton scattering.

## Method

The author uses the measured quark pressure profile from deeply virtual Compton scattering at 6 GeV electron beam energy to derive constraints on the EiBI gravity parameter κ. The analysis applies the von Laue condition (vanishing average total pressure) to the proton, equating the average physical pressure with the average effective gravitational pressure from EiBI gravity. The author expands the EiBI field equations to first order in κρ and κp to relate the effective gravitational pressure to the physical pressure and density, then uses the measured peak pressure and moments of the pressure distribution to set upper limits on |κ|.

## Key numbers

- Peak pressure near the center of the proton: approximately 10^35 Pa
- Upper limit on |κ| from peak pressure: |κ| ≲ 10^{-1} m^5 kg^{-1} s^{-2}
- This limit is roughly one order of magnitude stronger than the constraint from predicted neutron star core density (Pani 2011)
- Electron beam energy used for the measurement: 6 GeV
- Proton mass: 1.7 × 10^{-27} kg; proton radius: ~10^{-15} m
- Newtonian gravitational potential at proton surface: |φ_pr| ~ 10^{-39}

## Context

This work extends the application of EiBI gravity constraints from astrophysical objects (neutron stars, black holes, early universe) to subatomic scales, following the measurement of quark pressure distribution by Burkert (2018). The derived limit on κ is stronger than that from neutron star cores (Pani 2011) but weaker than constraints from Bhabha scattering in metric-affine gravity (Latorre 2017), though the latter may not account for loop corrections. Future experiments at 12 GeV at Jefferson Lab (Burkert 2018) and the Electron Ion Collider (Accardi 2012) could improve constraints by measuring the first and second moments of the pressure distribution.
