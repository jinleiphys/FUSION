# Physics of Strange Matter

> The properties of strange quark matter and strange hadronic matter and their finite counterparts (strangelets and MEMOs) are reviewed. The production of strange matter in heavy-ion collision and the appearance of strange matter in neutron stars are discussed.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that strange matter, either as strange quark matter (strangelets) or strange hadronic matter (MEMOs), may exist as metastable or even absolutely stable configurations, but emphasizes that such existence cannot be rigorously predicted by theory and ultimately requires experimental confirmation. The authors caution that the phenomenological bag-model calculations used to support these possibilities are subject to serious theoretical limitations, including the breakdown of perturbative expansions for certain parameter choices.

## Method

The paper reviews two distinct theoretical frameworks for strange matter: (1) a Fermi gas model of interacting quarks within the MIT bag model, using the thermodynamic potential with a bag constant B, strange quark mass m_s, and strong coupling constant α_c to compute the energy per baryon as a function of strangeness fraction f_s; and (2) a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for strange hadronic matter, which treats nucleons and hyperons as quasiparticles and reproduces properties of normal nuclei and hypernuclei. For finite strangelets, the authors employ a spherical MIT bag model with exact single-particle Dirac states, summing occupied energy eigenvalues plus the bag volume energy and including center-of-momentum and Coulomb corrections.

## Key numbers

- For Au(11.7 AGeV)Au collisions, the expected average production per event is 20 Λ's, 10 Σ's, and 1 Ξ.
- For Pb(160 AGeV)Pb collisions, the expected average production per event is 60 Λ's, 40 Σ's, and 5 Ξ.
- The potential depth of a Λ in nuclear matter is U_Λ^(N) = 27–30 MeV, compared to the nuclear depth of about 60 MeV.
- The potential depth of a Ξ in nuclear matter is U_Ξ^(N) = 21–24 MeV from Wood-Saxon potentials, or 28 MeV from RMF calculations.
- The ΛΛ interaction strength ΔB_ΛΛ is 4.7 ± 0.6 MeV for ^6_ΛΛHe, 4.3 ± 0.4 MeV for ^10_ΛΛBe, and 4.8 ± 0.7 MeV for ^13_ΛΛB.
- The baryon density of a strangelet would be about 1.5–2 times normal nuclear matter density.
- For bag parameters B^(1/4) lower than 190 MeV, strange quark droplets can only decay via weak interactions (metastable); for B^(1/4) ≲ 150 MeV, absolute stability becomes possible.
- The minimum energy per baryon for infinite strange quark matter occurs at strangeness fraction f_s ∼ 0.7 for m_s = 150 MeV.
- For small strangelets with baryon number N_B < 50, the energy per baryon is about 50–100 MeV/N larger than for infinite quark matter.

## Context

This review extends the earlier proposals of Bodmer (1971) on strange multiquark clusters and Witten (1984) on absolutely stable strange quark matter as a candidate for cold dark matter, as well as the work of Chin and Kerman (1979) on metastable strangelets using the MIT bag model. The paper also builds on the extensive hypernuclear physics program at AGS and CERN, and on the relativistic mean-field calculations of Rufa et al. (1987, 1990) and Schaffner et al. (1994). The review enables future experimental searches for strangelets and MEMOs in heavy-ion collisions at Brookhaven and CERN, and motivates further theoretical work on the role of strange matter in neutron star structure.
