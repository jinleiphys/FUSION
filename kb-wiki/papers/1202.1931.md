# Fixed energy potentials through an auxiliary inverse eigenvalue problem

> An inverse scattering method based on an auxiliary inverse Sturm-Liouville problem recently proposed by Horváth and Apagyi [Mod. Phys. Lett. B 22, 2137 (2008)] is examined in various aspects and developed further to (re)construct spherically symmetric fixed energy potentials of compact support realized in the three-dimensional Schrödinger equation. The method is generalized to obtain a family of inverse procedures characterized by two parameters originating, respectively, from the Liouville transformation and the solution of the inverse Sturm-Liouville problem. Both parameters affect the bound states arising in the auxiliary inverse spectral problem and one of them enables to reduce their number which is assessed by a simple method. Various solution techniques of the underlying moment problem are proposed including exact Cauchy matrix inversion method, usage of spurious bound state and assessment of the number of bound states. Examples include (re)productions of potentials from phase shifts known theoretically or derived from scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The paper develops a family of inverse scattering methods parameterized by two free parameters (one from the Liouville transformation and one from the inverse Sturm-Liouville problem) that can reconstruct spherically symmetric fixed-energy potentials of compact support from phase shifts, with the hedge that the method's success depends on assessing and possibly reducing the number of bound states in the auxiliary problem, which may require solving a highly nonlinear system when multiple bound states are present.

## Method

The authors transform the radial Schrödinger equation at fixed energy into a Sturm-Liouville problem via a Liouville transformation parameterized by a constant \(c\), then use the m-function values derived from the phase shifts to set up a moment problem for the spectral function. The inverse Sturm-Liouville problem is solved by expanding the kernel function \(F(x)\) in exponentials and inverting a Cauchy matrix, with the boundary condition parameter \(h\) serving as the second free parameter. For cases with one bound state, a linear solution procedure is given; for multiple bound states, a nonlinear system must be solved, and the number of bound states can be estimated from the constant-potential approximation using Bessel function zeros.

## Key numbers

* For a constant potential \(q(r)=1.2\) on \(r\leq a=2\) at \(k=1\), reconstruction with 40 input phase shifts and parameters \(c=-1\), \(h=0\) shows improved quality over 5, 10, or 20 phases.
* The optimal reconstruction of the constant potential \(q(r)=1.2\,H_2(r)\) from 11 phase shifts used parameters \(c=-0.30\) and \(h=-0.15\), yielding a smoothness measure \(s=0.011\).
* For the constant potential \(q(r)=1.2\,H_2(r)\) with \(c=-0.3\) and \(h=-0.5\), the spurious bound state parameter was \(\sqrt{-\lambda}=-1.4447\).
* The number of bound states for a constant potential at \(h=0\) is determined by \(\kappa a\): one bound state for \(0<\kappa a<3.8317\), two for \(3.8318<\kappa a<7.0156\), three for \(7.0156<\kappa a<10.174\), and four for \(10.174<\kappa a<13.324\).
* For \(3.83<\kappa a<5.52\) with two bound states at \(h=0\), varying \(h\) can reduce the number to one bound state.

## Context

This work extends the inverse scattering method of Horváth and Apagyi (2008), which only treated the one-bound-state case, by generalizing the Liouville transformation and the inverse Sturm-Liouville construction to handle zero, one, and multiple bound states. The method enables reconstruction of potentials from fixed-energy phase shifts, including those derived from scattering experiments, and provides techniques to reduce nonlinear problems to linear ones by tuning the parameters \(c\) and \(h\). The assessment of bound states using Bessel function zeros connects to the classical theory of Sturm-Liouville operators with exponential potentials.
