# Role of bulk viscosity in deuteron production in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> We use a Bayesian-calibrated multistage viscous hydrodynamic model to explore deuteron yield, mean transverse momentum and flow observables in LHC Pb-Pb collisions. We explore theoretical uncertainty in the production of deuterons, including (i) the contribution of thermal deuterons, (ii) models for the subsequent formation of deuterons (hadronic transport vs coalescence) and (iii) the overall sensitivity of the results to the hydrodynamic model -- in particular to bulk viscosity, which is often neglected in studies of deuteron production. Using physical parameters set by a comparison to only light hadron observables, we find good agreement with measurements of the mean transverse momentum $\langle p_T \rangle$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ of deuterons; however, tension is observed with experimental data for the deuteron multiplicity in central collisions. The results are found to be sensitive to each of the mentioned theoretical uncertainties, with a particular sensitivity to bulk viscosity, indicating that the latter is an important ingredient for an accurate treatment of deuteron production.

## Key claim

Using a Bayesian-calibrated multistage viscous hydrodynamic model whose parameters were set using only light hadron observables, the authors find good agreement with measured deuteron mean transverse momentum and elliptic flow, but tension with experimental data for deuteron multiplicity in central collisions; the results show particular sensitivity to bulk viscosity, which is often neglected in deuteron production studies.

## Method

The authors employed a hybrid hydrodynamic and hadronic transport model (JETSCAPE framework) with TRENTo initial conditions, MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics including both shear and bulk viscosity, and SMASH hadronic afterburner. They explored three deuteron production mechanisms: (i) sampling thermal deuterons at the Cooper-Frye particlization surface, (ii) deuteron production via three-body reactions in the hadronic transport phase, and (iii) a Wigner-function coalescence model applied to final-state nucleons from the afterburner.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron yield dN/dy in central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV shows tension with experimental data, with the model overpredicting the multiplicity
- Mean transverse momentum <p_T> of deuterons agrees well with measurements in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Elliptic flow v_2 of deuterons agrees well with experimental data in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- The maximum value of bulk viscosity over entropy density (zeta/s)_max = 0.13 was used in the calibrated model
- The switching temperature T_sw = 0.136 GeV was used for the transition from hydrodynamics to hadronic transport
- The Gaussian width parameter for the deuteron Wigner function is d = 3.2 fm

## Context

This work extends previous multistage hydrodynamic studies of light nuclei production (Oliinychenko 2018, 2020) by including bulk viscosity, which was neglected in those earlier simulations. The Bayesian calibration against light hadron observables from ALICE at the LHC and STAR at RHIC (JETSCAPE 2020) provides a controlled framework to test deuteron production models. The results indicate that deuteron observables can provide additional constraints on the bulk viscosity of QCD matter and on initial state parameters, enabling more precise determinations of these transport properties through combined Bayesian analyses including light nuclei.
