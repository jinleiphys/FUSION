# Isospin symmetry breaking in double-pion production in the region of ${d^*(2380)}$ and the scalar $σ$ meson

> The first attempt is made to provide a quantitative theoretical interpretation of the WASA-at-COSY experimental data on the basic double-pion production reactions $pn \to d π^0π^0$ and $pn \to d π^+π^-$ in the energy region $T_p =1$ - $1.3$ GeV [P. Adlarson et al., Phys. Lett. B 721, 229 (2013)]. The data are analyzed within a model based on production and decay of an intermediate $I(J^P)=0(3^+)$ dibaryon resonance $\mathcal{D}_{03}$ (denoted also as $d^*(2380)$). The observed decrease of the near-threshold enhancement (the so-called ABC effect) in the reaction $pn \to d π^+π^-$ in comparison to that in the reaction $pn \to d π^0π^0$ is explained (at least partially) to be due to isospin symmetry violation in the two-pion decay of an intermediate near-threshold scalar $σ$ meson emitted from the $\mathcal{D}_{03}$ dibaryon resonance under conditions of the partial chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of the near-threshold ABC enhancement in the reaction \(pn \to d \pi^+\pi^-\) compared to \(pn \to d \pi^0\pi^0\) can be explained (at least partially) by isospin symmetry breaking in the two-pion decay of an intermediate scalar \(\sigma\) meson emitted from the \(\mathcal{D}_{03}\) dibaryon resonance, under conditions of partial chiral symmetry restoration that shift the \(\sigma\) meson mass and width downward from their free-space values.

## Method

The authors model the reactions \(pn \to d \pi^0\pi^0\) and \(pn \to d \pi^+\pi^-\) in the energy region \(T_p = 1\)–\(1.3\) GeV using a coherent sum of three interfering decay mechanisms for the intermediate \(\mathcal{D}_{03}\) (or \(d^*(2380)\)) dibaryon resonance: (i) emission of a scalar \(\sigma\) meson that decays into two pions, (ii) sequential decay via an intermediate isovector \(\mathcal{D}_{12}(2150)\) dibaryon that emits a pion and forms a deuteron, and (iii) decay through an intermediate \(\Delta\Delta\) state that subsequently decays into two pions and two nucleons that merge into a deuteron. The pion mass difference between charged and neutral pions is explicitly included in the \(\sigma\)-meson decay kinematics, and the \(\sigma\)-meson mass and width are treated as adjustable parameters determined by a fit to the ABC peak data.

## Key numbers

- The \(\mathcal{D}_{03}\) dibaryon resonance has mass \(M_{D_{03}} \simeq 2.38\) GeV and width \(\Gamma_{D_{03}} \simeq 70\) MeV.
- The \(\mathcal{D}_{12}\) dibaryon resonance has mass \(M_{D_{12}} \simeq 2.15\) GeV and width \(\Gamma_{D_{12}} \simeq 110\) MeV.
- The \(\sigma\) meson (in the model) has mass \(m_\sigma = 303\) MeV and width \(\Gamma_\sigma = 126\) MeV, with a cutoff parameter \(\Lambda_{\pi\pi} = 0.09\) GeV.
- The total cross section for \(pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2.38\) GeV is \(\sigma = 0.255\) mb.
- The \(\mathcal{D}_{03} \to \Delta\Delta\) mode contributes 20% of the \(pn \to d \pi^0\pi^0\) cross section at \(\sqrt{s}=2.38\) GeV, corresponding to coupling constant \(g_{\Delta\Delta} = 1.23\).
- The \(\mathcal{D}_{03} \to \mathcal{D}_{12}\pi\) partial width is set to \(\Gamma^{(0)}_{D_{03}\to D_{12}\pi} = 31\) MeV, giving about 25% of the \(\mathcal{D}_{03} \to d\pi\pi\) partial width.
- The \(\mathcal{D}_{12} \to \pi d\) branching ratio is taken as 30%, corresponding to \(\Gamma^{(0)}_{D_{12}\to \pi d} = 33\) MeV.

## Context

This work provides the first quantitative theoretical interpretation of the WASA-at-COSY experimental data (Adlarson et al., Phys. Lett. B 721, 229 (2013)) on double-pion production in \(pn\) collisions, extending the authors' earlier model for the \(pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0\) reaction (Platonova and Kukulin, Phys. Rev. C 87, 024003 (2013)). The observed isospin symmetry violation (about 25% suppression of the ABC enhancement for charged dipion production) was previously attributed only to phase-space reduction from the 5-MeV pion mass difference; the present work shows that isospin breaking in the \(\sigma\)-meson decay, combined with partial chiral symmetry restoration in the \(\mathcal{D}_{03}\) dibaryon, provides an additional dynamical mechanism. The results enable future studies of chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons and the role of light scalar mesons in dibaryon decays, and they impose constraints on the relative weights of \(\mathcal{D}_{12}\pi\) and \(\Delta\Delta\) decay modes of the \(d^*(2380)\) resonance.
