# Universal Equilibration Condition for Heavy Quarks

> Kinetic equilibration at late times is physically required for heavy particles in a finite temperature medium. In Fokker-Planck dynamics, it is ensured by the Einstein relation between the drag and longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficients. However, in certain gauge field theories, this relation is violated at any nonzero heavy quark velocity. Recent work in strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM gauge theory shows that the Kolmogorov equation for the heavy quark phase space distribution (that reduces to Fokker-Planck form upon truncating the momentum transfer probability distribution to second moments) does equilibrate even though the Fokker-Planck equation does not. Going beyond these (to date theory-specific) insights, we derive a universal equilibration condition for the kernel of the Kolmogorov equation and, consequently, for the momentum transfer probability distribution that holds in any quantum field theory with any coupling strength. This condition, which is the generalization of the Einstein relation to quantum field theories which feature non-Gaussian fluctuations, reveals that the asymmetry between energy loss and energy gain in the momentum transfer probability distribution takes a simple, theory-independent, form.

# Universal Equilibration Condition for Heavy Quarks

## Key claim

The authors derive a universal equilibration condition for the Kolmogorov equation governing heavy quark phase space distributions, which generalizes the Einstein relation to any quantum field theory at any coupling strength. This condition, expressed as a symmetry of the momentum transfer probability distribution \(P({\bf k};{\bf v}) \exp[-{\bf v}\cdot{\bf k}/(2T)] = P(-{\bf k};{\bf v}) \exp[{\bf v}\cdot{\bf k}/(2T)]\), is always satisfied for heavy quarks with \(M \gg T\) moving at any velocity through a thermal medium, even though the Fokker-Planck truncation violates the Einstein relation at nonzero velocity.

## Method

The authors start from the Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) Lagrangian and express the momentum transfer probability distribution \(P({\bf k};{\bf v})\) in terms of a Wilson loop \(\langle W_{\bf v} \rangle({\bf L})\) that encodes the color rotation of a heavy quark propagating through the medium. They then derive a KMS-like analytic property \(S({\bf L};{\bf v}) = S(-{\bf L} + i{\bf v}/T;{\bf v})\) for the extensive part of the Wilson loop exponent, which follows from applying parity and time reversal transformations combined with an imaginary time translation. This property directly implies the equilibration condition \(S(i{\bf v}/T;{\bf v}) = 0\) for the Kolmogorov kernel \(K({\bf x},{\bf p}) = S(-i{\bf x};{\bf v}({\bf p}))\), and through Fourier transformation yields the symmetry condition on \(P({\bf k};{\bf v})\).

## Key numbers

- The Einstein relation \(2 T p\, \eta_D = v\, \kappa_L\) is violated in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM by a factor \(\gamma^{3/2}\), where \(\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1-v^2}\) (Gubser, 2006).
- The universal equilibration condition for the Kolmogorov kernel is \(K(-{\bf v}/T, {\bf p}) = 0\), or equivalently \(S(i{\bf v}/T;{\bf v}) = 0\).
- The asymmetry between energy loss and energy gain in \(P({\bf k};{\bf v})\) is always given by the factor \(\exp[{\bf v}\cdot{\bf k}/(2T)]\), independent of the theory.
- The connected moments of \(P({\bf k};{\bf v})\) satisfy \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty (-v)^n c_n(v)/n! = 0\), where \(c_n(v) \equiv \langle k_L^n \rangle_c / (t T^{n+1})\).
- In the Gaussian limit where higher moments vanish (\(c_n(v)=0\) for \(n>2\)), the Einstein relation \(2 c_1(v) = v\, c_2(v)\) holds; otherwise all moments contribute to equilibration.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM (Rajagopal, Scheihing-Hitschfeld, Wiedemann, 2025), where they showed that the full Kolmogorov equation equilibrates despite the Fokker-Planck truncation violating the Einstein relation first observed by Gubser (2006). The result generalizes the Einstein relation, which Sutherland, Einstein, and von Smoluchowski (1905-1906) established for Brownian motion and which Callen and Kubo (1951-1966) connected to the Fluctuation-Dissipation theorem. The universal condition enables first-principles constraints on heavy quark transport properties from quantum field theory calculations, including lattice QCD determinations of low-order moments by Petreczky (2005), Caron-Huot (2009), Banerjee (2011), Brambilla (2020-2022), and Altenkort (2020-2023), and provides a framework for interpreting heavy quark energy loss and flow measurements in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.
