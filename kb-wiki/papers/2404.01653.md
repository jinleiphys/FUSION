# Investigation of reaction and $α$ production cross sections with $^9$Be projectile

> In order to investigate the contribution of $α$ production in the reaction cross sections, measurements of elastic scattering and inclusive $α$ particle angular distributions have been carried out with the $^9$Be projectile on $^{89}$Y, $^{124}$Sn, $^{159}$Tb, $^{198}$Pt, and $^{209}$Bi targets over a wide angular range at energies near the Coulomb barrier. The measured elastic scattering angular distributions were fitted with optical model calculations, and reaction cross sections were extracted. The same data were also analysed using both global optical model potentials (Global OMP) and microscopic S$\tilde{a}$o Paulo potentials (SPP), to obtain the reaction cross sections. The data available in the literature for $^9$Be projectile includes the elastic scattering angular distributions, $α$ production cross sections, and complete fusion cross sections on these and other targets at several energies are also utilised for comparative studies. The reaction cross section extracted from the three potentials (Best Fit, Global OMP and SPP) are in reasonable agreement for all the targets except for the energies below the barrier where the results from SPP deviate by 30-50 \%. Inclusive $α$ particle production cross sections were also extracted by integrating the $α$ particle angular distributions. The present data and data available from literature of reaction and $α$-particle production cross sections were utilised to make systematic studies. Systematics of reaction and $α$-particle production cross sections revealed their universal behaviour.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for reactions induced by the weakly bound projectile $^9$Be, the sum of complete fusion (CF) cross sections and inclusive $\alpha$ production cross sections almost exhausts the total reaction cross section across a wide range of target masses and energies, but this conclusion is qualified by the observation that at sub-barrier energies the S$\tilde{a}$o Paulo potential (SPP) calculations deviate from the best-fit and global optical model potential results by 30-50%.

## Method

Elastic scattering angular distributions and inclusive $\alpha$ particle angular distributions were measured for $^9$Be projectiles on $^{89}$Y, $^{124}$Sn, $^{159}$Tb, $^{198}$Pt, and $^{209}$Bi targets at energies near the Coulomb barrier using multiple silicon detector telescopes at the BARC-TIFR Pelletron-LINAC Facility. The elastic scattering data were analyzed with optical model calculations using three approaches: a best-fit Woods-Saxon potential, the global optical model potential (Global OMP) from Yongli (2018), and the microscopic double-folding S$\tilde{a}$o Paulo potential (SPP). Inclusive $\alpha$ production cross sections were obtained by integrating the $\alpha$ particle angular distributions after subtracting compound nuclear contributions estimated with the statistical model code PACE2.

## Key numbers

* Reaction cross sections from the three potentials (Best Fit, Global OMP, SPP) are in reasonable agreement for all targets except at energies below the Coulomb barrier, where SPP results deviate by 30-50%.
* For $^9$Be+$^{209}$Bi at E$_{lab}$=35.7 MeV (E$_{c.m.}$/V$_B$=0.85), the inclusive $\alpha$ cross section is 203 $\pm$ 47 mb and the reaction cross section from best-fit is 55$\pm$18 mb.
* For $^9$Be+$^{159}$Tb at E$_{lab}$=35.3 MeV (E$_{c.m.}$/V$_B$=1.02), the inclusive $\alpha$ cross section is 609 $\pm$ 7.8 mb and the reaction cross section from best-fit is 447$\pm$50 mb.
* For $^9$Be+$^{124}$Sn at E$_{lab}$=29.1 MeV (E$_{c.m.}$/V$_B$=1.03), the inclusive $\alpha$ cross section is 460 $\pm$ 3.7 mb and the complete fusion cross section is 129.3 $\pm$ 5.3 mb.
* For $^9$Be+$^{89}$Y at E$_{lab}$=29.3 MeV (E$_{c.m.}$/V$_B$=1.24), the inclusive $\alpha$ cross section is 566 $\pm$ 4.8 mb and the complete fusion cross section is 495 $\pm$ 35 mb.
* Compound nuclear contributions to $\alpha$ production were negligible (about 1% or less) for heavier targets (Z=83, 78, 65, 50), but for the medium-mass $^{89}$Y target at E$_{c.m.}$=1.2 V$_B$ it was around 7%.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of $\alpha$ production with weakly bound projectiles, previously performed for $^{6,7}$Li by Santra (2012) and Pandit (2017), to the $^9$Be projectile, which has a Borromean cluster structure and low neutron separation energy. The results enable comparison of $\alpha$ production mechanisms across different weakly bound projectiles, showing a clear distinction between projectile types that correlates with $\alpha$ separation energies. The universal behavior observed in reaction and $\alpha$ production cross sections provides a foundation for predicting these quantities for other target-projectile combinations near the Coulomb barrier.
