# Test of the $h_1(1830)$ made of $K^{*}\bar{K}^*$ with the $η_c\rightarrow ϕK^{*}\bar{K}^{*}$ decay

> We present a new reaction, complementary to the $J/ψ\rightarrow ηK^{*0}\bar{K}^{*0}$ from which an $h_1$ resonance with mass around $1830$ MeV was reported from a BESIII experiment. The new reaction is $η_c\rightarrow ϕK^*\bar{K}^*$, or $η_c(2S)\rightarrow ϕK^*\bar{K}^*$. Using the information from the analysis of $J/ψ\rightarrow ηK^{*0}\bar{K}^{*0}$, we find that the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ invariant mass distribution for those two $η_c$ decays exhibits a clear peak around $1830$ MeV perfectly distinguishable from what one obtains with pure phase space. We suggest the implementation of these reactions to assert the existence of this elusive resonance which, by its nature as a vector-vector molecule with $0^-(1^{+-})$ quantum numbers, only couples to the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ channel.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ invariant mass distribution in $\eta_c\rightarrow \phi K^*\bar{K}^*$ decays will exhibit a clear peak around 1830 MeV, distinct from pure phase space, which would confirm the existence of the $h_1(1830)$ resonance as a vector-vector molecule. The claim is conditional on the reaction proceeding with $L=0$ (s-wave), which is the most natural assumption given the limited phase space of about 180 MeV.

## Method

The authors model the $\eta_c\rightarrow \phi K^*\bar{K}^*$ decay using a tree-level production vertex plus final-state interaction of the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ pair, summed via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The $K^*\bar{K}^*$ interaction potential is taken from the local hidden gauge approach of Geng et al. (2009), and the loop function $G$ is convoluted with the $K^*$ mass distribution to account for its width of about 50 MeV. The subtraction constant $a(\mu)=-1.0$ and other input parameters are taken from the analysis of Albayrak et al. (2014) that reproduced the BESIII $J/\psi\rightarrow \eta K^{*0}\bar{K}^{*0}$ data.

## Key numbers

* The $h_1$ resonance mass is around 1830 MeV, as deduced from the BESIII $J/\psi\rightarrow \eta K^{*0}\bar{K}^{*0}$ data analyzed by Albayrak et al. (2014).
* The nominal $K^*\bar{K}^*$ threshold is $2m_{K^*}=1780$ MeV.
* The phase space available for $\eta_c\rightarrow \phi K^*\bar{K}^*$ is $M_{\eta_c}-m_{\phi}-2m_{K^*}=180$ MeV.
* The $K^*$ meson has a width $\Gamma_{K^*}=50$ MeV.
* The branching ratio for $\eta_c\rightarrow \phi K \bar{K}$ is $2.9 \times 10^{-3}$ (PDG), used as a rough estimate for the expected rate of the proposed reaction.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $h_1(1830)$ resonance first observed in $J/\psi\rightarrow \eta K^{*0}\bar{K}^{*0}$ decays by BESIII (2012) and interpreted as a $K^*\bar{K}^*$ molecular state by Albayrak et al. (2014). The resonance was originally predicted as a dynamically generated state from vector-vector interactions in the local hidden gauge approach by Geng et al. (2009). The proposed $\eta_c$ decay provides a complementary reaction to confirm this elusive state, which has quantum numbers $0^-(1^{+-})$ and couples only to the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ channel, explaining why it has not been catalogued in the PDG. The authors also extend the prediction to $\eta_c(2S)$ decays, which offer larger phase space.
