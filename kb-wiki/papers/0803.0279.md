# Baryons and the phase diagram for a large number of colors and flavors

> We consider the possible phases of a non-Abelian gauge theory, as a function of temperature and quark chemical potential, when both the number of colors and flavors is very large. Generally, a large number of flavors washes out deconfining phase transitions. We show, however, that the degeneracy of even the lightest baryons is exponentially large. This implies that the baryon number (or fluctuations thereof, at zero chemical potential) is an order parameter in the limit of an infinite number of colors and flavors.

## Key claim

The authors show that when both the number of colors and flavors is very large, the degeneracy of even the lightest baryons grows exponentially with the number of colors, so that baryon number (or its fluctuations at zero chemical potential) becomes an order parameter for a phase transition, even though the usual deconfining order parameters (Polyakov loop, pressure) are washed out by the large flavor count.

## Method

The authors analyze a non-Abelian gauge theory in the limit where both the number of colors Nc and the number of flavors Nf are taken to infinity, with the ratio Nf/Nc held fixed. They compute the degeneracy of the lightest baryons (spin j+1/2) using Young tableaux for the SU(Nf) flavor symmetry, applying the "factors over hooks" rule to obtain the dimension of the representation. They then compare the Boltzmann suppression of these heavy baryons (mass ~ Nc) against the entropic enhancement from their exponentially large degeneracy to determine a "quarkyonic" temperature Tqk above which baryon fluctuations become unsuppressed.

## Key numbers

- The degeneracy of the lightest baryons grows as d_j ~ exp( (Nc - 1) f(xi) ), where xi = 2 Nf/(Nc - 1) and f(xi) = (1+xi) log(1+xi) - xi log(xi); for fixed Nf as Nc goes to infinity, the degeneracy scales as Nc^{-4 + 2 Nf}
- The quarkyonic temperature at zero chemical potential is Tqk = (m_B / Nc) * (1 / f(xi)), where m_B is the mass of the lightest baryon
- At nonzero quark chemical potential mu, Tqk(mu) = (m_B/Nc - mu) * (1/f(xi)), vanishing when mu = m_B/Nc
- For an extrapolation to QCD with three colors and two flavors, f(2) ≈ 1.91 and Tqk ≈ 160 MeV; for three colors and three flavors, f(3) ≈ 2.25 and Tqk ≈ 140 MeV, using m_baryon/3 = 313 MeV

## Context

This work extends the "quarkyonic" phase concept introduced by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) for large Nc and small Nf to the regime where Nf grows with Nc, building on the large-Nc and large-Nf analysis of Veneziano (1976). The authors show that when both Nc and Nf are large, the usual deconfining order parameters (Polyakov loop, pressure) become of order one in all phases, but baryon number provides a new order parameter. This enables a phase diagram where a hadronic phase (containing only mesons and glueballs) is separated from a quarkyonic phase (where baryons condense) by a transition line, with the deconfining transition eventually disappearing as Nf/Nc increases.
