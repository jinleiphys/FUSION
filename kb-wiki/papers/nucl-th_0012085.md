# Pion Interferometry at RHIC: Probing a thermalized Quark-Gluon-Plasma?

> We calculate the Gaussian radius parameters of the pion-emitting source in high energy heavy ion collisions, assuming a first order phase transition from a thermalized Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) to a gas of hadrons. Such a model leads to a very long-lived dissipative hadronic rescattering phase which dominates the properties of the two-pion correlation functions. The radii are found to depend only weakly on the thermalization time tau_i, the critical temperature T_c (and thus the latent heat), and the specific entropy of the QGP. The dissipative hadronic stage enforces large variations of the pion emission times around the mean. Therefore, the model calculations suggest a rapid increase of R_out/R_side as a function of K_T if a thermalized QGP were formed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if a thermalized Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) undergoing a first-order phase transition is formed in heavy ion collisions, the resulting long-lived dissipative hadronic rescattering phase, rather than the QGP properties themselves, dominates the Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) pion interferometry radii. The model calculations suggest a rapid increase of the ratio R_out/R_side as a function of transverse momentum K_T if a thermalized QGP were formed, but the authors hedge that the HBT radii depend only weakly on the thermalization time, critical temperature, latent heat, and specific entropy of the QGP.

## Method

The authors model the QGP as an ideal fluid undergoing hydrodynamic expansion with a bag model equation of state, assuming cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion and longitudinally boost-invariant scaling flow. Hadronization via a first-order phase transition is matched to a semi-classical transport model (UrQMD) that treats hadronic resonance formation/decay and 2-to-n scattering explicitly, propagating individual hadrons along classical trajectories. The Gaussian HBT radius parameters R_out, R_side, and R_long are extracted from the Wigner density of the pion source at freeze-out via saddle-point integration over the emission function.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time assumed: tau_i = 0.6 fm/c for RHIC initial conditions (compared to tau_i = 1 fm/c for SPS)
- Entropy per net baryon ratio: s/rho_B = 200 for RHIC (compared to s/rho_B = 45 for SPS)
- Transverse radius of wounded nucleon distribution: R_T = 6 fm
- Critical temperatures studied: T_c ~ 160 MeV and T_c ~ 200 MeV, corresponding to bag parameters B = 380 MeV/fm^3 and B = 720 MeV/fm^3 respectively
- Initial temperature at RHIC: T_i ~ 300 MeV
- Only 15-20% of pions freeze out after an elastic scattering; most emerge from hadronic resonance fragmentation or direct hadronization
- Preliminary STAR data at sqrt(s)=130A GeV gives R_out/R_side ~ 1.1 at small K_T
- At SPS energy (sqrt(s)=17.4A GeV), all three HBT radii are <= 10 fm

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach of Rischke and Gyulassy (1996) and the hybrid hydrodynamics+transport model of Bass and Dumitru (2000) to compute HBT radii at RHIC energies. It contrasts with the expectation from earlier work by Pratt (1986) and Schlei et al. (1992) that a first-order phase transition would produce unusually large HBT radii directly reflecting QGP properties. The paper enables experimental tests at RHIC: if a long-lived hadronic rescattering stage associated with QGP formation is present, R_out/R_side should rise with K_T rather than fall, and preliminary NA49 data at SPS already show a rising ratio similar to the model predictions for T_c ~ 160 MeV.
