# Monte-Carlo approach to particle-field interactions and the kinetics of the chiral phase transition

> The kinetics of the chiral phase transition is studied within a linear quark-meson-$σ$ model, using a Monte-Carlo approach to semiclassical particle-field dynamics. The meson fields are described on the mean-field level and quarks and antiquarks as ensembles of test particles. Collisions between quarks and antiquarks as well as the $q\overline{q}$ annihilation to $σ$ mesons and the decay of $σ$ mesons is treated, using the corresponding transition-matrix elements from the underlying quantum field theory, obeying strictly the rule of detailed balance and energy-momentum conservation. The approach allows to study fluctuations without making ad hoc assumptions concerning the statistical nature of the random process as necessary in Langevin-Fokker-Planck frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Monte-Carlo approach to semiclassical particle-field dynamics can simulate the off-equilibrium kinetics of the chiral phase transition in a linear quark-meson-σ model, including both elastic scattering and quark-number-changing processes, while strictly obeying energy-momentum conservation and detailed balance without ad hoc assumptions about the statistical nature of the random process.

## Method

The meson fields are treated on the mean-field level while quarks and antiquarks are represented as ensembles of test particles. The simulation implements elastic quark and antiquark scattering, as well as q-anti-q annihilation to σ mesons and σ decay back to q-anti-q pairs, using transition-matrix elements from the underlying quantum field theory. Energy-momentum transfer between particles and the mean field is handled via relativistic Gaussian wave packets, and the inverse decay process is simulated through a coarse-graining procedure that locally maps the field energy-momentum content to a Maxwell-Jüttner distribution.

## Key numbers

- The linear σ model uses a Yukawa coupling g in the range [3.3, 5.5], a meson coupling constant λ² = 20, a pion mass m_π ≈ 138 MeV, and a σ mass m_σ ≈ 600 MeV.
- The quark degeneracy factor is d_n = 2 N_f N_c = 12.
- In the thermal-box simulation, the total energy fluctuations are ΔE/E ≲ 5 × 10⁻⁵, while quark energy fluctuations are ΔE/E ∼ 10⁻³ and field energy fluctuations are ΔE/E ∼ 10⁻².
- The transition temperature for the chiral crossover at zero baryon chemical potential is T_c ≈ 160 MeV, as known from lattice QCD.

## Context

This work extends the use of effective chiral models (such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and the linear σ model) to study the chiral phase transition in off-equilibrium conditions relevant for heavy-ion collisions. It provides an alternative to Langevin-Fokker-Planck frameworks (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2013) that require ad hoc assumptions about the noise statistics, and it enables the study of how different transition scenarios (crossover, first-order, second-order) affect fluctuations of conserved charges in expanding fireballs, as relevant for the RHIC beam-energy scan program and future FAIR and NICA experiments.
