# Spin-dependent $γ$ softness or triaxiality in even-even $^{132-138}$Nd nuclei

> The properties of $γ$ instability in rapidly rotating even-even $^{132-138}$Nd isotopes have been investigated using the pairing-deformation self-consistent total-Routhian-surface calculations in a deformation space of ($β_2, γ, β_4$). It is found that even-even $^{134-138}$Nd nuclei exhibit the triaxiality in both ground and excited states, even up to high-spin ones. The lightest isotope possesses a well-deformed prolate shape without $γ$ deformation component. The current numerical results are compared with previous calculations and available observables, showing basically a general agreement with the observed trend of $γ$ correlations. The existing differences between theory and experiment are analyzed and discussed briefly.

# Spin-dependent γ softness or triaxiality in even-even 132-138Nd nuclei

## Key claim

Even-even 134-138Nd nuclei exhibit triaxiality in both ground and excited states up to high spin, while 132Nd possesses a well-deformed prolate shape without γ deformation; however, the present total-Routhian-surface method does not include rotation-vibration coupling or tilted-axis cranking, so the results provide only a qualitative description of γ correlations that are consistent with some observed properties to a limited extent.

## Method

Total-Routhian-surface (TRS) calculations were performed in a deformation space of (β₂, γ, β₄) using the pairing-deformation-frequency self-consistent cranked shell model with a Woods-Saxon potential. The macroscopic energy was obtained from the liquid-drop formula of Myers and Swiatecki, while shell corrections were computed via the Strutinsky method and pairing corrections via the Lipkin-Nogami method, including both monopole and doubly stretched quadrupole pairings. Routhians were interpolated using cubic spline functions between lattice points to determine equilibrium deformations at rotational frequencies from ℏω = 0.0 to 0.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state β₂ deformations for 132-138Nd increase as neutron number moves away from the closed shell N = 82; the calculations systematically underestimate β₂ compared to experimental values from reduced transition probabilities B(E2).
- 132Nd has γ = 0° (prolate shape); 138Nd has γ ≈ -29° at ground state, rotating around the medium axis, with a shape transition to oblate-triaxial (γ ≈ -84°) at ℏω ≈ 0.2 MeV, rotating around the long axis.
- The empirical quantity E_S/E(2₁⁺) decreases from 0.99 (132Nd) to -0.45 (138Nd), reflecting structural change from nearly axial rotor to large-amplitude γ-soft dynamics.
- 138Nd exhibits γ ≈ 29° in the ground state, with the lowest E_S/E(2₁⁺) value among the isotopes, making it the γ-softest nucleus in this mass region.
- The maximum spin values extend up to approximately 20ℏ (132Nd), 22ℏ (134Nd), 16ℏ (136Nd), and 14ℏ (138Nd) at ℏω = 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous TRS studies of γ softness in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, including calculations for even-even germanium and selenium isotopes (Nomura, 2012) and the authors' own investigations of octupole softness in rotating 106,108Te and neutron-deficient U isotopes (Wang, 2012). The results are compared with the finite-range droplet model calculations of Möller (1995, 2008) and experimental data from Raman (2001). The study enables future identification of high-spin γ-band levels in 132-138Nd to confirm the evolution of γ softness with rotation, and motivates more complete calculations incorporating rotation-vibration coupling and tilted-axis cranking for transitional soft nuclei.
