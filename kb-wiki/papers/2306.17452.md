# Repulsive $Λ$ potentials in dense neutron star matter and binding energy of $Λ$ in hypernuclei

> The repulsive three-body force between the lambda ($Λ$) hyperon and medium nucleons is a key element in solving the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars. We investigate the binding energies of $Λ$ hyperon in hypernuclei to verify the repulsive $Λ$ potentials from the chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) employing the Skyrme Hartree-Fock method. We find that the $χ$EFT $Λ$ potential with the $ΛNN$ three-body forces reproduces the existing hypernuclear binding energy data, whereas the $Λ$ binding energies are overestimated without the $ΛNN$ three-body force. Additionally, we search for the parameter space of the $Λ$ potentials by varying the Taylor coefficients of the $Λ$ potential and the effective mass of $Λ$ at the saturation density. Our analysis demonstrates that the parameter region consistent with the $Λ$ binding energy data spans a wide range of the parameter space, including even more repulsive potentials than the $χ$EFT prediction. We confirm that these strong repulsive $Λ$ potentials suppress the presence of $Λ$ in the neutron star matter. We found that the $Λ$ potentials repulsive at high densities are favored when the depth of the $Λ$ potential at the saturation density, $U_Λ(ρ_0)=J_Λ$, is $J_Λ\gtrsim-29~\text{MeV}$, while attractive ones are favored when $J_Λ\lesssim -31~\text{MeV}$. This suggests that the future high-resolution data of hypernuclei could rule out the scenario in which $Λ$s appear through the precise determination of $J_Λ$ within the accuracy of $1~\text{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) Lambda potential that includes Lambda-nucleon-nucleon three-body forces reproduces existing hypernuclear binding energy data, while the Lambda binding energies are overestimated without the three-body force; however, the parameter region consistent with the binding energy data spans a wide range, including even more repulsive potentials than the chiEFT prediction, with the depth of the Lambda potential at saturation density J_Lambda needing to be determined within 1 MeV accuracy to rule out the scenario in which Lambdas appear in neutron stars.

## Method

The authors employed the Skyrme Hartree-Fock method to compute the binding energies of Lambda hyperons in hypernuclei across mass numbers A=13 to 208. They parametrized the Lambda-nucleon interaction using a Skyrme-type potential fitted to the density and momentum dependence of the Lambda single-particle potential from chiral effective field theory calculations, including both two-body (Lambda N) and three-body (Lambda NN) interactions from Gerstung (2020) and Kohno (2018). The SLy4 parametrization was used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the Skyrme parameter a_3^Lambda was determined by reproducing the experimental binding energy of 13_Lambda C at 11.88 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The depth of the Lambda potential at saturation density for Chi3 (including three-body forces) is J_Lambda = -30.0 MeV, while for Chi2 (two-body only) it is J_Lambda = -33.5 MeV
- The normalized effective mass m*_Lambda/m_Lambda at saturation density is 0.70 for Chi3 and 0.73 for Chi2
- The mean squared deviation of calculated Lambda binding energies from experimental data is Delta B_Lambda = 0.72 MeV for Chi3, compared to 1.55 MeV for Chi2
- The Lambda potentials repulsive at high densities are favored when J_Lambda is greater than or approximately equal to -29 MeV, while attractive ones are favored when J_Lambda is less than or approximately equal to -31 MeV
- The experimental binding energy of 13_Lambda C used for calibration is 11.88 MeV, taken from Hashimoto (2006) with a 0.5 MeV correction from Gogami (2015)

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars by testing whether the repulsive Lambda potentials from chiral effective field theory, which can suppress Lambda appearance in neutron star matter as shown by Gerstung (2020), are consistent with existing hypernuclear binding energy data. The results confirm findings by Nagels (2015), Friedman (2022), and Friedman (2023) that three-baryon interactions are necessary to reproduce Lambda binding energies. The analysis demonstrates that future high-resolution hypernuclear data could determine J_Lambda within 1 MeV accuracy, potentially ruling out scenarios where Lambdas appear in neutron stars, and enables further constraints on the density dependence of the Lambda potential through comparison with heavy-ion collision observables.
