# Coupled-channel effects in the pp -> ppK+K- reaction

> The cross sections for the pp -> ppK+K- reaction were measured at three beam energies 2.65, 2.70, and 2.83 GeV at the COSY-ANKE facility. The shape of the K+K- spectrum at low invariant masses largely reflects the importance of K\bar{K} final state interactions. It is shown that these data can be understood in terms of an elastic K+K- rescattering plus a contribution coming from the production of a K0\bar{K}0 pair followed by a charge-exchange rescattering. Though the data are not yet sufficient to establish the size of the cusp at the K0\bar{K}0 threshold, the low mass behaviour suggests that isospin-zero production is dominant.

## Key claim
The low-mass enhancement in the $K^+K^-$ invariant mass spectrum from $pp\to ppK^+K^-$ can be explained by a combination of elastic $K^+K^-$ rescattering and production of $K^0\bar{K}^0$ pairs followed by charge-exchange rescattering, but the data are not sufficient to distinguish between a genuine cusp at the $K^0\bar{K}^0$ threshold and a smooth enhancement.

## Method
The authors measured differential and total cross sections for $pp\to ppK^+K^-$ at three beam energies (2.65, 2.70, and 2.83 GeV) using the COSY-ANKE facility. They divided the experimental $K^+K^-$ invariant mass spectra by a Monte Carlo simulation that included only $K^-p$ and $pp$ final state interactions, then fitted the resulting ratio with a K-matrix model incorporating both elastic $K^+K^-$ rescattering and $K^0\bar{K}^0\to K^+K^-$ charge-exchange rescattering, using published $K\bar{K}$ scattering lengths.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies: 2.65, 2.70, and 2.83 GeV, corresponding to excess energies $\varepsilon=50.6$, 66.6, and 108.0 MeV, respectively.
- The simulation underestimates experimental points for $M_{\rm inv}(KK) < 995$ MeV/c$^2$ at 2.65 GeV.
- Best-fit ratio of $I=1$ to $I=0$ production amplitudes: $C = 0.62_{-0.11}^{+0.16}$ with phase $\phi_C = -81^{+36}_{-25}$ degrees (full fit including both elastic and charge-exchange final state interactions), giving $\chi^2/{\rm ndf}=1.2$.
- Isoscalar scattering length taken as $A_0 = (-0.45 + i1.63)$ fm (from BES collaboration); isovector scattering length taken as $A_1 = (0.1 + i0.7)$ fm.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Maeda (2008) on $pp\to ppK^+K^-$ data from COSY-ANKE, which had included only $K^-p$ and $pp$ final state interactions but underestimated the low $K^+K^-$ mass region. The authors show that including $K\bar{K}$ final state interactions (both elastic and charge-exchange) can describe the data, and that the effect is analogous to cusp phenomena seen in $K^-d\to \Lambda p\pi^-$ at the $\Sigma N$ threshold (Tan, 1968). The results enable future experiments at lower excess energies to resolve whether the enhancement is a genuine cusp from $K^0\bar{K}^0$ threshold effects or a smooth enhancement, and they suggest that isospin-zero $K\bar{K}$ production dominates near threshold.
