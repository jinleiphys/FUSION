# Fission decay of $^{282}$Cn studied using cranking inertia

> Superheavy nuclei produced until now are decaying mainly by $α$ emission and spontaneous fission. Calculated $α$ decay half-lives are in agreement with experimental data within one order of magnitude. The discrepancy between theory and experiment can be as high as ten orders of magnitude for spontaneous fission. We analyze a way to improve the accuracy by using the action integral based on cranking inertia and a potential barrier computed by the macroscopic-microscopic method with a two-center shell model. Illustrations are given for $^{282}$Cn which has a measured fission half-life.

## Key claim
Using the cranking inertia method within a macroscopic-microscopic framework with a two-center shell model, the authors show that it is possible to reproduce the measured spontaneous fission half-life of \(^{282}\)Cn with a reasonable value of the zero-point vibration energy, whereas previous Werner-Wheeler approximations gave values too small by many orders of magnitude.

## Method
The deformation energy was computed using the macroscopic-microscopic method, combining a Yukawa-plus-exponential model (Y+EM) with shell-plus-pairing corrections from an asymmetric two-center shell model. The nuclear inertia tensor was calculated microscopically using the cranking formula with BCS pairing correlations, and the spontaneous fission half-life was obtained via the WKB action integral along a one-dimensional deformation path defined by the separation distance between fragments.

## Key numbers
- The experimental spontaneous fission half-life of \(^{282}\)Cn is \(\log_{10} T_f^{\text{exp}}(s) = -3.086\).
- For the fission channel \(^{282}\)Cn → \(^{130}\)Pd + \(^{152}\)Dy, the calculated half-life is \(\log_{10} T_f (s) = -3.4278\) using a zero-point vibration energy \(E_v = 0.50000\) MeV, and exactly \(-3.0860\) when \(E_v\) is adjusted to \(0.43680\) MeV.
- For the channel with light fragment \(^{134}\)Cd, the calculated half-life is \(\log_{10} T_f (s) = -2.4881\) at \(E_v = 0.50000\) MeV, and \(-3.0860\) at \(E_v = 0.60289\) MeV.
- For the channel with light fragment \(^{132}\)Sn, the calculated half-life is \(\log_{10} T_f (s) = 5.5076\) at \(E_v = 0.50000\) MeV, and \(-3.0860\) only when \(E_v\) is raised to \(1.96620\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Poenaru et al., 2013) which showed that \(\alpha\)-decay half-lives agree with experiment within one order of magnitude, but spontaneous fission half-lives could disagree by up to ten orders of magnitude when using the Werner-Wheeler approximation for nuclear inertia. The present study improves on that by adopting the microscopic cranking inertia introduced by Inglis (1954), following recent self-consistent density-functional calculations with cranking inertia by Staszczak et al. (2013). The results enable more reliable predictions of spontaneous fission half-lives for superheavy nuclei, particularly for those near the \(\beta\)-stability line expected to be synthesized in future experiments.
