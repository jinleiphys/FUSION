# Gluon Condensates, Quark Matter Equation of State and Quark Stars

> We consider here quark matter equation of state including strange quarks and taking into account a nontrivial vacuum structure for QCD with gluon condensates. The parameters of condendsate function are determined from minimisation of the thermodynamic potential. The scale parameter of the gluon condensates is fixed from the SVZ parameter in the context of QCD sum rules at zero temperature and zero baryon density. The equation of state for strange matter at zero temperature as derived is used to study quark star structure using Tolman Oppenheimer Volkoff equations. Stable solutions for quark stars are obtained with a large Chandrasekhar limit as 3.2 $M_\odot$ and radii around 17 kms.

## Key claim

The authors derive a nonperturbative equation of state for strange quark matter including gluon condensates and find that it is stiffer than the perturbative equation of state, yielding stable quark stars with a maximum mass of 3.2 solar masses, but only for baryon densities above a critical threshold of 0.55 baryons/fm³ where the gluon condensate vanishes.

## Method

The authors start from the QCD Lagrangian in Coulomb gauge and construct a nonperturbative vacuum with gluon condensates parameterized by a gaussian distribution function. They evaluate the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature including up, down, and strange quarks plus electrons under charge neutrality, then minimize it with respect to the condensate parameter to obtain the equation of state. The resulting pressure-density relation is used as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute quark star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum quark star mass: 3.2 solar masses, with corresponding radius around 17 km
- Stable quark star masses range from 2.2 to 3.2 solar masses with radii from 15.3 to 17 km
- Critical baryon density for gluon condensate vanishing: 0.55 baryons/fm³
- Bag constant (nonperturbative vacuum energy density): 38.3 MeV/fm³ ≡ (131 MeV)⁴
- Scale parameter 1/√B fixed at 321.4 MeV for coupling α_s = 0.8, from SVZ parameter ⟨(α_s/π)G²⟩ = 0.012 GeV⁴
- Strange quark mass taken as 180 MeV; up and down quark masses taken as zero
- Central energy density where dM/dε becomes negative: 950 MeV/fm³
- Maximum mass from perturbative equation of state with same coupling: 2.25 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier nonperturbative treatment of gluon condensates at finite baryon density (Mishra, Mishra, Misra 1993) by including strange quarks and charge neutrality to study quark stars. It contrasts with perturbative QCD calculations (Kapusta 1989) which yield a softer equation of state and lower maximum masses (2.25 solar masses). The results enable comparison with hybrid star models using effective condensate approaches (Ellis, Kapusta, Olive 1991) and provide predictions for quark star observables including surface gravitational redshift.
