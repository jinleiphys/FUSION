# Recent developments in heavy-ion fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier

> The nuclear fusion is a reaction to form a compound nucleus. It plays an important role in several circumstances in nuclear physics as well as in nuclear astrophysics, such as synthesis of superheavy elements and nucleosynthesis in stars. Here we discuss two recent theoretical developments in heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The first topic is a generalization of the Wong formula for fusion cross sections in a single-channel problem. By introducing an energy dependence to the barrier parameters, we show that the generalized formula leads to results practically indistinguishable from a full quantal calculation, even for light symmetric systems such as $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, for which fusion cross sections show an oscillatory behavior. We then discuss a semi-microscopic modeling of heavy-ion fusion reactions, which combine the coupled-channels approach to the state-of-the-art nuclear structure calculations for low-lying collective motions. We apply this method to subbarrier fusion reactions of $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni and $^{40}$Ca+$^{58}$Ni systems, and discuss the role of anharmonicity of the low-lying vibrational motions.

# Recent developments in heavy-ion fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim
The generalized Wong formula, which introduces energy dependence to the barrier parameters (height, position, and curvature), yields fusion cross sections practically indistinguishable from full quantal calculations even for light symmetric systems like ¹²C+¹²C, where the standard Wong formula fails. The semi-microscopic approach combining coupled-channels calculations with multi-reference density functional theory shows that anharmonicity of low-lying vibrational motions in ⁵⁸Ni smears the fusion barrier distribution, leading to a somewhat better agreement with experimental data compared to the harmonic oscillator limit.

## Method
The authors first generalized the Wong formula by evaluating the barrier height, barrier position, and barrier curvature at the grazing angular momentum for each incident energy, rather than using the s-wave values. They then performed coupled-channels calculations for ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni and ⁴⁰Ca+⁵⁸Ni systems, using transition strengths among collective states obtained from multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) with the PC-PK1 interaction, instead of assuming harmonic oscillator phonon couplings. The MR-CDFT calculation provided relative transition strengths among collective levels, while an overall scaling factor was applied to match the experimental transition from the lowest-lying collective state to the ground state.

## Key numbers
- For the ¹²C+¹²C system, the standard Wong formula largely overestimates fusion cross sections, while the generalized energy-dependent Wong formula reproduces the exact quantal results; the oscillatory correction term is given by σ_osc = 2πR_E²(ℏΩ_E/E)e^{-ξ}sin(πl_g), where ξ = (ℏΩ_E/(2l_g+1))·(πμR_E²/ℏ²).
- In the ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni system, the harmonic oscillator limit overestimates fusion cross sections at the two lowest energies (below approximately 95 MeV), while the MR-CDFT calculations underpredict fusion cross sections around 95 MeV.
- For the ⁴⁰Ca+⁵⁸Ni system, the effect of channel coupling is much smaller than in ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni due to a smaller charge product Z_PZ_T.
- The MR-CDFT calculation for ⁵⁸Ni shows that the B(E2) value from the 0⁺₂ state to the 2⁺₁ state is much smaller than that for the 4⁺₁→2⁺₁ and 2⁺₂→2⁺₁ transitions, deviating from the harmonic oscillator prediction where all two-phonon triplet states have B(E2) values exactly twice that of the 2⁺₁→0⁺₁ transition.

## Context
This work extends the classic Wong formula (Wong, 1973) for fusion cross sections, which works well for heavy systems like ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴Sm but fails for light systems such as ¹²C+¹²C. The semi-microscopic approach builds on the coupled-channels framework (Hagino and Takigawa, 2012; Dasgupta et al., 1998) that has successfully explained subbarrier fusion enhancement for many systems. The use of multi-reference density functional theory (Bender et al., 2003; Yao et al., 2014) provides a natural way to describe anharmonic multi-phonon excitations beyond the harmonic oscillator approximation. This work enables future fully microscopic treatments of heavy-ion elastic and inelastic scattering using the double-folding approach, with potential applications to nuclear data and nuclear transmutation problems.
