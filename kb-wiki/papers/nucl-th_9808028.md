# Photopion reactions on deltas preexisting in nuclei

> Reactions A(γ, pi^+p) are considered to proceed through the formation of pion-proton pairs on delta constituents in nuclei. We develop the nonrelativistic operator for Δ^{++}(γ, pi^+)p process in an arbitrary frame. The calculated cross section for C12(γ, π^+p) reaction is compared to the existing experimental data.

# Photopion reactions on deltas preexisting in nuclei

## Key claim

The observed cross section for the $^{12}C(\gamma,\pi^+p)$ reaction can be explained, at least partially, as a signature of preexisting $\Delta^{++}$ isobar admixture in the $^{12}C$ ground state, rather than solely by final-state interaction (FSI) processes. However, the calculated cross section progressively underestimates the data with increasing photon energy, and the quantitative conclusions remain ambiguous due to uncertainties in model parameters, particularly the $\Delta^{++}$ magnetic moment.

## Method

The authors develop a nonrelativistic operator for the elementary $\gamma\Delta^{++}\to\pi^+p$ process in an arbitrary frame, constructed using a one-particle exchange model with diagrams for nucleon current, delta current, pion pole, and contact terms. This elementary amplitude is then implemented into a nuclear calculation for the $^{12}C(\gamma,\pi^+p)$ reaction using the impulse approximation, a closure relation to sum over residual nucleus states, and a momentum distribution for bound deltas in $^{12}C$ derived from nuclear matter calculations. The triple differential cross section is averaged over the experimental angular acceptance ($50^\circ \leq \theta_\pi \leq 130^\circ$, $10^\circ \leq \theta_p \leq 150^\circ$) and compared to the data of McKenz97.

## Key numbers

- $\pi N\Delta$ coupling constant: $f_\Delta^2/4\pi = 0.37$, obtained from the $\Delta$ decay.
- Monopole form factor cut-off mass: $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
- $\Delta^{++}$ magnetic moment: $\mu_{\Delta^{++}} = -2.3$ n.m. (from Chao74 bootstrap model), with alternative values of 5.6 n.m. (SU(6)) and $4.2 \pm 0.5$ n.m. (from $\pi^+p\to\pi^+p\gamma$ analysis by Lin91).
- Energy-dependent resonance width: $\Gamma_\Delta(W) = 115 (q_{\pi N}/q_\Delta)^3 (M_\Delta/W)$ MeV, with $q_\Delta = 1.64\,m_\pi$.
- Square-well radius of $^{12}C$: $R = 3.2$ fm.
- $\Delta$ admixture in nuclear matter: approximately 7% per nucleon (from Cenni89), compared to $N_\Delta \approx 3.7\%$ for nuclear matter (Day76) and generally less than 4% for finite nuclei (Aren78).
- Photon energy range considered: $240$ MeV $\leq E_\gamma \leq 400$ MeV.
- Calculated cross section peaks at pion kinetic energy $T_\pi = 90$ MeV.
- Varying $\mu_{\Delta^{++}}$ from $-2.3$ to $5.6$ n.m. increases the cross section by about a factor of 5.

## Context

This work extends the study of isobar (delta) constituents in nuclei, following the comprehensive reviews by Green (1976) and Arenhoevel (1978), and specifically addresses the isobar knock-out process proposed by Geramb (1969). It directly responds to the exclusive measurement of $^{12}C(\gamma,\pi^+p)$ by McKenz97, which the Valencia model (VM) of Oset (1992), attributing the yield solely to final-state interactions including charge exchange, markedly underestimates. The results suggest that both the preexisting delta mechanism and FSI processes contribute to the observed cross section, and the authors call for true exclusive (non-angle-averaged) measurements and experiments on light nuclei (deuteron, $^3H$) to disentangle these contributions.
