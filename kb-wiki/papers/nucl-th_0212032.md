# Microscopics of meson degrees of freedom in nucleons and mesons in nuclei - what can be seen in the process of quasielastic knockout of mesons by high-energy electrons

> Developed earlier concept of quasielastic knock out of pions from nucleons by high-energy electrons is propounded as a tool for checking microscopical model ($^3P_0$ - fluctuation) for decay of N to different channels $π+B$ and Preparata model of nucleus structure.

# Microscopics of meson degrees of freedom in nucleons and mesons in nuclei - what can be seen in the process of quasielastic knockout of mesons by high-energy electrons

## Key claim

Quasielastic knockout of pions from nucleons by high-energy electrons, in the kinematic regime where the "pion in flight" diagram dominates (longitudinal virtual photons with $Q^2 \ge 1$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$ and total invariant mass $W \ge 2$ GeV/$c$ to exclude resonance contributions), can extract the momentum distribution of pions inside the nucleon and their spectroscopic factors, thereby testing the $^3P_0$ fluctuation model for $N \to \pi + B$ decays and the Preparata model of coherent pion-nucleus structure.

## Method

The authors analyze the longitudinal cross section of pion electroproduction $\gamma^* p \to n \pi^+$, where the "pion in flight" diagram (pion pole in the $t$-channel) dominates at sufficiently high momentum transfer. They express the hadronic part of this diagram in terms of a pion momentum distribution wave function $\Psi_p^{n \pi}(k)$ normalized to a spectroscopic factor $S_p^{n \pi}$, and relate the longitudinal cross section to $|\Psi_p^{n \pi}(k)|^2$ times the pion electromagnetic form factor $F_{\gamma \pi \pi}^2(Q^2)$. The same formalism is extended to the knockout of vector mesons via transverse virtual photons, with subsequent "deexcitation" into pions. For nuclei, the approach is applied to distinguish the momentum distribution of pions injected by the Preparata coherent mechanism from that of independent nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic condition for dominance of the pion pole diagram: $Q^2 \ge 1$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$ and $W \ge 2$ GeV/$c$.
- The momentum distributions $|\Psi_p^{B \pi}(k)|^2$ are calculated for channels $\pi B = \pi N$, $\pi \Delta$, $\pi N^*$ (with $N^* = N(1535)$), and $\pi N^{**}$ (with $N^{**} = N(1440)$), shown in figures as functions of $|k|$ in GeV/$c$ with units (GeV/$c$)$^{-3}$.
- In the Preparata model for nuclei, the estimated energy gain from coherent $\Delta$-nucleon, $\Delta$-resonance, and pion vibrations is 60 MeV per nucleon.
- The momentum distribution of pions in nuclei in the Preparata model is shown (solid line) versus the independent nucleon model (dashed line), plotted as $|\Psi_p^{n \pi}(k)|^2$ in (GeV/$c$)$^{-3}$ versus $|k|$ in GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the quasielastic knockout concept, previously applied to protons from nuclei and electrons from atoms and molecules, to the knockout of mesons from nucleons and nuclei. It proposes to test the $^3P_0$ fluctuation model for meson-baryon coupling constants, which relates pion-baryon, kaon-baryon, and vector meson-baryon couplings on the same footing, as developed in the authors' earlier papers. For nuclei, it addresses the long-standing question of pions in nuclei (discussed by Ericson and collaborators in the 1970s-1980s) and specifically the Preparata model of coherent pion condensate (Preparata, 1990s), which differs from the earlier Migdal pion condensate concept (Migdal, 1970s) that is now considered not realized. The proposed measurement of pion momentum distributions via quasielastic knockout could distinguish between the Preparata collective mechanism and conventional independent-nucleon injection of pions.
