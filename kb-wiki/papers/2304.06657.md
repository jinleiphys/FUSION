# Least action description of dynamic pairing correlations in the fission of Curium and Californium isotopes based on the Gogny energy density functional

> The impact of dynamic pairing correlations and their interplay with Coulomb antipairing effects on the systematic of the spontaneous fission half-lives for the nuclei $^{240-250}$Cm and $^{240-250}$Cf is analyzed, using a hierarchy of approximations based on the parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional (EDF). First, the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation is used to compute deformed mean-field configurations, zero-point quantum corrections and collective inertias either by using the Slater approximation to Coulomb exchange and neglecting Coulomb antipairing or by fully considering the exchange and pairing channels of the Coulomb interaction. Next, the properties of the {\it{least action}} and {\it{least energy}} fission paths are compared. In the {\it{least action}} case, pairing is identified as the relevant degree of freedom in order to minimize the action entering the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation to the tunneling probability through the fission barrier. Irrespective of the treatment of Coulomb exchange and antipairing, it is shown that the {\it{least action}} path obtained taking into account the pairing degree of freedom leads to stronger pairing correlations that significantly reduce the spontaneous fission half-lives $t_{SF}$ improving thereby the comparison with the experiment by several orders of magnitude. It is also shown that the Coulomb antipairing effect is, to a large extent, washed out by the {\it{least action}} procedure and therefore the $t_{SF}$ values obtained by the two different treatments of the Coulomb exchange and pairing are of similar quality.

## Key claim

The least action (LA) approach, which treats pairing as a dynamic degree of freedom, produces stronger pairing correlations than the least energy (LE) method, and this significantly reduces spontaneous fission half-lives by several orders of magnitude, improving agreement with experiment. The Coulomb antipairing effect is largely washed out by the LA procedure, so half-lives obtained with the Slater approximation to Coulomb exchange (CESlater) and with full Coulomb exchange and pairing (CEE) are of similar quality.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional were performed for isotopes 240-250Cm and 240-250Cf, with constraints on quadrupole Q20 and octupole Q30 operators for LE paths, and additional constraints on total particle number fluctuation ΔN² for LA paths. Collective potentials were corrected for zero-point rotational and vibrational energies, and collective inertias were computed using both the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) and Adiabatic Time-Dependent HFB (ATDHFB) approaches. The WKB approximation was used to compute spontaneous fission half-lives from the action integral along one-dimensional Q20-projected fission paths.

## Key numbers

- For 248Cf, the LE GCM half-life with CESlater is log10(tSF) = 21.31 (tSF in seconds), while the LA GCM half-life with CESlater is log10(tSF) = 10.57, a reduction of over 10 orders of magnitude
- For 248Cf, the LE ATDHFB half-life with CESlater is log10(tSF) = 25.82, while the LA ATDHFB half-life with CESlater is log10(tSF) = 12.42
- For 248Cf, the LE GCM half-life with CEE is log10(tSF) = 29.01, while the LA GCM half-life with CEE is log10(tSF) = 13.90
- For 248Cf, the LE ATDHFB half-life with CEE is log10(tSF) = 36.73, while the LA ATDHFB half-life with CEE is log10(tSF) = 14.40
- For 248Cf, the CESlater LE inner barrier height is 11.74 MeV (GCM) and 11.05 MeV (ATDHFB); the CESlater LA inner barrier height is 16.70 MeV (GCM) and 18.17 MeV (ATDHFB)
- For 248Cf, the CEE LE inner barrier height is 12.67 MeV (GCM) and 11.72 MeV (ATDHFB); the CEE LA inner barrier height is 19.81 MeV (GCM) and 20.72 MeV (ATDHFB)
- The ground state minimum of 248Cf is at Q20 = 16 b, the fission isomer at Q20 = 48 b, and the outer barrier top at Q20 = 68 b

## Context

This work extends previous Gogny-EDF studies of dynamic pairing correlations in fission by Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo (2022) and earlier LA calculations by the same group, now applied systematically to Cm and Cf isotopes where experimental data exist. The results contradict the expectation that Coulomb antipairing effects (which quench pairing and increase half-lives) would dominate; instead, the LA procedure produces pairing correlations strong enough to largely cancel Coulomb antipairing. This enables more reliable predictions of spontaneous fission half-lives for heavy and superheavy nuclei without requiring exact treatment of Coulomb exchange and pairing, and suggests that the LE barrier heights commonly compared to experiment may not be the most relevant quantity for fission dynamics.
