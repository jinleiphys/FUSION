# Squeezed Fermions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Colliders

> Large back-to-back correlations of observable fermion -- anti-fermion pairs are predicted to appear, if the mass of the fermions is modified in a thermalized medium. The back-to-back correlations of protons and anti-protons are experimentally observable in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, similarly to the Andreev reflection of electrons off the boundary of a superconductor. While quantum statistics suppresses the probability of observing pairs of fermions with nearby momenta, the fermionic back-to-back correlations are positive and of similar strength to bosonic back-to-back correlations.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that back-to-back correlations (BBC) of fermion--anti-fermion pairs (e.g., proton--antiproton) will appear if the fermion mass is modified in a thermalized medium, and that these correlations are positive and similar in strength to bosonic BBC, provided the freeze-out is sufficiently sudden (e.g., within 2 fm/c) and the net baryon density is low.

## Method

The authors model a thermalized gas of quasi-particles with an in-medium mass shift, using a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation to relate the in-medium creation/annihilation operators to the free-space ones. They evaluate the thermal averages of the single-particle and two-particle invariant momentum distributions for fermions and anti-fermions, assuming a sudden freeze-out and a momentum-independent scalar self-energy. The fermionic back-to-back correlation function is then computed analytically for an infinite homogeneous system, and numerical results are shown for proton--antiproton pairs at various net baryon densities and momenta.

## Key numbers

- The fermionic BBC for proton--antiproton pairs is shown for three net baryonic densities: normal nuclear matter density, one tenth of nuclear matter density, and $\rho_B = 0$ (baryon-free region), at a fixed temperature (not specified numerically in the text).
- For the numerical illustrations, a finite-time suppression factor with $\Delta t = 2$ fm/c is used, following Ref. [acg].
- The correlation is plotted for two typical momenta of thermal-looking proton spectra: $|{\bf k}| = 500$ MeV and $|{\bf k}| = 800$ MeV, for Pb + Pb collisions at CERN SPS.
- The BBC diverges as $C^{(+-)}_2({\bf k}, -{\bf k}) \propto 1 + 1/s^2_{{\bf k}} \rightarrow \infty$ for sufficiently large $|{\bf k}|$ and small mass shift.

## Context

This work extends the concept of back-to-back correlations, previously discovered for bosons (Asakawa and Csorgo, 1999; Asakawa, Csorgo, and Gyulassy, 1999), to the fermion sector, showing that fermion--anti-fermion pairs also exhibit such correlations when their in-medium mass is modified. The fermionic BBC is analogous to Andreev reflection in solid-state physics, but generalized to a scenario where superconductivity is suddenly switched off in the whole volume. The results suggest that the almost baryon-free mid-rapidity region of Au + Au collisions at RHIC is an ideal place to observe proton--antiproton or Lambda--anti-Lambda back-to-back correlations experimentally.
