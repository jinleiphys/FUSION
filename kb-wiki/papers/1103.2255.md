# Tensor properties of the nucleon

> We report in the present talk recent results of the tensor properties of the nucleon within the framework of the chiral quark-soliton model. The tensor and anomalous tensor magnetic form factors are calculated for the momentum transfer up to $Q^{2}\leq1\,\mathrm{GeV}^{2}$ and at a renormalization scale of $0.36\,\mathrm{GeV}^{2}$. The main results are summarized as follows: the flavor tensor charges of the nucleon are yielded as $δu=1.08$, $δd=-0.31$, $δs=-0.01$, while the up and down anomalous tensor magnetic moments are evaluated as $κ_{T}^{u}=3.56$ and $κ_{T}^{d}=1.83$, respectively. The strange anomalous tensor magnetic moment turns out to be $κ_{T}^{s}=0.2\sim -0.2$, compatible with zero. We discuss their physical implications, comparing them in particular with those from the lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM) predicts tensor charges and anomalous tensor magnetic moments for the nucleon that are broadly comparable to lattice QCD results, but the down-quark anomalous tensor magnetic moment (κ_T^d = 1.83) is smaller than the lattice value (2.35), and the Q^2-dependence of the form factors falls off more rapidly than lattice calculations, possibly due to the heavy pion mass used in the lattice work.

## Method

The authors computed the tensor charges and anomalous tensor magnetic form factors of the nucleon within the self-consistent SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM), a fully relativistic field-theoretic model with no adjustable parameters beyond the constituent quark mass fixed at 420 MeV. The calculations were performed for momentum transfer up to Q^2 ≤ 1 GeV^2 at a renormalization scale of 0.36 GeV^2, and the results were compared with lattice QCD data from Goeckeler et al. and Hagler et al.

## Key numbers

- Flavor tensor charges at renormalization scale 0.36 GeV^2: δu = 1.08, δd = -0.31, δs = -0.01
- Anomalous tensor magnetic moments: κ_T^u = 3.56, κ_T^d = 1.83, κ_T^s = 0.2 to -0.2 (compatible with zero)
- Ratio of up to down anomalous tensor magnetic moments: κ_T^u / κ_T^d = 1.95 (SU(3) χQSM) versus 1.58 (lattice)
- SU(3) χQSM singlet tensor charge g_T^0 = 0.76, triplet g_T^3 = 1.40, octet g_T^8 = 0.45
- SU(2) χQSM singlet tensor charge g_T^0 = 0.69, triplet g_T^3 = 1.45
- Lattice comparison for tensor charges (scaled to 0.36 GeV^2): δu = 1.05 ± 0.16, δd = -0.26 ± 0.01 (from Goeckeler et al.)
- Lattice comparison for anomalous tensor magnetic moments: κ_T^u = 3.70, κ_T^d = 2.35 (from Hagler et al.)

## Context

This work extends previous χQSM calculations of tensor charges (TensorSU2, TensorSU3) that suffered from symmetry-nonconserving quantization and gauge invariance breaking, and provides updated results following Ledwig (2010) and Ledwig (2010). The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations from Goeckeler et al. and Hagler et al., and with the non-relativistic quark model (NRQM) values. The present results can be used to describe the transverse spin structure of the nucleon, with corresponding investigation under way.
