# Can Transversity Be Measured?

> I review the ways that have been proposed to measure the quark transversity distribution in the nucleon. I then explain a proposal, developed by Xuemin Jin, Jian Tang and myself, to measure transversity through the final state interaction between two mesons ($ππ$, $K \bar K$, or $πK$) produced in the current fragmentation region in deep inelastic scattering on a transversely polarized nucleon.

## Key claim

The transversity distribution $\delta q(x,Q^2)$ can be measured through the final state interaction between two mesons ($\pi\pi$, $K\bar K$, or $\pi K$) produced in the current fragmentation region of deep inelastic scattering on a transversely polarized nucleon, provided the cross section is kept differential in the invariant mass and decay angles of the two-meson system to avoid averaging the final state interaction phase to zero.

## Method

The authors factor the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering process $eN\rightarrow e'\pi^+\pi^- X$ into a product of the quark distribution function, the hard partonic $eq\rightarrow e'q'$ cross section, a fragmentation function describing the $q\rightarrow (\sigma,\rho)$ transition, and the decay $(\sigma,\rho)\rightarrow\pi\pi$, all expressed as helicity density matrices. The final state interaction between the two pions is incorporated using measured $\pi\pi$ phase shifts $\delta_0$ and $\delta_1$ for $s$- and $p$-waves, and the interference between these partial waves generates a $\vec k_+ \times \vec k_- \cdot \vec S_\perp$ asymmetry that couples to the chiral-odd transversity distribution through a new chiral-odd interference fragmentation function $\delta\hat{q}_{_I}$.

## Key numbers

- The figure of merit for the $\pi\pi$ final state interaction, $\sin\delta_0 \sin\delta_1 \sin(\delta_0-\delta_1)$, reaches within 75% of its theoretical maximum value of $3\sqrt{3}/8 \approx 0.65$ in the $\rho$ meson mass region.
- The asymmetry formula contains a kinematic factor $\sqrt{6}(1-y)/[1+(1-y)^2]$ from the hard scattering, where $y = P\cdot q/P\cdot k$ is the standard DIS inelasticity variable.
- The polarization-dependent cross section is proportional to $\sin\Theta\cos\phi$, where $\Theta$ is the decay polar angle and $\phi$ is the azimuthal angle of the two-pion plane normal relative to the nucleon transverse spin.

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals to measure transversity, including the transversely polarized Drell-Yan process in $pp$ collisions (Ralston and Soper, 1979), twist-three pion production in DIS (Jaffe and Ji), and the Collins angle asymmetry (Collins, 1993). The two-meson interference approach avoids the suppression of twist-three mechanisms and the problem of phase averaging that plagues single-particle Collins effect measurements, since the meson-meson final state interaction phase remains fixed when summing over unobserved hadrons $X$. The analysis enables extraction of a charge-squared weighted quark transversity distribution at leading twist using existing or near-future facilities such as HERMES and COMPASS with transversely polarized targets.
