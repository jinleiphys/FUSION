# Quantum scalar fields in de Sitter space from the nonperturbative renormalization group

> We investigate scalar field theories in de Sitter space by means of nonperturbative renormalization group techniques. We compute the functional flow equation for the effective potential of O(N) theories in the local potential approximation and we study the onset of curvature-induced effects as quantum fluctuations are progressively integrated out from subhorizon to superhorizon scales. This results in a dimensional reduction of the original action to an effective zero-dimensional Euclidean theory. We show that the latter is equivalent both to the late-time equilibrium state of the stochastic approach of Starobinsky and Yokoyama and to the effective theory for the zero mode on Euclidean de Sitter space. We investigate the immediate consequences of this dimensional reduction: symmetry restoration and dynamical mass generation.

## Key claim

The authors show that the nonperturbative renormalization group flow of O(N) scalar field theories in de Sitter space undergoes an effective dimensional reduction to a zero-dimensional Euclidean theory at superhorizon scales, provided the curvature of the effective potential satisfies $V_\kappa'' \ll d^2/4$; this reduction is equivalent to both the late-time equilibrium state of the stochastic approach of Starobinsky and Yokoyama and the effective theory for the zero mode on Euclidean de Sitter space.

## Method

The authors compute the functional flow equation (Wetterich equation) for the effective potential of O(N) scalar theories in the local potential approximation (LPA) on the expanding Poincaré patch of de Sitter space-time in D = d+1 dimensions. They employ a Litim-type infrared regulator that depends on physical momentum $p = -K\eta$ to preserve the affine subgroup of de Sitter symmetries, and derive the beta function $\dot V_\kappa = \beta(V_\kappa'',\kappa)$ by solving the mode function equation with Bunch-Davies vacuum initial conditions. The flow is studied from subhorizon scales ($\kappa \gg 1$) to superhorizon scales ($\kappa \ll 1$), and the resulting infrared beta function is shown to describe an effective zero-dimensional Euclidean field theory.

## Key numbers

- The beta function in the deep infrared regime ($\kappa \ll 1$, $|V_\kappa''| \ll 1$) takes the form $\beta(V_\kappa'',\kappa) \approx \frac{1}{\Omega_{D+1}} \frac{\kappa^2}{\kappa^2 + V_\kappa''}$, where $\Omega_{D+1} = 4\pi^{d/2+1}/[d\Gamma(d/2)]$ is the volume of the unit sphere $S_D$ in D = d+1 dimensions.
- For $V_\kappa'' = d^2/4$ ($\nu_\kappa = 0$), the function $B_d(0,\kappa)$ shows a double logarithmic enhancement: $B_d(0,\kappa) = \frac{4d^2}{\pi^2}\ln^2(\kappa/2) - \frac{8d}{\pi^2}\ln(\kappa/2) + O(\kappa^0)$.
- For $\nu \in \mathbb{R}^+$ and $\kappa \lesssim 1$, the enhancement becomes a power law: $B_d(\nu,\kappa) \approx d(d+2\nu)\frac{\Gamma^2(\nu)}{\pi^2} (2/\kappa)^{2\nu}[1+O(\kappa^2)]$.
- In the Minkowski regime ($\kappa \gg 1$ or $V_\kappa'' \gg 1$), the beta function reduces to $\beta(V_\kappa'',\kappa) \approx \frac{8C_d}{\pi} \frac{\kappa^{d+2}}{\sqrt{\kappa^2+V_\kappa''}}$, with $C_d = \pi \Omega_d /[16 d(2\pi)^d]$ and $\Omega_d = 2\pi^{d/2}/\Gamma(d/2)$.

## Context

This work extends the NPRG analysis of Serreau (2013) by studying the full flow of the effective potential from subhorizon to superhorizon scales, rather than only the infrared limit. The dimensional reduction result establishes a direct link between the NPRG approach, the stochastic approach of Starobinsky and Yokoyama (1994), and Euclidean de Sitter calculations (Rajaraman 2010, Beneke 2012, Benedetti 2014). The authors show that the effective zero-dimensional theory obtained from integrating out superhorizon modes is equivalent to the late-time stationary solution of the Focker-Planck equation from the stochastic approach, and also to the effective theory for the zero mode on the compact Euclidean de Sitter sphere. This enables future studies of symmetry restoration and dynamical mass generation in de Sitter space using the simpler zero-dimensional framework.
