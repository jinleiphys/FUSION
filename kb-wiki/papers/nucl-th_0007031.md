# Local and non-local equivalent potentials for p-12C scattering

> A Newton-Sabatier fixed energy inversion scheme has been used to equate inherently non-local p-${}^{12}$C potentials at a variety of energies to pion threshold, with exactly phase equivalent local ones. Those energy dependent local potentials then have been recast in the form of non-local Frahn-Lemmer interactions.

## Key claim

The Newton-Sabatier fixed energy inversion scheme can produce local potentials that are exactly phase-equivalent to inherently non-local p-¹²C optical potentials, but the resulting local potentials have shapes that are not characteristic of traditional Woods-Saxon forms, and the subsequent mapping back to Frahn-Lemmer non-local forms retains a residual energy dependence that is at odds with the Frahn-Lemmer prescription of an energy-independent non-local potential.

## Method

The authors used phase shifts from g-folding (non-local) optical potential calculations for p-¹²C scattering as input to a modified Newton-Sabatier fixed energy inversion scheme, which solves a system of linear-algebraic equations to extract both central and spin-orbit local potentials. They then recast the energy-dependent local potentials into non-local Frahn-Lemmer interactions using a method following Apagyi et al., which involves considering local equivalent potentials at pairs of energies to determine the characteristic functions U(r), F(r), and Υ(r) that define the non-local form.

## Key numbers

- Energies studied: 65, 100, 135, 160, 200, and 250 MeV for proton scattering from ¹²C
- The s-wave phase shifts from inversion differed from input values by less than 4% at 65 and 100 MeV
- The pole term inherent in the inversion method influences results only within approximately 0.5 fm of the origin
- Cross section reproductions spanned eight orders of magnitude, with small divergences only below 10⁻² mb/sr
- Analyzing power reproduction was very good out to a center-of-mass scattering angle of 60°
- Non-locality ranges σ = 0.7, 1.0, and 1.4 fm were tested in the Frahn-Lemmer mapping
- Energy pairs used for Frahn-Lemmer mapping: 100-135 MeV, 135-160 MeV, and 160-200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the g-folding non-local optical potential calculations of Deb et al. for p-¹²C scattering, which had previously given good predictions of elastic scattering data across 40 to 800 MeV. The inversion method follows the modified Newton-Sabatier scheme proposed by Lun et al., and the mapping to Frahn-Lemmer forms follows the approach of Apagyi et al. The resulting local potentials enable applications in multi-step reaction theories and scattering into the continuum where local forms are more practical, while the Frahn-Lemmer mapping provides a utilitarian non-local form from the energy variation of the local potentials.
