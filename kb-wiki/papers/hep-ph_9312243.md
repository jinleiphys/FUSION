# Pion Loop Contribution to the Electromagnetic Pion Charge Radius

> A phenomenological Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD, formalised in terms of a QCD based model field theory, is used to calculate the electromagnetic charge radius of the pion. The contributions from the quark core and pion loop, as defined in this approach, are identified and compared. It is shown explicitly that the divergence of the charge radius in the chiral limit is due to the pion loop and that, at the physical value of the pion mass, this loop contributes less than 15\% to $\langle r^2_π\rangle$; i.e. the quark core is the dominant determining characteristic for the pion. This suggests that quark based models which fail to reproduce the $\ln\,m_π$ divergence of $\langle r^2_π\rangle$ may nevertheless incorporate the dominant characteristic of the pion: its quark core.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic charge radius of the pion receives contributions from both a "quark core" and a pion loop, and at the physical pion mass the pion loop contributes less than 15% to the charge radius, meaning the quark core is the dominant characteristic; however, the logarithmic divergence of the charge radius in the chiral limit is entirely due to the pion loop, so quark-based models that fail to reproduce this divergence may still capture the dominant physics.

## Method

The authors used a phenomenological Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD, formalized as the Global Colour-symmetry Model (GCM), to calculate the electromagnetic charge radius of the pion. They computed the "quark core" contribution using a generalized impulse approximation with dressed quark propagators, a pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and a dressed quark-photon vertex satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity, then added the pion loop contribution using the leading-order π-π scattering amplitude from the GCM with a constituent-mass approximation for the quark propagator.

## Key numbers

- At the physical pion mass, the pion loop contributes less than 15% to ⟨r²_π⟩ (the electromagnetic charge radius of the pion)
- The generalized impulse approximation (quark core) yields r_π = 0.53 fm using the simple interpolating form with Λ₁ = 0.527 GeV and Λ₂ = 0.573 GeV, and r_π = 0.59 fm using a more sophisticated analysis from Ref. RCSI93
- The experimental value of the pion charge radius is r_π = 0.66 fm (Ref. SRA86)
- The generalized impulse approximation underestimates the charge radius by less than 15%
- The pion decay constant obtained from the model is f_π = 0.093 GeV (experimental value also 0.093 GeV)
- The effective constituent quark mass M_c = 0.31 GeV was determined by requiring the model to reproduce f_π = 0.093 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Global Colour-symmetry Model (GCM) approach of Ref. RCP88 and Ref. RCSI93, which had previously described π-π scattering amplitudes well up to 600 MeV using only the quark core. The calculation explicitly demonstrates that the logarithmic divergence of the charge radius in the chiral limit, which appears in Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) at O(E⁴) through one-pion loops, is due to the pion loop contribution, while the quark core contribution remains regular. This enables a clearer understanding of which aspects of pion structure are captured by quark-based models versus meson-cloud effects, and suggests that models failing to reproduce the ln m_π divergence may still incorporate the dominant quark core physics.
