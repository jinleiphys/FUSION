# Faddeev calculation of a $K^- p p$ quasi-bound state

> We report on the first genuinely three-body ${\bar K}NN - πΣN$ coupled-channel Faddeev calculation in search for quasi-bound states in the $K^- p p$ system. The main absorptivity in the $K^- p$ subsystem is accounted for by fitting to $K^- p$ data near threshold. Our calculation yields one such quasi-bound state, with $I=1/2$, $J^π=0^-$, bound in the range $B \sim 55-70$ MeV, with a width of $Γ\sim 95-110$ MeV. These results differ substantially from previous estimates, and are at odds with the $K^- p p \to Λp$ signal observed by the FINUDA collaboration.

## Key claim

The first genuinely three-body coupled-channel Faddeev calculation for the $K^-pp$ system yields one quasi-bound state with quantum numbers $I=1/2$, $J^\pi=0^-$, but with a binding energy of $B \sim 55-70$ MeV and width $\Gamma \sim 90-110$ MeV that differ substantially from previous estimates and are at odds with the FINUDA collaboration's observed $K^-pp \to \Lambda p$ signal.

## Method

The authors solved non-relativistic three-body Faddeev equations in momentum space using the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form, extended to include explicit coupling between the $\bar{K}NN$ and $\pi\Sigma N$ channels as $3\times3$ matrices in channel space. Separable approximations for the two-body $T$-matrices were used, with the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ coupled-channel potentials fitted to reproduce the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance position, the $K^-p$ branching ratio at rest, and the $K^-p$ scattering length from KEK data. The calculation assumed $s$-wave pairwise interactions, isospin $I=1/2$, total spin $S=0$, and total orbital angular momentum $L=0$, resulting in a system of nine coupled integral equations solved on the unphysical sheet to locate the three-body pole.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy range: $B \sim 55-70$ MeV below the $K^-pp$ threshold, with width $\Gamma \sim 90-110$ MeV for the $I=1/2$, $J^\pi=0^-$ quasi-bound state.
- For the test calculation with $\bar{K}NN$ channels only (no coupling to $\pi\Sigma N$), a zero-width bound state was found at $E_{\bar{K}NN} = -43.7$ MeV below the $\bar{K}NN$ threshold.
- For the input $a_{K^-p}=(-0.78+i0.49)$ fm, the full calculation gave $E_{\bar{K}NN}^{(i)} = -55.8 - i49.1$ MeV (with $\Sigma N$ set i) and $E_{\bar{K}NN}^{(ii)} = -56.2 - i50.1$ MeV (set ii).
- For $a_{K^-p}=(-0.78+i0.65)$ fm, results were $E_{\bar{K}NN}^{(i)} = -69.4 - i46.8$ MeV and $E_{\bar{K}NN}^{(ii)} = -70.0 - i47.9$ MeV.
- For $a_{K^-p}=(-0.70+i0.60)$ fm, results were $E_{\bar{K}NN}^{(i)} = -66.0 - i54.7$ MeV and $E_{\bar{K}NN}^{(ii)} = -66.5 - i55.8$ MeV.
- The $\Sigma N$ interaction contributed only about 3 MeV to binding (less than 6%) and up to 2 MeV to width (less than 2%).
- Varying the range parameter $\beta$ from 3 to 4 fm$^{-1}$ changed binding by about 3 MeV and width from 115 MeV to 89 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002) who used an energy-independent complex $\bar{K}N$ potential and found a binding energy similar to the present calculation but a width of only 61 MeV, and by Nogami (1963) who estimated binding around 10 MeV. The results contradict the interpretation by the FINUDA collaboration (Agnello et al., 2005) of a $K^-pp$ bound state with $B \approx 115$ MeV and width $\Gamma \approx 67$ MeV from their $K^-pp \to \Lambda p$ signal. The calculation enables future extensions to include the $I=1$ $\pi\Lambda$ channel, $p$-wave interactions, and relativistic kinematics, though the authors expect no qualitative changes from such extensions.
