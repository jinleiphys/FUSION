# Time-evolution of fluctuations as signal of the phase transition dynamics in a QCD-assisted transport approach

> For the understanding of fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collisions it is crucial to develop quantitatively reliable dynamical descriptions which take the non-perturbative nature of QCD near the phase transition into account. We discuss a novel QCD-assisted transport approach based on non-equilibrium chiral fluid dynamics and the effective action of low energy QCD. In this framework, we study the time-evolution of fluctuation measures of the critical mode, notably the kurtosis, for a non-expanding system. From this, we can estimate the equilibration times of critical mode fluctuations in the QCD phase diagram. These allow us to identify both the phase boundary and the critical region near the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The authors develop a novel QCD-assisted transport approach that connects equilibrium QCD physics (beyond mean field) with non-equilibrium evolution, and find that while critical slowing down is clearly identifiable near the QCD critical point, the increase in equilibration time is only moderate (roughly a factor of two), suggesting that fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collisions may be dominated by equilibrium physics.

## Method

The authors compute equilibrium linear response functions (effective potential and spectral functions of the sigma meson) using the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) for a 2+1 flavour Quark-Meson model. They then solve a Langevin-type transport equation for the critical mode around these equilibrium states, studying the time-evolution of fluctuation measures (notably the kurtosis) for a non-expanding system subject to a sudden quench from high temperatures to specific points in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time unit: tau_0 approximately 0.4 fm/c, used to scale equilibration times in the QCD phase diagram
- Spectral function calculated at T=130 MeV, mu=0 MeV in the phase diagram, showing both transport peak and mass peak
- Near the critical point, the equilibration time enhancement is roughly a factor of two compared to points far from the critical endpoint

## Context

This work extends earlier non-equilibrium chiral fluid dynamics studies based on effective mean-field models for QCD (Nahrgang 2011, Herold 2016) by incorporating equilibrium physics from the Functional Renormalization Group beyond mean field. The approach enables systematic connection between equilibrium QCD phase structure and dynamical non-equilibrium evolution in heavy-ion collisions, with the next step being to improve from the temperature quench scenario toward the full dynamics of the underlying quantum field theory.
