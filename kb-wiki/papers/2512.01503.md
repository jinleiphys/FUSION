# Bayesian inferences on covariant density functionals from multimessenger astrophysical data: Influences of parametrizations of density dependent couplings

> Covariant density functionals have been successfully applied to the description of finite nuclei and dense nuclear matter. These functionals are often constructed by introducing density dependence into the nucleon-meson couplings, typically through functions that depend only on the vector, i.e., proper baryon density. In this work, we employ a Bayesian framework to investigate how different parametrizations, characterized by distinct functional forms and by their dependencies on vector and scalar densities, affect the properties of dense matter and compact stars. Our analysis demonstrates that although all considered parametrizations yield broadly comparable inferences, the differences in the equation of state and the symmetry energy remain significant at suprasaturation densities, reflecting the sensitivity to the chosen functional form of the density dependence. We find that allowing the nuclear saturation properties in the isoscalar channel, including the skewness coefficient $Q_{sat}$, to be freely adjusted provides adequate flexibility for the current modeling of nuclear and neutron star matter. In contrast, the isovector channel requires further refinement, with freedom extended at least up to the curvature coefficient $K_{sym}$ to capture variations in the symmetry energy and particle composition at high densities. This work advances prior studies by implementing a rational-function parametrization of the density dependence, informed and constrained by multimessenger astrophysical observations.

# Bayesian inferences on covariant density functionals from multimessenger astrophysical data: Influences of parametrizations of density dependent couplings

## Key claim

Although all considered parametrizations of density-dependent couplings in covariant density functionals yield broadly comparable inferences, the differences in the equation of state and symmetry energy remain significant at suprasaturation densities, reflecting sensitivity to the chosen functional form. The isoscalar channel with freedom up to the skewness coefficient Qsat provides adequate flexibility for current modeling, but the isovector channel requires freedom extended at least up to the curvature coefficient Ksym to capture variations in symmetry energy and particle composition at high densities.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference framework comparing five meson-exchange based covariant density functional models (VRE, MRE, MRE2, VRR, VRR2) that differ in whether the density dependence uses vector or scalar density, and whether the functional form for the isoscalar and isovector meson-nucleon couplings is rational or exponential. The models were constrained by identical multiphysics data: nuclear saturation properties, chiral effective field theory neutron matter calculations up to 1.5-2 times saturation density, pulsar mass measurements, gravitational wave tidal deformabilities from GW170817 and GW190425, and NICER mass-radius estimates for four millisecond pulsars.

## Key numbers

- The slope of symmetry energy from PREX-2 experiment is cited as 54 ± 8 MeV
- The chiral effective field theory constraints are valid up to approximately 1.5-2 times the saturation density
- Perturbative QCD calculations are applicable at around 40 times the saturation density
- The crust-core transition density is taken as n_sat/2
- The massive pulsar PSR J0348+0432 mass measurement is used as a constraint
- NICER data includes the ~2.0 M_sun pulsar PSR J0740+6620, the ~1.4 M_sun objects PSR J0030+0451 and J0437-4715, and the ~1.0 M_sun pulsar PSR J1231-1411

## Context

This work extends prior Bayesian analyses of density-dependent covariant density functionals (Lijj 2024c, Lijj 2025a, Lijj 2025b) by systematically comparing different functional forms for the density dependence of meson-nucleon couplings, including a rational-function parametrization for the isovector channel. The study contrasts with models having fewer degrees of freedom such as Malik (2022a), where Ksat and Qsat are strongly correlated by construction, and with point-coupling models analyzed by Xiacj (2024). The results enable future refinement of isovector channel parametrizations and highlight that with current astrophysical data, low-order saturation parameters remain only weakly constrained, requiring either more precise observations or complementary laboratory experiments.
