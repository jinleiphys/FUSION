# Microscopic Calculations of Nuclear EDM

> We carry out the microscopic calculations of the atomic EDM in deuterium, Xe and Hg atomic systems. Due to Schiff's theorem, we obtain the atomic EDM only from the finite nuclear size effects, but it is found that the atomic EDM is appreciably larger than expected. This is essentially due to the new mechanism in which the nuclear excitations are taken into account while atomic states stay in the ground state. The EDM of deuterium is found to be $d_D \simeq 0.017 d_n$ while the EDM of Xe and Hg become $d_{\rm Xe} \simeq 1.6 d_n$ and $d_{\rm Hg} \simeq -2.8 d_n$, respectively. It turns out that the new constraint on the neutron EDM becomes $d_n \simeq (0.37\pm 0.17 \pm 0.14)\times 10^{-28} {\rm e}\cdot {\rm cm}$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) in deuterium, Xe, and Hg arise from a new mechanism involving nuclear excitations as intermediate states while atomic electrons remain in the ground state, producing atomic EDMs appreciably larger than expected from the conventional Schiff moment approach. The key hedge is that this result depends on the finite nuclear size effects and the specific choice of nuclear intermediate states, and the authors note that the Schiff moment contribution from atomic excitations is extremely small and likely unobservable.

## Method

The authors performed second-order perturbation theory calculations of the atomic EDM starting from a microscopic Hamiltonian that includes the intrinsic EDM operators of electrons, protons, and neutrons. They considered three steps: first confirming Schiff shielding for a point nucleus, then calculating the finite-size contribution from atomic excitations (which yields the conventional Schiff moment result), and finally calculating the finite-size contribution from nuclear excitations with atomic electrons in the ground state. For deuterium, they used a simple deuteron wave function with binding energy parameter k_B = 46 MeV/c and evaluated the momentum-space integrals analytically. For Xe and Hg, they employed a single-particle shell model with the nuclear ground state described as a mixture of configurations, specifically for Xe using a neutron in the 3s_{1/2} orbital mixed with a 2d_{3/2}⊗2^+ configuration.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of atomic EDMs beyond the conventional Schiff moment approach of Khriplovich and Lamoreaux (1997) and Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984), which considered only atomic excitations and gave extremely small EDMs. The authors introduce a new mechanism where nuclear excited states serve as intermediate states in the second-order perturbation, producing atomic EDMs orders of magnitude larger than the Schiff moment contribution. This enables a new constraint on the neutron EDM that is three orders of magnitude more stringent than direct neutron measurements (from experiments around 2004), and suggests that the neutron EDM may be close to a finite measurable value. The authors note that their deuterium result (d_D ≃ 0.017 d_n) differs from the T- and P-odd nuclear force calculation of Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1986), who obtained d_D = 0.2 × 10^{-21} ξ e·cm, indicating that the two methods probe different physical mechanisms.
