# Shear viscosity of classical fields in scalar theory

> We investigate the shear viscosity of massless classical scalar fields in the $ϕ^4$ theory on a lattice by using the Green-Kubo formula. Based on the scaling property of the classical field, the shear viscosity is represented using a scaling function. Equilibrium expectation value of the time-correlation function of the energy-momentum tensor is evaluated as the ensemble average of the classical field configurations, whose time evolution is obtained by solving the classical equation of motion starting from the initial condition in thermal equilibrium. It is found that there are two distinct damping time scales in the time-correlation function, which is found to show damped oscillation behavior in the early stage around a slow monotonous decay with an exponential form, and the slow decay part is found to dominate the shear viscosity in the massless classical field theory. This kind of slow decay is also known to exist in the molecular dynamics simulation, then it may be a generic feature of dense matter.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of massless classical scalar fields in \(\phi^4\) theory is dominated by a slow exponential decay component in the time-correlation function of the energy-momentum tensor, which is absent in the massive case and makes the viscosity much larger than previously found for massive fields.

## Method

The authors performed classical field simulations on \(L^3\) lattices (with \(L = 16, 32, 64\)) for the massless \(\phi^4\) theory at couplings \(\lambda = 0.5, 1, 3, 5, 10, 30\). They generated thermal equilibrium configurations using a Langevin equation with diffusion coefficient \(\gamma = 0.1\) and temperature \(T = 1\), then switched off the Langevin terms and evolved the system with the classical equation of motion. The shear viscosity was extracted from the Green-Kubo formula by computing the time-correlation function of the off-diagonal energy-momentum tensor element \(\tau_{12}(t)\) and fitting its long-time exponential tail.

## Key numbers

- The fitted temperature from \(\langle |\pi(\bm{k})|^2 \rangle_{\rm eq}\) on a \(32^3\) lattice was \(T_{\rm fit} = 1.002 \pm 0.007\), confirming equilibrium within 0.2% relative error.
- The oscillation period in the early-stage damped oscillation was \(\tau_{\rm short} = 1.2\)–\(1.4\) (lattice units), corresponding to frequency \(\omega_{\rm short} \sim 4.5\)–\(5.2\).
- At \(\lambda = 1\) on a \(32^3\) lattice, the exponential decay rate was \(\Gamma/\lambda^2 = 5.2 \times 10^{-4}\) and the amplitude \(A = 12 \times 10^{-3}\), giving \(\eta = A/(T\Gamma) \approx 23\) in lattice units.
- The scaling function \(f_\eta(\lambda T) = \lambda^2 T \eta\) at small \(\lambda T\) on \(32^3\) and \(64^3\) lattices converged to \(f_\eta \simeq 17\).
- At \(\lambda T = 1\), the ratio \(\eta/T\) for the massless case exceeded that for the massive case (with \(m^2 = -0.5\)) by a factor greater than 20.

## Context

This work extends the study of shear viscosity in classical \(\phi^4\) theory by Homor (2015), which examined the massive case near the critical point of the second-order phase transition. The present massless case shows a qualitatively different behavior: a slow exponential decay in the time-correlation function that makes the shear viscosity much larger and more sensitive to the coupling constant. The authors note that the massless \(\phi^4\) theory is more relevant to classical Yang-Mills theory, which also has vanishing bare mass and scaling properties, and is used to describe the initial stage of high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future comparisons with full quantum simulations and provide a baseline for understanding transport in classical field theories.
