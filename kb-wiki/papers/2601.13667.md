# Pairing correlations, orientations and quantum fluctuations in one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions at sub-barrier energies

> This work investigates one- and two-neutron transfer in the $^{96}\text{Zr} + {}^{40}\text{Ca}$ reaction at sub-barrier energies using a microscopic framework based on time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TD-CDFT). Pairing correlations are incorporated via the time-dependent BCS approximation, which is shown to significantly enhance pair transfer, as evidenced by an increased two-neutron transfer probability. The oblate deformation of $^{96}$Zr causes the transfer probabilities to vary by orders of magnitude with orientation; a direct comparison with experiment is enabled by averaging results over thirteen systematically chosen orientations. While the orientation-averaged one-neutron transfer probabilities agree well with data, the two-neutron channel is suppressed below the Coulomb barrier. This suppression is attributed to missing quantum fluctuations in the semiclassical TD-CDFT approach. To test this, we employ the generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM), which confirms that quantum fluctuations are essential for an accurate description of sub-barrier two-neutron transfer dynamics.

## Key claim

The semiclassical time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TD-CDFT) with dynamical pairing correlations reproduces orientation-averaged one-neutron transfer probabilities for the 96Zr + 40Ca reaction at sub-barrier energies, but systematically underestimates two-neutron transfer probabilities; the generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) confirms that quantum fluctuations are essential for an accurate description of sub-barrier two-neutron transfer dynamics, though the current implementation does not include all relevant fluctuations.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TD-CDFT) calculations using the PC-PK1 relativistic density functional on a 3D lattice, incorporating pairing correlations via the time-dependent BCS approximation. They computed one- and two-neutron transfer probabilities for the 96Zr + 40Ca reaction at seven center-of-mass energies from 76 to 96 MeV, sampling 13 distinct orientations of the oblate 96Zr nucleus and averaging the results. To test the role of quantum fluctuations, they employed the generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) using five TD-CDFT trajectories with different initial orientations as generator states.

## Key numbers

- One- and two-neutron transfer probabilities calculated at center-of-mass energies E_c.m. = 76, 80, 84, 88, 92, 95, and 96 MeV, corresponding to impact parameters b = 2.76, 2.62, 2.50, 2.38, 2.28, 2.21, and 2.18 fm, respectively
- Coulomb barrier height for the system is E_c.m. = 96.5 MeV
- 96Zr ground state quadrupole deformation shifts from (beta=0.2, gamma=47 degrees) to (beta=0.2, gamma=60 degrees) when pairing correlations are included
- Pairing strengths: G_n = -0.31 MeV for neutrons, G_p = -0.35 MeV for protons in 96Zr
- Static calculations used a 24x24x24 lattice with 1 fm mesh spacing; dynamical calculations used a 60x24x30 lattice with the same spacing
- Time step of 0.2 fm/c; initial separation of 24 fm; fragments considered separated when center-of-mass distance exceeds 24 fm
- Transfer probabilities vary by orders of magnitude across different orientations of the oblate 96Zr nucleus
- For two-neutron transfer, the generalized TDGCM produces smaller probabilities than TD-CDFT in the incident energy range of 80 to 95 MeV, further from experimental data

## Context

This work extends previous experimental studies of few-nucleon transfer reactions near the Coulomb barrier, including 96Zr+40Ca (Corradi 2011), 116Sn+60Ni (Montanari 2014, Corradi 2022), 206Pb+118Sn (Szilner 2024), and 92Mo+54Fe (Mijatovic 2026), which consistently found two-nucleon transfer probabilities enhanced over predictions from uncorrelated sequential transfer models. The study addresses a systematic flaw in previous TDDFT calculations that overestimated one-neutron transfer while underestimating two-neutron transfer (Scamps 2015), and demonstrates that quantum fluctuations beyond the semiclassical TD-CDFT approach are necessary for describing sub-barrier pair transfer. The results enable future work toward a fully quantitative treatment of sub-barrier transfer dynamics by incorporating additional quantum fluctuations in the TDGCM framework.
