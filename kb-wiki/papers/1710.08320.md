# Masses and decay widths of scalar $D_0$ and $D_{s0}$ mesons in strange hadronic medium

> Masses and decay constants of scalar $D_0$ and $D_{s0}$ mesons in isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter at finite temperature are evaluated using QCD sum rules and chiral SU(3) model. In-medium light quark condensates, $\left\langle \bar{u}u\right\rangle_{Žü_{B}}$ and $\left\langle \bar{d}d\right\rangle_{Žü_{B}}$, the strange quark condensates, $\left\langle \bar{s}s\right\rangle_{Žü_{B}}$, and the gluon condensates, $\left\langle \frac{╬▒_{s}}ŽĆ {G^a}_{╬╝╬Į} {G^a}^{╬╝╬Į} \right\rangle_{Žü_{B}}$, needed in QCD sum rule calculations are evaluated using chiral SU(3) model. As an application, we calculate the in-medium partial decay width of scalar $D_0$ ($D_{s0}$) meson decaying to $D$ + $ŽĆ$ ($D_s$+$ŽĆ$) pseudoscalar mesons using $^3 P_0$ model. The medium effects in their decay widths are assimilated through the modification in the masses of these mesons. These results may be helpful to understand the possible outcomes of the future experiments like CBM and PANDA under the FAIR facility where the study of charmed hadrons is one of major goal.

## Key claim

The masses and decay constants of scalar $D_0$ and $D_{s0}$ mesons in isospin-asymmetric strange hadronic matter at finite temperature are evaluated using QCD sum rules combined with the chiral SU(3) model, with the medium effects on the partial decay widths of these mesons to $D+\pi$ ($D_s+\pi$) then calculated using the $^3P_0$ model, where the medium modifications enter solely through the in-medium masses of the participating mesons.

## Method

The in-medium light quark condensates ($\langle \bar{u}u\rangle_{\rho_B}$, $\langle \bar{d}d\rangle_{\rho_B}$), strange quark condensate ($\langle \bar{s}s\rangle_{\rho_B}$), and gluon condensate ($\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^a_{\mu\nu} G^{a\mu\nu}\rangle_{\rho_B}$) are first computed using the chiral SU(3) model in mean-field approximation, solving coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields $\sigma$, $\zeta$, $\delta$, and the dilaton field $\chi$ at finite baryon density, temperature, strangeness fraction, and isospin asymmetry. These condensates are then used as input to QCD sum rules for the scalar currents of $D_0$ and $D_{s0}$ mesons, where the forward scattering amplitude is parametrized in terms of unknown parameters $a$ and $b$ and the Borel-transformed sum rule is solved to extract the in-medium mass shifts and decay constant shifts. Finally, the in-medium partial decay widths for $D_0 \to D+\pi$ and $D_{s0} \to D_s+\pi$ are computed using the $^3P_0$ model, incorporating the medium-modified masses of $D_0$, $D_{s0}$, $D$, $D_s$, and $\pi$ mesons.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium mass shift $\delta m_{D_0/D_{s0}}$ is given by $\delta m = 2\pi \frac{m_N + m_{D_0/D_{s0}}}{m_N m_{D_0/D_{s0}}} \rho_B a_{D_0/D_{s0}}$, where $a_{D_0/D_{s0}}$ is the scattering length and $\rho_B$ is the baryon density.
- The in-medium decay constant shift $\delta f_{D_0/D_{s0}}$ is given by $\delta f = \frac{m_c^2}{2 f_{D_0/D_{s0}} m^4} \left( \frac{b \rho_B}{2 m_N} - \frac{4 f_{D_0/D_{s0}}^2 m_{D_0/D_{s0}}^3 \delta m}{m_c^2} \right)$, where $b$ is a parameter from the spectral density and $m_c$ is the charm quark mass.
- The nucleon expectation value of the mixed condensate is taken as $\langle \bar{q} g_s \sigma G q \rangle_{\rho_B} = \lambda^2 \langle \bar{q} q \rangle_{\rho_B} + 3.0 \text{ GeV}^2 \rho_B$.
- The nucleon expectation value of the twist-4 condensate is taken as $\langle \bar{q} i D_0 i D_0 q \rangle_{\rho_B} + \frac{1}{8} \langle \bar{q} g_s \sigma G q \rangle_{\rho_B} = 0.3 \text{ GeV}^2 \rho_B$.
- The values used for $\langle u^\dagger i D_0 u \rangle_N$ and $\langle s^\dagger i D_0 s \rangle_N$ are 0.18 GeV$^2$ $\rho_B$ and 0.018 GeV$^2$ $\rho_B$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum-rule analysis of scalar $D_0$ mesons in nuclear matter (Wang, 2015; Wang, 2016) to include both $D_0$ and $D_{s0}$ mesons in a strange, isospin-asymmetric hadronic medium at finite temperature, using the chiral SU(3) model to compute the in-medium condensates rather than the linear density approximation. It also builds on prior studies of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector $D$ mesons in the same framework (Chhabra and Kumar, 2017) and on the self-consistent coupled-channel study of scalar charm resonances $D_{s0}(2317)$ and $D_0(2400)$ (Tolos et al., 2008). The results are intended to inform future experiments at FAIR (CBM and PANDA) and J-PARC, where the in-medium behavior of charmed mesons is a key objective.
