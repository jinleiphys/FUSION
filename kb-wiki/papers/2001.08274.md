# Gauge boson mass as regulator

> Small-x divergences of Abelian gauge theory in the front form of Hamiltonian dynamics are regulated using a mass parameter for gauge bosons, introduced through a mechanism analogous to the spontaneous breaking of global gauge symmetry. A corresponding family of ultraviolet and infrared finite scale-dependent renormalized Hamiltonians, is calculable order-by-order using the renormalization group procedure for effective particles. The second-order terms described here suggest the magnitude of mass corrections that may be involved in resolving the small-x parton and front-form vacuum and zero-mode problems, assuming that the gauge boson mass that counts does not exceed the current upper bound on the photon mass.

## Key claim

Small-x divergences in front-form Abelian gauge theory can be regulated by introducing a gauge boson mass parameter via a mechanism analogous to spontaneous breaking of global gauge symmetry, but the practical viability of this approach depends on the gauge boson mass not exceeding the current upper bound on the photon mass.

## Method

The author applies the renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) to Soper's front-form version of massive QED, starting from the canonical Hamiltonian and solving a double-commutator flow equation to obtain scale-dependent effective Hamiltonians. The regularization is achieved through RGPEP form factors that depend on the gauge boson mass parameter κ, which enters the invariant mass squared of intermediate states through terms like (k⊥² + κ²)/x. Second-order mass-squared corrections for fermions and both types of gauge bosons (transverse A and longitudinal B) are computed analytically in terms of integrals involving complementary error functions and incomplete gamma functions.

## Key numbers

* For κ = m (boson mass equal to fermion mass) and effective particle size s = t^(1/4) with s·m = 1, the fermion mass-squared correction is g²δm²/m² = 3.19 × 10⁻¹³
* For κ = m and s·m = 0.1, the fermion mass-squared correction grows to g²δm²/m² = 3.67 × 10⁻¹
* For κ = m and s·m = 0.001, the fermion mass-squared correction reaches g²δm²/m² = 1.71 × 10⁴
* For κ = 10⁻²⁵ m (photon mass upper bound scale) and s·m = 1, the fermion mass-squared correction is g²δm²/m² = 1.62 × 10⁻¹
* For κ = 10⁻²⁵ m and s·m = 10⁻⁶, the fermion mass-squared correction becomes g²δm²/m² = 2.05 × 10¹¹
* For κ = 10⁻²⁵ m and s·m = 1, the transverse boson A mass-squared correction is g²δκ_A²/m² = 1.39 × 10⁻⁴
* For κ = 10⁻²⁵ m and s·m = 1, the longitudinal boson B mass-squared correction is g²δκ_B²/m² = 3.17 × 10⁻⁷⁰
* The coupling constant is set to α_g = g²/(4π) = 1/137 throughout

## Context

This work extends the RGPEP approach developed by Glazek and Wilson (1993) and Wegner's flow equation method to the specific problem of small-x divergences in front-form gauge theories, building on Soper's (1979) massive QED formulation. The mass regularization mechanism is analogous to the Higgs mechanism (Higgs, 1964; Englert and Brout, 1964) but applied to global rather than local symmetry breaking. The results suggest that the hierarchy problem of large mass corrections can be resolved by the finite size of effective particles, and the author indicates that non-Abelian gauge theories with infinitesimally spontaneously broken global gauge symmetry will be discussed in future work.
