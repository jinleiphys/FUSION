# Medium-induced optical effects for prompt photons

> Electromagnetic aspects of a QCD matter have been a hot topic in recent years. High-energy heavy-ion experiments revealed that flow harmonics of direct photons are not explained by most hydrodynamic models. In this work I discuss possible effects of refraction by the hot medium since it can work as a lens which converts its geometrical anisotropy into momentum anisotropy of photons. Then elliptic flow and higher-order harmonics of prompt photons are estimated numerically. The results indicate that they could be small but have phenomenologically non-trivial consequences.

## Key claim
Medium-induced refraction of prompt photons in the quark-gluon plasma can generate small but phenomenologically non-trivial elliptic flow and higher-order flow harmonics, though the effect may not be large enough to fully explain the observed direct photon flow puzzle.

## Method
The author models the hot QCD medium as an optical lens with a refractive index parametrized as \(n^2(T,\omega) = 1 - \omega_p^2(T)/\omega^2\), where \(\omega_p\) is the plasma frequency. Prompt photons are generated from a Monte-Carlo Glauber initial condition and propagated through a (2+1)-dimensional inviscid hydrodynamic medium using Fermat's principle, with the temperature gradient field smoothed by bilinear interpolation to avoid lattice artifacts. The flow harmonics are then computed from the resulting azimuthal momentum distributions.

## Key numbers
- Prompt photon elliptic flow \(v_2\) is calculated for impact parameter \(b = 6\) fm with refraction parameters \(a = 0, 0.5, 1, 2\) (where \(\omega_p^2 = a^2 T^2\)), showing positive values above \(p_T \sim \omega_p\).
- For \(a = 1\) and \(b = 6\) fm, the initial geometrical anisotropies are \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.202\), \(\varepsilon_3 = 0.146\), \(\varepsilon_4 = 0.166\), and \(\varepsilon_5 = 0.185\).
- The medium is transparent for \(p_T > 0.5\) GeV based on RHIC data, implying \(a < 1\)-\(2\).
- The plasma frequency at \(T \sim 300\) MeV is estimated as \(\omega_p(T) \sim 10^{-1}\) GeV from hard thermal loop calculations.
- The typical medium lifetime is \(\tau \sim 10\) fm/\(c\) and the thermalization time is \(\tau_\mathrm{th} \sim 10^{-1}\)-\(1\) fm/\(c\).

## Context
This work addresses the "photon \(v_n\) puzzle" observed at RHIC and LHC (Adare 2011, Lohner 2012), where direct photon flow harmonics exceed hydrodynamic predictions. It extends prior studies of direct photon flow (Chatterjee 2005-2014, Holopainen 2011, Shen 2013) by introducing a refractive mechanism that converts spatial anisotropy into momentum anisotropy, complementing thermal photon enhancement mechanisms. The approach enables future investigation of thermal photon refraction and constraints on the plasma frequency from ultra-low momentum photon spectra.
