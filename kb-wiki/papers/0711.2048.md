# Nucleon Structure from Lattice QCD

> Recent advances in lattice field theory, in computer technology and in chiral perturbation theory have enabled lattice QCD to emerge as a powerful quantitative tool in understanding hadron structure. I describe recent progress in the computation of the nucleon form factors and moments of parton distribution functions, before proceeding to describe lattice studies of the Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs). In particular, I show how lattice studies of GPDs contribute to building a three-dimensional picture of the proton. I conclude by describing the prospects for studying the structure of resonances from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD, combined with chiral perturbation theory, can now provide quantitative predictions for nucleon structure, but the results still require chiral extrapolations from pion masses as low as 360 MeV down to the physical point, and the disconnected contributions (which are computationally demanding) are omitted in most calculations of flavour non-singlet quantities.

## Method

The authors use a hybrid lattice approach: gauge configurations generated with 2+1 flavours of Asqtad dynamical quarks (by the MILC Collaboration) are combined with domain-wall valence quarks on HYP-smeared lattices. Nucleon structure is extracted from three-point correlation functions, where the operator of interest is inserted between an interpolating source and sink, and the matrix element is isolated after the ground state dominates at large Euclidean time separations. Moments of parton distributions and generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are obtained via the operator product expansion, which relates light-cone correlation functions to local operators measurable on a Euclidean lattice.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses down to around 360 MeV are used in the calculations.
- The isovector charge radius is extracted from the slope of the form factor F1 at Q^2 = 0; a naive linear fit in m_pi^2 would lie far below the experimental points, demonstrating the need for chiral extrapolation.
- The axial-vector charge g_A is computed and extrapolated to the physical point; the extrapolated lattice data are consistent with the experimental value.
- The ratio of electric-to-magnetic (EMR) and Coulomb-to-magnetic (CMR) transition form factors for the Delta-to-N transition are calculated with sufficient accuracy to exclude a zero value, indicating deformations in the N-Delta system.

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure (Mathur 1999) and benchmarks against experimental parametrizations (Kelly 2004). It builds on the hybrid Asqtad/DWF approach developed by the LHP Collaboration (Edwards 2006) and parallels efforts by the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration using two flavours of Wilson fermions (Gockeler 2003, 2007). The results enable a three-dimensional picture of the proton via GPDs, inform the decomposition of nucleon spin into quark orbital angular momentum (Ji 1996), and open the door to studying the structure of resonances such as the Roper (Aznauryan 2007).
