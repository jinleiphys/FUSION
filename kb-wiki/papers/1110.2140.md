# Forward-backward flow correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss the torque effect in the initial fireball formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, manifesting itself, on the event-by-event basis, in a relative angle between the principal axes of the transverse momentum distributions in the forward and backward rapidity regions. The torque follows from two natural features: 1) the sources of particles (e.g. wounded nucleons) emit predominantly in their forward hemispheres, and 2) there exist fluctuations in the transverse distribution of sources from the two colliding nuclei. On the average, the standard event-by-event deviation of the relative torque angle is about 20 degrees for the central and 10 degrees for the mid-peripheral collisions. The hydrodynamic expansion of a torqued fireball leads to a torqued collective flow of the fluid, which, in turn, yields torqued principal axes of the transverse-momentumdistributions at different rapidities. We discuss experimental measures based on cumulants involving particles in different rapidity regions, which allow for a quantitative extraction of the effect from the experimental data. We estimate the non-flow contributions from resonance decays with the help of THERMINATOR.

# Forward-backward flow correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The torque effect in the initial fireball, a relative angle between the principal axes of transverse momentum distributions in forward and backward rapidity regions, arises from the combination of forward-biased emission from wounded nucleons and event-by-event fluctuations in the transverse distribution of sources from the two colliding nuclei. The effect is estimated to produce a standard deviation of the relative torque angle of about 20 degrees for central collisions and 10 degrees for mid-peripheral collisions, but the authors caution that statistical hadronization noise and non-flow contributions from resonance decays must be carefully separated via cumulant measures.

## Method
The authors used a mixed Glauber initial-state model with 14% binary collisions added to wounded nucleons, then evolved the fireball with perfect 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamics using a realistic equation of state. They computed cumulant-based observables involving particles in forward and backward rapidity windows, and simulated the final hadron distributions with the THERMINATOR event generator for Au+Au collisions at the highest RHIC energy, comparing cases with and without the imposed torque.

## Key numbers
- Standard deviation of the relative torque angle between forward (eta_parallel ~ 3) and backward (eta_parallel ~ -3) regions: ~20 degrees for most central collisions, ~10 degrees for mid-central and mid-peripheral Au+Au collisions at the highest RHIC energies.
- For centrality 20-25%, the torque angle was fixed to a typical value of 8 degrees in the THERMINATOR simulations.
- THERMINATOR simulations used 100,000 events for Au+Au collisions at the highest RHIC energy for centrality 20-25%.
- Final charged pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons were selected with transverse momentum between 450 MeV and 3 GeV for the full simulation results.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of forward-backward rapidity correlations in heavy-ion collisions, building on the wounded nucleon model of Bialas (1976) and earlier analyses of d-Au and AA collisions by Bialas (2004) and Bzdak (2009). The torque effect is a new observable that probes event-by-event fluctuations of the longitudinal shape of the fireball at very early times, and the proposed cumulant measures enable experimental extraction from high-statistics RHIC data. The authors note that the effect should be generic for any model with asymmetric rapidity emission profiles, and that mid-central collisions (20-30% centrality) with exclusion of soft hadrons provide the best signal-to-noise ratio.
