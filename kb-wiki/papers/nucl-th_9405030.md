# Muon capture, continuum random phase approximation and in-medium renormalization of the axial-vector coupling constant

> We use the continuum random phase approximation to describe the muon capture on C-12, O-16 and Ca-40. We reproduce the experimental total capture rates on these nuclei to better than 10% using the free nucleon weak form factors and two different residual interactions. However, the calculated rates for the same residual interactions are significantly lower than the data if the in-medium quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant is employed.

## Key claim

The continuum random phase approximation (RPA) reproduces experimental total muon capture rates on C-12, O-16, and Ca-40 to better than 10% using free nucleon weak form factors, but the calculated rates become significantly lower than the data if the in-medium quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant (g_A reduced from 1.25 to about 1.0) is employed.

## Method

The authors applied the continuum random phase approximation (RPA) to calculate muon capture on C-12, O-16, and Ca-40, using two different residual interactions: a finite-range force based on the Bonn potential and a zero-range Landau-Migdal force. They evaluated the capture rate at each energy transfer for each multipole separately, using an accurate relativistic description of the initial bound muon and form factors with their q-dependence adopted from Beise (1991). The calculations were performed both with free nucleon form factors and with three models of in-medium renormalization of the axial-vector coupling constant g_A and the induced pseudoscalar coupling g_P.

## Key numbers

* Total muon capture rate on C-12: experiment 0.320 ± 0.01 × 10^5 s^-1; Bonn potential calculation 0.342 × 10^5 s^-1; Landau-Migdal calculation 0.334 × 10^5 s^-1
* Total muon capture rate on O-16: experiment 0.924 ± 0.01 × 10^5 s^-1; Bonn potential calculation 0.969 × 10^5 s^-1; Landau-Migdal calculation 0.919 × 10^5 s^-1
* Total muon capture rate on Ca-40: experiment 25.57 ± 0.14 × 10^5 s^-1; Bonn potential calculation 26.2 × 10^5 s^-1; Landau-Migdal calculation 23.4 × 10^5 s^-1
* With quenched g_A = 1.0 (Model 1), the calculated rates drop to: C-12 0.245 × 10^5 s^-1; O-16 0.682 × 10^5 s^-1; Ca-40 17.3 × 10^5 s^-1, all significantly below experimental values
* The 1^- and 2^- multipoles together contribute about 75% of the total O-16 capture rate and about 65% of the Ca-40 capture rate
* A 10% variation in the residual interaction strength changes the total muon capture rate by less than 3% for C-12 and Ca-40, and less than 2% for O-16

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA method, previously used successfully for nuclear response to weak and electromagnetic probes, to muon capture on T=0 nuclei. The results contradict the conclusion of Chiang, Oset, and Fernandez de Cordoba (1990), who found a factor-of-two reduction in capture rate from strong nuclear renormalization using a local density approximation. The calculations are compatible with the classical approach of Foldy and Walecka (1964) relating dipole capture to photo-absorption, and with the truncated shell model calculations of Eramzhyan et al. (1977) for O-16. The paper enables future work by establishing that muon capture rates, dominated by transitions to negative parity dipole and spin-dipole collective states, are best reproduced with free nucleon coupling constants, and that the quenching of g_A suggested by Gamow-Teller strength analyses would cause noticeable disagreement with data.
