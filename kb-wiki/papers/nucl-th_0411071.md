# On the Eigenvalue Problem of the su(1,1)-Algebra and the Coupling Scheme of Two su(1,1)-Spins

> After recapitulating the eigenvalue problem of the su(1,1)-algebra in the conventional form, the same problem is treated in an unconventional form, in which the eigenvalue is pure imaginary. Further, the coupling scheme of two su(1,1)-spins is discussed in the framework of two possibilities, in which certain new aspects appear. Finally, the coupling scheme developed in this paper is applied to a concrete example, which will serve boson realization of the so(4)- and the so(3,1)-algebra presented in the next paper.

## Key claim

The paper develops a coupling scheme for two su(1,1)-spins in two distinct types (a-type and b-type), where the b-type coupling requires the condition \(2x > 2y + 1 > 1\) for normalizability and produces a coupling rule \(t = x-y, x-y-1, \ldots, t_m\) that was not discussed in prior work; the unconventional eigenvalue problem for the su(1,1)-algebra yields pure imaginary eigenvalues for the operator \(\hat{T}^0\), which the authors argue can describe damped and amplified motion in a conservative formalism.

## Method

The authors first recapitulate the conventional eigenvalue problem of the su(1,1)-algebra, diagonalizing the Casimir operator \(\hat{\mathbf{T}}^2\) and \(\hat{T}_0\), and introduce the operator \(\hat{Q}_+\) for constructing eigenstates of \(\hat{T}^0 = (1/2)(\hat{T}_+ + \hat{T}_-)\). They then treat the unconventional eigenvalue problem where \(\hat{T}^0\) has pure imaginary eigenvalues \(it^0\), using a time-reversal conjugate bra state to define orthonormalization. For the coupling of two su(1,1)-spins, they define two sum forms: the a-type \(\hat{T}_{\pm,0} = \hat{X}_{\pm,0} + \hat{Y}_{\pm,0}\) and the b-type \(\tilde{T}_{\pm,0} = \hat{X}_{\pm,0} - \hat{Y}_{\mp,0}\), constructing the corresponding lowest-weight states and raising operators \(\hat{O}_+\) and \(\tilde{O}_+\). Finally, they apply the coupling scheme to a concrete boson realization involving three su(1,1)-algebras built from boson operators \(\hat{c}_{\pm,0}^*, \hat{c}_{\pm,0}\) and \(\hat{d}_0^*, \hat{d}_0\).

## Key numbers

* The normalization constant for the b-type lowest-weight state is \(N_{xy} = \frac{2x-1}{2x-2y-1}\), valid under the condition \(2x > 2y + 1 > 1\) (Section 4).
* For the a-type coupling, the total quantum number \(t\) takes values \(t = x+y, x+y+1, x+y+2, \cdots\) (Section 4).
* For the b-type coupling, \(t\) takes values \(t = x-y, x-y-1, \cdots, t_m\) where \(t_m\) is the minimum of \(t\) with \(0 < t_m \leq 1\) (Section 4).
* The normalization constant for the unconventional eigenstate is set as \(N_t = 2^{-2t}\) and \(N_{t,t^0} = \frac{(t^0-t)! \Gamma(t^0+t)}{\Gamma(2t)}\) (Section 3).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior development of the coupling scheme in the Holstein-Primakoff boson representation (Nishiyama et al., 2004, Ref. [7]) to a representation-independent form, and introduces the b-type coupling not covered in that earlier work. The unconventional eigenvalue problem with pure imaginary eigenvalues borrows from the treatment of damped and amplified harmonic oscillators in thermal circumstances (Nishiyama et al., 2004, Ref. [4]; also Refs. [4,5]). The results are intended to serve as the foundation for a boson realization of the so(4)- and so(3,1)-algebras in a subsequent paper, and the formalism for time evolution with complex eigenvalues suggests a possible description of damping and amplification in nuclear many-body systems.
