# Scalar meson mass from renormalized One Boson Exchange Potential

> We determine the mass and strength of the scalar meson from NN scattering data by renormalizing the One Boson Exchange Potential. This procedure provides a great insensitivity to the unknown short distance interaction making the vector mesons marginally important and allowing for SU(3) couplings in the 1S0 channel. The scalar meson parameters are tightly constrained by low energy np. We discuss whether this scalar should be compared to the recent findings based on the Roy equations analysis of pipi scattering.

## Key claim

By renormalizing the One Boson Exchange Potential (OBE) with a short-distance cutoff, the scalar meson mass and coupling become tightly constrained from low-energy np scattering data, and the omega meson becomes marginally important or irrelevant, in contrast to conventional fits which require fine-tuning and yield incompatible parameter sets.

## Method

The authors solve the s-wave Schrödinger equation for the \(^1S_0\) np channel using a renormalization procedure: they integrate the zero-energy wave function down to a cutoff radius \(r_c\) using the experimental scattering length \(\alpha_0\) as a boundary condition, impose self-adjointness to obtain the finite-energy wave function at \(r_c\), then integrate outward to determine the phase shift \(\delta_0(p)\), and finally take the limit \(r_c \to 0\) to remove cutoff dependence. The OBE potential includes pion, sigma, and omega exchanges, with the pion coupling fixed and the sigma mass, sigma-nucleon coupling, and omega-nucleon coupling treated as free parameters fitted to np scattering data from Stoks (1994).

## Key numbers

- Renormalized fit to np data yields \(m_\sigma = 490(18)\) MeV, \(g_{\sigma NN} = 8.8(6)\), \(g_{\omega NN} = 0(9)\) with \(\chi^2/DOF = 0.29\).
- Fixing \(g_{\omega NN} = 8.7\) (from SU(3) and KSFR relation) gives \(m_\sigma = 522(10)\) MeV, \(g_{\sigma NN} = 10.5(5)\) with \(\chi^2/DOF = 0.3\).
- Conventional (non-renormalized) fits yield two incompatible scenarios: \(m_\sigma = 477.0(5)\) MeV, \(g_{\sigma NN} = 8.76(4)\), \(g_{\omega NN} = 7.72(4)\) with \(\chi^2/DOF = 0.85\); and \(m_\sigma = 556.34(4)\) MeV, \(g_{\sigma NN} = 13.044(2)\), \(g_{\omega NN} = 12.952(2)\) with \(\chi^2/DOF = 0.52\).
- The np \(^1S_0\) scattering length is \(\alpha_0 \sim -23\) fm.
- Using a Bethe-Salpeter method with \(f_\pi = 92.3\) MeV and \(m_\pi = 139.6\) MeV, the \(\pi\pi\) scalar pole is found at \(m_\sigma - i\Gamma_\sigma/2 = 467 - i 192\) MeV.
- In the large \(N_c\) limit, the \(\pi\pi\) scalar mass approaches \(m_\sigma \to 506.8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005, 2007) to the specific case of the One Boson Exchange Potential, showing that the scalar meson parameters can be determined without the fine-tuning required in conventional OBE fits by Machleidt (1987, 2000). The results enable a comparison between the scalar meson extracted from NN scattering and the \(\sigma\) or \(f_0(600)\) resonance determined from \(\pi\pi\) scattering via Roy equations by Caprini, Colangelo, and Leutwyler (2006), suggesting that in the large \(N_c\) limit the two may correspond to the same state originally proposed by Johnson and Teller (1955).
