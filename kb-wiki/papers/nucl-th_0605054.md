# Suppression of statistical background in the event structure of away-side $Δϕ$ distribution

> An approach is proposed to analyze the azimuthal distribution of particles produced on the away side in heavy-ion collisions without background subtraction. Measures in terms of factorial moments are suggested that can suppress the statistical background, while giving clear distinction between one-jet and two-jet event structures on the away side. It is also possible to map the position and strength of the recoil jet to suitably chosen asymmetry moments.

## Key claim

Factorial moments (FMs) can suppress statistical background in the away-side azimuthal distribution event-by-event, making explicit background subtraction unnecessary, but only when the background density is below about 50 particles per radian in the interval of interest.

## Method

The authors define normalized factorial moments (NFMs) \(F_q\) for each event by dividing the horizontal average of factorial moments \(f_q\) by \(f_1^q\). They test the method on a simple Monte Carlo simulation where background particles are distributed randomly in \(\varphi\) (with \(-1.5 < \varphi < 1.5\)) and jet clusters of 5 particles are added in a small interval of width 0.04. They then compute event-averaged NFMs \(\langle F_q \rangle\) and asymmetry moments \(A_{qp} = \langle D_q^p \rangle / \langle S_q^p \rangle\) (where \(D_q = |F_q^+ - F_q^-|\) and \(S_q = F_q^+ + F_q^-\)) to distinguish one-jet from two-jet event structures.

## Key numbers

- For background-only events with \(N_i = 60\) particles, \(M = 30\) bins, and \({\cal N}_{\rm evt} = 2000\) events, \(\langle F_q \rangle\) for \(q=2,3,4\) are all less than but close to 1.
- For background plus one-jet and two-jet events under the same conditions, \(\langle F_q \rangle\) are distinctly higher than 1, especially for \(q > 2\).
- The method is effective for background densities \(dN/d\varphi|_{\rm bg} < 50\); at \(dN/d\varphi|_{\rm bg} \approx 200\) (corresponding to a lower bound of \(p_T^{\rm assoc} = 0.15\) GeV/c) the signal is at the 1% level and the method cannot be expected to work.
- For the asymmetry measure \(A_{qp}\) with \(q=3\) and 4, the dependence on \(p\) and bin size \(\delta\) is not sensitive, and one-jet and two-jet cases are clearly distinguishable.
- In the \(B_q\) analysis, for a case with background height 20 and double peaks at \(\varphi = 0.4\) and 0.8, \(B_4(\varphi_c)\) is nonzero but small; for background height 2 with a single peak at \(\varphi = 0.4\), \(B_4\) is around 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the use of factorial moments from earlier intermittency studies in multiparticle production (Bialas and Peschanski, 1986; Dremin, 1987) to the specific problem of away-side jet correlations in heavy-ion collisions. It addresses the difficulty that conventional background subtraction degrades signals when summing over events with diverse recoil scenarios (absorption, minijet, shock wave). The method enables quantitative comparison of one-jet and two-jet structures without event-by-event background subtraction, and could provide a common framework for analyzing RHIC jet correlation data under different experimental conditions and subtraction schemes.
