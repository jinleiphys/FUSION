# Schwinger-Keldysh approach to tunneling transport at a hadron-quark interface

> We theoretically discuss quantum tunneling transport and frictions at a hadron-quark matter interface based on the Schwinger-Keldysh approach combined with the tunneling Hamiltonian, which has been developed in the context of condensed matter physics. In the inner core of massive neutron stars, it is expected that cold quark matter appears at sufficiently high densities and hence exhibits color superconductivity, surrounded by nucleon superfluids at lower densities. The perturbative expressions of the tunneling current and the friction at the interface are obtained in terms of the non-equilibrium Green's functions. We demonstrate the DC Josephson current that occurs at the hadron-quark superfluid interface in the present scheme. Our framework can be applied to various conflagrations involving the interfaces relevant to astrophysical phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper derives perturbative expressions for the tunneling current and friction at a hadron-quark interface using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism combined with a tunneling Hamiltonian, but the demonstration of the DC Josephson current is performed only within second-order perturbation theory and under simplifying assumptions such as constant pairing gaps and momentum-independent tunneling amplitudes.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid model Hamiltonian consisting of relativistic baryon matter, quark matter, and a tunneling term that converts a color-singlet baryon into three quarks (and vice versa) via the antisymmetric color tensor. They then apply the Schwinger-Keldysh non-equilibrium Green's function formalism to derive perturbative expressions for the tunneling current operator and friction operator at the interface, expanding the S-matrix in powers of the tunneling amplitude. The Josephson current is evaluated at second order in the tunneling Hamiltonian using the Nambu-Gor'kov representation of the Green's functions, with the assumption of steady-state transport and the relativized quark model that ignores antiparticle and meson excitations.

## Key numbers

* The chemical potential bias driving the tunneling current is given by $\mu_B - 3\mu_Q$, where $\mu_B$ is the baryon chemical potential and $\mu_Q$ is the quark chemical potential.
* The phase difference of the gap parameters between the hadron and quark sides is $\Delta\phi = \phi_B - 3\phi_Q$, where $\Delta_B = |\Delta_B|e^{-i\phi_B}$ and $\Delta_Q = |\Delta_Q|e^{-i\phi_Q}$.
* The external bias parameter in the Josephson current expression is $\Delta\mu = \mu_B + \bm{v}_B \cdot \bm{K} - 3\mu_Q$, where $\bm{v}_B$ is the baryon matter velocity relative to the quark matter rest frame and $\bm{K}$ is the baryon momentum.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Keldysh approach, previously applied to superconducting systems in condensed matter physics (Zagoskin 1998, PhysRevB.54.7366) and strongly-interacting Fermi gases (PhysRevLett.118.105303, Tajima 2023), to the novel context of a hadron-quark interface in neutron stars. It addresses the challenge that the conventional Bogoliubov-de-Gennes approach is not straightforward for hadron-quark interfaces because baryon formation and particle-anti-particle creation occur in relativistic quark systems. The framework enables future studies of Josephson effects and Andreev reflection at interfaces between nucleon superfluids and color-superconducting quark matter, which are relevant to understanding neutron star cooling curves and pulsar glitches.
