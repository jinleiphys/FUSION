# Correlating confinement to topological fluctuations near the crossover transition in QCD

> We show the existence of strong (anti)correlations between the topological hot spots and the local values of the trace of the Polyakov loop in $2+1$ flavor QCD with physical quark mass, in the vicinity of the crossover transition corresponding to the simultaneous restoration of chiral symmetry and deconfinement. Using sophisticated lattice techniques, we have carefully identified the topological hot spots using quark zero modes and measured the short-distance fluctuations of the Polyakov loop about them, showing how the latter is repelled quite strongly around the peak of the zero modes. Though we could explain some aspects of these correlations within the instanton-dyon picture, our work sets the stage for a larger goal towards a systematic study of the role of different topological species that interact with the Polyakov loop, establishing the strong connection between topology and confinement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates strong local anticorrelations between topological hot spots (identified via quark zero modes) and the local value of the Polyakov loop in 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses, specifically in the temperature range just above the chiral crossover transition (1.1 Tc and 1.2 Tc), where the abstract's claim of "simultaneous restoration of chiral symmetry and deconfinement" is qualified by the fact that the study is limited to gauge configurations with topological charge |Q|=1 and does not include higher-charge sectors.

## Method

The authors used 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations generated by the HotQCD Collaboration using Möbius domain wall fermions and Iwasaki gauge action, with physical quark masses tuned to pion and kaon masses of 135 and 435 MeV respectively. They employed valence overlap fermions (which satisfy an exact index theorem) to identify topological zero modes on N_s=32, N_tau=8 lattices with spatial volume ~(4 fm)^3, at temperatures 1.1 Tc and 1.2 Tc (Tc=155(9) MeV). The local Polyakov loop was measured after 10 steps of hypercubic (HYP) smearing to remove ultraviolet fluctuations, and correlations were quantified using the observable C(Delta P, rho) which integrates the product of the zero mode density with the deviation of the local Polyakov loop from its spatial average.

## Key numbers

* Tc = 155(9) MeV for the ensembles used (pseudocritical temperature from Bhattacharya (2014))
* Lattice spacings in the range ~0.13-0.14 fm for the two temperatures studied
* Spatial volume ~(4 fm)^3, with N_s=32 sites per spatial direction and N_tau=8 temporal sites
* Goldstone pion mass = 135 MeV, kaon mass = 435 MeV (physical quark masses)
* Number of independent configurations with topological charge |Q|=1: 5 at 1.1 Tc, 12 at 1.2 Tc
* Residual chiral violation of domain wall configurations ~2x10^{-3}
* The real part of C(Delta P, rho) is distinctly finite and negative for all three fermion boundary phases (phi=pi, +/-pi/3) at both temperatures, while the imaginary part stays close to zero
* For L-dyons (phi=pi), the local Polyakov loop at the center is negative with real part -1/3 and imaginary part consistent with zero, matching the instanton-dyon prediction

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of instanton-dyons in QCD by Larsen (2018, 2019) and Bornyakov (2014, 2015, 2016), which identified different species of instanton-dyons and measured their separations, by directly correlating their positions with the local Polyakov loop. The results support the instanton-dyon picture of confinement proposed by Diakonov (2009) and earlier work by Shuryak (1982, 2014), while also providing a framework for future systematic studies of how different topological species (including vortices and monopoles) interact with the Polyakov loop to drive confinement from first principles.
