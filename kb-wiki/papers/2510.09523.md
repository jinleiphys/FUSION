# Probing the Dependence of Partonic Energy Loss on the Initial Energy Density of the Quark Gluon Plasma

> Considerable evidence now exists for partonic energy loss due to interaction with the hot, dense medium created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. A primary signal of this energy loss is the suppression of high transverse momentum $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ hadron yields in A-A collisions relative to appropriately scaled $pp$ collisions at the same energy. Measuring the collision energy dependence of this energy loss is vital to understanding the medium, but it is difficult to disentangle the medium-driven energy loss from the natural kinematic variance of the steeply-falling $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ spectra across different $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$. To decouple these effects, we utilize a phenomenologically motivated spectrum shift model to estimate the average transverse momentum loss $Δp_{\mathrm{T}}$ imparted on high $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ partons in A-A collisions, a proxy for the medium induced energy loss. We observe a striking correlation between $Δp_{\mathrm{T}}$ and Glauber-derived estimates of initial state energy density $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{Bj}}$, consistent across two orders of magnitude in collision energy for a variety of nuclear species. To access the path-length dependence of energy loss, we couple our model to geometric event shape estimates extracted from Glauber calculations to produce predictions for high-$p_{\mathrm{T}}$ hadron elliptic flow $v_2$ that agree reasonably with data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a striking correlation between the average transverse momentum loss (ΔpT) of high-pT partons and the Glauber-derived initial state energy density (εBj) of the quark-gluon plasma, which holds consistently across two orders of magnitude in collision energy and for multiple nuclear species. This correlation is extracted using a phenomenologically motivated spectrum shift model that decouples medium-driven energy loss from the natural kinematic variance of steeply-falling pT spectra across different √sNN.

## Method

The authors extracted the average transverse momentum loss ΔpT from experimentally measured charged particle pT spectra in pp and A-A collisions by fitting pp reference spectra to a Tsallis distribution, scaling by the nuclear overlap function TAA from Monte Carlo Glauber calculations, and then horizontally shifting the scaled pp fit to match the A-A data above a threshold pTmin determined from the local minimum of RAA. The initial energy density εBj was estimated using the Bjorken hydrodynamics limit from charged particle multiplicities dNch/dη and Glauber-derived transverse overlap areas A⊥, computed using eight different methods that grouped into two scaling classes (inclusive and width-based). To probe path-length dependence, the authors coupled their ΔpT model to geometric event shape estimates from Glauber calculations to predict high-pT hadron elliptic flow v2 assuming linear path-length dependent energy loss.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken energy density εBj was computed for collision systems spanning √sNN = 200 GeV (AuAu and CuCu at RHIC) to √sNN = 5.44 TeV (XeXe at LHC), covering approximately two orders of magnitude in collision energy.
- The Jacobian J for pseudorapidity-to-rapidity conversion ranges from 1.25 at √sNN = 200 GeV to 1.09 at √sNN = 5.44 TeV, with an assumed 3% uncertainty propagated to εBj.
- The QGP formation time τini was fixed at 0.6 fm/c for all collision energies (√sNN ≥ 200 GeV), and the constant bini was set to 15.5.
- The pT threshold pTmin was determined from the local minimum of RAA near pT ∼ 5 GeV/c in central collisions, above which collective effects are assumed negligible.
- The systematic uncertainty on ΔpT from the pT threshold choice was evaluated by varying the threshold by one pT bin above and below each dataset's local RAA minima.

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of partonic energy loss, including the Sloss measure first proposed by PHENIX (2004) and subsequent ΔpT methods used by Brewer (2018), Spousta (2015), and Arleo (2017). The analysis differs from prior approaches by assuming a fixed ΔpT across the high-pT range to estimate average energy loss for a given centrality, rather than a fractional energy loss. The correlation between ΔpT and εBj enables future studies to connect energy loss measurements directly to initial state properties of the QGP, while the v2 predictions provide a test of the path-length dependence assumption that can be compared with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, STAR, and PHENIX.
