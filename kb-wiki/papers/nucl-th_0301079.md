> invited talk at the International Workshop SCGT 02 "Strong Coupling Gauge Theories and Effective Field Theories", 10-13 December 2002, Nagoya, Japan

# Recent results in chiral nuclear dynamics

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (EFT) provides a systematic framework for describing nuclear forces and few-nucleon dynamics, but the three-nucleon force (3NF) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) requires two low-energy constants (LECs) that must be fixed from the triton binding energy and the nd doublet scattering length before parameter-free predictions can be made.

## Method
The authors apply chiral EFT to nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials by expanding S-matrix elements in powers of small external momenta and light quark masses, then solving the regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation for bound and scattering states. For three-nucleon systems, Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations are solved using the NNLO chiral 3NF with LECs fixed from triton data. For pion-deuteron scattering, a novel power counting is formulated that accounts for the deuteron binding momentum being significantly smaller than the pion mass.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy (BE) of ⁴He: 29.51 to 29.98 MeV for cutoff Λ = 500 to 600 MeV (empirical value corrected for isospin breaking: 29.8 ± 0.1 MeV)
- Deuteron BE in the chiral limit: 9.6 ± 1.9 +1.8/-1.0 MeV (physical value: 2.22456612(12) MeV)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment in the chiral limit: 0.247 ± 0.030 +0.008/-0.005 fm² (physical: 0.2859(3) fm²)
- Deuteron radius in the chiral limit: 1.266 ± 0.085 +0.044/-0.034 fm (physical: 1.9671(6) fm)
- Deuteron magnetic moment in the chiral limit: 0.820 ± 0.002 +0.003/-0.006 n.m. (physical: 0.8574382284(94) n.m.)
- S-wave scattering length in the chiral limit for ¹S₀: -4.1 ± 1.6 +0.0/-0.4 fm
- S-wave scattering length in the chiral limit for ³S₁: 1.5 ± 0.4 +0.2/-0.3 fm
- π-d scattering length from atomic-level shift: a_πd = (-0.0261 ± 0.0005) + i (0.0063 ± 0.0007) M_π⁻¹
- Extracted πN isovector scattering length a⁻: 0.0936 ± 0.0011 M_π⁻¹ (using bound state EFT hydrogen shift)
- Extracted πN isoscalar scattering length a⁺: -0.0029 ± 0.0009 M_π⁻¹ (using bound state EFT hydrogen shift)

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and developed further by Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000) for NN potentials. The three-nucleon force predictions are compared with conventional high-precision NN potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18) combined with non-chiral 3NFs (Tucson-Melbourne, Urbana IX). The pion-deuteron scattering analysis provides a model-independent extraction of πN scattering lengths that is consistent with the work of Ericson (2000) and enables improved constraints on QCD symmetry breaking. The chiral-limit calculations are intended for interpolating lattice gauge theory results, with the authors noting that lattice data for pion masses below 200 MeV are needed for stable interpolation to the physical pion mass.
