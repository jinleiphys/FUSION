# One percent determination of the primordial deuterium abundance

> We report a reanalysis of a near-pristine absorption system, located at a redshift z_abs=2.52564 toward the quasar Q1243+307, based on the combination of archival and new data obtained with the HIRES echelle spectrograph on the Keck telescope. This absorption system, which has an oxygen abundance [O/H]=-2.769+/-0.028 (~1/600 of the Solar abundance), is among the lowest metallicity systems currently known where a precise measurement of the deuterium abundance is afforded. Our detailed analysis of this system concludes, on the basis of eight D I absorption lines, that the deuterium abundance of this gas cloud is log_10(D/H) = -4.622+/-0.015, which is in very good agreement with the results previously reported by Kirkman et al. (2003), but with an improvement on the precision of this single measurement by a factor of ~3.5. Combining this new estimate with our previous sample of six high precision and homogeneously analyzed D/H measurements, we deduce that the primordial deuterium abundance is log_10(D/H)_P = -4.5974+/-0.0052 or, expressed as a linear quantity, (D/H)_P = (2.527+/-0.030)x10^-5; this value corresponds to a one percent determination of the primordial deuterium abundance. Combining our result with a BBN calculation that uses the latest nuclear physics input, we find that the baryon density derived from BBN agrees to within 2 sigma of the latest results from the Planck CMB data.

## Key claim

The primordial deuterium abundance is determined to one percent precision, (D/H)_P = (2.527 ± 0.030) × 10⁻⁵, based on a reanalysis of a near-pristine absorption system combined with six previous homogeneously analyzed measurements; the result agrees with the baryon density from Planck CMB data to within 2σ, but the paper does not claim a tension or new physics.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed a sub-damped Lyman-alpha system at redshift z_abs = 2.52564 toward the quasar Q1243+307 using both archival Keck HIRES data from 1999-2000 and new HIRES observations from 2016. They simultaneously fit the quasar continuum and absorption profiles of eight D I Lyman series lines, the H I Lyman series, and unblended metal lines across three independent datasets using χ² minimization, determining the D I and H I column densities from the weak D I equivalent widths and the damped Lyman-alpha profile respectively.

## Key numbers

- Oxygen abundance of the absorption system: [O/H] = −2.769 ± 0.028 (approximately 1/600 of solar abundance)
- Deuterium abundance of the individual gas cloud: log₁₀(D/H) = −4.622 ± 0.015, based on eight D I absorption lines
- Primordial deuterium abundance (combined sample of seven systems): log₁₀(D/H)_P = −4.5974 ± 0.0052, or (D/H)_P = (2.527 ± 0.030) × 10⁻⁵
- Precision improvement on the single measurement of this system relative to Kirkman et al. (2003): factor of approximately 3.5
- Instrumental resolution of the new data: v_FWHM = 6.28 ± 0.02 km s⁻¹, based on 2192 ThAr emission lines
- Signal-to-noise ratio of the new combined spectrum: S/N ≈ 80 per 2.5 km s⁻¹ pixel near the Lyman-alpha line, and S/N ≈ 25 near the Lyman limit at 3215 Å

## Context

This work extends the homogeneous D/H measurement program of Cooke et al. (2014, 2016) by adding a seventh high-precision system, improving upon the single-system measurement of Kirkman et al. (2003) by a factor of 3.5 in precision. The one-percent determination of the primordial deuterium abundance enables a test of Big Bang Nucleosynthesis predictions with the latest nuclear physics input, and when combined with Planck CMB data provides a consistency check on the standard cosmological model; the authors note a subtle but statistically insignificant decline of D/H with increasing metallicity that could be investigated further with future measurements.
