# Longitudinal dependence of B and D meson nuclear modifications in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC

> It is widely acknowledged that heavy flavor probes are sensitive to the properties of the quark-gluon plasma and are often considered an important tool for the plasma tomography studies. Forward rapidity observables can provide further insight on the dynamics of the medium due to the interplay between the medium size and the differences in the production spectra of heavy quark probes. In this proceedings we present the nuclear modification factor $R_\text{AA}$'s for B and D mesons, as well as heavy flavor leptons, in the rapidity range $-4.0 < y < 4.0$ obtained from relativistic Langevin equation with gluon radiation coupled with a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics medium background. We present comparison with experimental data at mid-rapidity as well as predictions for different rapidity ranges.

## Key claim
The nuclear modification factor R_AA for B and D mesons shows a larger suppression at forward rapidity compared to mid-rapidity at high transverse momentum, even though the medium size is smaller at forward rapidity, because the steeper initial heavy quark production spectra at large rapidity dominate over the path-length effect.

## Method
The authors coupled a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics medium background (from the CLVisc code) with a relativistic Langevin equation transport model that includes both collisional and radiative energy loss for heavy quarks. Heavy quarks were sampled from a Monte Carlo Glauber initial geometry with leading-order perturbative QCD production spectra including flavor excitation, pair production, and nuclear shadowing/anti-shadowing effects, and hadronization was treated via a hybrid fragmentation plus coalescence model.

## Key numbers
- D meson R_AA in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: calculations compared with STAR data show consistency for p_T >= 4 GeV at mid-rapidity.
- D meson R_AA in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV: good agreement with CMS data throughout the full p_T range at mid-rapidity.
- B meson R_AA predictions: same trend as D mesons, with larger suppression observed in larger rapidity bins at high p_T.
- Heavy flavor electron R_AA: good agreement with PHENIX data for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV at mid-rapidity; calculations slightly overestimate ALICE data for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- Heavy flavor muon R_AA: good agreement with ALICE forward-rapidity data for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV at high p_T.

## Context
This work extends prior heavy flavor tomography studies (Wang 1991, Qin 2015, Moore 2004) by providing the first systematic predictions for the rapidity dependence of B and D meson R_AA across RHIC and LHC energies. It builds on recent efforts to explore longitudinal dependence of heavy flavor observables (Das 2016, Chatterjee 2017, Nasim 2018, Zhang 2019, Adam 2019) and enables future experimental measurements at forward rapidity to constrain the interplay between medium size and initial production spectra in heavy quark energy loss models.
