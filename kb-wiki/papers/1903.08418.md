# Thermal quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations of stellar electron capture rates with the Skyrme effective interaction

> A microscopic thermodynamically consistent approach is applied to compute electron capture (EC) rates and cross sections on nuclei in hot stellar environments. The cross section calculations are based on the Donnelly-Walecka multipole expansion method for treatment of semi-leptonic processes in nuclei. To take into account thermal effects, we express the electron capture cross section in terms of temperature- and momentum-dependent spectral functions for respective multipole charge-changing operators. The spectral functions are computed by employing the self-consistent thermal quasiparticle RPA (TQRPA) with the Skyrme effective interaction. Three different Skyrme parametrizations (SkM$^*$, SGII and SLy4) are used to investigate thermal effects on EC for $^{56}$Fe and $^{78}$Ni. For $^{56}$Fe, the impact of thermally unblocked GT$_+$ transitions on EC is discussed and the results are compared with those from shell-model calculations. In particular, it is shown that for some temperature and density regimes the TQRPA rates exceed the shell-model rates due to violation of the Brink-Axel hypothesis within the TQRPA. For neutron-rich $^{78}$Ni the full momentum-dependence of multipole transition operators is considered and it is found that not only thermally unblocked allowed $1^+$ transitions but also thermally unblocked first-forbidden $1^-$ and $2^-$ transitions favour EC.

## Key claim

The authors show that for some temperature and density regimes, their TQRPA electron capture rates for \(^{56}\)Fe exceed those from shell-model calculations, specifically because the TQRPA violates the Brink-Axel hypothesis (which assumes GT\(_+\) strength distributions built on excited states are the same as for the ground state, shifted by excitation energy). For neutron-rich \(^{78}\)Ni, they find that thermally unblocked first-forbidden \(1^-\) and \(2^-\) transitions, in addition to allowed \(1^+\) transitions, favor electron capture when the full momentum-dependence of multipole transition operators is considered.

## Method

The authors compute electron capture cross sections and rates using the Donnelly-Walecka multipole expansion method, expressing the cross section in terms of temperature- and momentum-dependent spectral functions for charge-changing multipole operators. These spectral functions are calculated within the self-consistent thermal quasiparticle random-phase approximation (TQRPA) using the Skyrme energy density functional with three parametrizations (SkM\(^*\), SGII, and SLy4). The TQRPA is formulated in the grand-canonical ensemble and employs thermo field dynamics to treat both endoergic and exoergic transitions from thermally excited states, going beyond the hybrid model of Langanke et al. (2001) by including pairing correlations and de-excitation processes.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{56}\)Fe, the TQRPA rates exceed shell-model rates in some temperature and density regimes due to violation of the Brink-Axel hypothesis within the TQRPA.
- The neutron-proton mass difference \(\Delta M_{np} = 1.293\) MeV is used in the definition of transition energy \(\Delta E_{fi}\).
- The ratio of axial-vector to vector coupling constants is taken as \(g_A/g_V = -1.27\).
- For neutron-rich Ge isotopes in prior work (Dzhioev et al., 2009-2012), the weakening of pairing correlations with temperature leads to a downward shift of the GT\(_+\) strength of approximately 8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Skyrme-TQRPA model (Dzhioev et al., 2014-2017) to stellar electron capture on \(^{56}\)Fe and \(^{78}\)Ni, improving upon earlier TQRPA calculations that used the phenomenological quasiparticle-phonon model (Soloviev, 1992) with locally adjusted parameters. The results contradict the hybrid model of Langanke et al. (2001) and the finite-temperature RPA approaches of Paar et al. (2010), Fantina et al. (2012), and Niu et al. (2013) by showing that exoergic transitions from thermally excited states remove the reaction threshold and enhance low-energy cross sections, whereas those other models predict EC cross sections drop rapidly to zero below a threshold. This work enables more reliable theoretical predictions for weak interaction processes on neutron-rich nuclei far from stability, relevant for core-collapse supernova simulations.
