# Radiative Muon Capture in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The radiative muon capture(RMC) on a proton is analyzed by means of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The emitted photon energy spectrum is calculated and compared with the experimental data by taking the spin sum on the muonic atom states. We find that one-loop order corrections to the tree order amplitude modify the photon spectrum by less than five percent. This calculation supports that the theory is under a quantitative control as far as the chiral perturbation expansion is concerned and indicates that the discrepancy between the pseudo-scalar coupling constant required by the RMC experiment and the one deduced from ordinary muon capture, the value of which is also supported by chiral perturbation calculations, will remain unexplained from the theoretical side.

## Key claim

The one-loop (N2LO) corrections to the tree-level amplitude for radiative muon capture on a proton modify the photon energy spectrum by less than five percent, indicating that the discrepancy between the pseudo-scalar coupling constant required by RMC experiments and the value deduced from ordinary muon capture (which is supported by chiral perturbation calculations) cannot be resolved by higher-order strong-interaction corrections within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors calculated the amplitude for the reaction muon + proton -> neutron + neutrino + photon using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), which corresponds to one-loop order. They decomposed the weak current into vector and axial-vector parts, computed the nucleon form factors and the four-point vertex functions (photon-weak current-nucleon-nucleon), and evaluated the photon energy spectrum by taking the spin sum over muonic atom states. The low-energy constants in the N2LO Lagrangian were fixed from independent experimental inputs including anomalous magnetic moments, pion processes, nucleon radii, and Delta resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop (N2LO) corrections modify the photon spectrum by less than 5 percent relative to the tree-order amplitude.
- The pseudo-scalar coupling constant from ordinary muon capture is g_P = 8.7 ± 1.9 (experimental, from Bardin et al.).
- The HBChPT prediction for the pseudo-scalar coupling constant at q^2 = -0.88 m_mu^2 is g_P = 8.44 ± 0.16 (Bernard et al.) and g_P = 8.21 ± 0.09 (Fearing et al.).
- The PCAC prediction for the same momentum transfer is g_P = 8.42.
- The low-energy constants used include: b_6 = kappa_V = 3.71, b_7 = kappa_S = -0.12, alpha_9^(2) + alpha_10^(2) = 1.43 × 10^(-3), c_3 = 5.34, c_13 = 2.37, c_14 = -1.37, c_8 = -3.27, c_4 = -22.27, c_12 = -0.79.
- The chiral expansion scale is Lambda_chi ~ 1 GeV, and the muon mass m_mu ~ 0.1 Lambda_chi.

## Context

This work extends the tree-order calculation of radiative muon capture by Meissner et al. (1996) to one-loop order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the TRIUMF RMC experiment (Jonkmans et al.) which required a pseudo-scalar coupling enhanced by a factor of 1.5 relative to PCAC, and the consistent values obtained from ordinary muon capture (Bardin et al.) and chiral perturbation theory (Bernard et al., Fearing et al.). The result shows that higher-order strong-interaction corrections cannot explain this discrepancy, suggesting the need for new experimental measurements with higher precision (currently being planned by Hasinoff et al.) or possibly new physics beyond the standard strong-interaction treatment.
