# A Proof of Duality in Structure Functions Near x = 1

> A proof of Bloom-Gilman duality which relates an integral over the low-mass resonances in deep inelastic structure functions to an integral over the scaling region near x = 1 is given. It is based on general analytic properties of the corresponding virtual Compton amplitude but is insensitive to its asymptotic behaviour.

## Key claim

The paper provides a proof of Bloom-Gilman duality that is essentially independent of both the high-energy behavior and fixed-pole contributions of the virtual Compton amplitude, contrary to the original derivation which relied on assumptions about Regge asymptotics and the absence of fixed poles.

## Method

The proof uses analytic properties of the virtual Compton amplitude \(T_2(q^2,\nu)\) for fixed \(q^2\), exploiting a contour integral in the complex \(\nu\)-plane that wraps around the cuts from threshold \(\nu_0\) to \(\pm\bar\nu\) and closes on a circle of radius \(\bar\nu\). By applying the dispersion relation for \(T_2\) and changing variables to \(\omega' = (2M\nu + M_0^2)/Q^2\), the contour integral is transformed into an integral over the scaling region, yielding the duality sum rule without requiring assumptions about asymptotic behavior or exact scaling.

## Key numbers

- The duality sum rule is expressed as: \(\frac{2M}{Q^2}\int_0^{\bar\nu} d\nu F_2(\nu,Q^2) = \int_1^{\bar\omega'} d\omega' F_2(\omega')\), where \(\bar\omega' = (2M\bar\nu + M_0^2)/Q^2\) and \(M_0\) is a scale of order the nucleon mass \(M\).
- For the inclusive-exclusive connection, if \(F_2(q^2,\omega') \approx A(\omega' - 1)^p\) near \(\omega' \approx 1\), then \(\sum_r G_r^2(Q^2) \approx \frac{A}{p+1} \left(\frac{\bar s + M_0^2 - M^2}{Q^2}\right)^{p+1}\), with data suggesting \(p=3\) for the nucleon elastic form factor.
- Higher moment sum rules are given by: \(\left(\frac{2M}{-q^2}\right)^{2m+1}\int_0^{\bar\nu} d\nu \nu^{2m} F_2(\nu,q^2) = \int_1^{\bar\omega'} d\omega' F_2(q^2,\omega') \omega'^{2m}\), valid for \(Q^2/M_0^2 \gg m \ge 0\).

## Context

This work revisits the original Bloom-Gilman duality (Bloom and Gilman, 1971), which was derived using a superconvergence relation based on Regge phenomenology and assumptions about fixed poles. The proof given here is insensitive to those assumptions and relies only on general analytic properties, making it more robust. It enables further study of the inclusive-exclusive connection relating resonance form factors to scaling structure functions near \(x=1\), and opens the possibility of incorporating QCD logarithmic scaling violations into the duality framework, as discussed by Parisi (1973, 1974) and De Rujula, Georgi, and Politzer (1977).
