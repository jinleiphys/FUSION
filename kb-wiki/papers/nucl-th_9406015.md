# Two-nucleon knockout contributions to the $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ reaction in the dip and {$Δ$}(1232) regions

> The contributions from $^{12}$C$(e,e'pn)$ and $^{12}$C$(e,e'pp)$ to the semi-exclusive $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ cross section have been calculated in an unfactorized model for two-nucleon emission. We assume direct two-nucleon knockout after virtual photon coupling with the two-body pion-exchange currents in the target nucleus. Results are presented at several kinematical conditions in the dip and $Δ$(1232) regions. The calculated two-nucleon knockout strength is observed to account for a large fraction of the measured $(e,e'p)$ strength above the two-nucleon emission threshold.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two-nucleon knockout, mediated by meson-exchange currents and Delta currents, accounts for a large fraction of the measured semi-exclusive $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ strength above the two-nucleon emission threshold, but only for backward proton angles (large $\mid \theta_p \mid$); at forward angles the model underestimates the data by about a factor of two, indicating other mechanisms (such as pion production) contribute.

## Method

The authors calculated the $^{12}$C$(e,e'pp)$ and $^{12}$C$(e,e'pn)$ contributions to the semi-exclusive $^{12}$C$(e,e'p)$ cross section using an unfactorized model for two-nucleon emission, assuming direct knockout after virtual photon coupling with two-body pion-exchange and Delta currents. The calculations were performed in a mean-field approximation, using a partial wave expansion to account for final-state interactions of the ejected nucleons, and integrated over the solid angle of the undetected hadron. Results were compared with data from NIKHEF-K (dip region at $\omega$=212 MeV, q=270 MeV/c; Delta region at $\omega$=263 MeV, q=303 MeV/c) and MIT-Bates (Delta region at q=401 MeV/c).

## Key numbers

- At $\omega$=212 MeV and q=270 MeV/c (dip region), the calculations reproduce the NIKHEF data for $\mid \theta_p \mid \geq 74^\circ$ but underestimate the data by about a factor of two at smaller $\mid \theta_p \mid$.
- At $\omega \approx$ 270 MeV (Delta region), for $\theta_p=113^\circ$ the model gives a fair description of both the missing-energy dependence and magnitude of the data.
- The $(e,e'pn)$ to $(e,e'pp)$ ratio is less than ten in parallel kinematics ($\theta_p=0^\circ$) and steadily grows as the proton is detected at larger angles $\mid \theta_p \mid$.
- Coupling constants used: $f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi=0.079$, $f_{\pi N\Delta}^2/4\pi=0.37$, $f_{\gamma N\Delta}^2=0.014$; cut-off mass $\Lambda_\pi=1200$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier Fermi-gas model calculations by Van Orden and Donnelly (1981) and Dekker, Brussaard and Tjon (1991) that showed meson-exchange currents contribute significantly to inclusive $(e,e')$ strength in the dip region, and addresses the semi-exclusive $(e,e'p)$ measurements by Lourie et al. (1986) and Baghaei et al. (1989). The model is parameter-free and builds on a similar approach previously applied to $(\gamma,NN)$ reactions by Ryckebusch et al. (1994). The results enable future studies of nucleon-nucleon correlations in finite nuclei through semi-exclusive $(e,e'p)$ measurements at backward proton angles, where two-nucleon knockout dominates.
