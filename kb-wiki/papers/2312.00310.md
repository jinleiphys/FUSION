# Spherically symmetric anisotropic strange stars

> In this work, we made an extensive study about the possible presence of anisotropies in strange stars. To accomplish this task, we use three different configurations for the strange matter: the unpaired matter, a two-flavor super-conducting (2SC) strange matter, and a fully three-flavor super-conducting strange matter (CFL). For each configuration, we calculate the relevant quantities for the strange stars, such as the mass-radius relation, the dimensionless tidal parameter, the moment of inertia, and the surface curvature for different degrees of anisotropies. Whenever possible, we compare our results with constraints found in the literature, especially focusing on the existence of very massive pulsars (PSR J0952-0607), as well as very light compact objects (HESS J1731-347).

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates whether the inclusion of local pressure anisotropy, modeled via the Bowers-Liang (BL) parameter, allows strange stars built from three different quark matter models (unpaired, 2SC, and CFL) to simultaneously satisfy multiple observational constraints, including the speculative high mass of PSR J0952-0607 and the low mass and radius of HESS J1731-347, which are difficult to explain with isotropic models alone.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for spherically symmetric, anisotropic stars using the Bowers-Liang model, where the anisotropy parameter λ_BL quantifies the deviation between radial and tangential pressure. They employ three distinct equations of state for strange quark matter: a vector-enhanced MIT bag model for unpaired matter, and constant-sound-speed parametrizations for the two-flavor superconducting (2SC) and color-flavor-locked (CFL) phases. For each combination of EOS and λ_BL, they compute the mass-radius relation, dimensionless tidal deformability Λ, moment of inertia, and surface curvature (Kretschmann scalar), and compare these against observational constraints from pulsars, NICER, and gravitational-wave events.

## Key numbers

- Maximum isotropic masses: 2.09 M⊙ (unpaired), 2.16 M⊙ (2SC), 2.76 M⊙ (CFL).
- For λ_BL = +2.0, maximum mass increases by ~23% (MIT), ~25% (2SC), and ~22% (CFL); for λ_BL = -2.0, it decreases by ~16% in all three models.
- Dimensionless tidal deformability for the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star (isotropic): Λ₁.₄ = 383 (unpaired), 622 (2SC), 787 (CFL).
- For λ_BL = +2.0, Λ₁.₄ increases by ~26% (MIT), ~24% (2SC), and ~14% (CFL); for λ_BL = -2.0, it decreases by ~22% (MIT), ~20% (2SC), and ~11% (CFL).
- Moment of inertia for the canonical star (isotropic, in units of 10⁴⁵ g cm²): I₁.₄ = 1.47 (unpaired), 1.66 (2SC), 1.78 (CFL).
- For λ_BL = +2.0, I₁.₄ increases by 6.8% (MIT), 6.6% (2SC), and 3.4% (CFL); for λ_BL = -2.0, it decreases by 8.1% (MIT), 7.8% (2SC), and 4.5% (CFL).
- The unpaired matter with λ_BL > 0.5 can describe the HESS J1731-347 object (M = 0.77 M⊙, R = 10.4 km); with λ_BL > 1.0 it satisfies the PSR J0740+6620 radius (12.35 ± 0.35 km) and the PSR J0952-0607 mass (2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙); with λ_BL = 2.0 it can explain the GW190814 mass-gap object (2.50 – 2.67 M⊙).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lopes 2023) which considered only unpaired quark matter and the mass-radius and tidal deformability, by now including two color-superconducting phases (2SC and CFL) and also computing the moment of inertia and surface curvature. It complements studies on anisotropic strange stars such as those by Arbanil (2016) on radial oscillations, Maurya (2019) on charged anisotropic stars in Tolman–Kuchowicz geometry, and Deb (2021) on magnetic field effects. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming measurements of moment of inertia from pulsars like PSR J0737-3039(A) and with potential surface curvature observations from interferometers like LISA.
