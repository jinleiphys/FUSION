# MESON2000 Conference Summary

> This short contribution is a {\it lite} MESON2000 conference summary. As appropriate for the 600th anniversary of the Jagellonian University, it begins with a brief summary of the last 600 years of European history and its place in hadron physics. Next a ``physicist chirality'' order parameter PC is introduced. When applied to MESON2000 plenary speakers this order parameter illustrates the separation of hadron physicists into disjoint communities. The individual plenary talks in MESON2000 are next sorted according to the subconference associated with each of the 36 plenary speakers. Finally, I conclude with a previously unreported Feynman story regarding the use of models in hadron physics.

## Key claim

The paper is a conference summary with no original research claim; it introduces a "physicist chirality" order parameter to illustrate that hadron physicists at MESON2000 were separated into disjoint communities with little overlap, rather than presenting a unified field.

## Method

The author attended the MESON2000 conference and classified the 36 plenary speakers into five subconferences based on their talk content and language use. A "physicist chirality" order parameter was defined as (N_chiral - N_exotica)/(N_chiral + N_exotica) and applied to the first 18 plenary speakers by counting their uses of "chiral" versus "glueball", "hybrid", or "exotic". The five identified subconferences were: Condensed Matter Hadron, Good Auld Hadrons, Exotica, HEP, and Photon-Hadron.

## Key numbers

* 36 plenary speakers at MESON2000 were sorted into subconferences
* W.Weise used "chiral" 39 times, the highest count among plenary speakers
* A.Szczepaniak used "exotic" over 29 times, the highest count among plenary speakers
* The first experimental exotic meson candidates are the pi_1(1400) and pi_1(1600), with LGT predicting the 1-+ exotic level at about 2.0 GeV
* The Crystal Barrel candidate f_0(1500) was discussed as a glueball candidate in the 0++ sector
* The h_1(1590) candidate is considerably lighter than expected by Godfrey and Isgur for a 2P state

## Context

This is a conference summary for MESON2000, held during the 600th anniversary of the Jagellonian University. The paper reviews the state of hadron physics in 2000, covering chiral perturbation theory, hadron spectroscopy, exotic mesons, heavy flavor physics, and photon-hadron interactions. It enables future work by documenting the experimental status of exotic meson candidates (pi_1(1400) and pi_1(1600)) and noting that the universal curve for vector meson electroproduction and Close's work on diffractive scattering represent the most remarkable new results requiring further theoretical interpretation.
