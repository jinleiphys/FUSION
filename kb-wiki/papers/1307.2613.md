# Few-body semiclassical approach to nucleon transfer and emission reactions

> A three-body semiclassical model is proposed to describe the nucleon transfer and emission reactions in a heavy-ion collision. In this model the two heavy particles, i.e. nuclear cores A$_1(Z_{A_1}, M_{A_1})$ and A$_2(Z_{A_2}, M_{A_2})$, move along classical trajectories $\vec R_1(t)$ and $\vec R_2(t)$ respectively, while the dynamics of the lighter neutron, n, is considered from a quantum mechanical point of view. Here, $M_i$ are the nucleon masses and $Z_i$ are the Coulomb charges of the heavy nuclei ($i=1,2$). A Faddeev-type semiclassical formulation using realistic paired nuclear-nuclear potentials is applied so that all three channels (elastic, rearrangement and break-up) are described in an unified manner. In order to solve these time-dependent equations the Faddeev components of the total three-body wave-function are expanded in terms of the input and output channel target eigenfunctions. In the special case when the nuclear cores are identical (A$_1 \equiv$ A$_2$) and the two-level approximation in the expansion over target functions the time-dependent semiclassical Faddeev equations are resolved in an explicit way. To determine the realistic $\vec R_1(t)$ and $\vec R_2(t)$ trajectories of the nuclear cores a self-consistent approach based on the Feynman path integral theory is applied.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a three-body semiclassical model for nucleon transfer and emission reactions in heavy-ion collisions, where the two heavy nuclear cores move along classical trajectories while the neutron is treated quantum mechanically. The key advance is the use of a self-consistent Pechukas method based on Feynman path integrals to determine realistic (non-straight-line) trajectories for the heavy particles, rather than the simple straight-line trajectories used in prior semiclassical approaches such as Revai (1985).

## Method

The authors formulate time-dependent Faddeev-type equations for the three-body system (A1, A2, n) using local paired nuclear-nuclear potentials between each heavy core and the neutron. The Faddeev components of the total wave function are expanded in terms of the target eigenfunctions of the respective two-body subsystems, yielding a set of coupled time-dependent differential equations for the expansion coefficients. For the special case of identical nuclear cores and a two-level approximation, the coupled equations are solved analytically. The classical trajectories of the heavy cores are determined self-consistently using the Pechukas method, which derives Newtonian equations of motion from an effective potential that includes both the Coulomb interaction and quantum-mechanical corrections from the neutron dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The condition for treating heavy particles classically is given by the mass ratios: \(m_n / M_{A_1} \ll 1\) and \(m_n / M_{A_2} \ll 1\).
- The classical cross section for Rutherford scattering of the heavy cores is given by \((d\sigma/d\Omega)_{cl} = (Z_{A_1} Z_{A_2} e^2 / (4E \sin^2(\theta/2)))^2\).
- The transfer cross section formula (Eq. 48) contains an integral over the scattering angle \(\theta\) from 0 to \(\pi\) and an inner integral over the radial coordinate \(R\) from \(r_m\) to \(+\infty\).
- The impact parameter \(\rho\) is related to the scattering angle by \(\rho(\theta) = \cot(\theta/2) / (2E)\) for the Coulomb case.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Faddeev-type approach of Revai (1985), which used single-term separable (non-local) potentials and straight-line trajectories, by introducing local realistic nuclear-nuclear potentials and a self-consistent method for determining the heavy-particle trajectories. The Pechukas method, originally developed for atomic and molecular collisions (Pechukas 1969), is applied here to nuclear physics to account for the interplay between classical and quantum degrees of freedom. The model enables unified treatment of elastic, rearrangement (transfer), and break-up (emission) channels, and the analytical solution for identical cores and two-level approximation is noted as potentially useful for studying reactions such as \(^{13}\text{C} + ^{12}\text{C} \rightarrow ^{12}\text{C} + ^{13}\text{C}\) and the s-process neutron source \(^{13}\text{C}(\alpha,n)^{16}\text{O}\).
