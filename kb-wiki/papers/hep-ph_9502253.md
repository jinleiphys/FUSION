# VIRTUES OF THE HAAG EXPANSION IN QUANTUM FIELD THEORY

> I survey the use of the Haag expansion as a technique to solve quantum field theories. After an exposition of the asymptotic condition and the Haag expansion, I report the results of applying the Haag expansion to several quantum field theories, including galilean-invariant theories, matter at finite temperature (using the BCS model of superconductivity as an illustrative example), the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and the Schwinger model. I conclude with the outlook for further development of this method.

## Key claim

The Haag expansion provides a practical calculational method for quantum field theories that yields amplitudes which are both three-dimensional and explicitly covariant, with better ultraviolet behavior than totally off-shell time-ordered amplitudes, though the method has not yet been developed into a powerful calculation technique.

## Method

The author surveys the Haag expansion as a technique to solve quantum field theories by expressing interacting Lagrangian fields as sums of normal-ordered products of free asymptotic (in or out) fields, with the expansion coefficients (Haag amplitudes) being multiple retarded commutator functions with all but one leg on-shell. The method is applied to galilean-invariant nonrelativistic field theories with bound states, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and the Schwinger model, deriving equations for bound-state wavefunctions and scattering amplitudes from the equations of motion and canonical commutation relations.

## Key numbers

- For the nonrelativistic two-body bound state, the binding energy \(\epsilon_i\) appears in the bound-state energy as \(E = m_A + m_B - \epsilon_i + \mathbf{k}^2/2m_{AB}\), where \(m_{AB} = m_A + m_B\) (not \(m_{AB} - \epsilon_i\))
- The reduced mass \(\mu\) enters the Schr\\"odinger equation for the bound-state wavefunction via \(1/\mu = 1/m_A + 1/m_B\)
- The elastic scattering \(T\)-matrix on the momentum shell is given by \(t(\mathbf{k}_A,\mathbf{k}_B;\mathbf{p}_A,\mathbf{p}_B) = [\mathbf{p}_A^2/2m_A + \mathbf{p}_B^2/2m_B - \mathbf{k}_A^2/2m_A - \mathbf{k}_B^2/2m_B] \tilde{F}_{B;AB}(\mathbf{k};\mathbf{p})\)
- The canonical anticommutator \([A,A^\dagger]_+\) at equal times yields a generalized unitarity relation: \(\frac{1}{(2\pi)^{3/2}}(\tilde{F}_{B;AB}(\mathbf{k};\mathbf{p}) + \tilde{F}_{B;AB}^\ast(\mathbf{p};\mathbf{k})) = \sum_i \tilde{F}_{B;i}(\mathbf{k})\tilde{F}_{B;i}^\ast(\mathbf{p}) + \int d^3q \tilde{F}_{B;AB}(\mathbf{k};\mathbf{q})\tilde{F}_{B;AB}^\ast(\mathbf{p};\mathbf{q})\)

## Context

This work extends the foundational Haag expansion introduced by Haag (1955) from a tool for discussing questions of principle into a practical calculational method, building on the asymptotic condition formalism of Lehmann, Symanzik, and Zimmermann (LSZ). The approach contrasts with the Tamm-Dancoff expansion (which expands in states rather than fields) and with lattice methods for quantum chromodynamics, offering advantages such as explicit covariance and the ability to treat composite particles in parallel with elementary particles. The method enables future development of three-dimensional covariant equations for bound states and scattering amplitudes that are as close to completely on-shell as possible in field theory, with applications to relativistic theories including the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and the Schwinger model.
