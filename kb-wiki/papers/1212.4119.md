# Initial Conditions with Flow from a McLerran Venugopalan model with Transverse Dynamics

> Using a recursive solution of the Yang-Mills equation, we calculate analytic expressions for the gluon fields created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at small times $τ$. We have worked out explicit solutions for the fields and the energy momentum tensor up to 4th order in an expansion in $τ$. We generalize the McLerran-Venugopalan model to allow for a systematic treatment of averaged charge densities $μ^2$ that vary as a function of transverse coordinates. This allows us to calculate radial, elliptic and directed flow of gluon fields. Our results can serve as initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of nuclear collisions that include initial flow.

## Key claim

The paper shows that generalizing the McLerran-Venugopalan model to allow the average color charge density \(\mu^2\) to vary as a function of transverse coordinates yields analytic expressions for radial, elliptic, and directed flow of gluon fields at early times, provided the variation is slow enough that \(|\mu^2| \gg m^{-1} |\nabla^i \mu^2| \gg m^{-2} |\nabla^i \nabla^j \mu^2|\) (where \(m\) is an infrared regulator).

## Method

The authors solve the Yang-Mills equation recursively in an expansion in proper time \(\tau\) up to 4th order, obtaining analytic expressions for the gluon fields and the energy-momentum tensor. They then generalize the MV model by allowing \(\mu^2(\vec x_\perp)\) to vary transversely, compute the averaged two-gluon correlation function, and extract the transverse Poynting vector components \(B_i\) and \(B_i'\) which describe energy flow. The flow patterns are evaluated for gold nuclei with Woods-Saxon profiles at impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm.

## Key numbers

- The expansion of the gluon fields and energy-momentum tensor is carried out up to 4th order in an expansion in proper time \(\tau\).
- The transverse Poynting vector components are given by \(B_i = -\tau \frac{g^6 N_c (N_c^2-1)}{32 \pi} \nabla^i (\mu^2_1 \mu^2_2) \ln^2 \frac{Q^2}{(1.47m)^2}\) and \(B_i' = -\tau \frac{g^6 N_c (N_c^2-1)}{32 \pi} \ln^2 \frac{Q^2}{(1.47m)^2} [\mu^2_2 \nabla^i \mu^2_1 - \mu^2_2 \nabla^i \mu^2_1]\).
- The flow fields are computed for gold nuclei colliding with impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm.
- The infrared regulator \(m\) and saturation scale \(Q\) appear in the logarithmic factor \(\ln \frac{Q^2}{(1.47m)^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993) by introducing transverse spatial variation of the average charge density, enabling analytic study of flow phenomena that were previously only accessible numerically (e.g., Schenke, 2012). The results provide initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations that include initial flow, building on earlier matching procedures for ideal hydrodynamics (Fries, 2005; Fries, 2007). The directed flow component is qualitatively consistent with experimental data from STAR (Abelev, 2008; Adams, 2005).
