# Long-range interactions in double heavy tetraquarks $\bar Q \bar Q qq$

> At the large distances compared to the chiral symmetry breaking scale, a four-quark system $\bar Q \bar Q qq$ (with $Q$ as heavy and $q$ as light quarks) can be treated as two asymptotic mesons interacting via strong residual forces. The static heavy quark assumption enables using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, where one can compute the potential between two heavy antiquarks in the presence of two light quarks of finite mass -- a prescription utilized in several lattice QCD studies. To analyse the long-range strong force in a $\bar Q \bar Q qq$ system, we study the interaction between two bottom mesons in the heavy quark limit using chiral effective field theory and dispersion theory with unphysical pion mass. We present methods to obtain two-pion-exchange potential between two static heavy mesons at non-physical pion mass and compare our preliminary results with the corresponding lattice QCD potentials.

## Key claim

The long-range strong force in a $\bar Q \bar Q qq$ system can be described by chiral potentials between two static bottom mesons, but the intermediate-range interaction requires more rigorous treatment beyond the scattering-length approximation, particularly because at the unphysical pion mass of 340 MeV the $f_0(500)/\sigma$ pole lies very close to the $2m_\pi$ threshold and the $\pi B$ S-wave interaction develops a bound state.

## Method

The authors compute the one-pion-exchange (OPE) and two-pion-exchange (2PE) potentials between two bottom mesons in the heavy quark limit using heavy meson chiral perturbation theory (HM$\chi$PT) and dispersion theory. For the 2PE potential, they use the Omnès representation to incorporate $\pi\pi$ rescattering in the scalar-isoscalar channel, with the $B\bar B \to \pi\pi$ transition amplitude fixed by $\pi B$ scattering lengths computed from unitarized NLO $\pi H$ scattering amplitudes, with low-energy constants (LECs) extracted from fits to charmed-meson scattering lengths from Liu (2012). The chiral potentials are compared to lattice QCD data from Bicudo (2017) at $m_\pi = 340$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass used: $m_\pi = 340$ MeV
- Pion decay constant at this mass: $f_\pi(m_\pi=340) = 114$ MeV
- Heavy meson axial coupling: $g_\pi = 0.5$
- $\pi B$ scattering length in the isospin-even channel: $a^{(+)} = -0.54^{+0.19}_{-0.59}$ fm, computed in the static limit using Eq. (sla0)
- Binding energy of the $\bar b \bar b qq$ system from lattice QCD: $\mathcal O(100)$ MeV
- Hard cutoff for the two-particle loop function: $\Lambda \sim 700$ MeV, set by matching to dimensional regularization in the charm sector

## Context

This work extends the pioneering chiral perturbation theory analysis of doubly-heavy tetraquarks by Manohar and Wise (1992) and the earlier quark potential model exploration by Zouzou (1986). It provides a method to compute chiral potentials between heavy mesons that can be calibrated to lattice QCD data from Bicudo (2017) and then used to study other hadronic states related by QCD symmetries (heavy quark spin and flavour symmetries). The results enable future inclusion of all possible left-hand cuts and application of the Khuri-Treiman formalism to obtain intermediate-range interactions in different spin and isospin channels, such as correlated $\rho$ and $\omega$ meson exchanges.
