# Chiral Phase Transition at Finite Temperature in the Linear Sigma Model

> We study the chiral phase transition at finite temperature in the linear sigma model by employing a self-consistent Hartree approximation. This approximation is introduced by imposing self-consistency conditions on the effective meson mass equations which are derived from the finite temperature one-loop effective potential. It is shown that in the limit of vanishing pion mass, namely when the chiral symmetry is exact, the phase transition becomes a weak first order accompanying a gap in the order parameter as a function of temperature. This is caused by the long range fluctuations of meson fields whose effective masses become small in the transition region. It is shown, however, that with an explicit chiral symmetry breaking term in the Lagrangian which generates the realistic finite pion mass the transition is smoothed out irrespective of the choice of coupling strength.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in the linear sigma model is a weak first-order transition with a gap in the order parameter when the pion mass is zero (exact chiral symmetry), but this transition is smoothed out into a crossover when a realistic finite pion mass is introduced via an explicit symmetry-breaking term, regardless of the coupling strength.

## Method

The authors employed a self-consistent Hartree approximation, imposing self-consistency conditions on the effective meson mass equations derived from the finite-temperature one-loop effective potential. They computed the effective masses of sigma mesons and pions by solving gap equations that include thermal fluctuations, while excluding vacuum polarization effects due to renormalization difficulties in the nonperturbative scheme. The analysis focused on the meson sector of the linear sigma model at zero baryon density, using the O(4)-invariant Lagrangian with and without an explicit chiral symmetry breaking term.

## Key numbers

- In the exact chiral limit (vanishing pion mass), the phase transition exhibits a hysteresis loop characteristic of a first-order transition, with a gap in the order parameter as a function of temperature.
- When an explicit symmetry-breaking term is introduced to generate a realistic finite pion mass, the transition becomes smooth (crossover) irrespective of the choice of coupling strength.
- The vacuum relation \( m_\pi^2 f_\pi = \epsilon \) connects the pion mass, pion decay constant, and the symmetry-breaking parameter \(\epsilon\).
- The self-consistent Hartree approximation yields the Goldstone theorem relation \( M_\pi^2 \bs = \epsilon \), ensuring massless pions in the \(\epsilon \to 0\) limit.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature by Lee and Wick (1974), Baym and Grinstein (1975), and Dolan and Jackiw (1974), who used perturbative and one-loop methods in the linear sigma model. The self-consistent Hartree approximation improves upon the one-loop treatment by summing an infinite series of "superdaisy diagrams," as previously explored by Baym and Grinstein. The results clarify that the order of the chiral phase transition depends sensitively on the explicit breaking of chiral symmetry, which has implications for understanding the chiral transition in heavy-ion collisions as suggested by Bjorken (1983) and Rajagopal (1995).
