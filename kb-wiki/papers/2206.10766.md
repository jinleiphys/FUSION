# Probing high-density nuclear symmetry energy with $Ξ^{-}/Ξ^{0}$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 3$ GeV

> Recent beam energy scan (BES) experiments at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration (PLB {\bf 827},137003 (2022) and PRL {\bf 128}, 202303 (2022)) found that hadronic interactions dominate the collective flow and the proton cumulant ratios are driven by baryon number conservation in a region of high baryon density in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV Au+Au reactions, indicating the dense medium formed in such collisions is likely hadronic matter. Within an updated ART (A Relativistic Transport) model with momentum dependent isoscalar and isovector single-nucleon mean-field potentials corresponding to different symmetry energies at suprasaturation densities, the $n/p$, $π^{-}/π^{+}$, $K_{s}^{0}/K^{+}$, $Σ^{-}/Σ^{+}$ and $Ξ^{-}/Ξ^{0}$ ratios are studied for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV where the maximum central density reaches about $(3.6\sim 4.0)ρ_0$. The doubly strange $Ξ^{-}/Ξ^{0}$ ratio is found to have the strongest sensitivity to the variation of high-density nuclear symmetry energy. Thus, the $Ξ^{-}/Ξ^{0}$ ratio in relativistic heavy-ion reactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 3$ GeV may help probe sensitively the poorly known symmetry energy of dense neutron-rich matter critically important for understanding various properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The doubly strange baryon ratio \(\Xi^{-}/\Xi^{0}\) in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV shows the strongest sensitivity to the variation of high-density nuclear symmetry energy among several isospin multiplet ratios studied, provided the dense medium formed is hadronic matter (as indicated by recent STAR Collaboration results) and the maximum central density reaches about \((3.6\sim 4.0)\rho_0\).

## Method

The authors used an updated ART (A Relativistic Transport) model within the AMPT framework, incorporating momentum-dependent isoscalar and isovector single-nucleon mean-field potentials corresponding to two limiting forms of the symmetry energy (soft with parameter \(x=1\) and stiff with \(x=-1\)). They simulated central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV and calculated the time evolution and kinetic energy distributions of the \(n/p\), \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\), \(K_{s}^{0}/K^{+}\), \(\Sigma^{-}/\Sigma^{+}\), and \(\Xi^{-}/\Xi^{0}\) ratios, using the quark counting rule to determine mean-field potentials for strange baryons.

## Key numbers

- Maximum central compression density in the simulations reaches about \(3.6\rho_0\) (with momentum-dependent potentials).
- The soft symmetry energy (\(x=1\)) leads to an approximately 30% higher \(\Xi^{-}/\Xi^{0}\) ratio compared to the stiff one (\(x=-1\)).
- The \(\Xi^{-}/\Xi^{0}\) ratio saturates at about \(t = 10\) fm/c, while the individual yields saturate around \(t = 20\) fm/c.
- The \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio shows about 5% sensitivity to the symmetry energy at high kinetic energies.
- The \(\Sigma^{-}/\Sigma^{+}\) ratio shows up to 20% sensitivity at low kinetic energies.
- The \(K_{s}^{0}/K^{+}\) ratio shows no more than 6% sensitivity to the symmetry energy variation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear symmetry energy using heavy-ion collisions at lower beam energies (reviewed by Li et al. (2014) and others) to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energy of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV, where the STAR Collaboration (2022) found that the dense medium is likely hadronic matter. The study builds on earlier transport model investigations of \(\Xi\) production in heavy-ion collisions (Li et al. (2012), UrQMD collaboration (2014, 2016)) and on the use of isospin multiplet ratios such as \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) and \(n/p\) as probes of the symmetry energy. The results enable future experimental measurements at facilities like HIAF/China, FAIR/Germany, and NICA/Russia to constrain the poorly known symmetry energy of dense neutron-rich matter near the onset of quark deconfinement.
