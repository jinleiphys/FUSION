# Instantons, analytic continuation, and $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric field theory

> Ordinary Hermitian $λϕ^4$ theory is known to exist in $d<4$ dimensions when $λ>0$. For negative values of the coupling, it has been suggested that a physical meaningful definition of the interacting theory can be given in terms of ${\cal PT}$-symmetric field theory. In this work, we critically re-examine the relation between analytically continued Hermitian field theory with quartic interaction, and ${\cal PT}$-symmetric field theory, including $O(N)$ models. We find that in general ${\cal PT}$-symmetric field theory does not correspond to the analytic continuation of the Hermitian theory, except at high temperature where the instanton contribution present in the analytically continued theory can be neglected.

## Key claim

The paper finds that $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric field theory does not generally correspond to the analytic continuation of Hermitian $\lambda\phi^4$ theory, contrary to the ABS conjecture; the correspondence holds only at high temperature where instanton (bounce) contributions in the analytically continued theory become negligible.

## Method

The authors numerically compute partition functions for Hermitian $\lambda\phi^4$ theory analytically continued to negative coupling, for $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric theory defined via complex integration contours, and for a wedge-contour path integral, all in $d=0$ and $d=1$ (quantum mechanics). They compare these partition functions at varying temperatures (equivalently coupling strengths) and fit the difference between the analytically continued and $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric partition functions to an exponential form characteristic of bounce contributions. For the $d=1$ case, they diagonalize the Hamiltonian in the harmonic oscillator basis (up to 1000 states) to obtain high-precision eigenvalues, and also perform lattice path integral evaluations using the VEGAS integrator.

## Key numbers

* The difference between the $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric partition function $Z_{\mathcal{PT}}$ and the real-part analytically continued partition function $Z_1$ is fit by $-\log(Z_{\mathcal{PT}} - Z_1) \approx p_1^3 / \beta^3 + \log(p_2 + p_3 \beta^3)$ with parameters $p_1 \approx 3.963$, $p_2 \approx 0.307$, and $p_3 \approx 0.035$ over the range $\beta \in [1.4, 4.0]$.
* In $d=0$ for a single-component scalar, the $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric partition function equals the real part of the analytically continued Hermitian partition function: $Z^{d=0}_{\mathcal{PT}}(g) = \operatorname{Re}(Z^{d=0}(\lambda=-g))$, with the explicit form $Z^{d=0}(\lambda) = 2\Gamma(5/4) / (4\pi^2 \lambda)^{1/4}$.
* For $N>1$ component scalars in $d=0$, the cone contour yields a divergent partition function, while the wedge contour reproduces the real part of the analytic continuation.
* In the lattice path integral for $d=1$, the wedge contour with $K=10$ sites gives results matching the analytic continuation for $\beta < 3$.

## Context

This work critically tests the ABS conjecture proposed by Ai, Bender, and Sarkar (2022) that $\ln Z_{\mathcal{PT}}(g) = \operatorname{Re} \ln Z_{\mathcal{H}}(\lambda=-g)$, extending earlier $d=0$ checks by the same authors. The paper builds on the $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric quantum mechanics framework of Bender (1998) and the duality mapping of Jones (2006), and uses Lefschetz thimble analysis following Witten (2010) to explain the difference between contours. The results suggest that $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric field theory cannot provide a meaningful definition of $\lambda\phi^4$ theory at negative coupling in general dimensions, except possibly at high temperature, which limits proposals for defining scalar quantum field theory in four dimensions (Romatschke 2022, Grable 2023).
