# Effective field theory approaches to pion production in proton-proton collisions

> I critically review the status of computations of threshold pion production in proton-proton collisions in the framework of effective field theory approaches or variants thereof. I also present the results of a novel diagrammatic scheme.

## Key claim

Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory is likely not the appropriate framework for understanding threshold pion production in proton-proton collisions, because the extreme kinematics (proton momentum ~362 MeV) cause the 1/m expansion to fail, with infinitely many terms contributing to the same order and the series failing to converge.

## Method

The author critically reviews existing effective field theory (EFT) calculations of threshold pion production, focusing on the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory approach of the Seattle group and the one-loop calculation of Gedalin et al. He then presents a novel diagrammatic scheme that uses relativistic Feynman diagrams with chiral one-pion exchange, heavy meson exchanges (omega, rho, eta), and a factorization of final-state interactions (FSI) treated in the effective range approximation, with all parameters fixed from independent sources such as forward NN dispersion relations and dispersion-theoretical analyses.

## Key numbers

- Threshold proton center-of-mass momentum for neutral pion production: |p| = 362.2 MeV, derived from sqrt(M_pi * m) with m = 938.27 MeV and M_pi = 134.97 MeV
- Empirical S-wave threshold amplitude for pp -> pp pi0: A(emp) = (2.7 - i 0.3) fm^4, extracted from data assuming the form of eq. (stots)
- Imaginary part of A is about -1/9 of the real part, corresponding to the ^3P_0 pp phase shift delta = -6.3 degrees at T_lab = 279.65 MeV
- Empirical triplet threshold amplitude for pp -> pn pi+: B(emp) = (2.8 - i 1.5) fm^4, with Re B = 2.74 fm^4 reproduced by chiral one-pion exchange and short-range vector meson physics
- Modulus of threshold amplitude for pp -> pp eta: |C| = 1.32 fm^4, from CELSIUS data
- Real part of eta N scattering length: Re a_etaN = (0.717 ± 0.030) fm, about six times larger than the pi- p scattering length (0.125 fm)
- Extracted eta' - N coupling constant: g_eta'NN = 2.5 ± 0.7, with mixing parameter epsilon = 0.4 ± 0.1
- Threshold laboratory kinetic energy for eta production: T_lab = 1254.6 MeV, with M_eta = 547.45 MeV

## Context

This work critically reviews the EFT approaches of the Seattle group (van Kolck et al., 1998) and the one-loop calculation by Gedalin et al. (1998), and extends the author's own diagrammatic scheme (Meißner, 1998) to eta and eta' production. The key finding that the rescattering contribution in the chiral framework interferes destructively with direct production (opposite to meson-exchange models such as the Jülich model) indicates that heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory is not converging for this reaction. The author argues that the process pp -> d pi+ should be studied instead, as it is less contaminated by short-distance physics, and that EFT ideas should be used to constrain meson-exchange models rather than replace them.
