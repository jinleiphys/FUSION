# Chiral-odd transversity GPDs from a leading twist hard amplitude

> The chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon can be accessed experimentally through the exclusive photoproduction process gamma + N -> pi + rho + N', in the kinematics where the meson pair has a large invariant mass and the final nucleon has a small transverse momentum, provided the vector meson is produced in a transversally polarized state. We calculate perturbatively the scattering amplitude at leading order in alpha_s. We build a simple model for the dominant transversity GPD H_T(x,xi,t) based on the concept of double distribution. We estimate the unpolarized differential cross section for this process in the kinematics of the Jlab and COMPASS experiments. Counting rates show that the experiment looks feasible with the real photon beam characteristics expected at JLab@12 GeV, and with the quasi real photon beam in the COMPASS experiment.

## Key claim

The chiral-odd transversity GPDs of the nucleon can be accessed through the exclusive photoproduction process gamma + N -> pi + rho + N' at leading twist, provided the vector meson is produced in a transversally polarized state and the meson pair has a large invariant mass; the feasibility depends on the magnitude of the chiral-odd GPDs, which remain largely unknown.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering amplitude at leading order in alpha_s using collinear factorization, writing the amplitude as a convolution of the hard part, the transversity GPD H_T(x,xi,t), and the leading twist distribution amplitudes of the pion and the transversely polarized rho meson. They model the dominant transversity GPD H_T using double distributions with a profile function and transversity parton distribution functions from Anselmino (2008), and describe the t-dependence with a dipole form factor. The unpolarized differential cross section is then estimated for the kinematics of JLab and COMPASS experiments.

## Key numbers

* The chiral-odd decay constant for the transversely polarized rho meson is f_rho^perp = 160 MeV.
* The dipole form factor parameter for the t-dependence of H_T is C = 0.71 GeV^2.
* The integrated cross section at S_gamma N = 100 GeV^2 (COMPASS kinematics) is approximately 3 nb.
* The integrated cross section at S_gamma N = 10 GeV^2 (JLab kinematics) is approximately 15 nb.
* The integrated cross section at S_gamma N = 20 GeV^2 (JLab kinematics) is approximately 33 nb.

## Context

This work extends the strategy initiated by Ivanov, Pire, Szymanowski, and Wallon (2004) for accessing transversity GPDs through exclusive photoproduction of meson pairs, moving from diffractive production of two vector mesons to the process involving a pion and a transversely polarized rho meson. It builds on model calculations of chiral-odd GPDs by Scopetta (2004), Pasquini (2005), and others, as well as lattice calculations of GPD moments. The results enable experimental extraction of transversity GPDs at JLab (large xi region) and COMPASS (small xi region), providing complementary information on the three-dimensional structure of the nucleon.
