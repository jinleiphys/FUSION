# The neutron charge form factor in helium-3

> In order to measure the charge form factor of the neutron, $G^n_E(Q^2)$, one needs to use a neutron bound in a nuclear target. We calculate the change in the form factor for a neutron bound in $^3He$, with respect to the free case, using several versions of the quark meson coupling model. It is found that the form factor may be suppressed by as much as 12% at $Q^2 = 0.5{GeV}^2$ with respect to that of the free neutron.

# The neutron charge form factor in helium-3

## Key claim

The charge form factor of a neutron bound in helium-3 may be suppressed by as much as 12% at Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2 relative to the free neutron, but this result depends on the specific variant of the quark-meson coupling model used and on the bag radius chosen; the suppression is smaller (about 5%) in the standard QMC model with fixed bag constant.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which nucleons are treated as MIT bags containing confined valence quarks that couple self-consistently to scalar sigma and vector omega and rho meson fields in mean-field approximation. They calculated the neutron charge form factor in helium-3 by applying a local density approximation, using the neutron density distribution in helium-3 identified with the proton density in tritium, and evaluated the form factor in the Breit frame with Peierls-Thouless projection and Lorentz contraction corrections. Several variants of QMC were considered, including models where the bag constant decreases with density (DC-II and SCALE parametrizations) and where the pi-NN coupling constant is reduced in medium.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2, the standard QMC model gives roughly a 5% reduction of the neutron charge form factor in helium-3 relative to the free neutron.
- At Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2, the maximum allowed reduction reaches 12% when the bag constant is allowed to decrease with density (for bag radius R_0 = 1.0 fm).
- For R_0 = 0.8 fm, the combined possible reduction from density-dependent bag constant and reduced pi-NN coupling is about 8% at Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2.
- The charge radius decreases by 3.3% for R_0 = 1.0 fm and 3.8% for R_0 = 0.8 fm.
- The reduction of the pi-NN coupling constant in medium is about 3% at normal nuclear density in QMC, which alone changes the form factor by less than 2% at Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium nucleon electromagnetic form factors using the QMC model (Lu et al., 1997) and addresses the specific case of helium-3, which has higher binding energy and central density than the deuteron. The results are comparable to previous predictions from the generalized Skyrme model (Meissner, 1989), the chiral sigma model (Goeke et al., 1989), and the chiral bag model (Cheon, 1992), suggesting a degree of model-independence. The calculations are motivated by ongoing experiments at Bonn, Mainz, MIT Bates, and TJNAF that aim to extract the neutron charge form factor from polarized electron scattering on polarized helium-3 and deuteron targets, where current data at Q^2 approximately 0.35 GeV^2 show a factor-of-two difference between the two targets that cannot yet be conclusively interpreted.
