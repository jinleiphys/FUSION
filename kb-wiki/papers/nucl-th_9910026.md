# Pairing Gap and Polarisation Effects

> The phenomenological adjustment of the nuclear pairing strength is usually performed with respect to the odd-even staggering of the binding energies. We find that the results strongly depend on the way in which the ground states of the odd nuclei are computed. A thorough calculation including all time-even and time-odd polarisation effects induced by the odd nucleon produces about 30% reduced odd-even staggering as compared to the standard spherical calculations in the relativistic mean-field model. The pairing strength must be enhanced by about 20% to compensate for that effect. The enhanced strength has dramatic consequences for the predicted deformation properties of the underlying mean-field models, possibly implying that new adjustments of their parameters become necessary as well.

# Pairing Gap and Polarisation Effects

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering of nuclear binding energies, commonly used to determine the phenomenological pairing strength, is reduced by about 30% when full time-even and time-odd polarisation effects from the odd nucleon are included in relativistic mean-field calculations, requiring the pairing strength to be enhanced by about 20% to compensate. This enhanced pairing strength has dramatic consequences for predicted deformation properties, potentially necessitating new adjustments of the underlying mean-field model parameters.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the PL-40 parametrisation, including time-odd currents to account for broken time-reversal symmetry and its associated polarisation effects. Pairing was treated at the BCS level using a zero-range pairing force with a Woods-Saxon energy cut-off, and the neutron pairing strength was refitted to reproduce the experimental fourth-difference pairing gap in 126Sn while including full dynamical polarisation. The calculations were compared with spherical and deformed-but-time-even approximations to isolate the contribution of time-odd polarisation.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical polarisation reduces the fourth-difference neutron gap in 126Sn by 35% (from 1.2493 MeV in spherical calculations to 0.8104 MeV with full polarisation, using the same pairing strength of -346.0 MeV fm^3)
- The refitted neutron pairing strength required to restore the experimental gap (1.2587 MeV) in 126Sn is -412.4 MeV fm^3, an increase of 19% relative to the standard spherical-fit value of -346.0 MeV fm^3
- For the NL3 parametrisation in 126Sn, the gap sequence is: 1.32 MeV (spherical), 1.30 MeV (deformed time-even), 0.93 MeV (full dynamical polarisation)
- The two-neutron shell gap at the magic N=82 is reduced by 25% when using the refitted pairing strength
- The curvature at spherical shape for 110Sn is enhanced by about 130% with the refitted pairing strength; the low-lying 2+ state energy is increased by about 60%
- For 240Pu, fission barriers are lowered by 1-2 MeV with the enhanced pairing strength

## Context

This work extends the investigation of polarisation effects on odd-even staggering from the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model studied by Xu, Wyss, and Walker (1999) to the relativistic mean-field framework. The authors had previously studied odd nuclei near magic shells (Rutz et al., 1998) and here quantify how time-odd polarisation, previously neglected in standard spherical pairing adjustments, systematically reduces the apparent pairing gap. The results call into question the standard phenomenological adjustment of pairing strengths and indicate that deformation properties, fission barriers, and collective excitations are sensitive to the correct treatment of polarisation, motivating further studies with self-interaction corrected theories and other mean-field approaches.
