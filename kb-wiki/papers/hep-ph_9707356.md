# How the "H-particle" unravels the quark dynamics

> It is shown that the short-range part of the Goldstone boson exchange interaction between the constituent quarks which explains baryon spectroscopy and the short-range repulsion in the NN system induces a strong short-range repulsion in the flavour-singlet state of the $ΛΛ$ system with $J^P=0^+$. It then suggests that a deeply bound H-particle should not exist. We also compare our approach with other models employing different hyperfine interactions between quarks in the nonperturbative regime of QCD.

## Key claim

The short-range part of the Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) interaction between constituent quarks, which successfully explains baryon spectroscopy and the short-range repulsion in the NN system, induces a strong short-range repulsion in the flavour-singlet state of the ΛΛ system with J^P=0^+, suggesting that a deeply bound H-particle should not exist. This conclusion depends on the specific nonrelativistic parametrization of the GBE interaction used, which is an effective one that provides correct baryon energies with an s^3 ansatz for ground state wave functions.

## Method

The authors performed a nonrelativistic six-quark calculation using a Hamiltonian with a confining linear potential and a Goldstone boson exchange interaction (including π, K, η, and η' exchanges) between constituent quarks. They calculated the potential in the flavour-singlet S=-2 two-baryon system at zero separation between two s^3 clusters using the adiabatic (Born-Oppenheimer) approximation, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a basis of three allowed six-quark states with spatial symmetries [6]_O and [42]_O.

## Key numbers

- The schematic model gives C_χ = 29.3 MeV and ℏω ~ 250 MeV, determined from Δ-N and N(1440)-N splittings.
- The simple estimate for the lowest compact flavour-singlet 6Q state lies 305 MeV above the ΛΛ threshold (from 4 C_χ + 3/4 ℏω).
- The lowest eigenvalue of the flavour-singlet state J^P=0^+ is found to be 847 MeV above the ΛΛ threshold using the full Hamiltonian with constituent masses m_u,d = 340 MeV, m_s = 440 MeV.
- In extreme limits, the energy above the ΛΛ threshold ranges from 531 MeV (using m_s) to 1050 MeV (using m_u).
- The Λ hyperon mass obtained from the Hamiltonian is 1165.4 MeV at a harmonic oscillator parameter β = 0.449 fm.

## Context

This work extends the GBE model previously applied to baryon spectroscopy (Glozman and Riska, 1996) and the NN system (Stancu, Pepin, and Glozman, 1997) to the ΛΛ system and the H-particle channel. It contradicts predictions from the colour-magnetic interaction model (Jaffe, 1977) and subsequent quark-cluster calculations (Oka, Shimizu, and Yazaki, 1987; Straub et al., 1988; Koike, Shimizu, and Yazaki, 1990) that found a bound H-particle, as well as the extended variational calculation by Wolfe and Maltman (1997) that found a deeply bound state of about 250 MeV. The result is consistent with the experimental null result from Brookhaven (Stotzer et al., 1997) and with QCD sum rule estimates (Kodama, Oka, and Hatsuda, 1994) that find no qualitative difference between the two-nucleon and two-lambda systems. The work enables future fully dynamical calculations to determine whether a weakly bound ΛΛ molecule-like system of nuclear nature could exist.
