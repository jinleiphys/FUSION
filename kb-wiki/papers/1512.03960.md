# Disappearance of Mott oscillations in sub-barrier elastic scattering of identical nuclei and atomic ions

> The scattering of identical nuclei at low energies exhibits conspicuous Mott oscillations which can be used to investigate the presence of components in the predominantly Coulomb interaction arising from several physical effects. It is found that at a certain critical value of the Sommerfeld parameter the Mott oscillations disappear and the cross section becomes quite flat. We call this effect Transverse Isotropy (TI). The critical value of the Sommerfeld parameter at which TI sets in is found to be $η_{c} = \sqrt{3s +2}$, where $s$ is the spin of the nuclei participating in the scattering. No TI is found in the Mott scattering of identical Fermionic nuclei. The critical center of mass energy corresponding to $η_c$ is found to be $E_c$ = 0.40 MeV for $α+ α$ (s = 0) , 1.2 MeV for $^{6}$Li + $^{6}$LI (s = 1) and 7.1 MeV for $^{10}$B + $^{10}$B (s = 3). We further found that the inclusion of the nuclear interaction induces a significant modification in the TI. We suggest measurements at these sub-barrier energies for the purpose of extracting useful information about the nuclear interaction between light heavy ions. We also suggest extending the study of the TI to the scattering of identical atomic ions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for identical bosonic nuclei, Mott oscillations in sub-barrier elastic scattering disappear at a critical Sommerfeld parameter \(\eta_c = \sqrt{3s+2}\), producing a flat angular distribution called Transverse Isotropy (TI); however, this effect does not occur for identical fermionic nuclei, and the inclusion of the nuclear interaction shifts the critical energy and can produce multiple critical energies.

## Method
The authors analytically derive the second derivative of the Mott cross section at \(\theta = 90^\circ\) for unpolarized identical particles with spin \(s\), using the pure Coulomb scattering amplitude. They then incorporate the nuclear interaction via a first-order stationary-phase approximation with a Saxon-Woods potential, obtaining a modified expression for the second derivative. Numerical calculations using the Akyüz-Winther nuclear interaction for the \(\alpha + \alpha\) system are compared to experimental data from Abd El-Samad et al. (2006).

## Key numbers
- Critical Sommerfeld parameter for bosons: \(\eta_c = \sqrt{3s + 2}\), where \(s\) is the spin of the identical nuclei.
- Critical center-of-mass energies for pure Coulomb case: \(E_c = 0.40\) MeV for \(\alpha + \alpha\) (\(s = 0\)), 1.2 MeV for \(^6\text{Li} + ^6\text{Li}\) (\(s = 1\)), and 7.1 MeV for \(^{10}\text{B} + ^{10}\text{B}\) (\(s = 3\)).
- For \(\alpha + \alpha\) with nuclear interaction included, the lowest critical energy is \(E_0 = 0.4\) MeV (theoretical) and the data show a lowest critical energy of \(E_0 = 0.47\) MeV and a second critical energy at \(E_0' = 2.41\) MeV.
- Ratio \(E_0/V_B\) for candidate systems: 0.26 for \(^4\text{He}\) (\(s = 0\)), 0.405 for \(^6\text{Li}\) (\(s = 1\)), and 1.01 for \(^{10}\text{B}\) (\(s = 3\)).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Canto et al. (2001) and Canto et al. (2014) on the Transverse Isotropy effect in Mott scattering, and provides a theoretical framework for interpreting experimental data from Abd El-Samad et al. (2006) on \(\alpha + \alpha\) scattering. The paper suggests that measuring TI at sub-barrier energies in systems like \(^6\text{Li} + ^6\text{Li}\) and \(^{10}\text{B} + ^{10}\text{B}\) can extract information about the short-range nuclear interaction, complementing high-energy nuclear rainbow studies by Brandan and Satchler (1997) and Hussein and McVoy (1984). The authors also propose extending the analysis to identical atomic ion scattering, where polarization and Casimir-Polder potentials modify the TI condition.
