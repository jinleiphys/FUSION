# Revealing dressed-quarks via the proton's charge distribution

> The proton is arguably the most fundamental of Nature's readily detectable building blocks. It is at the heart of every nucleus and has never been observed to decay. It is nevertheless a composite object, defined by its valence-quark content: u+u+d -- i.e., two up (u) quarks and one down (d) quark; and the manner by which they influence, inter alia, the distribution of charge and magnetisation within this bound-state. Much of novelty has recently been learnt about these distributions; and it now appears possible that the proton's momentum-space charge distribution possesses a zero. Experiments in the coming decade should answer critical questions posed by this and related advances; and we explain how such new information may assist in charting the origin and impact of key emergent phenomena within the strong interaction. Specifically, we show that the possible existence and location of a zero in the proton's electric form factor are a measure of nonperturbative features of the quark-quark interaction in the Standard Model, with particular sensitivity to the running of the dressed-quark mass.

## Key claim

The possible existence and location of a zero in the proton's electric form factor ratio \(\mu_p G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)\) are a measure of nonperturbative features of the quark-quark interaction in the Standard Model, with particular sensitivity to the rate at which the dressed-quark mass runs from the nonperturbative into the perturbative domain of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The authors show that if the strength of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is suppressed, the zero is pushed to larger \(Q^2\) values or disappears entirely from the currently accessible experimental domain.

## Method

The authors performed a continuum computation of the proton's elastic form factors within the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework, solving the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation to obtain the proton's mass and amplitude. They introduced a damping factor \(\alpha\) into the dressed-quark propagator to modulate the rate at which the dressed-quark mass function drops toward its perturbative behaviour, thereby controlling the strength of DCSB. At each value of \(\alpha\), they constructed the electromagnetic current using a dressed-quark-photon vertex that includes both the longitudinal Ball-Chiu term and a transverse term expressing a dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment distribution, then computed the proton's elastic form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the reference form (\(\alpha=1.0\)), the ratio \(\mu_p G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)\) exhibits a zero at \(Q^2 \approx 8\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- As \(\alpha\) is increased from 1.0 to 2.0, the nucleon mass drops by less than 1%.
- When the chiral condensate \(\chi_0^\pi\) falls to roughly 80% of its unperturbed value (i.e., from \(-(0.250\ \text{GeV})^3\) toward smaller magnitudes), the zero disappears from the computed result on \(Q^2>0\).
- The chiral-limit condensate for \(\alpha=1.0\) is \(\langle \bar q q \rangle_0^\pi = -(0.250\ \text{GeV})^3\).
- The proton's electric form factor is particularly sensitive in the \(6\ \text{GeV}^2\) to \(10\ \text{GeV}^2\) range.

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based computation of proton elastic form factors from Cloet et al. (2008) and Chang et al. (2011), and contrasts with the earlier Rosenbluth-separation results that suggested \(\mu_p G_E/G_M \approx 1\) up to \(6\ \text{GeV}^2\). The authors build on polarisation-transfer experiments at Jefferson Lab (Jones 1999; Gayou 2001; Puckett 2010, 2011) that showed the ratio decreases almost linearly with \(Q^2\) and might become negative for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 8\ \text{GeV}^2\). The study enables future experiments to determine whether a zero exists in the proton's electric form factor, which would directly probe the momentum dependence of the dressed-quark mass function and the nature of DCSB in QCD.
