# Auxiliary Function Approach for Determining Symmetry Energy at Supra-saturation Densities

> Nuclear symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ at density $ρ$ is normally expanded or simply parameterized as a function of $χ=(ρ-ρ_0)/3ρ_0$ in the form of $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)\approx S+Lχ+2^{-1}K_{\rm{sym}}χ^2+6^{-1}J_{\rm{sym}}χ^3+\cdots$ using its magnitude $S$, slope $L $, curvature $K_{\rm{sym}}$ and skewness $J_{\rm{sym}}$ at the saturation density $ρ_0$ of nuclear matter. Much progress has been made in recent years in constraining especially the $S$ and $L$ parameters using various terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations. However, such kind of expansions/parameterizations do not converge at supra-saturation densities where $χ$ is not small enough, hindering an accurate determination of high-density $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ even if its characteristic parameters at $ρ_0$ are all well determined by experiments/observations. By expanding the $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ in terms of a properly chosen auxiliary function $Π_{\rm{sym}}(χ,Θ_{\rm{sym}})$ with a parameter $Θ_{\rm{sym}}$ fixed accurately by an experimental $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_{\rm{r}})$ value at a reference density $ρ_{\rm{r}}$, we show that the shortcomings of the $χ$-expansion can be completely removed or significantly reduced in determining the high-density behavior of $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$. In particular, using two significantly different auxiliary functions, we show that the new approach effectively incorporates higher $χ$-order contributions and converges to the same $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ much faster than the conventional $χ$-expansion at densities $\lesssim3ρ_0$. Several quantitative demonstrations using Monte Carlo simulations are given.

## Key claim

The conventional Taylor expansion of nuclear symmetry energy in terms of \(\chi = (\rho - \rho_0)/3\rho_0\) does not converge at supra-saturation densities because \(\chi\) is not small enough, but by expanding instead in terms of a properly chosen auxiliary function \(\Pi_{\rm{sym}}(\chi,\Theta_{\rm{sym}})\) whose parameter \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}}\) is fixed by an experimental value of \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_{\rm{r}})\) at a reference density \(\rho_{\rm{r}}\), the shortcomings of the \(\chi\)-expansion can be completely removed or significantly reduced for densities \(\lesssim 3\rho_0\), provided the auxiliary function is chosen appropriately.

## Method

The authors replace the conventional expansion variable \(\chi\) with a new expansion variable \(\widetilde{\nu}_{\rm{sym}}(\chi,\Theta_{\rm{sym}}) = \Pi_{\rm{sym}}(\chi,\Theta_{\rm{sym}}) - \Pi_{\rm{sym}}(0,\Theta_{\rm{sym}})\), where \(\Pi_{\rm{sym}}\) is an auxiliary function containing a parameter \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}}\). They test two significantly different forms for \(\Pi_{\rm{sym}}\): an exponential model \(\Pi_{\rm{sym}} = \exp[-\Theta_{\rm{sym}}(1+3\chi)]\) and an algebraic model \(\Pi_{\rm{sym}} = [1+\Theta_{\rm{sym}}(1+3\chi)]^{-1}\). The parameter \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}}\) is fixed by requiring the reconstructed \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho)\) to match the empirical value \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_{\rm{low}}) \approx 16.4 \pm 0.5\) MeV at \(\rho_{\rm{low}} \approx 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\). Monte Carlo simulations with \(n=10^4\) samples are performed using three different test sets for the characteristic parameters \(S, L, K_{\rm{sym}}, J_{\rm{sym}}\) at saturation density, and the reconstructed symmetry energies from the auxiliary-function-based expansion are compared with those from the conventional \(\chi\)-expansion.

## Key numbers

- The conventional \(\chi\)-expansion truncated at order \(\chi^3\) breaks down near \(2\rho_0\) when tested against the RMF/FSUGold model, while the auxiliary-function-based expansion to order \(\nu_{\rm{sym}}^3\) successfully reconstructs the same symmetry energy up to about \(5\rho_0\).
- For the exponential model with test set I (truncated at \(\nu_{\rm{sym}}^2\)), the auxiliary-function-based expansion gives \(E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0) = 44.8 \pm 5.9\) MeV and \(E_{\rm{sym}}(3\rho_0) = 48.6 \pm 8.9\) MeV; the conventional expansion gives \(E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0) = 43.7 \pm 8.4\) MeV and \(E_{\rm{sym}}(3\rho_0) = 36.0 \pm 23.7\) MeV.
- For the exponential model with test set II (truncated at \(\nu_{\rm{sym}}^3\)), the auxiliary-function-based expansion gives \(E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0) = 43.3 \pm 6.4\) MeV and \(E_{\rm{sym}}(3\rho_0) = 51.8 \pm 18.0\) MeV; the conventional expansion gives \(E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0) = 44.9 \pm 8.1\) MeV and \(E_{\rm{sym}}(3\rho_0) = 72.0 \pm 36.3\) MeV.
- For the exponential model with test set III (truncated at \(\nu_{\rm{sym}}^3\) with intrinsic relations for \(K_{\rm{sym}}\) and \(J_{\rm{sym}}\)), the auxiliary-function-based expansion gives \(E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0) = 43.7 \pm 7.6\) MeV and \(E_{\rm{sym}}(3\rho_0) = 44.7 \pm 22.3\) MeV; the conventional expansion gives \(E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0) = 40.3 \pm 10.5\) MeV and \(E_{\rm{sym}}(3\rho_0) = 13.4 \pm 46.0\) MeV.
- The parameter \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}}\) in the exponential model is found to be \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}} \approx 1.67 \pm 0.56\) for set I, \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}} \approx 1.41 \pm 0.88\) for set II, and \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}} \approx 1.74 \pm 0.81\) for set III.

## Context

This work extends the conventional Taylor-expansion approach to nuclear symmetry energy (e.g., as used in meta-modelings by Steiner (2005), Zhang (2018), and Xie (2019, 2020)) by addressing its well-known divergence at supra-saturation densities. The auxiliary-function method is analogous to using an effective spring constant in a forced oscillator problem to incorporate higher-order anharmonic effects. The approach enables a nearly model-independent determination of \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho)\) up to about \(3\rho_0\) using only the low-order characteristics \(S, L, K_{\rm{sym}}, J_{\rm{sym}}\) at saturation density, which is the density range most relevant for the radii and tidal deformations of canonical neutron stars. Future work should consider self-consistently determining \(\Theta_{\rm{sym}}\) together with the characteristic parameters from experimental data and astrophysical observations.
