# Cooling of Small and Massive Hyperonic Stars

> We perform cooling simulations for isolated neutron stars using recently developed equations of state for their core. The equations of state are obtained from new parametrizations of the FSU2 relativistic mean-field functional that reproduce the properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei, while fulfilling the restrictions on high-density matter deduced from heavy-ion collisions, measurements of massive 2$M_{\odot}$ neutron stars, and neutron star radii below 13 km. We find that two of the models studied, FSU2R (with nucleons) and in particular FSU2H (with nucleons and hyperons), show very good agreement with cooling observations, even without including extensive nucleon pairing. This suggests that the cooling observations are more compatible with an equation of state that produces a soft nuclear symmetry energy and, hence, generates small neutron star radii. However, both models favor large stellar masses, above $1.8 M_{\odot}$, to explain the colder isolated neutron stars that have been observed, even if nucleon pairing is present.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two specific relativistic mean-field models (FSU2R with nucleons and FSU2H with nucleons and hyperons) show very good agreement with observed neutron star cooling data even without extensive nucleon pairing, but this agreement requires that the colder isolated neutron stars have masses above 1.8 solar masses, even when nucleon pairing is included.

## Method

The authors performed cooling simulations for isolated neutron stars by solving the equations of thermal balance and thermal energy transport, using equations of state derived from new parametrizations of the FSU2 relativistic mean-field functional. They calculated neutrino emissivities, specific heats, and thermal conductivities for all allowed processes involving nucleons and hyperons, and compared the resulting surface temperatures as functions of stellar age against observational data from a set of prominent neutron stars including Cassiopeia A.

## Key numbers

- FSU2R(nuc) model predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 2.048 solar masses and a radius of 12.8 km for a 1.4 solar mass star
- FSU2H(hyp) model predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 2.023 solar masses and a radius of 13.2 km for a 1.4 solar mass star
- The symmetry energy slope parameter L is 46.9 MeV for FSU2R and 44.5 MeV for FSU2H
- The nucleonic direct URCA threshold density is 0.61 fm^{-3} for both FSU2R(nuc) and FSU2H(nuc)
- The hyperonic direct URCA threshold density (from Lambda particles) is 0.34 fm^{-3} for FSU2H(hyp)
- FSU2H(hyp) shows fast cooling for 2.0 solar mass stars (central density 0.71 fm^{-3}) and slow cooling for 1.4 solar mass stars (central density 0.34 fm^{-3})

## Context

This work extends the FSU2 relativistic mean-field model developed by Chen (2014) by creating new parametrizations (FSU2R and FSU2H) that reconcile 2 solar mass neutron star observations with recent analyses indicating neutron star radii below 13 km. The study builds on previous cooling investigations by Prakash (1992) and more recent work by Raduta (2018) on hyperonic stars, while also incorporating constraints from heavy-ion collision data by Danielewicz (2002) and gravitational-wave constraints from the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2017). The results enable future studies of hyperonic pairing, stellar rotation, and magnetic field effects on neutron star cooling.
