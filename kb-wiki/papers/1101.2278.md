# Fermi matrix element with isospin breaking

> Prompted by the level of accuracy now being achieved in tests of the unitarity of the CKM matrix, we consider the possible modification of the Fermi matrix element for the $β$-decay of a neutron, including possible in-medium and isospin violating corrections. While the nuclear modifications lead to very small corrections once the Behrends-Sirlin-Ademollo-Gatto theorem is respected, the effect of the $u-d$ mass difference on the conclusion concerning $V_{ud}$ is no longer insignificant. Indeed, we suggest that the correction to the value of $|V_{ud}|^2 \, + \, |V_{us}|^2 \, + \, |V_{ub}|^2$ is at the level of $10^{-4}$.

# Fermi matrix element with isospin breaking

## Key claim
The correction to the Fermi matrix element for neutron beta decay from the u-d quark mass difference, when calculated consistently with the Behrends-Sirlin-Ademollo-Gatto theorem, produces a deviation of order 2 × 10⁻⁵ at zero density, which combined with a pionic correction yields a total shift in the CKM unitarity sum of about 1.2 × 10⁻⁴, a factor of 5 below the current experimental error but no longer insignificant.

## Method
The authors use the MIT bag model to compute the overlap defect in the Fermi matrix element for the neutron-to-proton transition, starting from the isospin-symmetric limit and treating the quark mass difference δm = m_d − m_u as a perturbation to second order, ensuring consistency with the BSAG theorem. They evaluate the scalar matrix elements between the ground-state nucleon and excited bag eigenmodes as a function of nuclear density, using the QMC model to determine the effective quark mass in the medium. The result is combined with an earlier chiral perturbation theory estimate of pion mass difference effects by Kaiser (2001).

## Key numbers
- The overlap defect (deviation of the Fermi matrix element from unity) at zero density is 1.94 × 10⁻⁵, using a u-d mass difference of 5 MeV appropriate to the MIT bag.
- The overlap defect changes by only a few times 10⁻⁶ as density varies from zero to nuclear matter density.
- The pionic correction from Kaiser (2001) gives a reduction of about 4 × 10⁻⁵ from charged-neutral pion mass differences.
- The total correction to the vector coupling constant g_V is δg_V = 6 × 10⁻⁵.
- The corresponding increase in |V_ud|² + |V_us|² + |V_ub|² is about 1.2 × 10⁻⁴.
- The isovector scalar potential V_δ in heavy nuclei may be as large as 4 or 5 MeV, potentially producing a decrease in the apparent V_ud as large as 2 × 10⁻⁵.

## Context
This work addresses corrections to the Fermi matrix element for neutron beta decay that become relevant at the current level of precision in CKM unitarity tests, where the experimental sum |V_ud|² + |V_us|² + |V_ub|² = 0.99995 ± 0.00061. It corrects an earlier claim by Saito (1995) that the density-dependent difference between neutron and proton radii would violate the BSAG theorem, showing that the earlier result was an artefact of the static bag model. The calculation extends the treatment of isospin breaking in the d → u transition, which has received less attention than the s → u transition, and combines the valence quark overlap correction with the pionic correction of Kaiser (2001). The results enable a more precise extraction of V_ud from super-allowed nuclear beta decay and identify a new systematic error from the isovector scalar mean field in nuclei with neutron excess that may need to be accounted for in future measurements.
