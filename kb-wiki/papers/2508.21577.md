# Transport-model investigation of scaling of the number of constituent quarks and the hadronic-partonic transition in Au + Au collisions

> We investigate the elliptic flow ($v_2$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 3.0$ and 4.5 GeV using both hadronic and partonic transport models, including JAM, SMASH, AMPT-Hadronic Cascade, and AMPT-String Melting. At 3.0 GeV, the JAM model reproduces the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling violation observed by STAR, as well as the particle ordering ($K^0_S > p > π^+$). Model calculations of the centrality dependence indicate that the scaling violation mainly originates from hadronic interactions rather than spectator effects, while the rapidity dependence further constrains the mechanism of the scaling breaking and the underlying longitudinal dynamics. At 4.5 GeV, partonic interactions in the AMPT-String Melting mode significantly enhance NCQ scaling, and turning off final-state hadronic rescattering further clarifies the scaling pattern, highlighting the increasing role of partonic degrees of freedom. The energy dependence of the $p_T$-integrated $v_2$ is also examined within these models.

# Transport-model investigation of scaling of the number of constituent quarks and the hadronic-partonic transition in Au + Au collisions

## Key claim

At √sNN = 3.0 GeV, hadronic transport models (particularly JAM) reproduce the violation of number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling and the particle ordering (K⁰S > p > π⁺) observed by STAR, indicating that the scaling violation originates primarily from hadronic interactions rather than spectator effects; at 4.5 GeV, partonic interactions in the AMPT-String Melting mode significantly enhance NCQ scaling, with the scaling becoming even more pronounced when final-state hadronic rescattering is turned off.

## Method

The authors performed transport-model simulations of Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3.0 and 4.5 GeV using four models: JAM version 2.1, SMASH version 2.0, AMPT-Hadronic Cascade (AMPT-HC), and AMPT-String Melting (AMPT-SM). They calculated the elliptic flow (v₂) for π⁺, K⁺, K⁰S, p, and Λ as a function of transverse momentum (pT), centrality (0–10%, 10–40%, 40–80%), and rapidity (mid-rapidity 0 < |y| < 0.5 and forward rapidity 0.5 < |y| < 1.0), then examined NCQ scaling by plotting v₂/nq versus pT/nq. At 4.5 GeV, they compared three AMPT configurations: pure hadronic cascade, string melting with hadronic afterburner, and string melting without final-state hadronic interactions.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 3.0 GeV, the passing time of nuclear spectators in the center-of-mass frame is 11.22 fm/c; at 4.5 GeV, it decreases to 6.43 fm/c, a reduction of roughly 40%
- The spectator velocity increases from 0.78 c at 3.0 GeV to 0.91 c at 4.5 GeV
- The nuclear ground state density used in the mean-field potential is ρ₀ = 0.168 fm⁻³
- The nuclear incompressibility used for JAM and SMASH is K = 210 MeV (soft equation of state)
- The radius of a gold nucleus used in passing-time calculations is R = 6.98 fm

## Context

This work extends the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) program findings, where STAR observed that NCQ scaling holds down to √sNN ≈ 7.7 GeV (STAR, 2013; STAR, 2015) but breaks at 3.0 GeV (STAR, 2021), with new STAR measurements at 3.2–4.5 GeV (STAR, 2025) suggesting a possible partial reappearance of NCQ scaling. The study builds on a recent AMPT investigation of NCQ scaling at 3.0–7.7 GeV (Zhu, 2025) and provides systematic multi-model comparisons across centralities and rapidities. The results enable future experimental tests at FAIR-CBM, JINR-NICA, and HIRFL/HIAF-CEE, and motivate improvements to AMPT-SM for high-baryon-density collisions, including hadronic mean-field effects and finite nuclear thickness treatment.
