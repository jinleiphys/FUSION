# Local Thermodynamical Equilibrium and the Equation of State of Hot, Dense Matter Created in Au+Au Collisions at AGS

> Local kinetic and chemical equilibration is studied for Au+Au collisions at 10.7 AGeV in the microscopic Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD). The UrQMD model exhibits dramatic deviations from equilibrium during the high density phase of the collision. Thermal and chemical equilibration of the hadronic matter seems to be established in the later stages during a quasi-isentropic expansion, observed in the central reaction cell ($S/A=S/A=s/ρ_B ~\cong~$ 12). At latest stages of the reactions (of order of 10 fm/c) the hadron energy spectra in the cell are nicely reproduced by Boltzmann distributions with a common rapidly dropping temperature. Hadron yields change drastically and at the late expansion stage follow closely those of an ideal gas statistical model. The detailed comparison with the UrQMD box calculations for infinite equilibrated matter is done. It also shows that the system in the central zone reaches the equilibrium at late stages of the reaction. The equation of state seems to be simple at late times: $P\cong0.12ε$ which coincides with Shuryak predictions for the resonance gas. The contribution of the strange particles into the total energy density of the system in the central region of the reaction seems to be of order of 1-3 % (strange particle density in the central zone is negligibly small e.g. $ρ_S=0.008$). The time evolution of other thermodynamical variables in the cell is also presented.

## Key claim

Local thermodynamical equilibrium (LTE) is not reached during the high-density phase of Au+Au collisions at 10.7 AGeV, but appears to be established in the central reaction cell only during the later quasi-isentropic expansion stage (t = 10–18 fm/c), with the caveat that the system never achieves global equilibrium and the analysis is restricted to a small central cell where collective flow is minimal.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate 200 central (b=0 fm) Au+Au collisions at 10.7 AGeV. They analyzed a cubic cell of volume 125 fm³ (5×5×5 fm³) centered at the origin of the center-of-mass frame, calculating time-dependent energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density. These quantities were then used as input to an ideal hadron gas statistical model to extract temperature T, baryon chemical potential μ_B, and strange chemical potential μ_S by solving three coupled equations, and the resulting spectra and yields were compared to the UrQMD cell results and to UrQMD box calculations of infinite equilibrated matter.

## Key numbers

- Central cell volume: 125 fm³ (5×5×5 fm³)
- Maximum energy density in central cell: ε ≅ 21 ε₀ (where ε₀ is normal nuclear matter energy density), reached at t ≅ 4–5 fm/c
- Maximum baryon density in central cell: ρ_B ≅ 10 ρ₀ (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), reached at t ≅ 4–5 fm/c
- Temperature in central cell drops from T ≅ 150 MeV at t = 10 fm/c to T ≅ 100 MeV at t = 18 fm/c
- Baryon density in central cell drops from ρ_B ≅ 2 ρ₀ at t = 10 fm/c to 0.3 ρ₀ at t = 18 fm/c
- Equation of state at late times: P/ε ≅ 0.12 (constant for t = 10–18 fm/c)
- Speed of sound squared: c_s² ≅ 0.12
- Entropy per baryon in central cell: S/A = s/ρ_B ≅ 12 (nearly constant for t = 10–18 fm/c)
- Strange particle contribution to total energy density: 1–3%
- Strange particle density in central zone: ρ_S = 0.008 fm⁻³
- Temperature agreement across particle species: ΔT ≅ 10 MeV
- Average squared flow velocities in central cell at t = 10 fm/c: ⟨(v_z^flow)²⟩ ≅ 0.04, ⟨(v_{x,y}^flow)²⟩ ≅ 0.01
- Thermal velocity for nucleons at t = 10 fm/c: ⟨v_th²⟩ ≅ 3T/m_N ≅ 0.5

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic analyses of thermalization in heavy-ion collisions by Berenguer et al. and Goren et al. and Konopka et al. at lower energies, using the UrQMD model to study Au+Au collisions at AGS energy. The finding that the equation of state P ≅ 0.12ε matches the prediction of Shuryak for a resonance gas, and the observation that no phase transition is evident (no softest point in the EoS), contradicts expectations from one-fluid hydrodynamical models that assume local isotropy at all times. The results enable future studies of LTE at earlier collision phases and at higher energies (AGS to SPS), requiring smaller cell sizes and larger event samples.
