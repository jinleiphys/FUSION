# Systematic calculations of cluster radioactivity half-lives in trans-lead nuclei

> In the present work, based on Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) theory, considering the cluster preformation probability (Pc), we systematically investigate the cluster radioactivity half-lives of 22 trans-lead nuclei ranging from 221Fr to 242Cm. As for Pc, when the mass number of the emitted cluster Ac<28, it is obtained by the exponential relationship of Pc to the alpha decay preformation probability Pa proposed by R.Blendowskeis et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 61, 1930 (1988)], while Pa is calculated through cluster-formation model (CFM). Whereas Ac >= 28, it is achieved through the charge-number dependence of Pc on the decay products proposed by Ren et al. [Phys. Rev. C 70, 034304 (2004)]. The half-lives of cluster radioactivity have been calculated by the density-dependent cluster model [Phys. Rev. C 70, 034304 (2004)] and by the unified formula of half-lives for alpha decay and cluster radioactivity [Phys. Rev. C 78, 044310 (2008)]. For comparison, a universal decay law (UDL) proposed by Qi et al. [Phys. Rev. C 80, 044326 (2009)], a semi-empirical model for both alpha decay and cluster radioactivity proposed by Santhosh [J. Phys. G: Nucl. Part. Phys. 35, 085102 (2008)] and a unified formula of half-lives for alpha decay and cluster radioactivity [Phys. Rev. C 78, 044310 (2008)] are also used. The calculated results in our work, Ni's formula as well as UDL can well reproduce the experimental data and are better than those in Santhosh's model. In addition, we extend this model to predict the half-lives for 51 nuclei, whose cluster radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but yet not quantified in NUBASE2020.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate cluster radioactivity half-lives for 22 trans-lead nuclei using WKB theory with a cluster preformation probability (Pc) that is obtained through two different prescriptions depending on the mass number of the emitted cluster: an exponential relationship to alpha decay preformation probability for Ac less than 28, and a charge-number dependence on the decay products for Ac greater than or equal to 28. The calculated results reproduce experimental data well, with standard deviations of 1.035 for Ac less than 28 and 0.423 for Ac greater than or equal to 28, though the model shows discrepancies exceeding 1.8 for specific cases such as 231Pa to 208Pb plus 23F.

## Method

The authors employed the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation to calculate the barrier penetration probability for cluster emission, using a total interaction potential composed of a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential, a Coulomb potential for a uniformly charged sphere, and a Langer-modified centrifugal potential. For the cluster preformation probability Pc, when the emitted cluster mass number Ac is less than 28, they used the exponential relationship Pc = [Palpha]^((Ac-1)/3) proposed by Blendowske and Walliser (1988), with Palpha obtained from the cluster-formation model (CFM); for Ac greater than or equal to 28, they used the charge-number dependent empirical formula of Ren et al. (2004). The assault frequency was calculated as nu = pi hbar / (2 mu Rin^2), and half-lives were compared with those from the universal decay law (UDL) of Qi et al. (2009), Santhosh's semi-empirical model (2008), and Ni's unified formula (2008).

## Key numbers

* Standard deviation sigma between experimental and calculated half-lives for Ac less than 28: 1.035 for the authors' model (Cal1), compared to 0.365 for Ni's formula, 1.244 for UDL, and 1.880 for Santhosh's model.
* Standard deviation sigma for Ac greater than or equal to 28: 0.423 for Cal1, 0.425 for Ni's formula, 0.569 for UDL, and 3.594 for Santhosh's model.
* Overall standard deviation for all 22 nuclei: 0.910 for Cal1, 0.382 for Ni's formula, 1.102 for UDL, and 2.469 for Santhosh's model.
* For 222Ra to 208Pb plus 14C, experimental log10(T1/2) = 11.22 s, with Cal1 giving 12.10, Ni's formula giving 11.02, and UDL giving 10.07.
* For 242Cm to 208Pb plus 34Si, experimental log10(T1/2) = 23.15 s, with Cal1 giving 23.43, Ni's formula giving 23.48, and Santhosh's model giving 28.86 (discrepancy of 5.71).
* For 231Pa to 208Pb plus 23F, experimental log10(T1/2) = 26.02 s, with Cal1 giving 24.06 (discrepancy of 1.962) and Santhosh's model giving 21.71 (discrepancy of 4.31).
* The model was extended to predict half-lives for 51 nuclei whose cluster radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not quantified in NUBASE2020.

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent cluster model (DDCM) of Ren et al. (2004) and the unified formula of Ni et al. (2008) by incorporating a mass-number-dependent prescription for the cluster preformation probability, using CFM for lighter clusters and the charge-number dependence for heavier clusters. The results improve upon the semi-empirical model of Santhosh (2008), which showed larger standard deviations, and are comparable to the UDL of Qi et al. (2009) and Ni's formula. The predictions for 51 unquantified nuclei provide theoretical guidance for future experimental searches, and the authors note that future work should consider dynamical shape deformations of both the cluster and daughter nucleus, as recent studies have shown their importance in barrier penetration.
