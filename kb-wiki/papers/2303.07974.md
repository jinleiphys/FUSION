# Thermal effects in hot and dilute homogeneous asymmetric nuclear matter

> We present a comprehensive analysis of hot and dilute isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter employing the temperature-dependent effective-relativistic mean-field theory (E-RMF). The E-RMF is applied to study the effect of $δ$ and $ω-ρ$ meson cross-coupling on the thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter using two recently developed IOPB-I and G3 parameter sets. These sets are known to reproduce the nuclear matter properties in agreement with various experimental and observational constraints. We consider the nuclear matter to be homogeneous and study the equation of state (EoS) for densities, temperature and asymmetry which are relevant for astrophysical simulations such as supernovae explosion. The effect of temperature is investigated in reference to the density-dependent free symmetry energy and its higher-order derivatives using the well known parabolic approximation. The larger value of $λ_ω$ cross-coupling in G3 in addition to the $δ$ meson coupling in G3 smoothen the free symmetry energy. Thermal effects on various state variables are examined at fixed temperature and isospin asymmetry by separating their T=0 and the finite-T expressions. The thermal effects are mainly governed by effective mass with larger effective mass estimating larger thermal contribution. The effect of temperature on isothermal and isentropic incompressibility is discussed which is in harmony with various available microscopic calculations. The liquid-gas phase transition properties are examined in asymmetric matter with two conserved charges in the context of different slope parameter and comparable symmetry energy in IOPB-I and G3 set. The spinodal instability, binodal curve and critical properties are found to be influenced by the slope parameter $L_{sym}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the thermal properties of hot and dilute homogeneous asymmetric nuclear matter are governed primarily by the effective mass, with larger effective mass estimating larger thermal contributions; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of E-RMF parameter set (IOPB-I versus G3), which differ in their inclusion of delta meson coupling and omega-rho cross-coupling strength.

## Method

The authors employed the temperature-dependent effective-relativistic mean-field theory (E-RMF) to study homogeneous isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature. They used two recently developed parameter sets, IOPB-I and G3, which differ in their self and cross couplings of sigma, omega, rho, and delta mesons, and computed the equation of state for densities, temperatures, and asymmetries relevant to supernova simulations. The thermal effects were isolated by subtracting the T=0 expressions from the finite-T expressions for various thermodynamic functions, and the liquid-gas phase transition was analyzed using Gibbs conditions for a two-component system with conserved baryon number and isospin.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density for IOPB-I: 0.149 fm^-3; for G3: 0.148 fm^-3 (empirical range 0.148-0.185 fm^-3)
* Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: IOPB-I = -16.10 MeV; G3 = -16.02 MeV (empirical range -15.0 to -17.0 MeV)
* Symmetry energy J: IOPB-I = 33.30 MeV; G3 = 31.84 MeV (empirical range 30.0-33.70 MeV)
* Slope parameter L: IOPB-I = 63.58 MeV; G3 = 49.31 MeV (empirical range 35.0-70.0 MeV)
* Incompressibility K: IOPB-I = 222.65 MeV; G3 = 243.96 MeV (empirical range 220-260 MeV)
* Maximum neutron star mass: IOPB-I = 2.15 solar masses; G3 = 2.03 solar masses
* Canonical mass radius for G3: 12.69 km
* Maximum thermal index for IOPB-I: 2.1 for pure neutron matter, 1.97 for symmetric nuclear matter; for G3: 1.96 and 1.87 respectively
* Temperature of matter in supernova rises to 20 MeV; density can vary up to two times nuclear saturation density
* Asymmetry parameter in supernova calculations: approximately 0.4

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature study of asymmetric nuclear matter using E-RMF formalism, building on previous calculations by Constantinou (2014, 2015) for Skyrme and APR forces, and by Alam (2017) for supernova-relevant conditions. The results are compared with microscopic chiral effective field theory calculations by Hebeler (2013) and with many-body self-consistent Green's function method by Carbone (2019). The study enables the use of these newly developed parameter sets for astrophysical simulations of core-collapse supernovae, proto-neutron stars, and neutron star mergers, where the temperature-dependent equation of state and liquid-gas phase transition properties are essential inputs.
