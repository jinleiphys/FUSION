# Proton size anomaly

> A measurement of the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen yields a charge radius of the proton that is smaller than the CODATA value by about 5 standard deviations. We explore the possibility that new scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and tensor flavor-conserving nonuniversal interactions may be responsible for the discrepancy. We consider exotic particles that among leptons, couple preferentially to muons, and mediate an attractive nucleon-muon interaction. We find that the many constraints from low energy data disfavor new spin-0, spin-1 and spin-2 particles as an explanation.

# Proton size anomaly

## Key claim
New scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and tensor flavor-conserving nonuniversal interactions that couple preferentially to muons and mediate an attractive nucleon-muon interaction are disfavored as an explanation for the 5-standard-deviation discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen and CODATA proton charge radius, based on multiple low-energy constraints.

## Method
The authors computed the correction to the muonic Lamb shift from a Yukawa-type potential mediated by new spin-0, spin-1, and spin-2 particles coupling to muons and nucleons. They then compared the coupling strength required to explain the proton radius anomaly against independent experimental constraints from neutron-lead scattering, the muon anomalous magnetic moment, muonic atom X-ray transitions, and decays of Upsilon, J/psi, pi0, and eta mesons.

## Key numbers
- CODATA proton charge radius: 0.8768 ± 0.0069 fm (from atomic hydrogen spectroscopy and electron-proton scattering)
- Muonic hydrogen proton charge radius: 0.84184 ± 0.00067 fm (from Lamb shift measurement)
- Measured Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen (2P_{3/2}^{F=2} → 2S_{1/2}^{F=1}): 206.2949 ± 0.0032 meV
- Predicted Lamb shift formula: ΔE = 209.9779(49) - 5.2262⟨r_p²⟩ + 0.0347⟨r_p²⟩^{3/2} (radii in fm, energy in meV)
- Muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy: Δa_μ = (29 ± 9) × 10^{-10}
- For m_χ < 10 MeV, 95% C.L. upper bounds on muonic couplings: C_μ^S < 4.8 × 10^{-4}, C_μ^V < 5.9 × 10^{-4}, C_μ^T < 3.2 × 10^{-4}
- For m_χ < 10 MeV, combined 95% C.L. constraints: α_χ/α < (m_χ/2847)² (scalar), < (m_χ/2573)² (vector), < (m_χ/3477)² (tensor), with m_χ in MeV
- Neutron-lead scattering 95% C.L. bound on vector nucleon coupling: C_n^V ≤ (m_χ/206)², with m_χ in MeV
- A vector χ with mass between 25 MeV and 210 MeV is a viable candidate; a scalar χ with mass between 70 MeV and 210 MeV is marginally allowed; spin-2 χ is excluded

## Context
This paper addresses the "proton radius puzzle" first reported by Pohl (2010), where muonic hydrogen spectroscopy gave a proton charge radius 4% smaller than the CODATA value from electron-based measurements. The authors systematically test whether new force carriers could explain the discrepancy, extending earlier work by Marciano (2010) who suggested a new gauge interaction at the scale α m_μ. The analysis rules out simple spin-0, spin-1, and spin-2 explanations using existing low-energy data, pointing toward more complicated scenarios such as combined scalar-pseudoscalar interactions with tuned couplings, or isospin- or CP-violating interactions, as possible future directions.
