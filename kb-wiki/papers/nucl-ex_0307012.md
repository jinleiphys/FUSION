# Low-Q scaling, duality, and the EMC effect

> High energy lepton scattering has been the primary tool for mapping out the quark distributions of nucleons and nuclei. Data on the proton and deuteron have shown that there is a fundamental connection between the low and high energy regimes, referred to as quark-hadron duality. We present the results of similar studies to more carefully examine scaling, duality, and in particular the EMC effect in nuclei. We extract nuclear modifications to the structure function in the resonance region, and for the first time demonstrate that nuclear effects in the resonance region are identical to those measured in deep inelastic scattering. With the improved precision of the data at large $x$, we for the first time observe that the large-x crossover point appears to occur at lower $x$ values in carbon than in iron or gold.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that nuclear effects on the inclusive structure function measured in the resonance region (low W²) are identical to those measured in deep inelastic scattering (DIS), and that the large-x crossover point where the nuclear-to-deuterium ratio exceeds unity occurs at lower x values in carbon than in iron or gold, contradicting the expectation that heavier nuclei would show an earlier onset of the Fermi motion enhancement.

## Method

The authors extracted cross section ratios of carbon, iron, and gold to deuterium from Jefferson Lab Hall C inclusive electron scattering data taken at Q² ≈ 4 GeV² in the resonance region (1.2 < W² < 3.0 GeV²). They compared these ratios to previous DIS measurements from SLAC E139, SLAC E87, and BCDMS, applying Coulomb corrections and corrections for neutron excess assuming σn/σp = (1 − 0.8ξ). The structure functions were analyzed using the Nachtmann variable ξ to reduce target mass corrections at finite Q².

## Key numbers

- For carbon at ξ = 0.592 (W² = 2.86 GeV²), the isoscalar cross section ratio σC/σD = 0.921 ± 0.012
- For iron at ξ = 0.592, σFe/σD = 0.901 ± 0.013
- For gold at ξ = 0.592, σAu/σD = 0.844 ± 0.013
- For carbon at ξ = 0.810 (W² = 1.26 GeV²), the ratio rises above unity: σC/σD = 1.195 ± 0.014
- For iron at ξ = 0.810, σFe/σD = 1.133 ± 0.015
- For gold at ξ = 0.810, σAu/σD = 1.138 ± 0.024
- The resonance region structure functions for hydrogen reproduce the scaling curve to better than 2% globally and 5% locally around each resonance for Q² > 1 GeV²
- At Q² ≈ 2 GeV² and W² ≈ MΔ², scaling violations for deuterium are as large as 50%, but averaging over the Δ region yields EMC ratios differing from DIS by less than 10%

## Context

This work extends the study of quark-hadron duality, first observed by Bloom and Gilman (1971) for the proton, to nuclear targets and the EMC effect first observed by Aubert et al. (1983). The results contradict the factorization of A and x dependence predicted by an effective field theory calculation by Chen (2005), and show an A-dependent crossover at high ξ that was predicted by Gross and Liuti (1992) using a covariant convolution formula. The data enable improved constraints on conventional nuclear binding and Fermi motion effects, which must be understood before isolating more exotic explanations for the EMC effect, and motivate future measurements from Jefferson Lab experiments E03-103 and E00-116 that will extend coverage to light nuclei and higher ξ values.
