# Structure of A=3 Nuclear Systems Using Realistic Hamiltonians

> The structure of A=3 low-energy scattering states is described using the hyperspherical harmonics method with realistic Hamiltonian models, consisting of two- and three-nucleon interactions. Both coordinate and momentum space two-nucleon potential models are considered.

## Key claim

The hyperspherical harmonics method can successfully describe low-energy A=3 scattering states using both local and non-local two-nucleon potentials, but the doublet scattering lengths become model-independent only after a three-nucleon interaction is included and the triton binding energy is reproduced.

## Method

The authors solved the A-body Schrödinger equation for n-d and p-d scattering states using the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method, expanding the wave function in a complete set of HH basis states in either coordinate or momentum space. The unknown expansion coefficients were obtained via the Rayleigh-Ritz (Kohn) variational principle for bound (scattering) states, and matrix elements were calculated in the space most convenient for each operator. The calculations used the local AV18 potential and the non-local N3LO-Idaho potential, with and without the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon interaction, and included the Coulomb interaction for p-d systems.

## Key numbers

- n-d doublet scattering length with N3LO-Idaho/UIX: 0.623 fm (experimental: 0.65(4) fm)
- n-d quartet scattering length with N3LO-Idaho/UIX: 6.343 fm (experimental: 6.35(2) fm)
- p-d doublet scattering length with N3LO-Idaho/UIX: -0.007 fm
- p-d quartet scattering length with N3LO-Idaho/UIX: 13.647 fm
- Triton binding energy with N3LO-Idaho/UIX: 8.481 MeV
- Helium-3 binding energy with N3LO-Idaho/UIX: 7.730 MeV
- Helium-4 binding energy with N3LO-Idaho/UIX: 28.534 MeV
- The UIX TNI spin-isospin independent part was rescaled by a factor of 0.384 to fit the triton binding energy
- N3LO-Idaho and N3LO-Idaho/UIX results are accurate at the 10^-3 fm level

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical harmonics method to treat low-energy scattering with non-local two-nucleon interactions, following previous applications to bound states by Viviani (2006). The results confirm the well-known Phillips line correlation between the n-d doublet scattering length and triton binding energy, first noted by Phillips (1977). The authors report that a similar investigation for A=4 scattering lengths has been performed by Viviani (2007), and further work at higher energies is underway.
