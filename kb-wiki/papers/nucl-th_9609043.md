# Nuclear Shell Model by the Quantum Monte Carlo Diagonalization Method

> The feasibility of shell-model calculations is radically extended by the Quantum Monte Carlo Diagonalization method with various essential improvements. The major improvements are made in the sampling for the generation of shell-model basis vectors, and in the restoration of symmetries such as angular momentum and isospin. Consequently the level structure of low-lying states can be studied with realistic interactions. After testing this method on $^{24}$Mg, we present first results for energy levels and $E2$ properties of $^{64}$Ge, indicating its large and $γ$-soft deformation.

# Nuclear Shell Model by the Quantum Monte Carlo Diagonalization Method

## Key claim

The Quantum Monte Carlo Diagonalization (QMCD) method, with improved sampling based on local Hartree-Fock energy minima and explicit restoration of angular momentum and isospin symmetries, can perform large-scale shell-model calculations for nuclei with basis dimensions up to ~1×10^9, achieving ground-state energies within ~0.17 MeV of exact values and excitation energies within ~0.15 MeV, though the method carries systematic truncation errors of several hundred keV in total energies.

## Method

The authors decompose the shell-model Hamiltonian into a quadratic form of one-body operators, then apply the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to express the imaginary-time evolution operator as an integral over auxiliary fields. Basis states are generated as Slater determinants by stochastically sampling these auxiliary fields, with sampling guided by local Hartree-Fock energy minima and by ordering fields according to their coupling strength and excitation sum-rules. Angular momentum restoration is accelerated by including rotated states (J-drive) in the basis, and isospin is conserved for N=Z nuclei by initially using only isoscalar one-body operators.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁴Mg with the USD interaction: ground-state energy = −86.91 MeV with 800 QMCD basis states, compared to exact value of −87.08 MeV (deviation of 0.17 MeV); the m-scheme basis dimension for the ground state is 28,503, giving a reduction factor of 1/35.
- Excitation energies for ²⁴Mg with 400 QMCD basis states: E_x(2⁺₁) = 1.54 MeV (exact 1.51), E_x(2⁺₂) = 4.23 MeV (exact 4.12), E_x(4⁺₁) = 4.50 MeV (exact 4.37); deviations less than 0.15 MeV.
- For ⁶⁴Ge: the m-scheme dimension of the M=0 space is 1,087,455,228, approximately 550 times larger than for ⁴⁸Cr.
- For ⁶⁴Ge: B(E2; 2⁺₁→0⁺₁) = 5×10² e²fm⁴ (with e_p=1.33e, e_n=0.64e), corresponding to β₂ ≈ 0.28.
- For ⁶⁴Ge: ratio of excitation energies E_x(2⁺₂)/E_x(2⁺₁) = 1.9 (experiment 1.75) and E_x(4⁺₁)/E_x(2⁺₁) = 2.6 (experiment 2.27).
- For ⁶⁴Ge: B(E2; 2⁺₂→0⁺₁)/B(E2; 2⁺₂→2⁺₁) ≈ 2×10⁻³, suggesting γ ≈ 30° in triaxial deformation models.
- Statistical error for ⁶⁴Ge: approximately 100 keV for the 2⁺₂ energy level.

## Context

This work extends the QMCD method previously applied to the Interacting Boson Model (Honma, Mizusaki, Otsuka 1995) to realistic shell-model calculations with full pf-shell valence spaces, overcoming the limitations of direct diagonalization (which had been carried out up to ⁴⁸Cr by Caurier, Poves, and Martinez-Pinedo 1994) and of the Shell Model Monte Carlo method (Koonin, Dean, Langanke), which is primarily useful for ground-state and thermal properties. The method enables first shell-model calculations for ⁶⁴Ge, a proton-rich N=Z nucleus whose γ-soft structure had previously been inaccessible due to the ~10⁹ basis dimension, and opens the possibility of studying nuclei beyond the pf shell with realistic effective interactions.
