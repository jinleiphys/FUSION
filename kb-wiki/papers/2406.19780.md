# QCD with background electromagnetic fields on the lattice: a review

> This review provides a comprehensive summary of results on the physics of strongly interacting matter in the presence of background electromagnetic fields, obtained via numerical lattice simulations of the underlying theory, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Lattice QCD has guided our understanding of magnetized quarks and gluons via landmark results on the phase diagram, the equation of state, the confinemenent mechanism, anomalous transport phenomena as well as many more fascinating effects. Some of the lattice results lead to completely new paradigms in the description of hot magnetized quark matter and provided useful insights to a broad high-energy particle physics community. Since the first lattice QCD simulations with background fields, this field has been established as an independent research direction. We present the current status and recent developments of this field, together with an outlook including open questions to be answered in the near future.

## Key claim

This review summarizes lattice QCD results on strongly interacting matter in background electromagnetic fields, covering the phase diagram, equation of state, confinement, and anomalous transport. The abstract presents these as established findings, but the text reveals that key results such as the chiral magnetic effect (CME) conductivity vanish in equilibrium QCD when using properly conserved vector currents, contradicting earlier lattice claims.

## Method

The paper reviews numerical lattice simulations of QCD with background electromagnetic fields, using both quenched and dynamical fermion formulations (Wilson, staggered, overlap, domain wall). It covers equilibrium calculations of fermion bilinears, topological charge correlators, and current-current correlation functions, as well as out-of-equilibrium transport coefficients obtained via spectral reconstruction from Euclidean correlators. The CME and chiral separation effect (CSE) conductivities are extracted from derivatives of currents with respect to chemical potentials or magnetic fields at zero external parameters.

## Key numbers

* Electric conductivity in the absence of background fields: results from Brandt (2012), Amato (2013), Brandt (2015), and Astrakhantsev (2019) are summarized, normalized by temperature and charge factor C_em = sum_f (q_f/e)^2, shown in a figure for dynamical QCD.
* CME coefficient from non-conserved local current in quenched Wilson QCD: continuum estimate C_CME_loc ≈ 0.02–0.03 (Yamamoto, 2011), while the conserved current gives C_CME = 0 (Brandt, 2024).
* CME coefficient from dynamical staggered quarks with conserved current: C_CME = 0 for all temperatures studied (Brandt, 2024).
* CSE coefficient from dynamical staggered quarks with physical masses: approaches the massless free-case value of 1/(2π^2) ≈ 0.05 at high temperature, suppressed at low temperatures, with continuum extrapolation from four lattice spacings (Brandt, 2023).
* Axial susceptibility chi_5 from dynamical staggered quarks: approaches N_c/(2π^2) in the high-temperature limit, suppressed at low temperatures (Brandt, 2024).
* Local correlator C_f between topological charge and electric dipole moment: takes values around 0.1, an order of magnitude smaller than a model prediction (Bali, 2014).

## Context

This review extends the line of lattice QCD studies initiated by Buividovich (2009) on magnetized quark matter and covers developments through 2024. It synthesizes results from multiple groups including Bali (2014), Brandt (2012, 2015, 2023, 2024), Yamamoto (2011), and others, establishing that the equilibrium CME vanishes in QCD with conserved currents, consistent with Bloch's theorem as generalized by Yamamoto (2015). The review enables future work on out-of-equilibrium transport, inhomogeneous CME currents observed by Brandt (2024), and axion physics from topological charge correlators.
