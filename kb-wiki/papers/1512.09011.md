# Effects produced by multi-parton interactions and color reconnection in small systems

> Multi-parton interactions and color reconnection can produce QGP-like effects in small systems, specifically, radial flow-like patterns. For pp collisions simulated with Pythia 8.212, in this work we investigate their effects on different observables like event multiplicity, event shapes and transverse momentum distributions.

## Key claim

Multi-parton interactions and color reconnection in Pythia 8.212 can produce radial flow-like patterns in pp collisions, but the apparent collective effects are sensitive to the pseudorapidity interval used to measure charged particle density, and the jet bias from multiplicity selection can mimic or enhance these signatures.

## Method

The authors simulated non-diffractive inelastic pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV using Pythia 8.212, analyzing charged hadrons with transverse momentum above 0.15 GeV/c and |η| < 0.3 for spherocity and mean pT calculations. They classified events using transverse spherocity (S0) to separate jetty-like (S0 < 0.2) from isotropic (S0 > 0.8) events, and studied the dependence of observables on the pseudorapidity range used for multiplicity determination (|η| < 0.3, |η| < 1.0, and |η| < 2.5). A blast-wave fit was applied to identified particle pT spectra to extract average transverse velocity.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron selection threshold: pT > 0.15 GeV/c and |η| < 0.3 for spherocity and mean pT calculations
- At dNch/dη = 30, the average number of multi-parton interactions increases by approximately 30% when going from |η| < 0.3 to |η| < 2.5 for the multiplicity measurement
- At dNch/dη ≈ 41, the pT spectrum for |η| < 2.5 is softer by approximately 5% compared to those for |η| < 1 and |η| < 0.3
- Spherocity bins used: S0 < 0.2 (jetty-like) and S0 > 0.8 (isotropic)
- Leading parton pT intervals studied: pT_hat < 5 GeV and 10 < pT_hat < 20 GeV

## Context

This work extends the investigation of QGP-like effects in small systems, following observations by ALICE (Abelev 2013) of a second rise in mean pT versus multiplicity in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV, and earlier studies by Ortiz (2013) and Bierlich (2014) on collective effects from multi-parton interactions and color reconnection in Pythia and DIPSY. The results demonstrate that the apparent radial flow signatures extracted from blast-wave analysis are sensitive to jet bias from multiplicity selection, as previously shown by Cuautle (2015) using spherocity classification. This has implications for comparing pp and AA data, as noted by Zakharov (2014), Abelev (2014), and Adam (2015), since QGP-like effects in pp must be accounted for when using pp as a baseline for heavy-ion collisions.
