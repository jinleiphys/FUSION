# A(e,e'p) reactions at GeV energies

> An unfactorized and relativistic framework for calculating A(e,e'p) observables at typical JLAB energies is presented. Results of (e,e'p) model calculations for the target nuclei ^{12}C and ^{16}O are presented and compared to data from SLAC and JLAB.

## Key claim
The authors present an unfactorized and relativistic framework for calculating A(e,e'p) observables at GeV energies that can bridge the low-energy (optical potential) and high-energy (Glauber) regimes for final-state interactions, but the abstract glosses over the condition that the framework works only in kinematic regimes where the eikonal approximation appears plausible.

## Method
The model uses relativistic bound-state wave functions from the sigma-omega mean-field model and treats final-state interactions either through relativistic optical potentials (using the eikonal approximation) or through a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) based on elementary proton-nucleon scattering amplitudes. The framework avoids partial-wave expansions and computes cross sections directly from structure functions in the one-photon exchange approximation, using either CC1 or CC2 forms for the off-shell electron-proton coupling. Calculations were performed for 16O(e,e'p) at Q2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)2 and omega = 0.439 GeV, and for 12C nuclear transparency as a function of Q2.

## Key numbers
- For 16O(e,e'p) at Q2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)2 and omega = 0.439 GeV: spectroscopic factor for the 1/2- ground state is 0.79 (OMEA, CC1) and 0.80 (RMSGA, CC1); for the 3/2- state at 6.31 MeV excitation, spectroscopic factor is 0.96 for both OMEA and RMSGA with CC1 coupling.
- The de Broglie wavelength of the ejected proton in the 16O kinematics is approximately 0.2 fm.
- For 12C nuclear transparency: inclusion of short-range correlations causes an overall enhancement of the nuclear transparency by some 10 percent.
- The summed spectroscopic factor for 12C integrated over the first 60 MeV in missing energy of 11B almost exhausts the sum-rule value.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic eikonal approach of Debruyne and Ryckebusch to bridge the gap between low-energy optical-potential models (e.g., Cooper (1993) global fits) and high-energy Glauber models for A(e,e'p) reactions at Jefferson Laboratory energies. The results address the "quenching disappearance" phenomenon reported by Lapikas (2000) and Zhalov, where spectroscopic factors extracted at higher Q2 appear substantially larger than those from lower Q2 measurements (e.g., Ingo). The framework enables consistent comparison of optical-potential and Glauber treatments of final-state interactions, and supports further exploration of nuclear transparency and short-range correlations at facilities like JLAB, MAMI, and MIT-Bates.
