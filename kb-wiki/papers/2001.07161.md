# Phenomenological implications of asymmetric $AdS_5$ shockwave collision studies for heavy ion physics

> This paper discusses possible phenomenological implications for p+A and A+A collisions of the results of recent numerical AdS/CFT calculations examining asymmetric collisions of planar shocks. In view of the extreme Lorentz contraction, we model highly relativistic heavy ion collisions (HICs) as a superposition of collisions between many near-independent transverse "pixels" with differing incident longitudinal momenta. It was found that also for asymmetric collisions the hydrodynamization time is in good approximation a proper time, just like for symmetric collisions, depending on the geometric mean of the longitudinally integrated energy densities of the incident projectiles. For realistic collisions with fluctuations in the initial energy densities, these results imply a substantial increase in the hydrodynamization time for highly asymmetric pixels. However, even in this case the local hydrodynamization time still is significantly smaller than perturbative results for the thermalization time.

## Key claim

For asymmetric collisions of planar shocks in AdS/CFT, the hydrodynamization time is approximately a proper time depending on the geometric mean of the longitudinally integrated energy densities of the incident projectiles; when realistic fluctuations in initial energy densities are included, the mean hydrodynamization time increases substantially compared to the smooth case, but remains significantly smaller than perturbative estimates of the thermalization time.

## Method

The authors model highly relativistic heavy ion collisions as a superposition of collisions between many near-independent transverse "pixels" with differing incident longitudinal momenta, motivated by extreme Lorentz contraction. They combine results from numerical AdS/CFT calculations of asymmetric planar shock collisions with a phenomenological model of fluctuating initial conditions, where the energy scale of each pixel is proportional to a fluctuating amplitude drawn from a Gamma distribution with parameter k=1.4, as used in the hydrodynamics code of Bernhard (2016). The hydrodynamization time for each pixel pair is taken from the holographic result τ_hydro ≈ 2/√(μ_A μ_B), and the probability distribution of hydrodynamization times is computed analytically.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamization time for a pixel pair is τ_hydro ≈ 2/√(μ_A μ_B), where μ_A and μ_B are the energy scales of the colliding pixels.
- For the fluctuating model with k=1.4, the mean hydrodynamization time is τ̄_hydro = 4.06/μ, where μ is the average energy scale.
- The rms deviation of the hydrodynamization time is Δτ̄_hydro = 5.70/μ for k=1.4.
- There is a 70% probability that τ_hydro exceeds the non-fluctuating estimate of 2/μ, a 45% probability it exceeds 3/μ, and a 30% probability it exceeds 4/μ.
- For smooth p+Pb collisions compared to Pb+Pb, the momentum scale is smaller by a factor A^(1/6) ≈ 2.5, increasing the hydrodynamization time from 0.1-0.2 fm/c to 0.25-0.5 fm/c.
- The median hydrodynamization time is 2.76/μ for k=1.4.

## Context

This work extends the holographic shockwave collision studies of Waeber (2019) and Chesler (2015) by incorporating the fluctuating initial conditions required to explain the large odd azimuthal flow moments observed in heavy ion collisions, such as v_3 reported by Acharya (2018). The authors connect their results to the phenomenological analysis of Bernhard (2016), which found that the initial entropy density in heavy ion collisions is best described by the geometric mean of the thickness functions of the colliding nuclei. The findings suggest that the apparent tension between short hydrodynamization times from AdS/CFT (0.2-0.3 fm/c) and longer perturbative thermalization times (2-4 fm/c from Baier (2000) and Berges (2014)) is largely resolved by including large transverse fluctuations, which increase the mean hydrodynamization time to 0.4-0.6 fm/c while still remaining much shorter than perturbative estimates.
