# Production of para-- and orthopositronium at relativistic heavy ion colliders

> We consider the ortho-- and parapositronium production in the process $AA \to AA+$ Ps where A is a nucleus with the charge number Z. The inclusive cross section and the energy distribution of the relativistic Ps are calculated which are of primary interest from the experimental point of view. The accuracy of the corresponding cross sections is given by omitting terms $\sim (Zα)^2/L^2$ for the para--Ps and $\sim (Zα)^2/L$ for the ortho--Ps production where $L=\ln{γ^2} \approx 9$ and 16 for the RHIC and the LHC. Within this accuracy the multiphoton (Coulomb) corrections are taken into account. We show that the RHIC and the LHC will be Ps factories with a productions rate of about $10^5 ÷10^8$ relativistic Ps per day. The fraction of the ortho--Ps is expected to be of the same order as that of the para--Ps for Au--Au and Pb--Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate inclusive cross sections and energy distributions for relativistic ortho- and parapositronium production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, claiming production rates of about 10^5 to 10^8 relativistic Ps per day. The accuracy is limited by omitting terms of order (Zα)^2/L^2 for para-Ps and (Zα)^2/L for ortho-Ps, where L = ln γ^2 ≈ 9 for RHIC and 16 for LHC.

## Method

The authors compute the Born cross section for para-Ps production using the equivalent photon approximation and exact calculations for ultrarelativistic ions, then add Coulomb corrections from multiphoton exchange between the produced Ps and the nuclei. For ortho-Ps, they use the leading logarithmic approximation with Coulomb corrections included via hypergeometric functions, exploiting results from earlier work on electroproduction of Ps on nuclei (Kotkin et al., 1998). The cross sections are integrated over the relevant phase space regions to obtain total rates and energy spectra.

## Key numbers

- Para-Ps Born cross section: 17.8 mb for RHIC, 110 mb for LHC (Pb-Pb mode), 0.42 mb for LHC (Ca-Ca mode)
- Coulomb correction reduces para-Ps cross section by 43% for RHIC, 27% for LHC (Pb-Pb), and 2.2% for LHC (Ca-Ca)
- Ortho-Ps Coulomb correction relative to lowest order: -39% for RHIC, -40% for LHC (Pb-Pb), -3.6% for LHC (Ca-Ca)
- Production rate: 1 to 10^3 relativistic Ps per second, corresponding to 10^5 to 10^8 per day
- Ratio of ortho-Ps to para-Ps: 0.8 for RHIC, 0.3 for LHC (Pb-Pb), 0.02 for LHC (Ca-Ca)
- Para-Ps lifetime at rest: cτ₀ = 3.7 cm; ortho-Ps lifetime at rest: cτ₀ = 42 m
- Parameter L = ln γ² ≈ 9 for RHIC, ≈ 16 for LHC
- Coulomb correction parameter C(ν) = 0.6734 for Au, 0.7080 for Pb
- Ortho-Ps integral B(ν) = 0.3770 for Au, 0.3654 for Pb

## Context

This work extends earlier leading logarithmic approximation calculations for para-Ps production (MSS, year not specified) by providing full energy and angular distributions for both para- and orthopositronium, including Coulomb corrections. It builds on the authors' previous work on electroproduction of relativistic positronium (Kotkin et al., 1998) and applies those methods to heavy-ion colliders. The results enable experimental searches for relativistic Ps at RHIC and LHC, which could test CPT invariance (as reviewed by M, year not specified) and study the predicted anomalous transparency of Ps in thin layers (N and LP, years not specified).
