# Modification of parton distributions in nuclei

> Nuclear parton distributions are studied in a parton model with rescaling and recombination mechanisms. Parton x distributions are first calculated at Q^2=1 GeV^2 by the model, and they are evolved to larger Q^2 in order to be compared with various F_2 data. The experimental shadowing is explained by the parton-recombination effects. Then, the modification of sea-quark and gluon distributions is investigated. We show x and Q^2 dependent results of the ratios S_A/S_D and G_A/G_D. They indicate significant shadowing at small x and large ratios in the medium-x region. Our theoretical results should be tested by future experiments, for example, at RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider). Furthermore, our studies are important for finding a signature of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion reactions. We also indicate that nuclear effects in the deuteron should be taken into account for finding the accurate F_2 structure function of the neutron.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a parton model combining Q^2 rescaling and parton-recombination mechanisms can explain nuclear parton distributions from very small x to large x, but the model's success is conditional on using parton-recombination (PR) evolution equations rather than standard DGLAP evolution to match experimental shadowing data, and the model fails to explain the shadowing behavior at small Q^2 (around 1 GeV^2) for x=0.0085.

## Method

The authors first calculated nuclear parton x distributions at Q^2=1 GeV^2 using the MRS-R2 parton distributions as input, applying Q^2 rescaling to valence quark distributions and then parton-recombination effects. The resulting distributions were evolved to higher Q^2 (typically 5 GeV^2) using parton-recombination (PR) evolution equations based on the Mueller-Qiu formalism, and the F_2 structure functions were obtained by convolution with next-to-leading-order (NLO) coefficient functions for comparison with experimental data from NMC, Fermilab-E665, and SLAC-E139.

## Key numbers

* Rescaling parameter for calcium: xi_Ca = 1.74 at Q^2=1 GeV^2, determined by fitting F_2^Ca/F_2^D data at medium x
* Confinement size ratio for calcium: lambda_Ca/lambda_N = 1.194, which is 5.0 percent larger than the value (1.137) in the original rescaling paper
* Rescaling parameters for other nuclei at Q^2=1 GeV^2: xi_D = 1.186 (deuteron), xi_He = 1.437 (helium), xi_C = 1.557 (carbon), xi_Sn = 2.001 (tin)
* Deuteron effective radius parameter: R_0 = 1.56 fm, compared to the usual nuclear value R_0 = 1.1 fm
* Gottfried sum rule nuclear correction: delta_I_G = -0.013 for the integral from x_min = 0.004 at Q^2=5 GeV^2
* QCD scale parameter of MRS-R2: Lambda = 0.344 GeV

## Context

This work extends the parton model of Kumano (1993) to study sea-quark and gluon distributions in nuclei, updating the nucleon parton distributions to the MRS-R2 parametrization which incorporates HERA small-x data. The model combines the Q^2 rescaling mechanism originally proposed by Close, Roberts, and Ross (1983) for the medium-x EMC effect with the parton-recombination mechanism of Close, Qiu, and Roberts (1989) for small-x shadowing. The paper enables predictions for future experiments at RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) to test nuclear sea-quark and gluon distributions through Drell-Yan and direct-photon processes, and emphasizes that nuclear effects in the deuteron must be accounted for when extracting neutron structure functions for sum rules such as the Gottfried sum rule.
