# Probing the softest region of nuclear equation of state

> An attractive, energy-dependent mean-field potential for baryons is introduced in order to generate a soft region in the nuclear equation-of-state, as suggested by recent lattice QCD calculations of baryon free matter at finite temperature. Based on a hadronic transport model, we find that although this equation-of-state has negligible effects on the inclusive hadronic spectra, it leads to a minimum in the energy dependence of the transverse collective flow and a delayed expansion of the compressed matter. In particular, the transverse flow changes its direction as the colliding system passes through the softest region in the equation-of-state.

## Key claim

The authors introduce an attractive, energy-dependent mean-field potential for baryons within a hadronic transport model to mimic a softened nuclear equation-of-state (EoS) with a region of near-zero sound velocity, as suggested by lattice QCD. They find that while this softened EoS has negligible effects on inclusive hadronic spectra, it produces a minimum in the excitation function of transverse collective flow and a delayed expansion of compressed matter, with the transverse flow even changing sign as the system passes through the softest region.

## Method

The authors constructed a super-soft equation-of-state by requiring a constant pressure (zero pressure gradient) over a finite range of energy density, using a potential energy density that balances the increasing kinetic pressure. They implemented this EoS in the hadronic transport model ART (A Relativistic Transport model) and simulated Au+Au collisions at various beam momenta, comparing results from four different EoS: a cascade (no mean field), a normal soft Skyrme EoS, and two super-soft EoS with different locations of the softest region (energy density ranges of 1–2 GeV/fm³ and 1.5–2.5 GeV/fm³).

## Key numbers

- The softest region in the equation-of-state is placed in the energy density range of 1 < e < 2 GeV/fm³ (super-soft eos1) and 1.5 < e < 2.5 GeV/fm³ (super-soft eos2).
- The normal soft EoS from the Skyrme interaction is given by P = (2/5)E_f ρ − 179 ρ²/ρ₀ + 164 (ρ/ρ₀)^(7/6) ρ (MeV/fm³), where E_f is the Fermi energy and ρ₀ is normal nuclear matter density.
- Above the soft region, the pressure is taken as P = 200 (ρ/ρ₀)^(1/3) ρ − 153 (MeV/fm³), similar to a noninteracting quark gas in a MIT bag.
- For the Au+Au reaction at P_beam/A = 12 GeV/c, the flow parameter (slope of transverse momentum distribution at mid-rapidity) changes from positive to negative sign when using the super-soft eos1 compared to the cascade model.
- The radial density distributions of nucleons, pions, and kaons at 20 fm/c from Au+Au at impact parameter 2 fm and beam momentum 12 GeV/c show that the super-soft eos1 produces more compact particle distributions than the normal soft EoS.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamical model studies by Bra94, Hung95, Ris95, and Plo95, which predicted a minimum in transverse collective flow due to a softened EoS from QCD phase transition, by using a hadronic transport model (ART) that treats freeze-out consistently. The authors note that while neither QGP phase transition nor chiral symmetry restoration actually occurs in their hadronic approach, the results are useful for understanding signals of a real phase transition. The study enables experimental searches for signatures of chiral and deconfinement transitions through measurements of transverse collective flow at different incident energies, particularly by looking for a sign change in the flow parameter.
