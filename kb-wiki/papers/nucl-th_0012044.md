# In-medium spin-orbit NN potential in the Skyrme model

> It is considered how spin-orbit potential for two nucleons is changed when one of them is imbedded in nuclear matter. It is emphasized that there appears new tipe of potential antisymmetric under nucleon spins which is proportional to mass difference between free and imbedded nucleons. The longest range behaviour of such potential is estimated.

# In-medium spin-orbit NN potential in the Skyrme model

## Key claim
The Skyrme model predicts that when one nucleon is embedded in nuclear matter, a new antisymmetric spin-orbit potential proportional to \((\vec \sigma^{(1)} - \vec \sigma^{(2)})\vec l\) appears, driven by the difference between the free and in-medium nucleon mass and moment of inertia; this potential vanishes for two free nucleons and is estimated to scale approximately as \(\kappa (\rho/\rho_0)\) times the vacuum spin-orbit potential at not very high density.

## Method
The author uses the Skyrme soliton model with the product approximation for two interacting skyrmions, where one skyrmion is taken as free and the other is modified by nuclear matter via Brown-Rho scaling of the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\). The profile function of the in-medium skyrmion is constructed to interpolate between the chiral-limit scaling at short distances and the Yukawa form at long distances, with an adjustable parameter \(a\). The spin-orbit potentials \(V^{(\pm)}_{T=1}(R,\xi)\) are derived from the quadratic part of the Skyrme Lagrangian, and the antisymmetric component is isolated and expressed in terms of the vacuum spin-orbit potential and the scaling parameter \(\xi\).

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The scaling parameter \(\xi = F_\pi^\ast / F_\pi = 1 - \kappa (\rho/\rho_0)\); numerical calculations use \(\xi = 0.7\) and \(a = 2\).
- The antisymmetric isospin-dependent spin-orbit potential is approximated as \(V^{(-)}_{T=1}(R) \approx \kappa (\rho/\rho_0) V^{\rm vac}_{T=1,ls}(R)\).
- The antisymmetric isospin-independent spin-orbit potential is estimated as \(V_{T=0}^{(-)} \approx \kappa (\rho/\rho_0) V^{\rm vac}_{T=0,ls}(R)\).

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme-model treatment of NN interactions in vacuum (Skyrme, 1961; Riska, 1995) to the in-medium case, building on earlier studies of skyrmion properties in nuclear matter (Meissner, 1988; Rahimov, 1999) and on Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991). The antisymmetric spin-orbit potential is analogous to that appearing in hyperon-nucleon interactions due to mass differences (de Swart, 1963). The results suggest that experiments on nucleon quasifree scattering at 0.5–1 GeV energy scale could probe how nuclear matter modifies nucleon properties.
