# Kaon meson condensate in neutron star matter including hyperons

> The recent measurement of the mass of neutron stars (PSR J1614 - 2230, PSR J0348 + 0432, MSP J0740 + 6620) restricts the lower limit $\sim 2M_{\odot}$ of the maximum mass of such compact stars, making it possible for dense matter to exist in massive stars. The relativistic mean field theory with parameter sets FSUGold including Kaon condensation is used to describe the properties of neutron stars in $β$ equilibrium. Through careful choice of the parameter of the $σ$-cut $c_σ$, we are able to produce a maximum mass neutron star with Kaon condensation heavier than $2M_{\odot}$, and we find that the parameter $Λ_ν$ of the $ρ-ω$ interaction term in this model has a significant effect on $K^{-}$ condensation. In the case of using $σ$-cut scheme, $K^{-}$ condensation occurs only when the $ρ-ω$ interaction $Λ_ν$ is switched off.

# Kaon meson condensate in neutron star matter including hyperons

## Key claim

Using the FSUGold relativistic mean-field model with a σ-cut scheme, the authors produce neutron stars with kaon condensation that exceed 2 M⊙, but only when the ρ-ω interaction parameter Λν is set to zero; when Λν = 0.03, K− condensation does not occur at all under the σ-cut scheme, regardless of the kaon optical potential depth.

## Method

The authors employ relativistic mean-field theory with the FSUGold parameter set, extended to include all eight lightest baryons (n, p, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) and K− meson condensation in β-equilibrium neutron star matter. They apply a σ-cut scheme that adds a logarithmic potential term to stiffen the equation of state at supra-saturation densities without altering saturation properties, and vary the parameter cσ to control the stiffening strength. The kaon-nucleon interaction is fixed via the K− optical potential at saturation density, with values ranging from −120 MeV to −160 MeV, and the meson-hyperon couplings follow SU(6) symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons and K− condensation reaches 2.07 M⊙ for cσ = 0.1 (both Λν = 0.03 and Λν = 0), and 1.99 M⊙ for cσ = 0.15 with Λν = 0.03.
- Without the σ-cut scheme, the maximum mass including hyperons is 1.31 M⊙ (Λν = 0.03) and 1.32 M⊙ (Λν = 0).
- Threshold density for K− condensation without σ-cut: for U_K = −160 MeV, n_cr = 0.41 fm⁻³ (Λν = 0.03) and 0.27 fm⁻³ (Λν = 0); for U_K = −140 MeV, n_cr = 0.54 fm⁻³ (Λν = 0.03) and 0.30 fm⁻³ (Λν = 0).
- With σ-cut (cσ = 0.15) and Λν = 0, threshold densities are 0.36 fm⁻³ (U_K = −140 MeV) and 0.28 fm⁻³ (U_K = −160 MeV); no K− condensation occurs for any U_K when Λν = 0.03.
- The K− optical potential depths studied are −120, −140, and −160 MeV, with corresponding scalar coupling constants g_σK = 0.6030, 1.1775, and 1.7521 respectively.
- The saturation density of the FSUGold model is ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using the FSUGold model, which predicted a maximum neutron star mass of 1.72 M⊙ for nucleonic matter (Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz, 2005) and only 1.3 M⊙ when hyperons were included (Wu, 2011). The σ-cut scheme was previously proposed by Maslov (2015) to stiffen the equation of state at high density, but that study did not include hyperons. The present paper demonstrates that the σ-cut scheme can simultaneously accommodate hyperons, kaon condensation, and the 2 M⊙ mass constraint from pulsars such as PSR J0348+0432 and MSP J0740+6620, while also showing that the ρ-ω coupling Λν critically controls whether K− condensation can occur. The results enable future work on the interplay between exotic degrees of freedom and the equation of state in massive neutron stars.
