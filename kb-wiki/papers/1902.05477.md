# Approximate self-energy for Fermi systems with large s-wave scattering length: a step towards density functional theory

> In the present work, we start from a minimal Hamiltonian for Fermi systems where the s-wave scattering is the only low energy constant at play. Many-Body Perturbative approach that is usually valid at rather low density is first discussed. We then use the resummation technique with the ladder approximation to obtain compact expressions for both the energy and/or the on-shell self-energy in infinite spin-degenerated systems. Diagrammatic resummation technique has the advantage in general to be predictive in a region of density larger compared to many-body perturbation theory. It also leads to non-diverging limit as $|a_s| \rightarrow + \infty$. Still, the obtained expressions are rather complex functional of the Fermi momentum $k_F$. We introduce the full phase-space average or the partial phase-space methods respectively applied to the energy or to the self-energy to simplify their dependences in terms of $(a_s k_F)$ while keeping the correct limit at low density and the non-diverging property at large $|a_s k_F|$. Quasi-particle properties of Fermi system in various regime of density and scattering length are then illustrated. Our conclusion is that such simplified expressions where the direct link is made with the low energy constant without fine-tuning can provide a clear guidance to obtain density functional theory beyond the perturbative regime. However, quasi-particle properties close or near unitary cannot be reproduced unless this limit is explicitly used as a constraint. We finally discuss how such approximate treatment of quasi-particle can guide the development of density functional theory for strongly interacting Fermi systems.

## Key claim

The authors show that simplified analytical expressions for the energy and self-energy of Fermi systems with large s-wave scattering length can be obtained via phase-space averaging of resummed diagrammatic series, but quasi-particle properties near the unitary limit cannot be reproduced unless that limit is explicitly used as a constraint.

## Method

The authors start from a minimal Hamiltonian with only the s-wave scattering length \(a_s\) as a low-energy constant, using a zero-range contact interaction in momentum space. They apply diagrammatic resummation techniques in the ladder approximation to obtain compact expressions for the ground-state energy and on-shell self-energy in infinite spin-degenerated Fermi systems, then introduce full phase-space averaging (for the energy) and partial phase-space averaging (for the self-energy) to simplify the functional dependence on \((a_s k_F)\) while preserving the correct low-density limit and non-diverging behavior at large \(|a_s k_F|\).

## Key numbers

- The Lee-Yang expansion gives the first-order energy contribution as \(E^{(1)}/E_{\rm FG} = (g-1)(10/9\pi)(a_s k_F)\) and the second-order contribution as \(E^{(2)}/E_{\rm FG} = (g-1)(4/21\pi^2)(11-2\ln 2)(a_s k_F)^2\) for spin degeneracy \(g\).
- The Geometric series Exact Integration (GEI) yields a Bertsch parameter \(\xi_{\rm GEI} \simeq 0.24\) at unitarity, while the Arctangent series Exact Integration (AEI) gives \(\xi_{\rm AEI} \simeq 0.51\).
- The Geometric Phase-Space (GPS) approximation gives \(\xi_{\rm GPS} = 0.32\), and the Arctangent Phase-Space (APS) approximation gives \(\xi_{\rm APS} \simeq 0.36\), compared to the experimentally observed superfluid unitary gas value \(\xi_0 = 0.37\).
- The s-wave scattering length for neutron-neutron interaction is \(a_s \sim -20~\mathrm{fm}\), leading to a perturbative validity range \(\rho \lesssim 10^{-6}~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), far below nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 \sim 0.16~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic resummation approach of Steele (2000) and Kaiser (2011) for Fermi systems with large scattering length, and builds on the phase-space averaging technique introduced by Schwenk (2005) for the energy. The resulting simplified functionals provide analytical guidance for constructing non-empirical density functionals, as previously attempted in the ELYO functional of Lacroix (2016, 2017) and the hybrid functional of Yang (2016), and enable future development of DFT for strongly interacting Fermi systems beyond the perturbative regime, though the treatment of superfluidity and the effective mass near unitarity remain open challenges.
