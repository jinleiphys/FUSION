# The eta' in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We include in a systematic way the eta' in baryon chiral perturbation theory. The most general relativistic effective Lagrangian describing the interaction of the lowest lying baryon octet with the Goldstone boson octet and the eta' is presented up to linear order in the derivative expansion and its heavy baryon limit is obtained. As explicit examples, we calculate the baryon masses and the pi N sigma-term up to one-loop order in the heavy baryon formulation. A systematic expansion in the meson masses is possible, and appearing divergences are renormalized.

# The $\eta'$ in baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The $\eta'$ meson can be included as a dynamical field in baryon chiral perturbation theory without invoking large $N_c$ arguments, enabling a systematic expansion in both octet and singlet meson masses simultaneously. The relative size of the expansion parameters is given by $m_\eta / m_{\eta'}$, but the convergence of the expansion in the $\eta'$ mass (which is comparable in size to the baryon octet masses) is not addressed in this work.

## Method

The author constructs the most general relativistic effective Lagrangian for the interaction of the lowest-lying baryon octet with the Goldstone boson octet and the $\eta'$ singlet, up to linear order in the derivative expansion, and then derives its heavy baryon limit. As explicit examples, the baryon octet masses and the $\pi N$ $\sigma$-term are calculated up to one-loop order in the heavy baryon formulation, including only the $\eta$ and $\eta'$ loop contributions (neglecting $\pi$, $K$ loops and explicit symmetry breaking terms). Appearing divergences are renormalized by redefining the baryon mass in the chiral limit and the low-energy constant $b_0$.

## Key numbers

- $\eta'$ mass: $m_{\eta'} = 958$ MeV, used as input for numerical estimates
- $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle: $\theta \approx -20^\circ$, determined from two-photon decays of $\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\eta'$
- Pion decay constant: $F_\pi \simeq 92.4$ MeV
- Axial-vector couplings from semileptonic hyperon decays: $D = 0.80 \pm 0.01$, $F = 0.46 \pm 0.01$
- For the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ mass shifts from $\eta'$ loops, using $\lambda_2 = 0$ and $F_0 = F_\pi = 92.4$ MeV gives numerical values of $-907$ MeV and $701$ MeV respectively; these become only $-103$ MeV in both cases if $D \simeq 3 \lambda_2$
- The $\eta$ and $\eta'$ contributions to the $\sigma$-term shift to the Cheng-Dashen point ($t = 2 m_\pi^2$) are estimated at $0.21$ MeV (for $\lambda_2 = 0$) and $0.02$ MeV (for $\lambda_2 = D/3$), compared to the $\eta$ contribution of about $0.01$ MeV from conventional chiral perturbation theory without the $\eta'$
- The $\sigma$-term shift to the Cheng-Dashen point from pion loops alone is about $7.5$ MeV at one-loop order (BKM)

## Context

This work extends conventional baryon chiral perturbation theory (where the $\eta'$ is integrated out and appears only as a contribution to low-energy constants) by treating the $\eta'$ as a dynamical field, motivated by $\eta_0$-$\eta_8$ mixing observed experimentally and by large $N_c$ considerations where the axial anomaly is suppressed. The approach builds on the purely mesonic Lagrangian including the $\eta'$ developed by Leutwyler (1996) and Herrera-Siklody (1997). The calculation of baryon masses without the $\eta'$ was previously performed by Borasoy, Kaiser, and Meissner (1996). The new feature is the appearance of a tadpole diagram at leading order that produces a divergence, which is renormalized by redefining the baryon mass in the chiral limit and the coefficient $b_0$. Future work should address the convergence of the expansion in meson masses and the connection to large $N_c$ baryon chiral perturbation theory.
