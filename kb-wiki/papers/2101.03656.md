# Light-front dynamic analysis of the longitudinal charge density using the solvable scalar field model in (1+1) dimensions

> We investigate the electromagnetic form factor $F(q^2)$ of the meson by using the solvable $ϕ^{3}$ scalar field model in $(1+1)$ dimensions. As the transverse rotations are absent in $(1+1)$ dimensions, the advantage of the light-front dynamics (LFD) with the light-front time $x^+ = x^0 + x^3$ as the evolution parameter is maximized in contrast to the usual instant form dynamics (IFD) with the ordinary time $x^0$ as the evolution parameter. In LFD, the individual $x^+$-ordered amplitudes contributing to $F(q^2)$ are invariant under the boost, i.e., frame-independent, while the individual $x^0$-ordered amplitudes in IFD are not invariant under the boost but dependent on the reference frame. The LFD allows to get the analytic result for the one-loop triangle diagram which covers not only the spacelike ($q^{2}<0$) but also timelike region ($q^{2}>0$). Using the analytic results, we verify that the real and imaginary parts of the form factor satisfy the dispersion relations in the entire $q^{2}$ space. Comparing with the results in $(3+1)$ dimensions, we discuss the transverse momentum effects on $F(q^2)$ . We also discuss the longitudinal charge density in terms of the boost invariant variable $\tilde z = p^+ x^-$ in LFD.

## Key claim

Using a solvable scalar \(\phi^3\) field model in (1+1) dimensions, the authors show that the light-front dynamics (LFD) analysis of the electromagnetic form factor can be extended from the spacelike region to the entire timelike region, including the anomalous threshold, and that the longitudinal charge density can be defined in terms of the boost-invariant variable \(\tilde z = p^+ x^-\), but only when both valence and non-valence (Z-graph) contributions are included because the Drell-Yan-West frame is restricted to \(q^2=0\) in (1+1) dimensions.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop triangle diagram for a scalar meson in the \(\phi^3\) model in (1+1) dimensions using light-front time-ordered perturbation theory. They perform Cauchy integration over \(k^-\) to obtain analytic expressions for the valence and non-valence contributions to the form factor in both the spacelike (\(q^2<0\)) and timelike (\(q^2>0\)) regions, and verify the results against dispersion relations. The longitudinal charge density is then obtained via a one-dimensional Fourier transform of the form factor and compared with the Breit-frame density.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic behavior of the form factor in (1+1) dimensions is \(\lim_{Q^2\to\infty} F^{S}_{\cal M}(Q^2) \sim \ln Q^2 / Q^2\), compared to \(\ln^2 Q^2 / Q^2\) in (3+1) dimensions.
* For a "scalar pion" with \(M=0.14\) GeV and \(m_q=m_{\bar Q}=0.25\) GeV, the non-valence contribution dominates the form factor for \(Q^2 \geq 0.1\) GeV\(^2\).
* For a weakly bound state pion with \(M=0.14\) GeV and \(m_q=m_{\bar Q}=0.08\) GeV, the normal threshold is at \(q^2 = 0.0256\) GeV\(^2\) and the anomalous threshold appears at \(q^2 \simeq 0.0184\) GeV\(^2\).
* The imaginary part of the timelike form factor for a strong bound state is given by \(\mbox{Im}[I^q_{\rm T}(q^2)] = -\frac{g^2 \theta(\gamma_q -1)}{16 m_q^2 m_{\bar Q}^2 (1 - \omega^2 - \gamma_q)} \sqrt{(\gamma_q-1)/\gamma_q}\), where \(\gamma_q = q^2 / 4 m_q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier (1+1)-dimensional LFD analyses of Mankiewicz and Sawicki (1988, 1989) and Głazek and Sawicki (1990), which were restricted to the spacelike region, by providing analytic results for the timelike region and verifying dispersion relations. It also compares with the (3+1)-dimensional scalar \(\phi^3\) model results of Choi and Ji (1999) to highlight transverse momentum effects. The introduction of the boost-invariant variable \(\tilde z = p^+ x^-\) for longitudinal charge density follows the proposal of Miller and Brodsky (2019). The work enables future LFD studies of longitudinal charge densities in more realistic quark models and in the context of JLab and EIC experiments.
