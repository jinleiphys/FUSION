# Local spin polarizations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Based on a generalized side-jump formalism for massless chiral fermions, which naturally takes into account the spin-orbit coupling in the scattering of two chiral fermions and the chiral vortical effect in a rotating chiral fermion matter, we have developed a covariant and total angular momentum conserved chiral transport model to study both the global and local polarizations of this matter. For a system of massless quarks of random spin orientations and finite vorticity in a box, we have demonstrated that the model can exactly conserve the total angular momentum of the system and dynamically generate the quark spin polarization expected from a thermally equilibrated quark matter. Using this model to study the spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collision, we have found that the local quark spin polarizations depend strongly on the reference frame where they are evaluated as a result of the nontrivial axial charge distribution caused by the chiral vortical effect. We have further shown that because of the anomalous orbital or side-jump contribution to the quark spin polarization, the local quark polarizations calculated in the medium rest frame are qualitatively consistent with the local polarizations of Lambda hyperons measured in experiments.

## Key claim
The authors claim that their covariant chiral transport model, which conserves total angular momentum and includes side-jump effects, can qualitatively reproduce the measured local polarizations of Lambda hyperons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but only when the polarizations are evaluated in the medium rest frame rather than the laboratory frame.

## Method
The authors developed a covariant chiral transport model for massless quarks based on a generalized side-jump formalism that accounts for spin-orbit coupling in chiral fermion scattering and the chiral vortical effect. They implemented this model using an isotropic quark-quark scattering cross section of 10 mb, treating the scattering cross section as a parameter and allowing quarks to freely propagate between scatterings. The model was applied to both a box of rotating quark matter and to non-central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV at 30-50% centrality, with initial quark phase-space distributions taken from the AMPT model.

## Key numbers
- Quark-quark scattering cross section: 10 mb (isotropic, used as a parameter in the transport model)
- Collision system: Au-Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, 30-50% centrality
- Evolution time: 8 fm/c for the heavy-ion collision simulations
- The total angular momentum in the box system was conserved exactly during expansion, with the spin part increasing at the expense of the orbital part
- The final quark spin polarization in the box reproduced the thermal model expectation of omega/(2T), where omega is the angular velocity or vorticity and T is the temperature

## Context
This work extends the chiral kinetic approach of Sun (2018) and Sun (2017), which predicted global spin polarization and the sign of longitudinal local polarization P_z but failed to describe the measured local polarization P_y along the global angular momentum direction. The authors address shortcomings of those earlier approaches, which lacked total angular momentum conservation, were non-covariant, and treated the chiral vortical effect ambiguously. The paper offers a plausible explanation for the "spin puzzle" in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where measured Lambda local polarizations from STAR (2017) and Niida (2018) disagree with predictions from statistical models by Becattini (2013) and Pang (2016). The authors note that future work should extend the approach to quarks of finite masses and construct an angular-momentum conserved hadronization model to convert quark polarizations to hadron polarizations.
