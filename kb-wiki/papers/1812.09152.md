# Neutron transfer reactions in halo effective field theory

> Direct reaction experiments provide a powerful tool to probe the structure of neutron-rich nuclei like beryllium-11. We use halo effective field theory to calculate the cross section of the deuteron-induced neutron transfer reaction ${}^{10}\text{Be}(\text{d},\,\text{p}){}^{11}\text{Be}$. The effective theory contains dynamical fields for the beryllium-10 core, the neutron, and the proton. In contrast, the deuteron and the beryllium-11 halo nucleus are generated dynamically from contact interactions using experimental and ab initio input. The reaction amplitude is constructed up to next-to-leading order in an expansion in the ratio of the length scales characterizing the core and the halo. The Coulomb repulsion between core and proton is treated perturbatively. Finally, we compare our results to cross-section data and other calculations.

## Key claim

Halo effective field theory can describe the deuteron-induced neutron transfer reaction ${}^{10}\text{Be}(\text{d},\,\text{p}){}^{11}\text{Be}$ up to next-to-leading order in an expansion in the ratio of the core radius to the halo radius, provided the center-of-mass energy is well below the core excitation energy of 3.37 MeV, though the authors compare to data at energies of 7.78 MeV and above where the expansion may still be appropriate for lower experimental energies.

## Method

The authors construct a Faddeev equation for the transfer amplitude using halo effective field theory with dynamical fields for the beryllium-10 core, neutron, and proton, while the deuteron and beryllium-11 halo nucleus are generated dynamically from contact interactions matched to experimental and ab initio input. The reaction amplitude is computed at leading order and next-to-leading order by iterating neutron exchange diagrams and Coulomb diagrams (bubble and box diagrams) to all orders, with the Coulomb repulsion between core and proton treated perturbatively. The two-body propagators for beryllium-11 and the deuteron are expanded around their poles in powers of the ratio of core radius to halo radius, and the three-body force is used to renormalize the leading-order amplitude.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of beryllium-11 ground state: $B_\sigma = 0.50$ MeV, corresponding to binding momentum $\gamma_\sigma \approx 29$ MeV
* Binding energy of beryllium-11 first excited state: $B_\pi = 0.18$ MeV, corresponding to binding momentum $\gamma_\pi \approx 18$ MeV
* Binding energy of deuteron: $B_\text{d} = 2.22$ MeV, with binding momentum $\gamma_\text{d} \approx 46$ MeV and effective range $r_\text{d} = 1.75$ fm
* Core excitation energy of beryllium-10: $E_\text{x} = 3.37$ MeV
* Core radius: $R_\text{c} \sim 2\text{-}3$ fm; halo radius: $R_\text{h} \sim 7$ fm, giving expansion parameter $R_\text{c}/R_\text{h} \sim 0.4$
* Asymptotic normalization coefficient for beryllium-11 ground state from Calci et al. (2016): $A_\sigma = 0.786$ fm$^{-1/2}$, yielding effective range $r_\sigma \approx 3.5$ fm
* Asymptotic normalization coefficient for beryllium-11 excited state from Calci et al. (2016): $A_\pi = 0.129$ fm$^{-1/2}$, yielding $p$-wave effective range $r_\pi \approx -0.95$ fm$^{-1}$
* Coulomb momentum: $p_\text{c} = Q_\text{c}\,\alpha\,\mu_\text{Nc} \approx 25$ MeV
* Center-of-mass energies compared to data: $E \geq 7.78$ MeV

## Context

This work extends halo effective field theory, previously applied to electromagnetic transitions and Coulomb dissociation of one-neutron halo nuclei (Hammer and Phillips, 2011), to the experimentally important process of deuteron-induced neutron transfer reactions. The calculation builds on Faddeev approaches by Deltuva et al. (2016) that showed core excitations barely influence the cross section for energies below about 10 MeV, and on the adiabatic distorted wave approximation analysis by Yang and Capel (2018) that found the reaction is purely peripheral at lower beam energies and forward angles. The approach enables systematic uncertainty estimates for transfer reactions on neutron-rich nuclei and can be extended to other halo systems and reactions.
