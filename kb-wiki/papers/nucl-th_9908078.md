# Photoproduction of mesons in nuclei at GeV energies

> In a transport model that combines initial state interactions of the photon with final state interactions of the produced particles we present a calculation of inclusive photoproduction of mesons in nuclei in the energy range from 1 to 7 GeV. We give predictions for the photoproduction cross sections of pions, etas, kaons, antikaons, and $π^+π^-$ invariant mass spectra in ^{12}C and ^{208}Pb. The effects of nuclear shadowing and final state interaction of the produced particles are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The authors present a transport-model calculation of inclusive meson photoproduction in nuclei that goes beyond the standard Glauber approximation, but they caution that their results for antikaon production in nuclei are only valid under the caveat that their description of the elementary photon-nucleon collision is correct, since the string fragmentation model may overproduce antikaons relative to hyperons.

## Method

The authors use a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that treats final state interactions of produced particles in a full coupled-channel framework, including nucleonic resonances, hyperon resonances, and mesons. For photon energies above 1 GeV, nuclear shadowing of the incoming photon is implemented using a Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model with parameters from Bauer (1978), while elementary photon-nucleon collisions below 2.1 GeV invariant energy are described by explicit resonance production and above that by the FRITIOF string fragmentation model. Exclusive strangeness production is treated independently of the string model using parameterizations fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The shadowing factor s_N for 208Pb varies between 0.8 and 1.2 at 2 GeV photon energy, while at 7 GeV shadowing is much more important with interference structures visible.
- For 12C, the improved treatment of Delta-absorption reduces the pion yield by about 5%; for 208Pb, the reduction is about 20%.
- For 12C at 7 GeV, the calculation with zero formation time gives an enhancement of the pion yield by about 15% compared to the standard calculation with t_f=0.8 fm/c.
- The total eta photoproduction cross section increases by about a factor of 4 from 1 to 3 GeV photon energy.
- For 208Pb, secondary produced K+ mesons amount to about 30% of the total K+ yield; with zero formation time, the K+ cross section is enhanced by about 40% for 208Pb and about 20% for 12C at large photon energies.
- The exclusive strangeness production cross section parameterization uses a_0^Lambda=13 microbarn GeV^2 and a_0^Sigma=15 microbarn GeV^2; the inclusive strangeness cross section is overestimated by about 50% for photon energies above 4 GeV compared to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BUU transport model calculations from heavy-ion collisions and lower-energy photonuclear reactions (up to 800 MeV) into the 1-7 GeV range, providing predictions for experiments at ELSA and TJNAF. It goes beyond the earlier Glauber-approximation studies reviewed by Bauer (1978) by treating final state interactions in a coupled-channel framework that includes non-forward processes and secondary production chains. The calculations enable future experimental comparisons that could clarify the onset of nuclear shadowing at intermediate energies and test in-medium meson-nucleon interactions, particularly for kaons and antikaons, though the authors note that new experimental data on elementary photon-nucleon strangeness production are urgently needed.
