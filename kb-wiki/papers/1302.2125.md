# Chiral Plasma Instabilities

> We study the collective modes in relativistic electromagnetic or quark-gluon plasmas with an asymmetry between left- and right-handed chiral fermions, based on the recently formulated kinetic theory with Berry curvature corrections. We find that there exists an unstable mode, signaling the presence of a plasma instability. We argue the fate of this "chiral plasma instability" including the effect of collisions, and briefly discuss its relevance in heavy ion collisions and compact stars.

# Chiral Plasma Instabilities

## Key claim

The authors find that relativistic electromagnetic and quark-gluon plasmas with a finite chiral chemical potential μ₅ (asymmetry between left- and right-handed fermions) are dynamically unstable, with the instability arising specifically from Berry curvature corrections in the kinetic theory; the instability is predicted to reduce μ₅ by converting it to (color) electromagnetic fields with magnetic helicity, but the analysis is limited to the linear-response regime and assumes spatially homogeneous μ₅ and sufficiently weak gauge fields.

## Method

The authors use the recently formulated kinetic theory with Berry curvature corrections to compute the retarded self-energy Π^{μν} of the gauge field, including both parity-even and parity-odd contributions. They solve the linearized Vlasov equation for the deviation from thermal equilibrium, obtain the induced current via linear response theory, and derive the dispersion relations for collective modes by finding the poles of the photon propagator Δ^{ij}. The dispersion relations are analyzed in the quasistatic limit |ω| ≪ k to identify unstable modes, and the effect of collisions is incorporated via a relaxation-time approximation.

## Key numbers

- The unstable mode growth rate in QED: γ(k) = (4αμ)/(π²m_D²) k² (1 − πk/(αμ)) for 0 ≤ k ≤ αμ/π, with α = e²/(4π)
- Typical wavenumber of the unstable mode: k ∼ αμ
- Typical growth rate: |ω| ∼ α²μ
- Instability time scale in QED: τ_inst ∼ 1/(α²μ)
- Debye mass: m_D² = e²(T²/6 + μ²/(2π²))
- For QCD with μ₅ ∼ T: τ_QCD ∼ 1/(α_s² μ₅ ln α_s⁻¹)
- For QED with μ₅ ∼ T: τ_QED ∼ 1/(α² μ₅)
- At saturation: k ∼ αμ₅, A ∼ μ₅/α, B ∼ μ₅²

## Context

This work extends the study of collective modes in relativistic plasmas (reviewed by Blaizot (2001) without Berry curvature corrections) to include parity-violating effects from chiral asymmetry. It builds on the kinetic theory with Berry curvature developed by Son (2012), Stephanov (2012), and Son (2012), and is closely related to earlier studies of instabilities in the electroweak theory at large lepton chemical potential by Redlich (1984) and Rubakov (1985), as well as work on primordial magnetic field generation in the early Universe by Joyce (1997), Laine (2005), and Boyarsky (2011). The instability differs from the conventional Weibel instability (Weibel, 1959) because the assumptions of isotropic equilibrium and symmetric self-energy are violated by parity-violating effects. The results enable future numerical simulations of the dynamical evolution of chiral plasmas and may help explain magnetic helicity generation in heavy ion collisions and compact stars.
