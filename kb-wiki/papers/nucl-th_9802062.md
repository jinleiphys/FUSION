# Electroweak Processes in Few-Nucleon Systems

> After a brief introduction into the basic ingredients of electroweak theory as a spontaneously broken local, non-Abelian gauge symmetry, the general properties of the electromagnetic current and two-photon operators are discussed. The specific forms of vector and axial one-nucleon currents are given, together with lowest order $π$ meson exchange and isobar currents as well as meson production currents. A brief overview is given on the most important one- and two-boson processes. Electron scattering in the one-boson- approximation is then considered in greater detail. Formal expressions of the cross section for inclusive and exclusive processes are given, including parity violating contributions from $γ$-$Z$ interference as well as from parity violating components in the hadronic wave function. Specific electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron are then discussed with respect to the influence of meson exchange currents, isobar configurations in the deuteron groundstate, relativistic contributions and the role of $π$-meson retardation. Furthermore, recent results on coherent and incoherent $π$- and $η$-photoproduction are presented as well as a discussion of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and the effect of a parity violating deuteron component on inclusive electron scattering off the deuteron for quasifree kinematics. The review closes with a summary and a brief outlook.

# Electroweak Processes in Few-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

This review argues that the unified electroweak theory, when combined with effective hadronic degrees of freedom (nucleon, meson, and isobar), can successfully describe electromagnetic and weak reactions in few-nucleon systems, but the connection between these effective degrees of freedom and the underlying quark-gluon dynamics of QCD remains an open question. The paper emphasizes that the Siegert theorem and generalized Siegert operators allow reliable calculation of electric transition matrix elements from charge density alone, implicitly including a dominant part of meson exchange currents without explicit knowledge of those currents.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of electroweak interactions as a spontaneously broken SU(2)_L × U(1) gauge symmetry, then derives the gauge conditions for electromagnetic current and two-photon operators, including the continuity equation and low-energy theorems. It presents the multipole decomposition of charge and current density operators, the generalized Siegert theorem, and explicit forms for one-nucleon vector and axial currents with their nonrelativistic reductions, as well as the lowest-order pion exchange current and isobar currents. Specific applications to deuteron reactions are discussed, including photo- and electrodisintegration, coherent and incoherent π- and η-photoproduction, the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, and parity violation in electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- Fine structure constant α = 1/137, characterizing the weakness of the electromagnetic interaction
- Weak boson masses: M_W± = 80.22 GeV and M_Z = 91.19 GeV
- The relation G_F/√2 = g²/8M_W² connects the Fermi constant to the weak coupling and W boson mass
- Elementary electric charge given by e = g sinθ_W, where θ_W = tan⁻¹(g'/g) is the weak angle

## Context

This review extends the theoretical treatment of electroweak processes in few-nucleon systems that was developed in the author's earlier Schladming Lectures (Arenhoevel, 1994) and builds on foundational work including the Siegert theorem (Siegert, 1937), the Low theorem (Low, 1954), and the Gell-Mann and Goldberger low-energy theorem (Gell-Mann and Goldberger, 1954). The paper enables future calculations of electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron that incorporate meson exchange currents, isobar configurations, relativistic contributions, and π-meson retardation effects, while also providing a framework for studying parity-violating contributions from γ-Z interference and parity-violating components in hadronic wave functions.
