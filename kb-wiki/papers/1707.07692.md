# Multi-angle calculation of the matter-neutrino resonance near an accretion disk

> We perform a numerical study of the matter-neutrino resonance in a multi-angle calculation in the vicinity of an accretion disk. We assume thermally distributed neutrino and anti-neutrino fields emitted by two-dimensional disk that is homogeneous and isotropic; the electrons are assumed to be at constant density. We compare the the result of this computation to that obtained using single-angle approximation. We investigate the robustness of matter-neutrino resonance in environment surrounding accretion disks by progressively relaxing the single angle approximation. We find that the multi-angle results in the present simplified model do not support a robust resonance mechanism as suggested by the single angle treatment. We also discuss the context under which matter-neutrino resonance may be important in future studies.

## Key claim

Multi-angle calculations in a simplified accretion-disk model do not support a robust matter-neutrino resonance mechanism as suggested by single-angle treatments; the resonance appears to be an artifact of the single-angle approximation rather than a physical phenomenon.

## Method

The author performed numerical multi-angle flavor evolution calculations for neutrinos and anti-neutrinos emitted by a homogeneous, isotropic two-dimensional infinite disk with constant electron density, using a thermal energy distribution for each flavor. The single-angle approximation was progressively relaxed by replacing a single emission angle (45°) with a uniform range of emission angles (opening angle Δϑ), and the resulting self-interaction Hamiltonian was computed with the full angular dependence. The evolution of the self-interaction potential and survival probabilities was compared between single-angle and multi-angle calculations for both normal and inverted mass hierarchies.

## Key numbers

- Luminosities used: ν_e = 3.0×10⁵² erg/s, anti-ν_e = 4.5×10⁵² erg/s, ν_x = 9×10⁵¹ erg/s
- Temperatures: ν_e = 2.65 MeV, anti-ν_e = 3.85 MeV, ν_x = 4.35 MeV
- Chemical potential (dimensionless): η = 3 for all flavors
- Matter potential held constant at λ = 1000 km⁻¹ (≈ 3.16×10⁻²² erg)
- Vacuum mass splitting |Δm²| = 2.5×10⁻³ eV², vacuum mixing angle θ_V = 0.01 rad
- For inverted hierarchy, matter-neutrino resonance completely disappears by opening angle Δϑ = 1°
- For normal hierarchy, resonance persists for Δϑ = 0.5° and 1° but disappears by Δϑ = 2°
- The opening angle at which resonance disappears (≈2°) is much smaller than expected in realistic accretion-disk geometries (disk diameter ≥ 10 km)

## Context

This work extends the single-angle studies of matter-neutrino resonance in accretion disks by Malkus (2014), Zhu (2016), Wu (2015), and Frensel (2016) to multi-angle calculations, finding that the resonance is not robust. The results suggest that future studies of neutrino flavor evolution near neutron-star merger accretion disks must use multi-angle treatments, and that the resonance may only survive if spatial inhomogeneities (as studied by Duan (2014), Abbar (2015), Mirizzi (2015), Chakraborty (2015), and Cirigliano (2017)) are included.
