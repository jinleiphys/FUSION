# Low Energy Magnetic Radiation (LEMAR ) of Warm Nuclei

> The enhancement observed below 2 MeV in the radiative strength function of nuclei near closed shells is explained by shell model calculations as M1 transitions between excited states. In the open-shell a change to a bimodal structure composed of the zero energy spike and a scissors resonance is found. The features are caused by realignment of high-j orbitals.

## Key claim

The low-energy enhancement of the radiative strength function below 2 MeV in nuclei near closed shells is explained as M1 transitions between excited states (LEMAR), while in open-shell nuclei a bimodal structure emerges consisting of a zero-energy spike and a scissors resonance; both features are caused by realignment of high-j orbitals, and the paper argues this is a general feature expected for all deformed nuclei.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed for Mo isotopes (⁹⁰Zr, ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo) using active proton orbits π(0f₅/₂, 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0g₉/₂) and neutron orbits ν(0g₉/₂, 1d₅/₂, 0g₇/₂) relative to a ⁶⁸Ni core, with effective g-factors g_eff_s = 0.7 g_free_s. For Fe isotopes (⁶⁰,⁶⁴,⁶⁸Fe), calculations used the CA48PN model space with the CA48MH1 Hamiltonian and effective g-factors g_eff_s = 0.9 g_free_s. Reduced transition probabilities B(M1) were calculated for all transitions between states with spins J = 0–6 (Mo) or J = 0–10 (Fe), totaling more than 14000 M1 transitions per parity for Mo and more than 22000 for Fe, sorted into 100 keV bins by transition energy.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹⁴Mo, the temperature derived from the LEMAR spike is T ≈ 0.8 MeV, consistent with experimental level densities from Chang (2006) and gamma strength functions from Guttormsen (2005).
- For ⁶⁸Fe, the summed M1 strength from the ground state to all 1⁺ states is ∑B(M1,0⁺₁→1⁺) = 1.7 μ²_N.
- For ⁶⁸Fe, the integrated M1 strength in the scissors resonance region (2–5 MeV) is 6.6 μ²_N, which is 3.5 times larger than the ground-state excitation sum.
- For ⁶⁰,⁶⁴,⁶⁸Fe, the integrated M1 strength below 5 MeV varies by at most 8% from an average of 9.80 μ²_N.
- For ¹⁵¹,¹⁵³Sm, the integrated strength assigned to the scissors resonance is 7.8 μ²_N, which is 2.5 times larger than the summed strength of the SR excitation from the ground state of ¹⁵²Sm.
- For ⁶⁸Fe, the E2 strength function shows a bump at 1.2 MeV generated by stretched E2 transitions with moment of inertia J ≈ 12 MeV⁻¹, close to the rigid body value.

## Context

This work extends the shell model explanation of the low-energy M1 enhancement (LEMAR) first proposed by Schwengner (2013) for Mo isotopes, and later applied to Fe isotopes by Brown (2014) and Schwengner (2017). The paper reconciles the LEMAR spike with the scissors resonance known from deformed nuclei (Hamberger 1984), showing they form a bimodal structure in open-shell nuclei. The results enable improved calculations of neutron capture rates in stellar r-process environments, where Simon (2016) showed the low-energy enhancement can increase rates by factors of 100–1000. The paper also demonstrates that the LEMAR spike is consistent with the constant-temperature level density formula, with temperature T derived from experimental level densities matching that from the gamma strength functions.
