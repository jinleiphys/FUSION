# Binding of antikaons and Lambda(1405) clusters in light kaonic nuclei

> The energy spectra of light-mass kaonic nuclei were investigated using the theoretical framework of the $0s$-orbital model with zero-range $\bar{K} N$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K}$ interactions of effective single-channel real potentials. The energies of the $\bar{K} NN$, $\bar{K} NNN$, $\bar{K} NNNN$, $\bar{K}\bar{K} N$, and $\bar{K}\bar{K} NN$ systems were calculated in the cases of weak- and deep-binding of the $\bar{K} N$ interaction, which was adjusted to fit the $Λ(1405)$ mass with the energy of the $\bar{K} N$ bound state. The results qualitatively reproduced the energy systematics of kaonic nuclei calculated via other theoretical approaches. In the energy spectra of the $\bar{K} NN$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K} NN$ systems, the lowest states $\bar{K} NN(J^π,T=0^-,1/2)$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K} NN(0^+,0)$ were found to have binding energies approximately twice and four times as large as that of the $\bar{K} N(1/2^-,0)$ state, respectively. Higher $(J^π,T)$ states including $\bar{K} NN(1^-,1/2)$, $\bar{K}\bar{K} NN(0^+,1)$, and $\bar{K}\bar{K} NN(1^+,1)$ were predicted at energies of 9--25 MeV below the antikaon-decay threshold. The effective $Λ(1405)$-$Λ(1405)$ interaction in the $\bar{K}\bar{K} N N$ system was also investigated via a $\bar{K} N+\bar{K} N$-cluster model. Strong and weak $Λ(1405)$-$Λ(1405)$ attractions were obtained in the $S^π=0^+$ and $S^π=1^-$ channels, respectively. The $Λ(1405)$-$Λ(1405)$ interaction in the $$\bar{K}\bar{K} NN$ system was compared with the effective $d$-$d$ interaction in the $NNNN$ system, and the properties of dimer-dimer interactions in hadron and nuclear systems were discussed.

## Key claim

The energy spectra of light-mass kaonic nuclei were calculated using a simplified 0s-orbital model with zero-range effective interactions, and the results qualitatively reproduce the energy systematics from more sophisticated theoretical approaches, but the author explicitly states that the model is not intended to predict precise energy values due to its dependence on uncertain hadron-hadron interactions and model treatments.

## Method

The author applied a 0s-orbital model with one-range Gaussian wave functions and zero-range (delta function) effective single-channel real potentials for the antikaon-nucleon (Kbar N) and antikaon-antikaon (Kbar Kbar) interactions, with a finite-range Volkov NN interaction tuned to reproduce S-wave NN-scattering lengths. The Kbar N interaction strength was adjusted to fit the Lambda(1405) mass in two cases: a weak-binding case (set-I) with the Lambda* energy at -10 MeV based on chiral SU(3) analysis, and a deep-binding case (set-II) with the Lambda* energy at -27 MeV based on the Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) interaction. Energies were calculated for the Kbar N, Kbar NN, Kbar NNN, Kbar NNNN, Kbar Kbar N, and Kbar Kbar NN systems by counting spin and isospin components of the two-body pairs, and the effective Lambda*-Lambda* interaction in the Kbar Kbar NN system was investigated using a Kbar N + Kbar N cluster model.

## Key numbers

- Lambda(1405) energy used for weak-binding case (set-I): epsilon_Lambda* = -10 MeV, based on chiral SU(3) analysis by Hyodo (2007)
- Lambda(1405) energy used for deep-binding case (set-II): epsilon_Lambda* = -27 MeV, from the PDG value by Tanabashi (2018)
- For set-I (weak-binding): nu_N = 0.16 fm^-2, single-particle kinetic energy barT = 9.95 MeV, Kbar N T=0 interaction expectation value barV^{T=0}_{Kbar N} = -19.95 MeV
- For set-II (deep-binding): nu_N = 0.25 fm^-2, barT = 15.55 MeV, barV^{T=0}_{Kbar N} = -42.55 MeV
- The lowest Kbar NN(0^-,1/2) state has binding energy approximately twice that of the Kbar N(1/2^-,0) state
- The lowest Kbar Kbar NN(0^+,0) state has binding energy approximately four times that of the Kbar N(1/2^-,0) state
- Higher states Kbar NN(1^-,1/2), Kbar Kbar NN(0^+,1), and Kbar Kbar NN(1^+,1) were predicted at energies of 9-25 MeV below the antikaon-decay threshold
- For the Kbar Kbar NN system, strong and weak Lambda*-Lambda* attractions were obtained in the S^pi = 0^+ and S^pi = 1^- channels, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaonic nuclei that began with the Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) interaction (Akaishi, 2002; Yamazaki, 2007), which predicted deeply bound antikaon states, and contrasts with weaker interactions from chiral SU(3) effective field theory analyses (Hyodo, 2007) that give smaller binding energies. The paper enables comparison of the effective Lambda*-Lambda* dimer-dimer interaction in the Kbar Kbar NN system with the effective deuteron-deuteron (d-d) interaction in the NNNN system, and discusses the role of nucleon Fermi statistics and antikaon Bose statistics in determining the properties of dimer-dimer interactions in hadron and nuclear systems.
