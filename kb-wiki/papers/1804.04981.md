# TMD gluon distributions at small x in the CGC theory

> We review recent progress in the description of unpolarized transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) gluon distributions at small $x$ in the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory. We discuss the origin of the non-universality of TMD gluon distributions in the TMD factorization framework and in the CGC theory and the equivalence of the two approaches in their overlapping domain of validity. We show some applications of this equivalence, including recent results on the behavior of TMD gluon distributions at small $x$, and on the study of gluon saturation. We discuss recent advances in the unification of the TMD evolution and the non-linear small-$x$ evolution of gluon distributions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the equivalence between TMD factorization and CGC theory in their overlapping domain of validity at small x, showing that the non-universality of gluon TMDs can be understood within the CGC framework where a universal distribution of color charges restores universality among different processes, but only in the correlation limit where the momentum imbalance is much smaller than the jet transverse momentum.

## Method

The author reviews the derivation of effective TMD factorization for forward dijet production in proton-nucleus collisions, where the proton is described by collinear PDFs and the nucleus by TMD gluon distributions. The CGC cross section is derived using the hybrid approach with Wilson lines resumming multiple rescatterings off the dense gluon field of the nucleus. The equivalence is established by taking the small-x limit of the TMD factorized cross section and comparing it to the correlation limit (k_t much less than P_t) of the CGC result, showing that each gluon TMD can be defined as an operator of Wilson lines averaged over the universal CGC color charge distribution.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum Q_s characterizes the transition between linear and non-linear regimes in the CGC framework, providing the intrinsic momentum scale for gluon dynamics at small x
- The momentum imbalance k_t of the jet pair is given by k_t^2 = p_{1t}^2 + p_{2t}^2 + 2 p_{1t} p_{2t} cos(Delta phi), where p_{1t} and p_{2t} are the transverse momenta of the individual jets
- The longitudinal momentum fractions probed in the proton and nucleus are x_1 = (1/sqrt(s))(p_{1t} e^{y_1} + p_{2t} e^{y_2}) and x_2 = (1/sqrt(s))(p_{1t} e^{-y_1} + p_{2t} e^{-y_2}), where y_1 and y_2 are the jet rapidities
- For forward dijet production in pA collisions, the TMD factorization formula for finite N_c involves eight different unpolarized gluon TMDs

## Context

This review extends the work of Dominguez et al. (2010, 2011) who first established the equivalence between TMD factorization and CGC approaches for dijet production, and builds on the earlier observation by Kharzeev et al. (2003) of non-universality between dipole and Weizsacker-Williams gluon distributions. The paper enables future phenomenological studies of gluon saturation using the improved TMD factorization (ITMD) framework of Kotko et al. (2015), which interpolates between the TMD and high-energy factorization limits and can be applied to forward dijet production in pA collisions and ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions.
