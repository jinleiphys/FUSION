# Theory of the Lamb shift and Fine Structure in muonic $\mathrm{^4He}$ ions and the muonic $\mathrm{^3He-^4He}$ Isotope Shift

> We provide an up to date summary of the theory contributions to the 2S-2P Lamb shift and the fine structure of the 2P state in the muonic helium ion $(\mathrm{μ^4He})^+$. This summary serves as the basis for the extraction of the alpha particle charge radius from the muonic helium Lamb shift measurements at the Paul Scherrer Institute, Switzerland. Individual theory contributions needed for a charge radius extraction are compared and compiled into a consistent summary. The influence of the alpha particle charge distribution on the elastic two-photon exchange is studied to take into account possible model-dependencies of the energy levels on the electric form factor of the nucleus. We also discuss the theory uncertainty which enters the extraction of the $\mathrm{^3He-^4He}$ isotope shift from the muonic measurements. The theory uncertainty of the extraction is much smaller than a present discrepancy between previous isotope shift measurements. This work completes our series of $n=2$ theory compilations in light muonic atoms which we have performed already for muonic hydrogen, deuterium, and helium-3 ions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a comprehensive compilation of theory contributions to the 2S-2P Lamb shift and 2P fine structure in muonic helium-4 ions, serving as the basis for extracting the alpha particle charge radius from measurements at the Paul Scherrer Institute, but the authors note that several pure QED contributions have uncertainties not much better than the experimental accuracy goal of 0.06 meV, and that the inelastic two-photon exchange (polarizability) will dominate the extracted charge radius uncertainty.

## Method

The authors reviewed and compared individual theory contributions from multiple groups (Borie, Martynenko/Krutov, Karshenboim, Jentschura) for the 2S-2P Lamb shift and 2P fine structure in muonic helium-4 ions, compiling them into a consistent summary. They studied the influence of the alpha particle charge distribution on the elastic two-photon exchange by testing different charge distributions and form factor parameterizations to account for possible model-dependencies. For the isotope shift between muonic helium-3 and helium-4, they exploited correlations between model-dependent calculations by the TRIUMF/Hebrew group to reduce the theory uncertainty.

## Key numbers

* The finite size effect in muonic helium-4 amounts to approximately 300 meV, which is 20% of the Lamb shift.
* The frequency uncertainty of the muonic helium-4 Lamb shift measurements is on the order of 15 GHz, corresponding to 0.06 meV, which serves as the accuracy goal for theory contributions.
* The established alpha particle charge radius from electron scattering is 1.681(4) fm (Sick, 2008).
* The muonic hydrogen proton charge radius is 0.84087(26)exp(29)theo fm (Pohl, 2010; Antognini, 2013).
* The muonic deuterium deuteron charge radius is 2.12616(13)exp(89)theo fm (Pohl, 2016; Hernandez, 2018; Pachucki, 2018).
* The CODATA-2014 proton charge radius is 0.8751(61) fm.

## Context

This work completes the authors' series of n=2 theory compilations for light muonic atoms, following previous work on muonic hydrogen (Antognini, 2013), muonic deuterium (Krauth, 2016), and muonic helium-3 ions (Franke, 2016). The compilation extends and reconciles differences between earlier calculations by Borie (2014), Krutov (2014), Karshenboim (2012), and Jentschura (2011). The results will enable extraction of the alpha particle charge radius from CREMA measurements at PSI, which is expected to be about five times more precise than the established value from electron scattering (Sick, 2008). The combination with muonic helium-3 measurements will contribute to resolving a discrepancy between isotope shift measurements in electronic helium (Shiner, 1995; Cancio Pastor, 2012; Zheng, 2017).
