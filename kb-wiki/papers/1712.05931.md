# An exact solution to the Bertsch problem and the non-universality of the Unitary Fermi Gas

> We analyze the universality of the Unitary Fermi Gas in its ground state from a Wilsonian renormalization point of view and compute the effective range dependence of the Bertsch parameter $ξ$ exactly. To this end we construct an effective block-diagonal two-body separable interaction with the Fermi momentum as a cut-off which reduces the calculation to the mean field level. The interaction is separable in momentum space and is determined by Tabakin's inverse scattering formula. For a vanishing effective range we get $ξ= \frac{176}{9 π}-\frac{17}{3} = 0.56$. By using phase-equivalent similarity transformations we can show that there is a class of exact solutions with any value in the range $ 0.56 \ge ξ\ge -1/3$.

## Key claim

The Bertsch parameter ξ for the Unitary Fermi Gas is not a universal number; rather, there exists a class of exact solutions yielding any value in the range 0.56 ≥ ξ ≥ -1/3, all satisfying the original Bertsch scattering conditions, contradicting the widespread belief that ξ is uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors constructed a block-diagonal two-body separable interaction with the Fermi momentum k_F as the cutoff, reducing the many-body calculation to the mean-field level. They used Tabakin's inverse scattering formula to determine the separable potential that exactly reproduces the unitary limit phase shifts. They then applied phase-equivalent similarity transformations via the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) method to generate a continuous family of isospectral potentials, each yielding a different value of the Bertsch parameter.

## Key numbers

- For vanishing effective range (r_0 = 0), the exact Bertsch parameter from the separable potential is ξ = 176/(9π) - 17/3 = 0.56.
- The SRG evolution produces a continuous range of ξ values from 0.56 down to -1/3 at the fixed point (s → ∞, λ → 0).
- The "universal" value ξ = 0.37 is reproduced at SRG scale λ/k_F = 0.9.
- For r_0 = 0, the intercept ξ_∞ = 0 occurs at α_0 k_F = -7.5378; for α_0 → -∞, the intercept occurs at r_0 k_F = 2.038.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the assumption of universality underlying over 50 previous calculations and experiments listed in Drut (2012), which found ξ ≈ 0.37-0.40. It also disagrees with the Monte Carlo results of Conduit and Schonberg (2017), who reported ξ = 0.388(1). The authors suggest that the additional condition of locality in the interaction potential, present in both ultracold atomic experiments and Monte Carlo simulations using local potentials, may explain the observed numerical agreement among those studies, and propose this as an open question for future work.
