# The role of angle dependent phase rotations of reaction amplitudes in $η$ photoproduction on protons

> It has recently been proven that the invariance of observables with respect to angle dependent phase rotations of reaction amplitudes mixes multipoles changing also their relative strength [1]. All contemporary partial wave analyses (PWA) in $η$ photoproduction on protons, either energy dependent (ED) [2-5] or single energy (SE) [6] do not take this effect into consideration. It is commonly accepted that there exist quite some similarity in the $E0+$ multipole for all PWA, but notable differences in this, but also in remaining partial waves still remain. In this paper we demonstrate that once this phase rotations are properly taken into account, all contemporary ED and SE partial wave analysis become almost identical for the dominant $E0+$ multipole, and the agreement among all other multipoles becomes much better. We also show that the the measured observables are almost equally well reproduced for all PWA, and the remaining differences among multipoles can be attributed solely to the difference of predictions for unmeasured observables. So, new measurements are needed.

## Key claim

Once the angle-dependent phase rotations of reaction amplitudes (continuum ambiguity) are properly synchronized across all contemporary partial wave analyses, the dominant $E0+$ multipole for $\eta$ photoproduction on protons becomes almost identical for all models up to $W_{CM} \approx 1650$ MeV, and agreement among other multipoles improves significantly; the remaining differences can be attributed solely to predictions for unmeasured observables.

## Method

The authors reconstructed all four helicity amplitudes for seven different partial wave analyses (Mainz EtaMAID, Bonn-Gatchina, Kent State University, Juelich-Bonn, and three single-energy fixed-t solutions) from their published multipoles. They then applied a common phase rotation to all helicity amplitudes of each model, using the phase of the Bonn-Gatchina $H_1$ helicity amplitude as the reference convention, and performed a partial wave decomposition of the rotated amplitude sets to compare the resulting multipoles.

## Key numbers

* The $E0+$ multipole from all seven analyses became practically identical for center-of-mass energies $W_{CM} < 1650$ MeV after phase synchronization.
* The experimental data used for comparison included 2400 unpolarized differential cross section $\sigma_0$ data points from A2@MAMI (lab energies 710-1395 MeV, 20 angular bins from 18 to 162 degrees), 150 $\Sigma$ data points from GRAAL (lab energies 724-1472 MeV), and 144 $T$ and 144 $F$ data points from A2@MAMI (lab energies 725-1350 MeV).
* The $\chi^2/N_{data}$ values were compared across all seven solutions at energies including $W_{CM}=$ 1554, 1602, 1765, and 1840 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formal proof of continuum ambiguity effects in partial wave analyses (Svarc 2018) by demonstrating their practical impact on existing $\eta$ photoproduction analyses. It shows that the previously assumed differences among energy-dependent coupled-channel models (Bonn-Gatchina, Kent State University, Juelich-Bonn) and single-energy analyses (Mainz EtaMAID, fixed-t solutions) were largely due to uncontrolled phase mismatches rather than genuine model differences. The result enables future work to focus on resolving higher partial waves through new measurements of unmeasured observables, rather than debating the dominant S-wave.
