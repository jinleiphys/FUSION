# Differences in high p_t meson production between CERN SPS and RHIC heavy ion collisions

> In this talk we present a perturbative QCD improved parton model calculation for light meson production in high energy heavy ion collisions. In order to describe the experimental data properly, one needs to augment the standard pQCD model by the transverse momentum distribution of partons ("intrinsic k_T"). Proton-nucleus data indicate the presence of nuclear shadowing and multiscattering effects. Further corrections are needed in nucleus-nucleus collisions to explain the observed reduction of the cross section. We introduce the idea of proton dissociation and compare our calculations with the SPS and RHIC experimental data.

# Differences in high \(p_t\) meson production between CERN SPS and RHIC heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

A perturbative QCD improved parton model with intrinsic transverse momentum (\(k_T\)) and proton dissociation can describe light meson production in heavy ion collisions, but the observed suppression at RHIC is too large to be explained by proton dissociation alone, leaving room for jet quenching as an additional mechanism.

## Method

The authors performed leading-order pQCD calculations of pion production in \(pp\), \(pA\), and \(AA\) collisions, augmenting the standard parton model with a Gaussian intrinsic \(k_T\) distribution. The width parameter \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle\) was fitted to \(pp\) data across energies, then a nuclear broadening term \(C \cdot h_{pA}(b)\) was added for \(pA\) collisions, with parameters \(m=4\) and \(C = 0.4\) GeV\(^2\) extracted from fits to experimental data on Be, Ti, and W targets. For \(AA\) collisions, the model was further modified by introducing proton dissociation, where a nucleon is assumed to disintegrate after \(N_c\) inelastic collisions, implemented as a modification of the Glauber thickness function.

## Key numbers

- Best fit for \(pA\) broadening: \(C \approx 0.4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(m = 4\) (maximum number of contributing semihard collisions)
- For CERN SPS Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, the model without proton dissociation overpredicts experimental data by 40%
- Best fit for proton dissociation at SPS: \(N_c = 4\) collisions before dissociation, corresponding to a transverse momentum scale of \(\sqrt{N_c C} \approx 1.25\) GeV
- For Au+Au at RHIC (130 AGeV), proton dissociation with \(N_c=4\) reduces the cross section by 45%; with \(N_c=3\) the reduction is 60%
- The \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle\) values used for SPS: 1.6 GeV\(^2\) at 200 AGeV and 1.7 GeV\(^2\) at 158 AGeV; for RHIC: 2.0 GeV\(^2\) at 130 GeV

## Context

This work extends the pQCD parton model with intrinsic \(k_T\) developed by Owens (1987) and Wang (1997-1998) to heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies. The nuclear broadening parameterization follows the Cronin effect observed by Cronin et al. (1975) and uses nuclear shadowing parameterizations from Wang (1991) and Eskola (1999). The proton dissociation idea is motivated by Dokshitzer (2001) and is presented as an alternative or complement to jet quenching models. The results indicate that systematic \(pp\), \(pA\), and \(AA\) studies at the same facility (such as RHIC's planned \(pA\) program) are needed to disentangle the competing effects of intrinsic \(k_T\) enhancement, nuclear shadowing, the Cronin effect, proton dissociation, and jet quenching.
