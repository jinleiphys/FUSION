# Aspect of Clusters Correlation at Light Nuclei Excited State

> The correlation of $αα$ was probed via measuring the transverse momentum $p_{T}$ and width $δp_{T}$ of one $α$, for the first time, which represents the spatial and dynamical essentialities of the initial coupling state in $^{8}$Be nucleus. The weighted interaction vertex of 3$α$ reflected by the magnitudes of their relative momentums and relative emission angles proves the isosceles triangle configuration for 3$α$ at the high excited energy analogous Hoyle states.

## Key claim

The paper claims to have probed alpha-alpha correlation via the transverse momentum and its width of one alpha particle for the first time, representing spatial and dynamical properties of the initial coupling state in 8Be. The abstract's claim of "proving" the isosceles triangle configuration for 3-alpha at high excited analogous Hoyle states is hedged by the experimental limitation that only forward-angle (less than 9 degrees) three-alpha events from projectile decay were measured, not from isotropic target decay.

## Method

A 60 MeV/nucleon 12C beam bombarded a 3.5 mm 9Be target to produce a 25 MeV/nucleon 11C secondary beam, which was then directed onto a 50 mg/cm2 carbon target. The excited 8Be fragments from breakup reactions and excited 12C nuclei from one-alpha exchange reactions were detected using a zero-degree telescope system consisting of a double-sided silicon strip detector (DSSD) and a 2x2 CsI(Tl) scintillator array, with three parallel plate avalanche chambers (PPACs) for beam tracking. The 2+ excited state of 8Be was reconstructed from the invariant mass of two coincident alpha particles, and the 3-alpha correlation in 12C was analyzed using Dalitz plots and relative momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy of the 2+ excited state of 8Be: 3.09 +/- 0.05 MeV, with width Gamma = 3.20 +/- 0.13 MeV (excluding experimental setup resolution)
- Mean transverse momentum of alpha in 8Be: 75.03 +/- 1.02 MeV/c, with width (1 sigma) of 28.75 +/- 0.35 MeV/c
- Relative momentum of two alphas in the lab system for the 2+ state: 215.59 +/- 1.22 MeV/c, with width 51.20 +/- 1.35 MeV/c
- Correlation function decomposition yielded two peaks: prel1 = 48.11 +/- 2.37 MeV/c (sigma1 = 17.50 +/- 2.70 MeV/c) and prel2 = 239.76 +/- 6.32 MeV/c (sigma2 = 150.05 +/- 7.86 MeV/c)
- VMC calculation for relative alpha momentum: 240.68 MeV/c for 2+ state, 31.69 MeV/c for 0+ ground state

## Context

This work extends the study of cluster correlations in light nuclei, specifically the alpha-alpha correlation in 8Be and the 3-alpha configuration in 12C analogous Hoyle states. The measurement of transverse momentum and width of a single alpha to probe the initial coupling state is presented as a first-time approach. The paper references the VMC calculations of Wiringa and Pieper (2002) for momentum distributions, and the Dalitz plot analysis method follows techniques used in heavy-ion reactions such as 40Ca+40Ca and 48Ca+48Ca reactions (reference 21). The results contradict the compiled astrophysical width for the 2+ state of 8Be, finding a wider resonance (3.20 MeV versus compiled values), which the authors suggest may lead to lower 12C abundance from the Hoyle state and implies contributions from higher excited analogous Hoyle states.
