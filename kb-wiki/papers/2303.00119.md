# Deeply-virtual Compton process $e^- N \to e^- γπN$ to study nucleon to resonance transitions

> We study the deeply-virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) process $e^- N \to e^- γπN$ involving the transition between a nucleon and a nucleon resonance in the $πN$ system, within the framework of generalized parton distributions (GPDs). For the four lowest-lying nucleon resonances, $Δ(1232)$, $P_{11}(1440)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, and $S_{11}(1535)$, we express the DVCS amplitude in the Bjorken limit in terms of corresponding nucleon-to-resonance GPDs. Building upon the knowledge of the well studied electromagnetic nucleon-to-resonance transition form factors, which map the quark charge densities in transverse position space, the corresponding GPDs will open the prospect to also access the longitudinal momentum distributions of quarks in the transition. We provide estimates for cross sections and beam-spin asymmetries in the first and second $πN$ resonance regions in the kinematics of forthcoming CLAS12 data from Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the deeply-virtual Compton scattering process \(e^- N \to e^- \gamma \pi N\) can be used to access nucleon-to-resonance transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the four lowest-lying nucleon resonances, but only in the Bjorken limit at leading twist-2 level, and the estimates for cross sections and beam-spin asymmetries are provided specifically for the kinematics of forthcoming CLAS12 data from Jefferson Lab.

## Method

The authors construct an isobar model in which a nucleon resonance \(R\) (specifically \(\Delta(1232)\), \(P_{11}(1440)\), \(D_{13}(1520)\), and \(S_{11}(1535)\)) is produced via the \(e^- N \to e^- \gamma R\) process, which then decays into the \(\pi N\) system. The amplitude is separated into a Bethe-Heitler contribution, calculated exactly using empirical electromagnetic transition form factors from MAID2007/2008 analyses, and a deeply-virtual Compton scattering contribution parameterized in the Bjorken limit at leading twist-2 level in terms of transition GPDs. The first moments of the unpolarized GPDs are constrained by the electromagnetic transition form factors, while the polarized GPDs are constrained by PCAC relations relating axial transition form factors to the \(\pi N \Delta\) coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Breit-Wigner masses of the four resonances: \(M_R = 1.232\) GeV for \(\Delta(1232)\), \(M_R = 1.440\) GeV for \(P_{11}(1440)\), \(M_R = 1.520\) GeV for \(D_{13}(1520)\), and \(M_R = 1.535\) GeV for \(S_{11}(1535)\)
- Magnetic dipole transition form factor for \(N \to \Delta(1232)\) at \(t=0\): \(G^{*}_{M}(0) = 3.00\) (dimensionless, using the dipole form factor \(G_D(t) \equiv 1/(1 - t/0.71)^2\) with \(t\) in GeV\(^2\))
- Electric quadrupole transition form factor for \(N \to \Delta(1232)\) at \(t=0\): \(G^{*}_{E}(0) = 0.064\) (dimensionless)
- Coulomb quadrupole transition form factor for \(N \to \Delta(1232)\) at \(t=0\): \(G^{*}_{C}(0) = 0.124 \times 4M_R^2/(M_R^2-M_N^2)\) (dimensionless)
- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi \simeq 92.4\) MeV
- \(\pi N \Delta\) coupling constant: \(f_{\pi N \Delta} \simeq 2.08\) determined from the \(\Delta \to \pi N\) decay width

## Context

This work extends the pioneering studies of non-diagonal \(N \to \Delta\) DVCS by Frankfurt et al. (1999) and Guidal et al. (2003) that relied on the large-\(N_c\) limit framework, and builds upon the first analysis of CLAS6 data from Jefferson Lab presented by Moreno et al. (2009). The paper enables future extraction of longitudinal momentum distributions of quarks in nucleon-to-resonance transitions from forthcoming CLAS12 data, complementing existing knowledge of electromagnetic transition form factors from Pascalutsa et al. (2006) and Aznauryan et al. (2011) that map transverse charge densities.
