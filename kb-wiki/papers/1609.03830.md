# Exclusive photoproduction of a $γ\,ρ$ pair with a large invariant mass

> Exclusive photoproduction of a $γ\,ρ$ pair in the kinematics where the pair has a large invariant mass and the final nucleon has a small transverse momentum is described in the collinear factorization framework. The scattering amplitude is calculated at leading order in $α_s$ and the differential cross sections for the process where the $ρ-$meson is either longitudinally or transversely polarized are estimated in the kinematics of the JLab 12-GeV experiments.

## Key claim

Exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma \rho$ pair with large invariant mass can be described in the collinear QCD factorization framework, provided one avoids kinematical regions where small momentum transfer is exchanged in the upper blob or where strong final-state interactions between the $\rho$ meson and the nucleon are dominated by resonance effects.

## Method

The scattering amplitude for the process $\gamma^{(*)} N \to \gamma \rho^0 N'$ was calculated at leading order in $\alpha_s$ using collinear factorization, where the hard scale is the invariant mass $M_{\gamma\rho}$ of the final photon-meson pair. The amplitude was expressed as a convolution of a hard scattering subprocess with generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the nucleon transition and distribution amplitudes (DAs) for the $\rho$ meson, using the asymptotic form $\phi_{\parallel}(z) = \phi_{\bot}(z) = 6z(1-z)$ for the DAs. The GPDs $H$, $\tilde{H}$, and $H_T$ were modeled using Radyushkin-type double distributions based on GRV-98, GRSV-2000, and Anselmino et al. (2013) parton distribution functions, and differential cross sections were estimated for both longitudinally and transversely polarized $\rho$ mesons.

## Key numbers

* The $\rho$ meson decay constants used are $f_{\rho^0} = 216$ MeV for the longitudinally polarized case and $f_\rho^\bot = 160$ MeV for the transversely polarized case.
* The hard scale $M_{\gamma\rho}^2$ is varied from $2.2$ GeV$^2$ up to $10$ GeV$^2$ for the case $S_{\gamma N} = 20$ GeV$^2$.
* The total squared center-of-mass energy of the $\gamma$-$N$ system is $S_{\gamma N} = (1+\xi)s + M^2$, with typical values in the range $S_{\gamma N} \sim 6$-$22$ GeV$^2$ for JLab 12-GeV experiments.
* The skewness parameter $\xi$ is related to $M_{\gamma\rho}^2$ and $S_{\gamma N}$ by $\xi = M_{\gamma\rho}^2 / [2(S_{\gamma N}-M^2) - M_{\gamma\rho}^2]$, with a typical value $\xi = 0.1$ corresponding to $S_{\gamma N}=20$ GeV$^2$ and $M_{\gamma\rho}^2=3.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach previously applied to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production (Goeke et al., 2001; Diehl, 2003; Belitsky et al., 2005) to the exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma \rho$ pair, analogous to timelike Compton scattering (Mueller et al., 1998; Berger et al., 2001; Pire et al., 2011). The process is sensitive to both chiral-even and chiral-odd GPDs, enabling new ways to extract these distributions and test their universality, with feasibility estimates provided for the JLab 12-GeV upgrade detectors.
