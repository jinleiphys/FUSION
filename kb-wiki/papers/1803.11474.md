# Estimates of the baryon densities attainable in heavy-ion collisions from the beam energy scan program

> The baryon and energy densities attained in fragmentation regions in central Au+Au collisions in the energy range of the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) are estimated within the model of the three-fluid dynamics. It is shown that a considerable part of the baryon charge is stopped in the central fireball. Even at 39 GeV, approximately 70% of the total baryon charge turns out to be stopped. The fraction of this stopped baryon charge decreases with collision energy rise, from 100% at 7.7 GeV to $\sim$40% at 62 GeV. The highest initial baryon densities of the thermalized matter, $n_B/n_0 \approx$ 10, are reached in the central region of colliding nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 20--40 GeV. These highest densities develop up to quite moderate freeze-out baryon densities at the midrapidity because the matter of the central fireball is pushed out to fragmentation regions by one-dimensional expansion. Therefore, consequences of these high initial baryon densities can be observed only in the fragmentation regions of colliding nuclei in AFTER@LHC experiments in the fixed-target mode.

# Estimates of the baryon densities attainable in heavy-ion collisions from the beam energy scan program

## Key claim

The highest initial baryon densities of thermalized matter, reaching approximately 10 times normal nuclear density, occur in central Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies between 20 and 40 GeV, but these high densities are only observable in the fragmentation regions rather than at midrapidity because the central fireball matter is pushed outward by one-dimensional expansion.

## Method

The authors used the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile and target baryon-rich matter as two separate fluids plus a fireball fluid for newly produced particles, all coupled by friction terms. Simulations were performed for central Au+Au collisions (impact parameter 2 fm) at beam energy scan energies from 7.7 to 62 GeV, using two equations of state (first-order phase transition and crossover) with friction forces fitted to reproduce baryon stopping at high energies. The baryon density and energy density evolution were tracked in both a central box and along the beam axis to determine equilibration times and baryon charge distribution between central and fragmentation regions.

## Key numbers

- At 39 GeV, approximately 70% of the total baryon charge is stopped in the central fireball
- The fraction of stopped baryon charge decreases from 100% at 7.7 GeV to approximately 40% at 62 GeV
- The highest initial baryon densities of thermalized matter reach n_B/n_0 approximately 10 at center-of-mass energies of 20-40 GeV, where n_0 = 0.15 1/fm^3 is normal nuclear density
- At 11.5 GeV, 96% of baryon charge resides in the central fireball; at 19.6 GeV, 85-90%; at 39 GeV, 65-75%; at 62 GeV, approximately 30-50%
- Equilibration time is approximately twice the passage time of Lorentz-contracted nuclei, roughly 4 m_N R / sqrt(s_NN)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous single-energy analysis at 39 GeV (Ivanov 2017) to the full beam energy scan range at RHIC, and contrasts with the conventional view that maximal baryon density occurs at NICA-FAIR energies (Kekelidze 2017, Ablyazimov 2017). The results contradict the expectation from statistical model analyses (Randrup 2006, 2009) that baryon density peaks at midrapidity at lower energies. The findings suggest that AFTER@LHC fixed-target experiments (Brodsky 2012) could observe high baryon densities in fragmentation regions at equivalent center-of-mass energies up to 72 GeV, which are inaccessible to the current 3FD numerics above 39 GeV due to computational constraints from Lorentz contraction.
