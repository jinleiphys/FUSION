# Medium effects on charged pion ratio in heavy ion collisions

> We have recently studied in the delta-resonance--nucleon-hole model the dependence of the pion spectral function in hot dense asymmetric nuclear matter on the charge of the pion due to the pion p-wave interaction in nuclear medium. In a thermal model, this isospin-dependent effect enhances the ratio of negatively charged to positively charged pions in neutron-rich nuclear matter, and the effect is comparable to that due to the uncertainties in the theoretically predicted stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy at high densities. This effect is, however, reversed if we also take into account the s-wave interaction of the pion in nuclear medium as given by chiral perturbation theory, resulting instead in a slightly reduced ratio of negatively charged to positively charged pions. Relevance of our results to the determination of the nuclear symmetry energy from the ratio of negatively to positively charged pions produced in heavy ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The isospin-dependent pion in-medium effects from p-wave interactions enhance the ratio of negatively to positively charged pions in neutron-rich nuclear matter, and this enhancement is comparable to the effect from uncertainties in the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy at high densities; however, including s-wave interactions from chiral perturbation theory reverses this effect, resulting instead in a slightly reduced ratio.

## Method

The authors computed pion spectral functions in hot dense asymmetric nuclear matter using a self-consistent delta-resonance–nucleon-hole model for the pion p-wave interaction, including the Migdal parameter for short-range repulsion. They then used a thermal model assuming chemical equilibrium among pions, nucleons, and delta resonances, with fugacity parameters to account for chemical non-equilibrium, to calculate the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at conditions corresponding to Au+Au collisions at 0.4 AGeV beam energy. The calculation was repeated including s-wave pion self-energies from chiral perturbation theory to assess the combined effect.

## Key numbers

- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio with only p-wave interactions (Migdal parameter g′ = 1/3) is 2.32, 2.60, and 2.94 for symmetry energy parameters x = 0, 0.5, and 1, respectively, compared to values without pion medium effects (g′ = ∞) of 2.20, 2.40, and 2.60.
- The thermal model uses a temperature T ≈ 43.6 MeV, baryon density ρ_B = 2ρ_0 (with ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³), and isospin asymmetry δ_like ≈ 0.133 for the x = 1 case.
- The measured π⁻/π⁺ ratio from the FOPI Collaboration is about 3 (shown with error bar in Fig. 3).
- From chiral perturbation theory, mass shifts in asymmetric nuclear matter (ρ = 0.165 fm⁻³, δ = 0.2) are Δm_π⁻ = 13.8 MeV, Δm_π⁺ = −1.2 MeV, and Δm_π⁰ = 6.1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends transport model studies by Xiao et al. (2009) that used the IBUU model to extract the nuclear symmetry energy from FOPI Collaboration data, but which neglected pion medium effects. It also builds on earlier work by Xiong et al. (1993) showing that pion medium effects enhance low-energy pion production without changing total yield, and on Korpa et al. (1999) showing charge-dependent pion modifications in asymmetric matter. The results indicate that including both p-wave and s-wave pion in-medium effects will influence the extraction of the nuclear symmetry energy from measured π⁻/π⁺ ratios, and that the FOPI data, which without medium effects favored a very soft symmetry energy (softer than x = 1), is now best described by a less soft one when p-wave effects alone are included.
