# The effect of hadronic scatterings on the measurement of vector meson spin alignments in heavy-ion collisions

> Spin alignments of vector mesons and hyperons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions have been proposed as signals of the global polarization. The STAR experiment first observed the $\rm Λ$ polarization. Recently, the ALICE collaboration measured the transverse momentum ($p_T$) and the collision centrality dependence of $K^*$ and $ϕ$ spin alignments in Pb-Pb collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. A large signal is observed in the low $p_T$ region of mid-central collisions for $K^*$ while the signal is much smaller for $ϕ$, and these have not been understood yet. Since vector mesons have different lifetimes and their decay products have different scattering cross sections, they suffer from different hadronic effects. In this paper, we study the effect of hadronic interactions on the spin alignment of $K^*$, $ϕ$ and $ρ$ mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions with a multi-phase transport model. We find that hadronic scatterings lead to a deviation of the observed spin alignment matrix element $ρ_{00}$ away from the true value for $ρ$ and $K^*$ mesons (with a bigger effect on $ρ$) while the effect is negligible for the $ϕ$ meson. The effect depends on the kinematic acceptance: the observed $ρ_{00}$ value is lower than the true value when the pseudorapidity ($η$) coverage is small while there is little effect when the $η$ coverage is big. Our study thus provides valuable information to understand the vector meson spin alignment signals observed in the experiments.

# The effect of hadronic scatterings on the measurement of vector meson spin alignments in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Hadronic scatterings of decay daughters cause the observed spin alignment matrix element ρ₀₀ to deviate from the true value for ρ and K* mesons (with a bigger effect on ρ), while the effect is negligible for φ mesons; the deviation depends on the kinematic acceptance, with the observed ρ₀₀ lower than the true value when the pseudorapidity (η) coverage is small but little effect when the η coverage is large.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV with impact parameter b = 8 fm. They injected known input ρ₀₀ values (0.433, 0.333, and 0.183) by redistributing decay products according to the angular distribution formula, then compared the extracted ρ₀₀ before and after the hadronic cascade, applying a selection procedure that removes vector mesons whose decay daughters experienced momentum changes > 0.01 GeV/c due to elastic scatterings.

## Key numbers

- The input ρ₀₀ values used were 0.433, 0.333, and 0.183 for each vector meson species (φ, K*, ρ) in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.
- The impact parameter was set to b = 8 fm to mimic semi-central collisions.
- The global cutoff time for the hadron cascade in the AMPT model was set to 60 fm/c.
- The momentum change threshold for removing a resonance with scattered decay daughters was 0.01 GeV/c.
- Three pT intervals were studied: full pT range, 0.4 < pT < 1 GeV/c, and pT > 1 GeV/c.
- The η acceptance range studied was ηmax from 0.4 to 2.0.

## Context

This work addresses the open puzzle from ALICE measurements (Acharya 2019) showing that K* mesons have ρ₀₀ significantly below 1/3 at low pT in mid-central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, while φ mesons show ρ₀₀ much closer to 1/3, a difference not yet understood. The study extends prior work by Lan (2018) on acceptance effects and Sheng (2019) on strangeness currents, and provides a quantitative hadronic-scattering explanation for the species-dependent spin alignment signals. It enables future experimental measurements of ρ meson spin alignment, which the authors call for, and suggests that hadronic effects must be accounted for when interpreting vector meson spin alignment as a signal of global polarization or vorticity.
