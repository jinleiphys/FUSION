# Chiral Symmetry of Heavy-Light-Quark Hadrons in Hot/Dense Matter

> The recent discoveries by the BaBar and CLEO II collaborations on the splitting between $D_s$ and ${\tilde D}_s$ which exhibited surprises in the structure of heavy-light-quark systems are connected -- via the Harada-Yamawaki "vector manifestation" of hidden local symmetry -- to chiral symmetry restoration expected to take place at some critical temperature $T_c$ in heavy-ion collisions or at some critical density $n_c$ in the deep interior of compact stars, the main theme of this symposium. This unexpected connection exemplifies the diversity of astro-hadronic phenomena discussed in this meeting.

## Key claim

The mass splitting between chiral-doubled heavy-light mesons (e.g., the \(D_s\) and \(\tilde{D}_s\) states observed by BaBar and CLEO II) is proportional to the light-quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle\), and thus the splitting should vanish as the chiral phase transition point (critical temperature \(T_c\) or density \(n_c\)) is approached in the chiral limit. This connection is derived by assuming the "vector manifestation" (VM) fixed point of hidden local symmetry at chiral restoration, rather than from a bottom-up approach starting from zero temperature/density.

## Method

The author uses a "top-down" approach: starting from the vector manifestation (VM) fixed point of hidden local symmetry (HLS) at the chiral transition point, where the gauge coupling \(g\) goes to zero and the ratio \(a = F_\sigma^2/F_\pi^2\) goes to 1, the Lagrangian for heavy-light mesons is constructed. The mass splitting \(\Delta M\) between the chiral-doubled states is then obtained by matching the effective field theory correlators to QCD operator product expansion (OPE) correlators at a matching scale \(\Lambda \sim 1.1\) GeV, yielding a bare splitting proportional to \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle / F_D^2\). Quantum corrections from \(\rho\)-meson loops are then computed via a renormalization-group equation to evolve the splitting down to the physical scale.

## Key numbers

- The bare mass splitting at the matching scale is \(\Delta M_{\rm bare} \simeq 0.23\) GeV, obtained using \(F_D \simeq 0.205\) GeV and \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.243\ \text{GeV})^3\).
- The quantum correction factor is \(C_{\rm quantum} \approx 1.6\), giving a physical splitting \(\Delta M \simeq 0.37\) GeV, using \(\mu = m_\rho = 771\) MeV, \(\Lambda = 1.1\) GeV, \(g(m_\rho) = 6.27\), and \(k \simeq 0.59\).
- The second Casimir for three flavors is \(\mathcal{C}_2(N_f=3) = 4/3\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical prediction of chiral doubling in heavy-light mesons by Nowak, Rho, and Zahed (1993) and by Bardeen and Hill (1994), and connects it to the Harada-Yamawaki "vector manifestation" of hidden local symmetry (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003). The result provides a direct link between the observed \(D_s\)–\(\tilde{D}_s\) splitting and the light-quark condensate, suggesting that measuring the splitting in hot or dense matter could map out the chiral phase structure of QCD. It also implies that the \(\rho\) meson mass should go to zero at the chiral transition, contrary to the linear sigma model prediction, and opens the possibility of studying chiral symmetry restoration in heavy-light baryons and pentaquarks.
