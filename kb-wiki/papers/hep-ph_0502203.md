# QCD Predictions for Charm and Bottom Production at RHIC

> We make up-to-date QCD predictions for open charm and bottom production at RHIC in nucleon-nucleon collisions at \sqrt{S} = 200 GeV. We also calculate the electron spectrum resulting from heavy flavor decays to allow direct comparison to the data. A rigorous benchmark, including the theoretical uncertainties, is established against which nuclear collision data can be compared to obtain evidence for nuclear effects.

# QCD Predictions for Charm and Bottom Production at RHIC

## Key claim
The authors provide up-to-date QCD predictions for open charm and bottom production at RHIC in nucleon-nucleon collisions at sqrt(S) = 200 GeV, establishing a rigorous benchmark with theoretical uncertainties against which nuclear collision data can be compared. The predictions are presented as an uncertainty band estimated to have roughly 80-90% probability of containing the true theoretical prediction, rather than as a single central value.

## Method
The authors calculate heavy quark transverse momentum and rapidity distributions using the Fixed-Order plus Next-to-Leading-Log (FONLL) approach, which includes full fixed-order NLO QCD results and resums large perturbative terms proportional to alpha_s^n log^k(pT/m) to all orders with next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy. Heavy quarks are fragmented into hadrons using phenomenological functions extracted from e+e- data, and the resulting hadron decay into electrons is modeled using experimentally measured decay spectra and branching ratios from BaBar and CLEO. Theoretical uncertainties from heavy quark mass variations (m_c = 1.3-1.7 GeV, m_b = 4.5-5.0 GeV) and factorization/renormalization scale variations (seven combinations of xi_R, xi_F between 0.5 and 2) are added in quadrature to produce an uncertainty band.

## Key numbers
- Total c-cbar cross section at FONLL: 256 (+400, -146) microbarns at sqrt(S) = 200 GeV in pp collisions
- Total b-bbar cross section at FONLL: 1.87 (+0.99, -0.67) microbarns at sqrt(S) = 200 GeV in pp collisions
- Reference charm quark mass: m_c = 1.5 GeV, varied over 1.3-1.7 GeV
- Reference bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.75 GeV, varied over 4.5-5.0 GeV
- QCD scale Lambda^(5) = 0.226 GeV from CTEQ6M parton densities
- Central scale choice: mu_R = mu_F = mu_0 = sqrt(pT^2 + m^2)
- Branching ratios used: BR(B -> e) = 10.86%, BR(D -> e) = 10.3%, BR(B -> D -> e) = 9.6%
- Charm fragmentation parameter r = 0.1 for m_c = 1.5 GeV, r = 0.135 for m_c = 1.7 GeV, r = 0.06 for m_c = 1.3 GeV
- Bottom fragmentation parameter alpha = 29.1 for m_b = 4.75 GeV, alpha = 34 for m_b = 5 GeV, alpha = 25.6 for m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Electron spectrum from B decays exceeds that from D decays at pT approximately 4 GeV
- D meson and charm quark distributions begin to differ outside uncertainty bands only for pT > 9 GeV

## Context
This work extends the FONLL framework previously applied to p-pbar collisions (Cacciari, 1998; Nason, 1987; Beenakker, 1990) to RHIC energies, providing benchmark calculations for interpreting heavy flavor data from PHENIX and STAR collaborations. The predictions enable direct comparison to electron spectra and D meson distributions without experimental deconvolution to bare quark level, which the authors argue risks biasing data. The results can be used to search for nuclear effects in heavy ion collisions such as energy loss (Djordjevic, 2004; Armesto, 2003; Dokshitzer, 2001), transverse momentum broadening (Vogt, 2001), collective flow (Greco, 2003; Lin, 2003), and J/psi regeneration from open charm (Thews, 2000; Andronic, 2003; Kostyuk, 2003).
