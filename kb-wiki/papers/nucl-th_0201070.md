# Mechanical and chemical spinodal instabilities in finite quantum systems

> Self consistent quantum approaches are used to study the instabilities of finite nuclear systems. The frequencies of multipole density fluctuations are determined as a function of dilution and temperature, for several isotopes. The spinodal region of the phase diagrams is determined and it appears that instabilities are reduced by finite size effects. The role of surface and volume instabilities is discussed. It is indicated that the important chemical effects associated with mechanical disruption may lead to isospin fractionation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spinodal instabilities in finite nuclei are reduced compared to infinite nuclear matter, with the first unstable mode being the low-lying octupole vibration, a genuine quantum effect; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of Skyrme interaction (SLy4) and the scaling method used to prepare diluted hot systems.

## Method

The authors linearize the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation around a constrained Hartree-Fock state to obtain random-phase approximation (RPA) modes and their frequencies. They prepare hot, diluted nuclear states by applying a self-similar radial scaling to ground-state Hartree-Fock wavefunctions and then solving the RPA equations in a discrete two-quasiparticle basis. Calculations are performed for Ca and Sn isotopes using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction.

## Key numbers

- Fastest growth time for $^{40}$Ca: $\tau = 28$ fm/c for the $L=2$ mode at dilution $\alpha = 1.8$.
- Limiting temperature for instability: approximately 6 MeV for Sn isotopes and 4.5 MeV for Ca isotopes, compared to about 16 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter.
- In $^{120}$Sn, the octupole mode becomes unstable at dilution $\alpha \approx 1.1$.
- In $^{40}$Ca, the octupole mode becomes unstable around $\alpha = 1.2$.
- Instability growth time contours are drawn at $\tau = 100$ fm/c (dashed line) and $\tau = 50$ fm/c (dots) in the phase diagrams.

## Context

This work extends earlier semi-classical and hydrodynamical studies of spinodal instabilities in finite systems (Bertsch and Siemens 1983; Heiselberg, Pethick, and Ravenhall 1988; Colonna, Chomaz, and Randrup 1994; Jacquot, Ayik, Chomaz, and Colonna 1996) by providing a fully quantal RPA treatment. It also builds on earlier quantum investigations (Ayik, Colonna, and Chomaz 1995; Jacquot, Colonna, Ayik, and Chomaz 1997). The results enable future dynamical calculations of multifragmentation that incorporate the identified dominant unstable modes, and they connect to isospin fractionation phenomena studied in asymmetric nuclear matter (Li and Ko 1997; Baran, Colonna, Di Toro, and Greco 2001).
