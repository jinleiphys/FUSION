# Dilepton Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

> Starting from a realistic one--boson--exchange--model fitted to the amplitudes of elastic nucleon--nucleon scattering and the process $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ we perform a fully relativistic and gauge invariant calculation for the dilepton production in nucleon--nucleon collisions, including the important effect of propagating the $Δ$--resonance. We compare the results of our calculations with the latest experimental data on dilepton production. We also show how to implement various electromagnetic formfactors for the hadrons in our calculations without loosing gauge--invariance and discuss their influence on dilepton spectra.

# Dilepton Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

## Key claim

The one-boson-exchange model, fitted to elastic NN scattering and NN→NΔ data up to 3 GeV, can describe dilepton production in elementary NN collisions, but the agreement with p+Be data at 1 GeV is poor (the dip around 150 MeV invariant mass is not reproduced), and the cross section at high invariant mass is extremely sensitive to the choice of electromagnetic formfactors for the nucleon and Δ, with the strict vector-meson-dominance formfactor producing a shoulder-like structure not seen in data.

## Method

The authors construct a fully relativistic, gauge-invariant calculation of dilepton production in NN collisions using an effective one-boson-exchange (OBE) interaction with σ, ω, ρ, and π mesons, where coupling constants are made energy-dependent to fit elastic NN scattering data from 800 MeV to 3 GeV. The Δ resonance is included via NN→NΔ amplitudes fitted to data at 0.97, 1.48, and 2.02 GeV, with a momentum-dependent width; dilepton production is computed from nucleon bremsstrahlung, Δ decay, and charged-meson internal radiation, and gauge invariance is maintained when inserting electromagnetic formfactors by using the Ward-Takahashi identity to determine the required off-shell vertex terms.

## Key numbers

- The NΔπ coupling constant is determined from Δ→Nπ decay to be f_{NΔπ}=2.13.
- The NΔγ coupling constant is obtained from the Δ radiative decay branch (0.6%) as f_{NΔγ}=0.32.
- The Δ free width Γ₀ is 0.12 GeV, with κ=0.16 GeV in the momentum-dependent width formula.
- Fitted cutoff parameters for the Δ strong formfactors: Λ*_π = 1.421 GeV, Λ*_ρ = 2.273 GeV; the NΔρ coupling constant is f_{NΔρ}=7.4.
- The pn dilepton yield is larger than the pp yield by about a factor of 4 at 1 GeV and a factor of 2.5 at 2.1 GeV (both at invariant mass M=0.2 GeV).
- The pd/pp ratio of dilepton yields is calculated for lab energies from 1.03 GeV up to 4.9 GeV and reproduces the experimental trend, including the rise with M toward the kinematic limit at lower energies.
- Without any electromagnetic formfactor, the calculation underestimates the p+Be data around M=600 MeV by about a factor of 3 at 2.1 GeV; using the formfactor model of Brown (1986) overestimates the data drastically in the 600–700 MeV region.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Schäfer et al. (1991) for real photon production and by Haglin et al. (1989, 1991) for dileptons, which neglected Δ-resonance contributions and, in Haglin (1991), considered only back-to-back dilepton pairs. The present calculation includes both Δ propagation and radiation from neutron lines (via the anomalous magnetic moment), which were absent in those prior studies. The results enable future extraction of the nucleon electromagnetic formfactor in the timelike region around vector-meson masses (0 ≤ M ≤ 2m_N), a region otherwise inaccessible in on-shell experiments, and motivate precise measurements of the elementary pp→ppe⁺e⁻ process to disentangle the various contributions.
