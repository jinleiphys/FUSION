# Nonextensive effects on the phase structure of QHD

> We investigate nonextensive effects on phase transition in nuclear matter in the context Walecka many-body field theory. Changes in the general behavior are observed when the results calculated for the nuclear matter at vanishing baryon density are compared to those obtained through the standard Fermi-Dirac distribution. It is observed a dependence between the nonextensive parameter $q$ and the coupling constants $C_S^2$ of the phase transition. A numerical relation for this thermodynamical dependence is also proposed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the order of the phase transition in nuclear matter at zero baryon density within the Walecka mean-field theory depends not only on the coupling constant \(C_S^2\) but also on the nonextensive parameter \(q\) from Tsallis statistics; a specific numerical relation between \(q\) and \(C_S^2\) is proposed that separates first-order, second-order, and continuous transitions.

## Method

The authors replace the standard Fermi-Dirac distribution with the \(q\)-generalized quantum distribution for fermions (for \(q>1\)) in the mean-field equations of quantum hadrodynamics (QHD). They numerically solve the self-consistency equation for the effective nucleon mass \(M^*\) at vanishing baryon density and chemical potential, for a fixed coupling constant \(C_S^2 = 365\) and for the standard Walecka coupling constants \((g_\sigma/m_\sigma)^2 = 11.798\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) and \((g_\omega/m_\omega)^2 = 8.653\ \mathrm{fm}^2\). By analyzing the specific heat and the singularities of \(dM^*/dT\), they determine for each \(C_S^2\) the critical value of \(q\) at which the phase transition changes from first to second order.

## Key numbers

- The standard Walecka coupling constants used are \((g_\sigma/m_\sigma)^2 = 11.798\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) and \((g_\omega/m_\omega)^2 = 8.653\ \mathrm{fm}^2\), which give a binding energy \(E_{\mathrm{bind}} = -15.75\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.42\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- The example coupling constant \(C_S^2 = 365\) is used for the detailed analysis.
- For \(q=1\) (standard Fermi-Dirac), a first-order phase transition occurs around \(T \sim 185\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The critical value \(q = 1.0218\) is found to produce a second-order phase transition for the chosen \(C_S^2\).
- The convergence of all integrals requires \(1 < q < 5/4\) (i.e., \(q < 1.25\)).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of phase transitions in nuclear matter within the Walecka mean-field theory, previously studied by Theis et al. (1983) for the standard Fermi-Dirac case, and by Pereira et al. (2007) for nonextensive effects. It shows that the order of the phase transition can be tuned by the nonextensive parameter \(q\), enabling a mapping between \(q\) and the coupling constant \(C_S^2\) that separates first-order, second-order, and continuous thermodynamic behaviors. This provides a framework for interpreting possible nonextensive signatures in heavy-ion collision experiments or protoneutron star interiors.
