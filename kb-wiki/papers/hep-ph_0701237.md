# The sum rule for the polarized structure function $g_2$ corresponding to the moment at $n=0$

> In the small $Q^2$ region, the sum rule for the polarized structure function $g_2$ corresponding to the moment at $n=0$ is derived. This sum rule shows that there is a tight connection among the resonances,the elastic and the continuum in the $g_2$. Further, the Born term contribution in this sum rule is proportional to $Q^2$ and very small compared with that in the corresponding sum rule for the polarized structure function $g_1$. However, the Born term contribution divided by $Q^2/2$ which also appears in the Schwinger sum rule for the $g_2$ corresponding to the moment at $n=1$ has a very similar behavior with that in the sum rule for the $g_1$ corresponding to the moment at $n=0$.

## Key claim

In the small $Q^2$ region, the sum rule for the polarized structure function $g_2$ corresponding to the moment at $n=0$ is derived, showing a tight connection among resonances, the elastic contribution, and the continuum in $g_2$; the Born term contribution is proportional to $Q^2$ and very small compared with that in the corresponding sum rule for $g_1$, but when divided by $Q^2/2$ it behaves very similarly to the Born term in the $g_1$ sum rule at $n=0$.

## Method

The authors start from fixed-mass sum rules based on canonical quantization on the null-plane (Dicus, Jackiw, and Teplitz, 1971) and from current anti-commutation relations on the null-plane (Koretune, 2005). They differentiate the relation between the virtual-photon cross-section difference and the structure functions with respect to $Q^2$, take the limit $Q^2 \to 0$, and then separate the Born term contribution. The integral over the continuum is cut off at a laboratory-frame energy $E_c = 2$ GeV, and the sum rule is regularized by subtracting the high-energy behavior. The sum rule is applied both to the isovector-photon combination (current commutator case) and to the electroproduction case (current anti-commutator case) for a proton target.

## Key numbers

* The Born term contribution for the isovector-photon case is $B_2^{+-}(Q^2) = \frac{Q^2}{16m_p^2}\frac{1}{1+Q^2/(4m_p^2)} G_M^+(Q^2)(G_M^+(Q^2)-G_E^+(Q^2))$, where $G_E^p(0)=1$ and $G_M^p(0)=\mu_p=2.793$.
* The Born term contribution for the electroproduction case is $B_2^{ep}(Q^2) = \frac{Q^2}{8m_p^2}\frac{1}{1+Q^2/(4m_p^2)} G_M^p(Q^2)(G_M^p(Q^2)-G_E^p(Q^2))$.
* The combined Born term for $g_1^{ep}+g_2^{ep}$ at the moment $n=0$ is $B_1^{ep}(Q^2)+B_2^{ep}(Q^2) = \frac{1}{2}(\mu_p - G_M^p(Q^2)G_E^p(Q^2))$.
* The cutoff energy for the continuum integral is $E_c = 2$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach previously applied to $g_1$ by Koretune (2005, 2006) to the polarized structure function $g_2$ at low $Q^2$. It shows that the $g_2$ sum rule at moment $n=0$ has a structure analogous to the $g_1$ sum rule, with the Born term proportional to $Q^2$ and thus very small. The result is connected to the Schwinger sum rule (Schwinger, 1975) for $g_2$ at moment $n=1$ and to the Burkhart-Cottingham sum rule (Burkhart and Cottingham, 1970). The similarity between the $g_2$ Born term divided by $Q^2/2$ and the $g_1$ Born term may relate to the sign change of the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum (Soffer and Teryaev, 1993), but the authors state that no direct relation between $g_1$ and $g_2$ is established. The work enables further study of the interplay between elastic, resonance, and continuum contributions in polarized structure functions at low momentum transfer.
