# Two-point functions from chiral kinetic theory in magnetized plasma

> We study the two-point functions from chiral kinetic theory which characterize the response to perturbative vector and axial gauge fields in magnetized chiral plasma. In the lowest Landau level approximation, the solution of chiral kinetic equations gives density waves of electric and axial charges, which contain chiral magnetic wave implied by the axial anomaly and magnetic field. We then obtain the constitutive relations for covariant currents and stress tensor that involving the density waves. By considering the difference between consistent and covariant anomalies explicitly, the correlators of consistent currents and stress tensor satisfy derivative symmetry, and therefore allow an effective action for the perturbative gauge fields as the generating functional of the correlators. We also verify the derivative symmetry of the correlators agrees with the Onsager relations.

## Key claim

The paper derives two-point correlation functions for vector and axial currents and the stress tensor in a strongly magnetized chiral plasma using chiral kinetic theory in the lowest Landau level approximation, showing that the resulting consistent correlators satisfy derivative symmetry and Onsager relations, but only after explicitly accounting for the difference between consistent and covariant anomalies via Chern-Simons currents.

## Method

The author solves the Lorentz-covariant chiral kinetic equations in the lowest Landau level approximation and collisionless limit, expanding perturbatively in vector and axial gauge fields to first order in the fields and their gradients. The solution yields constitutive relations for covariant currents and stress tensor that incorporate density waves (including the chiral magnetic wave), from which the two-point correlation functions are obtained by functional differentiation with respect to the gauge fields.

## Key numbers

- Landau-level degeneracy factor: \( \frac{B}{2\pi} \) (appears throughout as prefactor in currents and correlators)
- Covariant anomaly divergence: \( \partial_\mu J^\mu = -\frac{1}{8\pi^2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\lambda}F_{\mu\nu}F_{\rho\lambda}^5 \) and \( \partial_\mu J_5^\mu = -\frac{1}{16\pi^2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\lambda}(F_{\mu\nu}F_{\rho\lambda}+F_{\mu\nu}^5F_{\rho\lambda}^5) \)
- Consistent anomaly divergence: \( \partial_\mu \mathcal{J}_5^\mu = -\frac{1}{48\pi^2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\lambda}(3F_{\mu\nu}F_{\rho\lambda}+F_{\mu\nu}^5F_{\rho\lambda}^5) \)
- Chern-Simons current relation: \( \mathcal{J}^\mu - \frac{1}{4\pi^2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\lambda}A_\nu^5 F_{\rho\lambda} = J^\mu \)
- Chiral magnetic wave dispersion: poles at \( q_0 \pm q_3 = 0 \) (gapless, propagating at speed of light in strong field limit)
- Background currents: \( J_{(0)}^\mu = \frac{\mu B}{2\pi^2}u^\mu + \frac{\mu_5 B}{2\pi^2}b^\mu \), \( J_{(0)5}^\mu = \frac{\mu_5 B}{2\pi^2}u^\mu + \frac{\mu B}{2\pi^2}b^\mu \)

## Context

This work extends chiral kinetic theory approaches to magnetized plasmas (Gao 2020, Lin 2021) by incorporating perturbative vector and axial gauge fields to compute two-point correlation functions, going beyond previous studies of photon self-energy and magneto-vortical effects. The results agree with field-theoretic calculations of current correlators in magnetized plasma (PhysRevD.83.111501) and provide an effective action for perturbative gauge fields analogous to the Heisenberg-Euler effective Lagrangian for vacuum polarization. The work enables further study of transport phenomena in strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma, particularly the chiral magnetic wave and anomalous transport coefficients.
