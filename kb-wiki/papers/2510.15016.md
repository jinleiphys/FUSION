# Adiabatic hydrodynamization and quasinormal modes of nonthermal attractors

> Nonthermal attractors govern the emergent self-similar dynamics of far-from-equilibrium quantum systems, from ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions to cold-atom experiments. Within the framework of adiabatic hydrodynamization, the approach to a nonthermal attractor is described by the decay of excited states of an effective Hamiltonian. Using an exactly solvable kinetic theory -- the longitudinally expanding, overoccupied gluon plasma dominated by small-angle elastic scattering -- we establish a direct correspondence between the eigenmodes of adiabatic hydrodynamization and the quasinormal mode spectrum of the nonthermal attractor. This equivalence suggests a general framework for identifying universal dynamical structures in nonequilibrium systems. As a byproduct, we derive analytic prescaling solutions for strongly longitudinally expanding systems.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a direct correspondence between the adiabatic hydrodynamization framework and the quasinormal mode spectrum for nonthermal attractors, specifically in the context of a longitudinally expanding, overoccupied gluon plasma described by an exactly solvable Fokker-Planck kinetic theory; this equivalence is demonstrated for the case where small-angle elastic scattering dominates, and the authors suggest it may generalize to a broader class of nonequilibrium systems.

## Method

The authors analyze the Fokker-Planck-type Boltzmann equation for a longitudinally expanding gluon plasma, which is exactly solvable through the adiabatic hydrodynamization framework introduced by Brewer et al. (2022). They derive analytic prescaling solutions by imposing a generalized self-similar ansatz and solving the resulting ordinary differential equations via separation of variables. The quasinormal modes are then obtained by linearizing the Boltzmann equation around the nonthermal fixed point solution in the prescaling frame, yielding an eigenvalue problem for the perturbation amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponents for the nonthermal fixed point are \((\alpha,\beta,\gamma) = (-2/3,0,1/3)\), matching the canonical "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2000)
- The amplitude rescaling function takes the form \(\bar{A}(\tau) = \left(\frac{\tau^2 \pm \tau_\ast^2}{\tau_{\rm ref}^2}\right)^{-1/3}\), which for \(\tau_\ast=0\) reduces to the BMSS scaling expression \((\tau/\tau_{\rm ref})^{-2/3}\)
- The quasinormal mode amplitudes decay as \(\bar{A}(\tau)^\Omega\), where \(\Omega\) is the QNM frequency determined by the eigenvalue equation
- The parameter \(\kappa\) relating the Gaussian width of the scaling function to the reference time is given by \(\kappa = \frac{9 \lambda_0}{2^8\pi^5} \bar{I}_{\chi}^2 \tau_{\text{ref}}^2\)

## Context

This work extends the quasinormal mode analysis of nonthermal fixed points, previously developed for isotropic, non-expanding systems by De Lescluze et al. (2025), to the case of strongly longitudinally expanding systems relevant for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. It connects the adiabatic hydrodynamization framework of Brewer et al. (2022) with the quasinormal mode approach, showing that the eigenmodes in the adiabatic picture correspond to the QNM spectrum of the nonthermal attractor. The analytic prescaling solutions derived here generalize the prescaling phenomenon observed in QCD effective kinetic theory simulations by Mazeliauskas and collaborators (2018) and provide a foundation for understanding universal dynamical structures in far-from-equilibrium quantum systems.
