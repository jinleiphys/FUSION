# Alpha-Particle Condensate in Nuclear Matter at Normal Density and Statistics of Composite Bosons

> It is proved that alpha-particle states are well described by the Elliott SU(3) model. This result is used to analyze the alpha-particle condensation effect. It is shown that these states possess the basic attributes of the alpha-condensate and, also, the normal nuclear density on frequent occasions. The statistics of alpha-particles (and of arbitrary composite bosons) turns out to be something other than the Bose-Einstein, Fermi-Dirac statistics, and parastatistics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that alpha-particle states in N=Z even nuclei are well described by the Elliott SU(3) model and that these states possess the basic attributes of an alpha-particle condensate even at normal nuclear density, but the rigorous definition of an alpha-particle condensate (all alpha-particles in the (0s) level) is forbidden by the Pauli principle for A>4, so the statistics of composite bosons like alpha-particles is neither Bose-Einstein, Fermi-Dirac, nor parastatistics.

## Method

The authors use the microscopic SU(3) Hamiltonian (generalized with Q^3 and QL^2 terms) to describe A-nucleon wave functions of N=Z even nuclei, and show that these eigenfunctions can be equivalently expressed in shell-model form and in multicluster form as antisymmetrized products of alpha-particle wave functions. They analyze the selection rules for the principal quantum number N, Elliott symbols (λμ), and angular momentum L to determine which multi-alpha-particle configurations survive antisymmetrization, and derive the allowed occupation numbers of alpha-particles at oscillator levels.

## Key numbers

- For the ground state of ⁴⁰Ca, ten alpha-particles can occupy the N_i=6 level (SU(3) α-configuration (60)¹⁰).
- For the ground state band of ⁸Be, the configuration is (00)¹(40)¹ with N_i=2 for the second alpha-particle.
- For the ground state band of ¹²C, the configuration is (00)¹(40)².
- The root-mean-square radius of the wave function (Sch) for the 11.26 MeV state in ¹⁶O is √⟨r²⟩ = 3.12 fm.
- The spectroscopic factor W_α = 0.64 for the N=20, (λμ)=(84), L=0 state in ¹⁶O, with excitation energy E* = 11.4 MeV.
- The width Γ_α = 4.8 MeV for the 14.0 MeV level in ¹⁶O is considered too large for a low-density condensed state; a more likely candidate is the 14.03 MeV level with Γ_α = 185 keV.
- The allowed number of alpha-particles k at a level with quantum number N_i satisfies k ≤ N_{i^{mag}}^> k_{mag}^> /(4N_{i^{mag}}^> - 3N_i), where N_{i^{mag}}^> and k_{mag}^> are values for the nearest heavier magic nucleus.
- The leading term for the allowed number of alpha-particles at level N_i scales as k ∼ (r/r_α)⁶, where r ≃ r_0 √(N_i+3/2).

## Context

This work extends the alpha-particle condensate concept introduced by Ropke (2000) and Tohsaki et al. (2001) from low-density nuclear matter to states at normal nuclear density, using the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) which describes ground and low-lying states of light N=Z even nuclei. The paper enables a systematic classification of multi-alpha-particle states across many nuclei and shows that the statistics of composite bosons (including mesons and atoms with even neutron number) differs fundamentally from Bose-Einstein statistics, with implications for searching for condensate-like states in nuclear systems and other small dense fermionic systems.
