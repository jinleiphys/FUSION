# Properties of Neutron Stars with Hyperons within a Relativistic Metamodel

> In this work, we study the effects of $Λ$-hyperons on neutron star properties employing a metamodel framework for the equation of state (EoS). Different choices for defining the hyperonic couplings with different levels of parametric freedom are discussed. In all models, the predicted NS maximum masses are reduced compared with the purely nucleonic composition as expected. In the case of relating hyperonic couplings via $SU(6)$-symmetry arguments to the nucleonic ones, we find that NS radii for intermediate mass stars are shifted to higher values compared with purely nucleonic stars, in agreement with the existing literature. However, allowing for more freedom for the hyperonic couplings, the effect is strongly reduced, and the distributions in the NS mass-radius plane of models with and without hyperons become very close. We have also investigated how different nucleonic density functionals influence the hyperon matter composition and neutron star properties.

## Key claim
The paper shows that when hyperonic couplings are fixed via SU(6) symmetry relations, neutron star radii for intermediate-mass stars shift to higher values compared with purely nucleonic stars; however, when the hyperonic couplings are allowed more freedom (the "Ratio" setting), this effect is strongly reduced and the mass-radius distributions of models with and without hyperons become very close.

## Method
The authors employ a relativistic mean-field metamodel with density-dependent couplings (GDFM type) to describe nucleonic and Λ-hyperonic matter in neutron stars. They generate two ensembles of equations of state: one where hyperonic vector couplings follow SU(6) symmetry relations from the valence quark model, and another ("Ratio") where the ratios of hyperonic to nucleonic couplings (R_ωΛ, R_ϕΛ, R_σΛ, R_σ*Λ) are randomly varied within specified ranges. A Bayesian analysis is performed using priors from chiral effective field theory calculations and nuclear mass tables, with posteriors constrained by pulsar mass measurements, tidal deformability from GW170817, and the Λ-hyperon potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation (Gaussian likelihood with mean -30 MeV and standard deviation 5 MeV).

## Key numbers
- The Λ-hyperon potential at saturation density is constrained to U_Λ^N(n_sat) ≈ -30 MeV, with a Gaussian likelihood of N(-30, 5) MeV applied in the Bayesian analysis.
- The most probable models show Λ-hyperon onset densities of n_Λ^onset = 0.395 fm^{-3} (SU(6) case) and 0.419 fm^{-3} (Ratio case).
- The most probable models have saturation densities n_sat = 0.1674 fm^{-3} (SU(6)) and 0.1695 fm^{-3} (Ratio), with symmetry energies E_sym = 29.2 MeV (SU(6)) and 30.6 MeV (Ratio).
- The maximum mass constraint from pulsar observations (PSR J0740+6620 at 2.08 ± 0.07 M_⊙) is used as a filter; both settings can produce maximum masses above 2.0 M_⊙.
- The posterior distributions for radii at masses M ≲ 1.4 M_⊙ are almost overlapping between the two settings; differences appear around 1.6 M_⊙ and above.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the "hyperon puzzle" (the difficulty of reconciling 2 M_⊙ pulsar masses with hyperon-softened equations of state) by systematically exploring the parameter space of hyperonic couplings beyond the restrictive SU(6) symmetry assumption. It builds on prior work by Malik (2022) and Traversi (2020) that found stiffer nucleonic equations of state are favored with hyperons, and by Huang (2024) that emphasized the importance of NICER measurements of PSR J0437-4715 favoring lower radii for canonical 1.4 M_⊙ neutron stars. The metamodel approach enables a more complete exploration of the parameter space than earlier studies that considered only limited nucleonic models or made strong assumptions about hyperon couplings. The results enable future comparisons with third-generation gravitational wave interferometer observations.
