# Neutrino-Nucleus scattering in the SuSA model

> The Super-Scaling Approach (SuSA) model, based on the analogies between electron and neutrino interactions with nuclei, is reviewed and its application to the description of neutrino-nucleus scattering is presented. The contribution of both one- and two-body relativistic currents is considered. A selection of results is presented where theoretical predictions are compared with cross section measurements from the main ongoing neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

The SuSA (Super-Scaling Approach) model, originally phenomenological and based on universal scaling functions extracted from electron scattering data, has been refined into SuSAv2 by incorporating relativistic mean field (RMF) effects at low-to-intermediate momentum transfer and Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA) at high momentum transfer, connected by a q-dependent blending function, thereby providing a description of neutrino-nucleus scattering that is validated against electron data but still requires further development for the full inelastic spectrum in the weak sector.

## Method

The SuSAv2 model constructs nuclear response functions for charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering by using a scaling function that depends on a single superscaling variable psi-prime, derived from the analysis of inclusive electron scattering data on nuclei. The model separates the quasielastic (1p1h) contribution, computed from the scaling function with relativistic mean field inputs for different spin and isospin channels, from the two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) contribution induced by weak meson-exchange currents, which is computed via a seven-dimensional numerical integration over the relativistic Fermi gas and then parametrized for use in Monte Carlo event generators. The inelastic region is treated by extending the scaling variable to account for excited states with invariant mass W, using phenomenological fits of single-nucleon inelastic structure functions from electron-proton and electron-deuteron data.

## Key numbers

- The SuSAv2 model reproduces the experimental longitudinal scaling function extracted from electron scattering data, which shows an asymmetric shape with a long tail extending to high energy transfer (large psi-prime values), in contrast to the symmetric Relativistic Fermi Gas scaling function.
- The 2p2h responses have been parametrized in the kinematic range 100 < q < 2000 MeV/c for implementation in the GENIE Monte Carlo event generator.
- The model fails to reproduce electron scattering data at very low energy and momentum transfers (q < 300 MeV/c and omega < 50 MeV), as do all impulse approximation models.
- The RMF longitudinal scaling functions show a minimum reduced-chi-square at q = 650 MeV/c when compared with electron scattering data from Jourdan.
- The SuSAv2-MEC model provides excellent agreement with inclusive electron-carbon cross section data across the full spectrum, including the dip region between the quasielastic and Delta peaks, where 2p2h excitations are essential.

## Context

This work extends the original SuSA model of Amaro (2004) by incorporating RMF effects from Caballero (2005) and Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014) to describe the different spin and isospin channels needed for weak interactions, and by including two-body currents from meson-exchange diagrams that excite 2p2h states, following the approach of Simo (2016). The SuSAv2 model enables more accurate predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments by providing a consistent framework that can be implemented in Monte Carlo simulations like GENIE, and it has been validated against electron scattering data on carbon, oxygen, argon, and titanium from JLab and other facilities. The model contrasts with the Energy-Dependent RMF approach of Gonzalez-Jimenez, which uses a similar blending function to scale down RMF potentials with increasing nucleon kinetic energy, and with the work of Nieves (2011) and Rocco (2018) on multinucleon knockout contributions.
