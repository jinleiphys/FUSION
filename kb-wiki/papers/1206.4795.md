# PACIAE 2.1: An updated issue of the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 2.0

> We have updated the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 2.0 for relativistic nuclear collisions (cf. Comput. Phys. Comm. 183(2012)333) to the new issue of PACIAE 2.1. The PACIAE model is based on PYTHIA. In the PYTHIA model, once the generated particle/parton transverse momentum $p_T$ is randomly selected, its component of $[p_x,p_y]$ was originally put on the circle with radius $p_T$ randomly. It is now replaced by put on the circumference of ellipse with half major and minor axes of $p_T(1+δ_p)$ and $p_T(1-δ_p)$, respectively, in order to investigate better the transverse momentum anisotropy in final hadronic state.

## Key claim

PACIAE 2.1 modifies the PYTHIA-based parton and hadron cascade model so that the transverse momentum components \(p_x, p_y\) are placed on an ellipse rather than a circle, in order to better investigate final-state transverse momentum anisotropy. This change is motivated by the observation that the original circular placement strongly cancels the final-state asymmetry developed from initial spatial asymmetry and is inconsistent with ATLAS data showing charged-particle transverse sphericity less than unity.

## Method

The authors updated the PACIAE 2.0 code (Sa et al., Comput. Phys. Comm. 183 (2012) 333) by replacing the random placement of \(p_x, p_y\) on a circle of radius \(p_T\) with random placement on the circumference of an ellipse having half-major axis \(p_T(1+\delta_p)\) and half-minor axis \(p_T(1-\delta_p)\). The model remains based on PYTHIA and is written in FORTRAN 77 or GFORTRAN, requiring approximately 1 GB of RAM.

## Key numbers

- Running time for 1000 events of \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV Non Single Diffractive pp collisions: 0.5 minute.
- Running time for 10 events of 10-40% most central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV using 21b.tar.gz: 5 minutes.
- Running time for 10 events of 10-40% most central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV using 21c.tar.gz: 17 minutes.

## Context

This work extends the PACIAE 2.0 model (Sa et al., 2012) by introducing an elliptical transverse momentum distribution to preserve final-state anisotropy, addressing a limitation noted in comparisons with ATLAS observations. The update enables more realistic simulations of anisotropic flow in relativistic nuclear collisions, particularly for studies of initial-state spatial asymmetry effects in heavy-ion experiments at RHIC and LHC energies.
