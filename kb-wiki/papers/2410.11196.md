# Universal Mass Equation for Equal-Quantum Excited-States Sets I

> The masses of fifteen baryon sets and twenty-four meson sets of three or more equal-quantum excited states, using Breit-Wigner PDG masses and their uncertainties at fixed $J^P$ for baryons and $J^{PC}$ for mesons, are fitted by a simple two-parameter logarithmic function, $M_n = αLn(n) + β$, where $n$ is the level of radial excitation. The conjecture is made that accurately measured masses of all equal-quantum baryons (including LHCb exotic $P_{c\bar{c}}^+$s) and meson excited states (including $s\bar{s}$, $s\bar{c}$, $c\bar{c}$, $c\bar{b}$, and $b\bar{b}$ states) are related by the logarithmic function used here; at least for the mass range of currently known excited states. The baryon ``star'' rating case is evaluated. The Cornell potential is an example of how a logarithmic behavior can be explained by an appropriate potential. Thus, a ``universal mass equation'' (UME) for equal-quantum excited-state sets is presented.

## Key claim

The masses of fifteen baryon sets and twenty-four meson sets of three or more equal-quantum excited states can be fitted by a simple two-parameter logarithmic function, M_n = alpha Ln(n) + beta, where n is the level of radial excitation; the authors conjecture this relation holds for all accurately measured equal-quantum baryons and meson excited states, at least for the mass range of currently known excited states, and note they have not found any theory in the literature explaining the logarithmic behavior.

## Method

Breit-Wigner masses and their uncertainties from the Particle Data Group (PDG) listings were used for baryon states at fixed J^P and meson states at fixed J^{PC}. A chi-squared minimization procedure was applied to fit the two-parameter logarithmic function M_n = alpha Ln(n) + beta to each data set of three or more excited states, with n assigned as the radial excitation level. The fit was used to identify missing states and predict masses of up to four higher excited states per set, with uncertainties propagated from the fit parameters.

## Key numbers

- N1/2^+ baryon set (neutron ground state plus five excited states): fit parameters alpha = 698.2 +/- 14.1 MeV, beta = 939.6 +/- 5.4e-7 MeV; chi^2/DoF = 0.6, CL = 68.7%
- N1/2^- baryon set (three known states): fit parameters alpha = 200.7 +/- 16.0 MeV, beta = 1522.7 +/- 13.3 MeV; chi^2/DoF = 1.1, CL = 29.1%
- Delta 3/2^+ baryon set (three known states including Delta(1232)): fit parameters alpha = 495.3 +/- 34.0 MeV, beta = 1232.0 +/- 2.0 MeV; chi^2/DoF = 0.1, CL = 93.6%
- P_c\bar{c}^+ exotic baryon set (four known LHCb states): fit parameters alpha = 90.7 +/- 2.9 MeV, beta = 4312.2 +/- 3.8 MeV; chi^2/DoF = 0.2, CL = 82.5%; predicted missing state at 4411.8 +/- 3.7 MeV
- Sigma 3/2^+ baryon set (five known states): fit parameters alpha = 519.4 +/- 10.6 MeV, beta = 1382.8 +/- 0.3 MeV; chi^2/DoF = 0.6, CL = 61.4%
- N5/2^+ baryon set (three known states): fit parameters alpha = 287.7 +/- 27.1 MeV, beta = 1685.0 +/- 5.0 MeV; chi^2/DoF = 0.002, CL = 96.3%

## Context

This work extends the study of hadron excited-state mass systematics beyond the initial investigation of proton/neutron (N1/2^+) states, which began with the discovery of the Roper resonance (Roper, 1964). The authors apply the logarithmic mass formula to 15 baryon sets and 24 meson sets from the PDG listings (Particle Data Group, 2024), including exotic LHCb P_c\bar{c}^+ pentaquarks (LHCb Collaboration, 2015, 2019). The Cornell potential is cited as an example of how a logarithmic behavior can be explained by an appropriate potential, but the authors state they have not found any existing theory in the literature that explains the logarithmic fits. The work enables predictions of missing and higher excited states for each quantum-number set, which can guide future experimental searches.
