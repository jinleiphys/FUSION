# Fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, protons and charged hadrons

> We present new sets of pion, kaon, proton and inclusive charged hadron fragmentation functions obtained in NLO combined analyses of single-inclusive hadron production in electron-positron annihilation, proton-proton collisions, and deep-inelastic lepton-proton scattering with either particles identified in the final state. At variance with all previous fits, the present analyses take into account data where hadrons of different electrical charge are identified, which allow to discriminate quark from anti-quark fragmentation functions.

# Fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, protons and charged hadrons

## Key claim
The authors present new next-to-leading-order (NLO) fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, protons, and inclusive charged hadrons that, unlike all previous fits, incorporate data with identified electrical charge of final-state hadrons, thereby enabling discrimination between quark and anti-quark fragmentation without relying on non-trivial flavor symmetry assumptions.

## Method
The authors performed a global NLO QCD analysis combining single-inclusive hadron production data from electron-positron annihilation, semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and proton-proton collisions. They adopted a flexible functional form for input fragmentation functions at an initial scale of mu_0 = 1 GeV (or quark masses for heavier partons) and imposed isospin symmetry assumptions for sea quark fragmentation, while allowing charge-separated data to constrain the quark/anti-quark differences.

## Key numbers
- Initial scale for fragmentation function parameterization: mu_0 = 1 GeV for lighter partons, with quark masses used for heavier partons.
- The functional form used for input distributions is: D_i^H(z, mu_0) = N_i z^{alpha_i} (1-z)^{beta_i} [1 + gamma_i (1-z)^{delta_i}].

## Context
This work extends previous fragmentation function extractions such as Bourhis et al., Kretzer, AKK, and Hirai et al., which were based primarily on electron-positron annihilation data and could not separate quark from anti-quark fragmentation. By including SIDIS and proton-proton collision data with charge-identified hadrons, the new fits provide better constraints on gluon fragmentation and large-z behavior, and enable tests of universality and factorization across different processes. The resulting fragmentation functions are available as Fortran routines upon request.
