# Extraction of the static magnetic form factor and the structrue function of the neutron from inclusive scattering on light nuclei

> We show that quasi-elastic inclusive scattering data on light nuclei for medium Q^2 furnish information on $G_M^n$, whereas the deep-inelastic region for large $Q^2$ provides the Structure Function $F_2^n(x,Q^2)$. Common to the two extractions is the possibility to de-convolute medium effects, which is most accurately done for light targets. Results are independent of the target.

# Extraction of the static magnetic form factor and the structure function of the neutron from inclusive scattering on light nuclei

## Key claim

Quasi-elastic inclusive electron scattering data on light nuclei at medium $Q^2$ can provide information on the neutron static magnetic form factor $G_M^n$, while deep-inelastic data at large $Q^2$ can yield the neutron structure function $F_2^n$, provided medium effects are deconvoluted using light targets where this deconvolution is most accurate; the extracted results are claimed to be independent of the target used.

## Method

The author analyzes inclusive electron scattering cross sections on light nuclei (deuterium and helium-4) by separating the nuclear structure functions into quasi-elastic (nucleon elastic) and deep-inelastic (partonic) components. For the quasi-elastic region ($x \gtrsim 1$), the nuclear structure functions are expressed in terms of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors and a computed spectral function $f^{PN,A}$ for point-nucleon nuclei, allowing extraction of $\alpha_n = G_M^n/(\mu_n G_d)$ from data. For the deep-inelastic region ($x \ll 1$), the author inverts the convolution relation between nuclear and nucleon structure functions using a parametrization $F_2^n(x,Q^2) = C(x,Q^2) F_2^p(x,Q^2)$ with $C$ expanded as a polynomial in $(1-x)$, and applies this to data from deuterium, carbon, and iron targets at $Q^2 \approx 3.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- $\alpha_n(Q^2) = G_M^n/(\mu_n G_d)$ extracted from $^4$He data at $Q^2 \approx 0.44$ GeV$^2$: $0.988 \pm 0.055$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from $^4$He at $Q^2 \approx 0.89$ GeV$^2$: $0.967 \pm 0.028$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from $^4$He at $Q^2 \approx 1.30$ GeV$^2$: $0.988 \pm 0.018$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from deuterium at $Q^2 \approx 0.99$ GeV$^2$: $1.039 \pm 0.020$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from deuterium at $Q^2 \approx 1.98$ GeV$^2$: $1.062 \pm 0.009$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from deuterium at $Q^2 \approx 1.75$ GeV$^2$ (Rosenbluth-separated transverse response): $1.004 \pm 0.014$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from deuterium at $Q^2 \approx 2.50$ GeV$^2$ (Rosenbluth-separated): $0.986 \pm 0.014$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from deuterium at $Q^2 \approx 3.25$ GeV$^2$ (Rosenbluth-separated): $0.940 \pm 0.013$
- $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ from deuterium at $Q^2 \approx 4.00$ GeV$^2$ (Rosenbluth-separated): $0.830 \pm 0.016$
- Ratio $C(x=1, Q^2=3.5$ GeV$^2) = F_2^n/F_2^p$ computed from the nucleon elastic limit: approximately $0.61$
- Extracted $C(1)$ from data on D, C, Fe at $Q^2=3.5$ GeV$^2$: approximately $0.54$ (drawn line), with a band reflecting spread across targets and methods

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of $G_M^n$ from deuterium data (Lung et al., 1993) and from helium-4 (Day et al., 1993) by adding new extraction points at intermediate $Q^2$ values and demonstrating target independence across deuterium and $^4$He. For the structure function extraction, the method improves upon the primitive ansatz $F_2^n \approx 2F_2^D - F_2^p$ (which is only reliable for $x \lesssim 0.3$) and upon earlier deuteron-only approaches (Melnitchouk and Thomas, 1996) by developing a formalism applicable to any light target and by using data from D, C, and Fe to test target independence. The results enable future extractions of neutron structure functions from inclusive scattering on light nuclei without requiring a free neutron target.
