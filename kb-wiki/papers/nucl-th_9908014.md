# K-Factors in Parton Cascades at RHIC and SPS

> Different treatments for the inclusion of higher-order perturbative QCD corrections in parton based transport models of relativistic heavy-ion collisions are studied and their influence on experimental observables is investigated. At RHIC, particle multiplicities may vary by more than 30%, depending on the correction scheme. A detailed analysis of the squared relative momentum transfers to be expected in parton (re)scatterings at RHIC casts doubt on the applicability of pQCD based transport approaches at the SPS and rules out the application of higher-order correction schemes based on a rescaling of the relative squared momentum transfers in the running coupling constant at all energies.

# K-Factors in Parton Cascades at RHIC and SPS

## Key claim

The choice of higher-order pQCD correction scheme in parton cascade models can alter predicted particle multiplicities at RHIC by more than 30%, but the specific claim that the Q²-rescaling method is unacceptable rests on the finding that at the average Q² ≈ 8.3 GeV² probed at RHIC, the rescaled coupling constant reaches ⟨α_s⟩ = 0.96, and at SPS energies (⟨Q²⟩ = 1.9 GeV²) it reaches 9.3, placing both regimes well beyond the domain of applicability of perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors compared two implementations of K-factors in the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) code VNI 4.12: a constant multiplicative factor (K = 1.0 to 2.5) and a Q²-rescaling method (η = 0.03 to 0.08) that shifts the scale in the running coupling α_s. They computed Q² distributions and average momentum transfers for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, and calculated the rapidity distribution of negatively charged hadrons to quantify the effect on observables.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, the average squared momentum transfer ⟨Q²⟩ = 8.3 GeV² for central Au+Au collisions, with a cut-off Q₀ = 2.1 GeV.
- At SPS, the average squared momentum transfer ⟨Q²⟩ = 1.9 GeV² for central Pb+Pb collisions, with a cut-off Q₀ = 1.0 GeV.
- Using a constant K-factor of 2.5 increases the h⁻ rapidity-density at mid-rapidity by roughly 50% compared to K=1.0; switching to the Q²-rescaling method increases the multiplicity by an additional 30%.
- The average strong coupling constant with constant K-factor at RHIC is ⟨α_s⟩ = 0.29; with Q²-rescaling it becomes ⟨α_s⟩ = 0.96.
- At SPS, ⟨α_s⟩ = 0.39 with constant K-factor; with Q²-rescaling it becomes ⟨α_s⟩ = 9.3.
- The Q² distribution at RHIC follows a power-law dσ/dQ² ∼ 1/Q⁶.
- The deviation between the two correction methods exceeds 50% below Q² ≈ 100 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model approach of Geiger (1992, 1995) and Wang (1991, 1992) by critically examining the K-factor implementations used in the VNI code (Geiger 1997) and in Pythia (Sjostrand). The findings cast doubt on earlier PCM applications to SPS collisions by Geiger and Srivastava, and motivate a major revision of the VNI code. The results enable future studies of whether hot dense partonic matter introduces a medium-dependence into the K-factor at RHIC.
