# Generalization of the $N_pN_n$ scheme to non-yrast levels of even-even nuclei

> In this paper we present the systematics of excitation energies for even-even nuclei in two regions: the 50$<$Z$\le$66, 82$<$N$\le$104 region, and the 66$<$Z$<$82, 82$<$N$\le$104 region. Using the $N_pN_n$ scheme, we obtain compact trajectories for the ground band as well as quasi-$β$ and quasi-$γ$ bands. This suggests that the $N_pN_n$ scheme is useful even if one extends it to non-yrast levels, and thus can serve as a general tool to disclose new types of structural evolution for higher excitations, besides the yrast states which have been investigated extensively. It is highlighted that deformations in non-yrast quasibands of nuclei with $Z \sim 80$ and $N \sim 104$ are often very different from those in the ground bands.

# Generalization of the $N_pN_n$ scheme to non-yrast levels of even-even nuclei

## Key claim
The $N_pN_n$ scheme, previously applied almost exclusively to yrast bands, produces compact trajectories for quasi-$\beta$ and quasi-$\gamma$ bands in even-even nuclei, suggesting it is a general tool for non-yrast structural evolution. However, the scheme highlights that deformations in non-yrast quasibands of nuclei with $Z \sim 80$ and $N \sim 104$ are often very different from those in the ground bands, indicating state- or band-dependent behavior even at low spin.

## Method
The authors compiled excitation energies for even-even nuclei in two regions (50 < Z ≤ 66, 82 < N ≤ 104 and 66 < Z < 82, 82 < N ≤ 104) using Sakai's quasiband classification. They plotted $E_{2_1^+}$ for ground bands and defined $E'_{3^+}(\gamma_1) = E_{3^+}(\gamma_1) - E_{2^+}(\gamma_1)$ and $E'_{2^+}(\beta_1) = E_{2^+}(\beta_1) - E_{0^+}(\beta_1)$ for quasi-$\gamma$ and quasi-$\beta$ bands, then examined correlations with the $N_pN_n$ product. Effective valence proton numbers were used for the Z=64 subshell region, and nuclei with Z > 76 were distinguished from Z ≤ 76 in the second region.

## Key numbers
- For the region 50 < Z ≤ 66, 82 < N ≤ 104, effective numbers of valence protons were taken from a fit of ground state deformations using the $N_pN_n$ scheme (Zhao, 2003).
- For nuclei with Z = 78 and N = 102, 104 ($^{180}$Pt$_{102}$ and $^{182}$Pt$_{104}$), the $E'_{3^+}(\gamma_1)$ values appear "quite normal" in the $N_pN_n$ plot, in contrast to the anomalous behavior of their ground band $2_1^+$ energies.
- The deformation of $^{184}$Hg$_{104}$ in the $2^+(\beta_1)$ state is suggested to be quite close to that of the ground state, while the deformation of $^{182}$Pt$_{104}$ in the $2^+(\beta_1)$ state is substantially smaller than in the ground state.

## Context
This work extends the $N_pN_n$ scheme developed by Casten (1985) for yrast states to non-yrast levels, using the quasiband classification proposed by Sakai (1967). The paper builds on prior studies of ground-state deformation anomalies for nuclei with Z ~ 78 and N ~ 104 (Zhao, 2003) and demonstrates that the $N_pN_n$ scheme can reveal unexpected structural differences between ground and excited bands. This enables future systematic studies of shape coexistence and band-dependent deformation in transitional and nearly spherical nuclei, particularly for Hg and Pt isotopes where data remain incomplete.
