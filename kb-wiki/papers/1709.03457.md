# Asymptotically Free Theory with Scale Invariant Thermodynamics

> A recently developed variational resummation technique, incorporating renormalization group properties consistently, has been shown to solve the scale dependence problem that plagues the evaluation of thermodynamical quantities, e.g., within the framework of approximations such as in the hard-thermal-loop resummed perturbation theory. This method is used in the present work to evaluate thermodynamical quantities within the two-dimensional nonlinear sigma model, which, apart from providing a technically simpler testing ground, shares some common features with Yang-Mills theories, like asymptotic freedom, trace anomaly and the nonperturbative generation of a mass gap. The present application confirms that nonperturbative results can be readily generated solely by considering the lowest-order (quasi-particle) contribution to the thermodynamic effective potential, when this quantity is required to be renormalization group invariant. We also show that when the next-to-leading correction from the method is accounted for, the results indicate convergence, apart from optimally preserving, within the approximations here considered, the sought-after scale invariance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Renormalization Group Optimized Perturbation Theory (RGOPT) can produce nonperturbative, approximately scale-invariant thermodynamic results for the two-dimensional nonlinear sigma model (NLSM) using only the lowest-order quasiparticle contribution, provided the effective potential is required to be renormalization group invariant; the next-to-leading correction indicates convergence and optimally preserves scale invariance within the approximations considered.

## Method

The authors apply the RGOPT to the two-dimensional O(N) nonlinear sigma model at finite temperature, first modifying the perturbative expansion by rescaling the mass parameter m and coupling g with an exponent a = γ₀/b₀ uniquely fixed by requiring RG invariance. They evaluate the thermodynamic pressure up to two-loop order using dimensional regularization in the MS-bar scheme, then apply the stationary mass optimization prescription (∂P/∂m = 0) simultaneously with the reduced RG relation to determine the variational mass and coupling. For comparison, they also derive standard perturbation theory, screened perturbation theory (SPT)/hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt), and large-N expansion results up to two-loop order.

## Key numbers

* The RG coefficient b₀ = (N-2)/(2π) and γ₀ = (N-3)/(8π) for the NLSM, giving the interpolation exponent a = γ₀/b₀ = (N-3)/[4(N-2)]
* For N=4, the exponent a = 1/16; for N→∞, a → 1/4
* The two-loop RG coefficients are b₁ = (N-2)/(2π)² and γ₁ = (N-2)/(8π²)
* The perturbative subtraction coefficient s₀ = 1 and s₁ = 0, determined from RG consistency at two-loop order

## Context

This work extends the RGOPT method, previously applied to the Gross-Neveu model (Kneur 2010) and the λφ⁴ scalar model (Pinto 2013, 2014), to the two-dimensional NLSM as a simpler testing ground for future thermal QCD applications. The NLSM shares key features with Yang-Mills theories including asymptotic freedom, trace anomaly, and nonperturbative mass gap generation, while allowing exact thermal integrals up to two-loop order. The results are compared with lattice simulations for N=3 (Giacosa 2012) and with the 1/N-expansion (Warringa 2002), and the RGOPT is shown to drastically improve the scale dependence problem that plagues standard HTLpt resummation (Andersen 2002, 2011) and SPT approaches.
