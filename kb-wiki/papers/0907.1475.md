# Spin Sum Rules at Low $Q^2$

> Recent precision spin-structure data from Jefferson Lab have significantly advanced our knowledge of nucleon structure at low $Q^2$. Results on the neutron spin sum rules and polarizabilities in the low to intermediate $Q^2$ region are presented. The Burkhardt-Cuttingham Sum Rule was verified within experimental uncertainties. When comparing with theoretical calculations, results on spin polarizability show surprising disagreements with Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions. Preliminary results on first moments at very low $Q^2$ are also presented.

# Spin Sum Rules at Low $Q^2$

## Key claim

The Burkhardt-Cuttingham Sum Rule was verified within experimental uncertainties for the neutron, but the spin polarizability $\delta_{LT}^n$ shows surprising disagreements with Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions even at the lowest measured $Q^2$ of 0.1 GeV$^2$, presenting a significant challenge to present Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations.

## Method

Neutron spin-structure functions $g_1$ and $g_2$ were extracted from data on a polarized $^3$He target in both longitudinal and transverse directions at Jefferson Lab Hall A. First moments $\Gamma_1^n$ and $\Gamma_2^n$, the extended Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral $I(Q^2)$, and generalized spin polarizabilities $\gamma_0^n$ and $\delta_{LT}^n$ were computed from these data over a range of low to intermediate $Q^2$ values.

## Key numbers

- The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule prediction at $Q^2=0$ is $I(0) = -232.8\ \mu$b.
- The lowest $Q^2$ settings for $\Gamma_1^n$ measurements were 0.04 to 0.1 GeV$^2$, where Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory and Relativistic Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions show reasonable agreement with data.
- The $\delta_{LT}^n$ polarizability data at $Q^2 = 0.1$ GeV$^2$ show significant disagreement with Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations.
- The generalized GDH integral $I(Q^2)$ varies smoothly to increasingly negative values as $Q^2$ varies from 0.9 GeV$^2$ towards zero.

## Context

This work extends precision spin-structure measurements from Jefferson Lab into the low $Q^2$ region, connecting the Bjorken sum rule (Bjorken, 1966) at high $Q^2$ with the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule (Gerasimov, 1965; Drell and Hearn, 1966) at $Q^2=0$. The results provide benchmark tests for Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations (Heavy Baryon ChPT and Relativistic Baryon ChPT including vector mesons and Delta contributions) and the MAID model (Drechsel et al., 2001). The surprising disagreement in $\delta_{LT}^n$ motivates a new approved proposal to measure $g_2^p$ with a transversely polarized proton target for isospin separation of the spin polarizabilities.
