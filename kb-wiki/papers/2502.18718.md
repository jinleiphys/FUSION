# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of neutron-$α$ Scattering via an Integral Relation

> Nuclear physics seeks to describe both bound and unbound states within a unified predictive framework. While coordinate-space Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods have successfully computed bound states for systems with $A \leq 12$, their application to unbound states remains limited. In this work, we extend the QMC approach to enable a broader range of unbound-state calculations. Our method infers long-range amplitudes in the wave function from integrals over the short-range interaction region. By evaluating these integrals using Green's Function Monte Carlo wave functions with the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential, we accurately reproduce existing results for neutron-alpha scattering. This approach provides a systematic pathway for studying more complex nuclear systems, including coupled-channel scattering and the effects of three-nucleon forces. It serves as a powerful tool for advancing $\textit{ab initio}$ calculations in nuclear reactions, paving the way for a unified framework that consistently describes both bound and scattering states within a single theoretical approach.

# Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of neutron-α Scattering via an Integral Relation

## Key claim

The integral method within the Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) framework can reproduce existing neutron-alpha scattering phase shifts using the Argonne v18 potential, but the method relies on a constrained path algorithm with approximately 80 unconstrained steps for unbound states to avoid introducing a bias of 10–20 keV in the energy evaluation.

## Method

The authors applied the integral method within the GFMC framework to calculate K-matrix elements and phase shifts for neutron-alpha scattering. They used imaginary time propagation of wave functions with the Argonne v18 potential, employing a method-of-images technique to map outside-the-box contributions to inside-the-box integrals, with linear extrapolation using the logarithmic derivative boundary condition near the box surface. The surface amplitudes A and B were computed using off-diagonal mixed estimates, where the propagated full A-body scattering wave function and the propagated (A-1) daughter wave function were combined with variational estimates.

## Key numbers

- VMC alpha binding energy: -23.75(1) MeV; GFMC result: -24.12(2) MeV, using the AV18 potential
- Imaginary time step used: Δτ = 0.0005 MeV⁻¹
- For unbound states in ⁵He, approximately 80 unconstrained steps are required to avoid introducing a bias of 10–20 keV in the energy evaluation
- The Green's function propagator is only significant when "near" the surface of the box, within approximately 1 fm
- Boundary condition example for the 1/2⁺ channel: ζ = -0.13 fm⁻¹ at 9 fm

## Context

This work extends the Quantum Monte Carlo approach to unbound states, which has previously been limited to bound states for systems with A ≤ 12. The results are compared with existing GFMC direct calculations by Nollett (2007) and Faddeev-Yakubovsky solutions by Lazauskas (2020), confirming statistical equivalence. The method enables future applications to coupled-channel scattering and the study of three-body interactions on neutron-alpha scattering, and provides a pathway for incorporating local chiral interactions alongside phenomenological potentials.
