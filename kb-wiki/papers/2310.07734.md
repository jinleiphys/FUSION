# Role of isospin composition in low energy nuclear fusion

> We employ a microscopic approach that examines the impact of isospin dynamics on the process of low energy nuclear fusion along an isotope chain and dependence on deformation. Our method utilizes the density constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory (DC-TDHF), where isoscalar and isovector characteristics of the energy density functional (EDF) are examined in turn. This approach is applied to a series of fusion interactions of $^{176}$Yb with increasingly neutron rich isotopes of Calcium. By evaluating the contributions from the isoscalar and isovector components of the EDF, we look to quantify the influence of isospin composition on the conditions under which fusion is most likely to take place. Our findings reveal that, in non-symmetric systems, the isovector dynamics play a significant role. It's typical effect is a reduction in the potential barrier, which turns into enhancement for neutron-rich systems.

## Key claim

The isovector component of the Skyrme energy density functional significantly modifies the fusion barrier in non-symmetric systems, but the direction of the effect depends on the neutron richness of the projectile: for the neutron-deficient projectile (⁴⁰Ca) the isovector contribution reduces the barrier, while for neutron-rich projectiles (⁴⁸Ca, ⁵²Ca, ⁵⁴Ca) it enhances the barrier, with the enhancement growing with neutron number.

## Method

The authors used the density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method with the Skyrme SLy4d energy density functional (EDF) decomposed into isoscalar and isovector parts. They computed ion-ion interaction potentials for collisions of prolate-deformed ¹⁷⁶Yb with spherical calcium isotopes (⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁴Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵²Ca, ⁵⁴Ca) at center-of-mass energies set to 1.05 times the corresponding Bass barrier, for two orientations of the deformed target (tip, β=0°, and side, β=90°). The isovector contribution was isolated by comparing the total potential to the sum of isoscalar and Coulomb potentials.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca+¹⁷⁶Yb at E_c.m. = 166.45 MeV: the isovector contribution produces a substantial reduction of the barrier (blue shaded region in Fig. 1), making the overall potential thinner with a slightly lower barrier height.
- For ⁴⁸Ca+¹⁷⁶Yb at E_c.m. = 161 MeV: the isovector contribution produces a small enhancement of the potential barrier (red shaded region in Fig. 2), reversing the behavior seen with ⁴⁰Ca.
- For ⁵²Ca+¹⁷⁶Yb at E_c.m. = 159.9 MeV: the isovector enhancement becomes significant (Fig. 3), with the isoscalar+Coulomb potential lower in peak energy and smaller in width than the total potential.
- For ⁵⁴Ca+¹⁷⁶Yb at E_c.m. = 158.98 MeV: the isovector enhancement is even more pronounced (Fig. 4).
- All calculations used a 60×32×32 fm³ Cartesian box with 1.0 fm mesh spacing and the basis-spline collocation method.

## Context

This work extends the DC-TDHF method (Umar 2006b, Simenel 2018) to systematically study isospin effects along a calcium isotope chain, building on earlier isospin-decomposed EDF studies for reactions (Vophuoc 2016, Godbey 2017) and structure (Dobaczewski 1995). The observed transition from isovector reduction to enhancement with increasing neutron number is analogous to experimental sub-barrier fusion enhancement differences between ¹³²Sn+⁴⁰Ca and ¹³²Sn+⁴⁸Ca (Kolata 2012). The results enable future microscopic studies of fusion involving exotic neutron-rich nuclei at rare isotope facilities and have implications for the nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy.
