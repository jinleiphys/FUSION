# Coulomb screening in the momentum-space description of the proton-deuteron elastic scattering: Why the renormalization is needed?

> Proton-deuteron elastic scattering is considered in the framework of momentum-space Faddeev equations with the screening method for the Coulomb interaction. It is shown how the interplay of the proton-proton Coulomb potential and the deuteron pole in the neutron-proton transition operator leads to coinciding singularities in the Faddeev equation. As a consequence, the renormalization of the scattering amplitude is needed in the unscreened Coulomb limit. This finding possibly explains why no need for the renormalization was conjectured in a previous work [Witała et al., Eur.~Phys.~J.~A 41, 369 (2009)] missing the coincidence of those singularities.

# Coulomb screening in the momentum-space description of the proton-deuteron elastic scattering: Why the renormalization is needed?

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the momentum-space Faddeev equations for proton-deuteron elastic scattering, the interplay between the proton-proton Coulomb potential singularity and the deuteron bound-state pole in the neutron-proton transition operator leads to coinciding singularities in the Faddeev equation, making the integral ill-defined. This contradicts the earlier claim by Witała et al. (2009) that no renormalization is needed, and instead shows that renormalization of the scattering amplitude is required in the unscreened Coulomb limit, naturally performed in terms of the point deuteron-proton system.

## Method

The analysis uses the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) version of the Faddeev equation for the symmetrized proton-deuteron transition operator, iterated once to expose the relevant terms. The author examines the singularity structure of the integrand in the proton-deuteron on-shell limit, showing that the off-shell proton-proton transition operator carries the Coulomb potential singularity, which coincides with the deuteron pole singularity from the free resolvent when the relative spectator-pair momentum approaches its on-shell value. The analysis is performed directly in three-dimensional momentum space without partial-wave decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The fine structure constant is given as α_e.m. ≈ 1/137, used in the Coulomb potential definition w(p_f - p_i) = α/(p_f - p_i)^2 where α = α_e.m./2π^2
- The deuteron binding energy is denoted ε_d, and the relative proton-deuteron on-shell momentum q_o satisfies E = q_o^2/2μ_pd - ε_d, where μ_pd is the proton-deuteron reduced mass
- The isospin weighting factor 2/3 arises when replacing the proton-proton transition operator by the Coulomb potential in the singularity analysis
- The form factor F_1 satisfies F_1(0) = 1, meaning the singular part of the matrix element equals that of proton scattering off a point deuteron at vanishing momentum transfer

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing disagreement in the literature regarding the treatment of Coulomb interactions in momentum-space three-nucleon calculations. Previous work by Alt et al. (1978, 1980, 2002) and Deltuva (2005) required renormalization of scattering amplitudes, while Witała et al. (2009) claimed the unscreened limit exists without renormalization. The present paper shows that Witała et al. (2009) missed the coincidence of singularities arising from the interplay of the off-shell proton-proton Coulomb transition matrix and the deuteron pole, leading to an erroneous conclusion. The finding enables proper treatment of Coulomb effects in proton-deuteron elastic scattering and breakup reactions using the screening and renormalization method, with the point deuteron-proton system serving as the reference problem for determining renormalization factors.
