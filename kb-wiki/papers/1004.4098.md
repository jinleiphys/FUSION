# Neutrino magnetic moment effects in electron-capture measurements at GSI

> I conjecture that the time modulated decay rates reported in single ion measurements of two body electron capture decay of hydrogen like heavy ions at GSI may be related to neutrino spin precession in the static magnetic field of the storage ring. These `GSI Oscillations' arise from interference between amplitudes of decay within and without the magnetic field, a scenario that requires a Dirac neutrino magnetic moment six times lower than the Borexino solar neutrino upper limit of 0.54 x 10E(-10) Bohr magneton. I also show in a way not discussed before that the time modulation associated with interference between massive neutrino amplitudes, if such interference could arise, is of a period at least four orders of magnitude shorter than reported and must average to zero given the time resolution of the GSI measurements.

# Neutrino magnetic moment effects in electron-capture measurements at GSI

## Key claim

The time-modulated decay rates (GSI Oscillations) reported in single-ion two-body electron capture decays of hydrogen-like heavy ions at GSI may be explained by neutrino spin precession in the static magnetic field of the storage ring, requiring a Dirac neutrino magnetic moment approximately six times lower than the Borexino solar neutrino upper limit of 0.54 × 10⁻¹⁰ Bohr magneton. However, the author acknowledges this explanation is schematic and controversial, and does not work for Majorana neutrinos, which are theoretically favored.

## Method

The author reanalyzes the time-dependent perturbation theory formalism used by Ivanov and Kienle (2009) for two-body electron capture decays D → R + ν, showing that a correct application yields oscillation periods over four orders of magnitude shorter than reported. The author then develops a schematic model where the static magnetic field (B = 1.19 T) of the ESR storage ring causes spin precession of the emitted Dirac neutrino, and interference arises between decay amplitudes that encounter the magnetic field (covering approximately 35% of the ring circumference) and those that do not, analogous to a two-slit experiment.

## Key numbers

- The reported GSI oscillation period is T_EC^lab ≈ 7.1 s, corresponding to angular frequency ω_EC^lab ≈ 0.89 s⁻¹ and rest-frame energy ℏω_EC ≈ 0.84 × 10⁻¹⁵ eV
- The neutrino squared-mass difference used is Δ(m_νc²)² = (0.76 ± 0.02) × 10⁻⁴ eV² from solar neutrino and KamLAND data
- The correct energy scale for neutrino mass-eigenstate interference is ℏΩ_νe ≈ 0.95 × 10⁻¹¹ eV, giving a period of approximately 4.4 × 10⁻⁴ s, over four orders of magnitude shorter than the reported 7 s period
- The static magnetic field in the ESR is B = 1.19 T for ¹⁴⁰Pr, with Lorentz factor γ = 1.43
- The required Dirac neutrino magnetic moment to match the GSI oscillation period is μ_ν ∼ 0.9 × 10⁻¹¹ μ_B, six times lower than the Borexino upper limit of 0.54 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B
- The magnetic field covers approximately 35% of the storage ring circumference
- The experimental time resolution is approximately 0.5 s

## Context

This paper extends the debate over the GSI Oscillations reported by Litvinov et al. (2008), where time-modulated electron capture decay rates were observed in hydrogen-like ¹⁴⁰Pr and ¹⁴²Pm ions. The author argues against the neutrino mass-eigenstate interference interpretation proposed by Ivanov and Kienle (2009) and Kleinert and Kienle (2008), showing their formalism actually yields periods too short to observe. The magnetic field mechanism proposed here is distinct from initial-state hyperfine effects considered by others. The work enables experimental tests by varying the magnetic field B or the ion velocity β, and predicts no modulation in three-body β⁺ decays, consistent with preliminary data from Kienle (2009) showing a_β⁺ < 0.03(3).
