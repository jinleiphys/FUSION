# Resonance states in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C modified Morse potential

> The resonances in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system described earlier using long range Morse potential determined from resonance data itself, are reexamined in the light of the recent development of the two new methods for identification of resonances in the scattering theory, namely Imaginary Test Potential and Imaginary Phase-shift methods. The high lying resonances are found to be not genuine as pointed out by Kato and Abe, and as such are discarded. This discomfiture is due to the (i) shallow behavior of the Morse potential at the outer edge and (ii) inappropriate insertion of Coulomb tail used in that work. These two deficiencies are now removed in the present study by finding a modified Morse potential with steep rise in the outer edge, and joining smoothly to it a term approximating the Coulomb tail. Calculation of the resonances for this modified Morse potential using the above two methods of identification of resonances in our study, yields more than 25 states with angular momenta $0^{+}$ - $12^{+}$ in the relevant energy regions. This reaffirms the diatomic-like rotational and vibrational picture of the nuclear molecular resonances in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system proposed earlier, and shows close resemblance with the physics of diatomic molecules, a phenomenon belonging to altogether a different area. It is revealing that the similarity extends right upto the level of potential which is Morse type in both the cases. This study also reveals new features of heavy ion potential.

# Resonance states in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C modified Morse potential

## Key claim
The high-lying resonances previously reported for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system using a long-range Morse potential are not genuine, as pointed out by Kato and Abe, due to the shallow behavior of the potential at the outer edge and an inappropriate insertion of the Coulomb tail. After constructing a modified Morse potential that corrects these two deficiencies, more than 25 genuine resonance states with angular momenta $0^{+}$ to $12^{+}$ are obtained, reaffirming the diatomic-like rotational and vibrational picture of nuclear molecular resonances.

## Method
The author constructed a modified Morse potential that retains the original Morse potential's depth (3.45 MeV), range (15 fm), and repulsive soft core (17.45 MeV), but adds a steep rise at the outer edge and a smoothly connected approximate Coulomb tail. Resonances were identified using two S-matrix-based methods developed by the author and collaborators: the Imaginary Test Potential (ITP) method, which adds a small imaginary potential to convert elastic scattering into a two-channel problem and identifies resonances as peaks in the reaction cross-section, and the Imaginary Phase Shift (IPS) method, which extracts both resonance energy and width from the behavior of the imaginary part of the phase shift.

## Key numbers
- The original Morse potential had parameters: $A=6.99$ MeV, $B=6.30$ MeV, $\beta=0.957$, $R_0=6.97$ fm.
- The modified Morse potential has parameters: $R_0=15$ fm, $d_1=0.25$, $d_2=15$, $V_0=1$ MeV, $\xi_1=0.45$, $\xi_2=3.45$, $V_I=17$ MeV, $a_I=0.57$ fm$^{-1}$.
- For $\ell=4$, five resonances were identified at energies 2.46, 3.67, 5.28, 7.29, and 9.6 MeV.
- For $\ell=10$, two high-lying states were found at 7.515 and 10.11 MeV.
- The Coulomb barrier of the modified Morse potential is 3.45 MeV.
- The highest state predicted with $L=12^{+}$ lies at 15.3 MeV.
- More than 25 resonance states with angular momenta $0^{+}$ to $12^{+}$ are reported as genuine.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier studies (Satpathy 1986, 1990, 1992) that first proposed a Morse potential for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system and accounted for more than 35 states. Kato and Abe (1997) reexamined those results and found that the high-lying resonances were not genuine but rather echoes. The present study corrects the two deficiencies identified by Kato and Abe by constructing a modified Morse potential with a steep outer edge and proper Coulomb tail. The work also relates to the band crossing model of Abe (1979) and the algebraic U(4) approach of Iachello (1981), as well as the fitting of 28 resonances by Erb and Bromley (1981) that yielded a rotational parameter of 0.76 MeV. The results enable further investigation into how such a heavy-ion potential can be generated from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, and support the Dynamic Potential Model proposed earlier by the author.
