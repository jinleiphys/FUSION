# Charged-current weak interaction processes in hot and dense matter and its impact on the spectra of neutrinos emitted from proto-neutron star cooling

> We have performed three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport radiation hydrodynamics simulations covering a period of 3 s after the formation of a protoneutron star in a core-collapse supernova explosion. Our results show that a treatment of charged-current neutrino interactions in hot and dense matter as suggested by Reddy et al. [Phys. Rev. D 58, 013009 (1998)] has a strong impact on the luminosities and spectra of the emitted neutrinos. When compared with simulations that neglect mean field effects on the neutrino opacities, we find that the luminosities of all neutrino flavors are reduced while the spectral differences between electron neutrino and antineutrino are increased. Their magnitude depends on the equation of state and in particular on the symmetry energy at sub-nuclear densities. These modifications reduce the proton-to-nucleon ratio of the outflow, increasing slightly their entropy. They are expected to have a substantial impact on the nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven winds, even though they do not result in conditions that favor an r-process. Contrarily to previous findings, our simulations show that the spectra of electron neutrinos remain substantially different from those of other (anti)neutrino flavors during the entire deleptonization phase of the protoneutron star. The obtained luminosity and spectral changes are also expected to have important consequences for neutrino flavor oscillations and neutrino detection on Earth.

## Key claim

Including mean-field corrections to charged-current neutrino opacities that are consistent with the underlying equation of state (as suggested by Reddy et al. (1998)) increases the spectral differences between electron neutrinos and antineutrinos during the first 3 seconds of protoneutron star cooling, and these differences persist throughout the deleptonization phase, contrary to previous findings that predicted the spectra would become practically indistinguishable after about 10 seconds.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport radiation hydrodynamics simulations of a 15 solar mass progenitor, evolved from core collapse through explosion to more than 3 seconds after bounce. They implemented charged-current neutrino opacities that include mean-field single-particle potentials for neutrons and protons derived from the Shen et al. (1998a) equation of state, using the elastic approximation (zero momentum transfer) and the formalism of Reddy et al. (1998). The results were compared to simulations that neglect mean-field effects on the opacities but still use chemical potentials from the same equation of state.

## Key numbers

- At conditions found at the antineutrinosphere for an 18 solar mass model at 1 second after bounce (density 2.1 × 10^13 g cm^-3, temperature 7.4 MeV, electron fraction 0.035), the mean-field potentials were U_n = -7.6 MeV and U_p = -14.2 MeV from the Shen et al. (1998a) equation of state.
- At high densities around 10^14 g cm^-3, the difference U_n - U_p reaches approximately 50 MeV, producing a large enhancement of the neutrino opacity compared to the non-interacting approximation.
- The simulations were evolved from core collapse through explosion onset at about 350 ms post bounce up to more than 3 seconds after bounce.
- Neutrino observables were sampled in a co-moving reference frame at a distance of 1000 km.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fischer et al. (2012), which showed that convergence of neutrino and antineutrino spectra at late times is due to suppression of charged-current processes at high densities, but used opacities assuming a non-interacting gas of nucleons. The results contradict earlier simulations by Huedepohl et al. (2010) and Fischer et al. (2010) that predicted neutrino and antineutrino spectra become practically indistinguishable after about 10 seconds. The findings enable improved predictions for nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven winds, neutrino flavor oscillation studies, and neutrino detection on Earth, and imply sensitivity of both luminosity evolution and spectral differences to the nuclear symmetry energy.
