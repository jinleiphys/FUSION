# Lattice Effective Field Theory for Medium-Mass Nuclei

> We extend Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) to medium-mass nuclei, and present results for the ground states of alpha nuclei from $^4$He to $^{28}$Si, calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the EFT expansion. This computational advance is made possible by extrapolations of lattice data using multiple initial and final states. For our soft two-nucleon interaction, we find that the overall contribution from multi-nucleon forces must change sign from attractive to repulsive with increasing nucleon number. This effect is not produced by three-nucleon forces at NNLO, but it can be approximated by an effective four-nucleon interaction. We discuss the convergence of the EFT expansion and the broad significance of our findings for future ab initio calculations.

## Key claim

Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) is extended to medium-mass alpha nuclei up to ²⁸Si, but the two-nucleon force alone overbinds nuclei beyond ¹⁶O, requiring the overall contribution from multi-nucleon forces to change sign from attractive to repulsive with increasing nucleon number, an effect not produced by three-nucleon forces at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) but approximated by an effective four-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors use NLEFT combined with Auxiliary-Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) on a periodic lattice with spacing 1.97 fm and length 11.82 fm, with a temporal lattice spacing of 1.32 fm. Ground-state energies are computed via Euclidean-time projection from trial states prepared using a Wigner SU(4) filter, with extrapolation to infinite projection time improved by simultaneously fitting data from 2–6 distinct trial states (a "triangulation" method). Higher-order corrections at NLO and NNLO are evaluated using perturbation theory, and the spectral density is approximated by a sum of three or four energy delta functions for correlated fits of transient energies and operator expectation values.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of ⁴He at NNLO including three-nucleon force (3NF): –28.93(7) MeV (empirical: –28.30 MeV)
- Ground-state energy of ¹²C at NNLO including 3NF: –91.7(2) MeV (empirical: –92.16 MeV)
- Ground-state energy of ¹⁶O at NNLO including 3NF: –138.8(5) MeV (empirical: –127.62 MeV), an overbinding of about 9%
- Ground-state energy of ²⁸Si at NNLO including 3NF: –308(3) MeV (empirical: –236.54 MeV), an overbinding of about 30%
- With the effective four-nucleon interaction added, ²⁸Si ground-state energy becomes –233(3) MeV (empirical: –236.54 MeV), reducing the error to about 3%
- Lattice spacing: a = 1.97 fm; periodic cube length L = 11.82 fm; temporal lattice spacing a_t = 1.32 fm
- Momentum cutoff scale: π/a ≈ 314 MeV

## Context

This work extends NLEFT, previously successful for nuclei up to A ≈ 12 (including the Hoyle state in ¹²C by Epelbaum et al. (2011, 2012)), to medium-mass nuclei up to ²⁸Si. The observed overbinding for A ≥ 16 mirrors similar difficulties encountered by other ab initio methods using soft chiral interactions, such as coupled-cluster expansions (Hagen et al., 2012), the no-core shell model (Jurgenson et al., 2013; Roth et al., 2011), and the in-medium similarity renormalization group approach (Hergert et al., 2012). The results enable future NLEFT calculations at smaller lattice spacings and at N³LO in the chiral expansion, and suggest a universal criterion for all ab initio methods: the softening of the two-nucleon interaction should not be pushed so far that heavier nuclei become significantly overbound by the two-nucleon force alone.
