# Fermion Interactions and Universal Behavior in Strongly Interacting Theories

> The theory of the strong interaction, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), describes the generation of hadronic masses and the state of hadronic matter during the early stages of the evolution of the universe. As a complement, experiments with ultracold fermionic atoms provide a clean environment to benchmark our understanding of dynamical formation of condensates and the generation of bound states in strongly interacting many-body systems. Renormalization group (RG) techniques offer great potential for theoretical advances in both hot and dense QCD as well as many-body physics, but their connections have not yet been investigated in great detail. We aim to take a further step to bridge this gap. A cross-fertilization is indeed promising since it may eventually provide us with an ab-initio description of hadronization, condensation, and bound-state formation in strongly interacting theories. After giving a thorough introduction to the derivation and analysis of fermionic RG flows, we give an introductory review of our present understanding of universal long-range behavior in various different theories, ranging from non-relativistic many-body problems to relativistic gauge theories, with an emphasis on scaling behavior of physical observables close to quantum phase transitions (i. e. phase transitions at zero temperature) as well as thermal phase transitions.

## Key claim

This review argues that renormalization group (RG) techniques, particularly the Wetterich equation, provide a unifying framework for understanding universal long-range behavior and critical phenomena across strongly interacting fermionic systems, from hot and dense QCD to ultracold atomic gases and nuclear physics, but the connections between these fields have not yet been investigated in great detail, and the paper aims to take a further step to bridge this gap.

## Method

The author employs the Wetterich equation, a non-perturbative functional RG flow equation for the effective average action, which interpolates between the classical action at an ultraviolet scale and the full quantum effective action in the infrared limit. The derivation and analysis of fermionic RG flows are introduced using a simple four-fermion theory as a toy model, which shares many aspects with more complicated theories such as QCD. The review then applies this RG framework to analyze scaling behavior of physical observables close to quantum phase transitions (zero temperature) and thermal phase transitions across various theories, including non-relativistic many-body problems, Gross-Neveu and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type models, and relativistic gauge theories.

- The cutoff function for non-relativistic fermionic many-body problems is given by R_k^ψ(p̅²) = k² r_ψ(Z) with Z = (p̅² - μ)/k², where μ is the chemical potential defining the Fermi surface.
- The RG "time" is defined as t = ln(k/Λ), where Λ is the ultraviolet scale and k is the infrared regulator scale.

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting RG studies of QCD at finite temperature and density (Gies 2002, Gies 2005, Braun 2005, Braun 2006, Braun 2008, Braun 2009) with RG studies of ultracold Fermi gases (Diehl 2007, Diehl 2009) and condensed matter systems (Honerkamp and collaborators). It also relates to studies of quantum phase transitions in effective theories of graphene such as QED₃ and the Thirring model (Drut 2007, Gies 2009, Gies 2010), and to walking technicolor scenarios for the Higgs sector beyond the standard model (Weinberg 1979, Holdom 1981, Sannino 2004, Dietrich 2005). The work enables a systematic approach to density functional theory beyond local density approximation for applications to trapped Fermi gases and heavy nuclei, and provides a foundation for ab-initio descriptions of hadronization, condensation, and bound-state formation in strongly interacting theories.
