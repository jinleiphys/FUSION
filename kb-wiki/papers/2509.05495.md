# Knudsen number and universal behavior of collective flows in conformal and non-conformal systems

> We investigate the role of the Knudsen number (Kn) as a scaling parameter governing the emergence of collective behavior in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using the Relativistic Boltzmann Transport approach, we explore different initial conditions for both conformal (massless) and non-conformal (massive) systems with a constant specific shear viscosity $η/s$. Observables such as the time evolution of anisotropic flow coefficients collapse onto universal curves for fixed classes of Knudsen number, when using a scaled time variable accounting for the system size and the speed of sound $c_s$. More differential quantities, such as $v_n(p_T/\langle E_T\rangle)$, show a larger sensitivity to $c_s$. We also study events with fluctuating initial profiles from the \trento\ model, simulating collision systems from O-O to Pb-Pb at RHIC and LHC energies. Universal scaling at a given Kn value also holds in these event-by-event simulations, suggesting that the Knudsen number provides a unified criterion for classifying collectivity across different systems, including small systems where thermalisation may not be fully realised.

## Key claim

The Knudsen number (Kn) serves as a universal scaling parameter that classifies collective behavior across different collision systems (from O-O to Pb-Pb at RHIC and LHC energies), even in systems where thermalisation may not be fully realized, provided that the scaled time variable accounts for the speed of sound in non-conformal (massive) systems.

## Method

The authors solved the Relativistic Boltzmann Transport equation with elastic 2-to-2 collisions for a one-component parton system, using a stochastic algorithm and test particle method with 15-20 million test particles per simulation. They simulated both conformal (massless) and non-conformal (massive) systems with constant specific shear viscosity η/s, using smooth Gaussian initial conditions with imposed eccentricities and event-by-event initial profiles from the Trento model for systems ranging from O-O to Pb-Pb.

## Key numbers

- Three universality classes were explored with inverse Knudsen numbers Kn_R = 1.30, 3.20, and 6.45, using systems with radii R = 3.0 fm and R = 5.0 fm and temperatures T_0 = 0.33 GeV and T_0 = 0.50 GeV
- For non-conformal systems with m/T_0 = [0, 1, 2], the relative splitting in v_2/ε_2 at Kn_R = 6.45 was less than 3%, while at Kn_R = 1.3 it was approximately 15%
- In event-by-event simulations, Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV (10-20% centrality) gave Kn_R = 6.5 with average radius ⟨R⟩ = 4.20 fm; O-O at 7 TeV (60-80% centrality) gave Kn_R = 1.8 with ⟨R⟩ = 1.80 fm
- The specific shear viscosity was fixed at η/s = 0.13 for all event-by-event simulations, with freeze-out energy density threshold e < 0.182 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the scaling analysis of Nugara et al. (2024) from conformal kinetic theory to non-conformal (massive) systems, and from smooth initial conditions to realistic fluctuating initial profiles from the Trento model. The study builds on prior work by Kurkela et al. (2019) and Ambrus et al. (2021) on opacity scaling in kinetic theory, and by Bhalerao et al. (2005) on the role of speed of sound in flow development. The results enable classifying collectivity across systems from O-O to Pb-Pb using a single parameter (Kn), potentially guiding experimental searches for the transition between hydrodynamic and particle-like regimes in medium-size collisions at LHC.
