# KamLAND results and the radiogenic terrestrial heat

> We find that recent results from the KamLAND collaboration on geologically produced antineutrinos, N(U+Th) = 28+16-15 events, correspond to a radiogenic heat production from Uranium and Thorium decay chains H(U+Th) = 38+35-33 TW. The 99% confidence limit on the geo-neutrino signal translates into the upper bound H(U+Th) < 162 TW, which is much weaker than that claimed by KamLAND, H(U+Th) < 60 TW, based on a too narrow class of geological models. We also performed an analysis of KamLAND data including recent high precision measurements of the C13(α,n)O16 cross section. The result, N(U+Th) = 31+14-13, corroborates the evidence (approx 2.5σ) for geo-neutrinos in KamLAND data.

# KamLAND results and the radiogenic terrestrial heat

## Key claim
The KamLAND collaboration's claim of a 60 TW upper bound on radiogenic heat from Uranium and Thorium is based on a too narrow class of geological models; the actual 99% confidence upper bound derived from the same data is 162 TW, which is too weak to constrain Earth models since anything above 37 TW is already geochemically unrealistic.

## Method
The authors re-analyze KamLAND geo-neutrino data by constructing a band of allowed signal-to-heat relationships using extreme but geochemically permissible distributions of Uranium and Thorium in the crust and mantle, rather than assuming a simple linear scaling from a single Bulk Silicate Earth (BSE) reference model. They also incorporate new high-precision measurements of the 13C(alpha,n)16O cross section to reduce the dominant background uncertainty, and perform both rate-only and binned-spectrum analyses.

## Key numbers
- KamLAND collaboration's reported geo-neutrino events from U+Th decay chains: N(U+Th) = 28+16-15 events (rate + spectrum analysis)
- Corresponding radiogenic heat production: H(U+Th) = 38+35-33 TW
- 99% confidence upper bound on H(U+Th): < 162 TW (compared to KamLAND's claimed < 60 TW)
- With new 13C(alpha,n)16O cross section data (4% absolute normalization accuracy), binned spectrum analysis yields N(U+Th) = 31+14-13 events, corresponding to H(U+Th) = 44+31-27 TW
- Evidence for geo-neutrinos in KamLAND data: approximately 2.5 sigma
- Slope dS/dH for physically interesting region (H(U+Th) <= 37 TW): approximately 1 TNU/TW

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior modeling of geo-neutrino signals and Earth heat production (Fiorentini 2003, Mantovani 2004, Fiorentini 2005) by applying it to the first KamLAND geo-neutrino results (Araki 2005). It contradicts the KamLAND collaboration's interpretation that their data imply a 60 TW upper bound, showing that the scaling assumption used by KamLAND is incompatible with well-known crustal geochemical constraints. The analysis enables future experimental design by establishing that discriminating between the BSE model (16 TW) and a fully radiogenic model (37 TW) requires detector accuracy of approximately 7 TNU, which demands new detectors far from nuclear power plants.
