# A study of charm quark dynamics in quark-gluon plasma with 3+1 D viscous hydrodynamics

> The drag and diffusion coefficients are studied within the framework of Fokker-Planck dynamics for the case of a charm quark propagating in an expanding quark-gluon plasma. The space-time evolution of the nuclear matter created in the relativistic heavy-ion collision is modelled using MUSIC, a 3+1 D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic approach. The effect of viscous corrections to the heavy quark transport coefficients is explored by considering scattering processes with thermal quarks and gluons in the medium. It is observed that the momentum diffusion of the heavy quarks is sensitive to the shear and bulk viscosity to entropy ratios. The collisional energy loss of the charm quark in the viscous quark-gluon plasma is analyzed.

## Key claim

The drag and momentum diffusion coefficients of charm quarks in an expanding quark-gluon plasma are sensitive to both shear and bulk viscosity to entropy ratios, with viscous corrections reaching up to 30% for bulk viscosity, but only when the medium evolution is modeled with a realistic 3+1 D viscous hydrodynamic framework rather than a static or boost-invariant approximation.

## Method

The authors modeled the space-time evolution of a Pb+Pb collision at 2.76 TeV using MUSIC, a 3+1 D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code with IP-Glasma initial conditions and a lattice QCD equation of state. They computed charm quark drag and diffusion coefficients from the Fokker-Planck equation by evaluating collision integrals for 2 to 2 elastic scattering with thermal quarks and gluons, incorporating shear and bulk viscous corrections to the parton distribution functions and to the Debye screening mass in the scattering matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient A for a charm quark with momentum p = 5 GeV drops as the QGP expands, with lower values at later times and lower temperatures
- Shear-viscous corrections to HQ drag and diffusion coefficients show variation up to 10% for eta/s = 0.13
- Bulk-viscous corrections modify HQ drag and diffusion coefficients up to 30% in the QGP medium
- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.5 GeV with effective number of degrees of freedom N_f = 2.5 and coupling constant alpha_s = 0.3
- Spatial diffusion coefficient D_s = T/(m_c * A(p to 0, T)) is compared with pQCD estimates from vanHees (2004), lattice QCD from Banerjee (2011), and quasiparticle model from Scardina (2017)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark dynamics in quark-gluon plasma by moving beyond static medium approximations and purely longitudinal boost-invariant expansion (GolamMustafa, 1997) to a full 3+1 D viscous hydrodynamic evolution. The results are consistent with leading order pQCD estimates from vanHees (2004) for the spatial diffusion coefficient, and the finding that bulk viscosity significantly modifies heavy quark transport aligns with the work of Ryu (2015) on bulk viscosity effects in hadronic observables. The framework enables future Langevin simulations that include non-perturbative effects and radiative energy loss to compute observables such as the nuclear suppression factor R_AA and elliptic flow.
