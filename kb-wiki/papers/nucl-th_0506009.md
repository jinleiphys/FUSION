# Strangeness production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions

> In these lectures we discuss the investigation of the strange meson production in proton-proton ($pp$) and in proton-nucleus ($pA$) reactions within an effective Lagrangian model. The kaon production proceeds mainly via the excitations of $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710), and $N^*$(1720) resonant intermediate nucleonic states, in the collision of two initial state nucleons. Therefore, the strangeness production is expected to provide information about the resonances lying at higher excitation energies. For beam energies very close to the kaon production threshold the hyperon-proton final state interaction effects are quite important. Thus, these studies provide a check on the models of hyperon-nucleon interactions. The in-medium production of kaons show strong sensitivity to the self energies of the intermediate mesons.

# Strangeness production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The effective Lagrangian model can describe the near-threshold data for $pp \to p\Lambda K^+$ and $pp \to p\Sigma^0 K^+$ reactions without introducing arbitrary normalization constants, but only if final state interaction effects are included via the Watson-Migdal theory and the $N^*$(1650) resonance dominates both reactions at excess energies below 30 MeV. The large ratio ($R \approx 28$) of $\Lambda K^+$ to $\Sigma^0 K^+$ cross sections near threshold arises from a combination of FSI effects (accounting for 60-80% of the ratio below 30 MeV) and the differing roles of the three nucleon resonances in the two channels.

## Method

The authors model strangeness production in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions using an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams, where the initial nucleon-nucleon interaction proceeds via $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\sigma$ meson exchange with coupling constants fitted to $NN$ elastic scattering data from 400 MeV to 4.0 GeV. The kaon production proceeds through excitation of $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710), and $N^*$(1720) intermediate baryonic resonances, with coupling constants determined from experimental branching ratios and from a coupled-channels analysis of $\pi N$ data. Final state interaction effects in $pp$ reactions are included via the Watson-Migdal theory using a Coulomb-modified effective range expansion of the phase shifts. For $pA$ reactions, the model uses Dirac spinors for bound nucleon and hyperon states, and includes medium effects through pion self-energies calculated from particle-hole and delta-hole excitations renormalized with the Landau-Migdal parameter $g^\prime$.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $R$ of total cross sections for $pp \to p\Lambda K^+$ to $pp \to p\Sigma^0 K^+$ at excess energy $\epsilon < 13$ MeV is $28^{+6}_{-9}$ (COSY-11 data, Seweryniak 1999)
- FSI effects account for about 60-80% of the observed ratio $R$ for $\epsilon < 30$ MeV, and about 50% beyond this energy
- The $Q$ values (excess energy at which a resonance becomes significant) for $pp \to pK^+\Lambda$ are 115 MeV ($N^*$1650), 150 MeV ($N^*$1710), and 185 MeV ($N^*$1720); for $pp \to pK^+\Sigma^0$ they are 36 MeV, 72 MeV, and 105 MeV respectively
- The $N^*$(1650) resonance dominates both reactions for $\epsilon < 30$ MeV
- At higher beam energies ($\epsilon > 300$ MeV), values of $R$ with and without FSI effects are almost identical
- For the $^{40}$Ca$(p,K^+)$$^{41}\!\!\!_\Lambda$Ca reaction at 1.5 GeV, differential cross sections near the peak are around 1-2 nb/sr
- The pion self-energy has a large effect on $pA$ cross sections, with strong sensitivity to the Landau-Migdal parameter $g^\prime$ in the range 0.5-0.7

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied by the author and collaborators to $pp \to pp\pi^0$, $pp \to pn\pi^+$ (Shyam 1996, 1998), $pp \to pK^+Y$ (Shyam 1999, 2001), and $pp \to ppe^+e^-$ (Shyam 2003) reactions. It addresses the large ratio $R$ observed by the COSY-11 collaboration (Seweryniak 1999), which earlier calculations by Gasparian (2000), Sibirtsev (2000), and Laget (2001) attempted to explain using only $\pi$- and $K$-exchange without including baryonic resonance excitations. The results enable future experiments at COSY and other facilities to use near-threshold kaon production as a probe of the $N^*$(1650) resonance properties, and the $pA$ cross section estimates (1-2 nb/sr) provide guidance for planning hypernuclear spectroscopy experiments. The work also demonstrates that a fully relativistic treatment of bound state spinors is essential for $A(p,K^+){_\Lambda}B$ reactions, as the upper and lower components of the Dirac spinors become comparable in magnitude at the momentum transfers involved.
