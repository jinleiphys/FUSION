# Extracting scattering parameters using the isospin chemical potential

> Hadronic scattering mediated through the strong interaction has been an area of great interest for both theory and experiment. Recently, lattice QCD calculations of scattering processes from first principles have seen remarkable progress, including multiple calculations of two-pion scattering lengths that agree with experiment to within a few percent. However, there exists a certain class of scattering processes, such as pion-nucleon scattering, that contain annihilation diagrams, which are often prohibitively expensive to simulate on the lattice. In this talk, I will present a method to extract certain parameters from this class of scattering processes by employing an isospin chemical potential, which can be simulated on the lattice as a result of its positive-definite fermion determinant.

# Extracting scattering parameters using the isospin chemical potential

## Key claim
The method can extract certain baryon-meson scattering parameters that involve annihilation diagrams (which are prohibitively expensive to compute via all-to-all propagators on the lattice) by instead measuring baryon mass shifts in the presence of a dynamically generated pion condensate, provided the effective field theory remains valid in the chiral regime.

## Method
The author proposes simulating lattice QCD with a finite isospin chemical potential, which generates a pion condensate above a critical value while maintaining a positive-definite fermion determinant. Baryon spectroscopy is then performed in the presence of this condensate, and the resulting mass shifts are matched to analytic expressions from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to extract low-energy constants (such as the sigma term and pion-nucleon scattering parameters) that encode the scattering information.

## Key numbers
- The critical isospin chemical potential for pion condensation is given by the condition μ = m_π, where m_π is the pion mass.
- The nucleon mass shift depends on the condensate through terms such as 4 c_1 (m_π^2 cos α + λ ε sin α), where c_1 is the sigma term with an estimated value of c_1 ∼ 0.9 GeV^{-1} from Frink (2004).
- The nucleon mass must be determined to between 0.5% and 5% precision depending on the pion mass (smaller pion masses require better resolution in the spectroscopy calculation).

## Context
This work extends the proposal of Bedaque (2009) to use an isospin chemical potential for extracting scattering parameters, and builds on prior lattice QCD calculations of two-pion scattering that agree with experiment to within a few percent (Yamazaki 2004, Beane 2005, Beane 2007, Feng 2009). It addresses the specific difficulty of annihilation diagrams in processes such as pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering, which have eluded direct lattice calculations due to the need for all-to-all propagators (Torok 2009). The method enables future extraction of low-energy constants at physical pion masses and could be extended to meson-meson scattering, heavy quark potentials, and thermodynamic studies of pion condensation in neutron star contexts.
