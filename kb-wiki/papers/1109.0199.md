# Delta and Omega masses in a three-quark covariant Faddeev approach

> We present the solution of the Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation for the Delta(1232) and Omega(1672) baryons. The covariant structure of the corresponding baryon amplitudes and their decomposition in terms of internal spin and orbital angular momentum is explicitly derived. The interaction kernel is truncated to a rainbow-ladder dressed-gluon exchange such that chiral symmetry and its dynamical breaking are correctly implemented. The resulting physical masses agree reasonably with experiment and their evolution with the pion mass compares favorably with lattice calculations. Evidence for the non-sphericity of the Delta-resonance is discussed as well.

## Key claim

The authors solve the fully Poincare-covariant three-quark Faddeev equation for the Delta(1232) and Omega(1672) baryons using a rainbow-ladder truncation, obtaining masses that agree reasonably with experiment; however, the effective interaction provides too much binding in the low pion-mass region where the Delta decay channel would open, shifting the calculated mass upward by approximately 100 MeV relative to the physical value.

## Method

The Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation for spin-3/2 baryons is solved in Euclidean momentum space and Landau gauge, with the interaction kernel truncated to a rainbow-ladder dressed-gluon exchange that satisfies the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The quark propagators are obtained consistently from the corresponding Dyson-Schwinger equation using the same effective interaction, which is modeled by an ansatz with one dimensionless parameter eta and an infrared scale Lambda fixed to reproduce the pion decay constant. The Faddeev amplitude is expanded in a basis of 128 elements classified by quark-spin (s = 1/2, 3/2) and relative orbital angular momentum (l = 0, 1, 2, 3), and the bound-state masses and amplitudes are obtained from the solution.

## Key numbers

* Delta(1232) mass at physical pion mass: 1.26 GeV (Faddeev result with eta = 1.8), compared to experimental value 1.23 GeV.
* Omega(1672) mass at m_pi = 690 MeV: 1.72 GeV (Faddeev result with eta = 1.8), compared to experimental value 1.67 GeV.
* Nucleon mass: 0.94 GeV (Faddeev result with eta = 1.8), matching the experimental value.
* Quark-diquark approach gives Delta mass 1.28 GeV and Omega mass 1.77 GeV for the same eta = 1.8.
* Infrared energy scale Lambda = 0.72 GeV is fixed to reproduce the pion decay constant.
* The effective interaction parameter eta is varied in the range 1.6 - 2.0 to test sensitivity.
* The s-quark mass is fixed to reproduce a fictitious pseudoscalar s\bar{s} state mass m_{s\bar{s}} = 690 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the covariant three-body Faddeev approach previously applied to the nucleon by Eichmann et al. (2009, 2011) to the spin-3/2 Delta and Omega baryons, providing the first full solution for these resonances. The results are compared to lattice data from Alexandrou et al. (2009), Engel et al. (2010), Zanotti et al. (2003), and Gattringer et al. (2008), showing good agreement especially at higher pion masses. The analysis of partial-wave contributions demonstrates that the Delta and Omega are not pure s-wave states, with non-negligible p- and d-wave components, which is a first step toward understanding baryon deformation from sphericity and enables future studies of electromagnetic form factors and the gamma N Delta transition.
