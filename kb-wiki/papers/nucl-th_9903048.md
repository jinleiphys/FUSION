# The phase diagram and bulk thermodynamical quantities in the NJL model at finite temperature and density

> We reexamine the recent instanton motivated studies of Alford, Rajagopal and Wilczek, and Berges and Rajagopal in the framework of the standard SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The chiral phase diagram is calculated in the temperature--density plane, and the pressure is evaluated as the function of the density. Obtaining simple approximate relations describing the $T$-$μ$ and $T$-$p_F$ phase transition lines we find that the results of the instanton based model and that of the NJL model are identical. The diquark transition line is also given.

# The phase diagram and bulk thermodynamical quantities in the NJL model at finite temperature and density

## Key claim

The chiral phase diagram and pressure-density relations obtained from the standard SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model are qualitatively identical to those from instanton-motivated four-fermion models (Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek 1998; Berges and Rajagopal 1999), provided one compares quantities as functions of Fermi momentum or density rather than chemical potential. The authors derive an approximate analytic expression for the chiral phase boundary that is independent of the NJL model parameters G and Λ, but this analytic curve differs from the full numerical result because the analytic expression takes the cutoff Λ → ∞ while the numerical calculation uses a finite cutoff.

## Method

The authors start from the standard SU(2) NJL Lagrangian with a four-fermion interaction and compute the mean-field gap equation for the dynamically generated quark mass m at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ, using a hard three-momentum cutoff Λ = 0.65 GeV and coupling G = 5.01 GeV⁻². They evaluate the pressure density from the thermodynamic potential and perform Maxwell constructions to obtain the full chiral phase diagram in the T–density plane. For the diquark (color superconducting) transition, they extend the thermodynamic potential to include a diquark condensate Δ, solve the gap equation numerically for the critical line, and fix the diquark coupling G₁ by demanding T_c = 40 MeV at μ = 0.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The critical Fermi momentum at the chiral phase transition (m → 0) at T = 0 is p_c = 0.307 GeV, derived from the parameters Λ = 0.65 GeV and G = 5.01 GeV⁻² with N_c = 3, N_f = 2.
- The maximum critical temperature on the chiral transition curve is T_m = (√3 p_c)/π ≃ 0.169 GeV.
- The vacuum energy density is ε_vac = −(407 MeV)⁴ for the chosen parameter set (Λ = 0.65 GeV, G = 5.01 GeV⁻², f_π = 93 MeV, ⟨ūu⟩ = (−250 MeV)³).
- The diquark coupling is set to G₁ = 3.10861 GeV⁻² (or G₁Λ² = 1.31) to match T_c = 40 MeV at μ = 0.4 GeV.
- The diquark gap Δ is approximately 35 MeV at the chiral transition point at zero temperature, increasing to about 95 MeV at μ = 0.53 GeV and zero temperature.

## Context

This work directly compares the NJL model results to the instanton-motivated studies of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Berges and Rajagopal (1999), showing that the thermodynamic potential of the instanton model reduces to the NJL form when the form factor is set to one and a hard cutoff is introduced. The authors note that the truncated NJL model of Jaminon, Nys, and Papp (1999) shows similar behavior but lacks the unstable backbending of the phase curve at high temperatures. The paper identifies a known limitation: the energy per quark at T = 0 does not possess a minimum at normal nuclear matter density n₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, in contrast to linear sigma model calculations (Pirner, 1999). The results enable future studies of diquark condensation in the chirally broken phase, though the authors find this is strongly parameter-dependent and not realized for their chosen G₁ value.
