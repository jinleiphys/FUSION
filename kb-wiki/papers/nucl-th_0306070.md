# Magnetization screening from gluonic currents and scaling law violation in the ratio of magnetic form factors for neutron and proton

> The ratio ${μ_p}G_E^p/G_M^p$ exhibits a decrease for four-momentum transfer Q^2 increasing beyond 1 GeV^2 indicating different spatial distributions for charge and for magnetization inside the proton. One-gluon exchange currents can explain this behaviour. The SU(6) breaking induced by gluonic currents predicts furthermore that the ratio of neutron to proton magnetic form factors ${μ_p}G_M^n/{μ_n}G_M^p$ falls with increasing Q^2. We find that the experimental data are consistent with our expectations of an almost linear decrease of the ratio ${μ_p}G_M^n/{μ_n}G_M^p$ with increasing Q^2, supporting the statement that the spatial distributions of magnetization are different for protons and for neutrons.

## Key claim
The ratio of neutron to proton magnetic form factors, μ_p G_M^n / μ_n G_M^p, falls with increasing Q^2, indicating that the spatial distributions of magnetization differ between protons and neutrons. This decrease is predicted by SU(6) breaking induced by one-gluon exchange (OGE) currents, and the experimental data are consistent with an almost linear decrease of this ratio with Q^2.

## Method
The authors used a non-relativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) with a harmonic oscillator confining potential and added OGE-induced interaction currents derived from the QCD interaction Lagrangian. These OGE currents describe quark-antiquark pair creation by the external photon followed by annihilation into a gluon absorbed by another quark, producing screening corrections to the charge and magnetization distributions. The model parameters (α_s = 0.4, b = 0.5 fm, m_q = 400 MeV) were obtained from a best fit to the proton ratio R_p = μ_p G_E^p / G_M^p, and then applied to predict the neutron-to-proton magnetic form factor ratio R_M.

## Key numbers
- The ratio R_M = μ_p G_M^n / μ_n G_M^p deviates from unity for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, showing a nearly linear decrease with increasing Q^2.
- The model uses α_s = 0.4, b = 0.5 fm (quark core radius), and m_q = 400 MeV, obtained from fitting the proton ratio R_p.
- The dipole form factor used is G_D(Q^2) = [1 + Q^2 / 0.71 GeV^2]^{-2}.
- The strong coupling constant α_s is varied within the range 0.2 to 0.6 around the optimum value of 0.4, producing the shaded uncertainty band in the theoretical prediction.
- The proton ratio R_p data extend up to Q^2 ~ 5.5 GeV^2 from JLab polarization transfer measurements.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Kaskulov and Grabmayr, 2003) which showed that OGE currents explain the decrease of the proton ratio μ_p G_E^p / G_M^p observed at JLab (Jones et al., 2000; Punjabi et al., 2005). The paper contradicts the simple scaling law G_E^p = G_M^p/μ_p = G_M^n/μ_n = G_D that follows from the non-relativistic constituent quark model of Isgur and Karl. The results enable future tests of nucleon structure models by combining the ratios R_p and R_M, and suggest that new high-precision data on neutron and proton magnetic form factors above Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 are needed to confirm the predictions.
