# Possibility of determinig the parity of the pentaquark $Θ^+$ from photoproduction near threshold

> We discuss the possibility of determining the parity of the $Θ^+$ baryon from photoproduction $γN\to KΘ^+$ process near threshold. We utilize the conservation laws of parity and angular momentum for the analysis of angular distributions and spin observables near threshold. Since the discussion is in essence a partial wave analysis of the production mechanism the result should be less dependent on the model parameters. Our analysis shows that the angular distribution and photon polarization asymmetry for the process of neutron target are sensitive to the parity of the $Θ^+$, but not for the case of proton target. In the case of proton target, the polarization asymmetries of target and recoiled $Θ^+$ are preferred for parity determination.

## Key claim

The parity of the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ can be determined from the photoproduction process $\gamma N \to K\Theta^+$ near threshold using conservation laws of parity and angular momentum, with the angular distribution and photon polarization asymmetry being sensitive to parity for neutron targets but not for proton targets, where target and recoil polarization asymmetries are instead preferred.

## Method

The authors applied conservation laws of parity and angular momentum to perform a partial wave analysis of the $\gamma N \to K\Theta^+$ process near threshold, identifying the leading multipole transitions (E$_{0+}$ for positive parity $\Theta^+$, E$_{1-}$ for negative parity $\Theta^+$) based on threshold kinematics. They then expanded the photoproduction current in terms of CGLN amplitudes and computed angular distributions and single polarization asymmetries using a hadron model with coupling constants derived from the $\Theta^+$ decay width of 1 MeV, including Born terms and $K^*$ and $K_1$ meson exchanges in the t-channel.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy $E_\gamma = 1.8$ GeV used for threshold-region analysis (slightly above the exact threshold of $E_\gamma = 1.75$ GeV where kaon momentum $|\bm{q}| = 0$)
- Coupling constant $g_{KN\Theta} = 0.984$ for positive parity $\Theta^+$ and $g_{KN\Theta} = 0.137$ for negative parity $\Theta^+$, both from $\Gamma_{\Theta^+ \to NK} = 1$ MeV
- Kinematic factor $\frac{k}{E+M} \simeq 0.37$ in the center-of-mass frame at $E_\gamma = 1.8$ GeV, which suppresses the $F_1^-$ amplitude for $\gamma n \to K^-\Theta^+$
- Photon polarization asymmetry $\Sigma^+ \approx 0$ for positive parity $\Theta^+$ and $\Sigma^- \approx -1$ for negative parity $\Theta^+$ in $\gamma n \to K^-\Theta^+$ near threshold

## Context

This work extends the approach of Thomas et al. (2004) and Hanhart et al. (2004) who applied conservation laws to the $pp \to \Sigma^+\Theta^+$ process, and of Nakayama and Lee (2004) who suggested parity determination via polarization asymmetries in $\gamma N \to K\Theta^+$. The analysis differs from Reka et al. (2004) by identifying a kinematic suppression factor ($k/(E+M) \approx 0.37$) that enables parity discrimination from unpolarized angular distributions in $\gamma n \to K^-\Theta^+$, whereas Reka et al. required spin observables for both neutron and proton targets. The results enable experimental parity determination of the $\Theta^+$ using either angular distributions and photon asymmetry for neutron targets or target/recoil polarization asymmetries for proton targets, with minimal model dependence near threshold.
