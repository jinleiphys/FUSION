# Neutronless $^{10}$be-Accompanied Ternary Fission of $^{252}$Cf}

> A new type of decay corresponding to the neutronless $^{10}$Be-accompanied fragmentation of $^{252}$Cf is studied. We employ a cluster model similar to the model used for the description of cluster radioactivity. No preformation factors were considered. The ternary relative isotopic yields were calculated as the ratio of the penetrability of a given ternary fragmentation over the sum of penetrabilities of all possible ternary neutronless fragmentations. The corresponding barriers between the light and heavy fragment and between the $^{10}$Be cluster and the two heavier fragments were computed with the help of a double folding potential generated by M3Y-$NN$ effective interaction and realistic fragment ground state deformations. Also, we studied the influence of the fragment excitation energies on the yields, by including the level densities and the $β$-stretching of the fragments. The new phenomenon could be experimentally observed by the triple gamma coincidence technique between the fragments and $^{10}$Be.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that neutronless $^{10}$Be-accompanied ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf should exist as a cold rearrangement process, but the authors qualify that the predicted yields are at least 100 times smaller than alpha-ternary yields (approximately 0.002% of total fission yield), and that experimental observation will require triple-gamma coincidence techniques to eliminate background from binary fission partners.

## Method

The authors employed a cluster model similar to that used for cluster radioactivity, neglecting preformation factors and calculating ternary relative isotopic yields as the ratio of the penetrability of a given ternary fragmentation over the sum of penetrabilities of all possible neutronless fragmentations. The potential barriers between the two heavier fragments and between the $^{10}$Be cluster and the heavier fragments were computed using a double folding potential generated by the M3Y nucleon-nucleon effective interaction with realistic fragment ground state deformations including quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecupole terms. The influence of fragment excitation energies on yields was studied by including level densities from the Back Shifted Fermi Gas Model and $\beta$-stretching of the fragments.

## Key numbers

- The cold (neutronless) binary yield represents only 0.2% from the total fission yield of $^{252}$Cf
- The $^{10}$Be-ternary yields are at least 100 times smaller than $\alpha$-ternary yields, i.e. 0.002% of total fission yield
- The $^{10}$Be decay energy from $^{252}$Cf is $Q_c = 8.71$ MeV
- The exit point from the potential barrier for the two heavier fragments is at $R_{HL}$ typically between 15 and 16 fm
- The barrier width between the two heavier fragments is about 2 to 3 fm
- The M3Y zero-range pseudopotential strength used was $\hat{J}_{00} = -276$ MeV fm$^3$
- The total experimental dataset consisted of $9.8 \times 10^9$ triple or higher fold coincidence events recorded with 72 Compton suppressed Ge detectors

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of cluster radioactivity (Sandulescu and Greiner, prior work) and cold binary fission of $^{252}$Cf (observed by Ramayya, Hamilton et al. using Gammasphere) to the case of ternary fission with a $^{10}$Be third fragment. The authors note that experimental indications of $^{10}$Be and $^{14}$C ternary fragmentations were already obtained in prior Gammasphere data (references 19,20 in the paper). The predictions are intended as a first guide for unfolding cold ternary yields from complex gamma-ray spectra, and the authors suggest that the mass distribution in cold $^{10}$Be ternary fission should be similar to that from cold binary fission of $^{242}$Pu.
