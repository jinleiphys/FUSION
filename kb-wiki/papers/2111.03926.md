# Perspectives in Small Systems : Heavy Quarks and HBT Radii

> Recent observations of QGP-like phenomena in small collision systems like p+p and p+A collisions have questioned our understanding of the basic paradigms of high energy heavy-ion physics. A brief discussion of these new aspects in small systems which in turn influence our understanding of hard probes like heavy quarks has been provided. Furthermore, a closer observation of the two-particle Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) interferometry measurements provide new insights in our understanding of medium-like phenomena in small systems. An outlook of future goals and measurements is also made.

## Key claim

Recent observations of QGP-like phenomena in small collision systems (p+p and p+A) challenge the traditional view that such systems are simple reference measurements for heavy-ion collisions; however, the interpretation of heavy quark suppression and collective flow in these small systems remains ambiguous because cold nuclear matter effects are not fully understood even at RHIC energies, and standard pQCD energy loss models explicitly assume a large QGP system.

## Method

The paper surveys experimental results on heavy quark production (quarkonia and open heavy flavor) and two-particle Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry measurements in p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It compares nuclear modification factors (R_AB, R_pA, R_CP) and elliptic flow coefficients (v_2) across system sizes, and examines the scaling behavior of three-dimensional HBT radii (R_out, R_side, R_long) extracted from Gaussian fits to correlation functions in the longitudinally co-moving system (LCMS).

## Key numbers

- CMS measurements at √s = 13 TeV show multi-particle cumulant v_2{4} and v_2{6} values in high-multiplicity p+p collisions are consistent with each other and similar to those in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions (Khachatryan 2016)
- ATLAS at √s = 13 TeV measured elliptic flow (v_2) for heavy-flavor decay muons in p+p collisions: bottom-decay muons show v_2 consistent with zero, while charm-decay muons have significant non-zero v_2 values (Aad 2019)
- HBT radii in small systems (p+p, d+A) at RHIC scale linearly with the cube root of charged particle pseudo-rapidity density ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩^(1/3), as also seen for Au+Au and Cu+Cu (Das 2007)
- At LHC energies, p+Pb radii are comparable with p+p collisions at low multiplicities, but diverge with increasing multiplicity (Adam 2015)
- ALICE measurements show HBT radii scaling with ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩^(1/3) across p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions at LHC, and across A+A collision energies at RHIC and SPS (Adam 2015)
- STAR at RHIC measured pion pair momentum dependence of HBT radii in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions; radii ratios (Au+Au/Cu+Cu) show common dependence on pair transverse momenta (Abelev 2009)

## Context

This perspective piece extends the discussion of QGP-like phenomena from heavy-ion (A+A) collisions into small systems (p+p, p+A), building on the foundational work of Matsui and Satz (1986) on quarkonium suppression and the HBT formalism reviewed by Lisa et al. (2005) and Heinz (1996). It directly addresses the challenge posed by recent CMS (Khachatryan 2016) and ATLAS (Aad 2019) observations of collective flow in p+p collisions, which contradict the traditional use of p+p as a simple reference for nuclear modification factors. The paper enables future investigations at LHC Run-3 and 4 and at the proposed Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to disentangle initial-state effects from final-state interactions in small systems, particularly through precision measurements of flavor-separated heavy quark flow and quarkonium HBT interferometry.
