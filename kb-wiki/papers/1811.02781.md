# Linear response theory for Gogny interaction

> We present the formalism of the linear response theory in symmetric nuclear matter for a finite-range central interaction interaction including zero-range spin-orbit and tensor components.

## Key claim

The paper presents a linear response formalism for finite-range Gogny interactions that includes spin-orbit and tensor terms, extending previous work that only treated central interactions or used approximate continued-fraction methods. The authors demonstrate that a multipolar expansion with a cutoff of \(L_{Max}=2\) is sufficient to obtain a reasonable description of the response function, though the formalism formally requires an infinite number of multipoles for finite-range interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the RPA propagator in symmetric nuclear matter by performing a multipolar expansion of the Hartree-Fock propagator and the particle-hole residual interaction in spherical harmonics. They discretize the resulting coupled integral equations over a uniform momentum mesh in the range \(k \in [0, q + k_F]\) and invert the matrix system numerically, truncating the multipole expansion at a maximum angular momentum \(L_{Max}\). The method is benchmarked against the known analytical solution for the Skyrme T44 interaction and against continued-fraction results for the Gogny D1S interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the Skyrme T44 interaction at saturation density and transferred momentum \(q = k_F\), the response functions from the multipolar expansion method match those from the standard technique, with small deviations attributed only to discretisation on a finite grid.
- For the Gogny D1S interaction at density \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and transferred momenta \(q = 0.5 k_F\) and \(q = k_F\), the response functions converge with a cutoff of \(L_{Max} = 2\).
- For the central term of the Gogny D1S interaction at density \(\rho = 0.174\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(q = k_F\), the multipolar expansion results agree excellently with the continued-fraction approximation of Dep16.
- For the Gogny D1S interaction at density \(\rho = 1.5 \rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is the saturation density), the response function in channel (0,0,1) exhibits a pole at critical momentum \(q_c = 2.53\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the linear response formalism previously developed for Skyrme functionals (Davesne et al., 2015; Pastore et al., 2012) to finite-range Gogny interactions, building on the central-only treatment of Margueron et al. (2005). The method improves over the continued-fraction approximation of Dep16 by including spin-orbit terms, which become important at larger transferred momenta, and by being numerically less expensive. The formalism enables direct detection of instabilities in the response function, which can be incorporated into fitting procedures for future Gogny parametrisations, particularly for nuclear astrophysics applications such as neutrino opacities in neutron stars.
