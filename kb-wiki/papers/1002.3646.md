# The Negele-Vautherin density matrix expansion applied to the Gogny force

> We use the Negele-Vautherin density matrix expansion to derive a quasi-local density functional for the description of systems of fermions interacting with short-ranged interactions composed of arbitrary finite-range central, spin-orbit, and tensor components. Terms that are absent in the original Negele-Vautherin approach owing to the angle averaging of the density matrix are fixed by employing a gauge invariance condition. We obtain the Kohn-Sham interaction energies in all spin-isospin channels, including the exchange terms, expressed as functions of the local densities and their derivatives up to second (next to leading) order. We illustrate the method by determining the coupling constants of the Skyrme functional or Skyrme force that correspond to the finite-range Gogny central force. The resulting self-consistent solutions reproduce the Gogny-force binding energies and radii within the precision of 1-2%.

## Key claim

The Negele-Vautherin density matrix expansion can be extended to derive a quasi-local density functional for fermions interacting with arbitrary finite-range central, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions, with the missing odd-power gradient terms fixed by a gauge invariance condition; the resulting self-consistent solutions reproduce Gogny-force binding energies and radii within 1-2% precision, but the separation of scales between interaction range and density variation is not well pronounced in nuclei (with $k_Fa$ values of 0.95 and 1.62 for the Gogny interaction), meaning the expansion cannot rely on moments decreasing with increasing order.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Negele-Vautherin density matrix expansion by introducing auxiliary functions $\pi_i(r)$ and $\nu_i(r)$ that damp the nonlocal density matrix at large relative distances, and enforce local gauge invariance through the condition $\nu_1^2(r)=\nu_0(r)\nu_2(r)$. They then expand the direct and exchange interaction energies to second order in gradients of local densities (scalar, vector, isoscalar, and isovector channels) for arbitrary finite-range central, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions, expressing the coupling constants in terms of moments of the interaction potentials weighted by these auxiliary functions. The method is illustrated by determining the Skyrme functional coupling constants that correspond to the Gogny central force and performing self-consistent calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Gogny interaction has two components with ranges $a=0.7$ fm and $1.2$ fm, while $k_F=1.35$ fm$^{-1}$, giving $k_Fa=0.95$ and $1.62$
- The self-consistent solutions reproduce Gogny-force binding energies and radii within 1-2% precision
- The NV functions $\pi_0(r)$, $\pi_2(r)$, and $\nu_0(r)$ are approximated by Gaussians with leading-order expansions: $\pi_0(r) \simeq 1 - (k_Fr)^4/504$, $\pi_2(r) \simeq 1 - (k_Fr)^2/18 + (k_Fr)^4/792$, and $\nu_0(r) \simeq 1 - (k_Fr)^2/10 + (k_Fr)^4/280$
- The direct term coupling constants $V_0$ and $V_2$ are given by $V_n = 4\pi \int dr\, r^{n+2} V(r)$

## Context

This work extends the original Negele-Vautherin density matrix expansion (Neg72) by adding odd-power gradient densities through local gauge invariance, treating spin vector densities analogously to scalar densities, and applying a different DME for direct terms. It establishes a firm link between the finite-range Gogny interaction (Dec80, Ber91b) and the local Skyrme functional (Ben03, Per04), enabling microscopic derivations of energy density functionals that can be tested against nuclear density properties as advocated by Geb09. The approach is motivated by effective field theory interpretations (Lep97) and addresses the ongoing question of to what extent finite-range effective interactions can be approximated as quasi-local density functionals.
