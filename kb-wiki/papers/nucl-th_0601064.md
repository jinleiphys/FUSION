# Pair counting, pion-exchange forces, and the structure of light nuclei

> A simple but useful guide for understanding the structure of light nuclei is presented. It is based on counting the number of interacting pairs in different spin-isospin (S,T) states for a given spatial symmetry, and estimating the overall binding according to the sum of sigma_i.sigma_j tau_i.tau_j expectation values, as suggested by one-pion-exchange. Applied to s- and p-shell nuclei, this simple picture accounts for the relative stability of nuclei as A increases and as T changes across isobars, the saturation of nuclear binding in the p-shell, and the tendency to form d, t, or alpha subclusters there. With allowance for pairwise tensor and spin-orbit forces, which are also generated or boosted by pion-exchange, the model explains why mixing of different spatial symmetries in ground states increases as T increases across isobars, and why for states of the same spatial symmetry, the ones with greater S are lower in the spectrum. The ordering of some sd-shell intruder levels can also be understood. The success of this simple model supports the idea that one-pion-exchange is the dominant force controlling the structure of light nuclei.

# Pair counting, pion-exchange forces, and the structure of light nuclei

## Key claim

One-pion-exchange (OPE) is the dominant force controlling the structure of light nuclei, as demonstrated by a simple pair-counting model that estimates binding energy from the sum of spin-isospin operator expectation values. The model works well for s- and p-shell nuclei up to approximately A=9 but begins to underestimate binding for heavier nuclei, indicating the growing importance of three-nucleon forces that are not captured by the pairwise OPE-based approach.

## Method

The author counts the number of nucleon pairs in each spin-isospin (S,T) state (S=0 or 1, T=0 or 1) for a given spatial symmetry specified by a Young diagram [n]. The total number of pairs in each (S,T) channel is determined from A, total spin S_A, total isospin T_A, and the spatial symmetry parameter p_[n]. The binding energy measure E_OPE is computed by multiplying each pair count by the OPE operator expectation value (which gives weights of +1 for (1,1), -3 for (1,0), -3 for (0,1), and +9 for (0,0) pairs) and a scaling constant C ≈ 1.5 MeV. This is applied to s-shell (A=2-5) and p-shell (A=6-12) nuclei, comparing predicted relative binding energies to experimental spectra and to Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations with realistic interactions such as AV18/IL2.

## Key numbers

- The scaling constant C = 1.5 MeV gives a reasonably good average scale factor for the E_OPE binding measure.
- The deuteron (²H) has one (S,T)=(1,0) pair with strength -3C; the triton (³H) has three pairs equally divided between (1,0) and (0,1) with total strength -9C; the alpha (⁴He) has six pairs with total strength -18C.
- GFMC calculations for AV18/IL2 give two-body potential expectation values in the ratio 1 : 2.7 : 6.3 for d : t : α, reasonably close to the E_OPE ratio of 1 : 3 : 6.
- For A=6-12 nuclei, the ratio of kinetic to potential energy expectation values varies only from 0.76 to 0.81 in GFMC calculations.
- The ⁶Li ground state is 1.47 MeV below the alpha-deuteron threshold; ⁶He is 0.97 MeV below the alpha-dineutron threshold.
- ⁷Li ground state is 2.47 MeV below the alpha-triton threshold; GFMC with AV18 alone gives only 0.3 MeV below this threshold.
- ⁹Be is bound by 1.57 MeV relative to alpha-alpha-neutron breakup; GFMC with AV18/IL2 gives 1.9 ± 0.5 MeV binding relative to ⁸Be, while AV18 alone gives only 0.1 ± 0.4 MeV.
- ¹⁰B and ¹⁰Be have experimental binding energies of 64.75 and 64.98 MeV respectively, both predicted as -39C by the model.
- ¹²C ground state has E_OPE = -54C (three times that of ⁴He); experimentally it is 7 MeV (8%) more bound than three alphas.
- OPE provides about 75% of the net potential energy expectation value in GFMC calculations for A ≤ 12 nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the supermultiplet theory of Wigner (1958) which focused on symmetry aspects of light nuclei but assumed primarily central and space-exchange forces, whereas the present model incorporates the spin-isospin dependence of one-pion-exchange. The pair-counting approach is informed by recent progress in realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen models) and accurate many-body methods including GFMC, no-core shell model (NCSM), and coupled-cluster methods that can now reproduce binding energies up to A=12. The model shares common ground with the shell-model work of Otsuka and collaborators (2001, 2005) emphasizing OPE spin-isospin and tensor interactions in determining single-particle energy shifts. The simple picture enables qualitative understanding of nuclear binding trends, saturation, cluster formation, and symmetry mixing in light nuclei, while also highlighting where three-nucleon forces become essential for quantitative agreement with experiment.
