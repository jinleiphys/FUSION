# Variational and parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter. IV. Spin-orbit interactions and linear response

> We develop the parquet-diagram summation method for neutron matter interacting via potentials that include spin, tensor, and spin-orbit components. For that purpose, we derive an exact expression for the sum of all ring-diagrams in terms effective local particle-hole interactions involving the above four operators. The parquet equations are closed by deriving the spin-orbit contribution to that particle-hole interaction. We show that many-body correlations screen the bare spin-orbit potential considerably, and the corrections of that screened spin-orbit potential to the other three interaction channels are quite small. We apply our method to the calculation of the response of neutron matter to density and both longitudinal and transverse spin-dependent external fields.

## Key claim

The paper develops a parquet-diagram summation method for neutron matter that includes spin-orbit interactions, showing that many-body correlations screen the bare spin-orbit potential considerably, while the corrections of this screened spin-orbit potential to the other three interaction channels (central, spin-spin, and tensor) are quite small.

## Method

The authors derive an exact expression for the sum of all ring-diagrams in terms of effective local particle-hole interactions involving spin, tensor, and spin-orbit operators, then close the parquet equations by deriving the spin-orbit contribution to that particle-hole interaction. They transform the spin and tensor operators into longitudinal and transverse operators to decouple the convolution product in individual channels, and define a local approximation for the effective interaction by calculating hole-state averages. The method is applied to calculate the response of neutron matter to density and both longitudinal and transverse spin-dependent external fields.

## Key numbers

- The localized effective spin-orbit interaction is given by: $\tilde W^{\rm LS}_{\rm local}(q;\omega) = \frac{1}{2}\frac{\tilde V_{\rm p-h}^{\rm LS}(q)}{1-\chi_0(q;\omega)\tilde V_{\rm p-h}^{\rm (c)}(q;\omega)} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{\tilde V_{\rm p-h}^{\rm LS}(q)}{1-\chi_0(q;\omega)\tilde V_{\rm p-h}^{\rm (T)}(q;\omega)}$, where $\tilde V_{\rm p-h}^{\rm (c)}$ and $\tilde V_{\rm p-h}^{\rm (T)}$ are energy-dependent particle-hole interactions in the central and transverse channels respectively.
- The trace over hole states for the spin-orbit operator yields a factor of $2/5$: $\frac{1}{\nu^2} \Tr_{\sigma\sigma'}\int \frac{d^3h d^3h'}{V_F^2} \hat W(\mathbf{q}) \left(-i\hat{\mathbf{q}}\times\Delta \mathbf{h}_F\right)\cdot\mathbf{S} = \frac{2}{5}\tilde W^{\rm LS}(q)$.
- The transverse Lindhard function is defined as $\chi_0^{(\perp)}(q;\omega)=\frac{1}{N}\Tr_\sigma\sum_{\mathbf{h}}|\hat{\mathbf{q}}\times\mathbf{h}_F|^2\chi_0(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{h};\omega)$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter with $v_6$ model interactions (Krotscheck and Wang, 2020) to include spin-orbit forces, which are the next step toward realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. Prior studies of spin-orbit interactions in nuclear matter were mostly confined to Bethe-Goldstone calculations (Polls et al., 2005), three-body coupled cluster (Day, 1981), or low-order constraint variational (LOCV) methods (Tafrihi, 2015-2018). The method enables full parquet-level calculations for neutron matter with $v_8$ potentials and can be applied to study $P$-wave pairing in neutron matter and the dynamic structure function.
