# $ω$ meson from lattice QCD

> Many excited states in the hadron spectrum have large branching ratios to three-hadron final states. Understanding such particles from first principles QCD requires input from lattice QCD with one-, two-, and three-meson interpolators as well as a reliable three-body formalism relating finite-volume spectra at unphysical pion mass values to the scattering amplitudes at the physical point. In this work, we provide the first-ever calculation of the resonance parameters of the $ω$ meson from lattice QCD, including an update of the formalism through matching to effective field theories. The main result of this pioneering study, the pole position of the $ω$ meson at $\sqrt{s_ω}= (778.0(11.2)-i\,3.0(5) )\,\mathrm{MeV}$, agrees reasonably well with experiment. In addition we provide an estimate of the $ω-ρ$ mass difference as $29(15)\,\mathrm{MeV}$.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first-ever lattice QCD calculation of the omega meson resonance parameters, reporting a pole position that agrees reasonably well with experiment. The result is conditional on using effective field theory to extrapolate from unphysical pion masses (208 and 305 MeV) to the physical point, and the quoted uncertainties include both statistical and systematic components.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using CLQCD gauge configurations with Nf=2+1 flavors of dynamical quarks at a single lattice spacing of 0.07746 fm, with two pion masses (approximately 208 and 305 MeV) and two volumes each. They constructed one-, two-, and three-meson interpolating operators in the T1- irreducible representation, computed correlation matrices using the distillation method, and extracted finite-volume energy spectra. The finite-volume spectra were analyzed using the finite-volume unitarity (FVU) three-body formalism, with the two- and three-body forces parametrized both generically and through matching to effective field theories containing vector mesons, enabling chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers
* Omega meson pole position: sqrt(s_omega) = (778.0(11.2) - i 3.0(5)) MeV, extrapolated to the physical pion mass
* Rho meson pole position: sqrt(s_rho) = (748.9(10.0) - i 63.5(1.8)) MeV, extrapolated to the physical pion mass
* Omega-rho mass difference: 29(15) MeV
* Lattice spacing: a = 0.07746(18) fm
* Two pion masses used: M_pi approximately 208 MeV and 305 MeV
* EFT4 fit parameters: g = 5.96(17), delta = 38.8(6.9) MeV, M_V = 737(12) MeV, a = 0.96(14) GeV^{-1}

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD study of resonances from two-body decays (such as the rho meson studied by Guo (2016), Bali (2015), and others) to three-body decays, filling a gap where no lattice calculation of the omega meson pole position existed previously. The authors use the FVU formalism developed by Mai (2017) and match to effective field theories with vector mesons following Meissner (1987), enabling the first chiral extrapolation of three-body resonance parameters. This enables future first-principles studies of other three-body resonances such as the Roper resonance and the Tcc state.
