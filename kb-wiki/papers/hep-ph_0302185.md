# Nonlinear corrections to the DGLAP equations; looking for the saturation limits

> The effects of the first nonlinear corrections to the DGLAP equations are studied in light of the HERA data. Saturation limits are determined in the DGLAP+GLRMQ approach for the free proton and for the Pb nucleus.

# Nonlinear corrections to the DGLAP equations; looking for the saturation limits

## Key claim
The first nonlinear (GLRMQ) corrections to the DGLAP evolution equations, when combined with modified initial parton distributions at the starting scale, can reproduce the HERA structure function data as well as or better than standard DGLAP fits (CTEQ6L), but the extracted saturation scales are upper limits because only the first correction term is included.

## Method
The authors generated three new sets of initial parton distributions at Q₀² = 1.4 GeV², constructed piecewise from CTEQ5L and CTEQ6L distributions evolved down from higher scales, with a power-law form tuned in the small-x region. They then evolved these distributions using the DGLAP equations augmented with the first nonlinear GLRMQ correction terms (gluon recombination) and compared the resulting F₂ structure function to H1 data from HERA. Saturation scales Q²_sat(x) were determined by finding where the DGLAP and GLRMQ terms in the gluon evolution equation become equal, both for the free proton and for a Pb nucleus (A=208).

## Key numbers
- χ² per data point (χ/N) for the DGLAP+GLRMQ fits to H1 data: 1.13 (set 1), 1.17 (set 2a), 0.88 (set 2b), compared to 1.32 for CTEQ6L.
- Initial scale for evolution: Q₀² = 1.4 GeV².
- Charm quark mass threshold: m_c = 1.3 GeV (sets 1 and 2a); set 2b used m_c = √1.4 GeV.
- Proton radius used in two-gluon density model: R = 1 fm.
- For Pb nucleus, the nonlinear correction is enhanced by a factor of A^(1/3) relative to the proton.
- Downward evolution for nuclear saturation limits started at Q² = 100 to 200 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis of Eskola et al. (2002) on nonlinear corrections to DGLAP evolution in light of H1 data, and contrasts with standard PDF sets such as CTEQ6 (Pumplin et al., 2002) and MRST2001 (Martin et al., 2001), which have difficulty fitting both small and large Q² data simultaneously. The saturation limits obtained are compared with the geometric saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff (1998) for the proton and with the Glauberized geometric model of Armesto (2002) for Pb; despite the different approaches, the slopes of the saturation curves are strikingly similar at the smallest x values. The results enable future full global fits of nuclear parton distribution functions along the lines of EKRS (Eskola et al., 1998) and HKM (Hirai et al., 2001).
