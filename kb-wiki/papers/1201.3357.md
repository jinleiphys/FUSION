# Statistics of resonance width shifts as a signature of eigenfunction non-orthogonality

> We consider an open (scattering) quantum system under the action of a perturbation of its closed counterpart. It is demonstrated that the resulting shift of resonance widths is a sensitive indicator of the non-orthogonality of resonance wavefunctions, being zero only if those were orthogonal. Focusing further on chaotic systems, we employ random matrix theory to introduce a new type of parametric statistics in open systems, and derive the distribution of the resonance width shifts in the regime of weak coupling to the continuum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the shift in resonance widths under a perturbation of the closed counterpart of an open quantum system is a sensitive indicator of eigenfunction non-orthogonality, being zero only if the resonance states were orthogonal. The authors derive the universal distribution of these width shifts for chaotic systems in the regime of weak coupling to the continuum, using random matrix theory.

## Method

The authors model the open system via an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian H_eff = H - iγWW^†, where H is the Hermitian Hamiltonian of the closed system and W contains decay amplitudes coupling N levels to M channels. They treat the perturbation αV of the closed counterpart and compute the first-order shift in the complex resonance energy, δE_n = α⟨L_n|V|R_n⟩, using biorthogonal perturbation theory. For weak coupling (γ≪1), they expand the left and right eigenvectors to leading order in γ and derive an explicit expression for the width shift δΓ_n. They then model H by a random matrix from the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE, β=1) or Gaussian unitary ensemble (GUE, β=2) and compute the probability distribution of the rescaled width shifts in the limit N≫1, using the joint probability density of eigenvalues and known results for the distribution of resonance widths.

## Key numbers

- The resonance width distribution for weakly open chaotic systems is given by a χ² distribution with ν=Mβ degrees of freedom: P_M^{(β)}(κ) = (2/β)^{Mβ/2} / Γ(Mβ/2) * κ^{Mβ/2-1} e^{-βκ/2}, where κ_n = πΓ_n/(2γΔ) is the width in units of the mean partial width per channel, with mean ⟨κ⟩=M and variance var(κ)=2/(Mβ)⟨κ⟩².
- The rescaled width shift y_n = δΓ_n / (2γ√(2β var(δE_n))) has a distribution given by Eq. (14) of the paper: P_M(y) = ∫₀^∞ dκ/√κ * P_M^{(β)}(κ) * φ^{(β)}(y/√κ), where φ^{(1)}(y) = (4+y²)/(6(1+y²)^{5/2}) for GOE and φ^{(2)}(y) = (35+14y²+3y⁴)/(12π(1+y²)⁴) for GUE.
- The far tails of the distribution decay as P_M(y) ∝ y^{-(β+2)} due to Wigner-Dyson level repulsion.
- In the limit of many weakly open channels M≫1 (but M≪N), the scaled variable ỹ=y/√M has the probability density φ^{(β)}(ỹ).

## Context

This work extends the study of parametric statistics from closed quantum systems (level velocities and curvatures, studied by Simons and Altshuler (1993), Zakrzewski and Delande (1993), and Fyodorov (1995)) to open systems, where resonances have finite lifetimes. It introduces a new type of parametric statistics for open chaotic systems, complementing prior work on scattering characteristics such as phase shifts, time delays, and S-matrix elements by Fyodorov and Savin (1996, 1997) and others. The results enable experimental verification of eigenfunction non-orthogonality via purely spectroscopic measurements in microwave billiards, photonic crystals, or elastic plates, where non-orthogonality has been studied by Schomerus (2000), Savin and Sokolov (2006), and Poli et al. (2009). The generalization to arbitrary modal overlap and connections to PT-symmetric systems and pseudospectra are identified as open problems.
