# Effective shear and bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma: QCD versus heavy-ion data

> In recent years, there has been a significant effort to extract the temperature-dependent shear ($η/s$) and bulk ($ζ/s$) viscosity over entropy ratios of the quark-gluon plasma from a global comparison of heavy-ion data with results of hydrodynamic simulations. However, anisotropic flow, which is arguably the most sensitive probe of viscosity, is only sensitive to an {\it effective\/} viscosity over entropy ratio, which is obtained by taking a weighted average over the temperature, and summing the contributions of shear and bulk. We estimate this effective viscosity using existing first-principles calculations, which give $0.17<(η/s)_{\rm eff}<0.21$, and $(ζ/s)_{\rm eff}<0.08$, implying that the damping of anisotropic flow at the LHC is mostly due to shear viscosity. The values extracted from global data analyses are compatible with these theory predictions.

# Effective shear and bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma: QCD versus heavy-ion data

## Key claim

Anisotropic flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$ at LHC energies are sensitive only to an effective viscosity over entropy ratio, obtained by a weighted average over temperature that sums shear and bulk contributions; first-principles QCD calculations give $0.17 < (\eta/s)_{\rm eff} < 0.21$ and $(\zeta/s)_{\rm eff} < 0.08$, implying that shear viscosity dominates the damping of anisotropic flow at the LHC, and these theory predictions are compatible with values extracted from global data analyses.

## Method

The authors define effective shear and bulk viscosities by taking weighted temperature averages of the temperature-dependent $\eta/s(T)$ and $\zeta/s(T)$ using sensitivity weights $w_n^{(\eta)}(T)$ and $w_n^{(\zeta)}(T)$ derived from linearized viscous hydrodynamic simulations for $v_2$ and $v_3$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV. They then evaluate these effective viscosities by integrating first-principles QCD calculations from Functional Renormalization Group methods and lattice QCD over the temperature range $150 \lesssim T \lesssim 350$ MeV, and compare them to effective viscosities inferred from Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion data by the Duke group and the JETSCAPE Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- First-principles QCD calculations give $0.17 < (\eta/s)_{\rm eff} < 0.21$ for the effective shear viscosity over entropy ratio.
- First-principles QCD calculations give $(\zeta/s)_{\rm eff} < 0.08$ for the effective bulk viscosity over entropy ratio.
- For $v_2$, the viscous suppression formula is $v_2({\rm viscous}) = v_2({\rm ideal}) \left(1 - 1.34 (\eta/s)_{\rm 2,eff} - 1.30 (\zeta/s)_{\rm 2,eff}\right)$.
- For $v_3$, the viscous suppression formula is $v_3({\rm viscous}) = v_3({\rm ideal}) \left(1 - 2.33 (\eta/s)_{\rm 3,eff} - 2.61 (\zeta/s)_{\rm 3,eff}\right)$.
- The viscous suppression is larger by a factor of approximately 2 for $v_3$ than for $v_2$.
- The analysis uses Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV in the 0-5% centrality window.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hydrodynamic study (Gardim 2020) which showed that anisotropic flow harmonics depend on $\eta/s(T)$ and $\zeta/s(T)$ only through a single effective viscosity per observable. The results contradict the expectation that bulk viscosity might be significant at LHC energies, instead confirming general arguments (Arnold 2006) that bulk viscosity should be negligible compared to shear viscosity. The approach enables future extension to other observables such as mean transverse momentum $\langle p_t \rangle$, where shear and bulk viscosity effects go in opposite directions, potentially allowing disentanglement of the two contributions.
