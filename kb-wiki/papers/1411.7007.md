# Medium-induced flavor conversion and kaon spectra in electron-ion collisions

> Multiple scattering and induced parton splitting lead to a medium modification of the QCD evolution for jet fragmentation functions and the final hadron spectra. Medium-induced parton splittings not only lead to energy loss of leading partons and suppression of leading hadron spectra, but also modify the flavor composition of a jet due to induced flavor conversion via gluon emission, quark pair production and annihilation. Through a numerical study of the medium-modified QCD evolution, leading $K^-$ strange meson spectra are found to be particularly sensitive to the induced flavor conversion in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scatterings (SIDIS) off a large nucleus. The induced flavor conversion can lead to increased number of gluons and sea quarks in a jet and, as a consequence, enhance the leading $K^-$ spectra to counter the effect of parton energy loss in SIDIS with large momentum fractions $x_B$ where the struck quarks are mostly valence quarks of the nucleus.

# Medium-induced flavor conversion and kaon spectra in electron-ion collisions

## Key claim
Medium-induced flavor conversion via gluon emission, quark pair production, and annihilation can enhance leading $K^-$ meson spectra in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scatterings (SIDIS) off a large nucleus at large momentum fractions $x_B$, countering the suppression expected from parton energy loss alone. This enhancement occurs specifically when the struck quarks are mostly valence quarks ($u$ and $d$) of the nucleus, making $K^-$ production from non-constituent initial quarks particularly sensitive to flavor conversion.

## Method
The authors numerically solve medium-modified Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (mDGLAP) evolution equations for parton fragmentation functions that include both vacuum and medium-induced splitting functions, using a high-twist approach to multiple parton scatterings. The initial conditions for the medium-modified fragmentation functions are obtained via a convoluted model that convolves vacuum fragmentation functions with a quenching weight calculated from a Poisson distribution of induced gluon emissions. The jet transport parameter $\hat q$ is assumed proportional to the nuclear density, with a central value $\hat q_0 = 0.020$ GeV$^2$/fm obtained from a $\chi^2$ fit to HERMES data.

## Key numbers
- Jet transport parameter at center of nucleus: $\hat q_0 = 0.020 \pm 0.005$ GeV$^2$/fm, from a $\chi^2$ fit to HERMES data on hadron suppression.
- At $x_B = 0.5$, valence quark distributions dominate: $u(x_B) = 0.5331$, $d(x_B) = 0.1532$, while strange quark distribution is $\bar s, s = 0.0018$ at $Q^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$ (CTEQ6 parameterization).
- At $x_B = 0.1$, quark distributions are: $u = 5.6736$, $d = 3.7867$, $\bar u = 0.9262$, $\bar d = 1.3961$, $\bar s, s = 0.4790$ at $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$ (CTEQ6 parameterization).
- Nuclear modification factor for $K^-$ at $x_B = 0.5$ with DSS07 parameterization shows enhancement (factor exceeding 1) at intermediate and large $z$ for initial quark energy $\nu = 10$ GeV.
- Change to nuclear modification factor for $K^-$ at $x_B = 0.5$ due to EMC effect in nuclear parton distributions is less than 1%; at $x_B = 0.1$, it reduces the factor by about 5%.

## Context
This work extends the high-twist approach to medium-modified QCD evolution developed by Wang (2002), Deng (2010), and Chang (2014) for describing parton energy loss and hadron suppression in SIDIS. It specifically addresses the flavor conversion processes previously discussed by Guo (2000) and Liu (2006, 2008), which modify the flavor composition of jets. The study predicts a novel enhancement of $K^-$ spectra at large $x_B$ that could be tested at a future electron-ion collider (EIC), providing an independent probe of the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ and helping to understand flavor hierarchy in jet quenching observed in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The results also highlight the need for improved vacuum fragmentation function parameterizations for $K^-$, as the HKN and DSS07 parameterizations give quantitatively different predictions.
