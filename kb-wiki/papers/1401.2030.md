# Short-range correlation effects on the nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double-$β$ decay

> We report the results of a calculation of the nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{48}$Ca, carried out taking into account nucleon-nucleon correlations in both coordinate- and spin-space. Our numerical results, obtained using nuclear matter correlation functions, suggest that inclusion of correlations leads to a $\sim$ $20\%$ decrease of the matrix element, with respect to the shell model prediction. This conclusion is supported by the results of an independent calculation, in which correlation effects are taken into account using the spectroscopic factors of $^{48}$Ca obtained from an {\em ab intitio} many body approach.

## Key claim

Inclusion of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations, modeled using spin- and isospin-dependent correlation functions derived from nuclear matter, leads to a ~20% decrease of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element of ⁴⁸Ca relative to the shell model prediction, but this result depends critically on the radial shape of the correlation function; using correlation functions that overshoot unity at intermediate distances can instead produce an enhancement.

## Method

The authors computed the Fermi and Gamow-Teller contributions to the 0νββ decay matrix element of ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti within the closure approximation, using harmonic oscillator wave functions and two-body transition densities from the literature. Short-range correlations were incorporated by applying a correlation operator with central and spin-dependent components (up to m ≤ 4 in the operator expansion) to the relative wave function of the decaying nucleon pair, with radial functions obtained from minimization of the ground-state energy of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at equilibrium density using the Argonne v₆′ NN potential. As an independent check, correlation effects were also estimated by renormalizing the shell-model states using the spectroscopic factor Zⁿ₁f₇/₂(⁴⁸Ca) = 0.91 from an ab initio Fermi Hyper-Netted Chain calculation.

## Key numbers

- The ratio M/M_SM (matrix element with correlations divided by shell-model value) is 0.77 when only central correlations f(r₁₂) are included, and 0.79 when both central and spin-dependent correlations f(r₁₂) + g(r₁₂)(σ₁·σ₂) are included.
- The independent calculation using spectroscopic factors yields M/M_SM = 0.83, based on Zⁿ₁f₇/₂(⁴⁸Ca) = 0.91 from Ref. bisconti.
- The average energy in the neutrino potential was set to ⟨E⟩ = 7.72 MeV; varying ⟨E⟩ from 2.5 MeV to 12.5 MeV changes the NME by less than 5%.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter was ℏω = 45 A⁻¹/³ − 25 A⁻²/³ MeV, and the coupling constants were g_V = 1, g_A = 1.25.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of short-range correlation effects on 0νββ decay matrix elements, particularly those of Ref. SRC1, which reported results ranging from ~20% suppression to ~20% enhancement depending on the choice of correlation function (Miller-Spencer, AV18, or CD-Bonn). The authors argue that correlation functions derived from nuclear matter calculations provide a more realistic description, properly accounting for differences between neutron-neutron and proton-neutron pairs, and that the lowest-order cluster approximation is remarkably accurate. The results enable more reliable predictions for the ⁴⁸Ca decay being searched for by the CANDLES and CARVEL experiments, and call for future calculations using correlation functions obtained from minimization of the ground-state energy of ⁴⁸Ca itself with the full operator structure.
