# Is it possible to determine the S-factor of the hep process from a laboratory experiment?

> We discuss the problem of solar hep neutrinos originating from the reaction p + 3He -> 4He + e+ + nu and obtain a relation between the astrophysical S-factor of the hep process and the cross section of the process e- + 4He -> 3H + n + nu near threshold. The relation is based on the isotopic invariance of strong interactions. The measurement of the latter cross section would allow to obtain experimental information on S(hep), the value of which, at the moment, is known only from theoretical calculations.

# Is it possible to determine the S-factor of the hep process from a laboratory experiment?

## Key claim
The astrophysical S-factor of the solar hep reaction (p + ³He → ⁴He + e⁺ + νₑ) can be determined indirectly from laboratory measurements of the cross section of the inverse process e⁻ + ⁴He → ³H + n + νₑ near threshold, using isotopic invariance of strong interactions, but only if the inverse process can be measured at electron energies of order 10 MeV above threshold, not at solar-core energies.

## Method
The authors derive an analytic relation between the total cross section of the inverse electron-capture reaction on ⁴He and the S-factor of the hep process, using charge symmetry (isospin rotation) to connect the hadronic matrix elements of the two weak transitions. They apply the standard low-energy parametrization σ(E) = (1/E) exp(-2πη) S(E) for charged-particle reactions, treat the transition as an allowed Gamow-Teller transition, and neglect the Coulomb interaction in the region of nuclear forces. The resulting formula expresses σ(e⁻ ⁴He → ³H n νₑ) as a function of (Eₑ − Eₜₕ)⁷/² times S(hep)/S₀(hep), with numerical coefficients evaluated using known masses and phase-space integrals.

## Key numbers
- The threshold energy for the inverse process e⁻ + ⁴He → ³H + n + νₑ is Eₜₕ ≈ 21.167 MeV.
- The Q-value (mass difference) for the hep reaction is Δ = mₚ + m_³He − m_⁴He = 19.284 MeV.
- The theoretical S-factor used in the Standard Solar Model is S₀(hep) = 2.3 × 10⁻²⁰ keV·b, with individual calculations giving S₁ = 1.44 × 10⁻²⁰ keV·b and S₂ = 3.14 × 10⁻²⁰ keV·b depending on the NN potential.
- The predicted total flux of hep neutrinos in the SSM is Φ(hep) = 2.1 × 10³ cm⁻² s⁻¹.
- If S(hep) = 20 S₀(hep), then at Eₑ − Eₜₕ ≈ 10 MeV the cross section of the inverse process is σ ≈ 4.0 × 10⁻⁴⁵ cm².
- The numerical relation derived is σ(e⁻ ⁴He → ³H n νₑ) ≈ 0.62 × 10⁻⁵⁰ cm² × ((Eₑ − Eₜₕ)/mₑ)⁷/² × (S(hep)/S₀(hep)).
- The reduced mass of the n-³H system is 704.1 MeV; of the p-³He system, 703.3 MeV.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing problem that the hep reaction cross section at solar energies (≲30 keV) is too small to measure directly in the laboratory, as noted by Bahcall and collaborators. Previous detailed calculations by Carlson and collaborators and by Schiavilla and collaborators gave S-factor values differing by more than a factor of two, with strong cancellations between one-body and two-body meson-exchange currents. The Super-Kamiokande experiment (504-day and 825-day data) found that fits allowing S(hep) as a free parameter gave S(hep)/S₀(hep) = 26 and 16 respectively, suggesting a possible enhancement. The proposed inverse reaction avoids the Coulomb penetration factor that suppresses charged-particle cross sections at low energy, and the authors suggest that microtron facilities with high-intensity electron beams could measure it, enabling an experimental determination of S(hep) that is currently available only from model-dependent nuclear theory.
