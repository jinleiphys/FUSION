# Calculating Track Thrust with Track Functions

> In e+e- event shapes studies at LEP, two different measurements were sometimes performed: a "calorimetric" measurement using both charged and neutral particles, and a "track-based" measurement using just charged particles. Whereas calorimetric measurements are infrared and collinear safe and therefore calculable in perturbative QCD, track-based measurements necessarily depend on non-perturbative hadronization effects. On the other hand, track-based measurements typically have smaller experimental uncertainties. In this paper, we present the first calculation of the event shape track thrust and compare to measurements performed at ALEPH and DELPHI. This calculation is made possible through the recently developed formalism of track functions, which are non-perturbative objects describing how energetic partons fragment into charged hadrons. By incorporating track functions into soft-collinear effective theory, we calculate the distribution for track thrust with next-to-leading logarithmic resummation. Due to a partial cancellation between non-perturbative parameters, the distributions for calorimeter thrust and track thrust are remarkably similar, a feature also seen in LEP data.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of the track thrust event shape distribution using the recently developed track function formalism, showing that calorimeter thrust and track thrust distributions are remarkably similar due to a partial cancellation between non-perturbative parameters, a feature also observed in LEP data.

## Method

The authors incorporated track functions, which describe how energetic partons fragment into charged hadrons, into soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to calculate the track thrust distribution at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy including O(alpha_s) fixed-order matching contributions (NLL' order). Track functions were extracted from Pythia 8 Monte Carlo event generator samples of pure quark and gluon jets, and the leading non-perturbative power correction was included through parameters Omega_1^tau and bar-Omega_1^tau for calorimeter and track thrust respectively.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy Q = 91 GeV (Z pole) for LEP measurements at ALEPH and DELPHI
- The exponent Delta that redistributes cross section between peak and tail regions equals Delta = (2 alpha_s C_F / pi) * (g_1^L - qL) with C_F = 4/3, and |Delta| is approximately 0.004
- The value of alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.125 was used for calculations
- The kinematic endpoint for three-parton thrust is tau = 1/3
- The NLL cumulative distribution approximation gives bar-Sigma(bar-tau^c) approximately equal to Sigma(bar-tau^c) times (3 bar-tau^c)^Delta

## Context

This work extends the track function formalism introduced by Chang, Procura, Thaler, and Waalewijn (2013) to include logarithmic resummation, enabling the first perturbative QCD calculation of track thrust. The paper compares results to measurements from ALEPH (Buskulic 1992) and DELPHI (Abreu 1996), which are the only two experiments with public data on track thrust. The formalism enables future calculations of other track-based observables at colliders like the LHC, where tracking information is used to improve jet measurements and mitigate pileup effects.
