# Enhanced Schiff and magnetic quadrupole moments in deformed nuclei and their connection to the search for axion dark matter

> Deformed nuclei possess enhanced moments violating time reversal invariance ($T$) and parity ($P$). Collective magnetic quadrupole moments (MQM) appear in nuclei with a quadrupole deformation (which have ordinary $T$,$P$-conserving collective electric quadrupole moments). Nuclei with an octupole deformation have a collective electric octupole moment, electric dipole moment (EDM), Schiff moment and MQM in the intrinsic frame which rotates with the nucleus. In a state with definite angular momentum in the laboratory frame, these moments are forbidden by $T$ and $P$ conservation, meaning their expectation values vanish due to nuclear rotation. However, nuclei with an octupole deformation have doublets of close opposite parity rotational states with the same spin, which are mixed by $T$,$P$-violating nuclear forces. This mixing polarises the orientation of the nuclear axis along the nuclear spin, and all moments existing in the intrinsic frame appear in the laboratory frame (provided the nuclear spin $I$ is sufficiently large to allow such a moment). Such a mechanism produces enhanced $T$,$P$-violating nuclear moments. This enhancement also takes place in nuclei with a soft octupole vibration mode. In this paper we present updated estimates for the enhanced Schiff moment in isotopes of Eu, Sm, Gd, Dy, Er, Fr, Rn, Ac, Ra, Th, Pa, U, Np and Pu in terms of the CP-violating $π$-meson--nucleon interaction constants $\bar{g}_{0},\bar{g}_{1}$ and $\bar{g}_{2}$, the QCD parameter $\barθ$ and the quark chromo-EDMs. The implications of the enhanced $T$,$P$-violating moments to the search for axion dark matter in solid state experiments are also discussed, with potential alternative candidate compounds in which we may expect enhanced effects suggested.

## Key claim

Deformed nuclei with octupole deformation or soft octupole vibration modes can produce enhanced Schiff moments and magnetic quadrupole moments that violate time-reversal and parity symmetries, but only when the nuclear spin I is sufficiently large to allow such moments to appear in the laboratory frame after mixing of opposite-parity rotational doublets by T,P-violating nuclear forces.

## Method

The authors used analytical expressions from Spevak (1997) to estimate Schiff moments in terms of the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂, octupole deformation parameter β₃, nuclear spin I, and the energy splitting E₊ − E₋ between opposite-parity doublet states. They extracted β₂ and β₃ values from the tabulation of Möller (2016) for ground states of nearly all nuclei, and for nuclei without static octupole deformation they estimated β₃² from collective B(E3) octupole transition probabilities of neighboring even-even nuclei. Energy splittings of octupole doublets were taken from the nuclear database nudat3.

## Key numbers

* K_S = 0.99 for ¹⁵³Eu, 2.4 for ¹⁵³Sm, 1.2 for ¹⁵⁵Gd, 4.3 for ¹⁶¹Dy, 0.31 for ¹⁶³Dy, 1.8 for ¹⁶⁵Er, 0.85 for ²²¹Fr, 1.2 for ²²³Fr, 1.7 for ²²³Rn, 5.5 for ²²⁵Ac, 2.4 for ²²⁵Ra, 6.0 for ²²⁷Ac, 1.2 for ²²⁹Th, 0.44 for ²³³U, 1.9 for ²³⁵U, 2.6 for ²³⁷Np, and 0.2 for ²³⁹Pu (dimensionless enhancement factor relative to ²²⁵Ra normalization)
* K_S = 560 (or 2040 if the 60 eV doublet in ²²⁹Pa is confirmed) for ²²⁹Pa
* Total enhancement factor K_Tot relative to ²⁰⁷Pb ranges from 23.3 (¹⁶³Dy) to 197000 (²²⁹Pa), with values including 61.6 (¹⁵³Eu), 318 (¹⁶¹Dy), 690 (²²⁵Ra), 1710 (²²⁵Ac), 1880 (²²⁷Ac), and 708 (²³⁵U)
* The Schiff moment of ²²⁵Ra expressed as S(²²⁵Ra,g) ≈ (−2.6gḡ₀ + 12.9gḡ₁ − 6.9gḡ₂) e·fm³, and S(²²⁵Ra,θ̄) ≈ −θ̄ e·fm³
* The Schiff moment of ²⁰⁷Pb (approximated equal to ¹⁹⁹Hg) is S(²⁰⁷Pb,θ) ≈ 0.005 θ̄ e·fm³

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Auerbach (1996), Spevak (1997), Engel (2003), and Dobaczewski (2018) who performed numerical Schiff moment calculations for a limited set of short-lived nuclei including ²²¹Fr, ²²³Fr, ²²³Ra, ²²⁵Ra, and ²²⁵Ac. The present paper updates and expands the candidate list to include stable and long-lived isotopes such as ¹⁵³Eu, ¹⁵⁵Gd, ¹⁶¹Dy, ¹⁶³Dy, ²²⁹Th, ²³³U, ²³⁵U, ²³⁷Np, and ²³⁹Pu as originally suggested by Feldmeier (2020), using consistent β₂ and β₃ parameters from a single source (Möller 2016). The results enable solid-state experiments searching for axion dark matter (such as CASPEr) to consider alternative compounds with enhanced nuclear moments, potentially improving sensitivity over the current Pb-based samples used by Mukhamedjanov (2005) and Bouchard (2008).
