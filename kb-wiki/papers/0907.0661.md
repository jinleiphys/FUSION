# The N* Physics Program at Jefferson Lab

> Recent measurements of nucleon resonance transition form factors with CLAS at Jefferson Lab are discussed. The new data confirm the assertion of the symmetric constituent quark model of the Roper as the first radial excitation of the nucleon. For the first time, the longitudinal transition amplitude to the S11(1535) was extracted from the data. New results on the transition amplitudes for the D13(1520) resonance are presented showing a rapid transition from helicity 3/2 dominance seen at the real photon point to helicity 1/2 dominance at higher Q2. I also discuss aspects of the JLab program to search for new excited nucleon states predicted by quark models.

# The N* Physics Program at Jefferson Lab

## Key claim
The CLAS data confirm that the Roper resonance \(P_{11}(1440)\) is the first radial excitation of the nucleon, as predicted by the symmetric constituent quark model, but only at high \(Q^2\); the low-\(Q^2\) behavior is not well described by light-front quark models, indicating that meson-baryon interactions at large distances may be missing.

## Method
The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab measured exclusive electroproduction of single pions (\(n\pi^+\), \(p\pi^0\)), \(\eta\) mesons, and kaons over a wide range in photon virtuality \(Q^2\) and hadronic mass \(W\). Transition amplitudes for nucleon resonances were extracted from differential cross sections and polarized beam asymmetry data using fixed-t dispersion relations and the unitary isobar model. For strangeness channels, beam-target and beam-recoil polarization observables were measured using linearly and circularly polarized photons and polarized targets (FROST, HD-Ice).

## Key numbers
- The magnetic transition form factor \(G_M^\Delta\) for the \(N\Delta(1232)\) transition drops much faster with \(Q^2\) than the elastic form factor \(G_m^p\), which follows the dipole form \(G_D\).
- \(R_{EM} \equiv E_{1+}/M_{1+}\) for the \(N\Delta(1232)\) transition remains negative, small, and nearly constant over \(0 < Q^2 < 6\) GeV\(^2\).
- The transverse amplitude \(A_{1/2}\) for the Roper \(P_{11}(1440)\) changes sign near \(Q^2 = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\); at \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) it has about the same magnitude but opposite sign as at \(Q^2 = 0\).
- The branching ratios for the \(S_{11}(1535)\) were adjusted to \(\beta_{N\pi} = 0.45\) and \(\beta_{N\eta} = 0.45\) to bring \(p\eta\) and \(n\pi^+\) data into agreement.
- The helicity asymmetry for the \(D_{13}(1520)\) shows a rapid transition from helicity 3/2 dominance at the real photon point to helicity 1/2 dominance at higher \(Q^2\).
- The spin transfer from circularly polarized photons to the \(\Lambda\) hyperon in \(K^+\Lambda\) production is consistent with \(R^2 = 1\) (complete polarization) throughout the measured region.
- A candidate \(P_{13}(1900)\) state is required to fit combined CLAS \(K^+\Lambda\) cross section and spin transfer data; it is listed as a 2-star candidate in the 2008 Review of Particle Physics.

## Context
This work extends the program of nucleon resonance studies using electromagnetic probes, building on earlier measurements at MAMI, Bates, and LEGS for the \(N\Delta(1232)\) transition. The Roper results rule out the hybrid baryon model of Li and Burkert (2000) and the meson-cloud model of Cano et al. (2000), while supporting the symmetric constituent quark model of Isgur (1978) and Capstick (1992). The \(D_{13}(1520)\) helicity flip confirms a prediction of Close (1979). The search for new states in strangeness channels, including the \(P_{13}(1900)\) candidate, provides evidence against the quark-diquark model of Santopinto (2005) and enables future complete measurements with polarized targets (FROST, HD-Ice) to resolve ambiguities in partial-wave analyses.
