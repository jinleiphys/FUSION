# Cross sections and beam asymmetries for $\vev{e}p \to enπ^+$ in the nucleon resonance region for $1.7 \le Q^2 \le 4.5 (GeV)^2$

> The exclusive electroproduction process $\vec{e}p \to e^\prime n π^+$ was measured in the range of the photon virtuality $Q^2 = 1.7 - 4.5 \rm{GeV^2}$, and the invariant mass range for the $nπ^+$ system of $W = 1.15 - 1.7 \rm{GeV}$ using the CEBAF Large Acceptance Spectrometer. For the first time, these kinematics are probed in exclusive $π^+$ production from protons with nearly full coverage in the azimuthal and polar angles of the $nπ^+$ center-of-mass system. The $nπ^+$ channel has particular sensitivity to the isospin 1/2 excited nucleon states, and together with the $pπ^0$ final state will serve to determine the transition form factors of a large number of resonances. The largest discrepancy between these results and present modes was seen in the $σ_{LT'}$ structure function. In this experiment, 31,295 cross section and 4,184 asymmetry data points were measured. Because of the large volume of data, only a reduced set of structure functions and Legendre polynomial moments can be presented that are obtained in model-independent fits to the differential cross sections.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first measurement of exclusive \(\pi^+\) electroproduction from protons with nearly full angular coverage in the \(n\pi^+\) center-of-mass system for \(Q^2\) between 1.7 and 4.5 GeV\(^2\) and \(W\) between 1.15 and 1.7 GeV, but only a reduced set of structure functions and Legendre polynomial moments obtained from model-independent fits can be presented due to the large volume of data (31,295 cross section and 4,184 asymmetry data points).

## Method
The exclusive electroproduction process \(\vec{e}p \to e^\prime n\pi^+\) was measured using the CEBAF Large Acceptance Spectrometer (CLAS) at Jefferson Lab with a 5.754 GeV polarized electron beam incident on a liquid-hydrogen target. The scattered electron and outgoing \(\pi^+\) were detected in coincidence, while the final-state neutron was reconstructed via missing-mass constraints using four-momentum conservation. Differential cross sections and polarized electron beam asymmetries were extracted and compared with model calculations including the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) model, several versions of the MAID model, the Sato-Lee (SL) model, and a unitary isobar model (UIM) developed by the Yerevan-JLab group.

## Key numbers
- Photon virtuality range: \(Q^2 = 1.7 - 4.5\) GeV\(^2\)
- Invariant mass range for the \(n\pi^+\) system: \(W = 1.15 - 1.7\) GeV
- Incident electron beam energy: 5.754 GeV
- Average beam polarization: 72.7%
- Total data points measured: 31,295 cross section points and 4,184 asymmetry data points
- Beam helicity switched at a rate of 30 sec\(^{-1}\)
- Instantaneous luminosity: \(10^{34}\) cm\(^{-2}\) sec\(^{-1}\)
- Accidental coincidence contributions: \(< 10^{-3}\)

## Context
This work extends previous measurements of exclusive \(\pi^+\) electroproduction that were limited in angular coverage and statistical accuracy (Breuker et al.) and provides the first nearly full angular coverage in the resonance region at these \(Q^2\) values. The \(n\pi^+\) channel is particularly sensitive to isospin 1/2 excited nucleon states, and together with the \(p\pi^0\) final state will enable determination of transition form factors for many resonances, including the \(N(1440)\), \(N(1520)\), \(N(1535)\), and \(N(1680)\) states. The largest discrepancy between these results and existing models was observed in the \(\sigma_{LT'}\) structure function, and the data will allow tests of constituent quark model predictions (e.g., Close and Gilman, Capstick and Keister) and lattice QCD calculations (Alexandrou et al., 2004, 2005) for the \(\gamma^* N \Delta\) transition form factors.
