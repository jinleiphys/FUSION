# Parity nonconserving proton-proton elastic scattering

> The parity nonconserving longitudinal analyzing power A_L is calculated in elastic pp scattering at the energies below the approximate inelastic region T_lab = 350 MeV. The short-ranged heavy meson rho and omega exchanges as well as the longer-ranged two pion exchanges are considered as the mediators of the parity nonconserving interactions. The DDH "best" coupling values are used as the parity nonconserving meson-NN couplings. Also three different parity nonconserving two-pion exchange potentials by various authors are compared.

# Parity nonconserving proton-proton elastic scattering

## Key claim

The two-pion exchange (TPE) contribution to the parity nonconserving longitudinal analyzing power in elastic pp scattering is about twice as large as the heavy meson (rho and omega) exchange contribution throughout the energy range from 1 to 350 MeV, when using the DDH "best" coupling values and the NPI TPE potential. However, the result is model-dependent: different TPE potentials and regularization schemes can change the magnitude and even the sign of the TPE contribution, and the true value of the weak pion-nucleon coupling h_pi^(1) remains uncertain.

## Method

The parity nonconserving longitudinal analyzing power A_L in elastic pp scattering was calculated using the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) and the optical theorem, with the Reid93 potential as the strong interaction input. The PNC potential included short-ranged rho and omega exchanges via the DDH potential with their "best" coupling values, and long-ranged two-pion exchange contributions from three different potentials: NPI (time-ordered perturbation theory), K (chiral perturbation theory), and DHAL (chiral perturbation theory with only NN intermediate states). The calculations included parity-admixed partial waves up to total angular momentum J=4, and both scattering and transmission asymmetries were evaluated with Coulomb effects treated.

## Key numbers

- Experimental data points for A_L: Bonn at 13.6 MeV: (-0.93 ± 0.21) × 10^{-7}; PSI at 45 MeV: (-1.50 ± 0.22) × 10^{-7}; TRIUMF at 221.3 MeV: (0.84 ± 0.29) × 10^{-7}
- DDH "best" weak coupling values used: h_rho^{pp} = -15.48 × 10^{-7}, h_omega^{pp} = -3.00 × 10^{-7}, h_pi^(1) = 4.6 × 10^{-7}
- Strong coupling constants: g_rho = 2.79, g_omega = 8.37; anomalous magnetic moments: chi_rho = 3.71, chi_omega = -0.12
- Heavy meson masses: m_rho = 770 MeV, m_omega = 782 MeV; average nucleon mass M = 939 MeV
- Cut-off masses for dipole form factors: Lambda_rho = 1.3 GeV, Lambda_omega = 1.5 GeV
- The ^1S_0-^3P_0 contribution vanishes at 224.7 MeV due to cancellation of partial wave phase shifts
- TPE effect is about twice as large as heavy meson exchange effect throughout the 1-350 MeV energy range
- The NPI TPE potential with DDH model gives an excellent match with experimental data
- The K TPE potential with dipole form factor and Lambda = 1.0 GeV cut-off gives nearly consistent results
- The DHAL TPE potential can exhibit a different sign when dipole form factor is used

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channels calculation (Partanen, Niskanen, Iqbal, 2011) that included NDelta(1232) intermediate states and TPE effects, which had cast doubt on the straightforward interpretation of the TRIUMF experiment's determination of the weak rho pp coupling. It compares three different PNC TPE potentials: the NPI potential from the authors' earlier work, the chiral perturbation theory potential by Kaiser (2007), and the DHAL potential by Desplanques (2007) which includes only NN intermediate states. The results show that TPE contributions are significant and should not be ignored in PNC pp scattering, contrary to the simpler single-meson exchange picture that had been the standard approach since the DDH potential (Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein, 1980). The model is also used in calculations of cold neutron spin rotation and gamma-asymmetry in np -> gamma d, where TPE reduces the one-pion exchange effect by about 10%. The ongoing NPDGamma experiment is expected to help determine the true value of h_pi^(1).
