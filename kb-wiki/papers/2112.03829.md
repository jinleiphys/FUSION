# Local variations of charge radii for nuclei with even $Z$ from 84 to 120

> Pronounced changes of nuclear charge radii provide a stringent benchmark on the theoretical models and play a vital role in recognizing various nuclear phenomena. In this work, the systematic evolutions of nuclear charge radii along even $Z$=84-120 isotopic chains are firstly investigated by the recently developed new ansatz under the covariant density functional. The calculated results show that the shell closure effects of nuclear charge radii are remarkably shown at the neutron numbers $N=126$ and 184. Interestingly, the arch-like shapes of charge radii between these two strong neutron closed shells are naturally observed. Across the $N=184$ shell closure, the abrupt increase in charge radii is still evidently emerged. In addition, the rapid raise of nuclear charge radii from the neutron numbers $N=138$ to $N=144$ is also disclosed clearly in superheavy regions due to the enhanced shape deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a recently developed ansatz under covariant density functional theory, which adds a semi-microscopic correction term to the charge radius formula, can reproduce local variations in nuclear charge radii for even-Z isotopes from Z=84 to 120, including shell closure effects at N=126 and N=184 and arch-like shapes between them. The hedge is that the modified term cannot be derived self-consistently from conventional density functional theories, and its underlying physical meaning requires further discussion, though its predictive power is non-negligible.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with BCS pairing (RMF(BCS)) and a modified version (RMF(BCS)*) that adds a term proportional to |Δ𝒟|/√A to the mean-square charge radius formula, where Δ𝒟 originates from differences in Cooper pair components between neutrons and protons. They solved the Dirac equations self-consistently with an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis, constraining the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂₀ from -0.50 to 0.50 in steps of 0.01, using the NL3 effective force and a pairing strength V₀ = 322.8 MeV fm³ for all calculations.

## Key numbers

- The normalization parameter a₀ = 0.22 in the modified charge radius formula.
- The finite size correction for the proton is 0.64 fm² added to the point-proton charge radius squared.
- The pairing strength used for all calculations is V₀ = 322.8 MeV fm³.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter β₂₀ reaches values around 0.30 in the shape transition regions near N=140, 142, and 144 for superheavy nuclei.
- The empirical pairing energy gap scales roughly as 12/√A, and the modified term in the charge radius formula includes a similar 1/√A dependence.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ansatz (An 2020), which was applied to calcium, potassium, copper, and indium isotopes, to the superheavy region for the first time. It builds on covariant density functional theory (CDFT) as developed by Vretenar (2005) and others, and the results are compared with calculations from the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 functional (Zhang 2022). The study enables predictions of charge radii for superheavy nuclei up to Z=120, where experimental data are scarce due to short lifetimes and low production cross sections, and it identifies specific neutron numbers (N=138 to 144) where shape-phase transitions cause abrupt increases in charge radii, which can guide future experimental tests.
