# Chiral Magnetic Effect in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is a remarkable phenomenon that stems from highly nontrivial interplay of QCD chiral symmetry, axial anomaly, and gluonic topology. It is of fundamental importance to search for the CME in experiments. The heavy ion collisions provide a unique environment where a hot chiral-symmetric quark-gluon plasma is created, gluonic topological fluctuations generate chirality imbalance, and very strong magnetic fields $|\vec{\bf B}|\sim m_π^2$ are present during the early stage of such collisions. Significant efforts have been made to look for CME signals in heavy ion collision experiments. In this contribution we give a brief overview on the status of such efforts.

## Key claim

The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is a phenomenon that generates an electric current along an external magnetic field in a chiral-symmetric quark-gluon plasma with a chirality imbalance, but its experimental detection in heavy ion collisions remains complicated by sizable background contributions from elliptic-flow-driven effects that must be separated to extract a definitive signal.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations of the CME, including its origin from the interplay of QCD chiral symmetry, the axial anomaly, and gluonic topological fluctuations (instantons and sphalerons). It then surveys experimental efforts to detect the CME via charge-dependent azimuthal correlations of final-state hadrons, focusing on the gamma-correlator and delta-correlator observables, and discusses recent analyses that attempt to separate flow-driven backgrounds from the CME signal using methods such as event-by-event extraction and binning by elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength in heavy ion collisions is estimated as \(|\vec{\bf B}|\sim m_\pi^2\) during the early stage.
- The chiral symmetry restoration temperature is \(T_c\sim 165\rm MeV\) from lattice QCD.
- The extracted signal \(H\) from STAR Beam Energy Scan data shows a clear charge dependence with nontrivial trends in beam energy.
- The isobaric collision proposal compares \(^{96}_{44}Ru + ^{96}_{44}Ru\) and \(^{96}_{40}Zr + ^{96}_{40}Zr\), with \(\sim 10\%\) variation in magnetic field strength (i.e. \(\sim 20\%\) variation in CME signal).
- Anomalous hydrodynamic simulations at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV for Au+Au collisions show CME signals agreeing with data within a factor of two.
- The Chiral Magnetic Wave induces a linear dependence of flow splitting \(\Delta v_2 = v_2^{-} -v_2^{+}\) on event charge asymmetry \(A_{\rm ch}\), with the extracted slope parameter \(r\) roughly in agreement with CMW computations.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on anomalous transport in QCD matter, building on foundational papers such as Kharzeev (2004, 2007) for the CME and Son and Zhitnitsky (2004) for the Chiral Separation Effect. It enables future quantitative modeling of CME signals using anomalous hydrodynamics and chiral kinetic theory, and motivates upcoming isobaric collision experiments at RHIC to definitively separate CME from background contributions.
