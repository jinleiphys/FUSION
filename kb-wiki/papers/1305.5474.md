# Production of exotic hypernuclei and hyper-matter

> Pioneering experiments on production of hypernuclei can be performed with nuclotron beams on fixed targets, and at the future NICA facility. The peripheral collisions of relativistic ions are very promising for searching mutli-strange and exotic hypernuclei which are not easy accessible with other experimental methods. In these experiments one can also get information on the Equation of State of hyper-matter around nuclear saturation density at low and moderate temperatures.

# Production of exotic hypernuclei and hyper-matter

## Key claim
Peripheral collisions of relativistic ions at the Nuclotron and future NICA facility can produce multi-strange and exotic hypernuclei, including weakly bound systems like Λ-neutron, that are not accessible via traditional hypernuclear methods. The feasibility depends on the capture of hyperons by spectator residues at moderate excitation energies (T ≈ 1–10 MeV), followed by statistical disintegration.

## Method
The author uses a hybrid approach combining a dynamical transport stage (Dubna cascade model, DCM, and UrQMD) to simulate hyperon production, propagation, and capture by spectator residues in peripheral relativistic ion collisions, with a subsequent statistical stage (generalized Fermi-break-up model) describing the decay of excited hyper-spectators into final hypernuclei. Calculations are performed for systems such as 6Li (2 A GeV) + 12C, p + Au, and Au + Au at beam energies of 2 and 20 A GeV, as well as at the proposed Nuclotron energy of 4.5 A GeV.

## Key numbers
- In the 6Li (2 A GeV) + 12C reaction, the observed production of 3ΛH is about a factor of three larger than that of 4ΛH.
- For p + Au at 2 GeV, the fraction of excited spectator residues with one captured Λ is around 0.1% of the total yield; at 20 GeV this rises to a few percent.
- At the Nuclotron beam energy of 4.5 A GeV, the estimated probability of one Λ capture is about 0.3% for p + Au and more than 1% for Au + Au collisions.
- The probability for capturing two Λs is nearly two orders of magnitude lower than for one Λ, and the probability for three Λs is around 10−6.
- The hyper-spectator systems are formed at low and moderate temperatures (T ≲ 10 MeV).

## Context
This work extends earlier experimental demonstrations of hypernucleus production in peripheral ion collisions, such as the LBL experiments (Nield, 1976) and JINR experiments (Avramenko, 1988), as well as recent results from the HypHI collaboration at GSI (Saito, 2012) showing production of light hypernuclei from 2 A GeV 6Li on 12C. The proposed mechanism enables the study of multi-strange hypernuclei beyond S = −2 and the Equation of State of hyper-matter at nuclear saturation density, which is relevant for neutron star cores (Schaffner, 1996) and complements traditional hypernuclear methods that are mostly limited to S = −1 systems in ground or weakly-excited states.
