# Dynamical approach to spectator fragmentation in Au+Au reactions at 35 MeV/A

> The characteristics of fragment emission in peripheral $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions 35 MeV/A are studied using the two clusterization approaches within framework of \emph{quantum molecular dynamics} model. Our model calculations using \emph{minimum spanning tree} (MST) algorithm and advanced clusterization method namely \emph{simulated annealing clusterization algorithm} (SACA) showed that fragment structure can be realized at an earlier time when spectators contribute significantly toward the fragment production even at such a low incident energy. Comparison of model predictions with experimental data reveals that SACA method can nicely reproduce the fragment charge yields and mean charge of the heaviest fragment. This reflects suitability of SACA method over conventional clusterization techniques to investigate spectator matter fragmentation in low energy domain.

# Dynamical approach to spectator fragmentation in Au+Au reactions at 35 MeV/A

## Key claim
The simulated annealing clusterization algorithm (SACA) can reproduce experimental fragment charge yields and mean charge of the heaviest fragment in peripheral Au+Au collisions at 35 MeV/A, whereas the conventional minimum spanning tree (MST) algorithm fails to break spectator matter into smaller fragments even at 300 fm/c. The SACA method allows identification of fragment structure at an earlier time (around 100 fm/c) when spectators contribute significantly to fragment production, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of a soft equation of state (kappa=200 MeV) and standard energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Method
The authors simulated peripheral 197Au+197Au collisions at 35 MeV/A using the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model, which treats each nucleon as a Gaussian wave packet with fixed width sqrt(L)=1.08 fm, propagating under a Hamiltonian with Skyrme-type, Yukawa, and Coulomb interactions. Two clusterization algorithms were applied to the QMD phase space: the minimum spanning tree (MST) method, which groups nucleons with inter-nucleon distance less than 4 fm, and the simulated annealing clusterization algorithm (SACA), which uses a Metropolis algorithm with decreasing control parameter to find the most bound configuration subject to a binding energy check of -4.0 MeV/nucleon for fragments with N_f >= 3. Fragment observables were computed at six peripheral geometries and compared with experimental data from the Multics-Miniball array.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy: 35 MeV/A for 197Au+197Au collisions
- Gaussian width sqrt(L) = 1.08 fm
- MST clustering distance cutoff: 4 fm
- Binding energy threshold E_bind = 4.0 MeV for fragments with N_f >= 3 (E_bind = 0 otherwise)
- Soft equation of state with compressibility kappa = 200 MeV
- SACA method identifies asymptotic fragment sizes at approximately 100 fm/c; MST method fails to identify fragments even at 300 fm/c
- Impact parameter intervals studied: 0.5 < b/b_max <= 0.6 and 0.6 < b/b_max <= 0.7 for charge distributions; b/b_max = 0.55 for rapidity distributions
- Mean charge of heaviest fragment <Z_max> shows increasing trend with impact parameter, reproduced by SACA but overpredicted by MST

## Context
This work extends the application of the SACA method, previously successful at relativistic energies (ALADiN data at >= 400 MeV/A), to the low-energy domain around 35 MeV/A where mean-field dynamics dominate and nucleon-nucleon collisions are largely Pauli blocked. The study contradicts the assumption that conventional MST clusterization can describe spectator fragmentation at low energies, and instead supports the dynamical fragmentation scenario also favored by AMD model studies of Ca+Ca collisions at 35 MeV/A (Furutachi, 2009). The results enable further investigation of spectator matter fragmentation and the role of mid-velocity emission in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, and suggest SACA as a general improvement over spatial-correlation-based clusterization algorithms.
