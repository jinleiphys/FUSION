# Microscopic description of the pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich Ca isotopes

> We study the effects of the phonon-phonon coupling on the low-energy electric dipole response within a microscopic model based on an effective Skyrme interaction. The finite rank separable approach for the quasiparticle random phase approximation is used. Choosing as an example the isotopic chain of Calcium, we show the ability of the method to describe the low-energy $E1$ strength distribution. With one and the same set of parameters we describe available experimental data for $^{48}$Ca and predict the electric dipole strength function for $^{50}$Ca.

## Key claim

The phonon-phonon coupling (PPC) effect, treated within the finite rank separable approximation (FRSA) for the Skyrme-QRPA, is essential to reproduce the low-energy electric dipole strength in $^{48}$Ca, but only slightly affects the summed $E1$ strength below 10 MeV in $^{50}$Ca, where the main effect is fragmentation and energy shift.

## Method

The authors start from Hartree-Fock-BCS (HF-BCS) calculations using the SLy5 and SLy5+T Skyrme energy density functionals, with a zero-range volume pairing force. They then apply the finite rank separable approximation (FRSA) to the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) to compute one-phonon states, and extend the model by including phonon-phonon coupling (PPC) to mix one- and two-phonon configurations in the wave functions of $1^{-}$ states. The $E1$ transition matrix elements are calculated with effective neutron and proton charges to eliminate spurious center-of-mass contamination.

## Key numbers

- For $^{48}$Ca, the PPC calculation gives a total dipole strength $\sum B(E1) = 0.063$ e$^2$fm$^2$ for states below 10 MeV; the experimental value is $0.0687(75)$ e$^2$fm$^2$ (Hartmann 2004) and $0.080(8)$ e$^2$fm$^2$ (Derya 2014).
- For $^{48}$Ca, the energy-weighted sum rule fraction attributed to the PDR region is 0.28% of the TRK sum rule from the PPC calculation, compared to 0.33(4)% from experiment (Hartmann 2004).
- For $^{50}$Ca, the QRPA calculation gives $\sum B(E1) = 0.54$ e$^2$fm$^2$ below 10 MeV; the PPC calculation gives 0.58 e$^2$fm$^2$, while the QRPA result is 0.57 e$^2$fm$^2$.
- The one-neutron separation energy decreases by 64% in $^{50}$Ca compared to $^{48}$Ca.

## Context

This work extends the FRSA approach previously applied to giant resonances and low-energy excitations (Severyukhin 2004, 2012, 2017) to the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich Ca isotopes. It directly compares with experimental photon scattering data for $^{48}$Ca (Hartmann 2004, Derya 2014) and with relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) results (Egorova 2016), which give $\sum B(E1) = 0.1$ e$^2$fm$^2$ for $^{48}$Ca and 0.46 e$^2$fm$^2$ for $^{50}$Ca. The results enable predictions for the $E1$ strength function in $^{50}$Ca and demonstrate the role of the $N=28$ shell closure in suppressing the PDR strength, with a sharp increase after crossing to $N=30$.
