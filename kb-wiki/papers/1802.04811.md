# One-proton emission from the $^6_О›$Li hypernucleus

> One-proton (1p) radioactive emission under the influence of the $О›^0$-hyperon inclusion is discussed. I investigate the hyper-1p emitter, $^6_О›$Li, with a time-dependent three-body model. Two-body interactions for $О±$-proton and $О±$-$О›^0$ subsystems are determined consistently to their resonant and bound energies, respectively. For a proton-$О›^0$ subsystem, a contact interaction, which can be linked to the vacuum-scattering length of the proton-$О›^0$ scattering, is employed. A noticeable sensitivity of the 1p-emission observables to the scattering length of the proton-$О›^0$ interaction is shown. The $О›^0$-hyperon inclusion leads to a remarkable fall of the 1p-resonance energy and width from the hyperon-less $О±$-proton resonance. For some empirical values of the proton-$О›^0$ scattering length, the 1p-resonance width is suggested to be of the order of $0.1-0.01$ MeV. Thus, the 1p emission from $^6_О›$Li may occur in the timescale of $10^{-20}-10^{-21}$ seconds, which is sufficiently shorter than the self-decay lifetime of $О›^0$, $10^{-10}$ seconds. By taking the spin-dependence of the proton-$О›^0$ interaction into account, a remarkable split of the $J^ПЂ=1^-$ and $2^-$ 1p-resonance states is predicted. It is also suggested that, if the spin-singlet proton-$О›^0$ interaction is sufficiently attractive, the 1p emission from the $1^-$ ground state is forbidden. From these results, I conclude that the 1p emission can be a suitable phenomenon to investigate the basic properties of the hypernuclear interaction, for which a direct measurement is still difficult.

## Context

This work extends studies of hypernuclear systems by Motoba (1983), Hiyama (1996, 2009, 2015), and Bely (2008) into the particle-unbound regime, where fewer investigations have been performed. The time-dependent method follows the approach previously applied to the two-proton emitter вЃ¶Be by Oishi (2014, 2017), and the results show that 1p emission from hypernuclei can serve as a probe of the hyperon-nucleon interaction, for which direct measurement remains difficult according to the review by Gal (2016) and Feliciello (2015). The predicted spin-dependent splitting of the 1вЃ» and 2вЃ» resonance states, with the possibility that sufficiently attractive spin-singlet proton-О› interaction forbids 1p emission from the 1вЃ» ground state, provides a testable signature for future experiments combining heavy-ion collisions with hypernuclear production as proposed by Rapp (2016) and Stein (2012).
