# One-proton emission from the $^6_Λ$Li hypernucleus

> One-proton (1p) radioactive emission under the influence of the $Λ^0$-hyperon inclusion is discussed. I investigate the hyper-1p emitter, $^6_Λ$Li, with a time-dependent three-body model. Two-body interactions for $α$-proton and $α$-$Λ^0$ subsystems are determined consistently to their resonant and bound energies, respectively. For a proton-$Λ^0$ subsystem, a contact interaction, which can be linked to the vacuum-scattering length of the proton-$Λ^0$ scattering, is employed. A noticeable sensitivity of the 1p-emission observables to the scattering length of the proton-$Λ^0$ interaction is shown. The $Λ^0$-hyperon inclusion leads to a remarkable fall of the 1p-resonance energy and width from the hyperon-less $α$-proton resonance. For some empirical values of the proton-$Λ^0$ scattering length, the 1p-resonance width is suggested to be of the order of $0.1-0.01$ MeV. Thus, the 1p emission from $^6_Λ$Li may occur in the timescale of $10^{-20}-10^{-21}$ seconds, which is sufficiently shorter than the self-decay lifetime of $Λ^0$, $10^{-10}$ seconds. By taking the spin-dependence of the proton-$Λ^0$ interaction into account, a remarkable split of the $J^π=1^-$ and $2^-$ 1p-resonance states is predicted. It is also suggested that, if the spin-singlet proton-$Λ^0$ interaction is sufficiently attractive, the 1p emission from the $1^-$ ground state is forbidden. From these results, I conclude that the 1p emission can be a suitable phenomenon to investigate the basic properties of the hypernuclear interaction, for which a direct measurement is still difficult.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a Λ⁰ hyperon in ⁶Li causes a remarkable reduction of the one-proton resonance energy and width compared to the hyperon-less α-proton system, with the 1p-emission observables showing noticeable sensitivity to the proton-Λ scattering length; however, whether 1p emission from the ground state actually occurs depends on whether the vacuum scattering length is less than approximately -3.1 fm (for Jπ=1⁻) or -3.3 fm (for Jπ=2⁻), and a large experimental ambiguity of order 0.1-1 MeV remains in the proton separation energy of ⁶ΛLi.

## Method

A time-dependent three-body model with α core, valence proton, and Λ hyperon was employed, using core-orbital coordinates and a Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon plus Coulomb potentials for the α-proton subsystem and a Woods-Saxon potential for the α-Λ subsystem, with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental α-nucleon resonances and the α-Λ binding energy of ⁵ΛHe (-3.12 MeV). The proton-Λ interaction was treated as a contact interaction linked to the vacuum scattering length, and the initial state for time evolution was prepared using a confining-potential procedure to match the expected Q-value of approximately 0.6 MeV for 1p emission.

## Key numbers

- The α-proton resonance in the p₃/₂ channel has energy E_α-p = 1.69 MeV and width Γ_α-p = 1.31 MeV (this work), compared to experimental values of 1.69 MeV and 1.06 MeV from Till (2002)
- The α-Λ binding energy in ⁵ΛHe is ε(s₁/₂) = -3.12 MeV, reproduced from experimental data of Gal (1975)
- For a_v = -1.6 fm, the three-body energy E_3b = -2.52 MeV (Jπ=1⁻) and -2.49 MeV (Jπ=2⁻), corresponding to Q_1p ≈ 0.6 MeV
- The 1p-resonance width is approximately 0.1 MeV for a_v = -1.6 fm, and decreases to approximately 0.01-0.1 MeV for other empirical scattering length values
- The 1p emission timescale is 10⁻²⁰-10⁻²¹ seconds, which is shorter than the Λ⁰ self-decay lifetime of 10⁻¹⁰ seconds
- The 1p emission is forbidden when a_v ≤ -3.1 fm for Jπ=1⁻ or a_v ≤ -3.3 fm for Jπ=2⁻
- With spin-dependent interaction, case I (a_v^(s) = -2.2 fm, a_v^(t) = -1.7 fm) gives Q_1p = +0.29 MeV for Jπ=1⁻ and +0.53 MeV for Jπ=2⁻
- With spin-dependent interaction, case II (a_v^(s) = -2.5 fm, a_v^(t) = -1.7 fm) gives Q_1p = +0.22 MeV for Jπ=1⁻ and +0.53 MeV for Jπ=2⁻

## Context

This work extends studies of hypernuclear systems by Motoba (1983), Hiyama (1996, 2009, 2015), and Bely (2008) into the particle-unbound regime, where fewer investigations have been performed. The time-dependent method follows the approach previously applied to the two-proton emitter ⁶Be by Oishi (2014, 2017), and the results show that 1p emission from hypernuclei can serve as a probe of the hyperon-nucleon interaction, for which direct measurement remains difficult according to the review by Gal (2016) and Feliciello (2015). The predicted spin-dependent splitting of the 1⁻ and 2⁻ resonance states, with the possibility that sufficiently attractive spin-singlet proton-Λ interaction forbids 1p emission from the 1⁻ ground state, provides a testable signature for future experiments combining heavy-ion collisions with hypernuclear production as proposed by Rapp (2016) and Stein (2012).
