# Intrinsic Transverse Motion of the Pion's Valence Quarks

> Starting with the solution to the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion, in a beyond rainbow-ladder truncation to QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), we determine the pion's $l_z=0$ and $|l_z|=1$ leading Fock-state light-front wave functions (LFWFs) [labeled by $ψ_{l_z}(x,k_T^2)$]. The leading-twist time-reversal even transverse momentum dependent parton distribution function (TMD) of the pion is then directly obtained from these LFWFs. A key characteristic of the LFWFs, which is driven by dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, is that at typical hadronic scales they are broad functions in the light-cone momentum fraction $x$. The LFWFs have a non-trivial $(x,k_T^2)$ dependence and in general do not factorize into separate functions of each variable. The $l_z=0$ LFWF is concave with a maximum at $x=1/2$, whereas orbital angular momentum effects causes the $|l_z|=1$ LFWF to have a slight {\it double-humped} structure for quark transverse momentum in the range $0.5 \lesssim k_T^2 \lesssim 5\,$GeV$^2$. For $k_T^2 \lesssim 1\,$GeV$^2$ the $k_T^2$ dependence of the LFWFs is well described by a Gaussian, however for $k_T^2 \gtrsim 10\,$GeV$^2$ these LFWFs behave as $ψ_0 \propto x(1-x)/k_T^2$ and $ψ_1 \propto x(1-x)/k_T^4$, and therefore exhibit the power-law behavior predicted by perturbative QCD. The pion's TMD inherits many features from the LFWFs, where for $k_T^2 \lesssim 1\,$GeV$^2$ the $k_T^2$ dependence is well described by a Gaussian, and for large $k_T^2$ the TMD behaves as $f_π^q \propto x^2(1-x)^2/k_T^4$. At the model scale we find the average transverse momentum, defined by a Bessel-weighted moment with $b_T=0.3\,$fm, to equal $\langle k_T^2\rangle = 0.19\,$GeV$^2$. The TMD evolution of our result is studied using both the $b^*$ and $ζ$ prescriptions which allows a qualitative comparison with existing Drell-Yan data.

## Key claim

The pion's leading Fock-state light-front wave functions (LFWFs) and transverse momentum dependent parton distribution function (TMD) are computed from a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) solution that goes beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation, revealing that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) produces broad LFWFs in the light-cone momentum fraction x at low transverse momentum, with the TMD showing two distinct scaling regimes at large k_T^2 before reaching the asymptotic perturbative QCD behavior.

## Method

The authors solve the quark gap equation and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion using a DCSB-improved truncation that includes an anomalous chromomagnetic moment term in the dressed quark-gluon vertex. They compute k_T^2-dependent moments of the pion's l_z=0 and |l_z|=1 LFWFs from the Euclidean-space Bethe-Salpeter wave function, then reconstruct the full LFWFs from these moments. The leading-twist time-reversal even TMD is obtained directly from the LFWFs via the overlap representation, and TMD evolution is applied using both the b* and zeta prescriptions to enable comparison with Drell-Yan data.

## Key numbers

- Average transverse momentum at the model scale (mu_0 = 0.52 GeV): <k_T^2> = 0.19 GeV^2, defined by a Bessel-weighted moment with b_T = 0.3 fm
- Gaussian fit to the TMD for k_T^2 < 1 GeV^2 gives <k_T^2> = 0.16 GeV^2 at the model scale
- After TMD evolution to mu = 6 GeV: <k_T^2> = 0.69 GeV^2 (Gaussian fit) and <k_T^2> = 0.49 GeV^2 (Bessel-weighted with b_T = 0.3 fm)
- Model scale determined as mu_0 = 0.52 GeV by matching valence quark momentum fraction to Drell-Yan analysis (2<x>_v = 0.47(2) at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2) and lattice QCD (2<x>_v = 0.48(4) at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2)
- LFWFs exhibit Gaussian behavior for k_T^2 less than about 1 GeV^2 and power-law behavior for k_T^2 greater than about 10 GeV^2: psi_0 proportional to x(1-x)/k_T^2 and psi_1 proportional to x(1-x)/k_T^4
- TMD asymptotic behavior: f_pi^q proportional to x^2(1-x)^2/k_T^4 for k_T^2 greater than about 100 GeV^2
- The |l_z|=1 LFWF shows a double-humped structure for quark transverse momentum in the range 0.5 less than k_T^2 less than 5 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach to light-front wave functions pioneered by Mezrag (2016) and applies it to compute the pion's TMD for the first time from a DCSB-improved truncation. The results enable comparison with the E-615 pion-induced Drell-Yan experiment (Conway, 1989) and provide a framework for interpreting future measurements at the proposed electron-ion collider. The calculation also connects to light-front constituent quark model results (Pasquini, 2014) and holographic QCD models (Bacchetta, 2017), where the double-humped structure in the TMD is more prominent than found here.
