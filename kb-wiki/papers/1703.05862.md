# Yield ratios of identified hadrons in p+p, p+Pb, Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Yield ratios of identified hadrons observed in high multiplicity p+p and p+Pb collisions at LHC show remarkable similarity with those in Pb+Pb collisions, indicating some important and universal underlying dynamics in hadron production for different quark gluon final states. We use the quark combination model to explain the data of yield ratios in these three collision systems. The observed $p/π$ and $Λ/K_s^0$ can be reproduced simultaneously by quark combination, and these two ratios reflect the rate of baryon production at hadronization which is the same in light sector and strange sector and is roughly constant in p+p, p+Pb and Pb+Pb collision systems over three orders of magnitude in charged particle multiplicity.The data of $K_s^0/π$, $Λ/π$, $Ξ/π$ and $Ω/π$ show a hierarchy behavior relating to the strangeness content, and are naturally explained by quark combination both in the saturate stage at high multiplicity and in the increase stage at moderate multiplicity. Our results suggest that the dynamical characteristic of quark combination is necessary in describing the production of hadrons in small systems created in p+p and p+Pb collisions.

## Key claim
The quark combination model can simultaneously reproduce the observed $p/\pi$ and $\Lambda/K_s^0$ yield ratios in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, indicating that the baryon production probability at hadronization is roughly constant across these three collision systems over three orders of magnitude in charged particle multiplicity, with the caveat that the baryon/meson competition factor $R_{B/M}$ shows a slight increase (less than 9%) as the total quark number (system size) decreases.

## Method
The authors applied a quark combination model (QCM) to calculate hadronic yields at midrapidity in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, and p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV. The model takes as inputs the average rapidity densities of up, down, and strange quarks before hadronization, characterized by the total quark number $\langle \mathrm{d}N_q/\mathrm{d}y \rangle$ and the strangeness suppression factor $\lambda_s = \langle \mathrm{d}N_s/\mathrm{d}y \rangle / \langle \mathrm{d} N_u/\mathrm{d}y \rangle$, along with the baryon/meson competition factor $R_{B/M} \equiv \overline{N}_B/\overline{N}_M$. The yields of ground-state mesons and baryons in flavor SU(3) were computed including strong and electromagnetic decays, with quark number fluctuations approximated by Poisson distributions.

## Key numbers
- In Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, the strangeness suppression factor $\lambda_s$ saturates at 0.405-0.412 for central to semi-central collisions (0-50% centrality) and decreases to 0.35 in peripheral (80-90%) collisions.
- The baryon/meson competition factor $R_{B/M}$ ranges from 1/11.9 in central Pb+Pb (0-5%) to 1/11.2 in peripheral Pb+Pb (80-90%), and is 1/11.0 across all p+Pb centralities.
- The $p/\pi$ ratio is approximately 0.048-0.054 and the $\Lambda/K_s^0$ ratio is approximately 0.25-0.28 across all three collision systems, as derived from the analytic approximations with $\lambda_s \sim 0.34-0.42$ and $R_{B/M} \sim 1/11-1/12$.
- The total quark number $\langle \mathrm{d}N_q/\mathrm{d}y \rangle$ at midrapidity ranges from 1720 in central Pb+Pb (0-5%) to 15 in peripheral Pb+Pb (80-90%), and from 52 in central p+Pb (0-5%) to 4.7 in peripheral p+Pb (80-100%).

## Context
This work extends the quark combination model, previously applied to $e^+e^-$ annihilations and relativistic heavy ion collisions (e.g., by Greco (2003), Fries (2003), and Hwa (2003)), to small collision systems at LHC energies. The results address the inability of popular event generators such as PYTHIA8, EPOS, DIPSY, PHOJET, and HERWIG to consistently explain the observed yield ratios, as reported by the ALICE collaboration (2016). The findings suggest that quark combination dynamics are necessary for describing hadron production in p+p and p+Pb collisions, and enable further study of universal hadronization mechanisms across system sizes spanning three orders of magnitude in multiplicity.
