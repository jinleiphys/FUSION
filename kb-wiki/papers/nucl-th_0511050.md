# Collective Rotation of Tetrahedral Nuclei in the Cranking Model

> The three-dimensional cranking model is used to investigate the microscopic aspects of the rotation of nuclei with the tetrahedral symmetry. Two classes of rotation axes are studied corresponding to two different discrete symmetries of the rotating hamiltonian. Self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations show that the tetrahedral minimum remains remarkably stable until the first single-particle crossing.

## Key claim

The tetrahedral minimum in nuclei remains remarkably stable as a function of rotational frequency, with practically no quadrupole polarization, at least up to the first single-particle crossing; this stability is demonstrated for two specific families of rotation axes (edge and tip axes) in self-consistent calculations that include pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors performed fully-microscopic three-dimensional cranking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the code hfodd (v2.17k) for the nucleus 110Zr, employing the SLy4 Skyrme force in the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta interaction in the particle-particle channel. Two classes of rotation axes were studied: the edge axis (passing through the middle of the tetrahedron edge) and the tip axis (passing through the tetrahedron tip), with symmetry-constrained calculations for the edge axis and symmetry-unconstrained calculations for the tip axis. The evolution of deformation parameters was tracked as a function of rotational frequency ω.

## Key numbers

- For the edge axis, the tetrahedral deformation α32 remained nearly constant as a function of rotational frequency ω, proving resilience of the tetrahedral symmetry to rotation.
- For the tip axis, the α33 deformation showed little deviation from the exact symmetry limit value (where cos²β = 1/3), with the deviation increasing only slightly with ω while remaining very small.
- Previous macroscopic-microscopic calculations (without pairing) suggested that at low rotational frequencies up to ω ~ 0.3 MeV/ℏ, no particular axis of rotation is favored.

## Context

This work extends earlier macroscopic-microscopic studies by Dudek et al. (2002) and Przemek et al. that predicted stable tetrahedral minima but assumed fixed deformation and omitted pairing correlations. The present fully-microscopic HFB results confirm the earlier findings and show that the tetrahedral minimum survives the increase of angular momentum, enabling the possibility of observing rotational bands in tetrahedral nuclei. The near-zero quadrupole polarization implies strongly hindered stretched-E2 transitions for any eventual rotational bands, which is a key signature for experimental searches.
