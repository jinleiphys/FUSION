# Relativistic descriptions of quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering: application to scaling and superscaling ideas

> The analysis of the recent experimental data on charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections measured at MiniBooNE requires fully relativistic theoretical descriptions also accounting for the role of final state interactions. In this work we evaluate inclusive quasielastic differential neutrino cross sections within the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation. Results based on the relativistic mean field potential are compared with the ones corresponding to the relativistic Green function approach. An analysis of scaling and superscaling properties provided by both models is also presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic descriptions of final state interactions (FSI) via the relativistic mean field (RMF) potential and the relativistic Green function (RGF) approach produce significantly different predictions for charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections and scaling functions, with the differences increasing at higher momentum transfers; the authors qualify that both models reproduce electron scattering data well, but their divergence for neutrino scattering indicates that FSI treatment remains a key uncertainty.

## Method

The authors evaluate inclusive quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections on 12C within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA), using Dirac-Hartree bound state wave functions from a relativistic mean field Lagrangian with the NLSH parameterization. Final state interactions are described via two approaches: the RMF model, which uses the same real scalar and vector potentials for both bound and scattering states, and the RGF technique, which employs complex phenomenological optical potentials (EDAD1 and EDAI-12C parameterizations) and reconstructs flux into inelastic channels through a spectral representation of the single-particle Green function. The differential cross sections and scaling functions are computed for various neutrino energies and momentum transfers, and compared with the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) and real relativistic optical potential (rROP) results.

## Key numbers

- Muon mass: m = 105.7 MeV (used in kinematics)
- Fermi constant: G = 1.166 x 10^-5 GeV^-2
- Cabibbo angle: cos^2(theta_C) = 0.975
- Axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.267
- Axial mass: M_A = 1.03 GeV
- Incident neutrino energies studied: E_nu = 1000, 1500, and 2000 MeV at fixed muon scattering angle theta_mu = 45 degrees
- Momentum transfer values around the cross section peak: q = 700 MeV/c for E_nu = 1000 MeV, q = 1100 MeV/c for E_nu = 1500 MeV, and q = 1400 MeV/c for E_nu = 2000 MeV
- Fixed momentum transfer calculations performed at q = 500 and 1000 MeV/c for E_nu = 1000 MeV
- Energy range of the phenomenological optical potentials: up to 1040 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of electron-nucleus scattering (Meucci et al., 2007; Caballero et al., 2007) to charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering, applying the same RMF and RGF models that successfully described (e,e') data and their scaling properties. The study directly addresses the MiniBooNE CCQE data (2010), where the relativistic Fermi gas model underestimates cross sections unless the axial mass is artificially increased, suggesting that nuclear effects beyond the RFG are important. The comparison between RMF and RGF predictions enables assessment of FSI uncertainties in neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for neutrino oscillation experiments, and tests the universality of scaling functions between electromagnetic and weak interactions as proposed in the SuSA approach (Amaro et al., 2005).
