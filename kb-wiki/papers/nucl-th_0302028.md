# Anapole moment of an exotic nucleus

> We demonstrate that there is no appreciable enhancement of the anapole moment of $^{11}$Be. The effect of small energy intervals is compensated for by a small overlap of the halo neutron wave function with core.

## Key claim

The anapole moment of the halo nucleus \(^{11}\)Be is not appreciably enhanced despite the anomalously small energy separation between its opposite-parity \(2s_{1/2}\) ground state and \(1p_{1/2}\) excited level; the contribution from the bound \(1p_{1/2}\) state is essentially cancelled by the contribution from the continuum, yielding a net value only slightly above the typical estimate for a light nucleus.

## Method

The authors computed the anapole moment of \(^{11}\)Be using the general sum-over-states expression for the anapole operator induced by parity-violating nuclear forces, then applied the closure approximation to sum over intermediate states including the continuum. They evaluated the expectation value of the core density \(\langle 0 |\rho(r)| 0\rangle\) using the same ground-state wave function and core density parameters as in prior work by Hu and collaborators, and estimated the continuum contribution by scaling from the closure result with the bound-state energy denominator.

## Key numbers

- Energy separation between the \(1p_{1/2}\) and \(2s_{1/2}\) levels: \(|\Delta E| = 0.32\) MeV.
- Binding energy of the odd neutron: \(|\Delta E_0| = 0.50\) MeV.
- Expectation value of the core density: \(\langle 0 |\rho(r)| 0\rangle = 0.01\) fm\(^{-3}\) (from \(\rho_0 = 0.20\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- Anapole constant from bound-state-only calculation: \(\kappa_1(^{11}{\rm Be}) = 0.17\, g_n\).
- Anapole constant from closure with \(\bar{\Delta} = 0.32\) MeV: \(\kappa(\bar{\Delta}=0.32\ {\rm MeV}) = -0.036\, g_n\).
- Final estimate for the anapole constant: \(\kappa(^{11}{\rm Be}) \simeq (0.07 - 0.08)\, g_n\) (for \(\bar{\Delta} = 0.6-0.7\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of anapole moments in halo nuclei initiated by Hu (2002) and Hu and collaborators, who predicted a 15-fold enhancement of the anapole moment in \(^{11}\)Be relative to the simple \(A^{2/3}\) scaling formula of Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1984). The present calculation shows that the continuum contribution, omitted in the earlier bound-state-only estimate, cancels most of the enhancement, consistent with the absence of such enhancement in the deuteron as shown by Khriplovich and Korkin (2001). The result implies that experimental searches for anapole moments in light halo nuclei will not benefit from the expected enhancement, and that reliable nuclear-structure calculations must include continuum effects.
