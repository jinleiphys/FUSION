# A Vision of Hadronic Physics

> We present a vision for the next decade of hadron physics in which the central question being addressed is how one might win new physical insight into the way hadronic systems work. The topics addressed include the relevance of model building, the role of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, spectroscopy, form factors and physics in the deep inelastic regime.

## Key claim

The paper presents a vision for the next decade of hadron physics where the central goal is winning new physical insight into how hadronic systems work, with the caveat that this quest will involve not only new data and higher-precision calculations but also new physical understanding and paradigms, often inspired by models.

## Method

This is a vision paper (conference summary) that reviews the role of models in hadron physics, covering topics such as flavor asymmetries in parton distributions, the proton spin crisis, lattice QCD extrapolations, hadron spectroscopy including exotic states, nucleon form factors, and deep inelastic scattering. The author discusses specific model predictions (e.g., from the cloudy bag model, MIT bag model, chiral quark soliton model) and compares them with experimental results and lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

- The cloudy bag model prediction of an excess of $\bar{d}$ over $\bar{u}$ quarks in the proton sea was confirmed by Drell-Yan experiments and semi-inclusive DIS measurements, consistent with the 1983 prediction.
- SU(3) symmetry breaking in the octet component of the nucleon spin ($g_A^8$) is suggested to be as large as 20% based on recent cloudy bag model studies.
- Lattice QCD studies of excited states have been performed at light quark masses two or three times the physical value, with as many as 4 or more excited states found for a given set of quantum numbers.
- Exotic hadrons are predicted by JLab lattice studies to exist at an excitation energy approximately 0.8 to 1.0 GeV above the corresponding non-exotic hadrons.
- Lattice QCD progress on nucleon form factors has been notable below 1.5 GeV$^2$.
- The ratio $G_E/G_M$ for the proton is of interest in the region 5–10 GeV$^2$, with a recent Dyson-Schwinger equation calculation linking its behavior to the transition between non-perturbative and perturbative behavior in the quark propagator.

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on hadron structure within QCD, particularly the role of chiral symmetry breaking and models such as the cloudy bag model (Theberge 1980, Thomas 1982) and the MIT bag model (Chodos 1974). It discusses the resolution of the proton spin crisis (Ashman 1987) through a combination of pion emission and one-gluon-exchange hyperfine interactions, and it highlights the importance of finite range regularization (Young 2002) for extrapolating lattice QCD results to physical quark masses. The paper enables future experimental searches at JLab 12 GeV, GSI-FAIR, J-PARC, and electron-ion colliders, as well as continued lattice QCD studies of excited states and exotic hadrons.
