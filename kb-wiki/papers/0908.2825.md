# Probing the momentum dependence of medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections

> The momentum dependence of the medium modifications on nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections is discussed with microscopic transport theories and numerically investigated with an updated UrQMD microscopic transport model. The semi-peripheral Au+Au reaction at beam energy $E_b=400A$ MeV is adopted as an example. It is found that the uncertainties of the momentum dependence on medium modifications of cross sections influence the yields of free nucleons and their collective flows as functions of their transverse momentum and rapidity. Among these observables, the elliptic flow is sensitively dependent on detailed forms of the momentum dependence and more attention should be paid. The elliptic flow is hardly influenced by the probable splitting effect of the neutron-neutron and proton-proton cross sections so that one might pin down the mass splitting effect of the mean-field level at high beam energies and high nuclear densities by exploring the elliptic flow of nucleons or light clusters.

## Key claim
The momentum dependence of medium modifications to nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections significantly affects elliptic flow observables in heavy-ion collisions, but the splitting effect between neutron-neutron and proton-proton cross sections has negligible influence on elliptic flow, allowing mean-field mass-splitting effects to be studied separately.

## Method
The authors used an updated UrQMD microscopic transport model with a soft equation of state and soft symmetry potential to simulate semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at 400A MeV beam energy with impact parameter 7 fm. They implemented four different parameter sets (FP1-FP4) for the momentum-dependent medium correction factor F(u,α,p) applied to free-space NN elastic cross sections, plus a case without momentum constraint, and compared the resulting rapidity distributions, directed flow v₁, and elliptic flow v₂ of free nucleons.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: E_b = 400A MeV for Au+Au reactions
- Impact parameter: b = 7 fm (semi-peripheral)
- 100,000 events calculated per case; freeze-out time: 100 fm/c
- Coalescence parameters: P_0 = 0.3 GeV/c, R_0 = 3.5 fm
- Reduced density u = 2 used for illustrating F_u^p functions
- Symmetry energy parameters: S_0 = 32 MeV, stiffness factor γ = 0.5
- FP1 parameters: f_0 = 1, p_0 = 0.425 GeV/c, κ = 5
- FP2 parameters: f_0 = 1, p_0 = 0.225 GeV/c, κ = 3
- FP3 parameters: f_0 = 1, p_0 = 0.625 GeV/c, κ = 8
- FP4 parameters: f_0 = 1.3, p_0 = 0.425 GeV/c, κ = 4 (30% enhancement at large p_NN)
- Sensitivity to medium modifications observed in p_NN region 0.3-0.6 GeV/c
- Elliptic flow sensitivity to momentum dependence seen at p_t between 0.5 and 1.0 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous studies of in-medium NN cross sections using self-consistent RBUU transport theory (Mao 1997, Li 2006) and QHD-II type effective Lagrangians, where medium modifications were factorized into density-dependent and momentum-dependent terms. The authors build on findings that elliptic flow is sensitive to mass-splitting effects in the mean field (Di Toro 2006, 2008) and demonstrate that the splitting effect in the collision term does not contaminate those mean-field signals. The results enable future systematic studies using self-consistent RBUU theory to investigate energy dependence of NN elastic cross sections in neutron-rich matter up to reduced density u ≤ 3 and temperature T ≤ 100 MeV.
