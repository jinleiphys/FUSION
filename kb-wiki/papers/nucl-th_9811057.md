# Spin 1 inversion: a Majorana tensor force for deuteron alpha scattering

> We demonstrate, for the first time, successful S-matrix to potential inversion for spin one projectiles with non-diagonal $S^j_{ll'}$ yielding a $T_{\rm R}$ interaction. The method is a generalization of the iterative-perturbative, IP, method. We present a test case indicating the degree of uniqueness of the potential. The method is adapted, using established procedures, into direct observable to potential inversion, fitting $σ$, ${\rm i}T_{11}$, $T_{20}$, $T_{21}$ and $T_{22}$ for d + alpha scattering over a range of energies near 10 MeV. The $T_{\rm R}$ interaction which we find is very different from that proposed elsewhere, both real and imaginary parts being very different for odd and even parity channels.

# Spin 1 inversion: a Majorana tensor force for deuteron alpha scattering

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate the first successful S-matrix to potential inversion for spin-1 projectiles with non-diagonal S-matrix elements, yielding a tensor interaction of the T_R form; however, the resulting T_R interaction is very different from that proposed elsewhere, with both real and imaginary parts being very different for odd and even parity channels, and the method is tested only for a specific case where the degree of uniqueness of the potential is indicated but not fully established.

## Method

The authors generalize the iterative-perturbative (IP) inversion method, previously successful for spin-1/2 projectiles, to handle coupled-channel inversion for spin-1 projectiles scattering from a spin-zero target, where total angular momentum j and parity pi are conserved and two orbital angular momenta l and l' are coupled by a tensor interaction. They incorporate this into a direct data-to-potential inversion procedure that simultaneously fits the observables sigma, iT_11, T_20, T_21, and T_22 for deuteron-alpha scattering at six energies near 10 MeV, using a single energy-dependent potential with fixed radial form where imaginary components scale with energy above threshold.

## Key numbers

* The test case for S to V inversion was performed at 20.2 MeV deuteron energy on He-4, using a target potential with a complex central term and real l-s and V^(t) terms but no Majorana components.
* The two inversion solutions (A and B) reproduced all four volume integrals to better than 1 percent after only 3-4 iterations.
* The direct data-to-potential inversion fitted data at 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13 MeV, yielding chi-squared per data point values of 8.59 and 8.08 for the data at all six energies.
* The unitarity limit was only broken to a small degree for L=2, J=2 for the chi-squared/N = 8.59 fit, and was not broken for the better fit.

## Context

This work extends the IP inversion method from spin-1/2 projectiles (Mackintosh and Kobos, 1982; Kukulin, Pomerantsev and Horacek, 1990; Cooper and Mackintosh, 1991-1996) to spin-1 projectiles, enabling the first direct inversion of deuteron-alpha scattering data to a potential containing a tensor interaction. The results contradict the parity-independent potential of Frick et al. (1980) with its remarkably large V^(t) interaction, and instead support theoretical predictions from RGM theory (Cooper and Mackintosh, 1996; Mackintosh and Cooper, 1995-1997) that interactions between light nuclei are parity dependent, with the strong parity dependence in the tensor term suggesting a new physical process possibly arising from a deuteron exchange mechanism (Kukulin et al., 1998). The general procedure can be applied to other cases with channel spin one such as proton-triton scattering, and enables direct comparison between theories for dynamic polarization and exchange contributions and phenomenology.
