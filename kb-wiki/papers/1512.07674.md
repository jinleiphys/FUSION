# Hypernuclei in Halo/Cluster Effective Field Theory

> The light double $Λ$ hypernuclei, ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\ \ 4}$H and ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\ \ 6}$He, are studied as three-body $ΛΛd$ and $ΛΛα$ cluster systems in halo/cluster effective field theory at leading order. We find that the $ΛΛd$ system in spin-0 channel does not exhibit a limit cycle whereas the $ΛΛd$ system in spin-1 channel and the $ΛΛα$ system in spin-0 channel do. The limit cycle is associated with the formation of bound states, known as Efimov states, in the unitary limit. For the $ΛΛd$ system in the spin-0 channel we estimate the scattering length $a_0$ for $S$-wave $Λ$ hyperon-hypertriton scattering as $a_0=16.0\pm 3.0$ fm. We also discuss that studying the cutoff dependences in the $ΛΛd$ and $ΛΛα$ systems, the bound state of ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\ \ 4}$H is not an Efimov state but formed due to a high energy mechanism whereas that of ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\ \ 6}$He may be regarded as an Efimov state.

## Key claim

The bound state of ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 4}$H is not an Efimov state but formed due to a high energy mechanism, whereas that of ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 6}$He may be regarded as an Efimov state, based on studying cutoff dependences in the $\Lambda\Lambda d$ and $\Lambda\Lambda\alpha$ systems.

## Method

The light double $\Lambda$ hypernuclei ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 4}$H and ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 6}$He were studied as three-body $\Lambda\Lambda d$ and $\Lambda\Lambda\alpha$ cluster systems in halo/cluster effective field theory at leading order. Integral equations for $S$-wave scattering were derived and examined in the asymptotic limit to determine whether the systems exhibit a limit cycle, which is associated with the formation of Efimov states. For channels exhibiting a limit cycle, three-body contact interactions were introduced at leading order to renormalize cyclic singularities, and bound state energies were computed as functions of the momentum cutoff.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda\Lambda d$ system in spin-0 channel: scattering length $a_0$ for $S$-wave $\Lambda$ hyperon-hypertriton scattering estimated as $a_0 = 16.0 \pm 3.0$ fm
- $\Lambda\Lambda d$ system in spin-1 channel: imaginary solution $s_0 = 0.4492\cdots$ with discrete scaling factor $e^{\pi/s_0} \simeq 1.09 \times 10^3$
- $\Lambda\Lambda\alpha$ system in spin-0 channel: imaginary solution $s_0 = 1.0496\cdots$ with discrete scaling factor $e^{\pi/s_0} \simeq 19.9$
- $\Lambda$ separation momentum of hypertriton: $\gamma_{\Lambda d} \simeq 13.5 \pm 2.6$ MeV
- $\Lambda$ separation momentum of ${}_\Lambda^5$He: $\gamma_{\Lambda\alpha} \simeq 73.2$ MeV
- Experimental two-$\Lambda$ separation energy of ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 6}$He: $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 6.93 \pm 0.16$ MeV (from NAGARA and MIKAGE events)
- $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering length: $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -1.2 \pm 0.6$ fm (from ${}^{12}$C($K^-,K^+\Lambda\Lambda X)$ reaction)

## Context

This work extends the application of halo/cluster effective field theory to strangeness $-2$ hypernuclear systems, following the approach of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck for three-body systems with limit cycles. The study contrasts with earlier potential model calculations by Filikhin and Gal (2002) and Nemura et al. (2002) for ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 4}$H, and with cluster model studies of ${}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\ \ 6}$He. The results enable future experimental searches for Efimov states in hypernuclear systems and provide a framework for extracting $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction parameters from hypernuclear binding energies.
