# Pion-Nucleon coupling at finite temperature

> The pion nucleon vertex function at finite temperature is studied in the framework of: (a) the thermal (linear) sigma model to leading (one-loop) order, and (b) a thermal QCD-Finite Energy Sum Rule. Results from both methods indicate that the strength of the pion-nucleon coupling decreases with increasing T, vanishing at a critical temperature. The associated mean-square radius is a monotonically increasing function of T, diverging at the critical temperature. This is interpreted as (analytical) evidence for the quark-gluon deconfinement phase transition.

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon coupling strength decreases with increasing temperature and vanishes at a critical temperature, while the associated mean-square radius diverges there; this is interpreted as analytical evidence for the quark-gluon deconfinement phase transition, though the specific numerical value of the critical temperature depends on model parameters and the one-loop approximation.

## Method

The authors study the πNN vertex function using two independent frameworks: (a) the thermal linear sigma model at leading (one-loop) order, using Dolan-Jackiw real-time propagators and dimensional regularization with the MS-bar scheme; and (b) a thermal QCD Finite Energy Sum Rule (FESR) for a three-point function involving nucleon and pion interpolating currents, with the hadronic continuum modeled by the QCD spectral function under global quark-hadron duality. Both methods compute the ratio of the coupling at finite temperature to its zero-temperature value, as well as the temperature-dependent mean-square radius.

## Key numbers

- In the sigma model, the critical temperature is T_d ≈ 150–300 MeV for a sigma-meson mass M_σ ≈ 1300–1600 MeV (Eq. 7).
- In the sigma model, the zero-temperature coupling from the Goldberger-Treiman relation gives g ≈ 10 using M_N ≈ 800 MeV and f_π ≈ 80 MeV in the chiral limit.
- In the QCD-FESR at zero temperature, the coupling is g_πNN ≈ 15 (from Eq. 17 with f_π ≈ 93 MeV), compared to the experimental value g_πNN ≈ 13.
- The QCD-FESR result shows the coupling ratio g_πNN(T)/g_πNN(0) vanishes at the critical temperature where f_π(T) vanishes (Fig. 4).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of thermal hadronic form factors, specifically the electromagnetic form factor of the pion (Joel, year not given) and the rho-pi-pi coupling (Mirela, year not given), which also vanish at a critical temperature. The sigma-model results are consistent with chiral perturbation theory calculations of f_π(T) (CHPT, year not given), where the T² approximation gives T_c ≈ 240 MeV but higher-order corrections lower this value. The QCD-FESR approach avoids an expansion in powers of temperature and provides independent support, enabling future use of the temperature-dependent πNN vertex in hadron gas models at finite temperature.
