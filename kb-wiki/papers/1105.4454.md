# Nucleon Polarizabilities: Theory

> We review recent developments in the theoretical investigation of the nucleon polarizabilities. We first report on the static polarizabilities as measured in real Compton scattering, comparing and interpreting the results from various theoretical approaches. In a second step, we extend the discussion to the generalized polarizabilities which can be accessed in virtual Compton scattering, showing how the information encoded in these quantities can provide a spatial interpretation of the induced polarization densities in the nucleon.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes theoretical developments in nucleon polarizabilities, but the abstract's claim that generalized polarizabilities (GPs) "can provide a spatial interpretation of the induced polarization densities" is conditional on the validity of low-energy expansions and dispersion relation frameworks, which introduce model dependence through parameters describing the high-energy regime and through extrapolation of photoproduction data to regions not covered by experiment.

## Method

The paper reviews static polarizabilities extracted from real Compton scattering (RCS) using three techniques: low-energy expansion (LEX) of Compton cross sections, unsubtracted and subtracted fixed-t dispersion relations (DRs), and chiral effective field theories including heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) and the "delta-counting" scheme. For virtual Compton scattering (VCS), the authors describe how generalized polarizabilities (GPs) are accessed through the ep → epγ reaction, with the VCS amplitude decomposed into Born and non-Born contributions, and the non-Born part parametrized by 6 independent GPs at low outgoing-photon energy.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric dipole polarizability: α_E1^p = 12.1 ± 0.3 (stat.) ∓ 0.4 (syst.) ± 0.3 (mod.) in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- Proton magnetic dipole polarizability: β_M1^p = 1.6 ± 0.4 (stat.) ± 0.4 (syst.) ± 0.4 (mod.) in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- Forward spin polarizability of the proton: γ_0 = -0.90 ± 0.08 (stat.) ± 0.11 (syst.) in units of 10^{-4} fm^4
- Backward spin polarizability of the proton: γ_π = -38.7 ± 1.8 (stat.+syst.) in units of 10^{-4} fm^4, including the π^0-pole contribution of γ_π^{π^0-pole} = -46.7
- Leading-order ChPT prediction for proton scalar polarizabilities: α_E1^p = 10β_M1^p = 12.2 in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- Lattice QCD result for β_M1^p at m_π ≈ 500 MeV: β_M1^p = 2.36 ± 1.20 in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- HBChPT at O(p^4) predictions: α_E1^p = 10.5 ± 2.0 and β_M1^p = 3.5 ± 3.6 in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- N^3LO delta-counting predictions: α_E1^p = 10.8 ± 0.7 and β_M1^p = 4.0 ± 0.7 in units of 10^{-4} fm^3

## Context

This review extends earlier comprehensive reviews of nucleon polarizabilities (Drechsel 2002, Drechsel 2007, Guichon 1998, Schumacher 2005) by comparing predictions from multiple theoretical approaches including HBChPT, Lorentz-covariant ChPT, fixed-t dispersion relations (Holstein 1999, Babusci 1998), hyperbolic dispersion relations (Drechsel 2002), and the unitary chiral approach of Gasparyan (2011). The paper enables future experimental discrimination between theoretical predictions through double-polarization measurements at MAMI and HIγS, and provides a framework for lattice QCD calculations to extrapolate from unphysical pion masses to the physical point using chiral perturbation theory.
