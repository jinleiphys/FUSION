# The Lattice Fermi Surface

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in 2+1 dimensions at high baryon chemical potential shows no evidence for a BCS condensate or gap; instead, the high-density phase exhibits critical behavior with continuously varying exponents, suggesting Kosterlitz-Thouless superfluidity realized in a new universality class determined by the presence of relativistic fermions, rather than conventional BCS condensation.

## Method

The author simulated the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in 2+1 spacetime dimensions on a lattice using Monte Carlo methods, including a diquark source term j to probe for BCS condensation. Measurements were taken of the diquark condensate ⟨qq+⟩ and the associated susceptibilities χ+ and χ- as functions of the source j, for chemical potentials both below and above the chiral restoration threshold μc ≈ 0.65. The fermion propagator was also studied at non-zero momentum to extract the quasiparticle dispersion relation and search for an energy gap.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration is μc ≈ 0.65 (in lattice units).
- For μ > μc, the diquark condensate scales as ⟨qq+⟩ ∝ j^α with α = α(μ) falling in the range 0.2 - 0.3 for the μ values studied.
- The susceptibility ratio |χ+/χ-| takes the constant value α in the high-density phase, with plateaux observed at μ = 0.8 and μ = 0.9.
- The critical exponent δ = α^{-1} is observed in the range δ ≈ 3 - 5, in contrast to the 2d XY model value δ(T) ≥ 15.
- On a 32^3 system at μ = 0.8, the Fermi momentum is found at k/π ≈ 0.28, with Fermi velocity βF = ∂E/∂k|k=kF ≈ 0.7, both differing from free-field values.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice simulations of the NJL model in 2+1 dimensions by Hands (2001) and collaborators, which had established the chiral restoration transition and baryon density behavior. The study directly tests predictions from 1/Nf expansions by Kogut, Rosenstein, and Warr (1990) and from BCS-type color superconductivity scenarios proposed by Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998) and Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998). The results contradict the conventional BCS description appropriate for superfluid 3He and instead support a Kosterlitz-Thouless mechanism for 2d superfluidity, as originally described by Kosterlitz and Thouless (1973). The findings enable future extension to NJL in 3+1 dimensions to test color superconductivity scenarios beyond the self-consistent approximation.
