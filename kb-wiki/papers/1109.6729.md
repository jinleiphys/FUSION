# On the universal critical behavior in 3-flavor QCD

> We analyze the universal critical behavior at the chiral critical point in QCD with three degenerate quark masses. We confirm that this critical point lies in the universality class of the three dimensional Ising model. The symmetry of the Ising model, which is Z(2), is not directly realized in the QCD Hamiltonian. After making an ansatz for the magnetization- and energy-like operators as linear admixtures of the chiral condensate and the gluonic action, we determine several non-universal mixing and normalization constants. These parameters determine an unambiguous mapping of the critical behavior in QCD to that of the 3d-Ising model. We verify its validity by showing that the thus obtained orderparameter scales in accordance with the magnetic equation of state of the 3d-Ising model.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the chiral critical point in 3-flavor QCD with degenerate quark masses lies in the universality class of the three-dimensional Ising model, but the mapping requires a careful linear ansatz for the scaling operators and fields, and the mixing parameters are subject to finite-volume ambiguities that prevent a fully consistent simultaneous determination of all parameters.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with standard staggered fermions and the Wilson gauge action on \(N_\tau=4\) lattices with spatial volumes of \(16^3\), \(24^3\), and in some cases \(32^3\), using an exact RHMC algorithm implemented on GPUs. They located the critical point by analyzing the fourth-order Binder cumulant of the chiral condensate and determined the mixing parameters \(r, s, A, B\) and normalization constants \(t_0, h_0\) that map the QCD operators (chiral condensate and gluonic action) onto the magnetization- and energy-like operators of the 3d-Ising model. The validity of the mapping was tested by fitting the constructed order parameter to the magnetic equation of state of the 3d-Ising model.

## Key numbers

* Critical bare quark mass from the intersection of Binder cumulants on \(16^3\) and \(24^3\) lattices: \(am_c = 0.0273(4)\) (combined fit restricted to the Ising universal value).
* Critical bare quark mass from the \(16^3\) lattice alone: \(am_c = 0.0268(6)\).
* Mixing parameter \(B\) (relating the scaling field directions): \(B = -0.47(1)\).
* Mixing parameter \(s\) from the condition that the order parameter vanishes at the critical point: \(s = 0.048(2)\).
* Mixing parameter \(s\) from the statistical independence condition: \(s = -0.8(1)\).
* Normalization constants from the fit to the magnetic equation of state: \(t_0 = 0.0210(9)\) and \(h_0 = 0.0005(1)\).
* Universal Binder cumulant value of the 3d-Ising model used for comparison: \(B_4^{\rm Ising} = 1.604\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice determinations of the critical endpoint in 3-flavor QCD by Karsch, Laermann, and Schmidt (2001) and de Forcrand and Philipsen (2007) to larger spatial volumes and focuses on the explicit construction of scaling operators and fields. The mapping procedure follows the approach of Karsch, Laermann, and Schmidt (2001) and the mixing analysis of Karsch, Laermann, and Schmidt (2001). The results enable future studies of the curvature of the critical surface at non-zero chemical potential, which is relevant for understanding the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter.
