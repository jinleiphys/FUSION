# Generalized seniority scheme in light Sn isotopes

> The yrast generalized seniority states are compared with the corresponding shell model states for the case of the Sn isotopes $^{104-112}$Sn. For most of the cases the energies agree within 100 keV and the overlaps of the wave functions are greater than 0.7.

## Key claim

The generalized seniority (GSEN) scheme provides a reasonable approximation for yrast states in light Sn isotopes up to eight valence neutrons, but the agreement degrades significantly for heavier isotopes; the abstract's claim of energy agreement within 100 keV and wave function overlaps greater than 0.7 holds only for the lightest isotopes and for version II of the scheme, while version I with fixed pair structure shows deviations up to 0.5 MeV and overlaps as low as 0.167 for $^{112}$Sn.

## Method

The authors performed complete shell-model calculations for $^{104-112}$Sn using a microscopically derived effective interaction based on the Bonn B nucleon-nucleon potential, with a model space consisting of the $0g_{7/2}$, $1d_{5/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$, and $0h_{11/2}$ orbits above a $^{100}$Sn core. They compared these exact shell-model results with two versions of the generalized seniority scheme: version I, where the pair amplitudes $C_j$ and $D^+$ operators are fixed from the two-particle system $^{102}$Sn, and version II, where these amplitudes are variationally determined for each isotope separately.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energies for $^{104}$Sn and $^{110}$Sn predicted from the GSEN formula using parameters fitted to $^{106}$Sn and $^{108}$Sn agree within 90 keV and 440 keV, respectively
- For $^{110}$Sn, the number of shell-model basis states for $2^+$ states is 86990, compared with 9 in the GSEN calculation
- Excitation energies of the $2^+_1$ state: shell-model 1.63 MeV vs. GSEN version I 2.17 MeV and version II 1.71 MeV for $^{110}$Sn
- Excitation energies of the $6^+_1$ state: shell-model 2.96 MeV vs. GSEN version I 2.50 MeV and version II 3.29 MeV for $^{112}$Sn
- Overlap squared of the $0^+_1$ ground state with GSEN version II: 0.966 for $^{104}$Sn, 0.909 for $^{112}$Sn
- Overlap squared of the $6^+_1$ state with GSEN version I: 0.918 for $^{104}$Sn, 0.167 for $^{112}$Sn
- Overlap squared of the $2^+_1$ state with GSEN version I: 0.931 for $^{104}$Sn, 0.420 for $^{112}$Sn

## Context

This work extends earlier applications of the generalized seniority scheme to heavier Sn isotopes by Ottaviani and Savoia (1969) and Gambhir, Rimini, and Weber (1971), who compared GSEN with quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff approximation results. The present study takes advantage of complete shell-model calculations by Engeland, Hjorth-Jensen, Holt, and Osnes (1995) to provide an exact benchmark. The results show that seniority-four configurations, previously found to contribute up to 20% admixture in heavier Sn isotopes by Bonsignori, Savoia, Allaart, van Egmont, and Te Velde (1985) and Allaart, Boeker, Bonsignori, Savoia, and Gambhir (1988), are essential for a reasonable description, and work including seniority-four and possibly seniority-six configurations is stated to be in progress.
