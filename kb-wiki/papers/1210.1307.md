# Quark-Hadron Matter at Finite Temperature and Density in a Two-Color PNJL model

> Quark-hadron matter at finite temperature and density is studied using a two-flavor, color SU(2) (P)NJL model. The hadronic effective Lagrangian, derived by bosonization of the quark fields and renormalized using the Eguchi method, emerges in the form of an extended linear sigma model with meson and diquark-baryon fields. Chiral and diquark condensates are studied as functions of temperature and baryon density. Masses of mesons and diquark-baryons are calculated with and without the Polyakov loop effect. We investigate the equation of state of quark-hadron matter by taking into account the contributions of mesons and diquark-baryons in addition to the quark quasiparticles.

## Key claim

The authors construct a unified description of quark-hadron matter in two-color QCD by deriving a hadronic effective Lagrangian from a PNJL model via bosonization and renormalization, but the quantitative results depend on the Gaussian approximation which ignores third- and fourth-order interaction terms among mesons and diquark-baryons.

## Method

Starting from a two-flavor, color SU(2) NJL Lagrangian with meson and diquark interaction channels, the authors perform bosonization to introduce auxiliary meson and diquark fields, then integrate out the quark fields to obtain an effective hadron Lagrangian. The Eguchi method is used for renormalization, yielding a generalized linear sigma model with meson and diquark-baryon fields. The thermodynamical potential is computed in the mean-field approximation for the quark sector, while hadron contributions are included via the Gaussian approximation (keeping only mass terms) to evaluate the partition function.

## Key numbers

- Momentum cutoff: \(\Lambda \sim 0.6\) GeV, used to regularize divergent quark loop integrals \(I_0\) and \(I_2\)
- Baryon chemical potential: \(\mu_B = 2\mu\), where \(\mu\) is the quark chemical potential
- Dynamical quark mass defined as \(m = m_0 + g_0 \sigma_0\), with \(m_0\) the bare quark mass and \(\sigma_0\) the chiral condensate
- Quasiparticle energy: \(E^{\pm}_{\Delta} = \sqrt{(E_p^{\pm})^2 + g_d^2 |\Delta_0|^2}\), where \(E_p^{\pm} = E_p \pm \mu\) and \(E_p = \sqrt{\vec{p}^2 + m^2}\)

## Context

This work extends a previous NJL model calculation by Ratti and Weise (2004) for two-color QCD by incorporating the Polyakov loop and deriving a full hadronic Lagrangian with both meson and diquark-baryon fields. It complements lattice QCD studies for color SU(2) by Hands et al. (2000, 2001, 2008), Kogut et al. (2001), and Takahashi et al. (2010), providing a model framework to interpret lattice results on equations of state and hadron masses at finite temperature and density. The approach enables future inclusion of higher-order hadron interactions beyond the Gaussian approximation.
