# Three-body halo states in effective field theory

> In this paper we study the renormalization of Halo effective field theory applied to the Helium-6 halo nucleus seen as an alpha-neutron-neutron three-body state. We include the 0+ dineutron channel together with both the 3/2- and 1/2- neutron-alpha channels into the field theory and study all of the six lowest-order three-body interactions that are present. Furthermore, we discuss three different prescriptions to handle the unphysical poles in the P-wave two-body sector. In the simpler field theory without the 1/2- channel present we find that the bound-state spectrum of the field theory is renormalized by the inclusion of a single three-body interaction. However, in the field theory with both the 3/2- and 1/2- included, the system can not be renormalized by only one three-body operator.

# Three-body halo states in effective field theory

## Key claim

The paper shows that a Halo EFT description of the Helium-6 ground state (as an alpha-neutron-neutron three-body system) can be renormalized with a single three-body interaction when only the dineutron and the 3/2- neutron-alpha channels are included, but that the system cannot be renormalized by only one three-body operator when both the 3/2- and 1/2- neutron-alpha channels are included, at least for cutoffs up to about 10^6 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a quantum field theory for the 0+ ground state of Helium-6 using Halo EFT, including the S-wave dineutron channel and both the 3/2- and 1/2- P-wave neutron-alpha channels. They derive the full set of coupled three-body integral equations from diagrammatic rules, write the six lowest-order three-body interactions, and solve the homogeneous bound-state equations numerically by finding zeros of the kernel determinant. Three different prescriptions for removing unphysical poles from the P-wave two-body propagators are compared: unitarity piece removal (UP), subtraction prescription (SP), and expansion prescription (EP).

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of Helium-6: 1 MeV
- One-nucleon separation energy of the alpha particle: 20 MeV (defines the breakdown scale)
- Helium-5 3/2- resonance: energy 0.798 MeV, width 0.648 MeV
- Helium-5 1/2- resonance: energy 2.07 MeV, width 5.57 MeV
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a0 = -18.9 fm
- P-wave 3/2- scattering length: a1 = -76.12 fm^3; effective range: r1 = -141.84 MeV
- P-wave 1/2- scattering length: a_tilde_1 = -60.37 fm^3; effective range: r_tilde_1 = 66.87 MeV
- The three-body parameter H1 or H4 is tuned to reproduce the bound-state energy E = -1 MeV for each cutoff value
- Cutoff range explored: up to approximately 10^5 MeV for EP, up to approximately 10^6 MeV for SP and UP in the full three-channel calculation

## Context

This work extends previous Halo EFT treatments of Helium-6 by Rotureau and van Kolck (2012) and Ji, Elster, and Phillips (2014), which only considered the simplest three-body counterterm (H1) and omitted the 1/2- neutron-alpha channel. The paper provides the first complete field-theoretical derivation of the integral equations including both P-wave channels and all six lowest-order three-body interactions. The results enable future applications of Halo EFT to D-wave systems such as low-lying resonances in Oxygen-25 and Oxygen-26, where similar spurious poles from the effective range expansion are expected.
