# Traces of nonextensivity in particle physics due to fluctuations

> LaTeX file and 2 eps files with figures. Presented at X International Workshop on Multiparticle Production - Correlations and Fluctuations in QCD, CRETE, GREECE 8-16 June 2002. To be published by World Scientific (2003) (style file ws-p8-50x6-00.cls attached)

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) in Tsallis statistics can be physically interpreted as a measure of intrinsic fluctuations in the system, specifically that \(q = 1 \pm \omega\) where \(\omega\) is the relative variance of the fluctuating quantity, provided those fluctuations follow a gamma distribution.

## Method

The authors derive a mathematical connection between power-law (Tsallis) distributions and exponential distributions with a fluctuating scale parameter, using a Mellin transform to show that averaging an exponential over a gamma-distributed inverse scale yields a Tsallis distribution. They then model the fluctuating scale parameter (e.g., mean free path or temperature) via a Langevin equation with white Gaussian noise, solving the corresponding Fokker-Planck equation to obtain the gamma distribution. The formalism is applied to cosmic ray depth distributions, transverse momentum spectra in heavy ion collisions, and nonexponential decays.

## Key numbers

* In cosmic ray data from the Pamir lead chamber, the depth distribution of cascade starting points was fitted with a power-law (Levy-type) formula with \(q = 1.3\), while an alternative explanation invoked fluctuations of the mean free path with relative variance \(\omega \ge 0.2\).
* For transverse momentum distributions in heavy ion collisions, the deviation from exponential is very small: \(|q-1| \sim 0.015\), which corresponds to relative temperature fluctuations \(\Delta T / T \simeq 0.12\).
* The heat capacity \(C\) of the system is related to the nonextensivity parameter by \(\sigma^2(\beta)/\langle\beta\rangle^2 = 1/C = \omega = q-1\), where \(\beta = 1/T\).
* For nonexponential decays with \(\nu\) decay channels of equal width, the relative variance of the fluctuating width is \(2/\nu\) and the nonextensivity parameter is \(q = 1 + 2/\nu\).
* In \(p-\bar{p}\) collisions, the mean multiplicity of neutral mesons as a function of mass and the transverse mass spectra of pions follow a power law \(C \cdot x^{-P}\) with \(P \simeq 10.1\) and \(P \simeq 9.6\) respectively, which corresponds to \(q = 1 + 1/P \sim 1.1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000) that fluctuations can be a source of nonextensivity, adding to previously known sources such as long-range correlations, memory effects, and fractal phase space (Tsallis, 1988). It connects to the Tsallis nonextensive entropy framework and provides a physical interpretation of the \(q\) parameter in terms of temperature fluctuations in multiparticle production, enabling event-by-event studies of thermodynamic properties like heat capacity. The paper also relates to the Hagedorn statistical model (Beck, 2000) and to random matrix approaches to nonexponential decays.
