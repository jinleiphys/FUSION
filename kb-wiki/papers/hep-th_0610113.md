# Elliptic Flow, Kasner Universe and Holographic Dual of RHIC Fireball

> We consider a holographic dual of hydrodynamics of N=4 SYM plasma that undergoes non-isotropic three-dimensional expansion relevant to RHIC fireball. Our model is a natural extension of the Bjorken's one-dimensional expansion, and it describes an elliptic flow whose v2 and eccentricity are given in terms of anisotropy parameters. Holographic renormalization shows that absence of conformal anomaly in the SYM theory constrains our local rest frame to be a Kasner spacetime. We show that the Kasner spcetime provides a simple description of the anisotropically expanding fluid within a well-controled approximation. We also find that the dual geometry determines some of the hydrodynamic quantities in terms of the initial condition and the fundamental constants.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the local rest frame of a strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma undergoing non-isotropic three-dimensional expansion can be described by a Kasner metric, and that this description is a well-controlled approximation valid in the late-time regime, near the central region, and for nearly central collisions.

## Method

The authors generalize Bjorken's one-dimensional expansion to three dimensions by introducing a Kasner metric with anisotropy parameters a, b, c as the local rest frame. They impose conformal invariance through holographic renormalization, which forces the Kasner conditions a+b+c=1 and a^2+b^2+c^2=1. The five-dimensional dual geometry is constructed by solving Einstein's equations with a negative cosmological constant in Fefferman-Graham coordinates, using the boundary energy-momentum tensor that includes shear viscosity with η ∝ T^3.

## Key numbers

- The Kasner conditions: a+b+c=1 and a^2+b^2+c^2=1, derived from requiring conformal invariance (vanishing conformal anomaly)
- The energy density solution: ρ(τ) = ρ₀/τ^(4/3) + (4/3)/(1/3-β) · η₀/τ^(1+β) for β ≠ 1/3, where β characterizes the time-dependence of shear viscosity η = η₀/τ^β
- For the strongly coupled N=4 SYM case with β=1: T = T₀(1/τ^(1/3) - (1/2)(η₀/ρ₀)(1/τ) + ...)
- The entropy per unit co-moving volume at infinite time: S_∞ = N_c² · (√2/π) · (2π²/N_c² · ρ₀/3)^(3/4)
- The temperature normalization: T₀ = (√2/π) · (2π²/N_c² · ρ₀/3)^(1/4)
- The entropy creation rate from gravity: (1/S)(dS/dτ) = η₀/(ρ₀τ^(5/3)) + O(τ^(-7/3))

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to Bjorken hydrodynamics developed by Janik and Peschanski (2006) and the authors' previous work on shear viscosity (Sin and Shin, 2006) from one-dimensional to three-dimensional anisotropic expansion relevant to RHIC fireballs. The Kasner metric description enables a simple hydrodynamic treatment of elliptic flow, with v₂ and eccentricity expressed in terms of the anisotropy parameters. The approach determines macroscopic hydrodynamic parameters (such as S_∞ and T₀) from microscopic data (ρ₀ and N_c) through the dual geometry, which hydrodynamics alone cannot provide. The results qualitatively reproduce the elliptic flow behavior seen in hydrodynamic simulations by Kolb and Heinz (2003) and Teaney (2003).
