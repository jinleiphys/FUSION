# Dirac Hartree-Fock for Finite Nuclei Employing realistic Forces

> We discuss two different approximation schemes for the self-consistent solution of the {\it relativistic} Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation for finite nuclei. In the first scheme, the Dirac effects are deduced from corresponding nuclear matter calculations, whereas in the second approach the local-density approximation is used to account for the effects of correlations. The results obtained by the two methods are very similar. Employing a realistic one-boson-exchange potential (Bonn~A), the predictions for energies and radii of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca come out in substantially better agreement with experiment as compared to non-relativistic approaches. As a by-product of our study, it turns out that the Fock exchange-terms, ignored in a previous investigation, are not negligible.

# Dirac Hartree-Fock for Finite Nuclei Employing Realistic Forces

## Key claim
The relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, using the Bonn A realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, predicts ground-state energies and charge radii for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca in substantially better agreement with experiment than non-relativistic approaches, provided that Fock exchange terms (neglected in a previous investigation) are included. The two approximation schemes tested yield very similar results only when Fock terms are incorporated, indicating that the earlier good agreement of the density-dependent Hartree (RDH) method was fortuitous.

## Method
Two approximation schemes for solving the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation for finite nuclei were implemented. In the first scheme (DBHF), pair correlations are calculated directly for the finite nucleus using the Bethe-Goldstone equation, while relativistic medium effects on Dirac spinors are incorporated via the local-density approximation. In the second scheme (RDHF), pair correlations are treated in local-density approximation while the Dirac equation is solved directly for the finite nucleus, using density-dependent coupling constants for σ and ω mesons extracted from nuclear matter DBHF results in a Hartree-Fock analysis. Both methods employ the Bonn A one-boson-exchange potential.

## Context
This work extends the Dirac Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach from nuclear matter (Brockmann and Machleidt, 1984; Horowitz and Serot, 1984) to finite nuclei, addressing the long-standing Coester band problem where non-relativistic BHF calculations could not simultaneously reproduce correct binding energies and radii (Coester et al., 1970). The results improve upon earlier finite-nucleus calculations by including Fock exchange terms that were neglected in the density-dependent Hartree approach of Brockmann and Toki (1992). The remaining small discrepancies between theory and experiment suggest that three-nucleon correlations or other improvements beyond relativistic BHF may be needed.
