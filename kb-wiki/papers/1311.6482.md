# Q^2-evolution of the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the proton

> The generalized Baldin sum rule at finite four-momentum transfer Q^2 is evaluated utilizing a structure function parameterization fit to recent experimental data. The most recent measurements on F_1 from Hall C at Jlab, as well as the F_2 structure function data from Hall B at Jlab and SLAC, were used in constructing our parameterization. We find that at Q^2 below 1 GeV^2 the dominant contribution to the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon comes from the resonance region.

## Key claim
The generalized Baldin sum rule is evaluated using a structure function parameterization fit to recent experimental data, finding that at Q² below 1 GeV² the dominant contribution to the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the proton comes from the resonance region (W < 2 GeV), though the authors note that further study is necessary at Q² < 0.2 GeV² to establish the transition to the real photon point.

## Method
The authors constructed a parameterization of the F₁ structure function by fitting recent experimental data from JLab Hall C (F₁ measurements) and Hall B (F₂ measurements), combined with SLAC data, using the ratio of longitudinal to transverse virtual photon cross sections to relate F₂ to F₁. In the resonance region (W < 1.9 GeV) they employed an isobar model with four resonances (P33, D13, F15, F37) plus a non-resonant background, while above the resonance region they adopted a Regge approach. The generalized Baldin sum rule was then evaluated using this parameterization to obtain the Q²-evolution of α + β for the proton.

## Key numbers
- The sum of electric and magnetic polarizabilities at Q² = 0 from the Baldin sum rule is (14.2 ± 0.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Damashek and Gilman, 1970) and (13.69 ± 0.14) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Babusci et al.)
- PDG averaged experimental values for the proton: α = (12.0 ± 0.6) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β = (1.9 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Chiral Perturbation Theory predicts α + β = (14.0 ± 4.1) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ for the proton at Q² = 0
- The ratio R = σ_L/σ_T was parameterized as R = 0.014 Q² [exp(-0.07 Q²) + 41 exp(-0.8 Q²)] with Q² in GeV²
- The model parameters were fitted at Q² > 0.225 GeV²
- Experimental results from Hall A are shown at Q² = 0.92 GeV² and 1.76 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the 2006 evaluation by Liang et al. (2006) of the Q²-dependence of the sum of electric and magnetic polarizabilities, using updated 2013 measurements from the E94-110 experiment at JLab Hall C. The results provide a parameterization of the F₁ structure function valid in the low-Q², low-W kinematic region, and enable comparison with Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions (Bernard et al.) and dispersion relation analyses from Hall A, though the authors note that further precise experiments are needed at Q² below 0.2 GeV² to establish the transition to the real photon point.
