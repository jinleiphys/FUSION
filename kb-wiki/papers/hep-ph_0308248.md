# Hard probes in heavy ion collisions at the LHC: PDFs, shadowing and $pA$ collisions

> This manuscript is the outcome of the subgroup ``PDFs, shadowing and $pA$ collisions'' from the CERN workshop ``Hard Probes in Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC''. In addition to the experimental parameters for $pA$ collisions at the LHC, the issues discussed are factorization in nuclear collisions, nuclear parton distributions (nPDFs), hard probes as the benchmark tests of factorization in $pA$ collisions at the LHC, and semi-hard probes as observables with potentially large nuclear effects. Also, novel QCD phenomena in $pA$ collisions at the LHC are considered. The importance of the $pA$ program at the LHC is emphasized.

# Hard probes in heavy ion collisions at the LHC: PDFs, shadowing and pA collisions

## Key claim

The proton-nucleus (pA) program at the LHC is essential for establishing reliable baseline spectra to disentangle known nuclear effects from new physics in heavy-ion collisions, but theoretical extrapolations of the Cronin effect from lower energies to the LHC suffer from large uncertainties, particularly from nuclear shadowing and antishadowing at small x, which may lead to up to a factor of 2 uncertainty in the height of the Cronin peak.

## Method

The paper surveys and compares five theoretical models for the Cronin effect in pA collisions: soft hadronic rescattering (Wang 1998), soft-saturated hadronic rescattering (Zhang 2001), the color dipole model (Kopeliovich 2002), and two hard partonic rescattering models (Accardi 2001 and Vitev 2002). Each model computes the Cronin ratio R(pT) of inclusive differential cross sections for proton scattering on two different targets, and the models are compared by extracting the Cronin parameters pM (transverse momentum at which R reaches its maximum) and RM (maximum value of R) at three collision energies: 27.4 GeV, 200 GeV, and 5.5 TeV.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 27.4 GeV for pW/pBe collisions, the soft model gives pM = 4.0 GeV and RM = 1.55 for charged pions; the soft-saturated model gives pM = 4.5 GeV and RM = 1.46 with HIJING shadowing; the color dipole model gives pM = 4.5 GeV and RM = 1.43 +/- 0.08; the hard GV model gives pM = 4 GeV and RM = 1.4.
- At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for pAu/pp collisions, the soft model gives pM = 3.5 GeV and RM = 1.35 +/- 0.2 for charged pions; the soft-saturated model gives pM = 2.9 GeV and RM = 1.15 with HIJING shadowing; the color dipole model gives pM = 2.7 GeV and RM = 1.1; the hard GV model gives pM = 3.0 GeV and RM = 1.3.
- At sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV for pPb/pp collisions, the soft model gives pM = 3.5 GeV and RM = 1.08 +/- 0.02 for charged pions; the soft-saturated model gives pM = 2.4 GeV and RM = 0.78 with HIJING shadowing; the color dipole model gives pM = 2.5 GeV and RM = 1.06; the hard GV model gives pM approximately 40 GeV and RM = 1.05.
- For parton production at LHC energies, the hard AT model predicts pM = 11 +/- 1.3 GeV with RM ranging from 1.2 to 2.1 depending on the choice of the infrared regulator mu.
- The infrared regulator in the hard AT model is parameterized as mu = p0(sqrt(s)) = 0.060 + 0.283 log(sqrt(s)) in GeV, based on fits to pp data from Eskola (2002).

## Context

This paper is the outcome of the subgroup "PDFs, shadowing and pA collisions" from the CERN workshop "Hard Probes in Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC" (2003). It extends the study of the Cronin effect, first discovered by Cronin (1975), from fixed-target energies to RHIC and LHC energies, motivated by the discovery at RHIC of unexpectedly small R < 1 in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV compared to pp collisions (Adcox 2001, Reygers 2002). The paper surveys models including those by Wang (1998), Zhang (2001), Kopeliovich (2002), Accardi (2001), and Vitev (2002), and emphasizes that a pA run at the LHC at the same energy as AA collisions would be of major importance to test theoretical models and provide reliable baseline spectra for extracting novel physical effects such as jet quenching and gluon saturation.
