# Isotopic chains around oxygen from evolved chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions

> We extend the formalism of self-consistent Green's function theory to include three-body interactions and apply it to isotopic chains around oxygen for the first time. The third-order algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(3)] equations for two-body Hamiltonians can be exploited upon defining system-dependent one- and two-body interactions coming from the three-body force, and correspondingly dropping interaction reducible diagrams. This goes beyond the standard normal ordering truncations recently used in ab-inito studies. The Koltun sum rule for the total binding energy acquires a correction due to the added three-body interaction. This formalism is then applied to study chiral two-nucleon (2N) and three-nucleon forces (3NF) evolved to low momentum cutoffs. We find that these interactions reproduce the binding energies of nitrogen, oxygen and fluorine isotopes to great accuracy, providing clear indication of the predictive power of this approach. All three neutron driplines are correctly predicted when full 3NF are included. The formalism introduced also allows to calculate form factors for nucleon transfer on doubly magic systems.

## Key claim

The paper extends self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory to include three-body interactions and applies it to isotopic chains around oxygen for the first time, finding that chiral three-nucleon forces at N²LO are crucial for reproducing experimental binding energies and driplines, but the accuracy is limited to about 5% on binding energies when accounting for many-body truncation and SRG evolution dependence.

## Method

The authors define system-dependent effective one- and two-body interactions from the three-body force by contracting with fully correlated density matrices, then solve the Dyson equation using the third-order algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(3)] method. The Koltun sum rule for total binding energy is corrected by adding the expectation value of the three-nucleon force, calculated at first order using dressed propagators. Calculations use chiral N³LO two-nucleon and N²LO three-nucleon interactions evolved via free-space similarity renormalization group (SRG) to low momentum cutoffs, with model spaces up to 12 harmonic oscillator shells.

## Key numbers

- ¹⁶O ground state energy: -130.8(1) MeV (extrapolated), in close agreement with -130.5(1) MeV from IM-SRG
- Convergence error estimate: 1% for calculated total binding energies
- ²⁵O predicted particle unbound by 1.54 MeV, compared to experimental value of 770 keV
- ²⁸O ground state resonance suggested unbound by 5.2 MeV with respect to ²⁴O
- ²⁹F calculated bound by about 890 keV with respect to ²⁵F, compared to experimental value of 3.55 MeV
- Discrepancy in ¹⁵O (²³O) reduces from 1.65 MeV (1.03 MeV) to 190 keV (20 keV) when center-of-mass correction is applied
- Variation of results with ℏω between 20 and 24 MeV: about 450 keV
- Contribution of ⟨W^TDA⟩ correction never bigger than 300 keV

## Context

This work extends SCGF theory to include three-body interactions, going beyond the normal ordering truncations used in previous ab-initio studies such as those by Hagen (2012) and Hergert (2013). It confirms and extends the finding of Otsuka (2010) that the Fujita-Miyazawa three-nucleon force explains the anomalous dripline at ²⁴O, showing that N²LO 3NFs similarly affect neighboring isotopic chains of nitrogen and fluorine. The formalism enables future calculations of form factors for nucleon transfer on doubly magic systems and can be extended to the Gorkov formalism for open-shell nuclei, as demonstrated in recent work by Idini (2012) and Soma (2013).
