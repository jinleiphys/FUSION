# Dynamic Time Scales in Colored Glass Nuclear Matter

> In Ultra high energy collisions, the concept of a glass law is invoked in the framework of 'low tension' QCD strings. It is shown that the excitation of QCD strings at low energy has a negative temperature and at high energy has a positive temperature always higher than the Hagedorn temperature, T_H. Very high energy strings T->T_H + 0+ move very slowly as a viscous melted glass with very high viscosity. However, in a very short collision time, it is difficult to transfer the initial collision kinetic energy into the internal energy of a few strings. The low energy strings at high T>>T_H have a low viscosity giving rise to an almost perfect fluid. The role of strings in a nucleus is closely analogous to the role of polymer chains in some viscous glass beads.

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD strings excited in ultra-high-energy nuclear collisions exhibit a glass-like dynamics governed by a Vogel-Fulcher-Tammann (VFT) law, where the string temperature can be either negative (for low energy transfer) or positive but always above the Hagedorn temperature (for high energy transfer), with the caveat that the actual time scales depend on whether the collision duration is sufficient to transfer kinetic energy into internal string excitation.

## Method

The authors derive the entropy of highly excited QCD strings from the degeneracy of string rotational and vibrational states, using the partition function of integer partitions to obtain the microcanonical entropy as a function of energy. They then compute the string temperature from the entropy-energy relation and apply a detailed-balance argument for transition rates to obtain the characteristic relaxation time scales, which follow the VFT law familiar from glass physics. The string fragmentation rate is computed using Fermi's golden rule applied to a two-state quark Hamiltonian in a color electric field.

## Key numbers

- String tension: \(\sigma \approx 0.18\ \mathrm{GeV}^2/(\hbar c) \approx 1.45\times 10^5\ \mathrm{Newton}\)
- Hagedorn temperature: \(k_B T_H \approx 207\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Activation energy: \(\Phi \equiv 7k_B T_H / 2 \approx 725\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Fermi velocity of nuclear matter: \(v_F / c \approx 1/4\)
- Nuclear radius parameter: \(R = a A^{1/3} \approx 1.2\times 10^{-13}\ \mathrm{cm}\ A^{1/3}\)

## Context

This work extends the color glass condensate framework (Gtulassy, Kharzeev, Iancu, Venugopalan, and others from the early 2000s) by explicitly connecting QCD string thermodynamics to glassy dynamics via the VFT law, which is familiar from polymer and glass physics (Vogel 1921, Fulcher 1925, Tammann 1926, Tanaka 2003). The paper enables a unified description of the transition from a low-viscosity "perfect fluid" regime (observed at RHIC by Adcox 2005 and Adams 2005) at high temperatures far above \(T_H\) to a highly viscous glassy regime near the Hagedorn temperature, with implications for the interpretation of time scales in heavy-ion collisions.
