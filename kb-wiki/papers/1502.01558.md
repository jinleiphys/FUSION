# Weak $K\to π$ generalized form factors and transverse transition quark-spin density from the instanton vacuum

> We investigate the generalized $K\to π$ transition vector and tensor form factors, from which we derive the transverse quark spin density in the course of the $K\toπ$ transition, based on the nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum. The results of the transition tensor form factor are in good agreement with recent data of lattice QCD. The behavior of the transverse quark spin density of the $K\toπ$ transition turn out to be very similar to those of the pion and the kaon.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the generalized $K\to\pi$ transition tensor form factor computed from the nonlocal chiral quark model (NL$\chi$QM) based on the instanton vacuum agrees well with lattice QCD data, but this agreement holds specifically after renormalization-group evolution from the model's natural scale $\mu=0.6$ GeV to the lattice scale $\mu=2.0$ GeV, and the comparison is made for the normalized $t$-dependence rather than absolute values.

## Method

The authors compute the $K^0\to\pi^-$ transition vector and tensor form factors by taking functional derivatives of the effective chiral action derived from the instanton vacuum, which incorporates momentum-dependent dynamical quark masses and explicit flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking with current quark masses $m_u=m_d=5$ MeV and $m_s=150$ MeV. The matrix elements are evaluated in the center-of-mass frame using the nonlocal chiral quark model with two phenomenological parameters (average instanton size $\bar{\rho}\approx 1/3$ fm and average inter-instanton distance $\bar{R}\approx 1$ fm), and the tensor form factor is evolved to $\mu=2.0$ GeV using next-to-leading order renormalization-group equations for comparison with lattice results. The transverse quark spin density in the impact-parameter plane is then obtained via Fourier transformation of the form factors parameterized in a $p$-pole form.

## Key numbers

- $B^{K^0\pi^-}_T(0) = 0.792$ at $\mu = 0.6$ GeV (model scale)
- $B^{K^0\pi^-}_T(0) = 0.709$ at $\mu = 2.0$ GeV (lattice scale)
- $f^{K\pi}_T(0) = 0.45$ at $\mu = 2.0$ GeV, compared to lattice result $f_T^{K\pi}(0) = 0.417 \pm 0.014(\text{stat}) \pm 0.05(\text{syst})$ at physical pion mass
- Fitted $p$-pole parameters at $\mu=0.6$ GeV: for $A_{1,0}^{K\pi}$, $p=0.850$ and $M_p = 1.312$ GeV; for $B_{T\,1,0}^{K\pi}$, $p=2.172$ and $M_p = 0.776$ GeV
- Current quark masses used: $m_u = m_d = 5$ MeV, $m_s = 150$ MeV; meson masses: $m_\pi = 140$ MeV, $m_K = 495$ MeV; decay constants: $F_\pi = 93$ MeV, $F_K = 113$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of $K_{l3}$ decay vector form factors (Nam and Kim, 2007) by computing both vector and tensor generalized form factors in the space-like region and deriving the transverse quark spin density for the $K\to\pi$ transition. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations by Baum et al. (2011) for the tensor form factor, showing good agreement after scale evolution. The transverse quark spin densities are found to be very similar to those of the pion and kaon computed previously by the same group (Nam and Kim, 2010; Nam and Kim, 2011), and the approach enables future studies of $K\to\pi$ transition generalized parton distributions and their Mellin moments.
