# Pseudoscalar Masses in the Effective Theory for Color-Flavor Locking in high density QCD

> The constants of the effective action describing the massless modes in the color-flavor-locking phase of QCD have been recently evaluated at high density. Values of the pseudoscalar masses for nonvanishing quark masses have also been given. The calculated values show however a puzzling feature: when $m_u=m_d=0$ and $m_s\not=0$ all the goldstones remain massless. We show that an additional invariant is present which avoids this feature. We costruct this invariant and discuss the emerging mass pattern for the pseudogoldstones. This provides for a complete scheme for the pseudogoldstone masses in the color-flavor-locking phase of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an additional invariant term in the effective Lagrangian for the color-flavor-locking (CFL) phase of QCD resolves a puzzling feature where all goldstones remained massless when \(m_u=m_d=0\) and \(m_s\neq0\); this new term produces a mass pattern of the usual hierarchical type and avoids the problem that a symmetry-breaking term in the fundamental Lagrangian would otherwise have no effect in the effective action.

## Method

The authors construct an additional invariant \(\Delta_2 = -f(\text{Tr}(M^\dagger M)) \text{Tr}[M^* \Sigma] V^2 + \text{h.c.}\) at order \(M^2\) in the effective theory for the CFL phase, where \(M\) is the quark mass matrix, \(\Sigma\) is the coset field, and \(V\) encodes the \(U(1)_A\) field. They match the shift of the vacuum energy from QCD at very high density to that of the effective theory for two cases: \(M = m \mathbf{1}_3\) and \(M = \text{diag}(0,0,m_s)\), using results from Son (1999) to determine the function \(f\) and the constant \(c\) for the previously known invariant \(\Delta_1\).

## Key numbers

- Vacuum energy shift for \(M = m \mathbf{1}_3\): \(\Delta \mathcal{E}_{\text{vac}} = -\frac{9\mu^2}{8\pi} m^2\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential.
- Vacuum energy shift for \(M = \text{diag}(0,0,m_s)\): \(\Delta \mathcal{E}_{\text{vac}} = -\frac{3\mu^2}{8\pi} m_s^2\).
- The function \(f\) is determined as \(f(\text{Tr}(M^\dagger M)) = -\frac{3\mu^2}{8\pi} \sqrt{\text{Tr}(M^\dagger M)}\).
- The constant \(c\) for the \(\Delta_1\) invariant is \(c = \frac{9(1-\sqrt{3})}{8\pi} \mu^2\).
- Decay constants from Son (1999): \(F_T^2 = (21 - 8\ln 2)\frac{\mu^2}{9\pi^2}\), \(f_T^2 = \frac{9\mu^2}{\pi^2} \approx f_A^2\).

## Context

This work extends the effective theory for the CFL phase developed by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999) and the evaluation of pseudoscalar masses by Son (1999) at very high density. It corrects a puzzling feature in Son (1999) where all goldstones remained massless for \(m_u=m_d=0\) and \(m_s\neq0\) by introducing an additional invariant that reproduces an Adler-Dashen-type mechanism with a condensate that vanishes with quark masses due to \(Z_2\) invariance. The result provides a complete scheme for pseudogoldstone masses in the CFL phase and enables further study of the transition between two-flavor and three-flavor high-density QCD.
