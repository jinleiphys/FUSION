# Coherent and incoherent antineutrino scattering on stable even-even isotopes of molybdenum detectors

> The recent observations of the coherent neutrino- and antineutrino-nucleus scattering have opened up a plethora of opportunities to probe physics within standard and non-standard theories of the electroweak interactions. In the present article, our goal is to explore the possibility of using the molybdenum material as detection medium for coherent and incoherent antineutrino- and neutrino- Mo scattering in the ongoing and future coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) experiments by using relevant (anti-)neutrino beams as e.g. stopped pion-decay neutrino beams, reactor antineutrino beams, astrophysical (solar or supernova) (anti)neutrino beams, etc. Our present coherent and incoherent scattering cross sections of Mo isotopes with neutrinos and antineutrinos are based on the deformed shell model (DSM) that has been previously employed for studying similar processes. On the other hand, in the past, CEνNS events obtained with this model provided us with better fits to COHERENT experimental data compared to phenomenological form factors.

# Coherent and incoherent antineutrino scattering on stable even-even isotopes of molybdenum detectors

## Key claim

The deformed shell model (DSM) predictions for coherent and incoherent antineutrino scattering cross sections on even-even molybdenum isotopes show that the coherent channel dominates the total cross section by more than 85% for all five isotopes studied, with the ratio of coherent to total cross section ranging from approximately 85% to 94% depending on the isotope and whether neutrinos or antineutrinos are considered. The authors note that these are Standard Model predictions, and that measured CEνNS event rates in experiments have been larger than SM predictions, leaving room for beyond-Standard-Model physics.

## Method

The authors employed the Donnelly-Walecka multipole decomposition method combined with deformed shell model (DSM) nuclear structure calculations to compute neutral-current neutrino-nucleus and antineutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections for the five stable even-even molybdenum isotopes (92,94,96,98,100Mo). For 92Mo, new DSM wave functions were generated using the GWBXG effective interaction with a 66Ni inert core, and tested against experimental data for yrast levels, magnetic moments, and B(E2) values. For the other four isotopes, previously published DSM wave functions were used. The differential cross sections were computed state-by-state for transitions from the ground state to low-lying excited states up to about 15 MeV excitation energy, then integrated over angles to obtain cross sections as a function of excitation energy.

- Coherent cross section for 100Mo: 1692.9 × 10^(-42) MeV^(-1) cm^2 (identical for neutrinos and antineutrinos)
- Incoherent cross section for 92Mo with neutrinos: 67.7 × 10^(-42) MeV^(-1) cm^2; with antineutrinos: 63.1 × 10^(-42) MeV^(-1) cm^2
- Incoherent cross section for 100Mo with neutrinos: 290.0 × 10^(-42) MeV^(-1) cm^2; with antineutrinos: 263.5 × 10^(-42) MeV^(-1) cm^2
- Ratio ρ (coherent/total) for 92Mo: 93.65% (neutrinos), 94.05% (antineutrinos)
- Ratio ρ for 94Mo: 88.57% (neutrinos), 89.77% (antineutrinos)
- Ratio ρ for 96Mo: 85.34% (neutrinos), 86.66% (antineutrinos)
- Ratio ρ for 98Mo: 91.07% (neutrinos), 91.82% (antineutrinos)
- Ratio ρ for 100Mo: 85.37% (neutrinos), 86.53% (antineutrinos)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DSM-based calculations for neutrino scattering on 94,96,98,100Mo (Kosmas et al., JPG2024; MDP1; MDP2) to include 92Mo and to provide antineutrino scattering cross sections for all five even-even molybdenum isotopes. The results are relevant for ongoing and future CEνNS experiments using molybdenum detectors, such as the NUCLEUS experiment (which aims for 20 eV nuclear recoil thresholds at the Chooz reactor) and the AMoRE neutrinoless double beta decay experiment. The authors note that the COHERENT collaboration's CEνNS data were better fit by DSM-based predictions than by phenomenological form factors, and that the CONUS+ experiment measured (395 ± 106) reactor antineutrino events on germanium (with SM prediction of 347 ± 59), indicating room for BSM physics such as neutrino magnetic moments, millicharges, or light mediators.
