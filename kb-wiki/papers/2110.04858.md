# Nuclear matter effects on jet production at electron-ion colliders

> In these proceedings we report recent progress on understanding hadron and jet production in electron-nucleus collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider [1,2]. These processes will play an essential role in the exploration of the partonic structure of nuclei and the study of parton shower evolution in strongly-interacting matter. We employ the framework of soft-collinear effective theory, generalized to include in-medium interactions, to present the first theoretical results for inclusive hadron and jet cross sections, as well as the jet charge modification in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei. We further demonstrate how to separate initial-state and final-state effects.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first theoretical results for inclusive hadron and jet cross sections and jet charge modification in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei using soft-collinear effective theory with in-medium interactions, but the results are conditional on transport parameters constrained by HERMES data and are specific to the kinematic regime of the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Method
The authors employed soft-collinear effective theory generalized to include in-medium interactions to calculate inclusive hadron and jet cross sections and jet charge in electron-nucleus collisions. They used QCD factorization with nuclear parton distribution functions and included medium corrections to fragmentation functions and semi-inclusive jet functions via in-medium splitting functions. The ratio of cross sections in electron-gold to electron-proton collisions was studied to isolate nuclear effects, and a strategy using different jet radii was developed to separate initial-state from final-state effects.

## Key numbers
- For 5 GeV(e) × 40 GeV(A) collisions, suppression of pions is largest in the nucleus-going direction and qualitatively similar to HERMES data
- For D-mesons in the same collision system, significant suppression for large z values evolves to enhancement for z < 0.65
- For 10 GeV(e) × 100 GeV(A) collisions with jet radius R=0.3, the suppression is close to a factor of two, similar to heavy-ion collisions
- For up-quark jets, the ratio ⟨Q_{κ,q}^{eA}⟩/⟨Q_{κ,q}^{ep}⟩ shows medium-induced scaling violations of order 10%
- For inclusive jets with 2<η<4 in 18 × 275 GeV e+Au collisions, the jet charge modification is about 30%

## Context
This work extends theoretical developments from heavy-ion collisions to the electron-nucleus environment at the future Electron-Ion Collider, where kinematics differ significantly from A+A collisions. The authors note that light hadron production in DIS on nuclei at HERMES (Airapetian 2007) has not been able to differentiate between competing models of parton energy loss, as reviewed by Accardi (2009). The results enable future studies of heavy flavor-tagged charm and bottom jets for tomography of cold nuclear matter and tests of the mass hierarchy of jet quenching effects.
