# Evidence for Statistical Production of J/psi Mesons in Nuclear Collisions at 158--200 A GeV

> The hypothesis of statistical production of J/psi mesons at hadronization is formulated and checked against experimental data. It explains in the natural way the observed scaling behavior of the J/psi to pion ratio at the CERN SPS energies. Using the multiplicities of J/psi and eta mesons the hadronization temperature T_H = 175 MeV is found, which agrees with the previous estimates of the temperature parameter based on the analysis of the hadron yield systematics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a dominant fraction of J/psi mesons in hadronic and nuclear collisions at CERN SPS energies is produced statistically at hadronization, rather than through hard creation of c-cbar pairs followed by suppression; this hypothesis explains the observed scaling of the J/psi-to-pion ratio without requiring accidental cancellation of large effects.

## Method

The authors formulate a statistical model in the grand canonical approximation where the J/psi multiplicity is proportional to the volume of hadronizing matter at a universal hadronization temperature T_H, and the pion multiplicity is also proportional to this volume because pions carry most of the entropy. They test the hypothesis by comparing the measured ratio of J/psi to eta meson multiplicities with the ratio predicted by the statistical model, using Eq. (4) to extract T_H.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature T_H = 170 ± 2 MeV, derived from the measured ratio ⟨J/ψ⟩/⟨η⟩ = (1.3 ± 0.3) × 10⁻⁵
- Additional systematic error on T_H of about 7 MeV, estimated from a maximum 50% uncertainty on the ⟨J/ψ⟩/⟨η⟩ ratio due to resonance decay contributions
- J/psi mass m_ψ ≈ 3097 MeV and eta mass m_η ≈ 547 MeV used in the statistical model calculation
- The ratio ⟨J/ψ⟩/⟨h⁻⟩ is approximately constant for center-of-mass energies √s ≳ 20 GeV, as shown in Fig. 2 for proton-nucleon interactions

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach previously applied to hadron multiplicities in e⁺+e⁻, p+p, and p+p̅ interactions by Becattini (1996) and to A+A collisions at SPS by Gorenstein (1999) and Becattini (1998). It directly contradicts the standard charmonium suppression picture (Matsui and Satz, 1986; Shuryak, 1978) where J/psi suppression in nuclear collisions is used as a probe of early-stage high-density matter, and specifically the anomalous suppression in central Pb+Pb collisions attributed to quark-gluon plasma formation (Satz, 1997). If correct, the paper implies that J/psi yields are not sensitive to the early stage of A+A collisions and that J/psi production is independent of open charm production.
