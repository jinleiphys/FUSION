# SQM2022: Theoretical Summary

## Key claim

The standard multi-stage reaction model for high-energy nuclear collisions (initial energy deposition, hydrodynamic expansion, Cooper-Frye hadronization, hadronic transport) may have fundamental flaws: causality violations in the early hydrodynamic phase are severe when fluid dynamics is initiated directly from IP-glasma initial conditions at τ = 0.4 fm/c, and even with a pre-hydrodynamic stage starting at τ = 0.8 fm/c, causality remains strongly violated near the fireball surface for approximately 20% of the hydrodynamic duration.

## Method

The author presents a theoretical summary lecture covering selected topics from the SQM2022 conference, including analysis of causality criteria in second-order viscous relativistic hydrodynamics applied to IP-glasma initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV; evaluation of core-corona models; discussion of critical point dynamics using the Hydro+ approach; assessment of the RHIC isobar blind analysis results for chiral magnetic effect signatures; and examination of statistical hadronization model predictions for hypertriton production including finite-size corrections.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: when fluid dynamics is initiated at τ = 0.4 fm/c directly from IP-glasma initial conditions, almost all fluid cells strongly violate causality; when preceded by free-streaming or kinetic transport and initialized at τ = 0.8 fm/c, causality remains strongly violated near the surface for approximately 20% of the hydrodynamic duration, during which more than half of the final elliptic flow is generated
- The transition to core dominance occurs at dNch/dη ≈ 20 for both Pb+Pb and p+p systems
- For central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 5 GeV: maximum energy density of slightly more than 1 GeV/fm³ and baryon number density nB ≈ 0.7 fm⁻³ is reached only at τ ≈ 4.5 fm/c
- The new lattice equation of state parametrization covers μB/T ≤ 3.5, corresponding to √sNN ≈ 6 GeV
- The STAR isobar blind analysis found that corrections reduce the expected ratio (Ru/Zr) for CME signatures by approximately 3% from unity to 0.97; measured values for all CME observables agree with this revised baseline within 0.5%
- The statistical hadronization model describes particle yields in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV over 9 orders of magnitude with a common formation temperature Tchem ≈ 156 MeV
- The hypertriton ³ΛH has an intrinsic radius a > 5 fm

## Context

This theoretical summary extends the overview presented by J. Kapusta at the same conference series and assesses progress across multiple fronts in heavy-ion physics. The causality violation findings challenge the standard multi-stage reaction model that has been validated in many previous investigations. The work connects to ongoing RHIC beam energy scan efforts (down to √sNN ≈ 6 GeV), the isobar comparison run results from STAR (2021), and lattice QCD equation of state developments. The discussion of hypertriton production reconciles statistical hadronization model predictions with coalescence model interpretations, while the Bayesian model-to-data comparisons by the Jyvaskyla group represent increasingly ambitious computational approaches. The field is also noted to be converging with neutron star astrophysics through constraints on the equation of state of dense nuclear matter.
