# The quark strange star in the enlarged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> The strange quark star is investigated within the enlarged SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL). The stable quark star exists till maximal configutation with rho_m=3.1, 10^15, g/cm^3 with M_m=1.61, M_Sun and R_m=8.74, km is reached. Strange quarks appear for density above rho_c=9.84 g/cm^3 for the quark star with radius R_c=8.003 km and M_c=0.77, M_Sun. The comparison of a quark star properties obtained in the Quark Mean Field (QMF) approach to a neutron star model constructed within the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory is presented.

## Key claim

Strange quark stars constructed within the enlarged SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, which includes vector mesons, are stable up to a maximum configuration with central density 3.1 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³, yielding a mass of 1.61 M⊙ and radius 8.74 km; however, the model lacks a confinement mechanism, so the quark star at low densities behaves unphysically like a neutron star or white dwarf unless an infrared cutoff is introduced.

## Method

The authors used the three-flavor NJL model in the mean-field approximation, incorporating scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector meson interactions via bosonization, and applied the Feynman-Bogolubov variational method to compute the thermodynamic potential and effective quark masses. The resulting equation of state (EoS) was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric quark star configurations under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality. The quark star properties were compared with those from the Quark Mean Field (QMF) model and the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory using the TM1 parameter set for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum stable quark star configuration: central density ρₘ = 3.1 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³, mass Mₘ = 1.61 M⊙, radius Rₘ = 8.74 km.
- Strange quarks appear above a critical density ρ_c = 9.84 g/cm³ (likely a typo for 9.84 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³ based on context), corresponding to a quark star with radius R_c = 8.003 km and mass M_c = 0.77 M⊙.
- The baryon number of the maximum strange quark star corresponds to a pure neutron star with baryon mass M_B = 2.126 M⊙.
- The threshold for strange quark appearance in the star is at baryon density n_B^s = 3.94 n_B⁰, where n_B⁰ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³ is the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter.
- The effective bag constant B_eff^{1/4} ranges from 150 to 180 MeV at high density.
- The infrared cutoff λ = δ (the dimensionless effective quark mass) was introduced to avoid free quark contributions at low densities.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strange quark stars using the NJL model, particularly following the three-flavor approach of Buballa and Oertel (1998) and the enlarged NJL model with vector mesons from Manka et al. (2001). It contrasts the NJL-based quark star with the Quark Mean Field (QMF) model of Shen and Toki (2000) and the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) neutron star model using the TM1 parameter set of Sugahara and Toki (1994). The results enable comparison of quark star mass-radius relations with neutron star models and suggest that a neutron star with baryon mass 2.126 M⊙ could transition to a strange quark star while conserving baryon number.
