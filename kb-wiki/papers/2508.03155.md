# Machine Learning-Driven High-Precision Model for $α$-Decay Energy and Half-Life Prediction of superheavy nuclei

> Based on Extreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost) framework optimized via Bayesian hyperparameter tuning, we investigated the α-decay energy and half-life of superheavy nuclei. By incorporating key nuclear structural features-including mass number, proton-to-neutron ratio, magic number proximity, and angular momentum transfer-the optimized model captures essential physical mechanisms governing $α$-decay. On the test set, the model achieves significantly lower mean absolute error (MAE) and root mean square error (RMSE) compared to empirical models such as Royer and Budaca, particularly in the low-energy region. SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) analysis confirms these mechanisms are dominated by decay energy, angular momentum barriers, and shell effects. This work establishes a physically consistent, data-driven tool for nuclear property prediction and offers valuable insights into $α$-decay processes from a machine learning perspective.

## Key claim
The XGBoost model achieves systematically lower prediction errors for α-decay energies and half-lives compared to empirical formulas like the Royer formula and Universal Decay Law, but this performance is conditional on the inclusion of physics-informed features such as magic-number proximity, minimum orbital angular-momentum transfer, and quadrupole deformation, and is evaluated on a test set where the model shows particular improvement in the low-energy region.

## Method
The authors constructed two separate XGBoost regression models: one for α-decay energy (Qα) using 1623 nuclei with Z between 50 and 118, and one for half-life (log10 T1/2) using 498 nuclei with Z between 64 and 118. Input features included mass number, proton and neutron numbers, isospin asymmetry, minimum orbital angular momentum lmin, quadrupole deformation parameter β2, and distance to nearest proton magic number. Model performance was evaluated using five-fold cross-validation, and feature importance was analyzed using SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) attribution. The half-life model used experimentally measured Qα values from AME2020 rather than predicted ones.

## Key numbers
- The Qα prediction dataset contained 1623 nuclei with Z from 50 to 118 and Qα > 0
- The half-life prediction dataset contained 498 nuclei with Z from 64 to 118
- Experimental α-decay energies in the half-life dataset spanned 2.20 to 11.84 MeV
- The model achieved lower mean absolute error (MAE) and root mean square error (RMSE) than the Royer formula and Budaca model on the test set
- The half-life dataset covered nuclei with mass numbers across the range of superheavy nuclei

## Context
This work extends the application of gradient-boosted decision trees to α-decay prediction, building on earlier machine-learning approaches including artificial neural networks (Jin 2023, Ma 2023), Bayesian neural networks, Gaussian process regression (Jalili 2024), and support vector machines. The authors compare their XGBoost model against the empirical Royer formula (Royer 2000) and the Universal Decay Law, which have fixed functional forms that limit their ability to capture shell effects, nuclear deformation, and angular-momentum hindrance. The physically informed feature design and SHAP interpretability analysis enable this framework to be applied for predicting half-lives in regions where experimental data remain scarce.
