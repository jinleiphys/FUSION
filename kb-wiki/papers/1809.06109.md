# Breaking and restoration of rotational symmetry in the low-energy spectrum of light alpha-conjugate nuclei on the lattice I: $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ and $^{12}\mathrm{C}$

> The breaking of rotational symmetry on the lattice for bound eigenstates of the two lightest alpha conjugate nuclei is explored. Moreover, a macroscopic alpha-cluster model is used for investigating the general problems associated with the representation of a physical many-body problem on a cubic lattice. In view of the descent from the 3D rotation group to the cubic group symmetry, the role of the squared total angular momentum operator in the classification of the lattice eigenstates in terms of SO(3) irreps is discussed. In particular, the behaviour of the average values of the latter operator, the Hamiltonian and the inter-particle distance as a function of lattice spacing and size is studied by considering the $0^+$, $2^+$, $4^+$ and $6^+$ (artificial) bound states of $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ and the lowest $0^+$, $2^+$ and $3^-$ multiplets of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how rotational symmetry is broken on a cubic lattice for bound states of light alpha-conjugate nuclei, and shows that the average value of the squared total angular momentum operator can be used to identify the SO(3) irreducible representations to which lattice eigenstates belong, provided the lattice spacing is not too large (e.g., a less than or about 1.5 fm for one configuration of 8Be) and the lattice volume is sufficiently large (e.g., L greater than or about 18 fm).

## Method

The authors adopt a macroscopic alpha-cluster model treating 4He nuclei as spinless particles interacting via an isotropic Ali-Bodmer type potential (a superposition of attractive and repulsive Gaussians) plus Coulomb and three-body Gaussian potentials. They discretize the Hamiltonian on a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions, implement improved derivative operators for the kinetic energy, and construct the lattice version of the squared total angular momentum operator. They then compute the average values of the Hamiltonian, the squared angular momentum, and inter-particle distances as functions of lattice spacing and box size for bound states of 8Be (0+, 2+, 4+, 6+ artificial states) and 12C (lowest 0+, 2+, 3- multiplets).

## Key numbers

* For 8Be, the squared angular momentum operator provides reliable identification of SO(3) multiplets when lattice spacing a is less than about 1.5 fm and lattice volume L is greater than about 18 fm for one configuration, and when a is less than about 0.65 fm and L greater than about 12 fm for another configuration.
* The Ali-Bodmer potential parameters are: eta_0^{-1} = 2.29 fm, V_0 = -216.3 MeV for the attractive Gaussian; eta_1^{-1} = 1.89 fm, V_1 = -353.5 MeV for the repulsive Gaussian.
* The Coulomb interaction uses an rms radius for the alpha particle of R_alpha = 1.44 fm.
* The three-body potential has range lambda = 0.005 fm^{-2} and amplitude V_0 = -4.41 MeV, originally fitted to reproduce the 12C binding energy and the spacing between the Hoyle state (0_2+ at 7.65 MeV) and the 2_1+ state (at 4.44 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume and discretization analysis of BNL (2014) to higher angular momentum multiplets in 8Be and 12C, using a macroscopic alpha-cluster model with foundations in Wheeler (1937) and following the recent literature on alpha-conjugate nuclei. The study enables systematic understanding of rotational symmetry breaking artifacts in lattice calculations of nuclear bound states, and the authors indicate that a similar analysis for 16O is the subject of a forthcoming paper.
