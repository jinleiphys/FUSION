# Lattice study of correlators of chromoelectric fields for heavy quarkonium dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma

> We perform a lattice calculation of the correlators of two chromoelectric fields in the adjoint representation connected by adjoint Wilson lines at non-zero temperature. These correlators arise in the study of quarkonium dynamics and of adjoint heavy quark diffusion in deconfined matter. We work in SU(3) gauge theory using either gradient flow or multi-level algorithms for noise reduction, and discuss the renormalization of the correlators on the lattice. We find that a Casimir factor rescaling relates the adjoint correlators corresponding to the diffusion of an adjoint heavy quark and the octet-octet quarkonium transitions to the chromoelectric correlator in the fundamental representation describing the diffusion of a heavy quark.

# Lattice study of correlators of chromoelectric fields for heavy quarkonium dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The nonperturbative lattice calculation shows that the symmetric adjoint chromoelectric correlators $G_E^{\mathrm{oct}}$ and $G_E^{\mathrm{sym}}$ have the same shape as the fundamental correlator $G_E^{\mathrm{fund}}$ studied previously, with ratios matching the leading-order perturbative prediction even in the nonperturbative regime. For the non-symmetric correlator $G_E$, the renormalization procedure using the Polyakov loop provides effective results that agree with NLO perturbation theory at high temperature $T = 10^4 T_c$.

## Method
The authors performed lattice calculations of chromoelectric field correlators in the adjoint representation in quenched SU(3) gauge theory at two temperatures ($T = 1.5 T_c$ and $T = 10^4 T_c$). They used gradient flow for noise reduction and renormalization of the chromoelectric field insertions, and additionally employed a multi-level algorithm for the non-symmetric correlator $G_E$ as a cross-check. The correlators were computed using both clover and 2-plaquette discretizations of the field strength tensor, with tree-level improvement applied to reduce discretization errors, followed by continuum extrapolation in $1/N_\tau^2$ and zero-flow-time extrapolation.

## Key numbers
- The ratio between octet-octet and fundamental diffusion coefficients is $\kappa_{\mathrm{oct}} = (5/4)\kappa_{\mathrm{fund}}$, derived from the LO relation $G_E^{\mathrm{oct}}/G_E^{\mathrm{fund}} = 2(N^2-4)/(N^2-1)$ with $N=3$.
- The ratio between symmetric adjoint and fundamental diffusion coefficients is $\kappa_{\mathrm{sym}} = (C_A/C_F)\kappa_{\mathrm{fund}}$, where $C_A/C_F = 9/4$ for SU(3).
- Lattice calculations were performed at $T = 1.5 T_c$ and $T = 10^4 T_c$, with temporal extents $N_\tau = 16, 20, 24, 28$ for the lower temperature and $N_\tau = 16, 20, 24$ for the higher temperature.
- The flow time condition used was $a \leq \sqrt{8\tau_F} \leq \tau/3$, with fixed ratios $\sqrt{8\tau_F}/\tau = 0.232$ and $0.291$ shown in continuum extrapolations.
- At $T = 10^4 T_c$, the NLO perturbative band was obtained by varying the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ renormalization scale in the range $[\Lambda/2, 2\Lambda]$ where $\Lambda \approx 6.74 T$.

## Context
This work extends the lattice study of chromoelectric correlators from the fundamental representation (Brambilla 2022) to the adjoint representation, which is required for describing quarkonium transport in the quark-gluon plasma within the pNRQCD and open quantum system framework (Brambilla 2016, Brambilla 2017). The results confirm that the leading-order Casimir scaling relations between fundamental and adjoint correlators hold nonperturbatively, enabling the extraction of momentum diffusion coefficients for octet-octet and singlet-octet transitions. Future work will focus on extracting the diffusion coefficient for the non-symmetric correlator using NLO spectral functions, and extending the study to zero temperature for applications such as gluelump masses and quarkonium P-wave decays (Brambilla 2001, Brambilla 2002, Brambilla 2020).
