# Modifications on parameters of $Z(4430)$ in a dense medium

> The charmonium-like resonance $ Z_c(3900) $ and its excited state $ Z(4430) $ are among the particles that are serious candidates for double heavy tetraquarks. Calculations of different parameters associated with these states both in the vacuum and the medium with finite density are of great importance. Such investigations help us clarify their nature, internal quark-gluon organization and quantum numbers. In this accordance, we extend our previous analyses on the ground state $ Z_c(3900) $ to investigate the medium modifications on different parameters of the excited $ Z(4430) $ state. In particular, we calculate the mass, vector self-energy and current coupling of $ Z(4430) $ in terms of density, up to a density comparable to the density of the cores of massive neutron stars. The obtained results may help experimental groups aiming to study the behavior of exotic states at higher densities.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the in-medium modifications of the mass, vector self-energy, and current coupling of the excited tetraquark state Z(4430) up to five times nuclear saturation density, finding that the in-medium mass reduces to approximately 74% of its vacuum value at saturation density, with the caveat that the sum rules give reliable results only up to 1.6 times saturation density, requiring fit functions to extend to higher densities.

## Method

The authors adopt a compact tetraquark/diquark-antidiquark interpretation of Z(4430) as the first radial excitation of Zc(3900) with JP=1+ and use QCD sum rules in cold nuclear matter. They derive the in-medium two-point correlation function using an interpolating current with quark content c̄cūd, include non-perturbative operators up to dimension five, and apply a "ground state + radially excited state + continuum" scheme to extract the parameters of Z(4430) using the previously calculated parameters of Zc(3900) as inputs.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum mass of Z(4430): 4486+112-113 MeV, consistent with the world experimental average of 4478+15-18 MeV (Particle Data Group 2018)
- In-medium mass at nuclear matter saturation density (ρsat = 0.113 GeV3): 3311+203-185 MeV, a reduction of approximately 26% from the vacuum value
- Vector self-energy at saturation density: 1310+101-86 MeV
- In-medium current coupling at saturation density: (1.34+0.78-0.45) × 10-2 GeV4
- Vacuum current coupling: (1.27+0.53-0.36) × 10-2 GeV4
- Working ranges: Borel mass parameter M2 in [3-5] GeV2, in-medium continuum threshold s0* in [21.9-23.8] GeV2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule analysis of the ground state Zc(3900) (Azizi 2020) to its radial excitation Z(4430), adopting the hypothesis from Maiani (2014) that the mass spacing in radial excitations resembles that in standard P-wave charmonia. The results can be tested by in-medium experiments at FAIR/GSI and NICA/Dubna, and by heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, which can produce exotic states at high densities comparable to the cores of massive neutron stars (up to 5ρsat).
