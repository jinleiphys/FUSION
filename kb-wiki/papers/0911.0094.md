# The Glasma and the Hard Ridge

> Correlation measurements indicate that excess two particle correlations extend over causally disconnected rapidity ranges. Although, this enhancement is broad in relative rapidity $η=η_1 - η_2$, it is focused in a narrow region in relative azimuthal angle $ϕ=ϕ_1 - ϕ_2$. The resulting structure looks like a ridge centered at $η= ϕ=0$. Similar ridge structures are observed in correlations of particles associated with a jet trigger (the hard ridge) and in correlations without a trigger (the soft ridge). The long range rapidity behavior requires that the correlation originates in the earliest stage of the collision, and probes properties of the production mechanism. Glasma initial conditions as predicted by the theory of Color Glass Condensate and provide a and early stage correlation that naturally extends far in rapidity. We have previously shown that the soft ridge is a consequence of particles forming from an initial Glasma phase that experience a later stage transverse flow. We extend this work to study the ridge dependence on the $p_t$ of the correlated pairs. We then determine the soft contribution to the hard ridge.

# The Glasma and the Hard Ridge

## Key claim
The soft ridge and hard ridge in two-particle correlations can be explained by a common mechanism: particle production from an initial Glasma phase followed by transverse flow, with the soft (bulk-bulk) contribution remaining significant even in the momentum ranges of the hard ridge measurement.

## Method
The authors model the initial state using Color Glass Condensate (CGC) theory, where particles are produced from longitudinal color flux tubes that are uniform in rapidity and localized in transverse area. They compute the final-state momentum correlation function by combining Glasma initial conditions with a blast-wave model for transverse flow, then extend this framework to include jet production and quenching following the model of Shuryak (2007). The relative contributions of bulk-bulk and jet-bulk correlations to the hard ridge are determined by decomposing the measured particle spectrum into thermal and jet components using a scale parameter.

## Key numbers
- Saturation scale: Q_s ~ 1-2 GeV
- Jet attenuation length: l_abs = 0.25 fm
- The scale at which the spectrum deviates from exponential behavior (jet fraction parameter): p_s = 1.25 GeV at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Bulk-bulk correlations make up approximately 75% of the total hard ridge amplitude
- The azimuthal width of the soft ridge is independent of energy and nearly independent of system size for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu at 200 and 62 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous model (Gavin 2008) that explained the soft ridge in Au+Au collisions, now applying it to Cu+Cu systems and to the momentum dependence of the ridge. It builds on the Glasma/CGC framework of Dumitru (2008) and incorporates jet quenching effects following Shuryak (2007). The results enable experimental discrimination between soft and hard contributions to ridge measurements at RHIC, and provide predictions for the centrality and momentum dependence of correlation observables in both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions.
