# $dm/dμ$ in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> Using the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model we study responses of the pion and kaon masses to changes in the chemical potential, $dm/dμ$, at zero and finite chemical potential. We find that the behavior of $dm/dμ$ for the pion is quite different from that for the kaon. Our results can give a clue for future studies of $dm/dμ$ on the lattice.

## Key claim

Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the authors find that the response of the pion mass to changes in chemical potential (dm/dμ) is quite different from that of the kaon, but caution that direct comparison with lattice QCD results is not possible because the lattice calculates dm/dμ for the screening mass while the NJL model calculates it for the pole mass.

## Method

The authors use the SU(3) NJL model with a 't Hooft determinant anomaly term, taking parameters from Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) with a momentum cutoff Λ = 631.4 MeV. They calculate the response of quark condensates to chemical potential by differentiating the gap equations, then use these to obtain dm/dμ for kaons and pions by differentiating the dispersion relations at fixed temperature. They consider both isoscalar (μ_S = μ_u + μ_s for kaon, μ_u + μ_d for pion) and isovector (μ_V = μ_s - μ_u for kaon, μ_d - μ_u for pion) chemical potentials, and work primarily with the "Case II" scenario where only the anomaly coupling g_D has temperature dependence.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff Λ = 631.4 MeV, with coupling constants g_SΛ² = 3.67 and g_DΛ⁵ = -9.29
- Current quark masses: m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 135.7 MeV
- For the kaon at zero chemical potential, dm_K/dμ_S is almost zero below T ~ 0.04 GeV
- For the kaon, a sign change in dm_K/dμ_S occurs between μ_u = μ_s = 0.06 GeV and 0.08 GeV
- For the pion with non-degenerate u and d quark masses (m_u = 4 MeV, m_d = 7 MeV), a transition point in dm_π/dμ_S for π⁻ appears between μ_u = μ_d = 0.004 GeV and 0.006 GeV
- The temperature dependence of the anomaly coupling uses T_0 = 0.1 GeV in the exponential factor exp[-(T/T_0)²]

## Context

This work extends NJL model calculations of hadron properties in dense matter, following the approach of Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) and earlier studies of dm/dμ by Ruivo and de Sousa (1996, 1997). The results are intended to provide guidance for lattice QCD simulations attempting to study matter at high baryon density, where the complex-action problem makes direct calculations difficult. The authors note that second-order responses (d²m/dμ²) are much larger than first-order responses, suggesting clearer signals for future lattice studies.
