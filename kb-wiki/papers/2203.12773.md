# Investigating Effects of Relativistic Kinematics, Dimensionality, Interactions, and Short-Range Correlations on the Ratio of Quartic over Quadratic Nuclear Symmetry Energies

> While ample evidence for the so-called empirical parabolic law of the Equation of State (EOS) of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) has been obtained in many studies within both non-relativistic and relativistic nuclear many-body theories using various interactions, it has been unclear if there is any fundamental physics reason for the small quartic symmetry energy compared to the quadratic one even as the ANM approaches pure neutron matter. Within both relativistic and non-relativistic Free Fermi Gas (FFG) models in coordinate spaces of arbitrary dimension $d$ with and without considering Short-Range Correlations (SRC) as well as non-linear Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models, we study effects of relativistic kinematics, dimensionality, interactions and SRC on the ratio $Ψ(ρ)$ of quartic over quadratic symmetry energies in ANM EOSs. We found that the ratio $Ψ(ρ)$ in the FFG model depends strongly on the dimension $d$. While it is very small already in the normal 3D space, it could be even smaller in spaces with reduced dimensions for sub-systems of particles in heavy-ion reactions and/or whole neutron stars due to constraints, collectivities and/or symmetries. We also found that the ratio $Ψ(ρ)$ could theoretically become very large only at the ultra-relativistic limit far above the density reachable in neutron stars. On the other hand, nuclear interaction directly and/or indirectly through SRC-induced high-momentum nucleons affect significantly the density dependence of $Ψ(ρ)$ compared to the relativistic FFG model prediction. The SRC affects significantly not only the kinetic energy of symmetric nuclear matter but also the ratio $Ψ(ρ)$ while the relativistic corrections are found negligible. The results may help better understand the EOS of dense neutron-rich matter.

## Key claim

The paper finds no fundamental physics reason for the small ratio of quartic over quadratic symmetry energies in asymmetric nuclear matter, but shows that the smallness arises naturally from the combination of three-dimensional space, non-relativistic kinematics, and the specific way nuclear interactions and short-range correlations affect the density dependence of this ratio.

## Method

The authors calculated the ratio Ψ(ρ) = E_sym,4(ρ)/E_sym(ρ) using relativistic and non-relativistic Free Fermi Gas models in coordinate spaces of arbitrary dimension d (1 to 4), with and without short-range correlations, as well as nonlinear Relativistic Mean Field models using the FSUGold parameter set and a constructed parameter set with modified coupling constants. They expanded the kinetic energy per nucleon in even powers of isospin asymmetry δ and extracted the coefficients analytically for the FFG models, while numerically evaluating the RMF predictions.

## Key numbers

- In the non-relativistic 3D FFG model, the ratio Ψ = 1/27 ≈ 3.7%, with E_sym,4^kin(ρ_0) ≈ 0.45 MeV and E_sym^kin(ρ_0) ≈ 12.3 MeV at saturation density ρ_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3
- In the relativistic FFG model, Ψ ranges from 1/27 (non-relativistic limit) to 5/54 ≈ 9.3% (ultra-relativistic limit), with the boundary ν = k_F/M ≈ 1 occurring at ρ ≈ 45 ρ_0
- The first-order relativistic correction to the kinetic symmetry energy at ρ_0 is about -0.48 MeV
- In the FSUGold RMF model, the ultra-relativistic limit gives Ψ_UR^RMF ≈ 7.8%, with kinetic contributions dominating over potential contributions (potential/kinetic ratio ≈ 0.24 for E_sym and ≈ 0.04 for E_sym,4)
- In the d-dimensional FFG model, the ratio Ψ_d^σ(j=2) = (1/12)(σ/d - 2)(σ/d - 1), giving Ψ = 0 in both 1D and 2D for σ=2, and Ψ = 1/2 for (σ=4, d=1)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the empirical parabolic law of the nuclear EOS by Bom91 and Lee98, which established that the quartic symmetry energy at saturation density is less than about 2 MeV. The authors build on findings by Cai16 that incorporating short-range correlations from experiments (Hen14, Due18, Sch19, Sch20) can increase the kinetic quartic symmetry energy to about 7 MeV at ρ_0. The results enable future investigations of nuclear EOS in reduced-dimensional subsystems that may arise in heavy-ion reactions or neutron star mergers, and clarify that relativistic corrections alone cannot explain the smallness of Ψ, while nuclear interactions and SRC effects are the dominant factors.
