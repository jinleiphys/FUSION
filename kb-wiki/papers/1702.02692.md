# Compton scattering off proton in the third resonance region

> Compton scattering off the proton in the third resonance region is analyzed for the first time, owing to the full combined analysis of pion- and photo-induced reactions in a coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model with K-matrix approximation. Two isospin $I=3/2$ resonances $D_{33}(1700)$ and $F_{35}(1930)$ are found to be essential in the range of 1.6 - 1.8 GeV. The recent beam asymmetry data of Compton scattering from the GRAAL facility are used to determine the helicity couplings of these resonances, and strong constraints are coming also from $πN$ and $KΣ$ photoproduction data. The possible spin and parity of new narrow resonances is discussed.

## Key claim

Compton scattering off the proton in the third resonance region (center-of-mass energy 1.6-1.8 GeV) is analyzed for the first time within a coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model, but the narrow structures seen in the GRAAL beam asymmetry data cannot be explained by standard threshold effects and require either new narrow resonances or alternative mechanisms such as triangle singularities.

## Method

The Giessen coupled-channel model solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation in K-matrix approximation, treating meson-baryon vertices from effective Lagrangians with chiral symmetry. The Compton scattering amplitude is obtained perturbatively by first solving the hadronic channels (pi N, 2pi N, eta N, omega N, K Lambda, K Sigma) and then evaluating the T-matrix for the gamma N initial and final states, with gauge invariance maintained by excluding gamma N intermediate states from the rescattering sum. The model includes s-, u-, and t-channel contributions from nucleon poles, resonances, and pi- and eta-meson exchanges.

## Key numbers

- Two isospin I=3/2 resonances are essential in the 1.6-1.8 GeV range: D33(1700) with Breit-Wigner mass 1673 MeV and total width 766 MeV; F35(1905) with Breit-Wigner mass 1842 MeV and total width 619 MeV
- Helicity couplings for D33(1700): A_{1/2} = 106 +/- 5 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), A_{3/2} = 142 +/- 12 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2})
- Helicity couplings for F35(1905): A_{1/2} = 61 +/- 10 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), A_{3/2} = -78 +/- 15 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2})
- Overall chi^2/num. = 4.6 with 547 data points for the full fit; for the 1.6-1.8 GeV region, chi^2/num. = 92.2 for differential cross sections and 1.5 for beam polarization before adjusting helicity couplings
- Narrow structures in GRAAL beam polarization data have masses 1.681 +/- 0.005 GeV and 1.726 +/- 0.005 GeV with widths 18 +/- 6 MeV and 21 +/- 7 MeV respectively
- Two candidate narrow resonances (S11 at 1681 MeV with width 2 +/- 1 MeV; P11 at 1726 MeV with width 2 +/- 1 MeV) improve description of beam polarization but worsen differential cross section fit to chi^2/num. = 60.2

## Context

This work extends the Giessen coupled-channel model (Feuster 1997, Penner 2002, Shklyar 2004-2014) beyond its previous energy limit of 1.6 GeV into the third resonance region, building on prior analyses of pi N, K Sigma, and eta N channels. It contrasts with the dispersion-theory approach of L'vov (1996) which identified D15(1675) and F15(1680) as important resonances, whereas this model finds D33(1700) and F35(1905) dominant. The analysis enables future study of Compton scattering at higher energies within dynamical models and provides constraints for searches for missing resonances in photo-induced reactions.
