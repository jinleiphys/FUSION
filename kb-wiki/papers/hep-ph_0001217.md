# Relativistic Charge Form Factor of the Deuteron from (np)-Scattering Phase Shifts

> Relativistic integral representation in terms of experimental neutron-proton scattering phase shifts is used to compute the charge form factor of the deuteron $ G_{Cd}(Q^2)$. The results of numerical calculations of $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ are presented in the interval of the four-momentum transfers squared $0 \leq Q^2 \leq 35 fm^{-2}$. Zero and the prominent secondary maximum in $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ are the direct consequences of the change of sign in the experimental $^3S_1 $- phase shifts. Till the point $Q^2 \simeq 20 fm^{-2}$ the calculated total relativistic correction to $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ is positive and reaches the maximal value of 25% at $Q^2 \simeq 14 fm^{-2}$.

# Relativistic Charge Form Factor of the Deuteron from (np)-Scattering Phase Shifts

## Key claim

The zero and secondary maximum in the deuteron charge form factor $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ arise directly from the change of sign (positive to negative) of the experimental $^3S_1$ neutron-proton scattering phase shifts at intermediate energies, but the precise position of the zero can shift between $Q^2 = 16$ and $23\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$ depending on which experimental phase shift set is used, and the height of the secondary maximum can vary by a factor of seven.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic integral representation that expresses the deuteron charge form factor in terms of experimental neutron-proton scattering phase shifts in the triplet channel ($^3S_1$, $^3D_1$, and the mixing parameter $\varepsilon_1$) and nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The approach employs a Jost matrix formalism to solve the linear conjugation problem for the electromagnetic current matrix elements of the n-p system, taking residues at the deuteron bound-state pole. The n-p phase shifts are taken from the Virginia Tech group analysis (0 to 1300 MeV laboratory energy), and the nucleon form factors are taken in a simple dipole parameterization with $G_{En} \equiv 0$ as a first approximation.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron charge form factor $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ is calculated over the interval $0 \leq Q^2 \leq 35\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$.
- The total relativistic correction to $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ is positive up to $Q^2 \simeq 20\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$ and reaches a maximum of 25% at $Q^2 \simeq 14\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$.
- The zero in $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ occurs near $Q^2 \simeq 20\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$ for the nominal phase shift set, but can shift to $Q^2 = 16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$ or $Q^2 = 23\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$ with different experimental phase shift analyses.
- The secondary maximum lies in the interval $26 \leq Q^2 \leq 32\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$, with its height varying by a factor of seven across different phase shift sets.
- The asymptotic D/S ratio of the deuteron wave function is taken as $\rho = 0.0277$.
- The dipole parameterization used for the proton electric form factor is $G_{Ep} = (1 + Q^2 / 18.23\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2})^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic impulse approximation approach previously developed by the authors and collaborators (Troitsky, Muzafarov, Romanov) to compute deuteron electromagnetic form factors directly from experimental n-p scattering phase shifts, avoiding the need for a deuteron wave function. The results are consistent with the MIT-Bates data (1990s) that provided the first experimental evidence for a zero in $G_{Cd}$ near $Q^2 = 20\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$, and the authors note that new Jefferson Lab data (E-94-018) will be important for testing the approach at higher momentum transfers. The calculation is compared with the relativistic approach of Van Orden and collaborators, which predicts a zero at lower $Q^2$ but a similar secondary maximum height. The strong sensitivity of the form factor to the intermediate-energy ($E_{\mathrm{lab}} \leq 1$ GeV) n-p phase shifts motivates more detailed phase shift analyses in this region.
