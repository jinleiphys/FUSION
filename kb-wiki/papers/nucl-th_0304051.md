# Application of the Adiabatic Selfconsistent-Collective-Coordinate Method to a Solvable Model of Prolate-Oblate Shape Coexistence

> The adiabatic selfconsistent collective coordinate method is applied to an exactly solvable multi-O(4) model which simulates nuclear shape coexistence phenomena. Collective mass and dynamics of large amplitude collective motions in this model system are analysed, and it is shown that the method can well describe the tunneling motions through the barrier between the prolate and oblate local minima in the collective potential. Emergence of the doublet pattern is well reproduced.

## Key claim

The adiabatic selfconsistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method can describe tunneling motions through the barrier between prolate and oblate local minima in a solvable multi-O(4) model, and it reproduces the emergence of the doublet pattern, but the model possesses an exact prolate-oblate symmetry that does not occur in real nuclei, making this a somewhat unrealistic test case.

## Method

The authors applied the ASCC method to an exactly solvable multi-O(4) model that simulates nuclear shape coexistence phenomena, treating it as a simplified version of the pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) interaction model with only the K=0 component of quadrupole deformation. They solved the Hartree-Bogoliubov (HB) equations to find equilibrium points, then solved quasiparticle RPA equations at those points to identify the most collective normal mode, and iteratively constructed the collective subspace by shifting the collective coordinate using the infinitesimal generator P(q). The collective mass and dynamics of large-amplitude collective motions were analyzed, and results were compared against exact solutions obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a large basis.

## Key numbers

* The pairing gap Delta(q) and deformation D(q) are defined as D(q) = 2 sum_i d_i sigma_i v_i^2 and Delta(q) = G sum_i u_i v_i, where u_i and v_i are the BCS coefficients.
* The quasiparticle energy is given by E_i = sqrt((e_i - lambda_0)^2 + Delta_0^2), where lambda_0 is the chemical potential and Delta_0 is the pairing gap at equilibrium.
* The RPA dispersion equation for the pairing-only case (chi=0) yields omega^2 { (omega^2 - 4Delta^2) (sum_i 1/(2E_i((2E_i)^2 - omega^2)))^2 - (sum_i (u_i^2 - v_i^2)/(2E_i((2E_i)^2 - omega^2)))^2 } = 0, involving pairing vibration (omega approx 2Delta) and pairing rotation (omega=0) modes.
* The RPA dispersion equation for the quadrupole-only case (neglecting residual pairing) yields 4 sum_i (2E_i d_i^2 u_i^2 v_i^2)/((2E_i)^2 - omega^2) = 1/(2chi), involving a mode analogous to beta vibrations in deformed nuclei.
* The collective coordinate scale is fixed by requiring B(q)=1 everywhere on the collective subspace, and the frequency omega(q) satisfies omega^2 = partial^2 V/partial q^2.

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method, proposed recently by the same group (Matsuo et al., 2000), to a solvable model that tests its ability to describe shape coexistence phenomena where multiple local minima compete. The multi-O(4) model has been widely used as a testing ground for microscopic theories of nuclear collective motion, including studies by Klein and Marshalek (1982) and others. The paper enables future application of the ASCC method to realistic nuclear problems such as shape coexistence in Pb and Se isotopes, where experimental data from studies like those on 186,188Pb and 68Se have been reported.
