# Multiscale fluctuations in nuclear response

> The nuclear collective response is investigated in the framework of a doorway picture in which the spreading width of the collective motion is described as a coupling to more and more complex configurations. It is shown that this coupling induces fluctuations of the observed strength. In the case of a hierarchy of overlapping decay channels, we observe Ericson fluctuations at different scales. Methods for extracting these scales and the related lifetimes are discussed. Finally, we show that the coupling of different states at one level of complexity to some common decay channels at the next level, may produce interference-like patterns in the nuclear response. This quantum effect leads to a new type of fluctuations with a typical width related to the level spacing.

# Multiscale fluctuations in nuclear response

## Key claim

The nuclear collective response, when described through a doorway hierarchy of increasingly complex configurations, exhibits fluctuations at multiple scales, including Ericson fluctuations at different levels, and the coupling of states at one complexity level to common decay channels at the next level can produce interference-like patterns, a quantum effect yielding a new type of fluctuation with a width related to the level spacing rather than to the decay width.

## Method

The authors model the damping of collective nuclear motion by introducing a hierarchy of Hilbert spaces: a collective doorway state coupled to first-level states (e.g., 2p-2h configurations), which are in turn coupled to second-level states (e.g., 3p-3h), and so on, with residual interactions at each step. They diagonalize the Hamiltonian at each level of complexity, compute the strength function from the linear response formalism, and analyze fluctuations using autocorrelation functions on both the strength and its derivative, as well as an entropy-index method adapted from Hwa (1998) to extract scaling behavior.

## Key numbers

- In the numerical illustration of Ericson fluctuations from a fluctuating residual interaction, the total width of the collective state is roughly 30 (arbitrary units), while the individual state width is Γ_μ₁ = 3 and the level spacing is ΔE₁ = 0.5 (arbitrary units).
- In the multiscale fluctuation examples, the total spreading width is Γ_Coll = 30, with two decay-channel scales Γ_μ₁ = 3 and Γ_μ₂ = 0.5 (arbitrary units).
- For the fragmented strength case with fine structure, the same scales Γ_Coll = 30, Γ_μ₁ = 3, and Γ_μ₂ = 0.5 are used.
- In the first-level doorway model with constant coupling, the collective width is given by Γ_Coll² = 4(v₁² + v₁⁴π²/ΔE₁²), which in the continuous limit (ΔE₁ → 0 with v₁²/ΔE₁ constant) reduces to Γ_Coll = 2πv₁²/ΔE₁.
- Typical experimental values cited: compound nucleus widths of order eV, collective escape widths of hundreds of keV.

## Context

This work extends the doorway-state picture of nuclear damping, as developed in earlier studies of giant resonances (Ber83, Ber94, Cho95) and the second RPA approach (Dro90), by explicitly analyzing the fluctuation patterns that arise from a hierarchy of complexity levels. It builds on the concept of Ericson fluctuations (Eri63) and addresses questions raised about the fractal nature of nuclear response (Dro95, Dro96, Ber99, Aib99). The paper enables experimental extraction of multiple lifetime scales from high-resolution nuclear response measurements and introduces the entropy-index method (Hwa98) as a tool for identifying well-separated scales versus continuous scaling behavior.
