# Thermal modification of open heavy-flavor mesons from an effective hadronic theory

> We have developed a self-consistent theoretical approach to study the modification of the properties of heavy mesons in hot mesonic matter which takes into account chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries. The heavy-light meson-meson unitarized scattering amplitudes in coupled channels incorporate thermal corrections by using the imaginary-time formalism, as well as the dressing of the heavy mesons with the self-energies. We report our results for the ground-state thermal spectral functions and the implications for the excited mesonic states generated dynamically in the heavy-light molecular model. We have applied these to the calculation of meson Euclidean correlators and transport coefficients for D mesons and summarize here our findings.

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent thermal effective field theory for open heavy-flavor mesons that incorporates both chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries, but the results are limited to temperatures below the critical temperature \(T_c\) and the self-energy calculations only include pion contributions because heavier light mesons are thermally suppressed at \(T \lesssim 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian based on chiral symmetry and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetry to describe interactions between open heavy-flavor mesons (\(D^{(*)}\), \(D_s^{(*)}\), \(\bar{B}^{(*)}\), \(\bar{B}_s^{(*)}\)) and pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons (\(\pi\), \(K\), \(\bar{K}\), \(\eta\)). The \(s\)-wave projected tree-level amplitudes are unitarized via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, and finite-temperature corrections are incorporated using the imaginary-time formalism with Lehmann representations for the meson propagators. The thermal loop functions, self-energies, and scattering amplitudes are solved self-consistently, with the heavy-meson spectral functions computed from the Dyson equation.

## Key numbers

- The masses of heavy ground states (\(D\), \(D_s\), \(\bar{B}\), \(\bar{B}_s\)) decrease by approximately 20–50 MeV at the highest temperature considered, \(T = 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The unphysical pion mass used in lattice comparisons is \(m_\pi = 384\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with the lattice pseudocritical temperature \(T_c = 185\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The spatial diffusion coefficient \(2\pi T D_s\) is computed below \(T_c\) and matches smoothly with lattice QCD calculations and Bayesian analyses above \(T_c\).
- The thermal loop function shows a Landau cut opening for energies below \(m_{D/D_s} - m_\Phi\), which grows with temperature and is largest for channels involving pions.

## Context

This work extends the vacuum unitarized chiral approach of Guo (2009), Liu (2012), Tolos (2013), and Guo (2018) to finite temperature, building on the author's previous self-consistent thermal calculations (Montana 2020). The Euclidean correlators are compared with lattice QCD data from Kelly (2018), and the transport coefficients are computed using an off-shell generalization of the Fokker-Planck equation following the Kadanoff-Baym approach. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD at lower temperatures and physical meson masses, and provide a framework for including thermal kaon contributions to the self-energy.
