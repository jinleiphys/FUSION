# Pairing correlations and resonant states in the relativistic mean field theory

> We present a simple scheme for taking into account the resonant continuum coupling in the Relativistic Mean Field- BCS (RMF-BCS) calculations. In this scheme, applied before in non-relativistic calculations, the effect of the resonant continuum on pairing correlations is introduced through the scattering wave functions located in the region of the resonant states. These states are found by solving the relativistic mean field equations with scattering-type boundary conditions for continuum spectrum. The calculations are done for the neutron-rich Zr isotopes. It is shown that the sudden increase of the neutron radii close to the neutron drip line, the so-called giant halo, is determined by a few resonant states close to the continuum threshold.

## Key claim
The paper presents a scheme for incorporating resonant continuum states into Relativistic Mean Field-BCS (RMF-BCS) calculations, showing that the sudden increase of neutron radii near the neutron drip line in Zr isotopes (the "giant halo") is determined by a few resonant states close to the continuum threshold, rather than requiring the full continuum treatment of Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic mean field equations with scattering-type boundary conditions to obtain continuum wave functions, then identify resonant states from the energy derivative of the phase shift using the Breit-Wigner form. These resonant states are incorporated into BCS equations using the resonant-BCS (rBCS) formalism, where matrix elements of a zero-range pairing force are calculated with scattering wave functions evaluated at resonance energies and normalized inside a finite volume. The RMF and rBCS equations are solved iteratively for neutron-rich Zr isotopes using the NLSH parameter set for the mean field and a delta pairing force with strength V0 = -275 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Pairing force strength: V0 = -275 MeV (can be increased to about -350 MeV without significantly affecting separation energies and nuclear radii)
- Neutron single-particle states analyzed for Zr isotopes from mass number A = 120 to A = 138
- Two-neutron separation energies remain close to zero from A = 124 to A = 138
- Neutron radii increase sharply from A = 122 to A = 124, attributed to filling of the 3p3/2 and 2f7/2 states which are very narrow resonances close to the continuum threshold at A = 124
- The resonant states 2f5/2, 1h9/2, and 1i13/2 remain resonant for all isotopes studied (A = 120-138)
- The states 2f7/2, 3p3/2, and 3p1/2 are resonant for A < 126 and become loosely bound for heavier isotopes
- Pairing correlation energy shows a minimum at A = 136, corresponding to filling of the 2f7/2 and 3p3/2 states

## Context
This work extends the non-relativistic HF-BCS treatment of resonant continuum (Sandulescu 2000, 2001) to the relativistic framework, providing an alternative to full RHB calculations (Ring 1996) that use box boundary conditions and discretize the continuum. The approach enables accurate treatment of resonant states without the large energy cutoffs (up to 120 MeV) used in RHB calculations (Meng 1998), and shows that only a few resonant states near threshold are needed to reproduce the giant halo structure in Zr isotopes previously predicted by RHB.
