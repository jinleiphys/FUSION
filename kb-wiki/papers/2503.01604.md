# The $α$-particle condensation in diluted $^{16}\text{O}$ at finite temperature

> We investigate the effect of temperature on $α$-particle clustering in the diluted nucleus $^{16}\text{O}$ using the multi-constrained finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with the DD-ME2 interaction. At a critical density the nucleus undergoes a Mott-like transition from a homogeneous to a localised configuration characterised by $α$-particle clustering and the emergence of a finite non-axial octupole deformation. We study the interplay between the onset of localisation under nuclear dilution and the suppression of deformation and $α$-particle clustering due to increasing temperature. Investigating the temperature-density plane, our findings indicate that temperature delays the formation of non-axial octupole deformation and $α$-particle clustering in dilute environments. Following the transition from homogeneous to clustered configurations, the non-axial octupole deformation continues to increase with further dilution of the system and becomes nearly independent of temperature. We found that $α$-particle clusters appear at temperatures up to $T = 4.10$ MeV and at a corresponding normalised density $ρ_{\text{Mott}}/ρ_0 \approx 0.09$.

## Key claim

Temperature delays the formation of non-axial octupole deformation and alpha-particle clustering in diluted oxygen-16, but once clustering develops, the magnitude of the tetrahedral deformation becomes nearly independent of temperature; alpha clusters persist up to T = 4.10 MeV at a normalized Mott density of approximately 0.09.

## Method

The authors performed multi-constrained finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (FT-RHB) calculations using the DD-ME2 interaction, imposing isotropic inflation of oxygen-16 by constraining the root-mean-square radius to larger values while keeping all quadrupole moments zero. The system was allowed to develop axial octupole (beta30) and non-axial octupole (beta32) deformations freely, and the free energy was computed as a function of constrained radius for temperatures from 0.0 to 2.5 MeV, with additional calculations up to 4.10 MeV to determine the cluster disappearance boundary.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state rms radius of oxygen-16: R_gs = 2.59 fm at T = 0.0 MeV
- Critical radius for Mott transition at zero temperature: R_c = 3.90 fm, corresponding to normalized Mott density rho_Mott/rho_0 = (R_gs/R_c)^3 approximately 0.3
- Mott radius at T = 0.5 MeV: R_Mott = 3.95 fm; at T = 1.0 MeV: 4.15 fm; at T = 1.5 MeV: 4.40 fm; at T = 2.0 MeV: 4.65 fm; at T = 2.5 MeV: 4.95 fm
- Maximum temperature for alpha cluster existence: T = 4.10 MeV, at normalized density rho_Mott/rho_0 approximately 0.09
- Zero-point energy correction: 30 MeV for well-separated alpha particles at R_rms = 6.00 fm

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature studies of the Mott-like phase transition in oxygen-16 using relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals (Girod and collaborators, 2018; Ebran and collaborators, 2012), which predicted a transition from homogeneous to tetrahedral four-alpha configuration at a critical density around rho_0/3. It also builds on finite-temperature studies of clustering in neon-20 (PhysRevC.106.054309) and investigations of cluster formation in hot dilute nuclear matter (Roepke and collaborators, 1982-1983; PhysRevC.81.015803). The results enable mapping of the temperature-density phase boundary for alpha clustering in finite nuclei, with implications for the nuclear equation of state at subsaturation densities relevant to supernova and heavy-ion collision environments.
