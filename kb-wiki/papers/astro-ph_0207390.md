# alpha :a constant that is not a constant?

> We review the observational information on the constancy of the fine structure constant alpha. We find that small improvements on the measurement of ^{187}Re lifetime can provide significant progress in exploring the range of variability suggested by QSO data. We also discuss the effects of a time varying alpha on stellar structure and evolution. We find that radioactive dating of ancient stars can offer a new observational window.

## Key claim

The paper reviews observational constraints on the constancy of the fine structure constant alpha and finds that the apparent conflict between the QSO positive result (suggesting a variation) and the stricter Oklo bound can be avoided if the time evolution of alpha is not linear; the authors also argue that small improvements in the measurement of the 187Re lifetime could provide significant progress in exploring the range of variability suggested by QSO data.

## Method

The authors compile and compare observational constraints on alpha variation from multiple sources: laboratory atomic clock comparisons, the Oklo natural fission reactor (two billion years ago), meteorite dating via 187Re/187Os, quasar absorption spectra using both the alkali doublet method and the many-multiplet method, cosmic microwave background data, and Big Bang nucleosynthesis. They also construct solar models and globular cluster isochrones with a linearly time-dependent alpha to test whether percent-level variations would be detectable in stellar structure and evolution.

## Key numbers

- QSO many-multiplet result: Delta alpha/alpha = (-0.72 +/- 0.18) x 10^-5 over redshift range z=1-3 (Webb et al.)
- QSO alkali doublet constraint: Delta alpha/alpha = (-4.6 +/- 4.3[stat] +/- 1.4[sys]) x 10^-5 at z=2-4 (Varshalovich et al.)
- Oklo bound: |Delta alpha/alpha| less than or approximately 1 x 10^-7 at look-back time 1.8 Gyr
- Meteorite constraint from 187Re/187Os: average Delta alpha/alpha = (1 +/- 1) x 10^-6 over 4.5 Gyr
- Laboratory constraint: |Delta alpha/alpha| less than or equal to 1.6 x 10^-14 over 4 x 10^-10 Gyr
- 187Re lifetime sensitivity: d(ln tau)/d(ln alpha) approximately +18000, with laboratory half-life (42.3 +/- 0.7) Gyr and meteorite-inferred half-life (41.6 +/- 0.42) Gyr
- Stellar dating constraint from comparing Uranium clock with globular cluster evolution: Delta alpha/alpha less than or approximately 10^-3 on a 12 Gyr scale
- CMB and BBN constraints: |Delta alpha/alpha| less than or equal to 5 x 10^-2 and 1 x 10^-2 respectively

## Context

This review extends the discussion of possible time variation of fundamental constants originally raised by Dirac and later revived in the context of string theories, where coupling constants are determined by evolving vacuum expectation values of dilaton and moduli fields. The paper compares the positive QSO result of Webb et al. (2001) with the stricter Oklo bound from Shlyakhter (1976) and subsequent detailed analyses, and notes that the meteorite bound from 187Re/187Os is close to the QSO signal. The authors propose that radioactive dating of ancient stars, using methods extended beyond the solar system by Truran and others, can offer a new observational window, particularly through comparison of Uranium and Thorium clocks as demonstrated by Cayrel et al.
