# Mixing of Pseudoscalar Mesons

> Eta-eta' mixing is discussed in the quark-flavor basis with the hypothesis that the decay constants follow the pattern of particle state mixing. On exploiting the divergences of the axial vector currents - which embody the axial vector anomaly - all mixing parameters are fixed to first order of flavor symmetry breaking. An alternative set of parameters is obtained from a phenomenological analysis. We also discuss mixing in the octet-singlet basis and show how the relevant mixing parameters are related to those in the quark-flavor basis. The dependence of the mixing parameters on the strength of the anomaly and the amount of flavor symmetry breaking is investigated. Finally, we present a few applications of the quark-flavor mixing scheme, such as radiative decays of vector mesons, the photon-pseudoscalar meson transition form factors, the coupling constants of eta and eta' to nucleons, and the isospin-singlet admixtures to the pi^0 meson.

# Mixing of Pseudoscalar Mesons

## Key claim

The quark-flavor basis provides a theoretically consistent description of eta-eta' mixing where the decay constants follow the pattern of state mixing, in contrast to the octet-singlet basis where two distinct mixing angles (theta_8 and theta_0) are required and differ considerably as a consequence of flavor symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors analyze eta-eta' mixing in the quark-flavor basis using the hypothesis that the decay constants follow the pattern of particle state mixing, with the non-strange and strange decay constants f_q and f_s and a single mixing angle phi. They exploit the divergences of axial vector currents, which embody the U_A(1) anomaly, to fix all mixing parameters to first order of flavor symmetry breaking, and also obtain an alternative set of parameters from a phenomenological analysis of experimental data including two-photon decays, radiative vector meson decays, and transition form factors.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical mixing angle phi = 42.4 degrees (to first order of flavor symmetry breaking)
- Phenomenological mixing angle phi = 39.3 degrees +/- 1.0 degrees (weighted average from multiple processes)
- Theoretical decay constant ratio y = f_q/f_s = 0.78; phenomenological value y = 0.81 +/- 0.03
- Theoretical anomaly parameter a^2 = 0.281 GeV^2; phenomenological value a^2 = 0.265 +/- 0.010 GeV^2
- Phenomenological decay constants: f_q/f_pi = 1.07 +/- 0.02, f_s/f_pi = 1.34 +/- 0.06
- Octet-singlet mixing angles from phenomenological analysis: theta_8 = -21.2 degrees, theta_0 = -9.2 degrees, theta = -15.4 degrees
- Predicted ratio R(psi') = 5.8 and R(Upsilon) = 6.5 for radiative decays of heavy quarkonia
- Predicted ratio R(phi) = (5.66 +/- 0.20) x 10^(-3) for radiative phi decays, in agreement with KLOE result (5.30 +/- 0.5 +/- 0.4) x 10^(-3)

## Context

This work extends and clarifies the quark-flavor mixing scheme previously developed by the authors (Feldmann, Kroll, and Stech, 1998-2000) and by Kaiser and Leutwyler (1998) using chiral perturbation theory. It resolves a known problem in earlier analyses (Donoghue, Gilman, Ball-Frere-Tytgat, 1995; Bramon, 1997) where values for the mixing angle phi varied from approximately 32 degrees to 45 degrees depending on the process. The scheme enables consistent extraction of mixing parameters from diverse experimental processes including radiative decays of vector mesons, photon-pseudoscalar meson transition form factors, and eta/eta' couplings to nucleons, and provides a framework for testing the role of the U_A(1) anomaly and flavor symmetry breaking in QCD.
