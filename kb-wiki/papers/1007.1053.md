# Testing for three-body quark forces in L=1 excited baryons

> We discuss the matching of the quark model to the effective mass operator of the 1/Nc expansion using the permutation group S_N. As an illustration of the general procedure we perform the matching of the Isgur-Karl model for the spectrum of the negative parity L=1 excited baryons. Assuming the most general two-body quark Hamiltonian, we derive two correlations among the masses and mixing angles of these states which should hold in any quark model. These correlations constrain the mixing angles and can be used to test for the presence of three-body quark forces.

## Key claim

The paper derives two universal correlations among the masses and mixing angles of negative parity L=1 excited baryons that must hold in any quark model containing only two-body quark interactions; violations of these correlations would indicate the presence of three-body quark forces, but current data show no evidence for such forces within experimental errors.

## Method

The authors match the most general two-body quark Hamiltonian onto the effective mass operator of the 1/N_c expansion using the permutation group S_N, applied to the Isgur-Karl model for negative parity L=1 baryons. They express the mass operator in terms of 10 nontrivial spin-flavor operators and find that the 11 coefficients contribute to the N* states in only 9 independent combinations, yielding two universal relations among the masses and mixing angles of the 9 multiplets.

## Key numbers

- The Isgur-Karl model with harmonic oscillator wave functions fixes the entire spectroscopy of L=1 baryons by one constant delta = M_Delta - M_N ~ 300 MeV, along with an additive constant c0.
- The first universal correlation (Eq. 4) relates the mixing angles theta_N1 and theta_N3 for the nonstrange J=1/2 and J=3/2 states, involving masses of N(1535), N(1650), N(1520), N(1700), Delta_1/2, Delta_3/2, and N_5/2.
- The second universal relation expresses the spin-weighted SU(3) singlet mass bar_Lambda = (1/6)(2 Lambda_1/2 + 4 Lambda_3/2) in terms of nonstrange hadronic parameters.

## Context

This work extends the matching procedure of Pirjol and Schat (2007) that connects quark model Hamiltonians to the 1/N_c expansion using permutation group methods, and applies it to the Isgur-Karl model of Isgur and Karl (1977). The derived correlations constrain the mixing angles and can be used to test for three-body quark forces, complementing earlier 1/N_c analyses of negative parity L=1 baryons by Carlson, Carone, Goity, and others (1998-2004). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations and more precise experimental determinations of masses and mixing angles to search for evidence of three-body forces.
