# Hadron Spectroscopy, Results and Ideas

> Invited talk, presented at International Workshop on New Partial Wave Analysis Tools for Next Generation Hadron Spectroscopy Experiments, June 20-22, 2012 Camogli

## Key claim

The BnGa partial wave analysis has identified several new nucleon resonances, but the author cautions that in the region above 1.8 GeV no unique solution was found, with distinct solutions yielding different numbers of resonances and large error bars depending on assumptions about high-mass states and channel couplings.

## Method

The BnGa group performed a coupled-channel partial wave analysis fitting a large set of photoproduction data (gamma p to p pi0, n pi+, p eta, Lambda K+, Sigma0 K+, Sigma+ K0, and p pi0 pi0) together with pion-induced inelastic reactions and the real and imaginary parts of pi N scattering amplitudes from the Karlsruhe-Helsinki and GWU analyses. The analysis used a K-matrix with up to eight constrained channels plus one unconstrained channel, with background described by reggeized t-channel exchange of vector mesons and Born diagrams for N and Delta exchange.

## Key numbers

- Seven new resonances were added to the Review of Particle Properties, including the one-star N(1685) reported as a narrow peak in analyses of gamma n to n eta
- Four new resonances from the BnGa analysis received two-star status: N(1880)1/2+, N(1895)1/2-, N(2060)5/2-, and N(2150)3/2-
- N(1875)3/2- was promoted to a three-star resonance, combining early observations from Manley (1984), Bell (1983), Cutkosky (1980), and Saxon (1979) with the BnGa result
- N(1900)3/2+ was upgraded from two to three stars
- In the BnGa analysis, 400 particle properties (N_pp) were determined in total
- The AdS/QCD mass formula M^2 = a*(L+N+3/2) - b*alpha_D reproduces the mass spectrum 2 times better than quark models, Skyrme models, or lattice QCD

## Context

This work extends the BnGa partial wave analysis program (Anisovich 2011) and reports on the resulting updates to the baryon resonance listings in the Review of Particle Properties. The paper discusses how the new resonances challenge diquark models (Anselmino 1993) as an explanation for the missing resonance problem, and presents evidence for parity doublets that may indicate chiral symmetry restoration at high excitation energies (Glozman 1999). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations (Edwards 2011) and motivate further experimental work with double polarization data to resolve ambiguities in the partial wave analyses.
