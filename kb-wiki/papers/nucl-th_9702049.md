# Electroinduced two-nucleon knockout and correlations in nuclei

> We present a model to calculate cross sections for electroinduced two-nucleon emission from finite nuclei. Short-range correlations in the wave functions and meson-exchange contributions to the photoabsorption process are implemented. Effects of the short-range correlations are studied with the aid of a perturbation expansion method with various choices of the Jastrow correlation function. The model is used to investigate the relative importance of the different reaction mechanisms contributing to the A(e,e$'$pn) and A(e,e$'$pp) process. Representative examples for the target nuclei $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O and for kinematical conditions accessible with contemporary high-duty cycle electron accelerators are presented. A procedure is outlined to calculate the two-nucleon knockout contribution to the semi-exclusive (e,e$'$p) cross section. Using this technique we investigate in how far semi-exclusive (e,e$'$p) reactions can be used to detect high-momentum components in the nuclear spectral function.

# Electroinduced two-nucleon knockout and correlations in nuclei

## Key claim
Short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclear wave functions can be studied through electroinduced two-nucleon knockout reactions, but their signals are likely to produce relatively small cross sections that compete strongly with meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions, particularly at low momentum transfer; better conditions to detect SRC effects are predicted at high momentum transfer.

## Method
The authors developed a model for exclusive A(e,e'pp) and A(e,e'pn) cross sections from finite nuclei using a partial-wave expansion of the final state in terms of two-hole-two-particle eigenstates of a mean-field potential, with final-state interactions treated in a direct knockout (spectator) approximation. Short-range correlations were implemented via a perturbation expansion method with various Jastrow correlation functions, while two-body meson-exchange currents (seagull and pion-in-flight) and isobaric Delta-33 currents with pion exchange were included as competing reaction mechanisms. A procedure was also outlined to calculate the two-nucleon knockout contribution to semi-exclusive (e,e'p) cross sections by analytically integrating over the undetected nucleon's phase space using the approximation of quasi-deuteron (back-to-back) kinematics.

## Key numbers
- The pion-exchange potential in coordinate space includes terms with factors of 1/(m_pi r) and 3/(m_pi r)^2 in the tensor part, where m_pi is the pion mass.
- The pi N Delta coupling constant squared over 4 pi is 0.37, and the gamma N Delta coupling constant is 0.12, taken from Oset (reference).
- The intermediate energy range considered for high-duty cycle electron facilities is epsilon = 0.5 - 4 GeV.
- The Delta-33 resonance width is given by Gamma_Delta^res = (2/3) (f_pi N Delta^2 / 4 pi) (|p_pi|^3 / m_pi^2) (M_N / sqrt(s)), where p_pi is the decay momentum in the center-of-mass frame of the pi N system.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of two-nucleon knockout using real photons (Gottfried 1958; cross, grab96, lamp96, harty96) to electroinduced reactions, where the additional longitudinal degree of freedom may better reveal ground-state correlations. It provides a framework to confront models of short-range correlations (as studied by Mahan, Pieper, Co, Della, Gua96) with data from NIKHEF-K (Zondervan, Leon) and upcoming experiments at MAMI and TJNAF. The paper also addresses the long-standing challenge of extracting the single-particle spectral function from (e,e'p) reactions (Ciofi96, Mutter), arguing that two-nucleon knockout represents an important fraction of the continuum (e,e'p) strength at high missing energies.
