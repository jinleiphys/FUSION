# Evaluated Experimental Isobaric Analogue States from $T = 1/2$ to $T = 3$ and associated IMME coefficients

> Isobaric multiplets can be used to provide reliable mass predictions through the Isobaric Multiplet Mass Equation (IMME). Isobaric analogue states (IAS) for isospin multiplets from $T=1/2$ to $T=3$ have been studied within the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation (Ame2012). Each IAS established from published experimental reaction data has been expressed in the form of a primary reaction $Q$-value, and if necessary, has been recalibrated. The evaluated IAS masses are provided here along with the associated IMME coefficients. Quadratic and higher order forms of the IMME have been considered, and global trends have been extracted. Particular nuclides, requiring experimental investigation, have been identified and discussed. This dataset is the most precise and extensive set of evaluated IAS to date.

## Key claim

The paper provides the most precise and extensive evaluated set of isobaric analogue state (IAS) masses to date, but the quadratic Isobaric Multiplet Mass Equation (IMME) description is not universally valid; higher-order terms (cubic and quartic) are required for some multiplets, and several cases show unresolved problems with data consistency or isospin mixing that limit the reliability of the extracted coefficients.

## Method

The authors compiled IAS data for isospin multiplets from T=1/2 to T=3 using the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation (Ame2012), the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (Ensdf), and Nubase2012. Each IAS was expressed as a primary reaction Q-value, recalibrated when necessary (notably for the Becchetti et al. (1971) dataset), and then fitted to quadratic and higher-order forms of the IMME. Fragmented states were excluded from the fitting procedure, and global trends in the b and c coefficients were extracted using a homogeneously charged spherical nucleus model.

## Key numbers

- The global b coefficient follows a linear function: b = Δ_nH − 0.979 × 0.720 × (A−1)/A^(1/3) + 702 keV, where Δ_nH is the neutron-¹H mass difference (782 keV).
- The global c coefficient is approximated by c_general ≈ 3 × (260 × 0.630/A^(1/3) + 21.5) keV, with shifts of +85 keV for T=1,(4n+2) and −42.5 keV for T=1,(4n) datasets.
- For the T=2, A=8 multiplet, the cubic d coefficient is 9.42 ± 1.88 keV and the quartic e coefficient is 1.40 ± 0.07 keV.
- For the T=2, A=32 multiplet, the cubic d coefficient is 0.89 ± 0.11 keV, with a 96% probability of being the correct model.
- The T=3/2, A=9 multiplet has an unambiguously non-zero d coefficient.
- The T=3/2, A=11 multiplet shows the T_z=−1/2 component (¹¹C^i) is 170 keV below the expected curve, more than 4σ away.
- The T=1, A=44 multiplet returns a doubtful negative c coefficient of −26 ± 91 keV, attributed to a systematic error of 270 ± 100 keV in the ⁴⁴V ground state mass.
- The T=2, A=52 multiplet yields an unusual quadratic c coefficient of 156.36 ± 3.31 keV when including ⁵²Co^i, roughly 25 keV below general tendencies.

## Context

This work extends the extensive compilation of Antony and Pape (1984, 1985, 1986, 1998) and the recent update by Yi Hua Lam, using the Ame2012 evaluation as the foundation. The dataset enables more reliable mass predictions via the IMME for unmeasured IAS, constrains the symmetry energy component in astrophysical modelling as discussed by Danielewicz (2013), and supports tests of the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis by Hardy and Towner (2009). The identification of problematic multiplets (e.g., A=9, 11, 44, 52) points to specific nuclides requiring new experimental measurements, particularly ¹¹C^i, ⁴⁴V, and ⁵²Co^i.
