# Selected problems in astrophysics of compact objects

> I review three problems in astrophysics of compacts stars: (i) the phase diagram of warm pair-correlated nuclear matter a sub-saturation densities at finite isospin asymmtery; (ii) the Standard Model neutrino emission from superfluid phases in neutron stars within the Landau theory of Fermi (superfluid) liquids; (iii) the beyond Standard Model physics of axionic cooling of compact stars by the Cooper pair-breaking processes.

# Selected problems in astrophysics of compact objects

## Key claim

The paper reviews three interconnected problems in compact star physics, arguing that the phase diagram of warm isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities includes novel superfluid phases (LOFF and phase-separated states) beyond the standard BCS-BEC crossover, and that axion emission via Cooper pair-breaking processes in superfluid neutron stars can provide competitive bounds on axion properties that depend on the poorly-known pairing gap.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations for three distinct problems: (i) a self-consistent solution of the gap equation and density expressions for neutrons and protons in dilute nuclear matter, allowing for BCS, BEC, LOFF, and phase-separated (PS) superfluid phases at finite isospin asymmetry; (ii) computation of neutrino emissivity from superfluid baryonic matter using finite-temperature Green's functions and the Landau theory of superfluid Fermi liquids, with vertex corrections for vector and axial-vector currents; (iii) calculation of axion emissivity from S-wave superfluid baryonic matter via the polarization tensor, using the two-loop diagrams from normal and anomalous propagators with bare axial-vector vertices.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 2.8 \times 10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$; sub-saturation regime considered at $\rho \le 0.5\,\rho_0$
- Tetra-critical point in the phase diagram occurs at $\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.22$ and $T = 2.85$ MeV for isospin asymmetry $\alpha_4 = 0.255$
- LOFF phase disappears at $\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.65$ and $T=0$ for $\alpha = 0.62$
- Critical temperature for superfluid phase transition in neutron stars: $T_c \simeq 0.5$ MeV
- Axion emissivity scales as $T^5$, while neutrino emissivity from axial-vector currents scales as $T^7$
- Lower bound on axion-baryon coupling: $f_a > 5.92 \times 10^{9}$ GeV $[0.1\text{ MeV}/\Delta(T)]$
- Upper bound on axion mass: $m_a \le 1.05 \times 10^{-3}$ eV $[\Delta(T)/0.1\text{ MeV}]$
- Fermi weak coupling constant: $G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$
- Axial vector coupling constant: $g_A = 1.25$

## Context

This review extends prior work on the BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter by Alm (1993), Baldo (1995), and Sedrakian (2005), incorporating the LOFF phase and phase separation into the phase diagram as developed by Stein (2012). The neutrino emission calculations build on the foundational work of Flowers (1976), Voskresensky (1987), and Kaminker (1999), with vertex corrections from Leinson (2006) and Sedrakian (2006, 2012). The axion bounds update earlier constraints from Iwamoto (1984) and Umeda (1997) by using the Cooper pair-breaking process computed by Keller (2012), and are complementary to supernova bounds from Raffelt (2006) and Brinkmann (1988). The results enable improved cooling simulations of neutron stars and provide a framework for testing beyond-Standard-Model physics using compact star observations.
