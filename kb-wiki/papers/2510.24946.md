# Updating GEANIE $^{239}$Pu prompt $γ$-ray experimental data using modern Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay model

> We calculate fission $γ$ rays for neutron-induced reactions on $^{239}$Pu with the Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay model. By applying the calculated fission $γ$ rays as a background contribution, the historical $^{239}$Pu(n,$x$n$γ$) reaction cross section data measured by the GEANIE (GErmanium Array for Neutron Induced Excitations) spectrometer are corrected. The correction also includes other (n,$x$n) reactions that have very similar energies to the $γ$ lines reported by GEANIE. In many cases, the original GEANIE data are strongly reduced and they become much closer to the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model predictions. The total inelastic scattering, (n,2n), and (n,3n) cross sections are inferred based on the corrected GEANIE data, and compared with available experimental data as well as the statistical model calculations. Expected $γ$-ray energy spectra for neutron-induced measurements on $^{239}$Pu are also discussed.

## Key claim

The historical GEANIE $^{239}$Pu(n,xn$\gamma$) cross section data are significantly reduced when corrected for prompt fission $\gamma$-ray background calculated with the modern Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay (HF$^3$D) model, bringing them much closer to statistical Hauser-Feshbach model predictions; however, the correction factors are model-code dependent and the authors emphasize they do not modify the original data to force agreement with calculations.

## Method

The authors used the statistical Hauser-Feshbach code CoH$_3$ to calculate $\gamma$-ray production cross sections for (n,n'), (n,2n), (n,3n), and (n,4n) reactions on $^{239}$Pu, and the BeoH code (based on the HF$^3$D model) to calculate prompt fission $\gamma$ rays from over 10,000 discrete transitions per fission. They applied a correction factor $f_c$ or $f'_c$ to the original GEANIE data by integrating the calculated $\gamma$-ray spectra over an energy window around each target $\gamma$ line, subtracting contributions from both fission fragments and other (n,xn) reactions with nearby $\gamma$ energies. The total (n,n'), (n,2n), and (n,3n) cross sections were then inferred by multiplying the corrected partial $\gamma$-ray production cross sections by the calculated ratio of total to partial cross section from CoH$_3$.

## Key numbers

* The average $s$-wave resonance spacing of $^{240}$Pu used to adjust the level density parameter is 2.07 eV (Mughabghab, 2006).
* The average $\gamma$-ray width used to adjust the M1 scissors mode is 43 meV (Mughabghab, 2006).
* For the 226.4-keV inelastic scattering $\gamma$ ray, the correction factor reduced the original GEANIE data by more than 20%, partly due to a 226.8-keV $\gamma$ line from $^{96}$Zr with a production cross section of 1-2 mb in the 1-20 MeV range.
* For the 157.4-keV (n,2n$\gamma$) line at 11.4 MeV incident energy, the correction factor $f_c$ was 0.761 while the more modest $f'_c$ was 0.982.
* The 459.9-keV (n,2n$\gamma$) ray from the 763.2-keV $5^-$ level (branching ratio 3.33%) showed a remarkable reduction after correction; the level production cross section is about 18 mb.
* The 273.3-keV (n,3n$\gamma$) ray has a branching ratio of 14.45%; the original GEANIE data would imply a (n,3n) cross section exceeding an unrealistic 1 barn at 20 MeV.
* The fission fragment $^{137}$Ba $\gamma$-ray production probability near 273 keV increases from $4.2\times10^{-4}$ per fission at 19 MeV to $1.0\times10^{-3}$ at 25 MeV.
* The optical model gives a total reaction cross section of 2900 mb at 20 MeV, with 2300 mb going to fission, leaving 600 mb for (n,n'), (n,2n), and (n,3n) combined.

## Context

This work revisits the historical GEANIE experiment on $^{239}$Pu originally reported by Bernstein et al. (2002), which used the GNASH code to infer (n,2n) cross sections that were systematically lower than activation measurements by Lougheed et al. (2002). The authors apply the modern HF$^3$D model developed by Okumura et al. (2018, 2022) and Lovell et al. (2021) to calculate discrete fission $\gamma$ rays, enabling correction of the GEANIE data for fission background that was previously impossible to estimate. The corrected (n,2n) cross sections agree well with the data of Meot et al. (2021) below 10 MeV, while the (n,3n) correction remains uncertain and may still have unknown strong backgrounds; the procedure is intended to aid future experiments such as GENESIS (Gordon, 2024) and GRAPhEME (Kerveno, 2021) for actinide measurements.
