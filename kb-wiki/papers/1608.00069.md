# Decay widths of bottomonium states in matter -- a field theoretic model for composite hadrons

> We compute the in-medium partial decay widths of the bottomonium states to open bottom mesons ($B\bar B$) using a field theoretical model for composite hadrons with quark constituents. These decay widths are calculated by using the explicit constructions for the bottomonium states and the open bottom mesons ($B$ and $\bar B$), and, the quark antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian written in terms of the constituent quark field operators. These decay widths in the hadronic medium are calculated as arising from the mass modifications of the bottomonium states and the $B$ and $\bar B$ mesons, obtained in a chiral effective model. The decay amplitude in the present model is multiplied with a strength parameter for the light quark pair creation, which is fitted from the observed vacuum partial decay width of the bottomonium state, $Υ(4S)$ to $B\bar B$. The effects of the isospin asymmetry, the strangeness fraction of the hadronic matter on the decay widths, arising due to the mass modifications due to these effects, have also been studied. There is observed to be appreciable effects from density, and the effects from isospin asymmetry on the parital decay widths of $Υ\rightarrow B\bar B$ are observed to be quite pronounced at high densities. These effects should show up in the asymmetric heavy ion collisions in Compressed baryonic matter (CBM) experiments planned at the future facility at FAIR. The study of the $Υ$ states will, however, require access to energies higher than the energy regime planned at the CBM experiment. The density effects on the decay widths of the bottomonium states should show up in the production of these states, as well as, in dilepton spectra at the Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) energies.

## Key claim

The in-medium partial decay widths of bottomonium states to open bottom mesons ($B\bar B$) are computed using a field-theoretic model for composite hadrons, with the decay amplitude multiplied by a strength parameter fitted from the observed vacuum partial decay width of $\Upsilon(4S) \to B\bar B$; the medium modifications arise solely from the mass modifications of the bottomonium states and the $B$ and $\bar B$ mesons obtained in a chiral effective model, and the effects of isospin asymmetry on the partial decay widths are observed to be quite pronounced at high densities.

## Method

The authors construct explicit quark-model wavefunctions for the bottomonium states $\Upsilon(1S)$, $\Upsilon(2S)$, $\Upsilon(3S)$, and $\Upsilon(4S)$ and for the open bottom mesons $B$ and $\bar B$, using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions with radii $R_{\Upsilon(NS)}$ and $R_B$. The decay amplitude is obtained from the quark-antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian written in terms of constituent quark field operators, and the matrix element is evaluated for the process $\Upsilon \to B\bar B$ at rest. The in-medium masses of the bottomonium states (modified by the scalar dilaton field simulating gluon condensates) and of the $B$ and $\bar B$ mesons (modified by interactions with baryons and scalar mesons in a chiral SU(3) model generalized to SU(5)) are used to compute the medium-dependent decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum masses used: $\Upsilon(1S)$ = 9460.3 MeV, $\Upsilon(2S)$ = 10023.26 MeV, $\Upsilon(3S)$ = 10355.2 MeV, $\Upsilon(4S)$ = 10579.4 MeV, and $B$($\bar B$) = 5279 MeV.
- The strength parameter for light quark pair creation is fitted from the observed vacuum partial decay width of $\Upsilon(4S) \to B\bar B$.
- The magnitude of the momentum $|{\bf p}|$ of the outgoing $B$ and $\bar B$ mesons in the decay is given by $|{\bf p}| = \left( \frac{m_\Upsilon^2}{4} - \frac{m_B^2 + m_{\bar B}^2}{2} + \frac{(m_B^2 - m_{\bar B}^2)^2}{4 m_\Upsilon^2} \right)^{1/2}$.
- The constituent quark mass $M_d$ for the light $d$ quark is taken as momentum independent, with the approximation $2M_d g \approx 1$ and $g \approx 1/(2M)$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous field-theoretic model for composite hadrons, which was applied to charmonium decays to $D\bar D$ and $D^* \to D\pi$ in matter (Mishra and Misra, 2015), to the bottomonium sector. The in-medium masses of the bottomonium states are taken from the same chiral effective model that incorporates scale symmetry breaking via a dilaton field (Mishra and Misra, 2015), and the open bottom meson masses are from a generalization of the chiral SU(3) model to SU(5) (Mishra and Misra, 2015). The results are relevant for asymmetric heavy-ion collisions at the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR and for dilepton spectra at Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) energies, though the study of $\Upsilon$ states requires energies higher than the CBM regime.
