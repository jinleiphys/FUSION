# Neutrino and antineutrino quasielastic interactions with nuclei

> We investigate the interaction of neutrinos and antineutrinos with nuclei. We explore in particular the role played by the multinucleon excitations which can contaminate the quasielastic cross section. For neutrinos the multinucleon term produces a sizable increase of the quasielastic cross section. Part of the effect arises from tensor correlations. For antineutrinos this influence is smaller owing to the axial-vector interference which increases the relative importance of the terms which are not affected by these multinucleon excitations.

## Key claim

The multinucleon (2p-2h) contribution to the quasielastic cross section is smaller for antineutrinos than for neutrinos, by a factor of about 1.7 at 700 MeV, because the axial-vector interference term suppresses the spin-isospin response for antineutrinos, leaving a larger relative role for the isovector response which is not affected by these multinucleon excitations.

## Method

The authors computed neutrino and antineutrino charged-current cross sections on 12C using nuclear response functions treated in the random phase approximation (RPA), including nucleon and Delta excitations. They evaluated the multinucleon (2p-2h) contribution using two parametrizations: one extrapolated from pion absorption calculations by Delorme and Guichon, and another based on a microscopic calculation by Alberico et al. of the 2p-2h contribution to the spin-transverse response measured in inclusive (e,e') scattering.

## Key numbers

- The MiniBooNE neutrino beam average energy is 788 MeV.
- The axial cut-off mass needed to fit MiniBooNE data in a relativistic Fermi gas model is M_A = 1.35 GeV, compared to the accepted value of M_A = 1.03 GeV.
- The ratio of the multinucleon component to the single-nucleon component for antineutrinos is reduced compared to neutrinos by a factor of 1.7 at E_nu = 700 MeV.
- For neutrinos, the flux-averaged cross sections (in units of 10^-39 cm^2 per neutron) are: QE = 7.46 (bare) or 6.40 (RPA); np-nh = 2.77 (bare) or 2.73 (RPA); QE+np-nh = 10.23 (bare) or 9.13 (RPA).
- For antineutrinos, the flux-averaged cross sections (in units of 10^-39 cm^2 per proton) are: QE = 2.09 (bare) or 1.60 (RPA); np-nh = 0.52 (bare) or 0.47 (RPA); QE+np-nh = 2.61 (bare) or 2.07 (RPA).
- The experimental CCQE neutrino-12C value measured by MiniBooNE is 9.429 x 10^-39 cm^2 with a total normalization error of 10.7%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Martini et al., 2009) which first identified multinucleon emission as a possible explanation for the MiniBooNE quasielastic cross section anomaly, where a relativistic Fermi gas model required an increased axial cut-off mass. The paper contrasts with the approach of Benhar and Meloni (2009), who used spectral functions from (e,e'p) data to study final-state interactions but could not explain the enhancement. The key prediction is that antineutrino measurements can test whether the excess cross section belongs to the spin-isospin channel, which would confirm the role of tensor correlations in neutrino-nucleus interactions.
