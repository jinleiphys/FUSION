# Scaling Analysis of Fluctuating Strength Function

> We propose a new method to analyze fluctuations in the strength function phenomena in highly excited nuclei. Extending the method of multifractal analysis to the cases where the strength fluctuations do not obey power scaling laws, we introduce a new measure of fluctuation, called the local scaling dimension, which characterizes scaling behavior of the strength fluctuation as a function of energy bin width subdividing the strength function. We discuss properties of the new measure by applying it to a model system which simulates the doorway damping mechanism of giant resonances. It is found that the local scaling dimension characterizes well fluctuations and their energy scales of fine structures in the strength function associated with the damped collective motions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a "local scaling dimension" to characterize strength function fluctuations in highly excited nuclei, extending multifractal analysis to cases where fluctuations do not obey power scaling laws. The method is demonstrated on a model system simulating the doorway damping mechanism of giant resonances, where the local scaling dimension is shown to characterize fluctuations and their energy scales even when the standard multifractal assumption of power-law scaling does not hold.

## Method

The authors define a partition function χ_m(ε) by binning the strength function S(E) into bins of width ε and computing moments of the binned strengths. They then introduce the local scaling dimension D_m(ε) as the logarithmic derivative of the partition function with respect to bin width, computed via finite differences with a factor of 2 change in ε. This measure is applied to a schematic model Hamiltonian where a collective state couples to doorway states embedded in a GOE background, with the background states having their own spreading width γ and the doorway states having spacing D.

## Key numbers

- The global strength distribution FWHM is set to Γ = 2000d, where d is the level spacing of background states
- Background spreading width γ is varied among γ = 64d, 128d, 256d, and 512d
- Doorway state spacing D is varied among D = 64d, 128d, and 256d
- Total number of background states is N_bg = 8191, giving Hamiltonian matrix dimension 8192
- For the Lorentzian distribution example, the steepest slope of D_2(ε) occurs at ε = 0.80Γ (or ε = 1.8Γ when plotted as a function of log ε)
- For the GOE strength fluctuation, the local scaling dimension D_m(ε) deviates significantly from the uniform limit D_m = 1 for bin widths up to about 100d, and the effect of energy level fluctuations becomes negligible for ε > 10d

## Context

This work extends the multifractal analysis approach previously applied to strength function fluctuations by Aiba and Gorski, where approximate power scaling was found in shell model calculations of giant dipole resonances. The method provides an alternative to the autocorrelation function analysis used by Hansen, Kilgus, and others to characterize fine structures in giant resonance strength functions, with the advantage that the local scaling dimension has smooth ε dependence and can access higher moments of the fluctuation. The approach enables systematic characterization of strength fluctuations across energy scales ranging from the level spacing order up to the doorway state scale, which is relevant for understanding the damping mechanism of giant resonances through doorway coupling as described by Bertsch.
