# Density dependent hadron field theory for asymmetric nuclear matter and exotic nuclei

> The density dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) theory is applied to strongly asymmetric nuclear matter and finite nuclei far off stability. A new set of in-medium meson-nucleon vertices is derived from Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations in asymmetric matter, now accounting also for the density dependence of isovector coupling constants. The scalar-isovector $δ$ meson is included. Nuclear matter calculations show that it is necessary to introduce a momentum correction in the extraction of coupling constants from the DBHF self-energies in order to reproduce the DBHF equation of state by DDRH mean-field calculations. The properties of DDRH vertices derived from the Groningen and the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials are compared in nuclear matter calculations and for finite nuclei. Relativistic Hartree results for binding energies, charge radii, separation energies and shell gaps for the Ni and Sn isotopic chains are presented. Using the momentum corrected vertices an overall agreement to data on a level of a few percent is obtained. In the accessible range of asymmetries the $δ$ meson contributions to the self-energies are found to be of minor importance but asymmetry dependent fluctuations may occur.

# Density dependent hadron field theory for asymmetric nuclear matter and exotic nuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that density dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) theory, with coupling constants extracted from Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations in asymmetric nuclear matter, can reproduce the DBHF equation of state only after introducing a momentum correction to the coupling constants; without this correction, the Hartree-level binding energy at saturation is about 2.5 MeV too weak. The delta meson contributions to self-energies are found to be of minor importance in the accessible range of asymmetries, though asymmetry-dependent fluctuations may occur.

## Method

The authors derive density-dependent meson-nucleon vertices for sigma, omega, rho, and delta mesons from DBHF self-energies in asymmetric nuclear matter using the Groningen and Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potentials. They introduce a momentum correction scheme where coupling constants are modified by a factor sqrt(1 + zeta_alpha * k_F^2) to account for the momentum dependence of DBHF self-energies, adjusting the constants zeta_alpha to reproduce the DBHF equation of state. The vertices are parameterized using a rational function of density, and the DDRH mean-field equations are solved for infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei in the Hartree approximation including rearrangement self-energies.

## Key numbers

- DBHF equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter has binding energy epsilon/rho_0 = -15.6 MeV at saturation density rho_0 = 0.182 fm^{-3}
- Without momentum correction, the DDRH Hartree calculation gives binding energy epsilon/rho_0 = -13.13 MeV at rho_0 = 0.161 fm^{-3}, a difference of about 2.5 MeV
- Momentum correction parameters: zeta_sigma = 0.00804 fm^{-2} and zeta_omega = 0.00103 fm^{-2} for symmetric matter
- Reference saturation density for the rational fit of coupling constants is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Overall agreement with data for Ni and Sn isotopic chains is at the level of a few percent for binding energies, charge radii, separation energies, and shell gaps

## Context

This work extends the DDRH theory previously developed by Lenske (1995) and Fuchs (1995) from symmetric matter to strongly asymmetric nuclear matter and exotic nuclei far from stability. It improves upon earlier approaches by Brockmann and Toki (1992) and Haddad (1993) that only included density dependence in the isoscalar channel, now incorporating density-dependent isovector coupling constants from asymmetric DBHF calculations by de Jong and Lenske (1998). The momentum correction addresses a known problem identified by Haddad (1993) and Cescato (1998) where Hartree-level calculations fail to reproduce the DBHF equation of state. The results enable systematic DDRH calculations for exotic nuclei and provide a foundation for extending the approach to neutron star conditions and hypernuclei as discussed by Keil (2000).
