# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay within QRPA with Proton-Neutron Pairing

> We have investigated the role of proton-neutron pairing in the context of the Quasiparticle Random Phase approximation formalism. This way the neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements of the experimentally interesting A= 48, 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130 and 136 systems have been calculated. We have found that the inclusion of proton-neutron pairing influences the neutrinoless double beta decay rates significantly, in all cases allowing for larger values of the expectation value of light neutrino masses. Using the best presently available experimental limits on the half life-time of neutrinoless double beta decay we have extracted the limits on lepton number violating parameters.

## Key claim

The inclusion of proton-neutron (p-n) pairing correlations in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) formalism significantly increases the calculated neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements for all nine nuclei studied, which in turn allows for larger extracted values of the average light neutrino mass from experimental half-life limits, but the effect is conditional on the specific renormalization parameters used (g_pp = 1.0 and g_ph = 0.8).

## Method

The authors extended the standard QRPA formalism for neutrinoless double beta decay by incorporating proton-neutron pairing correlations via a generalized 4x4 Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov transformation, replacing the usual separate proton and neutron BCS transformations. They then calculated the nuclear matrix elements for the light neutrino mass mechanism (M_GT, M_F), heavy neutrino exchange (M_FH, M_GTH), and right-handed current interference terms (M'_F, M'_GT, M'_T, M'_P, M_R, M_Fω, M_GTω) for nine nuclei, using a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the Bonn one-boson-exchange potential and a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon single-particle basis.

## Key numbers

- For the nine nuclei studied (A = 48, 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130, 136), the inclusion of p-n pairing allows for larger values of the extracted average light neutrino mass <m_ν> compared to calculations without p-n pairing.
- The experimental half-life limit used for extraction is T^{0ν}_{1/2} > 5.6 × 10^{24} years (from the 1996 literature), which yields a limit |<m_ν>| ≤ 0.8 eV when combined with nuclear matrix elements.
- The nuclear radius parameter is taken as r_0 ≃ 1.1 fm (R_0 = r_0 A^{1/3}).
- The nucleon finite size is approximately 0.8 fm.
- The renormalization parameters adopted are g_pp = 1.0 and g_ph = 0.8; for higher g_ph the particle-hole interaction becomes too strong and the QRPA ground state collapses.
- The proton and neutron number conservation in initial and final states is respected to within (N_np - <N_np>)/N_np ≤ 10^{-4}.
- The oscillator length parameter a is used, and the nucleon magnetic moment difference is μ = μ_p - μ_n + 1 = 4.7.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QRPA calculations for neutrinoless double beta decay (Pantis et al., 1996; Simkovic et al., earlier works) by incorporating proton-neutron pairing correlations that had previously been shown to be important for two-neutrino double beta decay (Vogel and Zirnbauer, 1986; Engel et al., 1988; Civitarese et al., 1990). The inclusion of p-n pairing modifies the quasiparticle transformation from separate proton/neutron 2x2 matrices to a generalized 4x4 matrix, and introduces additional phonon components (proton-proton and neutron-neutron quasiparticle pairs) into the QRPA states. This enables more reliable extraction of limits on lepton number violating parameters (light neutrino mass <m_ν>, heavy neutrino mass parameters, and right-handed current parameters λ and η) from experimental half-life limits, and provides nuclear matrix elements that can be used for other beyond-standard-model mechanisms such as exotic Higgs scalars and supersymmetric R-parity violating interactions.
