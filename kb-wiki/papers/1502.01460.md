# The effects of delta mesons on the baryonic direct Urca processes in neutron star matter

> In the framework of relativistic mean field theory, the relativistic neutrino emissivity of the nucleonic and hyperonic direct Urca processes in the degenerate baryon matter of neutron stars are studied. We investigate particularly the influence of the isovector scalar interaction which is considered by exchanging $δ$ meson on the nucleonic and hyperonic direct Urca processes. The results indicate that $δ$ mesons lead to obvious enhancement of the total neutrino emissivity, which must result in more rapid cooling rate of neutron star matter.

# The effects of delta mesons on the baryonic direct Urca processes in neutron star matter

## Key claim

The inclusion of the isovector scalar interaction mediated by the delta meson ($a_0(980)$) in relativistic mean field theory leads to an obvious enhancement of the total neutrino emissivity from nucleonic and hyperonic direct Urca processes in neutron star matter, which would accelerate the cooling rate of nonsuperfluid neutron stars in most mass ranges where these processes occur. This enhancement is conditional on the delta meson shifting baryonic threshold densities to lower values and increasing the fractions of protons, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma^0$, and $\Sigma^-$ hyperons.

## Method

The authors adopt a relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) Lagrangian including $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, and $\delta$ mesons with cubic and quartic nonlinearities of the $\sigma$ meson, solving the meson field equations self-consistently at fixed baryon density under $\beta$-equilibrium, charge neutrality, and baryon number conservation conditions at zero temperature. They compute the relativistic neutrino emissivity using the Fermi golden rule expression for nine distinct nucleonic and hyperonic direct Urca processes (Table 1), using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and determine the maximum stable neutron star masses.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass without $\delta$ mesons: 1.72 $M_\odot$, with corresponding radius 10.40 km and central density 1.23 fm$^{-3}$; with $\delta$ mesons: 1.70 $M_\odot$, radius 10.58 km, central density 1.20 fm$^{-3}$.
- Weak-coupling constant used: $G_F = 1.436 \times 10^{-49}$ erg cm$^3$.
- Cabibbo angle sine: $\sin\theta_c = 0.231 \pm 0.003$; axial-vector constants $F = 0.477 \pm 0.012$, $D = 0.756 \pm 0.011$.
- Coupling parameter $f_\delta = (g_\delta / m_\delta)^2 = 2.50$ fm$^2$ for the case with $\delta$ mesons; $f_\delta = 0.00$ fm$^2$ without.
- Hyperon coupling ratios to nucleon couplings: $g_{\sigma H} = 0.7 g_{\sigma N}$, $g_{\omega H} = 0.7 g_{\omega N}$, $g_{\rho H} = 0.7 g_{\rho N}$, $g_{\delta H} = 0.7 g_{\delta N}$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron star cooling that used non-relativistic neutrino emissivity formulae (e.g., earlier work by the same community cited as lab6, lab7, lab8) by adopting a fully relativistic treatment consistent with the relativistic mean field equation of state. It builds on studies showing that the $\delta$ meson contributes to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at high densities (works cited as lab16 through lab24). The results enable more accurate predictions of neutron star cooling curves for nonsuperfluid stars, particularly for masses where direct Urca processes are active, and suggest that models omitting the $\delta$ meson may underestimate cooling rates.
