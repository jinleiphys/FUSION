# Collective effects in a medium and a model of compound flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> A new analytical approach is presented for analysis of two-particle azimuthal correlations in heavy ion collisions at relativistic energies. This approach suggests that elliptic flow measured by experiment has a compound structure, namely, that it may come from superposition of several components. General expressions have been derived for the two-particle correlation function isolating the contribution due to anisotropic flow. The model of compound flow takes into account the number of jets per event, average multiplicity per jet, dependence of jet yield on the orientation with respect to the reaction plane, and independent "soft" particle production.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that experimentally measured elliptic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions may have a compound structure, arising from a superposition of contributions from "soft" particles (those with only flow correlations) and "hard" particles (those from jet fragmentation), each with potentially different flow coefficients \(v_2^s\) and \(v_2^h\).

## Method
The author derives general analytical expressions for two-particle azimuthal correlation functions that separate the contributions from anisotropic flow and from jets. The model incorporates the number of jets per event, average multiplicity per jet, the dependence of jet yield on orientation with respect to the reaction plane, and independent "soft" particle production. The formalism also derives conditional two-particle distributions where one particle is restricted to be in-plane or out-of-plane relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers
- The effective elliptic flow measured experimentally is given by \(\tilde{v_2} = (N_s v_2^s + N_h v_2^h) / (N_s + N_h)\), where \(N_s\) and \(N_h\) are the numbers of soft and hard particles per event.
- The number of in-plane or out-of-plane soft particles is \((N_{\text{out}}^{\text{in}})_s = \frac{N_s}{2\pi} (\pi \pm 4 v_2^s)\), and for hard particles \((N_{\text{out}}^{\text{in}})_h = \frac{N_h}{2\pi} (\pi \pm 4 v_2^h)\).
- The two-particle distribution for soft particle pairs is \(2\pi \frac{dn_{1,2}^s}{d(\Delta\phi)} = N_s^p [1 + 2 (v_2^s)^2 \cos 2\Delta\phi]\), where \(N_s^p = N_s (N_s - 1)\).
- The intra-jet two-particle distribution is parameterized as a double Gaussian with widths \(\sigma_N\) for the near-side peak (\(\Delta\phi=0\)) and \(\sigma_B\) for the opposite-side peak (\(\Delta\phi=\pi\)), plus a medium modification factor \(\varepsilon\).

## Context
This work extends the standard approach to elliptic flow analysis by proposing that the measured flow coefficient \(v_2\) is a composite of soft and hard components, rather than a single value. It builds on experimental observations of jet quenching and di-jet correlations at RHIC (Adams 2004, Adler 2003) and provides a framework to disentangle flow and jet contributions in two-particle azimuthal correlations. The model enables future experimental extraction of the separate flow parameters \(v_2^s\) and \(v_2^h\), as well as the number of jets per event, by combined fits to single-particle and conditional two-particle distributions.
