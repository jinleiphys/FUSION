# Octet Baryons at Finite Temperature: QCD Sum Rules vs. Chiral Symmetry

> Correlators of the octet baryons in the hot pion gas are studied in the framework of the QCD sum rule. The condensates appearing in the OPE side of the correlators become T-dependent through the interaction with thermal pions. We present an explicit demonstration that the $O(T^2)$-dependence of the condensates is completely compensated by the change of the pole residue and the $π+ B \rightarrow B'$ scattering effect in the spectral functions. Therefore the baryon masses are constant to this order, although $\langle\bar{u}u\rangle_T\simeq\langle\bar{u}u\rangle_0(1-T^2/8f_π^2)$, which is consistent with the chiral symmetry constraint by Leutwyler and Smilga.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the O(T²)-dependence of QCD condensates in the hot pion gas is exactly compensated by the π + B → B' scattering contribution in the spectral functions, so that octet baryon masses remain constant to O(T²) despite the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate ⟨ūu⟩_T ≃ ⟨ūu⟩_0(1 − T²/8f_π²).

## Method

The authors apply the QCD sum rule method to the retarded correlation functions of octet baryon interpolating fields (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ) at finite temperature, treating the medium as a dilute pion gas valid for T ≤ 150 MeV. They compute the operator product expansion (OPE) side of the correlators using thermal averages of local operators, applying the soft pion theorem to obtain the T-dependence of scalar condensates, and separately evaluate the π + N → N scattering contribution to the spectral function from both direct coupling of the current to pions and from the πNN vertex. The Borel sum rule is then used to extract the mass and pole residue, including the scattering term as an additional structure in the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate changes as ⟨ūu⟩_T ≃ ⟨ūu⟩_0(1 − T²/8f_π²) at O(T²), with ζ = (T²/f_π²)B₁(m_π/T) where B₁ approaches 1 for m_π ≪ T and is suppressed as e^(−m_π/T) for m_π ≫ T
- The nucleon pole residue changes as λ̃²_N(T) = λ̃²_N(0)(1 − (1 + g_A²)ζ/16) at O(T²)
- The π + N → N scattering contribution to the spectral function at q = 0 is given by (λ_N²ζ/(32M_N))(δ(ω−M_N) − δ(ω+M_N)){(1+g_A²)q̸ − (1−g_A²)M_N}
- The gluon condensate ⟨(α_s/π)G²⟩_T changes by only 0.5% at T = 200 MeV and is treated as T-independent

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature QCD sum rule approach for baryons, previously applied to the nucleon by Leutwyler and Smilga (1990) and Eletsky (1993), to the full octet of baryons (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ). It resolves an apparent contradiction between the naive expectation from Ioffe's mass formula (which would predict a dropping nucleon mass as the chiral condensate decreases) and the chiral symmetry constraint that baryon masses are constant to O(T²). The analysis enables future studies of baryon properties at higher temperatures and in systems with finite baryon density, and clarifies that the vacuum saturation assumption for four-quark condensates (⟨(q̅Γq)²⟩_T → ⟨q̅q⟩_T²) is not justified at finite temperature.
