# Inclusive $K^+$ and exclusive $K^+Y$ photoproduction on the deuteron: $Λ$- and $Σ$-threshold phenomena

> Inclusive $K^+$ and exclusive $K^+Y$ photoproduction on the deuteron are investigated theoretically. Modern hyperon-nucleon forces and a recently updated kaon photoproduction operator for the $γ+N\to K^++Y$ process are used. Sizable effects of the hyperon-nucleon final state interaction are found near the $K^+ΛN$ and $K^+ΣN$ thresholds in the inclusive reaction. Angular distributions for the exclusive process show clear $YN$ final state interaction effects in certain kinematic regions. Precise data especially for the inclusive process around the $K^+ΣN$ threshold would help to clarify the strength and property of the $ΛN-ΣN$ interaction.

## Key claim

Sizable effects of the hyperon-nucleon final state interaction are found near the K⁺ΛN and K⁺ΣN thresholds in the inclusive reaction, with the two modern YN potentials (NSC89 and NSC97f) giving predictions that differ by up to 35% near the K⁺ΣN threshold, reflecting different underlying S-matrix pole structures.

## Method

The authors solved a coupled set of integral equations in momentum space and partial-wave decomposed form for the γ(d,K⁺) inclusive and γ(d,K⁺Y)N exclusive processes, using the NSC89 and NSC97f hyperon-nucleon forces (which include Λ-Σ conversion) together with the Nijmegen93 NN potential for the deuteron wavefunction and an updated tree-level kaon photoproduction operator for γ+N→K⁺+Y. The inclusive cross section was computed at photon energy Eγ=1.3 GeV and kaon angle θK=0°, with final state interaction included up to total two-baryon angular momentum jmax=2.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section near the K⁺ΛN threshold: FSI enhances the cross section by up to 86% compared to plane wave impulse approximation
- Peak positions in the inclusive cross section: pK≈945 MeV/c and pK≈809 MeV/c (quasi-free peaks at θK=0°)
- Near the K⁺ΣN threshold: the two YN potentials (NSC89 and NSC97f) give predictions differing by up to 35%
- For exclusive Σ⁻p production at backward angles: results using NSC97f and NSC89 forces differ by more than 50%
- Photon energy used throughout: Eγ=1.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier pioneering calculations of inclusive and exclusive K⁺ photoproduction on the deuteron by Adelseck and Wright (1989) and Li and Wright (1990), which used simpler hyperon-nucleon forces. It also builds on studies of the S-matrix pole structure for the YN system by Miyagawa and Yamamura (1999), who found that different YN potentials produce poles near the Σ threshold at different locations on the unphysical Riemann sheets. The results enable future experimental measurements at Jefferson Lab (scheduled for later that year) to distinguish between different YN forces, and could potentially allow extraction of hyperon-nucleon scattering lengths from configurations where the relative energy between hyperon and nucleon goes to zero.
