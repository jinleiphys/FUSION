# MeV Sterile Neutrino in light of the Cabibbo-Angle Anomaly

> A modified neutrino sector could imprint a signature on precision measurements of the quark sector because many such measurements rely on the semi-leptonic decays of the charged currents. Currently, global fits of the determinations of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix elements point to a $3σ$-level deficit in the first-row CKM unitarity test, commonly referred to as the Cabibbo-angle anomaly. We find that a MeV sterile neutrino that mixes with the electron-type neutrino increases the extracted $|V_{ud}|$, accommodating the Cabibbo-angle anomaly. This MeV sterile neutrino affects the superallowed nuclear $β$ decays and neutron decay, but it barely modifies the other measurements of the CKM elements. While various constraints may apply to such a sterile neutrino, we present viable scenarios within an extension of the inverse seesaw model.

## Key claim
A MeV-scale sterile neutrino that mixes with the electron-type neutrino can resolve the Cabibbo-angle anomaly (a 2.8σ to 3.8σ deficit in first-row CKM unitarity) by increasing the extracted value of |V_ud|, but only if the sterile neutrino mass lies in an intermediate range where it modifies superallowed nuclear β decays and neutron decay while leaving other CKM measurements unchanged.

## Method
The authors performed global χ² fits combining data from ten superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ nuclear β decays, neutron decay measurements (treated separately for bottle ultracold neutron and in-beam methods), kaon semi-leptonic decays, inclusive hadronic τ decays, hyperon decays, and the K_μ2/π_μ2 ratio. They introduced a sterile neutrino mass m_ν4 and mixing angle U_e4 as free parameters, with the phase-space modification factor ε(m_ν4, δM) computed for each decay channel, and imposed unitarity |V_ud|²+|V_us|²=1 at the truth level while treating V_ud and a systematic nuisance parameter α as minimized variables.

## Key numbers
- The global CKM unitarity deficit Δ_CKM = -1.51(53)×10⁻³ (using bottle UCN neutron data) or -2.34(62)×10⁻³ (using in-beam neutron data), corresponding to 2.8σ and 3.8σ deviations respectively
- The favored mixing angle squared is U_e4² ≈ 10⁻³, with a plateau for sterile neutrino masses above the maximum neutrino energy of the ²⁶ᵐAl→²⁶Mg superallowed decay (Q_EC = 4.23 MeV)
- The pull from the SM hypothesis improves from 2.8σ to 2.1σ at the best-fit point with bottle UCN data, and from 3.8σ to 3.2σ with in-beam data
- The superallowed nuclear decay determination yields V_ud,0⁺ = 0.97369(32) in the sterile neutrino decoupling limit, consistent with the reference value 0.97373(31)
- The K_μ2/π_μ2 ratio gives (V_us/V_ud)_fit = 0.23108(51), and V_us from semi-leptonic decays is V_us,fit = 0.22314(51)

## Context
This work extends previous attempts to resolve the Cabibbo-angle anomaly using TeV-scale new physics models (Belfatto 2019, Coutinho 2019, Crivellin 2020-2022) by instead proposing a MeV-scale sterile neutrino that mixes with electron neutrinos. The authors show that heavy sterile neutrinos (above GeV) predict Δ_CKM > 0, contradicting the observed deficit, while massless sterile neutrinos predict Δ_CKM = 0, also incompatible with data. The MeV sterile neutrino scenario can be realized within an inverse seesaw model (Mohapatra 1986, Nandi 1985) that suppresses the neutrinoless double beta decay bound, and the pion decay constraint (R_e/μ^π from the PiENu experiment) can be evaded by a dimension-six scalar operator with a scale Λ = 20-30 TeV.
