# Pseudomagnetic effects for resonance neutrons in the search for time reversal invariance violation

> A general theory of pseudomagnetic effects for the propagation of polarized neutrons through polarized target in multi resonance approach is presented. Some applications related to the proposed search for time reversal invariance violation in neutron scattering are considered.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general multi-resonance formalism for pseudomagnetic effects in polarized neutron propagation through polarized targets, showing that the effective pseudomagnetic field has a noticeable energy dependence in the vicinity of p-wave resonances and is sensitive to target structure, polarization patterns, and partial neutron widths; this formalism is necessary because pseudomagnetic effects can reduce or mimic time reversal invariance violation (TRIV) observables in proposed neutron scattering experiments.

## Method

The authors derived a general expression for the neutron elastic forward scattering amplitude using the reaction matrix (T-matrix) in the Breit-Wigner resonance approximation, accounting for both s-wave and p-wave resonances with different total spins J. They then calculated the pseudomagnetic frequency (neutron spin precession frequency) from the difference of forward scattering amplitudes for neutron spin projections parallel and antiparallel to the nuclear polarization direction, and applied this formalism to the specific case of lanthanum aluminate (LaAlO₃) targets with polarized ¹³⁹La and ²⁷Al nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The pseudomagnetic field conversion factor: B_P/(1 T) = 5.47 ω_P/(1 GHz)
- Experimentally achieved ¹³⁹La polarization in lanthanum aluminate crystals: 47.5% (Hautle, 2000)
- For thermal neutrons, the pseudomagnetic frequency is given by ω_P = (4πNℏ/M_n) × [I/(2I+1)] × (a₊ - a₋)
- For ¹³⁹La: spin I = 7/2, abundance 0.9991, scattering length difference (a₊ - a₋) = 6.9 fm
- For ²⁷Al: spin I = 5/2, abundance 1, scattering length difference (a₊ - a₋) = 0.52 fm
- For ⁴⁵Sc: spin I = 7/2, abundance 1, scattering length difference (a₊ - a₋) = -12.08 fm
- For ⁵⁹Co: spin I = 7/2, abundance 1, scattering length difference (a₊ - a₋) = -12.79 fm
- Replacement fraction x to cancel pseudomagnetic field at thermal neutron energy: x = 0.59 for LaAl_xSc_{1-x}O₃, x = 0.56 for LaAl_xCo_{1-x}O₃

## Context

This work extends the theory of pseudomagnetic effects originally predicted by Baryshevsky (1964) for thermal neutrons and studied by Forte (1973) for single s-wave resonances, to the multi-resonance case including both s-wave and p-wave resonances with different parities. The formalism is motivated by proposed TRIV search experiments (Bowman, 2014) and builds on previous studies of pseudomagnetic effects as backgrounds to TRIV measurements (Gudkov, 1992; Masuda, 1992; Lamoreaux, 1994). The results enable optimization of target composition to cancel pseudomagnetic fields in specific energy regions and provide a new method to measure partial neutron widths through neutron transmission experiments.
