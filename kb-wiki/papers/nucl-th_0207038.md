# Sum Rules for Parity Violating Compton Amplitudes

> Sum rules for parity violating spin polarizabilities are derived and discussed. They hold both for hadron and nuclear stable targets of arbitrary spin and are exact in strong interactions. Examples of applications to the cases of proton and deuteron targets are given. The legitimacy of the dispersive approach in the Standard Model is discussed.

# Sum Rules for Parity Violating Compton Amplitudes

## Key claim
Sum rules for parity violating spin polarizabilities are derived that are exact in strong interactions for hadron and nuclear stable targets of arbitrary spin, but the practical application of these sum rules is limited by the fact that no comparable experimental data exist for parity violating analysis at the moment, so the paper focuses on correlating different parity violating observables via model-independent dispersive relations.

## Method
Forward Compton amplitudes are analyzed using dispersion relations derived from LSZ formalism, with analyticity following from causality and crossing properties independent of parity, charge conjugation, or time-reversal invariance. The parity violating amplitudes are separated from parity conserving ones, and their low-energy behavior is shown to be of order O(ω²) for targets of any spin based on the absence of electric dipole moment couplings for real photons. Sum rules for parity violating forward spin polarizabilities are then obtained by subtracting the dispersion relations at zero energy, and a superconvergence hypothesis is explored to derive a parity violating analogue of the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule.

## Key numbers
- For the proton target, the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) calculation gives β⁻_χ ≃ 4·10⁻⁸ and γ⁻_χ ≃ -1·10⁻⁸ for the parity violating polarizability parameters.
- Using threshold photoproduction cross sections integrated up to 200 MeV from HBχPT gives β⁻ ≃ 1·10⁻⁸ and γ⁻ ≃ -4·10⁻⁹, while using the Born-type exchange model of reference [r16] integrated over the full range gives β⁻ ≃ -5·10⁻⁹ and γ⁻ ≃ -1·10⁻⁸.
- For the deuteron target, the parity violating polarizability parameter γ⁻_D ≃ -2·10⁻⁴ in natural units, and the real part of the forward amplitude near the disintegration threshold (ω ≃ 2.23 MeV) is -Re f^{(-)γ}_{+1}(ω) · (e²/M_D)⁻¹ ≃ -1·10⁻⁸.
- Under the superconvergence hypothesis, the integral from 0.55 GeV to infinity of (σ^T_1 - σ^T_{-1})/ω' dω' ≃ -30 pb.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive sum rule approach, previously successful in parity conserving spin polarizabilities (as in the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule), to the parity violating domain. It addresses the inconsistency between different determinations of the weak πNN coupling constant h^(1)_{πNN} from nuclear physics experiments and atomic physics experiments, and provides a framework to test the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of Chen and Ji (2001) and Chen, Ji, and Wang (2001) for parity violating Compton scattering on the proton. The sum rules enable future low-energy Compton asymmetry measurements to probe higher-energy structure in parity violating photoproduction, and the deuteron results suggest that cusp effects near nuclear inelastic thresholds can enhance parity violating effects by an order of magnitude.
