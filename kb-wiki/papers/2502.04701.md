# Lattice perspectives on doubly heavy tetraquarks

> Doubly heavy tetraquarks have emerged as new probes to study the heavy hadron spectrum. With the experimental observation of the $J^P=1^+$ $T_{cc}^+$, they pose a unique opportunity to bring together efforts in experiment, phenomenology, and lattice QCD. In lattice calculations they are accessible as ground states, unlike hidden flavor tetraquarks, and this enables accurate determinations of the scattering parameters alongside the binding energies of these tetraquarks. Today, lattice calculations firmly predict $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bb}^{ud}$ and $T_{bb}^{us}$ as QCD bound states, while recent studies approaching the $J^P=1^+$ $T_{cc}^{ud}$ find it to be a virtual bound state at slightly non-physical input quark masses. Studies of the $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bc}^{ud}$ are ongoing and a new focus area. In light of these developments the evolution of this field until this point is reviewed. Emphasis is put on the methods in lattice spectroscopy that enable a robust evaluation of the lattice studies gathered. They are further reviewed towards their limitations and achievements. Current challenges and opportunities are discussed, including possibilities to approach the left-hand cut in the scattering analysis of the charm candidates and towards understanding the structure of those including two bottom quarks.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations firmly predict the $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bb}^{ud}$ and $T_{bb}^{us}$ as QCD bound states, while the $J^P=1^+$ $T_{cc}^{ud}$ is found to be a virtual bound state only at slightly non-physical input quark masses, meaning the experimental state's nature depends critically on the precise values of the light and charm quark masses used in the simulation.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice spectroscopy methods applied to doubly heavy tetraquarks, focusing on the finite-volume spectrum approach where tetraquark correlation functions are computed on Euclidean lattices and analyzed using the GEVP (generalized eigenvalue problem) method to extract energy levels. Scattering parameters are obtained by applying the Luscher quantization condition to the finite-volume energy spectrum, with particular attention to the left-hand cut complication in the charm sector. The review also covers the potential approach, including the HAL QCD method, where inter-meson potentials are extracted from lattice data and then used to solve the Schrodinger equation for binding energies.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bb}^{ud}$: $E_B = -189(10)$ MeV from Francis (2016) using the spectrum approach
* Binding energy of $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bb}^{us}$: $E_B = -98(10)$ MeV from Francis (2016) using the spectrum approach
* Binding energy of $J^P=1^+$ $T_{cc}^{ud}$: $E_B = -59^{+53}_{-99}$ MeV from Lyu (2023) using the HAL QCD potential method at lattice pion mass, with the value changing to $-45^{+41}_{-78}$ MeV after rescaling to physical pion mass
* Binding energy of $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bc}^{ud}$: $E_B = -43^{+6}_{-7}(^{+14}_{-24})$ MeV from Padmanath (2023) using a one-parameter effective range expansion
* Scattering length for $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bc}^{ud}$: $a_0 = 0.57^{+4}_{-5}(17)$ fm from Padmanath (2023)
* Binding energy of $J^P=0^+$ $T_{bc}^{ud}$: $E_B = -39^{+4}_{-6}(^{+8}_{-18})$ MeV from Radhakrishnan (2024) using a one-parameter effective range expansion
* Binding energy range for $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bb}^{ud}$ across all studies: $-190 \lesssim E_B \lesssim -70$ MeV
* Binding energy range for $J^P=1^+$ $T_{bb}^{us}$ across all studies: $-100 \lesssim E_B \lesssim -40$ MeV

## Context

This review covers lattice QCD research on doubly heavy tetraquarks from approximately 1990 to early 2024, extending the earlier potential-model work of Richards (1990) on inter-meson potentials and the static-potential studies of Bicudo (2012) that first predicted deeply bound $T_{bb}$ states. The field was significantly boosted by the experimental observation of the $T_{cc}^+$ by LHCb in 2021. The review enables future work by identifying key open challenges: controlling chiral extrapolation to physical pion masses (as shown in preliminary work by Colquhoun (2022)), handling discretization effects for heavy quarks, addressing the left-hand cut in scattering analyses of charm candidates, and developing a consensus on the structure of doubly heavy tetraquarks through properly defined observables like scattering parameters rather than operator overlaps.
