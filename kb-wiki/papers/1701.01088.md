# Resonant nuclear reaction $^{23}$Mg $(p,γ)$ $^{24}$Al in strongly screening magnetized neutron star crust

> Basing on the relativistic theory in superstrong magnetic fields (SMFs), we investigate the influence of strong electron screening (SES) on the rates of nuclear reaction $^{23}$Mg $(p, γ)$ $^{24}$Al by three models of Lai (LD), Fushiki et al. (FGP), and Liu et al. (LJ) on the surface of magnetars. Our results show that the rates can be greatly enhanced by three orders of magnitude due to the influence of SES. The rates in our model are in good agreement with those of LD and FGP at relatively low density environment (e.g. $ρ_7<0.01$) for $1<B_{12}<10^2$. On the other hand, in relatively high magnetic fields (e.g. $B_{12}>10^2$), the rates of our model can be 1.58 times and around three orders of magnitude larger than those of FGP and LD, respectively. The significant increase of the rates of our model for $^{23}$Mg $(p, γ)$ $^{24}$Al implies that more $^{23}$Mg will escape from the Ne-Na cycle due to SES in SMFs. As a consequence, the next reaction $^{24}$Al $(β^+, ν)$ $^{24}$Mg will produce more $^{24}$Mg to participate in the Mg-Al cycle. Thus, it may lead to synthesize a large amount of production of $A\geq 20$ nuclides (e.g. $^{26}$Al) on the surface of magnetars. These heavy elements (e.g. $^{26}$Al) may be thrown out due to the compact binary mergers of double neutron star (NS-NS) or black hole and neutron star (BH and NS) systems. Our results may help to understand why the $^{26}$Al is always overabundance in the interstellar space. Our conclusion may be helpful to the investigation of the nucleosynthesis of some heavy elements, the energy generation rate, and the numerical calculations of magnetars evolution.

## Key claim

The strong electron screening (SES) in superstrong magnetic fields (SMFs) can enhance the resonant nuclear reaction rates of \(^{23}\)Mg\((p,\gamma)^{24}\)Al by up to three orders of magnitude, but this enhancement is strongly conditional on the magnetic field strength and density regime: at relatively low densities (\(\rho_7 < 0.01\)) and moderate fields (\(1 < B_{12} < 10^2\)) the three models (LJ, LD, FGP) agree, while at high fields (\(B_{12} > 10^2\)) the authors' model (LJ) predicts rates 1.58 times larger than FGP and about three orders of magnitude larger than LD.

## Method

The authors computed the electron screening potential (ESP) in superstrong magnetic fields using three theoretical models: their own (LJ), Lai (LD), and Fushiki (FGP), each based on relativistic electron Landau quantization and Thomas-Fermi screening theory. They then calculated the screening enhancement factor (SEF) and the resonant reaction rate for \(^{23}\)Mg\((p,\gamma)^{24}\)Al by summing over four narrow resonances using a Breit-Wigner cross section, incorporating the ESP into the resonance energy shift. The rates were evaluated across a grid of magnetic field strengths (\(B_{12}\) from 1 to \(10^3\)), densities (\(\rho_7\) from 0.01 to 10), and temperatures (\(T_9\) from 0.1 to 0.7), and compared with the non-magnetic Dewitt and Liolios screening models.

## Key numbers

- The maximum ESP in the LJ model reaches 0.1 MeV.
- The maximum ESP in the LD model is 0.008442 MeV at \(B_{12}=80\).
- The maximum ESP in the FGP model is 0.0188 MeV at \(B_{12}=580.7\) and \(\rho_7=0.1\).
- The maximum SEF in the LD model is about 1.632 for \(B_{12}=78.17\) and \(T_9=0.2\).
- The maximum SEF in the FGP model is about 1.66 for \(B_{12}=84.18\) and \(\rho_7=0.01\), and 5.166 at \(\rho_7=1\) and \(T_9=0.2\).
- The maximum SEF in the LJ model reaches 5.056 for \(B_{12}=1000\), \(T_9=0.2\), and \(\rho_7=0.01\).
- At \(B_{12}=10^3\), \(\rho_7=0.01\), and \(T_9=0.1\), the SEF of the LJ model is 25.5680, while the LD model gives \(1.0725\times10^{-15}\) and the FGP model gives \(1.3472\times10^{-13}\).
- At \(B_{12}=10^3\), \(\rho_7=1\), and \(T_9=0.5\), the SEF of the FGP model is 1.9290, the LD model is 0.0011, and the LJ model is 0.9512.

## Context

This work extends the study of strong electron screening on thermonuclear reactions in magnetar crusts, building on prior screening models by Lai (1991, 2001) and Fushiki (1989), and on the authors' own earlier work (Liu 2016) which did not include a comparison among the three models in a superstrong magnetic field. The results suggest that SES in SMFs can significantly enhance the escape of \(^{23}\)Mg from the Ne-Na cycle into the Mg-Al cycle, potentially increasing the production of \(A \geq 20\) nuclides such as \(^{26}\)Al, which may help explain the overabundance of \(^{26}\)Al in interstellar space and inform future studies of magnetar nucleosynthesis, energy generation, and evolution.
