# Angular Correlations in Internal Pair Conversion of Aligned Heavy Nuclei

> We calculate the spatial correlation of electrons and positrons emitted by internal pair conversion of Coulomb excited nuclei in heavy ion collisions. The alignment or polarization of the nucleus results in an anisotropic emission of the electron-positron pairs which is closely related to the anisotropic emission of $γ$-rays. However, the angular correlation in the case of internal pair conversion exhibits diverse patterns. This might be relevant when investigating atomic processes in heavy-ion collisions performed at the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The angular correlation of electron-positron pairs emitted by internal pair conversion of aligned heavy nuclei exhibits diverse patterns that are closely related to, but distinct from, the anisotropic emission of gamma-rays; however, for large-Z nuclei the Born approximation is not justified, and the authors instead employ a distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) that accounts for the Coulomb field of the nucleus.

## Method

The authors calculate the triple angular correlation (opening angle, polar angle of the positron, and dihedral angle) for internal pair conversion of aligned heavy nuclei using relativistic scattering wave functions for both electron and positron, incorporating the Coulomb distortion from the nuclear charge. They express the transition probability in terms of statistical tensors describing the nuclear alignment, and evaluate the radial matrix elements numerically using a Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature with integration contour deformation in the complex plane. The calculations are performed for a uranium-like nucleus (Z=92) with generic transition energies and multipolarities, assuming alignment from Coulomb excitation.

## Key numbers

- For E1 transitions in a uranium-like nucleus (Z=92), the effect of finite nuclear size on the opening angle distribution amounts to less than 0.1%.
- For the electric monopole (E0) conversion, the pair conversion coefficient is given by dη/(dE dcosΘ) = (1/2)(dη/dE)(1 + ε cosΘ), where ε is the corrected anisotropy coefficient.
- The radial integrals are evaluated numerically; at r=20000 fm the integrand is smaller than 10^{-5} of its maximum value.
- The pair emission probability increases towards the maximum positron energy for large-Z nuclei.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Goldring, Rose and Warburton (1960s) that were performed within the Born approximation, neglecting the influence of the nuclear charge on the outgoing electron and positron. The authors correct an error in their own previous work (Hofmann 1990) regarding the sign of the scattering phase shifts of the positron. The formalism enables the calculation of angular correlations for internal pair conversion following Coulomb excitation or transfer reactions, and can be applied using the COULEX code of Alder and Winther to determine the statistical tensors for specific experimental setups.
