# Shell-model study of single-neutron strength fragmentation in 137Xe

> We have performed shell-model calculations for the nucleus $^{137}$Xe, which was recently studied experimentally using the $^{136}$Xe($d,p$) reaction in inverse kinematics. The main aim of our study has been to investigate the single-neutron properties of the observed states, focusing attention on the spectroscopic factors. We have employed a realistic low-momentum two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential that has already proved quite successful in describing the spectroscopic properties of nuclei in the $^{132}$Sn region. Comparison shows that our calculations reproduce very well the experimental excitation energies and yield spectroscopic factors that come close to those extracted from the data.

## Key claim

The shell-model calculations reproduce experimental excitation energies within 150 keV for most states and yield spectroscopic factors within 23% of experimental values, but the model overestimates the spectroscopic factor of the yrast 9/2⁻ state (0.72 calculated versus 0.43 experimental) and underestimates the fragmentation of the 9/2⁻ single-neutron strength, possibly due to a too strong neutron-proton interaction.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for ¹³⁷Xe using a realistic low-momentum two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the Vlow-k approach with a cutoff momentum Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹, followed by a Q-box folded-diagram expansion including diagrams up to second order. The model space assumed doubly magic ¹³²Sn core with four valence protons in the 50-82 shell orbits and one valence neutron in the 82-126 shell orbits, with single-particle energies taken from experimental levels in ¹³³Sb and ¹³³Sn. Calculations were performed using the OXBASH code, and results were compared with experimental data from the ¹³⁶Xe(d,p) reaction measured at Argonne National Laboratory using the HELIOS spectrometer.

## Key numbers

- Ground state 7/2⁻: calculated spectroscopic factor 0.86 versus experimental 0.94
- Yrast 9/2⁻ state at 1.218 MeV: calculated spectroscopic factor 0.72 versus experimental 0.43
- Second 9/2⁻ state at 1.590 MeV: calculated spectroscopic factor 0.01 versus experimental 0.24
- 13/2⁺ state at 1.751 MeV: calculated excitation energy 2.082 MeV (overestimated by about 330 keV), spectroscopic factor 0.75 versus experimental 0.84
- Energy difference between 0i₁₃/₂ and 0h₉/₂ neutron orbit centroids: 0.8 MeV, a decrease of about 0.3 MeV relative to the ¹³³Sn value
- Monopole component V^M(νi₁₃/₂ πg₇/₂) = -0.33 MeV, about twice the value of V^M(νh₉/₂ πg₇/₂)
- Proton occupancy of πg₇/₂ orbit in ¹³⁶Xe ground state: 3.1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei beyond ¹³²Sn (Coraggio 2009, Covello 2011) using the same realistic Hamiltonian without adjustable parameters, now applied to interpret the single-neutron structure of ¹³⁷Xe measured by Kay (2011) using the ¹³⁶Xe(d,p) reaction in inverse kinematics. The study confirms the reduction in separation between the 0i₁₃/₂ and 0h₉/₂ neutron orbits, previously attributed to the neutron-proton tensor force by Otsuka (2005) and Kay (2011), and demonstrates that the monopole components of the realistic effective Hamiltonian account for this effect. The results enable future shell-model predictions for other nuclei around ¹³²Sn that are accessible via (d,p) transfer reactions with radioactive ion beams.
