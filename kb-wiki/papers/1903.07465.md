# Proof by characters of the orthogonal-orthogonal duality and relations of Casimir invariants

> The theorem of orthogonal-orthogonal duality of Rowe, Repka, and Carvalho is proven by a method based on characters that is very different from theirs and akin to Helmers's half a century earlier proof of the analogous sympletic-symplectic duality. I demonstrate how three duality theorems listed by Rowe, Repka, and Carvalho allow very brief derivations of linear relations between the Casimir invariants of the connected representations based on the geometry of their Young diagrams, and discuss for which physical systems other than such already considered in the literature an analysis in terms of the orthogonal-orthogonal duality might be useful.

## Key claim

The paper proves the orthogonal-orthogonal duality theorem of Rowe, Repka, and Carvalho using a character-based method similar to Helmers's 1961 proof of the symplectic-symplectic duality, rather than the algebraic approach used by the original authors. The proof is valid for both even and odd dimensions of the one-kind configuration space, with the caveat that for even d and λ₁ > 0 the character expression gives the sum of two inequivalent irreps rather than a single irrep.

## Method

The author constructs two commuting orthogonal Lie algebras on the Fock space of k kinds of fermions sharing a d-dimensional one-kind configuration space: a number-conserving algebra lo(d) from infinitesimal orthogonal transformations of the configuration space, and a number-non-conserving algebra lo(2k) from bilinear combinations of creation and annihilation operators. The duality is proven by evaluating the character of the direct sum representation on the full Fock space as a product over single-particle states, then expanding this product using determinant identities for the characters of lo(d) and lo(2k) representations, separately treating the cases of even d and odd d.

## Key numbers

- For even d, the unfolded partitions [[l]] and [[m]] of the dual representations must together contain the numbers 0, 1, ..., Ω + k - 1, where d = 2Ω.
- For odd d, the unfolded partitions must together contain the numbers 1/2, 3/2, ..., Ω + k - 1/2, where d = 2Ω + 1.
- The lo(d) representation has no part greater than k, and the lo(2k) representation has no part greater than d/2.
- For each p, the pth row of the lo(d) Young diagram and the pth column of the lo(2k) Young diagram have total length k; for each τ, the τth row of the lo(2k) diagram and the τth column of the lo(d) diagram have total length d/2.

## Context

This work extends Helmers's (1961) character-based proof of the symplectic-symplectic duality to the orthogonal-orthogonal case, which was previously proven by Rowe, Repka, and Carvalho (2011) using a different algebraic method involving highest-weight states. The paper also demonstrates how the three duality theorems (general linear, symplectic, and orthogonal) allow brief derivations of linear relations between Casimir invariants of connected representations based on Young diagram geometry, and discusses potential applications to physical systems beyond those already considered in the literature, such as the lo(8) quasi-spin algebra of Flowers and Szpikowski (1964) used in studies of neutron-proton pairing and Cooper pair condensation.
