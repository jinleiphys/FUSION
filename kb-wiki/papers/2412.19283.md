# Probing medium response via strangeness enhancement around quenched jets

> Jet-induced medium excitation is a crucial part of jet interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and has recently been confirmed by experiment for the first time. Based on the AMPT model simulation, we propose the strangeness enhancement around quenched jets as a novel signature of jet-induced medium excitation. By applying the jet-particle correlation techniques, we calculate jet-induced particle yields around the jets and find a significant enhancement of the strange-to-non-strange-hadron ratio and the double-to-single-strange-hadron ratio correlated with jets in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions relative to proton-proton collisions. This enhancement increases with both the strength of jet-QGP interactions and the radial distance from jet axis. These observations align with the features of jet-induced medium excitation and parton coalescence in hadron formation, and await experimental validation in the future measurements.

## Key claim

Based on AMPT model simulations, the authors propose that the enhancement of strange-to-non-strange-hadron ratios and double-to-single-strange-hadron ratios around quenched jets in Pb+Pb collisions relative to proton-proton collisions serves as a novel signature of jet-induced medium excitation, but this prediction awaits experimental validation.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT model to simulate Pb+Pb and proton-proton collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair. They applied jet-particle correlation techniques with mixed-event and side-band background subtraction to extract the two-dimensional distributions of identified hadrons (pions, kaons, Lambdas, Xis, and phis) relative to reconstructed anti-kT jets with cone size R=0.4 and jet transverse momentum above 120 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Jet transverse momentum threshold: pTjet > 120 GeV, with jet cone size R = 0.4 and jet pseudorapidity |ηjet| < 1.6
- Partonic cross-section in the ZPC module set to 1.5 mb for Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- Identified particles selected with |η| < 2.4 and transverse momentum bins with boundaries at 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 8 GeV
- The strangeness enhancement for Λ/π, Ξ/π, and φ/π ratios peaks in the intermediate pT region of 2-4 GeV in central Pb+Pb collisions
- The enhancement of strange-to-non-strange ratios increases with radial distance from the jet axis and is stronger in more central collisions (0-30%) compared to peripheral collisions (50-100%)
- The AMPT calculation for the (K+ + K-)/(π+ + π-) ratio inside jets (Δr < 0.4) in 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions agrees with ALICE preliminary data within experimental uncertainties

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon enhancement around quenched jets by Chen (2021), Luo (2021), and Sirimanna (2022), and builds on the recent experimental confirmation of the diffusion wake energy depletion by the CMS Collaboration (2023). The authors use the AMPT model, which has been successfully applied to study anisotropic collective flow, dijet asymmetries, jet fragmentation functions, and baryon enhancement around jets. The results enable future experimental measurements of hadron chemistry inside and outside jets to serve as an unambiguous signature of medium response, complementing the Mach-cone and diffusion wake approaches proposed by Casalderrey-Solana (2004), Betz (2008), and Tachibana (2017).
