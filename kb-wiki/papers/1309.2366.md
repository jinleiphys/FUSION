# Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov descriptions of deformed weakly-bound nuclei in large coordinate spaces

> Weakly-bound deformed nuclei have been studied by the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach in large coordinate-space boxes. In particular, the box-size dependence of the HFB calculations of weakly-bound deformed nuclei are investigated, including the particle density and pairing density distributions at nuclear surfaces, the near-threshold resonant and continuum quasiparticle spectra, and energetic properties. The box size may have larger influences in pairing properties than in other bulk properties. We demonstrate that large-box calculations of weakly-bound nuclei are important to precisely describe exotic phenomena such as deformed halos and peninsulas of stability beyond drip lines.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that large-box (up to 36×36 fm) coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations are necessary to precisely describe deformed halo structures, pairing density distributions, broad quasiparticle resonances, and predictions of stability beyond the neutron drip line in weakly-bound deformed nuclei, but the authors note that precise predictions of deformed halos remain dependent upon the specific models and effective interactions used.

## Method

The Skyrme-HFB equation was solved using the hfbax code with B-spline techniques on a 2D lattice box for axially symmetric deformed nuclei, employing the SLy4 force for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent surface pairing interaction fitted to the neutron gap of ¹²⁰Sn. Calculations were performed with box sizes up to 36×36 fm, a maximum mesh size of 0.6 fm, and 12th-order B-splines, using hybrid MPI+OpenMP parallel programming on the Tianhe-1A supercomputer. The quasiparticle energy cutoff was taken as (60 − λ) MeV, and the continuum was discretized via box boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Box sizes up to 36×36 fm were used, compared to 30×30 fm in previous work (Pei 2013).
- For ³⁸Ne, the total binding energy changed from −220.29 MeV (24 fm box) to −220.33 MeV (36 fm box); the neutron pairing energy decreased from −68.10 MeV to −67.43 MeV over the same range.
- The neutron quadrupole deformation β₂ₙ of ³⁸Ne increased from 0.13 (24 fm box) to 0.34 (36 fm box).
- The neutron Fermi energy λₙ of ³⁸Ne decreased from −0.079 MeV (24 fm box) to −0.116 MeV (36 fm box).
- For ¹⁹⁸Nd (N=138), the coordinate-space HFB calculation gave λₙ = −35 keV (bound), while HFB-THO with 30 shells gave λₙ = +68 keV (unbound); ¹⁹⁸Nd has β₂=0.28 and Δₙ=0.59 MeV.
- The smoothing parameter for quasiparticle occupation numbers was 50 keV.

## Context

This work extends previous coordinate-space HFB studies of deformed weakly-bound nuclei (Pei 2013) by using larger box sizes and examining box-size dependence in detail. It contrasts with harmonic oscillator (HO) and transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis methods (hfbtho code), showing that those basis expansions fail to reproduce deformed halos in ³⁸Ne and ⁴⁴Mg and underestimate the widths of broad quasiparticle resonances such as the 1/2[300] state. The results enable more reliable predictions of deformed halos, pairing properties, and possible peninsulas of stability beyond the neutron drip line, such as the candidate ¹⁹⁸Nd near N=138, building on earlier discussions of islands of stability (Nazarewicz 1999) and deformed halos (Misu 1997, Zhou 2010).
