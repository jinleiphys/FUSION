# Spectra and Symmetry in Nuclear Pairing

> We apply the algebraic Bethe ansatz technique to the nuclear pairing problem with orbit dependent coupling constants and degenerate single particle energy levels. We find the exact energies and eigenstates. We show that for a given shell, there are degeneracies between the states corresponding to less and more than half full shell. We also provide a technique to solve the equations of Bethe ansatz.

## Key claim

The paper provides an algebraic Bethe ansatz proof for the exact eigenstates and energies of a nuclear pairing Hamiltonian with orbit-dependent coupling constants and degenerate single-particle energies, valid for any complex values of the coupling constants without requiring analytic continuation. The authors demonstrate a symmetry between states with N nucleon pairs and states with N_max+1−N nucleon pairs (i.e., less than and more than half-full shells), which holds for generic occupation amplitudes, not just in the quasispin limit.

## Method

The authors apply the algebraic Bethe ansatz technique to the pairing Hamiltonian Ĥ = −|G| Σ_{jj'} c*_j c_{j'} Ŝ⁺_j Ŝ⁻_{j'} (with degenerate single-particle levels), constructing eigenstates using operators Ŝ⁺(x) = Σ_j [c*_j / (1 − |c_j|²x)] Ŝ⁺_j acting on the empty-shell vacuum |0⟩, and Ŝ⁻(x) acting on the full-shell state |0̄⟩. They derive Bethe ansatz equations (BAE) that determine the parameters x, and show that the same BAE yield eigenstates for both particle pairs (less than half-full) and hole pairs (more than half-full) with identical energies. For the special case of two nuclear levels, they transform the BAE into a hypergeometric differential equation whose polynomial solutions give the roots analytically.

## Key numbers

- The energy of the full-shell state |0̄⟩ is E₁ = −|G| Σ_j Ω_j |c_j|², where Ω_j = j + 1/2 is the maximum number of pairs in level j.
- For a shell with two levels j₁ and j₂, the zero-energy one-pair state has parameter x^(1) = 1/|c_{j₂}|² + [Ω_{j₂}/(Ω_{j₁}+Ω_{j₂})] (1/|c_{j₁}|² − 1/|c_{j₂}|²).
- The Bethe ansatz equations for N pairs (zero-energy states) are: Σ_j [−Ω_j/2] / [1/|c_j|² − x^(N)_m] = Σ_{k≠m} 1/(x^(N)_m − x^(N)_k) for m = 1,…,N.
- The nonzero-energy states with N pairs have energy E_N = −|G| ( Σ_j Ω_j |c_j|² − Σ_{k=1}^{N−1} 2/z^(N)_k ), where the z^(N)_k satisfy: Σ_j [−Ω_j/2] / [1/|c_j|² − z^(N)_m] = 1/z^(N)_m + Σ_{k≠m} 1/(z^(N)_m − z^(N)_k) for m = 1,…,N−1.
- For two levels, zero-energy states exist only for N = 0,1,2,…,Ω_{j₁} (where Ω_{j₁} ≤ Ω_{j₂}), with exactly one zero-energy state per pair number.

## Context

This work extends the Richardson (1963) algebraic approach to nuclear pairing, which was historically complex and not widely used, and the quasispin formalism of Kerman (1961) that applied only to seniority-conserving interactions. The authors provide an alternative algebraic proof to the Bethe ansatz solution of Pan et al. (1997) that does not rely on analytic continuation, and they show the symmetry between particle and hole states explicitly. The results enable exact solutions for degenerate-shell pairing problems with arbitrary coupling constants, complementing numerical approaches in the quasispin basis (Volya, 2000).
