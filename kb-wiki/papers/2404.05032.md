# Influence of the chiral magnetic effect on particle-pair elliptic anisotropy

> Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is a phenomenon in which electric charge is separated by a strong magnetic field from local domains of chirality imbalance in quantum chromodynamics. The CME-sensitive, azimuthal correlator difference $Δγ$ between opposite-sign (OS) and same-sign (SS) charged hadron pairs is contaminated by a major physics background proportional to the particle elliptic anisotropy ($v_2$). The CME signal, on the other hand, contributes to the difference in the pair elliptic anisotropies between OS and SS pairs ($Δv_{2,\rm pair}$). We investigate $Δv_{2,\rm pair}$ and found its sensitivity to CME to be similar to that of the $Δγ$ observable.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the difference in elliptic anisotropy between opposite-sign and same-sign charged hadron pairs (Δv₂,pair) has similar sensitivity to the chiral magnetic effect as the standard Δγ correlator, and therefore does not provide significantly additional insights for CME searches.

## Method

The authors used the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV for 30-40% centrality, with three values of the axial charge density to entropy density ratio (n₅/s = 0, 0.1, and 0.2). They calculated the elliptic anisotropy of opposite-sign and same-sign charged hadron pairs (v₂,pair) as a function of invariant mass and as integrated quantities, and compared the difference Δv₂,pair to the standard Δγ observable and to Δγ − v₂Δδ.

## Key numbers

* Three axial charge density values were used: n₅/s = 0, 0.1, and 0.2, each with approximately 20 million events generated.
* Particles were selected within |η| < 1 and 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c, typical of STAR detector acceptance.
* The magnetic field lifetime was set to τB = 0.6 fm/c, comparable to the initial time of hydrodynamic evolution.
* The ρ resonance v₂ calculated by the invariant mass method (Eq. 4) was constant over n₅/s, suggesting CME influences on resonance elliptic anisotropies are small.
* Δv₂,pair at n₅/s = 0 shows a nonzero value, indicating a background contribution to this observable.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Xu (2023) that resonance v₂ and particle pair v₂ contain CME information, by systematically testing whether Δv₂,pair offers advantages over the standard Δγ observable. The authors find that Δv₂,pair suffers from similar signal-background ambiguity as Δγ, which has been the subject of extensive study by the STAR Collaboration (2019) and others. The paper concludes that the Δv₂,pair observable likely does not bring significantly additional insights beyond the Δγ observable for CME searches.
