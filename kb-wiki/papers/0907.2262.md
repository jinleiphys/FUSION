# Viscous hydrodynamics with bulk viscosity -- uncertainties from relaxation time and initial conditions

> Bulk viscosity suppresses elliptic flow v_2, as does shear viscosity. It can thus not be neglected when extracting the shear viscosity from elliptic flow data. We here explore uncertainties in the bulk viscous contribution to viscous v_2 suppression that arise from presently uncontrolled uncertainties in the initial value of the bulk viscous pressure and its microscopic relaxation time.

# Viscous hydrodynamics with bulk viscosity -- uncertainties from relaxation time and initial conditions

## Key claim
Bulk viscosity suppresses elliptic flow v2 similarly to shear viscosity, but the magnitude of this suppression is highly uncertain because it depends on both the unknown relaxation time for the bulk viscous pressure and the unknown initial value of that pressure; for a given measured v2, accounting for bulk viscosity at twice the "minimal" level reduces the extracted shear viscosity η/s by roughly a factor of 2/3.

## Method
The authors used the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 to solve transport equations for the energy-momentum tensor and the bulk viscous pressure Π, employing a phenomenological temperature-dependent specific bulk viscosity ζ/s(T) that peaks near Tc. They tested three choices for the bulk relaxation time τΠ (0.5 fm/c, 5 fm/c, and a temperature-dependent function implementing critical slowing down) and two initializations for Π (Navier-Stokes value and zero) at initial time τ0 = 0.6 fm/c for non-central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 7 fm.

## Key numbers
- For minimal bulk viscosity with short relaxation time τΠ = 0.5 fm/c, the v2 suppression at pT = 0.5 GeV is approximately 8% relative to ideal hydrodynamics, insensitive to initialization of Π.
- For τΠ = 5 fm/c with zero initialization, the v2 suppression at pT = 0.5 GeV/c is only about 2% relative to ideal hydrodynamics.
- For τΠ = 5 fm/c with Navier-Stokes initialization, the v2 suppression at pT = 0.5 GeV/c is about 10% relative to ideal hydrodynamics.
- For twice the minimal bulk viscosity (C = 2), the viscous v2 suppression increases from 20% (shear viscosity only, η/s = 1/4π) to 30% (including bulk viscosity), a 50% relative increase.
- The temperature-dependent τΠ(T) yields τΠ ≈ 0.6 fm/c at T = 350 MeV and τΠ ≈ 5 fm/c at Tc.

## Context
This work extends earlier viscous hydrodynamic studies by Song and Heinz (2008) and others (Romatschke 2007, Luzum 2008) that focused primarily on shear viscosity effects, adding systematic exploration of bulk viscosity uncertainties. The results show that extracting η/s from elliptic flow data, as first attempted by Luzum and Romatschke (2008) who found η/s|QGP < 5 × 1/4π, requires controlling bulk viscous contributions and their dependence on relaxation time and initial conditions. The paper identifies the need for additional research on initial conditions and relaxation times for the bulk viscous pressure before quantitative extraction of η/s is possible.
