# LHC jet suppression of light and heavy flavor observables

> Jet suppression of light and heavy flavor observables is considered to be an excellent tool to study the properties of QCD matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We calculate the suppression patterns of light hadrons, D mesons, non-photonic single electrons and non-prompt $J/ψ$ in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. We use a theoretical formalism that takes into account finite size {\it dynamical} QCD medium with finite magnetic mass effects and running coupling, which is integrated into a numerical procedure that uses no free parameters in model testing. We obtain a good agreement with the experimental results across different experiments/particle species. Our results show that the developed theoretical formalism can robustly explain suppression data in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions, which strongly suggests that pQCD in Quark-Gluon Plasma is able to provide a reasonable description of the underlying jet physics at LHC.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their theoretical formalism, which includes finite-size dynamical QCD medium effects, finite magnetic mass, and running coupling, can robustly explain jet suppression data for both light and heavy flavor observables at LHC, with the hedge that the agreement is demonstrated across six independent observables using no free parameters in model testing, with parameter values fixed in advance according to standard literature values.

## Method

The authors compute quenched spectra using a pQCD convolution that combines initial parton spectra (at NLO), an energy loss probability generalized to include both radiative and collisional energy loss in a finite-size dynamical QCD medium, fragmentation functions, and decay functions for heavy flavor. The radiative energy loss includes multi-gluon fluctuations and path-length fluctuations, while the collisional energy loss uses a Gaussian approximation. Running coupling is introduced by factorizing the coupling terms in both radiative and collisional energy loss, with the Debye mass obtained self-consistently and the running coupling defined by the standard pQCD expression.

## Key numbers

- QGP temperature: T = 304 MeV (as determined by ALICE)
- Perturbative QCD scale: Λ_QCD = 0.2 GeV
- Effective light quark flavors: n_f = 2.5
- Debye mass: μ_E ≈ 0.9 GeV (obtained by self-consistently solving Eq. (mu))
- Magnetic mass range: 0.4 μ_E < μ_M < 0.6 μ_E
- Charm quark mass: M = 1.2 GeV
- Bottom quark mass: M = 4.75 GeV
- Light quark thermal mass: M = μ_E/√6
- Gluon mass: m_g = μ_E/√2
- Collision system: 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, 0-5% most central (except non-photonic single electrons at 0-10% and non-prompt J/ψ at 0-100%)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism for first-order in opacity radiative energy loss in a finite-size dynamical QCD medium (Djordjevic 2009, Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) and their generalization to finite magnetic mass (Djordjevic and Djordjevic 2012), combined with collisional energy loss calculations (Djordjevic 2006). The paper enables comprehensive testing of pQCD-based jet suppression against a diverse set of LHC observables (light hadrons, D mesons, non-photonic single electrons, non-prompt J/ψ) within a single numerical framework, and together with the authors' previous study addressing the heavy flavor puzzle at RHIC (Djordjevic 2012), suggests that pQCD in QGP can describe underlying jet physics at LHC.
