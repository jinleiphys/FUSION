# Chiral anomaly from (anomalous) spin hydrodynamics

> We show that the low energy fluctuations of spinning black Dp branes are described by a theory of spin hydrodynamics on a spacetime $\mathbb{M}_{p+1}\times \mathbb{T}^{n+2}$ in which the fluid is flowing on $\mathbb{M}_{p+1}$ and spinning on $\mathbb{T}^{n+2}$. Focusing on the hydrodynamic regime of $\mathcal{N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, we provide a geometric interpretation of the R-current anomaly in terms of a gravitational anomaly from the ten-dimensional point of view. This follows from the holographic duality between a spinning fluid in ten dimensions and an anomalous chiral fluid in four dimensions. We comment on the relations between the theory of spin hydrodynamics introduced here and other theories of spin hydrodynamics in the context of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the low-energy fluctuations of spinning black D3 branes are described by a theory of spin hydrodynamics in ten dimensions, and that the R-current anomaly of four-dimensional N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory receives a geometric interpretation as a gravitational anomaly from the ten-dimensional perspective, specifically within the near-horizon limit and under a specific Kaluza-Klein reduction ansatz.

## Method

The authors extend the blackfold approach to strongly spinning Dp branes, treating the monopole stress tensor and spin current as equally important in a gradient expansion organized by a bookkeeping parameter. They extract the stress tensor and spin current from the asymptotic expansion of spinning D3 brane metrics using linearized gravity, then derive equations of motion from conservation of the full spacetime stress tensor. The equilibrium partition function is constructed from the temperature and spin chemical potential, and anomalous contributions to the spin current conservation law are obtained from the coupling of the worldvolume fluid to the five-form flux via anomaly inflow.

## Key numbers

* The spin current for spinning D3 branes takes the form S_a^{ij} = l J^A epsilon_A^{ij} u_a, where l = 1/lambda is the bookkeeping parameter and J^A is the angular momentum density on Cartan plane A = 1,2,3.
* The anomalous spin conservation law is given by tilde_nabla_a S^a_{ij} = (l^3/8) C_{ijklmn} epsilon^{abcd} Omega_{ab}^{kl} Omega_{cd}^{mn}, where C_{ijklmn} is a constant matrix of anomaly coefficients.
* The corrected constitutive equations include a chiral vortical effect with coefficient xi_{ij} = partial xi_tilde / partial (mu^{ij}/T) and a chiral spin effect with coefficient xi^S_{ijkl} = partial xi_tilde^S_{kl} / partial (mu^{ij}/T), where xi_tilde = C_{ijklmn} mu^{ij} mu^{kl} mu^{mn} / (3T) and xi_tilde^S_{ij} = C_{ijklmn} mu^{kl} mu^{mn} / (2T).

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of chiral transport in N=4 SYM, previously developed from a five-dimensional perspective by Erdmenger (2008), Banerjee (2008), and Son (2009) using R-charged black holes, by uplifting the description to ten dimensions. It connects two active research areas in heavy-ion collisions: the chiral anomaly (responsible for chiral vortical and chiral magnetic effects) and spin hydrodynamics (motivated by spin polarization of Lambda hyperons). The paper enables a geometric understanding of the chiral anomaly as a gravitational anomaly in ten dimensions and provides a framework for studying instabilities of Dp branes, such as the hydrodynamic instability in spin diffusion modes recently found by Gladden (2024) from a five-dimensional perspective.
