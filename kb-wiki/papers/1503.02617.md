# Parity violating effects in an exotic perturbation of the rigid rotator

> The perturbation of the free rigid rotator by the trigonometric Scarf potential is shown to conserve its energy excitation patterns and change only the wave functions towards spherical harmonics rescaled by a function of an unspecified parity, or mixtures of such rescaled harmonics of equal magnetic quantum numbers and different angular momenta. In effect, no parity can be assigned to the states of the rotational bands emerging in this exotic way, and the electric dipole operator is allowed to acquire non-vanishing expectation values.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the rigid rotator perturbed by the trigonometric Scarf potential produces energy spectra identical to the free rigid rotator, but the wave functions lose definite parity and become mixtures of rescaled spherical harmonics of different angular momenta. The authors explicitly hedge that this isospectrality holds for continuous values of the potential parameter \(b\), not only for discrete values where the Hamiltonian is related by a similarity transformation to the squared angular momentum operator.

## Method

The authors start from the free rigid rotator Hamiltonian proportional to \(\mathbf{J}^2\) and add the trigonometric Scarf potential \(\mathcal{V}(\theta) = b^2/\cos^2\theta - 2bM\tan\theta/\cos\theta - 1/4\). After a variable change \(U(\theta) = \sqrt{\cos\theta}\,\phi(\theta)\), the angular eigenvalue problem reduces to a one-dimensional Schrödinger equation with the Scarf I potential, which is exactly solvable via Jacobi polynomials. The resulting wave functions are expressed as rescaled spherical harmonics \(\widetilde{Y}_J^M = \sqrt{\cos\theta}\,\mathbf{W}(\theta) Y_J^M\) with \(\mathbf{W}(\theta) = [(1+\sin\theta)/(1-\sin\theta)]^{b/2}\), and for non-maximal \(|M|\) values they become mixtures of different \(J\) values.

## Key numbers

* The energy eigenvalues of the perturbed rotator are \(\epsilon_t = t(t+1)\) with \(t = |M| + n = 0,1,2,\dots\), identical to the free rotator energies \(E_J = \mathcal{B}J(J+1)\) (here \(\mathcal{B}=1\) in dimensionless units).
* The potential parameter \(b\) is constrained by \(|M|-b+1 > 0\) and \(|M|+b+1 > 0\); for \(|M|=0\) this implies \(|b| < 1\).
* In the example for \(t=2, |M|=1\), the wave function is \(\psi_2^1 = -b\,\widetilde{Y}_1^1 + (2/3)\,\widetilde{Y}_2^1\), mixing rescaled harmonics of opposite parity.
* The figure uses a generic continuous value \(b = 0.45\) to illustrate the non-equivalent probability density distributions for equal energy.

## Context

This work extends the concept of isospectral potentials from flat-space quantum mechanics (where pairs like Morse and hyperbolic Scarf potentials are known, as reviewed by Khare) to the angular part of the rigid rotator on the sphere. It parallels the classic question by Mark Kac (1966) on whether one can "hear the shape of a drum," showing that identical rotational frequency patterns can arise from distinct Hamiltonians. The results enable the possibility of exotic rotational bands in diatomic molecules, di-molecular systems, or quark systems where states carry non-vanishing electric dipole moments despite having the same energy spectrum as the canonical rigid rotator.
