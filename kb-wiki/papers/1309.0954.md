# Nucleosynthesis in the outflows associated with accretion disks of Type II collapsars

> We investigate nucleosynthesis inside the outflows from gamma-ray burst (GRB) accretion disks formed by the Type II collapsars. In these collapsars, massive stars undergo core collapse to form a proto-neutron star initially and a mild supernova explosion is driven. The supernova ejecta lack momentum and subsequently this newly formed neutron star gets transformed to a stellar mass black hole via massive fallback. The hydrodynamics and the nucleosynthesis in these accretion disks has been studied extensively in the past. Several heavy elements are synthesized in the disk and much of these heavy elements are ejected from the disk via winds and outflows. We study nucleosynthesis in the outflows launched from these disks by using an adiabatic, spherically expanding outflow model, to understand which of these elements thus synthesized in the disk survive in the outflow. While studying this we find that many new elements like isotopes of titanium, copper, zinc etc. are present in the outflows. 56Ni is abundantly synthesized in most of the cases in the outflow which implies that the outflows from these disks in a majority of cases will lead to an observable supernova explosion. It is mainly present when outflow is considered from the He-rich, 56Ni/54Fe rich zones of the disks. However, outflow from the Si-rich zone of the disk remains rich in silicon. Although, emission lines of many of these heavy elements have been observed in the X-ray afterglows of several GRBs by Chandra, BeppoSAX, XMM-Newton etc., Swift seems to have not detected these lines yet.

## Key claim
The outflows from Type II collapsar accretion disks can synthesize abundant 56Ni, implying that most such outflows will produce an observable supernova explosion, but this outcome depends on the outflow being launched from He-rich or 56Ni/54Fe-rich zones of the disk rather than from Si-rich zones.

## Method
The authors used an adiabatic, spherically expanding outflow model (Fujimoto et al. 2004) coupled to a nuclear network code with reaction rates from the JINA Reaclib Database. They launched outflows from different radii of accretion disks around a 3 solar mass Schwarzschild black hole, with accretion rates of 0.001 and 0.01 solar masses per second, considering both He-rich and Si-rich initial compositions, and varied the outflow entropy (equal to or double the disk entropy) and ejection velocity (corresponding to outflow-to-inflow mass ratios of 0.1 and 100).

## Key numbers
- Accretion rate for Type II collapsar disks: 10^-4 to 10^-2 solar masses per second (from Fujimoto et al. 2003)
- Disk models used: 0.001 and 0.01 solar masses per second
- Outflow launch radius: less than or equal to 200 R_g (gravitational radii)
- Ejection velocities considered: approximately 10^5 cm/s and 10^8 cm/s
- Entropy per baryon in outflow: S ~ 15k (same as disk) and S ~ 30k (double the disk entropy)
- 56Ni decays to 56Co with a half-life of 6 days, then to 56Fe with a half-life of 77 days

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nucleosynthesis in Type II collapsar accretion disks (Banerjee and Mukhopadhyay 2013a,b) by examining which disk-synthesized elements survive in outflows. It builds on the collapsar model of Woosley (1993) and MacFadyen and Woosley (1999), and on prior outflow nucleosynthesis studies by Fujimoto et al. (2004, 2007, 2008), Pruet et al. (2002, 2003), and Milosavljevic et al. (2012). The results enable predictions of which heavy-element emission lines should be observable in GRB afterglows, addressing the discrepancy that Swift has not detected lines seen by Chandra, BeppoSAX, and XMM-Newton.
