# Investigating the Seebeck effect of the QGP medium using a novel relaxation time approximation model

> The highly energetic particle medium formed in the ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision displays a notable difference in the temperatures between its central and peripheral regions. This temperature gradient can generate an electric field within the medium, a phenomenon referred to as the Seebeck effect. We have estimated the Seebeck coefficient for a dense quark-gluon plasma medium by using the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the recently developed novel relaxation time approximation (RTA) model within the kinetic theory framework. This study explores the Seebeck coefficient of individual quark flavors as well as the entire partonic medium. Our observation indicates that, for given current quark masses, the magnitude of the Seebeck coefficient for each quark flavor as well as for the partonic medium decreases as the temperature rises and increases as the chemical potential increases. Furthermore, we have investigated the Seebeck effect by considering the partonic interactions within the quasiparticle model. In addition, we have presented a comparison between our findings and the results of the standard RTA model. We have observed that the Seebeck coefficient of the QGP medium gets conspicuously decreased in the novel RTA model as compared to that in the standard RTA model. A decreased Seebeck coefficient in the novel RTA model describes a smaller magnitude of induced electric field in the medium than that estimated by the standard RTA model. However, the rate of decline gets gradually smaller as the medium gets hotter for both the current quark mass scenario and the quasiparticle mass scenario. It is also found that, in the noninteracting case, the Seebeck coefficient possesses a slightly negative value in the high temperature region, unlike the quasiparticle description, where the Seebeck coefficient remains positive for the entire temperature range.

## Key claim

The Seebeck coefficient of the QGP medium is conspicuously decreased in the novel relaxation time approximation model compared to the standard RTA model, indicating a smaller magnitude of induced electric field; however, the rate of decline becomes gradually smaller as the medium gets hotter for both the current quark mass scenario and the quasiparticle mass scenario.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation using a novel collision integral within the relaxation time approximation that preserves macroscopic conservation laws, unlike the standard RTA. They derived the Seebeck coefficient by setting the induced electric current density to zero in the presence of a small temperature gradient, computing the ratio of integrals \(Q_2/Q_1\) for each quark flavor (u, d, s) and for the combined partonic medium. The calculation was performed for both current quark masses (3 MeV for up, 5 MeV for down, 100 MeV for strange) and for quasiparticle masses incorporating thermal masses from hard thermal loop perturbation theory, with a momentum-dependent relaxation time \(\tau_{fp} \propto (\beta \omega_f)^{0.5}\).

## Key numbers

- Temperature range explored: 0.16 GeV to 0.64 GeV for quark chemical potential values of 0.04 GeV, 0.06 GeV, and 0.08 GeV.
- Current quark masses used: 3 MeV for up quark, 5 MeV for down quark, and 100 MeV for strange quark.
- The QCD running coupling constant uses \(\Lambda_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}} = 0.176\) GeV and \(\Lambda = 2\pi\sqrt{T^2 + \mu_f^2/\pi^2}\) for quarks.
- The momentum-independent relaxation time is given by \(\tau_f = 1 / [5.1 T \alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s) (1 + 0.12(2N_f+1))]\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the Seebeck effect in hot QCD matter, which had been investigated in the hadron resonance gas model by Bhatt (2018) and in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Abhishek (2020), as well as in magnetized QGP by Kurian (2021), Zhang (2020), Dey (2020, 2021), and Khan (2022). The novel RTA collision integral, developed by Rocha (2021) to resolve conservation law violations in the standard Anderson-Witting RTA, was previously applied to electrical and thermal transport by Rath (2024) and Shaikh (2024). The present results enable more accurate predictions of thermoelectric fields in heavy-ion collision fireballs and provide a benchmark for comparing kinetic theory approaches with lattice QCD calculations of transport coefficients.
