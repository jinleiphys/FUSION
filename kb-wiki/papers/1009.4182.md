# Suprathermal viscosity of dense matter

> Motivated by the existence of unstable modes of compact stars that eventually grow large, we study the bulk viscosity of dense matter, taking into account non-linear effects arising in the large amplitude regime, where the deviation mu_Delta of the chemical potentials from chemical equilibrium fulfills mu_Delta > T. We find that this supra-thermal bulk viscosity can provide a potential mechanism for saturating unstable modes in compact stars since the viscosity is strongly enhanced. Our study confirms previous results on strange quark matter and shows that the suprathermal enhancement is even stronger in the case of hadronic matter. We also comment on the competition of different weak channels and the presence of suprathermal effects in various color superconducting phases of dense quark matter.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of dense matter is strongly enhanced in the "supra-thermal" regime where the chemical potential deviation from equilibrium exceeds the temperature, and this enhancement could provide a saturation mechanism for unstable r-mode oscillations in neutron stars, stopping their growth at amplitudes below the threshold for other competing mechanisms.

## Method

The authors derive a general formalism for bulk viscosity under oscillating compression, solving the differential equation for the chemical potential deviation driven by density oscillations and damped by weak equilibration processes. They apply this formalism to both hadronic matter (using direct and modified Urca processes) and strange quark matter (using non-leptonic flavor-changing processes), computing the viscosity as a function of oscillation amplitude across a wide range of temperatures and densities. The analysis uses analytic approximations in the sub-thermal and intermediate regimes, supplemented by numerical solutions for the supra-thermal regime where non-linear terms dominate.

## Key numbers

- For hadronic matter with modified Urca, the weak rate parameter is $\tilde{\Gamma} = 4.68 \times 10^{-19} \, (x n / n_0)^{1/3}$ MeV$^{-3}$ with $\kappa = 6$
- For hadronic matter with direct Urca, $\tilde{\Gamma} = 5.24 \times 10^{-15} \, (x n / n_0)^{1/3}$ MeV$^{-1}$ with $\kappa = 4$
- For strange quark matter with non-leptonic reactions, $\tilde{\Gamma} = 6.59 \times 10^{-12} \, (\mu_q / 300 \text{ MeV})^5$ MeV$^{1}$ with $\kappa = 2$
- The maximum sub-thermal bulk viscosity is $\zeta_{max}^{<} = C^2 / (2\omega B)$, reached at temperature $T_{max} = (\omega / (\tilde{\Gamma} B))^{1/\kappa}$
- The supra-thermal viscosity scales as $\zeta \sim (\Delta n_* / \bar{n}_*)^{-2N/(2N+1)}$ at very large amplitudes, where $N = \kappa/2$
- For a frequency of $\omega = 8.4$ kHz and baryon density $\bar{n} = 2 n_0$, the maximum viscosity is reached at amplitudes of order $0.01$, $0.1$, and $1$ for strange quark matter, hadronic matter with direct Urca, and hadronic matter with modified Urca respectively

## Context

This work extends previous results by Madsen (1992) on the amplitude dependence of bulk viscosity in strange quark matter, showing that the supra-thermal enhancement is parametrically even stronger in nuclear matter. The results directly apply to the damping of unstable r-mode oscillations in neutron stars, where the viscosity could provide a saturation mechanism before other non-linear dynamic effects (Lindblom 2000, Gressman 2002, Lin 2004, Bondarescu 2007) become important. The formalism also extends to partly gapped phases like 2SC and 1SC color-superconducting quark matter, while in the fully gapped CFL phase the suprathermal enhancement is expected to be mild.
