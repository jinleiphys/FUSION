# Effects of a weakly interacting light U boson on the nuclear equation of state and properties of neutron stars in relativistic models

> We investigate the effects of the light vector U-boson that couples weakly to nucleons in relativistic mean-field models on the equation of state and subsequently the consequence in neutron stars. It is analyzed that the U-boson can lead to a much clearer rise of the neutron star maximum mass in models with the much softer equation of state. The inclusion of the U-boson may thus allow the existence of the non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the interior of large mass neutron stars initiated with the favorably soft EOS of normal nuclear matter. In addition, the sensitive role of the U-boson in the neutron star radius and its relation to the test of the non-Newtonian gravity that is herein addressed by the light U-boson are discussed.

## Key claim

The light vector U-boson, which couples weakly to nucleons, can significantly increase the maximum mass of neutron stars in relativistic mean-field models that have a much softer equation of state, potentially allowing non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the interior of large-mass neutron stars; this effect is most pronounced in models where the cancellation between repulsive and attractive forces is already substantial at high densities.

## Method

The authors incorporated a light vector U-boson field into the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian, adding a Yukawa-type repulsive contribution to the nuclear equation of state. They solved the mean-field equations self-consistently for npe matter at beta equilibrium using five standard RMF parameter sets (NL1, NL-SH, NL3, TM1, FSUGold) and two modified sets with softened symmetry energy (NL3Λ_V, TM1Λ_V). Neutron star masses and radii were then obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations with the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The ratio parameter \((g_u/m_u)^2\) was varied in the range \(0 \sim 100\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
- For the FSUGold model without the U-boson, the maximum neutron star mass is about \(1.7 M_\odot\); with hyperonization this can drop below \(1.4 M_\odot\).
- The observed neutron star J1614-2230 has a mass of \(2 M_\odot\) (measured via Shapiro delay).
- The LMXB 4U1608-52 has a measured mass of \(1.74 M_\odot\).
- The saturation density \(\rho_0\) for all models is in the range \(0.145-0.153\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The symmetry energy at saturation ranges from \(31.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (NL3Λ_V) to \(43.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (NL1).
- The incompressibility \(\kappa\) ranges from \(211.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (NL1) to \(355.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (NL-SH).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic study of the U-boson effect on neutron stars by Wen (2009) to the relativistic mean-field framework, using established RMF parameter sets such as NL3 (Lalazissis, 1997), TM1 (Sugahara, 1994), and FSUGold (Piekarewicz, 2005). The results show that the U-boson can reconcile soft equations of state (including those with super-soft symmetry energy from the FOPI/GSI data analysis by Xiao, 2009) with the existence of massive neutron stars, and the sensitive dependence of neutron star radius on the U-boson coupling may provide a test of non-Newtonian gravity through future precise mass-radius measurements.
