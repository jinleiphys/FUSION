# Helicity Evolution at Small $x$: Flavor Singlet and Non-Singlet Observables

> We extend our earlier results for the quark helicity evolution at small $x$ to derive the small-$x$ asymptotics of the flavor singlet and flavor non-singlet quark helicity TMDs and PDFs and of the $g_1$ structure function. In the flavor singlet case we re-derive the evolution equations obtained in our previous paper on the subject, performing additional cross-checks of our results. In the flavor non-singlet case we construct new small-$x$ evolution equations by employing the large-$N_c$ limit. All evolution equations resum double-logarithmic powers of $α_s \, \ln^2 (1/x)$ in the polarization-dependent evolution along with the single-logarithmic powers of $α_s \, \ln (1/x)$ in the unpolarized evolution which includes saturation effects. We solve the linearized flavor non-singlet equation analytically, obtaining an intercept which agrees with the one calculated earlier by Bartels, Ermolaev and Ryskin using the infra-red evolution equations. Our numerical solution of the linearized large-$N_c$ evolution equations for the flavor singlet case is presented in the accompanying Letter and is further discussed here.

## Key claim

The paper extends the authors' previous quark helicity evolution at small $x$ to derive the small-$x$ asymptotics of flavor singlet and flavor non-singlet quark helicity transverse momentum distributions (TMDs), parton distribution functions (PDFs), and the $g_1$ structure function, with the caveat that the flavor non-singlet case is constructed using the large-$N_c$ limit and the flavor singlet case re-derives earlier equations with additional cross-checks.

## Method

The authors construct small-$x$ evolution equations that resum double-logarithmic powers of $\alpha_s \ln^2 (1/x)$ for polarization-dependent evolution alongside single-logarithmic powers of $\alpha_s \ln (1/x)$ for unpolarized evolution including saturation effects. They relate helicity observables to a "polarized dipole amplitude" defined through Wilson line operators, derive evolution equations in the large-$N_c$ limit, and solve the linearized flavor non-singlet equation analytically while solving the flavor singlet case numerically.

## Key numbers

- Flavor singlet helicity intercept: $\alpha_{h} \approx 2.31 \sqrt{\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2 \pi}}$, leading to helicity PDFs scaling as $\Delta q \sim (1/x)^{\alpha_h} \sim (1/x)^{0.936}$ for $N_f = 0$ and $Q^2 = 3$ GeV$^2$
- For comparison, the earlier Bartels-Ermolaev-Ryskin (BER) result for the flavor singlet case with $N_f = 0$ and $\alpha_s (Q^2) = 0.343$ at $Q^2 = 3$ GeV$^2$ gave helicity PDF growth as $(1/x)^{1.481}$, approximately 35% larger than the authors' result
- Leading-order fixed-coupling BFKL evolution with the same parameters yields unpolarized PDF growth as $(1/x)^{1.908}$
- The flavor non-singlet intercept agrees with the earlier calculation by Bartels, Ermolaev and Ryskin (1995)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous helicity evolution results (Kovchegov, Pitonyak, Sievert 2015) to include both flavor singlet and flavor non-singlet observables, and provides detailed cross-checks of their equations. The paper reproduces the flavor non-singlet intercept obtained earlier by Bartels, Ermolaev and Ryskin (1995) using infrared evolution equations, while finding a significant discrepancy with the same authors' flavor singlet result (Bartels, Ermolaev, Ryskin 1996). The results enable assessment of the small-$x$ contribution to the proton spin puzzle and suggest that saturation effects may not be required to regulate the small-$x$ divergence of helicity PDFs, contrary to earlier expectations.
