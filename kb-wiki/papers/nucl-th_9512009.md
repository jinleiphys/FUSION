# 3D Solution of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equations for Drip-Line Nuclei

> We investigate the possibility of describing triaxial quadrupole deformations for nuclei close to the two-neutron drip line by the Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov method taking into account resonances in the continuum. We use a Skyrme interaction to describe the Hartee-Fock hamiltonian and a density-dependent zero-range interaction to evaluate the pairing field. The mean-field equations are solved in a three-dimensional cubic mesh. We study the stability of the two-neutron separation energies and of the description of the nuclear surface as a function of the number of active mean-field orbitals and of the size of the mesh. The even Ni isotopes are used as a test case and the accuracy as a function of quadrupole deformation is studied by performing constrained calculations. A first application to the study of the two-neutron separation energies in Ni isotopes up to the drip line is presented.

# 3D Solution of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equations for Drip-Line Nuclei

## Key claim
The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method solved in a three-dimensional cubic mesh can describe triaxial quadrupole deformations for nuclei near the two-neutron drip line, provided the continuum is discretized and resonances are properly separated from continuum states. The method achieves convergence for two-neutron separation energies to better than 100 keV when the box size and number of basis states are appropriately chosen, but the analysis is limited to an active pairing space extending only 5 MeV above the Fermi surface.

## Method
The HFB equations were solved by discretization in a three-dimensional cubic mesh using the two-basis method, with the imaginary time-step algorithm to determine eigenstates of the mean-field Hamiltonian. The particle-hole channel used the Skyrme SIII interaction, while the particle-particle channel used a zero-range density-dependent pairing force with surface-peaked character (V0 = 1128.75 MeV fm^3, rho_c = 0.13354 fm^-3). The pairing active space extended 5 MeV above the Fermi surface, and the calculations imposed three planes of symmetry for the nuclear density, storing wave-functions in one spatial octant.

## Key numbers
- The neutron Fermi energy of 84Ni was -0.98 MeV, close to zero.
- The energy of the 3s1/2 particle state decreased by 400 keV as the box size varied from 11 to 15 fm in HF calculations.
- For 84Ni, the R-converged total energy was determined with accuracy better than 100 keV when the box size was 12 fm with at least 60 basis states.
- The two-neutron separation energy for 84Ni could be calculated with precision better than 100 keV for R = 12 fm with at least 60 states.
- For 92Ni, total energy errors were estimated at 100 to 200 keV with 70 wave-functions.
- Decreasing the mesh size Delta x from 1 fm to 0.75 fm lowered S2n by less than 50 keV across the entire Ni isotope series.
- The two-neutron drip line for Ni isotopes was predicted at A = 94 (N = 66, 92Ni).

## Context
This work extends the HFB method developed for rotating nuclei (GBD94, THB95) to neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, addressing the problem that HF+BCS methods fail due to nucleon leakage into the continuum. It builds on spherical HFB studies of drip-line nuclei (DNW95) and on macroscopic-microscopic predictions for exotic nuclei (MN88, MNM95, YPD92, BCD94). The method enables future investigation of whether quadrupole deformation persists along the drip line and allows study of effective interactions relevant for the astrophysical r-process.
