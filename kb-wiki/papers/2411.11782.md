# Energy-Energy Correlator for jet production in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions

> In this Letter, we study the collinear limit of the Energy-Energy Correlator (EEC) in single-inclusive jet production in proton-proton ($pp$) and proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions. We introduce a non-perturbative model that allows us to describe the EEC in the entire angular region of the current experiments. Our results for $pp$ collisions show excellent agreement with CMS and ALICE data over a wide range of jet transverse momenta. For $pA$ collisions, we include modifications from the nuclear medium, and our predictions align well with the trends observed in recent ALICE measurements.

# Energy-Energy Correlator for jet production in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions

## Key claim
The authors present a theoretical framework that describes the Energy-Energy Correlator (EEC) in the collinear limit for both proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions across the entire angular region, including a non-perturbative model. The agreement with experimental data depends on fitting a free parameter $a_0$ separately for CMS and ALICE data, with the difference attributed to experimental definitions of jet transverse momentum (charged vs. inclusive jets).

## Method
The authors factorize the EEC distribution into a hard function (from collinear factorization with parton distribution functions) and an EEC jet function that incorporates collinear fragmentation. They compute the perturbative jet function at leading-logarithmic (LL) accuracy using renormalization group evolution, and model non-perturbative effects using a TMD-inspired Sudakov factor $j_\text{np}(b) = \exp(-a_0 b)$ in position space with a $b_*$-prescription. For proton-nucleus collisions, they replace one proton PDF with a nuclear PDF and add medium-induced modifications to both the perturbative jet function (via a higher-twist correction $F_\text{med}$ depending on jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ and path length $L$) and the non-perturbative sector (adding a $b^2$ term $\exp(-a_1 b^2)$ for diffusive broadening).

## Key numbers
- Non-perturbative parameter $a_0 = \SI{3.8}{GeV}$ for CMS data and $a_0 = \SI{2.5}{GeV}$ for ALICE data, fitted to experimental data; rescaling the ALICE value by $3/2$ yields $a_0 \approx \SI{3.75}{GeV}$, consistent with CMS
- $K$-factor of $K \approx 0.90$ for CMS and $K \approx 0.75$ for ALICE to account for higher-order corrections beyond LL
- Jet quenching parameter in cold nuclear matter $\hat q = \SI{0.02}{GeV^2/fm}$ with path length $L = \SI{3}{fm}$
- Medium-dependent non-perturbative parameter $a_1 = \SI{0.25}{GeV^2}$
- Jet radius $R = 0.4$ used for evolution, consistent with experimental data
- $b_*$-prescription with $b_\text{max} = 2e^{-\gamma_E} \, \si{GeV^{-1}}$
- ALICE experimental fit for $p$Pb to $pp$ ratio: $R^\text{ALICE}_{p\text{Pb}} = 0.87 + 0.121(\log_{10}(R_L) + 1.831)^2$

## Context
This work extends the study of Energy-Energy Correlators from vacuum $pp$ collisions (where previous work by Dixon (2019), Lee (2022), and Komiske (2022) established perturbative and non-perturbative behavior) to proton-nucleus collisions with cold nuclear matter. It builds on TMD factorization approaches by Kang (2024) and Liu (2024) for the non-perturbative Sudakov factor, and on medium modification calculations by Barata (2023), Yang (2023), and Andres (2022) for EECs in nuclear environments. The framework enables future studies of medium-induced jet modifications in $pA$ and potentially $AA$ collisions, and provides a foundation for extracting universal non-perturbative fragmentation effects across different experimental systems.
