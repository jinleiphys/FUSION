# Hadronic description for Omega baryon photoproduction

> In the present work, we investigate subsequential production of three kaons and $Ω^-$ baryon based on an effective Lagrangian approach. We only consider the intermediate states with the light mass baryon to suggest the minimum of the total cross section. Coupling constants for verteces of meson-octet baryons are fixed from the empirical data and/or quark models together with SU(3) symmetry considerations and these for meson-decouplet are predicted not only quark model but also Chiral-quark soliton model calculation. Gauge invariance of the resulting amplitude is maintained by introducing the contact currents by extending the gauge-invariant approach of Haberzettl for one-meson photoproduction to two-meson photoproduction.

## Key claim

The authors estimate the minimum total cross section for the photoproduction reaction \(\gamma p \to K^+ K^+ K^0 \Omega^-\) by considering only the lightest-mass hyperon intermediate states, acknowledging that this approach provides a lower bound rather than a precise prediction.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach with sequential hadronic production of three kaons and an Omega baryon, extending the gauge-invariant formalism of Haberzettl from one-meson to two-meson photoproduction by introducing contact currents. Coupling constants for meson-octet baryon vertices are fixed from empirical data and/or quark models with SU(3) symmetry, while meson-decouplet couplings are predicted using both the quark model and the Chiral-quark soliton model. The calculation includes 24 diagrams corresponding to photon couplings at 8 positions (excluding the neutral kaon line) across three possible kaon orderings.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for \(\gamma p \to K^+ K^+ K^0 \Omega^-\) is shown as a function of photon energy up to 20 GeV (Figure 2 in the paper).
- The calculation uses parameters from Nakayama (2006) for baryon octet and from Yang (2013) and Kim (private communication) for baryon decuplet.

## Context

This work extends the cascade physics program initiated by the CLAS Collaboration at Jefferson Lab, which established the feasibility of Omega baryon spectroscopy via photoproduction reactions such as \(\gamma p \to K^+ K^+ K^0 \Omega^-\) (Strakovsky proposal). The study provides a baseline minimum cross section estimate for the ongoing dedicated experiment, and the authors plan to incorporate contributions from high-spin hyperon resonances in future work.
