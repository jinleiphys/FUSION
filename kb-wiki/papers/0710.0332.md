# (3+1)-Dimensional Hydrodynamic Expansion with a Critical Point from Realistic Initial Conditions

> We investigate a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion of the hot and dense system created in head-on collisions of Pb+Pb/Au+Au at beam energies from $5-200A$ GeV. An equation of state that incorporates a critical end point (CEP) in line with the lattice data is used. The necessary initial conditions for the hydrodynamic evolution are taken from a microscopic transport approach (UrQMD). We compare the properties of the initial state and the full hydrodynamical calculation with an isentropic expansion employing an initial state from a simple overlap model. We find that the specific entropy ($S/A$) from both initial conditions is very similar and only depends on the underlying equation of state. Using the chiral (hadronic) equation of state we investigate the expansion paths for both initial conditions. Defining a critical area around the critical point, we show at what beam energies one can expect to have a sizable fraction of the system close to the critical point. Finally, we emphasise the importance of the equation of state of strongly interacting matter, in the (experimental) search for the CEP.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the specific entropy (S/A) from both UrQMD microscopic initial conditions and a simple geometric overlap model is very similar and depends only on the underlying equation of state, but the beam energy needed to reach the critical end point (CEP) depends strongly on the chosen equation of state, contradicting estimates from a free hadron gas model.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model to generate initial energy- and baryon-density distributions for head-on Pb+Pb/Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 5 to 200 A GeV, then evolved these distributions with a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model (SHASTA algorithm) using a chiral hadronic SU(3) equation of state that includes a critical end point. They compared these full hydrodynamic evolutions with isentropic expansion paths from a simple Lorentz-contracted overlap model initial condition, and defined a critical volume as cells within T_CEP +/- 10 MeV and mu_CEP +/- 10 MeV to quantify the fraction of the system near the critical point.

## Key numbers

* Beam energy range studied: 5-200 A GeV for Pb+Pb/Au+Au head-on collisions (impact parameter b=0 fm)
* Gaussian smearing width for mapping UrQMD particles to the hydrodynamic grid: sigma = 1 fm
* Hydrodynamic grid cell size: (0.2 fm)^3
* Critical volume of approximately 200 fm^3 predicted at E_lab = 60 A GeV for the chiral EoS critical point
* For the chiral EoS with completed resonance spectrum, beam energy of 40-60 A GeV is sufficient to reach the CEP, compared to 160 A GeV without heavy resonances and 40 A GeV for a free hadron gas
* Equation of state tabulated with step sizes Delta epsilon = 0.1 and Delta n = 0.05 in units of nuclear ground state densities (epsilon_0 = 138.5 MeV, n_0 = 0.15 fm^-3)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hydrodynamic expansion with critical points by using realistic initial conditions from the UrQMD transport model rather than simple geometric overlap assumptions. The results contrast with findings by Nonaka (2004) and Asakawa (2005) who observed focusing of hydrodynamic trajectories toward the critical end point, which the present authors do not observe. The paper supports the main statement from Arsene (2006) that the phase boundary to QGP/chiral restoration might be reached already at moderate beam energies of 5-10 A GeV, but emphasizes that the beam energy required to actually reach the critical end point depends strongly on the equation of state, with implications for experimental searches at facilities like the SPS and future FAIR.
