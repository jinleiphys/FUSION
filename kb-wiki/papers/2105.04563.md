# Improved Standard-Model prediction for $π^0\to e^+e^-$

> We present an improved Standard-Model (SM) prediction for the dilepton decay of the neutral pion. The loop amplitude is determined by the pion transition form factor for $π^0\toγ^*γ^*$, for which we employ a dispersive representation that incorporates both space-like and time-like data as well as short-distance constraints. The resulting SM branching fraction, $\text{BR}[π^0\to e^+e^-]=6.25(3)\times 10^{-8}$ , sharpens constraints on physics beyond the SM, including pseudoscalar and axial-vector mediators.

# Improved Standard-Model prediction for $\pi^0\to e^+e^-$

## Key claim

The Standard-Model branching fraction for $\pi^0\to e^+e^-$ is predicted to be $6.25(3)\times 10^{-8}$, with a precision of $0.5\%$, based on a dispersive representation of the pion transition form factor that incorporates both space-like and time-like data as well as short-distance constraints; this result is in a mild $1.8\sigma$ tension with the KTeV measurement of $6.85(27)(23)\times 10^{-8}$.

## Method

The loop amplitude for $\pi^0\to e^+e^-$ is determined by the pion transition form factor $F_{\pi^0\gamma^*\gamma^*}(q_1^2,q_2^2)$, which is decomposed into three contributions: a dispersive term accounting for low-energy singularities extracted from $e^+e^-\to 2\pi,3\pi$ data via Khuri-Treiman equations; an effective-pole term parameterizing higher intermediate states and constrained by space-like data at $Q^2 > 5\ \text{GeV}^2$; and an asymptotic term implementing short-distance constraints from perturbative QCD. The loop integral is evaluated using a double-spectral representation that enables reduction to standard loop functions, with numerical stability verified through multiple independent methods including LoopTools and semi-analytic expressions in terms of polylogarithms.

## Key numbers

* Branching fraction: $\text{BR}[\pi^0\to e^+e^-] = 6.25(3)\times 10^{-8}$ (Standard Model prediction, $0.5\%$ precision)
* Real part of reduced amplitude: $\Re\mathcal{A}(q^2)|_\text{SM} = 10.11(10)$
* KTeV measurement (with radiative corrections): $\text{BR}[\pi^0\to e^+e^-]|_\text{KTeV} = 6.85(27)(23)\times 10^{-8}$
* Unitarity bound: $\text{BR}[\pi^0\to e^+e^-] > 4.69\times 10^{-8}$
* Imaginary part from $\gamma\gamma$ cut: $\Im\mathcal{A}(q^2) = -17.52$
* Low-energy constant: $\chi^{(\text{r})}(\mu=0.77\ \text{GeV}) = 2.69(10)$
* $Z$-boson exchange contribution: $\Re\mathcal{A}(q^2)|_Z = -0.05(0)$
* Axial-vector coupling constraint: $C_A = (-280)^{+160}_{-150}\ \text{TeV}^{-2}$
* Pseudoscalar coupling constraint: $C_P = (-0.108)^{+0.062}_{-0.057}\ \text{TeV}^{-2}$ (at $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scale $\mu=2\ \text{GeV}$)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to the pion transition form factor previously developed for hadronic light-by-light scattering by Hoferichter et al. (2014, 2018) and for hadronic vacuum polarization by Hoferichter et al. (2019) and Hoid et al. (2020). The prediction improves upon earlier results from Masjuan et al. (2015) using Canterbury approximants ($6.23(5)\times 10^{-8}$) and Weil et al. (2017) using Dyson-Schwinger equations ($6.22(3)\times 10^{-8}$), while providing a fully data-driven uncertainty estimate. The result enables sharper constraints on pseudoscalar and axial-vector mediators beyond the Standard Model, including light $Z'$ bosons and axion-like particles, and can be compared with the anomalous magnetic moment of the electron measured with Cs atom interferometry by Parker et al. (2018).
