# Self consistent and covariant propagation of pions, nucleon and isobar resonances in cold nuclear matter

> We evaluate the in-medium spectral functions for pions, nucleon and isobar resonances in a self consistent and covariant manner. The calculations are based on a recently developed formulation which leads to predictions in terms of the pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts and a set of Migdal parameters describing important short range correlation effects. We do not observe significant softening of pion modes if we insist on reasonable isobar resonance properties but predict a considerable broadening of the N(1440) and N(1520) resonances in nuclear matter. Contrasted results are obtained for the s-wave N(1535) and N(1650) resonances which are affected by a nuclear environment very little. The properties of slowly moving isobar's in nuclear matter are found to depend very sensitively on a soft form factor in the piNN vertex, which is not controlled by the piN scattering data.

# Self consistent and covariant propagation of pions, nucleon and isobar resonances in cold nuclear matter

## Key claim

The authors do not observe significant softening of pion modes in nuclear matter if they insist on reasonable isobar resonance properties, but they predict a considerable broadening of the N(1440) and N(1520) resonances; the properties of slowly moving isobars depend very sensitively on a soft form factor in the piNN vertex, which is not controlled by piN scattering data.

## Method

The authors generalize a covariant framework previously developed for antikaon and hyperon-resonance propagation to the case of pions, nucleons, and isobar resonances, incorporating short-range correlation effects via Migdal parameters. The in-medium Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved self-consistently using free-space pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts (from the SM01 partial-wave analysis) and a set of Migdal parameters (g'_{11}, g'_{12}, g'_{22}) to describe short-range correlations, with the pion self energy reconstructed via a subtracted dispersion-integral representation. The scheme includes s-wave, p-wave, and d-wave channels for both nucleon and isobar resonances, and the self-consistent equations are solved numerically by iteration, typically converging after 5 to 6 iterations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density set to rho = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- For Migdal parameter set I (g'_{11}=g'_{12}=g'_{22}=0.8), the low-momentum pion self energy parameters are (alpha, beta) approximately (1.09 - i 0.05, 0.56 - i 0.25)
- For Migdal parameter set II (g'_{11}=0.585, g'_{12}=0.15, g'_{22}=0.60), the low-momentum pion self energy parameters are (alpha, beta) approximately (1.06 - i 0.07, 0.74 - i 0.44)
- At 0.5 rho_0 with set I, the parameters are (alpha, beta) approximately (1.09 - i 0.02, 0.46 - i 0.11)
- The attractive mass shift for the Delta(1232) isobar is about 80 MeV
- The function S(q) at |q| = 300 MeV is approximately 3.4 m_pi^2 for set I and 2.4 m_pi^2 for set II
- The sum rule for the pion spectral function holds within 1 percent
- The dispersion integral cutoff is lambda = 1.2 GeV
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is g_{piNN} = 13.34
- Nucleon and pion masses used are m_N = 939 MeV and m_pi = 139 MeV

## Context

This work extends a covariant framework previously developed for antikaons and hyperon-resonances (Lutz and Korpa, 2002) to the problem of pion, nucleon, and isobar resonance propagation in nuclear matter. It constitutes significant progress compared to earlier self-consistent calculations by Xia, Siemens, and Soyeur (1990s), Korpa and Malfliet (1990s), and Knoll (1990s), which were based only on p-wave pion-nucleon dynamics. The results confirm qualitatively the narrowing of the isobar resonance with increasing density due to Pauli blocking seen in earlier work by Oset and Salcedo (1980s), but differ from those earlier self-consistent works regarding the broadening of the isobar, which the authors attribute to the soft form factor used in those previous studies. The significant broadening of the N(1520) resonance is in qualitative agreement with phenomenological works by Kondratyuk (1990s), Alberico (1990s), Chen (1990s), and Boffi (1990s), and may help provide a microscopic understanding of photoabsorption data in the second resonance region. The approach enables future inclusion of in-medium modification of inelastic processes such as pi-Delta, rho-N, and omega-N channels.
