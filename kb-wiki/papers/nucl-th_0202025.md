# Polarization effects in elastic electron-proton scattering

> In these notes we present a detailed and complete scheme for the calculation of polarization phenomena in elastic electron-nucleon scattering. The longitudinal and transversal components of the polarization for the scattered nucleon, induced by the scattering of longitudinally polarized electrons and the asymmetries for the collision of polarized electrons with a polarized nucleon target are included. The calculations are done in the framework of the one-photon mechanism, in the Breit system, which is the most convenient for the analysis of polarization effects. We briefly discuss the region of applicability of such model and other mechanisms (radiative and electroweak corrections) which may come into this idealized picture.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete and detailed scheme for calculating polarization observables in elastic electron-nucleon scattering within the one-photon-exchange approximation, explicitly noting that radiative corrections, electroweak corrections (due to Z⁰-boson exchange), and effects from electron mass are neglected throughout the derivation.

## Method

The authors derive all polarization observables for elastic eN-scattering using the Breit reference frame, where the nucleon electromagnetic current simplifies and the Sachs form factors G_EN and G_MN appear naturally. They compute the hadronic and leptonic tensors explicitly in the Breit system, then transform the results to the laboratory frame to obtain expressions for the differential cross section (the Rosenbluth formula) and for two-spin polarization observables: the polarization of the final nucleon from longitudinally polarized electrons (e + N → e + N̅) and the asymmetries from scattering longitudinally polarized electrons on a polarized nucleon target (e̅ + N̅ → e + N). The formalism is developed for Dirac spinors with relativistic invariant normalization, using the standard representation of gamma matrices.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless variable τ = -q²/(4m²) ≥ 0 is introduced, where m is the nucleon mass and q² is the four-momentum transfer squared (negative in the space-like region).
- The relation between the electron scattering angle in the Lab system (θ_e) and in the Breit system (θ_B) is given by: cot²(θ_B/2) = cot²(θ_e/2) / (1 + τ).
- The Rosenbluth formula for the differential cross section in the Lab system is: dσ/dΩ_e = (α²/(-q²)) (ε_d/ε_u)² [2τ G_MN² + (cot²(θ_e/2)/(1+τ)) (G_EN² + τ G_MN²)], where α ≈ 1/137 is the fine structure constant.
- The Mott cross section σ_M is given by: σ_M = (α/(2ε_u))² cos²(θ_e/2) / sin⁴(θ_e/2) × 1/(1 + 2(ε_u/m) sin²(θ_e/2)).
- The ratio of proton magnetic to electric form factors satisfies G_Mp/G_Ep ≈ μ_p = 2.79, so G_Mp²/G_Ep² ≈ 8.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of polarization phenomena in elastic electron-nucleon scattering originally developed by Akhiezer (1958), Bjorken (1979), and earlier authors such as Rekalo (1968, 1974) and Dombey (1969). The paper provides a unified treatment in the Breit system that enables precise determination of the electric form factor G_EN at large momentum transfer, where the unpolarized Rosenbluth separation becomes insensitive to G_EN. The method has been successfully applied in experiments at Bates (Milbrath, 1998) and Jefferson Lab (Jones, 2000) for the proton, and in deuteron experiments for the neutron (references given as Gen1 and Gen2 in the text). The formalism also applies to inverse kinematics for relativistic proton beams scattering on atomic electrons, relevant for polarizing antiproton beams in storage rings.
