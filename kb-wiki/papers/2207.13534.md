# Bayesian Inference of the Specific Shear and Bulk Viscosities of the Quark-Gluon Plasma at Crossover from $ϕ$ and $Ω$ Observables

> Due to their weak final state interactions, the $ϕ$ meson and $Ω$ baryon provide unique probes of the properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using the quark recombination model with the quark phase-space information parameterized in a viscous blastwave, we study the transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flows of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. The viscous blastwave includes non-equilibrium deformations of thermal distributions due to shear and bulk stresses and thus carries information on the specific shear viscosity $η/s$ and the specific bulk viscosity $ζ/s$ of the QGP. We perform a model-to-data comparison with Bayesian inference and simultaneously obtain $η/s=(2.08^{+1.10}_{-1.09})/4π$ and $ζ/s= 0.06^{+0.04}_{-0.04}$ at $90\%$ C.L. for the baryon-free QGP at crossover temperature of about $160$ MeV. Our work provides a novel approach to simultaneously determine the $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ of the QGP at hadronization.

## Key claim

Using the quark recombination model with a viscous blastwave parameterization, the authors simultaneously constrain the specific shear viscosity η/s and specific bulk viscosity ζ/s of the quark-gluon plasma at the crossover temperature, obtaining η/s = (2.08^{+1.10}_{-1.09})/4π and ζ/s = 0.06^{+0.04}_{-0.04} at 90% confidence level, but these values apply specifically to the baryon-free QGP at hadronization and rely on the assumption that φ mesons and Ω baryons have negligible final-state hadronic interactions.

## Method

The authors used the quark recombination model to describe hadron formation from quarks, with the quark phase-space distribution parameterized by a viscous blastwave that includes non-equilibrium corrections from shear and bulk stresses. They performed a Bayesian model-to-data comparison using the MADAI package, fitting the transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flows v2 of φ and Ω in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV (STAR data) and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (ALICE data) across multiple centrality bins. The model had 21 free parameters including temperature T, flow parameters, and the specific viscosities η/s and ζ/s, with a Gaussian prior on T centered at 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- η/s = (2.08^{+1.10}_{-1.09})/4π at 90% C.L. for baryon-free QGP at crossover temperature ~160 MeV
- ζ/s = 0.06^{+0.04}_{-0.04} at 90% C.L. for the same system
- Recombination temperature T = 159 ± 8 MeV from the posterior distribution
- Using higher pT ranges (pT < 2.3 GeV/c for φ, pT < 3.6 GeV/c for Ω) gave η/s = (2.14 ± 0.44)/4π and ζ/s = 0.04 ± 0.03 at 90% C.L.
- Varying wave function widths by 33% (σM from 0.3 to 0.4, σB from 0.09 to 0.12) changed η/s by -4% and ζ/s by +8%
- The viscous corrections δf were less than 20% of f0 for the majority of phase-space points

## Context

This work provides a novel approach to simultaneously determine η/s and ζ/s using φ and Ω observables, which are considered clean probes due to their weak hadronic interactions. The results are compared with multi-stage methods from Duke (Bernhard 2019), JETSCAPE (2020), and Trajectum (Nijs 2022), as well as lattice QCD calculations (Meyer 2007, 2009; Borsanyi 2018), FRG (Christiansen 2014), T-matrix (Liu 2016), and pQCD (Ghiglieri 2018). The authors note significant discrepancies in ζ/s values across different approaches at Tpc, ranging from ~0.35 (lattice QCD) to ~0.02 (Trajectum), and suggest that future hybrid approaches combining hydrodynamic models with quark recombination could extract the temperature dependence of the viscosities.
