# Quark Distributions in a Medium

> We derive the formal expressions needed to discuss the change of the twist-two parton distribution functions when a hadron is placed in a medium with relativistic scalar and vector mean fields

## Key claim

The paper derives the formal expressions needed to compute how twist-two parton distribution functions change when a hadron is placed in a medium with relativistic scalar and vector mean fields, but the actual numerical application is deferred to a toy model of infinite isospin-symmetric quark matter rather than a realistic nuclear calculation.

## Method

The authors start from the standard handbag diagram expression for quark distributions in a bound proton, then expand the quark field operators in terms of free plane waves and insert complete sets of intermediate states. They introduce scalar and vector mean fields by shifting the quark and antiquark plus-momenta, and relate the bound-quark operators to free-quark operators through overlap integrals of Dirac spinors. The formalism is then applied to infinite quark matter using the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model framework, where the Dirac equation for quarks includes scalar and vector potentials.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear structure functions (divided by the number of constituent nucleons) lie within 10-20% of the free nucleon structure function, except near the kinematic boundary for the free nucleon.
- The maximum value for the in-medium Bjorken variable x* is 1 - 2V⁺, and the minimum value is V⁺, where V⁺ is the plus component of the vector potential.
- The integral of [q^BP(x*) - anti-q^BP(x*)] over the allowed x* range from V⁺ to 1 - 2V⁺ gives the number of valence quarks N^BP_q - N^BP_anti-q in the bound proton state.

## Context

This work extends the formal treatment of parton distribution functions by Jaffe (1983) from the free proton case to a proton embedded in scalar and vector mean fields, which are known to be large in nuclear matter from relativistic studies. The formalism is designed to enable future calculations of nuclear structure function modifications using models like the Quark Meson Coupling model of Guichon (1987) or the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with proper time regularization as used by Mineo (2003) and Bentz (2002). The authors note that much information on nucleon parton distribution functions comes from nuclear data, making this formal development important for interpreting those measurements.
