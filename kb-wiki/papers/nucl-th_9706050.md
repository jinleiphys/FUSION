# Can only flavor-nonsinglet H dibaryons be stable against strong decays?

> Using the QCD sum rule approach, we show that the flavor-nonsinglet $H$ dibaryon states with J$^π = 1^+$, J$^π = 0^+$, I=1 (27plet) are nearly degenerate with the J$^π = 0^+$, I=0 singlet $H_0$ dibaryon, which has been predicted to be stable against strong decay, but has not been observed. Our calculation, which does not require an instanton correction, suggests that the $H_0$ is slightly heavier than these flavor-nonsinglet $H$s over a wide range of the parameter space. If the singlet $H_0$ mass lies above the $ΛΛ$ threshold (2231~MeV), then the strong interaction breakup to $ΛΛ$ would produce a very broad resonance in the $ΛΛ$ invariant mass spectrum which would be very difficult to observe. On the other hand, if these flavor-nonsinglet J=0 and 1 $H$ dibaryons are also above the $ΛΛ$ threshold, but below the $Ξ^0n$ breakup threshold (2254 MeV), then because the direct, strong interaction decay to the $ΛΛ$ channel is forbidden, these flavor-nonsinglet states might be more amenable to experimental observation. The present results allow a possible reconciliation between the reported observation of $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei, which argue against a stable $H_0$, and the possible existence of $H$ dibaryons in general.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules without an instanton correction, the authors find that the flavor-singlet H0 dibaryon is slightly heavier than the flavor-nonsinglet Jπ = 1+, I=0 octet H and Jπ = 0+, I=1 27plet H dibaryons, contrary to the expectation from the MIT bag model that the singlet should be the lightest. If the nonsinglet states lie above the ΛΛ threshold (2231 MeV) but below the Ξ0n threshold (2254 MeV), they may be more observable than the singlet H0 because strong decay to ΛΛ is forbidden for these states.

## Method

The authors formulate QCD sum rules for the H0, the J=1, I=0 octet H, and the J=0, I=1 27plet H dibaryons using baryonic currents in the operator product expansion (OPE). They calculate the mass ratios mH0/mH rather than absolute masses, because the ratio carries smaller uncertainty and is more stable over a wide region of parameter space. The mass ratios are evaluated as functions of the Borel mass M, the quark condensate ⟨q̅q⟩, the continuum threshold s0, and the mixing parameter t, with central values t = -1.2, s0 = 5.694 GeV2, M = 1.5 GeV, and ⟨q̅q⟩ = (-0.250)3 GeV3.

## Key numbers

- The ΛΛ threshold is 2231 MeV; the Ξ0n breakup threshold is 2254 MeV.
- The mass difference between the H0 and the nonsinglet H states is about 0.1%, or approximately 2 MeV for mH ≈ 2 GeV.
- The mass ratio mH0/mH decreases linearly from 1.05 for a Borel mass of 1.3 GeV to 0.99 for a Borel mass of 1.6 GeV when the H0 Borel mass is fixed and the nonsinglet H Borel mass is varied.
- Variation of the quark condensate within a range consistent with the literature produces a mass difference effect of ≤ 2 MeV.
- Sensitivity of the mass ratio to the continuum threshold s0 and the parameter t is about 0.1%.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach previously applied to baryonic decuplet mass splittings by Ioffe, and follows the method of Oka for dibaryon sum rules. The results contradict the MIT bag model prediction by Jaffe that the flavor-singlet H0 should be the lightest dibaryon, and instead support earlier discussions by Oka that the H0 may be heavier. The findings offer a possible reconciliation between the reported observation of ΛΛ hypernuclei by Aoki (which argues against a stable H0) and the possible existence of H dibaryons in general, and could explain candidate nonsinglet H dibaryons reported by Shabazian et al.
