# Neutron star properties with nuclear BHF equations of state

> We study the properties of static and rotating neutron stars adopting non-relativistic equations of state (EOS) for asymmetric nuclear matter based on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) scheme. The BHF calculation, with the continuous choice for the single particle potential, appears to be very close to the full EOS, which includes the three-hole line contribution calculated by solving the Bethe-Fadeev equations within the gap choice for the single particle potential. Three-body forces are included in order to reproduce the correct saturation point for nuclear matter. A comparison with fully relativistic many-body calculations of nuclear matter EOS is made.

## Key claim
The BHF approximation with the continuous choice for the single-particle potential reproduces closely the full equation of state that includes three-hole line contributions from the Bethe-Fadeev equations, but only when three-body forces are added to fix the saturation point; the resulting non-relativistic EOS is softer than the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner (DBHF) EOS, leading to lower predicted neutron star masses and radii.

## Method
The authors solved the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix using the Argonne v14 two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the continuous choice for the single-particle potential. Three-body forces (Urbana model) were added to reproduce the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter. The resulting EOS for asymmetric nuclear matter in beta-equilibrium was used as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static stars and a refined version of Hartle's perturbative method for rotating stars at the Kepler frequency.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of the BHF+TBF EOS: n0 = 0.178 fm^-3, with energy per nucleon E0/A = -16.46 MeV and incompressibility K∞ = 253 MeV.
- Gravitational mass increase due to rotation at the Kepler frequency: up to 14% for BHF+TBF and up to 15% for DBHF, relative to the non-rotating star of the same central density.
- Central energy density ρ0 used as unit: 140 MeV/fm^3.

## Context
This work extends the BHF many-body calculations of Baldo (1991) and Song (previous work) to neutron star structure, including three-hole line contributions and three-body forces. It compares with the relativistic DBHF calculations of Li (1992) using the Bonn-A force, finding that relativistic effects produce a stiffer EOS. The results enable future comparisons with observational pulsar data on fast rotation and neutron star masses, as noted by Lattimer et al. (previous work) regarding the importance of proton fraction for direct Urca cooling processes.
