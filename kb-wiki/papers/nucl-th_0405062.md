# Uncorrelated scattering approximation revisited

> The formalism to describe the scattering of a weakly bound projectile nucleus by a heavy target is investigated, using the Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation. The main assumption involved is to neglect the correlation between the fragments of the projectile in the region where the interaction with the target is important. It is shown that the angular momentum of each fragment with respect to the target is conserved. Moreover, when suitable approximations are assumed, the kinetic energy of each fragment is also shown to be conserved. The S-matrix for the scattering of the composite system can be written as a combination of terms, each one being proportional to the product of the S-matrices of the fragments.

## Key claim

The Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation (USA) assumes that the interaction between the fragments of a weakly bound projectile can be neglected during the collision time, leading to conservation of each fragment's angular momentum and kinetic energy with respect to the target; the validity of the USA depends on the correlation time being long compared to the collision time, rather than on the strength of tidal forces as in the frozen-halo approximation.

## Method

The authors derived an approximate expression for the three-body S-matrix by expanding the T-matrix and neglecting the inter-fragment interaction v_AB in the propagator, replacing it with a renormalized Hamiltonian containing only kinetic energy terms. The resulting S-matrix for the composite system is expressed as a weighted superposition of products of the individual fragment-target S-matrices, evaluated at energies determined by a quadrature over the hyperangular variable β that partitions the total energy between the fragments. The method was applied to deuteron scattering on ⁵⁸Ni at 80 MeV and compared with Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations using the code FRESCO, with fragment-target interactions described by the Becchetti-Greenless global parameterization and the p-n interaction parametrized as a Gaussian.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron scattering on ⁵⁸Ni at E_d = 80 MeV was used as the test case.
- The p-n interaction was parametrized with v_0 = −72.15 MeV and a = 1.484 fm.
- Maximum excitation energies for CDCC partial waves ranged from 40 MeV (s-waves) to 60 MeV (h-waves).
- USA calculations with maximum hyperangular momentum K_m = 30 and K_m = 40 were compared; convergence was achieved at K_m = 40.
- Both CDCC and USA predicted a maximum of the integrated breakup cross section at total angular momentum J = 17.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formulation of the Uncorrelated Scattering Approximation (Moro 2001) by deriving the formalism from a T-matrix approach and providing closed expressions for elastic, inelastic, and breakup S-matrix elements. The method contrasts with the frozen-halo (adiabatic) approximation used by Glauber (1959) and others, which assumes constant relative coordinates during scattering and depends on tidal force strength. The USA enables calculation of three-body scattering observables using only two-body S-matrices, and the application to deuteron scattering on ⁵⁸Ni shows reasonable agreement with CDCC calculations (Aus87, Piy99, Ise86, Yah86) and experimental data from Stephenson (1983), though the USA overestimates breakup cross sections compared to CDCC.
