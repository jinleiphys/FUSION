# Form Invariance, Topological Fluctuations and Mass Gap of Yang-Mills Theory

> In order to have a new perspective on the long-standing problem of the mass gap in Yang-Mills theory, we study the quantum Yang-Mills theory in the presence of topologically nontrivial backgrounds in this paper. The topologically stable gauge fields are constrained by the form invariance condition and the topological properties. Obeying these constraints, the known classical solutions to the Yang-Mills equation in the 3- and 4-dimensional Euclidean spaces are recovered, and the other allowed configurations form the nontrivial topological fluctuations at quantum level. Together, they constitute the background configurations, upon which the quantum Yang-Mills theory can be constructed. We demonstrate that the theory mimics the Higgs mechanism in a certain limit and develops a mass gap at semi-classical level on a flat space with finite size or on a sphere.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the mass gap in pure Yang-Mills theory can emerge at semi-classical level from topological fluctuations (configurations that satisfy form invariance and topological boundary conditions but do not necessarily satisfy the classical field equations), mimicking the Higgs mechanism in a certain limit, but only on a flat space with finite size or on a sphere.

## Method

The authors impose a form invariance condition (Eq. 1.4) requiring that under constant Lorentz transformations the gauge field is invariant up to a constant gauge transformation, and combine this with topological constraints from Chern-Simons (3D) or second Chern number (4D) boundary conditions to construct Ansätze for topologically stable SU(2) gauge fields. They then solve the Yang-Mills equation for these Ansätze to recover known classical solutions (Wu-Yang monopole, instanton, meron, etc.), and subsequently treat the remaining allowed configurations as topological fluctuations around those solutions. Using Feynman's approximation and averaging over gauge orientations, they derive an effective theory and compute the two-point correlation function of gauge-invariant operators at semi-classical level, following Polyakov's approach.

## Key numbers

* 3D Wu-Yang monopole solution: p = 1/2 (constant), with boundary values p(τ=0) = 1/2 and p(τ=∞) = 1/2, winding number B = 0.
* 4D meron solution: p = 1/2 (constant), with boundary values p(τ=0) = 1/2 and p(τ=∞) = 1/2, winding number k = 0.
* 4D 1-instanton solution (regular gauge): p = τ/(τ + c₁), with boundary values p(τ=0) = 0 and p(τ=∞) = 1, winding number k = 1, where c₁ is a positive real constant.
* 4D 1-anti-instanton solution (singular gauge): p = c₁/(τ + c₂), with boundary values p(τ=0) = 1 and p(τ=∞) = 0, winding number k = -1, where c₁ and c₂ are positive real constants.
* 4D pure gauge solution: p = 1 (constant), winding number k = 0.
* 4D trivial vacuum solution: p = 0 (constant), winding number k = 0.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to explain the mass gap in pure Yang-Mills theory (the Mass Gap problem highlighted by the Clay Institute) by introducing a new background formalism that includes topological fluctuations not required to satisfy the field equation, unlike prior approaches that only expanded around classical solutions. The authors connect their results to lattice calculations (lattice-1 through lattice-4, cited in text) that found ghost field decoupling in the deep infrared, and to studies of effective strings by Flauger et al. (cited in text) that found evidence for a massive pseudoscalar on the QCD flux tube worldsheet. The approach enables a reduction of the Yang-Mills mass gap problem to the mass gap problem of a specific nonlinear Schrödinger equation, which is well-studied in the mathematical literature.
