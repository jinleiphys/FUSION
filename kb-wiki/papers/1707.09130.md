# Surface Magnetic Catalysis

> We study fermions in a magnetic field in a finite size cylinder. With the boundary condition for the fermion flux, we show that the energy spectra and the wave-functions are modified by the finite size effect; the boundary makes the degenerate Landau levels appear only partially for states with small angular momenta, while the boundary effect becomes stronger for states with large angular momenta. We find that mode accumulation at the boundary occurs for large angular momenta and the magnetic effect is enhanced on the boundary surface. Using a simple fermionic model, we quantify the magnetic catalysis, i.e. the magnetic enhancement of the fermion pair condensation, in a finite size cylinder. We confirm that the magnetic catalysis is strongly amplified at the boundary due to the mode accumulation.

## Key claim

The authors show that in a finite-size cylindrical system under a magnetic field, the conventional Landau quantization is incomplete: only states with small angular momenta exhibit degenerate Landau levels, while states with large angular momenta are strongly modified by the boundary, leading to mode accumulation at the surface and a consequent enhancement of magnetic catalysis (fermion pair condensation) that is spatially amplified near the boundary.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for fermions in a constant magnetic field within a cylinder of radius \(R\), imposing a zero-flux boundary condition at \(r=R\). They obtain discretized transverse momenta \(p_{l,k} = \sqrt{2eB\lambda_{l,k}}\) where \(\lambda_{l,k}\) are determined by zeros of confluent hypergeometric functions, and construct the fermion propagator from the exact eigenfunctions. Using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, they compute the spatially dependent chiral condensate and dynamical mass by implementing the finite-size wave functions and the modified integration measure.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless parameter \(\alpha \equiv eB R^2/2\) controls the competition between magnetic length \(l_B = 1/\sqrt{eB}\) and system size \(R\); for \(\alpha=22.5\), the lowest transverse momentum \(p_{l,1}\) is lifted from zero at around \(l\simeq 10\), indicating only about half the conventional Landau degeneracy factor.
- In the limit \(\alpha\to 0\) (vanishing magnetic field), the lowest momentum \(p_{0,1}\) approaches \(2.40483/R\), which is the first zero of the Bessel function \(J_0(\zeta)\).
- For \(\alpha=45\), the Landau zero modes remain approximately up to \(l\simeq 30\), whereas the conventional degeneracy factor would be \(\alpha=45\).
- For a weak magnetic field (\(\alpha=4.5\)), the weight factor for the \(l=0\) mode is \(N_{0,1}^2\simeq 0.19\); for a stronger field (\(\alpha=45\)), \(N_{0,1}^2\simeq 0.022\), consistent with the asymptotic \(1/\alpha\) scaling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of rotating fermions in a magnetic field (Chen et al., 2015) by explicitly incorporating finite-size boundary effects, which were previously assumed negligible in the limit \(R\gg l_B\). It also generalizes the treatment of fermions in finite cylindrical systems under rotation (Davies, 1996; Ambrus, 2015; Ebihara, 2016) to include a magnetic field. The results enable quantitative study of magnetic catalysis in realistic finite-size systems such as those encountered in heavy-ion collisions or compact stars, and provide the necessary framework to later couple finite-size effects with rotation and magnetic fields simultaneously.
