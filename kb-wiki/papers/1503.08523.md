# Constraining nucleon high momentum in nuclei

> Recent studies at Jefferson Lab show that there are a certain proportion of nucleons in nuclei have momenta greater than the so-called nuclear Fermi momentum $p_{F}$. Based on the transport model of nucleus-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies, nucleon high momentum caused by the neutron-proton short-range correlations in nuclei is constrained by comparing with $π$ and photon experimental data and considering some uncertainties. The high momentum cutoff value $p_{max}$ $\leq$ 2$p_{F}$ is obtained.

# Constraining nucleon high momentum in nuclei

## Key claim
The high-momentum cutoff parameter λ (the ratio of nucleon maximal momentum to the nuclear Fermi momentum) in nuclei is constrained to λ ≤ 2, which is smaller than values deduced in other lectures, based on comparing transport model calculations with π⁺ and hard photon experimental data while considering uncertainties from nuclear stability, photon production mechanisms, and off-shell transport effects.

## Method
The Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model was used to simulate nucleus-nucleus collisions, incorporating a nucleon momentum distribution with a high-momentum tail (HMT) following a 1/k⁴ dependence and a 20% depletion of the Fermi sea. The model included an isospin- and momentum-dependent mean-field single nucleon potential, in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross sections reduced by the square of the effective-to-free reduced mass ratio, and a one-boson-exchange model for hard photon production from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung. π⁺ production in Au+Au collisions at 0.4 and 1 GeV/nucleon and hard photon production in ¹²C+¹²C collisions at 60 MeV/nucleon were calculated for various λ values and compared to experimental data from FOPI and TAPS collaborations.

## Key numbers
- π⁺ production in Au+Au collisions at 0.4 GeV/nucleon is about 10 times more sensitive to λ than at 1 GeV/nucleon.
- The ratio of π⁺ multiplicities at 0.4 and 1 GeV/nucleon constrains λ to 1.5 ≤ λ ≤ 2.5 when compared to FOPI data.
- Hard photon production cross sections (ε_γ ≥ 150 MeV) in ¹²C+¹²C collisions at 60 MeV/nucleon constrain λ ≤ 2 when compared to experimental data.
- The combined constraint from both probes yields λ ≤ 2.
- The high-momentum tail contains about 25% of protons and 17% of neutrons with momenta above their respective Fermi momenta in ¹⁹⁷Au.
- The number of n-p SRC pairs is about 18 times that of p-p or n-n SRC pairs.

## Context
This work extends the Correlated Fermi Gas model introduced by Hen et al. (2015), which used λ as a free parameter to avoid divergence in calculating nucleon average kinetic energy assuming a C/k⁴ high-momentum tail. The study uses hadronic (π⁺) and electromagnetic (hard photon) probes in heavy-ion collisions to constrain λ, complementing previous constraints from high-energy electron scattering measurements by Egiyan (2006) and from deuteron momentum distributions by Hen et al. (2014, 2015). The result λ ≤ 2 has implications for nuclear kinetic symmetry energy, neutron star physics, and transport model calculations of isospin-sensitive observables such as the π⁻/π⁺ ratio and neutron-proton elliptic flow differences.
