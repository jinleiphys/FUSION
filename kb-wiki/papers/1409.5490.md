# Tidal Waves in $^{102}$Pd: A Phenomenological Analysis

> Rotational and electromagnetic properties of the yrast band in $^{102}$Pd are analyzed in terms of a phenomenological phonon model that includes anharmonic terms. Both the moment of inertia and $B(E2)$'s are well reproduced by the model, providing an independent confirmation of the multi-phonon picture recently proposed. The (empirical) dependence of the phonon-phonon interaction on the phonon frequency, in Ru, Pd, and Ru isotopes, follows the expectations from particle-vibration coupling.

## Key claim

The yrast band in $^{102}$Pd can be described by a phenomenological phonon model with anharmonic terms, providing an independent confirmation of the multi-phonon (tidal wave) picture, but only if the anharmonic coefficients are small compared to the linear term in the energy expansion.

## Method

The authors fit the experimental energies and B(E2) transition probabilities of the yrast band in $^{102}$Pd to polynomial expansions in angular momentum I, derived from an anharmonic phonon model. They extracted the phonon energy $\hbar\omega_0$ and phonon-phonon interaction $V_2$ from the fitted coefficients, then extended this analysis to neighboring Ru, Pd, and Cd isotopes to study the systematic dependence of $V_2$ on $\hbar\omega_0$.

## Key numbers

- For $^{102}$Pd, the moment of inertia fit parameters: a = 0.254(5) keV, b = 0.018(1) keV, c = -0.0004(4) keV
- For $^{102}$Pd, the B(E2) fit parameters: $\alpha = 4.7(9) \times 10^{-2} e^2b^2$, $\beta = -0.35(10) \times 10^{-2} e^2b^2$, $\gamma = 0.01(5) \times 10^{-2} e^2b^2$
- The phonon-phonon interaction $V_2$ shows a decreasing trend with increasing phonon frequency $\hbar\omega_0$ across Ru, Pd, and Cd isotopes

## Context

This work extends the tidal wave interpretation of yrast states in $^{102}$Pd proposed by Frauendorf et al. (2014) and supported by lifetime measurements from Ayangeakaa et al. (2014), using a complementary phenomenological anharmonic phonon model rather than the microscopic approach. The systematic dependence of $V_2$ on $\hbar\omega_0$ in Ru, Pd, and Cd isotopes follows expectations from particle-vibration coupling models by Bes and Broglia (1971) and Bertsch (1975), and the authors suggest future work should test whether more realistic calculations by Paar (1975) can reproduce the absolute scales.
