# Relations Between Anomalous Dimensions in the Regge Limit

> We extend the recent formalism developed for computing rapidity anomalous dimension of form factors using unitarity to the problem of high-energy near forward scattering. By combining the factorization of $2\rightarrow 2$ scattering in the effective field theory (EFT) for Glauber operators with definite signature amplitudes, we derive an expression that relates anomalous dimensions (including Regge trajectories) to cut amplitudes, leading to significant computational simplifications. We demonstrate this explicitly by computing the one and two-loop Regge trajectories. Our formalism can also be used to bootstrap anomalous dimensions of operators not related by symmetries. As an example, we show that the full anomalous dimensions (including both the Regge pole and cut pieces) of the two Glauber operator anti-symmetric octet operator, can be determined from the anomalous dimension of the single Glauber exchange operator. Many other such relations exist between other color channels at each order in $α$.

## Key claim

The authors derive general relations between anomalous dimensions (including Regge trajectories) in the Regge limit of high-energy near-forward scattering, showing that the full anomalous dimensions of multi-Glauber operators can be determined from the anomalous dimension of the single Glauber exchange operator, extending previous results that were limited to the anti-symmetric octet channel and next-to-leading-log accuracy.

## Method

The authors combine the factorization of 2-to-2 scattering in the effective field theory for Glauber operators (GSCET) with definite signature amplitudes, deriving an expression that relates anomalous dimensions to cut amplitudes. They compute the one-loop and two-loop Regge trajectories explicitly using this formalism, and demonstrate that the full anomalous dimensions (including both Regge pole and cut pieces) of the two-Glauber operator anti-symmetric octet can be bootstrapped from the anomalous dimension of the single Glauber exchange operator.

## Key numbers

* One-loop Regge trajectory: extracted from the order epsilon piece of a one-loop correction, with the relation alpha^(1) = -2 S_2^(0) where S_2^(0) is the tree-level two-Glauber soft function in the octet channel
* Two-loop Regge trajectory: given by alpha^(2) = -2(S_1^(1) - S_1^(1) S_2^(0)), where S_1^(1) is the one-loop single-Glauber soft function
* The formalism works in d = 4 - 2 epsilon dimensions with the MSbar factor defined as mu-bar^(2 epsilon) = mu^(2 epsilon) (4 pi)^(-epsilon) e^(epsilon gamma_E)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Moult et al. (2022) which showed iterative structure in the anti-symmetric octet channel using the EFT approach of Rothstein (2016). The authors generalize beyond the planar limit and beyond next-to-leading-log accuracy, building on the BFKL resummation of Kuraev, Fadin, Lipatov (1976) and Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, Lipatov (1978). The results enable systematic calculation of Regge trajectories and anomalous dimensions at higher loops through cut diagrams in the EFT, and reveal relations between anomalous dimensions in different color channels that are not related by symmetries.
