# The Phase Transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma and Its Effect on Hydrodynamic Flow

> It is shown that in ideal relativistic hydrodynamics a phase transition from hadron to quark and gluon degrees of freedom in the nuclear matter equation of state leads to a minimum in the excitation function of the transverse collective flow.

# The Phase Transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma and Its Effect on Hydrodynamic Flow

## Key claim

In ideal relativistic hydrodynamics, a phase transition from hadron to quark and gluon degrees of freedom in the nuclear matter equation of state produces a local minimum in the excitation function of directed transverse collective flow around 6 AGeV beam energy, but the exact position of this minimum depends strongly on the equation of state and may shift to higher beam energies if more hadronic resonances are included.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic calculations for Au+Au collisions at finite impact parameter (b=3 fm) using a SHASTA algorithm with first-order accuracy in time and a grid spacing of 0.3 fm. The equation of state was constructed by matching the MIT bag model for the quark-gluon plasma phase (bag constant B=(235 MeV)^4) with a sigma-omega model for hadronic matter via Gibbs' conditions of phase equilibrium, and results were compared to a pure hadronic equation of state without phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition sets in at E_Lab^kin ~ 1.5 AGeV and pure QGP is formed at E_Lab^kin ~ 4.1 AGeV for the constructed equation of state
- The "softest point" (local minimum of p/epsilon) occurs at E_Lab^kin ~ 4.1 AGeV
- The ground state incompressibility of the hadronic equation of state is K0 ~ 300 MeV with effective nucleon mass M0* ~ 0.635 M
- The critical temperature for the phase transition in baryon-free matter is Tc ~ 170 MeV
- For Au+Au collisions at 5 AGeV and b=3 fm, the directed transverse flow drops to essentially zero for the equation of state with phase transition
- The maximum transverse momentum for the pure hadronic equation of state is of order 300 MeV across the beam energies studied (3.5, 5, and 11.7 AGeV)
- The local minimum in the excitation function of directed transverse collective flow occurs around ~6 AGeV

## Context

This work extends earlier one-dimensional studies by the same group (Rischke et al., 1995) showing that a phase transition qualitatively changes hydrodynamic flow patterns, and agrees with earlier works by Csernai and Bravina that indicated a decrease of transverse collective flow when the equation of state features a phase transition to the QGP. The results contradict the expectation from microscopic transport models like RQMD, ARC, and ART that hadronic physics alone can explain single-particle observables. The paper enables experimental searches for the predicted minimum in the flow excitation function, noting that experiments between BEVALAC and AGS energies were underway at the time to test whether hadronic interactions or quark-gluon degrees of freedom dominate at these energies.
