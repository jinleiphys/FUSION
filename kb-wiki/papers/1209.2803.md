# The Spin Structure of the Nucleon

> This article reviews our present understanding of QCD spin physics: the proton spin puzzle and new developments aimed at understanding the transverse structure of the nucleon. We discuss present experimental investigations of the nucleon's internal spin structure, the theoretical interpretation of the different measurements and the open questions and challenges for future investigation.

## Key claim

This review paper consolidates the current understanding that the proton spin puzzle is primarily a valence quark effect, with the measured flavor-singlet axial-charge $g_A^{(0)} \sim 0.35$ being approximately saturated by valence quark contributions, while polarized glue may contribute up to 50% of the proton's spin at the scale of present experiments, and sea quark and gluon corrections remain small.

## Method

The authors review experimental results from polarized deep inelastic scattering (pDIS) experiments at SLAC, CERN, DESY, Jefferson Laboratory, and RHIC, including inclusive measurements of spin structure functions $g_1$ and $g_2$, semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS) for flavor-separated quark distributions, and polarized proton-proton collisions for gluon polarization. They also cover theoretical developments in generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMDs) that describe the three-dimensional structure of the nucleon and spin-orbit couplings.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon's flavor-singlet axial-charge measured in polarized deep inelastic scattering is $g_A^{(0)} \sim 0.35$
- Transverse single-spin asymmetries in proton-proton collisions revealed up to 40% asymmetries, with 5-10% in lepton-nucleon collisions with unpolarized leptons and transversely polarized nucleons
- In the static quark model, $\Delta u - \Delta d = 5/3$ and $\Delta u + \Delta d = 1$
- Relativistic quark models renormalize axial charges by a depolarization factor of 0.65
- Typical target polarizations achieved: 90% for NH$_3$, 85% for protons and 50% for deuterons in COMPASS, 38% for $^3$He, 22% for LiD
- Dilution factors for polarized solid state targets range between 0.1 and 0.2, with $^6$LiD at 0.4-0.5
- COMPASS spectrometer has about 250k read-out channels recordable at 20 kHz for event sizes of ~40 kByte
- COMPASS target system operates at about 60 mK in frozen spin mode with a 2.5 T solenoid and 0.6 T dipole magnet

## Context

This review extends the work of earlier reviews by Anselmino (1994), Ellis (1995), Bass (2004), and others, consolidating 25 years of experimental and theoretical progress since the EMC discovery in 1988 that quark spins contribute little to the proton's spin. The paper enables future programs including COMPASS-II, the 12 GeV upgrade of JLab, and experiments at RHIC and Fermilab aimed at mapping the three-dimensional structure of the nucleon through GPDs and TMDs, with key open questions including the role of orbital angular momentum and the nature of transverse spin asymmetries.
