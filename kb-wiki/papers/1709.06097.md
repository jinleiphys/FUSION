# Spectroscopy of Exotic Hadrons Formed from Dynamical Diquarks

> The dynamical diquark picture asserts that exotic hadrons can be formed from widely separated colored diquark or triquark components. We use the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) approximation to study the spectrum of states thus constructed, both in the basis of diquark spins and in the basis of heavy quark-antiquark spins. We develop a compact notation for naming these states, and use the results of lattice simulations for hybrid mesons to predict the lowest expected BO potentials for both tetraquarks and pentaquarks. We then compare to the set of exotic candidates with experimentally determined quantum numbers, and find that all of them can be accommodated. Once decay modes are also considered, one can develop selection rules of both exact ($J^{PC}$ conservation) and approximate (within the context of the BO approximation) types and test their effectiveness. We find that the most appealing way to satisfy both sets of selection rules requires including additional low-lying BO potentials, a hypothesis that can be checked on the lattice.

## Key claim

The dynamical diquark picture can accommodate all currently observed exotic hadron candidates with known quantum numbers, but satisfying both exact ($J^{PC}$ conservation) and approximate (Born-Oppenheimer) decay selection rules requires including additional low-lying BO potentials beyond the simplest expectations, a hypothesis that can be tested on the lattice.

## Method

The author applies the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) approximation to tetraquark states composed of a heavy diquark ($\delta$) and antidiquark ($\bar\delta$) pair, and to pentaquark states composed of a triquark ($\bar\theta$) and diquark ($\delta$), treating the colored diquark components as heavy, slowly moving sources connected by a flux tube. The spectrum is catalogued in both the diquark-spin basis and the heavy quark-antiquark spin basis, using lattice results for hybrid meson BO potentials to predict the lowest expected BO potentials for tetraquarks and pentaquarks. The predicted states are compared to the set of experimentally observed exotic candidates with determined quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- The mass gap between the lightest charmonium states ($\eta_c$, $J/\psi$) and the lightest true hybrids from lattice calculations is about 1.1–1.3 GeV.
- The difference $m_{\psi(2S)} - m_{J/\psi (1S)} \approx 600$ MeV, and $m_{\chi_{c} (1P)} - m_{J/\psi (1S)} \approx 400$ MeV, providing reference scales for possible dynamical diquark mass gaps.
- The final separation of the $\delta$-$\bar\delta$ pair in the $Z_c(4430)^-$ system was calculated to be 1.16 fm.
- For $L = 0, 1, 2, 3, \ldots$, one counts 2, 4, 4, 4, ... conventional quarkonium states and 24, 56, 64, 64, ... tetraquark states (including isospin).
- Not counting $I_3 = \pm 1$ charge conjugates as distinct, the tetraquark numbers reduce by 25% to 18, 42, 48, 48, ...
- 28 bosonic charmoniumlike exotics have been observed (not counting charge conjugates) as of the paper's writing.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical diquark picture (Brodsky, 2014) by developing it into a quantifiable model using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, which was originally applied to atoms and molecules (Born, 1927) and later to heavy quarkonium hybrids on the lattice (Griffiths, 1983; Juge, 1997; Juge, 1999; Juge, 2002; Liu, 2012). The approach differs from traditional Hamiltonian-based diquark models (Maiani, 2004; Maiani, 2014) by treating the state as bound only by confinement until decay, rather than as a static configuration, and from hadronic molecule models (Guo, 2017) by incorporating hidden-color subunits. The work enables future lattice checks of the predicted low-lying BO potentials and systematic predictions for masses, decay selection rules, and branching fractions under various model parameter assignments.
