# Wobbling motion coupled to gamma vibration at high spin

> We report a solution of the tilted-axis cranked HFB equation for $^{164}$Hf, which shows wobbling motion coupled to gamma vibration at high spin ($J\simeq 60\hbar$). Possible anharmonicity and splitting of energy levels are also discussed as a consequence of the wobbling motion with large amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper reports a solution of the tilted-axis cranked HFB equation for $^{164}$Hf that shows wobbling motion coupled to gamma vibration at high spin ($J\simeq 60\hbar$), but only under the condition that the nucleus has sizeable gamma deformation and the wobbling amplitude is large enough to induce cooperative gamma vibration.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent tilted-axis cranked HFB calculations for $^{164}$Hf using a Hamiltonian consisting of a spherical Nilsson Hamiltonian and a residual pairing-plus-$Q\cdot Q$ force, with the same model space and force parameters as in their previous work (Oi, Walker, and Ansari, 2001). The HFB equation was solved by the method of steepest descent under constraints on angular momentum and particle number, with the tilt angle $\varphi$ treated as a dynamical variable. The energy curve $E(\varphi)$ as a function of tilt angle was then used to solve a one-dimensional Schrodinger equation for the wobbling phonon, assuming the tilt angle can be treated as a dynamical variable and that $E(\varphi)$ corresponds to the potential $V(\varphi)$.

## Key numbers

* At $J\simeq 60\hbar$, the yrast states in $^{164}$Hf take on gamma deformation $\gamma\simeq -20^{\circ}$ and $\beta\simeq 0.13$
* The calculated wobbling phonon energy $\hbar\omega_{\rm w}$ is 70 keV at $J=60\hbar$ with quadrupole deformations $(\beta,\gamma)=(0.13,-20^{\circ})$ at $\varphi\simeq0^{\circ}$
* The energy curve near $\varphi\simeq 0^{\circ}$ is harmonic with $V(\varphi)\simeq a\varphi^2$ where $a=1.64$ MeV
* The harmonic shape is maintained up to $|\varphi|\alt 0.4$ (i.e., $23^{\circ}$), corresponding to wobbling phonon numbers $n_{\rm w} \alt 5$
* For $n_{\rm w} \agt 5$, the structure starts to deviate from harmonic
* The $\beta$ values are almost constant, $0.130\alt \beta \alt 0.135$, for the entire range of $\varphi$
* The gap energy vanishes at $J\simeq 50\hbar$ for protons and $J\simeq 20\hbar$ for neutrons

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of wobbling motion originally formulated by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) for triaxially deformed nuclei, and builds on the experimental discovery of wobbling in $^{163}$Lu reported by Odegard et al. (2001). The study uses the tilted-axis cranking model developed by Horibata, Oi, and Onishi (1995) and Frauendorf (2000), and applies it to the even-even nucleus $^{164}$Hf, which Bengtsson (2001) calculated would become triaxial super-deformed at $I\simeq 40\hbar$. The results enable future experimental searches for wobbling bands in even-even nuclei and predict anharmonicity and possible level splitting (a "wobbling quartet") from tunnelling effects between the four potential wells created by the coupling of wobbling and gamma vibration.
