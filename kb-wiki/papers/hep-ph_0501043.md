# Parity doublets in the baryon spectrum

> Physics of the low-lying and high-lying hadrons in the light flavors sector is reviewed. While the low-lying hadrons are strongly affected by both $U(1)_A$ and spontaneous $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ breakings, in the high-lying hadrons these symmetries are restored. A manifestation is a persistence of the chiral multiplet structure in both baryon and meson spectra. A fundamental origin of this phenomenon is that effects of quantum fluctuations of both quark and gluon fields must vanish at large $n$ or $J$ and a semiclassical description becomes adequate. A relation between the chiral symmetry restoration and the string picture of excited hadrons is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while low-lying hadrons are strongly affected by spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the $U(1)_A$ anomaly, high-lying hadrons exhibit effective chiral symmetry restoration (chiral symmetry restoration of the second kind), where the valence quarks decouple from the QCD vacuum condensates and the symmetries of the classical QCD Lagrangian become manifest.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical basis for chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons, using the operator product expansion of two-point correlation functions at large space-like momenta and analyticity via dispersion relations to argue that spectral densities become chirally symmetric at high energies. The paper then classifies observed meson and baryon resonances from the PDG compilation and LEAR data into chiral multiplets of the $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ and $U(2)_L \times U(2)_R$ groups, and introduces a quantitative measure called chiral asymmetry to characterize the degree of symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate: $\langle \bar q q \rangle \simeq -(240 \pm 10 \text{ MeV})^3$, representing the order parameter for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking
- Systematic parity doubling in the nucleon spectrum appears at masses of 1.7 GeV and above
- Parity doublets in the delta spectrum appear at masses of 1.9 GeV and higher
- For low-lying states, chiral asymmetry (defined as $\chi = |M_1 - M_2|/(M_1+M_2)$) is typically 0.2–0.6, e.g. comparing $\rho(770)$ and $a_1(1260)$
- At meson masses of about 2 GeV, chiral asymmetry is typically within 0.01
- For $J=2$ mesons, example chiral partners include: $\omega_2(0,2^{--})$ at $1975 \pm 20$ MeV with $f_2(0,2^{++})$ at $1934 \pm 20$ MeV; $\pi_2(1,2^{-+})$ at $2005 \pm 15$ MeV with $f_2(0,2^{++})$ at $2001 \pm 10$ MeV; $a_2(1,2^{++})$ at $2030 \pm 20$ MeV with $\eta_2(0,2^{-+})$ at $2030 \pm ?$ MeV

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Glozman (2001) and Cohen and Glozman (2002) on chiral symmetry restoration in baryons, and Glozman (2004) on mesons, by providing a unified theoretical framework based on the semiclassical regime. The paper contrasts with the traditional constituent quark model, which cannot explain parity doubling, and instead supports a Nambu string picture with chiral quarks at the ends (Glozman 2004). The work enables systematic experimental searches for missing chiral partners, particularly $a_0$ mesons and high-lying baryon states, which the author identifies as a priority for existing and forthcoming accelerators.
