# Study of electron-positron annihilation into $K\bar{K}π$ within resonance chiral theory

> In this paper, a coherent study of the $e^+e^-$ annihilation into $K^+K^-π^0$, $K^0_SK^0_Lπ^0$ and $K^0_SK^\pmπ^\mp$ is carried out within the framework of resonance chiral theory. The amplitudes are fixed by fitting to the experimental cross-section and invariant mass spectrum. With these amplitudes, one can calculate the hadronic vacuum polarization form factors of these processes. The leading order contributions of $σ(e^+e^- \to K\bar{K}π)$ to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $(g-2)_μ$, is obtained as $a_μ^{\rm HVP,LO}(e^+e^- \to K\bar{K}π)=(3.178\pm0.071)\times10^{-10}$ up to $E_{\rm cm}=2.3$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper provides a coherent theoretical description of three \(e^+e^- \to K\bar{K}\pi\) channels within resonance chiral theory, but the short-distance constraints derived from matching to QCD for tensor meson couplings are incompatible with experimental decay widths, so those couplings are left free and determined by fits to data rather than fixed by theory.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians within resonance chiral theory (RChT) for vector and tensor mesons, including newly built tensor-electromagnetic current-pseudoscalar (TJP) and tensor-vector-pseudoscalar (TVP) interaction terms. The form factors for \(e^+e^- \to K^+K^-\pi^0\), \(K^0_SK^0_L\pi^0\), and \(K^0_SK^\pm\pi^\mp\) are derived from Feynman diagrams at leading order in the \(1/N_C\) expansion, including contributions from the Wess-Zumino-Witten anomaly, vector resonances, and tensor resonances. The amplitudes are fixed by fitting to experimental cross-section and invariant mass spectrum data, with heavier resonance multiplets included via extended Breit-Wigner propagators.

## Key numbers

* The leading order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment from all three \(e^+e^- \to K\bar{K}\pi\) channels is \(a_\mu^{\rm HVP,LO}(e^+e^- \to K\bar{K}\pi) = (3.178 \pm 0.071) \times 10^{-10}\), integrated up to center-of-mass energy \(E_{\rm cm} = 2.3\) GeV.
* The pion decay constant is taken as \(F = 92.2\) MeV, from ParticleDataGroup (2022).
* The experimental average of the muon anomalous magnetic moment is \(a_\mu^{\rm avg}({\rm Exp}) = 116\,592\,059(22) \times 10^{-11}\) (0.19 ppm), and the discrepancy with the Standard Model is \(\Delta a_\mu = (249 \pm 48) \times 10^{-11}\) at 5.1 sigma significance, from Muong-2 (2023).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RChT studies of \(e^+e^- \to \pi\pi\), \(K\bar{K}\), \(\pi\gamma\), \(\eta\gamma\), \(\pi\pi\pi\), and \(\pi\pi\eta\) (Qin 2020, Wang 2023) to the \(K\bar{K}\pi\) final states, which involve tensor meson contributions not present in the three-pion case. The results enable improved data-driven calculations of the hadronic vacuum polarization for the muon \(g-2\), and the authors note that the latest CMD-3 (2023) measurement of \(e^+e^- \to \pi\pi\) gives a larger cross-section than previous experiments (BaBar 2012, KLOE-2 2017, BESIII 2015), implying a larger HVP contribution closer to lattice QCD results (Borsanyi 2020).
