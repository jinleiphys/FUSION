# Impact of octupole deformation on the nuclear electromagnetic response

> Background: Properties of giant dipole resonances, along with other nuclear resonances, provide valuable tools for refining theoretical models as they reflect collective features of nuclear matter. Among such collective phenomena is octupole deformation, whose impact on resonance features, however, is less studied. Purpose: Investigate the effect of reflection-symmetry-breaking octupole deformation on electric and magnetic transition strengths in atomic nuclei. Methods: Calculations were performed using linear response theory with the iterative finite amplitude method to solve quasiparticle random phase approximation-type equations. Underlying ground-state solutions were obtained within the framework of axially symmetric Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) using three different Skyrme functionals. Results: Electric and magnetic multipole responses were calculated for octupole-deformed even-even Rn, Ra, Th, U, Pu, and Cm isotopes. Calculations were performed on top of two distinct deformed ground-state solutions: one constrained to conserve parity, and the other allowing parity breaking. Sum rules were calculated from $M1$ transition strengths and compared with the expected correlations to certain ground-state properties. Conclusions: Based on our results, the octupole deformation has only a modest effect on the transition strengths in the resonances. In turn, $M1$ transition strengths have a greater effect at lower energies ($0\,\text{--}\,8\,\text{MeV}$), which encourages further investigation. Isoscalar $E3$ transition strength was confirmed to have a significant contribution from the rotational Nambu--Goldstone mode in the parity-breaking HFB solution, and thus, removing it was found necessary.

# Impact of octupole deformation on the nuclear electromagnetic response

## Key claim
Octupole deformation has only a modest effect on electric and magnetic transition strengths in giant resonances, but M1 transition strengths show a greater effect at lower energies (0–8 MeV), which warrants further investigation. The isoscalar E3 transition strength contains a significant contribution from the rotational Nambu-Goldstone mode in parity-breaking HFB solutions, making its removal necessary for obtaining physical results.

## Method
The authors performed linear response calculations using the iterative finite amplitude method (FAM) to solve quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) equations, built on top of axially symmetric Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground-state solutions obtained with the HFBTHO code. They compared two distinct ground-state solutions for each nucleus: one constrained to conserve parity (Q3=0) and the other allowing parity breaking (Q3≠0), using three different Skyrme functionals (SkM*, SLy4, and UNEDF1). The Nambu-Goldstone mode associated with broken rotational symmetry was removed from the calculated strength functions using the conjugate operator method.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state octupole moments Q3 for the studied nuclei range from 1.127 b^(3/2) (224Rn with SLy4) to 5.798 b^(3/2) (290Pu with SkM*)
- Deformation energies E_def(Q2, Q3) relative to reflection-symmetric minima range from -1.648 MeV (224U with SkM*) to -0.026 MeV (228Ra with SkM*)
- Pairing strengths used: V^n_0 = -265.2500 MeV (SkM*), -325.2500 MeV (SLy4), -223.2780 MeV (UNEDF1); V^p_0 = -340.0625 MeV (SkM* and SLy4), -247.8960 MeV (UNEDF1)
- FAM-QRPA calculations used a Lorentzian smearing of γ = 0.5 MeV and were solved at 0.3 MeV intervals from 0 to 38 MeV (SkM* and SLy4) or 0 to 20 MeV (UNEDF1)
- Harmonic oscillator basis size: N_sh = 20 for SkM* and SLy4, N_sh = 16 for UNEDF1
- Pairing cutoff set to 60 MeV with mixed pairing parameter α = 1/2

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear electromagnetic response in actinide nuclei, following the comprehensive FAM study by Tong (2024) that examined Pu isotopes and 226Ra, but focusing specifically on nuclei predicted to exhibit octupole deformation. The study complements recent work by Zhao (2024) comparing tetrahedral shapes to pear-shaped Zr isotopes, and builds on global mean-field predictions of octupole deformation by Cao (2020), Agbemava (2016), Ebata (2017), and Grams (2023). The results provide theoretical predictions for even-even Rn, Ra, Th, U, Pu, and Cm isotopes with mass numbers A=222-230 and A=288-290, for which essentially no experimental data currently exist, but which may become accessible through upcoming storage-ring measurements using inverse kinematics as demonstrated by Zamora (2016) and discussed by Reinhart (2014), Reifarth (2017), and Cooper (2024).
