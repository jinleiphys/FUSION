# Kaon fragmentation function from NJL-jet model

> The NJL-jet model provides a sound framework for calculating the fragmentation func- tions in an effective chiral quark theory, where the momentum and isospin sum rules are satisfied without the introduction of ad hoc parameters [1]. Earlier studies of the pion fragmentation func- tions using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model within this framework showed good qualitative agreement with the empirical parameterizations. Here we extend the NJL-jet model by including the strange quark. The corrections to the pion fragmentation function and corresponding kaon fragmen- tation functions are calculated using the elementary quark to quark-meson fragmentation functions from NJL. The results for the kaon fragmentation function exhibit a qualitative agreement with the empirical parameterizations, while the unfavored strange quark fragmentation to pions is shown to be of the same order of magnitude as the unfavored light quark's. The results of these studies are expected to provide important guidance for the analysis of a large variety of semi-inclusive data.

## Key claim

The NJL-jet model, extended to include the strange quark, produces kaon fragmentation functions that show qualitative agreement with empirical parameterizations, but the agreement is only within the large uncertainties of those parameterizations, and the unfavored strange quark fragmentation to pions is found to be of the same order of magnitude as the unfavored light quark's.

## Method

The authors extended the NJL-jet model by introducing the strange quark, calculating the elementary quark-to-quark-meson fragmentation functions for kaons using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Lepage-Brodsky invariant mass cutoff regularization. They solved the coupled integral equations for the total fragmentation functions of u, d, and s quarks to pions and kaons, then DGLAP evolved the results from the model scale of Q² = 0.18 GeV² to 4 GeV² at leading order for comparison with phenomenological parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- Strange constituent quark mass M_s = 0.45 GeV, determined by fitting the model-calculated kaon decay constant to the experimental value f_K = 0.114 GeV
- Quark-kaon coupling constant g_Kqq = 3
- Model scale Q_0² = 0.18 GeV² used as the starting point for DGLAP evolution
- DGLAP evolution performed to Q² = 4 GeV² at leading order
- Fragmentation to K⁺ is about half as likely as fragmentation to π⁺
- Numerical solutions obey momentum and isospin sum rules within numerical errors of less than one percent

## Context

This work extends the NJL-jet model of Ito (2009) by including the strange quark, which was previously omitted in calculations of pion fragmentation functions. The inclusion of strangeness softens the high-z region of D_u^{π⁺} compared to the earlier calculations, bringing the curves closer to the phenomenological parameterizations of Hirai (2007). The results are expected to provide guidance for analyzing semi-inclusive data, though the authors note that vector meson and nucleon-antinucleon channels need to be included for a more complete description, and Monte-Carlo simulations would be needed to access transverse momentum distributions.
