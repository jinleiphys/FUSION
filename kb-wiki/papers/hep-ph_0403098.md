# D meson enhancement in pp collisions at the LHC due to nonlinear gluon evolution

> When nonlinear effects on the gluon evolution are included with constraints from HERA, the gluon distribution in the free proton is enhanced at low momentum fractions, x < 0.01, and low scales, Q^2 < 10 GeV^2, relative to standard, DGLAP-evolved, gluon distributions. Consequently, such gluon distributions can enhance charm production in pp collisions at center of mass energy 14 TeV by up to a factor of five at midrapidity, y \sim 0, and transverse momentum p_T -> 0 in the most optimistic case. We show that most of this enhancement survives hadronization into D mesons. Assuming the same enhancement at leading and next-to-leading order, we show that the D enhancement may be measured by D^0 reconstruction in the K^-π^+ decay channel with the ALICE detector.

# D meson enhancement in pp collisions at the LHC due to nonlinear gluon evolution

## Key claim

When nonlinear (GLRMQ) corrections to the DGLAP evolution equations are included with constraints from HERA data, the gluon distribution in the free proton is enhanced at low momentum fractions (x < 0.01) and low scales (Q^2 < 10 GeV^2) relative to standard DGLAP-evolved gluon distributions; this enhancement can increase charm production in pp collisions at 14 TeV by up to a factor of five at midrapidity and zero transverse momentum in the most optimistic case, and most of this enhancement survives hadronization into D mesons.

## Method

The authors computed leading-order (LO) charm production cross sections using the nonlinearly-evolved EHKQS parton distribution functions (PDFs) relative to the DGLAP-evolved CTEQ61L PDFs, then assumed the same enhancement factor applies at next-to-leading order (NLO) by constructing an "alternative NLO" cross section that combines LO PDFs with one-loop alpha_s and NLO matrix elements with two-loop alpha_s. Charm quarks were hadronized into D mesons using the PYTHIA string fragmentation model, and the resulting D^0 p_T distributions were folded with the expected ALICE detector performance for reconstruction in the K^- pi^+ decay channel, including statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Charm production enhancement factor of up to 5 at midrapidity (y ~ 0) and p_T -> 0 for pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, using m_c = 1.3 GeV and Q^2 = m_T^2 with EHKQS set 1 relative to CTEQ61L
- D meson enhancement factor of approximately 3 for p_T^D -> 0 in the most optimistic case (m_c = 1.3 GeV, Q^2 = m_T^2), reduced from the factor of 5 charm enhancement after PYTHIA string fragmentation
- The enhancement is significant for p_T^D < 3 GeV and disappears for p_T^D > 5 GeV
- Statistical uncertainties correspond to 10^9 minimum-bias pp events (approximately 9 month run at luminosity ~5 x 10^30 cm^-2 s^-1)
- p_T-dependent systematic uncertainties of approximately 10% from detector acceptance and efficiency corrections, and approximately 8% from feed-down from B decays
- 5% p_T-independent systematic uncertainty from normalization to the pp inelastic cross section

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Eskola, Kolhinen, and Vogt (2003) which first proposed charm production as a probe of nonlinear gluon evolution effects at the LHC, but did not include hadronization or detector considerations. The nonlinear evolution equations (GLRMQ terms) were derived by Gribov, Levin, Ryskin, Mueller, and Qiu, and the EHKQS PDFs used here were constructed to reproduce HERA F_2 data while incorporating these nonlinearities. The paper enables experimental searches for nonlinear QCD effects at the LHC with the ALICE detector, and suggests that D meson measurements at the Tevatron (sqrt(s) = 1.96 TeV) might also detect an enhancement if the minimum p_T^D could be lowered to approximately 1 GeV, while at RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV) the effect is too small to be reliably observed.
