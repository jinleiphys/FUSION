# New parameterization of the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field model

> A new parameter set is generated for finite and infinite nuclear system within the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (ERMF) formalism. The isovector part of the ERMF model employed in the present study includes the coupling of nucleons to the δ and \r{ho} mesons and the cross-coupling of \r{ho} mesons to the σ and ω mesons. The results for the finite and infinite nuclear systems obtained using our parameter set are in harmony with the available experimental data. We find the maximum mass of the neutron star to be 2.03M\odot? and yet a relatively smaller radius at the canonical mass, 12.69 km, as required by the available data.

## Key claim

The new G3 parameter set for the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (ERMF) model simultaneously achieves a maximum neutron star mass of 2.03 solar masses and a relatively small canonical-mass radius of 12.69 km, while also improving agreement with experimental data on finite nuclei and neutron-skin thickness compared to earlier parameter sets like NL3, FSUGold2, FSUGarnet, and G2.

## Method

The authors calibrated the ERMF energy density functional using a simulated annealing method, fitting coupling constants to binding energies and charge radii of eight spherical nuclei (¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹⁰⁰Sn, ¹³²Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb) while constraining nuclear matter incompressibility to 210–245 MeV and symmetry energy to 28–35 MeV. The model includes couplings of nucleons to δ and ρ mesons, cross-couplings of ρ mesons to σ and ω mesons, and tensor couplings of mesons to nucleons, which were absent or incomplete in earlier parameterizations. Pairing correlations were treated with a quasibound BCS approach.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: 2.03 M⊙, with corresponding radius 11.03 km
- Radius at canonical mass (1.4 M⊙): 12.69 km
- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.148 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: −16.02 MeV
- Incompressibility coefficient K∞: 243.9 MeV
- Symmetry energy J: 31.8 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L: 47.3 MeV
- Effective mass ratio M*/M: 0.699
- Root-mean-square deviation for binding energies of 70 spherical nuclei: 2.308 MeV (compared to 2.977 MeV for NL3, 3.062 MeV for FSUGold2, 3.696 MeV for FSUGarnet, and 3.827 MeV for G2)
- Neutron-skin thickness for ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.180 fm
- Core-crust transition density: 0.074 fm⁻³
- Chemical potential at core-crust transition: 951.72 MeV
- Crust contribution to total mass: ∼0.015 M⊙
- Crust thickness at maximum mass: 0.39 km; at canonical mass: 1.06 km

## Context

This work extends the ERMF model parameterizations of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997) (the G1 and G2 sets) and the TM1* set, which had incompressibility and/or symmetry energy values too large compared to current estimates from giant resonance measurements in ²⁰⁸Pb. The new G3 set improves upon these by including δ-meson coupling and ω-ρ cross-coupling, yielding neutron-skin thicknesses consistent with antiproton scattering data from Trzcińska et al. (2001) and Jastrzębski et al. (2004). The resulting neutron star properties satisfy the 2 M⊙ observational constraint from PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 while giving a canonical-mass radius within the empirical range 10.7–13.1 km, which is smaller than most relativistic mean-field models that lack δ-meson contributions. The parameter set enables future covariance analysis to assess uncertainties and correlations among the model parameters.
