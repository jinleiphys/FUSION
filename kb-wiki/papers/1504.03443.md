# Empirical description of beta-delayed fission partial half-lives

> Background: The process of beta-delayed fission (bDF) provides a versatile tool to study low-energy fission in nuclei far away from the beta-stability line, especially for nuclei which do not fission spontaneously. Purpose: The aim of this paper is to investigate systematic trends in bDF partial half-lives. Method: A semi-phenomenological framework was developed to systematically account for the behavior of bDF partial half-lives. Results: The bDF partial half-life appears to exponentially depend on the difference between the Q value for beta decay of the parent nucleus and the fission-barrier energy of the daughter (after beta decay) product. Such dependence was found to arise naturally from some simple theoretical considerations. Conclusions: This systematic trend was confirmed for experimental bDF partial half-lives spanning over 7 orders of magnitudes when using fission barriers calculated from either the Thomas-Fermi or the liquid-drop fission model. The same dependence was also observed, although less pronounced, when comparing to fission barriers from the finite-range liquid-drop model or the Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky Integral method.

## Key claim

The beta-delayed fission partial half-life shows an exponential dependence on the difference between the Q-value for beta decay of the parent and the fission-barrier energy of the daughter nucleus, but this trend is confirmed most clearly only when using fission barriers from the Thomas-Fermi or liquid-drop fission models, with less pronounced agreement for the finite-range liquid-drop model or the Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky Integral method.

## Method

A semi-phenomenological framework was developed by approximating the beta-strength function as constant, the Fermi function for electron capture as proportional to (Q_beta - E)^2, and the fission-to-total decay width ratio as an exponential function of (B_f - E). These approximations lead to a predicted linear relationship between log10(T_1/2p,betaDF) and (Q_beta - B_f), which was tested against experimental partial half-lives for 27 betaDF precursors using fission barriers from four different theoretical models.

## Key numbers

- Experimental betaDF partial half-lives span over 7 orders of magnitude, from approximately 4 x 10^2 s for 178Tl to 4.0 x 10^8 s for 248Es
- For Thomas-Fermi barriers: fitted slope X = 3.0(2) MeV^-1, offset C' = 6.2(1) MeV, RMSD = 0.47
- For liquid-drop model barriers: fitted slope X = 2.2(2) MeV^-1, offset C' = 5.8(2) MeV, RMSD = 0.62
- For finite-range liquid-drop model barriers: fitted slope X = 1.7(4) MeV^-1, offset C' = 4.9(3) MeV, RMSD = 1.19
- For ETFSI barriers: fitted slope X = 2.1(7) MeV^-1, offset C' = 5.0(6) MeV, RMSD = 1.10
- Theoretical estimate from NRV for the exponential decay constant X is approximately 4 MeV^-1
- Q_beta values for neutron-deficient lead region precursors range from 9.4 to 11.6 MeV; for uranium region precursors from 3.1 to 5.9 MeV
- Fission barriers in the neutron-deficient lead region are approximately 7-10 MeV; in the uranium region approximately 4-6 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of betaDF probabilities by Shaughnessy (2000, 2002) that showed exponential dependence on Q_beta alone in a narrow region near uranium, by including newly obtained data from the neutron-deficient lead region with significantly different N/Z ratios (approximately 1.2-1.3 versus 1.4-1.5) and higher Q_beta values (approximately 9-11 MeV versus 3-6 MeV). The systematic trend enables prediction of betaDF partial half-lives for unmeasured nuclei, which is relevant for assessing the role of betaDF in r-process nucleosynthesis as discussed by Panov (2005) and Petermann (2012).
