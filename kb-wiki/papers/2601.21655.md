# Cone-Dependent Jet Collisional Energy Loss in Finite QCD Medium

> We derive a compact HTL-resummed expression for the leading-order jet collisional energy loss in a finite-size, finite-temperature QCD medium. Defining the jet energy inside a cone of radius $R$, we obtain the out-of-cone elastic energy loss with an explicit separation between contributions from the primary jet parton and recoiling medium partons. The result reproduces the known partonic limit as $R\!\to\!0$, vanishes for $R\!\to\!π$, and applies to both light- and heavy-flavor jets. Numerically, the elastic component shows a pronounced non-linear $R$ dependence relative to the radiative baseline, and its importance increases with $R$, becoming comparable to or exceeding the radiative contribution for sufficiently large jet radii. The path-length dependence remains close to linear for all $R$, while the medium-response contribution can exceed $10\%$ for realistic jet radii.

# Cone-Dependent Jet Collisional Energy Loss in Finite QCD Medium

## Key claim

The authors derive a compact HTL-resummed expression for leading-order jet collisional energy loss in a finite-size QCD medium, showing that the elastic out-of-cone component can become comparable to or exceed the radiative contribution for sufficiently large jet cone radii, contrary to the common partonic hierarchy where radiative loss dominates.

## Method

The authors extend the finite-size HTL formalism of Djordjevic (2006) from single partons to jets by defining the jet energy inside a cone of radius R and deriving the corresponding cone-restricted collisional energy loss from the one-loop diagram with one HTL-resummed gluon exchange. The calculation separates primary-parton and recoil contributions, incorporates finite-size effects through sine-squared interference factors, and evaluates angular integrals restricted to recoils outside the cone. Numerical results use a self-consistently determined Debye mass and one-loop running coupling, with comparisons to radiative out-of-cone losses from the formalism of Karmakar (2024).

## Key numbers

- For light-quark jets at E=50 GeV and L=4 fm, the collisional out-of-cone energy loss becomes the dominant contribution for R=0.6, exceeding the radiative component
- The ratio ΔE_el^(R)/ΔE_el^(0) stays close to unity for small R, drops rapidly at intermediate R, and at R=1 remains at the O(10%) level
- Medium-response contribution (ω<0 sector) reaches ~15% for R=0.6 at L=5 fm for light-quark jets
- Path-length dependence remains close to linear for all R, with only modest finite-size corrections relative to the infinite-medium approximation
- The cone dependence is only weakly sensitive to quark mass, with ratios for light, charm, and bottom nearly coinciding at fixed energy

## Context

This work extends the finite-size HTL formalism of Djordjevic (2006) from single partons to reconstructed jets, motivated by LHC measurements of inclusive jet suppression over a broad range of jet radii (ALICE 2024, ATLAS 2024). It contrasts with existing jet-quenching models that treat collisional energy loss through various approximations: MARTINI (Schenke 2009) uses drag/diffusion with soft recoils absorbed into the medium; PYQUEN/HYDJET++ (Lokhtin 2011) treats collisional loss as explicit out-of-cone; and hybrid approaches (Casalderrey-Solana 2014, 2017) assume rapid thermalization without tracking recoils. The results enable a controlled analytic baseline for jet collisional energy loss, demonstrating that neither extreme assumption, fully out-of-cone loss nor full in-cone recovery, is generally justified, and provide a framework for comparing with transport models like LBT (He 2015) and JEWEL (Elayavalli 2017) that propagate recoil partons explicitly.
