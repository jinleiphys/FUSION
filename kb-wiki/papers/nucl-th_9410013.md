# Variational Monte Carlo Calculations of $^3$H and $^4$He with a relativistic Hamiltonian - II

> In relativistic Hamiltonians the two-nucleon interaction is expressed as a sum of $\tilde{v}_{ij}$, the interaction in the ${\bf P}_{ij}=0$ rest frame, and the ``boost interaction'' $ﾎｴv({\bf P}_{ij})$ which depends upon the total momentum ${\bf P}_{ij}$ and vanishes in the rest frame. The $ﾎｴv$ can be regarded as a sum of four terms: $ﾎｴv_{RE}$, $ﾎｴv_{LC}$, $ﾎｴv_{TP}$ and $ﾎｴv_{QM}$; the first three originate from the relativistic energy-momentum relation, Lorentz contraction and Thomas precession, while the last is purely quantum. The contributions of $ﾎｴv_{RE}$ and $ﾎｴv_{LC}$ have been previously calculated with the variational Monte Carlo method for $^3$H and $^4$He. In this brief note we report the results of similar calculations for the contributions of $ﾎｴv_{TP}$ and $ﾎｴv_{QM}$. These are found to be rather small.

# Variational Monte Carlo Calculations of $^3$H and $^4$He with a Relativistic Hamiltonian - II

## Key claim

The contributions of the Thomas precession term ($\delta v_{TP}$) and the purely quantum term ($\delta v_{QM}$) to the boost interaction in relativistic Hamiltonians for $^3$H and $^4$He are found to be rather small, as expected, and would be exactly zero if there were no two-nucleon P-waves in these nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculated the expectation values of the $\delta v_{TP}$ and $\delta v_{QM}$ terms of the boost interaction using variational Monte Carlo methods with the same variational wave function as in their previous work (Carlson, Pandharipande, and Schiavilla, 1993). The $\tilde{v}_{ij}$ in the $(\boldsymbol{\sigma}_i - \boldsymbol{\sigma}_j)$ term of $\delta v(\mathbf{P}_{ij})$ was approximated by $\tilde{v}_{6,ij}$, the six-operator part of the Urbana $v_{14}$ interaction, which gives more than 98% of the $\langle \tilde{v}_{ij} \rangle$ in these nuclei. The Urbana model VII three-nucleon interaction was used, and its boost correction was neglected.

## Key numbers

- $\langle \sum_{i<j} \delta v_{TP}(\mathbf{P}_{ij}) \rangle$ in $^3$H: 0.016(2) MeV
- $\langle \sum_{i<j} \delta v_{TP}(\mathbf{P}_{ij}) \rangle$ in $^4$He: 0.074(4) MeV
- $\langle \sum_{i<j} \delta v_{QM}(\mathbf{P}_{ij}) \rangle$ in $^3$H: -0.004(2) MeV
- $\langle \sum_{i<j} \delta v_{QM}(\mathbf{P}_{ij}) \rangle$ in $^4$He: -0.014(4) MeV
- For comparison, $\langle \sum_{i<j} \delta v_{RE}(\mathbf{P}_{ij}) \rangle$ in $^3$H: 0.23(2) MeV and in $^4$He: 1.17(3) MeV
- For comparison, $\langle \sum_{i<j} \delta v_{LC}(\mathbf{P}_{ij}) \rangle$ in $^3$H: 0.10(1) MeV and in $^4$He: 0.53(1) MeV
- Total $\langle H \rangle$ for $^3$H: -8.07(3) MeV; for $^4$He: -25.90(8) MeV

## Context

This work extends the variational Monte Carlo calculations of Carlson, Pandharipande, and Schiavilla (1993), which previously computed the contributions of the relativistic energy ($\delta v_{RE}$) and Lorentz contraction ($\delta v_{LC}$) boost terms for $^3$H and $^4$He. The present results for the Thomas precession and quantum terms are much smaller than those earlier contributions, confirming the expectation that the $(\boldsymbol{\sigma}_i - \boldsymbol{\sigma}_j)$ term can be neglected in the boost interaction. Stadler and Gross (1994) estimated these contributions in $^3$H with a different method and obtained similar results. The findings enable further refinement of relativistic Hamiltonians for light nuclei and support the approximation of neglecting these small terms in future calculations.
