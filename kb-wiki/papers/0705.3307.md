# Coulomb dissociation, a tool for nuclear astrophysics

> A short status report on Coulomb dissociation, an indirect method for nuclear astrophysics is given. An analytically solvable approach to study electromagnetic excitation in ^{11}Be, the archetype of a halo nucleus, is proposed.

# Coulomb dissociation, a tool for nuclear astrophysics

## Key claim
Coulomb dissociation is presented as a viable indirect method for determining astrophysical S-factors, but the paper emphasizes that theoretical interpretation of experimental results is always necessary to arrive at an astrophysical S-factor. The authors propose an analytically solvable model for electromagnetic excitation in ¹¹Be that can serve as a benchmark for more sophisticated approaches like the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) model.

## Method
The authors develop a semiclassical sudden-approximation model for Coulomb excitation of neutron halo nuclei, using ¹¹Be as the archetype. They treat the dipole excitation amplitude analytically by expanding in terms of a dimensionless strength parameter y = q_Coul/(q_0 + q_1), calculating the lowest-order and next-to-leading-order contributions to the excitation probability. Total cross sections are obtained by integration over impact parameter with a minimum impact parameter cutoff and an adiabatic cutoff for the lowest-order term.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of ¹¹Be: E_0 = 504 keV (2s_{1/2} ground state) and E_1 = 184 keV (1p_{1/2} bound excited state)
- Core radius R = 2.78 fm, used to determine asymptotic normalization of the p-wave bound state
- Calculated B(E1) value for the 1/2⁺ → 1/2⁻ transition: 0.193 e²fm², compared to experimental value 0.105(12) e²fm² from GANIL data analysis
- Adiabatic cutoff energy ω = 320 keV, the energy of the 1/2⁻ state in ¹¹Be
- Scattering length for p_{1/2} state: a^{j=1/2}_{l=1} = 456 fm³, found from analysis of high-precision ¹¹Be Coulomb dissociation data (Palit)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analytically solvable model for higher-order electromagnetic excitation effects (Typel and Baur, 2001) to include a p-wave bound state, as occurs in ¹¹Be. It provides a simple, transparent alternative to the sophisticated XCDCC calculations of Summers et al. (2007) and can serve as a benchmark for testing more involved reaction models. The effective-range approach to low-lying E1 strength for one-neutron halo nuclei, developed in earlier work by the same authors (Typel and Baur, 2004, 2005), is applied to nuclei like ¹¹Be, ¹⁵C, and ¹⁹C, and the paper discusses future applications to r-process nuclei and 2p-capture processes via Coulomb dissociation of ¹⁷Ne.
