# Chiral Unitary Approach To The N*N*pi, N*N*eta Couplings For The N*(1535) Resonance

> Using a chiral unitary model in which the negative parity nucleon resonance $N^\ast \equiv N^\ast(1535)$ is generated dynamically by means of the Bethe Salpeter equation with coupled meson baryon channels in the $S = 0$ sector, we have obtained the $π^0 N^\ast N^\ast$ and $ηN^\ast N^\ast$ couplings. The $π^0 N^\ast N^\ast$ coupling has smaller strength and the same sign as the $π^0 N N$ coupling. This rules out the mirror assignment of chiral symmetry where the ground state nucleon $N$ and the negative parity resonance $N^\ast$ are envisaged as chiral partners in the baryon sector.

## Key claim

Using a chiral unitary model where the N*(1535) resonance is generated dynamically from coupled meson-baryon channels, the authors find that the pi0 N* N* coupling has smaller strength but the same sign as the pi0 N N coupling, which rules out the mirror assignment of chiral symmetry where the nucleon and N*(1535) would be chiral partners.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation with coupled meson-baryon channels in the S=0 sector (pi- p, pi0 n, eta n, K+ Sigma-, K0 Sigma0, K0 Lambda) using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian and regularization via a cutoff of 1 GeV plus subtraction constants fitted to low-energy scattering data. They extract the pi0 N* N* and eta N* N* couplings by inserting an external meson line into the dynamically generated resonance state and identifying the vertex functions that appear between two t-matrix elements for eta n to eta n scattering.

## Key numbers

* The pi N* N* coupling constant g_pi = +/- 0.55 and the eta N* N* coupling constant g_eta = -/+ 1.73 (relative sign determined from fitting the pi- p to eta n and eta n to eta n amplitudes).
* The partial decay widths of the N*(1535) are Gamma_pi = 43 MeV and Gamma_eta = 67 MeV, giving a total width Gamma_{pi+eta} = 110 MeV.
* The mass of the N*(1535) is found at around 1550 MeV from the peak of |Im t_{eta N to eta N}|, compatible with the PDG range of 1520-1555 MeV.
* The subtraction constants used are a_{pi N} = 35 MeV, a_{eta N} = 16 MeV, a_{K Lambda} = 40 MeV, and a_{K Sigma} = -21 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson-meson scattering (Oset et al., 1990s) and to the Lambda(1405) resonance (Kaiser et al., 1995; Oset and Ramos, 1998) into the S=0 sector where the N*(1535) is generated dynamically. The results contradict the mirror assignment proposed by Jido et al. (1990s) where the nucleon and N*(1535) would be chiral partners with opposite-sign axial couplings, and instead support the naive assignment where they belong to different chiral multiplets. This enables future studies of meson production in nuclei where the chiral assignment affects predicted rates.
