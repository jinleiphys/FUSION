# The s-wave repulsion and deeply bound pionic atoms: fact and fancy

> Fits to a large data set of pionic atoms show that the `missing' s-wave repulsion is accounted for when a density dependence suggested recently by Weise is included in the isovector term of the s-wave pion optical potential. The importance of using large data sets is demonstrated and the role of deeply bound pionic atom states is discussed.

# The s-wave repulsion and deeply bound pionic atoms: fact and fancy

## Key claim
The "missing" s-wave repulsion in pionic atoms is accounted for when a density dependence suggested by Weise (2001) is included in the isovector term of the s-wave pion optical potential, but only when fitting to a large data set of 106 points; fits to restricted data sets cannot provide solid evidence for in-medium enhancement of the isovector amplitude.

## Method
The authors performed global fits of the s-wave pion optical potential to 106 pionic-atom data points, implementing Weise's density-dependent isovector amplitude b1(ρ) and testing three potential models: a conventional potential (C), the Weise prescription (W), and the Weise prescription with a relativistic impulse approximation correction (WB2). They compared these global fits to restricted fits using only 1s states in light N=Z nuclei plus either the deeply bound 1s state of 205Pb or normal 1s states in neighboring N>Z nuclei, using fixed p-wave potentials from their earlier work (Friedman, 2002a).

## Key numbers
- Best-fit b1 for the conventional potential C using the global 1 data set (106 points): −0.113 ± 0.004 mπ⁻¹, enhanced over 20% relative to the free-space value of b1 = −0.0885⁺⁰·⁰⁰¹⁰₋₀·₀₀₂₁ mπ⁻¹ (Schroder et al., 2001)
- Best-fit b1 for the restricted data set of light N=Z nuclei plus deeply bound 205Pb (14 points): −0.113 ± 0.025 mπ⁻¹, with χ²/F = 3.0
- Best-fit b1 for the restricted data set of light N=Z nuclei plus light N>Z nuclei (16 points): −0.096 ± 0.014 mπ⁻¹, with χ²/F = 2.9
- Best-fit b1 for the global 2 data set (114 points, 12C to 238U): −0.112 ± 0.004 mπ⁻¹, with χ²/F = 2.1
- Errors on b0 from global fits: typically ±0.010 mπ⁻¹; errors on b1: ±0.004 mπ⁻¹
- ImB0 from global fits: 0.054 ± 0.002 mπ⁻⁴
- Combined effect of b0, b1, and ReB0 produces net repulsion of about 30 MeV inside heavy nuclei such as Pb

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Friedman (2002) by increasing the data set from 60 to 106 points and including several isotope series, testing Weise's (2001) suggestion that partial restoration of chiral symmetry in dense matter enhances the free pion-nucleon isovector s-wave amplitude b1. The authors contradict claims by Yamazaki et al. (2001) and Gierlik et al. (2002a) that a quadratic-density dispersive term (ReB0) can be excluded, showing that excluding it increases χ² by 90 units for the conventional potential. The work demonstrates that deeply bound pionic atom states in 205Pb do not provide new information beyond normal 1s states, consistent with earlier findings for 207Pb by Friedman and Gal (1998), because the strong repulsion that makes deeply bound states narrow also prevents their wavefunctions from penetrating the nuclear interior more than normal states.
