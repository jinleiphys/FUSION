# Overdamping Phenomena near the Critical Point in O($N$) Model

> We consider the dynamic critical behavior of the propagating mode for the order parameter fluctuation of the O($N$) Ginzburg-Landau theory, involving the canonical momentum as a degree of freedom. We reexamine the renormalization group analysis of the Langevin equation for the propagating mode. We find the fixed point for the propagating mode as well as that for the diffusive one, the former of which is unstable to the latter. This indicates that the propagating mode becomes overdamped near the critical point. We thus can have a sufficient understanding of the phonon mode in the structural phase transition of solids. We also discuss the implication for the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

The propagating mode for the order parameter fluctuation in the O($N$) Ginzburg-Landau theory becomes overdamped and crosses over into a diffusive mode near the critical point, because the fixed point for the propagating mode (with dynamic exponent $z=1$) is unstable to the fixed point for the diffusive mode (with $z=2$). This crossover explains the character change of the phonon mode in structural phase transitions and predicts the fate of the meson mode near the chiral phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed a renormalization group analysis of the Langevin equation for the propagating mode of the O($N$) Ginzburg-Landau model, which includes both the order parameter and its canonical momentum as slow variables. They employed the $\epsilon$-expansion ($\epsilon=4-d$) to lowest order, integrating out short-wavelength fluctuations and applying scale transformations to obtain recursion relations for the static parameters ($r_0$, $u$) and dynamic parameters ($\lambda$, $\Gamma$). The analysis identified two fixed points in the parameter space defined by $\rho \equiv \sqrt{\lambda}\Gamma/\Lambda$: one at $\rho=0$ (propagating mode, $z=1$) and one at $\rho=\infty$ (diffusive mode, $z=2$), and examined the renormalization group flow between them.

## Key numbers

- The propagating fixed point has dynamic critical exponent $z=1$; the diffusive fixed point has $z=2$.
- The expansion parameter is $\epsilon = 4-d$, where $d$ is the spatial dimension.
- The model includes the structural phase transition as the $N=1$ case and the chiral phase transition as the $N=4$ case.
- The recursion relations for dynamic parameters are $\frac{1}{\lambda'} = b^{2-2z}\frac{1}{\lambda}$ and $\Gamma' = b^{2-z}\Gamma$ to lowest order in $\epsilon$.

## Context

This work extends the dynamic universality classification scheme of Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) by showing that the canonical momentum conjugate to the order parameter must be included as a slow variable, making the scheme incomplete as originally formulated. The analysis reconciles two previously conflicting approaches to the chiral phase transition: those treating the meson mode as a diffusive mode (Wilczek, 1992) and those treating it as a propagating mode (Koide, 2003; Ohnishi, 2004). The finding that the propagating fixed point is unstable provides a renormalization-group explanation for the overdamping of the phonon mode observed experimentally in structural phase transitions (Cowley, 1980), and predicts that sigma mesons and pions lose their particle nature near the chiral phase transition.
