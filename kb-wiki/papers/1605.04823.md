# Two-flavor Simulations of the $ρ(770)$ and the Role of the $K\bar K$ Channel

> The $ρ(770)$ meson is the most extensively studied resonance in lattice QCD simulations in two ($N_f=2$) and three ($N_f=2+1$) flavor formulations. We analyze $N_f=2$ lattice scattering data using unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory, allowing not only for the extrapolation in mass but also in flavor, $N_f=2\to N_f=2+1$. The flavor extrapolation requires information from a global fit to $ππ$ and $πK$ phase shifts from experiment. While the chiral extrapolation of $N_f=2$ lattice data leads to masses of the $ρ(770)$ meson far below the experimental one, we find that the missing $K\bar{K}$ channel is able to explain this discrepancy.

## Key claim

The systematically low ρ(770) mass obtained from chiral extrapolations of Nf=2 lattice QCD simulations can be explained by the missing strange quark, specifically the virtual and real contributions of the K̅K channel, even though the ρ meson appears to decouple from K̅K in the elastic region.

## Method

The authors use unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory (UCHPT) in an SU(3) coupled-channel formulation to fit Nf=2 lattice ππ phase shift data, then extrapolate to the physical pion mass (138 MeV). Without changing the fitted parameters, they switch on the ππ→K̅K and K̅K→K̅K transitions, using low-energy constants taken from a global fit to experimental ππ and πK phase shifts. The resulting amplitudes are fitted with a Breit-Wigner parameterization to extract mρ and coupling g.

## Key numbers

- The Nf=2 chiral extrapolation of the GWU lattice data (Mπ=227 and 315 MeV) yields a ρ mass far below the experimental 770 MeV; including the K̅K channel shifts the mass upward by approximately 80 MeV toward the physical value
- For the RQCD data (Bali et al., 2015), including covariances of energy eigenvalues narrows the uncertainty band by about 30%
- Changing the subtraction constant from the default a=-1.28 to a=-0.8 or a=-1.7 changes the final mρ by less than 10 MeV and g by less than 0.08
- Using SU(2)-SU(3) matching relations for low-energy constants gives estimated changes in the ρ mass of less than 1 MeV from neglected ππ→ππ modifications

## Context

This work extends previous Nf=2 lattice studies (Aoki et al., 2007; Gockeler et al., 2008; Feng et al., 2010; Lang et al., 2011; Bali et al., 2015; Guo et al., 2016) that found systematically low ρ masses, and provides a mechanism for the discrepancy with experiment. The analysis uses the inverse amplitude method of Oller (1998) and builds on chiral extrapolation techniques from Hanhart (2008), Nebreda (2010), and Pelaez (2010). The results enable future Nf=2+1 lattice simulations to be directly extrapolated to the physical point with the K̅K channel properly accounted for, and motivate a full one-loop calculation for confirmation.
