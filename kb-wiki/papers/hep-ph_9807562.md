# Virtual Compton Scattering--Generalized Polarizabilities of Nucleons and Pions

> Virtual Compton scattering off nucleons and pions at low energies is discussed. Predictions for the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon are presented within the framework of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory and the linear sigma model. First results for the generalized polarizabilities of the charged pion in chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^4)$ are shown.

## Key claim

Predictions for the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon and charged pion are presented within chiral perturbation theory and the linear sigma model, but the nucleon results are at third order in external momenta (O(p^3)) and the pion results are at fourth order (O(p^4)), meaning the nucleon predictions are lower-order than the pion ones and both are subject to higher-order corrections.

## Method

The author calculates the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude for nucleons using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to O(p^3) and the linear sigma model (LSM) at one-loop level, extracting the generalized polarizabilities from the model-dependent part of the amplitude beyond the low-energy theorem. For the charged pion, the VCS amplitude is computed at O(p^4) in chiral perturbation theory, and the generalized polarizabilities are obtained by applying the multipole decomposition procedure of Drechsel et al. (1997). The pion calculation uses the LET of Fearing (1998) as a starting point and yields the full q^2 dependence of the electric and magnetic generalized polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- Charged pion electric polarizability at zero momentum transfer: alpha = (2.68 ± 0.42) × 10^(-4) fm^3, determined from the O(p^4) counter term combination 2l^r_5 - l^r_6 via the decay pi+ → e+ nu_e gamma.
- At O(p^6), corrections to the charged pion polarizabilities are 12% of the O(p^4) value for alpha and 24% for beta.
- The pion decay constant used is F_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- The fine-structure constant is given as e^2/4π ≈ 1/137.
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation m_N = g_piN F_pi holds in the linear sigma model with g_A = 1.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorems for real Compton scattering (Low 1954; Gell-Mann and Goldberger 1954) to the virtual photon case, following the model-independent analysis of Guichon et al. (1995) and Scherer (1996). The nucleon predictions are compared with the constituent quark model (Guichon et al. 1995), an effective Lagrangian model (Vanderhaeghen et al. 1996), and earlier chiral perturbation theory results (Hemmert et al. 1997a). The pion results are the first O(p^4) predictions for generalized polarizabilities and are directly relevant to the Fermilab E781 SELEX experiment (Moinester), which uses a 600 GeV pion beam to study VCS on atomic electrons.
