# Probing gluon Bose correlations in DIS

> We study correlations originating from the quantum nature of gluons in a hadronic wave function. Bose-Einstein correlation between identical particles lead to the enhancement in the number of pairs of gluons with the same quantum numbers and small relative momentum. We show that these preexisting correlations can be probed in Deep Inelastic Scattering experiments at high energy. Specifically, we consider diffractive dijet plus a third jet production. The azimuthal dependence displays a peak at the zero relative angle between the transverse momentum imbalance of the photon-going dijet and the transverse momentum of the hadron-going jet. Our calculations explicitly show that the peak originates from Bose enhancement. Comparing electron-proton to electron-nucleus collisions, we demonstrate that the nuclear target enhances the relative strength of the peak. With the future high luminosity Electron-Ion Collider the proposed measurements of gluon Bose enhancement become experimentally feasible.

## Key claim

The paper shows that Bose-Einstein correlations between identical gluons in a hadronic wave function can be probed in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) by measuring diffractive trijet production, specifically a peak at zero relative angle between the transverse momentum imbalance of the photon-going dijet and the transverse momentum of the hadron-going jet; the effect is enhanced by saturation in the target, but the peak is only visible in a restricted kinematic window where the momentum of the third jet is not too large relative to the dijet imbalance.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for diffractive production of a color-singlet quark-antiquark dijet plus a third gluon jet in high-energy electron-hadron collisions, using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory. The calculation expands the eikonal S-matrix to second order in the coupling (two-gluon exchange) and includes a dressing operator to account for final-state radiation from the hadron side. The hadron's gluon correlations are modeled via the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, and the dipole wavefunction for the virtual photon is used to describe the quark-antiquark pair. The observable is the triple-differential number of jets as a function of the azimuthal angle between the dijet momentum imbalance and the third jet momentum, with the magnitudes of these vectors set equal.

## Key numbers

- The peak at zero azimuthal angle is demonstrated for transverse momentum magnitudes \(|\vec{\Delta}| = |\vec{p}_3| = 10\) GeV, with \(p_1 = 10\) GeV, \(Q_s = 2\) GeV, and \(Q = 1\) GeV (Fig. 2).
- The zero-angle peak is present when the second quark jet momentum \(p_2 \lesssim 4\) GeV (Fig. 2); for \(p_2 \gtrsim 4\) GeV the peak is suppressed.
- The peak persists when scaling \(p_3 = \Delta\) between 5 and 15 GeV, and for \(p_1\) values between 11 and 15 GeV.
- The infrared regulator in the hadron part is varied in the range \(10^{-4}\) to \(0.2\) GeV without affecting numerical results.
- The saturation momentum of the hadron is taken as \(Q_s = 2\) GeV for the nuclear target; increasing \(Q_s\) enhances the relative strength of the peak (Fig. 4).

## Context

This work extends the study of Bose-Einstein correlations in gluon distributions, previously suggested as a mechanism for ridge correlations in proton-proton scattering by Altinoluk et al. (2015) and Kovner et al. (2018), to a cleaner DIS environment where final-state interactions are minimized. It proposes a new observable, diffractive trijet production with a rapidity gap, that is distinct from the trijet configurations considered by Boussarie et al. (2016) and Iancu et al. (2021), where all three jets are on the photon side. The results enable experimental searches at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), showing that the Bose enhancement signal becomes more pronounced with increasing nuclear saturation scale, providing a new probe of saturation physics.
