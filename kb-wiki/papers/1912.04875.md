# Radiative contribution to $p_{\perp}$-broadening of fast partons in a quark-gluon plasma

> The contribution of radiative processes to $p_\perp$-broadening of fast partons in a quark-gluon plasma is investigated. Calculations are performed beyond the soft gluon approximation. It is shown that the radiative correction to $\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle$ for conditions of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC is negative and can be comparable in absolute value with the nonradiative contribution. This prediction differs radically from the essentially positive contribution of radiative processes to $p_{\perp}$-broadening, which was predicted earlier in the literature.

## Key claim

The radiative correction to $\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle$ for fast partons in a quark-gluon plasma is negative and can be comparable in absolute value to the nonradiative contribution under RHIC and LHC conditions, contradicting earlier predictions of an essentially positive radiative contribution. This negative contribution arises from rescatterings of the initial parton and is not local, meaning it cannot be interpreted as a renormalization of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$.

## Method

The author used the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach, developed in earlier work (Zakharov, 1996, 2005), to calculate the double differential spectrum in the Feynman variable $x$ and transverse momentum for induced $a \to bc$ transitions in a homogeneous quark-gluon plasma. Calculations were performed beyond the soft gluon approximation, using the oscillator approximation for the dipole cross section. The radiative contribution to $p_\perp$-broadening was evaluated by analyzing the virtual process $a \to bc \to a$ and integrating over the phase space of the emitted gluon.

## Key numbers

- For typical parton path length $L \sim 5$ fm in the QGP for central heavy ion collisions, the radiative contribution to $\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle$ is negative and comparable in absolute value with the nonradiative contribution $\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle = \hat{q}L$.
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ at RHIC and LHC is approximately $0.1$ to $0.2$ in central collisions for particles with $p_{\perp} \sim 10$ to $20$ GeV.
- The deconfinement temperature is $T_c \approx 160$ MeV, and the QGP temperature in heavy ion collisions is $2$ to $4$ times higher than $T_c$.
- The proper time for QGP formation is $\tau \sim 0.5$ to $1$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier LCPI formalism (Zakharov, 1996, 2005) for induced gluon emission to the problem of $p_\perp$-broadening, and directly contradicts the earlier prediction by Mueller (2000) of a positive double-logarithmic radiative contribution to $\langle p_{\perp}^2\rangle$ that could be interpreted as renormalization of $\hat{q}$. The results imply that extracting $\hat{q}$ from jet $p_\perp$-broadening measurements at RHIC and LHC requires accounting for this negative radiative correction, and that such measurements could provide complementary information to the $\hat{q}$ values extracted from $R_{AA}$ data.
