# f0(980) and a0(980) resonances near γγ\to K^+K^- and γγ\to K^0\bar K^0 reaction thresholds

> High-statistics data on the reactions $γγ\to K^+K^-$ and $γγ\to K^0\bar K^0$ are the last missing link in investigations of the light scalar mesons $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ in photon-photon collisions. It is believed that $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances exhibit their four-quark structure in these reactions in a vary peculiar way. The work estimates the feasibility of measurements of scalar contributions near $γγ\to K^+K^-$ and $γγ\to K^0\bar K^0$ reaction thresholds at modern colliders.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that scalar contributions from the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances can be detected in the $\gamma\gamma\to K^+K^-$ cross section near threshold at the level of 5–10 nb, provided that the four-quark rescattering mechanism suppresses the large Born $S$-wave background; for $\gamma\gamma\to K^0\bar K^0$, the cross section should be at the level of $\lesssim$ 1 nb due to cancellation between isospin channels.

## Method

The authors model the $\gamma\gamma\to K^+K^-$ and $\gamma\gamma\to K^0\bar K^0$ reactions using an $S$-wave amplitude that includes the Born one-kaon exchange contribution (for the charged channel) modified by a rescattering factor $\xi(s)=[1+i\rho_{K^+}(s)T_{K^+K^-\to K^+K^-}(s)]$, where the $T$-matrix is dominated by $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances with strong $K\bar K$ couplings. They then estimate the number of two-photon events $N_{eeK^+K^-}$ using the effective $\gamma\gamma$ luminosity formula for $e^+e^-$ colliders, integrating over $\sqrt{s}$ intervals from $2m_{K^+}$ to 1.05 GeV and to 1.1 GeV, for several modern detector configurations.

## Key numbers

- The estimated $\gamma\gamma\to K^+K^-$ cross section from scalar contributions near threshold is 5–10 nb for $2m_{K^+}<\sqrt{s}<1.1$ GeV.
- The estimated $\gamma\gamma\to K^0\bar K^0$ cross section near threshold is $\lesssim$ 1 nb.
- For $E_{cm}=2$ GeV and $L_{ee}=1$ fb$^{-1}$ (VEPP-2000), the expected number of $S$-wave $K^+K^-$ events is $0.56\times10^3$ for $\sqrt{s}<1.05$ GeV and $0.74\times10^3$ for $\sqrt{s}<1.1$ GeV.
- For $E_{cm}=2.4$ GeV and $L_{ee}=5$ fb$^{-1}$ (DA$\Phi$NE), the expected number is $4.1\times10^3$ and $5.5\times10^3$, respectively.
- For $E_{cm}=3.77$ GeV and $L_{ee}=5$ fb$^{-1}$ (BEPC II), the expected number is $8.7\times10^3$ and $11.7\times10^3$, respectively.
- For $E_{cm}=10.58$ GeV and $L_{ee}=100$ fb$^{-1}$ (KEKB or PEP-II), the expected number is $5.1\times10^5$ and $6.9\times10^5$, respectively.
- The $\gamma\gamma\to K^0\bar K^0$ event rate is expected to be at least an order of magnitude smaller than for $K^+K^-$.

## Context

This work extends the program of high-statistics two-photon measurements of light scalar mesons performed by the Belle Collaboration (2007–2010) for $\pi^+\pi^-$, $\pi^0\pi^0$, $\pi^0\eta$, and $\eta\eta$ final states, and by BABAR (2011) for $\pi^0\eta$, to the missing $K^+K^-$ and $K^0\bar K^0$ channels. The authors build on their earlier theoretical framework (Achasov and Shestakov, 1991–1994) showing that the four-quark nature of $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ leads to suppression of the Born $S$-wave background via rescattering, and they provide event-rate estimates for upcoming experiments at BES-III (BEPC II), KLOE-2 (DA$\Phi$NE), CMD-3 and SND (VEPP-2000), and KEDR (VEPP-4M). Establishing an upper limit on the $S$-wave $\gamma\gamma\to K^0\bar K^0$ cross section would discriminate between theoretical models of the scalar mesons.
