# A note on recursive calculations of particular 9j coefficients

> The calculation of angular-momentum coupling transformation matrices can be very time consuming and alternative methods, even if they apply only in special cases, are helpful. We present a recursion relation for the calculation of particular 9j symbols used in the quantum theory of angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper presents a recursion relation for calculating particular $9j$ symbols of the form $\neufj{\ell_1}{\ell_2}{L}{j_1}{j_2}{L'}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{1}$ (with $L' = L, L\pm 1$), but the relation is only valid for these special cases where two of the angular momenta are fixed at $\nicefrac{1}{2}$ and one at $1$, and the recursion works specifically for the reduced coefficient $Q_L = \sqrt{2L+1}\neufj{\ell_1}{\ell_2}{L}{j_1}{j_2}{L}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{1}$.

## Method

The author derives a three-term recursion relation for the reduced $9j$ coefficient $Q_L$ by starting from an identity relating the $9j$ symbol to a $6j$ symbol via $\neufj{\ell_1}{\ell_2}{L}{j_1}{j_2}{L}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{1} = \frac{(-1)^{j_1+\ell_2+L+\nicefrac{1}{2}}}{\sqrt{6\bar{L}(2L+1)}}(\bar{\ell_1}+\bar{j_2}-\bar{j_1}-\bar{\ell_2})\sixj{\ell_1}{\ell_2}{L}{j_2}{j_1}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}$ and then applying the known recursion relation for $6j$ symbols from Schulten and Gordon (1975). The recursion is then reformulated in terms of the ratio $R_L = Q_L/Q_{L-1}$ for numerical stability with large arguments.

## Key numbers

- The recursion relation for $Q_L$ is: $D_{\ell_1,\ell_2,j_1,j_2}(L+1) Q_{L+1} - E_{\ell_1,\ell_2,j_1,j_2}(L) Q_L + D_{\ell_1,\ell_2,j_1,j_2}(L) Q_{L-1} = 0$, where $D$ and $E$ are explicit functions of the angular momentum arguments.
- The ratio recursion is $R_{L+1} = F_{\ell_1,\ell_2,j_1,j_2}(L) R_L + G_{\ell_1,\ell_2,j_1,j_2}(L)$ with $F = E/D(L+1)$ and $G = -D(L)/D(L+1)$.
- The $9j$ symbol $\neufj{\ell_1}{\ell_2}{L}{j_1}{j_2}{L-1}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{\nicefrac{1}{2}}{1}$ is expressed in terms of two $6j$ symbols with a prefactor $\sqrt{6L(4L^2-1)}$.

## Context

This work extends the general recursion relations for $9j$ symbols introduced by Karosiene (1965), Jucys and Bandzaitis (1977), and Varshalovich (1988), but focuses on the special case where two angular momenta are $\nicefrac{1}{2}$ and one is $1$, which is frequently encountered in atomic and nuclear physics (e.g., in $\beta$-decay calculations as noted by deShalit (1963) and Szybisz (1975)). The recursion enables efficient computation of these coefficients without evaluating the triple-sum series of Jucys and Bandzaitis (1977), and complements the tabulated algebraic expressions in table 10.3 of Varshalovich (1988) and the work of Wei (1998, 1999) on binomial coefficient algorithms.
