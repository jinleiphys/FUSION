# Edge and bulk components of lowest-Landau-level orbitals, correlated fractional quantum Hall effect incompressible states, and insulating behavior in finite graphene samples

> Many-body calculations of the total energy of interacting Dirac electrons in finite graphene samples exhibit joint occurrence of cusps at angular momenta corresponding to fractional fillings characteristic of formation of incompressible (gapped) correlated states (nu=1/3 in particular) and opening of an insulating energy gap (that increases with the magnetic field) at the Dirac point, in correspondence with experiments. Single-particle basis functions obeying the zigzag boundary condition at the sample edge are employed in exact diagonalization of the interelectron Coulomb interaction, showing, at all sizes, mixed equal-weight bulk and edge components. The consequent depletion of the bulk electron density attenuates the fractional-quantum-Hall-effect excitation energies and the edge charge accumulation results in a gap in the many-body spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the joint occurrence of fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE) incompressible states and insulating behavior in finite graphene samples arises from the mixed bulk-edge character of lowest-Landau-level (LLL) Dirac spinors, where the bulk and edge components have equal weights independent of sample size, leading to a 50% depletion of bulk electron density that attenuates FQHE excitation energies by a factor of four compared to boundless-graphene models.

## Method

The authors performed exact diagonalization (EXD) of the many-body Hamiltonian for interacting Dirac electrons occupying the LLL of finite circular graphene samples with zigzag boundary conditions (ZBC). Single-particle basis functions were constructed from solutions of the Dirac-Weyl equation using Kummer confluent hypergeometric functions, yielding spinors with equal-weight bulk (Darwin-Fock-type) and edge components. The two-body Coulomb interaction matrix elements were evaluated including bulk, edge, and cross bulk-edge contributions, each reduced by a factor of 1/4 due to the spinor structure.

## Key numbers

- FQHE excitation energy gap for the bulk component at nu=1/3: 0.0153 e^2/kappa l_B (N=6, M=45), 0.0160 (N=7, M=63), 0.0145 (N=8, M=84), 0.0150 (N=9, M=108), 0.0149 (N=10, M=135), 0.0147 (N=11, M=165)
- Extrapolated excitation energy gap for N to infinity: 0.0137 e^2/kappa l_B with standard error +/- 0.0009
- Experimentally measured value from Andrei (2009) and Kim (2009): 0.008 e^2/kappa l_B
- Insulating gap mu at nu=0 scales as B^gamma with gamma approximately 0.54 for R=1 micrometer
- Single-particle energy for m=3 (l=-3) at R=15 l_B: epsilon = 4.383 x 10^-22 (dimensionless)
- Single-particle energy for m=90 at R=15 l_B: epsilon = 0.349 (dimensionless)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical explorations of FQHE in graphene by Jain (2006) and Chakraborty (2006) who used spherical geometry and boundless-graphene models, but systematically overestimated measured FQHE excitation energies. The paper also addresses the joint occurrence of FQHE and insulating behavior observed experimentally by Andrei (2009) and Kim (2009) in small suspended single-sheet graphene samples. The findings enable future controlled treatments of graphene-sample boundaries and demonstrate that the graphene edge uniquely modulates interelectron interactions governing correlated many-body states, contrasting the common expectation that bulk properties are unaffected by surface conditions.
