# QCD Equation of State with $N_f=3$ Flavors up to the Electroweak Scale

> The equation of state of Quantum Chromodynamics with $N_f=3$ flavors is determined non-perturbatively in the range of temperatures between $3$ and $165$~GeV with a precision of about $0.5$-$1.0$\%. The calculation is carried out by numerical simulations of lattice gauge theory discretized à la Wilson with shifted boundary conditions in the compact direction. At each given temperature the entropy density is computed at several lattice spacings in order to extrapolate the results to the continuum limit. Taken at face value, data point straight to the Stefan-Boltzmann value by following a linear behavior in the strong coupling constant squared. They are also compatible with the known perturbative formula supplemented by higher order terms in the coupling constant, a parametrization which describes well our data together with those present in the literature down to $500$ MeV.

# QCD Equation of State with \(N_f=3\) Flavors up to the Electroweak Scale

## Key claim
The entropy density of QCD with three massless quark flavors is determined non-perturbatively from 3 GeV to 165 GeV with 0.5–1.0% precision, and the data are compatible with a linear behavior in the strong coupling constant squared when extrapolated to the Stefan-Boltzmann value; however, contributions beyond known perturbative orders are required to explain the data even at these very high temperatures.

## Method
The entropy density is computed via lattice QCD simulations using Wilson fermions with shifted boundary conditions in the compact direction, which avoids the zero-temperature subtraction of ultraviolet power divergences. The theory is renormalized by fixing a non-perturbatively defined Schrödinger functional running coupling at each temperature using step-scaling techniques, establishing lines of constant physics. At each of nine temperatures, the entropy density is evaluated at several lattice spacings (with \(L_0/a = 4, 6, 8, 10\)) and extrapolated to the continuum limit; the calculation involves computing the chiral condensate at 20 quark masses for the fermionic contribution and pure gauge simulations for the static quark limit.

## Key numbers
- Temperatures studied: \(T_0 = 165(6)\) GeV down to \(T_8 = 3.04(8)\) GeV (9 values total).
- Continuum entropy density at highest temperature: \(s/T^3 = 20.13(8)\) at \(T = 165(6)\) GeV.
- Continuum entropy density at lowest temperature: \(s/T^3 = 19.58(17)\) at \(T = 3.04(8)\) GeV.
- Stefan-Boltzmann value: \(s^{\rm SB}_0 = 2.969\) (normalized as \(32\pi^2/45 \times s_0\)).
- Best fit to Eq. (4) with \(s_0 = s^{\rm SB}_0\) gives \(s_2 = -5.1(9)\), \(s_3 = 5(5)\).
- Best parametrization for \(T \geq 500\) MeV yields \(q_c = -4.0(1.1)\times 10^3\) and \(s_7 = 7(4)\times 10^3\) with \(\chi^2/\chi^2_{\rm exp} = 0.79\).
- Combined result at 500 MeV: \(s/T^3 = 17.31(16)\) from averaging Refs. Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2017).

## Context
This work extends non-perturbative QCD equation-of-state calculations from the previously accessible range up to about 1 GeV (Borsanyi 2013, HotQCD 2014, Bazavov 2017) to the electroweak scale, where only perturbative expansions (Kajantie 2002) existed with poor convergence. It applies the renormalization strategy developed by Dalla Brida (2021) for screening masses to the entropy density, and enables precise cosmological predictions for the early universe, including gravitational wave backgrounds and dark matter abundance calculations, as well as providing essential input for heavy-ion collision phenomenology. The method is fully general and can be extended to QCD with four or five massive quarks.
