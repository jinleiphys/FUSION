# Importance of $α$-induced reactions and the inverse $(γ,α)$ on $p$-nuclei

> We have calculated astrophysical reaction cross-sections for $(γ,α)$ reactions of some nuclei important for the calculation of $p$-process reaction-decay network. Reaction rates for $α$-induced reactions are calculated with the semi-microscopic optical potential constructed using double folding method, where nuclear density distributions for finite nuclei along with the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction are the important components of the folded potential. For this purpose density distributions of target nuclei are obtained from relativistic mean field approach. Astrophysical reaction cross section for elastic scattering of $α$-particle from $^{92}$Mo target is compared with the existing experimental results to constrain the newly formed potential. Further, to check the credibility of the present theoretical framework, the astrophysical S-factor for ($α$,$γ$) reactions are compared with the experimental observation, wherever available. Finally, an estimate of dominant photodisintegration channels at various astrophysical temperature is discussed for $p$-nuclei $^{74}$Se and $^{96}$Ru.

## Key claim

The paper claims that semi-microscopic optical potentials constructed via double folding with densities from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations can reliably predict astrophysical reaction rates for \((\gamma,\alpha)\) and inverse \((\alpha,\gamma)\) reactions on \(p\)-nuclei, but the model parameters (real and imaginary potential scaling factors of 0.7 and 0.3) were fixed by fitting elastic scattering data for only one target (\({}^{92}\)Mo) and then applied universally without further adjustment.

## Method

The author constructed a semi-microscopic optical potential for \(\alpha\)-particle induced reactions using the double folding method, folding the M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with the density distribution of the \(\alpha\)-particle and target nuclei. Target densities were obtained from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the FSU Gold parameter set. The folded potential was implemented in the TALYS 1.8 code (modified to include the M3Y interaction) and the DFPOT code, and the real and imaginary parts were scaled by factors 0.7 and 0.3 respectively to fit elastic scattering data for \({}^{92}\)Mo\((\alpha,\alpha)^{92}\)Mo. Astrophysical S-factors and reaction rates were then calculated for \((\alpha,\gamma)\) reactions and, via the reciprocity theorem, for the inverse \((\gamma,\alpha)\) photodisintegration channels.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering cross sections for \({}^{92}\)Mo\((\alpha,\alpha)^{92}\)Mo were fitted by multiplying the double folded potential by factors 0.7 (real part) and 0.3 (imaginary part).
- For \({}^{74}\)Se at 2 GK temperature, the \((\gamma,\alpha)\) reaction rate is greater by \(\sim 10^2\) times than the \((\gamma,p)\) rate and \(\sim 10^4\) times than the \((\gamma,n)\) rate.
- The Gamow peak energy for \({}^{92}\)Mo+\(\alpha\) lies between 5.8 and 7.6 MeV for temperatures of 2-3 GK; for \({}^{151}\)Eu+\(\alpha\) it lies between 7.7 and 10.0 MeV in the same temperature window.
- For \({}^{74}\)Se, the \((\gamma,\alpha)\) channel dominates below about 4 GK, while around 4 GK all three photodisintegration channels become comparable; for \({}^{96}\)Ru, \((\gamma,\alpha)\) dominates at low temperature, \((\gamma,p)\) leads around 3 GK, and \((\gamma,n)\) dominates above 4 GK.

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic optical model approach previously applied to proton-nucleus and neutron-nucleus potentials (Lahiri and Gangopadhyay, earlier works; Dutta et al.) to \(\alpha\)-particle induced reactions relevant to the \(p\)-process nucleosynthesis network. It addresses the large uncertainties in statistical model calculations of \((\gamma,\alpha)\) reactions identified by Rapp et al. (2006) and Rauscher and Thielemann (earlier works), and provides reaction rates for key \(p\)-nuclei \({}^{74}\)Se and \({}^{96}\)Ru that can be used in network calculations to determine the dominant photodisintegration channels as a function of temperature. The results enable improved modeling of the \(p\)-process path, particularly the competition between \((\gamma,\alpha)\), \((\gamma,p)\), and \((\gamma,n)\) channels in the temperature range 2-4 GK characteristic of Type-II supernova environments.
