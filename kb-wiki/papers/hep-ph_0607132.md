# Hard exclusive reactions and hadron structure

> The generalized Bjorken regime of exclusive reactions opens new ways to explore the hadron structure. We shortly review the present status of this domain where generalized parton distributions, generalized distribution amplitudes and transition distribution amplitudes describe various aspects of confinement physics.

## Key claim

Exclusive reactions in the generalized Bjorken regime allow factorization of amplitudes into perturbative subprocesses and hadronic matrix elements, but the applicability of this framework is "quite precocious in terms of \(Q^2\)" based on experimental data from DESY and JLab, meaning the framework works at lower momentum transfers than originally expected.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical status of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), and transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) as tools to describe hadron structure in hard exclusive reactions. They present specific calculations for accessing transversity GPDs via the process \(\gamma^{(*)} p \to \rho_L^0 \rho_T^+ n\) using collinear factorization with two-gluon exchange, and for electroproduction of exotic mesons with \(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\) using leading-twist distribution amplitudes. They also define TDAs for processes such as backward virtual Compton scattering and \(\bar p p \to \gamma^* \gamma\), factorizing the amplitude into a hard scattering part and a three-quark exchange.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for \(\gamma^*_{L/T}(Q) p \to \rho^0_L \rho^+_T n\) is plotted as a function of \(p_T^2\) for \(\xi=0.3\) and \(Q^2 = 0, 1, 5, 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- The JLab CLAS-12 upgrade can observe two rho mesons only for relatively low \(p_T\) of the order of 1–1.5 GeV.
- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV is used in the definition of the leading twist TDAs for the \(p \to \pi^0\) transition.

## Context

This review extends the framework of generalized parton distributions (Muller, 1994) and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDA, 1999) to include transversity GPDs, exotic meson production, and transition distribution amplitudes (TDA, 2005). The work enables future experimental searches at eRHIC, JLab CLAS-12, and GSI-FAIR (PAX collaboration) for transversity GPDs, exotic mesons, and backward VCS processes. The impact picture of TDAs provides a new way to probe the transverse spatial structure of hadrons, complementing the spatial information obtained from GPDs (Impact, 2004).
