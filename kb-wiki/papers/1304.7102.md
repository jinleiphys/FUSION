# From field theory to superfluid hydrodynamics of dense quark matter

> Hydrodynamics of superfluids can be described by formally dividing the fluid into a normal fluid and a superfluid part. In color-flavor locked quark matter, at least one superfluid component is present due to spontaneous breaking of baryon number conservation, and an additional one due to the breaking of strangeness arises once one takes into account kaon condensation. We show how such a two-component description emerges from an underlying scalar field theory which can be viewed as an effective theory for kaons. Furthermore, the occurring hydrodynamic quantities in the low-temperature limit are related to the microscopic parameters provided by the Lagrangian which closes the gap between field theory and hydrodynamics, which are important for astrophysical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates how a two-component superfluid hydrodynamic description of kaon-condensed color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter emerges from an underlying scalar field theory, but only under the restrictive assumptions of small coupling, low temperature (much below the critical temperature and \(T\ll\mu\)), and a constant condensate modulus.

## Method

The authors start from a complex scalar \(\varphi^{4}\) model interpreted as an effective theory for kaons, introduce a condensate with constant modulus, and derive zero-temperature expressions for the Noether current and stress-energy tensor. At finite temperature, they use a one-loop effective action in the Matsubara formalism, then connect the resulting field-theoretic quantities to the canonical hydrodynamic formalism of Carter and the two-fluid Landau framework via entrainment coefficients \(\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},\mathcal{C}\).

## Key numbers

* Normal fluid density in the low-temperature limit: \(n_{n}\simeq\frac{4\pi^{2}}{5\sqrt{3}}\frac{T^{4}}{\mu}\frac{(1-\vec{v}_{s}^{2})^{2}}{(1-3\vec{v}_{s}^{2})^{3}}\) with a \(T^{6}/\mu^{3}\) correction term.
* Critical superflow velocity: \(v_{crit}=1/\sqrt{3}\).
* The maximum chemical potential inside a compact star is estimated as \(\mu_{star}\leq500\,\mathrm{MeV}\), while perturbative QCD calculations are only valid at \(\mu\simeq10^{8}\,\mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic two-fluid formalism originally developed by Landau for superfluid helium and the canonical framework of Carter to the specific case of kaon-condensed CFL quark matter. It builds upon earlier effective field theory treatments of kaons in CFL by Alford (2008) and the zero-temperature derivation by Son (2001). The results enable astrophysical calculations of superfluid hydrodynamics in compact stars, including sound velocities and transport properties, and provide a foundation for future numerical studies using the two-particle irreducible effective action to go beyond the low-temperature approximation.
