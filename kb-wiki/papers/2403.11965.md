# Effects of strangeness on the chiral pseudocritical line

> Within a 2+1 flavor Nambu\textendash Jona-Lasinio model, we calculate the curvature coefficients and check them against available lattice QCD estimations. With the observation that the flavor mixing due to the `t Hooft determinant term significantly affects the $κ_{2}^{S}$, we explore the effect of $μ_{S}$ on the $T-μ_{B}$ crossover lines. With the novel determination of negative $κ_{2}^{B}$ at large $μ_{S}$, we advocate the importance of studying the same in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Within a 2+1 flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, the curvature coefficient κ₂ᴮ becomes negative at sufficiently large strangeness chemical potential μₛ, a novel result that the authors advocate should be checked by lattice QCD simulations; this behavior is strongly dependent on the 't Hooft flavor-mixing coupling G_d, which is the most poorly constrained parameter in the three-flavor NJL model.

## Method

The authors use a 2+1 flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with isospin symmetry (μ_Q = 0) and two standard parameter sets (Set I from Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) and Set II from Rehberg et al. (1995)) that differ by about 30% in the dimensionless 't Hooft coupling G_dΛ⁵. They compute the pseudocritical temperature T_pc as the inflection point of the light-quark condensate as a function of temperature, for fixed baryon chemical potential μ_B and strangeness chemical potential μ_S, and extract curvature coefficients κ₂ and κ₄ by fitting the T_pc(μ_X) line to a Taylor expansion up to fourth order in μ_X/T_pc(0), restricting to μ_X/T_pc(0) ≤ 1.0.

## Key numbers

- T_pc(0) = 171.1 MeV for parameter Set I and 173.4 MeV for Set II
- κ₂ᴮ (μ_S = 0): 0.01627 (Set I), 0.01619 (Set II); lattice QCD (HotQCD 2018) gives 0.016(6)
- κ₂ˢ (μ_B = 0): 0.01345 (Set I), 0.01719 (Set II); lattice QCD (HotQCD 2018) gives 0.017(5)
- κ₂ᴮ along strangeness neutrality (n_S = 0): 0.01478 (Set I), 0.01350 (Set II); lattice QCD gives 0.012(4) (HotQCD 2018) and 0.0153(18) (Borsanyi 2020)
- κ₂ᴮ becomes negative at a critical μₛᶜ that differs between parameter sets, with Set II (larger G_d) showing a faster decrease
- κ₄ᴮ (μ_S = 0): 0.00006 (Set I), 0.00005 (Set II); lattice QCD gives 0.001(7) (HotQCD 2018)
- μ_S is varied from 0 to 200 MeV, restricted to half the kaon mass to avoid kaon condensation

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the chiral pseudocritical line within the NJL model (Buballa 2003) and lattice QCD (HotQCD 2018, Borsanyi 2020) by systematically exploring the effect of finite strangeness chemical potential on the curvature coefficients, which had not been done before in the 2+1 flavor NJL framework. The authors show that the flavor-mixing 't Hooft term, which was previously studied in two-flavor NJL for isospin-sensitive observables (Ali 2020, Ali 2021), can be constrained by comparing κ₂ˢ and the μ_S-dependence of κ₂ᴮ against future lattice QCD calculations at large μ_S (Ding 2024). The negative κ₂ᴮ at large μ_S is a novel prediction that, if confirmed by lattice QCD, would provide a way to determine the poorly constrained 't Hooft coupling G_d and improve effective models of QCD.
