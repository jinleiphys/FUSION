# Model-Independent Extraction of the N*(1535) Electrostrong Form Factor from Eta Electroproduction

> We analyze the existing data on electroproduction of eta mesons in the region of W~1.5GeV, and extract an electrostrong form factor for the N*(1535) electroexcitation and decay into the eta-N channel, which is found to be relatively insensitive to the uncertainties of the effective Lagrangian approach. this extracted quantity is of interest in the QCD description of relevant baryons.

# Model-Independent Extraction of the N*(1535) Electrostrong Form Factor from Eta Electroproduction

## Key claim

The paper extracts an electrostrong form factor for the N*(1535) resonance from existing eta electroproduction data that is relatively insensitive to uncertainties in the effective Lagrangian approach, but the extraction depends on assumed scenarios for the ratio of longitudinal to transverse helicity amplitudes (S1/2/A1/2) because the existing data are not accurate enough to determine the longitudinal strength independently.

## Method

The authors analyze existing differential cross section data for the reaction e + p → e' + p + η at center-of-mass energy W ≈ 1.5 GeV using an effective Lagrangian approach with pseudoscalar coupling at the ηNN*(1535) vertex. They fix Born terms for nucleon and vector meson exchanges with dipole form factors and vector dominance parametrization, then fit the data to determine the transverse helicity amplitude A1/2(Q2) under three different assumptions for the ratio RLT = S1/2/A1/2 (zero, quark shell model value, and value from Stanley and Weber). The extracted quantity ξT combines the transverse helicity amplitude with the strong decay amplitude for N* → pη.

## Key numbers

- The extracted electrostrong form factor ξT at Q2 = 0 from new Mainz photoproduction data is 2.20 ± 0.15 in units of 10^{-1} GeV^{-1}
- The analysis covers the region of center-of-mass energy W ≈ 1.5 GeV, corresponding to the N*(1535) S11 resonance with JπI = (1/2)-(1/2)
- The vector meson electromagnetic form factors are parametrized as G_{Vγη}(k2) = (1 - k2/m2V)^{-1} with mV ≈ (1/2)(mρ + mω)
- The onset of perturbative QCD scaling is suggested from crude experimental evidence around Q2 = 4-6 GeV2

## Context

This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian studies of real photon eta production (Benmerrouche et al., 1995) to the virtual photon (electroproduction) regime, using older data from previous generation accelerators. The extracted electrostrong form factor is of interest for QCD descriptions of baryons, and the authors note that current nonrelativistic quark models (including predictions from Stanley and Weber, 1992) cannot reproduce the Q2 dependence of this parameter. The results enable future precise measurements at CEBAF (Jefferson Lab) to determine the transverse helicity amplitude A1/2 alone as a function of Q2, when combined with strong decay studies at hadron facilities like SATURNE and COSY.
