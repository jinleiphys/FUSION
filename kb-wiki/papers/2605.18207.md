# Positivity of the effective range for finite range attractive potentials with a repulsive core

> In the phenomenological study of exotic hadrons, the sign of the effective range, $r_0$, is invoked as a criterion to distinguish between compact multiquark configurations (associated with $r_0 < 0$) and loosely bound hadronic molecules ($r_0 > 0$). Motivated by this, we investigate the fundamental constraints on the sign of the effective range for single-channel local interactions. We rigorously prove that for finite-range potentials, characterized by an inner repulsive core and an outer attractive tail, the effective range remains strictly positive provided that the scattering length is greater than the range of the potential ($a > R$).

# Positivity of the effective range for finite range attractive potentials with a repulsive core

## Key claim
For single-channel local interactions with finite range, an inner repulsive core and an outer attractive tail, the effective range remains strictly positive provided the scattering length exceeds the range of the potential (a > R). This means that adding arbitrary inner repulsion to a single-channel local potential is fundamentally insufficient to produce a negative effective range, contrary to what is sometimes assumed in phenomenological studies of exotic hadrons.

## Method
The author proves a theorem using the integral representation of the effective range, r0 = 2 ∫₀ᴿ [χ₀²(r) − u₀²(r)] dr, where χ₀ is the asymptotic zero-energy wave function and u₀ is the true zero-energy wave function. For potentials with V(r) ≥ 0 on (0, r₁) and V(r) ≤ 0 on (r₁, R), the author shows that Δ(r) = χ₀(r) − u₀(r) is non-negative throughout [0, R] by analyzing its concavity/convexity properties, which follows from the sign of U(r) u₀(r). The theorem is then illustrated with two numerical examples: a piecewise spherical barrier-well potential and a truncated superposition of repulsive and attractive Yukawa potentials.

## Key numbers
- For the spherical barrier-well potential with parameters r₁/R = 0.2, U₁ = 15, U₂ = 8: scattering length a = 1.17 R, effective range r₀ = 0.33 R; the purely attractive well with the same scattering length gives r₀* = 0.32 R.
- For the Yukawa potential with g₁² = 3, g₂² = 2.7, r₁ = 0.4 r₂, R = 30 r₂: scattering length a = 37.6 r₂, effective range r₀ = 1.23 r₂; the purely attractive Yukawa with the same scattering length gives r₀* = 1.01 r₂.
- In the analytically solvable limit of the barrier-well potential (unitarity limit, strong repulsive core), the effective range approximates r₀ ≈ R + r₁, compared to r₀ = R for a purely attractive square well in the unitarity limit.

## Context
This work extends the well-established result from Smorodinsky and Landau that purely attractive potentials supporting a shallow bound state yield a positive effective range. The theorem directly addresses the phenomenological criterion used in exotic hadron studies by Esposito (2021, 2025), Baru (2021), Shen (2024), and Kinugawa (2023), where negative effective range is associated with compact multiquark configurations and positive effective range with hadronic molecules. The result shows that single-channel local potentials with repulsive cores cannot produce negative r₀, implying that coupled-channel dynamics or energy-dependent interactions as discussed by Baru (2021) and Esposito (2023) are necessary to model compact exotic hadrons within potential models.
