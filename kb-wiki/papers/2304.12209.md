# Colour-Flavour Locked Quark Stars in Light of the Compact Object in HESS J1731-347 and the GW190814 Event

> The central compact object within HESS J1731- 347 possesses unique mass and radius properties that renders it a compelling candidate for a self-bound star. In this research, we examine the capability of quark stars composed of colour superconducting quark matter to explain the latter object by using its marginalised posterior distribution and imposing it as a constraint on the relevant parameter space. Namely, we investigate quark matter for $N_f=2,3$ in the colour superconducting phase, incorporating perturbative QCD corrections, and we derive their properties accordingly. The utilised thermodynamic potential of this work possesses an MIT bag model formalism with the parameters being established as flavour-independent. In this instance, we conclude the favour of 3-flavour over 2-flavour colour superconducting quark matter, isolating our interest on the former. The parameter space is further confined due to the additional requirement for a high maximum mass ($M_{\text{TOV}} \geq 2.6 M_{\odot}$), accounting for GW$190814$'s secondary companion. We pay a significant attention on the speed of sound and the trace anomaly (proposed as a measure of conformality [\href{https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.129.252702}{10.1103/PhysRevLett.129.252702}]). We conclude that it is possible for colour-flavour locked quark stars to reach high masses without violating the conformal bound or the $\langle Θ\rangle _{μ_B} \geq 0$ if the quartic coefficient value $α_4$ does not exceed an upper limit which is solely dependent on the established $M_{\text{TOV}}$. For $M_{\text{TOV}}=2.6 M_{\odot}$, we find that the limit reads $α_4 \leq 0.594$. Lastly, a further study takes place on the agreement of colour-flavour locked quark stars with additional astrophysical objects including the GW$170817$ and GW$190425$ events, followed by a relevant discussion.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that colour-flavour locked (CFL) quark stars can simultaneously explain the low-mass compact object in HESS J1731-347 and reach the high maximum mass required by GW190814's secondary companion, provided the quartic coefficient α₄ does not exceed an upper limit that depends on the adopted maximum mass; for M_TOV = 2.6 M⊙, this limit is α₄ ≤ 0.594, and the CFL phase is favoured over the 2SC phase within the specific flavour-independent MIT bag model formalism used.

## Method

The authors employed a thermodynamic potential with an MIT bag model formalism, incorporating perturbative QCD corrections via a quartic coefficient α₄ and a flavour-independent effective bag constant B_eff, to derive equations of state for both 2-flavour (2SC) and 3-flavour (CFL) colour superconducting quark matter. They imposed stability constraints (energy per baryon ≤ 930 MeV for strange matter, ≥ 934 MeV for u,d matter) and observational constraints from the marginalised posterior distribution of HESS J1731-347 (M = 0.77⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₁₇ M⊙, R = 10.4⁺⁰·⁸⁶₋₀·₇₈ km) and the GW190814 event (M_TOV ≥ 2.6 M⊙), then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and computed tidal deformabilities to test compatibility with additional astrophysical objects.

## Key numbers

- The compact object in HESS J1731-347 has mass M = 0.77⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₁₇ M⊙ and radius R = 10.4⁺⁰·⁸⁶₋₀·₇₈ km (Doroshenko 2022)
- The secondary companion in GW190814 has mass m₂ = 2.59⁺⁰·⁰⁸₋₀·⁰⁹ M⊙, with the requirement M_TOV ≥ 2.6 M⊙ for CFL quark stars to explain it
- For M_TOV = 2.6 M⊙, the upper limit on the quartic coefficient is α₄ ≤ 0.594
- The minimum superconducting gap required to satisfy the CFL stability condition is Δ > 14.556 MeV (corresponding to μ|_(P=0) ≤ 310 MeV)
- For α₄ = 0.65, the gap required to describe both HESS J1731-347 and GW190814 is Δ > 60 MeV; for α₄ = 0.75, Δ > 80 MeV
- The minimum gap values for 2SC quark matter range from Δ > 18.647 MeV (α₄ = 0.45) to Δ > 27.798 MeV (α₄ = 1.00)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of colour superconducting quark stars (Alford 2005, Lugones 2002, Horvath 2004) by applying constraints from the recently measured low-mass compact object in HESS J1731-347 (Doroshenko 2022) and the high-mass secondary companion in GW190814 (Abbott 2020a). The authors also incorporate the trace anomaly as a measure of conformality proposed by Fujimoto (2022) to further constrain the parameter space, and test the resulting CFL quark star models against additional astrophysical constraints from GW170817, GW190425, and observations of pulsars such as those reported by Romani (2022), Riley (2021), and Antoniadis (2013).
