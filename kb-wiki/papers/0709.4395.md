# The Spatial String Tension in the Deconfined Phase of SU(N) Gauge Theory and Gauge/String Duality

## Key claim

The spatial string tension in pure SU(N) gauge theory, calculated via gauge/string duality for temperatures between 1.2 T_c and 3 T_c, is given by a universal function of T/T_c that does not explicitly depend on N, and this result is remarkably consistent with available lattice data for N=2 and N=3. The author finds evidence that to leading order the large-distance physics of the string can be described by a five-dimensional geometry (a deformed Euclidean AdS_5 black hole) without needing the internal S^5 space, though the compactness of the internal space is crucial for the success of this five-dimensional description.

## Method

The author uses a 10-dimensional background metric that is a deformed product of the Euclidean AdS_5 black hole and a 5-sphere, with a z-dependent factor h = exp(½ c z²) that breaks conformal invariance and introduces Λ_QCD. Spatial Wilson loops are computed via the Nambu-Goto action in this background, with the string lying along a great circle of the internal sphere; the pseudo-potential is extracted from the regularized action and expressed in parametric form. Numerical analysis shows that at large quark-antiquark separation r, the parameter k (related to the angular separation in the internal space) becomes small, allowing an analytic expansion that yields the spatial string tension σ_s.

## Key numbers

- The spatial string tension is given by σ_s = σ (T²/T_c²) exp(T_c²/T² - 1), where σ is the physical string tension at zero temperature.
- The critical temperature is defined as T_c = (1/π) √(c/2), where c is a positive N-dependent parameter (for SU(3), c may be determined from the ρ meson trajectory with a discrepancy of order 10%).
- The calculation is valid for the temperature range 1.2 T_c ≤ T ≤ 3 T_c; the lower limit keeps the system out of the critical regime, the upper limit is determined by consistency.
- For SU(2), the spatial tension is normalized at T=0; for SU(3), it is normalized at T = 1.062560 T_c.
- The parameter k at large distances behaves as k = (1/π² T²) (θ²/r²) exp(-c/π² T²), confirming self-consistency as k → 0 when r → ∞.

## Context

This work extends the gauge/string duality approach of Andreev (2006) for thermodynamics of pure gauge theories to the spatial string tension, subjecting that proposal to further test. The results are compared with lattice simulations by Bali (for SU(2)) and Karsch (for SU(3)), showing remarkably good agreement, and are also close to the analytic result of Agasian for T ≲ 1.6 T_c. The paper enables a string-theoretic alternative to lattice calculations for strongly coupled gauge theories in the deconfined phase, and predicts that the spatial string tension in units of the physical tension is universal (N-independent) for 1.2 T_c ≤ T ≤ 3 T_c, a prediction that awaits lattice tests for larger N.
