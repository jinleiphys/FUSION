# Universal relations for compact stars with exotic degrees of freedom

> The nature of the highly dense matter inside the supernova remnant compact star is not constrained by terrestrial experiments and hence modeled phenomenologically to accommodate the astrophysical observations from compact stars. The observable properties of the compact stars are highly sensitive to the microscopic model of highly dense matter. However, some universal relations exist between some macroscopic properties of compact stars independent of the matter model. We study the universal relation including the stars containing exotic degrees of freedom such as heavier strange and non-strange baryons, strange quark matter in normal and superconducting phases, etc. We examine the universal relations for quantities moment of inertia - tidal love number - quadrupole moment. We also study the correlation of non-radial f-mode and p-mode frequencies with stellar properties. We find the f-mode frequency observes the universal relation with dimensionless tidal deformability but the p-mode frequency does not show a good correlation with stellar properties. The p-mode frequency is sensitive to the composition of the matter. We find that universal relation is also applicable for stars with exotic matter in the core of the star with several models of exotic matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that universal relations connecting moment of inertia, tidal Love number, and quadrupole moment, as well as the correlation between f-mode oscillation frequency and dimensionless tidal deformability, remain valid for compact stars containing exotic degrees of freedom such as hyperons, delta resonances, and strange quark matter in normal and superconducting phases; however, the p-mode frequency does not show a good correlation with stellar properties and is sensitive to the composition of matter.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for baryonic stars using the Relativistic Mean Field approach with density-dependent couplings (parametrizations DD2, DD-ME2, and DD-MEX), including nucleons, hyperons from the baryon octet, and delta resonances. For hybrid stars, they modeled the quark core using the MIT bag model with interactions, the vector bag model, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and the Color-Flavor-Locked superconducting phase, with Gibbs construction for the mixed phase transition between baryonic and quark matter. They then solved the stellar structure equations and computed non-radial oscillation frequencies using both the relativistic Cowling approximation and full general relativistic calculations, following the method of Lindblom and Detweiler.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density n0 ranges from 0.149 to 0.152 fm^-3 across the parametrizations used
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation E0 ranges from 16.02 to 16.14 MeV
- Incompressibility K0 ranges from 242.70 to 267.06 MeV
- Symmetry energy Esym ranges from 32.269 to 32.30 MeV
- For the MIT bag model, two parameter sets are used: alpha_s = 0.8 fm^2 with B^(1/4) = 160 MeV, and alpha_s = 0.3 fm^2 with B^(1/4) = 174 MeV
- For the vBAG model, two parameter sets are used: G_V = 0.25 fm^2 with B^(1/4) = 180 MeV, and G_V = 0.17 fm^2 with B^(1/4) = 172 MeV
- For the CFL phase, parameter sets include B^(1/4) = 190 MeV with Delta = 30 MeV, B^(1/4) = 205 MeV with Delta = 15 MeV, and B^(1/4) = 205 MeV with Delta = 30 MeV
- Every equation of state has central energy density corresponding to maximum mass less than 1250 MeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the universal relations framework originally discovered by Yagi and Yunes (2013) for pure nucleonic neutron stars to compact stars with exotic degrees of freedom, building on prior studies by Bandyopadhyay et al. (2018) that considered hyperons and anti-kaon condensates, and by Raduta et al. (2020) that examined hot stars with heavier baryons and quarks. The study also updates the empirical relations for f-mode frequencies from Andersson and Kokkotas (1998) and Benhar et al. (2004) by including exotic matter compositions. The results enable the inference of tidal deformability and quadrupole moment from measured moment of inertia or f-mode frequencies for a broader class of compact stars, which will be relevant for interpreting observations from next-generation gravitational wave detectors such as the Cosmic Explorer and the Einstein Telescope.
