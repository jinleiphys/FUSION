# QRPA treatment of the transverse wobbling mode reconsidered

> The quasiparticle random phase approximation is used to study the properties of the wobbling bands in $^{163}$Lu. Assuming that the wobbling mode represents pure isoscalar orientation oscillations results in too low wobbling frequencies and too strong M1 transitions between the one- and zero-phonon wobbling bands. The inclusion of an LL interaction, which couples the wobbling mode to the scissors mode, generates the right upshift of the wobbling frequencies and the right suppression of the B(M1)$_{out}$ values toward the experimental values. In analogy to the quenching of low-energy E1 transition by coupling to the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance, a general reduction of the M1 transitions between quasiparticle configurations caused by coupling to the scissors mode is suggested.

# QRPA treatment of the transverse wobbling mode reconsidered

## Key claim
The overestimation of B(M1) transition strengths between wobbling bands in 163Lu, which persists in both the Quasiparticle Triaxial Rotor model and standard isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole QRPA calculations, can be resolved by including an LL interaction that couples the wobbling mode to the scissors mode. This coupling upshifts wobbling frequencies and suppresses B(M1) values toward experimental data, analogous to how coupling to the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance quenches low-energy E1 transitions.

## Method
The authors performed QRPA calculations for the i13/2 triaxial strongly deformed band in 163Lu using a Hamiltonian with a rotating triaxial Nilsson potential, monopole pairing, and residual interactions. Two approaches were compared: a selfconsistent treatment where the isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole interaction determined both the mean field deformation and the residual interaction, and a symmetry-restoring approach using Nilsson-Strutinsky deformation parameters with locally restored rotational invariance. An additional LL interaction was introduced to couple the wobbling mode to the scissors mode, and the spurious rotational modes were removed following the method of Frauendorf and Dönau (2005).

## Key numbers
- Selfconsistent QRPA with ISQQ interaction gave wobbling frequencies that become zero at ω=0.45 MeV/ℏ, substantially below experimental values
- The calculated B(M1, I→I-1) values from the selfconsistent ISQQ interaction exceeded experimental values by a factor of ten
- The ratio B(E2)out/B(E2)in from the selfconsistent ISQQ calculation reached only one half of the measured values
- Nilsson-Strutinsky deformations gave γ ≈ 19-22 degrees, compared to selfconsistent γ ≈ 9-12 degrees
- Using γ=30 degrees and ε=0.4 at ℏω=0.3 MeV gave wobbling frequencies exceeding experimental values
- The LL interaction with strength κLL=0.5 MeV/ℏ² produced a fragmented B(M1) strength in 162Yb summing to 1.5 μN² from 0 to 4 MeV, interpreted as scissors strength

## Context
This work extends the QRPA treatment of wobbling bands previously applied by Matsuyanagi, Matsuzaki, Ohtsubo, Shimizu, and Shoji (2002-2008), who used isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole interactions and found similar overestimations of B(M1) values. It also connects to the Quasiparticle Triaxial Rotor model calculations by Frauendorf and Dönau (2014), which demonstrated that the transverse wobbling geometry explains the decreasing wobbling frequency but overestimates B(M1) by a factor of 3-10. The proposed mechanism of M1 suppression via coupling to the scissors mode opens a new direction for understanding magnetic transitions in wobbling bands and suggests a general reduction of M1 transitions between quasiparticle configurations, analogous to the well-known quenching of low-energy E1 transitions by the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance.
