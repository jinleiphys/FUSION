# Proton polarizabilities: status, relevance, prospects

> 4 pages, 1 figure; contribution to proceedings of the Workshop to Explore Physics Opportunities with Intense, Polarized Electron Beams up to 300 MeV, March 14-16, 2013 MIT, Cambridge, Massachusetts

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predictions for proton polarizabilities show a significant discrepancy with current Particle Data Group (PDG) values, which has been attributed to a possible sizeable underestimate of uncertainty in the TAPS and subsequently PDG values rather than a failure of the theory.

## Method

The paper reviews calculations of proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities in both Heavy-Baryon ChPT (HBChPT) and manifestly Lorentz-invariant Baryon ChPT (BChPT) at leading order (p^3) and including Delta(1232)-isobar contributions (p^4/Delta). The author then applies a dispersion relation for the momentum-transfer dependence of the magnetic polarizability, using a p^3 HBChPT calculation of the imaginary part, to evaluate the subtraction contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order (p^3) BChPT predictions: electric polarizability alpha_E1 = 6.8, magnetic polarizability beta_M1 = -1.8 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Leading-order (p^3) HBChPT predictions: alpha_E1 = 12.2, beta_M1 = 1.2 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Combined p^3 + p^4/Delta BChPT predictions: alpha_E1 = 10.8, beta_M1 = 4.0 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Combined p^3 + p^4/Delta HBChPT predictions: alpha_E1 = 20.8, beta_M1 = 14.7 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Baldin sum rule evaluation yields alpha_E1 + beta_M1 = 13.8(5) (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- The inelastic contribution to the muonic hydrogen 2S level shift is approximately -13 micro-eV
- The p^3 HBChPT calculation of the magnetic polarizability subtraction contribution to the muonic hydrogen 2S level shift yields approximately 1.4 micro-eV

## Context

This review extends the ongoing theoretical effort to understand nucleon polarizabilities using ChPT, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984). The paper addresses the "proton charge radius puzzle" identified by Pohl (2010) and Antognini (2013), where a 7-sigma discrepancy exists between proton charge radius extractions from electronic and muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements. The author shows that the magnetic polarizability contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, which Miller (2012) suggested could be as large as -310 micro-eV to resolve the puzzle, is instead calculated to be only about 1.4 micro-eV in p^3 HBChPT, making it very unlikely to resolve the discrepancy. The work enables future experimental programs at MAMI (Mainz) and HIGS (Duke University) to improve empirical knowledge of static polarizabilities and their momentum-transfer dependence.
