# The impact of nuclear masses near $N=82$ on $r$-process abundances

> We have performed for the first time a complete $r$-process mass sensitivity study in the $N=82$ region. We take into account how an uncertainty in a single nuclear mass propagates to influence important quantities of neighboring nuclei, including Q-values and reaction rates. We demonstrate that nuclear mass uncertainties of $\pm0.5$ MeV in the $N=82$ region result in up to an order of magnitude local change in $r$-process abundance predictions. We identify key nuclei in the study whose mass has a substantial impact on final $r$-process abundances and could be measured at future radioactive beam facilities.

## Key claim

Nuclear mass uncertainties of ±0.5 MeV in the N=82 region can produce up to an order of magnitude local change in r-process abundance predictions, but this sensitivity is specific to a hot wind r-process scenario where equilibrium and photo-dissociation effects dominate; in cold wind or neutron star merger environments the sensitivity pattern would differ.

## Method

The authors performed a complete r-process mass sensitivity study in the N=82 region by varying one nuclear mass at a time, then recalculating all dependent quantities (Q-values, neutron capture rates, photo-dissociation rates, beta-decay rates, and beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities) for neighboring nuclei using the FRDM mass model and TALYS statistical model code. They used a parameterized hot wind r-process trajectory with entropy of 200 k_B, electron fraction Y_e=0.3, and timescale of 80 ms, running a dedicated r-process reaction network to compare final abundance patterns against a baseline simulation.

## Key numbers

- FRDM mass model has a root-mean-square error of approximately 0.65 MeV compared to known masses from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2012.
- Adding 0.5 MeV to the mass of 140Sn produced an F-value of 31.24 when all effects were propagated, with contributions of F=1.4 from neutron capture effects, F=16.0 from weak decay effects, and F=29.96 from equilibrium path and photo-dissociation effects.
- A mass increase of 0.5 MeV to 140Sn lowered the half-life of this nucleus by 45%.
- For mass uncertainties as low as ±0.1 MeV, the maximum F-value reached approximately 15 for the most influential nuclei.
- For the most neutron-rich r-process nuclei, beta-decay Q-values are above 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous sensitivity studies of individual nuclear properties (Surman 2009, Beun 2009, Mumpower 2012, Brett 2012) by self-consistently propagating mass uncertainties to all dependent nuclear properties simultaneously, rather than treating each property independently. The distribution of important nuclei agrees qualitatively with Brett (2012), but the authors find that sensitivities increase when all properties are propagated consistently due to the dependence of weak decay properties on nuclear masses. The results identify key nuclei measurable at future radioactive beam facilities like CARIBU and FRIB, and the authors plan to extend calculations beyond the N=82 region and consider a range of astrophysical environments and nuclear models.
