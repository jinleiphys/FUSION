# Simultaneous microscopic description of nuclear level density and radiative strength function

> Nuclear level density (NLD) and radiative strength function (RSF) are described simultaneously within a microscopic approach, which takes into account the thermal effects of the exact pairing as well as the giant resonances within the phonon-damping model. The good agreement between the results of calculations and experimental data extracted by the Oslo group for $^{170, 171, 172}$Yb isotopes shows the importance of exact thermal pairing in the description of NLD at low and intermediate excitation energies and invalidates the assumption based on the Brink-Axel hypothesis in the description of the RSF.

## Key claim

The authors propose a unified microscopic approach that simultaneously describes nuclear level density (NLD) and radiative strength function (RSF) for the first time, using exact thermal pairing and temperature-dependent giant resonance widths; the good agreement with experimental data for 170,171,172Yb invalidates the Brink-Axel hypothesis for RSF description, but the approach is demonstrated only for these three isotopes and uses parameters taken from previous calculations.

## Method

The method combines exact canonical-ensemble pairing (EP) for levels around the Fermi surface with an independent-particle model (IPM) for the remaining single-particle spectrum to compute the partition function, from which NLD is obtained via inverse Laplace transformation. The RSF is calculated within the phonon-damping model (PDM), which includes a temperature-dependent resonance width derived microscopically from quantal and thermal contributions, using the exact thermal pairing gap as input. Calculations are performed for 170,171,172Yb using single-particle spectra from an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential, with the pairing strength G adjusted to reproduce experimental pairing gaps at T=0.

## Key numbers

- Exact neutron and proton pairing gaps remain finite up to T as large as 3 MeV, well above the BCS critical temperature Tc ~ 0.57Δ(T=0)
- The RSF calculated at T = 0.7 MeV agrees well with experimental data for all three Yb isotopes
- The quadrupole deformation parameters β2 are 0.295 for 170,171Yb and 0.296 for 172Yb
- Diagonalization of the pairing Hamiltonian uses 12 doubly degenerate single-particle levels (6 above and 6 below the Fermi surface), yielding 73,789 eigenstates for each particle type
- The calculation takes less than five minutes even for a heavy nucleus

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exact-pairing treatment of NLD (Hung 2010, 2011) and E1 strength functions (Dang 2013, 2014) by combining both into a unified framework. It contradicts the Brink-Axel hypothesis (Brink 1955, Axel 1962) used in experimental RSF extraction by the Oslo group, and improves upon the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov plus combinatorial method (HFBC) of Hilaire and Goriely (2006), which requires renormalization with two phenomenological parameters. The approach enables microscopic predictions of NLD and RSF without fitting to experimental data beyond the pairing strength, and can be applied to heavy nuclei on a standard PC.
