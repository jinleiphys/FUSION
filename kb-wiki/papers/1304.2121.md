# Equation of state for neutron stars in SU(3) flavor symmetry

> Using several relativistic mean field models (such as GM1, GM3, NL3, TM1, FSUGold and IU-FSU) as well as the quark-meson coupling model, we calculate the particle fractions, the equation of state, the maximum mass and radius of a neutron star within relativistic Hartree approximation. In determining the couplings of the isoscalar, vector mesons to the octet baryons, we examine the extension of SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry to SU(3) flavor symmetry. Furthermore, we consider the strange ($σ^{\ast}$ and $ϕ$) mesons, and study how they affect the equation of state. We find that the equation of state in SU(3) symmetry can sustain a neutron star with mass of $(1.8 \sim 2.1) M_{\odot}$ even if hyperons exist inside the core. In addition, the strange vector ($ϕ$) meson and the variation of baryon structure in matter also play important roles in supporting a massive neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that extending the usual SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry to SU(3) flavor symmetry for the isoscalar vector-meson couplings to octet baryons, together with the inclusion of strange mesons and the variation of baryon internal structure in matter, allows several relativistic mean-field models to support neutron stars with masses between 1.8 and 2.1 solar masses even when hyperons are present in the core, whereas SU(6) symmetry typically produces softer equations of state that cannot reach such high masses.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (GM1, GM3, NL3, TM1, FSUGold, IU-FSU) and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) and chiral quark-meson coupling (CQMC) models within the relativistic Hartree approximation to calculate particle fractions, equation of state, and neutron star structure. They extended the usual SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry to SU(3) flavor symmetry using mixing parameters from the Nijmegen extended-soft-core (ESC) model, with mixing angle 37.50 degrees and coupling ratio z = 0.1949, and included strange mesons (sigma* and phi) to study their effects on the equation of state. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation was solved to obtain maximum masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass range of 1.8 to 2.1 solar masses (M_sun) for equations of state in SU(3) symmetry with hyperons present
- The binary millisecond pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has observed mass 1.97 +/- 0.04 M_sun, used as a benchmark
- The binary pulsar PSR B1913+16 has mass 1.4398 +/- 0.0002 M_sun
- Typical neutron star mass is around 1.4 M_sun
- The SU(3) mixing angle from the Nijmegen ESC model is 37.50 degrees, with coupling ratio z = 0.1949
- The ideal mixing angle for SU(6) symmetry is approximately 35.26 degrees, with z = 0.4082
- Hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter: U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = +30 MeV, U_Xi = -18 MeV
- The Nagara event suggests the Lambda-Lambda potential depth is about -5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Miyatsu, Cheoun, and Saito (2011, 2012) that used relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation with SU(3) symmetry and found that only the Xi- hyperon appears in the core, unlike SU(6) symmetry where all octet baryons appear below about 1.2 fm^-3. The paper contradicts the common finding from Glendenning (1991) and Schaffner-Bielich (2008) that hyperons soften the equation of state and reduce maximum neutron star mass, by showing that SU(3) symmetry and baryon structure variation can harden the equation of state sufficiently to support massive neutron stars. The results enable future studies of neutron star structure using simpler RMF models that incorporate SU(3) symmetry, and suggest that the FSUGold and IU-FSU models, which include the Lambda_omega_rho coupling, may produce different hyperon populations in the core.
