# Finite size effect on gluon dissociation of J/psi in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Thermal quantities, including the the entropy density and gluon spectrum, of quark matter within a box that is finite in the longitudinal direction are calculated using a bag model. Under the assumption of entropy conservation, the corresponding gluon dissociation rate of J/psi is studied. It reaches a maximum at a certain longitudinal size L_m, below which the suppression is weak even if the temperature becomes higher than that without the finite size effect, and above which the dissociation rate approaches to the thermodynamic limit gradually with increasing longitudinal size of the fireball.

# Finite size effect on gluon dissociation of J/ψ in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The gluon dissociation rate of J/ψ in a finite longitudinal box reaches a maximum at a specific longitudinal size L_m ≈ π/ε (where ε is the binding energy), below which suppression is weak even if the temperature exceeds that of the infinite-volume case at the same entropy density, and above which the rate gradually approaches the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma as an ideal gas of partons (massless gluons, u/d quarks, and massive s quarks with m_s = 150 MeV) confined in a box with finite longitudinal size L but infinite transverse extent, using a bag model with bag constant B = (236 MeV)^4. The entropy density and gluon spectrum are computed from the partition function with discrete longitudinal momenta p_z = n_zπ/L, and the J/ψ dissociation rate is calculated via the operator product expansion cross section for gluon dissociation. Under the assumption of entropy conservation during adiabatic expansion, the temperature evolution and survival probability are compared between finite and infinite systems at the same entropy density.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant: B = (236 MeV)^4, yielding critical temperature T_c = 165 MeV in infinite space
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV
- J/ψ mass: m = 3.097 GeV; charm quark mass: m_c = 1.87 GeV; threshold energy: ε = 0.71 GeV
- Longitudinal size maximizing dissociation rate: L_m = 0.87 fm for J/ψ
- For other quarkonia: ψ' (m = 3.686 GeV, ε = 0.054 GeV, L_m = 11.6 fm); Υ(1S) (m = 9.460 GeV, ε = 1.16 GeV, L_m = 0.53 fm); Υ(2S) (m = 10.023 GeV, ε = 0.55 GeV, L_m = 1.1 fm); Υ(3S) (m = 10.355 GeV, ε = 0.21 GeV, L_m = 3.0 fm)
- Survival probability for J/ψ at p_T = 0 GeV with initial T = 250 MeV at t = 1.0 fm: 0.482 (calculation I), 0.412 (II), 0.416 (III)
- Survival probability for J/ψ at p_T = 3 GeV with initial T = 250 MeV at t = 1.0 fm: 0.511 (I), 0.436 (II), 0.433 (III)
- Asymptotic coefficients for entropy ratio: b_+ ≈ 0.561 (bosons), b_- ≈ 0.654 (fermions), b_cl ≈ 0.589 (classical)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of finite-size effects on J/ψ suppression by Guo (unpublished) and Cheng (2019), who examined the gluon dissociation rate at fixed temperature and the binding energy modification in a spherical cavity. Unlike those works, the present study incorporates the finite-size modification of the equation of state and the consequent shift in temperature evolution under entropy conservation, which is the standard assumption in 2+1D hydrodynamic models. The results enable a parameter-free calculation of J/ψ survival probability by setting the initial time t_0 = 0, avoiding the usual dependence on an arbitrary formation time parameter, and suggest that different quarkonium states (ψ', Υ states) experience maximal dissociation at characteristically different fireball sizes.
