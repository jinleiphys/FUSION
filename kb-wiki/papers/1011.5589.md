# Energy and system-size dependence of the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> The energy dependence of the local ${\cal P}$ and ${\cal CP}$ violation in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions in a large energy range is estimated within a simple phenomenological model. It is expected that at LHC the chiral magnetic effect will be about 20 times weaker than at RHIC. At lower energy range, covered by the low-energy scan at RHIC and future NICA/FAIR facilities, the created magnetic field strength and energy density of deconfined matter are rather high providing necessary conditions for the chiral magnetic effect. However, the particular model for the chiral magnetic effect predicts that this effect should vanish sharply at energy somewhere above the top SPS one. To elucidate CME background effects the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model including electromagnetic fields is put forward. Importance of new planning experiments at LHC and for the low-energy RHIC scan program is emphasized.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is expected to be about 20 times weaker at LHC than at RHIC, and the model predicts the effect should vanish sharply at an energy somewhere above the top SPS energy, with the exact energy dependence and magnitude depending sensitively on the chosen critical magnetic field threshold and the treatment of the interaction time.

## Method

The authors estimate the CME using a phenomenological model based on the saturation momentum Q_s, relating the topological charge (winding number) to the hadron multiplicity and the lifetime of the magnetic field. They extract the magnetic field lifetime from UrQMD model calculations of the time evolution of the magnetic field strength eB_y and energy density epsilon in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions, then tune the critical magnetic field threshold eB_crit and initial time corrections to reproduce STAR correlator measurements at sqrt(s_NN)=200 and 62 GeV. They also propose a kinetic approach using the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model including electromagnetic fields to study CME background effects.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=4.5 TeV), the predicted correlator a^2 = 0.016 x 10^{-4} for Au+Au at b=10 fm with eB_crit=0.2 m_pi^2, about 20 times smaller than at RHIC
- At RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV), the measured same-charge correlator <cos(psi_alpha+psi_beta-2Psi_RP)> = -(0.312 +/- 0.027) x 10^{-3} for Au+Au at b=10 fm (40-50% centrality)
- For Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=62 GeV, the model predicts a^2 = 0.93 x 10^{-3} with eB_crit=0.2 m_pi^2, but the experimental increase is only about 20% relative to 200 GeV
- The magnetic field strength eB_y decreases by a factor of about 10 from sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV to E_lab=11 GeV, while the energy density decreases by a factor of 250
- For Cu+Cu at b=4.2 fm (40-50% centrality) with eB_crit=0.2 m_pi^2, a^2 = 0.49 x 10^{-3} at 200 GeV and 1.40 x 10^{-3} at 62 GeV
- The almond overlap area ratio S_Cu(b=4.2)/S_Au(b=10) is approximately 1.65

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological CME framework of Kharzeev (2004) and the magnetic field calculations of Skokov, Illarionov, and Toneev (2009) to study energy and system-size dependence. The authors analyze STAR Collaboration data (Voloshin, 2009) and find that the simple model overestimates the energy dependence between 200 and 62 GeV, requiring careful tuning of the critical magnetic field threshold and initial time corrections. The results enable predictions for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, NICA/FAIR facilities, and LHC experiments, while the proposed HSD transport approach with electromagnetic fields provides a path toward dynamical CME background calculations.
