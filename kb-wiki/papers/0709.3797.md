# Two-proton radioactivity

> In the first part of the present review paper, experimental results which lead to the discovery of two-proton radioactivity are reviewed. Beyond two-proton emission from nuclear ground states, we also discuss experimental studies of two-proton emission from excited states populated either by nuclear beta decay or by inelastic reactions. In the second part, we review the modern theory of two-proton radioactivity. An outlook to future experimental studies and theoretical developments will conclude this review.

# Two-proton radioactivity

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the experimental and theoretical status of two-proton radioactivity, a decay mode predicted more than 40 years before its first observation in 2002 in the decay of 45Fe. The authors note that while two-proton emission has now been observed from ground states (45Fe, 54Zn, and likely 48Ni) and from excited states populated by beta decay or nuclear reactions, the decay mechanism remains ambiguous because only total decay energies and half-lives have been measured, and no unambiguous signature of correlated (diproton) versus sequential or direct three-body emission has been established experimentally.

## Method

The authors review experimental studies of two-proton emission from nuclear ground states (including 6Be, 12O, 16Ne, 19Mg, 45Fe, 54Zn, 48Ni) and from excited states populated either by beta decay (nine identified β2p emitters including 22Al, 31Ar, 35Ca) or by inelastic reactions (14O, 17Ne, 18Ne). They then review theoretical approaches including extended R-matrix theory, the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), the Gamow Shell Model (GSM), three-body models with outgoing flux, complex coordinate scaling, and Faddeev equations.

## Key numbers

- Two ground-state two-proton emitters were definitively identified at the time of writing: 45Fe and 54Zn, with 48Ni as a likely candidate
- The half-life of 19Mg was measured as T1/2 = 4.0(15) ps with a two-proton Q-value of Q2p = 0.75(5) MeV
- The 2p decay branch from the 7.77 MeV resonance in 14O was determined to be 0.16(3)%
- The first excited state of 17Ne is unbound with respect to 2p decay by 344 keV
- A partial 2p half-life for the first excited state of 17Ne larger than 26 ps was deduced
- The 2p Q-value for 16Ne was measured as 1.41(2) MeV
- Nine β-delayed two-proton emitters have been observed: 22Al, 23Si, 26P, 27S, 31Ar, 35Ca, 39Ti, 43Cr, and 50Ni
- The lower half-life limit for observing proton radioactivity is about 1 μs due to experimental constraints

## Context

This review extends earlier reviews by Goldanskii (1966) and Aysto (1989) written before the discovery of ground-state two-proton radioactivity. The experimental discovery of 2p radioactivity in 45Fe by Giovinazzo (2002) and Pfutzner (2002) confirmed predictions by Brown (1991), Cole (1996), and Ormand (1996) that 39Ti, 42Cr, 45Fe, and 49,48Ni would be the best candidates. The review enables future work by identifying that measurements of individual proton energies and proton-proton angular correlations in the center-of-mass frame are needed to distinguish between sequential, direct three-body, and diproton decay mechanisms, requiring development of specific detector arrays.
