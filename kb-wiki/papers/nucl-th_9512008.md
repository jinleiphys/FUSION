# Mean-field description of ground-state properties of drip-line nuclei: Pairing and continuum effects

> Ground-state properties of exotic even-even nuclei with extreme neutron-to-proton ratios are described in the framework of the self-consistent mean-field theory with pairing formulated in coordinate space. This theory properly accounts for the influence of the particle continuum, which is particularly important for weakly bound systems. The pairing properties of nuclei far from stability are studied with several interactions emphasizing different aspects, such as the range and density dependence of the effective interaction. Measurable consequences of spatially extended pairing fields are presented, and the sensitivity of the theoretical predictions to model details is discussed.

## Key claim

Ground-state properties of drip-line nuclei can be described by self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory in coordinate space, but the predictions are strongly sensitive to the density dependence and range of the effective pairing interaction, particularly because for weakly bound systems the pairing component can no longer be treated as a residual perturbation.

## Method

The authors solved the spherical HFB equations in coordinate space using several effective interactions: Skyrme parametrizations SkP, SkP\(^{\delta}\), SkP\(^{\delta\rho}\), SIII\(^{\delta}\), and SkM\(^{\delta}\) (with zero-range pairing forces, including a density-dependent delta interaction), and the finite-range Gogny force D1S. The HFB formalism in coordinate space explicitly includes the particle continuum, and the canonical basis was used to obtain localized single-particle wave functions even for positive-energy states.

## Key numbers

- For the SkP Skyrme interaction, the effective mass for symmetric nuclear matter is \(m^*/m = 1\); for SIII it is 0.76; for SkM\(^*\) it is 0.79; for the Gogny D1S force it is 0.70.
- The Gogny D1S force yields a pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation of \(\Delta \simeq 0.7\) MeV.
- The saturation density used in the density-dependent delta interaction is \(\rho_c \approx \rho(\mathbf{r}=0)\).
- The singlet-\(S\) pairing in nuclear matter at the saturation point (\(k_F = 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\)) is very small according to early calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier macroscopic-microscopic studies of drip-line nuclei by Nazarewicz (1994) to a fully self-consistent HFB framework. It directly addresses the open questions of the range and density dependence of the pairing interaction, which were previously treated with schematic seniority or multipole pairing forces. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutron drip lines, neutron halos and skins, and have implications for the r-process mechanism and for theories of superfluidity in neutron stars.
