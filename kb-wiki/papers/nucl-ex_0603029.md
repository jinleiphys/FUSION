# Inclusive quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering

> This article presents a review of the field of inclusive quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering. It discusses the approach used to measure the data and includes a compilation of data available in numerical form. The theoretical approaches used to interpret the data are presented. A number of results obtained from the comparison between experiment and calculation are then reviewed. The analogies and differences to other fields of physics exploiting quasi-elastic scattering from composite systems are pointed out.

## Key claim

This paper is a review of the field of inclusive quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering, not a presentation of new experimental or theoretical results. The abstract’s claim of presenting “a number of results obtained from the comparison between experiment and calculation” is hedged by the paper’s own admission that the field has matured to the point where “the dominant process is elastic scattering from individual nucleons” and “the different scale-breaking mechanisms have been identified,” but that the review’s purpose is to compile existing data and theoretical approaches rather than to establish a single new finding.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for inclusive quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering, starting from the Born-approximation cross section expressed in terms of nuclear structure functions \(W_1\) and \(W_2\) or the longitudinal and transverse response functions \(R_L\) and \(R_T\). It then details the impulse approximation (IA) scheme, in which the nuclear current is written as a sum of one-body currents and the cross section factorizes into a nucleon spectral function \(S_N(\mathbf{k},E)\) and the electron-nucleon scattering tensor. The review discusses how the spectral function is calculated using nonrelativistic nuclear many-body theory (NMBT) for light nuclei and nuclear matter, and how the local density approximation (LDA) is used to model spectral functions for heavier nuclei. It also covers the deForest prescription for restoring gauge invariance in the IA and the treatment of final-state interactions and meson-exchange currents.

## Key numbers

*   Momentum transfer threshold for the quasi-elastic peak to dominate the spectrum: above approximately 500 MeV/c.
*   Fermi momentum scale for nucleons in nuclei: approximately 250 MeV/c.
*   Energy scale of shell-model states: approximately 30 MeV.
*   Range of the nuclear matter spectral function background: extends up to \(E \sim 200\) MeV and \(|\mathbf{k}| \sim 800\) MeV/c.
*   Number of NN scattering data used to fit the two-nucleon potential: approximately 4000.
*   Mass number range for exact solution of the many-body Schrodinger equation using stochastic methods: \(A \leq 12\).

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Benhar (1989) and others on the nuclear matter spectral function, and of Ciofi degli Atti (1980) and Meier-Hajduk (1983) on three-nucleon systems. It synthesizes the theoretical framework of impulse approximation, spectral functions, and scaling that had been developed over the preceding two decades, and it compiles the experimental data from many experiments. The review enables future work by providing a comprehensive reference for the status of the field, identifying the kinematical regions where more complicated processes (such as meson-exchange currents) are important, and pointing out that the main open question is the precise role of medium modifications of nucleon form factors and the need for better control of final-state interactions.
