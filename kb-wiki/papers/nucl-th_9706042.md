# Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Nucleon

> We reanalyze the world data on the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon. The calculations are performed in the framework of an algebraic model of the nucleon combined with vector meson dominance.

# Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Nucleon

## Key claim
The algebraic model of the nucleon combined with vector meson dominance can simultaneously describe all four electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon for momentum transfers up to Q² = 10 (GeV/c)², but the neutron electric form factor remains the least constrained and its description depends on whether it is included in the fit or replaced by charge radius constraints.

## Method
The authors combined a collective algebraic model of the nucleon (treating baryon resonances as vibrational and rotational excitations of an oblate top) with vector meson dominance couplings to isoscalar (ω, φ) and isovector (ρ) mesons. They parametrized the Dirac and Pauli form factors with a common intrinsic dipole form factor g(Q²) multiplied by meson pole terms, and performed simultaneous fits to world data on all four Sachs form factors (G_E^p, G_M^p, G_E^n, G_M^n) for Q² ≤ 10 (GeV/c)². The large width of the ρ meson (Γ_ρ = 151 MeV) was accounted for by a specific replacement in the propagator.

## Key numbers
- The fit covers momentum transfers up to Q² = 10 (GeV/c)².
- The ρ meson width used is Γ_ρ = 151 MeV.
- The standard dipole form factor used for scaling is F_D = 1/(1 + Q²/0.71)².
- The intrinsic dipole form factor is g(Q²) = 1/(1 + γ Q²)².
- The ratio G_M^n/G_M^p is found to be -2/3 in the bare algebraic model without meson cloud couplings.
- For high Q², the Dirac form factors scale as F_1^{S/V} ~ 1/Q⁴ and Pauli form factors as F_2^{S/V} ~ 1/Q⁶.

## Context
This work extends the vector meson dominance approach of Iachello, Jackson and Lande (IJL) and the hybrid model of Gari and Krümpelmann (GK) by incorporating an algebraic collective model of the nucleon that naturally accounts for the relative energies of the Roper resonance and negative parity resonances. The analysis uses recent re-measurements and reanalyses of nucleon form factors by Bosted, Ostrick, and Schoch. The results enable future extension to inelastic transition form factors of baryon resonances, which the authors plan to address within the same framework.
