# How to test path-length dependence in energy loss mechanisms: analysis leading to a new observable

> When traversing QCD medium, high $p_\perp$ partons lose energy, which is typically measured by suppression, and also predicted by various energy loss models. A crucial test of different energy loss mechanisms is how the energy loss depends on the length of traversed medium (so-called path-length dependence). The upcoming experimental results will allow to, at least in principle, for the first time, clearly observe how the energy loss changes with the size of the medium, in particular, by comparing already available $Pb+Pb$ measurements with now upcoming $Xe+Xe$ data at the LHC. However, in practice, to actually perform such test, it becomes crucial to chose an optimal observable. With respect to this, a ratio of observed suppression for the two systems may seem a natural (and frequently mentioned) choice. We, however, show that extracting the path-length dependence from this observable would not be possible. We here provide an analytical derivation based on simple scaling arguments, as well as detailed numerical calculations based on our advanced energy loss framework, showing that a different observable is suitable for this purpose. We call this observable path-length sensitive suppression ratio ($R_L^{AB}$) and provide our predictions before experimental data become available. This predictions also clearly show that this observable will allow a simple comparison of the related theoretical models with the experimental data, and consequently to distinguish between different (underlying) energy loss mechanisms, which is in turn crucial for understanding properties of created QCD medium.

## Key claim

The ratio of nuclear suppression factors ($R_{AA}$) between two collision systems of different size (e.g., Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb) is not suitable for extracting the path-length dependence of parton energy loss, because it approaches unity at high transverse momentum regardless of the underlying path-length dependence; instead, the proposed observable $R_L^{AB} \equiv (1-R_{AA}^{A})/(1-R_{AA}^{B})$ isolates the path-length dependence and approaches a constant value at high $p_\perp$ that directly reflects the exponent $b$ in the energy loss scaling $\Delta E/E \sim L^b$.

## Method

The authors used the DREENA-C framework, based on a dynamical energy loss formalism that includes both radiative and collisional energy loss in a finite-size QCD medium, to compute $R_{AA}$ predictions for light (charged hadrons) and heavy (D and B meson) probes in 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions at various centralities. They then performed an analytical derivation using scaling arguments for fractional energy loss $\Delta E/E \sim \eta T^a L^b$ to show that the ratio $R_{XeXe}/R_{PbPb}$ depends on jet energy, temperature, and centrality in a way that conceals the path-length exponent $b$, while the new observable $R_L^{XePb} \equiv (1-R_{XeXe})/(1-R_{PbPb})$ reduces to $(A_{Xe}/A_{Pb})^{b/3}$ at high $p_\perp$ and high centrality, depending only on the atomic numbers and the path-length exponent.

## Key numbers

* For linear path-length dependence ($b=1$), $R_L^{XePb} \approx 0.85$; for quadratic (LPM-like) dependence ($b=2$), $R_L^{XePb} \approx 0.73$.
* At high $p_\perp \rightarrow 100$ GeV, the numerical prediction for $R_L^{XePb}$ reaches a limiting value of 0.8, corresponding to a path-length exponent $b=1.4$ (between linear and quadratic) for the dynamical energy loss formalism.
* The atomic numbers are $A=208$ for Pb and $A=129$ for Xe; the collision energies are 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb and 5.44 TeV for Xe+Xe.
* When collision energy changes from 2.76 to 5.02 TeV (almost a factor of two), the influence on suppression is less than 10%.

## Context

This work extends the study of path-length dependence in energy loss mechanisms, which is crucial for distinguishing between radiative versus collisional energy loss and for resolving the $v_2$ puzzle (the inability of many models to jointly explain $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ measurements, as discussed by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2016)). The proposed observable $R_L^{AB}$ enables a direct comparison of theoretical models with upcoming LHC data from Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions, and is intended to advance high-precision QGP tomography by constraining QGP properties from both high and low $p_\perp$ data.
