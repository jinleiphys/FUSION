# Two-body nonleptonic decays of the heavy mesons in the factorization approach

> In the framework of the factorization approach we calculate the branching fractions of 100 two-body nonleptonic decay channels in total, including 44 channels of the charm meson decays and 56 channels of the bottom meson decays. For charm meson decays, we test and confirm the previous observation that taking the limit for the number of colors $N\to\infty$ significantly improves theoretical predictions. For bottom meson decays, the penguin contributions are included in addition. As an essential input, we employ the weak decay form factors obtained in the framework of the relativistic quark model based on the quasi-potential approach. These form factors have well been tested by calculating observables in the semileptonic $D$ and $B$ meson decays and confronting obtained results with experimental data. In general, the predictions for the nonleptonic decay branching fractions are acceptable. However, for a quantitative calculation it is necessary to account for a more subtle effects of the final-state interaction.

## Key claim

The factorization approach, using form factors from a relativistic quark model, yields acceptable predictions for branching fractions of two-body nonleptonic charm and bottom meson decays, but quantitative accuracy requires accounting for final-state interaction effects; for charm decays, the empirical rule of taking the limit \(N\to\infty\) for the number of colors significantly improves agreement with experiment, though this rule is purely empirical and does not generalize to bottom decays.

## Method

The authors calculated branching fractions for 100 two-body nonleptonic decay channels (44 charm, 56 bottom) using the factorization approach based on the effective weak Hamiltonian. Weak decay form factors were taken from the relativistic quark model (RQM) based on the quasi-potential approach, previously tested in semileptonic decays. For charm decays, penguin contributions were neglected and calculations were performed for both \(N=3\) and the \(N\to\infty\) limit; for bottom decays, both tree-level and penguin loop-level contributions were included using Wilson coefficients at the scale \(\mu=m_b\).

- Total of 100 two-body nonleptonic decay channels calculated: 44 charm meson decays and 56 bottom meson decays.
- Fermi coupling constant: \(G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5} \text{ GeV}^{-2}\).
- Wilson coefficients for charm decays at scale \(\mu=m_c\): \(c_1 = 1.26\), \(c_2 = -0.51\).
- Wilson coefficients for bottom decays at scale \(\mu=m_b\): \(c_1 = 1.105\), \(c_2 = -0.228\), \(c_3 = 0.013\), \(c_4 = -0.029\), \(c_5 = 0.009\), \(c_6 = -0.033\), \(c_7/\alpha = 0.005\), \(c_8/\alpha = 0.060\), \(c_9/\alpha = -1.283\), \(c_{10}/\alpha = 0.266\).
- Decay constants used (in MeV): \(f_\pi = 130.2\), \(f_K = 155.6\), \(f_{K^*} = 217\), \(f_{D^+} = 212.7\), \(f_{D^0} = 211.6\), \(f_{D_s} = 249.9\), \(f_\rho = 205\), \(f_\omega = 187\), \(f_\phi = 215\).
- Running quark masses at scale \(\mu=m_b\) (in MeV): \(m_u = 1.86\), \(m_d = 4.22\), \(m_c = 901\), \(m_s = 80\), \(m_b = 4200\).
- CKM parameters: \(\lambda = 0.2265\), \(A = 0.790\), \(\overline{\rho} = 0.141\), \(\overline{\eta} = 0.357\).
- \(\eta-\eta'\) mixing angle: \(\theta = -15.4^\circ\) (corresponding to \(\phi = 39.3^\circ\)).
- For charm decays with \(N\to\infty\), results for channels such as \(D^+\to\pi^0\pi^+\), \(D^0\to K^+K^-\), \(D^0\to K^-\rho^+\) were altered by a factor of about 2 compared to \(N=3\); for \(D^+\to\pi^+\phi\), \(D^0\to\eta\eta\), \(D_s\to K^+\bar K^0\) and \(D_s\to K^+\pi^0\), results changed by one or two orders of magnitude.
- For bottom decays, channels such as \(B^+\to \rho^+ \eta^{(')}\) and \(B_s\to D^{*-}_s \rho^+\) matched experimental values within \(1.5\,\sigma\) uncertainty for \(N=3\).
- For the penguin-governed decay \(B^+\to\pi^+\phi\), the result for \(N=2\) deviated from \(N=3\) by two orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonleptonic heavy meson decays using the factorization approach, notably by employing form factors from the relativistic quark model of Faustov and Galkin (2019, 2022) that were previously tested in semileptonic decays. For charm decays, it confirms the empirical observation from Tadic (1982), Bauer (1984), and Buras (1985) that taking \(N\to\infty\) improves predictions, and discusses the compensation between factorizable \(1/N\) terms and nonfactorizable contributions as demonstrated by Halperin (1994) using light cone sum rules. For bottom decays, the results are compared with the generalized factorization approach of Chen (1999), and the need for final-state interaction effects is highlighted, particularly for color-suppressed channels such as \(B^0\to\pi^0\pi^0\) and \(B^0\to\rho^0\rho^0\), where strong interactions in the \(\pi\pi-K\bar K\) system (studied by the authors in previous work) play an essential role.
