# Sensitivity of nucleon-nucleus scattering to the off-shell behavior of on-shell equivalent NN potentials

> The sensitivity of nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering to the off-shell behavior of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions is investigated when on-shell equivalent nucleon-nucleon potentials are used. The study is based on applications of the full-folding optical model potential for an explicit treatment of the off-shell behavior of the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction. Applications were made at beam energies between 40 and 500 MeV for proton scattering from 40Ca and 208Pb. We use the momentum-dependent Paris potential and its local on-shell equivalent as obtained with the Gelfand-Levitan and Marchenko inversion formalism for the two nucleon Schroedinger equation. Full-folding calculations for nucleon-nucleus scattering show small fluctuations in the corresponding observables. This implies that off-shell features of the NN interaction cannot be unambiguously identified with these processes. Inversion potentials were also constructed directly from NN phase-shift data (SM94) in the 0-1.3 GeV energy range. Their use in proton-nucleus scattering above 200 MeV provide a superior description of the observables relative to those obtained from current realistic NN potentials. Limitations and scope of our findings are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering observables show only small fluctuations (up to about 10% difference) when using on-shell equivalent NN potentials that differ in their off-shell behavior, implying that off-shell features of the NN interaction cannot be unambiguously identified through these scattering processes. However, inversion potentials constructed directly from NN phase-shift data (SM94) in the 0-1.3 GeV energy range provide a superior description of proton-nucleus scattering observables above 200 MeV compared to results from current realistic NN potentials.

## Method

The authors used the full-folding optical model potential to treat the off-shell behavior of the NN effective interaction explicitly, calculating both free t-matrices and in-medium g-matrices from the Paris potential and its local on-shell equivalent obtained via Gelfand-Levitan and Marchenko inversion formalism. They solved the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation for symmetric nuclear matter to obtain medium-dependent g-matrices at several densities (Fermi momenta up to 1.4 fm^-1) and total NN center-of-mass momenta in the 0-7 fm^-1 interval, then computed differential cross sections and analyzing powers for proton elastic scattering from 40Ca and 208Pb at beam energies between 40 and 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 40 to 500 MeV for proton scattering from 40Ca and 208Pb
- Differences in NA observables between Paris and its inversion equivalent: up to 10% in magnitude of calculated observables
- Inversion potentials constructed from NN phase-shift data (SM94) in the 0-1.3 GeV energy range
- g-matrices calculated at Fermi momenta up to 1.4 fm^-1 and total NN c.m. momenta in the 0-7 fm^-1 interval
- Phase-shift equivalence verified for NN channels with L ≤ 2 in the 0-1.2 GeV energy range
- Inversion scheme used for all NN channels with total angular momentum J ≤ 2

## Context

This work extends previous full-folding model studies by Arellano et al. (1990, 1994, 1995) that demonstrated the need for accurate treatment of off-shell behavior in NN interactions for nucleon-nucleus scattering. The results contradict earlier expectations that on-shell equivalent NN potentials would provide distinctive predictions in NN bremsstrahlung (Jetter and von Geramb, 1995), showing instead that even the more complete off-shell sampling in full-folding calculations cannot unambiguously discriminate among such potentials. The findings enable the use of quantum inversion as a practical connection between NN and NA elastic scattering, allowing model-independent two-body data to be linked with many-body data, and suggest that NN potentials constructed via inversion from phase-shift data can provide reliable predictions for full-folding model calculations.
