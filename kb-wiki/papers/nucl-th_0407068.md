# Pion photoproduction on nucleons in a covariant hadron-exchange model

> We present a relativistic dynamical model of pion photoproduction on the nucleon in the resonance region. It offers several advances over the existing approaches. The model is obtained by extending our $πN$-scattering description to the electromagnetic channels. The resulting photopion amplitude is thus unitary in the $πN$, $\ga N$ channel space, Watson's theorem is exactly satisfied. At this stage we have included the pion, nucleon, $\De(1232)$-resonance degrees of freedom. The $ρ$ and $ω$ meson exchanges are also included, but play a minor role in the considered energy domain (up to $\sqrt{s}=1.5$ GeV). In this energy range the model provides a good description of all the important multipoles. We have allowed for only two free parameters -- the photocouplings of the $Δ$-resonance. These couplings are adjusted to reproduce the strength of corresponding resonant-multipoles $M_{1+}$ and $E_{1+}$ at the resonance position.

## Key claim

The model provides a good description of all important pion-photoproduction multipoles up to center-of-mass energy 1.5 GeV using only two free parameters (the photocouplings of the Delta resonance), but the authors note that the problematic M1- multipoles could be corrected by including an explicit Roper-resonance exchange.

## Method

The authors extend their previous relativistic dynamical model of pion-nucleon scattering to electromagnetic channels by solving a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in the pi N and gamma N channel space, using an equal-time quasipotential reduction. The driving force includes single-particle exchanges of the pion, nucleon, Delta(1232) resonance, and rho and omega mesons, with the pion-nucleon amplitude fully constrained by prior fits to pi N scattering data. Gauge invariance is maintained by implementing minimal substitution on the form factors themselves, following the method of Gross and Riska.

## Key numbers

* The model is applied up to center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 1.5 GeV, with rho and omega meson exchanges playing a minor role in this energy domain.
* The Delta resonance mass is taken as m_Delta = 1232 MeV, with nucleon mass m_N = 0.9383 GeV and pion mass m_pi = 0.139 GeV.
* The fitted Delta photocouplings are g_M = 2.8 +/- 0.2 and g_E = 1.5 +/- 0.5.
* The bare E2/M1 ratio for the gamma N to Delta transition is R_EM^(bare) = (3.8 +/- 1.6)%, while the physical multipole ratio is R_EM^(multipoles) = (-2.6 +/- 0.6)%.
* At the Delta resonance position, the imaginary parts of the resonant multipoles are Im E1+^(3/2) = -1.0 +/- 0.2 and Im M1+^(3/2) = 38.5 +/- 1.5 (in units of 10^{-3}/m_pi).
* Threshold electric dipole multipole predictions include E0+(pi+ n) = 26.3, E0+(pi- p) = -29.6, E0+(pi0 p) = -1.4, and E0+(pi0 n) = 1.0 (in units of 10^{-3}/m_pi+).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dynamical model of pion-nucleon interaction (Pascalutsa and Tjon, 1998, 2000) to photoproduction, providing a unitary framework that automatically satisfies Watson's theorem. It differs from the DMT model by generating resonance contributions dynamically rather than using fitted Breit-Wigner forms, and from the model of Sato and Lee by using a field-theoretic Bethe-Salpeter approach with renormalization of dressed baryon-pole contributions. The model enables extraction of Delta resonance photocouplings and the E2/M1 ratio in a covariant framework that can be extended to higher energies and embedded in few-nucleon calculations.
