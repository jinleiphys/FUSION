# Exploring binding energy and separation energy dependences of HBT strength

> Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) results of the nucleon-nucleon correlation function have been presented for the nuclear reactions with neutron-rich projectiles (Be isotopes) using an event-generator, the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. We explore that the relationship between the binding energy per nucleon of the projectiles and the strength of the neutron-proton HBT at small relative momentum. Moreover, we reveal the relationship between the single neutron separation energy and the strength of the halo neutron-proton HBT. Results show that neutron-proton HBT results are sensitive to binding energy or separation energy.

## Key claim

The strength of the neutron-proton HBT correlation function at small relative momentum is sensitive to the binding energy per nucleon of the projectile, and the strength of the proton-halo neutron HBT correlation function is sensitive to the single-neutron separation energy, but the latter correlation function is almost impossible to measure experimentally and is only of theoretical interest.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) transport model to simulate head-on collisions of Be isotopes (projectile) on a \(^{12}\)C target at 800 MeV/nucleon incident energy. They constructed two-particle correlation functions from the phase-space distributions at freeze-out, using a coalescence model to define emitted nucleons (clusters with \(A \geq 2\) require nucleons within \(r_{min} \leq 3.5\) fm and \(p_{min} \leq 300\) MeV/c). They extracted the correlation strength at a relative momentum of 5 MeV/c and plotted it against the binding energy per nucleon and the single-neutron separation energy of the projectile.

## Key numbers

- The correlation strength \(C_{PN}\) at 5 MeV/c rises with increasing binding energy per nucleon \(E_{binding}\) for Be isotopes, as shown by a linear fit in the insert of Fig. 2.
- The correlation strength \(C_{PH}\) at 5 MeV/c rises with increasing single-neutron separation energy \(E_{sep}\) for Be isotopes, as shown by a linear fit in Fig. 3.
- The IDQMD model uses a soft equation of state with incompressibility \(K = 200\) MeV and symmetry strength \(C = 32\) MeV.
- The in-medium NN cross section uses a factor \(f \approx -0.2\) relative to the free cross section \(\sigma_{NN}^{free}\).
- The two-halo neutron correlation function from \(^{14}\)Be fragmentation into \(^{12}\)Be + 2n at 35 MeV/nucleon on a \(^{12}\)C target reproduces experimental data (Fig. 1).

## Context

This work extends the application of HBT intensity interferometry from high-energy heavy-ion collisions (used to study quark-gluon plasma and source geometry) to the study of exotic, neutron-rich nuclei at intermediate energies. It builds on prior studies of halo nuclei such as \(^{14}\)Be, \(^{11}\)Li, and \(^{11}\)Be (Tanihata 1985, Kobayashi 1988, Suzuki 1992) and on previous HBT investigations of isospin dependence (Ghetti 2002), impact parameter (Gong 1991), and nuclear symmetry energy (Chen 2003). The results suggest that HBT correlation functions could serve as a new probe of nuclear structure, particularly binding and separation energies, though the proton-halo neutron correlation function is not experimentally accessible.
