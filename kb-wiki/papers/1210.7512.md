# Bottomonia in the Quark Gluon Plasma

> I review recent calculations of the suppression of bottomonium states in heavy ion collisions. A non-relativistic potential is used which is complex valued. This allows one to extract the binding energies and decay widths of the ground and excited states of bottomonium as a function of the typical plasma particle momentum and momentum-space anisotropy. The decay widths determined are used as input and integrated over space-time taking into account the dynamical evolution of the typical particle momentum and momentum-space anisotropy. The suppression of Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), chi_b1, and chi_b2 is obtained as a function of centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum. The obtained results are compared with data from the STAR and CMS collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a dynamical model incorporating a complex-valued heavy-quark potential and momentum-space anisotropy can reproduce the measured suppression of bottomonium states at both RHIC and LHC energies, but the comparison is limited by large experimental uncertainties (e.g., a ±14% global uncertainty for Upsilon(1s) and ±21% for Upsilon(2s) from CMS), making firm conclusions about the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio difficult.

## Method

The author uses a non-relativistic potential model with a complex-valued potential, where the real part is derived from the internal energy of the system and the imaginary part from leading-order perturbative calculations in the small anisotropy limit. The 3D Schrodinger equation is solved numerically to obtain the real and imaginary parts of the binding energy for Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), chi_b1, and chi_b2 as functions of the hard momentum scale p_hard and the momentum-space anisotropy parameter xi. These widths are then integrated over space-time using a dynamical model that evolves p_hard and xi, and the resulting suppression factor R_AA is computed by averaging over the transverse plane weighted by the binary collision distribution from a Glauber model.

## Key numbers

- Isotropic dissociation scale for Upsilon(1s): 593 MeV (value of p_hard at which real and imaginary parts of binding energy become equal)
- Isotropic dissociation scale for Upsilon(2s): 228 MeV
- Bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.2 GeV; charm quark mass: m_c = 1.3 GeV
- Upsilon state mass: m_Upsilon = 10 GeV
- RHIC collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV/nucleon
- LHC collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV/nucleon
- Central temperatures at LHC for different viscosity assumptions: T_0 = {520, 504, 494} MeV at tau_0 = 0.3 fm/c for 4 pi eta/S = {1, 2, 3} respectively
- Central temperature at RHIC: 433 MeV (adjusted to 461 MeV for different initial condition type)
- Charged particle multiplicity at RHIC: dN_ch/dy = 620; at LHC: dN_ch/dy = 1400
- Feed-down fractions for Upsilon(1s): f_i = {0.510, 0.107, 0.008, 0.27, 0.105} for Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), chi_b1, chi_b2 respectively
- Feed-down fractions for inclusive Upsilon(2s): f_i = {0.500, 0.500} for Upsilon(2s) and Upsilon(3s)
- Estimated shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from both RHIC and LHC data: 0.08 < eta/S < 0.24

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical developments on heavy quarkonium in the quark gluon plasma, including first-principles calculations of the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential by Laine et al. (2006) and the effective field theory approach by Brambilla et al. (2008). It also builds on earlier studies of momentum-space anisotropy effects on quarkonium binding by Dumitru et al. (2007, 2009) and Burnier et al. (2009). The results enable comparison with experimental data from the STAR and CMS collaborations, and the framework can be refined with future LHC data to more precisely determine the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the quark gluon plasma.
