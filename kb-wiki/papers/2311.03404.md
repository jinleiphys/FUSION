# The Weakly Bound States in Gaussian Wells: From the Binding Energy of Deuteron to the Electronic Structure of Quantum Dots

> Gaussian potentials serve as a valuable tool for the comprehensive modeling of short-range interactions, spanning applications from nuclear physics to the artificial confinement of electrons within quantum dots. This study focuses on examining the lowest states within Gaussian wells, with particular emphasis on the weakly bound regime. The analysis delves into the asymptotic behavior of the exact wave function at both small and large distances, motivating the development of a few-parametric Ansatz which is locally accurate and yields to a fast convergent basis set. To validate its efficacy, we assess its convergence rate using a toy model of Nuclear Physics, specifically for Deuteron. Furthermore, we employ the expansion of the energy close to the threshold to derive an analytical formula for the binding energy of the deuteron whose accuracy improves as the effective parameter approaches the critical. In concluding our investigation, we evaluate the performance of our Ansatz as an orbital in the exploration of the electronic structure of a two-electron quantum dot.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a compact, few-parametric trial wave function, constructed from the asymptotic behavior of the exact solution at small and large distances, yields rapid convergence for the binding energy of the deuteron and competitive results for two-electron quantum dots, but only when the effective potential parameter is sufficiently far from the critical value where the state becomes unbound.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Schrödinger equation for a particle in a Gaussian well in two and three dimensions using the Lagrange Mesh Method (LMM) to obtain highly accurate reference energies and critical parameters. They then constructed a three-parameter variational Ansatz by interpolating the asymptotic expansions of the wave function at small and large distances. For the deuteron, they used a linear superposition of up to three such Ansatz functions to compute the ground-state energy, and they also applied an energy expansion near the threshold to derive an analytical formula for the deuteron binding energy. For the two-electron quantum dot, they used the compact Ansatz as a single-particle orbital to construct the two-electron wave function.

## Key numbers

*   For the deuteron with \(\Lambda = 4\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), the three-term variational Ansatz yields an energy of \(-2.2242\ \text{MeV}\), matching the LMM result and comparing with \(-2.224(1)\ \text{MeV}\) from an artificial neural network (ANN-VMC) and \(-2.223(1)\ \text{MeV}\) from conventional VMC with a Jastrow spline (VMC-JS).
*   For the deuteron with \(\Lambda = 6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), the three-term Ansatz gives \(-2.2244\ \text{MeV}\), compared to \(-2.224(4)\ \text{MeV}\) (ANN-VMC) and \(-2.220(1)\ \text{MeV}\) (VMC-JS).
*   The analytical expansion near the threshold (Equation 19) estimates the deuteron binding energy as \(-2.2300\ \text{MeV}\) for \(\Lambda = 4\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and \(-2.2263\ \text{MeV}\) for \(\Lambda = 6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), with an error of about 0.3% relative to the most accurate results.
*   The critical parameter \(v_0^c\) for the 1s state in three dimensions is \(1.342\,002\) (Hartrees), in agreement with the value \(1.342\,0023\) from Liverts (2011).
*   For the 2s state in three dimensions, the critical parameter is \(8.897\,850\), matching the value \(8.897\,85\) from Liverts (2011).

## Context

This work extends prior studies on Gaussian wells, which are used to model short-range interactions in nuclear physics (e.g., for the deuteron as in Barnea (2015) and Carleo (2021)) and artificial confinement in quantum dots (e.g., as in Chaudhuri and Boyacioglu (2007)). The compact variational Ansatz provides a computationally efficient alternative to more complex methods such as artificial neural networks (Carleo 2021) or spline-based Jastrow functions (Barnea 2015). The results enable rapid estimation of binding energies for weakly bound states and can be applied to more complex few-body systems in both nuclear and condensed-matter physics.
