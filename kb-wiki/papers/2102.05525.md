# The relation between instant and light-front formulations of quantum field theory

> The scattering equivalence of quantum field theories formulated with light-front and instant-form kinematic subgroups is established using non-perturbative methods. The difficulty with field theoretic formulations of Dirac's forms of dynamics is that the free and interacting unitary representations of the Poincaré group are defined on inequivalent representations of the Hilbert space, which means that the concept of kinematic transformations must be modified on the Hilbert space of the field theory. This work addresses this problem by assuming the existence of a field theory with the expected properties and constructs equivalent representations with instant and front form kinematic subgroups. In this construction both the light-front and instant-form formulations share the same vacuum and one-particle states. The free field Fock space plays no role. There is no "quantization" of a classical theory. The property that survives from the perturbative approach is the notion of a kinematic subgroup, which means kinematic Poincaré transformations can be trivially implemented by acting on suitable basis vectors. This non-perturbative approach avoids dealing with issues that arise in perturbative treatments where is it necessary to have a consistent treatment of renormalization, rotational covariance, and the structure of the light-front vacuum. While addressing these issues in a computational framework is important for applications, this work may provide some insight into the nature of the expected resolution and identifies the origin of some of differences between the perturbative and non-perturbative approaches.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the scattering equivalence of quantum field theories formulated with light-front and instant-form kinematic subgroups using non-perturbative methods, assuming the existence of a field theory with expected axiomatic properties rather than starting from a perturbative free-field Fock space. The construction avoids issues arising from Haag's theorem by using a two-Hilbert space representation where both formulations share the same vacuum and one-particle states, but the author notes that addressing these issues in a computational framework remains the most important unanswered question for applications.

## Method

The author assumes the existence of a quantum field theory with a unitary representation of the Poincaré group, a unique vacuum, and asymptotically complete Haag-Ruelle scattering states. Using a two-Hilbert space formulation, injection operators are constructed that map an asymptotic Hilbert space (a Fock space with physical particle masses) to the field theory Hilbert space, with the property that kinematic Poincaré transformations on the asymptotic space are mapped to the corresponding kinematic subgroup on the field theory Hilbert space. Ekstein's theorem (1960) on S-matrix preserving unitary transformations is then applied to construct scattering-equivalent representations with instant-form and light-front kinematic subgroups, where the unitary transformations leave the vacuum and one-particle states unchanged.

## Key numbers

- The light-front kinematic subgroup is a seven-parameter subgroup of the Poincaré group, while the instant-form kinematic subgroup is generated by rotations and spatial translations (three rotations plus three translations).
- The change of basis between instant-form and light-front basis states involves a factor \(\sqrt{p^+ / \omega_m(\mathbf{p})}\) and a Melosh rotation \(D^s_{\mu_F \mu_I}[B_F^{-1}(p/m) B_I(p/m)]\).
- The mass Casimir operator is assumed to have point-spectrum eigenstates with strictly positive discrete mass eigenvalues representing particles of the theory.

## Context

This work extends the equivalence of different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics established by Sokolov and Shatnyi (1977) and Keister (1996) to the field theory case, where Haag's theorem (1955) prevents the decomposition of the Hamiltonian into free plus interacting parts on a single Hilbert space. The non-perturbative approach avoids the complications of perturbative treatments discussed extensively in the literature by Chang (1969), Soper, Leutwyler (1970), Rohrlich (1971), Yan (1973), Brodsky (1998), and others, particularly the need for consistent renormalization, rotational covariance, and understanding of the light-front vacuum structure. The construction enables a rigorous comparison between instant-form and light-front formulations without assuming a free-field Fock space, and may provide insight into the origin of differences between perturbative and non-perturbative approaches.
