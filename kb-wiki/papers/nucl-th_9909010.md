# Effects of new non-linear couplings in relativistic effective field theory

> We extend the relativistic mean field theory model of Sugahara and Toki (TM1) by adding new couplings suggested by modern effective field theories. An improved set of parameters (TM1*) is developed with the goal to test the ability of the models based on effective field theory to describe the properties of finite nuclei and, at the same time, to be consistent with the trends of Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations at densities away from the saturation region. We compare our calculations with other relativistic nuclear force parameters for various nuclear phenomena.

# Effects of new non-linear couplings in relativistic effective field theory

## Key claim
The paper extends the TM1 relativistic mean field model by adding scalar-vector and vector-vector couplings motivated by effective field theory, producing a new parameter set (TM1*) that improves agreement with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations at high density while maintaining good finite-nuclei descriptions. The authors emphasize that their goal is not to produce a new optimal parameter set competing with established sets like NL3, but rather to learn whether the new EFT couplings can simultaneously describe finite nuclei and tune the density dependence of scalar and vector self-energies.

## Method
The authors start from the TM1 parameter set of Sugahara and Toki (1994) and add the non-linear couplings $\zeta_0$ (quartic vector self-interaction), $\eta_1$ (third-order scalar-vector coupling), and $\eta_2$ (fourth-order scalar-vector coupling) to the nuclear matter sector, fixing saturation properties to those of TM1. For finite nuclei, they additionally adjust the surface parameters $\alpha_1$, $\alpha_2$, and the tensor coupling $f_v$ by fitting to the binding energy, charge radius, and 1p spin-orbit splitting of $^{16}$O, while the isovector parameters $\eta_\rho$ and $f_\rho$ are determined from the energy of $^{208}$Pb and a fixed bulk symmetry energy of 36.9 MeV. The Dirac equation is solved by transforming it into a Schrodinger-like equation using a standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock code.

## Key numbers
- Saturation properties of TM1* are fixed to those of TM1: the specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text, but the meson masses are set to $m_s = 511.198$ MeV, $m_v = 783$ MeV, $m_\rho = 770$ MeV, and the nucleon mass to $M = 938$ MeV.
- The chosen values for the new couplings are $\zeta_0 = 3.6$, $\eta_1 = 1.1$, and $\eta_2 = 0.1$, which keep all coefficients natural (order unity).
- The bulk symmetry energy coefficient is fixed to $J = 36.9$ MeV, corresponding to the difference between neutron and nuclear matter DBHF energies per particle at saturation density.
- TM1* reproduces experimental binding energies of magic nuclei within approximately 0.8%.
- The pairing gap for even-even nuclei is taken as $\Delta = 11.2/\sqrt{A}$ MeV, following the Bohr and Mottelson formula.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean field model of Sugahara and Toki (1994), specifically the TM1 parameter set, by incorporating additional non-linear couplings from the effective field theory framework developed by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1996, 1997). The study builds on earlier findings that adding a quartic vector self-interaction (as in Bodmer (1989) and Sugahara and Toki (1994)) improves agreement with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, but notes that those models omitted other couplings that should be present in a fourth-order EFT truncation. The TM1* parameter set enables future studies of nuclear structure phenomena including isotopic energy differences, charge radius changes, and nuclei with large neutron or proton excess, while also providing a softer high-density equation of state consistent with observed neutron star masses as discussed by Serot and Walecka (1996).
