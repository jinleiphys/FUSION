# Probe the tilted Quark-Gluon Plasma with charmonium directed flow

> Charmonium directed flows are studied based on transport model coupled with the realistic three dimensional expansions of the bulk medium. The non-central symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions can generate the rotating quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with symmetry-breaking longitudinal distributions. In $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au-Au semi-central collisions, charmonium are primordially produced in the initial hard process, they are mainly dissociated by the initial tilted source with high temperatures and then move out of the bulk medium to keep the early information of the medium. Charmonia are less affected by the hydrodynamic expansions of QGP where its tilted shape is being diluted. This biased dissociation can generate directed flows of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ which are much larger than the values of light charged hadrons and open heavy flavor. They are sensitive and clean to the effect of QGP rapidity-odd initial energy densities.

## Key claim

Charmonium directed flows are proposed as a clean probe of the rapidity-odd (tilted) initial energy density distribution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in non-central heavy-ion collisions, because charmonia are primarily dissociated in the early, highly tilted stage of the QGP and are less affected by later hydrodynamic expansion or external electromagnetic fields.

## Method

The authors couple a transport model for charmonium evolution (including dissociation via inelastic scattering with gluons, but neglecting regeneration) to a (3+1)D hydrodynamic simulation (MUSIC) of the bulk QGP medium in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV Au+Au collisions. The initial QGP entropy density is parametrized with a rapidity-odd tilt controlled by a parameter \(\eta_T\), and charmonium primordial distributions are obtained from pp cross sections scaled by cold nuclear matter effects (EPS09 NLO shadowing, \(\mathcal{R}_{AB}=0.85\)). The transport equation is solved from \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/c, with charmonia initially free-streaming before that time.

## Key numbers

- \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) directed flows in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV Au+Au collisions at impact parameter \(b=8.3\) fm (centrality 0-80%) are several times larger than charged hadron \(v_1 \sim 0.005\).
- For \(p_T>4\) GeV/c, prompt \(J/\psi\) directed flow reaches \(\sim 0.05\) and prompt \(\psi(2S)\) reaches \(\sim 0.11\) at forward/backward rapidity \(|\eta|\sim 2\).
- The median values of \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) directed flows in \(p_T>4\) GeV/c are \(0.03\) and \(0.07\) respectively in the backward rapidity, about 50% larger than the \(p_T>0\) case.
- The tilt parameter \(\eta_T\) is varied between \(\eta_T=2\) (larger tilt) and \(\eta_T=4\) (smaller tilt) to show sensitivity; the default value is \(\eta_T=3.36\).
- The shadowing factor is taken as \(\mathcal{R}_{AB}=0.85\); the mean squared transverse momentum at central rapidity is \(\langle p_T^2\rangle|_{y_p=0}=4.14\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of directed flow from light charged hadrons (Bozek 2010, Adamczyk 2014) and D-mesons (Chatterjee 2017, Adam 2019) to charmonia, arguing that charmonium \(v_1\) is a cleaner probe of the initial QGP tilt because it arises from biased dissociation rather than from hydrodynamic drag. The transport model builds on prior rate-equation and transport approaches (Zhu 2004, Yan 2006, Yao 2018) and uses the (3+1)D hydrodynamic code MUSIC (Schenke 2010). The results suggest that measuring charmonium directed flow at RHIC energies can help constrain the magnitude of the rapidity-odd tilt in the initial QGP energy density, a measurement that is more ambiguous at LHC energies where regeneration dominates.
