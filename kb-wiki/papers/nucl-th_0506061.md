# Multifragmentation reactions and properties of hot stellar matter at subnuclear densities

> We point out similarity of thermodynamic conditions reached in intermediate energy nuclear collisions and in supernova explosions. We show that a statistical approach, which has been previously applied for nuclear multifragmentation reactions, can be very useful for description of electro-neutral stellar matter. Then properties of hot unstable nuclei extracted from analysis of multifragmentation data can be used for construction of a realistic equation of state of supernova matter.

## Key claim

The thermodynamic conditions (temperatures of 0.5–10 MeV and baryon densities of 10⁻⁵–2 ρ₀) reached in intermediate-energy nuclear collisions are similar to those in supernova explosions, so a statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) previously applied to nuclear reactions can be adapted to describe electro-neutral stellar matter, provided that possible deviations from equilibration for weak and electromagnetic interactions are explicitly taken into account.

## Method

The authors modified the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) by including electrons and neutrinos in the statistical ensemble, formulated for different assumptions about weak reaction rates. The model assumes statistical equilibrium at a low-density freeze-out stage, treating light nuclei (A ≤ 4) as a nuclear gas and heavier nuclei (A > 4) as heated liquid drops, with Coulomb interactions described via the Wigner-Seitz approximation. Different break-up channels are generated by Monte Carlo sampling according to their statistical weights (∝ exp(S_f)), and after break-up, Coulomb acceleration and secondary de-excitation of primary hot fragments are taken into account.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures reached in supernova conditions: T ≈ (0.5–10) MeV.
- Baryon densities reached in supernova conditions: ρ_B ≈ (10⁻⁵–2) ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ (normal nuclear density).
- At excitation energies above 12 MeV/nucleon, the vaporization channel (all fragments A ≤ 4) wins over the compound nucleus channel for ²³⁸U; the compound nucleus channel dies out at 2–3 MeV/nucleon.
- At 5 MeV/nucleon excitation energy, the total ensemble of fragmentation channels has a 0.2 higher entropy per nucleon than the compound nucleus channel for ²³⁸U, giving a relative probability of the CN channel of exp(ΔS) = exp(-0.2·238) ≈ 2·10⁻²¹.
- Characteristic reaction times for nuclear interactions at supernova temperatures and densities: 10 to 10⁶ fm/c.
- At very low densities (< 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶ ρ₀), photo-nuclear reactions are more efficient than nuclear interactions.
- At high densities (ρ_B ≳ 10⁻³ ρ₀), neutrinos are trapped in a nascent neutron star.

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation approach (SMM) from nuclear reactions to astrophysical conditions, directly addressing shortcomings of the widely used equation of state (EOS) from Lamb and Lattimer (1981), which was based on cold or nearly cold nuclei (T ≲ 1 MeV) and reduced the ensemble of hot nuclei to a single "average nucleus." The authors previously introduced this modified SMM for supernova conditions in Botvina (2004). The approach enables construction of a more realistic EOS for supernova matter that can be implemented into modern hydrodynamical simulations (e.g., Janka, 2001) and may also apply to neutrino-driven protoneutron star winds (Thompson, 2003).
