# Effects of correlations on the total neutron-Nucleus cross section at high energies

> The total neutron-Nucleus cross section has been calculated within an approach which takes into account nucleon-nucleon correlations, Glauber multiple scattering and inelastic shadowing corrections. Nuclear targets ranging from 4He to 208Pb and neutron incident momentum ranging from 3 to 300 GeV/c, have been considered. Correlations have been introduced by two different approaches leading to the same results. The commonly used approximation, consisting in treating nuclear effects only by a product of one-body densities, is carefully analyzed and it is shown that the effects of realistic correlations resulting from modern nucleon-nucleon interactions and realistic correlations resulting from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and microscopic ground state calculation of nuclear properties cannot be disregarded.

## Key claim

The authors show that two-nucleon correlations increase the total neutron-nucleus cross section by about 10% compared to calculations using only the one-body density approximation, and that this effect is comparable in magnitude to the effect of Gribov inelastic shadowing. The abstract's claim that correlations "cannot be disregarded" is qualified by the finding that three- and four-nucleon correlations produce negligible effects on the total cross section.

## Method

The total neutron-nucleus cross section was calculated using Glauber multiple scattering theory with Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections, incorporating realistic nucleon-nucleon correlations from the Argonne V8' interaction. Two independent approaches were used: an expansion of the nuclear wave function in terms of one- and two-body density matrices with two-body contractions, and a cluster expansion method based on a distorted one-body density matrix. The calculations used nuclear targets from 4He to 208Pb and neutron incident momenta from 3 to 300 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Correlations increase the total cross section by about 10% compared to the one-body density approximation
- The optical limit approximation for A=16 is valid within 1%
- Nuclear targets considered: 4He, 12C, 16O, and 208Pb
- Neutron incident momentum range: 3 to 300 GeV/c
- Three- and four-nucleon correlations produce negligible effects on the total cross section (tested for 4He)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of total neutron-nucleus cross sections that used only the one-body density approximation (Glauber 1959, Gribov 1969) and inelastic shadowing corrections (Murthy 1975, Nikolaev 1975). The authors show that the common approximation of neglecting all terms beyond the first in the expansion of the nuclear wave function in terms of density matrices (Foldy 1956) is inadequate, as correlations produce effects comparable to inelastic shadowing. The method enables future calculations of nuclear and color transparencies in (e,e'p) and (p,2p) reactions using fully correlated wave functions for complex nuclei.
