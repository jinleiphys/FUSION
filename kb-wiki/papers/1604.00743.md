# Calculation of Spin Observables for Proton-Neutron Elastic Scattering in the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter equation applied to proton-neutron elastic scattering reveals a breaking of charge independence in the nucleon-nucleon system, expressed as a disagreement between the isospin T=1 parts of the M matrix for proton-proton and proton-neutron systems (M^1_{p-p} ≠ M^1_{p-n}), which persists even when electromagnetic interactions are turned off. The origin of this T_z dependence is attributed to the pseudovector coupling constant of the pion being required to change from the standard value f ~ 1 to approximately 1/20 to reproduce proton-proton elastic scattering data.

## Method
The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is expanded using a set of Gamma matrices, yielding simultaneous equations that describe both bound (deuteron) and scattering states of the nucleon-nucleon two-body system. The M matrix is constructed for the proton-neutron system by summing over isospin T=0 and T=1 components (using 1/2 Σ_{T=0,1} instead of the Σ_{T=1} used for proton-proton scattering), and spin observables are calculated from the density matrix elements a^T_{μντρ}. The irreducible kernel uses a meson-exchange model with a cut-off parameter Λ_0 ~ 500 MeV for the pion exchange interaction, and higher-order corrections beyond the Born approximation are evaluated by substituting the exact scattering state solution with an approximate one under an inverse square potential.

## Key numbers
- The cut-off parameter for the Born term is fixed at Λ_0 ~ 500 MeV.
- At 200 MeV laboratory energy, the differential cross section I_0(θ) is calculated as a function of center-of-mass scattering angle θ.
- For the axial-vector equation at 500 MeV laboratory energy, introducing a factor c = 1.2 shifts the spin correlation parameter A_yy(θ=90°) to ~ 0.2, close to the experimental data ~ 0.1 at 425 MeV laboratory energy.
- With c = 1.2, the spin correlation A_zz(90°) ~ 0.2, a rather smaller value than the experimental data of Ditzler.
- With c = 1.2, the spin correlation A_xx(90°) ~ 0.2.
- For the axial-vector equation with c = 1.2, the depolarization D_nn(θ) maximum value at θ ~ 75° changes to ~ 0.8.
- At 500 MeV laboratory energy, the differential cross section values at θ = 0° and 180° reduce to approximately 40% of the axial-vector case with no ^1P_1 resonance effect (14.8 × 0.4 ~ 6).
- At 200 MeV laboratory energy, the differential cross section changes to ~ 4.5 at θ = 0° and 180°.
- The spin transfer K_LL(θ) at θ ≥ 60° is reduced roughly ~ 0.1 by the ^3P_2 state correction for the axial-vector case without the ^1P_1 resonance effect.
- The spin transfer K_SS(θ) is reduced by approximately 0.1 at θ ≥ 120° by the ^3P_2 correction, and with the ^1P_1 resonance the curve decreases from ~ 0 at θ = 120° to ~ -0.17 at θ = 180°.

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter formalism previously applied to proton-proton elastic scattering by Kinpara to the proton-neutron system, calculating spin observables that are less experimentally constrained than the proton-proton case. The study enables comparison between axial-vector and pseudoscalar equations for the spin singlet wave, and demonstrates that higher-order corrections to the ^3P_2 state and resonant effects in the ^1P_1 state (possibly mediated by the sigma meson or rho^0 meson) are necessary to approach experimental data from Keeler, Clough, Bandyopadhyay, Ditzler, Shima, Warner, Barlett, McNaughton, and Axen. The work opens the question of whether the axial-vector or pseudoscalar component dominates the spin singlet wave, and suggests that virtual meson creation processes are required to fill remaining gaps between calculations and experiments.
