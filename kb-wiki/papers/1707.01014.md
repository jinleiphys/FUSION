# Effect of the charged-lepton's mass on the quasielastic neutrino cross sections

> Martini et al. [Phys. Rev. C 94, 015501 (2016)] recently observed that when the produced-lepton's mass plays an important role, the charged-current quasielastic cross section for muon neutrinos can be higher than that for electron neutrinos. Here I argue that this effect appears solely in the theoretical descriptions of nuclear effects in which nucleon knockout requires the energy and momentum transfers to lie in a narrow range of the kinematically allowed values.

# Effect of the charged-lepton's mass on the quasielastic neutrino cross sections

## Key claim
The observation by Martini et al. (2016) that charged-current quasielastic cross sections for muon neutrinos can exceed those for electron neutrinos at low energies and small scattering angles is not a general physical effect, but appears only in theoretical descriptions where nucleon knockout requires energy and momentum transfers to lie in a narrow range of kinematically allowed values.

## Method
The author compares cross-section ratios for muon and electron neutrino charged-current quasielastic scattering off carbon using the relativistic Fermi gas model (with and without Pauli blocking) and the spectral function approach (mean-field and full). The kinematically allowed regions of energy transfer ω and momentum transfer |q| are computed for neutrino energies of 200 MeV and 600 MeV at scattering angles of 5° and 60°, using standard nuclear parameters (Fermi momentum 221 MeV, separation energy 25 MeV).

## Key numbers
- For the relativistic Fermi gas model with Pauli blocking at 200 MeV and 5° scattering angle, the ratio dσ(νμ)/dσ(νe) = 1.57 (muon neutrino cross section higher than electron neutrino).
- For the relativistic Fermi gas model without Pauli blocking at 200 MeV and 5°, the ratio drops to 0.73 (electron neutrino cross section higher).
- For the full spectral function approach at 200 MeV and 5°, the ratio is 0.71; for the mean-field spectral function at the same kinematics, the ratio is 0.72.
- At 600 MeV and 60°, all models give ratios between 0.97 and 0.98, showing negligible mass effect.
- The muon mass is 105.66 MeV and the electron mass is 0.51 MeV.

## Context
This work directly addresses the finding of Martini et al. (2016) that muon neutrino quasielastic cross sections can exceed electron neutrino ones, which was obtained using continuum random phase approximation and local Fermi gas models. The author shows that this result is not reproduced by the relativistic Fermi gas model without Pauli blocking or by spectral function approaches (Benhar et al., 1989, 1994) that incorporate realistic nuclear structure and the observed lack of one-to-one correspondence between struck nucleon momentum and residual system excitation energy. The analysis enables better understanding of how nuclear model assumptions affect neutrino cross-section predictions relevant to oscillation experiments.
