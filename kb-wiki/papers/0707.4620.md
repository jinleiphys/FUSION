# Nuclear equation of state at high baryonic density and compact star constraints

> A mean field calculation is carried out to obtain the equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter from a density dependent M3Y interaction (DDM3Y). The energy per nucleon is minimized to obtain ground state of the symmetric nuclear matter (SNM). The constants of density dependence of the effective interaction are obtained by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon and the saturation density of SNM. The energy variation of the exchange potential is treated properly in the negative energy domain of nuclear matter. The EoS of SNM, thus obtained, is not only free from the superluminosity problem but also provides excellent estimate of nuclear incompressibility. The EoS of asymmetric nuclear matter is calculated by adding to the isoscalar part, the isovector component of M3Y interaction. The SNM and pure neutron matter EoS are used to calculate the nuclear symmetry energy which is found to be consistent with that extracted from the isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. The $β$ equilibrium proton fraction calculated from the symmetry energy and related theoretical findings are consistent with the constraints derived from the observations on compact stars.

# Nuclear equation of state at high baryonic density and compact star constraints

## Key claim
The density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) interaction, with the energy variation of the zero-range potential treated properly by allowing it to vary freely with the kinetic energy part of the energy per nucleon over the entire range, yields an equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter that is free from the superluminosity problem and provides an incompressibility of 274.7 ± 7.4 MeV, while the resulting nuclear symmetry energy is "soft" (peaking at about 1.95 times saturation density and becoming negative at about 4.7 times saturation density) and satisfies astrophysical constraints from compact star cooling.

## Method
A mean-field calculation using the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was performed to obtain the equation of state of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. The constants of density dependence (C and β) were determined by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon (−15.26 MeV) and saturation density (0.1533 fm⁻³) of cold symmetric nuclear matter, with the energy dependence of the zero-range potential treated as varying with the kinetic energy part of the energy per nucleon. The equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter was obtained by adding the isovector component of the M3Y interaction, and the nuclear symmetry energy was calculated from the difference between pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter energies.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³
- Saturation energy per nucleon: ε₀ = −15.26 MeV (volume energy coefficient from Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula)
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K₀ = 274.7 ± 7.4 MeV
- Density dependence constants: C = 2.2497 ± 0.0420, β = 1.5934 ± 0.0085 fm² (for n = 2/3)
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(ρ₀) = 30.71 ± 0.26 MeV (using the definition E_sym = ε(ρ,1) − ε(ρ,0)); alternative definition gives 30.03 ± 0.26 MeV
- Maximum β-equilibrium proton fraction: x_β ≈ 0.044 at ρ ≈ 1.35 ρ₀, going to zero at ρ ≈ 4.5 ρ₀
- Nuclear symmetry energy peaks at ρ ≈ 1.95 ρ₀ and becomes negative at ρ ≈ 4.7 ρ₀
- Incompressibility values for isospin asymmetry X = 0.0: 274.7 MeV; X = 0.1: 270.4 MeV; X = 0.2: 257.7 MeV; X = 0.3: 236.6 MeV; X = 0.4: 207.6 MeV; X = 0.5: 171.2 MeV
- Superluminosity in pure neutron matter occurs only at ρ > 12 ρ₀

## Context
This work extends the DDM3Y formalism from earlier calculations by Basu, Chowdhury, and Samanta (2006) that treated the energy dependence of the zero-range potential as fixed, which caused superluminosity problems. The present treatment resolves that issue and yields an incompressibility consistent with experimental values from giant monopole resonance (GMR) measurements (Sharma 1988: 300 ± 25 MeV) and hard photon production in heavy-ion collisions (Schutz 1996: 290 ± 50 MeV), as well as with relativistic mean-field models (NL3 parameter set gives 271.76 MeV). The symmetry energy agrees with the value extracted from isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions by Chen et al. (2005) at subnormal densities and with the volume symmetry coefficient from atomic masses (Möller 2006: 30.048 ± 0.004 MeV). The β-equilibrium proton fraction forbids direct URCA cooling, consistent with the theoretical constraint that acceptable equations of state should not allow direct URCA in neutron stars below 1.5 solar masses (Klähn 2006). The work enables unified description of nuclear matter properties, proton radioactivity lifetimes, and compact star constraints.
