# Short-ranged radial and tensor correlations in nuclear many-body systems

> The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) is applied to realistic potentials. The effects of tensor correlations are investigated. The resulting phase shift equivalent correlated interactions are used in the no-core shell model for light nuclei and for mean-field calculations in the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics model for nuclei up to mass A=48.

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) can incorporate short-range radial and tensor correlations into a phase-shift-equivalent correlated interaction, but the long-range part of tensor correlations must still be described by the many-body state because restricting the tensor correlator range avoids large induced three-body and higher terms in the correlated Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors apply a unitary transformation consisting of a radial correlator \(C_r\) that shifts particle pairs away from the repulsive core and a tensor correlator \(C_\Omega\) that displaces probability density perpendicular to the relative distance vector, depending on spin directions. The resulting correlated Hamiltonian in two-body approximation is then used in no-core shell model calculations for \(^4\)He and in mean-field calculations within the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) model for nuclei up to mass A=48.

## Key numbers

- For \(^4\)He with the Argonne V8' potential, the radially correlated kinetic energy increases but is overcompensated by a gain of about -25 MeV per particle in correlated potential energy; after adding tensor correlations the total energy reaches about -8 MeV per particle.
- With the medium-ranged tensor correlator \(\beta\), the no-core shell model for \(^4\)He converges at about \(8\ \hbar\Omega\) excitations; at \(\hbar\Omega = 25\ \text{MeV}\) the binding energy with excitations up to \(18\ \hbar\Omega\) is only -6 MeV for the bare interaction.
- With the short-ranged tensor correlator \(\alpha\), the optimal single Slater determinant for \(^4\)He lies 7 MeV above the exact eigenvalue, and overbinding is reduced to about 2 MeV.
- The correlated interaction matrix elements in momentum representation become close to zero beyond momentum transfers of about 2 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the UCOM approach previously introduced by Neff and Feldmeier (2003) and Feldmeier et al. (1998) to include tensor correlations explicitly, going beyond earlier treatments that focused only on short-range repulsion. The resulting correlated interactions are shown to be in good agreement with the \(V_{low-k}\) obtained by renormalization group methods (Bogner et al., 2001). The method enables no-core shell model calculations with much smaller model spaces than required for bare interactions, and allows mean-field calculations in the FMD model (Feldmeier and Schnack, 2000) for nuclei up to A=48, with configuration mixing calculations for rotations and vibrations under way.
