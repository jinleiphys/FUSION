# Hindrance of heavy-ion fusion due to nuclear incompressibility

> We propose a new mechanism to explain the unexpected steep falloff of fusion cross sections at energies far below the Coulomb barrier. The saturation properties of nuclear matter are causing a hindrance to large overlap of the reacting nuclei and consequently a sensitive change of the nuclear potential inside the barrier. We report in this letter a good agreement with the data of coupled-channels calculation for the {64}Ni+{64}Ni combination using the double-folding potential with M3Y-Reid effective N-N forces supplemented with a repulsive core that reproduces the nuclear incompressibility for total overlap.

# Hindrance of heavy-ion fusion due to nuclear incompressibility

## Key claim
The steep falloff of fusion cross sections at energies far below the Coulomb barrier is caused by nuclear matter saturation properties, which produce a repulsive core in the ion-ion potential that creates a shallow pocket and thicker barrier; this mechanism reproduces the data for 64Ni+64Ni when the double-folding M3Y+repulsion potential is used in coupled-channels calculations, but the agreement depends on tuning the repulsive core strength to match the nuclear incompressibility of the compound nucleus.

## Method
The authors constructed a heavy-ion potential by double-folding Fermi-Dirac density distributions with the M3Y-Reid effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, then added a repulsive core via a zero-range N-N interaction folded with densities of reduced diffusivity (a ≤ 0.4 fm). The repulsive core strength was calibrated so that the potential at R=0 reproduces the nuclear incompressibility K=228 MeV predicted by the Thomas-Fermi model for the compound nucleus 128Ba. Coupled-channels calculations were performed including linear and quadratic couplings to low-lying 2+ and 3- states, mutual excitations, and two-phonon quadrupole excitations, solved in the rotating frame approximation with ingoing-wave boundary conditions at the potential minimum.

## Key numbers
- For 64Ni+64Ni, the M3Y+repulsion potential yields a minimum chi-squared per point of χ²/N = 0.86 with an energy shift ΔE = 0.16 MeV, compared to χ²/N = 10.1 with ΔE = 0.9 MeV for the Winther-Akyüz potential.
- The repulsive core strength used was V_core = 496 MeV, with a diffuseness of approximately 0.4 fm for both protons and neutrons.
- The nuclear incompressibility used was K = 228 MeV, from the Thomas-Fermi model for 128Ba.
- The potential minimum occurs at V_min ≈ 85 MeV, while the compound nucleus threshold is at approximately 49 MeV.
- Proton density parameters: r_0p = 1.065 fm, a_p = 0.575 fm; neutron density parameters: r_0n = 1.085 fm, a_n = 0.534 fm.

## Context
This work extends the experimental observations of Jiang (2004, 2005) on fusion hindrance in medium-heavy systems such as 58Ni+58Ni, 64Ni+64Ni, and 90Zr+90Zr, which could not be explained by standard coupled-channels calculations using the Winther-Akyüz potential. The mechanism builds on the suggestion by Dasso (2003) that the steep falloff is related to the disappearance of the classically allowed region, and on earlier work using repulsive cores for light systems such as 12C+12C by Michaud (1973). The approach enables improved predictions for sub-barrier fusion relevant to heavy-element synthesis in stars, and suggests that resonant structures seen in lighter quasi-molecular systems (e.g., Patterson 1971) are absent in these heavier systems due to the shallower potential pocket.
