# The role of the $P_{11}(1710)$ in the $NN{\to}NΣK$ reaction

> Using the resonance model, which was successfully applied for the study of the $pp{\to}pΛK^+$ reaction, we investigate $NN{\to}NΣK$ reactions that should provide cleaner information about resonance excitations and meson exchange contributions. For this purpose we demonstrate that the invariant mass distribution for the $ΣK$ system, as well as the Dalitz plot for the $NN{\to}NΣK$ reaction, provide direct information about the $ΣK$ production mechanism, which can be tested in the near future by experiments at COSY.

# Wiki Page: The role of the $P_{11}(1710)$ in the $NN{\to}N\Sigma K$ reaction

## Key claim

The $N(1710)$ resonance (also denoted $P_{11}$) provides the dominant contribution to $NN{\to}N\Sigma K$ reactions via nonstrange meson exchange, making the $\Sigma K$ invariant mass distribution a cleaner probe of baryonic resonance excitation than the $\Lambda K$ channel, provided the excess energy exceeds the resonance width (approximately 100–200 MeV). The authors hedge that this conclusion holds within a resonance model that excludes kaon exchange and that the signal is most clearly separable from final-state interaction effects at excess energies above 100 MeV, ideally at 200 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed a one-boson exchange model with $\pi$, $\eta$, and $\rho$ meson exchanges followed by excitation of intermediate baryonic resonances ($N(1710)$, $N(1720)$, and an effective $\Delta(1920)$ representing six $\Delta$ resonances) that decay to $\Sigma K$. Coupling constants and cutoff parameters were fixed from previous fits to $\pi N\to YK$ and $pp\to p\Lambda K^+$ total cross section data, using experimentally measured branching ratios from the Particle Data Group. The model computed total cross sections, $\Sigma K$ invariant mass distributions, and Dalitz plots for various $NN\to N\Sigma K$ reaction channels, comparing with available bubble chamber data from the CERN 2 m hydrogen chamber.

## Key numbers

- The $N(1710)$ resonance has a total width of 100 MeV and a $\Sigma K$ branching ratio of 0.02–0.10 (adopted value 0.060).
- The $g_{K\Sigma N(1710)}^2/4\pi$ coupling constant is 4.66, one order of magnitude larger than $g_{K\Sigma N(1720)}^2/4\pi = 2.99\times10^{-1}$ and $g_{K\Sigma\Delta(1920)}^2/4\pi = 3.08\times10^{-1}$.
- The $\rho NN(1720)$ coupling constant squared is $1.43\times10^{2}$, the largest in the model, but is compensated by the small $K\Sigma N(1720)$ coupling.
- The $\Sigma K$ invariant mass peak from $N(1710)$ excitation appears at $M_{\Sigma K} \simeq 1.76$ GeV at an excess energy of 200 MeV.
- The model reproduces $pp\to N\Sigma K$ total cross sections reasonably well but overestimates $np\to p\Sigma K$ data at excess energies above 1 GeV by up to a factor of approximately 2.
- The $\eta NN$ coupling constant used is $g_{\eta NN}=7.9$ (from the Bonn potential), but varying it between 1.0 and 9.0 changes the $\eta$-exchange contribution by factors of 1/60 to 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the resonance model previously applied to $pp\to p\Lambda K^+$ by Sibirtsev, Tsushima, Cassing, and Thomas (1997–1998) to the $\Sigma K$ production channel, which is argued to provide cleaner information because kaon exchange is negligible due to the small $KN\Sigma$ coupling constant. The study was motivated by recent data from the COSY-TOF Collaboration (1998) showing deviations from phase space in the $\Lambda K$ invariant mass spectrum. The paper identifies systematic discrepancies in existing $np\to N\Sigma K$ data from Ansorge et al. (1970s) and calls for new measurements at COSY. The analysis enables experimental searches for the $N(1710)$ resonance signal in $\Sigma K$ invariant mass distributions and Dalitz plots at excess energies of 100–200 MeV, where final-state interactions from $\Sigma N$ and $\Lambda N$ channel coupling (as studied by the Nijmegen and Jülich groups) are expected to be manageable.
