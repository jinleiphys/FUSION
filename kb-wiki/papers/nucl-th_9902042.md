# Charge independence breaking and charge symmetry breaking in the nucleon-nucleon interaction from effective field theory

> We discuss charge symmetry and charge independence breaking in an effective field theory approach for few-nucleon systems. We systematically introduce strong isospin-violating and electromagnetic operators in the theory. The charge dependence observed in the nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths is due to one-pion exchange and one electromagnetic four-nucleon contact term. This gives a parameter free expression for the charge dependence of the corresponding effective ranges, which is in agreement with the rather small and uncertain empirical determinations. We also compare the low energy phase shifts of the $nn$ and the $np$ system.

## Key claim

Charge independence breaking (CIB) and charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in the nucleon-nucleon interaction can be systematically described within the effective field theory framework of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (KSW), where the leading CIB effect arises from a combination of the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference in one-pion exchange and an electromagnetic four-nucleon contact term, with the latter being a mandatory new feature not present in meson-exchange models.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian including strong isospin-violating and electromagnetic operators up to second order, treating the pion mass difference via an expanded pion propagator. They used the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme to resum the lowest-order four-nucleon contact terms and treated pion exchange perturbatively, fitting the strong parameters \(C_0, C_2, D_2\) to the \(np\) scattering length and effective range, and the new CIB and CSB parameters \(E_0^{(1)}\) and \(E_0^{(2)}\) to the \(nn\) and \(pp\) scattering lengths. The resulting \(^1S_0\) phase shifts for \(nn\) and \(np\) systems were then compared.

## Key numbers

- \(\Delta a_{\rm CIB} = \frac{1}{2}(a_{nn} + a_{pp}) - a_{np} = 5.7 \pm 0.3\) fm (charge independence breaking in scattering lengths, based on Nijmegen potential with Coulomb subtraction)
- \(\Delta r_{\rm CIB} = \frac{1}{2}(r_{nn} + r_{pp}) - r_{np} = 0.05 \pm 0.08\) fm (charge independence breaking in effective ranges)
- \(a_{np} = (-23.714 \pm 0.013)\) fm (neutron-proton scattering length)
- \(\Delta a_{\rm CSB} = a_{pp} - a_{nn} = 1.5 \pm 0.5\) fm (charge symmetry breaking in scattering lengths)
- \(\Delta r_{\rm CSB} = r_{pp} - r_{nn} = 0.10 \pm 0.12\) fm (charge symmetry breaking in effective ranges)
- \(\Delta a_{\rm CIB}^{\rm OPE} \sim 3.6 \pm 0.2\) fm (one-pion exchange contribution to CIB from potential models)
- \(\Delta a_{\rm CSB}^{\rho-\omega} \sim 1.2 \pm 0.4\) fm (rho-omega mixing contribution to CSB from potential models)
- Fitted parameters at \(\mu = m\): \(C_0 = -3.46\) fm\(^2\), \(C_2 = 2.75\) fm\(^4\), \(D_2 = 0.07\) fm\(^4\), \(E_0^{(1)} = -6.47\) fm\(^2\), \(E_0^{(2)} = 1.10\) fm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends the KSW effective field theory approach (Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1998) to include isospin violation in the two-nucleon system, providing a systematic alternative to traditional meson-exchange potential models that attributed CIB primarily to pion mass differences and CSB to rho-omega mixing. The formalism enables parameter-free predictions for the charge dependence of effective ranges and provides a classification of leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order contributions to CIB and CSB, with potential extensions to other partial waves and higher energies such as pion production.
