# Excitations of the Quark- Gluon Plasma

> We will discuss the spectrum of the eta mesons making use of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model supplemented with a model of confinement. We will go on to discuss the properties of mesons at finite temperature and the phenomenon of deconfinement. We will then discuss some excited states of the quark-gluon plasma calculated in lattice QCD models.These resonances are thought to be created in heavy-ion collisions.We consider the role these states play in leading to a hydrodynamic description of the plasma at early stages of its formation.

## Key claim
The paper argues that low-energy quark-antiquark resonance states in the quark-gluon plasma, extracted from lattice QCD via the maximum entropy method (MEM), can explain the very small quark mean-free-paths that lead to nearly ideal fluid behavior in heavy-ion collisions, but this conclusion depends on the specific chiral quark model parameters used to represent instanton dynamics.

## Method
The authors used a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model supplemented with a covariant confinement potential and temperature-dependent coupling constants to calculate meson spectra, hadronic current correlation functions, and spectral functions. They employed the real-time finite-temperature formalism to compute polarization functions and used dispersion relations to obtain the real parts, then extracted screening masses from coordinate-space correlation functions and calculated quark optical potentials and mean-free-paths.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of light mesons using the NJL model with confinement (Shakin and Wang, Physical Review D, 2002) and their calculations of hadronic current correlation functions at finite temperature (He, Li, Shakin, and Sun, Physical Review C, 2003). The paper connects to lattice QCD results for charmonium by Datta, Karsch, Petreczky, and Wetzorke (2003) and to the "sticky molasses" description of the quark-gluon plasma advocated by the Stony Brook group. The results enable a microscopic understanding of how low-energy resonances in the plasma lead to hydrodynamic behavior at early stages of heavy-ion collisions.
