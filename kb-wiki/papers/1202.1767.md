# Tidal wave in 102Pd: Rotating condensate of up to seven d-bosons

> The yrast states of even even vibrational and transitional nuclei are inter- preted as a rotating condensate of interacting d-bosons and the corresponding semi-classical tidal wave concept. A simple experimental manifestation of the anharmonicity caused by the boson interaction is found. The interpretation is substantiated by calculations based on the Collective Model and the Cranking Model.

## Key claim

The yrast states of even-even vibrational and transitional nuclei, specifically up to the seven-boson state in \(^{102}\)Pd, are interpreted as a rotating condensate of interacting d-bosons (the "tidal wave" concept), where the anharmonicity caused by boson interaction manifests as a constant offset in the moment of inertia and \(B(E2)\) values as functions of spin, rather than a deviation from linearity.

## Method

The authors analyze experimental yrast-state energies and \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities from recent lifetime measurements in \(^{102}\)Pd, comparing them to the free-boson harmonic limit and to calculations using the Generalized Collective Model (GCM) with a quartic potential fitted to low-lying collective states. They also perform microscopic Shell Correction Tilted Axis Cranking (SCTAC) calculations using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, minimizing energy with respect to deformation parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) for each angular momentum, and construct a mixed GCMTAC approach by fitting the GCM potential to the microscopic TAC potential energy surface.

## Key numbers

* The seven-boson yrast state is identified at spin \(I=14\) in \(^{102}\)Pd, with the \(B(E2,16_1^+ \rightarrow 14_1^+)\) transition connecting the \(16_1^+\) two-quasiparticle state to the \(14_1^+\) seven-boson state.
* The moment of inertia \(J(I)\) and \(B(E2)\) values are nearly linear functions of spin \(I\), with the ratio \(B(E2)/J\) constant within experimental uncertainties for \(^{102}\)Pd.
* The free-boson limit gives \(E = \Omega(n + 5/2)\) and \(I = 2n\) for \(n\) non-interacting d-bosons, with \(\omega = \Omega/2\) constant.
* The GCM calculation with parameters fitted to energy ratios of the lowest \(0^+_2\), \(2^+_1\), \(2^+_2\), \(3^+_1\), and \(4^+_1\) states reproduces the slope and offset of experimental curves for \(^{102}\)Pd.
* The TAC calculations reproduce the \(J(I)\) function for \(^{102}\)Pd very well but underestimate the absolute \(B(E2)\) values by a factor of about 2/3.
* In \(^{110}\)Cd, the TAC calculation reproduces the \(B(E2)\) values for the \(h_{11/2}\) two-quasineutron configuration at \(I \ge 12\) but underestimates the tidal wave branch for \(I \le 10\).
* The mass parameter for the GCMTAC calculation was adjusted to \(B = 90\hbar^2\) MeV\(^{-1}\) to match the experimental energy scale.

## Context

This work extends the tidal wave concept introduced by Frauendorf (2010) for interpreting yrast states in vibrational and transitional nuclei as rotating condensates of d-bosons, and provides experimental evidence for up to seven bosons in \(^{102}\)Pd from lifetime measurements by Ayangeakaa, Garg, and the ANL group. The results contradict the Interacting Boson Model (IBM 1), which fails to describe multi-phonon yrast states due to its boson number cutoff (maximum 5 for \(Z=46, N=56\)), whereas the GCM with a quartic potential (Caprio, 2011) and the SCTAC cranking model (Frauendorf, 2010) both describe the energies well. The work enables prediction of \(B(E2)\) values for yrast states with \(I>2\) using only the \(2^+ \rightarrow 0^+\) transition energy and Grodzin's rule, and raises open questions about why microscopic approaches systematically underestimate \(B(E2)\) values for the tidal wave branch while reproducing high-spin two-quasiparticle configurations correctly.
