# Nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics: Isovector terms

> We extend a recent calculation of the nuclear energy density functional in the framework of chiral perturbation theory by computing the isovector surface and spin-orbit terms: $(\vec \nabla ρ_p- \vec \nabla ρ_n)^2 G_d(ρ)+ (\vec \nabla ρ_p- \vec \nabla ρ_n)\cdot(\vec J_p-\vec J_n) G_{so(ρ)+(\vec J_p-\vec J_n)^2 G_J(ρ)$ pertaining to different proton and neutron densities. Our calculation treats systematically the effects from $1π$-exchange, iterated $1π$-exchange, and irreducible $2π$-exchange with intermediate $Δ$-isobar excitations, including Pauli-blocking corrections up to three-loop order. Using an improved density-matrix expansion, we obtain results for the strength functions $G_d(ρ)$, $G_{so}(ρ)$ and $G_J(ρ)$ which are considerably larger than those of phenomenological Skyrme forces. These (parameter-free) predictions for the strength of the isovector surface and spin-orbit terms as provided by the long-range pion-exchange dynamics in the nuclear medium should be examined in nuclear structure calculations at large neutron excess.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous chiral perturbation theory calculation of the nuclear energy density functional to isospin-asymmetric systems, computing the isovector surface and spin-orbit strength functions \(G_d(\rho)\), \(G_{so}(\rho)\), and \(G_J(\rho)\) from long-range pion-exchange dynamics. The results are "considerably larger" than those of phenomenological Skyrme forces, but the paper hedges that these parameter-free predictions "should be examined in nuclear structure calculations at large neutron excess" rather than claiming they are definitively correct.

## Method

The calculation uses the improved density-matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet, and Bogner (2009) to treat the non-local exchange contributions from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics up to three-loop order. The authors compute contributions from \(1\pi\)-exchange, iterated \(1\pi\)-exchange, and irreducible \(2\pi\)-exchange with intermediate \(\Delta\)-isobar excitations, including Pauli-blocking corrections, for the isovector surface term \((\vec \nabla \rho_p- \vec \nabla \rho_n)^2 G_d(\rho)\), the isovector spin-orbit term \((\vec \nabla \rho_p- \vec \nabla \rho_n)\cdot(\vec J_p-\vec J_n) G_{so}(\rho)\), and the squared isovector spin-orbit density term \((\vec J_p-\vec J_n)^2 G_J(\rho)\).

## Key numbers

* The isovector spin-orbit strength function \(G_{so}(\rho)\) from long-range pion-exchange dynamics is compared to the empirical isoscalar spin-orbit coupling strength \(F_{so}^{(\rm emp)}\simeq 90\,\)MeV\(\,\)fm\(^5\), with the isovector channel expected to have a reduced weight of \(1/3\) from short-range contributions.
* The physical input parameters used are: \(g_A=1.3\) (nucleon axial vector coupling constant), \(f_\pi = 92.4\,\)MeV (pion decay constant), \(m_\pi = 135\,\)MeV (neutral pion mass), \(M=939\,\)MeV (nucleon mass), and \(\Delta = 293\,\)MeV (delta-nucleon mass splitting).
* The density region of interest is around \(\rho_0/2 = 0.08\,\)fm\(^{-3}\) where the spin-orbit interaction in nuclei gains most of its weight, with saturation density \(\rho_0=0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}\).
* The strength function \(G_d(\rho)\) from the iterated \(1\pi\)-exchange alone (dashed line in Fig. 3) is almost unchanged by inclusion of three-body contributions, and shows weak density dependence in the region \(0 < \rho <0.2\,{\rm fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the isoscalar calculation of Kaiser (2009) for the nuclear energy density functional from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics to the isovector channel, using the improved density-matrix expansion of Gebremariam, Duguet, and Bogner (2009) rather than the original Negele and Vautherin (1972) expansion. The results are contrasted with phenomenological Skyrme forces such as SkM\(^*\), SkP, and Sly, whose constant values for \(G_d^{(\rm Sk)}\), \(G_{so}^{(\rm Sk)}\), and \(G_J^{(\rm Sk)}\) are of similar magnitude but opposite sign or considerably smaller in magnitude. The paper enables future nuclear structure calculations at large neutron excess to test whether the predicted much larger negative values of \(G_d(\rho)\) and the proportionality of the neutron spin-orbit potential to \(\vec \nabla \rho_n\) agree with experimental data, particularly isotope shifts of charge radii in the Pb region around the shell-closure \(N=126\) as discussed by Reinhard and Flocard (1995).
