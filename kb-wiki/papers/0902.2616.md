# Scattering effects on nuclear thermodynamic observables

> The nuclear thermodynamic observables like the temperature, volume and the specific heat as obtained from isotopic ratios in hot disassembled nuclear matter are examined in the light of the S-matrix approach to the nuclear equation of state. The values of the observables, as extracted without inclusion of scattering effects are found to be modified appreciably in some cases when scattering between the fragment species is taken care of.

# Scattering effects on nuclear thermodynamic observables

## Key claim

The temperature, freeze-out volume, and specific heat extracted from isotopic ratios using the standard nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) model are modified appreciably when strong interaction scattering among fragment species is included via the S-matrix approach; the extracted temperature is always lower than the NSE value, and the correction is largest near the condensation temperature, with the magnitude depending on density, asymmetry, and choice of thermometer.

## Method

The authors compute fragment multiplicities from the grand partition function of dilute nuclear matter using the S-matrix (SM) approach, which includes both bound-state contributions (ground and excited states of all nuclear species up to A=1000) and two-body scattering channels. For light channels (A ≤ 8), elastic scattering phase shifts for NN, Nt, N³He, Nα, and αα pairs are used; for heavier channels, scattering is approximated by resonances treated as ideal gas terms. The resulting fragment yields are then inserted into the standard Albergo-Costa-Costanzo-Rubbino (ACCR) double- and single-isotope-ratio formulas to extract an "output" temperature and free neutron density, which are compared to the input temperature and density used in the S-matrix calculation.

## Key numbers

- The ratio T^NSE/T^SM is always greater than 1; at ρ = 0.02 fm⁻³ and T ≈ 6 MeV, the ratio reaches a peak of approximately 1.3 for the t-He thermometer in symmetric matter.
- The freeze-out volume ratio V^SM/V^NSE can reach a factor of ~2 at T ≈ 6 MeV for ρ = 0.02 fm⁻³ in the heavy-species model (HSM) using the t-He thermometer and t/d single ratio.
- Specific heat C_V is calculated at densities ρ = 0.0001, 0.001, 0.01, and 0.02 fm⁻³; the peak in C_V is shifted to a somewhat lower temperature in the SM approach compared to NSE, with slightly higher magnitude.
- The maximum mass number included in the calculation is A_max = 1000; results are not very sensitive to further increase.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix approach to the nuclear equation of state previously developed by Das (1997), Horowitz and Schwenk (2006), and Malheiro et al. (2007) to specifically examine how strong interaction corrections affect the thermodynamic observables extracted via the ACCR method (Albergo et al., 1985). The results show that the standard NSE-based extraction systematically underestimates the true temperature and freeze-out volume, particularly near the condensation temperature where the specific heat peaks. A related study by Shlomo et al. (2007) addressed similar corrections using medium-modified binding energies for light fragments, but the present work uses model-independent S-matrix inputs and includes heavy species up to A=1000, enabling reconstruction of corrected values through iteration or tabulation.
