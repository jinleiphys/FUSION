# Effect of symmetry energy on two-nucleon correlation functions in heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei

> Using an isospin-dependent transport model, we study the effects of nuclear symmetry energy on two-nucleon correlation functions in heavy ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei. We find that the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy affects significantly the nucleon emission times in these collisions, leading to larger values of two-nucleon correlation functions for a symmetry energy that has a stronger density dependence. Two-nucleon correlation functions are thus useful tools for extracting information about the nuclear symmetry energy from heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy significantly affects nucleon emission times in heavy-ion collisions, leading to larger values of two-nucleon correlation functions for a symmetry energy that has a stronger density dependence; the effect is most pronounced for nucleon pairs with high total momentum, especially neutron-proton pairs with low relative momenta.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central collisions of 52Ca + 48Ca at E=80 MeV/nucleon. They parameterized the nuclear symmetry energy as E_sym(ρ) = E_sym(ρ0)·u^γ with γ=0.5 (soft) and γ=2 (stiff), and evaluated two-nucleon correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formalism with the Correlation After Burner code, gating on total momentum of nucleon pairs.

## Key numbers

- The bulk symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density is set to E_sym(ρ0)=35 MeV, with ρ0=0.16 fm^-3
- For neutron-neutron pairs with high total momentum (P>500 MeV/c) and low relative momentum q=5 MeV/c, the correlation function is about 20% higher for the stiff symmetry energy than for the soft symmetry energy
- For neutron-proton pairs with high total momentum (P>500 MeV/c) and low relative momentum q=5 MeV/c, the correlation function is about 30% higher for the stiff symmetry energy than for the soft symmetry energy
- For proton-proton pairs with high total momentum (P>500 MeV/c) and relative momentum q=20 MeV/c, the correlation function is about 20% higher for the stiff symmetry energy than for the soft symmetry energy
- Changing the incompressibility K0 from 380 to 210 MeV or using in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections changes both the neutron-proton and proton-proton correlation functions by about 5%

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear symmetry energy probes in heavy-ion collisions, including pre-equilibrium neutron/proton ratios (Li 1997), isospin fractionation (Xu 2000, Tan 2001, Baran 2002), isoscaling in multifragmentation (Tsang 2001), proton differential elliptic flow (Li 2002), neutron-proton differential transverse flow (Li 2000), and the π-/π+ ratio (Li 2002). The results demonstrate that two-nucleon correlation functions provide a new tool for extracting the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, complementing existing probes such as neutron-skin measurements (Oyamatsu 1998, Horowitz 2001) and parity-violating electron scatterings.
