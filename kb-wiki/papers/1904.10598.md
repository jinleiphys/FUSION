# Collinear true ternary fission as the consequence of the collective nuclear model

> The concept of collinear spontaneous true ternary fission of 252Cf is subject to critical analysis. The conclusion is that the collinear flight of the fragments turns out to be a natural and most probable mode. The collinearity arises in the model on the prescission stage as a result of the account of the principles of the collective Bohr's model. It is partly destroyed at the post-scission stage of spreading of the fragments due to their Coulomb interaction, with the allowance for the spin effects arising at the moment of scission. The final angular distribution of the fragments is calculated by means of the trajectory simulations. The calculated relative angle of the heavy and light fragments is kept 180 degrees with an uncertainty within 0.4 degree, which justifies search for a collinear tri-partition at the modern stage of experiment.

# Collinear true ternary fission as the consequence of the collective nuclear model

## Key claim

Collinear true ternary fission of 252Cf is the natural and most probable mode, arising from axial symmetry of the fissile nucleus on its path to scission as required by the collective Bohr model; the final relative angle between heavy and light fragments remains 180 degrees with an uncertainty within 0.4 degree, justifying experimental search for collinear tri-partition.

## Method

The author performed classical trajectory simulations of three representative fragments (132Sn, 48Ca, 72Ni) from spontaneous fission of 252Cf by solving Newton's equations of motion under mutual Coulomb repulsion, assuming spherical fragments. Initial positions were determined by minimizing Coulomb energy at a given distance between side fragments D, and initial transverse velocities were derived from the total relative angular momentum L of the fragments (varied from 0 to 20 ħ) arising from wriggling vibrations at scission. The equations were solved numerically using the Runge-Kutta-Nyström method for scission distances D from 25.6 to 40 fm.

## Key numbers

- The reaction channel studied: 252Cf → 132Sn + 48Ca + 72Ni, with Q = 251 MeV
- Total kinetic energy (TKE) ranges from T = Q = 251 MeV at D = 25.6 fm down to T = 160 MeV at D = 40 fm
- The ternary fragment (48Ca) kinetic energy is approximately 5 MeV, which is 15–20 times smaller than the energies of the main fragments
- The calculated angle Θ between heavy and light fragments differs from 180° by no more than 0.4° across all considered L values (0–20 ħ) and D values (25.6–40 fm)
- For D = 25.6 fm and L = 20, Θ = 179.6° and the divergence angle Φ between light and ternary fragments = 2.8°
- For D = 35 fm and L = 5, Θ = 179.9° and Φ = 0.6°
- The ternary fragment arrives at the detector with a delay of approximately 10⁻⁷ s relative to the light fragment

## Context

This work extends the collinear tripartition concept proposed by Solyakin et al. and the JINR experiments on FOBOS and mini-FOBOS setups (Fobos1, FobosROT, Kamanin), which found collinear TTF of 235U and spontaneous fission of 252Cf at levels up to 10⁻⁴–10⁻⁵. The author argues that earlier experiments (Muga, Iyer, Teo) searching for fragments at approximately 120° angles were misguided, and that the axial symmetry requirement from Bohr's collective model (Bohr and Mottelson) forces the "three in line" configuration. The work contradicts the approach of Tashkhodzhaev et al., which the author criticizes for unrealistic initial conditions and violation of momentum conservation. The results enable refined experimental searches for collinear tripartition with angular acceptance of 180° ± 2°.
