# Deuteron Coulomb Excitation in Peripheral Collisions with a Heavy Ion

> We develop and test an ab initio time-dependent Basis Function (tBF) method to solve non-perturbative and time-dependent problems in quantum mechanics. For our test problem, we apply this method to the Coulomb excitation of the deuteron by an impinging heavy ion. In the tBF method applied to deuterium, we employ wave functions for its bound and excited states to calculate its transition probabilities and the r.m.s. radius during the scattering process. For comparison, corresponding results based on first-order perturbation theory are also provided. For the Coulomb excitation process in a weak and time-varying Coulomb field, where higher-order effects are negligible, we obtain good agreement of the results based on these two methods. The tBF method is then applied to the Coulomb excitation process with stronger external field. The higher-order effects, such as those appearing in the reorientation of the polarization of the deuteron system, are analyzed.

# Deuteron Coulomb Excitation in Peripheral Collisions with a Heavy Ion

## Key claim
The time-dependent Basis Function (tBF) method reproduces first-order perturbation theory results for deuteron Coulomb excitation in weak fields, but reveals significant higher-order effects, including reorientation of polarization, when the field is strong (fine-structure constant increased to 1/13.7), which are missed by perturbation theory. The method is tested only for the E1 multipole component of the Coulomb field, with the deuteron confined by a harmonic oscillator trap and its center of mass fixed.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation non-perturbatively by expanding the deuteron (neutron-proton) wave function in a basis of eigenstates of the free Hamiltonian H0, which includes the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction and a weak external harmonic oscillator trap (ħω = 5 MeV). The time evolution is computed using the MSD2 scheme (a symmetric second-order difference method) applied to the interaction-picture equation of motion, with the Coulomb field of a moving heavy ion (124Sn, Z=50, v=0.1c) treated only through its E1 multipole component. For comparison, first-order perturbation theory results are obtained by truncating the time-ordered exponential to linear order in the interaction.

## Key numbers
- Impact parameter: 7.5 fm, chosen to exclude strong nuclear force effects.
- Projectile speed: 0.1c (constant along the z-axis).
- Exposure duration: 10 MeV⁻¹ (6.582 × 10⁻²¹ sec), during which the projectile travels approximately 200 fm.
- Model space truncation: Nmax = 60 for the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis.
- Harmonic oscillator trap strength: ħω = 5 MeV.
- Nucleon mass: 938.92 MeV.
- Deuteron r.m.s. radius in the trap: 1.472 fm, approximately 25% smaller than the physical deuteron r.m.s. radius of 1.975(3) fm.
- Weak field fine-structure constant: α = 1/137.04; strong field fine-structure constant: α = 1/13.7.
- Net r.m.s. radius expansion after evolution: on the order of 10⁻⁴ fm for both weak and strong fields.
- Charge of projectile: Z = 50 (fully stripped 124Sn).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous time-dependent Basis Light-Front Quantization (tBLFQ) method (Zhao 2013, Chen 2017) to a non-relativistic framework for studying Coulomb excitation. It builds on the standard theory of Coulomb excitation (Alder 1956, Eisenberg 1988) and uses the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction (Shirokov 2005, 2007, 2004). The method enables future studies of deuteron breakup reactions (Bertulani 1992, Canto 1997) and scattering processes where both electromagnetic and strong interactions modify the deuteron polarization (Ou 2015, Bar 2010, Seyfarth 2010). The approach can be generalized to other light nuclei such as 6Li or 12C.
