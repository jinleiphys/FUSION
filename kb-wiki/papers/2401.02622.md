# Elastic p-12C scattering by using a cluster effective field theory

> The elastic p-12C scattering at low energies is studied by using a cluster effective field theory (EFT), where the low-lying resonance states (s1/2, p3/2, d5/2) of 13N are treated as pertinent degrees of freedom. The low-energy constants of the Lagrangian are expressed in terms of the Coulomb-modified effective range parameters, which are determined to reproduce the experimental data for the differential cross-sections. The resulting theoretical predictions agree very well with the experimental data. The resulting theory is shown to give us almost identical phase shifts as obtained from the R-matrix approach. The role of the ground state of 13N below the threshold and the next-to-leading order in the EFT power counting are also discussed.

## Key claim

The cluster effective field theory (EFT) description of elastic p-12C scattering, treating the low-lying resonance states (s1/2, p3/2, d5/2) of 13N as explicit degrees of freedom, yields phase shifts nearly identical to those from the R-matrix approach, but the authors note that the EFT expansion parameter k_lo/k_hi is approximately 0.3-0.6, implying the convergence is modest and higher-order corrections may be significant.

## Method

The authors constructed a cluster EFT Lagrangian with proton, 12C, and dicluster fields for the s1/2, p3/2, and d5/2 resonance states of 13N, including Coulomb interactions via the Coulomb Green's function. They expressed the low-energy constants in terms of Coulomb-modified effective range parameters (scattering length, effective range, shape parameter) by matching the EFT scattering amplitudes to the effective range expansion. The effective range parameters were determined by fitting to experimental differential cross-section data using a chi-squared minimization with a theoretical uncertainty regulator proportional to k/Lambda, where Lambda = 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Resonance excitation energies of 13N treated as degrees of freedom: E_r = 0.457 MeV (1/2+), 1.686 MeV (3/2-), and 1.734 MeV (5/2+)
- Corresponding resonance momenta: 28 MeV, 54 MeV, and 55 MeV, respectively, using the reduced mass of the p-12C system
- High momentum scale k_hi set by the first excitation energy of 12C (E* = 4.44 MeV) giving approximately 90 MeV
- Coulomb momentum scale k_C = Z_C * alpha_EM * m_R approximately 38 MeV, where Z_C = 6 and alpha_EM approximately 1/137.036
- Ground state of 13N (J^pi = 1/2-) lies below threshold at E_r = -1.944 MeV
- EFT expansion ratio k_lo/k_hi = 0.3 to 0.6

## Context

This work extends the cluster EFT approach previously applied to alpha-alpha scattering (Higa, 2008) and 12C-alpha scattering (Ando, 2016, 2018) to the p-12C system, which is important for describing the 12C(p,gamma)13N reaction in the CNO cycle. The results are compared with the phenomenological R-matrix analysis of Azuma (2010), and the authors find nearly identical phase shifts. This enables future EFT calculations of the radiative capture cross section at astrophysical energies, where direct experimental measurement is difficult due to the Coulomb barrier.
