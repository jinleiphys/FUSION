# An investigation of nuclear collisions with a momentum-dependent Lattice Hamiltonian model

> We formulate a Lattice Hamiltonian approach for the modeling of intermediate energy heavy ion collisions. After verifying stationary ground state solutions, we implement this in a calculation of nuclear stopping power and compare our results with experimental data. Our findings support a relatively soft nuclear equation of state, with a momentum-dependent self-consistent mean field.

## Key claim

The Lattice Hamiltonian model with a momentum-dependent mean field (MDYI) can simultaneously reproduce both nuclear stopping power data and the mass of the heaviest remnant in Ar+Ag collisions, while a momentum-independent Skyrme mean field cannot satisfy both observables with the same compressibility. The findings support a relatively soft nuclear equation of state with compressibility K=215 MeV.

## Method

The authors formulated a Lattice Hamiltonian approach for intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions, discretizing the potential energy on a configuration-space lattice and solving Hamilton's equations for all nucleons. They implemented both a momentum-independent generalized Skyrme interaction and a momentum-dependent MDYI (Momentum-Dependent Yukawa Interaction) potential, and included elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering with an isospin-dependent cross section. Simulations were performed for 40Ar + 108Ag collisions at laboratory bombarding energies from approximately 20 to 120 MeV/A, using two impact parameters (b=b_max/3 and b=b_max/5) and two values of nuclear matter compressibility for each mean field.

## Key numbers

- Soft EOS compressibility: K=200 MeV for Skyrme interaction, K=215 MeV for MDYI interaction
- Stiff EOS compressibility: K=380 MeV for Skyrme interaction, K=373 MeV for MDYI interaction
- Experimental beam energies studied: from approximately 8 to 115 MeV/A for 40Ar+108Ag
- Binding energy per nucleon used as constraint: E_B=16 MeV
- Equilibrium nuclear matter density: rho_0=0.16 fm^-3
- Single particle potential constraints: U(rho=rho_0, p=0)=-75 MeV and U(rho=rho_0, p^2/2m=300 MeV)=0
- Binding energy calculation performed for approximately 30 nuclei with mass numbers A: 4 to 260

## Context

This work extends the Lattice Hamiltonian method of Lenk and Pandharipande (1989) to include momentum-dependent mean fields, building on prior BUU and QMD transport models by Bertsch and Das Gupta (1988) and Aichelin (1991). The study uses experimental stopping data from Conlin et al. (1998) at the NSCL at MSU to discriminate between different mean field parameterizations, following earlier work by Zhang, Das Gupta and Gale (1994) that used similar parameterizations. The results enable more precise determination of the nuclear equation of state from heavy ion collision data, particularly by showing that momentum-dependent mean fields reduce sensitivity to compressibility in stopping observables while remaining distinguishable through remnant mass measurements.
