# Velocity operator approach to quantum fluid dynamics in a three-dimensional neutron-proton system

> In the preceeding paper, introducing isospin-dependent density operators and defining exact momenta (collective variables), we could get an exact canonically momenta approach to a one-dimensional (1D) neutron-proton (NP) system. In this paper, we attempt at a velocity operator approach to a 3D NP system. Following Sunakawa, after introducing momentum density operators, we define velocity operators, denoting classical fluid velocities. We derive a collective Hamiltonian in terms of the collective variables.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous exact canonical-momenta approach for a one-dimensional neutron-proton system to a three-dimensional system using a velocity operator formalism, but the derivation of the collective Hamiltonian involves approximations (e.g., restricting to irrotational flow where vortex operators vanish) and expansions to order \(1/\sqrt{A}\), so the result is not exact in 3D as it was in 1D.

## Method

The authors introduce isospin-dependent density operators \(\rho^{0,0}_{\mathbf{k}}\) and momentum density operators \(\mathbf{g}^{0,0}_{\mathbf{k}}\) for a 3D neutron-proton Fermi system, then define velocity operators \(\mathbf{v}^{0,0}_{\mathbf{k}}\) via a discrete integral equation following Sunakawa. They expand the kinetic energy operator \(T\) in powers of the velocity operators and determine the expansion coefficients by evaluating commutators with the density operators, working in a subspace where the vortex operator vanishes (irrotational flow). The constant term \(C_0\) is computed using Grassmann variables, and the full Hamiltonian is expressed in terms of \(\rho^{0,0}_{\mathbf{k}}\) and \(\mathbf{v}^{0,0}_{\mathbf{k}}\) up to order \(1/\sqrt{A}\), then diagonalized via a Bogoliubov transformation.

## Key numbers

* The density operator at zero momentum is \(\rho^{0,0}_0 = \sqrt{A}\), where \(A = N+Z\) is the total nucleon number.
* The constant term in the collective Hamiltonian is \(C_0 \cong \sum_{\mathbf{k}} \frac{\hbar^2 \mathbf{k}^2}{2m}\), which coincides with the constant term from Tomonaga's method.
* The lowest-order Hamiltonian \(H_0\) is diagonalized with excitation energies \(E_{\mathbf{k}}\) and ground-state energy \(E^G_0 = -\frac{A(A+2)}{8\Omega} \nu(0) - \frac{1}{2} \sum_{\mathbf{k} \neq 0} \left( E_{\mathbf{k}} - \frac{\hbar^2 \mathbf{k}^2}{2m} \right)\).
* The interaction term includes factors \(-\frac{A}{8\Omega} \nu(\mathbf{k})\) and \(-\frac{A}{4\Omega} \nu(\mathbf{k})\) specific to the 3D isospin \(T=0\) system.

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional exact canonical-momenta approach of Nishiyama and da Providencia (2015) to three dimensions, following the velocity operator method of Sunakawa (1962) originally developed for Bose systems. The resulting Hamiltonian structure parallels Sunakawa's form for a Bose system but differs by terms arising from Fermi statistics (e.g., the constant term \(\frac{3}{2} \sum_{\mathbf{k}} \frac{\hbar^2 \mathbf{k}^2}{2m}\)). The diagonalization via Bogoliubov transformation (Bogoliubov, 1947) enables future studies of collective excitations in neutron-proton systems, and the approach may be extended to include vortex motion beyond the irrotational approximation.
