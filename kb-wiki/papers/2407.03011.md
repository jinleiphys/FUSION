# Backward DVCS on the pion in Sullivan processes

> The purpose of this work is to do a systematic feasibility study of measuring in backward region deeply virtual Compton scattering on the pion in Sullivan processes in the framework of collinear QCD factorization where pion to photon transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) describe the photon content of the $π$ meson. Our approach employs TDAs based on the overlap of light front wave functions, using a previously developed pion light-front wave function and deriving a consistent model for the light front wave functions of the photon. This work is expected to lead us to an estimate of the cross-sections that could be measured in the future U.S. and China's electron-ion colliders. It will also provide a comparison with the forward Sullivan DVCS case, which gives access to pion GPDs and for which a strong signal is expected.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic feasibility study for measuring backward deeply virtual Compton scattering (bDVCS) on the pion in Sullivan processes, using collinear QCD factorization with pion-to-photon transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs). The authors note that this is a preliminary result, and that the full amplitude computation requires including a second contribution to the TDA from aligned quark helicities, as well as evolving the TDA to scales relevant for current and future experimental facilities.

## Method

The authors model the backward DVCS amplitude as a convolution of a short-distance coefficient function, a pion distribution amplitude, and a pion-to-gamma TDA, following the factorization proof for meson forward deep electroproduction. They construct the TDA using the overlap method of light-front wave functions, employing a previously developed pion light-front wave function with a mass scale M = 318 MeV and deriving a consistent model for the photon light-front wave functions. The TDA is computed in the DGLAP region (x >= |xi|) in closed form, and the ERBL region (x <= |xi|) is obtained through a covariant extension strategy using the Radon transform of double distributions.

## Key numbers

* The pion light-front wave function uses a mass scale M = 318 MeV, fitted to the pion.
* The leading order coefficient function C_F contains terms with denominators (xi - x - i epsilon) and (xi + x - i epsilon), with quark charges e_q and e_q' for q != q'.
* The d-quark contribution to the TDA in the DGLAP region, A^{pi+ gamma}_{d(uparrow downarrow)}(x,xi,u), is expressed in terms of tau = -((1-x)^2/(1-xi^2)) * (u/(4M^2)), where M = 318 MeV.
* The double distribution at u = 0 for the d(uparrow downarrow) contribution is a polynomial in beta and alpha with coefficients including fractions such as 1/3, -10/3, 4, -4, and 7.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed for forward Sullivan DVCS, which gives access to pion generalized parton distributions (GPDs) as proposed by Amrath et al. (2008), to the backward region where pion-to-gamma TDAs describe the photon content of the pion. The study is motivated by renewed interest in backward processes, as noted by Gayoso et al. (2021), and builds on existing TDA models by Tiburzi (2005), Lansberg et al. (2006), and Courtoy et al. (2007). The results are expected to enable estimates of cross-sections measurable at future U.S. and China electron-ion colliders, and to provide comparison with the forward Sullivan DVCS case where a strong signal is expected.
