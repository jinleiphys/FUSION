# Exclusive photoproduction of light and heavy vector mesons: thresholds to very high energies

> A reaction model for $γ+ p \to V + p$, $V=ρ^0, ϕ, J/ψ, Υ$, which exposes the quark-antiquark content of the photon in making the transition $γ\to {q} \bar{q} + \mathbb P \to V$, where ${q}$ depends on $V$, and couples the intermediate ${q} \bar{q}$ system to the proton's valence quarks via Pomeron ($\mathbb P$) exchange, is used to deliver a unified description of available data -- both differential and total cross sections -- from near threshold to very high energies, $W$, for all the $V$-mesons. For the $Υ$, this means $10\lesssim W/{\rm GeV} \lesssim 2\,000$. Also provided are predictions for the power-law exponents that are empirically used to characterise the large-$W$ behaviour of the total cross sections and slope parameters characterising the near-threshold differential cross sections. Appealing to notions of vector meson dominance, the latter have been interpreted as vector-meson--proton scattering lengths. The body of results indicate that it is premature to link any $γ+ p \to V + p$ data with, for instance, in-proton gluon distributions, the quantum chromodynamics trace anomaly, or pentaquark production. Further developments in reaction theory and higher precision data are required before the validity of any such links can be assessed.

## Key claim
The paper presents a unified reaction model for exclusive photoproduction of light and heavy vector mesons (ρ⁰, φ, J/ψ, Υ) that successfully describes data from near threshold to very high energies, but the authors conclude that it is premature to link any γ + p → V + p data with in-proton gluon distributions, the QCD trace anomaly, or pentaquark production, as further developments in reaction theory and higher precision data are required before such links can be assessed.

## Method
The authors calculate the γ → qq̅ + ℙ → V transition matrix element using continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) to obtain dressed quark propagators, dressed photon-quark vertices, and vector-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, then couple the intermediate qq̅ system to the proton's valence quarks via Pomeron (ℙ) exchange. The reaction model uses a single soft Pomeron with different trajectory parameters for light mesons (ρ⁰, φ) versus heavy mesons (J/ψ, Υ), with Pomeron-quark couplings β_q determined by fits to high-energy total cross-section data for each meson. The authors also compare their dynamical model (ℙ-dyn) with a simplified vector-meson-dominance-inspired model (ℙ-am) that replaces the calculated transition matrix element with a phenomenological form factor.

## Key numbers
- For Υ photoproduction, the model covers the range 10 ≲ W/GeV ≲ 2000
- The Pomeron trajectory for light mesons (ρ⁰, φ) uses α₀ = 1.1, α₁ = 0.33 GeV⁻²; for heavy mesons (J/ψ, Υ) uses α₀ = 1.2, α₁ = 0.115 GeV⁻²
- Pomeron-quark couplings β_q for the ℙ-dyn model: ρ⁰ (light quarks) = 3.5 GeV⁻¹, φ (strange) = 1.89 GeV⁻¹, J/ψ (charm) = 0.11 GeV⁻¹, Υ (bottom) = 0.016 GeV⁻¹
- The proton mass m_p = 0.939 GeV is used for threshold calculations
- The nucleon isoscalar form factor uses t₀ = 0.71 GeV²
- The phenomenological form factor F_V(t) uses μ₀ = 1.1 GeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Pichowsky (1996) and Tang (2024) for J/ψ photoproduction to provide a unified description of all four vector mesons (ρ⁰, φ, J/ψ, Υ) from threshold to very high energies. The model represents a significant departure from earlier factorisation-based approaches (Nemchik 1994, Frankfurt 1998, Brodsky 2000) that relied on light-front wave function Ansätze and were only considered plausible at energies far above threshold. The results enable future studies to test Pomeron exchange universality and provide predictions for power-law exponents at large W and slope parameters near threshold that can be compared with new data from Jefferson Lab (GlueX) and CERN (LHCb).
