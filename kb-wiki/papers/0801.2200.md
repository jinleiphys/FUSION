# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering and Fusion Cross Section Data for the $^{12}$C+$^{208}$Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies by using a Folding Potential

> Simultaneous $χ^{2}$ analyses are performed for elastic scattering and fusion cross section data for the $^{12}$C+$^{208}$Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies by using the extended optical model approach in which the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts. Use is made of the double folding potential as a bare potential. It is found that the experimental elastic scattering and fusion data are well reproduced without introducing any normalization factor for the double folding potential and also that both DR and fusion parts of the polarization potential determined from the $χ^{2}$ analyses satisfy separately the dispersion relation. Furthermore, it is shown that the imaginary parts of both DR and fusion potentials at the strong absorption radius change very rapidly, which results in a typical threshold anomaly in the total imaginary potential as observed with tightly bound projectiles such as $α$-particle and $^{16}$O.

## Key claim

For the tightly bound projectile $^{12}$C on $^{208}$Pb, the experimental elastic scattering and fusion data are well reproduced by the extended optical model using a double folding potential without any normalization factor, and both the direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts of the polarization potential satisfy the dispersion relation separately. The imaginary parts of both DR and fusion potentials at the strong absorption radius change very rapidly, producing a threshold anomaly typical of tightly bound projectiles such as $\alpha$-particle and $^{16}$O, in contrast to the behavior seen for loosely bound projectiles like $^{6}$Li.

## Method

Simultaneous $\chi^{2}$ analyses were performed on elastic scattering angular distributions, fusion cross sections, and semi-experimental direct reaction (DR) cross sections for the $^{12}$C+$^{208}$Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. The optical potential was decomposed into a double folding bare potential (using the M3Y interaction) plus complex polarization potentials for fusion (volume-type) and DR (surface-derivative-type), with fixed geometrical parameters ($r_F=1.40$ fm, $a_F=0.35$ fm; $r_D=1.50$ fm, $a_D=0.55$ fm). The energy-dependent strength parameters $V_F(E)$, $W_F(E)$, $V_D(E)$, and $W_D(E)$ were determined in a two-step fitting procedure, and the real strengths were subsequently generated from the imaginary strengths via the dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energies extracted from linear systematics: $E_{0,F}^{\textrm{exp}} = 53.7$ MeV for fusion and $E_{0,D}^{\textrm{semi-exp}} = 55.6$ MeV for DR, showing DR channels open about 2 MeV higher than fusion channels.
- The DR imaginary strength $W_D(E)$ is parameterized as: zero for $E \leq 55.6$ MeV; $0.147(E-55.6)$ for $55.6 < E \leq 59.0$ MeV; $0.007(E-59.0)+0.15$ for $59.0 < E \leq 111.0$ MeV; constant $0.50$ MeV for $E > 111.0$ MeV.
- The fusion imaginary strength $W_F(E)$ is parameterized as: zero for $E \leq 53.7$ MeV; $0.485(E-53.7)$ for $53.7 < E \leq 60.3$ MeV; constant $3.20$ MeV for $E > 60.3$ MeV.
- At $E_{c.m.}=55.7$ MeV, the experimental fusion cross section is 14 mb and the semi-experimental DR cross section is 1 mb; at $E_{c.m.}=80.3$ MeV, fusion is 1045 mb and DR is 327 mb.
- The interaction distance $d_I$ is about 1.65 fm for $^{12}$C+$^{208}$Pb, compared to 1.9 fm for $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb.
- The strong absorption radius used for the $^{12}$C+$^{208}$Pb system is $R_{sa}=12.3$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the extended optical model approach previously applied by So, Udagawa, Hong, and Kim (2007) to loosely bound projectiles ($^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, $^{9}$Be) to the tightly bound $^{12}$C projectile on $^{208}$Pb. It contrasts with the earlier findings for $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb (Keeley et al., 1994; So et al., 2007), where DR dominated the reaction and the threshold anomaly appeared only in the fusion potential; here both DR and fusion potentials show rapid energy variation at the strong absorption radius, consistent with the threshold anomaly seen for strongly bound projectiles by Baeza et al. (1984), Lilley et al. (1985), and Fulton et al. (1985). The results enable future application of the same framework to other tightly bound systems and confirm that no normalization factor is needed for the double folding potential when breakup effects are weak.
