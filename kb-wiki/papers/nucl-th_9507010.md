# Charge Symmetry Violation in Nuclear Physics

> The study of charge symmetry violation in nuclear physics is a potentially enormous subject. Through a few topical examples we aim to show that it is not a subject of peripheral interest but rather goes to the heart of our understanding of hadronic systems.

# Charge Symmetry Violation in Nuclear Physics

## Key claim
Charge symmetry violation (CSV) in nuclear physics is not a peripheral subject but goes to the heart of understanding hadronic systems; the paper argues through selected examples that CSV effects in the nucleon-nucleon system, mirror nuclei, and CKM matrix unitarity tests reveal fundamental physics that conventional treatments with point-like nucleons cannot capture.

## Method
The paper reviews three topical areas of CSV: (1) class IV nucleon-nucleon forces, where the asymmetry difference between polarized neutron-proton and proton-neutron scattering is measured; (2) the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer (ONS) anomaly in mirror nuclei binding energy differences, treated within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model using self-consistent mean-field meson exchange with explicit quark degrees of freedom; and (3) the extraction of the CKM matrix element V_ud from super-allowed 0+ to 0+ nuclear beta decays, where the QMC model is used to compute density-dependent changes in the quark vector current overlap integrals.

## Key numbers
- The experimental asymmetry difference Delta A = A_n(theta) - A_p(theta) at IUCF is 33 +/- 5.9 +/- 4.3 (in units of 10^-4); theoretical predictions are 20 without rho-omega mixing and 35 with rho-omega mixing.
- For the ONS anomaly in mirror nuclei, the QMC model with bag radius R_0 = 0.8 fm yields discrepancies of 0.32 MeV for 15O-15N (observed 0.16 +/- 0.04 MeV), 0.25 MeV for 17F-17O (observed 0.31 +/- 0.06 MeV), 0.41 MeV for 39Ca-39K (observed 0.22 +/- 0.08 MeV), and 0.38 MeV for 41Sc-41Ca (observed 0.59 +/- 0.10 MeV).
- For the CKM unitarity test, the fractional increase in the ft-value at normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3 is between 0.15% and 0.20% depending on bag radius, compared with a violation of unitarity of 0.35 +/- 0.15% from the analysis of Towner and Hardy.
- The overlap integral I_ii'(rho_B)/I_ii'(0) decreases linearly with density with coefficient a_ii' approximately (2.4, 2.9, 3.3) x 10^-4 for R_0 = (0.6, 0.8, 1.0) fm respectively.

## Context
This review extends the long-standing work on CSV in nuclear physics, particularly the ONS anomaly first identified by Okamoto (1964) and Nolen and Schiffer (1969), and the class IV NN force classification of Henley and Miller. It addresses the theoretical controversy over whether the rho-omega mixing amplitude vanishes at q^2 = 0, following the work of Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (GHT) and subsequent studies by O'Connell et al. and Mitchell et al. The paper enables future experimental work by emphasizing the need for a second-generation measurement of the class IV NN asymmetry below 200 MeV, and it opens the possibility that quark-level CSV effects in nuclear matter could partially resolve the apparent CKM matrix unitarity violation.
