# High Temperature Superfluid and Feshbach Resonance

> We study an effective field theory describing cold fermionic atoms near a Feshbach resonance. The theory gives a unique description of the dynamics in the limit that the energy of the Feshbach resonance is tuned to be twice that of the Fermi surface. We show that in this limit the zero temperature superfluid condensate is of order the Fermi energy, and obtain a critical temperature $T_C \simeq 0.43 T_F$

## Key claim

The authors show that when a Feshbach resonance is tuned so that its energy in the medium is exactly zero (zero detuning), the zero-temperature superfluid gap reaches a robust value of order the Fermi energy, specifically \(\Delta = e^{-1/2}E_F\), and the critical temperature is \(T_C \simeq 0.43 T_F\) after accounting for atom number conservation.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for cold fermionic atoms near a Feshbach resonance, including a scalar resonance field coupled to fermions via a Yukawa interaction. They compute the Coleman-Weinberg (mean-field) potential for the resonance field in the medium, renormalizing it at the Fermi energy scale, and determine the superfluid gap from the minimum of this potential. Finite-temperature effects are included via the Matsubara formalism, and the critical temperature is obtained by imposing that the gap vanishes, with a subsequent correction from atom number conservation.

## Key numbers

- Zero-temperature superfluid gap at zero detuning: \(\Delta = e^{-1/2}E_F \approx 0.607 E_F\).
- Critical temperature at the Feshbach resonance in medium (before number conservation): \(T_C = 0.68 E_F/k_B\).
- Final critical temperature after number conservation: \(T_C \simeq 0.43 T_F\).
- For \(^{40}\mathrm{K}\) atoms, the Feshbach resonance occurs at a magnetic field of \(201.5 \pm 1.4\ \mathrm{G}\) with a width of \(8.0 \pm 1.1\ \mathrm{G}\), corresponding to \(\hat{E}_R = 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{eV}\) and \(\Gamma = 4\times 10^{-8}\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- For experimentally realizable ultracold atoms, \(E_F/k_B \sim 600\ \mathrm{nK}\) or \(E_F \sim 6\times 10^{-11}\ \mathrm{eV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Holland et al. (2001), Timmermans et al. (2001), and Griffin (2002) that the superfluid gap in ultracold fermionic gases could be made as large as the Fermi energy using a Feshbach resonance. The authors provide a rigorous effective field theory derivation of the gap and critical temperature, obtaining a critical temperature slightly larger than that of Holland et al. (2001) but then correcting it downward by roughly one third due to atom number conservation. The result enables experimental searches for high-temperature superfluidity in ultracold Fermi gases, such as \(^{40}\mathrm{K}\) and \(^{6}\mathrm{Li}\), by tuning the Feshbach resonance to zero detuning.
