# Relativistic Structure of the Nucleon Self-Energy in Asymmetric Nuclei

> The Dirac structure of the nucleon self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter cannot reliably be deduced from the momentum dependence of the single-particle energies. It is demonstrated that such attempts yield an isospin dependence with even a wrong sign. Relativistic studies of finite nuclei have been based on such studies of asymmetric nuclear matter. The effects of these isospin components on the results for finite nuclei are investigated.

# Relativistic Structure of the Nucleon Self-Energy in Asymmetric Nuclei

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of the nucleon self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter cannot be reliably extracted from the momentum dependence of single-particle energies; such attempts yield an isovector dependence with even the wrong sign, producing effective masses for protons that are smaller than those for neutrons, contrary to the correct result from a direct analysis of the Dirac structure.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic parametrization of the G-matrix from Boersma and Malfliet (parameter set III), which decomposes the G-matrix into five Lorentz-invariant amplitudes expressed as sums of Yukawa functions. They solve the Dirac-Hartree-Fock equation self-consistently to obtain the scalar, time-like vector, and space-like vector components of the nucleon self-energy for protons and neutrons in asymmetric nuclear matter at a baryon density of 0.185 nucleon fm$^{-3}$ and asymmetry parameter $\alpha = 0.35$. They then compare these directly computed self-energy components with those extracted by fitting the single-particle energy $\epsilon_i(k)$ at two momenta ($k_1 = 0.7 k_F$, $k_2 = k_F$) to a Hartree form, simulating the procedure of Engvik et al. and Lee et al. Finally, they use the density-dependent coupling constants derived from both methods in Dirac-Hartree calculations for finite nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{22}$O.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density used for asymmetric nuclear matter calculations: $\rho = 0.185$ nucleon fm$^{-3}$, corresponding roughly to saturation density.
- Asymmetry parameter for the main analysis: $\alpha = 0.35$ (proton fraction).
- Momentum variation of scalar and vector self-energy components: around 20 MeV for momenta below the Fermi momentum, about 5 percent of the total value.
- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ($k_F \approx 1.4$ fm$^{-1}$), the effective masses from the two methods are rather similar.
- For finite nuclei, the difference in calculated binding energy between the two methods is as large as 0.6 MeV per nucleon for $^{16}$O and around 0.9 MeV per nucleon for $^{22}$O.
- The effect of including isovector mesons ($\delta$ and $\rho$) is only around 0.3 MeV per nucleon, smaller than the discrepancy between the two extraction methods.

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach to asymmetric nuclear matter, building on prior DBHF calculations for symmetric nuclear matter by Brockmann and Machleidt, and by Boersma and Malfliet. It directly contradicts the isospin dependence reported by Engvik et al. and Lee et al., who extracted the Dirac structure from single-particle energies and obtained effective masses for protons smaller than those for neutrons. The authors show this is an artifact of the extraction method. The results enable improved density-dependent coupling constants for the Relativistic Mean Field with Density-dependent couplings (RMFD) approach used by Shen et al. for finite nuclei, correcting the sign of the isovector meson contributions.
