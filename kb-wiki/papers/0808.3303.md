# Deformation of Lambda hypernuclei

> We study the deformation property of $Λ$ hypernuclei using the relativistic mean field (RMF) method. We find that $^{29}_Λ$Si and $^{13}_Λ$C hypernuclei have spherical shape as a consequence of the additional $Λ$ particle, whereas the corresponding core nuclei, $^{28}$Si and $^{12}$C, are oblately deformed. Most of other hypernuclei have a similar deformation parameter to the core nucleus, in accordance with the previous study with the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method. We discuss the sensitivity of our results to the choice of pairing interaction and to the parameter set of the RMF Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding a single Lambda hyperon to the oblate core nuclei 28Si and 12C can change their shape to spherical, but this effect is not universal: most other hypernuclei studied have a deformation parameter similar to their core nucleus. The result for 28Si is robust across two RMF parameter sets (NL3 and NLSH) and three treatments of pairing, while the result for 30Si and 32Si shows strong dependence on the parameter set and pairing treatment.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations for Lambda hypernuclei using the NL3 and NLSH parameter sets, with the Lambda particle coupled only to sigma and omega mesons (coupling constants g_omega_Lambda = 2/3 g_omega and g_sigma_Lambda = 0.621 g_sigma). They modified the axially-symmetric code RMFAXIAL to include the Lambda particle, solved the equations self-consistently with harmonic oscillator expansion, and computed the quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2 from the intrinsic quadrupole moment. Pairing correlations among nucleons were treated in the BCS approximation using either constant gap, constant force (seniority pairing), or no pairing.

## Key numbers

- 28Si core nucleus has beta_2 = -0.31 (oblate) with NL3 constant-Delta pairing; 28+Lambda Si has beta_2 = 0.00 (spherical) under the same conditions.
- 12C core nucleus has beta_2 = -0.25 (oblate) with NLSH constant-Delta pairing; 12+Lambda C has beta_2 = 0.00 (spherical) under the same conditions.
- For 22Ne, the energy difference between oblate and prolate minima is 3.04 MeV, and between spherical and prolate configurations is 3.63 MeV; the prolate minimum is little affected by adding a Lambda.
- For 28Si, the energy difference between the oblate and spherical configurations is 0.754 MeV, which is easily inverted when a Lambda particle is added.
- For 12C with NLSH, the energy difference between oblate and spherical configurations is 0.13 MeV; adding a Lambda makes the ground state spherical.

## Context

This work extends the study of hypernuclear deformation from the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method (Zhou et al., 2007) to the relativistic mean field approach, motivated by the argument that the spin-orbit potential change due to the Lambda is more emphasized in RMF than in non-relativistic approaches (Vretenar et al., 1998). The paper finds that while most hypernuclei have similar deformation to their cores (consistent with Zhou et al., 2007), 12C and 28Si are exceptions where the Lambda induces a shape change from oblate to spherical. This enables future experimental searches for the first 4+ state energy deviation from rotational spectra, and theoretical work using generator coordinate method calculations to compute excitation spectra for comparison with data.
