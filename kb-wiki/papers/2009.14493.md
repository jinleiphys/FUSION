# Stable double-heavy tetraquarks: spectrum and structure

> Bound states of double-heavy tetraquarks are studied in a constituent quark model. Two bound states are found for isospin and spin-parity I(J^P ) = 0(1^+) in the bb\bar{u}\bar{d} channel. One is deeply bound and compact made of colored diquarks, while the other is shallow and extended as a BB^* molecule. The former agrees well with lattice QCD results. A systematic decrease in the binding energy is seen by replacing one of the heavy quarks to a lighter one. Altogether we find ten bound states. It is shown for the first time that hadrons with totally different natures emerge from a single Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a single constituent quark model Hamiltonian can produce both a deeply bound, compact tetraquark state and a shallow, extended molecular state in the same \(bb\bar{u}\bar{d}\) channel with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\), but the shallow state's existence may be modified by long-range meson-exchange interactions that are not included in the model.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation using a non-relativistic quark model Hamiltonian with a power-law confinement term and a gluon-exchange potential, employing the Gaussian expansion method for variational wave functions. They tuned the Hamiltonian parameters to reproduce experimental heavy meson masses within 30 MeV, then searched for bound tetraquark states across various flavor combinations of two heavy quarks (\(b\) or \(c\)) and two light antiquarks (\(u\), \(d\), or \(s\)) with spin-parities \(J^P = 0^+, 1^+, 2^+\).

## Key numbers

- Deeply bound \(bb\bar{u}\bar{d}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\): binding energy 173 MeV below the \(BB^*\) threshold
- Shallow \(bb\bar{u}\bar{d}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\): binding energy 4 MeV below the \(BB^*\) threshold
- \(bc\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\): binding energy 40 MeV
- \(cc\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\): binding energy 23 MeV
- \(bs\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\): binding energy 5 MeV
- \(bb\bar{s}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=1/2(1^+)\): binding energy 59 MeV
- \(bc\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(0^+)\): binding energy 37 MeV below the \(BD\) threshold
- \(bs\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(0^+)\): binding energy 7 MeV
- \(bb\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=1(2^+)\): binding energy 3 MeV below the \(B^*B^*\) threshold
- \(bc\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state with \(I(J^P)=0(2^+)\): binding energy 5 MeV below the \(D^*B^*\) threshold
- Mean distance between the two \(b\) quarks in the deeply bound \(bb\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state: 0.34 fm
- Mean distance between the two \(b\) quarks in the shallow \(bb\bar{q}\bar{q}\) state: 1.09 fm

## Context

This work extends the diquark-based predictions of Karliner (2017) and Eichten (2017) for double-heavy tetraquarks, and agrees well with lattice QCD results from Francis (2016), Junnarkar (2018), Hudspith (2020), and Mohanta (2020) for channels containing \(cc\) or \(bb\) heavy quarks. The finding of both a compact diquark state and a molecular \(BB^*\) state in the same channel is a hadronic analogue of cluster formation in light nuclei described by Ikeda (1968), and enables future experimental searches for these states at facilities such as LHC, KEK, and BEP.
