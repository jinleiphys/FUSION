# Halo phenomenon in finite many-fermion systems. Atom-positron complexes and large-scale study of atomic nuclei

> The analysis method proposed in Ref. \cite{rotival07a} is applied to characterize halo properties in finite many-fermion systems. First, the versatility of the method is highlighted by applying it to light and medium-mass nuclei as well as to atom-positron and ion-positronium complexes. Second, the dependence of nuclear halo properties on the characteristics of the energy density functional used in self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations is studied. It is found that (a) the low-density behavior of the pairing functional and the regularization/renormalization scheme must be chosen coherently and with care to provide meaningful predictions, (b) the impact of pairing correlations on halo properties is significant and is the result of two competing effects, (c) the detailed characteristics of the pairing functional has however only little importance, (d) halo properties depend significantly on any ingredient of the energy density functional that influences the location of single-particle levels; i.e. the effective mass, the tensor terms and the saturation density of nuclear matter. The latter dependencies give insights to how experimental data on medium-mass drip-line nuclei can be used in the distant future to constrain some characteristics of the nuclear energy density functional. Last but not least, large scale predictions of halos among all spherical even-even nuclei are performed using specific sets of particle-hole and particle-particle energy functionals. It is shown that halos in the ground state of medium-mass nuclei will only be found at the very limit of neutron stability and for a limited number of elements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that halos in medium-mass nuclei will only appear at the very limit of neutron stability and for a limited number of elements, with the analysis method showing that the formation of halos is hindered as mass increases because of increased collectivity, contrary to the more extreme halo structures seen in light nuclei or atom-positron complexes.

## Method

The authors applied a model-independent analysis method based on the internal one-body density to characterize halo properties in finite many-fermion systems. They performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using Skyrme energy density functionals for spherical even-even nuclei, and fixed-core stochastic variational method calculations for atom-positron and ion-positronium complexes. The dependence of nuclear halo properties on the characteristics of the energy density functional was studied by varying the regularization/renormalization scheme for the pairing functional, the angular-momentum cutoff, the isoscalar effective mass, saturation density, and tensor terms.

## Context

This work extends the analysis method proposed by Rotival et al. (2007) for characterizing halo properties in finite many-fermion systems, applying it to systems of different scales from light nuclei studied through coupled-channels calculations by Nunes et al. (1996) to atom-positron complexes computed with the fixed-core stochastic variational method by Mitroy et al. (2001-2005). The results contradict earlier interpretations based on the Helm model by Mizutori et al. (2000) that suggested halos in tin isotopes, showing instead that the kink in neutron r.m.s. radius at N=82 is purely a shell effect. The study enables future constraints on nuclear energy density functionals using experimental data from next-generation radioactive ion beam facilities (FAIR, RIBF, REX-ISOLDE, SPIRAL2) that may reach the neutron drip-line up to about Z=26.
