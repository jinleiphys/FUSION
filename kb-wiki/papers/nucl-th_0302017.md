# Analysis of previous microscopic calculations for second $0^+$ state in $^{12}$C in terms of 3-alpha particle Bose-condensed state

> The wave function of the second $0^+$ state of $^{12}$C which was obtained long time ago by solving the microscopic 3$α$ problem is shown to be almost completely equivalent to the wave function of the 3$α$ condensed state which has been proposed recently by the present authors. This equivalence of the wave functions is shown to hold in two cases where different effective two-nucleon forces are adopted. This finding gives strong support for interpreting the second $0^+$ state of $^{12}$C which is the key state for the synthesis of $^{12}$C in stars ('Hoyle' state), and which is one of the typical mysterious $0^+$ states in light nuclei, as a gas-like structure of three $α$ particles, Bose-condensed into an identical s-wave function.

## Key claim

The wave function of the second 0+ state of 12C obtained from earlier microscopic 3-alpha cluster model calculations is shown to be almost completely equivalent to the wave function of a 3-alpha Bose-condensed state, holding for two different effective two-nucleon forces (Volkov No.1 with M=0.575 and Volkov No.2 with M=0.59). This equivalence supports interpreting the Hoyle state as a gas-like structure of three alpha particles condensed into an identical s-wave function.

## Method

The authors constructed a 3-alpha condensate wave function with spatial deformation, allowing axial symmetry (beta_x = beta_y), and performed Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) calculations by superposing these wave functions over 48 grid points in the (beta_x, beta_z) parameter space. They compared the resulting GCM eigen energies, root-mean-square radii, and monopole matrix elements for the 0+1, 0+2, and 2+1 states against the exact solutions of the full microscopic 3-alpha problem previously obtained by Uegaki et al. and Kamimura et al., using the same effective two-nucleon forces and oscillator parameters (b=1.41 fm for force I, b=1.35 fm for force II).

## Key numbers

- GCM minimum energy for the 0+2 state with force II: -81.55 MeV, compared to the full 3-alpha calculation value of -81.7 MeV by Kamimura et al.
- GCM minimum energy for the 0+2 state with force I: -79.83 MeV, compared to the full 3-alpha calculation value of -79.3 MeV by Uegaki et al.
- Squared overlap between the GCM 0+2 wave function and the orthogonalized condensate state: 0.95 for force I and 0.97 for force II.
- Spherical condensate component in the GCM 0+2 wave function: 92% for force I and 91% for force II.
- GCM r.m.s. radius for the 0+2 state: 4.44 fm (force I) and 3.83 fm (force II), compared to 3.50 fm and 3.47 fm from the full 3-alpha calculations.
- GCM monopole matrix element M(0+2 -> 0+1): 5.36 fm2 (force I) and 6.45 fm2 (force II), compared to 6.6 fm2 and 6.7 fm2 from the full 3-alpha calculations.

## Context

This work extends the earlier microscopic 3-alpha cluster model calculations by Uegaki et al. and Kamimura et al. (both published approximately a quarter century before this paper), which had already predicted a large r.m.s. radius for the 0+2 state and a gas-like structure based on reduced width amplitude analysis. The paper also builds on the authors' own recent proposal of alpha-particle condensation in low-density nuclear matter (Ropke, Schuck, and coauthors) and their previous application of condensate wave functions to 12C and 16O. The finding that the exact 3-alpha wave functions are equivalent to a Bose-condensed state enables a new physical interpretation of the Hoyle state and suggests that similar condensate states may exist in other self-conjugate 4n nuclei near the n-alpha threshold.
