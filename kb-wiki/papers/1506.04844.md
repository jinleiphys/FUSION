# Microscopic dynamical description of proton-induced fission with the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) Model

> The microscopic description of nuclear fission still remains a topic of intense basic research. Un- derstanding nuclear fission, apart from a theoretical point of view, is of practical importance for energy production and the transmutation of nuclear waste. In nuclear astrophysics, fission sets the upper limit to the nucleosynthesis of heavy elements via the r-process. In this work we initiated a systematic study of intermediate energy proton-induced fission using the Constrained Molecu- lar Dynamics (CoMD) code. The CoMD code implements an effective interaction with a nuclear matter compressibility of K=200 (soft EOS) with several forms of the density dependence of the nucleon-nucleon symmetry potential. Moreover, a constraint is imposed in the phase-space occu- pation for each nucleon restoring the Pauli principle at each time step of the collision. A proper choice of the surface parameter of the effective interaction has been made to describe fission. In this work, we present results of fission calculations for proton-induced reactions on : a) 232 Th at 27 and 63 MeV, b) 235 U at 10, 30, 60 and 100 MeV, and c) 238 U at 100 and 660 MeV. The calculated observables include fission-fragment mass distributions, total fission energies, neutron multiplicities and fission times. These observables are compared to available experimental data. We show that the microscopic CoMD code is able to describe the complicated many-body dynamics of the fission process at intermediate and high energy and give a reasonable estimate of the fission time scale. Sensitivity of the results to the density dependence of the nucleon symmetry potential (and, thus, the nuclear symmetry energy) is found. Further improvements of the code are necessary to achieve a satisfactory description of low energy fission in which shell effects play a dominant role.

## Key claim

The Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model can describe the many-body dynamics of proton-induced fission at intermediate and high energies (above approximately 20 MeV) and give a reasonable estimate of the fission time scale, but it cannot reproduce the asymmetric mass distributions of low-energy fission because the model lacks spin-orbit coupling and therefore does not produce the correct shell effects.

## Method

The authors used the CoMD semiclassical N-body transport code, which represents nucleons as Gaussian wave packets and imposes a phase-space occupation constraint to restore the Pauli principle at each time step. They employed a soft equation of state (compressibility K=200) with two forms of the density-dependent symmetry potential ("standard" and "soft"), set the surface term to zero, and collected 3000-5000 events per reaction with impact parameters from 0-6 fm, following each event up to 15000 fm/c and registering fragments every 50 fm/c using the minimum spanning tree method.

## Key numbers

* For p + 232Th at 27 MeV, the CoMD calculations produced a symmetric mass distribution with a flat top, in contrast to the asymmetric experimental data; at 63 MeV, the agreement improved as the experimental distribution became more symmetric.
* For p + 235U at 10 MeV, calculations with the standard symmetry potential gave a symmetric distribution, while the soft symmetry potential showed a hint of asymmetry; at 30 MeV, the peak-to-valley ratio in the experimental data decreased and the CoMD symmetric calculations were in better agreement.
* For p + 238U at 660 MeV, the CoMD calculations with both symmetry potentials were in reasonable agreement with experimental data near symmetric mass splits, with the yield axis on a logarithmic scale.
* The fission time for a typical p + 232Th event at 63 MeV was t_fission = 2500 fm/c, with the system followed for an additional 2000 fm/c after scission for fragment de-excitation.
* Ground state configurations of target nuclei were tested for stability for 2000-3000 fm/c before use in fission simulations.

## Context

This work extends dynamical fission studies using the CoMD model (Papa 2001) to proton-induced reactions on thorium and uranium, complementing statistical approaches such as GEMINI++ and the SMM code, as well as Langevin-type dynamical models (Nadtochy 2013, Randrup 2013). The results show that CoMD can describe intermediate and high-energy fission where shell effects are washed out, but the lack of spin-orbit coupling prevents accurate description of low-energy asymmetric fission, motivating future inclusion of spin dependence in the interaction (following recent BUU work from 2014) and coupling to a de-excitation code for fragment evaporation.
