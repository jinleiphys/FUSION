# High-resolution (p,t) reaction measurements as spectroscopic tests of {\it ab-initio} theory in the mid $pf$-shell

> Detailed spectroscopic measurements of excited states in $^{50}$Cr and $^{62}$Zn were performed using 24~MeV (p,t) transfer reactions on $^{52}$Cr and $^{64}$Zn, respectively. In total, forty-five states in $^{50}$Cr and sixty-seven states in $^{62}$Zn were observed up to excitation energies of 5.5~MeV, including several previously unobserved states. These experimental results are compared to {\it ab-initio} shell-model calculations using chiral effective field theory ($χ$-EFT) with the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) method. This comparison demonstrates good agreement in the level orderings with these new theoretical methods, albeit with a slight over binding in the calculations. This work is part of a continued push to benchmark {\it ab-initio} theoretical techniques to nuclear structure data in $0^+\rightarrow0^+$ superallowed Fermi $β$ decay systems.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that VS-IMSRG ab-initio calculations using chiral effective field theory show good agreement with experimental level orderings in the mid pf-shell nuclei 50Cr and 62Zn, but with a systematic overbinding (calculated energies are too high) that is more severe in 62Zn than in 50Cr, and the calculations do not fully capture the physics of highly collective nuclei.

## Method
High-resolution (p,t) transfer reactions were performed using 24 MeV proton beams on isotopically enriched 52Cr and 64Zn targets at the Maier-Leibnitz-Laboratorium Q3D magnetic spectrograph. Outgoing tritons were detected at multiple angles between 10° and 60° using a cathode-strip detector with approximately 10 keV FWHM energy resolution, and differential cross-sections were extracted. Distorted-wave Born-approximation calculations using the FRESCO code with global optical-model parameters (Varner et al. 1991 for protons, Li, Liang, and Cai 2007 for tritons) were used to assign spin-parities, and the experimental results were compared to VS-IMSRG ab-initio calculations using the 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral interaction.

## Key numbers
- 45 states observed in 50Cr and 67 states observed in 62Zn up to excitation energies of 5.5 MeV
- 24 MeV proton beam energy used for both (p,t) reactions
- Energy resolution of roughly 10 keV FWHM
- Ground state (p,t) cross-section at 10°: 6524(327) μb/sr for 50Cr and 6836(344) μb/sr for 62Zn
- First 2+ state in 50Cr at 783.32(3) keV with cross-section 133(7) μb/sr at 10°
- First 2+ state in 62Zn at 953.84(9) keV with cross-section 133(7) μb/sr at 10°
- 1- state in 50Cr assigned at 4130.5(4) keV, removing previous ambiguity of (1,2+)
- 1- state in 62Zn firmly assigned at 3374(2) keV, previously tentative (1-,2+)
- 5- state in 62Zn at 4306(6) keV, previously unobserved
- 5- state in 62Zn at 4869(9) keV, previously unobserved
- 2+ state in 62Zn at 3181.1(3) keV, previously tentative (1+), now firmly assigned as 2+

## Context
This work extends the benchmarking of VS-IMSRG ab-initio calculations using chiral effective field theory into the mid pf-shell for the first time, specifically for the superallowed beta-decay daughter nuclei 50Cr and 62Zn. Previous VS-IMSRG studies using the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction had been applied to lighter pf-shell isotopes (Leis 2018, Moug 2018, Izzo 2017, Stephens 2017, Garnsworthy 2017), but mid-shell nuclei with highly collective features present a greater challenge. The results enable future improvements to ab-initio calculations by demonstrating the need to include three-body operator effects to produce more compressed spectra, and support the continued effort to benchmark theoretical techniques for nuclear-structure corrections in superallowed Fermi beta decay systems.
