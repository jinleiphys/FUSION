# Wounded quarks at the LHC

> We review the results of the wounded quark model, with a stress on eccentricity observables in small systems. A new element is a presentation of symmetric cumulants for the elliptic and triangular flow correlations, obtained in the wounded-quark approach.

# Wounded quarks at the LHC

## Key claim

The wounded quark model, which assumes that particle production scales linearly with the number of wounded (participant) quarks, can reproduce the negative sign and centrality dependence of symmetric cumulants for elliptic and triangular flow correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, and predicts large correlations in small systems such as p+p, p+Pb, and d+Au that may be tested in future data analyses.

## Method

The authors implement the wounded quark model within a Glauber-based framework, where each nucleon is composed of three constituent quarks that can be wounded independently in a collision. They compute the initial-state eccentricities ε₂ (ellipticity) and ε₃ (triangularity) of the fireball from the spatial distribution of wounded quarks, using a Gaussian smearing parameter σ of either 0.4 fm or 0.2 fm. The symmetric cumulant SC(ε₂,ε₃) and Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(ε₂,ε₃) are then evaluated from the eccentricity distributions and compared to ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions, with predictions extended to p+p, p+Pb, and d+Au systems.

## Key numbers

- For p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV, both ellipticity ε₂ and triangularity ε₃ are large, with values shown as functions of mean charged multiplicity at |η| < 2.4 for two values of the Gaussian smearing parameter (σ = 0.4 fm and σ = 0.2 fm).
- The symmetric cumulant SC(ε₂,ε₃) for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC is negative and falls off with centrality, with the wounded quark model following the trend of ALICE data (ALICE 2016).
- For p+Pb and d+Au reactions, the Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(ε₂,ε₃) closely follows the symmetric cumulant measure, while for p+p collisions the two measures are substantially different.

## Context

This work extends the wounded quark model originally introduced by Bialas (1977) and Anisovich (1977), which was applied to RHIC data by Eremin and Voloshin (2003) and later advocated by the PHENIX Collaboration (2013, 2015). The results contribute to the ongoing discussion about whether the relevant degrees of freedom in the early stage of ultra-relativistic collisions are nucleons, quarks, partons, or gluonic hot-spots. The predictions for small systems (p+p, p+Pb, d+Au) provide testable expectations for future flow correlation measurements at the LHC, building on the symmetric cumulant formalism introduced by the ALICE Collaboration (2016).
