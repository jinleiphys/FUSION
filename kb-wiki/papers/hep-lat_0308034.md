# $J/ψ$ and $η_c$ in the Deconfined Plasma from Lattice QCD

> Analyzing correlation functions of charmonia at finite temperature ($T$) on $32^3\times(32-96)$ anisotropic lattices by the maximum entropy method (MEM), we find that $J/ψ$ and $η_c$ survive as distinct resonances in the plasma even up to $T \simeq 1.6 T_c$ and that they eventually dissociate between $1.6 T_c$ and $1.9 T_c$ ($T_c$ is the critical temperature of deconfinement). This suggests that the deconfined plasma is non-perturbative enough to hold heavy-quark bound states. The importance of having sufficient number of temporal data points in MEM analyses is also emphasized.

## Key claim

Using quenched lattice QCD with anisotropic lattices and the maximum entropy method (MEM), the authors find that \(J/\psi\) and \(\eta_c\) survive as distinct resonances up to \(T \simeq 1.6 T_c\) and dissociate between \(1.6 T_c\) and \(1.9 T_c\), but they caution that the width of the peak partly reflects unphysical broadening due to limited statistics, so they cannot draw definite conclusions on thermal mass shift or broadening.

## Method

The authors performed quenched QCD simulations on \(32^3 \times N_\tau\) anisotropic lattices with renormalized anisotropy \(\xi = a_\sigma / a_\tau = 4.0\) and temporal lattice spacing \(a_\tau = 9.75 \times 10^{-3}\) fm. They measured point-point correlation functions for vector (\(J/\psi\)) and pseudoscalar (\(\eta_c\)) channels at five temperatures (\(T/T_c = 0.78, 1.38, 1.62, 1.87, 2.33\)) and extracted spectral functions using the maximum entropy method (MEM), with careful tests of MEM error analysis and sensitivity to the number of temporal data points \(N_{\rm data}\).

## Key numbers

- The temporal lattice spacing is \(a_\tau = 9.75 \times 10^{-3}\) fm, determined from the \(\rho\) meson mass in the chiral limit.
- The zero-temperature masses on the \(T=0\) lattice are \(m_{J/\psi} = 3.10\) GeV and \(m_{\eta_c} = 3.03\) GeV.
- The number of temporal lattice sites \(N_\tau\) used are 32, 40, 46, 54, and 96, corresponding to \(T/T_c = 2.33, 1.87, 1.62, 1.38, 0.78\) respectively.
- The number of gauge configurations \(N_{\rm gauge}\) ranges from 141 to 194 across the five temperatures.
- The default model values used are \(m(\omega) / (3\omega^2) = 0.40\) for the vector channel and \(m(\omega) / \omega^2 = 1.15\) for the pseudoscalar channel.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous MEM-based lattice QCD studies of spectral functions at zero temperature (Asakawa et al. 1999) and of \(s\bar{s}\) mesons at finite temperature (Asakawa et al. 2002) to the charmonium sector. It contradicts the expectation that heavy-quark bound states dissociate immediately at the deconfinement temperature, and instead supports the picture that the quark-gluon plasma is strongly interacting enough to hold hadronic resonances up to \(1.6 T_c\). The results enable future full QCD simulations with dynamical quarks and studies of the spatial structure of these resonances, as well as comparisons with non-relativistic potential approaches.
