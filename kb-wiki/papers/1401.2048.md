# In-medium QCD sum rules for D mesons: A projection method for higher order contributions

> D mesons serve as excellent probes of hot and/or dense strongly interacting matter. They can provide insight into the restoration of chiral symmetry. The chiral condensate as well as other chirally odd condensates, such as certain four-quark condensates, are linked to order parameters of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. Thus, the evaluation of these higher order condensate contributions in the framework of QCD sum rules is of high interest. We present a general method for projecting Lorentz indices of ground state expectation values providing a crucial step towards a comprehensive calculation of higher order corrections to the operator product expansion of hadrons, especially D mesons, in a strongly interacting medium.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general method for projecting Lorentz indices of ground state expectation values, which is a crucial step toward a comprehensive calculation of higher order corrections to the operator product expansion (OPE) of hadrons, especially D mesons, in a strongly interacting medium, but the method itself is formal and does not yet deliver numerical results for the Wilson coefficients.

## Method

The authors develop a projection method for Lorentz indices of expectation values carrying up to five Lorentz indices, using the metric tensor, the totally anti-symmetric pseudo-tensor, and the medium velocity vector as basis structures. They construct projection vectors for each number of indices, define a matrix from their contractions, and invert it to obtain the coefficients that express the expectation value as a linear combination of the allowed Lorentz structures. The method separates vacuum and medium-specific contributions by applying the projection with and without the medium velocity.

## Key numbers

* The chiral condensate in medium is given by ⟨q̄q⟩_{T,n} = ⟨q̄q⟩ (1 − T²/(8f²_π) − σ_N n/(m²_π f²_π)), where f_π is the pion decay constant, m_π the pion mass, and σ_N the nucleon sigma term.
* Four-quark condensate contributions to D meson sum rules appear at leading order in α_s on tree level.
* The projection method is explicitly constructed for expectation values with up to n = 5 Lorentz indices.

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule evaluations for light mesons, where chirally odd four-quark condensates were found to have significant impact (Hilger 2012), and for D mesons in medium where Wilson coefficients were previously determined only up to mass dimension 5 (Hilger 2009). The method enables the calculation of higher-order condensate contributions, particularly four-quark condensates, which are linked to order parameters of chiral symmetry breaking and are relevant for interpreting experiments at FAIR (CBM and Panda collaborations).
