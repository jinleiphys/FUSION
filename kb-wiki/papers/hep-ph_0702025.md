# Fluctuations and the Phase Transition in a Chiral Model with Polyakov Loops

> We explore the NJL model with Polyakov loops for a system of three colors and two flavors within the mean-field approximation, where both chiral symmetry and confinement are taken into account. We focus on the phase structure of the model and study the chiral and Polyakov loop susceptibilities.

## Key claim

The PNJL model, within mean-field approximation, shows that the deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions do not generally coincide; their coincidence is accidental, and the deconfinement transition signaled by the broad bump in the Polyakov loop susceptibility sets in earlier than the chiral transition at vanishing net quark density.

## Method

The authors compute the chiral susceptibility χ_mm and the Polyakov loop susceptibility χ_l\bar{l} = ⟨Φ̄Φ⟩ − ⟨Φ̄⟩⟨Φ⟩ as functions of temperature at zero chemical potential in the PNJL model with non-local four-fermion interactions, using a polynomial effective gluon potential fitted to pure gauge lattice QCD. They also examine the phase diagram in the (T,μ) plane by locating the peak positions of these susceptibilities, and compare results obtained with an improved logarithmic effective potential that respects the full SU(3) group structure.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point (TCP) is located at (T_c = 157 MeV, μ_c = 266 MeV) in the chiral limit.
- The pure gauge deconfinement transition temperature is T_0 = 270 MeV, used to fix the coefficients of the effective Polyakov loop potential.
- The chiral susceptibility χ_mm exhibits a narrow divergent peak at the chiral critical temperature T_ch, while χ_l\bar{l} remains finite at all temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model studies of Sasaki, Friman, and Redlich (2007) by focusing on order-parameter susceptibilities, following earlier PNJL formulations by the same group and others (e.g., PNJL model papers from 2004-2006). The results contradict lattice QCD findings that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration appear along the same critical line; the authors note that the polynomial effective potential does not possess the complete group structure of color SU(3), and an improved potential from RRW (2007) yields positive Polyakov loop susceptibilities in qualitative agreement with lattice results. The work enables further study of how dynamical quark effects modify the effective gluon potential and its chemical potential dependence.
