# Separate chemical freeze-out of strange particles with conservation laws

> The Hadron Resonance Gas Model with two chemical freeze-outs, connected by conservation laws is considered. We are arguing that the chemical freeze-out of strange hadrons should occur earlier than the chemical freeze-out of non-strange hadrons. The hadron multiplicities measured in the heavy ion collisions for the center of mass energy range 2.7 - 200 GeV are described well by such a model. Based on a success of such an approach, a radical way to improve the Hadron Resonance Gas Model performance is suggested. Thus, we suggest to identify the hadronic reactions that freeze-out noticeably earlier or later that most of the others reactions (for different collision energies they may be different) and to consider a separate freeze-out for them.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chemical freeze-out of strange hadrons should occur earlier than the chemical freeze-out of non-strange hadrons in heavy ion collisions, based on the hierarchy of reaction cross-sections and particle concentrations; the model achieves a good description of hadron multiplicities across a wide energy range, but the improvement is most significant at intermediate collision energies (around 6.3, 12, and 17 GeV) and not at low energies or the highest RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors construct a Hadron Resonance Gas Model with two separate chemical freeze-outs: one for all strange particles (SFO) and one for non-strange hadrons (FO). The two freeze-outs are connected by conservation laws for baryon number, isospin third projection, and entropy, which reduces the number of additional free parameters to one (the SFO temperature) per collision energy. The model includes multicomponent hard-core repulsion with different radii for pions (0.1 fm), kaons (0.38 fm), other mesons (0.4 fm), and baryons (0.2 fm), and accounts for resonance decays using Breit-Wigner averaging.

## Key numbers

- The model achieves χ²/dof = 58.5/55 = 1.06 for the two-freeze-out fit, compared to 1.16 for the single freeze-out fit.
- The analysis covers center-of-mass energies from √S_NN = 2.7 GeV to 200 GeV.
- At √S_NN = 6.3, 12, and 17 GeV, the SFO approach improves description of all ratios with more than one σ deviation; at √S_NN = 17 GeV, six out of seven problematic ratios from the one-freeze-out fit move to deviations smaller than σ.
- The SFO temperatures show largest deviations from FO temperatures at low and high energies, but do not exceed 20%.
- The hard-core radii used are: R_b = 0.2 fm (baryons), R_m = 0.4 fm (other mesons), R_π = 0.1 fm (pions), R_K = 0.38 fm (kaons).

## Context

This work extends the earlier SFO concept of Bugaev et al. (2013) by incorporating conservation laws (baryon number, isospin, entropy) between the two freeze-outs, unlike the approach of Gupta et al. (2013) which used six independent parameters and violated conservation laws. The model addresses the long-standing problem of strange particle deviations from chemical equilibrium, which was previously handled by the ad-hoc strangeness suppression factor γ_s introduced by Rafelski (1982). The results enable a systematic method to identify hadronic reactions that freeze-out at different times for each collision energy, potentially applicable to charmed particles.
