# Magnetic corrections to the boson self-coupling and boson-fermion coupling in the linear sigma model with quarks

> We compute the magnetic field-induced modifications to the boson self-coupling and the boson-fermion coupling, in the static limit, using an effective model of QCD, the linear sigma model with quarks. The former is computed for arbitrary field strengths as well as using the strong field approximation. The latter is obtained in the strong field limit. The arbitrary field result for the boson self-coupling depends on the ultraviolet renormalization scale and this dependence cannot be removed by a simple vacuum subtraction. Using the strong field result as a guide, we find the appropriate choice for this scale and discuss the physical implications. The boson-fermion coupling depends on the Schwinger's phase and we show how this phase can be treated consistently in such a way that the magnetic field induced vertex modification is both gauge invariant and can be written with an explicit factor corresponding to energy-momentum conservation for the external particles. Both couplings show a modest decrease with the field strength.

## Key claim

The magnetic field-induced modifications to the boson self-coupling and the boson-fermion coupling in the linear sigma model with quarks show a modest decrease with field strength, but the arbitrary-field result for the boson self-coupling depends on the ultraviolet renormalization scale in a way that cannot be removed by simple vacuum subtraction, requiring a scale choice that matches the strong-field limit.

## Method

The authors computed one-loop magnetic field corrections to the boson self-coupling for arbitrary field strengths and in the strong-field (lowest Landau level) approximation, and to the boson-fermion coupling in the strong-field limit, using the linear sigma model with quarks. For the boson-fermion coupling, they treated the Schwinger phase consistently to obtain a gauge-invariant vertex modification that respects energy-momentum conservation for external particles described as plane waves. The calculations were performed in the static limit where external momenta are taken to zero.

## Key numbers

- The effective boson self-coupling shows a relative change from the vacuum value of order 0.8% for the chosen parameters (lambda = 0.5, m_pi = 0.140 GeV).
- The strong-field limit result for the boson self-coupling correction is Gamma_lambda^LLL = -(lambda/(6 pi^2)) * (|q_b B| / (|q_b B| + m_pi^2)).
- The pion mass is set to m_pi = 140 MeV and the sigma mass range is m_sigma = 400-600 MeV, giving the parameter a = 225-390 MeV.
- The boson-fermion coupling correction in the LLL approximation involves an integral over a parameter u from 0 to 1 with alpha = (m_pi^2 + |eB|)/m_f^2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inverse magnetic catalysis in the linear sigma model with quarks (Ayala et al., 2015-2019) and the behavior of neutral pion masses in weak magnetic fields (pionmassmag, 2019). It addresses the need to compute magnetic field corrections to interaction vertices to describe pion masses over a wider range of field intensities, as lattice QCD results (Bali et al., 2018; Ding et al., 2019) show that neutral pion mass decreases and charged pion mass increases with field strength, which cannot be fully reproduced by effective models without accounting for coupling modifications (Das, 2019; Li, 2019). The results enable improved calculations of meson properties in magnetized matter relevant for heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.
