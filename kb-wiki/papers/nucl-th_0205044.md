# Non-conventional statistical effects in relativistic heavy-ions collisions

> We show that non-conventional statistical effects (due to the presence of long range forces, memory effects, correlations and fluctuations) can be very relevant in the interpretation of the experimental observables in relativistic heavy-ions collisions. Transverse mass spectrum, transverse momentum fluctuations and rapidity spectra are analysed in the framework of the non-extensive statistical mechanics.

## Key claim

Non-conventional statistical effects (due to long-range forces, memory effects, correlations, and fluctuations) can be very relevant for interpreting experimental observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but a microscopic justification of these effects is still lacking.

## Method

The authors analyze transverse mass spectra, transverse momentum fluctuations, and rapidity spectra within the framework of Tsallis non-extensive statistical mechanics. For the rapidity spectra, they employ a non-linear relativistic Fokker-Planck equation with anomalous diffusion, whose stationary solution is a Tsallis-like distribution, and integrate over transverse mass to obtain the time-dependent rapidity distribution.

## Key numbers

- Transverse mass spectrum of hadrons in S+S collisions (NA35 data) is reproduced with entropic parameter q = 1.038
- Transverse momentum fluctuations in NA49 analysis are reproduced with q = 1.038, the same value used for transverse momentum spectra
- Rapidity spectra for net proton production in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c (NA49 data) are reproduced with q = 1.25 and interaction time tau = 0.82
- The linear case (q = 1) requires tau = 1.2 but does not reproduce the broad experimental shape
- Net proton spectra are normalized to 164 protons with beam rapidity y_cm = 2.9 in the center-of-mass frame

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis non-extensive statistics (Tsallis, 1988) to heavy-ion collision observables, following earlier work by Alberico, Lavagno, and Quarati (2000, 2001). The non-linear Fokker-Planck approach for rapidity spectra contrasts with the linear drift model of Wolschin (1999). The results enable further investigation of non-perturbative QCD effects near hadronic deconfinement as a microscopic basis for the non-extensive statistical regime.
