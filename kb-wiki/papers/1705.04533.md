# Rapidity Distributions of Hadrons in Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> We study proton-lead collisions with a new model for the Fock states of the incoming proton. The number of collisions which the proton experiences selects the appropriate Fock state of the proton which generates a multiple of pp-like rapidity distributions. We take as input the pp maximum entropy distributions shifting the respective cm-rapidities and reducing the available energies. A comparison with existing data at 5 TeV is made and results for 8 TeV are presented. We also explore the high multiplicity data in this model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed multiplicity ratio between proton-nucleus and proton-proton collisions, approximately (⟨n⟩+1)/2 where ⟨n⟩ is the mean number of collisions, can be explained by a model where the number of collisions selects a specific Fock state of the incoming proton, with the caveat that the model slightly underestimates the asymmetry of the measured pseudorapidity distribution at 5 TeV.

## Method

The authors model the incoming proton as a superposition of Fock states containing valence-like constituents (carrying momentum fractions 1/m) and negligible sea-like constituents. Each Fock state is actualized by a specific number of collisions with target nucleons, with probabilities given by Glauber theory cross sections. The hadronization of each substate is described using a maximum entropy distribution previously developed for pp collisions, with the cm-rapidity shifted and the available energy reduced according to the momentum fraction of the substate. The total charged-particle pseudorapidity distribution is obtained by summing contributions from all Fock states weighted by their Glauber probabilities, averaging over the components within each state.

## Key numbers

* The inelastic pp cross section used at 5 TeV is σ_in = 70 mb.
* For p-Pb collisions at 5 TeV, the mean number of collisions is ⟨n⟩ = 7.25.
* The effective transverse temperature λ(s) at √s = 5 TeV is 0.274 GeV; the longitudinal softness w(s) at the same energy is 7.67.
* The maximum entropy distribution parameters are fitted for cm-energies 0.2 TeV < √s < 7.0 TeV, with λ(s) = (0.023 + 0.03 log(√s/s_0)) GeV and w(s) = -3.66 + 1.33 log(√s/s_0), where s_0 = 1 GeV².
* For high-multiplicity events, the model selects the highest component of each Fock state, giving a multiplicity ratio r_high = ⟨n⟩, and an impact parameter cut 0 < b < 1.76 fm corresponds to 0–5% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the maximum entropy model of Pirner (2011, 2012) from symmetric pp and AA collisions to asymmetric pA collisions. It contrasts with saturation approaches, Monte Carlo event generators, and perturbative QCD-based calculations compiled by Albacete (2017), instead using phenomenological pp rapidity distributions as input. The Fock state decomposition builds on the AGK cutting rules of Abramovsky, Gribov, and Kancheli (1973) and the Glauber model, and the valence qqbar states are interpreted as high-energy manifestations of the pion cloud. The model enables predictions for 8 TeV p-Pb collisions and provides a framework for understanding fluctuations in the proton wave function that may explain observed azimuthal asymmetries, as studied by Dusling (2017).
