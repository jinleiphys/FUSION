# Parton-hadron dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of partons and hadrons in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed within the novel Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for the partonic phase (DQPM) including a dynamical hadronization scheme. The PHSD approach is applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions from low SPS to LHC energies. The traces of partonic interactions are found in particular in the elliptic flow of hadrons and in their transverse mass spectra. We investigate also the equilibrium properties of strongly-interacting infinite parton-hadron matter characterized by transport coefficients such as shear and bulk viscosities and the electric conductivity in comparison to lattice QCD results.

## Key claim
The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which includes a dynamical quasiparticle model for the partonic phase and a dynamical hadronization scheme, can describe single-particle and collective observables from low SPS to LHC energies, with the caveat that even central collisions at top-SPS energy (√sNN = 17.3 GeV) show a large fraction of nonpartonic, hadronic or string-like matter (a "hadronic corona"), meaning neither purely hadronic nor purely partonic models alone can extract physical conclusions.

## Method
The authors applied the PHSD transport approach, which is based on Kadanoff-Baym equations with self-energies from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), to nucleus-nucleus collisions from √sNN ~ 5 to 200 GeV. The partonic dynamics uses spectral functions for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons with temperature-dependent masses and widths fitted to lattice QCD results, while hadronization proceeds via covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion or three-quark fusion obeying flavor current conservation, color neutrality, and energy-momentum conservation. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electric conductivity) were extracted from simulations of infinite matter in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions using the Kubo formalism and relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature Tc ≈ 160 MeV from the DQPM fit to lattice QCD results of Aoki et al. (2009)
- Critical energy density εc ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density η/s from PHSD shows a minimum of about 0.1 close to Tc
- The scalar mean field for quarks and antiquarks is in the order of a few GeV for scalar parton density ρs > 10 fm⁻³
- The electric conductivity over temperature σ₀/T rises approximately linearly with T up to T = 2.5 Tc, then approaches a constant above 5 Tc

## Context
This work extends the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) approach of Ehehalt and Cassing (1996) by incorporating explicit partonic degrees of freedom, addressing the long-standing problem that HSD and UrQMD calculations underestimated kaon transverse mass slopes and elliptic flow at higher energies. The PHSD results for transport coefficients show qualitative and partly quantitative agreement with lattice QCD calculations, and the extracted η/s minimum near Tc is consistent with viscous hydrodynamic calculations requiring η/s of 0.08–0.24 to reproduce RHIC elliptic flow data. The approach enables future studies of photon and dilepton emission rates from the QGP, which are controlled by the electric conductivity.
