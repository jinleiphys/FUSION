# Strong Isospin Breaking in the Decay Constants of Heavy-Light Mesons from Local-Duality QCD Sum Rules

> We explore, by a rather peculiar limit of QCD sum rules subject to a Borel transformation (realized by allowing the related Borel mass parameter to approach infinity), how the small difference of the masses of up and down quark translates into the leptonic decay constants of pseudoscalar or vector heavy-light mesons. For the charmed and bottom mesons, we find that the decay constants of their lowest-lying charged and neutral representatives should differ by an amount of the order of 1 MeV.

## Key claim
The small difference between up and down quark masses, approximately 2.5 MeV at a scale of 2 GeV, leads to a splitting of the leptonic decay constants of charged and neutral heavy-light mesons. The authors find that for charmed and bottom mesons, these decay constants should differ by an amount of the order of 1 MeV, using the local-duality limit of Borelized QCD sum rules.

## Method
The authors apply the local-duality limit (letting the Borel parameter τ → 0) of standard Borel-transformed QCD sum rules for pseudoscalar and vector heavy-light mesons. They choose the case N = 0, where all power corrections vanish in this limit, reducing the sum rule to a spectral integral over the perturbative density. The effective threshold function z_eff = √s_eff − m_Q − m_q is adjusted to reproduce lattice-QCD results for decay constants at physical quark masses, using three ansatzes (constant, linear, and linear + chiral) for its light-quark-mass dependence.

## Key numbers
- The up-down quark mass difference at 2 GeV is (m_d − m_u)(2 GeV) ≈ 2.5 MeV.
- For charmed pseudoscalar mesons: f_{D^±} − f_{D^0} = (0.96 ± 0.09) MeV.
- For charmed vector mesons: f_{D^{*±}} − f_{D^{*0}} = (1.18 ± 0.35) MeV.
- For bottom pseudoscalar mesons: f_{B^0} − f_{B^±} = (1.01 ± 0.10) MeV.
- For bottom vector mesons: f_{B^{*0}} − f_{B^{*±}} = (0.89 ± 0.30) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous local-duality QCD sum-rule studies (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2015-2018) and agrees nearly perfectly with conventional QCD sum-rule results for isospin breaking in heavy-light decay constants (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2016). The approach enables predictions for the isospin splitting of decay constants without relying on the Borel-parameter dependence of the effective threshold, and the results are consistent with lattice-QCD inputs for the decay constants at physical quark masses.
