# Bound-free pair production cross section in heavy-ion colliders from the equivalent photon approach

> Exact calculations of the electron-positron pair production by a single photon in the Coulomb field of a nucleus with simultaneous capture of the electron into the K-shell are discussed for different nuclear charges. Using the equivalent photon method of Weizsaecker and Williams, a simple expression for the bound-free production of electron-positron pairs by colliding very-high-energy fully stripped heavy ions is derived for nuclei of arbitrary charge.

# Bound-free pair production cross section in heavy-ion colliders from the equivalent photon approach

## Key claim
The equivalent photon method of Weizsäcker and Williams can produce a simple analytic expression for bound-free electron-positron pair production with K-shell capture in relativistic heavy-ion collisions that agrees with exact numerical calculations to better than 2% for all nuclear charges up to Z=92, but only when the single-photon cross section is corrected by a charge-dependent suppression factor f(Z_t) that becomes significant for heavy nuclei.

## Method
The author applies the equivalent photon method, treating the projectile ion as a source of virtual photons whose spectrum is integrated from a minimum impact parameter equal to the Compton wavelength to infinity. The single-photon bound-free pair production cross section with K-shell capture is computed using exact numerical solutions of the Dirac equation for the bound electron and Sommerfeld-Maue wave functions for the positron, then folded with the equivalent photon spectrum. The resulting cross section is expressed in the form σ_RHI = A log γ_p + B, with coefficients A and B given as polynomial fits in Z_t.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC Cu-Cu collisions at 100 GeV/nucleon (γ_c ≈ 107), the bound-free pair production cross section is 0.198 barn, in agreement with experimental measurements at RHIC.
- For LHC Pb-Pb collisions at γ_c = 2957, the K-shell capture cross section is 220 barn, compared to 225 barn from prior calculations by Meier et al.
- For Z_p = Z_t = 20 at γ_c = 125, the K-shell capture cross section is 1.57 × 10⁻² barn; at γ_c = 3750, it is 2.96 × 10⁻² barn.
- The suppression factor f(Z_t) for single-photon cross sections takes values: f(92) = 0.196, f(82) = 0.216, f(29) = 0.484, f(1) = 0.971.
- The analytic fit for f(Z_t) is given by f(Z_t) = (53/100 + 2αZ_t/9)^(2παZ_t), accurate to 2% for 0 ≤ Z_t ≤ 92.
- The overall cross section formula is σ_RHI = 1.58 × 10⁻¹¹ Z_t⁵ Z_p² f(Z_t) [c₁(Z_t) log(γ_p) - c₂(Z_t)] barn, with c₁ = 0.21746 and c₂ = 0.58706 at Z_t = 0.

## Context
This work extends the equivalent photon approach of Weizsäcker and Williams to bound-free pair production, providing a simple analytic alternative to the numerical calculations of Meier et al. and Baltz et al. The results are relevant for estimating beam losses at RHIC and the LHC, where electron capture by heavy ions can quench superconducting magnets. The paper also compares its results to earlier semi-relativistic approaches by Bertulani and Baur (which underestimate cross sections for heavy nuclei by a factor of about 2) and by Dolci (which overestimates them by factors exceeding 2), demonstrating the necessity of exact Dirac solutions for reliable predictions.
