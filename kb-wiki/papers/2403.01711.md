# Density-dependent quark mean-field model for nuclear matter and neutron stars

> We develop a density-dependent quark mean-field (DDQMF) model to study the properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars, where the coupling strength between $σ$ meson and nucleon is generated by the degree of freedom of quarks, while other meson coupling constants are regarded as density-dependent ones. Two values for the nucleon effective mass, $M^*_{N0}/M_N=0.556,~0.70$ at the saturation density are chosen based on the consideration of the core-collapse supernova simulation and finite nuclei when the meson-nucleon coupling constants are fixed. We find that the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter, the symmetry energy, the mass-radius relations, and the tidal deformabilities of neutron stars with larger nucleon effective mass are more sensitive to the skewness coefficient $J_0$. The EOSs with $M^*_{N0}/M_N=0.70$ are softer when the skewness coefficient $J_0=-800$ MeV. However, the maximum masses of the neutron star can be around $2.32M_\odot$ with $J_0=400$ MeV regardless of the value of the nucleon effective mass. By manipulating the coupling strength of the isovector meson to generate different slopes of symmetry energy, we construct the neutron star EOSs that can satisfy the different variables from the simultaneous mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620 by the NICER collaboration, the mass-radius relations of HESS J1731-347, and the radius constraints from the gravitational-wave signal GW170817 in the framework of DDQMF model. At the same time, most of these constructed EOSs can also satisfy the constraints of the tidal deformability from GW170817 event.

## Key claim

The paper develops a density-dependent quark mean-field (DDQMF) model where the coupling strength between the sigma meson and nucleon is derived from quark degrees of freedom, while other meson coupling constants are treated as density-dependent; the model can simultaneously satisfy observational constraints from NICER, GW170817, and HESS J1731-347, but only for specific choices of the nucleon effective mass and skewness coefficient, with the condition that the equation of state with larger effective mass (0.70) is more sensitive to the skewness coefficient.

## Method

The authors construct a DDQMF model by first solving the Dirac equation for constituent quarks (mass 350 MeV) confined by a harmonic oscillator potential, obtaining the in-medium nucleon mass including center-of-mass, pion, and gluon corrections. They then use this quark-level nucleon mass in a relativistic mean-field framework with density-dependent omega and rho meson-nucleon coupling constants, fitting the parameters to reproduce saturation properties of nuclear matter from the DDME-X model. The symmetry energy slope is controlled by adjusting the isovector meson coupling while fixing the symmetry energy at a sub-saturation density of 0.11 fm^-3, and neutron star properties are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Two values of nucleon effective mass at saturation density are chosen: M*_N0/M_N = 0.556 and 0.70
- Saturation density rho_B0 = 0.152 fm^-3, binding energy E/A = -16.1 MeV, incompressibility K_0 = 267 MeV
- Skewness coefficient J_0 values: -800 MeV and 400 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass approximately 2.32 M_sun with J_0 = 400 MeV regardless of effective mass value
- For M*_N0/M_N = 0.556 and J_0 = -800 MeV, maximum mass about 2.1 M_sun; for M*_N0/M_N = 0.70 and J_0 = -800 MeV, maximum mass decreases to about 1.6 M_sun
- Tidal deformability Lambda_1.4 for M*_N0/M_N = 0.556 ranges from 410-420 (J_0 = -800 MeV) to 602-612 (J_0 = 400 MeV)
- Tidal deformability Lambda_1.4 for M*_N0/M_N = 0.70 ranges from 90-100 (J_0 = -800 MeV) to 558-620 (J_0 = 400 MeV)
- HESS J1731-347 mass M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M_sun and radius R = 10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78} km
- PSR J0030+0451 mass 1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} M_sun with radius 12.71^{+1.24}_{-1.06} km
- PSR J0740+6620 mass 2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M_sun with radius 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km
- GW170817 radius constraint at 1.4 M_sun: R_1.4 = 11.9 +/- 1.4 km
- GW170817 tidal deformability constraint: Lambda_1.4 = 190^{+390}_{-120}

## Context

This work extends the quark mean-field (QMF) model of Toki (1998) and Shen (2000) by introducing density-dependent meson couplings, similar to the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) approach of Brockmann and Toki (1992) and Typel (1999), but with the sigma-nucleon coupling derived from quark-level dynamics rather than parameterized. The model enables simultaneous description of multiple neutron star observables including NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, the light compact object HESS J1731-347, and gravitational-wave constraints from GW170817, while also addressing the tension between PREX-II and CREX measurements of neutron skin thickness through adjustable symmetry energy slopes.
