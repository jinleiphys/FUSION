# Has J-PARC E07 observed a $Ξ^-_{1s}$ nuclear state?

> The Strangeness ${\cal S}=-2$ J-PARC E07 emulsion experiment published recently two new $Ξ^-$--$^{14}$N capture events, IRRAWADDY and IBUKI, identified by observing weak-decay sequences of pairs of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei and interpreted as $Ξ^{-}_{1s}$ and $Ξ^{-}_{1p}$ nuclear bound states, respectively, with binding energies $B_{Ξ^-}^{1s}$=6.27$\pm$0.27 MeV and $B_{Ξ^-}^{1p}$=1.27$\pm$0.21 MeV. $Ξ^-$ capture events in emulsion play a major role in determining the $Ξ$-nuclear and the $ΛΛ$ potential strengths. Here we question the assignment of a $Ξ^{-}_{1s}$ nuclear bound state to IRRAWADDY and offer an alternative assignment as a near-threshold $Ξ^0_{1p}$--$^{14}$C nuclear bound state, slightly admixed with a $Ξ^{-}_{1p}$--$^{14}$N nuclear bound state component corresponding to IBUKI. We also question using IBUKI's $B_{Ξ^-}^{1p}$ value as is to determine the $Ξ$-nuclear potential depth. Altogether, $Ξ^-$ capture events in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O should prove less ambiguous than in $^{14}$N.

## Key claim
The paper questions the J-PARC E07 assignment of the IRRAWADDY event as a $\Xi^{-}_{1s}$ nuclear bound state in $^{14}$N, and instead proposes it is a near-threshold $\Xi^{0}_{1p}$--$^{14}$C nuclear bound state, slightly admixed with a $\Xi^{-}_{1p}$--$^{14}$N component corresponding to the IBUKI event. The authors also argue that the IBUKI event alone is unfit to reliably constrain the $\Xi$-nuclear potential depth.

## Method
The authors use an optical model potential with density-dependent $\Xi N$ scattering amplitudes, fitted to $\Xi^-$--$^{12}$C capture events, to calculate $\Xi^0_{1p}$ binding in $^{14}$C. They evaluate the mixing between $\Xi^-_{1p}$--$^{14}$N and $\Xi^0_{1p}$--$^{14}$C states induced by the $\Xi N$ strong-interaction charge-exchange term $V_{\sigma\tau}$, using realistic shell-model wavefunctions for $^{14}$N and $^{14}$C ground states. The radiative $E1$ transition rates from the $\Xi^-_{3D}$ atomic state to both $1p$ nuclear states are compared.

## Key numbers
- IRRAWADDY binding energy: $B_{\Xi^-}=6.27\pm0.27$ MeV, originally assigned as $\Xi^{-}_{1s}$--$^{14}$N (J-PARC E07).
- IBUKI binding energy: $B_{\Xi^-}^{1p}=1.27\pm0.21$ MeV, assigned as $\Xi^{-}_{1p}$--$^{14}$N (J-PARC E07).
- $\Xi^0$--$^{14}$C threshold relative to $\Xi^-$--$^{14}$N: $-(6.18\pm0.21)$ MeV, calculated from $m_{\Xi^-}-m_{\Xi^0}=6.85\pm0.21$ MeV and nuclear mass difference $M(^{14}{\rm C})-M(^{14}{\rm N})=0.67$ MeV.
- Varying $b_1$ between 0.270 and 0.420 fm produces $\Xi^0_{1p}$ binding energies in $^{14}$C ranging from $-200$ to $178$ keV, with widths between 274 and 409 keV.
- The mixing matrix element between $\Xi^0_{1p}$--$^{14}$C and $\Xi^-_{1p}$--$^{14}$N is approximately $-0.453$ MeV, leading to an admixture amplitude of $-0.0906$ and a squared amplitude of $0.00821$.
- The $E1$ transition rate ratio favoring the lower $\Xi^0_{1p}$--$^{14}$C state over the $\Xi^-_{1p}$--$^{14}$N state is $0.00821\times171=1.40$, making the two rates roughly equal.
- Using IRRAWADDY as input gives a $\Xi$ nuclear potential depth at nuclear-matter density of $-V_{\Xi}(\rho_0)=13.8$ MeV, compared to $21.9\pm0.7$ MeV from KEK E176 $^{12}$C events.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous optical model analysis of $\Xi^-$--$^{12}$C capture events (Friedman and Gal, 2021) to the $^{14}$N system. It contradicts the J-PARC E07 interpretation of IRRAWADDY as a $\Xi^{-}_{1s}$ state (Yoshimoto et al., 2021) and questions the use of IBUKI's binding energy alone to determine the $\Xi$-nuclear potential depth. The alternative assignment as a $\Xi^0_{1p}$--$^{14}$C bound state is enabled by the near-coincidence of the $\Xi^0$--$^{14}$C threshold with IRRAWADDY's binding energy. The work suggests that $\Xi^-$ capture events in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, where $\Xi^-p\leftrightarrow\Xi^0n$ charge exchange is kinematically suppressed, should prove less ambiguous for determining $\Xi$-nuclear potentials.
