# Phenomenological mass model for exotic hadrons and predictions for masses of non-strange dibaryons as hexaquarks

> We investigate the mass spectra of exotic hadrons known as hexaquarks in the form of dibaryons. We use a phenomenological model based on an extended version of the Gürsey-Radicati mass formula for hadrons to include non-charmed baryons, charmed baryons, and non-strange dibaryons to be able to predict masses of potential dibaryon states. We perform six numerical fits of this model to input data for three different sets of masses of baryons and dibaryons. We find that the model can fit some of the data sets well, especially the sets including charmed baryons and non-strange dibaryons, and observe that the predicted mass of one of the dibaryons is close to the measured mass of the observed hexaquark candidate $d^*(2380)$ reported by the WASA-at-COSY experiment. The predicted mass of the deuteron is slightly larger than its measured mass. Finally, for the data sets including charmed baryon and non-strange dibaryon masses, we find that the predicted masses of potential dibaryon states are all in the range from 1900 MeV to 3700 MeV.

## Key claim
The extended Gürsey-Radicati mass formula, when fitted to data sets that include charmed baryons and non-strange dibaryons, can predict a mass for the hexaquark candidate d*(2380) that is close to the experimental value, but the model cannot reproduce the deuteron mass to better than 200 MeV precision. The model fits are performed with both experimental uncertainties and a uniform 1% error, with the latter yielding much lower chi-squared values.

## Method
The authors extended the Gürsey-Radicati mass formula by adding terms for the SU(3) Casimir operator eigenvalue and the number of charm quarks, then performed six numerical fits to three data sets: eight non-charmed baryons (Set I), those plus eight charmed baryons (Set II), and those plus the two experimentally known dibaryons (Set III). For each set, fits were done using both experimental uncertainties and a uniform 1% error, minimizing chi-squared with Mathematica and the basin-hopping method. The fitted parameters were then used to predict masses of six non-strange S-wave dibaryon states D_IJ.

## Key numbers
- The predicted mass of D_03(Delta Delta) from fit II' is 2324 MeV, compared to the experimental d*(2380) mass of 2380 +/- 10 MeV.
- The predicted mass of D_01(NN) from fit II' is 2100 MeV, compared to the experimental deuteron mass of 1875.612943 MeV.
- For fits including charmed baryons and dibaryons (Sets II and III), all predicted dibaryon masses lie in the range 1900 MeV to 3700 MeV.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the 1% error fits ranges from 0.6417 (fit I') to 2.180 (fit III'), while for the experimental-uncertainty fits it ranges from 2083 (fit I) to 26025 (fit III).
- The parameter B (hypercharge coefficient) is stable across all fits, ranging from -198.7 MeV to -189.0 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Huang et al. (2018), who applied the Gürsey-Radicati formula to non-charmed baryons and dibaryons, by additionally including charmed baryons and using only the two experimentally verified dibaryon masses (deuteron and d*(2380)). The results are compared to the earlier dynamical calculations of Dyson and Xuong (1964) and the chromomagnetic model studies of Leandri and Silvestre-Brac (1997). The findings suggest that while the simple mass formula can approximately reproduce the d*(2380) mass, it cannot simultaneously give the correct deuteron mass, and the predicted masses for unobserved dibaryon states (D_10, D_30, D_12, D_21) could guide future experimental searches at facilities such as WASA-at-COSY.
