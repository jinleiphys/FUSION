# Gauge invariance in quantum hadrodynamics

> This paper describes the derivation of a new representation of an $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ locally-invariant model of the strong interactions called quantum hadrodynamics 3 (QHD-III). QHD-III is a gauge-invariant theory based on the linear $σ$ model, the gauge bosons being the $ρ$ and the $a_1$. The new representation considerably simplifies the lagrangian. To derive the new representation, the gauge invariance of the model is exploited. The role of the gauge boson masses in gauge-invariant models of the strong interactions based on the $σ$ model is also discussed, and it is shown that these masses are necessary if the pion is to survive as a physical field.

# Gauge invariance in quantum hadrodynamics

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a gauge-invariant hadronic model based on the linear sigma model requires massive gauge bosons (the rho and a1) to retain the pion as a physical field; otherwise, the pion is "eaten" by the a1 and disappears. The new representation of QHD-III exploits gauge invariance to diagonalize the Lagrangian via a constant rescaling of the pion field by the ratio m_rho/m_a, rather than introducing momentum-dependent vertices.

## Method

The author starts from the gauged sigma-omega model with pions (QHD-II), makes the SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R symmetry local, and shows that spontaneous symmetry breaking in the sigma sector gives mass to the a1 but leaves the rho massless and causes the pion to become an auxiliary field (pi-prime) with a gauge-dependent mass. To restore the physical pion, a Higgs sector with two complex doublets is added to generate masses for both the rho and a1 from outside the sigma sector, preserving gauge invariance. The gauge invariance is then exploited to diagonalize the full QHD-III Lagrangian using a gauge-fixing function that cancels bilinear terms, leading to a constant rescaling of the pion field rather than the momentum-dependent change of variables used in the original QHD-III formulation.

## Key numbers

- The a1 mass is given by m_a^2 = m_rho^2 + g_rho^2 sigma_o^2, making the a1 naturally heavier than the rho.
- The pion field is rescaled by the ratio m_rho/m_a, where m_rho is the rho mass and m_a is the a1 mass.
- The Higgs sector vacuum expectation value satisfies u^2 = 4m_rho^2/g_rho^2.
- The Higgs mass parameter is m_H, with lambda_H = m_H^2 g_rho^2/m_rho^2.
- The pi-prime field acquires a gauge-dependent mass m_pi-prime^2 = xi m_a^2 in the gauged sigma model without Higgs sector.
- The physical pion mass in QHD-III is m_pi = (m_rho/m_a) m_pi-prime.

## Context

This work extends the QHD-III model developed by Walecka and collaborators, which introduced a gauge-invariant hadronic quantum field theory based on the gauged sigma-omega model with pions. The paper addresses a problem previously noted by Meissner: that the physical pion disappears in a gauged sigma model unless the gauge bosons are given mass. The new representation simplifies the original QHD-III Lagrangian, which previously required complicated momentum-dependent diagonalization procedures as used in the original QHD-III paper. This simplification makes the model more accessible for calculations such as pion-pion scattering to one loop in the chiral limit.
