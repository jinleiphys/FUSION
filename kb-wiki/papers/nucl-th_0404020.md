# Mass splittings of nuclear isotopes in chiral soliton approach

> The differences of the masses of nuclear isotopes with atomic numbers between \~10 and ~30 can be described within the chiral soliton approach in satisfactory agreement with data. Rescaling of the model is necessary for this purpose - decrease of the Skyrme constant by about 30%, providing the "nuclear variant" of the model. The asymmetric term in Weizsaecker-Bethe- Bacher mass formula for nuclei can be obtained as the isospin dependent quantum correction to the nucleus energy. Some predictions for the binding energies of neutron rich nuclides are made in this way, from, e.g. Be-16 and B-19 to Ne-31 and Na-32. Neutron rich nuclides with high values of isospin are unstable relative to strong interactions. The SK4 (Skyrme) variant of the model, as well as SK6 variant (6-th order term in chiral derivatives in the lagrangian as solitons stabilizer) are considered, and the rational map approximation is used to describe multiskyrmions.

## Key claim

The chiral soliton approach can describe mass differences of nuclear isotopes with atomic numbers between about 10 and 30 in satisfactory agreement with data, but only after rescaling the Skyrme constant downward by about 30% (from e=4.12 to e=3.0 for the SK4 variant) to produce a "nuclear variant" of the model; without this rescaling, agreement is only qualitative.

## Method

The authors use the SU(2) Skyrme model (SK4 variant) and a variant including a sixth-order chiral derivative term (SK6 variant) to describe multiskyrmions with baryon numbers B up to 32. The rational map approximation is employed to construct multiskyrmion configurations, and the mass splittings between isotopes with different isospin are obtained from the rotational energy of collective isospin rotations, using the isospin moment of inertia calculated for each baryon number. The Coulomb energy is subtracted from experimental binding energies using the finite-range liquid-drop model formula with nuclear-radius constant r0 = 1.16 fm and proton root-mean-square radius rp = 0.80 fm.

## Key numbers

* Skyrme constant rescaled from e=4.12 to e=3.0 for the SK4 "nuclear variant"; for SK6 variant the rescaled constant is e'=2.84
* For A=12, the theoretical mass difference between isosinglet and isotriplet states is 9.5 MeV (rescaled SK4), compared to experimental value of about 15.35 MeV
* For A=16, the theoretical mass difference is 7.9 MeV (rescaled SK4), compared to experimental value of about 14.69 MeV
* For A=8, the theoretical mass difference is 16.8 MeV (rescaled SK4), compared to experimental value of about 17 MeV
* The isospin moment of inertia for B=12 in the rescaled SK4 variant is 114 GeV^{-1}; for B=16 it is 148 GeV^{-1}; for B=32 it is 275 GeV^{-1}
* The symmetry energy coefficient b_sym is approximately 50 MeV, obtained from the isospin quantum correction E_sym = I(I+1)/(2*Theta_I)
* Predicted binding energy for Be-16 is about 60 MeV, making it unstable against decay into Be-14 and two neutrons
* Predicted binding energy for B-18 is 87.1 MeV, in good agreement with the extrapolation result of 89 MeV from Audi and Wapstra (1995)

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton approach previously applied to light nuclei (deuteron, He-3, H-3, He-4) by Braaten and Carson (1988) and others to heavier nuclides up to A=32. The asymmetric term in the Weizsacker-Bethe-Bacher mass formula is derived from isospin-dependent quantum corrections rather than from Fermi-motion kinetic energies as in the conventional liquid-drop model. The results enable predictions of binding energies for neutron-rich nuclides such as Be-16, B-19, Ne-31, and Na-32, and suggest that neutron-rich nuclides with high isospin values are unstable relative to strong interactions.
