# Strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon scattering in covariant chiral effective field theory

> Motivated by the successes of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory in one-baryon systems and in heavy-light systems, we study relevance of relativistic effects in hyperon-nucleon interactions with strangeness $S=-1$. In this exploratory work, we follow the covariant framework developed by Epelbaum and Gegelia to calculate the $YN$ scattering amplitude at leading order. By fitting the five low-energy constants to the experimental data, we find that the cutoff dependence is mitigated, compared with the heavy-baryon approach. Nevertheless, the description of the experimental data remains quantitatively similar at leading order.

# Strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon scattering in covariant chiral effective field theory

## Key claim

The covariant (EG) approach to hyperon-nucleon scattering at leading order mitigates cutoff dependence compared to the heavy-baryon (HB) approach, but the description of experimental data remains quantitatively similar at leading order when an optimal cutoff is chosen.

## Method

The authors apply the covariant framework developed by Epelbaum and Gegelia (EG) to calculate $\Lambda N - \Sigma N$ scattering amplitudes at leading order, using the Kadyshevsky equation to iterate the potentials instead of the nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation used in the heavy-baryon approach. Five low-energy constants (LECs) for $S$-wave contact terms are fitted to 36 experimental data points including total cross sections of $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ reactions with laboratory momentum below 300 MeV/$c$, plus the $\Sigma^- p$ inelastic capture ratio at rest. The potentials include nonderivative four-baryon contact terms and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges, regularized with a Gaussian form factor.

## Key numbers

- The optimal $\chi^2$ occurs at $\Lambda_F \simeq 600$ MeV for both approaches, with values of 28.23 (EG) and 28.52 (HB) at that cutoff.
- At $\Lambda_F = 600$ MeV, the fitted LECs in the EG approach are: $C^{\Lambda \Lambda}_{1S0} = -0.04795(151) \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$, $C^{\Sigma \Sigma}_{1S0} = -0.07546(81) \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$, $C^{\Lambda \Lambda}_{3S1} = -0.01727(124) \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$, $C^{\Sigma \Sigma}_{3S1} = 0.36367(30310) \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$, and $C^{\Lambda \Sigma}_{3S1} = 0.01271(471) \times 10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- The cutoff range studied is $\Lambda_F = 500 - 850$ MeV for the main $\chi^2$ comparison, extended to $\Lambda_F = 0.5 - 6$ GeV for hard-cutoff analysis.
- For $\Lambda p$ scattering at $P_\mathrm{lab} = 900$ MeV/$c$, the center-of-mass momentum of the proton is 384.8 MeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the covariant chiral effective field theory framework developed by Epelbaum and Gegelia (2012, 2015) for nucleon-nucleon scattering to the hyperon-nucleon sector with strangeness $S=-1$. It builds upon prior heavy-baryon ChEFT studies of $YN$ interactions by Polinder (2006) and Haidenbauer (2013), as well as meson-exchange models such as the Nijmegen model (Nagels, 1976; Rijken, 1998) and the Juelich model (Holzenkamp, 1989; Haidenbauer, 2005). The results enable future work on including higher-order corrections and investigating whether promoting $^3P_0$ and $^3P_1$ counterterms to leading order can remove residual cutoff dependence, as well as studying $SU(3)$ aspects of short-range interactions in light of renormalization.
