# Strangeness Form Factors of the Proton in the Chiral Quark Model

> The chiral quark model describes the strangeness components of the light quarks as fluctuations into strange mesons and quarks. The single strange pseudoscalar and vector meson loop fluctuations of the constituent $u$- and $d$-quarks give rise to only very small strangeness form factors for the proton. This result is in line with recent experimental results, given their wide uncertainty range.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model predicts very small strangeness form factors for the proton, with the calculated values falling within the wide uncertainty ranges of experimental results from HAPPEX and SAMPLE, provided that the weak axial form factor of the nucleon is taken to be small and positive rather than large and negative.

## Method

The authors calculate strangeness form factors of the proton by considering single kaon (K) and vector kaon (K*) meson loop fluctuations of the constituent u- and d-quarks in the chiral quark model, using pseudovector and Proca-type couplings respectively. Loop integrals are regularized with a dipole cutoff at the chiral restoration scale (Λχ = 4πfπ = 1.2 GeV), and the quark-level form factors are scaled to the proton using an equipartition assumption (mq = mp/3). Contributions from K* → Kγ transition loops are also included using empirical radiative decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The kaon-quark pseudoscalar coupling constant squared is g_Kqs^2/4π = 0.75, using constituent quark masses m_q = 340 MeV and m_s = 460 MeV.
- The K* loop contribution to the proton strangeness Dirac form factor F_1^s reaches only -0.086 around Q^2 = 1 (GeV/c)^2.
- The calculated strangeness magnetic form factor at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 is G_M^s(0.1) = -0.06.
- The calculated combination G_E^s(0.48) + 0.39 G_M^s(0.48) = -0.08 at Q^2 = 0.48 (GeV/c)^2.
- The strangeness radius is approximately 0.02 fm^2.
- The chiral quark model value for the strangeness magnetic moment G_M^s(0) is approximately -0.06, which falls within the uncertainty range of a lattice QCD result (-0.16 ± 0.18).

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model calculation of the strangeness magnetic moment (Hannelius, 1999) to the full Q^2 dependence of the strangeness charge and magnetic form factors. The results are consistent with the small strangeness form factors observed by the HAPPEX and SAMPLE experiments, and the calculated momentum dependence of G_E^s agrees fairly well with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Hemmert, 1999). The approach provides an alternative to hadronic loop calculations (Jaffe, 1989; Musolf, 1991; Barz, 1998) where large amplitudes require strong cancellations, and enables comparison with ongoing and future parity-violating electron scattering experiments.
