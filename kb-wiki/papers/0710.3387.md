# Energy Dependence of the Delta Resonance: Chiral Dynamics in Action

> There is an important connection between the low energy theorems of QCD and the energy dependence of the Delta resonance in pi-N scattering, as well as the closely related gamma^{*} N -> pi N reaction. The resonance shape is due not only to the strong pi-N interaction in the p wave but the small interaction in the s wave; the latter is due to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD (i.e. the Nambu-Goldstone nature of the pion). A brief overview of experimental tests of chiral perturbation theory and chiral based models is presented

# Energy Dependence of the Delta Resonance: Chiral Dynamics in Action

## Key claim

The isolated, textbook shape of the Δ resonance in π-N scattering and γ*N → πN reactions arises not only from the strong p-wave π-N interaction that creates the resonance, but also from the weakness of the s-wave amplitude near threshold, which is a direct consequence of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD (the Nambu-Goldstone nature of the pion). The paper presents this connection as an overview of experimental tests rather than a new measurement, emphasizing that the resonance appears "textbook" only because of this chiral suppression of the s-wave.

## Method

The authors compare total cross sections for π⁺p and π⁻p scattering and for γp → π⁰p and γp → π⁺n reactions from threshold through the Δ resonance region, using data from the SAID compilation. They also examine the δ₃₃ phase shift in πN scattering and the M₁₊(I=3/2) multipole in photo-pion production, and compare experimental results to calculations from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) pion cloud model, and the MAID phenomenological model.

## Key numbers

- Δ resonance center-of-mass energy W = 1232 MeV, width Γ = 118 MeV (from Particle Data Book)
- The s-wave π-hadron scattering length a(π,h) ≈ 1/Λₓ ≈ 0.1 fm, where Λₓ = 4πFπ ≈ 1 GeV is the chiral symmetry breaking scale
- D₀ = e·gπN/(8πM) = 24 × 10⁻³ (1/mπ) for the low-energy photo-pion production amplitude
- Threshold for γp → π⁰p is 144.7 MeV; the π⁺n threshold is 151.4 MeV
- Electroproduction data at Q² = 0.05 GeV²/c² (Mainz) show a discrepancy with O(p³) ChPT calculations
- Bates data for σLT at Q² = 0.127 GeV²/c², W = 1232 MeV show sensitivity to the Coulomb quadrupole C2 amplitude

## Context

This paper extends the connection between low-energy theorems of QCD (derived from current algebra and chiral perturbation theory) and the observed energy dependence of the Δ resonance, building on work by Weinberg (pion scattering lengths), the SAID group, and the DMT model of Yang and collaborators. It reviews experimental tests of ChPT calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (BKM) for photo-pion production, and contrasts these with the DMT and MAID models. The paper enables future experiments at the HIγS facility at Duke, which will use polarized photons and transversely polarized targets to measure πN scattering in charge channels not accessible to pion beams, including a test of isospin conservation and a determination of the charge-exchange scattering length.
