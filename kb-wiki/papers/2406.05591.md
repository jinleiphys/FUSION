# High precision measurements of alpha_s at the future EIC

> We present a projection study for the first moments of the inclusive spin structure function for the proton and neutron from simulated doubly-polarized e+p and e+3He collision data expected from the Electron-Ion collider. For detection and extraction of the neutron spin asymmetries from e+3He collisions, we used the double-tagging method which significantly reduces the uncertainty over the traditional inclusive method. Using the Bjorken sum rule, the projected results allow us to determine that the QCD coupling at the Z-pole alpha_s can be measured with a relative precision of 1.3%. This underscores the significance of the EIC for achieving precision determinations of alpha_s.

## Key claim

The projected EIC data on the Bjorken sum rule, using the double-tagging method for neutron extraction, would allow a determination of the QCD coupling at the Z-pole with a relative precision of 1.3%, but this result depends on fitting over a specific Q² range (2.4 to 75 GeV²) and includes separate contributions from fit uncertainty and pQCD series truncation uncertainty.

## Method

The authors simulated doubly-polarized e+p and e+³He collision events using the DJANGOH event generator for three energy settings each, with an integrated luminosity of 10 fb⁻¹ per setting and 70% beam polarization. Events were passed through a GEANT4-based full detector simulation of the ECCE detector design, then unfolded to Born-level kinematics using a 4-iteration Bayesian algorithm. For neutron extraction from ³He, the double-tagging method detected both spectator protons from the breakup, requiring |p_s1 + p_s2| < 0.1 GeV to minimize nuclear effects. The projected spin asymmetries A₁ were multiplied by unpolarized structure functions F₁ from the JAM22 global fit to obtain g₁, which was integrated over x to form Γ₁, and the Q²-evolution of the Bjorken sum Γ₁^(p-n) was fitted using the pQCD series at N⁴LO to extract the QCD scale Λ_s and thereby α_s(M_Z²).

## Key numbers

- Projected relative precision on α_s at the Z-pole: Δα_s/α_s = 1.3%, composed of ±8.3×10⁻³ from the fit and ±6.4×10⁻³ from pQCD series truncation
- The Bjorken sum Γ₁^(p-n) values range from 0.1817 ± 0.0016 (stat) ± 0.0006 (pol) at Q² = 2.37 GeV² to 0.1632 ± 0.0019 (stat) ± 0.0019 (pol) at Q² = 74.99 GeV²
- The unmeasured low-x contribution to Γ₁^(p-n) ranges from -15.74×10⁻³ at Q² = 2.37 GeV² to +32.26×10⁻³ at Q² = 74.99 GeV²
- Beam polarizations assumed: (70 ± 1)% for both electron and hadron beams
- Integrated luminosity: 10 fb⁻¹ per energy setting, corresponding to 27.3 months (5×41 GeV), 2.7 months (10×100 GeV), and 7.8 months (18×275 GeV) of operation

## Context

This work extends the approach of Deur (2014) and Altarelli (1996) for extracting α_s from the Bjorken sum rule, using the projected capabilities of the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) as described by Accardi (2012) and Abdul Khalek (2022). The double-tagging method for neutron spin asymmetries from ³He, developed by Friscic (2021), significantly reduces nuclear correction uncertainties compared to traditional inclusive measurements. The projected 1.3% precision would improve upon the current world average uncertainty of 0.85% from the Particle Data Group (2020) and approach the sub-percent level needed for precision Standard Model tests, though the result depends on the availability of higher-order pQCD corrections and improved low-x Regge parameter estimates from future JLab 12 GeV experiments (Dalton 2020).
