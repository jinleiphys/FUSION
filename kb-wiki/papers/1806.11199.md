# Pion Mass Modification in presence of external magnetic field

> In this work, the self energies of $π^0$ and $π^{\pm}$ up to one loop order have been calculated in the limit of weak external magnetic field. The effective masses are explicitly dependent on the magnetic field which are modified significantly for the pseudoscalar coupling due to weak field approximation of the external field. On the other hand, for the pseudovector coupling, there is a modest increment in the effective masses of the pions. These theoretical developments are relevant for the study of the phenomenological aspect of mesons in the context of neutron stars as well as heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The effective masses of both neutral and charged pions are modified in the presence of a weak external magnetic field, but the direction of the change depends on the coupling scheme: the mass decreases for pseudoscalar (PS) coupling, while it shows a modest increase for pseudovector (PV) coupling. These results are calculated under the condition that the magnetic field is weak, specifically satisfying \((eB \ll m_\pi^2)\), and are presented in the absence of any medium.

## Method

The authors calculated the pion self-energies for \(\pi^0\) and \(\pi^\pm\) up to one-loop order using Schwinger's proper-time method to incorporate the magnetic field into the fermion propagators. The propagators were expanded to order \((eB)^2\) under the weak-field approximation, and the self-energies were evaluated for both pseudoscalar and pseudovector pion-nucleon interaction Lagrangians. The effective pion masses were then defined by the pole position of the propagator at zero three-momentum, \(m^{*2}_\pi = m^2_\pi + \text{Re}\,\Pi(m^{*2}_\pi, \mathbf{q}=0, B)\).

## Key numbers

- The weak-field condition is defined as \((eB \ll m_\pi^2)\), with the calculation performed up to order \((eB)^2\).
- For context, magnetic fields in heavy ion collisions are cited as \(\sim 0.02\) GeV\(^2\) at RHIC and \(\sim 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) at LHC; field strengths of \(\sim 10^{15}\) Gauss are found in some neutron stars.
- The effective pion mass decreases for the pseudoscalar (PS) interaction, while there is a slight increase for the pseudovector (PV) interaction (no specific numerical mass shifts are provided in the text).

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic field effects on hadron properties, relevant to the phenomenology of mesons in neutron stars and heavy ion collisions, as previously explored by Tuchin (2013), Ayala (2015), Colucci (2013), and Ghosh (2016). The calculation uses the weak-field expansion of the fermion propagator following the approach of Schwinger (1951) and Adhya (2017). The results are presented in vacuum, and the authors state that the modification of dispersion relations in the presence of both medium and magnetic field will be reported in a subsequent publication.
