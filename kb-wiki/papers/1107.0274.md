# Quasi-particle continuum and resonances in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> The quasi-particle energy spectrum of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations contains discrete bound states, resonances, and non-resonant continuum states. We study the structure of the unbound quasi-particle spectrum of weakly bound nuclei within several methods that do not rely on imposing scattering or outgoing boundary conditions. Various approximations are examined to estimate resonance widths. It is shown that the stabilization method works well for all HFB resonances except for very narrow ones. The Thomas-Fermi approximation to the non-resonant continuum has been shown to be very effective, especially for coordinate-space HFB calculations in large boxes that involve huge amounts of discretized quasi-particle continuum states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the high-energy non-resonant quasi-particle continuum in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory can be accurately approximated using a Thomas-Fermi (TF) local-density approach, and that the stabilization method works well for all HFB resonances except for very narrow ones, enabling a hybrid strategy that avoids explicit computation of all continuum states.

## Method

The authors applied the Thomas-Fermi approximation to the high-energy quasi-particle continuum in Skyrme HFB calculations, deriving expressions for continuum contributions to particle density, kinetic density, pairing density, and pairing potential. They tested this approximation against full box-discretization solutions obtained with the 2D-HFB solver HFB-AX for the nucleus ⁷⁰Zn using the SkM* energy density functional and mixed pairing interaction. For deep-hole resonances, they examined three methods: a smoothing and fitting method that extracts resonance parameters from discretized occupation number distributions, the box stabilization method that uses box-size dependence of quasi-particle eigenvalues to compute phase shifts, and a perturbative Fermi Golden rule expression for resonance widths.

## Key numbers

- The continuum contributions from unbound states between E_c = 40 MeV and E_m = 60 MeV in ⁷⁰Zn showed that continuum contributions to pairing densities are larger than continuum particle densities by two orders of magnitude.
- In the hybrid HFB method for ⁷⁰Zn, the total binding energy is perfectly stable for neutron cutoff E_c + λ_n > 30 MeV, and at a low cutoff of E_c + λ_n = 15 MeV the error is only about 100 keV.
- For the neutron 1s₁/₂ deep-hole state in ⁷⁰Zn, the quasi-particle energy from full HFB diagonalization is E_i = 38.071 MeV, while the corresponding HF single-particle energy is -ε_i = 38.055 MeV.
- The smoothing parameter Γ around 0.8 MeV was found to be appropriate for the smoothing and fitting method, with the condition Γ ≫ Γ_r.

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Fermi approximation previously applied to 1D Fermi gases (Reidl, 1997; Liu, 2007) to nuclear Skyrme HFB theory, and adapts the stabilization method from atomic physics (Hazi, 1978; Mandelshtam, 1994) and single-particle nuclear resonances (Giai, 2004) to quasi-particle resonances. The hybrid HFB strategy enables efficient coordinate-space calculations in large boxes, which is crucial for describing weakly bound nuclei, nuclear fission, and fusion where enormous continuum spaces would otherwise be intractable. The methods enable future extension of the stabilization approach to deformed nuclei and integration with self-consistent QRPA approaches (Rodin, 2006; Hagino, 2006; Terasaki, 2006; Mizuyama, 2007).
