# Temperature Effects on Core g-modes of Neutron Stars

> Neutron stars provide a unique physical laboratory to study the properties of matter at high density. We study a diagnostic of the composition of high-density matter, namely, g-mode oscillations, which are driven by buoyancy forces. These oscillations can be excited by tidal forces and couple to gravitational waves. We extend prior results for the g-mode spectrum of cold neutron star matter to temperatures that are expected to be achieved in neutron star mergers using a parameterization for finite-temperature effects recently proposed by Raithel, Özel and Psaltis. We find that the g-modes of canonical mass neutron stars ($\approx$1.4$M_{\odot}$) are suppressed at high temperature, and core $g$-modes are supported only in the most massive ($\geq $2$M_{\odot}$) of hot neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors find that composition-driven g-modes in canonical-mass neutron stars (≈1.4 M⊙) are suppressed at high temperatures (≥30 MeV), and that core g-modes are supported only in the most massive (≥2 M⊙) hot neutron stars, with the suppression arising from thermal contributions that make the equilibrium sound speed exceed the adiabatic sound speed.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic fluid perturbation equations within the Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) as an eigenvalue problem for the fundamental (n=1, l=2) g-mode frequency. They incorporate finite-temperature effects using the analytic parameterization of Raithel, Özel and Psaltis (2019, 2021), which separates cold and thermal contributions to the equation of state, and apply this to two nuclear models: the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) equation of state and the Zhao-Lattimer (ZL) equation of state, treating the crust as a homogeneous fluid with no g-modes.

## Key numbers

- For the APR equation of state at T=0, the maximum neutron star mass is 2.12 M⊙; at T=30 MeV, the maximum mass changes only slightly to 2.15 M⊙.
- For the APR equation of state, a 1.4 M⊙ star at low temperature has a radius of about 12.45 km; at 30 MeV, a star of similar mass has a radius of 13.68 km.
- The dimensionless tidal polarizability for the cold APR equation of state is Λ₁.₄^APR = 492; for the ZL equation of state it is Λ₁.₄^ZL = 357, both within the constraint 70 ≤ Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 from GW170817.
- At temperatures of 50 MeV or more, no stable g-modes are found for either equation of state.
- Including neutrinos as a thermal component (with f_s = 43/8 above trapping temperatures of 10 MeV) produces a systematic reduction of approximately 15–20% in the g-mode frequency.

## Context

This work extends prior results for the g-mode spectrum of cold neutron stars, such as those by Reisenegger and Goldreich (1992) on composition g-modes and by Zhao and Lattimer (2021) on zero-temperature g-modes, to temperatures expected in neutron star mergers (∼10–50 MeV). The study uses the finite-temperature parameterization of Raithel, Özel and Psaltis (2019, 2021), which was developed for merger simulations. The results suggest that g-modes from hot merger remnants may be detectable only from the most massive stars, enabling future gravitational wave detectors such as the Cosmic Explorer or Einstein Telescope to constrain the composition of high-density matter.
