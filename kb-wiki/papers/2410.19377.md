# First Experimental Test of the Ratio Method

> The ratio is a new reaction observable suggested to extract accurately structure information on halo nuclei. It corresponds to the ratio of differential cross sections for scattering and breakup, which is predicted to remove the uncertainty related to the reaction dynamics. We present here the first experimental test of the method for the 11Be + 12C collision at ELab = 20A MeV performed at Texas A&M University. Differential cross sections for scattering and inclusive one-neutron breakup have been measured with the new detector array BlueSTEAl. The ratio of cross sections is very smooth and independent of the projectile-target interaction, which demonstrates the validity of the ratio method. We extend our analysis to existing 11Be + 208Pb data, confirming that the method works well on any target.

## Key claim
The ratio of differential cross sections for elastic scattering and inclusive one-neutron breakup of halo nuclei is experimentally shown to be smooth and independent of the projectile-target interaction, validating the ratio method as a tool to extract structure information while removing the uncertainty from reaction dynamics. The method works for both light (carbon) and heavy (lead) targets, though the test is limited to the specific case of 11Be.

## Method
Differential cross sections for elastic scattering and inclusive one-neutron breakup were measured for the 11Be + 12C collision at ELab = 20A MeV using the BlueSTEAl detector array at the Texas A&M University K500 superconducting cyclotron. A 11Be beam of about 60% purity at 22.8A MeV was produced via fragmentation of a 30A MeV 13C primary beam on a 9Be target, then directed onto a natural carbon target. The measured cross sections were compared to dynamical reaction calculations using two different choices of optical potentials, and the ratio of the two cross sections was computed.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: ELab = 20A MeV (with actual beam energy at target of 22.8A MeV)
- 11Be beam purity: about 60%
- Individual differential cross sections for scattering and breakup vary by about three orders of magnitude over the measured angular range
- Reanalysis of existing 11Be + 208Pb data at 19.1A MeV (Duan et al., 2022) confirms the method works on a heavy target

## Context
This work provides the first experimental test of the ratio method, which was previously proposed theoretically by Capel et al. (2011, 2013) based on the Recoil Excitation and Breakup model (REB) of Johnson et al. (1997). The method extends earlier observations by Capel et al. (2010) that elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei exhibit similar diffraction patterns. The successful experimental validation enables future studies of heavier halo nuclei such as 19C using the high beam intensity at FRIB.
