# Non-perturbative diffusion of Heavy Quark moving in a hot and magnetised Quark Gluon Plasma

> Heavy Quarks (HQs) serve as excellent probes to understand various characteristics of deconfined hot QCD medium, comprising light quarks and gluons, created in the Heavy Ion Collisions (HICs). Strong magnetic fields in non-central HICs may significantly affect HQ dynamics in this medium. Exploring the impact of the magnetic field on the perturbative and non-perturbative transport coefficients of HQ is an intriguing endeavor. This necessitates the development of a comprehensive theoretical framework accommodating the non-perturbative Quantum Chromo Dynamics(npQCD) description alongside perturbative QCD(pQCD). The present work provides such a formulation in which Quarkonium potential in the hot and magnetic QCD medium is implemented as the effective gluon propagator to calculate the rate of elastic scattering between HQ and the light partons inside a medium of hot Quark Gluon Plasma(QGP) in the presence of a strong but uniform magnetic field. Diffusion coefficients of a charm quark have been computed for a short-range Yukawa potential ( pQCD) as well as a long-range confining/non-perturbative potential (npQCD) for two cases in which the velocity of the charm quark is parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field respectively. Non-perturbative contribution is seen to dominate over the perturbative one in the regime of low temperatures and low to intermediate momenta of charm quark. As the momentum of charm quark as well as the temperature of the medium increase pQCD starts to gain on the npQCD contribution, ultimately prevailing at higher temperatures and charm momenta.

## Key claim

The paper provides a theoretical framework that incorporates both perturbative (pQCD) and non-perturbative (npQCD) contributions to heavy quark diffusion in a hot and magnetized Quark Gluon Plasma, using the quarkonium potential as an effective gluon propagator. The non-perturbative contribution dominates over the perturbative one specifically in the regime of low temperatures and low to intermediate momenta of the charm quark, with pQCD prevailing only at higher temperatures and charm momenta.

## Method

The authors map the Cornell-type heavy quark potential (Yukawa plus string terms) into momentum space for a hot QCD medium with a strong magnetic field satisfying the scale hierarchy M² ≫ eB ≫ T², using the potential derived in PRD97:2018. They calculate elastic scattering rates of a charm quark with light quarks (in the Lowest Landau Levels) and thermal gluons via two processes: Coulombic (Yukawa/pQCD) and Compton (string/npQCD), using the imaginary time formalism and spectral representations of propagators. Diffusion coefficients are computed for two geometric configurations: charm quark velocity parallel to the magnetic field (yielding two independent components, κ∥LL and κ∥TT) and perpendicular to the magnetic field (yielding three independent components, κ⊥LT, κ⊥TT, and κ⊥TL).

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass M = 1.2 GeV used throughout the calculations.
* Magnetic field strength eB = 0.3 GeV² ≈ 15 m²π used in the numerical evaluation.
* Zero-temperature string tension σ₀ = 0.450 GeV² used in the temperature-dependent string tension σ(T) = σ₀ √(1 − πT²/(3σ₀)).
* QCD running coupling αs(T) = 6π/[(33−2Nf) log(2πT/Λ)] with Λ = 0.176 GeV.
* At T = 200 MeV, the npQCD contribution to the longitudinal diffusion coefficient (κ∥LL) exceeds the pQCD contribution at low charm momentum; at T = 300 MeV, pQCD becomes comparable to or exceeds npQCD.
* For the perpendicular velocity case (v ⟂ B), the hierarchy among diffusion components is κ⊥LT > κ⊥TL > κ⊥TT at T = 0.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the T-matrix approach to heavy quark transport coefficients (PRL100:2008, PRC86:2012) and the Gribov-Zwanziger action method (gribov:najmul) by incorporating both magnetic field effects and non-perturbative dynamics simultaneously. It builds on the complex heavy quark potential in a hot and magnetized medium calculated in PRD97:2018, and uses a parameterized Cornell-type potential as an effective gluon propagator following the approach of arxiv2112:2021. The results enable future calculations of experimental observables such as RAA, v₂, and the jet quenching parameter q̂ for charm quarks at low to intermediate pT, where pQCD alone has been insufficient to explain data from RHIC and LHC.
