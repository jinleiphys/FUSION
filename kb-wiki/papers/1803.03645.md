# The groomed and ungroomed jet mass distribution for inclusive jet production at the LHC

> We study jet mass distributions measured in the single inclusive jet production in proton-proton collisions $pp\to \text{jet}+X$ at the LHC. We consider both standard ungroomed jets as well as soft drop groomed jets. Within the Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), we establish QCD factorization theorems for both cases and we study their relation. The developed framework allows for the joint resummation of several classes of logarithmic corrections to all orders in the strong coupling constant. For the ungroomed case, we resum logarithms in the jet radius parameter and in the small jet mass. For the groomed case, we resum in addition the logarithms in the soft threshold parameter $z_{\text{cut}}$ which is introduced by the soft drop grooming algorithm. In this way, we are able to reliably determine the absolute normalization of the groomed jet mass distribution in proton-proton collisions. All logarithmic corrections are resummed to the next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy. We present numerical results and compare with the available data from the LHC. For both the groomed and ungroomed jet mass distributions we find very good agreement after including non-perturbative corrections.

## Key claim

The paper establishes QCD factorization theorems for both groomed and ungroomed jet mass distributions in inclusive jet production, enabling joint resummation of logarithms in the jet radius parameter, small jet mass, and (for groomed jets) the soft threshold parameter \(z_{\text{cut}}\) to next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy; the absolute normalization of the groomed jet mass distribution in proton-proton collisions is determined for the first time up to non-global logarithms.

## Method

The authors use Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to derive a two-step factorization: first, hard collinear factorization separates the semi-inclusive jet mass functions (siJMFs) from the hard scattering functions, allowing resummation of single logarithms in the jet radius \(R\) via DGLAP evolution; second, soft collinear factorization refactorizes the siJMFs into hard matching functions, collinear functions, and soft functions, enabling resummation of double logarithms in the jet mass \(\tau = m_J^2/p_T^2\) and, for groomed jets, additional logarithms in \(z_{\text{cut}}\). The soft drop grooming algorithm is incorporated by modifying the soft functions while leaving the collinear functions unchanged to leading power in \(z_{\text{cut}}\). All resummations are performed at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy.

## Key numbers

- Jet radius parameter \(R = 0.8\) used for numerical comparisons with LHC data.
- Soft threshold parameter \(z_{\text{cut}} = 0.1\) used for groomed jet mass distributions.
- The collinear functions have characteristic scale \(\mu_C \sim p_T \tau^{1/2} = m_J\).
- The soft functions have characteristic scale \(\mu_S \sim p_T \tau / R = m_J^2 / (p_T R)\).
- The jet scale is \(\mu_J \sim p_T R\).
- The hard scale is \(\mu_H \sim p_T\).
- The groomed jet mass distribution is studied in the region \(\tau_{\text{gr}} / R^2 \ll z_{\text{cut}} \ll 1\), with \(\tau_{\text{gr}}\) as low as \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-4})\).
- The ungroomed jet mass distribution is studied in the region \(\tau \ll R^2\).
- The factorization for the ungroomed case is derived for \(R \ll 1\) but found to be a good approximation even for \(R \sim 0.7\) and above.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies on inclusive jet mass distributions by Li et al. (2011, 2012) and Liu et al. (2014), which focused on threshold di-jet limits or process-independent jet functions, and by Dasgupta et al. (2012) and Chien et al. (2012) on jet mass in \(\gamma/Z\)+jet and di-jet processes. It also builds on groomed jet mass calculations by Frye et al. (2016) and Marzani et al. (2017), achieving for the first time the resummation of logarithms in \(z_{\text{cut}}\) for inclusive jets in proton-proton collisions. The results enable direct comparison with LHC data from CMS and ATLAS, and provide a foundation for studying jet mass distributions in heavy-ion collisions where grooming is used to probe medium modification of fragmenting partons.
