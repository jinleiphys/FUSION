# Weak structure functions in $ν_l-N$ and $ν_l-A$ scattering with nonperturbative and higher order perturbative QCD effects

> We study the effect of various perturbative and nonperturbative QCD corrections on the free nucleon structure functions ($F_{iN}^{WI}(x,Q^2); ~i=1-3$) and their implications in the determination of nuclear structure functions. The evaluation of the nucleon structure functions has been performed by using the MMHT 2014 PDFs parameterization, and the TMC and HT effects are incorporated following the works of Schienbein et al. and Dasgupta et al., respectively. These nucleon structure functions are taken as input in the determination of nuclear structure functions. The numerical calculations for the $ν_l/\barν_l-A$ DIS process have been performed by incorporating the nuclear medium effects like Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions, shadowing and antishadowing in several nuclear targets such as carbon, polystyrene scintillator, iron and lead which are being used in MINERvA, and in argon nucleus which is relevant for the ArgoNeuT and DUNE experiments. The differential scattering cross sections $\frac{d^2σ_A^{WI}}{dx dy}$ and $(\frac{dσ_A^{WI}}{dx}/\frac{dσ_{CH}^{WI}}{dx})$ have also been studied in the kinematic region of MINERvA experiment. The theoretical results are compared with the recent experimental data of MINERvA and the earlier data of NuTeV, CCFR, CDHSW and CHORUS collaborations. Moreover, a comparative analysis of the present results for the ratio $(\frac{dσ_A^{WI}}{dx}/\frac{dσ_{CH}^{WI}}{dx})$, and the results from the MC generator GENIE and other phenomenological models of Bodek and Yang, and Cloet et al., has been performed in the context of MINERvA experiment. The predictions have also been made for $\barν_l-A$ cross section relevant for MINERvA experiment.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate weak nucleon and nuclear structure functions by incorporating both perturbative QCD corrections (up to NNLO) and nonperturbative effects (target mass corrections and higher twist contributions), finding that these corrections significantly affect the cross sections in the low-to-moderate $Q^2$ region (1-5 GeV$^2$) relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments, though the abstract does not specify that the agreement with MINERvA data is achieved only after including all these corrections simultaneously.

## Method

The nucleon structure functions $F_{iN}^{WI}(x,Q^2)$ ($i=1-3$) were computed using MMHT 2014 parton distribution functions at NNLO, with target mass corrections following Schienbein et al. and higher twist effects via the renormalon approach of Dasgupta et al. These free nucleon structure functions were then used as input to compute nuclear structure functions for carbon, polystyrene scintillator, iron, lead, and argon by incorporating nuclear medium effects: Fermi motion, binding energy, and nucleon correlations through a relativistic spectral function; mesonic contributions; and shadowing/antishadowing following Kulagin and Petti. The differential cross sections $\frac{d^2\sigma_A^{WI}}{dx dy}$ and the ratio $\frac{d\sigma_A^{WI}}{dx}/\frac{d\sigma_{CH}^{WI}}{dx}$ were calculated in the kinematic region of the MINERvA experiment.

## Key numbers

- The average neutrino energy in the MINERvA experiment is approximately 6 GeV.
- A cut of $Q^2 \ge 1$ GeV$^2$ and center-of-mass energy $W \ge 2$ GeV was applied for the DIS analysis of neutrino-induced processes.
- There is a large variation of approximately 20% when comparing the three phenomenological models (GENIE, Bodek-Yang, Cloet et al.) used by MINERvA.
- The uncertainty from nuclear medium effects in the low $Q^2$ region (1-5 GeV$^2$) contributes approximately 25% to the systematics in neutrino oscillation analysis.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of electromagnetic structure functions (Zaidi et al., 2019) to the weak sector, and builds upon the nuclear medium effect studies of Kulagin and Petti and Haider et al. The results are compared with experimental data from MINERvA, NuTeV, CCFR, CDHSW, and CHORUS collaborations, and with phenomenological models from GENIE, Bodek and Yang, and Cloet et al. The predictions for antineutrino-nucleus cross sections are relevant for the MINERvA experiment, while the argon results are relevant for ArgoNeuT and DUNE experiments, where more than 50% of event rates are expected from the intermediate region of DIS and resonance production.
