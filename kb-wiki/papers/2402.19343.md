# Infinite Order Hydrodynamics: An Analytical Example

> We construct a kinetic model for matter-radiation interactions whose hydrodynamic gradient expansion can be computed analytically up to infinite order in derivatives, in the fully nonlinear regime, and for arbitrary flows. The frequency dependence of the opacity of matter is chosen to mimic the relaxation time of a self-interacting scalar field. In this way, the transient sector simulates that of a realistic quantum field theory. The gradient series is found to diverge for most flows, in agreement with previous findings. We identify, for the model at hand, the mechanism at the origin of the divergence, and we provide a successful regularization scheme. Additionally, we propose a universal qualitative framework for predicting the breakdown of the gradient expansion of an arbitrary microscopic system undergoing a given flow. This framework correctly recovers all previously known instances of gradient expansion divergence. As a new prediction, we show that the gradient expansion diverges when the energy-dependent mean free path is unbounded above.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a kinetic model of matter-radiation interactions where the hydrodynamic gradient expansion can be computed analytically to infinite order, but the series is found to diverge for most flows; the divergence arises because the Taylor expansion of the fluid fields is interchanged with a spacetime integral that is not dominated-convergent, and the breakdown scale of the full gradient expansion coincides with that of Navier-Stokes (Knudsen number ~0.1), meaning higher-order derivative corrections do not expand the regime of applicability beyond first order.

## Method

The author models a radiation-hydrodynamic system where a material medium with zero mean free path (ideal fluid) interacts with a dilute radiation gas via absorption and emission, neglecting scattering. The Boltzmann equation for the radiation distribution function is of relaxation type with a momentum-dependent relaxation time tau_p = -u_mu p^mu / g, chosen to mimic the transient behavior of a self-interacting scalar field theory. The exact non-local constitutive relation for the total stress-energy tensor is obtained analytically as an integral over the past lightcone, and the gradient expansion is derived by Taylor-expanding the equilibrium distribution function and performing the optical-depth integral term by term.

## Key numbers

* The gradient series coefficients T^{mu nu}_n in the Knudsen expansion are proportional to (n+3)!, making the series factorially divergent.
* For a linearized sinusoidal flow with temperature T(t) proportional to 1 - a sin(t/L) and Kn = T/(gL), the exact viscous energy density Pi^{00} at t=0 is given by an integral over dimensionless energy z: Pi^{00}/(a T^4) = integral_0^infty dz/(2 pi^2) * (Kn z^5 e^{-z})/(1 + Kn^2 z^2).
* The gradient series for this flow, truncated at order 70, breaks down at Kn ~ 0.1, which is the same scale where Navier-Stokes ceases to hold.
* The regularized series with a cutoff Lambda/T < Kn^{-1} restores agreement with the exact result, with an error that scales as (Lambda/T)^3 e^{-Lambda/T} for Lambda >> T.

## Context

This work extends the study of gradient expansion divergence in relativistic hydrodynamics, previously observed in holographic models and kinetic theory by Heller (2021, 2022) and Denicol and Noronha (2016). The model provides an explicit analytical example where the divergence mechanism can be identified: the energy-dependent mean free path is unbounded above, causing the Taylor series interchange with the spacetime integral to fail. The paper also explains why Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics has a divergent gradient expansion in Bjorken flow (Romatschke, 2018) and why Borel summation works in that context (Heller, 2015). The framework predicts that all kinetic models of QCD plasmas with tau(E) proportional to E^q (q>0) have a jet-induced divergence of the gradient series.
