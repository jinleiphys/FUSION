# The neutrino signal at HALO: learning about the primary supernova neutrino fluxes and neutrino properties

> Core-collapse supernova neutrinos undergo a variety of phenomena when they travel from the high neutrino density region and large matter densities to the Earth. We perform analytical calculations of the supernova neutrino fluxes including collective effects due to the neutrino-neutrino interactions, the Mikheev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) effect due to the neutrino interactions with the background matter and decoherence of the wave packets as they propagate in space. We predict the numbers of one- and two-neutron charged and neutral-current electron-neutrino scattering on lead events. We show that, due to the energy thresholds, the ratios of one- to two-neutron events are sensitive to the pinching parameters of neutrino fluxes at the neutrinosphere, almost independently of the presently unknown neutrino properties. Besides, such events have an interesting sensitivity to the spectral split features that depend upon the presence/absence of energy equipartition among neutrino flavors. Our calculations show that a lead-based observatory like the Helium And Lead Observatory (HALO) has the potential to pin down important characteristics of the neutrino fluxes at the neutrinosphere, and provide us with information on the neutrino transport in the supernova core.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the ratio of one-neutron to two-neutron events in a lead-based detector like HALO is sensitive to the pinching parameters of the primary supernova neutrino fluxes at the neutrinosphere, and to the presence or absence of energy equipartition among neutrino flavors, but this sensitivity is almost independent of the presently unknown neutrino properties (mass hierarchy and the third mixing angle θ13).

## Method

The authors performed analytical calculations of supernova neutrino flavor evolution from the neutrinosphere to Earth, including collective effects from neutrino-neutrino interactions (parametrized as sharp spectral splits), the Mikheev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) effect in the outer stellar layers, and decoherence of neutrino wave packets. They then computed the expected numbers of one-neutron and two-neutron events from charged-current and neutral-current electron-neutrino scattering on lead for the HALO detector, using both Fermi-Dirac and power-law parametrizations of the primary neutrino fluxes with varying pinching parameters and average energies.

## Key numbers

* For the primary neutrino fluxes, the average energies were fixed at ⟨E⁰_νe⟩ = 10 MeV, ⟨E⁰_ν̄e⟩ = 13 MeV, and ⟨E⁰_νx⟩ was varied among 13, 15, 18, 21, and 25 MeV.
* The pinching parameter for non-electron-type neutrinos was varied over α_νx ∈ [2,7] for the power-law distribution and η_νx ∈ [0,5] for the Fermi-Dirac distribution.
* The low and high spectral split energies used in the collective effect parametrization were Eˢ_l = 8 MeV and Eˢ_h = 23 MeV.
* The total neutrino luminosity was fixed at L^TOT_ν = 10⁵² erg s⁻¹ for both the equal luminosity case (L_νl = 1/6 × 10⁵² erg s⁻¹ per flavor) and the non-equal case (L_νx = 2 L_νe, with L_νe = L_ν̄e = 1 × 10⁵¹ erg s⁻¹).
* The one-neutron and two-neutron detection efficiencies for HALO phase-I are about 50% and 25%, respectively, with a time resolution of about 30 ms.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on neutrino detection using lead targets, such as Fuller (1998) which showed the utility of one- and two-neutron events for extracting supernova neutrino temperatures, and Engel (2002) which demonstrated sensitivity to the third mixing angle θ13. The present paper includes for the first time collective neutrino-neutrino interaction effects in the predictions for HALO, and shows that the event ratios can probe the primary neutrino flux pinching and the equipartition of energy among flavors, enabling future detectors to constrain supernova core physics and neutrino transport models.
