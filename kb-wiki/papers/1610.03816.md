# Spectral and thermodynamic properties of the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model

> We study spectral and thermodynamic properties of the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model, a variant of the $k$-body embedded random ensembles studied for several decades in the context of nuclear physics and quantum chaos. We show analytically that the fourth and sixth order energy cumulants vanish in the limit of large number of particles $N \to \infty$ which is consistent with a Gaussian spectral density. However, for finite $N$, the tail of the average spectral density is well approximated by a semi-circle law. The specific heat coefficient, determined numerically from the low temperature behavior of the partition function, is consistent with the value obtained by previous analytical calculations. For energy scales of the order of the mean level spacing we show that level statistics are well described by random matrix theory. Due to the underlying Clifford algebra of the model, the universality class of the spectral correlations depends on $N$. For larger energy separations we identify an energy scale that grows with $N$, reminiscent of the Thouless energy in mesoscopic physics, where deviations from random matrix theory are observed. Our results are a further confirmation that the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model is quantum chaotic for all time scales. According to recent claims in the literature, this is an expected feature in field theories with a gravity-dual.

## Key claim

The paper shows analytically that the fourth and sixth order energy cumulants vanish in the large-\(N\) limit, consistent with a Gaussian spectral density, but for finite \(N\) the tail of the average spectral density is well approximated by a semi-circle law rather than a Gaussian.

## Method

The authors compute the moments \(M_{2p} = \langle \Tr H^{2p} \rangle\) of the SYK Hamiltonian with \(N\) Majorana fermions and random four-body interactions, using Wick contractions and properties of Euclidean Dirac \(\gamma\) matrices in \(N\) dimensions. They evaluate the fourth and sixth normalized cumulants analytically as functions of \(N\) and compare them to exact diagonalization results for up to \(N=36\) Majorana fermions. Thermodynamic properties are extracted by fitting the low-temperature partition function derived from the tail of the spectral density, and spectral correlations are studied via the level spacing distribution and number variance.

## Key numbers

* Fourth normalized cumulant \(\kappa_4(N) = -32(N-4)(N^2-11N+36)/[N(N-1)(N-2)(N-3)]\), with large-\(N\) asymptotics \(-32/N\).
* Sixth normalized cumulant \(\kappa_6(N) = 512 (N-4)(11N^5-304N^4+3535N^3-21302N^2+65856N-82656)/[(N-3)^2(N-2)^2(N-1)^2 N^2]\), with large-\(N\) asymptotics \(512\times 11/N^2\).
* Ground state energy \(E_0 = -0.055 - 0.029 N\) from numerical fitting.
* Zero temperature entropy \(S_0 = 0.21 N\), in rough agreement with the analytical result \(\sim 0.23 N\) from Maldacena and Stanford (2016).
* Specific heat coefficient \(c/N = 0.43 \pm 0.10\), consistent with the analytical estimate \(c/N = 0.396\) from Maldacena and Stanford (2016).
* Exponent \(q = 1.53 \pm 0.20\) controlling the one-loop quantum correction \(\beta^{-q}\) in the partition function, consistent with the analytical prediction \(q = 3/2\) from Maldacena and Stanford (2016).

## Context

This work extends the study of \(k\)-body embedded random ensembles, originally developed in nuclear physics by Bohigas (1971) and French (1970), to the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model, which has recently attracted attention in holographic duality. The results confirm that the SYK model is quantum chaotic for all time scales, with level statistics matching random matrix theory predictions (GUE, GSE, or GOE depending on \(N\) modulo 8) and with a Thouless-like energy scale that grows with \(N\). The findings enable further comparison between the SYK model and its conjectured gravity dual, and support the claim that disorder may not be strictly necessary for a gravity-dual interpretation, as suggested by Witten (2016).
