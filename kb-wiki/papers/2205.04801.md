# Deconfinement phase transition and quark condensate in compact stars

> We investigate systematically the possible deconfinement phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter in compact stars. The properties of nuclear matter are fixed by expanding its binding energy to the order of $ρ^3$, while those of quark matter are predicted by an equivparticle model. The Maxwell construction is then applied for the quark-hadron mixed phase. By confronting compact star structures with pulsar observations, we obtain several EOSs that are compatible with the latest observations while supporting quark cores inside the most massive stars. It is found that the quark core is rather small and does not emerge for compact stars with $M\lesssim 2M_\odot$. The in-medium quark condensate of the stellar matter in those stars are then extracted within the framework of an equivparticle model, which decreases nonlinearly with density. At larger densities with pure quark matter, the quark condensate is still large and does not necessary decrease with density, indicating significant nonperturbative contributions within the density regions covered by compact stars.

# Deconfinement phase transition and quark condensate in compact stars

## Key claim

The paper claims that for hybrid stars consistent with pulsar observations, the quark core is rather small and does not emerge for compact stars with masses below approximately 2 solar masses, and that the in-medium quark condensate remains large even at the highest densities reached inside these stars, indicating significant nonperturbative contributions. The key hedge is that these conclusions depend on the specific parameter choices for both nuclear matter (Taylor expansion to order ρ³) and quark matter (equivparticle model with linear confinement and perturbative interactions), and on the adoption of the Maxwell construction for the mixed phase.

## Method

The authors perform a Taylor expansion of the nuclear matter binding energy to order ρ³, fixing parameters such as incompressibility K, skewness J, symmetry energy slope L, and curvature K_sym within ranges constrained by nuclear experiments. For quark matter, they use an equivparticle model with density-dependent quark masses including a confinement term D/ρ^{1/3} and a perturbative term C ρ^{1/3}, varying D and C over wide ranges. They generate 1,810,620 equations of state by combining nuclear and quark matter parameters, apply the Maxwell construction for the quark-hadron mixed phase, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for hybrid star structures, and select only those EOSs that satisfy constraints from pulsar observations (tidal deformability, radii, and maximum mass). Finally, they extract the in-medium quark condensate from the equivparticle model framework.

## Key numbers

- The onset density for deconfinement phase transition ranges from ρ_t ≈ 4.3 ρ₀ to 5.1 ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the nuclear saturation density.
- The relative energy density jump at the phase transition ranges from ΔE/E_t ≈ 1.4 to 2.3.
- The density of quark matter at the center of hybrid stars ranges from ρ^Q ≈ 5.8 ρ₀ to 8.7 ρ₀.
- The maximum sound velocity v_max ranges from 0.83 c to 0.99 c, occurring at ρ ≈ 3.8 ρ₀ to 4.4 ρ₀.
- The constrained nuclear matter parameters include L = 40 to 70 MeV, K ≈ 260 MeV, J ≈ -200 MeV, K_sym ≈ -400 to -200 MeV, and J_sym ≈ 400 to 800 MeV.
- The constrained quark matter parameters include √D = 155 to 265 MeV and C = -0.9 to 0.1.
- The vacuum quark condensate reference density n* = 2.141 fm⁻³ is obtained from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation with m_π ≈ 140 MeV, f_π ≈ 93.2 MeV, and m₀ = 3.45 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zhang (2018) for nuclear matter binding energy expansion and the equivparticle model of Peng (2000, 2002) and Xia (2014, 2018) for quark matter, applying them to hybrid star structure calculations. The results are consistent with the Bayesian analysis of Pfaff (2022) using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for the quark phase, and with studies by Minamikawa (2021) and Jin (2022) that assume a smooth crossover rather than a first-order phase transition. The finding that the quark condensate remains large at high densities suggests that chiral symmetry is only partially restored in hybrid stars, which enables further investigation of nonperturbative QCD effects in the density regime accessible in compact stars.
