# Quasiparticles in Neon using the Faddeev Random Phase Approximation

> The spectral function of the closed-shell Neon atom is computed by expanding the electron self-energy through a set of Faddeev equations. This method describes the coupling of single-particle degrees of freedom with correlated two-electron, two-hole, and electron-hole pairs. The excitation spectra are obtained using the Random Phase Approximation, rather than the Tamm-Dancoff framework employed in the third-order algebraic diagrammatic contruction [ADC(3)] method. The difference between these two approaches is studied, as well as the interplay between ladder and ring diagrams in the self-energy. Satisfactory results are obtained for the ionization energies as well as the energy of the ground state with the Faddeev-RPA scheme that is also appropriate for the high-density electron gas.

## Key claim

The Faddeev-RPA (F-RPA) formalism, which couples random phase approximation phonons in both particle-hole and particle-particle/hole-hole channels through a Faddeev expansion, provides a more satisfactory description of quasiparticle properties in finite systems than the Tamm-Dancoff-based ADC(3) method, while remaining applicable to extended systems like the high-density electron gas.

## Method

The electron self-energy for the closed-shell Neon atom was expanded by solving a set of Faddeev equations that couple single-particle degrees of freedom with correlated two-electron, two-hole, and electron-hole pairs. The intermediate polarization and two-particle propagators were computed using the generalized Random Phase Approximation (GRPA) rather than the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA), and the resulting Faddeev equations were solved in the 2p1h/2h1h spaces. Calculations were performed using both a quantumchemical Gaussian basis set (aug-cc-pVTZ) and a Hartree-Fock plus continuum basis set, with partial self-consistency achieved by iterating the static self-energy with the correlated one-body density matrix.

## Key numbers

- F-RPAc (partially self-consistent) 2p ionization energy: -0.801 H with HF+continuum basis, compared to experimental -0.793 H (strength 0.94 vs experimental 0.92)
- F-RPAc 2s ionization energy: -1.795 H with HF+continuum basis, compared to experimental -1.782 H (strength 0.91 vs experimental 0.85)
- F-RPAc 1s ionization energy: -32.104 H with HF+continuum basis, compared to experimental -31.70 H (strength 0.81)
- F-RPAc total electronic binding energy: -128.888 H with HF+continuum basis, compared to experimental -128.928 H
- Replacement of TDA with RPA phonons shifted ionization energies toward experimental values by approximately 5-10 mH for valence states
- Partial self-consistency provided about 5 mH more binding for 2p and 2s, 15 mH for 1s, and 60 mH for total binding energy
- Second-order self-energy gave 2p ionization energy of -0.747 H, 46 mH above experiment
- F-RPA (ring only) gave 2p ionization energy of -0.730 H with strength 0.80
- F-RPA (ladder only) gave 2p ionization energy of -0.781 H with strength 0.96

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-RPA formalism previously applied to nuclear structure problems (Barbieri 2001, Barbieri 2002, Dickhoff 2004, Barbieri 2006) to electronic systems for the first time. It improves upon the ADC(3) method of Schirmer (1983) and the extended 2p1h TDA of Walter and Schirmer (1981), which use TDA phonons and are equivalent to the present F-TDA approach. The F-RPA method also addresses deficiencies in the G0W0 and generalized G0W0 approaches (Verdozzi 2006), which neglect Pauli correlations with the third particle and double-count the second-order self-energy. The formalism enables systematic improvement of quasiparticle descriptions in both finite and extended systems, and can inform the development of quasiparticle density functional theory as proposed by Van Neck (2006).
