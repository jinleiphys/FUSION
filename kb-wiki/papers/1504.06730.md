# The dual quark condensate in local and nonlocal NJL models: an order parameter for deconfinement?

> We study the beahviour of the dual quark condensate $Σ_1$ in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its nonlocal variant. In quantum chromodynamics $Σ_1$ can be realted to the breaking of the center symmetry and is therefore an (approximate) order parameter of confinement. The deconfinement transition is then signaled by a strong rise of $Σ_1$ as a function of temperature. However, a similar behaviour is also seen in the NJL model, which is known to have no confinement. Indeed, it was shown that in this model the rise of $Σ_1$ is triggered by the chiral phase transition. In order to shed more light on this issue, we calculate $Σ_1$ for several variants of the NJL model, some of which have been suggested to be confining. Switching between "confining" and "non-confining" models and parametrizations we find no qualitative difference in the behaviour of $Σ_1$, namely, it always rises in the region of the chiral phase transition. We conclude that without having established a relation to the center symmetry in a given model, $Σ_1$ should not blindly be regarded as an order parameter of confinement.

## Key claim

The dual quark condensate Σ₁ rises in the region of the chiral phase transition in all NJL-type models studied, regardless of whether the model is confining or not; therefore, without an established relation to center symmetry in a given model, Σ₁ should not be regarded as an order parameter for deconfinement.

## Method

The authors calculated the dual quark condensate Σ₁ and the chiral condensate as functions of temperature in three variants of the NJL model: a local NJL model regularized with proper-time cutoffs (including an infrared cutoff to remove propagator poles), and a nonlocal NJL model with a Gaussian regulator in two parameter sets, one where the quark propagator has complex poles (interpreted as confining) and one where it has real poles (non-confining). Twisted boundary conditions were implemented by shifting the fermionic Matsubara frequencies, and Σ₁ was obtained as the n=1 Fourier component of the generalized condensate with respect to the twist angle φ.

## Key numbers

- In the proper-time regularized local NJL model (chiral limit): critical temperature for the second-order chiral phase transition T_c = 216.2 MeV; vacuum constituent quark mass M = 358 MeV; vacuum chiral condensate per flavor ⟨ūu⟩^{1/3} = −243 MeV.
- In the nonlocal NJL model with parameter set A (confining in vacuum): deconfinement temperature (where the first propagator pole becomes real) T_d = 110 MeV; chiral transition temperature T_c = 126.5 MeV.
- In the nonlocal NJL model with parameter set B (always deconfined): no deconfinement transition occurs; the rise of Σ₁ still coincides with the chiral transition.
- For parameter set A: critical gap parameter σ̄_crit = 326 MeV; vacuum gap parameter σ̄_0 = 404 MeV; regulator scale Λ = 760 MeV; coupling G = 3.6×10⁻⁵ MeV⁻².
- For parameter set B: σ̄_crit = 392 MeV; σ̄_0 = 325 MeV; Λ = 914 MeV; G = 2.1×10⁻⁵ MeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends earlier studies showing that Σ₁ rises at the chiral transition in the standard NJL model (TKM, 2010; Benic, 2011), despite that model having no confinement. It tests whether adding "confining" features, either via proper-time regularization with an infrared cutoff (Ebert, 1996; Hellstern, 1997) or via nonlocal interactions producing complex propagator poles (Bowler, 1994; Scoccola, 2001), changes the behavior of Σ₁. The authors find no qualitative difference, concluding that Σ₁ is not a reliable deconfinement order parameter in models lacking center symmetry. The result does not invalidate Σ₁ as a deconfinement order parameter in QCD, where a connection to center symmetry exists (EBF, 2008), but suggests that the coincidence of chiral and deconfinement crossovers in lattice QCD may be explained by the chiral transition driving the rise of Σ₁.
