# Axiomatic nonextensive statistics at NICA energies

> We discuss the possibility of implementing axiomatic nonextensive statistics, where it is conjectured that the phase-space volume determines the (non)extensive entropy, on the particle production at NICA energies. Both Boltzmann-Gibbs and Tsallis statistics are very special cases of this generic (non)extensivity. We conclude that the lattice thermodynamics is {\it ab initio} extensive and additive and thus the nonextensive approaches including Tsallis statistics categorically are not matching with them, while the particle production, for instance the particle ratios at various center-of-mass energies, is likely a nonextensive process but certainly not of Tsallis type. The resulting freezeout parameters, the temperature and the chemical potentials, are approximately compatible with the ones deduced from Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that lattice QCD thermodynamics is inherently extensive and additive, and therefore cannot be reproduced by nonextensive approaches such as Tsallis statistics; however, particle production at NICA energies is likely a nonextensive process, but one that is not of the Tsallis type, and can instead be described by a generic axiomatic nonextensive statistics where the phase-space volume determines the (non)extensive entropy.

## Method

The author constructs an axiomatic nonextensive partition function based on a generalized entropy classified by two asymptotic exponents \(c\) and \(d\), using the incomplete gamma function and Lambert-W function to define the distribution function \(\varepsilon_{c,d,r}(x)\). This partition function is applied to the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to compute thermodynamic quantities (pressure and energy density) at vanishing chemical potential and compare them to lattice QCD simulations, and to fit particle ratios at center-of-mass energies of 200 GeV and 7.7 GeV. The resulting freezeout parameters (temperature and baryon chemical potential) are extracted from the fits.

## Key numbers

- At 200 GeV, the fitted exponents are \(c = 0.975\) and \(d = 0.965\), with a fit quality of \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.105\); the resulting freezeout temperature is \(T_{ch} = 148.05\) MeV and baryon chemical potential \(\mu_b = 23.94\) MeV.
- At 7.7 GeV (compatible with NICA energies), the fitted exponents are \(c = 0.9912\) and \(d = 0.945\), with a fit quality of \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 7.785\); the resulting freezeout temperature is \(T_{ch} = 145.32\) MeV and baryon chemical potential \(\mu_b = 384.3\) MeV.
- The nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies at NICA cover the range from 4 to 11 GeV at a luminosity of about \(10^{27}\) cm\(^{-2}\cdot\) s\(^{-1}\) for Au\(^{79+}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis (Tawfik 2015) showing that blind implementation of Tsallis algebra in the HRG model fails to reproduce lattice QCD and yields very low freezeout temperatures. It also builds on the classification of generalized entropies by Thurner (2010) and the concept of superstatistics proposed by Beck (2009). The paper enables future analyses of particle yields, ratios, fluctuations, and higher-order moments at NICA using the proposed generic nonextensive approach, with the expectation that NICA precise measurements will help determine the energy dependence of the exponents \(c\) and \(d\).
