# Pairing properties and specific heat of the inner crust of a neutron star

> We investigate the pairing properties at finite temperature of the Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust of a neutron star obtained with the recent Brussels-Montreal Skyrme functional BSk21. In particular we analyze the phenomena of persistence and reentrance of pairing correlations and their impact on the specific heat in the low-density region of the inner crust.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pairing correlations in low-density Wigner-Seitz cells of a neutron star's inner crust exhibit richer thermal behavior than standard BCS theory predicts, including persistence and reentrance of superfluidity, and that this behavior depends strongly on the specific nuclear cluster composition (Zirconium vs. Tin isotopes) and the coupling between bound and continuum states.

## Method

The author solved the Finite Temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-HFB) equations in spherical Wigner-Seitz cells using the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme functional BSk21, with Dirichlet-Neumann mixed boundary conditions. The chemical composition of the inner crust was taken from prior ETFSI calculations by Pearson et al. (2014) and Ducoin et al. (2012), using Zirconium isotopes. The neutron pairing field, average pairing gap, and specific heat were computed as functions of temperature for selected cells, including comparisons with Tin isotopes and homogeneous neutron gas.

## Key numbers

- The BSk21 functional reproduces all experimentally known atomic masses (approximately 2000) with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.58 MeV.
- For the WS cell $^{204}$Sn, the neutron pairing gap drops to zero at T = 60 KeV, then reenters at T = 0.24 MeV, and is completely suppressed above T = 1.04 MeV.
- For $^{158}$Zr, the neutron pairing gap at T=0 is 0.28 MeV, increases to a maximum of 0.96 MeV at T = 0.30 MeV, and disappears beyond T = 0.55 MeV.
- The critical temperature for $^{130}$Zr is T = 0.40 MeV; for $^{158}$Zr it is T = 0.55 MeV, an increase of approximately 30% from adding 28 neutrons forming a diluted gas.
- For $^{176}$Sn the critical temperature is T = 1.0 MeV; for $^{204}$Sn the difference is only approximately 4%.
- The first critical temperature for $^{158}$Zr and $^{204}$Sn is T = 60 KeV, coinciding with the critical temperature of the uniform neutron gas with density $\rho_{gas} = 5 \times 10^{-5}$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The two-neutron drip-line nuclei for BSk21 are $^{130}$Zr and $^{176}$Sn.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature studies of Fortin et al. (2010) and the author's prior work (Pastore 2012, 2014) on pairing correlations in Wigner-Seitz cells, and builds on the analysis by Margueron and Khan (2012) regarding the role of bound-continuum coupling in suppressing and persisting pairing correlations. The results show that at low temperatures the specific heat is dominated by the external neutron gas and is roughly independent of cluster composition, but at higher temperatures the cluster's role becomes important; this motivates future work treating lattice phonons, vibrational states, and pairing correlations on equal footing for a realistic description of the low-density inner crust.
