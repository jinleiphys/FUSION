# Overview of Antikaon-Nuclear Quasi-Bound States

> Experimental evidence for antikaon-nuclear quasi-bound states is briefly reviewed. Theoretical and phenomenological arguments for and against the existence of such states are considered, based on constructing antikaon-nuclear optical potentials from various sources. Results of RMF calculations that provide a lower limit of Gamma(antikaon) = 50 +/- 10 MeV on the width of antikaon-nuclear quasi-bound states are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that antikaon-nuclear quasi-bound states likely exist with binding energies in the range of 100–200 MeV, but that their widths have a lower limit of approximately 50 ± 10 MeV, making them broader than some experimental claims suggest. The abstract's mention of a "lower limit" glosses over the condition that this width estimate applies specifically for binding energies exceeding 100 MeV, where the dominant decay channels switch from mesonic to nonmesonic modes.

## Method

The author reviews experimental evidence from stopped-kaon and in-flight reactions on various nuclear targets, then compares theoretical approaches for constructing antikaon-nucleus optical potentials. These include chirally motivated models with self-consistent in-medium coupled-channel calculations, global fits to kaonic-atom data using density-dependent potentials, and relativistic mean-field (RMF) dynamical calculations that couple the antikaon to scalar and vector meson fields and solve the Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations self-consistently, including a complex absorptive potential with phase-space suppression factors for decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Re V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ -(150–200) MeV from global fits to K- atom data (density-dependent potentials)
- Re V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ -(40–60) MeV from self-consistent chirally motivated models
- Re V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ -110 MeV from non-self-consistent in-medium coupled-channel approach (Eq. 3)
- Re V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ -55 MeV from leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg term alone (Eq. 1)
- QCD sum-rule estimate: V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ -150 MeV (from Eqs. 4–5)
- KaoS measurement estimate: V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ -80 MeV from BUU transport calculations
- Lower limit on width: Gamma_Kbar = 50 ± 10 MeV for binding energies B_Kbar ~ 100–200 MeV
- For K- pp system: B ~ 50 MeV, Gamma ~ 60 MeV (Yamazaki and Akaishi, single-channel); B ~ 60 MeV, Gamma ~ 100 MeV (Shevchenko et al., coupled-channel Faddeev)
- FINUDA claim for K- pp: B ~ 115 MeV (back-to-back Lambda p pairs)
- OBELIX claim for K- pp: B ~ 160 MeV, Gamma ~ 30 MeV
- FINUDA preliminary evidence for Kbar NNN I=0 state: B ~ 50–60 MeV, Gamma ~ 35 MeV
- Dynamical RMF calculation for K- 1s in 12C: B = 172 MeV (vs. 132 MeV static)
- Calculated width of deeply bound atomic 1s and 2p states in 208Pb: less than 2 MeV

## Context

This review extends the long-standing debate between "shallow" potentials (40–60 MeV depth) from chirally motivated models by Weise (2000), Ramos (2000), and Cieply (2001) and "deep" potentials (150–200 MeV) from global fits to kaonic-atom data by Friedman, Gal, and collaborators (1993–2006). The work enables future dedicated searches at J-PARC, particularly the reaction K- + 3He → (K- pp) + n proposed by Nagae (2006), and provides a theoretical benchmark for interpreting controversial experimental claims from KEK-PS, FINUDA, and OBELIX. The RMF dynamical calculations by Mareš, Friedman, and Gal (2005–2006) show that nuclear polarization effects increase densities in light nuclei but that widths remain substantial, contradicting the narrower states (Gamma ~ 20 MeV) conjectured by Wycech (1986).
