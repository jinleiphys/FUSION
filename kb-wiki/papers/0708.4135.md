# Strangeness content of the nucleon in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus reactions

> We present a systematic study of the sensitivity of quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections at intermediate energies to the strange quark sea of the nucleon. To this end, we investigate the impact of the weak strangeness form factors on the ratio of proton-to-neutron knockout, the ratio of neutral-to-charged current cross sections, on the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, and on the longitudinal helicity asymmetry. The influence of axial as well as vector strangeness effects is discussed. For the latter, we introduce strangeness parameters from various hadron models and from a recent fit to data from parity violating electron scattering. In our model, the nuclear target is described in terms of a relativistic mean-field approach. The effects of final-state interactions on the outgoing nucleon are quantified within a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approach. Our results are illustrated with cross sections for the scattering of 1 GeV neutrinos and antineutrinos off a $^{12}$C target. Folding with a proposed FINeSSE (anti)neutrino energy-distribution has no qualitative influence on the overall sensitivity of the cross-section ratios to strangeness mechanisms. We show that vector strangeness effects are large and strongly $Q^2$ dependent.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates the sensitivity of quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross-section ratios to the strange quark sea of the nucleon, showing that vector strangeness effects are large and strongly $Q^2$ dependent, and that the influence of vector and axial strangeness on these ratios is strongly intertwined, making the extraction of axial strangeness properties a challenging task.

## Method

The authors computed neutral-current and charged-current quasielastic cross sections for 1 GeV (anti)neutrinos scattering off a $^{12}$C target using a relativistic mean-field model for the nuclear target and a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) to quantify final-state interactions. They introduced strangeness parameters from four hadron models (VMD, K$\Lambda$, NJL, CQS(K)) and a fit to G0 parity-violating electron scattering data, and examined the impact on the proton-to-neutron knockout ratio, neutral-to-charged current ratio, Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, and longitudinal helicity asymmetry as functions of the outgoing nucleon kinetic energy and $Q^2$.

## Key numbers

- The axial strangeness form factor $g_A^s = -0.19$ enhances the neutrino-induced proton-to-neutron ratio $R_{p/n}^{\nu}$ by approximately 40% compared to the $g_A^s = 0$ case.
- For 1 GeV neutrinos on $^{12}$C, the VMD model predicts $\mu_s = -0.31\ \mu_N$ and $r_s^2 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^2$; the K$\Lambda$ model predicts $\mu_s = -0.35\ \mu_N$ and $r_s^2 = -0.007\ \text{fm}^2$; the NJL model predicts $\mu_s = -0.45\ \mu_N$ and $r_s^2 = -0.17\ \text{fm}^2$; the CQS(K) model predicts $\mu_s = 0.115\ \mu_N$ and $r_s^2 = -0.095\ \text{fm}^2$.
- The proposed FINeSSE experiment has an average beam energy $\langle \varepsilon \rangle \approx 700$ MeV, with tails up to 2 GeV; folding with this flux has no qualitative influence on the sensitivity of the neutral-to-charged current ratio to strangeness.
- The antineutrino longitudinal helicity asymmetry $A_l^{\overline{\nu}}$ shows the strongest sensitivity to strangeness effects among all ratios studied, being more sensitive to vector than to axial strangeness parameters.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of neutrino-nucleus scattering at intermediate energies by Vanderventel, Meucci, Praet, and Lava, and provides a systematic comparison of multiple cross-section ratios for extracting strangeness information. The results are relevant for interpreting data from the proposed FINeSSE experiment and the MINER$\nu$A experiment, and complement parity-violating electron scattering measurements from HAPPEX, SAMPLE, PVA4, and G0. The paper shows that the mutual influence of vector and axial strangeness on cross-section ratios, as noted by Pate (2005), is a major source of uncertainty, and that combined analysis of PVES and neutrino-scattering data is a prerequisite for understanding the proton's strangeness properties.
