# Parton coalescence and spacetime

> The influence of spacetime dynamics in hadronization via parton coalescence at RHIC is investigated using covariant parton transport theory. Key observables, the quark number scaling of elliptic flow and the enhancement of the p/pi ratio, show strong dynamical effects and differ from earlier results based on the simple coalescence formulas.

## Key claim

The dynamical spacetime evolution of parton freezeout in heavy-ion collisions produces strong modifications to the simple coalescence formula predictions for baryon/meson ratios and elliptic flow scaling, potentially undermining the quark number scaling explanation for RHIC data.

## Method

The author implemented the Gyulassy-Frankel-Remler (GFR) weak-binding coalescence formalism within the covariant Molnar's Parton Cascade (MPC) transport algorithm for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. Parton evolution was computed with Debye-screened 2→2 cross sections for gluon-gluon cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb, then hadronization proceeded via GFR coalescence with box Wigner functions (size 1 fm) for meson and baryon channels, with remaining partons fragmented independently via JETSET 7.4.10.

## Key numbers

- Impact parameter b = 8 fm (≈30% centrality) for Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Debye screening mass μD = 0.71 GeV
- Two gluon-gluon cross sections explored: σgg = 3 mb (typical pQCD estimate) and 10 mb
- Quark-quark cross section suppressed by (4/9)² relative to σgg
- Box Wigner function spatial cutoff xm = 1 fm
- Parton transport quenches high-pT spectra by factor of ~3 (σgg = 3 mb) to ~10 (10 mb)
- Coalescence enhances both pion and proton RAA by up to a factor of 3 in the "coalescence window" 1.5 < pT < 4.5 GeV
- About two-thirds of partons, including essentially all above pT > 2.5 GeV, hadronize via fragmentation
- Proton enhancement from coalescence is only ≈5% higher than pion enhancement
- Direct hadron v2 from dynamical coalescence is 20% (pions) and 30% (protons) smaller than predictions from the scaling formula v2hadron(pT) = n v2constituent(pT/n)
- Final proton and pion v2 magnitudes are 30-50% below experimental data from PHENIX
- Immediate freezeout at τ = 0.1 fm/c enhances p/π ratio by factor 1.5-1.7

## Context

This work extends the parton coalescence model of Voloshin (2003) and Hwa & Yang (2003) by incorporating realistic spacetime dynamics from covariant transport, rather than assuming sudden freezeout on a 3D hypersurface. The results contradict earlier findings from Duke (2003) and Texas A&M (2003) that simple coalescence formulas could explain the quark number scaling of elliptic flow and the enhanced p/π ratio at RHIC. The study enables future investigations into whether the QCD coalescence process can be approximated as weakly-bound state formation, or whether the spacetime evolution in heavy-ion collisions is not yet understood well enough.
