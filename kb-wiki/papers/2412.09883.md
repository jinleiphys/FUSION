# In-medium electromagnetic form factors of pseudoscalar mesons from the quark model

> We explore the modifications of hadron structure in a nuclear medium, focusing on the spacelike electromagnetic form factors (EMFFs) of light and heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons. By combining the light-front quark model (LFQM) with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which reasonably reproduces EMFFs in free space and the saturation properties of nuclear matter, respectively, we systematically analyze the in-medium EMFFs and charge radii of mesons with various quark flavors. Our findings show that the EMFFs of charged (neutral) mesons exhibit a faster fall-off (increase) with increasing four-momentum transfer squared and nuclear density. Consequently, the absolute value of the charge radii of mesons increases with nuclear density, where the rate of increase depends on their quark flavor contents. We observe that the EMFFs of pions and kaons undergo significant modifications in the nuclear medium, while heavy-light mesons are only slightly modified. By decomposing the quark flavor contributions to EMFFs, we show that the medium effects primarily impact the light-quark sector, leaving the heavy-quark sector nearly unaffected. The results of this study further suggest the importance of the medium effects at the quark level.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic form factors of charged (neutral) pseudoscalar mesons exhibit a faster fall-off (increase) with increasing four-momentum transfer squared and nuclear density, with the absolute value of charge radii increasing with nuclear density at a rate that depends on quark flavor content; however, these modifications are significant for pions and kaons but only slight for heavy-light mesons, as medium effects primarily impact the light-quark sector while leaving the heavy-quark sector nearly unaffected.

## Method

The authors combined the light-front quark model (LFQM) with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction with a Gaussian vertex function for the meson wave functions and the MIT bag model for nucleon structure in symmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium electromagnetic form factors were computed in the Drell-Yan-West frame (q⁺=0) using the plus current component, with the scalar σ mean field modifying the light quark mass and the vector ω mean field shifting the light quark energy. The quark sector form factors were decomposed by flavor to isolate medium effects on light versus heavy quarks.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear matter saturation density is ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ (k_F = 1.305 fm⁻¹), with binding energy per nucleon of -15.7 MeV.
- The incompressibility of nuclear matter is K ≃ 200–300 MeV, achieved without nonlinear meson field couplings.
- The EMFFs of charged mesons fall off faster with Q² at nuclear density ρ₀ compared to free space, while neutral meson EMFFs increase.
- The charge radii of mesons increase with nuclear density, with the rate of increase depending on quark flavor content (pions and kaons show significant modifications; heavy-light mesons show only slight modifications).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Arifi 2023) on in-medium decay constants and distribution amplitudes of light and heavy-light mesons using the same LFQM+QMC approach. It contrasts with earlier studies that focused only on pion and kaon EMFFs in medium using the NJL model combined with QMC (Hutauruk 2018, 2019) or the Bethe-Salpeter approach (deMelo 2014, Yabusaki 2023). The results enable systematic predictions for future measurements at JLab 12 GeV and the Electron-Ion Collider, particularly for the flavor-dependent medium modifications that could be tested in deeply bound pionic and kaonic atom experiments.
