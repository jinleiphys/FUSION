# 16O within the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model and the importance of the Pauli Exclusion Principle

> The Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) is applied to 16O, assumed to consist of a system of four alpha-clusters. For the 4-alpha cluster system a microscopic model space is constructed, which observes the Pauli-Exclusion-Principle (PEP) and is symmetric under permutation of the 4-alpha-particles. A phenomenological Hamiltonian is used, justifying the name Semi in the SACM. The spectrum and transition values are determined. One of the main objectives is to test the importance of the Pauli Exclusion Principle (PEP), comparing the results with the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM), which does not include the PEP, and claims that the 16O shows evidence of a tetrahedral structure, which can be explained easily by symmetry arguments. We show that PEP is very important and cannot be neglected, otherwise it leads to a wrong interpretation of the band structure and to too many states at low energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Pauli Exclusion Principle (PEP) is essential for correctly modeling the 4-alpha cluster structure of 16O; neglecting it, as done in the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM), leads to a denser low-energy spectrum and a wrong assignment of rotational bands, including spurious spin doublets that are not present when the PEP is enforced.

## Method

The Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) was applied to 16O as a system of four alpha particles. A microscopic model space was constructed that respects the PEP via the Wildermuth condition and imposes full symmetry under permutation of the four alpha particles. A phenomenological Hamiltonian with 8 free parameters (including an SU(3)-SO(4) mixing term) was used to calculate the energy spectrum and B(EL) transition values, and the results were compared to both experimental data and the predictions of the ACM (Bijker 2016, 2017) which ignores the PEP.

## Key numbers

* The harmonic oscillator energy was fixed at 13.92 MeV for 16O using the formula 45 × A^(−1/3) − 25 × A^(−2/3).
* The ground state band consists of a single state: the 0_1^+ state, which is 100% pure (0,0) SU(3) irrep.
* The B(E2; 2_1^+ → 0_1^+) transition was calculated as 0.003 e^2 fm^4 (SACM) versus 10.9 e^2 fm^4 (ACM), compared to the experimental value of 3.1 ± 0.1 e^2 fm^4.
* The B(E2; 2_1^+ → 0_2^+) transition was calculated as 18.55 e^2 fm^4 (SACM) versus 2.50 e^2 fm^4 (ACM), compared to the experimental value of 27.0 ± 3.0 e^2 fm^4.
* The B(E3; 3_1^- → 0_1^+) transition was calculated as 20.08 e^2 fm^6 (SACM) versus 5.27 e^2 fm^6 (ACM), compared to the experimental value of 13.5 ± 0.7 e^2 fm^6.
* The octupole deformation parameter β_30 was estimated geometrically as 0.125.

## Context

This work extends the SACM analysis previously applied to 12C (Hess 2018) to the 16O nucleus, directly challenging the claims of Bijker (2016, 2017) that the ACM supports a tetrahedral structure for 16O. The paper shows that while a classical geometrical mapping of the 4-alpha system yields a tetrahedral configuration by default, antisymmetrization destroys the T_4 dynamical symmetry band structure and shifts states to higher energy. The SACM reproduces experimental data better than the ACM for several key transitions, and the results align with microscopic full-scale calculations (Dytrych, 2010) and alpha-cluster condensation studies (Schuck, 2013; Funaki, 2018) that all respect the PEP. The work enables future searches for more complete experimental spectra to distinguish between the SACM and ACM predictions, particularly regarding the existence of low-energy spin doublets.
