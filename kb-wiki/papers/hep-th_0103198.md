# Partial Masslessness of Higher Spins in (A)dS

> Massive spin s>=3/2 fields can become partially massless in cosmological backgrounds. In the plane spanned by m^2 and Λ, there are lines where new gauge invariances permit intermediate sets of higher helicities, rather than the usual flat space extremes of all 2s+1 massive or just 2 massless helicities. These gauge lines divide the (m^2,Λ) plane into unitarily allowed or forbidden intermediate regions where all 2s+1 massive helicities propagate but lower helicity states can have negative norms. We derive these consequences for s=3/2,2 by studying both their canonical (anti)commutators and the transmutation of massive constraints to partially massless Bianchi identities. For s=2, a Hamiltonian analysis exhibits the absence of zero helicity modes in the partially massless sector. For s=5/2,3 we derive Bianchi identities and their accompanying gauge invariances for the various partially massless theories with propagating helicities (+/-5/2,+/-3/2) and (+/-3,+/-2), (+/-3,+/-2,+/-1), respectively. Of these, only the s=3 models are unitary. To these ends, we also provide the half integer generalization of the integer spin wave operators of Lichnerowicz. Partial masslessness applies to all higher spins in (A)dS as seen by their degree of freedom counts. Finally a derivation of massive d=4 constraints by dimensional reduction from their d=5 massless Bianchi identity ancestors is given.

## Key claim

Massive spin s ≥ 3/2 fields in (Anti-)de Sitter backgrounds can become "partially massless" along specific lines in the (m², Λ) plane, where new gauge invariances remove only some lower helicity states rather than all or none; the unitarity of these partially massless theories depends on the ordering of gauge lines when traversed from the Minkowski region, with only s = 2 and s = 3 models being unitary among the cases studied.

## Method

The authors studied the canonical (anti)commutators for spins s ≤ 2 in cosmological backgrounds using synchronous de Sitter metrics, extracting equal-time commutators of time components to determine sign flips of lower helicity norms across gauge lines. For higher spins s = 5/2 and s = 3, they derived Bianchi identities and their accompanying gauge invariances by analyzing the divergence and gamma-trace constraints of the field equations, using the half-integer generalization of Lichnerowicz wave operators. A Hamiltonian analysis was performed for the s = 2 partially massless theory at m² = 2Λ/3 to demonstrate the absence of zero helicity modes.

## Key numbers

- For s = 3/2: the strictly massless gauge line occurs at m² = −Λ/3 (AdS), with unitarity holding for m² > −Λ/3 and non-unitarity for m² < −Λ/3.
- For s = 2: partially massless gauge line at m² = 2Λ/3 (dS) with 4 propagating degrees of freedom (helicities ±2, ±1); strictly massless line at m² = 0 with 2 degrees of freedom; unitarity holds for m² > 2Λ/3.
- For s = 5/2: partially massless theories with propagating helicities (±5/2, ±3/2) exist but are non-unitary.
- For s = 3: partially massless theories with propagating helicities (±3, ±2) and (±3, ±2, ±1) exist, and only the s = 3 models are unitary.
- The equal-time commutator for the s = 2 time-time component contains the denominator factor (3m² − 2Λ), which vanishes at the partially massless line.

## Context

This work extends the earlier discovery of partial masslessness for spin 2 by Deser (1983) and the unitarity analysis of massive spin 2 in de Sitter by Higuchi (1987). It provides the first systematic classification of partially massless theories for higher spins s = 5/2 and s = 3, and generalizes the integer spin wave operators of Lichnerowicz (1961) to half-integer spins. The results enable future studies of higher-spin gauge theories in cosmological backgrounds and clarify which partially massless models can be unitary, with implications for supergravity and string theory in de Sitter space.
