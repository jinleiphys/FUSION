# $־±$-cluster excited states in $^{32}$S

> $־±$-cluster excited states in $^{32}$S are investigated with an extended $^{28}$Si+$־±$ cluster model, in which the $^{28}$Si core deformation and rotation, and the $־±$-cluster breaking are incorporated. In the GCM calculation with the extended $^{28}$Si+$־±$ cluster model, the $־±$-cluster excited states are obtained near the $^{28}$Si+$־±$ threshold energy. The $^{28}$Si core deformation and rotation effects, and also the $־±$-clusters breaking in the $^{28}$Si+$־±$ system are discussed. It is found that the rotation of the oblately deformed $^{28}$Si core gives a significant effect to the $־±$-cluster excited states whereas the $־±$-cluster breaking gives only a minor effect.

## Key claim

The authors find that rotation of the oblately deformed $^{28}$Si core significantly reduces the excitation energies of $\alpha$-cluster excited states in $^{32}$S (by about 5 MeV), whereas $\alpha$-cluster breaking gives only a minor effect; however, they cannot quantitatively predict the bandhead energy because of the ambiguity of the interaction parameters.

## Method

The authors constructed an extended $^{28}$Si+$\alpha$ cluster model where the $^{28}$Si core is described by an extended 7$\alpha$-cluster model with cluster breaking due to the spin-orbit interaction, and the $\alpha$ cluster can also undergo breaking. They performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations by superposing parity- and total-angular-momentum-projected wave functions with varying inter-cluster distance $R$ (1ג€“10 fm), core orientation angle $\theta$ (0ֲ°, 30ֲ°, 60ֲ°, 90ֲ°), and $^{28}$Si core deformation parameter $\Lambda_c$ (0.38 for oblate, 0.80 for spherical). The $\alpha$-cluster breaking parameter $\lambda_\alpha$ was fixed to zero in the full GCM calculation.

## Key numbers

- The $\alpha$-cluster excited states form a rotational band up to the $6^+$ state with rotational constant $k = 140 \sim 150$ keV.
- The rotation of the deformed $^{28}$Si core reduces the $\alpha$-cluster band energy by about 5 MeV compared to the fixed-core-orientation calculation.
- The $^{28}$Si core cluster breaking gives a large energy reduction of 18 MeV for the isolated $^{28}$Si from the 7$\alpha$-cluster model without cluster breaking.
- The dimensionless reduced $\alpha$ widths estimated from $^{28}$Si($0^+_{g.s.}$)+$\alpha$ components are $\theta^2_\alpha(a) = 0.26 \sim 0.32$ at $a = 6$ fm.
- The $^{28}$Si core excitation from the frozen core to the full GCM calculation decreases the ground state energy by about 1 MeV.
- The $^{28}$Si+$\alpha$ threshold reduction from the $0^+$ projection of $^{28}$Si is 4.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the conventional Brink-Bloch $\alpha$-cluster model by incorporating core deformation, rotation, and cluster breaking effects, building on prior studies of $^{28}$Si using the 7$\alpha$-cluster model by Bauhoff (1982) and AMD calculations by Kanada-En'yo (2005). The calculated $\alpha$-cluster band may correspond to experimentally reported bands by Lonnroth (2011) and Itoh (2012), and the rotational constant reasonably agrees with the experimental value from Lonnroth (2011). The analysis of $\alpha$-cluster breaking at the nuclear surface extends the method of Itagaki (2011) from the $^{16}$O+$\alpha$ system to the $^{28}$Si+$\alpha$ system, revealing that Pauli blocking from occupied $0d_{5/2}$ orbits in $^{28}$Si suppresses $\alpha$-cluster breaking compared to the $^{16}$O+$\alpha$ system.
