# Covariant theory of particle-vibrational coupling and its effect on the single-particle spectrum

> The Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach describing the motion of independent particles in effective meson fields is extended by a microscopic theory of particle vibrational coupling. It leads to an energy dependence of the relativistic mass operator in the Dyson equation for the single-particle propagator. This equation is solved in the shell-model of Dirac states. As a result of the dynamics of particle-vibrational coupling we observe a noticeable increase of the level density near the Fermi surface. The shifts of the single-particle levels in the odd nuclei surrounding 208-Pb and the corresponding distributions of the single-particle strength are discussed and compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach by incorporating a microscopic theory of particle-vibrational coupling, which introduces an energy-dependent mass operator in the Dyson equation. The authors show that this coupling noticeably increases the level density near the Fermi surface, but the effect is quantitatively significant only for states within one major shell above and below the Fermi surface; beyond that, the concept of Landau quasi-particles breaks down due to strong fragmentation.

## Method

The authors start from the RMF framework and add an energy-dependent self-energy (mass operator) derived from a particle-phonon coupling model, where the phonon vertices and frequencies are obtained from relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA) calculations. The Dyson equation for the single-particle Green's function is solved in the shell-model Dirac basis under spherical symmetry, using a diagonal approximation for the mass operator. The resulting non-linear eigenvalue problem is reduced to diagonalizing a matrix whose eigenvalues give the single-particle energies and whose eigenvectors yield spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron 1j15/2 state in 208Pb, the RMF energy is -0.48 MeV; after full particle-phonon coupling (phα version) the dominant level shifts to -1.16 MeV with a spectroscopic factor of 0.70.
- For the neutron 2f7/2 hole state in 208Pb, the RMF energy is -11.11 MeV; after full coupling the dominant level shifts to -10.20 MeV with a spectroscopic factor of 0.72.
- The lowest 3- phonon in 208Pb is calculated at 2.74 MeV with B(E3)↑ = 7.46×10^5 e^2fm^6, compared to the experimental energy 2.61 MeV and B(E3)↑ = 5.40(30)×10^5 e^2fm^6.
- The lowest 2+ phonon in 208Pb is calculated at 4.98 MeV with B(E2)↑ = 2.69×10^3 e^2fm^4, compared to the experimental energy 4.07 MeV and B(E2)↑ = 2.97×10^3 e^2fm^4.
- The particle-phonon space for realistic calculations included phonons up to 35 MeV, with particle-hole configurations up to 100 MeV above the Fermi surface and antiparticle-hole configurations down to -1800 MeV relative to the positive continuum.
- Typical dimension of the diagonalization matrix is about two thousand, varying depending on the single-particle state k.

## Context

This work extends the Relativistic Mean Field approach (RMF) by adding particle-vibrational coupling, addressing the known deficiency that mean-field calculations produce a reduced level density at the Fermi surface compared to experiment, as discussed by Mahaux and collaborators (1976). The method is analogous to non-relativistic particle-vibrational coupling calculations by Ring and Werner (1973), but implemented in a covariant framework using the NL3 parameter set. The results enable improved description of single-particle spectra in doubly magic 208Pb and its neighboring odd nuclei, and provide a foundation for extending such calculations to exotic nuclei far from stability.
