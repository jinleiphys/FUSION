# Magnetic aspects of QCD and compact stars

> Magnetic properties of quark matter are discussed. The possibility of ferromagnetic transition is studied by using the one-gluon-exchange interaction. Magnetic susceptibility is evaluated within Landau Fermi liquid theory, and the important roles of the screening for the gluon propagation are elucidated. Static screening for the longitudinal gluons improves the infrared singularities, while the transverse gluons receive only dynamic screening. The latter property gives rise to a novel non-Fermi-liquid behaviour in the magnetic susceptibility. The critical density is estimated to be around the nuclear density and the Curie temperature several tens MeV. The spin density wave is also discussed at moderate densities, where chiral transition becomes important. Pseudoscalar condensate as well as scalar one takes a spatially non-uniform form in a chirally invariant way. Accordingly magnetization oscillates like spin density wave. These results should have some implications on compact star phenomena.

# Magnetic aspects of QCD and compact stars

## Key claim
Quark matter may undergo a ferromagnetic transition at densities around nuclear density, with a Curie temperature of several tens of MeV, due to the Bloch mechanism mediated by one-gluon-exchange interactions; however, the screening of gluon propagation, particularly the dynamic screening of transverse gluons, introduces non-Fermi-liquid behavior that modifies the magnetic susceptibility in a way qualitatively different from electron gas.

## Method
The author evaluates the magnetic susceptibility of quark matter within Landau Fermi liquid theory, using the one-gluon-exchange interaction and incorporating hard-dense-loop (HDL) resummation to account for screening effects. Static Debye screening is applied to longitudinal gluons, while transverse gluons receive only dynamic screening via Landau damping. The study also examines a dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase using the NJL model, where non-uniform scalar and pseudoscalar condensates form a chirally invariant order parameter.

## Key numbers
- Critical density for ferromagnetic transition is estimated to be around nuclear density (no explicit numerical value given in text beyond "around the nuclear density").
- Curie temperature is estimated to be several tens of MeV.
- Wave number q of the DCDW phase is of order O(2k_F), where k_F is the Fermi momentum.
- The screening contribution to the inverse magnetic susceptibility at T=0 scales as Δχ_M^{-1} ∝ κ ln(2/κ), with κ = m_D^2 / (2k_F^2), where m_D is the Debye screening mass.
- The finite-temperature correction to the inverse magnetic susceptibility includes a term δχ_M^{-1} ∝ T^2 ln(m_D/T), alongside the usual T^2 term.
- The Debye screening mass is given by m_D^2 = Σ_{flavors} g^2/(2π^2) k_{F,i} E_{F,i}.
- The quark self-energy near the Fermi surface contains a term Re Σ_+(ω) ∝ -(C_f g^2 v_F)/(12π^2) (ω-μ) ln(m_D/|ω-μ|).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Tatsumi (2000) on spontaneous magnetization of quark matter via the Bloch mechanism, and contrasts with microscopic nuclear-matter calculations that showed negative results for ferromagnetism in hadronic matter. The DCDW phase is related to earlier work on chiral density waves in the large N_c limit and to pion condensation in hadronic matter, where nucleons exhibit anti-ferromagnetic spin ordering in a classical pion field (prior work cited as "pio"). The non-Fermi-liquid behavior in the magnetic susceptibility parallels similar effects found in the specific heat (Holstein, year not given) and the gap function in color superconductivity (Son, year not given), and differs from electron gas where spin fluctuations give a T^3 ln T term (Beal-Monod, year not given). The results have implications for the magnetic evolution of compact stars, including magnetars with fields up to 10^15 G, and may affect thermal evolution through Nambu-Goldstone bosons from spontaneous symmetry breaking.
