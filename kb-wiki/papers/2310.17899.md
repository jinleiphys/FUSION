# Nuclear chiral rotation within Relativistic Configuration-interaction Density functional theory

> The Relativistic Configuration-interaction Density functional (ReCD) theory that combines the advantages of large-scale configuration-interaction shell model and relativistic density functional theory is extended to study nuclear chiral rotation. The energy spectra and transition probabilities of the chiral doublet bands are reproduced satisfactorily without any free parameters. By analyzing the probability amplitudes of the wavefunctions, the significant roles of configuration mixing and four quasiparticle states to the chiral doublets are revealed. The evolution from chiral vibration to static chirality are clearly illustrated by the K plot and azimuthal plot. The present investigation provides both microscopic and quantal descriptions for nuclear chirality for the first time and demonstrates the robustness of chiral geometry against the configuration mixing as well as the four quasiparticle states.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Configuration-interaction Density functional (ReCD) theory is extended to study nuclear chiral rotation, providing the first microscopic and quantal description of nuclear chirality. The paper demonstrates that chiral geometry is robust against configuration mixing and four-quasiparticle states, but notes that energy levels with \(I \geq 15\hbar\) are slightly overestimated and that the staggering of \(B(M1)\) values may indicate the triaxial deformation \(\gamma\) of \(^{130}\)Cs is slightly underestimated by the relativistic DFT.

## Method

The ReCD theory combines relativistic density functional theory with large-scale configuration-interaction shell model techniques. First, a triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (TRHB) equation is solved iteratively using the PC-PK1 Lagrangian to obtain a self-consistent intrinsic state for the odd-odd nucleus \(^{130}\)Cs. A configuration space is then constructed including two-quasiparticle and four-quasiparticle states built on that intrinsic state, with a quasiparticle excitation energy cutoff of 5.0 MeV. Three-dimensional angular momentum projection is applied to each basis state, and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the projected basis to obtain energy spectra and transition probabilities without any free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for \(^{130}\)Cs: \((\beta, \gamma) = (0.20, 21^\circ)\)
- Pairing strength used: \(G = 728\) MeV fm\(^3\)
- Quasiparticle excitation energy cutoff: \(E_{\mathrm{cut}} = 5.0\) MeV
- At \(I = 11\hbar\), the azimuthal plot for Band A shows a single peak at \((\theta, \phi) = (63^\circ, 90^\circ)\); for Band B, two peaks appear at \((\theta, \phi) = (59^\circ, 48^\circ)\) and \((59^\circ, 132^\circ)\)
- At \(I = 16\hbar\) and \(17\hbar\), static chirality is indicated by two peaks in the azimuthal plot: for Band A at \((\theta, \phi) \approx (77^\circ, 55^\circ)\) and \((77^\circ, 125^\circ)\); for Band B at \((\theta, \phi) \approx (69^\circ, 45^\circ)\) and \((69^\circ, 135^\circ)\)

## Context

This work extends the ReCD theory, previously developed for axial (Zhao 2016) and triaxial (Wang 2023) cases, to the study of nuclear chirality. It goes beyond the semiclassical treatment of the three-dimensional tilted axis cranking model (3D-TAC-DFT) (Zhao 2017) and the time-dependent relativistic 3D-TAC-DFT (Ren 2022), which remained within the mean-field approximation. The paper enables future microscopic and quantal studies of multiple chiral doublets (M\(\chi\)D), first predicted by Meng (2006) and confirmed experimentally by Ayangeakaa (2013), and can be applied to other nuclei where chiral doublet bands have been observed.
