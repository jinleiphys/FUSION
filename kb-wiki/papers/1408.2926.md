# Old neutron stars as probes of isospin-violating dark matter

> Isospin-violating dark matter (IVDM), which couples differently with protons and neutrons, provides a promising mechanism to ameliorate the tension among recent direct detection experiments. Assuming DM is non-interacting bosonic asymmetric IVDM, we investigate how the existence of old neutron stars limits the DM-proton scattering cross-section $σ_{\rm p}$, especially the effects of the isospin violating DM-nucleon interactions and the symmetry energy in the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. Our calculations are completely based on general relativity and especially the structure of neutron stars is obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with nuclear matter equation of state constrained by terrestrial experiments. We find that, by considering the more realistic neutron star model rather than a simple uniform neutron sphere as usual, the $σ_{\rm p}$ bounds from old neutron stars can be varied by more than an order of magnitude depending on the specific values of the DM neutron-to-proton coupling ratio $f_{\rm n}/f_{\rm p}$, and they can be further varied by more than a factor of two depending on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. In particular, we demonstrate that the observed nearby isolated old neutron star PSR B1257+12 can set a very strong limit on $σ_{\rm p}$ for low-mass DM particles ($ \le 20 \, {\rm GeV}$) that reaches a sensitivity beyond the current best limits from direct detection experiments and disfavors the DM interpretation of previously-reported positive experimental results, including the IVDM.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that old neutron stars can set limits on the DM-proton scattering cross-section that are stronger than current direct detection experiments for low-mass DM particles (≤ 20 GeV), but only under the specific assumptions that DM is non-interacting bosonic asymmetric isospin-violating dark matter and that the neutron star structure is modeled realistically rather than as a simple uniform neutron sphere.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with nuclear matter equations of state from the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach to obtain realistic neutron star structures composed of β-stable npeμ matter. They calculate DM capture rates using general relativistic geodesic equations, accounting for Pauli blocking and Fermi motion corrections to DM-nucleon scattering, and determine black hole formation conditions for non-interacting bosonic DM including Bose-Einstein condensate effects.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Goldman and Nussinov (1989) and later works by Kouvaris (2011-2013) and others that used simple uniform neutron sphere models, by incorporating realistic neutron star structure with β-stable npeμ matter. The results enable more accurate constraints on isospin-violating dark matter models that have been proposed to reconcile tensions between direct detection experiments such as CoGeNT (2011), DAMA (2009), CRESST-II (2012), and CDMS-II-Si (2013) versus null results from XENON100 (2011-2012), LUX (2013), and SuperCDMS-Ge (2014). The paper demonstrates that the old neutron star PSR B1257+12 can exclude the DM interpretation of all previously-reported positive experimental results, including the IVDM interpretation.
