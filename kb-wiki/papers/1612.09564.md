# Fine-tuning the Color-Glass Condensate with the nuclear configurational entropy

> The dipole-nucleus forward scattering amplitude rules the onset of the gluon anomalous dimension, in the Color-Glass Condensate regime. In this model, the onset of quantum regime is here derived as a critical stable point in the nuclear configurational entropy, matching the fitted experimental data in the literature with accuracy of 1%. It corroborates with the informational entropy paradigm in high energy nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper derives the onset of the quantum regime in the Color-Glass Condensate as a critical stable point in the nuclear configurational entropy, matching the fitted experimental data in the literature with accuracy of approximately 1%. The specific hedge is that the critical point corresponds to minima in the configurational entropy curves, and the best agreement with the previously assumed value of the parameter \(c=4\) occurs for different Bjorken \(x\) values with errors ranging from about 0.94% to 1.23%.

## Method

The author computes the nuclear configurational entropy from the Fourier transform of the saturated component of the total hadronic cross section, using the KKT (Kovchegov-Kharzeev-Tuchin) model for the dipole-nucleus forward scattering amplitude. The saturated cross section is expressed in terms of the dipole-proton cross section and a Gaussian proton wave function, with the anomalous dimension \(\gamma(y, r^2)\) containing a free parameter \(c\) that describes the onset of the quantum regime. The configurational entropy is then evaluated as a function of this parameter \(c\) for fixed values of the Bjorken variable \(x\), and the minima of the entropy curves are identified.

## Key numbers

- The minima of the configurational entropy occur at \(c = 4.038\) for \(x = 10^{-7}\), at \(c = 4.041\) for \(x = 10^{-5}\), and at \(c = 4.050\) for \(x = 10^{-4}\).
- For \(x = 10^{-7}\), the error relative to the literature value \(c = 4\) is approximately 0.94%; for \(x = 10^{-4}\), the error is approximately 1.23%.
- The saturation exponent \(\lambda\) is approximately 0.3, fixed by DIS data.
- The saturation scale for gold nuclei at RHIC energies is expected to be approximately 2 GeV.
- The initial condition parameters are \(Q_0^2 = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x_0 = 0.3 \times 10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of configurational entropy, previously used for meson and glueball stability in holographic QCD (Bernardini, 2016) and for Bose-Einstein condensates of gravitons (Casadio, 2016), to the Color-Glass Condensate regime of high-energy nuclear physics. The results corroborate the choice \(c = 4\) made in the KKT model (Kovchegov, Kharzeev, and Tuchin, 2005) and in the minijet saturation model (Carvalho, 2008), and they enable a fine-tuning of the onset of quantum evolution using informational entropy as a criterion. Future directions include exploring quantum fluctuations and topological defects in the wave function, as well as modified versions of the KKT model with different values of the rapidity onset \(y_0\).
