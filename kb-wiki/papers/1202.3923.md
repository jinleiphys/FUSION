# Analytic proof of partial conservation of seniority in $j=9/2$ shells

> A partial conservation of the seniority quantum number in $j=9/2$ shells has been found recently in a numerical application. In this paper a complete analytic proof for this problem is derived as an extension of the work by Zamick and P. Van Isacker [Phys. Rev. C 78 (2008) 044327]. We analyze the properties of the non-diagonal matrix elements with the help of the one-particle and two-particle coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp's). It is found that all non-diagonal (and the relevant diagonal) matrix elements can be re-expressed in simple ways and are proportional to certain one-particle cfp's. This remarkable occurrence of partial dynamic symmetry is the consequence of the peculiar property of the $j=9/2$ shell, where all $v=3$ and 5 states are uniquely defined.

# Analytic proof of partial conservation of seniority in $j=9/2$ shells

## Key claim
The paper provides a complete analytic proof that in the $j=9/2$ shell, certain $v=4$ states with $I=4$ and $I=6$ have vanishing non-diagonal matrix elements with $v=2$ and other $v=4$ states for *any* spherically symmetric two-body interaction, not only for seniority-conserving interactions. This partial conservation of seniority holds because all $v=3$ and $v=5$ states in the $j=9/2$ shell are uniquely defined by their angular momentum and seniority.

## Method
The authors analyze the Hamiltonian matrix elements for four identical fermions in a $j=9/2$ shell using one-particle and two-particle coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp's). They apply recursion relations for the cfp's, including a modified Redmond recursion relation, to re-express all non-diagonal and relevant diagonal matrix elements in simple forms proportional to certain one-particle cfp's. The special $v=4$ states are constructed via the principal-parent procedure, and the vanishing of non-diagonal matrix elements is verified explicitly for all $J$ values through both analytic derivation and symbolic calculation of the $M^I(J)$ matrices.

## Key numbers
- For $I=4$ states, the ratio of non-diagonal matrix elements between $v=2$ and the two $v=4$ states is $M^I_{2\alpha_1}(J)/M^I_{2\alpha_2}(J) = \sqrt{182}/(10\sqrt{510})$, independent of $J$.
- For $I=6$ states, the corresponding ratio is $M^I_{2\alpha_1}(J)/M^I_{2\alpha_2}(J) = -5\sqrt{15}/(6\sqrt{2261})$, independent of $J$.
- The diagonal matrix elements $M^{I=4}_{aa}(J)$ for the special $v=4$ state $|a\rangle$ are: 34/99 for $J=2$, 1/6 for $J=4$, 13/90 for $J=6$, and 19/55 for $J=8$.
- The diagonal matrix elements $M^{I=6}_{aa}(J)$ for the special $v=4$ state $|a\rangle$ are: 19/66 for $J=2$, 2/13 for $J=4$, 1/6 for $J=6$, and 56/143 for $J=8$.
- The overlap of the special $I=4$ state with the $|j^4[J_\alpha=2 J_\beta=2],v=4,I\rangle$ state is $\alpha = 10\sqrt{255}/\sqrt{25591}$.
- The overlap of the special $I=6$ state with the $|j^4[J_\alpha=2 J_\beta=4],v=4,I\rangle$ state is $\alpha = 2\sqrt{6783}/\sqrt{27257}$.

## Context
This work extends the numerical discovery of partial seniority conservation in $j=9/2$ shells by Escuderos and Zamick (2006) and Zamick and Van Isacker (2008), who found that $I=4$ and $I=6$ states have good seniority for arbitrary interactions. The paper provides a complete analytic proof building on the principal-parent procedure introduced by Qi (2011) and the recursion relations of Talmi (1993), Sun (1989), and Zamick (2006). This result enables the identification of solvable eigenstates in systems where seniority is not a good quantum number, with applications to nuclear structure and Bose-Einstein condensates as noted by Isacker (2010).
