# Quark (Anti) Nugget Dark Matter

> Prepared for Snowmass 2013 e-Proceedings. We review a testable dark matter model outside of the standard WIMP paradigm in which the observed ratio Omega_{dark} ~ 5 Omega_{visible} for visible and dark matter densities finds its natural explanation as a result of their common QCD origin. Special emphasis is placed on the observational consequences of this model and on the detection prospects for present or planned experiments. In particular, we argue that the relative intensities for a number of observed excesses of emission (covering more than 11 orders of magnitude) can be explained by this model without any new fundamental parameters as all relative intensities for these emissions are determined by standard and well established physics.

## Key claim

The observed ratio of dark matter to visible matter densities (Ω_dark ≈ 5·Ω_visible) can be explained by a common QCD origin, provided that baryogenesis is actually a charge separation process where the global baryon number of the universe remains zero and the unobserved antibaryons comprise the dark matter in the form of macroscopically large quark (anti)nuggets.

## Method

The authors review a model in which baryogenesis occurs at the QCD phase transition, where quarks and antiquarks bind into heavy nuggets of quark matter in a colour superconducting phase, with CP-violating processes associated with the axion θ term resulting in preferential formation of antinuggets. The relative intensities of observed excess emissions across more than 11 orders of magnitude in frequency are computed using standard nuclear physics and QED, with the only free parameter being the average baryon charge ⟨B⟩ of the nuggets, fixed by assuming the model saturates the observed 511 keV line from the galactic centre. The model's predictions for direct detection are evaluated by considering the flux of nuggets through the Earth's atmosphere and ice, and comparing to existing and planned cosmic ray detectors.

## Key numbers

- The observed ratio of energy densities is Ω_dark ≈ 5·Ω_visible.
- The baryon to entropy ratio at present time is η ≡ (n_B - n_antiB)/n_γ ≈ n_B/n_γ ~ 10^{-10}.
- The formation temperature at which nugget formation completes is T_form ≈ 41 MeV.
- The gap when colour superconductivity sets in inside nuggets is Δ ~ 100 MeV.
- The critical temperature for colour superconductivity is T_c ≈ 0.6 Δ.
- The baryon charge of nuggets ranges from 10^{23} ≲ B ≲ 10^{32} for axion masses 10^{-6} eV ≤ m_a ≤ 10^{-3} eV.
- The typical size of nuggets ranges from 10^{-6} cm ≲ R ≲ 10^{-3} cm.
- The axion domain wall tension is σ_a ≈ 1.8·10^8 GeV^3 for m_a ~ 10^{-6} eV.
- For B ~ 10^{25}, the flux of nuggets is dN/(dA dt) = nv ≈ (10^{25}/B) km^{-2} yr^{-1}.
- For a nugget with radius 10^{-5} cm, annihilation of all molecules along its path would generate ~10^7 J.
- The effective interaction cross-section per mass is σ/M ~ 10^{-10} cm^2/g, well below astrophysical limits of σ/M < 1 cm^2/g.

## Context

This review extends the strangelet model of Witten (1984) by incorporating antimatter nuggets stabilized by axion domain walls, as originally proposed by Zhitnitsky (2002) and Oaknin and Zhitnitsky (2003). The model offers a unified explanation for several observed excess emissions that are not easily explained by conventional astrophysical sources, including the 511 keV line from SPI/INTEGRAL, the 1-30 MeV excess from COMPTEL, the ~10 keV plasma observed by Chandra, the WMAP haze, and the ARCADE2 radio excess. The charge separation mechanism underlying the model can be experimentally tested in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where local CP violation with θ ~ 1 has been observed and interpreted by Kharzeev (2007) and Zhitnitsky (2010, 2012).
