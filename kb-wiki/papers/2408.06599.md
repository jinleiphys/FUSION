# Radial oscillations of proto-neutron stars

> We investigate radial oscillations of proto-neutron stars, employing equations of state described by the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory or the relativistic mean field model, and assuming isentropy and fixed lepton fractions for the internal structure. We calculate the eigenfrequencies and corresponding oscillation functions, which show different characteristics in different mass regions. In the low-mass region around 1.4 solar mass, the radial oscillation frequencies are lowered by large entropy and neutrino trapping, along with a reduction of the average adiabatic index. In the region close to the maximum mass, the fundamental oscillation frequency drops rapidly and vanishes at the maximum mass, in accordance with the critical stability criterion $\partial M /\partial ρ_c = 0$, as for cold neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for proto-neutron stars (PNSs) with masses around 1.4 solar masses, radial oscillation frequencies are lowered by large entropy and neutrino trapping, but this effect is conditional on the mass region: it is significant only in the low-mass regime, while near the maximum mass the fundamental frequency drops rapidly and vanishes at the maximum mass, following the same stability criterion as cold neutron stars.

## Method

The authors constructed isentropic equations of state for PNSs using either the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory with the Argonne V18 potential and three-body forces, or the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the Shen 2020 TM1e parametrization. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for hydrostatic equilibrium and the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue equations for radial oscillations, assuming beta equilibrium with fixed lepton fractions (Y_{L_e}=0.4, Y_{L_\mu}=0) for neutrino trapping and isentropic conditions with entropy per baryon S/A = 0, 1, or 2.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of cold NS: 2.36 solar masses (BHF) and 2.12 solar masses (RMF), with corresponding radii 10.7 km and 11.8 km respectively
- For S2 (entropy per baryon = 2) with BHF, maximum mass decreases by about 0.03 solar masses and radius increases by about 0.4 km compared to cold NS
- For S2 with RMF, maximum mass increases by about 0.08 solar masses and radius increases by about 1.3 km compared to cold NS
- For a 1.4 solar mass PNS with S2Y0.4 (entropy 2, trapped neutrinos), radius is about 50% larger than the cold NS with same mass for both EOSs
- Fundamental frequency f1 for a 1.4 solar mass cold NS: 3.15 kHz (BHF) and 2.90 kHz (RMF)
- For a 1.4 solar mass PNS with S2Y0.4, f1 decreases to 1.97 kHz (BHF) and 1.62 kHz (RMF), a reduction of about 1/3 compared to cold NS
- Second mode frequency f2 for 1.4 solar mass cold NS: 6.76 kHz (BHF) and 6.55 kHz (RMF); for S2Y0.4: 3.18 kHz (BHF) and 2.46 kHz (RMF), a reduction of more than 50%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of radial oscillations in cold neutron stars by Sun (2021) and Zheng (2023) to the hot, lepton-rich environment of proto-neutron stars, which are formed after supernova explosions or binary neutron star mergers. It builds on the finite-temperature BHF EOS developed by Lu (2019) and the RMF EOS by Shen (2020), and complements limited prior work on PNS oscillations by Gondek (1997), Zhu (2018), and Sotani (2021). The results enable future asteroseismology of PNSs, where measurement of radial oscillation frequencies could help determine the actual lepton fraction and constrain the hot dense matter equation of state, potentially informing observations of gravitational waves from PNSs or short gamma-ray bursts.
