# The dual properties of chiral and isospin asymmetric dense quark matter formed of two-color quarks

> In this paper the phase structure of dense baryon matter composed of $u$ and $d$ quarks with two colors has been investigated in the presence of baryon $μ_B$, isospin $μ_I$ and chiral isospin $μ_{I5}$ chemical potentials in the framework of Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. In the chiral limit, it has been shown that the duality between phases with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and condensation of charged pions, found in the three color case, remains valid in the two color case. In addition, it has been shown that there are two more dualities in the phase diagram in two color case, namely (as in the case with $μ_{I5}=0$), at $μ_{I5}\ne 0$ the general $(μ,μ_I,μ_{I5})$-phase portrait of the model has dual symmetry between the phase with condensation of charged pions and the phase with diquark condensation. This duality stays exact even in the physical point, $m_0\ne 0$. And at $m_0=0$ the $(μ,μ_I,μ_{I5})$-phase portrait becomes even more symmetrical, since dual symmetry between phases with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and diquark condensation appears. It is shown that due to the dualities the phase diagram is extremely symmetric and has interlacing structure. One can show that the phase portrait of two-color NJL model can be obtained just by duality properties from the results of investigations of three-color NJL model (it was noticed only after the numerical calculations have been performed). Three-color case shares only one duality of the two color one, and one can only see a facet of this enormously symmetric picture in the case of three colors. Using dualities only, it is possible to show that there are no mixed phases (phases with two non-zero condensates). This prediction of dualities is of great use, because for sure it can be shown by the direct calculations but it would be enormously more complicated and time-consuming numerically.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the two-color Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with baryon, isospin, and chiral isospin chemical potentials, the phase diagram exhibits three distinct dual symmetries (between chiral symmetry breaking and charged pion condensation, between charged pion condensation and diquark condensation, and between chiral symmetry breaking and diquark condensation), with the latter two being unique to the two-color case and not present in the three-color case; the duality between charged pion condensation and diquark condensation remains exact even at nonzero bare quark mass.

## Method

The authors study a (3+1)-dimensional two-color NJL model with u and d quarks, including both quark-antiquark and diquark interaction channels, in the presence of baryon (μ_B), isospin (μ_I), and chiral isospin (μ_{I5}) chemical potentials. They compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field (one fermion-loop) approximation at zero temperature, using a sharp three-momentum cutoff Λ = 657 MeV, coupling H = 7.23 GeV^{-2}, and bare quark mass m_0 = 5.4 MeV (though numerical calculations are simplified by working in the chiral limit m_0 = 0). The phase structure is analyzed by identifying dual symmetries of the thermodynamic potential under interchanges of chemical potentials and corresponding condensates (chiral, pion, and diquark).

## Key numbers

- Cutoff parameter: Λ = 657 MeV
- Four-fermion coupling: H = 7.23 GeV^{-2}
- Bare quark mass (physical point): m_0 = 5.4 MeV
- Critical baryon chemical potential for transition to diquark condensation at m_0 ≠ 0: μ_c = m_π/2 (where m_π is the pion mass)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Khunjua, Klimenko, Zhokhov, 2018-2019) on duality between chiral symmetry breaking and charged pion condensation in three-color NJL models with chiral isospin asymmetry, now incorporating diquark condensation (color superconductivity) relevant at higher baryon densities. The two-color approach is motivated by the absence of a sign problem in lattice QCD with two colors, and the results are compared to earlier two-color NJL studies by Weise (2000) and others (Brauner, Andersen, 2010-2015). The dualities enable prediction of the full phase diagram without exhaustive numerical computation and show that mixed phases (with two nonzero condensates) are absent.
