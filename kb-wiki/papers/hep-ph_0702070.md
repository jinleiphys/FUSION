# Absorption in the final state in reactions and decays in the medium

> The role of strong absorption of particles in intermidiate and final states has been considered. The range of applicability of phenomenological model of absorption has been studied. This model is nonuniversal. Its applicability depends on the type of interaction Hamiltonian and matrix element used. We also demonstrate that the violation of the unitarity condition can produce a qualitative error in the results. The absorption (decay) in the final state does not tend to suppress the total process probability as well as the probability of the channel corresponding to absorption. This is true for the reactions, decays and $n\bar{n}$ conversion in the medium.

# Absorption in the final state in reactions and decays in the medium

## Key claim
The phenomenological model of absorption using a non-hermitian Hamiltonian (optical potential) is nonuniversal and, when applied to processes such as $n\bar{n}$ conversion in the medium or decays with final-state absorption, produces a qualitative error: it predicts that absorption suppresses the total process probability, whereas a correct unitary treatment shows that opening an absorption channel increases the total probability.

## Method
The author compares two approaches for describing absorption in the final state: (1) a phenomenological model using a non-hermitian Hamiltonian ${\cal H} = i\,{\rm Im}U_{\bar{n}}\bar{\Psi}_{\bar{n}}\Psi_{\bar{n}}$ (or equivalently an optical potential with imaginary part $-i\Gamma/2$), and (2) a unitary model using a hermitian annihilation Hamiltonian ${\cal H}_a$. For the $n\bar{n}$ conversion in the medium, the coupled Schrödinger equations with complex potential are solved in lowest order in the small $n\bar{n}$ mixing parameter $\epsilon$, and the total transition probability $W_t$ is computed via $W_t = 2\,{\rm Im}T_{ii}$. For the free-space process $n \rightarrow \bar{n} \rightarrow \bar{p}e^+\nu$, the same phenomenological model is applied and compared with the direct field-theoretical calculation using a hermitian Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- For $n\bar{n}$ conversion in the medium with $\Gamma t \gg 1$, the phenomenological model gives $W_t(t) = 4\epsilon^2 t / \Gamma$ (Eq. 10), showing ${\rm d}W_t/{\rm d}\Gamma < 0$.
- For the same process with $|Vt| \ll 1$, the model gives $W_t(t) = \epsilon^2 t^2 (1 - \Gamma t/6)$ (Eq. 11), again with ${\rm d}W_t/{\rm d}\Gamma < 0$.
- For the free-space process $n \rightarrow \bar{n} \rightarrow \bar{p}e^+\nu$, the phenomenological model yields $W_t^\beta(t) \approx 4\epsilon^2 t / \Gamma_\beta$ (Eq. 16), while the free-space $n\bar{n}$ transition probability without decay is $W_f = \epsilon^2 t^2$, giving $W_t^\beta/W_f \sim 1/(\Gamma_\beta t) \ll 1$.
- The unitary model for decay $a \rightarrow b\bar{n}$ in the medium gives $W_a^h \sim \Gamma$ (Eq. 38), opposite to the phenomenological $W_t \sim 1/\Gamma$.
- The value of $2\,{\rm Im}T_{ii}(t_0)$ for $n\bar{n}$ conversion is estimated as $< 10^{-31}$ (Eq. 19) using standard parameters.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier study (Nazaruk, 2006) showing that field-theoretical and phenomenological models give opposite effects for final-state absorption. It directly contradicts the standard approach used in calculations of $n\bar{n}$ transitions in nuclear matter (Dover et al., 1983; Kondratyuk et al., 1982; Oset et al., 1994) and in phenomenological treatments of absorption via optical potentials (Feshbach, 1958). The paper demonstrates that the phenomenological model (5) violates unitarity, leading to incorrect suppression of $n\bar{n}$ conversion and decay probabilities. The correct unitary treatment shows that absorption in the final state does not suppress the total process probability, which has implications for calculations of $n\bar{n}$ conversion in nuclei, nuclear decays with particle absorption, and reactions in the medium where final-state interactions are important.
