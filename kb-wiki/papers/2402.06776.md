# Consistent inclusion of fluctuations in first-order causal and stable relativistic hydrodynamics

> We construct, for the first time, a Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) theory for linear stochastic fluctuations, which is proved to be mathematically consistent, causal, and covariantly stable. The Martin-Siggia-Rose action is shown to be bilocal in most cases, and the noise is not white. The presence of nonhydrodynamic modes induces long-range correlations in the primary fluid variables (temperature, chemical potential, and flow velocity). However, correlators of conserved densities remain localized in space, and coincide with those calculated within fluctuating Isreal-Stewart theory. We show that, in some cases, there is a nonlocal change of variables that maps the Israel-Stewart action into the BDNK action.

## Key claim
The authors construct, for the first time, a linear stochastic fluctuating version of BDNK (Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun) theory that is mathematically consistent, causal, and covariantly stable, provided the entropy functional is a Lyapunov functional of the non-fluctuating theory in all reference frames. The noise is not white and the Martin-Siggia-Rose action is bilocal in most cases, but correlators of conserved densities remain localized in space and coincide with those from fluctuating Israel-Stewart theory.

## Method
The authors apply the Fox-Uhlenbeck approach to stochastic hydrodynamics, using the information current (a timelike future-directed vector field) to construct the fluctuation-dissipation theorem for linearized BDNK theory. They derive the two-point correlator and the Martin-Siggia-Rose effective action entirely from the classical equation of motion operator and the information current, without needing a detailed noise model. The regularization scheme suppresses non-hydrodynamic contributions to ensure well-defined equal-time correlators.

## Key numbers
- The factor \(e^{S_n-S_e}\) in the fluctuation-dissipation theorem scales like \(\sim e^{-N\epsilon^2}\), where \(N\) is the number of particles in a fluid parcel; for water parcels of 1 mm radius, \(N \sim 10^{20}\) water molecules, while in pp collisions \(N \sim 5\) particle degrees of freedom in a fluid parcel.
- The dispersion relations \(\omega_n(\textbf{k})\) satisfy \(\mathfrak{Im} \, \omega_n \leq |\mathfrak{Im} \, k^x|\) for \(\textbf{k}=(k^x,0,0)\), which is the consistency criterion with microcausality.

## Context
This work extends the program of including stochastic fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics, which previously succeeded for Israel-Stewart theory (Mullins 2023, Mullins 2023) but suffered from unphysical UV divergences in BDNK theory (Abbasi 2022, Mullins 2023). The authors prove that if the classical theory admits an information current (Gavassino 2021, Gavassino 2021), the fluctuating theory is automatically causal, stable, and mathematically consistent. This enables consistent inclusion of noise in hydrodynamic simulations of small systems such as pp collisions, where fluctuating phenomena cannot be neglected (Yan 2015).
