# Closed shells at drip-line nuclei

> The shell structure of magic nuclei far from stability is discussed in terms of the self-consistent spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. In particular, the sensitivity of the shell-gap sizes and the two-neutron separation energies to the choice of particle-hole and particle-particle components of the effective interaction is investigated.

## Key claim

The size of neutron shell gaps at magic numbers decreases substantially with increasing neutron excess when calculated with the self-consistent spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory using the SkP interaction, but this quenching is highly sensitive to the choice of both the particle-hole (effective mass) and particle-particle (pairing) components of the effective interaction; the authors caution that their spherical calculations cannot reproduce experimental isotopic trends that include deformation effects.

## Method

The authors performed spherical HFB calculations for chains of proton-magic isotopes using the Skyrme interaction in the particle-hole channel (parametrizations SIII, SkM*, and SkP) and three types of contact pairing forces in the particle-particle channel: the SkP force itself, a pure contact force, and a density-dependent contact force. For each combination, the pairing strength was adjusted to reproduce the average neutron pairing gap of 1.256 MeV in ¹¹⁹Sn, and the resulting two-neutron separation energies and shell-gap sizes (defined as the difference S₂n(N) − S₂n(N+2)) were compared to experimental data from Audi (1993) and to predictions from the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Möller (1993).

## Key numbers

* The average neutron pairing gap in ¹¹⁹Sn used for fitting all pairing forces was set to 1.256 MeV.
* In the HF+SkP model, the N=82 shell gap decreases from about 9 MeV to 5 MeV as Z goes from 86 down to 40; in the HFB+SkP model, the same gap decreases from 7 MeV to almost zero.
* The effective masses for the three Skyrme forces are m*/m = 0.76 (SIII), 0.79 (SkM*), and 1.0 (SkP).
* Experimental two-neutron separation energies for proton-magic isotopes are available down to approximately S₂n ≈ 10 MeV; the r-process path lies near S₂n ≈ 2 MeV and the two-neutron drip line at S₂n = 0.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent HFB approach in spatial coordinates introduced by Dobaczewski (1984) with the SkP parametrization, and confronts it with other standard Skyrme forces (SIII from Beiner (1975) and SkM* from Bartel (1982)) and with the microscopic-macroscopic FRDM of Möller (1993). The paper shows that the SkP force, with its effective mass of unity and density-dependent pairing, predicts a strong quenching of shell gaps in neutron-rich nuclei, consistent with the analysis of solar-system r-process abundances by Kratz (1994). This enables future systematic work toward a universal Skyrme-type interaction that can reliably predict shell effects far from stability, including the need to incorporate isovector density dependence and the interplay between deformation and pairing in open-shell drip-line nuclei.
