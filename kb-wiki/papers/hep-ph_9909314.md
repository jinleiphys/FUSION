# Perturbative Color Transparency in Electron Beam Experiments via Impact-Parameter Factorization

> Color transparency is a prediction of perturbative QCD. Yet detailed calculations have been lacking, and aspects of the required factorization have been controversial. We report on the first complete calculations entirely within a perturbative QCD framework. We also comment on the underlying factorization method and assumptions.

# Perturbative Color Transparency in Electron Beam Experiments via Impact-Parameter Factorization

## Key claim
The authors report the first complete perturbative QCD calculations of color transparency in electron beam experiments, but the results depend critically on the choice of trial wave functions and the method of impact-parameter factorization, which avoids the limit interchange problem between large $Q^2$ and large $A$ that plagues the asymptotic Lepage-Brodsky factorization.

## Method
The authors perform Monte Carlo integrations over 9-dimensional phase space for proton knockout and analogous calculations for pion knockout, using impact-parameter factorization that incorporates transverse quark separations via Fourier transform to configuration (impact-parameter $b$) space. Nuclear filtering is implemented through an eikonal factor $f_A(b; B) = exp(-\int_{z}^{\infty} dz' \sigma(b) \rho(B, z')/2)$ with $\sigma(b)$ parametrized as $k b^2$, and the full amplitude integrates over nuclear density $\rho(B,z)$ weighted by the hard-scattering and filtering factors. The transparency ratio is obtained by dividing the nuclear target rate by a cross section including the free-space form factor.

## Key numbers
- The Glauber model attenuation cross section value is 36 mb, used as a baseline for comparison with extracted effective attenuation cross sections $\sigma_{eff}(Q^2)$.
- The extracted $\sigma_{eff}(Q^2)$ decreases significantly with increasing $Q^2$ to values well below 36 mb, indicating color transparency.
- Uncertainties in nuclear correlations at the 10% level were a major concern, in some cases exceeding theoretical uncertainties from the rest of the calculation.

## Context
This work extends the impact-parameter factorization method developed by Ralston and Pire (1990) and refined by Li and Sterman, and by Kundu et al., to nuclear targets for electron beam experiments. It addresses the inadequacy of the asymptotic Lepage-Brodsky (1980) factorization for describing color transparency at laboratory energies, where the limits of large $Q^2$ and large $A$ do not commute. The calculations enable quantitative predictions for upcoming Jefferson Laboratory $(e, e'p)$ experiments and show that the slope of the transparency ratio versus $Q^2$ can distinguish between centrally peaked and endpoint-dominated wave function models such as the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky and King-Sachrajda models.
