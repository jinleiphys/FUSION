# Screening and antiscreening in anisotropic QED and QCD plasmas

> We use a transport-theory approach to construct the static propagator of a gauge boson in a plasma with a general axially- and reflection-symmetric momentum distribution. Non-zero magnetic screening is found if the distribution is anisotropic, confirming the results of a closed-time-path-integral approach. We find that the electric and magnetic screening effects depend on both the orientation of the momentum carried by the boson and the orientation of its polarization. In some orientations there can be antiscreening, reflecting the instabilities of such a medium. We present some fairly general conditions on the dependence of these effects on the anisotropy.

## Key claim

Using a transport-theory approach, the authors derive the static gauge boson propagator in a plasma with a general axially- and reflection-symmetric momentum distribution, confirming that non-zero magnetic screening arises from anisotropy. However, the screening or antiscreening depends on both the orientation of the boson momentum and its polarization, and antiscreening (negative screening masses) can occur for some components, reflecting the instabilities of the non-equilibrium medium.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann equation for a gas of electrons interacting with a weak electromagnetic field, linearize around an unperturbed but possibly anisotropic distribution function, and solve for the induced current. The polarization tensor is then obtained from the Fourier-transformed induced current in the static limit (k0=0), assuming massless particles and a cylindrically symmetric, reflection-symmetric momentum distribution. The resulting static polarization tensor components are expressed as integrals over momentum, and the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the spatial part are analyzed to determine screening or antiscreening behavior for different field orientations.

## Key numbers

*   The magnetic screening mass for an anisotropic plasma is given by an integral expression: \(m_g^2(\hat {\bf k})=\frac{e^2}{2}\int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3}\left[ [1 +({\bf \hat p \cdot \hat k})^2] \frac{f({\bf p})}{|{\bf p}|} + [1 -({\bf \hat p \cdot \hat k})^2] \,\frac{{\bf \hat k \cdot \nabla_{p}}f({\bf p})}{{\bf \hat k \cdot \hat p}}\right]\).
*   For an oblate momentum distribution (where \(\langle p_z^2\rangle<\frac{1}{2}\langle p_\rho^2\rangle\)), the eigenvalue \(\bar\Pi_2\) (for fields in the y-direction) is negative, indicating antiscreening.
*   For a prolate momentum distribution, the eigenvalue \(\bar\Pi_2\) is positive, indicating screening.
*   When the field momentum is perpendicular to the collision axis (kz=0), fields in the y-direction are unscreened (\(\bar\Pi_2=0\)).

## Context

This work extends the closed-time-path-integral approach of Fred (2003) for magnetic screening in anisotropic plasmas, confirming those results via a more physically transparent transport-equation method. It also agrees with the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approach of Romatschke and Strickland (2003), who assumed a more restricted functional form for the anisotropic distribution, and with the instability analyses of Mrowczynski (1998) and Arnold et al. (2003). The results enable further study of equilibration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the dynamical aspects such as damping rates and unstable modes in anisotropic systems.
