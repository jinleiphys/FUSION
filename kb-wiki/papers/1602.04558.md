# Empirical formula of crustal torsional oscillations

> Crustal torsional oscillations depend on not only crust properties but also the stellar mass and radius. Thus, one could extract stellar information by identifying the observed frequencies of stellar oscillations with the crustal torsional oscillations. Owing to the confinement of torsional oscillations inside the crust region of neutron stars, we successfully derive an empirical formula for the fundamental crustal torsional oscillations as a function of the stellar mass, radius, the so-called slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy, and the angular index of oscillations, with which one can estimate the frequencies with high accuracy. This empirical formula could be valuable in both the astrophysics and nuclear physics communities.

## Key claim

The paper derives an empirical formula for the fundamental frequencies of crustal torsional oscillations in neutron stars as a function of stellar mass, radius, the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy (L), and the angular index of oscillations (ℓ), with the hedge that the formula is valid only for the specific parameter ranges considered and that the accuracy for ℓ=2 is lower (less than 9%) than for ℓ>2 (less than a few percent).

## Method

The author constructs neutron star crust equilibrium models by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation inward from the stellar surface to the crust basis, using the phenomenological Oyamatsu-Iida equation of state (OI-EOS) parameterized by the saturation parameters K₀ and L. The fundamental torsional oscillation frequencies are computed by solving the relativistic perturbation equation with the Cowling approximation, using the canonical shear modulus for a body-centered cubic lattice, and incorporating the effect of neutron superfluidity. The resulting frequencies are then fitted sequentially as functions of L, ℓ, M, and R through a series of polynomial and linear fits to produce the final empirical formula.

## Key numbers

- The empirical formula is derived for stellar models in the ranges 1.4 ≤ M/M⊙ ≤ 1.8 and 10 ≤ R ≤ 14 km, with L in 0 ≤ L ≤ 160 MeV and K₀ in 180 ≤ K₀ ≤ 360 MeV.
- For ℓ=2, the empirical formula estimates frequencies with less than 9% accuracy across all stellar models considered.
- For ℓ>2, the empirical formula estimates frequencies with less than 3.5% accuracy independently of stellar model and L.
- For a typical neutron star model with M=1.4M⊙ and R=12 km, a -3% (+3%) error in frequency estimation yields an inverse determination of L with +6.3% (-6.1%) accuracy at L=50 MeV, and +3.7% (-3.4%) accuracy at L=100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Sotani et al. (2012, 2013a, 2013b, 2016) that showed the fundamental torsional frequencies depend primarily on L and are almost independent of K₀, but lacked a practical empirical formula combining all relevant parameters. The derived formula enables direct estimation of crustal torsional oscillation frequencies without solving the full relativistic eigenvalue problem, which could be valuable for interpreting quasiperiodic oscillations observed in soft-gamma repeaters (as reported by Israel 2005, Strohmayer and Watts 2005, 2006) and for constraining the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy. The paper notes that overtones of crustal torsional oscillations may provide information about K₀ and crust thickness, but that the pasta phase at the crust-core interface must be considered for such analyses.
