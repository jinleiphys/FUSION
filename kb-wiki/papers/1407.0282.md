# Quantum statistical calculation of cluster abundances in hot dense matter

> The cluster abundances are calculated from a quantum statistical approach taking into account in-medium corrections. For arbitrary cluster size the self-energy and Pauli blocking shifts are considered. Exploratory calculations are performed for symmetric matter at temperature $T=5$ MeV and baryon density $\varrho=0.0156$ fm$^{-3}$ to be compared with the solar element distribution. It is shown that the abundances of weakly bound nuclei with mass number $4<A<12$ are strongly suppressed due to Pauli blocking effects.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for symmetric nuclear matter at temperature \(T=5\) MeV and baryon density \(\varrho=0.0156\) fm\(^{-3}\), the abundances of weakly bound nuclei with mass number \(4<A<12\) are strongly suppressed due to Pauli blocking effects, but this result is exploratory and depends on several approximations including the use of empirical nuclear radii and density distributions.

## Method

The cluster abundances are calculated from a quantum statistical approach using a self-consistent ladder Hartree-Fock approximation for the self-energy, with the cluster energy decomposed into a Coulomb shift, a self-energy shift, and a Pauli blocking shift. For light clusters (\(A<12\)), the Pauli shift is evaluated using harmonic oscillator wave functions, while for heavier clusters (\(A\geq 12\)) a Fermi gas model is employed; the calculation also includes a Debye-Thomas-Fermi treatment of Coulomb screening and a density of states for excited nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Temperature \(T = 5\) MeV and baryon density \(\varrho = 0.0156\) fm\(^{-3}\) are the parameter values used for the exploratory calculation.
- The saturation density is given as \(\varrho_0 \approx 0.166\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The chemical potential at the chosen parameters is \(\mu/k_BT = 0.892\), and \(k_BT/\varrho \times d\varrho/d\mu = 0.668\).
- The nuclear radius parameter is \(r_0 = 1.2\) fm, used in \(R_A = r_0 A^{1/3}\).
- The single-particle self-energy shift is given by \(\Delta(\varrho) = -792.975 \varrho + 2711.9 \varrho^2 + 125.226 \varrho^{5/3}\) (units MeV, fm).
- The harmonic oscillator parameter is \(\hbar\omega = 40 A^{-1/3}\) MeV.
- The level density parameter is \(a = A/15\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- The Pauli blocking shift for large clusters uses a fitting parameter \(\delta = 20.926\) for \(P\Lambda = 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical treatment of hot nuclear matter from earlier work by Ropke (1983) and Ropke, Munster, and collaborators, which focused on light clusters (\(A \leq 4\)), to include heavier clusters up to arbitrary mass number. The results are compared with the solar element distribution and are relevant for understanding the evaporation of matter from the surface of dense hot nuclear systems; the approach contrasts with the phenomenological excluded volume concept used by Hempel (2010) for the nuclear matter equation of state.
