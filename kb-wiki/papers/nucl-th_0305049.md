# Meson and baryon elliptic flow at high pT from parton coalescence

> The large and saturating differential elliptic flow v2(pT) observed in Au+Au reactions at RHIC so far could only be explained assuming an order of magnitude denser initial parton system than estimated from perturbative QCD. Hadronization via parton coalescence can resolve this ``opacity puzzle'' because it enhances hadron elliptic flow at large pT relative to that of partons at the same transverse momentum. An experimentally testable consequence of the coalescence scenario is that v2(pT) saturates at about 50% higher values for baryons than for mesons. In addition, if strange quarks have weaker flow than light quarks, hadron v2 at high pT decreases with relative strangeness content.

# Meson and baryon elliptic flow at high pT from parton coalescence

## Key claim

Parton coalescence during hadronization can resolve the "opacity puzzle" at RHIC because it amplifies hadron elliptic flow at high transverse momentum relative to parton flow, meaning that the observed large and saturating v2(pT) can be reproduced with parton opacities only 3-6 times smaller than previously required, rather than an order of magnitude above perturbative QCD estimates.

## Method

The author applies a local parton coalescence model where hadron spectra are constructed from parton distributions assuming equal momenta for coalescing partons, yielding meson spectra proportional to the square of the quark distribution at pT/2 and baryon spectra proportional to the cube at pT/3. From these relations, the elliptic flow coefficients for mesons and baryons are derived as v2,M(pT) ≈ 2 v2,q(pT/2) and v2,B(pT) ≈ 3 v2,q(pT/3), assuming v2 is small. These analytic results are compared with preliminary numerical calculations using the MPC parton transport model for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200A GeV with b=8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Baryon elliptic flow is predicted to be about 50% higher than meson elliptic flow at high pT: v2^B ≈ 1.5 v2^M for pT > 2-3 GeV
- The charged particle v2 data from RHIC can be reproduced with parton opacities σ dN/dη(b=0) ~ 7000-15000 mb, compared to ~45000 mb required without coalescence
- After accounting for non-flow effects (15-20%), the required opacity reduces to σ dN/dη(b=0) ~ 5000-10000 mb
- For a 3 mb gg→gg cross section, this corresponds to initial parton density dN/dη(b=0) ~ 1500-3000, only 1.5-3 times above the EKRT perturbative estimate of ~1000
- The saturation of v2(pT) occurs at about 50% higher pT for baryons than for mesons
- Parton coalescence enhances v2 by a factor of 2 for mesons and 3 for baryons relative to parton v2

## Context

This work extends earlier parton transport studies (Molnar, 2002) that found quantitative agreement with RHIC v2 data required an order of magnitude enhancement of parton opacities beyond perturbative QCD estimates. The coalescence mechanism resolves this "opacity puzzle" by amplifying hadron flow relative to parton flow, a result that differs from the independent fragmentation and 1-parton-to-1-pion hadronization schemes used previously. The predictions for baryon-meson flow ordering and strangeness-dependent flow patterns can be tested against PHENIX and STAR data, and the approach enables extraction of parton species-specific elliptic flow from hadron measurements. The results contrast with those of Lin and Ko (2002), who found similar hadron and parton elliptic flow at high pT because their coalescence model did not account for the momentum dependence of coalescence probabilities.
