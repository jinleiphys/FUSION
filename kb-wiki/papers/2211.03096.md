# Geometric confinement in gauge theories

> In 1978, Friedberg and Lee introduced the phenomenological soliton bag model of hadrons, generalizing the MIT bag model developed in 1974 shortly after the formulation of QCD. In this model, quarks and gluons are confined due to coupling with a real scalar field $ρ$ which tends to zero outside some compact region $S\subset{\mathbb R}^3$ determined dynamically from the equations of motion. The gauge coupling in the soliton bag model is running as the inverse power of $ρ$ already at the semiclassical level. We show that this model arises naturally as a consequence of introducing the warped product metric ${\mathrm{d}}s^2_M + ρ^2{\mathrm{d}}s^2_G$ on the principal $G$-bundle $P(M,G)\cong M\times G$ with a non-Abelian group $G$ over Minkowski space $M={\mathbb R}^{3,1}$. Confinement of quarks and gluons in a compact domain $S\subset{\mathbb R}^3$ is a consequence of the collapse of the bundle manifold $M\times G$ to $M$ outside $S$ due to shrinking of the group manifold $G$ to a point. We describe the formation of such regions $S$ as a dynamical process controlled by the order parameter field $ρ$.

## Key claim

The Friedberg-Lee soliton bag model of hadrons, which phenomenologically introduces a scalar field to confine quarks and gluons, is shown to arise naturally from the geometry of a principal bundle with a warped product metric, rather than being an ad hoc construction; confinement is a consequence of the collapse of the bundle manifold \(M\times G\) to \(M\) outside a compact region \(S\) due to shrinking of the group manifold \(G\) to a point, with the scalar field \(\rho\) acting as an order parameter controlling this dynamical process.

## Method

The author constructs a principal bundle \(\widetilde P = M \times \widetilde G\) over Minkowski space \(M = \mathbb{R}^{3,1}\) with structure group \(\widetilde G = \mathbb{R}^+ \times G\) (the conformal extension of a non-Abelian group \(G = \mathrm{SU}(N)\)), and introduces a warped product metric \(\mathrm{d}s^2_M + \rho^2 \mathrm{d}s^2_G\) on this bundle. A connection \(\widetilde{\mathcal{A}} = \mathcal{A} + a \mathbb{1}_N\) on \(\widetilde P\) is defined, and the associated vector bundle \(\widetilde E\) is endowed with a metric on its fibres that is conformal to the standard Hermitian metric by the factor \(\rho^2\). The Lagrangian for gauge fields, scalars, and fermions is then derived by acting on the standard Yang-Mills and mass terms with the conformal frame \(\Phi = \rho^{-1}\phi \in \widetilde{\mathcal{G}}\), yielding expressions that depend on \(\rho\) and the connection, and the dynamics of the scalar field \(\rho\) is described as an order parameter for the formation of the confining region \(S\).

## Key numbers

* The gauge coupling in the soliton bag model runs as the inverse power of \(\rho\) at the semiclassical level, with the effective coupling given by \(1/g^2_{\text{eff}} = (1/g^2_{\text{YM}})\,\rho^2\) (from the transformation of the Yang-Mills Lagrangian in Eq. 4.2).
* The mass term in the Lagrangian includes a parameter \(v_* \ge 0\) and a mass \(m = v_* g_*\) (from Eq. 4.3 and surrounding text).
* The metric on the group \(\widetilde G\) is \(\mathrm{d}s^2_{\widetilde G} = \sigma_0^2 (\mathrm{d}\rho^2 + \rho^2 \mathrm{d}s^2_G)\) with constant factor \(\sigma_0^2\) (Eq. 2.25), and \(\sigma_0^{-1}\) is identified with the gauge coupling constant \(g_* \equiv g_{\text{YM}} > 0\) (from text near Eq. 4.1).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological soliton bag model of Friedberg and Lee (1978) and the earlier MIT bag model (1974) by providing a geometric derivation from a principal bundle with a warped product metric, rather than postulating confinement via boundary conditions or an ad hoc scalar field. It contrasts with other confinement models (e.g., those reviewed by Greensite) that have not produced satisfactory results for QCD over approximately 50 years. The approach enables a description of bag-type Lagrangians derived from bundle geometry, with confinement emerging as a consequence of the collapse of the group manifold, and it suggests that the scalar field \(\rho\) can be treated as a dynamical order parameter for the formation of confining regions.
