# Norm kernels and the closeness relation for Pauli-allowed basis functions

> The norm kernel of the generator-coordinate method is shown to be a symmetric kernel of an integral equation with eigenfunctions defined in the Fock--Bargmann space and forming a complete set of orthonormalized states (classified with the use of SU(3) symmetry indices) satisfying the Pauli exclusion principle. This interpretation allows to develop a method which, even in the presence of the SU(3) degeneracy, provides for a consistent way to introduce additional quantum numbers for the classification of the basis states. In order to set the asymptotic boundary conditions for the expansion coefficients of a wave function in the SU(3) basis, a complementary basis of functions with partial angular momenta as good quantum numbers is needed. Norm kernels of the binary systems 6He+p, 6He+n, 6He+4He, and 8He+4He are considered in detail.

## Key claim

The norm kernel of the generator-coordinate method can be interpreted as a symmetric kernel of a Fredholm integral equation of the second kind in the Fock-Bargmann space, whose eigenfunctions form a complete orthonormalized set of Pauli-allowed basis states; this interpretation provides a consistent way to introduce additional quantum numbers for classification even in the presence of SU(3) degeneracy, but a complementary basis with partial angular momenta as good quantum numbers is needed to set asymptotic boundary conditions for wave function expansion coefficients.

## Method

The authors construct norm kernels for two-cluster systems using modified Brink orbitals in the Fock-Bargmann representation, where the generator parameters are complex vectors. They expand the norm kernel in a Hilbert-Schmidt series over SU(3) irreducible representations, obtaining eigenvalues and eigenfunctions without directly solving the integral equation. For systems with SU(3) degeneracy, they project the norm kernel onto states with definite SU(3) symmetry using Casimir operators, and then perform unitary transformations to a "physical" basis where partial angular momenta of clusters and relative motion are good quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- For the $^8$Be ($\alpha+\alpha$) system: eigenvalues $\lambda_{2n,l,m}=1-4/2^{2n}$ for the norm kernel, with $n$ the total number of oscillator quanta
- For the $^6$He$+p$ system: eigenvalues $\lambda_n=1-(-1/6)^n$ for $n\geq1$, with $\lambda_1=1.1667$, $\lambda_2=0.9722$, $\lambda_3=1.0046$, $\lambda_4=0.9992$, $\lambda_5=1.0001$, and $\lambda_n=1.0000$ for $n\geq6$
- For the $^6$He$+n$ system: eigenvalues depend on SU(3) irrep; for $n=2$ (even), $\lambda_{(4,0)}=1.3611$, $\lambda_{(2,1)}=0.9722$, $\lambda_{(0,2)}=0.7778$; for $n=3$ (odd), $\lambda_{(5,0)}=0.9074$, $\lambda_{(3,1)}=0.9884$, $\lambda_{(1,2)}=1.0370$
- Dimensionality of SU(3) irrep $(\lambda,\mu)$: $\dim[\lambda\mu]=(\lambda+1)(\mu+1)(\lambda+\mu+2)/2$
- For $^6$He$+p$: total number of states for given $n$ equals $3(n+1)(n+2)$

## Context

This work extends the treatment of norm kernels by Horiuchi (1970) and Horiuchi and Fujiwara, who showed that diagonalization of the norm kernel requires labeling with SU(3) quantum numbers $(\lambda,\mu)$. The authors build on Saito's (1977) treatment of the $^8$Be system and Elliott's SU(3) model, developing a method that avoids explicit use of SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The approach enables analysis of norm kernels for neutron-rich systems such as $^6$He$+p$, $^6$He$+n$, $^6$He$+^4$He, and $^8$He$+^4$He, which are relevant to scattering experiments with radioactive nuclear beams. The method provides a foundation for the algebraic version of the resonating-group method (AV RGM) and for formulating asymptotic boundary conditions in continuum calculations.
