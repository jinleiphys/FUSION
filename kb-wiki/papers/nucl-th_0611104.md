# High temperature phase of QCD

> I give a brief overview of our present understanding of the high temperature phase of QCD, trying to clarify some of the theoretical issues involved in the current discussions that emphasize the strongly coupled character of the quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC.

# High temperature phase of QCD

## Key claim

The quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC may appear strongly coupled not because the gauge coupling is large, but because many quasi-degenerate active degrees of freedom and a hierarchy of thermal fluctuation scales complicate the theoretical description; weak-coupling techniques can be reorganized to provide smooth extrapolations toward the strong-coupling regime, and the entropy density at T ~ 3Tc lies halfway between its weak-coupling and strong-coupling limits, so neither expansion is clearly favored at that temperature.

## Method

The paper reviews the perturbative expansion of the QCD pressure to order g^6 ln g, the construction of dimensionally reduced effective theories for the infrared sector, and the use of Φ-derivable two-loop approximations (2PI formalism) to compute the entropy density from massive quasiparticles with residual interactions. It also presents functional renormalization group (fRG) calculations in the local potential approximation for an O(N) scalar φ^4 theory as a test case, comparing the flow of the coupling and the pressure with perturbative and 2PI results.

## Key numbers

- The entropy density in the strong-coupling limit of supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory is S/S0 = 3/4 + (45/32) ζ(3) / λ^(3/2), where λ ≡ 2 g^2 N_c and S0 is the non-interacting entropy; as λ → ∞, S/S0 → 3/4.
- At T ≃ 3 Tc, the entropy density of pure-glue SU(3) gauge theory is halfway between its weak-coupling and strong-coupling values.
- The gauge coupling just above Tc is g ≃ 2.
- The Debye screening mass is m_D ∼ g T.
- The pressure in the fRG-LPA calculation for scalar φ^4 theory is compared with perturbative results at order g^2 and g^3, and with a simple 2PI approximation, for coupling values up to g(2πT) = 1.1.

## Context

This review extends the standard picture of the high-temperature QCD phase, where asymptotic freedom predicts an ideal gas of quarks and gluons (Collins 1974, Cabibbo 1975), by addressing the strongly coupled behavior observed at RHIC (Arsene 2004, Back 2004, Adcox 2004, Adams 2005). It contrasts perturbative calculations that oscillate wildly for not-too-small couplings (Blaizot 2003) with strong-coupling results from gauge-gravity duality (Gubser 1998) and with lattice data (Boyd 1996, Karsch 2001). The work enables further development of resummation schemes and effective theories, such as screened perturbation theory (Andersen, Braaten, Strickland 1999, 2002) and the 2PI entropy approach (Blaizot 1999, 2000), to describe the quark-gluon plasma in the temperature range Tc ≲ T ≲ 3 Tc, where the physics remains poorly understood and open questions include the role of bound states, Polyakov-loop effective theories (Pisarski 2000, Vuorinen 2006, Ratti 2005), and the nature of charge carriers (Ejiri 2005, Gavai 2005).
