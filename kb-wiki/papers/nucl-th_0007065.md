# Collective Evolution of Hot QCD Matter from the QGP to Freeze-Out

> We present results on the evolution of <p_t> with energy, for hadrons emerging from hadronization of a quark-gluon plasma possibly produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We find that BNL-RHIC energy corresponds to the previously predicted plateau of <p_t>, reminiscent of the assumed first-order QCD phase transition. Heavy hadrons are the best messengers of the transverse flow stall.

# Collective Evolution of Hot QCD Matter from the QGP to Freeze-Out

## Key claim

The paper predicts that BNL-RHIC energy corresponds to a plateau of average transverse momentum ⟨p_t⟩ for charged hadrons, reminiscent of a first-order QCD phase transition, but this result depends on the assumption that a phase mixture (mixed phase) is applicable to high-energy collisions and on the specific modeling of the post-hadronization stage via microscopic transport rather than pure hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors model the expansion of a quark-gluon plasma using relativistic hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition, solving the continuity equation for the energy-momentum tensor in the QGP and mixed phase. On the hadronization hypersurface, they switch to the relativistic Boltzmann equation with a collision kernel based on known hadronic cross sections, treating almost all hadrons from the Particle Data Group explicitly. The initial conditions for CERN-SPS energy are set at proper time τ_i = 1 fm with entropy per net baryon s/n_B = 45, while for BNL-RHIC energy (√s = 200A GeV) they use τ_i = 0.6 fm and s/n_B = 205.

## Key numbers

- For CERN-SPS energy (√s = 18A GeV), the initial condition uses τ_i = 1 fm proper time hypersurface and entropy per net baryon s/n_B = 45
- For BNL-RHIC energy (√s = 200A GeV), the parameters are τ_i = 0.6 fm and s/n_B = 205, yielding dN_ch/dy ≈ 800 at y = 0
- The QCD phase transition temperature is roughly T ∼ 200 MeV
- The freeze-out volume of pions at central rapidity increases linearly with multiplicity, with a "hollow" volume V_0 that increases with p_t

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of the QCD phase transition by Kataja, Ruuskanen, McLerran, and von Gersdorff (1986) and by Blaizot and Ollitrault (1987), which predicted a plateau in ⟨p_t⟩ but suffered from simplifications such as overestimating the latent heat by considering only pions in the hadronic phase and neglecting resonance decays. The authors improve upon these studies by coupling hydrodynamics to microscopic transport via the Boltzmann equation, following their own prior work Dumitru, Bass, Bleicher, Stöcker, and Greiner (1999) and Bass and Dumitru (2000). The results enable predictions for the LHC energy (√s = 5500A GeV) where very large ⟨p_t⟩ is expected if initial energy densities follow saturation models, while RHIC sits in the predicted plateau region.
