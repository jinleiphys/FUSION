# Alberg and Miller Reply to arXiv:arXiv:1206.3671

> In a recent Comment [arXiv:1206.3671] on our calculation of the pion contributions to the self-energy of the nucleon [arXiv:1201.4184], Ji, Melnitchouk and Thomas (JMT) correctly state that we obtain the same result as given by the pseudovector (PV) theory. We point out that this result is expected by the equivalence theorem, since our pion-nucleon effective theory puts the intermediate nucleon on its mass shell. We make no claim that pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector interactions are equivalent in general. JMT also argue that our theory will not give the correct PV result for the pion momentum distribution $f^N_π$. We note that the discrepancies are much smaller than the uncertainties due to $πN$ form factors. To summarize, nothing in the Comment by JMT changes the conclusions or numerical results of our work in any substantive way.

## Key claim

The authors maintain that their original calculation of the pion contribution to the nucleon self-energy is correct, and that the criticism by Ji, Melnitchouk, and Thomas (JMT) does not change any conclusions or numerical results. The key hedge is that the equivalence between pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) theories holds only when the intermediate nucleon is placed on its mass shell, and the authors make no claim of general equivalence.

## Method

The authors use a light-front formulation of the pion-nucleon effective theory with PV interactions, employing the identity for the nucleon propagator that separates an on-shell Dirac light-front spinor term from a contact term. They show that the contact term vanishes when form factors are included, by performing a principal value integration over the light-front momentum \(k^+\). The on-shell intermediate nucleon allows the use of experimentally measured \(\pi N\) form factors.

## Key numbers

- The self-energy \(\Sigma_\pi\) is linearly dependent on the pion mass \(\mu\) for larger values, consistent with lattice QCD calculations, and the chiral limit is not yet numerically relevant for current QCD lattice calculations.
- For the pion momentum distribution \(f_\pi^N\), the effect discussed by JMT increases a term that has a very small contribution by a factor of 4/3, but this is much smaller than the uncertainty from \(\pi N\) form factors that are not constrained by measurements.

## Context

This reply addresses a Comment by Ji, Melnitchouk, and Thomas (2012) on the authors' earlier calculation (Alberg and Miller, 2012) of pion contributions to the nucleon self-energy. The authors argue that their result is expected from the equivalence theorem and that the discrepancies JMT raise are negligible compared to form factor uncertainties. The work of Thomas (2000) and Detmold (2001) is cited as showing the small size of the effect on the pion distribution. The reply enables continued use of the authors' approach for connecting chiral perturbation theory to lattice QCD results.
