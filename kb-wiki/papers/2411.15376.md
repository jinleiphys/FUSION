# Kaon Distribution Functions from Empirical Information

> Using available information from Drell-Yan data on pion and kaon structure functions, an approach is described which enables the development of pointwise profiles for all pion and kaon parton distribution functions (DFs) without reference to theories of hadron structure. The key steps are construction of structure-function-constrained probability-weighted ensembles of valence DF replicas and use of an evolution scheme for parton DFs that is all-orders exact. The DFs obtained express qualitatively sound features of light-meson structure, e.g., the effects of Higgs boson couplings into QCD and the size of heavy-quark momentum fractions in light hadrons. In order to improve the results, additional and more precise data on the $u$-quark-in-kaon, $u^K$, to $u$-quark-in-pion, $u^π$, DF ratio would be necessary. Of greater value would be extraction of $u^K$ alone, thereby avoiding inference from the ratio: currently, the data-based form of $u^K$ is materially influenced by results for $u^π$.

## Key claim

Using Drell-Yan data on pion and kaon structure functions, the authors construct data-driven pointwise profiles for all pion and kaon parton distribution functions (DFs) without reference to theories of hadron structure, but note that the data-based form of the u-quark-in-kaon DF is materially influenced by results for the u-quark-in-pion DF, and additional more precise data on the u-quark-in-kaon to u-quark-in-pion ratio would be necessary to improve results.

## Method

The authors construct structure-function-constrained probability-weighted ensembles of valence DF replicas using least-squares minimization on E615 Drell-Yan data for the pion and Badier (1980) data for the kaon-to-pion ratio, then employ an all-orders exact evolution scheme (the AO scheme from Yin (2023)) to evolve DFs between the hadron scale (zeta_H = 0.331(2) GeV) and empirical scales (zeta_2 = 2 GeV, zeta_5 = 5.2 GeV). The valence DFs at the hadron scale are parameterized using a functional form that respects predicted endpoint behavior and expresses both emergent hadron mass (EHM)-induced dilation and Higgs-boson-coupling-induced skewing, with parameters determined by fitting Mellin moments of the evolved replicas.

## Key numbers

* Pion valence u-quark first moment at zeta_5: <x>_{u^pi}^{zeta_5} = 0.207(4), matching the CSM prediction of 0.20(2) from Cui (2020) and the lattice QCD average of 0.214(25)
* Kaon-to-pion u-quark DF ratio endpoint values at zeta_5: 1.050(24) at x->0 and 0.77(11) at x->1
* Hadron-scale parameters from Eq. (qDF1): rho_pi = 0.051(15), rho_K = 0.054(13), gamma_K = 0.23(12)
* Kaon valence u-quark first moment at hadron scale: <x>_{u^K}^{zeta_H} = 0.473(13) from method (a) and 0.477(12) from method (b)
* Kaon valence u-quark momentum fraction at zeta_2: 0.230(6)(10); at zeta_5: 0.197(5)(9)
* Kaon glue momentum fraction at zeta_2: 0.428(18); at zeta_5: 0.461(20)
* Kaon strange valence quark momentum fraction at zeta_2: 0.252(6)(11); at zeta_5: 0.216(5)(9)
* Kaon u-quark DF peak location at hadron scale: x = 0.42(4), compared to pion peak at x = 1/2

## Context

This work extends the all-orders evolution scheme developed by Yin (2023) and the continuum Schwinger method predictions for meson DFs from Cui (2020) by using actual Drell-Yan data (E615 from Conway (1989) and Badier (1980)) to produce data-driven ensembles rather than purely theoretical predictions. The results enable future comparisons with anticipated high-luminosity accelerator measurements of kaon structure (Roberts 2021, Chen 2020, Arrington 2021) and provide benchmarks for contemporary phenomenology of Nambu-Goldstone boson structure, including lattice QCD studies such as Alexandrou (2024) and the analysis of Higgs boson couplings into QCD effects on light-quark sectors from Cui (2020).
