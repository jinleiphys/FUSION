# Multi-channel phase-equivalent transformation and supersymmetry

> Phase-equivalent transformation of local interaction is generalized to the multi-channel case. Generally, the transformation does not change the number of the bound states in the system and their energies. However, with a special choice of the parameters, the transformation removes one of the bound states and is equivalent to the multi-channel supersymmetry transformation recently suggested by Sparenberg and Baye. Using the transformation, it is also possible to add a bound state to the discrete spectrum of the system at a given energy $E<0$ if the angular momentum at least in one of the coupled channels $l\ge 2$.

# Multi-channel phase-equivalent transformation and supersymmetry

## Key claim
The authors generalize the single-channel phase-equivalent transformation of local interactions to the multi-channel case, producing a transformation that depends on continuous parameters and can either preserve all bound states or, with a special choice of parameters, remove one bound state (equivalent to the multi-channel supersymmetry transformation of Sparenberg and Baye). The transformation can also add a bound state at a given energy E<0, but only if the angular momentum in at least one coupled channel is l ≥ 2.

## Method
Starting from the multi-channel Schrödinger equation with a Hermitian potential matrix V_{ij}(r), the authors define a transformed potential \tilde{V}_{ij}(r) = V_{ij}(r) + v_{ij}(r) where v_{ij}(r) is constructed from the bound-state wave function at energy E_0 and three real parameters A, C, and a. They show that the resulting wave functions satisfy an inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation, and that by choosing a=0 or a=∞ the right-hand side vanishes, yielding a homogeneous equation and thus a phase-equivalent transformation. The supersymmetry case corresponds to C=1, a=∞, A=1, which removes the bound state; the inverse supersymmetry case uses a wave function irregular at the origin to add a bound state when l ≥ 2.

## Key numbers
- The transformation removes a bound state when parameters are set to C=1, a=∞, A=1 (the supersymmetry limit).
- The inverse supersymmetry transformation can add a bound state at a given energy E<0 only if the maximum angular momentum L = max{l_i} ≥ 2.
- For L ≤ 1, the transformation with an irregular wave function produces a "transparent" potential with phase shift δ=0 at all energies, without adding a bound state.
- The bound-state wave function after transformation (a=0 case) is given by \tilde{φ}_i(E_0,r) = √[A(A+C)] φ_i(E_0,r) / (A + C Σ_j ∫_0^r |φ_j(E_0,s)|^2 ds), with normalization constant √[(A+C)/A].

## Context
This work extends the single-channel phase-equivalent transformation of Newton (inverse scattering theory) to multi-channel systems, and generalizes the multi-channel supersymmetry transformation of Sparenberg and Baye (1999) by introducing continuous parameters A and C. The transformation enables fine-tuning of two-body interactions for few-body calculations, addressing the known failure of realistic NN potentials (e.g., Nijmegen potential, Moscow potential) to reproduce triton binding energy without three-body forces, as studied by Picklesimer et al. The method can be applied to coupled partial waves in NN scattering, nucleon-cluster, and cluster-cluster systems, and is complementary to the non-local phase-equivalent transformations used by Ivanov and Sitnikov (1998) and in ⁶He cluster-model calculations.
