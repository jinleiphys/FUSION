# Fusion versus Breakup: Observation of Large Fusion Suppression for ^9Be + ^{208}Pb

> Complete fusion excitation functions for $^{9}$Be + $^{208}$Pb have been measured to high precision at near barrier energies. The experimental fusion barrier distribution extracted from these data allows reliable prediction of the expected complete fusion cross-sections. However, the measured cross-sections are only 68% of those predicted. The large cross-sections observed for incomplete fusion products support the interpretation that this suppression of fusion is caused by $^{9}$Be breaking up into charged fragments before reaching the fusion barrier. Implications for the fusion of radioactive nuclei are discussed.

# Fusion versus Breakup: Observation of Large Fusion Suppression for ^9Be + ^{208}Pb

## Key claim

Complete fusion of ^9Be with ^{208}Pb is suppressed to 68% of the cross-section predicted by coupled channels calculations that correctly reproduce the measured fusion barrier distribution, with the suppression attributed to breakup of ^9Be into charged fragments before reaching the fusion barrier. The suppression factor of 0.68 has an uncertainty of ±0.07 arising from the uncertainty in the mean barrier energy, and at above-barrier energies there is no evidence for an energy dependence of the suppression factor, though a weak dependence cannot be excluded.

## Method

Complete fusion excitation functions for ^9Be + ^{208}Pb were measured with high precision using pulsed ^9Be beams from the 14UD tandem accelerator at the Australian National University, covering bombarding energies from 35.0 to 51.0 MeV. Fusion evaporation residues were identified by their distinctive alpha-energies and half-lives (270 ns to 138 days) using catcher foils and silicon detectors, while fission following fusion was measured using position-sensitive multi-wire proportional counters. The experimental fusion barrier distribution was extracted from the cross-section data using a point-difference formula with a center-of-mass energy step of 1.92 MeV, and coupled channels calculations including rotational states in ^9Be and octupole phonon states in ^{208}Pb were performed to predict the expected fusion cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Measured complete fusion cross-sections are only 68% of those predicted by coupled channels calculations that reproduce the average barrier position and barrier distribution shape
- The average barrier position obtained from the experimental barrier distribution is 38.3 ± 0.6 MeV
- The suppression factor of 0.68 has an uncertainty of ±0.07 arising from the uncertainty in the mean barrier energy
- The ^9Be beam energy range was 35.0 to 51.0 MeV
- The center-of-mass energy step used for the barrier distribution extraction was 1.92 MeV
- The ^9Be + ^{208}Pb reaction produces the compound nucleus ^{217}Rn, which cools dominantly by neutron evaporation (2n, 3n, 4n, and 5n channels)
- For the control reaction ^{13}C + ^{204}Hg forming the same compound nucleus, ^{211,212}Po alpha-decay cross-sections were <5 mb, compared with ~160 mb total for the ^9Be + ^{208}Pb reaction
- The two most favourable charged fragmentation channels for ^9Be are: ^9Be → n + 2α (Q = -1.57 MeV) and ^9Be → α + ^5He (Q = -2.47 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the study of fusion suppression due to breakup, building on prior measurements of fusion for ^9,11Be + ^{238}U (Fekou-Youimbi 1995) and ^9,10,11Be + ^{209}Bi (Yoshida 1996), where the role of breakup was unclear because the stable ^9Be was used as a reference. The precise barrier distribution technique, previously applied successfully to tightly bound projectiles (Leigh 1995; Morton 1994; Stefanini 1995), here reveals that ^9Be breakup into charged fragments causes a substantial suppression of complete fusion. The finding that ^9Be shows more significant breakup effects than the radioactive ^11Be (which has a lower neutron separation energy of 0.50 MeV versus 1.67 MeV for ^9Be) contradicts expectations based solely on neutron separation energy (Signorini 1997) and explains why earlier measurements (Signorini 1998) did not show the anticipated suppression for ^11Be. This work enables a complete theoretical description of fusion and breakup and is favourable for using radioactive beams at near-barrier energies to form new neutron-rich nuclei.
