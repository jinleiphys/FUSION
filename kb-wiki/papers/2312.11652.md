# Wee partons in QCD and gravity: double copy and universality

> We discuss a quantitative "double copy" between radiation from shockwave collisions in Einstein gravity and in QCD. The correspondence extends to $2\rightarrow N$ amplitudes in Regge asymptotics. The classicalization and unitarization of these amplitudes at maximal occupancy, corresponding to black hole and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) states respectively, are described by the emergent Goldstone dynamics of wee partons. We outline some consequences of the universal dynamics on both sides of the correspondence.

# Wee partons in QCD and gravity: double copy and universality

## Key claim
The paper argues for a quantitative "double copy" correspondence between radiation from shockwave collisions in Einstein gravity and in QCD, extending to 2 to N amplitudes in Regge asymptotics. The authors claim that the classicalization and unitarization of these amplitudes at maximal occupancy, corresponding to black hole and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) states respectively, are described by emergent Goldstone dynamics that is universal at the critical boundary where the interaction strength times occupancy equals one.

## Method
The authors compare the Lipatov vertex for gravitational 2 to 3 scattering amplitudes with the QCD Lipatov vertex, showing that the gravitational vertex can be expressed as a combination of the QCD vertex and the QED soft photon bremsstrahlung vertex. They compute the inclusive gravitational wave field produced in collisions of shockwaves in Einstein gravity and show it matches the QCD radiation field under a classical double copy replacement of color charges by momenta and coupling constants. The Goldstone dynamics of wee partons at criticality is then analyzed by comparing the decay timescales, entropy relations, and coupling structures of black holes and CGC states.

## Key numbers
- The QCD Lipatov vertex is obtained from solutions of Yang-Mills equations where the radiation field is given by A^mu,a(k) = -(g^3/k^2) integral (d^2 q_2/(2pi)^2) (rho_A(q_1)/q_1^2) (rho_B(q_2)/q_2^2) T^b T^c i f^abc C^mu(q_1, q_2)
- The gravitational wave field is A^mu,nu(k) = (kappa^3 s)/(2 k^2) integral (d^2 q_2/(2pi)^2) (rho_A(q_1)/q_1^2) (rho_B(q_2)/q_2^2) Gamma^mu,nu(q_1, q_2), where kappa is proportional to Newton's constant and s is the squared center-of-mass energy
- In QCD, the multiplicity of wee gluons grows as approximately e^(lambda y), where y is rapidity and lambda is the growth rate
- For a 4-D black hole, N = M_BH^2 / M_Planck^2, where M_BH is black hole mass and M_Planck is the Planck mass
- The Schwarzschild radius R_S = 1/Q_S recovers parametrically R_S = 2 G M_BH
- The Goldstone decay constant in QCD is f_G^2 = (R_S partial_- A^a)(R_S partial_+ A^a) ~ N/R_S^2
- The lifetime of the classical lump in terms of Goldstone scattering rate is tau_G = (Gamma_G)^(-1) = (sigma n N)^(-1) = (alpha_S Q_S)^(-1), where sigma = alpha_S^2/f_G^2, n = N Q_S^3, and N = 1/alpha_S at criticality

## Context
This work extends the color-kinematics duality discovered by Bern, Carrasco and Johansson (BCJ) and the earlier double copy relation for Regge asymptotics derived by Lipatov. It builds on the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (CGC EFT) for QCD and the Black Hole N-Portrait (BHNP) of Dvali and collaborators, which describes black holes as self-bound overoccupied states of wee gravitons. The paper suggests that strong field techniques developed for the Regge regime of high occupancies in QCD may have quantitative correspondence to similar dynamics in gravity, and that the Goldstone dynamics of both systems is universal at criticality. This enables future work on understanding black hole states as fixed points of an analogous renormalization group equation at a critical impact parameter, and extracting variations in gravitational wave spectra from close black hole encounters at future observatories.
