# Implication of shell quenching in scandium isotopes around N=20

> Shell closure structures are commonly observed phenomena associated with nuclear charge radii throughout the nuclide chart. Inspired by recent studies demonstrating that the abrupt change can be clearly observed in the charge radii of the scandium isotopic chain across the neutron number $N=20$, we further review the underlying mechanism of the enlarged charge radii for $^{42}$Sc based on the covariant density functional theory. The pairing correlations are tackled by solving the state-dependent Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer equations. Meanwhile, the neutron-proton correlation around the Fermi surface derived from the simultaneously unpaired proton and neutron is appropriately considered in describing the systematic evolution of nuclear charge radii. The calculated results suggest that the abrupt increase in charge radii across the $N=20$ shell closure seems to be improved along the scandium isotopic chain if the strong neutron-proton correlation is properly included.

## Key claim

The abrupt increase in charge radii from $^{41}$Sc to $^{42}$Sc across the $N=20$ shell closure can be better reproduced if the strong neutron-proton correlation from the simultaneously unpaired neutron and proton is properly included, but the model still underestimates the charge radius of $^{42}$Sc, suggesting that the neutron-proton correlation around the Fermi surface is significantly underestimated in the current calculations.

## Method

The authors employ covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the NL3 and PK1 effective forces, solving state-dependent Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer equations for pairing correlations. They use a modified mean-square charge radii formula that includes a phenomenological term for the neutron-proton correlation derived from the simultaneously unpaired neutron and proton, with parameters calibrated to potassium and calcium isotopes. Various blocking configurations of the last unpaired proton and neutron in $^{42}$Sc are examined by projecting angular momentum components of the 1d$_{3/2}$ and 1f$_{7/2}$ subshells, and the quadrupole deformation is constrained in self-consistent iterations.

## Key numbers

- Charge radius of $^{40}$Sc: 3.514 $\pm$ 0.025 fm (from collinear laser spectroscopy, PhysRevLett.131.102501)
- Charge radius of $^{41}$Sc: 3.503 $\pm$ 0.020 fm (from collinear laser spectroscopy, PhysRevLett.131.102501)
- Charge radius of $^{42}$Sc: 3.557 $\pm$ 0.014 fm (from Avgoulea_2011)
- Quadrupole deformation $\beta_{20}$ for $^{42}$Sc: about -0.07 (almost spherical shape)
- Pairing strength: $V_{0}=350$ MeV fm$^{3}$ for NL3 set
- Calibrated parameters in charge radii formula: $a_{0}=0.561$, $\delta=0.355$ (from fitting potassium and calcium isotopes under NL3)
- Proton radius value used: 0.84 fm

## Context

This work extends the recent experimental finding by PhysRevLett.131.102501 that charge radii of $^{40,41}$Sc show a pronounced kink at $N=20$, which is absent in neighboring Ca, K, and Ar isotopic chains. The authors build on their earlier model (PhysRevC.109.064302) that incorporated neutron-proton correlation for simultaneously unpaired nucleons, but found that the charge radius of $^{42}$Sc was underestimated. The present study examines blocking configurations and finds that even with enhanced neutron-proton correlation terms (up to $4\delta/\sqrt{A}$), the experimental charge radius of $^{42}$Sc remains underestimated, indicating that beyond-mean-field corrections or more microscopic treatments of the neutron-proton correlation (such as wave function overlap as suggested by MILLER2019360) are needed.
