# Effect of ground-state deformation on the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance and the first observation of overtones of the Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance in rare-earth Nd isotopes

> The strength distributions of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) and Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance (ISGQR) in 142,146-150Nd have been determined via inelastic alpha-particle scattering with the Grand Raiden (GR) Spectrometer at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP), Japan. In the deformed nuclei 146-150Nd, the ISGMR strength distributions exhibit a splitting into two components, while the nearly spherical nucleus 142Nd displays a single peak in the ISGMR strength distribution. A noteworthy achievement in this study is the first-time detection of overtones in the Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance (ISGQR) strength distributions within Nd isotopes at an excitation energy around 25 MeV obtained through Multipole Decomposition Analysis (MDA).

## Key claim

The ISGMR strength distribution splits into two components in deformed Nd isotopes (146-150Nd) due to coupling with the K=0 component of the ISGQR, while the nearly spherical 142Nd shows a single peak; the first observation of overtone modes of the ISGQR in Nd isotopes is reported at excitation energies around 25 MeV, but the authors note that further experimental evidence in other nuclei is needed to corroborate these findings.

## Method

Inelastic alpha-particle scattering at 386 MeV was performed on 142,146,148,150Nd targets using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at RCNP, Japan, over laboratory angles 0 to 10 degrees. After particle identification, instrumental background subtraction, and removal of hydrogen and oxygen contaminants, Multipole Decomposition Analysis (MDA) was applied to extract strength distributions for the ISGMR and ISGQR using Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations with optical-model parameters derived from elastic scattering on 142Nd. The ISGMR strength distributions were fitted with a single Lorentzian for 142Nd and double-Lorentzian functions for 146-150Nd, while the ISGQR strength distributions were fitted with double-Lorentzian functions over 10-30 MeV to separate maintone and overtone components.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR centroid energy for 142Nd: 15.3 ± 0.1 MeV, width 3.3 ± 0.2 MeV, exhausting 103.9% of EWSR
- ISGMR low-energy component in 146Nd: 11.5 ± 0.4 MeV, width 3.0 ± 0.8 MeV, 17.6% EWSR; high-energy component: 15.3 ± 0.2 MeV, width 3.0 ± 0.3 MeV, 67.8% EWSR
- ISGMR low-energy component in 150Nd: 12.2 ± 0.4 MeV, width 3.8 ± 1.0 MeV, 27.6% EWSR; high-energy component: 15.6 ± 0.2 MeV, width 4.0 ± 0.4 MeV, 90.9% EWSR
- ISGQR overtone mode centroid in 142Nd: 25.5 ± 0.4 MeV, width 10.5 ± 1.3 MeV, 78.8% EWSR
- ISGQR overtone mode centroid in 146Nd: 25.9 ± 0.4 MeV, width 9.3 ± 1.1 MeV, 77.8% EWSR
- ISGQR overtone mode centroid in 150Nd: 25.7 ± 0.4 MeV, width 8.7 ± 1.1 MeV, 70.7% EWSR
- ISGQR maintone mode centroid in 142Nd: 12.4 ± 0.1 MeV, width 6.3 ± 0.2 MeV, 109.6% EWSR
- Beam energy: 386 MeV; energy resolution: approximately 175 keV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isoscalar giant resonances in deformed nuclei, particularly measurements in Sm isotopes by Itoh et al. (2002, 2003) and earlier alpha-scattering on Nd isotopes at 129 MeV by Garg et al. (1984) where strength distributions were not extracted. The results agree with QRPA calculations using Skyrme energy density functionals by Yoshida (2013) and Kvasil et al. (2016). The first observation of quadrupole overtone modes in Nd isotopes follows the initial detection of such modes in proton decay of 208Pb by Hunyadi et al. (2003) and provides a third compression mode alongside the ISGMR and ISGDR, enabling further determination of nuclear incompressibility from centroid energies of these overtone modes.
