# Pair decay width of the Hoyle state and carbon production in stars

> Electron scattering off the first excited 0+ state in 12C (the Hoyle state) has been performed at low momentum transfers at the S-DALINAC. The new data together with a novel model-independent analysis of the world data set covering a wide momentum transfer range result in a highly improved transition charge density from which a pair decay width Gamma_pi = (62.3 +- 2.0) micro-eV of the Hoyle state was extracted reducing the uncertainty of the literature values by more than a factor of three. A precise knowledge of Gamma_pi is mandatory for quantitative studies of some key issues in the modeling of supernovae and of asymptotic giant branch stars, the most likely site of the slow-neutron nucleosynthesis process.

## Key claim
The pair decay width of the Hoyle state is extracted as Gamma_pi = (62.3 ± 2.0) micro-eV, reducing the uncertainty of literature values by more than a factor of three, but this result depends on a novel model-independent global fit that is shown to be less prone to systematic errors than a Fourier-Bessel analysis, which gave a significantly lower value of 52.0 ± 1.4 micro-eV.

## Method
Inelastic electron scattering on 12C was performed at the S-DALINAC at beam energies between 29 MeV and 78 MeV and scattering angles from 69° to 141°, covering momentum transfers from 0.21 fm^-1 to 0.66 fm^-1. The new data were combined with the world data set up to q = 3.1 fm^-1 and analyzed using a model-independent ansatz for the transition form factor as a Gaussian times a polynomial in q^2, with a PWBA transformation to correct for Coulomb distortion. The transition radius <r^2>_tr was extracted from the global fit and used to compute Gamma_pi via the relation Gamma_pi = C(Z,E_x) * (8 alpha^2)/(27 pi) * B(E_x)/(hbar c)^4 * F(E_x) * <r^2>_tr.

## Key numbers
- Gamma_pi = (62.3 ± 2.0) micro-eV for the Hoyle state (first excited 0+ state in 12C at E_x = 7.654 MeV), extracted from the global model-independent fit.
- Transition radius <r^2>_tr = (5.47 ± 0.09) fm^2 from the global fit.
- Transition radius <r^4>_tr = (115 ± 8) fm^4 from the global fit.
- Low-q fit to S-DALINAC data alone gives <r^2>_tr^lowq = (5.29 ± 0.14) fm^2 and <r^4>_tr^lowq = (96 ± 10) fm^4.
- Previous Fourier-Bessel analysis by Crannell et al. (2005) gave Gamma_pi = 52.0 ± 1.4 micro-eV; the present work explains this discrepancy as due to insufficient Fourier-Bessel expansion order (nu_max = 8 versus at least 10 needed).
- The reduced E0 transition probability exhausts about 7.5% of the energy-weighted sum rule.

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy between the averaged value Gamma_pi = 60.5 ± 3.9 micro-eV quoted in Ajzenberg-Selove (1990) from low-q measurements and the more recent Fourier-Bessel analysis by Crannell et al. (2005) giving 52.0 ± 1.4 micro-eV. The improved precision enables quantitative modeling of the triple-alpha reaction rate in supernovae and asymptotic giant branch stars, which are the most likely site of the slow-neutron nucleosynthesis process. The extracted transition density also provides a test for state-of-the-art models of the Hoyle state, such as the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics model and the alpha-particle gas (Bose-Einstein condensate) model, which both predict <r^2>_tr about 20% too large.
