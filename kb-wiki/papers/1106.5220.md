# Two heavy fermions bound via Higgs boson exchange

> A system of two heavy fermions, leptons or quarks of the fourth generation, which are bound together via the Higgs boson exchange is studied. The conventional Yukawa-type interaction produced by this exchange is accompanied by several important corrections. We derived the Hamiltonian, which describes the correction arising from the retardation (compare the Breit correction in QED); we also calculated the relativistic and radiative corrections. The Higgs-induced bound state appears for the fermion mass m>m_{cr} \approx 500 GeV. When the long-range Coulomb interaction or the gluon exchange are included, the bound states exist for any mass, but the Higgs exchange drastically increases the binding energy of these states when m is approaching m_{cr}. In the region m>m_{cr} the gluon exchange gives a sizable correction to the Higgs induced binding energy. This correction greatly exceeds typical binding energies in the states produced via the gluon exchange only. The possibility of detection of the considered bound states at LHC is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two heavy fourth-generation fermions (leptons or quarks) can form a bound state via Higgs boson exchange only when the fermion mass exceeds a critical value \(m > m_{\text{cr}} \approx 500\) GeV (for Higgs mass \(m_h = 100\) GeV), but that when long-range Coulomb or gluon exchange is included, bound states exist for any mass, with the Higgs exchange dramatically increasing the binding energy as the mass approaches \(m_{\text{cr}}\).

## Method

The authors derived a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian for the Higgs-induced Yukawa potential including all \(v^2/c^2\) relativistic corrections (analogous to the Breit correction in QED) and retardation corrections, then solved the Schrödinger equation variationally using a hydrogen-like trial wave function. They also calculated radiative corrections using conventional methods from Lamb shift calculations, expressing them in terms of the renormalized Higgs vertex correction and the Higgs polarization operator for fermion loops.

## Key numbers

- Critical fermion mass for Higgs-only binding: \(m_{\text{cr}} \approx (m_h / 100\ \text{GeV})^{1/3} \times 500\ \text{GeV}\); for \(m_h = 100\) GeV, \(m_{\text{cr}} \approx 503\) GeV
- Effective Higgs coupling constant at critical mass: \(\alpha_h(m_{\text{cr}}) = 0.33\)
- Relativistic correction to critical mass: \(\delta m_{\text{cr}} \approx 0.014 m_{\text{cr}} \approx 7\) GeV; after including radiative corrections, total shift \(\delta m_{\text{cr}} \approx 5\) GeV
- For \(m > 0.75\) TeV, the analytical variational energy differs from numerical Schrödinger equation results by only a few percent
- In the limit \(m \gg m_h\), the nonrelativistic binding energy tends to \(E_0 \approx -(\alpha_h + a)^2 m / 4\)
- For leptons bound by Coulomb attraction near critical mass, the Higgs exchange enhances binding energy by a factor \(\alpha_h / \alpha \approx 30\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Higgs-induced bound states, including multifermion "magic" bags of 12 particles (Flambaum and Kuchiev, 2008-2011) and the two-fermion problem addressed by Ishiwata (2011). The authors derive a more complete Hamiltonian including retardation and relativistic corrections, similar to how the Breit correction is treated in QED (Berestetskii, Lifshitz, and Pitaevskii). The results enable predictions for detecting such bound states at LHC and provide analytical formulas for binding energies as functions of Higgs and fermion masses.
