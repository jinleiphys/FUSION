# The Rayleigh Bubble in Quark Matter under a Strong Magnetic Field

> The transition of QGP from fluid-dynamical regime to freeze-out is accompanied by the onset of instabilities. In the present paper we investigate the impact of the magnetic field on the Rayleigh instability. We show that extremely strong field generated in peripheral heavy ion collisions has an insignificant influence on the Rayleigh bubble dynamics. Magnetic "friction" turns out to be much weaker than the viscous one.

## Key claim

The authors show that even the extremely strong magnetic fields generated in peripheral heavy ion collisions (up to \(10^{19}\) G initially) have an insignificant influence on the Rayleigh bubble dynamics in quark-gluon plasma, with magnetic "friction" being much weaker than viscous damping.

## Method

The authors derive a Rayleigh-Plesset equation for a spherical bubble in an incompressible, irrotational, Newtonian fluid, adding a magnetic force term obtained from magnetohydrodynamics under the assumption of a small magnetic Reynolds number. They compare the magnetic term to the viscous term by estimating the Hartmann number using typical QGP parameters (conductivity \(\sigma \sim 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), shear viscosity \(\eta \sim 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\), bubble radius \(R \sim 1\) fm) and a reduced magnetic field of \(10^{14}\) G.

## Key numbers

* Hartmann number \(\operatorname{Ha} \simeq 1.6 \times 10^{-5}\) for \(B = 10^{14}\) G, \(\sigma = 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(l = R = 1\) fm, and \(\eta = 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\), showing magnetic effects are negligible compared to viscosity.
* The critical shear viscosity for "soft landing" collapse is \(\eta \simeq 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\) at \(T = 170\) MeV and \(\rho = 0.3\) GeV/fm\(^3\), based on the condition \(\eta T/\rho \ge 0.6\) from Shuryak and Staig (2013).
* The magnetic force parameter \(\sigma B^2 \simeq 10^{-13}\) GeV\(^5\) for \(B = 10^{14}\) G and \(\sigma = 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* The bubble wall velocity scaling near collapse is \(\dot{R}(t) \sim (t_C - t)^{-3/5}\) from the inviscid Rayleigh solution.

## Context

This work extends the study of cavitation and Rayleigh collapse in quark-gluon plasma initiated by Shuryak and Staig (2013), who considered viscous dissipation as a possible mechanism for "soft landing" collapse. The authors find that magnetic fields, despite their known strong effects on QCD phase diagrams and transport coefficients in other contexts, have negligible impact on bubble dynamics because the QGP shear viscosity is very large. The result enables future work to safely ignore magnetic field effects when modeling Rayleigh collapse in heavy-ion collisions, even as the field evolves in time.
