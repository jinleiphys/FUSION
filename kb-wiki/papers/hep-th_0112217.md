# Searching for Quantum Solitons in a 3+1 Dimensional Chiral Yukawa Model

> We search for static solitons stabilized by heavy fermions in a 3+1 dimensional Yukawa model. We compute the renormalized energy functional, including the exact one-loop quantum corrections, and perform a variational search for configurations that minimize the energy for a fixed fermion number. We compute the quantum corrections using a phase shift parameterization, in which we renormalize by identifying orders of the Born series with corresponding Feynman diagrams. For higher-order terms in the Born series, we develop a simplified calculational method. When applicable, we use the derivative expansion to check our results. We observe marginally bound configurations at large Yukawa coupling, and discuss their interpretation as soliton solutions subject to general limitations of the model.

## Key claim

The authors search for static solitons stabilized by heavy fermions in a 3+1 dimensional chiral Yukawa model and observe only marginally bound configurations at large Yukawa coupling, but these configurations may not be reliable because the model's Landau pole appears at an energy scale comparable to the inverse soliton width.

## Method

The authors compute the renormalized energy functional including exact one-loop quantum corrections from fermions, using a phase shift parameterization of the vacuum polarization energy. They perform a variational search over a four-parameter ansatz for a spherical hedgehog Higgs field configuration, minimizing the total energy for fixed fermion number one. The quantum corrections are renormalized by identifying orders of the Born series with corresponding Feynman diagrams, and a simplified calculational method is developed for higher-order Born terms; results are checked against the derivative expansion where applicable.

## Key numbers

- The Higgs particle mass is set to \(m_H = 0.35 v\) throughout the numerical analysis.
- For a configuration with width parameter \(w=1.0\) and coupling \(g=20.0\), the relative deviation \(\Delta_1\) between the full calculation and the derivative expansion is \(-0.308\), indicating poor agreement at small width.
- For \(w=3.0\) and \(g=20.0\), the relative deviation \(\Delta_1\) is \(0.006\), showing agreement with the derivative expansion at large width.
- The total energy \(E_{\rm tot}/m\) becomes less than 1 (indicating a stable soliton) only for large Yukawa coupling \(g\), with a shallow local minimum observed near variational parameters \(b_1 \approx -0.8\) for small and moderate \(g\) where the total energy remains above the free fermion mass \(m\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Farhi, Graham, Jaffe, Weigel) that demonstrated quantum-stabilized chiral solitons exist in the one-dimensional analog of this model. It improves upon earlier attempts to compute the one-fermion-loop energy for three-dimensional chiral backgrounds, which used discretization methods (Alford 1996, Ripka 1987), expansions for slowly varying fields (Aitchison 1985) or rapidly varying fields (Ripka 1987, Baacke 1986), truncations in the potential (Adkins 1991), heat kernel expansions (Kajantie 1992), and subsets of field configurations with less severe ultraviolet divergences (Baacke). The present method allows exact evaluation of the one-loop fermion contribution while maintaining a fixed renormalization scheme, enabling a full variational search. The results suggest that including SU(2)_L gauge fields may be necessary to obtain stable solitons, and work on that extension is underway.
