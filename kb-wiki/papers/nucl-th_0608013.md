# Neutron star matter in an effective model

> We study the equation of state (EOS) for dense matter in the core of the compact star with hyperons and calculate the star structure in an effective model in the mean field approach. With varying incompressibility and effective nucleon mass, we analyse the resulting EOS with hyperons in beta equilibrium and its underlying effect on the gross properties of the compact star sequences. The results obtained in our analysis are compared with predictions of other theoretical models and observations. The maximum mass of the compact star lies in the range $1.21-1.96 ~M_{\odot}$ for the different EOS obtained, in the model.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of a compact star with hyperons in beta equilibrium, calculated using an effective hadronic model with varying incompressibility (210–380 MeV) and effective nucleon mass (0.80–0.90 m_N), lies in the range 1.21–1.96 M_⊙, but this range depends strongly on the chosen parameter set and the softest set (Set V) produces a maximum mass (1.21 M_⊙) that falls below several recent observational mass measurements.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state (EOS) for dense neutron star matter containing the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) using a relativistic mean-field model with a dynamically generated vector meson mass and higher-order scalar self-interactions. They solved the coupled field equations self-consistently at fixed baryon density under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the mass, radius, central density, and gravitational redshift for five parameter sets differing in incompressibility K (210, 300, 380 MeV) and effective nucleon mass m*_N/m_N (0.80, 0.85, 0.90).

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass range across all five EOS: 1.21–1.96 M_⊙
- For Set I (K=210 MeV, m*_N/m_N=0.85): maximum mass = 1.66 M_⊙, radius = 16.78 km, central density = 7.90 × 10^14 g cm^−3
- For Set II (K=300 MeV, m*_N/m_N=0.85): maximum mass = 1.65 M_⊙, radius = 16.70 km, central density = 7.99 × 10^14 g cm^−3
- For Set III (K=380 MeV, m*_N/m_N=0.85): maximum mass = 1.63 M_⊙, radius = 16.62 km, central density = 7.99 × 10^14 g cm^−3
- For Set IV (K=300 MeV, m*_N/m_N=0.80): maximum mass = 1.96 M_⊙, radius = 17.44 km, central density = 7.72 × 10^14 g cm^−3
- For Set V (K=300 MeV, m*_N/m_N=0.90): maximum mass = 1.21 M_⊙, radius = 15.03 km, central density = 9.34 × 10^14 g cm^−3
- Gravitational redshift Z at maximum mass: 0.15–0.22 across all sets
- Hyperons first appear at approximately 2ρ_0 (twice normal nuclear matter density, where ρ_0 = 0.153 fm^−3)
- Saturation properties: energy per nucleon = −16 MeV at saturation density 0.153 fm^−3, asymmetry energy coefficient ≈ 32 MeV

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean-field studies of neutron star matter with hyperons, comparing with results from the non-linear Walecka model (which gives maximum masses around 1.90 M_⊙) and the quark-meson coupling model (around 1.98 M_⊙) as reported by Panda (2005). The authors find that varying the effective nucleon mass has a much stronger effect on the EOS and star properties than varying the incompressibility, and their results for Sets I–IV agree with observational mass measurements of neutron stars such as Vela X-1 (1.86 ± 0.16 M_⊙) and 4U 1636-536 (2.0 ± 0.1 M_⊙). The study enables future work on rotating neutron star structure and phase transitions within this effective model framework.
