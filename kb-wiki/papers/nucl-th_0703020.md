# Inclusive electron scattering off 4He

> Inclusive electron scattering off 4He is investigated for low and medium energy and momentum transfers. The final state interaction, given by the simple semirealistic Malfliet-Tjon potential, is treated rigorously applying the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method. Besides the nonrelativistic one-body current a consistent meson exchange current is constructed and implemented. Results are presented for both longitudinal and transverse response functions at various momentum transfers. Good agreement with experimental data is found for the longitudinal response function, while some strength is missing in the transverse response function on the low-energy side of the quasi-elastic peak.

## Key claim

The authors find good agreement with experimental data for the longitudinal response function of 4He using the semirealistic Malfliet-Tjon potential, but some strength is missing in the transverse response function on the low-energy side of the quasi-elastic peak, indicating that the semirealistic interaction and the consistent meson exchange current may not fully capture the reaction mechanism.

## Method

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method was applied to rigorously treat final state interactions in inclusive electron scattering off 4He, using the semirealistic Malfliet-Tjon MTI-III potential. The nuclear wave functions were expanded in hyperspherical harmonics with the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) approach, and a consistent meson exchange current was constructed from the isospin-dependent part of the potential to satisfy current conservation. Both longitudinal and transverse response functions were calculated by decomposing the charge and current operators into Coulomb, electric, and magnetic multipoles, with convergence checked by increasing the maximum grand-angular momentum Kmax and the maximum multipole Jmax.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of 4He with the MTI-III potential: E0 = -30.57 MeV, converged at Kmax = 10
- For the longitudinal response at q = 500 MeV/c, the Darwin-Foldy relativistic correction leads to a damping of about 6% of the total longitudinal strength in the quasi-elastic peak
- For the transverse response at q = 300 MeV/c in impulse approximation, the convection current produces an enhancement of 3-4% near the peak energy omega = 60 MeV
- At q = 300 MeV/c, the impulse approximation transverse response at the maximum is about 10% lower than Bates data and 20% lower than Saclay data
- A 0+ resonance in 4He at 20.10 +/- 0.05 MeV with a width of 270 +/- 50 keV was determined in an earlier electron scattering experiment at momentum transfers q < 100 MeV/c (Wal70)

## Context

This work extends the LIT method to the transverse response function of 4He, which had previously only been applied to the longitudinal response for 3H, 3He (Efros 2004, 2005) and 4He with the TN potential (Efros 1997, 1998). It contrasts with earlier plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations that neglected final state interactions and two-body currents, and with Green Function Monte Carlo calculations (Carlson 1992, 1994) that computed Laplace transforms (Euclidean responses) and found FSI important and MEC enhancing the transverse response by about 20%. The results enable direct comparison with experimental data from Bates and Saclay, and the approach can be extended to more realistic nuclear forces and to other observables such as neutrino scattering.
