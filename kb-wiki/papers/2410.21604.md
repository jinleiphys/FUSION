# Transverse Orbital Angular Momentum in the Proton

> Using the equations of motion of QCD and Lorentz invariance relations, we show a new, more direct way of obtaining the decomposition of the proton's quark transverse angular momentum into its spin and orbital components. The new decomposition can be understood as a twist-three relation, with the quark transverse spin described by the parton distribution $g_T$. We find that the orbital component can be expressed in terms of a moment in the quark transverse momentum, $k_T$, of the generalized transverse momentum-dependent distribution $F_{12}$, or alternatively in terms of the twist-three generalized parton distributions $H_{2T}$ and $\widetilde{E}_{2T}$.

# Transverse Orbital Angular Momentum in the Proton

## Key claim

Using QCD equations of motion and Lorentz invariance relations, the authors derive a decomposition of the proton's transverse quark angular momentum (Pauli-Lubanski vector) into spin and orbital components, where the orbital part can be expressed either through twist-three generalized parton distributions (GPDs) $H_{2T}$ and $\widetilde{E}_{2T}$ or through a transverse momentum moment of the generalized transverse momentum-dependent distribution (GTMD) $F_{12}$, with the caveat that the relation holds in the forward limit and depends on the choice of the proton rest frame center of mass as the reference point for the angular momentum tensor.

## Method

The authors apply QCD equations of motion and Lorentz invariance relations to the unintegrated quark-quark correlation function, connecting twist-three GPDs with $k_T^2$-moments of GTMDs in the specific polarization configuration defining transverse spin (proton helicity flip). They integrate over $k_T$ and take the forward limit ($\xi \rightarrow 0$, $\Delta_T \rightarrow 0$) to obtain sum rules for the transverse Pauli-Lubanski vector components, focusing on the Ji decomposition of angular momentum while also addressing the extension to the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition through gauge link dependence.

## Key numbers

- The transverse Pauli-Lubanski vector for total quark angular momentum is $W_{J,trans}^3 = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 dx\, x(H+E)$, matching the longitudinal angular momentum $J_{long}^3$
- The transverse spin component is $W_{S,trans}^3 = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 dx\, g_T = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 dx\, H'_{2T}$, where $g_T = g_1 + g_2$
- The transverse orbital component is $W_{L,trans}^3 = \int_0^1 dx\, x \left( \widetilde{E}_{2T} + H + E + \lim_{\xi \rightarrow 0} \frac{H_{2T}}{\xi} \right) = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^1 dx \ \lim_{\xi \rightarrow 0} \frac{F_{12}^{(1)}}{\xi}$
- The additional term $\lim_{\xi \rightarrow 0} H_{2T}/\xi$ satisfies $\int_0^1 dx\, x \lim_{\xi \rightarrow 0} \frac{H_{2T}}{\xi} = 0$, an orbital counterpart to the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule
- In the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation (dropping quark mass and quark-gluon-quark terms): $\widetilde{E}_{2T} + \lim_{\xi \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\xi} H_{2T} = -\int_x^1 \frac{dy}{y} (H+E) - \int_x^1 \frac{dy}{y^2} \widetilde{H}$

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Rajan et al. (2016, 2017) for longitudinal angular momentum decomposition to the transverse component of the Pauli-Lubanski vector, building on the Jaffe-Manohar (1990) and Ji (1996) frameworks for proton spin decomposition. The results confirm the finding of Ji (2020) that $W_{J,trans}$ is described by the same GPD combination $H+E$ as longitudinal angular momentum, while providing a direct identification of the orbital component through twist-three GPDs and GTMDs that can be accessed in deeply virtual exclusive experiments at facilities such as the future Electron Ion Collider. The GTMD representation via $F_{12}$ suggests a Wigner function interpretation and enables extension to the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition through gauge link dependence, analogous to the longitudinal case using $F_{14}$ as shown by Hatta (2011), Ji (2012), and Burkardt (2012).
