# Parton distributions in the presence of target mass corrections

> We study the consistency of parton distribution functions in the presence of target mass corrections (TMCs) at low Q^2. We review the standard operator product expansion derivation of TMCs in both x and moment space, and present the results in closed form for all unpolarized structure functions and their moments. To avoid the unphysical region at x > 1 in the standard TMC analysis, we propose an expansion of the target mass corrected structure functions order by order in M^2/Q^2, and assess the convergence properties of the resulting forms numerically.

## Key claim

The standard operator product expansion (OPE) treatment of target mass corrections (TMCs) forces a choice between universal parton distributions that violate energy-momentum conservation, or energy-momentum conserving but process-dependent distributions; the authors propose an alternative series expansion in \(M^2/Q^2\) that avoids the Nachtmann variable \(\xi\) and preserves both universality and conservation, but only over a restricted range of \(x\) and \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors review the OPE derivation of TMCs for all unpolarized structure functions (\(F_1, F_2, F_L, F_3, F_4, F_5\)) in both \(x\)-space and moment space, then demonstrate the inconsistency of the standard Georgi-Politzer (GP) inversion by showing that the \(n=2\) moment of \(F_2\) computed from the GP \(x\)-space formula does not recover the original OPE moment expression unless integration extends into the unphysical region \(x>1\). They propose inverting the moments term-by-term in a power series in \(\mu = M^2/Q^2\), absorbing combinatorial factors into derivatives, which yields new series expansions for each structure function that involve only the parton distributions evaluated at \(x\) (not \(\xi\)).

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), convergence of the series for \(F_2\) is "fairly rapid" for \(x \lesssim 0.5\), with the first two or three terms already giving a stable result.
- At \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), convergence is well controlled down to hadronic final state mass \(W \approx 1.3\) GeV, just above the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance peak.
- At \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), the first three terms (\(j<3\)) converge well up to \(x \approx 0.8\), corresponding to \(W \gtrsim 1.4\) GeV.
- The example parton distribution used is \(x f_2(x) = (35/32) \sqrt{x} (1-x)^3\), normalized so that \(\int_0^1 dx\, f_2(x) = 1\).

## Context

This work extends the OPE-based TMC formalism of Georgi and Politzer (1976) and De Rujula et al. (1977), which suffers from nonvanishing structure functions at \(x=1\) and violation of energy-momentum conservation. It also relates to the collinear factorization approaches of Ellis, Furmanski and Petronzio (1983) and later works by Aivazis et al. (1994), Kretzer and Reno (2002, 2004), and Accardi and Qiu (2008), which avoid the Nachtmann variable but retain the problem of nonzero structure functions at \(x=1\). The proposed series expansion enables consistent inclusion of low-\(Q^2\) data (down to \(\sim 1-2\) GeV\(^2\)) in global PDF analyses such as those by the ABKM, CJ, ABM, and NNPDF collaborations, and is relevant for upcoming high-\(x\) measurements from the BONuS and MARATHON experiments.
