# The effects of nonextensive statistics on fluctuations investigated in event-by-event analysis of data

> We investigate the effect of nonextensive statistics as applied to the chemical fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions discussed recently using the event-by-event analysis of data. It turns out that very minuite nonextensitivity changes drastically the expected experimental output for the fluctuation measure. This results is in agreement with similar studies of nonextensity performed recently for the transverse momentum fluctuations in the same reactions.

# The effects of nonextensive statistics on fluctuations investigated in event-by-event analysis of data

## Key claim

Very minute nonextensitivity (deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics characterized by a parameter q as small as q = 1.01–1.015) drastically changes the expected experimental output for the chemical fluctuation measure Φ in high-energy nuclear collisions, and this sensitivity mirrors similar effects previously found for transverse momentum fluctuations in the same reactions.

## Method

The authors computed the event-by-event fluctuation measure Φ for a system of two particle types (π⁻ and K⁻) using the standard definition of Φ based on event-by-event averages of particle multiplicities. They then replaced the mean occupation numbers in the fluctuation formulas with their q-equivalents from nonextensive (Tsallis) statistics, using an analytical approximation valid for small |1−q|, while retaining the factorized form for correlations ⟨nᵢnⱼ⟩ = ⟨nᵢ⟩⟨nⱼ⟩ used in prior work by other authors.

## Key numbers

- The nonextensitivity parameter q = 1.015 was chosen to fit the transverse momentum spectra of produced secondaries (shown in Fig. 3 of the paper).
- The range q = 1.01–1.015 was previously found to eliminate the discrepancy between the first NA49 data on transverse momentum fluctuations and the ideal quantum gas (Boltzmann statistics) estimation.
- The calculations were performed for central Pb–Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV, using the NA49 experimental conditions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Utyuzh, Wilk, Wlodarczyk, 1999) of nonextensive effects on transverse momentum fluctuations to the case of chemical (particle-type) fluctuations, which had been predicted for the NA49 experiment using standard Boltzmann statistics by other authors (referred to as M and MN in the text). The results show that the same small value of q that brings the transverse momentum spectra into agreement with experiment also modifies the chemical fluctuation measure significantly, suggesting that both observables are similarly affected by memory effects and long-range color interactions in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The paper enables future experimental tests: if the chemical fluctuation data from NA49 show a similar q value, it would signal that both fluctuation observables can be parametrized phenomenologically by a single measure of nonextensitivity.
