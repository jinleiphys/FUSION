# Kaon and pion maximal emission times extraction from the femtoscopy analysis of $5.02 A$ TeV LHC collisions within the integrated hydrokinetic model

> A simple method for the extraction of the times of maximal emission for kaons and pions using the combined fitting of their transverse momentum spectra and the longitudinal interferometry radii dependencies on the pair transverse mass $m_T$ is applied to Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. The method is based on the analytical formulas, that were earlier successfully utilized in the studies of Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. To test the method, the spectra, radii and particle radiation picture are calculated within the integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM), that includes all the stages of the matter evolution in high-energy A+A collisions: the system's formation, its thermalization, viscous hydrodynamics evolution, particlization and subsequent hadronic cascade. The model describes and predicts well already published LHC data in "soft physics? kinematic region. Thus, the fitting results for maximal emission times of kaons and pions are compared to the approximate maximal emission time values, estimated based on the emission function plots, obtained in iHKM. The developed simple method is intended for use in experimental analysis of femtoscopy data in relativistic A+A collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for extracting the times of maximal emission for kaons and pions from combined fits of transverse momentum spectra and longitudinal femtoscopy radii, but the fits for the 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions are less stable than at 2.76 TeV, requiring manual selection of parameters because the spectrum shape deviates noticeably from exponential.

## Method

The authors apply analytical formulas derived from a modified Cooper-Frye prescription with a constant proper-time hypersurface approximation to fit the transverse momentum spectra and longitudinal interferometry radii of pions and kaons calculated by the integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM) for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. The fitting procedure first determines the effective temperature T and the flow parameter alpha from combined fits of pion and kaon p_T spectra, then uses these values to fit the R_long(m_T) dependencies to extract the maximal emission times tau_pi and tau_K. For kaons, the alpha parameter must be left free during the radii fit to account for non-Gaussian correlation function shapes from wider q intervals.

## Key numbers

* For central collisions (c=0-5%): common temperature T=138 MeV, alpha_pi=4.8 +/- 1.1, alpha_K=2.4 +/- 0.6 from spectra fit; from radii fit: tau_pi=9.14 fm/c, tau_K=12.73 fm/c, alpha_K=0.062
* For c=20-30%: T=125 MeV, alpha_pi=3.94 from spectra fit; from radii fit: tau_pi=7.59 fm/c, tau_K=9.87 fm/c, alpha_K=0.35
* For c=40-50%: T=127 MeV, alpha_pi=4.20 from spectra fit; from radii fit: tau_pi=5.88 fm/c, tau_K=7.35 fm/c, alpha_K=0.53
* The spectra fitting was performed in the momentum range 0.45 < p_T < 1.0 GeV/c
* The radii were extracted from correlation function fits using the q range |q| < 0.2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV (Shapoval and Sinyukov, 2019) and to Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy (Shapoval and Sinyukov, 2020) within the iHKM framework. The results confirm the earlier finding from the ALICE Collaboration (2019) that kaons emit later than pions due to K*(892) resonance decays and intensive hadron-hadron scatterings, and predict k_T scaling for pion and kaon femtoscopy radii at the highest LHC energy, awaiting experimental confirmation from LHC Collaborations.
