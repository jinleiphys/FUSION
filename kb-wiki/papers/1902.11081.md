# Generalized Crossover in Interacting Fermions within the Low-Energy Expansion

> We generalize the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer-Bose-Einstein-condensation (BCS-BEC) crossover of two-component fermions, which is realized by tuning the $s$-wave scattering length $a$ between the fermions, to the case of an arbitrary effective range $r_{\rm e}$. By using the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) approach, we show another crossover by changing $r_{\rm e}$ and present several similarities and differences between these two crossovers. Furthermore, the region ($r_{\rm e}>a/2$) where the effective range expansion breaks down and the Hamiltonian becomes non-Hermitian is found, being consistent with the Wigner's causality bound. Our results are universal for low-density interacting fermions with low-energy constants $a$ and $r_{\rm e}$ and are directly relevant to ultracold Fermi atomic gases as well as dilute neutron matter.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the BCS-BEC crossover of two-component fermions from the usual tuning of the s-wave scattering length to an additional crossover controlled by the effective range, but this generalized crossover is only valid within the region where the effective range expansion does not break down (r_e < a/2 for positive scattering length).

## Method

The author uses the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) approach, which incorporates strong pairing fluctuations beyond mean-field theory, applied to a two-channel Hamiltonian with momentum-dependent Feshbach coupling. The superfluid critical temperature T_c is obtained from the Thouless criterion, while the chemical potential at T_c is determined self-consistently from the number equation including the many-body correction from pairing fluctuations. The model is constructed so that higher-order coefficients beyond the effective range expansion are exactly zero, allowing a clean study of the effects of scattering length a and effective range r_e.

## Key numbers

- The large-negative-effective-range limit yields T_c = 0.204 T_F (the "LNR" limit), which differs from the ordinary BEC limit T_BEC = 0.218 T_F due to thermal excited fermions
- For pure neutron matter (PNM), the parameters are a = -18.5 fm and r_e = 2.8 fm, giving r_e/a = -2.8/18.5
- The effective range expansion breaks down when r_e > a/2, where the cutoff parameter p_c = (1 + sqrt(1 - 2r_e/a))/r_e becomes complex
- At the boundary r_e = a/2, the critical binding energy is E_b,c/2 = 2/(m a^2)
- The weak-coupling critical temperature follows T_c ~ T_F exp[-(pi/2)(-1/(k_F a) + r_e k_F/2)]

## Context

This work extends the standard BCS-BEC crossover theory, originally developed for zero-range interactions by Eagles (1969), Leggett (1980), and Nozières and Schmitt-Rink (1985), to include finite effective range effects relevant to both ultracold Fermi gases and dilute neutron matter. The results connect to prior work on effective-range effects in neutron matter by Gezerlis and Carlson (2008) and in cold atoms by Ho (2004) and Parish (2011), while also identifying a breakdown region consistent with Wigner's causality bound (1955). The findings enable future experimental tests using optical control of both scattering length and effective range in ultracold atomic gases, as demonstrated by Bauer (2009) and Wu (2011), and provide a framework for understanding superfluid properties in neutron star interiors.
