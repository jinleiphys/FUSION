# J/Psi Suppression in Heavy Ion Collisions at the CERN SPS

> We reexamine the production of J/Psi and other charmonium states for a variety of target-projectile choices at the SPS. For this study we use a newly constructed cascade code LUCIFER II, which yields acceptable descriptions of both hard and soft processes, specifically Drell-Yan and hidden charm production, and soft energy loss and meson production, at the SPS. Glauber calculations of other authors are redone, and compared directly to the cascade results. The modeling of the charmonium states differs from that of earlier workers in its unified treatment of the hidden charm meson spectrum, which is introduced from the outset as a set of coupled states. The result is a description of the NA38 and NA50 data in terms of a conventional hadronic picture. The apparently anomalous suppression found in the most massive Pb+Pb system arises from three sources: destruction in the initial nucleon-nucleon cascade, use of coupled channels to exploit the larger breakup in the less bound Chi and Psi' states, and comover interaction in the final low energy phase.

## Key claim

The apparently anomalous J/ψ suppression observed in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS can be explained within a purely hadronic picture, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation, through the combined effects of destruction in the initial nucleon-nucleon cascade, coupled-channel exploitation of larger breakup in less bound χ and ψ' states, and comover interaction in the final low-energy phase.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-phase cascade code, LUCIFER II, which separates the ion-ion collision into an initial high-energy phase involving hard interactions of original nucleons with no soft energy loss, followed by a reinitialisation and a second low-energy cascade among secondary particles. Charmonium states (ψ, χ, ψ') were treated as a set of coupled channels from the outset, with production normalized to pp measurements and breakup handled via an interaction matrix. The cascade results were compared directly to Glauber calculations and to NA38 and NA50 data for S+U at 200 GeV/c and Pb+Pb at 158 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ absorption cross-section from p+A data: σ_abs ≈ 7.0 mb (hard sphere nuclear density) for pure J/ψ dynamics without coupled channels
- With coupled channels, direct J/ψ breakup cross-section reduced to σ_abs(J/ψ) = 5–6.0 mb, with σ_abs(χ) = 3 × σ_abs(J/ψ) for χ states
- χ to J/ψ production ratio in pp taken as χ/ψ ≈ 4.5, based on ISR data
- ψ' to J/ψ production ratio in pp taken as 0.33, yielding final ψ' to J/ψ ratio ≈ 0.15 after decays
- ψ' branching ratio into J/ψ: 57%
- χ branching ratio into J/ψ: 18% (weighted average over χ¹ and χ²)
- Meson formation time parameter τ_f ≈ 0.5–1 fm/c
- Generic meson masses centered near M_q\bar{q} = 700 MeV, range 0.3–1.0 GeV
- Generic baryon masses in range M_qqq ≈ 0.94–2.0 GeV
- Decay time for generic excitations fixed at τ_d ≈ 1/125 MeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the hadronic Glauber-based calculations of Kharzeev and Satz (1997) and the comover annihilation picture of Gavin (1997) by implementing a full dynamical cascade treatment of charmonium production and destruction. The authors directly compare their cascade results to Glauber calculations, finding that the first high-energy phase produces dynamics very similar to Glauber, but the inclusion of coupled channels and comover interactions in the second phase can account for the Pb+Pb suppression previously interpreted as a QGP signal. The code LUCIFER II is made publicly available, enabling future tests at RHIC and LHC energies.
