# Neutron star cooling - a challenge to the nuclear mean field

> The two recent density-dependent versions of the finite-range M3Y interaction (CDM3Y$n$ and M3Y-P$n$) have been probed against the bulk properties of asymmetric nuclear matter (NM) in the nonrelativistic Hartree Fock (HF) formalism. The same HF study has also been done with the famous Skyrme (SLy4) and Gogny (D1S and D1N) interactions which were well tested in the nuclear structure calculations. Our HF results are compared with those given by other many-body calculations like the Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock approach or ab-initio variational calculation using free nucleon-nucleon interaction, and by both the nonrelativistic and relativistic mean-field studies using different model parameters. Although the two considered density-dependent versions of the M3Y interaction were proven to be quite realistic in the nuclear structure or reaction studies, they give two distinct behaviors of the NM symmetry energy at high densities, like the Asy-soft and Asy-stiff scenarios found earlier with other mean-field interactions. As a consequence, we obtain two different behaviors of the proton fraction in the $β$-equilibrium which in turn can imply two drastically different mechanisms for the neutron star cooling. While some preference of the Asy-stiff scenario was found based on predictions of the latest microscopic many-body calculations or empirical NM pressure and isospin diffusion data deduced from heavy-ion collisions, a consistent mean-field description of nuclear structure database is more often given by some Asy-soft type interaction like the Gogny or M3Y-P$n$ ones. Such a dilemma poses an interesting challenge to the modern mean-field approaches.

## Key claim
The two density-dependent M3Y interaction families (CDM3Y\(n\) and M3Y-P\(n\)), while both realistic in nuclear structure or reaction studies, predict opposite high-density behaviors of the nuclear-matter symmetry energy (Asy-stiff versus Asy-soft), leading to drastically different proton fractions in \(\beta\)-equilibrium and thus to mutually exclusive mechanisms for neutron star cooling. The paper does not resolve this dilemma but highlights that a consistent mean-field description of nuclear structure data tends to favor Asy-soft interactions (like Gogny or M3Y-P\(n\)), whereas empirical constraints from heavy-ion collisions and microscopic many-body calculations favor the Asy-stiff scenario.

## Method
The authors performed nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature using two sets of density-dependent finite-range M3Y interactions (CDM3Y\(n\) with \(n=3,4,6\) and M3Y-P\(n\) with \(n=3,4,5\)), as well as the Skyrme SLy4 and Gogny D1S and D1N interactions. They computed the nuclear-matter binding energy per particle, pressure, incompressibility, symmetry energy \(S(\rho)\), and its slope and curvature parameters at saturation density, and then derived the proton fraction in \(\beta\)-equilibrium from the symmetry energy to assess the possibility of the direct Urca cooling process in neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density \(\rho_0\) for CDM3Y\(n\) interactions: 0.17 fm\(^{-3}\); for M3Y-P\(n\), Gogny, and SLy4 interactions: 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\).
- Symmetry energy coefficient \(J = S(\rho_0)\) ranges from 29.0 MeV (CDM3Y3, CDM3Y4) to 32.1 MeV (SLy4); M3Y-P\(n\) values are 30.0–31.0 MeV.
- Slope parameter \(L\) at \(\rho_0\): 62.5–62.9 MeV for CDM3Y\(n\); 21.1–28.3 MeV for M3Y-P\(n\); 23.7 MeV (D1S), 32.4 MeV (D1N), 46.0 MeV (SLy4).
- Incompressibility \(K\) of symmetric nuclear matter: 217–252 MeV for CDM3Y\(n\); 234–245 MeV for M3Y-P\(n\); 203 MeV (D1S), 221 MeV (D1N), 230 MeV (SLy4).
- Direct Urca threshold proton fraction \(x_{\rm DU} \approx 1/9\) at low densities (muon-free approximation), increasing slightly with density due to muon presence.
- For CDM3Y\(n\) interactions, the proton fraction reaches the DU threshold at \(\rho \approx 0.6\) fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to a neutron star mass \(M \approx 1.6 M_\odot\).
- For the DBHF study using Bonn A, the proton fraction reaches the DU threshold at \(\rho \approx 0.45\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the MDI (x=-1) interaction, the proton fraction reaches the DU threshold at \(\rho \approx 0.3\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context
This work extends earlier mean-field studies of asymmetric nuclear matter using density-dependent M3Y interactions (Khoa 1997, 2007) and compares them with the M3Y-P\(n\) interactions parametrized by Nakada (2008) for nuclear structure, as well as with Gogny and Skyrme forces. The results highlight a persistent tension between Asy-stiff interactions (favored by heavy-ion flow data from Danielewicz (2002) and microscopic APR calculations by Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998)) and Asy-soft interactions (which better describe ground-state properties of neutron-rich nuclei). The paper enables future work to resolve this dilemma, for instance by developing effective interactions that simultaneously satisfy constraints from nuclear structure, heavy-ion collisions, and neutron star observations, or by incorporating three-body forces as in the BHF calculations of Li (2006) which stiffen the symmetry energy at high densities.
