# Relativistic pionic effects in quasielastic electron scattering

> The impact of relativistic pionic correlations and meson-exchange currents on the response functions for electromagnetic quasielastic electron scattering from nuclei is studied in detail. Results in first-order perturbation theory are obtained for one-particle emission electronuclear reactions within the context of the relativistic Fermi gas model. Improving upon previous analyses where non-relativistic reductions of the currents were performed, here a fully relativistic analysis in which both forces and currents are treated consistently is presented. Lorentz covariance is shown to play a crucial role in enforcing the gauge invariance of the theory. Effects stemming uniquely from relativity in the pionic correlations are identified and, in particular, a comprehensive study of the self-energy contributions and of the currents associated with the pion is presented. First- and second-kind scaling for high momentum transfer is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully relativistic treatment of pionic correlations and meson-exchange currents (MEC) in the relativistic Fermi gas model is necessary to preserve gauge invariance in quasielastic electron scattering, and that non-relativistic reductions of the currents fail to satisfy current conservation. The authors identify relativistic effects in the self-energy term that have no counterpart in non-relativistic analyses and are essential for a consistent theory.

## Method

The authors computed the longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions for inclusive quasielastic electron scattering within the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model, using first-order perturbation theory in the pion-nucleon coupling. They evaluated all Feynman diagrams carrying one pionic line, including seagull, pion-in-flight, and correlation (self-energy and vertex) currents, without any non-relativistic expansion of the current operators, and verified gauge invariance by showing that the sum of the two-body current matrix elements satisfies the continuity equation \(Q_\mu (j_\mu^C + j_\mu^S + j_\mu^P) = 0\) when the electromagnetic form factors are chosen consistently (\(F_\pi = F_1^V\)).

## Key numbers

- The self-energy particle-hole matrix element is split into two contributions: one from renormalization of the energy and one from the lower components of the Dirac wave functions; the latter has no counterpart in non-relativistic analyses.
- Gauge invariance is proven analytically: \(Q^\mu (j_\mu^C + j_\mu^S + j_\mu^P) = 0\) holds when the pion electromagnetic form factor is set equal to the isovector nucleon form factor (\(F_\pi = F_1^V\)).
- The direct term of the two-body current matrix elements vanishes for MEC (seagull and pion-in-flight) and correlation currents in spin-isospin saturated systems, leaving only the exchange term.
- Scaling of the first kind is attained at high momentum transfer \(q\), while scaling of the second kind is broken by a dependence roughly on the Fermi momentum squared \(k_F^2\) for all values of \(q\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Amaro et al. (1990) by implementing a fully relativistic treatment of pionic currents and correlations, in contrast to earlier non-relativistic analyses that violated gauge invariance (e.g., Blunden et al. 1989). It improves upon previous work by Amaro et al. (1998, 1999) where expansions in powers of \(1/m\) were used, by now treating both forces and currents consistently without approximation. The results enable future studies of parity-violating responses, contributions from heavier mesons, and 2p-2h excitations within the same relativistic framework, as well as a forthcoming analysis of the \(\Delta\) isobar current.
