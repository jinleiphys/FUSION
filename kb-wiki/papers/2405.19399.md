# Hard processes in multi-TeV ion collisions

> Motivated by the ion-collision program at the Large Hadron Collider, plans for its high-luminosity upgrade, and on-going discussions for multi-TeV future hadron colliders, we systematically investigate hard-scattering, Standard Model processes in many-TeV ion-ion collisions. We focus on the symmetric beam configurations $^{208}$Pb-$^{208}$Pb, $^{131}$Xe-$^{131}$Xe, $^{12}$C-$^{12}$C, and $pp$, and we catalog total and fiducial cross sections for dozens of processes, ranging from associated-Higgs and multiboson production to associated-top pair production, at next-to-leading order in QCD for nucleon-nucleon collision energies from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=1$ to $100$ TeV. We report the residual scale uncertainties at this order as well as the uncertainties originating from fits of nuclear parton densities. We also discuss the propagation of nuclear dynamics (as encoded in nuclear parton densities) into parton luminosities, and ultimately into predictions for cross sections. Finally, we report on the emergence of trends and the reliability of extrapolating cross sections across different nuclei. For Pb-Pb collisions at a hypothetical Future Circular Collider with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=39$ TeV, $\mathcal{O}(10^{8})$ weak bosons, $\mathcal{O}(10^5)$ diboson pairs, $\mathcal{O}(10^4)$ $WH$ and $ZH$ pairs, $\mathcal{O}(10^3)$ triboson events, $\mathcal{O}(10^5)$ high-$p_T$ photons events, and $\mathcal{O}(10^7)$ $t\overline{t}$ pairs can be produced with $\mathcal{L}=33$~nb$^{-1}$ of data. At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.52$ TeV, one can expect $\mathcal{O}(10-10^6)$ single, multiboson, and top events per $1$ nb$^{-1}$. Decay rates and experimental selection/acceptance rates will impact final event yields, and merits further study; as an illustrative example, we focus on select diboson and triboson channels in lead-lead collisions and discuss their observability at the high-luminosity phase of the LHC and the FCC.

## Key claim
The paper systematically catalogs next-to-leading-order (NLO) in QCD cross sections for dozens of Standard Model hard-scattering processes in symmetric ion-ion collisions (Pb-Pb, Xe-Xe, C-C, and pp) across nucleon-nucleon collision energies from 1 to 100 TeV, but the abstract's event-yield projections (e.g., O(10^8) weak bosons at FCC) are explicitly qualified as requiring further study of decay rates and experimental selection/acceptance before final observability can be assessed.

## Method
The authors computed total and fiducial cross sections for associated-Higgs, multiboson, and associated-top pair production at NLO in QCD using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, with nuclear parton densities (nPDFs) from the EPPS16 and nCTEQ15 families. They evaluated residual scale uncertainties by varying the renormalization and factorization scales by a factor of two, and PDF uncertainties from the nPDF error sets. The propagation of nuclear dynamics into parton luminosities and cross-section predictions was analyzed, and trends across different nuclei (Pb, Xe, C, p) were examined.

## Key numbers
- For Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=39 TeV (FCC), expected yields per 33 nb^-1: O(10^8) weak bosons, O(10^5) diboson pairs, O(10^4) WH and ZH pairs, O(10^3) triboson events, O(10^5) high-pT photon events, and O(10^7) ttbar pairs.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.52 TeV, expected yields per 1 nb^-1: O(10-10^6) single, multiboson, and top events.
- NLO cross section for NN -> W^pm in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV: 5.714 x 10^-2 microbarn, with scale uncertainty +4%/-6% and PDF uncertainty +8%/-8%.
- NLO cross section for NN -> ttbar in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV: 5.032 x 10^-5 microbarn, with scale uncertainty +10%/-12% and PDF uncertainty +19%/-19%.
- NLO cross section for NN -> W^pm H in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV: 3.417 x 10^-7 microbarn, with scale uncertainty +2%/-1% and PDF uncertainty +4%/-4%.
- NLO cross section for NN -> W^+W^-W^pm in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV: 3.112 x 10^-8 microbarn, with scale uncertainty +6%/-4% and PDF uncertainty +7%/-7%.

## Context
This work extends the systematic cross-section catalogs previously available for proton-proton collisions (e.g., from the LHC Higgs Cross Section Working Group) to heavy-ion collisions, covering Pb, Xe, and C beams across a wide energy range relevant to the LHC, its high-luminosity upgrade, and proposed future colliders like the FCC. The results enable experimental projections for rare Standard Model processes in ion-ion collisions, including Higgs and multiboson production, and provide benchmarks for assessing the impact of nuclear PDF uncertainties. The authors note that decay rates and experimental selection efficiencies require further dedicated study before final observability can be established.
