# Few-body techniques using momentum space for bound and continuum states

> These notes were written for a set of three lectures given in a school at the Max Planck Institute for the Physics of Complex Systems in October/2017 before the workshop "Critical Stability of Quantum Few-Body Systems". These lectures are primarily dedicated to the students and represent a very idiosyncratic vision of the author, mainly in the last part of the text related to applications. These notes are only a tentative to show a technique, among many others, to solve problems in a very rich area of the contemporary physics - the Few-Body Physics - many times unknown by a considerable part of the students.

## Key claim

These lecture notes present a pedagogical introduction to momentum-space few-body techniques for bound and continuum states, with the explicit caveat that they represent the author's "very idiosyncratic vision" and are "only a tentative to show a technique, among many others," rather than a comprehensive or definitive treatment of the field.

## Method

The notes begin by developing formal scattering theory in the interaction picture, deriving the Møller operators, the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and the transition operator (T-matrix) for two-body systems. The author then demonstrates that the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation has a unique solution when the kernel is square-integrable, but shows that for three-body problems the kernel is non-compact and the equation loses uniqueness. The Faddeev equations are introduced as the solution to this non-uniqueness problem, derived by splitting the full transition operator into three components T_i corresponding to each pairwise interaction and rewriting the coupled equations in a matrix form that eliminates disconnected diagrams after one iteration.

## Key numbers

- The free Green function for an outgoing spherical wave is given as G_0^(+)(r, r') = -(1/4π) e^(ik|r-r'|)/|r-r'|.
- The scattering length is defined as a = (m/(2πℏ^2)) ∫ d^3r V(r) Ψ_α^(±)(r).
- The reduced mass for a two-body system is μ = m_1 m_2/(m_1+m_2).
- For a three-body system with odd-man-out notation, the reduced masses are μ_1 = m_2 m_3/(m_2+m_3) and M_1 = m_1(m_2+m_3)/(m_1+m_2+m_3).

## Context

These lecture notes were prepared for a school at the Max Planck Institute for the Physics of Complex Systems in October 2017, preceding the workshop "Critical Stability of Quantum Few-Body Systems." The work extends the formal scattering theory found in textbooks by Joachain and Newton, and specifically addresses the non-uniqueness problem of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for three or more particles, which was solved by Faddeev (1960). The notes enable students to apply momentum-space Faddeev techniques to calculate three-body bound and scattering states, with applications in systems ranging from quarks to atoms and molecules.
