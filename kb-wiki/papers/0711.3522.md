# Hadronic atoms in QCD + QED

> We review the theory of hadronic atoms in QCD + QED, based on a non-relativistic effective Lagrangian framework. We first provide an introduction to the theory, and then describe several applications: meson-meson, meson-nucleon atoms and meson-deuteron compounds. Finally, we compare the quantum field theory framework used here with the traditional approach, which is based on quantum-mechanical potential scattering.

## Key claim
This review paper argues that a non-relativistic effective Lagrangian framework, matched to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), provides a systematic and model-independent method to relate hadronic atom observables (energy shifts and decay widths) to fundamental QCD parameters (scattering lengths), overcoming ambiguities present in traditional potential-scattering approaches.

## Method
The authors construct a non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) valid at momenta much smaller than the pion mass, where bound states are described by a Schrödinger equation rather than the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The parameters of this EFT are determined by matching to ChPT in the scattering sector, and the Feshbach formalism is used to calculate bound-state observables perturbatively in the fine-structure constant α. The framework is applied to several hadronic atoms: pionium (π⁺π⁻), πK atoms, pionic hydrogen (π⁻p), kaonic hydrogen (K⁻p), pionic deuterium (π⁻d), and kaonic deuterium (K⁻d).

## Key numbers
- Pionic hydrogen ground-state strong shift: ΔE₁^(str) = -7.120 ± 0.011 eV (from the PSI upgrade, preliminary value)
- Pionic hydrogen ground-state width: Γ₁ = 0.823 ± 0.019 eV (from the PSI upgrade, preliminary value)
- Pionic deuterium ground-state strong shift: ΔE₁^(str,d) = 2.46 ± 0.048 eV (from Hauser et al., 1998)
- Pionic deuterium ground-state width: Γ₁^(d) = 1.194 ± 0.105 eV (from Hauser et al., 1998)
- Pionium ground-state lifetime: τ₁ = (2.91⁺⁰·⁴⁹₋₀·₆₂) × 10⁻¹⁵ s (DIRAC collaboration result)
- ChPT prediction for ππ scattering length difference: a₀ − a₂ = 0.265 ± 0.004 (from Colangelo et al., 2001)
- Kaonic hydrogen ground-state shift: ΔE₁^(str) = 193 ± 37 (stat) eV (DEAR/SIDDHARTA collaboration)
- Bohr radius of pionic hydrogen: r_B ≈ 220 fm
- Pionium decay width relation: Γ_(1,2π⁰) = (2/9) α³ p₁* (a₀ − a₂)² + ... (leading order in α)

## Context
This work extends the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring (DGBT) relation (Deser et al., 1954) for hadronic atoms to a full quantum field theory framework, addressing ambiguities in earlier potential-model calculations. It enables high-precision extraction of ππ, πK, and πN scattering lengths from experiments at CERN (DIRAC), PSI (Pionic Hydrogen Collaboration), and LNF-INFN (DEAR/SIDDHARTA), which in turn test chiral symmetry breaking in QCD and determine fundamental parameters like the πN σ-term and the πNN coupling constant via the Goldberger-Myazawa-Oehme sum rule (GMO, 1965). The approach is complementary to lattice QCD simulations and Roy equation analyses of scattering data.
