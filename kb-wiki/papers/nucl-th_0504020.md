# The phases of isospin asymmetric matter in the two flavor NJL model

> We investigate the phase diagram of isospin asymmetric matter at T=0 in the two flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Our approach describes the single nucleon as a confined quark-diquark state, the saturation properties of nuclear matter at normal densities, and the phase transition to normal or color superconducting quark matter at higher densities. The resulting equation of state of charge neutral matter is discussed.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of isospin asymmetric matter at T=0 is investigated using a two-flavor NJL model that describes the nucleon as a confined quark-diquark state, with the key hedge being that the vector-type interactions are set to zero in quark matter and the infrared cutoff is removed, while the diquark coupling strength in quark matter is treated as a free parameter to explore its effect on the phase structure.

## Method

The authors use a flavor SU(2) NJL Lagrangian with quark-antiquark interactions in scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector meson channels, plus a scalar diquark interaction term. The nucleon is constructed as a quark-diquark bound state via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the static approximation, and both nuclear matter and quark matter phases are derived from the same Lagrangian using mean-field approximations with a proper-time regularization scheme. The phase diagrams are constructed by comparing effective potentials for nuclear matter, normal quark matter, and color-superconducting quark matter at fixed baryon and isospin chemical potentials, with charge neutrality enforced via the Glendenning method for mixed phases.

## Key numbers

- Empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter: baryon density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and binding energy E_B/A = 15 MeV; the model gives a minimum at ρ = 0.22 fm⁻³ and E_B/A = 17 MeV.
- Empirical symmetry energy: a₄ = 32 MeV at ρ₀.
- Vacuum parameters: f_π = 93 MeV, m_π = 140 MeV, constituent quark mass M₀ = 400 MeV; infrared cutoff Λ_IR = 200 MeV for nuclear matter, set to 0 for quark matter.
- For r_s = 0 (no pairing), the transition to the NM/NQM mixed phase occurs at baryon density around 3.4 ρ₀, with stable hybrid stars for central densities between 3.4 ρ₀ and 5.6 ρ₀; the maximum mass star has radius 11.6 km with mixed phase within r = 5.8 km.
- For r_s = 0.25 (strong pairing), the transition to the NM/SQM mixed phase occurs at around 2.3 ρ₀, neutral SQM is reached around 4.6 ρ₀, and stable quark stars exist for central densities between 5.6 ρ₀ and 13.4 ρ₀; the maximum mass star has radius 8.2 km with SQM within r = 6.0 km.
- The diquark gap in the relevant density region is between 300 and 400 MeV for the proper-time regularization scheme used.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of symmetric nuclear matter by Bentz and Thomas (2001) to the isospin asymmetric case, and builds on the hadronization techniques of Bentz et al. (1997) that allow both nuclear matter and quark matter to be described within a single NJL model. The results are qualitatively similar to those reported in Alford and Rajagopal (2002) and Alford et al. (2001) for color-superconducting quark matter, and the method for constructing mixed phases follows the approach of Glendenning (1992). The paper enables future studies of mixed nuclear matter/superconducting quark matter phases in compact stars, and notes that inclusion of pion exchange and axial vector diquarks (as in Hecht et al., 2002; Leinweber et al., 2003) would allow a common value of the diquark coupling for both the single nucleon and superconducting quark matter.
