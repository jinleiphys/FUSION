# Structure of center-vortex matter in SU(4) Yang-Mills theory

> The structure of center vortices is studied in SU(4) Yang-Mills theory for the first time to illuminate the interplay between elementary (center charge $\pm 1$) and doubly charged vortices. Unlike in SU(3), where charge $+2$ vortices are simply elementary vortices with reversed orientations in spacetime, these possibilities are physically distinct in SU(4). Visualizations of the vortex structure in three-dimensional slices reveal the various ways in which doubly charged objects manifest, as the convergence and matching of elementary vortices or as isolated doubly charged loops. An algorithm is described to classify every doubly charged chain as one of these three types. A collection of vortex statistics is considered to quantify the vortex structure. Many of these pertain to the novel doubly charged objects, including their relative proportions and chain lengths, which are analyzed to highlight the differences between each chain type. Three different lattice spacings are employed to investigate the approach to the continuum limit. Vortex matching chains are found to be shorter on average but also more prevalent than vortex convergences, ascribed to their interpretation as extended center monopoles. In addition, the lengths of both vortex convergences and vortex matchings are observed to follow an exponential distribution, allowing the introduction of a constant probability for a doubly charged chain to split into two elementary vortices as it propagates. Combined, these findings provide a characterization of the vortices that comprise center-vortex structures in SU(4) Yang-Mills theory.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first characterization of center-vortex structure in SU(4) Yang-Mills theory, showing that doubly charged vortices are physically distinct from elementary vortices and persist in the continuum limit, contrary to the possibility that they might resolve into elementary vortices at finer lattice spacings.

## Method

The authors generate 100 SU(4) lattice gauge configurations at three different lattice spacings using the standard Wilson action with a pseudo-heat-bath algorithm, then apply direct maximal center gauge fixing and center projection to identify vortex locations. They develop a recursive algorithm to classify every doubly charged vortex chain as either a vortex convergence (VC), vortex matching (VM), or isolated doubly charged loop (L2), and analyze the proportions, lengths, and length distributions of these objects across three lattice spacings with extrapolation to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- Doubly charged vortices contribute approximately 10-15% of all pierced plaquettes, with p2 = 0.127(1) at a = 0.239 sigma^{-1/2}, 0.133(1) at a = 0.149 sigma^{-1/2}, and 0.144(1) at a = 0.103 sigma^{-1/2}
- The relative proportion of vortex matching chains to vortex convergence chains is approximately 54:46, with p_VM = 0.537(2) and p_VC = 0.461(2) at the coarsest lattice spacing
- Average physical length of VC chains extrapolated to the continuum limit is 0.754(4) sigma^{-1/2}, and VM chains is 0.706(4) sigma^{-1/2}
- Average L2 chain length in lattice units is 4.26(5) at a = 0.239 sigma^{-1/2}, 4.53(16) at a = 0.149 sigma^{-1/2}, and 4.71(22) at a = 0.103 sigma^{-1/2}
- L2 chains contribute a negligible fraction of doubly charged vortex matter, with p_L2 = 0.159(11) x 10^{-2} at the coarsest lattice spacing

## Context

This work extends prior visualizations of SU(3) center-vortex structures by Biddle (2019) and Mickley (2024) to the SU(4) case, where charge +2 vortices are physically distinct from reversed elementary vortices unlike in SU(3). The findings confirm and refine the random vortex world-surface model for SU(4) proposed by Engelhardt (2005), and the observation that VM chains can be interpreted as extended center monopoles connects to earlier work on center monopoles in SU(3) by Langfeld (2003) and Spengler (2018). The characterization of doubly charged vortex chains enables future studies of confinement mechanisms in SU(N) Yang-Mills theory and tests of effective descriptions for the confining flux tube.
