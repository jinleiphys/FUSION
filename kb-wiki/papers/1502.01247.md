# Reconstructing the final state of Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> We fit the single-hadron transverse-momentum spectra measured in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV with the blast-wave model that includes production via resonance decays. Common fit to pions, kaons, (anti)protons, and lambdas yields centrality dependence of the freeze-out temperature and transverse expansion velocity. In the most central collisions we see $T=98$ MeV and $\langle v_t \rangle = 0.654$. The $K^*$ resonance fits into this picture but the $ϕ$ meson might freeze-out a little earlier. Multistrange baryons seem to decouple at higher temperature and weaker transverse flow. Within our model we observe hints of chemical potential for the charged pions.

## Key claim

The blast-wave model including resonance decays can simultaneously fit transverse-momentum spectra of pions, kaons, (anti)protons, and lambdas from Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the fit quality depends on the chosen high-pt cuts and the model fails to describe multistrange baryons with the same freeze-out parameters, suggesting these species decouple earlier and with weaker transverse flow.

## Method

The authors used the DRAGON Monte Carlo implementation of the blast-wave model, which generates hadrons from a thermalized source with a radial velocity profile and then decays resonances according to their widths and phase space. They fitted the model parameters (freeze-out temperature T, transverse flow gradient eta_f, and velocity profile exponent n) by minimizing chi-squared between simulated and measured transverse-momentum spectra for pions, kaons, (anti)protons, K0, and lambdas across six centrality classes. The chemical freeze-out composition was fixed at T_ch = 152 MeV and mu_B = 0, and each spectrum was normalized individually to match the experimental normalization.

## Key numbers

* Most central collisions (0-5% centrality): T = 98 MeV, mean transverse velocity <v_t> = 0.654, chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.214 (194 dof)
* Most peripheral collisions (60-80% centrality): T = 166 MeV, <v_t> = 0.449, chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.449 (168 dof)
* For multistrange baryons (Xi and Omega) in 0-10% centrality: T = 126 MeV, <v_t> = 0.605, chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.375 (41 dof)
* For multistrange baryons in 60-80% centrality: T = 250 MeV, <v_t> = 0.382, chi-squared per degree of freedom = 0.795 (39 dof)
* Pion low-pt cut applied at 400 MeV/c; high-pt cuts were determined iteratively by excluding bins where the ratio of experimental to simulated data fell outside the range 0.9 to 1.1

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave analysis of ALICE (2012) by including resonance decays, which were omitted in the original ALICE fits that used only direct thermal production. The authors compare their results to the Cracow single freeze-out model used by Begun (2013, 2014) and Chatterjee (2014). The analysis enables more reliable extraction of kinetic freeze-out parameters and shows that multistrange baryons require separate treatment, motivating future studies of sequential freeze-out and partial chemical equilibrium scenarios.
