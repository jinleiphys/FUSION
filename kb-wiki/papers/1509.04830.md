# Kaon pair production in proton-nucleus collisions at 2.83 GeV kinetic energy

> The production of non-phi K+K- pairs by protons of 2.83 GeV kinetic energy on C, Cu, Ag, and Au targets has been investigated using the COSY-ANKE magnetic spectrometer. The K- momentum dependence of the differential cross section has been measured at small angles over the 0.2--0.9 GeV/c range. The comparison of the data with detailed model calculations indicates an attractive K- -nucleus potential of about -60 MeV at normal nuclear matter density at a mean momentum of 0.5 GeV/c. However, this approach has difficulty in reproducing the smallness of the observed cross sections at low K- momenta.

## Key claim
The comparison of measured non-phi K+K- pair production cross sections with model calculations indicates an attractive K- -nucleus potential of about -60 MeV at normal nuclear matter density at a mean momentum of 0.5 GeV/c, but this approach has difficulty reproducing the smallness of the observed cross sections at low K- momenta.

## Method
The COSY-ANKE magnetic spectrometer was used to measure K+K- pairs produced by 2.83 GeV protons incident on C, Cu, Ag, and Au targets. Events from phi meson decay were excluded by applying an invariant mass cut of IM ≤ 1.005 GeV/c^2, and the double differential cross sections for non-resonant K+K- production were obtained as functions of K- laboratory momentum over the range 0.2-0.9 GeV/c at polar angles θ ≤ 12°. The measured cross sections were compared with model calculations based on the nuclear spectral function for incoherent primary proton-nucleon and secondary pion-nucleon pair-creation processes, assuming different values for the K- nuclear potential depth.

## Key numbers
- K- potential depth extracted from integrated cross sections: U = -(63+15-12) MeV at normal nuclear matter density (ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3) and mean K- momentum of 0.5 GeV/c; including 13% systematic uncertainty gives U = -(63+50-31) MeV
- Target mass dependence exponent for integrated non-resonant K+K- cross sections: α = 0.42 ± 0.02, compared to αφ = 0.56 ± 0.03 for phi mesons
- Double differential cross sections for non-resonant K+K- production on C at K- momentum bin 450-550 MeV/c: 12.7 ± 0.6 (stat) ± 0.8 (syst) μb/(GeV/c)^2 sr^2
- Double differential cross sections for non-resonant K+K- production on Au at K- momentum bin 450-550 MeV/c: 41.7 ± 2.8 (stat) ± 2.4 (syst) μb/(GeV/c)^2 sr^2
- Integrated cross section over K- momentum range 200-900 MeV/c for C: 4.7 ± 0.2 μb/(GeV/c) sr^2; for Au: 15.1 ± 0.8 μb/(GeV/c) sr^2

## Context
This work extends measurements of K- production in proton-nucleus collisions at subthreshold energies, following earlier KaoS data (Scheinast, 2006) on inclusive K-/K+ ratios from pA collisions at 2.5 GeV and ITEP measurements (Akindinov, 2007; Akindinov, 2010) of inclusive K- momentum distributions. The extracted potential of about -60 MeV is consistent with predictions from chiral Lagrangian calculations (Lutz, 1998; Ramos, 2000; Tolos, 2006) and meson-exchange potential models (Tolos, 2001; Tolos, 2002) that predict depths of -50 to -80 MeV, but is difficult to reconcile with the deep potential of about -200 MeV claimed from in-flight (K-, N) reactions (Kishimoto, 2007; Kishimoto, 2009). The results enable further constraints on in-medium antikaon properties relevant to chiral symmetry restoration and the possible existence of K- condensates in neutron stars.
