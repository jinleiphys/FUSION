# Bose polaron in spherically symmetric trap potentials: Ground states with zero and lower angular momenta

> Single-atomic impurities immersed in a dilute Bose gas in the spherically symmetric harmonic trap potentials are studied at zero temperature. In order to find the ground state of the polarons, we present a conditional variational method with fixed expectation values of the total angular momentum operators, $\hat{J}^2$ and $\hat{J}_z$, of the system, using a cranking gauge-transformation for bosons to move them in the frame co-rotating with the impurity. In the formulation, the expectation value $\langle \hat{J^2}\rangle$ is shown to be shared in impurity and bosons, but the value $\langle \hat{J}_z\rangle$ is carried by the impurity due to the rotational symmetry. We also analyze the ground-state properties numerically obtained in this variational method for the system of the attractive impurity-boson interaction, and find that excited boson distributions around the impurity overlap largely with impurity's wave function in their quantum-number spaces and also in the real space because of the attractive interaction employed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a conditional variational method with fixed expectation values of total angular momentum operators to find the ground state of Bose polarons in spherically symmetric harmonic traps, but the method is valid only when the number of excited bosons is less than or equal to the number of impurities (unity in this calculation) and the two-level approximation in the impurity wave function loses validity when the variational parameter F_{1J} exceeds approximately 0.7.

## Method

The authors develop a Fröhlich-type effective Hamiltonian for a single atomic impurity interacting with a dilute Bose gas in spherically symmetric harmonic trap potentials at zero temperature, using the Bogoliubov approximation b_0 ≈ √N_0 for the condensed bosons. They introduce a cranking gauge transformation S(φ,θ) = e^{-iφ M_z} e^{-iθ M_y} that rotates the boson cloud into the co-rotating frame of the impurity, then employ a coherent state for excited bosons with quantum numbers (n,l,0) and a two-level Ritz-type variational state for the impurity wave function truncated to principal quantum numbers n=0 and n=1. The variational energy functional is minimized with respect to both the impurity coefficients F_{nJ} and the boson coherent state parameters f_{nl}.

## Key numbers

- Trap frequencies: ω_I = 200 Hz for the impurity (K-40), ω_b = 100 Hz for the medium bosons (Rb-87)
- Condensed boson number: N_0 = 10^4
- The summation in the interaction energy is truncated up to (n,l) = (30,10), with numerical changes within a few percent when raising maximum values by 100%
- The two-level approximation loses validity when F_{1J} ≥ 1/√2 ≈ 0.7
- The energy shift is comparable with experimental results for 1/(a k_ref) < -2

## Context

This work extends the study of Bose polarons from spatially uniform systems to spherically symmetric trapped systems, where angular momentum provides conserved quantum numbers instead of spatial momenta. The cranking method was previously introduced by Schmidt and Lemeshko (2015-2017) for linear rotor impurities in bosonic environments, and a simpler version was used by Nakano, Yabu, and Ichihara (2018) for axially symmetric trap potentials. The results enable calculation of ground-state energies for trapped Bose polarons with fixed total angular momentum and clarify the distribution of angular momentum between impurity and excited bosons, with potential application to radio-frequency spectroscopy measurements.
