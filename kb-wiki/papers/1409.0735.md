# In-medium effects for nuclear matter in the Fermi energy domain

> We study nuclear stopping in central collisions for heavy-ion induced reactions in the Fermi energy domain, between $15$ and $100$ A\,\textrm{MeV}. Using the large dataset of exclusive measurements provided by the $4π$ array \emph{INDRA}, we determine the relative degree of stopping as a function of system mass and bombarding energy. We show that the stopping can be directly related to the transport properties in the nuclear medium. By looking specifically at free nucleons (here protons), we present for the first time a comprehensive body of experimental results concerning the mean free path, the nucleon-nucleon cross-section and in-medium effects in nuclear matter. It is shown that the mean free path exhibits a maximum at $λ_{NN}=9.5 \pm 2$ \textrm{fm}, around $E_{inc}=35-40$ A\,\textrm{MeV} incident energy and decreases toward an asymptotic value $λ_{NN}= 4.5 \pm 1$ \textrm{fm} at $E_{inc} = 100$ A\,\textrm{MeV}. After accounting for Pauli blocking of elastic nucleon-nucleon collisions, it is shown that the effective in-medium \emph{NN} cross section is further reduced compared to the free value in this energy range. Therefore, in-medium effects cannot be neglected in the Fermi energy range. These results bring new fundamental inputs for microscopic descriptions of nuclear reactions in the Fermi energy domain.

# In-medium effects for nuclear matter in the Fermi energy domain

## Key claim

The paper claims that in-medium effects cannot be neglected in the Fermi energy range (15 to 100 A MeV), and that the effective in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section is further reduced compared to the free value even after accounting for Pauli blocking of elastic nucleon-nucleon collisions. The hedge is that the extracted in-medium cross sections are systematically lower than free values, but the Pauli blocking correction may be underestimated because it was determined only in momentum space rather than full phase space.

## Method

The authors analyzed exclusive data from the INDRA 4π array for 42 symmetric or nearly-symmetric heavy-ion systems with total masses between 72 and 476 amu at incident energies from 15 to 100 A MeV. They selected central collisions using a charged-particle multiplicity cut and computed the isotropy ratio R_E for protons only, then derived a stopping ratio S by comparing to two Fermi-sphere models representing full transparency and full stopping. Using a Monte Carlo simulation of elastic nucleon-nucleon collisions between two Fermi spheres, they established an empirical relation between the stopping ratio S and the fraction C of accepted NN collisions, then extracted the nucleon mean free path λ_NN and in-medium NN cross section σ_NN from the scaled quantity C/A_tot^(1/3).

## Key numbers

- Mean free path maximum: λ_NN = 9.5 ± 2 fm at incident energy E_inc = 35-40 A MeV
- Mean free path asymptotic value: λ_NN = 4.5 ± 1 fm at E_inc = 100 A MeV
- In-medium NN cross section asymptotic value: approximately 12 mb at high energy (above 100 A MeV)
- In-medium reduction factor F = σ_NN^(in-medium) / σ_NN^(free) ranges between 0.2 and 0.5 for incident energies 35-100 A MeV
- Reduction factor corresponds to 80% reduction at E_inc = 35 A MeV and 50% reduction at E_inc = 100 A MeV
- The scaling exponent γ that collapses all data onto a single curve is γ ≈ 1/3
- The empirical parameter β in the relation C ≈ S^β varies from β = 1.32 at E_inc = 30 A MeV to β = 0.74 at E_inc = 110 A MeV
- The characteristic length L used for mean free path extraction is L = r_0 A^(1/3) with r_0 = 1.2 fm and A = A_tot/2
- Density used for cross section extraction: ρ = 1.2 ρ_0 with ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^(-3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lehaut 2010) which observed a transition from 1-body to 2-body dissipation around 35 A MeV using the isotropy ratio for all particles. The present analysis focuses specifically on protons to probe nucleon properties, and provides the first comprehensive experimental determination of the nucleon mean free path and in-medium NN cross section in the Fermi energy domain. The results are compared to theoretical predictions from Danielewicz (2002), Li and Machleidt (1993), Schnell et al. (1998), and others; the Danielewicz parametrization shows excellent agreement with the experimental in-medium reduction factor. The findings enable improved microscopic descriptions of nuclear reactions in the Fermi energy domain and provide inputs for transport models based on the Boltzmann equation, with implications for understanding supernova core collapse and neutron star formation.
