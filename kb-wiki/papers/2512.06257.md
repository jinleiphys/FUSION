# Maximally Symmetric Boost-Invariant Solutions of the Boltzmann Equation in Foliated Geometries

> In this work we study the relativistic kinetic theory of a boost-invariant conformal gas on a static, maximally symmetric background $dS_3\times \mathbb{R}$, considering all constant-curvature slicings of $dS_3$ - flat, spherical, or hyperbolic- and their associated symmetry groups. Using a symmetry-driven cotangent-bundle approach, we show that the isometry group of each slicing acts on phase space in such a way that only its Casimir invariants and the time-like coordinate unconstrained, so the distribution function depends solely on these quantities. This yields a unified boost-invariant exact solution of the Boltzmann equation valid for each constant-curvature foliation of $dS_3\times \mathbb{R}$. Specializing this general solution to the flat and spherical foliations reproduces the Bjorken and Gubser flows, respectively, while its restriction to the hyperbolic foliation produces a genuinely new analytic solution (`Grozdanov flow'). Hydrodynamics and free streaming emerge naturally as limiting regimes of this novel exact solution. We further comment on several relevant aspects of the new boost-invariant solution on the hyperbolic slicing and on their interpretation once mapped back to Minkowski space.

## Key claim

The paper derives a unified exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a conformal gas on a maximally symmetric background \(dS_3\times \mathbb{R}\), valid for all three constant-curvature foliations (flat, spherical, hyperbolic). The hyperbolic foliation yields a genuinely new analytic solution ("Grozdanov flow") that is on equal conceptual footing with the previously known Bjorken and Gubser flows, and from which hydrodynamics and free streaming emerge as limiting regimes.

## Method

The authors apply a cotangent-bundle formulation of relativistic kinetic theory to a boost-invariant conformal gas on a static \(dS_3\times \mathbb{R}\) background. For each constant-curvature foliation of \(dS_3\) (flat with group \(ISO(2)\), spherical with \(SO(3)\), hyperbolic with \(SO(2,1)\)), they identify the lifted Killing vector fields on phase space, compute the associated Casimir invariants, and show that the isometry group acts with cohomogeneity zero so that the distribution function depends only on these Casimirs and the time-like coordinate. The Boltzmann equation with a relaxation-time-approximation (RTA) collision kernel is then reduced to a first-order PDE and solved analytically for each foliation.

## Key numbers

* The relaxation time in the RTA collision kernel is \(\tau_r = \gamma/T\), with \(\gamma = 5\,\eta/\mathcal{S}\) relating to the shear viscosity over entropy ratio; in practice \(\tau_r = c\,\eta/(\mathcal{S}T)\) with \(\eta/\mathcal{S}\) taken as a fixed input and \(c\) an adjustable constant.
* The metric for the flat foliation (\(\kappa=0\)) has scale factor \(S_0(\rho) = e^{-\rho}\) with \(\rho\) ranging over \(-\infty < \rho < \infty\).
* The metric for the spherical foliation (\(\kappa=+1\)) has scale factor \(S_{+1}(\rho) = \cosh\rho\) with \(\rho\) ranging over \(-\infty < \rho < \infty\).
* The metric for the hyperbolic foliation (\(\kappa=-1\)) has scale factor \(S_{-1}(\rho) = \sinh\rho\) with \(\rho\) ranging over \(0 < \rho < \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the geometric framework of Gubser and Yarom (2010) and Grozdanov (2025) by providing the microscopic kinetic-theory description of the three maximally symmetric boost-invariant flows (Bjorken, Gubser, and Grozdanov) within a single formalism. It reproduces the known kinetic solutions for Bjorken flow (Baym, 1984) and Gubser flow (Denicol et al., 2014) and obtains a new exact solution for the hyperbolic foliation. The results enable systematic study of far-from-equilibrium dynamics and provide benchmarks for testing Boltzmann transport algorithms.
