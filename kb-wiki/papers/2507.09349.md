# Investigating QCD Dynamical Entropy in high-energy nuclear collisions

> In this work, the concept of QCD dynamical entropy is extended to heavy ion systems. This notion of entropy can be understood as a relative entropy and can also be used to estimate the initial entropy density in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The key quantity used to calculate this entropy is the nuclear unintegrated gluon distribution (nUGD), which provides a transverse momentum probability density. In the numerical analysis, both the geometric scaling phenomenon and the Glauber-Gribov approach have been used to evaluate realistic models for the nUGD. It is shown that the normalization procedure and the geometric scaling property make the dynamical entropy almost independent of the nucleus mass number $A$. Results are presented for the dynamical entropy density, $dS_D/dy$, in terms of the rapidity.

## Key claim

The QCD dynamical entropy, when computed using the geometric scaling property and the normalization procedure for nuclear unintegrated gluon distributions, becomes almost independent of the nucleus mass number \(A\); the nuclear result reduces to the proton case, while the entropy density retains a dependence on the nuclear radius \(R_A^2\).

## Method

The authors extended the QCD dynamical entropy formalism from proton-proton to proton-nucleus collisions by constructing nuclear unintegrated gluon distributions (nUGDs) via two strategies: (i) geometric scaling, where the nuclear saturation scale \(Q_{s,A}^2\) absorbs the \(A\)-dependence, and (ii) the Glauber-Gribov approach, where the dipole-proton cross-section is replaced by a nuclear dipole cross-section using the Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile. The transverse momentum probability distributions \(P(k,Y)\) were obtained by normalizing the nUGDs from three phenomenological models (GBW, MPM, and Levin-Tuchin), and the dynamical entropy \(\Sigma^{Y_0\rightarrow Y}\) and its density \(dS_D/dy\) were computed as functions of rapidity evolution \(\Delta Y\).

## Key numbers

* The GBW saturation scale parameters used are \(x_0 = 4.2 \times 10^{-5}\) and \(\lambda = 0.248\), with \(Q_0 = 1\) GeV (fitted from data).
* The MPM model uses \(\lambda = 0.33\) with parameters \(a = 0.055\) and \(b = 0.204\).
* The Levin-Tuchin model uses \(\epsilon \approx 0.2\) for the saddle point condition and \(\gamma_s \approx 0.63\) for the anomalous dimension near the saturation line.
* The GBW dynamical entropy is given analytically as \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{GBW}}^{Y_0\rightarrow Y}(\Delta Y) = 2\left(e^{\lambda \Delta Y} - 1 - \lambda\Delta Y\right)\).
* The initial rapidity is set to \(Y_0 \approx 4.6\) (corresponding to \(x_0 = 0.01\)), and results are shown for \(\Delta Y\) in the range \([0, 15]\).
* For the geometric scaling strategy, the nuclear saturation scale follows \(Q_{s,A}^2(Y) = (R_p^2 A / R_A^2)^\Delta Q_s^2(Y)\) with \(\Delta \approx 1.27\) and \(R_p \approx 3.56\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the QCD dynamical entropy formalism of Peschanski (2013) from proton-proton to proton-nucleus collisions, building on the authors' previous study for \(pp\) collisions (Ramos et al., 2022). It contrasts with other entropy measures in the Color Glass Condensate framework: the von Neumann entanglement entropy (Kovner, 2015; Duan, 2020) and the Wehrl entropy (Hagiwara, 2017; Hatta, 2016) both scale as \(S \propto S_\perp Q_s^2\) and are additive with \(A\), whereas the dynamical entropy is not extensive. The results enable future comparisons with initial entropy density estimates for the glasma phase in heavy-ion collisions and with the parton entanglement entropy of Kharzeev and Levin (2017).
