# Nuclear in-medium effects of strange particles in proton-nucleus collisions

> Dynamics of strange particles produced in proton induced nuclear reactions near threshold energies has been investigated within the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model. The in-medium modifications on particle production in dense nuclear matter are considered through corrections on the elementary cross sections via the effective mass and the mean-field potentials. It is found that the attractive antikaon-nucleon potential enhances the subthreshold $\bar{K}$ production and also influences the structure of inclusive spectra. The strangeness production is strongly suppressed in proton induced reactions in comparison to heavy-ion collisions. The measured K$^{-}$/K$^{+}$ ratios in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and p+$^{12}$C collisions from KaoS collaboration have been well explained with inclusion of the in-medium potentials.

# Nuclear in-medium effects of strange particles in proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
The LQMD transport model reproduces measured K$^{-}$/K$^{+}$ ratios from the KaoS collaboration in both heavy-ion and proton-nucleus collisions, but only when a weakly repulsive kaon-nucleon potential of about 28 MeV and a deeply attractive antikaon-nucleon potential of about -100 MeV at saturation density are included. The paper claims that proton-nucleus collisions offer a cleaner probe of these in-medium potentials than heavy-ion collisions because the nuclear density is fixed near saturation density, avoiding uncertainties from varying baryon densities.

## Method
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate proton-nucleus collisions near threshold energies for strangeness production. The in-medium modifications were implemented through corrections to elementary cross sections via effective masses and mean-field potentials derived from a chiral Lagrangian approach, with kaon and antikaon energies computed including isospin-dependent terms. The model included all coupled channels for producing pions, hyperons, kaons, and antikaons, with baryon and meson evolution governed by Hamilton's equations of motion using a Skyrme-type momentum-dependent interaction.

## Key numbers
- Repulsive kaon-nucleon potential strength: 27.8 MeV at saturation baryon density for isospin symmetric matter
- Attractive antikaon-nucleon potential strength: -100.3 MeV at saturation baryon density for isospin symmetric matter
- Effective mass ratio for kaons: $m^{\ast}_{K}/m_{K} = 1.056$ at normal baryon density
- Effective mass ratio for antikaons: $m^{\ast}_{\overline{K}}/m_{\overline{K}} = 0.797$ at normal baryon density
- Parameter $b_{K} = 3/(8f_{\pi}^{\ast 2}) \approx 0.333$ GeV fm$^{3}$
- Parameter $a_{K} = 0.18$ GeV$^{2}$ fm$^{3}$ for kaons and $a_{\overline{K}} = 0.31$ GeV$^{2}$ fm$^{3}$ for antikaons
- Parameter $c_{K} = 0.0298$ GeV$^{2}$ fm$^{3}$ and $d_{K} = 0.111$ GeV fm$^{3}$ for isospin splitting
- Deeply attractive $K^{-}N$ potential of -110$\pm$15 MeV and weakly repulsive $K^{+}N$ potential of 25$\pm$10 MeV were obtained from heavy-ion collisions in prior work (Li 1997, Ca 1999, Fu 2006, Ha 2012)

## Context
This work extends prior studies of in-medium strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions (Li 1997, Ca 1999, Fu 2006, Ha 2012) to proton-nucleus collisions, where the nuclear density is fixed near saturation density, avoiding the complications of varying baryon densities in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results confirm the attractive antikaon-nucleon potential and repulsive kaon-nucleon potential previously extracted from heavy-ion data, and enable more precise constraints on these potentials for applications in hypernucleus formation and neutron star core structure. The measured K$^{-}$/K$^{+}$ ratios from the KaoS collaboration for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au, p+$^{12}$C, and p+$^{197}$Au are all reproduced with the same in-medium potentials.
