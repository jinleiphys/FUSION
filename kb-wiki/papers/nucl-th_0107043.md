# Doublet channel neutron-deuteron scattering in leading order effective field theory

> The doublet channel neutron-deuteron scattering amplitude is calculated in leading order effective field theory (EFT). It is shown that this amplitude does not depend on a constant contact interaction three-body force. Satisfactory agreement with available data is obtained when only two-body forces are included.

## Key claim

The doublet channel neutron-deuteron scattering amplitude in leading order effective field theory does not depend on a constant contact interaction three-body force; satisfactory agreement with available data is obtained when only two-body forces are included, provided the physical amplitude is identified as the non-oscillating solution among infinitely many solutions to the scattering equation.

## Method

The authors solved the S-TM (Skornyakov–Ter-Martirosyan) integral equations for the doublet channel neutron-deuteron system in leading order effective field theory, using only two-body forces with zero-range interactions. They isolated the physical scattering amplitude from the infinite family of solutions by requiring that the amplitude vanish asymptotically faster than 1/p, which eliminates the spurious oscillatory behavior arising from homogeneous solutions of the integral equation. The resulting amplitude was then compared to experimental phase shift data.

## Key numbers

- The two roots of the characteristic equation for the asymptotic behavior of the amplitude in the three-boson case (λ=1) are s = ±i s₀, where s₀ ≈ 1.00624.
- The two-body scattering lengths used are a₂ᵗ (triplet channel) and a₂ˢ (singlet channel), with a₂ˢ being negative (no explicit numerical values given in the text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on three-boson systems (Gegelia 2000, Blankleider 2000) to the neutron-deuteron case, where they previously showed that cutoff dependence can be resolved without a three-body force by properly identifying the physical solution. It contradicts the argument of Bedaque (1999) that a one-parameter three-body force counter-term is necessary to eliminate cutoff dependence. The result is consistent with the later work of Gabbiani (2001), who found no dependence on a constant three-body force when effective range parameters are included. This enables calculations of neutron-deuteron scattering in leading order EFT without introducing three-body forces, though the authors note that their numerical method does not allow high accuracy at low momenta.
