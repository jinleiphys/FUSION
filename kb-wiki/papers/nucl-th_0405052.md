# Modelling Pentaquark and Heptaquark States

> A schematic model for hadronic states, based on constituent quarks and antiquarks and gluon pairs, is discussed. The phenomenological interaction between quarks and gluons is QCD motivated. The obtained hadronic spectrum leads to the identification of nucleon and $Δ$ resonances and to pentaquark and heptaquark states. The predicted lowest pentaquark state ($J^π = {1/2}^-$) lies at the energy of 1.5 GeV and it is associated to the observed $Θ^+(1540)$ state. For heptaquarks ($J^π = {1/2}^+, {3/2}^+$) the model predicts the lowest state at 2.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The authors present a schematic model that predicts the lowest pentaquark state (J^π = 1/2^-) at 1.5 GeV, which they associate with the observed Θ^+(1540) state, and the lowest heptaquark state (J^π = 1/2^+, 3/2^+) at 2.5 GeV; however, these predictions rely on a phenomenological interaction with parameters fixed to meson, nucleon, and Δ resonance data, and the model does not contain flavor mixing terms, requiring corrections to compare with data.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic model with two fermionic levels for quarks and antiquarks and a gluonic state containing pairs of gluons, classified in a flavor-spin coupling scheme. The interaction among these degrees of freedom is described by excitations of quark-antiquark pairs mediated by the exchange of gluon pairs, with the Hamiltonian solved using the boson expansion method. Basis states are classified using group theoretical methods via the group chain U(4Ω) ⊃ U(Ω/3) ⊗ U(12), with Ω=9 accounting for three color and three flavor degrees of freedom, and the parameters D1, D2, E1, E2 are adjusted to nucleon and Δ resonances.

## Key numbers

- Distance between fermion levels: 2ω_f = 0.66 GeV
- Energy of the glueball: ω_b = 1.6 GeV
- Interaction strengths: V_{00}=0.0337 GeV, V_{01}=0.0422 GeV, V_{10}=0.1573 GeV, V_{11}=0.0177 GeV
- Adjusted parameters: D_1=-1.442 GeV, D_2=-0.4388 GeV, E_1=-1.1873 GeV, E_2=-0.3622 GeV
- Lowest pentaquark state (J^π = 1/2^-) predicted at 1.5 GeV, associated with the observed Θ^+(1540) state
- Lowest heptaquark state (J^π = 1/2^+, 3/2^+) predicted at 2.5 GeV
- Theoretical Roper resonance (first excited nucleon resonance) lies near the experimental energy of 1.44 GeV
- First negative parity nucleon state with J^π=1/2^- appears at 1.51 GeV
- Δ resonance with J^π=3/2^- predicted at 1.79 GeV
- Lowest Δ resonance from coupling valence quarks in (1,1)1/2 configuration with (1,1)S q\bar{q} states at 1.51 GeV, compared to experimental value at 1.62 GeV

## Context

This work extends a schematic model previously applied to meson spectra (Nunez et al., earlier publications) to baryonic states, including nucleon and Δ resonances as well as exotic pentaquark and heptaquark states. The model belongs to a class of exactly solvable models of coupled fermion and boson systems (Lipkin, Schutte, Pittel, Civitarese and Reboiro), and the predicted negative parity for the lowest pentaquark agrees with QCD sum-rules and lattice gauge calculations. The results enable further classification of higher multiquark states and provide a framework for comparing with experimental data on exotic baryons, with a more complete classification of states promised in a follow-up paper (Nunez et al., long).
