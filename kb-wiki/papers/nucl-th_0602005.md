# First application of the continuum-QRPA to description of the double beta decay

> A continuum-QRPA approach to calculation of the $2νββ$- and $0νββ$-amplitudes has been formulated. For $^{130}$Te a regular suppression (about 20%) of the high-multipole contributions to the $0νββ$-amplitude has been found which can be associated with additional ground state correlations appearing from the transitions to collective states in the continuum. At the same time the total calculated $0νββ$-amplitude for $^{130}$Te gets suppressed by about 20% as compared to the result of the usual, discretized, QRPA.

## Key claim

The continuum-QRPA approach, applied for the first time to double beta decay, finds a regular suppression of about 20% in the high-multipole contributions to the 0νββ-amplitude for ¹³⁰Te, which the authors associate with additional ground state correlations from transitions to collective states in the continuum; the total calculated 0νββ-amplitude is suppressed by about 20% compared to the usual discretized QRPA result.

## Method

The authors formulate a continuum-QRPA method in coordinate representation using four-component radial transition densities and Green's functions to include the single-particle continuum, avoiding the basis truncation of standard QRPA. They apply this method to ¹³⁰Te, fitting the particle-hole spin-isovector strength to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance energy and the particle-particle spin-isovector strength to reproduce the experimental 2νββ matrix element. The 0νββ-amplitude is then calculated by summing multipole contributions up to J^π = 6⁻ (L = 0 to 5) using a neutrino potential with Jastrow short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- The total calculated 0νββ-amplitude M⁰ν for ¹³⁰Te is 1.79 in the continuum-QRPA, compared to 2.24 in the discretized QRPA (with g_A = 1.25).
- The fitted particle-hole spin-isovector strength f¹_ph is 0.65 in the continuum-QRPA and 0.60 in the discretized QRPA.
- The fitted particle-particle spin-isovector strength g¹_pp is 1.15 in the continuum-QRPA and 1.20 in the discretized QRPA.
- A regular suppression of about 20% is found for the high-multipole (L ≥ 2) contributions to the 0νββ-amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the continuum-QRPA approach previously developed for Gamow-Teller strength distributions in semi-magic nuclei (Rodin 2003) to the calculation of double beta decay matrix elements. It directly addresses the uncertainty in standard QRPA calculations of 0νββ matrix elements (Rodin 2003, Rodin 2005) by including the single-particle continuum, which was previously neglected. The method enables systematic analysis of other double-beta-decaying nuclei and provides a way to assess the accuracy of the usual discretized QRPA results.
