# Response of Mica to Weakly Interacting Massive Particles

> We calculate spin-dependent cross sections for the scattering from mica of hypothetical weakly interacting dark-matter particles such as neutralinos. The most abundant odd-A isotopes in mica, Al27 and K39, require different shell-model treatments. The calculated cross sections will allow the interpretation of ongoing experiments that look for tracks due to the interaction of dark-matter particles with nuclei in ancient mica.

# Response of Mica to Weakly Interacting Massive Particles

## Key claim

The spin-dependent cross sections for WIMP scattering from mica are calculated for the odd-A isotopes Al-27 and K-39, but the results carry a roughly 30% uncertainty for aluminum due to the need for ad hoc quenching to reproduce beta-decay lifetimes, and the potassium calculation requires second-order perturbation theory rather than a full shell-model diagonalization because of computational complexity.

## Method

The authors calculated spin-dependent structure functions for Al-27 by diagonalizing the Wildenthal "universal" sd-shell interaction in the full 0-hbar-omega space using the Lanczos m-scheme shell-model code CRUNCHER, with 80115 Slater determinants. For K-39, which lies at the boundary of the sd and pf shells, they used second-order perturbation theory with core-polarization corrections, evaluating intermediate-state summations up to 8-hbar-omega and extrapolating geometrically, using two different residual interactions: a Bonn-based weak-tensor force and a Paris-based strong-tensor G-matrix. Structure functions were computed with both harmonic oscillator and Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions for Al-27, and polynomial fits were provided for both nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Measured magnetic moment of Al-27: +3.6415 mu_N; calculated value with free-nucleon g-factors: +3.584 mu_N; single-particle value: +4.793 mu_N
- Oscillator length parameter b = 1.73 fm for Al-27; for K-39, hbar-omega = 11.1 MeV corresponding to b = 1.934 fm
- For K-39, the structure function S_00(q) is reduced to 20-25% of the single-particle value depending on the residual interaction used
- Detection threshold for mica: recoil energy of about 20 keV, corresponding to q^2 of about 10^-3 GeV^2 in Al and about 1.6 x 10^-3 GeV^2 in K
- For K-39, the core-polarization corrections produce about two-thirds of the required reduction in the Gamow-Teller matrix element to match experiment

## Context

This work extends the spin-dependent WIMP scattering calculations previously performed for Si-29 by Ressell et al. (1993) and builds on the general formalism for spin-dependent scattering developed by Engel (1991) and Engel, Pittel, and Vogel (1992). The calculations enable interpretation of ongoing mica track-detection experiments by Snowden-Ifft, Freeman, and Price, and are also relevant to a sapphire-crystal (Al2O3) detector under development by Cooper et al. The results are general enough to apply to any heavy Majorana particle, not just neutralinos, and the polynomial fits for structure functions allow calculation of spin-dependent cross sections for any such particle.
