# Efimov States in Nuclear and Particle Physics

> Particles with resonant short-range interactions have universal properties that do not depend on the details of their structure or their interactions at short distances. In the three-body system, these properties include the existence of a geometric spectrum of three-body Efimov states and a discrete scaling symmetry leading to log-periodic dependence of observables on the scattering length. Similar universal properties appear in the four-body system and possibly higher-body systems as well. For example, universal four-body states have recently been predicted and observed in experiment. These phenomena are often referred to as "Efimov Physics". We review their theoretical description and discuss applications in different areas of physics with a special emphasis on nuclear and particle physics.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical description of Efimov physics, universal properties of three-body systems with resonant short-range interactions, and argues that these phenomena apply broadly across nuclear and particle physics, not just in cold atoms, despite the fact that in nuclear systems the scattering length cannot be varied experimentally and the ratio of range to scattering length is often only about one third.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review using the hyperspherical formalism to derive the hyperradial equation for three identical bosons, showing that in the unitary limit (infinite scattering length) the effective potential is an attractive \(1/R^2\) term. They then introduce a short-distance three-body force (a surface delta function at radius \(1/\Lambda\)) to regularize the spectrum, which breaks continuous scale invariance to discrete scale invariance with scaling factor \(e^{\pi/s_0}\). The formalism is extended to finite scattering length and to four-body systems using effective field theory (EFT) with an ultraviolet limit cycle.

## Key numbers

* For three identical bosons, the transcendental equation for \(s_0\) gives \(s_0 \approx 1.00624\), yielding a discrete scaling factor \(e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7\).
* The asymptotic number of three-body bound states for large \(|a|/\ell\) is \(N \longrightarrow (s_0/\pi) \ln(|a|/\ell)\).
* In the unitary limit, the geometric spectrum of Efimov trimers is \(B_t^{(n)} = (e^{-2\pi/s_0})^{n-n_*} \hbar^2 \kappa_*^2 / m\).
* For universal four-body states in the unitary limit, the binding energies relative to the trimer binding energy \(B_t\) are \(B_4^{(0)} \approx 5\, B_t\) and \(B_4^{(1)} \approx 1.01\, B_t\) (Hammer and Platter, 2006), and with higher numerical accuracy \(B_4^{(0)} \approx 4.57\, B_t\) and \(B_4^{(1)} \approx 1.01\, B_t\) (von Stecher, d'Incao and Greene, 2008).
* The scattering lengths at which tetramer states become zero binding are \(a^*_{4,0} \approx 0.43 a'_*\) and \(a^*_{4,1} \approx 0.92 a'_*\), where \(a'_*\) is the negative scattering length at which the associated Efimov trimer has zero binding energy.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of Efimov physics, originally discovered by Efimov (1970) and connected to the Thomas collapse (1935), to applications in nuclear and particle physics including few-nucleon systems, halo nuclei, and weakly bound hadronic molecules. It builds on the effective field theory implementation by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) and the hyperspherical methods reviewed by Braaten (2004). The work enables the interpretation of the triton as an Efimov state and provides the theoretical basis for recent experimental observations of universal tetramer states in ultracold cesium atoms by Ferlaino et al. (2009), confirming predictions by Hammer and Platter (2006) and von Stecher, d'Incao and Greene (2008).
