# Jet Energy Loss in Hot and Dense Matter

> We discuss the the GLV Reaction Operator formalism for computing the non-abelian energy loss of jets propagating through hot and dense matter. We incorporate our results on induced gluon bremsstrahlung together with the effects of nuclear shadowing and multiple scattering in a two component soft+hard description of heavy ion reactions at RHIC energies. We demonstrate that good agreement between data and theory can be achieved in the measured moderate p_T <= 5 GeV window at root(s)=130 AGeV and we extend our predictions for the high p_T part of the hadronic spectra. We focus on the perspectives of using jet tomographic methods for probing not only the density and geometry of the quark-gluon plasma but also its parton composition at various center of mass energies.

## Key claim

The GLV reaction operator formalism for non-abelian energy loss, when combined with a two-component soft+hard model that includes nuclear shadowing and multiple scattering, can achieve good agreement with RHIC data at √s = 130 AGeV in the moderate p_T ≤ 5 GeV window, but only if the jet energy loss is driven by an initial gluon rapidity density dN^g/dy ≃ 800, which is about 30% lower than existing estimates.

## Method

The authors compute the non-abelian energy loss of jets using the GLV reaction operator approach, which expands the induced gluon radiation in powers of the opacity χ = L/λ_g of the medium. They incorporate numerical corrections up to third order in opacity and include the longitudinal Bjorken expansion of the system, then fold the energy loss spectrum into a perturbative QCD calculation of hadron production that also includes a phenomenological soft component motivated by string and baryon junction models.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon rapidity density driving jet energy loss: dN^g/dy ≃ 800, which is ~30% lower than existing estimates
- Moderate p_T window where agreement with data is achieved: p_T ≤ 5 GeV at √s = 130 AGeV
- For a gluon jet propagating through gluon plasma versus a quark jet through quark-antiquark plasma, the difference in effective fractional energy loss is a factor of (C_A/C_F)^2 ≃ 5
- At √s = 200 AGeV Au+Au, the number of quanta per unit rapidity increases by only ~15% relative to √s = 130 AGeV
- Detectable increase in π^0 suppression factor at √s = 200 AGeV requires ~30% change, only possible with transition to nearly gluon-dominated background
- At SPS energies (√s ~ 20 AGeV), a still significant suppression R_AA ~ 0.5 remains even with reduced energy loss, inconsistent with SPS results

## Context

This work extends the GLV reaction operator formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev) for non-abelian energy loss to comparisons with RHIC data from PHENIX and STAR, following earlier theoretical work on coherent scattering effects by Landau and Pomeranchuk, Migdal, and the non-abelian generalization by Bertsch and Gunion. The results enable jet tomographic methods to probe not only the density and geometry of the quark-gluon plasma but also its parton composition (gluon-dominated versus quark-antiquark plasma) across different center-of-mass energies, and suggest that the large energy loss from gluon jets in a gluon medium is excluded by SPS data.
