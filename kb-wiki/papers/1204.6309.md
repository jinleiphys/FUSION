# Sum rule analysis of vector and axial-vector spectral functions with excited states in vacuum

> We simultaneously analyze vector and axial-vector spectral functions in vacuum using hadronic models constrained by experimental data and the requirement that Weinberg-type sum rules are satisfied. Upon explicit inclusion of an excited vector state, viz. rho', and the requirement that the perturbative continua are degenerate in vector and axial-vector channels, we deduce the existence of an excited axial-vector resonance state, a1', in order that the Weinberg sum rules are satisfied. The resulting spectral functions are further tested with QCD sum rules.

## Key claim

By explicitly including an excited vector state (ρ′) and requiring that the perturbative continua are degenerate in vector and axial-vector channels, the authors deduce the existence of an excited axial-vector resonance state (a1′) as necessary to satisfy Weinberg-type sum rules, rather than directly from experimental data.

## Method

The authors constructed hadronic models for vector and axial-vector spectral functions in vacuum, dividing each into three parts: a ground-state resonance, a first excited state, and a universal continuum. The ρ(770) ground state used a microscopic spectral function from effective hadronic Lagrangians, while the a1, ρ′, and a1′ states were modeled with Breit-Wigner forms with energy-dependent widths. The model parameters (16 total) were determined by fitting to ALEPH τ-decay data and imposing the first three Weinberg-type sum rules (WSR 0, 1, and 2) as constraints.

## Key numbers

- a1 meson mass: 1.246 GeV, width: 0.612 GeV, coupling: 6.15
- ρ′ meson mass: 1.565 GeV, width: 0.32 GeV, coupling: 11.44
- a1′ meson mass: 1.802 GeV, width: 0.2 GeV, coupling: 28.70
- Continuum threshold energy (Eth): 1.60 GeV, with δ = 0.227 GeV
- Vector channel fit quality: R² = 0.991; axial-vector channel fit quality: R² = 0.997
- WSR 1 and WSR 2 achieved ∼0% disagreement between left-hand side and right-hand side; WSR 0 showed -1.28% disagreement; WSR 3 showed -96% disagreement
- Pion decay constant fπ = 92.4 MeV; pion mass mπ = 139.6 MeV; average current light-quark mass mq = 5 MeV; chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ = (-0.25 GeV)³

## Context

This work extends previous sum rule analyses that approximated spectral functions with simpler δ-function or Breit-Wigner shapes for the ρ peak (Leupold 1997, Kwon 2008, Kwon 2010) and that did not include excited states explicitly. The authors build upon Weinberg's original sum rule work (Weinberg 1967) and the Kapusta-Shuryak sum rule (Kapusta 1993), while using high-precision τ-decay data from ALEPH (Barate 1998) and OPAL (Ackerstaff 1998). The constructed vacuum spectral functions enable future studies of in-medium modifications and chiral symmetry restoration patterns, particularly for the axial-vector channel where no experimental in-medium measurement exists to date.
