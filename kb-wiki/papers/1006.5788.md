# Description of the $2ννββ$ decay within a fully renormalized pnQRPA approach with a restored gauge symmetry

> A many body Hamiltonian involving the mean field for a projected spherical single particle basis, the pairing interactions for alike nucleons and the dipole-dipole proton-neutron interactions in the particle-hole ($ph$) and particle-particle ($pp$) channels is treated by the projected gauge fully renormalized proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PGFRpnQRPA) approach. The resulting wave functions and energies for the mother and the daughter nuclei are used to calculate the $2νββ$ decay rate and the process half life. For illustration, the formalism is applied for the decay $^{100}$Mo$ \to$ $^{100}$Ru. The results are in good agreement with the corresponding experimental data. The Ikeda sum rule ($ISR$) is obeyed. The gauge projection makes the $pp$ interaction inefficient.

# Description of the $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay within a fully renormalized pnQRPA approach with a restored gauge symmetry

## Key claim
The projected gauge fully renormalized proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PGFRpnQRPA) yields a realistic description of the $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay rate for $^{100}$Mo $\to$ $^{100}$Ru while obeying the Ikeda sum rule (ISR), and the gauge projection makes the particle-particle ($pp$) interaction inefficient, so it need not be included in the many-body treatment.

## Method
The authors treat a many-body Hamiltonian with mean field, pairing interactions, and dipole-dipole proton-neutron interactions in the particle-hole ($ph$) and particle-particle ($pp$) channels using a projected spherical single-particle basis derived from an axially deformed coherent state. They split the proton-neutron dipole configuration space into three subspaces ($\mathcal{S}_+$, $\mathcal{S}_-$, and spurious) and apply a fully renormalized pnQRPA with gauge symmetry restoration, solving the dispersion equations and phonon amplitudes iteratively. The resulting wave functions and energies for mother and daughter nuclei are used to compute the Gamow-Teller transition amplitude $M_{GT}$ and the $2\nu\beta\beta$ half-life via a factorized expression involving a phase-space integral.

## Key numbers
- $|M_{GT}| = 0.234$ for the $^{100}$Mo $\to$ $^{100}$Ru transition.
- Calculated half-life: $T_{1/2} = 7.89 \times 10^{18}$ yr.
- Experimental half-life comparison: $T_{1/2} = (8.0 \pm 0.16) \times 10^{18}$ yr (Elliot and Vogel, 2002) and $T_{1/2} = (0.115^{+0.03}_{-0.02}) \times 10^{20}$ yr (Ejiri et al., 1991).
- Summed $\beta^-$ strength: $\sum B_{GT-} = 26.69$; quenching by factor 0.6 gives 28.9.
- $\Delta E = 2.026$ MeV used in the half-life denominator.
- Dipole interaction strength: $\chi = 5.2 / A^{0.7}$ MeV, yielding $\chi = 0.207$ MeV for the isotopes.
- Ikeda sum rule (ISR) values: 16.01 for $^{100}$Mo, 12.01 for $^{100}$Ru.
- $\log ft$ values for $^{100}$Tc transitions: $\beta^+/EC$ to $^{100}$Mo: theoretical 4.5 vs. experimental 5.29; $\beta^-$ to $^{100}$Ru: theoretical 4.66 vs. experimental 5.41.
- Dimensions of $\mathcal{S}_+$ and $\mathcal{S}_-$ spaces: (163, 1) for mother, (158, 5) for daughter; total $\mathcal{S}$ dimension is 208 for both.

## Context
This work extends the fully renormalized pnQRPA approach of Raduta, Raduta, Kaminski, and Faessler (1998) and Raduta, Raduta, and Codirla (2000) by restoring gauge symmetry, which previous higher pnQRPA procedures violated the Ikeda sum rule by about 20-30% (Raduta, Simkovich, and Faessler, 2000). The method improves on the gauge-restoration formulation of Raduta and Raduta (2005) by using a projected spherical single-particle basis suitable for both spherical and deformed nuclei, and by splitting the configuration space into three subspaces. The results enable more reliable nuclear matrix element calculations for $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay, which serve as a test for matrix elements used in $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay searches. The authors note that the gauge projection makes the $pp$ interaction unnecessary, contrasting with standard pnQRPA where the $pp$ strength $g_{pp}$ is critical and near a instability point.
