# Neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei with $α$-particle correlations and the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy

> The formation of $α$-particle clusters on the surface of heavy nuclei is described in a generalized relativistic mean-field model with explicit cluster degrees of freedom. The effects on the size of the neutron skin of Sn nuclei and ${}^{208}$Pb are investigated as a function of the mass number and the isospin-dependent part of the effective interaction, respectively. The correlation of the neutron skin thickness with the difference of the neutron and proton numbers and with the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy is modified as compared to the mean-field calculation without alpha-cluster correlations.

## Key claim

The formation of α-particle clusters on the surface of heavy nuclei reduces the neutron skin thickness compared to mean-field calculations without such correlations, thereby modifying the established correlation between neutron skin thickness and the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy; the effect is most pronounced for nuclei with moderate neutron excess and vanishes for very neutron-rich systems where α-particles cannot form efficiently.

## Method

The author employs a generalized relativistic mean-field model with explicit α-particle degrees of freedom, using an extended relativistic Thomas-Fermi (RTF) approximation within spherical Wigner-Seitz cells. The α-particle wave function is obtained self-consistently via a WKB approximation, with the number of α-particles determined variationally such that the effective position-dependent α energy does not exceed the α-particle chemical potential. The calculations use a modified DD2 parametrization with a rescaled σ meson mass (from 546.2 MeV to 577.9 MeV) and correspondingly adjusted coupling, applied to Sn isotopes and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb, including α-particle correlations reduces the neutron skin thickness by approximately 0.02 fm relative to calculations without α correlations.
- The effective α-particle number Nα in Sn nuclei is non-integer (due to the statistical description) and decreases continuously from its largest value at low mass number A ≈ 107 to zero at high A.
- The potential pocket for α-particles at the nuclear surface has typical width of 1–2 fm and depth of 10–20 MeV.
- Six parametrizations of the DD2 model were created with slope coefficients L ranging from 25.00 MeV (DD2⁻⁻) to 100.00 MeV (DD2⁺⁺⁺), with corresponding symmetry energies J from 28.22 MeV to 35.34 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the generalized relativistic mean-field model with cluster degrees of freedom previously developed by Typel and collaborators (Typel 2010, 2014) for warm dilute matter to zero-temperature heavy nuclei in vacuum. It directly addresses the well-known correlation between neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy slope L established in mean-field calculations (Brown 2000, Typel 2001, Furnstahl 2001, Vinas 2013), showing that α-clustering introduces a systematic modification. The results imply that extracting L from neutron skin measurements (e.g., the PREX experiment on ²⁰⁸Pb) requires accounting for cluster correlations, and suggest experimental verification via quasi-free (p,pα) reactions (Aumann 2014).
