# Three-body treatment of the penetration through the Coulomb field of a two-fragment nucleus

> On the basis of the Faddeev integral equations method and the Watson- Feshbach concept of the effective (optical) interaction potential, the first fully consistent three-body approach to the description of the penetration of a charged particle through the Coulomb field of a two-particle bound complex (composed of one charged and one neutral particles) has been developed. A general formalism has been elaborated and on its basis, to a first approximation in the Sommerfeld parameter, the influence of the nuclear structure on the probability of the penetration of a charged particle (the muon, the pion, the kaon and the proton) through the Gamow barrier of a two-fragment nucleus (the deuteron and the two lightest lambda hypernuclei, lambda hypertriton and lambda hyperhelium-5, has been calculated and studied.

## Key claim

The paper develops the first fully consistent three-body approach to describe the penetration of a charged particle through the Coulomb field of a two-fragment nucleus, but the numerical calculations are performed only to a first approximation in the Sommerfeld parameter, meaning the results are limited to leading-order Coulomb corrections.

## Method

The authors formulate the problem as three-body Coulomb scattering of a charged particle (1) by a bound complex of one charged (2) and one neutral (3) particle, using Faddeev integral equations with a separable nuclear potential for the bound pair and a screened Coulomb potential between particles 1 and 2. They apply the Watson-Feshbach concept of effective interaction to reduce the three-body problem to a two-body scattering problem, then use the Gorshkov-Vesselova limiting procedure to remove the screening and obtain regularized integral equations for the unscreened Coulomb case. The penetration factor is computed from the effective scattering wave function at the center-of-mass point of the two-fragment nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The penetration factor for the Coulomb field of a point charge is given by \(P_0 = |C(\eta)|^2 = 2\pi\eta/(e^{2\pi\eta}-1)\), where \(\eta = \mu_1 e_1 e_2/(\hbar^2 p_0)\) is the Sommerfeld parameter.
- The diagonal elements of the form factors \(I\) and \(J\) on the energy shell equal 1: \(I({\bf p}_0,{\bf p}_0; \epsilon) = J({\bf p}_0,{\bf p}_0; \epsilon) = 1\).
- The diagonal element of the form factor \(L\) on the energy shell is \(L({\bf p}_0,{\bf p}_0; \epsilon) = -\int d{\bf k}/(2\pi)^3 \, u^2(k)/(b + k^2/2\mu_{23})^3\).

## Context

This work extends the rigorous few-body treatment of Coulomb scattering problems, building on the Faddeev integral equations method (Faddeev, 1965) and the Gorshkov-Vesselova recipe for regularizing Coulomb interactions in momentum space (Gorshkov, 1961; Vesselova, 1970). It generalizes earlier proton-deuteron scattering studies (papers from 1990s) and analytical work on polarization potentials (papers from 1990s) to compute penetration factors for composite nuclei. The formalism enables future calculations of how nuclear structure modifies Gamow barrier penetration for systems including the deuteron and lambda hypernuclei \(^3_{\Lambda}\text{H}\) and \(^5_{\Lambda}\text{He}\).
