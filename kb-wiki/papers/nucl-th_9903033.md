# Isospin violation and meson-exchange models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Traditionally isospin violation (charge symmetry breaking and charge dependence) has been modeled by the exchange of mesons between two nucleons. Sources of isospin violation in these models include meson mass differences, meson mixing (of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons) and isospin violating couplings of mesons with nucleons. I will review the calculated results of this theoretical approach and the NN data on isospin violation.

## Key claim

Meson-exchange models provide a reasonable picture of class III charge symmetry breaking but are less successful in quantitatively describing class II charge dependence; the sum of class II forces from meson mass differences and gamma-pion exchange falls short of the experimental value, contrary to previous conclusions because a recent calculation of the gamma-pion potential found its effect to be an order of magnitude smaller than earlier flawed estimates.

## Method

The author reviews meson-exchange models of isospin violation in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, where single or two-meson exchange graphs are reduced to non-relativistic potentials that break charge independence or charge symmetry. These potentials are added to a charge independent phenomenological potential, and the resulting changes in scattering parameters are compared to experimental measures of class II, III, and IV forces from low-energy NN scattering data and analyzing power measurements.

## Key numbers

- np spin-singlet scattering length: a_np ≈ -24.0 fm; effective range: r_np ≈ 2.68 fm
- pp spin-singlet scattering length: a_pp ≈ -17.4 fm; effective range: r_pp ≈ 2.83 fm
- nn scattering length from pi- d → gamma nn: a_nn = -18.5 ± 0.4 fm; r_nn = 2.80 ± 0.11 fm
- Class II force effect on scattering length: Δa^II ≈ 6.0 fm; on effective range: Δr^II_0 ≈ -0.13 fm
- Class III force effect on scattering length: Δa^III ≈ 1.10 fm; on effective range: Δr^III_0 ≈ -0.03 fm
- Remaining trinucleon binding energy difference attributed to class III forces: ΔE_exp ≈ 76 ± 24 keV
- Class IV analyzing power difference at 477 MeV: ΔA = (47 ± 22 ± 8) × 10^{-4}
- Class IV analyzing power difference at 183 MeV: ΔA = (34.8 ± 6.2 ± 4.1) × 10^{-4}
- Class IV analyzing power difference at 347 MeV: ΔA = (59 ± 7 ± 2) × 10^{-4}
- Pion mass difference contribution to class II: Δa^II ≈ +2.9 fm; rho mass difference contribution: Δa^II ≈ +0.1 fm
- Gamma-pion exchange contribution to class II: Δa^II_γ-π ≈ -0.15 ± 0.03 fm
- Two-pion exchange contribution to class II: Δa^II_2π ≈ +0.9 fm
- Pseudoscalar meson mixing contribution to class III: Δa^III_πηη' ≈ +0.26 fm; ΔE ≈ 32 keV
- Vector meson mixing contribution to class III: Δa^III_ρω ≈ +1.5 fm; ΔE ≈ 90 keV
- Axial-vector meson mixing contribution to class III: Δa^III_a1f1 ≈ +0.13 fm; ΔE ≈ 15 keV
- Baryon mass difference in two-pion exchange contribution to class III: Δa^III ≈ +1.0 fm; ΔE ≈ 43 keV

## Context

This review extends the meson-exchange approach to isospin violation developed by Henley and Miller (1980s) and compares predictions with experimental data from the Nijmegen phase shift analyses and TRIUMF/IUCF measurements. The work enables future studies using chiral effective field theories, as suggested by Weinberg and van Kolck, which link isospin violation directly to QCD symmetries, and provides a foundation for understanding the trinucleon binding energy difference between 3H and 3He through Faddeev and quantum Monte Carlo calculations with modern NN potentials.
