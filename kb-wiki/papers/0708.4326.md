# The Tensor Part of the Skyrme Energy Density Functional

> We systematically study the effect of the J^2 tensor terms in the Skyrme energy functional on properties of spherical nuclei. We build a set of 36 parameterizations covering a wide range of the corresponding parameter space. We analyze the impact of the tensor terms on the evolution of single-particle-level splittings along chains of semi-magic nuclei in spherical calculations. We find that positive values of the coupling constants of proton-neutron and like-particle tensor terms allow for a qualitative description of the evolution of neutron and proton single-particle level splittings in chains of Ca, Ni and Sn isotopes.

## Key claim

Positive values of the coupling constants of proton-neutron and like-particle tensor terms allow for a qualitative description of the evolution of neutron and proton single-particle level splittings in chains of Ca, Ni and Sn isotopes, but the addition of these terms does not provide a global improvement of single-particle spectra and even deteriorates some aspects in doubly-magic nuclei.

## Method

The authors built a set of 36 parameterizations of the Skyrme energy density functional covering a wide range of the tensor-term parameter space, with coupling parameters α (like-particle) and β (proton-neutron) fixed independently while all other parameters were fitted according to a protocol similar to the Saclay-Lyon parameterizations. They analyzed the impact of the J² tensor terms on the evolution of single-particle-level splittings along chains of semi-magic nuclei in spherical calculations, examining the spin-orbit field modification when J varies due to filling of single-particle states.

## Key numbers

- The proton-neutron coupling β was set to 120 MeV·fm⁵ to reproduce the magnitude of single-particle-level spacing shifts in the tin chain
- The like-particle coupling α was constrained to the range α ≈ 120–150 MeV·fm⁵ from analysis of neutron level splittings in Ca and Ni isotopes
- The combined best-fit values from both constraints are α ∼ β ∼ 120 MeV·fm⁵
- The parameterizations are labelled TIJ, with α = 60·(J−2) MeV·fm⁵ and β = 60·(I−2) MeV·fm⁵

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tensor terms in nuclear energy density functionals by Otsuka (2005, 2006), Dobaczewski, and others, providing a systematic scan of the tensor parameter space rather than searching for a single "best" parameterization. The finding that tensor terms alone do not improve global single-particle spectra indicates that complementary work on the central and spin-orbit parts of the Skyrme functional is needed, as the current forms appear not flexible enough to accommodate large tensor terms.
