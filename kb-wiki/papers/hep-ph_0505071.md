# Towards a Precision Determination of alpha in B \to pi pi Decays

> An assumption of isospin symmetry permits the determination of sin(2 alpha) from the experimental study of B\to pi pi decays. Isospin, however, is merely an approximate symmetry; its breaking predicates a theoretical systematical error sigma_α^{\rm IB} in the extraction of alpha. We focus on the impact of π^0-η,η^\prime mixing, as well as the manner in which it is amenable to empirical constraint, and determine that σ_α^{\rm IB} can potentially be controlled to {\cal O}(1^\circ).

## Key claim

The paper argues that the irreducible theoretical error in extracting the CKM angle α from B→ππ decays due to isospin breaking from π⁰-η,η′ mixing can potentially be controlled to O(1°), but only if SU(3)ₓ symmetry breaking effects in the B→η(′) form factors are properly accounted for, contrary to earlier work by Gronau and Zupan (2005) which assumed SU(3)ₓ symmetry.

## Method

The author analyzes isospin breaking in B→ππ decays by focusing on π⁰-η,η′ mixing, parametrizing the effect through mixing angles ε and ε′ and computing the resulting shift in the isospin analysis using QCD factorization. The amplitudes for B→πη(′) and B→ππ are related through form factor ratios and hard-scattering kernels, and the parameter ξ that breaks the triangle relation is computed using the Feldmann-Kroll-Stech scheme for η-η′ mixing with Beneke and Neubert's (2003) QCD factorization results.

## Key numbers

* The form factor ratio F₀^{B→η}(0)/F₀^{B→π}(0) ranges from 0.83±0.02 to 0.89, compared to the SU(3)ₓ value of √(2/3) ≈ 0.82
* The form factor ratio F₀^{B→η′}(0)/F₀^{B→π}(0) ranges from 0.68±0.02 to 1.1, compared to the SU(3)ₓ value of √(1/3) ≈ 0.58
* The ratio Σ(η_qπ)/Σ(ππ) deviates from unity by approximately 1 - 5×10⁻³ due to SU(3)ₓ breaking in light-cone distribution functions
* The CP-averaged branching ratio for B→π⁺π⁰ is (5.5±0.6)×10⁻⁶ from HFAG (2005)
* The CP-averaged branching ratio for B→π⁺π⁻ is (4.5±0.4)×10⁻⁶ from HFAG (2005)
* The CP-averaged branching ratio for B→π⁰π⁰ is (1.45±0.29)×10⁻⁶ from HFAG (2005)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Gardner (2005) on π⁰-η,η′ mixing in B→ππ decays and corrects the reanalysis by Gronau and Zupan (2005), who neglected SU(3)ₓ breaking effects beyond η-η′ mixing. The paper shows that the SU(3)ₓ-symmetric assumption of Gronau and Zupan (2005) underestimates the form factor ratio for the η′ by nearly a factor of two. The results enable a more reliable estimate of the irreducible theoretical error in the extraction of α from B-factory data, and the author proposes that empirical data on B→πη(′) decays can provide direct constraints on the isospin-breaking parameter ξ.
