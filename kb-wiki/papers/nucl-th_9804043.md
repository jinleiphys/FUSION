# Photoproduction of phi mesons from the proton: Polarization observables and strangeness in the nucleon

> The polarization observables in phi meson photoproduction is studied to probe the strangeness content of the nucleon. In addition to the dominant diffractive production and the one-pion-exchange process, we take into account the direct knockout mechanism that arises from the possible hidden strangeness content of the nucleon. We find that some double polarization observables are very sensitive to the strangeness content of the proton because of the different spin structures of the amplitudes associated with different mechanisms. This suggests that such measurements could be very useful in probing the strangeness content in the proton. The orbitally excited quark-cluster configurations in the proton are included in the calculation and found to have little effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that certain double polarization observables in phi meson photoproduction are highly sensitive to the hidden strangeness content of the proton, because the direct knockout mechanism (arising from a possible intrinsic s̄s component in the nucleon) has a spin structure distinct from the dominant diffractive and one-pion-exchange processes. The authors hedge that this sensitivity is a theoretical prediction based on a specific relativistic quark model, and that the orbitally excited quark-cluster configurations in the proton have little effect on the observables.

## Method

The authors model phi meson photoproduction (γ + p → φ + p) by combining three mechanisms: diffractive production via Pomeron exchange (described with a microscopic quark-Pomeron interaction and gauge-invariant VDM amplitude), one-pion-exchange (OPE) with effective φγπ and πNN couplings and Benecke-Dürr form factors, and direct knockout of an s̄s or uud cluster from a 5-quark component of the proton wave function. They compute helicity amplitudes for each process and evaluate single and double polarization observables using the density matrix formalism, assuming a strangeness admixture B² of 3–5% in the proton.

## Key numbers

- The diffractive production cross section is parameterized with b_φ = 4.01 GeV⁻² and σ_γ(W) = 0.2 μb around W = 2–3 GeV.
- The φ → γπ⁰ decay width is Γ = 5.8 × 10⁻⁶ GeV, giving an effective coupling g̃_{φγπ} = 0.042 GeV⁻¹.
- The pure VDM prediction for Γ(φ → γπ) is 1.65 × 10⁻⁵ GeV, overestimating the experimental width by a factor of 3.
- The φ → γη decay width is 5.58 × 10⁻⁵ GeV, with coupling g_{φγη} = 0.218 GeV⁻¹.
- The πNN coupling constant is g_{πNN}²/(4π) = 14.3.
- The strangeness admixture B² in the proton is estimated to be 3–5% from earlier electroproduction work by the same authors (Titov et al., 1994; Titov et al., 1997), and 1–19% from p̄p annihilation analyses by Ellis et al. (1995).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Henley et al. (1992) and Hwang et al. (1992) on φ electroproduction, which used nonrelativistic quark models and found the knockout contribution comparable to VDM with a 10–20% strange sea admixture. The present authors improved those calculations using a relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model (Titov et al., 1994; Titov et al., 1997), showing that the upper bound on strangeness content could be lowered to 3–5%. The paper also builds on the suggestion by Henley et al. (1992) and Laget (1993) that spin observables could distinguish knockout from VDM, and it provides a more complete set of polarization observables than the authors' earlier work (Titov et al., 1997). The results enable experimental tests of hidden strangeness in the nucleon through double polarization measurements in φ photoproduction, complementing studies of OZI violation in p̄p annihilation (e.g., Asterix, Crystal Barrel, and Obelix collaborations).
