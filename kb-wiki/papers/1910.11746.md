# Hadron-in-jet production at partonic threshold

> We consider the longitudinal momentum distribution of hadrons inside jets in proton-proton collisions. At partonic threshold large double logarithmic corrections arise which need to be resummed to all orders. We develop a factorization formalism within SCET that allows for the joint resummation of threshold and jet radius logarithms. We achieve next-to-leading logarithmic NLL$'$ accuracy by including non-global logarithms in the leading-color approximation. Overall, we find that the threshold resummation leads to a sizable enhancement of the cross section and a reduced QCD scale dependence, suggesting that the all-order resummation can be important for the reliable extraction of fragmentation functions in global analyses when jet substructure data is included.

## Key claim
The authors develop a joint resummation framework for threshold logarithms and jet radius logarithms in hadron-in-jet production, finding that threshold resummation leads to a sizable enhancement of the cross section and reduced QCD scale dependence, which may be important for reliable extraction of fragmentation functions when jet substructure data is included.

## Method
The authors use Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to refactorize the jet function at partonic threshold (z_h → 1), separating it into hard matching coefficients, a collinear-soft function, a non-global logarithm soft function, and fragmentation functions. They solve the renormalization group evolution equations in Mellin moment space to resum threshold logarithms to NLL' accuracy, while jet radius logarithms are resummed through DGLAP evolution of the jet function between scales p_T R and p_T. Non-global logarithms are included at leading-logarithmic accuracy in the leading-color approximation using results from Dasgupta and Salam (2001).

## Key numbers
- The threshold resummed cross section shows a ratio to NLO of approximately 1.4–1.6 for z_h values near 0.8–0.9 at LHC kinematics with p_T = 100 GeV, √s = 13 TeV, and R = 0.4 (from Figure 1, reading approximate values)
- The NLO cross section in the threshold limit relative to full NLO shows a ratio of approximately 0.6–0.8 for z_h near 0.8–0.9 (from Figure 1, reading approximate values)
- The gluon channel contribution relative to the total cross section shows a ratio of approximately 0.3–0.5 for z_h near 0.8–0.9 (from Figure 2, reading approximate values)
- The leading non-global logarithms at NNLO are given by S^NG_d(z_h,μ) = δ(1-z_h) - (α_s^2 C_A C_d / 6) (ln(1-z_h)/(1-z_h))_+

## Context
This work extends the theoretical description of the longitudinal momentum distribution of hadrons inside jets (first studied in exclusive jet production by Procura and Stewart (2009) and Jain et al. (2011)) to the threshold region z_h → 1. It builds on NLO calculations by Arleo et al. (2013) and the narrow jet approximation with jet radius logarithm resummation by Kaufmann et al. (2015) and Kang et al. (2016). The joint resummation framework enables future extractions of threshold-resummed fragmentation functions in global analyses that include jet substructure data, and can be extended to transverse momentum dependent jet substructure studies.
