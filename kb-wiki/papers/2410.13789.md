# Neutron-neutron distribution of the triton from pionless EFT

> We compute the neutron-neutron relative-energy distribution of the triton following the hard knockout of the proton in pionless effective field theory. This distribution can be used to study universality as well as to obtain information on the neutron-neutron interaction. Especially, one can infer the scattering length from fitting theory predictions for the shape of the distribution to experimental data. To obtain the distribution for the triton, we first solve the ground-state three-body problem using momentum-space Faddeev equations. Next, we include the neutron-neutron final-state interaction by applying the corresponding Møller operator to the ground state. We present leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) pionless effective field theory results with quantified uncertainties. At NLO, we include the effective ranges semi-perturbatively. We conclude, that pionless EFT works reliably as expected and that the neutron-neutron distribution of the triton shows a significant sensitivity to the scattering length.

## Key claim

Pionless effective field theory (EFT) provides reliable predictions for the neutron-neutron relative-energy distribution of the triton following hard proton knockout, and the distribution shows significant sensitivity to the neutron-neutron scattering length, enabling its extraction from experimental data; however, the ground-state distribution without final-state interactions shows negligible sensitivity to the scattering length.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body ground-state problem for the triton using momentum-space Faddeev equations in pionless EFT at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), with the triton binding energy serving as the renormalization condition for the three-body force. They then included the neutron-neutron final-state interaction by applying the corresponding Møller operator to the ground-state wave function, using an exact t-matrix method that incorporates the neutron-neutron t-matrix and the ground-state wave function. At NLO, the effective ranges were included semi-perturbatively by expanding the reduced t-matrix in the effective range term to avoid spurious poles.

## Key numbers

- The triton binding energy used as renormalization condition: \(B_3 = 8.48\) MeV.
- The neutron-neutron scattering length was varied over four values: \(\{-16.7, -18.7, -20.7, -23.714\}\) fm.
- The neutron-proton spin-singlet scattering length: \(a_{np} = -23.736\) fm.
- The deuteron binding momentum: \(\gamma_d = 45.7021\) MeV.
- The neutron-neutron effective range: \(r_{nn} = 2.84\) fm; neutron-proton effective range: \(r_{np} = 2.704\) fm; deuteron effective range: \(\rho_d = 1.752\) fm.
- The peak of the ground-state distribution occurs at \(E_{nn} = 2.62\) MeV for all scattering length values at both LO and NLO.
- The relative deviation of the NLO ground-state result from the LO result is less than 1% at relative energies of 1 MeV and less than 10% at 10 MeV.
- The relative difference between the LO and NLO curves with final-state interactions for \(a_{nn} = -18.7\) fm is less than 5% for energies \(E_{nn} \leq 1\) MeV and reaches about 10% at \(E_{nn} = 2\) MeV.
- Changing the cutoff from 700 MeV to 1050 MeV changes the distribution below 2 MeV by up to about 1%.

## Context

This work extends the methodology previously applied to the two-neutron halo nucleus \(^6\)He by Gobel et al. (2021) to the triton system, using pionless EFT rather than halo EFT. The calculation is motivated by a proposed experiment at RIKEN/RIBF by Aumann et al. (2018) to determine the neutron-neutron scattering length from the reactions \(^6\)He(\(p,p\alpha\))\(nn\) and \(t(p,2p)nn\) in inverse kinematics. The results enable a theory-driven extraction of the neutron-neutron scattering length from future experimental data, addressing the current tension between different experimental determinations of this fundamental parameter.
