# Scaling within the Spectral Function approach

> Scaling features of the nuclear electromagnetic response functions unveil aspects of nuclear dynamics that are crucial for interpretating neutrino- and electron-scattering data. In the large momentum-transfer regime, the nucleon-density response function defines a universal scaling function, which is independent of the nature of the probe. In this work, we analyze the nucleon-density response function of $^{12}$C, neglecting collective excitations. We employ particle and hole spectral functions obtained within two distinct many-body methods, both widely used to describe electroweak reactions in nuclei. We show that the two approaches provide compatible nucleon-density scaling functions that for large momentum transfers satisfy first-kind scaling. Both methods yield scaling functions characterized by an asymmetric shape, although less pronounced than that of experimental scaling functions. This asymmetry, only mildly affected by final state interactions, is mostly due to nucleon-nucleon correlations, encoded in the continuum component of the hole SF.

## Key claim

The nucleon-density scaling functions computed from two distinct many-body methods (CBF-SF and LDA-based) are compatible and satisfy first-kind scaling at large momentum transfers, but both yield a less pronounced asymmetric shape than experimental scaling functions; this asymmetry is primarily due to nucleon-nucleon correlations encoded in the continuum component of the hole spectral function, with final-state interactions having only a mild effect.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleon-density response function of 12C using two approaches: (1) the Correlated Basis Function Spectral Function (CBF-SF) formalism, which employs realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions and a non-relativistic many-body treatment of the hole spectral function, with final-state interactions included via a convolution scheme; and (2) a Local Density Approximation (LDA) approach based on a semi-phenomenological nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter, where relativistic corrections are included and the particle spectral function accounts for final-state interactions. Both methods were used to evaluate the scaling function f(ψ) = p_F × 2κ S(q,ω) / N, and results were compared with experimental scaling functions extracted from electron-scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy per nucleon in carbon obtained from the LDA model is |ε_A| = 7.8 MeV, with the parameter C ≈ 0.8 fm² providing around 25-30 MeV repulsion at ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The CBF-SF hole spectral function includes a 1h contribution from modified mean-field states (s and p states of 12C) with spectroscopic factors Z_α < 1 and widths taken from (e,e′p) data analyses (Mougey 1976, Dutta 2003).
- The LDA model uses a Fermi momentum p_F = (3πρ/2)^(1/3) for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, with the chemical potential determined self-consistently from the nucleon self-energy.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of scaling properties of nuclear electromagnetic responses from the Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) approach of Rocco (2017) to the spectral function formalism, which allows identification of the mechanisms responsible for the asymmetric shape of scaling functions. The CBF-SF model has been previously used to describe electron-scattering data (Benhar 2006, Ankowski 2014) and neutrino-nucleus interactions (Benhar 2005, 2006, 2009, 2010, Vagnoni 2017), while the LDA approach has been employed in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering studies (Nieves 2004, 2005). The results enable validation of spectral function methods against experimental scaling functions and support the use of empirical scaling functions extracted from electron data to predict neutrino-nucleus cross sections.
