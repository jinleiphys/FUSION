# Studying explicit $U(1)_A$ symmetry breaking in hot and magnetised two flavour non-local NJL model constrained using lattice results

> We study the two flavour non-local Nambu\textemdash Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the presence of a magnetic field and explore the chiral crossover in presence of a non-local form of the 't Hooft determinant term. Its coupling is governed by a dimensionless parameter $c$. This term is responsible for the explicit breaking of $U(1)_A$ symmetry. We have attempted a systematic analysis of the model parameters by fitting to self-consistent lattice QCD calculations. Three parameters of the model are fixed by $eB=0$ results from published lattice QCD on the chiral condensate, the pion decay constant ($F_π$), and the pion mass ($m_π$). The difference of the $u$ and $d$ quark condensates in the presence of a magnetic field ($eB$) is quite sensitive to $c$ and we fix $c$ using published lattice QCD results for this observable. We see no evidence that $c$ depends on $eB$. The crossover temperature decreases with increasing $eB$ only for condensate values at the lower end of the allowed values (as already seen in~\cite{Pagura:2016pwr}) and $F_π$ at the upper end of the allowed values. We further check our model predictions by calculating the topological susceptibility with the fitted $c$ values and comparing it with lattice results. Since the topological susceptibility is related to the extent of the $U(1)_A$ symmetry breaking, we find that it is sensitive to the value of $c$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a non-local NJL model with a 't Hooft determinant term, whose coupling strength is governed by a dimensionless parameter \(c\), can describe lattice QCD results for the difference of \(u\) and \(d\) quark condensates in a magnetic field using a \(c\) that does not depend on \(eB\); the crossover temperature decreases with increasing \(eB\) only for condensate values at the lower end of the allowed range and pion decay constant at the upper end.

## Method

The authors study a two-flavour non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Gaussian form factor, including a non-local 't Hooft determinant term that explicitly breaks \(U(1)_A\) symmetry. Three model parameters (the four-Fermi coupling \(G_0\), the cutoff \(\Lambda\), and the bare quark mass \(m\)) are fixed at \(eB=0\) using lattice QCD results for the chiral condensate, pion decay constant \(F_\pi\), and pion mass \(m_\pi\). The \(U(1)_A\) breaking parameter \(c\) is then fixed by fitting to lattice QCD results for the difference of \(u\) and \(d\) quark condensates as a function of magnetic field at zero temperature. The model is extended to finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism, and the gap equations for the mean fields \(\sigma_s\) and \(\pi_s^3\) are solved numerically.

## Key numbers

* The chiral condensate at \(eB=0\) is taken from lattice QCD with central value \(\langle\bar{\psi}_f\psi_f\rangle^{1/3}=240\ {\rm MeV}\) (JLQCD collaboration, renormalisation scale 2 GeV) and an alternative value of \(261\ {\rm MeV}\) (Brandt et al.).
* The parameter \(c\) is constrained to the range \(c\in[0,1/2]\); \(c=1/2\) corresponds to the standard NJL model where the \(U(1)_A\) symmetric and breaking terms have equal strength.
* The crossover temperature decreases with increasing \(eB\) only for condensate values at the lower end of the allowed range (between \(210\) and \(240\ {\rm MeV}\) as considered in Pagura (2016)) and \(F_\pi\) at the upper end of its allowed values.

## Context

This work extends the non-local NJL model studies of Pagura (2016) and Gomez Dumm (2006, 2017) by adding an arbitrary-strength 't Hooft determinant term and performing a systematic fit to self-consistent lattice QCD results. It builds on the observation that the non-local NJL model naturally produces inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) near the crossover temperature, in contrast to local NJL models which require an explicit \(eB\)-dependent coupling. The fitted \(c\) values enable predictions for the topological susceptibility as a function of temperature and magnetic field, which can be compared with future lattice calculations to further test the model.
