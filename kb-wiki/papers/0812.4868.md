# The neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb and the density dependence of the symmetry energy

> We explore neutron skin predictions for $^{208}$Pb in relation to the symmetry pressure in various microscopic models based on realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials and either the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach or the conventional Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework implemented with three-body forces. We also discuss the correlation between the neutron skin and the radius of a fixed-mass neutron star.

## Key claim

The neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb correlates with the symmetry pressure $L$, but the relation is not strictly linear across different microscopic models because the symmetric matter equation of state also influences the neutron distribution; a measurement of the skin to 0.05 fm accuracy should discriminate among these microscopic models.

## Method

The authors calculated proton and neutron density distributions for $^{208}$Pb using an energy functional based on the semi-empirical mass formula, with the volume and symmetry terms taken from the isospin-asymmetric equation of state. They employed their own Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) model using the Bonn B nucleon-nucleon potential, and compared results with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations that include three-body forces (Argonne V18, Bonn B, Nijmegen 93, and Urbana UIX). Density distributions were obtained by minimizing the energy assuming Thomas-Fermi distributions.

## Key numbers

- DBHF model predicts proton root-mean-square radius of $^{208}$Pb: 5.39 fm; neutron root-mean-square radius: 5.56 fm.
- Symmetry energy at saturation density for all models ranges from 29 to 34 MeV.
- The $L$ parameter (symmetry pressure) for the microscopic models ranges from about 70 to 106 MeV.
- Heavy-ion data impose boundaries for $L$ at $85 \pm 25$ MeV (Chen 2007, Danielewicz 2007).
- Neutron star radius for a 1.4 $M_\odot$ star: DBHF predicts 12.4 km; UIX predicts 11.8 km; V18 predicts 12.3 km; BOB predicts 12.4 km; N93 predicts 13.0 km.
- Central energy density for a 1.4 $M_\odot$ neutron star: DBHF predicts $6.5 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$; UIX predicts $9.05 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$; V18 predicts $6.89 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$; BOB predicts $6.16 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$; N93 predicts $5.91 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DBHF calculations (Sammarruca 2008) and compares them with BHF calculations including three-body forces from the Catania group (2008). It builds on the established correlation between neutron skin and symmetry pressure found in phenomenological Skyrme models (Li 2005) and heavy-ion data constraints (Chen 2007, Danielewski 2007). The results enable future discrimination among microscopic models using the upcoming parity-violating electron scattering measurement of the $^{208}$Pb neutron radius at Jefferson Laboratory (Horowitz 2001, Piekarewicz 2006), and inform predictions for neutron star radii.
