# First-order QCD transition in a primordial magnetic field

> Recalling the expectation of an extremely strong primordial magnetic field $H$, we recheck transitions among the phases of chiral symmetry restoration ($χSR$), chiral symmetry breaking ($χSB$), and pion superfluidity ($πSF$)in the QCD epoch of the early Universe. For homogeneous phases in a finite $H$, a sensible scheme is adopted to determine the phase boundaries of $πSF$, which is also superconductivity phase itself. In the first part, the QCD phase diagrams are studied in detail within the chiral effective Polyakov-Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and the transitions involving $πSF$ are found to be of first order at relatively small $H$. As expected from the Meissner effect, the regime of $πSF$ shrinks with increasing $H$ and completely vanishes beyond a threshold value. In the second part, the bubble dynamics is illuminated for the stronger first-order transition, $χSR\rightarrowπSF$, in the more convenient Polyakov-quark-meson model. The coupled equations of motion of pion condensate and magnetic field are solved consistently to give the bubble structure. Then, based on bubble collisions, we explore gravitational wave (GW) emission by developing a simple toy model in advance; and the characteristic frequency of the relic GW is estimated to be of the order $0.1-1\,{\rm K}$ or $10^9-10^{10}\,{\rm Hz}$ in our galaxy.

## Key claim

First-order QCD transitions involving pion superfluidity, previously expected to be second-order, can occur in the early Universe when a sufficiently strong primordial magnetic field is present; the pion superfluidity regime shrinks with increasing magnetic field due to the Meissner effect and vanishes entirely beyond a threshold value.

## Method

The author uses the three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to compute QCD phase diagrams with homogeneous phases in a finite external magnetic field, treating the magnetic field as a thermodynamic variable via a Gibbs free energy transformation to account for the Meissner effect in the superconducting pion superfluidity phase. For the stronger first-order transition from chiral symmetry restoration to pion superfluidity, the Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model is employed to solve the coupled equations of motion for the pion condensate and magnetic field, yielding bubble structure; a toy model of bubble collisions is then developed to estimate gravitational wave emission.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic frequency of the relic gravitational wave is estimated to be of order 0.1–1 K or 10⁹–10¹⁰ Hz in our galaxy.
- The reduced temperature parameter for the Polyakov loop potential is T₀ = 0.27 GeV.
- The threshold magnetic field beyond which pion superfluidity completely vanishes is not given numerically in the text.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study Cao (2021) by incorporating a primordial magnetic field into the QCD epoch phase diagram, where charged pion superfluidity was previously found to be favored at large light lepton densities. While gravitational wave emission from first-order electroweak transitions has been widely studied (Kosowsky 1992, Child 2012, Lewicki 2020, Wei 2022), this is presented as the first time a reliable first-order QCD transition is applied to generate gravitational waves directly in the QCD epoch without inflation effects. The work enables future studies of gravitational wave signatures from the early Universe's QCD transition.
