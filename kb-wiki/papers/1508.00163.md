# Excited-state contamination in nucleon correlators from chiral perturbation theory

> Techniques to compute hadron properties from lattice QCD rely upon the limit of long time separation. For baryons, the signal-to-noise problem often restricts one to time separations that are not ideally long, and for which couplings to excited states can obstruct the isolation of ground-state baryon properties. We consider excited-state contamination in nucleon two- and three-point functions. Using chiral perturbation theory, we determine couplings to pion-nucleon and pion-delta excited states. In two-point functions, these contributions are small, in accordance with general properties of the spectral weights on a torus. For the axial-current correlation function in the nucleon, the sign of excited-state contributions suggests overestimation of the nucleon axial charge. Thus contamination from pion-nucleon excited states will not likely explain the trend in lattice QCD data.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory, the sign of excited-state contamination from pion-nucleon states in the nucleon axial-current three-point function suggests an *overestimation* of the nucleon axial charge, which is opposite to the trend observed in lattice QCD data, where plateaus are approached from below; therefore such contamination cannot explain the underestimation seen in lattice calculations.

## Method

The author computed nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions in Euclidean time using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, mapping lattice nucleon interpolating operators (which transform as (2_L,1_R) ⊕ (1_L,2_R) under chiral symmetry) into effective field theory operators. For the two-point function, the spectral weights for discrete pion-nucleon states on a periodic spatial torus were derived; for the three-point function of the isovector axial-vector current, the ratio G_A(τ,t) was evaluated as a function of source-sink separation τ and current-insertion time t.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f = 132 MeV.
- Lattice spacing used in plots: a = 0.1 fm on a lattice of size L = 48 a.
- For the two-point function effective mass plot, pion-nucleon excited-state contamination is at the few-percent level for typical lattice parameters; for τ/a > 5, the correlator is saturated by pion-nucleon states with momentum mode numbers satisfying √(n²) < 4.
- For the axial three-point function, plots show G_A(τ,t) for source-sink separations τ/a = 10, 12, and 14, with a = 0.1 fm and L = 48 a; excited states drive the correlator upward, potentially leading to overestimation of g_A by a few percent at most.

## Context

This work extends earlier continuum calculations of excited-state contamination in nucleon correlators (Tiburzi 2009) by treating the discrete pion-nucleon and pion-delta states available on a periodic lattice (Tiburzi 2015). It also builds on the mapping of lattice nucleon operators into chiral perturbation theory (Bar 2015) and on studies of chiral properties of baryon operators (Nagata 2008). The results enable lattice practitioners to interpret the sign and magnitude of excited-state contamination in nucleon axial-charge extractions, and suggest that future work should compute contamination for other observables (scalar charge, tensor charge, quark momentum fraction) and compare with lattice data using well-localized interpolating fields.
