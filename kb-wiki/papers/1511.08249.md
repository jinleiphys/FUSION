# Comment on "Evidence for narrow resonant structures at $W\approx$ 1.68 GeV and $W\approx$ 1.72 GeV in real Compton scattering off the proton"

> We comment on the statement by Kuznetsov et al. that the structure around W=1.72 GeV seen in the beam asymmetry in Compton scattering off the proton is not observed in the total cross section of $η$ photoproduction on the neutron.

# Comment on "Evidence for narrow resonant structures at W≈1.68 GeV and W≈1.72 GeV in real Compton scattering off the proton"

## Key claim

Contrary to the statement by Kuznetsov et al. that the structure around W=1.72 GeV seen in the beam asymmetry in Compton scattering off the proton is not observed in the total cross section of eta photoproduction on the neutron, the authors demonstrate that a narrow, small but statistically significant structure is in fact present at the same invariant mass in already published data for eta photoproduction off the neutron from the MAMI facility.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed previously published excitation functions for quasifree photoproduction of eta mesons off neutrons bound in deuterium and in helium-3 nuclei, using finer energy binning than in the original publications. They fitted the neutron data with the same parametrization used in Werthmueller (2014), adding an additional Breit-Wigner function to account for the structure near W=1720 MeV, and compared the results with the beam asymmetry data from Kuznetsov et al. (2015).

## Key numbers

- The position of the narrow structure in eta photoproduction off the neutron was determined to be M = (1728 ± 1) MeV, with a width of Γ = (15 ± 4) MeV (statistical uncertainties only).
- The structure at smaller W in Compton scattering is observed at 1681 MeV, with a counterpart in eta photoproduction off the neutron where peak positions from different measurements deviate by up to 15 MeV.
- The production threshold of omega mesons off the free neutron is W_thr ≈ 1722 MeV, close to the observed structure position.

## Context

This comment corrects a claim in Kuznetsov et al. (2015) regarding the absence of the 1.72 GeV structure in eta photoproduction off the neutron, which had been studied at GRAAL (Kuznetsov 2007), ELSA (Jaegle 2008, 2011), and MAMI (Werthmueller 2013, Witthauer 2013, Werthmueller 2014). The prominent structure at W≈1670 MeV in eta production has been interpreted by Anisovich (2015) in the Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis as an interference pattern in the S11 partial wave between the N(1535)1/2- and N(1650)1/2- states. The comment enables a combined analysis of eta photoproduction and Compton scattering off both protons and neutrons to clarify the nature of these spectral features, as already suggested by Kuznetsov et al. (2015).
