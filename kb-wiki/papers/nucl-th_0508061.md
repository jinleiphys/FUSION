# Dynamics of hyperon--antihyperon production

> The dynamics of the strangeness-exchange reactions antiproton-proton to AntiLambda-Lambda, AntiLambda-Sigma or AntiSigma-Sigma is discussed, either at the hadron level, in terms of strange-meson exchange, or at the quark level, in terms of quark--antiquark creation or annihilation.

# Dynamics of hyperon–antihyperon production

## Key claim

The meson-exchange and quark-inspired models both reproduce the main features of strangeness-exchange reactions such as antiproton-proton to AntiLambda-Lambda, but they differ in their predictions for spin observables; the actual data fell between the two sets of predictions, indicating that additional effects such as limited knowledge of the initial-state spin correlations and the final-state interaction must be accounted for.

## Method

The paper reviews two complementary theoretical approaches to strangeness-exchange reactions: a hadronic picture based on strange-meson exchange (K, K*) generalized from the nucleon–antinucleon potential via the G-parity rule, and a quark-level picture based on quark–antiquark annihilation and creation (including the 3P0 model and sea-quark contributions). Both approaches are supplemented by models of initial- and final-state interactions, and their predictions are compared to data from the PS185 experiment at LEAR, which used a transversely-polarized target to measure spin observables.

## Key numbers

- The analyzing power and energy-shifts of the P-state levels of protonium are well reproduced by meson-exchange models, with annihilation acting up to about 1 fm.
- The cross-section for AntiLambda-Lambda production showed a peak-like structure as a function of incoming energy in early data, but the peak was not confirmed in a more detailed scan of the energy range just above threshold.
- For the reaction pp to AntiSigma+Sigma-, Holinde et al. found that once all ingredients of their calculation are included, the production is not too much suppressed, contrary to the expectation from simple quark diagrams requiring exotic exchange.
- Haidenbauer et al. estimated the cross-section for AntiXi0Xi0 to be significantly smaller than for AntiXi+Xi-.

## Context

This review extends the meson-exchange approach developed for nucleon–antinucleon scattering (Bryan and Philips, Dover and Richard, Kohno and Weise) to strangeness-exchange channels, and contrasts it with quark-model approaches (Alberg et al., Le Yaouanc et al.). The PS185 experiment provided spin observables that discriminate between these pictures, and the work enables future studies at hadron facilities of related questions such as resonance decay, flavor SU(3) symmetry violation, and the OZI rule. The paper also discusses the possibility of extracting amplitudes for the 1/2+1/2 to 1/2+1/2 reaction, as attempted by Bugg (2004) and the PS185 collaboration.
