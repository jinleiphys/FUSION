# Ab initio calculation of the electron capture spectrum of 163Ho: Auger-Meitner decay into continuum states

> Determining the electron neutrino mass by electron capture in $^{163}$Ho relies on an accurate understanding of the differential electron capture nuclear decay rate as a function of the distribution of the total decay energy between the neutrino and electronic excitations. The resulting spectrum is dominated by resonances due to local atomic multiplet states with core holes. Coulomb scattering between electrons couple the discrete atomic states, via Auger-Meitner decay, to final states with free electrons. The atomic multiplets are above the auto-ionisation energy, such that the delta functions representing these discrete levels turn into a superposition of Lorentzian, Mahan- and Fano-like line-shapes. We present an \textit{ab initio} method to calculate nuclear decay modifications due to such processes. It includes states with multiple correlated holes in local atomic orbitals interacting with unbound Auger-Meitner electrons. A strong energy-dependent, asymmetric broadening of the resonances in good agreement with recent experiments is found. We present a detailed analysis of the mechanisms determining the final spectral line-shape and discuss both the Fano interference between different resonances, as well as the energy dependence of the Auger-Meitner Coulomb matrix elements. The latter mechanism is shown to be the dominant channel responsible for the asymmetric line-shape of the resonances in the electron capture spectrum of $^{163}$Ho.

## Key claim

The asymmetric line-shape of resonances in the electron capture spectrum of 163Ho is dominated by the energy dependence of the Auger-Meitner Coulomb matrix elements, not primarily by Fano interference between different resonances, though both mechanisms contribute.

## Method

The authors extended their previous method (Brass 2018) by including Auger-Meitner decay into continuum states via a self-energy formalism. They solved the Dirac Coulomb equations for a single neutral isolated Ho atom, treating the weak interaction as a perturbation, and calculated the self-energy matrix (226 by 226) coupling bound-state resonances to states with one free Auger-Meitner electron. The resulting differential electron capture decay rate was computed using Green's functions in the Lehmann representation.

## Key numbers

- The assumed total decay energy Q = 2838 eV, taken from experiment (Eliseev 2015, Clemens 2019)
- The M1 edge (capture of a 3s electron) has its experimental maximum at 2040 eV, at 2019 eV in the theory including only bound states, and at 2028 eV in the theory including the self energy due to Auger-Meitner decay
- The ground-state energy correction due to unbound orbitals shifts the spectrum by 15.76 eV
- The theoretical spectra were broadened by a Gaussian of 8 eV to account for experimental resolution
- The Coulomb-Slater integral for the [Ho]4s to [Ho]4p4d + e-ϵf channel has a maximum at 322 eV kinetic energy of the Auger-Meitner electron and a zero at 2037 eV

## Context

This work extends the bound-state-only calculations of Brass (2018) by including Auger-Meitner decay into continuum states, and addresses the asymmetric line-shape observed experimentally by Clemens (2019). The method is adapted from core-level x-ray spectroscopy techniques (deGroot 2008, Haverkort 2012, 2014). The results enable more accurate modeling of the endpoint region of the 163Ho electron capture spectrum, which is critical for determining the electron neutrino mass in experiments such as ECHO. Previous work by Robertson (2015) concluded that the spectrum shape was not understood well enough for a sensitive neutrino mass determination; this work provides a more complete theoretical description.
