# Searching for strange hidden-charm pentaquark state $P_{cs}(4459)$ in $γp\to{}K^{+}P_{cs}(4459)$ reaction

> We investigate the possibility of studying the strange hidden-charm pentaquark state $P_{cs}(4459)$ by photon-induced reactions on a proton target in an effective Lagrangian approach. The production process is described by the $t$-channel $K^{-}$ exchange, the $u$-channel $Λ$ exchange, the contract term, and the $s$- channel nucleon pole. Our theoretical approach is based on the assumption that $P_{cs}(4459)$ with $J^{P}=1/2^{-}$ or $J^{P}=3/2^{-}$ can be interpreted as a molecule composed of $\bar{D}^{*}Ξ_c$. Using the coupling constants of the $P^{J^P}_{cs}$ to $γΛ$ and $K^{-}p$ channels obtained from molecule picture of the $P^{J^{P}}_{cs}(4459)$, the total cross-sections of the process $γp\to{}P^{J^P}_{cs}K^{+}$ is evaluated. Our calculation indicates that the cross-section for $γp\to{}P^{1/2^{-}}_{cs}K^{+}$ and $γp\to{}P^{3/2^{-}}_{cs}K^{+}$ are of the order of 10.0 pb and 5.0 pb, respectively. In addition, we compute the cross-section by assuming $P_{cs}(4459)$ as a compact pentaquark and find it is quite different from the results of $\bar{D}^{*}Ξ_c$ molecule. Those results can be measured in future experiments, such as the Electron-Ion Collider in China and the United States. And can be used to test the nature of the $P_{cs}$.

## Key claim

The cross-sections for photoproduction of the strange hidden-charm pentaquark state \(P_{cs}(4459)\) via \(\gamma p \to K^{+}P_{cs}\) are predicted to be of order 10 pb for \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) and 5 pb for \(J^{P}=3/2^{-}\) under the molecular \(\bar{D}^{*}\Xi_c\) interpretation, but these values depend on the assumed spin-parity and on the model parameters such as the cutoff \(\Lambda_{K^{-}}\); the cross-sections differ substantially if the state is instead a compact pentaquark, providing a way to distinguish the two interpretations.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach to compute the total cross-section of \(\gamma p \to K^{+}P_{cs}(4459)\) at photon beam momenta from threshold up to 22.0 GeV. They consider four Feynman diagrams: \(t\)-channel \(K^{-}\) exchange, \(u\)-channel \(\Lambda\) exchange, \(s\)-channel nucleon pole, and a contact term required for gauge invariance. Coupling constants for the \(P_{cs}\gamma\Lambda\) and \(P_{cs}K^{-}p\) vertices are obtained from the partial decay widths of \(P_{cs}\) into \(\gamma\Lambda\) and \(K^{-}p\), which are calculated via hadronic loops assuming a \(\bar{D}^{*}\Xi_c\) molecular structure with either \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) or \(3/2^{-}\). For comparison, the cross-section is also evaluated assuming \(P_{cs}(4459)\) is a compact pentaquark using vector meson dominance and the known decay width \(\Gamma(P_{cs}\to J/\psi\Lambda)=7.94\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* Mass of \(P_{cs}(4459)\): \(M = 4458.8 \pm 2.9^{+4.7}_{-1.1}\) MeV; width: \(\Gamma = 17.3 \pm 6.5^{+8.0}_{-5.7}\) MeV (LHCb measurement).
* For the molecular \(\bar{D}^{*}\Xi_c\) picture with \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\): partial decay width \(\Gamma(P_{cs}\to\gamma\Lambda)=63.83\) keV, coupling \(h=0.035\); \(\Gamma(P_{cs}\to K^{-}p)=2.05\) keV, coupling \(g_{KNP_{cs}}=0.0041\).
* For the molecular picture with \(J^{P}=3/2^{-}\): \(\Gamma(P_{cs}\to\gamma\Lambda)=34.18\) keV, coupling \(h_1=0.05\), \(h_2=0\); \(\Gamma(P_{cs}\to K^{-}p)=0.24\) keV, coupling \(g_{KNP_{cs}}=0.0017\).
* Total cross-section for molecular \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) at \(E_{\gamma}=19.0\) GeV: 5.515 pb; for \(J^{P}=3/2^{-}\) at the same energy: 1.464 pb.
* For the compact pentaquark assumption with \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) at \(E_{\gamma}=20.0\) GeV: total cross-section 1509.36 pb; for \(J^{P}=3/2^{-}\) at the same energy: 7.84 pb.
* Coupling constant for compact pentaquark: \(g^{1/2^{-}}_{P_{cs}\Lambda J/\psi}=0.299\), \(g^{3/2^{-}}_{P_{cs}\Lambda J/\psi}=0.453\), derived from \(\Gamma(P_{cs}\to J/\psi\Lambda)=7.94\) MeV and \(m_{P_{cs}}=4458\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the \(P_{cs}(4459)\) state, first observed by LHCb (2020) in \(\Xi_b^{-}\to J/\psi\Lambda K^{-}\) decays, by proposing photoproduction as an alternative production mechanism at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC) and the US Electron-Ion Collider (US-EIC). The molecular interpretation follows earlier studies by Chen (2020), Xiao (2021), Peng (2020), Yang (2021), and Zhu (2021) who assigned \(P_{cs}(4459)\) as a \(\bar{D}^{*}\Xi_c\) molecule, while the compact pentaquark scenario follows predictions by Santopinto (2016). The results enable experimental tests of the internal structure of \(P_{cs}(4459)\) by comparing measured photoproduction cross-sections with the distinct predictions for the molecular versus compact pentaquark interpretations.
