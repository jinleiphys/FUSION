# Influence of temperature dependent shear viscosity on elliptic flow at back- and forward rapidities in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We explore the influence of a temperature-dependent shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $η/s$ on the azimuthal anisotropies v_2 and v_4 of hadrons at various rapidities. We find that in Au+Au collisions at full RHIC energy, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the flow anisotropies are dominated by hadronic viscosity at all rapidities, whereas in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760$ GeV, the flow coefficients are affected by the viscosity both in the plasma and hadronic phases at midrapidity, but the further away from midrapidity, the more dominant the hadronic viscosity is. We find that the centrality and rapidity dependence of the elliptic and quadrangular flows can help to distinguish different parametrizations of $(η/s)(T)$. We also find that at midrapidity the flow harmonics are almost independent of the decoupling criterion, but show some sensitivity to the criterion at back- and forward rapidities.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the rapidity dependence of elliptic and quadrangular flow can help distinguish different parametrizations of the temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, but this distinction is only possible because at RHIC energies the flow anisotropies are dominated by hadronic viscosity at all rapidities, whereas at LHC energies the flow coefficients are affected by viscosity in both the plasma and hadronic phases at midrapidity, with hadronic viscosity becoming more dominant further away from midrapidity.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional dissipative fluid-dynamical simulations using four different parametrizations of the temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, with a lattice QCD-based equation of state and an optical Glauber model for initial conditions with longitudinal scaling flow and exponential tails. They solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation-derived relaxation equations for the shear-stress tensor, and used both constant-temperature freeze-out at 100 MeV and a dynamical freeze-out criterion based on the Knudsen number to evaluate particle spectra via the Cooper-Frye formula.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density at RHIC: 17.0 GeV/fm³ for ideal fluid, 15.8 GeV/fm³ for LH-LQ and HH-LQ, 14.9 GeV/fm³ for LH-HQ and HH-HQ
- Maximum initial energy density at LHC: 57.5 GeV/fm³ for ideal fluid, 54.5 GeV/fm³ for LH-LQ and HH-LQ, 49.5 GeV/fm³ for LH-HQ and HH-HQ
- Transition temperature T_tr = 180 MeV where (eta_s/s)(T_tr) = 0.08 for all parametrizations
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem = 150 MeV used in the equation of state
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T_dec = 100 MeV chosen by comparison to pion, kaon and proton p_T spectra
- Initial time tau_0 = 1 fm/c proper time for all calculations
- Longitudinal parameters: c_eta = 4 at RHIC and c_eta = 2 at LHC, with eta_0 = 2.0 for both
- Width of rapidity distribution: sigma_eta = 1.0 at RHIC and sigma_eta = 1.8 at LHC
- Average impact parameter for 0-5% centrality: 2.24 fm at RHIC, 2.32 fm at LHC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Niemi 2011, Niemi 2012) that used boost-invariant calculations with constant eta_s/s, by relaxing boost invariance and solving the evolution equations in three dimensions to study rapidity dependence. It follows the approach of Song (2011) in tuning different parametrizations to reproduce anisotropies at one collision energy and checking whether measurements at different centralities, rapidities, and collision energies can distinguish between them. The results enable future experimental measurements of v_n at back- and forward rapidities to provide further constraints on the temperature dependence of eta_s/s, and suggest that the rapidity dependence of flow harmonics can help distinguish different parametrizations that are indistinguishable at midrapidity alone.
