# Low-m_t charged pion asymmetry enhancement from hadronization of QGP

> We show that in sudden hadronization of QGP a non-equilibrium value of the pion phase space occupancy parameter gamma_q>1 is expected in order to accommodate the entropy excess in QGP and the process of gluon fragmentation. When gamma_q is near to its maximum allowed value, pion overabundance is shown to arise at low $m_\bot$, where the charged pion asymmetry is also amplified. These effects are considered and we show that their magnitude suffices to explain pertinent experimental data obtained in 160-200AGeV Pb- and S- induced reactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a non-equilibrium pion phase-space occupancy parameter \(\gamma_q > 1\) is expected from sudden hadronization of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to accommodate entropy excess and gluon fragmentation, and that when \(\gamma_q\) is near its maximum allowed value, the resulting pion overabundance at low transverse mass \(m_\bot\) amplifies the charged pion asymmetry enough to explain experimental data from 160–200 \(A\) GeV Pb- and S-induced reactions.

## Method

The authors apply a statistical Fermi model with chemical non-equilibrium, introducing a light quark phase-space occupancy parameter \(\gamma_q\) and fugacities \(\lambda_u, \lambda_d\) to describe hadron abundances. They use Bose-Einstein distributions for pions (Eq. \ref{piBos}) and compute the charged pion asymmetry \(D_q^\pi(m_\bot) \equiv (N(\pi^-)-N(\pi^+))/(N(\pi^-)+N(\pi^+))\) as a function of \(m_\bot\) for rapidity window \(-0.5 < y < 0.5\), varying \(\gamma_q\) from 1 to 1.6 in steps of 0.1. The analysis uses chemical freeze-out parameters from fits to Pb–Pb 158 \(A\) GeV and S–Au/W/Pb 200 \(A\) GeV data (Table \ref{param}), and compares the predicted asymmetry to NA44 collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- For Pb–Pb 158 \(A\) GeV collisions, the chemical freeze-out temperature is \(T_f = 142 \pm 3\) MeV, baryochemical potential \(\mu_B = 200\)–210 MeV, and light quark phase-space occupancy \(\gamma_q = 1.6 \pm 0.5\) (Table \ref{param}).
- For S–Au/W/Pb 200 \(A\) GeV collisions, \(T_f = 144 \pm 2\) MeV and \(\gamma_q = 1.41 \pm 0.08\) (Table \ref{param}).
- The fireball volume at chemical freeze-out is estimated as \(V \simeq 2100\) fm\(^3\) for Pb–Pb, based on the Coulomb potential measure \(\lambda_s = 1.10\).
- Among the 200 negative hadrons at central rapidity \(\Delta y = 1\) in Pb–Pb, about 145 are directly produced \(\pi^-\), 20 are \(\bar p\) and \(K^-\), and about 30 \(\pi^-\) arise from resonance decays.
- The charged pion asymmetry \(D_q^\pi(m_\bot)\) for \(m_\bot - m_\pi > 0.3\) GeV is approximated by \(((\lambda_d/\lambda_u)^2 - 1)/((\lambda_d/\lambda_u)^2 + 1)\), with \(\delta\mu/\mu_q = 0.07\) for Pb–Pb (Table \ref{param}).
- The Bose singularity bound for pions is \(\gamma_\pi < \gamma_\pi^c = e^{m_\pi/T}\), and the entropy density at \(\gamma_\pi \simeq \gamma_\pi^c\) is nearly twice that at \(\gamma_\pi = 1\) (Fig. \ref{abssne}).

## Context

This work extends the sudden hadronization model previously applied to strange hadrons (Rafelski and Letessier, 1999) to the dominant pion component, showing that the same \(\gamma_q > 1\) parameter explains soft pion spectra and charged pion asymmetry measured by NA44 and WA98 collaborations. It contrasts with earlier approaches that attributed the asymmetry to Coulomb field effects (Osaka et al., 1997; Barz et al., 1998; Ayala et al., 1999) or strangeness asymmetry (Arbex et al., 1997), and with chemical equilibrium fits (Gorenstein and Miller, 1997; Tomasik, Wiedemann, and Heinz, 1999) that could not reproduce the low-\(m_\bot\) asymmetry. The findings enable a new diagnostic for QGP hadronization at RHIC, particularly for the PHOBOS experiment, by using the shape of low-\(m_\bot\) charged pion asymmetry to determine \(\gamma_q\).
