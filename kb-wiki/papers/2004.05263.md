# Chiral Effective Field Theory Calculations of Weak Transitions in Light Nuclei

> We report Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of weak transitions in $A\leq 10$ nuclei, based on the Norfolk two- and three-nucleon chiral interactions, and associated one- and two-body axial currents. We find that the contribution from two-body currents is at the $2$ - $3\%$ level, with the exception of matrix elements entering the rates of $^8$Li, $^8$B, and $^8$He beta decays. These matrix elements are suppressed in impulse approximation based on the (leading order) Gamow Teller transition operator alone; two-body currents provide a $20$ - $30\%$ correction, which is, however, insufficient to bring theory in agreement with experimental data. For the other transitions, the agreement with the data is satisfactory, and the results exhibit a negligible to mild model dependence when different combinations of Norfolk interactions are utilized to construct the nuclear wave functions. We report a complete study of two-body weak transition densities which reveals the expected universal behavior of two-body currents at short distances throughout the range of $A\,$=$\,3$ to $A\,$=$\,10$ systems considered here.

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of weak transitions in nuclei with A ≤ 10, using chiral interactions and consistent axial currents, show that two-body currents contribute at the 2–3% level for most transitions, but provide a 20–30% correction for the suppressed matrix elements in ⁸Li, ⁸B, and ⁸He beta decays; however, even this large correction is insufficient to bring theory into agreement with experimental data for those specific A=8 systems.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of Gamow-Teller matrix elements for beta-decay and electron-capture transitions in A=3–10 nuclei. The nuclear wave functions were constructed using the Norfolk two- and three-nucleon chiral interactions (NV2+3), and the axial currents included one- and two-body operators derived consistently within the same chiral effective field theory framework up to tree-level N3LO, with the two-body currents comprising N2LO relativistic corrections, N2LO Δ-excitation terms, N3LO one-pion-exchange, and N3LO contact terms.

## Key numbers

- Two-body currents contribute at the 2–3% level for most transitions, except for A=8 systems where they provide a 20–30% correction
- For ⁸Li(2⁺;1) → ⁸Be(2⁺;0), the total Gamow-Teller reduced matrix element ranges from 0.123 to 0.188 across different NV2+3 models, compared to experimental values of 0.284 (Ref. 1986WA01) and 0.190 (Ref. 1989BA31)
- For ⁸B(2⁺;1) → ⁸Be(2⁺;0), the total RME ranges from 0.122 to 0.188, compared to the experimental value of 0.269(20)
- For ⁸He(0⁺;2) → ⁸Li(1⁺;1), the total RME ranges from 0.391 to 0.510, compared to the experimental value of 0.512(6)
- For ⁶He(0⁺;1) → ⁶Li(1⁺;0), the total RME ranges from 2.197 to 2.306 across models, compared to the experimental value of 2.1609(40)
- For ¹⁰C(0⁺;1) → ¹⁰B(1⁺;0), the total RME ranges from 1.978 to 2.159, compared to the experimental value of 1.8331(34)
- The N3LO contact term (N3LO-CT) contribution varies from −0.001 to −0.212 depending on the transition and the NV2+3 model

## Context

This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo studies by Pastore (2017) that used the Argonne-v18 and Illinois-7 interactions, where two-body currents contributed only ≈3% and agreement with data was attributed to correlated wave functions rather than two-body effects. The present study addresses a discrepancy with no-core shell-model calculations by Gysbers (2019) that found opposite signs for two-body current corrections, by using fully consistent chiral interactions and currents (the Norfolk NV2+3 models) rather than hybrid approaches. The results enable improved theoretical predictions for beta-decay rates relevant to searches for physics beyond the Standard Model at facilities such as FRIB, and provide input for calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements.
