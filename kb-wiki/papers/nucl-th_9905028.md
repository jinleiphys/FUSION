# Resonance Transport and Kinetic Entropy

> Within the real-time formulation of nonequilibrium field theory, generalized transport equations are derived avoiding the standard quasiparticle approximation. They permit to include unstable particles into the transport scheme. In order to achieve a self-consistent, conserving and thermodynamically consistent description, we generalize the Baym's $Φ$-functional method to genuine nonequilibrium processes. The developed transport description naturally includes all those quantum features already inherent in the corresponding equilibrium limit. Memory effects appearing in collision term diagrams of higher order are discussed. The variational properties of $Φ$-functional permit to derive a generalized expression for the non-equilibrium kinetic entropy flow, which includes fluctuations and mass width effects. In special cases an $H$-theorem is demonstrated implying that the entropy can only increase with time. Memory effects in the kinetic terms provide corrections to the kinetic entropy flow that in equilibrium limit recover the famous bosonic type $T^3 \ln T$ correction to the specific heat of Fermi liquids like Helium-3.

## Key claim

The authors derive generalized transport equations for nonequilibrium quantum field theory that avoid the standard quasiparticle approximation, thereby allowing the consistent inclusion of unstable particles with finite lifetime (mass width). The formalism is claimed to be self-consistent, conserving, and thermodynamically consistent, but this holds only when the generalized Baym's Phi-functional method is extended to genuine nonequilibrium processes and when the resulting equations are truncated at a given order of the loop expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the real-time (Schwinger-Keldysh) contour formulation of nonequilibrium field theory and generalize Baym's Phi-functional method to the nonequilibrium domain. They derive transport equations for the full (dressed) propagators without imposing the quasiparticle approximation, which allows the spectral function to have a finite width. The collision terms are obtained from the functional derivative of the Phi-functional with respect to the propagators, and the kinetic entropy flow is derived from the variational properties of the same functional, including contributions from fluctuations and mass width effects.

## Key numbers

* The formalism recovers the famous bosonic-type T^3 ln T correction to the specific heat of Fermi liquids like Helium-3 in the equilibrium limit, arising from memory effects in the kinetic terms.
* An H-theorem is demonstrated for special cases, implying that the entropy can only increase with time; no specific numerical bound on the entropy production rate is given.
* No other explicit numerical results (cross sections, decay widths, or transport coefficients) are reported in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium Phi-functional method of Baym (1962) and the nonequilibrium transport theory of Kadanoff and Baym (1962) to systems where particles have finite lifetime and cannot be treated as stable quasiparticles. It generalizes earlier work by Danielewicz (1984) and Botermans and Malfliet (1990) on quantum transport with off-shell effects. The formalism enables first-principles calculations of transport coefficients and entropy production in dense nuclear matter and heavy-ion collisions, where resonance lifetimes are comparable to collision times. Open questions include the practical truncation of the Phi-functional at higher loop orders and the numerical implementation of the memory integrals in the collision terms.
