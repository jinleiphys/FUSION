# Roberge-Weiss phase transitions and extended Z3 symmetry

> Using the Polyakov extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with imaginary chemical potential, the relation between the Roberge-Weiss (RW) phase transition and the extended Z3 symmetry is studied. At low temperature, there is approximate continuous symmetry under the phase transformation of the Polyakov loop with the shift of the imaginary chemical potential. Due to this continuous symmetry, the Polyakov loop can oscillate smoothly as the imaginary chemical potential increases. At high temperature, this continuous symmetry is broken to an exact discrete symmetry, the extended Z3 symmetry, and the Polyakov loop can not oscillate smoothly. This symmetry breaking of the continuous symmetry causes a discontinuity of the Polyakov loop. That is the RW phase transition.

## Key claim

The Roberge-Weiss (RW) phase transition arises from the breaking of an approximate continuous symmetry (under simultaneous phase rotation of the Polyakov loop and shift of imaginary chemical potential) to an exact discrete extended Z₃ symmetry at high temperature; the discontinuity of the Polyakov loop is caused by this symmetry breaking, not by the Z₃ symmetry alone.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Polyakov extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean-field approximation with an imaginary chemical potential μ = iθ/β. They analyze the thermodynamic potential Ω(θ) as a function of the phase θ and the Polyakov loop Φ, examining its invariance under continuous phase transformations and under the extended Z₃ transformation. By comparing the low-temperature and high-temperature regimes, they show how the approximate continuous symmetry is broken to the discrete extended Z₃ symmetry through the factor e^(±3iθ) in the potential.

## Key numbers

- The RW periodicity is Z(θ + 2πk/N) = Z(θ) for any integer k, with N = 3 for SU(3) gauge theory.
- The RW phase transition occurs at θ = (2k+1)π/3 in the high-temperature phase, where the Polyakov loop becomes discontinuous.
- The thermodynamic potential Ω contains terms with factor e^(±βμ_B) = e^(±3iθ), which break the continuous symmetry at high temperature.

## Context

This work extends the original finding of Roberge and Weiss (1986) that the partition function of SU(N) gauge theory with imaginary chemical potential has periodicity 2π/N and exhibits a phase transition at high temperature. The authors use the PNJL model (see references in Kouno et al. 2008) to provide a symmetry-based explanation for the RW transition, connecting it to the extended Z₃ symmetry. This enables a deeper understanding of the relation between confinement properties and the RW transition, and provides a framework for studying QCD phase structure at imaginary chemical potential, which is relevant for lattice QCD simulations.
