# Macroscopic manifestations of rotating triaxial superfluid nuclei

> Recently, Allmond and Wood [Phys. Lett. B 767, 226 (2017)] were able to extract the three moments of inertia $I_k$ of a dozen of superfluid triaxial nuclei from experimental data. The observed dependence of the $I_k$ on the deformation parameters is rather smooth. Here we show that these moments of inertia can be surprisingly well explained by a semiclassical cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculation in which the velocity field is a simple superposition of rigid and irrotational flows.

## Key claim

The moments of inertia of a dozen triaxial superfluid nuclei, extracted experimentally by Allmond and Wood (2017), can be surprisingly well reproduced by a semiclassical cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation where the velocity field is a simple superposition of rigid and irrotational flows, despite the absence of shell effects in the semiclassical approach.

## Method

The authors applied a semiclassical transport model originally developed for rotating superfluid atomic clouds (Farine et al., 2000; Urban et al., 2003), itself based on Migdal's (1959) statistical description of rotating superfluid nuclei, to triaxially deformed nuclei. They used experimental deformation parameters β and γ from Allmond and Wood (2017) and pairing gaps from either HFB calculations with the D1S Gogny force or from the 5-point formula applied to experimental nuclear masses (Wang et al., 2017). The moments of inertia were computed using analytic formulas that interpolate between rigid and irrotational flow depending on the pairing gap.

## Key numbers

- For the 12 triaxial nuclei studied, the theoretical moments of inertia lie on a smooth curve between the rigid-body and irrotational limits when plotted against β²sin²(γ−2πk/3), which is proportional to the irrotational moment of inertia.
- For ¹⁵⁰Nd, the calculation gives a ratio of moments of inertia about the x and z axes of I₁/I₃ ≃ 17, close to the experimental ratio of I₁/I₃ ≃ 14.
- The rigid-body moment of inertia is approximated as I_{k,rigid} = B_rigid (1 − √(5/4π) β cos(γ − 2πk/3)) with B_rigid = 0.0138 A^(5/3) ℏ² MeV⁻¹.
- The irrotational moment of inertia to leading order in β is I_{k,irrot} = (15/4π) B_rigid β² sin²(γ − 2πk/3).
- Pairing gaps from the 5-point formula tend to be smaller than those from HFB with the D1S Gogny force; for example, for ¹¹⁰Ru, Δ_n^(D1S) = 0.82 MeV and Δ_p^(D1S) = 1.10 MeV versus Δ_n^(5) = 1.20 MeV and Δ_p^(5) = 1.40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical transport approach of Farine et al. (2000) and Urban et al. (2003), originally developed for rotating superfluid atomic Fermi gases, to triaxially deformed superfluid nuclei. It directly addresses the experimental data of Allmond and Wood (2017), who extracted the three moments of inertia for a dozen triaxial nuclei. The good agreement between the semiclassical calculation and experiment, despite the absence of shell effects, suggests that triaxial superfluid nuclei behave macroscopically like rotating liquid drops, with the moments of inertia lying halfway between rigid rotation and irrotational flow.
