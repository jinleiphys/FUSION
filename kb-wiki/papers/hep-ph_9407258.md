# The Spin Structure of the Proton in a Non-relativistic Quark Model

> The spin structure of the proton is investigated in the framework of an extended quark potential model which in addition to the conventional $3q$--structure also takes into account $(3q)(q\bar q)$--admixtures in the proton wave function. For reasons of parity such admixtures contain an odd orbital angular momentum thus allowing the proton spin to be shared among quark spins and orbital angular momenta. We show that only certain admixtures are suited for a significant reduction of the quark spin content of the proton as suggested by the EMC--result. Within a Hamiltonian model quark spin contributions to the proton spin down to $0.5$ can be reproduced easily.

## Key claim

The paper shows that within a non-relativistic quark model extended to include (3q)(q\(\bar q\)) admixtures, the quark spin contribution to the proton spin can be reduced to about 0.5, but a reduction to values compatible with zero (as suggested by the EMC result) cannot be achieved with a reasonable parameter set that also reproduces other nucleon properties.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic quark potential model with one-gluon exchange potentials, including annihilation and pair creation/destruction terms, and solved the Schrodinger equation using the Refined Resonating Group Method. They constructed a proton wave function as a superposition of a conventional 3-quark component and 66 different (3q)(q\(\bar q\)) baryon-meson channels, with the expansion coefficients determined by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian rather than treated as free parameters. The spin properties of individual channels were analyzed analytically, and then numerical calculations were performed to find the resulting quark spin content for various parameter sets.

## Key numbers

* Quark spin contribution to the proton spin \(\Sigma(\Psi_p^{\text{tot}}) \approx 0.80\) was obtained with the starting parameter set (\(m_u=m_d=330\) MeV, \(\alpha_s=1.7\), \(a=-19\) MeV/fm\(^2\)), corresponding to a 3q probability amplitude \(\nu_0^2 \approx 0.85\).
* A quark spin contribution \(\Sigma(\Psi_p^{\text{tot}}) \approx 0.5\) was achieved by fixing parameters at \(m_q=250\) MeV, \(\alpha_s=4.0\), and \(a=-170\) MeV/fm\(^2\).
* The calculated \(\beta\)-decay constant \((g_A/g_V)_{\text{np}} = 1.23\) was obtained with this parameter set, in agreement with experiment.
* The analysis showed that to achieve \(\Sigma(\Psi_p^{\text{tot}}) \approx 0\), the squared amplitude of the 3q structure must satisfy \(\nu_0^2 \leq 0.3\); for \(\Sigma(\Psi_p^{\text{tot}}) \approx 0.5\), \(\nu_0^2\) must be less than \(\approx 0.7\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier approach of Jaffe and Lipkin (1991), who used a simpler model with only two 5q components and free fit parameters to reproduce a near-zero quark spin content. The present paper shows that in a consistent Hamiltonian model with dynamical determination of the coefficients, the Jaffe-Lipkin result cannot be reproduced, and the lower bound for the quark spin contribution is about 0.5. This demonstrates the limitations of non-relativistic quark models in explaining the EMC spin crisis and suggests that further reduction would require unacceptable modifications to the model parameters.
