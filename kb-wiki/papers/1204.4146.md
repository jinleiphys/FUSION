# Restoration of Rotational Symmetry in the Continuum Limit of Lattice Field Theories

> We explore how rotational invariance is systematically recovered from calculations on hyper-cubic lattices through the use of smeared lattice operators that smoothly evolve into continuum operators with definite angular momentum as the lattice-spacing is reduced. Perturbative calculations of the angular momentum violation associated with such operators at tree-level and at one-loop are presented in phi^4 theory and QCD. Contributions from these operators that violate rotational invariance occur at tree-level, with coefficients that are suppressed by O(a^2) in the continuum limit. Quantum loops do not modify this behavior in phi^4, nor in QCD if the gauge-fields are smeared over a comparable spatial region. Consequently, the use of this type of operator should, in principle, allow for Lattice QCD calculations of the higher moments of the hadron structure functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates analytically that smeared lattice operators, constructed to have definite angular momentum in the continuum limit, exhibit violations of rotational symmetry that are suppressed by O(a²) at tree level, and that quantum loops in λφ⁴ theory and in QCD (provided gauge fields are also smeared) do not alter this scaling, contrary to the naive expectation that power-divergent mixing with lower-dimensional operators would spoil the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors construct a bilinear lattice operator smeared over a spherical region of physical size 1/Λ = Na, with spherical harmonic weighting to project onto definite angular momentum (L,M). They perform a multipole expansion of this operator in terms of derivative operators, then use Poisson resummation to analytically extract the scaling of coefficients that violate rotational symmetry or mix with different angular momenta as N → ∞ (a → 0 at fixed Λ). One-loop corrections are computed in λφ⁴ theory and in QCD to determine whether quantum fluctuations modify the tree-level O(a²) suppression of rotational symmetry violations.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level violations of rotational symmetry are suppressed by O(a²) = O(1/N²) as a → 0, demonstrated analytically for general L, M via the scaling I ∼ 1/N² in Eq. (12)
- The leading coefficient for the L=3 operator in the continuum limit is C_{30;30}^{(d)} = (15/4)√(7/π) (d²-1)/(d+4)! for d=3,5,... (Eq. 6)
- One-loop corrections in λφ⁴ produce NLO contributions scaling as 1/N² (Eq. 19), confirming quantum corrections do not alter the tree-level O(a²) suppression
- In QCD, the leading rotational invariance violating contributions from one-loop renormalization are suppressed by O(α_s a²), where α_s = g_s²/(4π)

## Context

This work provides an analytic, perturbative foundation for the numerical observations of Dudek et al. (2008-2010) and Meyer & Teper (2003-2004) that smeared lattice operators can effectively project onto definite angular momentum states. It extends the ideas of Dawson et al. (1998) and Detmold & Lin (2006) for suppressing power-divergent operator mixing through UV filtering. The results enable systematic Lattice QCD calculations of higher moments of hadron structure functions and higher-spin states, where the reduced symmetry of the cubic lattice would otherwise cause intractable mixing with lower-dimensional operators.
