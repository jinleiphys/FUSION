# Shallow and diffuse spin-orbit potential for proton elastic scattering from neutron-rich helium isotopes at 71 MeV/nucleon

> Vector analyzing powers for proton elastic scattering from 8He at 71 MeV/nucleon have been measured using a solid polarized proton target operated in a low magnetic field of 0.1 T. The spin-orbit potential obtained from a phenomenological optical model analysis is found to be significantly shallower and more diffuse than the global systematics of stable nuclei, which is an indication that the spin-orbit potential is modified for scattering involving neutron-rich nuclei. A close similarity between the matter radius and the root-mean-square radius of the spin-orbit potential is also identified.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit potential for proton elastic scattering from neutron-rich helium isotopes (⁶He and ⁸He) at 71 MeV/nucleon is significantly shallower and more diffuse than the global systematics established for stable nuclei, but this conclusion is based on a phenomenological optical model analysis with limited statistics and without including an imaginary spin-orbit term.

## Method

Vector analyzing powers for p–⁸He elastic scattering at 71 MeV/nucleon were measured using a solid polarized proton target operated at a low magnetic field of 0.1 T (91 mT) at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory. The data were analyzed with a phenomenological optical model using a Woods-Saxon form factor with a Thomas-type spin-orbit term, fitting both differential cross sections and analyzing powers with the ECIS79 code. The initial parameters were taken from a set for p–⁶Li elastic scattering at 72 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Average target polarization was 11.3 ± 2.6%
- ⁸He beam energy was 71.0 ± 1.4 MeV/nucleon at the center of the secondary target
- Beam intensity was 1.5 × 10⁵ pps with 77% purity
- Reduced chi-square values for p–⁸He: χ²_σ/ν_σ = 1.91 and χ²_Ay/ν_Ay = 0.37
- Spin-orbit potential depth V_s for ⁸He (Set-A): 3.68 MeV, with radius r_0s = 1.11 fm and diffuseness a_s = 0.91 fm
- Spin-orbit potential depth V_s for ⁶He: 2.02 MeV, with radius r_0s = 1.29 fm and diffuseness a_s = 0.76 fm
- RMS radius of spin-orbit potential ⟨r_ls²⟩¹/² for ⁸He: 3.58⁺⁰·²⁵₋₀·₂₀ fm; for ⁶He: 3.33⁺⁰·²³₋₀·₂₆ fm
- Peak amplitude V_s/2a_s for ⁸He: 2.03⁺⁰·⁵⁸₋₀·⁵⁴ MeV fm; for ⁶He: 1.32⁺⁰·²⁵₋₀·²¹ MeV fm (compared to stable nuclei range 3.5–5.5 MeV fm)

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of vector analyzing powers for p–⁶He elastic scattering at 71 MeV/nucleon (Uesaka 2010, Sakaguchi 2011) using the same solid polarized proton target technique. The results contradict the mass-number independence of spin-orbit potential shape and magnitude established for stable nuclei by global systematics such as Koning and Delaroche (2003) and Varner et al. (1991), and instead support theoretical predictions of modified spin-orbit coupling in neutron-rich nuclei (Dobaczewski 1994, Otsuka 2006). The close similarity between matter radius and spin-orbit potential RMS radius enables future studies of how nuclear surface structure affects the spin-orbit interaction in exotic nuclei.
