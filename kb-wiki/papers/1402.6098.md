# Shape evolution and shape coexistence in Pt isotopes: comparing interacting boson model configuration mixing and Gogny mean-field energy surfaces

> The evolution of the total energy surface and the nuclear shape in the isotopic chain $^{172-194}$Pt are studied in the framework of the interacting boson model, including configuration mixing. The results are compared with a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation using the Gogny-D1S interaction and a good agreement between both approaches shows up. The evolution of the deformation parameters points towards the presence of two different coexisting configurations in the region 176 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 186.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) and self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1S interaction produce qualitatively similar energy surfaces for the Pt isotopic chain, but with significant quantitative differences in energy scale and the precise neutron number at which deformation sets in. The abstract's claim of "good agreement" glosses over the fact that the spherical barrier height differs by a factor of about 3 between the two approaches (approximately 1.8 MeV in IBM-CM versus up to 6 MeV in HFB), and that the onset of deformation occurs at different isotopes (176Pt for IBM-CM versus 174Pt for HFB).

## Method

The authors computed total energy surfaces for the even-even Pt isotopes 172-194 using two independent approaches. First, they used the IBM-CM with parameters previously fitted to experimental excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities (Garcia-Ramos 2009, 2011), employing the intrinsic state formalism with a 2x2 matrix coherent-state method to mix regular [N] and intruder [N+2] boson configurations. Second, they performed constrained HFB calculations with the Gogny-D1S interaction expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis of 13 major shells, computing energy surfaces as functions of the axial quadrupole deformation parameter beta and the triaxiality parameter gamma. The IBM-CM beta values were mapped to the geometric beta using a linear transformation with fitted parameters delta=1.37 and xi=0.07.

## Key numbers

- The spherical barrier height in IBM-CM reaches a maximum of approximately 1.8 MeV, while in HFB it reaches up to 6 MeV (for 182Pt)
- The equilibrium deformation beta for the most deformed isotopes (178-186Pt) is approximately 0.3 in both approaches
- For the heaviest isotopes, beta decreases from approximately 0.2 in 188Pt to approximately 0.13 in 194Pt in both approaches
- The spherical barrier in IBM-CM remains approximately 1 MeV for 188-192Pt but reduces to 0.3 MeV in 194Pt
- The HFB spherical barrier height increases from 3.5 MeV in 174Pt to more than 6 MeV in 182Pt
- The onset of deformation occurs at 176Pt in IBM-CM and at 174Pt in HFB
- The intruder configuration becomes the ground state between 176-178Pt and 186-188Pt, delimiting the region where the intruder configuration is lowest in energy

## Context

This work extends previous IBM-CM studies of Pt isotopes by Garcia-Ramos (2009, 2011) that focused on spectroscopic properties, now comparing the derived energy surfaces with mean-field calculations. It contrasts with the approach of Nomura (2011a, 2012, 2013), who mapped HFB energy surfaces onto IBM Hamiltonians, whereas the present work starts from a Hamiltonian fitted to experimental data. The results are compared with earlier phenomenological studies using deformed Woods-Saxon potentials by Bengtsson (1987) and with the IBM-CM energy surface analysis of Morales (2008) for 182-204Pt using parameters from Harder (1997). The paper enables future investigations into the origin of the energy scale discrepancy between phenomenological and microscopic approaches, and suggests that the IBM-CM description of shape coexistence in Pt is complementary to the mean-field picture where a single symmetry-broken configuration already captures the deformation trend that IBM-CM attributes to intruder configurations.
