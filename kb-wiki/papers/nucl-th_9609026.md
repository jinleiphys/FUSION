# Non-nucleonic Effects Studied by Nuclear Moments

> Measured magnetic moments of single-particle states in the Pb region differ significantly from their Schmidt values. We discuss the reasons for this in terms of meson-exchange currents, isobar currents, core polarisation and other effects.

## Key claim

Measured magnetic moments of single-particle states in the Pb region differ significantly from their Schmidt values, and these deviations can be explained by a combination of meson-exchange currents, isobar currents, core polarisation, and other effects; however, the agreement between theory and experiment is within 0.15 nuclear magnetons for all cases except the neutron i13/2 state in 207Pb, where the cancellation between core polarisation and meson-exchange currents appears too severe.

## Method

The paper constructs two-body meson-exchange current (MEC) operators using an extended S-matrix formalism with a phenomenological chiral Lagrangian for isovector mesons (pi, rho, A1) and standard isoscalar meson Lagrangians for sigma and omega mesons, including "external" and "vertex" corrections. Core polarisation corrections are computed in the random phase approximation (RPA) using a one-boson-exchange potential, and additional corrections from isobar currents, coupling to core-vibrational states, relativistic corrections, and higher-order processes (second-order core polarisation and "crossing terms") are evaluated. All corrections are expressed as an equivalent effective one-body operator with modified orbital (gL), spin (gS), and tensor (gP) factors, and matrix elements are computed for closed-shell-plus-one or minus-one configurations around 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- For a proton 0h9/2 state in 209Bi, the total MEC correction to the magnetic moment is Delta mu = 0.72 mu_N, with contributions from pion-pair (1.16 mu_N) and pion-current (-0.94 mu_N) showing significant cancellation
- For a neutron 0i13/2 state in 209Pb, the total MEC correction is Delta mu = -0.59 mu_N
- The empirical best-fit values from systematic analysis of Pb-region data are: delta gL = 0.15 +/- 0.02 for protons and delta gL = -0.03 +/- 0.02 for neutrons
- The computed delta gL values are 0.106 for protons and -0.064 for neutrons
- Core polarisation (RPA) reduces the spin gS factor by about 25% compared to the free-nucleon value, with delta gS = -1.167 for the proton 0h9/2 state and delta gS = 1.034 for the neutron 0i13/2 state
- The total spin quenching is delta gS/gS = -40% for protons and -42% for neutrons, consistent with the empirical relation gS_eff = 0.6 gS
- The summed correction for the proton h9/2 state in 209Bi is Delta mu = 1.36 mu_N, compared to the experimental value of 1.49(0) mu_N
- For the neutron i13/2 state in 207Pb, the summed correction is Delta mu = 0.44 mu_N, compared to the experimental value of 0.90(3) mu_N
- For the pi g7/2 state at 132Sn, the summed correction is Delta mu = 1.21 mu_N, compared to the experimental value of 1.28(1) mu_N

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of meson-exchange currents by Miyazawa (1951), who first estimated delta gL approximately 0.1 from pion-exchange currents, and the systematic experimental analysis by Yamazaki (1979) of magnetic moment data in the Pb region. The calculations are compared with the results of Arima, Shimizu, Bentz and Hyuga (1987) from core-polarisation and meson-exchange current processes, and the paper enables improved predictions for magnetic moments and M1 transitions in heavy nuclei, including the newly measured 133Sb at the 132Sn doubly-closed shell core.
