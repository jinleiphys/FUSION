# Nuclear matrix elements for Majoron emitting double-$β$ decay

> A complete calculation of the nuclear matrix elements (NME) for Majoron emitting neutrinoless double beta decay within the framework of IBM-2 for spectral indices $n=1,3,7$ is presented. By combining the results of this calculation with previously calculated phase space factors (PSF) we give predictions for expected half-lives. By comparing with experimental limits on the half-lives we set limits on the coupling constants $\left\langle g_{ee}^{M}\right\rangle $ of all proposed Majoron-emitting models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete calculation of nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for Majoron-emitting neutrinoless double-beta decay within the IBM-2 framework for spectral indices n=1, 3, and 7, but the authors note that the matrix element M3 depends strongly on the closure energy and carries an estimated total error of 30%, while M1 and M2 have estimated errors of 16% and 20% respectively.

## Method

The authors computed NMEs for 18 candidate isotopes using the microscopic interacting boson model IBM-2 with isospin restoration, converting the formalism of Hirsch et al. to their notation and adding higher-order tensor terms not included in the original derivation. They constructed neutrino potentials for three classes of Majoron models (M1, M2, M3) using closure energies of 1.12A^(1/2) MeV, form factors with mV=0.84 GeV and mA=1.09 GeV, and short-range correlations as in their previous IBM-2 calculations. The resulting NMEs were combined with previously calculated phase space factors to derive half-life predictions and limits on the effective Majoron-neutrino coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge, the NME M1 = 6.642, M2 = 0.381, and M3 = 2.556 x 10^(-3) (using quenched gA = 1.0)
- For 136Xe, the NME M1 = 3.603, M2 = 0.160, and M3 = 1.112 x 10^(-3)
- The most stringent limit on the coupling constant for n=1 models comes from KamLAND-Zen on 136Xe: |<g_chi_ee^M>| < 8.5 x 10^(-6) (for gA=1)
- The error estimate for M1 is 16% for all nuclei, for M2 is 20%, and for M3 is 30%
- The error in using old phase space factor calculations versus new ones is 6% in 76Ge and 28% in 136Xe

## Context

This work extends the earlier NME calculations of Hirsch et al. by adding tensor matrix elements and using the IBM-2 framework with improved phase space factors from Kotila and Iachello (2012) that include exact Dirac wave functions, nuclear finite size, and electron screening. The results enable experimental collaborations (GERDA, NEMO3, CUORE, EXO, KamLAND-Zen, CUPID-0, CUPID-Mo) to extract limits on Majoron-neutrino coupling constants from their half-life measurements, with the most stringent limits coming from KamLAND-Zen and EXO for n=1 spectral index models.
