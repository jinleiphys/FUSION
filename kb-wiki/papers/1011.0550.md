# The triple alpha reaction rate and the 2$^+$ resonances in $^{12}$C

> The triple alpha rate is obtained from the three-body bound and continuum states computed in a large box. The results from this genuine full three-body calculation are compared with standard reference rates obtained by two sequential two-body processes. The fairly good agreement relies on two different assumptions about the lowest $2^+$ resonance energy. With the same $2^+$ energy the rates from the full three-body calculation are smaller than those of the standard reference. We discuss the rate dependence on the experimentally unknown $2^+$ energy. Substantial deviations from previous results appear for temperatures above 3 GK.

# The triple alpha reaction rate and the 2⁺ resonances in ¹²C

## Key claim

The full three-body calculation of the triple alpha reaction rate gives smaller rates than the standard sequential (NACRE) approximation when both calculations use the same energy for the lowest 2⁺₂ resonance in ¹²C; the fairly good agreement between the two methods shown in the paper relies on using different 2⁺₂ resonance energies (1.38 MeV in the three-body calculation versus 1.75 MeV in the sequential calculation).

## Method

The authors compute the triple alpha reaction rate using a full three-body hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method with box boundary conditions, where both the bound final state (¹²C) and the continuum three-alpha initial states are computed from the same α-α interaction. They compare this to the standard sequential approach of Angulo (1999) (NACRE), where the reaction proceeds via two sequential two-body captures: first two α-particles form ⁸Be, then ⁸Be captures a third α to populate a ¹²C resonance that decays by photon emission. The strength functions dℬ/dE for the E2 transitions are extracted from both methods and compared.

## Key numbers

- The three-body calculation gives a 2⁺₂ resonance in ¹²C at 1.38 MeV above the three-body threshold with a width of 0.13 MeV; the sequential NACRE calculation uses a 2⁺₂ resonance at 1.75 MeV with a width of 0.56 MeV.
- The ⁸Be 0⁺ ground state resonance energy is 0.09189 MeV above threshold with a width of 6.8 ± 1.7 × 10⁻⁶ MeV.
- The Hoyle state (0⁺₂) in ¹²C is at 0.2877 MeV above the three-body threshold with an α-width of 8.3 × 10⁻⁶ MeV and a γ-width of 3.7 × 10⁻⁹ MeV.
- For temperatures below 3 GK, the Hoyle state enhances the reaction rate by about two orders of magnitude.
- For temperatures above 3 GK, substantial deviations from previous results appear; at T = 9 GK the sequential calculation gives an integrand about 25% larger than the three-body calculation.
- If the lowest 2⁺₂ resonance is at about 4 MeV (the experimentally established one), the three-body calculation gives a rate up to about one order of magnitude above the sequential result at high temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the full three-body method of de Diego (2010) to compute the triple alpha reaction rate and compares it with the standard NACRE reference rates of Angulo (1999), which assume a sequential two-step capture process. The paper addresses the experimentally uncertain energy of the lowest 2⁺₂ resonance in ¹²C, which has been predicted at 1.38 MeV by Alvarez-Rodriguez (2007) using three-body S-matrix poles, at 1.75 MeV by Angulo (1999) and Descouvemont (1987), and at about 2.3 MeV by Freer (2009) experimentally, while Fynbo (2005) found no evidence for such a resonance. The results enable more reliable triple alpha reaction rates for astrophysical modeling at temperatures above 3 GK, particularly for type-II supernova hot bubble environments, and show that non-resonant continuum 2⁺ states (neglected in sequential calculations) are important for accurate rates.
