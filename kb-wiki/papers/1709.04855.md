# Thermal states of neutron stars with a consistent model of interior

> We model the thermal states of both isolated neutron stars and accreting neutron stars in X-ray transients in quiescence and confront them with observations. We use an equation of state and superfluid baryon gaps, which are consistently calculated. We conclude that the direct Urca process is required to be consistent with low-luminous accreting neutron stars. In addition, proton superfluidity and sufficiently weak neutron superfluidity are necessary to explain the cooling of middle-aged neutron stars and to obtain a realistic distribution of neutron star masses.

## Key claim
The authors conclude that the direct Urca process is required to match observations of low-luminous accreting neutron stars, and that proton superfluidity combined with sufficiently weak neutron superfluidity is necessary to explain the cooling of middle-aged isolated neutron stars and to obtain a realistic neutron star mass distribution. This conclusion is conditional on using an equation of state and superfluid gaps that are consistently calculated from the same nuclear interaction model (Argonne V18 plus Urbana UIX three-body force within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation).

## Method
The authors computed the equation of state of nuclear matter using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with the Argonne V18 two-body potential and a phenomenological Urbana-type three-nucleon force, obtaining a unified equation of state for the outer crust, inner crust, and core. They solved the gap equation for neutron and proton pairing in the 1S0 and 3P2 channels using the same nuclear interaction and medium effects, then used the one-dimensional code NSCool to compute cooling curves for isolated neutron stars and heating curves for accreting neutron stars in X-ray transients in quiescence, varying global scaling factors for the proton 1S0 and neutron 3P2 gaps.

## Key numbers
- Direct Urca process onset at baryon density \(n_B = 0.44\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (proton fraction \(x_p = 0.136\)), corresponding to a neutron star mass of \(1.10\ M_\odot\).
- Maximum neutron star mass for the BHF equation of state: \(M_\text{max} = 2.01\ M_\odot\).
- Total deep crustal heating per accreted nucleon: \(Q_\text{DCH} = 1.9\ \text{MeV}\).
- Neutron drip density in the accreted crust model: \(6 \times 10^{11}\ \text{g cm}^{-3}\).
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 32 MeV; slope of symmetry energy: 53 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the cooling study of middle-aged isolated neutron stars initiated in Taranto (2016) to the case of accreting neutron stars in X-ray transients in quiescence, using the same consistent microscopic model. It contrasts with studies employing smaller nuclear symmetry energies and later direct Urca onset (e.g., Gusakov 2005, Page 2011, Yakovlev 2011, Blaschke 2012), and enables testing of microscopic calculations starting from nuclear forces against combined observations of isolated and accreting neutron stars. The results allow selection of an optimal superfluid model consistent with all available observational data.
