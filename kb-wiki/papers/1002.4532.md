# Probing nucleon-nucleon interactions in breakup of a one-neutron halo $^{11}$Be on a proton target

> A comparison between full few-body Faddeev/Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (Faddeev/AGS) and continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations is made for the resonant and nonresonant breakup of $^{11}$Be on proton target at 63.7 MeV/u incident energy. A simplified two-body model is used for $^{11}$Be which involves an inert $^{10}$Be(0$^+$) core and a valence neutron. The sensitivity of the calculated observables to the nucleon-nucleon potential dynamical input is analysed. We show that with the present NN and N-core dynamics the results remain a puzzle for the few-body problem of scattering from light exotic halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that breakup observables for \(^{11}\)Be on a proton target are very sensitive to the underlying nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, particularly the P-waves, but even with realistic NN potentials the calculated angular distributions still underestimate the experimental data by about 40%, leaving the reaction puzzle unresolved.

## Method

Full few-body Faddeev/Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (Faddeev/AGS) and continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations were performed for the resonant and nonresonant breakup of \(^{11}\)Be on a proton target at 63.7 MeV/u incident energy, using a simplified two-body model of \(^{11}\)Be as an inert \(^{10}\)Be(0\(^+\)) core plus a valence neutron. The sensitivity of the calculated observables to the NN potential was analyzed by comparing results using realistic CD Bonn and AV18 potentials against those using a simple \(L\)-independent Gaussian potential (G\(_{TE}\)) and modified Gaussian potentials with adjusted partial waves.

## Key numbers

* Breakup angular distribution at 63.7 MeV/u incident energy integrated over relative core-neutron energy \(E_{\rm rel} = 0-2.5\) MeV around the resonance \(E_{\rm r} = 1.275\) MeV: Faddeev/AGS with CD Bonn NN potential underestimates the data at \(\theta_{\rm c.m.} = 20\) degrees by about 40%.
* Gaussian \(L\)-independent potential G\(_{TE}\) parameters: depth \(v_0 = 72.15\) MeV and range \(r_0 = 1.484\) fm, adjusted to fit the deuteron binding energy and low-energy \(^3S_1\) proton-neutron phase-shifts.
* Faddeev/AGS calculations included \(n\)-\(p\) partial waves with relative orbital angular momentum \(L \leq 6\), \(p\)-\(^{10}\)Be with \(L \leq 21\), and \(n\)-\(^{10}\)Be with \(L \leq 6\); three-body total angular momentum up to 40.
* CDCC calculations discretized the n-\(^{10}\)Be continuum for partial waves \(L \leq 4\) up to a maximum excitation energy of 40 MeV, with total angular momentum up to 60 and matching distance of 60 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous benchmark calculations (benchmark1) comparing Faddeev/AGS and CDCC frameworks for \(p+^{11}\)Be scattering, and addresses discrepancies found between earlier Faddeev/AGS results (Cravo 2009) and CDCC calculations (Shivastava 2004, Summers). The study shows that the two formalisms agree fairly well when using the same dynamical inputs for the selected energy regions, better than in the earlier benchmark, but that realistic NN potentials are essential for reliable predictions. The results enable future work to understand why the calculated breakup angular distributions still underestimate experimental data, and motivate extensions of CDCC codes to incorporate realistic NN potentials for nucleon-nucleus scattering.
