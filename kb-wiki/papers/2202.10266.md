# Spectroscopic properties of 4He within a multiphonon approach

> Bulk and spectroscopic properties of 4He are studied within an equation of motion phonon method. Such a method generates a basis of n-phonon (n = 0, 1, 2, 3...) states composed of tensor products of particle-hole Tamm-Dancoff phonons and then solves the full eigenvalue problem in such a basis. The method does not rely on any approximation and is free of any contamination induced by the center of mass, in virtue of a procedure exploiting the singular value decomposition of rectangular matrices. Two potentials, both derived from the chiral effective field theory, are adopted in a self-consistent calculation performed within a space including up to three phonons. The latter basis states are treated under a simplifying assumption. A comparative analysis with the experimental data points out the different performances of the two potentials. It shows also that the calculation succeeds only partially in the description of the spectroscopic properties and suggests a recipe for further improvements.

## Key claim

The equation of motion phonon method (EMPM), applied to ⁴He using two chiral potentials (NNLOsat and Daejeon16) within a space including up to three phonons (with three-phonon states treated under a diagonal approximation), succeeds only partially in describing spectroscopic properties; the calculation suggests that a more complete treatment of three-phonon states and the inclusion of residual three-body forces in the multiphonon calculation are needed for further improvement.

## Method

The authors generate an orthonormal multiphonon basis from particle-hole Tamm-Dancoff phonons using an equation of motion method, solving the full eigenvalue problem in this basis without approximation except for the truncation of the multiphonon space. Center-of-mass spurious admixtures are removed via a singular value decomposition procedure. Two chiral effective field theory potentials, NNLOsat (V_S) and Daejeon16 (V_D), are used in self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations within harmonic oscillator spaces up to N_max=12, including up to three phonons; three-phonon states are treated in the diagonal approximation, neglecting phonon-phonon interactions.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy with V_S: -26.866 MeV after c.m. removal (N_max=12, ℏω=20 MeV); experimental value is approximately -28.3 MeV.
- Ground-state energy with V_D: -28.205 MeV after c.m. removal (N_max=12, ℏω=20 MeV).
- For V_S, the two-phonon correlations contribute ~10% of the ground-state wave function (W_0=0.893, W_2=0.099); for V_D, the HF component dominates (W_0=0.975, W_2=0.024).
- The diagonal approximation for three-phonon states yields deviations from exact results ranging from ~90 keV to ~250 keV for V_D and from ~30 keV to ~500 keV for V_S, except for the 2⁻₁ state with V_S which deviates by ~1 MeV.
- The lowest seven negative-parity states computed with V_S are ~1 MeV above experimental levels; those with V_D are ~3 MeV above.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous EMPM calculation for ⁴He (De Gregorio 2021) from a restricted N_max=5 space to larger spaces up to N_max=12, using two chiral potentials. It contrasts with no-core shell model calculations (Navratil 2007, Barrett 2013), coupled cluster (Hagen 2014), and in-medium SRG (Hergert 2016) approaches that have focused on bulk properties or giant resonances, while this study covers both ground and excited states. The results enable future applications of the c.m.-free EMPM to heavier closed-shell nuclei and suggest that a full treatment of three-phonon states and inclusion of three-body forces in the multiphonon calculation are necessary for improved agreement with experiment.
