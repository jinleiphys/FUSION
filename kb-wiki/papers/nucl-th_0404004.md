# N-N Interactions in the Extended Chiral SU(3) Quark Model

> The chiral SU(3) quark model is extended to include coupling between vector chiral field and quarks. By using this model, the phase shifts of NN scattering for different partial waves are studied. The results are very similar to those of the chiral SU(3) quark model calculation, in which one gluon exchange (OGE) plays dominate role in the short range part of the quark-quark interactions. Only in the $^1S_0$ case, the one channel phase shifts of the extended chiral SU(3) quark model are obviously improved.

## Key claim

The extended chiral SU(3) quark model, which adds vector meson exchanges to the original chiral SU(3) quark model, produces NN scattering phase shifts very similar to those of the original model where one gluon exchange (OGE) dominates the short-range part; only in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave are the one-channel phase shifts "obviously improved."

## Method

The authors extended the chiral SU(3) quark model by including couplings between vector meson fields and quarks in the interaction Lagrangian, with both vector and tensor coupling terms. They fitted the model parameters (including the \(\sigma\) meson mass, OGE coupling constant \(g_u\), and confinement strength) to baryon ground-state masses and the deuteron binding energy, then solved the Resonating Group Method (RGM) equation to compute NN scattering phase shifts for \(S\), \(P\), \(D\), \(F\), and \(G\) partial waves. Two parameter sets were used: set I with no tensor coupling (\(f_{chv}/g_{chv}=0\)) and set II with \(f_{chv}/g_{chv}=2/3\).

## Key numbers

- The OGE coupling constant \(\alpha_s = g_u^2\) is reduced from 0.785 in the chiral SU(3) quark model to 0.086 (set I) and 0.159 (set II) in the extended model.
- The size parameter \(b_u\) is 0.50 fm in the chiral SU(3) quark model and 0.45 fm in the extended model.
- The deuteron binding energy is fitted to 2.13 MeV (chiral SU(3) model), 2.19 MeV (set I), and 2.14 MeV (set II).
- The \(\sigma\) meson mass \(m_\sigma\) is 595 MeV in the chiral SU(3) model, 535 MeV (set I), and 547 MeV (set II).
- The vector meson coupling constant \(g_{chv}\) is 2.351 (set I) and 1.972 (set II); the tensor-to-vector ratio \(f_{chv}/g_{chv}\) is 0 (set I) and 2/3 (set II).
- The cut-off mass \(\Lambda\) is 1100 MeV (\(\Lambda^{-1}=0.18\) fm).

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model of Zhang et al. (prior work by the same group) by adding vector meson exchanges, motivated by studies of baryon structure by Riska and Glozman (early 2000s) where vector meson coupling replaced OGE. The results show that vector meson exchanges can largely substitute for OGE in the short-range NN interaction, reducing the OGE coupling constant dramatically. The model enables further study of whether OGE or vector meson exchange (or both) is the correct mechanism for short-range quark-quark interactions, and suggests that different coupling constants for \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons may be needed to improve higher partial waves (\(^3P_0\), \(^3P_2\), \(^3D_2\), \(^3D_3\)).
