# Effective hadron masses and effective interactions in nuclear matter

> Relations among the effective hadron masses and the effective interactions in nuclear matter are studied by using the generalized relativistic mean field theory. We found that we can make the effective omega-meson mass smaller and the equation of state softer if we require that the omega-meson mean field is proportional to the baryon density. In this case, there is a simple and exact relation between the effective omega-meson mass and the effective omega-nucleon coupling. Because of this relation, the effective omega-nucleon coupling automatically decreases if the effective omega-meson mass decreases. Consequently, the equation of state becomes softer. A trial to embed the QCD sum-rule results into the hadron field theory is also shown.

## Key claim

The authors show that requiring the ω-meson mean field to be proportional to the baryon density creates a simple exact relation between the effective ω-meson mass and the effective ω-nucleon coupling, such that reducing the effective ω-meson mass automatically reduces the effective coupling and softens the equation of state, contrary to the usual case where ω-meson mass reduction stiffens the equation of state.

## Method

The authors generalize the relativistic mean field theory by including both higher-order effective interactions (beyond Hartree) and explicit density dependence of effective masses and couplings through auxiliary scalar (S) and vector (V) fields. They derive a density evolution equation for the effective potential, which acts as a thermodynamic consistency condition relating the mesonic potential to the nucleon self-energies. Using this framework, they analytically derive the relation between incompressibility, effective meson masses, and effective couplings, then numerically study the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter using the NL3 parameter set with additional higher-order couplings.

## Key numbers

- Normal baryon density ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³ (from the NL3 parameter set)
- Binding energy per nucleon a₁ = 16.299 MeV (from the NL3 parameter set)
- Effective nucleon mass at normal density m₀* = 0.60 m (from the NL3 parameter set)
- Incompressibility K = 271.76 MeV (from the NL3 parameter set)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analytical study by Tuchitani et al. (2004), who showed that ω-meson self-interaction enhances the effective ω-meson mass and softens the equation of state, but only included simple Hartree contributions for density fluctuations. The present paper generalizes that model by incorporating explicit density dependence following the density-dependent hadron field theory approach of Fuchs, Lenske, and Wolter (1995). The results enable embedding QCD sum-rule predictions for vector meson mass reduction into hadron field theory while maintaining thermodynamic consistency, which the authors note is equivalent to the chemical potential flow equation proposed by Berges, Jungnickel, and Wetterich (2002).
