# Hydrodynamics of massive particles with spin 1/2

> The formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics for massive particles with spin 1/2 is shortly reviewed. The proposed framework is based on the Wigner function treated in a semi-classical approximation or, alternatively, on a classical treatment of spin 1/2. Several theoretical issues regarding the choice of the energy-momentum and spin tensors used to construct the hydrodynamic framework with spin are discussed in parallel.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics for massive spin-1/2 particles that extends the standard concept of local thermodynamic equilibrium to a "local spin-thermodynamic equilibrium," where the spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) can differ from the thermal vorticity \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}\), rather than being forced equal to it as in earlier models.

## Method

The framework is constructed by defining a density operator \(\rho_{\rm LEQ}\) that depends on temperature \(T(x)\), flow four-vector \(u^\mu(x)\), chemical potential \(\mu(x)\), and a spin chemical potential \(\Omega_{\mu\nu}(x) = \omega_{\mu\nu}(x) T(x)\). Expectation values of the energy-momentum tensor, spin tensor, and baryon current are then computed, yielding constitutive equations. The hydrodynamic equations are obtained from conservation of energy-momentum, conservation of total angular momentum (which gives a non-trivial equation for the spin tensor), and conservation of baryon current. A semiclassical kinetic approach using equilibrium Wigner functions is also employed, and the resulting equations are compared with those from a canonical pseudo-gauge transformation.

## Key numbers

* The hydrodynamic system consists of **eleven equations** for **eleven unknown functions**: temperature, three independent components of the fluid four-velocity, chemical potential, and six independent components of the tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\).
* The spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) has **six independent components**.
* The paper reports that the first numerical solutions of the GLW-type hydrodynamic equations (Eqs. (13) in the text) were obtained in Florkowski (2019).

## Context

This work extends the earlier hydrodynamic description of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, which identified spin polarization with thermal vorticity \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}\) as in Becattini (2007, 2009, 2016) and was used to explain the global polarization of \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) hyperons measured by the STAR Collaboration. The paper addresses a known problem: the theoretically predicted longitudinal polarization of \(\Lambda\)'s (Becattini, 2017) had the opposite sign of azimuthal-angle dependence compared to the STAR data (Niida, 2018). The proposed generalization to local spin-thermodynamic equilibrium, originally introduced in Florkowski (2017) and developed in subsequent works, allows the spin polarization tensor to evolve independently of thermal vorticity. The framework enables future inclusion of dissipation effects, with first steps taken in Hattori (2019) and Bhadury (2020), and aims to clarify the relation between spin polarization and thermal vorticity in a complete viscous hydrodynamics with spin.
