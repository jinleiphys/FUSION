# Thermal x-ray studies of neutron stars and the equation of state

> The understanding of neutron star equation of state hinges on a comprehensive analysis of multi-messenger, multi-wavelength data. The recent scrutiny of PSR J0030+0451 data by NICER introduces complexities, unveiling a tension with another X-ray observation of the central compact object in HESS J1731-347, specifically concerning the mass-radius constraint of low-mass neutron stars. This tension persists when integrating NICER's updated data with LIGO/Virgo's gravitational-wave data from the GW170817 binary neutron star merger. Despite attempts to reconcile these disparate observations, the current combined data still can not distinguish different types of neutron stars -- whether they are pure neutron stars or hybrid stars. Bayesian inference indicates only modest changes in the posterior ranges of parameters related to the nuclear matter and deconfinement phase transition. This ongoing exploration underscores the intricate challenges in precisely characterizing neutron stars. It also points out that it is possible to probe the equation of state at different density regimes from future more accurate radii of neutron stars with various masses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that current combined multi-messenger data (NICER, XMM-Newton, LIGO/Virgo) still cannot distinguish between pure neutron stars and hybrid stars containing deconfined quark matter, with Bayesian inference showing only modest changes in posterior ranges for nuclear matter and phase transition parameters.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference on neutron star equation of state (EoS) parameters using mass-radius measurements from NICER (with and without XMM-Newton data) for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, the central compact object in HESS J1731-347, and tidal deformability constraints from GW170817. They modeled the EoS using relativistic mean-field theory with nucleonic degrees of freedom and separately with a hadron-quark phase transition, employing bivariate Gaussian distributions to approximate the NICER posteriors and a high-precision interpolation method for the gravitational wave likelihood.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0030+0451 (NICER only, PDT-U model): mass = 1.41 +/- 0.20 solar masses, radius = 13.12 (+1.35/-1.21) km
- PSR J0030+0451 (NICER + XMM-Newton, PDT-U model): mass = 1.70 (+0.18/-0.19) solar masses, radius = 14.44 (+0.88/-1.05) km
- PSR J0740+6620 (NICER + XMM-Newton): mass = 2.072 (+0.067/-0.066) solar masses, radius = 12.39 (+1.30/-0.98) km
- HESS J1731-347 (XMM-Newton + Suzaku): mass = 0.77 (+0.20/-0.17) solar masses, radius = 10.4 (+0.86/-0.78) km
- GW170817 tidal deformability: Lambda_tilde <= 800
- Minimum spin period from pulsar surveys: P_min = 1.43 (+0.03/-0.09) ms

## Context

This work extends the NICER collaboration analyses of PSR J0030+0451 by Riley (2019) and Miller (2019) and the reanalysis by Vinciguerra (2024) that included XMM-Newton data, revealing tensions with the low-mass, small-radius measurement of HESS J1731-347 by Doroshenko (2022). The study also incorporates gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 reported by the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2017). The results enable future work to probe the EoS at different density regimes using more accurate radii measurements of neutron stars with various masses.
