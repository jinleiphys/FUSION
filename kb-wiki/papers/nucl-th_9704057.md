# Gauge-invariant theory of pion photoproduction with dressed hadrons

> Based on an effective field theory of hadrons in which quantum chromodynamics is assumed to provide the necessary bare cutoff functions, a gauge-invariant theory of pion photoproduction with fully dressed nucleons is developed. The formalism provides consistent dynamical descriptions of pi-N --> pi-N scattering and Gamma-N --> pi-N production mechanisms in terms of nonlinear integral equations for fully dressed hadrons. Defining electromagnetic currents via the gauging of hadronic n-point Green's functions, dynamically detailed currents for dressed nucleons are introduced. The dressed hadron currents and the pion photoproduction current are explicitly shown to satisfy gauge invariance in a self-consistent manner. Approximations are discussed that make the nonlinear formalism manageable in practice and yet preserve gauge invariance. This is achieved by recasting the gauge conditions for all contributing interaction currents as continuity equations with ``surface'' terms for the individual particle legs coming into or going out of the hadronic interaction region. General procedures are given that approximate any type of (global) interaction current in a gauge-invariance preserving manner as a sum of single-particle ``surface'' currents. It is argued that these prescriptions carry over to other reactions, irrespective of the number or type of contributing hadrons or hadronic systems.

## Key claim

The paper develops a gauge-invariant formalism for pion photoproduction using fully dressed hadrons, where the gauge invariance is preserved self-consistently even when the nonlinear integral equations are approximated for practical calculations. The key hedge is that the formalism assumes quantum chromodynamics provides the necessary bare cutoff functions, and the proof of gauge invariance is one of self-consistency rather than an independent derivation.

## Method

The author constructs a nonlinear effective field theory for πN → πN scattering and γN → πN production, defining electromagnetic currents via a "gauge derivative" operation applied to hadronic n-point Green's functions rather than the usual minimal substitution and functional derivative approach. The formalism recasts gauge conditions for all contributing interaction currents as continuity equations with "surface" terms for individual particle legs entering or leaving the hadronic interaction region, then provides general procedures to approximate any global interaction current as a sum of single-particle surface currents while preserving gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The dressed nucleon propagator S(p) has a pole at the nucleon mass m with unit residue, achieved by choosing renormalization parameters Zδm = Σ(m) and Z = 1 + dΣ(p̸)/dp̸ evaluated at p̸ = m
- The bare πNN vertex function is F(p',p) = g₀ G₅ f(p',p), where g₀ = √Z gB is the renormalized bare coupling constant
- The dressed pion propagator Δ⁻¹(q) = (q² − μ²)Zπ + Π(mπ²) − Π(q²), with mπ the physical pion mass and Π(q²) the pion self-energy
- The bare nucleon current is j₀^μ = QN Z γ^μ, where QN is the nucleon charge and Z the field renormalization constant

## Context

This work extends prior approaches by Gross (1974), Afnan and collaborators (1980s), Ohta (1989), Friar (1977), and Coester (1977) that attempted to account for the internal structure of hadrons in photoproduction reactions. The formalism provides a comprehensive theoretical framework for gauge-invariant interactions of physical (fully dressed) hadrons with photons, and the author argues the approximation scheme carries over to other reactions irrespective of the number or type of contributing hadrons. The paper enables practical calculations that preserve gauge invariance even when the nonlinear πN scattering equations are linearized for computational tractability.
