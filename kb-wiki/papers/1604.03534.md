# In-medium covariant propagator of baryons under a strong magnetic field: effect of the intrinsic magnetic moments

> We obtain the covariant propagator at finite temperature for interacting baryons immersed in a strong magnetic field. The effect of the intrinsic magnetic moments on the Green function are fully taken into account. We make an expansion in terms of eigenfunctions of a Dirac field, which leads us to a compact form of its propagator. We present some simple applications of these propagators, where the statistical averages of nuclear currents and energy density are evaluated.

# In-medium covariant propagator of baryons under a strong magnetic field: effect of the intrinsic magnetic moments

## Key claim
The authors derive a covariant propagator for baryons (protons and neutrons) at finite temperature in a strong magnetic field that fully incorporates the intrinsic magnetic moments of the nucleons, extending previous work that neglected these moments. The propagator is constructed for magnetic field strengths above approximately 5 × 10^17 G, where the magnetic moment contribution becomes significant for determining stable matter configurations and thermodynamic properties.

## Method
The authors solve the Dirac equation for nucleons in a constant external magnetic field along the z-axis, including the anomalous magnetic moment term via a Pauli coupling. They expand the Dirac fields in terms of the complete set of eigenfunctions for both charged (proton) and neutral (neutron) states, using Landau level quantization for protons and plane waves for neutrons. The in-medium causal propagator is then constructed using the standard thermal field theory approach, with the statistical averages evaluated using Fermi-Dirac occupation numbers at temperature T and chemical potential μ_b. The results are expressed in a compact form using the eigenfunction expansion and are interpreted within the context of Quantum Hadro-Dynamics (QHD) in the mean field approximation (MFA).

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field intensities in heavy ion collisions: e B ∼ 10^2 MeV^2 (from Kharzeev, 2008) and up to e B ∼ m_π^2 ∼ 2 × 10^4 MeV^2 (from Skokov, 2009)
- Magnetar surface magnetic field: approximately 10^15 G, with potentially higher values in the stellar interior
- Threshold for magnetic moment importance: 5 × 10^17 G, above which intrinsic magnetic moments must be included in matter stability calculations
- Anomalous magnetic moments: χ_p = 2.79 for protons, χ_n = -1.91 for neutrons (in units of nuclear magneton μ_N)
- Proton energy dispersion for n=0 Landau level: E_0 = sqrt(p_z^2 + (m_p - κ_p B)^2)
- Proton energy dispersion for n≥1: E_{n s} = sqrt(p_z^2 + (Δ_n - s κ_p B)^2) where Δ_n = sqrt(m_p^2 + 2 n q B)
- Neutron energy dispersion: E_{p s} = sqrt(p_z^2 + (Δ - s κ_n B)^2) where Δ = sqrt(m_n^2 + p_x^2 + p_y^2)

## Context
This work extends the covariant propagator formalism of Schwinger (1951) and Ritus (1972) for charged particles in magnetic fields by including intrinsic magnetic moments, which were previously neglected. It also extends the vacuum propagator of Cheoun (2011) for protons to the in-medium case at finite temperature and density. The results are shown to reproduce the QHD mean field calculations of Broderick (2000) for nuclear scalar density, baryon density, and energy density in the zero-temperature limit. The propagator enables future diagrammatic calculations of quantum corrections and statistical averages for nuclear processes under strong magnetic fields, relevant for magnetar physics and heavy ion collision phenomenology.
