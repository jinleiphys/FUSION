# Universality aspects of quantum corrections to transverse momentum broadening in QCD media

> We study non-linear quantum corrections to transverse momentum broadening (TMB) of a fast parton propagating in dense QCD matter in the leading logarithmic approximation. These non-local corrections yield an anomalous super-diffusive behavior characterized by a heavy tailed distribution which is associated with Lévy random walks. Using a formal analogy with the physics of traveling waves, we show that at late times the transverse momentum distribution tends to a universal scaling regime. We derive analytic solutions in terms of an asymptotic expansion around the scaling limit for both fixed and running coupling. We note that our analytic approach yields a good agreement with the exact numerical solutions down to realistic values of medium length. Finally, we discuss the interplay between system size and energy dependence of the diffusion coefficient $\hat q$ and its connection with the gluon distribution function that is manifest at large transverse momentum transfer.

## Key claim

Non-linear quantum corrections to transverse momentum broadening in dense QCD matter, resummed in the double logarithmic approximation, produce a universal scaling regime at late times where the transverse momentum distribution becomes independent of the initial, tree-level value of the jet quenching parameter, though this universality is strictly asymptotic and requires the system size to be large enough for the asymptotic expansion to be accurate.

## Method

The authors study the evolution equation for the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) in the double logarithmic approximation (DLA), which resums terms of order \(\alpha_s^n \ln^{2n}(L)\) to all orders, using a formal analogy with the Fisher-Kolmogoroff-Petrovsky-Piscounoff (FKPP) equation describing traveling waves. They derive analytic asymptotic expansions for both fixed and running coupling cases, solving the non-linear evolution equation for the saturation scale \(Q_s(L)\) and the quenching parameter \(\hat q(L, k_\perp^2)\) as functions of the medium length \(L\), and compare these analytic results to exact numerical solutions.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale \(Q_s\) scales asymptotically as \(L^{1/2 + \sqrt{\bar \alpha}}\) for fixed coupling, where \(\bar \alpha = \alpha_s N_c / \pi\), growing faster than the diffusive \(L^{1/2}\) scaling characteristic of normal diffusion.
* The large-\(k_\perp\) tail of the asymptotic transverse momentum distribution behaves as \(1/k_\perp^{4 - 2\sqrt{\bar \alpha}}\), which is harder than the tree-level Rutherford tail \(1/k_\perp^4\).
* The double logarithmic correction to the mean transverse momentum squared at one loop is \(\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle_{\text{1-loop,DL}} \simeq \hat q_0 L \left(1 + \frac{\bar \alpha}{2} \ln^2 \frac{L}{\tau_0}\right)\), where \(\tau_0\) is the mean free path scale and \(\hat q_0\) is the tree-level quenching parameter constant.

## Context

This work extends the resummation program for radiative corrections to transverse momentum broadening initiated by Liou, Mueller, and Wu (2013), who computed single logarithmic corrections, and by Blaizot, Iancu, and collaborators (2014) and Iancu (2014), who formulated the non-linear evolution equation in DLA. The authors provide the first full analytic solution of the non-linear evolution equation, going beyond previous studies that were limited to its linearized version. The results enable precision phenomenology for jet quenching at RHIC and LHC, and connect to the physics of Levy flights and anomalous diffusion in QCD media, with implications for the energy dependence of the jet quenching parameter and its relation to gluon distribution functions in deep inelastic scattering.
