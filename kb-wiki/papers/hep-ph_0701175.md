# Spin Filtering of Stored (Anti)Protons: from FILTEX to COSY to AD to FAIR

> We review the theory of spin filtering of stored (anti)protons by multiple passage through the polarized internal target (PIT). Implications for the antiproton polarization buildup in the proposed PAX experiment at FAIR GSI are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Meyer interpretation of the FILTEX spin-filtering result, which attributed a significant role to polarization transfer from polarized target electrons to protons, is incorrect; instead, the Budker and Jülich groups find that the electron contribution to spin filtering is entirely canceled by the transmission filtering term, leaving nuclear spin-flip scattering as the dominant mechanism.

## Method

The authors derive a kinetic equation for the spin-momentum density matrix of a stored beam passing through a polarized internal target (PIT), including both transmission filtering (removal of particles scattering outside the ring acceptance angle) and feedback from scattering within the ring acceptance angle (SWRAA). They analyze the cancellation pattern between these two terms for scattering off electrons and protons separately, using the SAID phase-shift database for nuclear amplitudes and accounting for Coulomb-Nuclear Interference (CNI) effects.

## Key numbers

- FILTEX measured polarization cross section: σ_P(FILTEX, 1993) = 63 ± 3 (stat.) mb; reanalysis gave σ_P(FILTEX, 2004) = 72.5 ± 5.8 (stat. + sys.) mb
- Meyer's calculated net polarization cross section: σ_P = 135 mb (from CNI + proton SWRAA) + (-70 mb from electron SWRAA) = 65 mb
- Budker-Jülich calculated polarization cross section: σ_P ≈ 85.6 mb, from σ_1(CNI; θ > θ_acc) + Δσ_1
- All-angle nuclear σ_1 from SAID phase shifts: 122 mb; after CNI correction for θ > θ_acc (4.4 mrad): 83 mb
- Characteristic angles at TSR (23 MeV protons): θ_min ≈ 2×10⁻² mrad, θ_e ≈ 5×10⁻¹ mrad, θ_acc = 4.4 mrad, θ_CNI ≈ 100 mrad
- Nuclear spin-flip cross section estimate: Δσ_0 ≲ σ_tot × θ_acc² ≲ 10⁻⁴ σ_tot

## Context

This work extends the theory of spin filtering developed for the FILTEX experiment (1993) at the TSR ring in Heidelberg, which demonstrated polarization buildup of 23 MeV protons. It directly contradicts the earlier analysis by H. O. Meyer (2004) that attributed a major role to electron-to-proton polarization transfer in explaining the FILTEX result. The resolution is critical for the proposed PAX experiment at FAIR GSI, which plans to use spin filtering to produce polarized antiprotons for Drell-Yan measurements of transversity; the authors propose experimental tests at COSY using proton depolarization with a ⁴He target and energy-dependence measurements to discriminate between the competing theoretical treatments.
