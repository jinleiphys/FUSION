# Electric dipole moment of $^{13}$C

> We calculate for the first time the electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{13}$C generated by the isovector CP-odd pion exchange nuclear force in the $α$-cluster model, which describes well the structures of low lying states of the $^{13}$C nucleus. The linear dependence of the EDM of $^{13}$C on the neutron EDM and the isovector CP-odd nuclear coupling is found to be $d_{^{13}{\rm C}} = -0.33 d_n - 0.0020 \bar G_π^{(1)}$. The linear enhancement factor of the CP-odd nuclear coupling is smaller than that of the deuteron, due to the difference of the structure between the $1/2^-_1$ state and the opposite parity ($1/2^+$) states. We clarify the role of the structure played in the enhancement of the EDM. This result provides good guiding principles to search for other nuclei with large enhancement factor. We also mention the role of the EDM of $^{13}$C in determining the new physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{13}$C is calculated for the first time using the $\alpha$-cluster model, and the linear enhancement factor of the isovector CP-odd nuclear coupling is found to be smaller than that of the deuteron, due to the structural difference between the $1/2^-_1$ ground state and the opposite parity ($1/2^+$) states, with the ground state EDM being dominated by transitions to continuum states rather than bound states.

## Method

The EDM of $^{13}$C was calculated within a $3\alpha + n$ four-body cluster model using the Gaussian expansion method, solving the Schrödinger equation by variational principle with 43904 basis states (128 angular momentum channels with 343 bases each). The CP-odd nuclear force was taken as the isovector one-pion exchange potential, folded with the $\alpha$-cluster density to obtain the $\alpha$-$N$ CP-odd interaction, and the nuclear EDM was computed via first-order perturbation theory as the sum over parity-mixing matrix elements between the $1/2^-_1$ ground state and $1/2^+$ opposite parity states.

## Key numbers

- $d_{^{13}{\rm C}} = -0.33 d_n - 0.0020 \bar G_\pi^{(1)} e\,{\rm fm}$: linear dependence of the $^{13}$C EDM on the neutron EDM $d_n$ and the isovector CP-odd nuclear coupling $\bar G_\pi^{(1)}$.
- $\langle \sqrt{r^2} \rangle_{1/2^-_1} = 2.81$ fm: root mean square core-valence distance of the $^{13}$C ground state.
- $\langle \sqrt{r^2} \rangle_{1/2^+_1} = 3.95$ fm: root mean square core-valence distance of the $^{13}$C first excited $1/2^+$ state.
- $d^{\rm bound}_{^{13}{\rm C}} = 0.00025 \, \bar G_\pi^{(1)} e\,{\rm fm}$: contribution to the ground state EDM from the bound $1/2^-_1$ to $1/2^+_1$ transition only.
- $d^{\rm rem}_{^{13}{\rm C}} = -0.0022 \, \bar G_\pi^{(1)} e\,{\rm fm}$: remaining ground state EDM contribution from transitions to continuum states and resonances.
- $d_{^{13}{\rm C}^*}^{\rm (pol)} = 0.0244 \, \bar G_\pi^{(1)} e\,{\rm fm}$: EDM of the first excited state (in the spectrum with CP-odd nuclear force).
- $\mu_{^{13}{\rm C}} = 0.63$: estimated nuclear magnetic moment from the $p$-wave core+valence model, compared to the experimental value $\mu_{^{13}{\rm C}} = 0.702411(1)$.

## Context

This work extends the cluster-model EDM calculations of Yamanaka et al. (previous work on $^6$Li and $^9$Be) to the four-body $3\alpha + n$ system, finding that the $^{13}$C ground state EDM is an order of magnitude smaller than the deuteron EDM due to poor overlap between the $1/2^-_1$ and $1/2^+_1$ states and the dominance of continuum transitions. The result provides guiding principles for searching for nuclei with larger EDM enhancement factors, and the authors note that measuring the $^{13}$C EDM alongside other nuclear EDMs is necessary to constrain the multiple unknown CP-odd hadron-level couplings in chiral effective field theory.
