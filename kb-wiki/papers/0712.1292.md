# Two-body scattering without angular-momentum decomposition

> Two-body scattering is studied by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space without angular-momentum decomposition for a local spin dependent short range interaction plus Coulomb. The screening and renormalization approach is employed to treat the Coulomb interaction. Benchmark calculations are performed by comparing our procedure with partial-wave calculations in configuration space for p-10Be, p-16O and 12C-10Be elastic scattering, using a simple optical potential model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-body scattering can be solved in momentum space without angular-momentum decomposition for a local spin-dependent short-range interaction plus Coulomb, using the screening and renormalization approach; the method is shown to be reliable for benchmark cases but the authors note that convergence with the screening radius is slower for reactions involving two heavy nuclei than for proton-nucleus systems.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space as a two-dimensional integral equation in the off-shell wave vector magnitude and the cosine of the scattering angle, avoiding partial-wave decomposition. For spin-orbit interactions, the T-matrix is factorized into a known azimuthal phase factor and a reduced amplitude that satisfies a coupled set of two-variable integral equations. The Coulomb interaction is treated via a screening and renormalization approach using a new screening function that has an analytical Fourier transform, and the pure Coulomb amplitude is subtracted using a two-potential formula.

## Key numbers

- For p-10Be elastic scattering at 50, 100, 150, and 200 MeV, the screening radius was taken as R=5 fm; for p-16O at the same energies, R=7 fm; for 12C-10Be at 49.3 MeV per nucleon, R=12 fm.
- The CPU time for p-10Be calculations was about 10 minutes on a single AMD Opteron (2.4 GHz) processor using n_phi=40, n_theta=64, and n_q=64 mesh points.
- The CPU time for p-16O calculations was about 30 minutes on the same processor using n_phi=50, n_theta=80, and n_q=80 mesh points.
- The CPU time for 12C-10Be calculations was about 1 hour using sixteen processors with n_phi=80, n_q=300, and n_theta=240 mesh points divided in three regions.
- Convergence of the differential cross section relative to Rutherford for p-10Be at 50 MeV was achieved at R=5 fm, with all curves falling on top of each other from that screening radius onward.

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization approach previously applied to three- and four-nucleon scattering by Deltuva et al. (2005-2007) and to direct nuclear reactions by Deltuva et al. (2006) and Crespo et al. (2007), which all used partial-wave decomposition. The method contrasts with earlier momentum-space approaches without partial-wave decomposition by Elster et al. (1998) and Fonseca et al. (2000) that did not include an exact treatment of Coulomb, and with the Vincent and Phatak (1974) method that works for proton-proton scattering but not accurately for proton-nucleus scattering at intermediate energies. The results enable extension to exact three-body calculations without partial-wave decomposition for reactions at higher energies or involving two heavy nuclei, where partial-wave expansions converge slowly or become unstable.
