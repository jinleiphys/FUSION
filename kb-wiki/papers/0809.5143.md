# Baryon Dissociation in a Strongly Coupled Plasma

> Using the dual string theory, we study a circular baryonic configuration in a wind of strongly coupled N=4 Yang-Mills plasma blowing in the plane of the baryon, before and after a quark has dissociated from it. A simple enough model that captures many interesting features is when there are four quarks in the baryon. As a step towards phenomenology, we compare representative dissociated configurations, and make some comments about their energetics and other properties. Related results that we find include the observation that the screening length formula L_s T ~ (1-v^2)^{1/4} obtained previously for other color singlet configurations, is robust for circular baryons as well.

## Key claim

Using the dual string theory (AdS/CFT correspondence), the author studies a circular baryonic configuration with four quarks in a wind of strongly coupled N=4 Yang-Mills plasma, finding that the screening length formula L_s T ~ (1-v^2)^{1/4} previously obtained for other color singlet configurations is robust for circular baryons as well, and that for dissociated configurations, the energy of quarks aligned parallel to the wind is higher than for those aligned perpendicular, suggesting quarks along the wind may dissociate first.

## Method

The author models a baryon with N_c=4 external quarks using a D5-brane baryon vertex wrapping an S^5 in the AdS_5 x S^5 bulk with a black hole interior, where the plasma wind is simulated by boosting the black hole metric. The Nambu-Goto action for the strings and the D5-brane action are used to compute static configurations, with the energy regulated by subtracting the energy of a free string stretching from the boundary to the horizon. For dissociated configurations, the author compares two extreme scenarios: three remaining quarks spaced equidistantly along a line parallel to the wind (case I) versus perpendicular to the wind (case II), solving the force balance conditions at the D5-brane and integrating the string equations of motion numerically.

## Key numbers

- The screening length satisfies L_s T ~ (1-v^2)^{1/4} for large boosts, confirmed for circular baryons with N_c=4.
- The Hawking temperature is given by T = r_0 / (pi R^2), where r_0 is the black hole horizon radius.
- The boosted metric parameters are defined with r_1^4 = r_0^4 cosh^2 eta and r_2^4 = r_0^4 sinh^2 eta, where v = -tanh eta.
- The D5-brane action is S_D5 = (N_c R T) / (8 pi alpha') sqrt(A(r_e)), with N_c=4.
- The regulated energy expression for the undissociated baryon is E = T sqrt(lambda) [ (1/rho) integral_1^infty dy ( sqrt((y^4 - rho^4 cosh^2 eta)/(y^4 - rho^4 - beta^2)) - 1 ) + 1 - 1/rho + (1/rho) integral_1^infty dy ( (y^4 - rho^4 cosh^2 eta)/sqrt((y^4 - rho^4)(y^4 - rho^4 cosh^2 eta - alpha^2)) - 1 ) + 1 - 1/rho + sqrt(1 - rho^4 cosh^2 eta)/(2rho) ].
- For the dissociated configurations, the trailing string solution forces beta_4 = rho^2 sinh eta.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon screening in strongly coupled plasmas using AdS/CFT, particularly the results of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006) on meson screening and the baryon configurations considered by Aharony, Sonnenschein, and Yankielowicz (2006). The author confirms that the screening length formula L_s T ~ (1-v^2)^{1/4} is robust for circular baryons, matching results from earlier work on squashed baryon configurations. The comparison of longitudinal versus transverse dissociated configurations provides a first step toward understanding preferred dissociation directions in heavy quark baryons, which could be relevant for phenomenology at RHIC or LHC, though the author emphasizes that conclusive evidence would require dynamical calculations beyond the static configurations considered here.
