# Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay in the interacting boson model based on the nuclear energy density functionals

> The neutrinoless $ββ$ ($0νββ$) decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) are calculated in the interacting boson model (IBM), which is based on the nuclear energy density functional (EDF) theory. The Hamiltonian of the IBM that gives rise to the energies and wave functions of the ground and excited states of $0νββ$ decay emitting isotopes and corresponding final nuclei is determined by mapping the self-consistent mean-field deformation-energy surface obtained with a given EDF onto the corresponding bosonic energy surface. The transition operators are formulated using the generalized seniority scheme, and the pair structure constants are determined by the inputs provided by the self-consistent calculations. The predicted values of the $0νββ$-decay NMEs with the nonrelativistic and relativistic EDFs are compared with those resulting from different many-body methods. Sensitivities of the predicted NMEs to the model parameters and assumptions are discussed.

## Key claim

The neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) calculated in the interacting boson model (IBM) based on nuclear energy density functional (EDF) theory show sensitivity to the choice of EDF (relativistic vs. nonrelativistic), the IBM Hamiltonian parameters, the SCMF-to-IBM mapping procedure, and the pair structure constants for the transition operators, with the predicted NMEs differing by several factors compared to other many-body methods.

## Method

The IBM-2 Hamiltonian parameters for parent and daughter even-even nuclei were determined by mapping self-consistent mean-field deformation-energy surfaces obtained from constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with the DD-PC1 EDF and from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1M EDF onto the corresponding bosonic energy surfaces. The transition operators for Gamow-Teller, Fermi, and tensor components were formulated using the generalized seniority scheme, with pair structure constants derived from occupation probabilities provided by the self-consistent mean-field calculations. The IBM-2 Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the laboratory frame to obtain wave functions for the initial and final 0⁺ states, and the NMEs were computed under the closure approximation for ten candidate emitters: ⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ⁹⁶Zr, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹¹⁶Cd, ¹²⁸Te, ¹³⁰Te, ¹³⁶Xe, and ¹⁵⁰Nd.

## Key numbers

- The axial-vector coupling constant gA is taken as 1.269 and the vector coupling constant gV as 1.0 for the NME calculations.
- The short-range correlation Jastrow function uses the Argonne parametrization with a = 1.59 fm⁻², b = 1.45 fm⁻², and c = 0.92.
- The nuclear radius is taken as R = 1.2A^{1/3} fm to make the NME dimensionless.
- The quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction strength κ derived from the mapping takes values larger than those in phenomenological IBM-2 calculations by a factor of approximately 2-4.
- The mapped IBM-2 systematically overestimates the 0⁺₂ energy levels for parent and daughter nuclei, particularly with the RHB input.

## Context

This work extends the IBM-2 approach to neutrinoless double-beta decay NMEs by using EDF-based mapping (Nomura 2008, Nomura 2010) rather than fitting Hamiltonian parameters to experimental spectra as in earlier IBM-2 studies (Barea 2009, Barea 2013, Barea 2015). The method builds on previous two-neutrino double-beta decay calculations (Nomura 2022, Nomura 2024) that used the same mapping procedure for even-even cores. The predicted NMEs are compared with results from other many-body approaches including QRPA (Simkovic 2018), nuclear shell model (Horoi 2016), generator coordinate method (Rodriguez 2010), and ab initio methods (Yao 2020, Novario 2021), which differ by several factors, and this work aims to identify uncertainties in the IBM-based predictions.
