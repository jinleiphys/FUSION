# BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter with the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

> Based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory, the influence of the pairing interaction strength on the di-neutron correlations and the crossover from superfluidity of neutron Cooper pairs in the $^{1}S_{0}$ channel to Bose-Einstein condensation of di-neutron pairs is systematically investigated in the nuclear matter. The bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn-B is taken in the particle-particle channel with an effective factor to simulate the medium effects and take into account the possible ambiguity of pairing force, and the effective interaction PK1 is used in the particle-hole channel. If the effective factor is larger than 1.10, a di-neutron BEC state appears in the low-density limit, and if it is smaller than 0.85, the neutron Cooper pairs are found totally in the weak coupling BCS region. The reference values of several characteristic quantities which characterize the BCS-BEC crossover are obtained respectively from the dimensionless parameter $1/(k_{\rm Fn}a)$ with $a$ the scattering length and $k_{\rm{Fn}}$ the neutron Fermi momentum, the zero-momentum transfer density correlation function D(0) and the effective chemical potential $ν_{\rm n}$.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates how the strength of the pairing interaction (controlled by an effective factor λ multiplying the bare Bonn-B potential) determines whether neutron Cooper pairs in the ¹S₀ channel exist in a weak-coupling BCS state or form a di-neutron Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) in low-density nuclear matter. The abstract's claim that a BEC state appears for λ > 1.10 and a BCS state for λ < 0.85 is qualified by the fact that these boundaries depend on which diagnostic quantity (dimensionless parameter 1/(k_Fn a), density correlation function D(0), or effective chemical potential ν_n) is used, with the BEC threshold ranging from λ ≈ 1.05 to λ ≈ 1.10 depending on the chosen criterion.

## Method

The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory was applied to infinite pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, using the PK1 relativistic mean-field interaction in the particle-hole channel and the bare Bonn-B potential in the particle-particle channel. An effective factor λ (varied from 0.8 to 1.4) was introduced to multiply the pairing interaction, simulating medium polarization effects and the ambiguity of the pairing force. The BCS-BEC crossover was characterized by computing the dimensionless parameter 1/(k_Fn a), the zero-momentum transfer density correlation function D(0), and the effective chemical potential ν_n as functions of neutron Fermi momentum k_Fn and λ.

## Key numbers

- The maximum pairing gap at the Fermi surface Δ_Fn for λ = 1.0 (bare Bonn-B) is 3.1 MeV at k_Fn ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹.
- For λ = 0.8, the maximum Δ_Fn is about 1.4 MeV; for λ = 1.4, it is about 6.5 MeV, both at k_Fn ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹.
- For λ = 1.10, the maximum Δ_Fn is 4.12 MeV at k_Fn ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹; for λ = 0.85, it is 1.78 MeV at the same density.
- The BEC boundary (1/(k_Fn a) = 1) starts at λ ≈ 1.10 in the low-density limit; the BCS boundary (1/(k_Fn a) = -1) starts at λ ≈ 1.03 and reaches a minimum λ ≈ 0.85 at k_Fn ≈ 0.35 fm⁻¹.
- The unitarity limit (1/(k_Fn a) = 0) starts at λ ≈ 1.07 in the low-density limit.
- The D(0) = 0 boundary starts at λ ≈ 1.05 in the low-density limit, with a minimum λ ≈ 1.02 at k_Fn ≈ 0.20 fm⁻¹.
- The ν_n = 0 boundary starts at λ ≈ 1.09 in the low-density limit.
- For λ = 1.2 at 1/(k_Fn a) = +1, the ratio ν_n/e_Fn = -0.84 (pure neutron matter) and -0.87 (symmetric nuclear matter).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter using non-relativistic frameworks, such as the finite-range Gogny interaction (Matsuo, 2006) and the regularized contact interaction model (Engelbrecht, 1997), by employing a fully relativistic self-consistent approach with the bare Bonn-B potential. It also builds on the authors' prior work (Sun, 2010) which found no BEC state with the bare Bonn-B potential alone (λ = 1.0). The results provide reference values for characteristic quantities (P(d_n), ξ_rms/d_n, Δ_Fn/e_Fn, ν_n/e_Fn) at the BCS-BEC crossover boundaries, enabling future studies of di-neutron correlations in finite nuclei and neutron-rich systems, and clarifying how medium polarization effects (simulated by λ) can shift the system from BCS to BEC regimes.
