# Non-relativistic bound states at finite temperature (I): the hydrogen atom

> We illustrate how to apply modern effective field theory techniques and dimensional regularization to factorize the various scales which appear in non-relativistic bound states at finite temperature. We focus here on the simplest case: the hydrogen atom. We discuss in detail the interplay of the hard, soft and ultrasoft scales of the non-relativistic system at zero temperature with the additional scales induced at finite temperature. We also comment on the implications of our results for heavy quarkonium bound states in the quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that modern effective field theory techniques (NRQED and pNRQED) can be systematically extended to finite temperature to factorize the multiple scales appearing in non-relativistic bound states, using the hydrogen atom as a test case, with the caveat that the approach works only when the temperature is much smaller than the electron mass (T ≪ m) to avoid complications from hard thermal loops.

## Method

The authors apply non-relativistic QED (NRQED) and potential NRQED (pNRQED) in the real-time formalism to separate the hard (m), soft (p ∼ mα/n), and ultrasoft (E ∼ mα²/n²) scales at zero temperature, then incorporate the new scale T by matching at different temperature regimes. They analyze three distinct temperature regimes: T ∼ E (ultrasoft photons only), m ≫ T ≫ E (temperature-dependent potentials from matching NRQED to pNRQED), and T ∼ m (matching QED to NRQED with hard thermal loops). The matching calculations use dimensional regularization and the modified minimal subtraction (MS-bar) scheme to handle infrared divergences.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy at zero temperature: E = -mα²/2n² (ultrasoft scale), with typical momentum p = mα/n (soft scale)
- For T ≪ E regime: thermal energy shift δE_n = -(4π³α/45β⁴)⟨n|x(bar P_n/(H₀-E_n))x|n⟩(1+O((n²/βmα)²)), with zero thermal decay width
- For T ≫ E regime: thermal decay width δΓ_n = 4Z²α³/(3βn²) at leading order
- For m ≫ T ≫ E regime: thermal energy shift δE_n = απ/(3mβ²) - πα³/(6mβ²n²) + (2α/3π)Σ_r|⟨n|v|r⟩|²(E_n-E_r)(ln(2π/β|E_n-E_r|)-γ)
- Debye mass squared for T ∼ m: m_D² ∼ 8α√(πm³/2β)e^{-mβ} (exponentially suppressed when T < m)
- New dynamical scale m_d² ∼ (16αm/β²)^{2/3}e^{-2mβ/3}, parametrically larger than m_D

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach for non-relativistic bound states, previously developed for zero-temperature QED by Caswell (1985) and Pineda (1997, 1998), to finite temperature, providing a systematic framework that earlier finite-temperature studies by Farley (1982), Donoghue (1984), and Cha (1984) lacked. The results enable precision calculations for heavy quarkonium in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, building on recent work by Laine (2006, 2007) and Burnier (2007), and recover the QCD results of Laine (2006) in the m → 0 limit by appropriate substitutions.
