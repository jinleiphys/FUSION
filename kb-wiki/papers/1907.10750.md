# Relaxation Time for Strange Quark Spin in Rotating Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) have measured the net polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons and attributed it to a coupling between their spin and the vorticity of the fluid created in heavy ion collisions. Equipartition of energy is generally assumed, but the dynamical mechanism which polarizes them has yet to be determined. We consider two such mechanisms: vorticity fluctuations and helicity flip in scatterings between strange quarks and light quarks and gluons. With reasonable parameters both mechanisms lead to equilibration times orders of magnitude too large to be relevant to heavy ion collisions. Our conclusion is that strange quark spin or helicity is unchanged from the time they are created to the time they hadronize. A corollary is that vorticity fluctuations do not affect the hyperon spin either.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two proposed dynamical mechanisms for polarizing strange quarks in a rotating quark-gluon plasma, vorticity fluctuations and helicity-flip scatterings, both produce equilibration times orders of magnitude too large to be relevant for heavy ion collisions, so strange quark spin or helicity remains unchanged from creation to hadronization.

## Method

The authors derived the Dirac Hamiltonian for a massive strange quark in a rotating frame using a tetrad formalism, then computed relaxation rates via two independent approaches: (1) second-order response theory for spin relaxation due to fluctuating vorticity fields, and (2) lowest-order QCD perturbation theory for helicity-flip scattering rates of strange quarks with massless up/down quarks and gluons. For the scattering mechanism, they calculated the helicity-flip cross sections and thermal-averaged rates in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- For vorticity fluctuations: relaxation time τ ≈ 10⁻²⁰ s for typical RHIC parameters (ω₀ ≈ 10 MeV, correlation time τ_c ≈ 1 fm/c, fluctuation amplitude √(⟨ω²⟩) ≈ ω₀), which is about 10⁴ times longer than the plasma lifetime (~10⁻²³ s)
- For helicity-flip scattering: relaxation time τ ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ s for strange quark mass m_s ≈ 100 MeV at temperature T ≈ 300 MeV, which is about 10⁶ times longer than the plasma lifetime
- The vorticity magnitude in RHIC collisions is typically on the order of several MeV (ω₀ ≈ 10 MeV)
- Strange quark current mass is in the range 100–120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of spin-vorticity coupling in heavy ion collisions, previously applied to Λ hyperon polarization measurements by the STAR collaboration (2017, 2018). It contradicts the common assumption of equipartition of energy between spin and vorticity (Becattini 2013, 2015) by showing that the dynamical equilibration mechanisms are too slow. The results enable future work to focus on alternative polarization mechanisms, such as hadronization-stage effects, and imply that vorticity fluctuations (Fabian 1998) do not affect hyperon spin either.
