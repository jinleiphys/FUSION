# Universal relation between dipole polarizability of finite nuclei and neutron-star compactness

> The nuclear equation of state, which determines the structure and properties of neutron stars, remains subject to substantial theoretical uncertainties, leading to model dependence in predicted observables. Universal relations have emerged as a powerful tool to mitigate this dependence by linking neutron star observables in a framework-independent manner. In this work, we introduce a new universal relation that \emph{bridges} finite nuclei and neutron stars through the dimensionless quantity $ζ= β_{1.4}\tilde{L}^{-1}$, which couples the compactness of a $1.4~M_{\odot}$ neutron star to the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation. The relation is examined under a broad set of relativistic energy density functionals with point-coupling and meson-exchange interactions, as well as non-relativistic Skyrme functionals. We demonstrate that $ζ$ exhibits a strong exponential correlation with the electric dipole polarizability $α_D$ in finite nuclei across all considered equations of state. By exploiting experimental $α_D$ data for selected neutron-rich nuclei, we constrain $ζ$ and translate these constraints into equation-of-state independent bounds on the neutron star radius $R_{1.4}$ and the symmetry-energy slope $L$, providing insights into the properties of neutron star matter.

# Universal relation between dipole polarizability of finite nuclei and neutron-star compactness

## Key claim

The authors introduce a new universal relation connecting the electric dipole polarizability of finite nuclei to the compactness of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star through the dimensionless quantity ζ = β₁.₄ L̃⁻¹, which they claim exhibits a strong exponential correlation across all considered equations of state. The hedge is that the relation is demonstrated only for the specific set of relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals employed, and the constraints derived from experimental α_D data depend on which subset of nuclei is used (CNSP-4 versus CNSP-10), with the latter introducing additional model dependence from reconstructed dipole strength above 20 MeV.

## Method

The authors computed electric dipole polarizabilities α_D for a set of neutron-rich nuclei using the quasiparticle random phase approximation within both relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functional frameworks, employing 12 density-dependent point-coupling, 7 density-dependent meson-exchange, 6 nonlinear meson-exchange, and 13 Skyrme functionals. They constructed the dimensionless quantity ζ = β₁.₄ L̃⁻¹, where β₁.₄ is the compactness of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star and L̃ = L/100 MeV, and fitted an empirical exponential relation ζ(α_D, A, δ) = c₁(A)e^{-c₂(A)α_D} + c₃(δ) to the calculated values across fourteen nuclei spanning A ∼ 48–208 and δ ∼ 0.11–0.33. Using experimental α_D data for ten neutron-rich nuclei, they constrained ζ and translated these constraints into bounds on the product R₁.₄L, then combined with the correlation R₁.₄ = 7.049(L/MeV)^{0.146} ± 0.581 km to extract separate limits on R₁.₄ and L.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless quantity ζ from the CNSP-4 subset (⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁸Ni, ¹²⁰Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb) is constrained to ζ = 0.390 ± 0.052
- The dimensionless quantity ζ from the CNSP-10 subset (CNSP-4 plus ¹¹²⁻¹²⁴Sn) is constrained to ζ = 0.435 ± 0.047
- The product R₁.₄L from CNSP-4 is 554.4 ± 73.4 km MeV
- The product R₁.₄L from CNSP-10 is 440.6 ± 47.9 km MeV
- The CNSP-4 constraints yield R₁.₄ between 12.057 km and 12.472 km, and L between 39.896 MeV and 50.335 MeV
- The CNSP-10 constraints yield R₁.₄ between 11.750 km and 12.080 km, and L between 33.418 MeV and 40.437 MeV
- The global fit achieves a coefficient of determination R² ≥ 0.9 across the training set of fourteen nuclei
- The EOSs employed cover incompressibility K ∼ 211–356 MeV, symmetry energy J ∼ 27–44 MeV, and symmetry energy slope L ∼ 19–140 MeV

## Context

This work extends the family of universal relations for neutron stars, following the I-Love-Q relations discovered by Yagi and Yunes (2013) and the binding energy-compactness relations of Lattimer and Prakash (2001). It specifically bridges finite-nucleus observables to neutron-star properties, complementing prior work connecting neutron skin thickness to the symmetry energy slope by Rocamaza et al. (2015) and Birkhan et al. (2017). The relation enables equation-of-state independent constraints on neutron star radii and the symmetry energy slope from terrestrial dipole polarizability measurements, and provides a predictive tool for α_D in nuclei such as ⁵²Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ¹³²Sn where experimental data are not yet available.
