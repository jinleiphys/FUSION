# Nucleon axial-vector form factor and radius from future neutrino experiments

> Precision measurements of antineutrino elastic scattering on hydrogen from future neutrino experiments offer a unique opportunity to access the low-energy structure of protons and neutrons. We discuss the determination of the nucleon axial-vector form factor and radius from antineutrino interactions on hydrogen that can be collected at the future Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility, and study the sources of theoretical and experimental uncertainties. The projected accuracy would improve existing measurements by $1$ order of magnitude and be competitive with contemporary lattice-QCD determinations, potentially helping to resolve the corresponding tension with measurements from (anti)neutrino elastic scattering on deuterium. We find that the current knowledge of the nucleon vector form factors could be one of the dominant sources of uncertainty. We also evaluate the constraints that can be simultaneously obtained on the absolute $\bar ν_μ$ flux normalization.

# Nucleon axial-vector form factor and radius from future neutrino experiments

## Key claim
The projected accuracy of antineutrino-hydrogen elastic scattering measurements at the future Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility (LBNF) would improve existing measurements of the nucleon axial-vector form factor by one order of magnitude, but the current knowledge of the nucleon vector form factors could become one of the dominant sources of systematic uncertainty, potentially limiting the extraction.

## Method
The authors generate cross sections for antineutrino-proton elastic scattering using the vector form factors from Borah (2020) and the axial-vector form factor from Meyer (2016), then compute predicted event rates by integrating over the LBNF antineutrino flux spectrum in 30 bins of momentum transfer Q^2 up to 1.5 GeV^2. They perform fits to the Q^2 distributions using a z-expansion of the axial-vector form factor with up to five free parameters, incorporating projected statistical and systematic uncertainties from the solid hydrogen target technique, and evaluate the impact of vector form factor uncertainties and radiative corrections.

## Key numbers
- Projected uncertainty on the nucleon axial-vector radius: 0.016 fm (systematics only, including vector form factor uncertainties) for an exposure of 5.5 x 10^21 protons on target
- Projected uncertainty on the nucleon axial-vector radius: 0.012 fm (statistics plus systematics, without vector form factor uncertainties)
- The measurement is statistics-dominated up to exposures of about 3 x 10^21 protons on target
- With normalization systematics less than or equal to 2%, the absolute antineutrino flux can be constrained below 1.9%
- The uncertainty on the axial-vector radius is roughly independent of the Q^2 range once the upper cutoff is above 0.75 GeV^2
- The projected accuracy of 0.016 fm compares with existing determinations: 0.160 fm from neutrino-deuterium (z expansion, Meyer 2016), 0.170 fm from antineutrino-hydrogen (MINERvA 2023), and lattice-QCD values ranging from 0.038 fm (ETMC, Alexandrou 2020) to 0.065 fm (RQCD 2019)

## Context
This work extends the program of precision antineutrino-hydrogen scattering using the solid hydrogen target technique proposed by Petti (2019, 2022) and Duyang (2018, 2019), applied to the DUNE near detector at LBNF. Existing measurements of the axial-vector form factor from bubble chamber experiments on deuterium (Mann 1973, Barish 1977, Miller 1982, Baker 1981, Kitagaki 1983) suffer from limited statistics and inconsistent systematic treatments, while a recent MINERvA measurement (2023) on plastic scintillator has too large uncertainty to resolve tensions between deuterium data and lattice-QCD determinations (Meyer 2022). The projected DUNE measurement could resolve this tension and enable combined fits with electron-proton and electron-deuteron scattering data to improve knowledge of both vector and axial-vector form factors, as well as test isospin symmetry relations.
