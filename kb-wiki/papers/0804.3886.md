# Nuclear fusion in muonic molecules and in deuterated metals

> Study of the fusion reactions between hydrogen isotopes in muonic molecules is the first example of the accurate accounting of the nucleus charge screening by a muon in the fusion process. At LUNA installation the measurements of astrophysical reaction cross sections were extended down to collision energies of a few keV. The screening by atomic electrons of the target became substantial. The possibility to look over screening from unbound electrons is given by metal-hydrides used as targets in dd reaction measurements. The classical Debye screening in plasma, applied to quasi-free electrons in metal, provides an explanation of unexpectedly large screening potentials found for some metals in the research through the Periodic table of elements.

# Nuclear fusion in muonic molecules and in deuterated metals

## Key claim
The classical Debye screening model applied to quasi-free electrons in metals provides a quantitative explanation for the unexpectedly large screening potentials (up to ~600 eV) observed in deuterated metal targets, which are an order of magnitude larger than the ~25 eV screening potential from atomic electrons in gas targets. However, the author notes that there is no theoretical justification for why the simple Debye model works so well, and it may be treated only as a powerful parametrization with predictive power.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental measurements of fusion cross sections for reactions such as d(d,p)t and d(d,n)³He using deuterated metal targets (Al, Zr, Ta, Pt, Co, and others) at beam energies from 5 to 60 keV, comparing the extracted screening potentials with those from gas-target experiments and from muon-catalyzed fusion in muonic molecules (pdμ, ddμ, dμ³He). The screening potential U_e is extracted by fitting the enhancement factor f(E) = σ_exp(E)/σ_bare(E) to the exponential form exp(πηU_e/E), and the results are compared with predictions from the Debye model using the effective number of valence electrons per atom and the Hall coefficient.

## Key numbers
- Screening potential for d(d,p)t in gas target: U_e = 25 ± 5 eV (Greife, 1995)
- Screening potential for d(d,p)t in deuterated Ta: U_e = 340 ± 24 eV (Raiola, 2002)
- Screening potential for d(d,p)t in deuterated Pd: U_e = 250 ± 15 eV (Yuki, 1998)
- Screening potential for d(d,p)t in deuterated Au/Pd/PdO multilayer: U_e = 601 ± 23 eV (Yuki, 1998)
- Screening potentials for d(d,p)t in Al: U_e = 190 ± 15 eV; Zr: U_e = 297 ± 8 eV; Ta: U_e = 322 ± 15 eV (Czerski, 2001)
- Screening potential for d(³He,p)⁴He in metals: U_e = 680 ± 60 eV, compared to Debye model prediction of 720 eV (Raiola, 2004)
- Ratio of neutron to proton fusion yields in ddμ molecule for p-wave fusion: R_p(n/p) = 1.42 ± 0.03; for s-wave fusion: R_s = 1.06 ± 0.04 (Petitjean, 1995)
- Extrapolated S(0) for p(d,γ)³He from LUNA: S(0) = 0.216 ± 0.010 eV·b (LUNA, 2002)
- Extrapolated S_0(0) for s-wave from TUNL: S_0(0) = 0.109 ± 0.01 eV·b (Rice, 1995)
- Extrapolated S_0(0) for s-wave from PSI muonic molecule data: S_0(0) = 0.105 ± 0.01 eV·b (Petitjean, 1995)

## Context
This review extends the work on electron screening in nuclear reactions from gas-target experiments (Greife, 1995) and LUNA measurements (Aliotta, 2001) to the systematic study of screening in deuterated metals across the Periodic table (Raiola, 2002; Bonomo, 2003; Raiola, 2004). The muon-catalyzed fusion results from PSI (Petitjean, 1995) and TUNL (Rice, 1995) provide verification of bare-nuclei S-functions at zero energy. The Debye screening model applied to quasi-free electrons in metals, first proposed by Bonomo (2003) and Raiola (2004), explains the large screening potentials but lacks theoretical foundation; alternative explanations involving deuteron fluidity (Kasagi, 2004) and quantum-tail effects (Starostin, 2004) are also discussed. The work enables future studies of low-energy nuclear reactions in various environments, with implications for understanding screening in stellar and terrestrial plasmas.
