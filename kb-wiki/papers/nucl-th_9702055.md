# Effects of Relativistic Dynamics in $pp \to pp π^0$ near Threshold

> The cross-section for threshold $π^0$ production in proton-proton collisions is evaluated in the framework of the covariant spectator description. The negative energy intermediate states are included non-perturbatively and seen to yield a considerably smaller contribution, when compared to perturbative treatments. A family of OBE-models with different off-shell scalar coupling is considered.

# Effects of Relativistic Dynamics in $pp \to pp \pi^0$ near Threshold

## Key claim

The covariant spectator calculation shows that negative-energy intermediate states ("Z-diagrams"), when included non-perturbatively, contribute significantly less (by a factor of more than 3) than their perturbative treatment would suggest, and the total covariant result underestimates the experimental threshold amplitude by a factor of 2-3 for all one-boson-exchange models considered.

## Method

The authors evaluate the threshold cross section for $pp \to pp \pi^0$ using the covariant spectator formalism, computing the Feynman diagrams for single-nucleon pion emission (impulse approximation) with both positive and negative energy intermediate states included non-perturbatively. They employ a family of one-boson-exchange (OBE) models with varying off-shell scalar coupling parameter $\nu$, and compare the covariant results to perturbative calculations using the same nucleon wave functions and meson parameters. The calculation is restricted to the dominant $^3P_0$ initial and $^1S_0$ final partial waves, with an approximate treatment of the final-state energy dependence via factorization of the half-off-shell $NN$ scattering matrix.

## Key numbers

- Fitted experimental threshold amplitude: $|{\cal M}_{exp}| = 30.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-3}$ (using modified phase space density)
- Covariant total amplitudes range from $-11.88 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-3}$ (for $\nu = 0$) to $-14.21 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-3}$ (for $\nu = 1.0$), all 2-3 times smaller than the experimental value
- Perturbative Z-diagram contribution: $-23.3 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-3}$ (for $\nu = 0$), more than 3 times larger than the covariant negative-energy sum ${\cal M}^- = -6.71 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-3}$ (for $\nu = 0$)
- Perturbative total amplitude: $-30.6 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-3}$ (for $\nu = 0$), which reproduces the experimental amplitude well (before accounting for Coulomb suppression of about 40%)
- The largest covariant amplitude (at $\nu = 1.0$) is about 20% larger than the smallest (at $\nu = 0$), translating to more than 40% variation in total cross section
- Off-shell nucleon form factors decrease the covariant results by up to 10%

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator description of $NN$ interactions (Gross, Van Orden, and Holinde, 1992) to threshold pion production, and directly contrasts with perturbative treatments (van Kolck, Miller, and Riska, 1996; Horowitz, 1993; Miller and Sauer, 1991) where Z-diagrams were found to dominate short-range reaction mechanisms. The finding that non-perturbative negative-energy contributions are substantially smaller than their perturbative counterparts parallels a similar effect reported by Zuilhof and Tjon (1979) for elastic electron scattering on deuteron, though at much higher momentum transfer. The results enable future inclusion of model-dependent mechanisms such as pion rescattering in a consistent relativistic framework, and suggest that the sensitivity to the off-shell scalar coupling parameter $\nu$ (which affects $NN$ observables and triton binding energy) is weaker when the full interaction is included than in Born approximation.
