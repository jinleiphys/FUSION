# Statistical Multifragmentation Model Within the Extended Morphological Thermodynamics Approach

> On the basis of morphological thermodynamics we develop an exactly solvable version of statistical mutifragmentation model for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. It is shown that the hard-core repulsion between spherical nuclei generates only the bulk (volume), surface and curvature parts of the free energy of the nucleus, while the Gaussian curvature one does not appear in the derivation. The phase diagram of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is studied for a truncated version of the developed model.

## Key claim

The paper develops an exactly solvable version of the statistical multifragmentation model using morphological thermodynamics, but the phase diagram is studied only for a truncated version of the model, and the true phase transition exists only in the limit of an infinite number of nuclear cluster sizes (N → ∞).

## Method

The authors start from the virial expansion of the grand canonical pressure for a multicomponent gas of Boltzmann particles with hard-core repulsion, where nuclear clusters of k nucleons have hard-core radii R_k = R_1 k^(1/3) with R_1 ≈ 0.72 fm. By evaluating the second virial coefficients and regrouping terms with the same powers of R_k, they derive a system of equations for the pressure p, induced surface tension Σ, and induced curvature tension K. For the truncated version, they simplify the system by absorbing curvature effects into the surface tension term with a parameter α = 1.5, and solve the coupled equations for the phase transition curve assuming p = p_L (liquid pressure).

## Key numbers

- Hard-core radius of a single nucleon: R_1 ≈ 0.72 fm
- Normal nuclear density: ρ_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^(-3)
- Bulk binding energy per nucleon at T=0: W_0 = 16 MeV
- Fermi motion parameter: ε_0 = 16 MeV
- Proper surface tension coefficients: σ_01 = 18 MeV, σ_02 = 24.76 MeV
- Critical temperature: T_cep = 18 MeV
- Fisher exponent: τ = 1.9
- Parameter α = 1.5 (for the truncated version)
- Parameter a_2 = 1.261 × 10^(-2) MeV^(-1) with ν = 2
- Nucleon degeneracy factor: z_1 = 4
- Expected critical density: ρ_c ≈ ρ_0/3

## Context

This work extends the induced surface and curvature tensions (ISCT) equation of state, previously developed for classical hard spheres and hard discs by Kucherenko and collaborators (Refs. [Kucherenko:Ref4–Kucherenko:Ref8]), to the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of nuclear matter. The approach builds on the Fisher droplet model (Kucherenko:Ref11) and earlier solvable versions of SMM (Kucherenko:Ref9, Kucherenko:Ref10), but now incorporates curvature tension effects that are necessary for the existence of a (tri)critical point. The model enables future study of the total surface tension coefficient at supercritical temperatures and the location of the nuclear matter critical endpoint.
