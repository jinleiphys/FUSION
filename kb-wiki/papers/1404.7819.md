# Proposed method for laser spectroscopy of pionic helium atoms to determine the charged-pion mass

> Metastable pionic helium ($π{\rm He}^+$) is a three-body atom composed of a helium nucleus, an electron occupying the $1s$ ground state, and a negatively charged pion $π^-$ in a Rydberg state with principal- and orbital angular momentum quantum numbers of $n\sim \ell+1\sim 16$. We calculate the spin-independent energies of the $π{\rm ^3He}^+$ and $π{\rm ^4He}^+$ isotopes in the region $n=15$--19. These include relativistic and quantum electrodynamics corrections of orders $R_{\infty}α^2$ and $R_{\infty}α^3$ in atomic units, where $R_{\infty}$ and $α$ denote the Rydberg and fine structure constants. The fine-structure splitting due to the coupling between the electron spin and the orbital angular momentum of the $π^-$, and the radiative and Auger decay rates of the states are also calculated. Some states $(n,\ell)=(16,15)$ and $(17,16)$ retain nanosecond-scale lifetimes against $π^-$ absorption into the helium nucleus. We propose to use laser pulses to induce $π^-$ transitions from these metastable states, to states with large ($\sim 10^{11}$ s$^{-1}$) Auger rates. The $π{\rm He}^{2+}$ ion that remains after Auger emission of the $1s$ electron undergoes Stark mixing with the $s$, $p$, and $d$ states during collisions with the helium atoms in the experimental target. This leads to immediate nuclear absorption of the $π^-$. The resonance condition between the laser beam and the atom is thus revealed as a sharp spike in the rates of neutrons, protons, deuterons, and tritons that emerge....(continued)

## Key claim

The paper proposes that laser spectroscopy of metastable pionic helium atoms can determine the charged-pion mass with a fractional precision of $10^{-8}-10^{-6}$, provided that systematic uncertainties can be controlled. The authors hedge that the experiment is difficult because laser excitation of a meson has never been observed, the metastability lifetime of $\sim 7$ ns is shorter than any exotic atom studied by laser spectroscopy, and the probability for inducing a $\pi^-$ transition is small.

## Method

The authors calculated spin-independent energies of $\pi{\rm ^3He}^+$ and $\pi{\rm ^4He}^+$ isotopes in the $n=15$--19 region using the complex-coordinate rotation method with variational basis sets of 2000 functions, including relativistic and quantum electrodynamics corrections of orders $R_{\infty}\alpha^2$ and $R_{\infty}\alpha^3$. They computed fine-structure splitting from the electron spin-orbital angular momentum coupling, radiative and Auger decay rates, and simulated the population evolutions of metastable states using a cascade model. The proposed experiment uses laser pulses to induce $\pi^-$ transitions from metastable states to states with large Auger rates, where the resulting $\pi{\rm He}^{2+}$ ion undergoes Stark mixing during collisions leading to nuclear absorption detected as neutrons, protons, deuterons, and tritons.

## Key numbers

- The charged-pion mass used in calculations: $M_{\pi^-}=139.57018(35)~{\rm MeV}/c^2$
- Metastable states $(16,15)$ and $(17,16)$ retain nanosecond-scale lifetimes against $\pi^-$ absorption; the state $(16,15)$ has a 60-ns lifetime against Auger decay
- Auger rates: $A_{(15,14)}=2\times 10^{12}~{\rm s}^{-1}$ and $A_{(16,15)}=4\times 10^9~{\rm s}^{-1}$ from Russell (1969); the authors' calculated Auger rates are 10--100 times smaller
- Radiative decay rates range from $\sim 3\times 10^6~{\rm s}^{-1}$ for $(19,18)\rightarrow(18,17)$ to $\sim 8\times 10^6~{\rm s}^{-1}$ for $(16,15)\rightarrow(15,14)$
- The $\pi^-\rightarrow\mu^-+\overline{\nu}_{\mu}$ decay rate: $\gamma_{\pi}=3.8\times 10^7~{\rm s}^{-1}$
- Fine structure splitting increases from $\Delta\nu_{\rm fs}=16.6$ to 32.6 GHz for $\pi{\rm ^4He}^+$ states in the $n=16$--18 region
- $(2.30\pm 0.07)\%$ of $\pi^-$ stopped in liquid helium retain a lifetime of $7.3\pm 0.1$ ns (Nakamura 1992)
- Laser repetition rates: $f_{\rm las}=0.1$--1 kHz with average powers 1--100 W
- $\pi^-$ beam intensity: $>10^8~{\rm s}^{-1}$ with momenta $\ge 80$ MeV/c and momentum spread 5--10%
- Neutrons, protons, deuterons, and tritons emerge with kinetic energies up to 30--90 MeV

## Context

This work extends the methodology developed for antiprotonic helium spectroscopy, where transition frequencies were measured to fractional precision $(2.3-5)\times 10^{-9}$ and the antiproton-to-electron mass ratio was determined as $M_{\overline{p}}/m_e=1836.1526736(23)$ (Hori 2011). The paper addresses the long-standing puzzle of anomalously long-lived $\pi^-$ in helium first observed in bubble chamber experiments (Fetkovich 1963, Block 1963) and later measured directly at TRIUMF (Nakamura 1992). The proposed method enables the first laser spectroscopy of a meson-containing atom and could determine the charged-pion mass with precision comparable to or better than existing measurements.
