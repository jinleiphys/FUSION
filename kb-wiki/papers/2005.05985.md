# From odd-even staggering to the pairing gap in neutron matter

> The properties of neutron matter are integral to the correct description of neutron stars as well as the description of neutron-rich nuclei. One key property of neutron matter is its superfluid behaviour in a range of densities relevant to the inner crust of neutron stars. This work investigates the finite-size effects in the pairing gap of a pure neutron matter superfluid system at densities found in the inner crust of cold neutron stars. The BCS (Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer) treatment of superfluidity gives rise to the mean-field pairing gap while a projection after variation (PBCS Theory) can provide a beyond-mean-field pairing gap through an odd-even staggering formula. While these two pairing gap results should agree in the thermodynamic limit, in this work we demonstrate that this is the case for systems far from the thermodynamic limit as well. This is a first step towards a model-independent extraction of the pairing gap in neutron matter.

# From odd-even staggering to the pairing gap in neutron matter

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the mean-field BCS pairing gap and the beyond-mean-field pairing gap extracted from odd-even staggering (OES) agree with each other not only in the thermodynamic limit but also for finite systems far from that limit, provided the OES gap is computed using a projection-after-variation (PBCS) approach. This agreement holds specifically for pure neutron matter at densities relevant to the inner crust of cold neutron stars, using a simplified pairing Hamiltonian that ignores normal-state interactions.

## Method
The authors solved the BCS gap equations self-consistently for finite systems of neutrons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using an S-wave pairing interaction tuned to reproduce the neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range. They computed the mean-field pairing gap as the minimum of the quasiparticle excitation energy for both even and odd particle-number systems (the latter via blocked states). For the beyond-mean-field treatment, they employed a projection-after-variation (PBCS) approach to restore particle-number symmetry, extracting the pairing gap from the odd-even staggering formula using the energies of systems with even and odd particle numbers.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a ≈ -18.5 fm
- Neutron-neutron effective range: r_e ≈ 2.7 fm
- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The pairing gap was studied at interaction strengths corresponding to k_F a = -10 and k_F a = -5

## Context
This work extends previous BCS calculations of the ¹S₀ pairing gap in neutron matter (Dean 2003, Gezerlis 2008) and connects to beyond-mean-field studies using correlated basis functions (Pavlou 2017) and Quantum Monte Carlo techniques (Gezerlis 2010, Carlson 2003, Carlson 2005). The results provide a bridge between ab initio approaches that extract pairing gaps from odd-even staggering in finite systems and mean-field calculations, enabling a more model-independent extraction of the pairing gap in neutron matter. This is relevant for understanding neutron star cooling (Yakovlev 2004, Page 2004, Page 2011) and pulsar glitches (Baym 1969, Watanabe 2017).
