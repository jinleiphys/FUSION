# Scaling Behavior of Transverse Kinetic Energy Distributions in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV

> With the experimental data from STAR on the centrality dependence of transverse momentum $p_T$ spectra of pions and protons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 {\rm GeV}$, we investigate the scaling properties of transverse energy $E_T$ distributions at different centralities. In the framework of cluster formation and decay mechanism for particle production, the universal transverse energy distributions for pion and proton can be described separately but not simultaneously.

## Key claim
The transverse kinetic energy ($E_T$) distributions for pions and protons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV each exhibit a universal scaling behavior separately, but the cluster formation and decay mechanism cannot describe both particle species simultaneously with the same cluster distribution parameters.

## Method
The authors transformed STAR experimental data on transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra into transverse kinetic energy ($E_T$) distributions using the relation $E_T = \sqrt{p_T^2 + m_0^2} - m_0$. They applied a scaling procedure with free parameters $A$ and $K$ to collapse data from different centrality bins onto a single curve, then normalized the scaling function to unit mean. They modeled the scaling functions using a cluster decay framework where a Gamma distribution describes cluster sizes and a fragmentation function describes hadron production from clusters.

## Key numbers
- For protons, the normalized scaling function for $E_T$ is $\Psi(u) = 1.4846 \exp(2.394v - 5.79v^2 + 0.977v^3)$ with $v = \ln(1+u)$ and $u = E_T/\langle E_T \rangle$.
- For pions, the normalized scaling function for $p_T$ is $\Psi(u) = 0.288 (1 + u^2/7.868)^{-4.3} (1 + 13.6 e^{-1.9u})$ with $u = p_T/\langle p_T \rangle$.
- The scaling parameter $K$ (mean $E_T$ or mean $p_T$) for pions increases from 0.4192 (0-12% centrality) to 0.4571 (60-80% centrality), while for protons it decreases from 0.5786 (0-12%) to 0.5437 (60-80%).
- In the cluster decay fit, the parameter $k$ (shape of cluster size distribution) is fixed to 4.3164 for both pions and protons; the fragmentation parameter $a$ is -1.0842 for pions and 0.1890 for protons.

## Context
This work extends earlier scaling studies by the same group (Zhu et al., 2007; Zheng et al., 2006) that found scaling behavior in $p_T$ spectra for pions and protons separately. The authors show that $E_T$ scaling describes experimental data better than $p_T$ scaling, particularly at low and high transverse momenta. The paper tests the cluster formation and decay mechanism based on parton percolation theory (Braun et al., 2003; Pajares et al., 2004) and finds it cannot simultaneously describe pion and proton spectra with the same cluster distribution, ruling it out as a universal production mechanism and motivating the search for alternative explanations.
