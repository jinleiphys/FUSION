# Transverse wobbling motion in $^{134}$Ce and $^{136}$Nd

> The existence of one-phonon and possible two-phonon transverse wobbling bands is proposed for the first time in two even-even nuclei, $^{134}$Ce and $^{136}$Nd. The predominant $E2$ character of the $ΔI = 1$ transitions connecting the one-phonon wobbling band in $^{134}$Ce to the two-quasiparticle yrast band supports the wobbling interpretation. The extracted wobbling frequencies decrease with increasing spin, indicating the transverse character of the wobbling motion, with the angular momenta of the two quasiparticles aligned perpendicular to the axis of collective rotation. A candidate for two-phonon wobbling motion is also proposed in $^{136}$Nd. The wobbling frequencies calculated in the harmonic frozen approximation are in good agreement with the experimental ones for both the$^{134}$Ce and $^{136}$Nd nuclei.

## Key claim

One-phonon and possible two-phonon transverse wobbling bands are proposed for the first time in two even-even nuclei, $^{134}$Ce and $^{136}$Nd, based on the decrease of wobbling frequencies with increasing spin and the predominant $E2$ character of $\Delta I = 1$ transitions in $^{134}$Ce; however, the wobbling interpretation for several bands in $^{136}$Nd remains uncertain pending future measurements of the electromagnetic character of the connecting transitions.

## Method

The authors analyzed previously reported and newly identified $\gamma$-ray transitions in $^{134}$Ce and $^{136}$Nd from experimental level schemes, extracting wobbling frequencies as energy differences between one-phonon ($n_w=1$) bands and interpolated $n_w=0$ band energies. They compared these experimental wobbling frequencies to calculations using the Harmonic Frozen Approximation (HFA), with moments of inertia adopted from RPA calculations for $^{138}$Nd and deformations from the Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model. The predominant $E2$ character of $\Delta I=1$ transitions in $^{134}$Ce was determined from mixing ratios extracted via angular distribution measurements.

## Key numbers

* In $^{134}$Ce, the mixing ratios of the 664-keV and 741-keV $\Delta I=1$ transitions are 2.13(48) and 1.73(52), respectively, indicating 82(5)% and 75(6)% $E2$ components.
* For the $\nu h_{11/2}^{-2}$ configuration in $^{134}$Ce, the CNS deformation changes from $(\varepsilon_2 \sim 0.14, \gamma \sim -35^{\circ})$ at spin $I=12$ to $(\varepsilon_2 \sim 0.12, \gamma \sim -49^{\circ})$ at spin $I=22$.
* For the $\pi h_{11/2}^{2}$ configuration in $^{136}$Nd, the CNS deformation changes from $(\varepsilon_2 \sim 0.21, \gamma \sim 25^{\circ})$ at spin $I=12$ to $(\varepsilon_2 \sim 0.19, \gamma \sim 25^{\circ})$ at spin $I=22$.
* The HFA calculations used moments of inertia (in $\hbar^2$/MeV) of $\mathcal{J}_1=16$, $\mathcal{J}_2=32$, $\mathcal{J}_3=8$ for the $\nu h_{11/2}^{-2}$ configuration and $\mathcal{J}_1=12$, $\mathcal{J}_2=48$, $\mathcal{J}_3=24$ for the $\pi h_{11/2}^{2}$ configuration.

## Context

This work extends the transverse wobbling mode previously discovered in the odd-even nucleus $^{135}$Pr (Frauendorf 2014) and in $^{133}$Ce (Petrache 2016) to even-even nuclei with two quasiparticles, building on earlier wobbling interpretations in $^{138}$Nd (Mukhopadhyay 2007). The results enable future experiments to measure the electromagnetic character of $\Delta I=1$ transitions in $^{136}$Nd and more detailed RPA calculations on two-quasiparticle configurations to confirm the transverse wobbling interpretation.
