# QUANTUM FIELD THEORY OF FERMION MIXING

> The fermion mixing transformations are studied in the quantum field theory framework. In particular neutrino mixing is considered and the Fock space of definite flavor states is shown to be unitarily inequivalent to the Fock space of definite mass states. The flavor oscillation formula is computed for two and three flavors mixing and the oscillation amplitude is found to be momentum dependent, a result which may be subject to experimental test. The flavor vacuum state exhibits the structure of $SU(2)$ generalized coherent state.

## Key claim

The flavor Fock space is unitarily inequivalent to the mass Fock space in the infinite volume limit, meaning the flavor vacuum is not the same as the mass vacuum; the oscillation amplitude computed from this QFT framework is momentum dependent, a result that may be subject to experimental test.

## Method

The authors study the Pontecorvo mixing relations for Dirac neutrino fields in the LSZ formalism of quantum field theory. They construct the generator of the mixing transformations as an SU(2) operator and compute the flavor vacuum state as a generalized coherent state. The flavor oscillation formula is derived by evaluating the expectation value of the flavor number operator in the time-evolved one-particle flavor state, using the dynamical map that expresses flavor annihilation operators in terms of mass operators.

## Key numbers

- The condensation density of the flavor vacuum is given by \(_{e,\mu}\langle0|N^{k}_{\sigma_i}|0\rangle_{e,\mu} = Z_k \sin^2\theta\) for \(\sigma=\alpha,\beta\) and \(i=1,2\), where \(Z_k = \frac{k^2[(\omega_{k,2}+m_2)-(\omega_{k,1}+m_1)]^2}{2\omega_{k,1}\omega_{k,2}(\omega_{k,1}+m_1)(\omega_{k,2}+m_2)}\).
- The overlap between mass and flavor vacua in the infinite volume limit is \(\lim_{V\rightarrow\infty} {}_{1,2}\langle0|0\rangle_{e,\mu} = 0\).
- The coefficients satisfy \(|U_k|^2 + |V_k|^2 = 1\) and \(|V_k|^2 = \frac{1}{2}Z_k\), with \(|U_k|\) and \(|V_k|\) defined in terms of \(\omega_{k,i} = \sqrt{k^2 + m_i^2}\).
- The conventional oscillation probability for two flavors is \(1 - \sin^2 2\theta \sin^2(\Delta\omega t/2)\), where \(\Delta\omega = \omega_2 - \omega_1\).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of neutrino mixing, which identifies the flavor vacuum with the mass vacuum (Pontecorvo, 1968), by showing that in QFT this identification fails due to unitary inequivalence. The result that the oscillation amplitude becomes momentum dependent is a novel feature compared to conventional analysis. This enables future experimental tests of the momentum dependence and further theoretical study of the coherent state structure of the flavor vacuum, including its relation to entropy and Bogoliubov transformations.
