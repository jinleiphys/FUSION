# Null-test signal for T-invariance violation in $pd$ scattering

> The integrated proton-deuteron cross section $\widetildeσ$ for the case of the incident proton vector polarization $p_y^p$ and tensor polarization $P_{xz}$ of the deuteron target provides a null test signal for time-reversal invariance violating but P-parity conserving (TVPC) effects. We study the null-test observable $\widetildeσ$ within the Glauber theory of the double-polarized $pd$ scattering. Full spin dependence of the ordinary strong $pN$ scattering amplitudes and different types of the hypothetical TVPC pN-amplitudes are taken into account. We show that the contribution from the exchange of the lowest-mass meson allowed in the TVPC interaction, i.e. the $ρ$-meson, to the null-test observable $\widetildeσ$ is zero. The axial $h_1$ meson exchange makes a non-zero contribution. We find that inclusion of the Coulomb interaction does not lead to divergence of the cross section $\widetildeσ$ and calculate its energy dependence at the proton beam energy 100-1000 MeV.

## Key claim

The integrated cross section $\widetilde\sigma$ in double-polarized $pd$ scattering provides a null-test signal for time-reversal invariance violating but P-parity conserving (TVPC) effects; however, the contribution from the lowest-mass meson allowed in TVPC interaction (the $\rho$-meson) to this observable is zero, while the axial $h_1$ meson exchange makes a non-zero contribution, and inclusion of Coulomb interaction does not lead to divergence of the cross section.

## Method

The authors applied the Glauber theory of double-polarized $pd$ scattering, using the generalized optical theorem to relate the forward elastic scattering amplitude to total spin-dependent cross sections. They constructed the Glauber scattering operator including full spin dependence of ordinary strong $pN$ scattering amplitudes and hypothetical TVPC $pN$ amplitudes, considering both single and double scattering mechanisms with S- and D-components of the deuteron wave function. The TVPC amplitudes were modeled via meson exchange interactions, specifically the $\rho$-meson (corresponding to the $g^\prime$ term) and the axial $h_1$ meson (corresponding to the $h$ term), with Coulomb interaction added through the antisymmetric Coulomb amplitude of $pp$ scattering.

## Key numbers

- The experiment at COSY will be performed at a beam energy of 135 MeV, aiming to diminish the upper bound on TVPC effects previously obtained in ${\vec n}^{167}$Ho scattering by one order of magnitude.
- The existing constraint from polarized neutron transmission through polarized $^{165}$Ho target gives $\alpha_T \leq 7.1 \times 10^{-4}$ or $\bar g_\rho \leq 5.9\times 10^{-2}$.
- Charge symmetry breaking measurements give $\alpha_T \leq 8 \times 10^{-5}$ (or $\bar g_\rho < 6.7\times 10^{-3}$).
- Indirect model-dependent estimation based on EDM constraints gives $\alpha_T \leq 1.1 \times 10^{-5}$ ($\bar g_\rho \leq 10^{-3}$).
- The energy dependence of $\widetilde\sigma$ is calculated for the proton beam energy range 100-1000 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of the null-test observable $\widetilde\sigma$ previously performed by Beyer (2015) in the impulse approximation, by using the Glauber theory with full spin dependence and including both single and double scattering mechanisms. The formalism builds on the spin-dependent Glauber approach developed in earlier work (Uzikov and Temerbayev, 2014) that successfully described spin observables of elastic $pd$ scattering at 135 MeV. The results enable the TRIC experiment at COSY to interpret a potential non-zero signal as direct evidence of TVPC interactions beyond the Standard Model, while showing that the $\rho$-meson exchange contribution vanishes and only the axial $h_1$ meson exchange contributes to the null-test signal.
