# Effects of Screening on Quark-Antiquark Cross Sections in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Lowest-order cross sections for $q\bar q$ production and annihilation can be approximately corrected for higher-order QCD effects by using a corrective $K$-factor. For energies where quark masses cannot be ignored, the $K$-factor is dominated by the wave function distortion arising from the initial- or final-state interaction between the quark and the antiquark. We evaluate this $K$-factor for $q \bar q$ production and annihilation in a quark-gluon plasma by taking into account the effects of Debye screening through a color-Yukawa potential. We present the corrective $K$-factor as a function of dimensionless parameters which may find applications in other systems involving attractive or repulsive Yukawa interactions. Prominent peaks of the $K$-factor occur for an attractive $q$-$\bar q$ color-Yukawa interaction with Debye screening lengths of 0.835 and 3.23 times the Bohr radius, corresponding to two lowest $s$-wave $q\bar q$ bound states moving into the continuum to become $q\bar q$ resonances as the Debye screening length decreases. These resonances, especially the $c\bar c$ and the $b \bar b$ resonances, may be utilized to search for the quark-gluon plasma by studying the systematics of the temperature dependence of heavy-quark pair production just above the threshold.

## Key claim

The corrective $K$-factor for quark-antiquark production and annihilation in a quark-gluon plasma, evaluated using a color-Yukawa potential with Debye screening, exhibits prominent peaks at specific screening lengths (0.835 and 3.23 times the Bohr radius) corresponding to the emergence of bound states into the continuum as resonances; these screening resonances, particularly for charm and bottom quarks, may provide a temperature-dependent signal for the presence of a quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation for the relative motion of a quark and antiquark interacting via a screened color-Yukawa potential, using the phase-amplitude method of Calogero to compute the s-wave wave function distortion. The corrective K-factor was obtained from the square of the wave function at contact, expressed as a function of two dimensionless parameters: the Coulomb parameter xi (effective coupling divided by relative velocity) and the screening length parameter eta (Debye screening length divided by Bohr radius). For charm and strange quark systems, relativistic kinematics were incorporated by using the center-of-mass energy to define the effective momentum and energy parameters.

## Key numbers

- The K-factor peaks occur at Debye screening lengths of 0.835 and 3.23 times the Bohr radius, corresponding to the two lowest s-wave bound states emerging into the continuum.
- For a perturbative QCD estimate with alpha_s = 0.3 and N_f = 3, the Debye screening length is approximately 0.4 fm at a temperature of 200 MeV and 0.2 fm at 400 MeV.
- The charm quark mass used is 1.5 GeV and the strange quark mass is 0.15 GeV.
- For charm quarks with lambda_D = 0.4 fm, the effective coupling is approximately alpha_eff = 0.4, giving a Bohr radius of about 0.5 fm and eta approximately 0.8 (near the peak at 0.835).
- For strange quarks with lambda_D = 0.4 fm, eta is approximately 0.08, and with lambda_D = 0.2 fm, eta is approximately 0.04.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier treatment of the K-factor for unscreened color-Coulomb interactions (Wong and Chatterjee, 1995) by incorporating Debye screening appropriate for a quark-gluon plasma. The screening resonances identified here are analogous to the s-wave resonances observed in low-energy neutron scattering as a function of nuclear mass number (Bohr and Mottelson, 1967) and to electron scattering from atoms (Morse and collaborators, 1932). The results enable systematic study of heavy-quark pair production near threshold as a function of temperature, potentially providing a signature for deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions.
