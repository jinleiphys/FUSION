# Study of the heavy quarks energy loss through medium polarization, elastic collision and radiative processes

> Heavy quarks serve as crucial probes for exploring the properties of the hot and dense medium formed in heavy-ion collision experiments. Understanding the modification of their energy as they traverse the medium is a focal point of research, with various authors extensively studying this phenomenon. This study specifically concentrates on the equilibrium phase, the quark-gluon plasma, and offers a comparative analysis of heavy quark energy loss through medium polarization, elastic collisions, and radiation. Notably, while previous studies have compared polarization loss and radiation, our work extends this by incorporating elastic collisions for a more comprehensive examination. The significance of medium polarization, particularly at low momentum, is underscored, as it has been found to contribute substantially. The formalism for energy loss and drag coefficient in each case is presented, followed by the calculation of the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) for a holistic comparative study.

## Key claim

This study provides a comparative analysis of heavy quark energy loss through medium polarization, elastic collisions, and radiation in quark-gluon plasma, extending prior work that compared only polarization and radiation by incorporating elastic collisions for a more comprehensive examination. The authors find that medium polarization contributes substantially at low momentum, but the relative dominance of each mechanism depends strongly on heavy quark flavor, momentum, and medium temperature.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark energy loss from three processes: medium polarization using the Wong equations and induced chromoelectric field from the Boltzmann transport equation; elastic collisions using the Fokker-Planck equation derived from the Boltzmann transport equation with 2→2 scattering matrix elements; and inelastic collisions (radiation) using 2→3 scattering amplitudes with the dead-cone factor and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression. The drag coefficient is obtained from the energy loss, and the nuclear modification factor R_AA is calculated using Langevin dynamics with the drag and diffusion coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to 1.5 GeV and bottom quark mass set to 4.5 GeV
- Energy loss and drag coefficient computed at T = 360 MeV (RHIC initial temperature) and T = 480 MeV (LHC initial temperature)
- For charm quarks at T = 360 MeV, radiation surpasses elastic collision at momentum p ~ 7 GeV, giving the highest contribution
- For bottom quarks at T = 360 MeV, elastic collision dominates throughout, even at p ~ 18 GeV
- R_AA evolution time set to τ_f = 7 fm/c
- Debye screening mass m_D defined with running coupling, where T_c = 0.155 GeV and Λ_MS = T_c/0.77

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comparison of polarization loss with inelastic collision (radiation) (Prakash 2023) by incorporating elastic collisions for completeness. The formalism for elastic collisions follows Svetitsky (1987) treating heavy quark evolution as Brownian motion, while the polarization loss formalism builds on earlier work by Carrington (2015, 2016), Jamal (2020), and YousufJamal (2019). The study enables a more complete understanding of which energy loss mechanisms dominate for different heavy quark flavors and momentum regimes in quark-gluon plasma, with implications for interpreting heavy-ion collision data from RHIC and LHC.
