# Time-dependent relativistic mean-field theory

> The relativistic mean-field theory provides a framework in which the nuclear many-body problem is described as a self-consistent system of nucleons and mesons. In the mean-field approximation, the self-consistent time evolution of the nuclear system describes the dynamics of collective motion: double giant resonances, nuclear compressibility from monopole resonances, regular and chaotic dynamics of isoscalar and isovector collective vibrations.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, when extended to time-dependent initial-value problems, can describe the dynamics of collective nuclear vibrations including double giant resonances, monopole resonances for nuclear compressibility, and the transition from regular to chaotic dynamics, but only for isoscalar modes, while isovector modes exhibit chaotic behavior due to out-of-phase proton-neutron motion.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dirac equation for A nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields (σ, ω, ρ) and the photon, using the no-sea approximation for densities and currents. Collective vibrations are excited by imposing initial deformations or velocity fields on the ground-state Slater determinant, then evolving the system in time and extracting eigenmode frequencies via Fourier analysis of dynamical quantities such as the quadrupole or monopole moment. For double giant resonances, a semi-classical quantization condition is applied by requiring the mean-field action along periodic orbits to equal integer multiples of Planck's constant.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) in ⁴⁰Ca: single-phonon state at 18.5 MeV, two-phonon state at 37 MeV (from Fourier spectrum and action quantization with I=1h and I=2h, respectively), compared to experimental single GQR at 17.5 MeV and two-phonon structure at 34±2 MeV.
- Double giant dipole resonance (GDR) in ²⁰⁸Pb: single GDR at 12.9 MeV (I=1h), double GDR at 25.9 MeV (I=2h), compared to experimental single GDR at 13.3±0.1 MeV and double GDR at 25.6±0.9 MeV.
- Isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) in ²⁰⁸Pb: calculated at 14.1 MeV with NL3 parameter set (K_nm = 271.8 MeV), compared to experimental value 13.7±0.3 MeV; NL1 parameter set (K_nm = 211.7 MeV) gives approximately 1 MeV lower.
- Nuclear matter compression modulus K_nm ≈ 250–270 MeV from RMF calculations, approximately 20% larger than non-relativistic Hartree-Fock values (210–220 MeV).
- Isoscalar GMR energies follow empirical curve E_x ≈ 80 A^{-1/3} MeV for NL3 parameter set.
- Isovector monopole mode in ²⁰⁸Pb: main Fourier peaks in energy region 25–30 MeV, compared to experimental 26±3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field framework (Rin.96) to time-dependent dynamics, building on earlier stationary RMF calculations of nuclear structure. The double giant resonance results confirm and extend the experimental findings from heavy-ion inelastic scattering and relativistic Coulomb excitation. The monopole resonance analysis addresses the long-standing discrepancy between non-relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA calculations (Skyrme and Gogny forces, giving K_nm ≈ 210–220 MeV) and relativistic calculations (K_nm ≈ 250–270 MeV), using six parameter sets including the new NL3 interaction (LKR.97). The study of regular versus chaotic dynamics shows that isoscalar collective modes can remain regular even when single-particle motion is chaotic (confirming earlier studies), but isovector modes become chaotic because protons and neutrons move in out-of-phase self-consistent potentials. This enables future work on dissipation mechanisms in collective nuclear motion and more precise determination of nuclear matter incompressibility.
