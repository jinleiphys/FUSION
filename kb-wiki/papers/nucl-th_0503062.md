# Semi-relativistic description of quasielastic neutrino reactions and superscaling in a continuum shell model

> The so-called semi-relativistic expansion of the weak charged current in powers of the initial nucleon momentum is performed to describe charge-changing, quasielastic neutrino reactions $(ν_μ,μ^-)$ at intermediate energies. The quality of the expansion is tested by comparing with the relativistic Fermi gas model using several choices of kinematics of interest for ongoing neutrino oscillation experiments. The new current is then implemented in a continuum shell model together with relativistic kinematics to investigate the scaling properties of $(e,e')$ and $(ν_μ,μ^-)$ cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper develops a semi-relativistic (SR) expansion of the weak charged current that treats the dependence on momentum transfer exactly while expanding only in the initial nucleon momentum, and shows that this approximation reproduces the fully relativistic Fermi gas results for quasielastic neutrino reactions at intermediate energies, provided relativistic kinematics are also implemented.

## Method

The authors perform a first-order expansion of the relativistic charged-current operator in powers of the initial nucleon momentum, keeping exact dependence on the momentum and energy transfer. They implement this SR current together with relativistic kinematics (via a substitution in the energy relation) in a continuum shell model using Woods-Saxon potentials for closed-shell nuclei (12C, 16O, 40Ca). The quality of the expansion is tested by comparing the resulting response functions with those from the exact relativistic Fermi gas model, and the scaling properties of both electron scattering and neutrino scattering cross sections are investigated within the distorted-wave impulse approximation.

## Context

This work extends the semi-relativistic expansion previously developed for electromagnetic and weak neutral currents (Amaro et al., 1996) to charged-current neutrino reactions for the first time. It builds on prior studies of relativistic nuclear dynamics in neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering (Alberico et al., 1997-1999; Meucci et al., 2004) and on scaling analyses of electron scattering data (Donnelly et al., 1988-2004). The approach enables the use of existing non-relativistic nuclear models for neutrino reaction calculations at GeV energies, and allows testing of the scaling hypothesis used in earlier work (Amaro et al., 2005) to predict neutrino cross sections from electron scattering data.
