# Relativistic Effects in Electromagnetic Meson-Exchange Currents for One-Particle Emission Reactions

> Following recent studies of non-relativistic reductions of the single-nucleon electromagnetic current operator, here we extend the treatment to include meson exchange current operators. We focus on one-particle emission electronuclear reactions. In contrast to the traditional scheme where approximations are made for the transferred momentum, transferred energy and momenta of the initial-state struck nucleons, we treat the problem exactly for the transferred energy and momentum, thus obtaining new current operators which retain important aspects of relativity not taken into account in the traditional non-relativistic reductions. We calculate the matrix elements of our current operators between the Fermi sphere and a particle-hole state for several choices of kinematics. We present a comparison between our results using approximate current operators and those obtained using the fully-relativistic operators, as well as with results obtained using the traditional non-relativistic current operators.

## Key claim

The authors derive new non-relativistic meson-exchange current (MEC) operators for one-particle emission reactions that are expanded only in the small initial-state nucleon momenta divided by the nucleon mass (η = p/m_N), while treating the transferred momentum (κ) and transferred energy (λ) exactly, thus retaining relativistic effects that are lost in traditional non-relativistic reductions where κ and λ are also assumed small.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic seagull and pion-in-flight MEC operators and perform a non-relativistic reduction by expanding to first order in the small parameters η_i = p_i/m_N (the initial-state nucleon momenta), while keeping the transferred momentum κ = q/2m_N and transferred energy λ = ω/2m_N unexpanded. They then compute the matrix elements of these new operators between the Fermi sphere and a 1p-1h state and compare them with matrix elements from the fully-relativistic operators and from traditional non-relativistic operators that also expand in κ and λ.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter η ≡ p/m_N is characteristically of order 1/4 for nucleons in typical initial-state nuclear wave functions, with the Fermi momentum giving η_F ≈ 0.29 for heavy nuclei.
- The seagull current time component acquires a multiplicative factor 1/√(1+τ) relative to the traditional non-relativistic expression, where τ = κ² − λ² = −Q²/4m_N².
- In the limit η_F → 0, the time component of the seagull current vanishes identically, confirming it is of O(η_F).
- The single-nucleon current time component acquires a factor κ/√τ multiplying G_E and a factor 1/√(1+τ) multiplying the spin-orbit term, replacing the traditional non-relativistic result J⁰_nonrel = G_E.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach for single-nucleon electromagnetic currents (Amaro 1996, 1996b; Jessup 1998) to include pion-exchange MEC operators, specifically the seagull and pion-in-flight contributions. The new operators can be used with existing non-relativistic nuclear wave functions while incorporating relativistic corrections that are missing in traditional non-relativistic MEC treatments (Duba; Kohn 1981). The work enables future calculations of electromagnetic response functions with improved MEC operators and plans to extend the treatment to other meson exchanges and 2p-2h configurations.
