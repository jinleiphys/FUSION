# Ab initio calculations of monopole sum rules: From finite nuclei to infinite nuclear matter

> We compute moments of the isoscalar monopole response of N = Z closed-shell nuclei based on chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interactions. We employ the random phase approximation (RPA) and two ab initio many-body approaches, the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) and coupled-cluster theory (CC). In the IMSRG framework, the moments are obtained as ground-state expectation values, whereas in the CC approach, they are evaluated through excited-state calculations. We find good agreement between the IMSRG and CC results across all nuclei studied. RPA provides a reasonable approximation to the correlated methods if the interaction is soft. From the calculated moments, we extract average energies of the monopole response, compute finite-nucleus incompressibilities, and estimate the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter by a fit to a leptodermous expansion. Our extrapolated values are lower than those obtained in nuclear matter calculations with the same interactions, but the values are consistent with phenomenological ranges.

## Key claim

The extrapolated incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter obtained from ab initio calculations of monopole sum rules in finite nuclei is lower than values from direct nuclear matter calculations using the same chiral interactions, but remains consistent with phenomenological ranges.

## Method

The authors computed moments (k = -1, 0, 1) of the isoscalar monopole response for N=Z closed-shell nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 56Ni, 100Sn) using the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) at the IMSRG(2) truncation and coupled-cluster theory (CC) at the CCSD level, with the Lorentz integral transform technique (LIT-CC) for excited-state calculations. They employed two chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interactions (NNLOsat and DeltaNNLOGO(394)) expanded in harmonic-oscillator basis states with up to 15 major shells and frequencies between 12 and 16 MeV. The moments were used to extract average energies of the monopole response and finite-nucleus incompressibilities, from which the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter was estimated via a leptodermous expansion.

## Key numbers

* For 16O with the NNLOsat interaction, the IMSRG average energy sqrt(m1/m-1) is 21.6(8) MeV and the CC value is 21.3(4) MeV, compared to the experimental value of 19.63(38) MeV from Lui (2001).
* For 40Ca with the DeltaNNLOGO(394) interaction, the IMSRG average energy is 20.9(3) MeV and the CC value is 20.4(2) MeV, compared to the experimental value of 17.29(12) MeV from Youngblood (2007).
* For 56Ni with the NNLOsat interaction, the IMSRG average energy is 19.6(5) MeV and the CC value is 19.0(2) MeV, compared to the experimental centroid energy of 19.3(5) MeV from Monrozeau (2008).
* For 100Sn with the DeltaNNLOGO(394) interaction, the IMSRG average energy is 19.0(8) MeV and the CC value is 17.7(4) MeV.
* The m0 moment for 56Ni with NNLOsat at emax=10 and hbar-omega=16 MeV increases from 3040 fm4 to 3160 fm4 when triples corrections are included in CC, reducing the difference with the IMSRG(2) result of 3630 fm4 from 17% to 13%.
* For the soft DeltaNNLOGO(394) interaction, relative deviations between IMSRG, CC, and RPA results for m-1 are only 3%, and model-space convergence is 1% to 3% in emax and about 5% for hbar-omega variations.

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of monopole response from light nuclei to medium-mass systems up to 100Sn, building on prior LIT-CC studies by Bacca (2013, 2014) and IMSRG response work by Parzuchowski (2016, 2017) and Porro (2025). The results demonstrate consistency between IMSRG and CC frameworks for collective excitations, and show that RPA provides a reasonable approximation only when using soft interactions like DeltaNNLOGO(394). The extracted nuclear matter incompressibility values are lower than those from direct nuclear matter calculations with the same interactions (e.g., from Ekstrom 2015 and Jiang 2020), but fall within phenomenological ranges, enabling improved constraints on the nuclear equation of state relevant for neutron star physics.
