# Net-charge fluctuations and balance functions at the LHC

> The measurement of event-by-event fluctuations and charge-dependent particle correlations are used to study properties of nuclear matter at high temperatures as produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We present results for event-by-event net-charge fluctuations and charge balance functions in Δη and Δϕ in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)= 2.76 TeV.

## Key claim

Net-charge fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV lie below the expectation for a hadron resonance gas but above the expectation for a quark-gluon plasma, indicating that the system is not in a pure QGP state and that the primordial fluctuations are diluted during the evolution from hadronization to kinetic freeze-out.

## Method

The analysis used data from the ALICE Time Projection Chamber (TPC) to measure event-by-event net-charge fluctuations via the quantity nu_+-,dyn, which is related to the fluctuation variable D, and charge balance functions in relative pseudorapidity Delta_eta and relative azimuthal angle Delta_phi. The net-charge fluctuations were corrected for global charge conservation and finite acceptance, and the balance functions were compared to predictions from the HIJING event generator, the AMPT model with string melting, and a thermal blast wave model.

## Key numbers

- The variable D (related to net-charge fluctuations) is close to 1 for a QGP and close to 3 for a hadron resonance gas, as predicted by theory.
- The corrected quantity <N_ch> * nu_+-,dyn^corr shows a monotonic decreasing dependence with the mean number of participating nucleons <N_part> for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, measured in pseudorapidity windows Delta_eta=1 and Delta_eta=1.6.
- The pseudorapidity dependence of the net-charge fluctuations is parametrized with an error function erf(Delta_eta/(sqrt(8)*sigma_f)), where sigma_f is a measure of diffusion.
- Balance functions for the 5% most central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV show a strong narrowing with increasing centrality compared to peripheral (60-70%) collisions; HIJING results for B(Delta_eta) are consistent with ALICE data only in the most peripheral events.

## Context

This work extends the study of net-charge fluctuations and balance functions from RHIC energies to LHC energies, using the ALICE detector at the LHC. The results contradict the predictions of the HIJING event generator for central collisions, which does not include collective flow, while the AMPT model with string melting reproduces the balance functions in Delta_phi due to its inclusion of collective flow. The analysis enables future studies of higher moments of net-charge fluctuations and balance functions for identified particles with transverse momentum and event-plane dependence.
