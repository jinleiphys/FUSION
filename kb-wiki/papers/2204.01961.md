# Molecular states from $\bar{B}^{(*)}N$ interactions

> In 2019, two new structures $Λ_b(6146)$ and $Λ_b(6152)$ were observed by the LHCb Collaboration at the invariant mass spectrum of $Λ_b^0π^{+}π^{-}$, which aroused a hot discussion about their inner structures. The $Λ_b(6146)$ and $Λ_b(6152)$ might still be molecular states because their masses are close to threshold of a $\bar{B}$ meson and a nucleon. In this work, we perform a systematical investigation of possible heavy baryonic molecular states from the $\bar{B}N$ interaction. Since the $\bar{B}N$ channel strongly couples to the $\bar{B}^{*}N$ channel, the possible $\bar{B}N-\bar{B}^{*}N$ bound states are also studied. The interaction of the system considered is described by the $t$-channel $σ$, $π$, $η$ ,$ω$, and $ρ$ mesons exchanges. By solving the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with the obtained one-boson-exchange potentials, the $\bar{B}^{(*)}N$ bound states with different quantum numbers are searched. The calculation suggests that recently observed $Λ_b(6146)$ can be assigned as a $P$-wave $\bar{B}N$ molecular state with spin parity $J^P=3/2^{+}$ or a $\bar{B}N-\bar{B}^{*}N$ bound state. However, assignment of $Λ_b(6152)$ as an $F$-wave $\bar{B}N$ molecular is disfavored. The $Λ_b(6152)$ can be explained as meson-baryon molecular state with a small $\bar{B}N$ component. The calculation also predict the existence of two $S$-wave $\bar{B}N-\bar{B}^{*}N$ bound states that can be related to the experimentally observed $Λ_b(5912)$ and $Λ_b(5920)$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the LHCb-observed $\Lambda_b(6146)$ can be assigned as a $P$-wave $\bar{B}N$ molecular state with spin parity $J^P=3/2^{+}$ or as a $\bar{B}N-\bar{B}^{*}N$ bound state, while the assignment of $\Lambda_b(6152)$ as an $F$-wave $\bar{B}N$ molecular state is disfavored; instead, $\Lambda_b(6152)$ may be explained as a meson-baryon molecular state with only a small $\bar{B}N$ component.

## Method

The authors describe the $\bar{B}^{(*)}N$ interaction using a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model with $t$-channel exchanges of $\sigma$, $\pi$, $\eta$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons. They compute the effective potentials in momentum space via the Breit approximation, then Fourier-transform them to coordinate space and solve the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation to search for bound states with various quantum numbers, including coupled-channel $\bar{B}N-\bar{B}^{*}N$ systems.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_b(6146)$: mass = $6146.17 \pm 0.33 \pm 0.22 \pm 0.16$ MeV, width = $2.9 \pm 1.3 \pm 0.3$ MeV, $I(J^P)=0(3/2^{+})$
- $\Lambda_b(6152)$: mass = $6152.51 \pm 0.26 \pm 0.22 \pm 0.16$ MeV, width = $2.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.3$ MeV, $I(J^P)=0(5/2^{+})$
- $\bar{B}N$ threshold: $m_{\bar{B}} = 5279.34 \pm 0.12$ MeV, $m_p = 938.27$ MeV (from PDG), giving a threshold near 6217.6 MeV; $\Lambda_b(6146)$ and $\Lambda_b(6152)$ lie about 65 MeV and 71 MeV below this threshold, respectively
- Coupling constants used: $g_{\cal{V}} = 5.9$, $g = 0.59$, $\lambda = 0.56$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\beta = 0.9$, $f_{\pi} = 132$ MeV, $F = 0.51$, $D = 0.75$, $f = 93$ MeV
- $g = 4.64$ obtained from the strong decay width $\Gamma_{K^{*+}} = 50.3 \pm 0.8$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the molecular picture previously applied to hidden-charm pentaquarks $P_c$ (LHCb, 2019) and $P_{cs}$ (LHCb, 2020) to the bottom sector, specifically to the $\Lambda_b$ baryons observed by LHCb in 2019 and confirmed by CMS in 2020. The study builds on earlier investigations of $\bar{B}N$ and $\bar{B}^{*}N$ molecular states by Romanets (2013), Lu (2014), Liang (2014), Garcia-Recio (2012), and Huang (2021), and predicts that two $S$-wave $\bar{B}N-\bar{B}^{*}N$ bound states can be related to the experimentally observed $\Lambda_b(5912)$ and $\Lambda_b(5920)$, which have previously been interpreted as either three-quark states or molecules.
