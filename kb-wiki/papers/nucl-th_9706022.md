# Electromagnetic probes of dense matter in heavy-ion collisions

> Dilepton and photon production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies are studied in the relativistic transport model that incorporates self-consistently the change of hadron masses in dense matter. It is found that the dilepton spectra in proton-nucleus reactions can be well described by the conventional mechanism of Dalitz decay and direct vector meson decay. However, to provide a quantitative explanation of the observed dilepton spectra in central heavy-ion collisions requires contributions other than these direct decays and also various medium effects. Introducing a decrease of vector meson masses in hot dense medium, we find that these heavy-ion data can be satisfactorily explained. Furthermore, the single photon spectra in our calculations with either free or in-medium meson masses do not exceed the upper bound deduced from the experiments by the WA80 Collaboration.

# Electromagnetic probes of dense matter in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Dilepton spectra in central heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies can be satisfactorily explained only when a decrease of vector meson masses in hot dense medium is introduced, while conventional mechanisms (Dalitz decay and direct vector meson decay) that describe proton-nucleus reactions are insufficient; the single photon spectra calculated with either free or in-medium meson masses do not exceed the experimental upper bound from the WA80 Collaboration.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on the Walecka-type model, with initial hadron abundances and distributions from string fragmentation in the RQMD model. The scalar field was determined self-consistently from the coupling of light quarks to scalar and vector fields using the meson-quark coupling model, with nucleon and meson masses modified according to constituent quark model relations and the Weinberg sum rule. Dilepton yields were calculated by integrating over the time evolution of the colliding system up to the freeze-out time of about 20 fm/c, including contributions from Dalitz decay, direct vector meson decay, pion-pion annihilation, and kaon-antikaon annihilation.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out time: about 20 fm/c
- Dielectron channel coefficients for vector meson decay: 8.814 x 10^-6 (rho), 0.767 x 10^-6 (omega), and 1.344 x 10^-6 (phi)
- In-medium meson masses produce a factor of 2-3 enhancement of dilepton yield in the mass region from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV in S+Au collisions at 200 AGeV
- Average charged particle pseudo-rapidity density dN_ch/d_eta_ is about 440 for central Pb+Au collisions at 160 AGeV
- Thermal photon results show omega meson radiative decay is important for photons with transverse momenta above 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic transport model approach to heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies, building on earlier work by the authors and others (Ko 1987, Sorge 1989) and the dropping vector meson mass proposal by Brown and Rho (1991). It directly addresses the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES and HELIOS-3 collaborations, which could not be explained by conventional sources alone. The results are consistent with similar findings by Cassing et al. (1995) using Hadron-String Dynamics and by Hung et al. (1996) and Redlich et al. (1996). The photon calculations challenge the interpretation from hydrodynamical models that the absence of thermal photons indicates quark-gluon plasma formation, instead supporting the conclusion of Cleymans et al. (1996) that including all hadron resonances below 2.5 GeV can explain the WA80 upper bound without invoking a phase transition.
