# Accessing the pion 3D structure at US and China Electron-Ion Colliders

> We present in this letter the first systematic feasibility study of accessing generalised parton distributions of the pion at an electron-ion collider through deeply virtual Compton scattering at next-to-leading order. It relies on a state-of-the-art model, able to fulfil by construction all the theoretical constraints imposed on generalised parton distributions. Strikingly, our analysis shows that quarks and gluons interfere destructively and that gluon dominance could be spotted by a sign change of the DVCS beam spin asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic feasibility study showing that deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off a virtual pion at next-to-leading order (NLO) will be measurable at future electron-ion colliders, provided that the one-pion exchange is the dominant process. The analysis reveals that quarks and gluons interfere destructively, and that gluon dominance could be identified by a sign change of the DVCS beam spin asymmetry.

## Method

The authors model the pion's generalised parton distributions (GPDs) using a state-of-the-art framework based on Continuum Schwinger Methods, with quark PDF parameters from Ding (2019) and a covariant extension to satisfy all theoretical constraints. They compute the Sullivan process amplitude at NLO using the PARTONS and Apfel++ codes, then estimate event rates and beam spin asymmetries via Monte-Carlo simulation for both the US Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Chinese Electron-Ion Collider (EicC), comparing LO, NLO with gluons, and NLO without gluons.

## Key numbers

* The pion PDF is modelled at a low reference scale of 331 MeV, with parameters N_q = 213, gamma = 2.29, and rho = -2.93 from Ding (2019).
* The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is g_{pi NN} = 13.05, with a phenomenological cutoff Lambda = 800 MeV for the pion-nucleon vertex form factor.
* The maximum pion virtuality for the Sullivan process is taken as |t|_max = 0.6 GeV^2, with a minimum s_pi of 4 GeV^2.
* For EIC, the integrated luminosity is 10 fb^{-1}/year with lepton beam energies of 5, 10, or 18 GeV and hadron beam energies of 41, 100, or 275 GeV; for EicC, the integrated luminosity is 50 fb^{-1}/year with a 3.5 GeV lepton beam and a 20 GeV hadron beam.
* For Q^2 above 12 GeV^2 with x_B^pi in [10^{-3}; 10^{-2}], the beam spin asymmetry is reduced by more than a factor of 2 when gluons are included, and for Q^2 below 2 GeV^2 the BSA changes sign.
* In the valence region accessible through EicC (x_B^pi in [0.1; 0.5]), the BSA amplitude without gluons reaches about 0.2, while with gluons it almost vanishes at low Q^2 and remains smaller by a factor of 2 compared to the gluonless case.

## Context

This work extends the Sullivan process approach previously applied to pion electromagnetic form factors by Huber (2008) and to pion PDF extraction, as discussed in the EIC Yellow Report (AbdulKhalek 2021). It builds on the pion GPD model of Chavez (2021) and the continuum Schwinger methods framework of Binosi (2016) and Ding (2019). The results demonstrate that DVCS off a virtual pion is feasible at both EIC and EicC, enabling experimental access to the 3D structure of the pion and providing a clear signal for gluon dominance that motivates next-to-next-to-leading order calculations of the DVCS kernel.
