# Can the maximum mass of neutron stars rule out any equation of state of dense stellar matter before gravity is well understood?

> Probably No! As an example, using soft EOSs consistent with existing terrestrial nuclear laboratory experiments for hybrid neutron stars containing a quark core described with MIT bag model using reasonable parameters, we show that the recently discovered new holder of neutron star maximum mass PSR J1614-2230 of $1.97\pm0.04M_{\odot}$ can be well described by incorporating a Yukawa gravitational correction that is consistent with existing constraints from neutron-proton and neutron-lead scatterings as well as the spectroscopy of antiproton atoms.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of neutron stars alone cannot rule out any equation of state (EOS) of dense stellar matter before strong-field gravity is well understood; using soft EOSs consistent with terrestrial nuclear experiments for hybrid stars with a quark core, the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses) can be reproduced by incorporating a Yukawa gravitational correction that is consistent with existing constraints from neutron-proton and neutron-lead scatterings as well as antiproton atom spectroscopy.

## Method

The authors model hybrid neutron stars containing a quark core described by the MIT bag model, with the hyperonic EOS modeled using an extended isospin- and momentum-dependent effective interaction (MDI) for the baryon octet. A Yukawa gravitational correction term is added to the EOS, parameterized by the combination g²/μ² (where g is the boson-baryon coupling constant and μ is the boson mass), and the TOV equation is solved to obtain mass-radius relations. The Gibbs construction is used for the hadron-quark phase transition, and the inner and outer crusts are described by a parameterized EOS and the BPS EOS, respectively.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses (the new holder of the maximum mass of neutron stars at the time of publication).
- Without the Yukawa correction, the maximum stellar mass supported is about 1.46 solar masses (for bag constant B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV).
- With B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV and g²/μ² = 50 GeV⁻², the maximum mass reaches 1.97 solar masses, with a corresponding radius of about 12.4 km.
- With B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV, a smaller value of g²/μ² = 40 GeV⁻² is needed to obtain a maximum mass consistent with the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230.
- The hadron-quark mixed phase covers density ranges of ρ/ρ₀ = 1.31 to 6.56 (for B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV) and ρ/ρ₀ = 2.19 to 8.63 (for B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of whether the precisely measured mass of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010) can rule out soft EOSs, as previously argued by Demorest et al. (2010), Ozel et al. (2010), and Xu et al. (2010). The authors show that the degeneracy between the EOS and the theory of gravity means that soft EOSs, which would be ruled out under General Relativity, can be reconciled with the observed maximum mass by incorporating a Yukawa-type modification to gravity, as proposed by Fujii (1971) and constrained by terrestrial experiments (Kam08, BARB75, POKO06, Nes08). This enables future work to break the degeneracy by using at least two independent observables, as suggested by Yunes et al. (2010).
