# Generalized susceptibilities of net-baryon number based on the 3-dimensional Ising universality class

> Assuming the equilibrium of the QCD system, we have investigated the critical behavior of sixth-, eighth- and tenth-order susceptibilities of net-baryon number, through mapping the results in the three-dimensional Ising model to that of QCD. Both the leading critical contribution as well as sub-leading critical contribution from the Ising model are discussed. When considering only the leading critical contribution, the density plots for susceptibilities of the same order demonstrate a consistent general pattern independent on values of mapping parameters. As the critical point is approached from the crossover side, a negative dip followed by a positive peak is observed in the $μ_B$ dependence of the three different orders of susceptibilities. When sub-leading critical contribution is taken into account, modifications become apparent in the density plots of the susceptibilities. The emergence of negative dips in the $μ_B$ dependence of the susceptibilities is not an absolute phenomenon, while the positive peak structure is a more robust feature of the critical point.

## Key claim
Assuming equilibrium QCD and a specific critical point at (107, 635) MeV, the sixth-, eighth-, and tenth-order net-baryon number susceptibilities show a negative dip followed by a positive peak in their chemical potential dependence when only the leading critical contribution from the 3D Ising model is considered; however, when sub-leading critical contributions are included, the negative dip is not an absolute phenomenon and depends on mapping parameters and freeze-out distance, while the positive peak remains a robust feature.

## Method
The author maps the parametric representation of the three-dimensional Ising model (using variables R and theta for magnetization and reduced temperature) onto the QCD temperature-chemical potential plane via a linear transformation with six parameters, fixing alpha1 = 10.8 degrees and alpha2 = 100.8 degrees (orthogonal axes). The critical point is placed at (T_C, mu_BC) = (107, 635) MeV based on functional renormalization group results, and the QCD phase transition line is parameterized using lattice QCD results for the curvature kappa = 0.015. The sixth-, eighth-, and tenth-order susceptibilities of net-baryon number are computed from derivatives of the Ising pressure, considering both leading (magnetization-only) and sub-leading (mixed magnetization-energy) singular contributions, and evaluated along freeze-out curves parallel to the phase transition line at various temperature offsets Delta T.

## Key numbers
- Critical point adopted: (T_C, mu_BC) = (107, 635) MeV from Fuweijie-PRD101
- Transition temperature at mu_B = 0: T_0 = 156.5 MeV (Bazavov 2019)
- Curvature parameter: kappa = 0.015
- Fourth-order curvature parameter: lambda = 0.000256
- Ising critical exponents: beta = 0.326, delta = 4.8, alpha = 0.11
- Normalization constants: m_0 = 0.605, h_0 = 0.364
- Mapping angles: alpha_1 = 10.8 degrees, alpha_2 = 100.8 degrees
- For the leading contribution with w=0.4, rho=0.8 at Delta T=1.0 MeV, the ratio of negative dip depth to positive peak height is approximately 0.163 for chi_6, 0.275 for chi_8, and 0.361 for chi_10
- For the sub-leading contribution with w=1.6, rho=1.6 at Delta T=0.2 MeV, the ratio of negative dip depth to positive peak height is approximately 0.016 for chi_6, 0.058 for chi_8, and 0.099 for chi_10

## Context
This work extends the approach of Stephanov (2009, 2011) and Asakawa and Stephanov (2005) who mapped the 3D Ising model to QCD to study critical fluctuations, and follows the earlier work of Pan (2016) on sixth-order cumulants in the Ising model. It specifically addresses the role of sub-leading critical contributions, which Stephanov (2021) showed can eliminate the negative dip in the fourth-order cumulant when alpha_2 - alpha_1 = 90 degrees. The results enable experimental searches for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions at the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, particularly for higher-order cumulants of net-proton number beyond sixth order, and provide guidance on which features (positive peak vs. negative dip) are robust signals of the critical point.
