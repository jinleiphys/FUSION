# Heavy Quarkonium Production at Collider Energies: Factorization and Evolution

> We present a factorization formalism for inclusive production of heavy quarkonia of large transverse momentum, $p_T$ at collider energies, including both leading power (LP) and next-to-leading power (NLP) behavior in $p_T$. We demonstrate that both LP and NLP contributions can be factorized in terms of perturbatively calculable short-distance partonic coefficient functions and universal non-perturbative fragmentation functions, and derive the evolution equations that are implied by the factorization. We identify projection operators for all channels of the factorized LP and NLP infrared safe short-distance partonic hard parts, and corresponding operator definitions of fragmentation functions. For the NLP, we focus on the contributions involving the production of a heavy quark pair, a necessary condition for producing a heavy quarkonium. We evaluate the first non-trivial order of evolution kernels for all relevant fragmentation functions, and discuss the role of NLP contributions.

## Key claim
The paper presents a perturbative QCD factorization formalism for inclusive heavy quarkonium production at large transverse momentum that systematically includes both leading power (LP, scaling as 1/p_T^4) and next-to-leading power (NLP, scaling as 1/p_T^6) contributions, where the NLP terms involve direct production of a heavy quark pair at short distances rather than solely through single-parton fragmentation.

## Method
The authors expand the production cross section in powers of 1/p_T^2 and demonstrate that both LP and NLP contributions can be factorized into perturbatively calculable short-distance partonic coefficient functions convolved with universal non-perturbative fragmentation functions. They identify projection operators for all channels of the factorized LP and NLP infrared-safe short-distance hard parts, derive the corresponding operator definitions of fragmentation functions, and evaluate the first non-trivial order (α_s^2 for single-parton to heavy quark pair evolution, α_s for heavy quark pair to heavy quark pair evolution) of the evolution kernels for all relevant fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers
- The LO color singlet model cross section falls off as 1/p_T^8, more than two orders of magnitude smaller than Tevatron J/ψ data at high p_T
- At NLO in the color singlet model, the cross section behaves as 1/p_T^6 rather than 1/p_T^8, with the NLO contribution more than a factor of 10 larger than the LO result for p_T values characteristic of Tevatron data
- The leading power partonic production rate scales as 1/p_T^4, dominated by single gluon production at short distances (~O(1/p_T))
- The NLP contributions scale as 1/p_T^6, with heavy quark pairs produced at distance scale O(1/p_T)
- Evolution kernels are calculated at order α_s^2 for single-parton to double-parton transitions and at order α_s for heavy quark pair to heavy quark pair transitions

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier factorization approach (Kang 2011, Kang 2011) for heavy quarkonium production and addresses limitations of the NRQCD factorization approach (Bodwin 1994) and color singlet model at high p_T. The NLO color singlet model calculations (Campbell 2007, Artoisenet 2007, Artoisenet 2008) showed surprising power enhancements that motivated this systematic power expansion. The formalism enables resummation of logarithms of p_T^2/m_Q^2 through evolution equations and provides a framework for incorporating NRQCD long-distance matrix elements as input fragmentation functions at a scale μ_0 ≳ 2m_Q, as demonstrated in model calculations for S-wave and P-wave quarkonia (Ma 2013, Ma 2014).
