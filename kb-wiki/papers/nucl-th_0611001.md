# Longitudinal flow and onset of deconfinement

> The effects of the onset of deconfinement on longitudinal and transverse flow are studied. First, we analyze longitudinal pion spectra from $E_{\rm lab}= 2A$ GeV to $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV within Landau's hydrodynamical model and the UrQMD transport approach. From the measured data on the widths of the pion rapidity spectra, we extract the sound velocity $c_s^2$ in the early stage of the reactions. It is found that the sound velocity has a local minimum (indicating a softest point in the equation of state, EoS) at $E_{\rm beam}=30A$ GeV. This softening of the EoS is compatible with the assumption of the formation of a mixed phase at the onset of deconfinement. Furthermore, the energy excitation function of elliptic flow ($v_2$) from $E_{\rm beam}=90A$ MeV to $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV is explored within the UrQMD framework and discussed in the context of the available data. The transverse flow should also be sensitive to changes in the equation of state. Therefore, the underestimation of elliptic flow by the UrQMD model calculation above $E_{\rm lab}= 30A$ GeV might also be explained by assuming a phase transition from a hadron gas to the quark gluon plasma around this energy. This would be consistent with the model calculations, indicating a transition from hadronic matter to ``string matter'' in this energy range.

## Key claim

The sound velocity extracted from pion rapidity widths has a local minimum (softest point) at a beam energy of 30A GeV, which is compatible with the formation of a mixed phase at the onset of deconfinement; however, the nature of this softest point remains unclear because the UrQMD transport model, which lacks explicit partonic degrees of freedom, also reproduces the rapidity widths.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental pion rapidity distributions from central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions across beam energies from 2A GeV to sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, fitting them with Gaussians to extract the root mean square width sigma_y. They inverted Landau's hydrodynamical model formula to extract the speed of sound c_s^2 from the measured sigma_y values. Separately, they calculated the excitation function of elliptic flow v_2 within the UrQMD transport model (v2.2) from 90A MeV to sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and compared it to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound c_s^2 extracted from data shows a local minimum (softest point) at E_beam = 30A GeV.
- The fraction of energy density in "unformed hadrons" (string fragments within their formation time) reaches 90% already around 30A GeV beam energy.
- At the highest RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV), the data show about 5% elliptic flow while the UrQMD model calculation provides only half of this value.
- The UrQMD model underestimates elliptic flow above E_lab = 160A GeV.
- The rapidity width data span from E_lab = 2A GeV to sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The elliptic flow excitation function covers from E_beam = 90A MeV to sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for the onset of deconfinement by examining longitudinal flow observables, complementing prior studies that found non-monotonous structures in the K+/pi+ ratio (Afanasiev 2002, Gazdzicki 2004), a step in the transverse momentum excitation function (Gazdzicki 2004, NA49 collaboration), and increased ratio fluctuations (Roland 2005) around 30A GeV. The finding of a softest point in the speed of sound is consistent with predictions by Hung and Rischke (1994, 1995) for a mixed phase at the transition from hadronic to partonic matter, and the underestimation of elliptic flow by UrQMD above 30A GeV suggests that partonic interactions may need to be explicitly included, as proposed by Bratkovskaya (2004) and Weber (1998).
