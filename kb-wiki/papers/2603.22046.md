# Exact center symmetry and first-order phase transition in QCD with three degenerate dynamical quarks

> We study QCD with three degenerate flavors of dynamical quarks using first-principles lattice simulations. For a specific choice of imaginary isospin chemical potential, this theory possesses an exact center symmetry, just like pure gauge theory. This exact symmetry is expected to be intact at low temperatures and spontaneously broken in the high-temperature regime. By analyzing the finite-size scaling of the Polyakov loop distribution, obtained with a dedicated multi-histogram approach, we demonstrate that there is a first-order deconfinement phase transition in between. Our results are obtained employing stout-smeared rooted staggered quarks at one lattice spacing. Using simulations at different quark masses we sketch the behavior of QCD in the mass-isospin chemical potential plane, shedding new light on this corner of the fundamental phase diagram of the strong interactions and the relationship between chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that QCD with three degenerate dynamical quarks at a specific imaginary isospin chemical potential ($i\mu_I/T = 4\pi/3$) exhibits an exact center symmetry and a first-order deconfinement phase transition, based on finite-size scaling analysis of the Polyakov loop distribution. The authors hedge that their main results are obtained at one lattice spacing ($N_t=6$) and at the physical strange quark mass, though they argue the conclusion persists for any massive theory.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using stout-smeared rooted staggered quarks on $N_s^3 \times N_t$ lattices with $N_t=6$ and spatial extents $N_s \in \{16, 20, 24, 28, 32\}$. They employed a multi-histogram reweighting method that interpolates in both the inverse gauge coupling $\beta$ and the quark mass, enabling efficient finite-size scaling analysis of the rotated Polyakov loop and its higher moments (susceptibility, skewness, kurtosis). The simulations were conducted at imaginary isospin chemical potential $i\mu_I/T = 4\pi/3$ with $\mu_B = \mu_S = 0$, where the theory possesses exact $\mathrm{Z}(3)$ center symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Critical inverse gauge coupling: $\beta_c = 3.835(2)$, determined from the zero-crossing of the skewness of the rotated Polyakov loop distribution
- Temperature range explored: $232\ \mathrm{MeV} \le T \le 333\ \mathrm{MeV}$, corresponding to $3.79 \le \beta \le 3.85$
- For physical strange quark mass: $T_c w_0 = 0.248(1)$
- For physical light quark mass ($m = m_s^{\mathrm{phys}}/28.15$): $T_c w_0 = 0.239(1)$
- For heavy quark mass ($m = 3 \cdot m_s^{\mathrm{phys}}$): $T_c w_0 = 0.2505(5)$
- The pure gauge theory value for comparison: $T_c w_0 = 0.2507(2)$ (Borsanyi 2021)
- The physical $(2+1)$-flavor QCD crossover value for comparison: $T_c w_0 \approx 0.14$ (BMW 2012)

## Context

This work extends the Roberge-Weiss analysis (Roberge 1986) to three degenerate flavors at imaginary isospin chemical potential, where the exact $\mathrm{Z}(3)$ center symmetry was previously identified by Kouno (2012) and Cherman (2017). The study fills a gap in the literature by providing the first fully non-perturbative finite-size scaling demonstration that the transition is first order, going beyond previous model calculations (Kouno 2012, 2013, 2015), perturbation theory (Hirakida 2020, Poppitz 2021), and lattice simulations (Iritani 2015, Misumi 2015) that lacked such scaling analysis. The results connect the first-order transition at imaginary isospin chemical potential to the first-order region at $\mu_I=0$ and high quark masses in the Columbia plot (Brown 1990), and enable future studies of the relationship between chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement in this corner of the QCD phase diagram.
