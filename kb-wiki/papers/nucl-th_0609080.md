# Chiral Expansion, Renormalization and the Nuclear Force

> The renormalization of singular chiral potentials as applied to NN scattering and the structure of the deuteron is discussed. It is shown how zero range theories may be implemented non-perturbatively as constrained from known long range NN forces.

# Chiral Expansion, Renormalization and the Nuclear Force

## Key claim
The paper argues that fully iterated (non-perturbative) chiral potentials can reproduce NN scattering observables and deuteron properties with fewer counterterms than perturbative treatments, provided the short-distance cut-off is removed under orthogonality constraints. However, this non-perturbative approach lacks a systematic dimensional power counting, and Weinberg's power counting is found incompatible with renormalization for both one-pion exchange (OPE) and two-pion exchange (TPE) potentials.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space for s-wave NN scattering (¹S₀ channel) and the deuteron (³S₁–³D₁ channel) using chiral potentials expanded to leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They impose energy-independent boundary conditions at short distances derived from orthogonality of scattering states, fix the scattering length to its physical value, and then predict the effective range and higher threshold parameters without additional short-distance counterterms. The cut-off dependence is studied by taking the short-distance limit r_c → 0.

## Key numbers
- ¹S₀ effective range r₀ at NNLO (fully iterated): 2.86 fm (experimental: 2.77(5) fm; Nijm II: 2.67 fm)
- ¹S₀ scattering length α₀: input fixed to -23.74(2) fm (experimental) or -23.73 fm (Nijm II)
- Deuteron binding energy B = 2.224575(9) MeV (used as input)
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio η: 0.0256(4) (experimental); TPE gives 0.276(3) for quadrupole moment Q_d
- Deuteron asymptotic normalization A_S: 0.8846(9) fm^{-1/2} (experimental); TPE gives 0.884(4) fm^{-1/2}
- Chiral constants determined: c₁ = -1.2 ± 0.2 GeV^{-1}, c₃ = -2.6 ± 0.1 GeV^{-1}, c₄ = +3.3 ± 0.1 GeV^{-1}
- Analytical formula for effective range at LO: r₀ = 1.308 – 4.548/α₀ + 5.193/α₀² (fm)
- Analytical formula for effective range at NNLO: r₀ = 2.672 – 5.755/α₀ + 6.031/α₀² (fm)

## Context
This work extends the renormalization program for singular chiral potentials previously developed by the same authors (Pavon Valderrama 2003, 2004, 2005) and confirms the finding of Nogga (2005) that Weinberg's power counting fails for OPE when renormalization is taken seriously. The paper contrasts perturbative approaches (which require many counterterms) with fully iterated potentials (which need only one counterterm per partial wave) and shows that TPE at NNLO saturates experimental observables within uncertainties, making N³LO unnecessary. The results enable model-independent predictions of long-distance chiral correlations and suggest a connection to the liquid drop model via Van der Waals components of the nuclear force.
