# Minimal Relativity and $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ Pairing in Symmetric Nuclear Matter

> We present solutions of the coupled, non-relativistic $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ gap equations for neutron-proton pairing in symmetric nuclear matter, and estimate relativistic effects by solving the same gap equations modified according to minimal relativity and using single-particle energies from a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation. As a main result we find that relativistic effects decrease the value of the gap at the saturation density $k_F=1.36 fm^{-1}$ considerably, in conformity with the lack of evidence for strong neutron-proton pairing in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects, incorporated via minimal relativity and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock single-particle energies, reduce the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ neutron-proton pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter at the empirical saturation density from about 6 MeV to nearly zero, consistent with the lack of empirical evidence for strong neutron-proton pairing in finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled non-relativistic $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ BCS gap equations for neutron-proton pairing in symmetric nuclear matter using the Bonn A potential. To estimate relativistic effects, they modified the gap equations according to minimal relativity, replacing the non-relativistic single-particle energies with those from a self-consistent Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculation and multiplying the free NN interaction by a factor $\tilde{m}^2/\tilde{E}_k\tilde{E}_{k'}$. The gap equations were solved self-consistently together with the particle number conservation equation for the chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Non-relativistic $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ energy gap at empirical saturation density $k_F=1.36$ fm$^{-1}$: approximately 6 MeV.
* Relativistic $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ energy gap at $k_F=1.36$ fm$^{-1}$: nearly zero.
* Non-relativistic saturation density from BHF with Bonn A: $k_F \approx 1.8$ fm$^{-1}$.
* At $k_F=1.4$ fm$^{-1}$, non-relativistic effective mass $m^*/m = 0.6751$ and single-particle potential $U_0 = -97.2755$ MeV.
* At $k_F=1.4$ fm$^{-1}$, relativistic scalar field $U_S = -384.89$ MeV and vector field $U_V = 300.18$ MeV.
* Weak-coupling estimate gives $D_F^R/D_F^{NR} \approx 0.5$ at $k_F=1.4$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic calculations of the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ gap by Alm et al. (1990), Vonderfecht et al. (1991), Takatsuka and Tamagaki (1993), and Baldo et al. (1995), which all found large gaps of several MeV at saturation density. It contrasts with those results by incorporating relativistic effects through the DBHF approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990), which reproduces the empirical saturation point. The finding that additional repulsive effects destroy pairing at empirical saturation density resolves the discrepancy between non-relativistic predictions and the lack of empirical evidence for strong neutron-proton pairing in finite nuclei, and suggests that future work should examine induced interactions and three-body forces as alternative mechanisms for gap suppression.
