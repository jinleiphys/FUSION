# Nuclei in Strongly Magnetised Neutron Star Crusts

> We discuss the ground state properties of matter in outer and inner crusts of neutron stars under the influence of strong magnetic fields. In particular, we demonstrate the effects of Landau quantization of electrons on compositions of neutron star crusts. First we revisit the sequence of nuclei and the equation of state of the outer crust adopting the Baym, Pethick and Sutherland (BPS) model in the presence of strong magnetic fields and most recent versions of the theoretical and experimental nuclear mass tables. Next we deal with nuclei in the inner crust. Nuclei which are arranged in a lattice, are immersed in a nucleonic gas as well as a uniform background of electrons in the inner crust. The Wigner-Seitz approximation is adopted in this calculation and each lattice volume is replaced by a spherical cell. The coexistence of two phases of nuclear matter - liquid and gas, is considered in this case. We obtain the equilibrium nucleus corresponding to each baryon density by minimizing the free energy of the cell. We perform this calculation using Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interaction with different parameter sets. We find nuclei with larger mass and charge numbers in the inner crust in the presence of strong magnetic fields than those of the zero field case for all nucleon-nucleon interactions considered here. However, SLy4 interaction has dramatic effects on the proton fraction as well as masses and charges of nuclei. This may be attributed to the behaviour of symmetry energy with density in the sub-saturation density regime. Further we discuss the implications of our results to shear mode oscillations of magnetars.

# Nuclei in Strongly Magnetised Neutron Star Crusts

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields (up to ~10^17 G) significantly alter the composition of both outer and inner neutron star crusts through Landau quantization of electrons, producing nuclei with larger mass and charge numbers than in the zero-field case; however, the effect on torsional shear mode frequencies is negligible for the fundamental mode, with only radial overtones showing sensitivity to magnetic field strength.

## Method

The authors revisit the Baym, Pethick and Sutherland (BPS) model for the outer crust under strong magnetic fields, using recent experimental and theoretical nuclear mass tables and including finite-size corrections to the lattice energy. For the inner crust, they adopt the Wigner-Seitz approximation with a Thomas-Fermi model at zero temperature, treating the coexistence of nuclear liquid and gas phases and minimizing the free energy per cell using Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interactions (SkM, SLy4, and Sk272 parameter sets). The equilibrium nucleus at each baryon density is obtained by subtracting the gas-phase solution from the nucleus-plus-gas solution, with electrons treated via Landau quantization and protons influenced through charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for electron Landau quantization: B_c = 4.414 × 10^13 G
- Magnetic field strengths considered: B_* = B/B_c = 10^3 (≈ 4.414 × 10^16 G) and B_* = 10^4 (≈ 4.414 × 10^17 G)
- For B_* = 10^4, only the zeroth Landau level is populated by electrons at densities below 0.005 fm^-3; above this density, electrons populate higher Landau levels
- Shear modulus is calculated for neutron star mass of 1.4 M_⊙ and magnetic fields up to 4.414 × 10^17 G
- Observed QPO frequencies from SGR 1900+14 (B = 4 × 10^14 G) range from 18 Hz to 1800 Hz and match frequencies estimated using the magnetised crust model for a 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star
- Shear modulus for B_* = 10^4 (4.414 × 10^17 G) is enhanced appreciably compared with the zero-field case

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strong magnetic field effects on dense matter in neutron star interiors by Debades and Pal, and the BPS model of outer crust composition by Baym, Pethick and Sutherland (1971). It also builds on prior calculations of magnetised crust properties by Lai (1991) and the authors' own earlier work (Nandi and Bandyopadhyay). The results enable improved modelling of torsional shear mode oscillations in magnetars, following the framework of Sotani et al. (2007, 2008) and comparisons with observed QPOs from SGR 1900+14 and SGR 1806-20. The finding that fundamental shear mode frequencies are insensitive to magnetic field while radial overtones are affected agrees with predictions by Piro (2005).
