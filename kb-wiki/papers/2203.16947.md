# Accessing pion GPDs through the Sullivan process: is it feasible?

> Describing hadronic structure is one of the most intriguing problems in physics. In this respect, generalized parton distributions (GPDs) constitute an outstanding tool, allowing to draw "three dimensional pictures" of hadron's inside. Starting from contemporary models for pion's GPDs fulfilling all constraints imposed by QCD, we compute Compton form factors (CFFs) of pions subjected to deeply virtual Compton scattering. We show the behaviour of CFFs to be gluon-dominated at EIC's kinematics. Finally we evaluate lepton-beam-spin asymmetries in the Sullivan process, demonstrating the existence of such and thus triggering optimism about the possibility of probing pion's 3D structure at electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim

Using state-of-the-art pion GPD models that satisfy all QCD constraints, the authors compute Compton form factors and lepton-beam-spin asymmetries for the Sullivan process, finding that gluon contributions dominate at EIC kinematics and that a non-zero asymmetry is observable, but only if the full next-to-leading-order gluon content is included; leading-order calculations alone would give a qualitatively different (sign-flipped) asymmetry.

## Method

The authors start from a pion GPD model built from continuum Schwinger-method PDFs (Ding et al., 2019) with DGLAP-to-ERBL completion via the covariant extension formalism (Chouika et al., 2017; Chavez et al., 2021). They evolve the GPDs from a hadronic scale of 0.331 GeV to experimental scales using LO QCD evolution implemented in Apfel++. Compton form factors are computed at LO, NLO without gluon GPDs, and full NLO using the PARTONS framework. Lepton-beam-spin asymmetries for the Sullivan process are evaluated via Monte Carlo event generation, applying the one-pion-exchange approximation cuts from Amrath et al. (2008) and EIC detector specifications from AbdulKhalek et al. (2021).

## Key numbers

* The hadronic scale at which the pion's parton content is described by two dressed valence quarks is set to μ_H = 0.331 GeV (Cui et al., 2020).
* The Sullivan process one-pion-exchange approximation requires |t| ≲ 0.6 GeV² and the invariant mass of the nucleon-π system cut at M_Nπ² ≳ 4 GeV².
* At full NLO, the gluon-dominated CFF roughly behaves as ξ^{-1.4} at a factorization scale of 2 GeV².
* Beam-spin asymmetries are computed for ξ ∈ [10^{-3}, 10^{-2}] across four Q² bins; the full NLO asymmetry changes sign at low Q² relative to LO and NLO-without-gluon results, and its amplitude is reduced at moderate Q².

## Context

This work extends the contemporary pion GPD models of Chavez et al. (2021) and Raya et al. (2021), which were the first to satisfy all quantum-field-theory constraints, to predictions for the Sullivan process at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). It builds on the Sullivan process formalism of Sullivan (1971) and Amrath et al. (2008), and on DVCS dispersion-relation results from Diehl et al. (2007). The results enable experimental planning for accessing pion three-dimensional structure at EIC, and demonstrate that gluon dominance can be identified through a sign change in the beam-spin asymmetry.
