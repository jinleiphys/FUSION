# Study of Resonant Structures in a Deformed Mean-Field by the Contour Deformation Method in Momentum Space

> Solution of the momentum space Schrödinger equation in the case of deformed fields is being addressed. In particular it is shown that a complete set of single particle states which includes bound, resonant and complex continuum states may be obtained by the Contour Deformation Method. This generalized basis in the complex energy plane is known as a Berggren basis. The momentum space Schrödinger equation is an integral equation which is easily solved by matrix diagonalization routines even for the case of deformed fields. The method is demonstrated for axial symmetry and a fictitious "deformed $^5$He", but may be extended to more general deformation and applied to truly deformed halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The Contour Deformation Method (CDM) in momentum space can generate a complete Berggren basis, including bound, resonant, and complex continuum states, for deformed mean-field potentials, demonstrated here for an axially symmetric Gaussian potential, with the caveat that the method is shown only for a fictitious "deformed \(^5\)He" and axial symmetry, but is claimed to be extendable to more general deformations and realistic halo nuclei.

## Method

The momentum-space Schrödinger equation for a deformed Gaussian potential was solved by analytically continuing the integral equation into the complex \(k\)-plane via the Contour Deformation Method, using an inversion-symmetric contour \(L^+\) defined by a rotation angle \(\theta\) and a translation \(C\). The three-dimensional integral equation was reduced to a set of one-dimensional angular-momentum-coupled integral equations by partial-wave expansion, then discretized on a quadrature grid and solved by matrix diagonalization, yielding a complete set of single-particle states including bound, resonant, and discretized non-resonant continuum states.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical Gaussian potential (\(V_0 = -53.5\) MeV, \(\alpha_0 = 0.188\) fm\(^{-2}\)), the \(l^\pi = 0^+\) bound state energy was \(E = -14.9044\) MeV, and the \(l^\pi = 1^-\) resonance energy was \(E = 0.7268 - 0.3096i\) MeV, reproduced with \(N = 25\) discretization points.
- For deformation \(\delta = 0.9\) (prolate), the \(m^\pi = 0^+\) ground state converged to \(E = -12.0983\) MeV (with \(l_{\text{max}} = 10\)); for \(\delta = -0.9\) (oblate), it converged to \(E = -14.6551\) MeV (with \(l_{\text{max}} = 4\)).
- For \(\delta = 0.5\) (prolate), the \(m^\pi = 0^-\) state became bound at \(E = -0.6803\) MeV, while the \(m^\pi = 1^-\) resonance had energy \(E = 1.4410 - 0.9620i\) MeV (with \(l_{\text{max}} = 7\)).
- For \(\delta = -0.5\) (oblate), the \(m^\pi = 0^-\) resonance had energy \(E = 1.5168 - 1.0402i\) MeV, and the \(m^\pi = 1^-\) resonance had energy \(E = 0.3601 - 0.1041i\) MeV (with \(l_{\text{max}} = 7\)).

## Context

This work extends the Contour Deformation Method, previously applied to spherically symmetric fields by Hagen et al. (2005), to deformed mean-field potentials, addressing a gap where deformed resonances had only been studied for special cases, e.g., Michel (2005) used a Berggren basis from a spherical Woods-Saxon in position space, and another approach (deform1) solved coupled-channel equations with outgoing asymptotics but did not easily generalize to the non-resonant continuum. The method enables construction of a complete Berggren basis for deformed fields, suitable for Gamow Shell Model calculations and spectral representations of Green's functions in scattering and many-body problems for weakly bound and unbound nuclei.
