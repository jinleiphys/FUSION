# N_p N_n Scheme Based on New Empirical Formula for Excitation Energy

> We examine the $N_p N_n$ scheme based on a recently proposed simple empirical formula which is highly valid for the excitation energy of the first excited natural parity even multipole states in even-even nuclei. We demonstrate explicitly that the $N_p N_n$ scheme for the excitation energy emerges from the separate exponential dependence of the excitation energy on the valence nucleon numbers $N_p$ and $N_n$ together with the fact that only a limited set of numbers is allowed for the $N_p$ and $N_n$ of the existing nuclei.

# N_p N_n Scheme Based on New Empirical Formula for Excitation Energy

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the well-known N_p N_n scheme for excitation energies of first excited natural parity even multipole states in even-even nuclei emerges naturally from a separate exponential dependence of the excitation energy on the valence proton number N_p and valence neutron number N_n, combined with the restricted set of N_p and N_n values that actually occur in existing nuclei, even though the empirical formula contains no explicit dependence on the product N_p N_n.

## Method

The authors apply a previously proposed empirical formula E_x = α A^{-γ} + β [exp(-λ N_p) + exp(-λ N_n)] to the excitation energies of 2_1^+, 4_1^+, 6_1^+, 8_1^+, and 10_1^+ states in even-even nuclei. They compare N_p N_n-plots of measured excitation energies with those calculated from the formula, and systematically vary the allowed ranges of N_p and N_n (from unrestricted even numbers, to valence-nucleon numbers, to only those of actually existing nuclei) to isolate the origin of the N_p N_n scheme behavior.

## Key numbers

- For 2_1^+ states, the fitted parameters are α = 34.9 MeV, β = 1.00 MeV, γ = 1.19, and λ = 0.36.
- For 4_1^+ states, the fitted parameters are α = 94.9 MeV, β = 1.49 MeV, γ = 1.15, and λ = 0.30.
- For 6_1^+ states, the fitted parameters are α = 441.4 MeV, β = 1.51 MeV, γ = 1.31, and λ = 0.25.
- For 8_1^+ states, the fitted parameters are α = 1511.5 MeV, β = 1.41 MeV, γ = 1.46, and λ = 0.19.
- For 10_1^+ states, the fitted parameters are α = 2489.0 MeV, β = 1.50 MeV, γ = 1.49, and λ = 0.17.
- The minimum measured excitation energy for major shell IV (Z = 30 to 50) is 0.13 MeV; for shell V (Z = 52 to 82) it is 0.07 MeV; for shell VI (Z = 84 to 126) it is 0.04 MeV.
- The maximum N_p N_n value for shell IV is 140; for shell V it is 308; for shell VI it is 540.

## Context

This work extends the N_p N_n scheme originally developed by Casten (1985) and collaborators, which showed that plotting nuclear observables against the product N_p N_n produces remarkably simple patterns across the nuclear chart. The authors' earlier empirical formula (Ha and Cha, 2007) successfully reproduced excitation energies of 2_1^+ states, and was later extended to higher multipole states (Kim, Ha, and Cha, 2007). The present study clarifies that the N_p N_n scheme is not necessarily evidence for the product N_p N_n being the fundamental controlling variable, but rather emerges from the separate exponential dependence on N_p and N_n together with the limited valence nucleon numbers of real nuclei. This reinterpretation suggests that the empirical formula itself may be the more fundamental expression, and the authors note that extension to B(E2) values is in progress.
