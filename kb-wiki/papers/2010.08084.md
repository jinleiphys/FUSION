# Bound states in the B-matrix formalism for the three-body scattering

> We consider a model of relativistic three-body scattering with a bound state in the two-body sub-channel. We show that the naïve K-matrix type parametrization, here referred to as the B-matrix, has nonphysical singularities near the physical region. We show how to eliminate such singularities by using dispersion relations and also show how to reproduce unitarity relations by taking into account all relevant open channels.

## Key claim

The naive B-matrix (K-matrix type) parametrization for relativistic three-body scattering, when a two-body bound state exists, produces nonphysical singularities near the physical region that can hinder the formation of genuine three-body singularities; these spurious singularities can be eliminated using dispersion relations and by explicitly including the bound-state–particle channel.

## Method

The authors consider a simplified model of three-body scattering where long-range one-particle exchanges are neglected and only short-range contact interactions are included, with a bound state allowed in the two-body sub-channel. They analyze the analytic properties of the B-matrix ladder equation for S-wave scattering, using an effective range expansion for the two-body isobar amplitude, and compare the integration limits in the B-matrix formalism (with lower limit at the two-particle threshold 4m²) to those in effective field theory approaches. They then develop a multi-channel generalization of the B-matrix representation that includes the bound-state–particle channel explicitly, and show how dispersion relations can remove the nonphysical left-hand singularities.

## Key numbers

- The bound-state pole position is given by σ_b = 4m² / [1 + (32π/a₀)²], where a₀ is the dimensionless scattering length parameter.
- The bound-state residue is g = (32π / √(2a₀)) (σ_b / m).
- The two-body phase space factor is ρ(σ_q) = (1/32π) √(1 – 4m²/σ_q).
- The bound-state–spectator phase space factor is ρ₂(s) = λ^(1/2)(s, σ_b, m²) / (16π s).
- The three-body threshold is s_th,3 = (3m)² and the bound-state–particle threshold is s_th,2 = (M+m)², where M < 2m is the bound-state mass.

## Context

This work extends the B-matrix formalism for three-body scattering developed by Jackura et al. (2018, 2019) and Mikhasenko et al. (2019), and contrasts with effective field theory approaches such as those of Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) and Mai et al. (2017). The paper shows that the B-matrix approach, which uses only on-shell amplitudes and physical integration limits, cannot directly produce the bound-state–spectator scattering amplitude from the three-body amplitude alone, unlike the EFT approaches. The multi-channel generalization enables proper treatment of bound-state–particle scattering and provides a framework for analyzing systems like the χ_c1(3872) as a possible hadronic molecule, where the distinction between long-range and short-range interactions is crucial.
