# Description of electric dipole excitations in the tin isotopes within the quasiparticle time blocking approximation

> The quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) is applied to describe E1 excitations in the even-even tin isotopes. Within the model pairing correlations, two-quasiparticle (2q), and 2q*phonon configurations are included. Thus the QTBA is an extension of the quasiparticle random phase approximation to include quasiparticle-phonon coupling. Calculational formulas are presented in case of neutral excitations in the spherically symmetric system. The main equations are written in the coordinate representation that allows to take into account single-particle continuum completely. The E1 photoabsorption cross sections have been calculated in nuclei 116,120,124Sn. It has been obtained that the 2q*phonon configurations provide noticeable fragmentation of the giant dipole resonance resulting in appearance of significant spreading width. The results are compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA), which extends the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) by including two-quasiparticle (2q) and 2q⊗phonon configurations, can describe the fragmentation and spreading width of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) in open-shell nuclei, but the results depend on the specific choice of zero-range effective interaction and the BCS approximation for pairing.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the effective response function in the coordinate representation, using a correlated propagator that includes 2q and 2q⊗phonon configurations. They adopted zero-range Landau-Migdal forces as the effective interaction and the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approximation for pairing, then reduced the linear response matrix equation to a system of coupled channel equations for particle-hole, particle-particle, and hole-hole excitations in spherically symmetric systems. The E1 photoabsorption cross sections were calculated for the even-even tin isotopes 116Sn, 120Sn, and 124Sn.

## Key numbers

- For 116Sn, 120Sn, and 124Sn, the E1 photoabsorption cross sections were calculated within both QRPA and QTBA, and compared with available experimental data.
- The QTBA calculation produced a noticeable fragmentation of the GDR, leading to a significant spreading width, whereas the QRPA alone did not reproduce this width.
- The paper reports that the 2q⊗phonon configurations provide the mechanism for the spreading width, but no explicit numerical values for cross sections, resonance energies, or widths are given in the provided text.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) formulated by Tselyaev (2005) to include pairing correlations and applies it to open-shell nuclei for the first time. It builds on earlier models that go beyond the QRPA by including quasiparticle-phonon coupling, such as those reviewed by Kamerdzhiev et al. (1997) and the method of chronological decoupling of diagrams (MCDD) developed by Tselyaev (1989). The results enable future calculations of giant resonances in other open-shell nuclei and provide a framework for incorporating continuum effects and spreading widths in a fully microscopic approach.
