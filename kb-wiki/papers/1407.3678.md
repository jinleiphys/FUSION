# Strong P invariance, neutron EDM and minimal Left-Right parity at LHC

> In the minimal Left-Right model the choice of left-right symmetry is twofold: either generalized parity $\mathcal P$ or charge conjugation $\mathcal C$. In the minimal model with spontaneously broken strict $\mathcal P$, a large tree-level contribution to strong CP violation can be computed in terms of the spontaneous phase $α$. Searches for the neutron electric dipole moments then constrain the size of $α$. Following the latest update on indirect CP violation in the kaon sector, a bound on $W_R$ mass at $20 \text{TeV}$ is set. Possible ways out of this bound, either by an additional relaxation mechanism or explicit breaking of $\mathcal P$ are considered. To this end, the chiral loop of the neutron electric dipole moment at next-to-leading order is re-computed and provides an estimate of the weak contribution. Combining this constraint with other CP violating observables in the kaon sector allows for $M_{W_R} \gtrsim 3 \text{TeV}$. On the other hand, $\mathcal C$-symmetry is free from such constraints, leaving the right-handed scale within the experimental reach.

## Key claim

In the minimal Left-Right model with spontaneously broken generalized parity (P), the neutron electric dipole moment forces the spontaneous phase α to be extremely small, which then conflicts with the need for a large α to accommodate indirect CP violation in kaons at TeV-scale W_R masses; this tension can be avoided by introducing a Peccei-Quinn axion or by explicitly breaking P in the strong sector.

## Method

The authors computed the tree-level contribution to the strong CP phase θ̅ in terms of the spontaneous phase α and the ratio of Higgs vacuum expectation values v₂/v₁, using the hermiticity condition on Yukawa matrices imposed by P symmetry. They re-calculated the neutron EDM from chiral loops at next-to-leading order using relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory with extended-on-mass-shell subtraction, obtaining the CP-violating pion-nucleon couplings from both θ̅ and the left-right current-current operator. The bound on the W_R mass was then derived by combining the neutron EDM constraint with the latest update on indirect CP violation ε_K in the kaon sector, including heavy Higgs contributions to the effective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM experimental limit: d_n^exp < 2.9 × 10⁻²⁶ e cm (Baker 2006)
- θ̅ limit from neutron EDM: θ̅ < 1.5 × 10⁻¹⁰ (adopting Borasoy 2000, Ottnad 2009)
- Bound on spontaneous phase: sin α · t₂β ≲ 10⁻¹¹
- Lower bound on W_R mass from combined P and nEDM and ε_K constraints: M_WR ≳ 20 TeV (for favorable sign choice s_c s_t = -1)
- Lower bound on W_R mass when Peccei-Quinn axion is added: M_WR ≳ 3 TeV
- Chiral loop coupling from θ̅: ḡ₊ = -ḡ_n ≈ 0.05 θ̅
- Chiral loop coupling from LR current-current operator: ḡ₊ ≈ -10⁻⁷ c_LR, ḡ_n ≈ 2.7 × 10⁻⁵ c_LR
- One-loop quantum correction to θ̅: δθ̅_1loop ≲ 10⁻¹⁴
- Two-loop quantum correction to θ̅: δθ̅_2loop ≲ 10⁻¹³

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the minimal Left-Right symmetric model (LRSM) by Maiezza (2010) and Bertolini (2014), who showed that kaon and B physics allow W_R masses within LHC reach. The authors resolve a discrepancy with earlier chiral loop calculations by He (1989) and Xu (2010) that overestimated the weak contribution to the neutron EDM, finding instead that the strong CP contribution from the spontaneous phase dominates by roughly seven orders of magnitude. The result enables future searches: for the P-symmetric case, the 20 TeV bound pushes the right-handed scale beyond LHC reach unless a relaxation mechanism is invoked, while the C-symmetric case remains testable at LHC with W_R masses around 3 TeV.
