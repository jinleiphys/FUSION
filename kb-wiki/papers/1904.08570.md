# Effective gauge theories of superfluidity with topological order

> We discuss the low-energy dynamics of superfluidity with topological order in $(3+1)$ spacetime dimensions. We generalize a topological $BF$ theory by introducing a non-square $K$ matrix, and this generalized $BF$ theory can describe massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons and anyonic statistics between vortices and quasiparticles. We discuss the general structure of discrete and continuous higher-form symmetries in this theory, which can be used to classify quantum phases. We describe how to identify the appearance of topological order in such systems and discuss its relation to a mixed 't Hooft anomaly between discrete higher-form symmetries. We apply this framework to the color-flavor locked phase of dense QCD, which shows anyonic particle-vortex statistics while no topological order appears. An explicit example of superfluidity with topological order is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general low-energy effective field theory for superfluids that can also exhibit topological order in (3+1) dimensions, using a generalized BF theory with a non-square K matrix. The authors show that topological order appears only when the discrete higher-form symmetries are spontaneously broken, and they find that the color-flavor locked phase of dense QCD exhibits anyonic particle-vortex statistics but no topological order despite having such statistics.

## Method

The authors start from a gauged Abelian-Higgs model with multiple U(1) symmetries and dynamical gauge fields, then perform an Abelian dual transformation to obtain a generalized BF theory with a non-square integer-valued K matrix. They classify the massless and massive sectors by analyzing the kernel and cokernel of the K matrix, identify the continuous and discrete higher-form symmetries of the effective action, and compute the braiding phase between test particles and vortices using the correlation function of Wilson loops and vortex operators.

## Key numbers

* The mass matrix for the 2-form gauge fields b_i is (M^b)^2 = (1/4π^2)(G^b)^{-1/2} K (G^a)^{-1} K^T (G^b)^{-1/2}
* The mass matrix for the 1-form gauge fields a_A is (M^a)^2 = (1/4π^2)(G^a)^{-1/2} K^T (G^b)^{-1} K (G^a)^{-1/2}
* The number of massless Nambu-Goldstone modes is |\barα| = dim(coker K), and the number of massless photons is |α| = dim(ker K)
* The Fredholm index theorem gives |\barα| - |α| = |i| - |A|, where |i| is the number of scalar fields and |A| is the number of gauge fields
* The braiding phase between a test particle of charge q and a vortex of charge p is exp[2πi (q^T Q K^+ R p)], where Lk(S,C) is the linking number

## Context

This work extends the topological BF theory description of superconductors (where the K matrix is square and invertible) to the case of superfluids with possible topological order, generalizing the framework of Wen (1992) for Abelian anyons. The authors apply their formalism to the color-flavor locked phase of dense QCD, following up on their previous work (Hirono 2018), and provide an explicit example of a superfluid with genuine topological order. The framework enables classification of quantum phases based on higher-form symmetries and their mixed 't Hooft anomalies, and can be applied to study vortex continuity between nuclear superfluids and color superconductors as discussed by Alford (2018) and Chatterjee (2018).
