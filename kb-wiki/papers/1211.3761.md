# Dilepton production at SIS energies with the GiBUU transport model

> We present dilepton spectra from nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS energies, which were simulated with the GiBUU transport model in a resonance-model approach. These spectra are compared to the data published by the HADES collaboration. We argue that the interpretation of dilepton spectra at SIS energies critically depends on the couplings between the ρ meson and the baryonic resonances.

## Key claim

The interpretation of dilepton spectra at SIS energies critically depends on the couplings between the rho meson and the baryonic resonances; the GiBUU transport model provides a reasonable description of C+C collisions but significantly underestimates the Ar+KCl data in the intermediate mass region when using vacuum spectral functions, suggesting possible in-medium effects.

## Method

The authors used the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) hadronic transport model with a resonance-model description of NN and pi N collisions to simulate dilepton spectra from C+C collisions at 1.0 and 2.0 AGeV and Ar+KCl collisions at 1.756 AGeV. The simulations included angular distributions for baryon resonance production via NN -> NR using d sigma/dt proportional to 1/t, a form factor for the Delta Dalitz decay from Ramalho (2012), and soft-photon approximation for pi N Bremsstrahlung. All spectra were filtered through the HADES acceptance filter with a dilepton opening angle cut of theta_ee > 9 degrees and single-lepton momentum cuts matching experimental analysis.

## Key numbers

- C+C collisions at 1.0 AGeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.32 GeV): dilepton mass spectrum shows underestimation of data around 300-400 MeV
- C+C collisions at 2.0 AGeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.70 GeV): excellent description of data, with only slight overestimation in the omega mass region
- Ar+KCl collisions at 1.756 AGeV (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.61 GeV): model underestimates data in the intermediate mass range of 150 to 500 MeV
- Single-lepton momentum cuts: 0.05 to 1.8 GeV for C+C, 0.10 to 1.1 GeV for Ar+KCl
- Maximum impact parameters: 5.0 fm for C+C, 6.5 fm for Ar+KCl
- Dilepton opening angle cut: theta_ee > 9 degrees applied in all cases

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model previously applied to elementary HADES and DLS data (Weil, 2012) to nucleus-nucleus collisions measured by the HADES collaboration. The results address the "DLS puzzle" where earlier DLS data showed excess yield that could not be explained by in-medium effects, and which HADES has now confirmed. The paper shows that while C+C collisions can be treated as a superposition of elementary NN collisions (consistent with Agakishiev, 2009), the Ar+KCl data shows an excess over the elementary reference cocktail that may indicate medium effects such as broadening of the rho meson spectral function or modifications of nucleon resonances like the D13(1520). This enables future investigations of whether secondary collisions (e.g., pi N Bremsstrahlung) or in-medium spectral modifications can account for the missing yield, with the prospect of observing even larger effects in Au+Au systems.
