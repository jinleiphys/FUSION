# Evidence of Double Phonon Excitations in ^{16}O + ^{208}Pb Reaction

> The fusion cross-sections for ^{16}O + ^{208}Pb, measured to high precision, enable the extraction of the distribution of fusion barriers. This shows a structure markedly different from the single-barrier which might be expected for fusion of two doubly-closed shell nuclei. The results of exact coupled channel calculations performed to understand the observations are presented. These calculations indicate that coupling to a double octupole phonon excited state in ^{208}Pb is necessary to explain the experimental barrier distributions.

# Evidence of Double Phonon Excitations in ^{16}O + ^{208}Pb Reaction

## Key claim

The fusion barrier distribution extracted from high-precision fusion cross-section measurements for the ^{16}O + ^{208}Pb system shows a structure that cannot be reproduced by coupled-channel calculations including only single-phonon excitations in ^{208}Pb; inclusion of a double octupole phonon (3^- ‚äó 3^-) excited state in ^{208}Pb is necessary to reproduce the observed barrier distribution, though a lower-energy barrier component remains unexplained.

## Method

Fusion cross-sections were measured using pulsed ^{16}O beams from the 14UD Pelletron accelerator, with fission fragments detected in large-area position-sensitive multiwire proportional counters and evaporation residues collected on catcher foils and identified via alpha-decay activity. The total fusion cross-section was obtained by summing fission and evaporation residue cross-sections, and the barrier distribution was extracted as d¬≤(EŌÉ)/dE¬≤ using a point-difference formula with a step length of 1.86 MeV in the center-of-mass frame. ## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channel analyses by Thompson et al. (1989), which could not reproduce the fusion cross-section and mean square angular momentum for the ^{16}O + ^{208}Pb system despite including the lowest 2‚Āļ, 3‚ĀĽ, and 5‚ĀĽ states of ^{208}Pb, the 3‚ĀĽ state of ^{16}O, and various transfer channels. The experimental observation of double octupole phonon excitations in ^{208}Pb by Yeh et al. (1996) motivated the inclusion of the 3‚ĀĽ ‚äó 3‚ĀĽ state in the present calculations. The results demonstrate that even for reactions between doubly closed-shell nuclei, complex surface vibrations such as two-phonon states affect fusion dynamics, and the remaining discrepancies suggest that anharmonicities of the two-phonon state or other subtle effects require further theoretical investigation.
