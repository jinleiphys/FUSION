# Two-hadron correlations in the Color Glass Condensate formalism

> Two-hadron correlations are a sensitive probe of the dynamics of gluon saturation and the Color Glass Condensate formalism where the degrees of freedom are Wilson lines of the gluon field. It is shown that unlike structure functions in DIS and single hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions, higher point functions of Wilson lines appear in two-hadron production cross section. We derive equations for the energy ($x$) evolution of these higher point functions using the JIMWLK Hamiltonian and show that dipole approximation, employed in the literature to fit the di-hadron data measured by the STAR collaboration in the forward rapidity region, breaks down. This necessitates an investigation of the full hierarchy of the JIMWLK evolution equations for these higher point functions and their solutions. This can then be used to make a quantitative analysis of the di-hadron correlations in the forward rapidity region in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC and in the long range rapidity correlations observed in proton-proton collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Unlike structure functions in DIS and single hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions, two-hadron production cross sections involve higher-point functions of Wilson lines; the dipole approximation commonly used to fit di-hadron data from the STAR collaboration in the forward rapidity region breaks down under JIMWLK evolution, meaning a quantitative understanding of forward-rapidity two-hadron correlations from the Color Glass Condensate formalism is still lacking.

## Method

The paper derives the JIMWLK evolution equations for the 4-point and 6-point functions of Wilson lines that appear in the two-hadron (quark+gluon) production cross section in deuteron-gold collisions. It then shows that the leading-\(N_c\) dipole approximation, which replaces averages of products of many Wilson lines by products of two-point functions, misses many leading-\(N_c\) contributions to these evolution equations. The analysis also derives the evolution equation for the product of four color-charge densities \(\rho\) in the dilute regime, relevant for two-gluon production in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale \(Q_s^2(x)\) is defined by the condition \(\frac{\alpha_s \, xG(x, Q^2, b_t)}{S_\perp Q^2} \sim 1\); for a nucleus it scales as \(A^{1/3}\).
- The number of \(N_c^2\)-suppressed terms in the evolution equation is 8 for the 4-point function \(O_4\) and \(\sim 20\) for the 6-point function \(O_6\).
- The gluon distribution function grows as \((1/x)^\lambda\) with \(\lambda \sim 0.3\) from resummation of \(\ln 1/x\) corrections.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate formalism beyond single-inclusive observables (e.g., DIS structure functions and single hadron production, described by the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation) to two-hadron correlations. It directly contradicts the dipole approximation used by Albacete and Marquet (2010) to fit STAR di-hadron data, showing that approximation misses leading-\(N_c\) terms. The derived evolution equations for higher-point functions are necessary for quantitative analysis of di-hadron correlations in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC and long-range rapidity correlations observed in proton-proton collisions at the LHC by the CMS collaboration.
