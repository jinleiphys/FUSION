# New insight into $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ process at thermal energy with pionless effective field theory

> We take a new look at the neutron radiative capture by a deuteron at thermal energy with the pionless effective field theory (EFT($π\!\!\!/$)) approach. We present in detail the calculation of $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ amplitudes for incoming doublet and quartet channels leading to the formation of a triton fully in the projection method based on the cluster-configuration space approach. In the present work, we consider all possible one-body and two-body photon interaction diagrams. In fact, additional diagrams that make significant changes in the results of the calculation of the total cross section in the $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ process are included in this study. The properly normalized triton wave function is calculated and taken into consideration. We compare the cross section of the dominant magnetic M1-transition of $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ up to next-to-next-to-leading order $\textrm{N}^2\textrm{LO}$ with the results of the previous model-dependent theoretical calculations and experimental data. The more acceptable results for cross section $σ^{(2)}_{tot}=0.297\;(\textrm{LO})+0.124\;(\textrm{NLO})+0.048\;(\textrm{N}^2\textrm{LO})=[0.469\pm0.033]\:\textrm{mb}$ show order by order convergence and cutoff independence. No three-body currents are needed to renormalize observables up to $\textrm{N}^2\textrm{LO}$ in this process.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the total cross section for the neutron radiative capture on a deuteron at thermal energy using pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO), finding that the inclusion of additional one-body and two-body photon interaction diagrams not considered in prior EFT(π̸) calculations yields a result of 0.469 ± 0.033 mb that shows order-by-order convergence and cutoff independence, with no three-body currents required for renormalization up to N²LO.

## Method

The authors computed the M1 transition amplitudes for the nd → ³Hγ process at thermal energy (2.5×10⁻⁸ MeV) using the projection operator method in cluster-configuration space within pionless effective field theory. They included all possible one-body and two-body photon interaction diagrams up to N²LO, incorporating diagrams with radiation from external nucleon legs, external deuteron legs, on-shell two-body bubbles, and the ³S₁ → ³S₁ M1 transition, and used a properly normalized triton wave function derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section at N²LO: σ⁽²⁾_tot = 0.297 (LO) + 0.124 (NLO) + 0.048 (N²LO) = [0.469 ± 0.033] mb
- Experimental cross section for comparison: σ_exp_tot = 0.508 ± 0.015 mb (Jurney et al.)
- Deuteron binding momentum: γ_t = 45.7025 MeV
- Triton binding energy: B_t = 8.48 MeV
- Singlet scattering length: a_s = -23.714 fm
- Triplet effective range: ρ_d = 1.764 fm
- Singlet effective range: r_0 = 2.73 fm
- Neutron radiative capture cross section np → dγ at thermal energy: σ_exp_np→dγ = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb
- Two-body coupling constants: L₁ = -4.427 ± 0.015 fm, L₂ = -0.4 fm
- Nucleon magnetic moments: k₀ = 0.4399 (isoscalar), k₁ = 2.35294 (isovector)

## Context

This work extends prior EFT(π̸) calculations of the nd → ³Hγ process by Sadeghi, Bayegan, and Grieshammer by including additional diagrams (radiation from external legs, on-shell two-body bubble, and the ³S₁→³S₁ M1 transition) and using a properly normalized triton wave function. The results improve agreement with experimental data from Jurney et al. (0.508 ± 0.015 mb) and earlier model-dependent calculations by Faldt et al. and Viviani et al., demonstrating that no three-body currents are needed up to N²LO, which enables systematic extension to parity-violating radiative capture calculations at thermal energy.
