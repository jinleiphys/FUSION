# Three-body problem in heteronuclear mixtures with resonant interspecies interaction

> We use the zero-range approximation to study a system of two identical bosons interacting resonantly with a third particle. The method is derived from effective field theory. It reduces the three-body problem to an integral equation which we then solve numerically. We also develop an alternative approach which gives analytic solutions of the integral equation in coordinate representation in the limit of vanishing total energy. The atom-dimer scattering length, the rates of atom-dimer relaxation and three-body recombination to shallow and to deep molecular states are calculated either analytically or numerically with a well controlled accuracy for various energies as functions of the mass ratio, scattering length, and three-body parameter. We discuss in detail the relative positions of the recombination loss peaks, which in the universal limit depend only on the mass ratio. Our results have implications for ongoing and future experiments on Bose-Bose and Bose-Fermi atomic mixtures.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for heteronuclear mixtures with resonant interspecies interaction, the relative positions of three-body recombination loss peaks (specifically the ratios \(a_*/|a_-|\) and \(|a_-|/a_{0*}\)) depend only on the mass ratio \(\delta = m_1/m_2\) in the universal limit, independent of the three-body parameter. This universality holds under the zero-range approximation and assumes a large interspecies scattering length \(a \gg \ell_{vdW}\) with nonresonant intraspecies interactions neglected.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory with zero-range interactions, reducing the three-body problem to an integral equation for the atom-dimer scattering amplitude, which is solved numerically after projecting onto relative \(s\)-waves. They also develop an alternative analytic approach in coordinate representation by solving the Schrodinger equation with Fermi pseudopotentials at zero total energy, deriving closed-form expressions for recombination and relaxation rate constants. The three-body parameter is implemented via a cutoff \(\Lambda\) in the integral equation, and the effect of deep dimers is incorporated by analytically continuing the three-body parameter into the complex plane via \(\Lambda \rightarrow \Lambda \exp(i\eta_*/s_0)\).

## Key numbers

* For equal masses (\(\delta=1\)), the discrete scaling factor is \(\exp(\pi/s_0) \approx 1986.1\) (with \(s_0 \approx 0.4137\)), much larger than the value 22.7 for three identical bosons.
* For the \(^7\)Li-\(^{87}\)Rb-\(^{87}\)Rb system (\(\delta=0.081\)), the authors find \(s_0 = 1.523\) and their coefficients \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) for the atom-dimer scattering length agree with the Born-Oppenheimer approximation of Efremov (2009) to within 2-3%.
* For \(\eta_*=0.1\) and \(\delta=0.1\), the atom-dimer relaxation peak at the dimer breakup threshold (\(E=0\)) is smaller by a factor of 706 than the peak at the scattering threshold (\(E=-B_d\)); for \(\delta=10\) this ratio is 19.1.
* For \(\delta=0.471\) (K-Rb-Rb system), the resonance position shifts from \(a_*\) to a maximum of \(2.04 a_*\) at \(E/B_d \approx -0.25\), then back to \(|a_-| = 1.89 a_*\) at the dimer breakup threshold.

## Context

This work extends the universal theory of Efimov physics (Efimov 1970) from identical bosons to heteronuclear mixtures with two species, where only the interspecies interaction is resonant. It provides quantitative predictions for ongoing experiments on Bose-Bose mixtures such as \(^{41}\)K-\(^{87}\)Rb (Barontini 2009) and Bose-Fermi mixtures like \(^{87}\)Rb-\(^{40}\)K (Zirbel). The results enable experimental tests of discrete scaling invariance in systems with mass ratios different from unity, where the scaling factor can be significantly smaller than 22.7 (e.g., for two heavy and one light atom), making multiple Efimov resonances more accessible.
