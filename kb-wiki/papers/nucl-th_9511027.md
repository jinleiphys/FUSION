# Direct Neutron Capture for Magic-Shell Nuclei

> In neutron capture for magic--shell nuclei the direct reaction mechanism can be important and may even dominate. As an example we investigated the reaction $^{48}$Ca(n,$γ)^{49}$Ca for projectile energies below 250\,keV in a direct capture model using the folding procedure for optical and bound state potentials. The obtained theoretical cross sections are in agreement with the experimental data showing the dominance of the direct reaction mechanism in this case. The above method was also used to calculate the cross section for $^{50}$Ca(n,$γ)^{51}$Ca.

## Key claim

For magic-shell nuclei such as ⁴⁸Ca, the direct reaction mechanism can dominate neutron capture even at low projectile energies (below 250 keV), contrary to the usual expectation that compound-nucleus formation prevails below 10–20 MeV. The authors show that the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model's agreement with the measured 30 keV cross section for ⁴⁸Ca is "completely fortuitous," with 95% of the capture rate actually arising from direct processes.

## Method

The authors calculated direct neutron capture cross sections using a potential model with folding potentials for both the optical potential in the entrance channel and the bound state potentials in the exit channel. For ⁴⁸Ca(n,γ)⁴⁹Ca, the folding potential strength in the entrance channel (λᵢ = 0.93357) was determined by fitting to pure s-wave neutron scattering data with σ = 0.019 b, while exit channel strengths were fitted to reproduce experimental neutron separation energies. The direct capture cross section was computed as a sum over final states weighted by spectroscopic factors from (d,p) reaction analysis, using the code TEDCA with folding potentials from the code VOLD.

## Key numbers

- Maxwellian-averaged direct capture cross section for ⁴⁸Ca(n,γ)⁴⁹Ca at 30 keV: ⟨σᵗʰ⟩ = 1.04 mb (sum over all final states)
- Maxwellian-averaged direct capture cross section for ⁵⁰Ca(n,γ)⁵¹Ca at 30 keV: ⟨σᴰᶜ⟩ = 0.7 mb
- Thermal (0.0253 eV) direct capture cross section for ⁴⁸Ca(n,γ)⁴⁹Ca: 1.13 b, compared to experimental value (1.09 ± 0.14) b
- At 97 keV: theoretical direct capture cross section 0.58 mb, experimental (0.55 ± 0.09) mb
- Neutron separation energy Sₙ = 5.142 MeV for ⁴⁹Ca; Sₙ = 4.4 MeV for ⁵¹Ca
- Resonance capture cross section for ⁵⁰Ca(n,γ)⁵¹Ca from compound-nucleus mechanism: ⟨σᶜᴺ⟩ ≃ 8.5 × 10⁻¹⁵ mb (negligible compared to direct capture)

## Context

This work extends the direct capture model using folding potentials previously developed by Oberhummer and Staudt (1991) to magic-shell nuclei, where statistical Hauser-Feshbach models (as implemented in the SMOKER code by Cowan, Thielemann, and Truran, 1991) fail due to low level densities. The authors show that for ⁴⁸Ca, the compound-nucleus contribution from resonances (Breit-Wigner) is only about 5% of the total capture rate, contradicting earlier statistical-model calculations that gave the correct 30 keV cross section by coincidence. For ⁵⁰Ca, the direct capture cross section of 0.7 mb is comparable to that of ⁴⁸Ca, enabling the neutron-capture path to continue beyond ⁴⁸Ca in astrophysical r- and α-process calculations, whereas previously the statistical model predicted a vanishing cross section that would halt the path at ⁴⁸Ca.
