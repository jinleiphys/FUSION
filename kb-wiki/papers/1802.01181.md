# Effects of unconventional breakup modes on incomplete fusion of weakly bound nuclei

> The incomplete fusion dynamics of $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi collisions at energies above the Coulomb barrier is investigated. The classical dynamical model implemented in the {\sc platypus} code is used to understand and quantify the impact of both $^6$Li resonance states and transfer-triggered breakup modes (involving short-lived projectile-like nuclei such as $^8$Be and $^5$Li) on the formation of incomplete fusion products. Model calculations explain the experimental incomplete-fusion excitation function fairly well, indicating that (i) delayed direct breakup of $^6$Li reduces the incomplete fusion cross-sections, and (ii) the neutron-stripping channel practically determines those cross-sections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the neutron-stripping channel, producing short-lived $^5$Li, dominates the incomplete fusion cross-section in $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi collisions, contrary to most quantum mechanical models which assume direct breakup of $^6$Li is the dominant channel. The model calculations only work reliably at energies above the Coulomb barrier due to the absence of quantum tunneling in the classical treatment.

## Method

The authors used the classical dynamical model implemented in the PLATYPUS code, which employs classical trajectories with stochastic breakup via Monte-Carlo sampling of experimentally determined breakup functions. They introduced a branching-ratio parameter to distinguish between prompt (non-resonant) and delayed (resonant) breakup, and incorporated transfer-triggered breakup channels involving $^5$Li (from neutron stripping) and $^8$Be (from deuteron pickup) as initial projectiles with modified incident energies based on transfer Q-values. The Woods-Saxon nuclear potential parameters were determined by approximately matching Sao-Paulo potential barriers for each binary system.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier for $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi system: approximately 29.8 MeV
- Half-lives of $^6$Li resonant states: $4.2 \times 10^{-22}$ s ($1^+$ state) and $3.7 \times 10^{-22}$ s ($2^+$ state); typical collision time is approximately $10^{-21}$ s
- Coulomb barrier heights: alpha particle with $^{209}$Bi target approximately 21.25 MeV; deuteron with $^{209}$Bi target approximately 10.1 MeV (difference of approximately 11 MeV)
- Transfer Q-values: -1.059 MeV (neutron stripping) and 13.338 MeV (deuteron pickup)
- $^5$Li ground state: resonant alpha-proton p-state with $(E_{res},\Gamma_{res}) \equiv (1.96, 2.0)$ MeV and half-life approximately $3.3 \times 10^{-22}$ s
- $^8$Be first $2^+$ resonant state: $(E_{res},\Gamma_{res}) \equiv (3.0, 1.5)$ MeV; ground state half-life approximately $10^{-16}$ s
- $^6$Li resonant states: $(E_{res},\Gamma_{res})$ values of (4.18, 1.5) MeV ($1^+$), (2.84, 1.7) MeV ($2^+$), and (0.716, 0.024) MeV ($3^+$)
- Breakup function constants ($\alpha$, $\beta$): (-0.353738, 1.26228) for $^6$Li + $^{209}$Bi, (-0.799239, 8.03581) for $^5$Li + $^{210}$Bi, (-1.57787, 14.3403) for $^8$Be + $^{207}$Pb
- Maximal internal energy and angular momentum for sampling breakup: $\epsilon_{max}=6$ MeV and $l_{max}=4\hbar$
- Orbital angular momenta of projectiles: $L_0 \leq 100\hbar$

## Context

This work extends the classical dynamical model of Diaz-Torres et al. (PLATYPUS code) to include both resonant states of $^6$Li and transfer-triggered breakup channels, building on sub-barrier breakup measurements by Huy et al. (2013) and Kalkal et al. The results contradict the assumption in Kailas (2016) that all observed incomplete fusion products originate from $^6$Li direct breakup, and instead show the neutron-stripping channel dominates. The findings enable improved interpretation of incomplete fusion data from Dasgupta et al. and provide a framework for understanding the competition between direct breakup and transfer-triggered processes in reactions with weakly bound nuclei.
