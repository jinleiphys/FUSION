# (In-)Consistencies in the relativistic description of excited states in the Bethe-Salpeter equation

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation provides the most widely used technique to extract bound states and resonances in a relativistic Quantum Field Theory. Nevertheless a thorough discussion how to identify its solutions with physical states is still missing. The occurrence of complex eigenvalues of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation complicates this issue further. Using a perturbative expansion in the mass difference of the constituents we demonstrate for scalar fields bound by a scalar exchange that the underlying mechanism which results in complex eigenvalues is the crossing of a normal (or abnormal) with an abnormal state. Based on an investigation of the renormalization of one-particle properties we argue that these crossings happen beyond the applicability region of the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation. The implications for a fermion-antifermion bound state in QED are discussed, and a consistent interpretation of the bound state spectrum of QED is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that complex eigenvalues in the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar constituents of unequal masses arise from the coupling of normal and abnormal states of opposite relative-time parity, but argues that these crossings occur at coupling constants beyond the region where the ladder approximation with bare propagators is valid.

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative expansion in the mass difference of the constituents, decomposing the Bethe-Salpeter operator into a part for equal masses and a perturbation proportional to the mass difference. They solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation numerically in ladder approximation for scalar fields with a scalar exchange, and also derive a consistency condition from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation that must be satisfied by all solutions. The renormalization of one-particle properties is investigated using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism to determine the domain of validity of the ladder approximation.

## Key numbers

- For mass ratio delta = m1/m2 = 4 and exchange particle mass mu = m2, the first two excited states become degenerate at eta = M/(m1+m2) approximately 0.81, with complex eigenvalues appearing in the interval approximately 0.25 less than eta less than 0.81.
- The maximum exchange particle mass for which complex eigenvalues exist for the first two excited states is estimated as mu_max approximately 0.85 m1 + 1.66 m2.
- In the Wick-Cutkosky model with equal masses, abnormal states exist only for lambda = g^2/(16 pi^2 m^2) greater than lambda_c = 1/4.
- For a massless bound state (eta to 0), the eigenvalues are given by lambda = (n+kappa)(n+kappa+1) = N(N+1), where kappa = 0 for normal solutions and kappa = 1,2,... for abnormal solutions.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954) and addresses the long-standing problem of complex eigenvalues first noted by Kaufmann (1969) and later studied by Fukui and Seto (1993). The paper supports an old conjecture by Wick (1954) that abnormal solutions are likely spurious consequences of the ladder approximation. The results enable a consistent interpretation of the bound state spectrum in QED by identifying the region where the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation remains physically reliable.
