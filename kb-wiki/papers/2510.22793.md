# Baryon and electric charge stoppings in nuclear collisions and the role of strangeness

> It has been challenging to quantitatively understand the stopping of incoming nucleons in nuclear collisions, and recently it has been proposed that comparing the baryon stopping with electric charge stopping can help address the question. Here we focus on the $B/Q\times Z/A$ ratio, which can strongly depend on rapidity although its value is one for the full phase space. We find that this ratio is very sensitive to the difference between strange and anti-strange rapidity distributions (the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry), and slightly more anti-strange quarks at mid-rapidity would lead to a ratio well below one. This is the case for Zr+Zr and Ru+Ru isobar collisions at $200A$ GeV from a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. Without the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry, the AMPT model would give a mid-rapidity $B/Q\times Z/A$ ratio at or above one. In addition, the AMPT model gives $B/ΔQ\times ΔZ/A<1$ at mid-rapidity for isobar collisions at all centralities, which strongly contradicts the recent data from the STAR Collaboration. We further find that the $B/ΔQ\times ΔZ/A$ ratio is very sensitive to the net-light quark ($u,d$) stoppings, but it is less sensitive to the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry than the $B/Q\times Z/A$ ratio by a factor of 3.

# Baryon and electric charge stoppings in nuclear collisions and the role of strangeness

## Key claim

The ratio $B/Q \times Z/A$ at mid-rapidity is very sensitive to the difference between strange and anti-strange quark rapidity distributions (the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry), and having slightly more anti-strange than strange quarks at mid-rapidity leads to a ratio well below one; this sensitivity is a factor of 3 stronger than for the isobar difference ratio $B/\Delta Q \times \Delta Z/A$, which instead depends primarily on net-light quark ($u,d$) stoppings.

## Method

The authors use the string melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, which includes initial conditions from HIJING1, partonic interactions, quark coalescence for hadronization, and a hadronic cascade, with a modification to remove an artifact in the ordering of nucleons by z-coordinate that affected initial electric charge stopping. They simulate Zr+Zr and Ru+Ru isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and compute the rapidity distributions of net-baryon number and net-electric charge at multiple evolution stages (initial partons, final partons, initial hadrons, final hadrons), also testing a modified version (AMPTsym) where initial strange and anti-strange quark distributions are symmetrized.

## Key numbers

- The $B/Q \times Z/A$ ratio at mid-rapidity from the normal AMPT-SM model is well below unity for minimum bias Zr+Zr collisions at $200A$ GeV, across all evolution stages.
- The $s-\bar s$ asymmetry at mid-rapidity ($f_{\bar s}/f_s - 1$) decreases from $\sim 9\%$ in peripheral collisions to $\sim 7\%$ in central collisions.
- The net-electric charge from light quarks in Ru+Ru collisions at mid-rapidity is about $20\%$ higher than that from Zr+Zr collisions, rather than the $10\%$ naively expected from $\Delta Z$.
- The $B/\Delta Q \times \Delta Z/A$ ratio from both the normal AMPT-SM and AMPTsym models is well below unity at mid-rapidity for all centralities, while STAR data are higher by a factor of 2 or more.
- The $B/Q \times Z/A$ ratio is more sensitive to the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry than the $B/\Delta Q \times \Delta Z/A$ ratio by a factor of 3.

## Context

This work extends recent studies using transport models such as UrQMD (Lewis 2022, Lv 2023) and AMPT (Lin 2024) that found $B/Q \times Z/A < 1$ at mid-rapidity, and it identifies the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry as the primary cause. The paper directly addresses the STAR Collaboration (2024) measurement of $B/\Delta Q \times \Delta Z/A$ in isobar collisions, showing that current models (AMPT, UrQMD) all give values well below the data, leaving an open puzzle about baryon versus electric charge stopping. The results enable future work to include neutron skin effects (Xu 2021, Pihan 2024) and deformations of isobar nuclei, and they motivate experimental measurements of net-baryon and net-electric charge from different hadron species to constrain the unmeasured $B/Q \times Z/A$ ratio.
