# Formation of an ordered phase in neutron star matter

> In this work, we explore the possible formation of ordered phases in hadronic matter, related to the presence of hyperons at high densities. We analyze a microscopic mechanism which can lead to the crystallization of the hyperonic sector by the confinement of the hyperons on the nodes of a lattice. For this purpose, we introduce a simplified model of the hadronic plasma, in which the nuclear interaction between protons, neutrons and hyperons is mediated by meson fields. We find that, for some reasonable sets of values of the model parameters, such ordered phases are energetically favoured as density increases beyond a threshold value.

## Key claim

For some reasonable sets of model parameters, an ordered phase where hyperons are confined to the nodes of a cubic lattice becomes energetically favored over the standard Fermi liquid configuration as baryonic density increases beyond a threshold, but the existence and stability of such ordered states is sensitive to the choice of poorly known hyperonic coupling constants and the level of description of the nuclear interaction.

## Method

The authors introduce a simplified relativistic phenomenological Lagrangian model (Walecka-type) where protons, neutrons, and one species of neutral hyperon (Lambda) interact through scalar sigma and vector omega meson exchange. They compare two ground state configurations at fixed baryonic density under beta equilibrium: a standard Fermi liquid where all baryons form uniform Fermi fluids, and an ordered phase where pairs of hyperons with antiparallel spins occupy the ground state of harmonic oscillator potentials centered on the nodes of a cubic lattice, with the nucleons treated as a uniform Fermi fluid in the spatially averaged mean fields. The self-consistent oscillator frequency is determined from the curvature of the lattice potential generated by the gaussian hyperon clouds, and the relative stability is evaluated by comparing total energy densities.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.197 fm^{-3}, with binding energy Eb = -15.77 MeV, obtained at Fermi momentum pf0 = 1.42 fm^{-1}
- Threshold density for hyperon creation: nt = 1.75 n0 (set A), nt = 1.79 n0 (set B), nt = 1.72 n0 (set C)
- For set B (including first-order corrections but no form factors): liquid phase more stable from threshold up to density 4.3 n0, where a transition to the ordered phase occurs
- For set C (including form factors with Lambda_sigma = 1.5 GeV, Lambda_omega = 2.0 GeV): liquid phase more stable only in narrow range between threshold and n ~ 2.2 n0
- Hyperon coupling ratios used: set A (x_sigma = 0.45, x_omega = 0.483), set B (x_sigma = 0.5, x_omega = 0.546), set C (x_sigma = 0.4, x_omega = 0.42)
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV; nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV; hyperon mass m_h = m_Lambda = 1115 MeV
- Meson-nucleon coupling constants: g_sigmaN^2/4pi = 7.29, g_omegaN^2/4pi = 10.83
- Ratio of intra-cell interaction energy to potential well depth remains less than 0.2 for sets B and C, reaching 0.4 at n ~ 5 n0 for set A

## Context

This work extends earlier considerations of solid phases in neutron star matter, including pion condensation (Japanese authors, 1970s), proton confinement by repulsive n-p interactions (Kutschera), and ordering in mixed quark-hadron phases (Glendenning). The authors build on the extensive study of hyperonic matter in relativistic mean field models (Lattimer) and the possibility of H-dibaryon bound states (Jaffe, 1977; Glendenning and Faessler). The results suggest that if such ordered phases exist, they could have observable consequences for neutron star glitches (braking index discontinuities), gravitational wave emission from triaxial deformation, and neutrino cooling (Haensel, Prakash). The simplified model serves as a laboratory for developing techniques to analyze ordered configurations, with more realistic treatments of the nucleon response to the lattice potential deferred to future work.
