# Amplification of Quantum Meson Modes in the Late Time of the Chiral Phase Transition

> We investigate the time evolution of the quantum meson modes in the late time of chiral phase transition. In particular, it is shown that there exists a possible solution to the equation of motion for the quantum meson modes, which reveals a parametric resonance and/or resonance through forced oscillation induced by the small oscillation of the chiral condensate. After that, we demonstrate the unstable regions for the quantum meson modes in both the cases of a uniform and spatially expanding system.

# Amplification of Quantum Meson Modes in the Late Time of the Chiral Phase Transition

## Key claim

The authors show that quantum pion modes can be amplified in the late time of the chiral phase transition through forced oscillation and beat phenomena, not only through parametric resonance, provided the chiral condensate oscillates with small amplitude around its vacuum value. The amplification occurs only for the lowest and first excited pion modes (n=0 and n=1), while sigma meson modes and higher pion modes are not amplified.

## Method

The authors use the time-dependent variational approach (TDVA) with a Gaussian wave functional in the functional Schrodinger picture, applied to the O(4) linear sigma model. They solve the coupled equations of motion for the chiral condensate and quantum meson mode functions numerically in a box of side length 10 fm with periodic boundary conditions, including momentum modes up to a cutoff of approximately 990 MeV. The stability of solutions is determined by comparing time integrals of mode function magnitudes over successive intervals.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters are fixed to reproduce vacuum values: pion mass M_pi = 138 MeV, sigma meson mass M_0 = 500 MeV, and pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, using m^2 = -(519.70 MeV)^2, lambda = 84.96, and h = (78.13 MeV)^3.
- The box normalization uses length L = 10 fm in each direction, with momentum cutoff Lambda approximately 990 MeV corresponding to including modes up to n^2 = 8^2.
- The initial chiral condensate is set to bar_varphi_0(t=0) = 40 MeV with zero initial time derivative.
- For the uniform system, stability is judged by time integration over 10 fm/c, evaluated up to 100 fm/c.
- For the expanding system, the time integration interval is 4 fm/c, evaluated up to 40 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of dynamical chiral phase transition in the O(4) linear sigma model using the Gaussian wave functional approximation, building on prior variational approaches by the same authors and collaborators (Watanabe and Tsue, 2006). The paper introduces forced oscillation as an additional amplification mechanism beyond parametric resonance, which was previously studied in this framework. The results suggest that in realistic heavy-ion collisions, the amplification of pion modes depends sensitively on initial conditions and whether the system parameters fall into unstable regions, with spatial expansion suppressing the forced oscillation effect due to strong damping.
