# Proton-proton scattering above 3 GeV/c

> A large set of data on proton-proton differential cross sections, analyzing powers and the double polarization parameter A_NN is analyzed employing the Regge formalism. We find that the data available at proton beam momenta from 3 GeV/c to 50 GeV/c exhibit features that are very well in line with the general characteristics of Regge phenomenology and can be described with a model that includes the rho, omega, f_2, and a_2 trajectories and single Pomeron exchange. Additional data, specifically for spin-dependent observables at forward angles, would be very helpful for testing and refining our Regge model.

# Proton-proton scattering above 3 GeV/c

## Key claim
The Regge formalism, including rho, omega, f2, and a2 trajectories plus single Pomeron exchange, can describe proton-proton differential cross sections, analyzing powers, and the double polarization parameter A_NN for beam momenta from 3 GeV/c to 50 GeV/c, though the description is limited by the lack of forward-angle spin-dependent data and shows approximately 20% discrepancies with some lower-energy data sets due to normalization differences between experiments.

## Method
The authors constructed a Regge model with five independent s-channel helicity amplitudes for proton-proton scattering, incorporating the rho, omega, f2, and a2 Regge trajectories and a single Pomeron pole. They performed a global fit to differential cross sections, analyzing powers, and the double polarization parameter A_NN using data from 3 GeV/c to 50 GeV/c, with the trajectories taken from previous analyses of pion charge-exchange and photoproduction reactions, and the Pomeron parameters from Donnachie (1984, 1986, 1992). The fit included 30 free parameters for the coupling constants and form factors, with the asymptotic relations phi_1 = phi_3 and phi_2 = -phi_4 imposed to reduce the number of free parameters.

## Key numbers
- Beam momentum range analyzed: 3 GeV/c to 50 GeV/c, corresponding to invariant collision energies 2.77 < sqrt(s) < 10 GeV
- Pomeron trajectory: alpha_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25 t
- Rho trajectory: alpha_rho(t) = 0.53 + 0.8 t
- Omega trajectory: alpha_omega(t) = 0.64 + 0.8 t
- f2 trajectory: alpha_f2(t) = 0.71 + 0.83 t
- a2 trajectory: alpha_a2(t) = 0.45 + 0.91 t
- Chi-squared per data point: 1.69
- Average discrepancy between model and some lower-energy data: approximately 20%
- Pomeron coupling constant c_1 = -10.9 +/- 2.3 GeV*sqrt(mb)
- Pomeron coupling constant c_2 = 524 +/- 41 GeV*sqrt(mb)
- Pomeron coupling constant c_5 = -0.98 +/- 0.15 GeV*sqrt(mb)
- Pomeron slope parameter b_1 = 3.8 +/- 0.14 GeV^{-2}
- Pomeron slope parameter b_2 = 7.45 +/- 0.42 GeV^{-2}
- Pomeron slope parameter b_5 = 2.67 +/- 0.25 GeV^{-2}

## Context
This work extends the Regge formalism approach previously applied by the same group to pion-nucleon scattering (Sibirtsev 2007, 2009; Huang 2009) and addresses the failure of relativistic meson-exchange models above 2 GeV/c, as documented by Eyser (2004) and Machleidt (2001). The analysis covers the energy region between the COSY-EDDA and SATURNE experiments (up to approximately 3.8 GeV/c) and higher momenta up to 50 GeV/c, where traditional nuclear models cannot be extended but perturbative QCD is not yet applicable. The results enable future tests and refinements using upcoming data from the ANKE Collaboration at COSY, which will provide precision measurements at forward angles (10 to 30 degrees) for beam momenta from 2.4 to 3.6 GeV/c.
