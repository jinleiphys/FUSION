# Doubly magic nuclei from Lattice QCD forces at $M_{PS}=$469 MeV/c$^2$

> We perform ab initio self-consistent Green's function calculations of the closed shell nuclei $^{\rm 4}$He, $^{\rm 16}$O and $^{\rm 40}$Ca, based on two-nucleon potentials derived from Lattice QCD simulations, in the flavor SU(3) limit and at the pseudo-scalar meson mass of 469~MeV/c$^{\rm 2}$. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is obtained using the HAL QCD method and its short-distance repulsion is treated by means of ladder resummations outside the model space. Our results show that this approach diagonalises ultraviolet degrees of freedom correctly. Therefore, ground state energies can be obtained from infrared extrapolations even for the relatively hard potentials of HAL QCD. Comparing to previous Brueckner Hartree-Fock calculations, the total binding energies are sensibly improved by the full account of many-body correlations. The results suggest an interesting possible behaviour in which nuclei are unbound at very large pion masses and islands of stability appear at first around the traditional doubly-magic numbers when the pion mass is lowered toward its physical value. The calculated one-nucleon spectral distributions are qualitatively close to those of real nuclei even for the pseudo-scalar meson mass considered here.

## Key claim

The authors perform ab initio self-consistent Green's function calculations for doubly magic nuclei using a Lattice QCD-derived nucleon-nucleon potential at a pseudo-scalar meson mass of 469 MeV/c^2, finding that the approach can handle the relatively hard HAL QCD potentials through ladder resummations, but the results suggest that at this large pion mass only ^4He and ^40Ca are bound while ^16O is likely unstable against breakup into alpha particles, implying a transition region near M_PS ~ 500 MeV/c^2 where bound nuclei first emerge.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) method in the third-order algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(3)] approximation within a spherical harmonic oscillator model space, combined with Bethe-Goldstone equation (BGE) resummations of ladder diagrams in the excluded Hilbert space to handle the short-range repulsion of the HAL QCD potential. The HAL469 interaction (derived from Lattice QCD in the flavor SU(3) limit at M_PS = 469 MeV/c^2) was used, including only the ^1S_0, ^3S_1 and ^3D_1 partial waves, with a nucleon mass of m_N = 1161.1 MeV/c^2. Ground state energies were obtained from infrared extrapolations using the exponential convergence formula E_0^A = E_inf + C exp(-2 k_inf L_2) based on the effective box radius L_2.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ^4He: -4.80(0.03) MeV from the T^BGE(omega)+ADC(3) approach, compared to -5.09 MeV from exact Stochastic Variational calculations (benchmark deviation less than 10%)
- Ground state energy of ^16O: -17.9 (0.3) (1.8) MeV from T^BGE(omega)+ADC(3), compared to -34.7 MeV from BHF and -127.7 MeV experimentally
- Ground state energy of ^40Ca: -75.4 (6.7) (7.5) MeV from T^BGE(omega)+ADC(3), compared to -112.7 MeV from BHF and -342.0 MeV experimentally
- Breakup energy for ^16O into 4-alpha clusters: -2.46 (0.3) (1.8) MeV (unstable)
- Breakup energy for ^40Ca into alpha clusters: 24.5 (6.7) (7.5) MeV (stable)
- Charge radius of ^4He: 1.89 fm (experiment: 1.67 fm)
- Charge radius of ^16O: 2.79 fm (experiment: 2.73 fm)
- Charge radius of ^40Ca: 3.10 fm (experiment: 3.48 fm)
- Nucleon mass used: m_N = 1161.1 MeV/c^2
- Pseudo-scalar meson mass: M_PS = 469 MeV/c^2

## Context

This work extends previous Brueckner Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations by Inoue (2013, 2014) on the same HAL QCD potentials, finding that the full account of many-body correlations in SCGF yields sensibly less bound ground states than BHF, which the authors interpret as a limitation of BHF theory for finite nuclei. The approach enables future studies of Lattice QCD-derived potentials at lower pion masses, with LQCD simulations at nearly physical quark masses currently in progress by Doi (2015), Ishii (2016), Sasaki (2016), and Nemura (2016). The results suggest that M_PS ~ 500 MeV/c^2 marks a transition between an unbound nuclear chart and the emergence of bound isotopes, with traditional magic numbers appearing first as the pion mass is lowered toward its physical value.
