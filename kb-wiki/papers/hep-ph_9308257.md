# Parton Interaction Rates in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The transport interaction rates of elastic scattering processes of thermal partons in the quark-gluon plasma are calculated beyond the leading logarithm approximation using the effective perturbation theory for QCD at finite temperatures developed by Braaten and Pisarski. The results for the ordinary and transport interaction rates obtained from the effective perturbation theory are compared to perturbative approximations based on an infrared cut-off by the Debye screening mass. The relevance of those interaction rates for a quark-gluon plasma possibly formed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

The transport interaction rates for elastic scattering of thermal partons in the quark-gluon plasma are calculated beyond the leading logarithm approximation using the Braaten-Pisarski effective perturbation theory, but the results become negative for realistic values of the strong coupling constant (αs > 0.2), making them unphysical for application to ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Method

The author computes both ordinary and transport interaction rates for elastic scattering processes (gg→gg, gq→gq, qq→qq) using the Braaten-Pisarski resummation method, which replaces bare gluon propagators with effective ones containing hard thermal loops. For the transport rates, the Braaten-Yuan method is employed, separating soft (q < q*) and hard (q > q*) momentum transfer contributions, with the soft part using resummed propagators and the hard part using bare Green's functions, ensuring cancellation of the arbitrary separation scale q*. The transport cross section includes a weight factor proportional to sin²θ instead of 1−cosθ to regulate u-channel divergences and restore t-u symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal part of the quark interaction rate: Γq^l = 1.098 CF αs T, where CF = 4/3
- Transverse part of the quark interaction rate: Γq^t = CF αs T ln(κ/αs), with κ an undetermined coefficient
- Estimated total quark interaction rate: Γq ≈ (2 ± 1) CF αs T for αs ≈ 0.3
- Gluon interaction rate: Γg = (CA/CF) Γq = (9/4) Γq
- Gluon relaxation time: τg ≈ (0.5 ± 0.3) fm/c at T = 300 MeV and αs = 0.3
- Quark relaxation time: τq ≈ (1.0 ± 0.5) fm/c at T = 300 MeV and αs = 0.3
- Gluon transport rate in pure gluon plasma: Γg,trans(Nf=0) = (24π αs² T³/s) ln(0.25/αs) ≈ 5.2 αs² T ln(0.25/αs)
- Gluon transport rate in QGP with two flavors: Γg,trans(Nf=2) ≈ 6.6 αs² T ln(0.17/αs)
- Quark transport rate in QGP with two flavors: Γq,trans(Nf=2) ≈ 2.5 αs² T ln(0.14/αs)

## Context

This work extends the leading logarithm approximation for transport interaction rates previously used by the author and others (Thoma, 1993; Hosoya and Kajantie, 1985) by computing the constant under the logarithm, which is essential for extrapolating to realistic coupling strengths. The results contradict the simple approximation of using the Debye screening mass as an infrared cutoff in naive perturbation theory, which underestimates ordinary interaction rates by about a factor of two. The negative transport rates for αs > 0.2 indicate that the weak coupling limit (g ≪ 1) required by the Braaten-Pisarski method is not satisfied at temperatures accessible in heavy ion collisions, and going beyond lowest order in g would be necessary to obtain positive, gauge-invariant results for realistic coupling constants.
