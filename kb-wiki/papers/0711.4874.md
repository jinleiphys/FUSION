# Asymmetric Nuclear Matter with Pion Dressing

> We discuss a self-consistent method to calculate the properties of cold asymmetric nuclear matter which is dressed with isoscalar scalar pion condensates. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is mediated by these pion pairs, omega- and rho- mesons. The parameters of these interactions are evaluated self-consistently using the saturation properties of nuclear matter like binding energy, pressure, compressibility and symmetry energy. The computed equation of state of pure neutron matter (PNM) is used to calculate mass and radius of a pure neutron star.

# Asymmetric Nuclear Matter with Pion Dressing

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent, nonperturbative variational formalism for cold asymmetric nuclear matter where the medium- and long-range attraction normally provided by sigma mesons in relativistic mean-field models is instead simulated by s-wave pion pairs (pion dressing), with the short-range repulsion and isospin asymmetry handled by omega and rho mesons respectively. The authors claim that this approach yields results similar to ab initio DBHF models, variational models, and RMF models without invoking sigma mesons, but note that the compressibility modulus must be in the range 260–280 MeV for the parameter search to converge, and they select K = 270 MeV as the working value.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Lagrangian with pion-nucleon interaction, reduce it to a non-relativistic effective Hamiltonian, and construct a unitary transformation that creates an arbitrarily large number of scalar isoscalar pion pairs (pion dressing) from the vacuum. The ansatz function for the pion-pair amplitude is determined variationally, with a phenomenological repulsion term (parameters a and R_pi) added to regularize the divergent pion-field energy. The omega-meson repulsion is parametrized as h_omega = lambda_omega * rho^2 and the rho-meson isospin term as h_rho = lambda_rho * rho_3^2; all four parameters (a, R_pi, lambda_omega, lambda_rho) are fixed self-consistently using the saturation properties of nuclear matter at density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}: binding energy -16 MeV, pressure 0, symmetry energy 31 MeV, and compressibility K = 270 MeV. The equation of state for pure neutron matter is then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to compute neutron star mass and radius.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E^0_sym = 31 MeV
- Compressibility modulus: K = 270 MeV (selected from the convergent range 260–280 MeV)
- Effective mass ratio at saturation: M*/M = 0.81
- Omega-meson coupling parameter: lambda_omega = 3.164 fm^2 (for K = 270 MeV)
- Rho-meson coupling parameter: lambda_rho = 0.650 fm^2
- Pion-pair repulsion strength: a = 115.26 MeV (for K = 270 MeV)
- Pion-pair repulsion length scale: R_pi = 1.061 fm (for K = 270 MeV)
- Symmetry energy density-dependence exponent: gamma = 0.85 (from fit E_sym = E^0_sym (rho/rho_0)^gamma)
- Maximum neutron star mass (pure neutron matter): 2.25 solar masses (M_sun)
- Neutron star radius at maximum mass: 11.7 km

## Context

This work extends the pion-dressing formalism previously developed by Mishra et al. (1990, 1992) for symmetric nuclear matter to the case of asymmetric nuclear matter, and applies it to neutron star structure. The approach belongs to the class of phenomenological density functional models, similar to relativistic mean-field models (Walecka, 1974; Serot, 1986; Ring, 1996) but replaces the sigma meson with pion-pair condensates. The results are compared with DBHF calculations using the Bonn A potential (Li et al., 1992), the variational A18+delta-v+UIX* model of the Argonne group (Akmal et al., 1998), and various RMF and Skyrme models. The symmetry energy results are compared with experimental data from heavy-ion reactions analyzed by Shetty et al. (2007), and the authors find a stiff dependence at supra-saturation densities with gamma = 0.85, consistent with the stiff end of the range gamma ~ 0.55–1.05 reported in the literature. The formalism enables future extension to finite-temperature asymmetric nuclear matter and may have experimental consequences for hard photon production from pion-pair annihilation.
