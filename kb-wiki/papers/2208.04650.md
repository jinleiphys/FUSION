# Measurement of Light Nuclei Production in Heavy-ion Collisions by the STAR experiment

> In these proceedings, we present the measurements of centrality, transverse momentum and rapidity dependences of proton ($p$) and light nuclei ($d$ ($\overline{d}$), $t$, $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ ($\overline{^{3}\mathrm{He}}$), and $^{4}\mathrm{He}$) production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 3 GeV, and isobaric (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV. The compound yield ratios in central collisions at 3 GeV are found to be larger than the transport model calculations. Furthermore, the kinetic freeze-out parameters at 3 GeV show a different trend compared to those of light hadrons ($π$, $K$, $p$) at higher energies.

# Measurement of Light Nuclei Production in Heavy-ion Collisions by the STAR Experiment

## Key claim
The compound yield ratios (Np × Nt / Nd²) in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3 GeV are larger than transport model calculations (AMPT, SMASH, and UrQMD), and the kinetic freeze-out parameters at this energy show a different trend compared to those of light hadrons (π, K, p) at higher energies, suggesting the hot and dense medium created at 3 GeV may be different from that at higher collision energies.

## Method
The STAR experiment identified light nuclei (p, d, d̅, t, ³He, ³He̅, ⁴He) using specific energy loss in the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) at low transverse momenta and Time of Flight (TOF) detector at higher momenta, with corrections for TPC tracking efficiency, TOF matching efficiency, and energy loss efficiency. Transverse momentum spectra were fit individually with the Blast-Wave model function to extract kinetic freeze-out parameters (temperature Tkin and average radial flow velocity ⟨βT⟩), and yield ratios were compared to hadronic transport models (JAM, SMASH, UrQMD) and thermal model calculations.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3 GeV: baryon chemical potential μB ∼ 750 MeV
- Isobaric (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV: ∼2 billion events per collision system
- Feed-down contribution from weak decay of strange baryons to protons at 3 GeV: only about 1.5%
- Target rapidity at 3 GeV: ytarget = −1.045
- The Np × Nt / Nd² yield ratios in central Au+Au collisions at 3 GeV show values larger than transport model calculations, with an increasing tendency towards target rapidity (−1.0 < y < −0.6)
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature Tkin of deuterons is systematically higher than that of protons at 3 GeV, while ⟨βT⟩ of deuterons is systematically lower

## Context
This work extends the STAR Beam Energy Scan program measurements of light nuclei production from the first phase (Au+Au at √sNN = 7.7–200 GeV, reported in STAR (2019) for deuterons and Zhang (2020) for tritons) down to √sNN = 3 GeV using fixed-target mode, and adds new isobaric collision data (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) at 200 GeV. The results enable tests of hadronic transport models (JAM, SMASH, UrQMD) and thermal model predictions at high baryon density, and provide constraints on the QCD phase structure at large μB. The compound yield ratios being larger than transport model calculations indicate that current models may need refinement in their description of light nuclei formation mechanisms at these energies.
