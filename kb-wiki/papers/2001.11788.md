# Momentum and its affiliated transport coefficients for a hot QCD matter in a strong magnetic field

> We have studied the effects of anisotropies on the momentum transport in a QCD matter by shear ($η$) and bulk ($ζ$) viscosities. The anisotropies arise either by the strong magnetic field or by the preferential expansion. This study helps to understand the fluidity and location of transition point of matter through $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ ($s$ is entropy density), respectively, the sound attenuation through the Prandtl number (Pl), the nature of flow by the Reynolds number (Rl), and the competition between momentum and charge diffusions. The viscosities are calculated in the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory within the quasiparticle model. Compared to isotropic medium, both $η$ and $ζ$ get increased in magnetic field-driven (B-driven) anisotropy, contrary to the decrease in expansion-driven anisotropy. $η$ increases with temperature faster in former case than in latter case whereas $ζ$ in former case decreases with temperature and in latter case, it is meagre and diminishes at a specific temperature. So the viscosities can distinguish aforesaid anisotropies. Thus, $η/s$ gets enhanced in former case and in latter case, it becomes smaller than isotropic one. Similarly $ζ/s$ gets amplified but decreases faster with the temperature in a strong magnetic field. The Prandtl number gets increased in B-induced anisotropy and gets decreased in expansion-induced anisotropy, compared to isotropic one. Since, Pl is found larger than 1, the sound attenuation is governed by momentum diffusion. The B-driven anisotropy makes the Reynolds number smaller than one, whereas the expansion-driven anisotropy makes it larger. The ratio ($\fracη{s}/\frac{σ_{\rm el}}{T}$) gets amplified in B-driven anisotropy whereas it gets reduced in expansion-driven anisotropy. Since, the ratio is always more than one, the momentum diffusion prevails over the charge diffusion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shear and bulk viscosities, and their affiliated transport coefficients (η/s, ζ/s, Prandtl number, Reynolds number, and the ratio of momentum to charge diffusion), can distinguish between magnetic field-driven anisotropy and expansion-driven anisotropy in hot QCD matter, with the caveat that these results are obtained within the relaxation-time approximation of kinetic theory using a quasiparticle model and are valid only for weak momentum anisotropy.

## Method

The authors calculated shear (η) and bulk (ζ) viscosities using the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation-time approximation, with parton interactions embodied through medium-generated thermal masses for quarks and gluons (quasiparticle model). For the magnetic field-driven anisotropy, only the lowest Landau level (LLL) quarks/antiquarks contributed to the longitudinal components of viscosities along the magnetic field direction, while for expansion-driven anisotropy, anisotropic distribution functions were constructed by contracting isotropic distributions in the direction of anisotropy. The viscosities were then used to compute the ratios η/s and ζ/s, the Prandtl number (Pl), the Reynolds number (Rl), and the ratio (η/s)/(σ_el/T) for both anisotropic cases and compared with the isotropic medium.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity η increases faster with temperature in B-driven anisotropy than in expansion-driven anisotropy; bulk viscosity ζ in B-driven anisotropy decreases monotonically with temperature, while in expansion-driven anisotropy ζ is meagre and diminishes at a specific temperature (>T_c).
- η/s is enhanced in B-driven anisotropy and becomes smaller than the isotropic value in expansion-driven anisotropy; ζ/s gets amplified in B-driven anisotropy but decreases faster with temperature.
- The Prandtl number (Pl) is always found larger than 1 in all cases, indicating sound attenuation is governed by momentum diffusion.
- The Reynolds number (Rl) becomes smaller than 1 in B-driven anisotropy and larger than 1 in expansion-driven anisotropy.
- The ratio (η/s)/(σ_el/T) is always more than 1, so momentum diffusion prevails over charge diffusion in all cases.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same authors on electrical and thermal conductivities in anisotropic QCD matter (Rath and Patra, 2019) and builds upon prior calculations of shear and bulk viscosities using kinetic theory (Danielewicz, 1985; Sasaki and Redlich, 2009; Thakur et al., 2017) and holographic models (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005). The results enable understanding of how strong magnetic fields affect momentum transport in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and are relevant to other systems with strong magnetic fields such as magnetar cores and the early universe. The paper contrasts with earlier works on viscosities in magnetic fields using correlator techniques (Hattori et al., 2017), perturbative QCD (Li and Yee, 2018), and Chapman-Enskog methods (Kurian and Chandra, 2019).
