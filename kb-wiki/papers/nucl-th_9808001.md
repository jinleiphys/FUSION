# Nuclear Deformation Effects in the Cluster Radioactivity

> We investigate the influence of the nuclear deformation on the decay rates of some cluster emission processes. The interaction between the daughter and the cluster is given by a double folding potential including quadrupole and hexadecupole deformed densities of both fragments. The nuclear part of the nucleus-nucleus interaction is density dependent and at small distances a repulsive core in the potential will occur. In the frame of the WKB-approximation the assault frequency of the cluster will depend on the geometric properties of the potential pocket whereas the penetrability will be sensitive to changes in the barrier location. The results obtained in this paper point out that various combinations of cluster and daughter deformations may account for the measured values of the decay rate.T he decay rather are however more sensitive to the changes in the daughter deformation due to the large mass asymmetry of the process.

# Nuclear Deformation Effects in the Cluster Radioactivity

## Key claim

The decay rate for cluster emission is more sensitive to changes in the deformation of the daughter nucleus than to changes in the deformation of the emitted cluster, due to the large mass asymmetry of the process; various combinations of quadrupole and hexadecupole deformations of both fragments can account for measured decay rates, but the daughter deformation plays the dominant role.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleus-nucleus interaction potential using a double folding integral with Skyrme-type effective nucleon-nucleon forces, incorporating quadrupole and hexadecupole deformed Fermi distributions for both the daughter and cluster densities. The decay rate was calculated in a modified Gamow approach using the WKB approximation, where the assault frequency depends on the geometry of the potential pocket and the penetrability depends on the barrier location and height. The preformation probability was taken from an empirical formula by Blendowske et al. (1991) based on the cluster mass number.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{14}$C decay of $^{224}$Ra, a hexadecupole deformation of the daughter $\beta_4^D \approx 0.04$ (with zero quadrupole deformation) can account for the experimental decay rate.
- The ground state hexadecupole deformation of $^{210}$Pb (the daughter in $^{14}$C decay of $^{224}$Ra) is $\beta_4^D = 0.008$.
- The $\alpha$-spectroscopic factor used for preformation probability is $P_0^\alpha = 6.3 \times 10^{-3}$ for even-even nuclei and $3.2 \times 10^{-3}$ for odd nuclei.
- The diffusivity parameter $a$ is taken as 0.63 fm for the daughter and 0.67 fm for the cluster.
- The saturation density $\rho_{00} = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ is used in the Fermi distributions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sandulescu et al. (1990s) that considered only quadrupole deformation of the cluster with a spherical daughter, using the M3Y double folding potential. The authors incorporate for the first time both quadrupole and hexadecupole deformations of the daughter nucleus, and use a Skyrme-based potential that includes a repulsive core at small distances, unlike the density-independent M3Y interaction used previously. The results enable future experimental searches for deformed daughters in cluster radioactivity and suggest that hexadecupole deformations of the daughter, in particular, may be essential to reproduce observed decay rates without requiring large cluster deformations.
