# Studies of two-solar-mass hybrid stars within the framework of Dyson-Schwinger equations

> In this paper we introduce an equation of state (EOS) of quark matter within the framework of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to study the structure of compact stars. The smooth crossover from hadronic matter to quark matter in the hybrid star is studied. We compare different strategies to obtain crossover EOSs and find a new way to construct two-solar-mass hybrid stars with even a relatively soft quark EOS, while earlier works show that the quark EOS should be stiff enough to support a massive hybrid star.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a two-solar-mass hybrid star can be constructed using a relatively soft quark matter equation of state (EOS) derived from Dyson-Schwinger equations, provided the smooth crossover from hadronic to quark matter is performed in the pressure-chemical-potential plane rather than the pressure-density plane; this contradicts the earlier claim by Masuda, Hatsuda, and Takatsuka (2013) that the quark EOS must be stiff to support such a massive hybrid star.

## Method

The quark matter EOS is obtained from the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) using rainbow truncation and the Qin-Chang gluon propagator model, with a new parameter set fitted to yield a stiffer EOS than previous DSE-based work. The hadronic phase is described by the APR EOS (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) with A18+δv+UIX* interaction. A smooth crossover between the two phases is constructed using a hyperbolic tangent interpolation function applied either in the pressure-density (P-ρ) plane or in the pressure-chemical-potential (P-μ_B) plane, with thermodynamic consistency enforced by an extra term Δε. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are then integrated to obtain the mass-radius relation for hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of the hybrid star obtained from the P-μ_B interpolation is 2.35 solar masses.
- The crossover region in the P-μ_B plane lies between baryon chemical potentials of 1.4 GeV and 3.4 GeV, with the first-order phase transition point at approximately 2.4 GeV.
- The sound velocity from the P-μ_B interpolation remains below 0.4 times the speed of light for the P-ρ interpolation, while the P-μ_B interpolation yields a much larger sound velocity.
- The quark number density of the s quark turns nonzero at a critical chemical potential μ_c = 520 MeV.
- The vacuum bag constant is set to B = (110 MeV)^4.
- The maximum mass of a pure quark star with this EOS is about 1.65 solar masses.
- Different interpolation functions change the maximum mass by less than 5 percent.

## Context

This work extends the smooth crossover approach to hybrid stars pioneered by Masuda, Hatsuda, and Takatsuka (2013) and Kojo, Powell, Song, and Baym (2015), who performed interpolation in the pressure-density plane and concluded that a stiff quark EOS is necessary for two-solar-mass hybrid stars. By instead interpolating in the pressure-chemical-potential plane, the authors obtain a much stiffer hybrid EOS even with a relatively soft quark EOS, enabling the construction of hybrid stars exceeding two solar masses. This result opens the possibility that massive hybrid stars may exist even if the quark matter EOS is not extremely stiff, and it motivates further study of the role of hyperons and the choice of interpolation scheme in hybrid star models.
