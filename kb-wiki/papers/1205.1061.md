# Neutrino- and antineutrino-induced reactions with nuclei between 1 and 50 GeV

> Background: Nuclear effects can have a significant impact on neutrino-nucleus interactions. In particular, data from neutrino experiments with broad energy distributions require complex theoretical models that are able to take all the relevant channels into account as well as incorporate nuclear effects in both initial and final-state interactions. Purpose: We investigate neutrino and antineutrino scattering on iron and carbon in the energy range from 1 to 30 GeV, which is relevant to current and coming experiments (MINOS, NOvA, and Minerva). Method: The Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model, which implements all reaction channels relevant for neutrino energies under consideration, is used for an investigation of neutrino-nucleus reactions. Results: Our calculations are compared with the recent NOMAD and MINOS data for the integrated inclusive cross sections. Predictions are made for the differential cross sections for semiinclusive final states (pions, kaons, and nucleons) for the MINOS and NOvA beams. Conclusions: Nuclear effects in the initial-state interactions may slightly change the inclusive nuclear cross section as compared to the free nucleon ones. Final-state interactions noticeably change the spectra of the outgoing hadrons. In the Minerva and NOvA experiments these effects should be visible in the kinetic energy distributions of the final pions, kaons, and nucleons. Secondary interactions play an important role for strangeness production.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects in initial-state interactions may slightly change the inclusive nuclear cross section compared to free nucleon ones, but final-state interactions noticeably alter the spectra of outgoing hadrons; secondary interactions make it very difficult to extract the neutrino-induced strangeness production cross section in experiments using nuclear targets.

## Method

The Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model was used to simulate neutrino and antineutrino scattering on iron and carbon targets. The model implements quasielastic scattering, resonance production (13 resonances with electromagnetic properties from MAID analysis), non-resonant background, and deep inelastic scattering via the PYTHIA event generator (version 6.4.26), with a smooth transition between resonance and DIS descriptions at hadronic invariant masses between 1.6 and 2.05 GeV. Final-state interactions were modeled by solving the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation for the propagation of outgoing hadrons through the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- World average values for isoscalar target at E_nu > 30 GeV: sigma_tot^nu/E_nu = 0.667 +/- 0.014 x 10^-38 cm^2/GeV for neutrinos and sigma_tot^bar-nu/E_bar-nu = 0.334 +/- 0.008 x 10^-38 cm^2/GeV for antineutrinos
- For neutrinos, DIS contribution becomes larger than Delta resonance contribution at about 3 GeV, reaches 60% of total at 5 GeV, and 95% at higher energies
- For antineutrinos, DIS contribution becomes larger than Delta channel at about 4 GeV, reaches 40% of total at E_bar-nu ~ 5 GeV, and 95% at higher energies
- The difference between various prescriptions for nuclear corrections in DIS is about 2% at the lowest energy and less at the highest energy
- QE cross section on iron and free neutron become practically indistinguishable for Q^2 > 0.2 GeV^2
- The ratio of antineutrino to neutrino cross sections on iron rises with energy and approaches an asymptotic value of about 0.5 for an isoscalar-corrected target at high energies

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport model, previously tested for photon-induced and electron-induced reactions from a few hundred MeV to 200 GeV (Effenberger 1999, Krusche 2004, Buss 2007, Gallmeister 2007, Leitner 2008), to neutrino and antineutrino reactions on nuclei at intermediate energies. It provides predictions for the MINOS, NOvA, and Minerva experiments, complementing the work of Kulagin and Petti (2007) and Haider et al. (2011) on nuclear structure functions by offering a practical event generator that can calculate semi-inclusive final states. The results enable future comparisons with Minerva data on multiple nuclear targets to clarify the controversial topic of nuclear corrections in neutrino reactions, particularly the EMC effect and its dependence on Bjorken-x.
