# X(3915) and X(4350) as new members in P-wave charmonium family

> The analysis of the mass spectrum and the calculation of the strong decay of P-wave charmonium states strongly support to explain the newly observed X(3915) and X(4350) as new members in P-wave charmonium family, i.e., $χ_{c0}^\prime$ for X(3915) and $χ_{c2}^{\prime\prime}$ for X(4350). Under the P-wave charmonium assignment to X(3915) and X(4350), the $J^{PC}$ quantum numbers of X(3915) and X(4350) must be $0^{++}$ and $2^{++}$ respectively, which provide the important criterion to test P-wave charmonium explanation for X(3915) and X(4350) proposed by this letter. The decay behavior of the remaining two P-wave charmonium states with the second radial excitation is predicted, and experimental search for them is suggested.

# X(3915) and X(4350) as new members in P-wave charmonium family

## Key claim

The newly observed X(3915) and X(4350) can be explained as conventional P-wave charmonium states, specifically the first radial excitation χ′c0 for X(3915) and the second radial excitation χ′′c2 for X(4350), provided that their JPC quantum numbers are confirmed to be 0++ and 2++ respectively by future angular distribution measurements.

## Method

The authors analyzed the mass spectrum of established and candidate P-wave charmonium states, comparing the masses of X(3915) and X(4350) with predictions from the Godfrey-Isgur relativized potential model and the Resonance Spectrum Expansion (RSE) model. They then calculated the open-charm strong decay widths of the proposed assignments using the Quark Pair Creation (QPC) model, with the harmonic oscillator parameter R varied to match the experimental total widths of X(3915) and Z(3930) (the latter serving as a test case for χ′c2).

## Key numbers

- X(3915) mass: M = 3915 ± 3 (stat) ± 2 (sys) MeV; width: Γ = 17 ± 10 (stat) ± 3 (sys) MeV (Belle Collaboration)
- X(4350) mass: m = 4350 +4.6 −5.1 (stat) ± 0.7 (sys) MeV; width: Γ = 13.3 +17.9 −9.1 (stat) ± 4.1 (sys) MeV (Belle Collaboration)
- The Godfrey-Isgur relativized potential model predicted the mass of χ′c0 around 3916 MeV and the mass of χ′′c2 around 4337 MeV
- The RSE model predicts a mass gap of 380 MeV between states with radial quantum numbers n and n+1
- For χ′c0 (X(3915) assignment), the total open-charm decay width falls in the experimental range when R = 1.80 to 1.99 GeV−1, with R = 1.92 GeV−1 giving the central value
- For χ′′c2 (X(4350) assignment), the total open-charm decay width is consistent with Belle data for R = 1.9 to 2.3 GeV−1
- Predicted total decay width of χ′′c0: 82 to 110 MeV for R = 2.0 to 3.0 GeV−1
- Predicted total decay width of χ′′c1: 47 to 140 MeV for R = 2.0 to 3.0 GeV−1

## Context

This work extends the charmonium spectroscopy program by assigning the Belle Collaboration's X(3915) and X(4350) (observed in γγ fusion to J/ψω and J/ψφ respectively) to specific radial excitations of P-wave charmonium, filling the missing χ′c0 state and proposing χ′′c2 as a second radial excitation. The authors build on the prior identification of Z(3930) as χ′c2 by Uehara et al. (2005) and the X(3872) as χ′c1 by Choi et al. (2003), Kalashnikova (2005), and Li et al. (2009). The analysis enables experimental searches for the remaining two second-radial-excitation states χ′′c0 and χ′′c1, with predicted dominant decay channels (D Dbar and Ds+ Ds− for χ′′c0; D Dbar* + h.c. and Ds+ Ds*− + h.c. for χ′′c1) serving as search guidance. The authors also note that measuring the D Dbar decay of X(3915) can distinguish their conventional charmonium interpretation from the D* Dbar* molecule explanation proposed by Nielsen et al. (2009).
