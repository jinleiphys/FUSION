# A Universal Upper Bound on the Pressure-to-Energy Density Ratio in Neutron Stars

> The equation-of-state (EOS) parameter $ϕ\equiv P/\varepsilon$, defined as the ratio of pressure to energy density, encapsulates the fundamental response of matter under extreme compression. Its value at the center of the most massive neutron star (NS), $\x \equiv ϕ_{\rm c} = P_{\rm c}/\varepsilon_{\rm c}$, sets a universal upper bound on the maximum denseness attainable by any form of visible matter anywhere in the Universe. Remarkably, owing to the intrinsically nonlinear structure of the EOS in General Relativity (GR), this bound is forced to lie far below the naive Special Relativity (SR) limit of unity. In this work, we refine the theoretical upper bound on $\x$ in a self-consistent manner by incorporating, in addition to the causality constraint from SR, the mass-sphere stability condition associated with the mass evolution pattern in the vicinity of the NS center. This condition is formulated within the intrinsic-and-perturbative analysis of the dimensionless Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff equations (IPAD-TOV) framework. The combined constraints yield an improved bound, $\x \lesssim 0.385$, which is slightly above but fully consistent with the previously derived causal-only limit, $\x \lesssim 0.374$. We further derive an improved scaling relation for NS compactness and verify its universality across a broad set of 284 realistic EOSs, including models with first-order phase transitions, exotic degrees of freedom, continuous crossover behavior, and deconfined quark cores. The resulting bound on $\x$ thus provides a new, EOS-independent window into the microphysics of cold superdense matter compressed by strong-field gravity in GR.

## Key claim

The paper refines the universal upper bound on the central pressure-to-energy density ratio in neutron stars, X = P_c/epsilon_c, by incorporating both the causality constraint from special relativity and a newly formulated mass-sphere stability condition from general relativity, yielding an improved bound of X less than or approximately 0.385, which is slightly above but consistent with the previously derived causality-only limit of X less than or approximately 0.374.

## Method

The authors apply the intrinsic-and-perturbative analysis of dimensionless Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations (IPAD-TOV) framework to study the central equation-of-state parameter X = P_c/epsilon_c in neutron stars. They combine the causality constraint requiring central sound-speed squared s_c^2 less than or equal to 1 with a mass-sphere stability condition derived from the second derivative of the coefficient A(X) = -a_2(X) with respect to X, where a_2 is the leading expansion coefficient of the energy density near the stellar center. The consistency between the causality boundary and the mass-sphere instability onset is used to refine the upper bound, and the result is verified against a set of 284 realistic equations of state.

## Key numbers

* The refined upper bound on the central equation-of-state parameter is X less than or approximately 0.385, compared to the previous causality-only limit of X less than or approximately 0.374.
* The causality boundary from the central sound-speed squared condition s_c^2 = 1 gives X_+ approximately 0.374 without the a_2-term correction, and X_+ approximately 0.381 when the a_2-term is included.
* The mass-sphere stability condition d^2A/dX^2 = 0 yields X_bar approximately 0.377 without the a_2-term correction, and X_bar approximately 0.368 when the a_2-term is included.
* The singularity of the central sound-speed squared occurs at X_unphys = 1/sqrt(3) approximately 0.577, representing an unphysical state.
* The scaling constant Q for the characteristic length is approximately 10 km times (epsilon_c/600 MeV/fm^3)^(-1/2).

## Context

This work extends the IPAD-TOV framework previously developed by the same group (Cai, Li, Ma, 2023-2025) for studying neutron star core properties, scaling relations, and causality boundaries. The refined bound on X provides an equation-of-state-independent window into the microphysics of cold superdense matter under strong-field gravity, analogous to the role of the Bertsch parameter in unitary Fermi gas physics (Ku 2012). The result enables improved neutron star compactness scaling relations and offers a new benchmark for understanding how general relativity, quantum many-body physics, and quantum chromodynamics jointly govern matter at the highest densities.
