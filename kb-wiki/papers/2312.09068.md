# Global and local polarization of $Λ$ hyperons across RHIC-BES energies

> We report our recent study on the global and local polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies within the (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics framework. We present our numerical results for the global polarization as the function of collision energies and the local polarization along the beam direction as functions of azimuthal angle in $20-50$% centrality at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7 GeV Au+Au collision energy. We have discussed the effects of initial conditions, Spin Hall effect and baryon diffusion.

## Key claim
The global polarization of Λ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies is sensitive to the choice of initial condition model (AMPT vs. SMASH) at low collision energies, while both models agree with STAR experimental data within error bars; the local polarization along the beam direction receives a positive contribution from the Spin Hall effect that is itself sensitive to initial conditions and baryon diffusion.

## Method
The authors used the (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics framework to simulate quark-gluon plasma evolution, with two different initial condition models (AMPT and SMASH). The spin polarization vector was computed via the modified Cooper-Frye formula, including contributions from thermal vorticity, shear tensor, fluid acceleration, and the Spin Hall effect, while magnetic field contributions were neglected. The polarization was then Lorentz-transformed to the Λ rest frame and integrated over rapidity and transverse momentum to obtain the global polarization P^y and local longitudinal polarization P^z as functions of azimuthal angle.

## Key numbers
- Global polarization P^y for Λ and anti-Λ hyperons was computed across RHIC-BES energies in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions, with mid-rapidity |η| < 1 and p_T range [0.5, 3.0] GeV.
- Local longitudinal polarization P^z was studied at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions.
- Baryon diffusion coefficient C_B values of 1.2 and 0.0 were compared for their effect on P^z at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological studies of Λ hyperon polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the STAR measurements (STAR Collaboration, 2019) and earlier theoretical work by Liang and Wang (2004) and STAR (2017). The study addresses model uncertainties by comparing AMPT (Lin, 2004) and SMASH (SMASH Collaboration, 2016) initial conditions, and investigates the roles of shear-induced polarization, Spin Hall effect, and baryon diffusion that were discussed in prior works by Fu (2021), Becattini (2021), Yi (2021), and Alzhrani (2022). The results indicate that further systematic studies are needed to reduce uncertainties in the numerical framework for spin polarization calculations.
