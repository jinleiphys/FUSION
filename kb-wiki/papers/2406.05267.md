# Characterizing the nuclear models informed by PREX and CREX: a view from Bayesian inference

> New measurements of the weak charge density distributions of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb challenge existing nuclear models. In the post-PREX-CREX era, it is unclear if current models can simultaneously describe weak charge distributions along with accurate measurements of binding energy and charge radii. In this letter, we explore the parameter space of relativistic and non-relativistic models to study the differences between the electric and weak form factors, $ΔF=F_{ch}-F_{W}$, in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. We show, for the first time, which aspects of mean-field models are the most important in determining the relative magnitude of the neutron skin in lead and calcium nuclei. We carefully disentangle the tension between the PREX-2/CREX constraints and the ability of the RMF and Skyrme models to accurately describe binding energies and charge radii. We find that the nuclear symmetry energy coefficient $S_V$ and the isovector spin-orbit coefficient $b'_4$ play different roles in determining $ΔF$ of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. Consequently, adjusting $S_V$ or $b'_4$ shifts predicted $ΔF$ values toward or away from PREX-2/CREX measurements. Additionally, $S_V$ and the slope L are marginally correlated given the prior constraints of our Bayesian inference, allowing us to infer them separately from PREX-2/CREX data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the nuclear symmetry energy coefficient \(S_V\) and the isovector spin-orbit coefficient \(b'_4\) play different roles in determining the difference between electric and weak form factors \(\Delta F = F_{ch} - F_W\) in \({}^{48}\)Ca and \({}^{208}\)Pb, and that adjusting these parameters can shift predicted \(\Delta F\) values toward or away from PREX-2/CREX measurements, with the caveat that the tension between these measurements and model descriptions of binding energies and charge radii is carefully disentangled rather than fully resolved.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference over the parameter spaces of both relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (including \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), and \(\delta\) mesons) and non-relativistic Skyrme models, sampling posterior distributions constrained by ground-state properties (binding energies and charge radii) of \({}^{48}\)Ca, \({}^{90}\)Zr, and \({}^{208}\)Pb, together with PREX-2 and CREX measurements of weak charge form factors. They derived approximate analytic relationships between the spin-orbit parameters in RMF and Skyrme models via a non-relativistic reduction of the Dirac equation, and fit linear formulas connecting \(\Delta F\) values to \(S_V\), \(L\), and \(b'_4\).

## Key numbers

- For Skyrme models, the joint PREX-2/CREX constraints yield mean values \((\bar{S}_V, \bar{L}) = (56.7, 66.8)\) MeV with covariance matrix diagonal elements \(\sqrt{\text{cov}}_{11} = 19.6\) MeV and \(\sqrt{\text{cov}}_{22} = 56.5\) MeV.
- For RMF models, the joint constraints yield \((\bar{S}_V, \bar{L}) = (53.8, 30.9)\) MeV with \(\sqrt{\text{cov}}_{11} = 19.5\) MeV and \(\sqrt{\text{cov}}_{22} = 66.3\) MeV.
- The probability of inconsistency between PREX-2 and CREX constraints is 93% (1.8-\(\sigma\)) for Skyrme models and 76% (1.2-\(\sigma\)) for RMF models.
- The isovector spin-orbit parameter \(b'_4\) from the joint fit gives \(b'_4 = 1.37 \pm 0.49\) fm\(^4\) for Skyrme models (90% lower bound \(b'_4 > 0.744\) fm\(^4\)) and \(b'_4 = 1.02 \pm 0.37\) fm\(^4\) for RMF models (90% lower bound \(b'_4 > 0.544\) fm\(^4\)).
- The newly proposed CPREX1 model has \(b'_4 = 0.652\) fm\(^4\), a maximum neutron star mass \(M_{\text{max}} = 2.04 M_\odot\), and dimensionless tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 584\).
- The CPREX2 model has an intermediate \(b'_4 = 0.386\) fm\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of nuclear models constrained by PREX-2 and CREX data, such as those by Reed (2021), Reinhard (2022), Zhang (2022), and Salinas (2023), by systematically exploring both RMF and Skyrme parameter spaces and explicitly quantifying the role of the isovector spin-orbit interaction. The paper enables future model development by identifying that large isovector spin-orbit couplings (e.g., \(b'_4 > 0.544\) fm\(^4\) in RMF models) are required to simultaneously describe PREX-2 and CREX measurements, and provides new parameter sets (CPREX1 and CPREX2) that achieve this while maintaining reasonable nuclear and neutron star properties.
