# Flash of Prompt Photons from the Early Stage of Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We briefly recall the tremendous strides in the studies of the parton cascade model made by Klaus Geiger. Next, we argue that photons may provide confirmation of several of these ideas. Thus we know that, copious internetted partonic cascades may develop in the wake of relativistic collisions of nuclei at CERN SPS and BNL RHIC energies, We use the parton cascade model to estimate the emission of single photons generated from such cascades due to the branching of quarks $q \to q γ$, scattering of quarks and gluons, and annihilation of quarks. The formation of a hot and dense partonic matter is shown to be preceded by an intense radiation of photons from the QED branching of quarks. This is similar to the QCD branching $q \to qg$ which along with the gluon multiplication ($g \to gg$) which is known to play a crucial role in the formation of the dense partonic plasma.

## Key claim

Prompt photons from QED branching of quarks (q -> q gamma) in the early pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions may dominate the photon yield at low transverse momentum (p_T <= 3 GeV), preceding the formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma, but the result depends on the choice of the virtuality cut-off mu_0 for the branching and on the inclusion of multiple scatterings and the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect.

## Method

The parton cascade model (PCM), implemented in the Monte Carlo code VNI, was used to simulate the space-time evolution of parton phase-space distributions in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. Photon production was calculated from two mechanisms: (1) parton scattering processes (Compton scattering q+g -> q+gamma and annihilation q+anti-q -> g+gamma) treated within perturbative QCD for transverse momenta above a cut-off p_T^0, and (2) electromagnetic bremsstrahlung from time-like cascading quarks (q -> q gamma) treated on equal footing with QCD branchings (q -> q g) but without angular ordering or the same virtuality cut-off restrictions.

## Key numbers

- The relative probability for q -> q gamma versus q -> q g branching is proportional to (alpha_em * <e_q^2>) / (alpha_s * C_F) ~ 1/200, for alpha_em = 1/137, alpha_s = 0.25, <e_q^2> = 0.22, and C_F = 4/3.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at SPS (158 GeV/nucleon), prompt photons from quark branching dominate the yield for p_T <= 3 GeV, while collision processes dominate at higher p_T.
- The ratio of nuclear overlap functions for central collisions is T_Pb-Pb / T_S-S ~ 15.4 at SPS energies; the number of hard scatterings in Pb+Pb is about 17 times that in S+S.
- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/nucleon), the ratio T_Au-Au / T_S-S ~ 14.2, but the integrated photon yield for Au+Au is only about 12 times that for S+S, indicating the onset of the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect.
- The virtuality cut-off mu_0 for the q -> q gamma branching was taken as either 1 GeV or 0.01 GeV; reducing the cut-off enhances photon production at lower p_T.

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade model developed by Geiger (1992-1995) to the production of single prompt photons, following earlier PCM studies of dileptons by Geiger and Kapusta (1993). It contrasts with hydrodynamic calculations by Cleymans, Redlich, and Srivastava (1997) that assume a thermalized plasma or hadronic matter formed at 1 fm/c, as the PCM provides a causal, assumption-free description of the pre-equilibrium stage. The results enable comparison with preliminary WA98 data for Pb+Pb at SPS and suggest that pre-equilibrium photon contributions are essential for describing single photon spectra at higher p_T, while also motivating searches for low-mass dileptons from similar branching processes.
