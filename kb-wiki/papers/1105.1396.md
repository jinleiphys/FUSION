# Equilibration and relaxation times at the chiral phase transition including reheating

> We investigate the relaxational dynamics of the order parameter of chiral symmetry breaking, the sigma mean-field, with a heat bath consisting of quarks and antiquarks. A semiclassical stochastic Langevin equation of motion is obtained from the linear sigma model with constituent quarks. The equilibration of the system is studied for a first order phase transition and a critical point, where a different behavior is found. At the first order phase transition we observe the phase coexistence and at a critical point the phenomenon of critical slowing down with large relaxation times. We go beyond existing Langevin studies and include reheating of the heat bath by determining the energy dissipation during the relaxational process. The energy of the entire system is conserved. In a critical point scenario we again observe critical slowing down.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that when reheating of the heat bath is included via energy conservation, the relaxational dynamics of the sigma field differs qualitatively between a first-order phase transition and a critical point: at the first-order transition the system does not equilibrate because reheating pushes the temperature above the transition temperature, while at the critical point the system does equilibrate but with longest relaxation times at the critical temperature, consistent with critical slowing down.

## Method
The authors use a semiclassical stochastic Langevin equation for the sigma mean-field, derived from the linear sigma model with constituent quarks via the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action formalism. The quarks and antiquarks constitute a heat bath assumed to be in local thermal equilibrium. The damping coefficient and noise correlator are obtained from the dissipation-fluctuation theorem, and the energy exchange between the sigma field and the heat bath is computed from the energy-momentum tensor to include reheating. Numerical simulations are performed on a 32^3 lattice with time step 0.02 fm and lattice spacing 0.2 fm, averaging over 5 to 20 noise configurations.

## Key numbers
- For the first-order phase transition scenario, the coupling is set to g=5.5 with transition temperature T_c=123.27 MeV; the spinodal region extends from approximately 108 MeV to 128 MeV.
- For the critical point scenario, the coupling is g=3.63 with transition temperature T_c=139.88 MeV.
- The vacuum sigma mass is m_sigma ≈ 604 MeV (from λ^2=20, f_pi=93 MeV, m_pi=138 MeV); the constituent quark mass in vacuum is m_q=306.9 MeV (from g=3.3).
- For the first-order scenario with reheating, a quench from T_ini=160 MeV to T_sys=20 MeV yields final temperature T_fin=121.41 MeV and sigma field σ_fin=85.60 MeV, close to the equilibrium value σ_eq=84.82 MeV; quenches to T_sys=100, 80, 50 MeV all yield final temperatures above T_c (131.89, 127.69, 123.59 MeV respectively) with sigma fields far from equilibrium.
- For the critical point scenario with reheating, a quench from T_ini=160 MeV to T_sys=130 MeV yields T_fin=138.96 ± 0.47 MeV and σ_fin=53.43 ± 1.52 MeV, close to σ_eq=54.52 MeV; relaxation times are longest for this quench which comes closest to T_c.

## Context
This work extends previous Langevin studies of the chiral phase transition (Greiner 1996, Biro 1997, Rischke 1998, Cassol-Seewald 2007) by including the back-reaction of the sigma field on the heat bath via energy conservation, which was missing in those approaches. Chiral fluid dynamic models (Mishustin 1998, Paech 2003) typically disregarded the Langevin dynamics. The results enable future full nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics simulations that include both the Langevin equation for the order parameter and the fluid dynamic expansion of the heat bath, as noted by the authors.
