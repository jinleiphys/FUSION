# Two-photon exchange in elastic electron-nucleon scattering

> A detailed study of two-photon exchange in unpolarized and polarized elastic electron--nucleon scattering is presented, taking particular account of nucleon finite size effects. Contributions from nucleon elastic intermediate states are found to have a strong angular dependence, which leads to a partial resolution of the discrepancy between the Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of the proton electric to magnetic form factor ratio, G_E/G_M. The two-photon exchange contribution to the longitudinal polarization transfer P_L is small, whereas the contribution to the transverse polarization transfer P_T is enhanced at backward angles by several percent, increasing with Q^2. This gives rise to a small, ~3% suppression of G_E/G_M obtained from the polarization transfer ratio P_T/P_L at large Q^2. We also compare the two-photon exchange effects with data on the ratio of e^+ p to e^- p cross sections, which is predicted to be enhanced at backward angles. Finally, we evaluate the corrections to the form factors of the neutron, and estimate the elastic intermediate state contribution to the ^3He form factors.

## Key claim

Two-photon exchange corrections from nucleon elastic intermediate states, calculated with realistic form factors, can resolve approximately half to most of the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio \(G_E/G_M\), but the effect is a partial resolution and the exact magnitude depends on the \(\varepsilon\) range used in the linear fit.

## Method

The authors computed the two-photon exchange amplitude for elastic electron-nucleon scattering, including both box and crossed-box diagrams, using phenomenological form factors parameterized as sums of monopoles fitted to data. The loop integrals were evaluated numerically using Passarino-Veltman functions, and the correction \(\Delta\) was defined as the difference between the full two-photon exchange contribution and the standard Mo-Tsai infrared approximation, isolating the finite, structure-dependent part.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange correction \(\Delta\) at \(Q^2 = 6\) GeV\(^2\) shows significant deviations from linearity in \(\varepsilon\), especially at small \(\varepsilon\).
- The shift in the form factor ratio \(R = \mu G_E/G_M\) from two-photon exchange is sufficient to bring Rosenbluth and polarization transfer data almost to agreement when the \(\varepsilon\) range is taken as 0.2–0.9.
- The two-photon exchange contribution to the transverse polarization transfer \(P_T\) is enhanced at backward angles by several percent, increasing with \(Q^2\).
- The suppression of \(G_E/G_M\) from the polarization transfer ratio \(P_T/P_L\) is less than 3% at large \(Q^2\).
- The ratio of \(e^+ p\) to \(e^- p\) cross sections is predicted to be enhanced by up to \(\sim 10\%\) at backward angles.
- The model dependence of the correction \(\Delta\) at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) is very weak, with essentially no change in slope; at \(Q^2 = 6\) GeV\(^2\) differences are slightly larger but the trend remains unchanged.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2003) by using realistic multi-pole form factors instead of simple monopole forms, and systematically applies the two-photon exchange formalism to unpolarized scattering, polarization transfer, and positron scattering. It partially resolves the long-standing discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation results (which gave \(R \approx 1\)) and Jefferson Lab polarization transfer measurements (which found \(R \approx 1 - 0.135 (Q^2/\text{GeV}^2 - 0.24)\)). The results enable future experiments, such as a proposed Jefferson Lab measurement of \(e^+ p\) vs \(e^- p\) cross sections and a VEPP-3 proposal at \(Q^2 = 1.6\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\varepsilon \approx 0.4\), to test the two-photon exchange mechanism directly.
