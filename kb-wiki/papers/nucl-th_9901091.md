# Dyson schwinger equations: Connecting small and large length-scales

> I illustrate the phenomenological application of Dyson-Schwinger equations to the calculation of meson properties observable at TJNAF. Particular emphasis is given to this framework's ability to unify long-range effects constrained by chiral symmetry with short-range effects prescribed by perturbation theory, and interpolate between them.

## Key claim
Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a nonperturbative framework that can unify long-range effects constrained by chiral symmetry with short-range effects prescribed by perturbation theory, and interpolate between them; the paper illustrates this using the electromagnetic pion form factor and the gamma* pi -> gamma transition form factor as exemplars, with the hedge that the quantitative infrared behavior of the quark mass function carries some model-dependence.

## Method
The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation is solved for the dressed quark propagator using a model for the dressed-gluon propagator and dressed-quark-gluon vertex. The pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is obtained from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation with a kernel that preserves vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities. The electromagnetic pion form factor and the gamma* pi -> gamma transition form factor are then computed in the impulse approximation using algebraic parametrizations of the dressed-quark propagator and an Ansatz for the dressed-quark-photon vertex.

## Key numbers
- The renormalisation-point-independent vacuum quark condensate is approximately - (0.24 GeV)^3, required for a good description of light-meson observables.
- Euclidean constituent-quark masses obtained from solutions of the quark DSE: M^E_{u,d} = 0.56 GeV, M^E_s = 0.70 GeV, M^E_c = 1.3 GeV, M^E_b = 4.6 GeV.
- Current-quark masses at renormalisation point zeta = 19 GeV: m_{u,d}^zeta = 3.7 MeV, m_s^zeta = 82 MeV, m_c^zeta = 0.58 GeV, m_b^zeta = 3.8 GeV.
- The transition from quadratic to linear mass relation in the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner formula occurs at current-quark mass approximately 2 times m_s.
- At large Q^2, the gamma* pi -> gamma transition form factor behaves as T(Q^2) approx (4/3) * (4 pi^2 f_pi) / Q^2.

## Context
This work extends the DSE approach to QCD phenomenology, building on prior studies of the quark DSE by Roberts and collaborators (1997) and the systematic construction of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel by Bender and collaborators (1996). The framework enables unified calculations of meson properties across all energy scales, with the most pressing challenge being extension to baryon observables. The results connect to experimental programs at TJNAF (Jefferson Lab) and provide predictions for pion form factors that can be tested against data from experiments such as those by Amendolia and collaborators (1986) and Bebek and collaborators (1978).
