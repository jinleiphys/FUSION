# Factorizing quarkonium LDMEs and TMDSTFs using effective field theory

> We use effective field theory to factorize production matrix elements that appear in the NRQCD framework for quarkonium cross sections. By applying a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and appropriate field redefinitions, we show that the soft and ultrasoft sectors of NRQCD can be decoupled from the heavy quark and antiquark fields in a hybrid vNRQCD/pNRQCD Lagrangian at leading order in the velocity power-counting. This enables us to re-factorize quarkonium production matrix elements in terms of matrix elements of color-singlet composite fields, which we can write as the wave-function at the origin, and state independent vacuum correlators of chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic gluon fields. This approach verifies powerful relationships between the LDMEs of different S-wave quarkonia originally derived using pNRQCD. Additionally, it allows us to derive new relationships for the production matrix elements used in the transverse momentum dependent factorization (TMD) framework, known as TMD soft transition functions, providing a much stronger set of constraints on these nonperturbative operators. This work significantly advances our understanding of quarkonium production, particularly in the TMD framework.

## Key claim
Using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation within a hybrid vNRQCD/pNRQCD Lagrangian at leading order in the velocity power-counting, the soft and ultrasoft sectors can be decoupled from heavy quark fields *only* in production matrix elements because the quark-antiquark fields are produced at a point, enabling factorization that reproduces earlier pNRQCD results and extends to TMD soft transition functions.

## Method
The authors applied a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to the vNRQCD Lagrangian to replace four-fermion operators with auxiliary color-singlet and color-octet composite fields coupled to quark-antiquark pairs. They then matched the NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) for S-wave and P-wave channels onto transition operators, integrated out the quark and antiquark fields, and separated the soft and ultrasoft Hilbert spaces to factorize the matrix elements into wave-function-at-the-origin factors and state-independent vacuum correlators of chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic gluon fields. The same technique was extended to factorize TMD soft transition functions (TMDSTFs) in the transverse momentum dependent factorization framework.

## Key numbers
- The color-octet S-wave LDME for the \(^1S_0^{[8]}\) channel factorizes as \(\Oosz = \frac{1}{3 N_c M^2} \braket{{\cal B}} \frac{3|R_V(0)|^2}{2\pi}\), where \(\braket{{\cal B}}\) is a chromo-magnetic correlator.
- The color-octet S-wave LDME for the \(^3S_1^{[8]}\) channel factorizes as \(\Otsooct = \frac{1}{N_c M^2} \braket{{\cal{EE}}} \frac{3|R_V(0)|^2}{2\pi}\), where \(\braket{{\cal{EE}}}\) is a double electric field correlator.
- The leading P-wave LDME for the \(^3P_J^{[8]}\) channel factorizes as \(\Otpz = \frac{1}{36 N_c} (2J+1) \frac{3|R_V(0)|^2}{2\pi} \braket{{\cal E}}\), where \(\braket{{\cal E}}\) is a chromo-electric field correlator.
- A subleading P-wave contribution gives \(\Otpz^{(1)} = \frac{(m \braket{\vb^2}_V)^2}{108 N_c} (2J+1) \frac{3|R_V(0)|^2}{2\pi} \braket{{\cal E}^{(1)}}\), proportional to the binding energy \(m \braket{\vb^2}_V = M_V - 2m\).
- The TMDSTF for the \(^1S_0^{[8]}\) channel factorizes as \(T^V_{^1S_0^{[8]} }(\bv_T; \mu, \eta) = \frac{1}{3N_cM^2} \braket{{{\cal B}_n}(\bv_T; \mu, \eta)} \frac{3|R_V(0)|^2}{2\pi}\).
- Ratios of LDMEs between different S-wave vector states \(V, V'\) satisfy relations such as \(\braket{O^V(^3S_1^{[8]})} = \frac{m_{Q'}^2}{m_{Q}^2} {\cal R}_{VV'}^2 \braket{O^{V'}(^3S_1^{[8]})}\) and \(\braket{O^V(^1S_0^{[8]})}= \frac{m_{Q'}^2}{m_{Q}^2}\frac{c_F^2(m_{Q})}{c_F^2(m_{Q'})}{\cal R}_{VV'}^2 \braket{O^{V'}(^1S_0^{[8]})}\), where \({\cal R}_{VV'}^2 = |R_V (0)|^2/|R_{V'}(0)|^2\).
- TMDSTFs for different states are related by \(T^V_{^1S_0^{[8]} }(\bv_T;\mu, \eta) = \frac{m_{Q'}^2}{m_Q^2}\frac{c_F^2(m_Q,\mu)}{c_F^2(m_{Q'},\mu)}{\cal R}_{VV'}^2 T^{V'}_{^1S_0^{[8]} }(\bv_T;\mu, \eta)\).

## Context
This work extends the pNRQCD-based factorization of LDMEs by Brambilla et al. (2022), which reduced the number of free parameters in NRQCD production cross sections from 12 to 3, by reproducing those results in the vNRQCD framework and verifying the equivalence of the two formalisms for production. It also derives new relationships for TMD soft transition functions (TMDSTFs) originally defined by Copeland (2025) and Echevarria et al. (2024, 2025), which are process-dependent quantities that previously had limited predictive power. The new relations enable TMDSTFs for different quarkonium states (e.g., \(J/\psi\), \(\psi(2S)\), \(\Upsilon(nS)\)) to be connected by simple rescaling factors, allowing extraction of gluon TMDPDFs from measurements at facilities like the EIC and reducing the number of nonperturbative parameters in the TMD framework.
