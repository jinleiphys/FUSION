# Leading chiral three-nucleon forces along isotope chains in the calcium region

> Three-nucleon forces (3NFs), and in particular terms of the Fujita-Miyazawa type, strongly influence the structure of neutron-rich exotic isotopes. Ab-initio calculations have shown that chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions correctly reproduce binding energy systematics and neutron driplines of oxygen and nearby isotopes. Exploiting the novel self-consistent Gorkov-Green's function approach, we present the first investigation of Ar, K, Ca, Sc and Ti isotopic chains. Leading chiral 3N interactions are mandatory to reproduce the trend of binding energies throughout these chains and to obtain a good description of two-neutron separation energies. At the same time, nuclei in this mass region are systematically overbound by about 40 MeV and the $N=20$ magic gap is significantly overestimated. We conclude that ab-initio many-body calculations of mid-mass isotopic chains challenge modern theories of elementary nuclear interactions.

## Key claim

Leading chiral three-nucleon forces (3NFs) are mandatory to reproduce the correct trend of binding energies and two-neutron separation energies along Ar, K, Ca, Sc, and Ti isotopic chains, but the same interactions systematically overbind nuclei in this mass region by about 40 MeV and significantly overestimate the N=20 magic gap, challenging modern theories of elementary nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors employed the self-consistent Gorkov-Green's function (Gorkov-GF) approach at second order, extended to include three-nucleon forces. They started from chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at N3LO with cutoff 500 MeV and local N2LO 3NFs with cutoff 400 MeV, evolved via free-space similarity renormalization group (SRG) to a momentum scale of 2.0 fm^-1. Calculations were performed in harmonic oscillator model spaces up to 14 shells (Nmax=13) with frequency 28 MeV, solving the Gorkov equation iteratively to obtain correlated density matrices and computing binding energies via the Koltun sum rule corrected for 3NFs.

## Key numbers

- Systematic overbinding of nuclei in the Ca region by about 40 MeV
- For 40Ca, 48Ca, and 52Ca, ADC(3)-corrected energies with full 3NF overbind by 0.90, 0.73, and 0.72 MeV per nucleon, respectively
- The experimental Z=20 magic gap across 48Ca is 6.2 MeV; calculations give 16.5 MeV with NN plus induced 3NF and 12.4 MeV with full 3NF
- The difference between second-order Gorkov and Dyson-ADC(3) results for 40Ca, 48Ca, and 52Ca is -10.6, -12.1, and -12.6 MeV, respectively (about 2.7% of total binding energy)
- Changing model space from Nmax=12 to 13 lowers the ground-state energy of 51K by 2.1 MeV (about 0.5% of total binding energy)
- Convergence error on two-neutron separation energies is estimated at most about 500 keV

## Context

This work extends ab-initio calculations beyond the oxygen mass region, where previous studies (Cipollone 2013, Hergert 2013) showed that NNLO chiral 3NFs correctly reproduce binding energies and dripline locations for oxygen, nitrogen, and fluorine isotopes. The present results confirm and extend microscopic shell model calculations (Holt 2011) and coupled cluster calculations (Hagen 2012) for Ca isotopes, but within a full-fledged ab-initio approach for the first time. The findings challenge current chiral interactions and suggest that improvements are needed to correctly describe saturation properties of nuclear matter and shell structure in mid-mass nuclei.
