# Production of new neutron-rich heavy nuclei with $Z=56-80$ in the multinucleon transfer reactions of $^{136}$Xe+$^{198}$Pt

> The multinucleon transfer reactions in collisions of $^{136}$Xe+$^{198}$Pt at incident energies $E_{\textrm{lab}}=$5.25, 6.20, 7.98, 10.0, and 15.0 MeV/nucleon are investigated by using the improved quantum molecular dynamics model. It is found that 6.20 MeV/nucleon is the optimal incident energy for producing the neutron-rich heavy nuclei. About 80 unknown neutron-rich nuclei might be produced in this reaction with cross sections from 10$^{-6}$ to 10$^{-2}$ mb. The angular distributions of the neutron-rich isotopes are predicted.

# Production of new neutron-rich heavy nuclei with Z=56-80 in the multinucleon transfer reactions of 136Xe+198Pt

## Key claim
Using the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, the authors find that 6.20 MeV/nucleon is the optimal incident energy for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclei in 136Xe+198Pt collisions, with about 80 unknown neutron-rich nuclei predicted to be produced at cross sections between 10^-6 and 10^-2 mb. The claim is conditional on the model's predictions and the specific detection angular range of 20° to 60° in the laboratory frame being suitable for experimental observation.

## Method
The authors employed the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model with the IQ2 parameter set to simulate 136Xe+198Pt collisions at five incident energies (5.25, 6.20, 7.98, 10.0, and 15.0 MeV/nucleon). The dynamic simulation was run for 2000 fm/c, after which the GEMINI code handled the de-excitation of primary fragments via evaporation. Only two-fragment exit channels were considered, and the model was validated against experimental data from Watanabe et al. (2015) at 7.98 MeV/nucleon for isotopic distributions of projectile-like and target-like fragments.

## Key numbers
- Optimal incident energy for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclei: 6.20 MeV/nucleon
- About 80 unknown neutron-rich nuclei with atomic numbers Z=56-80 are predicted to be produced with cross sections ranging from 10^-6 to 10^-2 mb
- 200W predicted to be produced with cross section 4.4 microbarns
- 199Ta predicted to be produced with cross section 8.1 microbarns
- Suitable angular range for detecting new neutron-rich heavy nuclei: 20° < theta_lab < 60°
- Grazing angle for target-like fragments at 6.20 MeV/nucleon: approximately 60°
- Five incident energies studied: 5.25, 6.20, 7.98, 10.0, and 15.0 MeV/nucleon

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of multinucleon transfer reactions using the ImQMD model, specifically following the authors' earlier work on 136Xe+198Pt at 7.98 MeV/nucleon (Li et al., 2015). The study is motivated by the experiment of Watanabe et al. (2015) at GANIL, which found that production cross sections of N=126 isotones were significantly larger than those from fragmentation reactions of 208Pb on Be targets, but did not observe new neutron-rich nuclei due to the VAMOS++ detection limit of 10^-2 mb. The results provide theoretical guidance for planned experiments using the Fragment Mass Analyzer at Argonne National Laboratory (Zhu et al.) and the gas-filled separator at the High Intensity heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (Huang et al.), enabling selection of optimal beam energy and detector placement for producing new neutron-rich heavy nuclei.
