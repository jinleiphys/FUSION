# Study of the crossover transition of a gas of extended hadrons

> We formulate a simple model for a gas of extended hadrons at zero chemical potential by taking inspiration from the compressible bag model. We show that a crossover transition qualitatively similar to lattice QCD can be reproduced by such a system by including some appropriate additional dynamics. Under certain conditions, at high temperature, the system consists of a finite number of infinitely extended bags, which occupy the entire space. In this situation the system behaves as an ideal gas of quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

A gas of extended hadrons, modeled as compressible bags of quark-gluon plasma with excluded volume corrections and elastic interactions, can produce a crossover transition qualitatively similar to lattice QCD, but only for a specific range of the mass spectrum parameter alpha (1 ≤ α ≤ 5/2). The crossover emerges without explicitly including deconfined quark and gluon degrees of freedom in the partition function.

## Method

The authors constructed a partition function for a gas of extended hadrons at zero chemical potential, treating hadrons as compressible bags of quark-gluon plasma with a Hagedorn-type mass spectrum ρ(m) ∝ exp(m/T₀)/m^α. They imposed a pressure balance condition p_r = B + p_k(V,T) between the internal bag pressure p_r, the bag constant B, and the thermally generated kinetic pressure p_k, which leads to a self-consistency relation for the partition function. The analysis used Boltzmann statistics and a non-relativistic framework, which is valid near the transition region.

## Key numbers

- For α > 5/2 the system exhibits a first order phase transition; for α ≤ 5/2 the transition becomes a sharp crossover.
- For α = 0 and 1/2, the entropy density ratio s/T³ grows with temperature with larger slopes for smaller α.
- For 1 ≤ α ≤ 5/2, the entropy density ratio s/T³ settles onto constant asymptotic values, converging to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit from above as α changes from 1 to 5/2.
- There exists a limiting value α₀ between 2.12 and 2.13 such that for α₀ < α ≤ 5/2 the particle density vanishes at high temperature, while for α < α₀ the particle density grows with temperature.

## Context

This work extends the compressible bag model approach of Gorenstein (1998) and the authors' own detailed study (Ferroni and Koch, 2008) by showing that elastic interactions between hadrons and excluded volume corrections can produce a crossover transition without explicit deconfinement. The results enable further investigation of how a purely hadronic description can mimic the thermodynamic behavior seen in lattice QCD calculations, particularly the approach to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high temperature.
