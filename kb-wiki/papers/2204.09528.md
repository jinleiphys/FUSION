# Study of $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ structures via $γ^*p\to N^*$ transitions

> The helicity amplitudes of the $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ resonances in the $γ^*p\to N^*$ electromagnetic transition are studied in the constituent quark model using the impulse approximation, with the proton and resonances assumed to be in three-quark configurations. The comparison of theoretical results and experimental data on the helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$, $A_{3/2}$, and $S_{1/2}$ indicates that the $N(1520)$ and $N(1535)$ resonances are primarily composed of three-quark $L=1$ states but may contain additional components. However, it is improbable that contributions from meson clouds will be dominant at low $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The helicity amplitudes of the N(1520) and N(1535) resonances in the gamma*p to N* transition are primarily described by three-quark L=1 states, but the fits show significant discrepancies for the A3/2 and S1/2 amplitudes at low and medium Q2, indicating that additional components beyond the three-quark picture are needed; however, the authors argue that meson-cloud contributions are unlikely to be dominant at low Q2 because the A1/2 amplitudes are well fitted over the entire Q2 range.

## Method

The authors used a constituent quark model with the impulse approximation, where the electromagnetic current is induced by free single-quark currents, and assumed both the proton and the resonances are in three-quark configurations. They performed three fitting procedures: Fitting A (N(1520) and N(1535) fitted together with the same L=1 three-quark state), Fitting B (N(1520) alone as an L=1 state), and Fitting C (N(1535) alone as an L=1 state). The proton spatial wave function was adopted from a previous study of the proton electric form factor (PhysRevD.105.016008), and the spin, flavor, and color wave functions were characterized by SU(2), SU(3), and SU(3) symmetries respectively.

## Key numbers

- The three-quark picture provides reasonable fits for all helicity amplitudes at large square momentum transfers, Q2 greater than 2.0 GeV2, for both N(1520) and N(1535) resonances.
- The u and d quark masses were taken as m = 5 MeV in the calculations.
- The experimental data for A1/2 of N(1520) split into two clusters: an upper cluster from Aznauryan (2009) and a lower cluster from Drechsel (2007) and Tiator (2009).
- The A1/2 amplitudes for both N(1520) and N(1535) are well fitted over the entire Q2 range, contradicting the argument that meson-cloud contributions dominate at low Q2.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach previously applied to the proton electric form factor and the gamma*p to N(1440) transition (PhysRevD.105.016008) to the N(1520) and N(1535) resonances. The results are consistent with other quark model calculations at high Q2, such as the light-front relativistic quark model (Capstick, 1994) and covariant spectator quark model (PhysRevD.95.054008), but the discrepancies at low and medium Q2 suggest the need for additional components like meson clouds, meson-baryon molecules, or pentaquarks, as previously explored in studies including pentaquark components (PhysRevC.77.065207; An, 2009) and the mass ordering problem (PhysRevD.101.076025). The authors state that calculations of higher-order contributions from meson clouds, gluon exchange diagrams, and pentaquark components are currently underway.
