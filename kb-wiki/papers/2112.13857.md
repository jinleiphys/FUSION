# Energy dependence of the chiral magnetic effect in expanding holographic plasma

> Based on a holographic far-from-equilibrium calculation of the chiral magnetic effect~(CME) in an expanding quark gluon plasma, we study collisions at various energies. We compute the time evolution of the CME current in the presence of a time-dependent axial charge density and subject to a time-dependent magnetic field. The plasma expansion leads to a dilution of the CME current. We study distinct combinations of how the initial magnetic field and initial axial charge behave with changing initial energy as proposed in previous literature. Most scenarios we consider lead to an increasing time-integrated CME current, when increasing the initial energy. This would make it more likely to observe the CME at higher collision energies.

## Key claim
The authors find that in most scenarios considered, the time-integrated chiral magnetic effect (CME) current increases with increasing initial collision energy, making the CME more likely to be observed at higher collision energies, contrary to a previous non-expanding holographic study (Ghosh 2021) which found significant CME current at RHIC energies but not at LHC energies.

## Method
The authors construct a holographic model of strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma undergoing Bjorken expansion along the beamline, with a time-dependent magnetic field along one transverse direction and a time-dependent axial charge density. They solve the five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations numerically in generalized Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, evolving from a known initial state at proper time τ₀ through far-from-equilibrium dynamics. The initial conditions fix the magnetic field, axial charge density, and vanishing electric field, while the initial CME current is set to zero, allowing the current to grow and be diluted as the plasma expands.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field decays as B ∝ τ⁻¹, and the axial charge density decays as ⟨J⁰₍₅₎⟩ ∝ τ⁻¹, consistent with Bjorken-like magnetohydrodynamic expectations.
- The Chern-Simons coupling is set to α_QCD = 6/19 to match the QCD chiral anomaly.
- The number of colors is set to N_c = 3, and the AdS radius is chosen as L = 1 fm.
- The initial time is τ₀, and the initial CME current is zero for all evolutions: ⟨J¹(τ₀)⟩ = 0.

## Context
This work extends the non-expanding holographic CME study of Ghosh (2021) by including plasma expansion, time-dependent axial charge and magnetic field, and transverse anisotropy. It also extends ideal magnetohydrodynamic Bjorken-expanding treatments such as Siddique (2019). The results address uncertainties in interpreting the STAR Collaboration's isobar run data at 200 GeV, where no CME signal was found under pre-blind criteria, by showing that higher collision energies may produce larger CME signals in most scenarios. The model enables future studies of how initial state parameters affect CME observables in heavy-ion collisions.
