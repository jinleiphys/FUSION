# Production of strange and charmed baryons in pion induced reactions

> We study the strangeness and charm productions induced by the pion beam, i.e. the $π^-$p $\rightarrow$ $K^{*0}Λ$ and $π^-$p $\rightarrow$ $D^{*-}Λ_c^+$ reactions, based on two different theoretical frameworks: an effective Lagrangian method and a Regge approach. In order to estimate the magnitude of the charm production relative to that of the strangeness production, we assume that the coupling constants for the charmed meson and baryon vertices are the same as those for the strangeness channel. We found that the total cross section for the charm production was about $10^3 - 10^6$ times smaller than that for the strangeness production, depending on theoretical approaches and kinematical regions. We also discuss each contribution to observables for both reactions. In general, the Regge approach explains the experimental data very well over the whole energy region.

# Production of strange and charmed baryons in pion induced reactions

## Key claim

The total cross section for charm production ($\pi^- p \to D^{*-}\Lambda_c^+$) is estimated to be $10^3$ to $10^6$ times smaller than that for strangeness production ($\pi^- p \to K^{*0}\Lambda$), under the assumption that the coupling constants for charmed meson and baryon vertices are taken to be the same as those for the strangeness channel, with the scaling $g_{\pi D^* D^*} = (M_{K^*}/M_{D^*}) \cdot g_{\pi K^* K^*}$ applied for the dimensionful coupling.

## Method

The authors employed two theoretical frameworks: an effective Lagrangian method at tree level with $t$-, $s$-, and $u$-channel exchanges and a Regge approach using $K$, $K^*$, and $\Sigma$ trajectories. For the effective Lagrangian method, coupling constants were taken from the Nijmegen soft-core potential (NSC97a) and experimental decay widths, with cutoff masses $\Lambda_{K,K^*} = 0.55$ GeV and $\Lambda_{N,\Sigma} = 0.60$ GeV for form factors. The Regge amplitudes were derived by replacing Feynman propagators with Regge propagators using trajectories from Brisudova (2000) and Storrow (1984), with energy scale parameters determined from the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) of Kaidalov and collaborators, and an additional residual factor $C_{\mathrm{ex}}(p^2) = a/(1 - p^2/\Lambda^2)^2$ was introduced to improve the $s$ and $t$ dependence.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant $g_{\pi K K^*} = 6.56$ determined from the decay width $\Gamma(K^* \to K\pi)$
- Coupling constant $g_{\pi K^* K^*} = 7.45$ GeV$^{-1}$ from hidden local gauge symmetry and flavor SU(3)
- Coupling constants from Nijmegen potential: $g_{\pi N N} = 13.3$, $g_{\pi \Sigma \Lambda} = 11.9$, $g_{K N \Lambda} = -13.4$, $g_{K^* N \Lambda} = -4.26$, $\kappa_{K^* N \Lambda} = 2.91$, $g_{K^* N \Sigma} = -2.46$, $\kappa_{K^* N \Sigma} = -0.529$
- Threshold $s_{\mathrm{th}} = (m_{K^*} + m_\Lambda)^2 = 4.05$ GeV$^2$ for strangeness production; $s_{\mathrm{th}} = (m_{D^*} + m_{\Lambda_c})^2 = 18.4$ GeV$^2$ for charm production
- Total cross section for charm production is about $10^4$ times smaller than for strangeness near threshold and about $10^3$ times smaller at $s/s_{\mathrm{th}} = 10$ in the effective Lagrangian method
- Regge trajectories: $\alpha_K(t) = -0.151 + 0.617t$, $\alpha_{K^*}(t) = 0.414 + 0.707t$, $\alpha_\Sigma(u) = -0.79 + 0.87u$
- Energy scale parameters from QGSM: $s_K = 1.752$, $s_{K^*} = 1.662$, $s_\Sigma = 1.569$
- Cutoff masses for effective Lagrangian: $\Lambda_{K,K^*} = 0.55$ GeV, $\Lambda_{N,\Sigma} = 0.60$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier investigation by Kim (2014) which studied differential cross sections for the same reactions using a simple Regge model, and builds on the quark-gluon string model of Kaidalov and collaborators (1982-1994), particularly the study by Boreskov (1983) of $\pi^- p \to D^-\Lambda_c^+$ using only the $D^*$ reggeon. The present work includes contributions from $D$, $D^*$, and $\Sigma_c$ reggeons for the charm channel. The results are relevant to a new proposal at J-PARC for studying charmed baryons via pion-induced reactions at a high-momentum beam line, following the only earlier experimental search at Brookhaven National Laboratory by Christenson (1985). The effective Lagrangian method describes data well near threshold but deviates at higher energies, while the Regge approach is expected to explain experimental data over the whole energy region with unitarity preserved.
