# On spectroscopy of rho', rho'', and omega', omega'' resonances

> Based on coupling constants extracted from fitting various data, the selected branching ratios and full widths of rho', rho'', and omega', omega'' resonances are calculated, and some topics on the spectroscopy of these states are discussed.

## Key claim

Based on coupling constants extracted from fits to various data, the selected branching ratios and full widths of rho', rho'', omega', and omega'' resonances are calculated, but the authors caution that large widths, rapid phase space growth with energy, and resonance mixing cause visible peaks in cross sections to shift significantly from bare masses, so one should be careful in attributing a specific peak to a specific spectroscopy state.

## Method

The authors use a field-theory-inspired approach to resonance mixing, constructing a matrix of inverse propagators for two or more mixed states and diagonalizing it to obtain physical states and couplings. They fit data in a three-resonance scheme for each channel where indications of specific resonances exist, extracting coupling constants from reactions including e+e- annihilation, tau lepton decays, and J/psi decays. The partial widths are calculated from the extracted coupling constants, accounting for the strong energy dependence of decay widths and the mass shifts induced by mixing.

## Key numbers

- For rho'_1 (rho(1450)): mass from e+e- -> pi+pi- is 1370 (+90, -70) MeV; total width from same channel is 763 +/- 500 MeV; leptonic width is 6.4 (+1.2, -1.4) keV; branching ratio to omega pi0 is 86.5 +/- 41.5 percent
- For rho'_2 (rho(1700)): mass from e+e- -> omega pi0 is 1710 +/- 90 MeV; total width from e+e- -> omega pi0 is 1886 +/- 613 MeV; branching ratio to 4pi from same channel is 61.2 +/- 7.8 percent
- For omega'_1 (omega(1420)): mass from e+e- -> pi+pi-pi0 is 1430 (+110, -70) MeV; total width from same channel is approximately 903 MeV; leptonic width is 144 (+94, -58) eV
- For omega'_2 (omega(1600)): mass from e+e- -> omega pi+pi- is 2000 +/- 180 MeV; total width from same channel is approximately 5757 MeV; leptonic width is 531 +/- 225 eV
- Estimated partial widths: Gamma(rho'_1 -> omega pi) ~ 280 MeV at m ~ 1400 MeV; Gamma(omega'_1 -> rho pi) ~ 820 MeV at m ~ 1400 MeV; Gamma(rho'_2 -> omega pi) ~ 880 MeV at m ~ 1750 MeV; Gamma(omega'_2 -> rho pi) ~ 2600 MeV at m ~ 1750 MeV
- The coupling constants extracted from fits are in the intervals |g(rho'_1 omega pi)| = 10-18 GeV^{-1} and |g(rho'_2 omega pi)| = 2-13 GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of vector meson spectroscopy (Achasov and Kozhevnikov, 1997, 1998, 2000) and uses data from the SND detector (Achasov et al., 1999, 2001) and the LASS experiment (Aston et al., 1992). The results suggest that rho(1300) observed by LASS should be attributed to the same state as rho(1450) in the Particle Data Group listings, and that omega(1200) observed by SND is the same state as omega(1420). The very large widths found for rho'_2 and omega'_2 contradict the assignment to the 1^3D_1 state predicted by Godfrey and Isgur (1985) to be relatively narrow, though large errors prevent a final conclusion. The authors note that improved statistics from the VEPP-2000 collider will help resolve these issues.
