# Quarkonium-hadron interactions in QCD

> The leading order non-relativistic QCD result for the quarkonium-hadron interaction cross section is derived by using the QCD factorization formula combined with the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the heavy quark bound state. Our result shows the equivalence of this approach with the operator product expansion even for the bound state scattering to leading order in QCD. We also apply the formula to the charmonium system and investigate the relativistic correction coming from the phase space of the reaction. We find that although $J/ψ$-gluon cross-section gets a large relativistic correction at higher energies, the correction becomes small in the $J/ψ$-hadron cross section due to the increase of the gluon distribution at smaller $x$ inside the hadron.

# Quarkonium-Hadron Interactions in QCD

## Key claim

The leading-order non-relativistic QCD result for quarkonium-hadron scattering cross sections, previously derived using the operator product expansion (OPE) by Peskin (1979) and Bhanot and Peskin (1979), can be equivalently obtained using the QCD factorization formula combined with the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the heavy quark bound state; however, the relativistic correction from phase space, while large for the J/ψ-gluon cross section at higher energies, becomes small in the J/ψ-hadron cross section because the gluon distribution inside the hadron increases at small x, meaning the hadron-level cross section is dominated by the reliably calculated low-energy behavior of the gluon-level cross section.

## Method

The authors derive the Φ-gluon scattering amplitude using the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the heavy quarkonium bound state in the non-relativistic limit, obtaining the scattering cross section σΦg from the ground-state Coulombic wave function. They then compute the Φ-hadron cross section σΦh via the QCD factorization formula σΦh(ν) = ∫₀¹ dx σΦg(xν) g(x), where g(x) is the leading-twist gluon distribution function of the hadron, using three different parameterizations for the gluon distribution (BP, MRST, and GRV-LO). Relativistic corrections are investigated by replacing the non-relativistic phase space integral with the full relativistic phase space calculation.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy ε₀ = 780 MeV and heavy quark mass m = 1.95 GeV, determined by fitting J/ψ and ψ'(2S) masses to a Coulombic spectrum (Bhanot and Peskin, 1979).
- For the 1S ground state (J/ψ): σΦg(λ) = (128 g²)/(3 N_c) a₀² (λ/ε₀ − 1)^(3/2) / (λ/ε₀)⁵, where a₀ = 16π/(g² N_c m).
- For the 2S excited state (ψ'): σΦ'g = (16 g²)/(N_c²) a₀² (λ/ε₀ − 1/4)^(3/2) (λ/ε₀ − 3/4)² / (λ/ε₀)⁷.
- The ratio σψ'(2S)-p / σJ/ψ-p peaks at √s ≈ 4.2 GeV with a peak value of about 2000–5000, converging to 4–20 at higher energies depending on the gluon distribution function.
- The J/ψ → e⁺e⁻ decay width is given by Γ(Φ → e⁺e⁻) = (16π α_em² Q_h² N_c)/(3 M_Φ²) |ψ(r=0)|².

## Context

This work extends the foundational operator product expansion approach of Peskin (1979) and Bhanot and Peskin (1979) by demonstrating equivalence with the QCD factorization method for bound-state scattering, and applies the formalism to charmonium. The results are relevant for understanding J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions (Matsui and Satz, 1986; Vogt, 1999), where the J/ψ-hadron inelastic scattering cross section determines the amount of suppression from hadronic final-state interactions. The authors note that existing model calculations using meson exchange models, quark exchange models (Wong, Swanson, and Barnes, 2001), and QCD sum rules (Navarra, Nielsen, Maia, and Krein, 2002) give very different energy dependence and magnitude near threshold, highlighting the importance of nonperturbative effects. This work enables future calculations of higher-twist gluonic effects and extension to χ states, which influence J/ψ production from χ decays in p-A or A-A reactions.
