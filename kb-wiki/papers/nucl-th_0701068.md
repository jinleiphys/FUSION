# Are spectroscopic factors from transfer reactions consistent with asymptotic normalisation coefficients?

> It is extremely important to devise a reliable method to extract spectroscopic factors from transfer cross sections. We analyse the standard DWBA procedure and combine it with the asymptotic normalisation coefficient, extracted from an independent data set. We find that the single particle parameters used in the past generate inconsistent asymptotic normalization coefficients. In order to obtain a consistent spectroscopic factor, non-standard parameters for the single particle overlap functions can be used but, as a consequence, often reduced spectroscopic strengths emerge. Different choices of optical potentials and higher order effects in the reaction model are also studied. Our test cases consist of: $^{14}$C(d,p)$^{15}$C(g.s.) at $E_d^{lab}=14$ MeV, $^{16}$O(d,p)$^{17}$O(g.s.) at $E_d^{lab}=15$ MeV and $^{40}$Ca(d,p)$^{41}$Ca(g.s.) at $E_d^{lab}=11$ MeV. We underline the importance of performing experiments specifically designed to extract ANCs for these systems.

## Key claim

The standard DWBA procedure for extracting spectroscopic factors from (d,p) transfer reactions produces results that are inconsistent with asymptotic normalisation coefficients (ANCs) obtained from independent measurements, unless non-standard single-particle parameters are used, which then often yield reduced spectroscopic strengths.

## Method

The authors performed finite-range post-form DWBA calculations for three (d,p) transfer reactions: 14C(d,p)15C(g.s.) at Ed=14 MeV, 16O(d,p)17O(g.s.) at Ed=15 MeV, and 40Ca(d,p)41Ca(g.s.) at Ed=11 MeV. They varied the single-particle radius r0 of the bound-state Woods-Saxon potential while keeping diffuseness fixed at a=0.65 fm, extracted spectroscopic factors from normalising the calculated transfer peak to experimental data, and computed the resulting ANCs from the relation C2 = S * b2. They compared these ANCs to independently measured values from sub-Coulomb reactions or knockout data, and also tested the effects of using fitted deuteron optical potentials, the adiabatic deuteron breakup approximation (ADWA), coupled reaction channels (CRC), and coupled channel Born approximation (CCBA) including target excitations.

## Key numbers

- For 14C(d,p)15C(g.s.) at Ed=14 MeV, the experimental ANC is C2_s1/2 = 1.48 ± 0.18 fm-1; with standard radius r0=1.21 fm and global deuteron potential, the extracted SF is 1.27 and the resulting ANC is 2.541 fm-1, inconsistent with experiment.
- For 16O(d,p)17O(g.s.) at Ed=15 MeV, the experimental ANC is C2_d5/2 = 0.67 ± 0.05 fm-1; with standard r0=1.21 fm and global deuteron potential, the extracted SF is 1.05 and the resulting ANC is 0.745 fm-1.
- For 40Ca(d,p)41Ca(g.s.) at Ed=11 MeV, the experimental ANC is C2_f7/2 = 8.36 ± 0.42 fm-1; with standard r0=1.21 fm and global deuteron potential, the extracted SF is 0.834 and the resulting ANC is 3.647 fm-1, nearly a factor of 2 below the experimental value.
- Using fitted deuteron optical potentials (DWBAf), the SF/ANC inconsistency remained for 14C and 40Ca, but for 16O a spectroscopic factor of 0.6-1 produced ANCs much closer to the experimental value of 0.67 fm-1.
- ADWA calculations reduced spectroscopic factors overall but did not resolve the inconsistency, particularly for 40Ca where a persistent factor of 2 remained.
- CRC effects for 40Ca(d,p)41Ca increased the transfer cross section by about 20%, producing lower SFs and making the inconsistency more severe.

## Context

This work extends the combined method introduced by the same authors (Pang, Nunes, Mukhamedzhanov, 2006) that uses ANCs from independent peripheral measurements to constrain the exterior part of the transfer amplitude and reduce bound-state parameter ambiguity. It contradicts the assumption that standard single-particle parameters (r0=1.2 fm, a=0.65 fm) which yield unit SFs for closed-shell nuclei are consistent with measured ANCs, and shows that even with improved reaction models (ADWA, CRC, CCBA) the inconsistency persists for most cases. The results enable future work by emphasising the need for experiments specifically designed to extract ANCs for these systems, and by demonstrating that reliable spectroscopic factors from transfer reactions require both accurate ANCs and careful treatment of reaction mechanism uncertainties.
