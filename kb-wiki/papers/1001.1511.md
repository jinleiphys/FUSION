# Electromagnetic properties of the Be-11 nucleus in Halo EFT

> We compute electromagnetic properties of the Be-11 nucleus using an effective field theory that exploits the separation of scales in this halo system. We fix the parameters of the EFT from measured data on levels and scattering lengths in the Be-10 plus neutron system. We then obtain predictions for the B(E1) strength of the 1/2^+ to 1/2^- transition in the Be-11 nucleus. We also compute the charge radius of the ground state of Be-11. Agreement with experiment within the expected accuracy of a leading-order computation in this EFT is obtained. We also indicate how higher-order corrections that affect both s-wave and p-wave Be-10-neutron interactions will affect our results.

## Key claim

The leading-order Halo EFT predictions for the charge radius and B(E1) strength of Be-11 agree with experiment only to within the expected ~40% accuracy of the expansion parameter (R_core/R_halo ≈ 0.4), not to the precision of the data; the abstract's "agreement with experiment" is therefore qualified by the large theoretical uncertainty at leading order.

## Method

The authors construct a Halo EFT with Be-10 core and neutron degrees of freedom, including contact interactions for s-wave and p-wave channels that generate the shallow 1/2+ (504 keV separation) and 1/2- (184 keV separation) bound states. Parameters are fixed from the binding energies and the measured n-Be-10 p-wave scattering volume a_1 = 457 ± 67 fm^3. Electromagnetic couplings are introduced via minimal substitution, and the charge form factor of the ground state and the E1 transition amplitude between the s-wave and p-wave states are computed at leading order using the dressed propagators for the σ (s-wave) and π (p-wave) dimer fields.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energies: 504 keV for the 1/2+ ground state, 184 keV for the 1/2- excited state
- p-wave scattering volume: a_1 = 457 ± 67 fm^3
- Leading-order Halo EFT prediction for the charge radius of the Be-11 ground state: ⟨r_c²⟩^{1/2} = 1.73 fm (compared to experimental atomic-physics value 2.463(16) fm)
- Leading-order Halo EFT prediction for B(E1)(1/2+ → 1/2-): 0.085 e² fm² (compared to experimental value 0.105(12) e² fm² from Coulomb excitation)
- Momentum scales: γ_0 = 0.15 fm⁻¹ (from 504 keV binding), γ_1 = 0.09 fm⁻¹ (from 184 keV binding)
- p-wave effective range at LO: r_1 = -0.54 fm⁻¹; at NLO: r_1 = -0.72 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT formalism developed by Bertulani, Hammer, and van Kolck (2002) and Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (2003) to electromagnetic observables in Be-11, providing a systematic alternative to earlier analytic halo models (Typel and Baur, 2004, 2005, 2008) that suffered from regulator dependence. The approach is complementary to ab initio calculations (Forssen et al., 2005, 2009; Quaglioni and Navratil, 2008) which struggle with the widely separated core and halo scales. The next step is to fix the s-wave effective range r_0 from breakup data to obtain NLO predictions, and to apply the method to other one-neutron halos and two-neutron halos such as Li-11.
