# Nuclear isomers: structures and applications

> Isomeric states in the nuclei along the rapid proton capture process path are studied by the projected shell model. Emphasis is given to two waiting point nuclei Se-68 and Kr-72 that are characterized by shape coexistence. Energy surface calculations indicate that the ground state of these nuclei corresponds to an oblate-deformed minimum, while the lowest state at the prolate-deformed minimum can be considered as a shape isomer. Due to occupation of the orbitals with large K-components, states built upon two-quasiparticle excitations at the oblate-deformed minimum may form high $K$-isomers. The impact of the isomer states on isotopic abundance in X-ray bursts is studied in a multi-mass-zone X-ray burst model by assuming an upper-lower limit approach.

# Nuclear isomers: structures and applications

## Key claim
The paper predicts that shape isomers and high-K isomers in the waiting point nuclei 68Se and 72Kr could alter the rp-process reaction flow in X-ray bursts, but the actual impact on isotopic abundances is relatively modest under the upper-lower limit assumptions used in the multi-mass-zone model.

## Method
The projected shell model was used to calculate energy surfaces and level schemes for 68Se and 72Kr as functions of the deformation variable ε2. The impact of the predicted isomer states on isotopic abundances was then studied in a multi-mass-zone X-ray burst model by comparing two extreme cases: no isomer contribution versus full reaction flow through the shape isomers.

## Key numbers
- The prolate minimum lies 1.1 MeV above the oblate ground state in 68Se and 0.7 MeV above in 72Kr.
- The observed shape isomer in 72Kr has an excitation energy of 671 keV with half-life τ = 38 ± 3 ns (Bouchez et al.).
- At the peak X-ray burst temperature of 1.1 GK, thermal population of the isomer states is ≤ 0.02% for 68Se (1.1 MeV isomer) and ≤ 0.5% for 72Kr (0.67 MeV isomer).
- The estimated lifetime against proton capture on the isomers is in the range of approximately 100 ns to 10 μs, depending on environmental density.
- The estimated proton decay lifetimes for 69Br and 73Rb are less than 24 ns and 30 ns, respectively (Pfaff et al.).

## Context
This work extends earlier projected shell model calculations for the A ≈ 70-80 mass region (Sun 2004) and builds on the observation of a 671 keV shape isomer in 72Kr by Bouchez et al. The paper also references Kaneko et al. (2004) for similar isomer calculations. The results enable future studies of how isomers with sufficiently long lifetimes (probably longer than microseconds) could alter rp-process reaction paths and elemental abundances, though the authors note this aspect of nuclear isomers is still in its infancy (Aprahamian 2005).
