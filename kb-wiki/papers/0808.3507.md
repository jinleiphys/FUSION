# Pairing Vibrations Study with the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> We study pairing vibrations in $^{18,20,22}$O and $^{42,44,46}$Ca nuclei solving the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation in coordinate space with spherical symmetry. We use the SLy4 Skyrme functional in the normal part of the energy density functional and a local density dependent functional in its pairing part. Pairing vibrations are excited by two-neutron transfer operators. Strength distributions are obtained using the Fourier transform of the time-dependent response of two-neutron pair-transfer observables in the linear regime. Results are in overall agreement with quasiparticle random phase approximation calculations for Oxygen isotopes, though differences appear when increasing the neutron number. Both low lying pairing modes and giant pairing vibrations (GPV) are discussed. The GPV is observed in the Oxygen but not in the Calcium isotopes.

# Pairing Vibrations Study with the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

## Key claim

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equation with a full Skyrme functional can reproduce quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) results for pairing vibrations in Oxygen isotopes, but differences appear when increasing neutron number; the giant pairing vibration (GPV) is observed in Oxygen isotopes but not in Calcium isotopes, contrary to what might be expected from QRPA systematics.

## Method

The authors solved the TDHFB equation in coordinate space with spherical symmetry, using the SLy4 Skyrme functional for the normal part of the energy density functional and a local density dependent functional for the pairing part. Pairing vibrations were excited by applying a two-neutron transfer operator as a boost at initial time, and strength distributions were obtained by Fourier transforming the time-dependent response of pair-transfer observables in the linear regime. The calculations used a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 80 MeV and a mesh step of 0.15 fm, with time steps of 0.003 fm/c for Oxygen and 0.002 fm/c for Calcium isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Pairing coupling constant: g = -275.25 MeV, with parameters rho_c = 0.32 fm^-3 and gamma = 1, fitted to reproduce a neutron spectral gap of 1.25 MeV in ^120Sn
- Energy cutoff for quasiparticle spectrum regularization: 80 MeV, allowing two-quasiparticle excitations up to 160 MeV
- Maximum total angular momentum for neutrons: j_c = 19/2 for Oxygen isotopes, 23/2 for Calcium isotopes
- Box radius: 22.5 fm for Oxygen isotopes, 30 fm for Calcium isotopes
- Total time interval for calculations: T = 1200 fm/c
- Energy conservation maintained up to 15 keV; deviations in total particle number of order 10^-7
- In ^18O, two separated peaks observed at 6.5 MeV and 14.3 MeV, with additional peaks between 20 and 26 MeV

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach developed by Simenel (2003) and others to include pairing correlations through the TDHFB formalism, which had previously been limited to BCS ansatz with simple functionals (Negele, 1978; Cusson, 1979) or simple systems (Blocki and Flocard). The results are compared to QRPA calculations by Khan et al. (2008) for Oxygen isotopes. This study enables future investigations of pairing correlations in nuclear dynamics, including low-energy reaction mechanisms and anharmonicities beyond the QRPA range, and provides a framework for studying giant pairing vibrations that remain experimentally unobserved but may become accessible with radioactive ion beams.
