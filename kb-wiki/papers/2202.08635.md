# Leading twist GTMDs at nonzero skewness and Wigner distributions in boost-invariant longitudinal position space

> We investigate the leading twist quark generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) at nonzero skewness in a light-front quark-diquark model for the nucleon motivated by soft-wall AdS/QCD. The boost-invariant longitudinal coordinate, $σ=\frac{1}{2} b^- P^+$, is identified as the Fourier conjugate of the skewness. The Fourier transform of the GTMDs with respect to the skewness variable $ξ$ can be employed to provide the Wigner distributions in the boost-invariant longitudinal position space $σ$, the coordinate conjugate to light-front time, $τ=t+z/c$. The Wigner distributions in the longitudinal position space exhibit diffraction patterns, which are analogous to the diffractive scattering of a wave in optics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Fourier transform of generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) with respect to the skewness variable xi provides Wigner distributions in a boost-invariant longitudinal position space sigma, and that these distributions exhibit diffraction patterns analogous to optical diffractive scattering; however, this result is obtained within a specific light-front quark-diquark model motivated by soft-wall AdS/QCD, not from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The authors compute all sixteen leading-twist quark GTMDs at nonzero skewness within the DGLAP region using a light-front quark-diquark model for the nucleon, where both scalar and axial-vector diquark components are included and the light-front wave functions are constructed from two-particle effective wave functions obtained from soft-wall AdS/QCD. The quark-quark correlator is expressed in terms of overlaps of these light-front wave functions for different helicity combinations and Dirac structures (gamma+, gamma+ gamma5, and i sigma^{j+} gamma5), and the GTMDs are extracted via bilinear decomposition. The Wigner distributions in longitudinal position space are then obtained by Fourier transforming the GTMDs with respect to the skewness variable xi, where the boost-invariant longitudinal coordinate sigma = (1/2) b^- P^+ is identified as the Fourier conjugate.

## Key numbers

- The AdS/QCD scale parameter kappa = 0.4 GeV is used for the light-front wave functions
- The square of the total momentum transfer is given by -t = (4 xi^2 M^2 + bf d^2)/(1 - xi^2), where bf d is the transverse momentum transfer
- The model parameters a_i^nu, b_i^nu, and delta^nu are determined from fitting flavor-decomposed Dirac and Pauli form factors data, as listed in Refs. Maji (2016) and Maji (2017)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of nucleon GTMDs, which were mostly performed at zero skewness (xi=0), to the physically relevant nonzero skewness region; prior model calculations include the light-cone constituent quark model (Lorce, 2011), the light-front dressed quark model (Mukherjee, 2014; Mukherjee, 2015; More, 2017), the chiral soliton model (Lorce, 2011), the light-cone spectator model (Liu, 2015), and the light-front quark-diquark model at zero skewness (Chakrabarti, 2016; Chakrabarti, 2017; Chakrabarti, 2019; Gutsche, 2016; Kaur, 2019; Kumar, 2017). The investigation of Wigner distributions in boost-invariant longitudinal position space via Fourier transform in xi follows the approach of Brodsky (2006) for DVCS amplitudes and GPDs, and the results enable direct analysis of experimental data where xi is always nonzero.
