# Low--Energy Theorems for Weak Pion Production

> We derive novel low--energy theorems for single pion production off nucleons through the isovector axial current. We find that the $k^2$-dependence of the multipole $L_{0+}^{(+)}$ at threshold is given by the nucleon scalar form factor, namely $σ(k^2-M_π^2 ) /(3 πM_πF_π)$. The relation to PCAC results for soft pions including electroweak form factors is also clarified.

## Key claim

The paper derives novel low-energy theorems for single pion production off nucleons via the isovector axial current, showing that the \(k^2\)-dependence of the threshold multipole \(L_{0+}^{(+)}\) is given by the nucleon scalar form factor \(\sigma(k^2-M_\pi^2)\). These results go beyond previous soft-pion PCAC approaches by including explicit chiral symmetry breaking corrections linear in the pion mass, which are non-analytic in the quark masses.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to compute the threshold S-wave multipoles for axial pion production up to one-loop order (\(\mathcal{O}(q^3)\)). They evaluate tree-level diagrams from the \(\mathcal{O}(q)\) and \(\mathcal{O}(q^2)\) Lagrangians and one-loop diagrams with insertions from the \(\mathcal{O}(q)\) Lagrangian, plus tree graphs with one insertion from \(\mathcal{O}(q^3)\). The resulting multipoles are expressed in terms of the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\), the axial coupling \(g_A\), the isovector anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_V\), the S-wave \(\pi N\) scattering lengths \(a^\pm\), the nucleon scalar form factor \(\sigma(t)\), and the isovector electric and magnetic Sachs form factors \(G_{E,M}^V(t)\).

## Key numbers

* The isospin-even S-wave multipole \(L_{0+}^{(+)}\) at threshold is given by \(L_{0+}^{(+)} = \frac{1}{3 \pi M_\pi F_\pi} \left\{ \sigma(k^2 - M_\pi^2) - \frac{1}{4} \sigma(0) \right\} - \frac{a^+ F_\pi}{M_\pi} - \frac{g_A^2 M_\pi}{16 \pi m F_\pi} + C_L^{(+)} M_\pi^2 + \mathcal{O}(q^3)\), with \(M_\pi = 139.57\) MeV, \(m = 938.27\) MeV, \(F_\pi = 93\) MeV, \(g_A = 1.26\), \(a^+ = -0.83 \times 10^{-2} / M_\pi\), and \(\sigma(0) = 45\) MeV.
* The slope of \(L_{0+}^{(+)}\) at the photon point \(k^2 = 0\) is \(\left. \frac{\partial L_{0+}^{(+)}}{\partial k^2} \right|_{k^2=0} = \frac{g_A^2}{128 \pi^2 F_\pi^3} \left( \frac{6}{5} - \arctan\frac{1}{2} \right) + \mathcal{O}(M_\pi)\).
* The isospin-even multipole \(H_{0+}^{(+)}\) at \(k^2 = 0\) is \(H_{0+}^{(+)} = 32.8 \, \text{GeV}^{-2} + (\sigma(0) - \sigma(-M_\pi^2)) \cdot 14.1 \, \text{GeV}^{-3} - 0.14 \, \text{GeV}^{-2}\), with the uncertainty in \(a^+\) (\(\delta a^+ = \pm 0.38 \times 10^{-2}/M_\pi\)) giving a comparable contribution to the scalar form factor term.
* The constants \(C_H^{(+)}\) and \(C_H^{(-)}\) are numerically \(C_H^{(+)} = -1.0\) GeV\(^{-3}\) and \(C_H^{(-)} = 0.5\) GeV\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier soft-pion results of Adler (1968), Adler and Dothan (1966), and Nambu, Lurie and Shrauner (1962) by including explicit chiral symmetry breaking corrections via chiral perturbation theory, which were absent in those PCAC-based approaches. It builds on the authors' previous work on pion photo- and electroproduction (Bernard, Gasser, Kaiser, Meissner, 1991; Bernard, Kaiser, Meissner, 1992) and generalizes those results to weak pion production. The derived low-energy theorems enable a new determination of the nucleon scalar form factor \(\sigma(t)\) and the \(\pi N\) sigma-term from neutrino-induced single pion production experiments, though the authors note that a complete calculation including the isovector vector current and the \(\Delta\) resonance is needed before experimental separation of the \(L_{0+}^{(+)}\) multipole is feasible.
