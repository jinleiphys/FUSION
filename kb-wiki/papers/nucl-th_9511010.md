# Restoration of Overlap Functions and Spectroscopic Factors in Nuclei

> An asymptotic restoration procedure is applied for analyzing bound--state overlap functions, separation energies and single--nucleon spectroscopic factors by means of a model one--body density matrix emerging from the Jastrow correlation method in its lowest order approximation for $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$ nuclei . Comparison is made with available experimental data and mean--field and natural orbital representation results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an asymptotic restoration procedure, applied to a model one-body density matrix that includes short-range correlations via the Jastrow method, can yield bound-state overlap functions, separation energies, and spectroscopic factors for \(^{16}\text{O}\) and \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) that are in acceptable agreement with experiment, though the calculated spectroscopic factors are systematically larger than experimental values due to the crude approximation used for the density matrix.

## Method

The authors apply the asymptotic restoration procedure of [r27] to a model one-body density matrix derived from the Jastrow correlation method in its lowest-order approximation, using a harmonic-oscillator Slater determinant and a Gaussian state-independent correlation factor. For each set of quantum numbers \(nl\) (the model does not split states with respect to \(j = l \pm 1/2\)), they extract the lowest bound-state overlap function, separation energy, and spectroscopic factor from the asymptotic behavior of the density matrix at large radii, then subtract its contribution to obtain higher bound states. The procedure is applied within an asymptotic region where the diagonal density matrix element is less than 10% of its maximal value, using a least-squares minimization to determine the decay constant and normalization.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{16}\text{O}\), the calculated separation energies for neutron states (e.g., \(1p_{1/2}\), \(1p_{3/2}\), \(1s_{1/2}\)) are in acceptable agreement with Hartree-Fock results and experimental data, though specific numerical values are not listed in the truncated text.
* For \(^{40}\text{Ca}\), the spectroscopic factor for the \(2s_{1/2}\) state is larger than that of the lower \(1s\) state, which the authors attribute to the crude density matrix approximation.
* The calculated spectroscopic factors \(S_\alpha\) satisfy the inequality \(S_{nlj} \le N_{nlj}^{\max}\), where \(N_{nlj}^{\max}\) is the largest natural occupation number for the same \(lj\) subspace, as required by theory.

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic restoration procedure of [r27] by applying it to a correlated one-body density matrix from the Jastrow method, rather than a mean-field or uncorrelated shell-model density. The results are compared with Hartree-Fock calculations [r36] and experimental data from \((e,e'p)\) reactions [r6], showing that short-range correlations cause depletion of hole states and filling of particle states, consistent with prior studies of natural orbitals [r31] and overlap functions [r16]. The method enables future application to more sophisticated density matrices (e.g., from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, variational Monte Carlo, or symplectic model calculations) to obtain realistic spectroscopic factors for one-nucleon removal reactions.
