# Nonextensive Nuclear Liquid-Gas Phase Transition

> We study an effective relativistic mean-field model of nuclear matter with arbitrary proton fraction at finite temperature in the framework of nonextensive statistical mechanics, characterized by power-law quantum distributions. We investigate the presence of thermodynamic instability in a warm and asymmetric nuclear medium and study the consequent nuclear liquid-gas phase transition by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number and electric charge fraction. We show that nonextensive statistical effects play a crucial role in the equation of state and in the formation of mixed phase also for small deviations from the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

# Nonextensive Nuclear Liquid-Gas Phase Transition

## Key claim

For small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (q = 1.05), the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric matter is significantly modified: the equation of state becomes stiffer, the chemical instability region becomes more pronounced, and the isospin fractionation effect is reduced, even though the nonextensive parameter q is close to unity.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field model (TM1 parameter set) with σ, ω, and ρ meson exchanges to describe nuclear matter at finite temperature and arbitrary proton fraction. They replaced the standard Fermi-Dirac distributions with q-deformed power-law distributions from Tsallis nonextensive statistical mechanics, then applied Gibbs conditions for global conservation of baryon number and electric charge to determine the liquid-gas phase coexistence boundaries and instability regions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature studied: T = 10 MeV for most calculations
- Proton fraction range investigated: 0 < y ≤ 0.5
- Nonextensive parameter values compared: q = 1 (standard Boltzmann-Gibbs) and q = 1.05
- Pressure values where chemical instability appears: below P = 0.35 MeV/fm³ at T = 10 MeV
- For y_A ≥ 0.3, the proton fractions of the two phases are closer to the initial value in the nonextensive case compared to the standard case
- The critical temperature T_c is reduced in the nonextensive case relative to the standard case

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field study of asymmetric nuclear matter by Müller and Serot (1995) by incorporating Tsallis nonextensive statistics, motivated by observations that high-energy heavy-ion collision data often exhibit power-law distributions consistent with q > 1 (Bediaga et al., Wilk and Wlodarczyk, Biro et al.). The results show that nonextensive effects produce similar qualitative changes to those previously seen when Coulomb interactions are included (Baran et al.), suggesting that the q-parameter may phenomenologically account for long-range correlations. This enables future comparisons with radioactive beam experiments and neutron star astrophysics, where the isospin dependence of the equation of state is critical.
