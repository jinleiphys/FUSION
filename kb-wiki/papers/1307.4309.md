# Relativistic description of final-state interactions in neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino cross sections

> We evaluate semi-inclusive neutral-current quasielastic differential neutrino and antineutrino cross sections within the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation. The results of the relativistic mean field and of the relativistic Green's function models are compared. The sensitivity to the strange-quark content of the nucleon form factor is also discussed. The results of the models are compared with the MiniBooNE experimental data for neutrino scattering. Numerical predictions for flux-averaged antineutrino scattering cross sections are also presented.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model provides an upper limit and the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) a lower limit for semi-inclusive neutral-current elastic (NCE) cross sections, with the RGF results in reasonable agreement with MiniBooNE data without increasing the axial mass beyond the standard value of 1.03 GeV/c², whereas the relativistic mean field (RMF) model generally underpredicts the data.

## Method

The authors evaluate semi-inclusive neutral-current quasielastic differential neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on ¹²C within the relativistic impulse approximation, comparing the relativistic mean field (RMF) model and the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model. The RGF model uses a complex optical potential whose imaginary part redistributes flux among all final-state channels, while the RMF uses the same strong energy-independent real potential for both bound and scattering states. Bound nucleon states are taken as self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions from a relativistic mean field approach with σ, ω, and ρ mesons, and the standard axial mass M_A = 1.03 GeV is used throughout.

## Key numbers

- RMF cross sections are generally 30% lower than RPWIA ones at small outgoing nucleon kinetic energy T_N
- For neutrino scattering, the proton/neutron ratio uncertainty between different models is at most ~4-5% at intermediate T_N, rising to ~15% at ε_ν = 500 MeV and ~8% at ε_ν = 1 and 2 GeV
- For antineutrino scattering, the uncertainty between models at intermediate nucleon energies is ~12-14%
- The MiniBooNE flux-averaged NCE neutrino cross section data are compared with RMF and RGF results as a function of Q²_QE = 2m_N T
- The strange axial coupling constant Δs = 0.08 ± 0.26 was extracted by MiniBooNE using the relativistic Fermi gas with M_A = 1.35 GeV/c²
- The RMF analysis yielded Δs = 0.04 ± 0.28
- COMPASS reported Δs = -0.08 ± 0.01 (stat.) ± 0.02 (syst.)
- For neutrinos, the proton/neutron ratio changes by a factor of 2 when going from Δs = +0.15 to Δs = -0.15

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of RMF and RGF models from inclusive quasielastic electron scattering (Meucci 2009) and charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering (Meucci 2011) to neutral-current elastic scattering. The study addresses the long-standing discrepancy where the relativistic Fermi gas model required an enhanced axial mass M_A = 1.39 GeV/c² to fit MiniBooNE data, while multinucleon excitation models (Martini 2009, Nieves 2011) could explain the data without increasing M_A. The results enable improved interpretation of upcoming neutrino oscillation experiments by clarifying the role of final-state interactions and nuclear model uncertainties in NCE cross sections, particularly for extracting the strange-quark content of the nucleon.
