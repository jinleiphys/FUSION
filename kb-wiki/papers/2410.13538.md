# Magnetic moments of $\frac{1}{2}^-$ baryon resonances in hot and dense strange hadronic matter

> This work primarily focusses on determining the magnetic moments of $\frac{1}{2}^-$ baryon resonances in the presence of hot and dense hadronic matter. In the chiral $SU(3)$ quark mean field model approach, we have essentially accounted for the effects on in-medium scalar meson fields to investigate the impact of high densities on the in-medium baryon masses and their constituent quarks. In light of chiral constituent quark model $χ$CQM, we have calculated the magnetic moments of $\frac{1}{2}^-$ baryon resonances and scrutinized the effects due to its internal constituents: the valence quarks, sea quarks and the orbital moment of sea quarks. Furthermore, we have investigated the effective baryonic magnetic moments for the finite magnitudes of isospin asymmetry and strangeness fraction.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the magnetic moments of low-lying \( \frac{1}{2}^- \) baryon resonances in hot and dense hadronic matter, extending beyond previous free-space calculations by incorporating medium modifications through the chiral \( SU(3) \) quark mean field model, but the results depend on the specific choice of parameters for the confining potential and the spin-spin interaction term fitted to vacuum masses.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled equations of motion for scalar (\(\sigma, \zeta, \delta\)) and vector (\(\omega, \rho, \phi\)) meson fields in the chiral \( SU(3) \) quark mean field model at finite temperature and baryon density, considering only positive parity octet baryons in the medium. Using the resulting density- and temperature-dependent field values, they compute the in-medium constituent quark masses and effective baryon masses for the \( \frac{1}{2}^- \) resonances via the Dirac equation with a confining potential and spurious center-of-mass corrections. The magnetic moments are then obtained within the chiral constituent quark model (\(\chi\)CQM) by summing contributions from valence quarks, sea quarks, and the orbital angular momentum of sea quarks, using the Cheng-Li mechanism with fixed transition probability parameters (\(a = 0.12\), \(\alpha = \beta = 0.45\), \(\zeta' = -0.15\)).

## Key numbers

- The vacuum light constituent quark mass is fitted to 256 MeV at nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The confining potential coupling constant is \(k_c = 98\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-2}\).
- The pion and kaon leptonic decay constants used are \(f_\pi = 92.8\) MeV and \(f_K = 115\) MeV.
- The dilaton field in vacuum is \(\chi_0 = 254.6\) MeV and the vector meson self-coupling is \(g_4 = 37.4\).
- The strange quark current mass offset is \(\Delta m = 77\) MeV.
- The binding energy at saturation density is \(-16.0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the free-space magnetic moment calculations of \( \frac{1}{2}^- \) baryon resonances by Torres et al. (2019) to finite temperature and density, using the chiral \( SU(3) \) quark mean field model previously applied to octet baryons by Kumar et al. (2017) and decuplet baryons by Dutt et al. (2020). The inclusion of isospin asymmetry and strangeness fraction enables comparison with medium modifications expected in heavy-ion collision experiments such as PANDA at FAIR and HADES at GSI, and the results can be used to guide future experimental searches for in-medium electromagnetic properties of baryon resonances.
