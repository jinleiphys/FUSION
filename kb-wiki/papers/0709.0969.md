# Chiral vibrations in the A=135 region

> Chiral vibrations are studied with the RPA plus self-consistent tilted axis cranking formalism in the A=135 region. In this method chiral vibrations appear as a precursor to the static chiral regime. The properties of the RPA phonons are discussed and compared to experimental data. We discuss the limits the chiral region and the transition to the non harmonic regime.

# Chiral vibrations in the A=135 region

## Key claim

Chiral vibrations appear as a precursor to static chirality in the A=135 region, described within a Random Phase Approximation (RPA) plus self-consistent tilted axis cranking (TAC) formalism; the RPA phonon energy gives the splitting between chiral partner bands only in the harmonic regime before the transition to static chirality, where the RPA energy goes to zero and the approach breaks down.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent TAC calculations for the 2qp configuration πh₁₁/₂ ⊗ νh₁₁/₂ in N=75 isotones and Z=57 isotopes, using a Hamiltonian with a residual quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interaction and monopole pairing fields. They then applied the RPA in the quasi-boson approximation to compute the energy of the lowest chiral phonon and the inter-band transition rates, solving the RPA equation via the strength function method. The TAC mean field solutions were obtained for rotational frequencies ω = 0.1–0.5 MeV/ℏ, with the self-consistency conditions fixing the deformation parameters (ε, γ) and the tilt angles (ϑ, φ) of the angular momentum vector relative to the principal axes.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³⁰Cs at ω = 0.20 MeV/ℏ: ε = 0.19, γ = 33°, ϑ = 59°, φ = 0°, lowest RPA phonon energy Ω = 0.30 MeV/ℏ
- For ¹³⁴Pr at ω = 0.35 MeV/ℏ: ε = 0.21, γ = 22°, ϑ = 61°, φ = 0°, Ω = 0.11 MeV/ℏ; at ω = 0.40 MeV/ℏ the TAC solution becomes static chiral with φ = 32°
- For ¹³⁶Pm at ω = 0.10 MeV/ℏ: ε = 0.24, γ = 24°, ϑ = 50°, φ = 0°, Ω = 0.14 MeV/ℏ; at ω = 0.20 MeV/ℏ static chirality sets in with φ = 20°; at ω = 0.40 MeV/ℏ φ = 90° and Ω = 0.15 MeV/ℏ
- For ¹³⁸Eu at ω = 0.10 MeV/ℏ: ε = 0.29, γ = 21°, ϑ = 47°, φ = 90°, Ω = 0.07 MeV/ℏ
- For ¹³⁰La at ω = 0.10 MeV/ℏ: ε = 0.26, γ = 9°, ϑ = 61°, φ = 0°, Ω = 0.38 MeV/ℏ
- Quadrupole force strength κ₀ values range from 0.00319 MeV fm⁻⁴ (¹³⁴La) to 0.00359 MeV fm⁻⁴ (¹³⁰La)

## Context

This work extends the TAC mean-field description of chiral bands (Frauendorf 2001, Dimitrov and Frauendorf 2000, Olbratowski and Dobaczewski 2004) by incorporating RPA to describe the energy splitting between chiral partner bands, which the mean-field alone cannot provide. It contrasts with earlier two-particle-rotor models (Frauendorf and Meng 1997, Starosta and Chiara 2002) and IBA-based approaches (Bizzeti and Visscher 2004, Tonev and Angelov 2006) by offering a microscopic treatment starting from the TAC mean field. The results enable comparison with experimental data for candidate chiral bands in ¹³⁰Cs, ¹³²La, ¹³⁴Pr, ¹³⁶Pm, ¹³⁸Eu, and ¹⁴⁰Tb, and motivate future large-amplitude anharmonic calculations (Almehed, Doenau, Frauendorf 2011) to understand the nearly constant energy splitting observed in some nuclei.
