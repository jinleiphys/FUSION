# Neutron density distribution and neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb

> We present and discuss numerical predictions for the neutron density distribution of $^{208}$Pb using various non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field models for the nuclear structure. Our results are compared with the very recent pion photoproduction data from Mainz. The parity-violating asymmetry parameter for elastic electron scattering at the kinematics of the PREX experiment at JLab and the neutron skin thickness are compared with the available data. We consider also the dependence between the neutron skin and the parameters of the expansion of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper shows that no single non-relativistic or relativistic mean-field model can simultaneously reproduce the experimental half-height radius and diffuseness of the neutron density distribution of 208Pb measured by the Mainz pion photoproduction experiment, and that the PREX parity-violating asymmetry data have too large experimental uncertainty to discriminate among the models.

## Method

The authors computed neutron density distributions for 208Pb using 18 different non-relativistic (Skyrme and Gogny) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, including nonlinear meson couplings, point-coupling interactions, and density-dependent vertex functions. They parameterized the resulting neutron distributions as symmetrized two-parameter Fermi (2pF) functions with half-height radius R and diffuseness a, and compared these with experimental data from the Mainz (γ,π0) measurement and the PREX parity-violating electron scattering experiment at JLab. They also examined the correlations between the neutron skin thickness and the slope L and curvature Ksym of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- Experimental neutron half-height radius from Mainz: R = 6.70 ± 0.03 fm (stat) with systematic errors +0.02/−0.03 fm
- Experimental neutron diffuseness from Mainz: a = 0.55 ± 0.01 fm (stat) with systematic errors +0.02/−0.03 fm
- PREX parity-violating asymmetry: Apv = 0.656 ± 0.060 (stat) ± 0.014 (syst) ppm
- PREX neutron skin thickness: Rskin = 0.33 +0.16/−0.18 fm
- PREX neutron rms radius: Rn = 5.78 +0.16/−0.18 fm
- Weighted average half-height radius across all models: Rmean = 6.822 ± 0.001 fm
- Weighted average diffuseness across all models: amean = 0.558 ± 0.001 fm
- Mainz (γ,π0) experiment suggests L in range 5 ≤ L ≤ 55 MeV
- PREX central value yields L ≈ 150 MeV
- PREX-II aims for Rskin accuracy of ±0.06 fm, constraining L to ≈ ±40 MeV and Ksym to ≈ ±150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of charge densities along isotopic chains (Meucci et al., 2012) and isotonic chains, as well as their earlier comparison of the DDME2 interaction with PREX results. The paper compares multiple theoretical models against the recent Mainz pion photoproduction data (2013) and the PREX experiment (Abrahamyan et al., 2012), finding that no single model reproduces both the radius and diffuseness simultaneously. The results enable future experiments like PREX-II and CREX to constrain the slope and curvature of the symmetry energy, which has implications for neutron star properties as discussed by Lattimer (2014) and others.
