# Nuclear matter properties with nucleon-nucleon forces up to fifth order in the chiral expansion

> The properties of nuclear matter are studied using state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon forces up to fifth order in chiral effective field theory. The equations of state of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are calculated in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory. We discuss in detail the convergence pattern of the chiral expansion and the regulator dependence of the calculated equations of state and provide an estimation of the truncation uncertainty. For all employed values of the regulator, the fifth-order chiral two-nucleon potential is found to generate nuclear saturation properties similar to the available phenomenological high precision potentials. We also extract the symmetry energy of nuclear matter, which is shown to be quite robust with respect to the chiral order and the value of the regulator.

## Key claim

The fifth-order (N4LO) chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials, when used in Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory without three-nucleon forces, produce nuclear saturation properties similar to phenomenological high-precision potentials like AV18, but the saturation densities and binding energies remain far from empirical data due to the missing three-nucleon force contributions.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix using chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials from leading order (LO) through fifth order (N4LO) in chiral effective field theory, with partial waves up to J=6. The long-range parts of the potentials were regularized in coordinate space using a cutoff function with radius R ranging from 0.8 to 1.2 fm, while contact interactions used a non-local Gaussian regulator in momentum space with cutoff Λ = 2/R. The single-particle potential was treated with the continuous choice, and the coupled equations were solved self-consistently to obtain the energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter at N4LO ranges from 0.28 fm⁻³ (R=0.8 fm) to 0.40 fm⁻³ (R=1.2 fm)
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation at N4LO ranges from -17.14 MeV (R=0.8 fm) to -23.28 MeV (R=1.2 fm)
- Effective mass M*/M at saturation at N4LO ranges from 0.71 (R=1.2 fm) to 0.74 (R=0.8 fm)
- Symmetry energy at empirical saturation density (0.16 fm⁻³) from N4LO potentials: 27.9 to 30.5 MeV
- Slope parameter L at empirical saturation density from N4LO potentials: 49.4 to 55.0 MeV
- Estimated theoretical uncertainty at N4LO for energy per particle: ±0.3 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter and ±0.7 MeV for pure neutron matter at saturation density
- Estimated theoretical uncertainty at N2LO for energy per particle: ±1.5 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter and ±2 MeV for pure neutron matter at saturation density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear matter using chiral potentials, particularly the work of Sammarruca et al. (2015) who studied convergence up to N3LO using nonlocal potentials, and the Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of Gezerlis et al. (2013) for pure neutron matter. The authors use the novel uncertainty quantification approach developed by Epelbaum et al. (2015) and Binder et al. (2016) that does not rely on cutoff variation, finding that cutoff variation alone underestimates theoretical uncertainty at NLO and N3LO. The results enable future work incorporating consistently regularized three-nucleon forces at N3LO and N4LO, which are needed to bring saturation properties closer to empirical values (ρsat ∼ 0.16 fm⁻³ and E/A ∼ -16 MeV).
