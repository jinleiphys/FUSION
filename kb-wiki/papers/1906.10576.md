# Elastic hadron-nucleus scattering in neutrino-nucleus reactions and transverse kinematics measurements

> Rescattering following a neutrino-nucleus reaction changes the number, energy, and direction of detectable hadrons. In turn, this affects the selection and kinematic distributions of subsamples of neutrino events used for interaction or oscillation analysis. This technical note focuses on three forms of two-body rescattering. Elastic hadron+nucleus scattering primarily changes the direction of the hadron with very little energy transfer. Secondly, a hadron+nucleon quasi-elastic process leads to the knockout of a single struck nucleon, possibly with charge exchange between the two hadrons. Also, a pion can be absorbed leading to the ejection of two nucleons. There was an error in the code of the {\small GENIE} neutrino event generator that affects these processes. We present examples of the change with the fixed version of the scattering process, but also compare these specifically to turning off elastic scattering completely, which is similar to other neutrino event generator configurations or a potential Equick-fix to already generated samples. Three examples are taken from current topics of interest: transverse kinematics observables in quasielastic neutrino reactions, the pion angle with respect to the incoming and outgoing lepton for $Δ$ reactions with a charged pion in the final state, and the angle between two protons in reactions with no pions present. Elastic hadron+nucleus scattering in its unfixed form makes a large distortion in distributions of transverse kinematic imbalances scattering, but only mild distortion in other observables. The distortion of the other two processes is also mild for all distributions considered. The correct form of hadron+nucleus scattering process could play a role in describing the width and center of the sharp peak in the inferred Fermi-motion of the struck nucleon or be benchmarked using (e,e'p) data.

## Key claim

The paper identifies and corrects a flaw in the GENIE neutrino event generator's TwoBodyKinematics routine that caused elastic hadron+nucleus scattering to produce an unphysical pile-up of events near perfect coplanarity in transverse kinematic imbalance distributions, with the distortion being most severe for elastic scattering while the other two-body rescattering processes (single nucleon knockout and pion absorption) show only mild distortions across all distributions considered.

## Method

The authors modified the GENIE 2.12.10 hA model's TwoBodyKinematics routine, which handles elastic hadron+nucleus scattering, single-nucleon knockout with charge exchange, and pion absorption on two nucleons, correcting a mistake that improperly used lab frame information during the center-of-momentum scattering calculation. They generated monoenergetic 3 GeV neutrino interactions on carbon using the MINERvA tune (MnvGENIE-v1) with RPA screening, Valencia 2p2h, and pion production suppression, then compared five configurations: original flawed code, elastic-only fix, elastic set to zero scattering angle, full fix, and the hA2015 model which turns off elastic scattering entirely. The comparisons focused on three event selections: charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) events with transverse kinematic imbalance observables, delta resonance events with charged pions, and two-proton events with no pions.

## Key numbers

- 36% of protons from CCQE reactions on carbon experience no FSI at all, while for oxygen, argon, iron, and lead the no-FSI fractions are 32%, 18%, 17%, and 10% respectively
- The original elastic code produced 11.7% of events passing selection versus 9.5% with the fixed code, a 25% increase in elastic-scattered protons accepted into the sample
- After all selection cuts, the original simulation gave 38.4% of the total sample versus 36.2% for the fixed simulation, a 6% overall excess
- The anomalous energy distortion from the flawed code was less than 2 MeV, producing negligible effect on proton momentum selection
- The single-nucleon knockout component differences between fixed elastic only and full fix are less than 10% of those subcomponents and less than 2% of the total

## Context

This work extends the understanding of final state interactions in neutrino-nucleus reactions, building on foundational studies by K2K, MiniBooNE, and NOMAD (Gran 2006, Aguilar-Arevalo 2007, Lyubushkin 2008) that used different generators (NEUT, NUANCE, DPMJET) with varying FSI models. The paper directly addresses the transverse kinematic imbalance observables proposed by Lu (2015, 2019) and Furmanski (2016) and measured by MINERvA (Lu 2018) and T2K (Abe 2018, Dolan 2018), showing that the GENIE flaw created a prominent feature in coplanarity distributions that was not present in data. The corrected code enables proper benchmarking against (e,e'p) data and accurate modeling of Fermi-motion peak width and center for future precision measurements.
