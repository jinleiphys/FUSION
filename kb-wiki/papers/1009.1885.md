# Radiation of a circulating quark in strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory

> The energy density and angular distribution of power radiated by a quark undergoing circular motion in strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory is computed using gauge/gravity duality. The results are qualitatively similar to that of synchrotron radiation produced by an electron in circular motion in classical electrodynamics: At large velocities the quark emits radiation in a narrow beam along its velocity vector with a characteristic opening angle $α\sim 1/γ$ and radial thickness scaling like $\sim 1/γ^3$.

## Key claim

The angular distribution of synchrotron radiation from a quark in strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory is qualitatively similar to that in classical electrodynamics, with narrow beams at high velocities, despite the expectation from prior work (Hofman 2008, Hatta 2008) that strong coupling would cause isotropization of radiation.

## Method

The authors use gauge/gravity duality to map the problem of a quark in circular motion in strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory onto a classical string rotating in an AdS5 background. They solve the Nambu-Goto action to determine the string profile, then compute linearized gravitational perturbations sourced by the string stress tensor, and extract the boundary stress-energy tensor via the gauge/gravity dictionary to obtain the energy density and angular distribution of radiated power.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic opening angle of the radiation beam scales as alpha ~ 1/gamma, where gamma = 1/sqrt(1-v^2) is the Lorentz factor of the quark
- The radial thickness of the radiation pulses scales as ~ 1/gamma^3
- The total power radiated down the string is P = (sqrt(lambda)/(2 pi)) a^2, where a = v omega_0 gamma^2 is the proper acceleration
- The time-averaged angular distribution of power is dP/dOmega = (v^2 gamma^3 omega_0^2 sqrt(lambda))/(32 pi^2) * (5 gamma^2 - 1 - v^2 gamma^2 cos(2 theta)) / (gamma^2 cos^2 theta + sin^2 theta)^(5/2)
- The condition for vacuum-like synchrotron radiation at finite temperature T is omega_0^2 gamma^3 >> pi^2 T^2

## Context

This work extends the study of radiation from accelerated quarks in strongly coupled gauge theories using gauge/gravity duality, building on the string profile calculations of Mikhailov (2003) and Fadafan (2008). It contradicts the expectation from Hofman (2008) and Hatta (2008) that strong coupling would cause isotropization of radiation through parton branching, showing instead that synchrotron radiation remains collimated. The results enable future studies of jet quenching by adding finite temperature to watch the synchrotron beam interact with and thermalize in a strongly coupled plasma, potentially modeling the behavior of jets in the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions.
