# Hybrid approach to perfect and dissipative spin hydrodynamics

> A hybrid framework of spin hydrodynamics is proposed that combines the results of kinetic theory for particles with spin 1/2 with the Israel-Stewart method of introducing nonequilibrium dynamics. The framework of kinetic theory is used to define the perfect-fluid description that conserves baryon number, energy, linear momentum and spin part of angular momentum. This leads to the entropy conservation although, in the presence of spin degrees of freedom, the perfect-fluid formalism includes extra terms whose structure is usually attributed to dissipation. The genuine dissipative terms appear from the condition of positive entropy production in nonequilibrium processes. They are responsible for the transfer between the spin and orbital parts of angular momentum, with the total angular momentum being conserved.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid framework for spin hydrodynamics that uses kinetic theory for spin-1/2 particles to define the perfect-fluid description, then adds dissipative effects via the Israel-Stewart method rather than through nonlocal collisions. The perfect-fluid formalism includes terms usually attributed to dissipation, but genuine dissipation appears only from positive entropy production in nonequilibrium processes and enables transfer between spin and orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The authors combine the kinetic-theory description of spin-1/2 particles (using classical spin treatment with the Mathisson-Papapetrou formalism) with the Israel-Stewart phenomenological approach to nonequilibrium dynamics. They derive generalized thermodynamic relations from the Fermi-Dirac distribution functions that include spin polarization, then expand these in the spin polarization tensor to second order. The dissipative terms are introduced through the condition of positive entropy production, remaining at the level of relativistic Navier-Stokes theory (linear in gradients).

## Key numbers

- The spin Casimir operator for spin-1/2 particles is \(\spin^2 = \frac{3}{4}\) (dimensionless, from the relation \(\spin^2 = \frac{1}{2}(1+\frac{1}{2})\))
- The spin polarization tensor \(\omega^{\alpha\beta} = \Omega^{\alpha\beta}/T\) is dimensionless in natural units (\(\hbar = c = k_B = 1\))
- The spin integration measure includes the factor \(\frac{m}{\pi \spin}\) with \(\spin = \sqrt{3/4}\)
- The expansion of distribution functions uses \(y_s = -\frac{1}{2}\omega(x):s\) as the small parameter
- The relation \(\omega:\omega \equiv \omega^{\alpha\beta}\omega_{\alpha\beta} = 2(k^2 - \omega^2)\) holds for the parametrization \(\omega_{\alpha\beta} = k_\alpha u_\beta - k_\beta u_\alpha + t_{\alpha\beta}\)

## Context

This work extends the generalized thermodynamic relations for spin hydrodynamics recently derived by Florkowski (2024) for Boltzmann statistics to the case of Fermi-Dirac statistics. It addresses inconsistencies in prior phenomenological approaches (Hattori 2019, Fukushima 2020, Daher 2022) that assumed the spin tensor form \(S^{\mu,\alpha\beta}_{\rm eq} = u^\mu S^{\alpha\beta}\) without microscopic justification, and in kinetic-theory approaches (Weickgenannt 2019, Wagner 2022) that required complicated nonlocal collisions to enable spin-orbit conversion. The hybrid framework enables future development of second-order dissipative spin hydrodynamics using the Israel-Stewart method.
