# Hydrodynamic results on multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> Multiplicity fluctuations are one of the most crucial observables in the Beam Energy Scan program of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. It is understood that they can be utilized to probe the whereabouts of the critical point on the phase diagram of the QCD matter. However, a significant portion of these fluctuations is, apart from that related to the QCD phase transition, attributed to the other origins, which we refer to as "noncritical" ones. The present study is dedicated to the noncritical aspects of the multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. In particular, we focus on those of dynamical origin, such as the hydrodynamic expansion of the system and the event-by-event initial fluctuations, in addition to the usual thermal fluctuations, finite volume corrections, and resonance decay at the freeze-out surface. The obtained results are compared to those of the hadronic resonance gas model as well as to the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a significant portion of multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions originates from "noncritical" dynamical sources (hydrodynamic expansion, event-by-event initial fluctuations, thermal fluctuations, finite volume corrections, and resonance decay) rather than from the QCD critical point, and that these noncritical contributions must be understood before critical point signals can be isolated.

## Method

The authors used the SPheRIO hydrodynamic code based on the Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) algorithm to simulate Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at various Beam Energy Scan energies. Each fluid element (SPH particle) on the freeze-out surface was treated as a grand canonical ensemble, and the Cooper-Frye formalism was used to compute particle spectra and multiplicity fluctuations. Event-by-event initial conditions were generated using the NeXuS event generator, and results were compared to the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, UrQMD model calculations, and experimental data from STAR and NA49 collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Simulations used 977 events for 0-3.5% Pb+Pb collisions at 8.8 GeV, and 756, 455, and 533 events for 0-5% Au+Au collisions at 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV respectively
- For the dynamical fluctuation ν_dyn,K/π at 8.8 GeV with quantum statistics: total value of 22.3 × 10^(-4), with thermal contribution 19.3 × 10^(-4) and event-by-event contribution 3.04 × 10^(-4)
- For ν_dyn,p/π at 8.8 GeV with quantum statistics: total value of 106 × 10^(-4), with thermal contribution -10.7 × 10^(-4) and event-by-event contribution 116 × 10^(-4)
- For ν_dyn,K/p at 8.8 GeV with quantum statistics: total value of 150 × 10^(-4), with thermal contribution 22.4 × 10^(-4) and event-by-event contribution 128 × 10^(-4)
- The difference between classical Boltzmann and quantum statistics for thermal fluctuations involving pions reached up to 30%
- For event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, the total energy discrepancy from the Cooper-Frye formalism was about 20-25%

## Context

This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas model calculations (Statistical Model, 2003; Statistical Model, 2004) by incorporating hydrodynamic expansion and event-by-event initial condition fluctuations into multiplicity fluctuation calculations. It contrasts with approaches focused on critical point signals such as chiral fluid dynamics (Hydro-Chiral, 2007) and Hydro+ (Hydro-Chiral-Sigma, 2015). The results enable better discrimination between critical and noncritical contributions to fluctuation observables in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, and suggest that event-by-event baryon density fluctuations from initial conditions (particularly from baryon stopping at low energies) may explain the "dip" structure in p/π and K/p fluctuations near √s ≈ 20 GeV observed by STAR.
