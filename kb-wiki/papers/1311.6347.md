# Anomalous Viscosity of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma is predicted to be lower than the collisional viscosity for weak coupling. The estimated ratio of the shear viscosity to entropy density is rather close to the ratio calculated by N = 4 super Yang-Mills theory for strong coupling, which indicates that the quark-gluon plasma might be strongly coupled. However, in presence of momentum anisotropy, the Weibel instability can arise and drive the turbulent transport. Shear viscosity can be lowered by enhanced collisionality due to turbulence, but the decorrelation time and its relation to underlying dynamics and color-magnetic fields have not been calculated self-consistently. In this paper, we use resonance broadening theory for strong turbulence to calculate the anomalous viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma for nonequilibrium. For saturated Weibel instability, we estimate the scalings of the decorrelation rate and viscosity and compare these with collisional transport. This calculation yields an explicit connection between the underlying momentum space anisotropy and the viscosity anomaly.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma can be lowered below the collisional viscosity by enhanced collisionality from Weibel instability-driven turbulence, but only under the assumption of an Abelian regime in 1+1 dimensions and for strong turbulence where the decorrelation rate dominates over the wave-particle resonance width.

## Method

The authors apply resonance broadening theory for strong turbulence to calculate nonlinear particle-wave interactions in the quark-gluon plasma, assuming an Abelian regime in 1+1 dimensions to determine the maximum intensity of plasma instabilities. They linearize the Vlasov equation for hard particles with an anisotropic momentum distribution, derive the nonlinear self-energy with a Lorentzian approximation for the broadened resonance, and compute the diffusion coefficient from color-magnetic field fluctuations. The decorrelation time is determined from the nonlinear dispersion relation at saturation of the Weibel instability, and the anomalous viscosity is obtained by taking the second moment of the diffusion equation for a spatially inhomogeneous distribution with a mean flow gradient.

## Key numbers

* The collisional viscosity for weak coupling scales as eta_C/s ~ 1/(g^4 ln(1/g)), where g is the coupling constant.
* The shear viscosity deduced from elliptic flow data is eta/s ~ (1 to 5)/(4 pi), close to the N=4 super Yang-Mills strong coupling prediction eta/s = 1/(4 pi).
* At soft momentum k ~ gT, the decorrelation time scales as t_c ~ 1/(gT), and the anomalous viscosity scales as eta_A ~ T^3/g.
* The anomalous viscosity at k ~ gT (eta_A ~ T^3/g) is much lower than the leading order collisional viscosity (eta_C ~ T^3/g^4).
* The saturated color-magnetic field scales as delta B ~ T^2 at k ~ gT.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Asakawa (2006) that instability effects can enhance collisionality and reduce viscosity in the quark-gluon plasma, providing a self-consistent calculation of the decorrelation time and its relation to color-magnetic fields. The approach adapts resonance broadening theory from electromagnetic plasma turbulence, following the work of Dupree and the method of Abe for calculating anomalous viscosity. The results indicate that the effective viscosity can be lowered to eta ~ T^3/g due to nonlinear particle-wave interaction, offering an alternative to the strong coupling explanation for the low viscosity observed in heavy ion collisions, and enabling further study of turbulent transport in non-Abelian plasmas.
