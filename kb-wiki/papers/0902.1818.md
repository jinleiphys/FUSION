# Theoretical study on pp --> p n pi+ reaction at medium energies

> The $pp\to p n π^+$ reaction is a channel with the largest total cross section for pp collision in COSY/CSR energy region. In this work, we investigate individual contributions from various $N^*$ and $Δ^{*}$ resonances with mass up to about 2 GeV for the $pp\to p n π^+$ reaction. We extend a resonance model, which can reproduce the observed total cross section quite well, to give theoretical predictions of various differential cross sections for the present reaction at $T_p=2.88$ GeV. It could serve as a reference for identifying new physics in the future experiments at HIRFL-CSR.

# Theoretical study on pp → p n π+ reaction at medium energies

## Key claim
The authors extend a resonance model, which reproduces the observed total cross section for beam energies from 0.8 GeV to 3.0 GeV, to predict differential cross sections for the pp → p n π+ reaction at T_p = 2.88 GeV. The model includes contributions from N* and Δ* resonances up to about 2 GeV/c², but the interference terms between different resonance-excitation processes are ignored, making the predictions a simple incoherent sum.

## Method
The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach with Feynman diagrams including t-channel meson exchange (π, η, σ, ρ) and s-channel excitation of N* and Δ* resonances. They compute total and differential cross sections via a Monte Carlo multi-particle phase space integration program, with coupling constants determined from experimentally observed partial decay widths. The model includes all well-established N* and Δ* resonances below 2 GeV/c² (status 3 or 4 stars) plus an effective N*(2065) resonance representing three predicted states.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for pp → p n π+ is reproduced for beam kinetic energies from 0.8 GeV to 3.0 GeV
- At T_p = 2.88 GeV, the overall system invariant mass is about 3 GeV
- The Δ(1232) resonance has g²/4π = 19.54 for Nπ coupling with cut-off 0.6 GeV
- The N*(1440) resonance has g²/4π = 0.51 for Nπ coupling (branching ratio 0.65) and g²/4π = 3.20 for Nσ coupling (branching ratio 0.075)
- The N*(2065) effective resonance has mass 2065 MeV/c², width 0.165 GeV, and g²/4π = 0.057 for Nπ coupling (scaled by factor 1.122)
- The N*(1680) contribution becomes significant for kinetic energy above 2.0 GeV
- HIRFL-CSR proton beam can reach T_p = 2.88 GeV with luminosity above 10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of pp → p n π+ for beam energies below 1.3 GeV (Ouyang, 2008) to higher energies, and follows the approach of Xie (2007) who found Δ*++(1620) dominance in pp → n K+ Σ+. The study is motivated by BES collaboration findings of a new N* peak around 2065 MeV/c² in J/ψ decays (BES, 2006) and the need to search for "missing" N* and Δ* states predicted by quark models (Capstick, 1992; Isgur, 1985). The predictions serve as a reference for upcoming experiments at HIRFL-CSR with its scheduled 4π hadron detector, and for comparison with COSY/Jülich results.
