# Form factors for the Nucleon-to-Roper electromagnetic transition at large-$Q^2$

> We report on a recent calculation of all Roper-related electromagnetic transtions form factors, covering the range of energies that next-to-come planned experiments are expected to map. Direct reliable calculations were performed, within a Poincaré covariant approach of the three-body bound-state problem, up to $Q^2/m^2_N$=6; approximated then by applying the Schlessinger point method and the results eventually extended up to $Q^2/m^2_N\simeq$12 via analytic continuation.

## Key claim

The authors report calculations of all nucleon-to-Roper electromagnetic transition form factors using a Poincaré-covariant quark-diquark approximation to the three-body bound-state problem, with direct reliable results up to \(Q^2/m_N^2 = 6\) and extrapolated via analytic continuation to \(Q^2/m_N^2 \simeq 12\). The hedge is that the dressed-quark core results omit meson-baryon finite-state interactions, which are expected to contribute materially at low \(Q^2\) and may affect the neutral channel more broadly.

## Method

The authors solve the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon and Roper as three-valence-quark bound states, using a quark-diquark truncation with isoscalar-scalar and isovector-pseudovector diquark correlations. They compute the electromagnetic vertex for the \(\gamma^\ast N \to R\) transition using six diagrams that probe the quark and diquark constituents, evaluating eight-dimensional integrals via Monte Carlo methods up to \(Q^2/m_N^2 = 6\). For the domain \(6 < Q^2/m_N^2 \le 12\), they apply the Schlessinger point method to construct analytic approximations from the direct results and continue them analytically.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon dressed-quark core mass: 1.18 GeV; Roper dressed-quark core mass: 1.73 GeV (from Faddeev equation solution)
- DCC bare-Roper mass from literature: 1.76 GeV (Suzuki 2009), compared to quark-core results of 1.73 GeV (this work), 1.82 GeV (Lu 2017), 1.72 GeV (Wilson 2011)
- Charged-Roper elastic charge radius: \(r_E M_N = 6.23\); proton elastic charge radius: \(r_E M_N = 3.65\) (where \(M_N = 1.18\) GeV is the nucleon core mass)
- Charged-Roper magnetic moment: \(\mu = 2.67\); proton magnetic moment: \(\mu = 2.50\)
- At \(x_{12} = Q^2/m_N^2 = 12\): \(F^\ast_{1,p} = 0.0121(14)\), \(-F^\ast_{1,n} = 0.0039(10)\), \(F^\ast_{2,p} = 0.0055(8)\), \(-F^\ast_{2,n} = 0.0034(7)\)
- Ratio \(F^\ast_{2,p}(0)/F^\ast_{2,n}(0) \approx -3/2\), consistent with available data from Tanabashi (2018)

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré-covariant continuum analysis of baryon structure previously applied to the nucleon, \(\Delta\)-baryon, and Roper resonance by Segovia (2014, 2015, 2016), Roberts (2015, 2018), Chen (2017), and Mezrag (2017). The calculations provide predictions for the \(\gamma^\ast N \to R\) transition form factors up to \(Q^2 \approx 12 m_N^2\), the domain that will be mapped by the CLAS12 detector at Jefferson Lab (JLab 12) as part of an international program to measure nucleon resonance electrocouplings (Mokeev 2018, Carman 2018, Cole 2018). The results enable tests of whether diquark correlations and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking manifest similarly across all baryons, and they set the stage for future analyses of negative-parity baryons and \(J=3/2\) states.
