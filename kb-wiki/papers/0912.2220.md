# Mechanisms producing fissionlike binary fragments in heavy collisions

> The mixing of the quasifission component to the fissionlike cross section causes ambiguity in the quantitative estimation of the complete fusion cross section from the observed angular and mass distributions of the binary products. We show that the partial cross section of quasifission component of binary fragments covers the whole range of the angular momentum values leading to capture. The calculated angular momentum distributions for the compound nucleus and dinuclear system going to quasifission may overlap: competition between complete fusion and quasifission takes place at all values of initial orbital angular momentum. Quasifission components formed at large angular momentum of the dinuclear system can show isotropic angular distribution and their mass distribution can be in mass symmetric region similar to the characteristics of fusion-fission components. As result the unintentional inclusion of the quasifission contribution into the fusion-fission fragment yields can lead to overestimation of the probability of the compound nucleus formation.

## Key claim

The authors show that quasifission can occur at all angular momenta leading to capture (0 < ℓ < ℓ_max), not only at high ℓ above the fusion limit as previously assumed; consequently, quasifission fragments can have isotropic angular distributions and mass-symmetric yields that are indistinguishable from fusion-fission products, leading to systematic overestimation of the compound-nucleus formation cross section.

## Method

The authors use the dinuclear system (DNS) model to calculate capture, quasifission, and fusion-fission cross sections for reactions such as ⁴⁸Ca + ¹⁵⁴Sm and ⁴⁸Ca + ¹⁴⁴Sm. They solve equations of motion for the capture stage, compute the lifetime of the rotating DNS via the Kramers rate for quasifission decay, and then determine the rotational angle of the DNS before breakup to predict the angular and mass distributions of the binary fragments.

## Key numbers

- For the ⁴⁸Ca + ¹⁵⁴Sm reaction, the theoretical quasifission cross section overestimates the experimental data (solid triangles) at all beam energies; the sum of experimental fusion-fission and quasifission cross sections matches the theoretical capture cross section for E_c.m. > 150 MeV.
- The characteristic lifetime of the DNS for quasifission is larger than 5 × 10⁻²² s (the time scale for deep-inelastic collisions).
- The DNS lifetime and rotational angle are calculated for the decay channel ⁵⁸Cr + ¹⁴⁴Ce in the ⁴⁸Ca + ¹⁵⁴Sm reaction at beam energies from E_c.m. = 157 to 191 MeV; the lifetime increases and the rotational angle grows as beam energy decreases.
- The mass distribution of quasifission products in the ⁴⁸Ca + ¹⁴⁴Sm reaction at E_c.m. = 150 MeV shows evolution over DNS lifetimes longer than 1.5–2 × 10⁻²¹ s.
- For the ⁴⁸Ca + ¹⁵⁴Sm reaction, mass distributions of quasifission yields are shown at E_c.m. = 140 MeV and E_c.m. = 160 MeV as a function of DNS lifetime.

## Context

This work extends the DNS model of Volkov (1970s) and earlier studies by Back (1980s) that assumed quasifission occurs only for ℓ > ℓ_fus. It directly addresses the experimental analyses of Knyazheva (2007) for ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca + ¹⁴⁴,¹⁵⁴Sm reactions, where quasifission components were excluded for masses A < 55. The results enable more accurate extraction of complete fusion cross sections in superheavy-element synthesis reactions, particularly for hot-fusion reactions with ⁴⁸Ca beams on actinide targets used by Oganessian (2004, 2006) and Hofmann (2007).
