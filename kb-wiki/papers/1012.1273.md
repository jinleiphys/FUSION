# Superfluid nucleon matter in and out of equilibrium and weak interactions

> The Larkin-Migdal approach to a cold superfluid Fermi liquid is generalized for a non-equilibrium system. The Schwinger-Keldysh diagram technique is applied. The developed formalism is applicable to the pairing in the states with arbitrary angular momenta. We consider the white body radiation problem by calculating probabilities of different direct reactions from a piece of a fermion superfluid. The closed diagram technique is formulated in terms of the full Green's functions for systems with the pairing correlation. The cutting rules are used to classify the diagrams representing one-nucleon, two-nucleon, etc. processes in the matter. The important role of multi-piece diagrams for the vector-current conservation is demonstrated. In the case of equilibrated systems, dealing with dressed Green's functions, we demonstrate correspondence between calculations in the Schwinger-Kadanoff-Baym-Keldysh formalism and the ordinary Matsubara technique. As an example we consider neutrino radiation from the neutron pair breaking and formation processes in case of a singlet pairing. Necessary correlation effects are included. The in-medium renormalization of normal and anomalous vertices is performed.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Larkin-Migdal approach to cold superfluid Fermi liquids for non-equilibrium systems using the Schwinger-Keldysh diagram technique, applicable to pairing in states with arbitrary angular momenta. The authors demonstrate that proper inclusion of vector current conservation through in-medium renormalization of weak vertices reduces the neutrino emissivity from pair breaking and formation (PBF) processes by a factor proportional to \(v_{\rm F}^2\) rather than the more severe \(v_{\rm F}^4\) suppression argued in prior work (LP).

## Method

The authors formulate the non-equilibrium Dyson equations on the closed real-time Schwinger-Keldysh contour for normal and superfluid nucleon matter, incorporating the in-medium pion exchange and nucleon-nucleon interactions within the Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid approach. They develop cutting rules to classify closed diagrams expanded in series with respect to the number of full \(G^{-+}\) Green's functions, treating one-nucleon, two-nucleon, and multi-piece processes on equal footing. As a specific application, they calculate the current-current correlator and the neutrino emissivity from neutron PBF processes in the \(^1S_0\) singlet pairing state, performing in-medium renormalization of normal and anomalous weak vertices to ensure vector current conservation.

## Key numbers

* Neutrino emissivity from PBF processes is suppressed by a factor \(\propto v_{\rm F}^2\) (Fermi velocity squared) rather than \(\propto v_{\rm F}^4\), after proper inclusion of vector current conservation.
* The critical temperature for neutrino opacity in neutron stars is \(T_{\rm opac} \sim (1 - 2)\) MeV, below which neutrinos can be radiated directly without rescattering.
* The critical density for the direct Urca process, \(n_{c}^{\rm DU}\), is given as approximately \(5 n_0\) for the Urbana-Argone equation of state (APR98), where \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the nuclear saturation density.
* For non-superfluid systems, one-nucleon processes have emissivity \(\epsilon_{\nu} \propto T^6\), while two-nucleon processes scale as \(\epsilon_{\nu} \propto T^8\).
* The suppression of PBF processes at low densities might serve as a possible explanation of the superburst ignition (GBSMK).

## Context

This work extends the Larkin-Migdal formalism (LM63) for zero-temperature superfluid Fermi liquids and the Leggett approach (Leg65a, Leg65) for finite-temperature superfluids to non-equilibrium systems, building on the Schwinger-Kadanoff-Baym-Keldysh technique (Schw61, KB62, Kel64). It addresses the problem of vector current conservation in neutrino emission from PBF processes, which was previously neglected in early works (FRS76, VS87, SV87, V01, KHY, YKL99, YLS99) and later argued to cause dramatic suppression (LP). The formalism enables consistent calculation of neutrino emissivity from superfluid neutron star matter, including medium-modified Urca (MMU) processes and PBF processes, and supports the "nuclear medium cooling scenario" (VS84, VS86, MSTV90, SVSWW97, BGV, GV) that links neutron star cooling behavior to star mass.
