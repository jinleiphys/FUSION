# Renormalization of equations governing nucleon dynamics and nonlocality in time of the NN interaction

> We discuss the problem of renormalization of dynamical equations which arises in an effective field theory description of nuclear forces. By using a toy model of the separable NN potential leading to logarithmic singularities in the Born series, we show that renormalization gives rise to nucleon dynamics which is governed by a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator. We show that this dynamical equation can open new possibilities for applying the EFT approach to the description of low-energy nucleon dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that renormalization of an effective field theory (EFT) description of nuclear forces, using a toy model of a separable NN potential with logarithmic singularities in the Born series, leads to nucleon dynamics governed by a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator, rather than the standard Schrodinger equation. The authors argue this nonlocality is a necessary consequence of renormalization when the form factors have "bad" ultraviolet behavior (alpha <= 1/2).

## Method

The authors analyze a separable NN potential model in the center-of-mass frame with form factors phi(p) ~ |p|^{-alpha} for large momentum. They solve the generalized dynamical equation (Eq. 5) for the T matrix, which is derived from unitarity and the Feynman superposition principle without assuming the Schrodinger equation. The asymptotic behavior of the T matrix as |z| -> infinity determines the form of the generalized interaction operator H_int(tau), and the authors classify cases based on the exponent alpha: alpha > 1/2 yields instantaneous (local) interactions, while alpha <= 1/2 yields nonlocal-in-time interactions. They then compare the T matrix obtained from this generalized dynamics with that obtained by dimensional regularization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the same singular potential.

## Key numbers

* For alpha = 1/2, the leading asymptotic coefficient b_1 = -(4 pi mu)^{-1}, where mu is the reduced mass.
* In the case alpha < 1/2, the leading asymptotic term is b_1 (-z)^{alpha - 1/2} with b_1 = -(1/2) cos(alpha pi) pi^{-2} (2 mu)^{alpha - 3/2}.
* The deuteron binding energy used to fix the parameter b_2 in the 3S1 channel is E_B = -2.2246 MeV.
* The fitted parameters for the 3S1(np) channel include alpha = 0.499, c_1 = 133.5 x 10^2, beta * rho = 433.8, d * rho = 766.2, and b_2 * rho^{1-2alpha} = 1.696 x 10^{-7}, where rho = 1 MeV^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the generalized quantum dynamics (GQD) framework developed by Gainutdinov (2000) to the specific problem of NN interactions in EFT, showing that renormalization forces a departure from Hamiltonian dynamics. The authors demonstrate that the T matrix obtained by dimensional regularization of the separable potential model (previously studied by the same group in 2001) satisfies the generalized dynamical equation but not the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. This enables a new parameterization of NN forces using nonlocal-in-time interactions, which the authors show can reproduce experimental phase shifts in the 3S1 and 1S0 channels, and opens the possibility of applying EFT approaches to low-energy nucleon dynamics without the ultraviolet divergences that plague the standard Schrodinger equation formulation.
