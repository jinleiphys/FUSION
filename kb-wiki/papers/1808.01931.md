# Quark matter under rotation in the NJL model with vector interaction

> We study the chiral phase transition of quark matter under rotation in two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. It is found that, in the rotating frame, the angular velocity plays the similar role as the baryon chemical potential and suppresses the chiral condensate, thus the chiral phase transition shows a critical end point not only in the temperature-chemical potential $T-μ$ plane, but also in the temperature-angular momentum $T-ω$ plane. One interesting observation is that in the $T-μ$ plane, the presence of the angular momentum only shifts down the critical temperature $T^E$ of the CEP and does not shift the critical chemical potential $μ^E$, and in the $T-ω$ plane, the increase of the chemical potential only shift down the critical temperature $T^E$ and does not change the critical angular momentum $ω^E$. The phase structure in the $T-μ$ plane is sensitive to the coupling strength in the vector channel, while the phase structure in $T-ω$ plane is not. It is also observed that the rotating angular velocity suppresses the kurtosis of the baryon number fluctuations, while it enhances the pressure density, energy density, the specific heat and the sound velocity.

# Quark matter under rotation in the NJL model with vector interaction

## Key claim
Angular velocity suppresses the chiral condensate similarly to baryon chemical potential, producing a critical end point (CEP) in both the temperature-chemical potential (T-μ) and temperature-angular velocity (T-ω) planes. However, the authors explicitly note that their calculation ignores boundary effects and is only valid when the angular velocity is much smaller than the inverse of the system's size, so the results are qualitative.

## Method
The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a vector interaction term, expanding the Lagrangian to leading order in angular velocity ω. They solve the mean-field gap equations for the constituent quark mass M and effective chemical potential μ̃, then locate the chiral phase transition boundaries and critical end points by finding the minima of the thermodynamic potential. The calculation is performed at a fixed transverse radial coordinate r = 0.1 GeV⁻¹, representing a typical radius in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- For GV = 0 and ω = 0, the CEP in the T-μ plane is located at Tᴱ = 0.032 GeV, μᴱ = 0.333 GeV
- For GV = 0 and ω = 0.1 GeV, the CEP in the T-μ plane shifts to Tᴱ = 0.002 GeV, μᴱ = 0.333 GeV (chemical potential unchanged)
- For GV = -0.5 GS and ω = 0, the CEP in the T-μ plane is at Tᴱ = 0.098 GeV, μᴱ = 0.241 GeV
- For GV = -0.5 GS and ω = 0.18 GeV, the CEP in the T-μ plane is at Tᴱ = 0.082 GeV, μᴱ = 0.241 GeV
- For GV = 0 and μ = 0, the CEP in the T-ω plane is at Tᴱ = 0.035 GeV, ωᴱ = 0.663 GeV
- For GV = 0 and μ = 0.05 GeV, the CEP in the T-ω plane is at Tᴱ = 0.024 GeV, ωᴱ = 0.663 GeV (angular velocity unchanged)
- The model parameters used: m = 5.5 MeV, GS = 5.04 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻², Λ = 651 MeV
- The critical angular velocity in the T-ω plane is approximately 0.7 GeV, which is much larger than the angular velocity created in heavy-ion collisions (approximately 0.1 GeV)

## Context
This work extends studies of rotating QCD matter by Jiang et al. (2016), who found a generic suppression effect on quark-antiquark pairing under rotation and identified a CEP in the temperature-rotation parameter space. The authors compare their 3D phase diagram in (T, μ, ω) to the 3D phase structure in (T, μ, eB) from Chao et al. (2013), noting that angular momentum plays a qualitatively different role from magnetic fields: rotation only suppresses chiral symmetry breaking, while magnetic fields exhibit both magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis. The results enable future studies of gluonic matter under fast rotation and provide estimates for how rotation affects baryon number fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collision experiments.
