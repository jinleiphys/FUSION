# A critical look at the vibrational model for ^{92}Pd and comparison with ^{48}Cr

> We compare the effects of turning off and turning on the T=0 interaction on the spectra of 92Pd and 48Cr--both 8 particle systems. We find qualitative differences for the 2 nuclei.Turning the T=0 interaction off destroys the linearity in 92Pd but not in 48Cr. We also consider B(E2)'s and static quadruple moments.

## Key claim

The near-equal spacing of yrast 2⁺, 4⁺, and 6⁺ states in ⁹²Pd, which suggests a vibrational band, may not be definitive evidence for maximum J-pairing (T=0 pairing), because turning off the T=0 interaction destroys the linearity in ⁹²Pd but not in ⁴⁸Cr, and the crucial J=8 state has not yet been measured experimentally.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations using the Antoine program with the JUN45 interaction for ⁹²Pd and the FPD6 interaction for ⁴⁸Cr, both treated as eight-particle (or eight-hole) systems with equal numbers of protons and neutrons. They compared results from the full interaction with results from a modified interaction (T0JUN45 or T0FPD6) where all T=0 two-body matrix elements were set to zero, examining energy spectra, static quadrupole moments, and B(E2) transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹²Pd with JUN45: E(2)=0.840 MeV, E(4)=1.721 MeV, E(6)=2.515 MeV, E(8)=3.217 MeV; with T0JUN45: E(2)=1.292 MeV, E(4)=2.406 MeV, E(6)=2.913 MeV, E(8)=3.007 MeV; experimental values: E(2)=0.874 MeV, E(4)=1.786 MeV, E(6)=2.535 MeV
- For ⁹²Pd with JUN45: B(E2:6→4)=364.1 e²fm⁴, B(E2:8→6)=315.1 e²fm⁴; with T0JUN45: B(E2:6→4)=5.018 e²fm⁴, B(E2:8→6)=2.474 e²fm⁴
- For ⁹²Pd: static quadrupole moment Q(2)=+3.514 e fm² with JUN45, Q(2)=-3.861 e fm² with T0JUN45
- For ⁴⁸Cr: ratio of B(E2:6→4) with T0FPD6 to full FPD6 is 0.399; corresponding ratio in ⁹²Pd is 0.013
- For ⁴⁸Cr: static quadrupole moment Q(2)=-35.4 e fm² in full fp-space calculation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Robinson et al., 2014) on the vibrational model for ⁹²Pd and compares with earlier f₇/₂ shell work by McCullen, Bayman and Zamick (1964) and Ginocchio and French. The paper responds to claims by Cederwall et al. (2011) and Qi et al. (2011) that the vibrational spectrum in ⁹²Pd indicates maximum J-pairing, while also addressing criticism by Fu, Zhao and Arima (2014) that the g₉/₂ configuration purity is below 33%. The analysis shows that measuring the 8⁺ state and its B(E2) to the 6⁺ state is needed to distinguish the role of T=0 pairing, and that the behavior differs markedly from the mid-shell nucleus ⁴⁸Cr where turning off T=0 interactions preserves the linear spectrum.
