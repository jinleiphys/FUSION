# Perturbative QCD Calculations of Elliptic Flow and Shear Viscosity in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> The elliptic flow $v_2$ and the ratio of the shear viscosity over the entropy density, $η/s$, of gluon matter are calculated from the perturbative QCD (pQCD) based parton cascade Boltzmann approach of multiparton scatterings. For Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$A GeV the gluon plasma generates large $v_2$ values measured at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Standard pQCD yields $η/s\approx 0.08-0.15$ as small as the lower bound found from the anti-de Sitter/conformal field theory conjecture.

# Perturbative QCD Calculations of Elliptic Flow and Shear Viscosity in Au+Au Collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

## Key claim
Standard perturbative QCD interactions alone, specifically gluon bremsstrahlung and its backreactions, can generate the large elliptic flow values observed at RHIC and yield a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio as low as 0.08-0.15, matching the AdS/CFT lower bound without invoking supersymmetric Yang-Mills theories or higher-dimensional physics.

## Method
The authors used the Boltzmann Approach of Multiparton Scatterings (BAMPS), a relativistic pQCD-based on-shell parton cascade, to simulate gluon matter in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200A GeV. The simulation included elastic gg to gg scatterings with a pQCD cross section and pQCD-inspired bremsstrahlung gg to ggg with an effective matrix element, incorporating Debye screening and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect via a step function. Initial gluon distributions were taken as minijets with transverse momenta above 1.4 GeV from semihard nucleon-nucleon collisions using Glauber geometry with a Woods-Saxon profile, and interactions were stopped when local energy density dropped below 1 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers
- For coupling constant alpha_s=0.6, eta/s = 0.076 (static gluon gas) and approximately 0.08 (dynamic BAMPS simulation)
- For coupling constant alpha_s=0.3, eta/s = 0.13 (static gluon gas) and approximately 0.15 (dynamic BAMPS simulation)
- The eta/s ratio obtained from simulations with various impact parameters (b=4, 6.3, and 8.6 fm) were almost the same for a given alpha_s
- Final transverse energy per rapidity dE_T/dy for 0%-6% centrality: 380 GeV (alpha_s=0.3) and 324 GeV (alpha_s=0.6), compared to PHOBOS value of 368 GeV for charged hadrons
- Results with alpha_s=0.6 agreed perfectly with experimental v2 data except for central centrality; results with alpha_s=0.3 were roughly 20% smaller than data
- v2 generation ended at 3 fm/c, where critical energy density was reached for all gluons at midrapidity

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous development of the BAMPS parton cascade (Xu and Greiner, 2005, 2007) and their static gluon gas viscosity calculations (Xu and Greiner, 2007). It directly addresses the failure of earlier hadronic cascade calculations (Bass and Dumitru, 2002) and partonic cascade calculations with only 2-to-2 processes (Molnar and Gyulassy, 2002) to reproduce the large v2 values at RHIC. The results demonstrate that pQCD interactions can explain the nearly perfect fluid behavior of the QGP without requiring the AdS/CFT conjecture, and enable future work incorporating quarks, hadronization, and hadronic cascades to refine the description.
