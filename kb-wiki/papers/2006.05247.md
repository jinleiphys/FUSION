# The spin-polarized ferromagnetic state of a cold Fermi gas

> The spin-polarized ferromagnetic state of a cold Fermi gas is investigated for interacting and non-interacting charge-neutral and $β$-equilibrated gases. The standard minimal couplings between the magnetic field and the fermions' charges and magnetic dipole moments define the fermions' interaction with the magnetic field. Assuming a variable coupling strength between the magnetic field and the fermion (baryon) dipole moments, it is shown that a ferromagnetized state can be achieved that corresponds to a lower energy spin-polarized state with a magnetic field entirely due to the gas's magnetic response. We find that, depending on the density, a very large increase in the baryon dipole moments is needed to achieve this ferromagnetized state. While the required increase seems unlikely, the induced magnetic field is of the order $\sim10^{17}$ gauss. Furthermore, while externally magnetized Fermi gases have an anisotropic pressure, the pressure of the ferromagnetized gas is completely isotropic and the thermodynamically preferred magnetized state.

## Key claim

Assuming a variable coupling strength between the magnetic field and the baryon dipole moments, a ferromagnetized state with lower energy and a magnetic field entirely due to the gas's magnetic response can be achieved, but this requires an increase in the baryon dipole moments by a factor of roughly 30 to 40, which the authors consider physically unlikely.

## Method

The authors investigate the conditions for a self-consistent ferromagnetic state (H=0, M=B) in cold Fermi gases by solving the self-consistency equation derived from minimizing the Gibbs free energy with respect to magnetization at fixed density and temperature. They consider both non-interacting and interacting (relativistic mean-field models NL3, FSU, FSU2) charge-neutral and β-equilibrated gases of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons, varying the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) coupling strength of baryons by a multiplicative factor x to find the phase boundary where M=B≠0.

## Key numbers

- The proton and neutron AMM-couplings at normal densities are g_p = -0.793 μ_N/2 and g_n = 1.913 μ_N/2 (in the chosen convention), where μ_N is the nuclear magneton.
- The induced magnetic field in the ferromagnetic state is of order ~10^17 gauss.
- For a non-interacting neutron gas, the phase transition requires g_n = 30 g_n^(0) (a factor of 30 increase).
- For interacting neutron gas with the FSU parameter set, the phase transition requires g = 45.75 g^(0).
- For interacting β-equilibrated matter with the FSU parameter set, the phase transition requires g = 33.5 g^(0).
- The mean multiple of g^(0) required for the phase transition is around 40, representing an increase from about 1% of the nucleon rest mass to about 25% of the nucleon rest mass.
- Larger proton fractions can decrease the required AMM-coupling by more than 20%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin-polarized states in dense neutron matter by Brownell and Negele (late 1960s), Silver (1970s), and Lee (1970s), which did not include the magnetic field's contribution to the equation of state, and by Broderick et al. (2000), which did not treat the magnetic field self-consistently. The authors build on the formalism of Ferrer et al. (2010), Chatterjee et al. (2015), and Strickland (2019) for magnetized Fermi gases, and their results contradict the recent claim by Schenk (2020) that such a phase transition is excluded by observed neutron star maximum masses, noting that Schenk did not include the magnetic field's influence. The finding that the ferromagnetic state has completely isotropic pressure, unlike externally magnetized systems, could have significant implications for neutron star equation of state modeling if such a state exists.
