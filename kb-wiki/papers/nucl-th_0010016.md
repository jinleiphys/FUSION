# Shell Model Description of $^{158}$Gd

> The pseudo-SU(3) model is used to describe the low-energy spectra as well as $E2$ and $M1$ transition strengths in $^{158}$Gd. The Hamiltonian includes spherical single-particle energies, the quadrupole-quadrupole and proton and neutron pairing interactions, plus four rotor-like terms. The parameters of the Hamiltonian were fixed by systematics with the rotor-like terms determined through a least-squares analysis. The basis states are built as linear combinations of SU(3) states which are the direct product of SU(3) proton and neutron states with pseudo-spin zero. The calculated results compare favorably with the available experimental data, which demonstrates the ability of the model to describe such nuclei.

## Key claim

The pseudo-SU(3) model can describe the low-energy spectra, E2 and M1 transition strengths in \(^{158}\)Gd, but only after the single-particle strengths are reduced by a factor of four from the so-called realistic values, and with the quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing strengths fixed by systematics rather than fit to data.

## Method

The Hamiltonian included spherical single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole and proton/neutron pairing interactions, plus four rotor-like terms (proportional to \(J^2\), \(K_J^2\), \(\tilde C_3\), and \(\tilde C_2\)). Basis states were built as linear combinations of SU(3) states from the direct product of proton and neutron SU(3) irreps with pseudo-spin zero, truncated to the 18 irreps with the largest values of \(C_2 = 1/4 Q \cdot Q + 3/4 L^2\). The pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole strengths were taken from systematics (\(G_\pi = 21/A\), \(G_\nu = 17/A\), \(\chi = 35 A^{-5/3}\)), while the rotor-like terms were determined through a least-squares fit to energy levels.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole-quadrupole strength \(\chi = 0.0077\) (dimensionless, from \(35 A^{-5/3}\) with \(A=158\)).
- Proton pairing strength \(G_\pi = 0.133\) (from \(21/A\)).
- Neutron pairing strength \(G_\nu = 0.108\) (from \(17/A\)).
- Rotor-like term coefficients: \(a = -0.003\), \(b = 0.23\), \(a_{sym} = 0.00154\), \(a_3 = 0.0000760\).
- Ground state band \(0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1\) B(E2) = 5.031 \(e^2b^2\) (experimental: 5.023 \(e^2b^2\)).
- Ground state band \(2^+_1 \rightarrow 4^+_1\) B(E2) = 2.590 \(e^2b^2\) (experimental: 2.639 \(e^2b^2\)).
- Ground state band \(6^+_1 \rightarrow 8^+_1\) B(E2) = 2.121 \(e^2b^2\) (experimental: 2.123 \(e^2b^2\)).
- Interband \(2_2 \rightarrow 0_1\) B(E2) = 0.2395 \(e^2b^2\) (experimental: 0.0177 \(e^2b^2\)).
- Only five SU(3) irreps contribute more than 2% to the lowest four bands; the dominant irrep in the ground state band is (28,8) with 78.1% for the \(0^+\) state.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) model applications of Beuschel (2000) from three low-lying bands to a more complete description of \(^{158}\)Gd, and parallels similar calculations for \(^{156}\)Gd and \(^{160}\)Gd reported by Popa (2000). The results demonstrate that the model can reproduce the four lowest energy bands, E2 transition probabilities, and the M1 sum rule, but the need to reduce single-particle strengths by a factor of four from the realistic values of Ring (1979) indicates a limitation. This enables future systematic studies of rare-earth nuclei to test whether the pseudo-SU(3) model can provide a consistent explanation of low-lying band structure and M1 properties across the region.
