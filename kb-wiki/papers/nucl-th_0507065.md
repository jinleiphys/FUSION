# Retardation of Particle Evaporation from Excited Nuclear Systems Due to Thermal Expansion

> Particle evaporation rates from excited nuclear systems at equilibrium matter density are studied within the Harmonic-Interaction Fermi Gas Model (HIFGM) combined with Weisskopf's detailed balance approach. It is found that thermal expansion of a hot nucleus, as described quantitatively by HIFGM, leads to a significant retardation of particle emission, greatly extending the validity of Weisskopf's approach. The decay of such highly excited nuclei is strongly influenced by surface instabilities.

## Key claim

Thermal expansion of a hot nucleus to equilibrium matter density, as described by the Harmonic-Interaction Fermi Gas Model (HIFGM), significantly retards particle evaporation, extending the validity of Weisskopf's detailed balance approach to much higher excitation energies than commonly thought.

## Method

The authors combined the HIFGM, which provides the equilibrium matter density and entropy as functions of total excitation energy, with Weisskopf's detailed balance formula for particle evaporation widths. They calculated nucleon evaporation time scales by integrating the decay width expression using the entropy difference between the mother and daughter nuclei, accounting for the density-dependent level density parameter and compressional energy. The calculations were performed for nuclei at both ground-state density and equilibrium (expanded) density to isolate the effect of thermal expansion.

## Key numbers

- For a nucleus excited to \(E^*_{tot}/A \geq 8\) MeV, thermal expansion to equilibrium density restores a degree of metastability against nucleon evaporation comparable to that of a ground-state density nucleus at \(E^*_{tot}/A \sim 4.5\) MeV.
- Nucleon evaporation times at \(T \sim 5\) MeV are estimated to be \(t_{evap} \approx 20\) fm/c for prompt decay scenarios referenced in the literature.
- The decay width \(\Gamma\) is related to the decay time scale \(\tau\) via \(\tau = \hbar / \Gamma\), with \(\hbar\) being Planck's constant divided by \(2\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the Weisskopf evaporation model (Weisskopf 1937) to higher excitation energies by incorporating thermal expansion effects, addressing concerns raised by earlier studies (Bortignon 1990, Danielewicz 1984) that estimated very short evaporation times at high temperatures. The HIFGM approach builds on prior work by Toke and collaborators on surface entropy and equilibrium density (Toke 2002, Toke 2003). The results enable a consistent interpretation of experimental observations (Lott, Djerroud, IWMF 2003) showing sequential decay of hot projectile-like fragments at Fermi energies, and suggest that surface instabilities and cluster emission modes may compete with nucleon emission at high excitation energies.
