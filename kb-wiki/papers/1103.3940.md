# Nuclear Alpha-Particle Condensates

> The $α$-particle condensate in nuclei is a novel state described by a product state of $α$'s, all with their c.o.m. in the lowest 0S orbit. We demonstrate that a typical $α$-particle condensate is the Hoyle state ($E_{x}=7.65$ MeV, $0^+_2$ state in $^{12}$C), which plays a crucial role for the synthesis of $^{12}$C in the universe. The influence of antisymmentrization in the Hoyle state on the bosonic character of the $α$ particle is discussed in detail. It is shown to be weak. The bosonic aspects in the Hoyle state, therefore, are predominant. It is conjectured that $α$-particle condensate states also exist in heavier $nα$ nuclei, like $^{16}$O, $^{20}$Ne, etc. For instance the $0^+_6$ state of $^{16}$O at $E_{x}=15.1$ MeV is identified from a theoretical analysis as being a strong candidate of a $4α$ condensate. The calculated small width (34 keV) of $0^+_6$, consistent with data, lends credit to the existence of heavier Hoyle-analogue states. In non-self-conjugated nuclei such as $^{11}$B and $^{13}$C, we discuss candidates for the product states of clusters, composed of $α$'s, triton's, and neutrons etc. The relationship of $α$-particle condensation in finite nuclei to quartetting in symmetric nuclear matter is investigated with the help of an in-medium modified four-nucleon equation. A nonlinear order parameter equation for quartet condensation is derived and solved for $α$ particle condensation in infinite nuclear matter. The strong qualitative difference with the pairing case is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Hoyle state (the $0^+_2$ state of $^{12}$C at $E_x = 7.65$ MeV) is a typical alpha-particle condensate, but the claim is hedged by the finding that antisymmetrization effects, though weak, reduce the occupation of the lowest $0S$ orbit to over 70% rather than 100%, meaning the condensate is not perfect but predominant in its bosonic character.

## Method

The authors employ the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Roepke (THSR) wave function, a product-state ansatz for alpha particles all occupying the lowest $0S$ orbit, and the Orthogonality Condition Model (OCM) to describe $n\alpha$ systems. They solve the Hill-Wheeler equation for the THSR wave function and the OCM equation with the Gaussian expansion method to obtain eigenstates, and they compute the single-alpha-particle density matrix to extract occupation probabilities for the alpha orbits.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state in $^{12}$C is the $0^+_2$ state at $E_x = 7.65$ MeV, located about 300 keV above the $3\alpha$ disintegration threshold.
- The $0^+_6$ state of $^{16}$O at $E_x = 15.1$ MeV is identified as a strong candidate for a $4\alpha$ condensate, with a calculated width of 34 keV consistent with data.
- The alpha condensate fraction in the Hoyle state is over 70% for the $0S$ orbit.
- The $0^+_6$ state of $^{16}$O has a large alpha condensate fraction of 60%.
- The ground state of $^{8}$Be has an average density only about one third of nuclear saturation density.
- The Hoyle state has about triple to quadruple the volume of the $^{12}$C ground state.
- The $1/2^{+}_{2}$ state in $^{11}$B at $E_x = 11.95$ MeV and the $1/2^{+}_3$ state in $^{13}$C at $E_x = 12.14$ MeV are candidates for Hoyle-analogue states, located just above their respective cluster disintegration thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the concept of alpha-particle condensation first proposed by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Roepke (2001) from the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C to heavier self-conjugate nuclei like $^{16}$O and to non-self-conjugated nuclei such as $^{11}$B and $^{13}$C. It also connects the finite-nucleus alpha condensate to quartetting in infinite symmetric nuclear matter via an in-medium modified four-nucleon equation, showing a strong qualitative difference from the pairing case. The results enable further experimental searches for Hoyle-analogue states and theoretical studies of alpha clustering near disintegration thresholds.
