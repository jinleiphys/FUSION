# Analytic Solutions of Transverse Magneto-hydrodynamics under Bjorken Expansion

> We review the recent developments of analytic solutions in transverse magneto-hydrodynamics under Bjorken expansion. It is found that the time dependence of magnetic fields can either increase or reduce the energy density depending on the decay exponent of magnetic fields. Moreover, perturbative solutions under weak magnetic fields with spatial inhomogeneity results in transverse flow, where the directions of flow also depend on the decay exponent of magnetic fields in time.

## Key claim

The time dependence of magnetic fields can either increase or reduce the energy density of the medium depending on the decay exponent of the magnetic field; additionally, when spatial inhomogeneity is introduced, the direction of the induced transverse flow also depends on this decay exponent.

## Method

The authors consider an inviscid conformal fluid coupled to an external magnetic field in Milne coordinates, assuming Bjorken expansion with boost invariance. For time-dependent magnetic fields, they solve the conservation equations analytically for arbitrary field profiles. For spatially inhomogeneous magnetic fields, they employ a perturbative expansion in the weak-field limit, decompose the magnetic field into Fourier series, and solve the resulting ordinary differential equations for each Fourier moment.

## Key numbers

- For a power-law decaying magnetic field \(B_y(\tau)=B_0\tau^{n/2}\) with \(n<0\), the energy density is \(\epsilon(\tau)=\epsilon_c/\tau^{4/3} - 3B_0^2(2+n)\tau^n/(8+6n)\) for \(n\neq -4/3\).
- For the special case \(n=-4/3\), the energy density becomes \(\epsilon(\tau)=\epsilon_c/\tau^{4/3} - B_0^2\log\tau/(3\tau^{4/3})\).
- For \(n=2\), the correction from magnetic fields vanishes due to the "frozen flux theorem".
- For the particular exponent \(n=-1\), the transverse velocity vanishes (\(u_x=0\)) and the energy density correction simplifies to \(\epsilon_1 = -3\tilde{B}_y^2(x)/(2\tau)\) for arbitrary \(\tilde{B}_y(x)\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic solutions of transverse magneto-hydrodynamics under Bjorken expansion first derived by Roy et al. (2015), which considered only time-dependent magnetic fields. The authors then incorporate the spatial inhomogeneity of magnetic fields as developed in Pu and Yang (2016), which induces transverse flow. These simplified analytic models provide physical intuition for numerical simulations of magneto-hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, such as those by Pang et al. (2016) and Inghirami et al. (2016), though the influence of magnetic fields on QGP collective behavior remains inconclusive.
