# On gauge invariant nucleon spin decomposition

> A non-uniqueness problem of gauge invariant separation of quark and gluon contributions to nucleon spin is considered. We show that there is a wide number of gauge invariant spin decompositions each of them reduces to the canonical one in a special gauge. A class of physical gauge equivalent nucleon spin decompositions is selected by requirements of consistence with helicity notion described within E(2) little group representation theory and with gluon helicity Δg measured in experiment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that there is no unique gauge-invariant decomposition of nucleon spin into quark and gluon contributions; instead, a wide class of gauge-equivalent decompositions exists, and the physically relevant subclass is selected by requiring consistency with the helicity notion from the E(2) little group and with the experimentally measured gluon helicity Δg.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical angular momentum decomposition in QCD and introduce a general split of the gauge potential into a pure-gauge part (background field) and a physical part (quantum field). They then construct two explicit gauge-invariant decomposition schemes: one based on gauge-invariant variables defined via a time-ordered exponent that yields a generalized covariant Coulomb gauge condition, and another using a generalized axial or light-cone gauge constraint that expresses the physical field as a non-local functional of the field strength. Both schemes are shown to satisfy the helicity gauge conditions (A₀^{phys}=0, A₃^{phys}=0) on mass-shell, ensuring consistency with the E(2) little-group description of helicity.

## Key numbers

- The gluon helicity Δg measured in experiment is related to the nucleon expectation value of the transverse part of the gluon spin operator via ⟨N|x^μ S_μ^{gluon}|N⟩ = -(sx) Δg, where s_μ is the nucleon spin four-vector.
- The physical gauge potential in the axial/light-cone scheme is given by A_μ^{phys}(x) = -∫₀^∞ dλ n^ν F_{νμ}(x+λ n), with n^μ specifying the gauge direction.
- The pure-gauge field A_μ^{pure} = A_μ - A_μ^{phys} satisfies the pure-gauge condition F_{μν}^{pure}=0.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate on gauge-invariant nucleon spin decompositions initiated by Chen et al. (2008-2009) and followed by Wakamatsu (2010), Cho et al. (2011), and Hatta (2011). It directly addresses the non-uniqueness problem highlighted by Leader (2011) and the Lorentz-frame dependence issue in the Chen et al. decomposition. The paper enables a consistent definition of gluon spin operators that are frame-independent (via helicity) and connects them to the experimentally measurable Δg, while noting that other decomposition schemes (e.g., with dynamic quark momentum) may be more relevant for strongly bound hadrons.
