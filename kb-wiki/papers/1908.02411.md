# Numerical exploration of three relativistic particles in a finite volume including two-particle resonances and bound states

> In this work, we use an extension of the quantization condition, given in Ref. [1], to numerically explore the finite-volume spectrum of three relativistic particles, in the case that two-particle subsets are either resonant or bound. The original form of the relativistic three-particle quantization condition was derived under a technical assumption on the two-particle K matrix that required the absence of two-particle bound states or narrow two-particle resonances. Here we describe how this restriction can be lifted in a simple way using the freedom in the definition of the K-matrix-like quantity that enters the quantization condition. With this in hand, we extend previous numerical studies of the quantization condition to explore the finite-volume signature for a variety of two- and three-particle interactions. We determine the spectrum for parameters such that the system contains both dimers (two-particle bound states) and one or more trimers (in which all three particles are bound), and also for cases where the two-particle subchannel is resonant. We also show how the quantization condition provides a tool for determining infinite-volume dimer-particle scattering amplitudes for energies below the dimer breakup. We illustrate this for a series of examples, including one that parallels physical deuteron-nucleon scattering. All calculations presented here are restricted to the case of three identical scalar particles.

## Key claim

The authors lift the restriction that the relativistic three-particle quantization condition requires the absence of two-particle bound states or narrow resonances, by exploiting freedom in the definition of the K-matrix-like quantity that enters the quantization condition, using a modified principal value prescription rather than introducing unphysical channels.

## Method

The authors modify the principal value prescription used in the original relativistic field theory (RFT) quantization condition of Hansen and Sharpe (2014), shifting the inverse of the modified two-particle K matrix by an arbitrary real function of the two-particle center-of-mass momentum squared. This removes singularities that previously prevented application to systems with two-particle bound states or resonances. They then numerically solve the resulting quantization condition for three identical scalar particles in a finite cubic volume, using both the isotropic approximation (ℓmax=0) and including s- and d-wave mixing (ℓmax=2), with the two-particle interactions parametrized by effective range expansions or Breit-Wigner forms.

## Key numbers

- The quantization condition is valid for energies below 5m, where m is the particle mass, i.e., below the five-particle production threshold.
- For the s-wave effective range expansion, the authors use q* cot δ_s = -1/a_0 + (1/2) r_0 q*^2 + O(q*^4), and in numerical examples set r_0 = 0.
- For the d-wave case, the modified K matrix uses the form 1/(a_2^5 q*^4) for the leading term in the d-wave effective range expansion.
- The cutoff function H(k) uses parameter α = -1, and the KSS form H_2(a,b) = exp[-α_KSS (a*^2 - q*^2)] is used for ultraviolet regulation.
- The Breit-Wigner resonance parametrization uses Γ(E*) = (g^2/6π) (m_R^2/E*^2) q*, where m_R is the resonance mass and g its coupling to the two-particle channel.

## Context

This work extends the numerical studies of the RFT quantization condition previously performed by Briceno, Hansen, Sharpe (2018) for the isotropic approximation and by Blanton, Romero-Lopez, Sharpe (2019) for d-wave interactions, which were restricted to cases without two-particle bound states or resonances. The new approach brings the RFT formalism to the same status as the non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) method of Hammer, Pang, and Platter (2017) and the finite-volume unitarity (FVU) approach of Mai and Doring (2017), which do not require restrictions on the two-particle K matrix. The formalism enables determination of infinite-volume dimer-particle scattering amplitudes from finite-volume spectra, including a toy model of deuteron-nucleon scattering that reproduces the Phillips line of nuclear effective field theory (Phillips, 1968; Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck, 1999).
