# $α$-decay half-lives and $α$-cluster preformation factors of nuclei around $N=Z$ line

> In this work, a microscopic effective nucleon-nucleon interaction based on the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock $G$ matrix starting from a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction is used to explore the $α$-decay half-lives of the nuclei near $N=Z$ line. Specifically, the $α$-nucleus potential is constructed by doubly folding the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with respect to the density distributions of both the $α$-cluster and daughter nucleus. Moreover, the $α$-cluster preformation factor is extracted by a cluster formation model. It is shown that the calculated half-lives can reproduce the experimental data well. Then, the $α$-decay half-lives that are experimentally unavailable for the nuclei around $N=Z$ are predicted, which are helpful for searching for the new candidates of $α$-decay in future experiments. In addition, by analyzing the proton-neutron correlation energy and two protons-two neutrons correlation energy of $Z=52$ and $Z=54$ isotopes, the $α$-cluster preformation factor evolution with $N$ is explained. Furthermore, it is found that the two protons-two neutrons interaction plays more important role in $α$-cluster preformation than the proton-neutron interaction. Meanwhile, proton-neutron interaction results in the odd-even effect of the $α$-cluster preformation factor.

## Key claim

The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, using a microscopic effective nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, can reproduce experimental alpha-decay half-lives for nuclei near the N=Z line with a standard deviation of 0.62, which is better than the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) value of 0.78, but the calculated half-life for 109Te shows a significant discrepancy (log10 HF = -0.48) and some predicted half-lives rely on the WS4 mass model where experimental binding energies are unavailable.

## Method

The alpha-nucleus potential was constructed by doubly folding the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction from the DBHF G matrix with the density distributions of both the alpha cluster and daughter nucleus, using a Gaussian form for the alpha density and a Fermi function for the daughter density. The alpha-cluster preformation factor was extracted using a cluster formation model (CFM) that calculates formation energy from experimental or WS4 mass table separation energies. The alpha-decay half-life was then computed from the decay width using the WKB approximation for barrier penetration, with the renormalization factor determined by the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition.

## Key numbers

- For 112Xe, the calculated half-life is 4.27 x 10^2 s, compared to the experimental value of 2.25 x 10^2 s
- The optimal range parameter t_R in the effective interaction was determined to be 0.9 fm by chi-squared minimization
- The standard deviation between calculated and experimental half-lives for the DBHF model is 0.62, compared to 0.78 for the GLDM
- For 104Te, the predicted half-life is 5.84 x 10^-8 s (experimental upper limit < 4 x 10^-9 s)
- For 108Xe, the calculated half-life is 5.94 x 10^-5 s (experimental value 7.2 x 10^-5 s)
- The well depth of the total interaction potential for 112Xe is about -115 MeV, with turning points at 6.97 fm and 44.93 fm

## Context

This work extends the DBHF approach previously applied to superheavy nuclei by Zhang (2010) to the medium-mass region near the N=Z line, where recent experiments such as Auranen (2018) observed the alpha-decay chain 108Xe to 104Te to 100Sn. The study enables predictions of alpha-decay half-lives for experimentally unavailable nuclei around N=Z, which can guide future searches for new alpha emitters, and explains the evolution of the alpha-cluster preformation factor with neutron number by analyzing proton-neutron and two-proton-two-neutron correlation energies.
