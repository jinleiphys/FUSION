# Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa unitarity deficit reduction via finite nuclear size

> We revisit the extraction of the $|V_{ud}|$ CKM matrix element from the superallowed transition decay rate of $^{26m}$Al$\rightarrow$$^{26}$Mg, focusing on finite nuclear size effects. The decay rate dependence on the $^{26m}$Al charge radius is found to be four times higher than previously believed, necessitating precise determination. However, for a short-lived isotope of an odd $Z$ element such as $^{26m}$Al, radius extraction relies on challenging many-body atomic calculations. We performed the needed calculations, finding an excellent agreement with previous ones, which used a different methodology. This sets a new standard for the reliability of isotope shift factor calculations in many-electron systems. The $\mathcal{F}t$ value obtained from our analysis is lower by $2.2\,σ$ than the corresponding value in the previous critical survey, resulting in an increase in $|V_{ud}|^2$ by $0.9\,σ$. Adopting $|V_{ud}|$ from this decay alone reduces the CKM unitarity deficit by one standard deviation, irrespective of the choice of $|V_{us}|$.

# Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa unitarity deficit reduction via finite nuclear size

## Key claim

The CKM unitarity deficit can be reduced by one standard deviation when using an updated $|V_{ud}|$ value extracted solely from the $^{26m}$Al $\rightarrow$ $^{26}$Mg superallowed decay, but only if the finite nuclear size dependence of the statistical rate function $f$ is properly accounted for, an effect four times stronger than previously assumed and not included in prior analyses.

## Method

The authors revisited the extraction of $|V_{ud}|$ from the $^{26m}$Al $\rightarrow$ $^{26}$Mg superallowed $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ beta decay by parameterizing the dependence of the corrected $\mathcal{F}t$ value on the nuclear charge radii of both the parent ($^{26m}$Al) and daughter ($^{26}$Mg) nuclei, including both the isospin symmetry-breaking correction $\delta_C$ and the finite nuclear size dependence of the statistical rate function $f$. They updated the reference radii of $^{26}$Mg and $^{27}$Al using muonic atom X-ray data with improved nuclear polarization corrections, and determined the differential radius $\delta\langle r^2\rangle_{26m,27}$ from optical isotope shift measurements combined with independent relativistic coupled-cluster calculations of the mass shift and field shift factors.

## Key numbers

- The dependence of $\mathcal{F}$ on $r(^{26m}\text{Al})$ is four times stronger than previously believed: $\mathcal{F} \approx 483.92(8)(14)(9) - 5.6[r(^{26m}\text{Al})/\text{fm} - 3.0400] + 3.3[r(^{26}\text{Mg})/\text{fm} - 3.0337]$
- Updated reference radii: $r(^{26}\text{Mg}) = 3.0307(25)$ fm and $r(^{27}\text{Al}) = 3.0614(29)$ fm
- Differential radius: $\delta\langle r^2\rangle_{26m,27} = 0.461(49)$ fm$^2$, yielding $r(^{26m}\text{Al}) = 3.136(8)$ fm
- Mass shift factor $K = -240.8(1.2)$ GHz u; field shift factor $F = 77.8(5)$ MHz fm$^2$
- Updated $\mathcal{F}t = 3070.01(1.23)_\text{stat}(0.20)_{\delta_r'}(1.44)_{\delta_\text{NS}}$ s, lower by $2.2\sigma$ than the value in the previous critical survey (Hardy and Towner, 2020)
- Resulting $|V_{ud}|^2 = 0.94861(62)$ from this decay alone, $0.9\sigma$ above the Particle Data Group value of $0.94803(62)$
- With this $|V_{ud}|$, CKM unitarity is satisfied within $1.1\sigma$ using leptonic kaon decay determinations of $|V_{us}|/|V_{ud}|$, and within $2.3\sigma$ using semileptonic kaon decay determinations of $|V_{us}|$

## Context

This work extends the data-driven approach of Seng et al. (2022, 2023) for extracting $|V_{ud}|$ from superallowed decays, which identified the previously overlooked finite nuclear size dependence of the statistical rate function $f$. It directly updates the analysis of Hardy and Towner (2020), whose critical survey did not account for this dependence, and corrects the partial update by the laser spectroscopy collaboration (2023) that only considered the impact of the new $^{26m}$Al radius on $\delta_C$. The improved atomic many-body calculations set a new standard for reliability of isotope shift factor calculations in many-electron systems, and the methodology establishes criteria that must be satisfied by all superallowed nuclear decays for reliable $|V_{ud}|$ extraction. Future improvements require higher-precision optical isotope shift measurements or muonic atom measurements on microgram targets of $^{26}$Al.
