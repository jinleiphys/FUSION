# Recoil Studies in the Reaction of 12-C Ions with the Enriched Isotope 118-Sn

> The recoil properties of the product nuclei from the interaction of 2.2 GeV/nucleon 12-C ions from Nuclotron of the Laboratory of High Energies (LHE), Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) at Dubna with a 118-Sn target have been studied using catcher foils. The experimental data were analyzed using the mathematical formalism of the standard two-step vector model. The results for 12-C ions are compared with those for deuterons and protons. Three different Los Alamos versions of the Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM) were used for comparison with our experimental data.

## Key claim

The recoil properties of product nuclei from 2.2 GeV/nucleon 12-C ion interactions with 118-Sn show that limiting fragmentation and factorization hold within experimental errors for projectile energies above 2 GeV/nucleon, but the experimental data disagree with all three versions of the LAQGSM model except for products in the mass region 24 < A < 70.

## Method

Thick-target thick-catcher foils of enriched 118-Sn (98.7% enrichment, 66.7 mg/cm² thickness each, 11 target piles) were irradiated with 2.2 GeV/nucleon 12-C ion beams at the JINR Nuclotron for 10 hours. Forward and backward catcher foils and target foils were assayed nondestructively with high-purity Ge detectors for one year, and the kinematic characteristics of approximately twenty product nuclei were extracted using the standard two-step vector model formalism. The experimental results were compared with three versions of the Los Alamos Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM03.01, LAQGSM03.S1, and LAQGSM03.G1) and with previous data from proton and deuteron induced reactions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 2.2 GeV/nucleon for 12-C ions (total energy 26.4 GeV)
- Target enrichment: 98.7% for 118-Sn; target foil thickness: 66.7 mg/cm²
- Beam monitoring reaction: 27-Al(12-C,X)24-Na with cross section 14.2 ± 0.2 mb
- Forward-to-backward ratios (F/B) range from approximately 1.02 (for 67-Gd) to 4.47 (for 109-In)
- Mean ranges 2W(F+B) vary from 0.039 ± 0.008 mg/cm² (for 110-Sn) to 4.82 ± 1.02 mg/cm² (for 24-Na)
- Kinetic energies T_kin range from 0.029 ± 0.006 MeV (for 110-Sn) to 21.51 ± 4.56 MeV (for 52-Mn)
- Weighted average of F/B ratios for 12-C ions versus 3.65 GeV protons: 0.77 ± 0.16
- Weighted average of F/B ratios for 12-C ions versus 7.3 GeV deuterons: 1.03 ± 0.20
- Weighted average of v_II ratios for 12-C ions versus protons: 0.84 ± 0.25
- Weighted average of v_II ratios for 12-C ions versus deuterons: 1.11 ± 0.49

## Context

This work extends previous recoil studies by the same group (Balabekyan et al., earlier publications) on 118-Sn targets using 3.65 GeV protons and 7.3 GeV deuterons, now using 26.4 GeV 12-C ions to test limiting fragmentation and factorization hypotheses originally associated with peripheral hadron-hadron interactions (Boguta, year not specified). The results enable comparison of heavy-ion induced reactions with hadron-induced reactions at comparable energy per nucleon, and the disagreement with LAQGSM models suggests that the two-step vector model assumptions used in data extraction may need reconciliation with the intranuclear cascade and multifragmentation approaches used in the calculations. The data also conform well with earlier 18.5 GeV 12-C ion results across targets from Cu to U (Cole et al., year not specified).
