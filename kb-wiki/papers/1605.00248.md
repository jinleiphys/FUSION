# Electromagnetic response of 12C: a first-principles calculation

> The longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions of $^{12}$C are computed in a "first-principles" Green's function Monte Carlo calculation, based on realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions and associated one- and two-body currents. We find excellent agreement between theory and experiment and, in particular, no evidence for the quenching of measured versus calculated longitudinal response. This is further corroborated by a re-analysis of the Coulomb sum rule, in which the contributions from the low-lying $J^π\,$=$\, 2^+$, $0^+_2$ (Hoyle), and $4^+$ states in $^{12}$C are accounted for explicitly in evaluating the total inelastic strength.

## Key claim
The authors claim that first-principles Green's function Monte Carlo calculations, using realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions with free-nucleon electromagnetic form factors, reproduce the measured longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response of 12C without any evidence for the quenching of the longitudinal response. This conclusion depends on explicitly removing the contributions of the low-lying 2+, 0+2 (Hoyle), and 4+ states from the total inelastic strength before comparing to quasi-elastic experimental data.

## Method
The authors used Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods with the AV18+IL7 two- and three-nucleon potentials and associated one- and two-body electromagnetic currents to compute the Euclidean (Laplace-transformed) response functions of 12C. They then applied maximum-entropy techniques to invert the Laplace transform and obtain the longitudinal and transverse response functions R_L(q,omega) and R_T(q,omega) for momentum transfers q = 300, 380, and 570 MeV/c. The contributions from the low-lying 2+, 0+2, and 4+ states were subtracted using experimental excitation energies and transition form factors before inversion.

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal transition form factors to the 2+ state: 0.128 at q=300 MeV/c, 0.0743 at q=380 MeV/c, 0.0043 at q=570 MeV/c (divided by proton electric form factor)
- Longitudinal transition form factors to the 0+2 (Hoyle) state: 0.0313 at q=300 MeV/c, 0.0052 at q=380 MeV/c, 0.0045 at q=570 MeV/c
- Longitudinal transition form factors to the 4+ state: 0.0010 at q=300 MeV/c, 0.0012 at q=380 MeV/c, 0.00059 at q=570 MeV/c
- Excitation energies of low-lying states: 2+ at 4.44 MeV, 0+2 (Hoyle) at 7.65 MeV, 4+ at 14.08 MeV
- The correction from low-lying states to the longitudinal response is significant at q=300 MeV/c but completely negligible at q=570 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the GFMC approach previously applied to nuclear spectra, radii, magnetic moments, and form factors (Carlson 2015, Pastore 2013, Pastore 2014, Wiringa 1998, Lovato 2013) to the full electromagnetic response functions of 12C. It contradicts models that invoke in-medium modifications of nucleon form factors to explain the apparent quenching of the longitudinal response, such as the quark-meson coupling approach (Cloet 2016). The results enable more reliable calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant for oscillation parameter extraction at DUNE, building on earlier sum-rule studies of weak response (Lovato 2014).
