# Hedin's equations and enumeration of Feynman's diagrams

> Hedin's equations are solved perturbatively in zero dimension to count Feynman graphs for self-energy, polarization, propagator, effective potential and vertex function in a many-body theory of fermions with two-body interaction. Counting numbers are also obtained in the GW approximation.

# Hedin's equations and enumeration of Feynman diagrams

## Key claim
The paper shows that by translating Hedin's equations to zero space-time dimensions, where they become algebraic, one can count the number of Feynman diagrams contributing to each perturbative order for the self-energy, polarization, propagator, effective potential, and vertex function in a many-body theory of fermions with two-body interaction. The counting is performed under the constraint that Hartree-type insertions (tadpoles) are already accounted for in the Hartree propagator, making the enumeration simpler than the usual functional integral approach.

## Method
The five Hedin's equations for the propagator G, effective potential W, self-energy Σ, polarization Π, and vertex Γ are reduced to algebraic equations in zero space-time dimensions by removing imaginary factors and replacing the fermionic loop factor (-2) with a parameter ℓ that counts fermionic loops. The vertex equation is rewritten using the Hartree propagator g instead of the exact propagator G, turning the functional derivative into an ordinary derivative. The equations are then solved perturbatively as series expansions in the variables v (interaction strength) and ℓ, with coefficients that count all Feynman graphs with weight one at each perturbative order and for each number of fermionic loops.

## Key numbers
- Self-energy expansion (full theory, ℓ=1): Σ/vg = 1 + 3x + 20x² + 189x³ + 2232x⁴ + 31130x⁵ + ... where x = g²v
- Self-energy expansion with loop counting: Σ/vg = 1 + (2+ℓ)x + (10+9ℓ+ℓ²)x² + (74+91ℓ+23ℓ²+ℓ³)x³ + (706+1063ℓ+416ℓ²+46ℓ³+ℓ⁴)x⁴ + ...
- Vertex expansion (ℓ=1): Γ = 1 + x + 9x² + 100x³ + 1323x⁴ + 20088x⁵ + ...
- Polarization expansion (ℓ=1): Π/g²ℓ = 1 + 3x + 20x² + 189x³ + 2232x⁴ + 31130x⁵ + ...
- GW approximation self-energy (ℓ=1): Σ_GW/vg = 1 + 2x + 7x² + 30x³ + 143x⁴ + 728x⁵ + ...
- GW approximation has a finite radius of convergence x_c = 4/27, implying the number of self-energy diagrams of order n grows as (27/4)ⁿ

## Context
This work extends the tradition of diagram counting in many-body perturbation theory, which has applications in conserving approximations for the self-energy as developed by Takada (1995) and Schindlmayr (2001), and in the r_s-expansion of correlation energy for the electron gas studied by Endo. The zero-dimensional translation method is simpler than the usual functional integral approach used by Cvitanovic and Argyres. The results enable practitioners to know in advance how many diagrams are required at each order of an approximation and how many are left out, which is useful for partial resummation schemes such as the random phase approximation and the ladder expansion for the vertex and T-matrix described by Mahan.
