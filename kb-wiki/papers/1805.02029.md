# Signatures of few-body resonances in finite volume

> We study systems of bosons and fermions in finite periodic boxes and show how the existence and properties of few-body resonances can be extracted from studying the volume dependence of the calculated energy spectra. Using a plane-wave-based discrete variable representation to conveniently implement periodic boundary conditions, we establish that avoided level crossings occur in the spectra of up to four particles and can be linked to the existence of multi-body resonances. To benchmark our method we use two-body calculations, where resonance properties can be determined with other methods, as well as a three-boson model interaction known to generate a three-boson resonance state. Finding good agreement for these cases, we then predict three-body and four-body resonances for models using a shifted Gaussian potential. Our results establish few-body finite-volume calculations as a new tool to study few-body resonances. In particular, the approach can be used to study few-neutron systems, where such states have been conjectured to exist.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that avoided level crossings in the finite-volume energy spectra of up to four particles can be linked to the existence of multibody resonances, establishing few-body finite-volume calculations as a new tool to study such resonances. The authors note that this signature is expected to be necessary but not sufficient for the existence of resonance states.

## Method

The authors use a plane-wave-based discrete variable representation (DVR) to implement periodic boundary conditions for systems of bosons and fermions in finite periodic boxes. They calculate energy spectra for two-, three-, and four-body systems as a function of box size L, identifying resonances through avoided level crossings between discrete scattering states. The method is benchmarked against two-body calculations using phase-shift analysis and pole searches on the second energy sheet, as well as a three-boson model interaction known to generate a three-boson resonance state.

## Key numbers

* Two-body resonance for shifted Gaussian potential with V0=6.0: resonance energy ER=2.983 and width Gamma=0.001 (extracted from phase shift); pole position ER-i*Gamma/2 = 2.9821(3) - i*0.00035(5) (from momentum-space S-matrix pole search)
* Two-body resonance for shifted Gaussian potential with V0=2.0: resonance energy ER=1.606 and width Gamma=0.097 (from phase shift); pole position ER-i*Gamma/2 = 1.606(1) - i*0.047(2) (from momentum-space S-matrix pole search)
* Potential parameters for shifted Gaussian: a=3, R0=1.5 (in natural units with hbar=c=1 and m=1)

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume resonance identification method of Wiese (1989) from two-body systems to few-body systems with three and four particles. It builds on the formal framework of Luscher (1986, 1991) for two-body finite-volume quantization and connects to ongoing theoretical work on three-body finite-volume quantization conditions by Polejaeva and Rusetsky (2012), Hansen and Sharpe (2015), Briceno and Davoudi (2013), Hammer et al. (2017), and Mai and Doring (2017). The approach enables future studies of few-neutron systems, where multibody resonances have been conjectured to exist, and can test and help extend formal derivations of few-body finite-volume quantization conditions.
