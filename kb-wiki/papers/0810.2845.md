# Revisiting the Vector and Axial-vector Vacuum Susceptibilities

> We re-investigate the vector and axial-vector vacuum susceptibilities by taking advantage of the vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities. We show analytically that, in the chiral limit, the vector vacuum susceptibility is zero and the axial-vector vacuum susceptibility equals three fourths of the square of the pion decay constant. Besides, our analysis reproduces the Weinberg sum rule.

# Revisiting the Vector and Axial-vector Vacuum Susceptibilities

## Key claim
In the chiral limit, the vector vacuum susceptibility is exactly zero and the axial-vector vacuum susceptibility equals three-fourths of the square of the pion decay constant, provided the Ward-Takahashi identities are satisfied and a translationally invariant regularization is adopted. The authors prove these results analytically without relying on model-dependent approximations such as the rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations used in prior work.

## Method
The authors start from the definition of vacuum susceptibilities in the QCD sum rule external field approach, expressed in terms of the linear response of the dressed quark propagator to an external current. They use the vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities in the chiral limit to relate the vacuum polarization tensors at zero total momentum to the quark propagator and its derivatives, then evaluate the resulting integrals using translationally invariant regularization. For the axial-vector case, they separate the vertex into regular and pion-pole contributions, using the pion decay constant to evaluate the pole term.

## Key numbers
- Vector vacuum susceptibility: \(\kappa_V = 0\) in the chiral limit (exact result from gauge invariance).
- Axial-vector vacuum susceptibility: \(\kappa_A \langle \bar{q}q \rangle = \frac{3}{4} f_\pi^2\) in the chiral limit, where \(f_\pi\) is the pion decay constant.
- Transverse axial-vector vacuum polarization at zero momentum: \(\Pi_T^A(P^2=0) = f_\pi^2\).
- Longitudinal axial-vector vacuum polarization at zero momentum: \(\Pi_L^A(P^2=0) = 0\).
- The difference \(\Pi_T^V(P^2=0) - \Pi_T^A(P^2=0) = -f_\pi^2\) reproduces the Weinberg sum rule.

## Context
This work extends the earlier model-dependent calculations of vacuum susceptibilities by Zong et al. (2005-2007), which used the rainbow-ladder approximation of the Dyson-Schwinger approach and obtained a vector susceptibility of order \(10^{-5}\) (numerically consistent with zero). The authors provide a model-independent analytic proof that the vector susceptibility vanishes exactly due to gauge invariance, and derive the axial-vector susceptibility in terms of the pion decay constant. This enables more reliable applications of the QCD sum rule external field method, particularly for calculations of hadron properties that depend on these susceptibilities, such as the pion-nucleon coupling studied by Henley (1996) and the tensor charge of the nucleon investigated by He (1996), Belyaev (1997), and others.
