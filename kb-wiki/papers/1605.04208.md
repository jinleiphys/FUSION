# A Statistical Approach to Describe Highly Excited Heavy and Superheavy Nuclei

> A statistical approach based on the Weisskopf evaporation theory has been developed to describe the de-excitation process of highly excited heavy and superheavy nuclei, in particular for the proton-rich nuclei. The excited nucleus is cooled by evaporating $γ$-ray, light particles (neutrons, protons, $α$ etc) in competition with the binary fission, in which the structure effects (shell correction, fission barrier, particle separation energy) contribute to the processes. The formation of residual nuclei is evaluated via sequential emission of possible particles above the separation energies. The available data of fusion-evaporation excitation functions in the $^{28}$Si+$^{198}$Pt reaction can be reproduced nicely well within the approach.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Weisskopf evaporation theory to include light charged particle evaporation (protons, alphas) alongside neutrons and fission, enabling the description of de-excitation for proton-rich heavy and superheavy nuclei. The approach reproduces experimental fusion-evaporation excitation functions for the \({}^{28}\)Si+\({}^{198}\)Pt reaction, but the authors note that the survival probability magnitude "strongly depends on the ingredients in the statistical model, such as level density, separation energy, shell correction, fission barrier etc."

## Method

The de-excitation of a highly excited compound nucleus is modeled via sequential evaporation of \(\gamma\)-rays, neutrons, protons, and alpha particles in competition with binary fission, using partial decay widths from the Weisskopf evaporation theory. The level density is calculated from the Fermi-gas model with shell corrections and a collective enhancement factor, and the fission width is computed following the method of Feng (2007, 2009). The survival probability for each evaporation channel is obtained by multiplying the partial-width ratios and a realization probability based on the Jackson formula, then summed over partial angular momenta with capture and compound-nucleus formation probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The giant dipole resonance width for heavy nuclei is set to \(\Gamma_G = 5\) MeV.
- The pairing correction energy \(\delta\) is \(12/\sqrt{A}\), \(0\), \(-12/\sqrt{A}\) for even-even, even-odd, and odd-odd nuclei, respectively.
- The level density parameter uses \(\alpha = 0.114\), \(\beta = 0.098\), \(b_s = 1.0\), and \(\epsilon = 0.4\).
- The inverse-cross-section penetration probability for charged particles uses \(\hbar\omega = 5\) MeV for protons and \(\hbar\omega = 8\) MeV for alphas.
- The fusion-evaporation excitation functions for the \({}^{28}\)Si + \({}^{198}\)Pt reaction are reproduced, with channels such as \(1\alpha xn\) (\(x=3-6\)) showing larger cross sections than pure neutron evaporation.

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach of Feng (2006, 2007, 2009) by explicitly including charged-particle evaporation channels, which are essential for proton-rich nuclei near the proton drip line. The method enables evaluation of production cross sections for superheavy nuclei and proton-rich isotopes in fusion-evaporation and multi-nucleon transfer reactions, with particular relevance to planned experiments at the HIRFL facility in Lanzhou. The approach contrasts with earlier models that considered only neutron evaporation, as in Feng (2006).
