# Hadron Structure from Lattice QCD

> Recent progress in lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure will be presented. Calculations of nucleon matrix elements and form factors have long been difficult to reconcile with experiment, but with advances in both methodology and computing resources, this situation is improving. Some calculations have produced agreement with experiment for key observables such as the axial charge and electromagnetic form factors, and the improved understanding of systematic errors will help to increase confidence in predictions of unmeasured quantities. The long-omitted disconnected contributions are now seeing considerable attention and some recent calculations of them will be discussed.

# Hadron Structure from Lattice QCD

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure are making progress toward agreement with experiment for benchmark observables such as the axial charge and electromagnetic form factors, but the axial charge remains problematic because lattice values tend to lie below the experimental value rather than being scattered on both sides, even when agreement within errors is obtained.

## Method
The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of nucleon matrix elements using two-point and three-point correlation functions on discretized Euclidean hypercubic spacetime lattices. Connected diagrams are computed exactly using the sequential propagator method, while disconnected diagrams require stochastic estimation of all-to-all quark propagators. Excited-state contamination is addressed through ratio methods, summation methods, and multi-state fits at varying source-sink separations.

## Key numbers
- Experimental axial charge: g_A = 1.2723(23) from the Particle Data Group (2014)
- LHPC data indicate a -5% shift in g_A caused by excited states when using the ratio method with source-sink separation slightly below 1 fm
- Finite-volume effects on g_A at m_pi L = 4 produce a shift of -1.1(5)% from a fit to multiple datasets
- Isovector average quark momentum fraction: <x>_{u-d} = 0.140(21) in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV, from a calculation using the summation method and extrapolating to the physical pion mass
- Proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy: r_E^p = 0.84087(39) fm
- Proton charge radius from CODATA (electron scattering and spectroscopy): r_E^p = 0.8775(51) fm
- Minimum momentum transfer in a (5.6 fm)^3 volume: Q^2 = 0.05 GeV^2
- Disconnected-to-connected ratio for isoscalar axial charge: R(g_A^{u+d}) = -0.12(2) from ETMC and LHPC, -0.19(2) from LANL
- Disconnected-to-connected ratio for isoscalar tensor charge: R(g_T^{u+d}) = -0.002(2) from ETMC, -0.005(10) from LHPC, -0.039(8) from LANL
- Disconnected-to-connected ratio for isoscalar scalar charge: R(g_S^{u+d}) = 0.101(15) from ETMC, 1.756(94) from LHPC, 0.328(25) from LANL

## Context
This review extends the line of lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure that have long struggled to reconcile with experiment, as documented in the comprehensive review by Aoki (2013). The paper discusses how recent calculations by the Mainz group, LHPC, ETMC, RQCD, QCDSF, PNDME, and others have begun to address systematic errors including excited-state contamination, finite-volume effects, and non-physical quark masses. The work enables future first-principles calculations of the proton charge radius with sufficient accuracy to discriminate between the two experimental values from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy and electron scattering, and provides constraints on beyond-the-Standard-Model physics from beta-decay experiments through calculations of the scalar and tensor charges.
