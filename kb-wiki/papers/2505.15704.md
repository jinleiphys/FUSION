# Hidden-Strangeness Tetraquarks in the Dynamical Diquark Model

> The dynamical diquark model describes multiquark exotic hadrons in terms of diquark components nucleated by heavy quarks, and successfully explains multiple features of hidden-charm and -bottom exotics. Here we apply the model to the marginally heavy case of hidden-strange states to probe whether mesons near 2 GeV with peculiar properties, such as $ϕ(2170)$, $f_2(2340)$, and X(2370), are possible tetraquark candidates. We calculate spin-multiplet average masses using potentials obtained through lattice simulations and quark models, and we also describe the detailed spectra of the expected multiplets as a diagnostic to discern the nature of future hadrons likely to be discovered in this mass region by experiments at facilities such as BESIII, JLab, and the EIC.

## Key claim

The dynamical diquark model, previously successful for hidden-charm and -bottom tetraquarks, is applied to hidden-strange states near 2 GeV to test whether the diquark picture remains viable when the heaviest quarks are only strange quarks (where \(m_s/\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = O(1)\) and the usual heavy-quark approximations break down), with the caveat that the numerical inputs come from other QCD-based approaches and the results represent the predicted spectrum only if the same dynamical mechanisms persist in the strange sector.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation for the relative motion of a diquark and antidiquark in the Born-Oppenheimer \(\Sigma_g^+\) potential, using a Cornell-type potential \(V(r)=V_0 - \alpha/r + \sigma r\) with parameters from lattice QCD (Morningstar 2019) and the Relativistic Quark Model (Ebert 2005). Diquark masses for \((sq)\) and \((ss)\) systems are taken from lattice QCD (Babich 2007) and the Relativistic Quark Model (Ebert 2005), and the spin-multiplet average masses \(M_0 = m_\delta + m_{\bar\delta} + E_{nL}\) are computed for \(S\)-, \(P\)-, and \(D\)-wave states, with fine-structure effects (spin-spin, spin-orbit, tensor) discussed parametrically but not yet included in the numerical results.

## Key numbers

- For \(s\bar s q\bar q\) tetraquarks: the \(\Sigma_g^+(1S)\) multiplet-average mass is 1.842 GeV using LQCD diquark mass (0.710 GeV) and 1.954 GeV using RQM diquark mass (1.039 GeV)
- For \(s\bar s s\bar s\) tetraquarks: the \(\Sigma_g^+(1S)\) multiplet-average mass is 2.219 GeV using LQCD diquark mass (0.950 GeV) and 2.183 GeV using RQM diquark mass (1.203 GeV)
- The \(\Sigma_g^+(1P)\) multiplet-average mass for \(s\bar s s\bar s\) is 2.656 GeV (LQCD) and 2.786 GeV (RQM)
- The \(\Sigma_g^+(1D)\) multiplet-average mass for \(s\bar s s\bar s\) is 3.004 GeV (LQCD) and 3.141 GeV (RQM)
- The \((sq)\) diquark spin-averaged mass from RQM is 1.039 GeV, obtained from \(m^{S=0}=0.948\) GeV and \(m^{S=1}=1.069\) GeV
- The \((sq)\) diquark spin-averaged mass from LQCD is 0.710 GeV, obtained from \(m^{S=0}=0.588\) GeV and a \(1^+-0^+\) splitting of 0.162 GeV
- The \((ss)\) diquark mass from LQCD is 0.950 GeV; from RQM it is 1.203 GeV
- The \(\phi(2170)\) resonance has PDG mass \(2146 \pm 6\) MeV and width \(106^{+24}_{-18}\) MeV
- The \(f_2(1950)\) has width \(464 \pm 24\) MeV; \(f_2(2340)\) has width 331 MeV

## Context

This work extends the dynamical diquark model, previously applied to hidden-charm and -bottom tetraquarks (Giron 2019, 2020) and all-heavy tetraquarks (Giron 2020, Mutuk 2022), to the marginal case of hidden-strange states where the heavy-quark expansion no longer holds. It provides a spectrum of \(s\bar s q\bar q\) and \(s\bar s s\bar s\) tetraquark candidates in the 2.0–2.5 GeV region that can be tested against observed states such as \(\phi(2170)\), \(f_2(2340)\), and \(X(2370)\) at BESIII, JLab, and the EIC, and it enables future inclusion of fine-structure effects to distinguish tetraquarks from conventional \(s\bar s\) mesons, hybrids, and glueballs.
