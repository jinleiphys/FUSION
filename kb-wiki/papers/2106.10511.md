# Is two-poles' $Λ(1405)$ one state or two?

> To understand the nature of two poles for the $Λ(1405)$ state, we revisit the interactions of $\bar{K}N$ and $πΣ$ with their coupled channels, where two-poles structure is found in the second Riemann sheet. We also dynamically generate two poles in the single channel interaction of $\bar{K}N$ and $πΣ$, respectively. Moreover, we make a further study of two poles' properties by evaluating the couplings, the compositeness, the wave functions, and the radii for the interactions of four coupled channels, two coupled channels and the single channel. Our results show that the nature of two poles is unique. The higher-mass pole is a pure $\bar{K} N$ molecule, and the lower-mass one is a compositeness of mainly $πΣ$ with tiny component $\bar{K} N$. From our results, one can conclude that the $Λ(1405)$ state would be overlapped with two different states of the same quantum number.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the two poles associated with the Λ(1405) resonance represent two distinct physical states with the same quantum numbers, rather than a single state with a complex pole structure; the higher-mass pole is identified as a pure \(\bar{K}N\) molecule, while the lower-mass pole is predominantly a \(\pi\Sigma\) composite with only a tiny \(\bar{K}N\) component.

## Method

The authors employed the chiral unitary approach (ChUA) using the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with interaction potentials derived from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian. They analyzed the \(\bar{K}N\), \(\pi\Sigma\), \(\eta\Lambda\), and \(K\Xi\) coupled channels in the isospin \(I=0\), strangeness \(S=-1\) sector, and also performed separate single-channel calculations for \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\pi\Sigma\). The properties of the two poles were characterized by evaluating their couplings, compositeness, wave functions, and radii across four-channel, two-channel, and single-channel frameworks.

## Key numbers

* Two poles were found in the second Riemann sheet of the coupled-channel \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) interactions, with the higher-mass pole identified as a pure \(\bar{K}N\) molecule and the lower-mass pole as mainly \(\pi\Sigma\) with a tiny \(\bar{K}N\) component.
* The lower-mass pole is a compositeness of mainly \(\pi\Sigma\) with a tiny component of \(\bar{K}N\), as determined from the compositeness analysis.
* The higher-mass pole is a pure \(\bar{K}N\) molecule, based on the evaluation of couplings, compositeness, wave functions, and radii.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) resonance, first predicted by Fink (1990) and later confirmed by Oller and Meißner (2001) and Jido (2003) using chiral unitary approaches. The study builds on the methodology of Ahmed and Xiao (2020) for analyzing pole structures via compositeness, wave functions, and radii, and applies it to the baryonic sector. The results support the argument by Meißner (2020) that the two-pole structure should be recognized as a fact of QCD and potentially included in the main listings of the Particle Data Group, while also addressing the ongoing debate between single-pole and two-pole interpretations raised by Yamada and Morimatsu (2021).
