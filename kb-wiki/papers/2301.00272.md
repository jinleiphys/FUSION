# Quark spectral functions from spectra of mesons and vice versa

> We extract the spectral function of quarks using the QCD functional formalism.The reliability of approximations is controlled by the masses and decays of pseudoscalar mesons. For light quarks, we select the pion; for charm quarks, we choose the ground and excited states of the $η_c$ meson. To this end, we solved the ladder-rainbow approximation of the spectral Dyson-Schwinger equations for quarks coupled to the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion and pseudoscalar charmonia. The obtained dynamical charm mass function varies from 1 to 1.5 GeV in the timelike domain, which is significant for narrow charmonium states. This yields a notable response in the bound state spectrum. We obtained purely continuous spectral functions and discussed their connection with confinement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the running quark mass effect, when correctly accounted for in the ladder-rainbow approximation of spectral Dyson-Schwinger equations coupled to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, can reproduce the charmonium spectrum without introducing a confining potential by hand; however, this result depends on tuning the kernel parameters separately for light and charm quarks and on using a simplified gluon spectral function with two delta-function terms.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled system of spectral Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar mesons in Minkowski space, using the ladder-rainbow approximation. The gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex were replaced by an effective kernel consisting of a spectral function with two delta-function peaks at 0.6 GeV and 2 GeV for light quarks, with the parameters adjusted separately for charm quarks (0.433 GeV and 1.442 GeV). The quark DSE was solved by scanning intervals for the absorptive parts of the propagator functions to minimize spectral deviation, while the BSE was solved by an iterative eigenvalue method in complex momentum space using the spectral representation of the quark propagator.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: 140 MeV; pion decay constant: 90 MeV; these were used to fix the interaction kernel parameters for light quarks.
* Renormalization point for light quarks: mu^2 = 0.5 GeV^2, with Re b(mu) = 300 MeV.
* Renormalization point for charm quarks: mu^2 = 0.13 GeV^2, with Re M_c(mu) = Re b_c(mu) = 0.94 GeV.
* Gluon spectral function parameters for light quarks: m_g = 0.6 GeV, Lambda = 2 GeV; for charm quarks: m_g^c = 0.433 GeV, Lambda^c = 1.442 GeV.
* Dynamical charm mass function varies from 1 to 1.5 GeV in the timelike domain.
* Charm mass function at the ground state mass scale: M(M_eta_c/4) approximately 1.1 GeV.
* Calculated pseudoscalar charmonium masses: ground state at 2980 MeV (experiment: 2980 MeV); first excited state at 3442 MeV (experiment: 3638 MeV); second excited state at 4150 MeV; third excited state at 4720 MeV.
* Predicted but not yet observed excited eta_c states: 5436 MeV, 6186 MeV, 7030 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the spectral functional formalism for quarks previously developed by the same author (Sauli, 2008) and others (s1, s2), applying it to heavy quarkonia for the first time. It contrasts with nonrelativistic approaches that introduce a confining potential by hand, such as the Schrodinger equation with Coulomb plus linear potential used by Kogut and Susskind (1974) and many subsequent studies, arguing that the running quark mass effect from quantum field theory can substitute for such potentials. The results enable future extension to bottomonia and other meson sectors, though the author notes that spin-one bound states present additional complications due to infrared singularities.
