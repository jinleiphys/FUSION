# Estrellas híbridas: una aproximación semi-analítica a T finita

> Starting from the semi-analytic construction of a equation of state (EoS) which takes into account nuclear and quark matter at finite temperature, we study the possibility that proto-neutron stars, be proto-hybrid stars whose cores are composed of quark matter. We obtain the mass-radius relationship and discuss the latest constraints on masses and radii of neutron stars, considering the pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348 + 0432.

## Key claim

The authors construct a semi-analytic equation of state (EoS) for hybrid stars at finite temperature using the Correlator Method (MCC) for quark matter, and find that proto-neutron stars could be proto-hybrid stars with quark cores, but only for specific parameter choices (G2 = 0.011 GeV4, V1 = 10 or 100 MeV) and temperatures (5 or 20 MeV), and without including neutrino contributions.

## Method

The authors built a hybrid EoS by combining a hadronic EoS from the Relativistic Mean Field approximation (Shen 2010, 2011) with a quark-gluon plasma EoS derived from the Correlator Method (MCC), using a double power-series expansion in terms of mq2/(u2T2+mq2)2 and (μq-V1/2)/T to obtain semi-analytic thermodynamic quantities at finite temperature. They applied the Maxwell construction for a first-order phase transition at constant pressure and baryon chemical potential, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations for isothermal hybrid stars at T = 5 and 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses exceeding 2 M⊙ are obtained for the selected MCC parameter values (G2 = 0.011 GeV4, V1 = 10 or 100 MeV)
- The quark-gluon condensate parameter is set to G2 = 0.011 GeV4 (within the estimated range of 0.012 ± 50% GeV4)
- The confining potential V1 is taken as either 10 or 100 MeV
- Temperatures considered: T = 5 MeV and T = 20 MeV
- The lower radius bound from Chen (2015) is applied: neutron stars of 1.4 M⊙ must have radii greater than R = 10.7 km to avoid violating causality
- The observed masses used for comparison: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙ and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature application of the Correlator Method (MCC) to hybrid stars (Plumari 2013, Logoteta 2014, Burgio 2015) by incorporating finite-temperature effects, and addresses the challenge of explaining massive neutron stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) that may contain quark matter. The results are compared with the hadronic models that include hyperons (Yamamoto 2014, Bednarek 2011) and with the MIT bag model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approaches (Bonanno 2011, Orsaria 2013). Future work requires incorporating neutrinos from weak interactions and considering non-isothermal hybrid stars with constant entropy per baryon (Steiner 2000).
