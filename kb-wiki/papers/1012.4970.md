# Microscopic Nuclear Structure and Reaction Calculations in the FMD Approach

> We present here a first application of the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) approach to low-energy nuclear reactions, namely the $^3$He($öÝ$,$ö°$)$^7$Be radiative capture reaction. We divide the Hilbert space into an external region where the system is described as $^3$He and $^4$He clusters interacting only via the Coulomb interaction and an internal region where the nuclear interaction will polarize the clusters. Polarized configurations are obtained by a variation after parity and angular momentum projection procedure with respect to the parameters of all single particle states. A constraint on the radius of the intrinsic many-body state is employed to obtain polarized clusters at desired distances. The boundary conditions for bound and scattering states are implemented using the Bloch operator. The FMD calculations reproduce the correct energy for the centroid of the $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ bound states in $^7$Be. The charge radius of the ground state is in good agreement with recent experimental results. The FMD calculations also describe well the experimental phase shift data in the $1/2^+$, $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ channels that are important for the capture reaction at low energies. Using the bound and scattering many-body wave functions we calculate the radiative capture cross section. The calculated $S$ factor agrees very well, both in absolute normalization and energy dependence, with the recent experimental data from the Weizmann, LUNA, Seattle and ERNA experiments.

## Key claim

The Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) approach, when applied to the $^3$He($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^7$Be radiative capture reaction, reproduces the centroid energy of the bound states and the experimental $S$ factor in both absolute normalization and energy dependence, but it underestimates the splitting between the $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ states, which affects the branching ratio but not the total cross section.

## Method

The authors divide the Hilbert space into an external region where $^3$He and $^4$He clusters interact only via the Coulomb interaction and an internal region where the nuclear interaction polarizes the clusters. Polarized configurations are obtained by variation after parity and angular momentum projection of all single-particle parameters, using a constraint on the radius of the intrinsic many-body state to generate configurations at desired cluster distances. Boundary conditions for bound and scattering states are implemented using the Bloch operator within the microscopic $R$-matrix approach of the Brussels group (Baye 1977, Descouvemont 2010).

## Key numbers

- The $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ bound states in $^7$Be are bound by 1.49 MeV and 1.31 MeV, respectively, with respect to the cluster threshold in the full Hilbert space (compared to only 240 keV and 10 keV with frozen configurations alone).
- The calculated charge radius of the $^7$Be ground state is 2.67 fm, in good agreement with the experimental value of 2.647(17) fm (Noertershaeuser 2009).
- The FMD calculations reproduce the experimental phase shift data in the $1/2^+$, $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ channels important for the capture reaction at low energies.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic cluster model calculations (Langanke 1986, Kajino 1986) that assumed $^3$He+$^4$He cluster wave functions, and goes beyond potential model studies (Kim 1981, Mohr 2009) by using a fully antisymmetrized many-body description with a realistic low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from the Argonne V18 interaction via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM). It enables consistent ab initio calculations of low-energy radiative capture reactions, which previously had only been attempted for asymptotic normalization coefficients using Variational Monte Carlo (Nollett 2001) or the No-Core Shell Model (Navratil 2007).
