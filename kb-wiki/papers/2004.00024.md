# Deciphering the nature of X(3872) in heavy ion collisions

> Exploring the nature of exotic multiquark candidates such as the $X(3872)$ plays a pivotal role in understanding quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Despite significant efforts, consensus on their internal structures is still lacking. As a prime example, it remains a pressing open question to decipher the $X(3872)$ state between two popular exotic configurations: a loose hadronic molecule or a compact tetraquark. We demonstrate a novel approach to help address this problem by studying the $X(3872)$ production in heavy ion collisions, where a hot fireball with ample light as well as charm (anti-)quarks is available for producing the exotics. Adopting a multiphase transport model (AMPT) for describing such collisions and implementing appropriate production mechanism of either molecule or tetraquark picture, we compute and compare a series of observables for $X(3872)$ in Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. We find the fireball volume plays a crucial role, leading to a 2-order-of-magnitude difference in the $X(3872)$ yield and a markedly different centrality dependence between hadronic molecules and compact tetraquarks, thus offering a unique opportunity for distinguishing the two scenarios. We also make the first prediction of $X(3872)$ elliptic flow coefficient to be tested by future experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the fireball volume in heavy ion collisions leads to a 2-order-of-magnitude difference in the X(3872) yield and a markedly different centrality dependence between the hadronic molecule and compact tetraquark scenarios, offering a unique opportunity to distinguish the two configurations. The authors hedge that their calculations only account for late-time production at freeze-out and neglect potential contributions from early-produced c-cbar pairs that survive through the plasma, which could at most double the tetraquark yield.

## Method

The authors used the default version of the AMPT (A Multiphase Transport Model) event generator to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, with enhanced initial charm quark spectra calibrated against ALICE D meson data. For the hadronic molecule scenario, X(3872) formation was implemented via coalescence of D and D* mesons with relative distance between 5 and 7 fm and pair mass between 2M_D and 2M_D*. For the tetraquark scenario, X(3872) was formed via two-step coalescence: first diquarks (cq) and antidiquarks (cbar qbar) were created by matching c/cbar with the nearest light quark, then these were coalesced with relative distance less than 1 fm and pair mass between 3780 and 4020 MeV. Thermal model ratios with T = 160 MeV were used to separate spin channels for both scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive X(3872) yield in minimum bias Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV: approximately 220,000 for the molecule scenario versus approximately 900 for the tetraquark scenario, a factor of 250 difference (2 orders of magnitude)
- Molecular X(3872) effective range used: 5-7 fm (with note that varying from 2-10 fm changes yield by factor of 3.2)
- Tetraquark X(3872) formation constraint: relative distance less than 1 fm
- Mass limits for tetraquark heavy quark spin partners: 3780 MeV (lower) and 4020 MeV (upper)
- Thermal model temperature parameter: T = 160 MeV
- D* to D composition: 30% D*, 70% D; spin triplet to singlet diquark composition: 35% triplet, 65% singlet
- Varying T from 130 to 190 MeV changes D*/D composition from 25%/75% to 32%/68%

## Context

This work extends previous coalescence model studies of exotic hadron production in heavy ion collisions (Cho 2010, Cho 2011, Sun 2017, Sun 2018) and responds to the first experimental evidence of X(3872) in Pb-Pb collisions reported by the CMS Collaboration (2019). The approach differs from conventional electron-positron or proton-proton collision studies by leveraging the abundant charm quarks and antiquarks in the hot fireball of heavy ion collisions. The results enable future experimental tests of X(3872) elliptic flow predictions and suggest extending measurements across different collision systems (Pb-Pb, Au-Au, Xe-Xe, Cu-Cu, O-O, d-A/p-A) to probe system-size dependence.
