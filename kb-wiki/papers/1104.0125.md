# Scaling Function, Spectral Function and Nucleon Momentum Distribution in Nuclei

> The link between the scaling function extracted from the analysis of (e,e') cross sections and the spectral function/momentum distribution in nuclei is revisited. Several descriptions of the spectral function based on the independent particle model are employed, together with the inclusion of nucleon correlations, and effects of the energy dependence arising from the width of the hole states are investigated. Although some of these approaches provide rough overall agreement with data, they are not found to be capable of reproducing one of the distinctive features of the experimental scaling function, namely its asymmetry. However, the addition of final-state interactions, incorporated in the present study using either relativistic mean field theory or via a complex optical potential, does lead to asymmetric scaling functions in accordance with data. The present analysis seems to indicate that final-state interactions constitute an essential ingredient and are required to provide a proper description of the experimental scaling function.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that spectral function models based on the independent particle shell model, even when augmented with symmetric energy spreads to account for nucleon correlations, cannot reproduce the asymmetry of the experimental scaling function; only the inclusion of final-state interactions (FSI), modeled via relativistic mean field theory or a complex optical potential, yields asymmetric scaling functions consistent with data.

## Method
The authors constructed spectral functions for 16O and 12C using harmonic-oscillator single-particle wave functions and natural orbitals from the Jastrow correlation method, replacing delta-function energy dependencies with Gaussian or Lorentzian distributions of variable width. They computed the scaling function from these spectral functions via the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) formalism and compared with longitudinal experimental data at fixed momentum transfer q = 1 GeV/c. Final-state interactions were incorporated by computing inclusive cross sections using either relativistic mean field (RMF) theory or a Dirac optical potential, from which distorted momentum distributions and scaling functions were derived.

## Key numbers
- Gaussian widths σ varied from 10 MeV to 90 MeV for the 1s and 1p hole states in 16O; the main effect on the scaling function occurred when varying σ_1p, with the maximum increasing significantly as σ_1p decreased to 10 MeV.
- Using natural orbitals instead of harmonic-oscillator wave functions enhanced the maximum of the scaling function by approximately 10%.
- Scaling of the first kind (no explicit q-dependence) was fulfilled for momentum transfers q > 600–700 MeV/c, as shown for 16O with q ranging from 100 MeV/c to 2000 MeV/c in steps of 200 MeV/c.
- The RFG scaling function is given by f_RFG(ψ) = (3/4)(1 − ψ²)Θ(1 − ψ²), where ψ is the dimensionless scaling variable.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of y-scaling (Day 1990, Ciofi degli Atti 1987, 1992) and superscaling (Donnelly and Sick 1999, 2005; Antonov 2004–2007) by systematically testing whether spectral functions with symmetric energy spreads can reproduce the experimental scaling function asymmetry. The authors show that models based solely on the independent particle shell model or Jastrow correlations (Stoitsov 1993) fail, while FSI treatments using RMF (Caballero 2005) or optical potentials succeed. This enables future work to use the experimentally known scaling function to constrain the spectral function and momentum distribution, provided FSI are properly accounted for.
