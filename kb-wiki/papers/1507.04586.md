# Rotation-induced deep crustal heating of millisecond pulsars

> The spin-down of a neutron star, e.g. due to magneto-dipole losses, results in compression of the stellar matter and induces nuclear reactions at phase transitions between different nuclear species in the crust. We show that this mechanism is effective in heating recycled pulsars, in which the previous accretion process has already been compressing the crust, so it is not in nuclear equilibrium. We calculate the corresponding emissivity and confront it with available observations, showing that it might account for the likely thermal ultraviolet emission of PSR J0437-4715.

## Key claim

The spin-down of a neutron star compresses its crust and induces nuclear reactions at phase transitions between different nuclear species, providing a heating mechanism for recycled pulsars whose crusts are not in nuclear equilibrium due to prior accretion. The authors show this mechanism might account for the likely thermal ultraviolet emission of PSR J0437-4715, but note that the agreement is marginal given uncertainties in both measurements and theoretical models, and that other reheating mechanisms may also contribute.

## Method

The authors calculate the heating rate from nuclear reactions at isobaric phase transition surfaces in the crust as the star spins down, using three approaches: a simple Newtonian estimate, a general-relativistic slow-rotation approximation following the Hartle-Thorne formalism, and a full numerical calculation using the RNS code for rapidly rotating stars. They employ six equations of state (three BSk and three PAL) for the core, matched with an accreted crust equation of state from Haensel and Zdunik (1990), with phase transition pressures and energy releases taken from Haensel and Zdunik (2008) assuming pure iron ashes.

## Key numbers

- Heating rate scaling estimate: \(\dot{E} \approx -7 \times 10^{28} \, a \, (R_6^7 / M_{1.4}^2) \, \Omega_3 \, \dot{\Omega}_{-14} \sum_i (P_i / 10^{31}\,\mathrm{erg\,cm^{-3}}) (q_i / 1\,\mathrm{MeV})\,\mathrm{erg\,s^{-1}}\), where \(R_6 = R/(10^6\,\mathrm{cm})\), \(M_{1.4}=M/(1.4M_\odot)\), \(\Omega_3=\Omega/(10^3\,\mathrm{s^{-1}})\), \(\dot{\Omega}_{-14}=\dot\Omega/(10^{-14}\,\mathrm{s^{-2}})\)
- Heating rate relative to rotational energy loss: \(\dot{E}^\infty \approx (0.5-20) \times 10^{-5} \dot{E}_{\mathrm{rot}}\)
- For PSR J0437-4715: \(\Omega = 1091\,\mathrm{s^{-1}}\), \(\dot\Omega = 2.60 \times 10^{-15}\,\mathrm{s^{-2}}\)
- Observed redshifted effective temperature range for PSR J0437-4715: \(1.25 \times 10^5\,\mathrm{K} < T_{\mathrm{eff}}^\infty < 3.5 \times 10^5\,\mathrm{K}\) (Durant et al. 2012), assuming redshifted circumferential radius \(7.8\,\mathrm{km} < R_\infty < 15\,\mathrm{km}\)
- Extrapolated temperature at \(R_\infty = 17\,\mathrm{km}\): \(T_{\mathrm{eff}}^\infty \approx 0.97 \times 10^5\,\mathrm{K}\)
- Kepler frequency for BSk21 EOS with \(M=1.75 M_\odot\): \(\Omega_{\mathrm{K}} = 10807.9\,\mathrm{s^{-1}}\)

## Context

This work extends the concept of "deep crustal heating" (Haensel and Zdunik 1990), which occurs during accretion, to the spin-down phase of recycled pulsars. It contradicts the much lower heating rate found by Iida and Sato (1997), who the authors argue made an error in their Lagrangian displacement calculations. The mechanism is compared to rotochemical heating (Reisenegger 1995) and vortex creep (Alpar et al. 1984), but unlike those, it is insensitive to uncertain nucleon superfluidity parameters. The results enable interpretation of thermal ultraviolet observations of millisecond pulsars like PSR J0437-4715 (Kargaltsev et al. 2004; Durant et al. 2012) and can be combined with other heating mechanisms to explain observed surface temperatures.
