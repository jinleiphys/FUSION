# Thrust at N^3LL with Power Corrections and a Precision Global Fit for alphas(mZ)

> We give a factorization formula for the e+e- thrust distribution dsigma/dtau with tau=1-T based on soft-collinear effective theory. The result is applicable for all tau, i.e. in the peak, tail, and far-tail regions. The formula includes O(alphas^3) fixed-order QCD results, resummation of singular partonic alphas^j ln^k(tau)/tau terms with N^3LL accuracy, hadronization effects from fitting a universal nonperturbative soft function defined in field theory, bottom quark mass effects, QED corrections, and the dominant top mass dependent terms from the axial anomaly. We do not rely on Monte Carlo generators to determine nonperturbative effects since they are not compatible with higher order perturbative analyses. Instead our treatment is based on fitting nonperturbative matrix elements in field theory, which are moments Omega_i of a nonperturbative soft function. We present a global analysis of all available thrust data measured at center-of-mass energies Q=35 to 207 GeV in the tail region, where a two parameter fit to $α_s(m_Z)$ and the first moment Omega_1 suffices. We use a short distance scheme to define Omega_1, called the R-gap scheme, thus ensuring that the perturbative dsigma/dtau does not suffer from an O(Lambda_QCD) renormalon ambiguity. We find alphas(mZ)=0.1135 \pm (0.0002)_{expt} \pm (0.0005)_{hadr} \pm (0.0009)_{pert}, with chi^2/dof=0.91, where the displayed 1-sigma errors are the total experimental error, the hadronization uncertainty, and the perturbative theory uncertainty, respectively. The hadronization uncertainty in alphas is significantly decreased compared to earlier analyses by our two parameter fit, which determines Omega_1=0.323 GeV with 16% uncertainty.

## Key claim

The authors present a factorization formula for the e+e- thrust distribution that achieves N^3LL resummation accuracy and incorporates nonperturbative effects through a field-theoretic soft function, yielding a precision determination of alpha_s(mZ) = 0.1135 with a global fit to tail-region data, but this result depends on the specific R-gap scheme used to define the nonperturbative parameter Omega_1 and on the removal of an O(Lambda_QCD) renormalon ambiguity.

## Method

The authors derived a factorization formula for dsigma/dtau using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), combining O(alpha_s^3) fixed-order QCD results with N^3LL resummation of singular partonic alpha_s^j ln^k(tau)/tau terms. They performed a global two-parameter fit to alpha_s(mZ) and the first moment Omega_1 of a nonperturbative soft function, using all available thrust data at center-of-mass energies Q = 35 to 207 GeV in the tail region, with the Omega_1 parameter defined in the R-gap scheme at a reference scale R_Delta = mu_Delta = 2 GeV to remove infrared renormalon ambiguities.

## Key numbers

- alpha_s(mZ) = 0.1135 ± 0.0002 (experimental) ± 0.0005 (hadronization) ± 0.0009 (perturbative), with chi^2/dof = 0.91
- Omega_1 = 0.323 GeV with 16% uncertainty, determined from the two-parameter global fit
- The analysis used thrust data at center-of-mass energies Q = 35 to 207 GeV
- The fractional change in alpha_s from including the leading power correction at Q = mZ is estimated as delta_alpha_s/alpha_s ≈ -(9 ± 3)%, based on a scaling estimate with Lambda = 0.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the N^3LL resummation framework of Becher and Schwartz (2008) and the O(alpha_s^3) fixed-order calculations of Gehrmann et al. (2007) and Weinzierl (2008, 2009), while addressing the systematic limitation of using Monte Carlo generators for nonperturbative corrections as done by Dissertori et al. (2007, 2009) and Becher and Schwartz (2008). The authors' field-theoretic treatment of hadronization via the R-gap scheme and the universal nonperturbative soft function enables a more rigorous determination of alpha_s and provides a framework for future analyses of other event shapes using the same Omega_1 parameter.
