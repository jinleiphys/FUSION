# Weakness or Strength in the Golden Years of RHIC and LHC?

> Recent LHC data suggest that perturbative QCD provides a qualitatively consistent picture of jet quenching. Constrained to RHIC pi0 suppression, zero parameter WHDG energy loss predictions agree quantitatively with the charged hadron v2 and D meson RAA measured at LHC and qualitatively with the charged hadron RAA. On the other hand, RHIC-constrained LHC predictions from fully strongly-coupled AdS/CFT qualitatively oversuppress D mesons compared to data; light meson predictions are on less firm theoretical ground but also suggest oversuppression. More detailed data from heavy, especially B, mesons will continue to help clarify our picture of the physics of the quark-gluon plasma. Since the approach of pQCD predictions to LHC data occurs at momenta >~ 15 GeV/c, a robust consistency check between pQCD and both RHIC and LHC data requires RHIC jet measurements.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD (pQCD) provides a qualitatively consistent picture of jet quenching at LHC energies, but this agreement only becomes robust for momenta above approximately 15 GeV/c; below this threshold, non-perturbative effects appear large, and a full consistency check between pQCD and both RHIC and LHC data requires RHIC jet measurements that are not yet available.

## Method

The WHDG energy loss model was used, which incoherently convolves pQCD-based radiative energy loss from the DGLV opacity expansion with pQCD-based collisional energy loss. The model was constrained to RHIC pi0 suppression data, with the QGP medium density assumed to scale linearly with observed charged particle multiplicity, yielding zero-parameter predictions for LHC observables. For comparison, AdS/CFT energy loss predictions based on heavy quark drag derivations were also extrapolated to LHC using the same zero-parameter scaling approach.

## Key numbers

- pQCD predictions approach LHC data at momenta greater than approximately 15 GeV/c
- Zero-parameter WHDG predictions agree with LHC charged hadron v2 and D meson RAA data to within approximately plus or minus 50 percent
- AdS/CFT calculations for very high momentum quarks (approximately 100 GeV/c) strongly coupled to a constant-temperature medium predict thermalization in approximately 2.7 fm; with Bjorken expansion included, the thermalization distance increases to approximately 4.1 fm
- Both AdS/CFT and WHDG calculations are consistent with current CMS B meson suppression results (non-prompt J/psi mesons)

## Context

This work extends comparisons between pQCD-based energy loss calculations (Wicks 2005) and RHIC data to the new LHC measurements, following earlier successes in describing light hadron suppression at RHIC (Vitev 2002) and quantitative disagreements with heavy quark suppression and azimuthal anisotropy (Wicks 2005, Adare 2010). The paper contrasts pQCD results with AdS/CFT approaches that successfully described the small viscosity-to-entropy ratio (Kovtun 2004, Schenke 2010) and heavy quark suppression at RHIC (Akamatsu 2008, Horowitz 2010), finding that AdS/CFT oversuppresses D mesons at LHC. Future measurements of heavy mesons, particularly B mesons, and RHIC jet observables are identified as key to distinguishing between these theoretical frameworks.
