# Effect of hyperons on f-mode oscillations in Neutron Stars

> As the densities in the interior of neutron stars exceed those of terrestrial nuclear experiments, they provide a scope for studying the nature of dense matter under extreme conditions. The composition of the inner core of neutron stars is highly uncertain, and it is speculated that exotic forms of matter such as hyperons may appear there. Gravitational waves emitted by unstable oscillation modes in neutron stars contain information about their interior composition and therefore allow us to probe the interior directly. Recently, a systematic investigation of f-mode oscillations in neutron stars revealed the role of the uncertainty in nuclear saturation parameters, particularly the effective nucleon mass, on its frequency. In this work, we study the influence of the appearance of hyperons on f-mode oscillation frequencies and therefore on the emission of gravitational waves. We also speculate whether a future detection of f-mode frequencies could provide a possibility of probing the presence of hyperons in the neutron star core.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the inclusion of hyperons in neutron star cores affects f-mode oscillation frequencies, finding that hyperons produce significantly higher f-mode frequencies and lower maximum masses compared to purely nucleonic cores, but the effective nucleon mass remains the dominant source of uncertainty in the frequency predictions.

## Method

The authors use a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model to describe beta-equilibrated, charge-neutral hadronic matter composed of baryons from the baryon octet and leptons, with baryon-baryon interactions mediated by scalar, vector, and isovector mesons and hyperon-hyperon interactions mediated by strange scalar and strange vector mesons. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star mass and radius, then compute f-mode oscillation frequencies within the Cowling approximation by solving eigenvalue equations for perturbed fluid equations using Ridder's method.

## Key numbers

- f-mode frequency changes over an interval of approximately 0.09-0.15 kHz for a range of U_Xi from -40 MeV to +40 MeV for neutron stars with masses above 1.5 solar masses
- f-mode frequency as a function of neutron star mass changes within an interval of 1.8-2.6 kHz for the range of uncertainty in effective mass (0.55-0.65 m_N) compatible with the 2 solar mass maximum neutron star mass constraint
- For l=3 f-modes, frequency as a function of neutron star mass changes within an interval of 2.1-3.2 kHz
- For l=4 f-modes, frequency as a function of neutron star mass changes within an interval of 2.5-3.8 kHz
- The 2 solar mass constraint on maximum mass is satisfied for nucleon effective mass less than or equal to 0.7 m_N, while the 2.1 solar mass constraint is satisfied for m*/m_N less than or equal to 0.67

## Context

This work extends a systematic investigation by Sukrit (2020) of the role of nuclear saturation parameters on f-mode oscillations for nucleonic systems to include hyperonic degrees of freedom, and builds on prior studies by Chatterjee (2015, 2016) showing that repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions can produce stiffer equations of state. The results enable future gravitational wave detections of f-mode frequencies to potentially discriminate between purely nucleonic cores and cores containing hyperons, and the authors compare their asteroseismology relations to earlier empirical fits by Andersson (1996, 1998) and Benhar (2004) that used selected equations of state including exotic matter.
