# Deforming the Trail: Baseline Quantum Circuitry for $\text{SU(2)}_k$ Lattice Gauge Theory

> Quantifying quantum resources for simulating the fundamental forces of Nature is sensitive to the mapping of gauge fields onto finite quantum computational architectures. When locally truncating lattice gauge theories in the irreducible representation basis, it has been proposed to further deform the theory via quantum groups. The purpose of this deformation is (1) to provide an infinite tower of finite-dimensional ($d = k+1$) groups systematically approximating the infinite-dimensional gauge links and (2) to restore the physical unitarity of a plaquette operator diagonalization procedure analytically derived from the field continuum by recontracting vertex pairs. For the SU(2)$_k$ Yang-Mills pure-gauge theory, we provide a constructive strategy of gauge-variant completions to extend this unitarity to the entire computational Hilbert space, leading to well-defined time evolution unitaries as targets for optimized circuit synthesis. Leveraging basic circuit decompositions and symmetries of the diagonalized plaquette operator, we report resource upper-bounds on the generalized-controlled-X two-qudit gates for arbitrary local truncation $d$, reducing estimates and scaling relative to the non-deformed theory by three polynomial powers from $O(d^8)$ to $O(d^5)$. Examining the stronger q-deformed gauge constraint, which softens the total flux at vertices, we show that the physical Hilbert space dimension of the deformed plaquette operator scales equivalently to its non-deformed counterpart with a constant factor $0.2563(5)$. Thus, despite affecting interactions at all scales as exemplified by the observed flux hierarchy inversion symmetry, q-deformation continues to pass scrutiny as a reliable truncation offering advantages in quantum circuit synthesis.

## Key claim

The paper provides a constructive strategy for extending the unitarity of q-deformed F-moves to the entire computational Hilbert space via gauge-variant completions, enabling well-defined time evolution unitaries for SU(2)$_k$ lattice gauge theory, but this unitarity is only guaranteed within the physical subspace and requires careful operator completions for unphysical states.

## Method

The authors implement a sequence of q-deformed F-moves with carefully chosen phases to diagonalize the plaquette operator in SU(2)$_k$ Yang-Mills theory on a one-dimensional plaquette chain, reducing the active space to a single link. They apply gauge-variant completions in two stages: first during diagonalization to compress the operator, and second when identifying gates for device implementation, using the freedom to choose any completion for unphysical states since dynamics are isolated to the physical subspace.

## Key numbers

- The q-deformation parameter is set to $q = e^{i\frac{2\pi}{k+2}}$, where $k$ corresponds to local flux truncation $k/2$ and local Hilbert space dimension $d = k+1$
- Resource upper-bounds on generalized-controlled-X two-qudit gates are reduced from $\mathcal{O}(d^8)$ to $\mathcal{O}(d^5)$, a reduction of three polynomial powers relative to the non-deformed theory
- The physical Hilbert space dimension ratio between q-deformed and non-deformed theories converges to $0.2563(5)$ at large $k$, with the error bar expressing an estimate of systematics
- For the q-deformed theory, the physical Hilbert space dimension scales as an order-8 polynomial in $d = k+1$, with separate expressions for odd and even $k$ truncation
- The critical value for convergence of ground state observables is $k_c \simeq 4.4/g - 2.5$ on an infinite lattice of plaquettes

## Context

This work extends the q-deformation approach proposed by Zache (2023) for SU(2) lattice gauge theory, which itself builds on the F-move diagonalization procedure originally formulated by Robson (1982) for untruncated lattices. The paper addresses the challenge that F-moves are not generally unitary under local flux truncation, and provides a complete circuit synthesis strategy that improves upon non-deformed techniques by Jiang (2025) by reducing resource scaling from $\mathcal{O}(d^8)$ to $\mathcal{O}(d^5)$. This enables future quantum simulation of non-Abelian gauge theories with manageable local Hilbert space dimensions, with techniques extendable to SU(3).
