# On the radial expansion of tubular structures in a quark gluon plasma

> We study the radial expansion of cylindrical tubes in a hot QGP. These tubes are treated as perturbations in the energy density of the system which is formed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. We start from the equations of relativistic hydrodynamics in two spatial dimensions and cylindrical symmetry and perform an expansion of these equations in a small parameter, conserving the nonlinearity of the hydrodynamical formalism. We consider both ideal and viscous fluids and the latter are studied with a relativistic Navier-Stokes equation. We use the equation of state of the MIT bag model. In the case of ideal fluids we obtain a breaking wave equation for the energy density fluctuation, which is then solved numerically. We also show that, under certain assumptions, perturbations in a relativistic viscous fluid are governed by the Burgers equation. We estimate the typical expansion time of the tubes.

## Key claim

The paper derives nonlinear wave equations (a breaking wave equation for ideal fluids and a Burgers equation for viscous fluids) governing the radial expansion of cylindrical energy-density perturbations (tubes) in a quark-gluon plasma, and estimates the expansion time of these tubes. The hedge is that the derivation relies on a small-parameter expansion that conserves nonlinearity, and the results are obtained under the assumption of cylindrical symmetry and the MIT bag model equation of state, with the viscous case using a relativistic Navier-Stokes equation (which the authors note is not a causal theory).

## Method

The authors start from relativistic hydrodynamics in two spatial dimensions with cylindrical symmetry, using the MIT bag model equation of state. They perform a reductive perturbation method (stretched coordinates and series expansions in a small parameter) on the Euler and continuity equations for an ideal fluid, and on the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation for a viscous fluid, to derive wave equations for the energy density perturbation. The resulting equations are solved numerically with a Gaussian initial pulse to study the radial expansion of the tube, and the effect of background expansion is estimated using Bjorken cooling.

## Key numbers

- Initial tube radius range used in calculations: \(0.1 \, \text{fm} \leq r_0 \leq 0.8 \, \text{fm}\)
- Background temperatures considered: \(T_0 = 150 \, \text{MeV}\) and \(T_0 = 500 \, \text{MeV}\)
- Bag constant: \(\mathcal{B}^{1/4} = 170 \, \text{MeV}\), corresponding to \(T_B = 91 \, \text{MeV}\)
- Speed of sound: \(c_s^2 = 1/3\)
- Viscosity parameters used: \(\eta/s = 0.08\) and \(\zeta/s = 0\)
- Initial proper time for Bjorken cooling: \(\tau_0 = 1 \, \text{fm}\)
- Viscosity damps the pulse amplitude by a factor of ten in 1 fm (observed from comparison of figures)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of perturbations in a QGP background (Shuryak and others, around 2005-2010) by keeping nonlinear terms in the hydrodynamical equations, unlike the linearized approach used in those earlier works. It also builds on the authors' own prior work (Fogaca, Navarra, and Ferreira Filho, around 2010) on nonlinear waves in nuclear matter. The results enable estimates of how long tubular structures (color flux tubes) survive in the QGP formed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, which is relevant for understanding two-particle correlation structures observed in experimental data. The paper notes that a future version using the causal Muller-Israel-Stewart theory is in progress.
