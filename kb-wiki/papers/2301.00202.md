# A transport model description of Time-Dependent Generator Coordinate under Gaussian overlap approximation

> In this work, we derived a transport equation based on a generalized equation of time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) under the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA). The transport equation is obtained by using quantum-mechanics phase space distributions under a ``quasi-particle" picture and strategy of Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkood-Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy. The theoretical advantage of this transport equation is that time evolution of $s$-body phase space density distribution is coupled with $s+1$-body phase space density distributions, and thus, non-adiabatic effects and dynamical fluctuations could be involved by more collective degrees and entanglement of phase space trajectories. In future, we will perform the numerical calculations for fission nuclei after obtaining collective inertia and potential energy surface (PES).

## Key claim

The authors derive a transport equation from the generalized N-dimensional time-dependent generator coordinate method under Gaussian overlap approximation (TDGCM+GOA) that can incorporate non-adiabatic effects and dynamical fluctuations through higher-order correlations, but the work remains purely formal with no numerical calculations performed; the authors state they will perform numerical calculations for fission nuclei only after obtaining collective inertia and potential energy surface (PES) in the future.

## Method

The authors start from the generalized N-dimensional TDGCM+GOA equation and perform a Wigner transformation on the collective wave function to obtain quantum-mechanical phase space density distributions under a "quasi-particle" picture. They then apply the Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy strategy to derive a transport equation where the time evolution of the s-body phase space density distribution is coupled to the (s+1)-body phase space density distribution. For the specific case of two collective degrees (q1=β2, q2=β3), they provide a numerical recipe using a test particle method with random three-body collisions to approximate the collision term.

## Key numbers

- The typical time for individual nucleon motion inside a fission nucleus is roughly 10^{-22} s, while the time scale for collective deformation is 10^{-21} s, as cited from Verriere (2020)
- The initial collective wave packet is boosted in the q1 (β2) direction with amplitude k such that the average energy lies a few MeV above the inner fission barrier
- The test particle method uses N_test test particles to represent each quasi-particle, following the approach of Wong (1982) for the nuclear Vlasov model

## Context

This work extends the TDGCM+GOA framework, which has been applied by Regnier (2016, 2018) and Tao (2017) using only two collective degrees (typically q20, q30 or β2, β3) under the adiabatic assumption. The authors note that previous calculations by Moller (2001), Younes (2009), Dubray (2012), Schunck (2014), and Regnier (2017) showed the necessity of including more degrees of freedom to describe non-adiabatic effects. The transport equation approach enables future inclusion of dynamical fluctuations and higher-order correlations that could improve fission fragment distribution predictions, though the authors acknowledge that exact calculations of the collision term remain impossible since they always involve one more degree than currently included.
