# Properties of nuclei in the neutron star crust

> In the present study we investigate the static properties of nuclei in the inner crust of neutron stars. Using the Hartree-Fock method in coordinate space, together with the semiclassical approximation, we examine the patterns of phase transitions.

# Properties of nuclei in the neutron star crust

## Key claim
The shell effects associated with unbound neutrons, computed via a semiclassical approximation and Hartree-Fock method, are comparable in magnitude to the liquid drop energy differences between various phases of inhomogeneous nuclear matter in the inner crust, and may reverse the phase transition order predicted by liquid drop based approaches. The authors suggest that the inner crust may have a complicated, possibly completely disordered structure with several types of coexisting shapes, rather than a simple sequence of ordered phases.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations in coordinate space using a Skyrme effective interaction (SLy4 force), solving the Poisson equation for the electric potential within a box with periodic boundary conditions and zero total charge, abandoning the Wigner-Seitz approximation for the Coulomb interaction. They combined this with a semiclassical approximation to compute the shell energy contribution from unbound neutrons, treating both the liquid drop energy and shell effects on the same footing within an energy functional that includes Thomas-Fermi, internal shell, and external shell components.

## Key numbers
- The proton density outside nuclei becomes zero for densities below 0.08 fm$^{-3}$, allowing neglect of the contribution from unbound protons to the energy.
- The semiclassical shell energy for two obstacles (spherical, rod-like, or slab-like nuclei) at distance $d$ scales as $d^{-(6-i)/2}$ where $i=0,1,2$ for spheres, rods, and slabs respectively, with amplitude proportional to $(\rho_n^{out})^{(2+i)/6}$.
- Box sizes used in calculations were $d=26$ fm, $d=23.4$ fm, and $d=20.8$ fm for different nuclear configurations.

## Context
This work extends earlier liquid drop model studies of the inner crust phase transitions (nuclei → rods → plates → tubes → bubbles → uniform matter) by incorporating shell effects that were neglected in previous approaches. It builds on the semiclassical treatment of shell energy by Magierski et al. (2001) and the Hartree-Fock calculations of Negele and Vautherin (1973) and Douchin and Haensel (2001), but goes beyond them by treating shell effects from unbound neutrons and allowing multiple lattice geometries for the same nuclear shape. The results enable future studies of the static and dynamic properties of the inner crust, suggesting that the structure may be more complex and disordered than previously assumed.
