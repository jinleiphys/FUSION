# Exploring X17 and dark charges in the context of Standard Model tensions

> We report on recent results, according to which the hyphothetical X17 boson could affect the muon g-2 anomaly and the Lamb Shift. Moreover by considering the kinetic mixing between this new boson and the U(1)Y we establish possible contribution of the X17 to the W mass.

# Exploring X17 and dark charges in the context of Standard Model tensions

## Key claim
The hypothetical X17 boson, with a mass of approximately 17 MeV, could simultaneously explain the muon g-2 anomaly, the Lamb shift discrepancies in muonic hydrogen and deuterium, and the W boson mass tension, provided that the couplings to muon, proton, and neutron have opposite signs under a new U(1)X symmetry.

## Method
The authors computed one-loop corrections to the muon anomalous magnetic moment from X17 exchange, using the observed discrepancy Δaμ to set an upper bound on the muon-X17 coupling εμ. They then used first-order perturbation theory with a Yukawa potential from X17 exchange to calculate the additional contribution to the 2S1/2-2P3/2 Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen and muonic deuterium, inverting the experimental deviations to extract bounds on the proton and neutron couplings εp and εn. Finally, they applied kinetic mixing between the X17 boson and the U(1)Y hypercharge boson to derive an upper bound on the mixing parameter ξ from the CMS W mass measurement.

## Key numbers
- Muon g-2 discrepancy: Δaμ = (251 ± 59) × 10^(-11), corresponding to a 4.2σ deviation from the Standard Model
- Electron g-2 discrepancy: Δae = (4.8 ± 3.0) × 10^(-13)
- Upper bound on muon-X17 coupling: |εμ| < 2.154 × 10^(-4) for MX = 17 MeV
- Lamb shift deviation for muonic hydrogen: δEμ^H = (-0.363, -0.251) meV
- Lamb shift deviation for muonic deuterium: δEμ^D = (-0.475, -0.337) meV
- Lower bound on proton-X17 coupling: εp between -0.04260 and -0.02982 for MX in [16.7, 17.2] MeV and εμ = 2.154 × 10^(-4)
- Upper bound on neutron-X17 coupling: |εn| ≤ 10^(-2) for MX = 17 MeV
- CMS W boson mass: 80360 ± 9.9 MeV
- Upper bound on kinetic mixing parameter: |ξ| < 2.2 × 10^(-2) for ΔMW ≤ 10 MeV

## Context
This work extends the X17 hypothesis originally proposed by Feng et al. (2016) to explain the ATOMKI anomaly in 8Be* nuclear transitions, applying it to three additional Standard Model tensions. The authors build on the muon g-2 results from the Brookhaven (Bennett 2004) and Fermilab (Abi 2021) collaborations, the Lamb shift measurements from muonic atom experiments (muon1, muon2, muon3, muon4), and the recent CMS W mass measurement (W1). The analysis enables experimental searches for X17 with specific predicted coupling ranges and sign relationships, and motivates further theoretical work on the U(1)X symmetry structure and its possible connection to dark matter.
