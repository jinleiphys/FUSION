# New density-independent interactions for nuclear structure calculations

> We present a new two-body finite-range and momentum-dependent but density-independent effective interaction, which can be interpreted as a regularized zero-range force. We show that no three-body or density-dependent terms are needed for a correct description of saturation properties in infinite matter, that is, on the level of low-energy density functional, the physical three-body effects can be efficiently absorbed in effective two-body terms. The new interaction gives a very satisfying equation of state of nuclear matter and opens up extremely interesting perspectives for the mean-field and beyond-mean-field descriptions of atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors present a new two-body finite-range, momentum-dependent, density-independent effective interaction that can reproduce nuclear saturation properties without any three-body or density-dependent terms, but they note that the isoscalar effective mass remains rather low compared to empirical values.

## Method

The interaction is constructed as a regularized zero-range force using Gaussian form factors with range parameter a, combined with momentum-dependent operators up to second order. The authors derive analytic expressions for nuclear matter properties (energy per particle, incompressibility, effective mass, symmetry energy and its derivatives) in terms of auxiliary functions of the dimensionless variable ξ = k_F a. Two parameter sets (REG2a.130531 and REG2b.130531) are fitted to saturation properties and tested in Hartree-Fock calculations of doubly magic nuclei using the HFODD solver.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³ for both parameter sets
- Binding energy per nucleon E/A = -16 MeV at saturation for both sets
- Incompressibility K_∞ = 230 MeV for both sets
- Symmetry energy J = 32 MeV for both sets
- Slope of symmetry energy L = 100.2 MeV (REG2a.130531) and 58 MeV (REG2b.130531)
- Curvature of symmetry energy K_sym = 83.26 MeV (REG2a.130531) and -175 MeV (REG2b.130531)
- Isoscalar effective mass m*/m = 0.38 (REG2a.130531) and 0.41 (REG2b.130531)
- Range parameter a = 0.8 fm for both sets
- Spin-orbit strength W_0 = 209.30 MeV·fm⁵ (REG2a.130531) and 168.35 MeV·fm⁵ (REG2b.130531)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dobaczewski (2012) by implementing a finite-range regularized delta interaction as an alternative to the standard Skyrme and Gogny forces, which require density-dependent terms to achieve saturation. The new interaction addresses the problem identified by Duguet (2008) that density-dependent forces with non-integer powers prevent proper beyond-mean-field calculations using symmetry restoration or the Generator Coordinate Method. The results enable future mean-field and beyond-mean-field calculations without the need for additional momentum cutoffs, though the low effective mass remains a limitation to be addressed.
