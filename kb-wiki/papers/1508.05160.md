# Anisotropic emission of thermal dielectrons from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$~GeV with EPOS3

> Dileptons, as an electromagnetic probe, are crucial to study the properties of a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) created in heavy ion collisions. We calculated the invariant mass spectra and the anisotropic emission of thermal dielectrons from Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$~GeV based on EPOS3. This approach provides a realistic (3+1)-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic description of the expanding hot and dense matter with a very particular initial condition, and a large set of hadron data and direct photons (besides $v_{2}$ and $v_{3}$ !) can be successfully reproduced. Thermal dilepton emission from both the QGP phase and the hadronic gas are considered, with the emission rates based on Lattice QCD and a vector meson model, respectively. We find that the computed invariant mass spectra (thermal contribution + STAR cocktail) can reproduce the measured ones from STAR at different centralities. Different compared to other model predictions, the obtained elliptic flow of thermal dileptons is larger than the STAR measurement referring to all dileptons. We observe a clear centrality dependence of thermal dilepton not only for elliptic flow $v_{2}$ but also for higher orders. At a given centrality, $v_{n}$ of thermal dileptons decreases monotonically with $n$ for $2\leq n\leq5$.

## Key claim

The computed elliptic flow of thermal dielectrons from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV using EPOS3 is larger than the STAR measurement referring to all dileptons, which differs from other model predictions that typically yield smaller values.

## Method

The authors used EPOS3, a (3+1)-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic model with flux tube initial conditions, to simulate the space-time evolution of the hot and dense matter. Thermal dilepton emission rates were calculated from the QGP phase using an extrapolation of lattice QCD results to finite momenta, and from the hadronic gas phase using a vector meson dominance model for the $\rho$ meson with in-medium self-energy corrections from scattering with pions and nucleons. The invariant mass spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ were obtained by integrating these rates over the full hydrodynamic evolution and adding the STAR cocktail for non-thermal contributions.

## Key numbers

- The initial proper time for the hydrodynamic evolution was $\tau_0 = 0.35$ fm/c.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used was $\eta/S = 0.08$.
- The hadronization temperature was $T_H = 168$ MeV.
- The momentum integral for the invariant mass spectrum ranged from 0 to 5 GeV/c.
- The $\rho$ meson vacuum mass is $m_\rho = 775$ MeV, and the vacuum width is $\Gamma_\rho^{vac} = \frac{g_\rho^2}{48\pi}m_\rho(\frac{p_0}{\omega_0})^3$.
- For the QGP emission rate, the form factor used $\Lambda = 2T$ and the constant factor $K = 2$.
- The $\rho\pi$ forward scattering amplitude used Regge intercept $\alpha = 0.642$ with residues $r^{\rho N} = 28.59$ and $r^{\rho\pi} = 12.74$, and Pomeron intercept $\alpha = 1.093$ with residues $r^{\rho N} = 11.88$ and $r^{\rho\pi} = 7.508$.
- The $\rho$ meson self-energy was calculated for a fixed three-momentum $q = 0.3$ GeV/c at $T = 150$ MeV.
- The emission rates were shown for QGP phase at temperatures 150 MeV and 200 MeV, and for hadronic gas phase at temperatures 100 MeV and 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the EPOS3 framework previously used to successfully reproduce hadron data and direct photon elliptic and triangular flow (Fu 2015) to the calculation of thermal dielectron production. It contrasts with other approaches such as the forward scattering amplitude method combined with viscous hydrodynamics (Eletsky, Vujanovic) and the Parton-Hadron String Dynamics model (Linnyk, Cassing), which typically predict smaller elliptic flow for thermal dileptons. The results provide a benchmark for systematic comparison of hadron, dilepton, and photon observables, and offer predictions for higher-order flow harmonics ($v_3$, $v_4$, $v_5$) that await experimental verification.
