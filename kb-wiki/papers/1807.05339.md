# Generalized Seniority Schmidt Model and the g-factors in Semi-magic Nuclei

> We have recently applied the generalized seniority approach successfully to explain the B(E1)/B(E2)/B(E3) properties of the semi-magic nuclei. In the present paper, we extend this approach to the Schmidt model as Generalized Seniority Schmidt Model and calculate the g-factors of the various seniority states in the semi-magic nuclei. We find that the magnetic moments and the g-factors do show a particle number independent behavior in multi-j configurations, as expected in the seniority scheme. The calculated results explain the experimental trends quite well. We find that the g-factors of all the seniority states arising from a given multi-j configuration for identical nucleons is equal to the g-factor of the seniority $v = 1$ state from that configuration. Also, the g-factors are found to be a sensitive probe for fixing the multi-j configuration, which are fully consistent with the configurations assigned to explain the B(EL) properties in our previous works. We are also able to make definite predictions for many cases.

# Generalized Seniority Schmidt Model and the g-factors in Semi-magic Nuclei

## Key claim

The g-factors of all seniority states arising from a given multi-j configuration for identical nucleons are equal to the g-factor of the seniority v=1 state from that configuration, and these g-factors exhibit a particle number independent behavior in multi-j configurations, but only when the correct multi-j subspace (different before and after mid-shell) is chosen.

## Method

The authors extend the generalized seniority approach to the Schmidt model, creating the Generalized Seniority Schmidt Model (GSSM), by replacing the single-j orbital with a multi-j configuration $\tilde{j} = j \otimes j' \otimes ...$ in the standard Schmidt g-factor formulas. They apply this model to calculate g-factors for various seniority states (v=1, v=2, v=3) in Sn isotopes (Z=50), Pb isotopes (Z=82), and N=82 isotones, using the same multi-j configurations previously established from B(EL) systematics in their earlier works.

## Key numbers

- For the first excited 2+ states in Sn isotopes before mid-shell (lighter than $^{116}$Sn), the GSSM uses $\tilde{j}=g_{7/2} \otimes d_{5/2} \otimes s_{1/2} \otimes d_{3/2}=19/2$ with $\Omega=10$; after mid-shell, the configuration is $\tilde{j}= d_{5/2} \otimes s_{1/2} \otimes d_{3/2} \otimes h_{11/2}=23/2$ with $\Omega=12$
- The Schmidt single particle g-factors for pure $g_{7/2}$ neutrons (before middle) is 0.425 n.m. (positive) and for pure $h_{11/2}$ neutrons (after middle) is -0.348 n.m. (negative)
- For the ${11/2}^-$ states and ${10}^+$ isomers in Sn isotopes, the GSSM uses $\Omega=9$ corresponding to $\tilde{j}=d_{3/2} \otimes s_{1/2} \otimes h_{11/2}$ configuration
- For Pb isotopes, the GSSM uses $\Omega=13$ corresponding to $\tilde{j} = p_{3/2} \otimes f_{7/2} \otimes i_{13/2}$ configuration
- For the ${10}^+$ and ${27/2}^-$ isomers in N=82 isotones, the GSSM predicts g-factor values of the order of 1.27 n.m.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous generalized seniority studies of B(E1)/B(E2)/B(E3) properties in semi-magic nuclei (Maheshwari and Jain, 2015-2017) to magnetic moments and g-factors. It builds on the seniority scheme originally introduced by Racah and the generalized seniority concept of Arima and Ichimura (1960s), as well as Talmi's extension to non-degenerate orbitals. The results are compared with shell model calculations by Brown et al. (2005), QRPA calculations by Terasaki et al. (2002), RQRPA calculations by Ansari and Ring (2007), and Nucleon Pair Approximation calculations by Jia et al. (2007). The GSSM provides a simple framework to predict g-factors for unmeasured cases, particularly for ${27/2}^-$ isomers in Sn isotopes and various states in N=82 isotones where experimental data are lacking.
