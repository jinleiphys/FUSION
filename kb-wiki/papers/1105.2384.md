# The Radius of the Proton: Size Does Matter

> The measurement by Pohl et al. [1] of the 2S_1/2^F=1 to 2P_3/2^F=2 transition in muonic hydrogen and the subsequent analysis has led to a conclusion that the rms charge radius of the proton differs from the accepted (CODATA [2]) value by approximately 4%, leading to a 4.9 s.d. discrepancy. We investigate the muonic hydrogen spectrum relevant to this transition using bound-state QED with Dirac wave-functions and comment on the extent to which the perturbation-theory analysis which leads to the above conclusion can be confirmed.

## Key claim

The authors use bound-state QED with Dirac wave-functions to calculate the muonic hydrogen transition energy measured by Pohl et al. (2010), and they comment on the extent to which the perturbation-theory analysis that led to the 4.9 standard deviation discrepancy between the extracted proton radius and the CODATA value can be confirmed.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation numerically for the muon in muonic hydrogen using a reduced mass and a point-Coulomb potential, then introduce the vacuum polarization potential and finite-size Coulomb potentials (with exponential, Yukawa, or Gaussian charge distributions) to compute eigenvalue shifts. They calculate the Lamb shift, fine structure, and hyperfine structure by taking differences between converged eigenvalues for the relevant 2S and 2P states, and they check their numerical accuracy against analytic Dirac eigenvalues and the virial theorem.

## Key numbers

- Lamb shift (2S1/2 to 2P1/2) with point-Coulomb and point vacuum polarization potentials: 205.1822 meV
- Parameterization of Lamb shift dependence on rms charge radius with exponential finite-size Coulomb and finite vacuum polarization potentials: ΔE = 205.1822 - 5.2519 ⟨r_p²⟩ + 0.0546 ⟨r_p²⟩^{3/2} meV
- 2P fine structure splitting with exponential finite-Coulomb and finite vacuum polarization potentials: 8.4206(5) meV
- 2S1/2 hyperfine splitting (F=1 to F=0) with point-Coulomb and point vacuum polarization potentials: 22.8967(5) meV
- 2P1/2 hyperfine splitting with exponential finite-Coulomb and finite vacuum polarization potentials: 7.6204(5) meV
- 2P3/2 hyperfine splitting with exponential finite-Coulomb and finite vacuum polarization potentials: 3.0415(5) meV
- Numerical accuracy of Dirac eigenvalue for 2S1/2 state: reproduced to within 0.01 μeV; virial theorem test shows differences of 0.18 μeV for point-Coulomb and 0.45 μeV for finite-Coulomb potentials; authors conservatively take errors as ±0.5 μeV

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QED calculations of Borie (1982) and Martynenko (2004, 2006) by using a fully relativistic Dirac-equation approach to compute the muonic hydrogen spectrum relevant to the proton radius puzzle. The authors note that since this talk was presented, they have investigated an additional term that may account for all or part of the discrepancy reported by Pohl et al. (2010). The calculations enable a more rigorous check of the perturbative analysis and will be detailed in upcoming publications on Dirac calculations and form factor dependence.
