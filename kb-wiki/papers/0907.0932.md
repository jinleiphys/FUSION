# Deformed shell model results for two neutrino positron double beta decay of $^{74}$Se

> Half-lives $T_{1/2}^{2ν}$ for two neutrino positron double beta decay modes $β^{+}$EC/ECEC are calculated for $^{74}$Se, a nucleus of current experimental interest, using deformed shell model based on Hartree-Fock states employing a modified Kuo interaction in ($^{2}p_{3/2}$, $^{1}f_{5/2}$, $^{2}p_{1/2}$, $^{1}g_{9/2}$) space. The calculated half-life for the ECEC mode is $\sim 10^{26}$yr and it may be possible to observe this in the near future with improved sensitivity of experiments.

# Deformed shell model results for two neutrino positron double beta decay of $^{74}$Se

## Key claim
The deformed shell model (DSM) predicts a half-life for the two-neutrino electron capture / electron capture (ECEC) mode of $^{74}$Se of approximately $10^{26}$ years, which the authors suggest may be observable in the near future with improved experimental sensitivity; however, this is a theoretical estimate that has not yet been confirmed by experiment, and the current experimental limits are only $>10^{18}-10^{19}$ years.

## Method
The authors performed deformed shell model (DSM) calculations based on Hartree-Fock intrinsic states with angular momentum projection and band mixing, using a modified Kuo effective interaction in the ($^{2}p_{3/2}$, $^{1}f_{5/2}$, $^{2}p_{1/2}$, $^{1}g_{9/2}$) model space with $^{56}$Ni as an inert core. Only prolate intrinsic states were considered, with 10 configurations for $^{74}$Se, 24 for $^{74}$Ge, and 65 for the intermediate $^{74}$As nucleus; the integrated kinematical factors were calculated following the prescription of Doi and Kotani.

## Key numbers
- Calculated half-life for the ECEC mode of $^{74}$Se: $\sim 10^{26}$ yr
- Current experimental lower limits for $^{74}$Se $2\nu$ $\beta^{+}$EC and ECEC modes: $>10^{18}-10^{19}$ yr (from SuperNEMO project measurements using a 400 cm$^3$ HPGe detector)
- Single particle energies used in the model space: 0.0 MeV ($^{2}p_{3/2}$), 0.78 MeV ($^{1}f_{5/2}$), 1.08 MeV ($^{2}p_{1/2}$), and 4.5 MeV ($^{1}g_{9/2}$)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous DSM calculations for $^{78}$Kr (Shukla et al., 2009) to $^{74}$Se, a nucleus of current experimental interest for which no prior theoretical predictions existed. The DSM approach has been successfully applied to spectroscopic properties of Se isotopes and to $2\nu$ double beta decay in $^{76}$Ge, and the results are comparable to those from QRPA and PHFB models for similar nuclei. The calculations provide the first theoretical half-life estimates for positron double beta decay modes of $^{74}$Se, enabling comparison with ongoing experiments seeking to improve sensitivity beyond current limits.
