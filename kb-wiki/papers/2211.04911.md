# Faddeev fixed-center approximation to the $╬ĘK^*\bar{K}^*$, $ŽĆK^*\bar{K}^*$ and $KK^*\bar{K}^*$ systems

> The three-body $╬ĘK^*\bar{K}^*$, $ŽĆK^*\bar{K}^*$ and $KK^*\bar{K}^*$ systems are investigated within the framework of fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equations, where $K^*\bar{K}^*$ is treated as the scalar meson $f_0(1710)$. The interactions between $ŽĆ$, $╬Ę$, $K$ and $K^*$ are taking from the chiral unitary approach. By scattering the $╬Ę$ meson on the clusterized $(K^*\bar{K}^*)_{f_0(1710)}$ system, we find a peak in the modulus squared of the three-body scattering amplitude and it can be associated as a bound state with quantum numbers $I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(0^{-+})$. Its mass and width are around 2054 MeV and 60 MeV, respectively. This state could be associated to the $╬Ę(2100)$ meson. For the $ŽĆ(K^*\bar{K}^*)_{f_0(1710)}$ scattering, we find a bump structure around 1900-2000 MeV with quantum numbers $1^-({0^{-+}})$. While for the $K K^* \bar{K}^*)_{f_0(1710)}$ system, there are three structures. One of them is much stable and its mass is about 2130 MeV. It is expected that these theoretical predictions here could be tested by future experimental measurements, such as by the BESIII, BelleII and LHCb collaborations.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the $\eta K^*\bar{K}^*$ system, with $K^*\bar{K}^*$ treated as the $f_0(1710)$ scalar meson, produces a bound state with mass around 2054 MeV and width around 60 MeV that could be associated to the $\eta(2100)$ meson; however, the peak position shifts from 2070 to 2100 MeV depending on the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ used (945 or 1125 MeV), and the identification with $\eta(2100)$ is a tentative association rather than a definitive assignment.

## Method

The authors apply the fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equations, treating the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ subsystem as a bound cluster identified with the $f_0(1710)$ scalar meson. The two-body pseudoscalar-vector meson scattering amplitudes ($\eta K^*$, $\pi K^*$, $K K^*$, and their charge conjugates) are taken from the chiral unitary approach as developed by Roca (2005) and Geng (2006). The three-body scattering amplitude $T$ is constructed from the sum of single-scattering contributions $t_1$ and $t_2$ (weighted by a factor $M_{f_0(1710)}/m_{K^*}$) and the loop function $G_0$ describing the propagation of the third particle between the two constituents of the cluster, with the form factor of the $f_0(1710)$ state regularized by a cutoff $\Lambda$.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the $f_0(1710)$ cluster: 1704 MeV (with $\Lambda = 1125$ MeV) or 1733 MeV (with $\Lambda = 945$ MeV), corresponding to the "evaluation" and "average" values from the Review of Particle Physics (2020).
- For the $\eta(K^*\bar{K}^*)_{f_0(1710)}$ system: peak in $|T|^2$ at 2054 MeV (with $\Lambda = 1125$ MeV) or 2092 MeV (with $\Lambda = 945$ MeV), with quantum numbers $I^G(J^{PC}) = 0^+(0^{-+})$ and width about 60 MeV.
- For the $\pi(K^*\bar{K}^*)_{f_0(1710)}$ system: bump structure between 1900 and 2000 MeV (1929 MeV with $\Lambda = 1125$ MeV; 1963 MeV with $\Lambda = 945$ MeV) with quantum numbers $1^-(0^{-+})$.
- For the $K(K^*\bar{K}^*)_{f_0(1710)}$ system: three structures with masses approximately 2062, 2130, and 2209 MeV (with $\Lambda = 1125$ MeV); the state at 2130 MeV is stable against cutoff variation.
- Masses used for input particles: $m_K = 495.6455$ MeV, $m_\pi = 138.04$ MeV, $m_\eta = 547.862$ MeV, $m_{K^*} = 893.1$ MeV, $\Gamma_{K^*} = 49.1$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the fixed-center approximation methodology previously applied to the $\pi K^*\bar{K}$ system (Zhang, 2016), where the $\pi_1(1600)$ exotic state was dynamically generated, and to the $\eta K^*\bar{K}$ system (Zhang, 2019), which predicted exotic states around 1700 MeV. The present study moves to systems involving $K^*\bar{K}^*$ as the cluster, building on the interpretation of $f_0(1710)$ as a $K^*\bar{K}^*$ molecular state from vector-vector interaction studies (Geng, 2008; Geng, 2009). The predictions for the $\eta K^*\bar{K}^*$ system offer a three-body molecular interpretation for the $\eta(2100)$ meson observed by DM2 (1988) and BESIII (2016), while the $KK^*\bar{K}^*$ results suggest candidates for strange mesons in the 2000-3000 MeV region where the Review of Particle Physics lists few established states. The results are intended to guide experimental searches at BESIII, BelleII, and LHCb.
