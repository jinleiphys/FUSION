# Nonequilibrium kinetic freeze-out properties in relativistic heavy ion collisions from energies employed at the RHIC beam energy scan to those available at the LHC

> In this paper, we investigate the kinetic freeze-out properties in relativistic heavy ion collisions at different collision energies. We present a study of standard Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast-Wave (BGBW) fits and Tsallis Blast-Wave (TBW) fits performed on the transverse momentum spectra of identified hadrons produced in Au + Au collisions at collision energies of $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=$ 7.7 - 200 GeV at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), and in Pb + Pb collisions at collision energies of $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=$ 2.76 and 5.02 TeV at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The behavior of strange and multi-strange particles is also investigated. We found that the TBW model describes data better than the BGBW one overall, and the contrast is more prominent as the collision energy increases as the degree of non-equilibrium of the produced system is found to increase. From TBW fits, the kinetic freeze-out temperature at the same centrality shows a weak dependence of collision energy between 7.7 and 39 GeV, while it decreases as collision energy continues to increase up to 5.02 TeV. The radial flow is found to be consistent with zero in peripheral collisions at RHIC energies but sizable at LHC energies and central collisions at all RHIC energies. We also observed that the strange hadrons, with higher temperature and similar radial flow, approach equilibrium more quickly from peripheral to central collisions than light hadrons. The dependence of temperature and flow velocity on non-equilibrium parameter ($q-1$) is characterized by two second-order polynomials. Both $a$ and $dξ$ from the polynomials fit, related to the influence of the system bulk viscosity, increase toward lower RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The Tsallis Blast-Wave (TBW) model describes the transverse momentum spectra of identified hadrons better than the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast-Wave (BGBW) model across all collision energies from RHIC Beam Energy Scan to LHC, with the improvement becoming more prominent at higher collision energies because the degree of non-equilibrium of the produced system increases with energy.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous fits of the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast-Wave (BGBW) model and the Tsallis Blast-Wave (TBW) model to published transverse momentum spectra of identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons, and strange particles) from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV at the LHC. The fits extracted the kinetic freeze-out temperature T, average radial flow velocity <beta>, and the non-equilibrium parameter q for different centrality classes and collision energies, with the fit range limited to pT <= 3 GeV/c to focus on bulk properties.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T shows weak dependence on collision energy between sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 and 39 GeV, then decreases as collision energy continues to increase up to 5.02 TeV
- Average radial flow velocity <beta> in most central collisions is between 0.4 and 0.5 at RHIC energies and around 0.6 at LHC energies
- In most peripheral collisions at RHIC energies, <beta> drops to zero; at LHC energies it is less than 0.3
- The non-equilibrium parameter (q-1) in peripheral collisions increases from less than 0.04 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to more than 0.1 at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- At LHC energies, the increase of T from most central to most peripheral collisions is around 40% in BGBW but only about 5% in TBW

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kinetic freeze-out properties using blast-wave models, particularly the BGBW model of Schnedermann (1993) and the Tsallis statistics approach introduced by Wilk (1999) and Tang (2008). The systematic comparison across the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan to LHC energies resolves conflicting claims in the literature: some studies (Lao, 2016; Lao, 2017) found increasing kinetic freeze-out temperature with energy, others (Das, 2014; Zhang, 2016; Adamczyk, 2017) found decreasing trends, and some (Andronic, 2014; Abelev, 2012) found little dependence. The TBW model's ability to describe data with a weak centrality dependence of temperature and zero radial flow in peripheral RHIC collisions enables future studies of how non-equilibrium effects and bulk viscosity influence the system evolution from the partonic to hadronic phase.
