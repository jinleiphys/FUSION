# Conservation, crossing symmetry, and completeness in diagrammatic theories

> The diagrammatic analysis of interacting particle assemblies harbors a fundamental mismatch between two of its main implementations: Phi-derivable (conserving) approximations and parquet (crossing symmetric) models. No termwise expansion, short of the exact theory itself, can be both conserving and crossing symmetric. This work applies the Kraichnan embedded-Hamiltonian formalism for strongly coupled systems to investigate consistency of the interplay between purely pair-mediated correlations and pair-irreducible ones. The approach sheds a different light on the issue of crossing symmetry versus conservation. In the process, the parquet equations acquire a different formulation.

## Key claim

No termwise diagrammatic expansion, short of the exact theory, can simultaneously satisfy both microscopic conservation and crossing symmetry; this work uses the Kraichnan embedded-Hamiltonian formalism to show that the incompatibility arises from a loss of Fock-space completeness in any approximate model, rather than from a fundamental limitation of the diagrammatic method itself.

## Method

The author applies the Kraichnan two-step embedding procedure, which first replicates the physical Hamiltonian into an arbitrarily large number of identical copies with a collective index and then averages over a stochastic distribution of coupling phases that select only specific classes of diagrams (the s, t, and u pairwise channels). This construction yields a reduced Luttinger-Ward functional for the fluctuation-exchange (FLEX) model, whose variational derivatives are then compared with the parquet equations to isolate the origin of the conservation-crossing symmetry conflict.

## Key numbers

* The Kraichnan coupling is defined as φ ≡ 1 − (1−s)(1−t)(1−u), where s, t, and u are each complex exponentials with random phases uniformly distributed in [−π, π]; this construction prevents overcounting of coincident closed graphs in the Luttinger-Ward functional.
* The identity φ_{νν'|ν'ν} ≡ 1 holds for all collective indices ν, ν', ensuring that the response vertex contracts correctly when external perturbation lines are attached.
* The reduced Luttinger-Ward functional Φ^{stu} is obtained by averaging over the stochastic distribution of K couplings, yielding a kernel Λ that is crossing symmetric but whose second functional derivative δ²Φ^{stu}/δGδG necessarily generates structures beyond Λ.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Becker and Grosser (1970s) and Jackson and Smith (1980s) on the impossibility of combining conservation and crossing symmetry in approximate many-body expansions, and it directly addresses the parquet-like structure of Φ-derivable scattering amplitudes studied by Smith. The Kraichnan formalism provides a Hamiltonian-based framework that preserves all causal sum rules and conservation laws, enabling a systematic comparison between the FLEX model of Scalapino and co-authors and the parquet equations; the results clarify why the parquet self-energy, despite being crossing symmetric, must discard noncrossing terms generated by its own variational derivative, and they open the way to constructing controlled approximations that respect either conservation or crossing symmetry while understanding exactly which physical constraints are sacrificed.
