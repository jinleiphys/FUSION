# Kosterlitz-Thouless transition and vortex-antivortex lattice melting in two-dimensional Fermi gases with $p$- or $d$-wave pairing

> We present a theoretical study of the finite-temperature Kosterlitz-Thouless (KT) and vortex-antivortex lattice (VAL) melting transitions in two-dimensional Fermi gases with $p$- or $d$-wave pairing. For both pairings, when the interaction is tuned from weak to strong attractions, we observe a quantum phase transition from the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) superfluidity to the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of difermions. The KT and VAL transition temperatures increase during this BCS-BEC transition and approach constant values in the deep BEC region. The BCS-BEC transition is characterized by the non-analyticities of the chemical potential, the superfluid order parameter, and the sound velocities as functions of the interaction strength at both zero and finite temperatures; however, the temperature effect tends to weaken the non-analyticities comparing to the zero temperature case. The effect of mismatched Fermi surfaces on the $d$-wave pairing is also studied.

## Key claim

The BCS-BEC evolution in two-dimensional Fermi gases with p- or d-wave pairing is not a smooth crossover but a quantum phase transition characterized by non-analyticities in thermodynamic quantities at the point where the chemical potential vanishes; however, finite temperature weakens these non-analyticities compared to the zero-temperature case.

## Method

The authors employed a path-integral formalism with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to derive an effective action for the auxiliary bosonic pairing field, then expanded to Gaussian order to capture collective mode dynamics. They computed the superfluid density and sound velocity from the low-energy expansion of the Goldstone mode propagator, neglecting Landau damping at low temperature, and solved the gap equation, number equation, and KT/VAL critical temperature equations self-consistently for both isotropic and anisotropic p- and d-wave pairings.

## Key numbers

- For the deep BEC region of p-wave pairing, the KT transition temperature approaches a constant value \(T_{\rm KT} \simeq 0.0625 \epsilon_{\rm F}\) and the VAL melting temperature approaches \(T_{\rm M} \simeq (0.6/\pi) T_{\rm KT}\)
- The VAL melting temperature is given by \(T_{\rm M} = 0.3 \sqrt{J_x^{\rm p}(T_{\rm M}) J_y^{\rm p}(T_{\rm M})}\) where \(J_i = \rho_i^{\rm p}/(4m)\)
- The KT transition temperature satisfies \(T_{\rm KT} = (\pi/2) \sqrt{J_x^{\rm p}(T_{\rm KT}) J_y^{\rm p}(T_{\rm KT})}\)
- The second derivative of the coefficient \(\zeta_0^{\rm p}\) with respect to chemical potential diverges as \(\partial^2 \zeta_0^{\rm p}/\partial \mu^2 \sim (1/(T \Delta^4)) \ln(\mu/\Delta)\) near \(\mu = 0\)

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC evolution from s-wave pairing, where the transition is a smooth crossover (Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink, 1985; Sade Melo et al., 1993), to p- and d-wave pairing where a quantum phase transition occurs (Read, 2000; Botelho and Sade Melo, 2005; Cao et al., 2013). It also generalizes previous studies of KT and VAL transitions in two-dimensional s-wave Fermi gases (Loktev et al., 2001; Botelho and Sade Melo, 2006) to higher partial waves, and enables experimental probes of the BCS-BEC transition through the non-monotonic behavior of the sound velocity.
