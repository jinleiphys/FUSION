# Geometrical description of the phase transition in the Gaździcki Gorenstein model

> The derivation of the phase transition in the model of Gaździcki and Gorenstein is generalized and simplified by using a geometrical construction.

## Key claim

The phase transition in the Gaździcki–Gorenstein model, which produces the horn in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio, can be derived more simply and generally using a geometrical tangent construction on entropy-density curves, without relying on the Van der Waals–Maxwell formalism that is inapplicable here because the constant \(B\) must jump discontinuously between phases.

## Method

The author assumes that for each phase the entropy density \(s(\epsilon)\) is a monotonically increasing, concave-up function of energy density \(\epsilon\), with a single crossing point \(\epsilon^*\) where \(s_W(\epsilon^*) = s_Q(\epsilon^*)\). A common tangent is drawn touching the W-phase curve at \(\epsilon_1 < \epsilon^*\) and the Q-phase curve at \(\epsilon_2 > \epsilon^*\); the straight-line segment between these points represents mixed states with volume fraction \(\lambda\) of Q-phase. The equality of the slopes of the tangent at the two contact points implies equal temperatures, and the linear relation between entropy and energy densities implies equal pressures, establishing that the mixed state is in equilibrium and has higher entropy than either pure phase at the same overall energy density.

## Key numbers

- The horn in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio is observed around 9 GeV cms collision energy per nucleon pair.
- The transition temperature is about 200 MeV.
- In the W-phase, the mass of strange degrees of freedom is assumed to be 500 MeV; non-strange degrees of freedom are massless.
- The constant \(B\) in the equation of state \((p_i - B_i)V = N_i T\) is \(B_W = 0\) and \(B_Q \approx 0.6\) GeV.

## Context

This work simplifies and generalizes the original derivation by Gaździcki and Gorenstein (2000s), who predicted the horn as a signature of the transition from a hadronic (white) W-phase to a deconfined quark–gluon Q-phase. The standard Van der Waals–Maxwell construction is not applicable because the constant \(B\) jumps discontinuously between phases and energy and volume are not independent. The geometrical method makes the result almost obvious and requires fewer assumptions about the detailed properties of the two phases, enabling future extensions to other phase coexistence scenarios in heavy-ion collisions.
