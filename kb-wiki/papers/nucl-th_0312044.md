# Gluons, tadpoles, and color neutrality in a two-flavor color superconductor

> Considering cold, dense quark matter with two massless quark flavors, we demonstrate how, in a self-consistent calculation in the framework of QCD, the condensation of Cooper pairs induces a non-vanishing background color field. This background color field has precisely the right magnitude to cancel tadpole contributions and thus ensures overall color neutrality of the two-flavor color superconductor.

## Key claim

In a two-flavor color superconductor, a non-vanishing expectation value of the gluon field (specifically the eighth color component in the time direction) arises self-consistently from the Yang-Mills equation and cancels the tadpole contribution, thereby ensuring overall color neutrality without introducing a color-chemical potential "by hand."

## Method

The authors employed the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) effective action formalism for QCD in the mean-field approximation, including only the sunset-type two-particle irreducible diagram. They solved the Yang-Mills equation for the gluon field with the quark tadpole contribution computed self-consistently, allowing for a non-zero expectation value of the gluon field in the quark propagator. The tadpole integral was evaluated by splitting the momentum integration into three regions around the Fermi surface, assuming the difference in chemical potentials induced by the background field is parametrically smaller than the gap.

## Key numbers

- The tadpole contribution for vanishing background field is given by ${\cal T}^{\mu}_a = \frac{2g}{\pi^2} \mu \phi^2 \ln(2\mu/\phi) (T_a)_{33} g^{\mu0} = -\sqrt{6} \mu \phi^2 \delta_{a8} g^{\mu0}$, where $\phi \simeq 2\mu \exp[-3\pi^2/(\sqrt{2}g)]$ is the color-superconducting gap parameter.
- The effective color-chemical potential from the tadpole is $\mu_8 = -\sqrt{6} \pi^2 \phi^2/(g\mu)$, derived from $\mu_8 = -g (1/(3m_g^2)) \sqrt{6} \mu \phi^2$ with gluon mass $m_g = g\mu/(\sqrt{2}\pi)$.
- The self-consistent background field is $A \simeq -\sqrt{6} \pi^2 \phi^2/(g^2 \mu)$, giving $\mu_8 = gA$ of the same magnitude as the tadpole-derived value.
- The difference in chemical potentials between paired (colors 1,2) and unpaired (color 3) quarks is $\delta\mu \equiv \mu_2 - \mu_1 = -\sqrt{3}gA/2 \simeq (3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}) \phi^2/(g\mu)$, which is parametrically much smaller than the gap $\phi$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Gerhold and Rebhan (2003), who first identified that Cooper pairing in two-flavor color superconductors induces non-vanishing tadpole contributions and argued for color neutrality via a functional integral constraint. The present paper provides a self-consistent solution where the background gluon field cancels the tadpole, analogous to mechanisms in spontaneously broken gauge theories. This enables a fully consistent QCD treatment of color neutrality in color superconductivity, complementing earlier Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model approaches (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek, 1998) that introduced color-chemical potentials by hand. The result confirms that the previous gap equation solutions (Son, 1999; Pisarski, Rischke, 2000) remain correct to subleading order in the coupling constant.
