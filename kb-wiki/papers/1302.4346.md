# Exclusive diffractive photon bremsstrahlung at the LHC

> We calculate differential distributions for the $p p \to p p γ$ reaction at the LHC energy $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV. We consider diffractive classical bremsstrahlung mechanisms including effects of non point-like nature of protons. In addition, we take into account (vector meson)-pomeron, photon-pion as well as photon-pomeron exchange processes for the first time in the literature. Predictions for the total cross section and several observables related to these processes e.g. differential distributions in pseudorapidities and transverse momenta of photons or protons are shown and discussed. The integrated diffractive bremsstrahlung cross section ($E_γ>100$ GeV) is only of the order of $μ$b. We try to identify regions of the phase space where one of the mechanisms dominates. The classical bremsstrahlung dominates at large forward/backward photon pseudorapidities, close to the pseudorapidities of scattered protons. In contrast, the photon-pomeron (pomeron-photon) mechanism dominates at midrapidities but the related cross section is rather small. In comparison the virtual-omega rescattering mechanism contributes at smaller angles of photons (larger photon rapidities). Photons in the forward/backward region can be measured by the Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDCs) installed in experiments at the LHC while the midrapidity photons are difficult to measure (small cross section, small photon transverse momenta). Protons could be measured by ALFA detector (ATLAS) or TOTEM detector at CMS. The exclusivity could be checked with the help of main central detectors.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first detailed calculations of differential distributions for the exclusive $p p \to p p \gamma$ reaction at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV, including several new mechanisms (vector meson-pomeron, photon-pion, and photon-pomeron exchange) not previously considered in the literature; however, the integrated diffractive bremsstrahlung cross section for photon energies above 100 GeV is only of the order of microbarns, and the dominance of different mechanisms depends strongly on the photon pseudorapidity region.

## Method

The authors calculated amplitudes for four classical bremsstrahlung diagrams driven by pomeron exchange, including off-shell nucleon form factors with a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_N \sim 1$ GeV. They additionally computed amplitudes for virtual-omega meson rescattering (with $\omega \to \gamma$ conversion via vector dominance), photon-pion and pion-photon exchange processes, and photon-pomeron (pomeron-photon) exchange processes, using the Donnachie-Landshoff pomeron parametrization and standard electromagnetic and hadronic form factors.

## Key numbers

- The integrated diffractive bremsstrahlung cross section for $E_\gamma > 100$ GeV is of the order of $\mu$b at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV.
- The pomeron trajectory parameters used: $\alpha_{I\!\!P}(0) = 1.0808$, $\alpha'_{I\!\!P} = 0.25$ GeV$^{-2}$, with $B_{I\!\!P}^{NN} = 9$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $C_{I\!\!P}^{NN} = 21.7$ mb.
- The $\omega$ meson to nucleon coupling constant is taken as $g^2_{\omega N N}/4\pi = 10$, and the $\omega \to \gamma$ conversion factor is $C_{\omega \to \gamma} = \sqrt{\alpha_{em}/20.5} \simeq 0.02$.
- The $\gamma p$ total cross section parameters: $C_{I\!\!P}^{\gamma p} = 0.0677$ mb, $C_{I\!\!R}^{\gamma p} = 0.129$ mb, with $\alpha_{I\!\!R}(0) = 0.5475$.
- The ZDCs at CMS cover $|\eta| > 8.5$ and at ATLAS cover $|\eta| > 8.3$; the dipole mass squared for the proton Dirac form factor is $m_D^2 = 0.71$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the approximate treatment of Khoze (2010) for exclusive photon bremsstrahlung at the LHC, which treated protons as point-like particles and gave only approximate formulas for classical bremsstrahlung without differential distributions. The paper introduces for the first time vector meson-pomeron, photon-pion, and photon-pomeron exchange mechanisms for this reaction, building on the authors' prior work on $p p \to p p \omega$ (CLSS) and $p p \to p p \pi^0$ (LS13). The results enable experimental searches for exclusive photon production at the LHC using ZDCs for forward photons and ALFA or TOTEM detectors for forward protons, potentially allowing measurements of the elastic $pp$ cross section, luminosity, and detector alignment.
