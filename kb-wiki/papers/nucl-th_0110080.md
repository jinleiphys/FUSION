# Scaling in Relativistic Thomas-Fermi Approach for Nuclei

> By using the scaling method we derive the virial theorem for the relativistic mean field model of nuclei treated in the Thomas-Fermi approach. The Thomas-Fermi solutions statisfy the stability condition against scaling. We apply the formalism to study the excitation energy of the breathing mode in finite nuclei with several relativistic parameter sets of common use.

## Key claim

The scaling method can be used to derive the virial theorem for the relativistic mean field model in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, and the resulting Thomas-Fermi solutions satisfy the stability condition against scaling. The method is then applied to compute the excitation energy of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (breathing mode) in finite nuclei, but the authors note that no RMF parameter set seems capable of reproducing the mass-number dependence of the experimental data over the whole analyzed region, particularly in lighter nuclei.

## Method

The authors applied a scaling transformation to the baryon density and meson fields in the nonlinear sigma-omega model, deriving the virial theorem from the stationarity of the scaled energy at lambda=1. They then computed the second derivative of the scaled energy with respect to the scaling parameter (the restoring force) and combined it with the mass parameter to obtain the excitation energy of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance for spherical finite nuclei in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, using several relativistic parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility values for the parameter sets used: NL-Z2 (K_nm=172 MeV), NL1 (K_nm=212 MeV), NL3 (K_nm=272 MeV), NL-SH (K_nm=355 MeV), NL2 (K_nm=399 MeV)
- Monopole energy in 208Pb ranges from 12.3 MeV (NL-Z2) to 18.1 MeV (NL2)
- From nuclear matter formula, E_M = 92, 102, 115, 132 and 140/A^{1/3} MeV for NL-Z2, NL1, NL3, NL-SH and NL2 respectively
- Finite size effects reduce the nuclear matter prediction by a factor of ~1.4 in 40Ca to ~1.3 in 208Pb
- The empirical estimate for the ISGMR energy is E_M ~ 80/A^{1/3} MeV
- Based on 144Sm and 208Pb data, the authors suggest K_nm of a RMF interaction should belong to the range 225-255 MeV (or 230-260 MeV if disregarding NL-Z2)

## Context

This work extends earlier scaling-method calculations performed with the linear sigma-omega model by Nishizaki (1987) and Zhu (1991) to nonlinear parameter sets and avoids the leptodermous expansion used in some previous studies such as Stoitsov (1994). The results are compared with time-dependent RMF calculations by Vretenar (1997) and with the empirical law E_M ~ 80/A^{1/3} MeV. The paper enables a connection between the nuclear compression modulus K_nm and measured ISGMR energies, suggesting that RMF interactions should have K_nm in the range 225-255 MeV, which is higher than the 215+/-15 MeV determined from non-relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA analyses using Skyrme and Gogny interactions by Blaizot (1995) and Farine (1997).
