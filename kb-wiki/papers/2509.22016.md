# Dynamical gluon effects in the three-dimensional structure of pion

> We investigate the internal structure of the pion, including the contributions from one dynamical gluon, using the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach. By solving a light-front QCD Hamiltonian with a three-dimensional confining potential, we obtain the light-front wavefunctions (LFWFs) for both the quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock sectors. These wavefunctions are then employed to compute the unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and the transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) of valence quarks and gluons. We also extract the transverse spatial distributions, providing the squared radii of quark and gluon densities in the impact-parameter space. This work contributes toward a three-dimensional understanding of the pion's internal structure in both momentum and coordinate space.

## Key claim

The authors compute the three-dimensional structure of the pion using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) including both the quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock sectors, but the results depend on a phenomenological gluon mass parameter (0.60 GeV) and a confining potential with parameters fitted to light meson masses, meaning the calculation is not a first-principles QCD solution.

## Method

The authors solve a light-front QCD Hamiltonian with a three-dimensional confining potential using the BLFQ approach, obtaining light-front wavefunctions (LFWFs) for the \(|q\bar{q}\rangle\) and \(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\) Fock sectors. They then compute the unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) for valence quarks and gluons from overlaps of these LFWFs, and extract transverse spatial distributions via Fourier transform of the GPDs.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass parameter \(m_q = 0.39\) GeV, gluon mass parameter \(m_g = 0.60\) GeV, confinement strength \(\kappa = 0.65\) GeV, coupling constant \(g_s = 1.92\), and basis scale parameter \(b = 0.29\) GeV (Table I)
- First moment of quark TMD \(\langle k_\perp\rangle_q = 0.26\) GeV; second moment \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle_q = 0.087\) GeV\(^2\) (Table II)
- First moment of gluon TMD \(\langle k_\perp\rangle_g = 0.25\) GeV; second moment \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle_g = 0.086\) GeV\(^2\) (Table II)
- The gluon TMD peaks at longitudinal momentum fraction \(x \approx 0.33\)

## Context

This work extends earlier BLFQ studies of the pion that computed PDFs, electromagnetic form factors, and higher-twist TMDs using the same Fock sectors (Lan 2019, Lan 2021, Zhu 2023). It provides three-dimensional imaging of the pion that can be compared with phenomenological models such as the light-front constituent model (Lorce 2016), light-front holographic QCD model (Puhan 2023), and light-front quark model (Puhan 2023), and it enables benchmark predictions for future experiments at Jefferson Lab, the Electron-Ion Collider, and the Electron-Ion Collider in China.
