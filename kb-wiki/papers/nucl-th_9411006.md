# Investigation of the Neutron Form Factors by Inclusive Quasi-Elastic Scattering of Polarized Electrons off Polarized $^{3}$He: A Theoretical Overview

> The theory of quasi-elastic inclusive scattering of polarized leptons off polarized $^3$He is critically reviewed and the origin of different expressions for the polarized nuclear response function appearing in the literature is explained. The sensitivity of the longitudinal asymmetry upon the neutron form factors is thoroughly investigated and the role played by the polarization angle for minimizing the proton contribution is illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to earlier doubts raised in the literature, reliable information on neutron form factors can be extracted from inclusive quasi-elastic scattering of polarized electrons off polarized $^3$He, provided one chooses a proper kinematics (specifically a critical polarization angle) to minimize the otherwise sizeable proton contribution.

## Method

The authors derive the polarized nuclear structure functions $G_1^A$ and $G_2^A$ within the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) using a nucleon spin-dependent spectral function, and clarify the origin of discrepancies between different expressions in the literature by distinguishing two extraction prescriptions (I and II). They then compute the longitudinal asymmetry for $^3$He at quasi-elastic kinematics, compare with experimental data from MIT-Bates and MIT-Harvard, and systematically vary the polarization angle $\beta$ and incident electron energy to identify conditions where the proton contribution to the asymmetry is minimized.

## Key numbers

- At the top of the quasi-elastic peak for $\epsilon_1 = 574$ MeV and $\theta_e = 44^\circ$, the theoretical asymmetry is $A^{th} = 3.74\%$, with a proton contribution $A^{th}_p = 2.20\%$ (Gari-Kruempelmann form factors).
- After averaging over an energy transfer interval of about $100$ MeV, the experimental asymmetry from MIT-Caltech is $A^{exp}_{qe} = 2.41 \mp 1.29 \mp 0.51\%$, compared to $A^{th} = 1.65\%$ and $A^{th}_p \simeq 0$.
- For $\epsilon_1 = 578$ MeV, $\theta_e = 51.1^\circ$, and $\theta^* = 90.2^\circ$, the theoretical asymmetry at the quasi-elastic peak is $A^{th} = 4.00\%$ with $A^{th}_p = 2.78\%$, versus the MIT-Harvard result $A^{exp}_{qe} = 1.75 \mp 1.20 \mp 0.31\%$.
- For $\theta^* \approx 0^\circ$ at $\epsilon_1 = 574$ MeV and $\theta_e = 51.1^\circ$, the theoretical asymmetry without averaging is $-3.43\%$, with a proton contribution of $1.30\%$, compared to the averaged experimental value $A^{exp}_{qe} = -3.79 \mp 1.37 \mp 0.67\%$.
- A critical polarization angle $\beta_c$ exists near $105^\circ$ for $\theta_e = 75^\circ$ where the proton contribution is almost vanishing, and for $10^\circ \leq \theta_e \leq 80^\circ$ and $0.5$ GeV $\leq \epsilon_1 \leq 3$ GeV, $\beta_c$ varies between $89^\circ$ and $110^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1993) by correcting the extraction of polarized structure functions using prescription I instead of prescription II, resolving inconsistencies identified by other authors (1994). The paper addresses doubts raised in that prior work about the feasibility of extracting neutron form factors from inclusive asymmetry measurements, and demonstrates that by choosing a critical polarization angle (around $105^\circ$ for typical kinematics), the proton contribution can be minimized, enabling access to both $G_E^n$ and $G_M^n$ via the response functions $R_{T'}$ and $R_{TL'}$.
