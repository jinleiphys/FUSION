# Study of system- size effects in multi- fragmentation using Quantum Molecular Dynamics model

> We report, for the first time, the dependence of the multiplicity of different fragments on the system size employing a quantum molecular dynamics model. This dependence is extracted from the simulations of symmetric collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Nb+Nb, Xe+Xe, Er+Er, Au+Au and U+U at incident energies between 50 A MeV and 1 A GeV. We find that the multiplicity of different fragments scales with the size of the system which can be parameterized by a simple power law.

# Study of system-size effects in multi-fragmentation using Quantum Molecular Dynamics model

## Key claim

The multiplicity of all fragment types (nucleons, light, medium, and intermediate mass fragments) scales with the total mass of the colliding system according to a power law $c \cdot A_{tot}^\tau$ across incident energies from 50 A MeV to 1 A GeV and all impact parameters, but the exponent $\tau$ depends on fragment size, incident energy, and impact parameter, this is a different scaling than the mass-yield power law for a single system.

## Method

The authors performed Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) simulations of symmetric collisions for seven systems (Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Nb+Nb, Xe+Xe, Er+Er, Au+Au, U+U) at incident energies between 50 A MeV and 1 A GeV and at reduced impact parameters $\hat{b} = 0, 0.3, 0.6$. They used a static soft equation of state with energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections, propagated nucleons for 300 fm/c, then froze their coordinates and applied the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) method with a binding distance of 4 fm to identify fragments.

## Key numbers

- Systems studied: $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni, $^{93}$Nb+$^{93}$Nb, $^{131}$Xe+$^{131}$Xe, $^{168}$Er+$^{168}$Er, $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au, $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U (masses from 40 to 238)
- Incident energy range: 50 A MeV to 1 A GeV
- The power-law exponent $\tau$ is close to 2/3 at 50 A MeV and approximately 1 at 400 A MeV for most fragment types
- Exception: $\tau = 2.6$ for intermediate mass fragments (IMF's) at 400 A MeV and $\hat{b} = 0$
- Fragment categories: LMF's ($2 \le A \le 4$), MMF's ($3 \le A \le 14$), IMF's ($5 \le A \le \min[A_{tot}/6, 65]$)
- Additional energies tested: 100, 200, 600, and 1000 A MeV

## Context

This work extends experimental findings from the FOPI collaboration (Reisberg, 1999), who analyzed system-size effects in Ni+Ni, Ru+Ru, Xe+CsI, and Au+Au reactions but focused on participant-region scaling. The present study includes both participant and spectator regions across a wider mass range, and contrasts with the ALADIN experiment (Schuettauf, 1996) which varied reaction asymmetry rather than system size. The results enable systematic parameterization of fragment production across system sizes and suggest that expansion following compression is faster in heavier systems, with implications for understanding the role of surface-to-volume ratio in multi-fragmentation.
