# Pair production of quarkonia and electroweak bosons from double-parton scatterings in nuclear collisions at the LHC

> Cross sections for the concurrent production of pairs of quarkonia (J/psi, Upsilon) and/or gauge bosons (W, Z) from double-parton scatterings (DPS) in high-energy proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC are calculated. The estimates are based on next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD predictions, including nuclear modifications of the parton densities, for the corresponding single-scattering cross sections. Expected event rates for J/psi+J/psi, J/psi+Upsilon, J/psi+W, J/psi+Z, Upsilon+Upsilon, Upsilon+W, Upsilon+Z, and same-sign W+W production in their (di)leptonic decay modes, after typical acceptance and efficiency losses, are given for pPb and PbPb collisions.

## Key claim

The cross sections for double-parton scattering (DPS) production of quarkonia and electroweak boson pairs in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC are large enough to be observable, but the quoted event rates assume no net in-medium suppression or enhancement of quarkonia yields in PbPb collisions, which could reduce the yields by a factor of about 2.

## Method

The DPS cross sections are computed using the "pocket formula" that multiplies the next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD single-parton scattering (SPS) cross sections for each particle and divides by an effective cross section sigma_eff that characterizes the transverse area of hard partonic interactions. For pPb collisions, the effective cross section includes contributions where the two partons from the nucleus belong either to the same nucleon or to two different nucleons, computed via the Glauber nuclear thickness function. For PbPb collisions, the effective cross section includes three contributions: partons from the same pair of nucleons, partons from one nucleon in one nucleus colliding with two different nucleons in the other nucleus, and partons from two different nucleons in both nuclei. Nuclear modifications of the parton densities (EPS09 nuclear PDFs) are included in the SPS cross sections.

## Key numbers

- In PbPb at 5.5 TeV, the DPS cross section for double-J/psi production is 210 mb, yielding about 250 visible events per unit rapidity in the four combinations of dielectron and dimuon channels for 1 nb^-1 integrated luminosity
- In PbPb at 5.5 TeV, about 20% of single-J/psi collisions are accompanied by a second J/psi from DPS; for the most central PbPb collisions, about 35% have a second J/psi
- In pPb at 8.8 TeV, the same-sign WW DPS cross section is about 150 pb, a factor of 1.5 times higher than the SPS background
- In pPb at 8.8 TeV, about 10 DPS same-sign WW events are expected in 2 pb^-1 integrated luminosity after acceptance and reconstruction cuts
- The effective cross section for pPb DPS is sigma_eff,pA = 22.6 microbarns, and for PbPb DPS is sigma_eff,AA = 1.5 nb
- The DPS cross sections in pPb are enhanced by a factor of sigma_eff,pp / sigma_eff,pA approximately 600 compared to pp
- The DPS cross sections in PbPb are boosted by a factor of sigma_eff,pp / sigma_eff,AA proportional to A^3.3/5 approximately 9 x 10^6 compared to pp

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of DPS in pA and AA collisions (d'Enterria 2012, d'Enterria 2013) by providing a comprehensive compilation of cross sections for eight different quarkonium and electroweak boson pair production channels. The results enable experimental searches for DPS in LHC heavy-ion runs and show that double-J/psi production in PbPb should not be blindly interpreted as evidence for c-cbar regeneration in the quark-gluon plasma (Andronic 2010), since DPS constitutes an important component of the total J/psi yield. The study of DPS in pPb can help determine the effective sigma_eff parameter characterizing the transverse parton distribution in the nucleon independently of pp measurements.
