# Predictions of $α$ decay half-lives for even-even superheavy nuclei with $104 \leqslant Z \leqslant 128$ based on two-potential approach within cluster-formation model

> In present work, we systematically study the $α$ decay half-lives of 170 even-even nuclei with $60 \leqslant Z \leqslant 118$ within the two-potential approach while the $α$ decay preformation factor $P_α$ is obtained by the cluster-formation model. The calculated results can well reproduce the experimental data. In addition, we extend this model to predict the $α$ decay half-lives of 64 even-even nuclei with $104 \leqslant Z \leqslant 128$ whose $α$ decay is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified. For comparing, the two famous models i.e. SemFIS proposed by D. Poenaru ${et\ al.}$ [\href {https://doi.org/10.1209/0295-5075/77/62001}{Europhys. Lett. \textbf{77} (2007) 62001}] and UDL proposed by C. Qi ${et\ al.}$ [\href {https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.103.072501}{Phys. Rev. Lett. \textbf{103} (2009) 072501}] are used. The predicted results of these models are basically consistent. At the same time, through analyzing the changing trend of $α$ decay energy $Q_α$ of \emph{Z} = 118, 120, 122, 124, 126 and 128 isotopes nuclei with the increasing of neutron number \emph{N} and that of $α$ decay preformation factor $P_α$ of those isotopes even-even nuclei with the increasing of neutron number \emph{N}, \emph{N} = 178 may be a new neutron magic number.

## Key claim

The two-potential approach combined with the cluster-formation model can reproduce experimental alpha decay half-lives for 170 even-even nuclei with 60 ≤ Z ≤ 118, and the model is extended to predict half-lives for 64 even-even superheavy nuclei with 104 ≤ Z ≤ 128 whose alpha decay is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified. The analysis of Q_alpha and preformation factor trends suggests that N = 178 may be a new neutron magic number, though this is presented as a tentative finding based on systematic trends rather than a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors used the two-potential approach (TPA) to calculate alpha decay half-lives, where the alpha decay width is computed from the penetration probability via the WKB approximation and the assault frequency from the normalized factor. The alpha decay preformation factor P_alpha was obtained from the cluster-formation model (CFM), which calculates the formation energy of the alpha cluster from nuclear binding energies taken from AME2016 or WS3+ for unsynthesized nuclei. The nuclear potential used a cosh parameterized form with isospin-dependent depth parameters (a = 0.5958 fm, V_0 = 192.42 + 31.059*(N_d - Z_d)/A_d MeV), and the Coulomb potential assumed a uniformly charged sphere with radius R = 1.28 A^(1/3) - 0.76 + 0.8 A^(-1/3) fm.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation between calculated and experimental log10(T_1/2) for the TPA+CFM model: sigma_1 = 0.453 (for 170 even-even nuclei with 60 ≤ Z ≤ 118)
- Standard deviation for UDL model: sigma_2 = 0.384
- Standard deviation for SemFIS model: sigma_3 = 0.326
- Standard deviation for TPA with P_alpha = 0.43: sigma_4 = 0.394
- Nuclear potential parameters: a = 0.5958 fm, V_0 = (192.42 + 31.059*(N_d - Z_d)/A_d) MeV
- Nuclear potential sharp radius formula: R = 1.28 A^(1/3) - 0.76 + 0.8 A^(-1/3) fm
- The analysis of Q_alpha and P_alpha trends for Z = 118, 120, 122, 124, 126, and 128 isotopes suggests N = 178 as a possible new neutron magic number

## Context

This work extends the two-potential approach combined with the cluster-formation model, previously applied to nuclei around the closed shells Z = 82, N = 126 by the same group, to the superheavy region. The predictions are compared with the SemFIS model of Poenaru et al. (2007) and the universal decay law (UDL) of Qi et al. (2009), showing consistent results across all three models. The study provides theoretical half-life predictions for 64 even-even superheavy nuclei with Z = 104-128 that are not yet experimentally quantified, which can guide future experimental searches for new elements including Z = 119 and 120.
