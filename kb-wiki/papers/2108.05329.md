# Final State Gluon Emission in Deep-Inelastic Scattering at Next-to-Leading Twist

> A detailed reanalysis of the single gluon emission rate at next-to-leading twist is carried out. As was the case in prior efforts, the problem is cast in the framework of deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) of an electron off a large nucleus. The quark produced in the interaction propagates through the remaining nucleus and engenders scattering and gluon radiation, which is calculated in the limit of one re-scattering. This medium induced single gluon emission rate forms the basis of several energy loss calculations in both DIS and heavy-ion collisions. Unlike prior efforts, a complete transverse momentum gradient expansion of the hadronic tensor, including $N_c$ suppressed terms, phase terms and finite gluon momentum fraction terms, ignored previously, is carried out. These terms turn out to be surprisingly large. In contrast to prior efforts, the full next-to-leading twist gluon emission kernel is found to be positive definite and slowly increasing with the exchanged transverse momentum. Phenomenological consequences of these new contributions are discussed.

## Key claim

The full next-to-leading twist gluon emission kernel, including all previously neglected \(N_c\)-suppressed terms, phase terms, and finite gluon momentum fraction terms, is positive definite and slowly increasing with exchanged transverse momentum, contrary to prior incomplete calculations that produced oscillatory or negative contributions at large path lengths.

## Method

The authors carried out a complete transverse momentum gradient expansion of the hadronic tensor for deep-inelastic scattering of an electron off a large nucleus, retaining all terms in the phase factors and amplitudes that were dropped in prior work by Guo and Wang (2000) and by Aurenche, Zakharov, and Zaraket (2008). They evaluated all 19 Feynman diagrams contributing to double scattering at next-to-leading twist, including central cut diagrams with post-emission scatterings, diagrams with pre-emission scatterings, and left/right cut diagrams, using light-cone gauge and the collinear expansion formalism.

## Key numbers

* The GW kernel (Guo and Wang, 2000) contains only the term \(2 - 2\cos(\mu^2 \xi^- / 2q^-)\); the AZZ kernel (Aurenche et al., 2008) adds two extra terms \( -(\mu^2 \xi^- / 2q^-) \sin(\mu^2 \xi^- / 2q^-) + (\mu^2 \xi^- / 2q^-)^2 \cos(\mu^2 \xi^- / 2q^-) \), which drive the kernel oscillatory at large \(\xi^-\).
* The full kernel is positive definite and slowly increasing with exchanged transverse momentum, in contrast to the AZZ kernel which becomes negative at large path length.
* When integrated up to twice the mean formation time, the full calculation yields results numerically similar to the original subset of contributions evaluated in Guo and Wang (2000).

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing dispute between the original calculation of Guo and Wang (2000) and the later calculation of Aurenche, Zakharov, and Zaraket (2008) over the treatment of phase factors in the collinear expansion. The complete calculation shows that both prior efforts were incomplete: GW neglected \(k_\perp\)-dependence in phase factors, while AZZ included phase terms only for the most dominant diagram but omitted contributions from other diagram topologies. The result enables improved modeling of medium-induced gluon emission in multi-stage jet event generators such as MATTER, LBT, and MARTINI within the JETSCAPE framework, and provides a foundation for studying jet modification beyond the soft gluon approximation.
