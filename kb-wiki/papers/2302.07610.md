# Quadrupole and octupole states in $^{152}$Sm using the proton-neutron interacting boson model

> A scheme of solving the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) in terms of the SU(3) basis is introduced, by which the IBM-2 coupled with an octupole boson is applied to describe the low-energy structure of the critical point nucleus, $^{152}$Sm. The results indicate that the spectral properties of both the positive-parity bands and negative-parity bands in this nucleus can be well captured by the IBM-2 calculations through a simple Hamiltonian, thus providing an example of the IBM-2 in a unified description of quadrupole and octupole states in a transitional system. In addition, a statistical analysis of the low-spin states in the model is also provided.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a diagonalization scheme using a weak-coupling SU(3) basis to solve the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian and its extension with an octupole f boson, and applies it to show that both positive-parity and negative-parity states in the critical point nucleus $^{152}$Sm can be described by a simple Hamiltonian, but only under the approximation that the f boson is fixed as a proton boson based on prior evidence that low-lying negative states in this nucleus are dominated by the proton octupole configuration.

## Method
The authors constructed an IBM-2 Hamiltonian with a consistent-Q form for the sd-boson core, plus a single-f-boson term and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions between the f boson and the sd bosons, solved in an SU(3) basis using the Draayer-Akiyama orthogonal basis. The Hamiltonian matrix elements were decomposed into products of reduced matrix elements of single-boson operators and SU(3) to SO(3) isoscalar factors, with the f boson fixed as a proton boson for $^{152}$Sm. The model was applied to $^{152}$Sm with boson numbers $N_\pi=6$ and $N_\nu=4$, and the parameters were adjusted to reproduce experimental level energies and transition rates.

## Key numbers
- Boson numbers for $^{152}$Sm: $N_\pi=6$, $N_\nu=4$ (from valence shells $N_p=50-82$ and $N_n=82-126$).
- Model parameters used: $\varepsilon_d=0.52$ MeV, $\kappa=-0.074$ MeV, $\varepsilon_f=2.25$ MeV, $t_{\pi\pi}=-0.043$ MeV, $t_{\pi\nu}=0.012$ MeV, $\chi_\pi=-1.2$, $\chi_\nu=-1.0$.
- Calculated band-head energy of the second $\beta$ band: $E(0_3^+)\approx 1.2$ MeV, compared to experimental value of about 1.1 MeV and IBM-1 result of $E(0_3^+)\approx 1.5$ MeV from Zhang (2017).
- $B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+)$: experimental 145.0(16) W.u., theoretical 145.0 W.u.
- $B(E3;3_1^-\rightarrow 0_1^+)$: experimental 13.9(15) W.u., theoretical 13.9 W.u.
- $B(E1;1_1^-\rightarrow 0_1^+)$: experimental $5.8(5)\times10^{-3}$ W.u., theoretical $5.8\times10^{-3}$ W.u.
- Model space size: IBM-2 with $N_\pi+N_\nu=10$ produces 133 $0^+$ states and 414 $2^+$ states; with one $f_\pi$ boson, the number of $2^-$ states reaches 1344, involving more than $1.8\times10^6$ matrix elements.

## Context
This work extends prior IBM-2 studies of $^{152}$Sm that were mostly confined to positive-parity states (Iachello, 1987; Nomura, 2011; Nomura, 2019) by including octupole degrees of freedom via an f boson. It builds on earlier IBM-1+f calculations for octupole states (Scholten, 1978; Barfield, 1986; Barfield, 1988) and on recent sdf-IBM-2 studies of neutron-rich nuclei (Nomura, 2022), but introduces a new SU(3)-basis diagonalization scheme as an alternative to the traditional NPBOS algorithm using the U(5) basis. The results enable unified IBM-2 descriptions of quadrupole and octupole states in transitional nuclei, and the statistical analysis of low-spin states provides a benchmark for future microscopic calculations of octupole correlations.
