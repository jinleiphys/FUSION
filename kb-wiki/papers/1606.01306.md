# Geometrical symmetries of nuclear systems: D(3h) and T(d) symmetries in light nuclei

> The role of discrete (or point-group) symmetries in alpha-cluster nuclei is discussed in the framework of the algebraic cluster model which describes the relative motion of the alpha-particles. Particular attention is paid to the discrete symmetry of the geometric arrangement of the alpha-particles, and the consequences for the structure of the corresponding rotational bands. The method is applied to study cluster states in the nuclei 12C and 16O. The observed level sequences can be understood in a simple way as a consequence of the underlying discrete symmetry that characterizes the geometrical configuration of the alpha-particles, i.e. an equilateral triangle with D(3h) symmetry for 12C, and a tetrahedron with T(d) symmetry for 16O. The structure of rotational bands provides a fingerprint of the underlying geometrical configuration of alpha-particles.

## Key claim

The observed level sequences in the alpha-cluster nuclei 12C and 16O can be understood as a consequence of the underlying discrete point-group symmetry of the geometric arrangement of alpha particles (equilateral triangle with D(3h) symmetry for 12C, tetrahedron with T(d) symmetry for 16O), but the paper emphasizes that these special solutions do not correspond to a dynamical symmetry of the Hamiltonian and instead approximate formulas are derived in a semi-classical mean-field analysis.

## Method

The algebraic cluster model (ACM) is applied to describe the relative motion of two-, three-, and four-body alpha-cluster systems using a U(3k-2) spectrum generating algebra with vector bosons (L^P=1^-) and scalar bosons (L^P=0^+). The Hamiltonian is constructed to be invariant under the permutation group S_k for identical clusters, and the eigenstates are classified according to irreducible representations of the permutation group. Classical limits are studied via coherent state mean-field approximations, and electromagnetic transition form factors and B(EL) values are calculated in the long-wavelength limit for point-like and finite-size charge distributions.

## Key numbers

- For the equilateral triangle configuration (D(3h) symmetry, 12C): B(E2) = (Ze)^2 * (5/4pi) * (1/4) * beta^4, B(E3) = (Ze)^2 * (7/4pi) * (5/8) * beta^6, B(E4) = (Ze)^2 * (9/4pi) * (9/64) * beta^8, B(E5) = (Ze)^2 * (11/4pi) * (35/128) * beta^10, B(E6) = (Ze)^2 * (13/4pi) * (281/512) * beta^12
- For the tetrahedron configuration (T(d) symmetry, 16O): B(E3) = (Ze)^2 * (7/4pi) * (5/9) * beta^6, B(E4) = (Ze)^2 * (9/4pi) * (7/27) * beta^8, B(E6) = (Ze)^2 * (13/4pi) * (32/81) * beta^12; B(E1), B(E2), and B(E5) vanish
- The charge radius for finite-size alpha particles with Gaussian form factor is given by <r^2>^(1/2) = sqrt(3/(2*alpha) + beta^2)
- In the SO(3k-2) limit, the elastic form factor in the large N limit reduces to a spherical Bessel function for k even and a cylindrical Bessel function for k odd

## Context

This work extends the algebraic cluster model (ACM) previously applied to 12C (Bijker, 2012) and 16O (Bijker, 2014) by providing a systematic treatment of discrete point-group symmetries (D(3h) and T(d)) and their connection to permutation symmetry S_k. The paper builds on earlier alpha-cluster studies by Wheeler (1930s), Hafstad and Teller (1930s), Brink, and Robson, as well as more recent experimental work on rotational excitations in 12C by Freer and collaborators (2007, 2011) and Itoh. The results enable future studies of shape-phase transitions between the U(3k-3) harmonic oscillator and SO(3k-2) deformed oscillator limits, and provide a framework for calculating electromagnetic form factors and transition rates in alpha-cluster nuclei.
