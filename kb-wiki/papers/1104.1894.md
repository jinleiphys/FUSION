# The CDF dijet excess from intrinsic quarks

> The CDF collaboration reported an excess in the production of two jets in association with a $W$. We discuss constraints on possible new particle state interpretations of this excess. The fact of no statistically significant deviation from the SM expectation for {$Z$+dijet} events in CDF data disfavors the new particle explanation. We show that the nucleon intrinsic strange quarks provide an important contribution to the $W$ boson production in association with a single top quark production. Such {$W$+t} single top quark production can contribute to the CDF {$W$+dijet} excess, thus the nucleon intrinsic quarks can provide a possible explanation to the CDF excess in {$W$+dijet} but not in {$Z$+dijet} events.

## Key claim

The CDF dijet excess in W+dijet events can be explained by intrinsic strange quarks in the nucleon contributing to W+t single top quark production, rather than by new particle resonances; this explanation naturally accommodates the absence of a similar excess in Z+dijet events, which disfavors new particle interpretations.

## Method

The authors analyzed constraints on new particle interpretations (a Higgs boson or Z' gauge boson with mass around 150 GeV/c^2) for the CDF W+dijet excess, noting that such models generically predict similar excesses in Z+dijet events which are not observed. They then estimated the contribution of intrinsic strange quarks from the nucleon sea to W+t single top quark production at the Tevatron, using the baryon-meson fluctuation model and chiral quark model to compute relative probabilities of finding intrinsic s versus b quarks, combined with CKM mixing matrix elements, to derive the expected production rate.

## Key numbers

- The CDF W+dijet excess has a two-jet invariant mass in the range 120-160 GeV/c^2, with an associated W production cross section of about 4 pb.
- The SM WH production cross section times branching ratio for H to b bbar is only about 12 fb, insufficient to explain the excess.
- The predicted rate for W+t single top quark production at Tevatron (1.96 TeV/c^2) is 0.28 ± 0.06 pb for a top quark mass of 172.5 GeV/c^2.
- The ratio of intrinsic s to b quark probability is approximately (4.67 GeV / 101 MeV)^2 ≈ 2 × 10^3.
- The CKM suppression factor for s versus b contributions is (V_ts/V_tb)^2 ≈ (0.0407/0.999133)^2 ≈ 1.7 × 10^{-3}.
- The estimated rate for W+t production from intrinsic s quarks is 1 pb, with possible enhancement by a factor of 2 to 3 (giving 2 to 3 pb) due to s(x)/sbar(x) asymmetry at x ≥ 0.1.
- The d quark contribution to W+t production is estimated at 0.16 pb, with CKM suppression (V_td/V_tb)^2 ≈ (0.00874/0.999133)^2 ≈ 7.7 × 10^{-5}.

## Context

This work extends the baryon-meson fluctuation model (Brooks and Ma, 1996) and chiral quark model (Manohar and Georgi, 1984) previously used to explain the NuTeV anomaly (Zeller et al., 2002) via strange-antistrange asymmetry, and the Gottfried sum rule violation (NMC collaboration, 1991). It provides an alternative to new particle resonance models (such as those by Kingman et al., 2011) for the CDF dijet excess, and predicts a (W^-+t)/(W^++antit) asymmetry that can be tested with Tevatron data to probe the strange-antistrange asymmetry of the nucleon sea.
