# Relativistic Symmetry Suppresses Quark Spin-Orbit Splitting

> Experimental data indicate small spin-orbit splittings in hadrons. For heavy-light mesons we identify a relativistic symmetry that suppresses these splittings. We suggest an experimental test in electron-positron annihilation. Furthermore, we argue that the dynamics necessary for this symmetry are possible in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian that suppresses spin-orbit splittings in heavy-light mesons when the vector and scalar potentials experienced by the light quark differ only by a constant, \(V_V \approx V_S + U\); the authors argue this condition may arise naturally in non-perturbative QCD, rather than being an accidental cancellation between one-gluon-exchange and Thomas precession as conjectured by Isgur.

## Method

The authors analyze the Dirac Hamiltonian for a light quark moving in fixed vector and scalar potentials provided by a heavy antiquark, and show that when \(V_V = V_S + U\) the Hamiltonian commutes with a spin symmetry generator, producing degenerate spin doublets (e.g., \(p_{1/2}\) and \(p_{3/2}\) states). They compare this symmetry to the pseudospin symmetry previously identified in nuclear physics by Ginocchio (1997), and examine experimental meson mass data from CLEO, L3, OPAL, and lattice QCD to quantify the smallness of observed spin-orbit splittings. They also propose an experimental test via electron-positron annihilation to measure form factors of \(D\)-meson states.

## Key numbers

- For \(D\)-mesons, the \(p_{3/2}\)-\(p_{1/2}\) spin-orbit splitting is \(-2 \pm 50\) MeV, extracted from the CLEO broad \(J^P=1^+\) state at \(2461^{+41}_{-34}\pm 10 \pm 32\) MeV and the \(D_2^*\) at \(2459 \pm 2\) MeV.
- For \(K\)-mesons, the \(p_{3/2}\)-\(p_{1/2}\) splitting is \(-38 \pm 6\) MeV, with the \(p_{1/2}\) level at \(1409 \pm 5\) MeV and the \(p_{3/2}\) at \(1371 \pm 3\) MeV.
- For \(B\)-mesons, L3 reports a \(p_{3/2}\)-\(p_{1/2}\) splitting of \(97 \pm 11\) MeV, while OPAL reports \(-109 \pm 14\) MeV; lattice QCD estimates include \(155^{+9}_{-13} \pm 32\) MeV (Gupta, 1999) and \(31 \pm 18\) MeV (Lewis, 2000).
- The dimensionless ratio \(r = (p_{3/2}-p_{1/2}) / ((4p_{3/2}+2p_{1/2})/6 - s_{1/2})\) is \(0.00 \pm 0.10\) for \(D\)-mesons, \(-0.06 \pm 0.00\) for \(K\)-mesons, and \(0.23 \pm 0.04\) (L3) or \(-0.23 \pm 0.03\) (OPAL) for \(B\)-mesons; for Dirac Coulomb potentials the range is \(-0.7 \lesssim r \lesssim 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends the pseudospin symmetry identified in nuclear physics by Ginocchio (1997) to the quark sector, providing a dynamical explanation for the small spin-orbit splittings in hadrons that Isgur (1999) attributed to accidental cancellation. The symmetry predicts that the upper-component radial wavefunctions of spin-doublet states are identical, enabling a testable prediction for form factors in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation at the Beijing Electron Positron Collider or B-factories. The authors argue that the required potential relation \(V_V \approx V_S + U\) may arise from non-perturbative QCD via two-gluon exchange and scalar glueball exchange, building on arguments by Goldman (1999).
