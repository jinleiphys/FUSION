# Nuclear Shadowing and High-$p_T$ Hadron Spectra in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We explore how nuclear modifications to the nucleon parton distributions affect production of high-$p_T$ hadrons in heavy ion collisions. We perform a leading order calculation of the high-$p_T$ charged pion, kaon and proton spectra using standard fragmentation functions and shadowing parameterizations. We also consider alternate models of shadowing. Near midrapidity, shadowing is a small effect and cannot explain the large observed suppression of high-$p_T$ hadrons. We also consider the isospin difference between protons and nuclei and find that it is also a small effect on high-$p_T$ hadron production.

# Nuclear Shadowing and High-$p_T$ Hadron Spectra in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Nuclear shadowing and isospin effects are too small to explain the large suppression of high-$p_T$ hadrons observed at RHIC near midrapidity, with the calculated nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ remaining between 1.0 and 1.1 for charged mesons and approximately 1.0 for protons when restricted to $|y| \leq 1$, far above the measured values of $R_{AA} \approx 0.4$ for charged hadrons and $R_{AA} \approx 0.3$ for $\pi^0$.

## Method

The authors performed a leading order (LO) calculation of minijet production to compute high-$p_T$ charged pion, kaon, and proton spectra in gold-gold collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, using MRST LO proton parton distribution functions, the EKS98 parameterization of nuclear shadowing, and LO fragmentation functions fit to $e^+ e^-$ data. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ was calculated both integrated over all rapidities and restricted to $|y| \leq 1$ to match experimental acceptances, with the Cronin effect intentionally omitted to isolate the contributions of shadowing and isospin.

## Key numbers

- For charged hadrons at RHIC, $R_{AA}(p_T) \approx 0.4$ for $p_T > 2$ GeV/$c$ (PHENIX, STAR); for $\pi^0$, $R_{AA}(p_T) \approx 0.3$ (PHENIX); for protons, $R_{AA}(p_T) \approx 1.0$ (PHENIX)
- With EKS98 shadowing and isospin at midrapidity ($|y| \leq 1$), the calculated $R_{AA}$ is between 1.0 and 1.1 for charged mesons and approximately 1.0 for protons
- Without the $|y| \leq 1$ restriction, shadowing reduces $R_{AA}$ to approximately 0.7 at $p_T = 2$ GeV/$c$, rising to 0.8 at $p_T = 10$ GeV/$c$
- The crossover between gluon and quark dominance in hadron production occurs at $p_T \sim 3.5$ GeV/$c$ for kaons and $\sim 5$ GeV/$c$ for protons, compared to $\sim 10$ GeV/$c$ for pions
- At large $p_T$, approximately 75% of kaons and protons are produced by quarks
- The isospin effect on kaons and protons becomes larger than nuclear modifications for $p_T > 7.5$ GeV/$c$

## Context

This work provides quantitative estimates of nuclear shadowing and isospin effects on high-$p_T$ hadron production at RHIC, extending earlier analyses by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations (2002) that had qualitatively noted these effects would be small. The calculations use the EKS98 parameterization of Eskola, Kolhinen, and Ruuskanen (1998) and the fragmentation functions of Kniehl, Kramer, and Potter (2000). The authors conclude that parton energy loss in the medium, as studied by Vitev, Gyulassy, and Levai (2002) and Jeon, Jalilian-Marian, and Sarcevic (2002), remains the most likely explanation for the observed suppression. The results also establish a baseline for future LHC measurements at $\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV, where shadowing effects are predicted to be significantly larger due to the smaller $x$ values probed.
