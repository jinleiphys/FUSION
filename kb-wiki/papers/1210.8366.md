# Upsilon suppression in PbPb collisions at the LHC

> We suggest that the combined effect of screening, gluon-induced dissociation, collisional damping, and reduced feed-down explains most of the sequential suppression of Upsilon(nS) states that has been observed in PbPb relative to pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The suppression is thus a clear, albeit indirect, indication for the presence of a QGP. The Upsilon(1S) ground state suppression is essentially due to reduced feed-down, collisional damping and gluodissociation, whereas screening prevails for the suppression of the excited states.

## Key claim

The combined effect of screening, gluon-induced dissociation, collisional damping, and reduced feed-down explains most of the sequential suppression of Upsilon(nS) states observed in PbPb relative to pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, with the Upsilon(1S) ground state suppression due primarily to reduced feed-down, collisional damping, and gluodissociation, while screening prevails for excited states.

## Method

The authors developed a three-step model: (1) solving the Schrodinger equation with a complex, screened potential to obtain bottomium wave functions and decay widths for Debye screening, collisional damping, and gluodissociation for the Upsilon(1S,2S,3S) and chi_b(1P,2P) states; (2) calculating the suppression of these five states within an expanding and cooling fireball using Bjorken scaling for the time evolution; (3) computing the feed-down cascade and the ensuing fraction of dimuon decays, using initial populations deduced from CMS pp data at 2.76 TeV and CDF p-pbar data at 1.8 TeV.

## Key numbers

* Upsilon(1S) ground state dissociation temperature T_diss = 668 MeV, Upsilon(2S) T_diss = 217 MeV, chi_b(1P) T_diss = 206 MeV, with the higher excited states already dissolved for T greater than or approximately equal to T_c
* Calculated minimum bias R_AA(1S) = 0.46 for QGP lifetime t_QGP = 6 fm/c, compared to CMS data value 0.56 +/- 0.08 +/- 0.07
* Calculated minimum bias R_AA(2S) = 0.28 for t_QGP = 6 fm/c, compared to CMS data value 0.12 +/- 0.04 +/- 0.02
* Calculated minimum bias R_AA(3S) = 0.24 for t_QGP = 6 fm/c, compared to CMS data value 0.03 +/- 0.04 +/- 0.01
* Calculated double ratio (2S/1S)_PbPb/(2S/1S)_pp = 0.61 for t_QGP = 6 fm/c, compared to CMS data value 0.21 +/- 0.07 +/- 0.02
* Calculated double ratio (3S/1S)_PbPb/(3S/1S)_pp = 0.52 for t_QGP = 6 fm/c, compared to CMS data value 0.06 +/- 0.06 +/- 0.06
* Upsilon(1S) suppression factor R_AA(1S) varies with formation time: 0.74 for t_F = 1 fm/c, 0.63 for t_F = 0.5 fm/c, and 0.45 for t_F = 0.1 fm/c (minimum bias, t_QGP = 6 fm/c)

## Context

This work extends the study of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, which began with charmonium studies since 1986 (Patra and Srivastava, 2001; Kluberg and Satz, 2009; Kharzeev, 2007) and builds on the authors' prior work on gluodissociation (Brezinski and Wolschin, 2012). The model provides a description of Upsilon(1S) ground state suppression that agrees well with CMS data from the 2011 run (CMS Collaboration, 2012), but the calculated suppression for excited Upsilon(2S) and Upsilon(3S) states is substantially too large compared to experiment, particularly in peripheral collisions. The results suggest that additional suppression mechanisms not considered here may play a role, and that forthcoming pPb measurements at the LHC could help constrain cold nuclear matter effects.
