# Tsallis-thermometer: a QGP indicator for large and small collisional systems

> The transverse momentum distribution of identified hadrons from recent years are analyzed within the thermodynamically consistent formulation of non-extensive statistics. A wide range of center-of-mass energies and average event multiplicities are studied for various hadron species. We demonstrate that the average event multiplicity is a key variable in the study of high-energy collisions and a smooth transition in the description from small to large systems is possible. For this purpose the non-extensive statistical approach is more than appropriate. The validity of the non-extensive description is explored in multiple ways: such as the calculation of integrated yields per unit rapidity, the analysis of radial flow and the consistency check of the thermodynamical variables. The 'Tsallis-thermometer' is introduced as an indicator of quark-gluon plasma in small collisional systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the non-extensive (Tsallis) statistical framework can smoothly describe hadron transverse momentum spectra from small (pp, pA) to large (AA) collision systems, and that the "Tsallis-thermometer" (the correlation between the temperature parameter T and the non-extensivity parameter q) serves as an indicator of quark-gluon plasma formation in small collisional systems, contingent on the average event multiplicity being the key variable.

## Method

The authors performed thermodynamically consistent Tsallis-Pareto fits (using the function d²N/(2π p_T dp_T dy) = A m_T [1 + (q-1)/T (m_T - m)]^(-q/(q-1))) to publicly available mid-rapidity transverse momentum spectra of identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons) from pp, pA, and AA collisions measured by ALICE, CMS, BRAHMS, STAR, and PHENIX collaborations. They extracted the T and q parameters across a range of center-of-mass energies (62.4 GeV to 7 TeV) and event multiplicities, then analyzed the correlations between T and q as a function of multiplicity, hadron mass, and collision energy, fitting the linear relation T = E(δ² - (q-1)) derived from multiplicity fluctuation assumptions.

## Key numbers

* The fitted q₁ and q₂ parameters for charged pions in minimum bias pp collisions (√s ∈ [62.7; 7000] GeV) are q₁ = 1.001 ± 0.005 and q₂ = 0.016 ± 0.004; for kaons q₁ = 1.008 ± 0.024 and q₂ = 0.019 ± 0.005; for protons q₁ = 1.001 ± 0.044 and q₂ = 0.018 ± 0.007.
* The fitted T₁ and T₂ parameters for charged pions are T₁ = 0.038 ± 0.017 GeV and T₂ = 0.005 ± 0.002 GeV; for kaons T₁ = 0.013 ± 0.016 GeV and T₂ = 0.017 ± 0.002 GeV; for protons T₁ = 0.016 ± 0.011 GeV and T₂ = 0.023 ± 0.002 GeV.
* The δ² parameters extracted from √s = 7 TeV pp data are 0.39 ± 0.03 for pions, 0.43 ± 0.05 for kaons, and 0.42 ± 0.08 for protons, using average p_T values of 0.466 ± 0.010 GeV, 0.773 ± 0.016 GeV, and 0.900 ± 0.032 GeV respectively.
* The χ²/ndf values for all fits range from 0.02 to 14.7, with the worst value (14.7) for the most central neutral kaons from PbPb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.
* By going from small to large multiplicity, the change in T can be as large as O(100%) and in q as O(10%), with (q-1) changing by up to O(500%) depending on hadron species.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Biro et al., 2017; Biro et al., 2016) on the energy and mass dependence of Tsallis parameters in minimum bias pp collisions, now incorporating multiplicity-selected data from pp, pA, and AA systems. It builds on the observation of collective effects and strangeness enhancement in small systems (ALICE, 2017; Khachatryan, 2010) and the successful application of Tsallis-Pareto distributions to high-energy nuclear collisions (Cleymans, 2011; Wilk, 2015). The results enable a unified thermodynamic description across system sizes and suggest that the Tsallis-thermometer can be used to identify QGP-like signatures in small collisional systems, motivating further studies of the oscillation effect observed in central heavy-ion collisions.
