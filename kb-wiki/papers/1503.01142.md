# Charge Symmetry Violation in the Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Proton

> Experimental tests of QCD through its predictions for the strange-quark content of the proton have been drastically restricted by our lack of knowledge of the violation of charge symmetry (CSV). We find unexpectedly tiny CSV in the proton's electromagnetic form factors by performing the first extraction of these quantities based on an analysis of lattice QCD data. The resulting values are an order of magnitude smaller than current bounds on proton strangeness from parity violating electron-proton scattering experiments. This result paves the way for a new generation of experimental measurements of the proton's strange form factors to challenge the predictions of QCD.

## Key claim

Charge symmetry violation (CSV) in the proton's electromagnetic form factors is found to be unexpectedly tiny, at the level of 0.2% of the relevant proton form factors for momentum transfers up to approximately 1.4 GeV², which is an order of magnitude smaller than the precision of existing parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) experiments. This result is based on the first extraction of these quantities from lattice QCD data, but the analysis omits certain disconnected quark-line contributions that are estimated using a model with a conservative uncertainty.

## Method

The authors performed the first extraction of CSV in the proton's electromagnetic form factors by analyzing 2+1-flavor lattice QCD simulations at two different lattice spacings (a² = 0.0055(3) fm² and 0.0038(2) fm²) and pion masses down to 220 MeV, with momentum transfers Q² up to 1.4 GeV². They extrapolated the lattice results to infinite volume and physical pseudoscalar masses using connected chiral perturbation theory, extending previous work to incorporate unequal light quark masses (m_u ≠ m_d) by fixing low-energy parameters from fits to isospin-averaged baryon octet form factors. The omitted disconnected quark-line contributions were modeled using chiral loop expressions, with unknown low-energy constants set to zero and an uncertainty taken as twice the magnitude of the corresponding meson loop contributions evaluated with a dipole cutoff regulator at Λ = 0.8(2) GeV.

## Key numbers

- CSV form factors are at the level of 0.2% of the relevant proton form factors for momentum transfers up to approximately 1.4 GeV²
- The CSV difference in charge radii is equivalent to less than one attometer
- Lattice simulations used two sets: set I with a² = 0.0055(3) fm² and volume 32³ × 64, set II with a² = 0.0038(2) fm² and volume 48³ × 96
- Pion masses down to 220 MeV were included in the analysis
- Momentum transfer Q² values up to 1.4 GeV² were studied
- The light-quark mass ratio R = m_u/m_d was taken as 0.553(43) from meson decay rates

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of baryon octet electromagnetic form factors by Shanahan et al. (2014) and earlier CSV calculations of mass splittings, sigma terms, and parton distribution moments by the same group. The results contradict the wide range of theoretical predictions for CSV form factors that varied through several orders of magnitude (Wagman 2014, Kubis 2006, Miller 2006), which had halted experimental PVES programs at Mainz (Maas 2004, Baunack 2009) and JLab (Aniol 2005, Acha 2006). The finding that CSV is an order of magnitude smaller than current bounds on proton strangeness from PVES experiments enables a new generation of experimental measurements of the proton's strange form factors to challenge QCD predictions.
