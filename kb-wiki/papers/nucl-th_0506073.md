# Suppressed fusion cross section for neutron halo nuclei

> Fusion reactions of neutron-halo nuclei are investigated theoretically with a three-body model. The time-dependent wave-packet method is used to solve the three-body Schrodinger equation. The halo neutron behaves as a spectator during the Coulomb dissociation process of the projectile. The fusion cross sections of 11Be-209Bi and 6He-238U are calculated and are compared with measurements. Our calculation indicates that the fusion cross section is slightly hindered by the presence of weakly bound neutrons.

## Key claim

The fusion cross section for neutron-halo nuclei is slightly suppressed, not enhanced, by the presence of weakly bound neutrons; this conclusion contradicts earlier theoretical predictions of substantial sub-barrier enhancement and is consistent with recent experimental data showing no evidence for enhancement.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation using a time-dependent wave-packet method for the reactions ¹¹Be-²⁰⁹Bi and ⁶He-²³⁸U, treating the projectile as a core plus halo neutron(s) system. The calculation included a full range of impact parameters (total angular momentum J from 0 to 30) and used a body-fixed frame partial wave expansion with a discrete-variable representation, with the fusion cross section defined by flux loss due to an imaginary potential in the core-target interaction.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹Be-²⁰⁹Bi: the n-C potential produced a 2s bound orbital at -0.6 MeV, close to the experimental one-neutron separation energy
- For ⁶He-²³⁸U: the n-C potential produced a bound orbital at -0.97 MeV, close to the experimental two-neutron separation energy
- The model space used radial coordinates 0<R<50 fm and 0<r<60 fm with discretization steps (ΔR, Δr) = (0.2, 1.0) fm for ¹¹Be and (0.3, 0.6) fm for ⁶He
- Maximum n-C relative angular momentum l_max = 70 was required for converged results; l_max ≥ 30 was necessary to avoid spurious enhancement
- The no-Coriolis approximation (Ω_max = 0) was adopted for cross section calculations, with differences from Ω_max = 5 visible only for energies above 42 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous time-dependent wave-packet calculations (Yabana 2003, 2004; Nakatsukasa 2004) from head-on collisions (J=0) to full cross sections, and directly contradicts earlier coupled-channel calculations by Hagino (2000) and Diaz (2002) that predicted substantial sub-barrier fusion enhancement. The authors show that those earlier results were an artifact of insufficient l_max truncation (typically l_max=4). The calculations enable comparison with recent experimental data from Raabe (2004) and Signorini (2004), which show no evidence for fusion enhancement, and suggest that forward neutron detection in incomplete fusion could test the spectator picture.
