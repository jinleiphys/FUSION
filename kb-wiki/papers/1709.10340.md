# Superfluidity and Superconductivity in Neutron Stars

> This review focuses on applications of the ideas of superfluidity and superconductivity in neutron stars in a broader context, ranging from the microphysics of pairing in nucleonic superfluids to macroscopic manifestations of superfluidity in pulsars. The exposition of the basics of pairing, vorticity and mutual friction can serve as an introduction to the subject. We also review some topics of recent interest, including the various types of pinning of vortices, glitches, and oscillations in neutron stars containing superfluid phases of baryonic matter.

## Key claim

This review argues that superfluidity and superconductivity in neutron stars, ranging from microphysical pairing in nucleonic matter to macroscopic pulsar phenomena like glitches, can be understood within the BCS theory framework, but the quantitative predictions for pairing gaps remain uncertain because medium polarization (screening) corrections and the choice of effective interaction can reduce the gap by up to a factor of three compared to bare-interaction calculations.

## Method

The paper reviews the microscopic BCS theory of pairing in nucleonic matter, deriving the gap equation and its analytical solutions for contact interactions in the weak-coupling limit. It then examines how the effective pairing interaction is modified by screening effects using Landau Fermi liquid theory, where particle-hole diagrams are summed to renormalize the Landau parameters, and compares results from different theoretical approaches including Bruckner-Hartree-Fock, correlated basis functions, and auxiliary field Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

- Typical neutron star masses are approximately 1.4-2.0 solar masses with radii of 10-14 km, giving central densities exceeding nuclear saturation density n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3} by factors of a few up to ten
- Interior temperatures of mature neutron stars are T ~ 10^8 K (~0.01 MeV), while Fermi energies of fermions are epsilon_F ~ 10-100 MeV, and critical temperatures for superfluidity are T_c ~ 10^9 K
- The neutron pairing gap in the ^1S_0 channel vanishes at densities slightly above nuclear saturation density n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3}, where neutron Fermi energies reach epsilon_Fn ~ 60 MeV
- The BCS gap equation in the weak-coupling limit gives Delta(0) = 2 Lambda exp(-2/(G nu)) and the relation Delta(0) = 1.76 T_c between zero-temperature gap and critical temperature
- The deuteron binding energy is E_d = -2.2 MeV, and the ^1S_0 nn scattering phase shift becomes negative (repulsive) at laboratory energies above E_L = 250 MeV

## Context

This review extends the BCS theory of superconductivity (Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer, 1957) and the Cooper theorem (Cooper, 1956) to neutron star interiors, following the early conjectures of nuclear superfluidity by Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines (1958) and Migdal (1959). It synthesizes results from multiple theoretical approaches including Fermi liquid theory (Wambach, Ainsworth, and Pines, 1993; Sedrakian, 2003), correlated basis functions (Chen, Clark, and others, 1993), and auxiliary field Monte Carlo simulations (Gezerlis and Carlson, 2008), and provides the foundation for understanding pulsar glitches and neutron star oscillations through the dynamics of superfluid vortices and mutual friction.
