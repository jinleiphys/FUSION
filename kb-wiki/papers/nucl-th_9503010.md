# Nonperturbative treatment of the N-body system with dynamical mesons

> The variational approach, used by Feynman in the study of the polaron problem, is generalized to treat a system of N non-relativistic particles interacting with scalar and vector mesons. After integrating out the meson fields in the path integral formulation we perform a variational calculation for the effective N-body two-time action to obtain the energy and the mass of the system. The interplay of self energies and exchange terms in attaining binding and saturation is examined. We estimate the size of the particles in the medium and give the mean number of mesons as a function of N.

## Key claim
The paper generalizes Feynman’s variational path-integral method for the polaron to a system of N non-relativistic particles interacting via scalar and vector meson fields, and shows that the interplay between self-energy and exchange terms can produce binding and saturation, but the results depend on the variational ansatz and the specific form of the two-time effective action.

## Method
The meson fields are integrated out in a path-integral formulation, leaving an effective N-body two-time action for the particles. A variational calculation is then performed on this effective action, using a trial action inspired by Feynman’s polaron approach, to obtain the ground-state energy and the effective mass of the system. The size of the particles in the medium and the mean number of mesons per particle are extracted as functions of N.

## Context
This work extends the variational path-integral technique pioneered by Feynman for the polaron problem to a many-body nuclear-physics context with dynamical meson exchange. It examines how self-energy and exchange contributions compete to produce binding and saturation, a question central to understanding nuclear forces. The approach enables future studies of finite-N systems where nonperturbative meson effects are important, complementing earlier mean-field or perturbative treatments.
