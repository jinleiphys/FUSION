# Study of the $\vec{p}d \to n\{pp\}_{s}$ charge-exchange reaction using a polarised deuterium target

> The vector and tensor analysing powers, $A_y$ and $A_{yy}$, of the $\vec{p}d \to n\{pp\}_{s}$ charge-exchange reaction have been measured at a beam energy of 600 MeV at the COSY-ANKE facility by using an unpolarised proton beam incident on an internal storage cell target filled with polarised deuterium gas. The low energy recoiling protons were measured in a pair of silicon tracking telescopes placed on either side of the target. Putting a cut of 3 MeV on the diproton excitation energy ensured that the two protons were dominantly in the $^{1}S_{0}$ state, here denoted by $\{pp\}_{s}$. The polarisation of the deuterium gas was established through measurements in parallel of proton-deuteron elastic scattering. By analysing events where both protons entered the same telescope, the charge-exchange reaction was measured for momentum transfers $q\geq 160$ MeV/$c$. These data provide a good continuation of the earlier results at $q\leq 140$ MeV/$c$ obtained with a polarised deuteron beam. They are also consistent with impulse approximation predictions with little sign evident for any modifications due to multiple scatterings.

## Key claim

The vector and tensor analyzing powers of the \(\vec{p}d \to n\{pp\}_{s}\) charge-exchange reaction at 600 MeV are consistent with impulse approximation predictions, with little sign of modifications due to multiple scatterings, though double-scattering effects may shift the zero-crossing of \(A_{yy}\) by up to 20 MeV/\(c\) as an upper limit.

## Method

An unpolarized 600 MeV proton beam from the COSY-ANKE facility was incident on a polarized deuterium gas target in a storage cell. Low-energy recoiling protons from the charge-exchange reaction were detected in a pair of silicon tracking telescopes (STT) placed on either side of the target, and events where both protons entered the same STT were selected to access momentum transfers \(q \geq 160\) MeV/\(c\) with a cut of 3 MeV on the diproton excitation energy \(E_{pp}\) to isolate the \({}^{1}S_{0}\) state. The target polarization was determined in parallel by measuring proton-deuteron elastic scattering and using known analyzing powers from Argonne (1987), SATURNE (1984, 1988), and ANKE (2006) at nearby energies.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 600 MeV (proton kinetic energy).
- Cut on diproton excitation energy: \(E_{pp} < 3\) MeV to ensure dominance of the \({}^{1}S_{0}\) state.
- Momentum transfer range for new data: \(q \geq 160\) MeV/\(c\); earlier data covered \(q \leq 140\) MeV/\(c\).
- Average vector analyzing power over all momentum transfers: \(\langle A_y^d \rangle = 0.005 \pm 0.008\).
- \(E_{pp}\) resolution: around 0.3 MeV for \(E_{pp} < 1\) MeV and below 1 MeV for higher \(E_{pp}\).
- Average luminosity: \(L \approx 5 - 7 \times 10^{28}\) cm\(^{-2}\) s\(^{-1}\) over ten days of data taking.
- Target polarization values: for modes (1,2) \(\Delta Q_y = 1.46 \pm 0.01\) (stat) \(\pm 0.03\) (sys); for modes (3,4) \(\Delta Q_{yy} = -1.68 \pm 0.02\) (stat) \(\pm 0.14\) (sys).

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of the \(\vec{d}p \to n\{pp\}_{s}\) reaction by the ANKE collaboration at deuteron energies of 1.2, 1.6, 1.8, and 2.27 GeV (Chiladze 2006, Mchedlishvili 2013) to higher momentum transfers using inverse kinematics with a polarized deuterium target. The new data at \(q \geq 160\) MeV/\(c\) smoothly join the lower-\(q\) results and are consistent with impulse approximation predictions based on the SAID SP07 partial wave solution (Arndt 2007), enabling future studies at higher energies and measurements of spin-correlation coefficients with polarized proton beams.
