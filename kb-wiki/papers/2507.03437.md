# $M1$ Radiative Transition of Light Mesons in the Light-Front Quark Model

> We investigate the $M1$ radiative transitions between light vector $\mathcal{V}$ and pseudoscalar $\mathcal{P}$ mesons in the 1S and 2S states in the light-front quark model. To this end, we use the light-front wave functions (LFWFs) obtained from the QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian that includes smeared spin-spin interactions, where a few harmonic oscillator basis functions were employed as trial wave functions. The results, including their couplings and widths, obtained from LFWFs with different trial wave functions are compared with the available experimental data and other theoretical predictions.

# M1 Radiative Transition of Light Mesons in the Light-Front Quark Model

## Key claim

The light-front quark model with harmonic oscillator basis functions and a mixing angle parameter can reproduce M1 radiative transition couplings and widths for light vector-to-pseudoscalar meson decays, but the predicted values are systematically smaller than experimental data and other theoretical models, particularly for ground-state transitions of rho and kaon mesons.

## Method

The authors solve the QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation for quark-antiquark bound states, using light-front wave functions constructed from harmonic oscillator basis functions with two scenarios: pure 1S and 2S harmonic oscillator bases, and a linear combination of these bases with a mixing angle θ = 0° or 10°. The M1 transition form factors are computed from the convolution of initial and final light-front wave functions using plus and transverse current components, and the coupling constants are extracted at Q² = 0 to compute partial decay widths.

## Key numbers

- ρ±(1S) → π±(1S)γ coupling constant: 0.540 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 0°) and 0.522 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 10°)
- ρ±(1S) → π±(1S)γ decay width: 36.85 keV (θ = 0°) and 34.44 keV (θ = 10°), compared to PDG value of 69.28 keV
- K*(1S) → K±(1S)γ coupling constant: 0.841 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 0°) and 0.823 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 10°)
- K*(1S) → K±(1S)γ decay width: 49.8 keV (θ = 0°) and 47.7 keV (θ = 10°), compared to PDG value of 116 keV for the charged channel
- K*(1S) → K⁰(1S)γ coupling constant: −1.198 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 0°) and −1.177 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 10°)
- K*(1S) → K⁰(1S)γ decay width: 101 keV (θ = 0°) and 97.4 keV (θ = 10°), compared to PDG value of 50.37 keV
- ρ±(2S) → π±(2S)γ coupling constant: 0.159 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 0°) and 0.239 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 10°)
- ρ±(2S) → π±(2S)γ decay width: 0.233 keV (θ = 0°) and 0.525 keV (θ = 10°)
- ρ±(2S) → π±(1S)γ coupling constant: 0.095 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 0°) and 0.065 GeV⁻¹ (θ = 10°)
- ρ±(2S) → π±(1S)γ decay width: 8.430 keV (θ = 0°) and 3.878 keV (θ = 10°)
- Lattice QCD result for g_ρ(1S)→π(1S)γ: 0.607 GeV⁻¹ (obtained by dividing lattice result by pion mass m_π = 0.135 GeV)
- Lattice QCD result for ρ(1S) → π(1S)γ decay width: 84.2 keV

## Context

This work extends previous light-front quark model studies by Syahbana (2024) and Syahbana (2023) on mass spectra and decay constants of light mesons, now computing M1 radiative transitions including excited 2S states. It follows the same methodology as Ridwan (2024) for heavy quarkonia M1 transitions, applying it to light mesons. The results are compared with the relativized quark model by Godfrey (1985), the extended bag model by Simonis (2016), the relativistic potential model by Jena (2010), lattice QCD by Alexandrou (2018), and an earlier light-front quark model by Choi (1997) using a single Gaussian ansatz. The authors find their predictions are generally lower than both experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2024) and other theoretical models, which they attribute to smaller model parameters adopted from the prior spectroscopic analysis.
