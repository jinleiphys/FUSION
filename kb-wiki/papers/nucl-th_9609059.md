# Squeezed condensate of gluons and $η-η'$ mass difference

> We consider a mechanism to create the $η- η'$ mass difference by the gluon anomaly in a squeezed vacuum. We find that the mass shift of the $η_0$ governing this mass difference is determined by the magnetic part of the gluon condensate. For the squeezed vacuum this magnetic part coincides with the total gluon condensate, so that we get a relation between the gluon condensate and the mass shift of the $η_0$ as a function of the strong coupling constant $α_s$. The values of the gluon condensate obtained through this relation are compared with the value by Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov and the recent update values by Narison.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of a squeezed gluon vacuum, the mass shift of the $\eta_0$ meson (which governs the $\eta-\eta'$ mass difference) is determined entirely by the magnetic part of the gluon condensate, and for this vacuum the magnetic part coincides with the total gluon condensate. This yields a relation between the gluon condensate and the $\eta_0$ mass shift as a function of the strong coupling constant $\alpha_s$, without introducing additional phenomenological constants.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian that couples the $\eta_0$ singlet pseudoscalar to the gluon anomaly term $G^{\mu\nu a} \tilde{G}_{\mu\nu}^a$ alongside the standard gluon kinetic term. They construct the Hamiltonian, then eliminate the gluon degrees of freedom by averaging over a squeezed gluon vacuum state, where the squeezing operator acts on zero-momentum components of the gluon fields. The expectation values of the electric and mixed gluon condensates vanish in the large-volume limit, leaving only the magnetic condensate, which gives an effective Hamiltonian for the $\eta_0$ with a mass shift $\Delta m_0^2$ proportional to the magnetic gluon condensate density.

## Key numbers

* The $\eta-\eta'$ mass difference is $m_{\eta'}^2 - m_{\eta}^2 = 0.616$ GeV$^2$.
* The mixing angle between $\eta$ and $\eta'$ is $\phi = -(17 \pm 2)^\circ$.
* The pion decay constant is $f_\pi = 93$ MeV.
* Two estimates for the $\eta_0$ mass shift are used: $\Delta m_0^2 = 0.616$ GeV$^2$ (chiral limit, zero mixing) and $\Delta m_0^2 = 0.729$ GeV$^2$ (from the quadratic Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula).
* The gluon condensate value from Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (SVZ) is $\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle \simeq 0.04$ GeV$^4$.
* The update average gluon condensate value from Narison is $\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle = (0.071 \pm 0.009)$ GeV$^4$.
* The strong coupling constant used by Narison at low energy is $\alpha_s(1.3\ \text{GeV}) \simeq 0.64^{+0.36}_{-0.18} \pm 0.02$.

## Context

This work extends the approach to the U(1) problem by providing a nonperturbative relation between the $\eta_0$ mass shift and the gluon condensate, without relying on instanton-based mechanisms (as in t'Hooft (1976)) or on fitting an additional phenomenological constant $C$ in the kinetic term of the anomaly (as in Crewther (1979) and Volkov (1995)). The squeezed vacuum model, previously investigated by Celenza and Shakin (1988) and Forshaw (1995) for constructing a stable QCD vacuum, is here applied to the $\eta-\eta'$ system. The resulting gluon condensate values are compared with the SVZ value (Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov, 1979) and the update by Narison (1996), and the agreement suggests this mechanism can simultaneously resolve the U$_A$(1) problem and provide an estimate of the gluon condensate.
