# Proton recoil polarization in exclusive (e,e'pp) reactions

> The general formalism of nucleon recoil polarization in the (${\vec e},e'{\vec N}N$) reaction is given. Numerical predictions are presented for the components of the outgoing proton polarization and of the polarization transfer coefficient in the specific case of the exclusive $^{16}$O(${\vec e},e'{\vec p}p$)$^{14}$C knockout reaction leading to discrete states in the residual nucleus. Reaction calculations are performed in a direct knockout framework where final-state interactions and one-body and two-body currents are included. The two-nucleon overlap integrals are obtained from a calculation of the two-proton spectral function of $^{16}$O where long-range and short-range correlations are consistently included. The comparison of results obtained in different kinematics confirms that resolution of different final states in the $^{16}$O(${\vec e},e'{\vec p}p$)$^{14}$C reaction may act as a filter to disentangle and separately investigate the reaction processes due to short-range correlations and two-body currents and indicates that measurements of the components of the outgoing proton polarization may offer good opportunities to study short-range correlations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that measurements of outgoing proton polarization components in the exclusive $^{16}$O(${\vec e},e'{\vec p}p$)$^{14}$C reaction can offer good opportunities to study short-range correlations, but this is conditional on the specific final state selected and the kinematics employed; the resolution of different final states in the residual nucleus acts as a filter to disentangle contributions from short-range correlations and two-body currents.

## Method

The authors present the general formalism for nucleon recoil polarization in (${\vec e},e'{\vec N}N$) reactions in terms of structure functions and polarization observables. Numerical calculations are performed for the $^{16}$O(${\vec e},e'{\vec p}p$)$^{14}$C reaction using a direct knockout framework that includes final-state interactions via distorted waves from a phenomenological optical potential, one-body and two-body (Delta-isobar) currents, and two-nucleon overlap integrals from a calculation of the two-proton spectral function of $^{16}$O that consistently includes long-range and short-range correlations. Results are presented for transitions to the $0^+$ ground state and the $1^+$ state at 11.31 MeV in $^{14}$C under super-parallel, coplanar, and out-of-plane kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Incident electron energy of 584 MeV, $\omega = 212$ MeV, and $q = 300$ MeV/$c$ for the NIKHEF kinematics considered.
- Kinetic energy of the first outgoing proton $T'_1 = 137$ MeV and angle $\gamma_1 = 30^{\mathrm{o}}$ in the NIKHEF coplanar kinematics.
- For the $0^+$ ground state in super-parallel kinematics, the one-body current (short-range correlations) dominates $P^N$ up to recoil momenta of about $150-200$ MeV/$c$.
- In the out-of-plane kinematics with $\phi = 30^{\mathrm{o}}$, low recoil momentum values in the range between $-160$ and $160$ MeV/$c$ are forbidden.
- The transition to the $1^+$ state at 11.31 MeV excitation energy in $^{14}$C is dominated by the Delta isobar current.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied to unpolarized cross sections of the $^{16}$O($e,e'pp$)$^{14}$C reaction (Giusti and Pacati, 1998; earlier work by the same group) to include polarization observables. It builds on first theoretical predictions for nucleon recoil polarization in two-nucleon knockout by the Gent group (Gent1, Gent2) and on experimental measurements at NIKHEF (Gerco, 1998) and MAMI (Rosner, 1999) that resolved discrete final states in $^{14}$C. The results indicate that the component $P^N$ of the outgoing proton polarization is particularly well suited to study short-range correlations, while measurements of the polarization transfer coefficient $P'^L$ and $P'^S$ require a polarized electron beam and show intertwined contributions from one-body and two-body currents.
