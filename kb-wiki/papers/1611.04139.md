# Wobbling motion in $^{135}$Pr within a collective Hamiltonian

> The recently reported wobbling bands in $^{135}$Pr are investigated by the collective Hamiltonian, in which the collective parameters, including the collective potential and the mass parameter, are respectively determined from the tilted axis cranking (TAC) model and the harmonic frozen alignment (HFA) formula. It is shown that the experimental energy spectra of both yrast and wobbling bands are well reproduced by the collective Hamiltonian. It is confirmed that the wobbling mode in $^{135}$Pr changes from transverse to longitudinal with the rotational frequency. The mechanism of this transition is revealed by analyzing the effective moments of inertia of the three principal axes, and the corresponding variation trend of the wobbling frequency is determined by the softness and shapes of the collective potential.

## Key claim
The wobbling mode in $^{135}$Pr changes from transverse to longitudinal with increasing rotational frequency, and this transition is driven by the evolution of effective moments of inertia and the softness and shapes of the collective potential. The claim is hedged by the fact that the harmonic frozen alignment approximation used for the mass parameter works better at high spins than at low spins, where it overestimates the wobbling frequency.

## Method
The authors constructed a collective Hamiltonian using the azimuth angle $\varphi$ as the collective coordinate, with the collective potential extracted from tilted axis cranking (TAC) calculations and the mass parameter derived from the harmonic frozen alignment (HFA) formula. The TAC model used a single-$j$ shell Hamiltonian for the $\pi(1h_{11/2})^1$ configuration with fixed deformation parameters $\beta=0.17$ and $\gamma=-26.0^\circ$, and moments of inertia $\mathcal{J}_1=13.0$, $\mathcal{J}_2=21.0$, $\mathcal{J}_3=4.0\ \hbar^2/\mathrm{MeV}$. The collective Hamiltonian was solved by diagonalization to obtain energy levels and wave functions for both yrast and wobbling bands.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters: $\beta=0.17$ and $\gamma=-26.0^\circ$ for $^{135}$Pr.
- Moments of inertia: $\mathcal{J}_1=13.0$, $\mathcal{J}_2=21.0$, $\mathcal{J}_3=4.0\ \hbar^2/\mathrm{MeV}$.
- Critical rotational frequency for mode transition: $\hbar\omega = 0.40\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- At $\hbar\omega=0.70\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $\varphi_{\min}$ reaches $\sim \pm 65^\circ$.
- Potential barrier height $\Delta V$ increases from $0.09\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at $\hbar\omega=0.50\ \mathrm{MeV}$ to $0.65\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at $\hbar\omega=0.70\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- Wobbling frequency decreases from $E_{\mathrm{wob}}=0.86\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at $\hbar\omega=0.20\ \mathrm{MeV}$ to $E_{\mathrm{wob}}=0.53\ \mathrm{MeV}$ at $\hbar\omega=0.40\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- Kink in the $I$-$\hbar\omega$ relation at $\hbar\omega=0.4\ \mathrm{MeV}$ ($\sim 10\hbar$).

## Context
This work extends the collective Hamiltonian approach previously applied to chiral modes (Chen 2013) and wobbling modes (Chen 2014) to the specific case of $^{135}$Pr, which was recently reported as the only odd-$A$ wobbler outside the $A\sim 160$ mass region (Matta 2015). The results confirm the transverse-to-longitudinal wobbling transition predicted by Frauendorf and Donau (2014) and reproduce the particle rotor model (PRM) calculations from Matta (2015). The success of this single-$j$ shell approach enables future applications with more realistic TAC calculations, such as TAC covariant density functional theory.
