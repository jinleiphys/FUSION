# Model-independent extraction of two-photon effects in elastic electron-proton scattering

> We address the discrepancy between the Rosenbluth and polarization transfer data for the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon. Assuming that the effect of two-photon corrections on the polarization transfer data is negligible, we obtain a model-independent estimate of the two-photon correction Delta^(2γ). We analyze the polarization transfer data and the cross section data separately using dispersion relations. A central value as well as an error estimate for Delta^(2γ) is then obtained from a comparison of the two analyses. The resulting values for Delta^(2γ) are in good agreement with direct calculations available in the literature.

# Model-independent extraction of two-photon effects in elastic electron-proton scattering

## Key claim
Assuming that two-photon corrections negligibly affect polarization transfer data, the authors extract a model-independent estimate of the two-photon correction Δ^(2γ) by comparing dispersion-relation analyses of polarization transfer data and unpolarized cross section data separately. The extracted values agree with direct calculations by Blunden et al. (2005) within theoretical uncertainties, but the result carries the caveat that the method may also absorb other higher-order corrections beyond hard two-photon exchange.

## Method
The authors performed separate dispersion-relation analyses of the polarization transfer data (to obtain form factors free of two-photon effects) and of the unpolarized Rosenbluth cross section data (with standard Mo-Tsai radiative corrections and Coulomb corrections applied). They reconstructed the Born cross section from the polarization-transfer form factors and compared it to the cross section from the Rosenbluth analysis; the ratio gave the hard two-photon correction δ^(2γ). The total two-photon correction Δ^(2γ) was obtained by adding the Coulomb correction δ^C to δ^(2γ). The analysis was carried out in two variants: one using superconvergence (SC) relations only, and one including an explicit perturbative QCD (pQCD) term.

## Key numbers
- For the SC approach fit to cross section data: 14 free parameters, total χ²/dof = 1.4; χ²/dof for space-like data only (cross section + neutron form factor data) = 0.86.
- For the pQCD approach fit to cross section data: 20 free parameters, total χ²/dof = 1.14; χ²/dof for space-like data only = 0.91.
- The previous form factor fit (Belushkin 2006) within the SC approach had 17 free parameters and χ²/dof = 1.8; the pQCD approach had 14 free parameters and χ²/dof = 2.0.
- Δ^(2γ) was extracted at Q² = 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 3.0, 4.0, and 5.0 GeV² (values shown in Figure 2; numerical values not given in the text excerpt).
- The SC and pQCD results for Δ^(2γ) agree within theoretical 1σ error bands up to Q² ≈ 2 GeV²; at higher Q² the pQCD result predicts a larger Δ^(2γ).
- The SC result for Δ^(2γ) is in excellent agreement with the direct calculation by Blunden et al. (2005) up to Q² ≈ 4 GeV² within experimental error bars.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of the proton electromagnetic form factors, first highlighted by high-precision Jefferson Lab results (Jones 1999, Gayou 2001). It extends the dispersion-relation framework of Belushkin, Hammer, and Meißner (2006) by analyzing only unpolarized cross section data and comparing to the polarization-based form factors. The extracted two-photon corrections agree with direct Feynman-diagram calculations by Blunden et al. (2003, 2005) that included elastic intermediate states and by Kondratyuk et al. (2005) that included the Δ resonance. The results enable a consistent global reanalysis of unpolarized cross section data with all relevant corrections applied, potentially resolving the form factor discrepancy.
