# Origin of the structures observed in $e^+e^-$ annihilation into multipion states around the $\bar pp$ threshold

> We analyze the origin of the structures observed in the reactions $e^+e^-\to 3(π^+π^-)$, $2(π^+π^-π^0)$, $ωπ^+π^-π^0$, and $e^+e^-\to 2(π^+π^-)π^0$ around the antiproton-proton ($\bar pp$) threshold. We calculate the contribution of the two-step process $e^+e^-\to \bar NN \to$ multipions to the total reaction amplitude. The amplitude for $e^+e^-\to \bar NN$ is constrained from near-threshold data on the $e^+e^-\to \bar pp$ cross section and the one for $\bar NN \to$ multipions can be likewise fixed from available experimental information, for all those $5 π$ and $6π$ states. The resulting amplitude for $e^+e^-\to$ multipions turns out to be large enough to play a role for the considered $e^+e^-$ annihilation channels and, in three of the four reactions, even allows us to reproduce the data quantitatively near the $\bar NN$ threshold. The structures seen in the experiments emerges then as a threshold effect due to the opening of the $\bar NN$ channel.

## Key claim
The structures observed in $e^+e^-$ annihilation into multipion states near the $\bar pp$ threshold arise as a threshold effect from the opening of the $\bar NN$ channel, not necessarily from a $\bar NN$ bound state; the two-step process $e^+e^- \to \bar NN \to$ multipions is large enough to reproduce data quantitatively in three of four reactions, but the authors cannot make a firm conclusion about the existence of a bound state.

## Method
The authors calculated the contribution of the two-step process $e^+e^- \to \bar NN \to$ multipions to the total reaction amplitude using a chiral EFT $\bar NN$ potential (NNLO, cutoff $\{\Lambda, \tilde\Lambda\} = \{450,500\}$ MeV) that reproduces $\bar pp$ scattering data up to $T_{lab} \approx 200-250$ MeV. The $e^+e^- \to \bar NN$ amplitude was constrained from near-threshold $e^+e^- \to \bar pp$ cross section data, and the $\bar NN \to$ multipion transition potentials were parameterized by two contact terms and fitted to branching ratios of $\bar pp$ annihilation at rest and in-flight data for $3(\pi^+\pi^-)$ and $2(\pi^+\pi^-)\pi^0$. The background amplitude $A_{e^+e^- \to \nu}$ was parameterized similarly with complex constants and fitted to $e^+e^- \to \nu$ cross sections in the range $1750 \le E \le 1950$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- The $\bar pp$ threshold is at $1.8765$ GeV; phenomenological fits locate the structure at $1.91$ GeV (FOCUS1) or $1.86$–$1.88$ GeV (BaBar).
- For $\bar pp \to 2(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)$ at $p_{\rm lab} = 106$ MeV/c, the fitted cross section is $63.2$ mb; for $\bar pp \to \omega \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$, it is $57.5$ mb.
- The $e^+e^- \to \bar pp$ amplitude used a combination $T_{\bar pp} \approx 0.7\,T^{I=1} + 0.3\,T^{I=0}$; the $^3D_1$ contribution multiplies the $S$-wave cross section by a factor $1.5$.
- For the $e^+e^- \to 3(\pi^+\pi^-)$ channel, the $\bar NN$ loop contribution would need to be a factor four larger to explain the data.
- The $\bar NN$ interaction in the $I=0$ $^3S_1$–$^3D_1$ partial wave has a pole at $E_B = (+4.8 - i\,68.2)$ MeV (above threshold, with positive real part).

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous chiral EFT studies of $\bar NN$ scattering (Haidenbauer et al., 2014) and $e^+e^- \to \bar pp$ (Haidenbauer et al., 2014) to multipion production, providing the first quantitative calculation of the $\bar NN$ channel’s impact on $e^+e^- \to 5\pi, 6\pi$ reactions. It contradicts the speculation of a $\bar pp$ bound state (FOCUS1, CMD, CMD2) and supports the interpretation of Rosner (2006) that ordinary threshold effects (cusps) could explain the structures. The results enable future high-precision measurements near the $\bar NN$ threshold to distinguish between a cusp and a bound-state signal, and suggest similar effects may appear in $e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ and $4\pi$ channels.
