# Density dependent relativistic hadron theory for hadron matter with inclusion of pentaquark $Œò^+$

> The density dependent relativistic hadron field theory(DDRH) is extended to inclusions of pentaquark $Œò^+$. We investigate In-medium properties of $Œò^+$ and nucleons. As well as the neutron and proton,the effective $Œò^+$ mass decreases as the baryon density increases,and remains larger than nucleons'. The effective mass of $Œò^+$ is calculated,$M^*_{Œò^+}\simeq0.71M_{Œò^+}$ at normal nuclear density with the fraction of $Œò^+$ 0.2. The effective masses of all baryon increase with the increasing fraction of $Œò^+$. The binding energy is also studied. We find the binding energy is much larger than it in nuclear matter without $Œò^+$,and then discuss the stability of the system with conclusions of pentaquark $Œò^+$. It is shown that the system becomes bounder added small fraction of $Œò^+$. Finally we discuss the condition when the system is the most stable and calculate the minimum of binding energy and the fractions of all baryons as functions of baryon density.

## Key claim

The density dependent relativistic hadron field theory (DDRH) is extended to include the pentaquark \(\Theta^+\), and the system becomes more bound when a small fraction of \(\Theta^+\) is added, but becomes less bound when too much is added; the critical fraction of \(\Theta^+\) is 0.56 in isospin symmetric matter.

## Method

The authors extend the DDRH theory by adding a \(\Theta^+\) baryon field to the Lagrangian density, with meson-baryon vertices that are density-dependent functionals. In the mean-field approximation, the meson field equations and Dirac equations are solved self-consistently for infinite nuclear matter containing neutrons, protons, and \(\Theta^+\) pentaquarks. The \(\Theta^+\)-meson coupling constants are derived from a quark-meson model, taking \(\Gamma_{\alpha}^{\Theta^+}(\rho) = \frac{4}{3} \Gamma_{\alpha}^N(\rho)\) for the \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) mesons, while the \(\Theta^+\) (isospin \(I=0\)) does not couple to \(\rho\) or \(\delta\) mesons.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass of \(\Theta^+\) at normal nuclear density with \(\Theta^+\) fraction 0.2: \(M^*_{\Theta^+} \simeq 0.71 M_{\Theta^+}\).
- The absolute minimum of binding energy in isospin symmetric matter reaches nearly 24 MeV at baryon density \(1.4 \rho_0\) when the \(\Theta^+\) fraction is about 0.23.
- The critical fraction of \(\Theta^+\) in isospin symmetric matter is 0.56; above this fraction the system is no longer bound.
- The minimum of binding energy occurs at a density of about \(1.5 \rho_0\).
- Normal nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the DDRH theory, previously applied to asymmetric nuclear matter by Hofmann et al., to include the exotic pentaquark \(\Theta^+\) predicted by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and experimentally observed with mass near 1540 MeV and narrow width. It builds on the suggestion by Cabrera et al. that \(\Theta^+\) could be bound in nuclei, and provides a self-consistent mean-field calculation of in-medium effective masses and binding energies for multi-Theta matter. The results enable further studies of the stability and properties of exotic \(\Theta^+\) hypernuclei and matter with mixed baryon content.
