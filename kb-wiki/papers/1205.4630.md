# Coupled-Channel Effects in Collisions between Heavy Ions near the Coulomb Barrier

> With the recent availability of state-of-the-art heavy-ion stable and radioactive beams, there has been a renew interest in the investigation of nuclear reactions with heavy ions. I first present the role of inelastic and transfer channel couplings in fusion reactions induced by stable heavy ions. Analysis of experimental fusion cross sections by using standard coupled-channel calculations is discussed. The role of multi-neutron transfer is investigated in the fusion process below the Coulomb barrier by analyzing $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr as benchmark reactions. The enhancement of fusion cross sections for $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr is well reproduced at sub-barrier energies by NTFus code calculations including the coupling of the neutron-transfer channels following the Zagrebaev semi-classical model. Similar effects for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr fusion excitation functions are found. The breakup coupling in both the elastic scattering and in the fusion process induced by weakly bound stable projectiles is also shown to be crucial. In this lecture, full coupled-channel calculations of the fusion excitation functions are performed by using the breakup coupling for the more neutron-rich reaction and for the more weakly bound projectiles. I clearly demonstrate that Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel calculations are capable to reproduce the fusion enhancement from the breakup coupling in $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co.

# Coupled-Channel Effects in Collisions between Heavy Ions near the Coulomb Barrier

## Key claim
Multi-neutron transfer channels with positive Q-values produce significant enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections for the more neutron-rich system $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr, but this effect is absent for $^{32}$S+$^{90}$Zr where neutron transfer Q-values are negative. The Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) approach reproduces fusion enhancement from breakup coupling in $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, though the effect on total fusion is small below the barrier.

## Method
Fusion excitation functions for $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr were analyzed using the NTFus coupled-channel code, which incorporates multi-neutron transfer channels via the Zagrebaev semiclassical model. For $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, CDCC calculations with $\alpha$+$d$ breakup coupling to the continuum were performed and compared to elastic scattering angular distributions and fusion data. Barrier distributions were extracted using the three-point difference method of Rowley (1992).

## Key numbers
- Fusion enhancement for $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr at sub-barrier energies is reproduced by NTFus calculations including neutron-transfer channels up to +4n (k=4), with agreement up to +5n and +6n channels.
- The $^{96}$Zr nucleus has a 2$^{+}$ state at 1.748 MeV and a 3$^{-}$ state at 1.897 MeV with B(E3;3$^{-}$ $\rightarrow$ 0$^{+}$) = 51 W.u.
- For $^{32}$S+$^{90}$Zr, an over-estimation of fusion cross sections occurs between about 82 MeV and 90 MeV in the high-energy part of the excitation function.
- The BD-Fusion and BD-QEL barrier distributions for both $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr are almost identical up to E$_{c.m.}$ $\approx$ 85 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier coupled-channel analyses of $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr fusion by Timmers (1998) and quasi-elastic scattering measurements by Yang (2008). The NTFus code calculations confirm the Zagrebaev semiclassical model (Zagrebaev, 2003) for describing neutron-transfer effects on sub-barrier fusion. The CDCC analysis of $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co builds on prior work by Beck (2003, 2007) and provides a framework for understanding breakup coupling in reactions induced by weakly bound and halo nuclei, with implications for future experiments using SPIRAL beams at GANIL.
