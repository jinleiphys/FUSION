# How well do we know neutrino-electron scattering? EFT approach

> We determine the effective theory of neutrino-electron and neutrino-quark scattering and provide the most precise up-to-date prediction for neutrino-electron scattering cross sections quantifying errors for the first time to be of order $0.2-0.4~\%$. Radiative corrections in the theory with electron and neutrinos only involve three effective couplings. One is the Fermi constant which is known with sub-ppm accuracy. Another one has a small error of order $0.02~\%$. The uncertainty of the third one is limited by the knowledge of hadronic contributions to charge-isospin vector-vector correlation function.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first complete effective field theory calculation of neutrino-electron scattering cross sections with quantified errors of order 0.2–0.4%, where the dominant uncertainty comes from hadronic contributions to the charge-isospin vector-vector correlation function rather than from the electroweak matching.

## Method

The author constructs an effective QED field theory with contact four-fermion operators for neutrino-electron and neutrino-quark scattering, matching to the Standard Model at the electroweak scale through order O(α α_s) in the MS-bar renormalization scheme. The effective couplings are run to low energies using renormalization group equations, and the total cross sections and energy spectra are evaluated including virtual QED corrections (vertex and propagator corrections, closed fermion loops) and real photon bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- Fermi constant: G_F = 1.1663787(6) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻² (sub-ppm accuracy)
- Scale-independent constant for neutrino-electron scattering: G̃_e = 1.18083(21) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻² (0.02% error)
- Right-handed coupling at μ = 2 GeV: c_R(μ=2 GeV) = 0.7773(28) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Right-handed coupling at muon mass scale for ν_e e and ν_μ e: c_R(m_μ) = 0.7706(29) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Right-handed coupling at muon mass scale for ν_τ e: c_R(m_μ) = 0.7779(29) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Right-handed coupling at electron mass scale for ν_e e: c_R(m_e) = 0.7575(29) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Scale-independent constants for neutrino-quark interactions: G_u = 1.14570(23) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻², G_d = 1.18211(21) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Effective couplings at μ = 2 GeV for up quarks: c_L^u = 1.14065(10), c_R^u = -0.51173(37)
- Effective couplings at μ = 2 GeV for down quarks: c_L^d = -1.41478(10), c_R^d = 0.25617(19)
- Charged current coupling at μ = 2 GeV: c^{qq'}/V_{qq'}(M_Z) = 3.32685(8)

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculations of Weinberg (1967) and 't Hooft (1971) for leading-order cross sections, and the electroweak corrections of Marciano (1980), Aoki (1980), and Sarantakos (1982), as well as QED corrections with bremsstrahlung by multiple authors. It provides the first consistent effective field theory calculation with quantified error estimates, following the recent work of Tomalak (2019). The results enable precise neutrino flux calibration at accelerator-based experiments free from nuclear uncertainties, as discussed by Park (2015), Valencia (2019), and Marshall (2019), and support the broader program of neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-nucleus interactions.
