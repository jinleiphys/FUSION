# Level rearrangement in exotic-atom-like three-body systems

> We study systems of three bosons bound by a long-range interaction supplemented by a short-range potential of variable strength. This generalizes the usual two-body exotic atoms where the Coulomb interaction is modified by nuclear forces at short distances. The energy shift due to the short-range part of the interaction combines two-body terms similar to the ones entering the Trueman-Deser formula, and three-body contributions. A sudden variation of the energy levels is observed near the coupling thresholds of the short-range potential. But the patterns of rearrangement are significantly modified as compared to the two-body case.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in three-boson systems with a long-range plus short-range potential, the energy levels show a rearrangement pattern similar to the two-body exotic-atom case, but the transition is significantly modified: the excited state does not reach a second plateau, and the rearrangement occurs near the three-body Borromean threshold (λ ≈ 0.8) rather than the two-body binding threshold (λ = 1).

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for three identical bosons interacting via a superposition of a long-range exponential potential (fixed strength λ₀ = 2) and a shorter-range exponential potential of variable strength λ and range parameter μ = 10, 20, or 30. Energies were computed variationally using exponential or Gaussian trial wavefunctions, with range parameters chosen from geometric progressions or by random trials for excited states.

## Key numbers

- The three-body Borromean threshold occurs at λ₃^cr ≈ 0.8, meaning about 20% of the two-body binding threshold (λ₂^cr = 1) is a Borromean window.
- For μ = 30, the second excited three-body state becomes bound below the two-body threshold at λ ≈ 0.5.
- For a single exponential potential (no long-range component), the second excited state requires λ ≈ 5 to become bound.
- The long-range potential strength was fixed at λ₀ = 2, i.e., twice the two-body critical coupling.

## Context

This work extends the two-body exotic-atom framework (Deser-Trueman formula, level rearrangement) to three-body systems, building on prior studies of Borromean binding (Zhusupov 1993) and Efimov states. The authors note that an earlier three-body rearrangement study by Osborn (1967) suffered from numerical instability. This investigation enables future work on asymmetric systems such as (K⁻pp) and on the precise definition of a three-body short-range scattering length.
