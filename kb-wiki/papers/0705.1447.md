# Wakes in a Collisional Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Wakes created by a parton moving through a static and infinitely extended quark-gluon plasma are considered. In contrast to former investigations collisions within the quark-gluon plasma are taken into account using a transport theoretical approach (Boltzmann equation) with a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook collision term. Within this model it is shown that the wake structure changes significantly compared to the collisionless case.

# Wakes in a Collisional Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The wake structure created by a parton moving through a quark-gluon plasma changes significantly when collisions are included via a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision term in the Boltzmann equation, compared to the previously studied collisionless case. However, the authors state that the BGK approach allows only qualitative statements, and a consistent QCD approach with momentum-dependent collision rates is beyond the scope of this work.

## Method
The authors start from the Boltzmann equation with a BGK collision term, replacing the collision term by a momentum-independent collision rate taken as a free parameter. They derive analytic expressions for the longitudinal and transverse dielectric functions of a collisional QGP, then compute the induced charge density and wake potential from these dielectric functions using numerical integration. The wake potential is obtained from the Poisson equation combined with the linearized kinetic equation, yielding the same formal expression as in the collisionless case but with the collision-modified dielectric function.

## Key numbers
- Debye screening mass: \( m_D = \sqrt{1 + n_f/6} \, gT \), with example values \( n_f = 2 \), \( g = 2 \) (corresponding to \( \alpha_s = 1/\pi \)), and \( T = 250 \) MeV giving \( m_D = 580 \) MeV.
- Parton velocities studied: \( v = 0.55 \, c \) and \( v = 0.99 \, c \).
- Collision rates considered: from \( \nu = 0 \) to \( \nu = 0.8 \, m_D \).
- Induced charge densities are proportional to \( m_D^3 \) and scaled with \( m_D \) and the color charge \( Q^a \) of the moving parton.

## Context
This work extends prior investigations of wakes in a collisionless QGP by Chakraborty (2006), which used the Vlasov equation and dielectric functions to show that wakes lead to attractive potentials and Mach cones. The BGK approach for collisional QGP was previously applied to study dispersion relations by Carrington (2004) and instabilities by Schenke (2006). The results are analogous to effects observed in complex electromagnetic plasmas, where collisions between ions and neutral gas truncate wakes effectively, as shown by Lampe (2000). The findings enable future study of how collisions modify observable effects such as conical flow and wave excitations in heavy-ion collisions, and may affect the binding of diquark states propagating through the QGP.
