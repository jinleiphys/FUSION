# Shear and bulk viscosities of the gluon plasma in a quasiparticle description

> Shear and bulk viscosities of deconfined gluonic matter are investigated within an effective kinetic theory by describing the strongly interacting medium phenomenologically in terms of quasiparticle excitations with medium-dependent self-energies. We show that the resulting transport coefficients reproduce the parametric dependencies on temperature and coupling obtained in perturbative QCD at large temperatures and small running coupling. The extrapolation into the non-perturbative regime results in a decreasing specific shear viscosity with decreasing temperature, exhibiting a minimum in the vicinity of the deconfinement transition, while the specific bulk viscosity is sizeable in this region falling off rapidly with increasing temperature. The temperature dependence of specific shear and bulk viscosities found within this quasiparticle description of the pure gluon plasma is in agreement with available lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle description of the gluon plasma, combined with an effective kinetic theory in relaxation time approximation, produces shear and bulk viscosities that agree with available lattice QCD results, but only when the relaxation time parameters are tuned (Fit 2) to match those lattice data rather than the perturbative QCD limit; the specific shear viscosity exhibits a minimum near the deconfinement transition only for certain parameter choices.

## Method

The authors model the pure gluon plasma as quasiparticle excitations with a temperature-dependent self-energy Π(T) = T² G²(T)/2, where G²(T) is an effective coupling inspired by the QCD 1-loop running coupling. They compute shear and bulk viscosities from kinetic theory expressions in relaxation time approximation, using a phenomenological relaxation rate τ⁻¹ = a₁ T G⁴ ln(a₂/G²) derived from elastic gluon-gluon 2↔2 scattering with screening effects. The quasiparticle model parameters are fixed by reproducing lattice QCD results for the equation of state and interaction measure (ε-3P)/T⁴.

## Key numbers

* Specific shear viscosity η/s exhibits a minimum of (η/s)min ≈ 0.6 at T ≈ 1.75 Tc for Fit 1 parameters.
* For Fit 2 parameters, η/s shows agreement with lattice QCD results from Meyer (2007) over a wide temperature range, with relaxation time τ ≈ 0.4 fm/c for T ≥ 1.2 Tc.
* The relaxation time diverges at T* ≈ 1.32 Tc for Fit 1 and T* ≈ 1.1 Tc for Fit 2, where G²(T*) equals the parameter a₂.
* The squared speed of sound is parametrized for Tc < T ≤ 4 Tc with χ²/d.o.f. = 3.89×10⁻⁴/16; at T = 4 Tc it is 2.6% smaller than 1/3.
* The critical temperature is set to Tc = 271 MeV, with model parameters λ = 4.3 and Ts = 0.73 Tc, and integration constant B(Tc) = 0.19 Tc⁴.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model previously applied to equilibrium thermodynamics of the gluon plasma (Peshier 1994, 1996; Bluhm 2005, 2007) to transport coefficients, using a kinetic theory framework developed by Jeon (1995, 1996) and Chakraborty (2010). The results contradict naive extrapolations of perturbative QCD results (Arnold 2003) which give η/s ∼ 2.7 at comparable coupling, and instead support the conjecture from Csernai (2006) and Lacey (2007) that η/s exhibits a minimum near the deconfinement transition. The approach enables quantitative comparison with lattice QCD results from Meyer (2007), Nakamura (2005), and Sakai (2007), and provides relaxation times comparable to those used in hydrodynamic simulations by Song (2010) for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
