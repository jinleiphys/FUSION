# Effect of the N3LO three-nucleon contact interaction on p-d scattering observables

> A unitary transformation allows to remove redundant terms in the two-nucleon (2N) contact interaction at the fourth order (N3LO) in the low-energy expansion of Chiral Effective Field Theory. In so doing a three-nucleon (3N) interaction is generated. We express its short-range component in terms of five combinations of low-energy constants (LECs) parametrizing the N3LO 2N contact Lagrangian. Within a hybrid approach, in which this interaction is considered in conjunction with the phenomenological AV18 2N potential, we show that the involved LECs can be used to fit very accurate data on polarization observables of low-energy $p-d$ scattering, in particular the $A_y$ asymmetry. The resulting interaction is of the right order of magnitude for a N3LO contribution.

## Key claim
The five low-energy constants (LECs) that parametrize the N3LO three-nucleon contact interaction, generated by removing redundant terms from the two-nucleon contact interaction via a unitary transformation, can be fitted to reproduce low-energy p-d scattering polarization observables, in particular the long-standing Ay asymmetry problem, but only within a hybrid approach that uses the phenomenological AV18 two-nucleon potential rather than a fully consistent chiral calculation.

## Method
The authors start from the N3LO two-nucleon contact Hamiltonian with 17 LECs and apply a unitary transformation with five parameters to remove redundant terms, generating an induced three-nucleon contact interaction. They express this short-range three-nucleon force in terms of five combinations of the original two-nucleon LECs, then add it to the AV18 two-nucleon potential. Using the Hyperspherical Harmonics method, they solve the three-body Schrodinger equation for p-d scattering below the breakup threshold and fit the five LECs to 282 experimental data points at center-of-mass energy 2 MeV, including the differential cross section, vector and tensor analyzing powers, and n-d scattering lengths.

## Key numbers
- The global fit yields chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.7 for the 5-parameter fit, compared to 2.3 for a 3-parameter fit that excludes the P-dependent two-nucleon contact interaction (setting alpha_4 = alpha_5 = 0).
- The fitted doublet n-d scattering length is 0.648 fm (5-parameter fit) and 0.647 fm (3-parameter fit), compared to the experimental value (0.645 +/- 0.003 +/- 0.007) fm.
- The fitted quartet n-d scattering length is 6.31 fm (5-parameter fit) and 6.32 fm (3-parameter fit), compared to the experimental value (6.35 +/- 0.02) fm.
- The LO three-nucleon contact LEC E0 in natural units is e0 = 0.685 (5-parameter fit) and e0 = -1.570 (3-parameter fit), with e0 = E0 F_pi^4 Lambda where F_pi = 92.4 MeV and Lambda = 500 MeV.
- The fitted combinations of N3LO two-nucleon LECs (in units of 10^4 GeV^{-4}) from the 5-parameter fit are: D16 = -0.610, D17 = -0.536, 16D1+D2+4D3 = -3.96, 16D5+D6+4D7 = -0.593, D14+16D11+4D12+4D13 = 2.08.

## Context
This work extends the observation by Reinert (2018) that three of the fifteen N3LO two-nucleon contact interactions can be removed by a unitary transformation, and the authors' own previous work (Girlanda 2020) identifying two additional LECs (D16 and D17) that also generate three-nucleon forces. It directly addresses the long-standing Ay puzzle in low-energy p-d scattering, which has resisted solution by the AV18 potential alone or with the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. The results enable future fully consistent chiral effective field theory calculations at N3LO that could resolve the Ay problem without relying on phenomenological two-nucleon potentials.
