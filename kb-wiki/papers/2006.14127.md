# Novel dynamic critical phenomena induced by superfluidity and the chiral magnetic effect in Quantum Chromodynamics

> In this thesis, we study the novel dynamic critical phenomena induced by superfluidity and the chiral magnetic effect in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), respectively. In particular, we find the new dynamic universality class of the high-density QCD critical point between the nuclear and quark superfluid phases beyond the conventional Hohnenberg and Halperin's classification. We also consider the second-order chiral phase transition under an external magnetic field and find that the inclusion of the chiral magnetic effect changes the dynamic universality class from the model E into model A within the conventional classification.

### Key claim
This thesis claims that the high-density QCD critical point between nuclear and quark superfluid phases belongs to a new dynamic universality class beyond the conventional Hohenberg-Halperin classification, and that including the chiral magnetic effect changes the dynamic universality class of the second-order chiral phase transition under an external magnetic field from model E to model A.

### Method
The author constructs low-energy effective theories based on the symmetries and hydrodynamic variables of QCD. For the high-density critical point, the theory incorporates the interplay between the chiral order parameter and the superfluid phonon. For the chiral phase transition under a magnetic field, nonlinear Langevin equations incorporating quantum-anomaly effects are constructed and analyzed using the dynamic renormalization group.

### Key numbers
- The intrinsic scale of QCD is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim 200\) MeV, which sets the energy scale below which perturbation theory breaks down.
- The high-temperature regime studied is \(T \sim 10^{12}\) kelvin, and the high-density regime corresponds to a baryon chemical potential \(\mu_{\rm B}\) with mass density \(\sim 10^{12}\) kg/cm\(^3\).
- The running coupling constant satisfies \(\alpha_s(\mu) = 2\pi / [b_0 \log(\mu/\Lambda_{\rm QCD})]\) with \(b_0 = (11/3)N_c - (2/3)N_f\).
- The masses of up and down quarks are approximately \(m_u \approx 3\) MeV and \(m_d \approx 5\) MeV, both small compared to \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).

### Context
This work extends the study of dynamic critical phenomena in QCD beyond the high-temperature critical point, which was previously classified as model H by Fujii (2003, 2004), Son (2004), and Minami (2011). It also builds on the classification of the second-order chiral phase transition in massless two-flavor QCD as belonging to the same class as O(4) antiferromagnets by Rajagopal (1992). The results provide theoretical predictions testable in future low-energy heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR, NICA, J-PARC, and HIRF, and may offer indirect evidence for superfluidity in dense QCD matter.
