# Scattering as a tool to study nuclear structure toward the drip lines

> Results are presented for the elastic scattering of electrons and protons from the exotic He and Li isotopes. Comparison with scattering results from the stable He and Li nuclei allows for an investigation into the effects that the extensive neutron distributions have on the charge density. For comparison we also consider the proton halo nucleus 8B. The consequences and possible suggestions for proposed electron scattering facilities for exotic nuclei are discussed.

# Scattering as a tool to study nuclear structure toward the drip lines

## Key claim
Proton scattering is sensitive to neutron halos, but longitudinal electron scattering form factors of neutron-rich halo nuclei (6He, 11Li) show that the neutron halo does not affect the charge density; instead, the charge density is extended only through the addition of neutrons to the stable isotopes, irrespective of whether the exotic nucleus has a neutron halo or a skin. For proton halos (8B), the situation is entirely different, with both longitudinal and transverse form factors significantly reduced due to the extension of the charge density.

## Method
The authors used the Melbourne g-folding model for nucleon-nucleus scattering, which starts from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone g matrices for infinite nuclear matter to form a complex, energy- and density-dependent effective NN interaction, folded with shell-model one-body density matrix elements (OBDME) to generate optical potentials. For electron scattering, longitudinal and transverse form factors were calculated following the deForest and Walecka formalism using one-body operators with Siegert's theorem for transverse electric terms, with nuclear structure input from complete (0+2+4)ħω shell-model calculations using harmonic oscillator or Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions.

## Key numbers
- 6He neutron halo established from 6He-hydrogen scattering at 41A MeV (Lagoyannis et al., 2001) and confirmed at 25A MeV (Strojek et al., 2002)
- 8He elastic scattering from hydrogen at 16A MeV: similarity between HO and WS results confirms 8He has a neutron skin, not a halo
- 7He half-life: τ1/2 = 2.2 × 10⁻²¹ s (particle unstable)
- 10C and 11C scattering from hydrogen at 45A and 40A MeV, respectively: HO wave functions with b = 1.6 fm used; elastic scattering well reproduced without adjustment
- Inelastic scattering to 2⁺ state in 10C and 5/2⁻ state in 11C required renormalization of ~1.4 to match data
- 4He charge radius: predicted 1.71 fm, measured 1.671 ± 0.014 fm (Ottermann et al., 1985)
- 4He longitudinal form factor data reproduced up to 2.5 fm⁻¹
- 8B single proton separation energy: 137 keV (TUNL, 2004)
- 8B elastic proton scattering predicted at 65 MeV: halo manifests as reduction in cross section at large angles
- 8B longitudinal form factor dominated by C0 component below 1 fm⁻¹, C2 contribution significant above that value

## Context
This work extends the Melbourne model (Amos et al., 2000) for nucleon-nucleus scattering to exotic He and Li isotopes, comparing with earlier JLM calculations that required arbitrary renormalizations (e.g., λW = 0.2 for 8He, ~10% real potential renormalization for 10,11C). The results for 6He confirm the neutron halo established by Lagoyannis et al. (2001) and Strojek et al. (2002). The paper enables future experiments at electron-ion colliders and SCRIT facilities (Suda and Wakasugi, 2005) to directly probe charge densities of exotic nuclei, with complementary proton scattering allowing complete mapping of matter densities for microscopic tests of structure models.
