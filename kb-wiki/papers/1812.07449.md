# The Angular Power Spectrum of Heavy Ion Collisions

> Particles produced in heavy ion collisions carry information about anisotropies present already in the early state of the system and play a crucial role in understanding the Quark Gluon Plasma and its evolution. We explore the angular power spectrum of particle multiplicities in such heavy ion collisions to extract fluctuations in particle multiplicities on the surface a sphere. Results are presented for Pb-Pb data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\mathrm{TeV}$, extracted from the ALICE open data portal. We find that odd modes of the power spectrum display a power-law behavior with corresponding index $β$, which is found to be close to unity. We also demonstrate that the angular power spectrum allows us to extract accurately the flow coefficients of non-central collisions.

## Key claim

The angular power spectrum of particle multiplicities in heavy ion collisions can be used to extract flow coefficients, and the odd multipole modes display a power-law behavior with an exponent beta close to unity; however, the odd-mode power law appears to be triggered by a small forward-backward asymmetry from collisions shifted relative to the detector center, not by intrinsic physics of the Quark Gluon Plasma.

## Method

The authors used public ALICE Pb-Pb data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, mapping each event's particle distribution onto a Mollweide projection using the HEALPix pixelation scheme within pseudorapidity |eta| < 0.9 (polar angle 44 degrees to 136 degrees). They expanded the multiplicity distribution in spherical harmonics, defined a modified angular power spectrum C_l^{m != 0} that excludes the m=0 mode to remove detector acceptance artifacts, and extracted flow coefficients v_1, v_2, and v_3 by relating the power spectrum to the Fourier flow decomposition. The method was validated against Monte Carlo simulations and compared to the Q-cumulants two-particle correlation method applied to the same data.

## Key numbers

* The analysis uses Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, with data from the ALICE open data portal.
* For 0-5% centrality, event multiplicities range from approximately 2000 to 3000 charged particles.
* The sphere was divided into 768 equal-area pixels; the region |eta| < 0.9 contains 544 pixels.
* The maximum multipole used was l_max = 23.
* The power-law exponent beta for odd modes was found to be approximately 1.068, consistent with unity within 1 sigma.
* For flow extraction, the coefficient |b_31|^2 was found to be of order 10^2 smaller than |b_31|^2 (likely a typo in the text; the text states |b_31|^2 is of O(10^2) smaller than |b_31|^2, which is self-contradictory; the intended meaning is that |b_31|^2 is much smaller than |b_31|^2, but no explicit numerical value is given).

## Context

This work extends the approach proposed in CMB_heavy_paper1 (2015) of applying cosmic microwave background analysis tools to heavy ion collisions, and builds on earlier studies in powspec_paper2 and spanish_paper3. The method provides an alternative to standard flow analyses such as the Q-cumulants method of Bilandzic (2014) for two-particle correlations, and enables future studies of jet contributions to the angular power spectrum and investigations of particle distributions within different p_T intervals. The authors note that the small discrepancies between their v_n{C_l} and v_n{2, QC} for v_3 in peripheral centralities may reflect non-factorizable contributions in theta and phi that warrant further study.
