# QCD Sum Rule for Open Strange Meson $K_1^\pm$ in Nuclear Matter

> The properties of open strange meson $K_1^\pm$ in nuclear matter are estimated in the QCD sum rule approach. We obtain a relation between the in-medium mass and width of $K_1^-$ ($K_1^+$) in nuclear matter, and show that the upper limit of the mass shift is as large as -249 (-35) MeV. The spectral modification of the $K_1$ meson is possible to be probed by using kaon beams at J-PARC. Such measurement together with that of $K^*$ will shed light on how chiral symmetry is partially restored in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors use QCD sum rules to estimate the in-medium mass shift of the open strange meson \(K_1^\pm\) in nuclear matter, finding that the upper limit of the mass shift is as large as -249 MeV for \(K_1^-\) and -35 MeV for \(K_1^+\) at normal nuclear density, but these numbers represent the maximum possible shift because the in-medium width has been neglected in that part of the analysis.

## Method

The authors perform a QCD sum rule analysis of the axial-vector current correlation function for the \(K_1\) meson, using the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 6 in both vacuum and nuclear matter. In nuclear matter, they include twist-2 operators and use linear density approximation for the condensates, separating the correlation function into even and odd parts under charge conjugation to treat \(K_1^-\) and \(K_1^+\) separately. The in-medium mass shift is extracted by minimizing a function of the Borel-transformed sum rule over the Borel window, solving coupled linear equations for the density-dependent changes in the overlap strength, mass, and continuum threshold.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit of the mass shift for \(K_1^-\) at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.16~\text{fm}^{-3}\): \(\delta m_{K_1^-} = -249~\text{MeV}\) (20% reduction from vacuum mass)
- Upper limit of the mass shift for \(K_1^+\) at normal nuclear matter density: \(\delta m_{K_1^+} = -35~\text{MeV}\) (3% reduction from vacuum mass)
- Vacuum mass of \(K_1\) used in the sum rule: \(m_{K_1} = 1.27~\text{GeV}\)
- Vacuum continuum threshold: \(s_0 = 2.4~\text{GeV}^2\)
- Borel window: \(1.06 \leq M^2 \leq 2.17~\text{GeV}^2\)
- Overlap strength in vacuum: \(F_{K_1} \equiv m_{K_1}^2/g_{K_1}^2 \approx 0.048~\text{GeV}^2\)

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach previously applied to vector mesons such as \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\phi\) in nuclear matter by Hatsuda and Lee (1991) and to the \(f_1\) meson by Gubler and Lee (2016). The study is motivated by the fact that the mass difference between the axial-vector \(K_1\) and its chiral partner \(K^*\) is sensitive to the chiral order parameter, so measuring both in nuclear matter could probe partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The authors suggest that the spectral modification of \(K_1\) could be probed using kaon beams at J-PARC, complementing planned measurements of the \(\phi\) meson at J-PARC E16 and existing data on \(\omega\) and \(K^*\) from KEK-PS and CBELSA/TAPS.
