# Heavy Quarkonia in the Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Model

> The heavy quarkonia (Charmonium \(c\bar{c}\) and Bottomonium \(b\bar{b}\)) are investigated in the framework of the instantaneous BS-equation (Salpeter equation). We parametrize confinement alternatively by a linearly rising scalar or a vector interaction kernel and take into account the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) interaction in the instantaneous approximation. Mass spectra as well as leptonic, two-photon, E1 and M1 decay widths are calculated. Our results show that a reasonable description of the experimental data can be obtained with both spin structures for the confining kernel. The relativistic treatment leads to an improved description compared to nonrelativistic results for the two-photon width of the \(η_c\) and to some extent for the E1-transition widths. However, characteristic deviations indicate that within a relativistic framework confinement is not described adequately by a potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a reasonable description of heavy quarkonia mass spectra and decay widths can be obtained with both scalar and vector confining kernels in the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter (Salpeter) equation, but that characteristic deviations indicate confinement is not adequately described by a potential within a relativistic framework.

## Method

The authors solved the full Salpeter equation (instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation) for charmonium and bottomonium states, using a linearly rising scalar or vector confinement kernel plus a one-gluon-exchange (OGE) interaction in the instantaneous approximation with a running coupling constant regularized at short distances. Decay widths for leptonic, two-photon, E1, and M1 transitions were calculated using the Mandelstam formalism, with a covariant formulation of the Salpeter equation enabling correct treatment of boosts for radiative transitions.

## Key numbers

- Confinement slope \(b_c\) came out much larger than in nonrelativistic models: typical nonrelativistic value is \(b_c \approx 700\) MeV/fm, while semirelativistic models use \(b_c \approx 900-1000\) MeV/fm; the full Salpeter approach leads to further enlargement
- Regularization parameter for the OGE kernel was taken as \(r_0 = 0.1\) fm
- Used \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 200\) MeV and \(n_f = 3\) for the running coupling
- Leptonic decay widths of \(c\bar{c}\) s-wave vector mesons are generally too large by a factor of \(\sim 1.5\) for the \(J/\psi(1S)\) and more for higher radial excitations
- For light mesons, a large value of \(b_c = 1400\) MeV/fm (model V2) was needed to describe higher radial excitations and higher angular momenta \(J > 1\), while ignoring those states allowed a fit with \(b_c = 570\) MeV/fm

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations of heavy quarkonia by Tiemeijer and Tjon, Gara et al., and Murota, who only calculated mass spectra without decay widths. The authors had previously applied an analogous approach to light mesons (Resag and Muenz, 1994) using an instanton-induced interaction instead of OGE. The results enable comparison of scalar versus vector confinement in a relativistic framework, but the authors conclude that the Salpeter approach does not allow a satisfying description of higher radial excitations and higher angular momenta, suggesting that potential-based confinement is inadequate relativistically.
