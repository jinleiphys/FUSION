# Production of the exotic neutron-deficient isotopes near N, Z = 50 in multinucleon transfer reactions

> The multinucleon transfer reaction in the collisions of $^{40}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=128.5$ MeV is investigated by using the improved quantum molecular dynamics model. The measured angular distributions and isotopic distributions of the products are reproduced reasonably well by the calculations. The multinucleon transfer reactions of $^{40}$Ca+$^{112}$Sn, $^{58}$Ni+$^{112}$Sn, $^{106}$Cd+$^{112}$Sn, and $^{48}$Ca+$^{112}$Sn are also studied. It shows that the combinations of neutron-deficient projectile and target are advantageous to produce the exotic neutron-deficient nuclei near $N, Z$ = 50. The charged particles emission plays an important role at small impact parameters in the deexcitation processes of the system. The production cross sections of the exotic neutron-deficient nuclei in multinucleon transfer reactions are much larger than those measured in the fragmentation and fusion-evaporation reactions. Several new neutron-deficient nuclei can be produced in $^{106}$Cd+$^{112}$Sn reaction. The corresponding production cross sections for the new neutron-deficient nuclei, $^{101,102}$Sb, $^{103}$Te, and $^{106,107}$I, are 2.0 nb, 4.1 nb, 6.5 nb, 0.4 $μ$b and 1.0 $μ$b, respectively.

## Key claim

Multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions, specifically the $^{106}$Cd+$^{112}$Sn system at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=500$ MeV, can produce several new neutron-deficient nuclei near $N, Z = 50$ with production cross sections orders of magnitude larger than those from fragmentation or fusion-evaporation reactions, though the calculations are based on a semiclassical model (ImQMD) and the results for the new nuclei are predictions not yet experimentally confirmed.

## Method

The authors used the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, a semiclassical microscopic dynamics model based on effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, to simulate MNT reactions. The dynamical evolution was run for 1000 fm/$c$, after which the deexcitation of primary fragments was treated with the GEMINI code using Hauser-Feshbach theory, including evaporation channels for $n$, $p$, $d$, $t$, $^3$He, $\alpha$, $^6$He, $^{6-8}$Li, and $^{7-10}$Be. The model was first validated against experimental data for $^{40}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=128.5$ MeV, then applied to predict production cross sections for $^{48}$Ca+$^{112}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca+$^{112}$Sn, $^{58}$Ni+$^{112}$Sn, and $^{106}$Cd+$^{112}$Sn systems.

## Key numbers

- Production cross sections for the new neutron-deficient nuclei predicted in $^{106}$Cd+$^{112}$Sn at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=500$ MeV: $^{101}$Sb = 2.0 nb, $^{102}$Sb = 4.1 nb, $^{103}$Te = 6.5 nb, $^{106}$I = 0.4 $\mu$b, $^{107}$I = 1.0 $\mu$b.
- For $^{100}$Sn, the MNT reaction cross section predicted is $1.1\times10^{-3}$ mb, compared to $7.4\times10^{-10}$ mb from fragmentation (345 MeV/A $^{124}$Xe+Be) and $4.0\times10^{-5}$ mb from fusion-evaporation ($^{50}$Cr+$^{58}$Ni at $E_{\textrm{lab}}=255$ MeV).
- For $^{100}$In, the MNT cross section is $3.8\times10^{-2}$ mb, versus $8.6\times10^{-6}$ mb from fragmentation and $1.7\times10^{-3}$ mb from fusion-evaporation ($^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni at $E_{\textrm{lab}}=348$ MeV).
- The Coulomb barrier for $^{40}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn is 120.1 MeV; the reaction was studied at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=128.5$ MeV.
- The $^{106}$Cd+$^{112}$Sn system was studied at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=300$, 500, and 780 MeV; the saturation excitation energy for binary events at $b<5$ fm was about 145 MeV at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}=500$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the ImQMD model, previously used for fusion and fragmentation reactions, to MNT reactions for producing neutron-deficient nuclei near $^{100}$Sn. It directly compares predicted MNT cross sections with experimental data from fragmentation reactions at RIKEN (using a 345 MeV/A $^{124}$Xe beam on a Be target, as reported by Sn6 and Sn2) and fusion-evaporation reactions (e.g., $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni and $^{50}$Cr+$^{58}$Ni). The results suggest that MNT reactions offer a more advantageous route than fragmentation or fusion-evaporation for accessing new neutron-deficient isotopes in this region, and the predicted cross sections for $^{101,102}$Sb, $^{103}$Te, and $^{106,107}$I provide targets for future experimental searches.
