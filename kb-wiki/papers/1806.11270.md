# Quantitative analysis of tensor effects in the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> Tensor force is identified in each meson-nucleon coupling in the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory. It is found that all the meson-nucleon couplings, except the $σ$-scalar one, give rise to the tensor force. The effects of tensor force on various nuclear properties can now be investigated quantitatively, which allows fair and direct comparisons with the corresponding results in the non-relativistic framework. The tensor effects on nuclear binding energies and the evolutions of the $Z,\,N = 8,\,20$, and $28$ magic gaps are studied. The tensor contributions to the binding energies are shown to be tiny in general. The $Z,\,N = 8$ and $20$ gaps are sensitive to the tensor force, but the $Z,\,N = 28$ gaps are not.

## Key claim

The tensor force is identified in all meson-nucleon couplings except the σ-scalar one within the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory, but the quantitative analysis shows that the tensor contributions to nuclear binding energies are tiny in general, and the Z, N = 8 and 20 magic gaps are sensitive to the tensor force while the Z, N = 28 gaps are not.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic reduction of the relativistic two-body interactions in the RHF theory, expanding the operators in powers of 1/M to isolate the central, spin-orbit, and tensor force components in the conventional non-relativistic sense. They then evaluated the tensor contributions to nuclear binding energies and the evolution of magic gaps at Z, N = 8, 20, and 28 using the density-dependent effective interaction PKA1, with the tensor components identified from each meson-nucleon coupling (σ-S, ω-V, ρ-V, ρ-T, ρ-VT, and π-PV).

## Key numbers

- The tensor component from the π-PV coupling is the largest; at zero density with the PKA1 interaction, the ratio of the ω-V tensor strength to π-PV is -0.02 (for nn or pp channels, 0 for np), while at saturation density this ratio becomes -0.74.
- At saturation density with PKA1, the ρ-T tensor strength ratio to π-PV is -0.25, and the ρ-VT ratio is -0.16, while the ρ-V ratio is only -0.03.
- In the zero-density limit using the bare Bonn A interaction, the ω-V tensor ratio to π-PV is -0.02, ρ-T is -0.03, ρ-VT is -0.01, and ρ-V is -0.0009.

## Context

This work extends the earlier qualitative comparisons between relativistic and non-relativistic frameworks for tensor effects on shell evolution, such as those by Tarpanov (2008) and Moreno-Torres (2010), by providing a quantitative method to isolate tensor contributions within RHF theory. It enables direct and fair comparisons with non-relativistic results from Skyrme and Gogny HF theories, and builds on prior RHF studies by Long (2007, 2008) and Wang (2013) that identified the essential role of π-PV and ρ-T couplings in shell evolution.
