# Event-by-event pT fluctuations and multiparticle clusters in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We explore the dependence of the pT correlations in the event-by-event analysis of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC made recently by the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations. We point out that the observed scaling of strength of dynamical fluctuations with the inverse number of particles can be naturally explained by the formation of clusters. We argue that the large magnitude of the measured covariance implies that the clusters contain at least several particles. We also discuss whether the clusters may originate from jets. In addition, we provide numerical estimates of correlations coming from resonance decays and thermal clusters.

# Event-by-event pT fluctuations and multiparticle clusters in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed scaling of dynamical pT fluctuations with inverse multiplicity in RHIC data can be naturally explained by the formation of clusters containing at least several particles, though the physical origin of these clusters (jets, fluid droplets, or other mechanisms) cannot be conclusively determined from the scaling alone.

## Method

The authors analyze PHENIX and STAR event-by-event pT fluctuation data using statistical relations between the variance of average transverse momentum per event, the inclusive momentum variance, and the multiplicity distribution. They derive that the dynamical variance sigma_dyn^2 equals the sum of covariances between particle pairs divided by the square of the multiplicity, then model this covariance term using a cluster picture where particles within the same cluster are correlated. Numerical estimates of covariance from resonance decays and thermal clusters are computed using the single freeze-out model and the Therminator event generator.

## Key numbers

- For PHENIX data at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, sigma_dyn * sqrt(⟨n⟩) is approximately constant across centrality: 60.3 ± 1.6 MeV (0-5% centrality), 59.2 ± 1.5 MeV (0-10%), 59.8 ± 1.2 MeV (10-20%), 57.7 ± 1.1 MeV (20-30%)
- The inclusive momentum variance sigma_p is approximately 290 MeV and the mean transverse momentum ⟨M⟩ is approximately 523 MeV, both constant across the fiducial centrality range 0-30%
- From PHENIX data with estimated detector efficiency a ≈ 10%, the covariance per correlated pair is cov* ≈ 0.035 GeV^2 / (r-1), where r is the number of particles per cluster
- For STAR data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200, 130, 62, and 20 GeV, the product cov*(r-1) equals 0.058, 0.043, 0.035, and 0.014 GeV^2 respectively (assuming a = 0.75)
- Resonance decays produce covariance per pair cov*_res between 0.005 GeV^2 (at low resonance mass ~500 MeV) and -0.015 GeV^2 (at high mass ~1.2 GeV), changing sign around 700-800 MeV
- Thermal cluster calculations for pion pairs give cov*(pi,pi) = 0.0034 GeV^2 for freeze-out at T = 165 MeV with average flow 0.5c, and 0.01 GeV^2 for T = 100 MeV with average flow 0.6c

## Context

This work extends the analysis of event-by-event pT fluctuations measured by PHENIX (Adcox 2002) and STAR (Adams 2003, Adams 2005) at RHIC, providing a statistical framework to interpret the observed scaling behavior. The cluster interpretation connects to earlier work on fluctuations by Gazdzicki (1992), Stodolsky (1995), and Mrowczynski (1997), and addresses the question raised by Adler (2003) and Liu (2003) whether jets could be responsible for the correlations. The results enable future work to distinguish between jet-based and hydrodynamic cluster mechanisms by measuring the beam-energy dependence of the covariance and the number of particles per cluster.
