# Transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions for spin-1 hadrons

> We show transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) for spin-1 hadrons including twist-3 and 4 functions by taking the decomposition of a quark correlation function in the Lorentz-invariant way with the conditions of Hermiticity and parity invariance. We found 30 new TMDs in the tensor-polarized spin-1 hadron at twists 3 and 4 in addition to 10 TMDs at twist 2. Since time-reversal-odd terms of the collinear correlation function should vanish after integrals over the partonic transverse momentum, we obtained new sum rules for the time-reversal-odd structure functions, $\bm{\int d^2 k_T g_{LT} = \int d^2 k_T h_{LL} = \int d^2 k_T h_{3LL}}$$\bm{=0}$, at twists 3 and 4. We also indicated that transverse-momentum-dependent fragmentation functions exist in tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons. The TMDs can probe color degrees of freedom, so that they are valuable in providing unique opportunities for creating interdisciplinary physics fields such as gluon condensate, color Aharonov-Bohm effect, and color entanglement. We also found three new collinear PDFs at twists 3 and 4, and a twist-2 relation and a sum rule were derived in analogy to the Wandzura-Wilczek relation and the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule on the structure function $\bm{g_2}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims to have identified 30 new transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) for tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons at twist 3 and 4, but this result depends on the specific Lorentz-invariant decomposition of the quark correlation function under the conditions of Hermiticity and parity invariance, and the classification is limited to the tensor-polarized sector (LL, LT, TT) of the spin-1 hadron.

## Method

The authors decomposed the quark correlation function for spin-1 hadrons in a Lorentz-invariant way, imposing Hermiticity and parity invariance, and including terms with the lightcone vector to access twist-3 and twist-4 structure functions. They then took traces with various gamma matrices to extract the TMDs from the correlation function, and integrated the TMDs over transverse momentum to obtain collinear PDFs. Sum rules for time-reversal-odd TMDs were derived from the requirement that such terms vanish in the collinear limit.

## Key numbers

* 30 new TMDs were found for tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons at twists 3 and 4, in addition to the 10 TMDs already known at twist 2, for a total of 40 TMDs up to twist 4.
* Three new collinear PDFs were found at twists 3 and 4: \(e_{LL}\) (twist-3), \(f_{LT}\) (twist-3), and \(f_{3LL}\) (twist-4).
* Sum rules were derived for time-reversal-odd TMDs: \(\int d^2 k_T \, g_{LT} = \int d^2 k_T \, h_{LL} = \int d^2 k_T \, h_{3LT} = 0\) at twists 3 and 4.
* A twist-2 relation for \(f_{LT}\) was derived: \(f_{2LT}^+ (x) = -f_{1LL}^+ (x) + \int_x^1 \frac{dy}{y} f_{1LL}^+ (y)\), and a sum rule \(\int_0^1 dx \, f_{2LT}^+(x) = 0\) was obtained.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical formalism of TMDs, previously developed for spin-1/2 nucleons, to spin-1 hadrons such as the deuteron. It builds on the twist-2 formalism for spin-1 TMDs by Bacchetta and Mulders (2000) and on the authors' own recent work (Kumano and Song, 2021) on twist-3 and twist-4 structure functions. The results enable future experimental studies of spin-1 hadron structure at facilities such as Jefferson Lab, Fermilab, NICA, and planned electron-ion colliders, and provide a framework for probing color degrees of freedom through phenomena like the color Aharonov-Bohm effect and color entanglement.
