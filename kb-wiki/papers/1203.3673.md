# g9/2 nuclei and neutron-proton interaction

> We have performed shell-model calculations for nuclei below 100Sn, focusing attention on the two N=Z nuclei 96Cd and 92Pd, the latter having been recently the subject of great experimental and theoretical interest. We have considered nuclei for which the 0g9/2 orbit plays a dominant role and employed a realistic low-momentum two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. This implies that no phenomenological input enters our effective Hamiltonian. The calculated results for 92Pd are in very good agreement with the available experimental data, which gives confidence in our predictions for 96Cd. An analysis of the wave functions of both 96Cd and 92Pd is performed to investigate the role of the isoscalar spin-aligned coupling.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that isoscalar spin-aligned coupling (specifically J=9 neutron-proton pairs) plays a relevant role in the low-energy states of 92Pd and 96Cd, but argue that the evidence does not support interpreting this as a new phase of nuclear matter analogous to the pairing phase induced by identical-particle correlations.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in a single 0g9/2 orbital space for nuclei below 100Sn, using a realistic low-momentum two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the Vlow-k approach (cutoff momentum Λ = 2.1 fm−1). The effective interaction was computed using the Q-box folded-diagram expansion including diagrams up to third order in Vlow-k, with the harmonic oscillator parameter ℏω = 8.55 MeV. The NUSHELLX code was used for the shell-model diagonalizations.

## Key numbers

- The J=0, T=1 np matrix element is −2.317 MeV, the most attractive; the J=9, T=0 np matrix element is −1.402 MeV, comparable in magnitude to the J=1, T=0 value of −1.488 MeV
- For the 16+ isomer in 96Cd, the average number of isoscalar J=9 pairs is 2.5, compared to 2.2 for the 14+ state and 2.0 for the 12+ state
- The overlap of the 0+ ground state of 96Cd with the [(np)9 (np)9]0 state is 90%; for the 2+ state it is 97%; for the 8+ state it drops to 6%
- The 2+–0+ spacing in 92Pd is 1.03 MeV from the full calculation; the 10+–8+ spacing is 1.00 MeV
- The nn matrix elements are more attractive than pp ones by about 250–350 keV due to the Coulomb force
- The calculated energies for the 90Nb multiplet overestimate experimental values by at most 150 keV (for the 2+ state)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier shell-model calculations for N=50 nuclei below 100Sn (Coraggio, 2000) using a modern Vlow-k renormalization approach rather than the earlier G-matrix formalism. It directly addresses the interpretation by Cederwall (2011) that the nearly equidistant levels in 92Pd evidence a spin-aligned np paired phase, and agrees with the analyses of Zerguine (2011), Qi (2011), and Xu (2012) regarding the dominance of J=9, T=0 pairs. The calculations also explain the 16+ "spin-gap" isomer in 96Cd identified by Singh (2011). The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted spectrum for 96Cd and the np multiplet in 98In.
