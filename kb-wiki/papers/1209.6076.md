# Enhanced collectivity in neutron-deficient Sn isotopes in energy functional based collective Hamiltonian

> The low-lying collective states in Sn isotopes are studied by a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian with parameters determined from the triaxial relativistic mean-field calculations using the PC-PK1 energy density functional. The systematics for both the excitation energies of $2^+_1$ states and $B(E2;0^+_1\to 2^+_1)$ values are reproduced rather well, in particular, the enhanced E2 transitions in the neutron-deficient Sn isotopes with N<66. We show that the gradual degeneracy of neutron levels 1g7/2 and 2d5/2 around the Fermi surface leads to the increase of level density and consequently the enhanced paring correlations from N=66 to 58. It provokes a large quadrupole shape fluctuation around the spherical shape, and leads to an enhanced collectivity in the isotopes around N=58.

## Key claim

The enhanced E2 transitions in neutron-deficient Sn isotopes (N<66) arise from increasing quadrupole shape fluctuation around the spherical shape, induced by strengthening neutron pairing correlations; this pairing enhancement is driven by the gradual degeneracy of the neutron 1g7/2 and 2d5/2 levels near the Fermi surface, which increases the level density.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the PC-PK1 energy density functional for the particle-hole channel and a separable force for the particle-particle channel, with the Dirac spinors expanded in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis of 12 major shells. Intrinsic triaxially deformed states were obtained from self-consistent RMF+BCS equations constrained on the Bohr deformation parameters (β, γ) over β ∈ [0.0, 0.6] and γ ∈ [0°, 60°] with step sizes Δβ = 0.05 and Δγ = 10°. A five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) was then constructed from these constrained states, using cranking approximation to determine the collective potential, mass parameters, and moments of inertia, and diagonalized to obtain excitation energies and B(E2) transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The 5DCH calculation reproduces the increase of B(E2; 0⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) from ¹¹⁶Sn down to ¹⁰⁸Sn, which the shell model cannot describe.
- For ¹¹⁶Sn, the rms deviation for seven low-lying excitation states (first two 0⁺, three 2⁺, and two 4⁺) is σ = 0.279 MeV for the 5DCH, compared to 0.429 MeV using the Nijmegen1 effective interaction and 0.137 MeV in another shell-model calculation.
- The 5DCH overestimates the B(E2) strength by approximately 0.1 e²b² when approaching the neutron shell closure.
- The discrepancy between the 5DCH result and data for ¹²⁰–¹³⁰Sn ranges from about 200 keV to about 1 MeV in excitation energy of the 2⁺₁ state.

## Context

This work extends the application of the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on covariant density functional theory (CDFT) to the semi-magic Sn isotopes for the first time, following successful descriptions of shape transitions in other isotopic and isotonic chains by Li et al. (2009) and Niksic et al. (2009). The results contradict the parabolic trend predicted by the single j-shell seniority model (Talmi, 1993) and earlier shell-model calculations (Banu et al., 2005; Eks. 2008), which could not reproduce the enhanced B(E2) values in neutron-deficient Sn isotopes. The mechanism identified here, increased quadrupole shape fluctuation from strengthened pairing, is consistent with the interpretation from the quasiparticle-phonon model (Iud. 2011) but is expressed in the language of the mean-field approach, and the study enables future systematic investigations of low-lying collective states in other semi-magic chains using the same fully self-consistent, parameter-free framework.
