# Quarkonium suppression from coherent energy loss in fixed-target experiments using LHC beams

> Quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions is a powerful tool to disentangle cold nuclear matter effects. A model based on coherent energy loss is able to explain the available quarkonium suppression data in a broad range of rapidities, from fixed-target to collider energies, suggesting cold energy loss to be the dominant effect in quarkonium suppression in p-A collisions. This could be further tested in a high-energy fixed-target experiment using a proton or nucleus beam. The nuclear modification factors of J/$ψ$ and $Υ$ as a function of rapidity are computed in p-A collisions at $\sqrt{s}=114.6$ GeV, and in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=72$ GeV. These center-of-mass energies correspond to the collision on fixed-target nuclei of 7 TeV protons and 2.76 TeV lead nuclei available at the LHC.

## Key claim

The coherent energy loss model, which has already been shown to reproduce quarkonium suppression data from fixed-target to collider energies using a single free parameter, predicts that the nuclear modification factors of J/ψ and Υ in fixed-target collisions using LHC beams will exhibit moderate suppression (less than 20% for J/ψ) at forward rapidities and enhancement at very negative rapidities, though nuclear absorption may affect the results in the negative rapidity region where the quarkonium formation time becomes comparable to the nuclear medium length.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear modification factor RpA for J/ψ and Υ production using a coherent energy loss model where the p–A cross section is obtained by convoluting the p–p cross section with an energy loss probability distribution (quenching weight) derived from medium-induced coherent gluon radiation. The quenching weight depends on the accumulated transverse momentum transfer ℓA = √(q̂ L), where the transport coefficient q̂ is parameterized as q̂ = q̂0 [10⁻²/min(x0, x₂)]⁰·³, with q̂0 = 0.075 GeV²/fm determined from a fit to E866 p–W data at √s = 38.7 GeV. The p–p cross sections are parameterized as dσpp/dy ∝ (1 − 2M⊥/√s cosh y)^n, with exponents n interpolated from FNAL and RHIC data.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ suppression in p–Pb collisions at √s = 114.6 GeV is less than 20% and does not vary strongly with rapidity except at y < −2 where enhancement (RpA > 1) appears
- The critical rapidity y^crit below which nuclear absorption may affect results is approximately y^crit ≃ −1 for p–A collisions at √s = 114.6 GeV
- The crossing point where RpA(y₀) = 1 occurs at y₀ ≃ −1 for Υ, which is approximately one unit larger than for J/ψ, consistent with the expected difference y₀^Υ − y₀^J/ψ ∼ ln(M^Υ/M^J/ψ) ≃ 1.1
- In Pb–Pb collisions at √s = 72 GeV, the condition for hadronization outside both nuclei (y^crit < y < −y^crit) is only met around mid-rapidity, |y| ≲ 0.1
- The transport coefficient is q̂₀ = 0.075^{+0.015}_{-0.005} GeV²/fm at x = 10⁻²
- The medium lengths used are L = 1.5 fm for p, 5.69 fm for Ca, 6.67 fm for Cu, and 10.11 fm for Pb

## Context

This work extends the coherent energy loss model previously developed by Arleo and Peigné (2012, 2013) to predict quarkonium suppression in proposed fixed-target experiments using LHC beams, combining the advantages of fixed-target (high luminosity, large rapidity coverage) and collider (high energy) experiments. The model has already successfully described J/ψ and Υ suppression data from NA3, E866, RHIC, and LHC experiments, and these predictions provide testable baselines for the proposed LHC fixed-target program (Brodsky et al., 2012). The results also serve as cold nuclear matter baselines for heavy-ion collisions, where additional hot medium effects (Debye screening) are expected to cause further suppression.
