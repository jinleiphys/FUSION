# Survival rate of initial azimuthal anisotropy in a multi-phase transport model

> We investigate the survival rate of an initial momentum anisotropy (${v}_2^{ini}$), not spatial anisotropy, to the final state in a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200~GeV. It is found that both the final-state parton and charged hadron $v_2$ show a linear dependence versus $v_2^{ini}\{\rm PP\}$ with respect to the participant plane (PP). It is found that the slope of this linear dependence (referred to as the survive rate) increases with transverse momentum ($p_T$), reaching~$\sim$100\% at $p_T$$\sim$2.5 GeV/c for both parton and charged hadron. The survival rate decreases with collision centrality and energy, indicating decreasing survival rate with increasing interactions. It is further found that a $v_2^{ini}\{\rm Rnd\}$ with respect to a random direction does not survive in $v_2\{\rm PP\}$ but in the two-particle cumulant $v_2\{2\}$. The dependence of $v_2\{2\}$ on $v_2^{ini}\{\rm Rnd\}$ is quadratic rather than linear.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an initial momentum anisotropy (not spatial anisotropy) can survive to the final state in Au+Au collisions, with the survival rate reaching approximately 100% at high transverse momentum (~2.5 GeV/c), but this survival rate is conditional on the initial anisotropy being aligned with the participant plane; an initial anisotropy with respect to a random direction does not survive in the participant-plane flow measurement but does survive in the two-particle cumulant measurement.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting and 3 mb parton cross section to simulate Au+Au collisions. They artificially introduced an initial momentum anisotropy by rotating each parton's azimuthal angle according to a prescribed distribution, either aligned with the participant plane (PP) or with a random direction (Rnd). They then tracked the final-state parton and charged hadron elliptic flow (v2) as a function of transverse momentum, collision centrality, and beam energy, fitting the relationship between initial and final v2 with linear or quadratic functions to extract the survival rate.

## Key numbers

- Survival rate reaches ~100% at pT ~2.5 GeV/c for both partons and charged hadrons in b=8 fm Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- For partons in the highest pT bin studied, the survival rate can exceed unity (greater than 100%)
- The survival rate decreases with increasing collision energy and increasing centrality (decreasing impact parameter)
- For v2{2} with random-plane initial anisotropy, the relationship is quadratic: v2{2} = sqrt(v2{PP}(0)^2 + r^2 * v2^{ini}{Rnd}^2)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of initial momentum anisotropy in heavy ion collisions, which has been argued to arise from the Heisenberg uncertainty principle (Wang, 2014), classical Yang-Mills dynamics (Schenke, 2015), and color reconnection in proton-proton collisions (Ortiz, 2013). The results suggest that comparisons of data to hydrodynamic calculations that assume zero initial momentum anisotropy may not be reliable for extracting transport properties such as the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s). The authors note that repeating this study using hydrodynamics with non-zero initial momentum anisotropy would be an important next step.
