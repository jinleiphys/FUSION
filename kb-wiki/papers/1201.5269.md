# Phase space structure and dynamics within the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach

> We study the equilibration and relaxation processes within the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach using the Wigner distribution function. On the technical side we present a geometrically unrestricted framework which allows us to calculate the full six-dimensional Wigner distribution function. With the removal of geometrical constraints, we are now able to extend our previous phase-space analysis of heavy-ion collisions in the reaction plane to unrestricted mean-field simulations of nuclear matter on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice. From the physical point of view we provide a quantitative analysis on the stopping power in TDHF. This is linked to the effect of transparency. For the medium-heavy $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca system we examine the impact of different parametrizations of the Skyrme force, energy-dependence, and the significance of extra time-odd terms in the Skyrme functional. For the first time, transparency in TDHF is observed for a heavy system, $^{24}$Mg+$^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach, when analyzed via the full six-dimensional Wigner distribution function, shows that the two colliding fragments never truly combine to form a single phase-space distribution, indicating that two-body collisions beyond mean-field are necessary to achieve true equilibrium; transparency in TDHF is observed for the first time for a heavy system (24Mg+208Pb), but the degree of relaxation depends on the Skyrme force parametrization, center-of-mass energy, and the inclusion of time-odd terms.

## Method

The authors solved the TDHF equations on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice with a typical mesh spacing of 1 fm, using a Taylor-series expansion of the time-evolution operator up to sixth order with a time step of 0.2 fm/c. They computed the full six-dimensional Wigner distribution function from the density matrix via partial Fourier transform, employing Open MP parallelization and fast Fourier transforms. Global observables including the quadrupole deformation in momentum space (beta_k, gamma_k), the occupied phase-space volume V_k(t), and the coordinate-space quadrupole Q^{20}_r(t) were used to quantify relaxation processes in central and non-central collisions.

## Key numbers

* For a central 40Ca+40Ca collision at E_c.m. = 160 MeV, the maximum beta_k values for different Skyrme forces ranged from 0.0095 (SkI3, effective mass m*/m = 0.57) to 0.0116 (SkM*, effective mass m*/m = 0.79).
* The energy threshold for feed-through in central 24Mg+208Pb reactions was identified at E_c.m. approximately 350 MeV.
* For the 24Mg+208Pb system at E_c.m. = 350 MeV, a non-central collision with impact parameter b = 4 fm was compared to the central case.
* The Wigner function for one time step on a 24^2 x 36 grid for 16O+16O required approximately 140 Mb of disk space when reduced to the reaction plane.
* Center-of-mass energies for 40Ca+40Ca were varied from 2 MeV/nucleon (E_c.m. = 160 MeV) up to 3 MeV/nucleon (E_c.m. = 240 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous phase-space analyses of heavy-ion collisions that were restricted to one or two dimensions (Loebl, 2010; Maruhn, 1985) by providing a geometrically unrestricted framework for calculating the full six-dimensional Wigner distribution function. The study enables quantitative analysis of stopping power and transparency in TDHF, and the authors note that a systematic investigation of impact parameter and energy dependence for even heavier systems would be highly interesting but was beyond computational feasibility at the time.
