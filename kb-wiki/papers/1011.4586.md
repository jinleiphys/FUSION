# Dark Atoms of the Universe: towards OHe nuclear physics

> The nonbaryonic dark matter of the Universe is assumed to consist of new stable particles. A specific case is possible, when new stable particles bear ordinary electric charge and bind in heavy "atoms" by ordinary Coulomb interaction. Such possibility is severely restricted by the constraints on anomalous isotopes of light elements that form positively charged heavy species with ordinary electrons. The trouble is avoided, if stable particles $X^{--}$ with charge -2 are in excess over their antiparticles (with charge +2) and there are no stable particles with charges +1 and -1. Then primordial helium, formed in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, captures all $X^{--}$ in neutral "atoms" of O-helium (OHe). Schrodinger equation for system of nucleus and OHe is considered and reduced to an equation of relative motion in a spherically symmetrical potential, formed by the Yukawa tail of nuclear scalar isoscalar attraction potential, acting on He beyond the nucleus, and dipole Coulomb repulsion between the nucleus and OHe at small distances between nuclear surfaces of He and nucleus. The values of coupling strength and mass of $σ$-meson, mediating scalar isoscalar nuclear potential, are rather uncertain. Within these uncertainties and in the approximation of rectangular potential wells and wall we find a range of these parameters, at which the sodium nuclei have a few keV binding energy with OHe. The result also strongly depend on the precise value of parameter $d_o$ that determines the size of nuclei. At nuclear parameters, reproducing DAMA results, OHe-nucleus bound states can exist only for intermediate nuclei, thus excluding direct comparison with these results in detectors, containing very light (e.g. $^3He$) and heavy nuclei (like Xe).

## Key claim

Within the uncertainties of the scalar isoscalar nuclear potential parameters (sigma-meson mass and coupling) and using a rectangular well approximation, there exists a parameter range where sodium nuclei form a few keV bound state with O-helium, while iodine and thallium do not; however, the result is highly sensitive to the precise value of the nuclear size parameter d_o and to the treatment of the helium position in OHe, so the authors present their findings only as an illustration rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

The Schrodinger equation for the OHe-nucleus system was reduced to an equation of relative motion in a spherically symmetrical potential. This potential was approximated by a rectangular well consisting of: an inner well at r < R (nuclear radius), a dipole Coulomb barrier at R ≤ r < R + r_o + r_he, and an outer well formed by the Yukawa tail of the scalar isoscalar nuclear attraction potential and residual Coulomb interaction. The nuclear potential was taken as a Yukawa potential mediated by sigma-meson exchange, with the helium shell treated either as averaged over its orbit in OHe (case "m") or as fixed at the position closest to the nucleus (case "b"). The wave functions in each region were sewn together to determine the condition for a low-energy bound state.

## Key numbers

* OHe ionization potential: I_o ≈ 1.6 MeV (from equation with Z_x=2, Z_He=2, alpha, m_He)
* OHe size: R_o ≈ 2 × 10^(-13) cm (from equation with Z_x=2, Z_He=2, alpha, m_He)
* OHe-nucleus cross section: sigma_o ≈ 10^(-25) cm^2
* Sodium-OHe bound state energy: 4 keV (the target value used to explain DAMA results)
* Nuclear size parameter: d_o = 1.2/(200 MeV) as the experimentally most probable value, with uncertainty range 1.15/(200 MeV) < d_o < 1.3/(200 MeV)
* OHe mass taken as m_o = 1 TeV (results weakly depend on m_o > 1 TeV)
* For the case of nuclear Yukawa potential U_{3b} (He closest to nucleus), the range of nuclei with OHe bound states corresponds to the part of the periodic table between B and Ti
* For the case of potential U_{3m} (averaged over He orbit), there are no OHe bound states with nuclei lighter than Be or heavier than Ti

## Context

This work extends the O-helium (OHe) composite dark matter scenario, previously developed by the same authors and collaborators (Khlopov 2006, Khlopov 2008, Bled Workshop 2009), by providing a more detailed nuclear physics treatment of OHe-nucleus interactions. The paper aims to explain the positive results of DAMA/NaI and DAMA/LIBRA experiments (Bernabei 2003, Bernabei 2008) via radiative capture of OHe by sodium nuclei, while remaining consistent with null results from other experiments. The key finding that OHe-nucleus bound states exist only for intermediate nuclei (excluding very light nuclei like He-3 and heavy nuclei like xenon) implies that direct comparison with DAMA results in detectors with different compositions is not straightforward, and testing the DAMA claim becomes a nontrivial task. The authors note that their results are sensitive to the sigma-meson parameters, which are constrained by the relativistic sigma-omega model for nuclear matter (referenced as "nuclear" in the text).
