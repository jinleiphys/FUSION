# Varying the chiral magnetic effect relative to flow in a single nucleus-nucleus collision

> We propose a novel method to search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy ion collisions. We argue that the relative strength of the magnetic field (mainly from spectator protons and responsible for the CME) with respect to the reaction plane and the participant plane is opposite to that of the elliptic flow background arising from the fluctuating participant geometry. This opposite behavior in a single collision system, hence with small systematic uncertainties, can be exploited to extract the possible CME signal from the flow background. The method is applied to the existing data at RHIC, the outcome of which is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the relative strength of the magnetic field (mainly from spectator protons) with respect to the reaction plane versus the participant plane is opposite to that of the elliptic flow background, and this opposite behavior within a single collision system can be exploited to extract the chiral magnetic effect signal from the flow background with small systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors use Monte Carlo Glauber (MCG) calculations and A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model simulations (v2.26t5, string melting mode) for Au+Au, Cu+Cu, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr at RHIC and Pb+Pb at LHC energies. They compute the relative differences in eccentricity, magnetic field squared, and elliptic flow when measured with respect to the reaction plane versus the participant plane (or event plane), and apply the formalism to existing STAR data on the three-point azimuthal correlator Δγ.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference R_PP(ε₂) for Au+Au at RHIC ranges from approximately -0.240 (50-60% centrality) to -0.147 (20-30% centrality) in MCG calculations
- The relative difference R_EP(v₂) for Au+Au at RHIC ranges from approximately -0.478 (50-60% centrality) to -0.197 (20-30% centrality) in AMPT simulations
- The experimental relative difference R_exp(v₂) between v₂ measured by ZDC (first-order event plane) and FTPC (second-order event plane) in 200A GeV Au+Au collisions ranges from -0.19 (50-60% centrality) to -0.08 (20-30% centrality)
- The Δγ correlator measurements for 200A GeV Au+Au at 20-60% centrality are (2.067±0.007)×10⁻⁴ with respect to ψ₂ and (2.19±0.24)×10⁻⁴ with respect to ψ₁
- The nucleon-nucleon cross sections used are 42 mb for RHIC collisions and 62.4 mb for LHC Pb+Pb collisions
- The proton radius used in magnetic field calculations is 0.88 fm

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions, which has been pursued at RHIC and LHC through measurements of charge-dependent azimuthal correlations (Voloshin 2004). The method contrasts with the proposed isobaric Ru+Ru versus Zr+Zr comparison (Voloshin 2010), which the authors previously showed (Xu 2017) may suffer from large uncertainties due to nuclear density deviations from Woods-Saxon distributions. The approach enables extraction of the CME signal fraction from existing data without requiring comparisons between different collision systems, and the authors identify that improved statistics in first-order event plane measurements (via ZDC) are needed for meaningful constraints.
