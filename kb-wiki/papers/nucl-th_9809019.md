# Band Structures of $^{182}$ Os Studied by GCM based on 3D-CHFB

> Band structure properties of $^{182}$Os are investigated through a particle number and angular momentum constrained generator coordinate(GCM) calculation based on self-consistent three-dimensional cranking solutions. From the analysis of the wave function of the lowest GCM solution, we confirm that this nucleus shows a tilted rotational motion in its yrast states, at least with the present set of force parameters of the pairing-plus-quadrupole interaction Hamiltonian. A close examination of behavior of other GCM solutions reveals a sign of a possible occurrence of multi-band crossing in the nucleus. Furthermore, in the course of calculations, we have also found a new potential curve along the prime meridian on the globe of the $J=18\hbar$ sphere. Along this new solution the characters of proton and neutron gap parameters get interchanged. Namely, $Δ_p$ almost vanishes while $Δ_n$ grows to a finite value close to the one corresponding to the principal axis rotation(PAR). A state in the new solution curve at the PAR point turns out to have almost the same characteristic features of an yrare $s$-band state which gets located just above the $g$-band in our calculation. This fact suggests a new type of seesaw vibrational mode of the proton and the neutron pairing, occurring through a wobbling motion. The mode is considered to bridge the $g$-band states and the $s$-band states in the backbending region.

# Band Structures of $^{182}$Os Studied by GCM based on 3D-CHFB

## Key claim

The authors confirm that $^{182}$Os shows tilted rotational motion in its yrast states, at least with the present set of force parameters of the pairing-plus-quadrupole interaction Hamiltonian. A new potential curve along the prime meridian on the globe of the $J=18\hbar$ sphere is found, along which the proton and neutron gap parameters get interchanged, suggesting a new type of seesaw vibrational mode of proton and neutron pairing occurring through a wobbling motion that may bridge the g-band and s-band states in the backbending region.

## Method

The authors perform a particle-number and angular-momentum constrained generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation based on self-consistent three-dimensional cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (3D-CHFB) solutions for $^{182}$Os. The Hill-Wheeler equation is modified to include constraints on the expectation values of the squared angular momentum and particle number operators, using the north and south latitude angles along the prime meridian as the generator coordinate. The model Hamiltonian is a pairing-plus-quadrupole (Q-Q) force, and the force parameters are adjusted so that neutron rotation alignment occurs in the principal axis rotation (PAR) states.

## Key numbers

- Ground state deformation parameters: $\beta = 0.280$, $\Delta_p = 1.040$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 0.925$ MeV
- Band crossing between g-band and s-band occurs at $J \sim 20\hbar$
- At the crossing point in the g-band: proton gap parameter $\Delta_p = 0.87$ MeV, neutron gap parameter almost vanishes
- $\gamma$-deformation at band crossing: $\gamma_g = 7.36^{\circ}$ in the g-band, $\gamma_s = -10.19^{\circ}$ in the s-band
- TAR minimum occurs at $\psi = 24^{\circ}$ with depth $V_{TAR} = -0.166$ MeV
- First phase transition at $\psi = 10^{\circ}$ with energy $V_{first} = 0.189$ MeV
- Second phase transition at $\psi = 42^{\circ}$ with energy $V_{second} = 0.470$ MeV
- Energy at north pole: $V_{n-pole} = -0.167$ MeV
- Energy difference between yrast g-band state and yrare s-band state at $J = 18\hbar$: $E_{g-s} \sim 211$ keV
- Excitation energy of new solution at PAR point: $E_{tilt-back} \sim 164$ keV
- $\gamma$-deformation at $J = 18\hbar$: yrast state $\gamma = 5.88^{\circ}$, yrare state $\gamma = -9.77^{\circ}$
- Angular momentum fluctuation in cranking wave function: $\Delta J^2 \sim 100\hbar^2$
- Constrained value of squared angular momentum for $J = 18\hbar$ state: $J^2 \sim 420\hbar^2$
- Chemical potentials in regular region: $\mu^{(1)} = 6.20 \times 10^{-3}$, $\lambda_p^{(1)} = 4.35$ MeV, $\lambda_n^{(1)} = 3.66$ MeV
- Most probable tilt-angle in lowest GCM solution: $\psi = 12^{\circ}$, with range $12^{\circ} \sim 22^{\circ}$

## Context

This work extends previous 3D-CHFB studies of $^{182}$Os by Horibata et al. (1994, 1996) that found evidence for tilted axis rotation (TAR) states, and addresses difficulties in earlier GCM calculations by Horibata et al. (1995) which suffered from large energy splittings and unstable solutions at high angular momentum. The constrained GCM method follows the approach of Bonche (1990) and improves upon the authors' previous one-dimensional Hill-Wheeler integration by incorporating angular momentum and particle number constraints. The results suggest a new seesaw vibrational mode connecting g-band and s-band states in the backbending region, which could be further investigated using angular momentum projection techniques and two-dimensional Hill-Wheeler integration as proposed by Oi et al. (1998).
