# Production of charm quark/antiquark pairs at LHC

> We discuss charm production at LHC. The production of single $c \bar c$ pairs is calculated in the $k_t$-factorization approach. We use several unintegrated gluon distributions from the literature. The hadronization is included with the help of fragmentation functions found for the production of charmed mesons in $e^+ e^-$ collisions. Differential distributions for several charmed mesons are presented and compared to recent results of the ALICE and LHCb collaborations. Furthermore we discuss production of two pairs of $c \bar c$ within a simple formalism of double-parton scattering (DPS). Surprisingly large cross sections, comparable to single-parton scattering (SPS) contribution, are predicted for LHC energies. Both total inclusive cross section as a function of energy and differential distributions are shown. We include recently discussed evolution of double partons in the case of two scales. We discuss perspectives how to identify the double scattering contribution. We find much larger cross section for large rapidity distance between charm quarks from different hard parton scatterings compared to single scattering.

## Key claim

The double-parton scattering (DPS) contribution to inclusive charm production at LHC energies is surprisingly large and comparable to the single-parton scattering (SPS) contribution, but this result depends on the specific choice of unintegrated gluon distribution (the KMR model) and on the assumption of a factorized DPS formalism with an effective cross section of 15 mb.

## Method

The production of single \(c \bar c\) pairs was calculated in the \(k_t\)-factorization approach using several unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDFs) from the literature, with hadronization included via fragmentation functions from \(e^+ e^-\) collisions. Double-parton scattering production of two \(c \bar c\) pairs was computed using a simple probabilistic DPS formalism with the effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff} = 15\) mb, including recently discussed evolution of double parton distributions for two different scales. The results were compared to ALICE and LHCb data at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV, the DPS cross section for \(c \bar c c \bar c\) production is comparable to the SPS contribution, a new situation at LHC energies.
- The charm quark mass was taken as \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV, with uncertainties explored in the range \(m_c \in (1.2, 1.8)\) GeV.
- The effective cross section for DPS was taken as \(\sigma_{eff} = 15\) mb, based on Tevatron data.
- At low energies, the SPS production of \(c \bar c c \bar c\) is more than two orders of magnitude smaller than single \(c \bar c\) production.
- The KMR model of UGDFs gives a very good description of both ALICE and LHCb transverse momentum spectra, while other UGDF parametrizations clearly underestimate the experimental data points.

## Context

This work extends the \(k_t\)-factorization approach to charm production at LHC energies, comparing several UGDF models (including KMR) against ALICE and LHCb data. It also extends the DPS formalism, previously applied to double gauge boson production (GS2010) and recently generalized to different scales (C11), to the case of double charm pair production. The results enable experimental searches for DPS signatures, such as events with two \(D^0\) mesons or two \(\bar D^0\) mesons, and suggest that unitarity effects or parton saturation may need to be included at higher energies.
