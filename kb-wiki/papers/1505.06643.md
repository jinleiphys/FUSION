# Possible existence of a meson ($s\bar s$) S = 0 at M $\approx$ 762 MeV

> The possible existence of a ($ s\bar s$) S = 0 meson at M $\approx$ 762 MeV is discussed through a critical analysis of the existing data. Different experimental results are considered and show the possibility that the presence of such meson is not excluded by the data, but may be hidden by more excited mesons at nearby masses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a previously unobserved isoscalar pseudoscalar meson with a dominant \(s\bar s\) quark content and spin S=0 likely exists at a mass of approximately 762 MeV, but that it has been hidden in existing experimental data because its mass is close to that of the \(\omega\) meson and its decay modes are similar to those of the \(\eta\) meson, leading to misidentification.

## Method

The authors apply a simple hyperfine-splitting model using constituent quark masses (m(u)=309 MeV, m(s)=480 MeV, coupling constant c=159 MeV) to calculate the masses of low-lying mesons via the spin-spin interaction formula \(m(q\bar q) = m_1 + m_2 + A \vec{S}_1\cdot\vec{S}_2 / m_1 m_2\). They then re-examine published experimental data from proton-antiproton annihilation at LEAR (Crystal Barrel Collaboration), \(e^+e^-\) collider data from VEPP-2M, and LHC calorimeter calibration data, looking for a two-photon invariant-mass signal near 762 MeV that had previously been attributed to a missing photon from \(\omega\) decay.

## Key numbers

* The predicted mass of the proposed \(s\bar s\) S=0 meson from the hyperfine model is 762.3 MeV, using constituent quark masses m(u)=309 MeV, m(s)=480 MeV, and c=159 MeV.
* A second rough mass relation assuming \(a + b S(S+1)\) variation gives M(S=0) \(\approx\) 767 MeV for the same meson.
* From the Crystal Barrel two-photon scatter plot, the mean value of three extracted masses is M \(\approx\) 761 MeV (individual values: 768, 762, and 753 MeV).
* The relative amplitudes from a fit to \(\pi^0/\eta\)/"M"/\(\eta'\) in \(p\bar p \to \pi^+\pi^-\gamma\gamma\) events are approximately 490/40/1.5/1 respectively.
* The predicted total width of the "M" meson, obtained by interpolation from the widths of other \(\eta\) mesons, is \(\Gamma \approx\) 18 keV.
* The pseudoscalar mixing angle calculated using the "M" mass of 762 MeV is \(\theta_{PS} = 22.77^\circ\), yielding a wavefunction composition of M = 0.153 \((u\bar u + d\bar d) - 0.976 (s\bar s)\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing puzzle of the "missing" low-mass \(s\bar s\) pseudoscalar meson, which was noted as problematic in the relativized quark model by Godfrey and Isgur and in reviews of light meson spectroscopy by Amsler and collaborators. The paper contradicts the standard interpretation that signals near 760 MeV in two-photon invariant-mass spectra from \(p\bar p\) annihilation are entirely due to \(\omega\) mesons with an undetected soft photon, arguing instead that the data are consistent with a new state. If confirmed, this meson would complete the pseudoscalar nonet and resolve the mixing-angle difficulties for pseudoscalar mesons, enabling a consistent description of the SU(3) wavefunction mixtures for the \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) system.
