# Quasielastic Scattering at MiniBooNE Energies

> We present our description of neutrino induced charged current quasielastic scattering (CCQE) in nuclei at energies relevant for the MiniBooNE experiment. In our framework, the nucleons, with initial momentum distributions according to the Local Fermi Gas model, move in a density- and momentum-dependent mean field potential. The broadening of the outgoing nucleons due to nucleon-nucleon interactions is taken into account by spectral functions. Long range (RPA) correlations renormalizing the electroweak strength in the medium are also incorporated. The background from resonance excitation events that do not lead to pions in the final state is also predicted by propagating the outgoing hadrons with the Giessen semiclassical BUU model in coupled channels (GiBUU). We achieve a good description of the shape of the CCQE Q2 distribution extracted from data by MiniBooNE, thanks to the inclusion of RPA correlations, but underestimate the integrated cross section when the standard value of MA = 1 GeV is used. Possible reasons for this mismatch are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a good description of the shape of the MiniBooNE CCQE Q² distribution using a realistic nuclear model with RPA correlations and the standard axial mass M_A = 1 GeV, but they underestimate the integrated cross section by about 40% compared to the MiniBooNE value, suggesting that the experimental result may not be model independent.

## Method

The authors model neutrino-induced charged-current quasielastic scattering on carbon-12 using a Local Fermi Gas model with density- and momentum-dependent mean field potentials, spectral functions for outgoing nucleon broadening from nucleon-nucleon interactions, and long-range RPA correlations that renormalize the electroweak strength in the medium. They also predict the background from resonance excitation events (primarily Delta(1232) excitation followed by pion absorption) that mimic CCQE events, using the Giessen semiclassical BUU model in coupled channels (GiBUU) to propagate outgoing hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Integrated CCQE cross section from the full model: ⟨σ⟩ = 3.2 × 10⁻³⁸ cm² on ¹²C averaged over the MiniBooNE flux
- MiniBooNE integrated CCQE cross section: ⟨σ⟩ = 5.65 × 10⁻³⁸ cm² with 10.8% error
- Varying the Lorentz-Lorenz factor g' between 0.5 and 0.7 changes the integrated cross section to ⟨σ⟩ = 3.1–3.4 × 10⁻³⁸ cm²
- Fraction of fake CCQE background from Delta excitation: 10% of total CCQE-like events (compared to MiniBooNE Monte Carlo prediction of 12%)
- Standard axial mass used: M_A = 1 GeV, consistent with deuterium data (M_A = 1.0137 ± 0.0264 GeV) and NOMAD high-energy data (M_A = 1.05 ± 0.02 ± 0.06 GeV)
- MiniBooNE shape-only fit obtained M_A = 1.23 ± 0.20 GeV with κ = 1.019 ± 0.011; reanalysis with CC1π data gave M_A = 1.35 ± 0.17 GeV with κ = 1.007 ± 0.007

## Context

This work extends the Local Fermi Gas approach of Leitner (2008) by incorporating RPA correlations following the method of Nieves (2004), which had been successfully applied to photo- and electro-nuclear reactions and to low-energy LSND CCQE measurements. The model contradicts the modified Smith-Moniz Global Fermi Gas analysis used by MiniBooNE (2007) and Katori (2009), which required an enhanced axial mass M_A ≈ 1.2–1.35 GeV to fit data, while the present work achieves shape agreement with M_A = 1 GeV but underestimates the cross section. This discrepancy is common to other realistic models such as those of Meloni and Amaro (2006) that use scaling functions from electron scattering, and the authors suggest that meson exchange currents or model dependence in the MiniBooNE background subtraction may resolve the tension.
