# Is Large Weak Mixing in Heavy Nuclei Consistent with Atomic Experiments?

## Key claim
The hypothesis that the weak interaction matrix element mixing opposite-parity nuclear levels in heavy nuclei is as large as ~100 eV is inconsistent with the upper limits derived from atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) experiments. The authors show that existing atomic PNC measurements in lead already constrain the relevant weak matrix element to below 14 eV, contradicting the ~100 eV value needed to explain common-sign neutron asymmetries.

## Method
The authors extract an upper limit on the weak mixing matrix element in ²⁰⁷Pb by combining experimental data on nuclear-spin-dependent (NSD) PNC optical activity in atomic lead vapour with theoretical calculations of the anapole moment constant κ. They relate the dimensionless P-odd nuclear interaction constant g (derived from the atomic experiment) to the weak matrix element ⟨3d 5/2⁺|W|2f 5/2⁻⟩ using a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit interaction, obtaining a direct bound on the single-particle weak mixing.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit on the relative magnitude of NSD optical activity in atomic lead: P_NSD/P < 0.02 (95% confidence level)
- Upper limit on the anapole constant for ²⁰⁷Pb: κ(²⁰⁷Pb) < 1
- Upper limit on the effective neutron PNC constant: g_n < 10
- Calculated weak matrix element for the ²⁰⁷Pb transition: ⟨3d 5/2⁺|W|2f 5/2⁻⟩ = 1.4 g_n eV
- Resulting upper limit on the weak matrix element: ⟨3d 5/2⁺|W|2f 5/2⁻⟩ < 14 eV
- The proposed experiment sensitivity for the M4 γ-transition in ²⁰⁷Pb is expected to reach 5–13 eV

## Context
This work contradicts the hypothesis that weak mixing matrix elements in heavy nuclei can be as large as ~100 eV, which had been invoked to explain the common sign of PNC asymmetries in epithermal neutron scattering from ²³²Th (observed by Frankle et al., 1992) and large P-odd correlations in Mössbauer transitions in ¹¹⁹Sn and ⁵⁷Fe (Tsinoev et al., 1991). The authors extend the approach of Sushkov and Baranov (1994), who proposed a direct nuclear experiment to measure the weak mixing, by showing that atomic PNC experiments on lead vapour (Meekhof et al., 1993) already provide a comparable bound. This enables a cross-check between atomic and nuclear PNC measurements in the same nucleus, and the proposed ²⁰⁷Pb γ-transition experiment would be the first such comparison.
