# How transverse momentum conservation breaks azimuthal correlation factorization

> The breakdown of azimuthal two-particle correlation factorization, quantified by the ratios $r_2$ and $r_3$, serves as a sensitive probe of transverse-momentum-dependent flow fluctuations. While hydrodynamic models predict $r_3 \leq 1$, experimental data from CMS in p-Pb collisions exhibit $r_3 > 1$, presenting a clear puzzle. We show that transverse momentum conservation (TMC) is the key mechanism dictating this factorization breakdown in small systems. We systematically calculate the effect of TMC as a function of the momentum difference between particles across various multiplicity and momentum ranges. Our results are in quantitative agreement with CMS p-Pb data for both $r_2$ and $r_3$. A central finding is a sign rule: under TMC, the deviation $r_n - 1$ follows $\left ( - 1 \right )^{n+1} $, being negative for even and positive for odd harmonic orders $n$. This work establishes an analytical framework to quantify transverse-momentum-dependent flow fluctuations and provides new insights into the origin of collectivity in small colliding systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transverse momentum conservation (TMC), rather than hydrodynamic flow fluctuations, is the key mechanism that breaks azimuthal correlation factorization in small collision systems, specifically explaining why the experimental ratio \(r_3\) exceeds unity in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, contrary to hydrodynamic predictions which require \(r_3 \leq 1\).

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for the two-particle distribution function \(f_2\) under the constraint of transverse momentum conservation, using a Gaussian approximation based on the central limit theorem for the sum of transverse momenta of \(N\) particles. They expanded the exponential term in the two-particle distribution to second order for \(r_2\) and to third order for \(r_3\), then calculated the factorization ratios \(r_n\) as defined by the CMS collaboration, incorporating both collective flow parameters (\(v_2\), \(v_3\)) and TMC contributions. The calculations were compared directly with CMS p-Pb data at 5.02 TeV across four multiplicity ranges and four transverse momentum bins.

## Key numbers

- For \(r_2\) in the lowest multiplicity range (\(120 \leq N_{\text{ch}} \leq 150\)) and highest \(p_a\) bin (2.0-3.0 GeV/c), the TMC calculation reproduces the CMS data showing \(r_2 < 1\), with the deviation from unity increasing with \(p_a - p_b\).
- For \(r_3\) in the same low-multiplicity range (\(120 \leq N_{\text{ch}} \leq 150\)), the TMC calculation yields \(r_3 > 1\), with the magnitude of deviation changing by about 20% from top to bottom multiplicity rows, compared to only about 2% variation for \(r_2\).
- The mean transverse momentum in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV is approximately 0.78 GeV/c for high multiplicities, leading to \(\langle p^2 \rangle_F = 0.91\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The parameter \(v_{2F}\) is set to 0.025, and the number of particles \(N\) is taken as \(N = 1.5 N_{\text{ch}}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on the interplay between TMC and collective flow in small systems (Pei, Ma, Bzdak, earlier works) and directly addresses the puzzle raised by CMS data (CMS Collaboration, 2019) showing \(r_3 > 1\) in p-Pb collisions, which hydrodynamic models with MC-Glauber initial conditions cannot simultaneously describe for both \(r_2\) and \(r_3\). The analytical framework enables quantitative separation of TMC effects from flow fluctuations, providing a new tool for interpreting azimuthal correlations in small systems and constraining the origin of collectivity in p-Pb and p-p collisions.
