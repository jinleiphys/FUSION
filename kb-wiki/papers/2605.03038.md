# Squeezed-state radiation in shockwave scattering: QCD-Gravity double copy

> Gluon and graviton radiation in strong field shockwave scattering are described by effective Lipatov vertices, with the graviton Lipatov vertex proportional to the bilinear of its QCD counterpart. We show here that the n-particle gluon radiation spectrum can be described as a generalized Susskind-Glogower (gSG) squeezed coherent state and discuss the properties of such squeezed states. The double copy structure of the radiative frameworks suggests that multi-graviton radiation can be similarly described as a gSG state. We examine the physical parameter space and show that very large squeezing parameters $\sim \ln({\bar n})$ (where ${\bar n}$ is the mean graviton occupancy) are feasible for nearly minimal uncertainty configurations of the gSG state. Quantum noise in the corresponding gravitational wave spectrum is enhanced above the sensitivity of current and future gravitational wave detectors. Our results point to the importance of a comprehensive study of the strong field Lipatov regime of gravitational radiation.

## Key claim

The authors show that the n-gluon radiation spectrum in strong field shockwave scattering can be described as a generalized Susskind-Glogower (gSG) squeezed coherent state, and conjecture that multi-graviton radiation, via the double copy structure, can be similarly described, but they explicitly state this is a conjecture rather than a proven result.

## Method

The authors analyze the n-gluon distribution from shockwave scattering in the dilute-dilute limit of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, where the Yang-Mills equations are linearized to obtain solutions involving the Lipatov vertex. They demonstrate that the resulting negative binomial distribution (NBD) for gluon multiplicity corresponds to a deformed annihilation operator coherent state, which they identify as a generalized Susskind-Glogower state. The gravitational double copy is then examined by noting that the graviton Lipatov vertex is proportional to the bilinear of its QCD counterpart, suggesting an analogous squeezed state description for multi-graviton radiation.

## Key numbers

* The squeezing parameter for gravitational radiation can be as large as approximately ln(n-bar), where n-bar is the mean graviton occupancy, for nearly minimal uncertainty configurations of the gSG state.
* The parameter r characterizing the negative binomial distribution in QCD is given by r = zeta * (N_c^2 - 1) * S_perp * Q_S^2 / (2 pi), where zeta is an O(1) nonperturbative constant, N_c is the number of colors, S_perp is the transverse overlap area, and Q_S is the saturation scale.
* The variance of the NBD distribution is sigma^2 = n-bar + n-bar^2 / r, reducing to Poisson variance for r approaching infinity and Bose-Einstein variance for r = 1.
* The Planck scale root mean standard deviation in gravitational wave detector arms is sigma_Planck proportional to L_Planck approximately 10^{-35} m, while a squeezed state would give sigma_squeezed approximately e^{xi} * sigma_Planck, where xi is the squeezing parameter.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gluon radiation in the CGC effective field theory by Gelis, Lappi, and McLerran (2009), who derived the negative binomial distribution for gluon multiplicity in shockwave scattering, and connects it to quantum optical concepts of squeezed states. The gravitational double copy builds on the work of Raj, Venugopalan, and collaborators (2023) showing that inclusive graviton radiation in shockwave scattering is a double copy of gluon radiation. The possibility of detecting quantum noise in gravitational waves via squeezed states was previously proposed by Parikh, Wilczek, and Zaharaide (2020), and this paper identifies the strong field Lipatov regime of gravitational radiation as a promising context for such effects, motivating further comprehensive study of this regime.
