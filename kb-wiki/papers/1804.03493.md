# Fluctuating fluid dynamics for the QGP in the LHC and BES era

> In an era of high-precision determinations of QGP properties a full incorporation of fluid dynamical fluctuations into our models has become crucial, in particular, when describing the dynamics of small systems or near the conjectured QCD critical point. In this talk we discuss some effects of the propagation of these fluctuations. For LHC physics we focus on fluctuations in the energy-momentum tensor, while the impact of fluctuations in the diffusive net-baryon density is studied to improve our knowledge on the formation of critical fluctuations being searched in current and future BES programs.

# Fluctuating fluid dynamics for the QGP in the LHC and BES era

## Key claim

The full incorporation of intrinsic fluid dynamical fluctuations into models of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is crucial for high-precision determinations of QGP properties, particularly for small systems and near the conjectured QCD critical point, but the accurate treatment of nonlinear interactions and renormalization of cutoff-effects requires a systematic effort that is not yet complete.

## Method

The authors incorporate fluctuations into the energy-momentum tensor of a relativistic viscous fluid by adding a noise field tensor with correlators obtained from linear response theory in Gaussian white noise approximation, implemented in the 3+1D code vHLLE. For net-baryon density fluctuations, they solve a stochastic diffusion equation derived from a Gaussian free energy functional that includes a mass term dependent on the equilibrium correlation length from the 3D Ising model equation of state, using an implicit numerical scheme in a one-dimensional periodic system.

## Key numbers

- Specific shear viscosity set to η/s = 0.2 for the fluid in a static box simulation
- Initial homogeneous energy density e₀ = 10 GeV/fm³ in the box of length 100 fm
- Average energy density in the box reduced by about 3% at late times due to nonlinear interactions of fluctuations
- Noise fields smoothed over lengths of 1 fm to handle large gradients from increasing variance
- Critical density n_c = 1/(3 fm³) used in the stochastic diffusion model
- Mobility coefficient Γ = 1/(3T fm²) and correlation length scale ξ₀ = 0.48 fm
- Pseudo-critical temperature T_c = 0.15 GeV at which ξ(T_c) ≃ 6.4 ξ₀
- For L = 20 fm with surface tension K = 1/ξ₀, numerically realized correlation length of about 5.8 ξ₀

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous efforts (Nahrgang 2017) in developing dynamical models that propagate intrinsic fluctuations in fluid dynamics. For LHC physics, it addresses fluctuations in the energy-momentum tensor, while for beam energy scan (BES) programs it studies diffusive net-baryon density fluctuations to improve understanding of critical fluctuations near the conjectured QCD critical point, following theoretical foundations by Stephanov (1998, 1999, 2008) and Asakawa (2009). The results enable future work on small systems created in p-p(A) collisions (Yan 2015) and on non-Gaussian fluctuations in real-time dynamics.
