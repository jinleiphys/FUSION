# Low-energy dipole excitations in $^{20}$O with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> Low-energy dipole (LED) excitations in $^{20}$O were investigated by variation after $K$-projection of deformation($β$)-constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with the generator coordinate method. We obtained two LED states, namely, the $1_1^-$ state with one-proton excitation on the relatively weak deformation and the $1_2^-$ state with a parity asymmetric structure of the normal deformation. The former is characterized by a toroidal dipole (TD) mode with vortical nuclear current, whereas the latter is associated with a low-energy $E1$ mode caused by surface neutron oscillation along the prolate deformation. The TD (vortical) and $E1$ modes separately appear as the $K^π=1^-$ and $K^π=0^-$ components of the deformed states, respectively, but couple with each other in the $1_1^-$ and $1_2^-$ states of $^{20}$O because of $K$-mixing, and shape fluctuation. As a result of the mixing, TD and $E1$ transition strengths are fragmented into the $1_1^-$ and $1_2^-$ states. We also obtained the $K^π=0^+$, $K^π=0^-$, and $K^π=1^-$ bands with cluster structures in the energy region higher than the LED states.

# Low-energy dipole excitations in $^{20}$O with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

## Key claim
The two low-energy dipole (LED) states in $^{20}$O arise from distinct mechanisms that mix through $K$-mixing and shape fluctuation: the $1_1^-$ state is dominated by a toroidal dipole (TD) mode with vortical nuclear current from one-proton excitation on weak deformation, while the $1_2^-$ state is associated with a low-energy $E1$ mode from surface neutron oscillation along prolate deformation, but the TD and $E1$ transition strengths are fragmented between both states due to this mixing.

## Method
The authors applied variation after $K$-projection (K-VAP) of deformation($\beta$)-constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to $^{20}$O. They performed energy variation for parity- and $K$-projected AMD wave functions with $\beta$ constraints for $K^\pi=0^+$, $K^\pi=0^-$, and $K^\pi=1^-$, then superposed the basis wave functions with total-angular-momentum and parity projection by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation. Transition strengths were calculated for $E1$, toroidal dipole (TD), and compressive dipole (CD) operators using the resulting $J^\pi_k$ states.

## Key numbers
- Calculated binding energy of $^{20}$O: 141.7 MeV (experimental value: 151.36 MeV)
- $1_1^-$ state: excitation energy 6.25 MeV, $B(E1)=1.11\times10^{-2}$ $e^2$fm$^2$, ISD energy-weighted strength ratio $f_{\text{ISD}}=0.31\%$
- $1_2^-$ state: excitation energy 9.59 MeV, $B(E1)=1.56\times10^{-2}$ $e^2$fm$^2$, $f_{\text{ISD}}=0.15\%$
- Experimental comparison: $1_1^-$ at 5.36(5) MeV with $B(E1)=3.57(20)\times10^{-2}$ $e^2$fm$^2$ and $f_{\text{ISD}}=2.70(32)\%$; $1_2^-$ at 6.84(7) MeV with $B(E1)=3.79(26)\times10^{-2}$ $e^2$fm$^2$ and $f_{\text{ISD}}=0.67(12)\%$
- Calculated $B(E3;3^-_1\to 0^+_1)=87.5$ $e^2$fm$^6$ (experimental: $170\pm14$ $e^2$fm$^6$)
- Width parameter $\nu=0.16$ fm$^{-2}$ used for all nucleons

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of LED excitations in $^{18}$O (Shikata and Kanada-En'yo, 2020) using the same K-VAP and GCM framework, and compares results along the oxygen isotope chain $^{16}$O, $^{18}$O, and $^{20}$O. The study addresses the long-standing problem of describing observed LED strengths in neutron-rich nuclei, where prior mean-field approaches (Sagawa and Colo, 1999; Inakura, 2018) described LED strengths as noncollective single-particle excitations, while cluster structure studies (Gai, 1983; Furutachi, 2007; Baba, 2019) have been proposed for excited states. The results enable systematic understanding of how excess neutrons affect the competition between toroidal dipole modes and low-energy $E1$ modes in oxygen isotopes, and predict cluster bands with $\Cs+\alpha$ structure that could be tested in future experiments.
