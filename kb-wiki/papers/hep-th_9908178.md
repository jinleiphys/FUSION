# Aspects of Duality and Confining Strings

> We inspect the excitation energy spectrum of a confining string in terms of solitons in an effective field theory model. The spectrum can be characterized by a spectral function, and twisting and bending of the string is manifested by the invariance of this function under a duality transformation. Both general considerations and numerical simulations reveal that the spectral function can be approximated by a simple rational form, which we propose becomes exact in the Yang-Mills theory.

## Key claim

The excitation energy spectrum of a confining string can be characterized by a spectral function that, based on both general considerations and numerical simulations, can be approximated by a simple rational form; the authors propose that this rational form might become exact in the Yang-Mills theory, but the claim is hedged by noting that the effective field theory model is at least in the same universality class as a Lagrangian for which such a form would be exact.

## Method

The authors model a confining string as a soliton in an effective field theory described by a classical action with a three-component unit vector order parameter and a coupling constant. They analyze the energy of straight twisted strings using an axially symmetric ansatz in cylindrical coordinates, then perform numerical minimization of the energy functional to determine the profile function that minimizes the energy. The results are extended to bent strings by combining the free energy for bending from classical elasticity theory with the twist energy, and compared to previously published numerical data for closed knotted solitons.

## Key numbers

- The ground state energy of a long confining string is given by \(E_0(L) = \sigma \cdot L + \varepsilon + \frac{c}{L} + \mathcal{O}(1/L^2)\), where \(c = \frac{\pi}{12}\) is the Casimir energy of zero-point fluctuations.
- For a straight twisted string, the energy takes the form \(E_{0} + E_{twist} = \sigma \cdot L + \varepsilon + (2\pi^2 \mathcal{C} + \frac{c}{\Tw^2}) \cdot \frac{\Tw^2}{L} + \mathcal{O}(1/L^2)\), where \(\Tw\) counts the number of twists around the axis.
- The spectral function for the straight string energy is interpolated by \(E_{min}(k^2,\lambda) / (4\pi^2 e) = |\Ho| \cdot f(k^2, \lambda) \approx |\Ho| \cdot (a\lambda + b + c/\lambda)\), where \(a,b,c\) are \(k^2\)-dependent numerical coefficients and \(\lambda = (Le)/(2\pi|\Ho|)\).
- For closed knotted solitons, the energy spectrum is predicted to be \(E_{soliton} = \gamma \cdot (|\Ho| + \varepsilon)\), with \(|\Ho| = 1,2,3,\dots\) an integer, giving an equipartitioned spectrum \(E_n = \gamma \cdot (n + \varepsilon)\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to confining strings, building on the Nambu-Goto action in the Gaussian approximation (Kuti, 1990s) and the classical action of Faddeev (1990s) that emerges from Yang-Mills theory in a derivative expansion (Edwin, 1990s). The authors use results from the classical theory of elasticity (Landau, 1980s) to model twisting and bending, and compare their predictions to numerical data for knotted solitons from previous work (Nature, 1997; BS, 1999). The proposed twist-writhe duality and rational spectral function enable a systematic description of supercoiling in confining strings and suggest a universal form for the excitation energy spectrum that could be tested in future Yang-Mills simulations.
