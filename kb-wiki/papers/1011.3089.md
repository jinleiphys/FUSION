# A new approach for studying large numbers of fermions in the unitary regime

> A novel lattice approach is presented for studying systems comprising a large number of interacting nonrelativistic fermions. The construction is ideally suited for numerical study of fermions near unitarity--a strongly coupled regime corresponding to the two-particle s-wave scattering phase shift delta_0 = pi/2. Such systems may be achieved experimentally with trapped atoms, and provide a starting point for an effective field theory description of nuclear physics. We discuss the construction of our lattice theory, which allows us to study systems of up to (but by no means limited to) 38 fermions with high accuracy and modest computational resources, and offer an overview of several applications of the technique. A more detailed discussion of applications and simulation results will be described in companion proceedings by A. N. N. and J-W. L.

# A new approach for studying large numbers of fermions in the unitary regime

## Key claim
The authors present a lattice construction for nonrelativistic fermions near unitarity that is free of the sign problem and allows simulation of up to 38 fermions with modest computational resources, but the actual numerical results for the Bertsch parameter and pairing gap are deferred to companion proceedings and are not reported in this paper.

## Method
The authors construct a nonrelativistic lattice action with a perfect dispersion relation for free fermions, using a non-local gradient operator and a four-fermion contact interaction mediated by a Gaussian or Z2 auxiliary field. The fermion determinant is independent of the auxiliary field, making the simulation equivalent to a quenched simulation and eliminating the sign problem. Fermion propagators are computed recursively as products of random matrices, and multi-particle correlation functions are obtained by anti-symmetrizing direct products of single-particle wavefunctions via Slater determinants. Lattice couplings are tuned by matching exact lattice eigenvalues to continuum energies from Luscher's formula for the unitary limit where p cot delta_0 = 0.

## Key numbers
- Systems of up to 38 untrapped fermions and 20 trapped fermions were studied in initial exploratory work (results reported in companion proceedings).
- The computational cost of the Slater determinant step scales as O(N^4), but is negligible for N ~ 50-100 identical fermions on typical volumes L ~ 12-64.
- For a lattice with L=32, the percent deviation in eta between exact lattice eigenvalues and continuum Luscher eigenvalues is exactly zero for eigenvalues up to the k-th tuned value, and remains small for eigenvalues as high as 27 (corresponding to three filled shells) when using up to seven tuned C-values.
- The hard momentum cutoff is set to Lambda = pi.

## Context
This work extends prior variational approaches to unitary fermions, such as those by Chang (2004, 2007) and by Blume (2010), which offered only upper bounds on the Bertsch parameter and had unknown systematic errors on other quantities. The new method is nonvariational and thus free from such systematic errors in principle. The construction builds on the nonrelativistic lattice action first proposed by Chen (2003). The approach enables future extraction of the Bertsch parameter and pairing gap from lattice simulations, and can test Tan's universal relations (2008) involving the integrated contact density when moving away from unitarity.

**Key numbers:** Review-type paper: no original numerics. (The paper describes the method and tuning procedure but reports no final numerical results for physical quantities; those are in companion proceedings.)
