# How to renormalize coupled cluster theory

> Coupled cluster theory is an attractive tool to solve the quantum many-body problem because its singles and doubles (CCSD) approximation is computationally affordable and yields about 90% of the correlation energy. Capturing the remaining 10%, e.g. via including triples, is numerically expensive. Here we assume that short-range three-body correlations dominate and - following Lepage [How to renormalize the Schrödinger equation, arXiv:nucl-th/9706029] - that their effects can be included within CCSD by renormalizing the three-body contact interaction. We renormalize this contact in $^{16}$O and obtain accurate CCSD results for $^{24}$O, $^{20-34}$Ne, $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{78}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{100}$Sn.

## Key claim

The authors assume that short-range three-body correlations dominate the missing correlation energy in CCSD (about 10% of the total correlation energy) and that their effects can be captured within CCSD by renormalizing the three-body contact interaction, following Lepage (1997). The renormalization is performed in a single nucleus (16O) and then applied to other nuclei, with the condition that the approach works because triples correlations are dominantly short-ranged.

## Method

The authors employ two chiral nuclear Hamiltonians (1.8/2.0(EM) and ΔNNLOGO(394)) and renormalize their three-body contact cE in 16O by requiring that CCSD ground-state energies match triples results (Λ-CCSD(T) or CCSDT-1) to four significant digits. They then perform CCSD computations with the renormalized interactions for a range of nuclei using a model space of 15 harmonic oscillator shells with frequency ħω=16 MeV, and compare results to triples benchmarks and experimental binding energies.

## Key numbers

- CCSD yields about 90% of the correlation energy, while including triples yields about 98-99% of the correlation energy
- Renormalized cE for interaction A (1.8/2.0(EM)): -0.0665 (original: -0.12)
- Renormalized cE for interaction B (ΔNNLOGO(394)): 0.11 (original: -0.002)
- Binding energy of 16O with renormalized interaction A (CCSD): 127.8 MeV (experiment: 127.62 MeV)
- Binding energy of 100Sn with renormalized interaction A (CCSD): 842 MeV (triples benchmark: 836 MeV)
- Binding energy of 100Sn with renormalized interaction B (CCSD): 816 MeV (triples benchmark: 818 MeV)
- Largest deviation from triples benchmarks is less than 2% (in 40Ca for interaction B)
- CCSD with renormalized interactions improves accuracy by about an order of magnitude over CCSD with original interactions
- For neutron-rich neon isotopes near the dripline, accuracy improves by about a factor of four
- Renormalization breaks down at low densities (ρ ≈ 0.06 fm⁻³) and high densities (ρ ≈ 0.24 fm⁻³) in symmetric nuclear matter

## Context

This work extends Lepage's (1997) renormalization approach for the Schrödinger equation to coupled-cluster theory, showing that the missing triples correlations in CCSD can be absorbed into a renormalized three-body contact. The approach is particularly relevant for nuclear physics because three-body contacts already appear in chiral effective field theory (van Kolck, 1994; Epelbaum, 2002) and pionless effective field theory (Bedaque, 1999). The method significantly lowers computational cost for nuclear binding energies, making Hamiltonian-based mass-table computations more accessible for ab initio methods, and could also be applied to the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG), Green's function approaches, and variational Monte Carlo methods.
