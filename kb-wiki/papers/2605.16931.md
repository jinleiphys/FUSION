# Nonlinear response of the chiral magnetic effect in the D3/D7 holographic model

> We investigate the nonlinear response of the chiral magnetic current to an external magnetic field in a holographic setup. Using the D3/D7 brane system, where the chiral magnetic effect (CME) can be realized by considering rotating probe D7-branes, corresponding to introducing an axial chemical potential, we analyze the current-magnetic field relation beyond the linear regime. Focusing on the vicinity of the phase boundary between the insulating phase and the CME phase, we find that the chiral magnetic current exhibits a multi-valued dependence on the magnetic field, indicating a highly nonlinear response characteristic of this model. We further study the dynamical stability of the insulating phase near the transition point, and show that the presence of both an axial chemical potential and an external magnetic field cooperatively stabilize the system. Our results clarify the interplay between the axial chemical potential and the magnetic field in determining the phase structure and stability of the system, and reveal new nonlinear aspects of chiral transport in holographic gauge theories.

## Key claim
The chiral magnetic current in the D3/D7 holographic model exhibits a multi-valued (non-monotonic, folding) dependence on the external magnetic field near the phase boundary between the insulating phase and the CME phase, contrary to the simple linear relation implied by the term "chiral magnetic effect." This nonlinear behavior is characteristic of the model and is not a generic feature of all CME realizations.

## Method
The authors use the D3/D7 probe brane system in AdS/CFT, where an axial chemical potential is introduced via a rotating D7-brane (angular frequency ω = 2μ₅). They solve the equations of motion derived from the Dirac-Born-Infeld and Wess-Zumino actions for the D7-brane embedding fields (θ, φ) and the U(1) gauge field A_z, imposing boundary conditions at the world-volume horizon u_* (determined by g_tt + g_φφ ω² = 0) and at the AdS boundary. The chiral magnetic current ⟨J^z⟩ and pseudo-scalar condensate ⟨O_φ⟩ are extracted from the asymptotic expansions of the bulk fields, and the meson spectrum in the insulating phase is computed via linearized fluctuation analysis using the determinant method.

## Key numbers
- For ω/m = 1.00, the chiral magnetic current ⟨J^z⟩/m³ initially increases with B/m², then decreases and approaches zero at large B/m², with a multi-valued (folding) structure near the transition region (B/m² approximately 0.5–0.6).
- For ω/m = 5.0 and 5.2, two distinct branches appear in the ⟨J^z⟩ vs. B/m² plot, and a reconnection between them occurs at a certain value of ω/m.
- The meson mass squared M²/m² in the insulating phase becomes negative (tachyonic, unstable) as the solutions approach the critical embedding, but the turning point of the multi-valued curve does not coincide with the onset of instability; for ω/m = 1.0, both branches can be stable for certain B/m² values.
- At finite temperature, for ω/m = 1.0, increasing temperature increases ⟨J^z⟩ for a given B/m², but no qualitative changes in the phase structure are observed within the explored parameter range.

## Context
This work extends the original holographic realization of the CME in the D3/D7 model by Hoyos (2011) by focusing on the nonlinear regime near the phase boundary, which was not explored in that earlier study. The multi-valued current-magnetic field relation and the spiral structure of the chiral condensate are analogous to the nonlinear current response to external electric fields observed in the same model by Karch (2007), Nakamura (2010, 2012), and Ali-Akbari (2013). The results enable future studies of non-equilibrium phase transitions when an external electric field is applied along the same direction as the magnetic field, as suggested by the authors.
