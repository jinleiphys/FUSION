# Emergence of the $π(1300)$ Resonance from Lattice QCD

> The mass of the lightest hadron in nature, the pion, is one seventh of that of the nucleon, and one tenth of the mass of its first excited state, the $π(1300)$. This enormous energy difference opens an interesting window into the confinement of quarks and the structure of the lightest hadrons. In this work, we provide the first calculation of resonance parameters of the $π(1300)$ from lattice QCD. For this purpose, recently derived state-of-the-art tools are adapted and applied both in the construction of three-hadron operators and for mapping finite-volume spectra to infinite-volume amplitudes, subsequently analytically continuing these to complex energies. For our heavy pion mass ensembles, we find a clear signal of the resonance. Crucial input is provided through Chiral Perturbation Theory, allowing us to robustly extrapolate to the physical point. Applying model averaging, we extract a pole position of $M_{π(1300)}=(1169\pm46)-i\,(62_{-62}^{+168})\,\rm MeV$ supporting values from phenomenology.

## Key claim
This work provides the first lattice QCD calculation of the resonance parameters of the π(1300) state, finding a clear signal at a heavy pion mass of approximately 305 MeV and then extrapolating to the physical point using Chiral Perturbation Theory. The extracted pole position of M_π(1300) = (1169 ± 46) − i(62_{-62}^{+168}) MeV supports phenomenological values, though the authors note that at the lighter pion mass (approximately 208 MeV) the resonance is not reliably extractable because the expected pole lies outside the energy range accessible to current lattice methods.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on four ensembles with N_f = 2+1 dynamical quark flavors at a single lattice spacing a = 0.07746(18) fm, with pion masses M_π ≈ 305 MeV and M_π ≈ 208 MeV and spatial volumes L = 32 and L = 48. Finite-volume spectra were extracted from correlator matrices using a generalized eigenvalue problem, incorporating single-particle, two-pion (I=0,1,2), and three-pion (I=1) operators constructed with the OpTion package. The spectra were mapped to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes using state-of-the-art three-body quantization conditions, with two-body interactions parameterized via the modified Inverse Amplitude Method (mIAM) combined with Chiral Perturbation Theory, and three-body forces parameterized with a generic pole form.

## Key numbers
* Pole position of the π(1300) at the physical point: M_π(1300) = (1169 ± 46) − i(62 ± 169) MeV, obtained from AIC model averaging of fits to heavy pion mass data.
* Pole position of the ρ(770): M_ρ(770) = (727 ± 3) − i(72 ± 1) MeV, compared to PDG values.
* Pole position of the f_0(500): M_f0(500) = (433 ± 7) − i(250 ± 7) MeV, compared to PDG values.
* Lattice spacing a = 0.07746(18) fm for all four ensembles.
* Two pion mass values used: M_π ≈ 305 MeV (P30 ensembles) and M_π ≈ 208 MeV (P21 ensembles).
* Best fit quality: χ²/d.o.f. = 1.08 for the overall best fit (fit number 203 in the table).

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the π(1300) that did not treat it as a resonance state (Dudek 2010, McNeile 2006) by applying the full three-body finite-volume formalism, previously used for the a₁ (Mai 2021) and ω (Yan 2024) mesons. The results enable future investigations of the quark-mass dependence of the π(1300) at even heavier pion masses, and provide a first-principles foundation for comparing line-shapes and Dalitz plots to phenomenological studies of multi-hadron final states.
