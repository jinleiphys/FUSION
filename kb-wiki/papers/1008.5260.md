# Energy density functional study of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless $ββ$ decay

> We present an extensive study of nuclear matrix elements (NME) for the neutrinoless double beta decay of the nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{116}$Cd, $^{124}$Sn, $^{128}$Te, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe, and $^{150}$Nd based on state-of-the-art energy density functional methods using the Gogny D1S functional. Beyond mean-field effects are included within the generating coordinate method with particle number and angular momentum projection for both initial and final ground states. We obtain a rather constant value for the NME's around 4.7 with the exception of $^{48}$Ca and $^{150}$Nd, where smaller values are found. We analyze the role of deformation and pairing in the evaluation of the NME and present detailed results for the decay of $^{150}$Nd.

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements (NME) for neutrinoless double beta decay are rather constant around an average value of 4.7 for nine of the eleven studied isotopes, with the exceptions of ⁴⁸Ca and ¹⁵⁰Nd where significantly smaller values are found; the suppression in ¹⁵⁰Nd arises from the large difference in quadrupole deformation between mother and granddaughter nuclei, while in ⁴⁸Ca it stems from its double magic character and minimal pairing energy.

## Method

The authors used the Gogny D1S energy density functional with beyond-mean-field effects included via the generating coordinate method (GCM) with particle number and angular momentum projection for both initial and final ground states. Intrinsic axial symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions constrained to different quadrupole deformations were generated, and the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation was solved to obtain the collective wave functions and the NME. The closure approximation was employed to sum over intermediate states, and high-order currents, nucleon finite size corrections, and short-range correlations treated with the Unitary Correlator Method were included.

## Key numbers

- NME for ⁷⁶Ge: 4.60 (assuming ⟨m_ββ⟩ = 0.5 eV, predicted half-life 76.9 × 10²³ y)
- NME for ¹³⁰Te: 5.13 (predicted half-life 8.84 × 10²³ y)
- NME for ¹³⁶Xe: 4.20 (predicted half-life 12.7 × 10²³ y)
- NME for ¹⁵⁰Nd: 1.71, with Gamow-Teller component 1.28 and Fermi component 0.43 (predicted half-life 16.5 × 10²³ y)
- NME for ⁴⁸Ca: 2.37 (predicted half-life 28.5 × 10²³ y)
- NME for ⁹⁶Zr: 5.65, the largest value among the studied nuclei (predicted half-life 5.48 × 10²³ y)
- Computed Q-value for ¹⁵⁰Nd decay: 2.99 MeV (experimental 3.37 MeV)
- Intrinsic quadrupole deformation for ¹⁵⁰Nd ground state: β ≈ +0.40; for ¹⁵⁰Sm: β ≈ +0.25

## Context

This work extends previous Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) calculations by Chaturvedi et al. (2008) and Rath (2009) by using the Gogny D1S functional with a much larger single-particle basis (eleven major oscillator shells) and including particle number projection, angular momentum projection, and configuration mixing. The results are compared with Interacting Shell Model (ISM) calculations by Menendez et al. (2009), QRPA calculations by Kortelainen and Suhonen (2007) and Simkovic et al. (2008), and IBM-2 calculations by Barea and Iachello (2009). The GCM values are generally larger than ISM values (around 2.5) and show some differences from QRPA values, attributed to the spherical symmetry assumption in QRPA and the absence of quasiparticle excitations in the GCM approach. This work enables the first consistent evaluation of the 0νββ decay of ¹⁵⁰Nd and provides a framework for future studies including triaxial and time-reversal symmetry breaking effects.
