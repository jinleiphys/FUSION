# Holographic Signatures of Critical Collapse

> Critical phenomena in gravitational collapse exhibit the universal features of self-similarity, critical scaling, and the appearance of a naked singularity. We study critical collapse in AdS, focusing on holographic field theory observables. We demonstrate that the echoing period, critical exponent, and signatures of the naked singularity can all be extracted from the holographic stress tensor.

# Holographic Signatures of Critical Collapse

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the universal features of critical gravitational collapse (self-similar scale echoing, the critical exponent, and the naked singularity) can be extracted from the holographic stress tensor of the dual quantum field theory, but only within the regime where classical Einstein gravity remains valid; higher-derivative corrections would cut off the divergence at a scale set by the 't Hooft coupling.

## Method
The authors study critical collapse in global AdS5 using a pure gravity model with a homogeneous squashing of the S3 that preserves SO(3) x U(1) symmetry, evading Birkhoff's theorem. They evolve the Einstein equations in maximal slicing gauge using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with a spectral element mesh, adaptive mesh refinement, and horizon excision, then extract the boundary stress tensor from the near-boundary metric asymptotics to compute the pressure anisotropy.

## Key numbers
- Echoing period Delta = 0.46, consistent with the flat space result of Delta = 0.47 from Bizon (2005)
- Critical exponent gamma = 0.32 for subcritical data and gamma = 0.33 for supercritical data, compared to the flat space result gamma = 0.33 from Bizon (2005)
- Maximum echo frequency scales as f_max ~ |epsilon - epsilon_*|^(-gamma/2)
- Relative energy violation and constraint violation are within or below 10^(-6)
- Initial data width parameter sigma = 0.1
- Fine-tuning to criticality to about 1 - epsilon/epsilon_* ~ 10^(-7)
- Pressure anisotropy envelope scales as 1/(1 - t/t_*)
- Higher-derivative corrections become important when f_max ~ lambda^(1/4)/L, where L = 1 is the S3 radius

## Context
This work extends the study of critical collapse in AdS, previously examined by Pretorius (2000), Husain (2002), and Bizon (2011), by computing the dual QFT stress tensor rather than focusing solely on bulk gravitational observables. Prior attempts to compute the critical exponent from the QFT side by Bland (2007) and Alvarez-Gaume (2008) did not explore the dynamics of QFT observables themselves. The results enable the identification of universal signatures of critical thermalization in strongly coupled holographic QFTs, with the caveat that the phenomenon likely does not exist at weak coupling.
