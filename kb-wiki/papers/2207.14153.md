# Inner crust equations of state for CompOSE

> In this paper, we present some relativistic mean-field inner crust equations of state that have recently been uploaded in the CompOSE online repository. These equations of state fulfill experimental and microscopic constraints, and are also able to reproduce two solar-mass stars. We integrate the TOV equations to obtain the mass-radius relation, and we also calculate the tidal deformability, compactness, and effective tidal deformability to compare with the latest astrophysical data from NICER and LIGO and Virgo.

## Key claim

The paper presents eight relativistic mean-field inner crust equations of state uploaded to the CompOSE repository, claiming they fulfill experimental and microscopic constraints and can reproduce two-solar-mass stars; however, the models NL3ωρL55 and FSU2 are disfavored by the GW170817 tidal deformability constraints despite satisfying NICER mass-radius measurements.

## Method

The authors used the Thomas-Fermi approximation within the Wigner-Seitz cell method to describe non-homogeneous clusterized matter in the inner crust, employing eight relativistic mean-field models (NL3ωρL55, TM1e, FSU2, FSU2R, FSU2H, TW, DD2, and DDME2). They constructed unified equations of state by merging the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland outer crust with their Thomas-Fermi inner crust and homogeneous core EoS at the crust-core transition density, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and calculated tidal deformabilities for comparison with NICER and LIGO/Virgo data.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses range from 2.048 M⊙ (FSU2R) to 2.752 M⊙ (NL3ωρL55)
- Radii for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star range from 12.33 km (TW) to 13.89 km (FSU2)
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star ranges from 402 (TW) to 930 (NL3ωρL55)
- Crust thickness for 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars averages approximately 1.3 km across all models
- Core-crust transition densities range from 0.054 fm−3 (FSU2) to 0.089 fm−3 (TM1e)
- The two different crust treatments (unified versus BPS+polytropic) give approximately ±4% difference in radius for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star

## Context

This work extends previous inner crust calculations using the Thomas-Fermi approximation by Grill et al. (2014) and Avancini et al. (2017), and provides publicly available unified equations of state for the CompOSE repository. The results enable more realistic simulations of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, as the authors note that a realistic crust treatment affects neutron star radii by approximately ±4% compared to polytropic crust approximations, consistent with findings by Fortin et al. (2016) and Pais et al. (2016).
