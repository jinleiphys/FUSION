# Occupation probability of harmonic-oscillator quanta for microscopic cluster-model wave functions

> We present a new and simple method of calculating the occupation probability of the number of total harmonic-oscillator quanta for a microscopic cluster-model wave function. Examples of applications are given to the recent calculations including $α+n+n$-model for $^6$He, $α+t+n+n$-model for $^9$Li, and $α+α+n$-model for $^9$Be as well as the classical calculations of $α+p+n$-model for $^6$Li and $α+α+α$-model for $^{12}$C. The analysis is found to be useful for quantifying the amount of excitations across the major shell as well as the degree of clustering. The origin of the antistretching effect is discussed.

# Occupation probability of harmonic-oscillator quanta for microscopic cluster-model wave functions

## Key claim
The paper presents a method to calculate the occupation probability of total harmonic-oscillator (HO) quanta for microscopic cluster-model (MCM) wave functions, but the method requires that the wave function be free from spurious center-of-mass motion to yield a purely intrinsic probability; the choice of the HO size parameter γ also has some influence on the probability values.

## Method
The method computes the expectation value of an operator that projects onto a definite number of total HO quanta Q, using a contour integral over a unit circle in the complex plane. The single-particle matrix elements are evaluated analytically by expressing the Gaussian wave-packet basis functions in terms of HO generating functions, even when the cluster size parameter ν and the HO size parameter γ differ. The occupation probability P_Q is then obtained from the determinant of a matrix of these single-particle matrix elements between Slater determinants of the MCM wave function.

## Key numbers
- For the α+α model of ⁸Be with ν=0.25 fm⁻² and γ=0.15 fm⁻², P_Q values (Q=4,6,8) are shown for L=0,2,4,6 as a function of cluster separation S; a maximum of P_{Q=4} appears around S≈2.9 fm for L=0 and shifts to S≈2.2 fm for L=4.
- For the ground states of ⁶He, ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁸Li, ⁹Li, ⁹Be, and ¹²C, the lowest 0ħω component (Q_exc=0) is around 50-60% for most cases, and the sum of 0, 2, and 4ħω components accumulates to about 90%.
- The 0⁺₂ state of ¹²C has no dominant component; the calculated monopole matrix element is 4.0 fm², compared to the experimental value of 5.4±0.2 fm².
- For ⁶He, the probability with Q_exc(p)=1 and Q_exc(n)=1 is about 3%, while P_{0,2} and P_{2,0} are 9% and 11% respectively.
- The HO size parameter γ is set to 0.17 fm⁻² (ħω=14.4 MeV) for p-shell nuclei, but reduced to 0.13 fm⁻² for ⁶He and 0.14 fm⁻² for ⁶Li.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic cluster model (MCM) calculations of Suzuki, Varga, and collaborators (1995-1996) for multi-cluster systems including halo nuclei, by providing a quantitative link to the HO shell-model basis. The method contrasts with earlier efforts to relate MCM wave functions to SU(3) shell model (Hecht, 1970s) or symplectic shell model (Rowe, 1980s) configurations, and with the work of Kanada-En'yo (1995) which used single Slater determinants giving much smaller average HO quanta. The analysis enables comparison of the model space truncation between shell-model and cluster-model approaches, and explains the antistretching effect in rotational bands through the change of occupation probability with angular momentum.
