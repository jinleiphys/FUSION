# Upsilon states in magnetized nuclear matter

> The mass modifications of the bottomonium states ($Υ(NS)$,$N=1,2,3,4$ and $Υ(1D)$) in magnetized nuclear matter are studied using chiral effective model. The in-medium masses are calculated from the medium modification of the scalar dilaton field in the chiral effective model, which simulates the gluon condensates of QCD. The strengths of the wave functions (assumed to be harmonic oscillator wave functions) denoted by the parameter, $β$, of the $Υ(NS)$, $N=1,2,3,4$, are fitted from their observed decay widths to $e^+e^-$. The decay width for the channel, $Υ(1D) \rightarrow e^+ e^-$ %, which has not yet been observed experimentally, has been predicted in the present work, by using the value of the parameter, $β$ for $Υ(1D)$, interpolated from the $β$ versus mass relation, for the upsilon states. The effects of the isospin asymmetry of the nuclear medium on the masses of the upsilon states are investigated and are observed to be large for high densities. This should have observable consequences at the asymmetric heavy ion collisions at the Compressed baryonic matter (CBM) experiments at FAIR, GSI as well as at SPS, CERN. The study of the bottomonium states at CBM will however require access to higher energies than the energy regime planned at present. The effects of magnetic field on the masses of bottomonium states in nuclear matter are studied in the present work. These masses are investigated including the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of the nucleons, and compared to the results when the AMMs of nucleons are not taken into account. The effects of magnetic field as well as isospin asymmetry on the upsilon masses are obserevd to be large at high densities.

## Key claim

The masses of bottomonium states (Upsilon(1S) through Upsilon(4S) and Upsilon(1D)) in magnetized nuclear matter are modified due to changes in the scalar dilaton field, which simulates the gluon condensates of QCD; the effects of magnetic field and isospin asymmetry are large only at high baryon densities, and the inclusion of anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of nucleons reduces the mass drop compared to the case without AMM.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) effective model in the mean-field approximation to compute the in-medium values of the scalar meson fields (sigma, zeta, delta) and the dilaton field chi from coupled equations of motion, including the effects of a constant external magnetic field and the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons. The mass shift of each bottomonium state was then calculated from the medium modification of the dilaton field using equation (13), which relates the change in the scalar gluon condensate to the change in chi. The wave functions of the bottomonium states were assumed to be harmonic oscillator wave functions, with the strength parameter beta fitted from the observed leptonic decay widths to e+e- for the Upsilon(NS) states, and interpolated for the Upsilon(1D) state.

## Key numbers

*   Beta values fitted from leptonic decay widths: 1309.2 MeV (Upsilon(1S)), 915.4 MeV (Upsilon(2S)), 779.75 MeV (Upsilon(3S)), 638.6 MeV (Upsilon(4S)), using observed decay widths of 1.34, 0.612, 0.443, and 0.272 keV respectively.
*   Vacuum masses used: 9460.3 MeV (Upsilon(1S)), 10023.26 MeV (Upsilon(2S)), 10355.2 MeV (Upsilon(3S)), 10579.4 MeV (Upsilon(4S)), and 10163.7 MeV (Upsilon(1D)).
*   Predicted leptonic decay width for Upsilon(1D) -> e+e-: 0.00715 keV, using an interpolated beta value of 858 MeV.
*   For symmetric nuclear matter (eta=0) at density 4 rho_0, including AMM, mass shifts (in MeV) for Upsilon(1S) are -2.57 (eB=4m_pi^2), -2.463 (eB=8m_pi^2), -2.41 (eB=10m_pi^2), -2.32 (eB=12m_pi^2); for Upsilon(4S) they are -354.17, -339.44, -332, and -319.9 MeV respectively.
*   For symmetric nuclear matter at density 4 rho_0 without AMM, mass shifts for Upsilon(1S) are -2.707 MeV (eB=4m_pi^2) and -3.006 MeV (eB=12m_pi^2); for Upsilon(4S) they are -373.13 MeV and -414.17 MeV respectively.
*   For asymmetric nuclear matter (eta=0.3) at density 4 rho_0, including AMM, mass shifts for Upsilon(1S) are -2.535 MeV (eB=4m_pi^2) and -2.206 MeV (eB=12m_pi^2); for Upsilon(4S) they are -349.26 MeV and -303.92 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonium in medium using the chiral effective model, specifically the authors' prior work on charmonium states in magnetized matter (Charmonium_mag, 2017) and on upsilon states without magnetic fields (AM_DP_upsilon). It also builds on the QCD sum rule approach to heavy quarkonium in medium (Kim and Lee, 2001; Klingl et al., 1999; Lee and Ko, 2003) and on the chiral SU(3) model applied to finite nuclei (Paper3, 1997) and hot hyperonic matter (Kristof et al., 1998). The predicted mass shifts, especially the large effects at high densities for excited states, are relevant for interpreting data from asymmetric heavy-ion collisions at the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR and at SPS, CERN, though the authors note that CBM would require higher energies than currently planned.
