# Collective flow and QCD phase transition

> In the first part I discuss the sensitivity of collective matter expansion in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions to the transition between quark and hadronic matter (physics of the softest point of the Equation of State). A kink in the centrality dependence of elliptic flow has been suggested as a signature for the phase transition in hot QCD matter. Indeed, preliminary data of NA49 presented at this conference show first indications for the predicted kink. In the second part I have a look at the present theories of heavy-ion reactions. These remarks may also be seen as a critical comment to B. Mueller's summary talk (nucl-th/9906029) presented at this conference.

# Collective flow and QCD phase transition

## Key claim
A kink in the centrality dependence of scaled elliptic flow (v₂/ε) in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV is predicted as a signature of a first-order QCD phase transition, but only when non-equilibrium effects from finite system size are combined with the softest point of the equation of state, neither hydrodynamics with a phase transition nor transport without one produces the characteristic kink.

## Method
The RQMD transport model was modified to incorporate two different equations of state: a resonance-gas EOS (no phase transition) and an EOS with a first-order phase transition at T_c = 160 MeV with latent heat 467 MeV/fm³, extracted from a quasi-particle model fitted to lattice data. The scaled flow asymmetry A₂ = v₂/ε was calculated for pions as a function of impact parameter b in Pb(158 AGeV) on Pb collisions, and compared to hydrodynamic calculations and preliminary NA49 data.

## Key numbers
- Phase transition temperature: T_c = 160 MeV
- Latent heat of the first-order transition: 467 MeV/fm³
- Hydrodynamic scaled flow asymmetry values for pions range between 0.16 and 0.24, depending on the EOS
- The kink in v₂/ε appears at impact parameter b ≈ 5 fm in the RQMD calculation with phase transition
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit is approached at temperatures ≥ 3 T_c
- Chiral symmetry restoration occurs in the range 140 to 170 MeV

## Context
This work extends the suggestion by Sorge (1997) that elliptic flow is more sensitive to early pressure than radial flow, and the earlier proposal by van Hove (1982) that multiplicity dependence of average transverse momenta might show a plateau. The prediction contrasts with hydrodynamic calculations by Heinz and collaborators (1999) which found the net effect of the phase transition on elliptic flow to be largely canceled. The preliminary NA49 data presented by Poskanzer at this conference show first indications consistent with the predicted kink, though quantitative comparison requires better statistics for b < 5 fm. The second part of the paper constitutes a critical response to Mueller's summary talk (nucl-th/9906029), arguing against exclusive reliance on perturbative QCD approaches and defending the use of string-based transport models for heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies.
