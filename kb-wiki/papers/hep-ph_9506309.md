# Pion Observables in the Extended NJL Model with Vector and Axial-Vector Mesons

> The momentum-space bosonization method of a Nambu and Jona-Lasinio type model with vector and axial-vector mesons is applied to $ππ$ scattering. Unlike the case in earlier published papers, we obtain the $ππ$ scattering amplitude using the linear and nonlinear realizations of chiral symmetry and fully taking into account the momentum dependence of meson vertices. We show the full physical equivalence between these two approaches. The chiral expansion procedure in this model is discussed in detail. Chiral expansions of the quark mass, pion mass and constant $f_π$ are obtained. The low-energy $ππ$ phase shifts are compared to the available data. We also study the scalar form factor of the pion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the momentum-space bosonization method applied to an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector and axial-vector mesons yields fully equivalent \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitudes whether one uses the linear or nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry, provided the full momentum dependence of meson vertices is retained rather than truncated to a derivative expansion.

## Method

The authors start from an \(SU(2)_L \otimes SU(2)_R\) NJL Lagrangian with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector four-quark interactions, introducing boson fields via standard path-integral techniques. They then compute the \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitude by evaluating quark-loop diagrams (including box diagrams and meson-exchange contributions) using a modified Pauli-Villars regularization scheme, without resorting to a derivative expansion of the effective meson Lagrangian. The calculation is performed separately for both the linear and nonlinear realizations of chiral symmetry, and the resulting amplitudes are compared term by term to establish their equivalence.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda \sim 1\) GeV, characterizing the scale of quark-antiquark forces responsible for dynamic chiral symmetry breaking.
* The chiral expansion of the constituent quark mass is given by \(m = \mch \left[1 + \frac{\mpio^2 \stackrel{\circ}{\delta}}{4\mch^2} - \frac{3\mpio^4 \stackrel{\circ}{\delta}^2}{32\mch^4} \left(1 - \frac{\mch^2 \nh \stackrel{\circ}{\delta}}{2\pi^2 f^2}\right) + \mathcal{O}(\mpio^6)\right]\), where \(\mch\) is the chiral-limit constituent quark mass, \(\mpio\) is the chiral-limit pion mass, and \(f\) is the chiral-limit pion decay constant.
* The chiral-limit pion decay constant squared is \(f^2 = 4 \stackrel{\circ}{\delta} \mch^2 \stackrel{\circ}{I}_2\), where \(\stackrel{\circ}{I}_2\) is the chiral-limit value of the loop integral \(I_2(0)\) and \(\stackrel{\circ}{\delta} = 1 - 4 G_V f^2\).
* The pion mass in the chiral limit is \(\stackrel{\circ}{m}^2_\pi = \frac{\widehat{m} \mch}{G_S f^2}\), where \(\widehat{m}\) is the current quark mass.

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies by Bernard et al. (1995) and others that relied on derivative expansions of the effective meson Lagrangian, by developing a momentum-space bosonization method that avoids such truncations. The approach enables direct calculation of \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitudes and the scalar form factor of the pion without the limitations of low-order derivative expansions, and it establishes a bridge between linear and nonlinear chiral symmetry realizations in NJL models. The results enable future comparisons with chiral perturbation theory predictions and experimental \(\pi\pi\) phase shift data at low energies.
