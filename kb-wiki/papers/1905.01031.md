# QCD equation of state at finite baryon density with fugacity expansion

> We explore the QCD equation of state at finite baryon density through an expansion in baryon number fugacity, making use of the recent lattice data on the four leading Fourier coefficients of the expansion. A state-of-the-art description of the lattice data at both imaginary $μ_B$ and $μ_B = 0$ is provided by the cluster expansion model (CEM). The CEM pressure function has three temperature dependent input parameters, which we fix by parameterizing the available lattice QCD data. This results in a crossover model of the QCD equation of state at finite baryon density, which can be used in fluid dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions.

# QCD equation of state at finite baryon density with fugacity expansion

## Key claim
The cluster expansion model (CEM) provides a state-of-the-art description of lattice QCD data at both imaginary chemical potential and zero baryon density, but the determination of higher-order Fourier coefficients from the two leading ones is not unique, and the CEM is only one possible functional form that fits the available data. The model corresponds to a no-critical-point scenario for the QCD phase diagram, exhibiting crossover-type transition across the entire mu_B-T plane.

## Method
The authors construct the QCD equation of state by expanding the pressure in baryon number fugacity as a Laurent series, using the four leading Fourier coefficients of net baryon density from lattice QCD at imaginary chemical potential. They fix three temperature-dependent input parameters (p_0, b_1, b_2) by parameterizing lattice QCD data at both mu_B = 0 and imaginary mu_B, with smooth switching functions connecting the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model at low temperatures to lattice data at high temperatures. The CEM assumes that all higher-order Fourier coefficients (k >= 3) are determined by the two leading ones through the Stefan-Boltzmann limit ratios alpha_k^SB.

## Key numbers
- Four leading Fourier coefficients b_k(T) were evaluated in lattice QCD at physical point in the temperature range 135 < T < 230 MeV (Vovchenko 2017)
- The switching function parameters for b_1(T) are T_0 = 170 MeV and r = 7.95; for b(T) they are T_0 = 175.5 MeV and r = 8; for p_0(T) they are T_0 = 100 MeV and r = 7
- The baryonic eigenvolume parameter at low temperatures is b^hrg = 1 fm^3
- Seven entropy-per-baryon values are considered for isentropic trajectories: S/N_B = 10, 15, 20, 30, 50, 150, and 400, covering collision energies from low SPS to top RHIC
- The CEM describes lattice data for susceptibility ratios chi_4^B/chi_2^B and chi_6^B/chi_2^B in the temperature range 150 < T < 180 MeV, where notable differences from the rational function model (RFM) appear

## Context
This work extends the cluster expansion model formalism developed by Vovchenko (2017, 2018) to construct a full equation of state for fluid dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions. It incorporates recent lattice QCD constraints from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2013, Vovchenko 2017) on Fourier coefficients at imaginary chemical potential and from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2014, 2017) on baryon number susceptibilities at mu_B = 0. The CEM is compared to the rational function model of Almasi (2018), which gives a similarly good description of b_3 and b_4 on linear scale but shows deviations in the alpha_3 and alpha_4 ratios at T < 190 MeV that are not supported by lattice data. The resulting equation of state enables fluid dynamical simulations from the lowest SPS energies up to the highest LHC energies, and is available in tabulated format.
