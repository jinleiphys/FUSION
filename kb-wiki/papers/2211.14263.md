# Towards microscopic optical potentials in deformed nuclei

> A microscopic and consistent description of both nuclear structure and reactions is instrumental to extend the predictivity of models calculating scattering observables. In particular, this is crucial in the case of exotic nuclei not yet discovered. In this manuscript, we will present the plan of the Lund effort for a symmetry breaking description of bound and scattering observables using a generator coordinate method model based on an effective Hamiltonian constrained with a (Skyrme) functional. Following this, we will illustrate the steps to construct an optical potential for deformed nuclei from microscopic wavefunctions obtained with projected generator coordinate method from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov basis.

## Key claim

The authors outline a plan to construct microscopic optical potentials for deformed nuclei by using a generator coordinate method (GCM) with symmetry restoration, based on an effective Hamiltonian fitted to a Skyrme functional, but they present only the formalism and first steps (spectroscopic factors for 24Mg) rather than a completed optical potential or reaction calculation.

## Method

The authors use a Hamiltonian composed of a spherical mean field, seniority pairing, and a separable quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, fitted to reproduce axially constrained Skyrme (SLy4) calculations. They solve this Hamiltonian by generating a set of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) vacua over generator coordinates (deformation β, γ, pairing strengths, cranking frequencies), then restore particle number and angular momentum symmetry via projection operators. Spectroscopic factors between even-even and odd-mass GCM states are computed in the Bogoliubov quasiparticle basis, which are the ingredients needed to construct Green's functions and, ultimately, the optical potential.

## Key numbers

- The effective Hamiltonian includes a pairing strength G fixed according to the uniform spectra method (Ragnarsson 2005) and a quadrupole strength χ; the Hamiltonian is fitted to the SLy4 parametrization of the Skyrme functional, generating the "SLy4-H" effective Hamiltonian.
- First results for spectroscopic factors are reported for 24Mg (Bostrom 2022proc), though no numerical values for these factors are given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the Lund group's program for a symmetry-breaking description of bound and scattering observables, building on prior microscopic optical potential developments using Green's function methods (Idini 2018, 2019; Rotureau 2017, 2018) and multi-scattering approaches (Holt 2013; Vorabbi 2021; Burrows 2020). It enables future calculation of reaction observables for deformed nuclei, including exotic species not yet discovered, by providing a consistent framework that connects nuclear structure (GCM with Skyrme functionals) to reaction theory via the optical potential. A seminal small-scale example of GCM spectroscopic factors is given by Haakansson (1978).
