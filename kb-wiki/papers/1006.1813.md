# Meson production in high-energy electron-nucleus scattering

> Experimental studies of meson production through two-photon fusion in inelastic electron-nucleus scattering is now under way. A high-energy photon radiated by the incident electron is fused with a soft photon radiated by the nucleus. The process takes place in the small-angle-Coulomb region of nuclear scattering. We expound the theory for this production process as well as its interference with coherent-radiative-meson production. In particular, we investigate the distortion of the electron wave function due to multiple-Coulomb scattering.

## Key claim
The two-photon-fusion amplitude for meson production in high-energy electron-nucleus scattering is significantly modified by the Coulomb phase of the electron wave function, and this distortion couples the impact-parameter integrations for the electron and the pion in a way that cannot be factorized for pion production, though factorization remains a good approximation for eta production.

## Method
The author develops a Glauber (eikonal) approach to describe meson production via two-photon fusion in inelastic electron-nucleus scattering, including the interference with coherent photoproduction. The formalism is built in impact-parameter space, where the electron and nuclear photon fields are represented by vector potentials and profile functions, and the Coulomb distortion of the electron wave function is incorporated as an eikonal phase factor. Numerical contour plots of the squared amplitude ratios are presented for pion production on a lead target, and the factorization property of the Coulomb-corrected amplitudes is tested for both pion and eta production.

## Key numbers
- Incident electron energy: \(E(\mathbf{k}_1)=11\) GeV; scattered electron energy: \(E(\mathbf{k}_2)=300\) MeV; resulting pion energy: \(\omega(\mathbf{K})=10.7\) GeV.
- Pion decay constant: \(g_{\pi\gamma\gamma}=0.0375\), fixed by the decay rate \(\Gamma(\pi^0\rightarrow \gamma\gamma)\).
- Longitudinal momentum transfer for \(\pi^0\) production: \(Q_\|(m_{\pi^0})=0.85\) MeV/\(c\); for \(\eta\) production: \(Q_\|(m_\eta)=14.0\) MeV/\(c\).
- Cut-off impact parameter for the electron: \(b_{ec}=1/m_e=390\) fm; for pion production: \(b_{\pi c}(\pi)=230\) fm; for eta production: \(b_{\eta c}(\eta)=14\) fm.
- The Born amplitude \(K_B(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{Q})\) has a maximal value \(K_0=1/(4 Q_\| q_\|)\) at the double peak.

## Context
This work extends the Born-approximation treatment of the Primakoff effect for meson production, previously elaborated by Hadjimichael and Fallieros, by incorporating a full Glauber approach to account for multiple-Coulomb scattering of the electron. The formalism is developed in the context of the PrimEx Collaboration's experimental program at 11 GeV/\(c\). The results enable quantitative predictions for the interference between two-photon-fusion and coherent-photoproduction amplitudes, and the author notes that the Coulomb phase of the coherent photoproduction amplitude is known analytically for point-like nuclear charge distributions from prior work by Fäldt and collaborators.
