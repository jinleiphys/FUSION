# Selecting microscopic Equations of State

> We compare a set of equations of state derived within microscopic many-body approaches, and study their predictions as far as phenomenological data on nuclei from heavy ion collisions, and astrophysical observations on neutron stars are concerned. We find that all the data, taken together, put strong constraints not easy to be fulfilled accurately. However, no major discrepancies are found among the selected equations of state and with respect to the data. The results provide an estimate of the uncertainty on the theoretical prediction at a microscopic level of the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper finds that while combined constraints from heavy-ion collisions and neutron-star observations are stringent and not easily satisfied simultaneously, the selected microscopic equations of state show no major discrepancies among themselves or with the data, providing an estimate of the theoretical uncertainty at the microscopic level.

## Method

The authors compared five microscopic equations of state (EoS) for symmetric nuclear matter: three from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method using different three-body forces (Urbana UVIX or microscopic TBFs with Argonne v18 or Bonn B potentials), one from the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) method with the Bonn A potential, and one from the variational method (APR) using Argonne v18 with UVIX forces. They computed saturation properties and then tested each EoS against phenomenological constraints from heavy-ion collision data (flow, kaon production, symmetry energy) and astrophysical observations (neutron-star mass-radius relations).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for BHF with Av18+UVIX: 0.16 fm^-3; binding energy: -15.98 MeV; incompressibility K0: 212.4 MeV; symmetry energy S0: 31.9 MeV; slope L: 52.9 MeV
- Saturation density for BHF with Av18+microscopic TBF: 0.2 fm^-3; binding energy: -15.5 MeV; K0: 236 MeV; S0: 31.3 MeV; L: 82.7 MeV
- Saturation density for BHF with Bonn B+microscopic TBF: 0.17 fm^-3; binding energy: -16.0 MeV; K0: 254 MeV; S0: 30.3 MeV; L: 59.2 MeV
- Saturation density for APR with Av18+UVIX: 0.16 fm^-3; binding energy: -16.0 MeV; K0: 247.3 MeV; S0: 33.9 MeV; L: 53.8 MeV
- Saturation density for DBHF with Bonn A: 0.18 fm^-3; binding energy: -16.15 MeV; K0: 230 MeV; S0: 34.4 MeV; L: 69.4 MeV
- Empirical saturation density: 0.17 +/- 0.03 fm^-3; empirical binding energy: approximately -16 MeV
- Incompressibility constrained between 210 and 250 MeV from monopole resonance data
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: delta R_np = 0.211 (+0.054/-0.063) fm from Zenihiro et al.
- Slope L from pygmy dipole resonance analysis: 64.8 +/- 15.7 MeV; S0: 32.2 +/- 1.3 MeV (Carbone et al.)
- FRDM model gives S0 = 32.5 +/- 0.5 MeV and L = 70 +/- 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dutra et al. (2012), who tested Skyrme forces against heavy-ion and astrophysical constraints, by instead testing microscopic many-body EoS. The authors note that phenomenological Skyrme forces, as analyzed by Dutra (2012) and Steiner (2005), struggle to simultaneously satisfy all constraints. The present comparison of BHF, DBHF, and variational methods provides an estimate of theoretical uncertainty at the microscopic level and identifies which EoS are compatible with data from heavy-ion collisions (flow, kaon production from KaoS and FOPI collaborations) and neutron-star observations (mass-radius relations from Ozel). The results enable future work to refine microscopic three-body forces and to extend the analysis to include hyperonic degrees of freedom.
