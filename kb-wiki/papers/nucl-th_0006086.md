# Charm meson scattering cross sections by pion and rho meson

> Using the local flavor SU(4) gauge invariance in the limit of vanishing vector meson masses, we extend our previous study of charm meson scattering cross sections by pion and rho meson, which is based only on the pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector meson couplings, to include also contributions from the couplings among three vector mesons and among four particles. We find that diagrams with light meson exchanges usually dominate the cross sections. For the processes considered previously, the additional interactions lead only to diagrams involving charm meson exchanges and contact interactions, and the cross sections for these processes are thus not much affected. Nevertheless, these additional interactions introduce new processes with light meson exchanges and increase significantly the total scattering cross sections of charm mesons by pion and rho meson.

# Charm meson scattering cross sections by pion and rho meson

## Key claim

Extending the previous hadronic model (which included only pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector meson couplings) to include couplings among three vector mesons and among four particles does not significantly change the cross sections for processes considered previously, but introduces new processes with light meson exchanges that increase the total scattering cross sections of charm mesons by pion and rho meson. The dominance of light meson exchange diagrams is the key condition; charm meson exchange and contact interactions contribute little.

## Method

The authors constructed a hadronic Lagrangian using local flavor SU(4) gauge invariance in the limit of vanishing vector meson masses, starting from free pseudoscalar and vector meson Lagrangians and applying minimal substitution. They derived scattering amplitudes for eight processes involving π D, π D*, ρ D, and ρ D* scattering, including diagrams with light meson exchanges, charm meson exchanges, and contact interactions. Form factors with a monopole form were introduced at interaction vertices using cutoff parameters Λ = 1 or 2 GeV, and an imaginary self-energy of 50 MeV was added to exchanged meson propagators to handle on-shell divergences.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_{ρππ} = 6.1, g_{πDD*} = 4.4, g_{ρDD} = g_{ρD*D*} = 2.52 (empirical values)
- Total cross sections for π D, π D*, ρ D, and ρ D* scatterings are all roughly between 10 and 20 mb with form factors (Λ = 1 or 2 GeV)
- Thermal average of total cross section at T = 150 MeV shows little variation for imaginary self-energy values between 5 and 500 MeV
- Form factors suppress total cross sections by a factor of two or less compared to the case without form factors (Λ = ∞)
- The D* meson vacuum width is about 44 KeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lin, Di, and Ko, 2000) that considered only pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector meson couplings for charm meson scattering, and follows the approach of Matinyan and Müller (1998) and Haglin (2000) who applied local SU(4) gauge invariance to charmonium absorption. In contrast to charmonium absorption by pions (where additional couplings increased cross sections by an order of magnitude), charm meson scattering is dominated by light meson exchanges already present in the simpler model. The results enable more realistic modeling of charm meson interactions in hadronic matter formed in heavy ion collisions, relevant for understanding charm meson spectra and dilepton production at SPS energies.
