## Key claim

The paper presents an improved Standard-Model prediction for the branching ratios of \(K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-\) and \(K_L\to e^+e^-\) by calculating the long-distance contribution to the \(K_L\to\gamma^*\gamma^*\) form factor using dispersion theory, with the hedge that the result depends on input from multiple decay channels and the matching to asymptotic constraints, and that the quoted uncertainties could be further reduced by future experiments and lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors constructed a dispersive representation of the \(K_L\to\gamma^*\gamma^*\) transition form factor by decomposing it into isovector and isoscalar components, including contributions from two-pion intermediate states via OmnĂ¨s factors and from \(K^*\) resonances via dispersively improved Breitâ€“Wigner parameterizations. They fitted the form factor to data from the hadronic decay \(K_L\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) (spectrum from KTeV) and constrained the \(K^*\) contribution using the slope parameter extracted from the leptonic decays \(K_L\to\ell^+\ell^-\gamma\) via the BMS parameterization. The relative weights of isospin components were guided by vector-meson-dominance arguments, and the short-distance part was taken from previous calculations.

## Key numbers

* Standard-Model prediction for \(K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-\): \(\text{Br}[K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-] = 7.44^{+0.41}_{-0.34} \times 10^{-9}\)
* Standard-Model prediction for \(K_L\to e^+e^-\): \(\text{Br}[K_L\to e^+e^-] = 8.46(37) \times 10^{-12}\)
* Slope parameter of the \(K_L\to\gamma^*\gamma^*\) form factor from BMS model: \(b_{K_L} = 2.72(11)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\)
* Short-distance contribution to the local term: \(\chi_\text{SD}^\text{SM} = -1.80(6)\)
* Experimental branching fraction for \(K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-\): \(6.84(11) \times 10^{-9}\)
* Experimental branching fraction for \(K_L\to e^+e^-\): \(8.7^{+5.7}_{-4.1} \times 10^{-12}\)
* Normalization from \(K_L\to\gamma\gamma\): \(\text{Br}[K_L\to\gamma\gamma] = 5.47(4) \times 10^{-4}\)
* Fit to \(K_L\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) spectrum: \(N_{\pi\pi} = 3.47(27) \times 10^{-8}\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\), \(\bar b_{\pi\pi} = 10.0(1.9)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\bar\eta_{K^*} = 0.96(10)\), with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 34.1/27 = 1.26\) and \(p\)-value \(16.3\%\)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach previously applied to the \(\pi^0\to\gamma^*\gamma^*\) form factor (Hoferichter et al., 2018) and to \(\pi^0\to e^+e^-\) (Hoferichter et al., 2021) to the kaon sector, improving upon earlier chiral perturbation theory estimates (Gomez Dumm and Knecht, 1998; Knecht et al., 1999) and model parameterizations (D'Ambrosio, Isidori, and PortolĂ©s, 1997; BergstrĂ¶m, MassĂł, and Singer, 1983). The improved Standard-Model prediction enables more stringent constraints on physics beyond the Standard Model from the existing experimental results, and the authors delineate how future measurements at HIKE and KOTO II, together with lattice QCD calculations (Chao et al., 2023; Christ et al., 2022), could further reduce the uncertainties.
