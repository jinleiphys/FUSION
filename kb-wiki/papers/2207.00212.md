# Formation of Chiral Soliton Lattice

> The Chiral Soliton Lattice (CSL) is a lattice structure composed of domain walls aligned in parallel at equal intervals, which is energetically stable in the presence of a background magnetic field and a finite (baryon) chemical potential due to the topological term originated from the chiral anomaly. We study its formation from the vacuum state, with describing the CSL as a layer of domain-wall disks surrounded by the vortex or string loop, based on the Nambu-Goto-type effective theory. We show that the domain wall nucleates via quantum tunneling when the magnetic field is strong enough. We evaluate its nucleation rate and determine the critical magnetic field strength with which the nucleation rate is no longer exponentially suppressed. We apply this analysis to the neutral pion in the two-flavor QCD as well as the axion-like particles (ALPs) with a finite (baryon) chemical potential under an external magnetic field. In the former case, even though the CSL state is more energetically stable than the vacuum state and the nucleation rate becomes larger for sufficiently strong magnetic field, it cannot be large enough so that the nucleation of the domain walls is not exponentially suppressed and promoted, without suffering from the tachyonic instability of the charged pion fluctuations. In the latter case, we confirm that the effective interaction of the ALPs generically includes the topological term required for the CSL state to be energetically favored. We show that the ALP CSL formation is promoted if the magnetic field strength and the chemical potential of the system is slightly larger than the scale of the axion decay constant.

## Key claim

The authors show that even when the Chiral Soliton Lattice (CSL) state is energetically more favorable than the vacuum, its formation via quantum tunneling from the vacuum is exponentially suppressed unless the magnetic field exceeds a critical value \(B_c\) that is larger than the thermodynamic critical field \(B_{\mathrm{DW}}\); for the neutral pion in two-flavor QCD, the nucleation rate remains exponentially suppressed within the magnetic field range allowed by charged pion stability, while for axion-like particles (ALPs) the formation can be unsuppressed if the magnetic field and chemical potential are slightly larger than the axion decay constant.

## Method

The authors model the nucleation of a CSL as the quantum tunneling of a domain-wall disk surrounded by a string loop, described by a Nambu-Goto-type effective action in the thin-wall approximation. They reduce the infinite-dimensional field theory to one-dimensional quantum mechanics for the disk radius \(R\), compute the Euclidean bounce action for tunneling from zero radius to the critical radius \(R_2 = -2T/\sigma\) (where \(T\) is string tension and \(\sigma\) is wall tension), and evaluate the nucleation rate \(P \simeq \mathcal{A} e^{-\mathcal{B}}\) with bounce action \(\mathcal{B} = 16\pi T^3 / (3\sigma^2)\). They apply this formalism to the neutral pion in two-flavor QCD and to ALPs with a finite baryon chemical potential under an external magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for a single domain wall to become energetically favorable: \(B_{\mathrm{DW}} = 16\pi m f^2 / \mu\) (equation \ref{critical-mag}), where \(m\) is the pNGB mass and \(f\) is the decay constant.
- Critical magnetic field for unsuppressed nucleation: \(B_c = \frac{16\pi m f^2}{\mu} \left( \frac{(\ln (R_\mathrm{c}/r_\mathrm{c}))^{3/2}}{\sqrt{3}} \frac{4\pi^2 f}{m} + 1 \right)\) (equation \ref{Bnucl}), which exceeds \(B_{\mathrm{DW}}\).
- Bounce action for domain-wall disk nucleation: \(\mathcal{B} = 16\pi T^3 / (3\sigma^2)\) (equation \ref{analytical_form_bounce_action}), with wall tension \(\sigma = 8 m f^2 - \mu B/(2\pi)\) and string tension \(T \sim 2\pi \times 2f^2 \times \ln(R_\mathrm{c}/r_\mathrm{c})\).
- Critical radius of the nucleated domain-wall disk: \(R_2 = -2T/\sigma\) (from the \(\epsilon \to 0\) limit).
- Period of the CSL in the large magnetic field limit (\(B \gg B_{\mathrm{CSL}}\)): \(l \simeq 8\pi^3 f^2 / (\mu B)\).

## Context

This work extends the Nambu-Goto-type approach for topological defect nucleation originally developed by Basu (1991) for de Sitter backgrounds and recently applied to Schwinger effect and bubble nucleation by Ai (2020) and Hayashi (2021). It directly addresses the formation dynamics of the CSL state, whose equilibrium properties were previously studied in QCD by Brauner (2016) and in chiral magnets by Dzyaloshinskii (1964) and experimentally observed by Togawa (2016). The paper enables future studies of CSL formation in ALP dark matter scenarios and heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and opens the question of whether the Kibble-Zurek mechanism or quantum tunneling dominates CSL formation in different physical regimes.
