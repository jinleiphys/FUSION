# Extraction of Structure Functions from Quasielastic Electron Scattering (e,e') from Medium and Heavy Nuclei

> Using a relativistic mean-field single particle knock-out model for (e,e') reactions on nuclei, we investigate approximate treatments of Coulomb distortion effects and the extraction of longitudinal and transverse structure functions. We show that an effective momentum approximation (EMA) when coupled with a focusing factor provides a good description of the transverse contributions to the (e,e') cross sections for electron energies above 300 MeV on 208Pb. This approximation is not as good for the longitudinal contributions even for incident electron eneriges above 1 GeV and if one requires very precise extraction of longitudinal and transverse structure functions in the quasielastic region it is necessary to utilize distortion factors based on a nuclear model and a more accurate inclusion of Coulomb distortion effects.

## Key claim

The effective momentum approximation with a focusing factor (EMA-f) provides a good description of transverse contributions to quasielastic (e,e') cross sections on 208Pb for electron energies above 300 MeV, but fails to describe longitudinal contributions adequately even at incident energies above 1 GeV, requiring model-dependent distortion factors for precise structure function extraction.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field single-particle knock-out model for (e,e') reactions and compared three approaches to Coulomb distortion: a full distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) partial wave calculation, an "ad-hoc" model with r-dependent momentum transfer and averaged phase shifts, and the effective momentum approximation with focusing (EMA-f) where the Coulomb potential is evaluated at a fixed radius. They calculated longitudinal and transverse structure functions separately for 208Pb targets at various electron energies (310 MeV, 485 MeV, 800 MeV, and 2 GeV) and scattering angles, then compared the EMA-f results to the full DWBA calculations to quantify the approximation errors.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the Coulomb potential at the nuclear surface is about 20 MeV
- The EMA-f approximation works well for transverse contributions at electron energies above 300 MeV on 208Pb
- At 800 MeV electron energy on 208Pb, the discrepancy in the longitudinal contribution at the quasielastic peak is still almost 10%
- At 800 MeV, the transverse distortion factor D_T differs from unity by less than 3% across the quasielastic peak, while the longitudinal distortion factor D_L deviates by up to about 7%
- For electron energies above 600 MeV, the authors estimate errors in extracted structure functions using their proposed procedure should be less than 5%

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by Kim and Wright (2003) and the Basel group (Aste et al., 2004) on Coulomb distortion corrections in quasielastic electron scattering. The paper demonstrates that a simple effective momentum approximation with focusing is insufficient for precise longitudinal structure function extraction, even at high energies, and proposes a method using model-dependent distortion factors D_L and D_T that minimizes model dependence by only using the nuclear model to correct for Coulomb effects not captured by EMA-f. This enables more accurate Rosenbluth separations of longitudinal and transverse structure functions from experimental data on medium and heavy nuclei.
