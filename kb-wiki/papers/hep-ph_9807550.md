# Chiral Disorder and QCD Phase Transitions

> If QCD is to undergo a second order phase transition, the light quark return probability is universal for large times at the critical point. We show that this behavior is distinct from the one expected at the mobility edge of a metal-insulator transition or a percolation transition in d$\leq 4$. Our results are accessible to current lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

If QCD undergoes a second-order phase transition, the light quark return probability at the critical point scales universally as \(p(t)\sim (t_H/t)^{1/\delta}\) for large times, where \(\delta\) is a critical exponent; this behavior is distinct from that expected at a metal-insulator transition or a percolation transition in \(d\leq 4\).

## Method

The authors analytically compute the quark return probability \(p(t)\) in Euclidean QCD using the proper-time evolution operator of the massless Dirac operator in a fixed gluon field, averaged over gluon configurations with the unquenched QCD measure. They relate the return probability to the pion correlation function via analytical continuation of the current quark mass, and then apply scaling arguments for the pion parameters (wavefunction renormalization \(Z_\pi\) and susceptibility \(\chi_\pi\)) at the critical point of a second-order transition. The resulting expression is evaluated for large times in the ergodic regime, and the scaling exponent is compared to those from a metal-insulator transition (using multifractal wavefunction arguments) and a classical percolation transition (using fractal dimension and exponent \(\theta\)).

## Key numbers

- Vacuum diffusion constant: \(D \approx 0.22\) fm.
- Elastic time in vacuum: \(\tau_d = 1/2m_Q \approx 0.33\) fm, with constituent quark mass \(m_Q \approx 300\) MeV.
- Vacuum quark condensate: \(\Sigma \approx (250\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- Pion decay constant: \(F \approx 93\) MeV.
- Critical exponent for the metal-insulator transition in QCD: \(\eta/4 \approx 0.057\), yielding \(p(t) \sim |t|^{-0.943}\).
- For classical percolation in d=3: fractal dimension \(\tilde{D}=2.5\), exponent \(\theta=1.77\), giving \(\tilde{D}/(2+\theta)=0.66\); in d=4: \(\tilde{D}=3.2\), \(\theta=2.70\), giving \(\tilde{D}/(2+\theta)=0.68\).
- For asymmetric percolation from d=4 to d=1: \(p(t) \sim |t|^{-1/2}\).
- Temperature at which the ratio of spatial to temporal diffusion constants drops to \(\sim 0.1\): \(T \sim 180\) MeV, based on a 1-loop estimate in a linear sigma model with sigma mass \(m_\sigma \sim 500\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier framework (Janik et al., 1998) that treats light quarks in the QCD vacuum as diffusing in a disordered medium, with the quark return probability analogous to the electron return probability in Anderson localization (Anderson, 1958). The paper contrasts the predicted scaling at a second-order QCD phase transition with the multifractal behavior at a metal-insulator transition (Chalker, 1990; Kravtsov et al., 1994) and with classical percolation (Aharony, 1991; Satz, 1998). The results are directly testable in current lattice QCD simulations and provide a quantitative discriminant between different types of phase transitions.
