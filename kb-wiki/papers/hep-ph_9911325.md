# Covariant Perturbation Theory of Non-Abelian Kinetic Theory

> A double perturbation idea is presented in framework of the quark-gluon plasma kinetic theory. A solvable set of equations from the 'double perturbation' is derived and the equations are showed to be gauge-independent. The formalism of Landau damping rate for the plasmon at zero momentum is given and discussed.

# Covariant Perturbation Theory of Non-Abelian Kinetic Theory

## Key claim
The authors propose a "double perturbation" scheme for quark-gluon plasma kinetic theory that expands first in the gauge coupling constant and then iterates in powers of field strength, claiming this preserves SU(3) gauge invariance while providing solvability, a condition the abstract glosses over by not stating that the method is demonstrated only for the close-to-equilibrium case and that the full numerical result for the damping rate depends on an estimated (not exactly computed) contribution.

## Method
The authors start from semiclassical kinetic equations for quark-gluon plasma distribution functions and the background field equation. They expand the distribution functions in powers of the gauge coupling constant g, then further expand each order in powers of the field strength (the "double perturbation"), yielding a series of coupled equations for the fluctuations. As an example, they apply this formalism to compute the Landau damping rate for a plasmon at zero momentum in a close-to-equilibrium purely gluonic gas, taking the field-strength expansion up to second order.

## Key numbers
- Landau damping rate at zero momentum: γ^(σ)(0) = (a^s + a^c) × (N g^2 T) / (24π), where N is the number of colors and T the temperature.
- Numerical value of a^c: 5.973 (obtained from numerical integration).
- a^s is estimated to be of "well-matched order" with a^c (no exact numerical value given in the text).
- The calculation assumes the soft field amplitude condition a_μ ~ T, with g a_μ ~ gT of the same order as derivatives of slowly varying quantities.

## Context
This work extends prior attempts to solve non-Abelian kinetic equations while preserving gauge symmetry, which had suffered from either giving only Abelian-like leading order (expansion in coupling constant) or breaking non-Abelian gauge covariance (expansion in field strength alone, as criticized by earlier authors). The approach aims to provide a theoretical basis for resummation techniques used in hard thermal loop (HTL) calculations from field theory, such as those by previous authors. The result for the Landau damping rate shows formal correspondence to HTL results from field theory, and the method is claimed to be extendable to out-of-equilibrium situations, potentially enabling studies of dielectric physics in quark-gluon plasma beyond equilibrium.
