# Effect of the source charge on charged-beam interferometry

> We investigate quantal perturbations of the interferometric correlations of charged bosons by the Coulomb field of an instantaneous, charged source. The source charge increases the apparent source size by weakening the correlation at non-zero relative momenta. The effect is strongest for pairs with a small total momentum and is stronger for kaons than for pions of the same momenta. The experimental data currently available are well described by this effect without invoking Pratt's exploding source model. A simple expression is proposed to account for the effect.

# Effect of the source charge on charged-beam interferometry

## Key claim

The Coulomb field of an instantaneous, charged source increases the apparent source size extracted from two-boson interferometry, contradicting earlier qualitative and classical expectations that the effect would be small or in the opposite direction. The effect is strongest for pairs with small total momentum and is more pronounced for kaons than for pions, and the available experimental data can be described by this source-charge effect alone without invoking Pratt's exploding source model.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation numerically using partial-wave decomposition for the one-body scattering wave functions of charged bosons in the Coulomb field of a massive, instantaneous source, then constructed the two-boson correlation function from symmetrized products of these wave functions. They also developed an eikonal approximation to derive an analytic expression for the apparent source size as a function of the pair total momentum, and compared their exact numerical results to experimental data from Au+Au, Nb+Nb, Ar+Pb, and Ar+KCl collisions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au central collisions at 650 MeV/nucleon, the calculation with a source charge Z=158 and true source size R₀ = 1.0 fm reproduces the π⁺π⁺ data binned by total momentum P.
- For Nb+Nb collisions at 650 MeV/nucleon, the calculation with Z=82 and R₀ = 2.8 fm agrees with the π⁺π⁺ data.
- For Ar+Pb collisions at 1.8 GeV/nucleon, the calculation with Z=100 and R₀ = 2.8 fm describes the π⁻π⁻ data.
- For Ar+KCl collisions at 1.8 GeV/nucleon, the calculation with Z=36 and R₀ = 4.2 fm describes the π⁻π⁻ data.
- The exploding source model fit to the Ar+KCl data used two free parameters: a source size of 7.75 fm and an expansion rate T/γv of 100 MeV, while the source-charge model uses only one free parameter (R₀).
- The apparent size rises very rapidly, approximately as P⁻⁴, for low total momentum P.
- The effect becomes significant when the ratio ζ = (Ze²/R₀)/(P²/2m) is large, which occurs for large Z and low P (P smaller than a few hundred MeV/c).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of final-state interactions in intensity interferometry, which had examined Coulomb corrections between emitted particles (Gyulassy, 1979; Pratt, 1983; Bowler, 1985) and strong interactions with the source (Chu et al., 1996), but had neglected the Coulomb interaction between emitted particles and the source. The authors note that previous work by Baym (1977) and by Csörgő and Lörstad (1996) had only provided qualitative or classical discussions of this effect, and the present quantum-mechanical calculation finds the opposite sign for the effect. The results enable experimental disentanglement of source-charge effects from dynamical expansion effects (such as Pratt's exploding source model) by comparing the different total-momentum dependence of the apparent source size, provided data finely binned by total pair momentum become available.
