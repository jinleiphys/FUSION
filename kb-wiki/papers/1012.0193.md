# Hadronic contribution to the muon g-2 from a Dyson-Schwinger perspective

> A novel approach towards the hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_μ$ is presented, namely the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD. It has the advantage of being valid for all momentum scales and has the potential to address off-shell amplitudes. We present our first results for the pseudoscalar (PS) meson exchange and the quark loop contributions. The meson exchange ($π^0, η, η'$), $a_μ^{\textrm{LBL;PS}}=(84 \pm 13)\times 10^{-11}$, is commensurate with previous calculations, while the quark loop contribution $a_μ^{\textrm{LBL;quarkloop}} = (107 \pm 48)\times 10^{-11}$, is strongly enhanced by vertex dressing effects in the quark photon vertex. Taken seriously this leads to the estimate of $a_μ=116\,591\,865.0(96.6)\times 10^{-11}$, giving a 1.9 $σ$ deviation between theory and experiment.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) approach to the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2 yields a total estimate of \(a_\mu = 116\,591\,865.0(96.6) \times 10^{-11}\), which reduces the discrepancy between theory and experiment to 1.9 sigma, but this result depends on the inclusion of vertex dressing effects in the quark loop that strongly enhance the contribution compared to previous models.

## Method

The authors used Dyson-Schwinger equations and Bethe-Salpeter equations within a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD to compute the fully dressed quark propagator, the quark-photon vertex, and pseudoscalar meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The photon four-point function was approximated by a sum of a quark loop diagram and meson pole diagrams for \(\pi^0, \eta, \eta'\), with all internal quarks and vertices fully dressed. The quark loop contribution was evaluated both with bare vertices and with the leading part of the Ball-Chiu vertex construction (1BC) to assess vertex dressing effects.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar meson exchange total: \(a_\mu^{\textrm{LBL;PS}} = (84.3 \pm 12.8) \times 10^{-11}\), broken into \(\pi^0\): \((57.5 \pm 6.9) \times 10^{-11}\), \(\eta\): \((15.8 \pm 3.5) \times 10^{-11}\), \(\eta'\): \((11.0 \pm 2.4) \times 10^{-11}\)
- Quark loop with bare vertex: \(a_\mu^{\textrm{LBL;quarkloop (bare vertex)}} = (61 \pm 2) \times 10^{-11}\)
- Quark loop with 1BC vertex: \(a_\mu^{\textrm{LBL;quarkloop (1BC)}} = (107 \pm 2) \times 10^{-11}\)
- Total estimate: \(a_\mu = 116\,591\,865.0(96.6) \times 10^{-11}\), giving a 1.9 sigma deviation from the experimental value of \(116\,592\,089.0(63.0) \times 10^{-11}\)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of hadronic light-by-light scattering that used large-\(N_c\) and chiral effective theory, the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Bijnens 1995, Bijnens 2007), the hidden local symmetry model (Hayakawa 1995), vector meson dominance approaches (Knecht 2001, Melnikov 2003, Nyffeler 2009), and the nonlocal chiral quark model (Dorokhov 2004). The DSE approach is novel in that it is valid for all momentum scales and can address off-shell amplitudes without matching ultraviolet and infrared descriptions. The next step is to include the full non-perturbative quark-photon vertex and evaluate the full resummation of planar diagrams without the pole approximation.
