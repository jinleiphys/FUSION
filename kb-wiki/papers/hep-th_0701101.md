# Large transient states in quantum field theory

> A study is made of the scattering of two large composite projectiles, such as heavy ions, which are initially prepared in a pure quantum state. It is shown that the quantum field theoretic evolution equation for this system, under certain conditions, goes over in form to the master equation of classical statistical mechanics. Thus, the statistical mechanical description of heavy ion collision is viewed as an implied outcome of the Correspondence Principle, which states that in the limit of large quantum number, quantum dynamics goes over to classical dynamics. This hypothesis is explored within the master equation transcription with particular focus on the quark-gluon formation scenario.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quantum field theoretic evolution equation for the scattering of two large composite projectiles (such as heavy ions) initially prepared in a pure quantum state can, under certain conditions, be transcribed into the master equation of classical statistical mechanics; this transcription is presented as an implied outcome of the Correspondence Principle, but the author explicitly clarifies that the treatment does not pertain to statistical density matrices and is not related to quantum measurement theory, focusing instead on pure quantum states that can be described in thermodynamic and statistical mechanical terms only in the limit of large quantum number.

## Method

The author develops a three-step framework of projection, contraction, and evolution for large transient states in heavy ion collisions. In the projection step, formal criteria are specified for selecting events that emerge from large transient states using a projection operator that isolates such states within scattering theory. In the contraction step, the description of the transient state is tempered to a level adequate for experimental measurement, recognizing that complete theoretical knowledge is unnecessary because experimental resolution is grossly imperfect. Finally, the evolution step addresses the dynamical time development of the transient state, leading to a master equation that describes the evolution of two colliding heavy ions as a pure quantum state.

## Key numbers

- RHIC operates at 200 GeV/nucleon
- ALICE at LHC is planned to operate at 5.5 TeV/nucleon
- The central region of the transient state is described as approximately 3 units of central rapidity
- The formation period is defined between times \(t_c^- < 0\) and \(t_c^+ > 0\), where \(t_c^+ = |t_c^-|\) if both wavepackets are symmetrical about their respective centers

## Context

This work extends the large transient state hypothesis of heavy ion collision as a specific concept independent of the particulars of the constituents (such as quark-gluon plasma), building on earlier understanding of the transcription between pure quantum evolution and the master equation for general quantum mechanical systems by van Kampen (year not specified) and others. The author notes one previous effort by Te (year not specified) that addressed a similar problem. The paper aims to provide a formal link between conventional quantum field theoretic scattering and nonequilibrium statistical mechanics, enabling a statistical mechanical description of individual heavy ion collision events rather than ensemble averages.
