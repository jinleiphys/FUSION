# Quantum Magic and Computational Complexity in the Neutrino Sector

> We consider the quantum magic in systems of dense neutrinos undergoing coherent flavor transformations, relevant for supernova and neutron-star binary mergers. Mapping the three-flavor-neutrino system to qutrits, the evolution of quantum magic is explored in the single scattering angle limit for a selection of initial tensor-product pure states for $N_ν\le 8$ neutrinos. For $|ν_e\rangle^{\otimes N_ν}$ initial states, the magic, as measured by the $α=2$ stabilizer Renyi entropy $M_2$, is found to decrease with radial distance from the neutrino sphere, reaching a value that lies below the maximum for tensor-product qutrit states. Further, the asymptotic magic per neutrino, $M_2/N_ν$, decreases with increasing $N_ν$. In contrast, the magic evolving from states containing all three flavors reaches values only possible with entanglement, with the asymptotic $M_2/N_ν$ increasing with $N_ν$. These results highlight the connection between the complexity in simulating quantum physical systems and the parameters of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the quantum magic (non-stabilizerness) in dense neutrino systems undergoing coherent flavor transformations depends strongly on the initial flavor composition: for initial states of only electron-flavor neutrinos, the magic per neutrino decreases with system size, while for initial states containing all three flavors, the magic per neutrino increases with system size and reaches values only possible with entanglement, suggesting that quantum computational resources will be required to simulate these systems.

## Method

The authors map the three-flavor neutrino system to qutrits and compute the stabilizer Renyi entropy M2 (with alpha=2) as a measure of quantum magic. They evolve pure tensor-product initial states of up to Nν ≤ 8 neutrinos under a low-energy effective Hamiltonian that includes one-body free-space evolution and two-body coherent forward interactions in the single-angle limit, using a neutrino density profile with μ0 = 3.62 × 10^4 MeV and κRν = 32.2. The magic is computed from the probability distribution formed by the squared magnitudes of Pauli-string expectation values, and asymptotic values are determined by averaging over late-time intervals after κt ≳ 800.

## Key numbers

- For a single three-flavor neutrino, the magic is M2 = 0.891(14), compared to a maximum possible value of 1
- For a single neutrino in the effective two-flavor system, the magic is M2 = 0.195(23), compared to a maximum of 0.415 for real states and 0.585 for complex states
- For two-neutrino systems, the maximum asymptotic magic values are 0.871 (two flavors) and 1.491 (three flavors)
- The two-body interaction strength at the neutrino sphere is μ0 = 3.62 × 10^4 MeV, with κ = 10^(-17) MeV and κRν = 32.2
- The maximum magic for tensor-product qutrit states is M2 = Nν (one per neutrino)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of entanglement in dense neutrino systems (Illa 2022, Martin 2023) by introducing the concept of quantum magic as a measure of computational complexity beyond entanglement. It builds on the Gottesman-Knill theorem and stabilizer formalism to argue that both large-scale entanglement and significant magic are necessary to require quantum computing resources. The results connect to broader observations that Standard Model parameters may be near extremal points in entanglement power (Cervera-Lierta 2017, Beane 2018) and complement recent studies of magic in nuclear forces (Robin 2024) and lattice gauge models (Tarabunga 2023). The work enables future studies of larger neutrino systems and motivates incorporating magic analysis into realistic supernova simulations with full kinetics and scattering processes.
