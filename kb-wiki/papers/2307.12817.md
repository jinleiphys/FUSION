# Recent astrophysical observations reproduced by a short-range correlated van der Waals-type model?

> We perform an improvement in a van der Waals-type model by including it effects of short-range correlations (SRC). Attractive and repulsive parts of the nucleon-nucleon interaction are assumed to be density-dependent functions, more specifically, we adopt the Carnahan-Starling (CS) method for the latter, and a suitable expression for the former in order to reproduce the structure of the Clausius (C) real gas model. The parametrizations of the resulting model, named as CCS-SRC model, are shown to be capable of reproducing the flow constraint at the high-density regime of symmetric nuclear matter for incompressibility values inside the range of $K_0=(240\pm 20)$ MeV. In the context of stellar matter, our findings point out a good agreement of the CCS-SRC model with recent astrophysical observational data, namely, mass-radius contours and dimensionless tidal deformability regions and values, coming from gravitational waves data related to the GW170817 and GW190425 events, and from the NASA's Neutron star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) mission. Furthermore, the values for the symmetry energy slope of the model ($L_0$) are in agreement with a recent range found for this quantity, claimed to be consistent with results reported by the updated lead radius experiment (PREX-2) collaboration. In this case, higher values of $L_0$ are favored, while the opposite scenario does not allow simultaneous compatibility between the model and the astrophysical data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a van der Waals-type model improved by including short-range correlations (SRC) can simultaneously reproduce the flow constraint for symmetric nuclear matter and recent astrophysical observational data, but only for parametrizations that favor higher values of the symmetry energy slope \(L_0\); lower values of \(L_0\) do not allow compatibility with the astrophysical data.

## Method

The authors generalize a previously developed density-dependent van der Waals model (DD-vdW) by incorporating short-range correlations through a high-momentum tail (HMT) in the single-nucleon momentum distributions, using parameters fixed from experimental data on electron scattering and two-nucleon knockout reactions. The repulsive part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is treated with the Carnahan-Starling (CS) excluded volume method, and the attractive part follows the Clausius (C) form, yielding the CCS-SRC model. The four free parameters are determined by imposing saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(B_0 = -16\) MeV, symmetry energy \(J = 32\) MeV, and various values of incompressibility \(K_0\) within the range \(240 \pm 20\) MeV. The resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for neutron star structure and to compute tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: \(K_0 = (240 \pm 20)\) MeV, the range used for parametrizations.
- Saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Binding energy at saturation: \(B_0 = -16\) MeV.
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(J = 32\) MeV.
- Fraction of nucleons in the high-momentum tail: \(x^{\text{HMT}}_{\text{SNM}} = 28\%\) for symmetric nuclear matter and \(x^{\text{HMT}}_{\text{PNM}} = 1.5\%\) for pure neutron matter.
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}\) for a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star.
- Kinetic part of the symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{\text{sym}}^{\text{kin}}(\rho_0) = -14.7\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent van der Waals model developed by Lourenço et al. (2019) and the Clausius-CS model studied by Vovchenko et al. (2017) by incorporating short-range correlations, which were previously applied to relativistic mean-field models by Baoli et al. (2015, 2016) and Cai et al. (2016). The CCS-SRC model is shown to satisfy the flow constraint from Danielewicz et al. (2002) and to reproduce mass-radius contours and tidal deformability data from the GW170817 and GW190425 gravitational wave events and from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. The results enable further studies of the interplay between short-range correlations and excluded volume effects in neutron star matter, and they favor higher values of the symmetry energy slope consistent with the PREX-2 experiment.
