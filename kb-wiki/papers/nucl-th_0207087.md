# Q^2-evolution of Delta N gamma form factors up to 4 (GeV/c)^2 from JLab data

> We present the results on the ratios $E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}$ and $S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}$ for the $γ^* N \to Δ(1232)$ transition at $Q^2\leq 4 (GeV/c)^2$ extracted from the $p(e,e'p)π^0$ cross section using two approaches: dispersion relations and modified version of unitary isobar model. The obtained results are in good agreement with the results of other analyses obtained using truncated multipole expansion at $Q^2=0.4,~0.525,~0.65,~0.75,~0.9,~1.15,~1.45,~1.8 (GeV/c)^2 $ and within dynamical and unitary isobar models at $Q^2=2.8,~4 (GeV/c)^2 $. According to obtained results the ratio $E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}$ remains small in all investigated region of $Q^2$ with very unclear tendency to cross zero above $2 (GeV/c)^2$. The absolute value of the ratio $S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}$ is clearly increasing with increasing $Q^2$, while it should be a constant value in the pQCD asymptotics. So, at $Q^2\leq4 (GeV/c)^2$ there is no evidence of approaching pQCD regime for these ratios. None of the soft approaches gives satisfactory description of the obtained results.

## Key claim

The ratios \(E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) and \(S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) for the \(\gamma^* N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition, extracted from JLab data up to \(Q^2 = 4~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\), show no evidence of approaching the perturbative QCD regime; the absolute value of \(S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) is clearly increasing with \(Q^2\) rather than approaching a constant, and the small ratio \(E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) has only an unclear tendency to cross zero above \(2~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\).

## Method

The ratios were extracted from JLab \(p(e,e'p)\pi^0\) cross-section data in the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance region using two independent analysis methods: dispersion relations and a modified version of the unitary isobar model. The multipoles \(M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\), \(E_{1+}^{(3/2)}\), and \(S_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) were evaluated at the resonance position where the phase \(\delta_{1+}^{(3/2)} = \pi/2\), corresponding to the "dressed" \(\gamma N \Delta\) vertex.

## Key numbers

- \(E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) remains small across all \(Q^2\) up to \(4~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\), with an unclear tendency to cross zero above \(2~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\).
- The absolute value of \(S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) is clearly increasing with \(Q^2\) up to \(4~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\), contrary to the constant value expected in pQCD asymptotics.
- Results are reported at \(Q^2 = 0.4,~0.525,~0.65,~0.75,~0.9,~1.15,~1.45,~1.8,~2.8,~4~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\).
- Two sets of results exist at \(Q^2 = 0.65,~0.75,~0.9~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\) due to two different initial electron energies in the measurements.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of the \(\gamma^* N \to \Delta(1232)\) transition by providing results from JLab data using dispersion relations and a modified unitary isobar model, in agreement with truncated multipole expansion analyses at lower \(Q^2\) (up to \(1.8~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\)) and with dynamical and unitary isobar model results at \(Q^2 = 2.8\) and \(4~(\text{GeV}/c)^2\). The results contradict the expectation that pQCD behavior would set in at these momentum transfers, as argued by proponents of the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky wave function approach (Chernyak and Zhitnitsky, 1984). None of the existing soft approaches (light-cone relativistic quark models, relativized quark model, or interpolation models) simultaneously describe both ratios, indicating that more detailed investigations of soft mechanisms for this transition are needed, and measurements at higher \(Q^2\) are required to determine the scale where hard mechanisms begin to dominate.
