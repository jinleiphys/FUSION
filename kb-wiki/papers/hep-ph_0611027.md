# Constraints on Gravitino Dark Matter Scenarios with Long-Lived Charged Sleptons

> Considering scenarios in which the gravitino is the lightest supersymmetric particle and a charged slepton the next-to-lightest supersymmetric particle (NLSP), we discuss cosmological constraints on the masses of the gravitino and the NLSP slepton. The presented mass bounds are crucial for gravitino dark matter studies and potential gravitino signatures at future colliders.

## Key claim

Cosmological constraints on gravitino dark matter scenarios with a long-lived charged slepton as the next-to-lightest supersymmetric particle (NLSP) impose upper limits on the gravitino and stau NLSP masses, but these bounds depend critically on the assumed thermal abundance of the stau NLSP and on whether bound-state effects during big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) are included.

## Method

The author considers R-parity conserving supersymmetric scenarios where the gravitino is the lightest supersymmetric particle (LSP) and a pure right-handed stau is the NLSP. The gravitino relic density is computed as the sum of thermal production (TP) from the early Universe and non-thermal production (NTP) from stau NLSP decays, using analytic expressions for the thermal production rate and assuming stau freeze-out with a thermal abundance. Cosmological constraints are derived by requiring the total gravitino density not exceed the observed cold dark matter density, and by applying BBN limits on electromagnetic and hadronic energy injection from late stau decays, including a 4-body decay calculation for hadronic channels and bound-state formation effects on lithium production.

## Key numbers

- Observed cold dark matter density: $\Omega_{\mathrm{CDM}}h^2 = 0.105^{+0.014}_{-0.020}$ (95% CL)
- Reduced Planck scale: $\mathrm{M}_{\mathrm{P}} = 2.4 \times 10^{18}$ GeV
- Stau NLSP yield (for $m_{\tilde{\tau}_1} \ll m_{\tilde{e}_1,\tilde{\mu}_1}$ and bino mass $m_{\tilde{B}} = 1.1 m_{\tilde{\tau}_1}$): $Y_{\tilde{\tau}} = 0.725 \times 10^{-12} (m_{\tilde{\tau}_1} / 1\ \mathrm{TeV})$
- Free-streaming upper limits exclude $m_{\tilde{\tau}_1} \lesssim 0.7$ TeV for scenarios where $\Omega_{\tilde{G}}^{\mathrm{NTP}} \simeq \Omega_{\mathrm{CDM}}$
- Bound-state formation from long-lived staus implies $\tau_{\tilde{\tau}} \lesssim 4 \times 10^3$ seconds from enhanced $^6$Li production
- Stau NLSP lifetime formula: $\tau_{\tilde{\tau}} = (48 \pi m_{\tilde{G}}^2 \mathrm{M}_{\mathrm{P}}^2 / m_{\tilde{\tau}}^5) (1 - m_{\tilde{G}}^2 / m_{\tilde{\tau}}^2)^{-4}$
- Kinematical determination of $m_{\tilde{G}}$ appears feasible only for $m_{\tilde{G}} / m_{\tilde{\tau}} \gtrsim 0.1$

## Context

This work extends prior gravitino dark matter studies by Borgani (1996), Bolz (1998, 2000), Asaka (2000), and Feng (2004) by incorporating updated BBN constraints from Kawasaki (2004) and Cyburt (2002), as well as new bound-state effects on lithium production from Pospelov (2006) and Cyburt (2006). The mass bounds presented are crucial for interpreting potential long-lived stau signatures at future colliders, as proposed by Buchmuller (2004) and Martyn (2006), and for determining whether the gravitino can be identified as the LSP through collider measurements.
