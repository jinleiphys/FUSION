# Thermal field theory derivation of the source term induced by a fast parton from the quark energy-momentum tensor

> I derive the distribution of energy and momentum transmitted from a fast parton to a medium of thermalized quarks, or the source term, in perturbative thermal field theory directly from the quark energy-momentum tensor. The fast parton is coupled to the medium by adding an interaction term to the Lagrangian. The thermal expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor source term is then evaluated using standard Feynman rules at finite temperature. It is found that local excitations, which are important for exciting an observable Mach cone structure, fall sharply as a function of the energy of the fast parton. This may have implications for the trigger $p_T$ dependence of measurements of azimuthal dihadron particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions. In particular, a conical emission pattern would be less likely to be observed for increasing trigger $p_T$. I show that the results presented in this paper can be generalized to more realistic modeling of fast parton propagation, such as through a time dependent interaction term, in future studies.

# Thermal field theory derivation of the source term induced by a fast parton from the quark energy-momentum tensor

## Key claim
The source term describing energy and momentum transfer from a fast parton to a thermalized quark medium is derived directly from the quark energy-momentum tensor using perturbative thermal field theory. The local excitation coefficient lambda, which is crucial for generating observable Mach cone structures in the medium, falls sharply as a function of the fast parton's Lorentz factor gamma, implying that a conical emission pattern would be less likely to be observed for increasing trigger transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The fast parton is modeled as an external current coupling to the Lagrangian via an interaction term, and the thermal expectation value of the divergence of the quark energy-momentum tensor is evaluated using real-time finite-temperature Feynman rules. The calculation proceeds to leading order in perturbation theory for a medium of massless quarks and antiquarks, with the fast parton represented by an asymptotically propagating classical current. The full result is compared to the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, which reproduces a previous kinetic theory result, and the source term is then cast into a simplified ansatz parameterized by the total energy loss rate dE/dt and a local excitation coefficient lambda.

## Key numbers
- The total rate of energy transfer to the medium in the HTL approximation is given by dE/dt_HTL = (m_D^2 * C_2 * alpha_s / 2) * (1 - tanh^{-1}[u] / (gamma^2 * u)) * ln(p_max / p_min), where m_D^2 = g^2 * T^2 * N_F / 6 (quarks only), p_max ~ T, and p_min ~ m_D.
- The calculation uses experimentally relevant values of g = 1 and T = 400 MeV for the numerical evaluation of lambda.
- The separation scale q* for dividing the full and HTL contributions satisfies g*T << q* << T.

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological studies of the QGP response to fast partons, which have mostly used hydrodynamics with an unspecified source term (machphenom). It provides a first-principles derivation of the source term that previous studies (Neufeld and Renk) found to sensitively affect the azimuthal dihadron correlation spectrum. The HTL limit reproduces the kinetic theory result of the source term from a prior calculation (kineticsource). The result enables future studies using the derived Feynman rules for more realistic modeling, such as time-dependent interaction terms or inclusion of medium gluons, and makes contact with experimental measurements of trigger p_T dependence in azimuthal dihadron correlations at RHIC (Adare 2010).
