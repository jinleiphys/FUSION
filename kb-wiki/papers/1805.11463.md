# Kinetic freeze out conditions in nuclear collisions with $2A$ - $158A$ GeV beam energy within a non-boost-invariant blast-wave model

> We study the kinetic freeze out conditions of bulk hadrons in nuclear collisions. The transverse and longitudinal momentum spectra of the identified hadrons produced in central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, in the beam energy range of $E_b = 2A - 158 A$ GeV are analysed for this purpose, within a generalised non boost-invariant blast wave model. The kinetic freeze out temperature is found to vary in the range of 55 - 90 MeV, whereas the average transverse velocity of collective expansion is found to be around $0.5c$ to $0.6c$. The mean longitudinal velocity of the fireball is seen to increase monotonically with increasing longitudinal boost. The results would be useful to understand the gross collision dynamics for the upcoming experiments at the FAIR and NICA accelerator facilities.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature in central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions across beam energies from 2A to 158A GeV is found to be relatively low (55–90 MeV) when using a non-boost-invariant blast-wave model, contradicting earlier boost-invariant analyses that reported temperatures above 100 MeV; the lower temperature arises because the model includes resonance decay contributions and uses a fixed transverse flow profile exponent n=1.

## Method

The authors analyzed transverse momentum (pT) and rapidity spectra of pions, kaons, and protons from central Au+Au collisions at AGS (E895, 2–8A GeV), Pb+Pb collisions at SPS (NA49, 20–158A GeV), and Au+Au collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (STAR, 30.67 and 69.56A GeV). They employed a non-boost-invariant blast-wave model with an ellipsoidal fireball shape in space-time rapidity (η) and transverse radius, assuming a Hubble-like transverse flow profile (n=1) and including resonance decays up to Δ(1232). Three common parameters, kinetic freeze-out temperature Tkin, maximum space-time rapidity ηmax, and surface transverse velocity βT⁰, were extracted by simultaneously fitting the pT spectra of multiple hadron species at mid-rapidity, then used to describe the rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature Tkin ranges from 55.86 ± 1.38 MeV (at 4A GeV) to 84.11 ± 0.06 MeV (at 158A GeV), with the lowest value 61.72 ± 1.36 MeV at 2A GeV.
- Average transverse velocity ⟨βT⟩ ranges from 0.4838 ± 0.0034c (at 2A GeV) to 0.5655 ± 0.0031c (at 8A GeV), with values at SPS energies around 0.5177–0.538c.
- Maximum space-time rapidity ηmax increases monotonically from 0.995 ± 0.001 (at 2A GeV) to 2.621 ± 0.006 (at 158A GeV).
- χ²/NDF values for the simultaneous pT fits range from 3.8 (at 80A GeV) to 9.4 (at 6A GeV).

## Context

This work extends the non-boost-invariant blast-wave model of Dobler et al. (1999), which was applied only to 11.6A GeV Au+Au collisions, to a broad beam energy range from 2A to 158A GeV. The results contrast with earlier boost-invariant blast-wave analyses (Sandip et al.) that found higher freeze-out temperatures (Tkin > 100 MeV) at AGS energies, and with empirical two-slope fits by Klay et al. (2003) that reported inverse slope parameters T1 ~ 50 MeV and T2 ~ 130 MeV. The findings provide baseline thermodynamic conditions for interpreting upcoming experiments at FAIR and NICA facilities.
