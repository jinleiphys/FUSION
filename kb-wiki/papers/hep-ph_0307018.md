# Recent progress on the light meson resonance description from unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory and large Nc

> We present a brief account of the developments in the description of light meson resonances using unitarized extensions of the Chiral Perturbation Theory series, both in energy and temperature. In particular, we describe how these methods have been recently shown to describe simultaneously the low energy and resonance regions of meson-meson scattering. This approach could be of relevance to understand the light scalar mesons since it provides a formalism that respects chiral symmetry and unitarity and is able to generate resonant states without any a priori theoretical bias toward their existence, classification or spectroscopic nature. We will also review how this approach is also able to describe the thermal evolution of the $ρ$ and $σ$ mesons. In addition we review their extensions to higher orders, the most recent determination of the resonance pole properties, as well as their behavior in the large $N_c$ limit, which could be of relevance to understand their spectroscopic nature.

## Key claim

Unitarized extensions of Chiral Perturbation Theory can simultaneously describe the low-energy region and the resonance region of meson-meson scattering, generating resonant states without any a priori theoretical bias toward their existence, classification, or spectroscopic nature, while using chiral parameters compatible with standard determinations.

## Method

The authors unitarize the complete one-loop ChPT meson-meson scattering amplitudes using the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) in coupled channels, which respects chiral symmetry and unitarity. The IAM is derived from the unitarity condition by writing the partial wave amplitude as T ≃ T₂ (T₂ − T₄)⁻¹ T₂, where T₂ and T₄ are the leading and next-to-leading order ChPT contributions. The method is extended to two-loop ππ scattering, to channels with vanishing leading order (J>1), and to finite temperature by incorporating thermal loop corrections with Bose-Einstein phase space factors.

## Context

This work extends the earlier approximated coupled-channel IAM of Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1997) by completing all one-loop meson-meson amplitudes with full renormalization in the standard MS-1 scheme, enabling direct comparison with standard ChPT parameters from Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985). The approach enables systematic study of light scalar meson spectroscopy, including the controversial σ, κ, f₀(980), and a₀(980) states, and their behavior in the large N_c limit, which may help distinguish qq̄ states from other configurations. The thermal extension provides predictions for the temperature dependence of the ρ and σ poles relevant to heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
