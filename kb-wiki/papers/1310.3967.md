# Probing isospin- and momentum-dependent nuclear effective interactions in neutron-rich matter

> The single-particle potentials for nucleons and hyperons in neutron-rich matter generally depends on the density and isospin asymmetry of the medium as well as the momentum and isospin of the particle. It further depends on the temperature of the matter if the latter is in thermal equilibrium. We review here the extension of a Gogny-type isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction in several aspects made in recent years and their applications in studying intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions, thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter and properties of neutron stars. The importance of the isospin- and momentum-dependence of the single-particle potential, especially the momentum dependence of the isovector potential, is clearly revealed throughout these studies.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the extension of a Gogny-type isospin- and momentum-dependent (MDI) interaction and its applications, arguing that the momentum dependence of the isovector potential is critically important for understanding intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter, and neutron star properties, though the review itself does not present a single new experimental result.

## Method

The authors review the parametrization of the MDI interaction, which is a generalized isospin-dependent version of the momentum-dependent Yukawa interaction (MDYI), with parameters fitted to the single-particle potentials and nuclear matter equation of state predicted by the finite-range Gogny effective interaction using parameter set D1. The interaction is implemented in the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model, and the potential energy density is expressed in terms of neutron and proton densities, a density-dependent term, and a finite-range Yukawa-type momentum-dependent term. The parameter \(x\) is varied to produce different density dependences of the nuclear symmetry energy while keeping the symmetry energy at saturation density fixed at 30.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is fixed to 30.5 MeV in the MDI interaction.
* Incompressibility \(K_0\) of cold symmetric nuclear matter at \(\rho_0\) is set to 211 MeV.
* The parameter \(\sigma = 4/3\) follows the Gogny interaction; \(B = 106.35\) MeV in the MDI interaction.
* Interaction strength parameters: \(C_l = -11.7\) MeV and \(C_u = -103.4\) MeV for like and unlike nucleon interactions, respectively.
* The sum \(A_{l0} + A_{u0} = -216.55\) MeV is constant, independent of the parameter \(x\).

## Context

This review extends the work of Das et al. (2003) who introduced the MDI interaction, and builds on the earlier momentum-dependent Yukawa interaction (MDYI) of Welke et al. (1988) and the isospin-dependent BGBD interaction of Bombaci (2001). The paper enables future studies of heavy-ion collisions at rare isotope beam facilities (e.g., FRIB, FAIR, RIBF) and provides a framework for connecting nuclear symmetry energy measurements to neutron star properties, while highlighting that the density dependence of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities remains poorly constrained.
