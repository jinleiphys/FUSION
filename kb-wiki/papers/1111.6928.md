# Nonlinear equation for coherent gluon emission

> Motivated by the regime of QCD explored nowadays at LHC, where both the total energy of collision and momenta transfers are high, we investigate evolution equations of high energy factorization. In order to study such effects like parton saturation in final states one is inevitably led to investigate how to combine physics of the BK and CCFM evolution equations. In this paper we obtain a new exclusive form of the BK equation which suggests a possible form of the nonlinear extension of the CCFM equation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new exclusive form of the BK (Balitsky-Kovchegov) equation in which both the linear and nonlinear terms are folded with a Regge form factor, and then proposes a nonlinear extension of the CCFM (Catani-Ciafaloni-Fiorani-Marchesini) equation by replacing the Regge form factor with the non-Sudakov form factor and introducing angular ordering, though the authors note this extension is "to some extend arbitrary since at present we do not have diagrammatic picture of the triple pomeron vertex which would account for coherence."

## Method

The authors start from the momentum-space BK equation for the dipole amplitude and rewrite it as an integral equation following the Kwiecinski-Martin-Stasto (KMS) framework. They reintroduce angular dependence, decompose the linear part into resolved and unresolved real emissions using a resolution scale μ, perform a Mellin transform to resum virtual and unresolved real contributions, and invert the transform to obtain a new exclusive form where the Regge form factor exponentiates the singularities. They then propose a nonlinear extension of the CCFM equation by replacing the Regge form factor with the non-Sudakov form factor, introducing angular ordering via the θ(p - zq̄) condition, and adding a nonlinear term with a δ-function structure analogous to the exclusive BK form.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling combination used is ᾱ_s = N_c α_s / π, where N_c is the number of colors.
- The Regge form factor is defined as Δ_R(z, k, μ) ≡ exp(-ᾱ_s ln(1/z) ln(k²/μ²)).
- The non-Sudakov form factor is Δ_ns(z, k, q) = exp(-ᾱ_s ln(1/z) ln(k²/(z q²))).
- The Sudakov form factor is Δ_s(p, zq̄) = exp(-ᾱ_s ∫_{(zq̄)²}^{p²} (dq²/q²) ∫₀¹ dz/(1-z)).
- The angular ordering condition is expressed as θ(p - zq̄) where p is the hard scale and q̄ is the rescaled transverse momentum of the real gluon.

## Context

This work extends the BK equation (Balitsky 1995, Kovchegov 1999) and the CCFM equation (Ciafaloni 1987, Catani 1989) by combining their physics to study parton saturation in final states at LHC energies. The resummation procedure follows the approach of Kwiecinski (1996) for handling virtual and unresolved real emissions. The proposed nonlinear CCFM equation aims to enable studies of exclusive observables with saturation effects, complementing earlier approaches to nonlinear extensions by Kutak (2008), Avsar (2009, 2010), and the inter-jet resummation framework of Banfi (2002), Marchesini (2003), and Weigert (2003), with the exact mapping found by Hatta (2008).
