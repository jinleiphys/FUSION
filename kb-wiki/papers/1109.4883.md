# Charge-dependent Azimuthal Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions and Electromagnetic Effects

> We propose a scenario where the pattern of the recently observed charge-dependent azimuthal correlations can be understood qualitatively. This is based on the cluster picture and the assumption that the charged hadrons that flow outward from the surface of the overlapping region of the colliding nuclei move primarily parallel to the reaction plane. We also point out the there is a strong electric field induced by the transient magnetic field during the parton production in the initial phase of the relativistic heavy-ion collision and discuss its possible relevance to the scenario.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a qualitative scenario based on a cluster model and the assumption that charged hadrons emerging from surface clusters move primarily parallel to the reaction plane, which can explain the observed pattern of charge-dependent azimuthal correlations without invoking the chiral magnetic effect; the scenario is presented as a background interpretation, not a definitive explanation.

## Method

The author analyzes the STAR collaboration's charge-dependent azimuthal correlations, specifically the quantities ⟨sin(φ_α)sin(φ_β)⟩ and ⟨cos(φ_α)cos(φ_β)⟩ for like-sign and unlike-sign charged pairs, using a cluster model framework. The model assumes that correlated pairs originate from clusters on the surface of the nuclear overlap region, with outward-going hadron pairs moving nearly parallel to the reaction plane (small azimuthal angles) and inward-going pairs emerging at large azimuthal angles. The author also calculates the transient electric field induced by the rapidly changing magnetic field via Faraday's law during the initial phase of the collision and estimates its effect on parton momentum.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au collisions at √S_NN = 200 GeV and impact parameter b = 10 fm, the magnetic field eB_y at the center reaches a maximum of ~4.5 m_π² at t = 0.05 fm/c and decays to ~0.7 m_π² at t = 0.15 fm/c.
* The peak electric field from the Coulomb potential eE_x is ~0.5 m_π² at x = 1-3 fm and t = 0.05-0.1 fm/c, an order of magnitude smaller than the peak magnetic field.
* The induced electric field from Faraday's law is |eE_F| = 57 m_π² at r = 3 fm (the surface of the overlapping region for b = 10 fm), yielding a momentum transfer |Δp_∥| = 560 MeV/c.
* The effective cluster size M_cluster is ~3 in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, giving an average of ~4.5 pairs per cluster.
* With parameters c = -0.55, C_LS = S_US = C_US = 0.8, and λ = M_cluster = 3, the model yields ratios ⟨cos(φ_α)cos(φ_β)⟩_LS : ⟨sin(φ_α)sin(φ_β)⟩_US : ⟨cos(φ_α)cos(φ_β)⟩_US = -0.46 : 1 : 1.09, in quantitative agreement with experiment for 35%-65% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the cluster model analysis of azimuthal correlations previously applied to pp, Cu+Cu, and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV by the PHENIX collaboration, and addresses the STAR collaboration's measurements of charge-dependent azimuthal correlations originally designed to search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) proposed by Kharzeev (1998) and Kharzeev, McLerran, and Warringa (2008). The paper provides a non-CME background interpretation that could enable more precise extraction of any CME signal once the electromagnetic effects are incorporated into dynamical models such as Hadron String Dynamics (HSD), HIJING, AMPT, NeXus, the momentum kick model by Wong (2011), and 3D Relativistic Hydrodynamics.
