# Thermalization and Flow of Heavy Quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Elastic scattering of charm (c) and bottom (b) quarks via D- and B-meson resonance states in an expanding, strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma is investigated. Drag and diffusion coefficients are calculated from an effective model based on chiral symmetry and heavy-quark effective theory, and utilized in a relativistic Langevin simulation to obtain transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow (v_2) of c- and b-quarks. The hadronization to D- and B-mesons is described by coalescence and fragmentation, and the resulting decay-electron spectra are compared to recent RHIC data.

## Key claim

Elastic scattering of charm and bottom quarks via D- and B-meson resonance states in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma can explain the observed large elliptic flow and strong suppression of decay electrons at RHIC, but this mechanism depends on the assumption that these mesonic resonance states survive in the plasma up to temperatures of about twice the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors calculated drag and diffusion coefficients from an effective model based on chiral symmetry and heavy-quark effective theory, using resonant D- and B-meson cross sections supplemented with leading-order pQCD processes (with alpha_s = 0.4). These coefficients were used in a relativistic Langevin simulation of heavy-quark rescattering in an isentropically expanding QGP fireball corresponding to b=7 fm Au-Au collisions at RHIC, with hadronization described by a combined quark-coalescence and fragmentation model followed by semileptonic D- and B-meson three-body decays.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-quark thermalization times from resonance scattering are lower by a factor of about 3 compared to pQCD scattering alone
- Initial temperature of the QGP fireball: T_0 = 340 MeV, with formation time of 1/3 fm/c
- Cross-section ratio sigma_{bb}/sigma_{cc} = 4.9 x 10^{-3}
- Crossing of D- and B-decay electrons occurs at p_T approximately 5 GeV
- Resonance rescattering of charm quarks leads to R_AA down to 0.2 and v_2 up to 10%
- The strong coupling constant used: alpha_s = 0.4

## Context

This work extends the resonance-scattering approach of van Hees and Rapp (2004) by implementing it in a full Langevin simulation with expanding fireball dynamics, and extends the coalescence model of Greco, Ko, and Rapp (2004) by including thermal weight factors for D* meson production. The results contradict perturbative QCD approaches (such as those by Djordjevic, Gyulassy, and Wicks (2005) and Armesto et al. (2005)) which cannot reproduce the large elliptic flow of heavy quarks, and suggest that nonperturbative resonance formation may be essential for understanding heavy-quark thermalization in the strongly coupled QGP. The approach enables future studies of heavy-flavor observables as probes of the microscopic structure of the sQGP.
