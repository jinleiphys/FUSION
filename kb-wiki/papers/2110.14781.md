# Momentum-Current Gravitational Multipoles of Hadrons

> We study multipole expansion of the momentum currents in hadrons, with three series $S^{(J)}$, $\tilde T^{(J)}$, and $T^{(J)}$, in connection with the gravitational fields generated nearby. The momentum currents are related to their energy-momentum form factors, which in principle can be probed through processes like deeply-virtual Compton scattering currently studied at JLab 12 GeV facility and future Electron Ion Collider. We define the leading momentum-current multipoles, tensor monopole $τ$ ($T0$) and scalar quadrupole $\hat σ^{ij}$ ($S2$) moments, relating the former to the so-called $D$-term in the literature. We calculate the momentum current distribution in hydrogen atom and its monopole moment in the basic unit of $τ_0 =\hbar^2/4M$, showing that the sign of $D$-term has little to do with mechanical stability. The momentum current distribution also strongly modifies the static gravitational field inside hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the tensor monopole moment of the momentum-current distribution in hadrons, related to the so-called D-term, has little to do with mechanical stability, contrary to claims in prior literature; the sign of the D-term is not a reliable indicator of stability, as demonstrated by a hydrogen atom calculation where the monopole moment is positive.

## Method

The authors perform a static multipole expansion of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) for hadrons of various spins, decomposing the momentum-current density \(T^{ij}\) into three independent series: \(S^{(l)}\), \(\tilde T^{(l-1)}\), and \(T^{(l-2)}\). They relate these multipoles to gravitational form factors measurable in processes like deeply-virtual Compton scattering, and compute the momentum-current distribution in a hydrogen atom using a non-relativistic quantum mechanical EMT constructed from the electron wave function and the Coulomb fields of the proton and electron.

## Key numbers

* The tensor monopole moment \(\tau\) for a free spin-0 boson is \(\tau_{\text{boson}} = -\hbar^2/(4M)\), defining a fundamental unit \(\tau_0 = \hbar^2/(4M)\) with coupling \(g_b = -1\).
* For the hydrogen atom, after subtracting the Coulomb tail, the effective scalar-MC monopole moment is \(\tau = \hbar^2/(4M)\) (positive), up to fractional corrections of order \(\alpha = 1/137\) (the fine structure constant).
* The form factor \(C(q^2)\) for a charged particle has an infrared divergence: \(C(\vec{q})/(2M) \rightarrow \alpha\pi/(16|\vec{q}|) + \alpha/(6\pi M) \ln(\vec{q}^2/M^2)\), leading to a \(1/r^2\) correction to the space-time metric.

## Context

This work extends the multipole expansion formalism of Thorne (1980), Blanchet (1998), and Damour (1990) to the static momentum-current distributions in hadrons, connecting them to gravitational form factors. It contradicts the mechanical stability interpretation of the D-term proposed by Polyakov (2018) and Lorce (2018), using the hydrogen atom as a counterexample where the sign of the D-term is positive yet the system is stable. The results enable future experimental probes of these multipoles at the JLab 12 GeV facility and the future Electron Ion Collider.
