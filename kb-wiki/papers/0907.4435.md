# Signals of the QCD Critical Point in Hydrodynamic Evolutions

> The presence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram can deform the trajectories describing the evolution of the expanding fireball in the QCD phase diagram. The deformation of the hydrodynamic trajectories will change the transverse velocity dependence of the proton-antiproton ratio when the fireball passes in the vicinity of the critical point. An unusual transverse velocity dependence of the anti-proton/proton ratio in a narrow beam energy window would thus signal the presence of the critical point.

## Key claim

An unusual transverse-velocity dependence of the antiproton-to-proton ratio in a narrow beam energy window would signal the presence of the QCD critical point, but the actual signal depends on the size of the attractive region around the critical point and may require small beam energy steps to be observed.

## Method

The authors used a combined fully three-dimensional macroscopic/microscopic transport approach, employing relativistic 3D hydrodynamics for the early deconfined stage and a microscopic non-equilibrium model (UrQMD) for the later hadronic stage. They constructed an equation of state with a QCD critical point by mapping the 3D Ising model onto the QCD phase diagram, treating the location and size of the critical region as input parameters. They then compared hydrodynamic trajectories and antiproton-to-proton ratios for equations of state with and without the critical point, focusing on SPS energy region conditions.

## Key numbers

- The QCD critical point was placed at (μ_B, T) = (550, 159) MeV and the chemical freezeout point at (406, 105) MeV on the μ_B-T plane for demonstrative calculations.
- For the hybrid model calculation, maximum energy density was set to 2.0 GeV/fm³ and maximum baryon number density to 0.15 fm⁻³, corresponding to SPS energy region.
- The switching temperature from hydrodynamic model to UrQMD was set to 150 MeV; at this temperature, chemical potential was 430 MeV with QCP present versus 250 MeV without QCP.
- NA49 data show antiproton spectra at collision energies from 20 GeV to 158 GeV, with only the 40 GeV collision energy showing a steeper slope in P_T spectra of antiprotons compared to other energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Nonaka and Asakawa (2005) on isentropic trajectories near the QCD critical point and by Nonaka and Bass (2007) on hybrid hydrodynamic/transport models. It provides a quantitative phenomenological framework for searching the QCD critical point in heavy ion collisions, complementing lattice QCD calculations and effective theory studies that have not reached a solid conclusion about the critical point's existence. The approach enables experimental searches at RHIC and SPS energies, with the NA49 data at 40 GeV collision energy suggested as a possible candidate signal requiring further investigation with smaller beam energy steps.
