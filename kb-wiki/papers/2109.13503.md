# Nuclear-Level Effective Theory of $μ\rightarrow e$ Conversion

> The Mu2e and COMET $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion experiments are expected to significantly advance limits on new sources of charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV). Almost all theoretical work in the field has focused on just two operators. However, general symmetry arguments lead to a $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion rate with six response functions, each of which, in principle, is observable by varying nuclear properties of targets. We construct a nucleon-level nonrelativistic effective theory (NRET) to clarify the microscopic origin of these response functions and to relate rate measurements in different targets. This exercise identifies three operators and their small parameters that control the NRET operator expansion. We note inconsistencies in past treatments of these parameters. The NRET is technically challenging, involving 16 operators, several distorted electron partial waves, bound muon upper and lower components, and an exclusive nuclear matrix element. We introduce a trick for treating the electron Coulomb effects accurately, which enables us to include all of these effects while producing transition densities whose one-body matrix elements can be evaluated analytically, greatly simplifying the nuclear physics. We derive bounds on operator coefficients from existing and anticipated $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion experiments. We discuss how similar NRET formulations have impacted dark matter phenomenology, noting that the tools this community has developed could be adapted for CLFV studies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that previous theoretical treatments of μ→e conversion have been incomplete by focusing on only two nuclear operators and neglecting the nucleon velocity operator v_N, which is parametrically larger than the muon velocity operator v_μ and can produce coherent enhancements in certain nuclei like Al and Cu where the Fermi sea fills only one spin-orbit subshell.

## Method

The authors construct a nucleon-level nonrelativistic effective theory (NRET) for μ→e conversion using 16 independent operators built from lepton and nucleon identities, spin operators, the momentum transfer vector iq̂, the nucleon velocity operator v_N, and the muon velocity operator v_μ. They embed these operators in nuclei using shell-model wave functions from BIGSTICK with the USDB and KB3G interactions, and introduce an effective momentum approximation q_eff to treat electron Coulomb distortion, replacing the Dirac plane wave by a shifted momentum solution.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameters for Al are y = (qb/2)² = 0.24, |⟨v_N/2⟩| = 0.11, and |⟨v_μ/2⟩| = 0.026
- The effective momentum q_eff = 110.81 MeV for ²⁷Al and 112.43 MeV for ⁴⁸Ti
- The LEC limits from Al at branching ratio 10⁻¹⁷ give |c̃₁|,|c̃₁₁| ≤ 4.0×10⁻¹⁰ (τ=0) and 1.2×10⁻⁸ (τ=1)
- The LEC limits from Ti at branching ratio 6.1×10⁻¹³ give |c̃₁|,|c̃₁₁| ≤ 7.4×10⁻⁸ (τ=0) and 1.3×10⁻⁶ (τ=1)
- The coherent LECs c̃₁⁰ and c̃₁₁⁰ will be probed at scales ≳ 10⁴ TeV by Mu2e and COMET
- The Zeff approximation induces rate errors of ~1% in Al and 4% in Ti
- The v_μ operator contributes only a 5% numerical correction to Al nuclear form factors

## Context

This work extends the NRET formalism developed for dark matter phenomenology by Fitzpatrick et al. (2013) and Anand et al. (2014) to charged lepton flavor violation, addressing inconsistencies in past treatments that omitted the nucleon velocity operator v_N. The paper corrects the common practice of restricting electron partial waves to |κ|=1 when including v_μ, which the authors show produces errors larger than the v_μ correction itself. The formalism enables relating rate measurements across different nuclear targets and constraining 32 LEC degrees of freedom, with the next-generation targets ²⁷Al proving versatile in its range of sensitivities. The tools developed for DM direct detection can be adapted for CLFV studies.
