# Effects of symmetry energy on equation of state for simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers

> We construct a new equation of state (EOS) for numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers based on an extended relativistic mean-field model with a small symmetry energy slope $L$, which is compatible with both experimental nuclear data and recent observations of neutron stars. The new EOS table (EOS4) based on the extended TM1 (TM1e) model with $L=40$ MeV is designed in the same tabular form and compared with the commonly used Shen EOS (EOS2) based on the original TM1 model with $L=110.8$ MeV. This is convenient and useful for performing numerical simulations and examining the influences of symmetry energy and its density dependence on astrophysical phenomena. In comparison with the TM1 model used in EOS2, the TM1e model provides a similar maximum neutron-star mass but smaller radius and tidal deformability for a $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star, which is more consistent with current constraints. By comparing the phase diagram and thermodynamic quantities between EOS4 and EOS2, it is found that the TM1e model predicts relatively larger region of nonuniform matter and softer EOS for neutron-rich matter. Significant differences between EOS4 and EOS2 are observed in the case with low proton fraction, while the properties of symmetric matter remain unchanged.

## Key claim
The new EOS4 table, based on the extended TM1e model with a symmetry energy slope of L=40 MeV, provides a softer equation of state for neutron-rich matter and a larger region of nonuniform matter compared to the commonly used Shen EOS (EOS2) based on the original TM1 model with L=110.8 MeV, while properties of symmetric matter remain unchanged.

## Method
The authors constructed a new equation of state table (EOS4) using an extended relativistic mean-field (RMF) model (TM1e) that includes an additional omega-rho coupling term to modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Nonuniform matter was described using the Thomas-Fermi approximation with a parameterized nucleon distribution and a single nucleus approximation, while uniform matter was treated within the RMF framework. The new EOS4 was designed in the same tabular form as the previous EOS2 to facilitate direct comparison of symmetry energy effects in astrophysical simulations.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L = 40 MeV for TM1e model (EOS4) versus L = 110.8 MeV for original TM1 model (EOS2)
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.38 MeV (TM1e) versus 36.89 MeV (TM1)
- Maximum neutron-star mass: 2.12 solar masses (TM1e) versus 2.18 solar masses (TM1)
- Radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 13.1 km (TM1e) versus 14.2 km (TM1)
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 652 (TM1e) versus 1047 (TM1)
- Binding energy per nucleon of Pb-208 in RMF: 7.88 MeV (both models), consistent with experimental 7.87 MeV
- Charge radius of Pb-208 in RMF: 5.56 fm (TM1e) versus 5.54 fm (TM1), compared to experimental 5.50 fm

## Context
This work extends the Shen EOS family (Shen 1998a, 1998b, 2011) by introducing a smaller symmetry energy slope that is more consistent with recent astrophysical constraints from neutron-star observations, including GW170817 tidal deformability limits (Abbott 2017, 2018) and NICER radius measurements (Riley 2019, Miller 2019). The new EOS4 enables direct comparison with EOS2 in simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers to isolate the effects of symmetry energy density dependence, and is compatible with constraints from massive pulsars such as PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2019).
