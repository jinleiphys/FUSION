# Modes of decay in neutron-rich nuclei

> We calculate the ground, first intrinsic excited states and density distribution for neutron-rich thorium and uranium isotopes, within the framework of relativistic mean field(RMF) approach using axially deformed basis. The total nucleon densities are calculated, from which the cluster-structures inside the parent nuclei are determined. The possible modes of decay, like α-decay and \b{eta} -decay are analyzed. We find the neutron-rich isotopes are stable against α-decay, however they are very much unstable against \b{eta} -decay. The life time of these nuclei predicted to be tens of second against \b{eta} -decay.

## Key claim

The authors claim that neutron-rich thorium and uranium isotopes (with neutron numbers N=154-172) are stable against alpha-decay and spontaneous fission, but are highly unstable against beta-decay, with predicted beta-decay lifetimes on the order of tens of seconds for the specific isotopes \(^{254}\)Th and \(^{256}\)U. The hedge is that these lifetimes are a "rough estimation" from an empirical formula, and the authors note that the beta-decay half-lives obtained from QRPA calculations differ significantly from their empirical results.

## Method

The authors used an axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3 parameter set to calculate ground and first intrinsic excited states, density distributions, and binding energies for neutron-rich thorium and uranium isotopes. Alpha-decay half-lives were computed using a quantum mechanical WKB tunneling approach with a double-folding M3Y nucleon-nucleon potential, while beta-decay half-lives were estimated using the empirical formula of Fiset and Nix (1972) with binding energies from RMF, FRDM, INM, and HF(MSk7) models.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{254}\)Th: Q\(_\alpha\) = 0.78 MeV (from RMF), penetration probability P = 1.90 × 10\(^{-55}\), WKB half-life T\(_{1/2}^{WKB}\) = 3.6 × 10\(^{35}\) s, Viola-Seaborg half-life T\(_{1/2}^{\alpha}\) = 1 × 10\(^{107}\) s
- For \(^{256}\)U: Q\(_\alpha\) = 1.8 MeV (from RMF), penetration probability P = 3.783 × 10\(^{-44}\), WKB half-life T\(_{1/2}^{WKB}\) = 1.83 × 10\(^{22}\) s, Viola-Seaborg half-life T\(_{1/2}^{\alpha}\) = 1.218 × 10\(^{55}\) s
- For \(^{232}\)Th: Q\(_\alpha\) = 4.081 MeV (experimental), penetration probability P = 6.17 × 10\(^{-37}\), WKB half-life T\(_{1/2}^{WKB}\) = 1.12 × 10\(^{17}\) s, Viola-Seaborg half-life T\(_{1/2}^{\alpha}\) = 5.07 × 10\(^{17}\) s
- For \(^{238}\)U: Q\(_\alpha\) = 4.27 MeV (experimental), penetration probability P = 1.163 × 10\(^{-37}\), WKB half-life T\(_{1/2}^{WKB}\) = 5.95 × 10\(^{15}\) s, Viola-Seaborg half-life T\(_{1/2}^{\alpha}\) = 2.219 × 10\(^{17}\) s
- For \(^{216}\)Th: Q\(_\alpha\) = 8.072 MeV (experimental), penetration probability P = 2.109 × 10\(^{-21}\), WKB half-life T\(_{1/2}^{WKB}\) = 0.328 s
- For \(^{218}\)U: Q\(_\alpha\) = 8.773 MeV (experimental), penetration probability P = 1.26 × 10\(^{-20}\), WKB half-life T\(_{1/2}^{WKB}\) = 0.055 s
- The beta-decay lifetime for \(^{254}\)Th and \(^{256}\)U is predicted to be "tens of seconds"
- The neutron-to-proton ratio at the central region is approximately 1.31 for \(^{232}\)Th and 1.38 for \(^{254}\)Th; for \(^{230}\)U it is 1.21 and for \(^{256}\)U it is 1.35

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction by Satpathy, Patra and Choudhury (2008) that uranium and thorium isotopes with neutron numbers N=154-172 are thermally fissile with low fission barriers and broad barrier widths making them stable against spontaneous fission. The present study examines the alpha- and beta-decay stability of these same neutron-rich isotopes using RMF calculations, finding that while they are stable against alpha-decay and spontaneous fission, they are highly beta-unstable with lifetimes of tens of seconds. This enables future consideration of these isotopes as potential nuclear fuel, where the release of large numbers of neutrons during fission could allow reactors to achieve critical conditions much faster than with normal nuclear fuel.
