# Chiral magnetic wave at finite baryon density and the electric quadrupole moment of quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions

> Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) is a gapless collective excitation of quark-gluon plasma in the presence of external magnetic field that stems from the interplay of Chiral Magnetic (CME) and Chiral Separation Effects (CSE); it is composed by the waves of the electric and chiral charge densities coupled by the axial anomaly. We consider CMW at finite baryon density and find that it induces the electric quadrupole moment of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions: the "poles" of the produced fireball (pointing outside of the reaction plane) acquire additional positive electric charge, and the "equator" acquires additional negative charge. We point out that this electric quadrupole deformation lifts the degeneracy between the elliptic flows of positive and negative pions leading to $v_2(π^+) < v_2(π^-)$, and estimate the magnitude of the effect.

## Key claim

The Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) at finite baryon density induces an electric quadrupole moment in the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions, which lifts the degeneracy between the elliptic flows of positive and negative pions leading to \(v_2(\pi^+) < v_2(\pi^-)\). The authors estimate the magnitude of the effect but note that the lifetime of the magnetic field is the main source of uncertainty, and that backgrounds from Coulomb interactions and absorption differences must be carefully analyzed.

## Method

The authors solve the CMW equation numerically for the propagation of flavored chiral charge densities of \(u\) and \(d\) quarks in a static plasma, using the Sakai-Sugimoto model in the large \(N_c\) quenched approximation to obtain the wave velocities \(v_\chi^f\) and longitudinal diffusion constants \(D_L^f\). The initial "almond" shape of the QCD matter is taken from the KLN model based on parton saturation and \(k_T\) factorization, with realistic Woods-Saxon nuclear densities for Au-Au collisions. The total electric charge distribution is obtained by superimposing the waves of different flavors weighted by their charges, and the resulting electric quadrupole moment is translated into a charge-dependent elliptic flow of pions.

## Key numbers

* The normalized electric quadrupole moment \(r = 2 q_e / \bar{\rho}_e\) is approximately 0.04 for mid-central collisions with a magnetic field lifetime \(\tau = 8\) fm, temperature \(T = 165\) MeV, and maximal magnetic field \(eB|_{max} = m_\pi^2\).
* The charge asymmetry in the plasma at \(\sqrt{s} = 11\) GeV is estimated as \(A_{\pm} \simeq 0.3\).
* The estimated difference in elliptic flows is \(\Delta v_2^{CMW} \equiv v_2(\pi^-) - v_2(\pi^+) \simeq r \cdot A_{\pm} \simeq 0.01\).
* The pion elliptic flow at \(\sqrt{s} = 8.7\) GeV in mid-central Pb-Pb collisions is about \(v_2 \simeq 0.03\), making the difference potentially as large as \(\sim 30\%\).
* The magnetic field strength used is \(eB = m_\pi^2\), with impact parameter \(b = 3\) fm and temperature \(T = 165\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) introduced by Kharzeev and Yee (2010) to the case of finite baryon density, and builds on the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) established in holographic QCD models and lattice QCD computations. The predicted electric quadrupole moment provides a new signature of chiral symmetry restoration in heavy ion collisions, and the charge-dependent elliptic flow offers an experimentally observable consequence that could be tested at RHIC and LHC. The authors note that the main uncertainty is the lifetime of the magnetic field, which will need to be evaluated numerically within relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), and that possible backgrounds from Coulomb interactions and absorption differences in the hadronic afterburner phase must be carefully analyzed.
