# In-Medium properties of the omega meson in photon induced nuclear reactions

> We discuss the possibility to study the in-medium changes of the properties of the omega meson in reactions on ordinary nuclei with elementary electromagnetic probes. We present a tree-level calculation of the elementary gamma p -> omega p process which is extended to describe also the photoproduction of medium-modified omega mesons in nuclear matter. Using a semi-classical transport approach we obtain results for e+e- and pi0 gamma photoproduction off heavy nuclei in the invariant mass range of the rho and omega mesons. Both reactions are also studied experimentally and are presently being analyzed at accelerator facilities in Bonn and at Jefferson Lab. We show that the in-medium signals expected can be as large as those obtained in heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim

The in-medium signals of the omega meson in photon-induced nuclear reactions can be as large as those obtained in heavy-ion reactions, provided the in-medium changes are sizeable and more complex than a pure broadening of the peak.

## Method

The authors constructed a tree-level model of the elementary gamma p -> omega p process, including t-channel pion exchange, s- and u-channel nucleon diagrams, and the P11(1710) nucleon resonance, with parameters refitted to experimental data. This amplitude was extended to describe photoproduction of omega mesons in nuclear matter by allowing the omega to take arbitrary masses via an in-medium spectral function parameterized by a momentum-independent mass shift and collisional broadening. The production, propagation, and decay of vector mesons in finite nuclei were then modeled using a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport approach with a coupled-channel treatment.

## Key numbers

- The mass shift parameter alpha = -Delta m/m = 0.16 was used, following Klingl (1998).
- The pion mean free path in nuclear matter is approximately lambda ~ 1 fm.
- A cutoff on the three-momentum of the detected e+e- pair of 300 MeV was applied to enhance sensitivity to in-medium decays.
- A cutoff on the kinetic energy of the detected pion of T_pi >= 150 MeV was used to suppress distorted pi0 gamma pairs.
- The detector resolution for the pi0 gamma invariant mass spectrum was 25 MeV.
- The invariant mass range of interest for pi0 gamma pairs was 0.6-0.9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Muehlich 2003, 2004) on omega meson photoproduction in nuclei, which showed that final-state interactions of the pion do not spoil observation of the omega spectral density. The approach is motivated by QCD sum rule predictions (Leupold 2001, Zschocke 2002) that the in-medium hadron spectrum can reveal the density dependence of quark condensates. The results enable quantitative extraction of the omega mass shift in nuclei from experiments at JLab and ELSA, and are relevant to understanding high-density phenomena in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
