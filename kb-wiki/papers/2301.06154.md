# The Nambu-Goto string in QCD: Dipole interactions, scattering and entanglement

> We revisit some aspects of the stringy approach to dipole-dipole interactions, scattering and entanglement in QCD, using the Nambu-Goto (NG) string, without recourse to holography. We first show that the potential between two static dipoles exchanging closed NG strings is attractive at all separations. Underlining the exchange there is an emergent entropy, that is dominated by the tachyon at large separation, and vanishes at short separation, a measure of the confinement-deconfinement transition. The same tachyon is dominant in the scattering amplitude, as a correlator of two Wilson loops for two fixed dipole-like hadrons separated at large rapidity gap, where the contribution of the worldsheet fermions is included. While the tachyon causes the mean string bit density to grow exponentially with the rapidity, the total scattering cross section still satisfies the Froissart bound by quantum shadowing. The stringy scattering exchange also carries an entanglement entropy, that saturates when the bound is reached. For hadrons with varying dipole sizes, the tachyon exchange takes place in hyperbolic space in the conformal limit. The result for the full S-matrix is reminiscent of the one from Mueller$^\prime$s evolved dipole wavefunction, for the total dipole-dipole cross section in perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Goto string, without recourse to holography, can describe dipole-dipole interactions, scattering, and entanglement in QCD, with the tachyon mode dominating at large separations and rapidities; the total scattering cross section satisfies the Froissart bound through quantum shadowing despite exponential growth of the string bit density.

## Method

The authors computed the static dipole-dipole potential using the 2-particle irreducible (2PI) exchange of closed Nambu-Goto strings between two Wilson loops, evaluating the string partition function with cylindrical topology in conformal gauge. For scattering, they analytically continued the twisted dipole-dipole correlator from Euclidean to Minkowski signature, mapping the twist angle to rapidity, and resummed the 2PI contributions to obtain the full S-matrix. The emergent entropy was derived from the potential by treating the string circumference as an inverse effective temperature.

## Key numbers

- The tachyon mass for D_perp = 2 is m_0 approximately 1257 MeV, close to the lattice glueball mass m_{0^{++}} = 1475 MeV
- The critical distance below which the NG potential becomes imaginary is R_c = 1/3 fm
- The Pomeron intercept is alpha_P(0) = D_perp/12, giving alpha_P(0) = 1/6 for D_perp = 2
- The inverse Hagedorn temperature is beta_H = sqrt(pi D_perp / 3 sigma_T)

## Context

This work extends prior stringy approaches to QCD scattering that used holography in higher dimensions (Polchinski 2002, Brower 2006), by instead using the Nambu-Goto string in flat 4-dimensional space. The construction parallels earlier work by Basar (2012) and Stoffers (2012) on twisted dipoles, and the results for the dipole-dipole potential and scattering amplitude are connected through Euclidean rotation. The emergent entropy analysis relates to spatial entanglement in QCD4 at large N_c as discussed by Klebanov (2007), and the saturation of the cross section by quantum shadowing enables a non-perturbative description of unitarization in the Regge limit.
