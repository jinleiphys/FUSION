# Dipole and Quadrupole Moments of Mirror Nuclei 8B and 8li

> Magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the mirror nuclei 8Li and 8B are analysed in the framework of the multiparticle shell model by using two approaches : i) the one-particle spectroscopic factors and ii) the one-particle fractional parentage coefficients. These two approaches are compared both each to other and with a microscopic multicluster model. The one-particle nucleon states are calculated taking into account the continuum by the method of the expansion of the Sturm - Liouville functions. The experimental magnetic and quadrupole moments of 8Li and 8Bare reproduced well by using fractional parentage coefficients technique. The root mean-square radii and the radial density distributions are obtained for these nuclei.

## Key claim

The multiparticle shell model, using fractional parentage coefficients and single-particle wave functions calculated in a Woods-Saxon potential with continuum effects included via Sturm-Liouville expansion, can reproduce the experimental magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the mirror nuclei 8Li and 8B, but the results are described as "preliminary yet" and require future experimental scattering data for confirmation.

## Method

The authors analyzed the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of 8Li and 8B using two approaches within the multiparticle shell model: one using one-particle spectroscopic factors and the other using one-particle fractional parentage coefficients. Single-particle nucleon states were calculated in a Woods-Saxon potential, with continuum effects treated by expansion in Sturm-Liouville functions; for the 8B valence proton, the spin-orbit interaction was included to obtain the quasibound 1p1/2 state at positive energy. Configuration mixing coefficients βi were fitted to reproduce the experimental moments, and the resulting wave functions were used to compute root-mean-square radii and radial density distributions.

## Key numbers

- Experimental quadrupole moments: Q(8B) = 6.83 fm² and Q(8Li) = 3.27 fm² (from Minamiosono et al., 1992).
- Experimental magnetic moments: μNucl(8B) = 1.0355 and μNucl(8Li) = 1.65335 (from Ajzenberg-Selove, 1984).
- Best-fit multiparticle shell model (variant V, with spin-orbit) gives μNucl(8B) = 1.020 and μNucl(8Li) = 1.603, with effective charges e_peff = 1.109 and e_neff = 0.570.
- Proton rms radius for 8B: Rrms^p = 3.634 fm (variant V); neutron rms radius for 8Li: Rrms^n = 3.079 fm (variant V).
- Single-particle rms radii for 8B valence proton (with spin-orbit, Ec = 0): <r²_1p3/2>^1/2 = 4.360 fm, <r²_1p1/2>^1/2 = 4.667 fm; for 8Li valence neutron: <r²_1p3/2>^1/2 = 3.521 fm, <r²_1p1/2>^1/2 = 4.229 fm.
- Binding energy of valence proton in 8B: E_1p3/2^p = -0.137 MeV; the 1p1/2 state in 8B has positive energy 1.004 MeV (quasibound).
- Spin-orbit constant used: κ = 0.101, giving Vls ≈ 4 MeV (compared to 8–13 MeV for stable 1p-shell nuclei).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kitagawa and Sagawa (1993), who used the Cohen-Kurath and Millener-Kurath shell model with spectroscopic factors to calculate quadrupole moments of 8B and 8Li, by adding magnetic moment calculations and comparing with the fractional parentage coefficient method. The results are compared with the microscopic multicluster model of Varga, Suzuki, and Tanihata (1995, 1997), which reproduced the quadrupole moment and binding energy of 8B but could not describe the magnetic moments. The authors conclude that the multiparticle shell model gives a significant proton halo in 8B and a less pronounced neutron halo in 8Li, but emphasize that experimental electron scattering data or polarized proton scattering on secondary beams of these nuclei are needed to obtain more correct wave functions.
