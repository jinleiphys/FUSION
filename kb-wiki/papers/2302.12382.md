# In-medium properties of the light and heavy-light mesons in a light-front quark model

> We investigate the in-medium properties of pseudoscalar and vector mesons with the light-light and heavy-light quarks in a light-front quark model, using the in-medium quark properties computed by the quark-meson coupling model. Both models are constructed on an equal footing with the constituent quark degree of freedom. Here, we particularly focus on the weak decay constants and distribution amplitudes (DAs) of the mesons in symmetric nuclear matter. We find that the weak decay constants decrease as nuclear density increases for $π$, $K$, $D$, and $B$ pseudoscalar as well as $ρ$, $K^{*}$, $D^{*}$, and $B^*$ vector mesons, where their properties in free space have good agreement with the available experimental and lattice QCD data. A larger reduction is found for the light-light quark pseudoscalar mesons, while a smaller reduction is found for the heavy-light quark vector mesons, in particular, with the bottom quark. We discuss the effect of the vector potential on the weak decay constants and present our predictions for the in-medium modifications of DAs. Also, a comparison with the free space lattice QCD data is made.

## Key claim

The weak decay constants of pseudoscalar and vector mesons (π, K, D, B, ρ, K*, D*, B*) decrease with increasing nuclear density, with larger reductions for light-light pseudoscalar mesons and smaller reductions for heavy-light vector mesons (especially those containing bottom quarks), but these results depend on the assumption that the Gaussian wave function parameter β remains density-independent, which may cause the decay constants to decrease less rapidly than if β were allowed to vary.

## Method

The authors combined a light-front quark model (LFQM) with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to compute in-medium meson properties. The LFQM used a Gaussian radial wave function and a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian with linear confinement, Coulomb, and hyperfine potentials, with parameters fixed from free-space meson properties. The QMC model provided the in-medium light quark effective masses (modified by the scalar σ mean field) and energies (modified by the vector ω mean field) in symmetric nuclear matter, which were then input into the LFQM to compute weak decay constants and distribution amplitudes at various nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ (k_F = 1.305 fm⁻¹), the binding energy per nucleon is -15.7 MeV, used to fix QMC model parameters.
- The weak decay constant f_π decreases from its free-space value as nuclear density increases; the reduction is larger for light-light pseudoscalar mesons than for heavy-light vector mesons.
- The free-space decay constants for π, K, D, B, ρ, K*, D*, and B* mesons show good agreement with available experimental and lattice QCD data (specific numerical values not provided in the truncated text).
- The in-medium quark effective mass is given by m_q* = m_q - V_σ^q, where V_σ^q is the scalar potential from the σ mean field.

## Context

This work extends previous hybrid LF-QMC studies by deMelo et al. (2016, 2014, 2018) that focused only on π and ρ mesons, now systematically covering light-light and heavy-light pseudoscalar and vector mesons (π, K, D, B, ρ, K*, D*, B*). Unlike those earlier studies which used a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with a mass regulator, the present approach employs a Gaussian wave function and a Hamiltonian-based variational method. The results enable predictions for in-medium modifications of distribution amplitudes and provide input relevant for experiments at FAIR (PANDA, CBM), RHIC (PHENIX), KEK (BELLE, BELLE II), CERN (LHCb), and J-PARC.
