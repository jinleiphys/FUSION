# Quasielastic Charged-Current Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering with Nonrelativistic Nuclear Energy Density Functionals

> Charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering is studied in the quasielastic region with the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) nuclear energy density functional. We focus on the uncertainties stemming from the axial mass and the in-medium effective mass of the nucleon. Comparing the result of theory to the state-of-the-art data from MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINER$ν$A, we constrain the axial mass and the effective mass that are compatible with the data. We find that the total cross section is insensitive to the effective mass, so the axial mass could be determined independently of the uncertainty in the effective mass. Differential cross sections at different kinematics are, on the other hand, sensitive to the effective mass as well as the axial mass. Within the uncertainty of the axial mass constrained from the total cross section, dependence on the effective mass is examined. As a result we obtain the axial mass and the effective mass that are consistent with the experimental data.

# Quasielastic Charged-Current Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering with Nonrelativistic Nuclear Energy Density Functionals

## Key claim
The KIDS nuclear energy density functional reproduces experimental neutrino-nucleus scattering data at various kinematics, but the axial mass and in-medium effective mass have different impacts depending on the observable: total cross sections are insensitive to the effective mass, allowing the axial mass to be constrained independently, while differential cross sections are sensitive to both parameters.

## Method
The authors calculated charged-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering off a 12C target using nuclear wave functions obtained from the nonrelativistic KIDS energy density functional via Hartree-Fock equations. They compared flux-averaged double-differential and total cross sections to data from MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA, testing two values of the axial mass (1.032 GeV and 1.30 GeV) and four nuclear models with different isoscalar and isovector effective masses at saturation density.

## Key numbers
- Axial mass values tested: standard value M_A = 1.032 GeV and large value M_A = 1.30 GeV
- Isoscalar effective masses at saturation density (in units of free nucleon mass): KIDS0 with mu_s = 1.0, KIDS0-m*77 with mu_s = 0.7, KIDS0-m*99 with mu_s = 0.9, SLy4 with mu_s = 0.7
- For the neutrino total cross section, M_A = 1.30 GeV enhances the cross section by about 30% compared to M_A = 1.032 GeV; for the antineutrino, the enhancement is about 15%
- KDAR data at 236 MeV incident neutrino energy yield total cross section sigma = (2.7 +/- 1.2) x 10^{-39} cm^2/neutron
- For the KDAR kinematics, the effect of M_A gives a difference less than 10% between the two axial mass values

## Context
This work extends the KIDS energy density functional framework, previously applied successfully to finite nuclei, nuclear matter, and quasielastic electron scattering (Kim, Gil, and Hyun, 2020-2021), to charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering. It directly compares with experimental data from MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA collaborations. The results enable simultaneous constraints on the axial mass and the in-medium nucleon effective mass, with the total cross section providing a clean probe of the axial mass independent of effective mass uncertainties. The authors note that the large axial mass value (1.30 GeV) may effectively subsume higher-order contributions such as multi-meson-exchange currents or multi-particle-multi-hole processes that are not included in the impulse approximation.
