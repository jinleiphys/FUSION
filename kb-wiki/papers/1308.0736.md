# Toward UrQMD Model Description of pp and pC Interactions at High Energies

> It is found that UrQMD model version 3.3 does not describe NA61/SHINE Collaboration data on π-meson production in pp interactions at energies 20 - 80 GeV. At the same time, it describes quite well the NA49 Collaboration data on the meson production in pp and pC interactions at 158 GeV. The Collaborations do not consider feedback of η-meson decays. All versions of the UrQMD model assume that η-mesons are "stable". An inclusion of the decays into calculations leads to 2--3 % increase of the meson production which is not enough for description of the data. Possible ways of the model improvements are considered. Conclusions of the paper are: accounting of η-meson decays is not essential for a description of experimental data; a new tuning of the UrQMD model parameters is needed for a successful description of pp and pC interactions at high energies; inclusion of the low mass diffraction dissociation in the UrQMD model would be desirable.

## Key claim

UrQMD model version 3.3 fails to describe NA61/SHINE data on π-meson production in pp interactions at 20–80 GeV, yet it describes NA49 data at 158 GeV quite well; including η-meson decays increases meson production by only 2–3%, which is insufficient to resolve the discrepancy, so a new tuning of model parameters and inclusion of low-mass diffraction dissociation are needed.

## Method

The author modified the UrQMD 3.3 Monte Carlo code to force η-meson decays (by setting branching ratios: 39% to gg, 61% to 3π) and tested three additional modifications: decreasing binary reaction cross sections by a factor of 3, fixing the single-diffraction probability at 50%, and imitating low-mass diffraction dissociation by increasing the cross section of the NN → ΔN* process. The modified model was then compared to published NA49 and preliminary NA61/SHINE data on π±, K±, proton, and antiproton spectra in pp and pC interactions at beam momenta of 20, 31, 40, 80, and 158 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Including η-meson decays leads to a 2–3% increase in meson production in the central rapidity region.
- At 158 GeV/c, the UrQMD model with default parameters agrees with NA49 data on π-meson production in pp and pC interactions.
- Decreasing binary reaction cross sections by a factor of 3 allows description of meson production at 20, 31, 40, and 80 GeV/c but overestimates inclusive cross sections at higher energies.
- Fixing the single-diffraction probability at 50% allows description of data at 158 GeV/c but underestimates data at 80 GeV/c.
- The inelastic pC cross section used for normalization is 252 mb.
- The low-mass diffraction cross section is taken as σ_LMD ≃ 0.55 σ_el, and the NN → ΔN* cross section was set to 4.0 mb for sqrt(s) ≥ 17.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to describe NA61/SHINE and NA49 data using FLUKA, VENUS, FTF, and UrQMD models (Uzhinsky, 2013; Uzhinsky, 2013). The author shows that the UrQMD model, which uses the Fritiof model for high-energy hadronic interactions and simulates binary reactions such as NN → NΔ, NN → NN*, and NN → ΔΔ, requires retuning of binary reaction cross sections and proper inclusion of low-mass diffraction dissociation to describe pp and pC data across the full energy range. The results enable future improvements to the UrQMD code for applications at FAIR and NICA facilities, and highlight the need for accurate parameterizations of binary reaction cross sections and single-diffraction cross sections, as well as better hyperon production data from NA49 to resolve remaining discrepancies in baryon spectra.
