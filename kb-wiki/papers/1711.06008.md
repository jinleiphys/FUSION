# A quark coalescence model for polarized vector mesons and baryons

> A non-relativistic quark coalescence model is formulated for polarized vector mesons and baryons of spin-1/2 octet and spin-3/2 decuplet. With the spin density matrix, one can compute in a uniform way the polarizations of vector mesons and baryons from those of quarks and antiquarks with explicit momentum dependence. The results are compared to that obtained from kinetic and statistical models for hadrons.

## Key claim

A non-relativistic quark coalescence model based on the spin density matrix can compute the polarizations of vector mesons and baryons (spin-1/2 octet and spin-3/2 decuplet) from those of polarized quarks and antiquarks with explicit momentum dependence, but the deviation of the vector meson spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ from the unpolarized value of 1/3 is only of second order in the quark polarization.

## Method

The authors formulate a non-relativistic quark coalescence model in which the spin density matrix elements for vector mesons and baryons are obtained by projecting the quark-antiquark or three-quark density operator onto hadron states with Gaussian wave functions. The quark and antiquark weight functions are related to their polarizations, and the hadron spin density matrix elements are expressed as momentum-dependent integrals over the quark polarization functions and the hadron wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For vector mesons, the unpolarized spin density matrix element is ρ₀₀ = 1/3; any deviation from 1/3 indicates spin alignment.

## Context

This work extends the earlier quark recombination model of Liang and Wang (2004) for hyperon and vector meson polarization by including explicit momentum dependence, which was absent in that prior approach. The model provides a systematic framework to compute hadron polarizations from quark polarizations obtained from other approaches such as the Wigner function method, enabling comparisons with results from statistical-hydro models (e.g., Becattini et al., 2013; Karpenko et al., 2016) and with experimental measurements of ρ₀₀ for vector mesons from the STAR collaboration (Abelev et al., 2008).
