# Mass distributions for nuclear disintegration from fission to evaporation

> By a proper choice of the excitation energy per nucleon we analyze the mass distributions of the nuclear fragmentation at various excitation energies. Starting from low energies (between 0.1 and 1 MeV/nucleon) up to higher energies about 12 MeV/n, we classified the mass yield characteristics for heavy nuclei (A>200) on the basis of Statistical Multifragmentation Model. The evaluation of fragment distribution with the excitation energy show that the present results exhibit the same trend as the experimental ones.

# Mass distributions for nuclear disintegration from fission to evaporation

## Key claim
The Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) can reproduce the experimentally observed evolution of mass yield distributions for heavy nuclei (A>200) across a wide range of excitation energies from 0.1 to 12 MeV/nucleon, provided the excitation energy per nucleon is chosen appropriately. The authors claim agreement with experimental trends, but note that the results are model calculations that "exhibit the same trend as the experimental ones" rather than exact quantitative matches.

## Method
The authors applied the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to calculate mass yield distributions for the fission and fragmentation of heavy nuclei (238U and 226Ra) at excitation energies from 0.1 to 12 MeV/nucleon. For low-energy fission, they incorporated the Bohr-Wheeler approach for fission widths, with fission barriers from Myers and Swiatecki and level densities from Iljinov. For higher energies, they used a micro-canonical ensemble of breakup channels generated by Monte Carlo sampling, where fragments are treated as heated nuclear liquid drops with free energies parameterized as sums of bulk, surface, Coulomb, and symmetry energy contributions.

## Key numbers
- For 238U at 0.1 MeV/nucleon: a double-peaked (asymmetric fission) distribution is observed, with the mass ratio of the most probable fragments about 3 to 2.
- For 226Ra at 0.1 MeV/nucleon: a triple-peaked mass distribution is seen; at 0.2 MeV/nucleon, a single symmetric distribution appears.
- At excitation energy of 4 MeV/nucleon (corresponding temperature T ≤ 5 MeV): a U-shape distribution is observed for 226Ra fragmentation.
- At excitation energy of 8 MeV/nucleon (T ≥ 6 MeV): big fragments disappear and an exponential-like fall-off is observed for 226Ra.
- The model uses parameters from the Bethe-Weizsäcker formula: binding energy of infinite nuclear matter W0 = 16 MeV, surface coefficient B0 = 18 MeV, critical temperature Tc = 18 MeV, symmetry energy parameter γ = 25 MeV, and normal nuclear matter density ρ0 = 0.15 fm−3.

## Context
This work extends the application of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM), previously developed by Botvina (2004) and others, to systematically classify mass yield characteristics from low-energy fission through multifragmentation to evaporation. The calculations for 226Ra are compared directly with experimental results from Perry (who studied reactions induced by protons and deuterons on 226Ra). The results support the interpretation of a liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter at temperatures around 5-6 MeV, as discussed by Agostino, Trautmann, and Pochodzalla. The approach enables future applications to astrophysical events such as supernova explosions and neutron star formation, as noted by Botvina (2004).
