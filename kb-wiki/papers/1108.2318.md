# Production of a cascade hyperon in the K^- - proton interaction

> We investigate the production of a cascade hyperon (Xi) through the K^- + p --> K^+ (K^0) + Xi^- (Xi^0) reactions, within an effective Lagrangian model where these reactions proceed via excitations of Lambda and Sigma resonance intermediate states in s- and u-channels. The coupling constants at the various vertices are taken from previous studies and SU(3) symmetry considerations. The calculated total cross sections of these reactions, which are in good agreement with the available data, are dominated by the contributions from the Lambda(1520) intermediate resonant state. However, the K^+ meson angular distributions show selectivity to other resonant states in different angular regions and interference among these states leads to their strong backward peaking.

## Key claim

The total cross sections of the \(K^- + p \to K^+ + \Xi^-\) and \(K^- + p \to K^0 + \Xi^0\) reactions are dominated by the \(\Lambda(1520)\) intermediate resonant state, but the angular distributions of the outgoing \(K^+\) meson show strong sensitivity to other resonant states and their interference, contradicting the long-standing belief that the strong backward peaking results from \(u\)-channel dominance alone.

## Method

The authors employed a single-channel effective Lagrangian model with \(s\)- and \(u\)-channel diagrams, including as intermediate states the \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) hyperons together with eight of their three- and four-star resonances with masses up to 2.0 GeV [\(\Lambda(1405)\), \(\Lambda(1520)\), \(\Lambda(1670)\), \(\Lambda(1810)\), \(\Lambda(1890)\), \(\Sigma(1385)\), \(\Sigma(1670)\), and \(\Sigma(1750)\)]. Coupling constants at the various vertices were taken from previous studies and SU(3) symmetry considerations, with a form factor cutoff parameter \(\lambda = 1.2\) GeV applied at all vertices.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for \(K^- + p \to K^+ + \Xi^-\) peaks at incident \(K^-\) kinetic energy \(E_{K^-} \approx 1.1\) GeV.
* The \(\Lambda(1520)\) resonance (mass 1.520 GeV, width 0.016 GeV) dominates the total cross section over the entire energy regime below 3.0 GeV.
* The \(\Lambda(1520)\) coupling constants used are \(g_{KRN} = 27.46\) and \(g_{KR\Xi} = -16.610\).
* The \(\Lambda\) ground state coupling constants are \(g_{KRN} = -16.750\) and \(g_{KR\Xi} = 10.132\); the \(\Sigma\) ground state couplings are \(g_{KRN} = 5.580\) and \(g_{KR\Xi} = -13.50\).
* The \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance (mass 1.406 GeV, width 0.050 GeV) has couplings \(g_{KRN} = 1.585\) and \(g_{KR\Xi} = -0.956\).
* The \(\Sigma(1385)\) resonance (mass 1.383 GeV, width 0.036 GeV) has couplings \(g_{KRN} = -6.22\) and \(g_{KR\Xi} = -6.220\).

## Context

This work extends earlier simplified one-meson and two-meson exchange models by Ebelstein (1967) and Mirza (1982), which were unable to properly describe the existing data, by including a comprehensive set of intermediate resonance states. The study is a precursor to the theory of \(\Xi\)-hypernuclei production via \((K^-,K^+)\) reactions, which is important for programs at JPARC and GSI-FAIR, and for constraining the role of the \(\Xi\) hyperon in dense neutron star matter. The results also enable more accurate estimates of the cross section for production of a near-stable six-quark dibaryon resonance (\(H\)) as proposed by Jaffe (1977).
