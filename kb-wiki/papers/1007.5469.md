# Triangular flow in hydrodynamics and transport theory

> In ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, the Fourier decomposition of the relative azimuthal angle, Δϕ, distribution of particle pairs yields a large cos(3Δϕ) component, extending out to large rapidity separations Δη>1. This component captures a significant portion of the ridge and shoulder structures in the Δϕdistribution, which have been observed after contributions from elliptic flow are subtracted. An average finite triangularity due to event-by-event fluctuations in the initial matter distribution, followed by collective flow, naturally produces a cos(3Δϕ) correlation. Using ideal and viscous hydrodynamics, and transport theory, we study the physics of triangular (v_3) flow in comparison to elliptic (v_2), quadrangular (v_4) and pentagonal (v_5) flow. We make quantitative predictions for v_3 at RHIC and LHC as a function of centrality and transverse momentum. Our results for the centrality dependence of v_3 show a quantitative agreement with data extracted from previous correlation measurements by the STAR collaboration. This study supports previous results on the importance of triangular flow in the understanding of ridge and shoulder structures. Triangular flow is found to be a sensitive probe of initial geometry fluctuations and viscosity.

## Key claim

Triangular flow ($v_3$) arises naturally from event-by-event fluctuations in the initial matter distribution followed by collective flow, and the paper provides quantitative predictions showing that this mechanism can explain the ridge and shoulder structures observed in two-particle azimuthal correlations after subtracting elliptic flow contributions, with the caveat that the agreement with STAR data depends on the choice of initial conditions and viscosity.

## Method

The authors used ideal and viscous hydrodynamics (with Glauber and CGC initial conditions) and relativistic Boltzmann transport theory (for massless particles in 2+1 dimensions) to study the response of the system to harmonic deformations of the initial energy density profile. They computed anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ for $n=2,3,4,5$ as functions of deformation magnitude, time, transverse momentum, and viscosity, then scaled results by the root-mean-square eccentricities $\varepsilon_n^{\rm rms}$ from Monte-Carlo Glauber and KLN models to make predictions for $v_3$ at RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- $v_3/\varepsilon_3 \simeq 0.21$ in ideal hydrodynamics, comparable to $v_2/\varepsilon_2$, while $v_4/\varepsilon_4$ is significantly smaller
- Viscous correction parameter $A_3 = 4.2 \pm 0.3$ for $v_3$, compared to $A_2 = 1.4 \pm 0.1$ for $v_2$, indicating relative viscous correction is 3 times larger for $v_3$ than for $v_2$
- $v_3$ at RHIC for central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon: approximately 0.02-0.03 for pions with $0.8 < p_t < 4.0$ GeV/c, depending on initial conditions and viscosity
- Resonance decays multiply $v_3$ by approximately 0.75 at RHIC and 0.83 at LHC
- $\varepsilon_3^{\rm rms}$ varies with number of participants essentially as $(N_{\rm Part})^{-1/2}$

## Context

This work extends the argument of Alver (2010) that triangular flow from initial geometry fluctuations produces the ridge and shoulder structures in two-particle correlations, providing a systematic hydrodynamic and transport study. It builds on prior work on elliptic flow fluctuations (Alver 2010) and quadrangular flow (Gombeaud 2009), and enables quantitative extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ from $v_3$ measurements, since triangular flow is found to be a sensitive probe of both initial geometry fluctuations and viscosity.
