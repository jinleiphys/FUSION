# X(2370) glueball-like particle productions in $e^+e^-$ collisions at the BESIII energy and in pp collisions at the LHC energy with PACIAE model

> Inspired by the BESIII newest observation of X(2370) glueball-like particle, we search its productions in both $e^+e^-$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 4.95 GeV and proton-proton (pp) collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV with a parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE. In this model, the final partonic state (FPS) and the final hadronic state (FHS) are consecutively simulated and recorded. The X(2370) glueball- or tetraquark-state is then, respectively, recombined by two gluons or four quarks $ss\bar{s}\bar{s}$ in the FPS using the quantum statistical mechanics inspired dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model. The X(2370) molecular-state is recombined by the baryon-antibaryon of $Λ$-$\barΛ$ or $Σ$-$\barΣ$, or by three mesons of $π^+π^{-}η'$, $K^+K^-η'$, or $K_S^0K_S^0η'$ in the FHS using DCPC model. In both $e^+e^-$ and pp collisions, significant discrepancies in the yields, the transverse momentum spectra and the rapidity distributions among the X(2370) glueball-, tetraquark-, and molecular-state are observed. These discrepancies are proposed as valuable criteria identifying the X(2370) different states from each other. Our results not only support the BESIII observation of glueball-like particle $\rm X(2370)$ production in $e^+e^-$ collisions, but also serve as a prediction for the $\rm X(2370)$ production in pp collisions. We strongly suggest the experimental measurement of the X(2370) glueball-like particle production in pp collisions at the LHC energies.

# X(2370) glueball-like particle productions in e⁺e⁻ collisions at the BESIII energy and in pp collisions at the LHC energy with PACIAE model

## Key claim

The X(2370) glueball-, tetraquark-, and molecular-state configurations produce significantly different yields, transverse momentum spectra, and rapidity distributions in both e⁺e⁻ collisions at √s = 4.95 GeV and pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV, which the authors propose as criteria to identify the X(2370) nature, but only if the X(2370) is indeed a two-gluon glueball, as the BESIII observation favors, rather than a three-gluon glueball whose predicted mass (3.4–3.6 GeV/c²) is much heavier than the X(2370) mass.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE 3.0 parton and hadron cascade model to simulate 800 million e⁺e⁻ collisions at √s = 4.95 GeV and 100 million pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV, recording both the final partonic state (FPS) and final hadronic state (FHS). The X(2370) glueball-state was formed by coalescing two gluons in the FPS, the tetraquark-state by coalescing four quarks (ss\bar{s}\bar{s}) in the FPS, and the molecular-state by coalescing baryon-antibaryon pairs (Λ-Λ̅ or Σ-Σ̅) or three-meson combinations (π⁺π⁻η′, K⁺K⁻η′, or K_S⁰K_S⁰η′) in the FHS, all using the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model with a radius parameter R₀ = 1.0 fm for glueball and tetraquark states and R₀ = 1.0–2.0 fm for molecular states.

## Key numbers

- X(2370) glueball-state yield in e⁺e⁻ collisions at √s = 4.95 GeV: 1.42 × 10⁻³
- X(2370) B-anti-B molecular-state yield in e⁺e⁻ collisions at √s = 4.95 GeV: 2.63 × 10⁻⁴
- X(2370) 3-meson molecular-state yield in e⁺e⁻ collisions at √s = 4.95 GeV: 1.40 × 10⁻⁵
- X(2370) glueball-state yield in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 1.50
- X(2370) tetraquark-state yield in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 2.63 × 10⁻⁴
- X(2370) B-anti-B molecular-state yield in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 4.12 × 10⁻²
- X(2370) 3-meson molecular-state yield in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 3.13 × 10⁻³
- Extracted temperature of hadronic matter (HM) in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 160 MeV
- Extracted temperature of partonic matter (PM) in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV: 232 MeV
- Mass uncertainty parameter Δm for all X(2370) states: 94 MeV/c²
- Radius parameter R₀ for glueball and tetraquark states: 1.0 fm
- Radius parameter R₀ range for molecular states: 1.0–2.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the BESIII collaboration's observation of the X(2370) as a glueball-like particle in radiative J/ψ decays (BESIII, 2020, 2022, 2023) by providing predictions for its production in pp collisions at LHC energies, where no experimental data yet exist. The study builds on the authors' previous DCPC model applications to exotic hadrons such as X(3872), Z_c(3900), and P_c pentaquark states, and contrasts with the AMPT model results for X(3872) tetraquark and molecular states in nuclear collisions. The results enable experimental tests by ALICE (mid-rapidity) and LHCb (forward-rapidity) to distinguish between glueball, tetraquark, and molecular interpretations of X(2370), and suggest extending the investigation to Pb-Pb collisions where quark-gluon plasma may provide a gluon-rich environment for enhanced glueball production.
