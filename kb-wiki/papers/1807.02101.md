# Effect of flavor-dependent partonic transverse momentum on the determination of the $W$ boson mass in hadronic collisions

> Within the framework of transverse-momentum-dependent factorization, we investigate for the first time the impact of a flavor-dependent intrinsic transverse momentum of quarks on the production of $W^{\pm}$ bosons in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV. We estimate the shift in the extracted value of the $W$ boson mass $M_W$ induced by different choices of flavor-dependent parameters for the intrinsic quark transverse momentum by means of a template fit to the transverse-mass and the lepton transverse-momentum distributions of the $W$-decay products. We obtain $-6\leq ΔM_{W^+} \leq 9$ MeV and $-4\leq ΔM_{W^-} \leq 3$ MeV with a statistical uncertainty of $\pm 2.5$ MeV. Our findings call for more detailed investigations of flavor-dependent nonperturbative effects linked to the proton structure at hadron colliders.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that flavor-dependent intrinsic transverse momentum of quarks can shift the extracted W boson mass by up to 9 MeV for W⁺ and 3 MeV for W⁻ at the LHC, with a statistical uncertainty of ±2.5 MeV, indicating that flavor-blind analyses may be insufficient when total uncertainties below 10 MeV are targeted.

## Method

The authors modified the DYqT and DYRes codes to implement flavor-dependent Gaussian nonperturbative parameters g_NP^a for each parton flavor (u_v, d_v, u_s, d_s, s=c=b=g) in the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization framework. They generated 50 random sets of flavor-dependent parameters, selected 30 "Z-equivalent" sets that reproduced Z boson q_T pseudodata within χ²/d.o.f. < 1.3, and performed template fits to the transverse-mass (m_T) and lepton transverse-momentum (p_Tℓ) distributions of W⁺ and W⁻ bosons at √s = 7 TeV to extract shifts in M_W.

## Key numbers

- The shift in M_W⁺ ranges from -6 to +9 MeV, and for M_W⁻ from -4 to +3 MeV, with a statistical uncertainty of ±2.5 MeV from the template fit.
- For set 3, the shift in M_W⁺ from the p_Tℓ analysis is 9 MeV, compared to the ATLAS uncertainty of 3 MeV for the same observable.
- The flavor-independent nonperturbative parameter for Z boson production is g_NP ~ 0.8 GeV², corresponding to ~0.4 GeV² per parton.
- The evolution-related nonperturbative contribution g_evo ln(Q²/Q₀²) at Q = M_W and Q₀ = 1 GeV is approximately 0.3 GeV², varied in the range [0.2, 0.6] GeV².

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological extraction of flavor-dependent intrinsic transverse momentum from low-energy SIDIS data (Signori 2013) to W boson mass determination at the LHC, where previous experimental analyses by D0 (2013), CDF (2013), and ATLAS (2017) assumed flavor-independent nonperturbative parameters fitted from Z boson data. The results call for more detailed investigations of flavor-dependent nonperturbative effects from future SIDIS data at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV, COMPASS, and the Electron-Ion Collider, as well as from semi-inclusive e⁺e⁻ annihilation data, to reduce uncertainties in precision measurements at high energies.
