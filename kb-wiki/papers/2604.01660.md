# Femtoscopy of Strange Baryons in Heavy-ion Collisions at RHIC-STAR

> Studying the final state interactions and finding possible bound states is helpful for understanding the strong interactions and comprehending the equation-of-state (EoS) of the nuclear matter. In these proceedings, we present recent femtoscopy results of \pXi{}, \LaLa{}, \pOm{} femtoscopic correlations with high statistics Isobar (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr) and Au+Au collisions measured by the STAR experiment. For the \pXi{} and \pOm{} pairs, the centrality dependence of source size and the scattering parameters are extracted with the Lednický-Lyuboshitz approach. The results show that there is an attractive interaction in \pXi{} pairs and a bound state in \pOm{} pairs.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the measured p-Xi- correlation functions show an attractive interaction, and that the p-Om- pairs exhibit a bound state; however, the full text only presents results for p-Xi- pairs, and the bound-state claim for p-Om- appears only in the abstract without supporting numerical results in the provided text.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured two-particle correlation functions for p-Xi- pairs in Isobar (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr) and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The correlation functions were parameterized using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz (LL) model, which assumes a static spherical Gaussian source and an S-wave function with Coulomb and strong interactions, to extract the source size (R_G) and the spin-averaged scattering parameters (scattering length f_0 and effective range d_0) via a simultaneous Bayesian fit across three centrality bins.

## Key numbers

*   The scattering length f_0 for p-Xi- pairs is determined in the range [0.57, 0.90] fm, indicating a shallow attractive interaction.
*   The source size R_G for p-Xi- pairs shows a monotonic decrease from central to peripheral collisions and follows a linear distribution as a function of dN_ch/d_eta (charged particle rapidity density).
*   The extracted f_0 and d_0 values are consistent between Isobar and Au+Au collisions within 1 sigma.

## Context

This work extends femtoscopy measurements of strange baryons to the strangeness S = -2 sector, specifically p-Xi- pairs, which are proposed as a potential decay channel for the H-dibaryon. The results are compared with UrQMD transport model calculations coupled with HALQCD lattice QCD potentials, which describe the data reasonably well. This is the first extraction of strong interaction parameters for p-Xi- pairs, providing constraints for understanding hyperon-nucleon (Y-N) interactions relevant to the equation-of-state of nuclear matter and neutron star structure.
