# Thermal Model Description of p--Pb Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} $ = 5.02 TeV

> The ALICE data on light flavor hadron production obtained in $p-Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} $ = 5.02 TeV are studied in the thermal model using the canonical approach with exact strangeness conservation. The chemical freeze-out temperature is independent of centrality except for the lowest multiplicity bin, with values close to 160 MeV but consistent with those obtained in $Pb-Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. The value of the strangeness non-equilibrium factor $γ_s$ is slowly increasing with multiplicity from 0.9 to 0.96, i.e. it is always very close to full chemical equilibrium.

# Thermal Model Description of p–Pb Collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperature in p–Pb collisions is independent of centrality (except for the lowest multiplicity bin) and consistent with values from Pb–Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, while the strangeness non-equilibrium factor γ_s increases slowly with multiplicity from 0.9 to 0.96, indicating the system is always very close to full chemical equilibrium, with the largest deviations appearing only in the most peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors fit ALICE data on light-flavor hadron yields (π, K, p, Λ, Ξ, Ω, φ) in seven multiplicity bins using the THERMUS code in the strangeness canonical ensemble (SCE) with exact strangeness conservation, while baryon number and electric charge are treated grand-canonically with μ_B and μ_Q fixed to zero. A single radius R parameterizes the system volume, and a strangeness non-equilibrium factor γ_s multiplies each strange-particle density by γ_s^{|s|}, where |s| is the number of strange plus anti-strange quarks.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature for the 0–5% centrality bin: T_ch = 161.10 ± 3.10 MeV.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature for the 80–100% (most peripheral) bin: T_ch = 152.72 ± 2.96 MeV.
- Strangeness non-equilibrium factor γ_s ranges from 0.90 ± 0.032 (80–100% bin) to 0.96 ± 0.031 (0–5% bin).
- Radius R increases from 1.69 ± 0.09 fm (80–100%) to 3.06 ± 0.16 fm (0–5%).
- χ²/ndf values range from 20.07/4 (40–60% bin) to 74.35/4 (80–100% bin), with the worst fits in peripheral collisions.
- In the most peripheral bin (80–100%), the data/fit ratio for Ξ is 2.45 and for Ω is 4.6; the standard deviation for Ξ is 5.5.
- The overall temperature quoted is T_ch = 162 ± 3 MeV, excluding the lowest multiplicity bin.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model description, previously successful in central heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies (Floris 2016, Andronic 2017, Becattini 2017), to small-system p–Pb collisions, confirming that the chemical freeze-out temperature remains near 160 MeV and is independent of system size except for the most peripheral events. The results support the earlier finding by Cleymans (2001) that thermal parameters depend weakly on system size, and they argue against a separate correlation radius for strangeness suppression because the φ meson yield follows the same scaling as Ξ baryons. The analysis enables future comparisons with other small-system collisions (e.g., pp) and tests of whether the canonical suppression of strangeness at low multiplicity can be described without invoking additional parameters.
