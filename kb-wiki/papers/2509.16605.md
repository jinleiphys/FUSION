# Solution to the uncertainty problem of nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay

> The neutrinoless double-$β$ decay ($0νββ$) of nuclei is one of the major research subjects of neutrino physics nowadays because of its influence on particle physics and astrophysics. The predicted nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of the $0νββ$ decay have a large uncertainty depending on the models used to calculate them. This problem has affected the development of neutrino physics for many years. We have performed, recently, the calculation of the NMEs for the $0νββ$ and two-neutrino double-$β$ decay ($2νββ$) modes with a perturbed transition operator and found that the effective axial-vector current coupling ($g_A^\mathrm{eff}$) is similar for these two decay modes. Based on this finding, we calculate the $0νββ$ NMEs using the phenomenological $g_A^\mathrm{eff}$ that reproduces the measured half-life of the $2νββ$ decay. We apply this method to the NMEs for $^{136}$Xe obtained by several groups and show that the uncertainty of the $0νββ$ NME is dramatically reduced. Owing to this finding, we calculate the effective neutrino mass, consistent with the current experimental lower limit of the half-life for the $0νββ$ decay, and the results indicate that this effective neutrino mass value does not yet reach the inverted mass hierarchy region allowed by the neutrino oscillation data and the lightest neutrino mass assumed to be smaller than 10 meV.

## Key claim
The uncertainty in neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) can be dramatically reduced by using an effective axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A^\mathrm{eff}\) extracted from the measured two-neutrino double-beta decay half-life, because the ratio \(g_{A,0\nu}^\mathrm{eff}/g_{A,2\nu}^\mathrm{eff}\) is close to unity (1.14) and nearly converges at first-order perturbation. This method assumes that the leading-order NMEs for both decay modes are calculated with the same interaction and wave functions.

## Method
The authors extended the NME equation for \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay using second-order Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory with a perturbed transition operator, including vertex correction and two-body current terms. They calculated the perturbed NMEs for \(^{136}\)Xe using the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with the Skyrme interaction SkM\(^*\) and contact pairing interactions, then determined \(g_{A,0\nu}^\mathrm{eff}\) and \(g_{A,2\nu}^\mathrm{eff}\) by equating the half-lives computed with perturbed NMEs (using bare \(g_A\)) to those computed with leading-order NMEs (using effective \(g_A\)). They applied the resulting renormalization factor to NMEs from other groups (QRPA, shell model, interacting boson model) that satisfied the condition of using the same interactions for both decay modes.

## Key numbers
- The ratio \(g_{A,0\nu}^\mathrm{eff}(\mathrm{ld;pt}) / g_{A,2\nu}^\mathrm{eff}(\mathrm{ld;pt}) = 1.14\) for the SkM\(^*\) interaction.
- The spreading of the modified \(g_A^2 M_{0\nu}\) values is one-fourth that of the bare ones.
- The current experimental lower limit of the half-life for \(^{136}\)Xe is \(T_{1/2}^{0\nu} = 3.8 \times 10^{26}\) yr.
- For the modified NMEs, a half-life of \(T_{1/2}^{0\nu} = 6.20 \times 10^{27}\) yr places the highest calculated effective neutrino mass at the upper edge of the inverted mass hierarchy region allowed by oscillation data (lightest neutrino mass < 10 meV).
- A half-life of \(T_{1/2}^{0\nu} = 6.62 \times 10^{28}\) yr would place the highest calculated point at the lower edge of that allowed region.
- Bare \(g_A\) values used by other groups range from 1.25 to 1.27.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous perturbed-operator approach (Terasaki 2025) and applies it to resolve the long-standing NME uncertainty problem noted in reviews such as Agostini (2023). It contradicts the implication of many prior calculations that current experimental limits might already probe the inverted hierarchy; instead, the modified NMEs suggest that significantly longer half-lives (above \(6 \times 10^{27}\) yr) are needed. The method enables a unified comparison of results from QRPA (Mustonen and Engel 2013, Simkovic 2018, Fang 2018), shell model (Horoi and Brown 2013, Menendez 2018), and interacting boson model (Barea 2015) calculations for \(^{136}\)Xe.
