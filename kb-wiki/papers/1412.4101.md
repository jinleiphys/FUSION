# Unified description of $^6$Li structure and deuterium-$^4$He dynamics with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces

> Prototype for the study of weakly bound projectiles colliding on stable targets, the scattering of deuterium ($d$) on $^4$He ($α$) is an important milestone in the search for a fundamental understanding of low-energy reactions. At the same time, it is also important for its role in the Big-bang nucleosynthesis of $^6$Li and applications in the characterization of deuterium impurities in materials. We present the first unified {\em ab initio} study of the $^6$Li ground state and $d$-$^4$He elastic scattering using two- and three-nucleon forces derived within the framework of chiral effective field theory. The six-nucleon bound-state and scattering observables are calculated by means of the no-core shell model with continuum. %and are compared to available experimental data. We analyze the influence of the dynamic polarization of the deuterium and of the chiral three-nucleon force, and examine the role of the continuum degrees of freedom in shaping the low-lying spectrum of $^6$Li. We find that the adopted Hamiltonian correctly predicts the binding energy of $^6$Li, yielding an asymptotic $D$- to $S$-state ratio of the $^6$Li wave function in $d+α$ configuration of $-0.027$ in agreement with the value determined from a phase shift analysis of $^6$Li+$^4$He elastic scattering, but overestimates the excitation energy of the first $3^+$ state by $350$ keV. The bulk of the computed differential cross section is in good agreement with data.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first unified *ab initio* study of the $^6$Li ground state and $d$-$^4$He elastic scattering using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces, finding that the adopted Hamiltonian correctly predicts the $^6$Li binding energy and yields an asymptotic $D$- to $S$-state ratio of $-0.027$ in agreement with one experimental determination, but overestimates the excitation energy of the first $3^+$ state by 350 keV, pointing to remaining deficiencies in the three-nucleon force model.

## Method

The authors employed the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) to solve the six-nucleon Schrodinger equation, using a generalized cluster expansion that combines square-integrable $^6$Li eigenstates with continuous $d$-$^4$He binary-cluster basis states. The Hamiltonian was based on the chiral N$^3$LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction and N$^2$LO three-nucleon (3N) force, softened via similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution with momentum scale $\Lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, and calculations included up to 15 discrete $^6$Li eigenstates and up to seven deuteron pseudostates in the $^3S_1{-}{}^3D_1$, $^3D_2$ and $^3D_3{-}{}^3G_3$ channels at $N_{\rm max}=11$ with harmonic oscillator frequency $\hbar\Omega=20$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- $^6$Li ground-state binding energy: NCSMC result of -32.01 MeV at $N_{\rm max}=10$ compared to experimental value of -31.99 MeV
- Asymptotic $D$- to $S$-state ratio ($\eta = C_2/C_0$) of the $^6$Li wave function in $d+\alpha$ configuration: $-0.027$ from NCSMC with $NN+3N$ Hamiltonian, compared to experimental determinations of $-0.025(6)(10)$ (George 1999) and $0.0003(9)$ (Veal 1998)
- $S$-wave asymptotic normalization constant $C_0 = 2.695$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $D$-wave constant $C_2 = -0.074$ fm$^{-1/2}$ from NCSMC with $NN+3N$ Hamiltonian
- Overestimation of the first $3^+$ state excitation energy by 350 keV
- Extrapolated NCSM $^6$Li ground-state energy: $-32.2(3)$ MeV for $NN+3N$ Hamiltonian
- Extrapolated NCSM $3^+$ resonance centroid: $0.99(9)$ MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier *ab initio* studies of $d$-$^4$He scattering by Navratil (2011) that used only NN interactions without coupling to square-integrable $^6$Li eigenstates, and complements bound-state NCSM calculations by Pieper (2001), Navratil (2003), and Jurgenson (2011) that treated $^6$Li resonances as bound states without providing widths. The NCSMC approach, previously applied to nucleon-nucleus collisions by Baroni (2013) and to $p$-$^4$He scattering by Hupin (2014), is here extended to deuteron-nucleus dynamics. The results enable the first *ab initio* study of the $^2$H$(\alpha,\gamma)^6$Li radiative capture and serve as a stepping stone for calculations of deuterium-tritium fusion with chiral $NN+3N$ Hamiltonians.
