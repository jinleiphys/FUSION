# Triplet pairing in neutron matter in a comprehensive diagrammatic approach

> We apply a large-scale summation of Feynman diagrams, including the class of parquet diagrams plus important contributions outside the parquet class, for calculating effective pairing interactions and subsequently the superfluid gap in P-wave pairing in neutron matter. We use realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions of the $v_8$ type and perform calculations up to a Fermi momentum of 1.8 fm$^{-1}$. We find that many-body correlations lead to a strong reduction of the spin-orbit interaction, and, therefore, to a radical suppression of the $^3P_2-^3F_2$ gap and an enhancement of the $^3P_0$ gap.

## Key claim

Many-body correlations, particularly spin-flip processes beyond the parquet class of diagrams, radically suppress the in-medium spin-orbit interaction, leading to a very weak \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) superfluid gap and a non-negligible enhancement of the \(^3P_0\) gap in neutron matter up to a Fermi momentum of 1.8 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors used correlated basis function theory, starting from a BCS trial state and applying a correlation operator \(\hat{F}_N\) that encodes dynamical correlations. They performed a large-scale summation of Feynman diagrams including the parquet class (rings and ladders) plus important beyond-parquet diagrams that describe spin-flip processes in intermediate states, which are required by the operator structure of realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions of the \(v_8\) type. The effective pairing interactions were then used to calculate the superfluid gap in the triplet channels.

## Key numbers

- The \(^3P_0\) gap reaches 0.4–0.8 MeV for the interactions used in this work.
- Calculations were performed up to a Fermi momentum of 1.8 fm\(^{-1}\).
- The in-medium spin-orbit effective interaction is shown for the Reid \(v_8\) potential at different Fermi momenta, compared to the bare interaction (dashed line).

## Context

This work extends the correlated basis function approach to triplet pairing in neutron matter by including beyond-parquet diagrams, which previous microscopic calculations had ignored or treated phenomenologically. The results contradict earlier predictions of sizable \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) gaps and instead show a radical suppression of that channel, while the \(^3P_0\) gap becomes non-negligible. This has implications for neutron star cooling mechanisms, and future extensions could include the effect of three-body forces.
