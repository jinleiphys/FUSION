# Chiral Analysis of the Generalized Form Factors of the Nucleon

> We apply the methods of Chiral Perturbation Theory to the analysis of the first moments of the Generalized Parton Distributions in a Nucleon, usually known as generalized form factors. These quantities are currently also under investigation in Lattice QCD analyses of baryon structure, providing simulation results at large quark masses to be extrapolated to the "real world" via Chiral Effective Field Theory. We have performed a leading-one-loop calculation in the covariant framework of Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (BChPT), predicting both the momentum and the quark-mass dependence for all the vector and axial (generalized) form factors. In particular we discuss the results for the limit of vanishing four-momentum transfer where the GPD-moments reduce to the well known moments of Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs). We fit our results to available lattice QCD data, extrapolating down to the physical point. We conclude by presenting outstanding results from a combined fit to different GPDs-moments.

## Key claim

The authors perform a leading-one-loop calculation in covariant Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (BChPT) for the generalized form factors of the nucleon, and show that the covariant framework can smoothly extrapolate lattice QCD data from large quark masses down to the physical point, whereas the non-relativistic Heavy Baryon ChPT (HBChPT) truncation fails to do so.

## Method

The authors compute the first Mellin moments of the nucleon's generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at leading-one-loop order [O(p²)] in covariant BChPT with two light flavors, using a variant of infrared regularization. They fit the resulting chiral expressions for the isovector and isoscalar generalized form factors A₂₀ᵛ(0) and A₂₀ˢ(0) (which reduce to the PDF momentum fractions ⟨x⟩_{u-d} and ⟨x⟩_{u+d} at zero momentum transfer) to lattice QCD data from the LHPC and QCDSF collaborations. A combined fit is also performed for the isovector vector and axial moments simultaneously, exploiting common low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- For ⟨x⟩_{u-d}, Fit I (2-parameter fit to 4 LHPC lattice points, with Δa₂₀ᵛ fixed to 0.210) yields a₂₀ᵛ = 0.157 ± 0.006 and c₈ʳ(1 GeV) = -0.283 ± 0.011.
- For ⟨x⟩_{u-d}, Fit II (3-parameter fit to 6+1 points including the physical point) yields a₂₀ᵛ = 0.141 ± 0.0057, Δa₂₀ᵛ = 0.144 ± 0.034, and c₈ʳ(1 GeV) = -0.213 ± 0.03.
- For ⟨x⟩_{u+d}, a 2-parameter fit to LHPC and QCDSF lattice data gives a₂₀ˢ = 0.513 ± 0.006 and c₉ = -0.064 ± 0.005.
- The phenomenological value of ⟨x⟩_{u-d} at the physical point is 0.160 ± 0.006 (at μ = 2 GeV).

## Context

This work extends prior leading-one-loop analyses of nucleon generalized form factors performed in the non-relativistic Heavy Baryon ChPT framework (GPDs, 2005-2006) by providing the first covariant BChPT calculation for these quantities. The covariant result reproduces the HBChPT result in the small-pion-mass limit but includes an infinite tower of (m_π/M_0)ⁿ terms that enable smooth extrapolation to lattice data at pion masses up to ~600 MeV, where HBChPT fails. The combined fit of vector and axial moments demonstrates that common couplings between observables can reduce statistical uncertainties, pointing toward a strategy for more reliable chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results.
