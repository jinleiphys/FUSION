# Correlations in the properties of static and rapidly rotating compact stars

> Correlations in the properties of the static compact stars (CSs) and the ones rotating with the highest observed frequency of 1122Hz are studied using a large set of equations of state (EOSs). These EOSs span various approaches and their chemical composition vary from the nucleons to hyperons and quarks in $β$-equilibrium. It is found that the properties of static CS, like, the maximum gravitational mass $M_{\rm max}^{\rm stat}$ and radius $R_{1.4}^{\rm stat}$ corresponding to t he canonical mass and supramassive or non-supramassive nature of the CS rotating at 1122 Hz are strongly correlated. In particular, only those EOSs yield the CS rotating at 1122Hz to be non-supramassive for which $\left (\frac{M_{\rm max}^{\rm stat}}{M_\odot}\right )^{1/2} \left (\frac{10{\rm km}}{R_{1.4}^{\rm stat}})^{3/2} $ is greater than unity. Suitable parametric form which can be used to split the $M_{\rm max}^{\rm stat}$ $-$ $R_{1.4}^{\rm stat}$ plane into the regions of different supramassive nature of the CS rotating at 1122Hz is presented. Currently measured maximum gravitational mass 1.76$M_\odot$ of PSR J0437-4715 suggests that the CS rotating at 1122Hz can be non-supramassive provided $R_{1.4}^{\rm stat} \leqslant 12.4$ km.

# Correlations in the properties of static and rapidly rotating compact stars

## Key claim
The supramassive or non-supramassive nature of a compact star rotating at 1122 Hz is strongly correlated with a specific combination of the static star's maximum gravitational mass and its radius at the canonical mass of 1.4 solar masses. Specifically, a rotating star is non-supramassive only when (M_max^stat / M_sun)^(1/2) * (10 km / R_1.4^stat)^(3/2) is greater than unity, a condition that depends on the relative behavior of the equation of state at high versus low densities.

## Method
The authors computed the properties of static and axially symmetric rotating compact stars by solving Einstein's equations in 1D and 2D using the RNS code of Stergioulas and Friedman. They employed 24 diverse equations of state spanning variational approaches, relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field models, and the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model, with chemical compositions ranging from nucleons to hyperons and quarks in beta-equilibrium. The key quantity delta M_B, defined as the difference between the maximum baryonic mass of the static star and the minimum baryonic mass for the star rotating at 1122 Hz, was used to classify stars as supramassive (delta M_B less than 0) or non-supramassive (delta M_B greater than 0).

## Key numbers
- The highest observed rotation frequency considered is 1122 Hz, corresponding to the X-ray transient XTE J1739-285 discovered by Kaaret et al. (2006).
- For equations of state with R_1.4^stat greater than approximately 14 km, delta M_B is less than 0 (supramassive); for those with M_max^stat greater than 2 solar masses and R_1.4^stat around 10-11 km, delta M_B is approximately 0.75 solar masses.
- The best-fit parameters for the correlation delta M_B / M_sun = a_0 + a_1 * (M_max^stat / M_sun)^alpha * (10 km / R_1.4^stat)^beta are: a_0 = -2.75, a_1 = 2.5, alpha = 0.75, beta = 1.56.
- The currently measured maximum gravitational mass of 1.76 solar masses for PSR J0437-4715 (Verbiest 2008) implies that a compact star rotating at 1122 Hz can be non-supramassive only if R_1.4^stat is less than or equal to 12.4 km.
- The corresponding upper limit on the moment of inertia for a 1.4 solar mass static star is I_1.4^stat less than or equal to 1.53 x 10^45 g cm^-2, with best-fit parameters a_0' = -3.25, a_1' = 3.25, alpha' = 0.63, beta' = 0.85.
- The difference in gravitational mass delta M between the maximum and minimum equatorial radius configurations for stars rotating at 1122 Hz is less than or approximately 0.1 solar masses when delta M_B is negative.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Bejger et al. (2007) on the structure of compact stars rotating at 1122 Hz, and addresses the lack of systematic correlations between M_max^stat and R_1.4^stat alone noted by Bejger et al. (2005). The authors demonstrate that the combination of these two static properties, analogous to the Newtonian expression for Keplerian frequency, provides a clean diagnostic for whether a rapidly rotating star is supramassive. The results enable future work to predict the properties of stars rotating at 1122 Hz from only the static star parameters, and the authors suggest repeating the investigation for even higher rotation frequencies. The upper limit on R_1.4^stat of 12.4 km derived from the mass of PSR J0437-4715 is noted to be close to the lower limit of 12.9 km obtained from X-ray spectra of the quiescent low-mass X-ray binary X7 in 47 Tucanae (Heinke 2006).
