# Liquid-gas Phase Transition in Strange Hadronic Matter with Weak Y-Y Interaction

> The liquid-gas phase transition in strange hadronic matter is reexamined by using the new parameters about the $Λ- Λ$ interaction deduced from recent observation of $^{6}_{ΛΛ}He$ double hypernucleus. The extended Furnstahl-Serot-Tang model with nucleons and hyperons is utilized. The binodal surface, the limit pressure, the entropy, the specific heat capacity and the Caloric curves are addressed. We find that the liquid-gas phase transition can occur more easily in strange hadronic matter with weak Y-Y interaction than that of the strong Y-Y interaction.

## Key claim

The liquid-gas phase transition can occur more easily in strange hadronic matter with weak hyperon-hyperon (Y-Y) interaction than with strong Y-Y interaction, as indicated by a higher limit pressure and a larger binodal surface region; this conclusion depends on using new parameters for the Λ-Λ interaction derived from the observation of the ⁶_ΛΛHe double hypernucleus.

## Method

The authors employed the extended Furnstahl-Serot-Tang (FST) model in mean-field approximation, including nucleons and Λ, Ξ hyperons coupled to σ, ω, σ*, and φ mesons. They used parameters for weak Y-Y interaction (g²_σ*Λ = 28.73, g²_σ*Ξ = 129.06) deduced from recent experimental data, in contrast to earlier strong Y-Y parameters. The binodal surface, limit pressure, entropy, specific heat capacity, and caloric curves were calculated by solving the Gibbs equilibrium conditions for two-component strange hadronic matter at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Limit pressure for weak Y-Y interaction: p_lim^W = 0.50 MeV fm⁻³, compared to p_lim^S = 0.095 MeV fm⁻³ for strong Y-Y interaction.
- Λ-Λ interaction energy from ⁶_ΛΛHe observation: ΔB_ΛΛ = 1.01 ± 0.20⁺⁰·¹⁸_₋₀·₁₁ MeV, much smaller than the previous estimate of ΔB_ΛΛ ≈ 4–5 MeV.
- Coupling constants for weak Y-Y interaction: g²_σ*Λ = 28.73 and g²_σ*Ξ = 129.06; for strong Y-Y interaction: g²_σ*Λ = 48.31 and g²_σ*Ξ = 154.62.
- At T = 10 MeV, the inflection point on chemical potential isobars occurs at pressure pⁱ = 0.50 MeV fm⁻³ for weak Y-Y interaction.
- For f_S > 0.7 with weak Y-Y interaction at T = 10 MeV, the pressure increases monotonically with baryon density and no mechanical unstable regions exist.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of liquid-gas phase transition in strange hadronic matter using the extended FST model (Yang 2003), and contrasts with results obtained using strong Y-Y interaction parameters. The new weak Y-Y parameters are based on the observation of ⁶_ΛΛHe by Takahashi (2001), which gave a much smaller Λ-Λ binding energy than earlier experiments by Danysz (1963), Dalitz (1989), and others. The findings enable experimental discrimination between strong and weak Y-Y interactions through differences in caloric curves and phase transition temperatures, and are consistent with the conclusion from Song (2003) and Qian (2004) that strange hadronic matter with weak Y-Y interaction is less stable.
