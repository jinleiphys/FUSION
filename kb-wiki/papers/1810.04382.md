# $Ωd$ bound state

> The lattice QCD analyses of the HAL QCD Collaboration predicts a strongly attractive potential in the $ΩN$ $^5S_2$ channel which supports a bound state. In this paper we show that this $ΩN$ channel together with the $NN$ $^3S_1$ channel give rise to a $Ωd$ bound state in the state with maximal spin $(I,J^P)=(0,5/2^+)$ with a binding energy of $\sim$ 17 MeV.

# Ωd bound state

## Key claim
Using the HAL QCD Collaboration's lattice-QCD-derived ΩN interaction in the ⁵S₂ channel (which itself supports a bound state) together with a standard NN ³S₁ potential, the Ωd three-body system forms a bound state in the maximal spin channel (I,Jᴾ)=(0,5/2⁺) with a binding energy of approximately 17 MeV, though the actual calculated value depends on including Coulomb and imaginary contributions.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the ΩNN three-body bound-state problem in the (I,Jᴾ)=(0,5/2⁺) channel, using the ΩN ⁵S₂ potential from Sekihara, Kamiya and Hyodo (2018), which reproduces the HAL QCD scattering amplitude, and the Malfliet-Tjon model for the NN ³S₁ potential. The two-body t-matrices were obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and the three-body equations were solved by expanding amplitudes in Legendre polynomials. The imaginary part of the eigenvalue was computed via first-order perturbation theory using the wave function, and the Coulomb energy was added separately.

## Key numbers
- Ωd binding energy measured from the ΩNN threshold: 16.34 MeV (real part before Coulomb correction)
- Imaginary part of the eigenvalue from perturbation theory: -i 2.05 MeV
- Coulomb energy contribution: 0.85 MeV
- Final complex energy relative to ΩNN threshold: E = -17.19 - i 2.05 MeV
- ΩN two-body binding energy in the same model: 0.3 MeV (from Sekihara, Kamiya and Hyodo 2018)

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD predictions of the HAL QCD Collaboration (2018) for the ΩN ⁵S₂ channel, which showed a strongly attractive potential supporting a bound state, to the three-body Ωd system. It uses the ΩN potential model of Sekihara, Kamiya and Hyodo (2018) and the NN Malfliet-Tjon potential from Friar et al. (1990). The results provide a theoretical prediction that could motivate experimental searches in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as discussed by Morita et al. (2016), and may help design experiments to test lattice-QCD-based predictions for strange baryonic systems.
