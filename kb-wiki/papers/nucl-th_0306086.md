# Quantum Mechanical Three-Body Problem with Short-Range Interactions

> We have investigated S-wave bound states composed of three identical bosons interacting via regulated delta function potentials in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. For low-energy systems, these short-range potentials serve as an approximation to the underlying physics, leading to an effective field theory. A method for perturbatively expanding the three-body bound-state equation in inverse powers of the cutoff is developed. This allows us to extract some analytical results concerning the behavior of the system. Further results are obtained by solving the leading order equations numerically to 11 or 12 digits of accuracy. The limit-cycle behavior of the required three-body contact interaction is computed, and the cutoff-independence of bound-state energies is shown. By studying the relationship between the two- and three-body binding energies, we obtain a high accuracy numerical calculation of Efimov's universal function. Equations for the first order corrections, necessary for the study of cutoff dependence, are derived. However, a numerical solution of these equations is not attempted.

## Key claim
The paper develops a perturbative expansion of the three-body bound-state equation in inverse powers of a momentum cutoff, enabling analytical results for the system's behavior. The abstract's claim of "extracting some analytical results" is hedged by the fact that the first-order correction equations are derived but not solved numerically, limiting the practical demonstration of cutoff independence to leading order.

## Method
The author studies S-wave bound states of three identical bosons interacting via regulated delta-function potentials in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. A perturbative expansion in inverse powers of the cutoff is applied to the three-body bound-state equation, and the leading-order equations are solved numerically to high precision. The limit-cycle behavior of the three-body contact interaction is computed, and the relationship between two- and three-body binding energies is used to calculate Efimov's universal function.

## Key numbers
- Leading-order equations solved numerically to "11 or 12 digits of accuracy" (exact number not specified in text).
- The phase calculation for the integral equation shows exponential convergence, with a plateau indicating "approximately 13 digits" of accuracy (from error analysis in Appendix).
- The error in the numerical integration decays as exp(-2πr/a), where a is the integration point spacing and r is the analyticity strip half-width (from Appendix proof).

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to three-body systems with short-range interactions, building on the foundational ideas of Wilson (unpublished notes) and the effective range expansion for two-body scattering (Taylor, Newton). It provides a high-precision numerical calculation of Efimov's universal function, enabling future studies of cutoff dependence in three-body bound states, though the first-order correction equations remain unsolved numerically. The method contrasts with earlier square-well potential approximations by using regulated delta-function potentials and a systematic expansion in the cutoff.
