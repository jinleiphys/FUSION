# Ideal hydrodynamics and elliptic flow at SPS energies: Importance of the initial conditions

> The elliptic flow excitation function calculated in a full (3+1)d hybrid Boltzmann approach with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage for heavy ion reactions from GSI-SIS to the highest CERN-SPS energies is discussed in the context of the experimental data. In this study, we employ a hadron gas equation of state to investigate the differences in the dynamics and viscosity effects. The specific event-by-event setup with initial conditions and freeze-out from a non-equilibrium transport model allows for a direct comparison between ideal fluid dynamics and transport simulations. At higher SPS energies, where the pure transport calculation cannot account for the high elliptic flow values, the smaller mean free path in the hydrodynamic evolution leads to higher elliptic flow values. In contrast to previous studies within pure hydrodynamics, the more realistic initial conditions employed here and the inclusion of a sequential final state hadronic decoupling provides results that are in line with the experimental data almost over the whole energy range from $E_{\rm lab}=2-160A $GeV. Thus, this new approach leads to a substantially different shape of the $v_2/ε$ scaling curve as a function of $(1/S dN_{ch}/dy)$ in line with the experimental data compared to previous ideal hydrodynamic calculations. This hints to a strong influence of the initial conditions for the hydrodynamic evolution on the finally observed $v_2$ values, thus questioning the standard interpretation that the hydrodynamic limit is only reached at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that ideal hydrodynamics can reproduce elliptic flow data at SPS energies (not just at RHIC) when realistic initial conditions from a transport model and a gradual freeze-out procedure are used, contradicting the standard interpretation that the hydrodynamic limit is only reached at RHIC energies. The authors hedge that this result is obtained with a hadron gas equation of state (no phase transition) and that the hydrodynamic stage is short at lower energies, so the agreement depends strongly on the specific initial and final boundary conditions.

## Method

The authors use a (3+1)d hybrid Boltzmann+hydrodynamics approach (UrQMD + SHASTA algorithm) where the initial conditions and final hadronic freeze-out are generated by the UrQMD transport model on an event-by-event basis, with an intermediate ideal hydrodynamic stage using a hadron gas equation of state. Two freeze-out prescriptions are compared: an isochronous freeze-out on a constant-time hypersurface and a gradual freeze-out where transverse slices are converted to particles when each slice meets the freeze-out criterion. The elliptic flow excitation function is computed for Pb+Pb/Au+Au collisions from \(E_{\rm lab}=2-160A\) GeV and compared to pure transport calculations and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The average duration of the hydrodynamic evolution increases from \(\sim 3\) fm at low energies to around 8 fm at \(E_{\rm lab}=40A\) GeV and \(\sim 12\) fm at the highest SPS energy.
- The freeze-out criterion is set when the energy density drops below five times the ground state energy density (\(\sim 730\) MeV/fm\(^3\)).
- For the gradual freeze-out, transverse slices of thickness \(\Delta z = 0.2\) fm are used.
- The hybrid model calculation shows that ideal hydrodynamics is less than 20% away from the experimental data at higher SPS energies.
- At very low energies (around \(E_{\rm lab}=1A\) GeV), all different setups show the same results and are compatible with the data.
- For the gradual freeze-out, the \(v_2\) values are reduced by more than a factor of 2 compared to ideal hydrodynamics with simplified initial conditions.

## Context

This work extends previous pure transport calculations (e.g., Burau (2004), Petersen (2006), Lin (2001)) that found insufficient pressure to reproduce high elliptic flow at RHIC, and contrasts with earlier pure hydrodynamic calculations (e.g., Kolb (2000), Huovinen (2001)) that overpredicted elliptic flow at SPS energies. The hybrid approach with realistic initial conditions and gradual freeze-out produces a \(v_2/\epsilon\) scaling curve with a shape similar to that obtained in a two-dimensional hybrid calculation by Teaney (2000) but using only hadronic degrees of freedom. The results enable a re-evaluation of whether the hydrodynamic limit is uniquely reached at RHIC energies, and demonstrate that initial conditions and freeze-out treatment are crucial for any hydrodynamic calculation.
