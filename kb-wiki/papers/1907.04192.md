# Bulk viscosity of baryonic matter with trapped neutrinos

> We study bulk viscosity arising from weak current Urca processes in dense baryonic matter at and beyond nuclear saturation density. We consider the temperature regime where neutrinos are trapped and therefore have nonzero chemical potential. We model the nuclear matter in a relativistic density functional approach, taking into account the trapped neutrino component. We find that the resonant maximum of the bulk viscosity would occur at or below the neutrino trapping temperature, so in the neutrino trapped regime the bulk viscosity decreases with temperature as $T^{-2}$, this decrease being interrupted by a drop to zero at a special temperature where the proton fraction becomes density-independent and the material scale-invariant. The bulk viscosity is larger for matter with lower lepton fraction, i.e., larger isospin asymmetry. We find that bulk viscosity in the neutrino-trapped regime is smaller by several orders of magnitude than in the neutrino-transparent regime, which implies that bulk viscosity in neutrino-trapped matter is probably not strong enough to affect significantly the evolution of neutron star mergers. This also implies weak damping of gravitational waves emitted by the oscillations of the postmerger remnant in the high-temperature, neutrino-trapped phase of evolution.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of neutrino-trapped baryonic matter is smaller by several orders of magnitude than in the neutrino-transparent regime, implying that it is probably not strong enough to significantly affect neutron star merger evolution or damp gravitational waves from postmerger remnants, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the resonant maximum of bulk viscosity occurs at or below the neutrino trapping temperature (approximately 5 MeV).

## Method

The authors computed the bulk viscosity arising from direct Urca weak processes (neutron beta decay and electron capture on protons) in dense baryonic matter at densities 0.5 to 3 times nuclear saturation density and temperatures from approximately 5 to 50 MeV, using a relativistic density functional approach to model nuclear matter with a trapped neutrino component. They derived the beta-equilibration rates from three-dimensional phase-space integrals without using the Fermi surface approximation, then solved the linearized rate equations for density perturbations to obtain the bulk viscosity as a function of temperature, density, and lepton fraction.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino trapping temperature: approximately 5 MeV
- Density range studied: 0.5 to 3 times nuclear saturation density (n0 approximately 0.16 fm^-3)
- In the neutrino-trapped regime, bulk viscosity decreases with temperature as T^-2
- Bulk viscosity in neutrino-trapped matter is smaller by several orders of magnitude than in neutrino-transparent matter
- At temperatures of 30 MeV, the suppression factor for positron processes is approximately 0.01 relative to electron processes
- At temperatures of 50 MeV, the suppression factor for positron processes is approximately 0.1 relative to electron processes

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bulk viscosity in cold neutron star matter (Sawyer 1979, 1980, 1989; Haensel 1992; Alford 2010) and proto-neutron star matter to the high-temperature, neutrino-trapped regime relevant to neutron star mergers. It contrasts with the neutrino-transparent case treated in Alford (2019) and earlier work by Alford (2017) which suggested modified Urca processes could produce significant dissipation on millisecond timescales. The results enable assessment of whether bulk viscous damping of gravitational waves from postmerger remnants is significant, and provide a baseline for future studies of more complex phases of baryonic matter and adiabatic (rather than isothermal) treatments.
