# Estimate of the theoretical uncertainty of the cross sections for nucleon knockout in neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions

> Free nucleons propagating in water are known to produce gamma rays, which form a background to the searches for diffuse supernova neutrinos and sterile neutrinos carried out with Cherenkov detectors. As a consequence, the process of nucleon knockout induced by neutral-current quasielastic interactions of atmospheric (anti)neutrinos with oxygen needs to be under control at the quantitative level in the background simulations of the ongoing and future experiments. In this paper, we provide a quantitative assessment of the uncertainty associated with the theoretical description of the nuclear cross sections, estimating it from the discrepancies between the predictions of different models.

## Key claim

The theoretical uncertainty of neutral-current quasielastic nucleon-knockout cross sections on oxygen is estimated from the spread among predictions of six different nuclear models; the spread depends appreciably on energy and is larger for antineutrino-induced reactions, with the relativistic Green's function approach giving the highest cross sections and the superscaling approach the lowest.

## Method

The authors compare predictions for neutral-current quasielastic neutrino- and antineutrino-oxygen cross sections leading to neutron or proton knockout, using six theoretical approaches: spectral function (SF), relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), relativistic mean-field (RMF), relativistic Green's function (RGF) with two optical-potential parametrizations (EDAI and "democratic"), superscaling (SuSA), and relativistic Fermi gas (RFG). All calculations use the same axial mass \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV, dipole axial form factor, and electromagnetic form factors from Kelly and Riodan parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- For neutrino scattering, the discrepancy between RPWIA, RFG, and SF predictions is 21% at 0.6 GeV and 23% at 1.5 GeV, similar for neutron and proton knockout.
- For antineutrino scattering, the spread between RPWIA, RFG, and SF reaches 40% at 0.6 GeV and 29% at 1.5 GeV for neutron knockout.
- At \(E_\nu = 600\) MeV, SuSA cross sections are lower than RGF ones by about 37% for \((\nu,\nu^\prime N)\) and about 47% for \((\bar\nu,\bar\nu^\prime N)\).
- At \(E_\nu = 1.5\) GeV, SuSA cross sections are about 30–33% lower than RGF ones.
- The discrepancies between SF, RMF, and SuSA predictions do not exceed about 15% over the energy range 0.3 to 2.0 GeV.
- The RGF results using EDAI and "democratic" potentials differ by about 5–10% at \(E_\nu \sim 2\) GeV and about 10–25% in the energy region 0.3–0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the spectral-function-based estimates of gamma-ray backgrounds from nucleon knockout in water-Cherenkov detectors (Ankowski et al., 2014; Ankowski et al., 2015) by quantifying the model dependence across six theoretical frameworks. The results are directly relevant to the diffuse supernova neutrino search at Super-Kamiokande and the sterile neutrino search at T2K, where neutral-current quasielastic interactions on oxygen produce neutrons whose subsequent gamma emission constitutes a background. The analysis enables experimental collaborations to assign systematic uncertainties to their background simulations, though the authors caution that multinucleon emission processes not included in any of the models may alter the pattern.
