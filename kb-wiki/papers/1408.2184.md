# Nuclear spin-dependent interactions: Searches for WIMP, Axion and Topological Defect Dark Matter, and Tests of Fundamental Symmetries

> We calculate the proton and neutron spin contributions for nuclei using semi-empirical methods, as well as a novel hybrid \emph{ab initio}/semi-empirical method, for interpretation of experimental data. We demonstrate that core-polarisation corrections to \emph{ab initio} nuclear shell model calculations generally reduce discrepancies in proton and neutron spin expectation values from different calculations. We derive constraints on the spin-dependent P,T-violating interaction of a bound proton with nucleons, which for certain ranges of exchanged pseudoscalar boson masses improve on the most stringent laboratory limits by several orders of magnitude. We derive a limit on the CPT and Lorentz-invariance-violating parameter $|\tilde{b}_{\perp}^p| < 7.6 \times 10^{-33}$ GeV, which improves on the most stringent existing limit by a factor of 8, and demonstrate sensitivities to the parameters $\tilde{d}_{\perp}^p$ and $\tilde{g}_{ D\perp}^p$ at the level $\sim 10^{-29} - 10^{-28}$ GeV, which is a one order of magnitude improvement compared to the corresponding existing sensitivities. We extend previous analysis of nuclear anapole moment data for Cs to obtain new limits on several other CPT and Lorentz-invariance-violating parameters: $\left|b_0^p \right| < 7 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV, $\left|d_{00}^p \right| < 8 \times 10^{-8}$, $\left|b_0^n \right| < 3 \times 10^{-7}$ GeV and $\left|d_{00}^n \right| < 3 \times 10^{-7}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear many-body effects, specifically core-polarisation corrections, significantly alter the proton and neutron spin expectation values compared to the single-particle Schmidt model, and that these corrections are essential for correctly interpreting experimental data from dark matter searches and fundamental symmetry tests; the abstract's claim of "improvements by several orders of magnitude" applies only for certain ranges of exchanged pseudoscalar boson masses, not universally.

## Method

The authors calculated proton and neutron spin expectation values for a wide range of odd-proton and odd-neutron nuclei using three approaches: the Schmidt single-particle model, a minimal model that transfers spin between valence and core nucleons to match experimental magnetic dipole moments, and a preferred model that separately conserves total proton and total neutron angular momenta. They also developed a novel hybrid method that applies semi-empirical core-polarisation corrections to existing *ab initio* nuclear shell model calculations from Engel (1991), Ressell (1997), and Toivanen (2009), using experimental magnetic dipole moments as constraints. These spin content values were then applied to reanalyse data from ³He/¹²⁹Xe comagnetometer experiments and nuclear anapole moment measurements.

## Key numbers

- Limit on CPT and Lorentz-invariance-violating parameter |b̃_⊥^p| < 7.6 × 10⁻³³ GeV, improving on the most stringent existing limit from Brown (2010) by a factor of 8
- Sensitivities to parameters d̃_⊥^p and g̃_D⊥^p at the level ~10⁻²⁹ – 10⁻²⁸ GeV, a one order of magnitude improvement compared to sensitivities derived in Peck (2012)
- Limit on |b̃_⊥^n| < 1.5 × 10⁻³³ GeV from reanalysis of Allmendinger (2014) data
- New limits on CPT and Lorentz-invariance-violating parameters from Cs anapole moment data: |b₀^p| < 7 × 10⁻⁸ GeV, |d₀₀^p| < 8 × 10⁻⁸, |b₀^n| < 3 × 10⁻⁷ GeV, and |d₀₀^n| < 3 × 10⁻⁷ (many-body model)
- Spin expectation values for 25 odd-proton nuclei and 20 odd-neutron nuclei presented in Tables 1 and 2, with values ranging from -0.409 to 0.500 for Schmidt model and -0.297 to 0.500 for the preferred model

## Context

This work extends previous nuclear many-body calculations by Flambaum (2006) and Berengut (2011) to a broader set of nuclei relevant to dark matter searches and fundamental symmetry tests. The improved spin content values enable more accurate interpretation of comagnetometer experiments such as those by Gemmel (2010), Allmendinger (2014), Cane (2004), and Tullney (2013), and extend the anapole moment analysis of Roberts (2014) to additional CPT-violating parameters. The results enable future experiments searching for WIMP dark matter via spin-dependent scattering, axion dark matter via nuclear magnetic resonance (CASPEr), and topological defect dark matter via the GNOME network to properly account for nuclear structure effects.
