# Anomalous magnetic moment of the muon in a dispersive approach

> We present a new general dispersive formalism for evaluating the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. In the suggested approach, this correction is related to the imaginary part of the muon's electromagnetic vertex function. The latter may be directly related to measurable hadronic processes by means of unitarity and analyticity. As a test we apply the introduced formalism to the case of meson pole exchanges and find agreement with the direct two-loop calculation.

## Key claim

The authors present a new dispersive formalism for the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment that, unlike previous model-dependent approaches, relates the correction directly to measurable hadronic processes via unitarity and analyticity; as a test, they apply it to meson pole exchanges and find exact agreement with the direct two-loop calculation, but the full data-driven application to realistic channels (e.g., two-pion intermediate states) is left for future work.

## Method

The authors express the HLbL contribution to the muon's Pauli form factor \(F_2(k^2)\) as a dispersive integral over its discontinuity \(\mathrm{Disc}_{k^2}F_2(k^2)\) in the complex \(k^2\) plane. Using unitarity, this discontinuity is decomposed into nine topologically distinct contributions (unitarity diagrams) corresponding to on-shell intermediate hadronic states, such as \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs (C-odd) and \(\pi^0\) (C-even). As a test, they evaluate the dispersive integrals for the pseudoscalar meson pole approximation (large-\(N_c\) limit) and compare the result to the conventional two-loop Euclidean integration of Knecht et al. (2001).

## Key numbers

* The two main existing estimates of the HLbL contribution to \(a_\mu\) are \((116 \pm 39) \times 10^{-11}\) (Jegerlehner, 2009) and \((105 \pm 26) \times 10^{-11}\) (Prades, 2009).
* For the pole approximation at the \(\pi^0\) mass, the dispersive evaluation yields a total contribution of \(5.70 \times 10^{-10}\) (sum of both topologies), in exact agreement with the direct two-loop calculation.
* The two-particle cut contributes \(2.42 \times 10^{-10}\) and the three-particle cut contributes \(3.28 \times 10^{-10}\) to the total.
* The future experimental error target is \(\delta a_\mu(\mathrm{exp}) = \pm 16 \times 10^{-11}\), a factor of four improvement over the present value.

## Context

This work extends the data-driven approach to the HLbL contribution recently discussed by Colangelo et al. (2014) by using a different dispersive representation based on the muon's electromagnetic vertex function rather than the HLbL tensor directly. It aims to overcome the model dependence of earlier estimates (Jegerlehner, 2009; Prades, 2009) that relied on hadronic models and produced large, uncontrolled uncertainties. The formalism enables future reduction of hadronic uncertainties by relating the required discontinuities to experimentally measurable processes such as \(e^+e^- \to \mathrm{hadrons}\) and two-photon production, with the two-pion intermediate state identified as the largest source of uncertainty to be addressed next.
