# Dual algebraic structures for the two-level pairing model

> Duality relations are explicitly established relating the Hamiltonians and basis classification schemes associated with the number-conserving unitary and number-nonconserving quasispin algebras for the two-level system with pairing interactions. These relations are obtained in a unified formulation for both bosonic and fermionic systems, with arbitrary and, in general, unequal degeneracies for the two levels. Illustrative calculations are carried out comparing the bosonic and fermionic quantum phase transitions.

## Key claim

The paper explicitly establishes duality relations between the number-conserving unitary algebra and the number-nonconserving quasispin algebra for the two-level pairing model, showing that the Hamiltonians and basis classification schemes from these two algebraic formulations are equivalent for both bosonic and fermionic systems with arbitrary and possibly unequal level degeneracies.

## Method

The authors systematically construct the generators and subalgebra chains for the unitary algebra $\mathrm{U}(n_1+n_2)$ and the quasispin algebra (either $\mathcal{SU}(1,1)$ for bosons or $\mathcal{SU}(2)$ for fermions) for a two-level system with level degeneracies $n_1$ and $n_2$. They derive the branching rules for the irreducible representations in both bosonic and fermionic cases, then establish duality relations that connect the Casimir operators of the unitary chain to the pairing Hamiltonian expressed in quasispin operators, providing a unified formulation valid for arbitrary level degeneracies.

## Key numbers

* For the bosonic $\mathrm{U}(n)\rightarrow\mathrm{O}(n)$ branching, the seniority quantum number $v$ takes values $v = (N \bmod 2), \ldots, N-2, N$, where $N$ is the occupation number.
* For the fermionic $\mathrm{U}(n)\rightarrow\mathrm{Sp}(n)$ branching, the seniority $v$ takes values $v = (N' \bmod 2), \ldots, N'-2, N'$, where $N' \equiv \min(N, n-N)$ and $v \leq \tfrac12 n$.
* The branching rule for $\mathrm{O}(n_1+n_2)\rightarrow\mathrm{O}(n_1)\otimes\mathrm{O}(n_2)$ in the bosonic case is $v = v_1 + v_2 + 2n_v$ with $n_v = 0,1,\ldots,\lfloor v/2\rfloor$, independent of the level degeneracies $n_1$ and $n_2$.
* For the fermionic $\mathrm{Sp}(n_1+n_2)\rightarrow\mathrm{Sp}(n_1)\otimes\mathrm{Sp}(n_2)$ branching, an additional constraint applies: $|(v_1-v_2) - \tfrac12(n_1-n_2)| \leq \tfrac12(n_1+n_2) - v$.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic treatment of two-level pairing models beyond the special cases previously studied, which were limited to $s$-$b$ boson models (with $n_1=1$) or fermionic models with equal degeneracies ($n_1=n_2$), as discussed in references such as Caprio (2008) and Rowe (2010). The results provide a foundation for future studies of quantum phase transitions, excited state spectroscopic structure, and classical geometry in two-level and multi-level pairing models, enabling systematic comparison between bosonic and fermionic systems with arbitrary level degeneracies.
