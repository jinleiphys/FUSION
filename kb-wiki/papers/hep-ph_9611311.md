# Isospin Breaking and Instantons in QCD Nucleon Sum Rules

> We study isospin breaking instanton corrections to the operator product expansion of the nucleon correlation functions. After a comparison with quark model calculations based on the 't Hooft interaction, we examine the role of instantons in the corresponding QCD sum rules. Instanton contributions are found to be absent in the chirally even sum rule, but significant in the chirally-odd one. They improve the consistency of both sum rules and favor a value of the isovector quark condensate close to the chiral estimate.

## Key claim

Instanton corrections to the isospin-violating nucleon QCD sum rules are absent in the chirally even sum rule but significant in the chirally odd one, and they improve the consistency between both sum rules while favoring a value of the isovector quark condensate close to the chiral perturbation theory estimate.

## Method

The authors calculated the leading instanton contributions to the nucleon correlation functions in the semiclassical approximation, using the quark zero-mode propagator in the instanton background field and the 't Hooft vertex. They combined these instanton corrections with the conventional operator product expansion (OPE) of the nucleon correlators, applied the Borel transform, and set up the QCD sum rule for the difference of the neutron and proton correlators. The analysis used the Ioffe interpolating current and the instanton liquid vacuum model with average instanton size and density.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass difference: δm = 3.3 MeV (standard value from Gasser and Leutwyler)
- Average instanton size: ρ̄ ≈ 1/3 fm
- Instanton number density: n̄ ≈ 1/2 fm⁻⁴
- QCD scale: Λ_QCD = 150 MeV
- Renormalization point: μ = 500 MeV
- Gluon condensate: b = ⟨g_s² G²⟩ = 0.5 GeV⁴
- Range of isovector quark condensate parameter γ from literature: -1×10⁻² ≤ γ ≤ -2×10⁻³

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of instanton corrections to nucleon sum rules (Forkel 1993) and isospin-symmetric instanton effects in the pion channel (Forkel 1995). It addresses discrepancies found in sum rule calculations of the neutron-proton mass difference without instanton corrections by Yang et al. and by Jin et al., where the chirally even and odd sum rules gave inconsistent results. The paper also compares with bag model calculations by Yano and by Dorokhov that used the 't Hooft interaction but found no instanton contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference, explaining this discrepancy as arising from the neglect of isospin-violating vacuum fields in the bag model wavefunctions. The results enable improved QCD sum rule determinations of isospin breaking parameters in the nucleon sector.
