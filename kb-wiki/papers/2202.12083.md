# Delta-resonances and hyperons in proto-neutron stars and merger remnants

> The equation of state (EoS) and composition of dense and hot $Δ$-resonance admixed hypernuclear matter is studied under conditions that are characteristic of neutron star binary merger remnants and supernovas. The cold, neutrino free regime is also considered as a reference for the astrophysical constraints on the EoS of dense matter. Our formalism uses the covariant density functional (CDF) theory successfully adapted to include the full $J^P=1/2^+$ baryon octet and non-strange members of $J^P=3/2^+$ decouplet with density-dependent couplings that have been suitably adjusted to the existing laboratory and astrophysical data. The effect of $Δ$-resonances at finite temperatures is to soften the EoS of hypernuclear matter at intermediate densities and stiffen it at high densities. At low temperatures, the heavy baryons $Λ$, $Δ^-$,$Ξ^-$, $Ξ^0$ and $Δ^0$ appear in the given order if the $Δ$-meson couplings are close to those for the nucleon-meson couplings. As is the case for hyperons, the thresholds of $Δ$-resonances move to lower densities with the increase of temperature indicating a significant fraction of $Δ$'s in the low-density subnuclear regime. We find that the $Δ$-resonances comprise a significant fraction of baryonic matter, of the order of $10\%$ at temperatures of the order of several tens of MeV in the neutrino-trapped regime and, thus, may affect the supernova and binary neutron star dynamics by providing, for example, a new source for neutrino opacity or a new channel for bulk viscosity via the direct Urca processes. The mass-radius relation of isentropic static, spherically symmetric hot compact stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Delta-resonances can comprise a significant fraction (about 10%) of baryonic matter at temperatures of several tens of MeV in neutrino-trapped matter, but this result depends on the specific choice of Delta-meson couplings being close to those for nucleon-meson couplings; if the omega-meson coupling to Delta-resonances is too low (R_omegaDelta less than or equal to 0.8), the matter undergoes spinodal instability.

## Method

The authors employ covariant density functional theory with density-dependent couplings to compute the equation of state and composition of matter containing the full J^P = 1/2+ baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Xi-, Xi0, Sigma0, Sigma+, Sigma-) and the non-strange J^P = 3/2+ Delta-resonance quartet (Delta-, Delta0, Delta+, Delta++) under conditions relevant to neutron star binary mergers and supernovae. They use the DDME2 parameterization for the nucleonic sector, SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry for hyperon-vector meson couplings, and include hidden strangeness sigma* and phi mesons for hyperon interactions. The calculations are performed in the mean-field approximation at finite temperature, with separate treatments for neutrino-free (cold) and neutrino-trapped regimes, fixing lepton numbers appropriate for binary neutron star mergers (Y_L,e = Y_L,mu = 0.1) and supernovae (Y_L,e = 0.4, Y_L,mu = 0).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n_sat = 0.152 fm^-3 for the DDME2 parameterization
- Neutrino trapping temperature T_tr approximately 5 MeV, above which neutrinos are trapped in matter
- Delta-resonances comprise approximately 10% of baryonic matter at temperatures of several tens of MeV in the neutrino-trapped regime
- For supernova conditions, electron lepton number Y_L,e = 0.4 and muon lepton number Y_L,mu = 0
- For binary neutron star merger conditions, Y_L,e = Y_L,mu = 0.1
- The range of Sigma hyperon potential at saturation density is -10 to +30 MeV
- The range of Xi hyperon potential at saturation density is -24 to 0 MeV, with a specific value of -17 MeV from analysis of the 9Be(K-,K+) reaction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of hot hypernuclear matter (Sedrakian 2021) and builds upon the code of Colucci (2013) to include Delta-resonances, differing from recent finite-temperature studies by Malfatti (2019), Spinella (2020), and Raduta (2020) by using the DDME2 parameterization and including hidden strangeness sigma* and phi mesons in addition to sigma, omega, and rho mesons. The results enable improved modeling of proto-neutron star evolution and binary neutron star merger dynamics by providing a new source for neutrino opacity and a new channel for bulk viscosity via direct Urca processes involving Delta-resonances.
