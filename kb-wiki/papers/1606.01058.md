# Two-gluon exchange contribution to elastic $γγ\to γγ$ scattering and production of two-photons in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion and proton-proton collisions

> We discuss the two-gluon exchange contribution (formally three-loops) to elastic photon-photon scattering in the high-energy approximation. The elastic $γγ\to γγ$ amplitude is given in the impact-factor representation for all helicity configurations and finite quark masses. We discuss the importance of including the charm quark, which contribution, due to interference, can enhance the cross section considerably. We investigate the contribution to the $γγ\to γγ$ amplitude from the soft region, by studying its dependence on nonperturbative gluon mass. Helicity-flip contributions are shown to be much smaller than helicity-conserving ones. We identify region(s) of phase space where the two-gluon exchange contribution becomes important ingredient compared to box and nonperturbative VDM-Regge mechanisms considered in the literature. Consequences for the $A A \to A A γγ$ reaction are discussed. Several differential distributions are shown. A feasibility study to observe the effect of two-gluon exchange is presented. We perform a similar analysis for the $p p \to p p γγ$ reaction. Only by imposing severe cuts on $M_{γγ}$ and a narrow window on photon transverse momenta the two gluon contribution becomes comparable to the box contribution but the corresponding cross section is rather small.

## Key claim

The two-gluon exchange contribution to elastic photon-photon scattering can become comparable to the box contribution only under severe cuts on the diphoton invariant mass and a narrow window on photon transverse momenta, but the corresponding cross section is rather small; the charm quark contribution can enhance the cross section considerably through interference effects.

## Method

The authors computed the three-loop two-gluon exchange amplitude for elastic gamma-gamma scattering using an impact-factor representation in the high-energy approximation, including finite quark masses and all helicity configurations. They evaluated the amplitude for light (u,d,s) and charm quarks, studied the dependence on a nonperturbative gluon mass parameter (0 and 0.75 GeV), and compared the results with the fermion-box mechanism and the VDM-Regge mechanism. The elementary cross sections were then folded with equivalent photon fluxes for ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions and proton-proton collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 0.15 GeV, m_s = 0.3 GeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Gluon mass regularization parameter: m_g = 0.0 GeV and m_g = 0.75 GeV
- Helicity-flip contributions are about three orders of magnitude smaller than helicity-conserving contributions at W = 10 GeV
- At W = 50 GeV, a window exists where two-gluon exchange is visible: 1 GeV < p_t < 2 GeV
- At W = 200 GeV, the window where two-gluon exchange dominates extends to 1 GeV < p_t < 5 GeV
- For pp collisions at LHC with M_{γγ} > 10 GeV and 1 GeV < p_t < 2 GeV, the two-gluon exchange contribution is less than 10% of the box contribution
- For pp collisions, the two-gluon exchange distribution starts to dominate over the box contribution only above M_{γγ} > 50 GeV for 1 GeV < p_t < 5 GeV
- The difference between massive (m_g = 750 MeV) and massless gluon exchange amounts to almost one order of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculation of Ginzburg (1985) by including finite fermion masses, full momentum structure in the loops, and all helicity amplitudes. It builds on the recent calculation by Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek (2016) which found substantially larger cross sections for elastic photon-photon scattering via box and VDM-Regge mechanisms. The results enable feasibility studies for observing two-gluon exchange in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and in exclusive pp collisions, though the cross sections are small and would require forward calorimeters and large integrated luminosity.
