# Nucleons and Isobars at finite density ($ρ$) and temperature (T)

> The importance of studying matter at high $ρ$ increases as more astrophysical data becomes available from recently launched spacecrafts. The importance of high T studies derives from heavy ion data. In this paper we set up a formalism to study the nucleons and isobars with long and short range potentials non-pertubatively, bosonizing and expanding semi-classically the Feyman integrals up to one loop. We address the low density, finite T problem=A0 first, the case relevant to heavy ion collisions, hoping to adresss the high density case later. Interactions change the nucleon and isobar numbers at different $ρ$ and T non-trivially.

## Key claim

The paper sets up a non-perturbative formalism to study nucleons and isobars (Delta baryons) at finite density and temperature using bosonization and a semiclassical one-loop expansion of Feynman integrals, but explicitly addresses only the low-density, finite-temperature regime relevant to heavy ion collisions, deferring the high-density astrophysical case to future work.

## Method

The authors use a path integral technique to derive the grand canonical partition function for a system of nucleons and Delta isobars interacting via one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) and rho-meson exchange, with short-range correlations (SRC) included via a correlation function approximation. They bosonize the fermionic fields using a Hubbard-Stratonovitch transformation, then expand the partition function semiclassically up to one-loop order, obtaining the grand potential and expressions for the nucleon and Delta numbers as functions of chemical potential and temperature. The formalism yields generalized Lindhard functions at finite temperature for nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-Delta, and Delta-Delta particle-hole propagators, and the particle numbers are computed from derivatives of the grand potential with respect to chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

* Chemical potential is computed for five densities: rho/rho_0 = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0, where rho_0 denotes normal nuclear density.
* For short-range correlations, the pion cut-off momentum is q_c^pi = 770 MeV/c and the rho cut-off is q_c^rho = 1200 MeV/c.
* The rho-meson coupling constant used is C_rho = 2.3.
* Sensitivity of the ratio rho_Delta/rho_N to the pion cut-off is tested with q_c^pi = 650, 770, and 900 MeV/c; the ratio increases with q_c^pi in an intermediate temperature range, but the effect fades near 150 MeV.
* The Delta-nucleon mass splitting delta M = M_Delta - M is used in the free Delta Green's function.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-perturbative path integral methods developed by Cenni and co-workers (Alberico et al., 1987, 1988; Cenni and Saracco, 1994; Cenni et al., 1997) to finite temperature and includes Delta isobars explicitly. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Anand et al. (1979), which overestimated the Delta component of the nuclear ground state, and with non-interacting thermal models such as Bebie et al. (1992) and Dey et al. (1994). The formalism enables future calculations of effective masses, widths, and entropy in a hadron gas, and can be extended to include vector mesons, strange hyperons, and a temperature expansion.
