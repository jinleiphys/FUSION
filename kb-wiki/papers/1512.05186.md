# Heavy-flavour production in high-energy d-Au and p-Pb collisions

> Soft-hadron measurements in high-energy collisions of small systems like p-Pb and d-Au show peculiar qualitative features (long-range rapidity correlations, flattening of the $p_T$-spectra with increasing hadron mass and centrality, non-vanishing Fourier harmonics in the azimuthal particle distributions) suggestive of the formation of a strongly-interacting medium displaying a collective behaviour, with a hydrodynamic flow as a response to the pressure gradients in the initial conditions. Hard observables (high-$p_T$ jet and hadron spectra) on the other hand, within the current large systematic uncertainties, appear only midly modified with the respect to the benchmark case of minimum-bias p-p collisions. What should one expect for heavy-flavour particles, initially produced in hard processes but tending, in the nucleus-nucleus case, to approach kinetic equilibrium with the rest of the medium? This is the issue we address in the present study, showing how the current experimental findings are compatible with a picture in which the formation of a hot medium even in proton-nucleus collisions modifies the propagation and hadronization of heavy-flavour particles.

## Key claim

The paper argues that current experimental findings for heavy-flavour production in d-Au and p-Pb collisions are compatible with a picture in which a hot deconfined medium is formed even in these small systems, modifying the propagation and hadronization of heavy-flavour particles, rather than being explained solely by initial-state cold nuclear matter effects.

## Method

The authors simulate heavy-quark propagation in a hydrodynamic background using a relativistic Langevin equation, with initial conditions from a Monte-Carlo Glauber model that includes event-by-event fluctuations in nucleon positions. The hydrodynamic evolution of the medium is performed with the ECHO-QGP code using viscous runs with η/s=0.08, and heavy quarks are hadronized via a recombination mechanism with thermal light quarks from the medium, followed by string fragmentation in PYTHIA. Initial heavy-quark production is simulated with the POWHEG-BOX package including EPS09 nuclear PDF modifications and transverse-momentum broadening proportional to the number of binary collisions.

## Key numbers

- d-Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV: thermalization time τ0=0.25 fm/c, with smearing parameter σsmear=0.2-0.4 fm
- p-Pb collisions at √sNN=5.02 TeV: thermalization time τ0=0.25 fm/c, with smearing parameter σsmear=0.2-0.4 fm
- The decoupling temperature Tdec=155 MeV used for hadronization
- The medium lives for about 2-3 fm/c in the deconfined phase
- PHENIX data for non-photonic electrons in central d-Au collisions show RdAu≈1.4 over a pT-range from 1 to 5 GeV/c
- The charm and beauty RdAu curves display a bump at intermediate pT attributed to radial flow, with the effect larger at the hadron level than at the quark level
- Pion elliptic flow v2 in central p-Pb collisions is approximately reproduced by the hydrodynamic background with different smearing parameters

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous POWLANG transport framework, originally developed for A-A collisions (Alberico 2011, Alberico 2013, Beraudo 2014), to small systems like d-Au and p-Pb. It builds on experimental observations of collective effects in small systems at RHIC and LHC, including long-range rapidity correlations and non-vanishing flow harmonics. The study provides a dynamical microscopic justification for the earlier blast-wave analysis by Sickles (2013), which suggested that radial flow could explain the enhanced RdAu of heavy-flavour decay electrons. The results enable future event-by-event (3+1)D hydrodynamic analyses and provide a framework to interpret recent electron-hadron and muon-hadron correlation measurements in p-Pb collisions.
