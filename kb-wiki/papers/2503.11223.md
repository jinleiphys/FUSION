# Triple-charmed Hadron from Coalescence in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We investigate the production of the $Ω_{ccc}$ baryon in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Unlike proton-proton collisions, nuclear collisions produce both deconfined matter and abundant charm quark pairs, which can coalesce to form the $Ω_{ccc}$ baryon, thereby significantly enhancing its production. We employ the Langevin model and the Instantaneous Coalescence Model (LICM), coupled with hydrodynamic simulations, to study charm quark diffusion and coalescence into the $Ω_{ccc}$ baryon in expanding QCD matter. The production of the $Ω_{ccc}$ is governed by the charm quark densities and the in-medium wavefunctions of the $Ω_{ccc}$, which determines the coalescence probability for the three charm quarks. We calculate the $Ω_{ccc}$ production with realistic charm diffusions and different in-medium wave functions of $Ω_{ccc}$ baryon. We find that the production of the $Ω_{ccc}$ baryon is sensitive to these factors, which aids in understanding its properties in the hot QCD medium.

## Key claim

The production of the $\Omega_{ccc}$ baryon in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV is sensitive to both the degree of charm quark kinetic thermalization (controlled by the spatial diffusion coefficient $\mathcal{D}_s$) and the in-medium wave function of the $\Omega_{ccc}$ (parameterized by its root-mean-square radius), meaning that the predicted yield is not a single number but a band depending on these uncertain inputs.

## Method

The authors couple a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation (MUSIC) of the bulk QGP medium with a Langevin equation to evolve charm quarks event-by-event, incorporating drag, noise, and medium-induced gluon radiation. At a coalescence temperature of $T_{\text{coal}} = 1.2 T_c$ (with $T_c = 0.15$ GeV), the Instantaneous Coalescence Model is applied using a Gaussian Wigner function for the three-charm-quark system, where the Gaussian width is determined by the assumed root-mean-square radius of the $\Omega_{ccc}$ baryon. The yield is then obtained by integrating the coalescence probability over the phase-space distributions of three uncorrelated charm quarks, weighted by the cube of the charm pair number in the nuclear collision.

## Key numbers

- The charm quark mass is taken as $m_c = 1.5$ GeV.
- Three values of the spatial diffusion coefficient are used: $\mathcal{D}_s 2\pi T = 2.0, 4.0, 6.0$ for temperatures above $T_c = 0.15$ GeV.
- Three values of the root-mean-square radius of the $\Omega_{ccc}$ at the coalescence temperature are considered: $\sqrt{\langle r_\Omega^2 \rangle}(T_{\rm coal}) = 0.3, 0.36, 0.5$ fm.
- The differential charm pair production cross section in pp collisions at central rapidity is $d\sigma^{pp}_{c\bar{c}}/dy = 1.16$ mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV.
- The statistical factor for the spin-3/2 $\Omega_{ccc}$ is $g_B = 1/54$.
- The shadowing factor for charm pair production in central nuclear collisions is approximately $\mathcal{R}_S \approx 0.7$.
- The yield at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV with $\sqrt{\langle r_\Omega^2 \rangle}(T_{\rm coal}) = 0.5$ fm is about 4.0 times the yield calculated at 2.76 TeV by He (2014), consistent with the factor $(1.16/0.7)^3 \approx 4.5$ expected from the increased charm cross section.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin plus Instantaneous Coalescence Model (LICM) previously applied by Chen (2021) to open and hidden charmed mesons (D and J/psi) in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, now applied to the triple-charm baryon Omega_ccc. It improves upon earlier calculations by He (2014) and Minissale (2024) by incorporating realistic, non-thermalized charm quark momentum distributions evolved via the Langevin equation (rather than assuming full kinetic thermalization) and by systematically varying both the diffusion coefficient and the in-medium wave function radius to quantify uncertainties. The results provide a predicted band for Omega_ccc production that can guide experimental searches at the LHC and help constrain the in-medium triple-heavy-quark potential and charm quark diffusion coefficient.
