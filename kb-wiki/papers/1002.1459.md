# Cooper pair sizes in superfluid nuclei in a simplified model

> Cooper pair sizes are evaluated in a simple harmonic oscillator model reproducing the values of sophisticated HFB calculations. Underlying reasons for the very small sizes of 2.0-2.5 fm of Cooper pairs in the surface of nuclei are analysed. It is shown that the confining properties of the nuclear volume is the dominating effect. It is argued that for Cooper pair sizes LDA is particularly inadapted.

# Cooper pair sizes in superfluid nuclei in a simplified model

## Key claim

Cooper pair sizes in the surface of superfluid nuclei are surprisingly small (2.0–2.5 fm) not because of particularly strong pairing in the nuclear surface, but because the confining properties of the nuclear volume dominate the spatial extension of the single-particle wave functions near the Fermi energy. The coherence length is practically independent of the pairing strength for typical nuclear gaps where Δ << ħω, meaning the system is always close to the asymptotic limit of vanishing pairing for this quantity.

## Method

The authors construct a simplified spherical harmonic oscillator (HO) potential model (without spin-orbit) for the mean field, combined with a realistic treatment of pairing using the Gogny D1S force. They compute the anomalous density matrix in Wigner (phase) space using shell-averaged BCS amplitudes and gap parameters obtained from a gap equation with matrix elements averaged over major HO shells. The coherence length (CL) is evaluated as the local rms value of the Cooper pair wave function from the anomalous density, and the results are compared with full self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Cooper pair sizes in the nuclear surface are 2.0–2.5 fm, about a factor 2–3 times smaller than the smallest size in nuclear matter at low densities.
- For a symmetric nucleus with A=120, the oscillator length b ≈ 2.2 fm, comparable to the minimum coherence length.
- For the N=1 shell (A=12), the minimum coherence length is ξ(R)_min = 2.0629 fm at R_min = 2.1740 fm, with a maximum of ξ(R)_max = 4.3476 fm at R_max = 1.0315 fm.
- For the N=2 shell (A=28), the minimum coherence length is ξ(R)_min = 2.1838 fm at R_min = 3.4079 fm, with a maximum of ξ(R)_max = 6.5700 fm at R_max = 1.0960 fm.
- The minimum of the coherence length increases slowly with particle number, approximately as A^(1/6); for A=8000 the minimum value is about 4 fm.
- Intrashell mixing reduces the minimum coherence length by about 0.5 fm from non-averaged values in the HO model, and by about 30 percent in self-consistent HFB calculations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' preceding study (Vinas, Schuck, Pillet 2010) which identified finite nuclear size as the reason for small Cooper pair extensions, and earlier findings (Pillet 2007, Pastore 2008, Matsuo 2005) that revealed surprisingly small Cooper pair sizes in the surface of superfluid nuclei. The results contradict the common belief from Bohr and Mottelson (1975) that Cooper pair sizes in nuclei are about the nuclear dimension based on weak coupling estimates. The paper demonstrates that the Local Density Approximation (LDA) is particularly inadequate for estimating coherence lengths in finite nuclei, though it may give reasonable averages for other quantities such as pairing energy (Kucharek 1989). The findings enable understanding of pair transfer in nuclear reactions (Oertzen 2001) and suggest two distinct regimes: one for finite nuclei where ħω >> Δ (coherence length independent of pairing), and another for infinite matter or cold fermionic atoms in traps where ħω << Δ (coherence length inversely proportional to the gap).
